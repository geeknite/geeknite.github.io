## Overview
Dixit is one of those party games that sounds like a lullaby and then dizzyingly turns into a storytelling brain-teaser. The 2019 edition, often cataloged under the DIX01 code by Libellud and Asmodee, is a refreshed take on a cherished classic. It keeps the dreamy, surreal art that makes you squint and smile at the same time, while polishing the components and streamlining the setup so you can get to the storytelling without a deck-building ceremony. If you enjoy games that reward creativity over conquest, Dixit is your jam. If you also enjoy arguing about whether a clue actually fit, you’ll love Dixit even more.

The core idea is simple: a storyteller gives a clue about one of their cards, the other players pick a card that best matches that clue, and then everyone votes on which card is the storyteller’s. Simple enough for a family game night, yet elastic enough for witty banter that makes you feel you’ve just participated in a low-stakes indie theatre production. In the 2019 edition, the recipe remains the same, but the ingredients have a touch more polish and a touch less scent of “we printed this last decade, right?” The result is a game that feels simultaneously timeless and refreshed, like a favorite hoodie that finally got a spa day.

## How to Play (A Quick Primer)
### The roles and the flow
1. Each round, one player becomes the storyteller. They select a card from their hand and give a clue—this can be a single word, a phrase, a lyric, a pun, or a tiny story fragment.
2. The other players choose a card from their own hands that they think best matches the storyteller’s clue, without showing it to others.
3. The chosen cards are shuffled and laid face up. The storyteller reveals which card was theirs, and players vote on which card they think is the storyteller’s.
4. Points are awarded: the storyteller earns points if at least one other player picks their card, but not everyone; the players who guessed correctly also earn points; if no one guesses the storyteller’s card, the storyteller scores nothing and everyone else scores a little bit from strategic chaos.
5. A new round begins with a new storyteller. The game ends when the deck runs out of cards or at a previously agreed-upon score threshold.

That’s the bones. The skin—how it feels to play—comes from the storytelling and the art. The game thrives on the tension between clever clues and the risk of letting your group drift into too-obscure territory. It’s a social puzzle with a rhythm that changes with each group dynamic. Some players lean into poetry; others like clever pop culture references; some just love to watch the room squirm as they realize the clue is a perfect fit or a spectacular mismatch.

## Strategy, Tips, and House Rules
### For storytellers
- Start with a playful, evocative clue rather than something too on-the-nose. If your clue is too obvious, you risk giving away your card; if it’s too abstract, you risk your own victory. The sweet spot is a clue that hints at the card without naming it.
- Consider your group’s shared experiences. A clue tied to a popular movie, a local meme, or a common hobby can be powerful if others are likely to recognize it.
- Balance risk and reward. If you think most players will guess your card, you’ll earn a smaller number of points by letting others collide with your clue. If you suspect your clue won’t land, adjust your expectation to maximize reward when someone does pick your card.

### For guessers
- Read the room. When a storyteller’s clue is surprising, think about what they’re trying to evoke—puns, mood, or a narrative beat. Your goal is not to “solve” the clue, but to choose a card that could plausibly fit the story the storyteller is crafting.
- Embrace the unknown. Sometimes the best card is the one that seems almost-but-not-quite right. It’s the tension between alignment and misalignment that makes voting exciting.
- Use your knowledge of other players. If you know someone tends to prefer whimsical images, craft your vote accordingly. A little insight goes a long way toward victory without spoiling the communal fun.

## How It Compares: Dixit vs Dixit Odyssey and Other Party Games
Dixit Odyssey is a cousin rather than a twin. Odyssey introduces a board and expansion mechanics that alter scoring dynamics and pacing. If you crave longer campaigns or team-style play with a bit more structure, Odyssey scratches that itch. Dixit, especially in the 2019 edition, leans toward short, looping rounds where stories bloom and vanish quickly, which is perfect for a night when attention spans drift but imagination remains stubbornly awake.

Compared to other party games, Dixit occupies its own lane. It doesn’t rely on memory tests, word association speed, or hidden information warfare. Instead, it rewards storytelling, empathy, and a willingness to interpret imagery in courageously creative ways. You might walk away with a new inside joke, a surprisingly heartfelt story about a cat that learned to fly, or a debate about whether a card represents a dream or a fever dream. All of these outcomes are part of the Dixit charm and the reason it remains a staple in many game libraries.

## Accessibility, Age, and Play Style
Dixit is widely accessible to families, friends, and colleagues who want to connect through imagination. It’s recommended for ages eight and up, but I’ve watched younger players contribute delightful, earnest clues that remind everyone to appreciate the childlike wonder that games can unlock. The rule set is simple: tell a story, match a card, vote. The challenge is in crafting clues that are evocative, not immediately obvious, and occasionally delightfully wrong in the most entertaining way possible.

For larger groups, you can run multiple rounds in quick succession or split into teams to add a cooperative flavor. For smaller groups, one or two players can take the lead, creating a more intimate storytelling session. For veteran players who enjoy a more intellectual puzzle, you can experiment with variant rules like double-blind clues or timed rounds to tighten the pace and encourage sharper thinking. The 2019 edition supports these variations well, giving you flexibility without forcing gimmicks that break the mood.
