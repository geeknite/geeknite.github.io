## Fit, Feel, and Handling — Do They Play Nice With Your Cards?

### Outer Sleeve Fit

The outer sleeves are designed to cover standard Cerebria action and event cards. Expect a snug fit on most cards. They’re not so tight that you’ll need a crowbar to peel one off, but you’ll feel the difference if you try to force a card out without loosening the area first.

The thickness of the outer sleeves is in the middle ground: not flimsy, not tank-grade. It’s the “I want to protect my art but not add the weight of a protein shake to every shuffle” zone. Cards slide smoothly in and out, with minimal resistance, which is exactly what you want when your gameplay hinges on fast decisions.

### Inner Sleeves and Artwork Revelation

Inner sleeves protect the artwork, and they do it with a whisper rather than a shout. The inner-sleeve design uses a clear film with a slightly matte finish, which reduces glare during heavy lighting conditions. The effect is that you can appreciate Cerebria’s art without fighting reflections off the plastic, which is a huge win in long sessions where you might be staring at the board for hours on end.

The trade-off, of course, is a slightly stiffer hand feel. If you’re the kind of player who loves a “soft sheet” shuffle, you’ll notice the difference here. But the Inside World sleeves balance that by providing enough rigidity to prevent card bending during long campaigns, while keeping enough flexibility to avoid that dreaded “snap” when cards ricochet off the table after a misclick.

### Thickness and Shuffle Quality

A common complaint with fancy sleeves is that they can ruin the tactile feedback of shuffles. The Inside World set lands in a comfortable middle ground: the combination of outer + inner sleeves adds some bulk but not enough to impede shuffle rhythm. The fingers glide across the surface with the same grace you’d expect from a well-lubricated hinge. If you’re playing with a large hand or using a lot of special event cards, you’ll likely appreciate the extra fat of the outer sleeve; it helps the deck feel substantial in your hands rather than a fragile stack of cardstock.

Cerebria’s Inside World is a world of emotional architecture—colors that feel like a sunrise and shadows that hint at complexity underneath. ## Build Quality and Longevity

### Materials and Durability

The sleeves use a standard PET (polyethylene terephthalate) blend, which is common for mid-range sleeve sets. In tests, they resist yellowing after prolonged exposure to strong room lighting (important for those “sunlight is a villain” play spaces). Edge wear is minimal after repeated shuffles and table mishaps, which is big for players who like to keep their games pristine for resale value or for the next generation of tabletop heroes.

### Longevity and Aging

The real test of any sleeve is how long it lasts under daily use. The Inside World sleeves hold up to repeated sleeves, re-sleeving after top-deck reveals, and the occasional clumsy finger that slips and taps a card into someone’s shocked gaze. In our trials, after 40-60 games, the corners show little fraying, and the overall opacity remains stable. If you’re the type who replays a game weekly for years, you’ll want to budget for occasional replacements, but that’s true of most sleeves when you’re pushing a game’s life expectancy into the stratosphere.

### Cleaning and Maintenance

Sleeves are easy to clean with a damp cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could strip the art or the sleeve’s surface. A quick wipe after a spill keeps the sleeves looking sharp. We found that a light dusting with a microfiber cloth between sessions goes a long way toward keeping the art crisp and the edges unscuffed.
