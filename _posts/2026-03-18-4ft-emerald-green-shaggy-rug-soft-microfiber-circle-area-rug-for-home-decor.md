## Cleaning and maintenance

A rug this size and material is not exactly machine washable. The safe approach is regular vacuuming with a low-suction setting and a brush attachment to lift fibers that may have flattened. Vacuuming helps maintain the plush look and prevents dirt from embedding into the fibers. Use slow, deliberate passes rather than high-powered blasts that might push fibers in odd directions.

For spots and spills, use a mild detergent or rug-cleaning solution diluted with water. Blot rather than rub to prevent color transfer or fiber damage. For tougher stains, pre-treat with a small amount of stain remover suitable for microfiber, then blot and rinse carefully. Always test a small, inconspicuous area before treating larger patches.

If you’re dealing with pet accidents or food spills, act quickly. The quicker you address a stain, the better your long-term color retention will be. A quick vacuum after cleaning sessions helps restore the nap and prevents the fibers from clumping in certain directions.

Tip: Keep the rug away from direct heat sources like radiators or space heaters. Excess heat can affect the nap and color consistency over time. If you need to refresh the pile, a quick light steam from a clothing steamer, held at a distance, can help lift flattened fibers without saturating the backing.

## Durability and color longevity

Color longevity is a big concern with vivid greens. Proper care helps maintain the emerald vibrancy for years. Microfiber fibers tend to resist fading when exposed to typical indoor lighting; however, prolonged direct sunlight can cause color loss or uneven fading. If you have large sun exposure in the room, consider rotating the rug or using window treatments to curb UV exposure.

The rug’s pile is designed to withstand everyday use, but like all fabrics, it will show wear over time. Foot traffic tends to wear the nap at the most-trodden paths, creating a slightly lighter or darker look along those lines. This is a natural phenomenon and not a defect, and it can even add character over time. If you’re aiming for a consistently uniform appearance, you may want to rotate or flip the rug area occasionally to distribute wear more evenly.
