### Condition: NM and What It Means
NM stands for Near Mint, a grading that says: the card looks like it could fetch a mint sticker at a convention vendor, but might show the slightest signs of handling. It could be a few micro-texture lines or a whisper of edge wear that only the most obsessive inventory software can detect. For the collector who wants to sniff out a pristine specimen from a distance, NM is the sweet spot: flawless to the casual eye, but with a trace of personality that whispers, “you know this card has seen the table, not the garden.” In a Japanese print, NM can be even more alluring because of the die-cut glints in the holo pattern and the crispness of typography that the Japanese printers are known for.
