## Display, Care, and Preservation
### Storage Tips for NM Japanese Prints
Preservation is the quiet hero. A Gloom SV4a NM copy deserves a display home with proper humidity, dust control, and binder pages that aren’t overstuffed. Use high-clarity sleeves, add semi-rigid top-loaders for display-facing protection, and avoid direct sunlight. Your holo will thank you by staying vibrant for longer. The micro-texture that makes the foil feel alive loves stable environments.

### Display Options
Traditional binders with archival sleeves work reliably, but you can dial up the presentation with acrylic displays, tiered shelves, or a glass-front case that doesn’t demand constant dusting. If you opt for open displays, ensure the card remains flat, uncluttered, and free of edges that could rub the holo. A small LED strip above the display can bring out the shimmer without overheating the binder, and a small microfiber cloth nearby is ideal for a quick buff between admiration sessions.
