## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh
Let’s face it: area rugs take the brunt of everyday life. They host spilled drinks, dropped snacks, and the occasional foot-aslant incident when you realize you left the vacuum cleaner in the other room.

### Everyday Cleaning
The Mesa Sterling synthetic rug is designed for straightforward maintenance. Vacuum regularly to remove dust and debris; use the vacuum with the beater bar turned off or on a low setting to avoid pulling at the fibers. For small spills, blot with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent solution if necessary. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the fibers.

### Stain Resistance and Special Spills
Synthetic fibers like polypropylene resist many common stains, but there are exceptions. Red wine, coffee, and ketchup deserve a quick response. The key is to blot, not rub, and to treat promptly. For tougher stains, a gentle carpet cleaner recommended by the manufacturer is usually sufficient. Do not saturate the rug; you’re aiming for damp, not swamp.

### Edge, Backing, and Drying
After cleaning, allow the rug to dry thoroughly to prevent mold growth. If you live in a humid climate, consider a dehumidifier or a fan to speed up the process. The backing is designed to retain its shape while drying, so you won’t end up with a wavy, puddle of a rug.

## Eco-Friendliness and Safety: A Responsible Choice?
Synthetic rugs are often more resource-efficient than natural fibers because they can be produced with less water and less land use. The Mesa Sterling rug family emphasizes durability and long life as a sustainability proxy: fewer replacements, less waste.
