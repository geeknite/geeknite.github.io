## Assembly and Painting Tips (Minimal Spoilers)

Let’s be honest: you’re probably going to spend some time assembling these minis before you can play. The key to a satisfying result is patience and a tiny amount of planning. Here are some practical tips that can save you hours of frustration:

- Use a sharp hobby knife to trim flash and mold lines. A light touch goes a long way.
- Wash the minis with mild soap and warm water to remove any residue from manufacturing. It helps the primer stick better.
- Prime using a matte gray or black primer. White primer can reveal every misstroke, so unless you are aiming for a snowy necromancer vibe, consider gray or black.
- Start with base coats for skin tones, leathers, and metals. Build up with washes to bring out details.
- Use thin layers: thin paint is your friend. You want multiple translucent layers rather than a single thick one that pools in crevices.
- Seal with a matte varnish to protect your work. A light spray at a time is safer than a heavy hand.
