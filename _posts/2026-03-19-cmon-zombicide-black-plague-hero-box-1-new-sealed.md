## Assembly and Painting Tips (Minimal Spoilers, Major Fun)
Yes, you’re probably going to assemble these minis before you play. Here are practical tips, with extra flair for the more dramatic hobbyists among you:

- Use a sharp hobby knife to trim flash and mold lines. A light touch and a fresh blade are your best friends.
- A quick wash with mild soap and warm water removes manufacturing residue and helps primer stick. Patience is a virtue here, especially if you plan to paint in neon future-palette hues.
- Prime with matte gray or black. White primer can highlight every mistake and turn your shading into a tutorial on “how not to paint.”
- Base coats first, then build up with washes and layered highlights. Thin layers win the day; thick coats look like you tried to paint while riding a roller coaster.
- Do multiple thin layers rather than a single thick one. It’s less painful to the model and less frustrating for your brush.
- Seal with a matte varnish to protect your masterpiece from the inevitable dragon of coffee spillages and accidental nose prints from your cat during play.
