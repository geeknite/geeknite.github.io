## Assembly and Painting Tips (Minimal Spoilers, Major Fun)
Let’s lay out practical, no-nonsense tips for turning these minis into tabletop legends without ruining your weekend:

- Use a sharp hobby knife to trim flash and mold lines. A light touch saves you from the heartbreak of nicked edges on a prime-worthy miniature.
- A quick wash with mild soap and warm water removes manufacturing residue and helps primer stick. This is your pre-paint spa day.
- Prime with matte gray or black. White primer reveals every misstep and feels like a tutorial in “how not to shade.”
- Base coats first, then build up with washes and layered highlights. Thin layers win the day; thick coats look like you painted while riding a roller coaster.
- Do multiple thin layers rather than one thick one. It’s less painful for the model and less chaotic for your brush.
- Seal with a matte varnish to protect your masterpiece from coffee spills and accidental nosing-of-a-sword moments during dramatic reveals.
