## Materials and Maintenance: Polypropylene Power with Spot-Clean Simplicity
Polypropylene is the unsung hero of the budget-friendly rug world. It’s resistant to moisture, relatively stain-averse, and easy to clean—traits that matter if you’re tossing this rug into high-traffic areas like entryways, kitchens, or covered patios.

- Construction quality: The Luna rug uses a flat-weave/polypropylene blend that provides durability without excessive weight. It’s not an ultra-plush throne for your feet, but it’s sturdy enough to survive a wandering pair of muddy boots and a curious cat who believes the rug is a sandbox.
- Spot-clean friendly: When life hands you a spill, you don’t need a full cleaning rig. A simple spot-clean approach—blot, clean with mild detergent, rinse if needed, and air dry—will keep the design intact. The polypropylene fibers resist water absorption, which makes the stain removal process less dramatic and less likely to transform into a two-alarm incident.
- Maintenance routine: Regular vacuuming (without a beater bar on high) will keep dirt at bay and prevent grit from grinding into the fibers. If you’re cool with a quick weekly refresh and a deeper clean every few months, this rug will hold up without turning cleaning into a full-time hobby.

## Cleaning, Stain Resistance, and Long-Term Care
Spot-clean convenience is the name of the game here. The surface is designed to accept a mild detergent solution with minimal scrubbing, followed by a thorough rinse and air drying. A few pro tips to keep this rug looking sharp for years:

- Act fast on spills: Blots beat scrubbing. Soak up as much liquid as possible with a clean cloth, then treat with a gentle solution if needed. Avoid harsh chemicals that can alter color or fiber integrity.
- Vacuum regularly: Use a vacuum without a rotating beater bar or with a sensitive setting to avoid pulling fibers. Refrain from using a metal-toothed comb or sharp object on the surface—we don’t want stray fibers popping up like little gossip columns.
- Outdoor storage: If you remove it for winter storage, roll it rather than fold it to prevent creases. Store in a dry area to prevent mold growth, especially in humid climates.
- Deep cleaning: Every few months, bring it outside for a thorough rinse with a mild soap solution, then air dry fully before bringing it back inside. Avoid leaving it damp for extended periods, which can invite mildew in poorly ventilated spaces.
