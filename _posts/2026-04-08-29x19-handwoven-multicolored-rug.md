### Durability vs. flexibility
Hand-woven rugs in this size often balance durability with pliability. The fibers are designed to be stepping-stones in daily life rather than museum pieces that require velvet ropes. ### Dye behavior and colorfastness
Dyes on a multi-colored rug of this type tend to be robust but not overbearing. The important note here is that colorfastness can vary by batch, due to the natural variability in hand-dying processes. If you place this rug in bright sunlight for extended periods, you may notice slight fading in the sunniest patches. This is not a flaw; it’s a small reminder that the piece is alive with color, not a plastic laminate. If you want to preserve tone longevity, rotate the rug periodically and use UV-blocking window treatments when possible.

## Care and maintenance
### A practical care routine
- Vacuum regularly without the beater bar on the highest setting; a gentle pass or two will remove dust without pulling on delicate fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and color exposure. This is especially important with directional patterns or when sunlight hits one area more than another.
- Spot clean with a mild detergent and cold water. Do not rub aggressively; you want to minimize pilling and fiber distortion.
- If a spill occurs, act quickly. Blot gently with a clean cloth, then follow up with a damp cloth to lift the residue. Avoid hot water that could set stains.
- Professional cleaning every 1-2 years can extend the life of your rug, especially if it is in a high-traffic area or exposed to pets.

### How the rug handles real life
