Practical Tips for Getting the Most from Your Dice Set
------------------------------------------------------

- Develop a little pre-roll ritual: three taps, a breath, then roll. It sounds silly, but it can help you settle into the moment.
- Keep the dice accessible: a compact tray near the board keeps things smooth and reduces table shenanigans between players.
- Color code your own memory: if you’re color-blind-friendly, you can assign survivors to a color you’re comfortable with and designate zombies to the darker tone. The contrast helps with quick recognition during tense turns.
- Use a dice cup or a small bag to shake the dice; this can minimize edge-bouncing on a crowded table and prevent accidental scrambles of your plan mid-turn.
- Store with care: avoid sunlight to prevent color fading, and consider silica gel packs to keep humidity down, since plastic can sometimes collect a little dew in high-humidity rooms.
