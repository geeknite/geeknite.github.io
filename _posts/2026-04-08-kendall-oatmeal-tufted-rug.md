### Maintenance and Care: Keeping the Oatmeal Clean and Cozy

Maintenance is where many rug owners either fall in love or run screaming for a vacuum that uses nuclear power. Here are practical care tips for the Kendall Oatmeal Tufted Rug:

- Regular vacuuming: Use a low- to medium-power setting with a vacuum that has a beater bar or a gentle suction head. Go with long, straight passes rather than frantic zigzags to avoid lifting fibers. A weekly pass is reasonable for most households; more frequent if you have pets that shed or a kitchen spill merry-go-round.
- Spot cleaning: For small stains, blot (don’t rub) with a clean white cloth and a mild detergent solution. Work from the outside in to prevent spreading. Always test cleaners on a hidden corner first to ensure colorfastness.
- Deep cleaning: Depending on your climate and lifestyle, consider a professional clean every 12–18 months, or rent a rug cleaner and follow the product instructions. If you’re the DIY type, a gentle wash (cold water, light cleaning agent) can be feasible for a shallow refresh—but avoid soaking the rug in water or using aggressive solvents.
- Drying: After cleaning, air-dry the rug completely before placing it back in use. A damp rug can develop a musty odor or misbehave underfoot. Plan for a day with good ventilation and perhaps a fan or two to accelerate the drying process.
- Odor management: If your space tends to pick up cooking aromas or other household scents, consider rotating placement from time to time to keep the rug smelling fresh. It’s not a perfume line, but a little rotation goes a long way toward maintaining a neutral, inviting aura.

For those who like science-y notes: odor absorption on textiles is a whole rabbit hole. Kendall’s fiber blend offers some natural resistance, but nothing replaces good airflow and timely cleaning. Think of it as a little chemical experiment you can do at home with minimal risk and maximum payoff in comfort.
