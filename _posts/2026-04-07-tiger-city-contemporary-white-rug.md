## Maintenance and Cleaning
White rugs ask for love, attention, and a little ritual. They are high on aesthetics but not as high-maintenance as you might fear—provided you approach cleaning with a plan and not with a vacuum that thinks it is auditioning for a role in a demolition derby.

Vacuuming frequency depends on foot traffic. For households with pets and kids, vacuum once or twice weekly to prevent the surface from fading into a coffee stain constellation that only a seasoned detective could read. For low-traffic spaces, a weekly brush-down and spot cleaning should suffice.

Spot cleaning is the big one for white rugs. A gentle soap solution combined with a soft cloth can handle most mishaps. Avoid aggressive chemical cleaners that could compromise the fibers or cause discoloration. For tougher stains, test a small area first and consider a professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months if you want the rug to keep looking crisp for longer than a long video game campaign.

If you spill red wine, there is a canonical move: blot, don’t rub. Blotting draws the liquid out of the fibers, like pulling data from a corrupted hard drive. Then treat with a mild detergent solution and rinse with clean water. Pat dry with towels and let air dry. Do not run a heater or hair dryer on high; you do not want to melt the fibers or subject the rug to warp-speed heat that would make it feel less like a rug and more like a legal document from a fancy hotel.
