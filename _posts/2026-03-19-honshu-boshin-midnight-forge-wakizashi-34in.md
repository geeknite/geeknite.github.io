## Maintenance and Care: Keeping It Sharp (But Not Overzealous)

As with any blade, longevity comes from a regimen of basic maintenance. Here’s a practical checklist you can follow if you decide to own this wakizashi:

- Regular oiling: A light coat of blade oil to keep the edge and temper line from drying out. Do not over-oil; a thin layer is plenty for hobbyist usage.
- Cleaning after handling: Wipe down with a soft cloth to remove fingerprints and oils. This helps protect the finish and keeps the temper line visually striking.
- Inspect the fittings: Periodically check the tsuka, tsuba, and mekugi (the bamboo peg used to pin the tang in place) to ensure everything remains snug. Loose parts can affect balance and safety.
- Storage: Store the sword in a dry, ventilated space. Avoid high humidity, which can encourage rust on non-stainless steels. The wooden saya benefits from occasional conditioning if you live in dry climates.
- Sharpening: If you intend to sharpen or touch-up the edge, use proper sharpening stones and techniques. Don’t rush; slow, deliberate strokes yield better edge retention and a safer blade.
