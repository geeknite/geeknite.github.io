## Mechanics and Card Text
- Type: Creature — Alien Monster
- Power/Toughness: 5/4
- CMC: 4
- Rarity: Rare
- Abilities:
  - Mind Fracture — Whenever Reptoid deals combat damage to a player, that player discards a card and you draw a card.
  - Depths of Thought — At the beginning of your end step, if you control an Alien, you may draw a card.
- Flavor text: 'The mind is a labyrinth, and the Reptoid has the map.'

Note: The card text is intentionally crisp to minimize confusion in draft and sealed formats. The Mind Fracture ability is meant to be a tempo swing, forcing opponents to answer threats while you accumulate card advantage.

## Deck-building and Strategy
### Core synergies
Reptoid is designed to be a centerpiece in midrange or control shells that push through disruption while threatening a late-game inevitability. Its Mind Fracture is your primary engine for card advantage, but you must balance aggression and defense to avoid giving your opponent too many draw steps.

- Solid early plays: A couple of 2-drops that curve into Reptoid help you land the 5/4 body on turn four without getting blown out by first-strike removal.
- Mid-game plan: Use the Mind Fracture trigger to force discards and draws in a way that gives you two-for-one outcomes over a few turns.
- Late-game finish: If you’ve managed to stabilize board presence, Depths of Thought can net you additional cards and set up a blowout with big spells.

### Budget considerations
If you’re building on a strict budget, you can slot Reptoid into a deck centered on cheap removal and counterspells. Its ability to draw a card at the end step when you control another Alien keeps the deck viable even when the economy is tight.

### Counterplay and interactions
- Against heavy removal: Reptoid’s 5/4 body ensures it trades efficiently, but your opponent may aim to bounce or exile it. Plan redundancy in your creature suite.
- Against mass draw decks: Mind Fracture can force your opponent into awkward discards that ripple across their game plan.
- Against dedicated control: You’ll want to assemble multiple threats to overwhelm with value, then close the game with a big spell supported by Depths of Thought.
