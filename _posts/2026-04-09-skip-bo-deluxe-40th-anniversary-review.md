## How Skip-Bo Works Revisited
Skip-Bo remains delightfully simple to learn yet remarkably easy to misuse by rushing. The goal is to empty your stock pile by playing cards in ascending order from 1 to 12 on shared building piles, using wild cards to fill gaps as needed. The Deluxe edition does not alter the core mechanics, but it does improve readability and reduces the friction that can occur when a player tries to parse a slightly faded card in the heat of a moment. The result is a smoother experience that still carries the same playful chaos that makes Skip-Bo charming.

### Rules in Practice
The rule set is straightforward: each player has a stock pile face down to the table. A draw pile is available for replenishment, and a handful of building piles on the table serve as targets for the sequence. On your turn you can play from your stock pile or from your hand onto the building piles, and you can use wild cards to stand in for any number. The speed of play depends on the tempo of the table and the number of players, but you will generally get through a few rounds quickly, leaving room for a second game if the night is still young.
