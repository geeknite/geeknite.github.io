## Rule variations and house rules to keep things fresh

While the core UNO Attack rules are straightforward, experienced groups tend to adopt a few house rules to keep sessions lively. Here are a couple you might try, along with a caution about altering the tempo:

- The cascade rule remix: whenever the launcher ejects a big draw for a player, they get an extra turn, but they must decide whether to keep their hand or swap the top card with another player. This creates a risk-reward moment that can swing games in surprising ways.
- The timer pressure: set a soft timer for each turn. If a player takes too long, the launcher multiplies the pressure by spitting out extra cards. This keeps everyone on their toes and adds a cinematic intensity to the final few hands.
- The no-mulligan clause: in a tight match, disallow the player with the fewest cards from using the launcher to draw during the last round. This keeps the ending honest and avoids a runaway victory caused by a late cascade.

Be mindful that adding house rules can dramatically increase chaos. If your goal is a relaxed family game night, keep modifications light. If your crew is in a competitive mood, lean into the more intense variants and enjoy the spectacle.

## Practical care tips for long term love

If you intend to keep a vintage UNO Attack unit in rotation, there are a few care steps that help preserve the hardware and the cards:

- Keep the electronics clean and dry. Spills can corrode the connectors or gum up the card dispensing area. A gentle wipe with a dry cloth is usually enough after a game night hype.
- Store the cards in a cool, dry place. Cards can warp or fade if exposed to sunlight or humidity for extended periods.
- Inspect the launcher regularly for loose components. If you notice any wobble or unusual noises, a quick check of the screws and clips can prevent larger issues down the line.
- Replace batteries promptly when you see a dim light or slower operation. A low energy launcher can still be used, but you may miss the full dramatic effect.
- Keep spare batteries on hand. A quick swap during a tense final hand is the difference between chaos and catastrophe in a good way.
