## Maintenance and Longevity

Something that separates good replacement packs from the rest is care. Here are a few maintenance ideas that keep a replacement pack like the MIT515SL in top form:

- Regular full discharge and recharge cycles are not necessary for Li-ion packs, but occasional calibration cycles show the battery that you are actively using it and help the system manage voltage better over time.
- Keep the battery away from moisture and avoid sudden temperature swings. The electrolyte in Li-ion cells does not appreciate condensation and rapid cooling or heating.
- Store at around 40–60 percent charge if you are not going to use the pack for a while. This reduces the chance of capacity fade caused by stress on the cells during extended storage.
- Inspect for swelling or physical damage periodically. If you detect any bulging, do not use the pack and seek replacement immediately.
