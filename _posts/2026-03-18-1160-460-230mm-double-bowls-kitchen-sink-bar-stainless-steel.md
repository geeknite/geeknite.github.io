## What the specs actually mean

- Length 1160 mm: This is the total width of the bowl assembly, which matters for countertop cutouts and cabinet space. It’s not a race to 1.16 kilometers; just enough to give you two workable bowls without needing to invest in a custom brick-and-lumber kitchen.

- Width 460 mm: The depth across the cabinet from front to back. It’s not a spy movie; you’re not trying to hide a secret lair behind it, but you do want enough room to place a dish rack and still have space for a drying area.

- Depth 230 mm: The vertical dimension of the sink basin inside the cabinet space; this is a practical number for most kitchen layouts. If you’re the kind of person who bathes baby goats in their sink, this depth would be… too shallow. For normal humans, it’s perfect for tall pots and the occasional stock pot.

- Double bowls: Two separate basins allow you to separate prepping from washing, or soak pasta in one bowl while rinsing in the other. It’s not cheating, it’s multitasking with a sense of order.

In this 1160 x 460 x 230 mm model, you’re likely looking at a gauge in the range of 0.6 to 0.8 mm for commercial-grade bases, with a 18/10 or 18/8 composition that gives you the easy wipe-down dream. ## Installation and compatibility: can you install it in your cabinet?

Dimension 1160 x 460 mm is generally compatible with standard kitchen openings, but you must measure. You want at least 5 mm clearance on all sides to allow for the faucet, clips, and the sealant. Undermount vs top-mount is a major decision. Undermount looks sleek and makes countertop cleaning a breeze, but it requires a sturdy substrate and proper caulking. Top-mount is easier to install and can be a good choice if you want to do it yourself with a few basic tools.

If you’re upgrading from a single bowl to a double, you’ll also want to consider the space around the sink. You’ll need a cabinet that can accommodate the extra width, or you’ll be forced to trim your existing cabinet or buy a new one. In a worst-case scenario, you’ll channel your inner MacGyver to fashion a spacer from leftover pine or a rogue cereal box. It won’t be pretty, but it will be functional.

## Maintenance, care, and a little science

Stainless steel is exquisitely tolerant, but it isn’t invincible. Regular cleaning with a mild dish soap and warm water will keep the surface looking fresh. For stubborn water spots, a gentle polish with a non-abrasive cleaner will help. Do not use bleach or chlorine-based cleaners that can tarnish the surface over time. If you want to be fancy, you can use a lemon wedge to freshen the scent—though that’s more of a kitchen theater prop than a necessary step.

To keep the sink looking its best, avoid placing hot pots directly on the surface for long periods. The heat can cause discoloration in some grades. In practice, you can put the pot down after you stir the pasta sauce, but you probably shouldn’t braise your steak in it. The general guideline is to treat the stainless steel with respect: it’s tough, it’s durable, but it’s not invincible.

## FAQ

### Will it fit my kitchen?

Most cabinets with a 1160 mm opening will fit without drama. If your opening is slightly smaller, you might need to trim the cabinet or reframe. If you have a standard 600 mm base cabinet, you should be good, but measure twice, cut once, then pretend you measured again just to be safe.

### Is it compatible with a garbage disposal?

Yes, most disposer units will fit in the drain area, but you need to check the clearance beneath the sink for pipes and the disposal’s connection. A loose connection is the fastest way to turn your kitchen into a water feature you didn’t ask for.

### How do I keep water spots away?

Wipe after use with a microfiber cloth. For stubborn spots, a light polish with a non-abrasive cleaner and a soft rag does wonders. Avoid harsh abrasives that could scratch the finish. Regular cleaning is easier than dealing with stubborn stains that look like a crime scene on the metal.

### How long does installation take?

Depends on your experience. A confident DIYer might finish in a day or weekend if you’re not interrupted by a sudden love affair with cable management. Pros can install in a few hours. Plan for extra time if you’re changing outlets, wiring, or relocating plumbing.
