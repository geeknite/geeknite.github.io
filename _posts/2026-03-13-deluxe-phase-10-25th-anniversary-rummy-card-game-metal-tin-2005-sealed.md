## Preservation, Care, and Longevity

If you plan to keep the item sealed for the long haul, a few best practices will help preserve the tin and its contents. First, store the tin in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV exposure can cause the tin’s art to fade over time and can degrade the inner paper components. Humidity is the unkind ally of metal; humidity can hasten any corrosion on the tin’s surface and affect the seal around the plastic insert.

Second, avoid placing the tin in areas where heavy items could press down on it. The last thing you want is a bent lid or an dent that makes the seal look less pristine. Third, consider using an archival-safe sleeve or a soft cloth to wrap the tin if you’re stacking it with other memorabilia. This reduces the risk of scratches that would annoy you when you finally decide to display the item.

Finally, if your ultimate goal is to preserve the value for a collector, consider occasional professional appraisal or a chat with collectors who understand the specific edition. They can provide practical tips on storage and potential display options that keep the item in prime condition while still giving you the chance to enjoy it visually.
