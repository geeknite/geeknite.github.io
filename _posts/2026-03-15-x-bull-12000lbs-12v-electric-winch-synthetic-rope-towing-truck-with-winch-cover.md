## How to Choose the Right Winch for Your Rig
Not every winch is built the same. If your rig mostly stays on pavement and occasionally finds a muddy footprint, a modest rating with easy handling might be best. If you live for adventure and tow heavier loads, you’ll want a higher rating, a robust mounting system, and a couple of spare parts stashed in the glove box for emergencies. Consider rope type (synthetic vs steel), line length, mounting hardware compatibility, protective covers, and the availability of replacement parts. And yes, we know you’ll still fill the coffee mug with fear while you decide; that’s normal.

## Quick Start Guide
1) Mount on a bumper plate or sturdy mounting surface. 2) Connect power and ground with clean terminals. 3) Thread synthetic rope through the roller fairlead. 4) Attach the hook and the safety latch; keep your fingers out of the rotation. 5) Conduct a no-load test before applying any real load. If it seems off, recheck connections and alignment. 6) When you’re ready, re-check load calculations and proceed with the actual pull.
