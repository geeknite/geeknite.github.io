## Practicalities: cleaning, maintenance, and pet-friendly considerations

Wool has natural stain resistance and a degree of resilience that can handle light spills with quick blotting. The Tempo rug responds well to standard vacuuming. The top fiber lifts dirt up rather than grinding it in, which means you can avoid that dreaded vacuuming-that-sounds-like-a-geiger-counter moment when you hit a tough fiber knot. For stains, the usual first aid includes blotting rather than rubbing and a mild wool-safe cleaner. Always check the care label and test in an inconspicuous corner first; your rug deserves a fair trial, not a science experiment.

If you have pets, wool rugs offer good durability, but you will want to train those paws to avoid scratching the surface. The dense pile can hide pet hair, but it also means you’ll see it more readily after a lazy weekend where the dog decides to wrestle with the rug like a bear in a fairy tale. Regular grooming with a soft brush can keep the fibers looking fresh. For muddy paws, a damp cloth followed by light blotting is your friend. Sticky sauces may require a gentle wool-safe cleaner. It’s not magic; it’s methodical care.

## Care and maintenance tips to keep it looking new

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting and avoid beater bars on delicate fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean white cloth; avoid rubbing, which drives stains deeper.
- For set-in stains, use a wool-safe cleaning solution and test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if you have sun exposure or a living room that faces the window like a golden retriever chasing light.
- Use a rug pad to reduce bunching and improve underfoot comfort; a pad can also prolong the life of your rug by minimizing friction with the floor.

## Installation and pairing with your space

If you’ve ever installed a rug, you know the drama of choosing a pad and making sure you don’t end up with a curling edge that looks like it is auditioning for a suspense film. The Tempo rug plays nicely with standard rug pads and does not require a heavy underlay; a good quality pad will provide grip, insulate against cold floors, and help the rug maintain its shape. When placing the rug, aim for the main seating area to be on the rug surface with the surrounding furniture legs resting on the rug’s edge or just off it. This creates a cohesive look and makes the room feel balanced rather than fragmented.

If you have doors that swing in front of the rug, measure the clearance and account for the door’s path. A common trick is to tilt the rug slightly under the door to keep the edge from catching. It’s the little details that separate a well-furnished home from a room that looks curated by a furniture stylist and a tape measure. For hallways and long corridors, consider a runner version of Tempo if you crave the same greige vibe in a narrower footprint.
