## What is Fortune Row?
Fortune Row isn\'t just a location; it\'s a vibe shop where luck, debt, romance, and black-market knowledge all collide in a glittering bank vault of secrets. The Player\'s Guide frames Fortune Row as a microcosm of City of Mist\'s broader themes: humans with extraordinary abilities choosing to hide in plain sight, using powers and connections to influence the world—or at least their own corner of it.

The district\'s aesthetics lean toward noir elegance, popcorn neon, and a soundtrack you imagine in your head while pretending to sip a coffee you clearly cannot afford. The guide helps you translate those feelings into mechanical terms: stunts, assets, and social leverage that feel thematic rather than random. It\'s all about making your character\'s arc feel personal and cinematic while giving the GM hooks to pull you into the next scene with a theatrical flourish.

## What\'s in the Player\'s Guide?
The Fortune Row Player\'s Guide covers several essential elements. Here\'s the lay of the land with some tasting notes so you know what to expect when you open the binder, the PDF, or the digital sheet and stare at your own life choices with a mix of awe and dread.

### Character Concepts and Masks
In City of Mist, you\'re never just a guy with a car; you are a Mythic Archetype wearing a modern outfit. Fortune Row expands on this by giving you lenses that fit the district\'s themes: fortunes that tilt toward luck, debts that must be paid, or relationships that complicate your life in the most stylish way possible. The guide suggests a few ready-to-use concepts that slot neatly into the Row: the Fortune-Teller of a side-alley, the Corporate Scion who uses favors as currency, or the Street Artist who conjures illusions that feel just a tad illegal.

Masks are your identity, your secret superpowers, and your excuses for running late to every important event. Fortune Row not only reaffirms classic Masks but also introduces subtypes and tie-ins to the district\'s social networks. You get guidance on selecting a Mask that resonates with the Row\'s vibes and how that choice can shape your relationships with other investigators, factions, and the Row\'s enigmatic patrons.

### Powers, Refractions, and the Charm of the Row
Fortune Row emphasizes two things: narrative weight and mechanical clarity. The guide sorts powers into tiers and suggests how to narrate their use to maximize drama without forcing the GM to do cartwheels to keep up. The emphasis is on showing rather than telling—you\'re expected to describe your\'s character\'s influence on a scene instead of relying on a long stat block to do the talking for you.

A lot of fine print in other games can feel like sassy homework. Fortune Row makes room for your character\'s mischief and the consequences that follow. The guide includes helpful prompts to spark a scene, plus optional rules that let you push harder for narrative payoffs when a fate card decides to be dramatic in your life choices.

### Assets, Favors, and Wallet-Weight Negotiations
Fortune Row introduces assets and favors as currency, with the charm of a street market and the risk of an actual market crash. The guide helps you manage these resources with an eye toward storytelling rather than endless bookkeeping. Expect sections on:
- How to earn assets without becoming a human ledger
- How to spend favors without becoming a villain who needs a calendar to remember your victims
- How social leverage shapes scenes and helps or hinders your team

The net effect is a game where your character\'s social power feels tangible but never overwhelming. You\'re not just rolling dice to succeed; you\'re navigating relationships, obligations, and a city that loves to remind you that every choice has a price.

## Building a Character in Fortune Row: A Practical Walkthrough
Now that you know what\'s inside, let\'s put on the mask and walk through a character build. We\'ll sketch a sample investigator and then break down the steps you\'d take in your own group to bring someone to life in Fortune Row.

### Step 1: Pick a Mask and a Concept
Let\'s start with a mask that fits Fortune Row. Meet Nova Vale, a street-level journalist who uses the power of luck as leverage—she transforms a bad day into a headline and a headline into leverage for the undercity. Her concept leans into the Row\'s themes: fame, debt, and a network of patrons who look after their own. Nova\'s mask is a badge of legitimacy she can flash at the right moment, which buys time and access. Her story threads include a mystery about a forgotten fortune that may tie to the Row\'s most dangerous secrets.

### Step 2: Define Relationships and Motivations
Nova\'s relationships anchor her. She has a cautious mentor from the Row\'s upper echelons, a rival journalist who wants the same scoop, and a street artist who can generate distractions during a critical moment. Her motivations are twofold: uncover the truth behind a legendary fortune and protect the people who believed in her when she was still a struggling reporter. These ties matter in Fortune Row because the city rewards relationships with both favors and debts, and Nova accrues both in equal measure.

### Step 3: Assign Assets, Fortunes, and Powers
Assets might include a press badge that opens doors, a pocket camera with secret blurs, and a crowd of sympathetic extras who help in a pinch. Her Fortune could be a moment she survived against the odds—she learned to read the city\'s micro-matters, the tiny details that predict which deal goes sideways. Nova\'s powers lean toward influence and perception: the ability to nudge outcomes through talk, a keen sense of when a rumor contains a kernel of truth, and a knack for turning a disadvantage into a narrative advantage.

### Step 4: Create the Character Sheet Narrative
The Fortune Row Sheet prompts Nova\'s player to fill in relationship notes, a short backstory, and a single scene that defines her moral compass. The design ensures players can capture the most important bits of character in a single page. You\'ll want to keep a couple of ready-made prompts on hand for quick sessions—things like \"What\'s the one thing your mask would never admit in public?\" or \"Who owes you a favor right now?\" These prompts aren\'t filler; they\'re fuel for the dramatic engine of the Row.

### Step 5: Play, Reflect, Repeat
As you play Nova, the Row rewards you for leaning into your relationships and chasing that grand, messy secret. Your character grows not just through battles or solvable mysteries, but through the whispers that travel through Fortune Row\'s network. The group should aim for a tall-but-credible arc: a personal triumph that leaves a lasting mark on the city and a consequence that haunts the crew for the next mission.

### A Sample Scene: The Night They Found a Card in the Rain
In a rain-soaked alley, Nova discovers a tarot-like card that seems to glow faintly when the neon lights reflect off puddles. The card doesn\'t predict the future; it predicts choices. She must decide whether to reveal the card to her mentor—risking her hard-earned trust—or hide it, preserving her leverage but risking the Row\'s suspicion that she has secrets she\'d rather not share. The scene explodes with social maneuvering, a carefully staged photo, and a decision that locks in Nova\'s fate for the arc. This is the kind of moment Fortune Row invites: a balance between clever talking, risky decisions, and the consequences that follow.
