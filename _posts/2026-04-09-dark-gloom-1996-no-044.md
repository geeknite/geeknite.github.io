## Storage, Display, and Care: Keeping Dark Gloom Gloomy in a Good Way
A card from 1996 deserves care that mirrors a well-loved item from a favorite museum gift shop. Keep it in sleeves that are designed for vintage cards, ideally with anti-glare properties to preserve that moody aesthetic. If you choose a binder, go for a page layout that minimizes handling, because the oils from skin are harsh critics of any glossy surface. When you display it, consider lighting that is not too intense, to guard against color fade while still letting the artwork breathe in the room. The point is to appreciate the design without turning the card into a fossil that you show only to archeologists with a flashlight.
