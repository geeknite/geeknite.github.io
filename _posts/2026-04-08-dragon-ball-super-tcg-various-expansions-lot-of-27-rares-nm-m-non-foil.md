## Condition and Grading: NM to M Non-Foil
The lot is described as NM to M, non-foil. What does that mean in practical terms?

- Near Mint (NM): Cards look and feel fresh with very minor corner wear or edge nicks that do not affect playability. No scratches, no stains, no major whitening on the card face.
- Mint (M): Cards show minimal signs of handling, possibly with very light edge wear or tiny corner blunts that are not unusual from transit and sleeve storage. No serious defects.

Non-foil cards tend to hide surface flaws a bit more than foil cards because they lack the reflective glare that makes scratches obvious. That makes NM to M non-foil rares a strong choice for a budget or midrange deck, provided you store them properly and handle them with clean sleeves. In practice, expect some minor edge wear on a few cards; this is typical when a lot has circulated through multiple hands and game nights. The key here is consistency across the 27 cards. If most come in NM and a handful are M, that’s still a very reasonable bundle for deck-building or trading at a local shop.

Storage tip: always sleeve rares, use a rigid top-loader or card binder with acid-free pages, and avoid stacking heavy items on top of the pack. Humidity is the silent killer; keep the lot in a cool, dry place and away from direct sunlight. If you plan to transport to a shop or event, a small card box with dividers helps keep them flat and protected.

## Condition, Handling, and Shipping Tips
You’ve got a 27-card lot. You want to keep it NM to M over the long haul. Here are some practical tips that go beyond the basics:

- Sleeving strategy: Use standard-sleeve the cards and consider inner sleeves for extra protection during transit. If you’re transporting dozens of items, a 9-pocket page might do better than single rows of sleeves.
- Barrier protection: Keep the cards in a rigid plastic case with foam inserts if you plan to travel with them. Vibration and pressure can cause edge wear, especially on marginally graded NM cards.
- Temperature control: A steady room temperature is best. Extreme heat can warp sleeves and cause binders to warp, which is not kind to prints.
- Labeling and inventory: It saves headaches to catalog each card with a quick note on set and rarity. A small binder page or even a spreadsheet helps when you’re at a shop or trying to locate a specific card later on.

## Authenticity and Verification Tips
Authenticity is the bedrock of every DBS TCG collection. The market has legitimate pieces and counterfeit concerns, especially with high-demand sets. For a non-foil lot, you should still follow standard checks:

- Look for consistent card stock color and texture across the lot. If something looks off, compare it to a confirmed genuine card from the same set.
- Check the card back design for any misprints or misalignment. Even rare cards can have subtle print variants; learn to tell the genuine version from a plausible fake.
- When in doubt, request close-up photos of the front and back, including the corner and edge shots. A seller with nothing to hide will be happy to provide extra pics or a short video confirming the cards’ condition.
