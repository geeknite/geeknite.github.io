## Cleaning and Care: Keeping the Ivory Graceful

### Regular maintenance routine
Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting and avoid high powered suction that could affect the pile. A brush attachment can help remove debris without pushing it deeper into the fibers. Rotate the rug every 6 months to equalize wear, especially in high-traffic areas or spots with strong sunlight that might fade the color unevenly.

### Spot handling and stain removal
For most stains, blot the area gently with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent solution if needed. Work from the outside in to prevent the stain from spreading. Avoid aggressive scrubbing, which can fray the fibers. For more stubborn stains, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in wool blends; this is not a DIY situation where you want to risk irreversible damage. A reputable guide with practical steps is available here: https://www.apartmenttherapy.com/how-to-clean-a-rug.

### Long-term care tips
- Protect from direct sunlight when possible to minimize fading.
- Use a rug pad to reduce wear and prevent slipping.
- Address spills promptly to prevent staining.
- If you move furniture, lift it rather than dragging to avoid edges lifting or fraying.

### A few notes on sustainability and cleaning products
Look for mild, wool-safe cleaning agents and avoid chlorine-based products that can be harsh on natural fibers. The goal is to keep your rug vibrant while protecting the fibers for the long haul.
