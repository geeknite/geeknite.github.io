### Condition considerations and practical tips
When evaluating a 1996 Japanese print like Dark Gloom, check centration, edge wear, and surface gloss. The edges of early prints can show slight whitening if they have been shuffled a lot, which some buyers actually appreciate for the character it adds. In terms of investing, remember that the market can swing with celebrity card collectors or new print runs. The real satisfaction comes from owning a certifiable piece of pop culture that resonates with your own memories and the people you shared those memories with. If you plan to store it long-term, consider a top loader and a safe storage environment that remains temperate and dry. The card deserves to be shown thoughtfully, not just tossed into a stack of loose cards.
