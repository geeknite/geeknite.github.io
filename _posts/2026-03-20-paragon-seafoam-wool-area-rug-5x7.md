## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping Seafoam Fresh

Wool fibers love to be treated gently. For daily upkeep, a regular vacuum with a gentle suction setting removes surface dust and loose fibers. If you have a modern vacuum with a brush roll, turn it off or set to a low suction setting to avoid tugging at the fibers. A quick weekly pass keeps grit from settling in and turning the color into something less seafoam and more swamp-foam.

Spills happen. For most household mishaps, blot gently with a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb liquid rather than rubbing. Rubbing pushes the stain deeper and can dull the surface sheen. For tougher spills, a mild wool-safe cleaner can be used sparingly along with blotting. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to verify colorfastness. If you’re dealing with red wine, coffee, or pet accidents, address promptly. The longer a stain sits, the more it asserts itself in the dye alignment.

For year-to-year care, a professional cleaning every 12 to 24 months can refresh the fibers. Wool is relatively forgiving to professional treatment when compared to synthetic fibers that can bleach or melt under strong cleaners. If you’re a DIY enthusiast, a low-moisture cleaning approach can work, but avoid saturating the rug with liquid cleaners that might cause dye migration or backing damage.
