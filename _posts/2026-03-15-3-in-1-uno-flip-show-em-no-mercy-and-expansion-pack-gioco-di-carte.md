## Gameplay: Quick Start Guide
Here’s the fast lane to understanding how this beast operates. If you know Uno Flip, you’ll recognize the rhythm; if you’re new, brace yourself for two colors, two sides, and two moods:

1) Setup: Shuffle base Uno Flip cards; decide if you’re including expansions. Deal 7 cards per player. The draw pile sits center stage with the discard pile next to it.
2) Flip mechanic: When you play a card, the card on the top of the discard pile shows its face; flipping to the other color is the signature move of Uno Flip. The color flip can drastically alter what cards you can legally play.
3) Turn flow: On your turn, you can play a card that matches either color or number. If you can’t, draw one card. If you draw and can play, you may chain in a controlled fashion depending on house rules.
4) Show Em No Mercy mood: If you’re playing this variant, you’ll encounter extra penalties and actions designed to push dramatic outcomes. It’s not purely about having the right card; it’s about managing the social pressure and turning that pressure into a strategic advantage.
5) Endgame: When you’re down to the last card, you must say Uno. If you forget, you’ll likely suffer a humorous penalty from the table, ranging from silly dances to mock “timeout” moments.

This is a game where the chaotic energy is the feature, not a bug. The rules are simple enough for casual players, yet the strategic depth of the expansion cards can become a whispering game of “which card do I save for the big moment?”

## How to Play in 2 Minutes
If you want the distilled essence of the game without the philosophical commentary:
- Step 1: Setup and deal 7 cards each.
- Step 2: Flip the top card to determine the starting color side.
- Step 3: On your turn, match color or number, or play a special card.
- Step 4: If you can’t play, draw one card; if that card helps, you may play it.
- Step 5: When you have one card left, shout Uno. If you forget, you’ll pay a light-hearted penalty chosen by the group.

This is the short version. For the full experience, the Show Em No Mercy rules will equip you with more dramatic moves, higher stakes, and more opportunities to make dramatic faces at your friends.

## Strategy and Tactics
- Read the room: Show Em No Mercy punishes indecision. A confident, loud play often dissuades other players from using the wrong card at the wrong moment.
- Save flips for the moment you’re about to run out: color flips can be used to force an opponent into a no-win scenario.
- The expansion’s new cards aren’t just “more” — they reorganize the tempo. Use them to disrupt your opponents when they’re on a hot streak.
- Remember to mix in base Uno Flip strategy with social pressure. You’re not playing chess; you’re navigating a social experiment with a deck of cards.
