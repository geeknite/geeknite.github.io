## Installation: a tiny surgery with big rewards

Installing a PS5 NVMe SSD is a process that can feel like a home-improvement project for your gaming machine. If you’ve ever replaced a PC part, you’ll breeze through this. If not, do not panic—Sony’s official PS5 expansion guide is your friend and can be found here: https://www.playstation.com/en-us/support/hardware/ps5-m-2-ssd/. Here’s a quick, friendly walkthrough tailored to the SN850 2TB heatsink variant:

1) Power down completely and unplug the console. Give it a moment. It’s not a stubborn cat; it just needs to settle down after your last epic boss fight.
2) Remove the stand and the side panel. Inside, you’ll see the expansion bay cover. Remove the screws that hold the bay cover in place.
3) Prepare the M.2 slot by unscrewing the mounting screw. Do not lose the screw; it’s the key to your expansion dreams.
4) Insert the SN850 into the M.2 slot at the correct angle. The notch orientation matters—this isn’t a puzzle, but you do need to align it correctly.
5) Reattach the heatsink side by re-securing the mounting screw. Make sure the heatsink sits flush and doesn’t wobble; you want consistent contact for heat transfer.
6) Slide the bay cover back, reattach the screws, and reassemble your PS5 case. Then power on.
7) In PS5 settings, go to Storage > USB Extended Storage or your internal storage to confirm the SSD is recognized. Create a library location for PS5 games if you want to keep the system drive pristine.

If you prefer video walk-throughs, there are plenty from tech YouTubers that align with how you feel about a bit of elbow grease and a screwdriver. Remember: always consult Sony’s official guidance on expansion and never force bits where they don’t belong.
