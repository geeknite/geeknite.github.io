Material and Construction

Most standard Dragon Ball Super sleeves use polypropylene backed by a protective inner layer. The BT01 sleeves follow this model and are typically a clear outer film with a colored backside. The durability is decent for casual to mid level play, with edge wear appearing only after significant use or aggressive shuffling. If you are a tournament grinder or livestream enthusiast, you might consider swapping sleeves after a run of 3–5 days of serious play to keep visuals pristine on cam. The packaging is generally straightforward, with resealable strips in some bundles and a small insert about BT01 lore that is a nice nod to fans who build their collection like a tiny universe. 

Size and Fit

These sleeves are sized to fit the Dragon Ball Super Card Game cards in standard deck thickness. The fit tends to be snug but not overly tight, enabling smooth insertion and removal. The inner sleeve helps protect the card back during handling and can reduce edge wear a bit, though some players prefer to remove the inner sleeve for a notably looser feel in exchange for a touch more bulk in their deck. If you are a high volume shuffler, you will likely notice the difference between a deck with two layers and one with a single protective layer. In practice, most players find the BT01 setup balances protection and playability nicely.

Care and Maintenance

To maximize the life of your BT01 sleeves, store them away from direct sunlight and in a dry environment. Sunlight can fade the art over time, which is not the vibe you want when you are trying to look cool on cam. Clean sleeves with a soft microfiber cloth to remove dust, and avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the plastic or the art. If you notice edge wear, consider upgrading to a higher grade or more durable sleeves in your next purchase. The goal is to keep your cards legible and your table looking classy.
