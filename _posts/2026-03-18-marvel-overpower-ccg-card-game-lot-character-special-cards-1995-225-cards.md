### Condition matters: what to look for in a 1995 print

Condition is king in any vintage card collection, and a 1990s CCG lot doesn’t dodge this reality. Here are practical condition-check guidelines tailored for OverPower cards:

- Edges and corners: Look for whitening or edge wear. 90s printing often shows minor corner fray, but anything that threatens legibility or artwork should be noted.
- Centering and print quality: Some cards may exhibit slight misalignment or color shifts, which is common for print runs of this era. These quirks can be charming or frustrating depending on your tolerance.
- Surface and gloss: If the cards were stored poorly or exposed to humidity, you might see warping, staining, or surface wear. If they come with a protective sleeve, you might be lucky to avoid major issues.
- Backs and consistency: Ensure backs are uniform and there are no counterfeit markings or obvious alterations, especially with Special Cards where fakes try to mimic rare prints.

Pro tip: the best way to assess condition without a professional grader is to sort into three piles — mint/near-mint, good, and playable. Then decide what you actually need for your display or deck-building adventures.

### How to handle and store a 225-card lot properly

Storing a vintage CCG collection carefully is almost as important as buying it. Here’s a practical approach that won’t require a PhD in archival science:

- Card sleeves: Use high-quality clear sleeves for all cards you intend to play with. If you plan to display, consider slightly oversized protectors to reduce edge wear.
- Binders and sleeves: Invest in three-ring binders with sturdy inner sleeves or page protectors. Group by card type: Heroes, Villains, and Special Cards. Use dividers with labels rather than a free-floating pile of labels that could peel off.
- Temperature and humidity: Keep the collection in a stable environment. Avoid basements with damp air or attics that swing wildly in temperature.
- Handling etiquette: Clean hands, no food near the cards, and no sweaty gaming sessions that transfer oils. Your cards deserve spa days too.
- Display vs. storage: If you intend to show this lot, rotate a few pieces on a dedicated shelf so you don’t constantly handle the entire set. It’s a balancing act between the thrill of a display and the care of long-term preservation.

A well-preserved lot not only looks nicer, it also makes your block of nerdy joy a better long-term investment. And yes, we know investments aren’t the primary reason you’re into nostalgia, but if your 225-card cache appreciates, we won’t judge.
