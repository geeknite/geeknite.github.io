## Practicalities: Care, cleaning, and daily life with a sisal rug
Natural fiber rugs come with a certain ethos of maintenance. They are sturdy and resilient, but they require a slightly different regimen than synthetic pile rugs. Here are practical care tips to ensure your Brink Camel Peak remains presentable for years:

- Vacuuming: Use a vacuum without a beater bar or with the bar set to a gentle setting. This helps avoid fiber disturbance that can lead to fraying over time. Vacuum in the direction of the weave rather than against it to reduce stress on the fibers.
- Spills and stains: Stainless steel or coffee stains are easier to handle if you treat them promptly. Blot with clean cloth and use a mild, non-soap cleaner tested on a small area first. Avoid vigorous rubbing that can push stains deeper into the fibers.
- Sun exposure: Natural sisal can show slight fading with prolonged sun exposure. If your rug sits in a sunny spot, rotate it periodically to even out wear and color tone.
- Padding: A good pad not only cushions the feet but adds longevity by reducing fiber friction with the floor. It also helps prevent slipping in high-traffic zones. A pad with a non-slip bottom is a solid investment.

If your goal is a natural, eco-friendly vibe with minimal synthetic intrusion, sisal wins.
