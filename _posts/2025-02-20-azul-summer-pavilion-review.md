# Azul: Summer Pavilion Review

![Azul: Summer Pavilion](https://i.imgur.com/4Fj2C76m.jpg){: .align-right}

Welcome to Geeknite, the place where board game dreams come true (or crash and burn). Today, we're diving deep into **Azul: Summer Pavilion**. Spoiler alert: it's as elegant and satisfying as finding the last puzzle piece after your cat's been batting it around for weeks.

## Introduction

Azul: Summer Pavilion is the third installment in the Azul series, designed by Michael Kiesling and published by Next Move Games. If you're a fan of strategic tile placement and abstract games, this one might just be your new obsession. But does it live up to the hype? Grab your favorite beverage, sit back, and let's find out.

## Setup and Components

Setting up Azul: Summer Pavilion is a breeze compared to untangling your headphone wires. The game comes with:

- 132 diamond-shaped tiles in six colors
- A game board for each player
- A central scoring board
- A tower to hold discarded tiles
- 4 player mats
- A bag for drawing tiles

The components are high quality, and the artwork is stunning. It's like bringing the beauty of a summer garden to your tabletop. Just make sure your cat doesn't mistake the tiles for treats.

## Gameplay Mechanics

### The Basics

Azul: Summer Pavilion keeps the core tile-drafting mechanic of its predecessors but adds a few new twists. Players take turns drafting tiles from a central pool to place on their player mats, attempting to complete colorful patterns. The game is played over six rounds, each representing a different season.

### Scoring

Scoring in Azul: Summer Pavilion is as satisfying as hitting the snooze button on Monday morning. Players earn points by placing tiles strategically to complete patterns and surround bonus areas on their mats. The more tiles you place in adjacent spaces, the higher your score.

### Strategy

While Azul: Summer Pavilion is easy to learn, mastering it is a different story. The depth of strategy is akin to planning a heist - there's always more to consider than meets the eye. You need to balance immediate scoring opportunities with long-term goals, and sometimes, you have to take tiles just to block your opponents. It's competitive, but in that passive-aggressive way, like stealing the last slice of pizza when no one is looking.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Stunning Artwork**: The game is visually appealing and will make your tabletop look like an art exhibit.
- **High-Quality Components**: Durable tiles and boards that can withstand even the most intense gaming sessions.
- **Strategic Depth**: Offers plenty of strategic choices, keeping both casual and hardcore gamers engaged.
- **Great Replayability**: Different strategies and tile draws ensure that no two games are the same.

### Cons

- **Analysis Paralysis**: New players might spend a lot of time contemplating their moves, slowing down the game.
- **Limited Player Interaction**: While competitive, direct interaction is minimal, focusing more on strategic placement.
- **Setup Time**: While not extensive, setting up all the tiles can take a bit of time, especially if you have cats.

## Comparisons to Other Games

Azul: Summer Pavilion stands out in the crowded field of abstract strategy games. Compared to its siblings, Azul and Azul: Stained Glass of Sintra, Summer Pavilion offers more depth and complexity. If you enjoyed the original Azul but found it a bit straightforward, Summer Pavilion will provide the extra challenge you've been craving.

## Memorable Moments

One of our favorite moments in Azul: Summer Pavilion was when one player managed to complete an elaborate pattern and score a massive number of points. The table erupted in cheers and groans, and the player basked in the glory of their victory, only to realize they had left themselves vulnerable in the next round. It's these highs and lows that make Azul: Summer Pavilion a rollercoaster of emotions.

## Final Verdict

Azul: Summer Pavilion is a fantastic addition to any board game collection. Its beautiful design, strategic depth, and high replayability make it a must-have. Whether you're a seasoned gamer looking for a new challenge or a newbie seeking an accessible but engaging game, Azul: Summer Pavilion won't disappoint.
