## Tips and tricks for getting the most from your FX40
- Level the table: If you’re serious about practice, ensure the surface is level. A tiny tilt can turn a promising rally into a sprint for the edge, and edge plays are dramatic until you realize gravity exists.
- Develop a simple passing routine: Start with quick passes across the midfield to set up shots. As you gain confidence, mix in fake passes and quick center shots to keep opponents guessing. The key is rhythm, not brute force.
- Watch your grip: Keep wrists relaxed and use finger tips to guide the stick. Over-gripping can slow your reaction time and make you look like you’re auditioning for a slow-motion scene in a sci-fi movie.
- Rotation matters: Practice moving your players with coordinated hand-eye timing. It’s not just about aiming; it’s about creating a flow that makes your opponents think they’re in a strategy game rather than an all-out carnival brawl.
- Clean up after play: Wipe the playing surface to remove oils and grime. A clean surface delivers more consistent ball bounce and reduces the chance of sticky shots that ruin the illusion of professional play.
