## How to Play: Quick Refresher for Both Games in the Bundle
Here’s the high-level flavor of each game included in the Bjdesign bundle. If you’re already a seasoned veteran of Uno and Skip-Bo, skip ahead to the section on house rules and smart tactics to optimize your chaotic glory.

### Uno: The Colorful Party Game
- Objective: Be the first player to get rid of all your cards.
- Setup: Deal 7 cards to each player; place the remaining deck as a draw pile; flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Turn flow: Match the card on the discard pile by color or number. Use action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, Wild Draw Four) strategically or, you know, with wild abandon.
- Special notes: The tension skyrockets as you approach the final card; don’t forget to shout “UNO!” if you have one card left, or you’re the joke of the hour when you forget and someone else calls it before you—classics happen.

### Skip-Bo: Stock-Pile Strategy with a Dash of Chaos
- Objective: Be the first to play all the cards in your Stock Piles into the Build Piles in ascending order (1, 2, 3, …) using the Skip-Bo wild cards as needed.
- Setup: Each player gets a set of Stock Piles (face-down stacks); players also maintain multiple Build Piles in the center. The deck is a mix of Stock Pile cards and Skip-Bo wild cards.
- Turn flow: Draw up to five cards, use any combination to advance your Build Piles. Only the top card of your Stock Piles is visible, so you’re constantly calculating risk and reward. The wilds allow you to bridge gaps, but you’ll still have to navigate the social consequences of letting someone else steal your moment of glory.
- Endgame: The moment you place your last number from a Stock Pile, you triumph—though not without a dramatic flourish and perhaps a friendly glare at the person who stole your mid-game spark plugs.

The bundle shines when you mix the two. A quick Uno round to warm up, a few Skip-Bo rounds to test memory and tempo, and maybe back again to Uno when someone announces a rumored “house rule” that would ruin everyone’s day. It’s a cycle that feels natural, not forced, and that’s the key to long gaming nights that don’t end with someone sulking in the corner.

## House Rules, Variants, and Personal Touches: Making It Your Night
One of the joys of card games is turning standard rules into your own little tradition. Here are a few light house-rule ideas that fit nicely with the Uno + Skip-Bo blend without ruining the core balance:

- Skip-Bo Shuffle: Before the first round, agree on a rule where players can swap up to two cards from their Stock Piles with the top of their Build Piles once per game. This adds a pinch of strategic chaos that can come from nowhere and change the endgame dramatically.
- Uno Challenge Mode: When a player draws their last card, they can declare “Uno-ish” and play a quick 30-second mini-round where everyone discards cards with special power cards trying to create chain reactions. Keeps folks engaged even when the pace slows.
- Thematic Wilds: If you’re feeling fancy, assign a “color of the night” that changes every few rounds. Any card matching that color in Uno can grant a mini-utility (e.g., draw one less card next round or skip an opponent’s turn once per game).
- Time-boxed sessions: Set a timer (e.g., 45 minutes) and see who has the most successful endings by then. It’s a playful nudge to avoid marathon sessions that turn into a memory test for everyone’s back muscles and patience.

References to these variants continue the playful, nerdy vibe without stepping on the official rules. They’re designed to be fun additions rather than radical shifts that ruin the original balance.
