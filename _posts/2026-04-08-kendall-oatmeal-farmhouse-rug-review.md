## Materials and cleaning

### What fibers are we dealing with

Kendall positions this rug in a midrange synthetic fiber category designed for stain resistance and color retention. The blend aims to stay faithful to oatmeal under typical indoor lighting and resist wear from daily life. It is not marketed as a machine washable rug, and for most households that is a good thing. The fibers are chosen to resist common household spills while remaining comfortable to the touch and pleasant to live with.

### Cleaning guidelines and best practices

The standard care plan is simple:
- Vacuum regularly to remove dust and hair from the loops.
- Treat spills promptly with blotting and a mild cleaning solution.
- Avoid harsh solvents or high heat that could distort the shape or fade the color.
- For larger stains or persistent marks, consult a professional cleaner with experience in synthetic fiber rugs.

In practice, this means you can maintain a fresh look without needing a full carpet care routine. The rug behaves like a wise sidekick: helpful, not needy.

### The value of a rug pad

A high quality rug pad is highly recommended. It reduces sliding, prevents curling at the edges, and adds underfoot comfort. Pads come in various materials, from felt to rubberized options, and choosing the right thickness can influence the overall feel of the room. In our trials, a good pad kept edges down, improved insulation against cold floors, and made the room feel more cohesive from every seating angle.

## Sizing and room planning

### Available sizes and choosing the right one

The Kendall Oatmeal Farmhouse Rug is offered in several standard sizes including 5x8, 8x10, and 9x12. The 8x10 size is the sweet spot for most mid-to-large living rooms, providing enough coverage to anchor a seating area while leaving space for furniture around the edges. In smaller spaces, the 5x8 can define a conversation area without overwhelming the room. For expansive spaces with multiple lounging zones, the 9x12 offers a dramatic stage that still feels grounded thanks to the neutral color.

### Measuring tips and layout ideas

Before you buy, measure your furniture footprint, the floor area you want to cover, and the space between furniture legs. A common guideline is to have at least a 6-12 inch margin between the rug edge and the walls for a balanced look. If you want a layered look, consider pairing the Kendall rug with a smaller accent rug under a coffee table, but be mindful of rug stacking that creates a busy floor scene. If you plan to place a sofa with a long chaise nearby, a larger rug can make the space feel more integrated rather than segmented.

- Unroll the rug in the room center and allow it to relax for 24 to 48 hours before placing heavy furniture on it.
