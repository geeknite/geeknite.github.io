## Maintenance and Longevity
### Keeping the oak finish vibrant for years to come
Routine cleaning involves a soft microfiber cloth, mild cleaner, and a quick check of joints and hardware. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can dull the veneer or damage the protective coating. Wipe spills promptly to prevent staining and consider using a protective pad under hot cups to prevent heat damage. If you notice a scratch or scuff that bothers you, most veneer finishes respond well to a light application of furniture polish, followed by a gentle buff with a clean cloth. For deeper scratches, consult a professional refinisher if you want to preserve the finish without the patchy look of DIY shortcuts.

Periodically check the cam-locks and screws. A little tightening every few months can prevent wobble from turning into a structural issue. If your team grows or reorganizes a lot, you may want to keep a small spare hardware kit on hand for quick swaps. It is not thrilling, but it is the kind of planning that saves you a headache later on a Friday afternoon when the network goes down and the only thing standing between you and a caffeinated meltdown is the sturdiness of your desks.
