## Practicality and Care
### Cleaning and maintenance
Natural fiber rugs like sisal require a light touch. The Cloudburst rug benefits from regular vacuuming with a wand attachment to avoid catching the fibers on a rotating brush. Spills should be blotted immediately; avoid rubbing as that invites pile distortion. For stubborn spots, a mild detergent solution or a specialized rug cleaner designed for natural fibers can help, but always perform a spot test on a small, inconspicuous area first. Since sisal is relatively moisture-averse, it’s wise to keep areas prone to spills well protected and avoid saturating the rug. If you’re the adventurous type who loves deep cleans, you might consider professional cleaning every year or two depending on traffic. For general care tips, see this reputable guide on rug maintenance from a trusted home improvement resource: https://www.bobvila.com/articles/how-to-clean-rug/.

### Durability and daily wear
Sisal fibers are known for strength and natural resistance to wear. The Cloudburst rug should stand up to high-traffic spaces—the kind of space where chaos and kids collide with the floor and not the furniture. While it’s sturdy, don’t assume it’s invincible: sharp corners of furniture can cause wear over time if you’re not mindful. A well-chosen rug pad will help reduce movement, add a bit of cushioning, and extend the life of the fibers. The combination of a tight weave and natural fiber means that even after months or years, you’ll likely notice creases and color variation that add character rather than signs of neglect.
