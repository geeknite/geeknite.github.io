## Maintenance and Cleaning Tips

Velvet textures require a light touch, but not a fragile one. Here are practical tips gathered from long-term wear experiments and a few stubborn household experts. Remember, you are aiming for longevity and comfort, not a museum-level pristine surface that requires a white-glove team to exist in your home.

- Regular vacuuming with a low-suction setting and a bare floor nozzle helps maintain pile height. Use short strokes to avoid pulling fibers. If your vacuum has a beater bar, consider turning it off to prevent matting.
- Treat spills promptly. Blot with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the fibers and create a stubborn stain. If needed, use a mild detergent mixed with water in a small amount and test on an inconspicuous corner first.
- For routine refresh, light brushing against the nap with a soft brush can restore the velvet’s texture. Be gentle; velvet fibers can look lively, but they can also misbehave if you show them who is boss with a heavy hand.
- Deep cleaning should be left to professionals, especially for large, valuable rugs. If you want to DIY, use a carpet cleaner designed for delicate fibers and follow the manufacturer’s instructions precisely. Avoid saturating the rug or using harsh chemicals that could alter color or texture.
