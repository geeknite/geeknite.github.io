Maintenance for the Rainbow 8ft is pleasantly boring in a good way. Regular checks should include a visual inspection of the springs or the springless mechanism, a test of the enclosure zipper if one is present, and a once-a-season deeper cleaning to remove leaves and the occasional pinecone that wandered in during a gusty day. ## Safety first: tips and best practices

- Always supervise jumpers, especially kids under 6. The old adage safety video says it best: a trampoline is a place for fun, not a stage for impromptu acrobatics without setup.
- Use the enclosure if included. Enclosures dramatically reduce the chance of a blacktop tumble into the grass and a trip to the first aid kit.
- Clear the area around the trampoline of hard objects. Balls, bikes, and lawn chairs are not compatible with rainbow bounce therapy.
- Check anchor points and weather conditions before use. A windy day turns a backyard trampoline into a wind-whipping rodeo rider if not properly anchored.
- Remove jewelry and socks; jump barefoot if you can. It improves grip and reduces slips.
- Teach kids to take turns and land safely. No twisting while the ground is still vibrating from a sibling’s epic jump count.
