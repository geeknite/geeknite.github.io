## Accessibility, safety, and inclusivity at the table

Not every player will be comfortable with the NSFW deck’s content. It’s important to set expectations before the first shuffle. Here are some quick safety considerations:
- Content awareness: at the table read-through, identify any topics that could be sensitive for players. You can use a simple “green-yellow-red” card scheme to indicate what everyone is comfortable with.
- Group size and setting: NSFW humor tends to work best with mid-to-large groups where there’s a shared social energy and mutual trust. Smaller groups or professional settings may benefit from a lighter, less explicit variation.
- Night-night rule: if a joke lands poorly, the table can opt to swap the NSFW deck back for the base game or pause to re-align expectations. The goal is to celebrate humor, not to create discomfort.

The convenience of the deck is that you can opt in or out depending on the night. It’s not forceful; it’s additive. That’s a big win for a game that depends so much on social vibes and the mental gymnastics of bluffing.
