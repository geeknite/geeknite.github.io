## Installation and setup tips
If you are new to racks, measuring twice and drilling once is the motto. Start by assembling the cabinet frame on a flat surface. Attach the front and rear rails, then thread the rails into the post holes. The included hardware usually ranges from M6 to M8 screws depending on your mounting rails. After you mount the rails, install your patch panels, UPS, servers, and PDUs in an order that makes sense to you—preferably the order you will remember when you need to upgrade the firmware at 3 am. Don’t forget to connect your power distribution unit and verify ground connections. A quick airflow test is a good idea; boot a few devices and watch the intake and exhaust temperatures across the door and the top of the cabinet. If you see hot air pooling at the top, you may need to re-route cables or add fans to the rear.

## Maintenance and care
Maintenance is mostly wiping down the doors and vacuuming the dust from the mesh surface. The powder coat finish resists chipping with normal use, but you should check the rails and screws from time to time for any loosening. A quick check every few months ensures you do not wake up to a collapsed front panel due to a loose mount. If you are in a dusty environment, consider adding a simple air filter on the intake or an optional door filter to minimize dust infiltration. Your future self will thank you when you do not have to chase a spiderweb of cables behind your rack at 2 am during a firmware update.

## Tips and tricks for best results
- Plan your cabling before you mount servers; it saves time and increases airflow.
- Use color coded cables for data and power to avoid accidental disconnections during maintenance.
- Place the heaviest equipment at the bottom to lower the center of gravity and reduce rack swing.
- Use the rear door or side panels to keep cables away from the door when it is open for service.
- Regularly inspect door locks and latches for signs of wear.
