## Compatibility and Installation Tips
The 4mm housing is widely compatible with most road bikes, hybrids, and many mountain bikes that rely on standard shifter systems. If you’ve got a frame with unusual routing, you’ll want to measure precisely and consider the route’s minimum bend radii. A few minutes of planning saves you from a frustrating re-route later.

Installation steps, in quick form:
- Measure the route and cut the housing square. A clean cut is essential to seating the ferrules properly.
- Deburr the cut end to prevent fraying.
- Slide on ferrules if your frame uses them. Ferrules help keep the housing ends square and protect against deformation.
- Thread the inner cable through the housing. A helper can be handy for longer runs.
- Adjust tension using barrel adjusters until the shifts are crisp and precise.
- Test shift across all gears, under light and loaded conditions.

We should emphasize the importance of bend radii here. If you route the housing with too-tight corners, you risk kinking and creeping friction. A good rule of thumb is to avoid radii tighter than a few centimeters depending on the route. If you’re unsure, practice on a practice run with scrap lengths before committing to the final cut.
