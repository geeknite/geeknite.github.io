### Overclocking and tweaks

Overclocking DDR5 is a delicate dance. The CT2K16G48C40U5 kit is designed to perform reliably at its rated 4800 MT/s CL40 on many boards. If you want to push beyond that, here are practical notes:

- Enable XMP first. It’s the simplest path to 4800 MT/s without manual fuss. If it boots cleanly, you might already be hitting the sweet spot.
- If stability issues appear, back off to a slightly looser timing or a tiny voltage bump within safe limits. DDR5 can be more sensitive to voltage changes than DDR4, so go slow and test thoroughly.
- Always test after changes with a memory benchmark or real-world tasks to ensure you are gaining something tangible. The goal is reliable improvement, not chasing a number that looks good on a chart.
