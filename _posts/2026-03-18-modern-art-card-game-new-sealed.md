### The Mechanism, in a Nutshell
Modern Art: The Card Game plays like a speed-dating auction between six imaginary galleries and your dwindling funds. Each round, you bid on a card by playing money tokens. The highest bidder selects the art card, and the rest scramble for the “difference” as constraints, bonuses, or penalties. The catch? Some cards are worth negative points, some trigger end-of-round effects, and a few grant you sneaky bonuses if you stack the deck with bold ego. It’s a delightful mess of risk and reward, wrapped in glossy card stock and a gleeful shrug at ethics.

### Teaching the Game, Quick and Fun
When you teach newbies, you frame it as a caper: you’re the gallery director, the other players are rival curators, and the cards are the stock that makes or breaks reputations. A solid teaching approach is to demonstrate with a mini-round: show a couple of cards, place a few bids, reveal the end-of-round mechanics, and then let the group take the wheel. You’ll notice that players start predicting opponents’ moves, which is exactly what turns what could be a pure game of luck into a delightful strategy exercise.

### Turn-by-Turn Flow
1) Reveal a new card for bidding. 2) Each player secretly commits money tokens. 3) All bids are revealed; the highest bidder takes the card. 4) End-of-round bonuses or penalties are applied. 5) Repeat until the round ends.

If you’re teaching, a helpful tip is to use a one-card demonstration to illustrate end-of-round effects before you crank the dial to a full round. It’s a fast learning curve that pays off with big smiles rather than blank stares.
