## Materials, Construction, and Maintenance Reality Check
One of the big questions when shopping for a rug is maintenance. Wool carpets can be a touch more demanding than synthetics in some scenarios, but they reward you with longevity and ease of spot cleaning. Here are some practical notes based on what you’d expect from a high-quality wool rug like Paragon’s Seafoam 4x6:

- Vacuuming: Regular vacuuming (once a week, at minimum) helps prevent dirt from embedding in fibers. Use a vacuum without a beater bar that’s too aggressive on dense wool fibers; a gentler setting preserves pile height and reduces shedding.
- Spills and stains: Act fast. Blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth. For wool, you can lightly dampen with water or use a wool-safe cleaner if the stain is stubborn. Always test on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Odor and moisture: Wool isn’t a sponge, but it’s naturally resistant to moisture compared to many natural fibers. However, avoid soaking the rug. If you live in a humid climate, consider a breathable rug pad underneath to minimize dampness and help air flow.
- Sunlight: While wool is colorfast to a degree, long sun exposure can slowly fade the hue. If your space gets a lot of direct sun, rotate the rug periodically or use window treatments to protect it.
