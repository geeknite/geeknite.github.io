## Materials, Construction, and Maintenance Reality Check
Maintenance is where the rubber meets the rug, so to speak. Wool carpets can be a touch more exacting than synthetics in some scenarios, but they reward you with longevity and ease of spot cleaning when you approach them like a responsible dungeon master: with a plan.

Here are practical notes based on high-quality wool rug expectations:
- Vacuuming: Regular vacuuming (at least once a week) helps prevent dirt from embedding in fibers. Use a setting gentle enough for dense wool fibers; you want to preserve pile height and minimize shedding.
- Spills and stains: Act fast. Blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth. For wool, you can lightly dampen with water or use a wool-safe cleaner for stubborn stains. Always test on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Odor and moisture: Wool isn’t a sponge, but it’s naturally more moisture-resistant than many natural fibers. Avoid soaking. If you live in a humid climate, consider a breathable rug pad underneath to minimize dampness and improve air flow.
- Sunlight: Wool colorfastness is decent, but prolonged direct sun can fade hues over time. If you have direct windows, rotate the rug periodically or use window treatments to minimize exposure.
