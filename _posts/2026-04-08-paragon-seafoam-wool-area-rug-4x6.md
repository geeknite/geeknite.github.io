## Materials, Construction, and Maintenance Reality Check
Maintenance is the big practical question. Wool carpets demand a bit more care than synthetics in some respects, but they pay back with longevity and ease of spot cleaning. Practical notes:

- Vacuuming: Regular weekly vacuuming helps prevent dirt from embedding. Use a gentle setting on dense wool fibers to preserve pile height and minimize shedding.
- Spills and stains: Act fast. Blot, don’t rub. For wool, lightly dampen with water or use a wool-safe cleaner if needed. Always test on a small area first.
- Odor and moisture: Wool is naturally moisture resistant compared to many natural fibers, but avoid soaking. A breathable rug pad helps air flow in humidity and reduces dampness.
- Sunlight: Colorfastness is not infinite; avoid prolonged direct sun or rotate the rug periodically or use window treatments.

## Care and Cleaning Deep Dive
Liquid spills: Blot with an absorbent cloth immediately. Do not rub. For stubborn stains, a wool-safe cleaner can help; always test first in an inconspicuous spot.

Dried spill signs: If you see a stain after it has dried, you can apply a little wool-safe cleaner and blot again. For persistent marks, call in a pro cleaning service with wool-specific methods.

Sunlight exposure: If the room bathes in sun, rotate the rug every few months to maintain color uniformity.

Annual care routine: Consider professional cleaning every 12–24 months depending on traffic. This keeps the pile plush and the color fresh.
