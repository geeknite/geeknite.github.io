## Strategy and tips for success
### Reading the room without words
In a silent play world, facial microexpressions become terrain. A raised eyebrow can signal timing, a subtle nod can confirm a plan, and a slight tilt of the head might indicate the path forward. The trick is to establish a shared repertoire of cues before you start. This is not cheating; it is game choreography that makes the room look like a well rehearsed improv troupe that plays for keeps.

### Timing is everything
Patience is a virtue but not a luxury here. You will be deciding between waiting for more information or acting on the shards you already hold. A practical approach is to craft a rhythm: a short thoughtful pause, a coordinated move, then a brief silence to let the cues land and the group breathe. The best rounds feel like a well executed drumline piece with no actual drums, just the sound of collective brain power humming along.

### Distributing cognitive load
In party mode you want everyone contributing without turning the table into a debate club. Rotate the captain role for the round, propose small sub tasks, and use the soul energy tokens with purpose rather than panic. The aim is to keep the pace lively while ensuring the group stays aligned on the core objective: a clean, silent sequence that earns the table a moment of collective satisfaction.
