## How to play the Flip Card Game

Playing UNO Flip Card Game is not rocket surgery, but a little strategy mixed with chaotic charm. Here is a compact guide to get you started, with some tips to survive your next game night without sacrificing friendships.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player.
- Place the rest of the deck face down as the draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Decide who will be the first dealer and you are ready to start. The light side of the deck is your typical UNO experience, while the dark side is a reveal of the Flip mechanic.

### Core mechanics
- On your turn, you may play a card that matches either the color or the number of the top discard card. You can also use action cards just like in regular UNO (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four).
- If you cannot play, you draw a card from the draw pile. If that card can be played, you may put it down immediately.
- If you ever have one card left, you must shout UNO or risk the penalty if another player catches you before the next player takes their turn.

### The Flip mechanic
- The moment a Flip card is played (or a special Flip situation triggers the dark side), the entire discard pile is flipped over to the other side. If the light side had a red card top, the new top card on the discard becomes determined by the backside color and rules. It changes the color palette, the action cards, and can dramatically alter what you can or cannot play.
- The dark side brings its own action cards and penalties, which are generally more intense. It’s not uncommon to see a player flip into the dark side and suddenly watch as their previously safe strategy evaporates in a puff of neon vapor.

### Endgame and strategy nuggets
- The Flip can completely alter endgame odds. A player who is about to win on the light side might suddenly find themselves facing disarray on the dark side, where different penalties and color sets apply.
- Strategic awareness matters more on UNO Flip than many variants. It becomes a dance between maintaining safe plays on the light side and preparing for a bold, risky finish on the dark side.
- Bluffing and table talk become a formal sport in UNO Flip. You will hear more occasional threats about stacking cards and the cruelty of the final draw than in standard UNO, making social dynamics as important as card management.
