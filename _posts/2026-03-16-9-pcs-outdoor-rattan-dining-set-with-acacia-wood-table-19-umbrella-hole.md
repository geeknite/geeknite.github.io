## Assembly and Maintenance: From Cardboard to Cozy

### Assembly Steps: It’s a Puzzle, but You’re Not Wrong
Neatly packaged boxes arrive with hardware sorted into little bags that look suspiciously like a DIY treasure map. The assembly typically breaks down into a few simple steps:

If you’re relatively handy, you’ll likely finish in under two hours with a glass of something slightly stronger at the table’s edge to celebrate. Pro tip: keep a small container of the included hardware handy so you don’t accidentally chase a single screw around the yard like a tiny mechanical griffin.

### Care, Weather, and Seasonal Storage
Outdoor furniture longevity is a mix of design quality and maintenance habits. Here are a few practical tips to keep your set looking sharp year after year:

- Clean surfaces with mild soap and water. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the rattan weave or dull the Acacia’s finish.
- Use a soft-bristled brush to remove dirt from the weave; avoid stiff brushes that can fray the fibers.
- For the Acacia wood, consider sealing or oiling the surface every 6–12 months, depending on climate. This helps preserve color and resists cracking during dry seasons.
- Cushions, if included, should be stored in a dry place or covered with a breathable cushion cover during heavy rains.
- Use a lightweight cover to shield from the harshest sun during peak hours; this goes a long way toward limiting color fade and fiber fatigue.

If you live in a region with heavy moisture or snowfall, you’ll want to store the set indoors during the worst weather or use a high-quality patio cover. It’s not a requirement for all climates, but it’s a smart habit to extend the furniture’s life and keep the color consistent across the seasons.
