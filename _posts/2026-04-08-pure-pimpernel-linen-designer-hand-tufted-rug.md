## Maintenance and Durability
Linen is not a fiber known for reckless cleanliness, but it has the virtue of appearing tidier between cleanings than polyester alternatives. The hand tufted construction adds resilience, and the rug’s density reduces visible vacuum tracks—an aesthetic win for those who believe in a living room that looks polished at 2x faux-Florence. The cotton backing should be treated with care: avoid exposing it to heavy moisture or direct sun for extended periods to prevent fading or fiber bloom.

### Cleaning Protocols
Spill management is straightforward. Blot (don’t rub) with a clean white cloth, use a mild detergent if needed, and let air dry. We tested a coffee spill and a red wine spill with equal seriousness; both incidents recovered with little trace after Blot-Patrol 2000, a pretend product in our dreams. In real life, just blot and move on; treat big spills like a deadline you cannot ignore and call in professional cleaning if necessary. Periodic rotation is recommended to even out wear, especially in high-traffic zones. If you’re the type who rotates furniture monthly, you’ll enjoy the interplay of textures and the rug’s ability to stay interesting over time.

### Backing and Floor Protection
Vacuuming should be done with a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers and causing fuzz that looks like baby yarn on a pink sweater. The cotton backing provides grip on smooth surfaces without leaving a rubbery residue on your floors. If you have radiant heat or sensitive surfaces, test a small area first to ensure the backing doesn’t overreact to heat or friction.

## Care and Longevity (A Quick Recap)
To maximize longevity, vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting and a brush attachment to avoid coaxing loose fibers out of the pile. Rotate the rug twice a year to ensure even wear across high-traffic zones. If you have pets, trim nails to reduce snags, not just to avoid clobbering the furniture. For spills, blot immediately and use a mild cleaner if needed, but avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the linen fibers and backing.

Sunlight exposure can cause fading over time, so position the rug away from direct sun for long periods or use window treatments to mitigate UV exposure. A good compromise is to rotate the rug to prevent uneven fading from daylight patterns.
