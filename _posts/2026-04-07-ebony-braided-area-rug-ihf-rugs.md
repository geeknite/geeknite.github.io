Size selection guide: choosing the right footprint
--------------------------------------------------
Choosing the right size is part science, part instinct. A rug that is too small will float in a room like a digital ghost; too large and it can overwhelm a space the way a boss fight overwhelms a party of adventurers. A common rule of thumb is to leave a border of floor visible around the rug when placed under a sofa or bed. For a living area, a 6x9 or 8x10 rectangle can anchor a standard seating arrangement. If you want to highlight a coffee table, a 5x8 or 4x6 rectangle might be more appropriate. For oval shapes, measure the longest point to ensure a comfortable buffer for chairs and foot traffic. Always consider door swings and rug edge visibility when placing the rug. 

Maintenance and longevity
--------------------------
Caring for braided rugs is less about ritual preparation and more about consistent habits. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps to pull out dirt without pulling apart the braid. Rotate periodically to even out wear, especially in high-traffic zones. Spills should be blotted quickly with a dry cloth, then treated with a mild cleaning solution tested in a hidden corner. Avoid harsh chemicals and saturating the fibers, as moisture is the rug’s nemesis. For longer life, consider using a rug pad that gives cushion and prevents slippage. A good pad also reduces wear by distributing weight more evenly. In the lab of domestic reality, this rug passes the test: it remains visually steady after multiple vacuum cycles and a few spill incidents that would break lesser textiles.

FAQ

Q. Is the rug machine washable
A. No, most braided rugs are not machine washable. A gentle vacuum and careful spot cleaning are recommended. If you must refresh the look, consider a light steaming and a rotate routine to even out wear. 
Q. Do I need a rug pad
A. Yes. A rug pad helps prevent slipping, reduces wear, and adds a touch of cushion. It also makes vacuuming easier and reduces the risk of curling edges over time. 
Q. Can this rug be used outdoors
A. The Ebony braided rug is designed for indoor use. If you have a covered patio or a sunroom, you can explore weather-resistant braided options from IHFRugs, but for longevity and comfort, keep it indoors.
