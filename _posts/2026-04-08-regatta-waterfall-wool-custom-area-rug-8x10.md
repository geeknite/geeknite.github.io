## Size and Fit: Customization vs Standard 8x10

### Measuring Tips

If you’re leaning toward a custom size, the first rule of measurement club is to measure multiple times. Use a tape measure, not your memory of “the room is roughly 12 by 14.” Check door swing clearance, sofa width, and any built-in furniture edges that could snag the rug or create awkward gaps. Remember to account for rug padding if you want a little extra cushion and to prevent slipping. If you’re a nerd about precise geometry, you can calculate a rug that leaves a 8–12 inch border around seating for a balanced look; or you can go full anime poster and let the rug run to the walls for maximum drama.

### Pros and Cons of Custom

Pros:
- Perfect fit for unusual rooms, odd angles, or spaces with built-in features.
- Visual balance: you control how much rug sits under furniture, creating cleaner sightlines.
- Potentially better resale value: buyers appreciate a space that already feels designed.

Cons:
- Lead times can extend as custom orders go through production checks.
- Slightly higher price due to bespoke sizing and potential dye lot variations.
- The risk of mis-measures if you skip double-checks or rely on a single quick sketch.

### 8x10 Standard: Pros and Cons

Pros:
- Quick ordering, predictable delivery window.
- Cost tends to be lower than custom, assuming you don’t hit rush fees.
- A widely compatible size for most living rooms and apartments.

Cons:
- Might leave awkward gaps or misalignment with your furniture layout.
- If your space is quirky, the rug may feel like an afterthought rather than a designed feature.

### Cleaning and Maintenance

Wool is naturally resilient, but no fiber is a magic wand. Spills should be blotted quickly; avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper. A diluted wool-safe detergent can help with light stains, and the natural lanolin in wool adds a bit of stain resistance. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting is recommended, and you’ll want to flip or rotate the rug every so often to prevent uneven wear in high-traffic zones. For pet households, it’s wise to have a plan for fur and dander; a handheld vacuum or a carpet rake can help keep things tidy without pulling fibers loose.


## Durability and Care: Longevity in a Surface Layer

### Stain Resistance

Wool rugs aren’t a guarantee of stain-proof glory, but they’re notably better than many synthetics when it comes to absorbing spills in a controlled fashion and resisting deep-set stains. The Regatta’s quality hinges on how well the dye binds to wool and how quickly you respond to accidents. A quick blot, clean water, and a touch of wool-safe cleaner is the standard playbook. The waterfall pattern doesn’t hide stains, but it can help camouflage minor marks until you can treat them properly.

### Wool Longevity

With proper care, wool rugs can outlast many synthetic rivals. The fibers’ natural elasticity helps them spring back after compression, which is a fancy way of saying your feet aren’t permanently leaving their mark after every chair wheel. The caveat: wool can be sensitive to prolonged moisture, so keep spills minimal and address wet areas promptly to avoid warping or mildew growth.

### Cleaning Guide

- Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing.
- For stains, use a wool-safe cleaner and always test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12–24 months depending on traffic and household activity.
- Rotate the rug every few months to maintain even wear.
