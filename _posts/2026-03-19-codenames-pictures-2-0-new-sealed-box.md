## Setup and How to Play
Setup is brisk. Shuffle the image deck, lay out the table of cards, assign red and blue teams, and let the clue-givers prepare. The core loop remains the same: give a single clue that relates to multiple images, teammates debate, and then reveal or guess the cards. The twist here is that the clues are images, not words, which means your team must interpret a visual metaphor rather than a literal synonym. The pace can swing from rapid-fire to contemplative in the blink of an eye, which is perfect for different game-night moods.

### Rules in Plain Speak
- One clue per turn, followed by a guessing phase. The aim is to link as many of your team’s cards as possible without hitting the other team or triggering the assassin.
- The assassin card is a trap like the midnight clown in a horror film; when revealed, it ends the round for your team. The best strategy is to avoid it like you would dodge spoilers for a favorite show.
- If your team clears all its cards, you win the round. If you reveal too many enemy cards or land the assassin, the other team steals a march. The pace is up to you but the best rounds feel like a fun sprint rather than a tortured marathon.

### Visual Clues and Cognitive Tricks
Your brain loves a good association, and 2.0 gives it a playground. A single image could be the basis for several plausible clues. The trick is to select a clue that ties together the intended cards while steering your team away from misreads. A well-chosen clue feels almost like a mini-magic trick, where the right question reveals a hidden pattern. The more you play, the better you get at spotting those patterns quickly, which translates into faster rounds and fewer dead-end debates around the coffee table.

## Strategy and Tips for Your Next Session
- Start with safe, obvious links. A clue that clearly ties two or more images gives your team confidence and momentum. You can iterate toward riskier connections as players build comfort with the image language.
- Use neutral images strategically. They are the misdirection you can deploy to separate thinking time from fast decisions. A well-placed neutral card can derail the wrong train of thought without embarrassing anyone.
- Learn the table’s tempo. Some groups love fast rounds with quick associations; others enjoy debates and long discussions. Tailor your clues to the energy of the table rather than pushing a single approach.
- Embrace the chaos. Some of the best moments arrive when a clue is hilariously off but still somehow on target. Treat misreads as stories you will tell at the next game night rather than as failure.

## Quick Start Guide (At-a-glance)
- Set up a board with the image cards laid out for the round.
- Assign red and blue teams, with a clue-giver for each turn.
- The clue-giver utters a one-word clue that ties multiple images together and the associated color, then the team guesses.
- The guessing continues until the team ends the round or hits a forbidden card.
- Rotate to the other team and repeat until a winner emerges.
