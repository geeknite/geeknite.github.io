## Maintenance, care, and longevity
A blade like this rewards simple, consistent care. Here’s a quick boilerplate you can actually follow:
- Cleaning: After use, wipe the blade with a dry cloth to remove fingerprints and moisture. For stubborn residues, a little rubbing alcohol on a microfiber cloth works fine, followed by a light coat of synthetic oil.
- Drying: Always dry thoroughly. Carbon steel hates moisture in the form of long-term romance with rust.
- Storage: Store in a dry place. If your saya is a snug fit, avoid banging around the edge with hard objects. If you’re a display-first person, consider a display stand that protects the blade while allowing it to be seen from multiple angles.
- Polishing: If you’re into the look of a mirror edge or you simply enjoy the ritual of sharpening, a fine water stone or ceramic rod will help you maintain sharpness without removing too much of the blade material.
