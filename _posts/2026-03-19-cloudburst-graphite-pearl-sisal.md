## Practical Considerations: Maintenance, Cleaning, and Everyday Realities

### Routine Care: How to Keep a Graphite Pearl Gem Looking Stellar

Sisal rugs require a bit more mindful care than their plush cousins. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps avoid fiber pull and keeps the surface looking crisp. If your vacuum has a beater brush, disable it for sisal to prevent excess wear. Spot cleaning should be done promptly—blot the spill rather than rub, which can push liquids deeper into the fibers. For sticky or stubborn stains, a mild cleaning solution can be used; always test a small, inconspicuous area first. The goal is to preserve the texture and color integrity without turning your rug into a science experiment gone wrong.

For deeper cleanings, some homeowners opt for professional rug cleaning services that specialize in natural fiber rugs.
