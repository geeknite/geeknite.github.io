## Installation and Compatibility

Setting this up is refreshingly simple, which is the point:
- Step 1: Position the panel along your desk edge where you want your private zone.
- Step 2: Clamp the mounts on; tighten until snug—but not so tight you start to crack your desk on the first attempt.
- Step 3: Check that the panel sits flush with the desk and isn’t wobbling.
- Step 4: Clean the acrylic with a microfiber cloth; avoid abrasive cleaners that could leave micro-scratches. Pro tip: use ammonia-free cleaner or water with a dash of mild dish soap.

The compatibility note: Most desk edges up to a certain thickness will accommodate the clamps. If your desk has a thick edge (or a built-in desk grommet that interferes), you may need to reposition or adjust.

## Durability and Maintenance

Acrylic can scratch; this is something to be mindful of. The 2-inch thick panel is fabulous for rigidity but can show scuffs from rings, keys, or the occasional espresso spill. To mitigate:
- Use a microfiber cloth for daily wipe-downs.
- Avoid harsh abrasive cleaners; use mild soap and water or a dedicated acrylic cleaner.
- If scratches appear, light buffing with a specialized acrylic scratch remover can restore a near-new look, though deep gouges require professional attention (not recommended to practice on your desk divider).

## Cleaning Tips and Longevity

- Do not use ammonia-based cleaners; they can cloud the surface over time.
- Use water and mild dish soap for regular cleaning.
- For stubborn prints or marks, a diluted isopropyl alcohol solution (at low concentration) can help, but test in a corner first.
- Buff after cleaning with a dry microfiber cloth to avoid streaks.

## Sound and Privacy

Acrylic does not block sound; it’s not a sound barrier. If you need privacy in conversations, you’ll still need a good pair of headphones or a separate cubicle. The panel’s value is visual: it provides an immediate, tactile boundary that signals to others to respect your mental space.

## Callouts: Comparison and Alternatives

- Compared to glass: Acrylic is lighter and less prone to shattering, but scratches more easily. If your desk orientation includes heavy dust, you might see micro-scratches show up; if you’re careful, it’s fine.
- Compared to polycarbonate: Polycarbonate is more flexible and often used in windows; it can be tougher in some impact scenarios, but acrylic has a superior optical clarity for your monitor viewing.
- Alternative setups: If you don’t want to clamp, consider desk risers with sliding panels or modular partitions that attach to a desk via a clip system.
