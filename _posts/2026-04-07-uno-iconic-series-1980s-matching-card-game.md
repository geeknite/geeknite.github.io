### How UNO Works (The Core Matching Concept)
UNO is a sprint through color and number. On your turn, you must place a card that matches either the color or the number of the card on the discard pile. If you can’t play, you draw one card and pray to the gaming gods you won’t be stuck with two Draw Twos back-to-back. Special cards inject personality into the chaos:
- Draw Two makes the next player draw a pair of cards and cede the tempo.
- Skip makes the next player miss a turn, which is basically a small victory for social strategists.
- Reverse flips the direction of play, a tiny 1980s chaos in a plastic sleeve.
- Wild lets you choose the color you continue with, a flexible power that screams control in a world of chaos. The Wild Draw Four adds maximum drama and maximum complaints from the table.

The objective is deceptively simple: shed all your cards first. If you forget to say UNO when you have one card left and someone calls you out, you might be penalized with a little extra drawing—because nothing says fun like a communal groan about a missed call.
