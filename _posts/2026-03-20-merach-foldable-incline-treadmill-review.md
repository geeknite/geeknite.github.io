Maintenance tips from a gear-nerd perspective

- Regular belt cleaning to remove dust and hair that can reduce belt life and belt alignment.
- Occasional belt lubrication as recommended by the manual to reduce friction and noise. Over-lubricating can be messy, so follow the guidelines.
- Check the folding hinges and locking pins for smooth operation and to prevent accidental collapse.
- Inspect the power cord and plug for wear. If something looks frayed or suspicious, replace it promptly rather than risking a short circuit.
- If the treadmill sits in a high-humidity area (like a basement or loft converted to a gym), consider a dehumidifier to prevent corrosion on metal parts.
