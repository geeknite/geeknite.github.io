## Safety, precautions, and potential side effects

No medicine comes with a perfect guarantee, and dewormers are no exception. Milpro Allwormer for cats over 2 kg is generally well tolerated, but like all veterinary drugs, it can cause some side effects in a subset of cats. Common but less dramatic reactions may include:
- Temporary vomiting after administration
- Slight drooling or mouth-watering due to the taste
- Diarrhea or softer stools for a day or two
- Rare allergic reactions such as facial swelling or hives

If you notice anything unusual after giving the medication, contact your veterinarian promptly. Also keep an eye out for signs of a more serious reaction, such as persistent vomiting, severe lethargy, or any breathing trouble. In those cases, treat it as a medical emergency and seek veterinary care immediately.

Pregnant or lactating cats usually require veterinary guidance before deworming. Even though these medicines have a long track record of safe use, pregnancy and lactation can alter how a drug is processed by the body, and a vet can tailor the dosing to protect both the queen and the kittens.

Interacting medications can also affect how Milpro Allwormer works. If your cat is taking other dewormers, antiparasitic drugs, or certain supplements, check with your veterinarian to avoid unintended interactions that could reduce effectiveness or raise the risk of adverse effects.
