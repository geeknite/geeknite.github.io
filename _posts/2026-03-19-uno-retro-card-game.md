## How to Play: Rules and Twist (If Any)
UNO Retro keeps most of the classic UNO rules intact. If you’ve played UNO in any capacity since your childhood, you’ll recognize this flow:
- The goal is to be the first player to discard all your cards.
- On your turn, you must match a card from your hand to the color or number or symbol of the top discard. If you can’t, you draw a card.
- Special action cards add chaos: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four.
- When you have one card left, you must shout UNO. If you forget and get caught, you draw a penalty card (in many households, this is a quick way to reintroduce chaos to the room).

In UNO Retro, the changes are mostly aesthetic rather than mechanical. The game uses the same action cards, same draw rules, and same win condition. The “retro” effect is that the artwork and the packaging feel like a time capsule—an artifact you might dust off before a party to remind everyone that life was loud, long, and probably in color on a CRT monitor.

Here’s a quick rules snapshot for a six-person game night:
- Each player starts with seven cards.
- Players take turns matching color or number or playing a Wild card to change the color in play.
- Draw Two forces the next player to draw two and forfeit their turn. Skip makes the next player miss a turn. Reverse flips the direction of play.
- Wild lets you choose any color. Wild Draw Four makes the next player draw four cards and offers you a color pick.
- If you can’t play a card, you draw one from the draw pile.
- When you have one card left, say UNO. If you don’t and is caught, you're likely to hear a chorus of groans from the table and maybe a gentle tease about your life choices.
