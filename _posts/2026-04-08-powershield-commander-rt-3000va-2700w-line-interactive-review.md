## Technical Snapshot

### Power rating and efficiency

With 3000 VA and 2700 W max load, you can push a mid-tier gaming rig, a streaming PC, and a NAS at the same time and still have some battery buffer. Real-world efficiency is decent for a line-interactive device; it isn't a Tesla Powerwall, but it doesn't pretend to be—that would be overkill for a living room.

### Line-interactive and AVR

Line-interactive UPS with AVR is built to handle voltage sags without draining battery. When the incoming voltage dips, the AVR boosts the output to keep your gear safe. When the voltage spikes, the surge protection kicks in. Translation: your PC won't become a voxel-art statue when the grid goes on roller skates.

### Battery system

A user-replaceable SLA battery system means you can swap the power cells after a few years without needing a nuclear reactor license. The battery chemistry is tried-and-true, with good stability and predictable warm-up performance during recharge cycles.

### Noise, heat, and comfort

In typical operation, it hums softly—loud enough to hear over a mid-tier air conditioner and not so loud that you want to move to the closet. Heat dissipation is adequate; there are vents around the chassis; if you push it hard for long periods, expect a little warmth, but nothing alarming. If you run a noisy server closet, you will appreciate the difference vs a cheap, no-frills backup.

## Setup and Daily Use

Getting started with the Commander RT is straightforward:

1) Place the unit in a ventilated area with free airflow. Do not cage it behind the desk; it needs to breathe like a dragon after lunch.
2) Connect critical gear to the backed-up outlets. Keep nonessential devices on the surge-only outlets to save battery for the important things.
3) Plug the UPS into a properly grounded outlet. Avoid daisy-chaining power strips unless you want to test your creativity in how many adapters you can stack before the house blinks.
4) Use the LCD or software to monitor battery health and runtime estimates. The UI is intentionally simple, with enough information to make you feel like a certified tech wizard without requiring a computer science degree.

Maintenance tips:
- Periodically perform a self-test through the front panel to confirm healthy battery status. This helps you avoid that moment when you actually need the device and it refuses to cooperate.
- Replace batteries when the runtime drops below expected thresholds. SLA cells aren’t immortal; treat them with respect, like old school video game cartridges.
