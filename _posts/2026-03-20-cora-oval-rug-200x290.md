### Care and Maintenance
A practical rug needs practical care instructions. Vacuum the rug regularly on a low setting to avoid pulling fibers. When spills occur, blot immediately with a clean cloth—absorb rather than scrub, unless you want to audition a host of tiny whirlwind textures within the fibers. For persistent stains, a mild detergent solution can help, followed by thorough rinsing to avoid soapy residues that attract more dirt.

We recommend rotating the rug every few months to even out wear and prevent any pattern from fading due to sun exposure. If your space has high sunlight, consider UV blocking window treatments to preserve color over time. Cleaning frequency depends on traffic. In a family with kids and pets, you might clean every week or two; in a low-traffic living area, quarterly cleaning may suffice.

### DIY Underlay Setup
If you want to optimize comfort and prolong the rug life, a simple underlay can make a big difference. Look for a pad with a thickness of 6-10 mm for most standard homes. A good underlay reduces wear, minimizes slipping, and gives your underfoot experience a cushier feel without adding a lot of height. For renters or spaces where you cannot install a permanent underlay, consider temporary adhesive options or anti-slip mats that can be placed under the rug without damaging floors.
