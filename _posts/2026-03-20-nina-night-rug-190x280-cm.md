## In practice: how it feels on real floors

Walking on Nina Night is a little like stepping onto a blacksmith’s forge floor, minus the heat and with more plush. It is soft enough for barefoot mornings yet firm enough to support standing desk sessions without feeling like your feet forgot to grow. The surface has a slight, forgiving texture that helps hide minor footprints and dust, which is a feature you will appreciate if you live with kids, pets, or a weekly gaming marathon. If you spill coffee or a glass of red wine, the stain resistance helps, but it is not a magical shield. Quick action with a damp cloth will remove most common stains and prevent them from setting. For more stubborn stains, a gentle cleaning solution is recommended and always test a small inconspicuous area first.

## Maintenance and care

The approach to care is simple: treat it like you would treat your favorite hoodie. Vacuum weekly on a low to medium suction setting; avoid aggressive beater bars that could fuzz the surface. Rotate the rug every few months to distribute wear and prevent visible footprints from becoming a feature. If you have pets, you will appreciate how well this fiber hides fur, especially when you run the vacuum in the opposite direction of the hair growth pattern. For occasional deeper cleanings, a gentle carpet shampoo with rinsing and drying thoroughly is enough. Always allow it to dry completely before returning it to the floor to prevent mold or a mood swing in your living room.

## Room ideas and layout

- Living rooms: Place Nina Night under the main seating area, with the sofa or sectional balanced by a couple of coffee tables. The rug should extend beyond the front legs of the sofa to create a cohesive conversation space without isolating the seating from the rest of the room.
- Bedrooms: Extend the rug under the bed and toward the footboard so you can step onto soft fibers when you wake. In a larger bedroom, you may place a second rug under a seating area or a bench for additional comfort.
- Hallways and entryways: A rug of this size sets the tone for the rest of the house and reduces noise from foot traffic. The dark tone helps hide dirt that would otherwise appear in lighter colors.
