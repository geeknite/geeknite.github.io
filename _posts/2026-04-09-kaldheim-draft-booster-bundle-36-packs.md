## What’s in the Bundle

In the modern era of Magic product design, a 36-pack bundle isn’t just a handful of cardboard; it’s a carefully tuned machine for social interaction, strategy, and occasional frustration. Here’s what you typically get:

### 36 Booster Packs
Each booster brings 15 cards: usually 1 rare or mythic, 3–4 uncommons, 10 commons, and 1-2 slots that can be a land or additional goodies. The distribution isn’t a math problem; it’s a social contract: you’re here to draft, not to compute the best opening hand in a vacuum. You’ll witness a mix of showcase-style art, bold Norse-inspired frames, and the occasional card that looks like it belongs in a different game entirely. Expect some pull strengths; not every pack is going to spit out a mythic, but some are (and those are the moments you remember during the drive home).

### A Rare or Mythic in Every Pack? Sometimes, Yes
While not every pack contains a mythic, you’ll often glimpse a rare or two per bundle that makes you grin or groan depending on how many times you’ve opened a very specific card in a row. The mythics in Kaldheim lean into the set’s bold design—think limited-time spells, legendary creatures with big board impact, and some collaborative dual-color combos that push you toward certain color pairs. The thrill of seeing a legendary card that can anchor your draft strategy (or derail someone else’s) is a key draw of this product.

### Basic Lands and Multicolor Variants
As with most sets, land cards are essential, and Kaldheim offers a multi-faceted land system that can unlock color-heavy strategies or salvage obvious mana problems late in the game. You’ll see a hefty dose of lands with the art style that feels like it was designed by a Nordic woodshop—grainy textures, stormy skies, and enough runic detail to make your neighbor feel underdressed for a guild meeting.

### Foils and Variants
Foils make an appearance here and there. Whether you’re chasing foil rares, foil showcase cards, or the occasional foil mythic, you’ll want to stay hydrated and watch your wallet as you open more packs than you planned. Foil variants aren’t guaranteed but they’re a satisfying cherry on top when they show up.
