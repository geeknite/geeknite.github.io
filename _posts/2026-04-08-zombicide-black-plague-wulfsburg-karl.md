What is Zombicide Black Plague

Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative board game set in a dark fantasy world where medieval villages have more corpses than a cosplay funeral. Players take on the roles of Survivors, scavenge for weapons, fight hordes of zombies, and try to complete a set of objectives before the siege of undead overwhelm the party. The Black Plague edition reimagines the original Zombicide rules with more brutal combat, more brutal zombies, and more potential for glorious chaos. The core loop stays simple enough for a weekend binge, but the layers of equipment, enemy types, and event cards give veteran players something to chew on longer than a goblin tongue.

In brief, Zombicide is about synergy and mischief. You need to coordinate paths, protect vulnerable teammates, and decide which zombie is most likely to step on a trap you forgot you set. The game can tilt into dramatic heroics or cartoonish incompetence in a heartbeat, and that balance is part of the charm. For fans of apocalyptic fantasy with a heavy dose of planning and dice luck, Black Plague hits a sweet spot between grim noir and dungeon crawl chaos.

Enter Wulfsburg and Karl

Wulfsburg is an expansion that adds a new location to the map and a couple of fresh survivors, including Karl. The setting feels like a Gothic fable filtered through a dungeon crawler lens. Wulfsburg itself is a rune-carved village with more towers than a chessboard and enough werewolves lore to satisfy any room full of fantasy fans. Karl, the survivor you can play as, is the kind of reluctant hero who would rather brew a herbal tea for a drowning ally than wave a massive axe around—until a zombie drops in for a snack and all polite behavior goes out the window.

Karl: the man, the myth, the muffled scream

Karl has a style that fits the Wulfsburg vibe. He is tough, but he is not all brute force. Karl can move with surprising speed and has abilities that reward careful planning. He excels in mid range engagements, where you want to poke at the zombies with measured strikes rather than swing wildly. Karl’s presence in Wulfsburg also influences how you explore the board, because his special actions encourage you to think about the terrain and the line of sight in a way that is a little more tactical than pure brawn. You may find yourself setting up kill boxes, using walls for cover, and saving your best cards for the moment when Karl can exploit a weakness in the hoard.

The Wulfsburg contents at a glance

The Wulfsburg box is a compact, characterful add-on. It includes a couple of new survivor miniatures, new equipment cards, and a handful of event cards that reference the village and its folklore. The production values are solid; the sculpts have that ancient-yet-ominous look you would expect from a place named after wolves and fog. The new cards fit neatly into the core game and do not overwhelm the balance, though they do tilt the power curve just enough to feel like a new season of a favorite show rather than a rerun.

Setup and first impressions

Setting up Wulfsburg is similar to adding any expansion in Zombicide. You lay down the new tile, shuffle in the new deck, and pick a few survivors. Karl is a good pick for players who like to think a beat ahead. Do not expect to sprint across the board and smash everything in two turns; this expansion rewards planning and patience. The first few rounds feel a bit stingy if you are new to Zombicide, but the payoff arrives quickly as you unlock Karl’s potential through careful positioning and terrain usage.

Mechanics and how Wulfsburg changes the flow

Wulfsburg introduces a thematic layer that emphasizes line of sight, terrain advantages, and strategic resource management. The new terrain can offer cover, chokepoints, or dangerous spots where wolves and werewolves might lurk in the shadows. Karl thrives when the party can leverage these features—think of it as a chessboard where the pieces are stubborn knights, werewolf lore, and very cranky zombies.

Key rules and interactions to watch for

- Movement around the Wulfsburg tile can create interesting choke points. Use these to funnel zombies toward Karl so he can unleash his best combos without losing his life in a stray zombie bite.
- Terrain modifiers matter more here. Some tiles provide defensive bonuses, others block line of sight. Communicate with your team and make a plan each round before the dice start rolling.
- Karl’s abilities encourage controlled aggression. The more you conserve lives and keep civilians safe, the more opportunities you have to leverage his stronger attacks when the moment is right.
- The new equipment cards offer tools that pair nicely with Karl’s kit, such as ranged options that let you weaken a line of zombies before they reach the team.

Gameplay pacing with Karl and Wulfsburg

If you like a slower burn that transforms into a satisfying crescendo, Karl and Wulfsburg deliver. The early rounds focus on map control and zombie conversion management, while the mid to late game opens up with sharper fights and a few spectacular saves that would make a movie montage proud. The balance remains faithful to the Zombicide way of rewarding careful planning while still letting luck punch you in the gut when a zombie line breaks the defense in one disastrous turn.

Strategy and tips for Karl in Wulfsburg

- Positioning is king. Do not run headlong into a mob. Use walls and terrain to create kill lanes that funnel zombies toward you rather than into your teammates' faces.
- Manage your weapons. Karl's best tools shine when you use them at the right moment. Save your strongest attacks for clusters of enemies or when you have a guaranteed hit through a line of sight that would make a sniper blush.
- Work as a team to protect the weaker survivors. Karl shines when he has room to maneuver. Do not abandon him to the zombies just because you found a cute loot drop elsewhere.
- Watch for event cards that reference Wulfsburg lore. They often provide opportunities or penalties that can swing the round quickly if you are not paying attention.

By round four, the team had built a neat little fortress using the terrain to their advantage. Karl cut down a line of zombies with a decisive swing, triggering a chain reaction that forced the remaining undead to re-route toward the back line. If you enjoy that balance of tension and ridiculous luck, Wulfsburg delivers in spades.

What players say and why it matters

Players who lean into tactical planning tend to adore Wulfsburg. Those who prefer rapid, smash and grab play might prefer other expansions that emphasize pure brute force. Either way, Karl adds a voice to the table that is distinct from the rest of the survivors. Gameplay length and replayability

Like many Zombicide expansions, Wulfsburg does not dramatically extend the number of turns required for a win, but it changes how you reach those turns. The variety in terrain and the way Karl interacts with the map means no two sessions feel identical. The amount of replayability skyrockets when you mix in a few other survivors and assets from the core game. If you are a person who loves seeing new board layouts and trying different team compositions, you will get a lot of mileage from this expansion.

Final verdict

Zombicide Black Plague Wulfsburg Karl stands as a strong addition to the Black Plague line. It preserves the core tempo that players love while injecting a flavor of strategy that rewards planning and teamwork. Karl feels like a well designed character with a clear role, neither underpowered nor overpowered. The Wulfsburg terrain changes the map geometry in satisfying ways and creates memorable moments that you will be retelling to friends long after the last zombie has fallen.

If you are already a Zombicide fanatic, this expansion is a no brainer. If you are a casual gamer who wants a little more depth without sacrificing accessibility, Wulfsburg will likely win you over with its thematic charm and solid mechanics. And if you are completely new to Zombicide, starting with Black Plague along with Wulfsburg will give you a compact but rich experience to learn the ropes without being overwhelmed by too many moving parts.
