## Zombicide Special Guest Juanra Fernandez review

![](/assets/images/zombicide-special-guest-juanra-fernandez.jpg)

Zombicide has a long history of collaborations with big personalities, crossovers, and the occasional meme spreader. this time the studio has invited a special guest who is famous for hosting a high energy game show and for delivering dramatic suspense with a flourish. this review dives into how the special guest edition featuring Juanra Fernández lands in practice, whether it adds to the gameplay rather than stealing the spotlight, and how many times you will laugh while counting walkers. by the end you will know if this is a must add for your table or a fun side quest that stays on the shelf for weekend sessions only.

### Introduction
Zombicide is a cooperative dash through a zombie filled world where players work as a team to survive waves of the undead, rescue survivors, and complete missions before the clock ticks down. the base game rewards planning, coordination, and smart risk taking. the Juanra edition adds a host inspired vibe that pushes the action toward showtime, with a deck of guest cards and a rhythm that feels like a live broadcast. the aim is to strike a balance between theater and tactics, between humor and heart pounding crunch, between memorable lines and meaningful choices. the result is a product that wants you to smile even as the horde closes in.

### Whats in the box
the box comes with everything you expect from a solid zombicide expansion plus a few extras that push the theme into the spotlight. components are sturdy, with the usual mix of zombie minis, survivors minis, tokens, dice, and boards. you will find a new deck of Juanra guest cards, a stack of audience tokens that gauge the room vibe, and a host token that moves around the board to trigger prompts. the mission cards lean into the show format, inviting players to stage dramatic rescues, escape sequences, and prop room puzzles. the new art style blends studio lighting with a touch of horror palette to remind you that you are still fighting for your life while the audience watches.

in addition, there is a small handful of alternate survivor skins that give a theatrical edge to your team and help you distinguish who is playing which role under the show lights. the quality of the minis and cards is consistent with other zombicide expansions, with clear iconography and readable text that makes it easy to teach newcomers. the box itself is compact enough for a quick setup, but the content rewards longer sessions with varied scenarios and a playful approach to the rules.

### The special guest mechanic explained
the heart of this expansion is the special guest mechanic. during the game a new deck of guest cards can be drawn that introduces host led events and interactive prompts. these cards can trigger a variety of effects such as small side challenges, audience driven modifiers, or temporary changes to the rules that emphasize storytelling. the host is not a villain or a power role; instead the host acts as a narrative catalyst whose prompts are designed to boost the mood and speed of play rather than disrupt the core objective. the audience tokens act as a gauge for how much chaos you will face in a given round. when the audience is high, the host prompts can unlock more powerful actions or provide a safety net that helps you survive a tricky moment. when the audience dips, the prompts shift toward smaller adjustments that keep the session moving but less dramatic.

this mechanic achieves a rare balance. it is enough to keep the energy high without creating a constant stream of random penalties that would derail the players. it respects the players agency by offering choices in how they respond to prompts and it invites a little improv without turning the game into a theater troupe. for players who enjoy a heavier focus on tactical decisions, this is still manageable as the host prompts are supplementary rather than central. for players who crave social interaction, the host mechanic becomes a delightful catalyst for jokes, banter, and roleplay moments that leave a lasting impression.

### Flow of a typical session
setup is familiar if you played the base game, with the addition that you will shuffle the Juanra guest deck and the Reto cards separately. the Reto cards are the heart of the host prompts, and they are designed to be quick, witty, and optional to perform. when a round begins you execute your standard actions, then you may trigger or resolve a guest prompt depending on the card draw. the audience tokens provide a mood barometer that can affect how easily you can resolve a prompt or how generous the host is with his timing. the endgame triggers as in the base game, but the final mission often includes a show finale moment where the survivors must complete a last dramatic objective under the gaze of the host and the audience alike.

for players who like a steady rhythm, the Juanra edition offers a rhythm track baked into the mission structure. other players who love a burst of improv will find the host prompts to be a constant source of funny exchanges and memorable moments. the pacing is slightly unpredictable, but within the bounds of a well designed cooperative game. if you treat the host as a social facilitator rather than a random effect, the experience becomes easier to manage and more enjoyable for most groups.

### Scenarios and mission design
the expansion brings several show style missions that emphasize dramatic timing and social cooperation. think of stage doors, prop rooms, and backstage corridors as the stage where the action unfolds. each mission weaves in host prompts at key moments, creating a combined puzzle where you must manage both the zombie threat and the social dynamic. the missions are varied enough to provide two or three nights of play with different outcomes, and you can scale up the challenge by adding more guest cards or dial down by aiming for fewer prompts. the variety helps extend the life of the expansion beyond a single playthrough and keeps the replay value high for groups that enjoy fresh setups.

### Gameplay balance and difficulty
balancing is a tough call for any expansion that mixes humor with crunch. the Juanra edition leans toward a middle ground. host prompts are frequent enough to feel present but not so intrusive that they overshadow your core decisions. dice outcomes still matter; the host influences only through the prompts and audience mood, not by stealing core mechanics. this is crucial because it preserves the sense of accomplishment when a mission is completed while still letting the host be a memorable addition to the session. players who favor to plan several turns ahead will appreciate that you can still map a strategy around the show vibe rather than being forced to improvise every moment.

for players who enjoy a high level of randomness, the guest deck can sometimes sprinkle in a wild moment that shakes up the plan. if this is not your cup of tea, you can control the frequency by adjusting how many guest cards you draw per game. the result is a customizable chaos that can be tuned to your group size and preferred play tempo.

### Thematic tone and humor
the show vibe is well integrated with the survival theme. humor comes from the prompts, the occasional one liners, and the way players react to the host bending a rule for the sake of drama. this is not a stand up comedy set; it is a playful enhancement that creates inside jokes across sessions. as long as players engage with the prompts with willingness to lean into the theme, the humor lands rather nicely. there are moments when running jokes emerge about stage direction, lighting cues, and audience reactions that feel organic to the setting rather than forced puns.

### Artwork and production values
visuals stay faithful to zombicide aesthetics, with a slightly brighter, more theatrical overlay. the art direction reflects studio lighting, with zombies that look menacing but not cartoonish, while survivors carry a sense of character that makes it easy to narrate their actions in a show style. the cards are readable, the icons are intuitive, and the packaging is sturdy enough for repeated sessions. this is not a cheap novelty; it is a well crafted expansion that earns its keep on the table.

### Pros and cons at a glance
- pros: adds social layer without breaking balance, high replay value, good for groups that love banter, easy to integrate with base game and with other small expansions
- cons: some players may prefer a more serious crunch, too much improv if the group goes all in, can run longer than a straight zombie slog if prompts are frequent

### How it compares with other zombicide variants
when you compare with other expansions that add new zombie types or complicated rule sets, this one sits toward the lighter side of the spectrum and prioritizes social play. if you want more narrative content but still want a fast pace, this is a strong candidate. if you want total mechanical novelty and heavier crunch, you might want to look at other releases first and consider this one as a palate cleanser between heavier campaigns.

### House rules and variant ideas
for groups that want to customize further, here are a few ideas that have worked well in my playtests
- reduce the number of guest prompts in the first two rounds of a session to ease newcomers into the vibe
- add a special rule where a failed prompt triggers a friendly audience cheer that gives a tiny edge to a survivor next round
- allow a one time reflip of a guest card when the group completes a mission, to create a sense of live studio momentum
- combine with smaller deck expansions to keep the game shorter while maintaining show style energy
these are simple, flexible rules that help tailor the experience to different groups without turning the game into a theater comedy festival
