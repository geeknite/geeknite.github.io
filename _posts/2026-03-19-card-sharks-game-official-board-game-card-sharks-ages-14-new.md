## How to Play (A Quick Overview)
Card Sharks is a game of risk, memory, and bluffing, wrapped in a neat package that resembles a family game-night classic with a twist. Here is a high-level overview to give you the flavor without spoiling the entire recipe:

- Each round starts with a draw from the deck. The value on the revealed card sets the target contour for the rest of the round. Players take turns predicting whether the next card will be higher or lower than the current card.
- Bets are made using chips. The more confident you feel about the next card, the bigger your wager. Bluffing is not only allowed but encouraged, as it adds to the tension and makes the host reaction even more entertaining.
- If you guess correctly, your chips accumulate; if you are wrong, you lose chips and possibly funding for the next round. The balance between risk and reward is where the heart of Card Sharks lives.
- The board integrates a few reward and penalty spaces that can alter the momentum in surprising ways. This is the moment where the player who looks calm on the outside might be sweating on the inside and planning a comeback strategy while pretending to be nonchalant.
- The round continues until one player clears a certain threshold of chips or the deck cycles back through. The person with the most chips at the end is declared the winner, and the shark banner gets a small victory dance to celebrate the drama.

What makes Card Sharks tick is not only the luck of the draw but the players reading the room. The moment you sense fear or overconfidence from your opponents, you can push a little extra on your bets or tempt fate with a bold higher or lower call. The tension is the spice, and it comes with a dose of humor as players attempt to maintain cool under escalating bets and the unpredictable arc of the deck.

## Strategy vs Luck: Finding Your Game Rhythm
A lot of people come to Card Sharks expecting a pure skill game, only to realize that luck plays a good portion of the morning coffee. The reality is a delightful blend of both. Card counting is not a viable house trick here; instead, you need to read human patterns, manage your risk, and use psychological tools to influence other players. Here are a few strategies that can help you survive and perhaps thrive:

- Read the room: The best players are not always the ones who make the most accurate guesses, but the ones who leverage the mood at the table. If your friend is visibly sweating after two wrong bets, consider a more cautious approach to avoid giving them a dramatic rebound opportunity.
- Control the pace: Slow and deliberate bets can unsettle faster players who crave rapid decisions. But be careful not to become the human metronome that stalls the entire game; people will blame you for the clock and stage fright will take hold.
- Use tentativeness as a weapon: Sometimes saying nothing and letting the silence do the talking is a powerful bluff. If you can appear calm when the deck punishes you, you gain the psychological edge for future rounds.
- Manage chip flow: Chips are your currency of confidence. If you bet conservatively early on, you can build a reserve to pull off a dramatic late-game swoop. The key is to avoid a scenario where your last two bets leave you with a single chip and a dream.
- Learn the deck rhythm: While you cannot memorize every card sequence, you can sense when the deck is more forgiving and when it tends to bite. Use that intuition to calibrate your risk profile for each round.

With practice, you will develop a sense of when to press your luck and when to pull back. The nice thing about Card Sharks is that even beginners often get a sense of control after a few turns, which makes the table feel like a supportive playground rather than a relentless gauntlet.

## Variants, House Rules, and Modded Fun
This is a game that invites you to add your own flavor without breaking the core loop. Here are a few light house rules and variants you can try to spice things up:

- Speed round: Set a two-minute timer and force players to make quick decisions. This adds adrenaline and can lead to hilarious misreads as the clock ticks down.
- Bluff round: After a successful guess, allow the betting player to bluff about the next card value. If the bluff is correctly challenged, the challenger gets a bonus. This brings a little poker-esque deception into the mix.
- Team mode: Pair up players and run rounds as teams. Team members discuss bets privately, but the final decision is still made by the designated bettor. Team dynamics can lead to some surprisingly strategic collaborations and some dramatic betrayals.
- Thematic overlays: Use snacks and themed props to create micro themes for each round. For example, one round can be a space mission where space diet chips correspond to risk levels, or a detective noir chapter where stylized titles are whispered under smoky lighting.

House rules can improve the social experience or help beginners feel more comfortable. The key is to maintain a balance between tension and fun so that the game remains accessible for all players while still offering opportunities to surprise and delight.
