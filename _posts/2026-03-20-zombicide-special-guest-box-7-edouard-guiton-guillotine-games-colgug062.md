## Painting and hobby tips
If you plan to bring these minis to life, Box 7 provides a perfect opportunity to experiment with Edouard’s bold color language. Here are some practical tips to get you a table-ready table presence:
- Start with bold base colors for survivors. Edouard’s characters benefit from high-contrast palettes that cut through the tabletop lighting, especially under game room fluorescents.
- Apply a controlled wash to bring out facial features and folds in clothing without muddying the overall tone. The goal is crisp separation rather than depth-for-its-own-sake, which suits Zombicide’s stylized vibe.
- For zombies, keep textures legible. A light dry brush on ragged clothing helps create a narrative of corruption and rot without making it look grim for grim sake.
- Use metallics sparingly and with a gloss finish to catch light on blades or gear during dramatic turns. Subtle highlights can make the miniatures feel more alive without drawing attention away from the faces.
- Two-color per figure is a wise starting approach for newcomers. It reduces the time spent on base coats and helps you tell survivors apart at a glance during busy rounds.
- Seal your work with a matte finish to maintain durability and reduce wear during long campaign nights. Zombicide’s chaos loves to punish sloppy paint jobs, so a proper seal is your friend.
