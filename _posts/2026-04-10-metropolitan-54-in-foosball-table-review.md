Setup and Maintenance

Setup is not a chore so much as a ritual. As for maintenance, the Metropolitan is designed to be friendlier than your average arcade table: wipe down surfaces with a non-abrasive cleaner, keep the rods clean to avoid buildup that might impede spin, and check the leg brackets periodically. A little wax on the rods can keep them gliding smoothly, and yes, you can pretend you are a professional foosball mechanic while doing it because the Internet told you that such a job exists and you deserve it.

Maintenance tips worth repeating

- Regularly wipe down surfaces after long sessions to prevent sweat and snack debris from building up.
- Inspect rods for straightness and smooth operation; a slight bend can turn a casual shot into a world-class warp drive shot intended to embarrass a goalkeeper who is your best friend.
- Rotate players occasionally to distribute wear evenly; it also makes for a fun little dance when you do a mini-swap mid-game just to show you care about equal skill distribution.
- Use a light wax on the rods if you notice friction increasing; this helps maintain spin and reduces strain on your wrists during a marathon session.
- Keep spare balls around; a few identical balls help maintain consistent play and also provide a ready-made prop for dramatic celebrations.
