## Comfort, durability, and daily care

### Underfoot comfort and resilience
Comfort-wise, the NZ wool’s natural loft gives a comfortable cushion that’s gentle on bare feet. It’s not a memory-foam dream, but for a rug, it’s generous. The mid-range pile height makes it ideal for under coffee tables where you want to rest your feet while still allowing chairs to slide in and out without snagging. Durability comes from the wool fibers’ intrinsic resilience. Wool has natural elasticity, allowing it to recover after compression. In practical terms: you can sit, stand, and rearrange furniture on the rug without it looking worn down after a few months. That’s especially important if you frequently rearrange the living room or host movie nights with dim lights and heavy snacks.

#### Backing, padding, and maintenance basics
A robust backing is essential for longevity in a hand-tufted rug. The typical backing is fabric with a stabilized latex finish, which helps the tufted loop retain its shape and resist unraveling at the edges. To maximize life, pair with a high-quality rug pad. A pad prevents slipping on hard floors, reduces wear, and adds an extra layer of comfort underfoot. If you’re eco-conscious, look for pad materials sourced from recycled tires or natural jute options, which can offer a more sustainable pairing.

Daily care is straightforward: vacuum regularly with a beater bar on a low setting or a suction-only vacuum to avoid pulling fibers. If you have a shag-like pile, you’ll want to adjust the suction level to prevent fiber entanglement. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even exposure to sunlight and foot traffic, which prevents conspicuous color fading and wear patterns.

### Stains, spills, and general cleaning philosophy
Wool fibers resist staining better than some synthetics, but they’re not invincible. Treat spills promptly—blot, don’t rub. Use a clean, absorbent cloth to lift the liquid from the surface. For light stains, a mixture of water and mild wool-safe detergent can be applied with a blotting technique. Avoid hot water and strong chemicals that can degrade wool fibers or cause color bleed. If a stubborn stain persists, professional cleaning is a wise investment rather than brute-forcing with household solvents.

For routine refresh, plan a professional cleaning every 12-24 months depending on foot traffic, pets, and sun exposure. When you do, ensure the cleaner knows wool rugs and the Floral Vine pattern to avoid color distortion or pattern distortion. And yes, I know you love a stain-free, showroom finish vibe, but reality check: rugs are living pieces; some wear is part of their story.

### Sun exposure and color stability
Neutral palettes are beautiful, but they can be vulnerable to sun fading. If your rug sits near a sunny window, you might notice subtle color changes over time. Rotate or shade the rug with curtains or blinds to preserve color integrity. The floral motif may also reveal more depth in certain light, which is not a defect but a feature—your rug will transform as the sun travels across the room, giving you a micro-arc of mood lighting throughout the day.

## Care and cleaning: tips that keep it pristine longer

### Regular care routine
Vacuum weekly with care, especially around high-traffic zones. Use a low-power setting and a microfiber attachment to avoid snagging fibers. Rotate every few months to ensure color and pile wear evenly. If you have a home with pets, consider scotch guarding or protective sprays designed for wool rugs—these products create a barrier against liquid spills, which is essential for households with coffee-loving humans and curious cats.

### Quick stain response strategy
Spills happen. Adopting a quick response approach saves hours at the upholstery clinic. Blot the spill immediately with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and let the yarns rebound. For minor stains, a wool-safe detergent diluted with water can lift stains without altering the yarn’s natural elasticity. For more stubborn stains, call in a pro or schedule a professional cleaning. It’s not glamorous, but it’s effective.

### Deep cleaning and maintenance cycles
Professional cleaning every 12-24 months is typically enough for most households. If you’re in a dust-prone environment or have heavy traffic, you might opt for a more frequent schedule. Ensure the cleaners understand wool fibers to prevent color loss or fiber damage. After cleaning, replace or refresh the rug pad to maintain support and cushion. If you’re handy, you can refresh the fibers by gently brushing with a soft brush to keep the pile uniform and plush.
