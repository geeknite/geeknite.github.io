## Sizing, fit, and room planning

One of the tricky parts of rug shopping is determining the right size for your space. The Cambridge Grey Rug typically comes in several standard sizes: small, medium, large, and a few runners for hallways. The rule of thumb remains simple: you want at least 18 to 24 inches of exposed floor around the rug in seating areas, which helps define the space without making it feel boxed in. For open-plan rooms, a larger rug can anchor the seating arrangement and reduce visual clutter. If you have a sectional, think of the rug as a stage for the seating ensemble rather than a separate island.

In a bedroom, laying the Cambridge Grey Rug under the bed with about 2 feet extending on all sides can create a soft landing for the first steps in the morning. For a living room, a large rug under the coffee table that leaves room for side furniture to sit on the edge of the rug feels cohesive. If you live in a space where margins are tight, a shorter runner along the bed or the couch can achieve a similar effect without overwhelming the room.

## Cleaning, maintenance, and practical care

Stain resistance is a major talking point for rugs in this category. The Cambridge Grey Rug often features a treatment or fiber that resists staining from common household culprits: coffee, red wine, pet messes, and the occasional cereal accident after a late-night gaming session. The cleaning routine is intentionally straightforward. Blot spills as soon as they happen, avoid rubbing, and treat with a mild detergent if needed. For deeper cleanings, many retailers and manufacturers recommend professional cleaning or a gentle machine wash recommended for area rugs. Always check the care label and follow the manufacturer’s guidelines because your rug is not a lab specimen, even if you sometimes feel like you are debugging life in a lab with a coffee mug in one hand and a remote in the other.

Regular vacuuming is important to keep the fibers looking fresh. A vacuum with a low suction setting or a rug-specific attachment is ideal to prevent snagging or stretching. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones. If you have a dog that uses the living room as a runway for its own fashion show, you might opt for a rug pad to reduce slipping and add a touch of bounce for the joints of the canine model.

## Shopping tips and buyer's guide

- Determine your room’s traffic level and select a pile height accordingly. Higher piles feel luxurious but are easier to crush in busy hallways.

- If you spill something, act fast. The sooner you blot, the better your odds of preventing a stain from becoming a memory.
- Compare different sizes in your space by using painter tape to map out correct rug dimensions on the floor before you buy. It helps visualize how the rug will sit with furniture.
