The cooperative core remains intact: talk, plan, and survive together. The guest mechanic introduces a light difficulty toggle: as a group, you must decide when to lean into risk or to play it safe. Practical Tips for Playthroughs

## Setup Time

Expect a longer setup than a vanilla Zombicide session. The extra components and the need to organize special guest cards can eat into your initial playtime. The payoff, however, is a table ready to go with fewer rummaging breaks once you’re up and running. Pro tip: use the included trays and appoint a “setup whisperer” to keep chaos to a minimum.

## Learning Curve

If you’re familiar with Zombicide, you’ll catch on quickly. The guest mechanic adds an extra layer of decision-making that can feel like a tactical mini-game within the main game. The first playthrough may require a quick read-through of the guest card rules, but with a single session as a warm-up, you’ll glide through subsequent plays.

## House Rules

For groups that want to tailor the chaos, consider capping the number of guest activations per scenario or setting a house rule to convert a failed guest activation into a more predictable outcome. You can also mix in a quick “designer’s cut” to give Edouard more influence early in the campaign for a more dramatic arc. Games are about choice; in this edition, you’ll get to choose how dramatic your arc becomes.
