## Reliability considerations for used GPUs
Used GPUs come with a warranty-shaped question mark. Some sellers offer limited returns; others offer no returns at all. You’ll want to verify the card’s prior life (gaming, maybe a bit of crypto mining), and inspect signs of wear on the shroud, backplate, and IO bracket. Look for visible signs of excessive heat or burning (discoloration on the PCB is not a good sign). If you can, request proof of testing or a short burn-in run to confirm stability. If you’re lucky, you’ll have a clean history and a buyer-friendly return policy. If you’re unlucky, you’ll walk away with a card that’s been through the wringer.

## Case size and compatibility
Triple-fan cards are generally longer than the average card. Before purchasing, measure your case clearance and ensure there’s enough room for the card’s length. Verify PCIe slot alignment and ensure your power supply has the necessary connector. In compact builds, you may need to rearrange space or temporarily remove a drive cage to create airflow and clearance. If you’re unsure, bring a tape measure and your sense of humor to the checkout.

## OC headroom and warranty caveats
Overclocking a used card is a mixed bag. The factory overclock on WindForce OC is a comfortable head start, but pushing more can lead to diminishing returns and potential instability. If you decide to tinker, do so in small increments and monitor temperatures and stability closely. Used hardware has a higher risk that pushing the limits might shorten its remaining lifespan, and in some retail setups, it could even affect warranty options for future purchases. If you want to give it a mild boost, keep to safe limits and be mindful of long gaming sessions.

## Maintenance tips to keep a used card healthy
- Clean dust regularly with a soft brush or brief bursts of compressed air
- Reapply thermal paste if temps rise after long gaming sessions
- Check fan bearings for any wobble or noise and replace if needed
- Inspect power connectors for wear and replace cables if necessary
- Ensure your case has good airflow and clean dust filters
- Keep drivers up to date, but avoid unstable beta versions that could cause headaches
