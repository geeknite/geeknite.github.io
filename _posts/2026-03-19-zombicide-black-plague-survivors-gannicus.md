### The Card and the Concept (Fan Variant)

Before we dive into playtesting style, here is a compact, fan-generated concept for Gannicus that keeps the vibe of a gladiator who also happens to like a good post-apocalyptic brawl. Remember, this is not an official card; it is a tribute to the notion that a gladiator could fit into the grim, undead-filled streets of Zombicide Black Plague.

- Primary ability: Gladiator Roar. When Gannicus attacks a zombie, you may reroll one combat die. This mirrors a gladiator's ability to seize momentum and stamp authority on the battlefield.
- Secondary ability: Arena Bond. If you perform a successful kill within two spaces of another survivor who has attacked this turn, you gain one extra action. Teamwork under the arena lights, baby.
- Loadout focus: Gladius or a gladiator-inspired short sword as the primary melee weapon; a lightweight shield for flavor and minor protection; and a leather pauldron for aesthetic flair.
- Special trait: Endurance of a Champion. If you roll a die that results in a critical success on your turn, you may ignore one incoming zombie bite that round (once per round). Not official, but dramatic as heck.

### Playstyle: How Gannicus Shines (and Where He Wobbles)

Gannicus is a bold melee option with a couple of potential quirks that can tilt a game in your favor when used with clever positioning. Here is a lay of the land:

- Strengths: High melee output, a reliable way to force a zombie to retreat or be eliminated, and an ability that rewards aggressive play and team coordination. Gladiator energy means you want him up close and personal with the worst threats first.
- Weaknesses: If you split up your squad and forget him near a door, he might miss the opportunity to capitalize on his momentum. He also has a slightly slower start if you depend on extra actions that require proximity to teammates. Like any gladiator, he plays best when his allies understand his rhythm and support him with quick lane-clearing.
- Mobility: Gannicus can pivot the battle with a few well-placed moves. You want him to be the person who charges into the center of a zombie cluster, breaks the line, and allows ranged partners to target from behind his shield of bravado.
- Resource management: His reroll mechanic means you want to allocate a couple extra actions to offensive rounds where a big swing can shift a mission outcome. Don’t waste the reroll on a stray zombie; save it for the critical kill that keeps your squad breathing.

### Loadouts and Tactical Configurations

If you want to run Gannicus as a core melee anchor, here are two plausible loadouts that keep the gladiator flavor and add some utility for the team.

- Core Melee Loadout
  - Weapon: Gladius or equivalent short sword with high damage on a clean hit.
  - Armor: Gladiator leather or light plate for mobility.
  - Skill cards: Endurance and Rampage (or their thematic equivalents).
  - Tactics: Position Gannicus at doorways and chokepoints; use his reroll to ensure a big hit on the first zombie that tries to surprise the party.

- Team Support Loadout
  - Weapon: Spatha or long blade for reach and flex.
  - Armor: Shield heavy if you want blocking power for the team.
  - Skill cards: Rally the Troops and Arena Bond as a focus for synergy.
  - Tactics: Use Arena Bond to trigger extra actions when there is a nearby ally who has attacked this turn. This can feed the team with momentum and allow couple of tag-team takedowns on a tough cluster.

### Playthrough Scenarios: Where Gannicus Shines and What to Watch For

In a standard mission with several doors and long corridors, Gannicus excels at punching through the first wave of zombies and creating space for your ranged survivors. When you combine his Gladiator Roar reroll with a well-timed Rally the Troops, you can convert a dud roll into a decisive blow that leaves the corridor a little less crowded and a little more glorious.

On the other hand, if the board has wide open spaces with a lot of scattered zombies, Gannicus might feel a pinch in terms of mobility. In those cases, you want to shepherd him toward the center anyway, but you must also ensure there is safe peeling room for the rest of the squad to retreat if an overwhelming wave of brutes appears. The key is spacing and timing; think classic arena strategy where you create a kill zone rather than letting the horde collapse the entire party in a single wave.

From a pure mechanical perspective, a Gladiator style survivor should be prioritized in games where the party expects to clear a corridor and hold a line. The reroll gives an edge, but it remains a finite resource you must manage across the mission. You should plan to use the reroll on a key zombie, ideally the first that threatens a hero you care about. ### Strategy and Team Composition Tips

- Put Gannicus at the forefront of the team to soak damage and create space for the rest of the squad.
- Pair him with a ranged survivor who can take advantage of the space he clears to pick off zombies from a distance.
- Use Arena Bond to trigger extra actions at moments when you can chain kills with nearby teammates; this often translates into more actions for your squad to secure objectives or loot.
- Save the Gladiator Roar reroll for clutch moments when a single hit can decide a mission outcome. If you overuse it early, you may find yourself stranded mid-mill with fewer options.
- Don’t forget the visual flair. A well-painted Gannicus is a morale boost that sometimes counts as a strategic advantage because players feel cooler and thus play with more confidence.
