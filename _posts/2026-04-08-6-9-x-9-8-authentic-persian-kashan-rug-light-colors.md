## Maintenance and Care: Keeping the Light Colors Bright
Maintenance is where the practical magic happens. Here are some straightforward habits that keep your Kashan rug looking like a well-preserved piece of history rather than a memory of a spilled latte:

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting. Avoid the beater bars on high power; use the suction head or a high-quality rug rake to lift loose dirt from the surface.
- Rotate every 3–6 months. This prevents uneven wear and preserves the color balance across the field and border motifs.
- Attend to spills quickly. Blot, don’t rub. Use a clean cloth to absorb liquid; if needed, a mild detergent diluted with water can help, but test on a hidden corner first to avoid color surprises.
- Consider professional cleaning every 1–3 years, depending on traffic. Professional cleaning can refresh fibers and even out subtle color shifts that domestic vacuuming can miss.

Stains happen. They’re part of living life with pets, kids, or a couple of night owls who believe the floor is a stage. The goal isn’t perfection but resilience. A well-made Kashan rug will forgive many mishaps if cared for consistently and stored with the attentiveness you’d give a vintage gadget collection.

## How to Verify They’re Real (In Plain Geek, Not Panicked Language)
If you’re a data nerd or a forum lurker who needs a quick authenticity check, here are practical steps you can take:

- Inspect the back: The knots should be visible and consistent. An irregular back often signals lower craftsmanship.
- Feel the pile: Real wool feels warm and sturdy; synthetic blends may feel stiffer or smoother in an uncanny way.
- Check color consistency: Natural dyes age gracefully in Kashan pieces, but you want a uniform distribution across motifs rather than blotchy patches.
- Inquire about origin and knot density: The seller should be able to tell you approximate knot counts per square centimeter and confirm that the rug is hand-knotted rather than machine-produced.
- Seek professional appraisal if you’re in doubt: A reputable appraiser can give you peace of mind about authenticity and value.

## Maintenance Mindset: Long-Term Happiness with Your Kashan
The maintenance plan for this rug is simple, adaptable, and scalable for different lifestyles. If you’re a neat freak, you’ll love the fact that the light colorway responds well to regular care with minimal fuss. If you’re a messy but charming creator who drinks coffee with cinnamon on lazy Saturdays, you’ll appreciate the durability of wool and the forgiving nature of a well-tended pile. The goal is to keep the rug clean and vibrant without over-processing it with harsh cleaners that could degrade the pile or alter the color balance.

- Vacuum regularly with a brushless attachment to protect fibers. That old vacuum with a beater bar is great for shredding papers, not rug fibers.
- Treat spills promptly and gently. A dab of mild soap and water on a white cloth is usually enough for most liquids. Always test a small area first.
- Rotate to distribute wear and sun exposure evenly. This keeps color distribution balanced and reduces the risk of visible fade lines.
- Consider professional cleaning when needed. This preserves the fibers and color depth, especially for larger rooms or high-traffic areas.
