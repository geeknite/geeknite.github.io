## How to Play: The Quick Version

Herd Mentality is built around the premise that the best moments happen when we assume others will think a certain way and then realize we are all thinking in the same ridiculous direction. Each round features a prompt that asks for a quick, often humorous response. Everyone writes or selects their answer on a card or token. The twist is that you want to align with the majority, not stand out as the maverick who refuses to be sheepish. If your answer matches the majority, you score; if you are in the minority, you still gain something in some variants, but often the reward is not the same as those who match.

### Round Structure
1. A prompt is revealed. Examples might include prompts about opinions, common experiences, or wildly imaginative scenarios. 
2. Each player secretly chooses an answer that represents what they think the majority would say. Some prompts invite obvious choices, others reward counter opinions.
3. All answers are revealed simultaneously. Players compare their answer with the majority and score accordingly.
4. The round ends and the next prompt is drawn. The first player to reach the target score wins the game. 

The pacing can be brisk or relaxed depending on the group. The real joy comes from the moment when a player nails the exact majority and everyone suddenly realizes they were wrong to doubt their own instincts. It feels a bit like watching a herd of synchronized swimmers dive into the pool as you hear the chorus click into place.

## Strategies: How to Maximize Your Laughs and Your Points

While Herd Mentality rewards reading the room, there are a few tactics you can use without destroying the spirit of the game. Here are some practical tips that have worked well in our sessions:

- Listen first, announce later. The moment a prompt is revealed, take a breath and listen to what others are likely to say. A quick assessment can help you decide whether to blend in or intentionally diverge for a potential misdirection.
- Play to the crowd you want. If you are seated with players who love pop culture references, lean into popular opinions or trending jokes. If your group enjoys nostalgia, you can mine ideas from past experiences to align with the herd.
- Don’t overthink the obvious. Sometimes the majority is exactly what you would expect, and blasting a wildly unexpected answer can backfire if the group is not ready for it. Balance is key.
- Use humor as a tool. The best moments in Herd Mentality come when a perfectly timed quip triggers a cascade of agreement or laughter. If you can deliver a joke that resonates with the room, you can ride the wave to the top of the scoreboard.
- Watch the non-verbals. There is a subtle art to predicting a majority that goes beyond the words. A smirk, a glance, or a hesitation can all signal readiness to fall in line or to push the envelope.

## Tuning Your Experience: Variants and House Rules

If you are a power user who wants to push Herd Mentality to the edge, a few light variants can stretch the game without breaking the core vibe. Consider trying:

- Wordy prompts variant: Replace some prompts with longer prompts that allow players to write mini stories. This adds a storytelling layer that can yield epic results and even more unpredictable majority outcomes.
- Reverse mode: In some rounds, require players to guess the minority answer. This flip can create surprising shifts in strategy and bring a fresh twist to the party.
- Team mode: Pair players into teams and require team consensus. Team mode is fantastic for larger groups and it encourages collaboration and communication outside of the usual solo play.
- Prompt curation session: Allow players to veto overly spicy prompts before the game starts. This ensures everyone stays comfortable while still delivering maximum laughs.
