## How to Play UNO H2O
If you already know UNO, you know the gist. If not, here’s a quick refresher tailored to the H2O edition:

- Each player starts with seven cards. The remainder becomes a draw pile.
- Match the top card on the discard pile by color or number. Special cards add twists: Skip means the next player loses a turn, Reverse flips the play order, Draw Two forces the next player to pick two cards, Wild lets you choose a color, and Wild Draw Four makes the next player draw four (and you declare the new color).
- In UNO H2O, the water-resistant deck adds resilience to spills and quick pool-side games but doesn’t alter fundamental rules. The aim is still to shed all cards first.
- When a player has one card left, they must shout Uno. This edition keeps the same call, so your family’s giggles and dramatic “Uno!” moments remain intact.

Strategically, you still want to preserve your color sets, manage your Wild cards for late-game reversals, and time your Draw Two/D Wild Draw Four plays for maximum chaos. The water-friendly aspect slightly reduces the sting of accidental wetness in a splash zone, but if someone decides to fling a water bottle in celebration, you’ll still have to deal with a drenched draw pile. So yes, UNO H2O offers fun risk, and also a reminder to keep a towel nearby. 

### Rules Variants and House Rules
Okay, you’re not here for a boring read of the standard rules, right? Let’s spice it up Geeknite style. Here are a few house rules you can try at your next pool party or kitchen table tournament:

- Wet Draw: When a Draw Two or Draw Four is played, the next player must splash a small amount of water on their wrists and then reveal their two or four cards. It’s mostly ceremonial, but it adds a giggle or two.
- Rescue Uno: If someone shouts Uno and forgets to call it, a player can shout Rescue Uno and force them to draw one extra card. This is mostly for dramatic effect and to remind players to pay attention after a few rounds.
- Color Splash: On a Wild card, the color chosen must be the color of the player to the left. This can cause surprising shifts and lead to tense, hilarious moments as players scramble to adapt.
- Waterproof Stance: If the table is damp from splashes, performing a quick towel wipe is mandatory before the next draw; otherwise, penalties apply to the offender — usually in the form of a dramatic eye-roll and a playful taunt.
