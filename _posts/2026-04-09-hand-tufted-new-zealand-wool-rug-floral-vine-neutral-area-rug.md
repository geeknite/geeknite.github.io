## Materials, Craftsmanship, and Sustainability
### Hand tufted NZ wool
New Zealand wool is the backbone here. It is known for its elasticity, resilience, and natural stain resistance. The rug is hand tufted by skilled artisans, which means your floor gets a touch of human magic and a story to tell guests who ask where your furniture came from.

### Backing and construction
The base is a sturdy, stable foundation designed to handle daily traffic. The tufting technique creates a dense pile that feels solid underfoot and resists crushing over time. A well-made hand tufted rug can maintain its loft for years, as long as you give it the care it deserves.

### Sustainability and dyes
NZ wool is a renewable resource; the dyeing process for this rug uses low-impact dyes that minimize environmental impact while preserving color fastness. The net effect is a rug that looks great and carries a lighter footprint than a night of gaming marathons with snacks.

### Maintenance basics
Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting or a vacuum with a gentle beater bar turned off. Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. For more stubborn stains, consider a mild wool-safe cleanser on a small, inconspicuous area first. Avoid saturating the rug with water, as wool is hygroscopic and can take on moisture like a sponge.

## How to Buy and Care Tips
### Where to buy
You can find this rug from reputable retailers who specialize in natural fiber and handmade pieces. When shopping, look for certifications related to sustainable wool sourcing and confirm that the rug is indeed hand tufted and not machine stitched to resemble handwork.

### Care tips from the experts
- Rotate every 6-12 months
- Use a rug pad to prevent sliding and reduce wear on the fibers
- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting
- Address stains promptly with wool-safe cleaners
- Keep away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade over time

### Shipping and returns
Check shipping policies and return windows. Wool rugs can be heavy, so factor in shipping costs and the possibility of needing assistance for delivery. A thorough inspection on arrival is always a good idea.
