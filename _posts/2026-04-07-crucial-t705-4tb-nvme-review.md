## Compatibility: Will It Play Nice with Your System?
One of the main practical questions for any NVMe upgrade is compatibility. The M.2 2280 form factor is one of the most widely supported configurations across motherboards and laptops released in the past several years. The Crucial T705 is designed to slot into PCIe 4.0 or PCIe 3.0 environments, depending on motherboard support, with PCIe Gen4x4 delivering the best performance when possible.

Before you buy, check:
- Your motherboard supports M.2 2280 NVMe drives and PCIe Gen4 or Gen3 as appropriate.
- You have enough free PCIe lanes and a spare slot on a motherboard that doesn’t share bandwidth with graphics or other devices.
- Sufficient cooling in your chassis, especially if you plan on sustained heavy workloads.

For laptops, ensure that your machine’s BIOS/UEFI recognizes NVMe drives and that there’s no quirky BIOS setting preventing high-speed NVMe operation. In most modern laptops, the T705 will be a drop-in upgrade that makes bootable OS drives even more zippy, with the added bonus of 4 TB of space to host your OS, apps, and data.
