### Durability: When the Going Gets Woolly
If you have pets or kids, you’ll want to know how the rug holds up. Wool fibers resist wear well, and the overall density of the Illusion Author helps resist flattening under normal household use. High-traffic zones will show some wear over time, but with proper care, you’ll likely see this rug maintain its character for years. You may notice a degree of character-building shedding during initial months—this is normal for wool as it settles in. Regular grooming with a soft rug brush will help: a quick pass or two a week keeps the pile fluffy and the fibers aligned.

### Care and Cleaning: Keeping a Wool Rug Clean Without Tempting a DIY Dynamo
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting and a suction head that won’t pull threads. Avoid beater bars that attack the pile head-on.
- For spots, treat as you would with natural fiber textiles: blot, don’t rub, and use a mild detergent solution if needed. Quick action beats a stubborn stain, especially if coffee, red wine, or dramatic plot twists are involved.
- For deeper cleans, consider professional wool rug cleaning every 12-24 months depending on traffic. It’s worth it to preserve fullness and color depth.
- Rotate occasionally if your room has one window that bathes the rug in sun or a heavy piece of furniture that always sits in the same spot. This keeps colors even and avoids sun-bleed drama.

### Color Stability and Dyes
Wool dyes hold well, but color consistency depends on the dye lot and the room’s lighting. If you’re matching to a very specific palette, order samples if available, or hold a swatch next to your furniture leg to confirm you’re not accidentally selecting a shade that screams “cozy librarian chic” instead of “tech-forward bibliophile.”

If you’re leaning toward custom sizing (and you know you want to nail those exact inches), here’s a high-level outline of what to expect in the ordering journey:
