## Drafting with 24 Packs: Logistics and Real-World Realities
### How to run a 24-pack draft night without turning your kitchen into a mana-fueled disaster
- Seating and table arrangement: With 24 packs, you want a comfortable seating layout that allows players to view the cards clearly and maintain a sensible line of sight for drafting. A long table or several smaller tables arranged in a U-shape works well for multi-player formats.
- Draft order and distribution: A standard draft uses a clockwise pick order. With 24 boosters, you can run multiple rounds or a longer multi-round draft that rewards strategic picks and careful color-pie balancing. Assigning seats randomly helps keep the table lively and reduces the risk of collusion during late rounds.
- Time management: Plan for at least 2–3 hours for a brisk three-round draft, including deck-building and sideboard tuning. For a more relaxed evening with friends, you can stretch to 4–5 hours. The key is to keep the pace lively enough that players don’t get bored but slow enough that drafting decisions actually matter.
- Space for side events: If you crave more than just the main draft, a couple of side events like Archenemy style games or a minor sealed event using the remaining boosters can add extra value and laughter to the night.
