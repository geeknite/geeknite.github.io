## Blade Steel: 1085HC, What It Means to Your Edge
1085 stainless? Not quite. 1085HC is a high-carbon tool steel with a carbon content that makes it easy to sharpen and capable of taking a very sharp edge. It won’t resist corrosion like stainless steels—because let’s face it, carbon steels have that romance with the kitchen’s acidic, pepper-laden air—but with proper care, it behaves. The “HC” tag suggests a heat treatment regime that emphasizes edge retention and toughness, which is essential for a 12-inch blade that’s likely to meet choppers, bone, and stubborn protiens head-on. The steel’s composition supports a strong cutting edge, but it can show signs of wear or rust if you neglect oiling and drying, especially after juicy, acidic meals.

In practice, you’ll notice:
- Easy sharpening: A quick pass with oilstone or ceramic stone brings back a razor edge without requiring a lab-grade setup.
- Edge retention: It’s good enough to handle repeated slicing tasks in a home kitchen or a campsite without needing daily re-sharpening.
- Toughness: Relatively forgiving if you misjudge a chop or encounter a bone; 1085HC tolerates a bit of abuse better than many premium stainless steels.

Care tips:
- Keep the blade dry after use; wipe and oil lightly to prevent rusting.
- Use a proper sharpening angle, around 15-20 degrees per side, depending on your preference and technique.
- Avoid prolonged exposure to moisture or salt if you’re cooking near a briny sea breeze or a pool of pickle brine.

### Care and Maintenance When You’re Not Dreaming of Upgrade Upgrades
Let’s be honest: carbon steels require some TLC. The Honshu 1849 benefits from a routine that keeps rust from creeping into your cutting edge and your mood. Here’s a practical maintenance plan:
- After use: wipe clean and thoroughly dry the blade. Moisture is the enemy of carbon steel, except for the slight romance you feel when you’re sharpening it again.
- Oil the blade lightly with food-safe mineral oil or camellia oil. This protects against moisture and keeps your edge from drying out.
- Store in a dry environment; avoid humid climates without protective wrapping or the leather sheath that seals moisture in a controlled environment.
- Sharpen on a good stone in a consistent angle (around 15-20 degrees per side for most users). Start with a coarse stone if you’re restoring a dull edge, then move to finer stones to bring the edge to gleaming glory.
