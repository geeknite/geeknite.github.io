## The sealed box reality and authenticity checks

Buying a sealed Kickstarter exclusive is a bit like buying a time capsule from the future. There’s delight, but there’s also the practical need to verify that what you paid for is, indeed, new and untouched. Here are some sanity-check tips if you’re in the market for one of these:

- Examine the shrink wrap: a clean, uniformly tight wrap with no tears or obvious resealing marks is a good sign.
- Check the seal date and production batch labels if available. A tiny printed code on the seal is the kind of detail nerds notice and collectors discuss in fevered forum threads.
- Inspect the box edges for crushing or denting. A few minor scuffs are not the end of the world, but severe damage may hint at a rough journey.
- Compare the seller photos with standard product photography from the official page. Discrepancies in color or font can be a red flag.
- If possible, request close-ups of the interior contents before purchase. A fully sealed inner tray is a strong sign that nothing has been disturbed.

If you’re buying used or from a reseller, it’s wise to temper expectations. A sealed box carries a premium; once opened, its value can crater depending on the condition of the contents and the packaging. The sealed status is the real differentiator here, and you should price accordingly.
