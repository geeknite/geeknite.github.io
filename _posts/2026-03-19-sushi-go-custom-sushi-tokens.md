## What are Custom Sushi Tokens?

### Token Design

These tokens replace some of the fixed delivery cards from the base game with modular tokens that represent orders, sets, and combos. Think of it as Sushi Go! getting a few extra toppings and not apologizing for it. The tokens are beautiful, with bright colors and cute motifs, so you won’t forget which token does what—the same way you never forget the face of a rainbow nigiri after a night of too much wasabi.

### Scoring Changes

The tokens tweak scoring in two ways: (1) new token-based bonuses for completing certain sets, and (2) alternative scoring tracks for late-game drama. The aim is to maintain the frantic speed while adding a layer of deduction similar to a treasure hunt in a sci-fi anime.

### Implementing the Tokens: A Quick Setup Guide

- Shuffle tokens with the standard deck (the tokens are usually small cardboard disks with illustration).
- Determine which tokens will be active for this game session (you can do a quick 2-3 token setup for quick rounds or a full 5-6 for marathon sessions).
- Place a token draw pile at the center, or mix tokens into the card draw pile if you’re feeling extra fancy.

## Gameplay with Tokens

### The Flow

The core mechanic remains speed-drafting; you still pick one card and pass the rest, but now you also choose whether to claim a token that will interact with the scoring at the end of the round. The added decisions are light years more spicy than a wasabi dab.

### Strategy: When to Chase Tokens

- Early game: Acquire tokens that enable you to pivot into a flexible strategy without losing tempo.
- Mid game: Start planning for a token-heavy mid-round: a little more calculation, a little less accidently discarding a good card.
- End game: Tokens that grant bonus points for combo completion become your obsession, your obsession that is both legal and delicious.

### Token Design in Practice

The tokens themselves are visually delightful. They look like they could slot into a candy vending machine: glossy, compact, and with enough personality to spark a few extra giggles at the table. The tactile feel of turning a token over to reveal its effect adds a small layer of ritual to what would otherwise be a mechanical sprint.

## House Rules and Balancing

No two groups are alike, and no two Sushis go alike, either. Tokens are adjustable, but with that comes balancing complexity. Here are some practical guidelines:

- Start light: Use 2-3 tokens, then add more once everyone is comfortable.
- Power check: Avoid tokens that create dominant strategies; the good tokens should slightly skew probability, not break the game.
- Accessibility: If someone at the table has trouble with reading small text on tokens, consider bigger tokens or color-coded symbols.

### Common Pitfalls

- Token overkill: Too many tokens can bog down the game into a 90-minute presentation about sushi law and token physics.
- Misinterpretation: Keep token rules simple; if a token’s effect requires an entire playset of rules, you’re doing it wrong.

## Sample Token Concepts

- Rainbow Roll Token: At end of round, you earn 2 points for each color sushi card you collected that round (excluding duplicates beyond the color set). This encourages diversification rather than a single powerhouse color ramp.
- Chopstick Shuffle: Once per game, you may swap one card drawn with another card in your hand, representing a chopstick-assisted steal. It comes with a small risk: you must discard one card you were planning to keep.
- Maki Master: Extra points if you have more maki tokens than all opponents combined. The catch: those tokens only count if you drafted them early and didn’t squander them on a questionable sashimi plan.
- Nigiri Bonanza: If you collect a set containing at least three nigiri tokens, you get an additional 3 points. It’s a small but satisfying reward for going all-in on nigiri strategy.
- Sweet Sake: A risk token that lets you draw an extra card, but you must give up a point or two at the end if you fail to cash in on its potential. This is flavor as a moral victory: sometimes you win the round, sometimes you win bragging rights for that and that smile.
- Sashimi Slipstream: If you manage to collect the sashimi set across two rounds, you earn a bonus token that can be used to redraw one of your rounds completely. This one is the edge-of-seat moment that makes players sigh in relief and then laugh at their own audacity.

> The token list above is illustrative. Your group can customize or substitute tokens based on play style, age range, or the stubbornness of the game night cook.

## Implementation Details: Token Production Quality

Token production quality matters. The most annoying thing about token variants is when the tokens fall apart after two uses, or when their weight feels like you’re holding a heavy nickel. A good token set uses sturdy cardstock, rounded edges, and clear icons. If you’re supporting a family game night, you’ll want tokens that are easy to read from across the table and that don’t require a magnifying glass to decipher. Aesthetically, tokens should feel like a natural extension of Sushi Go!’s bright world, not an alien invasion of cardboard. This edition generally nails that balance: durable tokens, clear printing, and color-coding that matches with card colors. If you’re worried about storage, look for a small token tin or a zip bag labeled sushi bits to keep everything organized between sessions.
