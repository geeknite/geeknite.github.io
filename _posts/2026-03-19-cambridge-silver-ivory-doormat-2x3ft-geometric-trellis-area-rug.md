## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Pattern Sharp

Keeping a geometric pattern looking fresh isn’t rocket science, but there’s a method to the madness.

- Regular vacuuming: Use a vacuum attachment suitable for low-pile rugs. A quick weekly pass keeps dirt from embedding in the fibers and turning your foyer into a confessional of dust.
- Spot-clean stains: For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth. Avoid harsh chemicals that might strip color; mild detergents and water usually do the trick. If you’re dealing with something stubborn, test a small area first.
- Rotation: Occasionally rotate the mat if your entryway experiences uneven sun exposure or traffic patterns. It’s like giving your rug a tiny spa day so it doesn’t develop a “where did my form go?” vibe.
- Drying: If you ever get the doormat soaked, prop it up to dry in a well-ventilated area. Don’t shove it into a cramped closet or dryer—these fabrics don’t enjoy the hot romance with heat you might expect from other home goods.

The result is a rug that stays visually consistent for longer. Beige-to-silver gradients can mislead you into believing it’s a different color in morning light, so check it under multiple light conditions to truly appreciate its color story.
