## Thematic and aesthetic notes

### The vibe of the box

Zombicide Black Plague lives in that sweet spot between grim dark medieval fantasy and campy zombie apocalypse. ### Miniatures and paintability

The heroes offer distinct silhouettes, which makes painting easier for beginners yet satisfying for veterans who enjoy the challenge of shading leather, rusted metal, and blood-streaked cloth. The sculpts tend to favor clean lines and bold patches of color, making them excellent entry points for players who have only recently discovered the joy of painting. If you’re not into painting, you can still appreciate the miniatures as display pieces on a shelf, though their true value comes when you realize they can be used for actual gameplay with unique abilities.

### Tokens, boards, and surface usability

Tokens are usually well laid out with intuitive icons. They’re designed to be easy to grasp during a heated moment when you’re trying to remember if your hero can open a locked door or if you need to use a special action to withstand a monstrous attack. The boards tend to be thick and sturdy, and you’ll often get extra map tiles and encounter cards that keep your campaigns fresh and unpredictable. The balance of readability and density is important here: too many tokens and the table looks like a war room; too few and the rules become a labyrinth of clutter.
