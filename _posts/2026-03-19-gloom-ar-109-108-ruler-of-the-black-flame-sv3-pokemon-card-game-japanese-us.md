## Artwork analysis: the Ruler, the Flame, and the Moraine of Smoke
The AR treatment often amplifies the aura around a creature, and this Gloom is no exception. The central figure sits on a throne formed not of wood or ivory but of shadow and ember. The flame—the “black flame” in the title—has a peculiar hue: it’s not the bright orange of a campfire nor the purple-blue of a magical glyph, but a charcoal-lavender blend that communicates both mystery and danger. The flames don’t so much illuminate the room as insinuate themselves into the corners of the frame, curling along the edge of the throne like mischievous tendrils that know something the viewer doesn’t.

The Gloom itself has that classic, slightly sour expression that makes you wonder if it’s judging your deck-building choices or perhaps your snack choices at the table. The color palette is intentionally saturated: deep greens, smoky blacks, and a grayed-out background that makes Gloom pop like a neon sign in a dark alley of the Pokémon world. There’s a deliberate contrast between the organic shape of Gloom’s bulbous head and the harsh geometry of the throne—an juxtaposition that suggests a being who is both ancient and curiously modern in its taste for drama.

From an artistic standpoint, this AR hits a lot of the right notes for collectors who value storytelling in a card’s art. The scene implies a narrative: Gloom as ruler, flame as power, and the surrounding environment as a stage for a drama you can only guess at. You don’t just collect the card for the numbers; you collect it for the scene, the vibe, and the meme potential when you pull it during a casual Friday night with friends.

That said, if your priority is top-tier tournament viability, you’ll want to pair this AR with other synergistic SV3 cards that maximize its potential in your chosen archetype rather than relying on it in isolation.

Storage and care tips: protect the foil and color with high-quality sleeves, keep away from heat and moisture, and store in a cool, dry binder or deck box. ## Practical buying guide: where to find this card and how to verify authenticity
If you’re chasing this Gloom AR as a centerpiece or a sentimental favorite, here are practical tips to ensure you’re buying a genuine copy at a fair price:
- Check the holo quality: authentic ARs shine with consistent foil that catches light without dull patches across the card. If you see heavy edge whitening or missing holo, walk away.
- Examine the text legibility: print differences in US vs Japanese versions affect readability. If you can, inspect in person or request high-resolution photos before purchase.
- Compare sleeve fit and binder spots: AR cards can be slightly thicker due to foil layering. Ensure your chosen sleeves accommodate AR thickness without warping.
- Consider sealed product vs individual card: booster boxes and individual packs offer different odds for ARs. If you’re chasing a specific print (Japanese or US), factor that into price expectations.
