## Connectivity and power

One of the key talking points with portable monitors is how you actually power and drive them. The 16MR70 leans heavily on USB-C as the one-port solution, a trend that keeps things simple and reduces cable clutter. What you want in practice is the ability to run the monitor off the Gram’s USB-C PD while also using the same server-grade USB-C cable to drive the display and the laptop. The 16MR70 typically supports USB-C DisplayPort Alt Mode, which means your Gram can push video and data through the same cable your laptop already uses for charging. If you’re in a pinch and your Gram isn’t being charged by a PD-capable source, you can use an external charger to power the monitor while your laptop sits on a separate charger. It’s not glamorous, but it’s elegant in its minimalism.

The real-world trick here is not just about being able to attach the monitor, but about the simplicity of operation: connect, extend, arrange, and you’re done. No drivers, no convoluted software that tries to be your new best friend, just a straightforward extension of your workflow. And yes, you can drag apps between the Gram’s screen and the Plus View with the same familiarity you apply to window snapping on a desktop PC.

### Setup tips for a smooth start

- Start with the Gram in its standard laptop mode, then connect the Plus View via USB-C.
- In Windows (or your preferred OS), set the display mode to Extend. Duplicate mode is for the moment when you want a mirrored workspace, but Extend unlocks the real potential.
- Calibrate brightness for your typical environment. The afternoon cafe lighting and a dim hotel room require different brightness levels; you’ll find a sweet spot after a couple of days of usage.
- Use the stand on a stable surface. The stand helps keep the monitor at eye level, reducing neck strain and the occasional posture complaint from your future self.
- If you’re on a flight or train, consider using a lightweight angle booster or a book as a prop behind the monitor to improve stability and viewing comfort.
