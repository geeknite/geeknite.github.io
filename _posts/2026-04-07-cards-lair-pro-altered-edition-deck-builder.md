## Altered Edition — the twist that keeps you honest and mildly unsettled
### The philosophy behind altered constraints
Altered Edition intentionally nudges you away from the most naive version of deck design. You can set constraints like max copies per card, mandatory inclusion of at least one card from a specific rarity, or a mandatory synergy line that forces you to pick a specific chain of combos. The effect is that you must think about your deck in a new way, not just as a collection of powerful cards but as a living, breathing system with emergent behavior.

### Practical experiments
We tried a few experiments to see how the Altered Edition handles standard archetypes vs chaotic constraints.
- Archetype A under vanilla rules performed as expected, with good tempo and a robust late game.
- Archetype A under a random constraint ended up with some surprising mid game swings and a few cards you would swear are from a completely different game. It was chaotic but incredibly entertaining.
- A synergy heavy build with a constraint to include at least three cards with shared flavor text produced delightful combos that made the group lean in with grins and questionable confidence.

### Pros and cons of the Altered Edition mode
Pros:
- Encourages creative deck design and experimentation
- Helps break dependence on try-hard optimal builds
- Provides a natural guardrail against stale metagame repetition
Cons:
- Can feel punishing if you are chasing a perfectly tuned deck
- The random modifier can misalign with your planned theme, leading to a few sighs and a lot of giggles

## Best Practices and Pitfalls to Watch For
### Do this
- Start with a theme and collect a handful of cards that match it. The modifiers will push you toward interesting choices and the process becomes a creative exercise rather than a scavenger hunt for optimal cards.
- Use deck codes to compare iterations with friends. You will discover that the same deck can feel utterly different under several altered constraints.
- Keep a notes file. A few lines per session about what worked and what flopped can become a personal playbook that saves you time in future nights.

### Dont do this
- Do not chase a single perfect build. The Altered Edition is designed for exploration rather than repetition, so you will miss the point if you insist on perfection.
- Do not take the constraints too seriously. The fun is in the surprise and the laughing at the odd combos that emerge from the modifier.
