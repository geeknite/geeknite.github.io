## Setup, Maintenance, and Longevity
Maintenance on a foosball table is rarely glamorous, but it pays dividends in reliability. Regular checks should include tightening any loose screws, ensuring leg levelers stay stable, and paying attention to the ball return path. A misaligned ball return can lead to balls ping-ponging around the cabinet, which may cause you to believe your table has developed a personality. Clean the rods and bearings periodically with a dry cloth to remove dust, and consider a quick silicone spray if you notice a squeak that won’t quiet down. Avoid harsh solvents that can degrade plastics and laminates.

Longevity is a function of usage, environment, and a bit of luck. In a well-kept room with standard temperature and humidity, a good 47.2x24 inch foosball table can remain serviceable for several seasons of casual play. If you plan to use it in a high-traffic setting like a rec room, you’ll want to inspect the wear on the players and the cabinet more frequently. A minor investment in maintenance now pays off with fewer major repairs later.
