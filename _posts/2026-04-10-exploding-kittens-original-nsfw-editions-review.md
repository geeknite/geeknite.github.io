## How to Play: a quick-start guide
If you’ve played any party game with a “draw or play” structure, you’ve basically got this. Exploding Kittens is a push-your-luck, battle-card-dodge of a game. The core loop is simple: draw cards, play Defuse cards when you’re lucky (or not), try not to explode, and sabotage your friends while you’re at it.

- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a few Defuse cards to each player, and place the Exploding Kitten card face-down into the pile. The exact number of Defuse cards you start with can vary by house rules, but most groups begin with one to two. The rest of the Defuse cards mix back into the draw pile.
- Turn flow: On your turn, you may play cards to defuse, steal, or skip the inevitable exploding Kitten fate. If you draw the Exploding Kitten, you must use a Defuse card to survive; otherwise, you’re out of the game. The mood shifts from strategic derring-do to “oh snap, I actually pulled that off.”
- Endgame: The last player standing wins. It’s silly, it’s brutal, and it’s a little barbaric in the most charming way.

The game’s pacing is the secret sauce. It’s a quick session, often wrapping up in 15–20 minutes. That makes Exploding Kittens perfect for a quick break between more crunch-heavy titles or as a palate-cleanser after a long gaming marathon. And yes, the NSFW edition changes the ambiance significantly—think more raunchy humor, more adult references, and a few cards that will either make you groan lovingly or turn faintly red depending on your audience.

### A note on player count and group dynamics
The ideal player count is around 4–6, though you’ll see it work with as few as 2 or as many as 7 or more if your group thrives on chaos. The more players, the less predictability you’ll get, which typically translates to more laughs and more dramatic “no, seriously” moments when someone draws an Exploding Kitten in the final stretch. If you’re hosting family-friendly game night with younger audiences, the Original Edition is the safer bet. If you’re hosting a after-hours party, the NSFW Edition can be a surprisingly good fit—just set expectations and boundaries beforehand.

## Strategy tips: maximizing fun without burning friendships
- Don’t overthink Defuse cards. They’re not a guaranteed shield; treat them as precious safety nets that buy time to improvise a better reveal of your genius.
- Use “attack” or “steal” cards to create dramatic turns. The goal isn’t merely survival; it’s to turn the table into a mini soap opera of betrayal and backstabbing… with cats.
- Read the room. If your group values humor over precision, lean into the banter and play the risk. If you’re playing with newcomers, explain the rules clearly and avoid heavy rule-lawyering to keep the vibe joyful.
- For NSFW players, early boundary-setting helps. Have a quick chat about what’s off-limits and what’s fair game. The best nights come from shared laughter that doesn’t cross lines.
- Consider rotating hosts. A fresh card-slinging energy can reinvigorate the game night, letting different players practice their dramatic timing.
