Maintenance and Care
---------------------
A table this sturdy still benefits from a regular tune-up. Here are practical maintenance habits:

- Cleaning: Wipe the surface and rods with a soft microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints and dust. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the laminate finish.
- Lubrication: Light lubrication on the rod joints can keep the playing action silky. Use a product recommended for metal-on-plastic interfaces, and apply sparingly. Excess oil on the rods can attract dust and degrade performance over time.
- Tightening: Periodically check screws and leg bolts. A loose leg can ruin your alignment and your mood during a championship run.
- Storage and environment: Keep the table in a dry area. Extreme humidity or heat can warp panels or effect the smoothness of ball roll. If you have a game-night that evolves into a full party, the non-slip feet will help keep things stable even when wine is being spilled and teammates are celebrating prematurely.

From a tech-nerd perspective, you might treat the foosball table as a low-stakes micro-assembly project. It’s not a rocket, but it rewards careful maintenance with long-term reliability and fewer pop-quits mid-game.

Play Styles and Tips
----------------------
Foosball is as much a mental game as a physical one. Here are tips to get more from your matches:
- Learn basic shots: pull shots, push shots, and bank shots that leverage the walls. These moves become easier with good rod control and consistent ball spin.
- Positioning matters: Don’t chase the ball. Maintain a balanced stance, keep your hands ready, and anticipate passes across the field.
- Defense matters: Foosball rewards a solid defense. A quick transition from defense to offense can swing momentum in a hurry.
- Communication with teammates helps: If you’re playing in teams, call out intended passes and defensive moves to coordinate. It’s not just about who has the ball; it’s about how you navigate routes to the goal.
