## Strategy Tips and Tricks
- Focus on early opportunities: Since the game revolves around completing phases, identify which phases you’re most likely to complete soon once you draw a good hand. If you see two potential runs or sets early, you might want to prioritize a quick early finish rather than chasing a more valuable phase that requires a lot of card rearrangement.
- Card sequencing matters: In Phase 10 GO!, the order in which you place sets and runs can influence what your opponents see. Try to mask your intentions by maintaining variety; don’t telegraph your next move with obvious patterns.
- Keep an eye on the discard pile: The top card on the discard pile can be a game-deciding signal. If someone is chasing a quick run, discarding a card they’ve been anxious to pick up can slow them down—at the cost of denying you a potential upgrade to your own hand.
- Adaptability is a superpower: The pocket format rewards you for flexible planning. If your plan A is blocked by a lucky draw for an opponent, pivot quickly to plan B or C. The decks are small, but your brain should feel bigger.
- Practice makes efficient play: The first few rounds are learning rounds. After you’ve seen a couple of turns, you’ll be able to optimize your draws and plan your phases with a confidence that feels suspiciously like real gamer energy.
