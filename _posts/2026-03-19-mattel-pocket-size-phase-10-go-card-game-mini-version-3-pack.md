## Strategy Tips and Tricks
- Focus on early opportunities: since completing phases is the goal, identify which phases you can likely finish soon once you draw a decent hand. If you spot two potential runs or sets early, prioritize a quick finish rather than chasing a far off arc that requires heavy card rearrangement.
- Card sequencing matters: the order you place sets and runs can influence what your opponents see. Maintain variety and avoid telegraphing your next move with obvious patterns.
- Watch the discard pile: the top discard can signal what others are after. If someone is chasing a quick run, discarding a card they need can slow them down but might hurt your own odds too.
- Adaptability is a superpower: pocket format rewards flexible planning. If plan A is blocked by a lucky draw for an opponent, pivot quickly to plan B or C. The decks are small, but your brain can feel bigger.
- Practice makes efficient play: the first rounds are learning rounds. After a couple turns you’ll tune your draws and plan your phases with a confidence that feels suspiciously gamer-y.
- Use social cues to your advantage: pocket play invites a little drama. A well timed grin or a sly nudge can shift perceived priorities at the table and buy you a turn or two.
