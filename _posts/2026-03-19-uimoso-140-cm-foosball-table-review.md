## Maintenance, care, and longevity
Longevity is the invisible feature you notice only after you use the table for a while. Here are practical tips that help you keep the Uimoso in top shape:
- Clean the playfield regularly with a soft microfiber to reduce lint and dust that can affect ball speed.
- Lubricate the rods sparingly with a silicone spray or the lubricant recommended in the manual. Do not overdo it; a slick rod is not always a better rod.
- Keep the balls clean; dirty balls lead to unpredictable bounces and occasional misread plays.
- Tighten screws periodically, especially after games where you discover your inner goalie and go for dramatic dives. It is not necessary to reassemble every week, but a quick check is smart after heavy use.
- Use the levelers to ensure even play on imperfect floors. An uneven table turns a casual game into a bounce house experience and not in a good way.

From a durability standpoint, the cabinet holds up well to regular sessions and a few friendly shoves when players celebrate a goal with a little too much enthusiasm. The metal rods hold their shape, and the finish on the cabinet shows minimal signs of wear after a month of use. If you treat the table with care and avoid dropping heavy items onto the playing surface, you should get years of family tournaments out of it.

## Playstyle tips for the smooth operator in you
- Start with a simple drill: one-two passes along the back lines followed by a quick shot. This builds confidence and reduces stray passes that give your opponent an easy counterattack.
- Use the counterbalanced players to your advantage in defense. Keep your wrists steady and avoid frantic flicks that waste energy.
- Alternate between offense and defense every few games to avoid monotony. A little switch can refresh your approach and help you discover new strategies.
- Adjust the levelers to optimize ball bounce. A slightly higher or lower table changes how the ball reacts on contact with rails and can influence your shot selection.
- Keep the balls clean and ensure you have extras around during longer sessions. Nothing kills the vibe faster than a stubborn ball that refuses to bounce where you expect it.
