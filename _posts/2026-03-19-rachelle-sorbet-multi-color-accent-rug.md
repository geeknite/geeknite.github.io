## Performance and Maintenance: It Looks Great and Handles Life Like a Champion
### Stain resistance and cleaning routine
The rug’s dye profile suggests decent stain resistance for day-to-day spills (coffee, juice, accidental glitter glue mishaps from a late-night study session). Quick blotting is recommended for most mishaps; avoid harsh scrubs that could fray the color blocks. For tougher stains, mild detergent with cold water often does the trick, followed by air drying. A gentle vacuum on a low setting keeps the pile looking even, avoiding the dreaded treadmill effect that happens when you vacuum too aggressively in the same spot for hours. Pro-tip: rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and tear and to keep those color blocks from becoming a memory of the first week you owned it.
### Durability for daily chaos
If your space is a hub of activity—kids, pets, and the occasional impromptu yoga session—the Sorbet rug should hold up relatively well. The fibers aren’t ridiculously delicate, and the color zoning helps camouflage small stains in a way that hides fingerprints from your inner scientist who keeps touching the fabric with a white lab glove just because. It’s not invincible, but it’s more forgiving than a couch that’s seen one too many couch cushions turned inside out for a scavenger hunt.
