## How to play (the quick mental model for a sealed, fresh-from-the-warehouse experience)
If you’ve played CODENAMES before, you’re dusting off known chords; if you’re new, you’re about to learn a surprisingly lean system that supports big group laughter with minimal friction. The goal is simple in theory: one team attempts to locate all their agents on the grid using one-word clues, while the other team tries to do the same. The assassin card adds a delicious risk factor to every clue. Here’s the elegant lean-down version:

- There is a grid of word cards visible to all players.
- Two teams (red and blue) compete to identify all their agents first.
- A spymaster gives one-word clues with a number indicating how many cards on the grid relate to that clue.
- Teammates discuss and pick cards they believe match the clue, hoping to avoid the assassin and the opposing team’s agents.
- The first team to clear their agents wins; hitting the assassin card ends the round and likely hurts the leading team, emotionally or strategically.

In the Czech edition, the words are translated with care to preserve the exact cadence that makes or breaks the clue. The gatekeeping for “too risky a clue” remains, which is the engine that drives the delightful tension at the table. The game’s duration—perfect for a party night and light enough to re-run for multiple rounds—remains intact in this edition. The sealed copy lets you savor that first rush of setup: the grid, the starter team choosing their word, and the shared shenanigans that follow.

### The flow of a typical round (with added Czech flavor)
1. The spymaster looks at the key card to know which words belong to their team. 2. They whisper a one-word clue and a number, like “Kouzlo 3” or “Raketa 2”—your mileage may vary with language-specific word choices. 3. Teammates discuss, debate, and pick cards they believe fit the clue. 4. The chosen cards are revealed; blue or red markers apply; if a teammate hits the assassin, the round ends disastrously for the team that guessed wrong. 5. Play continues until one team's agents are all uncovered, or the assassin ends the run. 6. Rounds rotate to a new spymaster and, if you’re into variant flavor, you can mix with different rule tweaks for extra chaos.

The entire loop is designed to maximize conversation, misdirection, and comic misreads—a hallmark of CODENAMES that translates well across languages, including the Czech edition. It’s not just about winning; it’s about the stories you’ll tell afterward of the one clue that somehow collided with your teammate’s memory of a song lyric, a brand name, or your friend’s catastrophic but hilarious misinterpretation of a single word.
