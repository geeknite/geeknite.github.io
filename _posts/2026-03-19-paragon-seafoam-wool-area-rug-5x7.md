## Durability, care, and maintenance: how long will it last?

### Everyday spills and stain resistance

Wool’s natural properties come into play here: it’s less prone to water absorption than some cotton blends, but you should treat heavy stains quickly. For everyday coffee, tea, or juice mishaps, blot from the outside toward the center with a clean cloth. Avoid aggressive scrubbing; wool fibers can shrink or matt if abused. For more stubborn stains, a gentle wool-safe cleaner or a mixture of water and white vinegar (in a very light ratio) can help lift the stain without color-dulling effects. Always test a hidden area first. Your future self will thank you for not letting a stubborn spill become a permanent mural on your seafoam cover.

### Vacuuming and shedding

New wool rugs typically shed a bit at first, just like a new cat, a new snowboard, or your new gym clothes after the first wash. A few weeks of vacuuming on a low to medium setting should reduce loose fibers. Don’t use a high-suction setting on delicate wool; you can pull fibers and shorten the pile’s life. Over time, shedding should decrease, and the rug will settle into a stable, plush presence that won’t look like it’s wearing a wooly mane after a windy day.

### Care routines that work for real life

- Vacuum regularly, at least once a week, to maintain pile integrity. 
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if you have a high-traffic doorway nearby. 
- Use a rug pad under the rug. A pad prevents slipping, reduces wear on the fibers, and adds extra cushioning—more on that in the accessories section. 
- Treat spills promptly; don’t wait until tomorrow’s laundry day to tackle today’s misadventure.
