## How to play in broad strokes (without giving away all the magic)
Card Sharks is a social, luck-and-skill hybrid that is best understood as a bridge between a classic card game and a pop-culture event. The two players take turns attempting to forecast whether the next card in the deck will be higher or lower than the current card, all while racing up a money ladder that is printed on the board. The show s spirit, drama, suspense, and a little bit of psychological warfare translates to the table through simple rules and a board that makes the crowd in your living room feel like a studio audience.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and place the board in the center. Each player gets a handful of chips or markers to track their score. The first few rounds set the stage for who controls the tempo of the game. If you are playing with family members who cannot decide who should start, just flip a dice or randomly pick someone with a loud laugh; either method is valid.

### Turn structure
- A player reveals the top card and uses it as the anchor for the next guesses high or low. If their guess is right, they move up a segment on the ladder and collect the corresponding reward on the board; a wrong guess ends the turn and yields a smaller, but still respectable, prize.
- The board ladder has a personal drama built into it: the higher you climb, the more dramatic the music becomes in your head (and perhaps in your living room if someone forgot to mute the TV for the first minute of the show). Each correct sequence inches the player closer to the big prize on the top rung, where all the dreams you never told your mom you had live in the form of bright tokens.

### House rules and variations
- If your group enjoys speed: add a timer to each guess. The pressure cooker adds flavor and makes you forget about your actual chess club you never joined.
- For families with small children: simplify the ladder so it is easier to master. The joy here is not the complexity but the signal that you can still feel like a show contestant while keeping the game accessible.
- For players who love extra drama: introduce a guest host who can throw in a rule twist like double or nothing rounds that test not just your card skill but your ability to bluff or fake confidence.
