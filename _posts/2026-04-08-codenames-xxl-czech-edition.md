What is Codenames XXL?
----------------------
Codenames XXL is the oversized sibling of the iconic word game Codenames. The core rules remain intact: two teams (red and blue) vie to find their agents first using a one-word clue from the Spymaster that connects multiple word cards on the table. The twist here is scale. Expect larger word tiles, a bigger play area, and a setup that invites a crowd rather than a quiet living room. This edition is engineered for gatherings where someone’s attempting to set up a “festival of espionage” in a park, on a large dining room table, or on a stage with a microphone held by a friend who thinks they’re hosting a game show.

You’ll find that the essential tension—balance between risky connections and safe plays—remains intact. The clues you craft must link as many of your team’s agents as possible without accidentally steering into the assassin or the other team’s agents. The thrill of a well-chosen clue is the same on XXL as it is on base Codenames: it’s a mental tug-of-war where word associations collide with memory, pun, and sometimes pure luck. The XXL edition asks bigger questions, like: Can you maintain focus with a crowd cheering you on? Will your grandma be amused by a clue that requires a five-word explanation? The answer, in most cases, is yes, and the crowd will thank you for it.

Gameplay and Rules—What Changes (and What Stays the Same)
---------------------------------------------------------
If you’ve played the standard Codenames, you’ll recognize the core loop immediately:

- Two teams, red and blue, compete to uncover their agents on a grid of word cards.
- One Spymaster gives a one-word clue plus a number indicating how many words on the board are tied to that clue.
- Teammates discuss and guess, trying to locate their own agents while avoiding the opposing team’s agents, the civilians, and the assassin.
- The first team to find all their agents wins.

XXL changes some surface-level elements to accommodate the scale, but the DNA of the game remains intact:

- Spatial footprint: The play area is larger, which helps visibility for big groups or outdoor play. You’ll have more room to spread out the words, more room for banter, and more room for your friend to accidentally knock over a stack of cards while celebrating a guessed word.
- Clue length: The one-word clue rule still applies, but you’ll find that players are more forgiving of longer explanations due to the context provided by the oversized board. The Spymaster can also use longer, more descriptive cues if the setting invites it (think a park with a semicircle of chairs).
- Guessing dynamic: The act of guessing remains the same. The tension between confident, bold clues and risky gambles is amplified by the larger audience. The best Clue Givers in XXL harness group energy: a single well-chosen clue can spark a cascade of correct guesses, while a misstep can turn into a table-wide moment of chaos.
- Endgame: The race to reveal all your agents still ends with a dramatic delight or a collective groan when the other team steals a crucial word. The bigger cards make it feel more cinematic, especially when a single clue opens up a long chain that makes the entire table lean in with anticipation.

Setup tips for XXL:
- Arrange the 25 large word tiles in a 5x5 grid on the mat. The larger size gives you visibility from across the room, which is essential if you’re playing to a crowd.
- Place the assassin among the grid; civilians and agents should be distributed as in the standard version.
- Keep a clear line of sight for all players to the spymaster, whether you’re using a back row chair or a park bench.
- If you’re outside, shade the tiles to reduce glare so the words stay readable for everyone.

Strategy and Tips for Spymasters (XXL Edition)
---------------------------------------------
The Spymaster’s job in XXL is, ironically, both easier and a bit harder. Easier because the words are bigger and more readable; harder because the group energy is higher, and your clues will be discussed and debated more. Here are a few practical tips:

- Establish a theme early: If you plan to lean into a unifying theme for a round (e.g., films, foods, sci-fi), mention it at the start so the team can connect the larger word set. A clear theme reduces the mental arithmetic and boosts the confidence of your teammates.
- Use multi-word clue sequences sparingly: In XXL, the bigger they come, the more room you may need for explanation. If you can connect five cards with a single, concise clue, you’re doing it right. If you’re overexplaining, you risk tipping off the other team.
- Kommunication is key: Speak clearly, enunciate your clues, and avoid mumbling into a soda can. A Spymaster who speaks with confidence makes it easier for teammates to trust the guess path.
- Read the room, not just the board: In XXL, the table becomes a character in the game. If energy is high, shorter, punchier clues with quick decisions tend to work better than long, elaborate streams of thought.
- Embrace the drama: The giant format rewards showmanship. A well-timed pause before a guess can build anticipation, while a triumphant cheer after a decisive clue adds to the party atmosphere.

Strategy and Tips for Field Operatives
--------------------------------------
- Listen for the thread: Your Spymaster’s clue is a map. The smarter you listen, the fewer guesses you need and the safer your team becomes.
- Communicate efficiently: If you’re stuck, shout a plan, not a panic. A quick vote or a quick exchange of hints helps you avoid misreads and keeps the game moving.
- Protect the Harsh Truths: The assassin card is a killer in XXL as much as in standard. If a clue seems overly risky, skip it and choose a safer alternative. It’s better to lose a turn than to lose the round to one misstep.
