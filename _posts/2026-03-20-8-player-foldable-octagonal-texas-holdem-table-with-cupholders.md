The felt surface is where the magic happens. You want a texture that allows for predictable card slides when you’re racing through quick rounds, while also minimizing snags on pasted stock. A good table uses a surface that’s not too fast, not too slow—just enough friction to preserve the suspense of the next card turning while letting players read each other’s tells (or at least pretend to).

- Card action: With a proper speed, you get a crisp deal that won’t skip on the edge of the table. The surface should be flat and uniform, with the vinyl edges protecting from wear and tear as players lean or shuffle.

## Maintenance and Care Tips

To keep your folding octagon in top shape, a few maintenance rituals help:

- Clean spills promptly with a damp microfiber cloth. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the felt.
- Keep the surface dry and away from direct sunlight to prevent fading over time.
- Tighten screws and check the folding mechanism after a handful of sessions, especially if you hear creaks when you deploy or store the table.
- Store the table in a cool, dry place. If space is tight, use the included carry bag and keep the table upright to avoid bending.
- If the cup holders collect condensation, wipe them down to prevent drips onto the felt.

A little care goes a long way, and you’ll find the table holds up better than you expect. If you treat it with respect, it will reward you with many nights of poker bliss and minimal drama.

### Cleaning Cheat Sheet
- Wipe the surface after every session with a mild cleaner and microfiber cloth.
- Avoid abrasive materials that can scratch the felt.
- For sticky spots, use a gentle solvent recommended by the manufacturer and test on a small area first.
