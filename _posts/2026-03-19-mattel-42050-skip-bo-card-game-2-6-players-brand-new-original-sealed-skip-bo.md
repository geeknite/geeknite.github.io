## How to Play Skip-Bo: A Quick Start Guide (2-6 Players)
Skip-Bo isn’t about long-winded rules. It’s about fast action, quick turns, and the occasional “did I actually do that correctly?” moment. Here’s a concise primer to get you into the action, with some flavor commentary to keep the giggles going.

### Objective
The goal is simple: be the first player to play all of the cards from your stock pile by building numeric sequences on the central discard piles. In plain terms, you want to deplete your stock pile faster than your friends can squander their own excellent hands in a heroic display of questionable strategy.

### Card Basics
- Number cards: 1 through 12 in several decks, with color-coded variants in classic editions. Building ascending sequences on the central piles is the name of the game.
- Skip-Bo wild cards: These are the wild cards that can stand in for any number, acting as a lifeline when you’re staring down a stubborn hand.
- Action: On your turn, you’ll draw, play some cards onto work piles, and try to maneuver your stock pile toward emptiness.

### Setup
- Each player draws a hand of cards (the exact number varies by house rules or edition, but the common baseline is a fair number that lets you feel like you can do something meaningful on your turn).
- The stock pile is placed face down in front of the player. You won’t peek at it until you’re ready to reveal the next card to play.
- The central discard piles start off as a clean slate for everyone to push cards onto, climbing toward those glorious, satisfying sequences.

### Turn Structure (Fast and Fun)
1) Draw up to the hand limit. 2) You can play cards from your hand or stock to the central piles in ascending order starting at 1. 3) If you can’t play any cards, you must draw to refill your options. 4) End your turn when you play or when you choose to pass—yes, you can pass, but the table will judge you silently. 5) When your stock pile is empty, you win. The crowd goes mild. Or loud. It depends on your table’s vibe.

### Strategy Nuggets
- Early mobility matters more than late-game heroics. If you can shed a few cards early, you’ll gain options later when the board gets a tad more crowded.
- Use wilds strategically. Don’t waste a wild on a number you already have; reserve it for the card that unlocks a jammed sequence.
- Pay attention to opponents’ queues. If you notice someone’s stock pile shrinking, you might want to disrupt their flow with a tactical wild placement on a nearby pile.
- Keep a mental ledger of when you expect to draw strong cards to avoid overcommitting a hand to a weak sequence.
