### Cleaning, Care, and Longevity

Pet-friendly and kid-ready means you’ll likely encounter spills and crumbs. The rug’s construction responds well to routine care. Vacuum with a standard rider, avoiding aggressive suction to prevent pile flattening in high-traffic zones. A vacuum with adjustable height helps prevent snagging on the loops. If you spill something more stubborn (think wine or mustard), a mild detergent solution applied with a soft cloth and blotting is recommended. For tough stains, treat with a specialty rug cleaner following the product instructions, then blot and air-dry. Avoid bleach, as it can affect color integrity.

The rug dries in a reasonable time, especially if you flip it up and let both sides air out. The acrylic-based dyes resist fading for a long period, though consistent sun exposure can gradually shift color toward a softer gray-blue. If your space has big windows with direct sun, consider rotating the rug occasionally to keep color distribution uniform.
