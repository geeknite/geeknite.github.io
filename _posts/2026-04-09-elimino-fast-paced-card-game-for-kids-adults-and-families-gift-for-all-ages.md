## How to play (the quick version)
Elimino! is built on a simple core loop that even a non-gamer can grasp after two rounds of dramatic misplays:
1) Each player draws to a hand size (usually five cards to start). 
2) On your turn, you try to play cards that match the current “top” card on the play stack. Matches can be by number, suit, or symbol depending on the current card’s rule. 
3) If you can’t play a card, you draw, and your turn passes; the goal is to shed your hand — the fewer cards, the more peace you enjoy. 
4) Special action cards add spice: skip turns, force other players to draw, swap hands, and sometimes wilds that change the rule of engagement. 
5) When a player empties their hand, they call “Elimino!” and the round ends; the other players tally points from their remaining cards. The lowest score after a few rounds wins. Simple, right? Except for the part where you realize you’ve spent an entire Friday night arguing whether a card should be allowed to count as a “wild” in a moment of pure chaos.
