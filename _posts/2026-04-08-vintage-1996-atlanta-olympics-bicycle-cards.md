# Authentication Tips: How to Verify You’re Buying the Real Gold Deck

- Inspect the seal and the box for signs of tampering or replacement. A genuine sealed deck should show a consistent crisp seal and the printed imagery that looks aligned with the era. If the seal is suspiciously modern-looking or the printing looks off, you may be holding a counterfeit or a later reprint. 
- Examine the stock and finish: 1990s Bicycle stock tends to be stiffer and slightly different in feel to modern luxury finishes. The linen texture should be pronounced, not slick like modern coated deck runs.
- Compare the back design to high-resolution images from reputable vintage card catalogs (without relying on any single source). The symmetry and the linework should align; any misalignment can be a telltale sign of faked edges.
- Check the court cards for consistency: The Olympic branding would be present, and the character designs should match the era’s aesthetic—neither cartoonish to a fault nor so austere that it looks like a modern rebrand.
