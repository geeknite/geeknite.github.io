## Tips, Tricks, and Strategy Notes

- Read the room. If your group loves speed and chaos, lean into quick plays and early pressure. If your group enjoys the drama, stretch out turns with witty plays and feints.
- Use expansions to create tension without feeling unfair. The goal is energy, not victory by domination.
- Establish a light house rule: if someone immediately bluffs into a wrong move, they pay a snack tax to the table. It’s silly, it’s harmless, and it reinforces the social contract of playful mischief.
- Pay attention to bluffs. Part of the fun is discerning when someone is playing you and when you’re being played. The micro-moments of reading someone’s expression can be worth more than the actual card you hold.
- Don’t be afraid to be dramatic. The more you ham it up, the more everyone else buys in. The game loves performance, and you will be rewarded with contagious laughter.
