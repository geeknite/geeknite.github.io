## How to Play: The Quick Guide
Sushi Go! Pick & Pass is a card-drafting game for 2-5 players, but there is a glorious secret: it scales gracefully from two players to a full table of friends who somehow all brought their own verbal opinions about every single card in the deck. The core mechanic — pick one card from your hand, then pass the rest to your neighbor — is the star of the show. In this Tin version, you’ll typically play a handful of rounds that feel like the tempo of a chef courtesy of a blender: fast, a little chaotic, and somehow satisfying.

Here’s the quick flow:

1) Deal each player a hand of five cards (the exact number can vary by variant).
2) Simultaneously, everyone picks one card to keep and passes the rest to the player on their left (for the next round, roles may switch depending on the variant you’re playing).
3) When everyone has picked, show your cards and tally scoring according to the different sushi types and bonuses.
4) Repeat for a few rounds; the player with the most points wins.

In Pick & Pass, speed and memory become a factor. You need to know what you’ve passed away and what your neighbor might be hoping to nab next. The challenge is to strike a balance between securing a high-scoring card for yourself and preventing your opponent from building a super-charged combo on their side of the table.

The scoring categories in Sushi Go! are a blend of straightforward and punishing. You’re chasing sets of Nigiri, the sweet tang of Tempura, the playful chaos of Sashimi, the strategic placement of Wasabi to amplify Nigiri values, and the crowd-pleasing Maki bonuses that reward the top tree-tier roll call. If you’ve played the original Sushi Go!, you’ll recognize the same mechanics, but the Pick & Pass variant adds a meta-game of second-by-second decision making that keeps each round feeling fresh.
