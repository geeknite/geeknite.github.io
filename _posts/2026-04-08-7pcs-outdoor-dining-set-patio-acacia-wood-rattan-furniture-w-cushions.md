## Assembly and maintenance
### Assembly: like a puzzle, but with a more forgiving box
Most 7-piece sets arrive with components labeled and hardware in resealable bags. The assembly is straightforward but not a tea party trick. You need basic tools (often included) and a little patience. Expect about 1-2 hours for a solo assembly or less with a helper who understands the delicate art of aligning holes without cursing at the screws. If you have never assembled flat-pack furniture before, you are in for a rite of passage. If you have, you will likely finish in 25 minutes and then brag to your non-wooden furniture neighbor about your precise alignment of screws.

### Maintenance: treat the wood right, and it will treat you to a longer life
Maintenance is not a myth; it is a ritual. Wipe down the table and chairs with a mild soap solution to remove dust, pollen, and the occasional barbecue splatter. Periodically treat the wood with a suitable oil or sealant recommended by the manufacturer. This helps preserve color, prevent cracking, and keeps the finish looking fresh for seasons to come. Cushions require a different approach: remove, wash if the cover is machine washable, dry thoroughly, and never leave the cushions soaked in a puddle unless you want a tiny bouquet of mildew as a souvenir.

## Real-world performance
### Weather resistance: rain, sun, and the occasional sprinkler attack
Acacia wood is known for its durability outdoors, though it is not immune to the weather. With proper sealant and maintenance, the set can stand up to sun and rain with grace. In climates with heavy rain, you may want to store cushions indoors when not in use or use weatherproof cushion covers. Lightweight wind may rearrange the cushions if you forget to secure them. If you live in an area with strong UV rays, you will want to keep it oiled to prevent drying and cracking; this will preserve color and structural integrity as patio drama unfolds around the barbecue.

### Comfort in heat and humidity
In hot climates, the cushions can feel warm to the touch after direct sun. The fabric choice matters; breathable fabrics help keep temps down and dryness up. In humidity, you may notice swelling in wood if not sealed properly, but proper maintenance minimizes this risk. The rattan details remain comfortable to the touch and do not become overly hot, which is a win for your legs and thighs after a long dinner party.

## Assembly tips and caveats
- Always read the manual before brute-forcing a bolt. The manual exists for a reason, and it is not just to tell you to do things in a particular order.
- Lay out parts on a soft surface; you don't want to scratch the acacia or mar the rattan with a misplaced tool. A towel-covered floor often works wonders for the initial alignment.
- Tighten gradually in a star pattern; this ensures even pressure and reduces warping risks.
- Cushion care matters: check for colorfastness, wash covers if possible, and store cushions offline during heavy rain or winter to extend their life.
