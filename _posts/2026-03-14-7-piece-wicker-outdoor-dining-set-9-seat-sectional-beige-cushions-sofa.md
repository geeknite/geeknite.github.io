### Tips for a smooth build

- Sort screws and washers before you start; a magnetic tray is your friend.
- Thread screws by hand before using a driver to avoid cross-threading.
- Check that all modular connectors align cleanly to avoid misalignment that causes squeaks later.
- Have a helper for lifting and aligning heavy modular pieces; you are constructing a social hub, not a solo fortress.

## Weather resistance and durability

Outdoor furniture lives and dies by weather. The wicker weave used here typically resists moisture and sun exposure better than natural rattan, but not perfectly forever. Expect some color shift over years if your climate is relentlessly sunny. The frames, usually powder-coated aluminum or steel, resist rust well, provided you don’t leave cushions out in heavy precipitation for extended periods. Cushion covers are designed to shed water, but prolonged moisture can lead to mold if you do not give them air and sun when possible. Regularly store cushions indoors or use breathable covers during storms or winter months. A quick wipe with a mild soap solution keeps the wicker looking fresh, and a light spray of water over the weave helps rinse off dust and pollen that accumulate between afternoons of alfresco dining.

## Maintenance and cleaning routine

A simple maintenance routine goes a long way. Wipe the weave with a damp cloth to remove surface dirt. For the cushions, unzip and remove the covers and wash on gentle cycles as recommended by the manufacturer. Allow cushions to dry fully before returning them to the set to prevent moisture entrapment. If you notice slight fading, rotate cushions and seat locations to distribute wear evenly. For a deeper clean on the fabric, use a mild detergent and a soft brush, testing on a small area first to ensure colorfastness. A protective cover when the set is not in use can greatly extend the life of both the wicker and cushions without cramping your style.
