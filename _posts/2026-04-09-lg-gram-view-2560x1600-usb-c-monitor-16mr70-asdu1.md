Like any good gadget, the Gram View has its caveats. ## Calibration tips to get the most out of it

- Start with a neutral color temperature (around 6500K) and adjust gamma to your preference. If you’re editing content for the web, sRGB is a solid baseline.
- Use a colorimeter for a quick calibration pass. It will save you headaches in the long run and make you feel like a legitimate color wizard.
- Tweak the brightness to a comfortable level for your workspace. A too-bright monitor in a dim room is a recipe for eye strain; a too-dim monitor in a bright room makes you squint like a 90s detective.
- Consider enabling a light-dimming feature or ambient light compensation if your desk has variable lighting throughout the day. Small adjustments here can reduce fatigue.
