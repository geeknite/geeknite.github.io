## Care, Longevity, and Maintenance
Durability is a selling point for many rugs in this category. A low to medium pile is easier to keep clean than a high-pile cousin who eats dust like a dragon. Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting, avoid harsh brushes, and rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. Spills should be blotted promptly; avoid rubbing, which can push pigment into fibers. For stubborn stains, test a small inconspicuous area or consult the manufacturer’s care guide.

### Cleaning routines you can actually follow
- Vacuum weekly to keep grit from settling into the fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing.
- If you have pets, consider a pet-safe stain remover that is color-safe and fragrance-free.
- Rotate quarterly to ensure even wear, especially if sunlight hits one corner more than others.
- For deep cleaning, consider a professional cleaning every couple of years, especially if you entertain a lot of guests who insist on bringing their muddy boots into your carefully curated aesthetic.
