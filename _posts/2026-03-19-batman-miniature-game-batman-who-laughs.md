## Aesthetic and Painting Notes
### Getting the Scheme Right
The color palette for the Batman Who Laughs is a playground. The base dark tones of the outfit contrast with the pale, almost zombified facial features, and the metallic highlights can be used to accent the weaponry and armor plates. A common approach is to lean into the sinister white of the mask with deliberate weathering on the cloak to convey the sense of a villain who thrives in shadows. A subtle use of rust and grime can emphasize the chaotic nature of his weaponry without crossing into too cartoonish territory.

### Tabletop Presence
On the table, the Who Laughs reads tall and pointy in a way that breaks the monotony of standard human silhouettes. His silhouette is striking; the cloak arc frames him nicely and helps pull attention from the rest of the squad toward the center of the board. The miniature can be highlighted through a few simple techniques: edge highlighting on the cloak, dry brushing on the metallic areas, and a controlled glaze to bring the face into focus without making it look overdone.

### A Quick Painting Guide You Can Use
- Base coat: mid to dark gray for the suit, with a slightly cooler undertone to give an ominous vibe.
- Cloak: deep purple or a bluish black with magenta or teal accents along the edges to evoke a sense of otherworldly chaos.
- Skin/mask: pale tones with subtle pink or purple washes to create a slightly sickly look that matches the character’s narrative.
- Metallics: silver or gunmetal with black wash for definition; copper accents work well on any mechanical details.
- Final touch: a pinched white highlight on the eyes or mask to create an unsettling stare that seems to pierce the tabletop glare of your opponent.

If you want a more thematic route, you can push the color scheme toward a comic-book hue that makes the figure pop on a modern, high-contrast table. The trick is to keep the face and eyes as the focal point while letting the cloak blend into the shadows of the battlefield.
