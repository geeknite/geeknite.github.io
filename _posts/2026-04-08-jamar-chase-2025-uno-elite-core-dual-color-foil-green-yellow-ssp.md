## Grading and Preservation: Keeping the Sparkle

### Grading Considerations

When you’re dealing with foil variants, grading becomes a little trickier. Foil surfaces can show fingerprints and minor scratching under magnification, and color-shifting foils can be sensitive to light exposure. A near-mint to mint condition with a clean surface and sharp corners is the target. If you’re aiming for a gem mint grade, you’ll want a card that looks perfect under a loupe, without any foil bleed or edge creasing that becomes obvious when scanned at 10x magnification.

### Protection and Display

- Sleeves: Use a high-clarity, scratch-resistant sleeve to protect the foil finish without dulling the shine.
- Toploaders vs. semi-rigid: For display or transport, semi-rigid holders with solid backings help keep the card flat and safe.
- Storage: Avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight or extreme heat; foil can become discolored or lose some of its luster if kept in a hot room for months on end.
- Display technique: A lightbox or a desk lamp angled to mimic natural daylight can help you photograph the card for social posts without washing out the foil’s color shifts.

### Grading Services to Consider

If you’re planning on a value-driven purchase or a serious investment, consider professional grading from established services. The right grade can unlock a higher market value, and a pristine reveal can be a great talking point in photos and videos. Do your homework: compare recent graded results, understand the submission window timings, and factor in turnaround times.
