## What is Fortune Row?

Fortune Row is a themed subset of the City of Mist universe that focuses on a handful of characters who navigate the shadows of the city’s most morally questionable blocks. Think morally ambiguous icons, not squeaky-clean heroes. Each figurine is sculpted to capture a signature pose, a hint of backstory, and a style that screams city noir wrapped in folklore. This is not a random set of minis; it’s a curated cast designed to play a role in the stories you tell at the table.

### The lineup in brief

The Fortune Row assortment typically features a mix of archetypes that City of Mist players will recognize: a velvet-clad con artist with a heart of gold, a clockmaker who bends time in small, elegant ways, a street oracle who reads the city like a living tarot deck, a rooftop sentinel who sees the city breathe at night, and a few other performers of misdirection and fortune. While the exact characters can vary by release, the vibe remains constant: stylish, a little dangerous, and loaded with narrative potential. Each figure is paired with a compact set of reference cards that tie into the Fortune Row theme and help you slot them into the mythos ranks you’ve already built for your game night.

## Packaging, sculpting, and build quality

### The sculpt quality

Fortune Row uses standard plastic miniatures with a premium paint scheme. ## Painting, display, and care tips

### Getting the most from your figures

A little primer, a few washes, and a soft highlight can elevate the characters from “great display” to “table centerpiece.” Start with a basic spray primer to even out the surface texture, then block in the main colors before moving to shadows. A mid-tone wash works wonders on emblem motifs and fabric folds, especially on the capes and cloaks that give Fortune Row its signature noir swagger. If you’re feeling adventurous, a light dry-brush can bring the metalwork and jewelry to life without turning the figure into a Christmas ornament.

### Display ideas

With a set of six to eight figures, you’ve got an instant little diorama opportunity. A shallow display case, a light fog machine, and a backdrop printed with neon rain can turn your dining table into a micro-city vignette. If you prefer more modular setups, use clear acrylic bases and a few risers to create a layered street-scene effect. The goal is to let the silhouettes peek out from the fog and remind everyone at the table that this city is made of stories, each as tall as a rumor and as bright as a neon sign.

### Care and storage

Plastic miniatures like Fortune Row survive normal tabletop use, but they do best with a little care. Avoid leaving them under direct sun for long stretches; not because the plastic will melt, but because sunlit plastic can look sad and faded after a couple of seasons. When stored, keep them in their box or a dedicated figure case to prevent dust buildup and scuffs on the painted surfaces. A microfiber cloth and a gentle dusting every few months are enough to keep the lines sharp and the figures looking sharp during your next session.
