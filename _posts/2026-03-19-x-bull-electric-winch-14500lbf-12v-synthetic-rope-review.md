### Synthetic Rope vs Steel Cable
Here’s a nerdy debate that shows up in every off-road forum:
- Synthetic rope: lighter, safer to handle, floats on water, resists kinking, but costs more upfront and has to be cleaned and stored properly. It’s friendlier to you and your vehicle because it’s less likely to snap back and cause injury. 
- Steel cable: tougher, cheaper, and grimly reliable in heavy-duty, but extremely unforgiving if you kink or fray it. The risk of recoil is real and painful to experience in a video. 
The X-BULL uses synthetic rope, which suits the modern off-road crowd who value easier maintenance and less risk of injury during setup. This comes with the caveat that synthetic rope can be damaged by sharp rocks or UV exposure if left unattended. We recommend inspecting the rope regularly and using a protective sleeve where possible.
