Durability and Maintenance

Being a fixed blade, the Honshu Fighter I is less fussy than folding blades that require frequent lock maintenance. The full tang construction contributes to rigidity and durability during routine tasks. Stainless steel helps resist corrosion in humid environments, but you should still perform basic maintenance to extend its life. Wipe down after use, avoid sitting in damp leather for extended periods, and oil the tang/chord of the handle where metal meets grip material if you notice any dryness or micro gaps forming.

Like many knives in this price tier, you should not treat UC2629 as a swordsman’s blade. It is a practical tool designed for daily chores, camping hacks, and weekend DIY projects. If you rely on it heavily for batoned tasks or heavy prying, you might want to consider a blade with a thicker spine or a higher grade steel. For casual use, the Honshu Fighter I offers a balance between performance and price that makes it appealing to both new knife enthusiasts and seasoned collectors looking for a compact backup.

Maintenance and Care Tips

- Clean and dry after use to reduce moisture exposure and prevent corrosion on any budget steel.
- Oil the blade lightly at the tang junction and around the handle if you notice dryness.
- Check sheath retention screws or clips periodically to ensure it stays secure on your belt or pack.
- Keep the leather conditioned; untreated leather will age gracefully, but you should avoid soaking the sheath in water or leaving it damp for extended periods.
- Store it in a dry, ventilated area to minimize rust risk and keep the leather from cracking.
