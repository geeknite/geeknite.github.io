## Weather Resistance and Maintenance
Outdoor furniture is attacked by sun, rain, wind, and the occasional leaf blower enthusiast. The aluminum frame is built to resist corrosion, and the surface should hold color well with basic care. Wood slats, while offering a visually pleasing natural texture, require a little more ongoing attention to keep them looking pristine.

### Climate considerations by region
Coastal regions with salty air require a more proactive approach to corrosion protection, while areas with high rainfall demand effective drainage and moisture management in the wood. In arid climates, sun exposure can fade color and dry out the wood. A routine maintenance plan that includes periodic cleaning and protective sealing will greatly extend the table’s life and keep the gray finish looking uniform.

### Cleaning and conditioning the wood
A mild soap solution and soft brush are usually sufficient for everyday cleaning. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the surface. For conditioning, consider a marine-grade penetrating oil or a UV protective sealant designed for outdoor wood. Apply according to the manufacturer’s recommendations, typically every season or two, depending on how aggressively sun and rain affect your area. After conditioning, wipe away excess product and allow the surface to dry fully before use.

### Covers and storage
During off seasons or periods of heavy weather, a breathable cover is a smart investment. Covers protect against sun fading, rain penetration, and wind-blown debris. If your climate experiences heavy snowfall, consider moving the set indoors or into a sheltered space to minimize weight on the table and the risk of warping the wood slats.
