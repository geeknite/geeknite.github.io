## Care, Storage, and Longevity
### Keeping the Canvas Cards Pristine
Canvas cards may feel more durable, but they also show wear in different ways. For long-term preservation, keep the box sealed when not in use. If you’re a regular player, consider a proper card organizer that fits UNO-sized decks and has a dedicated space for the limited edition artwork to minimize wear on the surface. Washing hands before play is silly on the surface but helps prevent smudges on the vibrant artwork. No, you cannot put the cards through a laundry cycle to “refresh” the canvas—this is still a card game, not a museum artifact cleaning experiment. Use a soft cloth to wipe any fingerprint residue and store away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading on the inked designs.

### Handling Tips for Longevity
- Always shuffle gently to avoid edge wear on the canvas surface.
- Use a card sleeve alternative if you’re worried about scuffs; just ensure it doesn’t alter the card dimensions or bending radius.
- Keep the original box intact for display and resale value, especially if you’re collecting multiple limited editions.
- Avoid placing the deck near moisture or heat sources, as canvas textures can respond to humidity in ways that are neither helpful nor aesthetically pleasing.
