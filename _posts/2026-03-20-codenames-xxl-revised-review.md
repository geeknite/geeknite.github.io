## What is Codenames XXL (Revised)?

Codenames XXL is Czech Games Editions’ much-loved word association party game, scaled up for readability, chaos, and the occasional misheard clue. The Revised edition keeps the core gameplay intact—two teams, one Spymaster per team, and a grid of words that both teams will mercilessly pair with clues. But XXL adds a jumbo font, a sturdier board, and some quality-of-life tweaks that make you wonder how you ever survived with the ordinary edition.

The premise remains delightfully simple: two teams race to identify all their agents on a grid of words using one-word clues given by the Spymaster. Your clue should point to as many of your team’s words as possible while avoiding the opposing team’s words, your own assassin word, and, ideally, your own sanity.

For a game night that often ends with at least one person yelling, XXL Revised leans into the chaos with bigger text and easier handling. It’s the kind of product that makes you feel clever for decoding a clue while also realizing you can’t read the font from across the room unless you’re wearing your nanotech-grade bifocals. In short: it’s still Codenames, just louder and tougher on the eyes in a way that screams, we care about your reading comfort, friend.

## How XXL Revised changes the gameplay (spoilers avoided, mostly)

Codenames XXL Revised doesn’t reimagine the wheel; it refreshes the wheel’s font. The core mechanics stay intact: two teams, red and blue, take turns with a Spymaster giving one-word clues and a number. The clue should relate to as many of their team’s words as possible, but not all; if you guess a word that belongs to the opposing team, it’s your turn over; if you hit the assassin word, your team loses immediately. The beauty is in the risk-reward dynamic—clarity of the clue versus the risk of misinterpretation.

What XXL Revised subtly tweaks:
- Readability as a feature: bigger text reduces the eye-strain during intense, laughter-filled sessions. You can argue about the clue’s genius or the team’s misinterpretation, but at least your eyes won’t fail you mid-Nominalist or mid-Operative.
- Card spacing and layout: the revised edition places words with slightly more breathing room, reducing accidental overlaps that lead to accidental laughter and a cascade of confusion.
- Clue-crafting guidance: the included reference card now explicitly warns about common blind spots like synonyms that are too close to other words on the board. Your Spymaster can still be reckless, but the risk is more measured.

The pacing remains brisk: a typical round lasts as long as your group can avoid repeatedly yelling out the wrong words or doing an impression of a disappointed mascot when you hit the assassin word. The goal remains the same: identify all your agents before the other team does, all while keeping track of the ominous red glow that signals you are very close to doom.

## How to play with XXL Revised: a quick walk-through

- Setup: lay out the 5x5 word grid, deal the Spymaster’s cards, and decide which team goes first. The larger font means you can actually read the grid from the far end of the couch without planting your elbow into a plate of nachos.
- Turn order: the Spymaster looks at their team’s agent cards and provides a one-word clue plus a number indicating how many words on the board tie to that clue. The number is your confidence meter, so choose wisely or prepare for a chaotic, hilarious reveal.
- Guessing phase: team members discuss which words match the clue and collectively decide which to reveal. The Spymaster’s role is to nod, wink, or grimace when a guess lands on the assassin or the other team’s word.
- Endgame: the round ends when a team has identified all their words; if the assassin is revealed, the game ends in a mercy kill for that team. The first to find all their words wins, and the table collectively heaves a sigh of relief that the rules are finally working as intended.

The revised edition keeps the rhythm predictable enough for newbies but adds a little edge for veterans who want to push their luck with a bolder clue. It’s a game that rewards clever associations, a good read of your teammates, and a willingness to embrace the chaos that follows a “space pirates” clue when you really meant “secret agents.”

## Strategy tips for Spymasters and field operatives

- Be mindful of word ambiguity: choose clues that map cleanly to several of your words but avoid words that could be linked to multiple words on the board. Your team will thank you later when they don’t end up chasing the wrong unicorn.
- Embrace the group dynamic: XXL Revised is as much social as strategic. If you’ve got a loud friend who loves to take over the table, shape your clues to ride that wave—just don’t ride it into an assassin.
- Use the board’s color logic: the red/blue distinction matters, but so does the gray, the neutral words that neither team should touch. Use this to your advantage by guiding your team to riskier-but-safer choices in later rounds.
- Reading the room matters: a well-timed joke or reference can unlock group thinking. The key is to be funny without derailing the pace or making someone cry over a far-fetched clue.
