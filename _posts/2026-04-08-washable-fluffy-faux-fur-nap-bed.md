## Cleaning and care: the washing machine side quest
### Washability in practical terms
One of the nap bed’s biggest selling points is its machine washable cover. This means you do not have to fear the inevitable mud paw print or the occasional spilled smoothie during a particularly dramatic Netflix session. Remove the cover, toss it into the washing machine, and return to a fresh, fluffy fortress of calm in about an hour. We tested both gentle cycle operations and standard cycles, and both managed to revitalize the fur without compromising the underlying cushion. Drying is recommended on a low heat setting or air dry to preserve fiber integrity. It is not a tumble dryer romance novel; it is a practical care guide for real homes with real pets and real laundry cycles.

### Cleaning frequency and tips
If you have a pet who sheds or a human who snacks on the couch, you will want to wash the cover every 1 to 3 weeks depending on use. For light maintenance, a quick vacuum or lint roller run should keep things presentable in between full wash cycles. We recommend also spot cleaning with a mild detergent for minor stains instead of throwing the entire bed into the wash for every little splash. The goal is to keep fluff, not to turn the bed into a science experiment where you test how long it takes to reconstitute the nap after a wash.

### The care caveats
If your bed has been through a particularly intense play session or a puppy tornado of a morning, check the seams after washing. The last thing you want is a damaged zipper or loose edge causing panic in the middle of the night when your pet demands a rescue nap. The nap bed can handle a decent amount of abuse, but treat it with the care you would give a prized soft toy that your life depends on. Remember, fluff is not forever, but with proper care, it can last a good while.
