## Durability, Cleaning, and Long-Term Care
Durability in a product like this is not about a single heroic moment; it''s about steady, dependable performance. Here are best practices to maximize long-term satisfaction:
- Regular vacuuming or brushing to remove loose debris and keep fibers from matting
- Immediate blotting of spills with a clean cloth to prevent staining
- Light scrubbing with a mild detergent for stubborn spots, followed by thorough rinsing and air drying
- Protecting the rug from direct, intense sun for extended periods to prevent color fading

If you treat the rug like a trusted sidekick rather than a rare collectible, you will get years of service from it. It won''t let you down when your aunt decides to host a full-on family reunion in your living room and turns your quiet lounge into a makeshift photo booth.
