## Maintenance and Longevity
### Cleaning Guidelines: Quick Dabs to Hero Scares
Spills happen. In Geeknite, we test quick blotting with a clean cloth or paper towel. Avoid rubbing—this tends to push the stain deeper into the fibers. For stubborn spots, a mild detergent solution or a rug-specific cleaner works best, testing first in an inconspicuous area. Always blot, never scrub. Your future you will thank you when the stain hasn’t ghosted its way into the weave.

### Vacuuming Routine: Frequency and Techniques
Vacuuming weekly is a good baseline; more active spaces might require more frequent attention. Use a vacuum with adjustable height and a gentle beater bar setting to protect the fibers from unnecessary wear. The key is consistency: a routine beat the dirt and dust to the punch, maintaining the rug’s texture and color integrity.

### Fading, Pile, and Rotation
Ivory can be a bit sensitive to direct sun. If your living area basks in bright afternoon light, rotate the rug every few months to keep color from leaning unevenly. A rug that develops a slight color shift under sun is not a fashion malfunction; it’s a patina forming. If you’re into the idea of patina, you’ll love the subtle story the rug begins to tell over time.

### Longevity Outlook: The Geeknite Verdict
With proper care, a hand tufted rug of this scale can last for many years. The craftsmanship and materials—assuming ethical sourcing and good maintenance—provide a stable, durable product that can accompany you through remodels, changes of furniture, and the occasional gaming marathon where snacks become the most dangerous elements in the room.
