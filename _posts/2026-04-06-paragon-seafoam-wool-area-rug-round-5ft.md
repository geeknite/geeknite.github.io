## Care, Maintenance, and Practical Usage
Wool rugs are forgiving, but they’re not magic. Spills happen; stains happen; sunlight can fade color. Here’s how to approach it:

- Cleaning basics: Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the fibers.
- Vacuuming: Use a vacuum with a gentle setting or a handheld attachment. If your vacuum has a beater bar, consider turning it off to avoid fuzzing the surface.
- Sunlight and color: Prolonged direct sun can fade wool. If your space has intense sun exposure, rotate the rug occasionally to distribute wear and color evenly.
- Stain treatment: For common spills (coffee, wine, juice), treat quickly with a mild detergent solution, test on an inconspicuous area first, and avoid harsh solvents.
- Odor control: Wool typically handles odors well, but if you cook heavily or have pets, air it out or use a light wool-safe deodorizer.
- Rotating and flipping: In rooms with directional lighting, rotate every few months to ensure even wear and prevent color shading that looks like a trend you didn’t sign up for.

### Cleaning Shortcuts and Tools
A soft-bristle brush can help lift surface dust between deep cleanings. A spot-cleaning pen designed for wool fibers can be a lifesaver for stubborn stains, provided you test on a hidden area first. For heavier soils, consult a professional rug cleaner with wool-expertise to avoid over-wetting or dye transfer.
