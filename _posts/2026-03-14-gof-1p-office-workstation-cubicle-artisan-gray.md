## Design and Build Quality

### Materials and Finish

The GOF 1P uses a combination of metal framing and laminated particle board for the desk surface and the side panels. The finish is described as Artisan Gray, which translates to a mid range gray with a touch of warm undertone that looks professional yet tolerant of coffee stains. In real life the edges feel smooth to the touch and there is a respectable rigidity to the cubicle shell. No creaks that threaten to collapse into a dramatic collapse scene during an important Zoom call. The metal supports feel sturdy, and the desk surface is resistant enough to desk plants, notepads, and a perched gaming mouse if you occasionally turn the cubicle into a personal battlestation for a few minutes. The paint finish resists fingerprints to a reasonable degree, which is nice for people who maintain a fridge magnet level of desk organization.

### Dimensions and Fit

The GOF 1P takes up roughly 6 feet of depth and 7 feet of width in the main footprint. Height is around 4 feet plus a small additional profile depending on the exact model and whether you include any overhead storage shelf. The space is designed to accommodate a single monitor or two smaller displays, with a desk surface that offers enough width for a full keyboard, a numeric keypad, and still a cup of cold brew within reach. The cubicle walls provide enough separation to claim some privacy while not turning your world into a bunker. If you run a large triple-monitor setup, you might find yourself stretching the rule book a bit, but the unit can handle lighter multi monitor setups with a bit of strategic placement.

## Assembly Tips, Pitfalls, and Pro Tips

- Keep a magnetic tray for screws and small brackets; you will lose them otherwise and your cat may claim the missing fasteners as a new toy. If you don’t have a cat, a magnet on the fridge works too.
- Do not tighten all bolts at once; tighten in a star pattern to avoid racking the frame. Your future back will thank you.
- Use a power drill with a gentle setting for the initial assembly. You do not want to strip the metal threads while you still have the manual and the anxiety in your head.
- Check that the desk top is level by using a simple bubble level app on your phone before final tightening. It saves you from a wobbly keyboard experience during critical tasks.
- If you plan to run a multi-monitor setup, plan cable routing ahead of time. The GOF spine works great, but you’ll thank yourself for planning the path in advance rather than discovering a tangled spaghetti of adapters after the fact.

## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Longevity

The Artisan Gray finish cleans easily with a soft, damp cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the surface. For the desk top, a simple wipe after meals is enough to maintain that professional look. For the metal frames, a microfiber cloth and a light dusting should keep the unit looking sharp. If you notice any squeaks, a touch of light oil on the joints can help, but do not overdo it. A little oil is like a dash of hot sauce for furniture joints; a lot of hot sauce for furniture joints is not a good idea. Periodic tightening of fasteners is a good habit if you move your setup around or reconfigure accessories.
