## Build quality and materials
### Blade and edge geometry
The UC3176 is marketed as part of the Honshu Boshin family, which leans toward accessible aesthetics and cosplay appeal rather than competitive cutting. In practice, you should expect a competent edge suitable for display and light slicing on safe targets, but not for rigorous training or sharpening on a regular basis. The grind tends to be thicker than a tournament-grade blade, which translates to an increase in resilience against accidental nicks but a stiffer feel when you attempt to slice through a rolled tarp or a carrot without proper technique.

## Maintenance tips and care guide
### Basic upkeep for a budget blade
- Wipe the blade after handling to remove oils and sweat
- Apply a light mineral oil or sword oil to prevent rust on the exposed steel
- Inspect the tsuka-ito wrap for looseness and adjust if necessary
- Keep the saya clean and dry to avoid moisture buildup
- Do not use aggressive cleaners that could degrade the finish

### Storage and display safety
- Use a stable stand or wall rack to avoid accidental falls
- Keep away from kids and curious pets who want to swing it by the handle
- If you plan to use the blade for any cutting, do so on safe targets and under supervision if needed
- When not in use, store in a dry, ventilated area and avoid extreme temperatures
