## Blade Steel, Edge Retention, and Maintenance
### Why 1060 Carbon Steel Matters
1060 carbon steel is a classic choice for fixed blades. It’s tough, holds an edge reasonably well, and is a joy to touch up with a sharpening stone. The UC3430 uses a heat treatment typical of 1060 blends that aims for a balance between hardness and toughness. Expect around the high-50s to low-60s HRC range depending on batch specifics. That sweet spot makes it forgiving enough to sharpen at home but still capable of delivering clean cuts when you need to do serious work.

### Sharpening, Rust, and Care
Carbon steel isn’t a goldfish: it needs attention. The blade responds well to standard sharpening methods, from stones to guided systems. Since rust is a real concern with carbon steel, regular oiling after use and storage in a dry environment is recommended. If you’re one of those “I’ll oil it when I remember” folks, you’ll want to set a calendar reminder—before you forget the blade exists and your blade becomes a student of rust philosophy. A simple wipe-down with light mineral oil after use will do wonders for keeping corrosion at bay. A quick maintenance routine will keep the Quillon looking slick and performing reliably for years.
