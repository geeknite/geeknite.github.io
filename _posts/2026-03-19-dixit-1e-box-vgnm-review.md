### Box Contents and Condition (VG+ NM)
The 1st Edition base set of Dixit typically includes:
- 84 storytelling cards with Cardouat’s iconic illustrations
- 6 wooden or plastic pawns (the small avatars used for voting)
- 6 voting tokens (one for each player’s bid)
- A rulebook that, funnily enough, reads like a manifesto for storytelling rather than a rigid set of instructions
- A game board is optional depending on which version you own; some editions used a simple score track instead

In a VG+ NM copy, you want clean card edges, minimal card whitening (the cards should still feel luxurious to the touch and not brittle), and a box that closes snugly with almost no flex in the lid. Common signs of wear in this edition include: small corner bumps, a faint scuff along the spine, and perhaps a couple of fingerprint smudges on the backs of some cards. None of these are deal-breakers for gameplay, and many collectors prize the vintage patina as proof of a well-loved copy rather than a dispassionate minty machine.

From a practical standpoint, a VG+ NM Dixit 1e is a joy to open and sort. The cards are large enough to handle comfortably, the artwork remains legible and compelling, and the overall tactile experience matches the game’s dreamlike vibe. If you’re hunting for a pristine example, you’ll pay a premium; if you’re seeking a playable gem that still brings the magic, this grade is an excellent middle path between value and playability.

> Quick tip for buyers: check the card edges for whitening, feel the box lid for any warping, and do a quick shuffle check to catch any mis-cut or warped cards. You don’t want a moment of “the card is stuck” during a critical moment of the storytelling tournament.

### How Dixit Plays (A Quick Mechanic Primer)
If you’ve never played Dixit, imagine a storytelling game where the storyteller selects a card that hints at a clue and says a sentence or phrase that describes it—without naming the card outright. The other players pick a card from their hand that might match that clue and place it face down. The chosen cards are shuffled and revealed, and players secretly vote on which card they think was the storyteller’s. Scoring: if the majority or minority players guess the storyteller’s card, the storyteller earns points; if everyone or no one guesses correctly, the storyteller loses some points. The tension comes from balancing a clue that’s too obvious (everyone chooses it) with one that’s too obscure (no one picks it). It’s a game about guessing intent and shared imagination, which sounds simple until your friends start retrieving memories you didn’t know they had and your clue becomes a cosmic misdirection that defies logic.

Playtime varies with number of players, but a common sweet spot is 4–6 players. Any fewer and the bluffing dynamics dim; any more and it becomes a boisterous storytelling parade that may require extra rounds of breathing exercises to prevent chaos. The 1st Edition base box accommodates this range gracefully with a compact set of 84 cards that are easy to shuffle, deal, and appreciate visually during the storytelling arc.

### Components Durability and Card Stock: A Practical Look
The quality of the card stock in Dixit is a recurring topic among players who travel with their copies. The 1st Edition is generally robust, with thick cards that resist creasing under normal usage. In a VG+ NM copy, you’d expect the cards to show minimal whitening along the edges and no major scratches on the art surface. The linen-like finish often adds a tactile pleasure, making it satisfying to handle during those long storytelling sessions. That finish also helps with shuffle-ability; you want cards that can slide across the table without sticking or feeling second-hand.

One common issue with aging card games, especially those that are stored poorly or subjected to humidity changes, is edge wear and warping. Dixit cards are less prone to curling than some modern cardstock, but you should still store them flat and in a cool, dry place. A humid environment can cause slight swelling or stickiness of the card lamination, which is more of a nuisance than a fatal flaw. If your VG+ NM copy has minimal edge wear and the cards lie flat and clean, you’re in excellent shape to enjoy many more storytelling marathons.

### Keeping It Clean: Maintenance and Routine Care
- Regular dusting with a soft cloth to avoid sticky fingerprints on the card faces.
- A climate-controlled storage environment to prevent humidity-related issues.
- Card sleeves can preserve card art, but be mindful of sleeves that are too tight as they might cause wear on the edges.
- A box liner or small organizer helps you keep components in place during transport, which is especially valuable if you move between game nights or conventions.

A little care goes a long way in keeping the Dixit 1st Edition experience intact. The reward is a game that looks and feels as magical as the stories you’ll tell around the table.
