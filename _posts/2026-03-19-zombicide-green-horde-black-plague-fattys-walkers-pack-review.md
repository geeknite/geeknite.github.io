# Zombicide: Green Horde & Black Plague — Fattys & Walkers Pack Review

If you’ve wandered the post-apocalyptic stacks of your local game store and thought, “Man, I need more undead chaos in my life,” then the Fattys & Walkers Pack for Zombicide’s Green Horde and Black Plague campaigns might just be your next hobby-uplift. This is the kind of expansion that makes you mutter, “Okay, monster design 101, let’s add a bigger fish.” In this review we’ll dive into what the Fattys & Walkers Pack actually adds, how it meshes with both Green Horde and Black Plague, and whether it turns your cooperative nightmare into a glorious, chaotic symphony of dice and plastic limbs.

## Quick snapshot: what is Fattys & Walkers Pack?

The Fattys & Walkers Pack is a duo-packed expansion designed to bring two flavor profiles to Zombicide: the hulking, spongy-to-the-bones Fatty zombies and the swift, relentless Walkers that populate the late-game boards with a little more bite than your standard zombies. Think of Fattys as the heavyweight bout within your kitchen-table skirmish, and Walkers as the quick footwork that keeps you on your toes. If you’ve played any Zombicide content before, you know the routine: new zombie types, new spawn tokens, and a few new rules to keep you guessing who’s going to live to fight another round.

For Green Horde, the Fatty threat mixes beautifully with the horde-scouting, resource-gathering vibes of wild forest raids and green-tinged necromancy. For Black Plague, the Fatty threat slides into crowded plague-brooks with a sense of brutal inevitability, forcing you to choreograph your team’s movements with a little more ballet and a lot more coordination. If you’re a fan of “hurry up and wait” or “boss fight at every corner,” this pack is basically a love letter to those vibes.

## What’s in the box? A quick inventory list (spoiler-light)

While the official packaging includes a handful of punchy plastic perkin—Fatty miniatures, Walker zombies, and a handful of new standees and tokens—the real value lies in how these elements interact with both games. Here’s the mental model you should carry to the table:

- Fatty miniatures: big, tough, likely to soak a punch or two before shredding. They’re the kind of enemy that makes you rethink your weapon choice mid-round. They also come with separate bases and cards to track their health, reach, and special abilities.
- Walker zombies: the classic “fast enough to threaten flanks” unit. They provide the meat-and-potatoes of zombie hordes, with a slightly more urgent pace than your standard greens or blacks.
- New tokens and scenario-sparking cards: these are designed to tilt the balance without breaking the core rules. You’ll notice new spawn cards that push fatty and walker waves into the flow of the game, creating that satisfying crunch as you clear space while a fatty lumbers toward your group.
- A handful of art mats and reference sheets: these aren’t just pretty pictures. They’re the kind of quick-glance helpers that stop you from arguing about rules while the Fatty points its mass toward your healer.

If you’re looking for a pure number-crunch expansion, you’ll find plenty of numbers to crunch. If you prefer savoring the narrative of each zombie encounter, you’ll find a fresh flavor to riff on. It’s not a flimsy “new toy” bundle; it’s designed to load up the late game with meaningful decisions rather than just more noise.

## The design engine: how Fatty and Walker interactions play with Green Horde and Black Plague

Zombicide’s core vibe is cooperative chaos with a dash of tactical torchlight. The Fattys & Walkers Pack doesn’t rewrite the rules; it reframes them with a different tempo. Here are the key interaction points you’ll feel at the table:

- Fatty mechanics: These big guys are built to be a problem solver for your opponents. They soak hits, threaten multiple Survivors, and demand you rethink line-of-sight and melee reach. In practice, you’ll want to coordinate teams so someone is always drawing threat, while a second group picks at the fatty’s weaker angles. The payoff? A dramatic moment when your group finally coaxes the fatty into a trap, and you feel like you just beat a boss without using a cheat code.
- Walker dynamics: Walkers keep the pressure on without instantly devolving into chaos. They’re fast enough to threaten flanks and corners, but not so fast that they invalidate careful planning. The balance is delicate: you want them to feel dangerous but not so genocidal that you abandon thoughtful play in favor of mashing the “roll” button.
- Green Horde synergy: The Green Horde section tends to reward clever positioning and board control. Fattys in a forest-laden map can become a walking fortress if you let them clog chokepoints. The walkers can exploit that same terrain to create double-angled forks that split your team like a bad sci-fi budget film, which is exactly the kind of tension a good Zombicide session craves.
- Black Plague synergy: In Black Plague settings, the pack pushes you toward a more aggressive, “zone control” mindset. Fattys turn a corridor into a choke-point, and walkers feed off a crowding mechanic that makes you regret all those fancy area-control tokens you packed. The net effect is a reminder that even on “easy” setups, the board can go from manageable to “oh no” in a heartbeat.

Put simply: Fattys demand that you continuously re-evaluate your plan every few seconds, and Walkers force you to watch your backline. The combination is a masterclass in how to make a co-op feel like a tense sprint rather than a boring board-room meeting.

## The play experience: setup, first turns, and what to expect

If you’ve got your players dialed in, Fattys & Walkers slides into a normal Zombicide session with a few notable rhythm shifts:

- Setup: You’ll shuffle in the new zombies and tokens with the usual fanfare. The board will feel a little heavier, and the fatigue of fat walkers will be a mood you’ll have to accept. If you’re using a modular map, anticipate longer setup as you align fatty spawn zones and walk a few extra steps to chase the one fatty who decides to hide behind a ruin.
- First turns: Expect a few surprised screams and some friendly muttered curses as the first fatty makes a beeline toward your strongest fighter. If you’re playing Green Horde, your best bet is to funnel walkers into chokepoints you’ve already cleared and use terrain to your advantage. If you’re playing Black Plague, you’ll want to push the fatty into a trap with a heavy-use terrain feature and coordinate a quick burst from your ranged Survivors.
- Mid-game: The real meat hits in the mid-game as Fattys start to link up with multiple Walker waves. You’ll see players reading each other’s minds, trading last-resort items, and suddenly valuing a steel door over a long-range crossbow. The goal isn’t to win every fight; it’s to keep the board state under a manageable umbrella while slowly whittling down the pool of threats.
- Endgame: If you’ve been clever, you’ll be left with a handful of Survivors and a final wave that feels like the last boss in a classic RPG. The Fatty’s dramatic charge and a final walker flurry can be an all-or-nothing moment that either salvages a heroic victory or ends in a glorious, cinematic wipe. Either way, you’ll remember the session for the storytelling alone.

The pacing hinges on how well you manage threats, not just how you roll dice. The Fattys & Walkers Pack rewards players who communicate, who use terrain like a second character, and who prune their risk-calculus to preserve the group — not just chase glory with a single, flashy assault.

## Strategy and tactics: how to survive and maybe even dominate

If you’re serious about maximizing fun (and survival), here are a few time-tested strategies that emerge from multiple Fatty + Walker sessions:

- Spread the threat: Don’t let a fatty become a rock in the middle of your map. Force it to move with a decoy, then collapse on it with your strongest melee group. The trick is to keep at least two Survivors free to respond to walker flanks.
- Terrain is your ally: Use doors, walls, and elevation to funnel walkers into crossfire lanes. A fatty trapped between two walls can buy you a round or two for free, which means fewer headshots of your reputation.
- The ranged advantage shift: Ranged Survivors shine here, but they’re not invincible. Use them to peel off walkers before a fatty reaches your melee lines. You’ll maximize the lifespan of your squad and minimize last-day heroics.
- Resource management: Fattys demand a little extra attention on your item economy. You’ll want to allocate a few high-value weapons to your strongest fighters and save some tokens for healing or revives. If you blow everything in the first couple of rounds, you’ll be watching the fatty parade from the sidelines.
- Team synergy: The pack rewards players who plan together. Don’t split your team into two isolated groups. Fatty charge paths are easiest to defend when you’ve got a coordinated plan and a backup plan, preferably with a plan C for when all your plans fall apart.

If you’re new to Zombicide, don’t panic. The learning curve is there, but the payoff is in the small victories — the moment you pin a fatty behind a ruined gate or you survive a walker swarm with a single heroic swing. If you’re a veteran, you’ll appreciate the extra tempo and the new mental map you must create to stay ahead of the board state.

## Component quality and aesthetics: the good, the chunky, and the pretty

The Fattys & Walkers Pack doesn’t rely on fancy paint jobs to justify its existence; instead, it leans into the tactile joy of better miniatures and intuitive tokens. The Fatty models are satisfyingly chunky, the Walkers have enough variety to feel “new” but not so many that you’ll spend hours trying to differentiate them on a crowded board. The plastic quality sits comfortably in the “sturdy enough to survive a table’s worth of clumsy pizza slices” tier. The artwork on the new cards remains consistent with the Zombicide vibe: grim, a little comic, and always clear enough to read in low light after your fourth round of breakdowns and remakes.

The extra board overlays and tokens do their job without becoming a riddle. If you’ve ever worried about expansions becoming cluttered, this one keeps a neat balance between “new for the sake of new” and “new that actually affects how you play.” It’s the kind of packaging you’re happy to bring to a friend’s house for a full, sweaty, spaghetti-bottle night of gaming.

## Balancing act: is Fattys & Walkers Pack worth it for Green Horde, Black Plague, or both?

Short answer: yes, but with caveats. The pack shines most when you’re playing with both campaigns in rotation or as part of a long-term campaign that really leans into endurance and boss-mode vibes. If you only dabble in Zombicide and never commit to longer sessions, you might not feel the full payoff because the Fatty + Walker tension reveals itself best when you’re already deep in a couple of multi-hour quests.

Long answer: Green Horde players get a distinct flavor of forest-tinged dread, where Fattys threaten your caravan routes and walkers weave through trees in a way that makes your line of sight a little less predictable. Black Plague players get that brutal, cramped-catacomb energy; the Fatty acts as a mobile siege engine that can break a defensive line if you’re not careful. The dual-campaign approach ensures the pack remains versatile: you can drop Fatty waves into a single mission or scatter them across a longer arc for a sustained challenge.

If you’re a completionist, there’s the extra value of new narrative beats to mine for your campaign log. If you’re a casual gamer who just wants a couple of thrilling nights with friends, you’ll still get a strong “wow, that was close” moment in your very first Fatty encounter. It’s not merely “more zombies”; it’s more storytelling per turn, more tension per decision, and more reasons to beg your dice to bless your next swing.

## Final thoughts and recommendations

The Fattys & Walkers Pack is a welcome bump in the right direction for Zombicide’s Green Horde and Black Plague ecosystems. It doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it sharpens it. You’ll find more tension, more decision points, and yes, more moments where you’ll want to hug your survivors and also mutter a very ominous swear about that fatty whose reach somehow covers half the board.

Pro-level takeaway:
- If you love tight co-op pacing with a dash of boss-like pressure, this pack is a valuable addition.
- If you’re a fan of both campaigns and you enjoy a steady drip of new threat variety, you’ll get excellent mileage out of Fattys & Walkers.
- If you’re more into “light-hearted, casual play,” you’ll still enjoy the chaos but you may want to pair it with shorter scenarios to avoid commitment-fatigue.

Bottom line: Fattys & Walkers Pack elevates the moment-to-moment tension without breaking the game’s rhythm. It’s a solid, thematically rich expansion that pays off in memorable tabletop stories, especially when you’ve assembled a crew that communicates, improvises, and laughs in the face of imminent zombification.
