## Cleaning and Maintenance Tips
### Daily Care
Vacuum regularly to remove dust and loose debris. A simple routine keeps the fibers looking fresh and reduces the chance of dirt building up in the pattern. If you own pets, consider brushing the rug gently to loosen fur before vacuuming. This small step lasts longer than a full deep clean and makes the vacuum work easier.

### Stains and Spot Cleaning
For spills, blot first with a clean cloth rather than rubbing. Rubbing can push stain deeper into the fibers and extend the life of the problem. Use a mild solution of detergent and water, test a small inconspicuous area first, and then proceed to treat the rest. Avoid harsh chemicals that can fade the fibers or alter the color balance of ivory and aqua. After spot cleaning, allow the area to air dry completely before walking on it again to prevent re-soiling or streaks.

### Deep Cleaning and Professional Care
If you want to refresh the rug beyond routine spot cleaning, consider a professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months depending on traffic and exposure to spills. A professional service can refresh the pile and bring back that just-installed look without risking damage to the color or backing. For DIY deep cleaning, a low moisture method is recommended to preserve the backing and avoid warping. The key is to avoid over-wetting and to ensure thorough drying before you return to normal use.
