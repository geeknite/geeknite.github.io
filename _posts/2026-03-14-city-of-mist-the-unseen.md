## Introduction
City of Mist: The Unseen lands with a murmur in the alleys of noir myth and spreadsheet dragons. If you thought you had seen every way to punch ghosts with neon lighting, The Unseen swan-dives in with a grin and a few more hints of what you thought this was. In this review we are going to break down what's new, what's improved, and what remains delightfully ridiculous. We'll examine how The Unseen expands the base game, what it asks of players, and how it might reshape campaigns for both new players and veterans. Expect a lot of meta jokes, a few underhanded puns, and a running tally of how many times you can say the word mythic without triggering a fatigue demon.

We begin with the premise: City of Mist is not about superheroes as you remember them; it's about myths and legends wearing trench coats, walking the street, and trying to pay rent. The Unseen leans into that concept, focusing on the hidden currents: the undercities where the masks slip or stay on through sheer stubbornness. It is a book about the unseen actors behind the scenes — the pimps, angels, and rival gods of paperwork, all trying to get a good seat at the cosmic cabaret.

The tone remains unmistakably City of Mist: a noir narrative mixed with mythic resonance. But The Unseen adds a few new verbs to your verb—exposure, infiltration, and improvisation. The players can now chase rumors through back-alley synthwave, quickly pivot from pulp fiction to mythic investigation, and occasionally punch a god in the jaw with nothing but a clever line and a well-timed metaphor.

## Box contents and production value

### Box contents
The Unseen arrives in a sturdy, slightly smug box that looks like it was designed by someone who spent too long at a coffee shop sketching ancient sigils with a wax pencil. Inside you’ll find a core rulebook, a handful of chapter cards, a master index to the new arcs, and a laminated GM screen with a wealth of quick references. The dice, a beloved staple of City of Mist, are still tiny cousin-sized dice that users will either adore or resent as a reminder of the time they rolled three sixes and a narrative beat at the wrong moment.

### Art direction
The art direction continues to lean into the hybrid of noir and myth. The Unseen features more urban grunge aesthetic, with splash panels that feel like a dream sequence stitched into a police body cam. The colors lean into midnight blues and electric magentas, a palette that says we are serious about secrets but also very serious about style.

### Components
The boards and cards are of solid stock, not the flimsy stuff some publishers get away with. The gloss has a protective feel, and the typography is a joy for eye-nerds who like to pore over a font like a fortune cookie. The GM screen is sturdy enough to stand up to a marathon session; it also comes with a few one-liners you can drop on players when a scene stagnates.

## Mechanics and System

### The Unseen traits
The core system remains: players embody myths in a modern city by using the four apocryphal masks of City of Mist—the Legend, the Mystery, and the Emblem (some sections add fresh subtypes to these categories). The Unseen introduces new traits that revolve around the hidden networks: messenger routes, rumor theorems, and the delicate art of misdirection. The dice mechanic still rewards fragile balance between what characters want and what the city thinks of them.

### Combat and action economy
If you liked the original burn-and-bend approach to fights, you’ll feel right at home. The Unseen adds a few options for nonviolent conflict, including social maneuvering, staged confrontations, and a few spectacular misdirections that can end fights before they begin. There’s a nice emphasis on narrative pacing: a single scene can transition from a tense negotiation to a memory reel of the city’s past tragedies, with players deciding who gets to speak last and why.

### The unseen angles
Where the expansion truly shines is in its insertion of the unseen angles: back-alley markets, secret networks, and mythic subcultures that move beneath the city’s skin. The Unseen doesn’t just add new tools for the pocketed player; it adds a new set of problems for the GM—diplomatic assassins of rumor, a city that has memory and will, and a hubris-driven antagonist that isn’t villainous in the old sense, but bureaucratic in the most chilling manner.

## Setting and Theme

### The city and its myths
The city remains the stage on which legends put on their disguises. The Unseen leans into the idea that the city is a living organism that thrives on secrets, leaks, and whispered promises. The new material invites players to explore how lore stabilizes or destabilizes the city’s power balance: who benefits when a rumor becomes a law, and who pays when a myth becomes a crime.

### Tone and narrative potential
Tone wise, The Unseen preserves City of Mist’s signature vibe: stylish, slightly ridiculous, and deeply sincere about its own lore. The new chapters encourage longer arcs, but they also reward smaller, sharper sessions that feel like a film noir vignette where the hero can tilt the entire plot with a single clever line. It’s a game that rewards players who enjoy pouring metaphor into the world and watching it harden into tangible consequences.

## Gameplay Experience

### Solo play vs group play
The Unseen scales gracefully to both solo and group play, though the latter tends to deliver more of the chaotic fun when everyone buys into the idea that mythic forces are just as petty as office politics. Solo play works particularly well when you want to explore a city’s secret history and test how one person’s choices ripple through decades of rumors.

### GM tips
A GM should prepare three things: a rumor seed, a counter-myth, and a gate that leads somewhere unexpectedly. The Unseen rewards improvisation: you should be ready to pivot when a player’s plan reveals an entirely different layer of the city’s mythic network. For the GM, it’s a performance to hold—your job is to keep the city’s contradictions in motion while the players chase a piece of a puzzle that keeps moving.

### Accessibility
The rules at this point are still approachable for beginners who have read through the base game’s core concepts. The Unseen is not a mass of new rules so much as a reshaping of emphasis: a new toolkit, not a brand new operating system. The book includes example scenes and ready-to-run micro-arcs that help a brand-new group jump into a campaign without needing to invent a city from scratch.

## Campaign ideas and hooks

### One-shots in different neighborhoods
One strong concept: run a city-wide rumor that threads through three neighborhoods with distinct vibes—industrial district with neon smoke, a historical quarter with ghostly facades, and a waterfront zone where myth and commerce mingle. The players must decide which rumor is the truth and which rumor is a trap.

### Long-term arcs
For longer campaigns, consider a central conflict between the city’s memory and its forgetting function. Perhaps someone has learned to erase messages from mythic archives; or a group of unseen players uses rumor to rewrite a city’s past in real time. The Unseen is a lens that makes the city feel alive by letting players watch the city’s skeleton shift when certain legends rise into power.

## Comparisons with base City of Mist

### What Unseen adds
The expansion adds more ways to interact with the city’s myths, with a particular emphasis on social intrigue and behind-the-scenes power plays. It doesn’t totally reinvent the mechanic; rather it refocuses the glass to show how myth and urban life collide in more everyday settings.

### What Unseen takes away
If you hoped to sprint through a cinematic chase with minimal friction, you may find a few constraints in The Unseen that remind you to slow down and savor the storytelling. The added complexity is not heavy-handed; rather it nudges the narrative toward more relational play between characters and factions.

## Components and production value revisited

### Card quality and layout
In addition to what you would expect from City of Mist, The Unseen provides a new set of cards that elegantly pair with the new traits. The layout is clean and legible; typographic choices help to ensure that the lines between a character’s real-world actions and their myths remain legible.

### Book design
The core rulebook that ships with The Unseen is deeply readable; it’s the kind of book you want to dog-ear for quick inspiration. The writing is witty and well-paced, balancing the game’s heavier themes with occasional wry remarks from the city.

Fans have responded with a flurry of memes and panel art celebrating the city’s new neighborhoods. It’s the sort of product that invites fan art, cosplay, and speculative theories about what the city is up to behind those glowing rain-slicked streets.

## Final thoughts and recommendation

In a field crowded with expansions that feel like they were designed by someone who read a wiki page about mystery novels, City of Mist: The Unseen manages to feel both familiar and fresh. It respects the original’s strengths—narrative emphasis, mythic flavor, and the sense that the city is a living partner in the story—while adding a new set of tools that reward players who love detail, rumor-mongering, and improvisation. If you’re a GM who wants to run a campaign that moves with a sly, almost conspiratorial speed, this expansion offers a lot of value. If you’re a player who loves the feeling of uncovering a circle of conspiracies that are larger than your character, The Unseen gives you more anchors to cling to and more opportunities to feel clever.

If you already own City of Mist and enjoy playing it with friends, The Unseen will taste like a new peppermint in the same mug. It’s not a reimagining of the core system; it’s more like someone gave you a fresh bag of spices for a familiar dish. You can add it gradually, test the balance in a few sessions, and watch your city warp in satisfying ways.

If you’re new to City of Mist, this expansion may feel a little intimidating after the base book—though the new chapters include enough example scenes to get you started quickly. It’s a good next step for groups who want to move beyond a straightforward hero-narrative into something more intricate and layered, where every choice echoes through rumor networks and immortal silhouettes.

In sum, City of Mist: The Unseen is a welcome addition that respects the original game’s charm while pushing the storytelling envelope. It is a good buy for players who enjoy the subtle, stylish tension of a city that hides more secrets than there are coffee shops in your hometown, and it’s a solid entry point for new players who want to nibble on a more substantial bite than a one-shot.

Now, if you’re reading this and thinking, how do I use The Unseen to run a campaign that feels cinematic and personal at the same time? The answer is simple: prepare a set of three anchored rumors, a handful of visible-versus-hidden NPCs with conflicting goals, and a city map that looks indistinguishable from a rain-soaked rumor board. Then let the players decide which rumor to chase, which faction to side with, and which truth becomes the group’s guiding myth. The city will respond accordingly, bending to the power of narrative and a few well-timed dramatic pauses.

## Final recommendation

If you crave urban myth in the style of a neon-lit crime thriller with a wink and a sly smile, City of Mist: The Unseen deserves a spot on your shelf. It has the same pulse as the base game, but with more texture, more layers, and more ways to bring a story to life through rumor and memory. It is a strong choice for groups that enjoy long-form storytelling and the chance to develop a city’s mythology as an ongoing character.
