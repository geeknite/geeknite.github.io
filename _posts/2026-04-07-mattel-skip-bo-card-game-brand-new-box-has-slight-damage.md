## How to play in 10 sentences or less (for the busy friend)
Skip-Bo centers on shedding your stock pile by playing cards onto center building piles in ascending order from 1 to 12. You can use your hand cards or the top card of your stock pile for play, and you can also use Skip-Bo wild cards as any number. On your turn you can place as many playable cards as possible onto the building piles, and you draw back up to a five-card hand if you end your turn with playable moves. The first player to empty their stock pile wins the round. Simple enough to teach in a single sitting, and with enough variants to keep a recurring game night lively.

## Strategy and pacing: reading the table like a pro
Skip-Bo rewards players who balance speed with caution. Here are a few core strategies that often separate the newbies from the seasoned table commanders:

- Build piles early, but don’t rush to the top if you don’t have a safe way back. The middle game is where you shape tempo; a misplayed card can give your foe a chance to sprint ahead.
- Use wild Skip-Bo cards strategically. They’re your flexible friends, but you only want to burn them when you have a concrete path to finishing your stock pile, not when you’re stuck with a lot of unknowns.
- Pay attention to your opponents’ discards and the visible stock piles. If you notice a pattern, you can anticipate their likely next moves and tailor your plays to disrupt or outpace them.
- Create a clear endgame plan. The moment you glimpse a clean route to empty your stock pile, do not hesitate to commit. The table’s attention and your own momentum will carry you forward.
- Adapt to player count. In larger groups, the pace can accelerate quickly. In smaller groups, there’s more mental pressure to optimize every turn. Adjust your strategies accordingly.
