## The Two New Survivors: Gilbert and Mortimer
Gilbert and Mortimer bring two very different playstyles to the table, which is the dream team you didn’t know you needed until you tried. Gilbert tends to be the more resilient, defensive character—think a stompy knight who can shrug off a couple of zombie bites while your ranged teammates set up the crossfire. Mortimer, by contrast, leans into versatility—he’s the type who can pivot his actions to cover gaps, exploit openings in the zombie wave, and occasionally pull off a risk-laden play that pays off when you really need it.

From a design perspective, having two distinct personalities in one expansion often yields the most fun. It allows players to experiment with team composition and to talk themselves (and their friends) into 20-minute debates about who should take the lead on a given round. The interactions between Gilbert’s durability and Mortimer’s adaptive toolkit become a microcosm of the tabletop’s social dynamics: you’re balancing risk, resource management, and the occasional non-player character’s influence on the squad’s morale.

If you enjoy meta-driving decisions, you’ll appreciate how these two characters can shape mission pacing. Gilbert might be the anchor, soaking up damage and drawing aggro, while Mortimer darts around with clever card plays to prune the horde in opportunistic bursts. The dynamic creates memorable moments, like turning a tight turn into a near-miss victory and somehow surviving with a hilariously small number of healing tokens left. It’s campy, it’s dramatic, and it’s very Zombicide in spirit.
