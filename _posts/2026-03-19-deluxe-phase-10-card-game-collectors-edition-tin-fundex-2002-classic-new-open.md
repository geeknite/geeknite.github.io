## Build Quality and Condition

### New Open – What does it mean?

New Open means the box was opened; the contents may be pristine, or they may carry the gentle wear that years of storage produce. It’s a status that appeals to collectors who want the look and feel of a new-to-them item without paying the premium for a factory seal. If you’re comfortable with that, this edition offers a nice balance of nostalgia and functionality. The exterior should still exude that late-night shopping impulse you had when you found a box with bold colors and the promise of hours of lighthearted competition.

### Condition assessment and buyer tips

- Inspect tin corners for dents and rust; the metal can age gracefully, but you want it to be structurally sound.
- Check the lid’s alignment; a misaligned lid can signify deeper storage issues.
- Look at the card edges for whitening or curling. Edge wear is a common sign of age but should be minimal in a New Open item.
- Confirm that the rulebook and scoring pad are present and legible. A missing insert or heavily creased rulebook can dampen the value.
- If possible, request clear photos of the interior, especially the deck; a complete 108-card deck is the practical baseline here.
