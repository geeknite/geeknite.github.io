## What is the Steya Pure Wool Hand Tufted Rug?
This rug is a product of the classic hand tufting technique, where loops of wool are pushed through a base fabric to create a dense, plush surface. The result is a surface that feels substantial underfoot yet pliant enough to yield to your foot with a satisfying give. The wool itself is natural fiber, renowned for its resilience, insulating properties, and ability to recover after compression. In plain terms: it feels great, behaves well, and ages gracefully if cared for properly.

### Materials and construction
Steya uses pure wool sourced with care and attention to dye stability. The fibers are long, which reduces pilling and improves durability. The hand tufted method means a human artisan creates each column of loops by hand, then the pile is sheared to a uniform height. This is not machine-made sameness; you are getting a rug that has a tiny fingerprint in its texture, a micro-heroic tale told by the craftsman who touched every inch.

In terms of density, the Steya rug sits in that sweet spot where it feels plush enough for comfort, yet not so thick that you trip over it when you stand up to grab a snack during a late-night coding session. Wool fibers naturally resist staining, and their scales help wick away some minor spills. It is not miracle-proof, but it earns bonus points for being easier to spot-clean than a cotton rug after a pizza night.

### Hand tufted technique explained
Hand tufting starts with a backing material and a tufting gun or needle. The artisan pushes wool tufts through the base, creating loops that form the pile. After looping, the yarn is secured with a backing and often finished with a protective layer or binding along the edges. The result is a rug whose surface has dimension, a tactile texture that invites your fingers to explore. The Steya rug benefits from this technique by showing slight variations in pile height, which translates into depth and a more dynamic look under different lighting — perfect for geeks who enjoy seeing little details that shift as the sun moves across the room.

To give you a frame of reference, think of this as the textile version of a hand-assembled motherboard: meticulous, crafted, and a tad imperfect in the delightful way that only humans can achieve when they assemble something with care. If you are into precision engineering, you will notice the craftsmanship in the edges and the uniformity of the loops, and you will smile because those details matter.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
A good rug has a long life if you treat it with a healthy dose of common sense and a tiny bit of maintenance. The wool fiber is naturally resistant to staining, but nothing is stain-proof. The trick is to act promptly and use the right tools for the job.

### Everyday care
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers. Use a vacuum head without a beater bar on high traffic areas to protect the pile.
- Spot clean spills as soon as they occur. Blot with a clean cloth or paper towel; avoid rubbing, which can embed the stain deeper.
- For tougher spots, a mild wool-safe cleaner can be used, following the product directions. Always test an inconspicuous area first.

### Deep cleaning and professional care
Occasionally, a thorough clean may be warranted. A professional rug cleaner with wool expertise can refresh the fibers without damaging the backing. If you attempt a home wash, keep water temperatures modest and never wring the rug. Wool fibers hate that kind of drama.

### Stain resistance and ethics of care
Natural wool contains oils that help repel minor spills, but that does not give you a free pass for coffee disasters or red wine rituals of doom. Blot promptly, avoid harsh chemicals, and consider a stain protector designed for wool if you anticipate heavy spill-prone environments. If you are mindful of care, your Steya rug can stay vibrant for years, aging like a fine noodle—soft, comforting, and a little more textured with time.

## Sizing, Shapes, and Room Planning
Rug size and shape influence how space feels. Steya comes in multiple sizes to fit different room configurations. A rug that is too small will make a room feel unanchored; a rug that is too big can overwhelm the layout. The sweet spot is a rug that allows at least 18 inches of exposed floor around the edges under a typical sofa, or an entire seating area to sit comfortably on the rug with room to spare.

### Room setups and placement tips
- In a seating arrangement, place the front legs of sofas on the rug to unify the space. If you have a larger seating group, extend the rug under most of the furniture to create a continuous, cohesive zone.
- In smaller rooms, a smaller Steya rug can still manage to anchor the space if paired with a sofa or chair in a way that frames the seating area. The goal is balance, not border patrol.
- For open concept spaces, a bold Steya can serve as a visual divider, defining zones without the need for physical walls.

### Rug pads and installation basics
A rug pad is not optional—it's a feature. A good pad reduces slipping, protects the underside, and extends the life of the pile. Choose a pad with the right thickness for your floor type and its alignment with furniture weight. Non-slip pads work well on hard floors, while felt or rubber-bottom pads can offer extra grip under heavy traffic. A pad also helps reduce sound transmission and can improve overall comfort underfoot.
