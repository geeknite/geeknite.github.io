## What is BT01 Starter Deck?
BT01 Starter Deck is the inaugural preconstructed set in Dragon Ball Super Card Game’s early starter line. It’s designed to offer a self-contained experience: a ready-to-play deck that teaches the core mechanics while delivering that satisfying “snap, draw, attack” loop that fans adore. In practical terms, you get a main leader, a full deck of 50 cards, a handful of energy cards, and a few strategic tricks that help you get up to speed quickly without needing to attend a week-long boot camp for card-game enthusiasts.

This starter is meant for two things: learning the rhythm of the DBS Card Game and giving players a baseline to modify as they grow. You’ll likely swap in more optimized cards as you understand your preferred playstyle, whether you enjoy big play sequences with aggressive attackers or you’re more of a control mage who tries to outwait the opponent. BT01 won’t break the bank, but it does offer enough potential to feel like you’re getting more than “just the box.”

### The design philosophy behind BT01
The BT01 design ethos centers on accessibility and early-game tempo. It gives you a taste of energy acceleration, quick draws, and early threat application. The leader cards in BT01 typically provide a steady ramp or a reliable engine, so you can execute a couple of synergy lines without needing to pull the exact right 1- or 2-cost card in a critical moment. And yes, there is enough muscle in the deck to finish games before the clock runs out or before your opponent realizes you have been stacking your vanishing energy cards in a very niche way.

## How to play the BT01 Starter Deck
Playing BT01 is about learning a couple of core mechanics and then layering in your strategy as you gain experience. This section is designed to be beginner-friendly but also useful for a quick refresher if you haven’t touched the DBS Card Game in a while.

### Core mechanics you need to know
- Energy management: Your energy is a resource you’ll tap to play cards. You’ll want to maximize early tempo but avoid running out of gas in the late game.
- Leader ability synergy: The leader is your captain. Its effect often governs how aggressively you press or how defensively you trade.
- Attack and defend: Your attackers are your threat, while your blockers and removal help you survive long enough to land a bigger hit.
- Hand management: Cycling through your deck with draw effects, and knowing when to hold or discard, is crucial.

### The basic turn structure
1. Draw: You draw a card to refill your hand.
2. Energy: You may place cards into your energy zone according to your game plan.
3. Main phase: You summon units, cast spells, and attack. This is where you apply pressure.
4. End turn: You tidy up and stare down your opponent across the table as the clock begins its second hand.

This structure is intentionally straightforward, which makes BT01 a great vehicle for learning. You’ll nuance it with subtle decisions—the difference between tapping an energy to finish a blocker or saving it for a stronger next turn can swing a game.

### Beginner-friendly strategies for BT01
- Tempo rush: Play a couple of low-cost attackers quickly and force your opponent to spend resources dealing with them. This can create a tempo advantage that snowballs into a win before the late game arrives.
- Value trade: Use a mid-range unit to trade with a larger threat while preserving your own higher-threat cards for the pivotal moment.
- Early pressure with a trick: Use a draw engine to keep your hand fresh, then sneak a finisher card through when the opponent thinks you’ve slowed down.
- Resource denial: If your deck includes counter-mountains of energy denial or negation, lean on them to disrupt your opponent’s rhythm and set up a window for a clean kill.

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Overextending: Playing too many attackers into a single counter spell or removal window can leave you with nothing but a defeated turn and a sigh from your wallet.
- Mismanaging energy: Saving energy for a big finisher is smart until your opponent accelerates past you. It’s about timing and rhythm.
- Forgetting the win condition: Land a clear, credible threat and don’t forget to push for the finish when the window opens.

## A quick battle plan for BT01 beginners
Here’s a practical, no-nonsense blueprint you can try in your first couple of sessions:
- Turn 1–2: Hit energy efficiently, drop a safe blocker or a low-cost attacker, and start pressuring early.
- Turn 3–4: Use a draw engine to maintain card flow, while your lead attacker applies continuous pressure. If you have a clean finisher, set up the window to launch it.
- Turn 5+: If you’re still in the duel, position your deck to cash in on your strongest card at the right moment. Don’t oversell it too early—quality over flash wins the long game.
- Post-game: Staple a quick debrief with your opponent about what worked, what didn’t, and what you’d tweak in a future BT01 rebuild. This is not only good practice but it also makes you feel like a responsible card-game human being.

BT01 shines as a base, and the real fun starts when you tailor it to your playstyle. Here are a few avenues you might explore:
- Upgrade core engines: If you love the tempo play, you might swap in a couple of more aggressive attackers or a board-control spell to keep the opponent’s options in check.

Remember that a good BT01 upgrade path is not about throwing everything at the problem; it’s about incremental improvements that align with your preferred tempo. If you like a longer, grindy game, focus on draw and sustain. If you prefer a fast, “kill the opponent on turn 3” plan, add efficient early threats and a precise finisher.
