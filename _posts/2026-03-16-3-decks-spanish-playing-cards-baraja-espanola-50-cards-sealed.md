## Practical Handling: Shuffling, Cutting, and Card Care
### Shuffling Big Decks
Shuffling 150 cards is a test of stamina and technique. You’ll want a couple of good shuffles per round, and you’ll probably adopt a hybrid approach: a riffle shuffle for a big pile, followed by a gentle overhand or pile shuffle to mix the remainders. If you have shy hands or a tendency to sneeze when a card slips, consider a table with a soft surface to reduce bounce and noise. It’s not a crisis, but it’s a tiny art form—like choreographing a dance with 150 tiny, stubborn dancers.

### Cutting and Deck Management
With three separate decks, it’s practical to keep each deck distinct during the early rounds. You can label the decks with small stickers or simply instruct your players to keep card decks separate until the round ends. Once you get into the groove, you may opt for a combined deck or a rotating cut to keep everyone on their toes. The key is to avoid the “Where did half the deck go?” chaos that happens when you forget you mixed decks 1, 2, and 3 mid-round.

### Card Care and Longevity
The finish feels durable enough for a few dozen nights of heavy play. A mild linen or semi-gloss finish helps with handling; it’s not so slippery that you’ll host a card-slipping contest, but it’s not rough enough to abrade your fingertips after back-to-back games. Clean up spills promptly with a dry cloth. If you’re dealing with juice or soda, a quick wipe and air-dry should do. And if you’re worried about warping in humid environments, consider a dedicated card mat or a small rack to keep the stack crisp and ready for action.
