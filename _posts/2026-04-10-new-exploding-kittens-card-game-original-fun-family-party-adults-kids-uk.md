## How to play: the quick-start guide

Exploding Kittens is a take-turns, draw-and-play card game with a few special cards that spice things up. The UK edition adds some local flair and a couple of mechanics to keep players on their toes. Here’s the quick-start sketch:

1) Shuffle the deck and deal a few cards to each player. The exact number is in the rulebook, but in practice you’ll typically start with 4 or 5 cards depending on the table size.
2) On your turn, you play as many cards as you like from your hand, following the card-specific instructions. But the clock is ticking, because you must draw one card at the end of your turn.
3) If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re not out of the round yet—unless you don’t have a Defuse card or the right counter to survive the blast. Defuse cards give you a way to avoid the explosion, often with a stylish flourish.
4) If you play a Defuse and survive, you can restore your life by putting the Exploding Kitten back into the deck in a place of your choosing (mindfully, of course) to set up future suspense.
5) The round continues until all but one player remain holding it together. That lone victor claims the glory and, in some households, the last slice of pizza.

What makes the UK version sing is the balance of speed and strategy. If you’re quick on your feet, you’ll chain Defuses and sabotage, hampering your opponents with a wink and a nudge. If you’re slow to react, you’ll watch the deck run dry and your friends celebrate victory with a chorus of triumphant giggles.

## Strategy, luck, and how to tilt the odds in your favor

Like any party card game, Exploding Kittens blends luck with a touch of strategic misdirection. You don’t have to be a brainiac to enjoy it, but a little strategic thinking goes a long way. Here are some tips to edge out your rivals:

- Read the room: In a family setting, people often reveal patterns in how they react to kit or defuse cards. If someone overplans, you can use a quick-fire sequence of cards to surprise them and steal the momentum.
- Save your Defuse cards for the right moment: It’s tempting to play Defuse early, but you’ll want to preserve this lifeline for a critical moment when a high-stake draw looms.
- Use explosive cards tactically: Some cards modify how the deck behaves. Use them to scramble the order or to force someone else to draw at the wrong time.
- Control the tempo: The UK edition encourages a friendly pace. If you drag things, the game can feel long; if you rush, you risk giving away your strategy. Find your rhythm, then break it with style.

In practice, the most satisfying wins come from moments when you contrive a near-miss that has everyone gasping, then pivot to celebrate with a ridiculous victory dance. You know the one—arms flailing, stomping your foot, and a victory grin that says you just pulled off a legendary play without even needing to cheat (hypothetically).
