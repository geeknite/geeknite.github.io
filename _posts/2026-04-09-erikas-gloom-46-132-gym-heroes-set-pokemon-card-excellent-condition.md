## Grading and Condition: What Does Excellent Really Mean?

Here’s the heart of the matter: “Excellent condition” is not the same as “mint condition.” It’s a label that addresses surface wear and the wear-and-tear of the binder-life. For a Gym Heroes Erika’s Gloom, excellent typically means a few minor edge nicks, no heavy scuffs, and centering that is roughly acceptable (though a little to the left or right is common for older prints). The problem with older cards is that some corners are more brittle than your sense of humor during a Pokemon stadium finals.

### How to Assess Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface

- Centering: True centering is perfect; a slight tilt is acceptable. If the card seems to lean toward the left edge by more than a couple of millimeters, you’re looking at a condition grade that could dip into “Very Good” territory.
- Corners: Corner wear is the giveaway. A corner that looks more rounded than a jelly donut isn’t a good sign for a near-mint grade. In “Excellent” you can expect slight rounding on one or two corners.
- Edges: A thin white border around the card is normal for older prints; check for chipping along edges.
- Surface: Scratches, print lines, or scuffs on the holo foil (if printed) or the card face will degrade the condition drastically. In Erika’s Gloom, the surface should be smooth with no major scratches that would distract from the art.

## Preservation and Display Tips

- Sleeves first, of course: 2.5 mil or 4 mil top-loaders protect the edges and keep the face clean from curious coffee spills.
- Binders and page layout: Use a binder with acid-free pages and avoid heavy plastic that could cause stickiness over time. A ghostly acid-free sleeve will protect from the long-term yellowing that 90s plastics can bring.
- Environment: Keep away from direct sunlight; you don’t want Erika’s Gloom turning into a saffron-tinted memory. Store in cool, dry conditions.
