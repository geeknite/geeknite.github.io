## Gameplay Deep Dive
### Core Loop and Mechanics
Mind MGMT is a deduction-based, role-interaction-heavy experience. One of the central ideas is to manage information while simultaneously trying to glean information from other players about their alignments, intentions, and faked loyalties. The core loop typically involves: assign roles secretly, reveal or bluff as you move through a number of rounds, collect or spend resources to influence outcomes, and finally determine who among the players is aligned with the Mind MGMT agency and who are the sleepers or double agents.

On each turn, you will perform actions that can reveal a little more about a rival, plant a misdirection, or take a strategic pivot if the information on the table shifts unexpectedly. The game rewards attentive players who can juggle multiple data points without losing their thread of logic. The hidden information aspect means that every round you must decide whether to push information you hold or protect it to avoid tipping your hand too early. The tension comes from balancing risk and reward as you try to assemble a coherent picture from scattered clues while others actively try to obscures yours.

### Roles and Hidden Identities
A big hook is the variety of roles you can assume or deduce. Each role comes with distinct abilities or actions that can tilt the board in subtle ways. The balance is crucial: if every role felt identical, the game would drift into the land of pure logic pings, but Mind MGMT preserves flavor by ensuring different roles interact in ways that feel thematic and fun rather than punitive. The English edition preserves these identities with clean, readable text, so you aren’t squinting at a symbol deck while someone is bluffing you with a perfectly timed truth grenade.

The sleeping agents, the field operatives, and the hidden moles each carry a different weight on your decision-making scale. You can often sense which information is safe to reveal and which you should bury, a dynamic that creates memorable negotiation moments and occasional playful confrontations at the table. It is not a party game in the sense of quick rounds and big laughter; it is a social puzzle that demands concentration, a willingness to bluff with style, and a tolerance for polite paranoia.
