## Maintenance and Cleaning
Maintenance is the practical, non-sexy side of rug ownership, and Kendall Oatmeal makes it approachable. Regular vacuuming keeps dirt from embedding in the fibers, especially in the loop sections where crumbs like to hide. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible. Avoid aggressive scrubbing; the fibers respond better to a light touch that preserves the integrity of the weave.

Stain treatment should be mild and targeted. A gentle detergent solution applied with a soft cloth is usually enough for most household mishaps. For more persistent stains, a dedicated rug cleaner or a steam-cleaning cycle works well, followed by a thorough drying period to prevent any damp smell from developing. If you have kids who love paint, crayons, or pizza sauce, you’ll appreciate a spot-treatment routine you can apply without needing a forensic science kit.

Rotating the rug every few months helps distribute wear evenly and reduces the risk of certain sections becoming more worn than others. If you have a dog that loves to bask in the sun, you might notice color differences between the sunlit side and the rest. That’s normal and easily managed with a rotation schedule and a bit of sun exposure on the opposite side to balance the fade.

Seasonal care tips: during the humid months, keep the rug dry to prevent musty odors. A quick vacuum plus a few hours of air-drying in a sunny spot can keep things fresh. In dry winter climates, consider a light anti-static spray if your space tends to crackle with static electricity; just test first in a small corner to avoid any shiny surprises.
