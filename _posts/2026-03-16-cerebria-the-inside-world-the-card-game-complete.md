## Gameplay: Flow and Feel
This is where Cerebria the Card Game shows its steel nerves. The board game is all about big mood shifts, epic tactics, and long conficts. A typical session alternates between players drawing, playing cards, and resolving actions that push influence across regions. Each round ends with a mood phase where the board tracks update—think of it as the inner world sighing contentedly after a quarrel or wailing in protest when you step on its philosophical toes.

### Card types and actions
- Power cards: direct actions that modify the balance of influence, damage rivals, or unlock temporary powers that exist only for that turn
- Mood cards: add flavor and subtle shifts to how regions behave; they often affect the endgame scoring in clever ways
- Event cards: occasional global shoves that remind you that the world inside Cerebria can suddenly throw a curveball your way

### Scoring and endgame
You score by controlling regions of the inner world and by triggering events tied to your egoic narrative. The card game rewards timing and crowd-pleasing combos more than brute force, which is a nice change of pace if you have suffered through more punishing eurogames lately. ### Differences in pacing and depth
- Pacing is brisk; rounds typically last 10 to 15 minutes per player, depending on how many drinks you have lined up.

- The deck-building aspect is present but far less consequential than the board game; it acts as a spice rather than a core meal, which is ideal if you want a Cerebria experience without starving for rules.

### Suitability for different groups
