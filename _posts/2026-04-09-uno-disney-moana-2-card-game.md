## How to play: quickstart guide and what to expect
### Core rules, in a nutshell
If you’re already familiar with UNO, Moana’s edition won’t turn your life into a math problem. It sticks to the core rule set: match either color or number, play action cards to mix things up, and be the first to get rid of all your cards. You may encounter a few Moana-specific twists on certain cards—for example, you might have a character card that triggers special actions, or a wildcard that leans on a sea-themed effect. The important thing is that the game remains accessible to players nine years and older, with the potential for kid-friendly house rules to simplify or speed up rounds.

### Quick start for new players
- Shuffle the deck and deal each player a starting hand (usually 7 cards).
- The top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile and sets the color to match.
- On your turn, you can play a card that matches either color or number, or use a Wild card to set the color. The Moana edition’s twists, if any, usually revolve around a few theme cards—but the exact card list should be checked in the rule pamphlet for your particular batch.
- When you’re down to one card, shout UNO; if you forget, an opponent can call you out and you draw extra cards.
- The first player to empty their hand wins.

Note: If your box includes any additional variant cards, be sure to spin them into the rules gently and designate a “variant round” if you’re playing with mixed age groups who want a faster or slower game.

## Variants, house rules, and replayability
### Quick variants for spice
If you’re looking to juice up your UNO Moana nights, here are a few ideas:
- Ocean’s Echo: When you play a Wild card, you must share a quick memory of a family trip to the beach; if not, you draw two cards.
- Maui’s Challenge: A special “Power Card” that allows a player to cancel the next draw effect once per game, representing Maui’s trickster vibe.
- Te Fiti’s Gift: If you are the one who plays the last green card, you get a bonus draw protection for one turn, representing the benevolent gift of Te Fiti.

Note: check your rule sheet for any official variant cards; some editions include a few twists that can dramatically alter ending conditions. Use them sparingly if you’re new to UNO to avoid chaos on an average Tuesday night.
