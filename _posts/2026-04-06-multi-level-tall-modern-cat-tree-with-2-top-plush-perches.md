## Maintenance and Care
A weekly check is a good idea to ensure the screws remain tight and the sisal rope remains firmly in place. If you spot fraying rope, trim the loose ends and re-wrap with fresh rope if you’re up for a craft project. The plush tops can be spot-cleaned with a mild detergent and warm water. Avoid soaking the fabric; cats hate soggy throne rooms, and you hate the smell of damp upholstery.

Dust and fur will accumulate over time. A vacuum or lint roller on the fabric surfaces helps keep the furniture looking presentable. If you want to keep the wood veneer gleaming, a light furniture polish every month or two will do wonders—just avoid using anything oily that could make the cat’s paws slip off the perch.
