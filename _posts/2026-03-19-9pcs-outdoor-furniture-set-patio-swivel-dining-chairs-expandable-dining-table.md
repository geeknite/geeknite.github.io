## Build Quality and Materials
### Frame and hardware
Expect a sturdy frame with consistent welds or joints and corrosion-resistant hardware. Aluminum frames are common for their lightness and rust resistance; steel variants bring a heavier, more substantial feel but require rust protection and maintenance. The hardware should be robust—stainless steel bits or high-grade screws that won’t strip after a few winter cycles. A well-made set won’t creak with every movement or “settle” suspiciously after a season of use.
### Cushions and fabric
Weather-resistant cushions are a big selling point. Look for water-repellent fabric and quick-dry foam cores, rather than memory foam that clings to moisture. Removable covers are a plus for cleaning; zippers should be sturdy and not give up after a few machine washes. If cushions flatten easily after a few uses, that’s a red flag; the bliss of comfort should endure beyond a few sunny afternoons.
### Table mechanism quality
Extendable tables shine when they slide cleanly and lock firmly in place. A wobble-free experience matters if you’re balancing a bowl of chips and a tall beverage on the edge. The legs should distribute weight evenly and not shift when chairs are pulled out or people lean into the table for conversation—the last thing you want is a dramatic buffet slide due to a weak support leg.

Outdoor furniture isn’t immortal, but you can push the lifespan with a little care. Wipe down surfaces to remove pollen and dust, keep cushions in storage during heavy rain, and consider a furniture cover for winter months. The set’s materials should resist UV fading to a reasonable extent; if you notice chalky patches or plastics starting to become brittle after a year, that’s your cue to reassess exposure, shade, or rotation of furniture in sunny spots.

## Maintenance and Longevity
### Seasonal care tips
- Clean cushions and covers according to manufacturer guidelines; air dry completely before use.
- Use a protective cover during heavy rain or winter months.
- Wipe the table and chairs with a mild soap solution to remove pollen and grime.
- Inspect bolts and screws periodically, re-tightening as needed to prevent wobble.

### Troubleshooting common issues
- Wobbly chairs: check the leg pads and ensure all feet are firmly touching the ground. Realign wheels if the chairs are on a hard surface.
- Stiff extendable table: apply light silicone-based lubricant to rails and locks (avoid silicone on wooden surfaces if not recommended).
- Faded cushions: move cushions out of direct sun for a bit and consider UV-protective covers or replacement cushions after several seasons.
