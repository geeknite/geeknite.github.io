Collectibility and Market Insight
---------------------------------
### Market dynamics for Japanese ARs
The Japanese AR market tends to move a bit differently from English language counterparts. Japanese print runs often show tighter distribution and more consistent centering, but you also encounter a higher prevalence of pressing issues from local distributors. Condition matters more in high-demand ARs due to the glare-heavy foil and edge wear observed in some sets. If you are chasing a near mint example, you should meticulously examine corners and surface for whitening and scratches on the foil layers.

### Grading considerations
Grading an SV3 AR card is a blend of sunlight discipline and micro-detail. Look closely at edge wear, corner rounding, and any indents that might indicate handling. The matte backing on some Japanese cards can make whitening more noticeable, while the foil can hide minor scratches better than glossy finishes. If you are planning to submit for grading, keep in mind that the image on the card should present cleanly in a standard 1:1 photo setup to avoid misinterpretations of surface defects.

Condition and Near Mint Realities
---------------------------------
Near mint is a delicate standard to reach. For a Pokemon TCG card, Near Mint means no creases, no whitening on the edges you can feel, and the foil surface should be free from major scratches or clouding. Even a tiny misalignment in the holo layer or a faint scratch can drop the grade. When you handle this Gloom SV3 AR, you want to minimize contact with surface oils and oils from skin. A good practice is to wear gloves and place the card on a soft microfiber cloth during evaluation and storage.

Storage and Preservation
-------------------------
Store this card in a high-quality top loader inside a semi-rigid sleeve to preserve the holo integrity. For long-term storage, a binder with acid-free pages and a climate-controlled environment is ideal. Foil cards are particularly susceptible to temperature changes; erratic heat can lead to edge curl or micro-bulges that collectors notice more than casual observers. A cool, dry closet away from direct sunlight is your best friend here. If you want to take it to the next level, consider a slab with a humidity and temperature rating that aligns with your collection’s needs. This will preserve the card so your grandkids can argue about rarity for another generation.
