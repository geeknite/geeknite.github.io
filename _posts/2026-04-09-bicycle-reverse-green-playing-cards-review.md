## Deck overview: what makes Reverse special
If you’ve ever wondered why the folks at Bicycle rolled out a green Reverse deck, you’re not alone. The Reverse line isn’t about a magical ability to undo mistakes; it’s a design philosophy. The backs are printed in a bright, verdant green with the classic floral-lattice motif you expect from Bicycle, but reversed as a design choice that plays into the name. The idea is simple: the back design is symmetrical, which makes it look the same when you flip it during certain sandwich tricks, color changes, or when you’re messing around with a fan and accidentally reveal your chewed nails instead of your card values. In practice, it adds that cheeky element card magicians adore: a deck that looks ordinary until you tilt, twist, and reveal something unexpectedly dramatic.

For the uninitiated, the “Reverse” concept in Bicycle land often means color reversals, mirrored patterns, or back designs that seem to treat your eyes like a tiny magician’s assistant. In the green variant, the color pops in a way that’s both calming and a little suspicious—like a green screen that’s about to reveal the next trick.
