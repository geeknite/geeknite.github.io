## Assembly and Setup
- Time to assemble: Expect around 45-75 minutes with two people. The instruction manual is clear enough for a quick assembly, though there are some steps where you’ll wish for an extra pair of hands and a spare pair of eyes to inspect alignment.
- Tools included: Allen wrenches that come with their own opinions. Bring your own if you want to speed things up.
- Leveling: The feet have adjustable screws to get the table level. Make sure you do this; otherwise, the ball will roll unpredictably like a mischievous dwarf.

### Pro tips for setup
- Clear a baseline for ball drop: Put a small rug under the table if you’re on a hard floor to avoid scratching or sliding.
- Use a proper ball: A ball sized for the table matters. A ball that’s too small will vanish into the carpet, never to be seen again.
- Pre-stretch the rods: Gently flex the rods a few times before first use to reduce initial resistance.

## Playing Styles and Shot Techniques (Bonus Section)
Even if you’re new to foosball, the FX40 invites you to experiment with various styles. You can adopt a defensive wall formation, run a quick-strike offense, or go for a cheeky trick shot once you’ve built up enough wrist speed.
- The Quick Spin: A rapid wrist flick to rotate the rod and surprise your opponent with a sudden angle. Best used sparingly to avoid tipping your own balance.
- The Banker Pass: A short pass between two midfield players to open up space for a direct shot. Requires timing and a little bit of faith in your teammates—aka, the cat won’t do the passes, unfortunately.
- The Curtain Call: A high arc shot that clears the defense and shoots toward the far corner. It’s flashy, but not always reliable in tight spaces.
