## Care and Cleaning
Routine care includes regular vacuuming on a gentle setting and avoiding abrasive cleaners. For spills, blot with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent if needed. A rug pad prolongs life and helps prevent the backside from snagging on floor surfaces. If you want to refresh the color after years of tea and curry, a color-safe fabric cleaner can help reduce stubborn staining.

Pro tip: avoid heavy steam cleaners or strong solvents that can break down fibers over time. Light, consistent care preserves the rug’s character and avoids turning it into a fragile antique that only looks good under museum lighting.

## The Real-Life Durability Test
We tested the rug under conditions that would horrify a label reader: heavy chair wheels, a toddler who loves to drop things, and a sitcom marathon where snacks were involved. The rug held up well, showing minimal pilling and maintaining color after multiple passes with the vacuum. The backing remained stable on hardwood floors, with no noticeable curling along the edges.

For households with high heel traffic, the rug shows resilience but still benefits from a low-pile threshold that keeps the surface looking neat rather than freckled by every footstep.
