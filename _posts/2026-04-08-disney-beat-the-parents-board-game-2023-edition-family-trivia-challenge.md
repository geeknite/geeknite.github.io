How to play: a quick blueprint
- Arrange the board, shuffle the cards, and designate a Question Master to lead the charge.
- On each turn, a player draws a card and reads the prompt aloud. The clock is ticking—time to shine or humbly reveal your memory gaps.
- Correct answers earn points, while failing to answer on time can lead to a penalty or a chance to steal from the other teams.
- Special rounds offer opportunities to perform, improvise, or enact a short scene from a Disney movie—yes, even that obscure two second thunder Clap moment from a film you know you don’t remember.

Rounds and mechanics: what to expect
Disney Round
A curated set of questions focusing on the broad spectrum of Disney history, from early silent era cartoons to late additions from the streaming era. Expect prompts like Name the voice actor behind a certain beloved character or Which film features a character who proclaims a famous one liner in the middle of a dramatic moment? The pacing here tends to be brisk, with gamers leaning into their internal databases to fetch obscure facts.

Family Round
This is where the game shines as a family night experience: prompts designed to be accessible to younger players while still offering something for adults. Expect fun prompts that require knowledge of movie quotes, character catchphrases, or the best song titles from famous Disney musicals.

Speed Round
As time becomes a precious commodity, a rapid fire sequence tests quick recall. The timer shortens; questions become punchier. The tension rises, and you will find yourself slipping into analog record scratching mode as you scramble for your last second guesses.

Final Beat The Parents
The climactic showdown that puts the parents under pressure while kids attempt to claim victory with their fresh, unfiltered memory. It is the moment where the entire living room becomes a stage, and you will be shouting lines you only half remember, but in the moment they feel absolutely correct.

Variations and house rules
The base game is easy to pick up, but it also invites flexible play. Here are a few ideas you can try to tailor the experience to your crew:
- The memory scavenger: keep a small tray of Disney themed props and encourage players to pick an item before answering to gain extra points.
- The singing rule: any team that sings a song line that matches the prompt can earn bonus points if they can maintain the tune without laughing too much.
- The family swap: after a few rounds, switch teams to encourage cross family collaboration and reduce the we are the kids vs. the parents dynamic.
