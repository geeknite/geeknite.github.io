## Size Options: Custom Size vs 8x10

One of the most attractive parts of the Waterfall Shadow offering is the flexibility around sizing. It’s not a one-size-fits-nothing situation; it’s a “we designed this with your space in mind” approach.

If your floor plan includes alcoves, irregular walls, or a dedicated media wall that refuses to cooperate with standard dimensions, go custom. ### 8x10: the classic workhorse
For many rooms, an 8x10 is the kitchen-sink approach: it fits under most coffee tables, frames seating groups, and accommodates a generous under-foot feel. It’s the reliable option for living rooms, family rooms, and home offices that want a single rug to anchor the zone without overpowering it. While not every space can host a perfect custom dimension, 8x10 remains a versatile baseline that photographs well and scales gracefully with most decor palettes.

### How to decide
- Room proportions: If your sofa length is around 7 to 8 feet with a foot-in or foot-out rug under the front legs, 8x10 is often ideal. For a smaller room, you might lean toward a 6x9 or 5x8; in larger spaces with a grand seating arrangement, custom sizes can create a more cohesive look.
- Furniture layout: If you want all four legs of the sofa to rest on rug, you’ll likely need a larger rug. If you’re anchoring a reading nook, a custom shape that hugs a chair and ottoman can be striking.
- Traffic and cleaning: Wool is robust, but the larger the rug, the more surface area there is to clean. A well-thought-out pad under any rug helps with wear and underfoot comfort.

If you’re torn between two sizes, remember: the rug is one of the few decorative elements that can dictate the flow of a room. It’s not cheating to order two sizes and return the one that doesn’t feel right—just make sure you consult your retailer’s return policy first.

## Maintenance and Care: Keeping Wool Rug Vibes Alive

### Daily care
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to lift soil without crushing the pile.
- Avoid beater bars that can pull on fibers; a suction-only head often yields the best results.
- Wicking spills is your friend: blot, don’t rub. Wool repels a lot of moisture, but you still want to avoid saturation.

### Spot cleaning tips
- For organic spills (coffee, wine, tea): blot and apply a mild wool-safe cleaner or a solution of water and mild detergent. Test in an inconspicuous area first.
- For tougher stains: a wool-safe stain remover with dye-safe properties can help. Always follow the product instructions and perform a spot test.

### Deep cleaning and professional care
- A semi-annual refresh with a professional rug cleaning can revive pile height and color richness. Wool rugs dislike aggressive scrubbing and harsh chemicals; a pro who specializes in wool will know the right balance of moisture and heat to avoid dye bleed or fiber damage.

### Rug pads
A good pad underneath is essential. It reduces slippage, protects the rug’s backing, and adds a layer of comfort. Look for pads with non-slip coatings and a thickness that suits your room’s foot traffic. Some folks love a memory-foam underlayment for seating areas; others prefer a natural felt or rubberized pad for high-traffic spaces. The pad is as important as the rug itself in how it feels underfoot.

### Longevity expectations
Wool is durable, resilient, and naturally flame-resistant. With proper care, a high-quality wool rug can outlive many fashion cycles. Expect a patina that tells your room’s story: fewer crumbs, more character, and a texture you’ll still love a decade from now.

## Ordering and Customization: How to Get Your Perfect Rug

### Step-by-step: custom size path
1. Measure your space carefully, noting wall irregularities and seating zones. Use painter’s tape to visualize the rug’s footprint.
2. Choose a custom size dimension that aligns with your room’s real geometry. Some retailers offer rounding to the nearest inch or centimeter to fit perfectly.
3. Confirm backing and padding options. You’ll want a pad that aligns with your floor type (hardwood, laminate, tile) and provides the right level of friction.
4. Select a colorway and opinionated pattern intensity. Some light customization is often possible in dye saturation to better match your wall color or furniture legs.
5. Schedule shipping and installation. If you’re using a professional installer, share the rug dimensions and weight with them ahead of time.

### Step-by-step: 8x10 path
1. Verify your space can accommodate an 8x10 with at least 18 inches of clearance around the perimeter for airflow and task lighting.
2. Decide on the pile height and pad type that suits your usage. Higher traffic rooms benefit from a slightly denser pad and a more resilient pile.
3. Confirm dye stability and color accuracy through swatches if available. If you’re a color-obsessed decorator, you’ll want perfect alignment with your fabric samples.
4. Place the order and await delivery, then unroll, adjust, and enjoy. If it’s a hard floor, align furniture first, then place the rug to create a cohesive living zone.
