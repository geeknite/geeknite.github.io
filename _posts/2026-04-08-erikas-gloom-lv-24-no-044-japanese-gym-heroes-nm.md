## Condition, Grading, and the NM Seal
NM—Near Mint—describes a card that shows minimal to no signs of wear. In a world with modern gloves and microfiber sleeves everywhere, NM is a target for many collectors who want their time capsules to look pristine enough to pass for freshly minted. Erika’s Gloom LV. 24 with NM designation implies a few likely scenarios: the corners are sharp, the surface has little to no scratches, the gloss remains vibrant, and the edges aren’t marred by whitening or minor nicks that usually accompany decades of gentle card magic. The Japanese Gym Heroes line is known for a certain minting sheen that, when properly stored, can still gleam in the right light, making NM looks like a small miracle rather than a given.

But NM is not a vacuum. The market for vintage Pokemon cards, especially Japanese prints, has its own rhythm. A card in NM condition may fetch a higher price in a market that variables against the tide of reprints, the interest of new collectors who want something authenically vintage, and the general mood of the market on any given day. The key to NM—and this is a sober note wrapped in a friendly glove—is comprehensive, proper storage. Sleeves, toploaders, a climate-controlled cabinet with a gentle humidity, and a care routine that would make any houseplant jealous. If you’re reading this while thinking about the best way to store a NM Erika’s Gloom LV. 24, know that you’re in good company. The game’s enduring charm is that it rewards patience, a careful eye, and a little bit of luck when a card gets passed from hand to hand without a coffee ring on the corner.

From a graded perspective, NM cards in this era are often evaluated not only on surface wear but also on centering and edge wear. The symbol printed on the front—if you’re lucky enough to have it tallied in your binder—tells a story about the print run, the batch, and the machine that cut it. Collectors who love Gym Heroes JP editions often chase this combination: the classic Gloom image, the LV. 24 label visible just enough to tell a story, and a pristine surface that invites long-term storage—the kind of combination that makes a collector’s heart do a tiny nerdy skip, like a Pokemon using a gentle punch to say, “Keep calm and collection on.”
