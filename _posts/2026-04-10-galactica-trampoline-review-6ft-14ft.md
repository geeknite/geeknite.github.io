## Setup and first use

The box contents typically include frame pieces, the jumping mat, enclosure net, safety pads, the ladder, and installation hardware. Tools required usually include a wrench and a rubber mallet, plus a friend who enjoys counting to ten while you assemble. Expect some time if you want to do a clean job; rushing leads to misaligned springs and a bounce that resembles a faulty stadium seating arrangement.

To help you, we’ve included a quick setup checklist:

- Clear the area of rocks, dog toys, garden gnomes, and interpretive dance troops.
- Lay out all parts and check them against the instruction manual.
- Assemble the frame on a flat surface, paying attention to orientation and the ladder side.
- Attach the enclosure net and safety pads, ensuring the net zipper works properly.
- Stretch the mat and secure springs or bands according to the instructions.
- Do a slow bounce test with a light load before letting kids go full tilt.

Important safety note: ensure the space around the trampoline is clear of hard objects, such as fences or power tools. The recommended impact area is at least 8 feet in all directions, with soft ground or protective mats.

For safety guidelines and validation standards, you can check resources from the CPSC and ASTM. See https://www.cpsc.gov/Safety-Education and https://www.astm.org/Standards-F963.html for general guidelines. Remember that local rules may vary.

## Safety tips and usage guidelines

Backyard fun is great, but safety should be your top priority. Always supervise children during play and adhere to the manufacturer weight limit. Avoid flipping or stunts that exceed the recommended jump height and do not allow more than the recommended number of jumpers on the mat at a time.

Here are a few practical safety tips:

- Use the ladder to enter and exit rather than climbing on the net or side rails.
- Keep the area around the trampoline free of obstacles; this includes toys, garden tools, and the neighbor's grape vines.
- Use safety nets with properly functioning zippers and gates.
- Inspect the frame and mat for signs of wear or damage on a regular basis.
- If sections of the padding are worn or torn, replace them promptly.

For more formal guidelines, see the CPSC and ASTM pages linked earlier. Always follow local regulations regarding trampoline use and residential safety. The last thing you want is your HOA or the neighbor loudmouth yelling about another bounce party.
