## Maintenance: keeping the case happy

Like any gear that travels with you, the Skip-Bo Travel Case benefits from a little upkeep. Here are a few no-fuss tips to keep it in great shape:

- Wipe the exterior with a soft, damp cloth regularly. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade surfaces. A microfiber cloth does wonders for fingerprints and minor scuffs.
- Check hinges and latches after heavy use. If you hear creaks or notice looseness, a dab of silicone-based lubricant around the hinge (not on the cards) can smooth things out. Pro tip: avoid WD-40; it attracts dust and can degrade other materials over time.
- Inspect interior dividers for wear. If foam compresses, rotate or replace dividers to preserve protection.
- Remember the mantra: this is a portable organizer, not a magic library. If you’re packing a big library of game components, you may want a larger system or a more flexible approach.

Maintenance isn’t glamorous, but it’s the secret to making gear last long enough to become a trusted travel companion rather than a one-season fling.
