## Maintenance: keeping the case happy

Like any gear that travels with you, the Skip-Bo Travel Case benefits from a little regular maintenance. Here are a few no-fuss tips to keep it in great shape:

- Wipe down the exterior with a soft, damp cloth on a regular basis. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the surface. A microfiber cloth does wonders on dull spots and fingerprints.
- Check the hinges and latches after intense use. If you notice looseness or squeaks, a quick dab of silicone-based lubricant around the hinge (not on the cards) can smooth things out. Avoid WD-40; it can attract dust and dry out other materials over time.
- Inspect the interior dividers for wear. If the foam becomes compressed, you may need a replacement divider or to reposition your deck layout to preserve protection.
- Keep spirit rules for the case’s purpose in mind. This is not an infinite storage solution; it’s a portable organizer. If you’re packing a big library of game components, it’s probably time to consider a larger system or a more flexible approach.

Maintenance isn’t glamorous, but it’s the secret to making gear last long enough to become a beloved travel companion rather than a one-season fling.
