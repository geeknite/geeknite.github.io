## Practical considerations: care, maintenance, and longevity
A two-deck set of gold edition Rider Backs deserves some care to maximize longevity. Here are a few practical tips:
- Keep out of direct sunlight to prevent the foil from tasting like ultraviolet bleach over the years. UV exposure can dull metallic accents.
- Store in the tuck boxes when not in use; avoid keeping decks in humid, hot spaces where the finish can degrade.
- Replace the manuals with a soft microfiber cloth if you notice dust on the foil. Clean gently; you do not want to abrade the foil with rough cloth or aggressive rubbing.
- For performance, consider using separate storage for red and blue decks to avoid color transfer when stacked in your bag or on a table.

In our tests, these decks stood up to a robust schedule of daily practice for several weeks. They remained stiff enough for card control, but not brittle enough to crack under pressure. The foil did not scratch easily during casual handling, and there was no significant edge wear after weeks of shuffles and flourishes. If you are someone who uses your deck in a performance environment and needs a long-lasting foil accent for stage presence, you should be satisfied with the Gold edition's durability.
