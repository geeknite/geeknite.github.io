## How to play in 15 minutes
### The quick basics
Monopoly Deal is a set collection and portfolio management game. Players start with a small hand of cards and draw to a certain hand size. Each turn, you draw cards and play up to three cards. Your goal is to collect a full property set (three or four cards of the same color) and build it up with houses and hotels, then claim rent from opponents using your property holdings. The twist? You can steal cards with action cards and force deals, sometimes causing chaotic but highly entertaining moments as someone desperately tries to salvage their own holdings.

### Turn structure and rhythm
Turns are short and often sudden, which means that the table rhythm shifts quickly. A single misplay can open a floodgate of rent collection or card stealing, turning the table in seconds. The players need to be mindful that this is a game where shenanigans are part of the objective, not a side effect. The 15 minute estimate is usually accurate if you go in with a plan and a willingness to adapt mid-session.

### Strategy starter pack
Early game strategy should focus on starting property sets and protecting them with defense cards. You want to build a foundation you can amplify in later turns, but you must also stay flexible enough to exploit your opponents weaknesses. In this tin, the card colors and types are obvious enough to let you experiment with different strategies, from heavy rent pressure to stealthy property grabs. The real joy is in playing a few games and watching the meta evolve as you learn which players go for rent, which players accumulate defense, and which players go full tilt into auctions.
