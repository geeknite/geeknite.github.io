It re-centers the engine of City of Mist on three intertwined axes: space, luck, and narrative leverage. Here are the big-ticket items you’ll be talking about around the table after the first session:\n\n### A Distinctive District\n\nFortune Row introduces a neighborhood that feels almost a living character, with its own quirks, power players, and ritual spaces. It’s not merely a backdrop; it’s a driver of stories. Expect micro-societies within the district: a casino-arcade hybrid that operates like a haunted elevator, a memory market speakeasy, and a crime-lord’s estate perched atop a hill of old myths. The setting gives you strong anchors for episodes, while still allowing players to diverge into strange, mythic tangents.\n\n### Signature Items and Loci\n\nIf the core book’s signature items were a set of sharp knives, Fortune Row hands you a ceremonial blade, a lucky talisman, and a dice-rolling contraption that honestly looks like a clockwork cat. This is a place where magical artifacts feel tactile and narratively potent instead of purely decorative. The Loci (places in the city where mythic power leaks) in Fortune Row are color-coded by fortune type—Lucky, Risky, and Cursed—giving the GM a ready-made toolkit to string sessions together with thematic consistency.\n\n### Rumor Tree Encounters\n\nThe setting offers a structured rumor system that weaves through the investigations. Players chase a rumor from a back alley to a rooftop ceremony to an underground catacomb of memory traders. Each rumor carries a hint of mythic consequence and a risk-reward ladder: follow, you gain a boons-based advantage; misread it, and you trigger a consequence that echoes across the entire arc.\n\n### Factional Ecology\n\nFortune Row expands the social ecosystem with factions that aren’t just allegiances but living, breathing ecosystems. Each faction has an agenda, a patron myth, and a method for influencing luck around the district. The GM can orchestrate clashes between factions to escalate the stakes without forcing a “boss fight.” It’s a refreshing design philosophy: a fight in Fortune Row is about leverage and misdirection as much as brute force.\n\n## New Mechanics and Tools\n\nThe book doesn’t rewrite the wheel so much as refine the wheel’s chrome. A few systems stand out for their utility in ongoing campaigns:\n\n### Fortune Points Reimagined\n\nFortune Points are the lifeblood of the setting, but Fortune Row reframes them. They’re less about scorekeeping and more about negotiation with the city. You might spend Fortune to bend a rumor toward truth, to skip a devastating social consequence, or to reveal a tiny mythic facet about a character you’ve been tracking. The pacing encourages players to think strategically about risk, but it never feels punitive. It’s not pay-to-win; it’s pay-to-unfold.\n\n### Street-Scale Loci\n\nThe new Loci system scales down from city-wide to street-level. This is perfect for folks who want a compact, modular structure for sessions. Each Locus has a few flavorful triggers and a handful of narrative hooks. It’s the kind of tool you can drop into any session to accelerate setup without sacrificing mood.\n\n### Heist and Investigation Tracks\n\nFortune Row leans into two classic City of Mist play patterns—the heist and the investigation—by giving them their own tracks with levelled complications.
