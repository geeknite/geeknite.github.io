Care, Preservation, and Display: Keeping the Tin Shiny and The Cards Aligned

- Storage: Keep the tin in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. This minimizes fading on the artwork and helps preserve the tin’s finish. A display shelf is ideal, but a dedicated closet with a soft LED light can also work wonders for dramatic effect when guests enter your gaming realm.
- Handling: Cards that see heavy shuffle action can suffer from edge wear. If you intend to play, consider a protective card sleeve for the deck inside the tin. If you intend to display, you might skip sleeves and enjoy the gleam of the original stock.
- Cleaning: A soft microfiber cloth works for the tin. For the cards, a gentle wipe on the back to remove fingerprints is fine, but avoid harsh cleaners or water—this is always good advice for vintage game components.
- Display tips: Show off the tin on a stand or in a glass-front cabinet. If you have the space, a small Phase 10 shrine with the tin at the center looks delightfully nerdy and wholesome. You’ll thank your future self for curating the experience instead of letting it languish in a box in the attic.
