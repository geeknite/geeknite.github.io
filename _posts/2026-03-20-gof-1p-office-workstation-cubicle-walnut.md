## Ergonomics: comfort, posture, and the science of not waking up with a crick in your neck
Ergonomics on a budget is a tough game. The GOF 1P hits a comfy middle ground: the surface is sturdy, the edge is smooth, and you can place the monitor at a reasonable distance to minimize eye strain. The desk height combined with a capable chair can yield a posture that doesn’t feel like you’ve been perched on a pilates ball all day. If you’re tall, you may appreciate the 6' height option—it can provide a little extra clearance for your knees and the chair wheels to move without colliding with the underside of the frame.

A key ergonomic consideration is wrist posture. The desk depth is shallow enough that you don’t need a massive keyboard tray, but you’ll likely want to place your keyboard at a slight negative tilt to keep wrists aligned with your forearms. A compact keyboard and a slim mouse will help avoid that “stretching to reach the middle of the desk” sensation; if you’re a gamer who uses a number pad or a full-size keyboard with macro keys, you may need to strategically position the keyboard to prevent the wrists from drifting toward the edge of the desk.

For standing desk fans who dream of switching modes, this GOF unit is not designed to convert into a standing desk on a whim. It’s a fixed-height cube with a high‑quality veneer. If you want a standing option, you’ll need to add a separate adjustable desk riser or an under-desk converter. The good news: the compact footprint gives you more space to put a small portable standing platform beneath or beside it if you’re willing to experiment with a “mostly-sitting, occasionally-standing” routine.

## Maintenance and care: keeping the finish and hardware singing
To keep the walnut finish looking its best, use a soft microfiber cloth for dusting and occasional cleaning with a mild wood-safe cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that can strip the veneer or dull the finish. For hardware, a simple wipe with a light oil solution can keep screws turning smoothly; avoid over-lubricating, which can attract dust and grit. If you notice any loose fasteners after a few months of use, re-tighten in a star pattern to ensure even pressure and prevent warping. If a spill happens, blot gently rather than rubbing vigorously—especially if the liquid is acidic or colored, which could stain the veneer.
