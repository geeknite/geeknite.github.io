## Tips and tricks for optimizing the two person desk setup
### How to position two people effectively
- Align chairs with a shared central axis to minimize reach strain. 
- Position the primary monitor directly in front of the person who does most of the reference work. 
- Set both keyboards on a shared surface that leaves enough space for wrists and cup holders. 

### Cable choreography for two
- Designate left and right cable zones to prevent tangles. 
- Use labeled cable ties so you can quickly identify which cord goes where during a frantic docking moment before a meeting. 
- Consider a powered hub with surge protection to protect both laptops without creating a tangle of bricks on your desk.

### Accessories to consider
- A lightweight monitor arm for each side to fine tune angle and height. 
- A slim under-desk keyboard tray if you need extra surface on a busy day. 
- A desk mat to reduce scratches and add a splash of color. 
- A couple of mug coasters with goofy prints to keep coffee rings at bay.

## Troubleshooting common issues
### Wobble and shimmy
If the desk wobbles, check leg levelers and ensure the crossbar is snugly attached. Tighten screws gradually in a crisscross pattern until everything sits flat. If wobble persists, place a small non-slip pad under one leg to balance the surface.

### Cable spaghetti after a week
Take a small pause to re-route cables, group them by workstation, and dedicate a single power strip to each side rather than sharing a single source. It will reduce the chance of one person yanking on a cable during a dramatic screen transition.

### Monitor angle drift
If your monitor angles drift whenever someone taps the desk, consider upgrading to heavier monitor bases or adding a secondary clamp. The monitor stand built into the desk is a nice feature, but heavy screens over 32 inches may benefit from additional stabilization.
