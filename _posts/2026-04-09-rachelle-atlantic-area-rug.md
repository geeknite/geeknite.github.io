## Practicality: Cleaning, Maintenance, and Longevity
Rugs are not a one-and-done purchase. They’re a living part of your home, catching crumbs, offering a soft place for pets to nap, and occasionally hosting dance-offs when the mood strikes your inner Saturday-night DJ.

### Cleaning Mechanics
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle height setting to avoid rough tearing of fibers.
- For spills: blot, don’t rub. Use a mild detergent solution and blot again until dry.
- Stain care: For tougher stains, a gentle enzymatic cleaner designed for poly blends does the trick. Always test in a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Odor management: If your rug picks up smells (you know the ones), sprinkle baking soda lightly, let it sit, then vacuum.

### Do You Need a Rug Pad?
Yes. A pad reduces slippage, adds a touch of cushion, and can lengthen the rug’s life by decreasing wear on the bottom fibers. Choose a non-slip pad that’s slightly smaller than your rug’s dimensions to keep edges clean and intact.

### Longevity Expectation
With routine care, the Atlantic Atlantic (pardon the shipping joke) typically maintains its color and pattern for several seasons. It’s not a hug-the-wall heirloom, but it’s a solid workhorse for the busy home. If you’re chasing a “new rug glow” every couple of years, you might find yourself migrating this toward guest rooms or alternate spaces—but for a practical, stylish living area, it should age gracefully.
