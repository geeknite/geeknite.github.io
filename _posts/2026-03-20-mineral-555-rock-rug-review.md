Design and Materials

The Mineral 555 Rock Rug is a tribute to the subterranean. If you're thinking is this a real rock the answer is no, not a real rock. If you're thinking does this still feel delightfully rock-like under bare feet? the answer is a confident yes. The tinhat geologists among us might whisper that feldspar and mica might be present in spirit if not in fiber.

Practicality and Comfort

A rug that pretends to be a rock would have to juggle two contradictory requirements: look like stone and feel like something you want to walk on for hours. The Mineral 555 tries to pull this off by marrying a stiff top layer with a softer, cushiony base. In practice, you get enough support for long sits in front of the computer, a little give for your knees during stretching, and a surprising amount of warmth for those chilly mornings. The rug's thickness sits at around 0.5 inches to 0.75 inches depending on the colorway, which is enough to cushion your joints but not so thick that it triggers your standing desk to become a culinary lab on a Friday.

Maintenance and Cleaning

In a world where tumbling rocks do not appear in your living room, a rug like this begs the question how do you clean it when the inevitable coffee spill occurs? The Mineral 555 Rock Rug is machine-washable on a gentle cycle, with cold water and mild detergent. It’s not a miracle cure for coffee disasters, but it’s a serious upgrade over the rinse it with water and hope technique. For smaller spills, blotting with a clean cloth works well, followed by air-drying. Do not tumble-dry on high heat, as it may cause fibers to shrink or misbehave, which is the exact opposite of what a rock rug should do.

If your space sees a lot of pet traffic, you may notice that fur and dust cling to the surface as if attracted by a small gravitational pull. A quick vacuum with a standard floor attachment does the trick; for deeper cleaning, you can use a damp microfiber cloth to lift the dust without saturating the fibers. The product label recommends periodic deep cleaning every few months, which is fine if you own a time machine or a particularly aggressive choreography of set it and forget it. In short the rug cleans easier than you expect, and it doesn't require you to hire a hazmat team after every beverage spill.

Care and Safety

One of the major concerns with anything that looks like a stone is slip risk. The Mineral 555 Rock Rug solves this with a nonslip backing designed for hardwood and tile floors. If you have a particularly slippery surface, consider using an additional rug pad for extra grip.
