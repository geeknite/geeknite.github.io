## Cleaning and Care

Cotton blends respond well to spot cleaning and gentle maintenance, which makes this rug practical for everyday life. Here are some sane care guidelines:

- Vacuum regularly on a light setting to remove dust and pet hair. Avoid fierce suction that could pull at the fringe.
- For spills, blot—don’t rub. Rubbing can push stains deeper and fuzz up the fibers. A clean cloth or paper towel is your friend here.
- Use mild detergent and cold water for spot cleaning. Test on a small, inconspicuous corner first to ensure colorfastness. If the color shifts, you’ve trained your rug to be a mood ring; congrats, you’ve discovered a feature.
- Air dry completely before placing it back in high-traffic areas. A damp rug is less friction, which means it’s more likely to shift around and become a tripping hazard for you and your roommates.
- Professional cleaning is a good idea if you have persistent stains or require deeper extraction. Cotton blends aren’t the most fragile material, but they’re not invincible either.

## Durability and Longevity

Durability in a small, cotton blend rug is a matter of weave density, fiber quality, and how dramatically you treat it. Rope-weave patterns tactile texture; this translates into a robust surface that resists showing everyday wear relatively well for a rug of its size. The fringe, while charming, is a potential snag point if you have pets with a habit of stepping on edges, or if you’re constantly dragging furniture across the room. A little care goes a long way here: place felt pads under heavy furniture legs to minimize snagging, and avoid aggressive dragging across rough flooring.

With regular maintenance, the rug should retain its color and structure for several seasons, particularly if you live in a climate with moderate humidity. If you’re in a high-traffic home with kids, you’ll probably want to retreat damp spots quickly and consider a protective rug pad underneath to reduce movement and extend life. The pad also helps keep the rug from curling at the edges—a common frustration with lightweight textiles.

## FAQ
- How do I clean spills on this rug?
  Blot with a clean cloth, use a mild detergent solution for spot cleaning, and air dry. Avoid rubbing. If a bigger stain persists, consider professional cleaning.
- Can I machine wash this rug?
  Not recommended for most rug blends. Check care instructions on your rug’s tag and follow with spot cleaning rather than full immersion.
- Will it work with radiant floor heating?
  Cotton blends generally tolerate moderate heat, but check your rug’s care guidelines and the heating system’s manual to avoid any heat-related fiber damage.
