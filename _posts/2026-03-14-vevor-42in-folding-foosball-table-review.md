## Setup, maintenance, and care

### Quick setup checklist
1. Unbox and lay out all components. Inspect for missing hardware. If you find a random bolt, that’s your cue to take a mental breath and check the manual.
2. Attach legs and crossbars as directed. Tighten screws in a cross pattern to ensure even pressure distribution. If your DIY heart starts racing, take a little break and brew a cup of tea (or coffee).
3. Install the folding mechanism and test fold twice. This is where you learn if your space actually accommodates the folded state and whether you’ll need to clear a path for storage.
4. Place the table on a level surface. Use the leg levelers if needed to avoid that infamous “rocking” effect that makes all your dribbling shots into the air.
5. Insert balls and quickly test a practice pass. If the ball ping-pongs off the walls in a satisfying rhythm, you’re in business.

### Cleaning and maintenance
Foosball tables, like octopuses wearing monocles, have a few sensitive points. The rods and bearings appreciate occasional lubrication with a silicone-based spray. Wipe down the table surface regularly with a soft, damp cloth to prevent chalky build-up and to keep the ball from sticking during a heated final minute. Periodically inspect the rods for wobble; if you notice any looseness, re-tighten the mounting screws. A table that’s proud of its folding ability also benefits from occasional checks of the locking pins to ensure they don’t loosen with repeated use.

### Common issues and how to fix them quickly
- Ball getting stuck in the corner: make sure the rail is properly aligned and the surface is clean. A quick wipe often fixes it.
- Wobbly legs after folding: re-check the leg screws and the levelers. If needed, shim the surface slightly with a thin rubber pad under the leg base.
- Rods too tight to turn: a light lubrication on the bearings helps; avoid over-greasing, which can attract dust and create a sticky finish.
