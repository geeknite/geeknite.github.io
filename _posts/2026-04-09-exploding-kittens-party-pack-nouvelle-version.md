What s in the Nouvelle Version? The Party Pack is more than just a reprint. It brings a bigger deck, new Cat Cards with distinctive personalities, and a few house rules that unlock extra chaos. You get a richer selection of defuse cards to rescue yourself from the inevitable kitten catastrophe, a handful of role cards or mission prompts to create social dynamics, and an upgraded rulebook with quick start tips. The designer intent is clear the aim is to maximize laughter while keeping the game accessible to newcomers.

Setup and components.

The box feels substantial and the card stock has a satisfying heft. It sits on the table with a presence that says this is a party you should not ignore. Setup takes a little longer than the original, but not by much. We are still talking five to ten minutes at most, depending on how fancy your group wants to be with shuffling and card dispersal.

The art style remains bright and humorous, a mix of cute cats and chaotic splashes of color. The new Cat Cards have chosen distinct vibes rather than feeling like clones, which helps at the table when you need to call out a particular card mid play.

Gameplay and mechanics.

Core loop remains draw, play, and avoid the exploding kitten. The Nouvelle Version adds more cards, more interactions, and more opportunities to improvise. The draw pile remains the heartbeat, knocking at your door with a wagging tail and a sense of doom. The balance between luck and bluffing is still the lifeblood, but with extra players you get more opportunities for misdirection and for theatrical plays that might be less about math and more about performance.

Turn flow is simple. On your turn you may draw a card from the deck, play cards to trigger combos, skip penalties, or shuffle the deck in surprising ways. Cat Cards can chain into surprising effects that escalate the chaos in fun ways. Defuse cards can still feel like a lifeline, and there are more paths to recover from a near miss than in the base game.

The new variants allow you to tailor chaos to your group. If your circle loves memes and inside jokes, these variants will feel like they were written for your friends. It is a social game first and a party game second, which is exactly what many players crave.

Strategy in a party game is less about perfect play and more about making the table feel alive. The Nouvelle Version rewards bold, theatrical plays, cards that cause outrage, acts that trigger roars of laughter, and moments where someone accidentally flips the entire table in an existential crisis about whether a salad can count as strategy.

Humor, art and tone.

The humor remains unapologetically silly. The art is bold, bright, and intentionally goofy. This is not a game for someone who needs a serious aesthetic; it is a celebration of ridiculousness. The jokes land more reliably when delivered aloud by a willing narrator at the table, but the cards also carry enough jokes to keep players engaged even when narration is sparse.

Accessibility and audience.

The Party Pack is well suited for mixed groups of beginners and veteran gamers. It scales with the number of players, providing more opportunities for interaction as you include more people. If you are teaching new players, a few example plays and a quick read of the rule summary will get you to table in under ten minutes. For families, the content stays light and silly, though you should be mindful of standard PG-13 humor in the jokes; if you have younger players, screen a few cards ahead of time or join together for safe play.

Replayability and longevity.

The variety in the deck and the different Cat Cards give the Party Pack a longer life than a single session. The chaos becomes a feature, not a bug, as you rotate players and try new combinations. If you play with the same group weekly, you will find new memes emerge and old jokes resurface with new context. If you play sporadically, the novelty can last for several nights of game nights before the jokes start to repeat.

House rules and variants.

You can lean into the ridiculous with a few house rules or variants. Try Rule of Funny a card that gives a temporary advantage when the table laughs. Introduce a Photo Rule where photos on social media become the catalyst for effects. Or use Swap and Defuse to trade a card while defusing a kitten. The important thing is to keep things light and allowed by the group style.

The table experience.

What you will feel at the table is a social heat rising. People lean in, shout over a joke, and get excited about brand-new card interactions. The extended deck means more cards to read aloud, more moments that become legend at the end of the night, and more chances for someone to accidentally topple a glass while shouting a verdict at a cat.

The verdict and final take.

As a reviewer, I look for a balance of laughs and accessibility. Exploding Kittens Party Pack Nouvelle Version delivers on the promise of a party game that is fun to pick up and hard to master. It is not a deep strategic investment; it is a social event that thrives on the energy of the players. If you want a quick, chaotic, and comedic experience with friends and family, this is a strong pick. If your taste runs toward heavy Euro games or in-depth strategy, you should pass this and keep exploring other options.

Final recommendation.

- Best for parties with a mix of players who enjoy light humor and quick rounds
- A strong entry point for new players who want to understand modern meme-based card games
- A fun upgrade for fans of Exploding Kittens who want more chaos and more laughs
- A great gift for nerdy friends and coworkers who appreciate silly chaos

If you want to bring this to your next gathering, try it with a couple of variants, then add more if the table asks for it. The Party Pack is about turning a simple draw into a social spectacle, and you might find it cracks open a new tradition for your group.
