## Design and Build: materials, finish, and the “feel” test

The Oak Black finish sits somewhere between rustic charm and executive suite swagger. It’s not the flat black that absorbs every fingerprint and mutters, “We’ve got you with matte dryness; you’ve got nothing to worry about.” Nor is it the bleached oak that can look washed out in fluorescent lighting. This finish hits the sweet spot: warm wood tones with a protective veneer that resists desk-critter fingerprints and coffee rings—mostly. The veneer is paired with metal rails and a powder-coated frame that can handle a full shift’s worth of typing fingers, mouse swipes, and the occasional forgotten charger lying on the desk surface. It’s not a “premium feel” from a high-end luxury brand, but it’s a solid, practical, every-day champion. If you’ve ever wanted a desk that can take a 10-person team through a sprint without looking like a chaotic lab experiment, this finish is your friend.

### Construction quality
The build quality matters here because you’re basically commissioning a metro line for ten people. You’ll find multiple cable management troughs running along the underside, plus integrated grommets across the surface to keep power and data snug and out of the way. ## Features and functionality: why this thing might actually work (or not)

### Screen and privacy features
The centerpiece here is the central screen panel between the two work clusters. It can be configured as a privacy panel, a whiteboard surface, or a digital screen depending on your needs. In practice, privacy panels help reduce visual noise and create personal zones in a communal environment. They also help in reducing accidental desk hazards like “I’m not looking at your screen, I’m looking at my own screen, which is totally a different screen.” For teams doing design reviews or cross-functional stand-ups, this setup helps keep conversations productive and on-topic. If you’re into video meetings, you might want to mount a small USB-C monitor on each side facing outwards; that way, the group can share a screen with the rest of the floor without everyone turning their chairs into windmills.

### Cable management and power integration
There are cable channels and built-in grommets that aim to make the cables disappear rather than pop up like hydras from the desk surface. Expect to pair the unit with a dedicated power distribution strip and perhaps a motion-detecting nightlight for those late-night coding sprints where you forget you’re not in a dark cave. The system is designed to host multiple power sources (PCs, docking stations, chargers, USB hubs) and keep them both accessible and out of sight. Cable trays under the surface help with airflow and reduce the risk of trip hazards during a comedy of errors that only happens when you’re sharing a 6-meter desk with ten people who forgot their extension cords.

### Storage and accessories
Under-desk storage can be added via modular drawer units or pedestal cabinets—though you’ll want to consider the leg clearance if you decide to go with drawers on every module. You’ll also see space for monitor arms, keyboard trays, and document trays. The system is designed with modularity in mind; you can reconfigure the layout later as your team grows or shrinks. If your organization is into “desk by committee,” this modular approach ensures you can move people around without reworking the entire bench. The storage options are practical rather than luxurious; think “sleek, functional, and ready for a sprint.”

Ergonomics aren’t an afterthought here; they’re an expectation. The bench’s height is compatible with standard office chairs, and the design accommodates a comfortable elbow angle for typical keyboards and mice setups. Additionally, the Oak Black finish adds warmth to the space, which indirectly benefits posture by reducing the mental fatigue that comes with staring at stark, all-white surfaces. ## Setup, installation, and maintenance: what to expect in the real world

Unboxing a 6-meter desk is not your typical morning coffee run. Expect a two-to-three-person team (or a highly caffeinated single person) to wrestle the panels into place, align the modules, and anchor the frame to the floor or the wall as required by your space. Most suppliers will provide a detailed assembly guide and, ideally, a two-person installation service. The time investment will vary based on room layout and whether you’re installing the waterfall of cables under the desk or opting for a more minimal approach with exposed channels. Maintenance is fairly straightforward: wipe with a soft cloth, avoid harsh chemicals on the veneer, and keep the cable management channels clear to prevent a tangled sculpture of cords that could pass for abstract art.

Aesthetically, the oak veneer shows its character as you live with it. You’ll notice small scratches from daily usage—these aren’t necessarily flaws, but they tell the story of a desk that has supported thousands of ideas, design reviews, and the occasional late-night debugging session. If you’re worried about wear and tear, there are protective mats and desk pads that can preserve the finish while still letting the natural texture shine through.

## Real-world setup tips: how to unleash the potential without losing your mind

- Plan your wiring with a professional IT installer. The central screen panel is a magnet for cable clutter—the last thing you want is a spaghetti zoo in your workspace.

- Establish a common perimeter for the ten users. Even with a 6-meter desk, you’ll want to set expectations about noise, foot traffic, and private conversations.
- Consider optional accessories: monitor arms, keyboard trays, task lighting, and desk pads. Each of these can improve comfort and efficiency significantly without changing the bench’s fundamental footprint.
