## What is Exploding Kittens
Exploding Kittens is a light card game designed around a simple objective: be the last player standing. You start with a handful of cards and your goal is to play cards that either help you survive or sabotage others, all while avoiding the dreaded Exploding Kitten card. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card to neutralize it, you are out of the running. The Defuse cards let you defuse the bomb in a humorous, cat-themed way, typically by using a clever trick or a lucky saving throw. The turns are fast, the humor is cheeky, and the tension builds with every draw.

What makes Exploding Kittens distinctive is the blend of light strategy and chaotic randomness. You might hold the Defuse card that would save you from doom, but playing it at the right moment is a puzzle that your friends will find both infuriating and delightful. The card types range from attack, skip, shuffle, see the future, to nope, which is basically your group chat, but with more purring and less sarcasm. The nope card is a fan favorite, letting you cancel an opponent’s action, which can lead to glorious, chaotic chain reactions that make you suspect the laws of probability and gravity had a personal vendetta against your Sunday game night.

## Gameplay overview
Exploding Kittens is played in rounds with players taking turns to play cards and draw from the deck. On your turn, you generally do two things: play a card (optionally) and then draw a card from the deck. The draw is the heartbeat of the game; you don’t know exactly what you will pull. Will it be a harmless card that buys you another turn, or the dreaded Exploding Kitten waiting to ruin your day? The suspense is deliciously ridiculous.

A typical turn can go something like this:
- You play one or more cards from your hand to alter the course of the deck, target opponents, or unleash chaos.
- You draw a card from the deck. If it is an Exploding Kitten and you lack a Defuse card, you are out of the round. If you have a Defuse, you use it, place the kitten back into the deck somewhere (secretly, of course), and continue.

The action cards create a dynamic rhythm:
- Skip lets you avoid drawing for a turn, which can be a strategic clutch.
- See the Future allows you to peek at the top few cards of the deck, offering a glimpse into doom or a glimmer of hope.
- Shuffle reshuffles the deck; useful when the top of the deck is about to ruin your day.
- Attack forces the next player to take two turns in a row, which can set up a delightful chain of misfortune for your neighbor.
- Nope gives you the power to cancel another player’s action, which can lead to dramatic SHOWDOWN moments as you watch a cascade of cards bounce around the table.

The game ends when only one player remains after all others have drawn an exploding kitten and failed to defuse it. The last person standing is crowned the arcane, tabby-loving champion of chaos. It is simple enough for kids to understand, yet modern enough to generate real strategic wiggle room for adults who live for bluffing, misdirection, and the occasional head-nod to classic card tactics seen in more serious titles.

If you want a quick strategic read, here is a neat breakdown of how you can think about your choices on a turn: your goal is not just to avoid exploding; it is to shape a path for future draws that reduces the likelihood of ending up with an Exploding Kitten on your lap. That requires a mix of risk assessment, memory, and a dash of mind-games with your friends.

## Expansions, variants, and the 2018 edition shift
The 2018 Asmodee edition exists within a world of expansions and variants that broaden the scope of the base game. Asmodee has also invested in producing a sleek, more durable design, a tighter rulebook, and a packaging that makes it easier to balance a shelf full of games without sacrificing your sense of humor.
