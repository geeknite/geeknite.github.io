## Rules, flow, and the rhythm of the game
Zombicide is famous for encouraging cooperative play with a heavy dose of randomness. Rue Morgue Season 3 maintains the core pattern: players take turns performing actions to move, search, or fight the walking dead; then the zombies take their AI-driven actions. Add-ons like Guillotine influence the order or risk of certain actions, but the fundamental rhythm remains: plan, execute, laugh nervously, and suffer the consequences when the dice misbehave.

- Turn structure: Each turn offers an opportunity to move, search, or fight. You’ll frequently be balancing a desire to load up on loot with the risk that time is running out and the horde is closing in.
- Action economy: The base game’s action economy remains intact with one or two exceptions where Guillotine adds a weighty choice. It’s a good reminder that sometimes the best plan is the one that gets you to the next room without screaming in such a way that the neighbors call the police.
- AI Zombies: The zombies still operate with a simple but effective AI deck. They move toward survivors, with some types acting more aggressively. The design ensures you’ll feel the threat without needing to memorize three dozen corner cases.
- Victory conditions: Typical Rue Morgue victory conditions involve clearing the map, achieving an objective, and leaving with survivors intact. Guillotine introduces alternate win/lose paths that can spice up the final turns and push for shared risk-taking.

If you’re the type who loves crunchy rules and mechanical microgames, Rue Morgue Season 3 with Guillotine scratches that itch nicely. If you’re after a brisk, casually paced family game, you might want to play with fewer players or skip the add-on for a smoother evening. The fun is in the tension, the shared jokes about that one stubborn zombie, and the moment when your plan collapses into a dozen tiny heroic acts performed under fire.

## Strategy and player dynamics: who should play what roles?
The beauty of Zombicide, Rue Morgue included, is that players can adopt complementary roles. Some survivors excel at ranged combat, others at close-quarters melee, and a few shine in gathering resources and securing key objectives. Guillotine adds a twist that can influence which roles you prioritize in the early rounds. Here are a few practical takeaways:
- If you like to lead from the front, a strong survivor who can draw heat and soak up damage is essential. The lobby you’re defending will thank you for your bold front-liner when the horde shows up.
- If you’re more inclined to weave through rooms collecting gear, prioritize survivors with loot benefits and search bonuses. Guillotine can reward this “sneak and loot” plan, but it can also punish it if you misread the risk.
- Communication is king. The best runs happen when players react in concert to a sudden Guillotine push, coordinating to cover each other and share resources at the last possible second.
- Dice luck remains a fickle master. Even with good planning, you’ll still feel the sting when a critical melee attack misses by a sliver and a zombie arrives in the doorway with a chorus of groans.

If your group enjoys collaborative problem solving with a dash of dramatic chaos, Rue Morgue Season 3 plus Guillotine is a playground. If you prefer a stricter puzzle that requires fewer last-minute improvisations, you may want to bring in a house rule that smooths out the rare runaway moments the Guillotine module can cause.
