## Materials, build quality, and the long game
### Acacia wood: density, durability, and drama-free maintenance
Acacia wood is the evergreen underdog of the hardwood world. It’s dense, relatively resistant to moisture, and carries a warm, amber hue when finished. The durability is the big selling point for outdoor furniture. It’s not teak-level luxury, but it’s more budget-friendly and still holds its own against sun, humidity, and the occasional spilled sangria bottle that tries to audition for a role in your life.

That density translates into a few practical benefits:
- Resistance to minor scuffs and daily wear
- A natural gloss that can be enhanced with a sealant or oil
- A fairly forgiving finish when exposed to outdoor conditions (though not immune to weather extremes)

The caveat: acacia wood loves to move with moisture. With outdoor furniture, movement means potential cracking if the wood isn’t adequately sealed or if you skip re-oiling/finishing over time. Our advice: treat the surface with a UV-protective sealant intended for exterior wood every 6–12 months, depending on your climate. If you live in a sun-drenched desert or a rainforest-adjacent neighborhood, you’ll want to schedule maintenance with the precision of a playlist organizer for a road trip.

### Rattan chairs: form, function, and splash-proof swagger
The rattan-looking chairs deliver on the vibe you want for poolside lounging. They’re typically resin wicker or PE-woven accents over a sturdy frame. Why resin? It resists moisture, UV rays, and the occasional splash from a delighted dog who thinks the pool is a giant water bowl.

## Assembly, maintenance, and the day-to-day grind
### Assembly reality check
Most 5-piece outdoor sets will require basic assembly—the kind where you’ll need a screwdriver, perhaps an adjustable wrench, and the patience to decode a few instruction diagrams that look like they were drawn by a very artistic bat. Expect a 30–60 minute assembly window for two adults. The good news is that many sets ship with pre-drilled holes and labeled screws, which is the adult version of leveling up in a warehouse simulator. Pro tip: sort hardware by type and label your steps, especially if you’re assembling after a long day of pretending you’re a plant enthusiast.

### Maintenance: how to keep the wood and wicker happy
- Clean with mild soap and water; avoid harsh chemicals that strip oils from the wood
- Treat the wood with a UV-protective sealant or oil designed for exterior wood every 6–12 months, depending on climate
- If cushions are included or added, use waterproof covers and store cushions during heavy rain or winter months
- If you see signs of moisture-related problems (warping, swelling, or cracking), address quickly with sand-and-seal steps to prevent deeper damage
- Store or cover during extreme weather; even the best sets appreciate a little seasonal spa day

### Quick care checklist
A simple routine can extend the life of your set: sweep off debris weekly, wipe down with a damp cloth monthly, and inspect joints for looseness. If you catch a wobble early, tighten hardware and re-secure any loose screws. Your future self will thank your past self for not letting it become a wobble sculpture at the pool party.

## Maintenance calendar: a practical plan
- Month 1 after purchase: wipe down, inspect joints, apply a light sealant if recommended by the finish used on your set
- Months 6–12: reseal or oil the wood; inspect for any signs of cracking or moisture-induced issues
- Annually: deep clean and check for loose screws; consider a more robust sealant if your climate is harsh (sun, humidity, or salt air near the coast)
- Winter months: if you’re in a region with cold winters, store or cover the set to prevent moisture buildup

This cadence isn’t strict gospel; adapt to your climate. If you live in a sunny climate with heavy UV exposure, you’ll likely want to seal more often. If you’re in a mild climate with regular rainfall, a yearly refresh might be sufficient.
