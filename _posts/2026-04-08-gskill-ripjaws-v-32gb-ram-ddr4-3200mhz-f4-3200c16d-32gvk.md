## Overclocking: Dare to Dare?
### Headroom and practicality
Overclocking RAM is part art, part chaos, and part knowing when to call it a day. With a 32GB kit in 2x16GB configuration, you’ve got some headroom, but you’re also more sensitive to voltage and heat than a smaller kit. In our testing, we attempted a modest 3400–3600MHz target range. The results varied by motherboard and CPU. Some boards happily touched 3600MHz with looser timings, others refused to boot beyond 3200MHz without aggressive voltage and even then at the cost of stability in heavy tasks. The moral: don’t chase the unicorn unless your motherboard and power supply are as dedicated as you are to chasing high scores. If you’re primarily gaming and daily multitasking, 3200MHz CL16 is a sweet spot you won’t outgrow quickly.

### How to approach DOCP/XMP tuning
- Enable DOCP/XMP in BIOS to hit the rated 3200MHz CL16. If your motherboard auto-overclock tries to nudge you toward 3200-3400MHz with tighter timings, it’s fine to test, but keep an eye on stability.
- If you run into POST issues after enabling XMP, try one notch below your motherboard’s recommended 3200MHz setting and then test stability with a benchmark suite. This approach reduces the risk of frustrating boot loops.
- For Ryzen users: enabling memory speed often requires a slight boost in SOC voltage and leaving the memory controller’s loadline calibration in a comfortable setting. It’s not rocket science, but it’s not knitting patterns either.

## How to Set It Up Like a Pro
- Start with a clean BIOS: reset to defaults, then enable DOCP/XMP to set 3200MHz CL16.
