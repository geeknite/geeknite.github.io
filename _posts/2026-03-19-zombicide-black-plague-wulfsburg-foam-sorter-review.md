Foam inserts aren’t just about aesthetics; they’re about longevity and transport. The foam used for this insert is intended to resist compression from repeated insertions and removals of council-level numbers of minis, tokens, and cards. Additionally, the Wulfsburg expansion adds new terrain pieces and larger minis that can dwarf a normal storage approach. The Feldherr insert helps keep those pieces immobile and visually easy to locate under a quick glance. The downside: a slightly heavier box. A quick note on tear-down: the foam holds its shape, but if you tend to push minis into their spots with a little too much enthusiasm (you know who you are), you may eventually compress the foam in the most-loved mini slots. Over the course of many sessions, this can create a slight uneven feel. The fix is simple: rotate your minis or replace the foam after a year or two of heavy use. It’s not a design flaw; it’s a testament to the volume of miniatures you’re subjecting your foam to.
