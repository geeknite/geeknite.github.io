## How Phase 10 works, in case you forgot since the last time you swore you would be more productive

Phase 10 is a simple concept at heart: everybody draws cards and tries to complete a sequence of phases. Each phase has a different requirement (for example, a set of cards, a run, mixed sets, etc.). The trick is that you must complete the exact phase in order, moving to the next one after you complete the current one. The catch? If you fail to complete a phase in your hand, you keep playing and hope to complete the next phase, since only the first player to complete all ten phases wins the game.

This deluxe edition does not change the fundamental math of the game. It does, however, give you a slightly fancier stage for your memory to perform on. The cards are crisp enough that you won’t mistake a red 7 for a blue 7 (this is a real hazard in low-light game nights). The rulebook explains the phases with enough clarity to avoid the common pitfalls that plague casual games: misreading the requirements, miscounting the phase numbers, or misplacing the wild cards that can sash your strategy in unexpected ways.

A night with Phase 10 tends to tilt between two modes: the “we’re all in this together” mode and the “I can’t believe you just did that” mode. The deluxe tin adds a bit of ceremonial weight to the proceedings. You’re not just playing a card game; you’re pulling a little more history into your living space. ### A quick gameplay refresher (for those who need it)
- Each player receives a hand of cards. On your turn, you draw one card and discard one card, trying to assemble the current phase’s requirements.
- The phases progress from 1 to 10. Phase 1 typically asks for a basic set; Phase 2 might require a run; Phase 3 could mix sets and runs, depending on the rule set you’re using.
- You complete a phase by laying down the required cards. If you succeed, you advance to the next phase. If you don’t, your hand remains in flux and you press on, hoping the wheel of fortune tilts in your favor.
- The game ends when a player completes all ten phases. The rest is statistical dust, but it’s memorable dust rubbed into the dull corners of a living room by laughter and groans.

In a collectible edition, a lot of the entertainment comes from watching your friends approach phase 7 with the same calm expected from a calm ocean. Then a joker (or wild card, depending on your version) shows up, and suddenly the ocean becomes a kiddie pool at a summer fair. The antique tin provides a perfect soundtrack to those moments: a tiny clack when you shuffle, a soft rattle when you grab a card, and the satisfying click of a fully opened tin lid after a long night of questionable decisions.
