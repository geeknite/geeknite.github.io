## Gameplay: The Draft That Feels Like Speed Dating for Sushi
The core loop of Sushi Go Party is a fast, elegant drafting dance. In each round you deal a handful of cards in front of each player. On a cue, everyone picks one card from their hand and then passes the rest to the neighbor. The chosen cards are laid in front of you to build your evolving menu of combos. The scoring sheet tracks how well you align with the various recipes, from simple ones like Maki loves to more complicated stacks like Sashimi sets that require three cards to unlock the big points. The key to success is not simply grabbing the highest point card available but reading the room and predicting what your neighbor might pass. The presence of more menu options in Sushi Go Party means you have more dalliances to manage, more near misses, and more opportunities to snatch a surprise late in the round that tilts the entire game in your favor.

In the Deluxe Pick and Pass scenario, you get a little extra shimmy in your step: additional fancy options that can create dramatic snowball effects. The pick and pass rhythm remains a gauntlet of quick decisions, but the more cards you have in play, the more you need to weigh future turns against current gratification.
