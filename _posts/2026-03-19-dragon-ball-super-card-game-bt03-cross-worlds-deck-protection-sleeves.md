Material and durability

Sleeves live or die by their material. Cross Worlds sleeves use a standard protective PET film that feels sturdy without adding excessive bulk. The 60-card pack typically uses a 100–120 micron range for the outer protective layer, with an internal opacity that helps card text pop without sacrificing transparency for the art on the back. The matte back helps reduce glare from overhead lights, which is a blessing in tournament lounges that shine like a disco ball after round three. In practice, these sleeves hold up well against typical play wear: shuffles, taps, and the occasional tumble off the table when a desperate mulligan goes wrong. While not invincible, they should comfortably survive multiple tournament rounds if you handle them with the usual respect reserved for dragon balls and spicy memes.

Fit and compatibility with BT03 cards

BT03 Cross Worlds sleeves are designed for standard Dragon Ball Super Card Game deck sizes. They should accommodate your typical 50–60 card main deck, plus a small extra for the sideboard if your arena of choice allows it. They fit snugly enough that you won’t worry about mid-sleeve card sliding when you shuffle aggressively, but not so tight that you’re tearing the sleeve with every round. If you want to be ultra-safe for high-stress tournaments, you can double-sleeve in a pinch, though most players keep single sleeves for speed and comfort. The inner surface should be a smooth, low-friction experience that lets you slide cards in and out without friction burns and dramatic pops of plastic in the middle of a heated game. If your table shuffle is known for aggressive fanning, these sleeves should hold up without a dramatic “pop” that reveals the card’s back to your opponent.
