Material and durability

Sleeves live or die by their film. Cross Worlds uses a standard PET outer layer and a mid-weight construct designed to keep your cards feeling sturdy without turning your deck into a porcupine quill of bulk. The 60-card pack generally uses a 100–120 micron range for the outer protective layer, with internal opacity that helps card text pop while preserving the back art’s charm. The matte back reduces glare from overhead lighting, which is a blessing in tournaments where the ceiling lights seem to reproduce the sun’s glare and challenge your eyeballs. In practical use, these sleeves stand up to standard wear: shuffles, taps, table nudges, and the occasional accidental slide off the table edge when your mulligan goes spectacularly wrong. They’re not invincible, but they should handle several tournament days with your routine care and a little respect for dragon balls and spicy memes.
