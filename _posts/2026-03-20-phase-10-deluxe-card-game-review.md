## How to play Phase 10 Deluxe (the quick tour)

Phase 10 is a race to complete ten phases. Each phase requires a specific combination of cards. The first player to complete a phase lays down their cards in front of them and then draws again to begin their next phase once the round resets. The round ends when a player empties their hand, and scoring continues until someone hits a pre-determined target score or defeats everyone by running out of rounds first. The winner is the person with the lowest total score after all players have completed their phase run or after a certain number of rounds, depending on house rules and how aggressively you want to play.

### Setup
- Each player receives a color-coded set of tokens or a simple marker to track who is who. In Deluxe, you may also find a small reference card with the ten phase descriptions, so you do not have to memorize them mid-round and disrupt the flow with dramatic whispers of terror when you realize you forgot the fourth step.
- Shuffle the deck. Deal ten cards to each player, then place the rest in the stock pile. The discard pile sits nearby, ready to tempt your inner optimist into discarding the absolute wrong card at the wrong time.
- Decide the first player. The rest of the table can now pretend this is a carefully choreographed heist rather than a nightly ritual of mismanaged bets and accidental knowledge of how many cards you can legally hold in your hand before panic sets in.

### Turn structure
- On a turn, you draw one card from either the stock or the discard pile. This is where strategic planning meets desperate last-minute improvisation. The probability of you needing a particular card is never truly known, so shuffle and smile at your own bravery.
- After drawing, you may lay down your phase if you have the required cards in hand. This is where you bask briefly in the glow of validation before someone else steals your thunder with an even more perfect phase.
- You then discard one card to end your turn. The object is to avoid getting stuck with cards that do not help you progress toward your current phase, which is when the table’s mood can shift from cheerful to championship-grade petty.

### Phases and progression
The ten phases are the core mechanic that keeps Phase 10 rolling. They begin with relatively simple combos and escalate toward more complex arrangements. The exact phrasing of each phase can vary slightly by edition, but the spirit remains constant: you are assembling specific groups of cards while watching rivals do the same and hoping that fate smiles upon your chosen method. If you are curious about the official phase list, the Deluxe edition keeps this information accessible and clearly organized, which is a kindness to both memory and the nerves.

### Strategy notes for beginners
- Focus on flexibility: In early phases, you can mix sets and runs, and flexibility helps you adapt as the discard pile reveals new potential combos. Do not pin all your hopes on a single flawless path; the table is a moving target with humans at the helm.
- Use the discard wisely: You are allowed to pick from the discard pile, which is a temptation machine. If you see a card that can complete your current phase, do not be afraid to take the hit and claim it, even if it gives someone else a moment of righteous envy.
- Mind the timing: Phase 10 thrives on momentum. If you stall for too long, you risk not finishing your phase before the round ends and giving your competitors a nice head start on your score. Time management matters as much as card management.

### Strategy notes for veterans
- Diversify your approach: Once you have an inkling of how your opponents are stacking their plans, you may pivot. Do not insist on the same path every round; sometimes the best victory is the one you achieve by evolving your strategy mid-game.
- Watch the gaps: Runs can be long and elegant, but the real power often lies in catching a small set right after a long run. The shift between phases invites a moment of micro-prediction: what is the next card likely to be, and how can you leverage that to your benefit?
- Bluff responsibly: In a game that is essentially about counting and memory, bluffing is a pep talk to your own brain. If you can misdirect without breaking the pace, you add a layer of mind games that can tilt the table in your favor with minimal risk of physical violence or tears.
