## Maintenance and care: keeping Mia Wilhelmina in prime shape

The key to longevity with a hand-tufted wool rug is routine maintenance that respects the fibers. Wool is naturally resistant to many common stains, and the linen component helps with breathability and moisture management. Here are practical tips I learned during a two-week test drive:

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle or low-suction setting to prevent the pile from flattening. Use a brushless attachment to avoid pulling on the fibers.
- Rotate every few months to even out wear and avoid unsightly traffic patterns.
- If a stain happens, blot quickly with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the pile.
- For tougher spots, a mild wool-safe cleaner works wonders. Always test in a hidden corner first.
- Consider a rug pad underneath to improve durability and comfort. A good pad reduces movement and protects both rug and floor from sliding and scuff marks.

In terms of longevity, wool fibers are robust and naturally resilient. They resist crushing, which means the tufted surface stays uniform longer. Linen adds stability to the weave, reducing the chance of the pile slipping or developing loose threads after heavy foot traffic. This combination is a win for households that treat floors like a non-volatile storage system for their life’s clutter.
