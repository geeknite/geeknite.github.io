### Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance: A Quick Practical Guide

One of the strongest reasons to choose a synthetic rug is ease of maintenance. Spills? Blot it with a clean cloth. Pet accidents? Blot and treat with a gentle cleaner that’s safe for synthetic fibers. The key is to act quickly; the faster you respond, the less likely a stain will set. The Satori Canvas cleans up relatively easily because of its dense, non-absorbent surface. For routine care, a quick vacuum or a sweep with a dust mop is typically enough to keep it looking sharp. 

Avoid harsh chemicals or bleach-based cleaners that can degrade fiber integrity and color over time. If you do end up with a stubborn stain, test any cleaner on a small inconspicuous area first to confirm it won’t dull or discolor the tone. For high-traffic zones, you might consider rotating the rug every few months to even out wear. And if you have a little one who thinks the rug is a canvas for finger-paint masterpieces, a damp cloth and mild solution usually does the trick without harming the fibers. 

#### Non-Slip Backing and Placement Considerations

The backing on most canvas-style rugs adds some grip, but it’s wise to pair the rug with a non-slip pad, especially on slick floors like polished concrete or laminate. A pad helps reduce shifting, which in turn reduces the risk of slips and foot traffic creating a wavy edge along the border. If you’re using the rug in a doorway or hallway, a pad becomes your best friend. It’s the unsung hero of rug longevity.
