## What is Exploding Kittens

Exploding Kittens is a fast-paced, unpredictably silly card game built around the core mechanic of avoiding drawing an exploding kitten card while playing various action cards to influence the fate of the deck, your opponents, or your own hand. The game features bright, cartoonish art courtesy of The Oatmeal and a rule set designed to keep everyone involved, even when your strategy involves bluffing, stealing, and the occasional dramatic defuse with a hand full of Defuse cards. Simple to learn, surprisingly strategic, and loud enough to wake the cat (if you have one).

### The Core Turn Structure
A typical turn looks like this: you draw one card, then you may play as many action cards as you want, as long as you don’t break the bank of your luck. The draw phase continues until someone hits an exploding kitten and fails to defuse it, at which point that player is out of the round. The last kitten-avoiding soul standing wins the round, and the game continues for multiple rounds until someone reaches the target score. The simplicity of the flow is the engine that keeps it accessible for casual players while still offering enough randomness to keep players guessing.

## Setting Up for 2-5 Players

The 2-5 player configuration is the sweet spot that makes Exploding Kittens work as a family night staple. It scales from intimate duels to small shindigs with a few friends. Here’s a quick primer to get you ready for game night:

- Shuffle the deck and deal a set number of cards to each player (the exact number varies with player count, but you’ll always start with a few Defuse cards in hand to keep the tension check from turning into chaos).
- Each player gets a token of life, a little buffer against the inevitable explosion that might occur when the deck feels spicy.
- Place the Exploding Kitten deck-facing upward in the center, and keep the Defuse deckdf somewhere accessible—because defusal is your best friend and sometimes your only shield.

### 2-Player Mode: Intense and Strategic
In a two-player variant, the game becomes a micro chess match with the bomb deck. You’ll be calculating what your opponent can do, what odd card combinations will force them to draw, and whether you dare to bluff or hold your biggest cards for a late-game swing. It’s less chaotic in 1v1, more controlled in terms of who blinks first—and infinitely more dramatic when you pull a Defuse card at the exact moment the Exploding Kitten makes a grand entrance.

## How to Play: A Quick Run-Through

- Draw a card at the start of your turn from the central deck.
- Play any number of action cards from your hand. These can do things like “Attack,” which forces the next player to take two turns in a row, or “Skip,” which, unsurprisingly, lets you skip your draw and keep your head cool.
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out of the round. If you do have a Defuse card, you get to place the Exploding Kitten back into the deck anywhere you want, typically in a way that will haunt someone later.

Key card types include Defuse (the lifeboat), Attack (turns to overwhelm), Shuffle (applied randomness), See the Future (peek at the top cards), and Nope (as in, your neighbor blocks your plan with a smug silence). The balance of luck and strategic play is what makes each round feel like a unique story rather than a repetitive loop.

## Practical Tips for Family Game Night

- Start with a reset ritual: agree on the number of rounds you’ll play and set a fun, non-competitive objective that centers on shared experience rather than pure victory.
- Use house rules to tailor for your audience: if you have small children, consider limiting the number of Attack cards or removing overly chaotic combos that derail the fun.
- Rotate who explains the rules at the start of each game; that person becomes the designated DJ of the evening, delivering narration and humor as needed.
- Keep a small note nearby listing common combos and how to counter them. It’s surprising how a simple cheat sheet can keep the game moving and everyone smiling.
- Consider thematic snacks that match the cat-crazy vibe. A little edible chaos can amplify the experience without overpowering the game’s pace.
