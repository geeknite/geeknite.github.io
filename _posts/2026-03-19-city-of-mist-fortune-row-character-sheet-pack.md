Fortune Row draws a clear line between identity and powers. The result is a sheet that encourages character development rather than a sprint to the next fight.

## Playability: scenarios and table dynamics

### Quick-start usability
If you are a GM who wants players to dive into action quickly, Fortune Row helps you do that without forcing new vocabulary or a maze of new grids. A first-time player can pick up the sheet and understand their identity arc and power options at a glance. The sheets also reveal proximity to maxing out a mythic power and track downtime opportunities, which is particularly valuable when you have a mix of veterans and newcomers around the table.

### A session flow friendly design
The layout aligns with a standard City of Mist arc: identify and reveal, empower, conflict, resolve, and reflect. The sheets consolidate essential data so you do not need to juggle a dozen separate sheets or loose notes. If you love margins full of doodles and cross references, you will value having most the critical data on a single page with clean, legible sections.

### Tabletop coordination tips
Fortune Row shines when you combine it with a few table ready rituals. For example, pair your mythic cost tracking with a small set of tokens that sit near the sheet; each time a character taps into a power, you move a token. This tactile feedback enhances dramatic reveals and makes the moment feel costly and consequential. If you prefer dice dialogue, consider using color coded dice pools that represent different relationships, letting players narrate the action while you roll for outcomes.
