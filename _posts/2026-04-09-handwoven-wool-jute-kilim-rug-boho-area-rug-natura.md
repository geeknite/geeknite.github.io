## Practical Considerations: Care, Cleaning, and Comfort

### Care and Maintenance: Simple Steps for Long Life

Wool and jute respond well to regular vacuuming to remove surface dust and grit that can abrade fibers over time. Use a gentle setting and avoid aggressive beater brushes that could fuzz the surface. For spills, blot with a clean cloth and avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the weave. If you accidentally spill something stubborn like red wine, act fast and test a small area first with a mild detergent solution. Always follow up with thorough rinsing to avoid residue that could attract more grime. Because Natura is natural fiber heavy, avoid prolonged exposure to direct sun in the same spot to minimize any potential fading. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color balance across the floor.

### Shedding and Texture: What to Expect

A natural wool and jute blend may shed a little during the first weeks of use. Regular vacuuming will mitigate this, and you will notice the shedding decrease as the fibers settle in. The texture is tactile and inviting; you will notice a subtle rougher side where the jute is more prominent and a softer, woolier touch where fibers are denser. This dual-texture characteristic is part of Natura’s charm and makes it easier to place this rug in rooms with both high-traffic zones and seating areas where you want a more plush touch underfoot.

### Seasonal Care: Humidity and Moisture Considerations

Natural fibers are responsive to humidity. In very damp climates, you may want to place a breathable rug pad underneath to act as a moisture barrier and reduce creeping dampness. In dry environments, you may notice a tiny amount of static on low-humidity days; a simple humidifier in the room can help smooth things out. The goal is to keep the rug comfortable to walk on year-round without encouraging mold or other drama that textile enthusiasts pretend they never worry about but secretly do.
