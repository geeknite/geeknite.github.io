## Setup and getting started

Setting up is surprisingly quick. You spread the deck, shuffle, and deal to each player until everyone has a stack. In 2 player mode you can keep it simple with a standard take turn approach, while in the full 10 player mode you will likely want to set up teams or create a rotating dealer to keep the pace from stalling. The family pack includes a couple of house rule suggestions that speed things up and keep the vibe light and humorous.

- Shuffle and deal 7 cards to each player
- Place the rest of the deck face down as the draw pile
- Flip the top card to begin the discard pile and apply any starting effects
- In large groups, declare team based play; teams sit around the table and communicate strategy as needed

Tips for a smooth start: designate a captain or a chaos controller to remind everyone of the UNO rule to shout UNO when you have one card left. This reduces the going numbers of repeats as the night grows longer. Also consider designating a silly penalty for forgetful players, such as wearing a santa hat for the remainder of the round.

## How to play with 2 to 10 players

The core UNO gameplay remains the same, but the family pack enhances compatibility with multiple players through two key features: streamlined rules and optional group friendly variants.

### Two player mode
In two player mode, you can run classic UNO with a few light modifications to prevent the match from ending too quickly. A few fun tweaks include increasing the starting hand to 9 cards, adding a mini mulligan after the first two rounds, or simply keeping a strict pace for drawing cards so the endgame feels like a dramatic duel rather than a rapid fire event.

### Large group mode
With 6 to 10 players, the pack encourages team play, rotating dealers, and a few additional rules to accommodate a crowded table. A common approach is team play where teams alternate turns but share a hand, creating moments of strategy that require good communication and silent nods when a teammate slides out a crucial color. This approach also reduces table hogging and helps the group maintain energy.

A practical trick for larger groups is to use a timer. You set a 60 second window for a hand and if a player is caught overthinking while a time bomb of a Draw Two is looming, the other players can hold a chorus of light hearts and friendly prods. The idea is to keep it lively and ensure no single player holds up the game long enough to disrupt the holiday mood.

## House rules and variants

Every family is unique, and the UNO FAMILY pack invites a few optional house rules that can be mixed or matched. Here are a few favorites:

- Team mode with shared hand: players in the same team share a hand, encouraging cooperative bluffing and a surprising amount of group victory dances
- Color bonus rounds: once in a while you declare a color bonus round where any card of that color is worth extra points or a small prize for whoever finishes with it still in hand
- Sudden death draw: if the draw pile runs low, shuffle the discard pile to create a new draw deck with heightened tension
- Santa shuffle: a random rule card is revealed every few rounds that changes the objective slightly, making it feel fresh without breaking the core rules
