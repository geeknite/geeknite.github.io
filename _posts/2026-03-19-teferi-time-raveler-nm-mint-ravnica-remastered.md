## Practical Buying Guide: What to Look For in NM-Mint Copy

- Surface and edges: Look for clean borders, no whitening, and minimal scratches on the card face. NM-Mint means you’re shopping for a card that looks almost as good as the day it left the factory, with the kind of polish that makes it feel special in a binder or display.
- Foil versus non-foil: If you’re into foils, beware of warping in high-humidity environments. Non-foil NM-Mint is often easier to maintain long-term, but foils have their own unique charm when you want a little extra bling on your board.
- Color consistency: Verify the ink color looks crisp and even. Color variation is natural in older reprints, but serious NM-Mint copies show a uniform blue that doesn’t look washed out in bright room lighting.
- Printing alignment: A clean alignment from the top to bottom is a small but visible sign of good print quality. You don’t want a card with skewed text or a misaligned border when you’re proudly placing it on the display shelf.
- Authenticity cues: In the world of MTG you’ll want to avoid fakes and misprints. Look for the typical set symbol, mana cost placement, and the collector’s number on the bottom left, and make sure the back of the card matches the standard card back for the set.
