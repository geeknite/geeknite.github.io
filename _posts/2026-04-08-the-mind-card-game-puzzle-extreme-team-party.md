## Strategies: tips for teams that want to win (or at least not roast each other alive)
- Communicate through rhythm: Use hand taps, finger snaps, or a discreet nod to signal timing without shouting. The subtle cues become a dance as you learn when to push and when to slow the tempo.
- Assign rotating roles: Let one player handle the pacing, another manage the risk, and a third observe the collective mood. Rotating roles keeps the group from stagnating and adds a dash of improvisation.
- Read the room, not just the cards: The best teams tune into the energy in the room. If the table is tight-lipped and nervous, you might slow down; if it’s buzzing with energy, you might speed up to ride the crest of the wave.
- Divide and conquer on puzzle cues: Some rounds require compartmental thinking—split the puzzle into smaller sections and assign teammates to handle each section’s rhythm, then reassemble for the final pass.
- Embrace the misplays: It’s easy to get frustrated when a card is played out of order. Treat it as a plot twist and a chance to learn, not as a personal indictment. The table will thank you for it with louder laughs and brighter smiles.
