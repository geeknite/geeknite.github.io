## Ergonomics and Comfort

Ergonomics is not just a buzzword here; it’s something the designers clearly argued about at length, likely with coffee spills as a witness. Each seat can be adjusted for height in a surprisingly ergonomic fashion, and the space between seats is generous enough to avoid the classic elbow-in-face fights during a last-minute demo. The desk height favors a comfortable neutral posture for most adults, though tall folks might enjoy an extra inch or two of leg room by configuring the set into a slightly different pattern. If you have team members who love to stand more than sit, there is also an optional standing surface that can be integrated without ruining symmetry or the aesthetic of your open office ecosystem.

There’s also a subtle nod to privacy without isolating people completely. The result is a space that encourages collaboration without devolving into a noise-driven cave of chaos.

## Practical setup tips and maintenance notes

- Start with a pilot layout: take a single 2-person module and set it up in a temporary conference corner before expanding to a full ten-seat run. This helps you identify glare, noise, and foot traffic issues before you invest heavily.
- Label and color-code: cables under desks can quickly become a treasure map of spaghetti. A simple color-coding system for power, data, and headphone cables will save time and headaches when you reconfigure during onboarding.
- Schedule quarterly reviews: every three months, reassess how the space supports your workflows. If you find your team is staging meetings that resemble improv nights, adjust layout and maybe add a whiteboard or two.
- Keep the office vibe healthy: encourage ergonomics, but also encourage micro-breaks. A ten-seat bench is excellent for collaboration; it’s not a substitute for regular movement.
