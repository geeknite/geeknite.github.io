## Maintenance and care

No gadget survives on hope alone. Like any mechanical accessory, the EL300952 benefits from regular maintenance to maintain reliability over time. Here are practical tips that keep the feeder singing rather than screeching:

- Routine cleaning: Wipe the rollers gently with a lint-free cloth and a small amount of isopropyl alcohol if you notice residue or groaning noises. Do not soak anything; this is not a spa day for the feeder.
- Check sheet alignment: A few misfeeds can cascade into bigger jams. If you notice alignment drift, re-check the feed height adjustment and ensure the tray is seating evenly.
- Stock quality matters: Thin or warped stock can create feeding irregularities. Maintain a consistent stock quality to minimize issues.
- Inspect connectors: Periodically inspect the cable connections between the feeder and the host device. Loose connectors can cause intermittent feeding or misreads.

The maintenance ritual is not glamorous, but it is remarkably consistent. If you schedule a quarterly check and a monthly wipe down, you’ll reduce the chances of mid-run drama dramatically.

## Troubleshooting quick reference

Even the most well-built feeder has a nemesis: the occasional jam. Here is a compact cheat sheet to handle the common issues without turning the office into a melodrama:

- First step: power off and reseat the feeder. Sometimes a tiny reinitialization is all that is needed to calm the machine.
- Check the stack height: If you feed past the recommended maximum, you risk misfeeds. Observing the 250 sheet limit helps a lot here.
- Confirm stock variety: Nonstandard stock can cause feeding issues. If you must use it, stagger the input so the feeder has time to adjust.
- Look for obstructions: Dust, stray papers, or small paper clips can jam the path. Clear any visible obstructions and re-test with a smaller load.
- Recalibrate: Some models require recalibration after installation or after a jam. Follow the service manual to run a calibration cycle if available.

If the jam persists after these steps, consult Fujifilm support or check online user forums for model-specific tips. The key is to avoid force feeding; patience is your friend here.
