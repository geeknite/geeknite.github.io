## Practicality: Fit, Feel, and Function

### Card fit and shuffle experience

One of the biggest questions with any new sleeve line is whether it fits standard DBS cards snugly enough to prevent odd edges from catching during the shuffle. These sleeves are a touch looser than some ultra-tight options, but not in a way that invites card warping or accidental damage. They slide smoothly, the opening is easy enough to grab without bending the deck, and they don’t cling to your fingers like a clingy cosplay prop. In practice, you’ll be able to shuffle with confidence, and that’s half the battle won. The gloss finish—while shiny enough for social media—still provides enough grip to prevent slipping mid-swing, which is exactly what you want when you’re trying to bluff your opponent with a well-timed “I’m totally confident I just pulled a Nappa” face.

### Durability and sleeve feel

Durability matters when you’re staring down a tournament schedule that reads like a grocery list of stress. The Special Anniversary Sleeves boast a respectable thickness without feeling like you’re boxing your cards in a brick. They resist minor creases and hold up through a few typical shuffles and card taps. The finish resists fingerprints reasonably well, though, let’s be honest, you’ll still leave your own souvenir handprints on them after a long game. If you’re a casual player who loves to show off a glossy deck between matches, you’ll appreciate the way these sleeves hold up under normal wear and tear. If you’re a hardcore grinder who lives for long weekend tournaments, you may want to pair these with a protective playmat and a sturdy deck box to maximize longevity.
