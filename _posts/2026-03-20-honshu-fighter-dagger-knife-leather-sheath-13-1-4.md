## Maintenance: Keeping the Edge Alive
Stainless steel helps with corrosion resistance, but it isn’t magic. After use, wipe the blade dry to remove moisture and oils, then apply a light coat of oil to keep the steel from drying out and to slow the onset of rust in humid environments. Store the dagger in its sheath to protect both blade and leather from moisture and incidental knocks.

Sharpening is straightforward. A basic two-step approach (coarse and fine) will bring back most of the edge. For double-edged blades, make sure to maintain even bevels on both edges to avoid uneven wear. If you’re new to sharpening, consider a guided sharpener or a clamping jig to maintain consistent angles. The goal is to make the blade perform reliably rather than turning it into a surgical instrument that only a blade master can wield.
