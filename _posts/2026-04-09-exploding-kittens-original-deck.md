## What is Exploding Kittens Original Deck?
The Original Deck is a fast, chaotic, push-your-luck card game for 2-5 players (depending on how many nap rooms your group has). The aim is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card while using the rest of the cards to dodge, deflect, and steal your way to victory. It is like a microcosm of every RPG party, except without the dragons and with more explosions.

In technical terms: a deck of 56 cards, comprising Defuse cards, Attack, Skip, See the Future, Shuffle, Nope, and of course Exploding Kittens. You play a turn by drawing a card, then possibly playing one or more cards from your hand to influence the next draw. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you dont have a Defuse card, you are out. If you do have a Defuse, you survive—at the cost of your Defuse being used, and the kitten ends up back in the deck.

The humor is in the combination of absurd art and the quick speed. The art style is a playful take on web comics, with cute but sinister cats, explosive devices, and a surprising amount of sass.

## Gameplay: The Wild Dance of Cards
Captain Strategy aside, Exploding Kittens is a social game that thrives on chaos and misdirection. Gameplay is fast, the turns are short, and the tension builds like a kettle that refuses to stop whistling. Here are the core mechanics that your group will either master in one session or forget entirely in the next:

- Draw cards until someone pulls an Exploding Kitten
- Use Defuse cards to survive Exploding Kittens or to swap the bomb elsewhere
- Attack, See the Future, and Nope to mess with opponents
- Shuffle and Skip to control the tempo of the deck

The art does a lot of heavy lifting in this game. It communicates a lot with just a glance—the kitten grin when you fail, the smug look of the Defuse cards, the smugger look of your friends when they catch you with a card that will wipe out an entire round.

### Controls and Card Types
- Exploding Kitten: the one you never want to draw; draw it and you are out unless you have a Defuse.
- Defuse: gave you a second chance; you can save yourself and reshuffle the bomb back into the deck.
- See the Future: peek at the top three cards of the deck.
- Attack: make the next player take two turns.
- Skip: end your turn immediately.
- Nope: stop any action at any time.
- Shuffle: shuffle the draw pile.
- Other action cards: various comedic effects that either save you or ruin someone else day.

The deck is well-balanced for a casual group, but it is not designed for intense long-run strategy. The game shines in quick rounds that end in laughter rather than silent contemplation.

## Strategy: How to Win (Sometimes)
Look, Exploding Kittens is not a deep strategy game; it is a social game with occasional deep moments. The best strategy is always: don't pick the wrong kit. But since you are reading a Geeknite review about a game with a bomb joke at its core, here are some tips that might help you avoid the blast.

- Save Defuse cards for the moment you actually need them. Don't waste them on silly threats unless you're sure you'll survive.
- Use See the Future to map out your odds. If the top of the deck is full of bombs, you should consider passing the risk to someone else.
- Use Attack strategically to disrupt players who are near the end of the draw; you'll want to push a near-victory player into a more precarious position.
- Nope is a powerful card; you can stop any action, but you should think carefully about what you're stopping. Sometimes your own plan benefits from a little chaos.

- The element of social interaction is crucial. You will often be negotiating, bluffing, and even bluff-deflecting, which makes Exploding Kittens as much a party game as a strategy game.
