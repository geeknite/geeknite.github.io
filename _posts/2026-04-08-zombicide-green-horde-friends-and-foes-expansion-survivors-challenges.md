What’s in the box and what it changes

The Friends and Foes expansion brings a tidy little pile of new content that feels well integrated with the base Green Horde. You get a handful of new survivor cards, a couple of new enemy types that you only wish you could recruit to your side, a handful of new mission cards, and tokens to track the new actions. The design intent seems to be: give players more ways to influence the tide of battle without turning Zombicide into a full-blown campaign mode.

Survivors: four new survivors with distinct personalities and a few signature cards. They’re not just the standard guy with a gun or girl with a bow, but rather characters who feel like people who would join your group if you fed them enough chorizo and promises of glory. Each survivor has a couple of unique abilities that interact with the Green Horde’s green-skull theme. On the table, these abilities translate into new ways to manipulate the terrain, rescue teammates, or deal damage in clever, satisfying ways.

Foes: a couple of enemy types that shake up the normal rhythm. The Green Horde already throws the occasional clearing-horde in your face, but the Friends and Foes content adds new variants that require you to adapt. The enemies often have special skills, like latching on to your survivors or creating fog-of-war style zones that force careful planning.

Quests: two or more new mission cards that can slot into a game with your current scenario selection. They come with tailored rules that make them feel distinct without requiring you to memorize a whole second set of mechanics. The missions are designed to feel like quests in a fantasy-lanting world rather than slots in a cookie-cutter survival ... and yes, there are boss-like moments that require you to think through your approach rather than simply roll-down the doom.

Thematic feel and artwork

Green Horde has a very specific vibe: a swampy, hairy, orc-y chaos with a sense of humor and a willingness to lean into the chaos. The Friends and Foes expansion keeps that vibe intact, while opening new doors for role-playing-esque moments at the table. The art is sturdy and consistent with the base game; you won’t be distracted by mismatchy miniature sculpts.

Game balance and play times

One of the big questions with any expansion is whether it unbalances the game or just adds a few optional toys. In my testing, the expansion sits in an interesting place: it adds more tactical depth without straying too far from what makes Zombicide Green Horde so approachable in the first place. The missions are designed for the standard 1-2 hour range, depending on player count and skill; the new survivors can be potent, but not so potent that you can cheese your way to victory in a single turn.

Strategy tips

- Focus on exploration and positioning: the new survivors’ abilities often reward you for being good at map control. Use leaders and shoot-and-move sequences to maximize the effect of terrain interactions. 
- Don’t ignore the new foes: they require different tactics from the standard zombies. You might need to split your team or set up choke points to reduce their effectiveness.
- Mix and match: the expansion is meant to be integrated with base rules; keep your house rules gentle at first, then start layering the complexity as players get comfortable.

Pros and cons

Pros
- Adds meaningful new mechanics without overcomplicating the base game
- The survivors feel distinct and fun to play
- The foes spice things up and encourage tactical thinking
- Reasonable price point for the content you get

Cons
- Some players might wish for more missions or more alignment with the lore
- The new components can take up a little more shelf space
- If you’re a solo-only player, the expansion’s value is less obvious

Conclusion: is this expansion for you?

In short, if you own Zombicide Green Horde and you crave new flavors, the Friends and Foes expansion is a solid pick. It injects a friendly dose of chaos and adds some interesting strategies that keep the game fresh without forcing you into a full campaign mode. It’s not a must-have, but it’s a smart buy for players who want to extend the life of their table and add a few memorable moments to their nights of survival.

Final recommendation

If you’re a fan of Zombicide Green Horde and you want to push your table into new territory, buy the expansion. If you like mixing things up and you don’t mind juggling a few new rules, it’s worth it. If you’re new to Zombicide, start with the base game, then circle back to this one.
