## How to Play: A Quick Drive-Through

### Setup

Each player receives a stock pile and a hand of five cards. The draw pile sits in the center, ready to feed your strategic hunger. Your goal is simple in concept but surprisingly nuanced in execution: play to reveal all your stock cards by building piles that rise in sequential order from 1 to 12. When a player can no longer play, they draw back up to five cards from the draw pile and pass the turn. The first player to empty their stock pile shouts victory, a tradition that pairs nicely with celebratory high-fives, groans from the person who still has three stock cards, and a quiet empathy for the deck that is about to receive another refresh.

### Turn Structure

On your turn, you should look for every possible play: you can lay down from your hand onto the center piles, you can move cards between your hand and the center as long as you maintain the sequences, and you can use wild cards to advance a difficult pile. The moment you hit a stall—when you cannot place any card—farm out a draw from the draw pile and take your turn again. The dynamic is the heartbeat of the game: it rewards memory, planning, and the timely sacrifice of a card you hoped to save for a bigger move.

### Endgame and Variations

The endgame is a satisfying sprint. If you are playing with a family or a group of friends who enjoy a bit of chaos, you can introduce variants such as timer rounds, additional wild-card rules, or cooperative play where the entire table pools toward a shared stock pile with a team win condition. The 2011 edition remains flexible enough to support these, without requiring any house rule clarifications that would derail the kids who are still learning the concept of sequencing without a tutor. If you want to experiment, a common variant is to allow players to exchange cards from their hand with the top card of the draw pile as a way to re-balance mid-game trouble spots. The joy here is that the card management is intuitive enough for kids while complex enough to amuse adults who enjoy a short but satisfying puzzle.
