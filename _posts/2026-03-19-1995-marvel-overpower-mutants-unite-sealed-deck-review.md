## Display, Packaging, and Preservation

A sealed deck is a dream for display: it looks pristine on a shelf, can act as a focal point in a retro gaming corner, and doubles as a reminder of the tactile joy of opening something new—especially in a hobby where newness is a rare commodity after twenty‑plus years. The packaging itself is part of the artifact: the box art, the gloss level, and the internal layout all contribute to the deck’s presentable value. If you’re considering opening the deck for play, proceed with caution: the edges of older cards can become brittle, and the once‑crisp art can show minor wear after years of handling. Some collectors prefer to maintain a separate display copy and a separate play copy to preserve the original look while still enjoying the tactile pleasures of a live game night.
