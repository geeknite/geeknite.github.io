## Setup and Play Time

## How to Play: A Quick Overview
If you’ve played Sushi Go or any drafting game before, you’re already halfway there. Each player starts with a hand of cards. You simultaneously select a card and pass the rest to your neighbor. Your choice should maximize your scoring potential for that menu board’s rules while minimizing your opponent’s gains. In the party edition, the addition of multiple menus means there’s always something new to chase, whether it’s ectoplasm-colored midori or a neon set of sushi that would make a disco ball blush.

### The Rules, Made Simple (for This Post)
- Draft and pass: pick one card, pass the rest.
- End-of-round scoring: once all cards have been drafted, you tally points based on the current menu board’s scoring rules.
- Global tie-breakers: sometimes nothing feels better than a tie you somehow break with a single nigiri.
- Strategy: you’ll want to balance short-term gains with long-term goals, and you’ll often have to accept that your competitor will one-up you on some rounds.
