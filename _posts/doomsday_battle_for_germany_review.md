## The Basics

### What is The Doomsday Project?

**The Doomsday Project: Episode One, The Battle for Germany** is a turn-based strategy game set during World War II. Players take on the roles of key commanders and nations involved in the Battle for Germany, navigating the complexities of wartime strategy, diplomacy, and combat. It's like Risk on steroids, but with more historical accuracy and fewer arguments over who gets Australia.

### Components

The game comes with beautifully designed maps, detailed unit tokens, and a plethora of cards that represent various events, strategies, and resources. The artwork is so impressive, you might find yourself framing the map instead of playing the game. And let's not forget the rulebook, which is thicker than a novel but surprisingly readable.

## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setting up the game is akin to mobilizing for war – it requires patience, precision, and possibly a snack break. Once you've laid out the maps, tokens, and cards, you're ready to dive into the strategic depths of WWII.

### Turn Phases

Each turn consists of several phases:

1. **Planning Phase**: Players plan their movements, allocate resources, and decide on strategies.
2. **Action Phase**: Execute movements, engage in combat, and use event cards.
3. **Resolution Phase**: Resolve combat outcomes, manage casualties, and update the game board.

### Strategy and Diplomacy

The game shines in its strategic depth and diplomatic nuances. As a commander, you must carefully plan your moves, negotiate with other players, and anticipate enemy actions. It's like playing chess, but with tanks, planes, and a lot more shouting.
