## Condition, grading, and value in MP form
### What MP means and why it matters
Moderately Played is a widely understood descriptor in card grading circles. It typically signals that the card shows minor signs of use: corner wear, light edge whitening, maybe a micro nick here or there on the sleeve edges. It does not indicate deep creasing, major bends, or staining. For Erika's Gloom (46/132), an MP copy in Unlimited printing carries a charm all its own. The nostalgia is tangible; the visible wear can even lend a sense of “battle-ready romance” if you’re the kind of collector who appreciates the artifact’s story as much as its numerical grade.

### How to assess an MP Erika's Gloom
- Centering: Many older cards were printed with imperfect centering. If your MP Erika's Gloom shows decent centering (roughly 60/40 or better on left-to-right and top-to-bottom), you’re in a good range.
- Corners: Look for small dings or whitening at the tips. A few microscopic abrasions can still be acceptable for MP; bigger corner damage will nudge it toward heavily played territory.
- Edges: Bumping along the edges is common in older stock. MP copies should have minimal-edge chipping relative to the card’s wear; obvious edgewear will drop the grade.
- Surface: Flat surfaces with minimal scuffing or scratches are preferred. A light dusting or minor fingerprint marks may be present, but you’ll want to clean the surface as you would with a vintage binder to keep that nostalgic glow.
- Print quality: Some text or artwork faintness may occur due to aging ink. That’s normal for a card this age and often part of the charm.
