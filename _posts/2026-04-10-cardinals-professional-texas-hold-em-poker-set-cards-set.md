## Maintenance, care, and longevity: can this set withstand the long run?
A poker set is a long-term investment in both time and social currency. Here are maintenance tips that help you maximize longevity:

- Cards: Keep them dry, avoid direct sunlight, and store them in the case when not in use. The coating should resist fingerprints and smudges, but it won’t hurt to wipe the surface with a dry microfiber cloth every few weeks.
- Chips: If you spill something on the chips, wipe them with a damp cloth and let them air dry. Avoid harsh solvents; you don’t want to strip the color coating or produce a chemically enhanced “glow” under club lighting. If chips start chipping at the edges, you might want to retire them to the “practice pile” and grant them pension status.
- Case: Regularly check the latch and padding. If the case starts to feel loose or creaky, you’re not just transporting a poker set—you’re transporting potential heartbreak. Tighten any loose screws; replace padding if it wears thin.
- Routine checks: Do a quick physical audit before game night. Ensure there are no missing cards, misprinted chips, or rogue buttons masquerading as a blinds game. A quiet audit prevents chaos at the table and reduces the risk of someone claiming you’re “cheating” by misplacing a dealer button under the sofa.
