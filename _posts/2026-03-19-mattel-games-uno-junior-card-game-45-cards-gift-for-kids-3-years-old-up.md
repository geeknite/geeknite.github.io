## How to play UNO Junior: a quick primer
UNO Junior aims for approachable, short rounds that fit inside a living room time window. Here is a kid friendly how to play guide you can share with a preschool assistant or a caregiver during a family game night.

### Setup
- Shuffle the 45 cards well. In a two to four player setup, deal five cards to each player. If you have more players, consider adjusting the hand size to keep rounds snappy and fun for everyone.
- Place the remaining deck in the center as the draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile.

### The turn
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the icon on the top card of the discard pile.
- If you cannot make a legal play, draw one card from the draw pile. If that card can be played, you may place it immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.
- When you have one card left, there is a moment of suspense. In the original UNO, players shout UNO. In UNO Junior this can be optional, but it does help remind others to stay engaged and not miss the final moment of dramatic cunning by the other players.

### End of round
- The first player to shed all their cards wins the round. You can play multiple rounds and track wins, or simply play for the joy of a quick, giggly game before bedtime.

### Special notes for three year olds
- Reinforce color identification by calling out colors during play.
- Encourage gentle competitive behavior by praising good sportsmanship, regardless of who wins.
- Keep the pace light; kids in this age range thrive on quick rounds and clear, positive feedback.

## Getting the most out of UNO Junior: play ideas
- Theme nights: Pick a color, and everyone must only play cards in that color for a round. It’s a tiny twist that adds novelty without sacrificing simplicity.
