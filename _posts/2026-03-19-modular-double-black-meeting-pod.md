If you are shopping for a meeting pod, acoustic performance is the feature that will decide whether you get any actual work done or just a bunch of muted arguments with a side of minorly improved video call quality. The Modular Double Black Meeting Pod claims to offer high STC (Sound Transmission Class) ratings thanks to layered mineral wool, dense acoustic foam, and a sealed door gasket that makes the experience of closing the door feel like you just boarded a submarine. In practice, the pod did a credible job at muting conversations from adjacent pods, the printer that seems to be always running, and the fervent debate about whether pineapple belongs on pizza. The interior surfaces are designed to reflect sound in a controlled manner, preventing it from bouncing around like a popcorn kernel in a microwave. There is a caveat: the acoustics depend heavily on the seating arrangement inside. A pod that is too crowded may end up with the classic acoustic issue of echoes and polite disputes over the best place to position your microphone. Our recommendation is to use the included acoustic panels strategically and to avoid cramming more than the intended number of people in the pod.
