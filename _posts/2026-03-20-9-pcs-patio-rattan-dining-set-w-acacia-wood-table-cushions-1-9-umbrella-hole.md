## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping It Fresh Without Becoming a Full-Time Cleaner
Outdoor furniture faces sun, rain, wind, and the occasional curious raccoon sniffing your cushions. The ethic here is straightforward: invest in small maintenance steps to extend lifespan rather than wait for a major disaster.

### Cleaning and Care Tips
- Cushions: Removable cushion covers should be machine-washed according to the label’s instructions. If washing is not possible, spot-clean with mild detergent and water. Let them air-dry fully before reseating. Quick tip: storing cushions in a breathable bag during heavy rain or winter reduces mildew risk.
- Rattan: Wipe with a damp cloth. Avoid harsh abrasive cleaners that can weather the wicker. For stubborn stains, a mild soap solution works well; rinse and dry thoroughly.
- Table: Wipe the acacia top with a soft, damp cloth. If you have a protective sealer on the wood, reapply as recommended by the manufacturer. Avoid placing hot dishes directly on the surface to prevent heat damage.
- Umbrella: Ensure the umbrella fabric is dry before closing to prevent mildew. Check for any fading or wear in the pole and mechanism; lubricate moving parts as needed to keep the umbrella opening and closing smoothly.

### Weather Resistance: Will It Survive Your Climate?
Synthetic wicker (the “rattan” in most outdoor sets) is designed to resist moisture and UV exposure better than natural wicker. However, no outdoor furniture is 100% maintenance-free. If you’re in a coastal area with salty air, you’ll want to rinse the set occasionally and consider a furniture cover during extreme weather. For heavy snow regions, winter storage or heavy-duty covers protect the wood top and cushions from moisture and freeze-thaw cycles that can cause cracking.
