The suspense comes from the fact that your hand renews and you steal from your own draw pile while trying not to block yourself with a misdraw. It is competitive, fast, and surprisingly portable when you are not wrestling with the furniture to find a table that actually fits the three kids, two cousins, and your grandmother s questionable appetite for the popcorn bowl.

## Condition and testing notes for untested units

Untested devices carry both risk and romance. Risk because you could drop a small device into the category of doorstop rather than a functioning handheld. Romance because if it works, you get a direct tactile link to the era when handheld boredom required a little more processing power than your average calculator.

### Quick start tests
If you are brave enough to purchase an untested unit, here are some pragmatic steps you can perform when you receive it with clean hands and a bright mood:

1. Inspect the exterior for cracks, battery compartments, and signs of corrosion. The battery contacts should be clean and slightly springy. If you see white crusty deposits, that likely means alkaline leakage. Use a citrus cleaner to gently remove oxidation and then rub with a conductive grease or contact cleaner.
2. Open the battery compartment and verify the battery type labeled on the device. If there is a battery installed that looks questionable, do not power the device with a heavy extended buffer of power; rather swap with fresh fresh AAs or the recommended type. If you cannot tell the battery type, do not power it up until you know.
3. Insert fresh batteries and power on. If there is no response, you re-check the battery contacts, try a gentle wiggle of the battery, and then test with a known good battery. If still nothing, the device might require internal replacement parts, which may or may not be easily accessible for a device this old.
4. Observe the screen. If you get a dim glow or partial segments that flicker unpredictably, the display might be failing due to aging LCD or driver issues. Some units can be revived with careful cleaning of the glass and refresh of the contact surfaces, but this is a delicate operation and may require professional help.
5. Listen for audio. The speaker should produce a little blip. If there is nothing, the speaker or the audio amp may have degraded with time. A micro soldering patch could be a possibility for the brave, but it is not a beginner fixed for a retro device.

### Troubleshooting scenarios you might encounter
- If the unit powers on but the screen is blank: check the contrast knob or the module that handles the LCD bias, if accessible. Sometimes a loose connector or a cracked flex cable is the culprit in a long-living handheld.
- If you can see the digits but they ghost or smear: this is likely LCD degradation. You can sometimes revive by cleaning the glass and reflowing the contact points, but this is a delicate operation and may require professional help.
- If the unit powers on but the controls don t respond: check the button contacts; long-term use tends to create carbon build-ups that attract dust and hamper contact reliability. A careful cleaning and cleaning of the button switch surfaces can restore a lot of responsiveness in some units.

On safety: if you find any signs of battery leakage, do not attempt to power the device again. Leaking alkaline substances can be corrosive and hazardous. Wear gloves, ventilate the area, and consider proper disposal of compromised batteries before you salvage the device for parts.
