### Assembly tips and tricks
- Lay out all pieces first and check against the parts list before you commit to any bolt tightening. This saves you from the classic mix of missing washers and a stray hex key you swear you packed but truly did not. 
- Work together instead of solo; two people can align the corner modules far more easily than one person fighting gravity with a screwdriver.

## Durability and weather resistance: can it survive your climate?
### Sun, rain, and seasonal drama
Rattan sets built with HDPE weave and aluminum frames are designed to resist fading, moisture, and humidity in most temperate climates. They are not invincible, however. In areas with heavy winter snow, you might want to store or cover the set during the coldest months to prevent prolonged moisture exposure and potential corrosion at the base joints. If you live in a humid or coastal environment, rinse down occasionally to wash away salt spray and sand that can accumulate on the weave.

### Maintenance routine that won’t ruin your weekend
- Wipe down the frame with a mild soap solution and rinse with clean water; avoid pressure washers that can damage the weave. 
- Vacuum or brush cushions to remove loose dirt and pet hair. 
- Lift cushions periodically to check the base for dampness and air them out in a sunny spot to prevent mildew.
- Use a protective furniture cover when not in use for extended periods to extend the life of the weave and cushions.

## Care and maintenance checklist: keeping it fresh year-round
- Clean cushions every season and machine-wash covers as recommended by the manufacturer.
- Dust the rattan weave with a soft brush to prevent dirt buildup in crevices.
- Inspect joints and bolts after heavy use or extreme weather; tighten where needed to avoid wobble.
- Use a protective cover when not in use for extended periods.

During the rainy season, it helps to rotate cushions and ensure the furniture is not sitting in pooled water. If you live in a climate with hail or strong winds, consider disassembly and storage during severe weather events to preserve the longevity of the pieces.
