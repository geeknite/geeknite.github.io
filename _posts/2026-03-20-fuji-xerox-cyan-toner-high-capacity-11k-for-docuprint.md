## Troubleshooting Common Issues

Even the most reliable toner can run into hiccups. Here are common symptoms and quick fixes:

- Faint cyan or color drift: Run a calibration, verify the color settings, and reprint a color target to compare against your baseline.
- Streaks or blotches: This usually points to calibration drift or uneven toner distribution. Run cleaning cycles and reprint a test page.
- Ghosting or double-imaging in dense color areas: Might indicate high print intensity or a need to adjust toner density in the printer’s color management profile.
- Missing cyan in prints: Re-seat the cartridge, confirm it’s the correct 11K cyan model, and run a calibration. If issues persist, test with a new cartridge to rule out a defective unit.

If issues persist after calibration, consult your printer’s manual and check for firmware updates. Some older DocuPrint models respond better to recent firmware improvements that improve color management routines.

## Maintenance, Storage, and Environmental Notes

Toner cartridges are not a one-and-done solution; they’re part of a broader lifecycle. Store cartridges in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Avoid exposing them to extreme temperatures, which can impact toner flow and overall print quality. During installation, handle cartridges by the edges to minimize oil transfer from your skin onto the cartridge surface.

Many offices participate in recycling programs for toner cartridges. If sustainability is part of your corporate policy, check your vendor’s recycling options and local regulations. Reducing waste goes hand-in-hand with smarter print usage, such as duplex printing for color documents and using draft/educational modes for internal notes.
