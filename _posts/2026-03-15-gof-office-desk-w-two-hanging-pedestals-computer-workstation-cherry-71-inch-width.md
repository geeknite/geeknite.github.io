## Assembly and Setup: The How-To Comedy
Like any desk this size, assembly plays a big role in your initial love-hate relationship with the product. GOF tends to ship with reasonably clear instructions, but you should expect a bit of hardware juggling and at least one moment where you ask yourself if X is supposed to be Y or if you just found a clever way to create a new type of disassembly practice.

### First Things First
Clear a room or at least a large dining area. This is not a job for a coffee shop corner. This desk requires space to stand, lay out panels, and pretend you know the difference between an Allen key and a tiny instrument of doom. It is best done with at least two people: one to hold panels in place and one to pretend to be an experienced carpenter who refuses to look at the manual while still checking the steps off the list via a smartphone camera for later reference.

### The Process: A Rough Guide
- Unpack and sort hardware by size. Label, if you are into that kind of thing. 
- Attach pedestal frames to the desk surface as per the manual. Expect a few clinks of metal and a moment of wearing a confused expression as the pieces decide where they want to go.
- Mount the pedestal drawers and secure the cabinet faces. Aligning the drawers with the rails can be a little fiddly; take your time and be patient with yourself. 
- Attach the desk surface to the frame. The weight distribution matters here; you want to avoid a wobble as you slide from the keyboard to the monitor height like a productivity ninja. 
- Install cable management components and test with your gear. 

If you follow the steps with a calm mindset and a decent set of tools, you should be able to complete assembly in a few hours. It is not a one-sitting IKEA sprint; it is a marathon of nerdy glory that ends with you feeling accomplished rather than emotionally compromised.

### Tips to Speed Things Up
- Have a friend present who can hold pieces in place while you tighten screws. Two sets of hands are worth their weight in hex wrenches.
- Use a power screwdriver with a low torque setting to avoid overtightening. You want reliability, not a bent screw head that becomes a daily reminder of your questionable DIY decisions.
- Don’t rush the alignment. The last thing you want is a desk that squeaks with every keyboard stroke. A small amount of patience yields a quiet, smooth surface that glides through your day.
