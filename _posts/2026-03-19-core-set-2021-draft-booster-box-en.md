## Drafting with Core Set 2021

The drafting experience in M21 is designed to be friendly to new players while still giving seasoned drafters enough knobs to tweak. Here are a few practical takeaways:
- Early game matters, but late game can win the clutch. M21’s design encourages a balanced curve—early defense and board presence can set the stage for bigger threats in the mid game. If you find yourself stuck in a stalemate, look for spells that smooth your turns or diversify your threats across colors.
- Removal can be more valuable than raw card power. A single well-timed removal spell can swing a game by itself, especially in a format where tricks and combat tricks are common. Don’t undervalue a clean removal package just because it doesn’t end the game on turn three.
- Keep an eye on mana efficiency. The best drafts often revolve around a tight mana curve; you’ll want to avoid spells that require you to divest your tempo or color flexibility solely for a high-power card that doesn’t fit your deck. If you can play on-curve threats and can answer theirs, you’re on the right track.
- Avoid the sunk cost trap. It’s easy to fall for the “I already drafted this card, so I must include it” mentality. Sometimes the best pick is a card that fills your deck’s gaps rather than the one you’re emotionally attached to. It’s okay to pivot if your deck isn’t coming together.

## Practical deck-building tips for M21 drafts

- Start with a plan, but be ready to pivot. If you find yourself in a color pair with strong rares, lean into that synergy. Don’t force a third color if the deck’s mana base becomes a swamp of problems.
- Prioritize early plays. In a typical 2-3 color draft, you’ll want to secure a few early, efficient threats and solid removal that can stabilise the early game. Your curve should rise gradually, with a few high-impact plays in the midgame.
- Don’t sleep on removal. If you can remove threats without losing your own tempo, you’ll often win games where both players grind it out. A well-timed removal spell can swing the board in your favor and set up a clean finish.
- Watch your loom of color. The mana base matters more than you think in the mid to late game. A well-balanced deck that can cast its spells on curve will perform better than a flashy but inconsistent one.
