## How to play Nine Lives in a deck
### Commander and Lifegain synergies
Nine Lives is a natural addition to lifegain and pillow fort builds in Commander. It acts as a durable cornerstone that discourages aggressive pressure from opponents while you ramp up your life total and draw into more answers. If you enjoy the idea of a game where you accumulate life and slowly, stubbornly, outlast your opponents, Nine Lives can slot into many white-based lifegain shells. It pairs well with card draw engines, life-linked sweepers, and protective auras that keep your life total propped while you keep your board stable.

### Modern and other formats
In non-rotating formats, Nine Lives sees less play. There are decks that use it for a long grind, but the majority of competitive lists run faster combos or more proactive removal. That said, for casual Modern fun, this card can still exist as a jolting piece that makes your friends suspicious of your life total. In limited play, the foil Nine Lives can become a talking point as you glare at your opponent while you sneak out of a potentially lethal swing through careful damage prevention.

## Quality and collection tips
- Inspect edges and corners on LP foil copies. Foil wear is common and not always a deal-breaker, but you want to avoid significant whitening or creases that would hamper your play experience.
- Store foils in quality sleeves; foils are more prone to surface issues if they slide loosely in a binder.
- Consider a card saver or toploaders if you intend to display the foil; this will maintain the foil's shine and minimize contact with other cards if you transport it to meetups or cons.
