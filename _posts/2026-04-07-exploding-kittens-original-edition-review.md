## What’s in the box
In the Original Edition, you get a deck of 56 cards, a handful of adorable but lethal kittens, and a few defensive options to keep yourself from turning into a puddle of shame if you draw the wrong card at the wrong time. The kid-friendly version of the rules keeps the stakes light but the electricity high enough to coax giggles from the most stoic grown-ups.

### The lineup of cards includes:
- Exploding Kitten cards: the big booms that end your post-mortem street cred if drawn without a Defuse.
- Defuse cards: the universal save-me card, often the reason you continue to pretend you’re not terrified of the next draw.
- See the Future: a fun peek-ahead mechanic that lets you peek at the top three cards and plan your catastrophe with style.
- Attack: passes the deck’s misfortune to the next player, ideally in a way that makes them wonder if the universe is trolling them.
- Skip: your out of gravity card that pretends you didn’t see the doom coming.
- Shuffle: reorders the deck so that doom momentarily forgets where it was planning to strike.
- Cat cards: pairs and triples that you can trade in for crazy combinations, trading in cat puns and memes for bigger chaos.

The art is bright, playful, and a little deranged—in the best possible way. It communicates the mood instantly: this is a game about chaos, but it’s all in good fun, like a kitten wearing a party hat and accidentally knocking over a vase of your dignity.

## How to play (the friendly-fire version)
Exploding Kittens is designed to be accessible, fast, and loaded with personality. Here’s a deeper dive into how a typical session unfolds, especially when you’ve got a table full of kids and a few adults who still remember hitting the high score in arcade games.

### Setup
1) Shuffle the deck. 2) Deal 7 cards to each player (for 2 players, deal 7; for more players, adjust to keep pace). 3) Place the Exploding Kitten cards back into the deck so that there are enough random doom floats to keep you honest. 4) Give each player a Defuse card or two depending on your mercy level. 5) Decide who starts with a dramatic flourish and a dramatic sigh.

### Objective
The objective is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten without a Defuse. The first person who ends up with the magic doom is out of the round, and play continues until one victor remains. It’s like musical chairs, but with more fangs and fewer chairs. For younger players, the high-stakes vibe is offset by the goofy art and the Defuse mechanic, which provides a safety net that keeps things from tipping into real disappointment.

### Core mechanics
- Draw one card per turn: the heart of the chaos. Each draw could be harmless or catastrophic, so you keep your fingers crossed and your wits about you.
- Use Defuse cards when you draw an Exploding Kitten to dodge disaster. If you don’t have one, you’re on a countdown toward a sad exit.
- Play other cards to manipulate the flow: See the Future gives you a glimpse, Attack shifts the doom, and Skip/Shuffle can turn the tide in your favor or at least make your friends miserable in style.
- Cat cards form sets that are traded in for powerful effects, if you’re lucky enough to complete the combo. It’s a bit like a tiny, chaotic set collection that rewards confidence more than actual skill.

### Turn sequence in practice
A typical turn looks like this: you draw a card, hope you didn’t anger the feline gods, and then decide if you want to play a Defuse or any other card you’ve been hoarding like a dragon with a credit card. Players can hoard Defuses, See the Future, and the occasional acerbic commentary about the inevitability of doom.

### Endgame pacing
The game is designed to move quickly. If you’re lucky, it will be a 10-minute warmup; if you’re unlucky and blessed with a chain of Exploding Kitten draws, you could be staring at a tense, ridiculous 20-minute spectacle. Either way, you will laugh at least five times per round—if not at the literal explosions, then at the timing and the faces of players who miscalculate their strategy and stare into the abyss of a pile of cat-themed doom cards.

## Strategy tips for surviving the blast zone
Some tips are universal; others are kid-tested and parent-approved. Here are practical, party-friendly ideas to raise your odds without turning the table into a tense stand-off.
- Defuse early, don’t hoard them forever. You’ll need at least one Defuse late in the game, and hoarding them might save you from doom… for a moment. If you’re playing with kids, consider showing a few Defuses early so they understand there’s a safety net, which reduces anxiety and increases giggles.
- Don’t overcommit to See the Future. It’s powerful, but you’re not running a strategy simulator; it’s a party game. Use it to plan a joke, not a master plan. A well-timed glimpse can set up a ridiculous reveal that has everyone roaring, especially when someone misreads the top card and reacts like a cartoon character.
- Pay attention to other players’ tells. People tend to react in predictable ways when doom is near: a pause here, a gulp there, a dramatic sigh, or a whisper of cat proverb about fate. Use that insight to adjust your timing and pick your moment to strike with a cat-tastic pun that seals the round with laughter.
- Cat cards offer vibes more than raw power. Use them to craft combos that amuse, not just to snag a win. The real win is the laughter you collect along the way, and the shared memory of triumph when someone pulls off a cat combo that leaves everyone howling.
- For the younger players, emphasize the defusal moments and the silly art. They love the idea that they can avert doom with a goofy card and a grin. The more you highlight the silly, the less the game feels punitive and more like a carnival of chaos with you as ringmaster.
