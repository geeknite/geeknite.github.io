## Design and Materials

### PE Rattan and Metal Frames

The core of this set lies in its Polyethylene (PE) rattan weave applied over a corrosion-resistant metal frame, typically aluminum or steel. The PE rattan is designed to mimic the look and texture of natural rattan while being far more forgiving to the outdoors. We tested a few weave patterns—some are tight enough to make you think 'this is a modern art sculpture,' others a little looser and more forgiving of weight shifts during a dramatic sunset. The frame is usually powder-coated to resist rust and chalky whiteness that comes with constant exposure to sunlight. The result is a silhouette that reads as premium without needing a second mortgage.

### Cushions and Comfort

Cushions on outdoor sets are the hidden battleground of comfort. They need to be fluffy enough to give you an “ahh” when you lounge, yet durable enough to resist pancake-flat fatigue after a few months. The 7-piece set typically uses removable covers with zips (release the inner comfort of a memory-foam mix? Maybe not). The foam cores are commonly wrapped with a weather-resistant inner liner that helps prevent mold and mildew—a vital feature for folks living in humid climates or for those who occasionally forget their towels after a pool day. The whiteness of the cushions is a bold aesthetic choice; it makes a crisp statement but demands a bit more maintenance than darker tones.

Ethically speaking, PE rattan is the kind of synthetic that ages like fine resin. It doesn’t absorb water and dries quickly, which reduces the risk of mold and odors. It’s not entirely invincible: UV exposure eventually fades color; you’ll want to rotate cushions and consider a protective cover to maintain that pristine whiteness.

## Maintenance and Care Tips

- Cover when not in use: A breathable cover keeps UV rays away from your cushions and reduces dust buildup. It’s a small investment in the long-term look of the set.
- Clean spills promptly: Soap and water with a soft cloth is enough for most stains. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade fabric or fade color.
- Rotate cushions: Regularly switching cushion positions helps wear patterns to appear even, preventing a lopsided look after heavy use.
- Clean the frame: A damp cloth is usually sufficient for the frame. For stubborn spots, use a gentle cleaner that won’t corrode the metal fins.
- Check hardware: Periodically inspect screws and bolts. Loose hardware reduces stability and can be a safety risk during windy afternoons.
