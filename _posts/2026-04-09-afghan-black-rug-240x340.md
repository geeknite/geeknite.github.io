## How the Afghan black rug performs in real life

### Foot traffic and durability
The real test of any rug is how it handles daily life. The Afghan 240x340 is built to endure foot traffic, stray snacks, and the occasional pet mischief. Wool fibers naturally resist stains to some extent and have a degree of resilience that helps them bounce back after pressure. With regular care, you can expect many years of service. Of course, you will notice some wear in high traffic zones over time, especially in areas where the pile is thinner or the knots are under more stress. This is not a defect, it is a map of your life told in wool.

### Maintenance routine
Care is not fancy, but it is essential. A simple weekly vacuum on a low setting, with the beater bar turned off, will remove surface dust and keep the fibers from mashing into themselves. Rotate the rug every six to twelve months to ensure even wear and to avoid a sunlit patch becoming a feature in your living room. If you spill something, blot do not rub. Quick action minimizes staining and preserves the pile. For stronger stains, a professional cleaning is recommended every two to five years depending on usage. If you cannot schedule professional cleaning, a solution of mild detergent and water may do in a pinch, but test in a corner first to avoid color bleed.

Beyond routine cleaning, consider sun exposure. Prolonged direct sunlight can fade the color over time. If your space has a strong sun path, place the rug away from the strongest rays or use window coverings to keep the color from washing out. If you are worried about color loss, a fabric protector applied by a professional might be a good idea, but this is a topic to discuss with your rug cleaner rather than applying DIY coatings.

### Cleaning spills and stains
Spills happen to the best of us. For liquids, the key is to blot, not rub. Use a clean white cloth or paper towel to absorb the liquid, pressing gently rather than rubbing across the fibers. If a stain sticks around, use cold water and a mild detergent with a color safe stain remover. Avoid harsh chemicals that may alter the color or weaken the wool. For oils or grease, a little cornstarch or baking soda can help absorb before you blot with a mild detergent solution.

It is worth noting that for tough stains a professional cleaner with experience in wool and handmade rugs is the best choice. They understand the weave and know how to treat the fibers without distorting the knot or color. If you are in a shared space such as a home with multiple occupants, mark the rug care schedule on your calendar so everyone knows the rotation plan.

### Colorfastness, sun, and seasonal care
Direct sun can fade the color of wool over years. If your room is bathed in sunlight for most of the day, consider rotating the rug seasonally or investing in UV-blocking window treatments. A professional rug cleaner can also advise on colorfastness boosters for wool; this is a good idea if you live in a high-heat or high-sun climate.

### Underlay and padding
A good rug pad is not a luxury; it is a life support system for your Afghan rug. A dense felt pad adds cushioning, reduces wear, and helps keep the rug in place on hard floors. A rubberized non-slip pad is a great option if you have a slippery surface such as polished wood. For large sizes like 240x340, a multi-zone pad can help distribute weight evenly and prevent creasing. If you want to preserve pile height, look for a pad that does not compress too aggressively in high-traffic areas.

### Pet friendliness and everyday life
Pets bring chaos and charm in equal measure. The wool pile is relatively resistant to pet hair and can shed less than low-pile synthetics, but claws can still snag if the knots are finer. Regular short brushing or gentle vacuuming in pet zones can help maintain the look. If your cat loves to dig or scratch, consider placing a cat tree or a rug corner protector to deflect attention away from the main field.
