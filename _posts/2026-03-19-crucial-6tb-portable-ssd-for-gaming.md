- Smoother open‑world experience: If you keep a chunk of your frequently played titles on this drive, you can transition between games with minimal waiting. It may not replace the need for a fast internal SSD for the most memory‑demanding titles, but it acts as a superb supplementary asset pool.
- Content creation and capture: For streamers or YouTubers who need to archive raw footage locally, the 6TB capacity gives you plenty of breathing room. You can cap a project, stash the raw videos, and keep your editing drive lean, all while your library remains accessible and organized.

### A Note on Cable and Host Limits
The speeds you see depend heavily on the bottlenecks introduced by cables and hosts. A low‑quality USB-C cable or an older USB port can cap throughput dramatically. If you want the best possible performance, use a high‑quality USB-C cable that supports the higher bandwidth classes and plug into a USB 3.2 Gen 2x2 port or the latest USB-C controller on your motherboard or laptop. If you are unsure about your hardware, a quick test copy of a large file from your internal drive to the Crucial drive can reveal whether you are getting anywhere near the promised speeds or if you are stuck in the slow lane.
