## Painting and basing tips
This is where the real fun begins. If you’ve ever argued with a brush about whether to go full glory or subtle shading, you’ll appreciate how these five minis respond to paint. Here are a few practical approaches to bring them to life:

- Prep work: clean, rinse, and dry. Remove flash with a sharp hobby knife. Prime with a neutral gray or black to help your colors pop. A primer helps you avoid chipping when you base or reposition the figures while you’re working on them.
- Skin tones: start with a mid-tone base, then build up shadows with a slightly darker tone. A touch of pink or red on the cheeks, ears, and nose adds life without looking too theatrical. If you want a more dramatic undead vibe, lean into cooler tones with a blue-gray shadow.
- Clothing: think of the cloth as your canvas. Use a base color, then highlight with a lighter version of that color along the folds to create depth. Leather components look great with browns, while metal fittings benefit from a quick wash of black or brown, followed by a bright highlight.
- Weapons: simulate wear with a rust glaze or a quick wash that emphasizes pitted edges. For steel, a gradient from dark to light silver can give that reflective look without requiring fancy metallic paints.
- Bases: basing is where your five heroes show their personality. A cobblestone-style base says “city survivor,” while a dirt path with tufts communicates “native woods survivor.” Consider adding debris, small bones, or zombie bits around the edges to anchor their world. You can also use texture paints to add dust and earth, giving the figures a more grounded feel in their apocalyptic kitchen sink world.

Be patient with basing and painting. Five minis mean you’ll have enough variety to practice different techniques, but you also don’t want to spend weeks on every single one. A practical plan is to tackle one figure per night for a week and a half, mixing in wash steps and dry brushing to build a cohesive group look by the end of the project.

## Care, maintenance, and longevity
Your figures’ longevity depends on how you handle them. Here are a few practical tips to keep them looking good for years:
- Gentle handling: avoid dropping the minis during painting sessions. Use a cork mat or soft cloth to protect them. If you drop a piece, don’t panic; clean the break points, re-glue with plastic cement, and repaint the touched areas to preserve the continuity.
- Storage: keep them in a small, labeled container or a hobby tray. Loose minis have a habit of entertaining themselves by attempting dramatic escapes; proper storage reduces the probability of lost limbs and broken sword tips.
- Cleaning: after priming, wash with mild soap and water to remove dust and residue. Use a soft brush to scrub before painting; this will help you get better paint adhesion and a cleaner final product.
