## Authenticity: How to Tell a Carddas Card from a Counterfeit Mirage
This is where the hobbyist brain activates its lie-detecting muscles. Here are practical authenticity checks you can perform without needing a lab:
- Compare back designs: older Carddas backs have consistent font weights and layout. Counterfeits often misalign text blocks or use slightly different typefaces.
- Inspect the card stock: vintage or classic Carddas often use a specific texture. If your No.225 feels too glossy or too matte for the era, it could be a red flag.
- Foil and color accuracy: Very Rare variants may have subtle foil accents. A counterfeit might attempt foil, but it tends to look off in hue, brightness, or foil “fluff” around the edges.
- Print alignment: look at the border spacing and the portrait’s boundary. Inaccurate print alignment is a common giveaway in fakes.
- Wrapper context: If you bought the card in a sealed pack or from a reputable dealer, you’re already a step ahead. If you found the card in a “mystery box” with a non-descript source, you should proceed with extra caution.

## How to Care for and Display Your No.225 Card
Storage matters when you want to preserve gloss and color fidelity for decades. Here are user-friendly tips for keeping your Very Rare Son Goten safe and pique-worthy:
- Use acid-free sleeves and a proper binder with centralized pages. Avoid thin plastic that can cause edge wear.
- Keep out of direct sunlight to prevent fading of the art and back printing.
- Use a low-humidity environment; cards in too-damp environments may develop corner warping or slight discoloration on the edges.
- If you’re displaying, use a UV-filtered display case. It keeps dust at bay and your card’s enamel-y shine intact.
- Consider a professional grading service if you intend to monetize or display in a formal way, but beware of grading costs and turnaround times.
