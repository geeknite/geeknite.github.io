## Maintenance and storage tips for longevity
Since you’ve gone through the trouble of acquiring a sealed, new-in-package trio, you owe it to your future self to preserve that mint condition as long as possible. Here are practical tips:

- Store away from light: Prolonged exposure to sunlight can degrade the color on the faces and the back design. A dedicated shelf or a small cabinet is ideal.
- Humidity control matters: Card stock loves stable humidity. If you’re in a climate that fluctuates wildly, consider a dehumidifier in the room where you game.
- Gentle handling: If you’re one of those players who loves to riffle the deck like a wizard’s wand, watch for edge wear. The 808 stock is sturdy, but it’s not invincible; give the corners a little rest between big hands.

A sealed set is the best-case scenario for collectors, but if you ever decide to crack one open for a nostalgia night with friends, you’ll still have two intact decks to preserve the set’s overall value.
