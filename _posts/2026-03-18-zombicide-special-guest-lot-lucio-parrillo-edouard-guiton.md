### The sculpts: Parrillo meets Guiton
Parrillo's presence in miniature design typically brings cinematic lighting, dramatic shadows, and a sense of weight that makes every swing look earned. Guiton's influence introduces crisp edges and mid-brow comic-energy that reads well on the table while still looking lethal. The blend yields figures that photograph nicely and hold up under paint. For painters, the undercuts are ample enough to help shading without requiring a full-blown sculpting masterclass. For tabletop gamers, the silhouette language is strong: a quick glance will identify friend from foe, hero from horde, which is crucial when the board is a blur of movement and gore.

### Box art and basing options

### Painting and assembly tips
- Prime in a neutral gray to help with midtone shading.
- Use a three-color base (mid-tone, shadows, highlights) to avoid muddy results.
- For Parrillo figures, focus on dramatic light sources: a single strong highlight with deep shadows will read as cinematic even from across the gaming table.
- For Guiton influenced minis, crisp edge highlights on armor and weapons will maximize readability on a busy board.
- Dry brushing on the bases can bring out rubble textures without overpowering the sculpt.
- If you plan to display these, consider a light gloss on metallics to simulate wet steel or fresh gore for extra drama.
