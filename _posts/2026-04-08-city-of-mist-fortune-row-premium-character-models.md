## Painting, assembly, and handling tips
Fortune Row is painter-friendly, though it’s not a starter kit. Here are some practical tips to help you get from sprue to showroom:

- Pre-clean thoroughly: acetone-based cleaners or a gentle soap bath will remove mold-release without harming fine details.
- Use a gravity-based paint regime: base coat, shade, highlight. The models reward layering, so don’t rush to multiple colors on every minute detail.
- Dry brushes are your friend for texture: capes and fabrics will pop with light dry brushing, while metals benefit from a brighter edge highlight.
- Priming matters: a gray or white primer gives you a vibrant starting palette; black primer can help with moody, noir vibes.
- Handling with finesse: because the pieces have slender hands, cuffs, and jewelry, you’ll want fine-tipped brushes and metallic paints for delicate touches. Patience pays off here.

If you’re new to premium minis, you’re in for a learning curve, but Fortune Row doesn’t punish the patient painter. They reward careful prep, steady hands, and a willingness to experiment with contrast to bring the characters’ myths to life.

## Getting the most out of Fortune Row: painting and display ideas
If you’re itching to pull a flawless paint job, here are some targeted tips to elevate your Fortune Row figures:

- Skin tones: a base mid-tone with several thin glazes creates a lifelike look. Use a cool blue or violet glaze in the deepest recesses to propel the noir mood without saturating the face.
- Fabrics: leather and wool respond well to a cool shadow base with a mid-tone highlight. Lace and silk can benefit from a satin glaze that mimics reflective light without turning shiny.
- Metals: metallics demand micro-shading. Use a dark wash in the crevices, then a sharp edge highlight on raised areas to mimic reflections on polished surfaces.
- Lighting: consider a stage light approach—aim for a warm light source from one side and a cooler ambient light from the other to create drama and depth.
- Base storytelling: use tarot iconography or neon signage on the bases to reinforce the character’s mythic narrative. It’s a small touch that pays off when the table’s lighting changes mid-scene.

If you’re hosting a painting night, Fortune Row can be the centerpiece of the evening. They’re visually striking enough to justify a dedicated session around them, which is a rare win for hobby nights that often devolve into “paint on the mini, call it a day.”
