## The Bakugo Deck: Cards and New Mechanics
UNO Flip itself is a love letter to chaos: a color deck that flips to a black, more punishing side as soon as someone hits the flip card. The Bakugo edition leans into that chaos by weaving in character themed cards that feel like they are trying to enforce Bakugo level authority during a family game night. Here are the new card types you will encounter when you shuffle this deck:

### Explosions and Eruptions
Bakugo themed cards include explosion icons that mirror his signature move. When an Explosion card is played, it triggers a mini chain reaction in the gameplay. Not a full game breaker, but enough to remind your friends that you are playing with the spirit of an explosive hero and not just a friendly neighbor’s card set. The effect is typically a forced draw or a skip, depending on the card variant. Thematically, it adds a sense of urgency to every turn, as if Bakugo himself is shouting at the cards to hurry up. 

### The Hot Head Rule
A special Bakugo card introduces a temporary misfortune for the table: a hot head rule that ensures the player who plays it must narrate their next turn in a dramatic anime voice. This is not a mechanical change to the outcome of the game, but it increases the likelihood of popcorn noises and dramatic sighs, both of which are priceless when playing with family. 

### Wild Amongst the Burnout
The Bakugo edition adds a Wild Change card, a nod to Bakugo’s unpredictable mood swings. This card allows the player to change the color, but also to shudder at the thought of Bakugo losing his grip on his temper for more than one breath. It is not game breaking; it is mood lighting for the room. 

### New Cards and What They Do
Beyond the flavor, there are some card type add-ons that the Bakugo deck uses to emphasize the anime vibe without overcomplicating the core UNO rules:
- Wild Explosion: forces all players to draw 2 cards unless they play a matching colored card. This one turns the table into a small, chaotic mini-game that lasts a few seconds longer than the average round. 
- Bakugo Draw 4: a homage to the classic draw four with Bakugo’s face on the card, for extra intimidation. If you draw this card, you get to add a dramatic monologue to your next turn or risk losing control of your inner hero. 
- Eject Button: a one-turn skip that only applies if you can scream the correct Bakugo line. The wrong line means you skip your own turn, which is a cruel but inevitable twist in a family game. 

These additions are mostly cosmetic in terms of mechanical depth; they exist to amplify the feel of Bakugo rather than to rewrite UNO rules. If you are hoping for a pro-level tournament edition that changes how every card interacts, you will be disappointed. If you are hoping for a party game that creates stories and laughs, this edition hits the mark with a bang, a pop, and a little smoke.
