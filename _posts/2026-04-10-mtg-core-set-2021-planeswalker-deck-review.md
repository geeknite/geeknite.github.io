Welcome, fellow planeswalkers. If you have found yourself here, it is likely you are either a new player trying to figure out why your mana looks like a misbehaving dam, or a veteran who enjoys the smell of fresh cardboard and caffeinated optimism. Today we are diving into the Core Set 2021 Planeswalker Decks, a two deck duo designed to teach you the ropes while giving you a tiny, well-meaning shove into the world of build-your-own-arena. Planeswalker Decks are preconstructed, 60-card decks built around a planeswalker, and they come with a little strategy card that tries to explain the deck's vibe without requiring you to read Rule Zero while juggling a snack bag and a game die.

## Overview
Planeswalker Decks are the gateway to MTG for many players. They typically feature two 60-card decks, each anchored by a planeswalker. They are designed to be approachable, with a cohesive color pair and a theme that allows new players to see how a deck feels in action without hunting for a thousand different singles. The decks emphasize the planewalker’s colors and mechanics — be it heavy spells, creature synergy, or funky permanents — so you can speak the language of magic without sounding like a bot that just learned to conjugate mana.

### What's in the box?
While it varies by year and region, Core Set 2021 Planeswalker Decks usually include two ready-to-play 60-card decks, a quick-start guide, and a small reference card that helps you understand the basic mana curve and turn-by-turn logic. There may also be a life counter or a tiny dungeon of cardboard mischief included for theme, but the essential goods are two decks ready to rumble across the kitchen table. The idea is to lower the barrier of entry: you don’t have to worry about negotiating with a million different card choices; you get a focused, themed experience with a single plan that you can grok in one sitting.

## Deck A vs Deck B
In most years, the Core Set Planeswalker Decks pair two colors that complement each other in a way that feels almost like a science experiment from a wizarding lab. The two decks in Core Set 2021 follow this pattern: each deck is color-based and built to support the planeswalker that leads it. The idea is not only to showcase the planeswalker but to deliver a deck that can stand on its own while you learn the game’s basics.

### Deck One: the color pair and theme
This deck typically leans into a specific color identity and the planeswalker at its helm. Expect a balance of creatures to pressure your opponent and spells that accelerate your plan, while still keeping enough removal so you don’t get run over by a rampy green deck or a sudden red assault. The plan is clear: set up the board, drop the walkers, and then let the dude with the fancy hat do most of the heavy lifting.

### Deck Two: the contrasting color identity
The second deck offers a different flavor, often stepping into a contrasting or complementary pair. It’s designed to show how two magics can collaborate on a battlefield, albeit with different priorities. The synergy is not just in the cards themselves but in the teaching moment: you watch how the planeswalker’s abilities align with the deck’s creatures and removal, then imagine how to brew a version that matches your mood and your kitchen table.

## Gameplay and Strategy
So how does a Planeswalker Deck actually play out? The answer is simple and complicated at the same time. You want to ride your planeswalker’s loyalty ability to reach a winning board state, but you need to protect the walker from the onslaught of your opponent’s removal. The decks are designed to be intuitive: a solid mana base, a clear plan, and enough cheap threats to keep pressure on while you charge up loyalty counters.

### How to play with these decks
When you start a game with one of the Core Set 2021 Planeswalker Decks, the first few turns focus on building out your mana base and presenting early threats. If you’re lucky, you’ll drop the planeswalker on turn four or five and immediately start stacking up loyalty counters for the big swing. The synergy comes from how the planeswalker’s abilities support the deck’s style: a walker that increases card advantage pairs nicely with a deck full of efficient, small creatures; a walker that creates banned-for-one-turn obstacles can help you stabilize while the other cards increasingly pressure your opponent.

### Strengths and weaknesses
The main strength of these decks is their accessibility. They give you a coherent theme and a clear path to victory, which is ideal for players new to the game or returning after a long hiatus. Weaknesses? They are not the most optimized builds you’ll see in a professional tournament setting. There can be a few suboptimal card choices or awkward mana distribution that make late-game blowouts feel like a surprise party you didn’t want. But that is part of the learning curve: you’re supposed to stumble a bit, then figure out which cards you actually like to play and which ones you would replace with something shinier.

## Value and Collectability
Value is a tricky beast with Planeswalker Decks. On one hand, you get two complete, playable 60-card decks with a cohesive theme; on the other hand, you are sometimes paying for the convenience of a ready-made strategy rather than for a stack of top-tier rares. If you’re a new player, the value is often fair — you get a learning tool, a built-in budget starter, and a few cards that could slide into your more serious decks later. If you’re a collector hunting the rare chase, you may not find a lot of standout chase cards here, but you’ll still get a nice snapshot of a color pair’s identity in core set form.

## Design and Craftsmanship
From a design perspective, Core Set 2021 Planeswalker Decks aim to be coherent, approachable, and fun. The layout of each deck emphasizes a theme, with a cascade of synergy between the planeswalker’s abilities and the deck’s creature and spell suite. The card choices typically favor efficiency and tempo, offering creatures with early impact and removal that can curb an aggressive start. The planeswalkers provide a central, memorable hook that keeps the player's attention long enough to teach the rest of the game’s rules.

But there’s always room for critique. Some players feel that the decks sometimes skew toward a single strategy, which can be less interesting for players who prefer a bit of spice in their decks. Others might note that certain cards in the deck are only there to push the planeswalker’s loyalty up fast, rather than to contribute to the deck’s actual win condition. These critiques aren’t showstoppers; they are reminders that prebuilt decks are a starting point, not a final destination.

## Learning and Accessibility
For brand-new players, these decks excel. They come with a simple, predictable mana curve, straightforward creature threats, and a planeswalker as the star. The learning curve is pleasantly gentle compared with the abyss that is reading a thousand card lists and trying to figure out which synergy exists between which card. If you’ve ever started a board game and quickly realized you might have to consult a library of rulebooks to play, you know the relief that a Planeswalker Deck can provide.

If you’re a veteran who wants to try something new, these decks can still be fun. They provide a ready-made sandbox to test a color pairing you haven’t played much, to practice your mulligan discipline, and to imagine how to upgrade the deck into something that would stand up to proper optimization. And if you are the kind of player who likes to brew, you can use the deck as a springboard: take out the cards you don’t like, add in a few spicy new options, and voila, you’ve got a preposterously personalized version of a starting point.

## Value in Other Formats
One of the advantages of Planeswalker Decks is their portability. You can slip either deck into a small carry bag and bring it to a friend’s house or a local game store for a quick, friendly match. They also provide a quick reference that can be used to teach new players who may not yet be comfortable digging through a 300-card set. For folks who collect, these decks can be a nice cheap entry into a color pair’s identity, especially if you are not yet sure you want to invest in more expensive single cards.

## Final Thoughts and Recommendation
If you are a brand-new player or you have a friend who has never played a real game of MTG, the Core Set 2021 Planeswalker Decks are a solid starting point. They provide a guided entry into the game’s core mechanics, a clear theme, and the thrill of seeing a planeswalker do something cool without requiring a lawyer to explain the rules. They also make a good gift for someone who is curious about MTG but not yet ready for the complexities of a full set.

If you are a long-time player who loves to tinker, you may find the decks a bit too restrained for your tastes. You might prefer to upgrade a few cards and customize the deck to fit your preferred strategy. In that case, use the planeswalker deck as a base, but don’t expect it to replace your favorite bespoke brew you have been lovingly nurturing for months.

That said, the value proposition remains strong: two coherent starters that can teach a friend to enjoy the game, or a quick, casual play session with your own familiar color pair, all wrapped in the comforting aroma of new card stock. The mechanical hooks of the planeswalker underpin a learning curve that is inviting rather than intimidating.
