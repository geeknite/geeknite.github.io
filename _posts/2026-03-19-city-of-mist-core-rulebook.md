## Overview
City of Mist: Core Rulebook is a neon-drenched love letter to myth, noir, and the unshakeable belief that a city can be haunted by legends even when you’re just trying to get a coffee before sunrise. If you’ve ever watched a rain-soaked alley and thought, “This is a magical realist stage for a private eye with a side of demigod,” City of Mist invites you to make that thought a game night reality. The Core Rulebook is not a heavy crunch fest; it’s designed to be approachable, fast to pick up, and quick to put into the hands of players who want to tell stories without getting lost in a labyrinth of tables and charts.

In a genre-savvy move, the book leans into a blending of detective fiction with mythic power. Your character isn’t merely “super strong” or “incredibly wise”; they are a Legend in a mundane shell, a person who carries a myth around like a coffee mug that’s just a tad too big for their hand. The tone is intentionally pulpy and playful, with enough bite to remind you that the city isn’t just a prop—it’s a character with wants, secrets, and a tendency to unleash magical chaos when you’re least prepared.

If you want to understand the core idea in one sentence: City of Mist makes you a walking myth in modern clothes. The Core Rulebook gives you the tools to invent and narrate those myths while keeping your feet planted in noir realism. That balance of mythic scope and street-level grit is where the book shines, and it’s exactly the vibe I expect from a game that wants to feel like a comic-book chase scene brought to life in a real city’s rain-slicked streets.

## The Core Rulebook Layout and Presentation
The book’s organization mirrors its playstyle: you’ll find a lot of flavor before you hit the dice, and then the rules keep you moving without getting bogged down. The layout favors quick reference and narrative prompts over walls of dense text. The art direction embraces neon signs, rain-slick pavements, and a color palette that makes you want to hop into a vehicle with a fog machine attached. The result is a book that invites you to flip through it, not drone across a desk reading a legal brief.

### What the Chapters Are Doing For You
- Part I: Conceptual Foundations. This section is your primer on Legends, Masks, and the “city under the city” idea. It’s where you’re introduced to the core concept that myths exist because people tell stories about them—and those stories can become real.
- Part II: Character Creation. Here you’ll build a Legend by selecting Tags, origin stories, and the mundane life that your myth hides behind. The core mechanic is intentionally approachable: you don’t need to memorize a spell catalog or an encyclopedia of powers. You assemble a bag of Tag-based capabilities and decide how your Legend interacts with the ordinary world.
- Part III: Rules and Resolution. This is where the dice come in, along with the Tag system that is central to City of Mist. The system emphasizes the narrative potential: you’ll use your Tags to generate opportunities, and the GM will use opposing Tags to create complications, all while you weave a story that feels both epic and grounded.
- Part IV: World Building and Campaign Tools. A city is not a blank slate; it’s a living place with neighborhoods, factions, and myths that collide in the night. The book provides prompts to seed your city, hooks for campaigns, and a toolbox for GMs to escalate tension without turning every session into a chase sequence.
- Appendices and Play Aids. Quick-start rules, sample characters, and ready-to-run encounter seeds—perfect for a one-shot or a curated evening that still feels substantial.

The structure is designed to be forgiving for newcomers and a little investigative for veterans. If you’ve played games that lean heavily on tactical combat or grim dark worldbuilding, City of Mist nudges you toward storytelling first and mechanics second. That’s not to say the rules aren’t clever; it’s that the cleverness serves the vibe rather than hiding behind tactical complexity.

## Core Mechanics: How It Feels to Play
The heart of City of Mist is its tag-based resolution system. You don’t roll a dozen different kinds of dice for every action. Instead, you build your Legend by stacking Tags—each tag a facet of who you are, what you’re capable of, or what stands in your way. When you attempt something, the game asks you to lean on relevant Tags. The outcome is narrative-forward: you either succeed with a twist, fail with a complication, or realize you’re using the wrong tag entirely and pivot your plan.

### Tags, Legends, and Everyday Life
Tags are the engine that powers the character in City of Mist. Think of a Tag as a two-edged blade: it can help you accomplish a goal, or it can complicate things in interesting ways. A Legend might be something like “Streetwise Detective,” “Nighttime Alchemist,” or “Mercurial Trickster.” The mundane life section grounds the character—your day job, relationships, and the ordinary world that a myth hides within.

What I particularly appreciate is how Tag usage invites improvisation. You’re not consulting a lengthy spell list or a stack of numerical modifiers; you’re telling a story about how your Legend interacts with the city’s mysteries. The book presents prompts and examples that encourage players to describe actions in cinematic terms, which makes sessions feel dynamic and cinematic without requiring a Hollywood budget.

### Resolution and Narration Style
The resolution process emphasizes narrative cause and effect. A successful use of a Tag doesn’t just grant a feat; it opens up a narrative consequence. Maybe your Streetwise Tag helps you piece together a clue, but it also drags in a rival gang, or perhaps your Mythic Power draws the attention of a city official who has secrets of their own. The mechanics are designed to reward storytelling that feels inevitable, even when it’s unpredictable. This is a game that wants you to narrate: you describe the beats, the GM fills in the gaps, and the scene moves forward with momentum.

The book doesn’t drown you in tables and charts for every action. Instead, it provides a few core mechanics that scale with your scene. You can run tense interrogation scenes, chase sequences, or quiet character-driven moments with equal ease. It’s a lightweight system that asks you to lean on description and shared storytelling rather than crunch numbers for hours.

### Character Creation: A Quick Dive
Creating a Legend is akin to assembling a minimal viable myth. You pick a Mantle (the domain of power), a set of Tags, and a mundane life. The Mantle is your core, the aspect that gives you your hook into the city’s bigger mythology. Tags are your toolkit: they tell you what you can do right now, what you can improvise, and what you should avoid unless you want trouble to find you in the alley behind the bakery.

The Core Rulebook does a good job of guiding players through a fast setup. You’re not assembling a Excel sheet of bonuses; you’re sketching a character whose voice and presence feel specific and memorable. The interplay between the Mantle and the mundane world is where the game shines: your legend is both a mask you wear and a possibility you chase. It’s a duality that sits at the heart of noir fiction, and City of Mist leverages it to produce scenes that feel earned rather than manufactured.

### The GM’s Toolkit
For the GM, the Core Rulebook provides more than just a set of numbers. It offers a way to frame scenes around the city as a living, reactive character. The City is a place with neighborhoods that hide secrets and factions that pull strings. The GM is not merely a referee; they’re the narrator weaving a web into which the players step, heroic or flawed, and then realize the web has a will of its own.

The GM sections encourage modular storytelling: you can inject a legend’s backstory into a scene, twist a clue to reveal a larger conspiracy, or pivot to a personal drama that tests your characters’ beliefs. This is quintessential noir: the mystery isn’t solved by a single clever tactic; it’s unraveled through a chain of moments in which every choice matters.

The art direction uses high-contrast imagery, bold colors, and moody lighting to evoke the sense that legends are not abstract ideas but actual presences in the city. Accessibility-wise, the Core Rulebook does a respectable job of introducing the premise and rules without requiring a lifetime of tabletop RPG experience. The writing is punchy but clear, and the examples of play help new players absorb how the Tag-based system feels in actual scenes. It’s not a padding exercise; it’s a cinematography guide for a tabletop camera that wants to capture a moment of truth in a rain-soaked street.

If you like to compare with other systems that aim for a similar vibe, City of Mist sits closer to the narrative-heavy end of the spectrum than to the crunchy tactical end. It’s less about what you can do with a combat matrix and more about what your Legend can reveal about the city’s secrets in a single, well-placed line of dialogue.

## The Good, the Bad, and the Neon-Lit Middle Ground
There’s a lot to love in City of Mist: the mood, the storytelling emphasis, the clever use of Tags as a flexible power-plus-flaw mechanic, and the way the book invites you to describe rather than compute. The core rulebook does a great job of giving you enough structure to start playing quickly while leaving plenty of room for improvisation and personal flavor.

That said, there are small caveats worth noting:

- Rule drift in longer campaigns. Because the system is lightweight and narrative-driven, groups may diverge in how they interpret Tags and how strictly the resolution rules are applied. The book handles this with guidance rather than rigidity, which is either a blessing or a challenge depending on your group’s temperament.
- Power scale and balance. Since Legends are built from Tags rather than a formal power list, you can end up with a spectrum of capabilities that feels uneven across players. The GM will want to lean into narrative consequences to keep things feeling fair and dramatic rather than strictly mechanical.
- Table aura and theme control. City of Mist works best when the table commits to the noir mood. If your players lean toward a carefree, bright-sunshine vibe, you may need to do some table politics to keep the tone from veering off into camp or parody.

The book acknowledges these potential drift areas and provides play aids intended to keep sessions cohesive. It’s not a perfect package for every group, but it does a commendable job of giving you enough tools to tailor the experience to your table’s vibe.

## Running City of Mist: Tips for GMs and Players
If you’re approaching City of Mist as a first-time GM, here are a few practical tips to get you into the action without hijacking your night with a Rules Night of Doom:

- Start with a single Legend, one tag list, and a short Mystery. A two-hour session is plenty to demonstrate how the system works and to establish the mood.
- Use prompts rather than a rigid plot. The book’s prompts for neighborhoods, factions, and myths help you improvise while staying anchored in the city’s mythic logic.
- Lean into the Mythic-Mundane balance. The joy of City of Mist arises when a Legend’s mythic power collides with a day job, a neighbor’s secret, or a long-haul criminal network.
- Encourage players to narrate their own moments of truth. Let them tell you the tongue-in-cheek punchline for a Scene, and you’ll often get richer storytelling than if you forced a stricter sequence of events.
- Prepare a few “hooks” that can be dropped into any session. City of Mist thrives on the “what happened in the hood last night” vibe, so your hooks should be flexible enough to fit into a variety of scenes.

For players, the core advice is simple: describe what your Tag would feel like in the moment. If you’re playing a Detective who is also a Trickster, don’t just say you “use Streetwise to solve the case.” Describe how the pavement talks to you, how a clue glints with a half-remembered myth, and how the truth slides away like a slick streetlight reflected in rainwater.

## World-Building: The City as a Living Organism
City of Mist asks you to treat the city like a living organism with a nervous system of alleys, transit tunnels, and hidden rooms where myths like to hang out. The Core Rulebook offers a few structured tools to build this world while leaving room for improvisation. The Neighborhoods section helps you map different vibes: a gleaming corporate district where legends hide behind screens of glass, a seedy undercity where myths leak through the sewers, and a redeveloped riverfront that’s both beautiful and morally gray.

The factions, too, feel organic rather than contrived. They aren’t just obstacles; they’re players with agendas who push your Legend toward or away from moral decisions. My favorite moments arise when a faction’s plan intersects with a Legend’s personal storyline in a way that makes both feel consequential rather than preordained. It’s noir storytelling in its best form: you don’t know who’s corrupt until you see their actions under neon light, and even then you’re left with questions more than answers.

## Visual and Narrative Language: A Style Guide for Your Table
The Core Rulebook isn’t shy about tone. It wants you to feel the city’s pulse—the pulse of rain, of buses, of whispered legends in the back rooms of dive bars. The writing makes a point of keeping things “cool” rather than “cool-coded.” You won’t find a stiff formalism here; you’ll find an exuberant confidence that the story matters more than the systematics. This is why the book reads like a conversation with a veteran storyteller rather than a lecture from a professor of jurisprudence.

The artwork reinforces the mood without overwhelming the reader with style for its own sake. Neon signage, rain-slick streets, and close-ups of characters mid-declaration ensure that the world feels cinematic. It’s not about looking pretty; it’s about ensuring that every page invites you to describe a moment with color, texture, and emotional texture.

Pros
- Clear, narrative-first design that’s approachable for newcomers.
- Tag-based system that rewards descriptive storytelling and character flavor.
- Strong noir mood with mythic undercurrents; great for city-hopping campaigns and one-shots.
