### Practical Considerations: Size, Weight, and Maintenance

Size matters. This rug comes in a handful of dimensions that fit typical living spaces: 5x8, 6x9, and perhaps a larger 8x10 option for open-plan rooms. The weight is moderate, which means you’ll be able to reposition it without a small forklift but you’ll still want a good grip on the back to keep it in place. A non-slip rug pad is not optional here; it’s practically mandatory if you don’t want a puckish toddler or a curious cat to slide it every time someone slides by in socks.

Maintenance-wise, here’s the lay of the land:
- Vacuum regularly with a brush attachment to avoid flattening the pile.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid vigorous rubbing that can push moisture deeper into the fibers.
- For deeper stains, test a mild soap solution on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate every few months to even out wear and sun exposure.
- Annual professional cleaning is optional but recommended if you want to maximize longevity and maintain the weave’s integrity.

The maintenance philosophy here is “good enough to live with, not precious enough to treat like glass.” It’s a rug that understands you live in a home that occasionally looks like a tornado hit a craft store—and it forgives you for it.

### Cleaning and Care Notes: Keeping It Fresh

- Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting to avoid lifting the pile. Use a brush attachment rather than a beater bar.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, white cloth. Do not rub; rubbing invites fiber migration and stubborn stains.
- For liquid spills, a mild soap solution (a drop of dish soap in warm water) on a soft cloth works well. Blot, don’t scrub.
- If you notice flattening in high-traffic zones, consider rotating the rug or adding a non-slip pad to maintain a level surface.
- Professional cleaning every 12-18 months helps retain color and texture, especially if your home hosts coffee ceremonies, red wine experiments, or pet naps on the rug.
