## The You Pick mechanic: choosing your opening destiny

You Pick is the crown jewel of the base set’s design philosophy. At the start of a match, you are presented with a trio of starter options. You pick one, and that choice sequences your early game strategy. It’s a simple concept with surprisingly deep implications:

- Early game identity: your opening path dictates your tempo, risk appetite, and which sub-archetypes you’ll prioritize. 
- Replayability: different You Pick selections lead to markedly different early games, encouraging multiple playthroughs to experience the full spectrum.
- Accessibility with depth: beginners can latch onto a clear path, while veterans will appreciate the nuances that emerge from later pulls and interactions with other cards.

In practice, You Pick creates a sense of ownership and flavor from turn one. The three options are distinct enough to feel meaningful, but balanced enough that you won’t feel locked into a single track for the entire game. This mechanic sets the tone for the rest of the match and gives players a clear gateway into deck-building strategy without requiring a wall of pre-match math.

## The Chose mechanic: mid-game nuance that pays off

Chose is the mid-game, decision-rich layer that can swing momentum in a single turn. On certain cards or under certain conditions, you may choose among a short list of effects. The cost and payoff are tuned so you feel smart when you time a Chose decision perfectly, and a little silly when you misread the board and pay a price for a suboptimal pick.

Common flavors of Chose include options like:
- Chose: Draw an extra card, setting up a future turn with more tools
- Chose: Search your deck for a specific type of base card to accelerate your tempo
- Chose: Attack boost or a temporary defensive buff that keeps you in the game longer

The payoff for a well-timed Chose can be substantial, but you’ll also learn to love the near-misses when you realize a different option would have unlocked a different line of play. The net effect is a satisfying layer of decision-making that rewards planning, not luck alone.

## Strategy notes: early moves, mid-game pivots, and late-game threats

If you want to squeeze the most value from your BASE Cards, consider the following guidance:

- Start with a clear You Pick plan: decide what kind of early tempo you want and select a starter path that supports it. This reduces the cognitive load in the first few turns and increases your odds of pulling into your core engine.
- Use Chose with timing in mind: a well-timed Chose effect can turn a weak turn into a turning point. Don’t waste Chose on a minor improvement if a bigger payoff exists on the board.
- Deck balance matters: while it’s tempting to load up on high-damage cards, you’ll want a few utility or draw cards to keep your engine running when you’re stuck with a slow draw.
- Protect your engine: some early-game powerhouses can fail to connect if you don’t manage resources properly. Balance aggression with defense so you can survive to unleash your mid-game plan.
- Learn from losses: every misstep is data. After a few matches, you’ll start noticing which You Pick combinations consistently outperform others against certain archetypes.
