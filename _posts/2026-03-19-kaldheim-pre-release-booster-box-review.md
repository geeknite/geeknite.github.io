## Mechanics Spotlight: Foretell, Snow, and the Viking Vibe
Kaldheim embraces a varietal mix of mechanics, but foretell is the star that shines when you’re holding a handful of mana and a calendar. Foretell acts as a cost-reduction time-bomb: you can pay 2 mana (or an alternative cost) to exile a card and cast it later for its foretell cost. This dynamic changes deck-building and in-game timing. You might hold a crucial bomb until you can cast it with extra value, or you can disrupt your opponent’s plan by dropping a surprise threat out of nowhere.

Snow basics also play a part in the set’s flavor and function. Snow permanents interact with various cards, creating interesting synergy loops and a vibe where everything feels like it came from a frosty, fjord-draped world. If you’re a player who enjoys the “thinking ahead” mode of play, Kaldheim’s foretell plus snow interactions create a nice sandbox for planning, bluffing, and—of course—cheerfully misplaying your turns for laughs.

## Storing, Sleeving, and General Geek Hygiene
A pre-release box is a gateway to a stacking problem: once you’ve opened the packs, you’ll want to organize, sleeve, and store everything in a way that won’t collapse your entire world into a single card-dust avalanche. Here are a few practical tips that would make your future self either proud or consoled by a big stack of protective sleeves:

- Sleeve your rares and mythics in high-clarity plastic sleeves. If you’re serious, consider premium foil sleeves to preserve that shine.
- Use deck boxes labeled by color or theme. A tidy table is a happy table, and a happy table is a table where you can actually find your important cards without tears.
- Keep an inventory of what you pulled. A simple checklist helps you track what you want to trade, what you want to sleeve, and what you want to put into a binder for enjoyment later on.
- Store away from sunlight. Foil cards are delicate; you don’t want your gods to glare them into oblivion.
