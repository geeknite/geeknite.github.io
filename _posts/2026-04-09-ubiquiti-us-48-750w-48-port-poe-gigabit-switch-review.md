The US-48-750W comes in a solid 1U metal chassis. It is built for non stop operation and heat is managed with a couple of fans tucked away behind the front panel. ### Uplink options

Two SFP ports provide fiber or copper uplinks depending on your cabling needs. In a typical office environment, you will use the SFPs to connect to your core switch or a fiber ring that feeds a larger campus. The PoE budget does not limit uplink speed; your uplink can be a fast SFP+ line while your PoE devices get their power from the 750W budget.

## Features and specs

### Ports and PoE budget

- 48 x RJ45 PoE+ ports
- 2 x SFP ports for uplinks
- 750 W total PoE budget
- 1 U rack height
- Gigabit speeds per port

The 750W budget is the real differentiator here. If you are powering high wattage devices like high end cameras or multiple access points, this gives you headroom that smaller switches lack. It is also common to see a distribution plan where only a subset of ports is powering devices at high wattage while the rest are used for mid range devices.

### Management features

UniFi Controller integration is the key. The switch is designed to be adopted into the controller that runs on a PC, a dedicated appliance, or a cloud host. From there you can assign VLANs, configure port profiles, and enforce PoE policies. Features like PoE scheduling allow you to power devices only when needed, a nice touch for energy conscious deployments.

### Performance and reliability

In a typical deployment, you can expect stable performance on all ports, with PoE devices drawing power as they negotiate. The switch can handle typical office loads without issue, and the controller provides real time monitoring so you can spot port usage and PoE consumption at a glance.

## Setup and configuration

### Quick start steps

1. Rack the switch and connect the power cord.
2. Connect a computer to one of the management ports or a dedicated management VLAN.
3. Install or update the UniFi Controller and adopt the US-48-750W.
4. Create networks, define VLANs, and configure PoE budgets per port.
