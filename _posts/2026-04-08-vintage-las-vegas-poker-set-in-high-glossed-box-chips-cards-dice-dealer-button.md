## Care and restoration: keeping the legend alive
- Box care: Use a microfiber cloth to wipe down the exterior; avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could strip the gloss.
- Chip care: Inspect chips for chips—pun intended—at the edges where color may wear. If you notice chipping or cracking, you can carefully seal with a clear enamel, but test on a spare chip first.
- Card care: If the cards start to feel stiff, a light reshaping in a controlled environment can help. Do not bend them excessively; keeping them flat and stored properly will prolong their life.
- Dice care: Clean dice with a damp cloth; avoid soaking. If they become cloudy, a mild polish can help, but test on a non-essential die first to ensure no residue affects rolling.

These practices preserve the aesthetic for display and gameplay alike, ensuring that your set remains a talking point for years to come rather than a distant memory in a cluttered attic.
