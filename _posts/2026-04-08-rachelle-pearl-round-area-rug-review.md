## Durability and Care: How Long Will This Bud Last Before It Becomes a Family Heirloom or Dumpster Dive?
Durability is where many mid-range rugs either shine or retreat into the shadows. The Rachelle Pearl Round Area Rug lives in the middle of the pack in terms of long-term resilience, which is exactly where a sane person wants to be when investing in decor that isn’t a one-season fling.

- Stain resistance: Polyester blends tend to repel most common stains better than pure wool in some cases, thanks to surface finishes. Still, you’re not guaranteed miracle-clean results. Spills should be blotted promptly with a clean cloth. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent diluted in water usually does the trick. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the warp threads or alter the color.
- Vacuuming: Low-pile rugs are forgiving. Use a gentle vacuum setting and a standard brush head. If you own a high-powered vacuum with rotating brushes, test on a hidden corner first to ensure no shedding or fiber pull occurs.
- Wear and tear: In high-traffic zones, you can expect some flattening of the pile over time. This is normal; it quickly recovers with a light vacuum and a bit of foot traffic rearrangement. If you own pets with claws, you might notice occasional snags but nothing catastrophic if you stay proactive.
- Cleaning cadence: Rotate the rug every 2-4 months to promote even wear and fading. Sunlight will gradually “date” the rug, so if your space is sun-soaked, you may want to consider blinds or drapes that show mercy to your color story.

Care tips in a nutshell: blot spills, avoid soaking, test cleaners on a small area first, and keep an eye on the edges. If you treat it with a bit of respect, the Rachelle Pearl rounds out your room for years rather than seasons.

### Practical Demos: Cleaning Scenarios
- Chocolate-smeared cereal breakfast: blot with paper towels, mix a tiny bit of mild dish soap with water, dab gently, then rinse with a clean damp cloth. Let air-dry.
- Red wine (classic): blot, then mix equal parts water and white vinegar with a touch of dish soap and dab, then blot again. Repeat until the stain lightens. If it persists, call in a professional. Yes, even in the Rug Realm, there are professionals for wine crimes.
- Pet-related spills: blot, use a carpet-safe cleaner if needed, and consider a small area rug under the food bowl to catch the worst of the spills.
