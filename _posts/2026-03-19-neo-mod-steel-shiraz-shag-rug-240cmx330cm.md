## Durability and Maintenance: Will It Survive Real Life?
A rug that looks good but falls apart after a single coffee spill is not a good investment. The Neo Mod Steel Shiraz Shag Rug aims to ride the tightrope between luxury and practicality.

H2: Cleaning rituals
Maintenance for a shag rug is not as dramatic as it sounds. Regular vacuuming with a suited floor tool and a gentle rotation helps avoid uneven wear. For spills, blot—don’t rub—using a clean cloth, then dampen lightly with water and mild detergent. Always test a small area first. Deep-cleaning in a washing machine might be tempting but is usually not recommended for high-pile rugs; professional cleaning each year or two can be a good compromise for heavy traffic areas.

H2: Shedding and wear patterns
All shag rugs shed to some degree in the first weeks. Don’t panic if you find a handful of loose fibers on your shirt or laptop keyboard. This tends to settle as the pile compacts and the fibers align with traffic patterns. If you have pets or strong sun exposure, you may notice color fade over many months—though the steel Shiraz palette is relatively forgiving and hides minor fading better than a pale neutral would.

H2: Longevity expectations
A well-made shag rug of this scale can easily handle several years of daily life if cared for properly. You’re looking at routine maintenance, occasional deep cleaning, and a bit of mindful placement to avoid constant dragging of furniture that could snag the loops. The result is a rug that remains visibly plush and comfortable even after many Netflix marathons and late-night chess matches with your cat.
