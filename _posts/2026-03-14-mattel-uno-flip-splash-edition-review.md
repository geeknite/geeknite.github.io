### The flip mechanic in SPLASH
The double-sided deck is the star of the show. When you play a flip card (or when a set of flip rules on a given card triggers), the entire deck flips to the other side and the color set switches. This means the action cards you know and love (and hate) from one side may become something you secretly crave on the other. It also means you should keep track of your own color memory, because a color shift can suddenly upend your plan and hand someone else a shiny new opportunity to grab the spotlight—and the last card in their hand.

If you’ve played UNO Flip before, you know how location-based color control can shape strategy. In SPLASH, you discover that the splash side also has new card types such as wave cards that cause the next player to skip a turn while performing a water-themed mini-challenge that you might actually enjoy—like a quick rhyme, a goofy painting task, or a tiny scavenger hunt with your hand. It’s silly, but it’s the good kind of silly that makes game night unforgettable and guaranteed to cause at least one splash fight with a napkin.
