### How it changes the classic Jenga experience
If you’ve played Jenga a hundred times, you know the rhythm: remove a block, adjust your stance, admire the ceiling, and pray to the tower gods. ### Starting a game: setup and initial turns
The setup remains quick: stack the tower, ensure the blocks are aligned, and avoid the fatal mistake of letting the tower implode while someone is mid-celebration. ## Care, maintenance, and practical tips
### Cleaning and upkeep
A simple wipe with a dry cloth after play is usually sufficient. If you’ve got a spill, a damp cloth followed by air drying works fine; avoid soaking the blocks, as even the most ceremonial ebony finish doesn’t appreciate being waterlogged. If you’re worried about sealant wear, a light post-game rub with a small amount of recommended wood-safe conditioner can help maintain the surface’s lustrous look.

### Storage and transport tips
Keep the set in its original box or a padded container to prevent scuffing of the dark surface. If you carry it to gaming nights, consider a soft case so the block towers don’t rub against each other in a way that dulls the drama. A little care goes a long way toward keeping the aesthetic intact for the long-haul.

### Play etiquette and space considerations
Because this edition looks so good, you’ll want everyone to treat the tower with respect.
