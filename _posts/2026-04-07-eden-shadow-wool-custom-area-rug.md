H2: Care, maintenance, and everyday durability

H3: Cleaning basics and stain management

Wool rugs are more forgiving than you might think. The Eden Shadow line benefits from the natural resilience of wool, which helps it resist staining with the right approach. For most spills, the best immediate action is to blot (not rub) with a clean white cloth or paper towel to lift the liquid. If the spill is something deeply colored (think red wine or turmeric-level drama), you may want to treat it as a localized clean, using a wool-safe cleaner according to the cleaner’s instructions. Always check the rug’s care guidelines before applying anything, because even wool can be coaxed into a mood that hates water and soap the moment you misstep into “quick fix.”

H3: Vacuuming and traffic patterns

Vacuuming is your friend, not your frenemy, when you have a wool rug. Use a vacuum with adjustable suction and a gentle beater bar (or a brushless setting) to avoid fuzzing the pile. Regular light passes beat back the dirt without turning your Eden Shadow into a compressed memory of every snack you’ve eaten since 2018. In high-traffic areas, you may notice slight flattening over time; rotate the rug occasionally if possible, or rearrange furniture layout to distribute wear evenly. This is the part where your inner designer and inner host realize that planning for longevity is not glamorous, but it is absolutely necessary.
