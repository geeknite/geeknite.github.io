Specs and architecture
Crucial markets this duo as a 64 GB total kit running at 4800 MT/s. That speed sits comfortably in the mainstream DDR5 tier where you get good bandwidth without chasing the very highest clocks. On the bus, you re looking at a 64-bit wide data channel per DIMM, with two DIMMs in dual-channel configuration providing ample bandwidth to saturate typical gaming and productivity workloads. The DIMMs are unbuffered and non-ECC for consumer builds, which means broad compatibility with most desktop motherboards from Intel and AMD platforms.

XMP EXPO and profile handling
Most modern boards will offer an XMP or EXPO profile to push these sticks toward their rated speed. EXPO is the AMD-friendly profile that makes DDR5 a lot more approachable on AM5. If you have an Intel board, you re more likely to see XMP options in your BIOS. Either way, enabling the right profile is usually a one-click affair, and in our tests the kit behaved like a well-trained pet: it woke up, assumed a stable speed, and started fetching data without drama. If your motherboard doesn t natively support a preset, you can still tune timings manually, but you should know you re entering the land of marginal gains and potential instability if you push too far without adequate voltage headroom.
