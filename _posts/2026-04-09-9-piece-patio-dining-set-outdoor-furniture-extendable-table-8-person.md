## Comfort and seating experience

## Weather resistance and maintenance
Patio furniture lives in the wild, which means sun, rain, wind, and those occasional flying pine cones. The set we tested handles weather reasonably well, with a color-fast finish and cushions that tolerate light dampness. Some maintenance tips to extend life:

- Clean regularly: wipe with mild soap and water. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the frame or loosen the weave.
- Protect from heavy snow: when possible, store cushions indoors during heavy weather or use a high-quality outdoor storage bag. SNOW is not your friend; it is basically a tiny cold dragon that can bend frames if left unattended.
- Cover it: invest in a breathable patio cover to guard against UV and rainfall. A cover makes a big difference in the life of cushions and fabric.
- Rotate cushions: if your set has the cushions arranged in fixed positions, occasionally rotate them to wear evenly and avoid sun fade.

If you live in a particularly sunny climate, you may want to consider UV-resistant fabric and a light seat color that hides dirt better than pure white. Conversely, darker tones can show dust more readily but may hide stains better. Pick your poison accordingly and you will thank yourself later when you have guests over and your set still looks fresh.

- Versatility: can you relocate chairs easily for a party, or do you need to keep the dining area semi-permanent? Many sets allow for flexible seating configurations that help when you want to rearrange for a movie night outdoors.

For families with kids, you might want to consider a set with rounded table edges and chair heights that accommodate shorter diners. Safety can be a feature, not a fable, and this is especially true if your little ones will use the table often.
