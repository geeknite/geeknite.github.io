## Maintenance: keeping the coastal vibe alive with minimal effort

### Cleaning routines that won’t ruin your weekend
Let’s face it: outdoor spaces attract dirt, leaves, and the occasional picnic crumb that somehow multiplies into a quirk of fate. Ahoy is designed for quick cleanups. Regular vacuuming with a brush attachment helps remove loose debris. For more stubborn spots, a gentle rinse with a hose and a mild detergent typically does the trick. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can compromise colorfastness; stick to something that’s paintbrush-friendly rather than a chemical warfare. If you’re dealing with stubborn algae or mold in humid climates, a soft scrub with a mild cleaner and a rinse is usually enough to bring back the original look.

### Drying time and weather caveats
Outdoor rugs prefer air drying. After a shower or a drenching rain, Ahoy dries relatively quickly, which means less soggy footprint drama and more patio lounging. If your space tends to stay damp, you may want to position the rug in a sunny nook or elevate it slightly to improve airflow. Don’t tuck it away in a damp corner and pretend you didn’t see it; that’s how mildew ghosts get fashion-cred. The quick-dry design keeps your outdoor space from looking like a swamp while you’re waiting for the sun to return.

### Cleaning frequency and seasonal care
During peak outdoor season, you’ll likely give Ahoy a quick once-over every week or two, with a more thorough cleaning every month. In off-season climates, a periodic rinse-and-dry approach is sufficient to keep it in good shape. If you live near the sea and worry about salt buildup, a gentle rinse every now and then should do the trick. The goal is simple: prevent dirt from setting, maintain color integrity, and ensure everyone’s toes stay on the rug rather than slipping off the edge of a lazy afternoon.
