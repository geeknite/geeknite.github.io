## Setup and how to play
The setup is the part of a game that tests your ability to read instructions without becoming possessed by a Time Lord. Here is a clean, traveler-friendly way to get started with this Pictionary card deck.

### Setup (quick start)
1) Clear a small space on the table or lap desk. If you are on a plane, flatten the tray table as best you can without triggering the seatbelt sign. 
2) Shuffle the clue cards (as if you do not trust your friends with your own memory). If you have a habit of peeking during setup, consider sealing your eyes and destiny in a silly face while you shuffle.
3) Decide teams. If you are with a group that has never formed teams before, just pick a color and hope for the best. Friends who always argue about rules can become the most entertaining teammates. 
4) Place the timer within easy reach. The timer is your scolding aunt: it will remind you that time is a social currency, and it does not pay interest.
5) Distribute a notepad or scratch paper if included, or if you are a rebel, borrow a napkin and pretend it is a score sheet. 
6) Draw the first card and pass the device to the player who will draw while teammates guess. 

### How to play (classic mode)
- The clue giver draws the card and has 60 seconds to illustrate the word or phrase without using letters or spoken language. The art direction here is minimalism with a wink; your goal is to convey meaning with shapes, lines, and a smudge that vaguely resembles a flying toaster, if your toaster draws itself with enough confidence.
- Teammates shout guesses. The moment someone shouts the correct answer, the team scores a point. If you are playing with a large group, rotate the clue giver after each successful round so no one claims to be the master of stick figures for eternity.
- After a round ends, rotate to the next team. The pace should stay brisk; the 60-second timer is a hard-coded reminder that your artistry is a sprint, not a gallery opening.

### Far-out variations (if you want to mix it up on the road)
- Charades with cards: If your co-pilots prefer acting over drawing, flip the card and act it out while your team guesses. The card deck is flexible enough to handle this family-friendly chaos.
- Quick-fire rounds: Shorten the timer to 30 seconds for a more intense tempo. Warning: you may break into interpretive dance in the back row. 
- Team-based tournaments: Set up a small leaderboard using the notepad. The winner gets bragging rights that survive even the longest layover.
