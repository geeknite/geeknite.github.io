## Overclocking, profiles, and practical tuning

Overclocking DDR5 RAM is a delicate art. With a 2x32GB 64GB kit, your headroom for aggressive overclocks is constrained by the density of the modules and the stability of your memory controller. If you’re hoping to push beyond 4800 MT/s, you may encounter boot issues, higher voltages, and tight timings that could impact stability in daily use. In practice, a conservative overclock to 5200–5600 MT/s might be possible on certain boards with adequate cooling and a strong CPU memory controller, but it’s not guaranteed. For most users, enabling XMP/AMP profiles to hit 4800 MT/s CL40 is a reliable path to peak practical performance without getting into the weeds.

If you’re a hobbyist who likes tweaking, we recommend starting with the motherboard’s built-in memory profile (XMP/AMP) and ensuring your cooling is up to it. If instability arises, back off to the rated 4800 CL40 and call it a win for stability and reliability. The goal is to get a stable system that simply works without requiring you to babysit BIOS settings every week.
