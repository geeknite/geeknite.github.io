## Weather Resistance and Maintenance
### Real-World Weather Testing
Acacia wood is a good middle-ground choice: stronger than pine and more affordable than teak. It’s not immune to the sun, rain, or seasonal changes, but it’s capable of aging gracefully with regular care. The wicker’s synthetic construction stands up to UV exposure and moisture quite well, but it still appreciates preventative maintenance. Expect the wood to darken slightly with age, developing a deeper honey tone as sun exposure does its slow, natural thing. The finish helps lock in color, but a yearly light oiling (or a sealant refresh) will keep the wood vibrant rather than drying out and cracking.

### Maintenance Routine
- Oil or seal the wood every 6–12 months, depending on climate and sun exposure.
- Use table covers or a light, breathable fabric when storms are forecast to reduce moisture damage and salt spray if you live near the coast.
- Clean wicker with mild soap and water; avoid high-pressure washers that can loosen fibers.
- If you store the set, give the wood a light wipe-down before placing it in storage and use a breathable cover to avoid moisture buildup.

### Care Mistakes to Avoid
- Don’t over-oil; too much oil can lead to a sticky, gummy residue.
- Don’t neglect quick spills on wicker; left to sit, moisture can impact color and texture.
- Don’t leave cushions out in constant rain unless they’re specifically designed for outdoor use. Cushions are the weak link unless you’ve got a good cover.
