## How the rules get MHA-ified: what changes and what stays the same
UNO is one of those games that invites variations, house rules, and “I’ve got a friend who does it this way” stories. The MHA edition takes the base rules and sprinkles in three layers of flavor:

- Quirk Cards: Instead of skip/reverse/draw-two, you’ll see Quirk cards that grant temporary powers. For example, a “One For All” Quirk card might force all players to draw a single card unless they declare a specific hero aura (flip-side rules apply). The exact Quirk effects vary by deck, but they tend to encourage more interaction and give players juicy twists without blowing up the game too harshly.
- Hero Cards: These are like wild draws or wild color cards but themed around a hero’s ability. When played, they can swap hands, swap the top card of the discard pile, or force a mini-challenge (like shouting a famous line from the anime) before the next player acts. It’s goofy, it’s fun, and it adds a theatrical layer that fans adore.
- Villain Cards: The “twist the knife” cards that complicate your plans. Villain cards can reverse control, force you to draw extra cards, or reset the turn order in a way that mirrors the dramatic battles in the anime. They’re not brutally punishing; they’re more like a dramatic plot twist that happens mid-game, in the best anime tradition.

What stays the same: the core UNO flow—draw up to seven, match color or number, use action cards, yell “UNO!” when you’re down to one card. The MHA edition preserves the core rhythm: the game remains quick, accessible, and playful. The new cards don’t reinvent UNO; they remix it with flair. In a party context, that’s precisely what you want: enough structure to stay familiar but enough flavor to justify the purchase beyond “I like the characters.”

Gameplay pacing is intact for 3-6 players; with more players, you’ll want to ensure the Quirk/Hero/Villain cards don’t derail the table too aggressively. The deck handles well, and in a living room with snacks, chaos tends to remain charming rather than chaotic-chaos if you moderate the house rules.
