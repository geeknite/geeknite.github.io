## How to Play, Quick-Start Guide

### Setup

Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player, exactly as you would in classic UNO. The scorecard goes on the table, with a simple scale: the lower the score, the better you’re doing. Each player places a tee token on the 0 mark. The top card of the draw pile becomes the discard pile starter, and you are ready to swing.

### The Goal

This edition reframes victory. Instead of who runs out of cards first, you win by having the lowest cumulative score after a pre-agreed number of holes. Each hole has a par value, and you want to finish with strokes that match or beat the par. Like golf, you can brag about your birdies and lament bogeys—but here, those terms translate into card plays that move your marker on the scorecard rather than the real world greens.

### Turn Structure

On your turn, you may play one card that matches either the color or the number on the top card of the discard pile. If you cannot play, you draw one card. The twist in this Special Edition comes with golf-flavored action cards that alter your stroke count, move you along the scorecard, or punish an opponent for a questionable gamble.

- Birdie: Move your marker forward on the scorecard by one hole and reduce your current stroke count
- Par: Announce a par value on the table and subtract that amount from your running score for the hole
- Bogey: Force another player to draw a card and add strokes to that player's current hole score
- Hole-in-One: Instantly ends the current hole with a dramatic fireworks-like effect

The balance here is clever. The Birdie and Par cards reward smart, risk-aware plays, whereas the Bogey can swing momentum in an instant. It makes for moments where one clever, well-timed move can flip a round from snappy applause to enthusiastic groaning.

### Scoring Dynamics

Strokes accumulate as you lay down cards. The goal is to hit par or go a hair under with Birdies or Eagles, and the whole round hinges on how well you manage both your hand and the scorecard. When a hole ends, players tally their strokes to their hole score, and the total score across all holes determines the winner. This feels like UNO meeting a mini golf cafe—fast enough for a quick session, deep enough to justify a second round because you miscounted your Par on hole 2.

### Player Interaction

It’s a social sport, and this edition wants you yelling Nice putt! and also panicking when someone drops a Game-Changer card mid-round. Because every hole has par values and every Birdie can subtract from your current status, players talk a lot of strategy, feinting and bluffing about what color they might be able to play next.
