- Calibrate the players’ height by adjusting the feet on the legs to ensure the ball sits comfortably on the field between the rods. The perfect height is when you can cradle the ball with the fingers of one hand and still feel like you’re piloting a starfighter.

## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity: Keeping the Behemoth in Battle-Ready Condition

Maintenance is not optional; it’s the warranty that keeps your matches fair. Regular checks include:
- Cleaning the playfield: Wipe with a soft cloth to remove fingerprints and dust. A clean play surface translates into consistent ball behavior and fewer surprises when you line up a shot.
- Lubricating the rods: A light oil or silicone spray keeps the rods gliding smoothly. You’ll avoid squeaks that sound like tiny robots complaining about their existential dread.
- Inspecting the bearings and joints: Look for rust or wobble. If you notice any looseness, tighten with the included hardware or consult a professional. Loose components can ruin a fast-twitch game night and cause injuries to the ego and the table’s finish.
- Ball maintenance: Replace worn-out balls promptly. Chewed or nicked balls will veer unpredictably, undermining your trust in the table and in your own reflexes.

One practical tip: keep a small toolkit handy and store a spare set of bearing grease in your game room. If the table shows signs of stiffness after a long session, a quick re-lub will often restore that “new table” feel without requiring a full teardown.
