## The Foil Bundle in a Real-Life Scenario

### A three-table chaos test

We staged a game night with three tables: 6-8 players per table, all using cards from the foil expansion. The vibe was electric: the flicker of neon-tinged foil catching the light, the triumphant chaos as players attempted to fit the punchline to the prompt, and the inevitable spectacle of someone yelling a zinger too loudly and accidentally knocking over a drink. In short: it worked as a party accelerant, but it demanded a certain tolerance for outrageous humor.

### Practical table-ready tips

- Create a normal rotation: one player draws the prompt, two players offer cards, and the judge selects. This standard approach scales well with 600-card pools because everyone isn’t staring at a single, endless stack.
- Pre-sort: If your group tends toward long, labored setups, pre-sorting a deck into “big laughs” vs. “fast hits” can speed up rounds.
- Use a timer: Quick rounds keep momentum high when you’re juggling big card pools. A two-minute round window often yields more laughs than a two-hour debate about whether a prompt qualifies as a pun.

### House rules for maximum hilarity

- Duo-rounds: Occasionally run matches where two players combine their cards to answer a prompt, creating collaborative chaos.
- Thematic nights: Tie prompts to a theme (e.g., pop culture, workplace, or fantasy) and use the foil deck as the main source for those rounds.
- Game-end fireworks: If a player racks up a perfect run of wins, end the night with a ridiculous finale prompt so everyone walks away with a story to tell at work or school.
