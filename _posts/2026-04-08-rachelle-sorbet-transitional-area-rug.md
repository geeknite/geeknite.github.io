## Care and Maintenance

### Vacuuming and cleaning schedule

Vacuum frequency depends on foot traffic and pet presence. For typical families, a weekly vacuum with a gentle setting keeps the rug looking sharp. High traffic zones may require more frequent attention. A canister vacuum with a wide beater bar and an edge cleaning feature is ideal for getting into all the nooks and crannies along room edges.

### Stain removal tips

Treat spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing to avoid fiber damage. Use a mild cleaning solution appropriate for synthetic fibers, and test on a small inconspicuous area first. For more stubborn stains, a commercial rug cleaner following the manufacturer instructions is recommended. Avoid aggressive scrubbing that can distort the pile and alter the color distribution. In our tests, this approach preserved color integrity and texture after multiple incidents of accidental art projects by kids and pets alike.

### Odor control and freshness

Occasional odor issues can arise in busy homes. Airing the room, using a light cleaning spray that is safe for synthetic fibers, and keeping pets out of the room during drying times can help maintain a fresh feel. A rug pad not only prevents slipping but also reduces odor transfer from the floor below, aiding in long term freshness.

## Sizing, Placement, and Room Flow

### How to choose the right size

The right rug size anchors space rather than floating in the middle of it. For a typical 12x14 living room, a 8x10 rug is perfect under a seating arrangement that includes a sofa, two chairs, and a coffee table. A 5x8 rug suits smaller rooms or living areas with lean seating. If you are unsure, measure your space and lay out painter tape to simulate rug edges before purchasing. A little planning goes a long way toward achieving a polished look rather than a mismatched one.

### Placement tips for optimal balance

Place the rug so that at least the front legs of major furniture items sit on the rug. This creates cohesion and prevents the room from feeling chopped into disjointed sections. Align pattern orientation with room architecture; parallel it to windows or walls for a clean, intentional feel. If you cannot accommodate the full furniture footprint, ensuring the rug sits under the front legs of the primary seating cluster is a sensible compromise that still yields a harmonious layout.

### Combos with flooring types

On light wood floors, Sorbet adds warmth and tone without overwhelming the space. On darker woods, it provides a bright neutral foil that lifts the room. In spaces with tile or laminate, the rug softens the surface and reduces echo, which is a nice bonus for home theater or reading corners. For tropical or outdoor-adjacent rooms, ensure the rug is indoor only or choose a variant designed for high humidity environments as appropriate. In any case, pairing with a quality pad is non negotiable for comfort and longevity.
