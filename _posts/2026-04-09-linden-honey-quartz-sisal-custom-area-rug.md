## Comfort, durability, and daily maintenance
Natural sisal feels robust underfoot but is not a plush paradise. It’s a practical surface that rewards a sensible maintenance routine. Here’s what to expect:

- Underfoot feel: Crisp, slightly rough texture that many describe as “natural-luxe.” It’s not velvet-soft, but the experience is far from scratchy. People who love heavy textures or tactile rooms tend to adore sisal for how it ages with grace.
- Durability: Sisal fibers are among the sturdier natural fibers for indoor use. They resist wear in high-traffic areas, especially when backed by a stable foundation and a quality rug pad. Expect the surface to show a little patina over time—this is not an accidental sign of weakness but a telltale sign of a rug that has lived and loved a space.
- Cleaning and care: Vacuum regularly with a brush attachment, and address spills promptly. Liquid spills should be blotted gently with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can work liquid deeper into the fibers. For deeper cleaning or pet-related mishaps, many homeowners opt for professional cleaning every 12-18 months, with spot treatments as needed in between. Always check the backing and warranty guidelines from your retailer. For more detailed cleaning steps, see our recommended guide to cleaning sisal rugs: The Spruce’s sisal care guide.

Important caveats: natural fibers are susceptible to moisture and can stain if not treated. If you have pets that love to drag in dirt or liquids, you might want to pair this rug with a moisture-control mat at doors and a rug pad that offers some moisture resistance. A good rule of thumb: the more you can keep the rug dry and free from heavy moisture, the longer the color and texture will look fresh.

## FAQs
- Is this rug good for homes with pets? Yes, to a degree. The fiber is durable and resists minor wear, but nails and claws can create pulls over time. A good door mat and regular maintenance reduce risk.
- Can I wash it at home? Not a full wash. Natural fibers don’t respond well to heavy moisture. Quick spot-cleaning and professional cleaning every year or two are recommended, depending on usage.
- How do I prevent it from slipping? Use a high-quality rug pad underneath. It improves safety, extends the rug’s life, and reduces noise from foot traffic.
- Does color fade with sunlight? Prolonged exposure to direct sunlight can dull warm tones. Place the rug away from direct sun or rotate the rug to distribute exposure evenly.
- How long does it last? With proper care and a good pad, you can expect 5-10+ years in a typical living area, depending on traffic and how well you maintain the rug.
