## Performance in the wild: synthetic vs real-world numbers

### Synthetic benchmarks
When you read official specifications, you’ll see numbers that sound impressive on a spec sheet: read/write speeds that edge into the hundreds of MB/s. In the real world, actual speeds will depend on your USB controller, host device, and file sizes. If you run the drive through a consistent, clean test on a modern laptop with USB-C, you might approach those numbers for large sequential transfers. If you’re copying a folder of thousands of tiny files, you’ll see lower sustained speeds due to small-file overhead and file system fragmentation. In Geeknite terms: think of synthetic numbers as a glamour shot, and real-world numbers as the candid camera—still good, but the lighting is less forgiving.

If you’re plugged into a modern Windows machine, macOS laptop, or a Linux PC with a USB-C port, this drive should pop up as a standard external storage device. If you’re using Windows, you’ll typically format the drive once to NTFS and go about your business. macOS users may format to exFAT if they want cross-compatibility with Windows. ## How to get the most from your Crucial external SSD

- Keep it cool: even a passively cooled drive loves a little airflow when you’re copying large files. Don’t bury it in a pile of cables or inside a crowded laptop bag with heat sources.
- Use the right format: if you intend to share between Windows and macOS, choose exFAT for broad compatibility. If this drive is a workhorse on a single platform, a native format like NTFS (Windows) or APFS (Mac) might offer better performance with proper permissions and features.
- Enable TRIM where possible: modern operating systems support TRIM for external SSDs, which helps maintain performance over time. Check your OS docs to ensure TRIM is enabled for external drives if the system supports it.
- Backups beat panic: think of this as your portable safety net. Schedule recurring backups or use it to take a fresh snapshot of critical files before upgrades or changes to your workflow.
- Security considerations: if you’re moving sensitive data, consider an encrypted container on the drive or a drive with hardware encryption. We did not see a claim of hardware encryption on the base SKU, so verify the exact model you purchase if security is a priority.
