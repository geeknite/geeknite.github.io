## Care, maintenance, and long-term wear
### Routine care
For routine upkeep, vacuum weekly with a dry wand or a beater bar turned to a moderate setting. If you own a home with a vacuum that lives on a mission control level of suction, you’ll want to adjust to avoid excessive friction with the pile. Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and exposure to sunlight, which can slightly fade color in any textile offering a vintage vibe.
### Stains and spills
Treat spills promptly with blotting motion (don’t rub). If a stain persists, opt for a mild cleaning solution compatible with your rug’s fiber type and follow the manufacturer’s instructions. For persistent stains, a professional cleaning every 12–24 months keeps the rug looking fresh without allowing grime to set into the fibers. Remember: this is a product designed for daily living, not a showroom prop; a little wear is a sign that you’re living well.
### Sunlight and fading
Seafoam plays nicely with natural light, but prolonged, direct sun exposure can shift the hue slightly over many years. If your living room enjoys blazing afternoon sun, consider using sheer curtains or rotating the rug’s position seasonally to preserve the color story. Think of it as a light-based version of BIOS updates—nice to have, not strictly required, but it keeps things from becoming performance-lazy over time.
