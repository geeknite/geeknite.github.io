### Color guidance and painting tips
For Audrey Bookworm, a palette leaning towards earthy browns, creams, and bookish blues can evoke that stern librarian vibe you expect from a character who reads the room as well as the book. Consider subtle shading on the book spine, perhaps a faux-leather texture for the cover and a muted gold for metallic embellishments. A clean, bright eye color gives her a hint of intelligence, while a slightly weathered edge on the coat sells the survivor vibe.

For Amy Big Bang, go bolder. A neon accent on her energy accessory or a splash of cosmic purple in the blast effect can sell the “Big Bang” concept without turning the model into a cartoon. Metallic golds on her gadgets, a drizzle of turquoise on the cape, and careful dry-brushing on the boots can create a focal point that pops against a zombie tide. The trick is to maintain contrast: Audrey remains the calm in the eye of the storm; Amy, the spark that lights the fuse.

## Painting and display considerations
If you’re into painting, this duo is a solid test-bed for a few techniques:

- Audrey Bookworm: experiment with clean, crisp highlights on the book cover and a subtle gradient on the coat. The eyes are a great place to practice tiny white dots for “life in the tiny universe”—it’s a classic technique to keep a face from looking dead even when the world has gone dead.
- Amy Big Bang: emphasis on glow effects around energy accessories. Try a layered glaze from purple to blue to create that cosmic energy drift. The base can stay neutral, letting the two miniatures be the focal point during diorama photography.

Display options include a simple acrylic riser to give them a prime view or a small vignette that hints at the moment they reveal their powers. If you’re part of a paint club, a small weekly challenge to improve their shaders could produce an excellent bonding experience for your group.
