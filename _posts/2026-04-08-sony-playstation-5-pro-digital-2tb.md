Digital-only means no Ultra HD Blu-ray drive in the chassis. The digital-only route is perfect for people who want a neat, quiet, and modern box that slots neatly between your HDMI switcher and your 4K TV without collecting dust like a rare vinyl record. ### Storage, upgrades, and where the speed actually matters
Two terabytes of internal storage is a practical sweet spot for most players. You’ll fill this space with a handful of modern AAA titles and still have room for a bunch of indie gems that you intend to replay someday. The ability to move games between the internal SSD and an external drive is a big usability win. It means you can keep the “in rotation” games on the internal drive for quick access and push older or less frequently played titles to USB storage without feeling like you’re sacrificing performance.

The Pro Digital also benefits from improved IO pathing and a slightly more robust controller-micro architecture, which translates to less time spent waiting on patches and more time exploring virtual worlds. It’s not a sci-fi dream where you never see a loading screen again, but it’s close enough to feel like progress in a console era known for long install times and patch cycles.

If you’re debating whether to upgrade from a base PS5 or from the disc edition, weigh the value of digital convenience against your need for physical collectibles.
