## Material and craftsmanship
Resin is a fascinating material for hobbyists. It’s heavier than plastic but not as fragile as the glass-still calisthenics of some metal figures. The resin used here is typically cast to preserve fine details: little creases in uniforms, tiny belts, and the kind of facial scowls that say, “I took a dive, but I’m back on my feet.” Expect crisp edges and a smooth surface that is friendly to painting if you want to customize teams to your heart’s content.

The finish on the pieces leans toward semi-matte with a touch of gloss in the highlight areas; it gives the impression of a miniature forged in a sci-fi workshop rather than a toy assembly line. Mold lines—if any—are minimal and usually located at inconspicuous seams, which means you won’t be spending evenings with a hobby knife trying to rescue a figure’s face from a seam ridge. If you’re into priming and painting, you’ll find it extremely workable. A thin primer coat helps the paint adhere evenly and reduces the risk of chipping after a few quick clatters to the table sometime during a heated match.

Color options vary by seller, with some offering classic team colors, others offering marbled or metallic finishes. A common tactic among painters is to base coat in the primary color and then use a glaze to accent the crevices for that “hero in armor” vibe. The resin accepts washes and layered shading beautifully, so you can create crests, insignias, or even a tiny nod to your favorite sci-fi faction on each figure. If you’re into tiny storytelling on the field, this is a dream canvas.

## Maintenance and care
Resin figures aren’t magic—though they may feel magical when you’re gaming late at night and the lamp casts dramatic shadows over your tiny heroes. They require a bit of routine care to ensure longevity. Wipe after use with a soft cloth to remove fingerprints, dust, and the occasional dribble of that soda you forgot to seal away. A gentle soap solution works fine; avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the finish over time.

If you’re prone to playing on carpets that attract dog hair or cat fluff, consider keeping a small brush handy to clean the base and the rod contact points. Resin can accumulate micro-dust in the crevices, especially if you have a particularly dusty gaming corner. A light brush or a can of compressed air can be a lifesaver before you begin a tournament, saving you from a “why does the ball keep sticking” mystery just as you’re forcing your opponent to admit it’s a “house rule” victory for your team.

Maintenance tip: if you paint any of the pieces, you’ll want to seal the artwork with a clear coat to prevent chipping during the inevitable table-tilting moments that come with a heated match. It’s a small investment of time that pays off in long-term aesthetics.
