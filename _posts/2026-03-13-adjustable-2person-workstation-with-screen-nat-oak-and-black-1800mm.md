## Assembly and setup: how hard is it to assemble for two people who have opinions?

Two-person assembly is a different game from assembling a solo desk. You’ll likely want a second pair of hands for aligning the top with the frame and for guiding the central surface into place. The good news: this desk is designed for modular assembly, with straightforward steps, labeled hardware, and a reasonable number of components. The not-so-good news: there will be a moment where you both realize the Allen wrench you’ve been using is the wrong size for one of the cam locks. Here are our practical tips to survive the process without filing a formal complaint to the furniture gods.

- Clear the space and lay out components. Two heads are better than one for spotting parts that look identical but aren’t.
- Secure anchors and feet first. Make sure the base is stable before you start attaching the top surface. A wobbly base makes for a dramatic unboxing video, but not a practical workspace.
- Align the top with the frame before tightening. Small misalignments can snowball into larger issues when you go to attach the monitor arms or cable channels.
- Use a level. It helps ensure the surface is perfectly flat for both users and reduces the chance of one user’s keyboard sliding away when the other leans in.
- Don’t overtighten. Cam locks and screws should be snug but not overtightened, which can crack veneer or strip threads. If it feels like you’re coaxing perfection out of a stubborn marble statue, back off and re-align.
- Check for wobble. If the surface wobbles with a light touch, re-tighten where needed. A steady base is half the battle in a two-person workstation.

### Real-world setup tips for two people
- Mark your zones: draw a light line using painter’s tape along the back of the surface to define the two personal spaces. It helps you maintain boundaries without resorting to “engineering a fence” between you and your teammate.

- Test-drive the layout: have both users sit and test reach. If one user’s keyboard is too far, adjust the monitor height first, then reposition monitors or peripherals. It’s amazing how small adjustments can reduce fatigue over a long day.

## Maintenance and longevity: keeping the oak look fresh

Real-wood veneers love routine care. A soft cloth, mild cleaner, and occasional conditioning (as recommended by the veneer manufacturer) will help preserve the surface’s appearance. Avoid harsh solvents that could dull the finish or compromise the veneer wrap. For the metal frame, a simple wipe-down with a damp cloth prevents dust buildup, and you can apply a light coat of metal-safe polish if you’re aiming for showroom shine.

Two-person desks benefit from routine checks. Tighten loose screws on monitor arms and footings, rebalance the seating arrangement periodically, and ensure cables aren’t pulling the surface in unexpected ways. The goal is to maintain a stable, comfortable workspace so the desk remains a reliable partner in your daily workflow rather than a recurring source of drama.
