## The Core Loop: How to Play (2-5 Players)
Exploding Kittens is a take-that game at heart. The goal is simple: do not explode. Some players will claim that staying calm is not in the rulebook, and you’ll know exactly what they mean when someone plays a Nope card at the precise moment you’re about to draw the dreaded boom—the Exploding Kitten.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck, deal 7 cards to each player, and insert a number of Defuse cards into each player’s opening hand (usually 1-2, depending on the variant you’re playing).
- Remove all Exploding Kitten cards from the deck, then add the correct number of explosive threats back in depending on the number of players. In a 2-player game, you’ll want a lighter version of the deck; with 3-5 players, you can go standard, or even spice it up with a few extra threats from the Lot Of 2 setup.
- Each player starts with a small set of Defuse cards; this is your lifeline, your insurance policy, your ticket out of an instant zing.

### Turn Structure
On your turn you can play one or more of your action cards (Nope, See the Future, Attack, Shuffle, etc.), then draw one card from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you must defuse it using a Defuse card or you’re out of the round. If no Exploding Kitten cards are left, the round ends when the last player survives.

### Exploding Kitten and Defuse Tales
The Exploding Kitten card is the boogeyman of the deck, the moment where everyone collapses into faux-dramatic mourning. Defuse cards give you a second chance at life (and at bragging rights). They’re the lifebuoy in a sea of chaos, and in a Lot Of 2, you’ll see Defuse cards moving around faster than memes on a late-night chat thread.
