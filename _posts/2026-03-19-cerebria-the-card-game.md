## Gameplay Overview: a pulse across mood tracks
Cerebria: The Card Game uses a card-driven engine that blends drafting, hand management, and tactical placement to create a compelling loop.

### Turn structure (a typical round)
- Draw and refresh: You draw up to a hand limit, sometimes influenced by ongoing conditions or the mood on the board.
- Play phase: You select cards to play that upgrade your mood trajectory, trigger effects, or influence other players. Card text often includes a mix of immediate effects and longer-term planning hooks.
- Reaction window: Opponents get a chance to respond. This is the place where you feel the game’s social layer—the moments when a table realizes a daring plan has teeth and other players pivot accordingly.
- Mood track resolution: The board tracks the shifting emotional balance as you score small milestones or trigger global effects. You don’t necessarily need to dominate a single track to win; the game rewards flexible, multi-vector scoring and careful timing.
- End-of-round scoring: The round ends when a certain condition is met (the game designer’s signature twist). You tally points, refresh resources, and prepare for the next round.

This rhythm feels satisfying because it rewards both long-term strategy and short-term opportunism. You’ll appreciate the moments where a single card swap changes the entire table’s dynamic, and you’ll groan in good humor when you realize your plan has collapsed in spectacular fashion because you misread the mood on the table.

### Key mechanics you’ll actually use
- Drafting and hand management: You aren’t just playing cards; you’re constructing a tiny plan of attack that moves with the mood of the game. Your best moves come from understanding not only your own direction but also how the other players’ choices ripple through the mood tracks.
- Mood influence and board control: The central metaphor—emotions shaping the world—stays front and center. Your ability to steer those moods at the right moment becomes more valuable than raw card power.
- Event and reaction synergy: The game rewards clever timing, especially when you hold back a reaction card for a perfect moment. The sense of “I knew that was coming” becomes a satisfying laugh line at the table.

## Strategies and Tips: practical, non-spoilery mantras
Here are some practical, non-spoilery strategies to get you started, especially if you’re new to Cerebria: The Card Game:

- Balance short-term gains with long-term plans: Some cards give immediate points or disruption, while others set up for later. The best players don’t chase a single turn; they build momentum toward multiple scoring opportunities.
- Read the table’s mood: The utility of many cards hinges on how the mood tracks are moving globally. If you sense a high-risk mood shift coming, it might be worth pivoting to a more defensive stance rather than pushing for a risky payoff.
