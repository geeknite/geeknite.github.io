## Condition, Value, and Authenticity: The Collector’s Checklist

New and sealed is a beautiful aspirational state for a piece of gaming ephemera. It implies a time capsule that has not been opened, and it usually supports a premium price in the vintage market. However, value is not purely a function of seal status. Condition of the outer box, the absence of any deck warping, and an uncrushed edge can all influence market value.

Here are a few practical authenticity and condition tips for this particular expansion:

- Inspect the shrink wrap for uniformity. A jagged tear or reseal marks may indicate prior opening. Some dedicated sellers re-apply shrink wrap with ceremonial care; be aware that this is a red flag for some collectors.
- Check the card faces for uniformity. If possible, view close-up photos of the card backs to ensure consistent coloration and avoid signs of fading that would betray age or exposure to humidity.
- Verify the packaging barcode or any manufacturing stamps that might be present inside the lid. These tiny markers can reveal the production batch and date codes, which helps you confirm authenticity.
- Compare with known reference images. The vintage market is full of near-mint copies and cleverly aged fakes, but a careful eye can separate those from the real thing with a bit of patience.

Speaking of value, if you are considering resale value, remember that sealed condition matters more for some buyers than others. Some people want an expensive shelf ornament; others want a playable addition to their home game library. In either case, the No. 133 expansion is a difficult item to replace with a single keystroke search, which is part of its allure.
