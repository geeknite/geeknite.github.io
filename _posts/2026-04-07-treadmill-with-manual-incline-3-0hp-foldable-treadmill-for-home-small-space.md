### The Manual Incline Mechanism
Manual incline systems are the rational choice for compact machines. There’s no motor to burn through your electricity budget, no complex sensor arrays to confuse your cat, and no app that insists on extra permissions you didn’t sign up for. In this treadmill, incline is adjusted by a lever and locked into preset angles (0%, 3%, 6%). It means you’ll get a steadier climb when you need it and a flatter run when you’re just trying to maintain the cardio cadence between Zoom meetings.

The incline should be set before you start; attempting a mid‑run incline is a recipe for chaos, tripping, and a dramatic “why did this happen to me?” moment. The advantage, besides reliability, is that the weight distribution on the belt remains predictable. You aren’t fighting a torque machine; you’re negotiating with a mechanical lever. The result is a robust feeling that makes you trust the device before your feet even start to patronize gravity.

## A Few Handy Tips for Getting the Most from It
- Start with the incline at 0% for the first week, then slowly add 3% for short intervals if you’re building tolerance. Don’t bite off a 6% incline if you’re just waking up at 7 a.m. and you remember the kitchen is on the other side of the house.
