## How to Play: The Core Flow

Throw Throw Burrito is built for speed. The basic loop is simple: draw, match, throw, and dodge. The game accommodates multiple players, which means you get double the cards and triple the chances for something glorious to slip from someone else’s grip. Here is a distilled walkthrough that will have you playing within minutes:

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a hand to each player. The exact hand size is not a sacred number; it depends on how many folks are around and how brutal you want this to feel. A standard table of four to six players tends to work best because it produces the right amount of chaos without turning the living room into a full contact sport.
- Place burritos within easy reach. The idea is that you can huck them with minimal fuss, not that you should perform a cross-court tennis match. Accessibility matters here, and the soft burritos are actually comfortable to handle, which matters when your reflexes are already in panic mode.
- Create a central discard pile and set the score to zero. The card deck will dictate the pace of the night, and your ability to ignore the burn of a burrito to the arm will shape your destiny.

### The Basic Flow
On your turn, you will typically do two things in one moment: draw two cards and hope you do not accidentally reveal a burrito duel at the wrong moment. The deck is full of sets and action cards that accelerate the chaos. When a player completes a set, they call it out and add the points to their tally. Sets come in different shapes and colors, so there is a small layer of memory and pattern recognition required. The more sets you collect before someone triggers a burrito duel, the more you win the crowd’s favor and your own bragging rights.

But the real spice comes with burrito cards. When a burrito card surfaces, you and your rivals must physically throw a burrito toward another player. Not at them in anger, but toward their area of table space, in a way that creates the most comedic misfortune. If you land a burrito on someone, you might steal a card or force a swap, depending on the exact card draw. The result is a sprint through social physics where laughter often outruns the points themselves.

### The Burrito Duels

Duel moments are where the magic happens. When a burrito duel card is revealed, players lock in a mini duel with quick throws and dodges. The objective is to avoid getting hit while still trying to strike your target. Hit detection is forgiving but honest; there is no referee, just your friends with a healthy mix of competitive spirit and good sportsmanship. The duels tend to escalate quickly: a light toss becomes a dramatic arc, which becomes a panic sprint toward the nearest couch or chair to dodge the next burrito as if you are training for a championship that only exists in a sitcom.

For newbies, the initial burrito duels can feel a little chaotic, but chaos is the point. As you get familiar with the rhythm, the duels transform into a dance of misthrows, clever dodges, and the occasional accidental hug that is somehow both adorable and dangerous to fabric. The burritos themselves contribute to a surprisingly physical, goofy vibe that makes the game feel more like improv theater than a traditional board game session.

## Variant Rules and House Rules

Every group tends to adopt their own flavor, and Throw Throw Burrito is no exception. Here are some house rules we have tested and enjoyed, along with a few that we would avoid unless you want a longer game night:

- Short Match, Quick Start: Start with a smaller deck, deal fewer cards, and set a lower target score to speed up sessions for newcomers or people with a low tolerance for chaos.
- Burrito Freeze: Once a burrito duel is declared, allow a 10 second pause for dramatic effect to lift the tension before the throws commence. It is silly, it is fun, and it buys time for spectators to swap seats with their own snacks.
- The Gentle Toss Variant: Encourage soft throws that are more about proximity and timing than raw power. It reduces the risk of accidental table damage while preserving the comedic effect.
- Team Chaos: Pair players into teams to collect sets. The burrito duels then become a team sport, which adds strategic depth and reduces one player’s constant focus on victimization by a single rival.
