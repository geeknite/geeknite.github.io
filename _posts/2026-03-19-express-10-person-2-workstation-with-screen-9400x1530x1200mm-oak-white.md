## Assembly, maintenance, and the long-term love affair

### The assembly saga
Like any multi-person workstation with integrated screens, assembly is a two-act play: unboxing and buttoning up the ridiculous number of bolt patterns, and then cabling the backbone of your productivity. Expect a few hours for a team of two to four people to assemble, with more time if you opt for built-in screens or more elaborate cable management channels. The manufacturer usually includes a detailed instruction set and pre-labeled hardware to prevent you from doing the fast-forward version of bolt-on chaos. Pro tip: delegate the “curse of the Allen keys” to one person, and assign a second person to supervise cable routing with surgical precision.

### Maintenance and care
Monthly wipe-downs with a wood-safe cleaner preserve the veneer’s warmth. For the white laminate, avoid abrasive cleaners that might dull the surface. Conveyor-belt style dust bunnies love to nest under the surface, so a quick vacuum along the cable trays and under the desktops can keep things tidy. If you’re the kind of office that treats furniture as a long-term investment, you’ll want to tighten hardware screws every few months and check the screen mounts for any signs of wobble.

### Durability in a busy environment
The Oak White finish balances visual appeal with resilience. In high-traffic areas, you’ll see occasional scuffs near foot traffic zones; these are usually easy to buff out with the right cleaner. If your team has a tendency to use the desk as a staging area for prototypes, consider adding protective edge guards to minimize chips and scratches. Over time, you’ll notice the surface develops a patina that tells a story of long sprints, late-night debugging, and the occasional celebratory pizza party.
