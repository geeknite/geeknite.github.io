### How to Use and Dose Guidance
Proper dosing for large dogs depends largely on weight and individual response. The general guidance for Multicare Large Dogs is:

- Typical dosage: one tablet per day, given with meals to improve absorption and reduce stomach upset.
- Administration: tablets are chewable, which works well for many dogs. Some may prefer to gulp them like a treat, while others will require a little disguise—think peanut butter, soft cheese, or a slice of turkey—if they are tablet-resisters.
- If you are unsure about dosage due to weight, age, or health conditions, consult your veterinarian for a personalized plan.
- If your dog is on other medications, check for potential interactions or contraindications with your vet.

### Practical Administration Tips
Administering a supplement to a large, often food-morte canine audience is part art, part science. Here are some practical tips to improve compliance:

- Use a consistent time each day to create a routine your dog can anticipate.
- Pair the tablet with a meal or a small treat to improve palatability and reduce the chance of the dog discarding the pill.
- If your dog is suspicious of pills, try a gentle conditioning approach: offer a few small treats without tablets to create a positive association with the process.
- For picky eaters, consider formulating the tablet into a “guarded treat” with peanut butter and a sprinkle of unsweetened yogurt inside a hollowed-out bone-shaped cookie. The goal is a happy, swallowable bite, not a dare.
- Be mindful of other medications or supplements your dog is taking. Some combinations can affect absorption or interaction with the liver or kidneys. Always check with your vet if your dog has chronic conditions.

## Safety, Warnings, and Vet Guidance
Safety should always come first when adding any supplement to your dog’s regimen. Keep the following in mind:

- Always read the label and follow dosage guidance. Do not exceed recommended amounts without veterinary supervision.
- Monitor for digestive upset, changes in appetite, vomiting, or diarrhea. If you notice these symptoms persist, consult your veterinarian.
- Keep the product out of reach of children and pets who should not have access to medication.
- If your dog has known allergies, chronic conditions, or is on other medications, consult a veterinarian before starting Multicare or any supplement.
- This product is a supplement, not a replacement for veterinary treatment for significant joint disease or pain that impacts quality of life or mobility.
