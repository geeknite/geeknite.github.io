## What is Herd Mentality? (Rules in 2,000 Words or Fewer)
This section will avoid turning into a legal contract. If you’ve played any group decision games before, you’ll spot the bones quickly, but the meat is where the humor lives.

### Setup
- Each player takes a small herd board, a handful of moo points (tokens), and a set of response cards that spell out potential attitudes or actions.
- A deck of prompts sits in the middle. Prompts come in different colors or symbols indicating difficulty or how many players can align with the answer. The herd’s goal is to predict the majority answer while managing the chaos of dissenters.
- The round begins with one player acting as the “Leader” (or the “Moosterchief,” if you’re feeling bovine). The Leader reveals a prompt and chooses a position card that represents their own stance. The rest of the players secretly pick a response card from their hand that aligns with their guess of what the herd will say.

### Core Gameplay Loop
- The Leader reveals the prompt and their stance. Players play their response cards face-down.
- Everyone reveals simultaneously. If a sufficient number of players align with the Leader’s stance, they all earn moo points. If the Leader’s stance is unpopular but clever, they earn bonus points for predicting the herd’s curiosity and bias.
- The deck contains wild prompts that add curveballs—e.g., a sudden moo from the crowd, a surprising exception, or a rule change halfway through the round. This is where the chaos and laughter intensify.
- Round after round, players balance between following the herd for safety and breaking away for glory. The score track meanders unpredictably, like a cow deciding whether to graze on the left or the right side of a tastier-looking patch of grass.

### Key Mechanics
- Herd Consensus: The heart of the game. Players secretly pick a stance and then compare it to the actual majority. The more players that align, the bigger the score, but there’s always a risk the majority knew something you didn’t.
- Influence Tokens: A limited resource that lets you nudge the group, swap a card, or bluff your way into the majority. It creates tension as players attempt to out-bluff the other bluffers without tipping their own hand.
- Leader Rotation: The leadership role rotates, giving everyone a turn to steer the herd. This prevents the game from becoming a perpetual democracy of the same few players and helps the social dynamics stay fresh.

### Endgame and Victory Conditions
The game ends after a set number of rounds, or when a player hits a victory threshold tied to public perception and accuracy. It’s not always about being the loudest moo-er; the sly players who time their bets well—especially when the herd’s mood shifts—often win the prize: a shimmering plastic bell, or a tongue-in-cheek trophy that says, “Most Predictive Grazier.”

## Strategy and Depth: How Much There Is to It
Herd Mentality isn’t a brain-burner, but it does reward players who think about more than just the current round. Here are some strategic considerations you’ll encounter as you wrangle with the herd:

### Reading the Room Is a Skill
Much of the game’s depth comes from predicting how your fellow players will align with the majority. You’ll want to observe patterns: who tends to align with the crowd when the prompt is easy, who goes rogue when the reward is high, and who’s likely to bluff convincingly. The more you know your table, the better you’ll perform.

### Bluffing vs. Following
There are moments when the best move is to go with the herd and moments when you should pivot. The tension between following and bluffing creates dynamic interactions. Your goal isn’t just to pick the most popular answer but to anticipate how others will misread the herd’s mood and exploit it.

### Resource Management
Influence tokens add a resource-management layer. You’ll need to decide when to spend them for a strategic edge or to hold onto them for a potential late-round reversal. The scarcity of tokens prevents everyone from being a power-hungry mastermind all game long, which is a relief for players who love efficient, fast-paced sessions.

### Variants for Variety
If you’re the type who craves new challenges, the game offers some rule variants: higher-stakes prompts, a cooperative mode where everyone must reach a herd consensus, and a “free-for-all” mode where players can disrupt more aggressively. These variants keep the title fresh for repeat sessions and prevent the herd from getting bored.
