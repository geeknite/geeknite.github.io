## How to Play: The Blizzard Twist on Codename Mechanics

Codenames thrives on simple rules and immediate gratification. The Blizzard Edition keeps the core appeal—two teams, word clues, one winning board—while injecting a glow-stick of thematic flavor that glows brighter the more you lean into it.

### Setup and player count
- Player count remains flexible, commonly 4–8 players, split into two teams. 
- Each team has a spymaster and field operatives. The spymaster provides clues; the field operatives guess. The team with more Epic Loot tokens (earned by successful guesses) wins when the board is exhausted or when the boss mode timer hits zero.

### Core rule set with a Blizzard twist
- The base rule remains: a spymaster gives a one-word clue plus a number indicating how many words on the board relate to that clue. 
- The Blizzard twist introduces two layers: hero-balance cards and boss mode cues. Hero-balance cards grant a temporary advantage (or a humorous drawback) for a single round. Boss mode turns a single turn into a high-stakes mini-boss encounter, where the spymaster may reveal an additional “boss hint” that all players must interpret within a time limit.
- The “word bank” is curated to blend Blizzard terminology with ordinary nouns that could plausibly appear in a Blizzard game’s universe. Some players will groan at the puns; others will cheer as their favorite character is suddenly a metaphor for a riddle about a lunch break.

### Sample rounds and the humor quotient
- Round one: The spymaster says, “Nova 2.” The board includes “Starcraft,” “Lich,” and “Nova.” If the guessers connect correctly to “Nova” and one adjacent word like “Starport,” they’re on the right track, but a mis-aimed connection to “Lich” could spark a delightful debate about class balance and undead synergy.
- Round three: Boss mode triggers. The team must solve a three-word clue with a time limit, while the boss card introduces a mechanic like “double-turn,” forcing the opposing team to respond quickly or face a cascade of additional clues.

### Accessibility for new players
