## Installation and Setup

### Tools you might need
- A tape measure to confirm desk thickness
- A screwdriver for the screw on option
- A friend to hold the panel while you pretend to be a carpenter on a budget

### Step by step
1) Determine your preferred mounting method — clamp on for a no drill setup, screw on for a rock solid barrier.
2) For clamp on, position the panel so the clamps align with the desk edge and roughly center the panel length on the desk. Tighten the clamps until they are snug but not over tightening that may crush the desk veneer.
3) For screw on, mark the screw holes on the desk underside using the provided template. Drill pilot holes if needed and drive in the screws until the panel sits flush. Avoid over tightening which can crack the desk or warp the panel base.
4) Check for level and wobble. If the panel moves, tighten the clamps or screws a notch more. If the desk is extra wobbly, consider shimming or placing a small rubber pad under the clamp feet to distribute pressure.
5) Clean up and test. Do a quick walkaround to ensure cables are not pinched and your headset microphone is not catching on the edge of the panel.

### Quick tips
- If you are renting and may move locations, clamp on is your friend. It leaves no holes and is reversible.
- If your desk has a curved edge or unusual trim, the clamp on method may require some careful alignment to avoid the panel not seating properly.
- Use a microfiber cloth for the surface; avoid harsh solvents that can degrade the melamine surface.
