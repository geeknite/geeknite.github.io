## Setup and Assembly: Turning Flat into Rally-Ready
### Step-by-Step Assembly (In Plain Geek Speak)
If you’re a human with two working hands, you can assemble this in under 30 minutes if you take your time and don’t panic when the Allen key demands to be more of a launch device than a tool. Here’s the streamlined process:
1. Unpack and sort: confirm you have the table top, legs, wheels, and the clamp net. If you’re missing a wing of screws, blame the packaging, not your life choices.
2. Attach the legs to the underside of the tabletop. The design uses a couple of sturdy bolts and a few plastic brackets that behave themselves rather nicely.
3. Extend or collapse the legs as needed; you’ll notice they’re designed to offer stability on typical indoor floors without turning your living room into a dance floor of wobble. The wheels are a nice touch for shifting the whole assembly into place.
4. Mount the clamp net: clamp nets are deceptively simple in concept and surprisingly trickier in execution if you’re not paying attention to the height. The Uimoso net clamps onto the edges of the table top with a secure feel, and the net itself stretches to a reliable height that keeps the ball in play without turning into a collagen monster at the net line.
5. Clip the net to fit the standard height: the markings are intuitive enough that even a sleep-deprived gamer can slot it into place.
6. Level check and test bounce: you’ll want a quick bounce test across the table to ensure consistent bounce and that the net height hasn’t shifted during the excitement of your first rally. If you hear creaks or squeaks, stop and re-tighten a couple of screws.

If you don’t own a torque wrench, don’t worry: most people only need a basic Allen key and a little patience. The nice thing is that the table remains compact when folded, which means you can stash it behind a couch, under a bed, or in the closet of your inner monologue where you whisper “one day I’ll play tennis.”
