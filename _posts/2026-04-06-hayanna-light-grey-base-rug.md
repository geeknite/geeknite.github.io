## Care and maintenance

### Cleaning habits
Regular vacuuming is the trick to keeping the look fresh. The rug responds well to standard vacuum heads and won’t show every crumb like a white-collar spreadsheet after a coffee spill. For spills, blot—don’t rub—using a clean cloth, and treat the area with a mild cleaner suitable for synthetic fibers if necessary. Don’t rely on heavy-duty solvents or abrasive brushes; this is a rug, not a test chamber for chemistry class.

### Stain handling
Small stains can usually be tackled with a gentle cleaner or a dab of dish soap mixed with water. If you’re the kind who hosts game nights with mysterious sauces and fancy cocktails, you’ll appreciate that the fabric tends to resist permanent staining better than heavier textiles. If a big disaster hits, consider a professional cleaning every couple of years to maintain the rug’s color integrity and texture. It’s not a spaceship hull; it’s fabric, but it benefits from a steady maintenance schedule.

### Longevity tips
Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fading. This is especially important in rooms with strong sunlight or if your layout channels traffic in a single direction. A simple rotation keeps patterns even and helps the rug wear gracefully rather than developing obvious walking paths that make your guests feel watched by the floor itself.
