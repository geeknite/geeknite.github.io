## The diamond: 1.41 ct M VS2
### Carat, color, clarity explained
Carat weight in diamonds is the mass of the stone. 1.41 ct is a substantial size that often reads as a luxury without screaming huge price tag. It is not the bee's knees of carat monsters, but it is in that sweet spot where you can feel the sparkle from across the room without needing a disco ball in the living room. Color grade M sits in the near-colorless zone. It is a gentle whisper of color, often not noticed in ordinary lighting but detectable under a controlled white light. VS2 clarity means the diamond has very slight inclusions that are typically invisible to the naked eye, or at most visible under 10x magnification. In practical terms, VS2 offers a balance of value and invisibility to the average observer who might be wearing a hoodie and not a jeweler’s loupe around their neck.

### Round brilliant cut details
Round brilliant is the workhorse of diamonds for a reason. The cut is designed to maximize light return, so your pendant glitters like a small sun wearing too-tight sunglasses. The table width, depth percentage, crown height, and girdle smoothness all contribute to a performance that is basically a physics class on fire. A well cut round brilliant does not simply reflect light — it redirects it in such a way that the sparkle seems to follow your gaze, as if the diamond is winking at you from across a crowded room.

## Certification and the value behind the numbers
### Why GIA matters
GIA is widely considered the gold standard for diamond grading. A GIA certificate provides an impartial assessment of 4 Cs and the stone's other characteristics. When you are purchasing a pendant, the certificate assures you that the numbers match the stone you are buying, which adds to confidence in your investment.

### Reading a GIA report (non-technical)
A typical GIA report will list carat weight, color, clarity, cut, measurements, and any distinguishing features. For buyers, the report is a reference point for value and authenticity. It is not a warranty of resale value, but it gives you a consistent standard to compare with other options.
