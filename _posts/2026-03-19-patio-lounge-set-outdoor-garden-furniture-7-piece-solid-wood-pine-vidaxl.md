## Maintenance and care

Pine outdoor furniture wants to be your best friend, but it also needs a little love. Here are a few practical tips that actually work in real life, not just on the marketing brochure:

- Seal and re-seal: Start with a good outdoor sealant, and reapply according to climate conditions. A yearly refresh is not excessive, especially if you live in an area with harsh winters or heavy rainfall.
- Clean and dry: Wipe down with a mild soap solution, rinse, and dry thoroughly to prevent moisture from seeping into joints.
- Cushion care: If cushions are included, store them indoors when not in use or use a breathable storage solution to prevent mold and mildew. Dry cushions completely before returning them to the furniture.
- Sun exposure: Prolonged sun can fade finishes. Consider rotating the seating arrangement to distribute sunlight exposure evenly; this helps prevent one side from aging faster than the other.
- Scratch management: Minor scratches are part of life with wooden furniture. A quick touch-up with matching stain or a furniture wax can do wonders for keeping the set looking cohesive.

The key here is consistency. Pine wants consistency more than a diva wants a spotlight. If you give it a predictable care routine, it will look good for years and maybe even tell a few stories about your summers to the grandkids.
