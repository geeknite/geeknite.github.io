## Materials, Build Quality, and Care

### Materials

The Seyward runner uses a synthetic blend common to mid-range runners: polypropylene fibers with a touch of polyester for color brightness and durability. The backing includes a non-slip element that helps keep the rug in place on slick floors. This combination yields a rug that can take daily foot traffic, occasional spills, and the odd pet misadventure without turning into a heroic epic of fiber loss.

### Durability and Wear

In hallway tests, the rug endured weeks of constant foot traffic, multiple family members, and the occasional pet sprint. The low-pile construction resisted heavy wear and kept the trellis pattern readable after vacuum sessions. It’s not a heavy-duty kitchen rug, but for a hallway, it’s a sturdy, stylish option that won’t crumble under daily life.

### Cleaning and Maintenance

Spills should be blotted promptly to minimize staining. For routine cleaning, a standard vacuum suffices. For deeper cleaning, a mild detergent solution works well—test a small area first to confirm color stability. Avoid bleach or harsh solvents that could dull the fibers or alter the color. If you want to refresh the fibers without a full wash, a light steam or air-out can help restore a bit of bounce and brightness.

Care tips from the field:
- Vacuum regularly to keep dirt from dulling the trellis lines.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slippage and extend life.
- Avoid direct heat sources that could degrade fibers over time.
- Rotate the rug every few months to distribute wear evenly.
