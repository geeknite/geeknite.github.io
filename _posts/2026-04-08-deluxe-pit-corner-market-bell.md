### Setup quick-start

1) Lay out the market board and distribute the commodity cards face-down. 2) Give each player a starting set of coins. 3) Place the Campanello in the center. 4) Decide who goes first by a loud shout or by drawing the highest number on the market dice (if you insist on dice, though PIT usually excludes dice). 5) Start trading with a loud call of Sell Sell Sell and ring the Campanello when you feel the moment is right. 6) When a player completes a set of five identical commodities, a victory check happens and the joy ensues.

The deluxe components aren’t just for show. The weight of the metal coins adds an extra tactile thrill that makes you feel like a real trader on a 1920s wharf—without the risk of catching the gout from damp crates.

## How to play the Deluxe experience

PIT thrives on speed and chaos. The goal is to collect a complete set of five different commodities. In this Deluxe edition, you’ll race against the clock and your own schedule. The Campanello introduces a pause-break that lets players signal a call to trade, which shakes up the social dynamics around the table. If you love the adrenaline of real-time bidding but want to avoid the legal complications, this is your jam.

- Round structure: There’s no fixed turn order. Instead, players call out trade opportunities, shout their desired trades, and pass cards left or right. The bell signals the cadence of calls, but you can customize the bell to fit your group’s energy level.
- Trading logic: Any player can propose a trade by shouting YourTrade and offering specific commodities. If another player accepts, they exchange the cards; if not, the negotiation continues until someone accepts or the deck demands a reshuffle. The clock is always ticking, and your ability to read other players’ desperation becomes your most valuable skill.
- Victory condition: The first player to assemble one set of five different commodities wins. The Deluxe edition’s richer components reduce the chance that the game will devolve into a pure shouting match of who can scream the loudest. But the spirit remains intact: chaos, camaraderie, and the occasional bitter rivalry.

## Strategies and humor on the table

Like any good party game, Pit rewards social cunning and a little bit of deception. Here are some strategies to help you survive and possibly thrive in Corner The Market:

- Master the Bell: Use the Campanello to time your calls. A well-timed bell rung at the height of a trade frenzy can cause the table to pivot and pivot quickly, making it easier to secure your fifth commodity.
- Trust, or at least fake it: In Pit, trust is a currency as ephemeral as a good deal. Keep some trades close to your chest; pretend to be the slow and careful trader while secretly plotting your own five-set victory.
- The pace control: If you sense the table is about to collapse into chaos, call for a fast cadence by ringing the bell. It can help you steer the table back into a manageable tempo or push someone into a panic trade that you can capitalize on.
- When to ring vs when to hold: Sometimes the bell should be used to speed a trade. Other times, you hold the bell to lure other players into trading in a way that benefits you later on. Use your bell like a strategic tool, not a novelty hammer.
- House rules for maximum fun: If your group enjoys a longer evening, impose a house rule: if you succeed in building a five-set, you ring the Campanello and then perform a celebratory trade ritual. It could be a goofy victory dance or a competitor’s roast—whatever fits your group’s vibe.

## A quick note on balance and replayability

The Deluxe version improves the physical experience but does not magically solve balance issues inherent to Pit. The game is as much about social dynamics as it is about set collection. The increased tactile appeal of metal coins and high-quality cards adds to immersion, but it can also slow the pace slightly if the group is not careful with time management. If you are new to Pit, you might want a slower first game to acclimate to the tempo before unleashing the Campanello at full volume.

Replayability remains decent but highly dependent on player group. The chaos is the feature, not the bug. If you love unstructured social gaming with friends and you don’t mind occasional shouting matches, Pit Deluxe will bring out your inner commodities captain.

## Tips for hosting a Pit Deluxe night

- Clear a space big enough for the pit of chaos. The more players, the better the mayhem. But more players also mean more noise, so set expectations about volume.
- Appoint a dedicated bell handler. It’s easier to manage cadence when one player is responsible for ringing and signaling trades. This reduces the chaotic spiral and keeps the table from turning into a full-scale auction house.
- Establish a short ritual for early rounds: quick drinks, light snacks, and a loud pit pit pit chant that unites players and signals a bell ding of destiny.
- Use a timer to keep the game from dragging. The bell can only do so much; a gentle timer forces a pace that suits new players as well as veterans.
