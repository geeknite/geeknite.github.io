## New Hero: Venom
Venom as a hero is a departure from a straight-ahead damage-soak. The mechanic design leans into a balancing act: you want to stay in the sweet spot of threat removal while leaning into a few aggressive plays that feel like flipping a switch and yelling I AM VENOM. The hero card itself comes with unique stat-lines that favor multi-step combos and a willingness to accept a little risk for potential payoff.

### Venom’s Core Mechanic
The core mechanic centers on a set of Symbiote-linked cards that attach to your hero to unlock additional powers and new ways to interact with the encounter deck. This creates a play loop where you build momentum through tempo plays, then cash in momentum for big damage or board-control effects. It’s a design that rewards planning, but also deals out opportunities for dramatic swings when you miscalculate just a bit too boldly.

### Signature Cards and Playstyle
Venom’s signature cards poke at the edge of risk and reward. Some of them read like confident swagger—deciding whether you want to heal, draw, or smack a villain with an over-the-top attack. ## New Cards and Mechanics
This pack introduces new card types and keywords that expand your tactical toolbox without making every game feel like a clone of the last one. Expect a few new concepts that push you to think differently about how you manage resources and threats.
- Symbiote Attachments: These personal upgrades attach to Venom (and potentially allied heroes under the right conditions) to unlock temporary buffs and new interactions. They demand you to maintain pressure while protecting the attachment from removal by encounters.
- Host Upgrades: Located on the encounter side or quest-specific cards, these upgrades tilt the balance and encourage you to decide when to trigger a ‘host’ event for extra value.
- Aggressive Trump Cards: Venom’s kit includes several big-punch cards that only shine when you’ve met certain timing conditions. They reward good memory and careful sequencing more than pure luck.

From a design perspective, the symbiote system feels thematic and mechanically coherent. You’re not simply upgrading your hero for bigger numbers; you’re advocating for a micro-ecosystem on the table that grows, interacts, and occasionally betrays you in the most entertaining way possible. The new cards maintain the sense of hero-assembly that Marvel Champions is known for while injecting a fresh tempo that shakes up your usual rhythm.

## Deck-building and Strategy Notes
When you start building decks around Venom, you’ll want to consider how much you lean on the symbiote mechanics versus your core hero kit. Here are some practical tips that helped our group thrive (and occasionally explode in joyous failure):
- Tempo over brute force: Venom rewards you for hitting a turning point where you exploit momentum. Don’t waste a big payoff early unless you’re certain you can back it up with subsequent pressure.
- Multirole coordination: If you’re playing a four-player game, assign a primary board cleaner, a secondary threat responder, and a support deck for the rest. The symbiote layer can amplify even smaller decisions into meaningful outcomes if you chain them well.
- Resource pacing: Don’t hoard cards for a single “big turn.” Venom’s identity leans into continuing play rather than a one turn wipe. Draw when you can and spend when it hurts to wait another round.
- Edge-case planning: Some of Venom’s best moments come from reading the table and exploiting the right vulnerability window. Discuss timing with your team and practice your best “still sane after all this” plan in friendly games first.

A few deck ideas to try:
- Fast draw + tempo push: Combine Venom’s signature cards with allies or control cards that accelerate your draw count. The idea is to set up a small, consistent rhythm that culminates in a rewarding punch.
- Hybrid control: Mix scratch-damage cards with fear-of-missing-a-turn counters. You want to be able to clear threats while keeping pressure on the boss or main villain.
- Symbiote-forward aggression: Embrace the core mechanic fully—use attachments to unlock strong on-curve upgrades and then cash them in for a decisive finisher.

In practice, Venom is not a walk in the park—this is a hero pack that rewards experimentation. If you’re a player who enjoys small, well-timed swings that feel earned, Venom will likely become a favorite. If you prefer safe, predictable turns, you’ll still find some satisfying moments, just fewer of those jaw-dropping, table-flipping ones.
