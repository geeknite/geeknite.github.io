## Miniatures design and build quality

### Design language

The Orc Zombies are designed to feel like they belong to the same ecological niche as the Green Horde from the base game. They are tall, broad-shouldered silhouettes with a mix of crude armor and feral expressions. The sculpts lean into a comic brutalist fantasy: big teeth, gnarly tusks, and enough muscle to propose that these are the type of orcs that learned to survive on a steady diet of goblin jokes and sheer stubbornness.

### Build quality

Plastic quality is decent, not the heaviest or most detailed of the modern swing; however, it is robust for regular use. The knives and axes are cleanly cast, and you should not expect ancient artifact quality when you open a box. If you want to run a campaign where the Orcs survive through cunning as well as brute force, you will appreciate the variety in stances and weapon options. The boss Orc is a showpiece model that scales nicely with the larger monsters you might already own from other Zombicide sets, giving your table a sense of scale that feels like a proper battle montage rather than a toy box skirmish.

### Painting and customization

If you enjoy a good painting session, these Orcs offer a fun canvas. The greens range from swampy to neon in places, which makes for a pleasing grayscale approach when you want to highlight orcs with power-hungry intent. A few quick washes and dry brushing can yield dramatic results, and the variety of poses helps keep your painting board visually interesting. If you intend to run a weekend painting party, the Orcs are a solid centerpiece to keep your crew motivated.

## How it plays with the Green Horde core set

### Core mechanics alignment

The Green Horde line expands the Zombicide concept by adding fantasy fiends that require different strategies than your typical living dead. Orc Zombies contribute a few new mechanics such as higher armor in certain variants, aggressive movement patterns, and fear-inducing close combat. They also synergize with other Green Horde units by emphasizing area control, multi-target prioritization, and the classic door-to-face horror where you realize you forgot to bring a ranged weapon to a bludgeoning fight.

### Scenarios and mission design

If you enjoy the base campaigns, you will find that Orc Zombies shine in scenarios that emphasize choke points and wave management. Their presence increases the threat level on corridors and narrow rooms, forcing players to coordinate stacking and resource management. When you combine Orcs with the Green Horde ecosystem, you often get a sense that the entire board transforms from a map into a stage where the survivors must decide which box to check first: survive the current wave or position themselves for the next.

### The role of the Orc Boss

The boss Orc acts as a focal point for the encounter. It is not just a big health pool; it is a dynamic threat that can shift the tempo of a turn with unique actions. Expect the boss to push players into tougher decisions about whether to devote actions to blocking its advance or protecting a vulnerable ally. In practice, the boss adds a layer of dramatic tension that is often missing in standard zombie sets, providing a narrative arc to your skirmishes.

## Gameplay tips and tactics

### Early game priorities

- Identify the lines of approach and cluster Orcs into groups that threaten different zones of the board. Control the flow by using barriers and objective locations as nerves that you can sever if you split the Orcs into two smaller threats.
- Assign a focal survivor early on to draw the boss towards you while the rest of the team picks off standard Orcs. This lets you funnel risk and preserve your stronger heroes for the later waves.

### Mid game decisions

- When the boss Orc appears, consider using suppression or crowd-control measures if available in your hands. The tempo of a fight often hinges on whether you can neutralize the boss long enough to clear the surrounding squads.
- Make smart use of inventory items that increase range or provide extra actions. The Orcs push hard, and your survival strategy should hinge on hitting them with the right tool at the right time.

### End game considerations

- In higher difficulty games, Orc Zombies can create a last-minute scramble as they converge from multiple sides. Have a contingency plan for the final round where your survivors must execute a tight chain of actions to seal the door and escape the board before the final abomination chain collapses your defensive line.
