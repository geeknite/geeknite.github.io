## How to play: quick-start guide with a sample scenario

UNO’s basic rule set remains the backbone of UNO Elite NFL 2025, but the new NFL action cards require a quick refresher. Here’s a simple, practical guide to get you through a round without needing a doctorate in football stats.

1) Shuffle, deal, and set the pace: Each player is dealt seven cards. The top card of the draw pile is flipped to start the discard pile. The player to the left of the dealer goes first.
2) Color and team match: On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the top discard card. If you can’t play, you draw one card. If that drawn card can be played, you may immediately place it.
3) NFL actions come into play: If you play an NFL action card, resolve its effect immediately. Typical actions include ball-related twists like “Penalty” forcing players to skip or draw penalty cards, or “TD” that can affect turn order or cards in hand. The exact text depends on your edition, so read the card for specifics.
4) Getting to UNO: When you have one card left, shout “UNO!” If you fail to shout UNO and are caught before the next player’s turn, you may be forced to draw a penalty card (depends on rules you agree on as a group).
5) Win condition: The first player to discard all cards wins the round. Optional tournament rules can be added for longer sessions with scorekeeping across multiple rounds.

Sample scenario: You’re down to your last card, and the top card on the discard pile is a green number 5. You have a green 4, a blue 9, and an NFL-themed touchdown card. You play the green 4 to maintain color, you draw no penalty, you hold your breath—UNO code-door opens, you drop the TD card as your last move and yell, “TOUCHDOWN!” The table erupts, someone else tries a trick play, and the next thing you know you’re swapping memes and trading foil cards.
