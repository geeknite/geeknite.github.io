## Performance in the wild

### Real-world expectations

Let us set expectations with brutal sincerity: external SSDs are fast enough to feel magical, and also slow enough to remind you that you are not a supercomputer. The X6 offers speeds that are constrained by the USB-C interface, the internal controller, and the flash memory. In real-world sequencing, you can expect sustained reads in the 600-900 MB/s range on a modern USB-C port with a capable host. Writes tend to be a tad lower depending on the data pattern, often landing in a similar ballpark but sometimes dipping when you’re pushing random small files, which is precisely the kind of test that makes you question your life choices at 3 a.m.

In practical usage like backing up a photo archive from a laptop to this 500 GB drive, you’ll often experience the kind of transfer that feels like moving a high-speed snail. It won’t be the fastest thing you’ve ever connected, but it will be consistent, quiet, and, most importantly, reliable enough to get the job done without requiring a dedicated power supply, extra adapters, or a ritual to appease the SMB protocol gods.

### File transfers by category

- Large contiguous files (like RAW photo libraries or 4K video clips): expect strong sequential throughput, which is where external SSDs shine. You’ll see the big chunk moves occur with satisfaction, and you can watch the progress bar move with the precision of a metronome.
- Mixed small files (documents, configuration files, and game saves): the drive will still perform respectably, but you might notice a little more micro-delay as the controller maps blocks for countless tiny writes. It won’t be painful, just a reminder that nothing beats a modern NVMe drive for grunt tasks, even if it lives in a more travel-friendly form factor.
- Random I/O: this is where the true test happens. Expect reasonable performance for on-the-go editing, portable game saves, and the random backup of the day. It won’t be a production-grade editing station, but it will be enough to keep your workflow intact while you chase sunshine and deadlines in equal measures.
