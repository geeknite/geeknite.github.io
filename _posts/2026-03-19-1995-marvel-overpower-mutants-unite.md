## Condition and Preservation
### What sealed means in a practical sense
Brand-new sealed means you’re not pulling a random stack out of a clearance bin; you’re likely holding a near-pristine artifact. The outer packaging should be crisp, corners sharp, and shrink wrap intact. Internally, you expect the contents to be untouched and the card stock to be uncracked and uncreased. The reality is a bit more nuanced: conditions vary with storage. Humidity, temperature fluctuations, and sunlight can age both the wrap and the box in ways that don’t threaten playability but do affect resale value and display appeal. Inspect for seal integrity, any signs of tampering, or label changes that scream rewrap. If you plan to crack the seal for a vintage session, you’re trading mint pristine status for playable joy. If you’re keeping it sealed, you’re preserving the moment when a marketable moment could still be captured on glossy cardboard.

### Storage best practices
To maximize value and enjoyment, store sealed decks in a dry, cool environment away from direct sunlight. A display case with UV-protected glass is perfect for showpieces while a climate-controlled closet or cabinet helps keep the shrink wrap from brittling. If you display, consider a stand or case insert that prevents pressure on the corners. If you crack the seal for a retro game night, sleeves for individual cards become essential to maintain the artwork’s integrity. The key is to balance display, protection, and the simple joy of handling a piece of 1995 Marvel nostalgia.
