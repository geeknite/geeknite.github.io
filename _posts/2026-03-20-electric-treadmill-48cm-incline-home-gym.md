## Buying Guide: What to Look For in an Incline Treadmill

- Belt width and deck length: ensure your stride length fits the belt; 48 cm width is comfortable for most runners, but check that the deck length suits your gait.
- Incline range and ramp stability: higher incline support is nice, but stability under incline is crucial.
- Motor power and duty cycle: look for a motor that can sustain your pace for longer sessions without overheating.
- Cushioning and deck thump: a good balance between comfort and performance helps protect joints during high-impact workouts.
- Console complexity: choose a display and app ecosystem that you’ll actually use and enjoy.
- Warranty and service options: a solid warranty reduces long-term risk when you’re investing in home fitness.

## Care Tips to Extend Lifespan

- Clean the belt and deck after workouts to remove sweat and debris.
- Check the belt alignment and tension periodically to prevent drift and wear.
- Lubricate the deck as per manufacturer’s guidance to minimize friction.
- Inspect power cords and safety keys for wear; replace damaged parts promptly.
- Store in a dry environment to prevent corrosion and moisture-related damage.
