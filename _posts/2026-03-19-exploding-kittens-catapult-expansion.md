## How the catapult changes the game rules and tempo

The Catapult Expansion does not rewrite Exploding Kittens as a whole; it adds a few knobs and a new toy that changes how players interact with the deck. here are the core mechanics you will likely encounter on your first game night with catapult:

### new action cards and the core idea
- Catapult card: the centerpiece. it lets you launch a specific card from your hand into the center or at another player, triggering its effect as if you had played it on your own turn. the result can be a friendly miracle or a chaotic misfire, depending on what you hit.
- Catapult Target cards: some cards in the deck gain a more dramatic fate when launched by the catapult. they resolve with a splashy effect that can alter momentum in a hurry.
- Catapult tokens: these little tokens are spent to power shots or to avert doom once in a while. collecting a few tokens can shift late game odds in surprising ways.

The launcher itself does not add a new core resource system. you are not juggling ammo or fuel, but you are juggling opportunities. every time you press the lever, you get a small moment of suspense: will the launched card hit the intended target or bounce around the room like a cat who has found the laser pointer? the core game loop remains draw, resolve, and occasionally blue murder chaos. catapult simply adds a dramatic punctuation mark.

### table dynamics and player interaction
one of the most noticeable shifts is the increased tempo. a catapult shot can turn a quiet turn into a mini wildfire, because the card you threw into the middle might resolve with a new ability that affects several players. this makes your opponents nervous and your table louder, which is exactly what party games are for. it also smooths over some early round stalemates by injecting a random but timely disruption that gives a new path to victory.

The interaction with the nope mechanic remains intact but with a twist. you may use a catapult shot to trigger a card that would otherwise be blocked by a nope, and sometimes the catapult can bypass a shield and force a surprise effect through. think of it as a playful poke that tests whether your friends are paying attention or just waiting for the next exploding kitten to appear.

### pace and game length considerations
catapult tends to speed up some rounds and slow others, depending on how aggressively people use the launcher. if you have a table full of quick thinkers, expect a brisk game with several dramatic comebacks. if your group enjoys long, drawn out planning sessions, you might see a few rounds stretch as players weigh the risk of a catapult shot versus the reward of a clean hand. either way, the expansion deliberately nudges the pacing toward chaotic fun rather than precise outcome.

### potential pitfalls and how to avoid them
- setup friction: the catapult pieces can be fiddly, especially for new players. a quick practice shot or a pre-turn demonstration helps a lot.
- token management: if players start misplacing tokens, you lose track of shots. designate a small tray or bowl for tokens and call it the catapult stockpile.
- rule creep: with more cards and more effects, it is easy to forget a rule or misinterpret a combination. keep a one page quick reference handy and consider a rules readthrough before the first real round.
- table space: the launcher and tokens require a bit more room. if you play in a tight space, push the catapult to the center and allow everyone a bit more breathing room for dramatic reveals.

Despite these caveats, the vibe remains light and goofy. the Catapult Expansion leans into the silliness and does it well enough to justify the extra chaos for a party night or a friendly game night with friends who enjoy a loud chuckle more than strict balance.
