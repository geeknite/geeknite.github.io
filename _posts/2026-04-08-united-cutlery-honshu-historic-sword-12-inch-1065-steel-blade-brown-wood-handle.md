## Care, Maintenance, and Display Tips
1065 carbon steel isn’t stainless. That means you’ll want to keep a light oil coating on the blade if you’re storing it for long periods or if humidity spikes in your display area. A wipe with a soft cloth and a quick coat of mineral oil (food-grade works fine) can prevent corrosion and keep the finish looking like new. If you’re planning cosplay photos, a gentle wipe with a microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints before you shoot will help maintain the metallic shine that looks so good on camera.

Display care also means thinking about light exposure. Direct sunlight can fade wooden handles and encourage subtle finish changes over months, which isn’t a disaster but it does affect the look you’ll see in photos. A stable shelf, a display case, or a mount away from harsh sun can keep your Honshu sword looking museum-ready for as long as you want to show it off.

Cleaning tips: never immerse the blade in water. Use a dry cloth for quick cleanups and a light application of oil every few months (or when you notice a dull haze). For the wood handle, avoid soaking with water and keep it dry. If the wood grain starts to feel dry, a tiny amount of wood-safe conditioner can help maintain the log-cabin charm of the grip without altering color dramatically.

If you’re into cosplay, consider pairing the sword with a display stand that puts the blade at a heroic angle and a sheath that’s easy to access. For photos, a simple backdrop, a touch of side-lighting, and a clean desk can turn this compact sword into an eye-catching hero piece in seconds.
