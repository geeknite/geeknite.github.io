## Ergonomics and Team Dynamics
Five seats, five postures, one shared surface. The ergonomic intent is to give each participant independent control over chair height, desk height, and monitor position while still enforcing a sense of unity around the central display. We tested with a mix of tall programmers, short designers, and a nocturnal sysadmin who tends to overcompensate with a dramatic keyboard tilt. In all scenarios, the adjusts and the attention to ergonomic detail helped reduce the usual sprint-day neck strain. The secret sauce is the modular monitor arms and the ability to tune each seat’s screen height individually while keeping the group around the W Screen transfixing at the center.

### Seating and work zones
The five workstations are arranged to encourage collaboration without triggering the dreaded “silo syndrome.” The outer seats feel expansive but intimate enough to share a document on the screen without yelling across the room. The middle seat naturally becomes the facilitator zone during demos, with a clear path to the screen and a comfortable vantage point for the entire team. If you’re worried about the “three-seconds-to-decide-who-talks-next” dance, the W Screen offers a shared control mechanism that streamlines turn-taking and reduces the noise level that usually accompanies a group discussion.

### Acoustic considerations
Acoustic panels help dampen ambient noise, but this is not a soundproof fort. Expect some keyboard symphonies, printer chirps, and the occasional exasperated sigh when a build fails. For teams that require near-silent operation, add a set of noise-canceling headphones or reserve a quiet room for deep work. The design acknowledges the reality of collaboration in open-plan spaces while aiming to keep the noise from becoming the main feature of your office life.

The bench rewards basic care. Wipe down the surface with a microfiber cloth; avoid aggressive cleaners that could dull the matte finish. Durability-wise, this is built to withstand a reasonable amount of daily wear.
