## Overview

The Wulfsburg expansion lives in your box like a moody companion who insists on howling at the moon while you paint your minis. It introduces a wolves-and-witch-hunt vibe into the Black Plague setting; instead of just swamping your survivors with standard zombies, you’ll contend with new wolfish threats, cunning quest chains, and a handful of map tiles that turn every mission into a scavenger hunt for contraband bone-sicles. Yes, the metaphor gets gruesome. No, we do not apologize.

What exactly do you get in the Additional Rules & Quests booklet? Glad you asked. It is not merely a rehash of old content repackaged with a different font. The booklet contains:

- A set of expanded rules clarifications that address questions players didn’t know they had until they played their third round and realized the wolves were correcting their math.
- A collection of new quests that scale with players and with tempo. Some quests require stealth and cunning; others reward aggressive action and improvisation. The best quests remind you that you’re not the hero you hoped you’d be; you’re the hero you manage to keep alive long enough to see the credits roll.
- Three new map tiles with wolf-cenotes of doom. They alter line-of-sight, increase the risk of piggybacking on a trap, and give your survivors a reason to argue about route planning that would make a team of engineers weep with joy or despair.
- New threat cards that feature wolves, werewolves, and the occasional cryptic event that says things like, the moon is bright, the bite is worse, and the dice are merciless.

But we won’t pretend the booklet is all mood and no muscle. The real magic lies in how the new rules interact with existing mechanics. Zombicide is famous for teaching you how a poorly placed block of terrain can decide the fate of the city; Wulfsburg takes this to a new level by forcing you to consider time of day, noise, and whether your home-brew strategy can cope with the arrival of a particularly chatty werewolf. More on that in the Strategy section.

## How the Quests Change Your Table Experience

Quests are the star of the booklet. They are designed to be woven into your existing Black Plague campaigns or used as standalone one-offs with a slightly longer playtime. If you’re the sort who likes to improvise, the quests still reward creative thinking. Either way, the booklet gives you robust tools to craft a memorable encounter, and the increased threat density from the new tiles keeps you honest about your escape routes. It’s all about balancing tension and relief—moments of quiet planning followed by chaotic culmination as wolves close in from multiple angles.

## Scenarios: Quick-start Setup and Playthrough Tips

For those who want to dive into a session without spending four hours decoding a new rulebook, here are a few quick-start tips derived from the booklet’s guidance:

- Pre-plan your path through the new tiles: The wolves prefer to lurk along corridors where you might be forced to split up. Before the first turn, map out several potential routes and identify which survivors will be best suited to hold the line in choke points. You don’t have to be perfect; you just have to pretend you are.
- Use the new threat tokens to your advantage: Threat tokens are a great way to simulate a lurking danger you cannot fully see. Place them with intent, not at random, and you’ll experience a sense of rising dread that is the lifeblood of Zombicide campaigns.
- Put a couple of “priority targets” on the table: The quests often revolve around neutralizing specific wolf threats before they escalate. Designate one or two wolves as priority targets and coordinate your squad’s actions around those targets. The table will feel like a small command center, and you’ll thank your past self for the good planning.

Playthrough notes from my own sessions with the Wulfsburg booklet confirm that the added complexity pays off in a bigger sense: you’re performing heist-like coordinate plays with the thrill of a survival horror show. Some rounds are brutal; others are unexpectedly heroic. The key is to pace yourself, drink water, and let your survivors tell their own stories as the wolves whisper in the background.

## Strategy and Tactics: How to Win at Wolf-Hunting

Here are a handful of practical tips gleaned from experiences with the booklet’s content:

- Scout ahead, but don’t forget to commit: The new quests reward careful planning but also punish hesitation. If you hesitate too long, the wolves will herd your survivors into a corner and you’ll have to fight your way out with a plan that feels improvised and risky.
- Group movement matters: Wolf threats often appear in places that encourage group alignment. Keep your team together when you can and spread out only when you must. The new tiles make the spacing decisions critical in the early rounds; misjudging distances can lead to a cascade of lethal consequences.
- Target priority matters: Focus fire on the most dangerous wolf threats first, but do not ignore the smaller threats that can trap you or interrupt your path. The quest design rewards a measured approach rather than martyrdom or spraying bullets everywhere.
- Use terrain to your advantage: The new map tiles aren’t just pretty; they are tactical tools. Use chokepoints, line-of-sight blocks, and terrain features to minimize exposure to wolf packs while maximizing your survivors’ ability to recover quickly after a brutal turn.
- Don’t forget morale mechanics: The game often rewards group cohesion and swift, decisive actions rather than heroically bold solo runs. The quests encourage a collaborative vibe, which suits teams that enjoy roleplay and character-driven decisions as much as mechanical optimization.

A final word on strategy: Wulfsburg doesn’t turn Zombicide into a different game. It enhances the base mechanics with a more layered risk-reward structure and a narrative throughline that makes your campaign feel meaningful. If your table has ever asked, What would we do if the wolves attacked from three sides at once while a moonlit wind rattled the boards? Wulfsburg gives you that moment, repeatedly, with a grin and a howl. It’s not a perfect expansion, but it’s a very good one, and it has enough bite to satisfy veteran players while remaining approachable for new faces.
