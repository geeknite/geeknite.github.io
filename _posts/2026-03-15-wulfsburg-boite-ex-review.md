## How the game plays: core loops and boxes within boxes
In Boîte Fair EX, you will likely undertake multiple passes around the table: setup, first round, mid-game crunch, and the finale where you realize the box is heavier than your post-lunch nap. The game uses a combination of worker-placement, set collection, and tactical combat that requires you to juggle short-term gains with long-term plans. The interplay between planning and reaction is where Wulfsburg earns its stripes and also its headaches—because in a heavy CMON game, the tension is not whether you can win, but whether you can do so with your dignity intact.

### Core mechanics: a quick map
- Worker-placement core: Each player sends their workers to claim resources or activate districts on the map. The order of placement matters, which means you will fight for early initiative and then regret it when your neighbors start the chain reactions you ignored.
- Combat dice: The dice outcomes determine how fiercely you can press your advantage. The dice are not dastardly unfair, but they are not your best friend either. You’ll learn to live with the unpredictability as a form of narrative spice.
- Deck-building influences: Action cards alter the rhythm of the game, offering powerful combos at the cost of messing with your timing. It’s a reminder that you cannot predict every turn; you can only prepare for contingencies with style.
- Campaign and XP: The EX edition plants flags for your progression across scenarios, letting you track your heroes and their increasingly ridiculous decisions. This brings a sense of continuity that makes revisiting Wulfsburg feel like checking in with a favorite, mildly chaotic family member.
