## Painting and Assembly Tips
- Start with a clean base: a thin primer helps the paint cling and also reveals any molding lines that need a touch-up.
- Skin tones: the Batman Who Laughs has a pallid green skin tone in many depictions. Use a base of a desaturated green or lime with a touch of blue and gray to mute the brightness.
- Hair: white hair on a green-skinned figure can look dramatic but also unnatural if not handled carefully. Consider warming the whites with an off-white and a glaze of very light gray to highlight.
- Coat: for the trench coat, pick a mid-dark tone (navy blue or charcoal). Add dry-brushed highlights along the edges and emphasize the creases with a slightly lighter shade to bring out the folds without losing the sculpture’s depth.
- Accessories: the little buckles and pouches deserve attention. A wash of brown or black toner will push the details to pop, then a dry-brush of metallic or light tan will reveal the edges.
- Base: keep it consistent with your board aesthetic. If you intend to show the model on a destroyed rooftop, pick a slate gray or ash tone and add a splash of debris or tiny vegetation for a narrative touch.
- Tools and glues: use a fine-tipped brush for tiny details, and consider a pin-vise for delicate joints. Superglue or resin-safe glue works well, but take your time. The trick is to avoid “supergluin’ it” on the moment; you want the miniature to be a joy to handle.
