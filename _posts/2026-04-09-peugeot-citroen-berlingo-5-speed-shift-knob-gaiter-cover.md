## Compatibility and fitment specifics
Berlingo models span generations, and one size rarely fits all. Before you click buy, run these quick checks:
- Measure the current knob base diameter. If you can’t measure easily, check the kit instructions for adapter rings and internal thread sizing.
- Confirm the thread pitch. A lot of Peugeot/Citroen knobs use metric threads; bring the old knob to a shop or compare with the adapter set if you’re unsure.
- Look for an adapter set. If you’re online, a kit with multiple adapters reduces the risk of discovering your chosen knob won’t fit after it arrives.
- Gauge the gaiter opening. Some gaiters clamp at the top, others slide on. A well-fitting gaiter reduces fraying and keeps the interior clean.

If you’re shopping in person, bring the old knob and a measuring tape; if you’re shopping online, read the Q&A and customer photos to ensure you’re buying for a Berlingo, not a different Peugeot-Citroen model.

## Installation guide: how to swap the knob and gaiter on a Berlingo 5 speed
This is the moment of truth, the part where you become the nerdy hero your car deserves. You don’t need a full toolkit, but a couple of basics help:
- Small flathead screwdriver or trim removal tool
- Small hex key or Allen key (size depends on grub screws)
- Clean cloth and mild cleaner
- Optional thread locker for the new knob (blue is best for non-permanent mods)

Follow these steps for a clean swap:

### Preparation
1. Park the Berlingo on a level surface and engage the parking brake. You want to avoid the van rolling while you lean in for a gear change of a different kind.
2. If your current gaiter has a trim ring or boot clamp, gently pry it loose with a small flathead tool. Be careful not to scratch the paint or plastic panels.
3. If the knob is screwed on, you will feel it loosen as you turn counterclockwise. If it’s press-fit, carefully pull straight up to disengage the knob from the shift lever.

### Removal of old parts
4. With the knob removed, assess how the gaiter is attached. Some kits require you to slide the gaiter up and off the lever, while others use a retaining ring. Follow the kit instructions for removal. If there’s resistance, apply a little heat from a hair dryer to relax any adhesive—careful not to burn the leather.
5. Wipe down the exposed metal with the cloth to remove oil and sweat residue. A clean surface helps the new parts seat properly.

### Installation of new parts
6. Slip the new gaiter onto the shift lever, securing any rings or clamps as required. If you have a top ring, slide it into place and tighten gradually to avoid misalignment.
7. Test the fit by moving the lever through all gears with the bonnet open (lightly, not driving). You want to ensure there’s no rubbing or binding.
8. Install the new knob. If your kit uses a grub screw, line up the knob and tighten the screw firmly but not to the point of stripping the thread.
9. If you used thread locker, wait a few minutes for it to set before driving. If your kit uses a screw-on fit, give the knob a little twist and check for any play. If there’s movement, you may need a different adapter or a tighter fit.

### Testing and adjustments
10. Take a short test drive around the block. Shift through all gears multiple times. Listen for squeaks or binding. Adjust as needed.
11. Check the gaiter for any signs of wear after the first 50 miles. If the gaiter edge is lifting, re-seat and tighten the top ring.

Installation isn’t rocket science, but patience helps. The goal is a snug, silent upgrade that feels deliberate every time you reach for the shifter.

## Maintenance tips and care
A good upgrade should be low maintenance. Here are a few tips to keep your knob and gaiter looking sharp for years:
- Clean regularly with a mild cleaner appropriate for the material (leather, faux leather, silicone coatings). Avoid harsh solvents that can crack leather or degrade fabric.
- Check screws and clamps every few months. Vibrations during long drives can loosen fittings over time.
- Keep the gaiter clean and dry. Wet fabric can absorb odors and deform; quick wipe-down after rain helps maintain its shape.
- If you notice cracking or significant wear, consider replacing the gaiter with a more durable option. A sagging edge is not just a vibe killer; it can snag on clothing and hands.
