#### Care and Maintenance

Cotton is forgiving compared to silk or wool, but you still have to treat it well. Routine care includes:

- Vacuuming with a gentle setting to remove dust and particles that can abrade the surface.
- Spot-cleaning with a mild detergent solution for spills. Blot, don’t rub, to avoid color transfer or fiber damage.
- For deeper cleaning, hand wash or machine wash on delicate with cold water if the rug is small enough to fit. Check the care label—if there isn’t one, err on the side of caution or seek professional cleaning.
- Air-dry flat to avoid distortion; avoid direct sun for long periods to prevent color fading.

Edge curling is common in flat-woven rugs as they settle in. A bit of heat with a steam iron, or simply pressing with a clean towel and heavy books can flatten the edges over a couple of days.
