## Quick Rules Primer Across the Three Games

To save you from rescue-m Google searches during a candid family moment, here’s a crisp, Geeknite-style refresher for the trio. If you want the full official rules, the Mattel pages linked at the bottom will be your friend, but this quick guide should keep you competitive at your next game night.

### UNO O'NO 99: The Setup and Play

- Objective: Be the first to get rid of all your cards, but with a chaotic twist that can flip the table (in the most affectionate way).
- Setup: Shuffle the UNO O'NO 99 deck and deal a standard vanilla amount to each player. Keep the rest as a draw pile.
- Play: Players take turns matching color or number; the O'NO variant occasionally inserts wild cards or a twist card that changes the rules mid-game. Expect laughter, confusion, and a dramatic stare-down when someone plays the perfect “Nope” card at precisely the wrong moment.

### Skip-Bo: The Build-and-Shed Shuffle

- Objective: Be the first to play all of your stock pile cards in numerical order.
- Setup: Each player gets a stock pile and a hand of five cards? The exact numbers aren’t a tragedy; they’re a blueprint for chaos. You’ll quickly learn why players say “Just one more round.”
- Play: On your turn, draw until you have five, then play from either your stock pile or discard piles into the central construction piles. The strategic joy is in watching your opponents’ stacks grow while your own lead stack stays stubbornly low.

### The Third Wheel: Quick-Light Rules

- Objective: Complete a quick path to victory using the third game’s unique twist cards and layout.
- Setup: As described in the included insert, a few cards, a couple of piles, and a lot of opportunity for chaotic camaraderie.
- Play: A light, fast mechanism that ensures your group can squeeze in a couple of rounds before someone yells, “Pizza is here!” and the table goes to a psych-fest over toppings.
