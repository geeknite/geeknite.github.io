## Introduction
The Huntsmen Box for Zombicide Black Plague lands on the table with the confidence of a medieval blacksmith and the chaos of a tavern brawl. It is both an expansion and a box of new excuses to pretend you are a heroic hunter while you cry about the survivors who just wanted to loot a bakery.

## What's in the Huntsmen Box
### William the Huntsman
William the Huntsman is the breakout star of this box. He struts onto the board with a cloak that screams I read maps for fun and a weapon that looks like it swallowed a few trees. In gameplay terms, William brings a unique ability that lets you reroll a failed combat check once per round, which is as close as Zombicide gets to a reliable cheat code without breaking the vibe. He is the sort of survivor who looks at a horde and says, I have a plan, and plans typically involve swinging a big axe until the crowd goes away or until the tabletop complains.

### Huntsmen enemies and new threats
The box introduces a cadre of Huntsmen enemies who prefer the long game—ambushes, traps, and the occasional dramatic reveal from behind a stack of crates. They feel like the kind of undead that remembers your name, and then uses it while lurching menacingly toward the nearest fortification. They add rhythm to the game by changing when and where you need to pull the lever to win the round. 

### New equipment and cards
Expect a handful of new equipment cards, including ranged options and utility items that emphasize Williams hunting theme. There are traps and purifiers and possibly something that resembles a hunting horn that either intimidates zombies or makes the players laugh in relief when it actually does nothing. The kit also includes new encounter and objective cards to push your campaigns forward and to remind you that in Zombicide, the bakery is forever closed and the only thing left to loot is experience points.

### Components and box quality
Zombicide minis are usually crisp and full of personality, and the Huntsmen Box is no exception. William carries himself with enough character to distract you from a misbeat on a dice roll, and the other Huntsmen zombies are sculpted with enough personality to justify the extra plastic on the table. The Huntsmen zombies feature hooded cloaks that give them a distinct silhouette; this is useful for players who occasionally confuse the undead with fashion-conscious knights.

### A quick look at the cards and tiles
In addition to new survivors and enemies, the Huntsmen Box injects fresh manipulation into the game's economy. New equipment cards expand your inventory and let you tailor Williams hunting approach. The new terrain tiles open up more choke points and alternate paths, which means your team can herd the undead in new directions and watch as the clumsy AI fails to herd itself back into a straight line.

### A quick playthrough vignette
Imagine a corridor cluttered with crates and a lone door that seems uncrackable. William steps forward, his cloak sweeping the dust as he gestures for the group to brace for impact. The first pair of Huntsmen lurk in the shadows, their silhouettes visible only when your lamp catches their ominous kneecaps. You roll the dice, and in the best of times you fail, only to see William trigger his reroll. The critical success lands, the door opens, and the horde staggers into a trap you had set earlier with a mix of cunning and reckless optimism. The room erupts in chaotic but triumphant laughter as you collect experience points and awkwardly realize you forgot to loot the bakery once again. This is what Zombicide does best: it makes you feel like a tactical genius even when your dice are conspiring against you.

## Gameplay depth and balance (continued)
The extra components turn a sandbox campaign into a structured hunt. You will notice that the Huntsmen Box rewards players who coordinate. The traps and new terrain encourage planning, but you are never punished for experimentation. In a world where most expansions lean into raw power, Huntsmen chooses to refine the tactical toolbox: it rewards precise positioning, careful sequencing of fights, and the occasional bold gambit that reminds you why you fell in love with co-op games in the first place.

### Pros and cons at a glance
- Pros: William the Huntsman is a flavorful, useful survivor; new enemies spice up the pace; terrain and cards add meaningful choices; excellent for players who enjoy tactical planning and narrative moments.
- Cons: If your group wants quick, straight-to-the-action sessions, the additional complexity can feel like a speed bump; a few players may find the Huntsmen horde a touch relentless if you are not careful with the setup.

## Who should buy this expansion
- Players who already own Zombicide Black Plague core and want a fresh challenge.
- Groups that enjoy longer campaigns and more tactical planning.
- Hobbyists who love painting and assembling miniatures with a hunter theme.
- Teams that enjoy banter, camaraderie, and a little melodrama as the undead shuffle closer to the door.

If you are a casual gamer who prefers quick rounds and light breezes of luck, this is likely to be a “nice to have” rather than a must-have. The box adds value for those who relish a rich narrative experience and a more tactical, slower-burn game. It is a thoughtful expansion that respects the core mechanics while offering a fresh momentum to break up your usual play rhythm.

## Conclusion and rating
After spending several sessions with the Huntsmen Box, I can confidently say this is one of the better expansions to drop into Zombicide Black Plague. It does not reinvent the wheel but it injects a new flavor into the existing system. William the Huntsman feels like a character you want in your party and the Huntsmen antagonists deliver a meaningful challenge that multiplies tension without tipping into unfairness. The components are solid, the art direction is cohesive, and the gameplay loops deliver memorable moments that will give you something to talk about at the table for weeks.

Rating: 4.5 out of 5
