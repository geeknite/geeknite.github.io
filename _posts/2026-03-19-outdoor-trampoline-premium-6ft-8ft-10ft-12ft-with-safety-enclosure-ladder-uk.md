## Safety Considerations and Warnings
### Weight limits and user guidelines
Each size has a recommended weight limit that, when exceeded, can reduce bounce quality and potentially lead to frame fatigue. It is essential to adhere to these guidelines and monitor for signs of strain. For families with multiple users, rotating use and ensuring one person is on the bouncy surface at a time helps keep the unit in good shape.

### Ground preparation and anchoring
The ground under the trampoline should be level and free of sharp objects. Our UK version includes ground anchors designed to resist 40-60 mph gusts in extreme weather if properly installed. We strongly recommend anchoring in regions with high wind variability and using the included safety pads to cover the frame edges to prevent snagging clothing or hair during intense bounce sessions.

### Supervision and child safety
No trampoline is a substitute for adult supervision or safety gear. Always supervise children, avoid flips or somersaults until thoroughly trained, and keep pets and loose objects off the mat.
