## What is Netys PR RT 3300VA?
The Netys PR RT is Socomec's line for single-phase online UPS systems offering 3.3 kVA of apparent power. In practical terms, you get clean regulated 230 VAC power with automatic voltage regulation and precision at a price point that is less punishing than a midlife crisis. The PR RT designation signals two key features: modularity and form factor flexibility. This device can be installed as a tower or mounted in a 19 inch rack, and the hot-swappable battery modules give you runtime without requiring a forklift and a power user manual. It's the kind of product that makes desk owners feel like grown ups.

## Tower vs Rack: Installation and Cable Management
One of the strongest selling points of the Netys PR RT is its dual form factor. You can place it on the floor like a compact server, or you can slide it into a 19 inch rack with rail kits that fit like a glove. Hanging it in a rack improves air flow and cabling discipline; using it as a tower is simpler for a home lab where wall sockets are a myth and cable management is a mythic beast. The included mounting ears and screws are straightforward to fit, and the front panel LCD is legible from a distance, so you dont have to bring a magnifying glass to the data room.

## Battery and Runtime
Runtime numbers are the perennial party poopers at every UPS review. The Netys PR RT ships with a battery pack that can be swapped without powering down the load, and you can expand runtime by adding more battery modules. With a typical 3.0 kW load, expect roughly 8-15 minutes of runtime with the base battery, more with larger bank configurations. For longer grace periods during power outages, attach additional battery modules that slot into the back of the chassis; they slide in like Lego bricks and can be exchanged without a service call. In practice, that means you can plan graceful shutdowns for a small server, a home media PC, and a couple of network devices during a blackout.

## Power Management and Monitoring
The Netys PR RT ships with basic local management via the front LCD and a USB interface for direct connections to a PC. If you want to keep track of loads, run-time, and battery health from a central console, you can add a network management card for SNMP or other protocols. The beauty here is that you can monitor multiple units in a campus or office without spreadsheet gloom. A few clicks in the management software and you can schedule tests, check battery replacement dates, and generate runtime reports. Whether you are a sysadmin or a lab tinkerer, the Netys PR RT gives you a comfortable headroom.
