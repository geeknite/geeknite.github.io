## Ergonomics and comfort for six

Six people seated side by side, with a shared screen now and then, creates a unique ergonomic challenge. The design aims to keep keyboards and screens within a comfortable reach for each user, but you’ll want to verify several factors:

- Monitor height and alignment: The integrated screen system is a selling point, but ensure the screen height remains within a comfortable range for center seating. If it’s too high or too far forward, you’ll end up with neck strain that makes you dream of a desk with a hammock instead.
- Keyboard trays and negative space: With six work zones, each user requires room for a keyboard, mouse, notepad, and those indispensable sticky note bombs that proliferate during planning sessions. A good arrangement keeps wrists in neutral positions and reduces shoulder fatigue from swiveled chairs.
- Seat geometry and chair coordination: The desk is not a reason to skip ergonomic chairs. Pair the Eternal with chairs that offer adequate lumbar support and adjustable armrests. In a multi-user setup, you’ll want a seat schedule so every person has a chair that matches their body metrics; this is a surprisingly underrated optimization for long days of brainstorming and spreadsheet spelunking.

### Cable management and the dreaded spaghetti monster

Cable management is the lifeblood of any large workstation. This model is designed with a central spine and routed channels that should help minimize cable chaos. You’ll still want to implement vertical cable organizers and floor raceways if the room has to be reconfigured frequently. A six-person setup means more power strips, more USB hubs, and more risk of tripping over a stray HDMI cable when someone forgets to route an extension under the desk.

If you’re a neat freak, consider a cable-on-wall solution or under-desk cable trays to keep the rows of ports tidy. The integrated screen should have a clean avatar on the hub, but you’ll appreciate extra cable management accessories if you’re running multiple monitors on each side of the spine.

## Maintenance and care

The oak-white veneer is relatively forgiving, but some care steps help keep the surface pristine:
- Clean with a soft microfiber cloth and a mild wood-safe cleaner. Avoid harsh abrasives that could dull the finish.
- Wipe up spills promptly to prevent staining and warping. A quick leather-care approach for wood is not out of the question, provided you don’t get too fancy with polishes that leave a film.
- Use coasters and desk mats to protect the surface from hot cups and scratched keyboards.
- Check the frame bolts periodically; a short torque check every few months helps keep everything stable and groan-free.

If you’re the type who treats the desk as a living ecosystem, you’ll appreciate that the surface cleans up easily and that the modular spine can handle expansions of workspace rather than replacements.
