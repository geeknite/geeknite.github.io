## Ergonomics: Creating a Comfortable Workflow

### Desk Height and Monitor Positioning
Ergonomics matters more than the latest RGB keyboard. The GOF 3P is designed with adjustable legs or a standardized frame height to accommodate typical desk heights (roughly 28 to 30 inches from floor to desktop). The exact height can vary by model, but you should be able to position your monitor at eye level, with the top third of the screen roughly at your eye line. If your setup includes a dual-monitor array, you’ll want a slight tilt and a vertical align to minimize neck strain. A monitor arm or a simple adjustable stand can make a world of difference; just ensure the arm clamps securely to the desktop surface without marring the veneer.

### Keyboard and Mouse: The Fine Print
The desk top typically supports a full-sized keyboard with room to spare for a numeric keypad or a compact gaming keypad if that’s your jam. A keyboard tray, if included, can simplify posture by keeping wrists neutrally aligned. If there’s no tray, consider a low-profile keyboard platform. It’s not about vanity; it’s about reducing the risk of repetitive strain injuries. A practical trick is to keep the mouse at the same horizontal plane as the keyboard and to ensure there is at least a few inches of space to move your forearm freely without bumping knees or chair arms.

### Cable Management: The Real Hero
One of the hidden joys of a good cubicle system is its cable management pass-throughs. Look for grommets or cable routes that prevent the floor from turning into a spaghetti nest. A modular system should offer channels, clips, and ties to route power cables, data lines, and the occasional USB-C to USB-C chain. If your space hosts multiple desktops or laptops, you’ll appreciate a friendly route plan that reduces desk clutter and the frantic cable-fishing later at 6 PM when you’re trying to leave the office.

### Under-Desk Storage: Space Optimization
Under-desk storage options—whether in the form of modest credenzas, small file cabinets, or shelf modules—are a major win for a 3P setup. The GOF 3P often supports multiple under-desk units that align with the cubicle panel height, enabling you to keep documents, chargers, and a spare power strip within arm’s reach while maintaining legroom. ## Assembly: A Tale of Allen Keys, Patience, and Cup Runners

### Tools and Time
Most people can assemble a GOF 3P in a couple of hours with a basic toolkit: a Phillips-head screwdriver, a hex/Allen key, and perhaps a power drill on a low setting if you’re trusting your DPIs to hold steady while you twist. Don’t rush the process. Read the manual twice, lay out the hardware in labeled bags, and mock-assemble the parts on the floor before standing anything upright. A good approach is to start with the main desk frame, then attach the vertical panels, then the cable management channels, and finally the surface top and any drawers or under-desk components.

### Tips and Tricks
- Inventory the screws before you start. Some sets include extra screws for random holes; others deliberately include a spare in case of legendary misalignment.
- Use a small magnetic tray to keep screws from rolling away. You don’t want to chase a cam bolt under the couch while your coffee cools on the desk.
- Have a friend nearby for the heavy lifting. Cubicle walls do add stability, but you’ll want two sets of hands anyway for the larger components.
- Keep a cloth handy to wipe the surface after assembly; it’s easier to wipe off dust than to explain to your boss why the new desk has a fingerprint jungle on it.
