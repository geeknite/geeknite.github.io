## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping It Fresh
### Routine Cleaning
Vacuum regularly to prevent dirt from embedding into the ribbed texture. A standard household vacuum with a hard floor setting works well; avoid high-suction attachments that might snag the fibers. If you have a pet that sheds like a tiny fluffy meteor, consider a quick pass with a lint roller to keep the surface neat between deeper cleanings.

### Stain Management
For common household spills, blot and clean promptly. Avoid aggressive scrubbing, which can push stains deeper or damage the texture. A mild soap solution or a fabric-safe cleaner applied to a soft cloth should do the trick. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first.

### Deep Cleaning
Occasionally you might want to give the rug a more thorough refresh. A light wash with mild detergent and a gentle scrub, followed by thorough rinsing and air drying, can revitalize the surface. Make sure the rug is completely dry before placing it back on the floor or outside, as moisture can invite mold in vulnerable corners.

### Storage and Winter Care
If you plan to store the rug for a season, roll it up rather than folding to avoid creases. Store in a dry, ventilated area. Outdoor use in winter can be fine in many climates, but if there’s a harsh freeze, you might remove it from outdoor exposure to prevent potential damage.
