## How to play the 2 player duel without summoning the rules demon
A two player session is a different beast than a full four to five player game. The core concept stays the same: you want to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card while using your action cards to manipulate the deck and your opponent’s fate. The 2P variant adds streamlined interactions that keep the game brisk and fun for a couple who want something quick yet satisfying after a long day of quests and Netflix buffering.

Key pillars of the two player mode include:

- Draw and Resolve: On your turn you draw a card and then decide if you want to play an action card to influence the drawn card or your opponent’s fate. The pacing is punchy, which means you rarely hit a lull where you’re forced to contemplate a card that might as well be a resume for a college essay about strategic regret.
- Attack and Counter: You can launch an attack to skip your opponent’s draw phase, making the opponent draw more cards and fear the inevitable Boom event. The counter cards let you prove you have the sharper reflexes in this digital cat-themed duel. Yes, this is the card game version of “you shall not pass the cat.”
- Defuse and Final Countdown: Defuse cards are your lifeline. In two players, you’ll often end up saving one defuse for a clutch moment where the other person is about to celebrate something that could be better left unspoken. The auction of nerves at the end is one of the delightful parts of this edition.

The aim is simple enough to explain: force your opponent to draw the exploding kitten card, avoid getting exploded yourself, and pepper in a few defuses when the universe seems to be mocking your hand state. The two player edition emphasizes quick rounds, so expect the game to swing often and to reward bold plays that feel both reckless and brilliant in equal measure.
