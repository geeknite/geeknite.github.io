## Unboxing and Assembly: The Gentle Art of Not Wrapping Yourself in Cables

Unboxing a floor rack is a two-person job, ideally with a beverage in hand and a label maker within arm’s reach. Here’s the typical flow when the Avit 45RU lands on your loading dock.

### Unpack and Inspect
1. Open the carton and salute the rails, panels, and hardware bags. As with any metal ship, you want to ensure nothing arrived dented or plotting a mutiny under the plastic wrap.
2. Check the included components: base frame, top frame, front/rear doors, side panels, vertical rails, cable management accessories, and the mounting screws. If any piece looks suspicious, contact customer support before it becomes a gravity-practicing obstacle course.
3. Note the hardware quality: thread taps, corrosion resistance, and whether the screws have the satisfaction-ensuring “notch” on the head for a screwdriver with enough torque to convince a stubborn parent to stop nagging about power usage.

### Assembly: It’s a Rack, Not a Rocket
- Start with the base, then attach the vertical rails. Avit usually provides adjustable rails for EIA 19-inch gear, meaning you can shift things around as your rack mission evolves.
- Mount the top panel, ensuring the unit sits plumb and level. A level sentence about levelness is worth the time; your future self will thank you when you’re aligning blade servers and you don’t have to squint through a plumb line.
- Install doors and side panels if you want a closed system or a something you can count on to keep dust away from your precious fans.
- Route cables through the management channels and cable ties. Do not outright banish the cable spaghetti; you’re merely conductor of a symphony, not a chaos conductor.
- Mount your gear. The 600 mm depth accommodates most 2U-4U devices with room to spare for cables. Pro tip: install rear-access finger protections if your team likes to poke fingers into the backplane for fun, which we do not recommend.

Estimated time: two to four hours with a friend who claims to be “handy.” Real-world variance: depends on how stubborn your rack is and whether you’ve remembered to bring the spare thumbscrew you swore you didn’t need. The important bit is that assembly is a predictable, repeatable process—like a very sturdy puzzle with a lock on it.

## Thermal Performance: Keeping Your CPUs from Turning into Mini Suns

Thermals matter more than you’d think. A rack that can’t shed heat efficiently is a sleeping dragon, and you don’t want a dragon that eats your power bill for breakfast. The Avit 45RU is designed with airflow in mind—vented doors, solid side panels optional when you want to minimize noise, and the possibility to install fans where you need them most.

- Ventilated doors reduce hot air pockets at the front, which is especially important if you place high-DPI GPUs or dense compute nodes at the front. The back of the rack tends to be busy with power distribution units (PDUs) and cabling, so a clean airflow path makes opinions about noise easier to manage.
- Optional fans provide targeted cooling where your hottest components live. You can fine-tune airflow depending on your gear lineup, which is essential in a room that houses both storage and compute monsters.

In practice, expect a well-cooled environment if you’re careful with front intake and rear exhaust paths. If you’re cramming three 2U devices with high TDPs into a single bay and using the stock panels without adjustments, you’ll want to add fans or at least a cooling plan. The racks aren’t magic, but they’re designed to be sensible and adaptable—a rare combination.

## How to Choose a Floor Data Rack: A Quick Guide

If you’re shopping for a new rack, here are the quick questions to ask yourself:
- Do I need 19-inch equipment compatibility, and what depth do my devices require? If you’re deep in storage or expandability, a 600 mm depth is a good middle ground.
- How much space do I have in the rack room? Measure door frames and hallway widths to ensure you can actually move the rack into position.
- What’s my airflow plan? Will you use ventilated doors, fans, or both? Plan at least a simple airflow model to avoid hot corners.
- Do I need security features like locks and tamper-resistant panels? If you’re in a shared space or a high-traffic environment, this matters.
- How modular do I want my setup to be in the future? The Avit 45RU’s rails and channels are designed to be adaptable; consider if you’ll add more gear in 12–24 months.

A good rack fits your equipment and your workflow, not just your budget. It’s worth testing with a few devices before you commit, ideally using a mock-up of your actual gear to see how things feel in practice.
