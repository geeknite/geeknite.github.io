## Care, Display, and Long-Term Preservation

Foils aren’t just pretty wrappers; they’re delicate investments in the physical world. A few practical tips for Omenpaths and friends:
- Storage: Use rigid card slides or a binder with acid-free sleeves. Avoid sleeves that flex too much, especially for larger‑sized foil cards; you don’t want to crimp the foil’s shine.
- Handling: Wash hands. Don’t lick the foil (we’ve all done it for the luster, but please don’t). Fingertip oils can cloud the surface over time, so cotton or microfiber gloves aren’t overkill, especially if you’re handling multiple high-value foils in a single session.
- Display: A glass-fronted display case with diffuse LED lighting can make the Omenpaths foil prance with maximum drama without blinding you during late-night game sessions.
- Transportation: If you’re moving decks, keep foils in solid sleeves inside a deck box to protect from UV exposure and accidental bends.

Display care isn’t glamorous, but it’s a good habit if you’re serious about keeping a mid-range foil from becoming a mid-life tragedy for your collection. The more you treat your foils with respect, the more they’ll reward you with long-term memories and, yes, bragging rights at local meetups.
