## Overview

Uno Flip is a two sided card game that shakes up the classic color matching formula by flipping to a completely different deck mid game. On the light side you have standard Uno cards including numbers and action cards. Flip the deck and you unleash the dark side. The dark side adds a level of severity: Draw Five and Wild Draw Five appear, swapping a friendly tilt with a high barb of penalty. The flip mechanism means that keeping track of which side you are on matters almost as much as your card count. It feels like two games in one, stitched together with a neon thread and a strong habit of making you groan and laugh in equal measure.

## How to play in 60 seconds

1) Each player starts with 7 cards. 
2) The top card of the draw pile becomes the start of a color track. 
3) On your turn, you place a card that matches either color or number, or use a special action card. 
4) If you can not play, you draw one card. 
5) When someone plays the Flip card, the entire deck flips to the opposite side with its own set of rules and penalties. 
6) The game continues until a player has one card left and yells Uno. If you forget Uno, a penalty might come your way as per the dark side. 
7) The winner is the first player to discard all their cards. 

This formula means you get two waves of chaos: one on the light side and one after the flip that can drastically alter who is in the lead and who is staring at the sky, counting the ceiling tiles. As a quick tip, keep track of the color cards because the flip can undo a good color run in a blink.

## Strategy and tips

- Track the dark side cards: When the deck flips, the Draw Five and Wild Draw Five become more common than you might expect. This is a team sport in disguise; you want to watch the table and plan a little ahead rather than chasing a last minute Uno. 
- Manage your hand: balancing number cards with action cards helps you survive both sides of the flipping coin. The moment you get overrun with color mismatches, you are in trouble either way. 
- Use Wild Draw Five wisely: it is a game turning tool that can save you or ruin everyone else if you time it poorly. The best time to unleash it is when your opponents are about to finish but their last couple of cards are color locked. 
- The Uno etiquette: never overplay your hand; the goal is to win tactically, not to prove you own the most cards in the room. Keep the pace up, the table engaged, and the mood light.

### Fun with house rules

Uno Flip shines with light house rules that can add new drama.
