How to Play Phase 10 (A Quick Revisit for the Curious)
Phase 10 is a classic, and this version sticks to the same core rules that have kept it in rotation for decades. The goal is to complete ten distinct phases before your opponents do. Each phase requires a different combination of cards, such as sets and runs, and completing a phase advances your personal stage in the game. The exact see-saw of passing phases and picking up more cards creates a mix of strategy and luck that keeps people returning to the table. The 2 Jokers act as wild cards, which can be incredibly powerful when you’re staring down a stubborn run you can’t quite finish. The 2 Skip cards add a dash of antagonism, letting you slow down the person who’s about to pull off phase after phase. It’s a surprisingly friendly version of a take-that mechanic that doesn’t get violent, which is exactly the vibe you want for family game night.

Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a hand to each player (the standard 10 cards, though some house rules grant a few extras on the first round).
- Place the remainder of the deck in the draw pile and flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Decide who goes first and set the timer, or simply let the party decided by who can explain the phases best without coughing from laughter.

Basic Gameplay
Each player, on their turn, draws a card from either the draw pile or the discard pile. Then they discard one card, trying to complete their current phase. Phases aren’t universal; each player keeps track of their own progress. If you complete your phase, you lay down your cards in front of you and move on to the next phase in a way that looks like you’re presenting a science project to a very impressed jury of game-night judges. The rules are straightforward, but the strategy? That’s where the fun hides behind the art on the cards and the occasional lull in the conversation as players plan their next move while a parent tries to explain a tangent about a math concept to a child who’s lost interest in the discussion of card combos.

Ten Phases: A High-Level Overview
The exact phases in Phase 10 are a journey of increasing difficulty, and yes, there are ten of them. Here’s a high-level glimpse without spoilers that would spoil the fun:

- Phases 4 through 6 ramp up the complexity with longer runs, more combinatorial patterns, and the occasional twist that makes you reconsider how you hold a Joker.
