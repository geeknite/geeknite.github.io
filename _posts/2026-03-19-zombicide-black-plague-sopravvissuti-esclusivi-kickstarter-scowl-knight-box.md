### Gameplay feel: pacing, synergy, and the occasional balance quirk
Zombicide Black Plague is famous for its cooperative, dice-driven chaos. The Knight Box spices up the core formula by giving your team more knights to watch over. The balance of power between survivors and the zombie horde remains, more or less, consistent with prior expansions. The exclusive survivors often begin with unique starting equipment that nudges the early turns toward a more dynamic rhythm. As the number of zombies climbs, the sandbox-like feel of the game shines: you will pivot between blades-out assaults and strategic retreats more often than you will follow a single, rigid plan.

Of course, with exclusives come potential balance questions. Some players report that one or two of the exclusive survivors feel a touch overpowered in specific missions. Others argue that the forced synergy of a Knight Box campaign demands a different approach to mission selection, turning some scenarios into a puzzle where you have to manage both zombie density and the timing of your knight reinforcements. In practice, the differences are usually mild enough to be enjoyed rather than contested, but if you crave perfect symmetry, this is not a pure competitive experience.
