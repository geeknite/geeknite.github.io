## What's inside the Orc Zombies Set
The box announces what you care about most: an assortment of Orc zombies, a couple of new survivor options, and a set of mission cards designed to tilt the battlefield in favor of the green tide. In practice, you get a handful of highly detailed plastic Orc zombies in various poses. You also receive the core trappings you expect from Zombicide expansions: cards, tokens, and occasionally a special miniature that acts as a boss or a scenario anchor. The Orc zombies themselves are the star here: they are bulkier, more intimidating, and a little more theatrical than your standard zombie crop. The paint schemes lean toward mossy greens, weathered metal grays, and the occasional splash of bone white to highlight the teeth and claws. If you enjoy the visual storytelling of miniature gaming, this set delivers a lot of character at a glance. 

Alongside the miniatures you will find new mission cards that push you toward aggressive, charge-heavy play styles. The narrative thread is very much centered on the Green Horde reclaiming territory and expanding its influence, which in practical terms means more berserk orcs banging on your siege lines and a few clever tricks that reward planning and synergy between your survivors. 

### What about the rest of the content? 
- A compact set of storming objectives that encourage bold, unfold-and-stride tactics rather than cautious corner-camping. 
- New spawn and objective tokens to keep the board feeling fresh and slightly unbalanced in your favor—if you enjoy the thrill of a race to the last man standing, you will appreciate the little accelerants tucked in here. 
- Slightly more fiddly setup than a vanilla zombie deck, but not so much that you need a dedicated painter’s day just to assemble them. 

## The miniatures: design, scale, and paintability
The Orc zombies are not shy about size or silhouette. They are chunkier than the standard Zombicide zombies and carry the vibe of brutish, war-worn raiders who somehow have become undead and still refuse to act like it. The sculpts are crisp in the right places, with armor plates that catch light nicely and faces that show a mix of rage and hunger that actually sells the whole orcish menace without needing a lore page to explain it. 

From a paintability standpoint, these miniatures fall squarely into the category of “fun to paint, slightly annoying to do in under two hours” for casual hobbyists. The armor details provide excellent contrast opportunities; the exposed skin areas are not overly textured, which makes shading and highlighting straightforward rather than a slog. If you are new to painting orcs, you will find these approachable and forgiving. If you are a veteran, you will likely gravitate toward a quick base coat and then a couple of washes to bring out the crevices and the menace. 

One practical note: the base inlays on some Orcs have a slightly odd geometry that makes pinning or gluing a bit fiddly. It is not a deal breaker, but if you want a perfectly stable unit on a windy night, you might want to pin a few of the stances to their bases for long-term display. 

### The scale debate: compatibility with the rest of Zombicide
If you already own Zombicide Black Plague or other Green Horde expansions, you will be glad to know these Orc zombies fit the standard scale. They do not look comically oversized next to other miniatures, which is to say they do not look like they borrowed a foot from a D&D dragon. The scale is consistent enough that you can mix them into existing boards without breaking immersion or requiring a new terrain layout. This is important for those who run multi-expansion campaigns or those who are building a big, sprawling map of carnage. 

## Rules, balance, and how it plays on the table
The Orc Zombies Set does two things well in terms of gameplay: it adds punch and it adds risk. The orc zombies tend to have higher hit points for a zombie unit, and some variants come with special abilities that encourage you to adapt your standard runner-and-slugger approach. You will notice that the board state can overwhelm if you fall into a rhythm of predictable reactions. The new cards introduce a little more chaos in a controlled way, which is pleasant for players who crave a bit more unpredictability without tipping into dysfunction. 

Balance is not perfect, and that is part of the charm. The set is designed to be an “encounter pack” that spices up mid to late game scenarios rather than a full-blown game changer. If you like your games to stay surgical and careful, you might find that some Orc hordes can pivot a bit too quickly, forcing you to improvise on the fly. If you love the adrenaline of sudden surges and feints, you will adore the way these Orcs push you to adapt your strategies. 

For new players, there is a gentle springboard into the chaos. The rules feel familiar enough to pick up quickly but with enough new wrinkle to make the early games feel different enough to maintain interest. Veteran players will find that a few fresh mechanics reward team coordination and sharp planning, which is exactly what a cooperative game needs to stay engaging over multiple sessions.

## Painting, display, and care tips
If you plan to show off your collection, here are a few practical ideas to maximize the look of your Orcs without breaking the bank: 
- Start with a deep green base and dry brush in a lighter green to highlight armor edges. 
- Use a mid-tone for leather straps and a darker shade for metal plates to create contrast. 
- A touch of bone white on teeth and tusks gives a grim prelude to the violence. 
- Apply a wash to sink the details into a grimy environment; you will be rewarded with the sense that these Orcs have walked through decades of war. 
- Display them near a terrain feature that carries a story, like a siege engine or a ruined gate, to create drama at a glance. 

If you are new to painting minis, consider practicing on a spare Orc or two before committing to the entire set. The results are typically satisfying, and the practice will help you dial in the greens and metallics to your taste.

## A note on care and storage
After the last game of the evening, take a minute to count the survivors, check the health tracks, and stash the miniatures back in their trays. If you value your painting, consider labeling the bags so you do not mix up Orcs with other greens or Black Plague units. This is not a make-or-break tip, but it helps when you want to jump straight into the next session without a two minute identifications dance.
