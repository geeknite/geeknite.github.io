## Care, storage, and preservation tips
If you own a sealed 1979 UNO or come across one in sealed condition, some best practices will help preserve the experience:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to minimize color fade and card warping.
- Keep the box intact to prevent dust and humidity from creeping into the folds and corners.
- Avoid bending the cards; handling with a light touch will maintain their edge and color integrity for as long as possible.
- If you do manage to open the seal, handle the deck with clean hands; vintage card stock can be more susceptible to oils and residue than modern materials.
- Consider using a card tray or a wooden display case to showcase the deck when not in use, especially if you enjoy the aesthetic of a shelf full of retro board game artifacts.

Preservation can be a balancing act between accessibility and protection. If your goal is to showcase the sealed beauty while still keeping the option to play, consider a display-forward approach with a pull-out tray that allows you to enjoy the deck while preserving the outer seal.
