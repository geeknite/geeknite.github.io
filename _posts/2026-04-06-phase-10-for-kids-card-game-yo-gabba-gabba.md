If a player completes a phase, they get a goofy Yo Gabba Gabba sticker as a trophy. If not, they keep the rhythm going with the turn order and try again. ## How to play with kids
In Phase 10 for kids the core flow remains the classic: draw, play, discard. The major difference is you are aiming for kid friendly phase shapes rather than the slightly more intimidating adult patterns. The guide suggests approachable phases for younger players:
- Phase 1: two sets of three
- Phase 2: one set of three and one run of four
- Phase 3: one run of four and a small collection
- Phase 4: a run of seven or a couple of smaller patterns that are easy to recognize
The exact phase progression can be simplified by the adult to what the kids can comfortably identify. The aim is to introduce players to sequencing, matching, and planning ahead without turning the table into a math class. The Yo Gabba Gabba branding helps anchor memory; after all, kids remember songs and characters better than a bullet list of numbers.
