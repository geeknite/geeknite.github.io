## Care, Cleaning, and Routine Maintenance
Regular upkeep is simple and non-drama. A weekly vacuum is all you need to keep the pile looking uniform and the stripes sharp. Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth. For stains, a mild detergent mixed with water works well; test on a small inconspicuous area first to ensure no color bleed. For deeper cleaning, you can perform a light spot-clean or schedule a professional cleaning every 12-18 months if you want to extend the rug’s lifespan.

A few practical tips:
- Avoid harsh solvents, which can degrade the fibers and dull the stripe contrast.
- Do not steam clean; heat and moisture can warp the backing and dull the fiber sheen.
- Rotate the rug every 3-6 months if your sun exposure is uneven; this helps prevent localized fading on the high-contrast stripes.
- If shedding manifests in the first weeks, continue normal cleaning. It’s typical for synthetic rugs and usually subsides as the fibers settle in.

## Maintenance and Longevity in Real-Life Scenarios
Over time, a rug’s real test is how well it handles daily life. If you have kids, a dog, or a coffee-obsessed partner, you’ll want to be mindful of spills and dragging. The Rachelle rug performs well under typical daily wear, but there are a few factors to consider:
- Foot traffic: The stripes hide minor scuffs and dirt better than solid neutrals when the dirt is light and evenly distributed. For heavy foot traffic, consider a rug pad to minimize shifting and reduce wear on the pile.
- Sun exposure: Direct sun can fade the high-contrast stripes. Rotate or reposition if your brightest windows cast long, intense light onto the rug.
- Cleaning schedule: A light, regular cleaning schedule is more effective than infrequent deep cleans. If you wait too long between cleanings, some stains can become stubborn.
