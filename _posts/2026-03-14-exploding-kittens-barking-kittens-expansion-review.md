## Gameplay Flow: A Session with Barking Kittens
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll recognize the skeleton of the game: draw a card, play an action, resolve effects, and try not to explode. Barking Kittens adds a few layers without stacking too much on top of the existing rhythm.

### Setup
- Shuffle the expansion deck with the base deck (or set up separately and mix later, depending on your preference for chaos).
- Place any new tokens or markers within easy reach so you’re not rooting through reach-and-yell territory every time a card hits the table.
- Decide if you want to enable any house rules to accommodate the new kitten interactions. Your group may prefer a “no-dorking out” rule to keep things moving; your mileage may vary.

### Turn Structure with Barking Kittens in Play
- Draw a card unless you have a reason not to (the base rules apply here).
- Play an action or use a kitten effect, including Barking Kittens if available in your hand. The order of play matters more with these new cards because timing can determine whether you save a teammate or sacrifice them to the chaos gods.
- Resolve any reactions and check for explosions. When someone draws an Exploding Kitten, the chaos level rises and your table becomes a venue for dramatic pauses and accusations about who sabotaged whom with cat-nip knives.

One note about the pacing: Barking Kittens can accelerate the tempo, especially when players start chaining effects. If you’re someone who loves building a rhythm, you’ll enjoy the way these new cards push the tempo in a way that still respects the base game’s timing. If you’re more chill and enjoy long, thoughtful turns, you might need to re-balance your table’s expectations or adopt a neighborly “one extra-cool move per round” courtesy pause to prevent total table chaos.

### Strategies and Tips for Barking Kittens
- Learn the new card effects by heart. It sounds obvious, but you’ll thank yourself when you don’t have to pause and reread every other turn.
- Watch the table’s energy level. Barking Kittens adds a zingy momentum; use it to your advantage by timing your biggest plays when the group seems ready for the next round of hilarity.
- Don’t be afraid to lean into the chaos. Strategy in Exploding Kittens is often about risk assessment and social interaction; Barking Kittens nudges you toward wilder decisions, so lean into it and enjoy the ride.
- Coordinate with a friend who loves the “cat conga line” moment. They’ll enjoy setting up a sequence where every card played triggers the next maximal effect, creating a memorable, messy finale.
