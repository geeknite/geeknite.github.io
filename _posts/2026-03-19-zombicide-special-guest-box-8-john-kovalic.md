When you mix a zombie apocalypse with the irreverent wit of a comic artist and a company that loves plastic miniatures a little too much, you get Zombicide Special Guest Box 8. One of the first things you’ll notice is that the miniatures aren’t just different in paint scheme; they adopt a silhouette language that screams Kovalic-inspired energy. There’s a sneer, a stance, and a set of hands that communicate personality before you even read their cards. The sculpts hold poses well and are sturdy enough to survive normal board-game handling. As with many Zombicide expansions, base design matters. These bases don’t rely on heroic theatrics or overly complex pegs to stand. ### Painting tips and assembly notes: getting the most out of the set

If you’re new to Zombicide, start with a quick dry-brush demo on the non-posing studs to see how the color reads on the plastic. The Special Guest minis can absorb a bit more pigment than standard figures, thanks to slightly larger sculpted lines, which makes them forgiving for new painters. If you want the John Kovalic vibe without sacrificing speed, try a limited palette: two or three bold colors for outfits, a dark wash for crevices, and a light dry brush on edges to highlight the details. The result reads well on camera and on the tabletop, which is especially satisfying when you pull off a tricky board placement and look at your own work with a grin.

For seasoned painters, this box offers opportunities for characterful storytelling through color choices. You could assign each hero a color-coded backstory or recurring motif and let the team’s dynamics unfold as you progress through scenarios. The game’s balance remains stable even with these creative touches, so don’t be afraid to lean into your narrative preferences.
