## How to play GaGN Go Fish The Adult Edition
### Setup
- Shuffle the deck thoroughly. The adult-themed prompts require a moment of respect for the chaos about to happen.
- Deal a set number of cards to each player, depending on the number of participants. In a typical four-player game, you might deal five cards per person. The rest of the deck sits in the middle as the draw pile.
- Decide on any house rules you want to adopt before the first turn. House rules can range from speed rounds to special penalties for misquotes of famous lines.

### Basic turn structure
- On your turn, you ask another player for a card that would complete a set you are pursuing. The framework mirrors classic Go Fish: you must have a card in hand to ask for that rank or category.
- If the requested player has the card, they must hand it over, and you continue your turn. If not, you must draw from the pile, and your turn ends.
- When you collect a complete set, you lay it down in front of you and score points. The more sets you have, the more you control the table.

### Special twists for GaGN
- The prompt prompts you to answer with a quick line or a mini-consultation with the person you are asking. It adds a conversational challenge because you may need to justify your request with a punny justification.
- If you misquote a famous line or misinterpret a prompt, you face a light penalty or a forfeit of one card from your hand. The idea is to keep the game playful rather than punitive.
- Occasionally a wild card appears that can change the category or force players to swap hands. This adds chaotic fun and gives the table something to react to beyond the usual card-picking ritual.

### Endgame and scoring
- The game ends when the draw pile is exhausted or when a player runs out of cards (depending on the house rules you adopt).
- Points are awarded for complete sets and for successful bluff plays. The exact scoring can be customized, but a simple version is that each complete set is worth a fixed number of points and a bonus for the most sets.
- The winner is the player with the most points after all sets are claimed. In practice, this often means the table ends with a chorus of banter as the scores are tallied and a winner is crowned.

## Strategy tips and house rules
### Basic strategy for new players
- Pay attention to what you are asking and what you are given. While the prompts are funny, tracking who has what is essential for maximizing your sets.
- Use your bluffs sparingly. A well-timed bluff can win you an extra turn, but a reckless one can turn the table against you.
- Keep an eye on the draw pile. If the deck is thinning, you may want to press for more aggressive trades before the pile is exhausted.
- When in doubt, lean on collaboration. A friendly exchange of cards with a trusted ally can unlock a chain of sets that quickly becomes unmanageable for others.

### House rules to tailor the chaos
- Quick Night Mode: shrink the deck by removing low-value prompts to speed up rounds. Great for busy evenings.
- Truth or Quip: after a successful request, the requester must deliver a 5-second line about the prompt, adding a mini-performance element.
- Roast Round: once per game, a player can challenge the leader with a playful roast that rewards or penalizes points depending on audience reaction. 
- No Spoil Skips: if a card is drawn, players can only say their usual lines; no meta-hints about other players' hands are allowed. This keeps the game fair and funny.

### Strategy for longer sessions
If you want to stretch GaGN into a longer campaign, consider rotating the role of the dealer and introducing a progression system. As rounds accumulate, you can unlock slightly more elaborate prompts or special cards that alter the normal rules. The trick is to maintain a balance between laughter and competition so that the game remains enjoyable rather than exhausting.
