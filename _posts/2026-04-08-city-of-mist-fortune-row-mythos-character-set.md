## Overview: what is Fortune Row and why now?
Fortune Row is a Mythos Character Set designed to slot into your City of Mist campaigns with minimal friction and maximal flavor. The set includes a curated roster of heroes and antiheroes, a handful of villainous motifs, improved or new power cards, and a narrative toolkit that makes the city feel alive and reactive. It’s not just about adding more numbers to the dice cage; it is about giving you tools to tell more personal stories about luck, risk, and fate — with the city’s iconic blend of myth and metropolis as your stage.

This is the sort of expansion that can feel risky: too many moving parts, and your GM screen turns into a carnival wheel. Fortune Row manages to avoid this trap by presenting clear archetypes, well-integrated mythos themes, and a selection of prompts that gently guide play without shackling it to a predetermined plot. In short, it respects the core idea of City of Mist — that every person can be a legend in disguise — while giving you fresh fuel for the fire.

## The contents: what you actually get
### What sits in the box
Fortune Row comes with a compact but robust assortment of components. You’ll find a character booklet that introduces the core set of mystics, gangsters, and street prophets tied to Fortune Row’s themes. Then there are power cards and their variants, which reframe typical up-there-down-there conflicts into something more mythic and a little unruly in the best possible way. The kit also includes narrative prompts and a few GM-facing tools designed to reduce the time spent on prep while increasing the tempo of your sessions.

The presentation is sharp. The design language — blues and golds with noir shadows — makes it instantly legible on a table, which you will appreciate when you’re juggling multiple characters who all want to be the center of attention. The cards are sturdy, with a nice balance of text and iconography. The tokens and standees are not gimmicks; they’re functional, and they make it easier to visualize who possesses what mythic leverage mid-scene.

### What is unique to Fortune Row
Fortune Row introduces several motifs centered on luck, fate, risk, and moral ambiguity. The mythos themes aren’t merely cosmetic; they alter how you resolve actions and how narratives bend under pressure. For example, some motifs let a character “tempt fate” with a consequence that escalates dramatically if certain conditions are met. Others inject a sense of random luck into social conflict, letting players influence outcomes not just through skill checks but through narrative choices that feel meaningful to the city’s mythology.

This set also pushes the idea of the city as a living character. Fortune Row doesn’t preach a singleissimo tone of doom; it provides variations on mood: smoky jazz-lounge noir, neon-drenched shrine markets, and rain-slick back alleys where legends whisper in the wrong ear. The result is a toolbox that lets a GM shift gears on the fly, leaning into heartbreak or hilarity as required by the scene.

### Aesthetic and material quality
The art direction is cohesive, leaning into the game’s signature blend of urban myth and pulp ephemera. The illustrations manage to be iconic without resorting to clichés. They invite interpretation, which is essential in City of Mist where a single image often triggers a longer narrative thread. The typography is clean, with a modern, slightly retro vibe that matches the game’s hybrid feel — like a detective’s notebook from the future.

### How it compares to core and other expansions
If you own the core City of Mist rulebook, Fortune Row slots into your existing myths with minimal friction. It doesn’t reinvent the wheels; it reinforces and expands them. Compared to some earlier expansions that leaned hard on a fixed cast of archetypes, Fortune Row offers a flexible character toolkit that scales with your table. It’s easy to slot into a one-shot or to weave into a long campaign without sacrificing cohesion.

There’s a particular elegance in how Fortune Row handles the conflict of fortune vs fate. The set doesn’t pretend that luck absolves responsibility; it invites players to own both the risk and the consequence. That philosophical edge is what keeps Fortune Row feeling fresh after a dozen sessions, rather than a novelty item you pull out for a party trick.

## Character design: who you get and how they sing
### The core cast and their mythos hooks
Fortune Row introduces a multi-faceted cast that includes vigilante mystics, streetwise seers, and information brokers who barter in whispers and coffee stains. Each character comes with a short backstory that grounds their powers in the city’s mythic ecosystem and a few signature moves that feel cinematic without tipping into over-the-top power fantasies. The characters are designed to play nicely with established City of Mist archetypes, but they also offer enough variance to support completely new character concepts.

One of the standout additions is a set of characters who embody the idea that luck is a two-way street. They can tilt the odds, but the odds have a price — a price paid in relationships, secrets, or trust broken across the city’s mythscape. This is not a moralistic sermon; it’s a narrative invitation to explore the gray areas where heroes and villains often share the same shadowy street corner.

### Power cards and ability environment
The power cards in Fortune Row are not just a pile of numbers; they are prompts that push players to occupy the mythic space the set is trying to create. You will see cards that trigger when a scene’s stakes involve risk, when a character fights against a fate-dictated path, or when they attempt to bend luck to their will. The card design makes it easy to adjudicate in-the-moment decisions while preserving the sense that the city itself is listening and responding to every choice.

If you are a GM who loves improvisation, Fortune Row is your friend. The toolkit includes ready-made scene hooks and an array of situations that can escalate quickly or recede to a quiet moment of self-discovery. The risk-vs. reward dynamic is crisp, and it scales with party size. In a party of three, the tension feels intimate; in a party of six, it explodes into a carnival of coincidences and near-misses. Either way, the set stays true to the game’s core premise: legends aren’t born; they rise from choices under pressure.

### Narrative prompts that actually spark play
One of the book’s strengths is its curation of prompts that aren’t generic. They reference Fortune Row’s locales and motifs, asking players to consider how a legend’s past reappears in the city’s present. You’ll get prompts that invite players to reassess a once-trusted ally, challenge a mythic assumption, or unlock a secret about Fortune Row itself. These prompts feel like seeds you can plant into any ongoing thread without losing your own voice or the table’s momentum.

## Mechanics, balance, and table impact
### How Fortune Row blends with existing rules
Fortune Row respects the established mechanics of City of Mist, including the concept of Mysteries, Logos, and Mythos dice. It adds layers but doesn’t render the core system obsolete. The new motifs, when used thoughtfully, complement the existing Arches (the city’s power factions) and the Mythos cards that players lean on for narrative momentum. The balance is careful: the new elements feel potent enough to be meaningful, yet not so overpowered that they require a complete rework of your local table rules.

For GMs who worry about power creep, there’s a well-placed caution in the book about pacing. The designers encourage you to introduce Fortune Row’s tools gradually, measure scene tempo, and watch the players’ narrative levers pull the story toward tension rather than stall it with too many mechanical checks. The result is an expansion that invites experimentation while keeping your sessions moving.

### Accessibility and learning curve
Fortune Row is friendly to newcomers. The presentation is clear, with quick-start play options and well-documented examples. This is not a wander through a labyrinth of table rules; it’s a guided tour through a city’s legends. Experienced players will still find hidden corners worth exploring — the book rewards patient reading and a willingness to experiment with non-linear storytelling.

If you are a GM who has struggled with balancing multiple mythic threads, Fortune Row provides a structured approach. It is not a one-size-fits-all fix, but it gives you the right tools to tailor the mythos to your players’ appetites and your table’s pace. The playtesting notes sprinkled through the chapters can be a helpful map for tuning sessions and maintaining a satisfying narrative arc across arcs and campaigns.

## Thematic and narrative potential: what kind of stories does it unlock?
### Noir mythos meets festival of legends
Fortune Row shines when you lean into the clash between city grit and mythic grandeur. You can stage a chase through the rain-slick alleys of Fortune Row, where a legend’s promise tempts a character to push past personal limits. Or you can flip to a quieter moment in which a character confronts a fate they believed they escaped, only to realize the myth has always been there, watching from the corner of a streetlamp.

The set supports both intimate character-driven stories and larger-than-life capers. It invites players to consider what luck means in a world where legends are real, but the city remains stubbornly ordinary at first glance. The tension arises from the protagonist’s attempt to balance who they are with who Fortune Row insists they must become. It’s a delicious hinge that yields both dramatic peaks and small, human moments that feel earned rather than manufactured.

### Implementing Fortune Row in existing arcs
If you are in the middle of a campaign, you can weave Fortune Row into an existing arc by introducing a new motif at a critical turning point. For example, a decision point might come down to whether a character will risk a personal connection to save a city block or gamble on a mythic loophole that could alter the odds for everyone. Fortune Row’s cards provide ready-made triggers to get the action rolling without derailing your planned beats. The GM kit includes sample scenes that demonstrate how to escalate stakes gradually and how to bring back earlier plot threads as an echo of the new mythic reality.

If you run one-shots, Fortune Row is a gold mine. You can fit a complete arc into a single session with three to five acts, allowing players to experience the city’s legend arc flash in a compact, satisfying way. The motifs deliver enough punch to feel consequential while staying accessible to players who are still learning the ropes of City of Mist’s unique blend of roleplay and die-rolls.

## GM tips: running Fortune Row smoothly
- Start with a table-read: a quick scene in Fortunes Row that sets the tone and clarifies the mythic stakes. Let players discuss their goals before the dice start rolling.
- Use prompts as bite-sized anchors: if a scene starts drifting, drop a prompt and let players decide how to respond to fate’s nudge.
- Rotate spotlight carefully: Fortune Row rewards multiple characters having a moment to influence the outcome. Plan for micro-resolutions that let different players shine without stealing the show from others.
- Balance narrative risk with reward: the myths should matter, but the table shouldn’t feel punished for taking a bold path. Let failure feel interesting, not crushing.
- Tie it back to the city: remind players that Fortune Row’s legends are part of a larger texture. Small local details can become the seed of global consequences if you want to escalate into a larger arc.

### Suggested sessions and pacing
If you’re running a standard 3- to 5-hour session, Fortune Row can offer you a tight mini-arc that introduces a single mythic dilemma, followed by a midpoint twist that changes a character’s relationship to the city, and finally a cliffhanger that invites a second session. For longer campaigns, use Fortune Row’s motifs to seed ongoing subplots that can reappear as callbacks later in the story — memories, regrets, and promises that the city never forgets.
