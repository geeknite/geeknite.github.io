## Setup and Safety First

Outdoors means more space, but also more variables. The game prescribes a simple setup, and if you’ve set up a backyard obstacle course before, you’ll be right at home:

1. Clear a safe play area: remove glassware, fragile decorations, and any lawn flamingos that might become unwitting combatants. 
2. Layout the boundary lines: use the included mats or chalk to mark a wide play zone. The more space, the better—but you also want to avoid chasing someone into a street or a pool. 
3. Place the card deck in the center and distribute the burritos to each player or team. The edition emphasizes a fair wind-down from the initial flurry of throws. 
4. Review safety rules: no throwing toward faces, necks, or eyes. No throwing at pets. No aggressive hurling. The goal is laughter, not trip-and-falls.

The outdoors also invites a few common-sense cautions: watch the sun, hydrate, and keep a few towels handy for those inevitable moments when someone decides to wipe out in stilettos (okay, maybe not stilettos, but you get the idea). If you’re playing near kids who don’t know their own strength, consider a lighter touch. The foam burritos are designed for safety, and the breeze doesn’t excuse reckless throws—the same logic applies as in any yard game: you’re playing with friends, not trying to break your neighbor’s window.

As a frequent disclaimer in Geeknite posts: always take a breath, read the manual, and set expectations with the crew. Outdoors is about chaos, but chaos with consent.

## How the Gameplay Flows in Extreme Outdoor Edition

If you’ve played Throw Throw Burrito before, you already know the vibe: draw a card, look for a match, and when you hit a match, someone gets to chuck a burrito at someone else. The Outdoor Edition makes that flow feel more kinetic and visually chaotic, which is exactly what a party needs when the sun is going down and you’re still a spray-tanned hero defending your dignity.

Here’s a condensed playthrough to give you a sense of rhythm:

- On your turn, you draw a card and announce what you’re trying to complete. This is where the table chatter becomes the real sport: name-calling becomes strategy, and shouting “Burrito Assassin!” is a legitimate tactic depending on your group.
- If you draw a card that completes a set (the exact definitions vary by edition, but the vibe is “two-of-a-kind equals something, three-of-a-kind equals something wild”), you trigger a burrito throw.
- The burrito throws are the highlight. Foam becomes a projectile, enthusiasm becomes a strategy, and dodging becomes a cardio workout you didn’t sign up for. The Extreme Outdoor Edition makes dodging part of the scenic experience because you’re probably tumbling through a flowerbed at least once per game.
- Scoring is straightforward: you accumulate points for pairs or sets, with occasional bonus points for “outdoorsy feats” like successfully throwing a burrito while doing a celebratory pose. The rules encourage teamwork and a generous interpretation of “fun, not harm.”

Gameplay balance in outdoor settings hinges on space and social dynamics. If you’ve got hyper-competitive friends, you’ll witness sprinting and dramatic pileups. If you’ve got more chill gamers, expect a lot of laughter, some strategic burrito throws, and maybe a nap after the marathon round. Either way, you’ll end up with stories you’ll tell at future gatherings.

## Outdoor Edition Differences: What Actually Changes?

Compared to the indoor version, the Extreme Outdoor Edition tweaks a few balance points to accommodate open spaces:

- Longer, sturdier burritos: more weight, more visibility, and a better arc when thrown outdoors. This also means fewer accidental misses due to indoor carpet friction. 
- Wind awareness: you’ll see cards that instruct players to factor in breeze direction. Wind can flip a game in an instant, turning an easy hit into a full-on wind-delayed chase scene. 
- Boundary improvisation: outdoor spaces invite you to complicate the play area with makeshift markers, which adds a layer of improvisation to the game. 
- Temperature and humidity considerations: you’ll want to avoid brittle foam burritos in cold weather and ensure your hands aren’t numb from winter humidity before you launch into an epic arc. 
- Safety-first edits: the outdoor edition often includes a reminder to avoid chasing someone into the pool or off a cliff—clearly stated and clearly wise. 

In a nutshell, the Outdoor Edition is not “the same game, outdoors.” It’s “the same game, with more room to improvise and more opportunities to misplace a burrito in a neighbor’s yard.” The chaos factor goes up, but so does the laughter, which makes it a better fit for large groups and event-style gatherings.

## Strategies and Tactics for Outdoor Chaos

Here are a few field-tested tips to maximize fun while minimizing chaos-induced injuries:

- Assign a burrito shield. If you’re in a loosely coordinated team, designate a player to act as “shield” during the crucial throws. It’s not about denial; it’s about controlled chaos.
- Communicate loudly, and with style. If you’re going to miss, miss with flair. Shout a retro movie line, a superhero catchphrase, or a dramatic sports metaphor—whatever keeps the energy high and tensions friendly.
- Use wind wisdom. If a breeze is present, throw with the wind at your back or to your side to improve accuracy. Or, purposely throw against the wind to create a ridiculous arc that becomes a spectator sport in its own right.
- Mind the ankles. The outdoor environment invites uneven ground. Keep your feet under you, wear closed-toe shoes, and avoid running in sandals unless you’re prepared for a slapstick moment.
- Hydration is non-negotiable. Laughing while sprinting is exhausting. Stay hydrated, take breaks, and rotate players so no one loses their lunch to the chaos.

These strategies help you squeeze more chaotic joy out of the game without turning a backyard into an obstacle course of bruised egos and sore shins.

Given the sheer volume of chaos this edition invites, you’ll end up with both a sunburned memory and a playlist of ridiculous moments. It’s the kind of party game that works best when you’re with friends who love a good sport and an even better story.
