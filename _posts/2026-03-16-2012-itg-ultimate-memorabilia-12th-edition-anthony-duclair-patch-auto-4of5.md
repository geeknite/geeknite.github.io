## Condition, grading, and how to store this beauty

Grading is a multi-layered beast. For a 2012 card with a 4/5 serial, you’ll want to consider centering, edges, surface, and corners. The autograph should be clean and legible, with minimal pen bleed or signature misalignment. In vintage ITG products, you’ll often see minor off-centering that’s not fatal to a sale but can affect your ultimate grade. If you intend to grade, start with a reputable grading service that specializes in sports cards and has a known track record for vintage issues.

Storage is a simple equation: protect the card, display with care, and keep it away from direct sunlight and humidity. A single top loader inside an acid-free sleeve, then a semi-rigid card holder within a climate-controlled display case, is a minimal but robust approach. If you want to go the extra mile, consider a UV-protective glass case and a desiccant pack to keep moisture at bay. The long-term condition of a patch auto depends on your environment as much as your handling.
