Welcome back, fog-lovers and mask-wearers. If you thought you had solved the city’s enigmas with the base game of City of Mist, brace yourself for a new layer of noir mecha-spaghetti. Mysteries of the City is the first major expansion that dares to rummage through the dresser of the city's secrets and pull out a skeleton wearing a tailored suit and a tragic backstory. This review will take you on a tour of the expansion’s meat-and-bone—or, you know, its metadata and cardboard, which is basically the same thing when you’re playing a game about personas.

If you’re new here, City of Mist is a game about crime, myth, and wardrobe malfunctions. Players step into the roles of Makers, Legendary Identities, and Street-Level Mooks who all have a little bit of magic in their coffee and an even bigger appetite for dramatic entrances. The Mysteries of the City expansion adds a few of those beloved “what if the city itself had a strategy guide?” vibes, along with new mechanical toys to tinker with, some extra drama to mine, and a handful of villains who refuse to stay in their assigned lanes. Let’s dive in.

## Overview
City of Mist: Mysteries of the City expands the core experience by layering in three core areas: new Mystery Types, new Clues, and a handful of optional narrative delves that can be slotted into existing campaigns or run as standalones. Think of it as a mixtape for your city: a DJ mix of new aesthetic palettes, sharper plots, and a handful of cosmic bad moods. The expansion doesn’t replace core mechanics; it braids them more tightly together, so it feels as if your investigator’s identity choices now have a few extra degrees of freedom.

The big bets here are atmosphere and modularity. The designers clearly want you to pick a lane—Gritty Street Action, Cosmic Detective Noir, or Political Soap Opera by way of a moonlit alley—and then let you drive the story with the confidence of someone who’s learned to wield a coffee mug as a signal flare. In practice, this means more masks (the identity framework at the heart of the game), more types of clues, and more secrets to keep from your allies without accidentally tipping off the actual villain you left a clue note on the back of a napkin reading, “I am totally the mastermind.”

If you’ve previously grokked how City of Mist ticks and you’ve got a group that can handle a little extra conspiracy in every session, Mysteries of the City is your expansion. If your table is still figuring out which dice go with which character sheet, this expansion might scale you up too quickly—like giving a grappling hook to a cat who only knows how to meow dramatically. The intention is clear: more mood, more moment-to-moment drama, and a more cinematic play experience. Whether you’ll find that intoxicating or exhausting depends on your tolerance for melodrama and your group’s preference for meta-plotting.

## What’s in the Box
The physical contents of Mysteries of the City are a mix of artful props and robust rule expansions. Here’s a quick inventory and what it does for you on a session-to-session basis:

- New Mystery Type Decks: These are like new color palettes for your character’s internal wizards. They offer fresh prompts for clues, new dilemmas, and alternative arc directions. If you were worried the core game would stagnate after a dozen sessions, these decks promise to twist your ongoing narrative into new shapes.
- Clue Cards Revisited: The base game’s clue system works, but it’s a little too neat sometimes. The expansion sprinkles in expanded clue tiers, including “Obfuscated Clues” and “Blurred Clues,” which reward players for paying attention to the same clue with slightly different interpretations on subsequent sessions. Yes, you’ll be replaying the same coffee shop arc, but this time the clue card’s art contains a subtle hint you didn’t notice in its earlier print run. Sneaky. Sneaky good.
- Expanded Identity Deck: More masks means more moods. The expanded identity framework offers extra Identity Traits and a few “Name Your Own Myth” options that let players sketch a Legendary Identity that’s not quite the same as their existing one. Expect more dramatic entrances, more awkward apologies, and more existential questions about whether the city has a proper sense of humor.
- Campaign Booklet: A compact, modular campaign book with three distinct arcs that players can mix and match. The arcs aren’t just “fight bad guy, win”; they’re designed to thread into existing campaigns or to stand on their own as weekend-long one-shots that feel like feature-length episodes of a noir soap opera.
- GM Screen Inserts: A handful of quick-reference cards and a mini GM screen that makes it easier to juggle new mechanics mid-session. The GM gets a sanity-saver card for managing Clue fatigue, player anxiety about plot holes, and the ever-present threat of a dramatic monologue that runs longer than the session itself.
- Thematic Add-ons: New art prints, tokens, and a few table-friendly props to boost immersion. These artifacts aren’t essential, but they do a lot of heavy lifting for accents of mood—like a scarf that immediately says, “Trust me, I’m a mysterious Mask, and yes, I’ve got secrets to spill.”

From a production quality standpoint, the expansion sticks to City of Mist’s aesthetic: glossy, moody art, a palette-of-neon color scheme, and components that feel sturdy enough to survive a Saint-Louis heatwave if you’re a particularly dramatic GM. The art direction remains cohesive with the core game, which is a relief for players who worry about “expansion drift”: where new elements look nothing like the originals and you end up playing something that feels more like a cosplay show than a game.

## How It Changes Gameplay
Mysteries of the City doesn’t rewrite the rulebook, but it does rewrite the stage directions. The core mechanic—mages wearing human masks, clues forming a chain of cause-and-effect—remains intact, but several layers get turned up to eleven:

- Mask Diversity is Expanded: The Mask system continues to be the heart of the game, and this expansion leans into it with a broader set of Mask Archetypes. You’ll find Masks that lean into corporate intrigue, urban folklore, cyberpunk noir, and mythic tragedy. This gives players more flavor options when building a Legendary Identity, and it also provides GMs with fresh angles for NPCs and antagonists who share a city-wide vibe with the players’ heroes.
- Clues with Consequences: Clues now come with possible consequences that unfold over multiple sessions. You’re not just collecting bits of evidence; you’re planting seeds that sprout into subplots, alliances, or even betrayals. This is where the expansion shines: it rewards long-form storytelling without forcing you into one mega-arc if you don’t want it.
- Narrative Fatigue Management: The GM screen inserts and new clue types help you calibrate narrative intensity. If one player’s Legendary Identity threatens to outshine everyone else, you can lean into a “city-wide crisis” arc that draws in ensemble actions, distributing screen time more equitably and making the payoff feel earned rather than handed to the loudest voice at the table.
- Modularity and Play Styles: The three new arcs are designed to be modular. That means you can run a single arc over a single session or weave all three into a longer campaign. The designers clearly want you to tailor the experience to your group’s tolerances for sprawling meta-stories or tight, punchy mysteries. It’s a welcome flexibility most expansions pretend to offer but rarely deliver on.

For veterans, the expansion adds density without sacrificing clarity. For newcomers, the added options might feel a touch overwhelming at first, but the core principles stay intact: make a character, pick a mystery, and let the city corrupt your moral compass with neon-letter enthusiasm.

## New Mechanics: Masks, Clues, and the Mythos Lattice
To give you a sense of the heartbeat of Mysteries of the City, here are some of the “new toys” you’ll actually use in play:

- The Lattice of Mythos: An overlay mechanic that ties multiple Mystery Types together in a latticework of overlapping narratives. It’s not a hard rule-set; it’s a narrative weather system. It can bring a suspicion that the city itself is a character with a reputation, a moral center, and a terrible sense of humor.
- Expanded Clue Rings: Clues come in rings—core clues, peripheral clues, and echo clues. You can chain three or four of these into a scene, creating momentum that feels cinematic rather than procedural. For players who adore that “we only had a rumor and somehow it exploded into a full-blown conspiracy” vibe, this is your new best friend.
- Mythos-Driven Encounters: A collection of encounter prompts that adamantly refuse to be solved by sheer combat. Expect nonviolent resolutions and moral choices that test your Mask’s alignment against your legend’s ambition.
- Temporal Variation: The expansion introduces a loose time-keeping mechanism that affects clue availability and Mythos resonance. It’s subtle enough to not require a calendar in your living room but structured enough to discourage “we’ll solve it next session” drift.

Put simply, the mechanics serve atmosphere first and computation second. You aren’t chasing numbers; you’re chasing cinematic moments. And that’s where a lot of players find the expansion’s value: you’ll get scenes that feel naturally dramatic rather than artificially constructed to fit a game’s phases.

## Theme, Tone, and Visuals
Mysteries of the City sticks to City of Mist’s moody, neon-lit theme. The expansion leans harder into the city as a living, breathing character with moods, secrets, and the occasional existential crisis. The artwork amplifies this with stylized silhouettes, raindrop textures, and fonts that look like someone drew them with a dripping marker on a foggy window. If you’ve ever wanted to play a game that makes you feel like you stepped into a cyber-noir graphic novel, this expansion gives you that vibe in spades.

The artwork isn’t just window-dressing. It informs how you conceptualize your Masks and your Legendary Identity’s arc. The best art in the expansion communicates a story you can’t quite plot in a single sentence, which is exactly how good noir should feel—like the clues you gather are enough to sink a ship, but not enough to tell you who sank it until Chapter 7. Expect panels, posters, and icons that make your table feel like a dark alley of cinematic possibilities rather than a board game multiplied by a few extra black-and-white drawings.

In terms of accessibility, the expansion remains friendly to both new players and veterans. New players will appreciate the modular arcs and the clearer narrative anchors; veterans will enjoy the denser symbolism and the expanded toolbox for world-building. If you’re a GM who loves staging reveals and dramatic entrances, Mysteries of the City gives you more opportunities to design “why this scene matters” moments without needing a master’s degree in meta-plot planning.

## Components Quality and Setup Time
From a physical standpoint, the components are on par with City of Mist’s usual quality bar. The cards are sturdy, the art is evocative, and the little tokens are easy to keep track of without needing a handheld binary logbook. Setup time climbs a little—this is a larger expansion with more moving parts—but the payoff is a session that feels more cinematic and less like a structured puzzle you’re expected to solve in 62 minutes.

If you’ve ever asked yourself, “Do I really need a GMs’ screen insert with more reference cards?” the answer is yes, yes you do, because it saves mental energy and lets you focus on drenching your players in atmosphere rather than flipping pages. The expansion’s inserts are designed to cut down reaction time so you can step into the spotlight and deliver the melodrama with maximum effect.

## Campaign Play, Solo, and Group Dynamics
Mysteries of the City is surprisingly flexible. Here are a few play formats you can adopt:

- One-shot Mystery Nights: Run a single arc as a complete story in a single long session. This is great if you’re onboarding new players or if you’ve got a night where everyone’s schedule looks like a broken compass.
- Short Campaigns: Chain two or three arcs with transitional callbacks. It’s a natural fit for players who enjoy long-form storytelling but don’t want to dive into a year-long commitment.
- Long Campaigns with Beats: Use the three arcs as weighted beats in a longer campaign. The city itself evolves with each arc, providing continuity for characters who have chosen to stick around and see what destiny the street has drafted for them.
- Solo or Two-Player Variants: The expansion supports solo play and two-player variants, though the experience will skew toward “investigation drama” rather than “freewheeling duo combat.” If you want something closer to a noir mystery novel you can play with a friend, this works well—but it’s still very much a story-driven cooperative game rather than a solitary puzzle game.

The narrative focus suits groups that enjoy roleplaying, improvisation, and melodrama. If your table loves crunch and competitive anxiety, Mysteries of the City’ll feel meatier, but it may not be the first choice for a fast-and-light evening.

## Balancing, Accessibility, and Table Feel
One potential pitfall of expansions like this is “bloat.” If you deploy every new mechanic at once, you can end up with a table that spends more time consulting the rulebook than making dramatic entrances. Mysteries of the City avoids this trap by giving you optional modules. The difference in play time is real, but it doesn’t feel arbitrary; it feels like a narrative choice—“Are we addressing the city’s bigger crisis now, or should we resolve this personal mystery first?” The answer is generally: both, in alternating sessions.

That said, there’s a learning curve. If you’re introducing new players, consider a two-session arc: the first session introduces the base mechanics with the new clues at a minimal level; the second session goes deeper into the expanded Masks and the nested mysteries. It’s a gentle ramp that allows players to acclimate to the city’s new moods without feeling overwhelmed.

Accessibility-wise, the expansion is consistent with the core: it uses clear terminology, evocative art, and a storytelling framework that rewards narration and collaboration. It may require a few read-throughs to internalize the Lattice concept and the new clue rings, but once you’re past those initial pages, you’ll find the mechanics flow with a cinematic rhythm rather than a procedural drumbeat.

## Scenarios, Setup, and Session Flow
The three arcs in Mysteries of the City each offer a unique flavor of mystery:

- Arc One: The Neon Chapel – A mystery rooted in urban legends, with a mythic seed at its core. Expect conspiracies that link a spectral church with a corporate heist and a rumor about a masked choir that can bend time in tiny, disorienting ways.
- Arc Two: Mirror Network – A political-noir arc that pits factions against each other, with the city’s underbelly acting as a chessboard. Cluffs ripple out in unexpected directions as players discover that some masks are easier to control than others when the city’s power grid flickers in the rain.
- Arc Three: The Quiet Engine – A more personal mystery that looks inward at the psychological engines powering the investigators themselves. This arc tests motives, reveals secrets, and draws players into a web of personal consequences that feels both intimate and epic.

Each arc comes with a set of GMs’ aids: encounter prompts, scene suggestions, and pacing guidelines designed to keep the dramatic tempo high without devolving into “one more plot twist” fatigue. The arcs also include optional “table-ready” props you can print or improvise with minimal prep. If you like your storytelling lean and moody, these arcs deliver a satisfying glow rather than a full-on flashbang of events.

If you’re worried about content sensitivity or triggering themes, the expansion handles this with the same care City of Mist has shown in the past: provide clear content warnings in the arc introductions and offer a few safe-fallback prompts that let players opt out of the hardest material without derailing the session.

## The Final Verdict: Is Mysteries of the City Worth It?
If you’re a fan of the base game and you crave more flavor, more mood, and more ways to make the city feel alive, Mysteries of the City is a strong addition. It doesn’t reinvent the wheel, but it does install a better-on-runsocial network around your table. The new Mask archetypes, expanded Clue structures, and modular arcs mean you can tailor the experience to your group’s desires—whether you want punchy, session-length mysteries or sprawling campaigns about the city’s soul unraveling under neon rain.

There are a few caveats worth noting:

- Setup Time: Expect more pre-session organization if you plan to run multiple arcs in a row. The payoff, however, is a more immersive, cinematic feel.
- Learning Curve: New players should not feel overwhelmed, but a little patience goes a long way. A pre-session read-through helps, as does a short intro game that showcases the new mechanics in action.
- Group Fit: If your group loves crunch and direct conflict, you may find the mysteries more faction- and dialogue-driven. That’s not a flaw; it’s a design choice that aligns with the noir vibe.

If you’re asking whether Mysteries of the City “delivers” in terms of atmosphere and storytelling mechanics, the answer is a confident yes. It’s not a radical departure from the core game, but it’s a substantial upgrade in mood, scale, and narrative flexibility. It’s the sort of expansion that makes you want to queue up an endless cascade of sessions because you discovered a couple more doors in a city that’s always two steps away from a midnight monologue about fate.

## Recommendations by Player Type
- New to City of Mist: Start with the core game, then bring in the expansion once you’ve got a few sessions under your belt. The modular nature helps a new group find its rhythm without being overwhelmed.
- Veteran players who love story-driven play: This expansion is a treasure chest of narrative options. You’ll find yourself weaving arcs together with surprising ease and still finding fresh dramatic beats to land at the table.
- GM-focused groups: Use the GM screens and encounter prompts to drive sessions that feel cinematic rather than tactical. The new tools help you manage the pacing and the mood without burning through your energy reserves.
- Collectors and aesthetics-first players: The visual upgrade and the physical components are worth the price of admission on their own. If you’re into table presence, Mysteries of the City looks and feels fantastic.

If you’re deciding today, a practical approach is to pick one of the three arcs you’re most curious about and run it as a two-session mini-campaign. If you fall in love with the rhythm, you can sprinkle in the other arcs gradually, letting the city evolve with each new mystery you unlock.
