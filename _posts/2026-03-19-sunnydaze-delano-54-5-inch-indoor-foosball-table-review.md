## Design and Build
### Cabinet and Frame
The Delano features a classic foosball cabinet built for the daily grind of family chaos. The cabinet is described as a robust hardwood veneer with a stately, retro vibe that says, We take our foosball seriously, but not so seriously that we stop ordering pizza mid-game. The weight is substantial but manageable for a table that measures roughly 54.5 inches long; enough to create a sense of grandeur without requiring a crane to relocate to the guest room. The legs include adjustable levelers, which is a fancy way of saying you can tilt your ego to compensate for the occasional off-kilter shot. If you have a sloped living room floor, these will save you from a perpetual tilt-a-whirl that makes you look like a sad pirate.

### Rods and Players
Typical home foosball tables come with 11 or 16 players per side on caulk-and-plastic stars. The Delano uses metal rods with standard bearings and non-slip handles. They glide with a satisfying click, reminiscent of opening a treasure chest after a long quest. You get a reasonable amount of torque without having to bench press the table. The players themselves are durable enough to take a few fast breaks and emergency penalty kicks, yet not so heavy that your back will file a complaint with the furniture gods. It should be noted that the rods can require lubrication after several months of aggressive play, but that is a minor maintenance ritual that serious foosballers perform with the solemnity of a wizard casting a spell.

### Playing Surface and Rails
The Delano offers a standard width playing surface with rail systems designed to keep the ball in play and the banter in high gear. The rails are wrapped in a way that minimizes ball bounce, but still provides enough unpredictable hops to remind you that this is a game of skill and not a guaranteed homerun. The surface texture is chosen to balance grip and speed; enough to allow sneaky pass plays but not so sticky that you end up with a ball that refuses to move. The net effect is a smooth, reliable ball trajectory that lets you fake a pass and then instantly score a goal when your opponent looks away.

### Maintenance and Longevity
To keep the Delano performing its best, you’ll want to do a few regular maintenance rituals. Wipe down the playing surface after intense sessions to remove sweat and pizza grease, and lubricate the rods every few months with a light machine oil to keep them sliding smoothly. Tighten the leg levelers if you notice wobble during dramatic celebrations. A quick check of the bolts every couple of weeks will help ensure you don’t end up playing on a loose ship that creaks every time someone makes a dramatic save. With proper care, the Delano should deliver years of entertainment without turning into a squeaky museum piece.
