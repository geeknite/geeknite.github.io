## What is Senjutsu?

Senjutsu: Battle for Japan is a strategic, skirmish-style board game set in the tumultuous period of feudal Japan. Players take on the roles of legendary samurai warriors, each with unique abilities and techniques, striving for honor and dominance. The All In Deluxe Kickstarter Edition elevates the game to new heights with exclusive components, miniatures, and scenarios that promise endless tactical engagements.

### Components

The All In Deluxe Kickstarter Edition is packed with jaw-dropping components. From intricately designed miniatures to beautifully illustrated cards and game boards, every piece screams quality and attention to detail.

- **Miniatures**: Lovingly crafted samurai miniatures that are ready to duel. (Just keep them away from any pets that think they're chew toys.)
- **Cards**: High-quality cards depicting various abilities, actions, and special techniques. Shuffle carefully, or you might get paper cuts that make you look like you've been in a sword fight.
- **Game Board**: Double-sided boards featuring iconic locations of Japan, including the serene Cherry Blossom Grove and the ominous Battlefield of Shadows.
- **Tokens and Markers**: Used to track progress, health, and various effects during the game.

## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setting up Senjutsu is like preparing for an epic showdown. Lay out the game board, distribute cards, and position your miniatures. Choose your samurai and prepare for battle. (Warning: setting up may require a ceremonial tea break to maintain focus.)

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, players choose actions from their hand of cards. These actions could range from attacking and defending to utilizing special techniques that can turn the tide of battle. The key is to anticipate your opponent's moves and strike when they least expect it.

### Combat

Combat in Senjutsu is a blend of strategic planning and swift execution. Players engage in duels using their cards and miniatures, with each attack and defense requiring careful consideration. Did you plan an epic counterattack only to realize you misread your opponent's move? Welcome to Senjutsu, where even the best warriors can be humbled.
