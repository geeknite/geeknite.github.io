### Condition: Near Mint NM

“Near Mint” is the collector's shorthand for “look, I didn't throw this at a wall after opening it, but I might have allowed a tiny corner ding.” NM in Pokémon card terms typically means:

- Card is bright with minimal edge wear
- Surface is clean with no major scratches
- Corners are sharp, though some minor whitening on the edges can occur in transit

To verify NM, you’ll want to examine:

- The holo surface for scratching
- The corners under bright light
- The card’s centering (often a tiny tilt can disqualify NM in some grading standards)
- The back for any whitening or nicks

If you want to know how experts grade, check out the standard grade scales used by PSA and Beckett, but for this piece we’re focusing on the vibe rather than the 9.5+ to 10 rankings.

### Care and Display: How to Keep It Shiny

If you’re serious about maintaining NM condition, you’ll want to store this card in a safe environment. Here are a few practical tips:

- Use a high-clarity sleeve and a semi-rigid toploader to preserve the holo surface
- Keep away from direct sunlight; the Black Flame aesthetic looks cooler in the field than under UV light
- Store in a climate-controlled binder with acid-free pages
- Avoid loose coins, action figures, or other magnets of doom in the near locale

The goal here is to preserve the aesthetic: the moment you remove this from its packaging, you want the flame to look like a cinematic vision rather than a sunburn in a binder.
