## Assembly, Setup, and The Quickstart Adventure
If you’ve built a traditional desk before, the Eternal might require a different kind of patience: you’ll be assembling a modular giant that has a royal affinity for anchoring to the floor and resisting the impulse to pretend you’re on a spaceship. Expect a multi-hour project if you’re doing it solo; with a helper or two, you’ll still be diabolically tired but more cheerful about the results.

### What’s in the Box
- A robust frame with crossbracing.
- The long, elegant desk surface in oak-black finish.
- Central screen housing and mounting hardware.
- Cable management accessories: clips, channels, grommets.
- A set of Allen keys and warranty documents.

### Assembly Process (High-Level)
1) Clear the space and check the floor for level. The last thing you want is a wobbly central screen trying to host a five-person video call. 2) Start with the central spine; it’s the backbone of the whole operation. 3) Attach the long desk panels on either side, aligning with the spine. 4) Install cable channels and grommets. 5) Mount the central screen housing, ensuring it’s securely anchored. 6) Attach the side workstation modules and adjust foot-level height and leveling feet. 7) Connect power, test USB hubs, and boot the monitoring suite. 8) Calibrate keyboard trays and ergonomic positioning. 9) Invite the team to sit down, yell “Welcome to the future!” and pretend you didn’t just mutter a nerdy software license agreement.

Time estimates vary: a flush, team-assisted assembly can take a full afternoon, while a meticulous solo build could tempt you into a long weekend. The warranty is usually explicit about weight limits and the necessity of proper leveling. If you ignore these, you risk the central screen tilting, a scenario that is both comedic and sad at the same time.

### Practical Tips for Setup
- Use a spirit level to verify the central surface is perfectly flat. A 2–3 mm deviation across the length is acceptable, but anything more is a drama you don’t want in your stand-up meeting. 
- Pre-plan monitor and keyboard tray placement. I know it sounds obvious, but you’ll regret not allocating the space when the first pair sits down and tries to cram their 32-inch ultrawide into a 780 mm depth.
- Run the cable channels before docking the devices. You’ll thank yourself later for not having to manage nine power bricks on a Monday morning.
- Test everybody’s seating height and monitor tilt. The goal is reduce neck strain and ensure that the person at seat five doesn’t have to crane their neck like a sea creature to see the main screen.

## Cable Management: The Spaghetti Tamed (Mostly)
Cable chaos is the archenemy of big desks. The Eternal desk acknowledges this by providing a robust set of cable management tools. Expect:
- Grommets and cutouts aligned along the length for power, data, and peripherals.
- A central trough or channels to route cables away from the surface and floor.
- Velcro straps and clamps to keep clusters tidy, so that when someone spins the chair, you don’t witness a dramatic cascade of dongles and dongster adapters.
- Optional modular clips to secure cables to monitor arms and keyboard trays.
Reality check: even with all of this, you’ll still see a rogue cable or two hiding under a chair. That’s not a design flaw; it’s a sign that people truly love their gadgets and refuse to live a cable-free life. The important thing is that the structure is robust enough to handle the occasional spoonful of noodles that somehow ends up on your desk and the unpredictable habit of human cable-chasing that occurs during crisis debugging.
