## Materials and Craftsmanship

### The Hand-Tufted Process
Hand-tufted rugs sit somewhere between machine precision and artisanal charm. The artisan uses a tufting gun to push yarn through a fabric backing, creating a tight, dense surface. The strawberry motif is achieved through careful color changes and precise cutting. The result is a rug that preserves its pattern under foot traffic, resisting fuzz and pilling more effectively than a bargain bin rug from a big-box store. If you’re a data-driven person, think of it as a high-quality algorithm that balances complexity (pattern) with usability (durability).

### Wool Quality
Wool has a natural resilience that synthetic fibers often mimic poorly. The wool used here offers good loft, resilience to flattening, and a pleasant warmth. It’s not scratchy like some cheaper blends; you’ll notice a soft, almost cloud-like feel underfoot. Wool also hides minor stains better than you’d expect thanks to the natural lanolin in wool fibers. Note: that doesn’t mean you can spill a smoothie and pretend nothing happened. Spills still need prompt attention.

### Finishing Touches
The edges are clean, with a reinforced border that helps the rug maintain its shape over time. The backside shows a sturdy latex or cotton backing (depending on batch), ensuring the rug stays flat on the floor and doesn’t curl like a frightened cat when vacuumed. The overall craftsmanship is visible in the crisp motif edges and uniform pile height.

Wool’s natural resilience helps with minor stains. Blot spills quickly with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can embed stains deeper. For deeper clean, you’ll want a mild wool-safe cleaner and a little patience. Regular vacuuming with a beater bar set on a low setting helps keep the pile crisp. If you’re a nerd about maintenance, schedule a quarterly deep-clean approach like you would for a PC tower: open up the fans (lift the rug a bit) and let the dust have a day off.

## Care and Maintenance

### Routine Care
- Vacuum regularly with the beater bar on a low setting or use a hand-held vacuum to avoid flattening the pile.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing in the stain.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and fading from sunlight.

### Dealing with Stains
For common stains (juice, wine, coffee), blot, then treat with a wool-safe cleaner. Test in a small inconspicuous area first. For more stubborn stains, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in hand-tufted wool.

### Longevity Tips
- Use a quality rug pad to prevent slipping and to extend the rug’s life.
- Keep sunlight exposure in check; UV light can fade colors over long periods.
