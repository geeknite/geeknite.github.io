## Maintenance and durability: living with a synthetic rug
Maintenance is where synthetic fibers often shine and sometimes hiss at the same time. The Cottage Marina is designed to be stain resistant and easy to clean, which is the dream scenario for households with coffee spills and red wine experiments that go sideways.

### Cleaning basics
- Vacuum regularly using a beater bar on low to avoid damaging the pile. Don’t go full infrared vacuum mode unless you like scuffed fibers.
- For spills, blot rather than rub. A mild detergent solution or a gentle carpet cleaner works well on most common stains. Always test a small inconspicuous area first to confirm colorfastness.
- For tougher stains, you can often use a gentle scrub with a soft-bristled brush, followed by a thorough rinse with clean water. Avoid bleaching agents unless the stain is truly heroic and the rug is labeled bleach-safe, which is rarely the case for synthetic textiles.

### Pet and kid scenarios
- Pet accidents are a fact of life in many homes. The synthetic composition is typically more resistant to moisture penetration than natural fibers, making spot treatment more forgiving. Quick action helps prevent lingering odors.
- Kids will drag in all sorts of debris from play sessions. The rug’s color palette helps hide common dirt, and the surface is generally easy to wipe or vacuum clean without special equipment.

### Longevity and wear expectations
With light-to-moderate traffic, a rug like Cottage Marina can remain visually appealing for several years. The key is rotation, avoiding direct sun in bright rooms to prevent uneven fading, and pairing with a防滑 rug pad to reduce movement and piling wear over time.

## Installation tips and sizing: fitting it into your space
If you are DIY-curious, here are practical steps to get the most out of a Cottage Marina rug in both custom and standard sizes.

### Measuring like a pro
- Measure width and length for the exact space you want to fill. Remember to account for furniture clearance, especially around coffee tables and seating clusters.
- If you plan to place the rug under a sofa, measure the seating area to ensure a clean border around the furniture, not a random patch where the rug stops just at the armrest.

### Room dynamics and visual balance
- A larger rug can anchor a seating area, but you don’t want the rug so large that it dwarfs the room. Conversely, a rug that’s too small can create a choppy feeling. The 8x10 sweet spot often works for mid-to-large living rooms with standard sofa setups.
- In open-plan spaces, consider extending the rug under at least the front legs of the main seating to unify the area without making it feel crowded.

### Installation steps
1. Clear the room and remove furniture if possible.
2. Vacuum the floor to remove debris that could snag fibers.
3. Place the rug in the intended orientation and adjust edges so they align with walls or furniture lines.
4. If you are using a rug pad, cut it to fit and place beneath the rug to reduce slipping.
5. Return furniture gradually and ensure the rug stays flat and smooth as you bring items back into position.

### Shipping and handling notes
Custom orders typically require more lead time than standard 8x10 rugs. If you need a deadline met for a move or event, consider whether a standard size might get you there on time, or if you can pleasantly accommodate the wait with a transitional floor solution like a smaller rug layered with a larger one.
