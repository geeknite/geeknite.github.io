The Accessories Bundle is designed to translate that mood into tangible tools: cards you can handle, tokens you can lift, and mementos that prompt scenes faster than you can say sociodrama. ## GM tips and using Fortune Row to pace a session
Here are practical ways to get the most from the bundle without turning your table into a prop museum:

- Pre-select a handful of Fate Cards that tie to your current arc. Keep them within reach on the table as a visual cue; it reduces mid-scene rummaging and keeps the pace snappy.
- Treat Mementos as evolving motifs. When a player interacts with a Memento, consider making it a recurring symbol that returns in later scenes. It creates a satisfying sense of cause and effect.
- Let Location Tokens drive scene structure. Use them to signal the location’s mood, a change in faction presence, or the emergence of a new rival, all without needing to narrate every detail aloud.
- Lean into micro-scenes. If the session stalls, a Fate Card can prompt a 5- to 10-minute flashback or a quick, dramatic confrontation that reveals a secret tie to the present problem.
- Embrace the props, but don’t worship them. If a specific Memento clashes with a player’s character concept, adapt on the fly; the power of the bundle is in its adaptability, not its rigidity.
