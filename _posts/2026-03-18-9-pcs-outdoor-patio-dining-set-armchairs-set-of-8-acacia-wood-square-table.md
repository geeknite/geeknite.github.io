## Assembly and Maintenance Tips
- Pre-assembly prep: Check all hardware before you begin. It’s a time-saver to sort bolts, washers, and caps into labeled containers. It also saves you from the existential crisis of realizing a critical piece is missing once you’ve already attached the rest of the chair.
- Fastening: Hand-tighten first, then snug with a screwdriver. Over-tightening can lead to wood cracking, and nobody wants to see a chair crack under the weight of family enthusiasm.
- Finishing: Apply a season-appropriate wood sealer or furniture oil to preserve the natural color and protect the surface from water damage and coffee stains. A little maintenance goes a long way toward keeping the set looking “new room in a new home” rather than “veteran of three summers.”
- Cleaning: Wipe with a damp cloth and mild soap; avoid abrasive cleaners that might dull the grain. For stubborn spots, a soft brush and a mix of water and vinegar can work wonders without harming the finish.
