## Mounting Options: Clamp-on vs Screw-on
One of the standout features of the VIVO panel is the dual mounting approach. Depending on your desk design and your tolerance for hardware, you can choose clamp-on for quick installation or screw-on for a more permanent, rock-solid setup.
- Clamp-on: This is the go-to option if you’re renting or if you regularly rearrange your workspace. The clamps grip the desk edge without drilling holes, and most desks with a 0.75" to 1.5" thickness can handle them. In practice, the clamps need to be snug, and you may want to test on a glass-topped desk just to see if the edge protection is up to the task. Users report minimal wobble when the clamps are fully engaged, but if you’re flinging your laptop around like a caffeinated raccoon, you might notice the occasional jiggle.
- Screw-on: The screw-on variant offers greater rigidity, which is useful if you have a heavier monitor setup or if you’re concerned about desk sways during those dramatic Zoom calls. Drilling into a desk is not a decision to be taken lightly, but on a solid desk the screws bite into the core and the panel feels anchored. If you’re sharing a desk or you want a more permanent privacy barrier, this is the way to go.

### Pros and Cons at a Glance
- Clamp-on Pros: Quick install, no holes, great for renters, easily removable.
- Clamp-on Cons: Potential wobble on soft edges; limited to desk thickness range.
- Screw-on Pros: Maximum stability, no wobble, more permanent fix.
- Screw-on Cons: Requires drilling; may impact desk warranty; harder to relocate.

If you’re undecided, a good rule of thumb is: use clamp-on for portable or shared desks and screw-on for your primary workstation. For additional rigidity in corner setups or L-shaped desks, I recommend locking the panel with the screws to minimize any iffy movements.

## Installation and Compatibility Notes
Here’s the practical rundown to get your panel mounted without cursing in the process:
- Desk thickness compatibility: clamps suit roughly 0.75" to 1.5". If your desk is above or below that range, you might need to opt for the screws only or use shims. Do not crimp the clamps so hard you crush the desk edge; the goal is a snug grip without desk dent drama.
- Edge clearance: ensure your keyboard tray or trackpad area isn’t blocked. The 60-inch width leaves room on both sides for peripherals, but they’re not invisible plan your monitor placement accordingly.
- Surface finish: inspect the desk edge for splinters or irregularities; the clamps will press against the edge and could cause scratching if you’re not careful. A thin protective pad can help here.
- Cable management: the panel itself doesn’t have built-in grommets, so you’ll want to plan cable routes along the back edge. Some users retrofit small cable clips along the bottom rail for clean aesthetics.
