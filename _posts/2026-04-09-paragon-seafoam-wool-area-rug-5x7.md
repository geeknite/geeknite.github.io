## Durability, Longevity, and Maintenance

Wool is a classic for a reason. It handles traffic well and tends to recover after compression—a big deal if you’ve got kids, a dog, or that roommate who insists on rearranging the furniture every other weekend.

### Cleaning and Spill Management
- Immediate action: blot spills with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing; friction can push stains deeper into the wool.
- Stain treatment: use a mild wool-safe detergent solution and test on an inconspicuous area first. For delicate soils, consider a professional cleaning service with wool rug experience.
- Deep clean cadence: every 12-24 months, depending on traffic, exposure to sunlight, and household pets. Regular vacuuming helps prevent grit from becoming embedded in the weave.

### Vacuuming and Shedding
Wool rugs can shed lightly at first as the fibers settle; this is typical for natural fibers and should taper off with continued use and proper vacuuming. Use a low to medium suction setting and avoid aggressive agitation that could damage the pile.

### Sun Exposure and Color Stability
Direct sun can fade colors over time. If your rug sits in a sunny spot, rotate it periodically or use window treatments to minimize UV exposure. This simple habit can majorly preserve the seafoam hue across years of living room life.

### Pet and Child Considerations
Wool is naturally resilient to wear, but claws can snag fibers. If you have pets with sharp nails, keep them trimmed and consider a high-quality underlay to reduce wear on the bottom surface. For kids, the rug’s surface is comfortable for playtime but be mindful of spills; quick blotting helps maintain appearance.

## Underlay, Placement, and Safety

An underlay is not just a good idea—it’s a practical necessity in many rooms. A non-slip rug pad keeps the rug from sliding away during enthusiastic dance breaks or when you chase the remote under the sofa. It also elevates comfort and helps distribute wear evenly.

### Underlay Recommendations
- Type: Non-slip, latex-free or latex-backed versions work well on hardwood and laminate floors.
- Size: The pad should be slightly smaller than the rug to prevent edge curling and to allow the rug to breathe at the edges.
- Thickness: 1/4" to 1/2" is typical; thicker pads are nice for bedrooms but can obscure the rug’s face if they’re too thick.

### Placement Tips
Center the rug in your space to create a visual anchor; align seating so front legs sit atop the rug’s surface. If your furniture is heavy, consider placing a sofa or couch on the rug to prevent curling and ensure a balanced composition. You don’t want a situation where a couch’s footings are half-on, half-off, creating a “rug vestigial” effect, which is not a design trend anyone actually asked for.

## The Environmental and Ethical Angle

Wool is a natural, renewable fiber. Wool production can be sustainable when sourced responsibly, with attention to animal welfare and land stewardship. If sustainability is a factor in your purchasing decisions, look for dye certifications and supplier transparency. Wool’s biodegradability means it has the potential to be recycled or repurposed at the end of its life, reducing long-term landfill impact.

As with any textile, the full lifecycle matters—from how the sheep are treated to how the product is manufactured and transported. Supporting brands that publish clear sustainability statements can help you align purchases with your values, whether you’re a nerd, a yodeling bard, or just a person who loves clean air and good design.
