## Safety, authenticity, and storage
### Authenticity concerns
Bulk lots can be a mixed bag when it comes to authenticity because the main intent is the content mix rather than a brand-new box of pristine product. If you’re buying from a reputable seller with return policies, your risk is much lower. Before you unwrap, examine the seller’s rating, photos, and any posted decklists or inventory details. If you spot obvious red flags (like unusually cheap price, heavily damaged packaging, or inconsistent art styles across holos), trust your gut and step away.

### Storage tips for longevity
- Sleeves: Use standard Dragon Ball-backed sleeves for protection. Consider soft sleeves if you want extra padding for holo cards.
- Deck boxes: A rigid, lidded box keeps your 150-card stash intact and prevents bending indices.
- Avoid heat and humidity: A dry, cool space helps preserve the holo shine and prevents warping.
- Regular sorting: Trade or exchange duplicates to keep your collection fresh while you identify the best cards to sleeve into their final decks.

### Handling tips
Holographic cards are flashy but can be slippery in the heat of a tabletop clash. Keep a steady grip, avoid slamming stack transfers, and use soft paper towels to wipe hands between plays if you’re doing a long session. And yes, you can pretend you’re a DBS character while you do it—just don’t try a ki blast with your sleeves on.
