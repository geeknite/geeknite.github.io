## How Skip-Bo Works

The heart of the game is the stock pile mechanic. Each player starts with a stock pile face down. The top card is turned face up to begin the pile. On your turn you draw cards from the draw pile to your hand, observe the top stock card, and attempt to place cards onto the four building piles in the center. Building piles must be filled in ascending order from 1 to 12; once a pile is completed it disappears and you create a new one. Wild Skip-Bo cards can stand in for any number, which adds a little drama when the right card is elusive.

Skip-Bo is a system that rewards forward planning but never forgets the chaos factor. You can glimpse the outcomes you want, yet the order of the cards in the draw pile means you often improvise when a wild card doesn’t show up at the exact moment you hoped. This is where the game earns its heart: you constantly weigh the risk of using a wild card now against the confidence that you can fetch a better replacement on your next draw. The setup time is minimal, and the learning curve is nearly flat for anyone who has handled a deck of numbered cards before. 

## Turn Structure

- Draw phase: At the start of your turn you draw up to five cards from the draw pile to your hand, depending on house rules
- Play phase: You may play any card from your hand or the top card of your stock pile to a building pile, or move cards between discard piles if your rules allow
- Continue until you cannot or choose not to play
- End phase: You may discard the remaining cards in your hand to your own discard piles
- Victory condition: The first player to empty their stock pile wins the round

To keep things brisk, our table often uses a five-card draw and requires that you at least place one card on a building pile if possible. In a friendly crowd, you might allow additional tricks like moving a card from one discard pile to another as a single action or permitting a quick peek at the next card in a draw pile before committing. These small tweaks can dramatically affect the tempo and the satisfaction of a well-timed play.

### Basic Strategy for Beginners

- Prioritize stock pile management: your stock pile is your lifeline in Skip-Bo
- Plan your builds: keep the four building piles flexible so you can slip a card down when the moment arises
- Use wilds judiciously: reserve them for a critical moment when you can take a big chunk of stock piles down
- Don’t overcommit: avoid using down cards unless you can safely replace them with a new stock card on your next turn
- Keep the pace: if you stall too long you risk giving your opponents the chance to steal the wind

### Advanced Game Plans

- Opponents' tendencies: try to deduce what numbers they are eyeing and compensate by saving or discarding those numbers
- Card counting lightly: keep track of how many of each number you have seen and what is likely left in the draw
- The art of the discard: use discards to manage what you expose on each turn
- Resource balancing: sometimes you should delay a move to maintain a backup plan for your next draw
