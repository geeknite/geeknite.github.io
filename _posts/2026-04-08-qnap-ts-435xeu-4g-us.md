## Software and Features: The OS That Keeps on Giving
QNAP has a long-standing habit of delivering a feature-rich operating system that appeals to enthusiasts and professionals alike. The TS-435XeU-4G-US ships with QNAP's OS, which is considered by many to be one of the most capable NAS platforms on the market. The OS combines a friendly interface with deep configuration options for power users.

### The OS arms race: QTS vs QuTS hero
QNAP typically ships devices with QTS, their general-purpose NAS operating system, with the option to upgrade to QuTS hero for enhanced data integrity features using the ZFS-based pool. If your primary tasks include backups, media libraries, and light virtualization, QTS is more than enough. If you crave advanced data integrity features like snapshots at scale and more aggressive data protection, QuTS hero might be worth evaluating, especially if your workflow relies on strong data preservation guarantees.

### Core apps and services for the TS-435XeU-4G-US
- File sharing and sync with Qsync and SMB/AFP/NFS support.
- Backup options that cover local, remote, and cloud destinations.
- Media applications for photos, music, and video libraries with integration to Plex and other players.
- Container Station for lightweight virtualization and microservices.
- Virtualization Station if you need a more complete VM experience, though you may hit resource limits with only 4 GB RAM in certain configurations.
- Snapshot and BTRFS options for data protection and versioning (if supported by the OS version).
- Security features including 2-step verification, VPN access, and encryption options to protect your data.

If you want a deeper dive into app selection and optimization, the OS has a broad ecosystem. The App Center can be a little overwhelming at first glance, but with a little exploration you can tailor the system to your use cases without becoming overwhelmed by the options. A few favorites include Container Station for running Docker containers, Virtualization Station for light VMs, and QVR Pro for surveillance tasks when you want to turn the NAS into a surveillance hub.

### Media and streaming capabilities
For a home-lab as well as a home theater, the TS-435XeU-4G-US can function as a media hub. Media libraries can be organized in Photo Station, Video Station, and Music Station, or integrated into Plex for broader compatibility with client devices. If you plan to transcode on the NAS, you will want to consider your CPU power and RAM. Transcoding is resource-intensive, and a four-core CPU with 4 GB of RAM can handle basic 1080p streaming; 4K transcoding is more challenging and may require fewer simultaneous streams or a lower quality profile.

### Docker, containers, and virtualization
Container Station brings Docker-based workloads into the NAS ecosystem without the need for separate servers. You can deploy small services, home automation, or lightweight web apps directly from the NAS. For more isolated environments, Virtualization Station can run lightweight VMs, though you should monitor resource usage. In a small home-lab, these capabilities let you experiment with DevOps workflows, test environments, and sandboxed apps without dedicating a separate computer to the task.

#### Practical tips for software usage
- Start with a solid backup plan before you introduce containers or VMs. The best way to protect your data is to have redundant backups and known recovery procedures.
- Use snapshots if your OS supports them, especially before doing major updates or installing beta software in containers.
- Regularly review app permissions and network exposure. Keep containers minimal and only expose what you need to keep your attack surface small.
