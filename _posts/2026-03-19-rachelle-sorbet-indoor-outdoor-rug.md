## Materials, Build, and Eco-Friendliness

The Sorbet rug is made for the long haul, using fibers that balance softness with the resilience you expect from an outdoor-ready textile. The exact blend is often marketed as a polyamide/propylene mix with a recycled plastic content that gives it a lower carbon footprint than your typical single-use textile. In practice, this means you get a rug that feels comfortable under bare feet and resists the rot you dread from aggressive sun exposure.

From the durability angle, the rug holds up well against typical outdoor life: sun, rain, and the occasional stroller pass. The pile is dense enough to resist flattening from daily traffic but not so dense that it feels like you are stepping on a carpeted floorboard. The result is a surface that remains comfortable for sitting, kneeling, or performing questionable puzzle-solving marathons in the living room.

Cleaning is where this rug shines. Most spills wipe away with a little blotting and a damp cloth. For tougher stains, a gentle soap solution and a rinse will usually do the trick. The key is to avoid harsh bleach or abrasive scrubbing, which could compromise the texture. For outdoor use, a quick spray with a garden hose and a bit of sun-drying will typically refresh the fibers without requiring a full wash cycle. Pro tip: do not leave it soggy for too long; dampness loves to accumulate in the corners and invites the microscopic party crashers we call mold and mildew.

If you are concerned about sustainability, you should know that many retailers emphasize the recycled content and low-waste manufacturing processes used in production. While no rug is a perfect eco-pod, Sorbet toes the line between comfort and responsibility, which is a vibe we can get behind without turning the room into a guilt trip about textiles.

## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance

Maintenance is the boring-but-critical part of rug ownership, and Sorbet understands this without sighing about it. Routine care includes vacuuming to remove loose grit and occasional shaking to refresh the fibers. If you have kids or pets, you will likely do more spot-cleaning than the average adult. In practice, most stains respond well to a mild detergent solution, a gentle scrub with a soft brush, and a thorough rinse. Avoid bleach or solvents that could break down the fibers or cause color bleeding.

For outdoor use, the key is to prevent water from sitting on the surface for extended periods. If water pools in low spots, relocate the rug or adjust the placement so it dries evenly. Mildew is not fashionable, even if your inner goth appreciates a moody vibe. A quick cleanse with a vinegar-water solution can help whiten the lighter palettes if you notice dulling or staining over time.

Storage when not in use is another consideration. If you expect heavy off-season storage, roll the rug rather than folding to prevent creases. Store in a dry, cool place away from direct sunlight to preserve color and texture for the next season of lounging and guest-hosting experiments.
