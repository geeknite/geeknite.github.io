## Burn, Wick, and Throw: How It Performs
The Meadow Mist candle uses a cotton wick and soy wax blend. The burn line is clean and even on initial burn, with a recommended burn time of 3 to 4 hours per session to achieve an even melt pool on the first burn. This is important because a poor first burn can create tunneling, and tunneling is basically the opposite of field magic. It means your candle would rather be a candle than a meadow, which is a tragedy we do not want to endorse.

In terms of throw, you can expect a pleasant, room-filling scent in a typical living space of about 250-350 square feet. ## Care, Burn, and Safety Tips for Meadow Mist
Candle care is part ritual, part maintenance. Here are a few tips to maximize Meadow Mist performance and safety:

- First burn matters: Allow the melt pool to reach the edges to prevent tunneling. A properly cured first burn means fewer trips to the repair shop of your own patience.
- Trim the wick to 1/4 inch before each burn if necessary. A long wick can cause an uneven flame and excess soot, which is not the vibe you want when you are trying to keep your coffee table presentable during a livestream.
- Keep away from drafts. Drafts turn a meadow into a wind tunnel and that is not what we want in this fragrance narrative.
- Never leave a burning candle unattended. This is a good life rule in general and particularly relevant when you have a full stack of post-it notes with clever ideas and your neuron little elves are shouting coffee time.
- Extinguish and re-cap properly: snuffing with a wick dipper is a gentle way to preserve the scent and protect the candle from soot. If you must blow it out, do so with care and avoid blowing wax onto surfaces—lesson learned the hard way after a young, curious cat manifested an accidental wind of meadow fragrance into the air.
