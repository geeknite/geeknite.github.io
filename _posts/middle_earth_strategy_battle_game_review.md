## What is Middle-Earth Strategy Battle Game?

Middle-Earth Strategy Battle Game (MESBG) is a tabletop miniature wargame produced by Games Workshop. It allows players to recreate their favorite battles from *The Lord of the Rings* and *The Hobbit*, using beautifully crafted miniatures and detailed rules. Whether you're leading a squad of brave Rohirrim or commanding the sinister forces of Mordor, the game offers an immersive experience that will transport you to Middle-Earth.

### The Basics

In MESBG, players build armies composed of various heroes, warriors, and monsters from the Tolkien universe. Each character and unit has unique stats and abilities, which can turn the tide of battle. The game is played on a detailed board representing different terrains, from the grassy fields of Rohan to the dark depths of Moria.

### Components

The game comes with:
- **Miniatures**: Highly detailed figures representing characters from Middle-Earth. Your army might include heroes like Aragorn, Legolas, and Gandalf, or villains like Sauron and Saruman.
- **Rulebooks**: Extensive manuals that explain the game's mechanics, including movement, combat, and special abilities.
- **Game Board and Terrain Pieces**: Various terrains that enhance the game's visual appeal and strategic depth.
- **Dice, Tokens, and Cards**: Essential for tracking progress, determining outcomes, and adding flavor to the game.

**Pro Tip**: Ensure your miniatures are assembled and painted before the game. Unpainted figures might be mistaken for orcs by your cat.

## How to Play

### Setup

Setting up MESBG is akin to preparing for a grand battle. Lay out the game board, position terrain pieces, and arrange your miniatures. Each player selects their army and sets up according to the scenario. Ready your dice and prepare to dive into the action.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn consists of several phases:
1. **Priority Phase**: Determine which player will have the initiative for the turn.
2. **Movement Phase**: Move your miniatures across the board, considering terrain and enemy positions.
3. **Shooting Phase**: If your units have ranged weapons, you can attack from a distance.
4. **Combat Phase**: Engage in hand-to-hand combat when units are within melee range.
5. **End Phase**: Resolve any special effects and prepare for the next turn.

### Combat

Combat in MESBG involves rolling dice and comparing stats. Heroes have powerful abilities that can sway the outcome, while ordinary warriors rely on numbers and positioning. Each battle is a blend of strategy and luck, ensuring that no encounter is predictable.
