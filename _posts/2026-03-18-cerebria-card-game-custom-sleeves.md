## H2: What are sleeves and why do they matter for Cerebria?
### H3: The basics of card sleeves
Sleeves are little sleeves for your cards. They come in sizes, thicknesses, transparencies, finishes, and color palettes. In practice, they protect cards from wear, help shuffle, and let you maintain legibility across playgroups with different lighting setups. For a game like Cerebria, where you flip through many action cards, power cards, and track sheets, sleeves become a defensive line: you slide your favorite cards in and out without turning your deck into a coffee-stained modern art piece.

### H3: Why custom sleeves for Cerebria specifically?

## H2: Size, fit, and the practicalities of Cerebria sleeves
### H3: Size matters (even if you don’t want to admit it)
Most Cerebria card sets land in the mid-size category for sleeves. The important thing is to pick sleeves that fit snugly without bending the corners. Overly loose sleeves invite over-shuffling and warp, while sleeves that are too tight can cause card edges to wear out quicker and make flipping feel like you’re performing surgery with a butter knife. If you’re into adding a few protective layers (top loaders or inner sleeves), keep the overall thickness in mind to maintain shuffling smoothness.

### H3: Finishes: gloss, matte, and the unforgettable sheen
Gloss sleeves look sharp and let art pop, but they slide a bit more prone to sticking under bright lights. Matte finishes cut glare and feel nice in the hand, but sometimes the art gets a touch dull under certain room lighting. For Cerebria, matte sleeves are a fantastic middle-ground: readable, protect-y, and less glare when you’re squinting at victory conditions across a sunlit table.

### H3: Color and readability
Color-coded sleeves aren’t just for aesthetics; they’re functional. You can assign a color per faction or per board phase, making it easier to navigate complex turns in real time. If you’re playing with a larger group or streaming, color-coded sets help teammates quickly identify which cards belong to whom, reducing mis-sends and accidental reveals.

## H2: Early game tips and long-term maintenance
### H3: First-time sleeve setup tips
- Pre-sleeve the core deck in a standard color that you’ll always use; this becomes your “home base” deck and will help you learn to recognize key cards during play.
- Consider labeling the back of the sleeve with a small marker for quick identification in a pinch (just be mindful of not marking the actual card).
- Keep spare sleeves for future expansions or playgroups; nothing derails a session like discovering you’ve run out of sleeves mid-game.

### H3: Maintenance and care
- Wipe sleeves with a dry microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints after a session; avoid harsh cleaners that could degrade the sleeve material.
- Store sleeved decks flat or upright in a protective box to reduce curling; if you travel with Cerebria, consider a padded case that fits sleeves and tokens.
- Rotate colors if you’re doing an ongoing campaign: it gives you a refreshing look and prevents color fatigue for regular players.
