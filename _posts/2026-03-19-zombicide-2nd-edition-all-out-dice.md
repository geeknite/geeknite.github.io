## What are All Out Dice?

In practical terms, All Out Dice are a curated set that you substitute into the normal dice pool during an All Out phase. The premise is deliciously simple: you commit to a ramped-up, high-risk action, and you’re rewarded with more powerful outcomes—think more hits, potentially bigger zone effects, and a higher chance of clearing a room in one glorious sweep. They’re designed to inject cinematic chaos into the late-game tense moments while still playing within Zombicide’s core mechanics. The faces on the All Out Dice are distinct from the standard ones, with special icons indicating higher yield or alternative outcomes that can alter how a room clears and which zombies fall first.

The resin is clear, the pips are bold, and the extra faces pop in a way that’s immediately legible even when your brain is trying to remember which zombie type is sprinting diagonally toward your survivor. ## How to use All Out Dice in play

### Declaring All Out

When your group decides to declare All Out, you switch from standard attack dice to All Out Dice for the pertinent phase. The exact rules can vary a little by edition or house rules, but the core idea remains the same: commit to a risk-heavy sequence with the potential for a blowout success. It feels like stepping from a calm, measured approach into a montage sequence where everything goes loud, fast, and a little silly in the best possible way.

### Effects and outcomes

The All Out Dice bring faces that provide more hits, but also often introduce consequences: extra zombies, more complex positioning, or board effects that can complicate the following turns. These are not “free wins.” They are high-risk, high-reward tools that require you to read the board and understand the risk you’re taking. When it lands, it’s glorious; when it backfires, it’s a reminder that a good plan can be undone by a single extra face on a die.

### Interactions with base dice

In most configurations, All Out Dice don’t replace the base dice in perpetuity; they function as a conditional upgrade. You’ll swap in during an All Out window and revert when the sequence ends, which means you can mix regular and All Out sequences across a session. This keeps your game dynamic and ensures you don’t burn through your entire crew in a single haymaker of a turn.

## Strategy and tactics

### When to roll All Out Dice

The “when” is almost as important as the “how.” You should consider the board state, the survivors’ health, and how many zombies remain in the corridor in front of you. If a single big swing saves multiple survivors or secures a critical objective, All Out might be worth it. If your board is already on the edge of collapse, you might want to save the All Out swing for a moment when it could actually tilt the balance rather than just adding a dramatic moment to your chronicle.

### Survivors, weapons and dice synergy

Certain survivors and weapons synergize particularly well with All Out Dice. Weapons with area effects or abilities that trigger on successful hits can chain reactions with the higher hit rates from All Out Faces. Survivors with extra actions, bonuses to dice, or the ability to carry additional equipment can further magnify the payoff. The best strategy is to map out your turn as a puzzle: where can you position your survivors to leverage the big hit without inviting a wipe?

### House rules and tweaks

If your group leans toward faster, leaner sessions, you can dial down the chaos with simple tweaks: limit All Out to one or two rounds per scenario, or require survivors to be above a certain threshold of health to declare All Out. If you’re chasing story moments, you can escalate the effect of All Out by pairing it with unique event cards or scenario-specific modifiers. The joy of All Out is that it invites creative rules refinement without tripping over itself in the process.

## Comparisons and alternatives

All Out Dice occupy a niche: they’re not a core replacement for base mechanics, and they aren’t a full-blown alternative system. They’re a flavor-enhancer that emphasizes spectacle. If you’re chasing absolute efficiency and a tight game length, you may prefer not to use All Out Dice except in rare cases. If you crave cinematic chaos and dramatic, turn-by-turn momentum, All Out Dice are likely to become your go-to spice for late-game spice and showmanship. It’s not about which is “better” overall, but which suits your playgroup’s mood: tight and tactical or big and cinematic.

## Scenarios and replay value

All Out Dice can renew interest in older scenarios by changing the risk profile of standard rooms. You’ll find yourself re-evaluating which doors to breach, where to funnel the zombies, and how to sequence your actions for maximum effect. The replay value comes from the dynamic of trying different combinations: varying survivor setups, different weapon builds, and alternate timing for All Out declarations. Each session can feel like a different movie moment rather than a repeated cut of the same scene, which is precisely the kind of gaming variety that keeps tabletop nights lively.

## Accessibility and value

Price is a factor—these aren’t budget dice, and you’re paying for the cinematic experience as much as for any tangible mechanical upgrade. If you’re a casual player who meets monthly but not weekly, you’ll want to weigh the cost against how often you’ll actually use All Out sequences. For dedicated groups who meet regularly and enjoy long, dramatic runs, All Out Dice can offer a significant boost to the sense of scale and excitement. Remember, the value isn’t only in the dice; it’s in the stories you’ll tell after the session—the kind of tales that begin with “you wouldn’t believe what happened when I rolled that face.”

## Behind the scenes: design philosophy

Designers who create All Out Dice are balancing two tensions: risk and reward, like a microcosm of any good comic-book arc. The goal is to preserve Zombicide’s core tempo while giving players a moment where everything changes. The design team aims for a tactile sense of “this is the moment the hero pulls it off” without tipping into unfairness or needless grind. The dice must be legible, visually striking, and consistent with the rest of the game’s world-building. And yes, they must feel substantial enough to justify the price tag and to earn their place at your table rather than gathering dust in a shelf somewhere.

## Streaming and social tips

If you run a live game stream or post-game wrap-ups, All Out Dice can be perfect for dramatic cutaways. Plan a few moments where you reveal the dice and narrate the tension as you prepare to roll. - If you prefer tight, predictable sessions and straightforward mechanical resolution, you may find the extra chaos more distracting than delightful.
- If your group plays regularly and enjoys long, narrative-driven nights, All Out Dice offer a reliable source of memorable moments and a few surprising outcomes that keep the campaign feeling fresh.
