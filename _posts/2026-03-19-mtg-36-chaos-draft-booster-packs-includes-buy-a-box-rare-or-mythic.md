## Strategy for chaos drafts: what to chase and what to avoid
- Early picks: prioritize mana fixing and your strongest role-player cards that can slot into multiple strategies.
- Middle picks: look for resilient cards that support secondary colors or flexible mana costs. Cards with modal or alternative effects can become a lifeline when your colors fail to align.
- Endgame picks: you’ll want to collect tools that improve your late-game consistency—cards that bridge your gaps or regain tempo after a rough start.
- Don’t chase the big flashy mythics if they require a brittle mana base. Chaos is more forgiving to flexible creatures and spells that scale with board state rather than pure, one-shot power.
- Build your deck with a plan, but be ready to pivot. The chaos pool rewards improvisation and great table talk almost as much as it rewards perfect card-drafting discipline.

## Building the deck: a practical walkthrough
1) Open the packs in small clusters, giving everyone a chance to look at a few cards before selecting.
2) Start with a rough color strategy, but stay flexible. If you see a flood of uncommons that support a niche theme, pivot rather than fight the pool.
3) Draft cards with tempo and synergy in mind. Don’t just chase a single “game-ending” card; look for co-pilots who make your deck function when your top-end cards wobble.
4) Use sideboard slots (or an agreed-on swap method) to adapt to the table’s pace. Chaos implies a dynamic game state where the board often changes faster than your plan can keep up with it.
5) Post-draft, be honest with yourself about the most valuable piece of your pool. Sometimes a synergy piece you overlooked in the process becomes the heart of a surprising deck.
