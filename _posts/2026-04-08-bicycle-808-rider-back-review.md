## Care, storage, and long-term value

### Keeping your sealed decks pristine

New in package means exactly what it says: the plastic wrap has not been torn, the deck has not been opened, and the inside card stock remains in prime condition. For collectors, the seal is a promise of mint status, a nice talking point when you are negotiating with fellow collectors or browsing marketplaces. The best practice here is simple: avoid exposing the decks to heat, humidity, and sunlight for extended periods. A cool, dry shelf or a dedicated card cabinet is ideal. If you decide to open one or more decks, treat them with the same care you would give to a new sculpture. The edges are prone to wear in rough handling, but the material is resilient enough to withstand normal use.

### Longevity and environmental considerations

Card stock is not immune to the passage of time, especially when stored in bright environments. If you care about long-term value, invest in sleeves or a protective display case that also doubles as a conversation piece. A good display is not only about looks; it also helps preserve the finish and reduces the risk of scuffs that can occur with frequent handling. A neat trick for long-term storage is to rotate which deck you actually use; this ensures that no single deck gets overly worn while others maintain their mint condition as long as possible.
