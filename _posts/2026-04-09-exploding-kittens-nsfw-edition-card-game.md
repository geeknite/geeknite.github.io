## How to Play: Mechanics and Flow
If you already know Exploding Kittens, you’ll recognize the skeleton: draw cards, play cards to influence draws, dodge exploding moments, and rely on Defuse cards to avert catastrophe. The NSFW Edition tweaks the humor but leaves the core mechanics intact. If you’re new to the franchise, here’s a quick refresher.

### Core Rules in a Nutshell
- Each player takes a turn by drawing a card from the deck. The aim is to avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten unless you have a Defuse card.
- On your turn, you may play any number of action cards to affect other players, skip turns, steal cards, or force someone else to draw.
- When the Exploding Kitten is drawn, you blow up—unless you can play a Defuse card to survive.
- The last player standing wins.

The NSFW Edition introduces more humorous, adult-targeted action cards. These cards often force ridiculous consequences, add snarky taunts, or modify how draws occur. The cadence of the game remains fast enough for a quick session, but it can stretch out if your party is chatty, which is perfect for those who enjoy banter with their chaos.

### Turn Order and Strategy Basics
1. Draw: You always draw at the end of your turn unless your card effect interrupts.
2. Play: Use non-Defuse cards strategically to slow down opponents, shield yourself, or bend the rules in your favor. Expect banter and maybe a few dramatic gestures.
3. Defuse: If you have a Defuse, you can use it to neutralize an Exploding Kitten, punch-enough line into the game that the moment feels almost cinematic.
4. Endgame: As players fall away, the table grows louder and more ridiculous. This is not a game for quiet contemplation; it’s a late-night improv exercise with dice roles, puns, and a lot of color.
