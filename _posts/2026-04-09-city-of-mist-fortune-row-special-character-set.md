### What you’re getting in Fortune Row
Fortune Row is a Special Character Set, which means you’re not merely adding a handful of villains or bystander tokens to a standard board. You’re augmenting the very structure of how characters are conceived in this game. The set introduces new character archetypes, intricate character sheets designed to push players toward story-driven play, and a handful of narrative prompts that encourage improvisation rather than the usual dice-twiddling. In short: Fortune Row is more about giving you better excuses to tell a story than giving you better dice results, but it still sneaks in some snappy mechanical options along the way.

## New characters and archetypes
Fortune Row leans into its title by offering a rowdy lineup of fortune-driven archetypes. Here’s a snapshot of the standout options, including the narrative hooks that will keep your table leaning in for more.

### The Glimmer Agent
This character is all about information as currency. They’re the kind of ally who sees plot twists coming a mile away but never lets on until it matters. The Glimmer Agent has a few unique moves that let them manipulate risk and opportunity with the same ease a street magician pulls coins from a sleeve. Their backstory frequently revolves around a faded street deal with consequences that pop up in the most unexpected moments.

### The Velvet Warden
A protector with a price, they walk the line between mercy and leverage. The Velvet Warden isn’t just about physical prowess; their power lies in their ability to negotiate outcomes that feel like choices but actually steer the narrative toward a particular emotional beat. Expect moral ambiguity, tense stand-offs, and a surprising amount of heart.

### The Cipher of Fortune
This character uses misdirection as a serious tool. Their abilities revolve around misinforming the antagonists and guiding the story through a labyrinth of symbols and clues. The Cipher of Fortune can turn a simple social encounter into a chess match where every move reveals another layer of the mystery’s architecture.

Each character comes with a distinct set of prompts, ties to city factions, and bespoke trait lines that you can drop into an ongoing case with minimal prep. The strength of Fortune Row is how these archetypes feel both aspirational and grounded—mythic in ambition, but street-level in texture.

### Character cards and sheet design
The character sheets in Fortune Row are a treat for the eye and a relief for the hands. They provide a clean layout that emphasizes narrative prompts, relationship links, and mood-based mechanics. The cards for hooks, twists, and scenes are color-coded and consistent with the core game’s aesthetic, which makes it easy to weave the new content into an existing campaign without having to memorize a separate system.

The design also respects the “show, don’t tell” ethos of City of Mist. Instead of heavy exposition on the sheet, most of the weaved-in details are meant to be revealed at the table. This fosters player engagement and reduces the teacherly vibe that sometimes infects RPG sessions when players feel like they’re reading a textbook rather than stepping into a mythic city.

## Mechanics and play experience
Fortune Row doesn’t throw away the core rules. If anything, it expands the ways you can engage with them. The base dice mechanic remains a reliable mix of probability, storytelling, and improvisation. The new character options add layers to how you spend fate points and how you generate narrative momentum.

### Narrative momentum and Fate points
Fate points continue to function as the currency of dramatic turns. Fortune Row expands the ways you can earn or spend these points, especially through the new prompts and hooks that the cards provide. The effect is a more fluid storytelling flow: players push for twists, the MC (or GM) nudges toward thematic revelations, and the story arrives at a satisfying, if sometimes chaotic, crescendo.

If you enjoy the “twist of fate” mechanic in the core game, Fortune Row doubles down on that exhilaration. It invites players to tailor a story around a central mystery and then drop in a few wildcard elements that force the plot to reorient itself in entertaining ways.

### Balance and power curve
As with many expansions of this kind, balance is a careful dance. The new characters are potent, but they’re designed to feed back into the story rather than dominate the table. You’ll notice that the more narrative you want to chase, the more you have to invest in collaboration and timing. The risk with a strong, theme-driven expansion is overcommitment to a single arc, which can derail the moral nuance City of Mist is known for. Fortune Row sidesteps that pitfall by embedding multiple hooks and prompts that allow a party to explore different angles simultaneously.

### Scenarios and table feel
One of Fortune Row’s real strengths is the ready-to-run micro-scenarios. These are not mere “dungeons in neon” but carefully structured vignettes that encourage roleplay, dramatic irony, and character-driven conflict. They work well for one-shot sessions and for sprinkling into longer campaigns as memorable “beats” that anchor a larger mystery. If you enjoy running city-wide campaigns with a noir mood, Fortune Row will feel like a natural extension rather than a gimmick.

## Artwork, components, and production quality
Production in Fortune Row continues the high standard established by City of Mist. The art direction favors bold silhouettes, neon color accents, and a moody sense of place. The new cards and sheets are visually cohesive with the base game, which is essential for a product ecosystem you’ll be using for months or even years.

The tactile experience matters: the cards shuffle smoothly, the tokens feel sturdy, and the box itself sits well on a shelf next to the rest of your CoM library. There’s a charm in the choice of typography for the prompts and the way the mood imagery nudges players toward the right scene without ever telling them what to feel. In a sense, Fortune Row invites players to participate in the storytelling choreography rather than passively watch it unfold.

## Box value and replayability
Fortune Row aims to be a long-tail addition. It isn’t a simple one-time flavor; it’s a toolkit intended to be integrated with multiple campaigns. The new archetypes offer enough variety that even if you replay the same mystery, you’ll encounter new angles, new twists, and new roleplay beats that feel fresh. If you’re a GM who loves crafting morally gray stories, you’ll find Fortune Row particularly rewarding as a source of prompts and structure.

On replayability, the micro-scenarios are the star feature. They’re short enough to slot into a single session, but written with enough nuance that they can seed longer arcs if you want to develop them. The prompts encourage players to dig into backstory and relationships, which, in turn, creates a richer city fabric for your future sessions. The downside is that if you’re not invested in roleplay or you’re more dice-forward, you may not get as much mileage out of the narrative components.

## Strengths and weaknesses
Strengths:
- Strong mood and art direction that aligns with City of Mist’s noir-meets-myth vibe
- Thoughtful integration of new character archetypes with practical prompts for table leadership
- High-quality production that feels premium without breaking the bank
- Reusable narrative prompts and hooks that fuel long-term campaigns
- Solid balance that favors story and player engagement over raw power creep

Weaknesses:
- The narrative focus might be a barrier for groups that prefer crunchy combat mechanics
- Some prompts may feel trope-heavy if your table leans toward overdone urban fantasy clichés
- A subset of players may find the new mechanics a bit dense without a dedicated read-through

## How it fits into your City of Mist campaign
Fortune Row shines when integrated into an existing campaign with a strong mood and a flexible narrative structure. If your table runs a long-form mystery with episodic reveals, the micro-scenarios and hooks fit naturally as “episodes” that punctuate the arc. For new groups, you might want to pair Fortune Row with a shorter introductory arc to help players acclimate to the tone and tempo before diving into more complex, multi-episode mysteries.

For GMs who want to maximize storytelling, Fortune Row is a powerful ally. It invites you to build mysteries around character threads rather than a single villain. You’ll be surprised how often a backstory thread you lightly seeded in a previous session becomes the session’s emotional core a few chapters later. The set’s prompts act as a reminder to emphasize character voice over mechanics, which is exactly what City of Mist has always done well.

## Play tips and tricks
- Read the new prompts aloud as a group during the first session to get everyone on the same storytelling wavelength.
- Use the new archetypes to seed relationships early. Have players declare how their characters relate to at least one Fortune Row figure, which will yield natural conflict and collaboration moments.
- Don’t be afraid to bend, not break, the set’s rules. This is a storytelling game; rules exist to support the drama, not to police it.
- Schedule regular catches of mood shifts and foreshadowing moments. Fortune Row’s design rewards players who pay attention to the emotional undercurrent of the mystery.
- If your campaign has a faction system, tie Fortune Row’s hooks into faction agendas to deepen political intrigue and urban complexity.

## Accessibility and value
Fortune Row offers substantial value if you are committed to long-term City of Mist play. The price point sits in the middle of the expansion market—not cheap, not exorbitant, but respectable given the amount of narrative content and character depth you gain. It’s not a postcard-sized add-on; it’s a toolkit designed to amplifying what you already enjoy about the base game: storytelling, mood, and mythic identity clashing with the city’s grit.

If you’re brand new to City of Mist, you may want to start with the Core Box to build familiarity with the system before investing in Fortune Row. That said, Fortune Row is still accessible to beginners if you pair it with a patient GM who can explain the prompts and help the players find their footing in the narrative. The expanded character options can be intimidating at first, but the payoff is a deepened table experience once you get the hang of it.

## Final verdict
Fortune Row – Special Character Set is an excellent addition for players who want to lean into the noir-meets-myth vibe City of Mist does best. It doesn’t drastically overhaul the core rules and instead enriches the storytelling toolkit with new archetypes, mood-driven mechanics, and ready-to-run micro-scenarios. If you love sessions that feel cinematic, with moments of moral ambiguity and dramatic choice, Fortune Row should be on your radar. It’s confident in its mood, generous with its prompts, and stylish enough to deserve a place on the shelf next to your favorite urban fantasy game.
