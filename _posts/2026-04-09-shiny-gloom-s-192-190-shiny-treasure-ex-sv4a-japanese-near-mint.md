## Collectors' Notes: Rarity and Grading
Rarity in the Japanese Shiny Treasure ex subset is a moving target. Some print runs were tighter, others more forgiving; some boxes yield a surprising number of shiny variants, and others yield a cluster of normal cards that will be used for player decks rather than display pieces. When you’re evaluating a Near Mint status, you’ll want to inspect for:
- Centering: The Gloom’s image should be crisp with the holo art engaged across most of the card’s width. A slight misalignment here can push a card into 9.5 territory or even 9, depending on other factors.
- Edges: The edge wear on holo cards shows up as tiny micro-bites when you tilt the card under bright light. In Near Mint, you should not see chipping or visible whitening along the edges.
- Surface: The holo surface should be flat with minimal scratches. A few micro-scratches are common on older holo cards, but perfection is the goal.
- Corner stress: The rare flex lines or dents may reduce the grade; a near-mint example will have crisp corners that look uniform across all four corners.

For grading services, the standard scale is 1 to 10, with 10 being a pristine gem and 9 to 9.5 as excellent near-mint specimens. Some third-party services assign sub-grades for centering, surface, corners, and edges, which can provide a more nuanced picture of the card’s condition. If you’re chasing the S or SV4a variants, you’re not just chasing a number; you’re chasing a story about production runs, distribution channels, and the collector community that loves shiny things almost religiously.

## Grading and Protection: How to Preserve Your Gloom
The best-time protection begins at the sleeves and top loaders. Here are practical steps:
- Use semi-rigid sleeves to protect the surface from micro-scratches. If you’re proud of your holo pattern, avoid sleeves that add friction or cause haze.
- Place in a solid top loader for transport to events or toy fairs.
- Store in a cool, dry environment away from direct sunlight to avoid fading of the holo finish.
- If you’re stacking multiple holo cards, consider an upright display or a soft storage binder to minimize pressure on corners.

In terms of storage, climate control matters. Humidity can cause minor warping in the card stock. Keep your environment consistent and your display case tidy. A small desiccant pack in your storage binder can help maintain dryness. The system is simple, but the results matter when you’re evaluating the card over months and years.
