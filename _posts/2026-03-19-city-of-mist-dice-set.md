## Design and Ergonomics
### Material and Feel
Most City of Mist dice are resin or acrylic, chosen for durability and a certain “narrative weight.” They have crisp edges, smooth surfaces, and a tactile confidence that tells you: you’re about to narrate something epic. They aren’t as slippery as bargain-bin plastic, which makes them friendlier to players with sweaty palms during tense moments.

### Readability
The faces carry numerals and symbols chosen to harmonize with the game’s themes. They’re large enough to read at a glance, even in dim lighting—crucial when you’re trying to stage a noir moment while your cat investigates the keyboard or the suspicious neighbor downstairs starts tapping a mysterious rhythm on the radiator.

### Thematic Fit
When these dice appear on the table, the mood shifts toward heroic myth and noir intrigue. They function as storytelling props as much as game components, which is exactly the kind of accessory a table wants—something that signals, without shouting, that your table is about to tell a story rather than simply roll dice.

### Durability and Maintenance
The finish on these dice tends to be resilient to fingerprints and minor scuffs, especially the resin varieties. A microfiber cloth and a gentle wipe after sessions will keep the noir sheen intact. If you’re tempted to customize, test on a spare die first; altering weight distribution or surface finish can affect how they roll, which is the exact opposite of what you want when you’re chasing dramatic outcomes.

## Color Theory, Readability, and Symbols
City of Mist dice aren’t just about pretty colors; they’re about signaling narrative stakes. The colors map to tones and plot beats—something like: dark for danger, blue for calm but revealing, amber for urgency, and red for risk. The symbols on faces (often stylized city signage or mythic glyphs) are designed to cue what kind of action is happening in the scene, not just how well you roll.

For players with visual impairments or colorblindness, the design typically relies on texture or distinct iconography beyond color alone. If you’re running with a table that includes a non-visual cue player, you can describe the symbol’s meaning aloud and let words carry the mechanic’s weight rather than a color cue alone. The best use is to pair the dice with a short verbal cue: “The glyph for fate glows,” or “City favors you—blue light pulses in the glass.” It’s a small ritual that enhances immersion.

Readability at table is also a function of lighting. City of Mist moments thrive in moody illumination; you’ll want a lamp with a warm hue, not a brutal glare, so the pips don’t get washed out. If you’re playing in a coffee shop, a small clip-on light can be your best friend. The goal is to have the dice read at a glance while allowing your players to lean forward, listening for the city’s response as their character’s legend grows.

## Use in Play: Narratives, Moods, and Mechanics
City of Mist shines in cinematic moments where the stakes feel larger than life. The dice are there to flag when a moment should feel bigger than normal and to cue tone shifts that align with the mythic framework of the game.

### How to Use the Dice Effectively
- Establish a color vocabulary: Before sessions begin, agree on what each color or symbol signifies. This gives players a shared mental map for describing actions in mythic terms. For example, a symbol could mark an action that is “mythic, risky, and consequential.”
- Use the lore card as a storytelling prompt: Treat it as a session-opening vibe check. A simple “what if” guided by the lore card can unlock a surprising path for the story.
- Let the table describe: When players roll well or poorly, invite them to narrate a consequence that feels like the city itself is reacting—through signage flicker, rain intensification, or a siren’s chorus.
- Pair with atmospheric cues: Noir playlists, rainy soundscapes, coffee steam fogging the window—these elements amplify the dice-driven mood shifts and help players inhabit their Mythic City persona.

### Thematic Hooks and Narrative Payoffs
The City of Mist dice are perfect for moments when the line between hero and monster blurs. A successful roll might reveal a city emblem etched in neon on a villain’s jacket, or a hidden ally’s silhouette stepping from the alley’s shadow. A failed roll might unleash a twist in which the city’s gaze shifts to a different character who holds a long-kept secret. The dice do not just decide outcomes; they co-author the scene’s dramatic arc.

### Lamp-Post Moments: Tips for Cinematic Climax
- Build your scene around a single dice roll: Let the roll define who acts first and what symbol’s effect you’ll lean on.
- Use evocative descriptors: Instead of “the door opens,” say, “the rain halts for a breath as a neon halo spills across the doorway.” The dice reward poetic description.
- Embrace the glitch: Sometimes a fail is a feature. A misstep can force a pivot toward a character’s hidden vulnerability or a city’s secret mechanism kicking in.

## Thematic Integration and Role-Play
City of Mist is a game about myth and reality colliding in a modern urban setting. The dice should support that collision—colors to represent powers and vulnerabilities; symbols that evoke a sense of myth rather than blunt numbers. The goal isn’t to optimize the odds so much as to unlock a narrative voice at the table. When you roll well, describe a moment where the city itself seems to respond to your character’s choices. When you roll poorly, lean into the city’s flaws: a backhanded ally, a stubborn rumor, or a corner that refuses to forget your past.

A great practice is to declare a short “mythic anchor” at character creation. This anchor is a personal symbol that appears on a die face or as a color cue. It helps players stay in character during moments of high stakes and lets the dice become a more personal storytelling instrument rather than a neutral probability engine.

## Practical Tips for Collectors
- Inspect batches: Limited batches with subtle color shifts are common among small-run dice. If you want a precise hue, compare product photos from recent batches to pick the closest match.
- Storage matters: The included tin is sturdy, portable, and fits neatly on a shelf or in a gaming bag. It’s certainly more durable than a shoebox and far less likely to threaten your sanity (and your dice) with a spontaneous tumble.
- Customization: Small touches—tiny icons, painted faces, or subtle varnish—can increase readability for certain colors. If you modify, do a small test run first. Balance can shift if you remove a coating or apply additional paint.
- Maintenance: A quick wipe after sessions keeps the finish pristine and prevents oil buildup from hands, which can dull the sheen over time.
