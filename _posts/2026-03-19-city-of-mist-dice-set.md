## Introduction
City of Mist is not just a tabletop RPG; it's a vibe. If storytelling had a mascot, it would be a trench-coated detective sipping coffee while a dragon makes a cameo in a neon-lit skyline. The dice set for City of Mist is not your standard polyhedron parade; it is a mood instrument that tells a story before you roll. Put simply, these dice exist to remind you that the city is always watching, and sometimes it is winkingly judgmental about your dice-rolling skills.

## Design and Ergonomics
### Material and Feel
The dice are typically resin or acrylic. They have crisp edges, smooth surfaces, and a feel that says you are about to narrate something epic. They are not as slippery as cheap plastic, which makes them friendlier to players with sweaty palms after a tense scene.

### Readability
The numerals and symbols on the faces are chosen to resonate with the game's themes. They are large enough to read at a glance, even under dim lighting, which is essential when you are trying to run a noir moment while your cat walks across the keyboard.

### Thematic Fit
When you see these dice on the table, the mood automatically shifts toward heroic myth and noir intrigue. They are a storytelling prop as much as a game component, and that is the best kind of accessory to have in a role-playing toolkit.

## Thematic Integration and Role-Play
City of Mist is a game about myth and reality intersecting in a modern urban setting. The dice should support that collision. Use colors to represent your character's powers and vulnerabilities, and pick symbols that evoke a sense of myth rather than simply numbers. The goal is to use the dice as a storytelling instrument rather than as a mere probability tool. When you roll well, describe a moment where the city itself seems to respond to your character's choices.
