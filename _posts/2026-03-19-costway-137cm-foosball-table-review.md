## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping It Fresh

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it’s necessary. Here are our practical tips:
- Periodically check leg levelers and tighten any loose screws. A wobbly table is not your friend when you’re attempting a perfect flip-shot. 
- Wipe the playfield with a mild cleaner to keep it free of grime that could affect ball bounce. Use a microfiber cloth so you don’t scratch the surface.
- Inspect rod bearings for any play; a tiny squeak now is a big squeak later if ignored. Lubricate lightly with silicone-based lubricant if needed. Do not use stove oil; your table does not need a kitchen tragedy.
- Keep the balls in a dedicated container; nothing stops a serious game faster than losing the ball in the couch cushions. A small ball return tray is a nice addition if your room allows.
- Protect the finish from direct sunlight which can cause color fading over months of sun-drenched duels. Unless you’re trying to recreate an epic desert sci-fi scene, you probably shouldn’t bake your table.
