### Colorfastness and Stain Handling

The blue accents are a design feature that helps conceal everyday grime between cleanings, though no rug is stain-proof. We recommend blotting spills promptly to minimize any potential setting. The key is to avoid rubbing, which tends to push stains deeper into the fibers and can create a halo effect around the spill. A mild detergent solution, tested in an inconspicuous area first, is typically sufficient for most common stains.

### Back Stain Considerations

The label back stain is a nod to the rug backing design and its potential staining patterns on certain floor types. It does not imply that water stains will transfer from back to front, but as with any rug, you should be mindful when cleaning near the backing to avoid saturating the underside. A light touch with damp cloths around the edges is usually enough to keep the back area clean without compromising the adhesive or backing materials.

### Cleaning Protocols that Actually Work

- Regular vacuuming with a low-suction setting to avoid fraying the edges.
- Blot stains immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing that can spread the stain.
- For tough spots, use a mild detergent solution and a clean sponge, then blot dry.
- Allow ample air-drying time after cleaning to prevent moisture from lingering under the fibers.
