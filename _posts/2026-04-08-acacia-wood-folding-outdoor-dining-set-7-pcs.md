## Durability and weather handling
### How it handles sun and rain
Acacia wood holds up better than most softwood options when exposed to sunlight. It resists warping relatively well and tends to develop a natural patina that many people actually like. That said, prolonged exposure to heavy rain or snow is not ideal. If you have a covered patio, this set will last longer, while constant direct exposure to the elements will require more maintenance and a less vibrant finish over time. A good rule of thumb is to use a weather-resistant cover during monsoon seasons or when you are going to be away for extended periods.

### Maintenance routine
A simple care routine keeps the wood looking good. Clean with a soft cloth and mild soap to remove dirt, pollen, and that suspicious ring left by a forgotten mug. Rinse and dry, apply a protective oil or sealant every season, and re-tighten hardware as needed. If you notice the finish fading or small cracks appearing, a light sanding followed by resealing is advisable. Think of it as giving your outdoor furniture a spa day rather than a full makeover. The goal is to extend life while preserving the warm look of acacia wood.

- Clean regularly with mild soap and water. Avoid harsh chemicals that can strip the finish.
- Oil or seal the wood seasonally to preserve color and resilience.
