## H2 Design and build quality: materials, feel, and durability
### H3 Material choices and finish
Fortune Row dice tend to use standard resin or polymer dice bodies, sometimes with a resin core and metal-insert legends for the premium feel. The finishes vary from matte to glossy depending on the colorway. The goal is a smooth roll with a satisfying “click” and sufficient heft to feel substantial but not so heavy you fear dropping from a kitchen table Jack-and-Jill height. The legends, especially on premium lines, are crisp and well-inked. In some runs, you may find inlaid metallic foils or accent rings around the edges that catch the light like a city sign after rain.

### H3 Tolerances and balance
In a game like City of Mist, you want dice that aren’t the star players of the night by misbehaving with misbalanced faces. Fortune Row dice usually goes through a light quality check, though you should not expect tournament-grade precision every time. Some dice may show minor pips alignment quirks, which is common with artisanal or premium resin sets. If your table requires absolute fairness, you might rotate your set for different players or consider resin dice that have undergone weight sorting. For narrative play, the small irregularities rarely impact the story but can add a bit of personality to your dice bag.

### H3 Durability in real-world use
Premium dice aren’t immortal. You will roll them on wood, laminate, and occasionally kitchen counters. The Fortune Row line holds up reasonably well to daily use, but you should guard against direct sunlight on the painted faces, as color might dull over years. A quick wipe with a soft cloth after play keeps the luster and reduces the chance of oils dulling the finish. If you like to store your dice in a display case or a resin diorama, Fortune Row looks excellent on a shelf of noir miniatures and dreamlike cityscapes.

## H2 Care, maintenance, and practical tips for longevity
### H3 Cleaning and handling
Dust and oils can accumulate on painted faces over time. A gentle wipe with a dry or slightly damp microfiber cloth is usually enough. Avoid harsh solvents; they can degrade finishes. When possible, store dice away from sunlight to prevent fading. If you travel with them, consider a padded case to prevent chips and edge wear.

### H3 What to do if a face wears or chips
Minor wear is normal over long-term use. If a face starts to look unclear, try a careful buff with a soft cloth. For more serious chips, you can contact the manufacturer for a replacement policy or compare options from the retailer. In many cases, a slight imperfection becomes part of your table’s stories and a reminder of a memorable night in the city that never sleeps.
