## The Mint Checklist: How to Verify You Have a True Mint Copy

If you are hunting for a mint copy of The Saboteur PS3, here is a practical checklist to help you sort the wheat from the chaff:

- Box corners: Check for crisp edges and no creases. A truly mint copy will feel firm when you press your thumb against the corner, not floppy or softened by moisture.
- Box art: Look for clean prints with no fading or scuff marks. The art should be vibrant, and the spine should be intact with minimal rubbing.
- Disc: The disc should be free of scratches, swirl marks, or any sign of surface damage. If you can see any fingerprints or smudges, you might want to consider a professional cleaning before testing the disc.
- Manual inserts: They should lay flat, with no tearing or water damage. Fold lines should be clean and the paper should feel sturdy, not brittle.
- Warranty or insert cards: If present, confirm they are intact and not separated from their slots. A missing or damaged insert can drop the perceived mint status quickly.
- Glue and adhesives: If the insert has glue remnants, it can indicate a re-seal or improper storage, which is a red flag for mint status.
- Overall appearance: When you close the box, the seal should be firm and not loose. The box should not have a wobble or a misaligned lid that hints at a rough journey from warehouse to your display shelf.

If all these boxes are checked, you have a strong candidate for mint CIB status. If a seller offers free shipping on such a copy, you have not just a collectible; you have a transaction that respects the item as a fragile piece of gaming history.
