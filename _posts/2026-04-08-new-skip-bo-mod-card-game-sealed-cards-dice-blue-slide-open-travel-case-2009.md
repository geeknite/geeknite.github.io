## The MOD Mechanics: How It Changes the Game

### Core rules vs a new twist
Skip-Bo is a speed and sequencing game where players aim to accumulate and play cards in ascending order. The MOD variant introduces a few alterations that hinge on the included dice and the sealed cards. The dice act as wilds or modifiers: rolling a certain result may allow you to draw extra cards, flip the order, or force players to swap a card from hand with a sealed card facedown. The sealed cards are typically not visible at the start; you reveal them at certain triggers in the round, effectively injecting hidden information into the standard flow.

From a design perspective, this is a clever way to maintain familiar mechanics while injecting uncertainty. It introduces a tension between the known order of standard Skip-Bo and the unknowns tucked behind sealed cards that suddenly flip the table when revealed. The dice outcomes often require on-the-fly drafting of small rules, so expect moments of chaos punctuated by laughter, groans, and the occasional exasperated sigh when someone reveals a sealed card that completely changes the tableau.

### How the sealed deck affects strategy
With sealed cards, players must weigh the risk of revealing a new card now or keeping it hidden for a future turn. The low-level decision becomes: do I push the current run and risk hitting a trap beneath the seal, or do I slow-roll to glean more information? This adds a mini-game on top of the main game: information management.

Because you can also roll dice to influence mischief or advantage, the MOD experience rewards adaptive thinking. If you enjoy chess with less seriousness and more chaos, you will likely find this variant highly entertaining. If you prefer a strict, perfect information environment, this variant will feel more like a chaotic party game wearing a weekday shirt than a refined strategy game.

### Example round dynamics
- Player A begins a standard Skip-Bo run with the open stack. The dice roll reveals a wild card effect that forces a swap with a sealed card from Player B’s area.
- The sealed card is revealed mid-round, potentially changing the available runs for both players. Laughter erupts when a run is broken due to an unexpected reverse rule.
- A critical moment arrives when a sealed card reveals a card that unlocks a powerful play endgame move. The pressure escalates as players anticipate the next revelation.

The cadence of these moments creates a rhythm that is very different from base Skip-Bo. It is less about perfect execution and more about managing unknowns while maintaining a readable board state. The dice add variability that reduces the likelihood of a zig-zag-perfectly-planned path and increases the probability of a surprising, meme-worthy finish.
