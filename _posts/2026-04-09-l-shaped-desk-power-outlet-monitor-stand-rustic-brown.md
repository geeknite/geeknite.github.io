Assembly and Setup
If you’re a veteran of flat-pack furniture, you’ll feel at home here. The quick-start guide promises 15 minutes; in reality, you’ll want a solid 45 minutes to get everything aligned, connected, and emotionally ready for the boss battle that is cable management. The most critical choice is whether you want the monitor stand on the left or the right—this is not a reversible design mid-assembly, so plan accordingly. The track system is clever, letting you slide the monitor arm along the length of the desk to accommodate dual-monitor setups with a little reach left or right. 

Cable management is a win and a curse. The power module is easily accessible, but the channels for running cables along the inner legs can become a tangle of USB-C cables, HDMI cords, and the occasional power brick if you’re not neat about it. If you’re the kind of person who keeps power bricks to a minimum, you’ll feel the holy energy of Order again. If you’re the kind of person who has a nest of cables worth its own science fiction novella, you’ll wish for more grommets and a larger trough. The upside is that the desk keeps a clean silhouette while still offering practical hiding spots for the clutter that defines most home offices.

Monitors, Ergonomics, and the Real-World Test

The Corner Office Pro handles everyday use with grace: you can lean slightly when reaching for a coffee mug, you can push a heavy external hard drive to the edge, and you won’t feel like you’re about to crack the table into two like a poorly engineered light saber.
