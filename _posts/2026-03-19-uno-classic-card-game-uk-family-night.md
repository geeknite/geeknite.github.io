## How to play at a glance

UNO is known for its simplicity: you match a card by color or number, use action cards to complicate your opponents’ lives, and aim to be the first to get rid of all your cards. The UK edition keeps the core rules intact but adds a few modern touches that make it friendlier for families with younger players.

- Start with seven cards each; the rest of the deck is placed face down as a draw pile.
- Turn over the top card to start the discard pile. If it’s a wild or action card, the game momentum gets a little spicier from the start.
- On your turn, match a card by color or number or play an action card to affect others.
- If you can’t play any card, draw one card. If that card can be played, you may still play it immediately depending on your house rules.
- Call UNO when you have one card left. If you forget, others can yell “UNO!” and you might face penalty cards or a playful roast.

A note on UK-specific rules: many families like to set some gentle, common-sense house rules, such as “no shouting” during the final countdown, or “pick a colour with a smile” when wild cards show up. The English living room doesn’t need drama to overachieve; it wants clarity and quick laughs.

## Quick-start guide: setting up in minutes

UNO doesn’t pretend to be brain surgery. You can set it up and be underway in about five minutes, which means you can squeeze in a game before the kettle boils and the cat demands your attention.

- Unpack the deck.
- Shuffle thoroughly (this is where the fun begins; shuffle like you’re auditioning for a magician’s act).
- Each player draws seven cards.
- Flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- The youngest player goes first, and the chaos begins.

House rules vary; some families allow players to call UNO as soon as they have one card; others enforce the “one penalty when you forget” rule with a playful glare. The UK edition’s simple rule sheet is designed to be a quick reference so you don’t have to pause to re-read the manual while a child tries to stack their card on an ongoing penalty.
