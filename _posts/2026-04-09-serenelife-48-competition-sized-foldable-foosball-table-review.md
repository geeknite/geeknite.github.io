## Assembly and maintenance: a quick setup and a quick night cap
Assembling the SereneLife is a one-person job if you don’t mind a little wiggle-wobble and a two-minute patience test. Here’s a quick rundown of what to expect during assembly:
- Unpack and lay out components in a clean space: You’ll find the playfield, legs, rods, rods end-caps, handles, and a few screws. The two balls come tucked in a small bag—guard them like dragon eggs until you’re ready to drop them into play.
- Attach legs and support braces: The leg assembly is straightforward, with clearly labeled holes. Most people will be playing in under 30 minutes. If you’re me, you’ll still be reading the instruction sheet while humans grow impatient with the clock.
- Install the rods and players: It’s a two-step process; keep track of the player figures and the base alignments. If you lose a mirror in the process, you can blame it on the cat.
- Level and test: Once you stand the table upright and lock the legs in place, test the ball return and ensure the rails align properly. Minor adjustments may be necessary if your floor is not perfectly level, but that’s part of the “home arcade” charm.

Maintenance is simple. Wipe the playfield with a soft cloth, keep the handles clean, and inspect the ball returns occasionally to ensure nothing has jammed the mechanism. A light silicone spray can help maintain the smoothness of the rods if you find things a tad sticky after a long session. Do not bathe the table in water or use harsh cleaners—this is not an aquarium.
