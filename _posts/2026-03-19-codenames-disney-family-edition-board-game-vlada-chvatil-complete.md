## How to Play (Disney Edition in a Nutshell)
Like the base game, the Disney Family Edition centers on teams racing to identify their agents on the grid using one-word clues from the spymaster. But here’s the quick-fire version to get you started before the snacks arrive.

- Setup: The table gets a 5x5 grid of Disney character cards. Red and blue allies sit opposite each other; a neutral card and a single assassin (the dreaded blackout) lurk in the mix. Everyone knows which team they’re on, and the spymaster for each side has access to a clue deck and the floor map of the board.
- Objective: Be the first team to identify all of your agents on the grid. The tension comes from balancing clever wordplay with the risk of combining too many Disney references into a single clue.
- Turn structure: On a turn, the active spymaster gives a one-word clue followed by a number. The clue links several on-card Disney items with a single concept. For instance, a clue like Disney + 3 could point to three cards that share a Disney link in their minds—perhaps a movie, a character, and a place that share a thematic connection.
- Guessing: The team’s players discuss and point at the cards they think fit the clue. Each correct guess removes a card from the grid; wrong guesses end the team’s turn and favor the other side. If you hit the assassin, the game ends immediately and the other team wins. If you’re lucky and guess all your agents without triggering the assassin, you win—snacks all around.
- Endgame: The moment a team finds all its agents, the victory dance commences. If the assassin is triggered on the last card, nothing kills a vibe like a sudden exit from the party, so maybe don’t celebrate too early.

The core tension of the game remains intact: you want a clue that’s specific enough to point to multiple cards, yet broad enough to cover those cards without pulling in the enemy’s domain. Disney flavor means the clues can reference familiar movie moments or characters. For example, a well-chosen clue might tie together cards representing a princess, a pirate crew, and a sidekick—provided your team can map those into a single concept without dragging in a red herring.

## Strategies and Tips for Disney Edition Success
Here are some practical pointers to help your team ride the magic carpet without crashing into the lamp:

- Leverage shared knowledge: Use clues that connect multiple cards through a single, recognizable theme. Characters from a single franchise, or a movie + a sidekick pairing, often work well. If your teammates can’t recall a connection, the clue collapses under its own weight.
- Favor broad ties over niche references: A clue like era or genre can be more universal than a specific plot point. This reduces the risk of misinterpretation and keeps the guessing pace lively.
- Manage tempo: Don’t rush too quickly. The thrill of a fast-paced round can be great, but a measured pace often yields better consistency, especially when dealing with younger players who appreciate time to discuss.
- Safety play: If you’re tempted to use a clue that points too close to the enemy’s cards, reframe it. It’s better to be a touch safe and finish the round with a clean victory than to spark a dramatic turn that lets the other team steal the show.
- Adjust as needed: Remember that the rules are flexible enough to accommodate your group. If your family finds the clues become too opaque, shorten rounds or allow for additional hints in a structured way to keep everyone engaged.

These strategies are the bread and butter of any successful Codenames session, Disney edition or not. The Disney branding adds a layer of fun, but the underlying cognitive exercise remains the same: you’re solving a puzzle as a group, and the best clues are those that feel obvious in hindsight after you’ve had a good laugh.

## House Rules and Variants
One of the joys of Codenames is how easily players adapt the rules to their group. Disney Family Edition is no exception. Here are a few house rules that keep things lively while avoiding a meltdown at the dinner table:

- Speed Round Spark: Add a 60-second timer for clues to speed up turns and inject adrenaline into the room. If time runs out and the team hasn’t concluded, the opponent gets a small bonus to steal a card or two on their turn.
- Cooperative Night: If your group wants a lighter vibe, try turning the team dynamic into a cooperative puzzle where both teams share the same grid and work to identify all agents together against a shared clock. It’s less competitive, more collaborative, and a great option for very young players.
- Disney Card Roulette: Shuffle a handful of bonus cards with special rules (like autoskip on a certain number) into the deck to spice up rounds. This can keep seasoned players from getting bored and add a few surprises during the party.

Remember: house rules should enhance fun, not monopolize it. The Disney edition is forgiving by design, so it’s easy to tailor to your crew without breaking the core experience.
