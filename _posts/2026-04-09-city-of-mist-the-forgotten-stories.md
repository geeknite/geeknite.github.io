## Overview
City of Mist, the indie noir roleplaying game where investigators moonlight as legends, invites you to chase shadows through rain-soaked alleys and neon-lit dreams. The Forgotten Stories is the latest expedition into that same moody metropolis, a supplement that asks a very simple, very delicious question: what happens when a tale was never finished, or when a legend got erased from the city’s memory like a bad password on a forgotten laptop? The answer, as this book suggests, is not chaos but a different kind of order — the order of memory, of half-remembered faces, of stories that refuse to stay buried.

If you loved the core game for its tag-based action, if you enjoyed how a single line of dialogue can flip a scene from noir to mythic, then The Forgotten Stories wants to be your new favorite toy chest. It adds a toolbox full of memory-based mechanics, new prompts for character masks, and a clutch of adventures that play with what it means for a legend to be truly forgotten. It is not a replacement for the base game; it’s a welcome expansion that leans into the city’s fissures, those cracks where a whispered rumor can become a storm.

And yes, there are maps, tables, and art that could convince you the city has a personality and a grudge against you. If you’re in it for the vibes, you’ll be hooked by page one. If you’re in it for the rules, you’ll find a set of thoughtful, GM-friendly tools that actually feel useful at the table rather than decorative. The book does not shy away from heavy themes — memory, loss, identity, and the ethics of myth-making — but it handles them with a light touch and a wink, letting players lean into the fantasy without losing the grounded grit that keeps City of Mist feel like a late-night conversation with a friend who knows way too many legends.

## Mechanics and Systems: What The Forgotten Stories Brings to the Table
### Memory as a Resource
The core idea of The Forgotten Stories is to turn memory into a resource that players spend and renegotiate as the game unfolds. In practice, this means a new pool of tokens (Memory Tokens) that players earn by engaging with the city’s past via flashbacks, flash-forwards, and retroactive corrections to an event the group knows happened but which the city seems to want to forget. These memory tokens can be spent to recall a crucial detail, reframe a scene, or pull a legend back from the edge of obscurity for a moment of clarity.

This mechanic feels like a natural extension of City of Mist’s existing tag-based options. Rather than forcing a player to narrate a perfect action, memory tokens reward evocative play: a quiet aside about a long-forgotten alley, a whispered memory of a myth that once saved a city block, or a former fear that still echoes in a character’s dreams. It’s a clever way to reward storytelling without veering into power creep. The design invites players to lean into the game’s mythic vibe, while still keeping the action tense and grounded.

### Masks, Myths, and the Echoes of History
The Forgotten Stories introduces a handful of new Masks — not to replace the player’s core concept, but to extend it. Some masks act as fragments of legends still clinging to life in the city, while others are caretaker roles for the stories that the city chooses not to remember. A few example masks (renamed here to protect the innocent, i.e., the GM) include the Archivist of Ashes, the Whispering Cartographer, and the Night Silk of Unwritten Endings. Each mask comes with a set of unique tags and starter prompts for scenes, making it easier for GMs to drop a player into a story that feels both fresh and intimately connected to the city’s buried past.

There are also new mythos and logos options that mirror the idea of forgotten knowledge. For instance, the mythos branch includes entities that exist because people chose to forget the harm they caused, turning memory into a weapon that can be wielded for good or ill. The logos options address the act of remembering itself — how a fact becomes a story, and how a story can be weaponized to alter the present. It’s a thoughtful expansion that stays true to City of Mist’s duality of myth and street-level reality.

### Story Seeds and Session Frameworks
If you are a GM who loves a good sandbox, The Forgotten Stories has you covered. It offers a curated set of session frameworks designed to invoke a memory arc: a case that begins with a half-remembered rumor, then spirals into a network of recollections that may or may not be true. The book encourages a non-linear approach to investigation: players can pursue multiple memory threads in parallel, cross-linking clues through echo moments where the past bleeds into the present. In practical terms, this means more room for improvisation, and more opportunities for players to reveal a part of their character’s mythology that even they didn’t know existed.

One GM tip the book reinforces is the power of public memory vs private memory. The Forgotten Stories plays nicely with scenes where a crowd remembers something differently from an individual, or where a memory is shared across several Masks but is incomplete for any single character. This naturally leads to dramatic scenes where the truth emerges as a mosaic rather than a single smoking gun. If you enjoy the classic City of Mist rhythm of a neon-drenched interrogation punctuated by moments of mythic intrusion, you’ll feel right at home here.

### Encounters, Locations, and World-Building Props
The book leans on a handful of new location archetypes that feel native to the City of Mist vibe: a museum that houses the city’s unspoken past, a subway tunnel where memory leaks, a theater where legends perform as echoes, and a rooftop garden that thrives on forgotten promises. Each location comes with ready-to-use encounter hooks, NPC sheets, and an assortment of memory-based challenges. There are also printable assets: doors that won’t stay closed, posters that begin to rearrange themselves when you aren’t looking, and an antique jukebox that only plays songs that never existed in the city’s present timeline.

The inclusion of Jekyll-like oddities and a few meta-narrative devices helps the GM weave together personal character arcs with broader city-wide myths. If you like running one-shot experiments that feel like a film noir in a broken clock, you’ll find plenty to mine here.

### Illustrations, Layout, and Accessibility
The Forgotten Stories continues City of Mist’s tradition of moody, evocative art that supports the mood without being overly busy. The layout is clean, with generous white space that makes it easy to skim for a session. The typography keeps the game readable even when the subject matter glares at you from the page. Accessibility remains an important consideration: the book includes content advisories and a few optional cues for handling sensitive themes with care at the table. This is the kind of thoughtful design that makes me want to run a long campaign instead of a one-off, which is the highest compliment I can give a supplement that dares to be both daring and considerate.

## Contents Breakdown: What You Get Inside The Forgotten Stories
### Chapters and Core Sections
- The Memory Market: a system for trading memory tokens and negotiating with the past.
- Masks Reforged: a set of new masks and micro-supplementary rules for integrating them into ongoing campaigns.
- Echoes and Are We Still Here: a selection of narrative tools for linking scenes via memory echoes and shared history.
- The Forgotten City: a city-wide setting frame for campaigns that emphasize how memory shapes a metropolis over decades.
- Ready-to-Run Scenarios: three to four playable stories with full GM aids, including NPC sheets, map references, and memory prompts.
- GM Toolkit: a collection of tips, tables, and prompts designed to speed up prep and keep the focus on the storytelling rather than the mechanics.
- Artwork and Design Notes: commentary on the art direction and the rationale behind the memory-based aesthetic.

This is not a cookbook with recipe steps; it’s a craft kit for shaping a living city where legends breathe and then forget their own names. If you like the idea of a campaign that feels like a memory palace you can enter at multiple points, this book delivers.

### Sample Scenario: A Memory That Never Was
Three players investigate a rumor about a missing editor who supposedly wrote a story that could rewrite the city’s memory. The twist is that the editor never existed, but fragments of their memory are stored in a hidden archive beneath the city’s oldest theater. The players must navigate a maze of stage props that come alive as memories, all while a chorus of townsfolk insists that the editor was their savior, their thief, and their downfall. The truth is a mosaic: the city forgot something, but memory is stubborn. The scenario uses the memory token economy to pace reveals and invites players to interrogate what is real and what is manufactured by collective recollection.

### Sample Scenario: The Neon Archive
A bank of digital screens across the city begins to display alternate endings to stories that never occurred. The players must identify the cause — a forgotten legend leaking into the network, or a conspiracy that wants to rewrite history to protect a single truth that benefits someone powerful. The scenario uses the Echoes mechanic to weave together betrayals, long-buried friendships, and the cost of letting legends slip away without a trace.

### Scenario Flow: How to Use The Forgotten Stories at the Table
- Start with a memory seed: a rumor, a whispered line in a bar, a poster that appears to be partially torn away by time.
- Invoke memory tokens when a player wants to reveal a past detail or correct a misremembered fact in a way that drives plot forward.
- Use echoes to connect disparate scenes, letting players realize that their personal legends are part of a larger myth that spans the city.
- Resolve with a thematic consequence rather than a strict moral conclusion. The city’s memory is messy, and the players should feel that their choices have altered what the city remembers about itself.

## GMs Corner: Tips and Tricks for Running The Forgotten Stories
### Tone, Mood, and Theme
The Forgotten Stories thrives on mood more than gait. If your table loves neon noir with a dash of myth, you’re in the sweet spot. Keep the tone grounded in character conflict, then let memory quicken the pace with mythic interference. The book suggests scenarios that encourage players to question their own motives as much as the city’s. This is a game about legends, but it is also a game about people who carry those legends in their pockets like a set of keys that unlock both doors and consequences.

### Handling Sensitive Topics with Care
Memory is a loaded concept. The authors of the book clearly want you to use memory as a mirror rather than a sword. They provide optional safety tools and guidance on handling themes such as trauma, memory loss, and the ethics of erasing a past. As always, check in with your players and use a consent checklist before diving into heavy content. The Forgotten Stories respects boundaries, and that respect makes the more intense moments land with greater impact.

### Pacing and Session Flow
One pitfall to avoid is overloading a single session with too many memory threads. The book’s recommended approach is to pick two or three memory arcs per session and let them intersect in meaningful ways. This keeps scenes tight, the pressure on the investigation clear, and the payoff satisfying. If you are a GM who loves worldbuilding, you’ll find the extra tools gratifying; if you prefer a lean, procedural game, you can still enjoy the memory economy without feeling overwhelmed.

## Art, Beauty, and Production Quality
The Forgotten Stories continues City of Mist tradition in presenting art that channels the game’s distinctive vibe: moody, painterly, and just a hint surreal. The color palette leans into twilight blues and electric pinks, a combination that feels both nostalgic and fresh. The layout is accessible, with sidebars and quick-start prompts that make prep less painful and more playful. If you collect RPG books for the vibe alone, you will be rewarded here. The aesthetics align with the themes of memory and myth in a way that makes you want to bookmark pages for mood boards and future campaigns.

## How It Fits Into the City of Mist Universe
City of Mist lives in a metropolis where myth and memory collide in the most mundane places. The Forgotten Stories leans into this premise with a stronger emphasis on memory as power. It doesn’t rewrite the core rules in any radical way; rather, it augments them with a different lens on the same city. If you enjoyed the core book, you’ll recognize familiar DNA but be delighted by the new ways to approach investigation, character development, and storytelling tempo. If you are new to City of Mist, you can still enjoy The Forgotten Stories as a gateway into the game’s signature vibe, with enough scaffolding to help you get a sense of how the system supports cinematic storytelling.

## Character Creation: Integrating Forgotten Masks into Your Party
If you already have players deeply invested in their Masks, The Forgotten Stories offers a natural path to expanding those identities. The new masks are designed to slot into an ongoing campaign without demanding a wholesale reset. This is a nice balance: you can weave a new thread into an existing story arc without forcing players to drop everything and reconfigure their personas. The new masks also invite interesting roleplay angles: what is a mythic identity that can live within a modern city without erasing the human behind the legend? The material nudges players toward nuance rather than the simple binary of hero versus villain, which is exactly the maturity a good City of Mist session benefits from.

## Accessibility, Scope, and Length
The Forgotten Stories is a book that rewards dedicated play but remains accessible to newcomers who invest a little time in reading. The writing is clear, with plenty of examples to illustrate how each mechanic might feel at the table. The book includes ready-to-run content but also leaves space for GMs to improvise and customize. If you’re worried about AI-fueled crunch or aggressive power creep, rest easy — the memory economy is designed to stay under control, letting the story lead the action instead of the dice.

## Pros and Cons: A Quick Bite
- Pros:
  - Strong thematic focus on memory and legend that complements City of Mist’s vibe.
  - Practical memory token mechanics that feel intuitive and flavorful.
  - Flexible GM tools and ready-to-run scenarios that support both long campaigns and one-off sessions.
  - Excellent art and accessible layout; improved mood and tone for sessions.
- Cons:
  - Might feel a bit same-y for players who only want straight noir investigations without mythic interference.
  - A few masks and mechanics require careful integration to avoid overcomplication; mindful GM prep helps a lot.
  - Some sections assume a certain comfort level with the base game rules; new players may need a guided intro.

If you’re craving a supplement that treats memory as a resource rather than a theme, The Forgotten Stories nails the concept with style and heart. If you prefer your RPGs less about memory, more about action, you may want to skim the sections and focus on a couple of the ready-to-run scenarios first before committing to a full campaign.

## Final Recommendation: Who Should Pick This Up?
- The GM who loves narrative control and wants a toolset to weave past and present into one seamless evening.
- Players who want deeper character arcs and a mythic layer that makes every decision feel significant.
- Any group chasing the signature City of Mist feel — moody, stylish, and punctured with honest emotion — but who also crave a new lens on memory and legends.
- Fans of table-ready expansions that respect the core system while offering fresh play experiences rather than a full reset.

If this sounds like your table, you are likely to find The Forgotten Stories not just a supplement but a new way to tell the city’s stories. It respects the core you already know while inviting you to think about memory as a living, changing substance that shapes who the heroes are becoming.

### Final Thoughts on The Forgotten Stories
In a field crowded with expansions that feel like re-skins, The Forgotten Stories stands out by offering meaningful mechanics that matter at the table. It invites you to slow down, lean into memory, and let the legends do some of the heavy lifting. The result is a campaign toolkit that feels personal and large at the same time — a rare combination that makes a roleplaying game feel truly alive. If you want your City of Mist table to squeak with memory, to hum with echoes, and to end a session with a surprising, character-driven revelation, this is a book you should consider picking up.
