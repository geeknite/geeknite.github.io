## Assembly and Setup: A practical guide for brave souls
If you are a fan of puzzles, you will enjoy the assembly narrative of the Eternal. If you are not, you will pretend to enjoy it because the end result makes you look like you know what you are doing in front of your team. The system is designed so that two or three people can get it up in a day, but the real heroes are the folks who decide to do a full reconfiguration mid- sprint because the product owner insisted on a new collaboration workflow.

### Unboxing and initial assessment
Start by laying out the modules on a clean floor and confirm you have all the components. This is the moment you discover the real stars of the show: the modular islands, the central spine, the surface panels, the legs, and a forest of screws and handy plastic shims that look suspiciously like they belong in a spaceship hatch. The instruction manual is standard, clear, and occasionally humorous, which helps keep morale high. If you have a project manager who loves process documentation, they will appreciate the assembly checklist that almost feels like a sprint backlog in cardboard form.

### Step-by-step assembly overview
- Step 1: Identify all modules and verify compatibility with your room dimensions. The Eternal ships in configurations that scale to ten seats; confirm you have a path for the central spine to avoid awkward corners.
- Step 2: Assemble the central spine and attach the core frame. The spine acts as the desk’s backbone, so treat it with reverence and do not interrogate it about its feelings.
- Step 3: Install the island modules. These are the actual collaboration stations where people gather to brainstorm and occasionally debate the best color for the wall.
- Step 4: Attach the surface panels. Ensure the edges are flush and there is no wobble. If there is wobble, you probably installed it incorrectly; re-check the legs and shims.
- Step 5: Route cables through the channels. Label your cables if you value your future self’s sanity.
- Step 6: Install optional accessories such as monitor arms, whiteboard panels, and accessory rails. These additions are what push the Eternal from a desk into the realm of collaborative theater.
- Step 7: Power up and do a quick test drive. The monitor arms and connections should behave; if not, consult the manual or maybe the team’s resident tech wizard.

## Maintenance, upgrades, and keeping the vibe alive
The Eternal is designed to be low-maintenance but not maintenance-free. Here are practical tips to keep the desk in peak condition over the years:

- Regularly check the cable channels for dust and hair. It is amazing how quickly a thin string can become a security risk for a busy team.
- When upgrading, consider the compatibility of new islands with the existing backbone. The modular ethos thrives on predictable connections and simple alignment guides.
- Clean the surface with a mild cleaner and a soft cloth to preserve the surface finish and the color balance between islands.
- If you reconfigure for a new project, document the new layout for future folks who will inherit the arrangement. A small diagram on a sticky note is enough to avoid repeat puzzles.
