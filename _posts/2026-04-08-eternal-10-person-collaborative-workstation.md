### Unboxing and initial assessment
Spread modules across a clean floor and confirm you have all the parts. The manual is clear and occasionally humorous, which helps morale. If your PM loves a sprint backlog in cardboard form, the assembly checklist will feel like a warm up.

### Step by step assembly overview
- Step 1: Identify modules and verify compatibility with your room dimensions. The Eternal ships in configurations that scale to ten seats; ensure space for the central spine and adequate access routes.
- Step 2: Assemble the central spine and attach the core frame. The spine is the desk’s backbone; treat it with respect and refrain from interrogating its feelings.
- Step 3: Install island modules. These are the actual collaboration stations where people gather to brainstorm and debate the best color for the wall.
- Step 4: Attach surface panels. Confirm edges are flush and there is no wobble. If wobble exists, you probably installed it incorrectly; re-check the legs and shims.
- Step 5: Route cables through channels. Label cables for sanity later on.
- Step 6: Install optional accessories such as monitor arms, whiteboard panels, and accessory rails. These convert the Eternal from desk to stage for collaboration.
- Step 7: Power up and run a quick test drive. Ensure monitors and arms behave. If not, consult the manual or your resident tech wizard.

## Maintenance, upgrades, and keeping the vibe alive
The Eternal is designed to be low maintenance but not maintenance free. Here are practical tips to keep the desk in peak condition over the years:

- Regularly check the cable channels for dust and hair. A thin string can become a security risk for a busy team.
- When upgrading, verify compatibility of new islands with the existing backbone. The modular ethos thrives on predictable connections and simple alignment guides.
- Clean the surface with a mild cleaner and a soft cloth to preserve the surface finish and the color balance between islands.
- If you reconfigure for a new project, document the layout for future folks who will inherit the arrangement. A small diagram on a sticky note can save hours of reassembly.
