Storage and Organization
If you want your Sorcery Set 24 to stay pristine for years, you need to decide how you want to store the cards and how you will catalog them. Here are a few practical tips:
1) Sorting: Start with year and rarity as your primary keys. The secondary keys can be foil or language variants. This makes it easy to pull out a specific version when you want to compare printings.
2) Sleeves and protectors: Standard inner sleeves with a top loader can protect the cards during transport. If you plan to display many cards, consider thematically arranged binders so you can rotate the display without exposing cards to sunlight.
3) Storage environment: A stable humidity environment is essential to avoid warping. Keep away from direct sunlight and high heat; MTG cards do not age well under UV light unless you are purposely creating a vintage deck.
4) Cataloging: Use a simple spreadsheet or a cataloging app. Include fields such as card name, year, rarity, language, foil status, and any notes about misprints or border changes. A quick field like Condition (Near Mint, Lightly Played) can be helpful later on when you decide to sell or trade.

Authenticity and Fakes
In the world of MTG, counterfeit cards exist, though they are far more common for highly sought after and expensive cards. With a Sorcery Set 24 lot, you should be especially vigilant for:
- Incorrect borders or font anomalies that betray a counterfeit production
- Black light under the card that reveals non standard printing
- The card weight and stiffness that may differ from genuine cards
- Border color deviations or misprint illusions that can fool an untrained eye

A safe approach is to examine cards under good lighting and, if necessary, consult an expert or use a reputable grading service. A smart path is to work with known sellers and obtain a verified lot that includes a return policy in case of authenticity concerns.
