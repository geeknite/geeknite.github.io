## How to play in a party setting without losing your mind

Exploding Kittens is essentially a game of draw, bluff, and dodge. The Party Pack intensifies this by increasing the pool of cards and turning the tempo up a notch. Here is a quick guide to getting a party rolling without turning the table into a battlefield:

### The baseline rules you need to know
- Players take turns drawing cards from the deck until someone draws an exploding kitten card and loses the round unless they have a defuse card in hand. Defuse cards neutralize the exploding kitten, often in the most outrageous ways possible.
- On your turn you can perform a variety of actions that may prevent you from drawing the kitten by using defuse cards or by playing action cards that alter the deck composition or protection status.
- The goal in a party pack setting is to outlast the other players by staying calm, keeping track of the deck, and reading the table as if you are playing a quiet game of poker while wearing a clown nose.

### Tactics that work in practice
- Keep an eye on what cards have been played. A mental tally helps you gauge your risk as the deck thins out. This is not exact science, but it gives you something to do while the rest of the table is erupting in laughter.
- Use your defuse cards strategically. If you are near the end of a round with several players left, a defuse can be a lifesaver, turning a near loss into a small victory by letting you continue the game despite a near miss.
- Bluffing and misdirection can be priceless when you have a deck full of chaotic options. The Party Pack rewards players who can read a room and play a card that makes others doubt their own strategy.

### House rules that can spice things up
- Quick rounds rule: everyone draws one card and then the person with the exploding kitten immediately shares a brag or a penalty. This speeds up games and keeps the room bouncing.
- No look passes: you must show your hand to at least one other player before you draw, which often leads to dramatic reveals and hilarious accusations of cheating.
- Team play: pair up players and let teams decide who taps into the defuse action in critical moments. Team dynamics can be a riot and a headache in equal measure, but it is a fun experiment for a large group night.

## Practical tips for tonight’s game night

- Bring a snacks tray that is wide enough to hold a couple of defuse cards and not block the draw deck. You will thank yourself later when the table remains collectible and clean.
