## Practical Considerations: Cleaning and Maintenance

One of the most important questions with any rug is how forgiving it is with accidents and daily life. The Leilani Boho Chic Cream Rug is designed with everyday vacuuming in mind. It responds well to gentle suction; heavy agitation can cause loose fibers to fray over time, so treat it with respect, not a power-hungry vacuum. For small spills, a quick blot with a clean cloth usually handles most liquids. If you spill coffee, red wine, or your own daytime latte mishaps, the stain tends to stay contained in the top layer, which makes spot cleaning practical rather than a full rug revival.

In terms of maintenance, it’s worth using a rug pad to keep the rug in place and to provide extra cushion. A pad not only helps with wear and tear but also reduces the risk of accidental trips as you walk from the kitchen to the living room with a mug of tea. Cleaning agents should be used sparingly; harsh chemicals can dull the texture and alter the color unevenly, something no one wants when they are trying to achieve a chic, cohesive look. If you have pets, the rug is tolerant of fur and minor accidents, but you should be prepared to clean more regularly than you would with a plain carpet. A gentle enzymatic cleaner on older stains can be effective, but always test on a small patch first.

## Cleaning and Care Deep Dive

For stubborn stains and deeper cleaning, you can follow a simple routine:
- Blot, don’t rub, to avoid fiber damage.
- Use a mild detergent with water and apply sparingly.
- For pet accidents, a gentle enzymatic cleaner helps break down organic matter and odors.
- Allow the rug to dry completely before putting heavy furniture back on top to avoid color transfer.

Avoid steam cleaning unless your rug type is explicitly labeled as steam-safe. High heat can distort pile height and color. If you’re not sure, a quick consult with customer support from the retailer is a smart move.
