### What's in the Box Hitch?

The Huntsmen Box Hitch is not just a bag of toys; it’s a carefully curated set of tools meant to alter how you approach a Black Plague session. You’ll typically find:

- A cadre of Hunters: new survivor miniatures built around ranged and trap-based playstyles. They’re not just cardboard alternatives to your core survivors; they have unique abilities that reward planning and positioning.
- New Zombie Types: expect more undead with special rules that reward clever tactics. The dynamics between the Hunters and the undead are where the box sings, and if you enjoy a good chess match with swords and teeth, you’ll hear it clearly.
- Scenario Cards: these are the heart of the box’s replayability, offering varied setups and victory conditions that keep things fresh beyond your standard “kill all zombies” loop.
- Tokens and Boards: you’ll recognize the familiar punch of z-pose cards, goal markers, and trap tokens—components that look rugged enough to survive a dragon’s breath and a cat’s curiosity alike.
- A compact rule booklet: it won’t turn your brain into mush, but it will require some attention if you’re juggling multiple expansions at once.

If you’re coming from a vanilla Baseline or just dipping your toes into the Black Plague waters, you’ll want to sort through these items with an eye toward how they alter your risk calculus. The base mechanics—select a survivor, move, fight, scavenge—remain intact, but the Huntsmen introduce nuance that can tilt a tight turn into a potential misstep if you don’t respect the new tempo.

## Setup and Components: The Good, The Bad, and The Moldy Cheese

### Component Quality
The miniatures in Huntsmen are sharp, with crisp sculpting that benefits from a proper cleanup and a touch of plastic cement if you want them to survive a few campaign nights. The hunters themselves strike a balance between stylized fantasy and practical tabletop use: they’re distinct enough to tell apart at a distance, but clump-friendly for those who value silhouette over minute detail.

The zombie plastics show that CMON didn’t cut corners here; you’ll see the same design language as other Black Plague expansions, with some special “spawn” tokens that are thematically tied to the hunter doctrine. The cardboard tokens are sturdy, the boards are stable, and the rulebook—while dense—does a decent job of guiding you through the new rules and how they influence your scoring.

### Assembly and Prep Time
Let’s be honest: anything larger than a core set will require some assembly time. The Huntsmen can demand a few minutes of patience if you want your survivors to look like they care about their hygiene on a battlefield. If you’re aiming for a full-on display army, you’ll want clippers, a sharp hobby knife, plastic cement, and a few moments of careful glueing to avoid a misaligned crossbow or a helmet rolled halfway across the table.

If you’re the kind of person who hates opening a box and immediately diving in, you’ll love how the box’s layout invites you to pick a starting point. There’s enough impulse to immediately start a game, but enough complexity that you’ll want to inch your way into the full experience rather than sprinting through it in one parabolic sprint.

### Thematic Flair and Visuals
The Huntsmen Box Hitch leans into the hunter archetype—think leather straps, bristling quivers, and pouches full of improvised traps. The box art and the color palette scream of a medieval fantasy tavern that survived a siege and then drafted a policy forbidding hunting inside city walls after midnight. In short: it looks like a box you want on your shelf and a set of miniatures you’ll want on the table for a long time.

Technically, the design language aligns with Black Plague’s grim, gritty atmosphere, which is exactly the vibe most players want when they’re cracking open a box after a workday and pretending they’re a hero who deserves a tall ale and a full victory celebration.

## How It Plays: The Huntsmen Way

### New Survivors and their Shiny Abilities
The Huntsmen introduce survivors whose core hook is ranged engagement and trap-craft. They reward careful positioning, card management, and map control. Some survivors offer two actions per turn if they’re using the right terrain, while others trigger extra effects if you finish your move near a trap. It’s not a revolution, but it’s a welcomed nudge toward more tactical firefights than a straight humanoid surge.

Expect synergy with the core set mechanics: you still draw action cards, you still manage morale, and you still wrestle with AI-controlled undead. The difference is that instead of a straightforward “pin the undead down and swing,” you’ll be balancing the risk of exposure with the potential for a big, planned-finisher turn using traps and ranged firepower.

### New Enemies and Encounter Flow
The new zombie types add enough flavor to alter the pacing. Some come with special agendas that force you to make chess-like decisions about which zombie group to tackle first and how to manage line-of-sight hazards. The box encourages you to set up ambushes and keep your hunters in the right vantage points, turning a typical “walk up and smash” moment into something closer to a skirmish in a high-stakes siege.

The encounter flow remains familiar: spawn, move, attack, drain, repeat. Yet you’ll find that the Huntsmen pushes you toward a more deliberate tempo. When you dodge a wave with a well-placed trap, you feel like you earned it. When the dice betray you and the trap misses its mark, you still feel like you learned something about field position for the next game—this is exactly the kind of learning-by-doing that keeps long-running campaigns compelling.

### Rule Interactions and Complexity Curve
The silencers and quivers add a layer of tactical pain, but not to the point of gatekeeping new players. The learning curve is there, but it’s approachable if you take your time with the rulebook and a handful of practice turns. We recommend a quick “trial run” scenario before launching into a full campaign with the Huntsmen. This reduces table chatter and reduces the chance of someone misreading a special card, which, let’s be honest, is the fastest way to ignite a debate about who gets to roll dice at what moment.

## Value, Longevity, and Replayability

### Is It Worth the Price?
As with most mid-to-late-life expansions for a popular system, value hinges on how much you actually want to immerse yourself in the Huntsmen’s playstyle. If you’re a casual player who enjoys occasional one-offs, this box still offers a substantial set of new miniatures, scenario cards, and tokens that can breathe new life into a few sessions. If you’re a campaign-minded player who wants a steady trickle of new mechanics to rotate with other expansions, the Huntsmen delivers enough variety to justify its cost over a dozen gaming nights.

### Replayability Factor
You’ll find that the scenario cards alone are the box’s sticky-note-laden glue. They give you varied objectives, map layouts, and win conditions that force you to rethink your approach. It’s the kind of box that doesn’t “finish you” in one evening. It invites you to come back, try different survivors, and discover which trap setups yield the most satisfying successes. If you’re building a modular Black Plague stack, Huntsmen is one of those boxes that scales well with the rest, allowing you to mix-and-match factions and map types without feeling like you’re stepping into a brand-new hobby every time.

### Longevity in a Box Shelf World
The rule-set remains approachable, the miniatures have shelf appeal, and the components will hold stability with routine game nights. The box’s value is amplified if you own other expansions because you can layer hunters with other variants for insane, cinematic battles. If you are a solitary player, the box still gives you a more thoughtful solo experience, though you will need to adapt to a ruleset that sometimes expects a group of players to coordinate. For the most part, though, it scales well—the kind of expansion that helps you stretch your base game’s lifespan without turning into a full-blown campaign-addiction headache.

## Final Verdict and Rating

Overall, Zombicide Black Plague: Huntsmen Box Hitch is a well-judged expansion that respects the game’s roots while giving players a fresh angle on combat and positioning. It rewards planning, punishes overconfidence, and creates memorable moments around traps, crossfire, and that satisfying “overload the board” moment when the hunters finally corner the swarm. If you love tactical chases through a medieval battlefield with a wink and a nod to fantasy archery, this box is a solid bet. It doesn’t reinvent the wheel, but it polishes a familiar ride until it shines like a gleaming blade in a dungeon hallway.

Pros:
- Distinct hunter playstyle adds nuance without crippling the core rules.
- Scenario cards provide repeatable, varied experiences.
- High-quality miniatures and components with strong table presence.
- Flexible enough to mix with other expansions for deeper campaigns.

Cons:
