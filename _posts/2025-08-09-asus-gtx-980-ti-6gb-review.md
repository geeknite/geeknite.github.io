## Introduction

The Asus GTX 980 Ti 6GB was once the crown jewel of Nvidia's lineup, boasting impressive specs and performance that made gamers drool. But now, in the era of RTX and DLSS, does it still hold up? Can it handle modern games, or is it better off as a paperweight? Let's find out!

Here's a quick rundown of the GTX 980 Ti's specs:

| Feature | Specification |

| **Memory** | 6GB GDDR5 |

### Gaming

In its heyday, the GTX 980 Ti 6GB was a beast in gaming benchmarks. It could handle most games at 1080p and 1440p with high settings, delivering smooth gameplay and impressive visuals. But how does it fare now?

| Game | Resolution | Settings | Average FPS |
|------|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Cyberpunk 2077** | 1080p | Medium | 45 FPS |
| **Control** | 1080p | Medium | 50 FPS |
| **Assassin's Creed Valhalla** | 1080p | Medium | 55 FPS |

Not too shabby for a relic, right? If you're okay with dialing down the settings a bit, the 980 Ti can still deliver a decent gaming experience. Just don't expect it to keep up with the big boys like the RTX 4090.

### Productivity

For those who use their GPU for more than just gaming, the GTX 980 Ti still has some utility. It's not going to blow your mind with blazing fast render times, but it can handle video editing, 3D rendering, and basic machine learning tasks. Programs like Adobe Premiere Pro and Blender run decently, but you might want to grab a snack while waiting for those render times.

## Power Consumption and Cooling

With a TDP of 250W, the GTX 980 Ti is a power-hungry beast. Make sure you have a decent power supply to feed this monster. Cooling is equally important; dust off that aftermarket cooler or invest in a new one to keep temperatures in check.

## Design and Aesthetics

The Asus GTX 980 Ti follows the classic Nvidia design language, with a black and silver color scheme that looks pretty darn good in any build. It's not flashy, but it's a workhorse. Unfortunately, no built-in RGB lighting here, folks. But hey, who needs RGB when you have raw power?

## Price and Value

You can find used GTX 980 Ti cards for around $150-$200 nowadays. For budget builders, this might be an attractive option if you're looking to save some cash. Just remember, you get what you pay for.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Still decent performance for 1080p gaming**
- **Affordable for budget builds**
- **Reliable and durable**

### Cons

- **Power-hungry**
- **No modern features like Ray Tracing or DLSS**

## Final Verdict

The Asus GTX 980 Ti 6GB may be old, but it's not dead yet. It offers decent performance for 1080p gaming and can handle productivity tasks without breaking (too much of) a sweat. If you're on a tight budget or building a retro gaming rig, the 980 Ti is a solid choice.
