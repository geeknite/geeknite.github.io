## Practical tips: how to get the most out of your 1000-card haul

- Sort as soon as you can. Create piles for common, uncommon, rare, foil, and mythic. This reduces fatigue and makes it easier to spot duplicates you can trade or flip.
- Sleeve your foils and rarities. It protects the glow and preserves your ability to display or transport without scuffing.
- Trade with friends. One person’s “meh” rare is another person’s dream-piece. The social value of trading often dwarfs the monetary value of the cards themselves.
- Build two or three quick decks from the pool. Even if you don’t plan to play a lot, having ready-to-go decks helps you realize the box’s value in practical terms.
- Document your pulls. If you’re a content creator, a simple “pull recap” video or post can attract viewers who love the drama of opening a box together with you.
- Consider the local meta. If you’re playing with a casual group, many commons and uncommons become the backbone of quick, friendly formats like chaos draughts or sealed sessions.
