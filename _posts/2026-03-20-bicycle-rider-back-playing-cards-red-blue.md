## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

### Cleaning and Handling

A simple wipe with a dry microfiber cloth is enough to remove dust or fingerprints. Avoid aggressive cleaning with solvents; you don’t want to strip the finish or strip the deck of its personality. If you spill something on the cards, the best course is to lay them flat, dab gently, and let air circulation do the work. Don’t force-dry with heat—card stock hates heat as much as I hate loading a playlist with 97 versions of the same song.

### Storage and Humidity

Store in a cool, dry place. Excess humidity can cause warping or sticking, which is bad news for a deck that wants to glide like a well-oiled slide on a perfect playground. A simple deck box or tuck case can help protect the cards from the elements, accidental spills, and any ambitious household pets with a chaotic sense of curiosity.

### Longevity in a Word

If you treat them with reasonable care, these cards will outlast your enthusiasm for casual poker nights. They won’t be the burden of your grandkids’ math homework, but they will be a trusty companion for countless games and a few card tricks that might just earn you a nod from your cat for being mildly impressive.
