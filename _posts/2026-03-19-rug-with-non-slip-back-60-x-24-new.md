## Care and Maintenance

### Cleaning tips

Regular vacuuming with a standard setting keeps the surface clean without shredding the pile. For spills, blot gently, then wipe with a mild detergent if needed. Avoid harsh solvents that could deteriorate the backing or fade dyes. Allow the rug to dry completely before subjecting it to heavy traffic again to prevent indentations.

### Stains and removal

Stain removal is more predictable with polypropylene fibers. Most common spills respond to quick blotting and a mild cleaner. For tougher stains, test in a hidden corner first, then proceed with small sections to avoid color migration. The key is to treat quickly and avoid scrubbing harshly, which can loosen fibers or scratch the backing over time.

### Longevity and repairs

Over time, the backing can wear, especially in the busiest zones. If you notice creep or edge curling, consider a reorganization of the space or a rug pad as a supplementary layer. In some cases, a full rug replacement is the most cost-effective option when grip begins to fail or the surface shows significant wear.

Consider your floor type and how much cushion you want in daily life.
