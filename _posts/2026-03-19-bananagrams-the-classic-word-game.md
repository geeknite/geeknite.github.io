## How to play Bananagrams

### Setup
Dump all tiles into the banana bag. Each player draws a starting handful of tiles; a common starting point is around 18 tiles for two players, with adjustments for more players. The exact count is flexible because the banana bag is the regulatory body here. Your group can agree on a starter size, a time limit, or a vibe check that ends in applause or groans, depending on how your day is going.

### Objective
Build a grid of interconnected words using all your tiles. The word grid should be a valid structure that holds together with the stability of a well-timed punchline. When someone finishes their grid and uses every tile, they triumph and declare banana—may as well crown the victor with a crown made of plastic vowels.

### Turn flow
The game begins with a clock starting or a mutual agreement that time is now. Players race to reorganize their grids, drawing tiles from the pool to fill holes or replace poor choices. There is no fixed board; your grid grows across the table like a living, shouting letters sculpture. The chaos is part of the charm.

### Word validation
Words just have to be accepted by the group. There is no dictionary police; you are the supreme authority for your table. If a word seems dubious, you propose a compromise or swap in a friendlier term. Cross-checks happen as your grid expands; each word must be connected and valid within the group’s consensus. It is democracy with a banana peel on top.

## Strategy corner

### Tile management
The big secret is flexibility. Don’t hoard vowels as if they are sacred relics. A balanced mix of vowels and consonants acts like fuel for your grid engine. If you can juggle the vowels early, you unlock more cross connections later. Remember, the goal is not to craft the longest word but to assemble a grid that makes sense and can evolve under pressure.

### Word building
Anchor with short, reliable words to create a backbone, then layer longer terms that interlink through multiple tiles. A well-placed letter can open doors to new crosswords and new scoring opportunities in your own head and in the eyes of your tablemates.

### Time pressure tactics
Sped-up rounds reward decisive, clean moves. Don’t chase a fancy term if it means you’ll stumble on a vowel or two. If a layout looks stuck, consider a quick shuffle and a bold reframe. The ability to pivot under pressure is a skill that translates beyond the table into life, love, and getting the last slice of pizza.
