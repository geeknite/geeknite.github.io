## Box contents and unboxing: what you really get

### The cast: William and company

The Huntsmen Box orbits around William, the titular huntsman. William is a brusque, bow-wielding survivor whose kit includes a longbow, a hunting knife, and a few shiny tricks designed to keep the zombie apocalypse honest. He’s flinty, resourceful, and surprisingly good at playing keep-away with the undead—perfect for players who want to feel like a sniper in a plague apocalypse story rather than a tank marching into the teeth of the swarm.

Also included are three other survivors who complement William’s style: a skirmisher with a crossbow, a healer with a traditional toolkit of knives and herbs, and a trapper whose specialty is terrain manipulation. If you want to run a small-scale action sequence where archery keeps the horde at bay while your healer patches the party between misadventures, Huntsmen lets you pretend you’re in a fantasy war movie rather than a mono-lithic zombie express.

## Mechanics, balance, and how it plays with base Black Plague

### William’s kit: what does he actually do?

William isn’t just a character with a fancy bow; his skill set is designed to change the tempo of the game. His abilities encourage distance management, terrain control, and precise timing. ### Game flow with Huntsmen: pace and tension

The Huntsmen expansion emphasizes tempo over raw brawn. The designers mitigate this with modest per-turn limits on precision shots and mission modifiers that reward strategic play while keeping the table accessible. If your group leans casual, you might want to house-rule a cap on how often a single survivor can perform a precise shot in a round. If you love optimizing builds and feeling like a grandmaster archer of a plague zone, Huntsmen should feel incredibly satisfying rather than fiddly.

As with many Zombicide products, the card layout sticks to familiar formatting. The hunting motif is reinforced by icons for tracking, line of sight, and terrain interactions.
