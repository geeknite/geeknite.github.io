## Components quality and design philosophy

### Miniatures and plastic quality

The miniatures in Green Horde are CMON’s typical resin-lite plastics — they snap cleanly (with a bit of green stuff if you want to fix a misalignment) and hold paint nicely if you decide to paint. If you’re not a painter, they still read well on the table, with figures distinguished by silhouette and weapon loadout rather than relying on overly elaborate sculpts. The scale is consistent with other Zombicide sets, which makes mixing and matching from your shelf feel seamless rather than a mismatch of heroic proportions.

### Cards, boards, and tokens

The card stock on terrain and equipment cards is sturdy; sleeves are optional but recommended if you intend to play frequently, as sleeves can help prevent the occasional edge wear from enthusiastic table flips. The tiles fit snugly in the box and dangle no more than a fraction of a millimeter after a few drops or cat-led misadventures. Tokens are clearly printed, though you may find yourself counting them twice to avoid mixing them up with other boards if you keep multiple Zombicide sets out on the table.

### The dice and combat feel

Zombicide’s core is built on the tension of dice and board state. Green Horde follows suit, with a dice pool that rewards careful planning but doesn’t punish you beyond your own fumbles. You’ll roll to attack, defend, and sometimes to trigger specials that feel both cinematic and just chaotic enough to keep you laughing as you watch your tactical plan collapse into a glorious pile of green chaos.

### Aesthetics and flavor

The art direction leans heavily into a fantasy tone—orcish green hordes, cavernous terrain, and a sense that you are fighting a slightly more clever undead than your ordinary shamblers. It’s not grimdark all day, but it’s flavorful enough to earn a place on the shelf as a centerpiece for your fantasy board game night. If you like your fantasy with a side of survival horror and a lot of heroism in the face of overwhelming odds, Green Horde scratches that itch without becoming a full-blown dungeon crawler.

Coordination is the heartbeat of Zombicide.
