## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity

Carbon steel blades are glorious when well cared for, and they punish neglect with an oiling invoice you’ll regret. Here are some practical maintenance tips to keep your Crusader dagger in peak condition:

- Regular oiling: A light film of mineral oil or a dedicated blade oil is your friend. It prevents rust, reduces corrosion, and helps maintain a beautiful patina that adds to the knife’s character.
- Cleaning: After use, wipe with a dry cloth to remove moisture and residues. For more thorough cleaning, use a mild soap with water, dry completely, and re-oil.
- Storage: Keep the blade dry and store it in its sheath when possible. If you’re in a humid climate, consider desiccants in the storage area to reduce moisture exposure.
- Patina management: Some folks cherish patina as a sign of a blade’s life. If you want to maintain a pristine look, you’ll want to wipe and oil more aggressively to slow patina formation. Either path is valid—choose your aesthetic and stay consistent.
