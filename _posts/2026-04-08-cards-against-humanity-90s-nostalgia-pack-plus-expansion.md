## Nostalgia as design pressure: what works and what doesn’t

90s nostalgia can be a powerful, joyful drug, but it’s also a minefield. There are a few prompts that lean into well-worn clichés: fashion faux pas, tech embarrassments, and media moments that defined a generation. For some players, those references spark immediate recognition and a flood of “remember when” commentary that lasts longer than a playlist arc. For others, the jokes may feel bone-dry or overly specific to a subset of viewers who could recite the exact date of a particular pop-culture moment.

The Plus Expansion attempts to mitigate this risk with a more diverse mix of prompts. Some cards target more universal experiences (like the universal frustration of dial-up tones or the misfit joy of early wearable tech), while others lean into deeper cut references. The result is a game that can be hilariously relatable in a large group or delightfully obscure in a tight circle of 90s enthusiasts. If your group consists of people who attended the same yearbook-adjacent high school and still quote commercials, you’ll be in comedic heaven. If you’re playing with people who matter-of-factly declare “I was born in 2004,” brace yourself for the “you had to be there” moments that CAH thrives on.
