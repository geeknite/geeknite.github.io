H3: Wood and Finish
Acacia wood in this set is typically pressed into planks for slats on the tabletop and the chair seats. It’s not as dense as teak, but it’s fairly resilient against moisture when cared for. Expect some minor checking and shifting over time if you don’t seal it, especially in climates with big seasonal shifts. The finish on most production batches is a light sealant or oil that highlights the grain and provides a modest barrier against the elements. H2: Weather Resistance and Longevity
Outdoor furniture has to survive sun, rain, and occasional stray BBQ sauce mishaps. Acacia wood is fairly forgiving if properly maintained, but it isn’t invincible. Expect some color change and minor patina over time, especially if you neglect sealing oils or protective finishes.

H3: Weather Strategy
The recommended care routine is straightforward: clean occasionally with mild soap and water, dry promptly, and oil with a protector designed for exterior wood roughly every 6-12 months depending on climate. If you live in a humid or coastal area, you’ll want to treat more often to fend off mildew and the occasional salt bloom. If you neglect it entirely, you’ll end up with a dull, grayish patina that looks cool in a post-apocalyptic vibe but not so much in a backyard brunch scenario.

H3: Seasonal Storage and Life Expectancy
When winter hits or you know you won’t use the set for extended periods, store the pieces in a dry shelter or cover them with a breathable outdoor cover. Sun and moisture over the years can cause slight warping, but modern acacia tends to resist major distortion if you’ve kept it honest with oiling and storage. Expect 3–7 years with minimal maintenance to be a reasonable expectation in many environments, with proper care extending life well beyond that in a favorable climate.

H2: Care and Maintenance Quick Guide
- Cleaning: Wipe with mild soap and water. Don’t soak the wood; you’ll want to avoid standing moisture that invites mildew.
- Oil and protection: Apply a wood oil or exterior-grade sealer every 6–12 months, depending on climate. In harsher sun or salty zones, lean toward more frequent treatment.
- Storage: When not in use for extended periods, store indoors or cover with a breathable outdoor cover to minimize moisture exposure.
- Cushions: If you plan to add cushions, choose weather-resistant fabric and remove or store cushions during heavy rain to preserve fabric life and prevent mold.
