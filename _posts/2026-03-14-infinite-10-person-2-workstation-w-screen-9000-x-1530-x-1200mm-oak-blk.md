## Practical How-To and Tips for Getting the Most Out of It
- Plan the layout before you move the beast in. Draw a map of seat positions, screen coverage, and cable routes. The fewer “surprise corners” you encounter, the smoother your setup.
- Invest in modular cable management. Velcro straps, grommets, and zip ties are your friends; a messy cable jungle is not a good look on a desk you want to show off in a case study video.
- Calibrate the screen for uniform brightness across the seating positions. The last thing you want is a seat with a dim panel that makes design reviews a moral test of endurance.
- Consider a stand-up option for part of the day. Standing for a portion of meetings helps keep energy levels up and reduces the risk of a post-lunch slump turning into a conference call coma. For ergonomics, alternating between sit and stand helps keep posture dynamic.
