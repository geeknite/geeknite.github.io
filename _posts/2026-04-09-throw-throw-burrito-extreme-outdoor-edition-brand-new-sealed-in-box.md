## Setup and play space: outdoor rules of engagement

Outdoor play is a different beast. Indoor, you can trust the table to hold the tension of a tense standoff between a burrito and a card deck. Outdoors, the wind may become a rogue third player. Here are practical tips to get you from box-to-battlefield without sacrificing your sanity or your friends’ ankles:

- Space matters: aim for at least a 6x6 meter play area if you can. If you’re at a park, mark a circle with a chalk line or a laptop charging cord striped with caution tape—whatever helps you not slice the chaos into a physics lab.
- Surface considerations: grass and dirt are actually fine for this game; they cushion accidental dives and hide the odd burrito-brandishing footprint. Avoid slick surfaces; you don’t want a burrito ledge to become the star of a dramatic slip montage.
- Safety first: the two plush burritos are soft enough to be safe, but you should still keep a protective distance from faces and eyes. The rulebook suggests eye protection for the most enthusiastic players, which is a hilarious debate to have with your group while the burritos are airborne.
- Weather a factor: sun glare on cards is a real risk. Use a shaded spot if possible or bring a sun umbrella. The Outdoors Edition doesn’t promise sunburn immunity; it promises good times and a few dramatic sunbursts when the wind picks up.

## How to play: quick overview with some outdoor twists

If you’ve played Throw Throw Burrito before, you know the rhythm: collect cards, throw burritos, get hit, repeat. If you haven’t, here’s the core loop in the Extreme Outdoor Edition, tuned for open skies and questionable lawn care:

1) Shuffle and deal a hand of mission cards face-down. Your goal is to complete a set to win the round.
2) On your turn, you can draw a card and then attempt to throw one of the burritos toward an opponent. The target is a non-target area on their torso or the card pile they’re defending; the exact targets are explained in the rulebook with clear diagrams.
3) If you miss, congratulations: you’ve only added drama, not penalty. If you hit, you might steal a card or trigger a penalty—depends on the mission card in play.
4) The round ends when someone completes a set or when the burrito chaos causes a dramatic fall into a picnic basket (kidding, we don’t condone breakage… unless it’s a comedic moment in the show).

A few nuanced tips for the outdoors:
- Wind management matters more than you think. Shorter throws reduce drift; longer throws can become glorious arc shots if you have the patience of a fantasy archer.
- A soft target is your friend. The more you practice, the quicker you’ll learn how to bounce a burrito off a bench and land it exactly where you need it.
- Positioning is everything. Don’t stand in the direct line of a potential burrito ricochet. You’ll thank yourself later as you limp away from a heroic yet mildly embarrassing moment.

## Strategy tips for the outdoor arena

- Master the wind: learn to adjust your aim by subtly angling your throw against the wind. It’s not just about power; it’s about the arrow’s arc finding the target through gusts as if your burrito had a tiny parabolic destiny.
- Use the terrain: a bench, a tree stump, or a blanket edge can serve as an improvised obstacle or bounce pad. The best players treat the lawn like a mini-Greek amphitheater where every throw is a chorus line.
- Team up, but don’t cheat the game: coordinate with teammates to distract opponents or to cover a crucial card pile. The chaos multiplies when strategy sneaks in between bursts of laughter.
- Safety first, always: gentle throws, protective eyeglasses in glasses-only events, and a couple of napkins ready for sunscreen jelly or a rogue burrito residue on your sleeve.
