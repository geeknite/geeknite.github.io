## Maintenance: keeping the chaos under control

Foosball is a game of fine-tuned mechanical balance. Keeping the surface clean, the rods lubricated sparingly, and the bearings free of grit is essential for longevity. Here are some simple habits:

- Wipe the surface after each session to remove snacks, dust, and accidental drips from beverages. It helps preserve the playing surface and keeps the magnets of your brain from getting stuck on sticky residue.
- Lightly lubricate the rods with a silicone or dry-lube spray a few times per year (or after a long period of non-use). Avoid heavy oils that can attract dust and gum up bearings.
- Check leg levelers for stability. A wobble is not cute in a high-stakes rally; it is a performance penalty that your ego will remember long after the last goal.
- Inspect ball return paths and goal pockets for signs of wear. If you hear grinding or feel a rough edge when the ball returns, it’s time to address the metal-to-plastic contacts and, if needed, replace the worn parts before the next tournament.

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it is a form of self-respect for your future self who will thank you for preventing jams and mis-kicks during the next friendly tournament.
