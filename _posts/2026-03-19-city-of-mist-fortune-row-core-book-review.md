## What Fortune Row Is (And Isn’t)

Fortune Row is designed to be the beating heart of a City of Mist campaign. It isn’t simply a setting to describe a street or a district; it’s a fully realized locale with rhythms, factions, mythic strata, and a few secrets that require players to use the core mechanics in flexible, clever ways. The core book presents Fortune Row as a city block—no, a constellation of blocks—where every corner whispers a legend and every storefront has a rumor about it. It’s a place that invites both a detective’s careful logic and a myth-maker’s willingness to bend reality just enough to make it interesting.

The book uses the City of Mist system: players create Masks—personal mythic identities that are a blend of power set and personal flaw. They aren’t just “superheroes” in capes. They’re people whose public masks reflect private motives, fears, and desires. Fortune Row gives you new Masks tailored to the district’s secrets, new Mythos (the city’s gods, spirits, and legends), and new rules-adjacent tools to help you run sessions that feel both cinematic and personal.

Fortune Row’s design ethos: give the GM a toolbox with knobs labeled momentum, risk, consequence, and glamour, and then hand players a velvet rope of choices that makes them feel like they control the city’s narrative arc—while the rules keep it fair and playable. The result is a book that invites micro-plot seeds that can blossom into a long arc, as well as one-shot hooks for a single night when your group wants a quick neon caper.

## System and Mechanics: What Makes Fortune Row Tick

### Core Dice, Tags, and Checks

City of Mist uses a dice pool system where players roll two six-sided dice for actions and compare results to a target. The core mechanic is thoughtful rather than brute-force: players leverage Tags—adjectives or descriptors on their Mask or the world—to modify dice pools. Fortune Row refines these choices by giving you a curated set of Tags that feel like “local” and “mythic” options at the same time. You aren’t just saying you have a high-stakes tag; you’re stating a fact about Fortune Row itself—how the venue you’re in, the crowd you’re with, or the legend you’re invoking might tilt the odds.

This keeps the action anchored in the setting and reduces the risk of “GM says yes, but the dice say no” type clashes. It also helps the GM stage scenes with appropriate tone and pacing. If you enjoy the way a well-chosen Tag turns a routine stakeout into a scene with personal stakes, you’ll love Fortune Row’s emphasis on local texture. The book leans into how a city block with a hundred whispers can become a stage for a hundred different outcomes.

### Masks, Mythos, and Momentum

In Fortune Row, Masks aren’t static. The district’s myths push back, shaping what a Mask can do in a given moment. The Mythos chapter of the book offers an updated palette of legends—gods of graffiti, spirits of the subway, or urban legends of the rain that never ends. The interplay between Masks and Mythos is where the game hits its stride: players craft a personal myth that can be endangered by Fortune Row’s evolving secrets.

Momentum, as a resource, remains the lifeblood of sessions. You build Momentum through successful scenes, clever uses of Tags, and even through collaboration with other players. Fortunes, in Fortune Row’s design, aren’t random; they’re earned by players through choices that honor both their Masks and the city’s needs. The GM can escalate or deflate a scene by shifting the momentum balance, orchestrating a rhythm that feels like a heartbeat beneath neon signage.

### Investigative Play, Not Just Punch-Through-Doors

What Fortune Row does particularly well is encourage investigative play that stays intimate. You’re not forced into a cookie-cutter pattern of “investigate, fight, repeat.” Instead, you’ll find subplots that relate to a local business, a secretive guild, or a mythic residue that only appears when a character’s core desire is activated. Investigative play in Fortune Row rewards players who ask the right questions and who understand that a clue in this city might be a person’s fear as much as a document’s timestamp.

If you love the moment when a clue aligns with a character’s mask, Fortune Row gives you a steady stream of those moments—enough structure to feel purposeful, enough flexibility to avoid predictability.

## World-Build, Local Color, and Thematic Tone

Fortune Row is not about creating a generic city; it’s about building a district that breathes with character. The book leans into the idea that mythic forces are real in this landing zone and that people’s beliefs shape reality—sometimes in messy, beautiful ways. The writing is punchy and cinematic, with vivid descriptions that help you picture the rain-slick street signs, the flicker of neon signs, the graffiti that reads like a scripture, and the kind of crowd you only meet when you’re chasing a myth to its source.

The factions in Fortune Row feel distinct and sticky. You’ll have street-level crews who know the city’s secrets, developers who may be more compelled by power than by profit, and a few legendary figures who exist in a gray area between urban legend and actual influence. This mix creates natural friction and a readiness to overlap storylines in a way that makes sessions feel like living, breathing episodes rather than a sequence of “beat sheets.”

If you’re curious about how to frame a campaign that emphasizes both noir ethics and mythic scale, Fortune Row gives you a lot of bite-sized, play-ready hooks. A single scene can become a thread that ties into a larger mystery, and a single clue can lead to a confrontation with a legend who isn’t thrilled about being pulled into daylight. The book understands that in a city of myths, every street corner can be the edge of a revelation.

## Art, Layout, and Production: The Look That Matches the Mood

Fortune Row continues City of Mist’s signature neon-noir aesthetic. The art direction favors high-contrast compositions, moody lighting, and character portraits that feel like a mash-up of pulp fiction and urban folklore. The layout supports quick scanning during a session, which is crucial when you’re juggling a dozen NPCs, a couple of active Masks, and a timetable for the next scene.

From a production standpoint, the book is sturdy and well-buffered for regular table use. The typography remains readable, the chapter breaks are clear, and the index covers a broad array of keywords you’re likely to reach for while GMing. The maps and location cards included in Fortune Row help you translate text into tactile, on-table assets that you can hand to players. Although nothing replaces a dedicated GM screen, the included assets are a nice acceleration tool when you’re trying to get the session moving after a late arrival.

The cover art and interior visuals work together to sell the setting’s mood. You’ll find consistent color palettes and design motifs that reinforce the sense that this is a place where legends walk the same sidewalks as commuters. If you’re someone who buys RPG books for the vibe as much as the rules, Fortune Row hits that sweet spot with ease.

## Play Experience: What It Feels Like at the Table

Playing Fortune Row is a dance—one that starts with the city’s pulse and ends with a newly minted legend. The tone encourages risk-taking, but it punishes reckless behavior in clever, thematic ways. The GM is given tools to escalate danger gracefully, while players are given opportunities to reshape outcomes through creativity and collaboration. The Masks are a constant reminder that power is a character trait as much as a possession; the city rewards those who understand that a risk taken in good faith can become a turning point for everyone involved.

The pacing is well-tuned for both long arcs and one-off nights. You can run a sprawling investigation across several sessions and still have room for a self-contained caper that ends with a choice that has long-term consequences. The system’s emphasis on collaborative storytelling helps new players feel included while still offering veterans opportunities to shine with clever Tag usage and Mythos plays.

If you’ve ever played in a group where everyone tries to outmaneuver the other, Fortune Row offers a counterbalance: the city itself is a character who can call a bluff, reveal a hidden truth, or trigger a reveal that redefines what a Mask represents. It’s not just about who wins a confrontation; it’s about who uncovers a truth that changes how the group sees the city—and themselves.

## GM Tools, Session Management, and Practical Tips

Fortune Row includes practical GM tools that help you run sessions with confidence. There are ready-to-use encounter seeds, faction relations charts, and a toolbox of “scene chasses” that you can adapt to your table’s preferences. The book encourages you to think not only in terms of battles and heists but also in terms of social dynamics and mythic interference. A good GM can use Fortune Row to orchestrate a sense of inevitability without slipping into heavy-handed railroading.

One of the standout features is how the book handles downtime and consequences. Downtime isn’t a wasted moment; it’s a resource where Masks reflect on their actions and decide how to approach the city’s next challenge. Consequences are narrative rather than punitive—allowing a player’s Mask to become a bit more cautious, or a new vulnerability to emerge—creating a sense of character evolution across sessions.

If you’re new to running City of Mist, Fortune Row includes some excellent GM guidance on pacing and scene transitions. You’ll find practical tips on how to stage a chase through a crowded district, how to handle a mythic twist that changes someone’s perception of reality, and how to wrap a session with a moment that begs a sequel. It’s not mere flavor; it’s a practical approach to turning a night’s gaming into a memorable arc.

## How Fortune Row Stacks Up Against Other Entries

Compared to earlier City of Mist releases, Fortune Row feels like a refinement rather than a revolution. It narrows in on a location-heavy approach—making Fortune Row the anchor for campaigns—while preserving the system’s flexibility. If you enjoyed the core book’s divine-infused street-level play, Fortune Row scales up the world-building and deepens the district’s mythic economy. It also introduces more robust tools for managing city-wide stakes, giving you a sense that the district matters beyond a single session.

For those who compare City of Mist to other modern-noir systems, Fortune Row feels like a buffet with fewer dishes but more texture. The emphasis on Masks and Mythos remains a distinctive core, but the district-level design adds a layer of “how the city works” that’s often missing in more generic mystery RPGs. If you want something that looks, sounds, and plays like a comic-book neon city, Fortune Row will likely scratch that itch more reliably than many other urban fantasy cores.

## The Bottom Line: Should You Get Fortune Row?

Fortune Row delivers a strong, well-polished core experience for City of Mist players who want their game to feel like a late-night stakeout that keeps whispering a legend in your ear. It’s not a radical departure from the core system; it is a focused, city-forward extension that makes the district itself a playable character with agency and consequences. If you enjoy the idea of gloves-off mystery, neon noir, and mythic drama that operates at the speed of a close-quarters chase, Fortune Row is a dependable purchase that will keep your table engaged for multiple sessions.

That said, if you’re new to City of Mist and you prefer a more generalized urban fantasy toolkit without a named district as the anchor, you might start with the core book to acquaint yourself with Masks, Mythos, and the basic flow of play before you dive into the Fortune Row expansion. Either way, Fortune Row respects your time and your imagination—the kind of book that makes you feel clever for thinking of a routine nightclub as a potential gateway to an immortal myth.

## Final Recommendation

If you crave a neon-drenched, myth-powered detective experience with real urban texture and the tools to run a campaign that flexes with your players’ choices, Fortune Row earns a solid recommend. It’s the kind of book that makes you want to run a full arc just to see how the district’s secrets ripple through every character’s Mask.

### Quick Take
- Pros: strong district-focused design, rich myths, excellent GM tools, flexible pacing, strong art and layout

- Ideal for: players who like investigative noir, mythic intrigue, and district-level storytelling with a clear sense of mood and tone

### Final Verdict: High likelihood of becoming a favorite module in your City of Mist shelf, especially if you’ve enjoyed the core game’s vibe and want a richer playground for your Masks and Mythos.
