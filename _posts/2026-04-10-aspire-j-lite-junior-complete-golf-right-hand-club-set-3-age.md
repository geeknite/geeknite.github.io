## Durability and Maintenance: Keeping the Set Alive (Longer Than a Weekend)
The build quality on junior sets is typically robust, but not indestructible. Here are practical maintenance tips:
- Wipe down clubs after outdoor use and dry them before storage to prevent rust or grip hardening.
- Inspect the grips for wear. If they start to peel or feel slippery, consider replacing the grip wrap or upgrading to a new set at the appropriate growth stage.
- Keep the bag’s stand clean and lubricated. A squeaky stand is not the sound of a future pro; it’s a cry for help from the parent who carries the bag across the entire course.
- Store in a dry place. Moisture breeds not only mold but also that suspicious feeling that the club is plotting a mis-hit when you’re least prepared.

## How to Choose Junior Gear: A Quick Guide for Parents
If you’re shopping for a kid in the 3–6 age range, here are quick heuristics:
- Start with weight: lighter is better. A heavy club discourages practice and increases the risk of injuries.
- Consider length: too long or too short will hamper form. If in doubt, choose the shorter end and plan a future upgrade.
- Grip size matters: grips that are too small or too large will force awkward hand placements, which can translate into bad habits that are hard to unlearn later.
- Sets with practice balls are handy for indoor use. This matters a lot for rainy days and apartment living.
- Don’t ignore the bag and stand: kids will carry the set themselves if it’s easy to manage and fun to use.
