## The Basics: How Skip-Bo Plays (for New Players)
Skip-Bo is a race against the clock, the rules, and your own overconfidence. The core idea is simple: you want to be the first to play all the cards in your stock pile by creating sequential stacks from 1 to 12. The deck is a mix of numbers 1 through 12 and a handful of wild Skip-Bo cards that act as wild cards, substituting any number. The game supports 2 to 6 players, which means a lot of laughs, a few groans, and enough strategic missteps to fill a family-friendly montage.

In practice:
- Each player starts with a stock pile face down. You flip the top card to reveal your current goal.
- On your turn, you draw cards and try to play cards onto one or more building piles in ascending order. The first person to clear their stock pile wins.
- You can use the discard piles to rearrange and speed up your progress, but beware of your opponents sneaking ahead with a last-second trick.

The personalized box does not change the rules. It changes the vibes. The box becomes your personal haven where the numbers are always in order… or at least that is the dream. The product keeps the gameplay experience intact while adding a little flair to your shelf—because sometimes the best strategy is to look cool while you win.
