## Cleaning, care, and dealing with the chaos of daily life

Clean-up is where synthetic rugs usually win. Spills bead up and clean with a quick blot, and you’re back to game-night glory before the credits roll. The Quarry Fossil rug is no exception. Here are a few practical tips that won’t break your mood:

- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which just pushes the stain deeper into faux ancient strata.
- For light cleaning, a handheld vacuum or canister with a brush attachment keeps it looking sharp without turning your living room into a wind tunnel.
- For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution works well. Test in a small area first to ensure colorfastness. If you’re dealing with pet accidents, treat as you would with any synthetic rug—quick blot, enzyme cleaner if needed, then air dry. Do not toss in the washing machine unless you enjoy a dramatic laundry day and a potential coloring mismatch.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. Yes, you will look mildly obsessive doing this, and yes, that’s part of the fun of owning an interior design lab in your own home.

When it comes to shedding, the fibers are generally low-shedding compared to some budget synthetics. If shedding does pop up, a quick pass with a vacuum during routine cleaning keeps it under control and avoids the “dust bunny ecosystem” that sometimes forms around a living room throne—the couch.
