## How to Play: A Surf Through Rules Without Losing Your Mind
Skip-Bo’s core revolves around building stacks from 1 to 12 using your hand and the top cards from your personal discard piles. The Deluxe edition adds fancy flavor with replacement cards, but you still need the ability to count, plan, and psych out your friends a little. Here is a compact guide to the essentials, followed by how the single replacement mechanism nudges the entire experience.

### Setup
- Each player gets a set of discard piles (usually four, depending on house rules) and a personal draw pile consisting of cards from the combined SKIP-BO deck.
- The objective remains to be the first to empty your stock pile by playing cards in ascending order to your build piles.
- Replacement cards are shuffled into the mix, with a few rules determining when they can be used. The exact interaction depends on the printing, so you’ll want to skim the enshrined text before you start shouting, Deck, do your thing.
- The first round begins with a naïve plan and ends with a handful of laughter after someone realizes they could have saved themselves with a better discard strategy.

### Core Mechanics
- On your turn, you draw up to five or more cards (depending on edition) and then play cards from your hand into your build piles in ascending order if possible.
- You can play multiple cards in sequence if they fit, but if you get stuck, you can end your turn and pass the play to the next player.
- Replacement cards allow you to swap a card in your hand or discard with a specific replacement that interacts with the current game state. Use them wisely: they can accelerate your path to victory, or they can waste a precious turn if misused.
- Wild cards (Skip-Bo cards) function as wildcards that can stand for any number, adding a delicious element of “I’m not out yet” drama to every round.

### Strategy or How to Win Without Losing Your Sanity
- Early game focus: build reliable foundations by creating predictable sequences. A strong start reduces risk of being trapped by a sudden wave of replacement cards later.
- Mid-game pivot: when replacement cards are in play, you can creatively alter the course of where a given card must go. The best users deploy replacement cards to unlock a stubborn pile that seems reluctant to move.
- Endgame flourish: don’t let your own play deck stagnate. Replace cards to clear your hand faster, but beware of letting opponents catch their breath and re-engage with a new set of possibilities.
