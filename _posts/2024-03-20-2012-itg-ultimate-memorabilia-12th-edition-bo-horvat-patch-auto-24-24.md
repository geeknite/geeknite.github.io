## Grading, condition, and handling tips
### What to look for in grading
Grading a patch auto is a delicate dance. You want a clean autograph zone, a patch that is clearly present, and no major surface issues like deep scratches that look like someone tried to etch a doodle into the foil. Consider the following when evaluating this specific card:
- Centering and edges: Look for even margins and minimal chipping around the foil borders.
- Patch quality: The patch portion should be visible, with clear jersey fabric, not a smeared or missing segment.
- Autograph legibility: The signature should be readable and consistent with Horvat’s known autograph style; inconsistencies may affect grade but do not automatically ruin it.
- Surface condition: Watch for print lines or gloss imperfections that can affect grade; treat them as character, not crime, but get them noted if they impact the visual appeal.

### How to store and protect
For a card like this, you want a solid storage plan. A hard acrylic top loader inside a magnetic or screw-down case can protect the card from finger oils and accidental knocks. Keep the case away from direct sunlight to preserve the silver finish and prevent color shifting. If you plan to display, consider a light controlled display shelf with UV filtered glass. A clean microfiber cloth is your friend; use it to wipe fingerprints gently and keep the autograph area pristine. 

### Handling tips during shows and photos
When you bring this card to a show, handle it by the edges, keep it away from sweaty hands, and avoid those tactical photo sessions where you try to capture a perfect glare on a 24 24 patch. Lightly rotate and angle it to reveal the patch texture and the autograph. If you bring more ITG items, you can create a mini gallery of patches that shows off the lineage of the cards. The audience tends to love the patch variations and the way the foil catches the light; prepare a short, entertaining anecdote about the card to keep the conversation lively.
