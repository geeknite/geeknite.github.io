## Care and storage: keeping the glow intact
Storing a sealed two-deck set isn’t rocket science, but it does require a touch of respect for cardboard history. Here are practical tips that keep the vibes alive without turning your living room into a humidity chamber:

- Keep the decks away from direct sunlight. The gold foil loves sunshine, but your eyes will regret it when you see the color fade and the prints wash out.
- Maintain a stable humidity level. Excess moisture can warp the cardboard or cause the wrap to sweat its way into the packaging corners.
- Store upright in a cool, dry area—think along the lines of a dedicated cabinet with a glass door to flaunt the pair without exposing them to dust or curious pets.
- If you ever decide to examine the decks, use a clean, dry cotton glove to minimize fingerprints and avoid oils from your hands that could interact with the foil tones.
- Consider acid-free storage solutions and silica gel packs to fight off moisture if you’re serious about long-term preservation.

## Verification and authenticity: how to tell Version 1 from later printings
Authenticity is the collector’s best friend and sometimes its arch-nemesis. Version 1 is typically identified by a combination of printing cues, typography choices, and any edition labeling on the box lid or back. A careful collector will compare the box artwork, the font weight, and the color balance with known reference images from credible catalogs. When possible, look for a production date stamp or a serial code in a consistent corner of the packaging. If you’re buying online, request high-resolution photos of the seal, the inner edges, and any inner-corrugation patterns so you can assess alignment and edge crispness. A missing inner insert, a misaligned seal, or a slightly off-color gold foil can sometimes be telltale signs of a later reprint rather than an authentic Version 1 release. If you’re ever unsure, trust your gut and ask for more angles; a reputable seller will provide them.
