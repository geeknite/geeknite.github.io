## Real-World Installation Experience
If you’re planning a fresh install, here’s a practical playbook that doesn’t require you to audition for a job as a hardware guitarist:

1) Plan the layout on paper. Decide which devices will live where, and sketch airflow paths. This avoids rearranging the rack while servers are running and you’re wearing an onion-cutting grin.
2) Mount rails and level the cabinet. The leveling feet do a decent job of stabilizing the cabinet on uneven floors. Take a moment to shim or adjust until you feel confident that a breeze won’t topple your virtual empire.
3) Install the PDU and route power first. Get your power distribution set up before you start loading. It’s the most boring part of the job, but it saves you from a cascade of critical alarms later.
4) Mount the heaviest devices at the bottom. This improves center-of-gravity stability and reduces the risk of accidental tip-overs when you’re sliding a 2U blade into position.
5) Route data cables cleanly. Resist the temptation to cram six cables into a single organizer pocket and call it a day. A tidy cable layout makes maintenance sane and temperatures calmer.
6) Lock the doors and test. Lock the cabinet, close the doors, and perform a quick test to ensure the lock engages smoothly and that the PDU trims power as expected. You’ve earned a small victory lap at this point.
