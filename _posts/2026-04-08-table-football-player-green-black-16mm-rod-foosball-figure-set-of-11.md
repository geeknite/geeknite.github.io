## The Practical Side: Setup, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Setup and Start-Up
Setting up this set is a straightforward experience. If you have never mounted foosball figures before, you can approach it as a light puzzle. Check the rod alignment, place each figure in order, and run a quick test to ensure the ball moves as expected. The key is to avoid forcing figures onto the rods; a gentle wiggle and snappy placement is enough to guarantee a stable hold without bending or stressing the rod ends.

A pro tip: orient the figures so the feet point toward the goal lines. This alignment makes it easier to plan your first pass and instantly gives your table a more professional vibe. The first few rounds might feel a little clumsy, but as with all micro battles in life, practice makes permanent, and soon you will feel like a manager who runs a tiny league from a coffee table.

### Maintenance and Longevity
Cleaning foam dust and minor paint flecks from miniatures is part of the hobby ritual. Use a soft brush and a damp cloth, avoiding harsh solvents that could soften or remove the finish. If you find wear on the foot grips or the base, a tiny dab of super glue can fix a loose foot or a slightly loose leg, but be mindful about not overdoing it. The goal is to maintain articulation and grip, not to glue your players to the board permanently.

Storage matters as well. Keep the set in a display case or a dedicated box to prevent chipping during moves or accidental drops. A small foam insert tailored to the 11 figures helps protect fragile limbs and keeps them organized for the next league night.
