Throw Throw Burrito is built to be fast to learn and quick to forget. ## How to Play: A Quick Overview

### Turn Structure

Each round begins with a draw step, a moment of suspense as you quietly hope for a low-stakes card. The next step is a play step where you either try to complete a burrito-doodle pair or unleash an attack that forces other players into a small carnival of dodges and dives. The core loop is simple: draw, attempt to make a pair, and if you have to, fling a burrito to disrupt someone else’s strategy. The result is a rhythm that can be both strategic and ridiculous depending on your crew.

### The Burrito Attacks

The burrito attacks are not just for show. They exist to shake the table and introduce a physical, but soft, element to a game that would otherwise be a sequence of card flips. The plush burrito’s softness is a feature, not a flaw, because you want chaos without real hurt. You will see players sliding to dodge, flinging a burrito toward a target, and the whole room erupting in a chorus of laughter and mock outrage. The occasional misthrow becomes part of the story you tell later, a signature moment that will be cited in every future party game recap.

### Catching, Dodging, and Card Combos

A big chunk of the experience hinges on catching and dodging. You must balance the risk of catching a burrito (which can leave you exposed to more card effects) with the potential payoff of completing a pair that keeps you in the game. Card combos add a layer of strategy, even though the vibe stays light and zany. It is not a serious strategy game. It is a social bonding exercise disguised as a loud, colorful carnival of chaos.
