## Introduction
In the grand pantheon of desk gadgets, the Yealink VP59 stands out as a curious hybrid. It pretends to be a normal desk phone, but it wants to be a video conferencing device when it grows up. The VP59 is a USB video phone designed for PC based calls, not a stand alone SIP desk phone with a cradle and a pocket full of speed dials. If you are chasing corded convenience or cordless freedom, this device leans more toward the corded side of things. In this review we test how it performs as a docked video companion and then explore whether there is any realistic path to a cordless vibe without summoning a DECT base from the gadget gods.

## What is the VP59
The VP59 is Yealink s answer to modern desk based video calls. It is a video phone that you plug into a computer or a UC platform via USB. It features a camera, a touchscreen, built in audio capabilities and compatibility with popular UC platforms like Teams and Zoom when used in USB mode. It is not a traditional SIP desk phone that sits in a cradle with a handset. Instead it is a USB endpoint that lets you make video calls from a PC or laptop and carries Yealink s UI on the device.

![VP59 in office setup](./assets/yealink-vp59-office.jpg 'VP59 in an office environment')

## Corded reality: how the VP59 works
Setup is simple force of habit. Connect the VP59 to your computer with the USB C cable that comes in the box, power it via the PC or any USB C charger, and pair a headset if you want to go wireless on the audio side. The touchscreen wakes up, you navigate menus, adjust camera angle, configure audio and pick your UC platform. For teams users you will often run the VP59 in a video dock mode; for Zoom or Meet users you will select the VP59 as the video device in the meeting settings. The VP59 supports multiple SIP accounts and headsets and can act as a video endpoint for PC based calls while your PC handles call control, chat and calendar.

### Video and audio quality
The VP59 delivers 1080p video when your PC and network permit and a reasonably bright display that is readable from a desk. The built in camera offers a solid field of view and the microphone array plus speakerphone provide clear voice capture with noise suppression and echo cancellation. For crisper audio you can pair a USB headset or a Bluetooth headset that your PC supports. The experience is ultimately dependent on your PC drivers and the UC client settings, but in a typical office environment you get smooth video and reliable audio without needing a full conference room setup.

### Design and usability
Yealink has crafted a device that looks premium enough to sit on a manager s desk while staying compact. The VP59 chassis feels sturdy; the 5 inch display is touch enabled; the camera sits on a small pop up stand that retracts when not in use. The physical buttons are minimal and the UI aims for clean and intuitive navigation. If you prefer tactile physical controls over swiping on a screen, there s a pleasing tactile feel to the power and volume keys.

## Corded reality vs cordless dream
The short answer is that VP59 is not a cordless phone. It is a USB video phone designed to run off a PC or a UC platform. There isn t a built in DECT radio, there isn t a cradle for cordless roaming, and you can t pair it with a traditional cordless base to roam around the office. If you want cordless freedom from a video endpoint you need a different class of device with DECT or WLAN mobility, or you should embrace a laptop or tablet for mobility and use a headset to keep the audio from becoming a whispering widget.

### Corded advantages
- Simple setup: one USB cable and you re ready to roll.
- Solid video quality when paired with a capable PC and decent network.
- An integrated camera and display means no extra monitor or webcam is strictly required.
- Great for desk bound video calls and a PC based workflow.

### Cordless alternatives
- If you want cordless SIP desk phones there are DECT based options in Yealink s lineup and other brands; these bring roaming at the cost of extra hardware and sometimes more complex admin.
- For true mobility without a video endpoint, consider a laptop or tablet with a good webcam and a headset and a software based video solution. You ll trade hardware integration for portable flexibility.
- If cordless video is a must, you might assemble a cordless kit: a DECT base plus a lightweight video camera or a tablet on a stand plus a wireless headset. It isn t elegant, but it works.

## Setup tips
- Update firmware when offered. Firmware updates often improve UC compatibility and fix small bugs.
- Pick your UC client carefully. Teams, Zoom and other clients will treat the VP59 as a video endpoint; ensure you select the VP59 in the client s video device settings.
- Use a reliable USB cable. A cheap cable can cause drops in video or audio. If you re using a hub, be mindful of bandwidth limitations.
- Optimize the camera position: aim at eye level for natural eye contact during calls.
- Test with a headset: a good headset with micro noise cancellation can dramatically improve audio clarity, especially in open office environments.
- Mind the network: PC based video calls depend on your PC resources and network quality; heavy multitasking on the same device can reduce the smoothness of video.

## Price and value
The VP59 sits in a mid to high tier for USB video endpoints. It isn t the cheapest entry for basic SIP calls but in the right setup it offers a value proposition: a compact desk bound video endpoint that integrates camera, display and UC platform support without requiring a separate PC monitor or webcam. The absence of cordless roaming can be a downside for teams that prize mobility on the desk, but for many office workflows, the VP59 delivers a neat, focused experience.

It is a reliable and convenient option for individuals who want video calls with a PC based workflow without a laptop on every desk.
