## Size guide: custom size vs 8x10 — which should you choose?

### When to pick a custom size

- Your room has irregular dimensions or non-standard offsets that don’t align with common rug sizes.
- You’re trying to create a layered look with a larger top rug and smaller secondary pieces, and you need exact margins for balance.
- You want precise alignment with furniture groupings, such as keeping sofa legs or coffee table edges flush with rug boundaries.
- There are architectural features (stair landings, alcoves, vestibules) where a standard size would look off or feel cramped.

### When to pick an 8x10

- You want a safe, reliable option that easily fits most living rooms, under a coffee table, and under most seating groups.
- You’re on a tight timeline and need quicker fulfillment; standard sizes typically ship faster than fully custom orders.
- You’re mindful of budget and want to optimize cost-to-area ratio without sacrificing comfort.
- You’re experimenting with interior design and want a neutral base you can swap with other pieces later without repainting the entire room.

### Pros and cons at a glance

- Custom size pros: perfect fit, tailored proportions, unique layout possibilities.
- Custom size cons: longer lead times, higher price ceiling, potential for miscalculation if measuring isn’t precise.
- 8x10 pros: faster shipping, predictable price, broad compatibility with furniture layouts.
- 8x10 cons: not all rooms will be perfectly aligned with a standard size, sometimes you’ll have to “make do” with margins that aren’t ideal.

### Practical measurement tips

1) Measure the room length and width in several places to account for irregular walls; use the smallest width to ensure the rug will fit across the entire space when placed under key furniture.
2) Decide if you want a margin. Some designers like 6–12 inches of floor visible around the rug; others prefer more rug in view to anchor the seating area.
3) Account for furniture dimensions. If you’re placing under a sofa and coffee table, measure the clearance between furniture and walls to prevent rug creep or awkward gaps.
4) Consider door clearance. If you have doors that swing across the rug, ensure there is enough room for the door to open without catching the rug edge.

## Durability, care, and long-term maintenance

### Everyday care and common issues

Wool is naturally resilient, but it’s not magic. Regular vacuuming with a suction-only setting (no beater bar) helps remove grit that could abrade fibers over time. Rotate the rug every few months to distribute wear evenly, especially in high-traffic areas. Address spills quickly with blotting rather than rubbing to prevent color migration and fiber flattening. For most household spills, a mild wool-safe detergent diluted with water and a clean white cloth is enough to lift most stains. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability.

### Dealing with pet hair and spills

Pet hair tends to cling to wool like a tiny magnet. A high-velocity vacuum, sometimes with a pet hair attachment, is your friend here. For stubborn fur clusters, use a damp rubber squeegee or a lint roller before vacuuming. When it comes to accidents (it happens to the best of us), a quick blot, a mild cleaner, and a good rinse is usually sufficient. Do not aggressively scrub; you’ll ruin the texture and potentially alter color. If you’re using a spot cleaner, follow with a damp wipe to remove any residue.

### Stain avoidance and color care

No rug is stain-proof, but good color care helps. Avoid direct sun for extended periods to minimize fading. Rotate placement to reduce uneven sun exposure. If you have a particularly sunny corner, consider window treatments or UV-protective films to preserve the tone of the Toasted Coconut.

### Cleaning schedule tips

- Vacuum weekly in busy areas; biweekly elsewhere.
- Rotate the rug seasonally to even out wear.
- Deep clean once or twice a year (or whenever life happens). Use a wool-safe shampoo and ensure thorough rinse to prevent residue.
- When in doubt, hire a professional for a thorough upholstery-grade cleaning.
