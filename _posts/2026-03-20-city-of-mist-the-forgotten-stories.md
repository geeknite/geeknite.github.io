City of Mist is a game that thrives on mask wearing and mythic vibes, and The Forgotten Stories is the latest expansion that wants to turn a dozen shy glances into full blown dramatic entrances. If you thought the city already had enough legends, this collection of micro adventures proves that a few forgotten heroes can still steal the spotlight from the loud, dramatic legends you already know. The Forgotten Stories leans into the quieter corners of urban myth, where a single rumor can derail a citywide conspiracy and a single memory can topple a titan of legend. In plain geek terms, think of it as the side quest you didnt know you needed until the game master started narrating it with jazz hands.

## Overview
### What this is and why it exists
The Forgotten Stories is a supplementary tome designed to slot into your existing City of Mist campaign or to stand on its own as a starter kit for groups that want to sample the system without committing to a full new arc. It centers on forgotten figures from the citys past and present who rarely get a spotlight, but whose tales ripple through the current Mist like a dropped marble in a fountain. The book invites players to peel back the layers of the city, revealing that legends are not always loud and dramatic; sometimes they whisper in back alleys, scribble themselves on graffiti, or haunt a coffee shop with unreliable wifi.

### Tone and themes
If the core game is about mythic personas and mythos colliding with everyday life, The Forgotten Stories sits in the midrange between nostalgia and unease. The stories have a melancholy perfume: memories that haunt, regrets that refuse to fade, and the sense that the city is always a couple of steps away from being remembered in the right way. The writing balances reverence and mischief, so you can play a scene where a forgotten detective rewrites an old case or a ghostly author tries to publish a confession before the city forgets them again.

## Contents and structure
### What you get
The book presents six self contained micro adventures, each designed for a single session or a long evening that stretches into a weekend binge of detective courage and mistaken identities. Each entry includes a summary hook, key locations, a handful of suspects, a few twists, and ready to use NPCs whose motivations are tangled in the worlds past. The adventures come with ready made investigation prompts, scene ideas, and a handful of Mist infused twists that remind players that legends can be their own worst enemies.

### New tools for the GM
In addition to the six stories, there are GM tips on running investigations with minimal prep, quick encounter tables for when a scene needs a sudden push, and a short primer on how to weave forgotten names into ongoing plot threads without overpowering the present day. The Forgotten Stories also includes a few new flavor cards and a compact bestiary of minor foes that feel significant precisely because they are forgotten. It is the kind of book that makes you say, hey, we could do a whole campaign about the janitor who remembers every crime that ever happened in a subway station.

### The art and layout
The layout is clean, with generous margins that invite doodles and side notes. The art leans toward moody silhouettes and rain soaked streets, which is exactly the energy City of Mist fans adore. The cover art captures a silhouette in a noirish glow, plus a hint of something shimmering behind the mist. Inside, the color palette plays with blues and grays, punctuated by a few splashes of neon when a scene hits the high drama beats. If you are an aesthetic player, you will savor the atmospheric vibes even before you start rolling.

## Adventures at a glance
Here is a quick tour of the six stories, with a taste of the mood and what makes each one sing or sting.

### The Lantern in the Library
A forgotten caretaker who tended a tunnel of books that could reveal hidden memories. The lantern is both guide and trickster, leading investigators to the truth but also to misremembered outcomes. Expect quiet pursuit, whispered conversations, and a reveal that reframes a well known case through a new, brighter light.

### The Clockmaker's Moon
A city clock that keeps time by secrets rather than minutes. When the moon dialed back one second too many, a chain reaction uncovers a plot older than the city itself. The feel is steampunk noir with a pulse of cosmic dread as investigators chase shadows that move in and out of the mechanism itself.

### The Choir of Quiet Doors
A chorus of doorways that sing in the voice of people who walked through them long ago. Entering a door can put a memory on stage, the chorus demands a choice, and the resolution hinges on listening closely to what is not being said. This one bites with an emotional resonance that can sneak up on players who came for capes and dice but stay for human echoes.

### The Bark of the Waking City
A forgotten dog with a memory so long it can recall crimes before they happened. The dog acts as a living timeline, barking out hints that emerge from the fog of the present. Expect a mix of investigative puzzles and a moral choice that asks what it means to alter history even when the cost is a future you never planned.

### The Glass Needle
A fragment of a redacted confession hidden in a graffiti tunnel. The needle is a metaphor and a tool, able to prick away layers of memory until what remains is the bare truth or a clever lie dressed up in neon. The tension comes from balancing truth with the countrys desire to forget things that still hurt.

### The Last Letter in the Rain
An epistolary finale told through letters that arrived after their writer has long since passed. The letters reframe a case as a love story, a betrayal, and a final act of mercy. The mood is bittersweet, and the reveal lands with a soft force that lingers in the air like rain on a window.

## Mechanics and play experience
### How the tag system shapes play
City of Mist runs on a tag based dice mechanic where players narrate actions by combining their character tags with the scene requirements. The Forgotten Stories leans into this by expanding the pool of tags with forgotten names, old affiliations, and city wide rumors. The result is a game that rewards creative description and clever tag usage. In practical terms, you will see players weaving in a memory tag to justify why a seemingly mundane action suddenly becomes cinematic, or using nostalgia tags to unlock a memory based clue that would feel cheap if handled through a normal clue drop.

### The GM perspective
For the GM, the book is a gift and a challenge. The six stories are designed to be run with minimal prep, but the best sessions will improvise around the memories the players bring to the table. The GM is nudged to remind players that forgotten names carry power, that a single memory can alter a citys trajectory, and that legends do not forget their edges easily. The GM tools give you quick prompts for pace, tone, and escalation, plus a few one page scene ideas you can drop in when the party wants a breather or when a twist hits just a little too hard.

### Team chemistry and pacing
The Forgotten Stories shines when a table has good chemistry and a shared sense of humor. The humor in this book is not about one liners; it comes from players discovering the emotional gravity of these forgotten figures. The pacing shifts between noir suspense and mythic revelation, with occasional bursts of whimsy that keep the mood from tipping too dark. If your group leans heavy into grimdark, you will still find room for a wink, and if you are a lighter crew, the melancholic undercurrent will feel refreshing rather than dour.

## Artwork, accessibility, and accessibility notes
The book does a respectable job of balancing readability with atmosphere. The font choices are legible, the section headers are clear, and there are plenty of sidebars with quick tips for new GMs. For players with visual preferences, the art offers character silhouettes that are easy to imagine as masks rather than as exact depictions of a hero. The accessibility notes are not a long chapter, but they exist in the margins; if you need a simple layout for a one shot, you can run the six stories one after another with minimal adaptation.

## How this compares to other City of Mist supplements
Compared to earlier City of Mist volumes, The Forgotten Stories leans more toward personal memory and less toward world shakers. It feels like the window into the citys past is cracked open just enough to be irresistible, without losing the sense that the city is still a living, breathing character. Some fans will miss a big bad or a dramatic arc in every entry, but most will appreciate the intimate scope that allows a GM to explore individual fates with emotional honesty. If you want a slower burn that rewards roleplay and memory weaving, this is a strong contender; if you prefer sprawling conspiracies and epic reveals, you may want to pair this with a larger arc or its own separate campaign.

## Pros and cons
- Pros: strong thematic cohesion, six distinct stories that unlock memories rather than powers, solid GM aids, gorgeous moody art, accessible for new players, fits neatly into an existing campaign.
- Cons: not every table wants a nostalgia heavy mood; the micro adventures may feel short if your group loves sprawling campaigns; some players craving big fights may feel there is less punch per page than expected.

## Who should buy this
If you love City of Mist and enjoy delving into the quieter corners of the city, The Forgotten Stories is for you. GMs who want ready made sessions that can slot into a longer campaign will find it a time saving treasure. Players who are drawn to character driven scenes, memory mysteries, and moral choices will savor the emotional weight. Fans who enjoy a bit of noir, a dash of myth and a sprinkle of bittersweet revelation will feel right at home.

## Conclusion and final verdict
The Forgotten Stories is a love letter to the memory tug of a city that remembers more than it tells. It is not a blockbuster expansion about god killers and star alignments; it is a compact anthology that invites you to slow down, listen to a whispered rumor, and decide what you owe to those who are forgotten by the public ledger. If you are a fan of the human scale of City of Mist, you will finish these six stories with a sense of satisfaction and a few ideas for future sessions. My recommendation is to pick this up as a supplementary set for a city long hungry for intimate legends and small, meaningful reveals.
