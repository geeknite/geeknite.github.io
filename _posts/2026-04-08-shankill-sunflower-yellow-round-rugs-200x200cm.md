## Durability and Care

### Durability Benchmarks

Durability for a rug like this is a combination of fiber quality, pile height, and backing integrity. A mid-pile rug with a sturdy backing tends to fare well under regular traffic and occasional repositioning. The Shankill line is typically designed with everyday life in mind, balancing resilience with comfort. Expect routine wear patterns in high-traffic zones, but with proper care, the rug should maintain its shape and color over a reasonable period.

### Cleaning Routine

- Vacuum regularly using a suction setting appropriate for the pile height.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing which can push the liquid deeper into fibers.
- For spot cleaning, use a mild detergent or rug-specific cleaner following the manufacturer’s instructions. Always test on a hidden area first to ensure colorfastness.
- Avoid harsh solvents or bleach, as they can damage the color and fiber integrity.
- If the rug is made with a synthetic blend, it’s more resistant to water-based stains but may attract pet hair; a rotating vacuum head can help with that.

### Care Tips and Tricks

A circular rug can look perfect for years if you rotate placement to minimize wear in the same spots and avoid direct sun exposure that can cause color fading. If your space has strong afternoon sun, consider UV-protective window treatments to preserve the yellow hue. Additionally, using a rug pad not only stops slipping but also provides an extra buffer against wear and makes the rug feel plusher. If you notice pilling or loose threads, gently trim them with scissors to maintain a clean appearance—do not pull.

### Maintenance Schedule

- Vacuum 2–3 times a week in high-traffic areas.
- Rotate monthly to promote even wear.
- Wipe spills within minutes; inspect after 24–48 hours for any residual marks.
- Deep-clean every 12–18 months, depending on usage; consult the manufacturer’s care guide before professional cleaning.
