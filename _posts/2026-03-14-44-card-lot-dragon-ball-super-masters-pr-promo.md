## Authenticity and Condition Checkpoints
When evaluating a 44-card PR promo lot, you want to protect your investment and your time. Here are practical checkpoints to consider before finalizing a purchase:
- Ask for high-res photos of the front and back of each card; look for consistent holo stamps, border alignment, and text legibility
- Confirm the lot includes a full 44 cards and that there are no apparent missing pieces or duplicates in the listing
- Inspect the foil distribution; if foil cards look badly washed or have heavy scuffing, weigh whether you still want the overall set
- Request packaging details; a deck box can be nice, but a pristine sealed bag is the best indicator of a handled but preserved lot
- Check the seller s return policy; promos are tricky and the return window should be friendly if a card is misrepresented
- If possible, request a card-by-card value estimate for the most desirable pieces to align your expectations with reality

## Care and Storage Best Practices
A little care goes a long way. Here is a practical kit for keeping your 44-card lot pristine:
- Soft sleeves for the bulk of the deck, plus penny sleeves for the standout promos
- A rigid top loader for display or safe stacking
- A small binder or binder kit with labeled sections for promos, rares, and common cards
- A cool dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent foil oxidation or color fade
- Regular checks for humidity; Dragon Ball art loves to stay vibrant but not to be warped by moisture
