## Setup and Assembly Tips

1. Work on a soft surface to avoid scratching the top; a carpeted floor or thick blankets work well.
2. Lay out all hardware and identify each piece before starting—think of it as a micro-roll call for your desk.
3. Start with the main frame and attach cross supports; ensure all bolts are tightened evenly to avoid a wobbly wobble.
4. Attach the top panel and the raised shelf with the proper screws; do not overtighten to avoid cracking the laminate.
5. Route cables in the open channels; use zip ties to keep things tidy, but not so tight they cut into your cables.
6. Place the desk where you want it, adjust chair height, and take a test seat. If your wrists ache after 15 clicks on the keyboard, you may need a separate keyboard tray.

We recommend a quick desk setup test: sit at your preferred chair height, place the keyboard in front of you, and check the angle of your wrists.
