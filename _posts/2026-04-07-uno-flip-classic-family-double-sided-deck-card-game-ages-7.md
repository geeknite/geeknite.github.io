## How to play: the light side vs the dark side
At its core, Uno Flip! keeps the foundational UNO rhythm: players take turns placing a card that matches either color or number on the discard stack, and there are wilds and action cards to spice things up. The twist is the Flip mechanism:
- Light side: standard UNO rules apply. Match color or number, play action cards, say UNO when you’re down to one card, and watch out for card-punishment when someone else calls you out.
- Flip moment: at certain times (often triggered by a Flip card or when a specific condition is met), the draw pile flips to reveal the dark side. The top of the discard pile might now trigger new penalties and altered actions.
- Dark side: you’ll encounter more punishing effects and different action cards that escalate the stakes. Matches may become less forgiving, and draws can pile up quickly if you lose track of which side you’re on.

The pacing comes down to a rhythm: quick rounds on the light side can feel breezy and friendly, then a flip injects a sudden layer of challenge that keeps veteran players engaged and slightly paranoid about uno-ing at the wrong moment. It’s not a marathon; it’s a sprint with a dramatic twist.

### A closer look at flip dynamics
The core thrill of Uno Flip! is how a single flip can rearrange your strategy in an instant. On the light side you might be eyeing a red card to finish the round, only to flip and discover your potential winning card is now a color you can’t legally place. The dark side’s penalties (which can include multiple draws, wild cards that force color shifts, and other punitive moves) incentivize players to think several steps ahead, even as chaos swirls around the table.
