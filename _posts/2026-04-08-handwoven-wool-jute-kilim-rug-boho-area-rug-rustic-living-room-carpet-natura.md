### Materials at a Glance

This Kilim rug is a deliberate mashup of two natural powerhouses:

- Wool: The warm, cozy backbone. Wool fibers trap air, which translates to better insulation and a plushish feel underfoot. They’re naturally springy, which helps resist flattening from foot traffic and couch legs—though you’ll still see a telltale imprint after a long movie night.

## Materials Deep Dive: Wool, Jute, and the Kilim Way

### Wool: The Cozy Shield

Wool fibers bring warmth, resilience, and a forgiving texture. They’re naturally elastic, which helps the rug bounce back from chair leg imprints and foot traffic. Wool also carries moisture-regulating properties, absorbing humidity in damp climates and releasing it slowly, which contributes to that “soft-durability” feel that you want under bare feet after a long day. A couple of caveats: wool can attract moths if left unchecked, and high humidity combined with poor ventilation can encourage mold if liquids seep in. Regular vacuuming with a soft brush setting and prompt stain treatment is your friend here. 

### Jute: The Earthy Groundedness

Jute contributes a sturdy, earthy texture that anchors the rug’s look. It’s a plant-based fiber, which checks the eco-friendly box many shoppers chase. Jute is durable in high-traffic zones but sensitive to moisture, so avoid soaking spills or placing it in damp basements. The surface tends to show more texture than a plush wool sign-on. That texture is deliberate: it hides footprints better than a smooth, slick surface would, and it contributes to that rustic-chic aesthetic you’re chasing. 

### Kilim Weave: Flat, Fast, Fabulous

The flat weave is a design choice that emphasizes pattern and color over pile depth. It’s easy to clean in the sense that you won’t have deep fiber stains tugging at you like a stubborn boss. Vacuum regularly on low power to avoid pulling threads, and address spills promptly—blot, don’t rub. If a spill is serious (red wine, coffee, or dragon fruit smoothie you swore you’d never repeat), use a mild detergent with water and blot again. Avoid saturating the jute portion; moisture there isn’t your friend.

## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity

### Daily Care

- Vacuum with a gentle setting and a brush attachment to lift dirt without pulling threads.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color exposure; this helps prevent uneven fading if sunlight hits one side more than the other.

### Spills and Stains

- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Don’t rub; you’re not trying to spin the fiber in a blender, promise. 
- For tougher stains, use a mild solution of water and a gentle detergent, apply sparingly, and blot. Always test in an inconspicuous corner first to ensure colorfastness.
- If a stain seems stubborn, consider professional cleaning after assessing the rug’s material recommendations. Wool handles professional cleaning well, but moisture in jute areas needs careful handling.

### Cleaning Frequency and Environmental Considerations

The Natura rug isn’t a weekly bath buddy; it’s more of a “every few months” spa day. The natural fibers don’t like being soaked, so avoid steam cleaning on the jute sections. A light steam or professional wool cleaning can be considered if needed, but keep moisture away from the jute portion. If you live in a humid climate, consider a dehumidifier in storage seasons or move the rug to a drier room during wet months to prevent mold growth. 

### Storage and Seasonal Care

If you anticipate long periods of non-use (you know, humans forgetfulness), store the rug rolled, not folded, with a breathable cover and a small silica packet to deter moisture. Avoid plastic, which traps humidity and can invite musty smells.
