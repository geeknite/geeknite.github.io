### Temperature effects

 Li-ion cells are happiest in the mid-range temperatures. In the field, if you operate in extremes, expect the runtime to be compressed. If you’re running tests in the summer heat or in chilly environments, you may see shorter hold times. The reason is simple physics: chemical reactions slow down when it’s cold, and heat accelerates wear on the electrolyte. The MIT200SL is not a magical battery that defies physics; it’s a practical component that benefits from sensible environmental control just like your protein shake benefits from a cooler.
