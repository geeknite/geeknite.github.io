## How to install the derailleur shift cable housing (step-by-step)
Installing 4 mm housing is a doable weekend project that won’t turn your living room into a workshop from hell. Here’s a straightforward guide to help you replace or upgrade your derailleur housing without turning your frame into a jerky sculpture.

### Step 1: Gather your tools
- Cable cutters sharp enough to handle steel wires.
- A small set of pliers.
- A file or deburring tool (to smooth end caps if you’re trimming).
- End caps that fit into your derailleur housing (if not included).
- A Sharpie or tape for marking lengths.

### Step 2: Measure and plan
- Shift into the middle chainring and a mid-range cog so you have slack to work with.
- Trace the current cable path. If you’re replacing old housing, note where it bends and where it anchors at the frame boss or guides.
- Add a bit of extra length to allow for routing through derailleurs, cable guides, and down tubes if your bike requires it.

### Step 3: Remove the old housing and cable
- Loosen the barrel adjuster and pulls the cable free from the housing end caps.
- Remove the old housing in sections, ensuring you keep the cable straight as it exits the shifter and enters the derailleur housing.
- Inspect the cable for any kinks or corrosion. If it’s toast, replace the entire cable along with the housing for best results.

### Step 4: Cut and deburr the new housing
- Cut the 4 mm housing to the measured lengths using the cable cutters. Do not hack at it with pliers; you’ll fray the ends and defeat the purpose.
- Deburr the ends to ensure a clean interface with the ferrules and end caps.

### Step 5: Install the new housing and cable
- Feed the inner derailleur cable through the housing, starting from the derailleur end and moving toward the shifter so you don’t kink the cable along the way.
- Attach end caps as needed and ensure the housing ferrules mate snugly with the frame guides.
- Route the cable along the designed path and anchor at the shifter side.
- Tighten the housing stop or ferrules so that the alignment remains clean and there’s no slack that could lead to misalignment.

### Step 6: Fine-tune and test
- Revisit the barrel adjuster: make small revolutionary adjustments to fine-tune cable tension for precise shifts.
- Shift through all gears to ensure smooth transitions. If you hear grinding or feel skipping, re-check the end caps for snug fit and the routing for potential kinks.
- Take a short test ride and verify that shifting remains accurate under load and during climbs and sprints.

### Common pitfalls to avoid
- Over-tightening cables can cause frayed inner wires or stiff shifts. Always aim for a little free play and adjust with the barrel adjuster.
- Neglecting proper bend radii leads to friction and faster wear. Use gentle curves rather than sharp bends.
- Skipping end caps or ferrules means the housing ends will degrade quickly and require a redo.

## Maintenance tips for long-term reliability
- Regularly inspect the housing ends for cracks or brittle plastic and replace as needed. This is especially important in climates with constant moisture or road salt.
- Wipe away grit after riding on dusty roads to prevent abrasive wear from grinding into the housing.
- Lubricate with a light, bicycle-safe lubricant on the inner cable (not the housing itself) to reduce friction—excess lubrication can attract dirt and actually worsen performance.
- Check tension after a few rides; the derailleur might shift less crisply as the drivetrain settles in.
