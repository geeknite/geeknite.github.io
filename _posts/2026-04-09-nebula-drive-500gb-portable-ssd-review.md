## Performance: Read and Write Speeds That Reside in the Real World

When you see “USB 3.2 Gen 2” and “10Gbps” in the spec, you might imagine a top-of-the-line data dragon breathing fire at your files. The NebulaDrive does well in real-world usage, balancing peak speed with sustained performance across typical workloads like large media transfers, OS drive migration, and offline project archiving.

Key spec bullets:
- Interface: USB 3.2 Gen 2, up to 10 Gbps (theoretical)
- Real-world read: around 900–1050 MB/s (depending on host and firmware)
- Real-world write: around 850–1000 MB/s (depending on host and firmware)
- Drive capacity: 500 GB usable space after formatting varies by OS and block size
- Controller: efficient, purpose-built for portable use
- NAND type: TLC or equivalent for a good balance of speed and endurance (no bullroar, actual flash type can vary by batch)

Well-respected rule of thumb in the portable SSD space is that you won’t hit the quoted maximum in every scenario. USB buses can bottleneck performance, fences can be cruel, and Windows’ internal housekeeping can cause short-lived dips. With the NebulaDrive, we managed mostly steady results around the upper mid-range for sustained transfers, which is exactly what most creators and power users want for backups, media work, and game libraries.

Real-world copy tests:
- Copying a 20 GB folder of mixed media resulted in bursts of fast transfer speeds followed by short pauses as the drive caught up. The sustained rate hovered near 900 MB/s for large, contiguous files and dipped to sub-200 MB/s for a barrage of many small files. This pattern matches the usual behavior of consumer-grade NVMe-to-USB enclosures and is well within expectations.
- A 5 GB project folder with lots of little assets completed in under a minute, a comfortable pace for daily work rather than a race against a clock in a NASA test chamber.

If you’re comparing to the branding giants, the NebulaDrive sits comfortably between budget-friendly options and premium rugged drives. ## Post-Usage Tips and Best Practices

- Always safely eject the drive from your computer to avoid file system corruption. The NebulaDrive is robust, but it’s not invincible—data integrity loves a polite outro music, not a cliffhanger.
- If you’re using macOS for your primary workflow, consider formatting the drive as APFS for native performance on your Apple devices. For cross-platform work, exFAT is your friend.
- Back up important files in at least two locations. The best data protection strategy is the one you actually follow, not the one you dream about while doomscrolling.
- If you need encryption, pair NebulaDrive with OS-level encryption or a trusted third-party tool rather than relying on hardware encryption you didn’t enable.
