## Care and maintenance basics
### Regular cleaning routine
- Vacuum weekly using a non-beater setting to preserve the pile integrity.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and prevent any one area from flattening due to furniture placement.
- Use a gentle wool-safe cleaner for spills; blot, don’t rub, to avoid embedding stains deeper into the fibers.

### Stain management
Wool resists many stains naturally, but spills deserve prompt attention. Blot with a clean white cloth to absorb liquid. For organic spills like coffee or red wine, you can use a small amount of wool-safe detergent mixed with cool water, then blot again. Avoid heat, as heat can set stains and tighten the fibers in a way that reduces softness over time.

### Moth and pests
Like any natural fiber rug, wool can attract moths if left unchecked. Use a moth repellent or keep the area clean and dry. If you store the rug for a season, roll it, wrap it in breathable fabric, and keep it in a cool, dry location. The wool will thank you by staying resilient and plush when you unroll it again.

### Professional cleaning cadence
Every 1 to 2 years, consider a professional deep clean for maximum longevity. A professional can refresh the fibers, lift embedded dirt, and re-energize the weave’s natural bounce. If you have a high-traffic space or pets, more frequent cleanings can be beneficial.
