## Sorting, storage, and condition care for a large lot

Let’s talk practicalities. A large lot is a goldmine, but only if you treat it with a little discipline. Here are practical tips for sorting and preserving your cards so that the nostalgia stays intact for years to come.

### Sorting strategy
- Start by quality control: separate open cards from sealed, check for water damage, stains, creases, and major edge wear.
- Sort by type and rarity: character cards first, followed by plot, environmental, equipment, and then a final pass for any special or event cards.
- Within each pile, sort by character or theme: Wolverine adjacent to Rogue, Cyclops with Jean Grey—whatever helps you plan synergy quick.
- Create a simple binder system: three-ring binders with transparent sleeves allow you to display a team while keeping the cards safe. For larger lots, consider segmenting by rarity and by year to preserve that sense of progression you felt the first time you opened a pack.

### Handling and storage
- Use sleeves to protect cards from fingerprints and minor abrasion. For display, consider top-loaders for the key cards you want to proudly show off on a shelf.
- Avoid direct sunlight; the artwork on these cards looks best when walls glow with indirect light rather than harsh sunbeams burning through glossy finishes.
- Temperature and humidity matter. A dry, cool room is ideal. A damp environment invites curling, warping, and the occasional existential crisis in your binder notes.

### Condition grading basics
- Near mint: nearly no wear, clean edges, and minimal surface scratches under normal handling.
- Lightly played: minor edge wear or small surface scratches that do not obscure art or card text.
- Heavily played: more pronounced wear, creases, or staining. These can still be playable and collectible depending on your goals, but you’ll likely price them accordingly.
- Damaged: significant damage, such as large creases or warping. Consider keeping these as nostalgia pieces rather than display-worthy collectables.
