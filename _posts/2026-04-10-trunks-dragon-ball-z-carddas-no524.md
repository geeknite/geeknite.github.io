## A quick geography of sources and authenticity tips
If you’re new to authenticating Carddas cards, here are quick pointers that help you avoid the typical pitfalls:
- Check printing wear: Very Rare cards often show signs of reduced run quality. Look for consistent color saturation and proper border alignment.
- Compare back designs: Carddas backs have subtle patterns and fonts that can be replicated poorly by counterfeiters. If the back looks off, proceed with caution.
- Inspect foil and texture: If the card has foil or print-soft textures, ensure that they’re evenly distributed. Irregularities are red flags.
- Use trusted sellers: Reputable hobby shops or long-standing online marketplaces with return policies are your best friend when chasing scarce cards.
