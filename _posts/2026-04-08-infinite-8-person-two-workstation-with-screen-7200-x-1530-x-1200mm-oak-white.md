## Assembly and setup: what to expect
### Time and teams
Assembling a structure this large is not a one-person job. We recommend a two-to-four person crew with a few hours to spare for alignment, leveling, and the inevitable “which way does this go?” moment. The modular design helps; each module is labeled and attaches with standardized fasteners. Expect a few trips to the hardware store for extra brackets if you’re customizing beyond the base configuration.

### On-site challenges
- Floor irregularities: If your floor isn’t perfectly level, you’ll want a laser level and some shims. A slight tilt can cause the screen hub to appear misaligned, and nobody wants the central display to tilt like a sunken ship.
- Space planning: This is a long piece of furniture. Measure doorways and corridors before delivery. You don’t want to discover mid-delivery that you can’t fit it through the doorway without disassembly beyond the manufacturer’s recommended scope.
- Cable planning: This setup invites a tangle if you don’t pre-plan power and data paths. Map out run routes and label ports. The last thing you want is a spaghetti-laden trunk overtaking your workspace.

### Tips for a smooth build
1) Unbox and sort hardware by module. 2) Do a dry-fit before screwing anything in. 3) Use a line to align panels across the entire length. 4) Pre-run cables in the central hub to avoid rework. 5) Consider a high-quality gasket for floor protection if you have delicate flooring.
