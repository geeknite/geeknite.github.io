## What is Codex Card Time Strategy Core
Codex Card Time Strategy Core is a competitive card game where players manipulate time tokens to shape outcomes, collect artifacts, and outmaneuver rivals by bending causality without breaking your gaming group. The extras add new cards, new abilities, and a new headache for your organizer, but in a good way. The core mechanic involves building a personal time engine by drafting cards and aligning them with eras on a mutable timeline. The objective is to score more paradox points than rivals before the clock runs out. Yes, paradox points, folks. This game wears its love of sci-fi and slightly nerdy math on its sleeve.

## Core mechanics
### Time tokens
Time tokens are the lifeblood of your engine. On your turn you generate a pool of tokens you can spend to activate effects or advance the timeline. Tokens come in different values, and you can store them for a turn or two if you anticipate a future need. The trick is to balance immediate effect with long-term tempo. Spend too aggressively and you may stall your engine; hoard too much and you hand your opponents a free window to pounce on a paradox. The tokens feel satisfying in your hand and satisfying in your opponents’ rage-induced reactions when you suddenly realize a small misstep costs you the game.

### Card types
- Action cards let you alter tempo and respond to what your rivals are doing
- Event cards shift the calendar in dramatic ways, sometimes undoing progress or accelerating your own engine
- Artifact cards grant persistent bonuses that chain into future turns
- Paradox cards are the spicy meat of the game, creating risk with potentially huge rewards

### Paradoxes and timelines
Paradoxes are where the game earns its snarky title. When your actions create paradox events, you must resolve them by spending resources or sacrificing a card. The timeline board is modular and can be rearranged during play, which means the layout on the table can change more often than your social media feed. The concept of shifting eras feels clever rather than gimmicky, and it rewards players who plan ahead but also punish those who overcommit to one line of play.

### Extras overview
The extras expansion includes Chrono Factory, Epoch Boon deck, Mutable Clock, and new player mats. Chrono Factory adds more time tokens to the pool, making it easier to accelerate, but also raising the stakes as more tokens mean more potential paradoxes. Epoch Boons provide powerful single-use effects, with a tasty backfire mechanic to remind you that hyper-optimizing is fun until it isn’t. Mutable Clock rearranges time in clever ways so you can twist your own plan mid game, and the new mats add space for banter and micro-drama, which is essential for a table that values flavor almost as much as victory.

Each player takes a personal time engine mat, a starting deck, some time tokens, and a random artifact. The center of the table becomes a mutable timeline that you will rearrange with each card you play. The setup is quick, but the mental overhead is high, which is exactly what you want from a game marketed as a strategy core with extras. It is a good wake-up call for the brain after a long day of emails and memes.

### Turn cycle
- Draw phase
- Time generation

- Timeline manipulation
- Paradox check
- End phase
The key to a smooth session is balancing tempo with risk. If you overinvest in tempo early on, you can run out of resources and get hit by a paradox that makes your opponents laugh at you from the future. The design rewards players who can pivot on a dime and still act confident while explaining their choices with a straight face.

### Example round narrative
Imagine you are mid game. You draw a couple of flexible action cards, generate a healthy pile of time tokens, and realize your opponent is about to lock in a late game engine. You play a subtle event that nudges the timeline forward, then a paradox card to buy you one extra turn at a cost you can survive. The table erupts in cheer, or groans, depending on how much you love dramatic finales.

## Strategy and Deck Building
### Early game
- Focus on card draw to accelerate your tempo while keeping your options open
- Pick artifacts with recurring benefits that pay off across several turns
- Avoid committing to a single era too early unless you have a rock-solid plan

### Mid game
- Use Chrono Factory to secure a steady stream of time tokens
- Begin addressing opponents' timelines with controlled disruptions
- Monitor paradox triggers and remain ready to pivot your engine mid stream

### Late game
- Cluster artifacts that offer endgame scoring and synergy with your established engine
- Leverage epoch boons at optimal moments to swing the late turns in your favor
- Prepare a big paradox payoff that can clinch victory or at least provoke a memorable tilt from your tablemates
