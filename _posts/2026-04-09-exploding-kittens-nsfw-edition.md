## How the gameplay works in NSFW mode
The core mechanics mirror the original game: players take turns drawing cards until someone draws an exploding kitten and loses, unless they can defuse the explosion with a defuse card. The NSFW edition spices things up with extra action cards, more provocative defuses, and occasional side rules that shift the pace and tension.

### Setup and turn order
1) Shuffle the NSFW deck and deal a hand to each player per normal rules.
2) Remove any cards your group agrees are too spicy for the table; you can customize based on your crew’s comfort level.
3) Decide a starting player; the rest of the game proceeds in clockwise order as players take their turns.
4) The draw pile sits center, a ticking time bomb of hilarity and potential disaster.

On a turn, you can perform one of several actions before or after drawing a card. The exact sequencing varies depending on the edition, but the vibe remains consistent: misdirection, bluffing, and catastrophic luck in a neat little package.

### Card types and what they do
- Exploding Kitten cards: you don’t want to see these, unless you have a last-minute miracle.
- Defuse cards: usually easier to hoard until someone has a real moment of luck or defuses your destiny with style.
- Attack cards: force the next player to take multiple turns, increasing chaos while you sip your drink and bask in triumph.
- See the Future cards: peek at the top few cards of the deck to plan your next heist of fortune.
- Nope cards: a playful shield that can stop opponents, but be careful not to overuse; everyone has a limit on how often you can interrupt an epic reveal.

The NSFW edition adds its own twist cards that reference adult themes, pop culture punchlines, and occasionally absurd mini-challenges. The pacing can swing wildly: one round you’re calmly defusing, the next you’re dodging a barrage of outrageous cards that make your jaw drop and your bragging rights ascend to legendary status.

### Strategy and social play
- Information is a currency: use See the Future to glean risk, or bluff with misdirection to make opponents waste a good Defuse.
- Timing matters: you want to use attack or nope cards at moments that maximize fear and laughter, not when you need them most defensively.
- Audience matters: this game shines when players are willing to lean into the humor and bring the right amount of roast to the table without crossing a line that kills the vibe.

If you play with a group that loves improv, inside jokes, and quick bursts of energy, NSFW Edition can feel like a late night show with a deck as your stage. If your crew prefers quiet strategy and reserved humor, there are other party games that might deliver more consistent laughs with fewer shock factors.

## How to run a successful NSFW session without breaking the vibe
- Set expectations in advance: a quick sign that indicates comfortable boundaries goes a long way. A simple, We are here for laughs only, and no one should feel uncomfortable, sets the tone.
- Start with a demo round: show the kind of moves you can pull and the kind of reactions you can expect. It lowers anxiety and raises energy.
- Use the right space: a living room with a closed door or a basement with a projector is ideal. A kitchen table is fine if you don’t mind fragile egos and spilled drinks.
- Deck management: if you suspect a few players will veer into offense territory, pre-select the cards that push the envelope less and keep the spicy cards for after the first couple of rounds where you know your crew better.
