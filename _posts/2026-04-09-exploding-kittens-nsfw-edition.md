## How gameplay works in NSFW mode
The core mechanics resemble the original: players take turns drawing cards until someone draws an exploding kitten and loses, unless they can defuse the explosion with a defuse card. The NSFW edition spices things up with extra action cards, more provocative defuses, and occasional side rules that shift pacing and tension.

### Setup and turn order
1) Shuffle the NSFW deck and deal a hand to each player per standard rules.
2) Remove any cards your group agrees are too spicy for the table; you can customize based on your crew’s comfort level.
3) Decide a starting player; play proceeds clockwise.
4) The draw pile sits center, a ticking time bomb of hilarity and potential disaster.

On a turn, you can perform one of several actions before or after drawing a card. The exact sequencing varies by edition, but the vibe remains consistent: misdirection, bluffing, and catastrophic luck wrapped in a neat little package.

### Card types and what they do
- Exploding Kitten cards: you don’t want to see these, unless you have a last-minute miracle.
- Defuse cards: usually easier to hoard until a moment of luck or style saves you from doom.
- Attack cards: force the next player to take multiple turns, increasing chaos while you sip your drink and bask in triumph.
- See the Future cards: peek at the top few cards of the deck to plan your next fortune heist.
- Nope cards: a playful shield that can stop opponents, but don’t overuse; everyone has a limit on interruptions.

The NSFW edition adds its own twists—cards that reference adult themes, pop culture punchlines, and occasionally absurd mini-challenges. Pacing can swing wildly: one round you are calmly defusing, the next you are dodging a barrage of outrageous cards that surprise and delight in equal measure.

### Strategy and social play
- Information is currency: use See the Future to assess risk, bluff with misdirection to force opponents to burn a Defuse, or bait someone into the hot seat with a clever play.
- Timing matters: use attack or nope cards at moments that maximize fear and laughter, not just defensively.
- Audience matters: the game shines when players lean into improv, inside jokes, and quick bursts of energy without crossing lines that derail the vibe.

If you are playing with a crew that loves spontaneous improv and rapid-fire humor, NSFW Edition can feel like a late-night show with a deck as your stage. If your group prefers quiet strategy and reserved humor, there are other party games that deliver more consistent laughs with fewer shock factors.

## How to run a successful NSFW session without breaking the vibe
- Set expectations in advance: a quick sign of comfort and boundaries goes a long way. A simple statement like, we are here for laughs only, helps establish safety.
- Start with a demo round: show the kinds of moves you can pull and the reactions you can expect. It lowers anxiety and raises energy.
- Use the right space: a living room with a closed door or a basement with a projector works best. A kitchen table is fine if you are okay with potential spills and bold conversations.
- Deck management: if you suspect a few players will push the envelope, pre-select the cards that push the envelope less and reserve the spicier cards for later rounds when you know the crew better.
- Safety net: consider a consent card or a quick pause mechanism to allow anyone to opt out mid-game without breaking momentum.
