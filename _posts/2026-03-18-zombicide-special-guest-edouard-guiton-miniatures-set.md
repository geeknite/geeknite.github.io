- Pro: Clear, readable silhouettes that read well on the table, even when the board is busy with card backs and token piles.

### Paintability and techniques
Edouard’s aesthetic tends to favor bold highlights and a controlled use of washes to push shadow where it’s needed most. For the new survivors, you’ll find that skin tones benefit from a quick base wash and a couple of midtone steps to avoid that flat plastic look. The zombies are where experimentation shines: a bold purple or green wash in the recesses, followed by a thin glaze of a desaturated color to push the undead vibe without over-saturating the model.

If you’re a painter who loves a challenge, this set provides a friendly workload. If you’re a hobbyist who paints in three-minute sessions between gaming rounds, you’ll still get a satisfying result with minimal effort thanks to the sculpt readability and Edouard’s color cues.

## Painting and display ideas: tips from a geek who loves a good brush stroke
If you’re reading Geeknite, you probably enjoy the painting journey as much as the gameplay. Here are some practical tips to maximize the Edouard Guiton experience.

- Start with a clean primer. A light gray or neutral base helps the skin tones pop without fighting with underlying plastic shading.
- Use a controlled wash to define cheekbones and brow ridges on the survivors. Don’t flood the face; you want micro shadows that read well at table distance.
- For the zombies, try a quick layered approach: skin midtone, edge drybrush for texture, then a glaze to unify the color and add a cohesive undead look.
- Weapons and gear can benefit from metallics with a couple of dulling washes to keep them in the gritty world of Zombicide rather than looking like toy chrome.
- Display tip: place the art print in a place where the lighting catches the textures. The set is designed for dramatic shadows, so your light direction can dramatically alter how the miniatures read on your shelf.
