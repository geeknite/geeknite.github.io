## Maintenance and Care: Keeping the Beige Bright
### Cleaning Routine
- Vacuum weekly on low settings to preserve pile structure. For large, high-traffic zones, you may want to run a quick pass twice to pull out debris that would otherwise embed itself.
- For stains, treat promptly with a mild detergent solution. Blot rather than rub, to avoid piling and fiber distortion.
- If machine washable: use a gentle cycle with cold or warm water, mild detergent, and avoid high spin speeds that could distort the backing or edges. Lay flat to dry to prevent warping. Do not tumble dry.
- Avoid chlorine-based bleaches; they can dull the beige tone and cause irregular fading over time.

### Rotation and Longevity
Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fading. Beige hides traffic patterns well, but you don’t want one corner to bear the brunt of a gaming chair’s wheels or a dog’s enthusiastic sprint. Rotating helps the fibers wear evenly and keeps the color tone uniform across the entire surface.

### Edge Care
If you notice edge curling, re-align the rug and re-seat with a rug pad beneath. Small steps like this prevent tripping hazards and preserve the rug’s crisp silhouette. In long-term use, regular checks for fraying or loose threads can save you from a larger repair project later.
