## Assembly: a patient, modular wedding of components

If you enjoy assembling furniture as a sport, you will love this build. The modular blocks are designed to click together with a satisfying firmness. It is not a one-hour project; plan for a full team labor day with a few snacks and some drinking water. Here is a rough breakdown of what to expect:

- Stage 1: Unpack and inventory. Check every panel, leg, and connector. The labeling is clear, but you may still feel like you are playing a giant 3D puzzle.
- Stage 2: Frame assembly. The base frame goes first, with careful alignment to ensure the long row is perfectly straight. A laser level will become your best friend, or you can rely on a simpler spirit level if you enjoy error margin as a lifestyle.
- Stage 3: Panel installation. The desk surfaces attach to the frame with a handful of screws per section. It helps to have a magnetic screwdriver and a few extra hands to avoid losing screws into the abyss where only the Fates know them.
- Stage 4: Cable spine and panel routing. Route power and data cables through the central spine. A neatly organized cable system reduces maintenance headaches and makes IT happier than a server rack with no drama.
- Stage 5: Integration and testing. Install monitors, power strips, and a test run with a few users. Check for wobble and adjust the leveling feet as needed. You should end with a stable, silent workspace that feels premium rather than a DIY fort built in a hurry.

## Maintenance and care: keep the oak blk looking sharp

Cleaning a desk this size is surprisingly straightforward. A microfiber cloth, gentle wood cleaner appropriate for oak, and a damp wipe for spills will handle most everyday accidents. Avoid harsh chemicals that can dull the finish or degrade the protective coating on the steel frame. Regularly check the cable spine for loose connectors and tighten any loose screws; a rattling spine is a symptom of a larger problem and can drive you to the sofa in despair.

If you have a dedicated facilities team, set up a quarterly maintenance window to inspect leveling feet, monitor mounts, and any dividers you might be using. This ensures your nine-meter hero remains as glorious in year three as it was on opening day.
