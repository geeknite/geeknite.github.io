Contract-grade fabrics are designed to survive coffee spills, pen marks, and the occasional post-it storm. Cleaning typically involves a mild fabric cleaner and blotting rather than scrubbing. ## Practical considerations: space, power, and future-proofing

### Where to put it and what to run to it
Measure your room with surgical precision. Ensure doorways, corner radii, and HVAC plenum clearance align with the intended placement. Power and data routing should be planned to avoid tail-spinning cables along doorways and under foot traffic lanes. If you plan to add a USB hub or a personal monitor arm, check the desk width and load capacity to prevent sagging or wobble under the weight of modern dual-monitor setups.

### Future-proofing tips
- Plan for at least one extra data drop per cubicle for future devices. 
- Consider modular panels that can be swapped if the team expands or reconfigures.
- Use a shared cable tray that can be relocated without disassembly when you switch desks around.

### Maintenance schedule
Create a simple quarterly routine: vacuum seats lightly, wipe down surfaces with appropriate cleaners, and re-check screws and brackets for any looseness. The fabric will thank you in the long run, and so will your IT team when there’s no need to field a flood of “my monitor won’t connect” tickets.
