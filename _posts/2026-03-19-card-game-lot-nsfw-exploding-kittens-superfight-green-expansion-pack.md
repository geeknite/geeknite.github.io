## Superfight: the art of arguing your way to victory

Superfight is not a conventional combat game; it is a structured argument game dressed up as a party debate about who would win in a fight between two or more hypothetical opponents. The premise is simple: you’re given a scenario with two characters (for example, a “Ninja librarian” vs a “Robot chef”) and a deck of cards that adds modifiers like “has lasers,” “can fly,” or “partnered with a discount coupon.” Your job is to fashion a narrative that convinces the group that your champion would prevail.

### Mechanics and what makes it sing in a mixed crowd
The core mechanic is social: you argue, persuade, and sometimes wheedle other players into seeing your point. The best rounds hinge on quick wits, a dash of theater, and the confidence to sell your fight even when the odds appear ridiculous. The game encourages creativity: your reasoning can hinge on personality, absurd logic, or pop-culture references that land with the room. It’s not about gold-standard metrics or strict rules enforcement; it’s about the storytelling energy that erupts when people realize they’ve inadvertently drafted a better argument than the previous speaker.

In a lot of ways, Superfight mirrors improv theatre in a board game form: you set up a premise, you claim your advantage, and you invite the room to buy into your narrative.
