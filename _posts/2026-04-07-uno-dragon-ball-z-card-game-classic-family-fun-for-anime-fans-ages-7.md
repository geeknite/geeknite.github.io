## How to play in under 5 minutes
If you already know UNO, you are ninety percent there. The Dragon Ball Z spin adds a few flavor cards that spice up the standard rules. Here is a quick refresher that will have you ready for game night in a handful of breaths.

- The objective remains the same: shed all your cards first. The last player standing is the winner. If you want to go deep, you can add the usual UNO house rules like calling out UNO when you have one card left.
- The deck uses the standard color groups along with a DBZ twist on special action cards. Draw two, skip, reverse, and wild cards still function like classic UNO, but with DBZ flavor annotations on the powers that matter.
- Power move cards grant special abilities that align with DBZ moments but stay faithful to the pace of UNO. For example, a Kamehameha card might force opponents to draw extra cards or skip turns, while a Spirit Bomb card could wipe out a small block of penalty cards from a player’s hand. The exact effects are defined on the card, but the gist remains familiar: do not get stuck with a hand full of useless cards when a power move hits the table.
- Placement and play order follow standard UNO rhythm: players take turns playing a card that matches color or number, or lay down a wild card to change the color vibe. When you can’t play, you draw from the draw pile. The tension ramps up as the power moves come into play, especially when someone tries to sprint to the finish while your team is mid fusion planning.
- Five minute crash course: the simplest way to teach is to demonstrate the core mechanic, then reveal the power move cards as they appear. A quick example: you have a blue card in hand and a Kamehameha is active; you can chain a wild color shift to catch opponents off guard while keeping your lead intact. You will quickly pick up whether you need to hold back on the power moves during a tense late game or unleash them right away for maximum drama.

### Quick rules cheat sheet
- Start by shuffling the DBZ infused UNO deck and deal seven cards to each player.
- The top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile with the color or number matching the starter card.
- On your turn, you must either play a card that matches color or number or use a wild card to switch color. If you cannot, you draw one card.
- If a power move card is played, resolve its effect according to its card text. These effects are designed to be clear for younger players but fun for adults as well.
- When you are down to one card, declare UNO to avoid a penalty if you forget. The penalty is typically drawing extra cards, so remember the shout before it is too late.
- The first player to empty their hand wins the round. In a longer session, you can play best of three rounds or set a time limit for a quick party game night.
