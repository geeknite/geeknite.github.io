## Assembly Tips and Tricks

As with most large furniture, assembly can feel like a puzzle with a missing piece and a few dowels that roll away like tiny metal ants. Preparation is key. Here are a few tips I wish I had known before I started:

- Clear a large workspace: This is not a coffee table, this is a spaceship console. You need room to lay out panels, screw bags, and the inevitable spare spare parts.
- Sort the hardware by type: A small tackle box is a good investment here. The Allen wrenches, bolts, washers, and nuts are prone to misplacement if you try to memorize them by feel alone.
- Use the manual as a guide, not a bible: The instruction sheets are decent; if you get stuck, look for a step that has you align two big panels with holes that only align in one orientation.
- Two-person method optional but helpful: The heavier frame sections are easier to handle with two people. It prevents dropped pieces and minor panic attacks.
- Tighten gradually: Do not crank every bolt to maximum torque in one go. A little at a time ensures you don’t warp panels or strip threads.
- Leave cable managers for last: It is much easier to thread cables when the entire desk is assembled and stable.
