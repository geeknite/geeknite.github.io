## Practicality: Use, Care, and Everyday Life

### Durability and Wear

Cotton chenille is not the same as a heavy-duty wool; however, it’s surprisingly resilient for typical domestic use. The braided weave gives the rug a bit of bounce and helps to mask minor footpaths that would otherwise create a flat spot in a shorter pile rug. It’s not meant to be a heavy-traffic workhorse in a bustling family room with dogs that treat the living room as a track for their Olympic event, but for most households, it should perform well with regular vacuuming and seasonal rotations.

If you’re worried about snagging or pilling from pets with sharp claws or oversized shoes, the braided chenille should hold up better than loose-looped shag alternatives. Still, we recommend a gentle approach: use a vacuum with a beater bar wide set to low suction, and avoid aggressive carpet brushes that could fray the edges.

### Cleaning and Maintenance

The care label on this rug emphasizes spot cleaning and professional cleaning as needed. The general rule is to treat spills immediately with blotting rather than rubbing; use a mild detergent mixed with water for stubborn spots and test on an inconspicuous area first. For larger spills or areas with heavy traffic, you may want to rely on a professional rug cleaning service rather than attempting a DIY dye or bleach session, which can alter the ombre gradient.

Rotation matters with textures like chenille. If your room experiences strong sun at certain hours, rotate the rug every few months to even out exposure and avoid sun bleaching on the lighter center. Use furniture coasters or felt pads to prevent indentation from heavy chairs.

## Maintenance and Longevity: What to Expect

Rug longevity depends on usage, maintenance, and environment. This rug’s cotton chenille surface accepts a light, modern aesthetic’s treatment well, but it’s not designed for the most brutal corridors or entryways that see constant mud storms. To maximize the life of your rug:

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting.
- Rotate every 3-4 months to promote even wear.
- Use a rug pad to prevent sliding and to improve grip on wooden or tile floors.
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12-18 months depending on traffic.

With regular care, the rug should retain its shape, comfort, and gradient drama for several seasons.
