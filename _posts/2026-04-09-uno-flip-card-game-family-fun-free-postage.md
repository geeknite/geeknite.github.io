## How to play: from setup to shenanigans

### Setup and base rules

1) The deck is shuffled, and each player receives the standard number of cards (usually seven). The top card of the draw pile is flipped to begin the discard pile. Players take turns matching either color or number, using action cards to keep things spicy. The twist? If you have a Flip Card on the table, you trigger the flip mechanic to reveal the opposite color side and a new set of rules. Players must adapt quickly, and the game rewards memory and strategic risk-taking in equal measure.

2) The color-coded two-sided deck is a clever design choice. On the front, you play with bright colors and classic UNO actions (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild). On the back, you’ll encounter a different color palette and a refreshed set of actions, including Flip-specific powers and variations that can alter who is winning and who is about to draw a mountain of cards. The back side is what creates the “two games in one” effect—it’s like a mini expansion embedded in the original box.

3) When you have one card left, you shout “UNO!”—a chorus that never gets old, even if your family pretends not to hear you. If you forget and someone calls you out, a penalty draws a few extra cards. It’s light, it’s fair, and it ensures that even the most confident player doesn’t coast on a single clean run.

### Flip mechanics: the heart of the chaos

After a Flip Card is played, the deck flips to reveal the reverse side with new colors and rules. This sudden switch-up can alter luck, strategy, and endgame timing in a big way. The Flip side adds tactical depth: players must recalibrate their hand and choose when to press their advantage or hold back for a looming Flip turn that could topple the current leader. The sheer unpredictability is what makes UNO Flip more than a casual pastime; it becomes a living, breathing party game that evolves in real time with every flip.

### Special cards and strategy notes

- Flip Cards: The obvious catalyst for chaos. Use them when you’re ready to invert the game state and shift momentum.
- Wild Cards: Keep a flexible hand that can adapt to both sides of the deck.
- Draw Two and Skip: These are the social throttles—use them to slow down the leader or accelerate your own comeback.
- Color Choice Wild: The “choice” power can be clutch when you’re staring down a sea of mismatched colors. Think of it as a tiny puzzle with a royal flush of color options.

Strategy-wise, UNO Flip rewards both patience and opportunism. If you’re the kind of player who enjoys reading the room, you’ll relish the flip’s sudden momentum shifts. If you prefer to lean into calculated aggression, use Flip Cards to pivot the table as often as possible. However, beware the endgame: a few reckless flips can leave you exposed to a last-minute surge from a clever underdog who’s been quiet all night. The back-and-forth rhythm makes it ideal for a mixed-age group where the youngest players still get to contribute critical moves while the adults enjoy the bragging rights when the table tips in their favor.
