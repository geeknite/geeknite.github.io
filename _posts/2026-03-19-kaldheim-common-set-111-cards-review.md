## The 111 Commons: a color-by-color tour
In this section we break down the 111 common cards by color family, with notes on typical role, synergy, and where they fit in a draft or sealed format.

### White
White commons in Kaldheim typically emphasize efficient bodies and small, value-driven tricks. You’ll find a mix of early board presence—two- or three-drops that trade up on a threat—paired with a few cheap combat tricks that can swing a single combat into your favor. The white color-scheme often leans on resilience and marginal value: you’ll encounter lifelink on a bear, a small anthem effect at common, and a handful of creatures that help you stabilize the early game while your more ambitious plans come online.

Budget tip: look for a couple of two-drop creatures that can survive early trades and a removal or deterrent that buys you another turn to deploy your foretell payoff. White commons are great at pressuring a board and forcing the opponent into awkward blocks.

### Blue
Blue common cards often serve tempo and card-advantage roles. Expect cheap card-draw, efficient counters at low mana costs, and evasive creatures to pressure your opponent’s life total while you keep options open. The blue commons typically reward careful planning rather than brute force, encouraging you to sequence plays to maximize your foretell strategy and card efficiency.

Budget tip: keep an eye out for cantrips or draw engines that fit well with your foretell plans. A couple of tempo plays can outmaneuver a slower board and help you keep your opponent on the back foot.

### Black
Black commons frequently deliver value through efficient removal, evasive creatures, and graveyard interaction. You’ll see a range of two-for-ones in limited formats, enabling you to trade effectively and keep a lean curve while you build toward stronger threats from later in the game. In a Kaldheim context, you can leverage black’s removal to disrupt your opponent’s plan and then use graveyard synergy to pick up the pace when needed.

Budget tip: a couple of removal options combined with a cheap threat or two can create a strong tempo plan. Don’t discount the power of a well-timed discard or a graveyard-shaping play that accelerates your strategy.

### Red
Red is the color of speed and reach in Kaldheim commons. Aggression is the name of the game here, with a set of cheap threats and direct-damage options that can close out games quickly if left unchecked. Foretell can unlock surprising tempo plays—spend two mana off-turn, then unleash the payoff at a key moment to surprise your opponent. The red commons are great for players who want to push the action and put pressure on their opponent’s life total.

Budget tip: pick up early inevitabilities—two-drops and a couple of direct-damage spells—and pair them with a foretell threat to maximize pressure across turns.

### Green
Green commons anchor ramp, tokens, and big creatures at a scale that can overwhelm an opponent if left to run. In Kaldheim, green sometimes pairs with snow or foretell for extra value, but at common, the core you want is solid bodies, combat tricks, and reliable growth. The green common pool rewards players who protect their threats with a combination of pump effects and dependable bodies.

Budget tip: if you’re drafting into green, target a couple of cheap ways to accelerate your growth and perhaps one or two removal options to deal with opposing threats. A token-producing common can sometimes win games on its own when supported by bigger threats.

### Multicolor and Snow
Kaldheim’s multis and snow themes show up in the commons as well. Snow mana provides a secondary resource for certain spells, and some commons become more valuable within a snow-synergy deck. Multicolor choices give you access to trickier, more interactive lines at the cost of a slightly more demanding mana base.

Budget tip: multi-color decks can be surprisingly affordable if you pick efficient two-color combos with reliable mana; look for two-color commons that smooth your early curve and allow you to reach foretell or snow synergies later.

### Lands and mana fixing
The common pool isn’t all creatures and spells; you’ll find reliable land options that help you hit your color requirements and keep your turn structure intact. In a limited environment, a solid land base is as important as any spell, allowing you to deploy threats on-curve and still hold the mana for foretell or bigger finishers.

### Foretell mechanics and snow synergy
Foretell is one of Kaldheim’s signature mechanics, and you’ll notice it especially in the common pool. A foretell card is paid for and then can be cast from exile later for a cheaper mana cost. This creates interesting timing decisions: you can bank mana for a big swing or hold up a trick to protect your board. Snow synergy also appears in the common pool; certain cards reward a player who invests in snow permanents, adding a little sub-theme to your deckbuilding.

## Draft and gameplay tips
In draft, the commons are the glue that determines how efficient your curve will be across the first several turns. You want early bodies that prevent opponent aggression, midrange threats that scale, and a few bombs that can swing the late game once you’ve locked in the board.

Foretell can be a powerful tool if you learn to use it correctly. Don’t just cast foretell spells because they are cheap; consider the tempo implications of delaying a threat that your opponent will have to answer in a forced order. When you foretell, you set up a hidden third lane—your real tempo plan—where you can unleash a surprise threat when your opponent’s blockers and removal are tied up elsewhere.

In sealed, your life total is precious, and every decision matters. The 111 commons become even more critical when you’re less likely to see your exact colors in a multi-pack opening. You’ll need to rely on a flexible, well-rounded mana base and a couple of dependable creatures that can hold down the fort until your foretell plays can drop the hammer.

## Thematic coherence
Kaldheim’s flavor is woven through its mechanics and into the art of each common card. The Norse-inspired mythology is expressed through the characters on the cards, the mythic creatures that inhabit the set, and the overall design that emphasizes heroic actions and cunning. Even at common, these cards carry a sense of story—of dwarves and frost giants, of forged weaponry and wise kings—that makes drafting and playing the set feel like a mini-quest rather than a conventional card game.

## Final verdict
The Kaldheim Common Set is a well-designed, flavorful, and functional collection of 111 cards that offer solid value in both draft and sealed formats. It provides enough synergy to support diverse archetypes while maintaining a cohesive theme that makes sense within the set’s broader narrative. If you’re playing with a limited budget, these commons deliver more power than the typical starter pack and can still enable interesting, creative decks. The foretell mechanic elevates even a few common cards into real deck-building decisions, and the snow-theme adds a flavor dimension that makes the entire pool feel cohesive.
