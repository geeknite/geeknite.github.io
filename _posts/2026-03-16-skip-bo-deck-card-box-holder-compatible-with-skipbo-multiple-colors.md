### Sleeved cards: friend or foe?
Sleeved cards add bulk—usually a millimeter or two extra thickness per card—so if you’re an enthusiast who sleeves everything, be mindful. Some configurations may require a hair more clearance, especially if you’re using a thicker inner tray or an optional divider. On the upside, sleeves can dramatically improve durability during roughhousing and post-game card-slinging cleanup. If you’re a sleeve-snob, you might want to confirm the inner clearance before you commit to the color option you pick, because color shouldn’t be the only thing you’re considering when your sleeved Star-Skip-Bo deck meets the edge of destiny.

### Cleaning and maintenance

Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the finish, especially if you want the colors to stay vibrant after a year or two of non-stop Friday-night battles. If you’re the on-table snack king or queen, a quick wipe after use will keep things hygienic and ready for the next session.
