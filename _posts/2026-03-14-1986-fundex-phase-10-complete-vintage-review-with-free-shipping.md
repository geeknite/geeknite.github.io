## How to play in a pinch
If you want the fast version, here is the rough gist that will get you from setup to a chaotic finish in about 20 minutes per round:
1. deal a hand to each player and place the rest as a draw pile
2. on your turn, draw one card and discard one
3. aim to complete the current phase shown on the board by collecting sets, runs, and color combos
4. once you finish a phase, you advance to the next one and your score resets a little
5. the first player to finish all ten phases triggers the end of the round; points are tallied and the lowest score wins the game

the 10 phases typically escalate in difficulty, starting with simple sets and moving into more challenging combos that require a careful blend of luck and manipulation of the table talk.

The 1986 fundex edition keeps the rules spare and the pace brisk. there is no complicated scoring math beyond counting the cards you hold that didn't make a phase; this is perfect if your hiding strategy is to avoid learning the rules and still pretend you know what you are doing.
