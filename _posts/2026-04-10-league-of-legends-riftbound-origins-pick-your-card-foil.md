## The Pick Your Card mechanic
The star of this product line is the Pick Your Card mechanic. Instead of a fixed foil where you know what you’re getting from the moment you rip open the wrapper, you pick a card from a set, each card with a unique foil treatment. In practice, this means that your foil card is not only about art and rarity, but also a little mystery box you can display or use in casual play. The excitement comes from the moment of revelation—the moment you tilt the card toward a light and watch the holo pattern swirl like a nebula sailing across a monitor. It’s the card you didn’t know you wanted until it arrives with a fanfare of light.

### How it works
Think of it as a one-step deeper than a normal booster: you’re not guaranteed a foil, but you’re promised a card you’ll remember. The variety within the Pick Your Card set is designed to reward persistence; you’ll find some foils with subtle shimmer, others with dramatic color shifts that feel like they could be a moonrise on the Rift itself. The range of designs encourages collectors to chase different aesthetics rather than a single must-have rarity.

### Foil vs Non-foil
This is where the product gets interesting. Foil cards are more than just shinier versions of their regular counterparts. The foil layer changes color hues when seen from different angles; the embossed runes glow or crack slightly depending on the light, and the entire illustration can appear to breathe as you move. The non-foil card provides the same composition but without the reflective gimmick, which some players may prefer for readability or for avoiding glare at your local tournament. The decision to present both variants in the same release emphasizes a consumer truth: some players are here for performance, others for show, and many are both.
