### Grading and condition: what to look for in LP NM

When evaluating this card for LP NM, you want to inspect several key indicators. First, check the holo pattern for uniform sparkle across the surface. Any oily fingerprints, scratches, or muted holo is a red flag that drops the grade. Edge wear should be minimal and uniform; you do not want any large whitening on the borders or rounded corners that indicate wear. The back should have a clean, consistent color with no staining that could point to moisture exposure. The card’s corners should be sharp with no significant dings. For the Japanese print, ensure there is no misprint in the character text area, as JP text is subject to tiny alignment anomalies in some print runs.

LP NM is a flexible grade that reflects minor signs of handling rather than serious flaws. In this category, a small print line, a tiny scratch on the holo, or a minor border whitening can still be acceptable. The key is that the card should feel giftable, not battle-worn. If you can present it in a clean top loader with a soft sleeve and a solid binder page, you are likely in the LP NM zone rather than anything worse.

### Collector tips: care, storage, and display

- Storage: Use a high-clarity binder with acid-free pages or a hard acrylic case. Keep the card upright in a cool, dry space with stable humidity. Avoid direct sunlight, which can fade the holo or slightly alter the flame glow effect.
- Handling: Wear a soft microfiber glove or handle with clean hands. Oils from skin can accumulate over time and affect gloss and printing. Remember, you are not just handling a card; you are maintaining a tiny piece of the game’s soul.
- Sleeves: Use debate-worthy sleeves, ideally with a game-friendly thickness. For Japanese AR cards, consider sleeves with deeper color to preserve the look of the black flame against the border. A perfectly sized sleeve adds an extra layer of protection without crimping the card if you decide to store it in a deck box.
- Binders: A binder with high-contrast page design helps keep the flame effect visually prominent. A binder with a reflective back page can make the holo pop when you flip through the pages for friends and family who visit your gaming lair.
