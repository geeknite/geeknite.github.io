## Care and restoration tips for the tin and cards

- Clean with a soft, dry cloth to remove surface dust from the tin. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could scratch or corrode the metal.
- If the tin has stubborn grime in the lid seam, use a tiny brush or cotton swab lightly dampened with water. Do not soak the tin; moisture is the enemy of metal and paper alike.
- For card protection, consider sleeve protection if you plan to use the cards in a casual game night. This preserves the edges and helps maintain color integrity over time.
- Store in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. UV rays fade ink and graphics over the long term, even on glossy cards.
- If inserts are missing, think of replacements as part of the collection puzzle rather than a deal killer. Some collectors accept the absence of inserts as a sign of honest wear and the story of many nights of play.
