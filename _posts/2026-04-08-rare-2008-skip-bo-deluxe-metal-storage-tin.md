## Care and restoration tips for the tin and cards

- Clean with a soft, dry cloth to remove surface dust from the tin. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could scratch or corrode the metal.
- For stubborn grime in the lid seam, use a tiny brush or cotton swab lightly dampened with water. Do not soak the tin; moisture is the enemy of both metal and paper.
- Card protection: consider sleeves if you plan to use the cards in casual rounds. This preserves edges and color while keeping the nostalgia intact.
- Store in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. UV rays fade ink and graphics over time, even on glossy surfaces.
- If inserts are missing, view replacement as part of the collection story rather than a deal killer. Some collectors accept the absence of inserts as part of honest wear and the narrative of many nights of play.
