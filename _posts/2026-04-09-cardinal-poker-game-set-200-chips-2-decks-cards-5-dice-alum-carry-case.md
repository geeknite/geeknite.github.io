## Variant ideas and house-rule experiments

### The dice-augmented betting ladder

Introduce small, dice-based bets where players can wager an extra die’s value for a big bluff or a daring call. The dice add a sense of risk that complements the standard poker tension. You can devise a simple rule: if you win with a straight, you get an extra die in pot; if you lose with a high card, you give up a die. With two decks in play, you can run a quick memory game: after every round, players guess which card has appeared most often across both decks. It’s a surprising exercise in pattern recognition that can break up monotony and give your players a moment of nerdy satisfaction when someone nails the guess (and an equally satisfying moment when someone misses spectacularly).
