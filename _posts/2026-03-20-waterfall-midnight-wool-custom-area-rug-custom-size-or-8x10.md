## Size Matters: Custom Size vs 8x10
Here’s where the rug gets personal. You can opt for a custom size or the standard 8x10. Both choices come with nerd-level justification.

- Custom Size
  - Pros: You can tailor the exact footprint of your lounging zone. This is ideal for long walls, open floor plans, or a home theater where you need a particular edge to line up with seating. Custom sizing also means you can accommodate oddly shaped rooms, wrap around coffee tables, or create a convincing “rug island” in the middle of your living space.
  - Cons: The price tag can creep up if you’re not careful about trims and wastage. Also, the lead time will depend on the factory’s schedule and whether your apartment manager suspects you of building a launch pad for a spaceship in the living room.

- 8x10 Standard
  - Pros: Budget-friendly relative to bespoke editions, quicker delivery, and plenty of styling options. The 8x10 ratio sits nicely in many living rooms, offering generous coverage without overwhelming the space. It’s the Goldilocks of large area rugs: not too small, not too large, just right for mid-century modern palettes or maximalist statements.
  - Cons: If you’ve got a room with unusual dimensions or features, the standard size may require you to do a few furniture reconfigurations or put up with the “almost-but-not-quite” look. You’ll also want to consider transitions to adjacent tile or hardwood floors if your flooring plan needs a more custom approach.

From a usability perspective, the custom size option shines when you want the rug to anchor a specific area: around a seating arrangement, under a dining table with generous clearance, or as a dramatic entryway runner. The 8x10 is a reliable workhorse: easy to arrange, easy to clean, and easy to photograph for your next interior-design post.

To help you decide, imagine the space as a stage and your furniture as actors. Do you want the rug to frame the action precisely (custom) or provide a broad, forgiving backdrop (8x10)? Either way, Waterfall Midnight Wool is up for the role.

## Performance: Comfort, Footfall, and Noise Levels
In practical terms, the rug feels cushy enough to soften the impact of a mis-timed desk chair wheel, yet sturdy enough to stand up to a child’s jumpy energy and a dog’s enthusiastic zoomies. If you’re worried about sound, wool rugs tend to dampen footfall and help reduce echo in open-plan spaces. It’s not a miracles-on-floor kind of effect, but it’s a tangible improvement over a hard surface, especially in apartments or homes with hard floors that otherwise feel cavernous.

Stain resistance is a mixed bag with natural fibers: wool can handle spills if cleaned promptly, and Waterfall Midnight’s dye helps hide minor mishaps. You’ll want to treat it with a gentle cleaner and avoid harsh solvents. Regular vacuuming (without beater bars on the highest setting) helps the pile retain its bounce. For the occasional spill, blot not rub, and consider a wool-safe spot cleaner. The rug’s natural oils can assist with repellency—until they don’t—so a cautious approach will keep your piece from changing color or texture after a single heroic coffee incident.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh
Maintenance matters more with wool than most synthetic dancers at a nightclub. Here’s a practical regimen:

- Vacuum weekly with a suction-only setting and a rotating brush turned off.
- Rotate the rug every 3–6 months to ensure even wear, especially if you have a high-traffic zone or a sun-soaked corner.
- Address spills promptly. Blot with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can embed stains deeper.
- Use a wool-safe cleaner for spots; test on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Consider a rug pad. A good pad reduces friction, increases longevity, and helps with acoustic dampening.

If you’re a clock-watcher, you’ll appreciate that wool rugs tend to shed a little at first. Don’t panic; this is typical of many new wool pieces. A little vacuuming and patience usually resolves the issue within a few weeks. And yes, you can still vacuum the fringe if you have one, just go slowly and mind the wire filter on the vacuum head.

## Installation Tips: Placing It Like a Pro
Placement matters as much as the rug itself. Consider these tips:

- Measure the space precisely. If you’re going custom, account for furniture clearance and the oval shape of coffee-table legs.
- Use a non-slip rug pad to keep things aligned and reduce sliding during hot dance-floor moments in your living room.
- Align the rug’s longest axis with your main seating line for the most balanced look.
- If you have sliding doors, test clearance to avoid scuff marks on the rug edges.
- In open-plan spaces, let the rug anchor zones rather than trying to cover every inch with furniture. The Waterfall Midnight works best when used to define an area rather than cover every inch of floor space.
