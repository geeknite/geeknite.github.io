## How UNO works in this bundle

### The basics
UNO is a rhythm game for a group that enjoys yelling at the top of their lungs with friendly intent. The goal is to be the first player to get rid of all your cards by matching color or number with the top card of the discard pile. Special cards spice things up: Draw 2 increases the tension; Reverse flips the vibe; Skip interrupts your sister-in-law's thoughtful turn; Wild cards let you pick a color for the rest of the table. The rules are intentionally simple, but the social dynamics are deliciously complex.

### A typical turn
1. If you can play a matching color or number, you do it. If you cannot, you draw a card.
2. When you have one card left, you must shout UNO. The rules pin you to that line like a hall monitor with a badge of honor. If you forget, other players can call you out and force you to draw two cards.
3. The game continues until someone has no cards in hand. Victory means bragging rights and the last slice of pizza, which is a big deal.

### Strategy light and comical missteps
UNO is not a brain teaser, but there is still a delightful strategy in who you skip or switch to color on a wild card. The real skill is in reading your opponents signals and deciding whether to stall for a power move or press your advantage when you sense weakness in the table talk. It is a social experience more than a pure card game, which means you will suffer dramatic triumphs and equally dramatic losses in equal measure.

## How Skip-Bo works in this bundle

### The premise
Skip-Bo is a sequencing card game where players try to be the first to empty their stock pile by playing cards in ascending order into building piles. The kicker is that you can only play cards that are in the correct order, and you can use wild stock piles to accelerate your progress or block others. It is a different vibe from UNO but still very friendly for a family setting.

### Rules in a nutshell
- Each player has a stock pile and a personal hand of seven cards.
- You can play cards from your hand or from the top of your stock onto building piles in ascending order starting at 1.
- You can empty a hand or stock pile into the building piles to keep your turn going.
- The first player to deplete their stock pile wins the round.

### Bite-size strategy tips
Skip-Bo rewards careful planning and cautious risk-taking. You want to balance using your cards to advance now with reserving a few for the late game. The randomness keeps it honest and the player interaction keeps it spicy. If UNO is the chaotic cousin who shows up to the family barbecue, Skip-Bo is the patient sibling who quietly catalogs the moves before the big moment.
