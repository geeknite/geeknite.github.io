## How to Play: Rules at a Glance (With the Corner Twist)

PIT is a social, real-time trading game. The goal is to collect all cards of a single commodity type in your hand and then declare that commodity with the appropriate shout on your turn. The DELUXE Corner The Market edition tweaks the timing and adds corner control, but the essence remains: a chaotic sprint to the finish with your opponents hot on your heels.

Here’s a simplified run-through:

- Deal: Depending on the number of players (usually 3–6), deal out the appropriate number of cards to each player. The rest form a stock pile in the center.
- Corner setup: Place market tokens on the four corners of the central market map. These corners represent distributed market control and influence the flow of cards through the round. The more market tokens you control, the more leverage you have when trades occur—at least in theory.
- Objective: Collect all cards of a single commodity type in your hand. You can only win by shouting the commodity name when you have a complete set, which triggers the end of the round. The bell’s final ding accompanies your victory declaration.
- Trading: The core mechanic is real-time trading. Players simultaneously shout “Trade!” and offer cards to each other in quick succession. The catch is that you cannot hold onto cards you don’t need forever; you must trade away, which creates a dynamic of risk and reward. You’ll end up with players trying to “corner” a market by stacking noise, timing their trades, and leveraging the corner tokens.
- Corners and market pressure: The Corner The Market twist means you’ll be watching market corners and your own hand with more care. If you can push certain commodities toward a corner and hold tokens there, you may be able to speed up a round or gain a slight advantage in the critical moments. The exact benefits can vary by edition, so be sure to read the included variant rules.
- Endgame: When a player completes a set, they shout the commodity name (e.g., “Wheat!”) and slam their hand down on the table while the others react in a chorus of “PIT!”-themed chaos. The round ends; scores are tallied or simply reset for the next round depending on your preferred house rules.

This is not a game for the faint of heart. It requires attention, multi-tasking, and a tolerance for the delightful noise that emerges when five adults simultaneously try to trade, shout, and keep their poker faces intact. The Bell NEW variant adds the auditory cue that can be both a morale boost and a mild adrenaline spike. You’ll quickly associate the bell with “time to risk it all” and then laugh as one player accidentally trades the wrong card, causing a cascade of fiasco that somehow makes sense in the best possible way.

For players who love strategy, there’s more nuance than you might expect. The Corner The Market variant rewards players who track not just their own hand, but the visible market layout and the probable distribution of cards among opponents. You’ll spot opportunities to gently nudge a corner by trading strategically with the person who’s most likely to benefit from a corner. You’ll also learn to read the table’s energy: who’s on the edge of losing control, who’s quietly hoarding cards, and who’s just trying to yell the loudest while doing nothing intelligent. The chaos is the point, but there’s a surprising amount of strategic texture if you’re paying attention.


## Strategy: How to Win Without Losing Your Voice

Let’s be honest: the charm of PIT is that you don’t usually win by careful planning alone. You win by speed, psychology, and a fair bit of luck. That said, you can tilt the odds in your favor if you treat the corner mechanic as a subtle game within the game. Here are a few practical strategies to keep in mind:

- Know the corners, not just your cards: The Corner The Market variant rewards players who are good at spotting where the market is leaning and where it could go next. If you can sense that a particular commodity is about to be cornered by someone, you may be able to pivot to a different commodity earlier in the round, avoiding a painful standoff.
- Manage your hand risk: You’ll often be tempted to chase a single commodity at breakneck speed. Resist the urge to overcommit early; a balanced hand gives you more flexible trading options as the round evolves. Don’t be afraid to let a trade go if it improves your odds in the long game, even if it costs you a small edge in the moment.
- Guess the table’s energy: Some players swing between bold, loud declarations and calculated silence. Reading the room helps you time your own declarations for maximum impact. If the table is shouting like a choir, a calm, methodical move may feel rebellious and can work in your favor.
- Use the bell for timing, not just noise: The Deluxe bell isn’t just a toy. It marks a rhythm. Ring it when you want to accelerate a trade sequence, or to punctuate an important moment as you try to close out a round. It’s a tool for pacing, not just a sound effect.
- Don’t fear mistakes: The chaos is part of the joy. If you misplay a card, own it with a laugh, and pivot. The group energy often makes up for any misstep. The more you lean into the silly, the more fun everyone has, and that’s a win in itself.
