## Rarity, Condition, and Value: What to Expect
### Grading and Condition Considerations
In Carddas, the condition of VR cards with glossy finishes is a bit of a retail romance: you want flawless corners, perfect centering, a pristine surface, and minimal edge wear. Common issues to watch for include edge chipping from long-term sleeve storage, micro-scratches on the holo areas, and centering drift—basically, you want this card to look as if it rolled off a pristine printing press rather than found its way into a back-pocket duel at a bus stop.

If you’re aiming for a high-grade copy, teach yourself the language: corners, edges, surface, centering. ### How to Spot Authentic VR No. 262
- Check the print run alignment with the set it came from; authentic copies will align with known release windows for Japanese Carddas VR cards around No. 262. 
- Inspect the holo area for uniform foil patterns. If the foil looks like it’s doing the samba, you might have a counterfeit, or a misprint with unique charm.
- Inspect the back for consistent font, logo placement, and any set-specific watermark. Counterfeiters sometimes copy the front but get the back wrong—this is the moment you realize you’re not just buying a card, you’re buying a fingerprint of authenticity.

If you want to be thorough, grab a trusted friend with a magnifying glass and play “spot the tell” for a few minutes. If you both end up laughing and agreeing it’s real, you’ve earned some serious nerd street cred.

## Collectibility Tips: Caring for Your VR Card
- Storage: Use a rigid top loader or a PTG binder page designed for standard Dragon Ball Carddas cards. Keep the protective layer intact and avoid unnecessary handling to preserve the glossy surfaces.
- Environment: Avoid humidity and direct sunlight. Card stock can be finicky; too much sun can fade those luscious colors that define the Fusion era. A cool, dry place is your friend—some collectors even use silica gel packs in the binder to keep things crisp.
- Handling: Clean hands go a long way. Oils and fingerprints love to latch onto holo surfaces. Consider white cotton gloves for high-end displays or at least pause to wash hands before flipping through the stack.
- Display: If you’ve got a display shelf, consider a glass-front case. The goal is to invite admiration without inviting accidental sleeve-tearing or a curious cat from deciding your card is an entrée.
