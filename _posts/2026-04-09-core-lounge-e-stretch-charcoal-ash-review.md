- Frame: Lightweight aluminum alloy that balances stiffness with a bit of give. The intention is to keep mass low while ensuring rigidity under normal use.

- Upholstery: A performance fabric that’s soft to the touch but surprisingly resistant to frays and the occasional rogue zipper accident. It’s not faux leather drama, but it’s not lightweight mesh drama either.

The stretch feature is the chair’s party trick. The mechanism is engineered for reliability, not for propeller-level speed. It’s not going to fling you across the room in a heroic arc. ## Setup, Assembly, and Realistic Expectations

If you’re anything like me, you want a chair that ships in a box you can actually lift without calling in a small exoskeleton. The Core Lounge E Stretch ships in a compact box with straightforward assembly steps. It’s not a mind-bender like some flat-pack furniture nightmares; the directions are readable, the parts are labeled, and the required tools are minimal (usually just an included Allen wrench and your smartphone’s mental arithmetic). In under an hour, you’re ready to test the living daylights out of it.

### Assembly Tips
- Clear a little space: a chair and a half deserve a throne room with minimal turbulence.
- Organize parts before starting: keep screws separated from washers to avoid accidental “which piece is this?” moments.
- Don’t overtighten the bolts: you want a snug fit but you should be able to adjust the stretch mechanism without turning it into a Swiss cheese situation.
- Check the base for wobble after initial use: a quick re-tighten often cures any ghostly creaks.

The result is a chair that feels substantial without being overly heavy. It’s sturdy enough to feel premium but not so heavy that moving it around the room becomes a cardio workout. And yes, it reclines into a position that invites a moment of contemplation, which, in the age of remote work, is a precious commodity.

### Maintenance and Longevity
Care is relatively straightforward. Wipe with a damp cloth for minor spills; avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the fabric over time. For deeper cleaning, a mild fabric cleaner applied sparingly on a cloth works well. The foam and padding resist compression fairly well, but like all chairs that see real-life use, you’ll notice gradual contour changes after several months. The key to longevity is rotation and occasional micro-adjustments to keep the lumbar support aligned with your spine’s natural curve.

To extend life, consider a chair mat for hard floors and a rotating desk setup so you don’t get stuck in the “I’ve been staring at the same wall for three hours” syndrome.
