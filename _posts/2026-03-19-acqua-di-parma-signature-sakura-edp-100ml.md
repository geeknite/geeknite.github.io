Fragrance profile and notes
---------------------------
Top notes: The opening is bright and fresh, with citrusy zest that feels like a sunlit mandarin wedge and delicate bergamot. There’s a sparking, almost celebratory vibe here—think of the moment you raise a glass during a spring festival and your nose happens to snag a whiff of something zesty and clean.

Heart notes: Sakura—the star here—provides the floral heart. Cherry blossom is a nuanced note, offering a soft, powdery, slightly sweet floral that can feel breezy and non-offensive in the best possible way. It’s accompanied by light jasmine and possibly a touch of rose, which together give the fragrance a feminine-leaning but widely wearable character. The balance is key: nothing bursts too aggressively; everything feels measured, almost sculpted.

Base notes: The finish rests on a clean, comfortable bed of white musk with a whisper of cedar and amber. It’s not a heavy, resinous dry-down; it’s more like a cozy shawl that follows you through the afternoon. The musk adds a hint of warmth and a modern skin-tingle that helps the scent feel intimate rather than theatrical.

Sillage and longevity
----------------------
Sillage tends to hover in a moderate-to-strong range for the first couple of hours. It projects with a confident, breathable aura—enough to be noticed by colleagues without becoming the fragrance equivalent of a windows-into-the-office party. The blooming floral and citrus core behaves in a clean, linear fashion: it doesn’t explode into a million sub-notes, but it does linger. Expect about 6–8 hours on most skin types, which is respectable for a modern floral-leaning fragrance without heavy ingredients that tend to cling to fabric for days. It won’t become a nocturnal scent, but it will carry you through a full workday and into cool evenings if you don’t mind reapplying once or twice in a marathon day.

Notes for scent nerds and layering ideas
-----------------------------------------
If you’re into fragrance layering (which is a Geeknite staple), Sakura plays well with light citrus or delicate woods. A suggested layering approach might involve a citrus-forward base spray (a light bergamot or mandarin) underneath, then Sakura to introduce the floral core without becoming overly sweet. For nocturnal wear, consider pairing Sakura with a warmer vanilla-amber nuance for a night-out vibe that still holds onto daylight serenity. For the adventurous: a touch of green tea or soft musk on the outer layer can accentuate the freshness and extend the daylight hours a bit longer.

Note: If you’re into cross-brand layering, be mindful of balance. Sakura’s floral heart can be overwhelmed by heavy woods or resinous base notes, so use a light touch when pairing with powerful fragrances. The goal is a friendly encounter of scent notes, not a battle of chemical reactions in the air currents around you.
