## Strategy: Pacing, Bluffing, And Card Economy
The expansion pack throws fuel on the negotiation fire. Here are some practical strategy pointers you can take to the table on game night:

- Build a flexible plan that can adapt on the fly. Don’t chase a single color early unless you’re certain you can protect it. The new cards reward players who can pivot when an opponent intercepts their primary plan.
- Track the card economy. More cards mean more money and more distribution of cash and rents. Don’t drain your hand too quickly on a single move; you might need that hand late game when a wild card changes everything.
- Reserve wild cards for pivotal moments. It’s tempting to use a wild card to salvage a round, but the best plays often come from saving them for a finishing push or to disrupt someone who is about to close a set.
- Leverage the social element. Use the expansion’s increased negotiation pressure to your advantage. The roommate who is always friendly in the first two rounds might become a ruthless deal broker by Round 3 when new cards are in play.
