The Baraja Española is historically built around four suits: oros, copas, espadas, and bastos. The face cards—Rey (King), Caballo (Knight), and Sota (Jack)—carry the weight of centuries of tradition. ## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity Tips

### Cleaning and Storage
Keep the deck in its box when not in use. Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods, as color shifting and back patina can happen with extended exposure. If you need to clean the deck, do it gently with a dry microfiber; avoid moisture and harsh cleaners that could bleed the ink. For collectors, adding a silica gel pack in the storage box can help keep humidity in check and preserve the finish for a longer period of time.

### Handling Tips for Longevity
If you want your deck to age gracefully, treat it like a delicate instrument. Gentle shuffles, clean hands, and small, mindful dealing will go a long way. If you enjoy crowd-pleasing table talk during a game, use the opportunity to learn proper dealing technique, which will help you protect the deck and keep the game running smoothly.

### Maintenance Rituals
Develop a simple ritual: after each gaming session, return the deck to its box, wipe away any residue, and let it rest. If you’re a high-usage gamer, consider rotating between two or three decks to minimize wear on any one set. The idea isn’t to baby the deck, but to respect its role as the ceremonial tool of your game night.
