## How to Play with the Community Pack

If you’re already familiar with the base game, integrating the Community Pack is straightforward. If you’re new here, welcome to a world where a normal party game night can devolve into a Shakespearean roast while a referee with a clipboard tries to keep the chaos coherent. The community deck is designed to shuffle into the standard prompts, or it can be used as a standalone set for a dedicated community night. Here’s a practical guide to get you started:

### Setup

- Shuffle the base prompt deck and the community prompt deck together, or keep them separate depending on your mood. We recommend a 60/40 split for a balanced test of chaos.
- If you’re playing with the new scoring system, lay out the double-sided score sheet and choose which side you’ll use for this session.
- Designate a judge for the round. The judge role can rotate so that one person doesn’t feel personally victimized by every prompt.
- Prepare Wild Card tokens in a small bowl so you can reach for them without the dreaded “pause, what was the rule again?” moment.

### Core mechanics

- The prompt is drawn and read aloud. The players then craft their responses that fit the prompt in the wittiest way possible. If you’ve played this game before, you’ll recognize the structure; if not, think of it as a structured roast where the material is supplied by a deck of surprises.
- After everyone presents, the judge rates each response on a scale of 1 to 3 (or 1 to 5 if you’re feeling fancy). The highest-scoring response gains points for the respondent; the joke that earns the most laughs may earn bonus admiration points from the table.
- Wild Card tokens can be drawn to impose a quick twist: swap prompts, force a tie-break, or require players to answer in a specific style—like a movie trailer voice or a pirate accent. The tokens keep everyone on their toes and ensure no one picks a safe, boring line.

### Running a round

In a typical round, you’ll see a rhythm emerge:
- The prompt hits the table, and a hush of anticipation falls over the group.
- Each player crafts a line that stays on brand with the prompt but also betrays their personal style: your friend with the dry wit; your chaotic friend who swings for the fences; and you, probably, who forgot to eat lunch and is negotiating with the cheese plate instead of the prompt.
- The judge weighs the lines and declares a winner. The winner keeps the applause—physically or digitally—and the scoring reflects back into the ensemble’s energy, possibly shifting alliances or birthing entirely new inside jokes.

The big question for most groups is whether to embrace community prompts as a steady diet or as a spice rack. The truth is somewhere in the middle: community prompts excel as roasting fuel and performance challenges, while some base prompts are better for a relaxed night when you want to keep things breezy. A few community prompts push you toward bold choices that might be outside your comfort zone, which is exactly what makes the pack exciting. If your group thrives on improvisation, the Community Pack will feel like a new season of your favorite show—new arcs, new recurring gags, and a familiar rhythm you can lean into.

## Potential drawbacks and where to watch your step

No expansion is perfect, and the Community Pack is no exception. Here are a few caveats that can matter in certain groups:

- Content variety can lean spicy: Some prompts push into adult or edgy humor. If your group includes younger players or guests who aren’t comfortable with certain themes, use the safety sleeves and the optional filtering to keep things friendly. It’s better to calibrate before you oblige everyone to live with a line they’d rather forget.
- Volume of prompts: The deck is big, which is fantastic for experimentation, but it can overwhelm a first-time group. You may feel compelled to read every prompt aloud, which can stretch the night. Balance is key: rotate prompts and skip a few to keep the pace lively.
- Balancing competition and cooperation: Depending on your table, the scoring system can tilt toward a purely competitive environment or drift into collaborative storytelling. If you want a more cooperative vibe, adjust the scoring so the group as a whole wins or loses based on collective performance rather than per-person glory.
- Accessory clutter: The new score sheets and Wild Card tokens are delightful, but they add physical components to manage. If you’re a minimalist gamer or you jam-pack small tables, you’ll want to allocate a dedicated tray or box for tokens to prevent misplacement.

## Safety, consent, and inclusive play

A responsible publisher knows that humor can bruise if the prompts push too far. We appreciate the safety sleeves that come with the pack and the explicit guidance to opt into spicy prompts. On stream or in groups with mixed ages, it’s wise to set expectations before you begin and have a quick veto protocol for prompts that cross lines. The best nights are the ones where everyone is laughing, not the ones where someone is quietly counting the minutes until the round ends. The Community Pack gives you enough guardrails to keep things fun while still letting the chaos run free when your table is up for it.
