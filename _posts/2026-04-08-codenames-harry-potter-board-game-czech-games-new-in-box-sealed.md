## How to Play This Edition: A deeper dive

### Setup and First Turn

As with any Codenames game, you split players into two teams. Each round has a clue-giver who looks at the 5x5 grid of cards and tosses out a one-word clue with a number. In the HP edition, you’ll want clues that connect multiple HP terms without leaning too heavily on obscure references. The number tells your team how many cards on the board tie to the clue. The challenge is to craft a clue that nudges your team toward several of your agents while staying just clear enough to avoid hitting the enemy’s agents or, worse, the assassin (the infamous “killer word” in standard Codenames terms, rebranded here as a dangerous HP sequence or spell that should not be whispered aloud).

When you’re dealing with HP names and objects, it’s tempting to go big: “Felix” for multiple beneficial items, or “Horcrux” to hint at a collection. The trick is to keep it accessible for your teammates without turning the table into a trivia match about every HP character who ever sneezed.

The first few rounds teach you to savor the mental leaps. Some clues land like a perfectly timed Patronus; others splinter into a misfired spell and a rain of “how did you see that?” looks around the table. The HP theme provides extra social flavor, as players nod at the obvious connections and groan at the cheeky puns that link two or three cards into a single magical concept.

### The Flow of a Typical Round
1) Clue Giver announces a single word and a number.
2) Team members discuss and select board locations they believe match the clue.
3) If they hit their own agents, they remove those cards; hitting a neutral card has no immediate effect; hitting a bad card ends the turn and hands control to the other team.
4) If they hit an enemy agent, the opposing team narrows their chances on the next round, adding a dash of strategic misdirection.
5) The round continues until one team clears all their agents.

In this HP variant, you’ll encounter clues that nod to houses, magical objects, professors, and famous spells. The balance remains approachable; the clues are meant to be hints rather than HP trivia quizzes. For many players, this is perfect: you get the flavor without requiring an in-depth encyclopedia of wizarding minutiae.
