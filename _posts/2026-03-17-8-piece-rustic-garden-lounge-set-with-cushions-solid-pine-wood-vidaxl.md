## Durability and Weather Resistance
Outdoor furniture is a balance between aesthetics and weather endurance. Pine wood, when properly treated, resists warping and decay better than untreated timber, though it will not claim to be indestructible. Expect seasonal checks—tiny cracks that appear and disappear—as the wood breathes with temperature and humidity changes. The cushions’ fabric, when UV-stabilized, will resist color fading; however, prolonged exposure can still fade or degrade fabric over time. Regular maintenance and a little proactive care will keep the set looking rustic and fresh for several seasons.

### UV and moisture considerations
Extreme sun exposure can dry out wood and cause it to lose a bit of its natural luster. If you’re in an area with strong sun, consider applying a sealant or wood stain every couple of years to preserve color and reduce cracking. Moisture resistance is also a factor; pine absorbs moisture if not properly sealed, which can lead to warping or staining. A coordinated approach—protective sealant, proper storage, and a rain cover during storms—will help you get the most out of your investment.
