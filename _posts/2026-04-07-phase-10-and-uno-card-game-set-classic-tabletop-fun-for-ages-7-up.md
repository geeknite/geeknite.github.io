## How to play UNO and how to play Phase 10 side by side
UNO is the shorter of the two games, designed for quick rounds that can escalate into a lightning round where someone yells UNO and the rest of the table pretends they didn’t hear it. The aim is to get rid of all your cards before anyone else. You match colors or numbers, play action cards to mess with your opponents, and hope for a well-timed skip or reverse that saves you from a late-night rematch.

Phase 10 operates with a different tempo. It’s a progression game where players aim to complete a specific phase in sequence. Each phase demands a different combination of cards, such as a set of three or a run of seven. You complete a phase, advance, and the game moves toward the final phase with a mix of luck and required strategic choices about when to push for high-risk plays. The dual set in this Mattel package makes your game nights flexible: you can opt for UNO whenever you want quick chaotic fun, or dive into Phase 10 for the longer, more strategic grind that tests your ability to adapt mid-game.

The Phase 10 and UNO duo is most enjoyable when you adopt a few house rules that suit your crew. Here are some ideas:
