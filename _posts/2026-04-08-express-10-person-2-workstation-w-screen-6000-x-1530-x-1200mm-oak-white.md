## Practicality for a 10 person setup

### Layouts and ergonomics
A 10-person workstation is not a casual dining arrangement. It is more like a small think-tank where ideas crash into each other like enthusiastic pigeons. The Express offers a couple of layout options: a straight long bench that allows for quick roll-calls, or a two-cluster arrangement that helps teams own their own microcosm of chaos. Ergonomics wise the desk includes height adjustability and a central spine that helps you pretend you are a pro at posture while you are actually mastering the art of slouching with grace. The seating alignment matters as much as the desk surface; you want to create enough elbow room so that no one escapes with a badly timed elbow jab during a sprint planning meeting.

### Cable management
The cable raceways are the unsung heroes of any desk arrangement. Your power bricks will stay out of sight and off the floor, which is important if you want to avoid creating a new sculpture called the Tripping Hazard 3000. The central grommet array helps you pretend you organized your life while the real goal is to route the GPU power supply into a galaxy far, far away. A small tip: label every cable with color-coded tags so your future self will not scream at you when you realize you plugged the HDMI into a USB-C port and now your display is a black magic show.

## Assembly and maintenance

### Unboxing
Opening a 6 meter long beast of a desk is an event. You will likely find more hex keys than you thought existed and a set of Allen wrenches that seems to have its own gravitational pull. Plan a day where your dog is not around to claim the hex tool as a chew toy. The manual is helpful but not a stand-up routine; it will show you pictures of each step and the approximate torque values. Expect to sweat, curse softly, and then realize you own a tool belt by the end.

### Maintenance tips
Maintenance is all about keeping the oak grain looking fresh and the cables under control. Here are a few tips:
- Dust weekly with a dry microfiber cloth to avoid any residue that could attract more hair or dust bunnies.
- Use a mild wood conditioner every few months; your desk deserves spa day too.
- Check the mounting brackets every quarter to ensure there is no unwanted drift in the screen alignment.
- Avoid direct sunlight on the oak surface to prevent warping and existential dread about your desk aging faster than you do.
