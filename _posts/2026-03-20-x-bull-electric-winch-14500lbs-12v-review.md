Safety and Maintenance
- Always wear gloves when handling synthetic rope; the fibers can snag and hurt your palms, even if the rope itself is easy to handle.
- Use a winch line dampener in pulling scenarios to reduce the risk of ricochet in case of a sudden failure.
- Inspect the rope for frays and kinks prior to each trip and coil it properly after use to avoid tangles that will test your patience.
- Confirm your electrical system can handle the load; a 12V system may require a dedicated battery or dual battery setup for long, heavy pulls.
- Use a proper anchor point and avoid edgy obstacles that could cut the rope or damage your rig in mid-recovery.
