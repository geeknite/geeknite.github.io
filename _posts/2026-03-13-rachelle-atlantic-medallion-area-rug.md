## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Longevity
### Everyday care and stain resistance
With a polypropylene base, the rug tends to resist common stains—oil, wine, and the occasional dye transfer from a bevy of throw pillows. Spills should be blotted promptly with a clean cloth; avoid vigorous rubbing that can push the spill deeper into the fibers. For more stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution and a gentle scrub can do the trick. It is not a self-cleaning miracle; you will still need a vacuum and a bit of elbow grease. The rug responds well to routine maintenance, and with a good underpad, it stays looking fresh for years.

### Cleaning tips and tricks
- Vacuum regularly with a brush-less head or a gentle setting to avoid fiber distortion.
- Rotate every few months to prevent obvious wear patterns in high-traffic zones.
- Address pet accidents quickly; rinse with water and blot, then treat with a mild enzymatic cleaner if needed.
- For quick refreshes, sunlight can help, but avoid prolonged exposure that could fade the color.

### Longevity and value
Durability hinges on foot traffic and how well you rotate and cushion the rug. The medallion pattern’s bold lines are good at hiding light foot traffic marks, but deeper indentations can occur if you place heavy furniture directly on top without furniture coasters. With a quality underpad, many households report years of good service, which translates into a solid value proposition given the rug’s price point in the market. If your interior budget is tight, a rug in this category can provide a high-end look without the wallet erosion associated with pure wool or hand-knotted pieces.

## Size, Room Fit, and Styling Tips
### Common sizes and what they mean
Rug sizes commonly range from 5x8 to 9x12 feet. The Rachelle Atlantic Medallion often appears in 5x8, 8x10, and 9x12 configurations, among others. The rule of thumb here is: the bigger the room, the larger the rug you should choose to anchor the seating area. In small rooms, a 5x8 can work when paired with streamlined furniture; in larger spaces, an 8x10 or 9x12 helps define zones and reduces the feeling of floating furniture.

### Layering and room ideas
Layering a neutral runner or smaller coffee rug under a larger central rug can create a dynamic and cozy vibe. The Atlantic Medallion’s pattern scales nicely with room size, but ensure the surrounding furniture doesn’t compete with the medallion’s centrality. If your interior design leans traditional, this rug will harmonize with mahogany furniture and heavy drapery; if your vibe skews coastal, it will echo the sea-inspired palette without shouting.
