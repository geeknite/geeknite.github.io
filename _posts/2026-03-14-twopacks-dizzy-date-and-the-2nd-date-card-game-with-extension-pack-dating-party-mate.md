## What you get in the box
The Dizzy Date base set comes with a stack of glossy action cards, a few quirky character cards, a scoring pad, and a tiny timer that runs on the principle of optimistic adulthood. The art style leans toward playful caricature rather than high art, which is exactly what you want when the goal is to coax laughter out of a group rather than drape the room in velvet seriousness.

The 2nd Date Card Game adds a new deck of prompts that skew slightly more risqué, with a dash more dating into the spectrum of goofy scenarios. If the first game leans into harmless crush moments and mini challenges, the second date leans into the dramatic pauses that accompany dating in real life — except now you get to bluff your way through them with a straight face and a silly hat.

The Dating Party Mate extension packs in extra player cards, more event prompts, and a few wild cards that force players to rotate actions or swap hands. It is designed for larger groups and late night energy, where the only thing you want more of than snacks is chaos delivered with a smile. The build quality across the set remains consistent: sturdy cards, smooth edges, and colorblind friendly iconography that helps you keep track of who is winning without needing a PhD in card math.

## How to play: core mechanics and flow
### Setup
- Each player gets a player pad or a small score tracker and a handful of action cards from the Dizzy Date deck. The 2nd Date deck is shuffled separately and kept within reach of all players.
- The Dating Party Mate extension requires a few extra cards that live in a separate zone and a pair of player aids that remind you of the new rules. If you are playing with a big crew, a timer helps keep rounds snappy.
- Decide a first dater and pass the first hand around clockwise. The turn order determines who picks the prompt and who issues the challenge. It is basically a social scavenger hunt with a deck of cards that amplifies the awkwardness just enough to be charming rather than cringey.

### Core loop
On your turn you draw a card from the Dizzy Date deck and play a compatible action card to set the tone of the date. The prompts revolve around scenarios such as mini challenges, silly dares, or truth style questions. The goal is to accumulate points by completing prompts, making others laugh, and avoiding penalties from the ever present dating satire cards.

The 2nd Date deck introduces prompts that are slightly more involved. They might require players to act out a quick scene, describe a dream date in three sentences, or perform a light improv routine that others rate. Scoring tends to reward creativity, confidence, and willingness to embarrass yourself for the sake of a good time.

The Dating Party Mate extension adds a party dynamic. There are event cards that trigger group activities, like all players swapping seats, performing a quick team challenge, or revealing a hidden card that changes the scoring for everyone. The extension is designed to scale up as crowds grow, turning a cozy game night into a playful party where new alliances form and rivalries emerge with equal enthusiasm.

## Setup tips for different crowd sizes
### Small groups (3 to 4)
Keep the base decks in play with careful moderation on the extension. Encourage everyone to keep comments light and fun. Use the timer to prevent any single person from dominating the conversation.

### Medium groups (5 to 7)
Introduce the Dating Party Mate extension with a rotating event card every few rounds. This structure creates momentum and gives late arrivals something to latch onto as they join the table. Score distributed slightly looser to reflect the increased noise level and chaotic charm.

### Large groups (8 and up)
This is where the extension shines. Split into two teams for group challenges, rotate the deck between teams, and use the event cards to shuffle the vibe. The goal remains to have a good time, not to crown the ultimate dating ringmaster. If chaos goes off the rails, lean into it and declare a new winner by the end of the night — the one who brought the most laughter wins.
