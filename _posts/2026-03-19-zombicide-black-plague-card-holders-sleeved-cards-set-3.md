## Sleeved cards: compatibility and caveats

### Sleeve thickness and fit

A critical factor for anyone considering these holders is sleeve thickness. Sleeve thickness can vary from around 0.3 to 0.6 mm depending on brand and style. These holders are generally friendly to standard 2.0–2.5 mm thick sleeves. If you’re using thicker sleeves or a non-standard deck, you should perform a quick check by sliding a single sleeved card into a holder before committing to a full night of gaming. The worst-case scenario is a momentary pause to adjust one deck, which—let’s be honest—could be a perfect dramatic beat for your table’s story arc.

### Card type considerations

Zombicide’s cards aren’t merely flat rectangles; some are paler, some are a bit glossy, and others have a slightly textured finish. The holders are sized for typical playing-card dimensions common to mainstream sleeved decks. If you’re experimenting with oversized promo cards or nonstandard game components, you’ll want to confirm fit first. It’s not the end of the world if they don’t fit perfectly, but you won’t win any “best use of table space” awards that night.

### Durability concerns

The acrylic is reasonably scratch-resistant and easy to wipe clean. You’ll want to avoid magic markers and sharpies near the surface if you’re labeling decks, but dry-erase pens or low-tack labels should be fine. In practice, the biggest durability concern is if the holders are dropped onto a hard surface; the acrylic is resilient, but not unbreakable. Treat them with the same respect you’d give any other glass-like tabletop companion, and they’ll keep looking crisp across many campaigns.
