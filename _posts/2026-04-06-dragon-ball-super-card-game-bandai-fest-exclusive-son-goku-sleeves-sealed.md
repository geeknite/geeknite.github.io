## Practical Considerations for Play and Display
Part of owning a Bandai Fest exclusive is the choice between preserving the sealed value and enjoying the sleeves in the open. If your goal is to keep the item sealed, you should invest in a display case or a small locked cabinet to keep the box free from dust and environmental hazards. Temperature and humidity control help preserve the cardboard wrapper and the sleeve's edge finishes. If you decide to open the sealed item to actually sleeve your cards, handle the sleeves with clean hands and avoid exposing them to oils too long. A light wipe with a soft microfiber cloth can help remove any fingerprint residue after you have opened the product. When not in use, keep your deck in a dry place away from sunlight. Fading can occur with prolonged exposure to direct light, especially in bright gaming halls. If your gaming space includes a lot of fluorescent lighting or UV rays, consider rotating your decks and storing sleeves that are not in immediate use to minimize color shift.

A practical optimization is to use a dedicated deck box with a divider that keeps your Bandai Fest sleeves separate from other sleeves that you might own. This helps prevent color bleed or micro-scratches that can occur when sleeves rub against other sleeves in a crowded box. In a pinch, you can also opt for a soft cloth bag or a simple plastic storage solution that keeps the sleeves organized and safe. For fans who display their DBSCG collections, a small wall-mounted display case becomes an elegant way to showcase your Bandai Fest exclusive Son Goku without risking physical damage.
