## Maintenance and Cleaning

### Routine care

Regular vacuuming is the best friend of any rug, especially a mid-density runner. Use a vacuum without a heavy beater bar, and keep it on a low setting for a few passes instead of one aggressive pass that could flatten the nap. If you have pets, a quick weekly run to expel fur is gold. Spot clean with a mild detergent and water solution, blot rather than rub to avoid pushing stains deeper into the fibers. Immediate attention to spills is a good habit since stains love to claim rooms with poor lighting and caffeinated afternoons.

### Deep cleaning and rotations

Every few months consider a deeper clean. A professional rug cleaning service can restore luster and remove ground-in grime that a vacuum won’t touch. Rotating the rug about 180 degrees can also help distribute wear more evenly if your hallway sees more foot traffic on one side. If you’re into the science of wear patterns, this is your lab experiment: observe how the nap responds after rotation and whether the color remains vibrant across the width and length.

### Stain management and the back stain promise

If the marketing says back stain resistance, don’t let that make you complacent about front stains. Treat spills promptly, and consider a fabric protector spray designed for synthetic fibers if your lifestyle includes a lot of beverages, snacks, or accidental glitter explosions from craft nights. The right protection can extend the rug’s life and keep the teal crisp rather than a dull, damp fugitive of color.
