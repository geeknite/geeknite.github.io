### What is a foil in DBS TCG
In Dragon Ball Super Card Game foil cards go through a foil process that adds a shiny layer to the card front. The result is a surface that catches the light differently than the standard card, often with a rainbow-like sheen or a mirrored finish on the holo. Foils can be rarer than nonfoils in practice because printing can be uneven, and foil allocations sometimes skip batches that would be perceived as overproduced. The upshot is that foil cards are more visually striking, more collectible for some players, and sometimes a little fussy to store because of glare.

### Protecting and storing your foil bounty
Foils deserve better than luck. The best practice is to sleeve each foil card in a high clarity sleeve with a soft inner sleeve to prevent sleeve-by-sleeve rubbing. For transport, a rigid card protector may help. For long term storage, consider a binder with anti glare sheets or a deck box with foam to avoid bending. Temperature and humidity matter, so avoid damp basements and heat-suspect trunks. If you plan to flip some cards, maintain separate inventory to keep track of which foils go where, and consider marking duplicates so you do not accidentally trade away a prized foil by mistake.
