### Grading and Condition Considerations
Grading is the quiet, serious part of card collecting where your dreams meet a white-gloved reality. For this card, you want to look at the following:
- Centering: Banding around the portrait should be balanced; big off-centers can ding a grade, especially for SPs where precision matters.
- Foil integrity: The Wild Foil Flux should be even and free of major scratches or coating issues. Some minor handling marks are expected on low-grade specimens, but severe foil delamination can crush value.
- Corners and edges: Sharp corners with minimal nicks will help a lot. The risk here is that foil can amplify micro-damage, so a careful inspection under good lighting pays off.
- Surface: Look for print dots, mis-registration, or color bleed. Elite Core is premium, and anything that disrupts the surface harmony can affect a grade.

If you’re a first-time grader, consider sending a sample to a reputable service. Even if you don’t chase a top-tier grade, you’ll learn a lot about how your own collection behaves under scrutiny, and you’ll gain confidence in spotting the subtle cues that separate a desirable specimen from a dream that’s still in the concept phase.
