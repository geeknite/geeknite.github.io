## Quick-start guide for new players
If you’re introducing UNO to a group that hasn’t explored the 75th Anniversary edition yet, here is a crisp quick-start guide:

1) Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player. Place the rest of the deck face down to form the draw pile and flip the top card to start the discard pile.
2) The player to the left of the dealer goes first. On your turn, you may play a matching card by color or number, or play a Wild card. In this edition, you may encounter special anniversary variants that could alter draws or skips, so read the quick-start sheet to know which ones apply.
3) If you have no playable card, you must draw from the draw pile until you find one that you can play. If you draw a card that can be played immediately, you may do so in the same turn.
4) When you have one card left, you must shout UNO — or risk a playful penalty if someone catches you before your next turn. The penalty typically involves drawing an extra card, which can tilt the balance in a close race.
5) The first player to discard all their cards wins the round. The rest of the players count up their points according to card values, following the standard UNO scoring scheme or your preferred variant.

Remember, the new twists are optional. If your table prefers classic UNO, you can disable certain effects with a quick pre-game agreement and treat the anniversary edition as a premium variant deck rather than a completely different game.
