## Safety, Maintenance, and Longevity: Keeping It In The Family

A winch is a precision tool that benefits from routine care. Here’s a practical maintenance checklist that will help this unit stay relevant for more than a couple of trips:

- Regular inspection: Before every voyage, visually inspect the synthetic rope for frays, cuts, or signs of wear. A hidden kink can become a hazard when loaded. If you see damage, replace the rope promptly.
- Terminal and connections: Clean and tighten electrical connections. Corrosion or loose grounds can cause voltage fluctuations and unpredictable performance.
- Lubrication: If your unit features a gear train with serviceable lubrication, follow the manufacturer’s schedule. In dusty environments, a light wipe‑down can prevent gritty wear.
- Safe operation: Stand clear of the pull path, use a tree trunk protector to distribute the load, and avoid letting companions stand directly in line with the rope while under tension.
- Storage: When not in use, store in a dry place away from extreme temperatures. This minimizes corrosion and electronic fatigue over time.
