## What is Azul: Stained Glass of Sintra?

Azul: Stained Glass of Sintra is a standalone sequel to the original **Azul**. This time, players compete to create the most beautiful stained glass windows for the Palace of Sintra. With its gorgeous components and strategic gameplay, it's no wonder Azul: Stained Glass of Sintra has become a favorite among board game enthusiasts.

## Components

The game comes with:
- **Glass Panels**: Beautifully translucent tiles that look good enough to eat (please don’t).
- **Player Boards**: Each player gets their own board representing their window frame.
- **Pattern Strips**: Used to create the stained glass patterns on your window.
- **Glazier Pawns**: Because you need a fancy pawn to show off your craftsmanship.
- **Score Markers**: For keeping track of who's winning—or who's losing spectacularly.

**Pro Tip**: Avoid dropping the tiles on hard floors. Trust me, it's less fun than stepping on LEGO bricks.

## How to Play

### Setup

Setting up Azul: Stained Glass of Sintra is like preparing for an artistic battle. Lay out the glass panels, distribute player boards, and place the pattern strips. Now you're ready to create your masterpiece—or ruin your friend’s chances of winning.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, players take glass panels from the central board and place them on their pattern strips. You can use your glazier pawn to choose which strip to fill. The goal is to complete patterns, score points, and achieve artistic glory. Be mindful of your opponents—they're probably plotting to swipe the tiles you need.

### Scoring

Scoring in Azul: Stained Glass of Sintra involves completing pattern strips and creating beautiful windows. Bonus points are awarded for additional completed strips and for finishing rows of patterns. The victory goes to the player with the most points—so strategize wisely!

## The Glassy Challenge

As the game progresses, players must balance completing patterns with achieving high scores. The stained glass challenge mechanic requires strategic thinking, careful planning, and a touch of luck. Expect a few “Oh no, I needed that tile!” moments.

**Criticism Alert**: While the glass panel tiles are stunning, they can be slippery to handle, adding an unintended dexterity challenge. Beware of the accidental tile avalanche.
