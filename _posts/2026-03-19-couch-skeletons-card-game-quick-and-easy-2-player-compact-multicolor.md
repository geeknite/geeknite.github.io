How to Play: The Core Loop

Couch Skeletons is marketed as a two-player quick play game, and the core loop reflects that promise. The basic cycle goes something like this:

- Draw Phase: On your turn, you draw a card from the deck. The deck is not oversized, which means you will see familiar cards in short order.
- Action Phase: You play a card from hand to your side of the table, attempting to match color categories, skeleton set IDs, or a combination that triggers a scoring event. The exact scoring logic is a compact dance of risk and reward, but the gist is that you want to align your cards with your own goals while subtly thwarting your opponent.
- End Phase: You discard down to a hand limit if necessary and pass the turn. The round ends when a specific scoring condition is met (usually when a set is completed or a certain number of points has accrued).

This is the kind of loop that scales gracefully. In two-player mode, small decisions carry a lot of weight because there are fewer cards in circulation and fewer opponents to react to your every move. The game rewards pattern recognition and timing more than brute force, which is a delight if you enjoy thinking a couple of steps ahead while still keeping the mood light and snappy.
