## Design and installation experience
Let’s be real: you’re not going to build a spaceship in your kitchen to install this sticker. You’re going to peel a sticker off, line it up, and press gently. Here’s a simple guide to getting the most out of the experience without turning your credit card into a slippery banana peel.

### Compatibility and sizing
- Works best on standard 3.375 x 2.125 inch cards (the common credit card size in many countries).
- It may not perfectly fit oversized or custom business cards; if you’re using a transit pass with a thick protective sticker, you might have to trim.
- If your card has a metallic stripe or unusual curvature at the edges, you might want to test a small corner first.

### Step-by-step installation
1) Clean the surface: Use a microfiber cloth with a gentle cleaner to remove oils, dust, or the existential dread left by last month’s coffee run.
2) Dry and align: Place the sticker over the front of the card, aligning the logo and numbers so nothing looks off-center. If you’re a perfectionist like me, you’ll hold your breath for two seconds while you align.
3) Peel and apply: Peel the backing slowly and press from the center outward to avoid air bubbles.
4) Smoothing: Use a soft card to push out any air bubbles toward the edge. If you see a bubble that won’t budge, peel and reapply—this is not a patchwork quilt.
5) Set and wait: Let the sticker cure for a few hours before exposing to extreme heat or cold — or a future duel with a vending machine.

### Real-world fit and feel
The sticker has a slightly tacky feel when you first peel it; after a few days it rests into the card’s texture. It doesn’t add much bulk because the laminate is thin but sturdy. If your card uses RFID, you’ll be happy to know the adhesive doesn’t interfere with the wireless functionality; the sticker sits on the surface and leaves the chip and antenna accessible as designed by modern card manufacture technique. In practice, you’ll still be able to tap-to-pay, but your card’s new look will ensure you stand out in a crowd of standard-issue plastic.

## Durability, removal, and care
Durability depends on your life choices: if you’re a gym enthusiast, you’ll wear through skins faster; if you’re mostly desk-bound and drink coffee in a home office, you’ll be fine.

- Adhesive quality: mid-grade acrylics that resist moisture and typical office humidity. It’s not a permanent tattoo; it’s a temporary, removable sticker with decent staying power.
- Longevity: expect about 3–12 months of daily use depending on how often you slide, scrub, and fumble with your wallet. If you’re a quirky collector who rotates cards every other day, you’ll stretch that drastically.

## Practical considerations and safety
We’re talking about a skin for your card, not a superhero cape. The sticker is designed to be skin-safe and non-conductive. It doesn’t alter magnetic stripe data or the embedded chip (we hope). Still, there are a few caveats:

- If your card shoes a thick or raised embossing, you may have slight alignment issues near the edges. Trim carefully if needed.
- Some readers might struggle with very worn or damaged edge stickers. If your card edge is rounded or chipped, you may want to avoid using a peel-and-stick on that edge to prevent peeling later.
- There’s a non-zero chance a cashier with a suspicious glance might ask questions about your card’s design. Be prepared with a witty retort such as, It’s a vintage UNO card with a modern twist. Yes I realize it’s not the game’s currency.
