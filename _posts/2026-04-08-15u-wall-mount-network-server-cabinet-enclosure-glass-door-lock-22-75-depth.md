## Installation guide and tips
Installing a compact wall mount cabinet is a project that rewards planning. Here are practical steps distilled from field tests and user chatter:

- Prepare the wall: Locate studs or a solid backing. If you aren’t sure, use a stud finder and confirm with a test drill before committing the mount points.
- Plan device layout: Sketch a rough plan of where the switches, NAS, patch panels, and any uplink devices will sit. This helps keep cables organized and reduces rework later.
- Door orientation: Some cabinets allow the door to be mounted left or right hinged. Decide early which direction makes the most sense for access in your space.
- Cable management: Install vertical cable management rails if available. They dramatically improve the tidiness of the back area and reduce tangles when you add more devices later.
- Airflow planning: If you have perforated panels or ventilated options, consider enabling them to improve airflow. The last thing you want is a hot rack in a small room that doubles as a kitchen.

### Step by step quick setup
1) Mount the cabinet on the wall using the included hardware and your wall anchors after verifying wall strength. 2) Install vertical rails and align the rails for 19 inch gear. 3) Mount devices in the order that minimizes cable length and maximizes airflow. 4) Route cables with ties and add cable management where possible. 5) Attach the door and verify that the lock operates smoothly. 6) Power on each device and verify LED indicators, then tidy cables with a final pass of cable management.
