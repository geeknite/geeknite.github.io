## How Exploding Kittens Original works: a quick gameplay refresher

If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, you’re about to enter a world where the rules are simple but the stakes are hilariously high. The game is essentially a high-stakes draw-and-counter mechanism wrapped in a cat-themed comedy routine.

- Each player starts with a handful of cards, including at least one Defuse card. Defuse cards are your safety net, the legal counterspell to an imminent catastrophe.
- On your turn, you draw a card. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re almost certainly out—unless you have a Defuse card. The Defuse lets you neutralize the Kitten and place it back into the deck, ideally in a spot that will terrify your friends in future rounds.
- The rest are various action cards: Attack, Skip, See the Future, Shuffle, and so on. Each one offers a strategic nudge or a mischievous twist that makes your opponents regret their last move.

The pace is brisk. Rounds are short, and the chaos clock is always ticking. It’s the kind of game where a well-timed See the Future can turn continents (or at least a couple of players) against the lead, which is exactly the kind of drama you want when your friends insist on “friendly” game nights.
