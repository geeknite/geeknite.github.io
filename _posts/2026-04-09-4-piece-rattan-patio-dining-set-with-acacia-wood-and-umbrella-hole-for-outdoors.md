## Build Quality and Weather Resistance
Outdoor furniture has two jobs: look good and survive the outdoors. A well-made rattan set with an acacia core should be able to handle seasonal shifts, UV exposure, and occasional rain showers. Here’s what to look for:

- Coatings and finishes: A weather-resistant finish on the ACACIA wood helps resist water staining and warping. The rattan weave should be tightly woven and coated to repel moisture rather than absorb it.
- Joints and fasteners: Screws and bolts should be corrosion-resistant (galvanized or stainless steel). Check for loose screws after the first few uses and re-tighten as needed.
- Cushion fabrics: If cushions are included, ensure they’re water-repellent and have UV-resistant fabric. If not, consider covers or replacement cushions with moisture-wicking capabilities.

Quick tip: store cushions indoors during heavy rain or winter; even the best outdoor fabrics will not age well if constantly soaking. Use breathable storage bags if you don’t have a dedicated patio cabinet.

External considerations: a shaded, windless corner is a friend to furniture; a windy patio might benefit from corner anchoring or a lightweight furniture anchor kit to keep things in place.

## Practicality and Setup
### Assembly Expectations
Most 4-piece sets are modular and designed for straightforward assembly. Expect a few Allen wrenches, some screws, and a half-hour to assemble per chair—be honest about your DIY skill level and factor in a little patience. If the brand ships pre-assembled components, you’re lucky; if not, a bottle of cold brew and a friend who owes you a favor might speed things up.

### Maintenance Routine
Maintenance is where you can extend the life of a rattan/acacia set by years. A quick weekly wipe-down with a mild soap solution keeps the weave fresh. For the wood: a periodic coat of outdoor-grade sealant can maintain the warm glow and prevent minor cracking. If you live in a damp climate, dry the underside of chairs and table after rains to prevent moisture buildup.

## Maintenance Tips for Longevity
- Clean quarterly with a mild soap solution and rinse with clean water to prevent residue buildup.
- Treat the acacia wood annually with a dedicated outdoor sealer or oil to maintain color and prevent cracking.
- Use cushions with mildew-resistant fabrics; store cushions indoors during heavy rain or winter months.
- Protect the furniture from extreme weather by using covers or moving pieces into a sheltered area when possible.
- Inspect joints for wobble and tighten hardware as needed every few months.
