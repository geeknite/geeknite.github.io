## How to play: base rules and the flip twist
UNO Flip GDR44 sticks to the core UNO framework but injects a dramatic twist with its double-sided deck. Here are the essentials to get you playing fast:

- Setup: Each player starts with 7 cards. The rest of the deck goes in the center, with the top card flipped to begin the discard pile on the light side.
- Objective: Be the first to discard all your cards. Be careful: if you forget to say UNO when you’re down to one card, you’ll draw two as a penalty (because we all forget, even the gods of card luck).
- Turn flow: On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the card on the discard pile. If you can’t play, you draw one card. If that card can be played, you may put it down immediately (otherwise your turn ends).

- Flip mechanic: The real excitement happens when someone plays the Flip card (on either side). When you flip from light to dark, the entire gameplay switches tone. All players must adapt to new color schemes and action cards that apply to the dark side.

- Dark side rules: Once on the dark side, the action cards change the pace. The Dark Side introduces Draw Five, Wild, Wild Draw Five, Skip, and Reverse. The dark cards can change the color and cause more punishment for the next players. In practical terms: expect a swing in who’s in the lead and who’s about to draw a small ocean of cards.

- Endgame on both sides: When a player runs out of cards on either side, the round ends, and points are tallied if you’re playing a scoring variant. UNO Flip does allow house rules for free-play rounds where you just play for bragging rights.

A couple of practical tips: keep an eye on who’s hoarding the wild cards on the dark side, and don’t get too attached to a single strategy. In UNO Flip, the board can flip on you just when you think you’ve got the lead, which is both the charm and the chaos of the game.

## Strategy: how to win (or at least not be last)
UNO Flip is less about pure math and more about psychology, memory, and timing. Here are a few tips that actually work in practice:

- When to flip: The flip card is a resource you should treat like a fire extinguisher—only pull it out when the situation is truly ablaze. Flipping when someone is about to go out can be the difference between a clean victory and a dramatic, "I was this close" defeat.
- Manage the dark side: The dark side’s Draw Five and Wild Draw Five cards are your FOMO boosters. Use them to shuffle the table when you’re behind, but don’t overcommit; you’ll find yourself the target of a coordinated revenge move in round two.
- Color discipline: Memorize color distribution in your opponents’ hands as game progresses. It helps you anticipate which players are likely to hold wild cards on the dark side, which can guide your own plays.
- Card counting (in a friendly, not-too-nerdy way): Track what color cards have been played. This becomes more relevant as rounds tick by and the deck narrows. If you see a color shortage, you can push for a flip to a color you can control.
- Etiquette and timing: UNO is a social game with quick forgiveness. Don’t be That Player who delays “UNO!” too long; instead, blend humor with suspense—nudge your friends with a goofy catchphrase while slamming down a card.
