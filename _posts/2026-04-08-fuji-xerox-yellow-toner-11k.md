## Storage, Handling, and Longevity

Toner isn’t just a deliverable; it’s a long-term relationship. Proper storage preserves the cartridge’s performance and avoids headaches at install time. Here are best practices:

- Store in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight. The yellow pigment doesn’t appreciate a sauna.
- Don’t shake the cartridge aggressively; a gentle side-to-side tilt is enough to break any clumps if you’re worried about storage stability.
- Use the cartridge within a reasonable shelf life (manufacturers typically list a recommended “best before” date on the box). We’re talking about months, not years.
- Before installation, inspect the cartridge for any visible damage or loose packaging seals. If you see any signs of damage, contact support rather than risking a bad print job.
