## Display, Care, and Handling101

Caring for a high‑gloss, foil‑accent card is less about ritual and more about keeping oils, sunlight, and heavy magnets away from the finish. Here are practical tips that won’t turn your hobby into a full‑time job:

- Handling: If you’re shooting photos or preparing for grading submissions, wash your hands or use gloves. Oils from skin can accumulate over time, subtly dulling the gloss and inviting micro‑abrasions.
- Storage: Use 4‑mil to 6‑mil top loaders with penny sleeves. If you’re serious about display, UV‑resistant glass or acrylic cases help preserve color and avoid sunlight bleaching of silver foils.
- Cleaning: Microfiber only. Avoid rough cloths or detergents that could scratch the surface. A gentle, dry wipe is enough to remove dust without micro‑abrading the finish.
- Grading submission: If you submit for grading, ensure cards are protected from corner stress and edge chipping. A corner protector can be a quiet hero in your submission pipeline, preserving condition during transit and handling.

In a way, display is storytelling. The Eric Roy piece isn’t just a single data point; it’s a narrative device in a shelf‑top conversation about the era, the product line, and the way collectors sought something a little shiny to anchor a larger collection.
