### How to decide for your space
A quick design check can help you decide whether to opt for 5x7 or a custom size. Consider the following routine:
- Measure the room: note the positions of sofas, coffee tables, and the cat tree. A rug that spans under the front legs of your sofa will pull the room together far more gracefully than a rug that leaves the furniture with its own leftover stage.
- Visualize flow: ensure there is at least a foot of clearance around the rug for movement. In a high traffic zone, you might want a slightly smaller rug with a higher pile for comfortable positioning of feet and paws alike.
- Consider the cat's point of view: cats demand space to strut, rub, and claim as their own. If you have multiple cats, a larger custom size might be the better choice to accommodate multiple diva paws and curious whiskers.

## Durability, Maintenance, and Longevity
If you are the type who treats your decor like a good RPG character, durability is your stat priority. The Cat Eyes rug is built to hold up across seasons, daily use, and the occasional accidental spill, which is a polite way to say that life will occur. Here are practical maintenance tips to keep it in top form:

- Vacuum regularly: a gentle vacuum helps remove dust and keeps fibers looking crisp. Do not use aggressive cleaners that can loosen the wool or distort the pattern.
- Address stains promptly: dab, don’t rub, to prevent color spreading. For wine or coffee, a mild wool-safe cleaner can be used with care. Always test a small hidden area first.
- Rotate periodically: turning the rug every few months can help even out wear, especially in high traffic zones and in front of the cat tree where paw traffic is inevitable.
- Sun exposure: avoid prolonged direct sunlight on the rug, which can cause color fading over time. If sunlight is unavoidable, rotate the rug occasionally to even out exposure.

### Longevity expectations
A well cared for wool rug should maintain its structure and color for many years. The premium Wool and cat friendly motif means you’re investing less in replacement and more in a living piece that grows with your space.
