### How the game plays on a turn-by-turn basis

Saboteur is a game of hidden roles, where you want to reach the treasure, but your fellow miners might want to derail you first. In the Deluxe Version, the turn structure remains familiar: players play cards to extend paths toward the treasure and to install or sabotage, claim to be miners, or claim to be saboteurs. The new cards introduce slightly different interactions that require you to adapt your strategy on the fly.

- On your turn you typically do one action: lay a tunnel card, lay a path card, lay a equipment card, or play a special action card.
- The saboteur side is about misdirection and mischief; you’ll rarely find yourself yelling at a map because the map is firmly in your opponent’s hands. More often you’ll yip about how the strategy seems to be going suspiciously well until the last moment when it all unravels.
- The miners’ side is about collaboration, or at least plausible pretend collaboration. The treasure is not a solo victory; it’s a team effort with generous amounts of backstabbing and side-eye.

The game ends when a path reaches the treasure or when the deck runs out. The Deluxe Version specifically shines here because the new cards accelerate or complicate the endgame, depending on how much chaos you like to invite into your life. If you enjoy a climactic scramble where everyone pretends to be a hero while secretly counting gold coins, you will revel in these final moments.

### Strategy basics: who you root for and who you root against

- If you’re a miner, your strategy is to build a path that cannot be sabotaged outright by a single card draw. That means you’ll want to balance risk with the knowledge that your fellow miners might surprise you with a last-minute twist. Be mindful of your allies too, since trust is a currency that sometimes costs more than gold.
- If you’re the saboteur, your objective is to sow doubt and mislead your opponents into chasing perfect but impossible sequences of cards. You are not necessarily trying to steal the treasure outright; you’re trying to poison the well enough to make others falter, bicker, and end the round poorly for themselves.
- In the Deluxe Version, certain new roles and action cards shift the numbers a bit. You’ll find that the most effective strategy often involves a blend of patient plotting and quick-reacting improvisation. The best saboteur is also a good actor; the best miner is a good liar when the situation demands it.

If you enjoy games that reward bluffing, reading the table, and the occasional cunning misdirection, Saboteur Deluxe Version offers a richer sandbox for that chaos than the base game. The new mechanics don’t overshadow the core experience; they bolster it with familiar adrenaline plus fresh surprises that keep players on their toes for multiple rounds.

## How to optimize your first few plays

- Focus on the path: Early in the game, get a sense of which directions tend to be safer and which open up more sabotaging opportunities. This sense will guide your early decisions and reduce the chaos of the late game.
- Read the table, not just the cards: The most entertaining aspect of Saboteur is how players interact with one another. Your ability to read timing, bluffing, and the occasional tell is as important as card management.
