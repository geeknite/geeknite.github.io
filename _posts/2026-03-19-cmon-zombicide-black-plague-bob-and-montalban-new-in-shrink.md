## Who these survivors are and how they play

### Bob and Montalban at a glance

Bob brings a fast and flexible approach to zombie-killing with a mix of mobility and utility. His kit is designed for quick hits, repositioning, and enabling teammates to stage a better defense. Montalban, on the other hand, feels like a robust anchor with a slightly slower but more devastating interaction with the board state. The pairing encourages a dynamic where one hero establishes control lines while the other delivers the finishing blow or the critical rescue during a multi-zombie scrum. If you enjoy heavy crew synergy and a little bit of timing discipline, this duo gives you a fresh palette to experiment with.

### Unique abilities that actually matter in games

Bob features a set of actions that reward smart movement and saving precious action economy for critical turns. Think of him as a chess piece that slides into bleeding edge spots where a spill can either save the campe or doom them all. Montalban is the classic anchor, able to hold a lane long enough for reinforcements to arrive or for a hero to break free from a tight corner. The two of them also come with equipment interactions that feel like they belong in the late game rather than the early skirmish. You will not be overwhelmed by new rules; instead you will be gently reminded that Zombicide is still a game about resource management and the proper use of a spear or a cleaver when the crowd density hits the sweet spot.

### How they interact with base game mechanics

Bob and Montalban do not reinvent the wheel of Zombicide Black Plague. They do offer a few new synonyms for the same old verbs: move, attack, search, and craft. The new equipment cards are a nice touch and one or two of the items give you a new lever to press when the zombie density expands beyond your initial expectations. This is not a radical redevelopment of the game, but it is a gentle nudge toward more interesting turn planning and a better sense of role specialization. If you have played through a couple of campaigns, you will notice how these two add a slightly different feel to early game skirmishes and mid game objectives.

## How I would integrate Bob and Montalban into a campaign

### Early game cadence

In early rounds, place Bob in a location that allows him to ping zone control with minimal risk and have Montalban anchor a choke point to keep the undead from flooding your backline. The two together can create a tiny crossfire corridor that makes zombies think twice before laughing in your face. The net effect is a slightly more forgiving early games, giving your group a little space to breathe while not completely trivializing the first few turns.

### Mid game pivots

As the board densifies, use Bob to reposition and set up optimal rescue lines, while Montalban holds a key position and offers the shield that lets your other survivors perform critical searches or gather gear. This is where the twin strengths shine, creating a sense of momentum rather than a collapse into chaos. The synergy is not about brawling through everything in one go; it is about controlling tempo and engineering safer, more rewarding turns for your crew.

### Endgame opportunities

In late game scenarios, the duo can help you clamp down objective areas and protect fragile survivors as you sprint for the win. There will be moments when you realize you have done enough planning to coax a victory from a board that looked like it had given up. The satisfaction is real, and the laughs you share around the table during these victory laps are a big part of why we play board games in the first place.
