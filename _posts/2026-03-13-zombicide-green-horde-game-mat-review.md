A good mat should stand up to spills, stray marker ink, and the occasional napkin being pressed into service as a temporary bandage for a dented city. ## Maintenance, wear, and longevity
No reviewer should pretend a mat is indestructible. Like your favorite pair of gaming socks, a mat’s value increases with care. Neoprene mats tend to resist wear better than cloth over time, though cloth mats can preserve a neat surface if you’re worried about heat or humidity affecting the texture. The Green Horde mat handles spills well enough; a quick wipe with a damp cloth is usually all that’s needed. If you spill something particularly clingy or dye-heavy, a gentle soap wash and air dry will restore the surface without affecting the grid lines.

Storage matters more than you’d think. The mat should be rolled rather than folded if you want to keep the grid pristine. If you must fold, do so along the long edges to minimize permanent creases that deform your play area. In the long run, proper storage and occasional light cleaning will keep the mat looking sharp for dozens of sessions, which is the kind of durability we all secretly crave when we invest in board game accessories.
