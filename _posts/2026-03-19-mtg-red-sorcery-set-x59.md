## What Counts as a Sorcery in Red?

In Magic the Gathering, bullseye red spells usually revolve around high-impact, tempo-shifting plays that can swing the board on a single draw. Red sorceries tend to push direct damage to the face, pressure the opponent's board, or create moments of mischief that feel both cunning and a little reckless. In this hypothetical x59 set, the designers play with the concept of "sorcery as dramatic turning point," crafting spells that require precise timing to maximize effect. From a mechanical perspective, here are common themes you’ll find in red sorceries, adapted for the 59-card format:

- Direct damage on a scale: The classic burn spell that says hi and also roast some goblins in the middle seat.
- Board disruption with a twist: Effects that force tricky trades, like pinging creatures to force blocks or circumvent blockers in clever ways.
- Temporary resource manipulation: Spells that ramp mana or create temporary advantages that require careful planning to keep up the momentum.
- Chaos and unpredictability: Spells with conditional effects and some that rely on your opponent’s life total as a dial you can twist.

The 59 cards in the set explore a broader palette than you’d expect for red.
