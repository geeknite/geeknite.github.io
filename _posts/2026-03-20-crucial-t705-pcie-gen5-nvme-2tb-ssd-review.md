### Thermal and power considerations

Sustained peak speeds are only as good as your cooling plan. The T705 tends to run pleasantly cool under gaming loads if you have a typical mid tower with decent airflow. In our tests with room temp around 22 C and a modest cooling setup, thermals hovered in the mid 50s Celsius. That is comfortable headroom for long sessions and extensions beyond 60 minutes did not trigger alarming throttling. If you plan to push the drive in a small form factor with limited airflow, a lightweight heat spreader or a small heatsink is worth considering. Crappy thermals will throttle, and throttling is the kryptonite of big numbers.

Power consumption is in line with expectations for Gen5 drives: a higher peak during stream bursts balanced by low idle consumption. If you have a power-efficient system, you will appreciate that the T705 does not swing power vampires in the background while you are idling in the menu. The result is a quick and satisfying experience when you switch from idle to active tasks.

## Installation notes and compatibility tips

- Ensure you have an M.2 slot that supports PCIe Gen5 x4. Check your motherboard manual for lane specs to avoid bottlenecks.
- Back up important data. Even fast drives can fail, and you never want to find out the hard way that you forgot to back up your game saves after a catastrophic crash.
- Update firmware after installation. The Gen5 ecosystem is still maturing, and firmware updates often bring stability improvements and bug fixes that translate into actual performance gains.
- Pair with a fast system memory configuration. Gen5 shines when the rest of the chain can deliver; having adequate RAM and a capable CPU helps avoid CPU-driven bottlenecks that masquerade as slow storage.
