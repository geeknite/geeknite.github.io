## Visual quality and foil technology

Foil patterns vary widely. Some cards present a rainbow blade that shifts across the surface as you tilt the card. ### Card stock and finish

Foil surfaces require a delicate balance between rigidity and flex. Too glossy, and you risk fingerprint magnetism and scuff around the edges during transit. Too matte, and the holographic effect loses oomph. A good holo foil lot will have a card stock that feels sturdy in your grip, with a finish that handles shuffle and light play without showing wear after moderate testing. Expect the print quality to be crisp, with lines and text legible under standard lighting. When you place these next to nonfoil-base cards, the contrast is striking enough to make even your phone's autofocus go a little nuts.

## Handling and storage tips

### Immediate steps after purchase

- Inspect on arrival under strong light to spot obvious edge whitening, corner dings, or scratches
- Separate cards with heavy foil pattern clashes from those that appear cleaner to protect the rest during storage
- Sleeve the cards in clear protective sleeves to prevent fingerprints and scuffs

### Long-term storage strategies

- Use top-loading sleeves or card protectors for binder storage
- Keep away from direct sunlight to avoid foil fading and color shift over time
- Consider a binder with a dark or neutral page background to maximize foil glow without clashing

### Display suggestions

If you want to show off the 25-card glow, set up a small display with a light source angled for maximum refraction.
