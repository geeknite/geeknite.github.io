## Value, Pricing, and Market Reality
Let’s address the elephant in the binder: value. A 16 card lot can be a great investment if you’re patient, lucky, or very good at shopping around. Prices vary widely based on which promos you pull, your local market, and the ever-elusive condition of each card.

### Valuation Mechanics
- Demand: If a promo card was used in a popular build or is easy to obtain elsewhere, its resale value could be muted. If it’s a rarer promo that players were chasing at a particular event, demand can spike.
- Condition: Minty cards that could fool your local collector into thinking they’ve found a mint condition gem will fetch higher prices. A corner crease or edge wear can significantly depress the value, even for otherwise cool cards.
- Completeness: Some buyers prefer a full 16-card lot to a random assortment. The more “complete” a lot feels, the more confident buyers feel about its value.

### Market-Watching Tips
- Check completed sales on major marketplaces to avoid overpaying for cards that are sometimes listed at fantasy values.
- Compare foil variants and art differences. A single card with an alternate art can dramatically affect price if it’s hard to source.
- Keep an eye on upcoming events. New promos can either devalue older promos or increase interest in a particular subset of flavors from Masters sets.

## How to Verify Authenticity and Condition
Authenticity matters. Promo cards sometimes exist in multiple print runs with minor cosmetic differences. Here are quick checks:

- Holograms and foil texture: When applicable, ensure the foil pattern aligns with known promo specifics. Inconsistent foil shine can be a red flag.
- Card stock and finish: Pro-quality promos from events typically use consistent stock; if a card feels unusually thin or sticky, inspect further.
- Text clarity: Look for typographic alignment and font consistency. A misprint could be a sign of a counterfeit, or a misprint, which some collectors actually chase for the novelty.
- Set and card numbers: Verify the set symbol and card number to confirm it belongs in the Masters promo family, not a standard booster.

## Practical Buying Tips for the 16 Card Lot
- Ask for detailed photos: front and back, plus a close-up of any foil or watermark. If the seller provides only a few glances, request more angles.
- Request a video flip-through if possible. A quick video can reveal surface issues that photos hide.
- Confirm return policy: promo cards can be delicate; a seller confident in their description should offer a reasonable return window.
- Decide on your goal: pure collection, tournament viability, or a mix. Your goal shapes how much you’re willing to pay and how much risk you’re willing to take.
