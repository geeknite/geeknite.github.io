Cleaning and maintenance
Spills happen; it’s part of the fun of owning a home with human life in it. The Rachelle Opal Rug handles spilled drinks with poise. A quick blot with a clean cloth to avoid setting the stain is the recommended first move. Minor accidents respond well to a mild detergent and water solution; avoid harsh chemicals and bleach that could alter the fiber color or texture. For stubborn marks, you can use a low-suction vacuum with minimal agitation. A rotating brush can sometimes cause frizz on the fibers, so keep it on the gentle setting if you have that option. The rug dries relatively quickly, thanks to a design that considers common home climates. A good rule of thumb is to give it a thorough vacuum every week if you’re in a busy household, and every two weeks otherwise. This keeps the fibers aligned and the color looking crisp rather than worn and muddy.

Care tips from a nerdy pro
- Vacuum regularly with a low setting to keep the pile from crushing; rotate occasionally to avoid wear patterns.
- Blot, don’t rub, when dealing with spills; you want to raise the liquid, not the fibers’ romance with the stain.
- Use a mild soap solution for sticky residues and test on a small inconspicuous area first to avoid color bleed.
- If you have pets with fur that loves to cling to fleece fibers, a gentle lint roller helps between deep vacuums.
- Pairing the rug with a well-chosen underlay can improve longevity and reduce curling on the edges.
- For deep cleaning, consider a professional service a couple of times a year if your household sees heavy traffic or if you’re hosting a lot of guests who think pizza crust is a garnish.
