## Real-Life Performance: Durability, Foot Traffic, and Use Case Scenarios
### Foot traffic lab report (aka my hallways)
I tested the Kendall rug in two primary environments: a computing corner with a gaming chair and a living room that doubles as a tiny library. In both spaces, the rug absorbed impacts (walks, chair wheels, and a few rogue cat stumbles) without showing obvious wear after several weeks. It wasn’t perfect—wool will always show some foot traffic patterns if you stare at it long enough—but the changes were incremental, not catastrophic. The fibers retained their bounce, and vacuuming kept the pile looking plush rather than frayed.

### Spills and stains: what to expect
Wool’s natural repellency helps, but no fiber is invincible. Soda, coffee, or wine spilled on this rug can be addressed with a blot-and-dab approach. Avoid vigorous rubbing; you’ll push the liquid deeper into the fibers and turn your rug into a confection of stains. The general rule here: blot, don’t rub; use a mild detergent solution if necessary and test on an inconspicuous corner first. Wool fibers tend to hide minor stains well due to the color variation in oatmeal tones, but you’ll still want to treat spills promptly. In general, the rug cleans more easily than you’d fear, especially if you catch stains early.

### Cleaning cadence: how often and how hard
Regular vacuuming with a soft brush attachment is your best friend. Wool fibers resist static, so you won’t feel like you’re vacuuming up a small sarcophagus of dust after every use. Aim for a weekly pass in high-traffic zones and a monthly deeper clean (spot-cleaning plus a gentle wash) for maintenance. If you’ve got pets, you’ll appreciate wool’s resilience even more: hair tends to blend with the color and texture rather than clinging like a cosmic space-entity to a set of rules. If you’re uncertain about full-clean methods, consider consulting a professional cleaner who specializes in wool textiles; you’ll save yourself future anxiety and maybe a few grey hairs from worrying about carpet fate.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity: Keeping Kendall Looking Fresh
### Routine care tips
- Vacuum regularly with a soft brush attachment. 
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear.
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing. 
- For deeper cleaning, use a wool-safe detergent and test on a hidden corner first.

### What to watch for in the long run
Over time, you may notice slight wear patterns in high-traffic strips. This is natural and expected with wool rugs; it adds character rather than erasing it. If edges start curling, you can flatten them with gentle heat and moisture (think mild steam from a garment steamer used from a distance) and then re-press them with a heavy object overnight. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that degrade wool fibers; they won’t turn the rug into a superhero, they’ll turn it into a sad, frayed villain.

### Seasonal care and storage tips
If you’re relocating or putting the rug into storage, clean thoroughly, roll it, and wrap it in breathable fabric. Avoid plastic, which can trap moisture and cause odor or mildew over time. Wool breathes; let it breathe when you can. If you’re switching rooms or seasons frequently, label the rolls so you remember which space got what vibe—this helps you keep your interior design consistent across time like a mini time traveler with a terrific taste in textures.
