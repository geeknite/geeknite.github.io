## Maintenance and Longevity: Making Your Razer Mouse Last

### Cleaning Best Practices

**Weekly Cleaning (5 minutes):**

1. **Exterior Wipe-Down:**
   - **Tool:** Microfiber cloth slightly dampened with water
   - **Method:** Wipe shell, buttons, cable
   - **Avoid:** Harsh chemicals, alcohol (can damage coating)

2. **Sensor Lens Cleaning:**
   - **Tool:** Cotton swab (dry)
   - **Method:** Gently clean optical sensor lens on bottom
   - **Frequency:** Weekly if dusty environment, monthly otherwise

**Monthly Deep Clean (15 minutes):**

1. **Remove PTFE Feet:**
   - **Tool:** Plastic pry tool or guitar pick
   - **Method:** Gently lift feet, clean adhesive residue
   - **Replacement:** Replace if worn (Razer sells replacement feet sets)

2. **Screw Removal (Advanced):**
   - **Caution:** Voids warranty
   - **Method:** Remove screws under feet, open shell
   - **Clean:** Interior dust with compressed air
   - **Reassemble:** Ensure all clips secure before screwing

**Spill Emergency Protocol:**

1. **Immediately Disconnect:** Unplug USB (wired) or remove battery (wireless)
2. **Disassemble:** Remove screws, separate shell
3. **Clean PCB:** Isopropyl alcohol 90%+ on circuit board
4. **Dry Completely:** 24-48 hours air dry before reassembly
5. **Test:** Reconnect, verify all buttons/sensor function
