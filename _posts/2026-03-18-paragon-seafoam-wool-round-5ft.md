## Maintenance and Longevity: Care That Keeps on Giving
### Cleaning Basics
- Regular vacuuming from the top to the bottom hides soil and preserves the nap. Use a vacuum without a beater bar on high-pile or high-density rugs to reduce fiber wear.
- Blot spills immediately with clean, dry towels. Do not rub, as rubbing will push the liquid deeper into the fibers.
- For spot stains, test a mild detergent solution on an inconspicuous area first. Wool is generally forgiving, but some dyes can be reactive.
- For deeper cleaning, consider professional rug cleaning every 12-18 months, depending on traffic.

### Stain Resistance and Dyefastness
Wool has natural resilience that helps it resist staining, but dyefastness can vary between batches. Seafoam dyes can be more sensitive to certain cleaners or solvent-based products. Always perform a spot test and use wool-safe cleaners. If you’re a clingy color-curious person, consider rotating the rug with seasonal décor to keep the color feeling fresh in your space.

### Rug Pads and Underlays
A good rug pad is the silent hero of a living-room setup. It provides grip to prevent sliding, adds a layer of cushioning, and increases longevity by reducing wear on the wool fibers. Look for a non-slip, breathable pad that matches the rug’s diameter. Circular pads are a fair pursuit for this 5-foot round rug, but if you’re going for maximum friction, a thicker pad can help keep everything in place on slippery floors.
