## What the Core Book PDF Is Trying to Do

City of Mist is a game about mood as much as mechanics. The Core Book PDF is designed to codify that mood in a readable, accessible ruleset that won’t demand a law degree to interpret. The document aims to deliver:

- A compact, modular rules system that supports fast action and narrative control without grinding down into minutiae.
- A toolkit of Masks (the mythic identities characters adopt) and archetypes that let players born with a talent for drama jump into play without spending hours building backstory from scratch.
- A city-wide sandbox full of mysteries, factions, and mythic undercurrents, so the GM has ready-to-run content instead of needing to conjure everything ex nihilo.
- A clear separation between the noir detective vibe and the larger mythic undercurrent so sessions can swing between street-level grit and world-shaking revelations without whiplash.

The PDF leans into the dual identity concept—your character is both a person and a Masks persona—by codifying a handful of mechanical levers that feel intuitive once you’ve read a session or two. The design intent is to make it easy to say, “Yes, and then you reveal a new layer of your Mythos” rather than “No, you need a calculator and a complicating chart.” And for the most part, it does that well.

## Systems and Mechanics: A Quick Dive (Without Getting Too Mechanical)

City of Mist uses a rules-light approach that emphasizes storytelling over stat-heavy combat. The Core Book PDF carefully layers the mechanics so that players can lean into characterization and dramatic beats without feeling like they’re solving equations. Here are the core ideas you’ll encounter:

- Masks and Powers: Characters hold Masks—mythic identities that grant them access to a suite of Powers. These Powers are the engine for action, enabling all kinds of cinematic moments: a single reveal, a dramatic escape, or a power move that redefines the scene.
- Clues and Consequences: Clues drive the mystery forward, while the consequences of actions ripple through the city and the party’s shared narrative. The PDF presents streamlined prompts for the GM to seed the mystery and keep it moving.
- Stunts and Scenes: Action economy and narrative pacing are structured around Scenes. Scenes are where you test the limits of a Mask, roll dice, and decide how the mythic layer interacts with the street-level reality.
- Experience and Growth: Characters grow by embracing new Aspects of their Mask or unlocking new Powers—progression is tied to story outcomes as much as it is to XP tallies. This keeps advancement tied to narrative moments rather than monotonous grind.

Where the PDF truly shines is in how it makes these mechanics feel modular. You can lift a Scene idea from one page, bolt on a new Mask from another, and the rules stay coherent without requiring a full parse of the book every time you want to mix things up. The Core Book PDF does a commendable job of giving you a broad swath of Masks to play with, from classic PI vibes to mythic outsiders who have always felt out of sync with the city’s rhythm.

- Archetypes provide a quick-start ladder for players who want to jump in fast. Each Archetype embodies a different flavor—detective, mystic, trickster, survivor—so you can start at a narrative height you’re comfortable with.
- Power synergies let you build combos that feel thematic rather than arbitrary. The PDF guides you to think about how a Mask’s Power interacts with the city’s mythic layer, encouraging scenes where the mundane and the magical collide in surprising ways.
- Character creation is approachable. You don’t need to fill pages of backstory to begin; you draft a clean premise, pick a Mask, outline a few beats for your initial arc, and you’re ready to roll. The Core Book PDF rewards those who lean into the reveal, not those who try to micromanage every metric in the book.

## Practical GMing: Running the PDF at the Table

Here are some practical tips to maximize the PDF’s value in real sessions:

- Start with a strong opening Scene that foregrounds a detective problem and a mythic twist. The Core Book PDF provides you with hooks and a sense of mood; your first scene is your chance to claim it.
- Use Masks as both character identity and narrative leverage. Invite players to reveal how their Mask’s weakness or vulnerability influences the investigation. The more you lean into the Masks, the more you’ll unlock narrative momentum.
- Keep the city reactive. The book’s design assumes the world responds to the party’s actions. If you make every clue read as a one-way street toward the big reveal, you’ll lose the texture; instead, let the city push back with consequences that feel organic and unpredictable.
- Don’t fear downtime. The noir vibe isn’t all chase scenes; it’s about ambience, conversations in rain-slick alleys, and the slow reveal of the city’s secrets. Use these moments to deepen character and mood.
- Use the pre-built mysteries as a safety net. If your group is new, you’ll appreciate the built-in arcs; if you’re a veteran GM, you’ll savor the way you can remix them into something new with only light adjustment.
