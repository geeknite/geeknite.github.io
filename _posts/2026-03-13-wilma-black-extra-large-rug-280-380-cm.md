Maintenance and Care: Cleaning, Stains, and Longevity
-----------------------------------------------------

Maintenance is not a dirty four-letter word, even if vacuuming occasionally feels like cardio. Wilma’s pile responds well to regular vacuuming with a beater bar or a setting that’s not too aggressive. If you have a high-shedding pet or a messy eater in the household, you’ll want to schedule more frequent cleanups to prevent debris buildup. The black color helps disguise minor dust and lint between cleans, but this does not give you a free pass to neglect maintenance.

Cleaning Tips:
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid crushing the pile.
- Address spills quickly with a blot-and-dab method rather than rubbing, which can embed stains deeper.
- For larger stains, consider a mild carpet cleaner or a professional service if necessary. Always test a small inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate the rug occasionally if you’re concerned about uneven wear or fading from sunlight.

Sunlight and Fading: Black colorways can fade over long exposure to direct sunlight. If your room has strong sun, consider using curtains, blinds, or UV-protective window films to minimize shift in tone over time. A well-placed plant or bookshelf can help diffuse light in ways that keep Wilma looking fresh longer.

Care Warnings: Avoid aggressive cleaning agents. Harsh chemicals can degrade fibers and alter color. Stick to products formulated for synthetic carpets or consult a professional for stubborn stains.
