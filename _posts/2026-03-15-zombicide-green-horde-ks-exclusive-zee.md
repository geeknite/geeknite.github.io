## What is Zombicide Green Horde?
Zombicide Green Horde is a setting that leans into orcish green-on-green violence, a tonal shift from the plague-ridden streets of Black Plague. In this box, you’re not fighting a nameless horde of undead; you’re fighting green-skinned raiders with a preference for axes, gnashing teeth, and the occasional goblin shenanigan. The core rules you know and love from Zombicide are still the spine: a cooperative game where players explore, activate survivors, gather loot, and try not to become dinner for a particularly judgmental zombie.

Here’s what you get in the Green Horde core box:

- A new faction: the Green Horde with its own unique faction cards, equipment, and zombie types that lean into brutal but thematic mechanics.
- Enviro tiles that evoke bristling forests, sinister marshes, and ruined orcish fortifications.
- Miniatures that are more “thunderous and hulking” than their Black Plague cousins, with a level of detail that makes your brush paints weep with joy.
- A new set of objectives and scenarios that tilt the balance toward aggressive play and riskier moves.

The result is a product that feels like a whole new vibe without detaching you from the familiar bite-sized rhythm of Zombicide. It’s what you get when your party of survivors stumbles into a swampy orc bazaar and decides to start negotiating for loot with a crossbow in hand and a sense of confidence that should probably be illegal in most campaign settings.

### Visuals, components, and quality
If you’re the kind of person who gapes at plastic miniatures until your cheeks hurt, Green Horde is your candy shop. The sculpts are bold and dynamic; the greens are vivid; the beady little eyes have more personality than some human players during a long campaign. The boards and tokens hold up under repeated play, and the color palette hits that satisfying, punchy look that says: we’re here to slay, but we’re also here to look good doing it.

## What is Zombicide Black Plague?
Black Plague is the classic, moody elder sibling to Green Horde. It’s the original “plague-zombie apocalypse” vibe with a heavy dose of medieval fantasy: knights, rotting corpses, wizards with questionable moral compasses, and plenty of opportunities to mis-sort your loot because you were too busy yelling at a zombie to notice the goblin thief sneaking past you.

This box remains one of the most beloved in the Zombicide line for several reasons:
- It nails the grimdark fantasy aesthetic with strong, recognizable heroes and a cast of zombies that feel like they belong in a fire-lit doomscape.
- The core gameplay loop is tight and accessible: spawn, explore, activate, loot, survive—repeat.
- A broad roster of characters allows for varied strategies, from brute force to clever positioning.

The Black Plague box serves as a fantastic primer for the franchise, especially for players who crave a more “grimdark” setting and a slightly more punishing difficulty curve. It’s also a terrific base for cross-pollinating with other Zombicide expansions— Purple—uh, I mean Green Horde—being the most obvious match.

### Themes and tone
If Green Horde is a bright, riotous forest rampage, Black Plague is a candle-lit dungeon crawl where every decision could be fatal and every turn an opportunity to despair in the most delightful way. Both boxes share the same core mechanics—spawns happen, action points are spent, and fate is a fickle mistress—but the mood and thematic hooks swing in opposite directions, offering a satisfying yin-yang in any collector’s shelf.

## Gameplay and balance: how it actually plays
Zombicide’s magic lies in its brisk pace and the interplay between risk and reward. The Green Horde and Black Plague boxes share a language, but each adds its own flavor. Here are the core gameplay notes, with Zee in mind as a potential catalyst for divergence:

- Action economy: Each hero gets a fixed set of actions per turn. You’ll be juggling movement, loot collection, and combat, all while the undead crowd in from the edges of the board. The excitement comes from deciding whether to press your luck now or save resources for a later encounter.
- Spawn dynamics: Zombies spawn in waves, which means your plan can be vaporized by a lucky spawn card or a brutal zombie surge. This creates tension and encourages players to keep eyes on the prize without becoming paralyzed by analysis paralysis.
- Combat and loot: You’ll roll dice that translate hits into casualties for the undead, while loot cards offer upgrades, gear, and sometimes ridiculous one-off items that make you question your life choices—usually in a good way.
- Zee’s twist: Zee isn’t a game-breaker; he’s a shifty little accelerant. Expect turns where he can shift the table’s balance toward a win you were certain was out of reach, or conversely, push you into an overconfident move that costs you later. The beauty is in the uncertainty—Zee creates moments that get recapped in memes and brag boards for weeks.

The bottom line: both Green Horde and Black Plague keep the core cadence intact while offering different strategic avenues. Green Horde shines for players who like aggressive, assault-heavy play and a bit of brutal resilience; Black Plague rewards careful positioning and multi-scenario planning. Zee sits in the middle of these dynamics, nudging players toward that delicious “one more turn” sensation.
