## Build Quality and Durability
### Weather resistance
Patio furniture is a test of weather resilience: sun, rain, humidity, and the occasional gust of wind that turns a casual dinner into a beach-side buffet disaster. Aluminum frames resist rust and corrosion well, and the wood slats typically feature protective finishes (sealant, oil, or varnish) that help repel moisture. The result is a set that performs toward multi-season life rather than “three sunny weekends and you’re done.” If your climate experiences heavy rainfall or intense sun, you’ll appreciate a plan for periodic maintenance—this includes reapplying sealant or oil to the wood surfaces and ensuring the cushions, if included, are kept dry between uses. The frame’s finish tends to hold up surprisingly well against UV exposure, which means your gray aesthetic remains on-trend for longer than your average patio color revival cycle.

### Maintenance and care
Maintenance is the boring-but-important part of outdoor furniture life. Wipe down the frame with a mild soap solution and a soft cloth, and avoid abrasive cleaners that could scratch the matte gray finish. For the wood slats, consider a periodic oiling or sealant to protect against drying, cracking, and those pesky coffee spills that somehow become permanent dynamos of stain. If cushions are included, store them indoors during heavy rain or winter to preserve their fill and fabric integrity. If you’re the kind of homeowner who forgets to close the grill’s hood, you’ll be grateful for cushions that dry quickly and a frame that remains unfazed by humidity.

### Step-by-step (high level)
- Unbox and sort the pieces by category (table, chairs, hardware).
- Attach chair legs to seats and secure with screws; tighten sequentially to avoid wobble.
- Attach backrests and armrests where applicable; ensure alignment of slat edges with table edges for a clean line.
- Assemble the table base, then mount the table top, double-check that the slats align cleanly and level.
- Tighten all connections in a diagonal pattern to distribute load evenly and reduce wobble.

## Maintenance Tips and Longevity
- Clean regularly with a gentle soap solution; avoid high-pressure washers that could knock the finish loose.
- If the wood slats look thirsty, apply a protective sealant or oil to maintain moisture balance and prevent cracking.
- Store cushions (if included) in a dry space during rain, snow, or if you’re leaving for a week and the rainclouds are feeling dramatic.
- Check the screws and joints every few months; the occasional re-tightening helps prevent wobble and prolongs the set’s life.
- Use coasters or placemats to minimize stains from condiments and wine; your future self will thank you for the extra effort.
