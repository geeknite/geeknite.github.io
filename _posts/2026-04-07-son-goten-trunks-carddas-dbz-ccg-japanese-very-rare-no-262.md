### Grading and authentication concerns
If you’re serious about the No. 262, you’ll eventually consider grading. Japanese Carddass cards can be graded by professionals who understand print runs of that era. The card’s shine—particularly in foil variants—should be uniform across the surface unless you’re intentionally seeking a certain variant. Authentication is essential if a seller claims a rare foil or a first-run variant; you’ll want to confirm with credible sources or through a trusted community that understands the nuance of this exact card and set.

## Collectibility, care, and long-term storage
### Cleaning and handling tips
First, wash your hands. Then handle with care. Carddass cards are susceptible to fingerprints and oils, so a microfiber cloth can be your best friend during display or grading. Avoid direct sunlight for long periods; UV exposure can yellow or fade older inks, particularly on white card surfaces. If you keep a display, consider a simple UV-protective sleeve and a rigid acrylic stand so the No. 262 and its friends aren’t leaning into their own dramatic melodrama.

### Storage strategies for long-term preservation
A good system is simple: a binder with acid-free sleeves, or a protective top loader for singles you want to show off. Keep a small humidity note near your storage area—Carddass cards aren’t prehistoric fossils, but moisture and humidity swings can affect color and surface gloss over the years. If you’re chasing a complete set, consider a digitized catalog or a notebook where you log each card’s condition and trade history. Nostalgia is great, but a tidy archive makes the chase a little less stressful and a lot more enjoyable.

### Display ideas that honor the card and your space
A rod of fairy-light LEDs, a glass top, and a rotating display stand can turn your No. 262 into a talking point in your living room—without taking up a ton of space. For fans of retro aesthetics, pairing the card with a small poster or a vintage Dragon Ball zine can create a mini-exhibit—proof that a tiny rectangle can spark big stories and even bigger smiles.
