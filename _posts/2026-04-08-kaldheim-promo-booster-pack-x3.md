## The NM (Near Mint) Condition

When a card or booster is listed as NM, the bar is high. We’re talking crisp packaging that shows no edge wear, no creases, and a seal that doesn’t smell like it just survived a goblin-on-a-bike rampage.

- Packaging: The outer wrap should be intact with no holes or tears. It should feel new or nearly so, without obvious moisture damage or crumpling that would indicate rough handling in a warehouse.
- Inside: Booster packs should be securely sealed; no mis-ships; no crushed display cards; and, ideally, all the cards inside free of significant whitening or creases.

Pro-tip: careful check before purchase. If you’re buying from a seller, request high-res photos and confirm the NM status. If you’re buying in person, use the sniff test (paper plus glue equals nostalgia, glue equals possible moisture damage). Kidding aside, if it smells oppressive, walk away.
