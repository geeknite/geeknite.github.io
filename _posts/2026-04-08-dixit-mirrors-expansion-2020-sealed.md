## How the mirrors change the gameplay
The core Dixit mechanic is intact. A storyteller whispers a clue, players pick a card that best fits that clue, and everyone votes on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. Mirrors changes the game by adding a layer of cognitive overhead without breaking the flow. Here are the main shifts you can expect:

1) Dual imagery and refracting clues: The new cards intentionally invite players to think about the same concept from two angles. Your clue might need to reference both the literal and the figurative, or the near and the far. Smart players can earn extra points by crafting clues that align with this duality, while players who lean on single-layer clues may find themselves in the minority.
2) A new set of tokens: The expansion introduces a set of mirror-themed tokens that subtly influence scoring and round flow. They aren’t game-breaking, but they nudge you toward considering how a clue could be interpreted in more than one way. If you love puzzle-solving within party games, these tokens add a nice texture to the scoring and strategy.
3) Variants and house rules: The sealed edition includes optional rules that let you experiment with a slightly more strategic or more chaotic mode. For example, one variant encourages players to reveal a hint about the mirrored image rather than the card itself. Another variant introduces a round-end rule that rewards players for making their clues more ambiguous without sacrificing clarity. These variants are optional, but they offer a lot of replay value for players who like to experiment.
4) Consent and social dynamics: As with all Dixit expansions, the Mirror set asks players to engage with each other in a collaborative storytelling space. The mirror motif can boost inclusivity by inviting players to share different interpretations of the same artwork. It can also lead to a few tricky moments if someone takes the clue in a direction that others feel is too obscure. In a well-mixed group, the expansion shines by giving storytellers a broader palette to work with and giving guessers more to chew on.

Overall, you will not mistake this for a new genre of party game. It stays true to its roots but invites you to slow down, zoom in on parabolic imagery, and enjoy the little delight of catching your own shadow in a card. If you enjoy the narrative aspect of Dixit more than the pure social bluffing of Word-Guessing games, this expansion is likely to be a refreshing breath of freshly minted whimsy.

## Strategies and tips for players
- Embrace the mirror mindset: When you craft clues, deliberately reference dual meanings. A clue that nods to both a physical object and its symbolic counterpart tends to elicit more thoughtful guesses. The trick is to avoid making the connection so obvious that the mirror twist becomes a lecture rather than a puzzle.
- Read the room: Mirrors thrive on ambiguity. If your group gravitates toward snappy, punchy clues, you may want to dial back the sophistication and aim for a balance that still leaves room for interpretation.
- Use the new tokens for rhythm rather than power: Treat the tokens as a commentary on your round flow rather than a direct scoring lever. They help the group keep track of rounds and can guide your decision to lean into a more introspective clue or a bold misdirection.
- Pair with other Dixit expansions carefully: If you combine Mirrors with other expansions, the palette can become rich but potentially overwhelming. Start with one expansion at a time to appreciate how the mirror motif interacts with existing imagery and themes.
- Think about your audience: If you have a group that loves heavy metaphor, the Mirrors expansion will feel like a playground. If your group prefers quick, surface-level humor, you might want to keep your clues light and punchy to maintain momentum.
