Using the cards in play: tips and tricks

- Start slow. When introducing a brand-new card to a room full of players who are trying to recall what card they have chosen, you risk confusion if you attempt to jump straight into a complex description. Instead, present the card early as a new prompt one-shot, then let the storyteller or the group experiment with it in subsequent rounds.
- Create a theme night. The variety of imagery invites you to attempt a theme: Sea, Sky, and Secrets or Dreamscapes and Doodles. Your players can be asked to incorporate the mood of each card into their clue—without revealing the specifics of the art. The result is storytelling that is less a riddle and more a cinematic collage.
- Pair with expansions. If you are someone who plays Dixit with expansions often, test these five cards alongside existing packs to observe how the new images alter the balance of guesswork. Do these cards skew the favor toward more imaginative players or do they reward careful, cunning clues? You will likely discover a blend that suits your group.

Dixit mechanics refresher and how promos fit in

- The core mechanic of Dixit relies on players taking turns as the storyteller who chooses a card and gives a clue. Other players submit cards that they think fit the clue, and everything is shuffled and guessed. The scoring rewards the storyteller for unique guesses and for players who align with the storyteller s clue. The art s ambiguity is essential; it invites metaphor and playful interpretation.
- Promo cards do not typically change the stated rules; they add more imagery to choose from and thus expand the possibilities for clues. They can dilute or intensify the balance depending on how many players and how long you have been playing. In our group, the promos were a test: did they make the rounds more creative or did they lead to more inside jokes that only your group understands? The answer is usually both.
