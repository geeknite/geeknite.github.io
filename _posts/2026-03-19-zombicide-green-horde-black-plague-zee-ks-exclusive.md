## Zombicide primer: what Green Horde and Black Plague bring to the table
Zombicide has always been about teamwork, dice, and the occasional scream when a zombie card reveals a new type of undead that can sprint across the board with more confidence than your average Sunday driver. The Green Horde expansion (often marketed simply as Green Horde) adds a forest-y, RPG-flavored slice to the classic undead-on-rails experience. The Black Plague expansion leans into grim fantasy: knights, necromancers, plague-ridden elites, and a more medieval cadence that makes you ask your table if you’ve stepped into a dark corner of a D&D campaign or a tabletop blockbuster.

In both boxes, Zee stands out as a KS-exclusive, a figure whose model and card mechanics tilt the meta just enough to cause a few head shakes and many “I didn’t know I needed this.” Zee is not just a pretty piece; Zee is a system-level nudge. Zee changes how you plan your turns, what you value in your deck, and how you react when the table looks like a murder scene improvised by a very excited GM with a laser pointer.

If you’re new to Zombicide, think cooperative board game with a pinch of role-playing and a lot of dice-chucking chaos. You control a team of survivors (armed with board-game versions of swords, bows, axes, and occasionally magical devices) and try to clear tiles of zombies while completing flavor objectives. The twist? Zombies aren’t the only threat — you’ll be contending with limited resources, time-based windups, and the ever-looming fear that someone at your table might roll a critical failure while declaring they “totally had this.”

## Green Horde vs Black Plague: two flavors, one chaotic universe
### Core gameplay similarities
- Cooperative play with turn-based action selection.
- A modular board with tile-based zombie spawns that scale with the mission’s difficulty.
- A shared threat track that ensures the undead are never too far behind your plan, forcing you to trade time for safety.
- A deck-driven encounter system that introduces new zombie types, events, and objective cards as you progress.

### Thematic differences that actually matter on the table
- Green Horde leans into forest ambushes, ambulating orcish banners, and a vibe that makes you feel like you’re leading a ragtag group of survivors through a living wood that would rather chew on you for lunch. Expect forest tiles, shrubbery-based cover, and zombie types that feel suspiciously reminiscent of woodland creatures wearing armor.
- Black Plague flips the mood toward a medieval, plague-ridden nightmare. Think plague knights, necromancers, roving bands of raiders, and zombies that feel like they crawled out of a dungeon while someone was whispering “gritty realism” into the mic.

### Board aesthetics and component feel
If you love a warm bundle of medieval fantasy in your life, Black Plague’s aesthetics can scratch that itch. Green Horde offers a color palette that pops in a different way, giving your board a verdant, sinister feel that’s equally immersive. The minis are robust, the sculpts are characterful, and the plastic quality generally holds up to the rough handling that comes with “we’re going to fight our way out of this together.”
