It is where secrets pile up like coffee cups in a writer’s apartment and every corner hums with the possibility of mythic mischief. ### What Fortune Row Actually Is
Fortune Row is not a single fight club or a lone crime ring. It is a curated pocket of the city where fortunes bloom and wither in the same breath. The book maps out the physical spaces that shape who sits at the table and why the table is so damn important. You will meet new factions, new myths, and a roster of NPCs who could be your allies or your worst nightmare depending on how you roll diplomacy or deception. The district vibe is part corporate neon, part ritual street market, and all dramatic potential. It is a place where you can pull a fortune from a hat or snatch it from a myth in the middle of a rainstorm and still have time to argue about the boutique coffee stand that serves psychic espresso.

### The Core System in Fortune Row
If you already know how City of Mist handles mythoi, you will find Fortune Row friendly and, crucially, flexible. The core dice mechanic remains the backbone: you roll your presence, your road, your power, or your cunning to shape outcomes, with the fate dice offering a tactile sense of luck turning on a microscopic gear. Fortune Row does not reinvent the wheel; it adds chrome. It adds rules for urban rituals, for crowd dynamics in a place that is loud and crowded by design, and for the moral calculus of a myth in a human body when the city itself is spinning. The new rules feel polished, rarely tacked on. They help you answer questions like who is willing to bend the myth for money, which mythoi have a stake in particular alleys, and how a city band plays out if the power brokers are the myths themselves.

##### Power, Influence, and Street Lore
The book expands the notion of power beyond brute force. Yes, you can punch your way through a problem, but Fortune Row shines when you exploit influence—how to leverage a rumor into a contract, how a rumor buys access to a room where a single clause can rewrite the city’s power map. There is a robust framework for street level influence that feels grounded yet fantastically cinematic. The new mythoi bring a swirl of different archetypes to the table. Expect a few that make you blink in recognition and others that make you reframe what a hero looks like when the camera focuses on the background players rather than the star.

### Character Options and Archetypes
Fortune Row broadens the cast. The core book introduced iconic masks, the Mythos, and the possibility for players to lean into metaphorical identities. Fortune Row adds fresh templates and invites players to customize even more precise masks tied to a specific city slice. You will encounter archetypes that can anchor a campaign around a heist, an exorcism of a corrupt boardroom, or a ritual that unlocks a criminal fortune spiral. The character toolkit is expansive but not overwhelming. It respects the bite sized, cinematic pacing the game thrives on. Expect new moves that feel both thematically resonant and mechanically satisfying—the kind of twists that turn a session from okay to unforgettable in a single scene.

### A Tour of the District: Locations, Hooks, and Mood
Fortune Row offers a curated set of locations that feel lived in and crowded with possibility. There are night markets that pulse with magical energy, corporate towers that glisten with secrets, and backstreet arcades where a single misread password can open a portal to something ancient. The book does not merely list places; it furnishes hooks that scale from micro interactions to macroscopic schemes. A bartender who remembers every name and remembers nothing at all can pivot a conversation into a bargaining chip. A run down auto shop where a myth has parked itself as a mechanic can become the nerve center of a city wide power play. The location design is one of Fortune Row’s strongest suits: it gives you a sense of place that feels important to the genre while remaining pragmatic enough to run in a single evening.

### Visuals, Layout, and Accessibility
If you hate wading through wall to wall text, Fortune Row respects your time. The layout uses panel style prose with maps, quick bullet summaries, and sidebar lore that you can skim when you are trying to keep a group focused. The artwork leans neon noir with a contemporary edge, which aligns with City of Mist style without feeling like parroting a bygone era. The design choice allows new players to jump in easily while letting veterans savor the craft. The book also includes tips for running on a tight schedule, which is a blessing for tables that occasionally run two sessions a week and still want to hold onto life outside the microwave oven of a democracy rulebook.

### Mechanics in Practice: Grit, Glamour, and The Mythic Ledger
One of the book’s clever offerings is the Mythic Ledger. It is a compact bookkeeping tool that helps GMs track influence, assets, and liabilities tied to Fortune Row. It’s a neat way to represent the tension between public perception and private reality. You will be dealing with alliances that shift with a single public revelation, a rival mythos that complicates every plan, and a street level magic system that rewards clever improvisation. In practice, this leads to scenes that feel personal yet larger than life. The better you map out cause and effect, the more you realize how Fortune Row nudges players toward collaborative storytelling rather than solo heroics. Expect your table to argue about the ethics of influence, the cost of power, and whether a given myth should reveal itself at a crucial moment or wait for a more dramatic reveal.

### Thematic Resonance and Tone
Fortune Row leans into the theme that power in the City of Mist isn’t a single power button you press. It is a web of relationships, favors, and compromises that spin the city’s wheel. The atmosphere remains the neon soaked, rain slick, morally ambiguous vibe that fans love. If your table enjoys the tension of a good noir thriller with sparkles of mystic energy, Fortune Row will hit the sweet spot. It respects the liminal space between heroism and complicity and invites you to discuss what it means to be a hero when the city itself is a living myth. The tone management tips included in the book are especially valuable for GMs who want to preserve the mood without slipping into melodrama or genre parody.

### The Campaign Potential and Play Sessions
Fortune Row shines when used as a lens for ongoing campaigns. It presents enough material to support a multi-session arc without forcing a mega campaign from the start. The chapter on campaign pacing is thoughtful, offering structured beats for episodes that escalate in stakes but never blow past player comfort zones. The book suggests a few ready to run arcs, complete with escalating incidents, mid arc pivot points, and a climactic revelation that can reframe the city’s entire mythic economy. If you have a table that loves long-form drama as well as one shot chaos, Fortune Row gives you both. The seeds are strong enough that you can drop them into an ongoing mystery you are already running or, if you prefer, run a brand new Fortune Row mini arc as your entry point into City of Mist.

### Interactions with the Core Book: What It Complements
Fortune Row is designed to slot in neatly. It does not rewrite established rules; instead, it expands the dictionary of what you can do with them. It complements the core by filling in the ecological niche that City of Mist quietly assumes you crave: a living city where myths interact with everyday life in surprising ways. If you enjoy the core rules of misdirection, improvisation, and the interplay between mask and mortal, Fortune Row will deepen those dicey conversations and give you fresh reasons to whisper about the moral cost of magic at every turn. The district scale, the line of NPCs, and the ready made scenarios help you avoid the dreaded nothing happening syndrome that can plague long campaigns.

### How It Compares to Other Supplements
Compared to other City of Mist supplements, Fortune Row sits comfortably in the middle of the pack in terms of density. It offers more location driven hooks than some locale focused books and more micro rules for urban ritual and influence than pure power bump supplements. It is not the kind of add on that makes you toss the base book in the trash to begin with. It respects the foundation and adds something that feels distinct, especially for groups that enjoy social intrigue and street level ritual drama. If you want a book that can act as a bridge between a one shot and a long arc, Fortune Row is likely to deliver.

### For the GM: Session Prep and Running Tips
If you are a GM who loves a well-prepped table, Fortune Row will feel like a mentor who is also a seasoned con artist. The best prep advice in the book is practical rather than flowery. It suggests you map your session around three core beats: a public event in Fortune Row that invites conflict, a private moment for a myth to reveal itself, and a consequence scene where the players see the ripple effects of their choices. The pacing tips acknowledge that in City of Mist, time is a flexible asset. You can compress or expand scenes as needed to preserve mood and momentum, and Fortune Row provides tools to do exactly that without derailing narrative coherence.

### Artwork and Component Quality
The illustrations and layout support the book’s themes without overshadowing the text. A strong emphasis on mood boards, street signage, and character silhouettes makes it easy for new players to visualize the space and for veterans to mine the book for flavor. In short, it looks and feels like a product that respects players as much as it respects the city it portrays.

### A Quick Sample Arc: The Velvet Ledger Heist
If you want a quick mental sandbox to test Fortune Row, try a three act arc built around a ledger rumored to hold the city’s fortunes. Act one drops the players into a crowded festival in Fortune Row where a sudden correction of a rumor triggers a chain reaction. Act two turns the festival into a negotiation table where factions try to pull the ledger from under a rival myth. Act three culminates in a tense reveal that the ledger’s power is not what the factions assumed but something far more personal and intimate with the city. The arc should be playable in a single evening with room for expansion if you want to push into a longer arc. The book includes additional seeds that slide neatly into this concept, including variants where the ledger is a living CR mage reservoir or a ritual seed that reopens an ancient contract with the city’s founder.

### Final Thoughts and Recommendation
Fortune Row Core Book is a strong addition to City of Mist. It respects the design philosophy of the base game while injecting enough distinct flavor to justify a dedicated read. The district centric approach gives GMs a versatile sandbox that can support a light one shot or a longer arc while maintaining the neon noir energy that defines the franchise. If your table craves more urban myth and you enjoy stories where power is negotiated in backroom deals and ritual spaces, Fortune Row delivers. It is well written, thoughtfully organized, and loaded with practical tools that help you plan sessions without turning you into a full time logistics mastermind.

If you are curious about how to weave Fortune Row into your existing City of Mist campaigns, start with the core rules and then braid in the district’s rituals and factions gradually. Don’t be afraid to improvise and let the myths interact with the city’s mortal inhabitants in surprising ways. The product invites you to lean into the collaboration between players and GM, and that is where City of Mist truly shines.
