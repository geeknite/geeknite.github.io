## How to assess a card like this for your collection

When you pick up a card like ErikaGloom MP, you want to evaluate a few practical things that matter in a collector’s haul. Here is a quick starter kit for the modern collector who wants to get a feel for what matters in a vintage non holo card from late 90s Pokemon trading card game:

- Condition: MP means moderately played. Look for edge wear, surface scratches, and any creases that feel like a lost cause on a binder page. A clean back and a stable surface are a big plus.
- Centering: Vintage cards often have a tendency to be a little off center. This is common. If you plan to grade, you might see a wide variance in centering tolerance, especially on Gym Heroes cards that were printed in large batches with less quality control by today’s standards.
- Print defects: Faint printing lines, slight color bleed, or off-register art are not the end of the world but are good to note when you talk to a seller.
- Authenticity: There are many reprints and fakes floating around. If you are serious about grading, check the back for consistent print patterns and compare with known authentic examples. For the casual collector, the vibe matters more than perfect print alignment.
- Price vs. nostalgia: A non holo in MP can be a bargain that triggers joy. The trick is to decide whether the memory bite is worth the price tag you see on the day of purchase.

If you want a practical path to evaluate price, you can start with standard listings and then widen your search to include auction houses where vintage Pokemon cards are sold by people who know a thing or two about the market. But the best measure remains your own collector’s heart and whether the card makes you smile when you pull it from a sleeve.

### Display and care tips for vintage non holo cards

- Sleeve and top loader combo: A stiff outer shell that protects from scuffs and a snug inner sleeve to prevent edge wear.
- Storage: Keep your cards away from direct sunlight and away from heat. The sun loves to pretend it is a grading expert, and heat can pretend to be a friend until it fries your card edges.
- Handling: Wash hands or use clean gloves during handling. This helps you preserve the surface and avoids the dreaded fingerprint commitment on the artwork.
- Display: A simple glass-front display can show off the Gym Heroes mood while keeping dust away. Add a small LED light for dramatic purple glow that would make Erika approve of your fashion sense in the display case.
