## How to Play: The Quick Pitch

If you know Monopoly Deal already, Refresh will feel familiar but with an ergonomic twist toward speed. The core objective remains straightforward: accumulate three complete property sets. A complete set consists of property cards of a given color family; some cards count toward a color group via wildcards or multi-color options. You play cards to build your property sets, use rent cards to drain opponents’ resources, and deploy action cards to hinder opponents or protect your own plans.

The Refresh mechanic introduces a compact layer of decision points that keep the pace brisk without sacrificing the opportunity for strategic plays. It is not a complete overhaul of the rules; it’s a refinement that makes the late game less predictable and the mid game more entertaining. You will find yourself considering whether to push for a quick win or to trigger a Refresh effect that prevents an imminent wipeout of your efforts.

### Turn Structure

On your turn you may perform up to three actions from a discrete set: play a card from your hand, add cards to your bank, or draw cards. Some cards let you take extra actions or force an opponent to discard, injecting negotiation and bluffing into every round. The presence of Refresh cards reshapes a few of these actions; for instance, it may be advantageous to use a Refresh effect mid-round to re-balance a table or to retrieve a critical property card you fear losing to a wipeout play.

## Strategy Tips and Tointers

Here are practical, field-tested tips for extracting maximum value from Monopoly Deal Refresh:

- Target your first two rounds at securing one near-complete set and enough backup options to backfill later.
- Use rent cards with multi-color potential when you have access to several color sets; the value of multi-color rents compounds as the table grows more crowded.
- Keep a few high-utility action cards in hand for disruption. Just Say No style protections, block cards, and strategic discards can derail a lead if used cleverly.
- Don’t hoard too many cards; a full hand is a deck of potential heartbreak if you get forced to discard under pressure.
- The Refresh cards should be treated as strategic tools rather than random stoppers. Use them at moments when they shift momentum from opponents to you without tipping your own plans off balance.

## Quick Play: Setup, Play, and Then Repeat

Setup is fast: shuffle, deal five cards to each player, and set aside the bank. The draw pile remains in the middle for easy access. The first player starts by drawing and then performing up to three actions. A few rounds in, you will notice the rhythm: draw, play something, maybe steal a card with a timely action, and pass the turn. In most groups, a game wraps up in the 15–25 minute range, which makes it a perfect fit for a quick session between chores or during a party with a dozen players rotating through tables.

### Recommended Player Range
