## Specs and Materials: What You’re Really Getting
Let’s break down the numbers, because geeks love a good spec sheet almost as much as a good punchline.

- Blade material: 7CR13 stainless steel. Durable enough for the kitchen sink of survival scenarios, sharp enough to resemble a micro-sabreur when properly sharpened. If you’re chasing a higher-end edge retention, you’ll want to temper expectations here, but 7CR13 is a solid budget-friendly steel with decent toughness and corrosion resistance.
- Blade length: The blade itself sits in a sensible range for a tanto of this style, with enough belly to handle chopping tasks and a tip geometry that’s designed to pierce with authority. The overall length is a sweeping 17.25 inches, making this one of those blades that commands attention in any collection.
- Blade geometry: Tanto tip with a robust primary grind, offset by a secondary bevel that helps you keep a usable edge when you’re dealing with tougher materials.
- Handle and sub-hilt: The handle employs a traditional grip with a pronounced sub-hilt. This design helps in maintaining control during slashing and piercing tasks and can feel surprisingly comfortable if you have average-to-large hands.
- Sheath: Leather sheath, sturdy stitching, and a belt-loop carry option. The fit is snug and the retention strap holds firm without becoming a nuisance in daily carry.
- Weight and balance: The blade is heavy enough to feel substantial in the hand, but the sub-hilt helps with control, balancing the weight forward so it doesn’t feel like you’re wielding a long machete on a windy day.

## Maintenance: Keeping the Edge Honest
Let’s talk maintenance, because every blade deserves a routine that respects its design. The 7CR13 steel isn’t a high-end alloy, so it benefits from occasional maintenance to prevent corrosion in high-humidity environments or salt exposure. Here’s a quick maintenance checklist:

- Wipe down after use: moisture is the enemy, but a quick wipe and a drop of oil go a long way.
- Sharpen with a proper edge geometry: a field-strop or a light sharpening with a medium-grit stone will preserve the tanto’s edge. Don’t overdo it; you don’t need to remove satin to enjoy a clean finish.
- Inspect the sub-hilt and handle for loosening screws if any—though the handle on this model is compact, it’s still good practice to check occasionally.
- Leather sheath care: a small amount of leather conditioner can keep the sheath supple and avoid cracking, which keeps the knife secured while you’re on the go.

### Safety Considerations
Long blades demand respect. Here are a few safety tips you’ll want to keep at the ready:
- Always sheath the knife when not in use. The leather sheath provides decent protection, but you still want to avoid accidental contact with the edge.
- Wear gloves when batoning or performing heavy-duty tasks to protect your hands from unintended slips. The sub-hilt helps with grip, but it’s not a superhero cape.
- Keep the blade away from children and curious pets. A blade this long in a rush can turn a living room into a scene from a slasher film—no one wants that level of chaos.
