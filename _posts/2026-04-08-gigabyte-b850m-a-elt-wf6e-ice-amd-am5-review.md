## Compatibility tips and build notes
- Memory: DDR5 compatibility is broad, but always consult the QVL (Qualified Vendors List) for guaranteed compatibility with your exact memory kit. Even if your kit has a similar frequency, the board’s tuning can behave differently with each module vendor.
- CPU compatibility: The AM5 socket supports Ryzen 7000-series CPUs, with a good mix of efficiency and performance cores. If you’re targeting a high-core-count workload, pair this board with a reputable cooler to avoid thermal throttling during long sessions.
- GPU clearance: As with most micro-ATX boards, ensure your case has adequate clearance for your GPU, especially if you’re using a chunky cooling solution. The PCIe slot spacing and power connector locations on a micro-ATX board can be more sensitive to clearance constraints than full-size boards.
- Storage planning: With two or more NVMe drives and a couple of SATA disks, cable management becomes not just a suggestion but a requirement for airflow and aesthetics. Plan your cable routes before you mount drives to avoid a tangle that would shame your future self.
