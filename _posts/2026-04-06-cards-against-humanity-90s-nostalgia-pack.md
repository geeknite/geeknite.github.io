### How to play this pack: tips for a successful 90s night
- Size of group: CAH thrives on chaos, and the 90s flavor benefits from a mid-sized crew—enough players to generate a chorus of hilarious reactions without turning into a full-blown court drama. A party of 4–8 tends to be ideal, but you can flex with more if you’ve got the energy and a longer playtime.
- Time allotment: Plan for a 60–90 minute session if you’re mixing in other packs; if it’s a dedicated 90s night, give yourselves 2–3 rounds per deck and a few “bonus rounds” where players pitch their own prompts. The nostalgia is the fuel, but the punchlines are the engine.
- House rules: Consider a “no repeat cards” rule for the Black prompts if your group is slyly cheeky. It keeps the chains of laughter fresh and prevents you from reusing the exact same punchline you used last week.
- Theme nights: Embrace a vibe night—dress like you did in the 90s, bring Beanie Babies, and have a playlist that sounds like it was recorded through a toy microphone. The more you embrace the era, the higher the hilarity ceiling.
- Content awareness: CAH can veer into territory some players find uncomfortable. The 90s pack aims for cheeky nostalgia rather than cruelty, but group consent and mutual respect always outshine any joke. If a prompt hits a nerve, skip it and move on. The goal is laughs, not awkward silences that ruin the vibe.

## The humor: what to expect and what to brace for
### Core joke style
The pack leans into 90s tropes with affectionate satire. You’ll spot references to pop culture trivia, era-specific programming, fashion misfires, and the era’s brand of kid-friendly irreverence. ### Sensitivity and edge balance
As with any CAH expansion, there’s a spectrum of taste across groups. The 90s Nostalgia Pack isn’t inherently mean-spirited, but some prompts may feel dated or carry risk if your players’ experiences around certain topics were painful. The best approach is to establish a quick “playground rules” with your group at the top of the night: no punching down, consent to use certain prompts, and the option to skip prompts if they feel off. The collective experience should be about shared laughter, not discomfort.

## Practical tips for running a 90s CAH night
- Theme setup: Create a small 90s corner with posters, a mixtape playlist, and a few prop accessories (think headphones, neon bracelets, retro sunglasses). The atmosphere matters as much as the cards themselves.
- Icebreakers: Start with one or two “get-to-know-you” prompts that reference the era to warm up the room. It helps players loosen up and lowers the barrier for those who aren’t as quick with punchlines.
- Structure a few rounds: Run a few rounds with the possibility of “expert” prompts that rotate to keep energy high. For example, you can designate a session where players who grew up in the 90s get extra points for accuracy in the punchlines.
- Accessibility and pacing: If your group includes players with longer reading times or if some folks struggle with a heavy reference deck, consider a quick skim pass on the black prompts aloud to give everyone a fair chance to react. You’re aiming for quick wit, not long wind-ups.
- Hybrid play: Pair with a few other party games to diversify the night’s rhythm. A short round of a classic icebreaker game followed by CAH can be a recipe for soaring energy and roaring laughter.
