## Game Overview

### Objective

The goal of *Blank Slate* is simple: fill in the blank and try to match your answer with other players. The more matches you make, the more points you score. It's like *Family Feud* meets *Mad Libs*, but with less Steve Harvey and more hilarious moments.

### How to Play

1. One player draws a card with a word prompt that has a blank (e.g., "___ball").
2. Each player secretly writes down a word to fill in the blank (e.g., "snowball," "football," "meatball").
3. Everyone reveals their answers simultaneously.
4. Players score points based on how many people wrote the same word.

**Pro Tip:** Try to think like your friends. If you know your buddy is obsessed with sports, "football" might be a safe bet!

## The Good, The Bad, and The Blank

### The Good

1. **Easy to Learn**: *Blank Slate* is incredibly simple to pick up. You can explain the rules in under a minute, and everyone will be ready to play.
2. **Inclusive Fun**: Suitable for players of all ages and skill levels. Whether you're a word wizard or a novice, you'll have a blast.
3. **Group Dynamics**: The game encourages hilarious discussions and debates about the best words to use. Prepare for some friendly arguments!
4. **Replay Value**: With a large deck of word prompts, no two games are ever the same. You'll never run out of blanks to fill in.

### The Bad

1. **Limited Strategy**: If you're a fan of complex strategy games, *Blank Slate* might feel a bit too straightforward.
2. **Group Size**: The game is best with larger groups. Playing with only a few people can limit the fun.
3. **Vocabulary Wars**: Sometimes, players might argue over spelling or if a word fits the prompt. Keep a dictionary handy!
