## What is Go Fish? A quick refresher
If you have only seen Go Fish in the school cafeteria with cards bent from overuse and voices that squeak with the same excitement as a vending machine, you know the drill. The core loop is simple: ask for a rank, hope the other players have it, collect four of a kind, and draw when you miss. The winner is the first to empty their hand. The charm lies in memory and social reading, the way a simple question can become a dramatic moment of misdirection. The new twist preserves that core but adds a chorus of silly phrases and a few micro missions that can turn a regular hand into a mini play.

In Go Fuc Yourself the rhythm of calling for a rank remains, but the vibe shifts. The room becomes a stage, the cards become props, and your friends become performers in a spontaneous comedy show. If your group loves puns and quick humor, you are in for a treat. If not, you might want to steer back toward the original or pick a different party game.

### Core mechanics
- Setup: Shuffle the deck and deal five cards to each player. The draw pile sits in the middle, ready for dramatic reveals. Each card in the deck bears a small icon that triggers a mini-challenge when drawn. You will discover this detail on the card backs, so you are never surprised by a sudden stage dive in your living room.
- On your turn: You ask another player for a specific rank. If they have it, they hand it over and you add cards to your set and continue. If they do not, they say go fish and you draw a card from the draw pile. If the drawn card completes a set of four, you lay it down and earn another turn. The rhythm remains, but the cadence gets jazzed by humor opportunities.
- Special actions: Some faces on the deck grant you odd actions such as swap-a-card with the table, call a fake-fish bluff, or deliver a quick joke that the group must applaud. These action cards transform a memory problem into a memory-and-mayhem moment.
- The endgame: The first player to complete a four-of-a-kind wins the round. In this version we add best-of-three or best-of-five. Scores accumulate, and a Banter Meter decision can boost a score multiplier. Cheekiness has its price; the crowd can veto a line that veers into cringe before the table cracks.

### Accessibility and inclusivity
As with any party game worth its salt, accessibility matters. The design emphasizes readability: large font, high contrast, clear iconography. That makes room for players with vision or cognitive differences. The pace can be adjusted for a rapid-fire table or for longer storytelling sessions. Banter and performance are global; give players a moment to breathe if they are new to the dynamic. Go Fuc Yourself should be enjoyed with good drinks and better company, not used to exclude those who do not want to perform every turn.
