## What’s actually in a 1000-card vintage-style instant collection
### Uncommons
Uncommons are the bread-and-butter of any thoughtful instant collection. They represent the middle class of magic—the cards that do their jobs reliably without requiring you to mortgage your future to cast them. Expect a lot of efficient removal, cheap counterspells, and the occasional trick that would make your teacher say: "Now that’s cost-effective." You’ll see:
- Direct removal spells that cost 1-2 mana
- Counterspells that work, most of the time
- Surprises that cause your opponent’s plan to crumble in a most elegant fashion

### Rares
Ah, rares—the shiny, sometimes ridiculous centerpiece of any collection. In an instant-oriented lot, rares can be the difference between "ugh, that’s a dead draw" and "holy power nine, Batman, that one is good." Don’t expect all gems, but do expect the occasional game-winner that could carry a casual match for two or three turns.

### Instant collection in focus: color-by-color breakdown
- White: Exile-based answers, tempo plays, and radiant shields that somehow scream "I am the best friend of your untapped mana."
- Blue: Draw spells, counterfeit illusions, and the occasional mind-breaking spell that makes your deckbuilding partner spill their drink.
- Black: Removal with some extra spice, hand disruption, and the occasional "this is probably fine" effect that’s borderline evil.
- Red: Burn, tempo disruption, and the occasional "oops, I just killed my own board" moment that teaches you humility.
- Green: Flexible removal, pump spells, and big-swing tricks that remind you of stomping through a forest while wearing socks with dragons on them.

### From chaos to order: how to organize this pile
- Sorting by mana cost
- Sorting by color identity (because let’s be honest, you want to see the red cards together so you can light a candle for your burnt hand)
- Sorting by potential to end the game this turn vs. set up for the next
- Sorting by playability in Commander vs. 60-card formats
- Sorting by "will this look good on camera for your next deck-building livestream?"
