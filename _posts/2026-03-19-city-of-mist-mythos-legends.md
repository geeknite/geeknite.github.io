## Introduction

City of Mist Mythos & Legends lands with the subtlety of a neon sign in a back alley and the elegance of a noir cape. In case you missed the memo, this is an expansion for City of Mist, the indie RPG where myths are real and every mage, detective, and vigilante clings to a city that seems to have more layers than a lasagna. Mythos & Legends promises to deepen the mythic flavor, while also giving the GM some shiny new knobs to tweak when the city wants to go full rogue-lab. If you play long enough, you will notice that myth and crime in City of Mist are meant to share the same street corner, and Mythos & Legends doubles the street corner. If your table loves symbol iconography and witty dialogue, this book will feel like slipping on a well tailored coat that was made for a theatre stage.

## What is Mythos & Legends?

Mythos & Legends adds a lot without forcing you to throw away your existing characters. It introduces:

- A new player facing Mythos layer with 12 archetypes that slot into your detective or vigilante identity while giving a mythic anchor for your moves.
- A selection of themed playsets and quest hooks that map to mood frames, from urban myth to forgotten gods under streetlamps.
- Expanded Mythos and Legend tags that you can weave into scenes to call out identity, prophecy, curse, or oath with a single move.
- A suite of one page GM tools that reduce table fatigue and increase story velocity.
- A refreshed art direction with more mythic symbolism and a gloss that feels premium but not ostentatious.

The implication is that Mythos & Legends is not a replacement for the core system but a narrative upgrade kit, with a lot of clarity and texture that helps both players and GMs lean into mythic identity without losing the city grit.

## Mechanics and Design

City of Mist uses a engine that borrows life from myth and modern street talk. The Mythos layer is the star here. The trait package comes with a curated set of Power moves and a few Legend based options that function as capstones for arcs. The effect is to create a sense that your character has a mythic current flowing through daily life. The balancing act is careful. If you flood the table with mythic traits, you risk a godlike vibe that can stray from the detective city feel. Conversely, too little myth and you miss the point. Mythos & Legends nails this by distributing options across three tiers: minor myths, mid level legends and a few big mythic stake moments.

Mythos Sparks are quick prompts that you can drop into scenes to escalate tension. They feel like good prompts from a smart game master, enough to nudge players into a moral choice or a quick fight where the city reveals its true face. A few Sparks are specifically designed to push the city forward when the party is stuck or when you want to switch to a new mood and color palette for the session.

## Character Creation and Mythology

Character creation remains familiar but richer. Archetypes come with:

- Pre defined moves aligned with both Mythos and Legend outcomes
- Mythic Ties that unlock new options or provide symbolic leverage in key scenes
- A small pool of new props that you can mirror onto your sheet

The synergy between Mythos and Legend is the core of this expansion. When you attach a myth to a city deity or a hero of a forgotten pantheon, you gain not only personal stakes but a city wide ripple effect. It is exactly the moment when your detective stops simply solving crimes and starts to reveal a cultural memory that the city forgets but your character remembers.

If you want to go deeper, you can experiment with non standard mythic ties such as a border crossing that links a local myth to a national event or a prophecy that whispers through a radio frequency in the city night. You do not need to run with all of these, but having options gives you elasticity to pivot when the group shifts mood.

## GM Tools and Adventure Hooks

The GM tools are practical and not just decorative. The Mythos Compass is a central piece. It helps you map the city’s tilt: Awe, Fear, Wonder, and Doubt can swing in a session. It is useful for planning arcs that feel plausible while still mythic.

![]({{ '/assets/images/city-of-mist-mythos-legends-cover.jpg' | relative_url }})

The seeds section includes: The Prophecy at the Bus Stop, The Masked Idol Heist, The Alley of Echoes. These seeds are compatible with existing city maps and can be dropped into your next session as you see fit. And yes, you can also reuse the base game's investigative framework—this expansion is designed to slot into existing players and keep a consistent tone.

## Storytelling and Thematic Consistency

If you enjoy myth plus noir, Mythos & Legends is your sugar. The book leans into classical motifs but reinterprets them in a contemporary city. It uses myths for the best reasons: to illuminate character arcs, to create meaningful choices, and to give you a vocabulary for what the city hides in the shadows. The odd thing about City of Mist is that myth is never glamorous for long; it eventually reveals a social context or a personal wound. Mythos & Legends preserves that mood while giving you tools to push for a stronger narrative arc.

## Layout and Production

The book increases readability with generous margins, color-coded sections, and a layout that is both crisp and accessible. The index is improved; the page references line up with visual cues that a GM can locate during a live session. The art direction adds to the mood of myth meets metropolis, with ambiguous symbols, neon accents, and silhouettes that hint at stories behind every door.

## Play Experience

A good run with Mythos & Legends feels like stepping into a club whose doorman wears a helmet of a mythic crest. The vibe is strong, but the game still nails the balance between roleplay and action. The new tools allow you to skip long setup times and dive into scenes more quickly. If you play a long campaign, you will appreciate the ability to reframe an arc around a legend rather than forcing a new case each week.

In short, the expansion does not just supply new rules; it supplies new lenses for your table. You can use myth to interpret the city as a living, breathing character, or you can lean into the detective drama with myth as flavor rather than a constraint.

## Scenarios and Playtests

Two to three ready-to-run mini-scenarios provide a scaffold for sessions, and the seeds give you templates to craft new ones. The structure remains friendly to improvisation; you can easily adapt a seed to a different mood if the party is chasing a different style of myth or if the city wants to present a new challenge. The quality of the scenarios is high, with clearly signposted escalation and a satisfying payoff that respects both player agency and the mythic undercurrent.

## Accessibility and Replayability

A strong point of Mythos & Legends is its approach to accessibility. The archetypes are intuitive, the moves are clearly described, and the mythic ties are explained with minimal jargon. You can teach this quick to new players and still have depth for veterans. Replayability is improved thanks to the wide variety of myth paths and city settings. The City Walks feature is a fun way to fine tune a campaign to different moods, such as a mythic underworld stroll or a prophecy-laden night ride through the city.

## Comparison to Base Game and Other Expansions

Compared to City of Mist core, Mythos & Legends feels like a structured moral compass for mythic storytelling. It does not overhaul the core engine; it leans on the same mechanical logic but gives you more texture, more color, and more options to shape who your character is under the city lights. If you own other expansions, you will notice the book slots into your collection without disrupting established playstyles. It is a gentle upgrade with real impact.

## GM Tips and Warnings

- Start with a Mythos Map a city-wide map of symbolically significant places that you can reference during sessions.
- Use a Mythos Spark to escalate scenes in a controlled way. Sparks can shift mood and stakes without forcing a dramatic confrontation.
- Guard against myth overload: if every other scene is a prophecy, the players will stop reacting and start reciting myth lines. You want reactions, not recitations.
- Embrace the city as a living character: use the Compass to steer scenes so that the city itself has a role in the drama.
- Build consequences that matter: a mythic tie should have a cost as well as a benefit.

## Art, Tone, and Packaging

The production values are notable. The cover art is striking, the interior art supports the mood without crowding the prose, and the typography supports quick scanning during play. The color palette communicates a sense of mythic energy while preserving urban grit. If you show the book to your players, they will get excited; if you show it to non players, they will ask to borrow it for art references.
