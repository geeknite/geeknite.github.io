## Getting Started: Quick Play Guide in 10 Minutes
If you want the fast lane to fun, here is a quick start that sounds easy and is actually a sprint:

### Setup
- Mix base deck with the Recipes for Disaster expansion cards. The box supports 2-6 players, but we found 4-8 to be the sweet spot for the table to feel lively and chaotic without turning into a full-blown strategy lab.
- Each player gets five cards. The aim is simple, avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten and end the round with the most points.
- Shuffle in Exploding Kitten cards with the rest of the deck, and arrange a space on the table where everyone can reach for the recipe cards.

### Turn flow
- On your turn you may play a card or draw. If you draw and reveal an Exploding Kitten, you are out unless you have a defuse card or a goofy recipe-based save.
- Recipe cards let you perform cooking themed antics, like forcing someone to reveal their hand, swapping cards with another player, or playing a blunder that negates another fancy move.
- The expansion adds special recipe combos that trigger dramatic effects like a sudden dessert duel or a spicy sauce swap. It’s chaotic, yes, but the laughter is real.

### Card types in the recipes expansion
- Recipe cards that trigger silly kitchen mishaps
- Cooking Challenge cards that turn into mini-games with quirky restrictions
- Disaster cards that escalate tension and hilarity
- Defuse cards that keep players breathing and not panicking mid-round

## Strategy and Social Play: How to Win in Style
This expansion rewards social play more than cutthroat tactics. Here are some practical strategies:
- Read the table, then decide: The best moment to deploy a recipe card is often when someone is about to go on a winning streak. A well-timed force move can swing the momentum and put a new mischief-maker into the lead.
- Bluff and blurp: People love to guess what your hand contains. A confident bluff can deter others from calling your actions. The key is to stay consistently playful, not mean, and keep the room light.
- Manage the defuse economy: Defuse cards are a finite resource. Save them for moments that matter, or use them to trigger a chain of reactions that advantages you in the long run. It’s often better to bluff and threaten than to actually risk your own safety by overusing defuses in the early rounds.
- Use the Disaster cards as momentum swingers: Don’t just punish; create a moment that changes the table dynamic. A well-timed disaster can create a memorable clash between two players, filling the room with laughter and the scent of imaginary burnt toast.
- Embrace the party vibe: The point of this game is to entertain. If a round becomes too intense, switch to a variant that emphasizes storytelling or quick-fire questions. The more you lean into the comedic energy, the less likely you are to hit that awkward lull.
