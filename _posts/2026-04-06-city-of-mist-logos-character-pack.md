## Overview and first impressions
### What is the Logos Character Pack?
The Logos Character Pack is not a replacement for the core book. Think of it as a toolbox expansion: a curated collection of new logos (thematically distinct symbols that anchor a character’s mythic signature), refreshed art assets, and a handful of ready-to-run character ideas. It’s designed to slot into existing campaigns without forcing a total overhaul of your narrative, which in City of Mist terms means: more mythic prompts, fewer breaks in the noir vibe, and a little more room for your players to play fast and loose with their mythic identities.

The packaging feels deliberate and gamer-friendly rather than flashy pro-wrestler-cheerleader flashy. It’s the kind of product you’d happily leave on the table during a session break, just in case someone asks, “What does that logo mean again?” and the GM, with a patient sigh, explains that the Logo is not just a symbol—it’s a plot hook in a turtleneck.

Aesthetically, the art direction leans into City of Mist’s noir-urban aesthetic with bold linework, high-contrast colors, and a subtle nod to vintage signage. If you’ve ever wanted a set of logos that look as though they could be carved into a brick wall by a passing myth, you’ll feel seen. The visual language of these logos communicates power, motive, and myth with just a single glyph, which is exactly the graduate-level design you want in a game that thrives on symbolic storytelling.


### What’s inside the box (or PDF)
If you’re a physical-pack purist, the Logos pack includes a deck of logo cards, a small GM screen insert, and a compact, printable reference sheet for players. If you’re a digital-first table, there’s a perfectly usable PDF with printable logos that you can drop into your VTT or print at home, plus a few extra alt-art logos for variety.

The cards come with two main attributes per logo: the symbol’s name and a short blurb that explains how the logo ties into a character’s mythic identity. Some logos lean toward “power over matter” vibes, others toward “truth-bender” vibes, and there are a surprising number that imply social leverage—logos that function like license plates for your on-stage character narration. It’s the City of Mist way of telling you, “Your power is a story, so tell it with a caption.”

A few new logos also tease future thematic expansions. The pack doesn’t pretend to rewrite City of Mist’s core rules, but it nudges players toward fresh angles—the kind of nudge that makes a long campaign feel new again without requiring a total reset.


## Design and layout analysis
### Card quality and usability
The logo cards themselves strike a balance between form and function. The cardstock is sturdy enough to survive table roughhousing, yet thin enough to shuffle without turning your table into a papier-mâché experiment. The print resolution handles fine details without smearing; you’ll appreciate the crisp serif typography and the way the symbol sits crisply against colored backgrounds. The Logos Pack does a good job preserving quick-reference readability, with clean borders, a clear typographic hierarchy, and a color-coded system that helps you identify Logos at a glance during fast-paced scenes. The artworks pair well with City of Mist’s cinematic-noir tone. They feature stylized iconography—think glyphs within circles, cosmic sigils, and urban silhouettes. ## Thematic depth and storytelling potential
### A toolbox for character-driven narratives
City of Mist thrives when players lean into forced mythic symbolism rather than relying on brute force. Logos become the lexical toolkit by which a character articulates motive, flaw, and motive. The Logos Character Pack amplifies this by widening the constellation of symbols you can pick. The richer the symbol library, the richer the character’s story potential—without forcing a cookie-cutter archetype. A well-chosen logo can accelerate a scene’s emotional stakes, turning a routine alleyway encounter into a confrontation about legacy, identity, or moral compromise.

Thematic potential is where this pack shines. You’ll find logos that explicitly tap into memory, mythic lineage, urban power networks, and personal redemption arcs. For players who enjoy crafting mythic backstory as part of the gameplay, the pack becomes a creative catalyst. It’s less about stacking bonuses and more about giving players narrative levers to pull when the city wants to remind them who they are.

### GM-facing benefits and caveats
GMs get a repository of prompts that can jumpstart scenes. But beware: with more logos on the table comes a greater cognitive load. If your group tends to lean towards improvisation, you might hit a mental bottleneck when every logo has a story worth telling. In that case, pre-select a subset or create “logo pools” tailored to the current arc. The pack supports that approach, offering flexibility rather than forcing a global change to how you run sessions.

If you relish “twist first, explain later” moments, logos offer ideal misdirection tools. A logo might imply a character’s allegiance to a city faction or a hidden allegiance that emerges only in certain contexts. The best sessions from a Logos Pack-powered campaign are the ones where a symbol’s meaning resurfaces in the climactic moment, recontextualizing all prior scenes.
