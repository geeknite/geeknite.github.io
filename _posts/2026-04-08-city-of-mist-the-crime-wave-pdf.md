City of Mist: The Crime Wave - PDF is the kind of supplement that makes you grin like a caped vigilante even before you roll a single d6. It’s not just a collection of adventures wrapped in glossy pages; it’s a toolbox, a mood board, and a spark for a night of noir-infused roleplay that somehow makes you believe the city itself is complicit in the crimes you’re about to investigate. If you’ve played the core game, you know City of Mist thrives on that tug-of-war between myths and mundane life. The Crime Wave leans into that tension with a purpose-built arc that can drop into a standard one-shot or stretch into a condensed mini-campaign. In this review, we’ll unpack what the PDF does right, where it stumbles, and how to squeeze the most mythic juice from its pages.


## Overview

At its core, City of Mist uses a pulp-noir engine fueled by Mythos and Logos — the former powering your powers as legendary traits, the latter defining your mundane, everyday identity. The Crime Wave PDF leans into that duality with a city-wide spasm of crime that feels both familiar and deliciously strange. The setup centers on a wave of seemingly unconnected incidents that force players to assemble a pattern from disparate clues: a string of thefts in the warehouse district; a phantom car chase through the Brightstone avenues; a whisper campaign that turns a beloved soup kitchen into a front for something far more sinister. It’s the kind of premise that invites both investigative sleuthing and over-the-top heroics. And yes, there are enough twists to keep even jaded players guessing without collapsing into a cliché hurricane.

The presentation is clean, with a layout that respects your time. The Crime Wave isn’t trying to be a doorstop; it’s a focused, playable experience that you can run in a single session or stretch across a couple of evenings. The PDF includes ready-to-run scenes, NPC write-ups, clue lists, maps, and a GM-facing section full of tips that feel like they were written by someone who has actually run a City of Mist game at a convention and learned not to improvise on the fly while a table full of players stares you down. The art direction stays true to the game’s modern-noir vibe: moody silhouettes, neon glow, weathered documents, and enough stylized sigils to remind you that the city listens when you talk. If you’re a fan of the core rulebook’s aesthetic, you’ll be right at home.


## What you get

The Crime Wave PDF clocks in at a generous length for a single-arc supplement. It includes:

- A modular plot arc designed to accommodate a mid-sized table (4-6 players) with options to scale up or down.
- A cast of new NPCs and minor antagonists, each with Mythos-Logos hooks and a handful of unique quirks that push scenes into memorable beats.
- A collection of clue cards and investigator handouts that feel tangible rather than photos of printouts you’d scribble on. The cards are well-designed for quick reference, which helps when you’re juggling NPCs and the city’s shifting loyalties.
- Maps and locales that give you a sense of place without locking you into a single “how to run it” path. You can reroute the arc to emphasize social intrigue, crime-scene alchemy, or something more supernatural depending on your group’s appetite.
- A GM’s guide with scene prompts, pacing notes, and tips for handling investigation-heavy sessions. It’s clear someone spent time thinking about the rhythm of a City of Mist game and how players tend to drift toward dramatic versus procedural play.

If you’re a GM who loves the tactile side of roleplaying, the clue cards and handouts alone are worth the price. They reduce pre-game prep and give players concrete objects to interact with, which is crucial for a game that thrives on symbolic significance and interpretive clues.


### New and notable design choices
The expansion leans into three core improvements over many adventure downloads:

- Layered antagonist design: instead of a lone mastermind, Crime Wave builds a web of influence where each actor has a separate mythic motivation and feasible counter-move. This rewards players who push past obvious suspects and start mapping relationships.
- Clue weaving as a narrative engine: clues aren’t just plot devices; they’re intended to be connected by players through discussion, interrogation, and symbolic interpretation. The arc rewards patterns, not just correct answers.
- Pacing that respects investigation and drama: the pacing notes acknowledge table drift and offer flexible fallback beats so you can glide around interruptions without losing momentum.


## Mechanics and compatibility

City of Mist uses a streamlined system centered on resolving actions through Stats, Tags, and the twisting of Mythos and Logos. The Crime Wave doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it leans into the system’s strengths and adds a few optional tweaks that feel organic rather than gimmicky. Here are the highlights from a mechanics perspective:

- Tagged checks remain the backbone. You’ll find new Tags linked to the local underworld, a few “crime wave” motifs, and a couple of city-specific Themes that players can lean on. If you’ve got a character who’s built around a strong Mythic identity, you’ll appreciate how the arc nudges them toward meaningful choices rather than generic problem-solving.
- Clue management is well integrated. The PDF offers a flexible mechanic for weaving clues across scenes, encouraging players to connect dots in ways that feel natural rather than forced. It’s the kind of design that makes the “aha” moment feel earned instead of convenient.
- Villain design gets a boost with layered Truths and conflicting goals. The Crime Wave doesn’t rely on one big baddie; it creates a constellation of forces in tension with each other, which is a hallmark of City of Mist’s social-political noir flavor.
- The NPC roster is a small tournament of moral ambiguity. The new characters aren’t just stat blocks; they come with micro-arcs that can be threaded into ongoing campaigns or used to seed future adventures. If you’re the kind of GM who loves repurposing NPCs in other City of Mist settings, you’ll find the integration pleasantly seamless.

## Structure and play flow

The Crime Wave is structured to support a tight evening of play, but it’s flexible enough to stretch. The arc is broken into acts that mirror classic noir storytelling: setup and social maneuvering, escalation through investigation and suspicion, and a finale that blends revelation with action. The hour-by-hour pacing notes in the GM section are surprisingly practical; they acknowledge that players will likely derail a plan in pursuit of dramatic moments, and they offer fallback beats to keep the story on track without railroading. This is not a one-size-fits-all adventure; it’s a chassis you can adapt to your table’s appetite.

Act I establishes the Crime Wave’s atmosphere and introduces a trio of anchor suspects. Each suspect carries their own motive and a couple of signature moments that can be activated depending on player choices. Act II shifts to investigation, where players untangle the network of influences behind the crimes and start mapping the city’s hidden logic. Act III brings the tension to a boil with a set of escalating confrontations that test both wits and nerve. The finale ties together the threads and leaves room for a follow-up hook that can lead into other City of Mist projects.

In terms of pacing, the PDF handles tempo well. There are several mid-scene resets that allow players to regroup, reinterpret, or pivot to a different approach without losing momentum. If your table enjoys social leverage and clue-chasing as much as brawling, you’ll find the Crime Wave achieves a sturdy balance between the two.


### Play examples and micro-scenarios
The authors include compact playlets that can be dropped into a larger session or used as stand-alone beat sheets for a one-shot. Examples include a tense tea-with-a-suspect scene that hinges on a moral choice and a back-alley exchange where a hidden sigil on a ledger reveals a city-wide network. These scenes aren’t just set pieces; they’re opportunities to provoke roleplay that exposes a character’s Mythos or Logos in action. If you’ve ever felt the core rulebook’s mythic aspect could be teased out more in organised play, Crime Wave gives you concrete hooks that non-player characters can react to in unpredictable ways.

## Scenes, clues, and interactive moments

What makes City of Mist sing is the texture of scenes: the way a whispered rumor in a coffee shop can shift a suspect’s behavior, or how a single clue can cascade into a larger conspiracy. The Crime Wave leverages this beautifully. It offers:

- A set of signature scenes that can be played almost in order or shuffled to taste. The scenes are designed to reward players who listen to the city’s whispers and connect the right dots at the right moments.
- A robust set of clue cards and physical handouts. They’re not just tokens; they’re narrative prompts that encourage players to riff on the Mythos/Logos duality and find personal stakes in the story.
- Non-player characters who feel lived-in. They have agendas, alibis, and a few secrets that can be surfaced with clever roleplay. This isn’t a parade of cardboard cutouts; these are people with reasons to lie, cheat, or help, depending on how the players engage with them.

One of my favorite mechanical touches is the subtle integration of a city-wide mood tracker. As the Crime Wave unfolds, certain locations become more or less hospitable to the Players’ goals. It’s a clever way to externalize the city’s influence without turning the game into a spreadsheet exercise.

## Tokens, art, and layout

Visually, the Crime Wave leans into neon-lit shadows and rain-soaked streets. The PDF’s typography keeps the reading experience brisk; headers guide you through acts, sidebars offer GM tips, and the lower pages host the maps and handouts. The inclusion of two maps is particularly welcome: a district map that helps players visualize the city’s geography and a more intimate, event-focused map for the climactic scenes. The art direction is not flashy in a megacool poster sense; it’s the kind of moody, purposeful illustration that signals this city has secrets, and you’re not alone in perceiving them.

In terms of accessibility, there’s a clear attempt to keep the book readable for newer players while still offering depth for veterans. The content isn’t overly dense with exposition; it respects the player’s agency and pushes toward meaningful choices rather than a rigid beat-by-beat script. If you’ve found some City of Mist supplements heavy on setting but light on playability, you’ll appreciate the Crime Wave’s emphasis on narrative leverage and practical GM tools.


## Replayability and longevity

While the Crime Wave is designed as a standalone arc, it also opens doors for future play. The NPCs and their interconnected agendas can be dropped into other City of Mist campaigns or used as recurring antagonists at a lower scale. The modular structure invites GMs to pull threads and weave them into homebrew plots or to reuse suspects as recurring villains in a longer campaign. And because the arc integrates with the city’s Mythos/Logos engine rather than overshadowing it, players who enjoy personalization of their identities will find future sessions enhanced by paying off past choices.

If you’re the kind of player who likes to run with a few hot threads you can pick up in the middle of a session, you’ll relish how the Crime Wave provides flexible entry points. It’s not a linear railroad; it’s a map of possible routes through a city that remembers every choice you’ve made.


## Thematic fit and GM notes

Noir isn’t a genre; it’s a mood. City of Mist sells that mood by letting player characters embody myths that exist in the city’s everyday fabric. The Crime Wave leans into that by emphasizing the tension between the glamorous legends and the gritty realities of urban life. The GM notes are a treasure here: they don’t just tell you what to do; they suggest how to respond when the players bring unexpected insights or push back against the plot’s predetermined beats. This is where a City of Mist supplement can go from nice to have to absolutely essential for a group that wants to run a session that feels cinematic and personal.

If you’re worried about railroad-ing in a system that rewards improvisation, fear not. The Crime Wave includes contingency plans for when players go off the rails, including alternative clues that fit the same undercurrent of the plot and a handful of improvisational prompts for the GM. The result is a book that respects both structured plotting and the organic chaos that makes City of Mist so engaging.


## Pros and cons

Pros:
- Strong authorial voice that captures City of Mist’s noir-crossover vibe.
- Practical GM tools: clues, handouts, and scene prompts that cut prep time significantly.
- Flexible arc designed for 4–6 players, with scalable options.
- Beautiful, moody art direction and thoughtful layout.
- NPCs and antagonists with meaningful ties to the city’s Mythos/Logos fabric.

Cons:
- Some tables of stats for NPCs can feel a touch dense for new GMs; a few quick reference sheets would be welcome in a second edition.
- The pacing notes assume a certain level of player engagement with investigative play; if your group is heavy on combat, you’ll want to lean on the GM tips to re-balance scenes.

If you’re a veteran GM, you’ll likely breeze through the sections and want to cannibalize scenes and clues for your own campaign. If you’re newer to City of Mist, approach the arc as a curated sandbox rather than a script and remember that the city’s secrets reveal themselves in conversation as much as in action.


## Final verdict

City of Mist: The Crime Wave - PDF hits the sweet spot for groups who crave an urban mystery with a mythic heartbeat. It doesn’t pretend to solve every possibility; rather, it arms you with enough structure and flavor to let your table write the rest. The arc provides a memorable blend of investigative play and cinematic payoff, anchored by a cast of characters who feel like real people with real consequences. You’ll finish the night with a sense of accomplishment, a few new inside jokes about the city’s odd myths, and a clipboard full of clues you wish you could cross-examine in real life.

If you’re building a City of Mist campaign or you’re itching for a one-shot that respects player agency while delivering a punchy noir finale, this is a solid choice. It respects the core game’s themes and offers enough novelty to justify a purchase on its own.
