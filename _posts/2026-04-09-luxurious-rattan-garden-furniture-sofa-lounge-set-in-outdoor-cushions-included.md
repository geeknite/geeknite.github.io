## Weather Resistance and Maintenance
Patio furniture is a love/hate relationship with the weather. This set is designed to weather the weather without turning into a tragic mess.

### UV and Fade Resistance
The synthetic rattan weave is designed to resist UV-induced fading. Expect color retention across a full season of sun, with perhaps a minor shift if you leave the set under direct, brutal sun for months. If you’re a sun-worshipper who enjoys the “weave color patina” look, that’s a style choice. If not, a shade sail or pergola can help preserve hue over time.

### Water and Moisture Handling
Cushion covers are water-repellent and can dry quickly after a shower or a sprinkler attack. In heavy rain, you’ll want to store cushions indoors or invest in storage bags to keep moisture from becoming a tourist in your living room. The frame itself resists water exposure reasonably well, but prolonged wet days will require light drying and occasional maintenance to prevent any rust near metal joints.

### Cleaning and Care
Routine care is simple:
- Wipe with a damp cloth for day-to-day grime.
- Wash cushion covers in mild soap and water when needed; air-dry fully before reassembly.
- Use a gentle brush on the weave if dust accumulates in the pattern.
- Conduct an annual check of screws and bolts to ensure everything remains snug.

Avoid harsh solvents or power washing directly on the cushions or fabric; that’s how you void warranties and also turn your lounging area into a confetti explosion of fabric fibers. A soft-bristle brush can help dislodge stubborn dirt without harming the weave.

### Seasonal Storage Tips
If you live in a climate with intense winter weather, consider storing cushions and, if possible, removing modular pieces indoors during the harsh months. This not only extends the cushions’ life but also keeps the weave from encountering the brutal, unfiltered weather that can lead to brittle strands over time. A weatherproof cover can work wonders if storage isn’t feasible, but you’ll still want to remove covers for the off-season to prevent mildew.

## Assembly: Tips and Common Pitfalls
Assembly is a breeze with two people and a calm beverage nearby. Here are some practical tips to save you time and hair pulling:
- Unpack everything and lay out all pieces before you start. A tidy workspace reduces the risk of losing screws behind the grill or the patio rug.
- Check hardware for any shipping damage; if something is bent or missing, contact customer service rather than trying to jury-rig a repair with a paper clip.
- Start with the main sofa base and then attach the arms. The modular sections typically connect with straightforward bolts and keys.
- Don’t overtighten screws on the first pass; snug is usually enough and prevents cross-threading on future rearrangements.
- Allow cushions to break in a bit before evaluating comfort. They often settle after a couple of uses and give you the perfect lounging height.
