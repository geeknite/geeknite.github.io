Rattan and resin materials handle outdoor conditions better than you think. ## Durability and Maintenance
### Weather resistance
The main durability feature here is weather resistance. The frame uses powder-coated metal to reduce corrosion and rust, while the resin weave is designed to resist moisture, fading, and some UV exposure. That means a sudden summer rain or a full-on Florida sun is less likely to cause structural issues in the short term. Of course, prolonged exposure in harsh climates isn’t free of consequences; you may notice subtle color fade after long-term sun exposure. A retractable cover or a shaded area can do wonders for prolonging color and fabric life.

### Cleaning and upkeep
Be prepared for routine maintenance. Light cleaning with a mild soap solution and a soft brush is usually enough for day-to-day dirt. Avoid using harsh chemicals that could degrade the resin weave or the cushion fabric. For stubborn stains on cushions, a fabric cleaner designed for outdoor upholstery is a good idea. If you have pets, you might notice claw marks; a quick wipe-down and a bit of buffing can usually reduce the effect, but don’t expect perfection. The key is consistency: quick wipe-downs after use, periodic cushion washing, and storing cushions when not in use for longer periods.

### Longevity expectations
A well-built 7 piece modular set can last several seasons with proper care. The lifetime may be shorter in extreme climates or if you live in a region with heavy salt spray or frequent storms. Still, the modular design makes replacement easier; you can swap out individual pieces if a section wears out, rather than replacing the entire set. This modularity is a win for those who want to keep the look fresh without a full furniture makeover.
