### How the expansion changes the vibe
The base game is a clean, bright, and approachable party title that you can teach in under five minutes and forget the next morning. Not Safe For Work crushes in wearing a different aesthetic robe. It invites players to loosen up, wink at each other, and embrace the possibility that a silly card could derail your strategy with a wink and a nudge. The rules remain simple enough for a first timer to catch on mid laugh, but the jokes push toward a more adult, more social experience. If you want a party game that can be funny without hogging the spotlight, this expansion makes a strong case for stepping outside the kid friendly box without turning your living room into a roast battleground.

### Gameplay tips and strategies
- Read the room before you shuffle. If your group has a history of mischief and borderline inappropriate humor, you are in the target zone. If you have a family game night that includes grandparents, you might want to keep this expansion tucked away for a later rewatch of the show.
- Use timing like a pro. The Not Safe For Work cards are best when played with a beat where everyone knows that a joke is about to land. Do not rush them; let the room breathe then deliver.
- Balance risk and reward. Some cards lean into audacious plays that can ruin one player for the entire round. In a tight game, this is a tool to swing momentum, but in a rowdy party, it becomes a spectacle that the room will remember for weeks.
- Pair with strong moderation. If players get carried away, a quick friendly reminder about boundaries can ensure the experience stays fun for everyone involved.

### Safety and inclusivity notes
Adult humor does not equal exclusion. This expansion thrives when players share a sense of humor and know when to stop. It is wise to establish boundaries before the first card flips and to give players a safe word or a signal to pause a moment if any joke crosses a line for them. It is not a license to harm or embarrass; it is a license to laugh and to roast the moment without becoming mean. Remember that the point is to enjoy the chaos and celebrate how a simple card game can morph into a late night improv session with a handful of kittens in hand.
