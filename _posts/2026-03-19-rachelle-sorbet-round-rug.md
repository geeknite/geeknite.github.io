## Maintenance and Care
### Cleaning and Longevity
The rug is designed for real life, which means it expects some life to happen. For most minor spills, blotting with a clean cloth and a touch of mild detergent should be sufficient. Avoid harsh chemicals that could strip color or damage fibers; stick to gentle cleaners and test in an inconspicuous spot first. Regular vacuuming is recommended; use a vacuum without a beater bar on the high setting to preserve the plush nap. Over time, rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear and to prevent a single high-traffic path from creating a worn bald patch in the Sorbet spectrum.
### Durability in High-traffic Areas
If your living area is the stage for daily foot traffic, the rug’s density and fiber choice should hold up reasonably well for the price point. It will show the inevitable signs of living a life with humans, pets, and a few clumsy moments, but those signs should be manageable rather than dramatic. If you’re evaluating this rug for a high-energy kids zone or a busy entryway, you might consider selecting a darker colorway or a more rugged weave in the same family to minimize visible wear. Alternatively, rotating the rug every couple of months can distribute wear more evenly and preserve the color depth.
