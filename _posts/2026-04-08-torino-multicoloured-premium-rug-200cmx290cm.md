## Cleaning and Care

### Daily maintenance
Keeping the Torino Multicoloured Premium Rug vibrant requires a light touch most days. Regular vacuuming is your friend. Use a vacuum with a switch that can handle low-pile or medium-pile rugs without sucking up the fibers. A weekly pass should suffice for typical living rooms; for high-traffic areas or households with shedding pets, you might opt for more frequent though gentle cleanings to avoid a buildup of dust bunnies that could threaten your Netflix reliability.

### Spills and stains
Spills happen. The best approach is to blot, not rub, with a clean cloth to lift as much liquid as possible. If the stain is stubborn, treat with a mild detergent solution or a rug-care product recommended by the manufacturer. Always test in an inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness and avoid warping. Be mindful of color transfer in the initial weeks since multicolored fabrics can exhibit some migration if not fully cleaned promptly.

### Deep cleaning and maintenance
For deeper cleaning, a professional rug cleaning service is a wise option if your rug is large, heavy, and you’re concerned about preserving the dye across multiple years. If you’d rather DIY, check for a manufacturer-approved care guide and follow it to the letter. The goal is to maintain fiber integrity while preserving the rug’s bold color story. Dry-cleaning are not typically needed for synthetic fibers; hydro-based methods with caution are usually enough, but always verify the fiber content and cleaning instructions before committing.

### Longevity tips
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color exposure.
- Use furniture coasters or felt pads to reduce indentations and snags caused by heavy chairs.
- Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods to minimize fading of the colors. If you can’t avoid sunlight, use blinds or curtains to control intensity and keep the rug looking fresh.
