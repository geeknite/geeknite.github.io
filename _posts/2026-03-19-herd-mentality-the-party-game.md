Herd Mentality is the party game that asks a simple question: what would the herd do? The answer is usually: whatever the majority did, even if it makes no sense. If you want to test your social radar, your sense of self, and your ability to shout I meant to write cat in a room full of friends, you’ve found your destiny.

## What is Herd Mentality?
Herd Mentality is a light, fast-paced party game designed for big crowds, small egos, and people who have never learned to pronounce allegory without sounding like a teapot. The premise is simple: players respond to prompts with a single answer. Then everyone simultaneously reveals their answers, and the goal is to choose the most common—but not necessarily correct—answer, while still keeping the group’s vibes intact.

The deck includes prompts ranging from the silly to the surprisingly philosophical: (A place where you would most like to disappear) or (If your life had a theme song, what would it be?). The humor is baked in; the game expects you to be a human and also a tiny bit of a show-off. The real magic is the social phenomenon: the herd swallows the first answer whole and you decide whether to ride that current or pretend you were never part of the wave.

## Why the herd is irresistible
Humans are social animals, and the herd mentality game taps into the thrill of consensus with a dash of social pressure. The more you play, the more you notice the patterns: people like to pick familiar, safe answers — or wildly creative ones if they’re feeling spicy. Either way, it’s a study in group psychology performed with snacks and inside jokes. ## Gameplay overview: setup, rounds, and scoring
The game shines in large groups and on chaotic evenings. Here’s a practical guide to playing without causing an international incident.

### Setup
- Shuffle prompts and stack them face down.
- Each round, a reader is chosen. The reader writes their own answer to the prompt on a sheet.
- The remaining players write their own answers on slips of paper. No peeking, no forcing your roommate to reveal their deepest secret before coffee.

### Rounds and revelation
- The reader reveals the prompts and collects all the answer slips.
- Everyone votes for the answer they believe matches the majority. It’s not about yelling the loudest; it’s about predicting the crowd’s collective pulse.

### Scoring
- Points go to players whose answers match the majority. If you guessed a common answer, you typically score; if your answer is too obscure, you might end up with nothing—except bragging rights and the word maverick used in jest.

### Variants and expansions
- If your group wants more chaos, expansions add new prompts and scoring twists. It’s like adding a few extra fireworks to a birthday party: you’ll get more wow, but you’ll also have to clean up more glitter.

- If you crave deep strategy, look elsewhere. If you want a social experience that yields big laughs with minimal overhead, this is your jam.
