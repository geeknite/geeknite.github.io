### The Phase Structure and Card Design
The Phase 10 deck remains faithful to its core gameplay. The ten phases typically include combinations like two sets of three, a run of seven, and other patterns that require players to balance risk and planning. In the 25th anniversary edition, the card art and the back-of-card design carry the vestiges of mid-2000s graphic design, with bold color blocks, stylized numbers, and a sense of punch that helps you tell your piles from your opponents’ piles in a sea of multi-colored cards.

The deck’s Wild and Skip cards are essential tactical tools. Wild cards allow you to substitute any needed card to complete a phase, while Skip cards disrupt opponents and can shift the rhythm of the game. As with most Phase 10 variants, the sequencing of phases remains the same: players progress through each phase in order, encountering the occasional setback when an opponent skips their turn or when you draw a throwaway card that ruins your carefully laid plan.
