## Gameplay Mechanics: Core Loop with a Twist

### The deck-building engine

At its heart, the deck-building loop remains familiar: you draw a hand from your deck, you play actions to acquire new cards, you discard, and you cycle components through the discard pile. The Altered Edition adds a twist: Alterations can modify how draws happen, which cards are available for purchase, or how points are calculated. It’s like playing chess while a mischievous roommate rearranges the board after every move.

The core tactical moment comes when you decide which cards to purchase or play now, and which to save for later with altered costs or benefits. In a normal game, you might optimize for a clean engine, but in the Altered Edition you’ll discover that sometimes the best move is to buy a card you don’t immediately need because the Alteration of the round makes it suddenly priceless.

### The Alterations: how chaos enters the room

Alterations arrive as a color-coded overlay on the play area, and they stay active for a chunk of turns. They can do things like:

- Swap the order of card draws for the round.
- Change the cost curve of certain resource types.
- Gift a temporary power that disrupts the usual synergy on your board.
- Introduce a risk-reward mechanic where you can pay a cost to do something much more powerful—if your next turn goes wrong, the cost compounds.

The first few rounds contribute to a learning curve that feels fair and exciting. The Alterations aren’t random to a fault; there’s a rhythm to how they reveal themselves, and a certain satisfaction in predicting which Alteration will be active on which turn. The game rewards players who adopt a flexible strategy—your engine should be adaptable rather than hyper-optimized.

### Hand management under pressure

Because Alterations can influence what you can do next, hand management becomes a mental puzzle rather than a simple resource management problem. You’ll often find yourself holding back a card until a future Alteration reveals a high-value synergy—or deciding to burn a suboptimal card to prevent a worse disaster from hitting you later in the round.

The design team does a commendable job of ensuring that even when you feel forced into a subpar play, you can justify it through a chain of thought that feels logical. It’s not just luck; there’s a skill floor that rises with your familiarity with the Alterations, and a ceiling that rewards bold, high-variance plays when the stars align.

## Play Experience: A Field Report

Let me walk you through a hypothetical round to illustrate how the Altered Edition feels in practice. Imagine you’ve drawn a hand that signals a strong early engine, but the Alterations currently on the table favor mid-game tempo and disruption.

- Round 1: You start with a practical starter set. Your first move is to grab a card that chains into a future combo, but an Alteration active this round increases the cost of that exact card by 1 for every card in your hand. You adjust by pivoting to a different card with a safer early payoff.
- Round 2: An Alteration hits that reduces the cost of all offense cards, which suddenly makes your previously marginal engine viable. You pivot to a more aggressive path, buying a card that previously looked like a luxury but now produces a strong draw engine.
- Round 3: A disruption round. Your chosen engine hits a snag as you’re forced to discard a key card to satisfy a status effect. You decide to bite the bullet and press forward with a suboptimal hand, hoping the next Alteration will restore balance.
- Round 4+: The table enters a rhythm where players anticipate which Alterations will be active, and the deck becomes a chessboard of risk and reward. A well-timed draw can unlock a brutal late-game chain that wins you the round before the chaos consumes you.

This is not a game where you watch a single oak-tree of strategy grow; it’s a forest with shifting weather. The Alterations change the climate, and your strategy has to pivot with weather reports that are only partly predictable. The result is a dynamic that feels alive rather than scripted.

## Strategy Tips and Tactics

- Embrace disruption as a resource: Alterations aren’t obstacles to ignore; they’re potential catalysts. Look for cards that create synergy specifically when Alterations are active.
- Build flexibility into your engine: Prioritize cards that give you flexible paths to victory. Cards with multiple uses become invaluable when the Alterations swing the balance on a given turn.
- Timing is everything: Don’t chase a late-game plan too early. The Altered Edition often rewards patience and opportunistic plays that align with the current Alteration set.
