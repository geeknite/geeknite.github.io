## Durability and Maintenance
Durability is where the Kendall Oatmeal shines in practical terms, with caveats that are worth discussing openly. Polypropylene fibers are known for being resilient, water-repellent on a microscopic scale, and reasonably stain-resistant. Real-world tests show that common spills (coffee, red wine, the occasional smoothie disaster) can be dealt with quickly if you respond in time. The key is to blot—don’t rub—and use a mild detergent. The rug’s color helps to hide minor splashes and paw prints, though it is not a magic shield against pet messes, glitter, or the occasional DIY project’s decoupage mishap.

Cleaning-wise, you’ll want to vacuum regularly with a standard upright or canister vacuum. Because the pile is low, you’ll avoid suction issues and the dreaded “rug edge folds” that plague high-pile mats. For larger stains, some Geeknite reviewers tested a gentle steam-clean approach. Results varied by stain type and the occupant’s willingness to wait for air-drying, but most reports indicated that the rug recovers nicely after a careful treatment. If you have pets or kids, a rotating schedule for cleaning improves longevity and keeps the palette looking fresh.

Tip: a protective rug pad under the Kendall Oatmeal enhances grip, reduces sliding on slick floors, and dampens footstep noise. A good rug pad can transform the feel of the rug—and your hallway traffic from a clattery chaos to a smooth, almost cinematic glide. ## Size, Fit, and Room Placement
Size choice matters with any rug purchase, and Kendall Oatmeal is no exception. A 5x8 works brilliantly in smaller living rooms or under a coffee table, giving you enough space to anchor the seating area without overwhelming the floor plan. An 8x10 rug broadens the footprint for larger living rooms, dining nooks, or open-concept spaces where you want to unify seating and traffic with a single, soft surface underfoot. If you’re unsure, there’s a quick rule of thumb: leave at least 18–24 inches of bare floor around the rug in a typical living space to create a balanced, room-defining border. If you’re placing the rug under a dining table, plan for a 24-inch extra clearance beyond the chairs so you don’t trip when you scoot out for a snack.

For those with hallways or entryways that see frequent shoe traffic, a smaller version under a console or a mudroom can be a perfect fit. The key is to ensure the rug rests flat and doesn’t curl at the edges, which is both safer and more aesthetically pleasing in photos.

## Cleaning and Care Tips
- Vacuum regularly to remove dirt before it embeds. For high-traffic areas, consider a more frequent schedule.
- Blot spills immediately with a dry cloth, then clean with a mild detergent and warm water. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade color or fiber.
- For larger stains, a light steam clean can refresh the fibers, but ensure the rug dries completely to prevent mold.
- Use a rug pad for better longevity and to reduce shifting that can lead to edge curling.
- Rotate periodically to even out wear and fading from sunlight.
