## Weight, balance, and the physics of a good stack
14 grams per chip means a stack of 20 chips weighs almost 280 grams, which is roughly the weight of a small paperback and just far enough to remind you you’re playing with actual, tangible stakes. That weight lends predictability: when you push a pile forward, it doesn’t “go the distance” like a stack of lighter chips. It stays put, which makes dealing and bet calls feel deliberate rather than slapdash.

Balance is another factor. In well-made sets, chips are uniformly weighted so the center of mass remains constant mid-shovel, shuffle, or dash to the chip rack. A sloppy batch might have a few lighter chips that wobble when you’re counting out a pot, which can lead to the dreaded “nerdy calculator moment” where you count twice because a chip rolled into a crevice you didn’t anticipate. With a quality clay-like chip at 14 g, you minimize those miscounts and keep the table vibe elegant, even during the inevitable miscalls.

## Care, maintenance, and keeping chips looking sharp
Clay-based chips love minimal care and a dry environment. A quick wipe with a soft microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints and dust is enough for most home use. If you’re in a humid area or you’ve just finished a particularly sweaty session (we won’t tell anyone), you can wipe with a lightly damp cloth and then dry thoroughly. Avoid soaking or submerging the chips—the edge paint can wear, and the color codes can bleed if the finishes aren’t sealed. You’re aiming for rugged elegance, not a rustic palette meltdown.

Pro tip: store chips in a cool, dry place. If your home doubles as a laboratory of coffee and spilled soda experiments, the clay-like surface can absorb odors; a quick airing-out between nights helps keep the chips smelling non-descript and poker-table-anonymous.
