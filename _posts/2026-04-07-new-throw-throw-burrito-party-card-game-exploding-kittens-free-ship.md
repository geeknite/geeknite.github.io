## What is Throw Throw Burrito?

Throw Throw Burrito is a party game that asks you to both mind games and physical games at the same time. You will be handed a deck of cards with various character icons and color-coded actions, plus two foam burritos with which you will eventually fling your way to glory or embarrassment, depending on your athletic ability and your willingness to chase a foam burrito around a coffee table while screaming a victory chant like you are in a zany sports commercial.

The core mechanic is deceptively simple: you draw cards, you try to form sets and pairs, and when a pair is formed and the right conditions are met, you trigger a throw that sends a burrito sailing toward another player. The Burrito Throw moments are the heart of the chaos; it is in these moments that the table erupts in laughter, shrieks, and a few scared gasps as someone’s drink accidentally takes a flying burrito to the face (careful with the beverages, folks). The cards themselves are colorful, cartoonish, and designed to be friendly to both kids and adults, which is perhaps the most dangerous part of the game: it is so approachable that your aunt will want to play and then suddenly you are stuck explaining why there is a burrito in your living room and why the dog now thinks it is a chew toy.

And yes, there are real burritos in the game—foam burritos that bounce and wobble in the most satisfying way possible. If you have ever wanted to recreate a chaotic food fight in your own home without cleaning up actual nacho cheese, this is your moment. The burritos are soft enough not to cause harm (please supervise, kids under 6, mention to your insurance policy that you have purchased a burrito-based chaos system for family night).

The art direction is bright and slightly goofy, with the same lovable, exaggerated cartoon vibe that Exploding Kittens is known for. The box art pops off the shelf, a crucial factor when you are trying to grab attention in a crowded game night lineup. If you are into the aesthetic of finger food friendly chaos and cute animals doing ridiculous things, Throw Throw Burrito leans hard into that vibe.

## How to play: a high-energy overview

We wont pretend this is a dry, academic exercise. If you are here for the rules, you will find them in the quick-start section, but the real joy comes from how it feels to play a game where the burritos fly around the room and everyone acts like they are auditioning for a chaotic game show.

- Objective: Get enough points by successfully forming cards and triggering non-catastrophic burrito throws. The exact scoring can vary by house rules, but the thrust remains: keep collecting and dodging until someone finishes a round with a high score.
- Turn structure: Players draw two cards from the deck, with the option to draw additional cards or manipulate the board state through special actions. The deck includes action cards that can force someone to draw again, steal a burrito, or trigger a rapid-fire round where burritos are launched in rapid succession.
- The score a burrito moment: When a player matches a set of two cards, they trigger a burrito throw from a central pool. The target is chosen by the card text or by a house rule you all agree on pre-game. The result is a flurry of soft projectiles and shouts that would make a sports event blush.
- Endgame and victory: The game usually ends after a few rounds or when a time limit is reached. The player with the highest score wins. In practice, even the loser gets a story, which is the real treasure here.

Strategies, if you care about them (and let us be honest, you likely do):

- Move with purpose: In Toss-N-Go evenings, you will want to position yourself so you are not the easiest target for a last-minute burrito launch. This means sieving opponents by proximity and factoring in who has already thrown a burrito and who is more likely to miss entirely.
- Card counting lite: You do not need to be a math prodigy, but tracking who has drawn what can help you anticipate when a burrito storm is brewing. If you notice someone has been quiet for a while, they might be saving up for a dramatic finish—and that is your cue to either alliance or target them.
- Don’t overcommit: The more you throw, the more you will attract attention and heat. It is better to be a surgical burrito thrower—precision over volume—unless you are playing with a group that loves chaos, in which case volume is the best strategy.
- Read the room: The funniest part of the game is watching people react to the burritos. You want to be the one who keeps a straight face while calmly grabbing the foam projectile to deliver a dramatic arc into someone’s hairline—metaphorically, of course.

## Throw Throw Burrito vs Exploding Kittens: a funny, fiery cousin rivalry

If Exploding Kittens taught us anything, it is that a simple card game about a cartoon kitten with a killer appetite can become a cultural phenomenon with a few clever upgrades. Throw Throw Burrito takes that same DNA and turbocharges it with physical mischief. Here is how the two compare in a few key categories:

- Theme and tone: Exploding Kittens leans into absurd humor with a darker edge, while Throw Throw Burrito doubles down on physical silliness. If you want a quick, tactile party experience with a strong performance from your shoulder muscles, Burrito wins. If you want clever cards, cute kittens, and a dash of strategic deception, Exploding Kittens holds the crown.

You will see friends collide with burritos, wrestle for control of a dumpling-like artifact, and shout like you just discovered a new species of comedic energy. Exploding Kittens focuses on negotiation, risk management, and the joy of not drawing the bomb card—an experience that is more about brain and risk assessment than elbow grease.
- Longevity and expansions: Exploding Kittens has expanded into multiple editions and expansions, offering countless hours of gameplay. Throw Throw Burrito has fewer expansions but keeps the core energy: you can keep getting together for a loud, goofy night and still feel like you are playing a brand-new game every time due to the chaotic burrito element.

We love both for different reasons. If you want something that gets your heart racing and your dog trying to figure out whether you are playing a game or conducting a foam-burrito experiment, Throw Throw Burrito is your jam. If you want a game-night staple you can dissect at length, with light strategy and a lovable misfit vibe, Exploding Kittens is a safe, silly bet.

## Accessibility, safety, and space planning

A major strength of Throw Throw Burrito is that it is accessible to a wide audience. The rules are straightforward enough for a first-time player to pick up in a single round, and the physical components do most of the heavy lifting when it comes to engagement. You do not need complicated strategies to enjoy a good match; you only need a willingness to shout, laugh, and occasionally claim a dramatic victory with the swagger of a tournament champion.

That said, there are a few practical notes:

- Space: You will want some breathing room. If you are playing in a cramped apartment, you will quickly find out which coffee table corners are best for ricocheting burritos. If you have kids or pets, make sure to designate a burrito-free zone for the off-phase where the cards are being shuffled.
- Noise: This is not a whisper-game. Expect laughter, shouting, and the occasional scream of delight when someone dodges a burrito by inches. It is a party game, so you should be prepared to lean into the chaos.
- Safety: Foam burritos are generally safe, but you should still supervise children to prevent them from using the burritos as improvised juggling clubs. A quick reminder for adults: this is not a substitute for actual dodgeball; be kind to your furniture and your neighbors.
- Skill requirements: There is no heavy cognitive load here. The rules are light, the pace is quick, and the energy is high. If you are worried about losing a card or missing a throw, remember: this is a party game that prizes enthusiasm over precision.
