## How to Play UNO Attack

If you know UNO, you know most of what you need to know. Pick seven cards, match color or number, and play your turn. When you cannot play, you draw. The difference in UNO Attack is the draw phase. Instead of drawing from a normal deck, you load the attacker with a deck and press the draw button to release a variable number of cards onto the table.

The shooter is controlled by the draw button, a mechanism that adds an extra layer of tension. On a turn where a player cannot play, they press the button and see what fate has in store. The machine might spit out nothing at all, or a cascade of cards that dings, bings, and clatters across the table. It is part luck, part battle of nerves, and part theater.

In addition to the classic UNO cards like skip, reverse, draw two, and the wilds, the Uno Attack deck includes cards that specifically interact with the shooter. Some cards force all players to draw from the shooter, while others alter the direction of play and the number of cards that the shooter will dump on you. The exact distribution can vary by edition, but the vibe remains the same: you will either win with a clean late run or be buried beneath a literal avalanche of cards.

## House Rules and Variants

If you want to tweak UNO Attack for your group, here are a few ideas:

- The Quiet Round: designate a few rounds where the shooter is disabled and standard UNO rules apply. This gives players a breather and helps avoid fatigue.

- The Shooter Roulette: introduce a house rule that the shooter only triggers when the deck is tapped, making the number of cards drawn more predictable and still fun.

- The Bonus Draws: if someone plays a certain card, everyone passes a card to the player on their left, adding an extra layer of tension to the game.

- The Speed Round: set a timer and try to finish a round before the timer rings. The shooter becomes a peripheral role.

- Partner Play: pair up players and coordinate to exhaust your team’s cards. You can still use the shooter, but the team strategy takes center stage.

- Mixed Deck Mayhem: mix in standard UNO cards with Attack cards from a second deck for an on-brand chaotic mashup.
