## Maintenance and reliability notes

- Regular belt tension checks will preserve smooth operation and extend belt life. A belt that’s too loose or too tight can ruin your run experience fast.
- Wipe down the console and deck after sessions to prevent sticky buildup and ensure sensors stay responsive.
- Periodically calibrate incline accuracy if you’re doing long-term training. Inaccurate incline numbers aren’t dangerous, but they’re demotivating when your GPS says you climbed 800 feet and you know it was a stair-step away from the couch.
- Firmware updates are your friend. They fix bugs, add features, and sometimes improve energy efficiency—handy for those who like to run yet care about power bills.

If you’re thoughtful about maintenance, you’ll extend the life of your treadmill and your sanity. A well-kept unit feels like a well-tuned instrument—less squeak, more peak performance, and more poetry in your step.

## Common issues and quick fixes

Here are a few realistic niggles you might encounter and how to handle them without summoning a tech squad:

- Screen lag or unresponsiveness: Give it a solid restart and check for firmware updates. If the lag persists, clear cache or reset the app settings. If all else fails, you can still use the treadmill in manual mode—yes, the machine still runs without the app, and no, you won’t crumble into a chair the moment you’re not connected.
- Wifi drops during workout: Reconnect and ensure the router is stable. If you’re living in a high-congestion area, consider a maintenance-free approach like a dedicated 2.4 GHz band for the treadmill. It’s not glamorous, but it works.
- Unusual belt sounds: A little squeak isn’t unusual, but a grinding noise is a red flag. Stop immediately, inspect belt tension, and check for debris. If you’re unsure, consult the manual and arrange a service window—don’t ignore unusual noises, or you’ll end up with a treadmill that chews your favorite socks.
- Dashboard data mismatch: Calibrate, refresh, and re-sync. Data is nice, but it’s not worth obsessing over. The goal is consistent effort, not martyrdom over a few extra steps.
