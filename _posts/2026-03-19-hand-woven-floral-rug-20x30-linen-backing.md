Care and longevity: keeping the florals blooming
Care is simpler than you might imagine for a hand woven, linen-backed rug. Here are the essentials:
- Vacuum regularly with a non-beater attachment to protect fibers from deep-set dirt. Light, frequent passes beat dirt into oblivion while preserving the weave’s integrity.
- Rotate every few months to ensure even wear and to prevent color fade from sun exposure. If your room floods with daylight, consider rotating more frequently or using window treatments during peak sun hours.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing can push the stain deeper and disrupt the weave’s microstructure. For stubborn spills, use a mild detergent solution and test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- Avoid harsh chemical cleaners. They can degrade natural fibers and affect colorfastness. When in doubt, consult a rug care guide or a professional conservator.
- For deeper refreshes, a professional rug cleaning every 1-2 years (depending on foot traffic and pet activity) will help maintain color vibrancy and the fibers’ health.
