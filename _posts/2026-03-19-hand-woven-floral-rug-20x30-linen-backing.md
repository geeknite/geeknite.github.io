Care and longevity: keeping the florals blooming
Care is simpler than you’d expect for a hand woven, linen-backed rug. Here are the essentials:
- Vacuum regularly with a non-beater attachment to avoid damaging the fibers. Frequent, gentle passes beat deep dirt into oblivion while preserving the weave’s integrity.
- Rotate every few months to ensure even wear and to prevent color fading from sun exposure. If your room gets a lot of sunlight, consider rotating more often or using curtains during peak sun hours.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Don’t rub; you’ll push the stain deeper and disrupt the weave’s microstructure. For stubborn spills, use a mild detergent solution and test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- Avoid harsh chemical cleaners. They can degrade natural fibers and affect colorfastness. If in doubt, consult a rug care guide or talk to a professional conservator.
- For deeper refreshes, a professional rug cleaning every 1-2 years (depending on foot traffic and pet activity) will help keep the colors vibrant and the fibers healthy.

Color and pattern integrity: a note on fading and dye behavior
Floral patterns tend to hide minor fading better than solid colors because the eye brackets the edges of the motifs. Still, linens are responsive to UV exposure. If your rug sits in direct sun for extended periods, expect some lightening of the edges or certain color shifts over time. To preserve vibrancy, rotate, install curtains, or use window treatments during peak sun hours. If you want a rug that stays purple forever, you probably need to choose a synthetic option; but if you want character, you’ll enjoy the gentle aging that adds depth and story to the piece.
