### Ergonomics a.k.a. human-friendly torture or rescue mission?
The ergonomic story here is a mixed bag. On the bright side, you get shared legroom for the five who sit in a semi-circle around the table. That is wonderful for brainstorming sessions where everyone wants to chime in about a single design concept while also presenting their own unique perspective on clipboard physics. On the not-so-bright side, you don’t get personal desk height the way you do with individual desks; the height is fixed, and you’ll have to accommodate the five humans of varying heights through chair settings and possibly a stubborn awareness that your posture will be tested by group dynamics.

The W Screen is a big win for collaboration when it works as a single aggregated display, but you’ll want to ensure that every user is not fighting over the same in-frame content. A well-run session includes a rotating “owner of the frame” dynamic so that nobody claims the screen forever. If you’re a team that enjoys long-form, multi-person screen sessions where the same person dominates the feed, this desk will let you comfortably host a five-person screen session with enough glare to remind you of the sun in a desert. It’s all about team choreography: who sits closest, who controls the cursor, and who gets the call to reset the screen when the Wi-Fi throws a tantrum.

## Cable management and power strategy: tame the tangle

One of the biggest pain points with any mega-desk is power and cable management. The Infinite 5P desk ships with a built-in trough and a modular cable tray, which is a small miracle when you’re dealing with five workstations, five monitors (one shared screen among them), multiple docking stations, and several charging jackets for your devices. The trough is designed to keep the wind from your cables—by wind, we mean the chaos you’d otherwise get from five people plugging in and unplugging during a hackathon.

In practice, you’ll want to pre-plan power distribution. A high-density power strip placed in a central hub with surge protection is your friend. Label cables, use color-coding for the five zones, and consider a small, quiet UPS for the W Screen so you don’t watch your entire sprint crash mid-demo because of a power dip. It’s not glamorous, but it’s the difference between a successful showcase and a duct-taped chaos symphony.

But for deep work that requires blocks of uninterrupted time, individuals may need to retreat to a side room or adopt a portable privacy screen. The scale of the desk makes it less than ideal for solo sprints, but absolutely excellent for team alignment sessions when you need to see everyone’s expressions and reactions in real time.

If your team is into cross-functional collaboration and you have the space, the Infinite 5P is a spectacular stage for your theatre of work.
