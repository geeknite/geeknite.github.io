## Durability and Maintenance

### Cleaning and stains

Sisal is tough but not invincible. It hates excessive moisture and loves quick, decisive action. Spills should be blotted up as soon as possible with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers. For dry stains or ground-in dirt, a gentle vacuum or a soft brush works wonders. If a stain proves stubborn, you’ll want to consult a professional cleaner who has a natural-fiber playbook rather than haphazardly scrubbing with household chemicals.

### Vacuuming and upkeep

Vacuuming is your friend here, but you’ll want to adjust your technique. Use a low or medium suction to avoid catching on the weave. A brush head can help lift surface dust without damaging the fibers. Regular vacuuming keeps the rug looking fresh and prevents dirt from embedding in the texture, which can turn a hero rug into a grimy relic faster than you can say “permanent pet odor.”

### Odors and weathering

Natural fibers can pick up scents, especially in open-plan homes or spaces with cooking fume activity. The cure is regular airings and, when possible, flipping or rotating the rug to distribute wear and exposure evenly. In damp climates, keep an eye on moisture buildup; if you notice any mildew smell, take action quickly and consider stepping up the drying routine after cleaning.
