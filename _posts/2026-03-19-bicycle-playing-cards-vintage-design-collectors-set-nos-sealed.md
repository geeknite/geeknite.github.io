## Care and Preservation
Preservation is king when it comes to NOS NOS sets. Here are practical steps to keep your treasure in minty condition while still enjoying it visually:
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV light is a silent saboteur of colors and finishes.
- Avoid high humidity; an ideal range is roughly 40–60% relative humidity to minimize warping, curling, or mold growth on the packaging.
- Keep the deck out of the jawline of enthusiastic card-throwers who like to test the archival quality of a tuck box with a screwdriver bite. This is not a multitasking task for your countertop.
- If you handle the deck regularly, wash hands before dealing so as not to transfer oils that could accumulate in the corners over time.
- Display case or shadow box: a glass-front display helps you appreciate the art while keeping dust at bay.

For display purposes, the box makes a fine piece of still-life art on its own. If you decide to keep a few decks on a shelf, consider labeling each one with its year of production for a quick “time travel timeline” when friends ask about the piece they just found in your cabinet.

## Authentication and Value-Tracking Tips
- Inspect the seal: an intact, undisturbed seal is your best friend. A broken seal may not ruin the deck’s usability, but it does alter the resale value and the aura of collectibility.
- Look for consistent printing traits: the tuck box print, the border lines, the color balance on the back design. If something looks off or wrong for a vintage Bicycle, you may be looking at a reprint or a later edition.
- Check the card edges and corners: even if sealed, some wear on the outer corners can occur during storage. If you can view the deck without breaking the seal (some sellers provide photos of the inner edges), it helps to verify the condition.
- Ask for documentation: a quick note about the year, the printer, and the print run can add a lot of credible value to the listing.

## Care, Handling, and Display Recommendations
- For the shelf: a padded display case or a shallow shadow box helps prevent direct contact while allowing your deck to be admired.
