## Practical care tips: keeping the navy honest and the ivory bright

Maintenance matters, especially when you’re dealing with two colors that can reveal everything from dust to coffee spills. Here are practical tips to keep the rug looking as good as possible:

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting to avoid pulling fibers. The rule of thumb is to vacuum once or twice a week, more if you’ve got a sport-coat-wearing cat who enjoys dramatic leaps onto the rug.
- Treat spills fast. Blot—do not rub—with a clean, white cloth. Mild soap and water can help with shopping-day messes; test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate every few months to minimize wear patterns. Even distribution keeps the navy from looking worn in the most traveled patches while the ivory remains relatively bright.
- Consider a rug pad. It reduces slipping and extends wear, which is essential if you’ve got hardwood floors that become a dance floor when you’re entertaining.

Care isn’t glamorous, but it’s the adult version of a power-up in a video game. A few minutes of attention now saves you money later and keeps your living room from looking like a thrifty theater set that’s gone a bit too passionate with its color story.
