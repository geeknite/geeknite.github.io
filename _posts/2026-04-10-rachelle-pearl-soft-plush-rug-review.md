## Materials and build quality

The Rachelle Pearl has a two-layer construction: a plush, velvet-like pile on top and a soft backing that keeps it anchored to floor tiles and wood floors. The top layer uses polyester microfiber, chosen for its combination of softness and stain resistance. Polyester fibers are spun into a dense loop that traps air, creating that pillow-y cushion underfoot. The backing is a non-slip material, designed to minimize shifting on smooth floors.

In the lab (okay, our living room with a tape measure and a dog), we tested the rug for bounce, resilience, and surface pickup. ### Practical maintenance tips
- Routine vacuuming: weekly, with a shopper-friendly vacuum on low power to avoid pilling. 
- Spot cleaning: blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber breakage; a mild detergent solution helps remove stubborn stains.
- Deep cleaning: machine washing on a gentle cycle if you’re brave; air drying preferred to maintain fluffiness.

## Durability and wear

The rug’s high-pile plush fiber is resilient, but not invincible. Regular foot traffic will flatten the center if left unaddressed. To extend life: rotate the rug periodically and avoid dragging heavy furniture across it. The backing is designed to hold tight; you won’t see it bunching or curling at the edges after a month, which is the dream of every rug owner.

### Common wear patterns and how to manage them
If you notice high-traffic areas starting to look a little less fluffy, try a quick plump-up with a steam iron at a low setting, using a barrier cloth between the rug and iron. Just kidding—follow the manufacturer’s cleaning guide, not your impulse to invent DIY magic. In practice, occasional steam refresh can help re-fluff fibers, but be mindful of heat and moisture.
