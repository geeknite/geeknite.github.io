## Assembly and installation: the saga of parts, screws, and the one-man mission

As with any modular office furniture, the true test lies in assembly: how long does it take, how many tools do you need, and how likely are you to accidentally assemble a panel backward while balancing a coffee mug on your knee? The Version V is marketed with “one-man setup” in mind, but let’s be real: you’ll probably want a helpful friend to pass you screws and a level, and maybe a snack.

What you can expect in the box:
- Two main desk surfaces (the long and the short leg)
- The tempered-glass panels and their aluminum frames
- A set of instructions with pictures that resemble IKEA’s, but with a few more screws that mysteriously vanish during the first step
- A bag of hardware: screws, anchors, grommets, and cable-tie cards

Assembly flow (typical):
1) Unpack and verify all components against the parts list. The last thing you want is a missing cam connector when you’re balancing a panel on your knee.
2) Install the main frame on a level surface. This is where a spirit level becomes your friend and the dog looks at you like you’ve invited chaos into the living room.
3) Attach the desk surfaces and ensure the L-shape aligns with your intended orientation. If you’ve chosen the right-hand layout, make sure you’re not accidentally building a left-handed private jet.
4) Insert the glass panels and secure them with the provided hardware. Do not overtighten; tempered glass hates panic and overzealous clamps. A gentle snug is usually enough.
5) Cable management: thread power and data cables through grommets or the back channels. If you’re fancy, install a vertical cable spine to keep the tangle-monster away from your keyboard and coffee mug.
6) Final checks: ensure doors glide smoothly (if there are doors) and the panels don’t rattle with your chair’s every movement. A quick test with a swivel seat will reveal any alignment quirks.

In practice, most one-person setups report around 2–4 hours from unboxing to a fully usable workspace, assuming you’re not battling the universal office gremlins that love to hide small hex wrenches in the couch cushions. Pro tip: sort hardware by type before you start, and keep a trash bag handy for packaging and the inevitable stray screws that roll under the desk like tiny metallic planets.

## Glass, safety, and the mood you want to set in your cubicle

The glazing is tempered for safety and designed to resist scratches from your chair wheels and the occasional frantic mouse-drag across the desk. Practical tips for keeping your glass pristine:
- Wipe with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaning solution. Avoid ammonia-based cleaners that can fog the glass over time.
- Be mindful of sharp-edged accessories that could nick the glass during rearrangements.
- Use a desk mat to protect the wood or laminate surfaces at the base of the glass panels.

From a safety perspective, tempered glass is a solid choice for office furniture, offering resistance to everyday bumps and preventing catastrophic shards in the unlikely event of a panel impact. ## Maintenance and care: keeping your 4x4 unit looking crisp

Keeping the unit looking fresh is mostly about routine cleaning and mindful cable management. Here are practical quick wins:
- Regular dusting of the frame with a microfiber cloth keeps the powder-coated finish looking sharp.
- Glass panels: a weekly wipe-down with a non-ammonia cleaner preserves the clarity and reduces glare. Always dry thoroughly to avoid water streaks that look like a sci-fi map of the crevices of your desk.
- Cable management: use cable sleeves or Velcro ties to minimize cord clutter and reduce the risk of cables snagging on chair wheels.
- Surface care: a gentle cleaner on the desk surfaces helps prevent staining from coffee, tea, or the occasional late-night ramen run.

Maintenance doesn’t have to be ceremonial. A five-minute weekly routine beats a chaotic, monthly meltdown where you realize you’ve accidentally created a data-spiral of sticky notes and empty coffee cups.
