## Core features of the Fortune Row digital sheets

### Core Functionality

- Dynamic masks and personas: The sheets present the classic City of Mist slice of life—masks, alter egos, and the mythic powers—without forcing you to flip through ten different PDFs. A central dashboard keeps your major tags, power lists, and style phrases in one place. 
- Auto stat tracking: Your boxes refresh as you gain clues, overcome obstacles, or suffer trauma. No more cross-referencing six sheets to figure out the current edge or grit level. The sheet recalculates based on tags and stat combos, which is the sort of magic you miss when your GM hands you a cigarette and says, Good luck.
- Condition and trauma management: Little markers drift onto the sheet as narrative damage piles up. It feels like a progress bar for storytelling rather than an ominous spreadsheet doom.
- Initiative and combat quick-reads: A glance can tell your character’s action window, which is incredibly handy when the city itself seems to be throwing stop signs at you while a subwoofer hums in the background.

## Practical design notes and inclusions

Fortune Row digital sheets do not pretend to solve every table problem with a click. They are a tool for banding the table together, not replacing the storytellers. A few design choices stand out:

- The balance between automation and narrative control feels right. It streamlines the mechanical layer while still giving players and GMs space to improvise and escalate. 
- The UI gracefully declines to become a dungeon crawl of options. It focuses on the core elements that matter in a City of Mist session: masks, clues, and the mythic spark.
- Accessibility is not an afterthought. Color contrast, font legibility, and keyboard navigability are treated with care so you don’t get lost in a sea of neon letters.

While it is not a perfect product, the emphasis on a strong, readable design makes it a solid improvement over the old methods. If you have tried to juggle a GM screen, a laptop, and a spreadsheet all at once, you know the relief of seeing a single interface do most of the heavy lifting.
