## Care and maintenance
Care for a display or cosplay dagger is straightforward but benefits from consistency. Practical tips to keep your Quilan looking sharp and ready for the next con moment:

- Regularly wipe the blade with a dry microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints and moisture. This minimizes streaks and corrosion risk on coated surfaces.
- Apply a light coat of blade-safe oil after cleaning. A thin layer protects the finish without creating a gummy residue on the grip or sheath.
- Store in a dry, cool place away from humidity to preserve finish and prevent rust on any exposed metal.
- Inspect the tang and fittings from time to time. If you notice looseness or wobble in the handle, do not attempt to tighten yourself. Seek professional help or contact the retailer for guidance.
- When traveling or transporting for events, keep the dagger in its sheath and secured. A loose blade inside a bag invites scratches and potential damage to other items.

### Cleaning pitfalls to avoid
- Avoid aggressive solvents that can damage finishes or degrade synthetic handles.
- Do not soak the blade for extended periods. Water exposure can lead to staining or edge oxidation on coatings.
- Do not attempt to modify the blade or handle with tools you are uncomfortable using. A quick touch of a Dremel can ruin the finish and affect balance.
