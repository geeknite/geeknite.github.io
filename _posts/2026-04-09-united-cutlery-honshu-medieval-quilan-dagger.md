## Care and maintenance
Maintenance for a display or cosplay dagger is straightforward, but consistent. Here are practical tips to keep your Quilan looking sharp and ready for the next convention moment:

- Regularly wipe the blade with a dry microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints and moisture. This minimizes streaks and corrosion risk on coated surfaces.
- Use a light coat of oil designed for blades after cleaning. A thin layer is enough to protect the finish without creating a gummy residue on the grip or sheath.
- Keep the blade away from humidity if you are storing it long term. A dry, cool place is ideal for preserving the finish and preventing rust on any exposed metal.
- Inspect the tang and fittings from time to time. If you notice any looseness or wobble in the handle, do not force adjustments yourself. Seek professional help or contact the retailer for guidance on replacement hardware.
- When traveling or transporting to events, keep the dagger in its sheath and secure. A loose blade inside a bag can lead to scratches on the blade or sheath and increases the risk of damage to other belongings.

### Cleaning pitfalls to avoid
- Avoid aggressive solvents that can damage finishes or degrade the synthetic handle materials.
- Do not soak the blade for extended periods. Water exposure, especially with titanium-like coatings, can lead to staining or edge oxidation.
- Do not attempt to modify the blade or handle with tools you aren’t comfortable using. A quick flourish of a Dremel can ruin the finish and affect balance.
