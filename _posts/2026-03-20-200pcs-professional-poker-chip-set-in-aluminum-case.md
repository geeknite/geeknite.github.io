## Material quality and design: do they feel premium?

The chips themselves generally use a resin or clay composite core with a ceramic-like or “edge spot” finish that’s a notch above cheap plastic. In practice, you’ll notice a few things:

- Weight and feel: most 200-chip sets aim for a weight around 10–12 grams per chip. This makes the stack feel substantial without turning your wrist into a hydraulic press. It’s enough heft to signify “serious play” without the drifting sensation you get from lighter, cheaper sets.
- Edge spots and aesthetics: the edge spots—the little pips around the edge—tend to be bold. You want crisp edges and color consistency across all chips. If you notice color bleeding or rough edges, that’s a sign of a lower-tier batch. In most consumer-grade sets, you’ll get a clean look that’s more than adequate for casual games and even small tournaments.
- Color fidelity: color consistency matters because you don’t want the purple chip masquerading as a $25 chip when it’s supposed to be $100. The color palette is typically designed to be easily distinguished from a foot away. If your lighting is dim, you’ll want the colors to read clearly on the felt.
