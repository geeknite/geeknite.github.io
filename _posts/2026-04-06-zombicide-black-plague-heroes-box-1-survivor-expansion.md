## What’s in the Box?
The Heroes Box-1 Survivor Expansion is, as the name suggests, a compact expansion focusing on four new survivor miniatures that you can slot into your campaigns. The box typically includes:
- Four fully sculpted survivor miniatures on their character bases,
- Cards or tokens detailing each survivor’s special ability, and
- Quick-start reference materials to help new players slot these survivors into a game without needing a PhD in zombie demography.

In the world of Zombicide, those four little figures are more than just pretty faces with axes. Each survivor brings a specific utility to the table, from crowd-control to resource management to special actions that can tilt the course of a tight encounter. If you’ve played through several campaigns and started to crave more tactical options, this expansion nudges the meta in interesting directions without turning your table into a math lab.

The minis themselves sport the high-contrast, comic-book-esque sculpts you’ve come to expect from Black Plague. The detailing is crisp: chainmail folds that actually look heavy rather than decorative, leather belts that scream “I contain a bag of questionable rations,” and faces that say, without words, “We’re not afraid of your time management issues—we’re afraid of the undead.” The posing is dynamic enough to be used as centerpiece models for a dramatic tabletop moment or as reliable, repeatable miniatures for a long-running campaign.

If you’ve assembled other Zombicide minis, you’ll notice a similar base footprint, so you won’t have to re-engineer your entire board to place these four in a squad. This is a small-but-valued design choice that keeps the expansion approachable for veterans who hate fiddly shuffles and for newbies who still think “Base” means something in a video game. The quality is consistent with the rest of the line, and you’ll find no glaring miscasts or soft plastic that bends at the wrong moment when you’re stacking a dozen black-plague zombies in a single hex.

For the aesthetically minded, there’s a lot to admire here. The palette leans into the grimy, plague-doom atmosphere of the Black Plague setting without veering into grimdark humor that doesn’t belong on your dining room table. It’s the kind of packaging that feels at home next to your dragon dice and your exhausted, slightly smug bag of lichens you pretend is food when you’re out of snacks.

## The Minis: Design, Scale, and Quality
### Sculpt Quality and Character
The four survivor sculpts emphasize varied personalities, which is exactly what you want when you’re trying to game with friends who might otherwise spend 30 minutes debating whether a vampire should be allowed to exist in your “medieval fantasy” setting. Each figure is distinct enough that you can identify them at a glance, which matters when you’re quickly flipping between actions during a tense moment.

Sculpting detail stands out in the armor textures and weapon interfaces. You’ll notice subtle scratches in chain mail, dented shields, and a sense of movement in cloaks that you’ll only fully appreciate when the game’s terrain lighting is dialed up on your kitchen table at 9 PM. The sculpts avoid the “cartoonish” look that’s easy to fall into with zombie games and instead strike a balance that makes them look ready for a grim, wet battle rather than a Saturday morning cartoon ad-break.

### Size and Stance
Miniature bases align with standard Zombicide sizing, which means you won’t be wrestling to fit them into your current party. The survivors’ stances range from ready-for-action to a more protective, shield-bearing feel—an intuitive mix if you’re trying to choreograph your squad around a central objective. If you’ve set up a couple of campaigns where a bad dice roll can snowball into a full-blown zombie apocalypse, you’ll appreciate the way these minis hold their ground on the board without looking like they’re auditioning for a role in a fantasy perfume commercial.

### Materials and Assembly
Assembling Zombicide minis is rarely a heavy lift, and these are no exception. The plastic is solid enough to handle a beatdown without warping, and the connection points are clean enough that you won’t need a magnifying glass to separate them from sprues. If you’re a painter who loves tiny details, you’ll appreciate the small charm: the armor engravings, the belts, and the individual pouches all give you room to experiment with highlights or washes.

If you’re a hobbyist who uses pre-assembled figures to stay on schedule, you’ll be happy to know that the assembly time is modest. Four miniatures, a handful of bases, and you’re ready to stand them proudly on your table. The time you’ll actually invest in painting them depends on your zeal for tabletop cosplay; if you’re like me and your only dye job this week is coffee-stained fingers, they still look fantastic unpainted, simply because the sculpts are clean and expressive.

## How It Compares to Other Expansions
If you’ve dipped your toes into other Zombicide expansions, you’ll know not every add-on lands with the same level of satisfaction. Some expansions introduce a couple of gimmicks that feel tacked on; others feel like essential cogs in a well-oiled zombie-kicking machine. Heroes Box-1 sits closer to the latter.
- Pros: Adds strategic depth, complements existing classes, practical for longer campaigns, nice table presence, decent painting payoff.
- Cons: Not dramatically transformative for casual players who only play a single session; the four miniatures could feel minimal if you’re chasing a larger army of survivors. If you’re completing a full collection, you’ll likely want to pair this with other expansions to maintain variety.

From a gameplay standpoint, the expansion’s value is in its ability to diversify your face cards and your playstyle choices, which matters more in a cooperative game than raw power. It’s the kind of product that rewards repeat play, because the survivors’ abilities can interact with the chaos of a typical zombie swarm in unexpectedly delightful ways.
