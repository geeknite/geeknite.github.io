### Configuration and Modularity
Two workstations? Yes. Ten? Also yes. The real trick is that the system encourages modularity without requiring a degree in mechanical engineering to reconfigure after every quarterly review. ### Cable Management and Power Distribution
The no-screen design means you’re responsible for what power and data you want to bring to each seat. That’s a feature, not a bug, because it pushes teams to decide what tech actually needs to be near the desk. Our test setup included a central power bus along the length of the bench and small, discreet cable channels to route charging cables, Ethernet runs, HDMI/DisplayPort for the sessions that actually require a display, and a modest set of power strips to avoid the “dancing power strip” effect you get when you try to feed ten laptops and a projector with a single wall outlet.

Pro-tip: mount a few surge protectors at the central bus and run a separate data line for each group. It reduces that embarrassing moment when the room goes dark because someone plugged in a space heater by mistake (we’ve all been there, no judgment).
