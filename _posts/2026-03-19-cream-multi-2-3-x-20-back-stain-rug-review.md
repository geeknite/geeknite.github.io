## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Stain Resistance
Back Stain backing hints at a design that resists spots and makes cleanup easier, which is a big win for busy households. Routine maintenance includes regular vacuuming, a quick wipe for small spills with a clean cloth, and prompt attention to any accidents. For more stubborn stains, a mild detergent mixed with water can be applied to the spot and blotted gently rather than rubbed, to avoid damaging the fibers or pulling the pattern out of alignment.

Allow the rug to dry completely before walking on it again to avoid leaving footprints that become part of the texture. If odors become an issue, sprinkling a light layer of baking soda after cleaning, letting it sit for a few hours, and then vacuuming can be an effective way to refresh the rug. For homes with pets, you may want to schedule more frequent vacuuming and consider periodic deep cleaning to maintain color and durability.
