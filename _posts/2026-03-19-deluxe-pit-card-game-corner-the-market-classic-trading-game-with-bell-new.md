## Gameplay overview
- Setup: The game supports 3-6 players; you will see hands raised, voices raised, and a scramble for control while the table does a little dance. The dealer shuffles the PIT deck, distributes cards evenly, and places market tokens in a central stack. Each player starts with a handful of tokens.

- Turn structure: The core PIT experience remains fast paced bidding and trading, but with a twist. You trade, trade again, and hope to corner markets quickly while the bell gives a little musical cue to the chaos.

- The Corner mechanism: Each commodity has a status marker. When you hold the majority, you bank and earn a chunk of victory points. The coin and token mechanics allow you to flip bets and reshape rounds in surprising ways.

- End condition: The game ends when a player corners a market and banks the required points; tally scores and declare the winner.

## Strategies and tips
- Early game: Seek flexible trades that keep options open. Don’t lock into one commodity too early unless you are confident you can corner it before others.

- Mid-game chaos: The middle rounds are where the table sings. Chains of trades spill over into neighboring commodities as players scramble to protect or steal corners. Use the bell strategically to signal momentum or to rattle opponents.

- Endgame precision: If you are close to victory, resist the urge to show your triumph too early. Your smug grin will invite a counterstrike and the table will rejoice in your misfortune.

- House rules: Consider a gentleman agreement variant or a no-points-for-failed-trades twist to keep tension high.
