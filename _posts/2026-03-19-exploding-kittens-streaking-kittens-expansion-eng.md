Strategy: how to wield the chaos

Like any good party trick, timing is everything. Here are some practical tips to maximize the Streaking Kittens experience without turning your living room into a slapstick comedy accident:

- Watch the table’s rhythm. If the group is playing sloppy but friendly, you can push more aggressive plays. If someone is quietly calculating, bait them with a safe option first, then spring the double-cross when they’re most hungry for a turn.
- Card-counting matters more than you think. The new expansion’s cards interact with the existing deck in ways that reward memory and tempo. If you’ve seen three redraws in a row and the discard pile is starting to resemble a chaotic museum, you’re probably due for a big swing.
- Save the cheeky cards for late rounds if possible. It’s often more satisfying to land a bold move when everyone’s already in a goofy mood rather than at the very start when nerves are jangly and snacks still intact.
- Bluffs work. In this expansion, misdirection can be a surprisingly potent weapon. A well-timed joke or a “distrust me” statement can be a better shield than a literal shield card in other games.
- Know your crowd. If your group is more deadpan, lean on wordplay and visual gags. If they’re a flush of giggles, lean into the silliness and lean out of the strategy talk for a round or two.
