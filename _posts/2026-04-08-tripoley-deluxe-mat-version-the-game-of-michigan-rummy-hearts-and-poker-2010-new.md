## Gameplay overview

### The Michigan Rummy phase

During the Michigan Rummy phase, players aim to meld sets to accumulate points. The melds are the backbone of this part and influence later bets. The trick is to balance risk and reward because a big meld can swing the pot while also signaling to others which cards you hold suit your opponents to target. This phase rewards memory and pattern recognition, but you will still rely on a dollop of luck, because drawing into the right cards is never guaranteed.

### The Hearts phase

The Hearts phase introduces betting with chips. Players place bets on cards showing up in the center. The more accurate your bets, the more chips you win. The tension in this phase is social chemistry: you want to project confidence without tipping off others that you are bluffing with your eyes closed. The social dynamics here are the heart of Tripoley style play and often produce the most memorable moments, from triumphant cheers to hilarious misreads of probability.

### The Poker phase

The final Poker phase is where the chips from the other two games converge into a single pot, and players go all in with nerve and nerve alone. The pot grows, the poker faces grow stiffer, and a final flip of a card can shift the whole story. The blend of skill, deception and luck at this moment is why people schedule game nights around this kind of three-step experience rather than just a single game with a boring old timer.

- Assign a dedicated scorekeeper who also acts as host for the night. The triple game format benefits from a calm organizer with a sense of humor.
