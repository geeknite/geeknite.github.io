## New Mechanics and How They Change the Flow
The core claim of any Saboteur expansion is that it adds meaningful friction to the treasure hunt without breaking the underlying rhythm of the game. The Amigo expansion hits that balance with a few careful-but-bold moves. Here are the major mechanics you’ll likely encounter:

### 1) Event Cards that Restructure the Play Area
A few event cards trigger mid-game shifts: temporary map rewrites, a sudden mining hazard, or a brief window where all players must pass treasure cards clockwise before discovery. The idea is to inject unpredictability without making any single player immune to suspicion. It’s the “trust fall” moment of the game—do you trust the person who draws the hazard, or the person who insists that the hazard is a simple misread?

### 2) New Treasure Card Variants
The expansion introduces new treasure cards with varying point values, but with additional costs or conditions. Some require players to meet a specific set of conditions to claim them, others reward players who cooperate in a way that doesn’t feel like cooperation at all. The meta-narrative becomes: do you try to accelerate treasure discovery by sharing information (which sabotage cards will then punish), or do you grind toward wealth through deception? The expansion’s treasure variants encourage both, which is ideal for a game that wants to test your moral calculus as much as your resources.

### 3) Optional Role-Flip Mechanic
A subset of cards allows for temporary role flips—someone who was clearly trustworthy becomes “the saboteur” for a round, or an otherwise suspicious player gains a window of protection. The dynamic is chaotic and deliciously chaotic, akin to a very confident meta-narrative about who’s “in on it” and who’s just pretending to be optimistic about the group’s plans. This is not for purists who want the same pace every game; it’s for players who crave table-choreographed drama.

### 4) Saboteur Dossier and Countermeasures
The new cards give saboteurs more ways to subtly derail plans without a full-on explosion of chaos. Countermeasures, on the other hand, give treasure seekers tools to mitigate sabotage when you sense a play coming. The balance feels better tuned than in some past expansions, where you either get crushed by sabotage or propelled by pure luck. Here, there’s a feel of deliberate, tactical abuse—which, frankly, Saboteur fans adore as long as it stays in the realm of good-natured mischief.

### 5) Small Rules Clarifications with Big Impact
No expansion would be complete without some tweak that makes life easier for new players and speedier for groups who want a longer session. The included clarifications smooth out edge cases around when a treasure card must be claimed, how multiple sabotage cards stack, and what happens if a misdeal forces a redraw. The result is less table friction, more momentum, and a slightly more polished experience—especially for teams that frequently play with mixed experience levels.

## How It Plays with the Base Game: A Practical Perspective
If you’re already comfortable with Saboteur 2, the expansion’s value lies in its ability to increase the emotional stakes without turning the game into a sprawling epic. You’ll notice a few key shifts in the turn order, the way you bluff, and how you respond to unexpected events.

### Setup and Setup Time
Setup remains quick, which is essential for a game that wants to keep the party vibe intact. You’ll draw extra cards, sort them into new families, and then you’re ready to go. The real trick is recognizing the rhythm: the expansion nudges you to incorporate new card faces into the early rounds in a way that doesn’t punish new players who are still learning the base rules. If you tend to play with a rotating cast, this is a welcome change because it gives everyone something new to decode right away.

### Early Game: Establishing Bluffing Lanes
The new event and treasure cards create opportunities to set up early lures and late-game decoys. You’ll notice players attempting to stage early misdirections, while saboteurs look for the perfect moment to trigger a cascade of reveals. The tension is “one map away from gold” with a subtle tilt toward “one misplayed gesture away from chaos.” It’s the kind of micro-drama that makes a table feel alive rather than just a sequence of card flips.

### Mid-Game: The Slippery Slope of Trust
As treasures come into play, the room’s trust dynamics become more volatile. The event cards can cause sudden shifts that force players to re-evaluate alliances on the fly. It’s not about who’s honest; it’s about who can convincingly pretend to be honest while lying with a straight face—and perhaps a slightly pressed smile to prevent a tell from giving away too much.

### Endgame: The Final Pledge and the Mad Scramble
In the final stretch, the expansion’s new mechanics collide in a glorious, messy display of deduction, misdirection, and a little bit of math. Will players coordinate to secure treasure, or will a last-minute sabotage flip the table and reveal that every player is, in fact, a villain in search of a treasure chest? The answer is always “both,” and that duality makes for some of the most memorable endings in Saboteur 2 sessions.

## Strategy and Tactics: Making the Most of the Expansion
Whether you’re the mastermind saboteur or a treasure hunter with a shaky poker face, the expansion rewards careful planning, but not so much planning that you stop improvising. Here are practical tips for making the most of the new cards.

### For the Saboteurs
- Seed your sabotage cards to align with event card timings. If you can trigger a corridor hazard right after a player reveals a treasure, you’ll maximize disruption without tipping your hand too early.
- Use role-flip cards strategically. A temporary switch can create a moral panic at the table, which is often more valuable than a single failed attempt at damaging a treasure route.
- Watch the deck fatigue. The expansion adds new card families that behave differently than the base set. If you’re getting burned by a run of “good treasure” draws, switch to a more aggressive sabotage approach rather than a passive one.

### For the Treasure Seekers
- Coordinate but don’t collude openly. The expansion likes to reward subtle, non-obvious cooperation that doesn’t reveal too much to the saboteurs.
- Use new countermeasures sparingly, but at precise moments. The effect is rarely as dramatic as you’d expect on paper; the thrill is in the timing.
- Embrace risk when an event card opens a new path or reshuffles the board. Calculated risk in a Saboteur game often pays off more than cautious, methodical play.

### General Tips
- Don’t overreact to a single bad draw. Part of Saboteur’s charm is the way a single card can swing the narrative, not necessarily the gold value.
- Keep the pace lively. If you’re playing with newer players, explain the new mechanics clearly and avoid burying them under rules jargon. The expansion’s strengths lie in a few crisp, memorable interactions rather than a dense rulebook.
- Use post-game debriefs to talk about what each player’s bluffing style revealed. It’s a great way to learn how to read subtle tells in future sessions.
