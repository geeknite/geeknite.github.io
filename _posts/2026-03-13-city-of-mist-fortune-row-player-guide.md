## Fortune Row at a Glance
Fortune Row is the district you want to visit after your last case turned into a podcast, but before your coffee finally betrays your sleep schedule. It’s a place where fortunes are made, unmade, and then re-made in the same night by people who can swear on a stack of ancient mythologies that their debt is perfectly legitimate. It’s heavily themed around covenants, capital, and the way power behaves when it’s wearing a velvet glove with brass knuckles underneath.

In this module, Fortune Row serves as a hub of intrigue rather than a one-shot clue-mining operation. It’s designed for long-form investigations that weave personal stakes with city-wide consequences. Expect a city block full of merchants, secret societies, and a few Mythos-powered personalities who insist that their side hustle is a calling from the gods themselves.

Key vibes you’ll feel walking Fortune Row:

- Neon noir with a twist of mythic consequence
- A tight network of factions who all claim to know what the city owes them
- Personal arcs that intersect with the district’s economic and cultural pulse
- A rhythm of short scenes punctuated by longer, character-driven investigations

If you’re into the idea that a street vendor can be a front for a god-tier organization, Fortune Row will sing to you in a minor key. If you prefer your RPGs with no mood lighting and no existential dread, you might want to file a complaint with the Mist police about ambiance sabotage.

## System and Mechanics: What Makes Fortune Row Tick
City of Mist runs on a dual-themes system: Logos and Mythos. The Logos are your mortal identities—your day jobs, your personas, your outward faces—while the Mythos is the mythic identity you wield when you put on your mask and start solving (or sabotaging) things with style.

Fortune Row tunes this mix to a more urban, power-centered beat. Your Investigate actions don’t just uncover clues; they reveal the hidden agreements that keep the district’s machinery running. The rules emphasize narrative control—the GM and players co-create the world, and Fortune Row rewards clever misdirection, bold declarations, and the ability to read the room (which, in Mist terms, means reading the crowd’s allegiances and fear).

### Logos and Mythos: A Quick Primer for Fortune Row
- Logos: Pick a persona that fits Fortune Row’s tradeoffs. Detective with a divine-scale debt? Corporate fixer who secretly helps the undercity? You decide, but remember: every choice carries consequences in the district’s economy of favors.
- Mythos: Your mythic identity should reflect Fortune Row’s mood: something along the lines of “The Merchant,” “The Veiled Oracle,” or “The Velvet Arbiter.” This is the power you channel when the city needs a miracle (or a legal loophole, whichever suits your vibe).

Useful tip: Fortune Row’s power dynamics reward character arcs that intersect with the district’s major players. If your hero’s past includes a debt with a merchant prince or a history with a secret society, you’ll have a natural entry point into investigations that feel personal and consequential.

## Character Creation for Fortune Row: A Practical Pathway
Creating a character in City of Mist is less about rolling dice and more about stitching a personal myth to a living city. Fortune Row makes that stitching elegant and dangerous. Here’s a practical pathway you can use to craft a compelling PC for Fortune Row in about 60 minutes of pre-game prep (assuming you’re a fast thinker and a slower typer).

### Step 1: Pick a Logo That Belongs in Fortune Row
Fortune Row is best with Logos that lean into social leverage and urban cunning. Consider options like:
- The Merchant: A master of leverage, contracts, and the art of the subtle bribe.
- The Investigator: A shell of a public persona masking a keen, mythic drive to uncover the city’s secrets.
- The Influencer: Someone who navigates the city’s networks with charm and the occasional mythic nemesis.
- The Arbiter: A person who negotiates between factions and decides which debts get collected in a way that looks like justice.

Each Logo comes with a groundwork of skills and a personal flaw. Fortune Row characters thrive when their flaw forces them into morally gray decisions—because the district itself doesn’t offer clean choices, it offers choices with consequences that echo through the neon-lit night.

### Step 2: Align with a Mythos and a Personal Hook
Fortune Row’s hook should be something that makes your PC inseparable from the district’s fate. Your Mythos could be a blessing or a curse, but it should be something that compels action in Fortune Row’s space:
- Mythos: “Bound by a Pact,” “Favored by a Patron,” or “The Unseen Auditor.” Your Mythos should be something that can collide with a local power (the Syndicate, the Velvet Council, or the Neon Choir) in a way that creates ongoing tension.
- Hook: A personal stake like a vow to clear a family name, to protect a source of secret information, or to track down a vanished ally who disappeared in Fortune Row’s labyrinthine markets.

The synergy between Logos and Mythos is where the magic happens. When you connect your personal arc to Fortune Row’s macro-drama, your scenes will feel both intimate and consequential—and you’ll enjoy the drama of not just solving a mystery, but watching the city react to your choices.

### Step 3: Build a Personal Arc That Intersects with Fortune Row’s Shadows
Fortune Row isn’t a stand-alone playground. It’s a city block, a market, and a stage where every NPC has a motive, and every motive pulls on a thread that tugs at someone else’s cape. Your personal arc should have at least two functional beats:
- A motive connected to a key player or institution in Fortune Row (sponsor, rival, patron, or antagonist).
- A long-running consequence that unfolds across sessions (a debt paid, a secret revealed, a price exacted).

Example arcs:
- The Merchant’s Debt: Your family once owed a fortune to a merchant lording over Fortune Row. The Merchant’s heirs are now circling your own clan, offering a pro-rated “settlement” that isn’t a settlement at all.
- The Velvet Secret: You promised someone you’d uncover a secret society’s scheme that uses Fortune Row as a front for more serious transnational work. Your arc demands you play both sides and keep the city from tipping into outright war.
- The Oracle’s Whisper: A Mythos connection lets you hear whispers that seem to predict crime before it happens. Fortune Row’s neighborhoods become your crime-noir playground—and you’re haunted by what those whispers won’t tell you.

### Step 4: Abilities, Clues, and Clashes
Fortune Row uses the standard City of Mist mechanics (Glimmers of the Logos, clues, scenes, and the structure that supports collaborative storytelling). Here are a few practical tips:
- Clues: Look for clues that tie together two or more factions. Fortune Row loves cross-cutting reveals: “A vendor’s ledger reveals a link to a secret society,” or “A mural that doubles as a password for a private club is also a legal filing disguised as art.”
- Scenes: Alternate between social scenes (negotiations, interrogations, charity galas) and action scenes (chases through the markets, a tense standoff in the alley, or a ritual in a hidden chamber).
- Clashes: Your clashes should escalate the city’s risk level, revealing how the district tolerates or punishes your choice. The more a choice costs you in Fortune Row, the more weighty your character becomes.

Tip: Don’t shy away from “soft” clashes—conflicts of interest, reputations, and moral compromises. Fortune Row runs on relationships as much as on muscle or gadgetry.

### Step 5: Gear, Gadgets, and Aesthetic Choices
Fortune Row looks as good as it sounds. Your gear should reflect the district’s mood: gleaming, expensive, and a little suspicious. Consider items that have a story behind them, not just a stat block:
- A pocket oracle (a small device or artifact that hints at future events but demands a price)
- A signed contract that binds the user to a shadowy patron (and can be invoked to gain leverage in negotiations)
- A disguise kit that doubles as a toolkit for one-of-a-kind miracles (or misdirection)

Gear should serve the story. A neon sign serves as a weapon? Great. A pager from the 90s that still works because it’s ancient myth knowledge wearing a technological skin? Also great. The best gadgets in Fortune Row aren’t just functional; they are conversation starters that seed future scenes.

## The Anatomy of Fortune Row Play: Encounters and Scene Fluency
Fortune Row is built on the idea that a city block can be both a battlefield and a conversation pit. Here’s a blueprint for running sessions that feel both cinematic and grounded.

### The Meeting at the Edge of Fortune Row
Picture this: a late-night council meeting in a club tucked away in a back alley, the walls heavy with velvet and whispered contracts. The players arrive in masks—literal or metaphorical—and the host insists that every word weighs like a coin. The custodian of secrets is here, too: a character who knows the city’s debts and who is not afraid to collect.

What makes this scene sing:
- Stakes: The future of a local business, a family name, and a star-crossed romance that might end up being a liability.
- Complications: A rival faction uses the meeting as a cover to slip a “management change” notice into the guest list. A misdelivered speech reveals a hidden motive.
- Payoffs: A revelation that reframes the hero’s arc and creates a new alliance—or a new enemy—within Fortune Row.

### The Market as a Maze
Fortune Row’s markets aren’t just places to buy and sell; they are living maps of power. Vendors know who controls distribution, who enforces credit, and who collects favors with more zeal than moolah. A street-level encounter can morph into a systemic revelation when a simple purchase uncovers a cross-venue conspiracy.

- Clue patterns: Look for receipts that cross-reference multiple stores, a ledger that hints at offshore transactions, and a mural that doubles as a password.
- Social dynamics: The market crowd acts as both a gauge of risk and a chorus that whispers rumors about every player’s background. The crowd can complicate or assist a plan depending on how you read the room.
- Consequences: Forging a new vendor partnership might unlock a safe route through security or invite new trouble in the form of a protective enforcer.

### The High-Risk Gala
Gala scenes are where Fortune Row shows its high-glamour side. The air smells like expensive perfume, legal loopholes, and the sweet tang of imminent peril. The trick is to use the celebration to reveal tensions between factions: a patron’s desire to maintain appearances colliding with a merchant’s need to shift power to their own hands.

Elements to consider:
- The guest list, a who’s-who of Fortune Row power players.
- A secret that could topple a kingpin or at least disrupt their status quo.
- A moment where a hero’s mask cracks, forcing them to reveal their true Logos to the crowd in the most dramatic way possible.

## Faction and NPCs in Fortune Row: The Cast Behind the Curtain
Fortune Row’s NPCs aren’t just obstacles; they’re living catalysts for your character’s journey. Here are a few archetypal factions you’ll encounter (and how to use them in play):

- The Syndicate of Fortune Row (Merchants): Makers and breakers of markets, they thrive on leverage and long-term credit. Their favors are expensive, but they’re rarely useless. If you’re after contracts and access to exclusive goods, they’re your gateway. If you cross them, you’ll discover how debt is a form of social currency.
- The Velvet Council (Cultural Gatekeepers): A circle that curates taste, influence, and public opinion. They hold the strings of legitimacy and use festivals, galleries, and charity galas to maintain a façade of order. Against them, your hero could become the voice of a counter-narrative or a renegade spokesperson for the underclass.
- The Neon Choir (Media and Nightlife): A network of performers, journalists, and social-media mavens who can amplify or cave in a rumor. They’re essential for speed and reach, but their loyalties shift with a single scandal.
- The Arbiter’s Court (Judges and Purgatories): A quasi-legal faction that polices the fine lines between crime and consequence. They’re not your friends, but they’re efficient at making sure a plan has a predictable outcome—one that benefits someone, somewhere, somehow.

NPCs are not obstacles to be defeated; they’re fixtures in the city’s ecosystem. A smart plan treats them as stakeholders in the story who can grant favors, deny them, or demand a price for their help.

## GM Tips: Running Fortune Row Without Losing Your Mind
If you’re running a Fortune Row campaign, you should be prepared for the city to push back at your narrative authority. Here are some practical tips:
- Lean into consequence: For every big move, show a cost. Fortune Row’s power structure is built on the idea that every action has a ripple effect—usually with a late-night knock at your door.
- Use dynamic locations: Fortuna Row isn’t a flat map; it’s a living set of interconnected spaces. A location should change between sessions according to what player choices did the last time you played.
- Favor personal stakes: Players love arcs that feel personal. Tie NPC motives to each hero’s past, family, or moral code. If a villain seems undefeatable, shift the goal to a delayed victory rather than a total one.
- Encourage masks but reward honesty: The mask is a tool, not a costume. Encourage players to reveal their Logos when it matters most and reward them for staying true to their character’s essence.
- Balance the city vs. the self: Fortune Row is a city of pressure points. Don’t forget to give players chances to breathe, reflect, and decide what type of myth they want to be in the city’s grand scheme.

## Sample Play Scenarios: Quick Seeds for Your Table
To spark your table, here are two seed scenarios you can drop into Fortune Row in under 60 minutes. Each seed includes a hook, an obstacle, and a payoff that nudges the story forward.

Seed A: The Ledger and the Patron
Hook: A dried-on ledger, the kind that never dries, resurfaces in a vendor’s stall. It implicates a powerful patron within the Velvet Council.
Obstacle: The ledger has a dead-man’s switch—pull the thread, and a chain of events starts that could ruin a faction’s carefully maintained image.
Payoff: Align your Logo with a different patron or use the ledger to extract a concession that changes the balance of power in the district.

Seed B: The Unseen Auction
Hook: A clandestine auction in a private club sells access to a Mythos artifact that could rewrite a person’s loyalty, including the hero’s own.
Obstacle: The artifact attracts a rival faction’s attention, and the hero must decide whether to bid truth or power.
Payoff: The artifact’s buyer reveals their own mythic identity and forces the hero to decide on a path that could echo across Fortune Row for years.

These seeds are intentionally modular. You can weave them into ongoing plots, or use them to catalyze a multi-session arc that grows from a single item or event.

## Post-Session Round-Up: Where Fortune Row Ends and Starts Again
A good Fortune Row game ends with two things: a fresh question and a hint of future trouble. The city may not have solved its problem by session’s end, but your players should leave with something personal: a debt, a vow, a secret, or a new enemy. The best sessions leave a trackable residue—the kind that makes players want to come back and sear the story into ongoing campaign lore.

To energize your next game, consider these post-session rituals:
- Quick debrief: Ask players what choice mattered most and why. What changed about their relationship to Fortune Row?
- Fallout list: Write down three events that will impact the district in future sessions: a rival faction gains leverage, a new patron appears, a piece of a hero’s past resurfaces.
- Cliffhanger: End on a decision that weighs the hero against a ticking clock or a rumor that could topple a major power holder in Fortune Row.

## Final Verdict: Should You Run Fortune Row?
Short answer: Yes, absolutely—if you like urban noir with mythic stakes and a city that feels both alive and predatory. Fortune Row is a fantastic playground for players who want character-driven drama, long arcs, and the satisfaction of outmaneuvering some of the city’s most powerful players without ever breaking the mood. It rewards players who lean into personal stakes, and it punishes those who treat the district as a mere backdrop.

Longer answer: Fortune Row shines when you lean into its networks of influence. It rewards both clever social plays and daring, high-concept mythic interventions. The best experiences come from a player who embraces a dual identity—one that navigates the district’s commerce and another that answers to a larger mythic calling. And yes, you will probably buy at least one item you don’t strictly need, because Fortune Row’s style invites it—the kind of purchase that makes a scene richer and the city feel bigger.

If you’re a GM, plan your sessions as a series of interlocking scenes that gradually reveal each faction’s motives and the personal cost of each hero’s choices. If you’re a player, bring a robust hook that ties your Logos to Fortune Row’s broader drama, and don’t be afraid to let your Mask crack when the moment is right. The city has a way of rewarding bold, narratively meaningful choices—just be ready to deal with the consequences in real time, with neon reflections on rain-soaked sidewalks and a chorus of whispers that might be destiny, or just a really well-timed rumor.

For a fun, collaborative storytelling experience, Fortune Row is hard to beat. It’s not a one-shot mystery; it’s a hinge. It’s where the city’s fortunes tilt and where your own legend begins to take form—one scene, one contract, and one mythic decision at a time.

Final recommendation: if you’re into games that feel like a crime-noir-fantasy RPG with character-driven plots and a city that feels alive enough to devour your plans, Fortune Row is your jam. It will reward you with stories you’ll be telling months later about the night the city decided your fate—and you decided the city’s fate in return.
