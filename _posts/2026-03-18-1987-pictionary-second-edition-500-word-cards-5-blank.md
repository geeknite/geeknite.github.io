## The Five Blank Cards: DIY Gold and CHAOS
### Why five blanks matter
The five blank cards are the secret sauce in this edition. They give you the power to tailor the game to your crew—whether you’re honoring a recurring in-joke, a private meme, or a new family member who refuses to live without a custom challenge. The blanks are not a gimmick; they’re an invitation to host your own mini-arc within your game night. You can write on them with a standard Sharpie, a dry-erase marker (if your board is laminated), or you can even switch to a whiteboard-friendly method if you’re feeling fancy.

### Creative uses you’ll actually use
- Personal in-jokes: If your group has a beloved but obscure reference, you can immortalize it with a blank card. The moment someone recognizes the joke during a tense five-card sprint is the kind of communal joy that retro games excel at.
- Family customization: Introduce kid-friendly entries or “family night” prompts like “favorite movie” or “pizza topping.” It’s a gentle way to pull younger players into the action without turning into a meltdown factory when the timer starts.
- Tribute cards: Create mini-tributes to your gaming group’s history, such as “first game night,” “the epic saves on night X,” or “the time the dog ate the dice.” These memories become real game-night folklore.
- Hybrid play: The blank cards invite you to blend Pictionary with a quick charades variant. A blank for “act this without speaking” can be your bridge to more dynamic play as the night deepens.

### Practical tips for blank card writing
Keep it simple. One word or a short phrase is enough for a comedic payoff when the drawer tries to sketch something that corresponds to a private joke. If you want to go all-in, you can add a tiny clue line on the bottom for future repeats, but this might slow things down and often defeats the beginners’ charm of making up a card on the fly.
