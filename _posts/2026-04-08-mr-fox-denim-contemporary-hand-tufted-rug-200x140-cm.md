## Care and Maintenance

No rug is an island, even though this rug would prefer to be. Routine care is straightforward:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to avoid pulling fibers or creating a threadbare patch. If you have a canine or feline security team, you’ll want to remind them that the carpet is not a chew toy or a scratching post.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which spreads the mess and frays the edges of your dignity along with the pile.
- Rotate the rug every 6-12 months to ensure even wear and to keep those denim tones balanced across the surface.
- Protect from direct sun; UV will fade the color gradient over time, turning your regal indigo into a slightly more uncertain shade of “meh.”
- Professional cleaning is recommended every couple of years for heavy traffic areas, or whenever the couch suddenly develops a stronger bond with the rug than with its own cushions.

Edge care is also worth noting. The edges of hand-tufted rugs can be delicate if you drag heavy furniture across them. If you’re rearranging the living room, lift rather than pull, and consider a felt protector or rug pad to reduce wear along the border.
