## The Basics

### What is the Middle-earth Strategy Battle Game?

The Middle-earth Strategy Battle Game (MESBG) is a tabletop miniature wargame based on the works of J.R.R. Tolkien. It’s designed by Games Workshop and features characters, factions, and battles from both *The Lord of the Rings* and *The Hobbit*. Players collect, build, and paint miniatures, and then use them to recreate the epic skirmishes of Middle-earth.

### Components

Here’s what you can expect to find inside a standard MESBG box:
- **Miniatures**: Detailed models of heroes, villains, and troops from Middle-earth.
- **Rulebooks**: Comprehensive guides to the game’s mechanics and lore.
- **Templates and Dice**: Essential tools for measuring distances and determining outcomes.

**Pro Tip**: Before starting, make sure you have a dedicated workspace. Painting miniatures on your kitchen table while your cat tries to knock them over is a recipe for disaster.

## Painting Guide

### Choosing Your Colors

Selecting the right colors is crucial for bringing your miniatures to life. For instance, if you’re painting Aragorn, think about the earthy tones that reflect his ranger persona. Conversely, the forces of Mordor might require darker, more sinister shades.

**Funny Moment**: One of our contributors painted Gandalf with neon colors as a joke. Let’s just say, Gandalf the Fabulous wasn’t quite the look we were going for.

### Techniques

- **Base Coating**: Start with a solid base color to cover the entire miniature. This provides a foundation for further detailing.
- **Dry Brushing**: Use a lighter shade to brush over raised surfaces, highlighting textures and details.
- **Shading**: Apply washes to create shadows and add depth to your models.

### Advanced Tips

- **Layering**: Build up layers of paint gradually to create smooth transitions between colors.
- **Edge Highlighting**: Use a fine brush to apply highlights on the edges of armor and weapons.
- **Weathering**: Add realistic wear and tear to weapons and armor using special techniques and pigments.

## Modeling Guide

### Assembling Your Miniatures

Once your miniatures are painted, it’s time to assemble them. Use plastic glue for plastic models and super glue for metal ones. Remember, patience is key—rushing this step could result in a disassembled army faster than you can say “You shall not pass!”

### Creating Dioramas

Want to take your modeling to the next level? Create elaborate dioramas that display your miniatures in action. Use materials like sand, grass, and rocks to build realistic terrain.

**Pro Tip**: A hot glue gun can be your best friend when creating terrain pieces. Just be careful not to glue your fingers together.

### Customization

Add personal touches to your miniatures by modifying them. Whether it’s creating a unique banner for your army or repositioning a hero’s weapon, customization adds character to your collection.
