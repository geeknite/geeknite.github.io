## Setup tips and best practices
- Plan your rack space using a simple, written plan before you buy. It saves time and reduces the number of trips to the hardware store for extra screws. 
- Use vertical cable managers to organize fiber and copper runs; pair them with horizontal cable troughs to keep the airflow path clean. 
- Mount heavier equipment toward the bottom to lower the center of gravity and reduce sway when doors swing. 
- Use blanking panels to prevent air from short-circuiting hot spots; this is especially useful in mixed environments with high density. 
- If you are upgrading an existing rack, evaluate whether the 39-inch depth will interfere with door clearance or aisle width in your data closet. Measure twice, order once—that is the Bible of rack installations. 
- Document your cabling layout and keep a copy inside the cabinet door for quick reference during maintenance windows. 
- Keep a small toolkit handy for on-site adjustments; nothing derails a deployment like hunting for the right metric screw in a pile of screws that all look the same.
