## Performance: Speed and Real-World Testing

Speed is the big promise of any external SSD. The X10 Pro delivers with a typical sustained read around 1000–1050 MB/s and sustained write in the neighborhood of 900–1000 MB/s on USB-C 3.2 Gen 2-enabled hosts. Real-world tests vary, but in general you should expect to see transfers that nearly annihilate poor old spinning disks and make your backups feel almost glamorous. Practical experiences include quick OS backups, large video project copies, and the kind of mass file transfers that make you audibly thankful for fast storage.

Small caveats: your actual speeds depend heavily on the host controller, the USB-C cable quality, and the file system you have on hand. If you are on a USB-C hub with limited bandwidth, don’t be surprised if the numbers dip. But with a quality USB-C 3.2 Gen 2x2 cable and a capable host, the drive tends to stay within the promised envelope.

From a user experience perspective, the drive is snappy. Opening large photo libraries, importing 4K video thumbnails, and copying large RAW sequences feel almost instantaneous. It’s the kind of performance that makes you wonder how you ever lived with older externals that sounded like tiny lawnmowers under load. And yes, we tested sequential transfers with large contiguous files as well as mixed workloads with hundreds of smaller files. The drive holds up well across scenarios, which is exactly what you want in a portable daily driver.

Here is a quick flashcard of the performance numbers you can expect on a suitable host:

- Read: about 1,030 MB/s (typical)
- Write: about 970 MB/s (typical)
- Random 4K read: a few tens of thousands of IOPS depending on queue depth
- Random 4K write: similar ballpark to reads, with a bit more variance

These are not theoretical high-precision lab numbers, but real-world impressions you can expect when backing up your photo library or exporting a 4K timeline to deliver to a client on the same day. If you run multiple streams of data, you’ll likely see the typical small dips, but nothing dramatic enough to derail your workflow.

## Compatibility and Setup

One of the nice things about modern external SSDs is their universality. The X10 Pro is compatible with Windows and macOS without drama. For Windows users, the drive formats cleanly to NTFS by default for optimal performance and straightforward backups, though you can reformat to exFAT if you intend to share it with macOS or Linux without any third-party drivers. For Mac users, exFAT or APFS are both possible, with the trade-off that APFS will not provide cross-platform compatibility without auxiliary software. In short, compatibility is straightforward, and setup is essentially plug-and-play.

If you want to see the precise steps, you can refer to the official product page linked below and then return to the geeky goodness of this post. The important bit is: there are no special drivers you need to install for basic operation. Just plug it in and go. If you want to squeeze a little more performance or nuance out of your setup, consider enabling TRIM where supported and ensuring your host OS is up to date.
