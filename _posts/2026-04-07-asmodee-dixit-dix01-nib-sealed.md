## How Dixit actually works: rules in a glance

Dixit is deceptively simple at the core. You do not need a cryptographer degree to play, but you do need to embrace the possibility that the most obvious clue may fail and the most abstract clue may win. Here is the simplified flow you can rely on on the first night you crack open DIX01:

1) Each round, one player is the storyteller. They pick a card from their hand and give a clue that relates to the image. The clue can be a word, a sentence, a sound, or even a quote from a movie that might be radically misinterpreted. The important part is that the clue should be specific enough to be debatable but not so obvious that everyone chooses the storyteller’s card. 

2) The other players select one card from their own hand that best matches the storyteller’s clue and place it face down. 

3) The cards are shuffled and revealed. Everyone (including the storyteller) votes on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. 

4) Points are awarded. If all players or no players guess the storyteller’s card, the storyteller gains fewer points because their clue failed to guide the squad. Otherwise, the storyteller and those who guessed correctly flip points from the board’s track and cheer in a way that suggests a small victory or at least a good personal narrative. 

5) The role passes to the next player and the dance continues until the deck runs dry or the table agreement collapses under the weight of clever critiques and questionable puns.

The beauty lies in the tension between clarity and ambiguity. A good clue feels like a key that fits a lock you cannot see; a bad clue feels like you brought a camel to a jazz concert. It’s all about tone, timing, and the shared willingness to interpret imagery as if you are decoding a dream you had while dozing on a couch wrapped in a fuzzy throw.
