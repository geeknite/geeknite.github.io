## Design and materials: why poly rattan and what makes vidaXL tick

### Poly rattan explained
Poly rattan is a synthetic material designed to emulate the weave and texture of natural rattan while offering dramatic improvements in weather resistance and longevity. The synthetic fibers resist moisture, sunlight, and temperature swings better than natural rattan, which means you can leave this set out during a tropical downpour, a heatwave, or the seasonal epic of leaves and pollen without panicking about immediate decay. The weave pattern tends to be tight, uniform, and forgiving if you’re not a master wicker craftsman.

vidaXL typically uses powder-coated steel or corrosion-resistant aluminium framing inside poly rattan shells. The result is a sturdy-looking silhouette with good weight distribution. ## Durability, maintenance, and weather readiness

### Weather resistance and UV exposure
Poly rattan weaves are designed to withstand UV exposure better than natural rattan, but not immune. Prolonged sun exposure can lead to color fading over time, so if you’re in a sun-intense zone, consider rotating the set seasonally or using outdoor-friendly cushions with UV-protective fabric. The frame’s finish should resist corrosion if it’s aluminum or powder-coated steel, a critical factor when you’re dealing with sprinklers, rain, or the occasional winter freeze.

### Water management and dries quickly
The cushions are a strong suit here: water is not a death sentence for the cushions themselves, thanks to moisture-wicking fabrics and quick-dry foams. That said, when there is heavy rainfall, you should consider removing cushions and storing them indoors, or at least in a dry, ventilated space to prevent mold formation. A simple tip is to use outdoor cushion storage bags or bins during storms, which also helps keep the cushions free from dirt and dust while you are on vacation or frolicking at a barbecue.

### Maintenance routine that actually works
- Wipe the poly rattan frame with a soft cloth and mild soap if needed. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the weave. - Clean cushion covers according to the manufacturer’s instructions, usually a gentle wash cycle or hand wash when necessary. - Check for loose screws or bracket wear at least once every season and tighten as needed. - Protect against mildew by ensuring cushions dry thoroughly before storing. - Use a breathable outdoor cover during off-seasons to prolong the life of the weave and cushions.
