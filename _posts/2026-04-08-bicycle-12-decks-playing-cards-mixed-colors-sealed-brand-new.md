## Care and Maintenance

Caring for your decks is easier than arguing about the best board game for a rainy afternoon. Keep them in the original box or a dedicated card case to guard against humidity and temperature fluctuations. Avoid exposing them to direct sunlight for extended periods, which can cause color fading on some back designs, though with the classic Bicycle style here, you’ll see minimal, if any, fading after long exposure. If you spill coffee or a dramatic beverage that you regret, don’t panic—just wipe gently with a dry cloth and let them air-dry. Do not bend, fold, or attempt origami with your card stock unless you’re preparing for a performance that involves the incredible shrinking deck trick (which, honestly, is a bold move that may require its own tutorial).

Storage is your friend. A dry drawer or a dedicated card case is ideal. Card sleeves are not necessary for standard play, but if you’re someone who wants to keep a pristine surface for a long period, you might consider sleeve protection for the decks you plan to showcase on camera or keep as a collectible set. The humble rule remains: treat these as you would treat a beloved paperback collection—respect the bindings, don’t stack too high to crush something precious, and enjoy the quiet mana of a well-kept deck.
