## Materials, Craft, and the Batik Process
A rug is a tapestry of decisions—fiber, weave, dye, and the tiny rituals that make a handmade product feel special. Tiger City Batik Rug leans into a traditional approach with modern durability, something you can put under a coffee table without panicking about spills turning the whole piece into a watercolor disaster.

### What It’s Made Of
- Primary fiber: 70–80% cotton for the warp and weft to give you that easy-clean, medium-density feel underfoot.
- Accents: a blend of viscose or polyester in small percentages to improve colorfastness and reduce pilling.
- Backing: a sturdy canvas that helps maintain shape and keeps your cat from thinking it’s a new scratching post.

### The Craft Story: Wax, Dye, Repeat
Batik is a ritual—hot wax is brushed or stamped onto fabric to resist dye, then the dye baths fill the unsigned spaces. The process requires patience and exceptional attention to detail, because even the tiniest wax line can redirect color in surprising ways. For a rug, that translates into a surface that has a subtle hand-drawn feel, where edges aren’t perfectly crisp like a computer print, but warm and human like a sketch on parchment. You’re paying for the time and skill of artisans who balance wax, heat, and pigment to create a design that looks organic rather than mass-produced.

## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity
A rug that looks this alive deserves a care routine that respects its craft without turning your life into a daily spa day.

### Everyday Care
- Vacuum weekly with a suction setting that is gentle rather than aggressive. Don’t go full volcano with the machine; the goal is removal of dust and particles without pulling on the fibers.
- Rotate every three to six months. This helps prevent uneven wear and keeps the color interaction evenly distributed across the surface.
- Use a rug pad. It reduces slippage, protects the backing, and cushions foot traffic. It’s a small investment with a big payoff in lifespan and noise reduction.

### Stain and Spill Guide
- Act quickly, but stay cool. Blot spills with a clean white cloth—never rub, which can push the dye deeper into the fibers.
- Mild soap solution for stubborn spots. A dab of mild detergent with water on a cloth, then blot gently. Always test a hidden spot first.
- Oil-based stains may require professional cleaning. If you have a particularly stubborn area, don’t hesitate to consult a rug-cleaning pro who specializes in handmade textiles.

### Long-Term Maintenance
- Avoid direct sun on the entire rug for long stretches. Rotate or move furniture to minimize sun exposure in the same areas; this helps prevent uneven fading.
- Consider occasional professional cleaning. The intricacy of batik patterns benefits from a careful hand-cleaning approach.
- Storage: If you ever roll the rug for storage, keep it away from damp spaces to prevent mold and color migration. Wrap in breathable fabric and keep it out of direct light.
