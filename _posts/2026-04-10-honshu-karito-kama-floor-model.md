## Materials, Care, and Longevity
### Maintenance Essentials
For any blade that’s part of your everyday routine (even if it started as a floor model), maintenance makes the difference between a treasured tool and a shelf ornament. After you’ve unboxed and used it, hand-wash with mild soap, dry thoroughly, and store in a dry place. Oiling the blade lightly after use can help with rust resistance if your climate is humid, though high-carbon stainless steel tends to resist corrosion well enough on its own. The handle should be wiped clean and kept dry as well. Floor models are used by many people, so a quick wipe-down after each use is a good habit that will extend the life of the tool and its showroom luster.

### Care About the Display? Different from Care About Your Counter
Display blades get touched a lot by customers testing balance and edge feel. The floor-model reality is that you should not rely on the display’s edge to remain pristine forever. If you’re buying it, you’ll likely want to re-sharpen and test the edge yourself to ensure it meets your standards. If you’re just window-shopping, remember that the display blade is a snapshot in time, not a guarantee of performance for every cut you’ll make in your own kitchen.

### Storage and Safety
Store the Kama securely in a blade guard or in a dedicated knife block with plenty of air. Avoid stacking heavy things on top of it, as that can distort the blade edge or ding the tip. In a kitchen where kids might venture into the knife drawer, consider a locked cabinet or a magnetic strip with a blade guard.
