### Ergonomics and Comfort for Five People
Five people share one surface, and that has ergonomics written all over it, in big bold letters that look like neon signs at a sci-fi bar. The problem space is obvious: how do you keep individual ergonomics intact when a shared surface governs the seating arrangement? The design invites you to configure adjustable chairs around the central zone with the screen taking the main stage. The desk height remains comfortable for seated use, but you’ll want to ensure each user can reach their own tools without contorting into a pretzel. A few insertions of monitor arms or laptop docks can help tailor personal reach without shifting the entire ensemble.

Fresh air and acoustics become a factor too. In a five-person configuration, conversations can escalate quickly. A strategic plan for mic etiquette and noise control—perhaps a signal system or a “gentle hands” rule—will go a long way toward preserving team harmony. If your team is accustomed to chaotic collaboration, this desk amplifies that energy in a contained, surprisingly civilized manner.

### Cable Management and Power Strategy
Nine meters of surface means nine meters of potential cable drama. The manufacturer includes a cable-management backbone that routes power and data along the underframe, but you’ll still see a trail from the wall outlet to each seat’s zone. The trick is to designate a central power hub and then distribute from there, keeping the under-desk space relatively clear. We recommend labeling cables, using color-coded ties, and employing a modest “no-drama cable policy” during daily operations. A well-orchestrated cable plan makes this behemoth look like a purpose-built machine rather than a chaotic piñata for your IT team.

### Compatibility with Tech and Peripherals
The screen is the main star here, but what about the rest of your gear? The desk surface is friendly to keyboards, mice, drawing tablets, and the occasional VR controller that forgets it’s supposed to stay in the headset. You’ll likely want to implement docking stations for each seat or a shared dock with five ports to minimize the clutter. If your workflow requires multiple monitors per person, you’ll have to settle for a well-organized arm setup or mount extra displays to the sides. The beauty of such a design is the space it creates for teamwork; the complexity comes in making sure the tech doesn’t steal the show from the collaboration itself.

A piece like this benefits from a routine that blends practical maintenance with occasional upgrades. Wipe the surface with a microfiber cloth to keep fingerprints at bay; avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the finish. If you’re in a busy environment, plan for occasional disassembly or reconfiguration. The modular mindset that comes with such a desk is both its strength and its challenge: you’ll want to keep a record of how the pieces connect so you can rebuild without a scavenger hunt for screws.
