## Overview
Zombicide thrives on chaos, banter, and dice that inexplicably decide your fate at the worst possible moment. The Special Guest Edouard Guiton Expansion Set leans into that vibe with a designer cameo that feels earned, not forced. It slots into your base game like a well-timed joke at a party — you don’t crave it every time, but when it appears, the room lightens and the table starts coordinating like a well-rehearsed improv troupe.

This expansion is not a reset button or a radical rewrite. It drops in a new survivor card, guest-themed mission cards, tokens, and a compact rule sheet that integrates with the core game without tripping you into an avalanche of errata. If you enjoy a splash of flavorful flavor with a heaping helping of practical play, Edouard Guiton’s ideas yield more grins than groans as the setup unfolds.

For the purposes of this review we treat the Special Guest expansion as a cheeky designer-led seasoning — a spice that elevates a simmering session without turning the pot into a science experiment.

## Box contents and setup
### What you get in the box
The Special Guest expansion favours a lean, flexible payload that plays nicely with multiple players around a standard table. Expect:

- A survivor card for Edouard Guiton with an ability that interacts with the survivors’ dice pool in flavorful ways
- A batch of guest-themed mission cards that thread Edouard’s vibe into the narrative
- A small token sheet plus a couple of stand-alone objective cards for quick-play sessions

Artwork and production values sit in familiar Zombicide territory — cards with bold icons, readable text, and a color palette that sticks to the base game vibe. Setup remains straightforward — it is base Zombicide with Edouard’s hero card added to the survivor pool and the guest mission cards dropped into the Mission deck. Pro tip: separate Edouard’s card until you are preparing to trigger his ability. A knee-jerk read at the start of a crucial round can derail a perfectly good plan faster than a horde can swarm a doorway.

## Playing with the expansion
### Mechanics and how they fit the core game
The Special Guest set does not reinvent the wheel; it redefines its traction points. The new mission cards provide fresh objectives that can be woven into existing campaigns. Edouard’s hero interacts with core actions in a way that encourages timing, crowd-control tactics, and pathing decisions that you might otherwise overlook in a standard run. Expect:

- A stronger emphasis on coordinated turns and the timing of special actions
- Opportunities to trigger synergy effects with other Survivors when Edouard’s ability is used optimally
- A light but flavorful meta layer that rewards players who track guest cards and align their strategy around them

Rules remain crisp and approachable. The reminder sheet is concise, and the new cards are clearly labeled so you don’t accidentally mix guest objectives with ordinary mission goals. If you regularly play with multiple expansions, you will appreciate that Edouard’s mechanics feel complementary rather than conflicting with other add-ons you may own.

### Strategy tips for winning with a guest flavor
If you want to maximize value from this expansion, here are practical pointers:

- Start planning around Edouard’s turn early. Do not wait for the perfect moment; set up a sequence that yields a clear payoff a couple of rounds ahead
- Coordinate with a strong melee survivor. A well-timed engagement can maximize Edouard’s impact and provide a safer window for more fragile teammates
- Use guest mission cards to establish tempo. Let a couple of quick objectives on straightforward turns proceed, then unleash a more ambitious Edouard-influenced plan when the board has thinned
- Do not overestimate the power. It tilts a round, not the entire scenario. Treat it as a crafted nudge rather than a guaranteed win button

In short, the expansion rewards planning, teamwork, and a willingness to lean into a slightly chaotic rhythm for a few rounds of intense zombie chaos.
