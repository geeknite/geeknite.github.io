## Setup tips and maintenance you’ll actually use
### Quick-start checklist
- Unpack and inventory hardware. If you find a spare Allen key instead of a bolt, panic briefly, then locate the correct bolt and proceed.
- Level the table on all axes. Uneven surfaces are what turn a legendary shot into a skittering tragedy, and no one wants to explain a lost game to the dog via a wobble.
- Mount the players, align the rods, and attach the end caps. It sounds simple, and it is—until you realize you forgot to tighten something and the family laughs at your bulging to-do list.
- Calibrate the ball throw by testing a few practice shots. You’ll know it’s right when you can consistently slice a shot through the center path with a satisfying “thwack.”

### Cleaning and upkeep
A quick wipe-down after use keeps the surface playing nicely and looking sharp. Avoid harsh solvents that might degrade the finish. If you live in a humid climate, you’ll want to inspect the leg levelers and frame joints periodically to catch any creep of moisture or minor warping before it becomes a mood-killer at game night.
