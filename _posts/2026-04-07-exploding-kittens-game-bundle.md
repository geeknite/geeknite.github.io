### How to Play (A Short, Friendly Overview)

This isn’t a heavyweight strategy bible; it’s a quick-start guide to chaos. The core objective is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten. Each player starts with a handful of Defuse cards and a few other tricks to bend the flow of the game. The game proceeds in turns where you can play cards from your hand to alter the danger or to thwart others, then draw a card. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out of the round. The last player standing wins. The rules are forgiving enough for newbies but flexible enough to let veteran chaos-mages push the limits.

##### Setup

- Create the deck by shuffling Exploding Kitten cards with the other action cards. 
- Remove enough Exploding Kitten cards so that the number of players is just short of catastrophe. (That’s a fancy way of saying you’ll leave a kitten-laden path for someone else.)
- Deal seven cards to each player and give them a Defuse card to start if you want a longer game. The exact numbers aren’t sacred; the vibe is: start fast, finish with a roar.
- Place the draw pile in the center.

##### Turn Structure

On your turn you may play cards to affect the game state. Then you draw one card. If that draw is an Exploding Kitten, use a Defuse card to survive; otherwise, you’re out. The game continues until one player remains.
