Safety tips that go beyond the brochure

- Always supervise children while they are bouncing. A trampoline is a heroic invention, but it is not a substitute for adult presence.
- Use the enclosure net properly; ensure the zipper is closed before every bounce or while the kid is mid-air to prevent accidental exits.
- Clear the area around the trampoline of toys, hoses, and curious pets. A cause for drama is often a stray toy becoming a wingman to a mid-air tumble.
- Do not allow flips or somersaults on small trampolines. These are not professional apparatuses; they are playgrounds for little humans with less gravity discipline than a seasoned acrobat.
- Check weather conditions before use. In windy weather, the net can act like a sail, which is cute in a cartoon but not in real life.
- Teach kids to land on two feet, bend their knees, and roll away from the center rather than toward the edge. It’s not just safety; it also reduces the risk of stepping on a neighbor’s toe.
- Indoor use is possible with a ceiling clearance check. If you have a room with a low ceiling, you might want to seek a smaller alternative or a non-bouncing activity for that space.
