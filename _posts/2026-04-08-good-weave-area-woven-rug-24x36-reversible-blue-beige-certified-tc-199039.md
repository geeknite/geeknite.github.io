## Materials and Cleaning Tips for Real Life
Understanding the fiber content helps you set expectations for feel, warmth, and stain resistance. The rug’s blend aims to balance softness with durability. In practice, you’ll notice that foot traffic will flatten the high-pile regions over time, but the reversible nature mitigates the look by offering a fresh plane to step on. For spills, a quick blot with a clean cloth is the standard starting move. Avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper into the fibers. For stubborn spots, a mild detergent solution or a rug-specific cleaner is usually sufficient when used sparingly. Always test a hidden area first to verify that the color does not bleed. 

Vacuuming frequency depends on foot traffic. In busy entryways or living rooms where people play musical chairs around coffee tables and kids drag backpacks through, vacuum every few days on a low suction setting to maintain fiber integrity. If you have pets, you’ll appreciate the rug’s fiber resilience, but you’ll also want to keep a lint roller handy for fur runs. 

Rotation is your best anti-boredom strategy. When you flip the rug as part of the reversal feature, you’re not just swapping color; you’re giving the fibers a new performance circuit. This practice reduces visible wear in any single direction, helping your 24x36 canvas stay balanced over time.
