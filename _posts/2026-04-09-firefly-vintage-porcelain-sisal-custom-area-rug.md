## Materials and Build Quality

### Sisal fiber: nature’s own durability rep (with a dash of rugged charm)

Sisal is harvested from agave plants, meaning it’s a natural, renewable resource. It’s stiff enough to resist flattening in high-traffic zones but soft enough to avoid turning your feet into ice blocks during winter. The weave is tight and consistent, which translates into a neat silhouette that doesn’t baguette its edges into the room. Edge finishing options usually include hems or a self-bound edge. ### Why go custom?

If your space isn’t perfectly rectangular or you’re working within an open plan with multiple seating zones, a custom size is not a luxury; it’s a design necessity. Custom sizing lets you control the rug’s footprint, ensuring that it anchors the sofa, coffee table, and reading nook without creating awkward dead zones. ### Ordering guide: a nerdy step-by-step

- Step 1: Measure the room geometry with the precision of a sci‑fi engineer. Include door swings, baseboard heights, and any fixed furniture that might conflict with rug placement.
- Step 2: Decide on duration and underlay. For high-traffic spaces, pairing with a quality rug pad reduces movement and extends the life of the weave.
- Step 3: Choose thickness and pile height. A thicker pile can feel cozier but may reduce the rug’s overall perimeter clarity; a lower profile is more durable and easier to clean.
- Step 4: Select edge finish and backings. If you’re placing the rug under furniture, a backed option with a good grip will help prevent shifting.
- Step 5: Confirm color representation and samples. Digital swatches are great, but seeing a physical swatch can help you appreciate the subtle color shifts in Vintage Porcelain.
- Step 6: Place the order and wait. Custom pieces require patience, but the payoff is a rug that fits your room like a tailored suit.

The 8x10 is the hallway of rug sizes: not too small, not too big, just right for most living rooms. It accommodates a sofa and two chairs, or a sectional anchored by a coffee table. ## Care and Maintenance (Because Life Happens)

Sisal fibers thrive with routine care and gentle handling. Here are practical tips to keep your rug looking sharp:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting. Avoid beater bars; they can loosen fibers over time.
- Rotate every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, white cloth. Avoid rubbing, which pushes liquids deeper into the fiber.
- For stubborn stains, contact a professional rug cleaner with experience in natural fibers.
- Avoid prolonged direct sunlight to minimize fading. Vintage Porcelain is forgiving but not immortal.

Underlay matters. A quality rug pad adds underfoot comfort, reduces movement, and protects the rug’s edges from thinning. The pad also helps with acoustics in rooms with hard floors, which is a bonus for podcasting, gaming, or those late-night squeaky-floor simulations you secretly love.
