### Authentication and Grading: The Trust-but-Verify Game
As with any rare card, authentication matters. A JP variant will have signature touches: distinctive holo patterns, precise font metrics, and language-specific border details. For NM copies, you’ll want to verify centering, edge wear, surface gloss, and any micro-scratches that reveal themselves only under bright light. Grading services will consider foreign language cards carefully, and you should expect the process to take longer than your average English-language submission because the graders appreciate the subtle differences in print techniques. Expect a premium if the card’s designation (192/190) is corroborated by official documentation or credible community consensus.

### Storage and Preservation: The Binder is Your Fortress
This card deserves tender care: use protective sleeves designed for modern cards, place it in a rigid top-loader, and store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The Japanese edition’s glossy surface can be prone to showing micro abrasions if you shuffle with rough sleeves, so choose a sleeve with excellent slip properties. If you’re one of those people who keeps a humidity monitor beside their desk, you’re not alone; humidity can kiss the gloss goodbye by encouraging edge wear and fogging. And yes, this means you’ll have to resist the urge to buffet the card in a warm room while you’re finishing a double feature binge-watch of your favorite retro anime. Resist. Breathe. It’s not worth the risk.
