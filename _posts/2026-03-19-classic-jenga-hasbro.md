## What is Jenga
Jenga is a stacking block game with a deceptively simple premise. There are 54 wooden blocks, each the same size, stacked in layers of three. Players take turns removing one block from anywhere in the stack (except the topmost completed layer) and placing it on the top to complete another layer. The tower gradually becomes more unstable and the ground falls out from under players without mercy as gravity becomes the loudest player in the room. The Hasbro Classic Jenga uses light wood blocks with a smooth finish that feels satisfying in hand and makes the sliding and gripping feel almost ceremonial, like a tiny exercise in precision. It is the tactile counterpart to the phrase calm under pressure. And yes, the wooden blocks give that satisfying knock when a tower comes down, which is both sad and ridiculous in equal measure.

Setup is straightforward: you build 18 layers of three blocks each, with every other layer oriented perpendicular to the layer below it. It is a ritual more than a setup: you stack, you align, you whisper small prayers to the tower gods, and you hope that your fingernails remain intact.

## How to Play Core Rules
- Each player, in turn, removes a block from anywhere in the tower (except for the topmost completed layer) using only one hand. 
- After removing the block, place it on the top of the tower, rotating it to align with the current top layer. 
- The turn ends once the block is placed. Play continues until the tower collapses. 
- The player who caused the tower to fall is the loser.

These rules are intentionally lean. There is no complicated scoring. The fun comes from watching a perfect run of safe moves devolve into a comical wobble as the last blocks are extracted. Jenga thrives on minimalism—the fewer rules that can go wrong, the more dramatic the collapse when gravity decides to flex.

Hasbro keeps the official rules accessible on their site. ## Strategy and Skill
The beauty of Jenga is that you can play with all skill levels but the real fun emerges when you treat it as a micro chess game of wooden blocks. Here are a few high level tips to sharpen your craft:

1) Start with looser blocks from the center. The blocks in the middle columns often have the most grip and provide better leverage for future pulls.  
2) Try to keep your turns predictable. Avoid sudden aggressive pulls; a gentle experimented approach often yields a longer run.  
3) When in doubt, test the edge near the bottom. The bottom layers, when loosened, can allow your block to waltz out with minimal ripple through the top, buying you more moves. 
4) Watch the tower sway. The moment you detect a tilt is not the moment to panic but the moment to think. The wobble is your friend in disguise, a timed dance with gravity.

Practical skills improve with time, but the game does not require a formal education. The rules are simple; the execution is the art. We often have players who rely on luck and those who rely on method. The latter group tends to last longer, but the former yields the most dramatic final moments—think slow motion in a coffee shop, except with wood and friends.
