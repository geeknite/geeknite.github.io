## What is Codenames Pictures
Codenames Pictures is a two-team deduction game built on a 5 by 5 or 4 by 4 grid of image cards. Each card features a single image that can be interpreted in many ways, from literal to abstract. The spymaster for each team knows which images cluster around certain concepts thanks to a key card. The team tries to guess the right images using clues given by the spymaster. Clues are a single word followed by a number, indicating how many images the clue relates to. The catch is to avoid the assassin card, a dark turn that ends the round immediately in a loss for the guessing team. As with the word edition, speed is not the sole determinant of victory; the strength of your clues and the cohesion of your team matter just as much.

## How to play: flow and flow state
The core flow is familiar to anyone who has played Codenames in any form:
- Set up the grid with the image cards. 
- One team has the first clue, determined by the roll or a neutral coin flip. 
- The spymaster gives a one word cue plus a number. The cue should relate to as many target images as possible but not to the assassin or the opposing team’s images. The teammates discuss and choose cards to flip over one by one. The round ends when a team guesses enough cards to meet the number or when they are forced to stop due to a poor clue or a wrong guess. 
- Play alternates between teams until either team captures all its images or the assassin is revealed, ending the round in favor of the other team.

In practice, what sets Codenames Pictures apart is the freedom you get with clues. Because the cards are images rather than words, you can create a chain of associations that blends memories, media trivia, and even inside jokes. A spymaster who loves cinema might drop a clue about a director, and suddenly three images connect to a filmography thread. A family with a background in Dungeons and Dragons might turn a dragon image into a metaphor for a dragon slayer quest. This flexibility is the game’s secret sauce, and it rewards players who enjoy collaborative storytelling as much as competitive guessing.
