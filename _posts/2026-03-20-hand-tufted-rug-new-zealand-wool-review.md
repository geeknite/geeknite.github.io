## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping It Like New (Or At Least Like-New-Adjacent)

Caring for a premium woven rug is about steady routines and sensible decisions. Regular vacuuming with a suction-only setting protects the pile from snagging or unnecessary wear. Avoid beater bars that can yank fibers—the rug will forgive you; your vacuum will not.

Spill management is where you earn silent hero points. Blot immediately with a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb liquid. Avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fiber and make your cleaning efforts feel like a small science experiment gone wrong. For stubborn stains, a wool-safe cleaner applied to a cloth is preferable to spraying directly on the rug. Always test a small, inconspicuous area before applying any cleaning solution to ensure colorfastness and texture integrity.

Robust cleaning, if needed, is best handled by a professional cleaner every 1-2 years, depending on foot traffic and environmental conditions. A professional can refresh pile height, restore color vibrancy, and extract dirt that a vacuum can’t reach. ## Buying Guide: What to Look For When Selecting a Hand-Tufted Rug

When shopping for a hand-tufted rug, there are several telltale signs of quality. The pile height should be evenly cut, and the tuft density should feel uniform across the surface. A well-executed tufted piece exhibits consistent pattern alignment, indicating careful craftsmanship. The backing should be stable and secure, preventing fraying and edge unraveling over time. Edges should be cleanly bound—this is where the rug reveals its true durability in daily life.

If the rug includes a fiber content label, verify that wool is the primary component with a compatible backing that suits your flooring and foot traffic. The color should be even across the surface, with no patchy shading. A rug that feels substantial when you press into it, yet still responsive to foot pressure, is typically a sign of quality wool and thoughtful construction.
