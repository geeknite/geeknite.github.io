### The Rules in a Nutshell
UNO Stacko rules blend a few UNO mechanics with the physical gameplay of Jenga-style blocks. The goal is to be the last player who can still legally place a block without toppling the tower. In practice, players take turns removing a block from anywhere in the stack and placing it on top, all while observing UNO-based color or symbol matching rules. The exact mechanics can vary by edition, so be sure to consult your specific print; however, the flavor remains consistent: color and symbol matching drive the action, and misplacing a block invites a dramatic cascade that becomes the family’s new favorite meme.

Given the EUC COMPLETE condition, you will likely enjoy a few house rules to dial in the fun. Some groups prefer strict adherence to color matching, while others lean into Uno-like action cards that tell you to skip a turn or switch hands. Regardless of your variant, the core thrill remains: you are playing a game that feels inevitable when the tower teeters, and you become a tiny engineer balancing the tower’s fate with each precise placement.

## Maintenance, Condition, and How to Keep It Fresh
### Condition Codes and Care
EUC COMPLETE implies minimal wear, complete contents, and a box that still has some life left in its hinges. Real-world care means keeping the set in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading, and keeping the blocks free of dust so the colors stay crisp under the indoor lighting you use for your retro unboxings. If you notice any dry wood or cardboard fatigue, apply a very light touch with a protective spray or a gentle cloth wipe. Do not soak the blocks or submerge them in liquid; this is not a science experiment and liquid damage is never a good idea for vintage blocks.

### Completeness Checklist
- All blocks present and free of cracks
- Rule sheet legible and intact
- Box with original artwork and no severe crushing
- Any spare parts or inserts intact
- Any inserts or foam still in place that came with the edition

If you are missing a block or a page, there are friendly community sellers who specialize in vintage game parts. However, you will likely enjoy the game more with all its original parts—part of the ritual is the physical authenticity that only a complete set can provide.
