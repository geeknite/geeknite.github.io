## Strategy, hints, and house rules worth trying
If you want to maximize your odds of surviving the wild ride, here are some practical tips:
- Don’t hoard all the wild cards. You’ll want a few in reserve to counter an opponent’s big move, but you’ll benefit from having at least one reliable non-wild card in your hand as a fallback.
- Consider “color dominance” strategies with caution. For parties, a playful color strategy can be entertaining, but in a serious game, it can cause confusion and tempers to flare. Balance is key.
- Create a quick “house rules” cheat sheet before the game starts: who can stack, how many can stack, and what to do if someone shouts UNO while being blocked by a wild. If you’re playing with non-players, keep rules simpler and emphasize social interaction over perfect execution.
- If you’re new to the All Wild concept, run a quick practice round so everyone understands the scope of wilds. You’ll thank yourself later when someone doesn’t accidentally override a dozen rules in the middle of a heated moment.
