Safety Rules in Practice

No product makes children invincible, and a bounce structure is a tool that must be used with common sense. We recommend supervision, a clear area around the trampoline with enough ceiling height to avoid head bumps, and a no-shoes rule to keep the surface clean and free of grit that could chafe tiny toes. It’s also wise to establish a basic set of ground rules: one jumper at a time during complex moves, no roughhousing near the net, and a periodic check of the net’s tension and the frame’s stability. In fact, a quick weekly inspection of the springs, net attachment points, and padding is a small investment of time for a potentially large payoff in safety and longevity. For additional safety context, consider reviewing kid safety guidelines from reputable sources like consumer toy safety standards, and ensure your installation respects ceiling and door clearance—nobody wants a mid-air collision with a ceiling fan or a stubborn light fixture.

Maintenance and Longevity

The key to long life for any indoor trampoline is routine maintenance. Check the springs for rust or wear, inspect the net for tears or loose fasteners, and wipe the jumping surface to remove dust and sticky residues; kids produce plenty of those. Keep the padding clean and dry to prevent mold or mildew in damp climates. If you notice a sag in the net or a loose frame corner, tighten it promptly; small adjustments now save bigger headaches later. Most components are designed for daily use over many months, with occasional disassembly for deep cleaning not required unless you’re performing a full room makeover. With proper care, the Uimoso 60-inch trampoline can comfortably become a family staple, a reliable playtime anchor in the winter months and a pleasant energy outlet in the spring and summer storms.
