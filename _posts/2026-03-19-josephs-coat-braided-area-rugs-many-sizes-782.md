## Care tips to keep your rug looking legendary

Caring for braided rugs is not rocket science but it does require a plan. Start with routine vacuuming to prevent grit from embedding itself into the fibers. For spills, blot the area with a clean cloth rather than rubbing; rubbing tends to push the spill deeper into the braid, which can lead to staining or discoloration over time. For stubborn stains, test a mild cleaner on a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Avoid harsh chemical agents that may degrade the fibers and colors. If the rug begins to curl at edges, you can place a few heavy objects along the border for a day or two to flatten it back into place. Regular rotation can help ensure even wear across the surface, particularly if your space has a predictable sun path that can fade certain areas more quickly.

A little maintenance goes a long way, and the moment you adopt a simple care routine, your rug will reward you with years of use, a few extra compliments from guests, and perhaps the occasional nod from your own inner designer deity.
