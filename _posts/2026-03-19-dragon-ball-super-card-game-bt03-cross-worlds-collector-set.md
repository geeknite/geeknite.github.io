## Card Design and Mechanics
BT03 Cross Worlds leans into synergy that spans across universes without turning the game into a chess match across parallel dimensions. The design team seems to have heard the cries of players who want more deck-building flexibility and less barrier to entry for new archetypes. The core design carries a motif of multi-sphere interactions where a unit from Universe A can interact with a support from Universe B with new synergies. The practical upshot is that you get to explore new combos that feel both intuitive and surprising.

From a mechanical perspective, you’ll encounter a few new keywords and interactions that feel fresh but still very much in the family. The goal seems to be to reward players who plan ahead but also to reward smart plays in the heat of a game. There are design choices that encourage midrange tempo, while also offering flashier finishers for players who want to close out with a big play.

If you’ve already built a BT02 deck that relies on tempo and pressure, BT03 can slot into your plan by offering additional ways to loop or recycle key cards. On the other hand, if you tend to lean into heavy draw-power or "play-and-break" strategies, you may find BT03 introduces a few counters that keep you honest. The risk with any new set is that some interactions can be over-tuned or under-tested, but BT03 appears to strike a balance that should be accessible to both newcomers and veterans.

Artwork and flavor are consistent with the rest of the series, but with a few nods to cross-world lore. You’ll notice cross-font typography accents that give a sense of a world-wide collaboration (because it is). The characters are drawn with dynamic poses and bright backgrounds that look great in a binder display or a tournament stage.
