## How to Play: Quick Rules Refresher and the Joy of the Flip

UNO Flip! preserves the core cadence of classic UNO: you must match by color or by number, you yell UNO when you have one card left, and you hope your opponents forget to call you out for cheating the rules with a dramatic flourish. Where UNO Flip! diverges is the moment-to-moment experience when the deck flips to the darker side, turning a routine round into a show-stopping turn of events.

- Setup: Shuffle the light-side cards into the draw pile and deal seven cards to each player. The discard pile starts with one card from the light side.
- Turn flow: On your turn, play a card that matches color or number. If you can\'t play, draw a card. The tempo is familiar, the stakes are about to go up a notch.
- The flip moment: a Flip happens (either by a Flip card or by rule-based triggers) causing the deck to reveal its dark side. On the dark side, action cards behave differently—Draw Two on the light side might become Draw Five on the dark side, demands become harsher, and the tension skyrockets.
- Ending the round: When a player has one card, they must call UNO. If they forget and someone catches them, the classic penalty awaits.

The two-faced setup creates natural teachable moments. New players quickly learn the rhythm on the light side, and as soon as the flip hits, even veterans lean forward to anticipate the next twist. The paced uncertainty is the real joy here: you think you know what\'s coming, and suddenly you don\'t. It\'s like the color-blind friend who finally gets to enjoy a slightly less predictable spectrum of chaos.
