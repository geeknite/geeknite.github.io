## Maintenance and cleaning tips

Maintenance is where the practical magic happens. Regular vacuuming with a low-suction setting keeps dirt from nesting in the pile. If your vacuum has a beater bar, keep it off the rug to avoid unnecessary agitation. Place a doormat at every entry to curb the worst of the grime before it lands on the fibers. Spills should be blotted immediately rather than rubbed, starting from the outside and moving inward to prevent spreading. For more stubborn stains, a mild cleaner designed for synthetic fibers is usually enough. Avoid aggressive solvents that can strip color or damage the pile. After cleaning, air out the rug to ensure it dries evenly and avoids patchy look from damp spots.

If you have curious pets, rotating the rug occasionally helps distribute wear and avoids one patch of the rug becoming the dog’s favorite sunbeam throne. A rug pad underneath is a wise investment; it reduces slipping, adds a cushiony feel underfoot, and can extend the rug’s life by minimizing wear at the edges.
