## How to Play: The Core Loop
If you’ve played party games before, Card Sharks Trivia Game has a familiar cadence, but it adds enough spicy mechanisms to feel fresh. Here’s a practical walk-through so you can start your own kitchen-table episode of drama tonight:

### Step 1: Setup and Roles
- Each player or team gets a seat at the table, their loyalty to the group confirmed by the most competitive mug collection on the shelf.
- Decide on a moderator or host. If you don’t have one, you can rotate the role with a timer and a skeptical eyebrow raise for dramatic effect.
- Shuffle the trivia deck. Place the high/low deck within reach. Put the chips in the center, reachable only by the player who insists they have a better memory than the rest of the crew.

### Step 2: The Trivia Round
- A trivia card is drawn and read aloud. The question isn’t just “what’s the answer?” It’s “what do you think helps you survive the emotional ride of this round?”
- Players attempt to answer or pass to the next risk layer. If you answer correctly, you win a base prize; if you’re wildly incorrect, you might owe a penalty or suffer the mock-gavel of your tablemates.
- The host reveals a card from the high/low deck to determine the potential risk window for the round.

### Step 3: The High/Low Phase
- After the trivia question resolves, the active player chooses high or low on the next card, betting on the probability of the card’s value relative to what’s already shown. This is where math meets swagger, and you learn which of your friends is secretly plotting a math degree in their spare time.
- The outcome affects chips and set-aside rewards. If you guess right, you collect; if you’re wrong, you pay the price and adopt the stoic face of a person who just saw a calculator explode in slow motion.

### Step 4: The Tie-Breakers and Endgame
- In case of a tie, you can trigger a tie-breaker trivia duel or a sudden-death high/low challenge. The rules might vary by edition, but the spirit remains: stakes go up, egos go up even more, and laughter goes up like a Doppler radar on a pride parade.
- The game ends when a player or team reaches a pre-set score threshold, or when the group decides that a final, gloriously ridiculous round should crown the true Card Shark of the night.

## Strategy Corner: How to Think Like a Card Shark
We’re not saying you should cheat. We’re saying you should understand the language of risk. Here are some practical strategies to maximize your odds and maximize the fun:

### Read the Room, Then Read the Deck
- Watch your fellow players’ tells. Not the kind of tells that involve a broken smile or a sudden gulp, but the tells of how they play: do they lean into risk when they’re ahead, or do they buckle when the pressure is on?
- Remember that the deck isn’t a perfect randomizer. It has rhythms. If a string of high cards appears, the odds may swing in a surprising way for the next round. Use that to adjust your bets, not your dignity.

### Know Your Odds, Not Your Ego
- A rough rule of thumb is that you should not chase a risky bet when you’re behind unless your group is ready for a glorious disaster. But if you’re leading and you want to amplify the drama, you can occasionally swing for the fences—just be prepared to karaoke your defeat with style.

### Memorize the Question Categories
- Different categories have different probability curves. Memorizing where your strengths lie helps you place smarter bets or pass to a teammate who knows the culture of the niche topic better than you do.

### Time Your Gambits
- The timing of a Double Down or a bold high/low choice matters as much as the choice itself. A well-timed gambit can turn a quiet night into a bragging-rights festival that lasts until someone loses their keys and blames the referee.

### Practice Makes Confidence, Not Perfection
- The more you play, the more you’ll see the patterns. You’ll become less likely to panic at a tense moment and more likely to savor the moment when a perfectly calibrated bet pays off in a spectacular, show-stopping reveal.
