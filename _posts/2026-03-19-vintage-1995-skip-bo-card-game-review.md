## How to Play: The Core Mechanics in Plain Speak
Skip-Bo’s essence is deceptively simple: build piles in ascending order using the cards from your hand, your stock pile, and a few discard piles. The game is designed for 2 to 6 players and supports quick rounds that can swing with a single misplay or lucky draw.

### The Goal
Be the first player to empty your stock pile. The stock pile is face-down, and you can turn over the top card when you need to play to a Build pile. The more nimble you are with your hand and the wilds, the faster you will win. The number 1 through 12 cards comprise the building piles. You can only place the next needed card on a Build pile. Wild Skip-Bo cards can stand in for any number when constructing a Build pile.

### The Turn Structure (A Quick Sketch)
- Draw back up to five cards if you have fewer than five in hand.
- On your turn, you may play cards from your hand, the top of your stock pile, or your discards onto the four Build piles, in ascending order from 1 to 12.
- You can start as many Build piles as you can with the cards in your hand and stock, but you must follow the ascending order rule.
- When you can’t play, you end your turn and the next player goes.

The elegance of Skip-Bo is that you often chain a series of plays. One card from your hand might allow you to place a card from your stock, which in turn frees your hand to do a discard move or to reveal another card on your stock pile. The wilds (Skip-Bo cards) are your trump cards: they let you fill gaps or pivot a stalled Build pile forward when the table starts to resemble a bad game of Tetris with vertical piles instead of blocks.
