## Cleaning and Maintenance

### Stain Resistance

So you spilled something magical on it Do not panic. Blot from the outside in, avoid scrubbing, and consider a mild detergent if needed. The rug s fiber tends to hold onto stains less aggressively than a white shirt at a pizza party.

### Vacuuming and Hair

Pet hair is the comedy villain of any rug review; this rug handles it about as well as you d expect from a product designed for a modern, busy home. A standard vacuum picks up most of the hair without too much fuss. For longer hairs, a gentle brush attachment can help gather fuzz before vacuuming. We found that weekly maintenance kept the rug looking fresh and free from the lint trap look that makes you question your life choices.

### Moisture and Humidity

You don t want a swamp in your living room, and this rug isn t one. It dries quickly after small spills, and the backing doesn t retain moisture. However, you should be mindful in high humidity climates; the rug s fibers can feel slightly more damp for a short period after a spill, so lay it out in a well ventilated area if you re in a sauna-like environment.

## Aesthetics and Decor Synergy

### Pairing with Color Palettes

Grey is the ultimate chameleon, which is convenient for a geek who has a closet full of gray t shirts and a cat who only approves of chrome accents. The Tiger City Greyology Rug pairs well with bold accent colors as well as muted palettes. For a high-contrast look, try black and neon; for a subdued vibe, pair with soft blues and forest greens. The rug s zoning friendly color profile means it complements both industrial lofts and mid century modern spaces.

### Lighting and Mood

Lighting can change the rug s mood from serious business to cozy retreat. Warm LED lights bring out the stone undertones, while cooler lighting can reveal the subtle graphite flecks in the fibers. If you re a gamer, set the room to a moody blue to make the gray feel like it belongs in a spaceship.

### Room Scale and Rug Size Planning

Sizing matters. A rug that s too small can look like a miscalculated sticker; one that s too big can swallow the room. For a typical 12x14 living space, a rug in the 8x10 or 9x12 range provides a felt page that anchors furniture and creates a cohesive zone. In smaller rooms, consider an 6x9 rug to define a seating area without overpowering. The general rule is you want all legs of the sofa and chairs to rest on the rug or at least have the front legs on it, creating a unified plane.

## Care Tips and Longevity

- Rotate the rug every few months to distribute wear evenly
- Use an underlay to reduce movement and add extra padding
- Blot spills quickly; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber disruption
- Vacuum with the beater bar off or on a gentle setting to reduce the chance of flattening the fibers
- Clean with a mild detergent solution for stubborn stains; always test in an inconspicuous area first
