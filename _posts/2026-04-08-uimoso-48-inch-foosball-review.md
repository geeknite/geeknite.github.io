## Maintenance, Longevity, and Cleaning

Foosball tables, much like small mammals and software updates, require a little routine care to stay healthy. The Uimoso is no exception. Regular maintenance will keep it playing smoothly for years:

- Wipe the rods and handles with a microfiber cloth to remove dust and oil. A little maintenance goes a long way toward keeping the grip feeling new.
- Lubrication: if your rods feel sticky, a small amount of silicone-based lubricant on the joints can work wonders. Do not use WD-40 or home-brew remedies; your table is not a laboratory experiment and you will regret the oily fingerprints on your walls.
- Check the screws and caps occasionally. Loose hardware can lead to wobble or misalignment, and nobody wants a wobbly stadium where the ball ricochets into your coffee cup.
- Ball maintenance: if the balls get nicked or grooved, replace them. Surfaces with rough patches will affect ball trajectory and your mental health during crucial moments of a game.

Longevity-wise, the Uimoso is built to tolerate the typical wear-and-tear of home use and the occasional chaotic game-night. The aesthetic finish remains appealing even after months of heavy use, which is a small miracle in a world full of products that start impressive and end up looking like a scavenged shelf from a thrift store.
