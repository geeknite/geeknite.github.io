NIB 2015 Exploding Kittens Original Edition is a time capsule wrapped in pink neon and laughter. This is the version of the card game that started the entire meme economy of cat cards with bombs. It arrived in your friendly neighborhood mailer, sealed with a promise to ruin friendships in the most adorable way possible. If you remember a time when cats were internet royalty and every party needed a little chaos, this is your artifact.

## Quick pitch

Exploding Kittens is a party card game designed for 2 to 5 players, though hardcore fans claim you can stretch it to a few more with clever table diplomacy and liberal application of rules lawyering. The premise is simple and glorious: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten, or possess a Defuse card to negate the blast, and maybe create a little chaos for others along the way. The original edition from 2015 introduced the core deck, the Defuse cards, and the iconic bomb-kitty art that would haunt sleeved card sleeves for years.

## In the box and physical quality

The Original Edition box is a compact rectangle with bold, cheeky art by The Oatmeal, who did not apologize for making you fear a tactical kitten with a bomb-shaped grin. Inside, you’ll find a deck of cards and a few Defuse cards that act as lifelines when fate throws a glittery napalm at you.

In the 2015 run, the cardstock is sturdy enough to survive at least one game night, with moderate wear if you’re a chaotic player who likes shoving sleeves into the middle. The cards have a slightly glossy finish that catches light the way a cat catches a laser pointer: with alarming precision. The Defuse cards feature a white-labeled bomb deflector with a friendly vibe, which is essential since most of your opponents will want to save you only to humiliate you later.

## How to play in about 5 minutes (don’t blink)

If you’ve played any Dixit or Uno derivatives, you’ll adjust quickly. The deck comprises Exploding Kitten cards, Defuse cards, and a variety of action cards that push or pull the turn order, shuffle the deck, peek at future cards, or steal someone else’s Defuse—because why not turn a life-saving card into a bargaining chip?

Setup is fast: shuffle, deal a small hand to each player, and place the rest of the deck in the center. Each player retains one Defuse card for their personal safety net. On your turn, you draw a card. Most of the time, you just hope the next card isn’t the dreaded Exploding Kitten. If you draw one, you can attempt to Defuse it if you hold a Defuse card; otherwise, you’re out. The game continues until only one kitten-free human remains.

The pacing is where the magic happens. You’re balancing risk and bluffing your friends into discarding Defuse cards, using Attack and See the Future to tilt the odds, and praying someone else will explode before you. It’s a rollercoaster of micro-decisions that feel bigger than the box suggests.

### See the future, if you can stomach the suspense

One of the signature cards is See the Future, allowing you to peek at the top few cards. It’s a delightful moment of pretend clairvoyance: you pretend you can foresee the doom you’re about to inflict on your friends, while secretly praying your own fate is sealed with a safer card. It’s the kind of power that makes you feel like a wizard who learned magic from a cat-shaped textbook.

## The NIB experience and why it matters

New In Box status matters for nostalgia junkies and collectors who want to own a pristine copy of a game that became a cultural meme before it became a phenomenon. An unopened 2015 edition is a rare beast; the packaging is not just plastic. It’s a keepsake that signals to your friends that yes, you own the edition that started it all. If you’re a completionist shopper, you’ll appreciate the minty scent of untouched cardboard, the clean card stock, and the satisfaction of hearing the crisp rattle of plastic wrap as you slide the rulebook from the box.

Now, a reality check: the original edition has inspired countless reprints and variations. The original card art is distinct, the font has a particular ‘early meme glory’ vibe, and the game balance is tuned for a wild, unapologetic party experience. If you’re buying it today, you’re not just purchasing a game; you’re buying a slice of internet history that continues to ripple through memes and casual gaming circles.


## Artwork, humor, and culture clash

The Oatmeal’s art is loud, silly, and occasionally savage. It’s a visual language of friendly cruelty that matches the game’s tone: you’re encouraged to push, bluff, and occasionally betray your friends for a laugh. The humor lands best in social settings with the right mix of drunken bravado and friendly resentment. It’s not a game for every crowd. If your group values quiet strategy and long, tense ennui, this is not your jam. If you like chaotic energy, clever trash-talking, and the occasional cat pun that lands way too well, this is your jam.

Also, yes, there are tasteful but not universally safe-for-work jokes in the deck and the artwork. If you’re playing with kids, consider side-skirting some of the content or swapping for a family-friendly edition. The beauty of Exploding Kittens is that it can be spiky or wholesome depending on your crew, your drink of choice, and how much you value your friendships in a night of mid-weight chaos.


## Strategy, luck, and the art of not exploding

If you want to come off as a cunning mastermind, you’ll need a healthy mix of luck and calculation. The Defuse cards are players’ lifelines; keep track of how many Defuses are out there and who might still hold one. Attack cards can shove risk onto others, See the Future gives you information that reduces anxiety, and Shuffle can ruin a carefully laid plan, which is often the point. The more you play, the more you realize this is not a game about perfect play; it’s a game about controlling uncertainty and turning chaos into hilarious outcomes.

One memorable moment in any Exploding Kittens night is when someone reveals a card that completely flips the table in a glorious, silly way. A perfect defuse, followed by a big Attack, is the kind of combo that makes you feel like you’ve choreographed a mini-drama on the kitchen table. That’s the magic: simple rules, big laughs, and the occasional moment where someone screams in a way that sounds exactly like the cat on the cover.


## Variants, expansions, and the long tail of the brand

The 2015 Original Edition is just the beginning. Exploding Kittens expanded with the Imploding Kittens expansion in 2017, introducing new kitten-card mechanics and some flavorful chaos. A few years later, the Party Pack and other spin-offs pushed the same concept into different shapes: bigger groups, different rules, and the same vibe of ridiculous, chaotic fun. If you like the base game, you’ll probably enjoy the expansions for the added chaos and more opportunities to mess with friends. If you’re a purist, the original edition remains a fantastic gateway and keepsake that still plays well.

A note on reprints: newer printings often adjust card quality and balance, but the core feel remains. If you’re chasing that 2015 edition aesthetic, you’ll want to verify the edition on the box and the exact card art, as some reissues may have subtle changes. Collectors, though, will savor comparing print runs and tracking differences in the tiny details that only a true nerd would notice.


## Audience fit and social context

Who should buy Exploding Kittens Original Edition? People who enjoy fast, cheeky party games that don’t require long setup or deep strategic planning. It’s ideal for casual gatherings, game nights with friends who appreciate humor and mild strategic banter, and anyone who wants a game that’s easy to explain but hard to master because of the unpredictable card interactions.

Who should skip it? If you’re allergic to noisy laughter, you don’t have a sense of humor about cat-themed explosions, or you need a game with heavy strategy and long-term planning, you probably want something a little more restrained. Also, if you’re looking for a game that scales to more than five players regularly, you might be disappointed in the core version and should consider party-focused variants or social deduction games instead.


## Collectibility and resale value

Because the 2015 edition has that New In Box mystique, it often commands a premium on the secondary market. If you’re a collector or you just want to flaunt a pristine example of the genesis of Exploding Kittens, a sealed NIB copy can be a brag-worthy item. Prices vary with market demand, but the allure of owning the original art and packaging remains strong. The game isn’t rare or valuable in the sense of a vintage board game investment, but if you’re chasing nostalgia and the experience of unsealing a mint copy, the NIB 2015 run is the gold standard.


## The Geeknite verdict

Overall, Exploding Kittens Original Edition is a timeless party game that delivers more laughs per minute than almost any other title in the same weight class. It rewards bold play, clever card play, and a willingness to embrace chaos without taking yourself too seriously. Its New In Box status adds an extra layer of charm for collectors who love the moment of cracking open a mint-tasting plastic wrap and discovering the little treasures hidden inside.

Pros:
- Lightning-fast setup and quick rounds
- The humor and art land for most groups
- Defuse cards provide a dramatic lifeline when doom looms
- Pretty excellent for party nights and casual gaming sessions

Cons:
- Borderline chaotic for players who want steady, strategic play
- Some jokes may feel dated or edgy in certain crowds
- The base game can feel a bit short if everyone is on fire with hand luck

Tips for max fun:
- Agree on a light-hearted tone before you start so the table doesn’t devolve into petty rivalries
- Keep track of Defuse cards so you’re not surprised by an early bag of doom
- Use See the Future strategically to read the deck’s mood before the next draw

### Final tips for the curious reader
If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, borrow a copy from a friend first, if possible. If you’re jumping in with an NIB 2015 edition, enjoy the ritual of unsealing and letting the room’s mood shift with the first blast. And if you’re a seasoned player from the pre-imploding era, you’ll appreciate how this game carved a path for the modern meme-driven board game landscape. It’s not just a card game; it’s a performance piece for your living room, complete with a chorus of cat-shaped chaos.
