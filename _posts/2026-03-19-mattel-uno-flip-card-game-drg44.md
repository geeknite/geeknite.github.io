## How UNO Flip Works
UNO Flip is basically UNO with a plot twist and a mood swing. It starts just like regular UNO, then the deck bites you with a flip to the dark side. The core mechanic is simple: play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the top card in the discard pile. When you flip to the dark side, the action cards intensify, and the pace picks up like a rollercoaster with a stuck seatbelt.

### The Light Side
On the light side, you are back to familiar UNO rules. You match color or number, you can play wild cards to change color, and you try to avoid being the last player holding a handful of nervous energy. The light side has its own set of action cards such as Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, and Wild. The goal remains the same: get rid of all your cards and shout UNO before the other players notice your eye twitch.

### The Dark Side
Flip the deck and suddenly the game becomes a tiny bit more chaotic. The dark side brings more aggressive penalties like the infamous Draw Five and wild flips that can turn a calm table into a chaotic dance of groans and laughs. The rulebook keeps it straightforward: when the dark side is active, use the dark side action cards just as you would expect, which means more consequences and more opportunities to turn an ordinary Sunday into a wild party.

### The Flip Mechanism
The iconic Flip card is the gateway between light and dark. Flip it, and the entire game shifts. Suddenly your red, blue, yellow, and green palette is replaced by a bold, dramatic, high-contrast vibe. The flip adds a new layer of strategy: do you flip early to disrupt others or hold on to see what the dark side will throw at you? And yes, there are moments when someone forgets to flip at just the wrong moment, leading to a chorus of wait what from the spectators.

### Strategy and House Rules
No game is perfect, and UNO Flip invites a handful of house rules to tailor the chaos to your crowd. Common tweaks include:
- Allowing players to call UNO silently versus loudly
- Implementing a mercy rule after a dark side Draw Five spree
- Keeping a timer for fast rounds to keep anxiety levels in check
- Rotating the dealer to ensure nobody hogs the turn
Experiment with these rules and you may unlock a new level of family bonding or competitive shouting, depending on how much you enjoy your relatives.

At max capacity, UNO Flip becomes a group sport. You need more space for the deck and more laughter per minute. ## Quick Start Guide
1. Shuffle the UNO Flip deck and deal 7 cards to each player.
2. Place the remaining deck face down to form the draw pile.
3. Flip the top card to create the discard pile on the light side
4. On your turn, match color or number, or play a wild card to change color
5. If you have no playable card, draw from the draw pile
6. Flip to dark side when someone hits the turn threshold and watch the chaos unfold
7. The first player to get rid of all their cards wins the hand and earns bragging rights for at least one snack break

House rules
- UNO call with a fun twist
- Timer for fast rounds
- Mercy rules after heavy draws
