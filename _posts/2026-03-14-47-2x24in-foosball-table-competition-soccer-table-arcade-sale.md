## Assembly and maintenance: the practical bits
Like many gaming tables, this one ships in a few boxes that require a little elbow grease and a lot of patience. Assembly times can range from 1 to 3 hours depending on your level of assembly sublimity and how many YouTube tutorial shuffle videos you watch while the screws pretend to be shy. It’s a project, but not a dungeon crawl. The instructions are usually clear enough for a non-professional assembly enthusiast to complete with moderate success and the occasional emoji-laden feedback comment from an over-exuberant neighbor. If you want tips from the trenches: lay out all screws by type, don’t over-tighten the rods, and test the ball return after you’ve got the frame standing tall but before you decide to redecorate the room with foam darts and vintage posters.

Maintenance is minimal but not magical. Wipe the playfield with a damp microfiber cloth to remove dust and spills. Check the rod bearings periodically; you’ll feel increased resistance if a bearing is misaligned or if a small pebble has found its way into a groove. The key is to prevent rust and minimize grime that can degrade spin. Some players give their rods a light coat of silicone spray for smoother play; others prefer a dry lube for quiet operation. Pick your poison, but test on a small area first so you don’t accidentally turn the surface into a slip-and-slide arena.
