### How to play this edition: a practical quick-start
Everything you know about UNO Flip remains valid here, with a handful of extra spicy takes inspired by Bakugo’s persona. The rules generally go like this:
- Start with a shuffled deck; deal 7 cards to each player; the rest becomes draw pile.
- One card is flipped face-up to begin the discard pile.
- On your turn, you match the top card’s color or number, or you play a wild card as per standard UNO Flip rules.
- When the flip-side of the deck is active, the color palette changes and certain action cards take on different effects. This is where the Bakugo-themed additions float in: look for Attack-style cards, quick-draw sequences, or extra draw mechanics that simulate a “explosion” moment in the game.
- Some editions include a card that triggers when you draw on your turn; this can be a risk-reward moment where you’re tempted to gamble on the flip.
- The goal is to be the first to shed all your cards; if you’re the last one standing, you’re inevitably the lead hero of the table, even if you’re wearing your most chaotic “enjoy the chaos” face.
