## What is this edition all about?

## Gameplay Overview

### Setup and Turn Structure
Generally, you’ll deal each player five cards to start. On your turn, you draw two cards, then you can play up to three cards from your hand. You’ll curate a mix of property cards to build your color sets and use money and action cards to stall, defend, or steal from others. The goal is to assemble three full property sets. The Collector's Edition doesn’t change the math of the win condition; it simply makes the drive to that win feel a touch more elegant because the box looks like it belongs on a shelf next to a limited-edition action figure.

Turn order is clean but chaotic: you’ll face the classic decision tree of “Do I deploy a property now, hold for a better moment, or unleash a rent attack?” The correct choice is often: yes, but only if you can survive the counter-attack next turn. The tension is real, and the laughs are frequent—the game rewards bold moves and punishing mistakes with equal enthusiasm.

### Strategy and Tactics
If you’re playing for the long haul, the Collector's Edition doesn’t magically hand you extra brainpower. It does, however, encourage you to consider the following tactics:
- Early tempo: Aim to set up at least one potential three-set path by mid-game; rely on steal or rent cards to keep opponents’ cash in flux.
- Stacking and taxing: The more money you can extract from others with timely rent plays, the closer you are to victory. But beware the counter-strike: a well-timed Just Say No can ruin your day, and your plan for a dramatic and decorative finish.
- Card management: Don’t let your hand swell with useless cards. In the Collector's Edition, you may be tempted to hoard foiled beauties, but the deck still rewards lean, purposeful plays.
- Targeted aggression: Some players are easier to exploit than others. If your opponent is running a color-heavy strategy, consider a timely sale or steal to disrupt their rhythm.

### House Rules and Real-World Play
As with any party game, it’s common to adopt house rules to adapt the game to your group. A popular variant is the “3-Strike Rule,” where a player is out after three rounds of being unlucky with deals. Another is the “End-game Nerf,” which reduces the impact of one powerhouse action card to keep the ending from turning into a bureaucratic epic. The Collector's Edition is compatible with these tweaks, and the premium art sometimes inspires players to try wilder, more imaginative rules.
