### Care and maintenance basics
Proper care extends the life of any wool rug. Here are core practices:
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle suction to remove surface dust. Avoid rotating the rug against a stiff beater; instead, work from one edge to the other.
- Rotate periodically to promote even wear, especially in high-traffic areas or under heavy furniture.
- Address spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing; blot, don’t scrub. Work from the outside of the spill toward the center to avoid spreading.
- Consider a professional cleaning every year or two, depending on traffic and household conditions.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slippage and protect the weave from friction against hard floors. A pad also adds an extra layer of cushioning and can help with temperature regulation in winter.

## Practical tips for getting the most out of Kos in your home
- Place the rug in a high-traffic zone but avoid it being a complete see-through under a heavy coffee table. If possible, allow the front legs of your sofa to rest on the rug, which creates a visually cohesive seating area and protects the floor.
- Use a rug pad to reduce movement and provide extra cushion. A pad helps improve foot comfort and protects the rug’s backing from wear.
- Consider the rug’s direction in rooms with doorways. If your doorway’s swing could collide with fringe or edges, test placement and ensure that the rug’s outline remains intact when the door is opened.
- Rotate seasonally or as needed to ensure even wear, especially if your rug sits in a front-facing sunlit area. Wool handles sunlight differently than synthetic fibers, so rotating can keep the color stable for longer.
- When in doubt about cleaning, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in wool; avoid aggressive home machines that could misinterpret the weave and the fiber’s natural properties.
