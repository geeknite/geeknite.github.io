## Introduction
The Infinite 4P Cornerstation with Screens is not just a desk, it is a statement. A behemoth of wood and screens engineered for four humans who refuse to sit in a line like some boring factory assembly. This review dives into what it takes to live with a cornerstation that claims to be the hub of a four person creative cluster. We will talk fit for four, screen magic, cable mazes, and how this thing fits into a nerdy living room.

## What is this beast
The model ships as a massive L shaped footprint with four work zones that meet at the corner. Each zone has its own screen mount, keyboard tray, and a little privacy panel that doubles as a mini sound barrier when the room gets loud during a late night raid. The overall footprint is 3630 by 3030 millimeters and the height is 1200 millimeters. That is large enough to swallow a small coffee table and still leave space for a DnD map.

## Who is it for
If you run a small LAN party, a co working space for four, or a family that treats home desks as serious gaming hardware, this unit promises to be the shared brain of the room. It is not a cheap setup, but it aims to deliver a sense of command center vibe that a typical desk stack cannot.

## Design and build quality
Oak White finish is a daring choice in the age of glass and metal. The wood is real, the panels are thick, and the corners are rounded in a way that prevents accidental finger wizardry injuries. The assembly manual reads like a quest log, with step by step tasks that require a high tolerance for tiny screws and patience. The integration of screens into the edge of each station is where the product earns its sci fi vibes, as if you could summon a holographic map by flicking a switch.

### Materials and finish
The Oak White combination means the top surfaces show a warm oak grain while the frame surfaces are coated with a white, matte finish that resists sticky fingerprints from midday snack runners. The result is a desk that reads as premium but behaves like a real world object with a few quirks. The wood is solid with minimal warp risk in normal climate ranges; the paint coats are robust but not scratch resistant from heavy use.

### Hardware and assembly
The hardware kit includes a metric treasure chest of screws, washers, and a set of allen wrenches that would make a music festival organizer green with envy. The assembly is not for the faint of heart. It takes two people at least four hours if you want to avoid cursing at the instructions. The good news is that most pieces are labeled clearly and the alignment guides actually help you avoid misalignment of screen arms.

## Dimensions and space planning
Big products demand big spaces. The 3630 by 3030 footprint creates a corner that is both dramatic and efficient. The 1200 height allows comfortable elbow room for all four seats while still fitting under most home lighting fixtures. If you plan to place this in a room with a shallow ceiling, you will want to check the clearance above the top edge. The corners are chamfered to avoid snagging on wall trim. The stands provide a broad stance that reduces wobble, even when you push away from the desk during a heated gaming moment.

### Ergonomics
Ergonomics on a four seat unit is a challenging puzzle. Each seat should be at roughly the same distance from its respective screen to avoid neck strain. The modular nature of the corner sections means you can adjust each zone individually, at least in theory. The reality is that four grown adults will inevitably test the limits of the slider rails. If one person likes a higher monitor, and another likes a lower keyboard tray, you can accommodate by adjusting each zone and using the privacy panel to create the separation needed for a work group.

## Features and tech
The core feature is the integrated screen architecture. Each of the four corners has a mounting system that can support a pair of monitors up to 27 inches, or one ultrawide per station. The screens are tilt adjustable and can be rotated to portrait mode for code reviews or design sprint boards. The privacy panels double as acoustic dampeners that help to reduce the cascade of keyboard clacks and mouse clicks. A discrete cable management grommet line runs along the back of the desk, feeding cables through a central trough out of sight.

### Cable management and power
The unit ships with a power strip mounted in a central location. Cable management is good, though you will want additional ties for an optimal presentation. If you are heavy on USB-C dongles and external drives, you will want a separate docking station or two to keep the desk clutter under control. There is nothing magical about the cable management, but it is well thought out and will save you hours of slightly annoyed re-routing.

### Lighting and ambience
An optional LED light strip runs along the underside of the main beam. It can be controlled by a small, dedicated controller that sits in a pocket near the corner. The lighting adds a sci fi glow that makes late night play sessions more cinematic and less garage sale.

### Accessories and add-ons
One of the joys of a large desk is the ability to add a smartphone stand, a beverage cooler, or even a tiny coffee maker. The manufacturer offers a set of modular accessories that can be attached to the back edge of the desk. You can mount a webcam, a mic arm, or a small shelf for your favorite snacks.

### Comparisons
In the world of four person setups, there are a few players. Compare this model to a set of four separate desks pushed into a corner versus a premium single desk. The advantage of the Infinite unit is cohesion and a shared cable and power network. The downsides include higher upfront cost and a greater footprint. For a more budget friendly option, look at modular desks that can be arranged into a similar shape but built from cheaper materials. For a more premium experience, consider a full standing desk system with modular screens.

### Real world usage
In test environments with four participants, the unit performed well for gaming sessions, programming marathons, and group video calls. The screens are bright enough for dim rooms and the speaker system on the laptop stands is impractical for quality audio, but it is enough to hear the voice chat on Discord. For long sessions, the seating arrangement remains comfortable, though a couple of people reported minor fatigue from reaching to the far left and far right zones. The four seat arrangement encourages collaboration but also requires etiquette to avoid the occasional elbow in the wrong place.

### Maintenance and longevity
The oak finish holds up well to everyday use, if you avoid dragging heavy chairs across the top. The white frame resists staining from coffee and snack debris, but you should still wipe down weekly to prevent the dreaded sticky finger syndrome. The modular nature of the segments means you can reconfigure the layout if you decide to move to a different room layout or want to rearrange for a different four person team.

## How it stacks up vs the competition
There are other options that claim to offer four person desk clusters with built in tech. Some are cheaper, some are shinier, some are more industrial. The Infinite 4P Cornerstation is a mixed bag with a strong center, but the outer arms require careful tightening to avoid wobble on rough floors. If you want the most value, it may be worth looking at alternative setups that provide modular components, easier assembly, and a smaller footprint.

### If you are after a nerdy aesthetic
The aesthetic of the cornerstation is not for those who prefer minimalism. It is for people who want to feel like they are steering a starship built from wood and monitors. The oak grain with white accents creates a design that screams sci fi while still being functional in a domestic space.

### If you want performance and ergonomic customization
The four zones give you a chance to tailor each workspace for a specific task. You can align monitors for CAD work, 3D modeling, software development, or streaming with minimal cross talk. The immerse screen setups allow for a multi monitor workflow that is less likely to jam with cables than a standard single desk.

## Maintenance tips
- Keep a microfiber cloth close to remove dust from screens and the wood surfaces.
- Use a soft brush to clear dust from in and around the cable channels.
- Check the screws every few months to ensure the wing nuts do not loosen due to people shifting the desks.

## Final verdict and recommendations
The Infinite 4P Cornerstation with Screens is a bold, capable piece of furniture for the right audience. It offers a shared workspace for four people with integrated screens that is rare in the consumer market. It is not for everyone. If you value a cohesive aesthetic, built in monitor capabilities, and the ability to arrange your office into a creative command center, this unit is worth considering. If you have a tight budget or a small room, you may want to consider more modular options or a lighter footprint.
