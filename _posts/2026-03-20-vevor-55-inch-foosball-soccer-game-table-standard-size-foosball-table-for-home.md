## Assembly Experience: Step by Step (and a Few Practical Tips)

### Step 1: Positioning and level checks
Place the cabinet where you want it, and ensure there is enough clearance for players to stand and reach the table without bumping into walls. Level the table with the included feet until the surface is perfectly even. A slight tilt can cause the ball to roll toward the edges, which is not only annoying but also unfun for your friends who think they re clever enough to exploit it.

### Step 2: Rod installation
Install the rods in pairs, following the color-coded guides. Take your time in aligning the rods so the players don t rub against the cabinet sides. This is the portion where the drama unfolds if you decide to go in blind. But with patience, the rods will click into place and you will feel the table start to come alive with the looming promise of victory.

### Step 3: Player alignment and initial tests
Attach the players in the standard formation. The goalie pieces go on the end rods, with field players and defense lined up according to your chosen formation. A quick test run will reveal any misalignments or friction hotspots that you can adjust before your first official match. The goal is to achieve a smooth, fluid turnover with minimal resistance on every pass and shot.

### Step 4: Final tightening and playtime
Tighten all fasteners to avoid any wiggling during the high-speed moments of a game. Once everything is secure, give the table a few practice rounds to break in the rods and ensure the ball tracks straight. The first few games might feel a little off, but you will quickly find your rhythm, and the table will feel like a natural extension of your living room arcade ambitions.

### Strategy and playstyle tips
- Start with a solid defensive formation to learn positioning. The longer you keep the ball in your half, the more your opponent will regret their risk-taking.
- Use quick passes to break the defense and then a controlled shot to the far corner for accuracy.
- Master the hold-and-shoot move: hold the ball a moment for a fake, then whip it into action. It s not cheating; it s foosball artistry.
- In multi-player games, assign roles to players: one aggressive striker, one mid-field passer, one defensive anchor. The synergy can feel like a tiny soccer team inside your living room.
