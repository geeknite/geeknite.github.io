## Ergonomics and User Experience

### Screen Integration
Two workstation blocks in tandem deserve a coordinated display strategy. The “with Screen” promise usually means an integrated or closely aligned monitor setup for at least five users per block. If you’re the kind of team that loves interactive demos and frequent screen sharing, you’ll appreciate how screen alignment reduces neck strain and eliminates the “which monitor belongs to who” chaos that often plagues open desks.

Our take: ensure each screen has adjustable tilt and height, ideally with a standardized arm system. Shared screens are fantastic for collaboration, but individual comfort still wins office Olympics. A well‑executed screen mounting plan minimizes glare and lets team members choose their own zone of focus without competing for the same pixel real estate.

### Cable Management and Power
A board of this scale needs a thoughtful cable strategy. Expect integrated cable trays, grommets, and power rails along the central spine. It’s not a glamorous feature, but it’s the one that makes or breaks a day of work. The best examples keep cables out of sight yet easily accessible for quick swaps, with management channels that reduce cable tangling into a single, dramatic knot that would impress any origami artist.

If you’re worried about heat, you’ll be glad to know the Express line typically includes adequate ventilation channels and, depending on configuration, a clever routing path for power and data that reduces the risk of overloading a single outlet. Always confirm total wattage with your installer—new tech gadgets love to surprise you with “one more device” and a stubborn sticker price that threatens to unbalance your quarterly budget.

## Assembly and Maintenance

### Assembly Steps
If you’re a fan of Ikea‑style assembly videos, you’ll enjoy the Express setup process. Expect modular frames, preassembled panels, and a detailed instruction book that reads like a treasure map. A two‑person install crew with basic hand tools and a level will get this beast ticking within a few hours—assuming you’ve scheduled a free afternoon and not a “deadline today” sprint. Common steps include:

- Unpack and inventory all components
- Assemble core frames and connect the two workstation blocks
- Install monitor arms and screens
- Route cables through the central trunking system
- Calibrate leveling feet and check for plumb

We recommend labeling cables during assembly to avoid the afternoon “which is which” crisis. A quick tip: take a photo of the final cable layout before you close up the panels so you can replicate it if you ever move desks or reconfigure the space.

### Cleaning and Care
Oak White isn’t allergic to dust, but it does appreciate regular maintenance. A microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner (non‑abrasive) will keep the surface looking sharp. For deeper care, avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade finishes; you’ll thank yourself when you don’t have to explain to the chief why the desk looks polished but the screen looks foggy. Routine checks should include:

- Inspecting grommets for looseness
- Checking monitor arms for wobble
- Ensuring feet remain level on uneven floors
- Keeping vents unobstructed for airflow
