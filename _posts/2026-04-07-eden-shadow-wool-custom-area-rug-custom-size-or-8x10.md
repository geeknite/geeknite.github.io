## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping Eden Shadow Fresh
Maintenance is where many rug owners decide whether to love or tolerate their purchase. A dark, moody rug like Eden Shadow benefits from a light-touch approach:

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to prevent fiber damage and to lift loose dirt from the pile.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing—rubbing invites more fibers to stand up and start a dance party with the dirt.
- Treat stains promptly. For more stubborn spills, test your stain-removal method on a small, inconspicuous area first. Wool can be sensitive to certain cleaners, so keep things mild and pH-neutral.
- Protect high-traffic zones with a rug pad. A pad reduces wear, keeps the rug in place, and can help with insulation and sound dampening—an underrated benefit in apartment living.
- Rotate occasionally. Like any aging protagonist, the rug benefits from a change of scenery; rotating helps even out wear across the surface.

## How to Order: Tips for a Smooth Experience
Whether you’re choosing a custom size or the standard 8x10, follow these steps to maximize satisfaction:

1. Measure twice, order once. Use painter’s tape to map your space, including the distance to walls, furniture legs, and door swings. Remember: rugs don’t just fill space; they create a frame around your furniture.
2. Decide on the inclusions. If you’re going custom, confirm padding options, edge finishes, and delivery timelines. If you’re selecting the 8x10, confirm padding and shipping estimates.
3. Check the return policy. A good rug is a long-term commitment; a generous return policy is your friend when you’re experimenting with color and texture.
4. Consider the room’s lighting. Darker rugs look different under morning sun versus evening lamps. If you’ve got large windows or a sunlit space, your Eden Shadow might appear deeper or lighter depending on the hour of the day.
5. Prepare the floor. Ensure the surface is clean and dry before laying the rug. A pad helps with wear and keeps the rug from creeping under chairs during those intense board-game nights.
