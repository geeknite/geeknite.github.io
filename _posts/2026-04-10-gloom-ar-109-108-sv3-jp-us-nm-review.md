## How to appraise and store: practical tips
- Inspect the card under bright light for edge wear and surface scratches. NM US is a narrow margin, and the eye can pick up a lot if you are actively comparing multiple copies.
- Compare centering against the standard templates used for SV3 cards. A slightly off-center print can push a mint designation toward near-mint, which matters for grading.
- Keep the card in a proper sleeve and top-loader. The Gloom AR is not fragile beyond typical card handling, but the sleeves protect the surface from fingerprints and accidental damage.
- Store away from humidity and direct sunlight. The purple-green palette will fade if exposed to harsh light and high humidity, reducing the visual drama that makes the card appealing at a glance.
- Consider a display case with UV protection if you plan to feature the card in a hobby space. This helps preserve both color and clarity albeit with a price tag that can be a little sobering when you’re budgeting for a full binder.
