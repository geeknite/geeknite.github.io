## Maintenance and Cleaning

### Daily care and routine
Vacuum regularly with a standard vacuum. Avoid high suction settings that could pull fibers. A mild weekly routine helps keep crumbs and pet hair under control. Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear patterns and reduce the appearance of lines in high-traffic zones.

### Spills, stains, and stubborn marks
Act quickly with blotting to remove excess liquid. Blot — don’t rub — to prevent fiber depth distortion. For spills, use a mild detergent solution or a carpet-cleaner-safe product. Always test a small inconspicuous area first to confirm colorfastness. For stubborn stains, consider a targeted spot-clean treatment and a gentle brush to lift the residue without damaging the fibers. In a pinch, a dab of white vinegar solution can help neutralize odors, followed by a rinse with clean water and thorough blotting.

### Deep cleaning frequency
Depending on foot traffic and pet activity, plan a deeper clean every 6–12 months. A professional carpet cleaning service can refresh the rug’s fibers and lift embedded dirt without risking fiber damage. If you DIY, use a low-moisture cleaner that won’t saturate the backing; too much moisture can lead to mold concerns in certain environments. A drying period of 24–48 hours is a safe bet in moderate climates.

### Preventive care
- Use a quality rug pad to reduce slipping and extend lifespan.
- Place mats at entrances to reduce tracked-in dirt.
- Trim pet nails regularly to minimize snagging and fuzzing at the edges.
- Periodically fluff and rotate to preserve a uniform surface.
