## Maintenance and Durability: Keeping the Battle Station in Prime Form
Durability is the quiet hero of a good foosball table. The Uimoso is built to handle frequent games, occasional spills, and a lifetime of enthusiastic celebrations. The cabinet’s finish resists scuffs, and the rods are designed to resist bending under normal, club-like play. If you’ve got kids who like to swing the rods as if they’re lightsabers, you’ll be glad to know that the rods and player figures are sturdy enough to survive such experiments—though we do not recommend turning your living room into a battle arena every weekend.

Maintenance is straightforward:
- Wipe the playfield with a damp cloth to remove dust and fingerprints.
- Lubricate the bearings occasionally if you notice any squeaks or stiff movement; a light silicone spray works well, but avoid oversaturation (nobody wants a slippery battlefield).
- Check screws and legs for tightness after every few uses; the intense rallies can loosen things a bit, and a wobbly table is the enemy of a good shot.

If you’re worried about humidity or temperature affecting wood components, place the table in a climate-controlled space. Foosball tables aren’t memory foam mattresses; they don’t endure dramatic climate swings well. In a typical family room, though, they’re built to hold up to everyday life without asking for special treatment.
