## Safety tips and usage guidelines
### Basic safety rules
- Always supervise children while they are on the trampoline. - Make sure the enclosure net is properly zipped before anyone starts bouncing. - Remove shoes, jewelry, and anything that could cause injury or snag clothing. - Do a quick perimeter check for obstacles in the yard before use. - Do not perform risky stunts beyond the skill level of the jumpers. The internet does not mind, but your knees will.

### Seasonal care and maintenance
In harsher climates, protective covers and quarterly checks become more important. Inspect springs for rust or wear, ensure the net is not torn, and verify that the padding remains securely in place. If you live in an area with heavy rainfall, dry the mat after a storm to prevent mold growth and slippery surfaces. A little maintenance goes a long way toward keeping the unit safe and their energy high.

### Safety accessories worth considering
- Ground anchors to reduce movement on windy days. - A ladder or stepping stool for easier entry without jumping in and out. - Replacement nets or padding kits in case of wear and tear. - A weatherproof cover to protect the frame when not in use. These additions can extend the life of the trampoline and reduce the ongoing maintenance headache.
