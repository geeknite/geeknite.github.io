## The preowned angle: what to check before you buy

Before you commit to a preowned Zombicide Black Plague Core Box, run through a quick mental checklist. It will save you from a misfire that could ruin your weekend of gaming and lead to awkward questions about your life choices.

- Box condition: Look for rips, crushed corners, or water damage. A damaged outer shell is not fatal, but if the inner components took a soak, you might be in for a long cleanup job.
- Miniatures: Inspect for missing parts or heavy wear. If some zombies or survivors are missing, you can often source replacements or use substitutes that maintain the game's balance.

- Cleanliness: The preowned nature should not scare you away. A light wipe and a gentle scrub should bring the set to near-new feel. If the seller mentions immediate cleaning, consider asking for more details or requesting additional photos.

## How to approach cleaning and restoration (without becoming a hobby necromancer)

Cleaning preowned components is part of the rite of ownership. It is not glamorous, but it is practical, and it makes your first co‑op night much more enjoyable. Here is a simple, practical guide to cleaning and minor restoration that won't require a black belt in necromancy:

- Dusting: Use a soft brush to remove dust from miniatures and boards. A small makeup brush works surprisingly well for stubborn nooks.
- Washing: A bowl of warm water with a mild dish soap can clean plastic miniatures. Submerge briefly, rinse, and pat dry. Do not soak per army; the solvent in dish soap is not kind to some plastics if left too long.
- De-fluff: Use a soft cloth to wipe decks and cards. Cottony residues from the previous owner can cling to cards; a quick wipe helps readability and speed of setup.
- Flattening boards: If boards are warped, place them under a stack of heavy books for an hour or two. Do not overdo it, or you risk bending them more. The goal is a gentle reset rather than a structural transformation.
- Paint touch-ups: If some miniatures show chipping, you can do light touch-ups with acrylic paints. Do not overdo it; you want them to look used, not like a prank painting job gone wrong.

Caveat: if you encounter heavy damage or missing critical components, do not pretend it will be fine. Communicate with the seller, request replacements if possible, and remember that a partial experience is still a playable experience, just with a different kind of storytelling energy.
