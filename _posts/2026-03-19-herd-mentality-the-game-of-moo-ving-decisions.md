H2: What is Herd Mentality: The Game of Moo-ving Decisions

Herd Mentality is a party game in which players respond to prompts with answers they think the majority will choose. The catch? You gain points when your answer matches the majority, but you only know who picked which answer after everyone has voted. In this moo-ving edition, the prompts strut around on four legs: they’re punny, farm-flavored, and prepared to test the strength of your herd-mentality under pressure.

H3: The premise, adapted for cows and cow-lovers

In this version, the prompt cards are a blend of lighthearted farm-life questions and goofy scenarios, designed to spark the kind of quick-fire thinking that makes you blurt out something you’ll regret at brunch. The goal is simple: read the prompt, write or say your response, and then try to align with the herd. The more you align with the majority, the more points you nab. The exception, of course, is when the herd goes astray—then you might end up on the short end of the hay fork.

H2: How to play: the steps you’ll herd through the night

H3: Setup

1) Shuffle the prompt deck and deal a few to everyone’s player sheets. 2) Shuffle the majority-answer deck. 3) Decide the round structure and where you’ll place the udder meter. 4) Each player gets a handful of tokens to bid for the most popular answers. 5) Pick a first round host who will show the prompt to everyone.

H3: The core loop of a round

A round proceeds as follows: the host reveals a prompt card (for example, “Your dream vacation is suddenly: A) a beach, B) a cabin in the woods, C) a spaceship, D) a farm?”). Each player secretly selects an answer they think will be the majority response. Then everyone reveals their answers at once. The host then selects the correct or most popular answer? In classic Herd Mentality, players try to identify the majority choice; in this moo-version, we keep the same vibe but lean into farm humor. If your answer matches the majority, you score points. If not, you watch the points drift away like a lost hay bale on a windy day.

H3: Scoring and round wrap-up

After all answers are revealed, players compare their choices to the actual majority. Points are assigned, the udder meter slides forward, and the next round begins with a new host and a new prompt. The comedic tension comes from watching people pivot their reasoning in real time. It’s like watching a group of cows trying to decide which patch of grass to graze on next, except here you’re doing it with a pencil and a notepad instead of chewing cud on a fencepost.
