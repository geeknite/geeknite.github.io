## Maintenance: Care Without Becoming a Full-Time Job
### Routine Care: Vacuuming and Gentle Touches
Wool benefits from regular, gentle vacuuming to remove surface dust. Use a low suction setting or a brush attachment to avoid tugging the fibers. Frequency depends on traffic: daily living-room wear will require more frequent attention than a guest-room rug that’s rarely walked on.

### Stain & Spot Care: Quick Housekeeping Wins
Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing—rug fibers love a good drama, and rubbing can push stains deeper. For stubborn spots, use a mild wool-safe detergent diluted in water. Test on an inconspicuous area first. If you’re dealing with wine, coffee, or dramatic fruit juices, a gentle water rinse and pat-dry will usually do the trick without leaving you with a ghost-stain that haunts your cushion dreams.

### Sunlight and Color: UV is a Villain, But It’s Not All-Bad
Prolonged direct sunlight can fade a rug’s colors over time. If your sofa faces the window like a moth to light, consider rotating the rug periodically or using window treatments to balance exposure. That way, the ivory remains crisp, and you don’t end up with a Sunday coffee-stain color story on the weave.
