Zombicide 1st Edition is a game that smells faintly of plastic, luck, and late night strategy sessions where someone yells one more round and everyone pretends not to hear. The Special Guest Karl Kopinski edition includes two standout additions to the Zombicide 1st Edition core game: a limited edition Karl Kopinski miniature that feels like it sprang from a sketchbook, and a second figure named Red Cap Ben that instantly earns the nickname the hat that changes the night. Karl Kopinski involvement isnt mere marketing fluff. Its a deliberate, design minded collaboration that elevates both the art and the play. ### Karl Kopinski Art on Zombicide
Kopinski influence is most obvious in the survivor portrait cards that accompany the minis, which borrow his knack for silhouette and mood. The survivor concept looks like a hero who has learned to talk to shadows and outsmart a swarm by using the map itself as a character. The style choice matters because it shapes how players perceive the survivors not as cardboard cutouts, but as protagonists with a plan one that might involve a daring sprint into a doorway, a quick pivot, or a well timed gadget on the table.

The idea is to add character not to break the fundamental game rhythm.

From a narrative standpoint Kopinski s art encourages players to lean into cinematic moments. ## Painting Tips for Kopinski Minis
For the Kopinski survivor miniature start with a mid tone for skin a cool undertone to bring out the shadows and a saturated color palette for the clothing to emphasize the painterly style. Use washes to bring out the creases and a few crisp highlights to make the figure pop under lamp light. The Kopinski mini benefits from having the face and hands relatively clean to preserve the feel of a character who has spent too much time under a workshop light.

For Red Cap Ben focus on the cap and the face. The cap can be painted with a brighter red then dark wash the fold lines to bring out depth. The face should be slightly cooler with a pale complexion contrasted by the darker textures on the clothes. A final dry brush on the cap s edge helps the hat catch the eye as you rotate the board.

### Quick Painting Checklist
- Prime white or gray
- Block base colors
- Apply washes to introduce shadows
- Add highlights on raised surfaces
- Apply a matte varnish to protect the paint
- Mount to a base and add a small scarlet decor to ground the figure
