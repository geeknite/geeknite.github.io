## Assembly and setup: how hard is the build?

Let’s get real about assembly: a four-person corner desk of this scale is not a “pull it out of the box and it’s done” kind of project. It’s closer to a furniture IKEA-adjacent puzzle where you’ll want a second pair of hands, some time, and a playlist that includes enough instrumental tracks to keep you slightly caffeinated but not manic.

- Tools: expect the usual suspects—Allen keys, a cross-head screwdriver, and perhaps a torque-friendly driver if your kit ships with heavier bolts. A drill is optional but not discouraged; just don’t go full James Bond and start circular drilling through your lovely oak veneer.
- Steps: start with the corner frame, align the base panels, secure the central junction, then attach the desktops and the monitor arms. The most important part is to confirm all four work surfaces align with each user’s seat position and that the corner touch point feels stable. If you feel a wobble, re-check the anchors and tighten any loose bolts. A stable corner is the difference between “cozy team space” and “four people playing laser tag with their chairs.”
- Time estimate: depending on your experience and how many interruptions you have (cat videos do not count as interruptions, they’re essential research), plan a couple of hours for the full assembly and setup. If you’re part of a team, split tasks: one person handles the frame, one calibrates the monitor arms, one manages cable routing, and one adjusts the privacy screens. The team that organizes the build tends to value the process as much as the outcome.

Cable management shows up as the hero of the day. If you’re into cable artistry, this is your canvas: you’ll route power, USB-C hubs, HDMI distribution, and maybe a small NAS for shared files along the back channels. If you’re less into cable artistry, you’ll still appreciate the way the channels reduce the “spillover spaghetti effect” that makes every video call feel like a decoding exercise.

## Maintenance and longevity: keeping it fresh

Longevity comes from thoughtful maintenance. Here are a few tips to keep the Eternal desk looking sharp and performing well for years:

- Clean surfaces regularly with a soft cloth and a mild cleaner; avoid abrasive padding that can dull the oak veneer. A damp microfiber cloth followed by a dry wipe is usually enough for everyday grime.
- Protect the edges: if you’ve got curious kids or pets, consider edge guards or corner protectors to reduce the risk of chips or dings in high-traffic areas.
- Check the hardware periodically: a quick quarterly tighten of bolts and screen mounts will extend the life of the unit and prevent annoying wobble that creeps in with heavy usage.
- Monitor arms: if you opted for integrated monitor arms, ensure the joints stay smooth and free of lubricants that attract dust. A light wipe and a touch of silicone-based lubricant (sparingly) can keep arms moving without squeaks.
- Cable health: re-check cable lengths and routing after a few months. A tidy cable setup helps with airflow and reduces the chance of heat buildup around power strips—this is both safety-minded and helpful for performance stability.
