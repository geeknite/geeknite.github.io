## Practical considerations

### Rug pad and underfoot comfort

A rug pad is not a luxury; it is a floor-safety protocol. Using a high-quality pad under the Trailblazer Valley wool rug reduces slippage, increases underfoot cushioning, and helps with acoustic dampening in busy spaces. If you have hardwood or tile flooring, a rug pad becomes essential for both safety and comfort. The pad should be sized to match the rug, not oversized or undersized, to avoid curling edges and slippery boundaries.

### Maintenance and care

Wool fibers are naturally resilient, but they respond best to a simple maintenance routine. Vacuum regularly with a low- suction setting and a gentle brush or suction-only head. Avoid aggressive power heads that can loosen fibers over time. Blot spills promptly with a clean, white cloth and avoid soaking the wool. For stubborn stains, mild wool-safe cleaners are recommended, and always test a small inconspicuous area first. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones. Periodic professional cleaning can extend the rug’s life and restore its luster.

### Durability and pet-friendly use

Wool rugs are generally durable and can stand up to pets better than many synthetic fibers, especially when paired with a proper pad. Short-nap piles resist claw snagging, and the dense weave tends to hide minor hair shedding. If your pet is a document-snatching terrier or a perpetual chewer of remote controls, you may want to keep the rug in a more protected area or train your pet to respect the new floor artwork.

## Maintenance tips and care routine

- Vacuum weekly with a low- to medium-suction setting; avoid rotating brush agitation that can fuzz the surface.
- Address spills immediately with blotting techniques; do not rub as this pushes liquid deeper into the fibers.
- Use a color-fast stain remover approved for wool if needed; test on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate your rug seasonally to promote even wear and avoid heavy foot traffic patterns forming in a single area.
- Plan a professional cleaning every 12 to 24 months depending on footfall and pets.
