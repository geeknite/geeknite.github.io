## Gameplay: Classic UNO with a Splash of Anniversary Flavor
UNO’s core mechanic remains intact: match colors or numbers to shed cards; draw if you’re stuck; use Wilds and Wild Draw Fours strategically; last card must be announced with a yell of UNO. The 50th Anniversary Edition adds a few flavours to this ritual to keep things fresh on those nights when your group has already played a dozen classic matches in a row.

### Variants and House Rules
- Classic Mode: The baseline experience. Everything you know from regular UNO, plus a few extra celebratory cards that may grant brief chaos or playful punishments. It’s UNO as you remember it, with a glaze of premium components.
- Anniversary Mode: A more dramatic variant where players are nudged toward storytelling and role-play. The rulebook suggests narrating your draw moment and the table voting to award a bonus for dramatic actions. It’s cheesy, endearing, and yes, it can derail the tempo in the best possible way.
- Quick-Start Shuffle: A few cards are pre-shuffled into the deck to reduce downtime between shuffles. You’ll appreciate this if your group spends more time arguing about the first move than actually playing the first move.

These variants aren’t radical changes; they’re invitations to flavor your UNO night. If you’re hosting a board game night that looks more like a casual game night than a competitive tournament, Anniversary Mode is a fun lane to explore. If you’re playing with kids, the family-friendly framing of the rules helps keep the chaos within reasonable bounds while still offering moments of pure silliness.
