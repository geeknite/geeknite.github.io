## Tactics, Logistics, and the Mirage of Victory

The siege is, in part, a logistics problem. It is also a problem of how to maintain morale with poor rations, hot sun, and the perpetual threat of disease. The translation does not gloss over these aspects; indeed, it emphasizes the frictions that make siege life a kind of medieval boot camp. What is striking here is how the text underscores the fragility of even the most well-defended fortress. The author does not pretend that victory would be easy; rather, the narrative frames victory as a long, uncertain arc shaped by weather, politics, and the resilience of small units within large formations.
