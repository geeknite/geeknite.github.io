## Overview

Zombicide fans get a strange gift this season as CMON teams up with legendary fantasy artist Adrian Smith for a Special Guest miniature set. This is not your average squad of survivors; think signature line work and dramatic silhouettes frozen mid scream as you battle a tide of undead with a pencil heavy enough to ink a dungeon. The Adrian Smith Special Guest Miniature Set is designed to be both a visual treat and a practical add on for your Zombicide games. Whether you are chasing the completed collection, or you simply enjoy looking at things that look cooler than your last painting attempt, this box has something for you.

### Whats inside the packaging

The exact contents shift with release runs, but typically you get a handful of 28 mm scale miniatures featuring Smiths distinct style, a boss style zombie figure, and the usual set of Zombicide game tokens and cards that you would expect in a modern CMON release. You will also often find a small art card or a printable sheet featuring a larger Adrian Smith illustration that you can frame and pretend you are a game master rather than a person who reglue figurines for a living. The packaging leans into the painterly aesthetic, a small victory for fans who enjoy a box that resembles a gallery rather than a target practice sheet.

## The Sculpt and Craft Quality

Adrian Smiths involvement brings a certain sculpt style: dynamic posing, dramatic silhouettes, and a heavy emphasis on texture and storytelling. The sculpts are not just about accurate musculature or armor; they tell a micro story at table top scale. The textures in the cloaks, the torn fabrics, the rivets on armor read crisp under good lighting and hold up well under a standard hobby lamp. The joint connections on the multi part pieces are typical CMON: clean lines, reasonable assembly time, and an overall sturdiness that earns you a nod when you slam the box shut after a long session.

## The Adrian Smith Connection

This is where the set stops being a simple plastic parade and starts to feel like a collaboration artifact. Smiths art is known for its bold line work and dramatic contrasts, and the miniatures do a decent job translating that feel into 28 mm scale. You will notice sharp edges, dramatic capes, and faces that seem ready to glare across a tabletop at your players with a sardonic grin. If you have a collection of Smiths other works, this is a delightful physical extension of his portfolio. If you do not, it still provides a strong entry point into the aesthetic that many art driven collectors adore.

### Painting and Customization

If you are a painter who likes a little drama in your lunch break, this set is a treat. The surfaces are designed to take washes and dry brushing with minimal fuss, which means you can achieve a striking look without spending the better part of a weekend on one figure. The armor plates take shading well, and the capes respond nicely to edge highlighting. For those who enjoy conversions, several pieces are compatible with other Zombicide kits, allowing you to create what amounts to a small crossover event in your dining room. If you love battle damage, the fine texture on Smiths sculpts provides ample opportunities to experiment with chipped paint and metallic glints.

#### Paint schemes worth trying

- Classic Smith high contrast: deep blacks, bright edge highlights on edges, and a pale but intense skin tone.
- Weathered metal look: rusty plates with cyan or teal patina to add a pop without clashing with the rest of your board.
- Bold color blocking: try a fantasy palette with luminous cloaks that still reads as a zombie survival scenario.

### Basework and assembly tips

As with most CMON plastic minis, you want a steady hand and a good hobby knife. Clean the mold lines, wash the pieces, and assemble dry first to visualize how the parts fit. Use green stuff or a small bit of putty to fill minor gaps and plan the base to match your table texture. If you want that eye catching display option, consider a small scenic base with subtle stone textures and a hint of subterranean glow around the feet of the larger figures.

## Value and Buying Considerations

Is the Adrian Smith Special Guest set worth the price tag for a casual gamer? It depends on what you value most. If you are chasing a complete Zombicide collection and you enjoy the art driven content, the set provides a nice balance between model quality and collectible appeal. If you view it as a strictly gameplay oriented purchase, you may be better served allocating funds toward core CMON expansions or other in game add ons that provide more functional value on the board.

### Price versus content

The retail price sits at a premium for a smaller release. You are paying for the sculpture quality and the art license as much as for the game components. If you are a collector, you may view that as a fair premium; if you are primarily a player, you might want to wait for a sale or an online bundle. Either way, the product remains accessible to fans who enjoy both the aesthetics and the gaming experience that Zombicide attempts to create. The good news is that the miniatures themselves are robust enough to support a higher paint to play ratio, which means they will look fantastic on display or under a table lamp long after you have finished your last game night.

### Collector value

From a collector standpoint, limited editions, cross brand collaborations, and artist driven sets tend to hold value better than generic expansions. If you are thinking long term, store the figures carefully and consider keeping the art card or any exclusive inserts. A sealed box can be an investment, but remember that market values fluctuate and the value is often driven by scarcity and the fervor of fans.
