## Building, assembly, and first paint thoughts

### The build path and challenges

As with many multi-part kits, you’ll find that some of the smaller weapons and insignia pieces can be fiddly. The arms and shoulders need to be aligned in a way that makes the weapon sit comfortably in a natural, gravity-defying pose. The first build attempt might yield a micro-mnick of misalignment; that’s normal. Don’t panic. A little repositioning, a dab of plastic glue, and a gentle squeeze with your hobby pliers will correct most alignment issues within minutes.

The faces provide a palette of expressions: stoic, grim, and a few that look like they’re about to deliver a well-labeled memo. It’s a small, but meaningful choice that lets you personalize each Palanite Justicar so they don’t all look like clones, even though they are clones in the legal sense. If you’re into NMM (Non-Males’ Metallics) painting, you’ll appreciate how the armor shapes catch light and lend themselves to layered shading without requiring you to hire a pro-sculptor to tone down the lines.

### Tools you’ll want on hand

- A sharp hobby knife or sprue cutter to clean mold lines.
- A fine-grit sanding stick to smooth surfaces after trimming.
- Plastic glue or superglue for assembly. If you want to avoid gaps, a tiny bead of glue along joints works well.
- Plastic primers (gray or black) for a neutral base that helps contrast the final colors.
- Your favorite color palette: metallics for armor plates, deep blues or greens for the cloak or lower garments, and accent colors for insignia.

Painting the Palanite Justicars is a balanced experience. The armor plates are often a rich, cool metal that loves a wash and a light drybrush to reveal edges. The cloth fabrics—capes or tunics—tend to be the main color focal point. You can push the cloth with a slightly higher saturation to make the squad pop on a board, or you can keep them muted and let the weapons do the talking. The goal is to make each mini feel cohesive as a squad while still allowing for small variations that show off your painting style.

### A quick tip for speed paint jobs

If you’re painting in batches, use a common color for the main armor, and then pick a few contrast colors for insignia and small details. A well-chosen contrast color will make the squad look complete in a single pass, rather than requiring you to go back and forth with tiny details. And if you’re short on time, a clean tabletop finish will still look good, especially under the lighting of a typical gaming room or a streamer’s setup. The Palanite Justicars aren’t fussy—so long as they stand tall, look sharp, and communicate a sense of “professional law enforcement with a side of intimidation,” you’ve won the day.
