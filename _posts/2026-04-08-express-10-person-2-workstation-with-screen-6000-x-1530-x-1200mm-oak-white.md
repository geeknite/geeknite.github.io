## Ergonomics and Personal Comfort
Ergonomics matter in any workstation, but they become existential in a 6-meter setup. Here are some practical checks to ensure that your team stays healthy and productive over long sessions:

- Chair and desk alignment: The desks are standard height, and the surface depth should accommodate a keyboard and mouse comfortably with room for a monitor at arm’s length. If your team includes taller folks, you’ll want adjustable chair heights to prevent slouching. Consider a monitor arm setup for optimal line-of-sight and to free desk space for paperwork or snacks.
- Keyboard and mouse positioning: A common pitfall with large teams is the tendency to crowd devices toward the center. Distribute keyboards evenly to avoid shoulder strain, and consider a small pad or wrist support to reduce repetitive strain injuries. The wide top means you have plenty of real estate to spread out devices, but don’t overdo it—clear zones help reduce cramping and cognitive clutter.
- Monitor alignment: If each user has a screen, consider a modular monitor arm kit so you can adjust tilt and height without reassembling the entire desk. This is particularly useful if your team rotates roles or shifts between development, design, and QA.
- Lighting: The oak-white combo works with ambient lighting to reduce glare. If your space has strong overhead lighting, you might need consistent task lighting at each station to minimize eye strain during long debugging marathons.

Health and comfort aside, there’s a geeky joy to a well-ordered ergonomics system. It’s the sense that you’ve moved beyond “desks as a collection of surfaces” into “desks as a platform for efficient human-machine collaboration.” That’s the heart of a good office, even if your office is also a mini-data center for people with specialty coffee orders.
