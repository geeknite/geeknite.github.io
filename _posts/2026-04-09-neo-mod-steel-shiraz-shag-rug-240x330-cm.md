## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity
Durability is a keyword here. High-pile rugs require maintenance, but with the right approach, they last years and keep their fluff. Start with a regular vacuuming schedule, using a nozzle that reaches between the fibers without pulling. Rotate the rug a few degrees occasionally to ensure even wear and avoid a noticeable depression in one area. If you spill something, act quickly. Blot, don’t rub, with a clean cloth. For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution is often all you need. Test first in a hidden corner to ensure the color doesn’t bleed.

Professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months can extend the rug’s life, especially if you have kids, pets, or busy foot traffic. If you prefer to DIY a bit more, consider a rug shampoo designed for synthetic fibers, again testing on a small area first. The key is to preserve the fibers and keep the color from fading due to repeated cleaning products. A well-maintained shag rug remains plush, inviting, and a little magical to touch.

Where this rug shines is not only its comfort but its ability to remain visually appealing over time.
