## Overview

City of Mist sets itself apart by turning players into living myths called Icons. Each Icon represents a legend from a wide range of myths, urban legends, or purely invented fables. The Book of Legends expands the toolbox, offering new Icon concepts, new story hooks, and new mechanical play modes that push players toward more interwoven crime noir meets mythic epic storytelling. The tone remains sharp, witty, and surprisingly earnest about its own goofy premise. You get enough guidance to run a campaign with a cinematic vibe without surrendering your agency to the whims of a film editor with a penchant for slow zooms.

In practical terms, Book of Legends provides a curated set of new Icon types, expanded moves, and a handful of ready-to-use micro-scenarios. It also offers tips for balancing mythic power with street-level consequences, which is City of Mist at its best: a blend of mythic flair and grounded consequences that make your heroes feel both larger-than-life and uncomfortably breakable.

## Mechanics and Systems: What the Book Adds

City of Mist core rules revolve around Icon moves, surfacing the conflicts between a character s mundane identity and their emergent legend self. The Book of Legends builds on this by widening the catalog of potential Icons and by providing structured pathways to escalate a campaign without tipping into arena fight choreography. The book excels at two things: clarifying how Legends intersect with human frailty, and giving you clever tools to orchestrate a city-wide web of consequences that still plays fair at the table.

New Icons in this book tend to fall into a few broad categories:

- Civic Legends: Icons tied to institutions, districts, or social movements. These bring a sense of community scale to personal stakes.
- Shadow Icons: Dark mirrors that test the hero s moral lines and reveal the cost of power.
- Mythic Anomalies: Legends that are less about a person and more about a concept personified, like a Legend of Silence or a Legend of Fog.
- Urban Legends: These are practical, street-level legends that may interact with crime scenes, neighborhoods, and layered backstories in surprising ways.

Each Icon type is accompanied by a short narrative prompt, a recommended approach to blending with existing PCs, and a few ready-made story seeds. The seeds are intentionally modular, designed to slot into ongoing campaigns or spawn a one-shot that feels more like a micro-maga-script than a conventional game session.

Mechanically, the Book of Legends doesn t reinvent the wheel. It fine-tunes the wheel so it doesn t squeak when you go for a turn at high speed. The core emotional currency remains the same: players weigh their mundane lives against a looming mythic arc, then decide which path to take. But the expanded moves, and the new Icon prompts, give you more routes to steer the same ship through a storm of noir intrigue. This is where City of Mist thrives: it rewards narrative risk with meaningful consequences, without punishing players for choosing a flashy option.

The book also includes expanded guidelines for risk and reward. In practice, this means you can design scenes with multiple pressure points—police pressure, corporate machinations, and personal relationships—that converge toward a climactic revelation. The GM is given more levers to pull, but not so many that the board becomes a slot machine. It s a balance that City of Mist has historically handled with a light touch, and Book of Legends preserves that balance while increasing the potential payoff for ambitious sessions.

## Narrative Tools and GM Tips

A significant strength of the Book of Legends lies in its approach to GM guidance. The authors recognize that the appeal of City of Mist is not just in the legends themselves but in the way players and GMs co-create a city that feels alive. The book leans into collaborative storytelling with tips that respect both player agency and the inevitability of a good boss fight.

One of the recurring motifs is the idea that legends should be legible in the city s texture. The book recommends using environmental cues, recurring motifs, and almost musical callbacks to Legend moments. This approach gives the GM a toolkit to orchestrate a narrative pattern: hint, reveal, consequence, ripple. It s not a formula, but it is a reliable rhythm that helps sessions breathe and evolve without slipping into predictability.

Another practical tip concerns pacing. The Book of Legends suggests alternating between intimate scenes that reveal character depth and larger set pieces that present a mythic scale. This pacing helps avoid fatigue for players who crave both noir introspection and dramatic, mythic eruptions. In play, you ll notice the difference when you can trigger a legend moment that feels both personal and world-shaking on the same night.

There are also sections on tone management. City of Mist thrives on a playful humility: it invites you to lean into genre tropes without letting them define your table. The Book of Legends acknowledges this balance and gives you guardrails to maintain it. It s easy to spice up a scene with a legend s power, while also letting ordinary life intrude in ways that remind everyone why the city matters to the person underneath the mask.

2) The Silence in the Clocktower: A time-based heist where a Legend of Silence controls a clocktower that freezes conversations. The PCs must break the silence by confronting a human adversary who believes speech is a weapon more dangerous than guns. This session leans into character-driven drama and features a ticking clock that actually gulps air and rides on the city s rhythms.

3) Fog over the Opera: The city s cultural heart becomes a battlefield when a Legend of Fog edges into the stage with a misdirection spell. The players must decide whether to derail a spectacle that could save a community or let the performance unfold and risk a larger collapse of trust in public myth.

These seeds demonstrate the texture the Book of Legends brings to the table: a mix of personal stakes, city-wide consequences, and mythic costs that feel like a bargain you wouldn t immediately regret.
