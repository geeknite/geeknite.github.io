## Setup and day-to-day use

### Plug in and go? Not exactly, but almost

The setup is straightforward but not mindless. You connect the dock to your laptop using the USB-C/Thunderbolt cable, plug the power brick into the dock (and let it feed the laptop), and then connect your displays and peripherals. The dock negotiates power and data relationships with the host, which means you will likely see your laptop wake up and begin charging within seconds. If your machine supports USB-C alternate mode, the HDMI and DisplayPort will spring to life with minimal fuss.

### Driver considerations

If you are on Windows, you will likely be fine with the built-in drivers, but HP does not shy away from suggesting the optional drivers from their site for optimal performance. On macOS, the Thunderbolt ecosystem has its own vibe, and you will want to ensure you are on a supported macOS version for best results. Linux users can count on the good old TB3/TB4 interoperability with some tinkering, as is tradition with advanced docking setups. The moral of the story: expect a few moments of driver dance when you introduce new hardware to the party.

### Cooling and noise

There is a small fan inside the power adapter, and the dock itself remains quiet most of the time. Under heavy workloads, you might hear a subtle whir from the cooling path, but nothing that sounds like a jet engine in a tiny apartment. If you live in a cabin in the woods and want total silence, you might consider moving the dock away from your ears, or simply embrace the hum as a background track for your thinking sessions.
