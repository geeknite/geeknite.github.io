## Artwork and Theme
Karl Kopinski’s art has always been about energy—motion captured in a single line, a stare that says more than a paragraph, a weapon that seems to carry its own weather system. In this Special Guest Box, that energy translates into a tabletop that feels less like a board game and more like a living frame from a graphic novel. The survivors pop because there’s a coherent art direction across the deck: their outfits, weapons, and silhouettes tell you almost immediately who they are, what they’re good at, and what risks they’re taking.

The zombie designs lean into grotesque clarity—the more you look, the more you notice little anatomical details that Kopinski is famous for, without tipping into the grotesque-for-grotesque’s-sake zone. It’s enough to make your play area feel cinematic, which, in my opinion, is a strong emotional hook for a game that thrives on chaos and collaboration.

For collectors, the art-forward packaging is a win. The card backs, the hero portraits, and the scenic inserts create a consistent aesthetic that makes the set a natural display piece when the table isn’t in use. If you’re the sort of player who arranges your minis by color or artist and takes photos to share with a comic-book-nerd subset of your friends, you’ll find the Kopinski box to be a dream subject.
