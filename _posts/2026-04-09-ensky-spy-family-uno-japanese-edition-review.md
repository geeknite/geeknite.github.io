## What is in the box?

The box promises more than just a fancy cover. You get a full UNO deck with character art that is unmistakably Spy x Family—Loid as the suave cardmaster, Yor as the unpredictable wildcard queen, Bond as the lucky charm, and a cadre of supporting characters that pop up on number cards and action cards alike. The Japanese edition adds a few localized touches: kanji/katakana labeling, flavor text in Japanese on some of the card faces, and a packaging design that screams premium collector rather than “weekend gift” starter set.

## How to play with this edition

If you know UNO, you know the basics: match color or number, dump your hand, yell UNO when you’re down to one card, and don’t forget to draw when you miss a turn. The Ensky Spy x Family edition keeps those bones but sprinkles a layer of espionage flavor on top. Here’s what to expect:

- Standard UNO mechanics with a Spy x Family twist: expect card effects that lean into the family dynamics. Think ahead moves, misdirection, and the occasional dramatic reveal when someone drops a “Loid-style” card that forces a reshuffle or a strategic draw.
- The character art cards don’t change the core timing of the game; they simply intensify the flavor. You’ll still need to manage your hand and count to UNO, but the art cues can cue your memory on which action cards are in play, which can be a surprisingly helpful cognitive nudge during a heated match.
- The Japanese edition carries a few localized flavor text lines on select cards. These are optional but fun: they offer quick nods to fans and small chuckles that break the tension during a tense heated game.

For families and groups who enjoy a thematic overlay with familiar mechanics, the espionage angle adds a dash of misdirection that can make games last a little longer. You may find yourself bluffing about being “completely safe” while you’re silently counting the number of cards left in your opponents’ hands.

Tip for maximum fun: use the flavor cards to set a theme night. Make Loid your lead, Yor your unexpected rogue, and Bond your wildcard. Declare a “spy mission” round and let the table vote on extra house rules for the hour. The table will thank you with laughter and a few dramatic gasps when someone flips a Color-Changing card that clashes perfectly with a dramatic plot twist.
