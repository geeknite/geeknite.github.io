## What’s in the box? Cards, tiles, and a lot of potential for chaos

### Cards: new threats, new tools, new nightmares
The card decks in this dual expansion are the real workhorses. You’ll encounter a mix of new zombie behaviors, new equipment, and new mission-specific cards that push the game into unfamiliar territory. A few highlights include:

- Black Plague brings disease mechanics that can spread through the board if you don’t keep your survivors fed, watered, and well-vaccinated against ax-wielding necromancers (okay, maybe not vaccination, but at least some antiseptic-touched weapons).
- Green Horde adds a different flavor: more swarming, more teleporting, and more opportunities to mess with your co-op plans. It’s the kind of chaos that makes you wonder if you should have stayed in your safe house and not answered the call to loot another cathedral full of undead.
- Equipment and trait cards give your team new toys: unique weapons, gear sets, and morale-boosting bling that may or may not survive the next zombie surge. Some cards encourage hyper-aggressive play, others reward careful planning—both are valid strategies, as long as you’re ready to adapt when the zombie tide turns.

The card art is consistent with the rest of ZOMBICIDE’s aesthetic: saturated colors, dramatic silhouettes, and those nifty little icons that tell you everything you need to know at a glance—until you realize you’re about to forget all of it in the heat of battle.

### Tiles: map variety and new choke points
If you’ve ever played ZOMBICIDE and thought the board looked a little too friendly, these expansion tiles are your antidote. Expect new objective layouts, dangerous trap spaces, and environmental hazards that can turn simple scavenging into a sprint for your life. The Black Plague tiles add plague-themed hazards that require you to manage infection risk, while Green Horde tiles emphasize terrain that can funnel or mislead your survivors. The combination yields maps that feel both familiar and off-kilter—in a good way.

### Miniatures and counters
The expansion introduces new zombie variations and new survivors or modifiers for the existing survivors. ## Gameplay experience: chaos, cooperation, and comedy

ZOMBICIDE is at its core a co-op game about surviving a zombie apocalypse with your friends. The Black Plague & Green Horde expansions intensify that premise in two directions:

- They increase the frequency and severity of threats, which means you’ll be constantly juggling three or four different priorities at once: shield the weakest, secure the exit, pick up that loot, and somehow manage to stay alive long enough to brag about it later.
- They inject new tactical options that reward different play styles. Do you go all-out aggressive with high-risk, high-reward moves, or do you embrace the slow, methodical approach that minimizes danger but maximizes the chance of making it to the next round with a few survivors intact?

The humor of ZOMBICIDE comes from the tension between those two modes. You will roll dice that refuse to obey the sacred laws of probability. You will draw a card that changes the battlefield in a blink. And you will learn—through trial, error, and occasional betrayal—how much you’re willing to sacrifice to rescue yet another NPC who insists on sprinting toward a zombie hoard with a broken flashlight and a dream.

To illustrate, here’s a sample turn progression with the expansions in play:

1) The zombie pool revs up: you draw a plague-related card that reduces your healing efficiency unless you sacrifice a weapon, which you were planning to use to defend the flag in the next room.
2) A Green Horde spawn event creates a corridor of doom that forces you to pick a path that splits the party—split the party, or split the loot? Your team debates the ethical implications of loot distribution while one survivor makes a heroic dash to the objective.
3) A new card grants a temporary ally or an improvised weapon—sometimes both, sometimes neither—so you pivot your plan and hope the dice gods are in a merciful mood.

The net result is an experience that feels like a comic-tragic montage: ridiculous near-misses, banter that veers between “we’re doomed” and “we’ve got this,” and a mounting sense that you’re playing a board game where the rules are glass, and the zombies are wearing steel-toed boots.

## Strategy tips: getting the most from the combo
- Prioritize board control: with the added tiles and new zombie types, controlling chokepoints is more valuable than ever. Place your survivors in positions where they can cover multiple doors while still contributing to the objective.
- Manage the plague risk early: if the plague cards are in play, you’ll want a contingency plan for rapid healing and vaccination-like mitigation. Don’t underestimate how quickly a minor wound can turn into a major problem when the board is crowded.
- Balance aggression with retreat capability: some of the new cards reward aggressive moves, but the expansion also punishes players who ignore an obvious escape route. Remember, staying alive is a form of winning.
- Synergize equipment: the new gear cards can create powerful combos, but you’ll need to coordinate with teammates to deploy them effectively. A well-timed crossbow shot synchronized with a protective shield can turn a potential wipe into a heroic save.
- Learn the new zed-variants: each zombie type behaves differently under pressure. Spend a few cycles observing their patterns before you commit to a strategy. The time you invest in learning pays off in the later rounds when chaos compounds quickly.
