## Cable Management and Power

A multi-user desk must bury its power needs gracefully. This model includes a centralized power distribution hub with surge protection, modular outlets, and a careful distribution of USB-C and HDMI ports to support peripherals, docking stations, and the occasional wireless receiver. Expect a small waterfall of cables to appear around the central spine as the team configures monitors, microphones, and a shared webcam array for meetings.

### The spaghetti tango and how to avoid it
The key to keeping the spaghetti under control is planning. Start with a plan for where each device lives and how often it will be moved. Label cables, use color coding, and route cables through dedicated channels before you start connecting everything. A tidy cabling plan reduces the risk of accidental unplugging during important calls, especially during peak business hours when someone inevitably hits the wrong USB port and triggers a chain reaction of Windows updates mid-presentation.

### Power efficiency and safety
The power architecture is robust but not overbearing. If your office tends to run on a higher density of electronics, consider adding a smart power strip that can be controlled per module. It helps you turn off clusters of devices during lunch or after hours without compromising essential equipment.
