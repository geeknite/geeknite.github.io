## Cleaning and Maintenance
### Routine care
Vacuum the rug regularly with a low suction setting to avoid pulling fibers. Avoid aggressive beater bars that can fray edges or pull fibers loose. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent push stains deeper. Mild soap and water can handle most everyday spills; test on a small inconspicuous area first to be safe.

### Deep cleaning and stain removal
Deep cleaning can be done with a gentle carpet shampoo or a cleaner approved for synthetic fibers. Rinse well to remove soap residue and allow to dry flat to prevent warping. A rug rake after cleaning can help restore pile uniformity and maintain the surface texture. If odors linger, a light baking soda treatment followed by thorough vacuuming can refresh the rug without heavy fragrances.

### Pet hair and dander management
For pet hair, a rubber broom or a pet hair roller can remove loose fur before vacuuming. A regular grooming routine for your furry friends also reduces the amount of hair that ends up on the rug, keeping maintenance to a minimum. The goal is a rug that stays looking fresh even with a busy household and occasional mud track ins.
