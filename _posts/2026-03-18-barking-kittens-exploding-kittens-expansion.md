Introduction

If you thought Exploding Kittens needed more chaos, more meows, and more ways to flip tables in a friendly manner, congratulations: Barking Kittens is here to deliver exactly that. The new expansion pack invites a cadre of cat-backed shenanigans to the table and tallies it with a crown, a bark, and a lot of chaos energy. It’s not just more cards; it’s a whole mood. In the spirit of Geeknite’s fearless, slightly snarky approach to gaming gear, we’re diving deep into what Barking Kittens adds, how it plays, what it costs, and whether the Cat Crown is worth fighting over like a tiny, fur-covered throne.

What’s Barking Kittens? A quick recap
-----------------------------------
If Exploding Kittens gave you a few pings of panic and a handful of strategic wiggle-room, Barking Kittens doubles down on the silliness. The expansion leans into a feline-laden fantasy where cats are not just random art and flavor—they are the political players of a tabletop world where deck-building meets parliamentary shenanigans. Expect more cat-themed cards, more punny dialogue on the cards themselves, and a few new mechanics that tilt the balance from “bluff and react” to “bluff, react, and pounce.” The art remains cheeky, the humor remains sharp, and the board game vibe stays unmistakably Exploding Kittens: fast, frantic, and a little ridiculous.

--------------------------------
- 44 new cards with a feline flavor and a few canine cameos

The Cat Crown: a closer look
---------------------------
The Cat Crown is the star of Barking Kittens, and yes, it’s as ridiculous as it sounds. The card acts as a limited-use artifact that can swing a round in surprising ways, but it’s not a guaranteed win—this is still Exploding Kittens, after all. In practice, the Crown grants a once-per-game decree that changes how a specific card interaction resolves. Think of it as a royal edict that says, “If you’re about to explode someone, hold your cat paws and consider the crown’s whim.” Thematically, it’s a riot: you’re enacting cat sovereignty on the table, and your fellow players are suddenly conscious of fur length and throne etiquette.

- Mechanics: The Crown can be claimed by a player when a Cat Crown card is drawn or discarded under certain conditions. Once claimed, the player can declare one of several cat-themed effects that resolve before the usual card resolution. It’s a timing-heavy tool, which means it rewards players who can read the room and manage the tempo.
- Balance: The Crown isn’t a “I win” button. It’s a powerful pivot, not a guaranteed finishing move. If you’re looking for a power spike that ends games in two minutes, Barking Kittens won’t hand you that—what it does offer is a dramatic swing that can derail a hot streak and turn a stubborn stalemate into a frantic sprint. If anything, the Crown amplifies mischief more than it amplifies pure skill; you’ll see players suddenly pivot from conservative play to all-out cat chaos when that crown appears.

Box contents in detail

The expansion’s card design sticks with the standard Exploding Kittens aesthetic: bold, slightly chaotic art with a wink to every cat-lover and meme aficionado in the room. The new kitten characters range from the “King Purr” to the “Mischief Muffin,” each card bringing a distinct interaction or effect. The new deck can be shuffled with the base game or used to replace certain card groups, depending on how you want to dial up the mayhem.

How Barking Kittens plays in practice
-----------------------------------
Setup is similar to the base game: deal a handful of cards to each player, place the Exploding Kitten cards back in the deck in a way that suits your group’s desired level of chaos, and decide how aggressively you want to incorporate the Cat Crown each round. There are two modes of play within Barking Kittens—the standard integration and a more ritualistic “Cat Court” variant that grows the political atmosphere around the table.

- Standard mode: Players take turns, draw cards, and play action cards as usual. The Cat Crown is a wild-card effect you can use when the card appears or when you meet its trigger conditions. The crown can redirect, copy, or slightly modify card effects, but only within the constraints of a pre-agreed rule set so the table doesn’t descend into actual feudal disputes.
- Cat Court variant: This is where Barking Kittens truly shines. The Cat Crown is treated as if there’s an actual royal audience—players propose “laws” (house rules) that last until the end of the round, and the crown grants special veto rights. It’s chaotic but hilariously thematic, and it’s a superb way to keep experienced players happy while giving newbies a way in through the shell game of negotiation.

Teaching new players: who is Barking Kittens for?
-------------------------------------------------
If you’re teaching Exploding Kittens to a friend group that appreciates memes, cat videos, or a strong sense of theater, Barking Kittens is your best bet. The Cat Crown adds a layer of strategic flavor without overwhelming new players with a dozen new effects. You’ll want to emphasize these teaching points:

- The goal remains the same: avoid exploding, manage risk, and use cards to force your opponents into awkward, cat-coded decisions.
- The Cat Crown adds a new resource to track—cat status tokens—and a new timing dynamic. Teach the timing first, then the crown.
- The humor is inseparable from the gameplay. The moment someone lays down a Crown card with a dramatic flourish, you’ll hear laughter, groans, and a chorus of “I should have played my [fill-in-the-blank] card.”

Strategy and deck-building synergy
---------------------------------
Barking Kittens isn’t a full-blown deck-builder, but it does reward players who plan a few steps ahead. Here are some practical angles to consider:

- Tempo control: The Crown’s power sits in the timing. If you can delay the Crown’s activation until you’re in a position of advantage, you’ll maximize its impact. Learn to recognize the moment someone is about to lose tempo and pivot just enough to spark a turn that rewrites the board.
- Delegating risk: Some Cat Crown effects encourage you to pass risk to other players in exchange for a useful advantage. This encourages social manipulation and bluffing—two essential skill sets for Exploding Kittens veterans.
- Synergy with base game cards: The expansion interacts smoothly with existing cards like Attack, Skip, See the Future, and Shuffle. Track how your opponents use these cards in the presence of the Crown, and time your plays accordingly.
- Cat-themed combos: There are a handful of synergy boosters that reward stacking “cat” effects with a few non-cat cards. These synergies create micro-arcs of excitement when you pull off a chain of plays that makes your opponents realize they walked into a cat-themed trap long ago laid in a deck that was given to them by fate (and a designer with a knack for punny humor).

Exploding Kittens—and Barking Kittens by extension—leans into playful humor that’s mostly light-hearted. The key is to keep the laughter high while keeping the game accessible and inclusive for folks who aren’t fluent in “cat-brained” strategic jargon.
