## Sorting, storage, and organization (pro tips)
Once you own 150 mixed cards, organization becomes your best friend. Here are a few practical ideas to keep your sorting routine efficient:
- Use a simple three-bin method: commons, uncommons, rares/mythics. You can further refine into color sub-bins if you want to quickly assemble a prospective deck.
- Invest in a spreadsheet or note-taking app to log interesting pulls. A quick list of potential combos can save time during your next session.
- Use card sleeves and deck boxes to separate potential builds. It helps you avoid accidental merges of mixed-card decks and ensures that quality cards do not take unnecessary damage.
- Label tokens, dice, and life counters so you can quickly locate what you need mid-game. You will thank yourself when you do not have to rummage through the entire pile.

A good sorting routine is a ritual that adds a sense of control to the chaos. The more you sort, the more you realize that the matrix of 150 cards is not random chaos but a living, breathing toolkit for playful experimentation. And if you are a collector who enjoys the hunt, sorting is a ritual worth practicing.
