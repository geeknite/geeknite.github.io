## Authenticity and spotting a real German promo deck
In the wild, it’s possible to encounter counterfeit or misrepresented decks. Here are practical tips for spotting authenticity and avoiding a scammy swap meet disappointment:

- Check the source: Genuine promos usually appear through known retailers, conventions, or official Hasbro channels. If someone is selling a “German Special Promotional Edition” via a random social media post with no provenance, tread lightly.
- Examine the card text: Authentic German text should read naturally and match standard German vocabulary used in board games. If you see awkward phrasing, obvious translation errors, or English words sneaking into the German text, you’ve got a red flag.
- Packaging cues: Look for accurate branding, font usage, and any official Hasbro or regional distributor marks. A missing legal notice or questionable logo placement is a warning sign.
- Card back design: Many promo editions keep the same card backs as the standard edition. A noticeably different back or inconsistent color shading can be a tell.
- Rarity claims: If someone’s listing price sounds unrealistically high for a promo deck, you should investigate further. Promo decks are special, but they’re not unicorns with golden tickets.

If you want a more reliable path to authenticity, stick with reputable retailers and community recommendations. The Board Game Geek forums, Hasbro’s regional pages, and established collectors’ networks are good starting points for sanity checks before you drop a substantial amount on a deck that’s supposed to add zest to your evenings.
