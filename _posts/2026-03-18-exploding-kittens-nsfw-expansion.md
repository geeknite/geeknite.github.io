Key changes you’ll notice:

If you are teaching new players, a quick tip: run a quick practice round with only base cards first, then reveal a few NSFW cards to gauge comfort levels. You can always play a shorter game with the base deck plus a couple of NSFW cards for a test run before committing to a full expansion session.

## Accessibility and Player Experience

Let’s talk about access and inclusive play. The NSFW expansion is not designed as a universal solution; it assumes a table comfortable with explicit humor, or at least tolerant of it after a good chuckle. Accessibility in terms of physical components remains on par with the base game: standard card stock, easy-to-hold card sizes, and a readable font that scales decently when lighting isn’t perfect. The challenge arises in content sensitivity rather than physical design. Some players may find certain jokes or references uncomfortable, and that is a legitimate reaction worth acknowledging before you invite the whole crew to the table.

For players with sensory concerns, consider an abridged ruleset that reduces the amount of explicit content or a rotisserie approach where you keep a rotating deck of cards and choose what to include. House rules can preserve the social energy while staying mindful of comfort levels.

Worth noting: the expansion can be used with social deduction or other light party games to extend session length without turning the table into a no-go zone for certain players. It is all about moderation and consent, both of which are underrated powers at a game night.
