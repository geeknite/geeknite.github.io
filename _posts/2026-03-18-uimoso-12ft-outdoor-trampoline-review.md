## Safety, maintenance, and upkeep
### Routine checks and maintenance
- Inspect the net and zipper for signs of wear; replace immediately if seams show stress.
- Check padding for cracks or dislodged covers.
- Inspect springs for rust or deformation and ensure they are evenly attached.
- Tighten frame bolts as needed after a few uses; vibration can loosen connections over time.
- Clean the mat with mild soap and water, avoiding harsh chemicals that can degrade the UV-resistant coating.

### Weather and storage tips
Store the trampoline during severe weather to prolong its life. If you don’t have storage space, a protective tarp can reduce sun damage but doesn’t replace winter storage. In winter, you may want to check the frame for any signs of frost or ice forming on the springs, which can become brittle if left untreated.

### Safety tips for families
- Enforce one-at-a-time jumping rules for younger kids and avoid crowded sessions to reduce collisions.
- Use the enclosure net and make sure the zipper is fully closed before anyone hops on.
- Clear the area around the trampoline of obstacles and hard surfaces that could cause injuries in a fall.
- Supervise all jump sessions, especially with younger children or first-time jumpers.
- Consider investing in a ground tarp and a maintenance kit for periodic checks as part of your trampoline care routine.
