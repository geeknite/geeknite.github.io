## Maintenance and Longevity

### Cleaning and care

- Wipe the playing surface with a soft, damp microfiber cloth to remove dust and fingerprints. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the finish.
- Vacuum or sweep around the table to prevent dust buildup around the legs and cabinet joints.
- Lubricate rods sparingly if you notice stiffness or resistance in rod movement. A few drops of light machine oil on the rod ends is often enough. Don’t overdo it—excess oil can attract dust and create a slippery mess on your first shot.
- Check screws and brackets periodically. Overtightening can cause cracking, while loose hardware can turn the table into an unstable carnival ride.

### Durability considerations

- MDF can be sensitive to humidity; consider keeping the table away from bathrooms, cooking areas, or humid basements. A dehumidifier in a damp game room is not a bad idea if you live in a climate with serious humidity swings.
- The cabinet edges may show micromissures over time with heavy play. A modest touch-up with matching paint or veneer repair kit can keep the table looking fresh for longer.
