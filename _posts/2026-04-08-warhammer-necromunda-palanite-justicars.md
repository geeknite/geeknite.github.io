## What are Palanite Justicars?
Palanite Justicars are an enforcement arm of the Palanite sector—a faction designed to bring a legalistic, orderly approach to the chaos of Necromunda’s mean streets. Think interplanetary DMV meets undercover sheriff meets emergency response team, all rolled into one squad with better armor and louder boots. In terms of fluff, they exist to bring the hammer of the law down on gang activities, Redemptionists, and anyone who grows a mustache while committing a crime. In play, they aim to be disciplined, repeatable, and terrifyingly accurate. They’re not flashy berserkers or sneaky gangers; they’re the kind of unit that would politely ask a gang leader to stand still so they can take a precise measurement with their authority metric and then deliver a verdict—usually in the form of “you’re down.”

In practical terms, you’ll likely want to use them as a mobile team that takes advantage of long-range firepower and disciplined shooting to pin enemy gangs. They are not the kind of squad that rushes headlong into a melee with reckless bravado; they prefer to set up a well-timed crossfire, send a few booted commands, issue a couple of “no mercy” warnings, and then unload with surgical precision. They shine when the board is gritty and the cover is patchy; their guns do not need to sound dramatic to be effective. The theme here is not “heroic charge” but “methodical enforcement.” That’s a line you’ll hear echoed in the fluff as well: the Justicars are about enforcing order, collecting evidence (in the form of stray enemy models on the tabletop), and leaving behind a chrysanthemum of momentum counters for the opposition to cry about later.

## Painting and conversion tips
If you’re anything like me, you want your Palanite Justicars to look like the standard-issue law in an urban hellscape, not a cosplay prop from a sci-fi convention. A few painting tips can make a huge difference:

- Armor and leather contrast: Use a dark metal for armor plates, then dry-brush with a mid-tone gray to bring out the edge highlights. Pair this with leather browns for straps and belts; the tonal shift sells the “functional, field-worn” vibe.
- Coats and fabric: For the coats, choose a neutral color like charcoal or muted blue. Weather the edges slightly with a fine brush to simulate wear, especially around elbow joints and knee guards where movement would rub the fabric.
- Badges and insignia: The Palanite units are meant to look official. Use gold or brass for insignia on the chest and shoulder pads, and a bright color for rank stripes to read clearly on the tabletop. A tiny dot of enamel paint in a high-contrast hue can make the insignia pop from across the board.
- Weathering and grime: The Underhive is dirty, and your models should show it. Sponging on rust colors, dust, and grime on the lower legs and boots can make the figure look like it’s spent a decade on the streets. Don’t overdo it—polished armor with a thin layer of grime looks credible.
- Bases: Think industrial, not moorland. A gritty tile texture, some cracked concrete, and little debris bits will anchor them in the environment. If you want a bonus, add a few micro-scenic elements like a discarded cog or a servo-skull to hint at the tech-laden governance of the Palanites.

Let’s talk about what makes a Palanite Justicar squad tick in gameplay terms, but with enough generality that you can apply their core strengths to your table without needing a full rules digest. The essence is discipline and controlled aggression rather than pure power. Expect things like:

- Leadership and order: Justicars benefit from leadership bonuses, enabling them to keep shooting discipline even when the heat is on. In practice, this means they don’t crumble under pressure as quickly as more desperate gangs.
- Range control: They excel at mid-to-long-range engagements, punishing those who overextend into their firing lanes. If you’re a table commander who loves pinning enemy units behind cover, you’ll be in your element.
- Cashing in on aggression: Their typical toolkit allows you to convert controlled aggression into meaningful board presence. The idea is to force your opponent to improvise around your line of fire, rather than forcing them to improvise around you.
- Versatility: While not the absolute best at close-quarters brawls, they have enough gadgetry and tactical options to hold the line when the enemy attempts to breach your toehold. You want them to be the lane-control specialists who can still take a competent poke in a pinch.

If you’re a veteran Necromunda player, you’ll recognize some familiar mechanics in the Palanite roster: the emphasis on positioning, the value of terrain, and the reality that a well-timed unit can swing a whole game. If you’re newer, think of them as the “systems engineer” faction: they optimize the board state and punish mistakes with precise answers instead of raw, indiscriminate firepower.

## Painting anecdotes and hobby hacks
Hobby time is where the Palanite Justicars shine as a storytelling opportunity. Here are a few goofy but practical ideas to bring your units to life:

- Weathered badge schemes: Use metallic paints for the insignia to give them a “badge of office” feel. A tiny red or blue accent on the badge can hint at the sector they serve without overpowering the model.
- Authority glow: A subtle glow effect on weapon sights can imply advanced targeting tech. A dab of blue glaze on the gun barrel or optic lens looks futuristic without shouting sci-fi cartoon.
- Uniform variety: If you want variety without breaking costume coherence, mix coats with slightly different hues—one slightly lighter, one darker—and keep the armor palette consistent. It reads as “different platoons,” which is authentic to a law-enforcement force.
- Base storytelling: Scatter a few underhive debris pieces on the base and a tiny servo-skull perched on a crate. It suggests the Palanites are not just chasing criminals but also collecting evidence in a city where every alley has a story.
