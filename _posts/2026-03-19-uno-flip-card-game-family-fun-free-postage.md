## How to Play: Rules and Gameplay
### Core rules
At its core, UNO Flip follows the standard UNO logic: match by color or number, play action cards to disrupt other players, and shout UNO when you have one card left. However, the two-sided mechanic turns this into a strategic sprint rather than a straight sprint to the finish line. The dark side’s cards add new possibilities and new penalties, which means the moment you flip, you might gift someone a killer chance to catch up—or you might lock yourself into a brutal endgame. The core loop remains: play cards, flip strategically, and outrun your opponents before the deck confounds you with a sudden Meld of Chaos.

### Turn structure and key cards
On your turn you must play a card that matches either color or number, or you may play a Wild card to change color. If you’re stuck, you draw a card. If the card you flip reveals new action cards, those are activated immediately after the flip. The endgame is where it gets delicious: planning matters, but adaptivity wins the day. 

### Special cards you’ll encounter
- Flip: Flip the entire deck to the opposite color side; this is the heartbeat of the game.
- Draw 5: Forces the next player to pick up five cards, a massive swing in a small amount of time.
- Skip: Skip the next player’s turn; a classic that becomes more chaotic when combined with Flip in the same round.
- Reverse: Reverses the direction of play.
- Wild: You can declare any color to continue your run.
- Draw 2 (on light side) / Draw 5 (on dark side): These keep the pressure up and the tempo wild.

Rules can vary slightly by edition, so be sure to check the included quick-start sheet, but the core loop remains: play cards, flip strategically, and outrun your opponents before the deck confounds you with a sudden twist.

### Endgame and penalties
When a player has one card left, they must declare UNO. If they forget, other players can prod them into claiming victory with a well-timed penalty or a playful chorus of “UNO!” The dark side’s penalties tend to be bigger, so you’ll want to manage your color choices carefully. The endgame is a tense, glorious scramble: sometimes you win by a hair, sometimes you lose when a wild color choice bounces around the table like a pinball.

## House Rules and Variants
No two families run UNO Flip the exact same way. Some common house rules that tend to improve the vibe:
- Short rounds: Keep a timer and call a round when the timer hits, preventing anyone from dragging the game into midnight territory.
- No talking during flips: A playful restriction that adds suspense and challenge.
- Combo flips: Allow players to chain flips in one round if the first flip reveals an actionable card that can instantly be used. This can lead to outrageous comebacks or crushing defeats.
- The “color wheel” rule: If a player’s color choices confuse, you award them a point for creative color strategy. It’s silly, but it keeps the game light-hearted and full of laughter.
