## Setup Tips for the Five Age Groups

- Age Group 1 (3–5): Start with the shortest setting, focus on light contact and big, enthusiastic swings. The goal is to associate golf with fun and confidence.
- Age Group 2 (5–7): Introduce a more consistent stance and contact point. Shorten the backswing a touch to keep form clean.
- Age Group 3 (7–9): Begin to refine alignment and target practice. Use the alignment aids to reinforce a straight path.
- Age Group 4 (9–12): Increase the challenge with longer clubs, but maintain comfort. Emphasize distance control and accuracy.
- Age Group 5 (12–15+): Treat it like a real gym session for the golf swing—work on mechanics, tempo, and repeatable motion. Your kid may soon surprise you with a wind-assisted drive that actually lands on a fairway.

## Maintenance and Care

- Clean clubs after each use to prevent corrosion and grime buildup. A damp cloth works wonders for shafts and heads.
- Inspect grips regularly; replace grips if they become slick or worn. Kids tend to be harsher on equipment than adults realize.
- Dry the bag and gear after use to prevent mold or mildew. A sunlit curbside air-out is perfect for this.
- Store in a cool, dry place to preserve the composite materials and to prevent the bag’s padding from losing its loft.
