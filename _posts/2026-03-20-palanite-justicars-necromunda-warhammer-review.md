## Overview: what the Palanite Justicars actually are
The Palanite Justicars are the elite arm of the Palanite Enforcers—the long arm of the law in Necromunda. Think of them as the police with extra swagger, a badge that glows when they roll high, and a moral compass that’s been bent, tempered, and re-welded by a hive-wide conspiracy of crime, politics, and bad coffee.

In the hive, you don’t just arrest criminals; you arrest the idea of crime itself. Justicars are deployed when a crime is too multi-layered for your average ganger to wrap their head around, or when the local crimelords want to show you that even the police have a side hustle. The miniature range is designed to look like they mean business: sleek armor, data-slates, and a habit of standing in the center of a room while everyone else pretends not to notice the surveillance drone overhead. It’s flamboyantly lawful, if you catch my drift.

## Fluff and lore: the underhive’s moral weather report
Lore-wise, the Justicars straddle a very human line: they are the good guys who get their hands dirty faster than a mechanic at a working bench. They’re tasked with sweeping districts, interrogating suspects, and sometimes delivering “special cautions” to political enemies of the Hive’s power brokers. The imagery is classic noir: floodlights, rain-slick corridors, and a squad that feels like it’s there to remind you that order is a currency—and it’s expensive.

From a narrative perspective, the Justicars shine because they’re a symptom of Necromunda’s constant tension between order and chaos. They don’t just enforce the law; they enforce a version of it that looks suspiciously convenient for one faction or another. If you’re painting a campaign arc about “the costs of order,” Palanite Justicars are your best friends (and your worst nightmare) in miniature form. They’re a reminder that in the underhive, even the cops have a bad day and still show up with authority in spades.

## Rules and playstyle: the law you want to align with… most of the time
Note: Necromunda rules evolve with editions and supplements, so don’t be shocked if your local meta changes what a Justicar can do. In general, the Palanite Justicars are built around a few core ideas:

- Precision firepower and disciplined shooting. They’re not the kind of unit you throw into a meat grinder—they excel when you want to shape an engagement, deny a contested objective, and force a counter-attack from your opponent.
- Strong access to custody-type abilities: control, arrest-like mechanics, and chain-of-command style maneuvers that favor defensive play and punishing over-extensions by criminals.

Actual numeric profiles depend on your edition, but visually and tactically they lean into the vibe of “lawful neutral with a repentant streak.” You’ll find them performing well in bulletproof, mid-range skirmish roles, with a bias toward maintaining discipline in your lines. ### Tactics and synergy: how to actually use them in a list
- Core approach: Justicars work best when you run a tight, disciplined bulwark formation. Place them where the opponent’s forces must commit to a path through your crossfire and controlled zones. Their presence says: you want this objective? Prove it.
- Synergy with criminal networks: you’ll often see them paired with factions that generate leverage or data-rich resources. The flavor here is the law clamping down on the hive’s more chaotic elements, while still letting your criminal factions shine in their own right. If your narrative is “the law vs the underhive,” Justicars make the law look good—so your opponent will want to debate you, not rush you.
- Target priority: utility units, leaders with game-breaking skills, and fast movers that threaten your objective lines are the usual targets. The Justicars excel when they can deny the enemy’s best moves and force the fight to come to them on their terms.

## Painting, modeling, and hobby tips
The Palanite Justicars are a fun model to paint because their aesthetic is a blend of utilitarian police gear with a dash of sci-fi chic. Here are some practical tips to get that Geeknite vibe without losing your sanity:

- Color scheme ideas: Think matte steels with brass-gold badge accents and a splash of deep blue or crimson for authority. The badge should be instantly legible from across a table, which is to say, you want a halo of metallic shine around important symbols.
- Weathering and grime: The hive is a damp place. Use weathering powders to hint at diesel smoke and rain-slick surfaces. A little grime around joints and greaves sells the “lived-in” enforcement vibe without turning the models into walking tin cans.
- Badges and insignia: The Palanite mark is a visual hook. You can add tiny decals or careful decals to represent the enforcement corp. If you’re a conversion hobbyist, you can push the look with hooded capes, data-slates, or a couple of forensic tools clipped to belts.
- Bases: Basing is where a lot of the character lands. A cracked hive floor, a drain, or a palmled corridor with a faint glow in the distance adds narrative punch. If you’re feeling spicy, a tiny micro-drone modeled as a drone may become your model’s sidekick.
- Conversions: Palanite Justicars pair nicely with Palanite Enforcers kits if you want to run an allied or mixed force. You can swap heads, add arrest cuffs, and create a squad that has both “courtroom precision” and “alleyway resolve.” The result: dynamic, narrative-heavy games that still feel mechanically solid.

## Lore-friendly lists and alternatives: a few thoughts
If you’re torn between Palanite Justicars and other enforcement-themed troops, consider the following narrative anchors:
- Palanite Enforcers: the core, more robust enforcement option. They’ve got staying power and a straightforward anti-crime toolkit. Justicars complement them by adding precision and a more controlled tempo to your game.
- Escher and Delaque networks: mixing enforcers with crime lords adds a chessboard feel to your table. The Justicars act as the “state”—the hive’s official face—while Escher or Delaque pieces push political agendas and manipulate the battlefield in more roundabout ways.
- Goliath support teams: If you want a star in your narrative who can blunt enemy charges, a palanite squad can anchor a frontline while a Goliath-heavy unit does the brawling. It’s not purely about raw power; it’s about the show of law and the promise that consequences follow actions.

Experiment with these dynamics in friendly games first. It’s the best way to learn how much of the law you actually want on the board before your next tournament grinder.
