### Maintenance and cleaning: keeping the slate clean
In the world of real-life rug maintenance, the main villains are spills and pet hair. The Colleen Slate Woven Rug responds well to routine care. Vacuum regularly on a low setting to prevent shedding that looks like a small winter in your living room. If you spill something sticky, blot immediately—never rub, or you’ll push the mess deeper into the weave like a rogue programmer pushing a bug into a production release. For more stubborn spots, a mild detergent solution and a gentle blot can lift the issue without discoloring the surface. Do a quick spot test in an inconspicuous corner before committing to the full-clean procedure. The key is to respect the rug’s material integrity while giving it a spa day when needed.

If you’re dealing with pet fur, a quick pass with a brush or upholstery tool helps, but you’ll find that the slate color hides hair better than lighter shades. And yes, you can use a rug pad to minimize movement and add a layer of comfort; the foam or felt pads also help preserve the floor underneath, which is good for your standing desk when you do those long coding sessions that require you to stretch occasionally.
