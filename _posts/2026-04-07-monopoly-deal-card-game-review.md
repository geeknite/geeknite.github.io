On your turn, you draw two cards (or one if you’re short on life). You can then play several cards from your hand: you can put money into your bank, lay down property sets, or unleash an action card to disrupt others.\n\nKey mechanics:\n- Money management: You must pay rent with money cards; the bank acts as your wallet and your shield. Keeping a rainy-day reserve can be the difference between third place and the coveted title of “new landlord.”\n- Property sets: You need three complete color sets to win; some colors may require more than one set to complete. The tension comes from rivals snatching your dream colors away with a well-timed action card.\n- Action cards: The spicy pepper in your ramen. Deal Breaker can scoop a full property set from another player, Just Visiting charges rent with a twist, and Sly Trickery steals a card from another. These cards are the social grease that makes the game sing in a crowded room.\n- Hand management: You can discard any number of cards at the end of your turn; holding onto a useless card while the others chant for one more turn is a classic form of torture that Monopoly Deal somehow monetizes through laughter.\n\nA typical turn often looks like this: draw two, decide which cards to play (money into the bank, properties onto the table, or actions), potentially disrupt an opponent, then end your turn. The randomness of the draw means you seldom know what late rounds will require, but you can shape outcomes by how you spend your money and how you time your action cards.
