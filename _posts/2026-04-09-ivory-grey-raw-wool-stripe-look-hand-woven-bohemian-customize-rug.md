## Practicalities: Size, Weight, and Care

Size matters, as does the weight of the individual fibers. Raw wool tends to be denser than synthetic fibers, which can influence how the rug sits on various floor types. A properly chosen size ensures the rug remains in place, rather than slipping under your couch legs like a mischievous cat.

Care considerations for raw wool include:

- Vacuuming: Use a vacuum without a beater bar or with a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers.
- Spot cleaning: Attend to spills quickly with blotting techniques and a mild wool-safe cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that may degrade the natural fiber.
- Sun exposure: Prolonged direct sun can cause ivory to warm and grey to fade; rotate furniture and rotate the rug to distribute wear.
- Professional cleaning: Every couple of years, consider a professional clean to maintain texture and color fidelity.

Weight distribution is another factor for larger sizes. If your space has a high-traffic area, you might want to anchor the rug with furniture legs or add a non-slip rug pad underneath to prevent movement.

## Comparisons: How It Stands Among Similar Pieces

In the world of bohemian, stripe, raw wool rugs, there are a few common design archetypes. A homeowner might compare:

- Hand-knotted vs hand-woven: The hand-woven approach emphasizes the organic texture and irregularities; hand-knotted rugs can be more defined and dense, sometimes with higher price points.
- Raw wool vs treated wool: Raw wool retains natural fiber characteristics and slight fuzziness; treated wool might be easier to clean but can feel more manufactured.
- Stripe intensity: Some stripe rugs lean toward bold, contrasted stripes; this Ivory Grey piece leans more subtle, with soft transitions between ivory andgrey.
