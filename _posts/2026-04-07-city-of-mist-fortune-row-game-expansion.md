## Overview of Fortune Row
Fortune Row is not merely a new district; it is a different mode of storytelling tucked inside the existing City of Mist framework. The expansion adds a second layer to the city map, a new row of dramatic events that players can unlock through a series of scenes that feel like they are pulled from a noir movie stitched together with glitchy dream logic. The core idea is that Fortune Row is a place where fortunes rise and fall on a razor-edge—where luck favors and betrayals are a skill set.

This expansion introduces new factions who literally live on the edge of your local mythic reality: a banking cartel with a soft spot for itty bitty miracles, a street gang that believes fate is a currency, and a council of masked patrons who pretend they are running a charity while actually running a city-shaped board game with a vibe that says we are all in this together but also we all know someone has to lose.

Fortune Row also brings new types of scenes and encounters that require you to balance risk, opportunity, and storytelling momentum. The rules build on the base game by adding a new layer of mystery and a few more levers you can tug when the narrative pivots from a simple caper into something that looks suspiciously like fate wearing a fedora.

### What’s in the Box
The Fortune Row expansion includes a handful of distinct components that feel, in spirit, like they were designed to be photographed and then talked about endlessly at con tables. Here is a quick run-through of what you get, with a few notes on how each piece feels in actual play:

- A map-section that expands the urban grid into a compact, porous neighborhood with new landmarks and alleys. The map is modular, which means you can rearrange it to create a different rhythm for your sessions. It also invites players to think about positional storytelling in ways the base game sometimes makes feel optional.
- Faction cards that introduce fresh antagonists and allies. The factions aren’t just flavor; they alter how you approach scenes and how much leverage you have when dealing with the city’s power structures.
- A new set of cover art masks that fit the Fortune Row vibe. If your party has a lucky scoundrel or a jaded detective, these masks help visually cue your players into the kind of mythic identity they want to inhabit for the next arc. The art is vivid, occasionally a little chaotic, and absolutely on-brand for the neon-noir mood.
- A stack of scenario hooks and mission cards. These snappy prompts feel like cinematic trailers—quick, stylish, and a little dangerous. They push the session from a simple caper to a story where every choice ripples through the city and into the mythos you are building.
- A handful of new power cards and a couple of mechanics that tilt the balance toward risk-reward play. The new resources require careful management, and for good or ill, they reward players who lean into clever scene framing rather than brute dice dominance.
- A handful of “fortune dice” that tilt outcomes toward the dramatic, but not always toward the fairy-tale ending you hoped for. They are not mandatory, but they feel right for a city that thrives on dramatic tension and late-game reversals.
- A compact GM screen that reveals the key Fortune Row rules for quick reference. It keeps the action moving, which is important because Fortune Row can feel like a high-stakes parade of near-misses if you let it bubble up slowly.

The physical quality is on par with what you would expect from City of Mist expansions: sturdy cards, glossy art, and a compact footprint that makes it easy to slip into your existing setup. The number of components might be slightly higher than some players expect, but the payoff in terms of play variety is worth it if you are a group that loves to chase long-form campaigns rather than one-shot mysteries.

## Thematic Core and Narrative Potential
Fortune Row shines when you lean into its thematic premise: a street-level mythos that matters as much for the characters’ internal stories as for the external mythic battles. In Fortune Row, luck is a social currency as much as a mechanic. Your characters do not simply fight crime; they weave fate into conversations, relationships, and the way power flows through the city like a living, breathing organism. This expansion invites players to ask questions that the base game sometimes skirts: Who benefits from a moment of luck, and who pays the price? How do you craft a story when Fortune Row can turn a minor encounter into a city-shaking revelation if the dice happen to align with a character arc the players have been nurturing for sessions?

The new scenes emphasize moral ambiguity and the gray zones between heroism and opportunism. If your table likes to argue about who deserves what, Fortune Row gives you a stage large enough for those debates without forcing you into a punitive, rule-heavy argument. It encourages you to narrate consequences that feel meaningful, rather than a string of coincidences with flashy names attached.

From a playability perspective, the expansion gives your group something new to chase without requiring a full re-implementation of your campaign. The new factions can slot into ongoing arcs or be used to seed a fresh mini-arc that dovetails with what you already have. The rules maintain a gentle learning curve; you do not need to relearn the entire system to start using Fortune Row effectively. That said, the first few sessions with Fortune Row can feel a little chaotic as players test the edges of the new mechanics and discover how far they can tilt the narrative before someone calls the shot clock in a dramatic way.

If you want to widen the lens on the city’s larger-than-life mythos, Fortune Row is a good choice. If you prefer a tight, linear story with a fixed antagonist, you might want to approach with caution and a plan to weave Fortune Row into a longer arc rather than attempting a one-shot caper that tries to cram every new mechanic into a single night.

## Balance and Mechanics
The core balance question with any expansion is: does it reshuffle the power dynamics in a way that makes the base game feel stale, or does it breathe new life into the system without trampling what people already love? Fortune Row mostly errs on the side of additive design rather than radical overhaul. You will notice a few tweaks to resource management and a handful of new triggers for scenes, but the essence of the game remains recognizable and intuitive for players who have already internalized the base rules.

One neat trick Fortune Row pulls off is to integrate the concept of fortune into the narrative engine rather than just the die pool. You see the mythos and the mask intersect at critical moments, and the Fate-driven events feel less like random card draws and more like narrative punctuation that the players can guide, provided they negotiate the stakes honestly and creatively at the table.

There are a couple of moments where the expansion can feel a touch swingy. A lucky roll or a well-timed scene can swing momentum in a way that makes a few sessions feel like a countdown to a spectacular reveal. The GM will want to manage pacing carefully, especially in longer campaigns, to avoid placing too much control in the hands of a single lucky break. This is not a fault of Fortune Row so much as a reminder that the city rewards bold storytelling decisions and occasionally punishes them through narrative consequences.

From a solo or two-player perspective, Fortune Row can still shine, but the dynamic changes. The added complexity is easier to manage with a full table of role players who enjoy banter and collaborative storytelling. If you are a duo, you will want to dedicate more time to rule-finessing and gut-checking the narrative pace to ensure both players get the space to influence the story meaningfully.

## Components, Artwork, and Production Value
The visual language of Fortune Row is unmistakably City of Mist: bold contrasts, neon hues, and characters who look like they walked out of a gallery show dedicated to myths on the run. The art direction continues to lean into the stylish noir vibe with a modern twist, which makes the expansion feel like a natural extension of the base game rather than a separate aesthetic experiment.

The card stock and print quality are good, if not absolutely premium. You will find that the more you shuffle and re-use the new cards, the more you appreciate the design intention behind each symbol, icon, and color choice. The new cards feature clear icons that help keep gameplay flowing, even when players start to drift into the more baroque layers of the Fortune Row system.

The map section, while optional in some plays, offers a tangible sense of progression. It gives the table a sense of motion and helps visualize narrative beats in a way that a flat board sometimes struggles to achieve. The modular design means you can place the new map assets in ways that reflect your group’s preferred pacing, which is a nice touch that shows the designers thought about long-term playability as well as first-night awe.

## Interactions with Base Game and Other Expansions
Fortune Row is designed to be a bridge between the core game and a broader collection of expansions. It plays well with the base mechanics and creates opportunities to integrate with other City of Mist modules. If you have previously collected other expansions, you can weave Fortune Row into those narratives by leaning on shared mythos and cross-pollinated factions. The result is a mesh that feels cohesive rather than disjointed, which is something a lot of expansions claim to deliver but fewer actually manage.

From a practical standpoint, you should consider how you want to structure your session flow. Fortune Row invites more dynamic scene management and negotiation with the city’s power players. That means the GM will likely spend more time preparing, but the payoff is a session that feels more alive and less scripted. Players who enjoy a higher degree of agency will likely savor Fortune Row more than those who prefer a tighter, more deterministic arc.

If you are mixing Fortune Row with other City of Mist expansions, you will want to keep an eye on the power balance across factions. It is possible to end up with a couple of factions that feel overpowered or underutilized if you do not actively curate the narrative space for them to interact. A little pre-session planning can yield a campaign arc that uses multiple factions in a way that mirrors a city at war for control of its fate, while still letting your players feel like the heroes of a story they are actively helping to author.

## Examples of Scenes and Play Patterns
Fortune Row shines in moments where the stakes are personal and the city itself becomes a character. Here are a few play patterns that tend to sing when Fortune Row is on the table:

- The Glittering Backroom: A political gathering where fortunes are told and fortunes are bartered. Characters must decide who to trust and how far they will bend the truth to secure a future that might be morally questionable—but maybe necessary for a bigger goal.
- A Heist with a Mythic Edge: The crew plans a caper that relies on fortune as a resource. The plan unfolds in unexpected ways as masks reveal their threads of destiny, and the heist becomes more about what the city wants from them than what they want from the city.
- The Debt That Keeps a City Running: A faction leader threatens to pull the rug on a hero if they refuse to cooperate. This scenario forces players to weigh loyalty to their own mythos against survival and the greater good they claim to protect.

These patterns hinge on a rhythm of negotiation, risk-taking, and in-game storytelling that the base game established but Fortune Row enhances. The end results tend to be dramatic, often surprising, and frequently funny in the way only a neon-lit city full of flawed heroes can be.

## Reader Tips and House Rules
- Start small: If your table is new to Fortune Row, begin with one or two new factions and one extra scene seed per session. Let the players acclimate to the new mechanics before you unleash the full suite of Fortune Row tricks.
- Balance is a living thing: If you notice a single mechanic dominates the session, consider tweaking its availability or cost. The fun of Fortune Row is in the interplay, not in one character wielding the most efficient setup.
- Narration matters: The luxury of Fortunes Row is that it rewards strong storytelling. Encourage players to describe their decisions with mythic language and give the GM applause when a scene turns on a well-told turn of phrase.
- Use the post-roll dynamic to reset pacing: If a session starts to drag, introduce a Fortune Row micro-arc that forces a new decision, something that reset the emotional tempo and invites a character to reveal a secret that changes the negotiation landscape.

## Who Should Buy Fortune Row?
- Players who crave narrative depth: If you want your hero to feel like they matter within a living city, this expansion offers a richer playground.
- GMs who like table dynamics: The new factions and scenes are a gift to narrators who enjoy steering conversations and shaping the dramatic arc without railroading players.
- Groups that love re-playability: The modular map and the variety of scenarios mean you can revisit Fortune Row across multiple sessions without feeling repetitive.
- Players who enjoy a little chaos: If you like the thrill of a good risk-reward choice and watching your plans unravel into something spectacular, you will likely smile a lot with Fortune Row on the table.

If you fall into any of these camps, Fortune Row will feel like a fresh, flashy injection of ideas into your City of Mist table. If your group prefers a tight, single-molution plot and a fixed ending, you might want to approach Fortune Row as a separate mini-campaign rather than as a core part of your nightly routine.

## Playability with Other Expansions
Fortune Row integrates nicely with other City of Mist supplements, but you will want to coordinate with your group to align various factions and masks so that you are not duplicating mechanics or competing with a dozen narrative threads. The expansion does not demand other packs to shine; it stands on its own as a strong, vivid addition. However, if your patchwork city already has a robust set of factions and storylines, Fortune Row can function as a dramatic multiplier, multiplying both the conflicts and the opportunities for players to assert their mythic identity in a city that loves to reward bold storytelling.

From a practical perspective, you might want to re-skin some of the Fortune Row elements to fit your current campaign’s tone. The core mechanics adapt well to different vibes, whether your group leans toward noir comedy, heist drama, or mythic noir with a dash of surrealism. The expansion leaves enough design flexibility that you can tailor the mood and intensity to your table, rather than forcing your table to endure a one-size-fits-all experience.

## The Final Verdict
Fortune Row is a robust expansion that delivers both mechanical depth and narrative richness without losing the core identity of City of Mist. It invites players to explore a new micro-neighborhood with its own myths, its own power players, and its own set of moral questions. The new material fits smoothly with the base game and with most other expansions, providing additional play options that feel substantial rather than ornamental.

If you love City of Mist for its storytelling potential, Fortune Row is a compelling next stop. You will find yourself negotiating with new factions, orchestrating dramatic scenes that feel like cinematic set-pieces, and discovering that luck can be a weapon in more ways than one. The tone is consistent with the base game but energized by a sharper focus on risk, desire, and the city’s messy, glamorous underbelly. The result is a product that respects the players and their story while offering enough new ideas to keep the campaign feeling fresh rather than repetitive.

That said, Fortune Row is not a casual purchase. It expects a certain level of engagement from the players and the GM, and it rewards those who are ready to invest a little planning time to weave the new content into ongoing arcs. If your table treats roleplaying games as a comfortable, self-contained evening, Fortune Row might feel like overkill. If, on the other hand, you crave the thrill of a city that talks back and a mythology that refuses to stay quiet, this expansion will not disappoint.
