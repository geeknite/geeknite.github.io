## How to Play: Setup and Turn Structure
Phase 10 is a game of phases, runs, sets, and moderate chaos. The Deluxe Version preserves this structure but makes the physical experience nicer, so you can pretend your living room has a dedicated game night vibe rather than a chaotic afternoon by people who forgot to clean the coffee table. The setup is straightforward:

- Each player receives a hand of ten cards
- The rest of the deck becomes a draw pile
- The top card is flipped to form a discard pile
- The first player to complete the current phase on their sheet claims the round and advances to the next phase on their personal track

On a typical turn, you draw one card, then either lay down a valid phase combination or add to your existing run or set. The flow is simple but not trivial: you must assemble the required phase pattern before you can advance; you can also steal from opponents in some versions of the game by completing your phase and ending your turn with the right cards in your hand. If you cannot complete a phase in a round, you draw and wait for another opportunity. Play continues until one player finishes all ten phases and wins the game. For fans of the selective chaos that comes with card games, the Deluxe Version adds a tactile layer of satisfaction but does not alter how you count your phases or how the scoring works.
