Fortune Row lands in City of Mist as a neon drenched expansion that dares you to juggle myths and mortgages. If the core book is a streetlight guiding a sleepy city through fog, Fortune Row is the club next door with a velvet rope and a cash bar that accepts favors as currency. This review will walk you through what Fortune Row does well, what it tries to do and where it trips over its own high heels, and whether the book is worth your time, your table, and your favorite dice.

## Introduction

City of Mist has always been a love letter to detective fiction skewed through the prism of urban myth. Fortune Row, as a core supplement, leans into glamor, risk, and the shimmering edge where luck and liability collide. Think neon chandeliers, mossy back alleys, and the sort of characters who can turn a parking ticket into a mythic opportunity if they talk fast enough. This book is loud, it is confident, and it wants your table to feel like a late-night caper with a soundtrack that refuses to drop its beat.

Fortune Row also lives up to its promise of being a strong gateway for players who crave social maneuvering as much as puzzle-solving. If you loved the core book but found yourself wishing for more district flavor and more opportunities to lean into a character’s mythic identity, you will likely adore this supplement. If you prefer a leaner, procedural style of mystery, Fortune Row will still offer you plenty to grab onto, but you might need to prune some of the excess swagger to keep the investigative heartbeat steady.

In this review, we’ll cover design choices, mechanical fiddly bits, the table experience, and how Fortune Row fits into the ongoing City of Mist ecosystem. We’ll also share table-ready tips and a few play examples to help you imagine how the content translates from page to play.

## What Fortune Row Is And Isn’t

Fortune Row is a district-focused expansion that introduces new archetypes, new mechanics around luck and risk, and a curated set of ready-to-run adventures. It is not a complete rewrite of how City of Mist operates; rather, it sharpens the edges of the existing toolkit and gives GMs new levers to pull when crafting a mystery that smells like trouble and glitter. The tone remains neon-noir but with a stronger emphasis on social leverage, high-stakes bets, and the theater of the city as a stage for mythic acts.

The core ambition of Fortune Row is to provide a playground where investigators can flirt with danger while negotiating the terms of the city’s mythic economy. It asks: what happens when luck becomes a resource, when a deal is less about the evidence and more about the right story told at the right moment? If the core book is your detective toolbox, Fortune Row is the optional add-on kit that makes the toolbox flashier, louder, and a touch riskier to wield.

## Mechanical Shifts and Tools

### The Fortune Tokens and Card Economy

One of Fortune Row’s most visible additions is a refined set of tools around luck and momentum. Fortune Tokens enter the scene as a peripheral resource that players can spend to nudge outcomes, trigger a dramatic revelation, or tilt a social exchange in their favor. This feels like a natural extension of the core system’s tag-and-dice ethos, but with a sharper emphasis on social leverage and narrative acceleration.

In practice, Fortune Tokens encourage players to lean into the performance side of the game. You’ll see players narrating their actions with more flair because there’s a tangible benefit to doing so. The tokens also create a nice tension for the GM during investigations: if players invest in a bluff or a misdirection, the GM can respond with a clever counter-move that preserves balance while heightening drama.

### New Archetypes and Identity Tools

Fortune Row introduces archetypes that fit its neon-luxe vibe without destabilizing the core book’s balance. Expect high-ego personas, street-smart hustlers, and mystics who interpret luck as a living force rather than a passive trait. The character sheets include a set of Persona prompts that guide players toward a mythic identity aligned with Fortune Row’s themes. The intent is to nudge players toward roleplay that is both stylish and substantive, rather than mere costume.

The archetypes are designed to slot into existing investigator roles. You won’t need a whole new math system to support them; they sit in the same dice-pool framework and simply reference new tags, new outcomes, and new prompts. The result is a toolbox that feels familiar yet gives you a new palette for storytelling.

### Modular Heists and Narrative Seeds

A large portion of Fortune Row is built around modular heists—structured, table-ready scenarios that you can drop into an ongoing City of Mist campaign with minimal prep. Each heist comes with an intent line, a handful of key NPCs, a map note, a set of misdirection opportunities, and a closing beat that lands with a satisfying narrative snap.

The benefit here is twofold: first, you have a quick-start option when a session needs to be booked quickly; second, the modular structure lends itself to re-skinning. If your group loves a certain motif—casino intrigue, gilded bohemia, or a busted promenade—Fortune Row gives you a handful of seeds that can be re-styled with a sentence or two of GM fiat.

### Clues, Clocks, and Clout

The installation of district-level specificity brings a sense of place and recurring motif without forcing the GM to micromanage every corner of the city. Clues feel localized to Fortune Row’s milieu, and clocks keep players moving with visible, tangible progress markers. It’s not just about finding a clue; it’s about understanding how the clue interacts with Fortune Row’s social economy and mythic rules. The nuance here rewards tables that enjoy the gears turning as much as the lights flicker.

## Setting and Tone

Fortune Row keeps the neon-noir flavor of City of Mist but introduces a more explicit social theater flavor. You’ll encounter gilded ballrooms, back-alley pawnshops with sacred sigils, and rooftop gardens where deals are hashed over in whispers. The writing leans into swagger without neglecting the narrative gravity of a city where myths walk among us in tailored suits.

The district level focus helps maintain a consistent mood throughout sessions. GMs have a clearer target for atmosphere, which can be a relief at the table when you’re juggling multiple plot threads. The trade-off is that the book is pretty confident in its aesthetic. If your table loves a certain level of pageantry and a strong theme through-line, Fortune Row is a dream; if your group prefers minimalist vibes, you may want to skim the art-heavy sections and get to the mechanics and seeds more quickly.

## World-Building: Districts, Factions, and Clues

Fortune Row goes deeper into the concept of a city as a living organism, segmented into facets that correspond to mythic motifs. You’ll meet factions that embody different interpretations of luck, fate, and risk, each with its own agendas and negotiation styles. The book treats these factions less as enumerate-and-contra villains and more as partners in a larger dance of influence. It’s a smart choice because it invites players to project their own identity onto a lively faction, not just to fear an external antagonist.

Clues, when tied to Fortune Row’s motifs, have a distinct flavor. They don’t just point to a suspect; they hint at a system of power—who benefits, who is owed, who is bluffing. That alignment with the mythic economy helps maintain coherence across sessions, so even when a table diversifies into subplots, the threads feel like part of a single city fabric rather than a collection of loose ideas.

## GM Tools and Adventure Framework

Fortune Row shines in the GM-facing material. The book includes angle sheets, mood boards, and a set of ready-to-run acts that can anchor a campaign. There’s a tangible benefit to having this kind of scaffolding: you can pivot on a dime without losing narrative momentum. The risk, of course, is that the GM might lean too heavily on the provided hooks and miss opportunities to tailor the session to player interests. The cure here is to use the tools as templates rather than scripts. Treat the fortune economy as a leaping-off point for improvisation, not a path carved in stone.

For players, the advantage is a deepening of character through Fortune Row’s identities and motifs. The new archetypes encourage a more performative, social approach to problem solving without discarding the investigative core that makes City of Mist unique. The net effect is a game that can feel like a high-stakes masquerade while still rewarding careful observation and deduction.

## Sample Play and Table Experience

To give you a sense of how Fortune Row lands at the table, imagine a session where a gilded casino becomes the locus of a mythic wager. Investigators gather in a private room, where a velvet rope is more literal than metaphor: you must persuade the doorman to let you pass while a tide of rumors swirls around the neon sign outside. A Fortune Token is spent on a bluff that looks risky on the surface but cracks open a crucial misdirection. The GM counters with a gesture toward a clue that ties a patron’s fortune to a family curse, reframing the table’s understanding of who stands to win and who pays the price.

This is not a game that rewards pure brute force; it rewards players who lean into performance, analysis, and a willingness to lean into the mythic dimension of the city. The encounters are not just fights and chases; they’re social duels, moral tradeoffs, and strategic bets about who gets to shape the city’s future. The result is sessions that feel cinematic without sacrificing the detective logic that makes City of Mist compelling.

## Artwork, Layout, and Accessibility

Fortune Row maintains the striking aesthetic of the core book—bold typography, lush color palettes, and panels that invite the eye to linger. The art direction intentionally amplifies glamor and danger, which helps set the tone before players even roll a die. In terms of accessibility, the layout is dense in places and benefits from a calm table-read. Some pages lean into a stylized presentation that may require a brief moment of explanation for players who prefer straightforward, text-forward design. The authors do a solid job of balancing mood with readability, but as with any heavily stylized supplement, you’ll want to pace your table’s exposure to the art and flavor to avoid sensory overload.

## Comparisons: Fortune Row vs Core Book and Other Expansions

Compared to the core book, Fortune Row is more prescriptive about mood and setting, with a stronger emphasis on social maneuvering and high-stakes currency. It doesn’t replace the core mechanics; it expands them with a distinct style. If you own multiple City of Mist supplements, Fortune Row sits well with them and can serve as a thematic bridge between different districts and stories. It’s not so much a replacement as a stylistic upgrade for groups who want a more flamboyant, theater-driven play experience.

For groups curious about a lighter or leaner experience, it’s worth noting that Fortune Row can be used in shorter campaigns or as a recurring campaign arc without consuming all your table’s attention. The modular adventures can be deployed in a one-shot, a two-session arc, or an ongoing storyline, depending on how you pace your sessions. If you’re chasing a “big score” vibe without sacrificing investigative texture, Fortune Row will likely feel timely and satisfying.

The book includes a compact roster of NPCs and a handful of quick references that can speed up setup. If you like to improvise on the fly, these sheets serve as reliable anchors. You’ll find NPCs with clear motives aligned to Fortune Row’s motifs, as well as a few “face cards” that other groups have used to spark memorable moments. ## Playtest Thoughts and Real-World Use

In our playtests, Fortune Row tended to shine most when the table leaned into roleplay and social maneuvering. The tokens and archetypes provided opportunities for players to express character through dialogue, fashion, and strategic risk. When groups defaulted to combat-heavy play, the book still provided robust mechanics to accommodate a fight scene, but the magic comes from the negotiation and misdirection—the heart of what makes City of Mist distinctive.

If your group is already comfortable with the core system, Fortune Row offers a gentle learning curve that pays off in dramatic, memorable sessions. If you’re still feeling your way around the probabilistic dice engine, the Fortune Token layer is a manageable extra that won’t derail the core flow of the game.

## Final Verdict

Fortune Row is a stylish, purposeful expansion that delivers a strong district flavor and a robust toolbox for GMs and players who crave a more theatrical, social, and luck-forward mythic experience. It does not rewrite the foundational rules; it amplifies them with a specific mood and a set of modular, table-ready adventures that can be slotted into an ongoing campaign or used to seed a short arc. If you love City of Mist and you want more reasons to lean into your character’s mythic identity, Fortune Row is a worthy addition to your shelf.
