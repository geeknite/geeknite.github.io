## Setup and Assembly
### Tools Included
The box includes the usual handful of Allen wrenches and a few spare screws, the kind you later discover you actually need to secure after a misaligned component no one warned you about. The lesson here is to not panic when you see a bag with little hex wrenches that resembles a tiny space fleet. It is all part of the fun, and you will feel like a mechanical wizard by the end of it.

### Step-by-step Assembly Tips
- Start with the base legs and level them. A wobbly table is the ultimate cheat in foosball; you do not want to give your opponent a mental handicap via a tilting surface. 
- Attach the cabinet to the legs and ensure the side rails align. It is worth taking a moment to double-check alignment before you tighten everything down.
- Install the rods and ensure the cracks are properly lubricated. Do not over-lubricate; a little goes a long way, and a lot can create a slippery situation that saps your motivation to chase the ball.
- Attach the playing surface and mount the scorer if you chose to go with the included option. If you skip the scorer, you are saving weight but losing a tiny, satisfying nerdy flourish.

After about an hour of careful assembly, the table stood tall and confident, like a ship docking perfectly at a sci-fi harbor. The final step is to test the table with a few practice rounds, which doubles as a break-in and an opportunity to identify any creaks that might require a quick torque adjustment.

## Maintenance and Longevity
Like any sports equipment that sees frequent use, the VEVOR foosball table benefits from routine care. Wipe the playing surface to remove fingerprints, dust, and the occasional droplet of beverage from a victory celebration. Check the rod end caps and screws every few weeks to ensure nothing has loosened due to enthusiastic play. If you have pets or small children, consider a corner guard or a wall-mounted bumper to protect the paint from accidental bumps during post-goal celebrations.

Lubrication is optional but helps the rods glide even more smoothly. Use a light silicone-based lubricant sparingly, avoiding over-application that might create a slick bottle of chaos on the field. The key is consistent maintenance; it is not a one-off sprint but a marathon of small upkeep rituals that keep the table feeling brand new.
