## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Denim Deep
### Cleaning Guidelines
Routine maintenance is straightforward. Regular vacuuming with a suction setting that avoids aggressive power helps prevent the pile from flattening in high-traffic areas. If you do encounter stains, dab gently rather than rubbing, using a mild detergent solution and a clean cloth. For more stubborn marks, test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fading. If you spill coffee or red wine, act quickly and blot—not rub—for best results. A quick refresh with a gentle steam or light brush can revive the pile and restore its plush feel.

### Long-Term Care
Over time you may notice some natural shading that adds character rather than detracts from the look. That is not a defect but a feature of wool-blend rugs with denim-inspired tones. If you live in a climate with high humidity, a rug pad can help reduce edge curling and keep the rug in place on slick floors. In spaces with radiant floor heating, ensure your rug has a compatible backing to avoid any warp or heat-related issues. The manufacturer notes a cotton backing for stability, which typically handles normal household heat and humidity fairly well.
