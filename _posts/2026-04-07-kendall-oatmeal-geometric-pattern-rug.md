## Durability and care: clean living, soft landings
Durability in a rug is a bit like a stealth mission: you want it to be quiet, effective, and low-maintenance. Kendall’s synthetic fibers help resist common stains and wear, making it a practical pick for family rooms and dens where life happens at full volume. The low pile means vacuuming is quick, and shedding is minimal compared to high-pile shag adventures you might regret at 3 am during a binge-watching spree.

Care is straightforward:
- Vacuum regularly with a standard rug attachment to pull loose fibers upward rather than down into the backing.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth to prevent staining.
- For spot cleaning, use a mild detergent diluted with water and test on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the fibers or alter the color balance over time.

In the long haul, Kendall ages gracefully. It won’t claim to be antique, and it won’t impress your grandmother with heirloom vibes, but it will endure the daily routines of a busy home without complaining. If you are chasing a rug that travels well between a living room and a playroom, Kendall is the steady courier you want carrying your floors through the chaos.

### Maintenance myth vs reality
A lot of rug care myths involve overzealous vacuuming or shaming a rug for showing wear. Kendall answers with honest practicality: a quick weekly vacuum, prompt spill care, and a modest rotation every few months to prevent uneven wear. If you have pets with dramatic shedding, a lint roller might become your best friend, but a simple vacuum can do the heavy lifting too. The key is consistency, not heroics.
