## Overview

City of Mist has always been a game about myths wearing trench coats and ordinary people falling into legendary chaos. The Crime Wave is a PDF adventure that slides into that vibe with the grace of a shadow slipping through a neon alley. It promises a self-contained arc, a cast of memorable masks, and just enough melodrama to feel like you are starring in a film that never quite finishes its drink. If you have a group that enjoys noir tropes, morally gray choices, and a city that feels like a character with a caffeine addiction, this pdf is probably going to become your new favorite “one shot that lingers.” For newcomers, fear not: the document contains a quick-start section and clear guidance to get you rolling before the rain even starts to patter on the windowpanes.

The Crime Wave is not a cynical cash grab dressed as a detective novel. It respects the core City of Mist system while adding enough new flourishes to make a session feel fresh. It is, at its best, a well-paced caper that uses the game’s signature blend of myth, memory, and metropolitan mystery to create moments that feel cinematic without tipping into cinematic inertia. It is a product that understands that players come to City of Mist not to solve a puzzle in a vacuum but to feel like they are propelling a myth through a living, breathing city. If you want a campaign that can sit on your shelf as a reliable go-to, or a one-shot that carries the mood of a late-night heist through to the dawn, this pdf has you covered.

To begin, here is a quick map of what you get and how you might tailor it to your table. The Crime Wave comes with a curated sequence of acts that thread through a central mystery while letting the GM drop in side encounters that feel organic rather than tacked on. The tone sits comfortably between gritty noir and stylish mythic fantasy, so players can lean into drama or wink at the absurd without feeling pulled in two directions. The balance is one of the book’s strengths: it trusts the players to improvise around the scaffolding of the adventure, rather than forcing a fixed path that pins you down like a suspect in an interrogation room.

If you are the kind of GM who loves a robust obstacle course for your players to sprint through, you will enjoy the way the Crime Wave scales. It can be run at a brisk pace in a single session, or expanded into a longer arc if your group wants to weave it into an ongoing city-wide storyline. The included NPCs, factions, and plot hooks are crafted with enough texture to feel real, not like a set dressing of clichés. The city is alive in the text, and that energy translates into the session once you start rolling the dice.

The Crime Wave respects that foundation and provides an accessible entry point if your table is still getting used to masks, logos, and the mythos toolkit.

### GM Tips and Play Experience

Running this adventure is as much about mood as it is about mechanics. The Crime Wave includes practical tips for establishing tone, pacing, and safety boundaries. The recommended pacing is well-calibrated: the book gives you space to breathe between major scenes and enough adrenaline to keep players engaged without jolting them awake with a dozen forced cliffhangers.

A few highlights:
- Use setting as a character: the citys environment should feel like an ally to or an adversary against the players, reinforcing themes rather than serving as a backdrop.
- Embrace the mythic undercurrent: moments where myths intrude on the mundane offer great dramatic payoff when executed with restraint.
- Prepare a couple of flexible anchors: if players go off-script, you want to have easy-to-adapt threads that can keep the story coherent without forcing a detour that derails the arc.
- Respect safety and consent: there are notes on handling sensitive content that remind you to check in with players and steer toward a fun, inclusive experience.
- Accessibility matters: alt-text on images, clear scene descriptions, and navigable layout help players with different needs enjoy the material.
