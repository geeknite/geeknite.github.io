### Care, Cleaning, and Keeping It Fresh

The care routine for a wool rug isn’t intense, but it does require a rhythm. Here are practical steps to keep Cliffwood green looking sharp:

- Vacuum regularly, using a suction setting that’s gentle on the pile. A beater bar can pull fibers if you’re not careful, so consider a slightly reduced setting or a non-beater attachment for daily care.
- Address spills quickly. Blot with clean cloth—avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fiber and set a stain. If something stubborn lands on the rug, test a small area with a mild solution (a tiny amount of dish soap in water) to see how the color holds.
- Rotate periodically. Wool rugs can fade with sun exposure, so give the rug a little rotation every few months to keep wear even and avoid high-traffic cliffs forming in one corner.
- Spot clean with appropriate wool-safe cleaners. Avoid chlorine-based products on wool; opt for pH-balanced cleaners or water-based solutions designed for natural fibers.
- Consider professional cleaning every few years, depending on usage. A clean rug remains more comfortable underfoot and maintains color richness better than a perpetually dusty one.

The big picture here is that you don’t have to become a rug care expert to keep Cliffwood looking good. You do want a basic plan and some mindful habits—particularly about spills and sun exposure. Wool is naturally resilient, but it isn’t invincible, and a little proactive care goes a long way toward keeping that green glow alive across seasons.
