## Painting and display tips
If Kopinski’s style has your hobby heartbeat accelerating, here are practical pointers to translate that look onto your minis without turning your desk into a tiny planet of stress:
- Use high-contrast base coats to emphasize dramatic lighting and establish a focal point on each miniature.
- Limit your palette to two or three dominant colors with one or two accent tones to echo Kopinski’s restrained but expressive approach.
- Edge-highlight aggressively on armor and weapons to achieve crisp painterly outlines that pop on a dim tabletop.
- Layer with washes and selective glazes to create deep shadows and dramatic midtones reminiscent of Kopinski’s lighting schemes.
- Consider urban ruin basing: cracked concrete, ash, broken signage to echo the box art and the environment.
- Build a small diorama vignette for display—e.g., a survivor scouting a ruined storefront—and rotate the display to refresh the table atmosphere.
- Use non-toxic varnishes with a satin finish to preserve painterly look without making minis overly plastic-satiny.
- Document your process and share progress with the community; Kopinski’s influence shines brightest when you reveal your step-by-step journey.

### Beyond painting: display, photography, and streaming tips
