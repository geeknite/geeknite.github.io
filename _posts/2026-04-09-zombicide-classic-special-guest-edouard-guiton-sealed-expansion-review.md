## Gameplay and balance

Zombicide is a game about planning, improvisation, and dice luck. Adding a sealed expansion with a guest designer can tilt the balance in interesting ways. Here’s how the Edouard Guiton infusion tends to shape the game experience:

- The new survivors typically bring quirky abilities that encourage team coordination. They might offer surprising ways to trigger synergy with the new weapons or environments, rewarding players who coordinate objectives and timing.
- The new weapons and items provide alternative routes to victory. They can be the difference between a heroic splash and a grim, brain-splattering failure. The risk is that some items can feel either overpowered or underused depending on the mission’s layout and the zombie spawn pattern.
- The encounter deck introduces new tempo shifts. You’ll sometimes get a lull between waves and other times you’ll be hit with a sudden swarm that makes you question every decision you’ve ever made about line of sight and melee vs ranged.

The beauty of Zombicide is that it rewards clever play, not just good luck. The challenge is to make sure the new rules support that principle rather than undermine it.
