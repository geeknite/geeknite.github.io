## Welcome, Neon Sleuths: Fortune Row Arrives
City of Mist lets players stride into the neon-lit fog and pretend to be gods walking among mortals. Fortune Row keeps that spirit but adds new layers of mischief, micro mysteries, and social tangles. Instead of a radical overhaul, the expansion fits into the existing rhythm like a new rooftop party on the same city block. The result is a fresh toolbox that helps a GM craft sessions that feel cinematic, dangerous, and a little ridiculous in all the right ways. If you have already fallen for the base game, Fortune Row gives you more to chase, more masks to wear, and more reasons to lean into your groups storytelling chops.

Fortune Row centers on a new locus in the city, called Fortune Row. This is a district of secrets where every street corner could whisper a clue or threaten to swallow a character in a moral dilemma. The Fortune Row expansion introduces a new deck of fortune cards. Each card carries a scene seed and a twist that can push a session toward drama, characterization, or pure chaotic fun. There are new location cards that map this district into your campaign. The GM can drop these into a session to quickly generate a micro narrative, or weave them into a longer arc for a more integrated campaign.

A quick word about the look and feel. The art direction stays true to the core game. You will still see moody color palettes, action lines, and a sense of motion that makes it easy to visualize a fight, chase, or interrogation. The new art threads into the same style so you never feel like you are playing in a different game world. The physical quality is solid, too. The cards are thick enough to survive table talk and coffee rings, and the map foldouts feel sturdy rather than flimsy.

Fortune Row adds lightweight but meaningful mechanical flourishes. The Fortune Deck is the star here. Draw at key moments to reveal a prompt that nudges the scene in a new direction. The prompts reward players for looking for hidden connections, for pursuing a personal stake, or for daring to negotiate a second term in a secret bargain. The Row concept emphasizes social dynamics over direct confrontation. It invites players to use talk, influence, and moral compromise to shape outcomes. That does not mean combat disappears. There are always options, but the emphasis shifts toward layered storytelling.

Fortune Row is designed for speed and style at the table. You can assemble a session in minutes using the new location cards to seed a micro-arc around Fortune Row. The GM can decide how many opponents to bring into the action, how many fortunes to use, and what the outcome of the night will reveal about the city. You do not need a PhD in noir to enjoy this expansion, but a taste for moral ambiguity helps.

### Components
Fortune Row adds a deck of fortune cards, a handful of location cards for Fortune Row, a few antagonists, and a set of tokens that track the flow of influence at the row. The tokens are intuitive, and the cards have clear icons that reduce table chatter when the heat goes up. The design is friendly to beginners while still providing teeth for veteran players.

### Masks and Mythos
Fortune Row expands the toolkit for character options without forcing a full rebuild. If you have wanted a more social, intrigue-driven tilt, Fortune Row delivers. The new Masks and Mythos lines slot into the existing framework, letting you pursue a fresh arc type without derailing your long-running story. The goal is to give players new levers to pull without breaking the vibe of the base game.

### Running a Session
Fortune Row tends to slow the initial pace as players learn the rhythm, then accelerates once the deck and rows settle in. The GM benefits from crisp quick-start rules that guide how to weave Fortune Cards into the ongoing mystery arc. The players get more opportunities for personal moments, negotiating deals, and moral choice. If you are a GM who loves planning social scenes, you will feel right at home.

### Narrative Play: Hooks and Micro-Arcs
The expansion ships with a handful of hooks designed to slot into existing campaigns. The Fortune Market offers deals whispered after midnight. The Lost Fortune hints at a hidden lineage for one of the Masks. The Night of Rain and Neon triggers visions that align clues across Fortune Row. Use these as anchors for a session or as seeds for a longer arc. The hooks are intentionally modular to support both one-shot nights and longer campaigns.

### Thematic Fit and Production Quality
Thematic alignment remains faithful to neon noir and mythic city drama. The expansion respects the base game tone while empowering players with an extra layer of social puzzle solving. The art direction continues to deliver mood with clarity, which is essential for both GM planning and players following the story beat. The card stock, layout, and packaging hold up to regular table use, coffee rings, and the occasional snack crumb.

### Balance and Table Experience
Fortune Row is balanced to be add-on rather than overpowering. The new gear and methods are potent but rely on social leverage and narrative momentum rather than raw dice power. Table talk and roleplay shine here, which is a plus for groups that enjoy creamy dialogue and sharp character moments. If your group tends to favor consistency and speed, start with a smaller Fortune Deck load and gradually add cards as players warm up to the new tempo.

### Getting the Most from Fortune Row
To maximize value, start with a focused arc that uses a handful of Fortune Row location cards and a couple of Fortune Cards per session. Let the deck introduce twists that require collaboration or tough choices. Use the new antagonists as needed to spike tension; avoid too many simultaneous threats if your table is still learning the rhythm. The key is to keep the session feeling cinematic while ensuring everyone gets a chance to shine in a way that respects the group balance.

### Final Word
Fortune Row is a well-crafted extension that respects the City of Mist framework while offering a distinct mode of play centered on social intrigue, mystery, and moral complexity. It is particularly appealing to groups that enjoy heavy character moments and the sense that the city itself is an unpredictable partner in the investigation. If you crave richer interactions, sharper plotting, and a dash of fortune driven drama, Fortune Row will deliver the goods.
