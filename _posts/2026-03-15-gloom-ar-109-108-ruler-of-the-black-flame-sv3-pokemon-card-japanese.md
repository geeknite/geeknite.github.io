## Print Quality and Japanese NM Condition
If you’re chasing that “minty glacier” look, Japanese NM is often your best bet—the print quality tends to have less whitening and a tighter overall centering than the wobblier corners of some English-language editions. For this particular card, you’ll want to check:
- Centering: Look for almost-perfect symmetry; even a tiny shift can affect the overall aesthetic. The SV3 cascade of foil should kiss the card’s border with minimal light bleed.
- Corners: NM copies should show no rounding or chipping—just the crisp, square edges that remind you you’re dealing with a card that’s meant to be looked at as much as played.
- Surface: The AR foil coat should be even, with a uniform shimmer. Any fingerprint or micro-scratches can drop the card’s grade dramatically in the eyes of purists.
- Print lines: In many cases, ARs have subtle print lines that appear as the card warps in processing. If these lines are faint and the card remains visually stunning, the card still holds a strong display value.

In a Japan-only NM print, the color balance often leans toward a gentler saturation with a refined gloss, making the green pop in a way that’s tactile rather than flashy. ## Display, Care, and Storage Tips
As a Japanese NM AR, this Gloom wants to be displayed, not abused by a rogue bottle of cola. Here are practical tips to keep your gem in prime condition:
- Use a proper storage binder with acid-free sleeves and archival quality pages. Avoid sleeves that trap humidity or have UV-reactive properties.
- Consider top-loader protection for ultra-trim copies and glass-tlab matrices to guard edges softly without stressing the foil.
- Store away from direct sunlight, heat, and moisture. The foil can shift with temperature fluctuations, and nobody needs a shiny misalignment in a moment of purely aesthetic panic.
- When displaying, use a stand or a minimal light source to highlight the AR’s reflective properties without creating color-bounce that hides the details.
- Handling: wash hands, avoid lotion residues, and keep fingerprints off the surface. A microfiber cloth can be used gently to remove dust.
