## Painting, assembly, and table setup tips
If you’re painting or assembling these minis, here are a few practical tips to streamline your process:
- Pre-clean the minis with warm soapy water to remove any mold release residue. This helps paints adhere and reduces tackiness later.
- Dry-fit the pieces first. The special guest sculpts often have slightly different hand positions or weapon mounts; testing the fit before glue saves a lot of post-paint fiddling.
- Use a consistent primer shade across your base colors so the guest pieces don’t visually clash with your existing stock. A neutral gray or white primer usually works best for 28–32 mm scale.
- Consider contrast paints or washes for quick tabletop-ready results. This is especially practical if you’d like to keep the table moving during a campaign night rather than investing days in shading every keystone character.

Table setup-wise, the new tokens and cards will likely live near the command area of your board. Since the cards can influence turn order and ability timing, you’ll want a small, dedicated zone for the guest box components. A tidy mat that separates base game components from the expansion tweaks helps keep the session on track and minimizes confusion when new effects trigger mid-round.
