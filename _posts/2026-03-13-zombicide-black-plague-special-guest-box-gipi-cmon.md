## Painting, assembly, and table setup tips
If you’re painting or assembling these minis, here are some practical tips to streamline your workflow:
- Pre-clean the minis with warm soapy water to remove mold release residue. Paint adheres better and you’ll thank yourself later.
- Dry-fit the pieces first. The special guest sculpts often have slightly different hand positions or weapon mounts; testing the fit before glue saves post-paint fiddling.
- Use a consistent primer shade across your base colors so the guest pieces don’t clash with your existing stock. A neutral gray or white primer usually works best for 28–32 mm scale.
- Consider contrast paints or washes for quick tabletop-ready results. If you’re short on hobby time, you can still achieve a strong tabletop look without turning night into a painting marathon.

Table setup-wise, the new tokens and cards will likely live near the command area of your board. Since these components can influence turn order and timing, dedicate a tidy zone for the guest box pieces. A mat or tray that separates base game components from expansion tweaks helps keep the session on track and minimizes chaos when new effects trigger mid-round.
