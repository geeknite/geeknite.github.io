## Painting and assembly tips

Painting Audrey and Amy can be a joy or a tiny challenge, depending on your experience. Here are practical tips to get the best-looking results without turning it into a full-time hobby:

- Prep first: Remove any flash, clean mold lines, and rinse the parts. Dry before assembly so you don’t have to fight glue on wet plastic.
- Prime with a light gray or tan primer. The neutral base helps both warm library tones and cool lab tones pop without needing heavy layering.
- Audrey’s cardigan: Use a mid-to-dark brown as a base, followed by a few light highlights on the knit texture. Dry-brushing a beige or buff color over the cardigan edges can evoke a slightly worn look without washing out the detail.
- Book color: Pick a strong contrast color for the cover—deep red, royal blue, or emerald green work well against the neutral tones of Audrey’s outfit. Apply a thin wash to bring out the textural detail on the cover.
- Amy’s lab gear: For the lab coat, keep it clean and bright white with subtle gray shading in the folds. The goggles, clipboards, and any glassware benefit from a crisp highlight to read as glass or polished metal.
- Bases: The base textures are a fun place to be creative. A couple of dry-brushed grays or browns can create a sepia-toned library feel, while adding tiny specks of green or blue to simulate spilled chemical residues on Amy’s base can give a cinematic vibe.
- Washes and weathering: Light washes in brown or sepia across Audrey’s cardigan will emphasize the fabric texture; a blue-gray wash on Amy’s coat adds a cold, clinical atmosphere.
- Finishing: A matte varnish helps reduce unwanted glare during play, preserving detail for photos and display alike. If you want to highlight the book edges, a tiny dry brush of bone color will catch the light without overpowering the rest of the miniature.
