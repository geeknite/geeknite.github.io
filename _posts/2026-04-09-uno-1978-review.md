## Preservation: How to Keep Your 1978 UNO Deck Looking Legendary
Preservation is part science, part art. The simplest advice is to store the box in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. UV light can fade the bold 70s color palette faster than you can say UNO and may cause the orange and yellow hues to drift toward a sepia-tinted memory of the good old days. If you want to take it to the next level, use a storage box that protects the corners and keeps humidity in check. You can also consider climate-controlled storage or a dedicated display case for your favorite retro pieces. If you want to display the box, keep it in a position that minimizes direct sunlight and avoids humidity swings that could warp the cardboard. 

The interior cards should be checked periodically for any signs of warping, slight curling, or edge wear. If the cards show no deformation and the numbers are clearly visible, you’re in a good place. If you find slight bending, avoid attempting to flatten them aggressively; instead, store them flat and allow natural restoration to occur, or consult a professional conservator who specializes in vintage card stock restoration. Remember, the goal is to strike a balance between playability and authenticity. You don’t want to remove the charm of a well-loved deck by turning it into a fragile exhibit, nor do you want it to crumble into dust the moment you consider a casual game with friends.
