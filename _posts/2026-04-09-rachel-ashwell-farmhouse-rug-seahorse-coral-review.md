## Color, care, and keeping it fresh

Coastal color stories thrive with light and air, and Seahorse Coral does its job by reflecting that philosophy. The coral hue responds well to natural light, brightens up darker corners, and plays nicely with soft blues, whites, and warm woods. It’s not a color tantrum; it’s a color conversation starter that pairs with a range of palettes.

Caring for the rug is pretty straightforward: regular vacuuming on a gentle setting to remove surface dust, quick spot treatment for small spills with a mild detergent, and an occasional rotation to ensure even wear. If you have a spill from something prone to staining (think coffee or red wine in a dramatic fashion), treat it promptly with a mild cleaning solution and blot rather than rub to avoid pushing the stain deeper into the fibers. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fibers and dull the color. In short, treat it like a well-loved cardigan: gentle handling, occasional spa day, and no hot iron contact.

- Vacuum with suction control set to low; avoid beater bars on delicate fibers
- Blot spills quickly with a clean cloth rather than rubbing
- Use a damp cloth for light spot cleaning and a mild detergent if needed
- Consider a rug pad for grip and to protect underfoot comfort
