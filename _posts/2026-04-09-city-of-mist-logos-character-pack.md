## What’s in the Logos Character Pack
The Logos Character Pack is pitched as a toolbox meant for the GM to sprinkle into existing campaigns and for players who want a few extra masks, logos, and dramatic beats. The core idea is to give you ready-to-run character templates that pull from the Logos mythos — those grand ideas that power a city through lore and iconography rather than brute force. The contents are presented in a thematic, modular fashion so you can drop in a template without rewriting your entire arc. Below we break down the main buckets you’ll encounter.

### 1) Character Templates and Logos Packs
The pack ships with several ready-to-use character templates that carry a strong logo theme. Think of a handful of archetypes reinterpreted as masked identities with personal logos that reflect their public persona and private motive. These are not your standard classes; they are mythic motifs that you can plug into your sessions as either NPCs or backgrounds that players can eventually inhabit. Each template comes with:

- A concise backstory that ties into the city’s myths and the logo’s symbolism
- A suggested Mask and Power set aligned with the Core Rules of City of Mist
- A few quick-lute stat blocks that GM tools can adapt on the fly
- A few inspirational prompts to push scenes beyond the clich joyride

The benefit here is speed. If your table wants something more than a random encounter, these templates give you a narrative spine. They also serve as excellent seed ideas for a one-shot or a longer arc that leans into the mythic vibe rather than the street-level grind. If you have ever struggled to turn a cool logo into a compelling reason for a character to exist in your campaign, the templates in this pack aim to solve that puzzle with a neat little bow.

### 2) Portraits and Artwork
Artwork matters in City of Mist not because it makes you a better GM but because it helps players visualize the myths that drive the city. The Logos Character Pack provides a curated set of portraits and logo-inspired artwork designed to pair with the templates. Expect bold lines, noir color palettes, and a few pieces that look like they could be used as a vintage street poster while remaining firmly in the 21st century. The style choice is consistent with the game’s aesthetic: a blend of detective fiction and mythic symbolism, where every logo is a character in its own right. For a GM running in person, the portraits are perfect for NPC handouts or the in-game character sheet where a logo doubles as a symbol of a faction’s influence.

In digital campaigns, these assets transform into a quick visual reference that you can drop into a map view or a character portrait carousel without pulling your hair out trying to crop and format existing assets. The artwork also doubles as potential inspiration for scenes, as a logo often hints at a backstory you can turn into a scene hook. If you like the noir vibe with a splash of myth, you’ll probably want to print a few portraits for your table or pin them behind the screen for easy reference.

### 3) Logos Mechanics and Narration Templates
The Logos Pack leans into the central concept of logos as ideas that become people. To that end, it offers narration templates that help you convert abstract logos into actionable on-table moments. These sections aren’t just flavor text; they provide guidance on when and how to reveal a logo, how it interacts with mythic identity in play, and how a logo might empower a scene without turning your table into a power-syphon buffet. The mechanics templates suggest prompts for skill checks, scene transitions, and entanglements that feel meaningful rather than arbitrary. The aim is to keep the momentum moving while letting players discover what the logo stands for in the context of the story you are telling.

To avoid the dreaded, we-are-just-trying-to-fill-players’-sheets problem, the pack includes a handful of quick-start scenarios that revolve around logo-themed mysteries. These hooks are designed to be dropped into an ongoing campaign or used as the backbone of a one-shot. Expect ideas that revolve around public perception, propaganda, and the tension between a logo’s mythic identity and a character’s private motives. If you are a GM who loves to improvise, these hooks are particularly handy for shifting the table’s energy in unexpected ways without sacrificing the core vibe of City of Mist.

## Design and Theme: How Logos Feels on the Table
The Logos Character Pack emphasizes the core theme that logos are not just symbols; they are living ideas with power to shape the world. This duality mirrors City of Mist’s central mechanic — your persona is a mask that can become a mythic avatar when you give it power through actions in the game world. The pack embraces this duality by offering templates that highlight moment-to-moment decisions and by arming GMs with prompts to escalate scenes at the exact moments where a logo’s influence should feel inevitable.

If you are a fan of the meta-narrative vibe, you will likely enjoy how the pack invites you to lean into the symbolic language of logos in your scenes. ## Playability: Mechanics, Flow, and GM Tips
City of Mist is a game about letting myths and personas do the heavy lifting. The Logos Character Pack is designed to support that approach by providing ready-made anchors for your scenes. Here are some practical tips on getting the most from the pack during play:

- Use logos as scene triggers: When a logo appears in a scene, it should prompt players to reveal a facet of their own Logos identity. The pack’s templates provide prompts to help you do that without stalling the action.
- Pair logos with appropriate masks: A character’s Logo should push them toward a narrative arc that makes sense for their Mask and for the city. If a template’s logo doesn’t naturally extend a character’s arc, it can still be dropped in as a temporary obstacle that forces a choice.
- Leverage the visual hooks: Use the included portraits and logos to quickly establish mood and stakes. A strong image can anchor a scene and reduce the need for long verbal exposition.
- Incorporate the quick-start scenarios early in a campaign: They serve as launchpads for players to engage with the Logos concept, and they give you a framework for escalating conflict without page-flipping to find a suitable beat.
- Balance power with peril: Logos can be potent, but the pack reminds you that mythic ideas gain strength through narrative consequences. If a logo makes a character too powerful too quickly, lean into the price of power and the cost of maintaining the mask.

### Thematic Hooks: How to Use Each Template in a Session
Each template is designed with a narrative hook in mind. In practice, you can drop one into a scene as a non-player catalyst or invite a player to take the Mask for their own Logo’s ride. The pack’s flexibility shines when you adapt the hook to the mood of your group and the tone of your current arc.

## How to Integrate into Your Campaign
Getting the most from this pack is about integration rather than replacement. Here is a simple workflow to incorporate the Logos Character Pack into your next session:

1) Pick a template that fits the current arc or a scene you want to elevate. It could be a protagonist or an NPC who embodies a logo’s theme.
2) Read the backstory and the suggested actions for the logo. Identify at least two moments in your planned scene where the logo’s symbolism can break through the surface.
3) Introduce a logo early in the scene as a visual cue. Let it hint at a motive or reveal a consequence that the players must navigate.
4) Allow players to interact with the logo through their Masks. The Masks should provide a doorway for player choice and character growth, not just a power boost.
5) Use the quick-start scenarios as a tabletop sandbox for experimentation. They are great to test on a night you want to run something punchy and self-contained.
6) After the session, reflect on which logos resonated with your players. Layer those ideas into future sessions to build a connected arc rather than a collection of random encounters.
