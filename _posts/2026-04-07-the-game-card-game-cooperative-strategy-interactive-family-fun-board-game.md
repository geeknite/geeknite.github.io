## How to Play
### Setup
Shuffle the deck and deal cards to players. Each player gets a hand that will determine your fate for the next 15 to 20 minutes. The table is set, snacks are quarantined from wandering fingertips, and you prepare to coordinate like a squad of caffeinated squirrels who somehow found a whiteboard.

### Turn Structure
On your turn you assess the current state of the board, discuss options with your teammates, and choose a move that will nudge the group toward the objective. The tension doesn’t come from suspicion but from misalignment when two teammates both want to do something different and the third nods sagely like a movie director who knows nothing about the plan but everything about momentum.

### Endgame and Win Conditions
The win condition is typically to complete a set of tasks before the deck becomes unmanageable. If you flip too many cards at once you might trigger a cascade that sends the table into groans and frantic snack retrieval. If you pull off a perfectly coordinated move, the table erupts with laughter, relief, and that oddly touching moment when everyone realizes they actually enjoy being a nonviolent disaster together.

## Strategy and Tips
### Early decisions
Establish a shared mental model early on. Decide who will communicate clearly, who acts as plan B, and who quietly gathers snacks without violence. A well aligned team reduces the risk of random despair and increases the chances of triumphant chaos.

### Card management
Keep track of which cards have been played and which are still in play. Move with stealth and purpose, like a well dressed raid on the snack cabinet rather than a curious toddler on a sugar rush. Remember that some cards hammer the plan more than others, so prioritize what actually helps the group move forward.

### Cooperation moments
Turn every tough choice into a discussion that stays on track. If someone starts to panic, remind them that panic is contagious but cool logic is also contagious. Slow down, breathe, and calibrate. The beauty of this game sits in the calm decisions that arrive just before the clock hits zero.
