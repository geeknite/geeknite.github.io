## Practical care: keeping your vintage Gloom pristine (because nostalgia loves a careful owner)
The best way to preserve a 1996 print is not to treat it like a museum piece but to treat it like a cherished artifact that deserves care. Here are a few pragmatic tips to keep your Gloom LV.21 No.044 looking as good as the day you found it (well, sort of):
- Slab or sleeve: store it in a high-quality penny sleeve inside a rigid top-loader. For collectors who enjoy the “white glove” experience, consider a hard acrylic case with UV protection to minimize sunlight yellowing and surface crazing.
- Humidity control: avoid damp basements. The 1996 printer’s inks aren’t made to survive moldy wallpaper or damp glove compartments. Keep humidity around 40–50% to minimize warping and edge wear.
- Handling: wash your hands, dry them, and handle the card by the edges. It sounds obvious, but it’s the best way to preserve that Gloom smile without turning it into a smoky, fingerprinted memory.
- Cleaning: if there are fingerprints, use a very light, dry microfiber cloth. Do not apply water, cleaners, or magic spells—these are not press-on_laser-coded cards, and you don’t want to strip the print or-le thickness.
- Storage strategy: stack sleeves with a consistent orientation and avoid bending the corners. If possible, store in a binder with pages designed for single cards rather than oversized page pockets that can catch on the corners.
