## Durability and Care: Treat It Like a Cute, Nervous Tech Gadget

### Cleaning and Maintenance
Spills deserve quick attention, and wool rugs love a fast blot rather than a dramatic narrative. Blot with a clean towel—press, don’t rub—to prevent pigment migration. For deeper cleaning, a mild wool-safe cleaner or professional cleaning every couple of years will help keep dyes vibrant and fibers buoyant. Vacuum regularly with a low-beater setting and methodical passes. Wool is less prone to static, but avoid aggressive agitation that could loosen fibers.

### Pet and Kid Considerations
If you’ve got pets, wool fibers tend to resist staining better than many synthetics and are less likely to trap odors when cared for promptly. Kids will spill juice and crayons; seafoam helps mask the evidence, which is a practical superpower in any home. Consider a stain protection spray for extra peace of mind if you’re dealing with a high-traffic household that includes arts and crafts day, snack-barrage weekends, and spontaneous dance-offs.
