## Feel underfoot: comfort, durability, and practicalities
Underfoot, you’ll notice the cushiony bite that makes you want to wander barefoot from couch to coffee table with a contented sigh. Because it’s a viscose-wool blend, it’s softer than a pure wool rug but more forgiving than a pure viscose rug (which can be a little too glossy and delicate for high-traffic zones). Here’s what to expect:
- Softness: the wool provides a natural plushness, while viscose adds a satin-like glaze that’s delightful on bare feet but breathable enough for year-round use.
- Pile height: typically medium to mid-high, which translates to a generous give underfoot but also means you’ll want a decent underlay to keep the rug from creeping around the room during enthusiastic chair swivels.
- Durability: wool brings resilience; viscose is more prone to showing wear in high-traffic spots or on a wildly enthusiastic toddler’s footprint collection. With normal household use and sensible care, you’ll see long-term beauty without obvious flattening in the main paths.

If you’re worried about “flattening,” don’t panic. Rotate furniture periodically to distribute wear and use a high-quality underlay to keep the fibers aligned. For homes with pets, you can expect a few tip-tap stops rather than full-on rippings; the blend handles fur and small accidents better than pure viscose, though you’ll still want to address spills promptly with blotting and appropriate cleaners.

## Cleanliness and maintenance: keeping it pristine without a spa day
Maintenance is where many premium rugs earn their keep or lose it. The Monaco Tribeca rug benefits from reasonable care routines that won’t require a chemical engineering degree:
- Vacuuming: use a suction-only vacuum (no beater bars) to minimize fiber snagging. Do not go full power with the brush; the goal is smooth extraction, not shredding. Vacuum regularly—think weekly in high-traffic spaces and biweekly in calmer rooms.
- Spills: blot promptly with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing—you’ll push liquid deeper and fluffier fibers will complain about the disturbance. For water-based spills, a damp cloth can help pull liquid away; for oil-based spills, a mild detergent solution can work, but always test on a hidden spot first.
- Cleaning: professional cleaning every 12-24 months is a good baseline for large-area high-traffic rugs. If you’re in a dust-prone area, more frequent professional refreshes can keep the sheen from dulling.
- Stain resistance: the blend is better at resisting typical household stains than pure viscose alone, but it’s not a magical shield. Treat it with respect and attend to incidents quickly.
- Sun exposure: long-term sun may fade viscose’s sheen. If your space has generous natural light, rotate the rug or use window coverings to keep the color even over the years.

In short: the rug wants to be lived on. It’s not a ceremonial tapestry that needs a spa day every week; it’s a piece of furniture that mats with your life—and that’s the charm.
