## What Fortune Row Is

Fortune Row is a physical deck of Story Cards sold as a supplement for City of Mist. Each card is a prompt that nudges the GM toward a particular narrative beat—be it a location bolt, a character hook, a mood shift, or a dramatic twist. The text on each card is compact, evocative, and crafted to spark a concrete direction for a scene. The design intention is not to replace the GM’s creative control but to give it a spark that the table can fan into a flame.

### Card types and utility

You will encounter a spectrum of prompts that lean into classic City of Mist themes: secret identities, mythic legacies, urban rituals, and the pressure points where ordinary lives collide with extraordinary secrets. In practice, Fortune Row shines when you lean into the prompts as narrative constraints rather than rules to be solved. The design encourages a GM to lean into mood, motive, and misdirection—the core elements that City of Mist has always loved to explore.

Location seeds anchor a scene in a place that carries meaning in the city. ### Character hooks
Character hooks push a backstory into the foreground. A card may remind the GM to reveal a former identity’s consequence on the current life of a hero, or to present an NPC whose own myth challenges the team’s perception of who is good, who is evil, and who gets to decide. ### Event mischief
 Event prompts signal a turning point in the night, a disruption that forces the investigators to respond rather than observe. An event card might prompt a confrontation, a sudden revelation, or a choice that tests loyalty within a team. ## Narration, Pacing, and Flow: What to Expect at the Table

Fortune Row is a flexible instrument. It fosters moments of quick inspiration, but it can also slow the pace just enough to let a scene breathe. For most groups, the ideal rhythm is to draw a card at the start of a session to set a tonal anchor, then use additional cards at natural breakpoints—end of a scene to prompt a twist, or mid-scene to escalate stakes.

GM tips for maximizing Fortune Row:

- Use draws as truth triggers, not commandments. If a card says a secret encounter occurs, you still decide what that encounter reveals about the city and its legends.
- Pair a card with a known arc beat. If your campaign is pushing toward a turning point for a particular mythic threat, use a card to reinterpret that turning point in a fresh, thematic way.
- Encourage player interpretation. If a card hints at a location or a symbol, invite players to propose how their characters perceive, misread, or reinterpret that symbol through their own mythic lenses.

Narrative pacing benefits from deliberate pauses. Fortune Row provides built-in prompts to punctuate acts with mood shifts, which helps groups that love to savor their storytelling as much as they love the dramatic reveals.

## How to Run a Fortune Row Session: A Practical Example Arc

To give you a feel for how these prompts translate into play, here is compact, example arc you can try with your table. This is not a script, merely a blueprint you can adapt with your players’ invented legends and secrets.

1) Setup and Tone: At the start of the session, draw a Fortune Row card that defines the mood. Let the team look at it and propose what mythic thread might be surfacing in tonight’s case. The card might push you to establish a memorable setting—a dimly lit arcade, for instance—where a public symbol is hiding a private truth. The party discusses what legend their characters have forged and what ordinary life is trying to guard from mythic exposure.

2) Inciting Incident: A second card is drawn to spark a turning point. It reveals a conflict in the city that intersects with the team’s mythic commitments. The GM parries with a question, asking what personal stake each hero has in this incident and what it reveals about the city’s appetite for myth.

3) Rising Action: The cards continue to seed scenes as the investigators seek clues. A location prompt leads to a critical encounter with an NPC whose loyalties are shifting. A mood prompt reminds the players to weigh preference, fear, and hope, shaping how they respond to danger.

4) Climax: A final card reframes the central mystery and asks the team to decide what myth they want to own by the end of the night. Do the heroes accept a shadowed blessing, a dangerous bargain, or a public revelation that changes how the city sees them?

5) Aftermath and Hook for Next Session: A closing prompt gives the GM a natural jump-off point for the next session, whether it is a new symbol, a new enemy, or a twist that reframes a previously solved case.

This is not a rigid template, but rather a template for pacing and mood management. Fortune Row is best used as a companion to your chosen pacing model and your table’s taste for mythic noir. If your players love courtroom drama in a mythic setting, use a mood patch to guide outbursts. If they prefer intimate, character-driven scenes, lean into the relationship hooks.
