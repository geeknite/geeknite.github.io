## Connectivity, Ports, and Setup: A Minimalist’s Dream

This is where portable monitors shine: simple, fast, and forgiving. The 16MQ70 typically supports USB-C connectivity with DisplayPort Alternate Mode and Power Delivery, meaning you can drive the monitor and charge your Gram from the same USB-C port. Some setups allow a second USB-C port for data, but many users are thrilled with a single-USB-C daisy chain. Here’s the practical setup flow:

- Connect your Gram to the 16MQ70 with a single USB-C cable. If your laptop supports PD, the monitor can draw power from the laptop or pass power through to the laptop, depending on the exact port configuration.
- In Windows (or Mac), head to Display Settings and configure as Extended or Mirror, depending on whether you want a sprawling desk or a slick keept-off-zoom session.
- If your Gram has a dock or hub, you can route a second monitor, a keyboard, and a mouse through the dock for a bigger, more comfortable workspace while traveling.

The practical benefit here is the “set-and-forget” nature. You don’t need to wrestle with drivers or multi-monitor magic tricks—Windows and macOS typically recognize the monitor instantly, with hot-plug detection on most systems. It’s the modern version of a trusty external monitor, but with a lighter footprint and fewer cables to trip over when you’re trying to present in a conference room that’s sized for a board game night.
