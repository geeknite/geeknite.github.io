## Design and Materials
### Materials
This rug is 100% cotton, a fiber that’s friendly to most skin types, easy to clean, and tolerant of the occasional coffee spill that might happen in a modern apartment. Cotton fibers are breathable, durable, and when dyed well, they hold color beautifully without the high maintenance obligations of silk or wool. Because it’s a flat weave, you’re getting a low-profile surface—perfect for apartments with low-clearance door frames or for those who prefer to walk around their space like a cautious cat rather than a grand theater usher.

### Weave and Construction
Flat weave means the warp and weft threads sit flush with each other, producing a tight, even surface. There’s no pile to brush the dust under the rug, no shaggy drama—just a crisp, durable layer between your feet and the floor. Dhurries have a long lineage in India, where artisans have honed the craft for generations. It’s a labor of love performed on looms that can look like mechanical birdsong in motion, with the weaver’s rhythm guiding every stripe and every subtle irregularity that makes each piece unique.

## Care and Maintenance
### Daily care
- Vacuum regularly using a suction-only attachment; avoid aggressive beater bars that can fray cotton fibers.
- Rotate every few months to ensure even wear and to preempt fading in sun-exposed corners.
- Use a rug pad if placing on wood or tile to reduce slipping and extend life. Pads also add a bit of cushion without turning this flat-weave into a plush disaster.

### Stains and Cleaning
Spills happen. Blot immediately with a clean cloth to remove as much liquid as possible. For minor stains, a mild detergent solution can be dabbed on (never scrubbed) and then blotted dry. For tougher jobs, test a hidden area first to verify colorfastness. Remember: cotton is forgiving but not invincible; treat it with the same care you would extend to a beloved vintage gadget.

### Seasonal and long-term care
Every six to twelve months, consider a deeper refresh—gentle hand wash or a light clean if you’re dealing with heavy foot traffic or pets. Always air-dry flat to avoid warping. If you’re storing the rug during a long sabbatical from apartment life, roll it with the pile facing inward and wrap in breathable fabric to keep dust from gathering in the weave. Pro tip: avoid plastic wrap for long-term storage; moisture can get trapped and lead to mildew.

### Rug pads and placement
A quality pad under a flat weave rug reduces slipping and adds a touch of comfort. It also helps the rug bear up better against daily traffic. For wooden floors, choose a pad with a non-slip top layer and a breathable bottom layer to prevent moisture buildup. On carpet, a thinner pad can prevent bunching while preserving the rug’s crisp edge and linear stripes.

## Practical Buying Guide
### Quality indicators
- Uniform weft and warp alignment across the weave; tiny variations are normal in handmade pieces.
- Clean, tight edge finishing; if the edges appear loose or unravel quickly, ask the seller about finishing methods.
- Weave density and consistency; a denser weave generally indicates durability and better color retention.

### Sizing decisions
Think about traffic flow, furniture scale, and the room’s dimensions. In a small space, a 2x3 can frame a seating area; for a larger room, a 4x6 or 5x8 helps to unify a bigger furniture arrangement. If you’re unsure, order a slightly larger size and use rug underlay to keep it in place while you test the proportions.

### Cost versus value
Handmade cotton rugs tend to be priced higher than mass-produced equivalents, but you’re paying for craftsmanship, durability, and a piece with personality. If a price seems suspiciously low, it’s worth asking questions about the loom type, the hand-off process, and the artisans involved. If it’s unusually high, request details about the supplier’s certification of origin and ethical practices.

### Where to buy
Look for reputable sellers who provide clear dimensions, construction details, and care instructions. A trusted seller will often offer photos of the back, a description of the loom method, and a reasonable return policy.
