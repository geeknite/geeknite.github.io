## Maintenance: Cleaning, Stains, and Everyday Care

All rugs require some TLC, and the Sorbet is no exception. Here’s what to expect day-to-day:

- Vacuuming: Use a vacuum with a beater brush set to a gentle height. Don’t push the rug into high suction modes that could pull loose fibers. Regular vacuuming helps keep the plush look and reduces the risk of matting in high-traffic zones.
- Stain management: For most stains, blot, don’t rub. If you spill coffee or a red sauce, act quickly with a mild detergent solution and rinse gently. For tougher stains, consider a fabric-safe stain remover. Do a spot test on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.
- Fluffing: Over time, high-pile fibers can flatten in traffic zones. A quick fluffing with a comb-like brush or even hand-stimulating motions can restore the loft. You’ll notice the difference when the surface regains that soft, buoyant feel underfoot.
- Pet hair and fur: With shedding pets, you’ll want to run a vacuum (or a lint roller) across the surface more frequently. The fluff’s light color may show hair more easily, but the fur is easy to lift away if you stay consistent with maintenance.

For more systematic care, you can reference general rug care guides from reputable home care sources. The goal is consistent maintenance—not perfection—so you don’t turn rug ownership into a full-time hobby. A good routine is a weekly vacuum, a monthly deep clean, and a quarterly evaluation of where the rug sits—literally and metaphorically—in the room.

## Care Tips and Tricks: Pro Tips from a Sleep-Deprived Blogger

Here are a few practical dos and don’ts to keep your rug looking good and your life feeling a little less chaotic:

- Do rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and prevent obvious footpaths from forming. It also gives your cat a fresh scratch post to consider.
- Do use a quality rug pad underneath. It helps with grip, reduces bunching, and adds an extra layer of padding for comfort. If you have small children or elderly relatives, this can be a safety upgrade as well.
- Do spot-clean stains promptly. For tea, juice, or coffee spills, blot with a clean cloth and a mild detergent solution. Avoid rubbing, which pushes the stain deeper and loosens fibers.
- Don’t expose the rug to direct sunlight for extended periods. The pale shade can fade over time with prolonged sun exposure. If you must place it in a sunny area, consider rotating or using window treatments to buffer UV rays.
- Do vacuum regularly, but with care. The high pile can be picked up by strong suction, which may cause fiber damage over time. Use a gentle setting and a handheld vacuum for corners and edges.
- Don’t soak the rug to clean it. If you have a big spill, use blotting and a small amount of cleaning solution, then air-dry completely before replacing it on the floor. Moisture can lead to matting or mildew if left unattended.

For more advanced care, you can consult rug care guides from trusted sources, but remember: nothing beats common sense and a little elbow grease. Treat your Sorbet rug like a fluffy coworker who occasionally leaves coffee mugs on the desk—appreciate it, respect its space, and give it a clean, dry environment to thrive.
