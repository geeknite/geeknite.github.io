### The glazing effect and privacy trade-offs
Glazed surfaces have their lovers and their skeptics. ## Ergonomics and daily usability
Ergonomics is where the rubber meets the ergonomic mat. A 51 inch tall partition is a workable height for many office workers, but this can vary with chair height, desk depth, and the presence of monitor arms. Two main considerations stand out:

- Sightlines and posture: The opposing faces of the two stations should align in a way that reduces neck twist. You want knee clearance that actually lets you stretch your legs and not just pretend you are practicing good posture while the coffee machine decides to cough up steam at 9 a.m. You should have enough space to store a few essentials in desk drawers or under-desk organizers without feeling like you are performing a living-room-level Tetris every morning.

## Assembly, delivery, and the onboarding reality
As with most modular office ecosystems, assembly can be a mixed bag of mild excitement and mild frustration. You will likely experience four phases:

1) The unboxing carnival: cardboard confetti and a handful of screws that threaten to become a missing puzzle piece. 2-man units typically come with double the hardware, which is both a blessing and a curse. 2) The layout debate: you stand at one end and decide the orientation; the other person stands at the other end and fights for the window view. 3) The actual assembly: this usually involves an extra pair of hands, a screwdriver, and a moment where you question whether the manual is written by someone who intentionally misleads new homeowners. 4) The final check: a walk-around to ensure panels are flush, glass is clean, and there’s no wobble that would ruin your morning coffee ritual.

Key tips for smooth assembly:
- Pre-sort hardware by bag for easy access. Having a tiny parts tray is worth its weight in spare screws.
- Check your floor if you’re on a slightly uneven surface; adjustable feet can save you from a wobbly fate.
- Have a second person ready to help align the glass panels; misalignment can be dramatic, like a slow-motion montage you didn’t consent to.
- Plan cable routes before attaching panels. Glazed surfaces can reflect poor cable management in hilariously dramatic ways, especially if you enjoy late-night cable debugging sessions.

## Setup tips and layout ideas
Here are some practical tips to get the most out of this configuration:
- Position the two workstations back-to-back or face-to-face depending on your team’s collaboration style. If your team shifts between deep work and intense brainstorming, the mirrored layout helps preserve both private focus and dynamic idea exchange.
