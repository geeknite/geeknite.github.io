Performance and Maintenance

Braided polypropylene is forgiving, with straightforward cleaning. The Burgundy-Black colorway hides everyday wear and minor spills better than a pale beige, but you’ll still want to address accidents quickly to avoid set-in stains. Blot spills with a clean towel, avoid rubbing (which can push stains further into the braid), and use a mild detergent solution for spot cleaning. For larger stains or more thorough cleaning, a gentle wash per manufacturer guidelines is usually recommended—always test on a small hidden area first. Vacuuming is typically safe, but do so with care to avoid snagging the braids on moving vacuum brushes.

Regarding colorfastness, if you’re worried about the red tones bleeding onto light socks on day one, rest assured that reputable braided rugs in this palette are designed to resist color transfer with regular use. However, if you have indoor pets that like to hover their paws near the rug’s edges or a spill-prone kitchen, you might want to be a tad proactive with a quick wipe-down after meals.
