What is Exploding Kittens Party Pack?
The Party Pack is a playful extension to the base Exploding Kittens game designed to scale up to bigger groups and to lean into party style play. The core idea remains unchanged: you want to avoid drawing an exploding kitten card while using other cards to defuse, bypass, or sabotage. The Party Pack adds a batch of new cards with fresh effects, new art, and memes that feel right at home in any meme collector’s collection. The intention behind the Party Pack is to keep the core tension of risk and chance intact, while injecting extra social dynamics that make it easier for a larger crowd to participate, yell, and laugh without getting bogged down in complex rules.

In practice, this means longer evenings where you can pass a hand around faster, swap cards, and create moments that become inside jokes for your group. It’s not about adding deep strategy; it is about flexibility, quick decisions, and an energy that suits a party environment. If your group loves party games with a light touch, sharp humor, and a friendly sense of chaos, you will likely feel right at home with the Party Pack.

How to play the Party Pack
If you already know Exploding Kittens, you will recognize the core flow. Draw cards, attempt to avoid exploding, and use defuse cards to survive. The Party Pack adds a few new mechanics and optional modes that fit larger tables or a more collaborative vibe.

- Shuffle the new cards into the base deck or use the party rules to run a standalone round with just the Party Pack cards
- On a player’s turn, you can draw a card or use a special action card that may force other players to swap hands, peek at cards, or draw additional cards
- Exploding kittens remain the ultimate danger; avoid them or rely on a well placed defuse card to cheat death
- The party rules encourage faster turns and can allow multiple players to act in a single round, which is ideal when you have a long table and a short attention span

House rules and variants
What makes this pack feel fresh is not just the new cards but the possibility to tailor the experience to your group. Here are a few house rules you might try:
### Quick start variant
Each round lasts only a few seconds per player, dramatically increasing the pace and laughter
### Meme escalation
Every time a meme card is played, the group must recite a short joke or pun before the next action is resolved
### Defuse free for all
Instead of saving a single player, a defuse card may be used to save the entire table for a chaotic moment
### No peeking
A safety rule that prevents players from looking at other players hands, which can keep the social bluffing intact

Strategy and social dynamics
In party mode, the social dimension matters more than anything else. The right mix of risk and banter will crash the orthodox approach and create a vibe that is equal parts competition and stand up comedy. Here are a few practical tips:
- Observe the room: recognize who is likely to overreact to a meme card and lean into it with dramatic timing
- Use new cards to manipulate the pace: if you fear a late round, a meme or swap card might accelerate the game to reduce the chance of a perfect hand winning
- Bluff convincingly: pretend you are resetting your hand when you are actually saving a compensating card for a moment when someone else blunders

Player count and time
The Party Pack shines with groups larger than four. It scales well to six to eight players with the party rules, and it can handle bigger tables if you use a stand-alone variant. Expect each round to run anywhere from 5 to 15 minutes depending on the group and the variant you are using. In a live game night, that means you can run a solid two to three rounds per hour and still have energy left for snacks and dancing cats.
