The dual mounting approach is one of the panel s standout features. Clamp-on is quick, clean, and reversible. It s ideal for renters or folks who move desks around every few months. Installation Guide: Step by Step
--------------------------------

The actual installation is where the dual mounting shines. You have two paths, and both are friendly to a weekend warrior who wants results without chaos.

1. Prepare your desk space and confirm the available edge material. You ll need a clean surface, a couple of hand tools, and a willingness to measure twice and drill once if you choose the screw-on route.
2. Decide between clamp-on and screw-on. If you re in a rental or shared space, clamp-on is the safer choice. If you re setting up a semi-permanent fortress for screen privacy, screw-on adds a little extra stability.
3. Install the clamps. The clamps slide onto the desk edge and tighten with a simple screw mechanism. You should be able to snug them without slippage, and you should test by gently tugging on the panel to ensure it doesn t wobble.
4. Attach the panel. Align the panel with the desk edge and secure using the chosen mounting method. If you re using screws, avoid overtightening; you don t want to crack the desk top or warp the panel. A gentle but firm mindset works best here.
5. Cable management and final tweaks. Tuck cords away and consider a small cable clip at the bottom so the panel doesn t crowd your keyboard cable. The result is a clean, professional look that stays out of your way during actual work.

Installation Tips and Tricks
----------------------------

- Before you start, measure your desk edge to ensure you have the clearance for the panel height and clamps. A few millimeters can make the difference between a snug fit and a wobble town scenario.
- If you re using clamps, check the edge material. Some veneer or laminate edges are easier to clamp than others. A thin protective pad between the clamp and the desk can help avoid scuffs.
- When using screws, don t over-tighten. You only need enough tension to hold the panel firmly without distorting the desk surface.
- If your desk has a cable tray or legs with obstructions, test the panel s path first with a dry-run placement. This ensures there are no surprising snag points.
- For longer-term stability, consider pairing the panel with a desk mat that helps distribute weight and reduce desk movement during vigorous typing sessions.
