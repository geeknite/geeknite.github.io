### Tips and Tricks for Getting the Most Out of Your Table

- Practice two classic shots first: a quick pull and a fan pass. These are the bread and butter of most matches, allowing you to build consistent patterns.
- Keep the rods clean and lubricate lightly if you notice any sticking points. Smooth rods equal faster reactions.
- Use leg levelers to create a consistent plane for the ball to travel. It’s not cheating if you do it right.
- Teach kids to respect the table: no standing on chairs, avoid slamming the ball, and remind teenagers that the scoreboard is not a weapon.
- Create a family rotation schedule to keep the fun sustainable; foosball is a sport that rewards consistency and friendly trash talk in equal measure.
