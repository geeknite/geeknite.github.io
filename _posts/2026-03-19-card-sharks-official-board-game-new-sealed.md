## How Card Sharks actually plays

If you’ve seen the show, you know the core vibe: bluff, guess, and ride the numbers. If you haven’t, imagine a tension spiral with cards instead of suits and a host who asks you to justify your life choices in the form of a bet. The board game translation captures that by giving players (usually two teams) a sequence of cards and a demand to guess whether the next card will be higher or lower than a shown value. Each correct guess earns you points; a wrong one can end your turn, adding a neat risk/reward dynamic that basically screams: “We’re playing a game about probability with theatrical suspense.”

The rounds progress with a mix of simple and escalating challenges. As you gather more data from each revealed card, you’re forced to improvise on memory and nerve. It’s not about memorizing the deck; it’s about recognizing patterns and making a call that might feel silly but pays off if the stars align. The personality of the host in your imagination matters here; the board game uses the visual cues and quick scoring to approximate that showmanship, even if your actual host is your cat walking across the table mid-shuffle.


### A round-by-round flavour
- Round start: a single face-up card sets the baseline for the round. Your next move is to guess higher or lower. If you’re right, you accrue points; if not, you flip the next card and cross your fingers for a clean pass.
- Doubling down: every few rounds invites a riskier bet. The temptation to push your luck is real, and it’s the sweet spot where casual vibes meet high-stakes tension. The box doesn’t include a meter that explodes, but your heart rate might recommend a cup of tea after the third tight call.
- Final tally: rounds stack, scores climb, and a little luck can swing the winner from “this is comfortable” to “we’re in the final stretch now.” It’s not a marathon of decisions; it’s a sprint to prove you understood the math, or at least looked busy while pretending you did.
