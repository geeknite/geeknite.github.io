### Setup Tips for a Multi-Monitor Future
- Plan the monitor layout on the floor first. Ten monitors demand a layout that minimizes neck strain. We recommend a 6+4 arrangement (six screens on the main plane, four offset for secondary tasks) or a 4x3 grid depending on your desk depth.
- Use the included monitor arms in a staggered configuration to maximize reach and reduce the risk of your screens colliding during a panic alt-tab.
- Calibrate height per seat: your primary monitor line should be at eye height, with your secondary screens angled to reduce head-turning fatigue.
- Route power separately from data cables where possible; the last thing you want is a dragon’s hoard of EMI noise polluting your precious bandwidth.

## Ergonomics, Usability, and the Human Factor
### Comfort Over Ten Monitors
Ergonomics aren’t an afterthought; they’re the main plot. The Infinite Desk doesn’t promise magical posture alignment, but its height-adjustable rails combined with the ten-arm arrangement give you a broad range of ergonomic options. You can tilt, rotate, and align each monitor to minimize neck strain and maximize your ability to “see” all the data without turning into a human windshield wiper. The desk surface height is adjustable through a combination of cranks and motor-assisted options, allowing both sit and stand configurations. The risk with any such setup is over-correction; the Infinity Desk does a decent job of resisting extremes, helping you avoid the dreaded “standing desk limbo” where you’re neither seated nor fully upright.

### Keyboard and Mouse Positioning
With ten monitors, you’ll likely have a central keyboard and mouse tray, plus supplementary input devices for secondary tasks. The included rails can accommodate a keyboard tray that slides laterally; this is especially helpful for when you’re giving your neck a break and want to work on a dedicated reference screen. The general guideline is to keep your primary keyboard close to your body with elbows at about 90 degrees. The extra rails provide peripheral space for a trackball, a numeric keypad, or a gaming pad—whatever your inner geek desires.

### Work-Life Balance: The Real Test
Let’s be honest: a desk with ten screens is a conversation starter at best and a productivity amplifier at worst. It’s perfect for data analysts, stock traders, software architects, and obsessive PPT deck builders who like their charts with an acre of real estate.
