## How Cat Party Changes the Gameplay
The expansion isn’t trying to reinvent Exploding Kittens; it’s trying to broaden the room for mischief. It adds new tools that encourage players to engage more with others rather than simply managing their own hand. Here are the core shifts you’ll notice during a typical session:

### Turn Flow and Pace
Turn flow retains the familiar rhythm: draw, play, resolve, pass. What changes is the tempo and the opportunities for bluff, theater, and back-channel negotiation. Cat cards can introduce mini-combos that shorten or lengthen a round, depending on how you chain their effects with the base deck. Party cards, meanwhile, can inject sudden shifts—changing who’s in the hot seat, forcing negotiations, or triggering quick challenges that demand a quick wit and a louder voice. The pace becomes less predictable in a good way; you’ll feel as if a cat just ran across your keyboard and started typing its own victory conditions.

### Cat Cards: The Meow Factor
Cat cards function as catalysts for interaction. They often provide a benefit to the player who plays them at the cost of inviting others to react. Some cat cards target specific players in playful ways, others require discard actions, and a few trigger cat-to-cat interactions that chain into larger moments of chaos. The humor here isn’t a cheap gimmick; it’s an invitation to perform—read your opponent’s tells, deliver a dramatic pause, and watch the room bend toward a chorus of cat noises and laughter.

### Party Cards: The Social FX
Party cards act as social accelerants. They’re the rounds’ plot twists: you draw a card and suddenly find yourself swapping seats, drawing a consolation prize, or having to reveal a strategy you hoped was a secret. The social axis gets higher resolution with these cards. They reward players who can read the table, coax humor out of others, and execute a quick, well-timed gambit that leaves the table buzzing.

### Strategy Implications
The core strategy of Exploding Kittens remains about hand management, probability, and risk assessment. Cat Party nudges that strategy toward social dynamics. You’ll want to consider both what your own hand can do and how your opponents might respond to a cat card or a party effect. Some general tips:

- Read the room: If the table is full of performers, lean into dramatic reveals and bravado. If it’s a quieter group, you can adapt by choosing subtle effects that still create micro-conflicts.
- Time your cat plays: Don’t waste a powerful card on a round that’s already chaotic. Instead, save it for a moment you can amplify with a party card to maximize effect.
- Leverage misdirection: A well-timed action card paired with a party card can force opponents into poor decisions or misread your intentions.
- Don’t fear the fail moment: The expansion is meant to be fun even when things go wrong. Don’t punish yourself for a silly misplay; embrace the laughter and pivot toward a better moment next round.
