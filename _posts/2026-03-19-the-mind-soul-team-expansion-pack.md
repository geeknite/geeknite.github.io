## Introduction
The Mind is a game about trust, timing, and the gnawing suspicion that your friends are better at rhythm than you are. The Soul Team Expansion Pack leans into that vibe with the addition of soul tokens, a shared team dial, and prompts that make your table feel like a therapy circle hosted by a neon star. If your group enjoys the moment when someone nails a card by pure vibes, this expansion will feel like coming home in a spaceship.

## What this expansion aims to do
This expansion delivers a more social, more theater driven experience. It does not turn your table into a talk show, but it does invite you to speak less and listen more. The soul prompts take you into a space where feelings and moods become signals that influence card placement. The net effect is that timing becomes a group sport rather than a sprint for the strongest ego at the table. It is ideal for groups that love co op games with moments of genuine shared wonder.

## Setup and first round impressions
Setting up is a ritual you can complete in under ten minutes, even if your group spends five minutes debating whether the Team Dial should be placed left or right of the deck. You pair up with a partner, place the Team Dial in the center, and shuffle the Soul Cards into the main deck. Each team receives two Soul Tokens, placed within easy reach for the moments when tempo needs a gentle push. The goal is simple in concept: align your hearts and minds enough to make two cards land in a row within a narrow window.

The first few rounds feel a bit like a dance class where everyone forgot their shoes. The beauty of it is that, with a little practice, you start to predict the rhythm of your partner s breathing and blinking. The awkwardness fades, and you start to feel like you are in on a shared secret that only the cosmos could betray.

## How the soul mechanics change your play
The Soul Tokens add a light strategic layer. You can spend a token to nudge tempo by a notch, either speeding up or slowing down the pace to suit your team s approach. The Team Dial tracks the tempo of the two players in a team, indicating if you are in sync or if one partner is running ahead of the other. When both players in a team play their cards within a tight window and a synergy is detected, your team earns a Soul Point. These points can be used to reveal a hidden synergy or to re calibrate tempo for the current round.

The Soul Cards introduce prompts that reference emotions, mood shifts, or cosmic metaphors. Expect prompts like choose a card based on a feeling you know your partner shares, or select a card that mirrors the mood in the room. These prompts are not tests of memory alone; they are tests of how well you can listen to each other when your friends try to be quiet and dramatic at the same time.

Example play scenario
Team A sits across from each other and their partners are giving them the most serious looks while their phones buzz with memes. The lead picks a Soul Card prompt dealing with shared childhood memory. The partner nods subtly and uses a Soul Token to adjust tempo. The second player holds their breath and tries to read the energy in the room. The result is a smooth play, a clean match of the tempo, and a small chorus of relief from the table as the round ends with a perfect alignment.

## Strategy and tips
- Focus on your partner s nonverbal cues rather than trying to interpret the card out loud. The fun of the Soul Team expansion is in the subtle micro signals that the room picks up.
- Use Soul Tokens when you feel a misalignment potential. A careful token can avoid a disaster or turn a near miss into an elegant exchange.
- Don t overdo the team talk. Sometimes staying quiet and letting a moment pass is more powerful than a long explanation.
- Keep ELO style track of your team s performance. The Soul Points provide a tangible way to measure how well you read your partner s signals.

## House rules and optional tweaks
If you love experimenting with rules, try these fun tweaks:
- Silent round challenge: every round, players are not allowed to speak, even if the prompts call for it
- The double tempo: if you see two or more players moving in lockstep, apply a shared tempo boost
- Mood boost: each team picks a mood at the start; successful rounds grant a small mood bonus to next round
- The glow up: dim the lighting to emphasize the Soul Tokens glow, which enhances the vibe

## Using the expansion with other games
The Soul Team concept can work with other co op titles that rely on timing and coordination. You can adapt the team pairing approach to games with similar rhythm based mechanics. It might be a little random, but the flexibility makes it possible to experiment with a handful of games in a single night.
