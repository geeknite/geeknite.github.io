- Loitering detection for storefronts

## Onboard AI and Edge Processing
Edge AI keeps the heavy lifting on the camera itself. This reduces bandwidth usage and makes local alerts near-instant. The Eyeball supports on-device detection modules and can feed a central system with event data rather than raw streams. In practice, you’ll see push notifications when a person enters a defined zone or when a stray vehicle parks in a restricted area.

If you plan to keep this as a standalone unit with no NVR, you’ll still have access to historic event logs and local storage options. ## Installation tips and Best Practices
- Place the Eyeball in a high, central location to minimize blind spots; aim for at least 2.5 meters (8 feet) above the ground for the best coverage. Avoid direct head-on shots of bright windows.
- Use a proper mounting bracket to keep the dome sealed and minimize vibration. Screws and anchors should match the ceiling material for long-term stability.
- Run a quick test sweep: walk through the space and verify that the AI alerts fire in real-time and that the UI correctly displays the defined zones.
- Label zones clearly in the software to avoid accidental misclassification in high-traffic spaces.
- Consider a backup plan for storage: a small NVR or edge storage option gives you a local fallback in case of network issues.

## Real-World Troubleshooting Tips

- If audio is required for your use case, verify if the camera supports audio streams and adjust the settings accordingly.
- If the image appears washed out, experiment with exposure and WDR settings, or add ambient lighting to the space.
