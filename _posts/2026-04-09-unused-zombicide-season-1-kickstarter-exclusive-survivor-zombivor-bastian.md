## The Minis in Play: Stats, Balance, and Narrative Potential
One of the big questions with Kickstarter exclusives is whether they bring actual tabletop value or simply exist as collectible curios. In the case of Bastian, there are two forms to consider: the survivor and the Zombivor. Each has its own utility in the game, and both contribute to the varying flavor of a campaign that can otherwise begin to feel somewhat repetitive after a long crawl through the undead.

- Survivor Bastian: Expect him to be the practical, no-nonsense character who could plausibly wield a tool that doubles as a weapon. In Season 1, survivors often carry essential items: lockpicks, axes, or guns—depending on the scenario. The Bastian survivor is likely built around a toolkit vibe, with a skill set that emphasizes practical problem-solving, resilience, and perhaps a little streetwise cunning. His card art usually hints at a background in logistics, field medevac, or survival for the long haul through ruined urban environments.
- Zombivor Bastian: For those unfamiliar, Zombivors are the flipped, undead versions that create new threats and also new ways to approach the game. The Zombivor variant is a blast to paint because it allows you to explore the art of degenerate skin tones, exposed bone plates, and clothing that has seen better days. In gameplay, Zombivors typically pack a slightly different set of rules that make them a fun divergence rather than a strict upgrade or downgrade from their human counterparts. Expect a more brutal threat on the board and a reminder that your survivors aren’t the only ones who got a glow-up during the apocalypse.

From a balance perspective, exclusives can be a test bed for rules iterations. If Bastian came with special abilities or unique items, the question becomes: do these powers feel too strong, or do they slot in as flavorful additions that require clever play rather than brute force? The honest answer is often: with Season 1, you are dealing with early design choices that have grown more sophisticated in later editions. Bastian’s exclusivity is less about game-breaking mechanics and more about narrative resonance—the kind of figure you want to pull out when you’ve just rolled a clutch turn and need a hero to signal the turning of the tide.

## Painting and Display: Getting the Most out of Your Bastian
If you are the sort who paints minis for fun, Bastian presents some satisfying texture opportunities. His survivor version typically has cloth textures that reward a layered approach: base coat, wash for shadows, dry brush highlights on leather straps, and metallics on buckles and tools. The Zombivor, with its grotesque features, invites a slightly more experimental palette: pale sickly skin tones, yellowed teeth, and the occasional gore highlight to drive emphasis on the head and spine areas. The key here is to keep a consistent light source so the two variants feel like siblings rather than two entirely different models from two separate universes.

For tabletop enthusiasts, the practical painting advice is to pre-prime with a light gray or white to keep the metallics readable, then apply a glaze or wash to bring out the sculpt’s recessed details. If you are new to painting, consider this duo as a gentle introduction to contrast paints, which can dramatically simplify shading on busy textures. If you are more advanced, you can push your edge highlights on the survivor and treat the Zombivor as your canvas for advanced bleed-through glazing techniques. Either route you pick, the end result should feel cinematic rather than toy-like, a small victory in the ongoing project that is your display shelf.

## Collectibility, Authenticity, and the Price of Exclusivity
Let’s address the elephant in the post: scarcity. Kickstarter exclusives are infamous for surviving only in the memory banks of backers and the black market of resale sites. The Bastian duo is exactly the sort of item that can spike in price not because of practical in-game value, but because of the narrative aura around it. If you are hunting for an investment, be mindful that a lot of the value is nostalgic rather than functional. The box art, the backstory, the fact you can still tell your gaming group, “I have the unused Bastian,” are the kinds of intangible value that keep the hobby breathing.

That said, the practical reality is that not every used copy is a steal. Counterfeits and misrepresented items are common in the bazaar of exclusives. When shopping, ask for high-resolution photos of the minis, verify the presence of all cards and tokens, and consider verifying serial numbers if the edition included any. The Geeknite rule of thumb: exclusivity adds a premium, but the real joy is in how you use the minis in your game. If you can craft a narrative around Bastian that adds flavor to your sessions, the investment can be worth it, especially if you plan to keep the collection intact for years of convoys through the zombie apocalypse.

For those gentler on the wallet, there are always the kinder routes: buying the full Season 1 starter set if you can find it, or trading with a friend who already has the exclusive. The hobby thrives on community, and the true gatekeeping enemy here is FOMO, not the undead.

## A Quick Playthrough Guide: Using Bastian in Your Games
If you want a practical template to slot Bastian into your next session, here’s a crisp guide to get you started:

1) Start with a thematic intro: Bastian’s survivor card gives him access to a toolkit-based skill path. Introduce a mission where a broken gate requires a toolkit to unlock a safe zone. This ensures his practical utility shines through.
2) Bring in the Zombivor as a secondary threat: The Zombivor version should appear in a mid-game ambush, forcing the party to split or adapt their approach. This echoes the horror beat of the apocalypse while giving your group a fresh tactical challenge.
3) Balance the encounter with a risk meter: Limit the number of Zombivors that can appear in a single turn to avoid a sloppy cluster of miniatures that slows down the table. The fun comes from the tension, not from turning the table into a battlefield of static minis.
4) Reward creative problem solving: If a player uses Bastian’s toolkit creatively to bypass a locked door or salvage a critical item, give a small in-game reward. The goal is to celebrate clever thinking as much as raw dice luck.
