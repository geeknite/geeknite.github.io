### Traffic and durability considerations
A wool rug of this size should hold up well in medium to high-traffic areas if properly backed and maintained. Wool’s natural resilience helps it resist crushing under daily foot traffic, especially with regular rotating of the rug’s position to even out wear patterns. In homes with pets, especially those that enjoy the occasional lunar landing on the rug, wool again proves hardy relative to many synthetic fibers. The key is proactive care:
- Vacuum weekly with a beater bar on a gentle setting to avoid pilling.
- Address spills promptly; blot, don’t rub. For stains, use a wool-safe cleaner and test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- Consider a quality rug pad to reduce movement and extend the rug’s life.

### Care and maintenance essentials
Wool loves air; it does not love soaking. A light cleaning routine helps maintain appearance without weakening the fibers. Here is a practical care plan:
- Vacuum weekly, using the appropriate height setting for your vacuum to avoid rough agitation.
- Rotate the rug every 3 to 6 months to even out traffic patterns and sun exposure.
- Blot spills with a clean white cloth or paper towels. Do not rub, as rubbing can push the stain deeper into the fiber.
- For stubborn spots, use a wool-safe cleaner following the product directions. Always test in a small, hidden area first.
- Deep cleans: If your space experiences heavy use or pets that adore dramatic dive sequences onto the rug, consider a professional cleaning every 12 to 24 months depending on wear.

## FAQs: Quick hits for busy humans
Q: Will the seafoam color clash with my existing color palette?
A: Likely not. Seafoam tends to harmonize with neutrals like beige, taupe, gray, and white, while a few green-blue accents can pull the palette together. If you have bold colors elsewhere, the rug can serve as a soft, unifying anchor.

Q: Is wool suitable for homes with pets and kids?
A: Yes, with regular care. Wool resists staining better than many natural fibers and resists static. Spills should be blotted quickly. A rug pad helps prevent wear and protects floors from scratches and scuffs caused by heavy furniture movements.

Q: How do I maintain color vibrancy over time?
A: Vacuum regularly, avoid direct sunlight on the rug for long periods, and consider rotating the rug to prevent uneven sun exposure. If you notice fading, you may adjust light exposure or use window treatments to limit sun damage.
