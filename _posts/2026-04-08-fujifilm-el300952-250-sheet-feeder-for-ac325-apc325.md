## Maintenance and Longevity
Maintenance is the boring part of hardware ownership, but it’s the part that pays dividends in uptime. Here are practical guidelines that help keep the feeder singing:
- Clean regularly: Paper dust and stock debris accumulate on feed wheels and rails. A quick wipe-down with a lint-free cloth weekly is a good habit.
- Inspect feed wheels: If you notice slippage or misfeeds becoming more frequent, inspect the rollers for wear. Replacement parts are available through authorized channels, and it’s generally more cost-effective to address wear early than after a full-blown feed ordeal.
- Check alignment after repositioning: If you move the feeder or reconfigure your setup, re-run the calibration to ensure the feed path remains correct. A small misalignment can cascade into big jams.
- Firmware and software: Keep your printer and feeder software current. Compatibility between devices reduces the chance of odd errors and yields a smoother workflow.

The long-term message is simple: treat it as a precision device that rewards regular, light maintenance rather than heavy-handed “my desk is a workshop” style intervention. A little care goes a long way toward keeping the daily print queue calm and under control.
