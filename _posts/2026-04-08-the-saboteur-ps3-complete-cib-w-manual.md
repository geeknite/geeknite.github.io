## How to evaluate a The Saboteur PS3 complete in box with manual inserts

### Visual inspection checklist
- Box: Check the corners for wear, the spine for any cracking, and the surface for scuffs. A mint box should have sharp edges, no crushing, and minimal surface scratches.
- Disc: Spin the disc to ensure there are no visible divots or heavy scratches. A clean read surface is ideal; a few micro-scratches are common, but a true mint disc will show minimal to no signs of use.
- Manual: Open the manual gently; the spine should be intact, pages should lay flat, and there should be no dog-eared corners or stains.
- Inserts: Fold-out maps and extra inserts should lay flat and be free of tears. They should align with the packaging and not have misprints that scream changed presses.

### Functional checks
- Compatibility: Ensure the disc loads and runs without issues on a PS3. A mint, complete copy should power up with minimal hesitation and no loading errors that scream as if the game is about to declare a mutiny.
- Manual clarity: The control layout in the manual should match the in-game actions. If the manual feels out of date or describes button combos that don’t work, that’s a sign of a mismatch between the artifact and the experience.
