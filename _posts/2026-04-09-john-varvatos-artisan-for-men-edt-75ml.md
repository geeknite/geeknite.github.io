## Scent profile: what you’re really wearing
Artisan for Men is billed as a fresh, citrus-forward fragrance with herbal and woody accents. The top notes skew bright and zesty, then the heart adds a layer of green and aromatic character, while the base settles into a warm, masculine finish that lingers in a friendly, non-assertive way. The magic of this scent lies in its balance: it isn’t screaming with citrus or aggressively woody—it's more like a well-timed explainer who knows when to pipe down and let the room breathe.

Top notes include citrus elements that wake up the senses without crossing into the realm of toothpaste or carpet cleaner. You might catch hints of orange or clementine, fresh green touches, and a sparkle of herbs that give the scent a crisp, almost gym-bag-clean vibe without leaning too medicinal. As the fragrance settles, the heart notes weave in more subtle herbal and floral nuances, something that keeps it approachable rather than intimidating. The dry-down is where a woody warmth lingers—think softly carved wood and a touch of musk—enough to feel grounded while still being easy to wear in everyday life.

Scent layering is a fun topic here. Artisan plays well with a light play of notes rather than loud, show-off notes. It won’t overpower a casual outfit or a shirt and tie combo, and that makes it a flexible choice for coffee dates, office environments, or a casual night out where you want to smell clean and confident rather than loud and caffeinated.

If you’re into the nerdy fragrance details, the note pyramid can be summarized as citrus-green opening, aromatic heart, and a smooth woody base. But as with all scents, the true magic is in how it interacts with your skin chemistry. Your mileage may vary, and that is the delightful unpredictability that makes fragrance collecting feel a bit like a treasure hunt in a gadget store.

## Longevity, projection, and wearability in real terms
Let’s talk performance, because a fragrance is nothing without staying power. For most wearers, Artisan for Men will sit in the moderate-to-good range for longevity. You can expect about 6 to 8 hours of presence on the skin for typical climates. In hotter weather or with sensitive skin, you may notice it fades a little earlier, but not so quickly that you’re chasing scent down the street with air freshener spray. In cooler temps, the fragrance tends to settle more slowly and the woody notes can become more pronounced, offering a slightly more mature vibe.

Projection or “sillage” is generally friendly. You’ll get enough presence to be noticed by someone seated near you without turning the room into a cloud. This makes Artisan a sensible option for office wear, classroom environments, or casual social scenes where you want to leave a memory, not a disaster fragrance alarm.

Seasonality matters here too. Citrus-forward scents swim well in spring and early summer, while the woody undertones help anchor the fragrance in fall and winter evenings. Artisan’s versatility is its strength: it’s a fragrance you can wear from morning meetings to after-work meetups without feeling out of place.

### How to wear and when it shines
- Daily office wear: clean, professional, and not overpowering.
- Casual weekends: pairs well with denim, knitwear, and lightweight jackets.
- Date nights: a subtle, confident aura that won’t overshadow your conversation.
- Travel and gym days: light and refreshing enough to feel appropriate after a workout, without clashing with your deodorant.

Tips for best performance:
- Apply to pulse points (wrists, inner elbows, neck) but avoid over-spraying. A couple of light bursts typically suffice.
- Consider layering with a complementary body wash or lotion to extend the scent life slightly, but avoid overpowering combinations that might create an unusual chemical smell.
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve the fragrance’s integrity.
