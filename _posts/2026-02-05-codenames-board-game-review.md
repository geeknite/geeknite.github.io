## The Basics

### What is Codenames?

Codenames is a party game designed by Vlaada Chvátil and published by Czech Games Edition. It's perfect for 2-8 players, making it a fantastic choice for game nights, family gatherings, or any occasion where you want to mix fun and strategy. The game revolves around two teams, each with a spymaster who gives one-word clues to help their teammates identify the correct words on the board.

### Components

The game includes:
- **200 double-sided key cards**: These cards dictate which words belong to each team, which ones are neutral, and which word is the dreaded assassin.
- **40 agent cards**: Red and blue agent cards for each team.
- **1 double agent card**: Because spies love a good plot twist.
- **17 innocent bystander cards**: For those words that are just hanging out, minding their own business.
- **1 assassin card**: The word you definitely don't want to pick, unless you enjoy losing.
- **1 card stand**: To hold the key card.
- **A sand timer**: For those who enjoy living on the edge with timed games.

## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setup is quick and easy, much like sneaking into a top-secret meeting (but without the risk of being caught). Lay out 25 word cards in a 5x5 grid, draw a key card, and place it in the card stand. The key card will reveal which words belong to each team, which are neutral, and which word is the assassin.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, the spymaster gives a one-word clue and a number indicating how many words on the board relate to that clue. For example, if the words "piano" and "violin" are on the board, the clue might be "instrument: 2." The team members then discuss and try to guess the words. If they guess correctly, the word is covered with their team's agent card. If they guess wrong, it could be a neutral word, an opponent's word, or the assassin – which ends the game instantly.

### Winning the Game

The game ends when one team has successfully covered all their words with agent cards or when someone picks the assassin card, leading to an immediate loss for their team. The thrill of the game comes from the balance between giving clever clues and avoiding disastrous guesses.
