Maintenance and Cleaning
Spills happen; it is the nature of living. Polypropylene fibers resist many stains and are easy to wipe down. For tougher stains, use a mild detergent solution and test on a small corner first. Regular vacuuming with a low to medium suction setting helps prevent dirt from embedding. Avoid steam cleaning with high heat and aggressive agitation that can wear down the fibers over time. If you have pets, you will appreciate that fur doesn’t cling like a magnet as easily as on rougher textures. Always blot spills rather than rub to prevent spreading.

Care tips in practice
- Blot spills immediately and avoid rubbing
- Use a mild cleaning solution with water, test first
- Rotate the rug every few months to avoid uneven fading in direct sunlight
- Use a non-slip rug pad underneath on hard floors to improve stability and reduce movement

Visual pairing ideas and styling tips
- Modern minimal: white walls, minimal furniture, let the rug be the star
- Coastal casual: light woods, navy accents, and natural textures to echo the sea

Rug Pads and Flooring Compatibility
A rug pad helps stabilize the rug, protects the floor, and softly cushions footsteps. On wood or tile, choose a pad with a low profile and good grip. For carpet, a pad is less critical but can still help with movement and noise reduction. Options include felt and rubber blends; consider your floor type and lifestyle when selecting.
