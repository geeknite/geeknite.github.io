## Practical Considerations: Maintenance, Space, and Neighbors
### Space and Placement
A full-size foosball table takes up a healthy amount of room. If you’re living in a modest apartment, you’ll want to measure carefully. It’s not a couch-friendly folding table; this is a real thing that commands attention in your space. If you have kids or pets, consider a corner where the ball won’t become a frequent fetch item. The dimensional footprint of Model 26 is friendly for most home game rooms, but double-check your clearance for swinging rods and the 180-degree turns you’ll attempt when you’re on a hot streak.

### Maintenance and Regular Care
Light oil on the rods every few weeks will go a long way toward preserving smoothness. Wipe the playing surface occasionally to maintain grip and keep the board from gathering crumb trails from your late-night snacks. Replace balls if they start to lose their bounce; a ball that’s too light or too heavy will throw off the spin and ruin your momentum when you’re trying to pull off a dramatic last-second goal. The manual includes some helpful tips, but if you’re the kind of person who accidentally uses a chain oil on wood surfaces, maybe set the bottle aside and stick to the recommended maintenance schedule.
