## Storage, Upgrades, and Longevity
One of the major considerations with older budget desktops is how to extend their life. The 240 GB SSD is a friendly, fast start, but space is a precious resource. If you anticipate needing more space without juggling cloud storage forever, you will want to look at a couple of upgrade paths:
- Add a larger hard drive: A 1 TB or 2 TB HDD can be a cheap, reliable companion for data, backups, and media. This keeps your OS on the fast SSD while your media and bulk storage live in the spinning disk. The upgrade is relatively straightforward for most HP desktops; you will typically need a spare 3.5-inch bay and a SATA power connection from the PSU.
- Upgrade RAM: If you can find out the exact model of the motherboard, you may be able to pop in an extra 4 GB or even 8 GB of RAM, depending on the slots and the max supported. Many budget desktops have two DIMM slots; if you already have 8 GB in there, you might be capped for a while, but a RAM upgrade can help with multitasking and keeping background processes from stepping on your toes.
- Replace or augment GPU: This model is not going to host a modern discrete GPU with glee, but some budget desktops do support a low-profile GPU upgrade. If you are determined to add a bit more gaming punch, look for a compact GPU that fits the case size and check the PSU capacity and the motherboard clearance. Spoiler: you may be better off leaving it on the shelf for light gaming and keeping the GPU money for a future upgrade to a more capable system.

From a longevity perspective, this PC is the kind that can age gracefully with a few careful upgrades. It is not a cryptic time capsule with cutting-edge runtimes, but it is the kind of box that learns your workflow and quietly refuses to die when your spreadsheets get spicier. Regular maintenance, like dusting the fans and re-seating RAM sticks, will extend its life. The Windows 10 Pro license, while no longer the freshest, continues to be usable for many corporate environments and home offices that want a stable OS with familiar controls and compatibility. If you rely on legacy software that loves Windows 10, this PC can be a practical and budget-friendly option.

## Practical Tips for Owners
- Clean dust regularly: Desktops accumulate dust, and dust slows fans. A quick 5-minute dust-off every few months keeps temperatures reasonable and fan noise down.
- Re-seat RAM if you encounter random freezes: This is old-school tech support magic that still works surprisingly well on older hardware.
- Consider a storage upgrade: If you find yourself drowning in files, add a larger HDD for data and keep the SSD for OS and apps. It is the classic tiered storage strategy with a modern fast lane for your critical software.
- Optimize startup: Disable unnecessary startup programs to speed up boot times and reduce memory pressure right after login. A cleaner startup makes a big difference on systems like this.
- Update drivers selectively: Windows updates are helpful, but some older hardware drivers behave better with a well-curated, selective update approach. Keep essential drivers current, but avoid forcing every update if you notice instability.
