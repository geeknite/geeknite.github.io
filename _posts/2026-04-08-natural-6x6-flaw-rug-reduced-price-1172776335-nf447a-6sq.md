## Maintenance magic: cleaning without fear
Cleaning a natural fiber rug often raises the red flag of “it’s delicate.” This rug, however, is pleasantly forgiving if you treat it with a sensible plan:
- Vacuum weekly with a soft brush attachment. Avoid high-suction behemoths that happily eat fibers for breakfast.
- Spills: blot, do not rub. Blot again. If it’s a stubborn stain, a mild detergent diluted with water on a white cloth usually does the trick. Always test a small area first.
- Rotation: rotate every few months to even out any foot-traffic patterns. This helps prevent that sad “stripe” effect where your cat refuses to lay on half the rug because it’s too bored of the color.

We also tested a gentle spot-cleaning routine with a sample spill, and the result was an acceptable outcome. ## Maintenance myth-busting: how often should you rotate and refresh?
A common myth about rugs is that once you place them, they exist in perpetuity. In reality, a little movement goes a long way toward maintaining even wear. We recommend:
- Rotate every 3–6 months if the rug lives in a high-traffic zone or if you notice noticeable wear patterns.
- Vacuum regularly; natural fibers appreciate a gentle touch rather than being treated like a dust magnet with a turbo engine.
- Deep cleaning: if your rug ever faces a serious stain or odor, consult a local professional cleaner rather than attempting heroic DIY experiments on the internet. Your rug will thank you, and your friends won’t be able to tell you tried to soak it in your bathtub with dubious cleaning agents.
