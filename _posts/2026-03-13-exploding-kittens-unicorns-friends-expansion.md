## Introduction

Exploding Kittens has always worn its irreverent sense of humor like a badge that reads: Do Not Take Seriously. Unicorns and Friends keeps that tone intact while sprinkling in new mechanics that shift rhythm, risk, and ridiculousness. If the base game is a sprint through a chaotic obstacle course, Unicorns and Friends is where a fire-breathing glitter dragon suddenly joins the race and demands snacks mid-lap. In other words, it’s still silly, but with a dash more strategy and a splash of mythic nonsense.

The expansion is designed to be used with the core Exploding Kittens game. It doesn’t require you to redo your entire rulebook, but it does nudge you to learn a few new card types and a few new ways to bluff, tease, and time your turns. The art remains vibrant, punchy, and slightly deranged—perfect for people who believe that a unicorn is not a horse with a fancy haircut but a tiny, sparkly chaos engine. The kit doesn’t turn Exploding Kittens into a deep strategy game; it simply leans into the chaos with a different palette and a few new tools. The end result is a slightly taller stack of decisions, more opportunities to misread your friends, and a lot more glitter on the board.

For those who want to see where Unicorns and Friends sits in the grand tower of Exploding Kittens expansions, it falls somewhere between “additive chaos” and “nudge to strategy.” It doesn’t overhaul the core play. It doesn’t replace anything. It sits on top like a sparkly hat that asks, Are you ready to gamble with unicorns? Yes, you are, you masochist of joy.

## What’s in the box?

The Unicorns and Friends expansion adds a handful of new card types and tokens designed to layer in additional tactics without breaking the pace. The box is compact, as is typical for Exploding Kittens products, which means your shelf won’t collapse under a moonlit pile of unicorn confetti. Here’s what you get:

- New Unicorn cards that grant wild, unpredictable effects. These are the glitter bombs of the deck: beautiful to look at, kind of terrifying to play.
- New Friends cards that bloat your strategic options with character-driven abilities. Friends cards feel like the sitcom sidekicks that suddenly become your most valuable allies in a fight for kitten survival.
- A handful of sparkle tokens that you’ll probably forget exist until someone flips a unicorn card and the table erupts into a chorus of groans and laughter.
- A compact, updated rule sheet that succinctly covers how unicorns interact with classic Exploding Kittens mechanics like defuse cards and explode cards.
- A more vivid art direction that keeps things cartoony but with a touch more narrative flair. The unicorns aren’t shy; they’re aggressively cute and wearing tiny capes.

If you’re curious whether the new cards complicate things in a bad way, rest assured—the expansion is designed to layer on top of what you already know. It doesn’t require a doctorate in unicorn etiquette to figure out a turn, but it does reward players who pay attention to the new synergies.

## Unicorns: The glittery chaos engines

Unicorn cards are the center stage of Unicorns and Friends. They aren’t just pretty pictures; they inject new decision points and sometimes a touch of “oh no, here we go again.” Some unicorns have defensive properties that can save you from a doomed round, while others tilt the game in a way that makes you think about your odds in the moment rather than in hindsight. The best unicorn cards create interesting micro-moments: a turn you think you’ve nailed, only to realize that a sparkly horse has just sabotaged your plan in a polite, glittery manner.

From a game design perspective, unicorn cards are not just aesthetic; they’re pacing tools. They can shift the momentum from “one more turn, I swear” to “beware, the unicorns are awake.” The moment you see a unicorn card drawn or played, you can feel the room’s energy shift. People lean in, conversations slow down to a dramatic whisper, and suddenly someone is calculating probabilities in a way they haven’t since that one time they played dice with a calculator and a cat filter on their webcam.

For players who like to narrate their strategy, unicorn cards offer moments to spin a little lore around your choices. You can say, with a straight face and complete confidence, that you’re summoning a unicorn of destiny to protect your kitten and perhaps steal a friend’s dignity in the process. Works like a charm—almost every time, as long as your friends believe you as a confident, slightly delusional game-night scholar.

## Friends: The social layer

Friends cards in the Unicorns and Friends expansion function as the sitcom sidekicks that refuse to quit. They provide additional effects that can be invoked by specific actions or conditions. The social layer they bring introduces a new dynamic: you’re not just trying to survive the exploding kitten; you’re also managing a cast of quirky allies who demand attention and, occasionally, a little bribery with unicorn glitter.

One of the most interesting aspects of Friends cards is that they encourage players to think not just about their own hand but about the emotional ecosystem of the table. Who needs a mercy pass? Which friend should you leverage to force someone else into a risky move? The narrative possibilities are enormous, and the humor potential is off the charts. It’s very easy to slip into a running commentary where you assign ridiculous, over-the-top personalities to your opponents’ cards. It’s silly, yes, but it also keeps players engaged and fosters the kind of banter that makes a game night memorable rather than forgettable.

## How to play with Unicorns & Friends

To use Unicorns and Friends, you need the base Exploding Kittens game. The expansion slides into the core rules with a few additions that you can learn in a few minutes, but you’ll be tempted to test it out in the first few rounds. Here are some practical guidelines to get you started without needing a PhD in unicorn matriarchy:

- Shuffle the expansion deck with the base deck as directed in the rulebook, and ensure players understand the context of unicorn and friend cards before diving into a live game.
- When a unicorn card is drawn, look for its special instruction and decide whether to apply it immediately or save it for a later turn. The timing here matters a lot; unicorns reward bold decisions but punish indecision with glittery sarcasm.
- Friends cards usually trigger when you use or reveal certain actions. Pay attention to your neighbors: a well-timed Friends card can flip a round from disaster to triumph.
- Defuse cards remain the best defense in the game. Unicorns and Friends doesn’t render defusal obsolete, but it does make the risks feel bigger and more theatrical.

If you’ve played the base game with a couple of house rules, you’ll be delighted to know the unicorn and friends additions slot in neatly with minimal fiddling. The expansion is designed to be friendly to newcomers while still offering enough depth for veterans who like to argue about probability while wearing a silly hat.

## Strategy tips and house rules

Here are a few practical tips that can help you squeeze more laughter and fewer dramatic sighs out of your Unicorns and Friends sessions:

- Pay attention to the unicorn lineup. Some unicorns are defensive saviors; others are chaos engines. Sort your approach around which unicorns are in play and which are likely to land in your own hand on the next draw.
- Use Friends cards as “psych-out” tools. A well-timed Friends card can make your opponents hesitate, wondering whether you’re setting up a trap or just messing with them for the sheer joy of it. Don’t be afraid to lean into the storytelling aspect; players remember the moment you pretended your card was a portal to an alternate universe.
- Manage your defuse cards with more care. The expansion can push you into riskier plays; defuses remain the most valuable resource, so don’t squander them on non-critical moments—unless you want to earn the title of Most Dramatic Player.
- Create a narrative around your decisions. People remember a good joke or a clever setup much longer than a dry calculation. If you can justify your moves with a short, funny explanation, you’ll keep the table engaged and more forgiving when luck doesn’t go your way.
- Consider house-rule tweaks for accessibility. If you have newer players at your table, you can reduce the perceived severity of unicorn cards by introducing a “soft mode” where some effects are simplified on the first few rounds.

## Final verdict and recommendations

Overall, Exploding Kittens Unicorns and Friends is a strong addition for players who want more color and narrative flavor without rewriting the rules of the base game. It keeps the lighthearted humor intact while expanding the decision space in a way that feels natural and fun. It’s not a must-have for every group, but if you love the core game’s chaotic vibe and you want more opportunities to laugh, gasp, and occasionally throw your strategy plan out the window because a unicorn decided to use diplomacy in a way that makes no sense, this expansion is worth it.

- If you’re a casual gamer who thrives on quick rounds, Unicorns and Friends will be a welcome spice rather than a main course. It adds enough novelty to keep things fresh without derailing your ability to finish a night’s session in a reasonable amount of time.
- If you’re a veteran Exploding Kittens player who loves meta-games, role-play, and inside jokes, this expansion provides a playground for storytelling and social interaction to flourish. It’s a playground that occasionally hands you a glitter cannon, but hey, that’s part of the charm.
- If you’re a family-night enthusiast seeking a balance of silliness and strategy, you’ll find Unicorns and Friends approachable. The rules are straightforward enough for curious kids with adult supervision and a shared tolerance for chaos.

For a practical buying decision, consider your group size and preferred vibe. If you want more chaos and more chances to be witty at the same time, this expansion delivers. If you’re chasing a deeper strategic puzzle, you might enjoy other expansions or different party games that lean more toward strategy rather than storytelling.
