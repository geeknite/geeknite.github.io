## How UNO WILD differs from regular UNO
### The core rules stay the same (mostly)
If you know UNO, you know the basics: be the first to discard all your cards, matching color or number, and drop a Wild card when you want to flip the color of the board. The Wild Card Game keeps that baseline intact because nostalgia matters, and so do those dramatic “Uno!” shouts that make family gatherings feel like a tiny, loud festival.

### Enter the customizable wilds
The headline feature is the Customizable Wild Card. These are wild cards with a blank area where players can write a temporary rule for the duration of the game or until the card is played. The rules can be anything from “Skip every odd-numbered player on this round” to “Draw twice if you’re wearing blue socks.” The possibilities (and potential consequences) are limited only by your marker skills and your willingness to tolerate chaos.

To facilitate this, the kit includes a few suggested prompts in the instruction sheet, like:
- Write a challenge for the next player.
- Write a mini-task that must be completed before the next turn.
- Create a color-safe rule that the table agrees on before starting. (You know, because peace is a renewable resource.)

The goal isn’t to ruin everyone’s evening, but to make tonight’s UNO a little more cinematic. You’ll trade the tightest strategy for the most ridiculous outcomes, and honestly, that’s the point.

## Setup and gameplay flow
### Basic setup
Like classic UNO, shuffle the deck and deal 7 cards to each player. The top card of the draw pile becomes the starting card, and you’re off to the color-coded races of chaos. If you uncover a Wild card, you can declare the color for the next hand; with Customizable Wild Cards in play, you’ll be juggling both a color choice and a future impromptu rule.

### Turn structure with a twist
During your turn, you have a couple of options:
- Play a matching card (by color or number).
- Play a Wild card to change color.
- Play a Customizable Wild Card to impose a new temporary rule.
- Draw a card if you can’t legally play.

When a Customizable Wild Card is played, the player who wrote the rule (or designated the rule at the start of the game) should remind the table of the effect. If the rule is too spicy for the table’s taste, you can always veto it with a different Wild card—but beware the consequences of karma, and the possibility of someone writing a rule during the heat of the moment “for fairness.”

### Strategy and social dynamics
UNO WILD rewards not just fast thinking but quick negotiation. The customization layer invites social engineering: you’ll decide whether to employ a playful rule for your neighbor’s disadvantage or to implement a lighthearted buff for your own team. It becomes less about who holds the best cards and more about who can keep a straight face while the table tries to interpret your write-in. It’s cooperative chaos with a hint of friendly sabotage, which is basically the spice level you want on game night.

## The customization experience: rules you can write
### How to craft a good Customizable Wild Card
To get the most joy out of Customizable Wild Cards, consider these categories:
