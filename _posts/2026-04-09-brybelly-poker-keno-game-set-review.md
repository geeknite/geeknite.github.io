This is the heart of the review, because a game set is only as good as its playability. ### Setup and player flow
- Each player starts with a set number of chips, typically enough to last a few rounds. If you want a longer night, stash more chips and set a soft cap on how quickly they disappear.
- The Keno grid card is placed in the center, with the chips stacked in a tray beside it. The two decks of cards are shuffled and placed face down within easy reach.
- The dealer or a chosen player deals a starting hand from one deck while the rest of the group decides bets using the chips.

### Basic betting and number selection
- Players place bets on a selection of numbers on the Keno grid. You can decide how many numbers each player should pick, with a common range between 3 and 6 numbers depending on how heavy you want the risk.
- After bets are placed, the top card of the deck is drawn and the result is announced. The card values can be used to determine wild cards, additional draws, or payout multipliers depending on your chosen rules.
- The other deck can be used to determine hand outcomes, adding a poker style twist to the results. For example, you can treat certain card faces as multipliers for hits on the Keno grid, blending the luck of Keno with the strategy of poker hands.

### Scoring and payout ideas
- A simple approach is pays based on the number of hits against the original Keno picks. Hitting more numbers yields bigger payouts, with a mid range set of hits creating a mid tier payout. This keeps the game accessible for casual players while preserving the sense of risk that makes both poker and keno exciting.
- For a poker flavored twist, you can award a small bonus for a strong poker hand at the end of a round. For example, a straight or a flush could earn an extra chip payout, while a pair or three of a kind might grant a smaller bonus. This keeps players engaged beyond the grid and adds a layer of strategy to the bets.

### House rules and customization
You can tailor the kit to your group in countless ways. Here are a few ideas to get you started:
- Limit the number of numbers each player can pick to reduce table chaos.
- Implement a time limit for bets to keep the pace brisk and the energy high.
- Add a wild card rule that changes every round to keep players guessing. For instance, a wild card could double payouts on all hits for that round or swap numbers on the grid mid game.
- Use the chips for side bets on the strength of dice rolls or card draws, adding another axis to the competition without changing the basic core of the set.
