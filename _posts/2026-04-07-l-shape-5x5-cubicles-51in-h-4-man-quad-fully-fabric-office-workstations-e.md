## Ergonomics and Comfort
### Desk and Chair Height
Your desk surface should be around 29 inches high to align with most ergonomic chairs. This height ensures comfortable elbow angles and reduces shoulder tension. The panels do their job by providing a neutral boundary between teammates so you do not accidentally lean on someone else while reaching for the kettle.

### Monitor and Keyboard Setup
A standard monitor arm fits snugly onto the desk surface; place the monitor at about eye level to reduce neck strain. Your keyboard should be positioned so your elbows are at a 90-degree angle, with the wrists relaxed. The internal corner provides a natural cradle for your keyboard and mouse, allowing for a comfortable stance during long coding sessions.

### Lighting
If your office is not blessed with abundant natural light, consider a desk lamp with a warm color temperature. The inner corners can get a bit dark, especially when the crew forgets to turn on the overhead lighting. A bright but non glaring lamp is all you need to keep the eyes from crying by 3 pm.

## Acoustic and Privacy
### Noise Reduction
The fabric panels offer better noise dampening than bare cubicle surfaces. They do not block sound completely, but they reduce ambient noise to a level where you can hear your own thoughts again. If you crave more privacy, consider adding additional acoustic panels to the interior or installing a light door seal on your office.

### Privacy and Focus
The privacy afforded by the panels can help you focus on critical tasks. It also prevents your coworker from interrupting your flow at an inopportune moment, such as while you’re about to land a big bug fix or push a release. It strikes a balance between openness for collaboration and privacy for deep work.

## Cable Management and Storage
### Cable Routing
Keeping cables tidy is an art form, but the L-shape helps by providing built in channels and grommets to route power and data without having to dance around a tangle. Route power leads near the desk edge and data cables toward the interior to minimize trip hazards.

### Storage Options
Optional under desk drawers or small shelves can be added to increase storage. With four work zones, you can create a small storage network to keep your work area organized.

## Assembly and Maintenance
### Assembly Timing and Process
If you are working with a small team, plan for a 2-3 hour assembly window. The modular pieces are meant to fit together with relative ease; double-check alignment as you go. Expect to assemble the frame, then attach panels, then route cables, and finally tighten up the joints. The process is straightforward, but you might need an extra cup of coffee or two.

### Maintenance Best Practices
Keep the fabric clean by wiping with a damp microfiber cloth. For the frame, use a mild cleaner to wipe away fingerprints and dust. If you decide to rearrange or relocate, the modules are designed to be disassembled with ease.
