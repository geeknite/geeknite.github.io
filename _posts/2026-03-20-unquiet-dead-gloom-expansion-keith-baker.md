## Overview

Gloom is a card game about weaving elaborate tales of woe using transparent cards that let you stack curses on your family while trying to make your opponents cry with envy or pity. The Unquiet Dead expansion, crafted by Keith Baker, adds new relatives, extra event cards, and a few novel twists to the base game. The goal remains the same: to make your family the most tragically doomed while keeping your opponents from outscoring you with clever positioning of modifiers. The expansion is designed to slot into the base game without forcing a full rulebook rework, which is exactly what players want when they drag a box labeled Unquiet Dead into a game night and discover that their plans for a quick round might become a small epic of melancholy and whimsy.

If you are a complete newcomer to Gloom, a quick pro tip: start with the base game to learn the flow, then bring in Unquiet Dead for the extra flavor and the opportunity to grumble about the new relatives during setup. The expansion does not overwhelm the core loop, but it does require you to keep track of a few more relationships and a few extra event triggers. For experienced players, Unquiet Dead is a thoughtful increment that creates new narrative angles without turning the table into a logic puzzle that requires a degree in necromancy to solve.

> Snapshot glance: Unquiet Dead adds roughly 40 to 50 new cards, including fresh relatives with distinctive curses, new event cards that interact with the base deck in inventive ways, and a small handful of helpers that tilt the balance toward longer, more dramatic plays. The mood stays true to Gloom: theatrical, a bit gothic, and irresistibly punny.

### What’s in the box

The box, as with the core Gloom sets, is a tasteful blend of art and context. Inside you will find:

- A cadre of New Relatives: a mix of undead, spectral kin, and morally questionable heirs who carry their own unique modifiers. These characters are designed to layer onto your existing strategies or spark new ones entirely.
- Event Cards and Conditions: these cards add new plot twists and reversals so that even if you think you have the narrative arc under control, the deck reminds you that fate is a capricious editor.
- New Modifiers (Positive and Negative): these can transfer between characters or cling to a family line, enabling some surprising reversals and a few glorious wrecks of plans.
- Thematic Artwork and Flavor Text: Baker continues to deliver that signature blend of moody illustration and witty prose that makes a gloom night feel like a late-night theater show.

In short, Unquiet Dead expands the storytelling sandbox without demanding a new core rulebook. It respects the rhythm of base Gloom and offers players the chance to chase more intricate family sagas with a slightly larger cast and a few new levers to pull.

## Gameplay mechanics and how it changes the table

If you know Gloom, you know the core rhythm: place a tile of misfortune on your family, then kit out other family lines with modifiers to push your score down while trying to avoid tipping the scales into a catastrophe that helps your opponents. The Unquiet Dead expansion respects this structure and adds three key areas of evolution:

### 1) New Relatives, New Strategies
Relatives are the engine of Gloom, and Unquiet Dead gives you more to work with. The new relatives come with negative modifiers that stack in interesting ways and interact with existing family types to create surprising synergies. Some relatives reward you for a slow burn; others punish you for overreaching too soon. The effect is a more layered decision tree where you must weigh near-term gains against the longer, gloomier payoff. The net effect is a delicious tension: you want to plan three turns ahead but you must survive the immediate bad luck that your opponents will unleash on you to enrich the eventual payoff.

### 2) Event Card Interactions: The Plot Thickens
New events interact with both your new relatives and the old deck to deliver plot twists that can reorder the table’s fortunes. Some events accelerate the decline of a rival family, others create a momentary pause that forces everyone to recalibrate strategy mid-turn. The sense of drama is heightened when you realize that a single event can open a path to victory that previously seemed closed, provided you are willing to hedge your bets and risk a little more gloom in pursuit of a larger prize.

### 3) Positive Modifiers and Recovery Options
Gloom is famous (or infamous) for its heavy mood. Unquiet Dead introduces positive modifiers that can offset the inevitable doom in small but meaningful ways. These modifiers do not turn the game into sunshine and daisies, but they provide tactical options that reward clever play and help dip the mood from despair into a manageable melancholy. The balance between the new negative and positive modifiers is carefully tuned to preserve the core experience while offering new angles for players who crave complexity.

### The rhythm of play with the expansion
The expansion preserves the core turn structure. You draw, you play modifiers, you pass the turn, and the board shifts from one dramatic tableau to another. What changes is the tempo and the density of decisions. With more cards at your disposal, you will often encounter longer planning sequences, more potential misdirections, and the delightful feeling that any move might either sprint you toward victory or set you up for a spectacular misfortune later in the night. The pacing can be adjusted through house rules if your group prefers short, punchy rounds; otherwise, the expansion supports longer storytelling nights without forcing you to reconfigure the entire game.

### The psychology of doom and delight
Gloom is at its core a social experience. The humor is in the timing and the storytelling, not in the face of the misfortune itself. Unquiet Dead nudges the social dynamics in a direction that rewards narratively rich turns and pun-filled banter. If your table enjoys a little theatrical misfortune and the shared joy of watching each other dig deeper into a family saga, you will find that this expansion adds a new layer of character to the table that is both charming and strategically meaningful.
