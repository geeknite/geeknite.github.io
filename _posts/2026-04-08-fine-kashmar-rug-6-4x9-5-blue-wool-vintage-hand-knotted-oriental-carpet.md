## Materials, Craft, and Durability
### The Wool You Can Trust
Wool is the unsung hero of floor textiles. It’s resilient, naturally stain-resistant (to a degree), and has a memory that will outlive most of your tech gadgets. ### Hand-KnottIng: The Heartbeat of Authenticity
Hand-knotted rugs are the ones where the artisan ties thousands of individual knots per square inch. Each knot is a tiny promise that the rug will age gracefully, developing a richer patina rather than a fast-fashion face-plant. A high knot density translates to impressive durability and a more intricate design. If machine-made rugs are the cosplay of the rug world, hand-knotted rugs are the authentic-pretend-to-be-a-renaissance-painting type of art.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Daily Care Tips
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting. Avoid behemoth suction that rips the wool’s soul out of the fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with clean cloths; avoid rubbing which can push stain deeper into the fibers.
- Rotate every 6–12 months to prevent uneven wear and color fading caused by direct sunlight.

### Cleaning and Yearly Checkups
- Use a mild rug shampoo or a professional cleaning service every 1–3 years, depending on traffic. Vintage rugs deserve a spa day too, even if they don’t enjoy baths.
- Consider a rug pad to protect the fibers, reduce slippage, and improve overall comfort. A good pad is like a quiet wingman for your rug.

### Sunlight and Color Preservation
Prolonged exposure to sunlight can fade the indigo blues. If possible, position this rug away from direct sun or rotate it to preserve its youthful blue glow. A UV-protective window treatment could be your best friend here.

### Handling and Storage
When not in use, roll the rug (never fold) and store it in a cool, dry space. The careful storage will keep the wool from developing a sourpuss face and ensure it remains ready for your next interior design scheme.
