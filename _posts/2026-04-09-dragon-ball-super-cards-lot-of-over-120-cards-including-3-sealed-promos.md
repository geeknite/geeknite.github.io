### Card types and distribution in bulk lots

Dragon Ball Super Card Game cards tend to come in several core categories: Leaders, Battle cards, Extra cards, and various types of Z cards. In bulk lots, you will encounter a spectrum of these categories across common, uncommon, rare, and foil variants. A realistic distribution looks something like this:

- Leaders: a handful, typically 2 to 4 depending on the lot; these define the deck’s core strategy.
- Battle cards: the bulk of your pile; these are the ones that actually execute your plan in the arena.
- Extra cards: tools that provide effects, like removal, negates, and special play actions.
- Z or other special cards: a sprinkling that can alter the tempo of a match.
- Foils and promos: 1 to 5 items depending on the batch and promos included.

Keep an eye on leaders and a subset of powerful battle cards that synergize, rather than chasing raw numbers. A deck that plays well is often a deck that can flex between aggression and management of the opponent’s tempo.

### Sorting, storage, and preservation

With a bulk lot, organization is a non-negotiable. Invest in good binder sleeves and a few dividers. Label sections by rarity and by color to speed up deck-building sessions. Store the binder in a cool, dry place to minimize edge wear and avoid moisture that can degrade card stock over time. The simplest rule of thumb is to treat your treasured cards with care but not with excessive paranoia. The more you sort as you go, the faster future batch purchases will feel and the less distress you experience when you come across a silly misprint that somehow becomes your new favorite card artwork.
