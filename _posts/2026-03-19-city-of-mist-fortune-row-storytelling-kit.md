## Overview
City of Mist Fortune Row is a storytelling kit designed to slot into the City of Mist ecosystem and turn a night of roleplay into a live action crime noir with mythic overtones. The kit is built around a city district called Fortune Row, a place where deals are real and legends run on the same streets as neon signs. The idea is to give GMs a toolbox of prompts, character mashups, and scene seeds that can be dropped in for one shot sessions or stretched across a campaign arc. It sits alongside other City of Mist supplements that aim to weave investigative noir with mythic powers, where ordinary people awaken as legends of the modern world.

### The pitch and the vibe
Fortune Row wants to lean into two core ideas that make City of Mist feel unique: heroism is not about capes, it is about identity, and mythic power exists in the mundane cityscape. The kit promises to help players discover the myth they carry and to give the GM a set of hooks that feel cinematic without requiring a film budget. The tone lies somewhere between noir fiction and urban myth, with enough splash of wonder to spark a session that feels like a cross between a detective novel and a mythic fable.

### What is included
The box or digital bundle contains:
- A deck of prompt cards
- A set of archetype mashups
- A GM screen insert
- A map of Fortune Row
- An adventure hook pack
- Quick start guidelines for a three act session
- A short roster of pregenerated villains and allies
- A mood music suggestions sheet
- A wrap up reflection card for post game discussion

This is a lot of material for a kit that tries to keep things lean. The number feels right for a one night stand worth of sessions, and the mix of prompts with ready made characters can be a real timesaver when the party shows up with a plan to misbehave.

### How the prompts work
The heart of Fortune Row is not in the art direction alone but in the prompts that drive the session. Each prompt card pairs a local myth or urban legend with a modern occupation or role. The effect is not to force players into a strict binary hero/villain choice but to create a tension field where identity is a living thing. A player might be a street poet who embodies a legendary trickster, or a detective who channels a mythic guardian, or a bartender who is both confessor and oracle. The prompts encourage improvisation by offering a few pivot points at the end of a scene rather than a full script.

### Archetypes and mashups
One clever aspect is the cadre of archetypes that can be mashed in creative ways. Instead of presenting a stale set of hero classes, Fortune Row invites players to combine a personal myth with a modern job. The result can be something like a muralist who channels a living mural spirit or a courier who moves messages between factions as if they were living gossip. The mashups feel flexible enough to accommodate players who love deep lore and players who just want to roll dice and make clever lines.

### The GM experience
For the GM, the kit acts as a ready made sandbox. The Fortune Row map is more than pretty art; it is a working tool. It marks power nodes, faction lines, and rumor mills that your players can chase. The quick start guidelines explain how to stage scenes that feel cinematic without requiring a full cinematic budget. If you hate prepping, this kit is a balm for your scheduling anxiety. If you enjoy crafting your own flavor, you can still adapt the seeds into a bigger arc or weave them into an ongoing campaign.

### Visuals and production value
The components in the Fortune Row kit are thoughtfully designed. The art direction channels smoky downtown noir with a modern twist. The typography is crisp, the color palette is moody but legible, and the map has a level of detail that makes a table feel embedded in the city. The print quality on physical components is sturdy, and the digital assets render cleanly on a laptop or a tablet. The design choices support immersion and reduce the cognitive load on players who want to focus on story rather than color wheel decisions.

### Using Fortune Row with the core game
If you already own the City of Mist core rulebook, Fortune Row slides neatly into the engine without forcing a rewriting of rules. The kit expands options for themes, settings, and personal legends without altering the core mathematics of the game. It is not a replacement for the base rules but a layer that enriches the storytelling surface. For players who enjoy pushing the mythic identity concept, Fortune Row provides new angles on what a player is and what a city is willing to tolerate.

### How it plays in practice
We ran a session with a familiar group, a bunch of players who love noir movies but are equally into the mythic weirdness of the setting. The Fortune Row prompts yielded a set of driving questions that we used to guide scenes. The players found it easy to slot into the roles and to riff with the archetype mashups. We did not lock players into prewritten arcs; instead we let the prompts unlock a sense of place and urgency. The city felt alive, not because the map looked pretty, but because the prompts gave us both a reason to care and a reason to hurry.

### Strengths and potential drawbacks
On the plus side, Fortune Row excels at giving a GM a sense of place quickly. The prompts feel clever and the archetype mashups invite risk taking from players who worry about stereotypes. The mood sheet and ambient music suggestions are practical add ons that can elevate a session without requiring a playlist research project. On the downside, if you are chasing a plush campaign with epic long form arcs, Fortune Row might feel modular rather than linear. You get seeds rather than a full story; you get toolkits rather than a published act structure. That is not a failing so much as a design choice, but it is worth noting for planners who want a one night stand with a map to a three season arc.

### Accessibility and inclusivity
The kit makes an effort to include a broad range of identities and experiences in its prompts. The emphasis on urban myths and local legends can be navigated in many directions, which helps players who want to bring their own cultural stories to the table. There are prompts that are sensitive to power dynamics and prompts that avoid predatory tropes. The inclusive approach is not a guarantee of universal resonance, but it signals an intent to do better than a standard noir trope dump.

### Comparisons with other City of Mist materials
If you have previously explored other City of Mist supplements, you will notice a few patterns. Some kits lean into a fully fledged mystery arc, others lean into a toolbox of scenes that can be dropped into any night. Fortune Row resides somewhere in the middle: it provides strong anchor points and a strong sense of a single district while leaving room for improvisation. The result is less of a linear adventure and more of a living city module that grows with your table. Compared to a pure one shot deck, Fortune Row offers a long tail of reusability, and compared to a full campaign book, it is easier to digest and easier to customize.

### Practical tips for use at the table
- Start with a lane or rumor that matches the players interests and let the prompts do the legwork in the first act.
- Use the archetype mashups as character prompts when new players join late in the session; this helps them integrate quickly.
- Let the Fortune Row map guide your pacing; use location nodes to anchor scene transitions.
- Swap in personal legends from players to deepen engagement; the prompts can accommodate a wide range of myths.
- Don t forget ambient mood music; small touches compound immersion.

### A three act workshop outline
Act I opening hook and local flavor, Act II confrontation and shifting loyalties, Act III reveal and mythic payoff. The prompts give you a quick start for these acts and leave room for improvisation based on player choices. The result is a session that feels like a mini feature film with room to breathe between scenes.

### Variants and house rules
If your table likes to tweak the rules, Fortune Row stacks well with simple house rules. Want shorter sessions? Use a two act structure with a tighter map. Want a longer arc? Add an ongoing rumor mill that persists across sessions. The kit is designed to be a scaffold rather than a rigidity machine, so you can bend it to your table without breaking its mood.

### For players new to City of Mist
If you are new to the system, Fortune Row provides an accessible on ramp. The prompts function as a cognitive map and you can rely on pregenerated NPCs to help you get started. The core mechanics remain unchanged, so players can learn as they go. The kit does not overwhelm new players, it invites them to step into the mythic layer with a clear sense of what to do next in each scene.

### The city continues after the last session
Leftover threads from the Fortune Row run can become the seeds of the next session. The map and rumor mills are designed to persist, allowing you to thread a running subplot across nights. The sense that Fortune Row continues to exist when the GM lifts the table is the kit's subtle magic. You gain a sense that the city is watching and waiting for the next player choice, which is exactly the vibe this game aims to evoke.
