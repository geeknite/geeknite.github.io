## Build quality and assembly

The miniatures feel solid for price range. The plastics are rigid enough to survive, yet thin enough to avoid weight issues on small gaming tables. Details hold up after a basic wash.

### Plastic quality
These minis are mid range in plastic quality. They survive handling and light to moderate assembly. They are not the most delicate items on the market, but they do benefit from a careful approach.

### Assembly tips
- Dry fit before glue: test pose in hand
- Remove flash with hobby knife
- Warm water helps separate parts without stress
- Use super glue for permanent joins, standard glue for quick tests

### Painting readiness
Even casual painters can get good results. Base coat, wash, highlight, and basing can produce a satisfying finished look. The faces and weapons hold enough detail that you can practice shading for a realistic vibe or go fully cartoon for a more light hearted feel.

### Unboxing experience
The packaging is sturdy; internal trays keep figures separated during shipping. If you save the box for a future project, you will find it a nice starting kit.

## Aesthetics and design philosophy

The Season 1 minis keep the grim yet playful vibe. They look menacing at a distance but curious up close. The sculptors choose to emphasize character rather than ultra realism. That choice makes table top play friendlier to novices, while still providing enough flavor for veterans to appreciate the nuance in armament and stance.

### The design balance
The minis strike a balance between readability on a crowded board and subtle micro details that reward careful painting.

### Practicality on the board
The majority of poses are stable, with basing designed to keep figures anchored during board state changes.
