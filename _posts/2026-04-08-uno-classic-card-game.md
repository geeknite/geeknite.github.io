Here is a refreshed refresher, tailored for a modern crowd that wants to avoid rule-lawyering but still enjoys a good tense moment.\n\n### Setup and goal\n- Each player gets 7 cards. The rest forms the draw pile. The top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile. If that first card is an action card, the chaos level goes up from the start.\n- The goal is simple: be the first to empty your hand. When you get to one card, you must shout UNO. If you forget and someone spots it, the penalty is two extra cards. The suspense is delicious enough to make the family dog sit up and take notes.\n\n### Playing cards\n- On your turn, you must play a card that matches the color or the number of the top discard card. If you cannot play, you draw one card. If that card is playable, you may play it immediately.\n- Special cards shake things up:\n  - Skip: The next player is skipped.\n  - Reverse: Reverses play direction. In two-player games, this effectively switches who wants to win and who pretends they aren’t playing the long game.\n  - Draw Two: The next player draws two cards and loses their turn.\n  - Wild: Change the current color in play to any color you choose.\n  - Wild Draw Four: Change color and force the next player to draw four cards. The power move, if used wisely, but it can also earn you some stern glances if overused.\n- When you have one card left, you must say UNO. If another player sees you forget, the penalty is two cards. Social contracts matter here more than you might think.\n\n### House rules and variations\n\nUNO is a living ruleset. House rules are common enough that no two groups will have the exact same version. Some people insist on speaking in a pirate voice when playing a Wild card; others require you to bluff about your card to encourage a misplay. You can also add a play until you lose variant, where the last two or three players keep going until a dramatic finish. The most important thing is to keep the game moving and ensure everyone is having fun rather than turning the room into a tribunal for broken alliances. Popular house-rule twists include stacking Draw Two or Draw Four cards, the ability to "buy" a wild card with a special token, or random penalties for not shouting UNO with enough gusto.\n\n## Strategy, luck, and the art of not taking yourself too seriously\n\nUNO is not a deep strategic game; it is a social craft.
