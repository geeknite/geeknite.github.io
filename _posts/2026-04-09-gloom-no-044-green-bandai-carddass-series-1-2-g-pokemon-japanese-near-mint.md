## History of the Series 1 2 G and Gloom in Carddass context

Carddass Series 1 2 G represents a snapshot of Bandai's approach to collectible cards during late 1990s Japan. The cards were often sold through capsule vending machines; players would insert coins, turn a knob, and receive a little piece of plastic with art that sometimes matched the Pokemon franchise while other times embraced broader Bandai properties. The Gloom No 044 is part of that tradition: it is a collectible not simply for the Pokemon fan but for the vintage card enthusiast who loves to chase a specific print run and determine whether it was printed in a certain factory line or a separate batch.

In this era, Carddass cards were more accessible to casual collectors in Japan than many other trading card products. You could find them in arcades, small toy shops, and vending machines near the entrance to the local train station. The experience was part of the appeal: the thrill of spinning a tiny machine and hoping for a lucky print variant. The 1 2 G subset is particularly beloved because it combines classic card art with a printing style that has a tangible retro vibe.

From a collecting perspective, this card sits in the mid-range of interest. ## Authentication, grading, and care

### Authenticity checks
Gloom No 044 should be vetted by standard checks: card stock weight, print quality, and back design alignment. While Carddass prints do not have the same universal grading system as some modern cards, established graders look for consistency of color, texture, and print quality. Look for the Bandai Carddass stamp, the set symbol, and verify that the back pattern matches known authentic prints from the 1990s. If you are new to authentication, consult a reputable community resource or ask for a second pair of eyes in a collector's club. If you can confirm that the front art is clear, the back has uniform color, and there is no obvious edge wear or crease, you are likely in decent shape.

### Preservation and storage
Storage matters. Use sleeves designed for small trading cards, place the card in a binder page with stable tracking, and avoid direct sunlight. UV light can cause color fading that turns a vibrant green into a dull olive over decades. If you found this card in a protective sleeve from the era, you likely captured a small window into the past. A soft card protector also helps prevent edge wear, which is essential for maintaining Near Mint status.

### Grading vs. preserving value
Grading services can add a premium to a card, but Carddass mats and adhesives may complicate modern grading systems. If you plan to seek a grade from third-party services, ask about their compatibility with older Japanese cards and whether they have a specific category for Carddass prints. If the price of grading is close to the expected value, consider alternative options like certified appraisals or membership-based grading discussions in the community. For many collectors, preserving the card in a near mint state is more rewarding than chasing an elusive grade that may or may not yield a higher sale price later.
