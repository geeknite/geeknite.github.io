## Durability and Care: Keeping Your Rug Fresh in a Real-Life Flux Capacitor World
Wool deserves a good stewardship, not a covert mission. The Seafoam rug holds up well to everyday life, but like any natural fiber, it benefits from consistent care. Regular vacuuming with the beater bar off (to prevent fiber snagging) is your first line of defense. Spills should be blotted quickly with a clean, dry cloth; avoid vigorous rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers. For tougher stains, a mild dish soap solution and a gentle blot is usually sufficient; test a hidden corner first to ensure the color stays true.

Rotation is a forgotten trick that actually helps even out wear. If your room has heavy chair usage or foot traffic in a particular pattern, rotate the rug every few months to prevent obvious traffic lanes. It’s a tiny habit with a big payoff—the rug stays fresh-looking longer, and your guests won’t be able to spot the exact route you took to claim the prime couch corner.

Because this rug is wool, we advise against aggressive power-washing or soaking. Wool hates waterlogged situations; it prefers a gentle spa day: mild cleaning when necessary, quick drying, and careful storage when not in use. If you’ve got pets, consider a rug pad to reduce slippage and enhance resilience; it also helps with footfall across hard floors by offering a little extra “give”.

Care tips in a nutshell:
- Vacuum regularly with the suction height set to avoid pulling fibers.
- Blot spills immediately; don’t rub.
- Test stain removers in an inconspicuous spot.
- Use a rug pad to protect against wear and slippage.
- Consider professional cleaning if the rug takes on heavy stains or odors that DIY can’t fix.

## Where to Buy and How to Measure: A Practical Sizing Guide
To order, start with exact room measurements: length and width, including clearance around doors and furniture. If you’re dealing with an irregular room, create a quick diagram or use a simple grid to visualize how the rug will sit within the space. The aim is to find a proportion that feels rooted rather than floating. A well-sized rug unifies the space; a poorly sized rug can feel like a misplaced puzzle piece.

For those using custom dimensions, you’ll likely be asked for details such as pile height preference (for comfort underfoot), optional edge finishing (raw, serged, or bound), and the exact dims. Don’t skip the edge finish question—the finish can impact both aesthetics and durability, particularly in high-traffic zones or near entryways. If you’re unsure, ask for samples or a mood board from the retailer; it often helps you see how the color reads with your furniture fabric and wall color in different lighting.

## Geeknite Design Tips: Lighting, Layers, and Layouts
Lighting plays a surprising role in how Seafoam reads in a room. Warm bulbs can nudge the color toward a softer mint tint, while cool daylight tends to push it toward the seafoam-green spectrum. If you’re building a lighting plan, pair the rug with lamps or sconces that have adjustable color temperatures so you can switch from “studio in the morning” to “movie night in two hours” without repainting the room.

If you’re working with a smaller room, consider a smaller secondary rug as a layering piece to guide traffic and define seating areas without visually shrinking the room.
