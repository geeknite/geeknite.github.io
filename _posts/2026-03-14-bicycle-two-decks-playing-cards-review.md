## Maintenance and Longevity

### Keeping them in good shape

Maintenance is simple: keep them in their box when not in use, avoid exposing them to direct heat or moisture, and don’t stand on the cards during a dramatic moment of your performance. The two-deck setup makes it easier to rotate decks if you’re practicing a long routine, helping to prevent excessive edge wear on any one deck. If you notice a slight stickiness after spills, allow them to dry completely before use. No need for fancy cleaners—just air, patience, and a clean, dry surface.

### Longevity tips for serious hobbyists

- Rotate between red and blue decks to distribute wear evenly.
- Use a light dusting of talcum powder to reduce friction if you notice sticking during long sessions; wipe away any residue.
- Avoid exposing the cards to moisture or humidity; paper stock plus water is not a good combo.
- If you’re transporting them, consider a simple card clip or case to avoid bending in transit.
