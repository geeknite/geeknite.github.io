## Practical Performance: Durability and Cleaning
The true test of a rug is not how it looks in a showroom, but how it handles your everyday chaos. The Bodega Stone stands up well to foot traffic and the occasional spill. Wool fibers repel minor stains in a way that feels like the rug has an allergic reaction to coffee and red wine in a classy, non-hysterical manner. For more stubborn marks, blotting is your friend; avoid rubbing, which only spreads the stain and compels it to singe into your soul. If you’re worried about dirt, a quick vacuum across the pile keeps it looking crisp between deep cleans.

### Cleaning Hacks
- Blot, don’t rub. Blot, blot, blot. You’ll thank me later when your rug doesn’t look like a crime scene.
- For oil-based stains, apply a small amount of a gentle detergent solution and blot again. If you have a stain that seems to be auditioning for a soap opera, you might need a specialty rug cleaner.
- Deep cleaning every 12 to 18 months is a good rhythm for most households; if you have pets, you might push to every 9 months.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
The life of a rug is a long story of sun, shadow, and a friend’s dog who believes the living room is its personal spa. To keep the Bodega Stone looking fresh, rotate it every few months to promote even wear. Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting and avoid the beater bar if you’re worried about pulling loops. The backings are sturdy and, with proper padding, will extend the life of the rug for many, many seasons of couch talk.

If you’re concerned about fraying at the edges, you can consider edge-binding care. It’s a small investment that buys you peace of mind when the kids use the area as a pretend skate ramp.

## Practical Setup: Placement Tips

Placement also matters for acoustics. A rug can soften the sound of clattering dishes and the occasional remote control clicking. The Bodega Stone’s muted color helps the space feel calmer, even when you realize you’ve left your laptop open with 17 tabs on your screen.

## Comparisons: Why Choose Hand Tufted Over Machine-Made
If you’re a perfectionist with a strange obsession for symmetry in every fiber, you’ll notice differences between hand-tufted and machine-made rugs. The hand-tufted version has slight irregularities in the tufting that give the rug more character. It’s not perfect in a robotic way; it’s perfect in a human way. ## Quick Guide to Maintenance and Care

## FAQ: Quick Answers for Curious Nerds
- Is hand-tufted better than hand-knotted? Hand-tufted is generally faster and more cost-effective, with a plush surface. Hand-knotted is often more intricate and durable but comes with a higher price tag.
- Can I install this rug on carpet? Yes, but you’ll want a high-quality rug pad to prevent sliding and to ensure the pile wears evenly.
- Is wool ethical? Reputable brands often source wool from responsible suppliers; check the retailer’s sustainability statements for more details.
- How long will it last? With proper care, a well-made hand-tufted rug can last a decade or more depending on foot traffic and pets.
