## Handling and care: what you need to know to preserve the magic

Caring for a vintage UNO deck is a lot like caring for a vintage vinyl record. You want clean hands, a dust-free playing surface, and a stable climate. Here are practical tips to keep your 1978 DELUXE UNO in top shape:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV can fade those neon colors faster than you can say “UNO.”
- Use protective sleeves or a card cabinet with divider trays. A tidy deck is a deck that’s easier to shuffle without bending corners.
- If a card gets bent, avoid the classic “press under a heavy cookbook” approach. Instead, place the deck under a light stack and let gravity do the gentle realignment, or use a card flattening tool designed for gaming cards.
- For minor yellowing, consider a gentle wipe with a soft microfiber cloth. Do not soak the cards; moisture is a villain here.
- When sorting, preserve the original color groups. It helps reproduction games or any future re-roll of the rules look faithful and official-feeling.

## Practical care tips: getting the most out of your investment

- Maintain humidity in your play area. Cards love a stable climate; a damp basement is not their friend.
- When you’re not playing, keep the box closed and the deck seated in a dry, elevated area to prevent warping.
- Use a card holder or a small organizer to keep suits separated during sorting. The more you sort, the more you’ll appreciate the color language of the deck.
- If a card gets stiff after years of storage, flex it gently near the edge on a clean surface to loosen it up—do not force bend it or you’ll risk tearing.
- For display, consider a transparent card binder or glass cabinet to show off the 1970s art while keeping the deck safe from sunlight and curious pets.
