## Card at a Glance
### Card Fundamentals
Erika's Gloom is an Uncommon from Gym Heroes, numbered 046 out of a 132-card set. This is a pre-foil era print, where non-holo silhouettes ruled the landscape and the card art did most of the talking while the moves quietly whispered in the flavor text. It’s a small relic: the kind of card that quietly reminds you that the game’s heart once beat to the rhythm of a garden, a nurse’s station, and a gym leader who wore a smile that said, You’ll get there, kid—just not today.

- Type: Grass / Poison (as Gloom typically is in the era’s dual-typed world). 
- HP: A modest figure by today’s standards, optimized for nostalgia rather than ridiculous tankiness. Think something in the neighborhood of the common 40-50 HP mark that defined early 1999/2000 card design.
- Attacks/Text: The card text showcases a classic early-attacker vibe with a pollen-based aggression that feels like a botanical version of a Dragon-type roar, but with more gradual effect and less glare. It’s the kind of attack that makes you think, This card was designed for a different tempo: slower, more deliberate, and very much about the flavor of Erika’s gym garden rather than raw power.
- Retreat Cost / Weakness / Resistance: The basic-era balance notes show a retreat cost that’s easy on the fingers and a weakness aligned with common Grass types—just enough to encourage strategic thinking without turning the card into a wall of frustration. Resistance isn’t the star here; the charm is the storytelling of a trainer-led encounter in a card form.

### Artwork and Flavor Text
The Erika voice in the card’s art is not loud and punchy; it’s a garden party with a deck of possibilities. Erika’s Gloom is framed by flower beds, vines, and maybe a strategically placed Bewear of aroma that would make a non-inked bleed of the breeze. Gloom is drawn with a certain patient mischief—the kind you only notice after you read the flavor text aloud to your friends who pretend not to care, then promptly demand a turn in the holo binder rotation. The card embodies a moment when the game’s designers leaned into a distinct sense of place: a gym’s greenhouse, with Erika presiding like a calm botanist who happens to love pocket monsters as much as pruning shears.

The card’s typography—simple, clean, and legible—harks back to a time when you could almost hear the binder crackle with gum-stained pages the moment you drew Erika’s Gloom. It’s not flashy, but it is confident; a quiet wink to players who know that not every win needs to be earned on the field, sometimes it’s earned in the garden of your imagination.

- Condition Matters: Since Erika’s Gloom is not a holo, its value is strongly tied to its condition. Expect corners, edge wear, and surface gloss to influence price significantly. A near-mint copy will outperform a mid-condition piece in most collectors’ eyes.
- Binder vs. Slab: If you’re chasing nostalgia, a well-protected binder piece might be better than a graded copy for casual display. Graded Erika’s Gloom is rarer but not always the best “value” for every collector’s wallet. Consider your goals: display or investment, both have their charms.
- Grading Tips: If you’re planning to send a copy for grading, ensure gentle handling of the corners and avoid creases in the card text area. A clean, centered print with crisp edges tends to fetch higher grades.
- Budgeting for a Theme: If you’re designing a Gym Heroes-themed display, Erika’s Gloom can anchor that look without breaking the bank. It offers narrative weight that holo rares might not provide, making it a solid storyteller’s centerpiece in a vintage-themed shelf.
