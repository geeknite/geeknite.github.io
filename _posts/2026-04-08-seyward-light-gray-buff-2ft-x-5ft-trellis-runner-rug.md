### Placement Tips

1. Measure the path: ensure at least 6–8 inches of visible floor on either end. The goal is balance, not a rug that reads as an afterthought.
2. Check door clearance: doors should clear the rug; otherwise, you’ll be sliding the edge up when you try to open the door. A simple fix is to place the rug slightly inboard rather than flush with the door gap.
3. Use door mats: a small entry mat at one end can frame the runner and make breadcrumbs and pet hair a distant memory.

## Maintenance Hacks for Frazzled Lifestyles

Even the most stylish rug needs a little maintenance to stay fresh. Here are a few practical hacks that work in real homes without turning your weekend into a rug repair workshop:

- Vacuum weekly with a light suction setting to avoid pulling fibers. For high-traffic zones, consider a quick pass every other day.
- Address spills immediately. Blot with a clean cloth, don’t rub. For stubborn stains, use a mild detergent diluted with water.
- Rotate the rug seasonally to balance wear. This is especially useful in entries that see heavy traffic from guests and pets.
- Use door mats at both ends if the rug sits under door paths; this reduces grit transfer that can dull the pattern over time.
- Consider a rug pad for added cushion and grip. The pad improves underfoot feel and prevents slippage on slick floors.
