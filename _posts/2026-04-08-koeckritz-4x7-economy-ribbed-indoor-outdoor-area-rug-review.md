## Installation Tips and Maintenance Tips: Keeping It Fresh
- Use a rug pad for smoother surface and extra grip on wood or tile floors. It also reduces wear and helps with noise reduction when walking in high-traffic zones.
- For routine cleaning, a light vacuum or a quick rinse with a hose and mild detergent is usually enough. Let it air-dry completely before placing furniture back on top to prevent any mildew concerns.
- To maintain color and texture, rotate the rug every few months if you have strong sun exposure on one side. This prevents uneven fading and helps the fibers wear evenly over time.
- Blot spills rather than rubbing to avoid pushing liquids deeper into the pile. For stubborn stains, use a gentle upholstery cleaner and a soft brush, then rinse and dry.
- When storing for the off-season, roll it with the underside facing inward to minimize creasing and protect the fibers from dust buildup.
