## The Basics

### What are the Dwarfen Mountain Holds?

The Dwarfen Mountain Holds are the fortified homes of the dwarfs in the Warhammer Old World setting. These sprawling underground cities are filled with wondrous forges, glittering treasure vaults, and enough mead to make even the most stoic dwarf crack a smile. But they are more than just homes; they are symbols of the dwarfs' enduring spirit and resilience in the face of constant threats.

### The Dwarf Runesmith

Ah, the Dwarf Runesmith. This venerable artisan is the heart and soul of any Dwarfen Hold. With their mastery of rune magic, Runesmiths can inscribe powerful enchantments on weapons and armor, making them invaluable in both crafting and combat. And let's face it, who wouldn't want a beard as majestic as theirs?

## Lore and History

### The Origins

The Dwarfen Mountain Holds trace their origins back to the dawn of the Old World. Forged from the very rock of the mountains, these holds have stood the test of time and countless sieges. Each hold is a testament to the craftsmanship and determination of the dwarfs who built them.

### Notable Holds

#### Karaz-a-Karak

Known as the Everpeak, Karaz-a-Karak is the capital of the Dwarf empire. Its grand halls and impregnable defenses make it a symbol of dwarf unity and strength.

#### Karak Eight Peaks

Once a mighty hold, Karak Eight Peaks has seen its fair share of battles and sieges. Though currently overrun by greenskins, it remains a symbol of the dwarfs' unyielding spirit.

## Gameplay Mechanics

### Building Your Hold

In Warhammer The Old World, players can recreate the majesty of the Dwarfen Mountain Holds through detailed miniatures and strategic gameplay. The key to success lies in balancing resource management, defense planning, and, of course, crafting the finest runic gear.

### Runesmith Abilities

Runesmiths bring a unique flavor to the game with their rune magic. From enhancing weapons to providing powerful buffs, these abilities can turn the tide of battle. But beware, for the path of the Runesmith is fraught with challenges and requires careful planning.
