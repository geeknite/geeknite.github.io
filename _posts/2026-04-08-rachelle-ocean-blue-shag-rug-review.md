## Materials, Durability, and Maintenance
### What It’s Made Of
The Rachelle Ocean Blue Shag Rug leans into the best of both worlds: plush comfort and practical materials. The pile is a blend of synthetic fibers designed to balance softness with durability. The backing is a sturdy synthetic weave that holds the rug in place without requiring you to become a geomancer to keep it from creeping across the floor. The material choices also aim to resist staining and matting in daily life. If you have a clumsy coffee tendency or a world-class snack habit while watching late-night anime, you’ll appreciate the rug’s resilience.

### Cleanability and Care Routine
Cleaning is a practical concern for any shag rug. The big question is whether you’ll ever want to deep-clean this thing, or if you’ll simply accept that a heroic amount of foot traffic will build a patina that looks like character rather than wear. For most households, a routine of vacuuming on a low-pile setting and a quick weekly shake-out works well. For spills, treat the area quickly with blotting and a mild cleaning solution recommended by the manufacturer. Avoid hot water or aggressive agitation, which can disturb the pile’s shape. If you’re into the science of stain resistance, you’ll be pleased that the rug’s fibers have a degree of resilience that helps keep most household messes manageable.

### Durability in the Real World
In a busy living room or a game night den, durability is the name of the game. The rug holds up to foot traffic, pet traffic, and occasional furniture shoves with a quiet dignity. If you’re the type who drags chairs across the floor to reconfigure the seating layout, you’ll want to use felt pads. The rug’s pile is forgiving, but heavy furniture can flatten portions over time if left in place. Rotating the rug every few months helps maintain even wear and keeps the blue from looking patchy. The result is a rug that not only survives daily use but ages gracefully, like a fine wine, only with more feet and less tannin.

## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity in Real Homes
### Everyday Spills and Recovery Time
Spills happen. Coffee, tea, snack crumbs, pet mishaps — the usual. The key is to act fast and avoid heavy scrubbing. Blot with a clean cloth, work gently from the outside toward the center to minimize spreading, and let the fibers recover. Given the rug’s pile height, some stains can be less forgiving than others, but the overall fiber resilience helps reduce the look of wear. A light vacuuming on a regular schedule will help maintain that fluffy surface and avoid the dreaded matting effect in high-traffic zones. If you’re a pet owner, there’s a small risk of fur becoming embedded in long fibers, but a good shake-out and a weekly vacuum typically keeps this in check.

### Long-Term Maintenance and Storage
For long-term storage or seasonal changes, you can rotate the rug and store it carefully to minimize flattening. If you decide to roll or fold the rug temporarily, avoid tight creases that can set into the fibers. When you re-unroll, give the rug a gentle shake and a light vacuum to help restore the nap. With proper care, this rug can remain a beloved centerpiece for years, aging with grace and continuing to deliver that sense of soft, cozy magic.
