## Authenticity, Condition, and the Seal Talk
Because this is marketed as NEW SEALED from 2015, there are the usual buyer checks you should consider to ensure you don’t end up with a compromised artifact instead of a pristine collectible. The seal’s integrity is the first thing to verify. A seal that looks unbroken from all sides indicates that the deck likely hasn’t had any opening attempts, but a seal that looks suspiciously repetitive or has unusual markings is a red flag worth noting. Second, examine the tuck’s edges. Sharp corners and vivid color are good signs; dullness or a slightly rough edge hints at aging or handling that could reveal a used status rather than a new sealed one. Finally, check the inner glue if visible through the tuck. It shouldn’t smell strongly; a chemical-like odor would be a cause for pause. These checks aren’t a guarantee of authenticity but a good practice to minimize regret when you’re paying a premium for “new sealed.”
