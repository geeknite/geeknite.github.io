### Card types and role in the game
- Defuse cards that save you from explosion, usually the most valuable asset in your hand
- Exploding kitten cards that must be avoided at all costs
- Attack cards that force the next player to take extra turns
- Skip cards that allow you to dodge one draw
- See the Future cards that reveal the top few cards in the deck
- Shuffle cards that scramble the draw order
- Nope cards that can cancel an action from someone else
- Favor cards that swap cards with another player

## The core loop: play, draw, react
At its core Exploding Kittens is a quick loop game: play a card, draw until your turn ends or you get something undesirable, react to the reactions of others. It is a social mechanism as much as a mechanical one. You watch the table, gauge the mood, and decide whether to push your luck now or save your defuse for a moment when your life depends on it.

One of the clever elements is how the table atmosphere evolves as players become more invested. The first few hands are about learning who is playing cautiously, who is bluffing about the strength of their hand, and who is secretly praying for a resource that will help them defuse a hot moment. As you progress, the table grows louder, faster, and more unpredictable, which is exactly the energy a party game should deliver.
