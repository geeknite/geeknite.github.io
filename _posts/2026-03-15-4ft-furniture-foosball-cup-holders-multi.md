A common mistake is to over-tighten the goal posts—those poor little nets will warp your day if they are too tense.

## Maintenance and care tips

- Cleaning: Wipe down the surfaces with a gentle cleaner to avoid staining the laminate. Use a microfiber cloth to prevent micro-scratches.
- Lubrication: Occasionally lubricate the rods for smoother movement; avoid over-lubrication that could cause a slippery feel or attract dust.
- Ball replacement: If you lose the little balls to the couch or your brother-in-law’s dog, replace them with standard foosball balls that match the size. They are cheap, but a set of 16 standard balls ensures you won’t run out mid-game.
- Cup holders: Clean the cup holders with a damp cloth; you do not want leftover sugar granules in the recess when someone makes a fast shot.
- Storage and placement: If you are not using the table, cover it to keep dust off and to prevent curious pets from investigating the ball return mechanism.
