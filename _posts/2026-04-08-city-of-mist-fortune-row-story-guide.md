## Introduction

Welcome to the neon soaked backstreets where superstition rapes reality with glitter and grit. Fortune Row is less a street name and more a mood board for City of Mist players and GMs. It is a pocket of the city where legends whisper through flickering signs, where cash rules the corners, and every rumor might be a narrative key. This guide aims to help you run Fortune Row smoothly, whether you are gaming online or around a table that smells faintly of pizza and unfulfilled dreams. Let us dive into the vibes, the hooks, the NPCs, and the mysteries that make Fortune Row sing in a chorus of noir and myth.

![Fortune Row dusk]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/fortune-row-dusk.jpg)

### Why Fortune Row matters in City of Mist
Fortune Row is not just a place, it is a story engine. Its twisted alleys, casino glow, and secret rooms are triggers for tragic pasts and glittering futures. It rewards clever investigators, bold masks, and teams that blend street smarts with mythic power. For GMs, Fortune Row offers compact acts, a set of recurring factions, and a cast of faces that can be swapped to suit your table size. For players, it promises personal stakes that echo through the city and opportunities to shine as the legends they are becoming.

## Setting the mood

Fortune Row thrives on contrasts: rain on chrome, saints in satire, and promises that feel like a bet you should refuse but cannot. The aesthetic sits somewhere between late night talk shows, a seedy arcade, and a temple of forgotten miracles. The lighting should feel painterly and slightly unreal, with signs that promise luck while offering a warning that the house always remembers your name. When running this area, lean into sensory detail: the clack of coins, the hum of neon, the creak of old piano in a backroom, the sting of cheap perfume, and the metallic taste of a city that forgives nothing and forgets nothing.

## Core concepts and themes

- Fortune and fate vs free will: Myths and machines collide in a city that loves to bet on outcomes.
- Trust and betrayal: Alliances form quickly, but loyalties shift as masks reveal hidden histories.
- The price of glory: Characters gain power as members of Fortune Row remind themselves what they must pay to keep it.
- Community and conflict: Fortune Row is a neighborhood of hustlers, dreamers, and reluctant heroes who share a single, fragile skyline.

## Quick start for GMs

1. Establish the Row as a living organism: a handful of strongholds (a casino, a temple, a storefront cafe, a back-alley club) and a web of rumors that connect them.
2. Assign three recurring factions: a benevolent if chaotic charity group, a gilded crime ring with a fancy name, and a mysteriously benevolent mask that grants luck at a cost.
3. Give each player a personal stake tied to Fortune Row: a debt, a revelation, a lost family member, or a secret that could topple a myth.
4. Begin with a tight hook: a festival night when the Row shakes with laughter and danger and a single event reveals the truth beneath the glitter.

## The Hook: opening threads you can drop in this week

- A lucky night turns sour when a winner disappears along with the casino vault key. The Row insists it was an inside job, but the clues point toward a mythic patron who can erase memory.
- A masked performer in the backroom club reveals a prophecy that ties a PC’s mask to Fortune Row itself. It is a blessing and a warning, wrapped in a grin.
- A charity gala becomes a stage for a ritual that might summon a new Shape of Fortune. The ritual must be disrupted before the crowd realizes their own potential for ruin.

## Cast of Characters: the Faces of Fortune Row

- The Broker of Luck: an enigmatic facilitator who knows how to turn a secret into an advantage. Neutral on paper, deeply loyal or ruthlessly pragmatic in practice.
- The Velvet Cardinal: a philanthropic leader whose charity programs look generous but carry an exclusive price tag. Powerful, well connected, and suspiciously well protected.
- The Glitter Inquisitor: a performer with the gift to reveal a truth that others don t want to hear. Charismatic, dangerous, and blessed with more luck than sense.
- The Watcher in the Reflections: an observer who seems to appear at the exact moment a choice is made, then disappears into a mirrored corridor.

Each NPC carries a small motive tied to Fortune Row. Give them a single desire that drives their actions and a vulnerability that can be exploited by clever players. The best scenes often hinge on a character s secret or a hidden agenda rather than a flashy action sequence.

## Key Locations in Fortune Row

- The Neon Crossroads: a plaza wrapped in holographic gardens where people barter stories for coins. This is where rumors begin and often where investigations start.
- The Velvet Club: a backroom oasis where luck is allegedly manufactured. If you listen carefully, the piano tells stories of debts and bargains.
- The House of Mirrors: a temple-ish space where a single reflection can reveal a future or a past. Mirrors may trap you in a moment you wish to forget.
- The Luxe Street Market: stalls that glow with cheap magic and overpriced miracles. A great place to acquire unusual items or to pick up an unrelated but useful clue.
- The Vault Room: hidden behind a false wall in the casino, this space holds assets not meant for public view. Access is guarded by a puzzle, not a guard.

## Plot beats and pacing: a three act weave

### Act I – The Spark
- Hook the players with a personal stake that ties to Fortune Row.
- Establish the tone and the stakes, then reveal a complication: a rumor that someone within the Row is breaking the city s most sacred rules.
- Introduce a clue that seems inconsequential but becomes essential later.

### Act II – The Build
- The Row s factions begin to move. The players must navigate alliances and deception, choosing sides or making their own path.
- A revelation changes the game: the prophecy in the Velvet Club has bearing on at least two PCs, possibly the entire party.
- A false ally betrays the team, forcing a recalibration of plans and alliances.

### Act III – The Reckoning
- The true nature of Fortune Row is laid bare. The players confront the myth behind the Row and the price of its gifts.
- A choice is made: save the Row by accepting a cost, or dismantle the myth and risk tearing the city apart.
- Resolution comes with a personal consequence for each PC, ensuring the myth remains a living legend rather than a neat ending.

## Clues, mysteries, and investigations

- Coin of Fate: a token that turns up in various places, each time revealing a fragment of a larger secret when held up to a light.
- The Prophecy Grail: a tattered parchment that hints at a future event that may shift the balance of power in Fortune Row.
- A Mirror Registry: a ledger that records who saw which reflection at which time; it hints at who is plugged into the Row s rules and who is playing the role of the watcher.
- A Debt Ledger: each name tied to a debtor, listing their sins and the price they paid for a favor.

Investigations should weave together these clues with the PCs masks. The city itself is a clue machine; keep the threads interlocking and let the players decide which thread to pull. Use misdirection and red herrings as a tool to keep the noir vibe alive without frustrating the players with obvious traps.

## Scenes and encounters: a quick encounter table

- A one hour in the Neon Crossroads where a performer reveals a truth only a mask wearer could recognize.
- A tense negotiation in the Vault Room that ends with a dramatic reveal and a new clue.
- A chase through a mirrored corridor in the House of Mirrors that ends with a choice that shapes a PC s mask.
- A quiet conversation in the Luxe Street Market where a vendor offers a miracle cure for a price in a currency you cannot name.

Each scene should push the narrative toward the bigger questions of fortune, cost, and identity. Keep scenes modular so you can scale up or down depending on player engagement and table time.

## Mechanics notes: running Fortune Row in style

- Masks and moves: emphasize the manipulation of fate through masks. The GM can grant a temporary advantage that mirrors a mythic gift but at the cost of a future setback.
- Clues and investigations: treat clues as unlockable patterns rather than single items. Each pattern reveals a facet of the Row and its patrons.
- Factions and loyalties: factions should be humanized with flaws and opportunities for player-driven negotiation.
- Time pressure: use the festival or a countdown to escalate tension and highlight the fragile nature of Fortune Row s power.

## Mood, tone, and vibe: keeping it sharp

Fortune Row thrives on a balance of glamor and grit. The neon lights should feel almost sacred and the alleyways should feel like a puzzle with teeth. The tone should be a respectful homage to noir while embracing the mythic energy of City of Mist. Remember that it is okay for players to fail to solve a mystery on the first try; in this world, failure is often a step toward a deeper revelation.

## NPC stat ideas: simple and effective

- Broker of Luck: presence 3, speed 2, manipulation 4, defense 2; has a web of contacts and a knack for reading between lines.
- Velvet Cardinal: presence 4, charisma 5, manipulation 3, defense 2; carries an aura of trust that can be exploited or leveraged.
- Glitter Inquisitor: presence 3, insight 4, manipulation 2; excels at exposing truth through spectacle.
- Watcher in the Reflections: presence 2, wits 3, stealth 4; steps from every doorway and appears just when a plan is about to hatch.

These are starter guidelines. Feel free to tweak them for table balance or to align with your players masks and the level of myth you want to explore.

## How to present Fortune Row to players

- Start with a visual: describe the neon glow, the rain, the scent of fortune cookie powder, the open invitation of the street who promises luck and delivers a price.
- Give each PC a personal stake that can be traced back to Fortune Row s mythos. This ensures investment and momentum for the narrative.
- Introduce at least two factions early and give each a visible goal, a believable motive, and a vulnerability that a clever team can exploit or exploit against.
- Use mirrors and reflections as storytelling devices to reveal inner truths and hidden agendas.

## Story arcs and themes you can explore in Fortune Row

- Arc of the Mask: players discover that their masks are not disguises but keys that unlock a portion of Fortune Row s own story. The reveal reshapes who they are and how the Row views them.
- Arc of the Price: every boon from Fortune Row has a price. The price may be paid by someone else or by the机 player after a choice, but the debt always comes due.
- Arc of the City: the Row is a microcosm of the city s larger politics. The players must decide how to influence Fortune Row and whether to preserve its power or reform it from within.

## One shot vs a campaign thread

Fortune Row works well as a standalone session with a strong ending that leaves the door open for future adventures, or as a prologue to a longer arc in City of Mist. If you are running a campaign, consider layering in recurring visits to the Row with evolving stakes and shifting alliances.

## Art direction and vibes

- Use bold color palettes with neon blues and hot pinks to evoke a city that glitters and bites.
- Sound design tips: street sounds like distant horns, the clink of metal coins, a piano riff that repeats with slight variation.
- Costume ideas: characters lean into mythic motifs rather than literal armor. Think a scarf that looks like a currency symbol or a ring that resembles a lucky token.

## Final notes and GM tips

- Flexibility is king. Fortune Row should bend to your table s energy, not the other way around. If a player wants a different fate, lean into it and redesign the mystery on the fly.
- Use player choices to reveal myth: every choice should feel meaningful and have a ripple effect on the Row and the city s broader mythology.
- Respect the noir tone while sprinkling moments of awe. The blend of cynicism and myth should feel natural, not forced.
- Endings can be bittersweet, and that is fine. The city keeps moving, and Fortune Row remains a locus where luck and fate meet in a crowded, glittering room.

## Final recommendation

Fortune Row is a tight, potent slice of Myths and Mob City that shines when players are offered strong personal stakes and a handful of memorable NPCs with real motivation. If you love noir with a sprinkle of myth, this location is a must run. It rewards preparation and improvisation in equal measure and leaves players with a sense that fortune is something you chase, not something you catch. The Row may be dangerous, but it is also delicious and full of possibility.

### Quick tips for running Fortune Row at the table

- Start with a big reveal in the first 15 minutes to set the stakes.
- Give each PC a personal thread that ties to a meme of Fortune Row and a myth of their own mask.
- Use mirrors and reflections as narrative devices to reveal truth rather than violence.
- Keep the pace brisk but allow players to linger on moments that matter to their characters.
- End with a choice that will echo into future sessions, not just a clean victory.
