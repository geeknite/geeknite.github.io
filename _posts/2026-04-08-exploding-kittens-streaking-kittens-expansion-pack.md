## Strategy and Tactics: How to Master the Chaos
Let’s be honest: Exploding Kittens rewards emotional intelligence as much as card-reading ability. The Streaking Kittens expansion adds new levers for misdirection and timing. Here are practical strategies you can test at your next game night:

### Beginner Tips
- Learn the Streak cards quickly: identify which plays can trigger cascades and when to back off. In early rounds, prioritize survival and seeing where other players’ tension builds.
- Watch body language and table chatter: often the best tells come from the conversations around the table rather than the cards in your hand.
- Use bluffing sparingly: the more chaotic the table, the easier it is to pull the wool over someone’s eyes. Don’t overcomplicate it; sometimes a simple misdirect is enough.

### Intermediate Tips
- Pair Streak cards with defensive choices: you can plan to weather a cascade by building a clutch refusal or a protective move for a neighbor about to explode.
- Time your big plays: the longer you wait, the more everyone expects the meltdown. Early moves can be just as effective if they force a poor decision later on.
- Leverage others’ risk tolerance: in groups with mixed risk appetite, you’ll see some players aggressively chase the endgame while others conservative. Exploit that gap with a well-timed Streak trigger.

### Advanced Tips
- Create micro-chains: if you can engineer a small cascade that benefits you without triggering an immediate counter from a nearby rival, you’ll enjoy a pleasant swing. It’s a game of inches that rewards careful reading of the room.
- Manage table tempo: if the table becomes too noisy, you can slow the pace to give yourself a moment to re-evaluate the board. Narrate your thoughts in a light, humorous way; it adds to the fun and keeps pressure off your own decisions.
- Use house rules to tune chaos: if needed, you can implement a soft limit on the number of Streaks in play at once or designate a cap on the number of cascade cards in the middle of the table.
