## Limited play: how Obsessive Stitcher behaves in a draft

Draft formats reward tempo, synergy, and a willingness to gamble on what you will draw next. Obsessive Stitcher in Limited is a card that demands you think a few turns ahead: if you manage to keep a graveyard around and you can safely resurrect or re-use a key piece late in the game, you will feel like you have performed a small miracle with thread and glue. In practice, you want to pair this card with fellow graveyard-friendly picks that let you sac, recycle, or reenter the battlefield.

In a two-color or three-color pool, this card can anchor a theme: black is usually the color of graveyard matter in Core Set 2021, and Obsessive Stitcher acts as a flexible engine that benefits from a steady supply of fodder. Your best bet is to look for means to both fill and draw from the graveyard in a way that advances your tempo while not giving your opponents a reason to accelerate their own wins.

Drafting Tip: Keep an eye out for synergy cards that discard cards from your hand while shoring up removal and defense. A card that forces you to sacrifice a creature while enabling reanimation can be a strong two-step that allows you to grind value. Remember, in Limited every card is a piece of the mosaic, but this card is a piece with a thread running through it, tying the rest of your deck together.

## Constructed potential: where Obsessive Stitcher shines

In Constructed, the question becomes: does this card enable a deck to actually win on the board or does it merely provide a flavorful engine that looks cool on turn 6? The honest answer is that Obsessive Stitcher can support a strategy that loves gravely memories—reanimating, reusing, and royalty-free replays of creatures that once existed in your memory of the battlefield.

A typical strategy would be to pair Obsessive Stitcher with other graveyard-synergistic cards that do not rely on one single engine to win. Instead, you chain small plays: you bring back a creature from the graveyard, peck away at the opponent's life total with recurrent threats, and gradually accumulate value as your graveyard grows. The card may also slot into a death-and-rebirth or reanimation suite where the goal is to keep a creature on the battlefield longer than your opponent expects.

One of the joys of this kind of card is the feeling of gray-scale control: you decide when to reanimate, you decide what to bring back, and you decide how to respond to removal. It does not promise the world, but it promises thoughtful play, careful sequencing, and the satisfaction of turning scraps into a tapestry of threats.

If you like Sultai or Golgari-themed decks in modern formats, Obsessive Stitcher can slot in as a flexible engine that helps you cut through the late-game grind. It may not always be the star of the show, but it is the kind of card that shines when you build a deck that leans into repeated value rather than brute force.

Rule of thumb: the more resilient your graveyard is, the more this card will shine. If you are someone who loves to cut in with a few extra bodies, this card rewards you by letting your engine keep producing value long after the initial coup de grâce.

## Deck ideas and synergy ideas: patchwork and threads

- Graveyard recursion engine: Combine Obsessive Stitcher with cards that let you sacrifice and return creatures from your own graveyard. The synergy is not about one massive combo; it is about building a resilient pipeline that keeps giving you bodies.
- Endless fodder for removal: Some removal spells thrive on having targets; if you can generate a steady stream of cheap bodies, your removal becomes safer and more efficient.
- Reclaiming value: Cards with reanimation or ETB effects often become more valuable when you have a Stitcher-like engine in place. You get to choose which piece you want to reuse to threaten the board.
