## How Phase 10 Works: A Quick Rules Overview
Phase 10 is a race to complete a series of ten phases, which are combinations of cards such as sets, runs, or mixed collections. The basic flow goes like this:

- Each player is dealt a starting hand, usually 10 cards per person, with the rest forming a draw pile and a discard pile in the middle. The air in the room gets thicker as the plan to complete the first phase becomes everyone personal mission number one.
- On your turn, you draw one card from either the draw pile or the discard pile, then you discard one card to end your turn. The loop continues until a player completes the current phase and goes out by discarding the last card. The path from phase 1 to phase 10 is a magical journey of careful planning and last-minute card flips.
- Phase 1 is usually a simple two sets of three, Phase 2 adds a run, and by Phase 10 you are juggling multiple constraints that would have intimidated a chess grandmaster with a water balloon in a rainstorm.

In the 2 pack, the only real difference is scale. You now have two decks to manage, which means more opportunities to accidentally take a card away from yourself or accidentally steal a card from your sister-in-law who swears she has never played a card game in her life but somehow knows the exact moment you need a red card for your run. The game remains delightfully punishing and equally charming, which is a good reason to keep a sense of humor handy and a spare cup of coffee near by.

### Key Concepts to Keep in Mind
