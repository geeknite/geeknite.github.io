## The Core Mechanics: Flux and Covenant

Flux and Covenant sit at the heart of Arcane Allegiance, acting like two halves of a well-timed ritual.

### Flux
Flux is the set’s tempo engine. It’s not just a one-line trick; it’s a system that rewards you for sequencing your cheap spells and your early plays in ways that push you toward bigger effects later in the game. You’ll find Flux-enabled cards that expand their utility the more you commit to a particular spell- or creature-based tempo. The payoff often comes in the form of a bigger, splashier effect that validates your earlier investment in the Flux window. It’s a mechanic that wants you to think about the game in a broader arc, not just the current moment. For players who love counting the turns and optimizing card draw, Flux becomes a delicious puzzle with clear, tangible payoffs.

From a gameplay perspective, Flux adds a dimensional layer to both drafting and constructed play. In Limited, you’ll learn to identify Flux triggers and to gauge when to push a window and when to hold back for a more explosive turn. In Constructed, Flux cards can slot into a variety of shells, letting you design midrange and control builds that hinge on a carefully choreographed middle game. It’s not about “go big or go home” in every hand; it’s about finding the rational moment to tilt the board in your favor and then riding that momentum through a careful sequence of plays.

### Covenant
Covenant is the second pillar that binds the set’s identity. Covenant cards reward you for leaning into a specific strategy or identity across multiple cards. The payoff is thematic, but it’s also a tangible mechanical ladder: the more Covenant-ness you show, the more your board state compounds in your favor. Covenant decks often thrive on synergy density—cards that complement one another and create a network that’s hard to disrupt with a single answer.

This mechanic invites deck-building discipline. Players who enjoy the challenge of “staying in the lane” and maximizing payoff from a focused plan will appreciate Covenant’s design. It’s a nod to classic archetypes while offering fresh twists that can elevate a familiar build into something with surprising staying power.

### The Patter: How Flux Meets Covenant
The synergy between Flux and Covenant feels like a well-rehearsed duet rather than a couple of edgy solos. Flux creates tempo-driven moments that prime you for Covenant payoffs, and Covenant provides the consistency that keeps Flux decks from spinning out of control. The most satisfying games are those where you sense a chain of decisions leading toward a decisive point—an attack that forces your opponent to answer in a way that reveals their plan, followed by a Covenant-enabled follow-up that closes the door on their attempts to rebuild.

In practice, you’ll often find yourself choosing between a tempo play and a Covenant activation, balancing risk and reward with the clock ticking in the background. The set’s design pushes you toward that moment of precise calculation—the moment when the deck comes alive and your plan becomes a story you can narrate to your friends with a straight face and heroic enthusiasm.

Arcane Allegiance isn’t shy about color synergy. It offers two-color and three-color options that feel cohesive rather than fussy. ## Draft Experience: Crafting the Puzzle

Drafting Arcane Allegiance is a puzzle reduced to a dozen shapes that you must arrange in just the right order. Flux cards reward you for precise sequencing, while Covenant logos act as “identity cards” that tell you which direction to push your deck. The draft environment feels lively and engaging, avoiding the familiar trap of repetitive archetypes while still offering a comfortable entry point for new players.

From a practical perspective, here are some drafting tips:

- Look for Flux triggers on early drops; those are your tempo anchors.
- Prioritize Covenant cards that align with your two-color pair or your primary strategy.
- Don’t rush to finalize a plan; instead, identify a couple of reliable synergies you can rely on if the table shifts away from your first pick.
- Reserve one or two flexible picks that can slot into multiple shells; Arcane Allegiance rewards adaptable minds.

The result is a Limited format that feels brisk, rewarding, and (dare I say) occasionally magical in the most literal sense—the kind of experience that makes you grin at your own clever plays and groan at your own missteps with equal measure.

In constructed play, Arcane Allegiance offers a credible toolbox that interacts well with the broader card pool.
