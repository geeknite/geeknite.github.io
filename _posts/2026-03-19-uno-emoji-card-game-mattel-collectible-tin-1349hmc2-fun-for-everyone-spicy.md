## How to Play Quick Start: A Tiny Guide
- Setup: Each player gets seven cards; the rest form the draw pile; flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Objective: Be the first to discard all of your cards.
- On your turn: Match color or emoji on the discard pile with a card from your hand, or play a wild card to change color. In the spicy edition, watch for pepper emoji prompts that nudge you toward riskier plays.
- Special Cards: The typical UNO action cards appear, but you’ll also encounter emoji-themed variants that provide lighthearted twists rather than heavy-handed rule changes. If you’re new to UNO, this is a gentle onboarding with expressive flair.
- UNO Call: Don’t forget to shout UNO when you’re down to one card. It’s as much a social ritual as a rule—so dramatize it if you must.
- Endgame and Scoring: In traditional UNO, you sum up points for the cards left in opponents’ hands. With Emoji UNO, you can optionally score according to emoji outcomes or simply play for a few rounds of laughter and bragging rights.

This quick-start guide is designed to get you from box to chaos in under ten minutes, which is exactly what a party game should feel like: immediate fun, little friction, lots of smiles, and a few moments of “how did we end up with an emoji duel in Round 3?”
