## Preservation, grading, and long term care

### How to store sealed packs properly

Storage matters as much as the purchase itself. A sealed The Dark booster pack deserves a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Temperature fluctuations can affect plastic integrity over decades, and humidity can invite something less magical than mana to creep into the package. A protective sleeve for the outer packaging, a climate controlled display area, and a box that reduces vibration during shipping are all good ideas. Keep an eye on the wrapper for any signs of moisture, tearing, or discoloration. If you intend to move from a personal collection into a sale later, document the wrapper’s condition with clear photos obtained under neutral lighting.

### Grading sealed copies

Grading sealed products is a niche but legitimate path for serious collectors. If you are considering grading, weigh the cost against the potential premium of a sealed set. Grading typically applies to individual cards rather than full sealed boosters, so if your plan is to keep the pack sealed and focus on a displayable relic, grading may not apply directly to the sealed unit. However, some collectors pursue auction options that consider a sealed pack as a graded asset in a broader sense, factoring in packaging age, wrapper integrity, and the overall presentation. Do your homework, compare fees, timelines, and what the market currently values in the sense of sealed vintage product.
