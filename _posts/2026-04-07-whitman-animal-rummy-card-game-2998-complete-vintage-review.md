## The Rule Set and No Instructions
Here is where the Whitman charm collides with a practical world: there is no instruction sheet in this particular release. The original publisher seems to have trusted players to glean a system from the visual language and whatever comments survive in collectors’ forums. The absence of a rulebook creates a sandbox atmosphere: you propose a rule, someone challenges it with a better one, and you all converge on something entertaining enough to call a session a success.

From a modern perspective, this is part delight and part risk. If you crave a clean, repeatable experience, this may feel like boat-rocking. If you relish improvisation and a little chaos, the missing instructions become a feature, not a bug. This is a deck that exists as a prompt, not a script. You’ll likely end up with a few canonical variants after a couple of sessions—for instance, we found ourselves leaning into a “animal-based scoring” version where certain cards grant bonus points for witty animal-related narratives. It works, it’s silly, and it’s absolutely vintage in the best possible sense.

### How to Play Without Instructions
- Step one: shuffle the deck and deal. The number of cards per player is up to you, as the original rules aren’t keen on giving you a universal standard.
- Step two: establish a playful scoring system. You can adopt classic rummy elements, or you can invent bonuses tied to the animal imagery. Icebreaker: award the “Most Dramatic Rummy Moment” bonus to the player who tells the most entertaining story about a particular animal card.
- Step three: form sets and runs as you see fit. The exact mechanics are flexible; you’re testing your ability to narrate a game while building cards in sequences or sets. When you decide a round is complete, tally the points and crown a winner who delivered the best zoo-themed monologue.
- Step four: rinse and repeat with your preferred house rules. The joy of no instructions is precisely this: the deck becomes a canvas for your social play, not a rigid formula.
