### Condition matters: NM vs PSA 9/10 (and why you should care)
- NM means the card looks flawless to the naked eye under normal lighting, with adjacent edges and corners showing minimal wear.
- Professional grades (e.g., PSA 9/10) carry an extra layer of verification, which can dramatically influence value.
- The holo pattern and surface gloss can show micro-scratches or scuffs if you angle the card under bright glare. In many NM examples, those micro-details are either non-existent or utterly negligible to casual collectors.

Remember: there’s a difference between “Near Mint” and “Perfectly Mint.” In the wild, you’ll often see NM with minor corner touches or a whisper of surface micro-etching. If you’re planning to submit to a grader, ensure you’ve inspected under a good light and considered a trusted third-party appraisal before dropping your coins.

### Care, handling, and storage tips for NM shiny treasure
- Use soft card sleeves and a rigid top-loader or sleeve-and-binder system to prevent edge chipping.
- Keep away from direct sunlight to preserve holo finish and prevent color fade over time.
- Store cards in a climate-controlled space; humidity can warp the gloss, and also make your cat suspicious of the shimmering oddities.
- Consider a solid display case if you’re the kind of collector who enjoys showing off their shinies to guests who don’t understand why you’re so invested in a magenta flower party on cardboard.

### Artwork variants: knowing the difference between Japanese and English prints
