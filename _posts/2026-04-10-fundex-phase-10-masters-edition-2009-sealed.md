## Where to Buy and How to Verify Authenticity
If you’re hunting for a sealed Masters Edition tin, here are practical tips:

## Tips for Inspecting a Sealed Tin
- Inspect the seal integrity from multiple angles. Look for any cracking or obvious opening signs.
- Check the lid alignment. A misaligned tin lid can indicate tampering or rough handling.
- Look for corrosion or rust inside the lid or edges; this can be a red flag for packaging deterioration.
- Examine the card edges inside once opened (if you manage to), for whitening or creases. This can affect value.
- Request close-up photos if you’re buying online. A good seller will understand and provide them.

## Maintenance of Older Card Games: A Small Nerdy PSA
- Keep the cards away from extreme humidity. Card stock is not a fan of sauna vibes.
- Store away from direct sunlight to preserve color and edge wear.
- If you’re planning to actually play, consider sleeves to protect card faces and backings. It’s a small step that pays off when you pull a phase 8 and don’t cry into your soda.
- Don’t over-stack the tin. A heavy pile of other collectibles on top can warp the metal, which is a minor tragedy for tin lovers.
