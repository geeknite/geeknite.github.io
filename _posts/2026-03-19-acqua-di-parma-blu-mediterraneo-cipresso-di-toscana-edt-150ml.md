Scent profile: what the nose actually experiences
----------------------------------------------------

Cipresso di Toscana sits in that “green and clean” zone that fragrance fans either adore or confuse. It’s not a cloying pine monster; it’s a verdant, sunlit forest edge after a spring rain. The top notes deliver an immediately fresh, herbal breeze. Think pine needles mingling with a citrus zing, but in a way that doesn’t shout. As the heart opens, the scent reveals its more nuanced character: balsamic resins, a whisper of cypress, and a subtle herbaceous vibe that keeps the composition from tipping into “pine forest sauna.” The base is clean and dry, with light woods that offer a comfortable leash rather than an overwhelming tether.

This is not a powerhouse sillage; Cipresso is a thoughtful daily wearer. It projects enough to remind people you’re there without turning into a fragrance fog machine. Longevity is decent for an EDT: expect a solid half-day with a gentle reappearance around dusk, should you be the kind to re-apply (which, full disclosure, I am occasionally). The scent sits closer to the skin after a couple of hours, which is perfect if you’re in an office where you only want your colleague to notice you at “low-key hero” level rather than “I can smell you across the cube farm.”
