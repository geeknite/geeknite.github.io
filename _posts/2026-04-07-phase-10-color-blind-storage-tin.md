### A note on color and accessibility
Color Blind Accessibility is a real thing, not a marketing buzzword. The Phase 10 color blind accessible deck is designed with two main strategies: high-contrast color palettes and perceptible shapes or symbols on card backs and borders. In practice, this means that you can tell cards apart not only by color but by the tiny icons, shapes, or number cues that accompany them. While some color-blind players rely on texture, many will appreciate large, bold pips and a palette that avoids the typical red-green trap. In the Phase 10 kit, you will notice that the colors have been chosen to maximize contrast and to reduce the hard edges that can mislead the eye when you are moving quickly.
