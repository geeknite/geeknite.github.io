## What is GIPF?

GIPF is an abstract strategy game designed by Kris Burm, and it's the flagship game of the GIPF Project series. The game revolves around placing and moving pieces on a hexagonal board, aiming to create a line of four pieces while simultaneously blocking your opponent's moves. Sounds simple, right? Well, think again!

## Gameplay Mechanics

### Setup

The GIPF board consists of a hexagonal grid with intersections where pieces can be placed. Each player starts with three pieces on the board, and the goal is to create a line of four pieces while preventing your opponent from doing the same.

### Basic Moves

1. **Place a Piece**: Players take turns placing one of their pieces on an empty intersection.
2. **Move a Piece**: Players can move their pieces along the lines on the board, aiming to create strategic formations.
3. **Capture**: If a player creates a line of four pieces, they can remove those pieces along with any opponent's pieces connected to the line.

### Special Moves

GIPF introduces a twist with GIPF pieces, which are special pieces that can be used to enhance your strategies. These pieces can be moved in unique ways, adding an extra layer of complexity to the game.

## Strategy Tips

### Think Ahead

GIPF is a game of foresight. Always plan your moves several steps ahead, anticipating your opponent's actions and countering them effectively.

### Control the Center

Owning the central intersections gives you more control over the board, allowing you to execute your strategies more effectively.

### Use GIPF Pieces Wisely

GIPF pieces are powerful tools, but they must be used strategically. Deploy them when you have a clear advantage or need to turn the tide of the game.
