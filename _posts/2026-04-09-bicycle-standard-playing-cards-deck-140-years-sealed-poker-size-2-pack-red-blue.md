## Card Care, Storage, and Longevity

If you’re going to be serious about these cards, a little care goes a long way. Store the two-pack in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve the color integrity of the print. Humidity and heat are the arch-nemeses of any card stock; they can cause warping or warping-related stiffness over months and years. If you’re planning a long shelf life for your sealed decks, consider placing them in a small card-safe or a display cabinet with a stable climate.

For users who frequently travel with cards, a soft case or a small protective pouch helps prevent edge wear during transit. A mini silica gel packet can help reduce moisture in the wallet or bag where the two-pack is stored, though you’ll want to avoid chewing on it if a dog steals your luggage in a busy airport.

Cleaning: When needed, clean only with a soft, dry cloth. Avoid water, solvents, or any aggressive cleaners that could degrade the finish. Cards don’t like being metabolically dehydrated or soaked; they’re not little party favors you can rescue by dunking them in a pool. A gentle wipe, followed by air drying (out of direct light) should suffice for light fingerprint marks and minor smudges. If you’re a germaphobe and you insist that surfaces be spotless, invest in a second deck that’s designated for table performance while your primary deck rests in its pristine two-pack state.
