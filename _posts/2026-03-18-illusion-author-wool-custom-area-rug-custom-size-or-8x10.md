### Noise and Acoustics: The Quiet Hero

Yes, a rug can influence acoustics. Wool has denser fibers than many synthetics, and that density helps absorb sound in a room. For a home theater, study, or gaming nook, this can reduce the echo chamber seriousness and create a more intimate audio environment. It’s not a replacement for proper acoustic treatment, but it’s a free upgrade that doesn’t require extra cables or a graduate degree in sound engineering.

### Maintenance: Spills, Stains, and the Great Wool Rescue

Wool is naturally breathable and resilient, but it’s not invincible. The recommended approach for spills is to blot—don’t rub—being gentle with your precious fibers. For most common spills, a mild detergent solution and a light blot is enough, followed by a quick air-dry. If you’re dealing with a more stubborn stain, consider bringing in a professional cleaner who specializes in wool. A quick note: avoid harsh solvents, as they can alter the dye and texture. The long-term trick is to treat the rug well so it retains its shape, color, and whisper of a story for years to come.


## Durability and Care: A Wool Rug That Grows with Your Home

### The Long Game: How It Holds Up

Durability is where wool shines. The fibers resist pilling and maintain resilience—great for households with kids, pets, or an accidental dance-off. With proper care, a wool rug can endure the test of time better than many synthetics, which often show wear in high-traffic zones sooner. ### Cleaning Routine: A Routine that Doesn’t Require a Crew

- Vacuum with a beater bar on a low setting, or use a brush attachment for delicate areas.
- Address spills immediately by blotting; avoid rubbing as it pushes liquid deeper into the fibers.
- Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear and color consistency across the surface.
- For professional cleaning, specify wool fiber handling to ensure colorfastness and texture preservation.
