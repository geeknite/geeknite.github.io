## How to play the Spanish edition: a quick refresher

Monopoly Deal in any language is a fast game about building property sets and managing cash. The objective is simple: be the first player to collect three full property sets. ### Phase by phase walkthrough

- Draw phase: You draw up to a certain hand size. In practice, you want flexibility, not just raw cards. The Spanish edition clarifies some ambiguous phrases that might otherwise slow down players who are still translating on the fly.
- Play phase: You can play money to your bank, play property cards into sets, or use action cards to steal, charge rents, or insist on extra moves. The Spanish text ensures that action names are intuitive and that people can memorize the order of operations without thinking in two languages at once.
- End of turn: You discard down to the limit. It is common to see players debating whether a particular card should stay in hand or go to the discard pile. The Spanish translation helps reduce such debates by giving clear categories for each card type.

## Playing tips for mixed language tables

If you are hosting a game night where some players will read in English and others in Spanish, here are a few practical tips to ensure smooth play:

- Use a quiet, dedicated surface for your card deck to reduce card misreads. A white hand towel or felt mat can help reduce glare on glossy cards.
- Create a bilingual glossary for the key action names. You can have a quick reference card that shows both the English term and the Spanish translation for essential cards like rent, debt slam, or house and hotel replacements.
- Establish a language rotation rule. For example, in one round you allow only Spanish reads, and in the next round only English reads. This can help players practice language while preserving the pace of the game.
- Encourage players to paraphrase. If someone does not remember the exact card name in Spanish, they can paraphrase what the card does. As long as the table agrees on the outcome, the game can continue with less friction.
