## What Fortune Row Is and Why It Matters
Fortune Row is a Playbook designed for a crew whose defining feature is a shared sense of luck and a knack for turning near-misses into near-myths. In the City of Mist system, playbooks are the core vocabulary players use to shape how their characters see power, what they choose to do with it, and how the city responds to those choices. Fortune Row reorients the spotlight away from single heroic epics toward collaborative myth-making. The result is a table where players feel their choices ripple through the story like a chorus rather than a solo performance.

At its heart, Fortune Row rewards three things in equal measure: clever social manipulation, tight crew dynamics, and a willingness to gamble with the city’s rules. You will find mechanics that encourage the team to stage elaborate schemes, each one differently flavored by the crew’s archetypes, misdirection, and their own brand of luck. The play experience leans into a style we at Geeknite adore: cinematic, witty, and emotionally satisfying without becoming precious or self-serious.

In practice, Fortune Row shines when the group embraces a caper rhythm: plan a move, test the city’s patience, improvise when the plan cracks, and walk away with a story that your future selves will tell in the coffee shop between aurora borealis screens and neon rain. This Playbook invites you to run a city block with a crew that is as much a character as the legends they chase. It’s not about heroic feats in a vacuum; it’s about how a crew’s luck feels like a cooperative superpower that can bend outcomes, charm observers, and occasionally get everyone punched by the plot, not by a foe alone.

For those who love world-building, Fortune Row is a dream. You can seed the crew’s backstory with myths that echo through alleys, galleries, speakeasies, and abandoned ferries. You can thread a long-form arc about a big score that keeps the city awake at night, or you can run short, punchy sessions where the crew navigates a single night that seems to tilt toward their favor — for now. It also happily supports one-shot sessions that lean heavy on vibe, allowing the GM to tease out a mythos through a string of vignettes rather than a single, sprawling campaign. The Playbook is a toolset for the kind of storytelling we love: it lets clever players leverage luck as a narrative currency, with consequences that feel earned rather than handed out by a deus ex machina.


## The Playbook’s Core Mechanics: What Makes Fortune Row Tick
Fortune Row doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it tweaks the wheel’s hub to deliver a smoother ride for caper fans. If you know City of Mist, you know the system uses the Powered by the Apocalypse style of dice pools, where you roll a number of six-sided dice, count successes, and watch how those successes drive outcomes. Fortune Row adds distinct twists that align with the crew’s ethos while respecting the game’s mythic engine.

Key features you’ll encounter include:

- A crew-based resource called Fortune. Fortune is spent to grease the gears of a plan, to unlock social leverage, or to bend a scene toward a payoff that the crew deserves. The use of Fortune creates moments that feel like a montage in a movie: a quick montage of plan, execution, and reveal, all shot through a kaleidoscope of luck and quick thinking.
- Interlocking playbooks. Each member of the crew can lean into a niche that allows the team to combine powers in creative ways. The synergy is where the magic happens: you can chain a misdirection, a reveal, and a final flourish that lands like a soft boom in the narrative, not a hard crash.
- The Mythic Streetwise approach. The Playbook blends urban myth with real-world urban texture. You’ll find that your characters aren’t merely supercharged; they’re culturally resonant versions of myths that exist in the city’s shadow corners, making the mundane feel cosmic in scale without shattering realism.
- A focus on social gameplay. If you love trap-setting and social maneuvering, Fortune Row rewards you for reading the room, understanding the crowd, and turning the setting itself into a weapon. The city becomes a living character that can tilt the odds when your crew is in the right mood and the right moment.
- Consequences and fallout. The Playbook makes it clear that luck has a price. Each gain can awaken a different part of the city’s mythic machinery, sometimes with a price tag written in neon across the sky. That sense of consequence is essential to the City of Mist feel: you don’t accumulate power without the city wanting a return on its investment.

In short, Fortune Row is the kind of Playbook that invites collaborative play. It’s not about solo heroics; it’s about a team deciding together how they want the luck to unfold and then improvising in real time as the city pushes back with its own peculiar sense of humor.


## Theme, Tone, and GM Tools: Getting the Most Out of Fortune Row
This Playbook shines when the group commits to a specific vibe while staying flexible enough to adapt on the fly. If you want a dark night of witty cons and streetlight gods, Fortune Row is your soundtrack. If you crave a more pulp action vibe, there’s room for that too, provided you lean into social misdirection and location-based storytelling.

GM tips for maximizing Fortune Row:
- Lean into the crew’s dynamics. Use scenes that force the crew to rely on each other’s strengths. The best sessions are those where a plan hinges on trust and a timely misdirection rather than a single big reveal.
- Treat Fortune as a currency with a clock. When you set up a scene where Fortune could be spent, give players a visible stake — a ticking clock or a consequence that grows with every attempt to grind the plan forward. A little pressure goes a long way toward cinematic tension.
- Use the city as a partner, not a backdrop. The Fortune Row Playbook invites the GM to design these moments where the city’s mood shifts with the crew’s successes and failures. If a corner store owner recognizes the crew by the wrong badge, that moment becomes a catalyst rather than a one-off obstacle.
- Resist the temptation to over-plan. The city loves to surprise you, and Fortune Row thrives on smart improvisation. Prepare several connective threads rather than a single linear path, and let the players improvise their way toward the payoff.

For players, a quick-start approach works best: pick a core element that the crew’s luck will revolve around, weave in two supporting roles, and flesh out a few NPCs who carry seeds of future mischief. The Playbook gives you a font of hooks; you just need to pick one and start writing the page in your shared story.

## Mechanical Deep Dive: What a Session Might Look Like
To illustrate the flavor, imagine a one-shot night in Fortune Row. The crew has a target — a one-night-only gala where a mythical artifact is rumored to be on display. The plan is simple on paper: gain access to the gala, switch the artifact with a convincingly crafted replica, and exit with the real item in hand while leaving the scene looking like a carefully choreographed performance rather than a break-in.

The approach is anything but simple in practice. Fortune Row incentivizes the players to assemble a few clever scenes that each contribute to the final payoff. A sample sequence might run like this:

1) The Setup: A street-level contact offers a clue about the gala. The crew spends Fortune to unlock a social leverage point — perhaps a pretend charity auction where their crew can rub elbows with high-profile attendees. This is the moment when the city seems to lean in, offering a hint that not everything is on the square and that luck might be a two-way street.
2) The Diversion: A misdirection sequence plays out in a rotating crescendo. A celebrity sighting, a fake security breach, a meticulously timed distraction. The goal is to force the gala’s security to fragment just long enough for the crew to slip into the right place.
3) The Switch: With the artifact in view, a final glide happens. The crew must coordinate a simultaneous action that depends on trust and timing. Luck here does not erase the risk; it reshapes the risk into a moment where failure still leads to a story worth telling.
4) The Escape: The exit is a dance, not a sprint. The crew navigates the city’s pulse the way a jazz quartet navigates a chorus — with improvisation, communal understanding, and a plan that morphs as the scene evolves.

During each phase, the Fortune mechanic sits in the background like a quiet conductor. Players decide when to spend Fortune to tilt outcomes in their favor, but every tilt asks a price, whether in exposure, an NPC’s memory, or a future plot twist. The GM can layer consequences that echo future sessions, ensuring the crew remains a focal point of ongoing myth rather than a one-night flash in the neon rain.

The result is a rhythm that rewards teamwork, clever planning, and the ability to pivot when the city refuses to cooperate. It’s not about who hits the hardest; it’s about who reads the crowd best and who can turn a near disaster into a clever, narrative payoff.

## Running Fortune Row: Campaign Ideas and Session Palettes
If you’re planning a campaign, here are several tones and arcs you can explore with Fortune Row:

- The Velvet Coin Arc: The crew discovers a larger conspiracy in which every big score relies on a long chain of lucky events. They must decide whether to pursue a payday that benefits a powerful faction or to cut losses and protect a smaller, more intimate circle. This arc leans into ethical questions about luck and its uses, with several moral forks that can define the crew’s legacy.
- Neon Ghosts Night: A city-wide event reveals a parallel track of mythic activity. The crew must navigate a carnival of illusions that draws on city legends, family secrets, and the spectral footprints left by heroes of the past. This arc emphasizes atmosphere, mood, and the tension between spectacle and truth.
- The Curse of the Palimpsest: A single artifact’s power spills beyond the gala, rewriting memories across the city. The crew must decide how much to erase from the city’s collective memory and what is worth remembering. This arc invites introspection and can lead to a season-long reckoning about the cost of luck.
- Quick-and-dirty One-Shots: If you want a fast game night, Fortune Row scales down to a tight two to three hours. Use a seeded mystery, put the crew in a high-stakes social setting, and let fortune do the rest. The Playbook supports this mode with plenty of hooks and a crisp payoff, letting new players taste the flavor without a multi-session commitment.

## Pros and Cons of Fortune Row
- Pros:
  - Strong emphasis on crew collaboration and social savvy.
  - Flexible, modular seeds that support both long arcs and one-shots.
  - A vivid, urban-mythic vibe that fits well with modern city settings.
  - Fortune as a narrative currency encourages creative, cinematic storytelling.
- Cons:
  - The theme may require buy-in from the table; if your group favors solo heroics, Fortune Row can feel like a misaligned fit.
  - The reliance on social mechanics might feel slow for players who prefer fast combat and action dice.
  - Some players may crave more structured combat options, which can be lighter in this Playbook compared to other options.

Overall, Fortune Row earns its keep by valuing storytelling momentum and group dynamics. It rewards planning without punishing adaptability, making it a reliable pick for groups who want to tell a cheeky, myth-infused caper rather than a grind toward a single blockbuster victory.


## Final Recommendation
If you crave a City of Mist experience that rewards teamwork, clever misdirection, and a city-wide sense of magical possibility, Fortune Row is a solid addition to your table. The Playbook leans into social mechanics and caper-style plotting with a mythic edge, which is exactly the kind of vibe many City of Mist fans chase. It plays nicely with both long-form campaigns that evolve with the city and one-shot sessions that aim for a memorable, self-contained arc. The emphasis on crew dynamics and shared luck makes it feel fresh, even for players who have explored multiple City of Mist Playbooks before.

That said, it’s not a universal fit. If you and your group adore martial showdowns, split-second tactical combat, or solitary legends, this Playbook may require a shift in expectations. However, if your table enjoys scheming, social maneuvering, and watching a troupe of misfit mythic characters weave a night that ends with more questions than answers, Fortune Row will deliver nostalgia with a modern edge.

In short: Fortune Row is a worthwhile experiment for players who love the idea of luck as a team-based power and a city that notices when people gamble with its nerves. It’s not just a supplement; it’s a vibe, a technique, and a prompt for storytelling that can sustain sessions with the energy of a well-curated music video — fast, stylish, and a little dangerous.
