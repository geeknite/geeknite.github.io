## How to Play a Round (H2 for quick context)
If you’re new to Monopoly Deal, here’s the high-level flow. This is not a full ruleset, but enough to set expectations for this tin’s contents and the game’s energy:
- Each player starts with a hand of five cards. You draw two at the start of a turn and can play up to three cards, choosing to either place property cards in front of you or use action cards to affect other players.
- The core objective is to collect property sets of any color; the first player to complete a full set of three or more properties in a color group wins, depending on house rules or the exact edition you’re using.
- Action cards can demand rent, steal properties, or force trades; Just Say No cards can counter other players’ actions (think of it as the game’s spicy denial button).
- Money and house/hotel cards work as a currency and upgrade system that can be cashed in or used to strengthen your property portfolio.

In the tin version, you’re not just playing; you’re shoving the action center stage into a compact, tidy package. The rules translate well to a shorter play time than the original board game, which makes it ideal for families with limited patience for epic sessions and kids who want to see the “victory screen” before bedtime. It’s a balance of luck (the draw) and strategy (how you manage your hand and use your action cards right when it matters most).
