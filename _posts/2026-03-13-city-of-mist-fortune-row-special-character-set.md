## What is Fortune Row and why should you care?

Fortune Row is presented as a Special Character Set, which in the City of Mist universe means a curated set of new archetypes, assets, and flavor text that tilts the game toward luck, risk, and the kind of charisma that makes a card falling from a pocket feel like a cinematic moment. This isn’t just new mumbo-jumbo fluff; the set provides a handful of ready-to-play character sheets, fresh Fate-tag mechanics, and a bevy of micro-plot hooks to slot into your ongoing campaign. If your city needs a brighter neon glow around its myths, this is the patch that makes the glow more honest about its glow-ness.

As always with City of Mist products, the emphasis is on flavor first, accessibility second, and spectacle third. Fortune Row does not dump six pages of crunchy crunch into your head; it instead delivers an elegant window for players and GMs to peer through: who is this new Fortune in the Row, and what bets are they willing to make with fate itself?

## What you actually get in the Special Character Set

Fortune Row is not a full game in a bag; it’s a booster for the flavor engine that already powers City of Mist. Here is a rough breakdown of the nuts, bolts, and magical beans you get with the set:

- A handful of new character templates rooted in fortune, fate, risk, and the theatricality of chance.
- Fresh tags and mechanics that tie into Fortune Row's central conceit: that luck, not just power, can sway the world's surface.
- An assortment of NPCs and minor antagonists whose goals revolve around tight corners, closed doors, and the vibe of a late-night casino that may or may not exist in the moral landscape.
- A short guide to using Fate dice to influence outcomes in scenes that are already swinging between tragedy and slapstick.
- A few encounter hooks designed to dovetail with current story arcs or to jump-start a new arc focused on fortune and consequence.

Of course, the exact number of pieces may vary depending on print runs, but the spirit remains consistent: Fortune Row is a compact, potent add-on that pushes a game session toward the edge of possibility without tipping the balance into chaos.

### The core archetypes and what they bring to your table

Fortune Row shines brightest when you see the archetypes in action rather than merely reading their stat blocks. Think of these as new lenses through which players can frame their ambitions and fears. The set gives you at least six distinct archetypes, each built to slot into your city with minimal friction:

- The Cardsharp Adept: A fortune-teller with a deck of odds and a set of tricks designed to turn a losing hand into a win. They bring a mix of social leverage and quick manipulation that shines in social scenes and heist-based arcs.
- The Velvet Oracle: A mystic who reads the city’s signs in coffee stains, radio static, and wind-blown flyers. Their powers revolve around reading contingencies into the air and guiding the party toward high-value choices with high stakes.
- The Neon Gambler: A risk-taker who can push the city’s luck to the brink. This archetype naturally creates tension in scenes and excels at improvisation when assets slip away from the party.
- The Cashmere Sage: An information broker who trades in secrets rather than money. They turn knowledge into leverage and use fortune-based mechanics to reshape the city’s rumor mill.
- The Signal Runner: A courier of messages between worlds, weaving mythic influence into the grit of the street. Their strength lies in information routing and making sure the right truth lands at the right moment.
- The Whispering Fortune: A trickster who can bend probabilities in small, meaningful ways. They become the narrative pivot that makes a session feel cinematic without breaking the game’s balance.

Each of these archetypes comes with a short narrative hook, a ready-to-use stat block, and a handful of “Fortune Moves” that you can trigger during play. They are designed for quick onboarding: you can hand a player a template and a sheet, and within a single scene they’ll be leaning into a mythic identity, not merely a set of numbers.

### How the mechanics feel on the table

City of Mist uses a blend of myth and modern narrative mechanics. Fortune Row adds flavor without displacing the core engine. The main addition is a tightened arc around Fortune tags that players can spend to gain advantages or force unexpected outcomes. The effect is to reward narrative risk without turning the table into a numbers-only sprint.

In practice, this means better opportunities for players who enjoy improvisation. The Fortune moves are designed to feel cinematic—moments where a gloved hand slides a card across the table and everyone holds their breath as the fate of a scene hinges on the flip. If you enjoy the theatrical beat of a noir or a mythic heist, Fortune Row channels that energy with a little more leash than your average expansion.

That said, the set does not overreact to chaos. It provides structured lanes for fortune-based storytelling and makes sure GMs can steer scenes toward consequence rather than letting players drift into meta-game chaos. The result is a dynamic balance: you can chase luck and still keep the story anchored in character, setting, and theme.

## Final verdict: is Fortune Row worth your time and money?

If you have already fallen in love with City of Mist’s mask-and-myth vibe, Fortune Row is likely to feel like a natural upgrade to your table’s atmosphere. It is the kind of add-on that doesn’t pretend to reinvent the wheel; instead, it polishes a corner of the wheel you didn’t know needed polishing. The characters feel distinct, the mechanics integrate smoothly with existing rules, and the tone sits happily between noir grit and mythic whimsy. The set is especially strong for tables that enjoy strong social scenes, capricious antagonists, and plenty of room for players to improvise with destiny in their hands.

That said, Fortune Row is not a universal cure for a stalled campaign. If your table is more interested in crunch and tactical gameplay than narrative swing, you might not see the full value here. It’s also worth noting that the strongest wins come from GM mastery—the more comfortable you are weaving myth with street-level stakes, the more you will extract from Fortune Row.

For a table that thrives on character-driven moments, secret identities, and the magic of a city that never quite sleeps, Fortune Row delivers on its promise with style. It invites players to flirt with fate while staying emotionally grounded in a setting that feels both intimate and colossal in scope.
