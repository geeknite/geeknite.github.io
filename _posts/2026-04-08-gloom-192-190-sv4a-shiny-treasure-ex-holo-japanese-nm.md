### Condition and grading
NM, or Near Mint, is a real sweet spot for this card. The key to a strong NM grade is:
- Edge wear that is barely perceptible, if present at all
- Corners that are sharp with minimal beveling or whitening
- Centering that sits within the standard tolerance
- Surface free of scratches, print lines, or stain

With the Gloom Shiny Treasure Ex holo, you should inspect the holo pattern for any scuffing, light scratches, or rainbow lines that betray a rough surface beneath. In some cases, the Japanese printing can reveal tiny imperfections along the border where the foil meets the card stock. These minor flaws, while casual observer might miss them, can push the grade from 9 to 8 in the eyes of a grader.

### Authenticity and storage
Authenticity is not just about price tags; it is about preserving the moment you first saw the card. Some practical tips:
- Keep mint cards in semi-rigid sleeves and top-loaders to guard against bending and edge chipping
- Store in a climate-controlled environment away from direct sunlight to preserve holo brightness
- Use silica gel packs in storage boxes to prevent moisture damage
- When displaying, consider a layout that avoids touching the holo surface; wear white cotton gloves if you want to go full collector masochist
- Verify the back for uniform color and absence of misprints that could point to counterfeit cards
- Compare with known authentic examples from trusted dealers or community reference guides

For those who enjoy a more forensic approach, the print run and distribution for SV4a Japanese edition can reveal patterns. Some sellers will mention the exact print run or edition details; if you encounter someone who claims this is a limited release without any substantiation, treat that claim with skepticism. As with most collecting hobbies, the story is often as important as the card itself.
