Materials and Durability
------------------------
The Sorbet Round Rug positions itself as a mid-to-upper-mid-range textile in terms of material quality. It’s labeled as a synthetic-blend pile with a plush feel that is comfortable for bare feet. The fibers are designed to resist flattening over time, but, as with all rugs, heavy traffic in the same spot will eventually cause some compression. The edge construction is durable enough to handle routine vacuuming without shedding or fraying, which is a surprisingly underrated quality in a rug that loves to look fluffy but hates being aggressively tugged at by loose threads.

Care and Maintenance
---------------------
Maintenance for this rug is surprisingly friendly for non-rookie decorators. A regular vacuum on a low to medium setting keeps the pile looking vibrant. For deeper cleaning, a light hand-wash with a mild detergent is recommended, followed by air drying. Avoid high heat drying to prevent any potential fiber distortion. Spills are easier to manage than you’d expect: blot, don’t rub, because rubbing is not only fashionable but also tends to turn your coffee into a latte art experiment on the rug’s surface. If you have a pet—bless you—this rug resists hair build-up pretty well, but daily sweeping of invisible pet hair remains part of life when you own a fluffy floor companion.

Backing and Safety
------------------
The anti-slip backing is decent on kitchen tiles and smooth wood floors. If you’re placing this rug on highly polished surfaces or laminate with poor grip, you might want to add a rug pad for added security. The backing is not designed to handle extreme humidity or wet floors, so avoid bathroom or entryway scenarios where water exposure could reduce traction or lead to dampness.

Cleaning and Care: A Quick Reference
-----------------------------------
- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to avoid matting the pile.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean white cloth; do not rub, as rubbing invites friction and shame from the rug’s fibers.
- For deeper stains, use a mild detergent solution, test on a small inconspicuous area, and air-dry completely.
- Consider a rug pad for high-traffic rooms to preserve pile height and reduce slippage.
