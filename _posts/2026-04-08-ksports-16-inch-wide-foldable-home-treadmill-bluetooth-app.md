## Safety, maintenance, and user tips
Safety comes first in home cardio. The Ksports treadmill ships with an emergency stop clip you should attach to your clothing during every session. If you trip, the belt will stop, which can prevent a nasty tumble or at least a dramatic landing on a throw pillow. The fold mechanism has a locking pin that prevents sudden collapses during workouts, which adds a layer of security when you push to a brisk tempo.

Maintenance is straightforward. Wipe the belt after workouts to remove sweat that can cause corrosion over time. Check the screws periodically and re torque if you notice any looseness. If your manual suggests belt lubrication, do it on a schedule that suits your usage pattern. The belt edges should stay centered; if you notice drift, power down, relevel the deck, and recalibrate using the manual steps. These are routine steps for budget treadmills; they keep the equipment running smoothly and extend its life without requiring a degree in mechanical engineering.
