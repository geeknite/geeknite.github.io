## Safety, Maintenance, and Longevity

Winches are not toys. They are heavy-duty appliances designed to save your day when you need them most. Here are a few safety and maintenance tips that will extend the life of your SucceBuy winch and help you avoid embarrassment in front of your friends.

- Always wear gloves when handling synthetic rope. Frayed rope can have sharp fibers that cut you. Gloves protect your hands while you handle the rope during installation and rigging.
- Inspect the rope for wear or kinks after every few uses. If you see damage, retire the rope and replace it. A damaged rope can fail under load, which is dangerous and can ruin your day.
- Keep the motor cool. If you are doing long pulls or working in hot weather, rest the motor occasionally to prevent overheating. A cooled winch is a happy winch.
- Protect the wiring and connectors from moisture. The included contactor helps with safety, but ensure you have proper sealing on the electricals if you ride in rain or snow.
- Clean and lubricate the external gears and the moving parts. This is not a hobbyist item; it is a tool. A little maintenance goes a long way toward reliability.

## Synthetic Rope vs Steel Cable: The Great Debate (Lighthearted, but Practical)

Synthetic rope has become the default for many modern winches. It is lighter, easier to handle, and less prone to causing severe injury when it snaps back under load. Steel cable remains the choice for some heavy-duty applications because of its high tensile strength and durability under extreme loads. The SucceBuy 39 ft synthetic rope is a good compromise for most trail and field uses. It reduces the risk of injury and is easier to manage around a busy recovery scene.

From a geeky perspective, the rope is the handshake of your winch. It tells you what to expect during a recovery: smooth and forgiving under good conditions, tough and stubborn when the road refuses to yield. We recommend synthetic rope for most novices and adventurers who value ease of use and a safer environment for friends and pets. If you plan to repeatedly pull objects larger than the rated capacity or require the utmost reliability under extreme load, steel cable may still be your choice. In those cases, ensure you have the correct mounting kit and the appropriate winch snatch-blocks to maximize torque without overheating.
