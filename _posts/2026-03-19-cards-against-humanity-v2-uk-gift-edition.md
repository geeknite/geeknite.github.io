### Playing Style and House Rules

CAH is a party game that thrives on the energy of the table more than the quality of the jokes. The White Cards contain punchlines or humorous phrases, while the Black Cards ask players to fill in blanks with their White Cards. The judge rotates each round, and their job is to pick the funniest answer. The UK edition adds a few specifically British prompts that land best when the room has a shared cultural context, but the core experience remains the same: the funniest pairing wins and the table erupts with laughter or groans depending on the punchlines.

If you are hosting with a mixed-age group or with friends who have varying sensitivities, consider some house rules. You could implement safer play options, define a threshold for offensive content, or simply preface the game with a standard disclaimer: this game swings into questionable territory; proceed with enthusiasm and caution. ### How to Play: Quick Tutorial

1. Gather players, ideally 4 to 8 for a balanced table experience. More players means more chaos and more opportunities for improvisation.
2. Shuffle White and Black card piles separately. Keep them within reach of everyone.
3. The judge for the first round draws a Black Card and reads it aloud. The judge controls tempo and ensures that the room remains friendly and fun.
4. Players play one White Card face down. The judge either selects the funniest or reads them aloud to create dramatic effect.
5. Repeat with the next judge as the rounds progress. A good group spreads laughter and playful banter; a stiff room may require a few glasses of water and a breath mint.
6. Score it if you must. Some groups play for points; others for bragging rights or the sheer joy of hearing a particularly ridiculous line.
