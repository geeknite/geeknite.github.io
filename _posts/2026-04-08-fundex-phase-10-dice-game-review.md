## How to Play: A Lightweight Guide
### Setup
Set the tin on the table, shuffle the dice, and hand out the score cards. Depending on your group size, you may decide to pick a first player by mutual agreement, a die roll, or a who can tell the best dad joke contest. Each player gets a sheet, and the dice are ready to roll with the bravado of a squad of tiny, squeaky heroes.

### Objective
The objective is simple and deliciously merciless: complete a sequence of phases by rolling dice that match the criteria of each phase before your opponents. The twist that gives Phase 10 Dice Game its character is that you do not rely on a deck of playing cards; you rely on the randomness of dice while pretending to maintain a strategic plan. In other words, you will roll, re-roll, and sometimes accept that the luck gods are feeling sassy today.

### The phase ladder
In this dice incarnation, the phase ladder remains the backbone of the game. You will be asked to assemble sets, runs, or combinations across multiple rounds, using the dice to satisfy the requirements. The Phase 10 card game established a reputation for chasing phases; the dice version funnels that momentum into a tactile, roll-heavy experience. If you have played classic phase challenges, you will find this adaptation familiar yet refreshingly tactile.

### Turn structure
On your turn you roll the dice, then decide which dice to keep and which to re-roll. You can do up to a couple of re-rolls per turn, depending on how daring you feel. The challenge is balancing the lure of a perfect phase with the risk of losing momentum to the whims of probability. It is a microcosm of life: sometimes you chase the dream, sometimes you settle for a decent outcome and move forward.

## Strategy Bits for the Dice Devotee
### Reading the board, not the tea leaves
Strategy in a dice game often comes down to math, risk tolerance, and the ability to bluff about your actual plan. The dice you roll on a given turn can shape your decisions more than any printed card. The trick is to identify which dice are essential to your current phase and which dice you can sacrifice to pave the way for future turns. It helps to keep a rough mental map of which phases you can plausibly complete with what you have in hand, acknowledging that the dice gods sometimes render your plan moot with a shrug of the cosmic conductor.

### Freeze the big picture
Keep the end in sight. If a phase demands a run of consecutive numbers, do not chase perfect pairs if you are close to a stair-step ladder that will lead you to the next phase. If a phase wants a set of like numbers, do not hold out for a straight flush when the pool of dice is telling you that a pair would actually accelerate your progress. The best players are not always the luckiest; they are the ones who adapt quickly to the pattern the dice reveal after the first couple of rolls.

### Timing and interaction
In a group setting, you need patience and humor. The best moments often happen when a player nearly completes a phase but must settle for a partial success while watching the others tense up. The social energy matters more than the exact phase rules because you only get so many rounds before someone claims the title of Phase Master and you can proudly declare that you once rolled a two that ruined someone else phase for the second time in a row. This is not basketball; it is a tabletop stage for comedic chaos.
