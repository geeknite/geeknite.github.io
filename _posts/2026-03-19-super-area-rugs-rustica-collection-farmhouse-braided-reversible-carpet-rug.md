## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity
Maintenance is straightforward, which is exactly what you want in a product that will live in your daily life. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps keep dust and pet hair from building up in the braids. For spills, blot immediately with a clean cloth, avoiding rubbing which can push stains deeper. For spot cleaning, a mild detergent solution is usually sufficient; always test on a hidden area first to ensure colorfastness. The rug is not typically intended for machine washing, so rely on spot treatment and professional cleaning when needed.

To maximize longevity, consider using a rug pad. A pad reduces slippage on slick floors and provides a bit of extra cushioning, helping protect the rug edges from fraying and reducing wear in high-traffic zones. Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear across the surface, especially if your furniture sits on top of it in one fixed position.

Color fading is a common concern with any natural-toned rug. Avoid prolonged direct sunlight on lighter colorways, and if possible, place the rug in a room where drapes or blinds can manage the sun load. If you need to address stains or dinginess over time, a professional cleaning service is a safe option to preserve the color integrity and texture.
