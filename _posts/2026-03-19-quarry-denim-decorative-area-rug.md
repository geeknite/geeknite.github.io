## Durability and Maintenance
### Wear and Tear Under Real-Life Conditions
If you’re wondering whether this rug can handle a house full of active humans and one or two furry friends, the answer is yes, with caveats. The short pile is resistant to crushed fibers from foot traffic, which means you won’t see flattening in high-traffic zones after a few months. However, like denim jeans, the edges may show minimal fraying if you’re not careful with sharp-edged furniture or if you drag heavy items across it. It’s not a worry-for-life scenario; you’ll simply notice that after a long time, the rug has earned a few character marks that tell your space’s story.

### Stain Resistance and Cleaning Routines
Stain resistance is decent for the typical household. Most spills clean up with quick blotting and a gentle cleaning solution. For anything more stubborn than a sauce splash, you’ll want to address it sooner rather than later, as letting stains set can be a challenge with some denim-inspired dyes. We tested a few common culprits—coffee, red wine, and a rogue curry sauce—and found that prompt action keeps the rug looking presentable well beyond its warranty period. Vacuuming weekly helps maintain a uniform texture and prevents a build-up of dust that can dull the color over time. If you’re one of those people who forgets to rotate rugs, remember that a little rotation every quarter can help reduce uneven wear and keep the denim tones even.
