## Quality checks and practical tips
### Maintenance and care
Store the decks in the original box when you’re not using them, and avoid fanning them with the ferocity of a caffeine-fueled gamer in a midnight tournament. The goal is to preserve the air cushion finish and prevent chipping along the edges. If you drop them, don’t panic; the rider back pattern often hides minor edge wear, but you’ll notice after a few shuffles if the deck gets beat up. If you are particular about card longevity, consider a soft cloth deck holder or a dedicated card case to prevent warping.

### Shuffling and handling tips
- Use a standard bridge shuffle to break in the cards without stressing the stock.
- Practice a few basic false shuffles to feel the balance of the card under your fingertips.
- For cardists, experiment with split fan and two-tone color shuffles to showcase the red and blue contrast. You’ll get a satisfying visual when the deck fans out in a cascade of color.

### Cleaning and maintenance
If you spill something on the table during a game, wipe the surface quickly and avoid letting any moisture sit on the cards. Wipe with a dry cloth and let air air out. Avoid washing the cards; water is not your friend when it comes to printed stock. If the finish looks dull, a gentle run of air through a card straightener can help restore the shape, but don’t overdo it; you don’t want to create a deck that looks like it survived a tiny earthquake.
