## How to Authenticate and Verify a Lot (Smart Collector Moves)
- Cross-check the card edges against known prints from 1995. A subtle difference in border color or corner shape can hint at reprint or counterfeit pieces.
- Compare the back artwork and any set labels. Original print runs often have distinct typography and subtle watermark-like marks that newer reproductions try to mimic but fail to capture.
- If possible, request a small sampling of cards to inspect texture, gloss, and durability under normal handling.
- Inspect the lot for duplicates with identical wear patterns; sometimes sellers attempt to pass off rewrapped bundles. Real wear patterns across a diverse batch look more authentic.
