## Materials, care, and the art of maintenance

### Cleaning routine for a polypropylene rug
- Regular vacuuming is your best friend. Use a standard vacuum with a beater bar to lift debris from the pile.
- For spills, blot immediately with a clean cloth. Don’t rub; you’ll drive the stain deeper. If you’re dealing with a stubborn mark, a mild detergent solution (a drop of dish soap in warm water) works fine. Rinse with a clean damp cloth and blot dry.
- For deeper cleaning, you can use a carpet-cleaning solution designed for synthetic fibers. Always test in an inconspicuous corner first to ensure colorfastness.
- Drying is essential. Let the rug air dry completely before putting furniture back on it. A quick sun-dry on a gentle day can help, but avoid direct, prolonged sunlight to prevent color fade.
