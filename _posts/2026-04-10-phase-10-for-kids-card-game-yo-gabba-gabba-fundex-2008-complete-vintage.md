## How to Play: A Kid-Friendly Primer for Grown-Ups Too
Phase 10 is a set-collection game with ten distinct phases. Each phase requires you to collect a specific combination of cards (for example, a set of identical numbers or a run of consecutive numbers). The kids edition dials down the complexity to fit a younger audience while maintaining the core mechanic: complete your phase before your opponents do, but be mindful of the “wild” and “skip” cards that can derail a daydream of perfect planning.

- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a hand to each player, and reveal which player will go first. The Yo Gabba Gabba art helps the mind stay calm—until someone argues about who stole the last FroYo from the freezer.
- Turn flow: On your turn, you can either draw a card or pick from a discard pile. Then you attempt to complete the required phase for that round. If you succeed, you move on to the next phase with a new hand; otherwise, you keep your spot and try again next turn.
- Endgame: The first player to complete all ten phases wins. In a family setting, this usually triggers a chorus of celebratory shouting and a faint whiff of victory cake.

For younger players, the game emphasizes simple arithmetic, memory for what’s in your hand, and the social drama of table talk. For adults, it’s a pleasant reminder of how much faster kids move through phases than we do as seasoned gamers who still remember the exact stats of our favorite card sleeves.
