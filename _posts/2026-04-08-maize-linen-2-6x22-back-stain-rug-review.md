## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Durability
Back Stain Rug tech: The marketing copy calls it “Back Stain”; what that translates to in real life is a polymer or protective weave that resists common spills and makes cleaning easier. It is not a guarantee that spilled red wine will vanish instantly with a puff of fairy dust, but it helps you get to the stain faster and with less elbow grease.

Care guidelines:
- Vacuum regularly to prevent dirt build-up that can dull the color and texture.
- Blot, don’t rub, when a spill occurs. Rubbing pushes stains deeper and can wear down the pile.
- For hard stains, a mild detergent solution applied with a clean cloth should suffice. Test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate occasionally to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic hallways.

This rug is designed for living with daily life. It’s not a miracle, but in a busy family home, it’s a dependable ally that reduces the friction of everyday life.
