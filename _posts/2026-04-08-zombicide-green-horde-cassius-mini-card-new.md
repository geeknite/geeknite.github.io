## Cassius Mini: Design, Sculpt, and Assembly Tips

Cassius is the kind of mini that invites care and attention. Here are some practical notes to maximize your assembly and display game:

- Clean minor mold lines along the cloak edges and the arm holding the weapon. A quiet hobbyist is a deadly hobbyist, and quiet equals fewer battle shouts from your neighbors after you drop the mini in mid assemble. 
- Prime in thin layers. A light gray or black primer helps the greens pop and prevents you from painting the whole model twice because you forgot the base tone. 
- The cloak and armor pieces align cleanly with a snug press fit. Don t force the joints; a gentle wiggle is enough to seat Cassius properly. If you hear a click, you probably just unlocked your own inner painter. 
- The base texture can be quickly shaded with a brown wash and then dry brushed with a lighter brown to highlight the stonework. Voilà, your display piece looks like it belongs in a war council rather than a dust collection.

## Painting and Hobby Tips for Cassius

If you re a hobbyist who enjoys the painting side as much as the playing side, here are a few practical tips to maximize your results:

- Prime and thin: A primer in matte gray helps the greens settle in and makes details pop. Use thin layers to avoid obscuring fine lines. 
- Green gradients: Start with a dark green base and layer lighter greens toward the edges to create a natural gradient on the cloak and armor. A wash of dark brown around the crevices adds depth. 
- Metallic accents: The weapon benefits from metallic paints. Use a base metal tone followed by a wash to create weathering that reads well from a distance. 
- Bases matter: Don t ignore the base. A textured base with small tufts can ground the miniature in your diorama and improve overall presentation.
