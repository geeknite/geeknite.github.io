## Care, maintenance, and longevity
### Cleaning tips that actually work
- Vacuum regularly with a clean, low-suction setting to minimize fiber pulling. 
- For spot cleaning, dab gently with a mixture of mild detergent and water. Avoid harsh chemicals that might degrade fiber or alter color. 
- If you opt for a professional clean, schedule a test patch first to ensure the fibers don’t misbehave or shed a dramatic tear in public. 
- Regular rotation is recommended to prevent uneven wear, especially if you have a favorite chair that loves to plant itself on the rug for extended periods.

### Longevity expectations
With proper care, a Shankill rug can stay visually compelling for several years. The stems’ linearity means there are fewer circular patterns that look dated quickly, though color trends do shift. If a future interior trend demands a rug that looks like a blueprint for a botanical spaceship, the Shankill line might still hold its own because it is a classic in disguise. The major caveat is that colorfastness can vary by batch; check the dye lot and request a sample if you are particularly concerned about color drift after sun exposure.
