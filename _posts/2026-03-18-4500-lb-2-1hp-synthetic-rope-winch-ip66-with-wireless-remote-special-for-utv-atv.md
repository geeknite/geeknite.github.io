### Safety and best practices observed
- Wear gloves when handling rope to avoid frayed fibers and rope burn. 
- Stand clear of the rope path during operation and do not stand directly in line with the rope. 
- Use a tree strap to reduce the risk of damage to the anchor and to distribute the load.
- Inspect the rope for wear before, during, and after use. A small nick can become a larger problem if ignored.
- Keep the remote charged. Losing the remote in the middle of a recovery is bad news and a dramatic moment in your group chat.

## Maintenance and care
Synthetic rope is forgiving but not immortal. Regular inspection is essential. Look for unraveling fibers, flat spots, or any signs of moisture intrusion. If you see white powdery residue or a sour smell around the drum or rope, investigate further. Heat build up is another factor; after a series of pulls, give the unit a cool down period and avoid continuous operation that could cause unnecessary wear on the motor. Clean the exterior with a soft cloth and avoid harsh solvents that might degrade the protective coating. The controller, mount hardware, and electrical connections should be checked for corrosion after exposure to mud and water. If you respect the care guidelines, this winch can outlast the rough terrain you will throw at it on weekends and holiday trips.
