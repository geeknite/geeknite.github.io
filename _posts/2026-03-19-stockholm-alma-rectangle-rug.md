- Bedroom: position the rug under the lower two-thirds of the bed, so the rug is visible from both sides of the bed and you have a soft landing when getting out of bed.

And a note on alignment: if you use a rug pad, you will maximize its life and reduce curling at the edges. A simple felt pad or a padded rubber underlay works wonders. You want the pad to be slightly smaller than the rug to avoid gapping at the edges, which can catch your socks and trigger a dramatic but unwanted pratfall.

## daily use and durability tests

I put the Alma rug through a month of daily life with a mix of heavy foot traffic, a cat with a questionable love for cardboard boxes, and a couple of coffee disasters that would have made a less forgiving rug cry. The results were surprisingly positive. The fibers resisted staining better than you might expect, with common plant-based spills wiping away with a damp cloth. Red wine pretends to be dramatic, but with prompt action and a little gentle cleaning, you can keep the rug looking fine. For pet messes or stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution and a gentle blot, not scrub, works well. Remember that patience is a virtue here; aggressive scrubbing may cause the pile to flatten and create shiny patches that will remind you of a parking lot after a rainstorm in late autumn.

In terms of wear, the rug stayed plush after a month of daily use with a toddler building block towers on top and a gamer s chair rolling across the surface. The pile did not show heavy crushing, which is notable for synthetic blends used in busy living spaces. It is not a performance rug built for an art gallery; it will not survive the lava lamp as if nothing happened. But it holds up well in a real world setting where you are more likely to spill cocoa than to host a formal rug-testing lab.

## care, cleaning, and maintenance

Care is straightforward. Vacuum regularly with a low suction setting to avoid pulling fibers. Spills blot up promptly with a dry towel and then clean with a mild solution of water and gentle detergent. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners; they can damage the fibers and reduce stain resistance. If you do have a stubborn stain, test a small hidden area first and then treat the stain with a targeted cleaner. For those who like to keep things simple, an occasional rotate helps prevent uneven wear and keeps colors appearing balanced.

The backing is designed to resist slipping, but it s still wise to use a rug pad on hard floors. The pad will extend the rug s life and give you a more stable feel underfoot. If you have radiant floor heating, the rug s backing and fibers should tolerate it, but always check the manufacturer s care guidelines before enabling heat zones. In our test environment, heat did not cause noticeable warping or color bleed, which is a win in our book.
