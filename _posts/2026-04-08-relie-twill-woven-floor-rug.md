## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity
Durability is a big selling point here, and the care routine reflects that. Routine vacuuming with a standard attachment keeps surface dust from building up, and immediate blotting of spills prevents staining. The recommended approach is to treat fresh spills as soon as you notice them, using a mild detergent solution if needed. Avoid aggressive scrubbing, which can damage fiber texture or push liquid deeper into the backing. A quick, gentle pass with a damp cloth usually does the trick for most household accidents.

If you need more thorough cleaning, a shallow steam clean can be considered, but always follow the manufacturer’s guidance for your exact fiber blend. Do not misread the blog posts that promise miracle stain removal without consequences; the best outcomes come from a patient approach and a willingness to re-test on a small hidden area first. In the long run, the rug’s construction promises resilience. The twill pattern distributes wear more evenly than some flat weaves, helping to prevent unsightly flattening in high-traffic zones.

Edge durability is also a factor. The Relie Twill Woven Floor Rug typically features clean edges that stay neat after the occasional edge curling that happens to any rug over time. If you experience edge lift in a new rug, consider using a non-slip pad and, if needed, light ironing on the reverse side with a low heat setting—though you should exercise caution and follow any care instructions provided by the maker. The aim is to keep edges crisp and the entire surface aligned, which enhances longevity and keeps the room looking polished.
