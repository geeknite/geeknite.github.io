Two players (or two teams) duke it out, each controlling a bank of players on a single rod. A wrist flick nudges a player forward, while a careful grip maintains formation. The ball zips across the playfield with surprising velocity for its size, rewarding players who pair precise fingerwork with a dash of misdirection. ## Setup, Setup, and More Setup
Unboxing is the boring yet essential prelude to glory. In most models you’ll find the playfield, two banks of rods, players, screws, and possibly a tiny mounting tool. Assembly—if required—tends to be quick: attach the legs (where applicable), align the rods, and snap the players onto each rail. The goal is to get you playing faster than it takes to grind your beans for a perfect cup.

A few practical setup notes to save you from a wobble-induced existential crisis:
- Make sure the table sits level. If the surface is uneven, add a non-slip pad beneath the base to reduce wobble and improve accuracy.
- Tighten any loose components before the first match. You don’t want a rogue spring-loaded player launching the ball mid-tournament.
- Clean the playfield regularly. A clean surface reduces the risk of dust affecting ball bounce and helps ensure consistent play.
- For ongoing maintenance, a light wipe-down after play and occasional lubrication of joints keeps motion smooth and quiet.

In terms of space, plan for a surface roughly 18 to 24 inches long and 9 to 12 inches wide. It fits neatly on a coffee table, desk, or dedicated shelf, and it’s light enough to move without a forklift. If you plan to travel with it, a protective bag or case is a wise investment to protect the veneer from the inevitable conference room blues.
