Tips to up your game:

## Setup and Maintenance
Unboxing is typically straightforward. In most models you will find the playfield, two sets of rods, players, a few screws, and perhaps a tiny mounting tool. Assembly, if required, tends to be quick: attach the legs (if the model includes them), align the rods, and snap the players onto each rail. The goal is to get you playing in less time than it takes to brew your favorite coffee.

A few practical setup notes:
- Make sure the table sits evenly. If the surface is uneven, a non-slip pad beneath the base reduces wobble and improves accuracy.
- Tighten any loose components before your first match. You don’t want an accidental spring-loaded player launching the ball mid-tournament.
- Clean the playfield regularly. A clean surface reduces the risk of dust interfering with the ball’s roll and helps maintain consistent bounces.
- For maintenance, a light wipe-down after play and occasional lubrication on joints keeps the motion smooth and silent in day-to-day use.

In terms of space, plan for a surface about 18 to 24 inches long and 9 to 12 inches wide. It fits neatly on a coffee table, a desk, or a dedicated shelf, and it is light enough to move without requiring a forklift. If you intend to travel with it, consider a protective bag or case; the last thing you want is a scuffed veneer after a storm of friendly bragging.
