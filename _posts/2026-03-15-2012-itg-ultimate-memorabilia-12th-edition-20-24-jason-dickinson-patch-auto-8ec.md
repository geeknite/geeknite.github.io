## The Patch and Autograph: A Closer Look
### The Patch
Patch cards are the fancy cookies of the hobby: thick, a little glossy, and with a window that showcases a piece of real game-worn fabric. In ITG Ultimate Memorabilia, patches can run from two-tone to three- or four-color miracles that look like mini works of abstract art. The Jason Dickinson patch in this 20/24 card likely features a segment that nods to the team jersey—perhaps a strip of navy, red, and white, or whatever color scheme Dickinson wore during his ITG era. The patch is what draws many collectors in: it’s tangible, it’s from a real game, and it’s a reminder that a player wore the same fabric that fans and teammates saw in person.

In this card’s case, we don’t always get a full-quilt patch. Sometimes you get a clean two-color swatch that looks surprisingly stylish under a glass frame. The patch’s quality can be a talking point at card shows: “Look at that rectangle of cloth; you know that stripe probably zigged and zagged down the jersey sleeve like a river of color through a tundra.” The variability of patches is part of the thrill—and part of the reason we love or begrudge patch cards depending on how much you adore geometry and color-blocking in your display cabinet.
