## What is Fortune Row GM Screen?
Fortune Row GM Screen is a 3-panel (fold-out) game master screen designed for City of Mist, the neon-noir RPG about heroes who aren’t always heroic. The product ships with the usual combination of a sturdy cardboard exterior, a matte finish that resists fingerprints, and a set of inner panels that provide quick reference tables and setting prompts. On the design side, Fortune Row leans into the Fortune Row mythic arc—an in-setting street of fortune tellers, oddities, and secrets—while balancing readability with the need to keep crucial tables within easy reach of the GM.

## Design and Build
From the moment you handle Fortune Row, you can tell a lot of care went into the craft. The board is a dense cardboard stock with a glossy-matin finish that avoids glare even under a bright gaming lamp. The color palette is saturated but not oversaturated—neon pinks, electric blues, and a dash of brass to keep the look just a touch grittier than your average fantasy screen. The typography is crisp, with the essential blocks laid out in a way that begs the GM to rely on them rather than flip through the core rulebook every paragraph.

The edges are clean, the folds are sturdy, and the hinge mechanism feels durable without being too heavy for travel. The real trick is in the weight and balance: you can stand a basic map or a few tiles on the panels without fear of the screen tipping, and that matters when you’re juggling multiple NPCs, a grid, and a handful of Fate Points.

The Fortune Row art direction is intentionally noir with a splash of magical glam. If you’ve ever wondered what a fortune teller’s lair would look like if a superhero used it as a hideout, this screen gives you a taste of that vibe without making you feel like you’re in a cosplay photoshoot.

## How It Fits with City of Mist System
City of Mist is about superheroes who exist in a city of myths and legends. The system encourages both players and GM to speak in cinematic terms and to treat a single clue as a seed that could become a mythic arc throughout the night. Fortune Row, as a product, emphasizes that mythic arc in a tactile, physical format. The GM screen doesn’t pretend to be a rulebook replacement; rather, it consolidates the most invoked reference pages and the prompt-laden materials that help you keep the city’s mood intact.

From a design perspective, the Fortune Row screen aligns well with City of Mist’s tone: a stylish balance of noir grit and mythic tremors. The UI makes it easy to switch between investigative beats and action beats without pausing to flip through a core rulebook. The prompts that live on the back and inner sleeves add a touch of theater to the process, which is exactly what you want from a GM who’s trying to carry a session through a long night.

Of course, there are trade-offs. The Fortune Row GM Screen is intentionally lean. If you’re the kind of GM who wants every table, every page reference, and every percentile chart at your fingertips, you may find the screen’s density to be a little light. The design sacrifices exhaustive detail in favor of readability and speed. That’s a fair trade for many groups, but not all. If your table loves long, meticulous rule-churning, you may still want to keep the Core Rulebook at arm’s length. If you’re a GM who thrives on momentum, this is a practical companion.

Joy and humor is also a factor in City of Mist’s world, and Fortune Row respects that. Those on a long campaign will appreciate the occasional Easter egg: a note about a mysterious fortune that resolves into a mini-arc, an NPC who doubles as a “Fortune Row” informant, and a handful of prompts that you’ll actually reuse session after session.
