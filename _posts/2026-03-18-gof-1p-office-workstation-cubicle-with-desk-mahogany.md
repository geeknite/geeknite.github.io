## Assembly and Setup
### Tools and Time
Assembling the GOF 1P unit is a straightforward DIY project. You will likely need a Phillips head screwdriver, an adjustable wrench, and a patient attitude. Expect a first time assembly to take about 1 to 2 hours depending on how meticulously you line things up and whether you decide to skim the manual or read every diagram as if it were a treasure map. Pro tip: lay out parts in order, make sure you have a dry workspace, and avoid the catastrophic error of assembling in a narrow hallway where you can misplace a panel and both your sanity.

### Step by Step
1. Assemble the base frame and attach the side panels. Make sure everything sits square and level, because a skewed desk is the enemy of productivity.
2. Attach the main desk surface to the frame. It should sit flush and stable with even gaps and no wobbles.
3. Attach the cubicle panels according to the diagram in the manual. Double check for alignment; misaligned panels can lead to a rattling experience on windy days.
4. Install the cable management channels and storage compartments. Route cables neatly to keep air moving under the surface and to present a tidy appearance.
5. Add any optional finishing touches like trim pieces or storage bins if included. Tighten bolts and recheck all connections for stability.
6. Do a final wobble test. If there is wobble, adjust feet or levelers until the frame feels rock solid.

### Stabilization and Quality Control
A touch of wobble at first is not unusual, especially if you live in a residence with uneven floors. Tighten the bolts again after a day of use, recheck alignment, and reassure yourself that this is a normal part of the settling process. The veneer finish resists everyday scuffs and minor scratches, though no finish is invincible. Treat it with care and you will enjoy a desk that continues to look professional for years to come.
