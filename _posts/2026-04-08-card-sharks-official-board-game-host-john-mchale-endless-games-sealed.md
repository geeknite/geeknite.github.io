## Setup and rules overview (expanded)
### Get it together quickly
Setting up Card Sharks is a straightforward affair. Unseal the box, flip the board to the central track, give each player a color and a stack of chips, shuffle the deck and place it face down, and place the host card in a visible location. The objective is to accumulate the largest pile of chips or reach the set target score first, depending on the variant you decide to play. The sealed nature of this edition adds a dash of ceremonial drama to setup: you can pretend you’re unwrapping a classic relic, even if you’re just unsealing a modern game that was designed to emulate a stage spectacle.

### How a typical turn flows
On your turn you draw a card and decide whether the next card will be higher or lower. If you predict correctly, you advance along the track and gain chips. If you are wrong, you lose a turn or pass the card to the next player depending on the house rules you fancy. The pacing of a turn invites quick decisions and playful banter, which is what a card sharks-themed game should be all about. The rules include a few twists like bonus rounds or penalty boxes to keep the energy up and the table lively. It is not a heavy strategy game, but it rewards reading the room and knowing when to push your luck. The rulebook does a commendable job of balancing clarity with personality, so you feel like you’re following a game that knows its own mood.

Because this game leans into party vibes, the pacing is crucial. The faster the turn flows, the more energy you capture around the table.
