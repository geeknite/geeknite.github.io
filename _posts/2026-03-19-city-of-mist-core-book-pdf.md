## In-depth: The Mythos, Logos, and Keys

The heart of City of Mist rests on three ideas: Mythos, Logos, and Keys. Mythos is the power that myths supply to a hero; it’s the source of their unusual abilities and the cause of the occasional dramatic reveal. Logos is the modern world—the city, its institutions, its technology, and its relationships. When you combine Mythos with Logos, you get a hero who can draw on a mythic force precisely when their modern life allows it to surface in ways that can be dramatic or subtle. The Keys are the secret identities, the hidden facets of a character’s life that they keep shielded from the public eye. They are the narrative hooks that let a player pull in a thematic thread from scene to scene.

## Mechanics and playstyle

This PDF gives you the core dice mechanic to resolve tasks: a two-die pool that yields a spectrum of outcomes. The target number is not a rigid wall; rather, a roll can push the story toward success with style, or open a door to a complication that the GM can use to move the plot forward. The narrative emphasis is unmistakable: the game asks, what does the character believe about the legend they are becoming? The answer shapes how they act and how other characters respond. The rules are light enough that a new GM can pick them up quickly, while still offering a platform for players who enjoy digging into mythic symbolism and personal arcs.

## Character creation: an example walk-through

To illustrate how a character might form in this system, imagine a character named Mira Valen, a private investigator who doubles as a fallen goddess of memory. Mira’s Mythos is Memory itself, a source of uncanny recall and a tendency to misremember details when the stakes are high. Her Logos includes investigative technique, a network of informants, and a stubborn moral compass—that is, when her memory doesn’t betray her first. Her Keys could be “The Hidden Face” and “The Price of Truth.” The Hidden Face means she keeps her deity lineage and her mortal persona separate from her public persona, while The Price of Truth binds her fate to an obligation to recover what was lost. In your own group, you will find many different Tag-based combos that yield unique moves and personal storytelling opportunities.

The PDF’s sections walk you through this step-by-step, and there are prompts for you to fill out during a session. You can also use the sample character sheets included in the book, which show how to record Mythos points, how you interpret your Key’s prompts, and how you manage your public and hidden identities during a given scene.

## GM tools: building investigations and scenes

The GM toolkit in City of Mist Core Book PDF is structured to help you create a sequence of investigations where players uncover clues, encounter misdirection, and eventually build a picture of the city’s underlying myth. You begin with a mystery hook—something that is plausible in the real world, yet hints at a mythic layer. Then you add scenes that escalate the tension. The PDF suggests a few techniques to help you pace the investigation: a series of "turns" in which the players reveal their connections to the legends behind the city's façade, a section on how to “burn” a clue to push the plot toward escalation, and guidance on how to coordinate the discovery of clues with the players' Keys.

The sample mysteries demonstrate how a single clue can lead to a larger conspiracy. For example, a missing statue might reveal a deity’s hidden shrine beneath an abandoned subway station, while the statue’s disappearance becomes the seed for a conflict between a corporate power and an underground network of mythic communities. The PDF shows how to stage the reveal of the mythic layer in a way that feels earned rather than forced.
