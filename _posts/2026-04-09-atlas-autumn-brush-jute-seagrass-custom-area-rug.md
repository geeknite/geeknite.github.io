## Durability and wear

Jute and seagrass fibers are known for their durability, but not for their tenderness. They can handle high traffic and scrub-friendly lifestyles, but you should not expect the rug to forgive every petty accident. The rug is robust, with a sturdy weave that resists flattening under expected daily use. If you have a dog who likes to circle before lying down, the rug will stand up to their pacing and still feel comfortable under paw pads.

### Abrasion resistance

The interwoven fibers resist typical foot traffic and furniture dragging. If you move heavy chairs around, the underside remains stable and the top remains relatively intact. It does not shed a ton, which is a pleasant surprise for natural fibers.

### Stain resistance and cleaning

Stains on natural fiber rugs can be tricky. For light spills, blot gently with a clean cloth and avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper. Drying is essential since jute and seagrass fibers can become stiff or warped with moisture. For tougher stains, consider a mild cleaning solution and gentle agitation; avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade fiber integrity.

## Cleaning and maintenance

Maintenance is straightforward but not exactly lazy. Regular vacuuming with a low-power setting helps to keep dust and debris from lodging in the weave. A rug pad under the rug adds grip and helps to prevent curling at the edges. For spills, act quickly; absorb with a clean cloth and avoid saturating the fibers. If you notice pilling or fuzz, a fabric shaver can help; just be gentle to avoid cutting the fiber.

### Seasonal care

The rug benefits from occasional rotation to even out wear and sun exposure. If you place it in a sunny area, you may notice slight color changes over time, which is part of the natural charm. Rotate every few months to maintain uniform appearance and prevent color drift bias.

### Eco-minded tips

If sustainability is your jam, you will be pleased that the rug uses natural fibers and avoids synthetic dyes that can be less eco-friendly. Natural fibers prefer moderate humidity. In spaces with high humidity or moisture, ensure adequate airflow to prevent fiber swelling. If you live in a coastal area with damp air, consider dehumidification or a breathable underlay to preserve shape and texture over time.
