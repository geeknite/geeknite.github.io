## Setup Tips and House Rules
To maximize your enjoyment and minimize pregame chaos, here are a few practical tips:

- Pre-sort components by zone before the party arrives. It saves you 10 to 15 minutes of frantic rummaging while the first wave of undead shuffles toward your hero.
- Assign a designated Rules Master who can temporarily narrate the critical rules while others do the initial turns. This keeps the vibe fun and reduces table talk fatigue.
- Use an approximate time limit. If you want a single session, set an end timer of around 90 to 120 minutes for a compact but epic game night; if you want longer, you can stretch this to a 2 to 3 hour marathon, especially with a larger group.
- Have a snack plan that won’t require you to pause the action too often. Sausages and elbows work great if you can avoid sugary chaos that leads to sticky fingers on miniatures.

For players who want to experiment with house rules, you can introduce mild tweaks to spawn rates or healing mechanics to tailor the difficulty to your crew. It’s a flexible system in a way that rewards experimentation without breaking the core design. Be mindful, though: too many house rules can turn a tight experience into a spreadsheet simulation. Balance is key.
