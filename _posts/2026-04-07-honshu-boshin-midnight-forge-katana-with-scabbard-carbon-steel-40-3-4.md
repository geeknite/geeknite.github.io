## Maintenance and Care

Carbon steel requires a little more love than stainless steels. Here’s a practical routine you can follow without becoming a full-time steel alchemist:

- Clean after use: wipe with a dry cloth to remove oil and fingerprints. If needed, use a mild, non-abrasive cleaner and dry completely.
- Oil the blade lightly: a thin layer of light mineral oil or camellia oil will help prevent rust. Wipe off any excess.
- Check fittings and wrap: make sure the mekugi peg is secure, the tsuka wrap sits snugly, and there’s no looseness in the fittings.
- Store properly: keep the blade in a dry, low-humidity environment. If you’re storing the sword long-term, consider removing the blade from the saya for some time to allow air circulation. This is a general guideline; always follow the manufacturer’s specific recommendations when provided.

For cosplay and display, you can also consider adding a small silica gel pack in the display area, as long as it doesn’t come into direct contact with the blade or scabbard. But do not put silica gel inside the blade or the saya. No one wants a crunchy blade.
