### Grading considerations for NM
Near mint means surface looks clean, corners show no wear under a 10x loupe, and the holo still shines with a crisp, even finish. When evaluating NM for an AR card like this, look for the following:
- Centering: Eye-pleasing, with minor deviations allowed to the left or right but not enough to feel deliberate.
- Edges: No major whitening, only faint whispers of edge wear that are barely visible under natural light.
- Surface: No deep scratches, no fingerprint smudges, and no specks under the holo that would suggest a rough life in a binder.
- Foil stability: The holo should be flat and responsive to light, not peeled or bubbled.

If you find a copy that checks these boxes, you are likely looking at a NM specimen that will hold or appreciate value in the right market conditions. The actual market value swings with supply, the broader Pokemon card economy, and the overall hype around SV3 alt arts, but the card’s design and aesthetic make it a strong candidate for a shelf-claim over a mere playset in a baggie.

## Condition matters: what NM really means here
### A closer look at NM for this card
Near mint is a sweet spot for alt arts because it preserves both the visual wow factor and the structural integrity of the holo finish. For this Gloom variant, NM suggests that the foil finish looks lively, the surface holds up to light without scuffing, and the corners show little to no spine wear. If you are hunting for a pristine NM example, you should examine the card under a bright light source and check for any signs of border whitening or edge chipping. If your card passes these checks, you can confidently label it NM and place a fair value on it in your personal catalog.

### How to verify NM in practice
- Inspect under 45 to 60 lumens of light to catch micro-whitening along the edges.
- Gently tilt the card to check for any color bleed or haze on the holo; a perfectly NM card will maintain a clean holo bloom as you rotate.
- Compare centering to standard variants of the same print; while not exact replicas, generally SV3 alt arts cut with a similar tolerance. A visibly misaligned print can still be valuable, but it is not NM if the centering is off enough to draw attention.
