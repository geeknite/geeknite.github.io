For the curious minds among us, a quick origin story: kilim is a flat-woven textile created by weaving motifs into the warp and weft rather than piling up a plush surface. Dhurrie is a specific weaving technique that yields a dense, flat finish. The wool adds softness and color, while the jute provides rustic texture and a bit of earthiness. Choosing the right size matters. A rug that is too small looks like an afterthought; too large can overwhelm the space. Layering is another option. In bigger rooms, place a neutral larger rug under the seating area and use the Kilim Dhurrie on top to add color and texture. The reversible feature gives you two distinct layers of style without buying two separate rugs.

## Care and Maintenance

Natural fibers require a gentle touch. Vacuum on a low setting and with a brushless attachment to avoid fraying. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and to minimize sun fade on one side if your room gets strong sunlight.

Spills are manageable with quick action. Blot the spill with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the fibers. For stains, use a mild detergent solution on a small, inconspicuous area first to test colorfastness, then treat if needed. Never soak the rug; excess moisture can cause warping or dye bleed.

Shed is normal in the early weeks with wool. A short period of higher-frequency vacuuming will speed up the process. The reversible design can help distribute wear more evenly as you rotate which side is facing up.

### When to call a pro

If you spill something heavy like red wine and can’t lift it quickly, consider hiring a professional rug cleaner who specializes in natural fiber rugs. They can address dye migration and fiber separation issues without harming the weave.

## Buying Tips

- Always verify dimensions; a common misstep is buying a rug that looks right on the showroom floor but dwarfs the room once laid out.
- Inspect the weave and finish. A well finished edge should feel sturdy, with even stitching and a consistent weave across the surface.
- Inquire about dye fastness and color variation. Handmade rugs can have slight variation, which is part of their charm but worth knowing before purchase.
- If possible, request color samples to compare under your lighting. Natural light can drastically alter a rug’s appearance indoors.
