### UNO Mechanics in a Modern Bag
UNO is a game that wears its simplicity like a badge of honor. Each turn, you match the top card in color or number, with wild and special cards that bend reality (and sometimes your friends’ patience). This pack doesn’t reinvent UNO; it adds a coexisting universe with a Skip-Bo mechanic. The UNO portion remains fast-paced, chaotic shedding. If you’re not careful, the table will be yelling numbers back at you in a chorus of frustration and joy.

### The Skip-Bo Angle
Skip-Bo is all about building piles in ascending order. The name says it all: you skip to the final destination, ideally without tripping over your own cards. In the bonus pack, Skip-Bo is integrated as a companion experience rather than a stand-alone title. The synergy between building piles and shedding cards is not perfect, but surprisingly satisfying when the table collectively groans at a missed turn.

### A Quick Rules Primer: UNO in a Nutshell
- The goal is to shed all your cards first.
- You can place a card on a card of the same color or number.
- Special cards alter the flow: skip, reverse, draw two, wild, and wild draw four.
- The pack includes standard UNO rules plus small adjustments for merging with Skip-Bo.

### A Quick Rules Primer: Skip-Bo in a Nutshell
- Build piles from 1 to 12 in ascending order.
- Use stock piles to supply cards to the table.
- The first person to play all their cards wins.
- The included deck integrates with UNO in a way that can be confusing at first, but once you get the rhythm, it’s a satisfying puzzle.
