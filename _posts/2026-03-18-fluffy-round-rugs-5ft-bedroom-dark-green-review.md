## Durability and Care
### Cleaning tips for shaggy materials
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting. A handheld vacuum or a vacuum with an adjustable height works best to avoid snagging fibers.
- For small spills: blot, don’t rub. Use a mild detergent mixed with water, test on a hidden area first, and blot again.
- Deep cleaning: if your rug is machine-washable or has a removable cover, follow the manufacturer’s instructions. If not, you can opt for professional cleaning or a thorough DIY approach with a rug-cleaning solution suited to high-pile fabrics.

### Shedding and longevity
First-run shedding can occur with many plush rugs. Don’t panic if a few fibers shed after you unroll it; this tends to settle after a few weeks of regular vacuuming. To minimize shedding, avoid aggressive friction during cleaning and consider a soft-bristle brush to maintain the pile’s loft. With proper care, this rug should keep its fluffy charm for years without turning your bedroom into a pet mat of shedding confetti.

### Stain resistance and colorfastness
Dark green helps disguise minor stains, but spills should be attended promptly to prevent set-in marks. Consider a quick spot-clean routine for accidental spills from coffee, wine, or the occasional late-night snack-attack. A small, portable stain remover suitable for synthetic fibers can be a lifesaver here. If colorfastness is a concern, you can perform a small test on an inconspicuous edge before treating the stain.

## Maintenance Tips
### Routine vacuuming and rotating the rug
- Vacuum at least once or twice a week depending on foot traffic. Use a low suction setting to protect fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and prevent a sun-kissed patch if your room gets a lot of natural light.

### Spot-cleaning schedule
Set up a simple spot-clean routine: attend to spills within 15 minutes when possible, blot thoroughly, and test any cleaners on a small, hidden edge first. For stubborn spots, consult a rug-cleaning guide or consider calling a professional for delicate fabrics.

### Deep-clean cadence
Every 6–12 months, consider a deeper clean either by a professional service or a DIY method suitable for synthetic high-pile rugs. Deep-cleaning helps lift accumulated dirt, refresh the fiber loft, and extend the rug’s life. If you’re DIY-curious, there are plenty of rug-safe cleaners that won’t degrade the backing or the fibers.
