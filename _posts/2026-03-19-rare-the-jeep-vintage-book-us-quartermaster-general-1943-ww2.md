## Caring for a WWII era gem: storage, handling, and preservation tips

Even a sturdy book like this benefits from a little care. Here are practical tips for preserving a copy without turning your living room into a climate-controlled bunker:

- Keep it away from direct sunlight. UV light is the enemy of paper and ink, even when the ink isn’t all that fancy to begin with.
- Control humidity. A stable humidity around 40–50% helps prevent paper embrittlement and mold growth. If you don’t have a climate-controlled room, consider a display case with a desiccant packet and a small breathable cover.
- Handle with clean hands or cotton gloves. Oils and dirt from skin transfer can slowly degrade the binding and margins.
- Store flat or upright in appropriate archival boxes. If you must stack, make sure the weight doesn’t crush the spine.
- Avoid excessive handling. Some readers love margins with a history, but the longer the margins, the higher the chance of future loss. Thin corners, crisp pages, and a steady spine are the trifecta of a long-lived artifact.

If you’re truly devoted, seek professional restoration advice before attempting any major repair. A pro can stabilize paper, address brittle bindings, and preserve the volume’s integrity while leaving its character intact.
