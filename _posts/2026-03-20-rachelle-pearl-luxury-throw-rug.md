## Cleaning and Maintenance

Cleaning is a reality check for many high-end textiles. The Rachelle Pearl is easier to maintain than some of its premium competitors, but it is not maintenance-free. Here’s a practical maintenance plan:

- Vacuum weekly with a low to medium suction setting. High suction on delicate piles can cause micro-tears over time.
- For spills, blot—not rub—with a clean cloth. Rubbing can push the stain deeper into the fibers.
- For stubborn stains, use a mild cleaning solution and rinse with a damp cloth. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fiber.
- For odor control, sprinkle a small amount of baking soda after vacuuming and leave it for 15-20 minutes before brushing off.
- To prevent sliding, use a rug pad or gripper beneath the rug, especially on hard floors.
- Rotate every 3-6 months to ensure even wear and fade avoidance in areas with strong direct sunlight.

The cleaning guide is not a mystery manual; it’s straightforward, designed to help you preserve the rug’s beauty without turning cleaning into a chore. A well-maintained rug will last several years with proper care, a number that makes the initial price feel more reasonable.
