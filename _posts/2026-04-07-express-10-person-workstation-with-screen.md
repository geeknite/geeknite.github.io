### Materials and durability
The Express is built to endure the daily grind of a busy office or a startup’s late-nights whiteboard marathons. ### Cable management and power setup
Cable management is where the Express earns real world respect. There’s a central trough for power and data, plus discreet grommets at each seat. The goal is a tidy appearance and predictable maintenance rather than a scavenger hunt for a stray HDMI cable. Expect a few days of tuning to get everything perfectly aligned; once there, the visual tidy-up is glorious.

Power options range from traditional outlets to PoE-enabled devices. If you’re running LEDs under the desk or want peripherals to pull power directly from the network, PoE can simplify your life and reduce the number of wall adapters cluttering your space. The trade-off is that PoE may limit high-widelity peripherals if you push the wattage envelope, so plan accordingly.

### Assembly steps and setup tips
The assembly steps aren’t a ritual; they’re a workflow. Most of the heavy lifting falls to two people and a good set of metric tools. Expect the first hour to be a cooperative puzzle when you’re aligning the central hub with the individual monitor arms. After that, you’ll appreciate the modularity: moving seats, reconfiguring for a new team, or adding extra screens becomes a matter of snapping pieces in place rather than deconstructing a whole office.

Pro tip: label the power and data cables during setup. It cuts setup and future maintenance time by at least 40 percent. The engineering folks at the factory obviously designed the thing with this in mind, but human beings tend to forget where they plugged in the mysterious black box labeled Do Not Touch. Labeling makes life easier for the facilities team and your future self who will thank you for not spending a quarter of your career in a data cave looking for a missing HDMI cable.
