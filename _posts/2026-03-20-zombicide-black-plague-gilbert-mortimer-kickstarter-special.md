## Thematic and mechanical flavor

### What Gilbert and Mortimer bring to the table
In the world of Zombicide Black Plague, you control survivors battling a flood of undead. The Kickstarter Special introduces two new figures with distinct flavors: Gilbert is the smooth talker who excels at leadership and maneuvering your squad into better positions, while Mortimer is the eccentric alchemist whose experiments can tilt the odds in a pinch. The rule set for these two is designed to complement the base game rather than overshadow it. You will find yourself orchestrating a plan where Gilbert sets up Mortimer to pull off a fortuitous brew that improves your team’s survivability for a crucial round.

### Balancing and pacing
The power curve remains friendly to new players while offering a satisfying depth for veterans. Gilbert and Mortimer encourage coordination; their abilities reward planning, but they do not demand you to reengineer every tactic in your campaign. It is a light touch that expands your tactical toolkit without turning the game into a spreadsheet duel. If you enjoy watching a plan come together and then laughing at the inevitable buffet of zombies that crashes your plan, you will feel right at home.

## How to maximize your use of Gilbert and Mortimer in your campaigns

### Friendly onboarding for new players
If you are teaching Zombicide Black Plague to a new group, use Gilbert and Mortimer as a gentle introduction to the idea of coordinated actions and temporary buffs. Let newcomers play base survivors in the first few rounds, then unveil the special duo to demonstrate how synergy can shift the tide of a mission without overwhelming the table.

### House rules and optional tweaks
Some groups may wish to tweak the cooldowns or the buff windows to suit longer or shorter campaigns. A simple approach is to throttle Mortimer buff duration by one round in longer sessions or to adjust Gilbert’s coordination boost to trigger only once per turn sequence. The goal is to keep the experience fluid and fun, not to turn it into a simulation of a lab accident turned into a chess match. Remember, the most important part is to keep the table chatting and the pace moving, so folks stay engaged even when the zombies decide to march in force.
