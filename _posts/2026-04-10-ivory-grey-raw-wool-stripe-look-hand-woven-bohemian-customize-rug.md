### Durability, Cleaning, and Maintenance: The Real-Life Playbook

Durability is hall-of-fame-worthy when you live in a real home. A hand-woven wool rug with a stripe pattern should handle daily traffic well and maintain its character for years with just a little care. Here’s the practical maintenance playbook:
- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting and a gentle beater bar or suction-only attachment to avoid loosening fibers.
- Rotate the rug every 3–6 months to ensure even wear and to prevent the high-traffic zones from wearing a different shade than the rest.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can work the stain deeper into the fibers.
- For stubborn stains, consider a wool-safe cleaner or a professional cleaning after a year or so, depending on usage.
- Consider a rug pad to prevent slipping and to add a little extra cushioning underfoot.

These steps will help the rug maintain its crisp stripe rhythm and keep the ivory tone from yellowing due to sunlight exposure. If you’re concerned about sun exposure, rotate the rug and use window treatments to moderate brightness during peak hours.

### Care Instructions: Quick Guide for Long-Term Wow

- Vacuum weekly to remove surface dirt and sustain the rug’s crisp stripe definition.
- Rotate every 3–6 months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Blot spills gently with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber damage.
- For deep cleans, consider wool-safe detergents or professional cleaning every 12–24 months depending on use.
- Protect from direct sun where possible to reduce natural fading in ivory tones.

A little routine maintenance goes a long way with wool. It’s the kind of thing that pays you back with years of warm, stylish ground cover and a floor that doesn’t look like it survived a toddler’s art project every week.
