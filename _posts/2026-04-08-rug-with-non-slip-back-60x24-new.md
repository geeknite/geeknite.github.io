### Cleaning and maintenance
Life happens. Spills will happen. The top surface tolerates a quick blot with a cloth and a mild cleaner. For deeper cleaning, you can pull the rug and hand wash the top layer or use a gentle cycle in a washing machine if the manufacturer’s care instructions permit. The backing should not be submerged, as prolonged contact with water could degrade the adhesive over time. Regular vacuuming keeps dust at bay and helps sustain the non-slip performance by preventing dust from building up in the fibers.

A quick tip: if you live in a humid climate, ensure the rug is fully dry before laying it back on the floor. Moisture can reduce the backing’s grip until it evaporates. If you’re storing the rug during a season, roll it with the back facing inward to protect the rubber backing and to prevent any deformation.
