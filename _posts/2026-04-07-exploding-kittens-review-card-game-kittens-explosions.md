## What is Exploding Kittens?
Exploding Kittens is a card game designed by Elan Lee and Matthew Inman, drawn to life by The Oatmeal's art and a Kickstarter that showed us the internet can fund a lot of explosions and cats. The base game is a fast-paced, take-that style card game where players take turns drawing cards from a single stack. The goal is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten. If you draw one, you explode unless you have a Defuse card to save yourself. The cards in your hand let you skip turns, shove your misfortune onto someone else, peek at future draws, and even shout out No! as an interrupt classic that has become a signature moment in modern party gaming.

## Components
The base deck includes a mix of Exploding Kitten cards, Defuse cards, and action cards that modify the flow of play. The base set typically includes enough pieces for 2 to 5 players, with the possibility to expand via Party Pack or NSFW expansion to accommodate bigger groups. A good rule of thumb is to count how many players you have and then add a few Defuse cards so that early plays are not guaranteed doom.

- Exploding Kitten cards, which are the ticking time bombs of the party
- Defuse cards, the lifesavers that buy you a moment of meme-worthy relief
- Attack cards that force the next player to draw multiple cards
- Skip cards that let you dodge a draw entirely or pass the fate forward
- See the Future cards that let you peek at the top few cards of the deck
- See the Peace? No! card that serves as an interrupt on a best laid plan
- Nope cards to cancel someone else action or a big-move plan
- Shuffle and Alter the Draw to inject a little chaos into the order of events
- The art by The Oatmeal that makes every kitten seem either heroic or suspicious

Artwork on the cards is comic and cute, which works as a comedic layer that softens what would otherwise be a brutal “you lose” mechanic. The art style is intentionally over the top, and you will probably remember your favorite kitten with a superhero cape while your opponent swears that this is the one time the game will turn against them.

## Setup
Setting up Exploding Kittens is almost charmingly simple. Shuffle the Exploding Kitten cards and the Defuse cards together, then deal a starting hand to each player (usually four cards in the base game). The number of Defuse cards in the deck is tuned so that early plays are not guaranteed doom. After everyone has a hand, place the remaining deck in the center, draw a few initial cards to seed the game, and keep your eyes peeled for that first bright red explosion of chaos.

The number of players changes the flavor. With 2 players, the match becomes a fast duel; with 3 to 5 players, you get more social misdirection, triumphant laughter, and the occasional heated argument about who has to take the draw next. If you own the Party Pack, you can push this up to around 9 players, making it a true party starter that lasts long enough for a pizza break.

## How to Play: A Quick Tour
The core loop of Exploding Kittens is simple, but the timing matters. On your turn, you may play any number of cards from your hand to influence the deck or to avoid a direct draw. Most players begin by holding their Defuse cards close to their chest, because the moment you rely on one, the universe will reveal its own cruel sense of humor and you will likely draw anyway.

- Draw a card from the central deck to begin your turn. If it is an Exploding Kitten and you do not have a Defuse card, you are out. If you do have a Defuse, you can play it to save yourself, and then you must place the Exploding Kitten back into the deck at any position you prefer, altering the odds in your favor.
- You can play an action card to skip your turn, peek at future draw cards, steal a card from another player, or disrupt an opponent's plans. The goal is to avoid the explosion while forcing chaos upon others.
- No, you may not shout No when you draw, but you and your friends will, which makes the game feel like a chaotic anime montage set in a living room.

The Nope card is the star of the show. It allows you to negate a move from another player or from the deck itself, but you can only use it if you have one at the right moment. The timing of a Nope is a dramatic moment that can swing the entire round, especially when someone tries to sabotage your Defuse or demands a risky draw after you have just shown everyone a future peek.

See the Future cards bring a scientific edge to the chaos. You get to peek at the top three cards, which lets you plan or pretend you are in a strategic mastermind role even as you are about to draw into a catastrophe. The humor comes not from the complexity of rules but from the anticipation of what comes next. In an actual round, the future peek often becomes a dramatic pause as players debate whether to risk a high-stakes draw or bluff their way through the deck order.

## Strategy and Experience
Exploding Kittens thrives on social interaction more than on deep strategy. There is a degree of luck in the draw order and the composition of your hand, but there is also a surprising amount of meta-gamesmanship. Here are some practical tips for maximizing your joy and minimizing the existential dread of explosions.

- Hold Defuse cards until you truly need them. The best moment to play a Defuse is often when the deck feels like a ticking clock, not a random card draw. You want the Defuse to feel earned rather than forced.
- Use See the Future to map out the next few draws, then bluff your way through a risk that could take the entire room down. People love to pretend they are the genius strategist when really they are selecting a card to Dalai Lama-balance the deck.
- Nope is where the real game happens. If you can memorize who played what and when, you will know exactly the right moment to interrupt. The chaos of Nope is a social glue that binds players together in a chorus of laughter and disbelief.
- Attack and Shuffle cards create interesting shifts in power. Attack doubles the rate at which someone else will be forced to draw, while Shuffle reorders the threats of the deck, giving you a little room to breathe. The right mix of these cards can transform a near loss into a surprising save by passing the risk around the table.
- See the Future pairs well with bluffing. If you pretend you have a robust plan to avoid Exploding Kittens, others may overcommit to defense and basic panic. The social dynamics of Exploding Kittens come not from the rules but from the way players try to outwit one another with casual confidence and a dash of cat-based bravado.

## House Rules and Variants
If you want to customize Exploding Kittens beyond the official packs, here are a few playful house rules that can extend the fun:
- Two-team mode: Split players into two teams. You can share Defuse cards with teammates but not reveal your strategy to opponents. This creates a different dynamic, where coordination and misdirection happen within a team context.
- Double Defuse: If a Defuse is used, you get to draw another card before your turn ends. This adds a risk/reward element that can swing late-game momentum.
- Time-boxed rounds: Set a timer for three rounds per game. After the timer ends, the team with the least explosions wins. This makes for a high-energy session where you want to keep the pace brisk.
