## Design and usability: how it fits into real life

The GOF 3P is designed with the modern desk worker in mind. ### Cable management and accessibility
Cabling is the stealthy ninja of office furniture. The GOF 3P’s cable troughs, grommets, and clip systems are designed to guide wires cleanly from desk to power hub without turning your workstation into a maze of tangles. Effective cable management not only improves aesthetics but also reduces the risk of accidental unplugging and tripping, which is a public service to your colleagues and your CFO who loves a nice, clean expense sheet.

## Assembly and setup: the moment of truth

### Unboxing and first impressions
If you’ve ever opened a box and found a sentence like “Some assembly required,” you know the suspense of the first unboxing. ### Step-by-step assembly vibe
Expect a couple of hours for a casual installer to assemble the basic unit, more if you’re juggling a team and a playlist. The process is a mix of assembling the desks, attaching the privacy panels, securing the cable channels, and ensuring each module aligns with the others. The tool list is concise: basic Allen keys, a screwdriver, and a rubber mallet might come in handy for stubborn panel clamps. A good tip is to pre-sort fasteners by size and keep the instruction sheet within arm’s reach. A little planning saves a lot of swearing later.

### Post-assembly: do a quick reliability test
Before you invite your team to move in, give the unit a test run. Sit at each station, adjust the height, plug in a power strip, route cables through the troughs, and push lightly on panels to test wobble. If you find anything that seems unstable, torque the fasteners a touch more. A well-built cubicle should feel solid and whisper quiet during normal use. If you hear creaks or squeaks, you may have missed a bracket or misaligned a panel edge. Don’t panic; most issues are easily fixed with a bit more tightening and a second look at the alignment marks.
