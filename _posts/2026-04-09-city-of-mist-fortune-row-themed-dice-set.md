What’s in Fortune Row? A Look at the Dice and Their Thematic Language
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Fortune Row isn’t selling you just seven dice; it’s selling you seven little actors with a noir flair. In typical tabletop fashion, a standard dice set for a tabletop RPG includes d4, d6, d8, d10 (two numbers for percentile), d12, and a d20. Fortune Row sticks to that framework but dresses each die in a City of Mist coat of paint: coppery accents, smoky transparent resin, and runes that feel like a street magician’s prop shop exploded in a coffee shop.

- Dice count and types: The set contains the full complement of core polyhedral dice: d4, two d6s, d8, two d10s (percentile), d12, and d20. The two d10s are color-mashed to give you a quick percentile readout, which is essential for City of Mist’s many Fate checks and “unlock a clue” moments.
- Materials and feel: The dice are a resin-based concoction with a smoky, almost astronaut-glass clarity. The copper foil flecks catch the light and reflect the neon vibes of Fortune Row—the fictional district where secrets have a price and every corner hums with a possible origin story. The edges are chamfered to a comfortable bite; you’re less likely to nick a die, more likely to accidentally carve a destiny on your character sheet.
- Iconography and readability: Instead of standard numerals, Fortune Row uses stylized glyphs and noir-era symbols that hint at the City of Mist’s “mythic identity” mechanic. The glyphs are readable with a bit of habit (you’ll do the “read a die face” dance after a couple of sessions), and they translate into in-game actions with a satisfying, cinematic clarity. If you prefer clean numeric reads, you’ll still be able to interpret results quickly in the heat of a chase scene—just remember the glyphs correlate to your Fate aspects in play, not random abstract art.

The theme work here isn’t just cosmetic. The dice feel like they belong to a street-level myth—the kind of artifacts an investigator would dust for prints while a neon rain falls in a back-alley cityscape. If the City of Mist universe could be distilled into a tactile object, Fortune Row would be its most dapper cousin, tipping its hat as you roll for consequences that could tilt a scene from “mysterious suspect” to “reveal that changes everything.”

Design Language: Aesthetics That Speak Without Interrupting Gameplay
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Design is more than skin-deep in a well-made dice set; it’s a language you use when you roll. Fortune Row’s aesthetic language slides between “sleek urban villainy” and “curiously magical streetwear”—and it does so with intent. The dice look good on a table, but more importantly, they feel good in the hand. The surface texture is smooth enough to roll easily, but not so slick that you worry about losing control during a tense moment.

- Color palette: A smoky base with copper accents. It reads as both “femme fatale” and “gadget-laden informant” at the same time.
- Glyph system: Each die face replaces a number with a symbol that ties to Fate and Myth in City of Mist. The glyphs aren’t just pretty; they map to the game’s mechanics in ways that reward players who’ve internalized their character’s Identity, Power, and Legend.
- Durability: Resin dice like these can survive the rough love of many gaming nights, but you should still be mindful of resin’s tendencies to scratch if used as a doorstop (don’t be that gamer). A soft dice tray and a gentle hand will keep the foil accents intact longer than you expect.
