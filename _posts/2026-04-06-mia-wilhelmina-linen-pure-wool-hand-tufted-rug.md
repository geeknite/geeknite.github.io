## Comfort, Maintenance, and Everyday Life
### Feel underfoot
If you’re the sort who spends evenings lounging on the floor with a laptop or a glass of wine, this rug is surprisingly comfortable. The wool pile provides a cushioned surface that’s soft on feet but supportive enough for standing tasks. It won’t feel like walking on a marshmallow, but it won’t impersonate a gym mat, either. Your pets will appreciate it; their claws sink in with a satisfying little give, and a quick brushing after a shed season keeps things tidy.

### Vacuuming and fiber care
Wool tends to shed a little at first—this is normal for many new wool rugs. A few passes with a vacuum that has an adjustable height setting should reduce the loose fiber during the initial months. Use a vacuum with a gentle suction setting and avoid beater bars on high power. Over time, shedding settles down as the fibers settle into their place. A routine of monthly, thorough cleanings plus spot cleaning for spills will keep the rug looking sharp for years.

### Stain resistance and spills
Wool fibers are naturally stain resistant to an extent due to their structure, but that doesn’t mean you should treat it as invincible. Address spills quickly with blotting rather than rubbing. For many household spills, a mild detergent and water solution can do the trick. While wool can felt if treated roughly, most common household spills clean up without leaving obvious marks. If you spill coffee or red wine, you’re not out of luck—immediate blotting followed by a gentle cleaning typically yields good results. For tougher stains, consult a professional rug cleaner with wool-specific care instructions.

### Maintenance routine that won’t ruin your weekend
Weekly light vacuuming, plus a quarterly deep clean (spot-clean as needed), is a good baseline. Rotate the rug occasionally if you’re moving furniture around; this helps prevent permanent patterning under heavy chairs and foot traffic. A rug pad can extend life by providing cushioning and reducing sliding, especially on hard floors. If you’re the “set-it-and-forget-it” type, pair this with a simple maintenance calendar and you’ll barely notice the rug exists—until you walk across it and realize life feels a little better.
