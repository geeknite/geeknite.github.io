### Custom sizing vs standard 8x10: which should you pick?
Custom sizing is the party trick here. If your room dimensions are quirky, or you simply want a rug that anchors a large seating area with precise margins, the custom option is appealing. It lets you specify length and width to fit the exact footprint you have in mind. The 8x10 size, meanwhile, remains a classic go-to. It offers a familiar proportion for many living rooms and maintains versatility across furniture layouts.

Pros of custom sizing:
- Perfect fit for irregular spaces or large open floor plans
- Enables symmetrical margins around furniture groups for a polished look
- Reduces the need for rug cutting or awkward overhangs at the edges

Cons of custom sizing:
- Longer lead times due to production adjustments
- Potentially higher price compared to standard sizes
- In some cases, return options may be more restricted due to custom work

Pros of 8x10:
- Broad compatibility with common room layouts
- Generally faster shipping and simpler returns
- Easier to stage and redecorate later if your layout changes

Cons of 8x10:
- May require trimming or awkward gaps in extremely narrow rooms
- The same color and texture may appear differently across batch runs

In practice, the decision boils down to your space geometry and your willingness to align a piece with a design plan. If you want a rug that looks tailor made for your space, custom sizing is the hero; if you want a reliable, classic footprint that plays nicely with most furniture, 8x10 is the dependable choice.

### Care, maintenance, and keeping Cliffwood looking sharp
Wool fibers are naturally resilient, but every rug deserves a little love. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps keep the surface free of dust and debris. Use a vacuum with a beater bar on the lowest setting or a suction-only mode to minimize wear on the pile. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high trafic areas. Spills should be blotted up quickly with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers. For tougher stains, consult the care label or a professional rug cleaner with wool experience. The goal is to preserve the wool’s natural loft and color integrity while avoiding harsh chemical cleaners that could affect dye stability.

Dye stability matters too. Green dyes can shift slightly in certain light or after cleaning, so expect a gentle color evolution over the first year as the wool settles in. If you are particular about color fidelity, request a dye lot sample or a small swatch to compare against your wall color before committing to a full rug. This is the kind of practical detail that saves you from reworking a living room palette mid project.
