### How to set up and start playing
Setup is straightforward. Shuffle the deck, deal a starting hand to each player, place the BullsEye token in the center, and flip the top card to kick off the discard pile. The rules keep the base UNO flow intact: match color or number to play, and draw when you cannot. The magic is when a BullsEye card appears in play. A BullsEye card gives the shooter (the person who played it) a window to select a target for a special effect that can increase the pain while maintaining the rhythm of a friendly night out.

If you want a quick image of how this looks, think of a tense moment on a party game night where someone yells A B C, then says Target confirmed with a polite wink. There is a playful tension here that makes a 10 minute game feel like a mini-drama with a punchy ending.

### The core mechanics: how Bullseye changes the game
The BullsEye twist hinges on a voluntary or forced targeting mechanism. When a BullsEye card is played, the player who played it can select another player to receive a special effect. The effects vary by edition but typically include options like extra draws, skipping turns, or drawing from a penalty deck that adds chaotic flavor rather than straightforward punishment. The exact card text on the BullsEye cards is designed to be simple enough for casual players, but with enough variance to keep power dynamics shifting as the hand evolves.

The target must react to the BullsEye trigger as dictated by the card in play. If a target has a UNO threat ready, they may try to rush their own hand into effect, but in BullsEye the misdirection is half the fun. You get to decide who is on the receiving end of the chaos, and that can lead to playful back-and-forth banter, strategic shadow play, and those guilty glances that say I targeted them because they laughed at your last misplay.

Choose Your Target is the signature move of BullsEye. It creates a social mechanic rather than a purely mechanical one. ### House rules and variants
Every great UNO night has a few house rules, and BullsEye is no exception. Here are a few approaches you can try if you want to tailor the chaos:
- Gentle chaos mode: reduce the effects of BullsEye cards and allow more reaction options for the target rather than punitive outcomes.
- Turbo rounds: play multiple rounds with a shorter deck and faster rotation, ideal for larger crowds who want a quick win and more storytelling moments.
- Sneak peek Bullseye: allow targets to swap out a cards from their hand by discarding one of equal or lesser value before the BullsEye effect resolves. This adds a layer of risk management that casual players tend to enjoy.
- Cooperative twist: for a rare event, declare a round where targets cooperate to beat the BullsEye draw by collectively guiding the fate of the shooters. It sounds odd, but it can be a crowd favorite for a surprise ending.
