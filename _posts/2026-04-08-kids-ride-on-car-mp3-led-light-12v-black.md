## Safety and Maintenance
Safety is the smallest common denominator for ride-on toys: keep it safe, keep it clean, and keep it monitored. Here are practical tips to maximize safety and longevity:
- Always supervise kids during operation. No exceptions, not even for five minutes. The car is a baby with a tiny speedster ego; it needs an adult to ensure the track stays safe.
- Check the wiring and connectors during upkeep. Loose cables or rattling screws can lead to issues that are not only unsafe but also very annoying to fix.
- Inspect the seat belt and buckles regularly; a wobbly belt defeats the purpose of safety features.
- Clean LED panels with a soft cloth. Avoid harsh cleaners; you want them to stay shiny and bright for future adventures.

Maintenance is straightforward: a little lubrication on moving joints, periodic tightening of screws, and battery maintenance per the manual. If you hear unusual noises, don’t ignore them. A quick check typically reveals a loose wheel or a loose screw that can be tightened with a simple tool. The goal is to keep the ride smooth and the sound track leak-free.
