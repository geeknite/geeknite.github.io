## How to Play: Quick Start Guide (2-5 Players)
Keeping it simple is the trick for party games, and NSFW kicks this up with punchier action cards and more room for banter. Here’s a quick-start guide to get you into the chaos within minutes.

1) Setup:
- Shuffle the deck and deal a hand of 7 cards to each player.
- Give each player a Defuse card or two if you’re playing with a standard distribution (the exact number can be adjusted for your group).
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards in the deck according to the number of players (the fewer explodings in the deck, the longer the game tends to be).
- Decide who will go first. A few players can also choose to perform a silly pre-turn ritual to set the vibe (think: loudly announcing a ridiculous nickname for the next player).

2) On Your Turn:
- Draw a card from the deck (mandatory draw).
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re not out yet if you can Defuse the situation with a Defuse card. If you don’t or can’t Defuse, you’re eliminated, and the game continues with the remaining players.
- Play one card from your hand (most cards are action-based: peeks, steal, attack, or other cheeky plays). Some cards cause your opponents to draw more, others let you sabotage someone else’s engine of chaos.

3) End of Round:
- The round continues until only one player remains. The last kitten standing is the champion—at least until the next party night, when the deck is reshuffled and chaos reignites.

This is a fast game; your first run-through will likely take under 20 minutes. As you get the hang of timing, you’ll discover the art of baiting, bluffing, and punting your opponents into awkward decisions that feel equally brilliant and terrible. The NSFW edition doesn’t require advanced strategic theory; it rewards social interaction, quick improvisation, and a shared willingness to lean into the ridiculous.

## Strategy Notes: Tips to Survive and Charm Your Crowd
While this is a social game at heart, a few small tactics can improve your odds and your reputation at the table.
- Know when to bait. If you’re the target or a popular target, a well-timed counter-card can shift attention away from you and toward an easier target.
- Balance risk and reward. Defuse cards are precious, so use them when you’re not on the knife-edge of elimination. If you’re near the end and getting torn apart by special actions, a Defuse could be worth more than any single attack card.
- Read the table. The strong point of NSFW Edition is the banter. If people are enjoying a particular running joke, you can lean into it with your choices and play to the crowd. The table’s mood is often a bigger factor than any card’s raw power.
- Use “attack” cards strategically. Forcing others to draw more cards can dramatically shift the odds, especially if you’ve got a small hand or a near-empty deck. Just don’t be the only one left responsible for the chaos.
- Be mindful of safety boundaries. If you’re playing with new folks, consider a quick pre-game agreement about humor lines and what’s off-limits to ensure everyone has a good time.
