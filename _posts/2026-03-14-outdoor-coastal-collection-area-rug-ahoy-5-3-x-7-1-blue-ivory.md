## Maintenance and Care
### Routine Cleaning
For routine maintenance, a simple sweep or light vacuum does wonders to keep dirt from embedding in the fibers. When you see a spill, treat it like any other outdoor surface: blot, rinse, and move on. If you need a deeper clean, a mild detergent solution and a gentle scrub with a soft-bristle brush will usually do the trick. The goal isn’t to scour the rug into a brand-new shininess; it’s to maintain the balance between appearance and function so your guests don’t mistake the rug for a mud pit.

### Stains and Odors
Pet messes, food spills, and the occasional driftwood-chewing incident can occur. The Ahoy rug responds reasonably well to prompt action. A little baking soda can absorb odors, and a quick bath with soap can bring back its original brightness. If you’re dealing with persistent odors, a gentle enzyme cleaner is a friend, applied with care and in accordance with the product guidelines. Remember: outdoor rugs are not deodorizing machines; they’re outdoor furniture accessories that need occasional spa days like everyone else.

### Storage and Seasonal Use
If you live where winters demand storage or heavy snowfall, you’ll likely roll and store the Ahoy during the colder months. The rug rolls up easily, and its color and texture don’t degrade dramatically if you roll it with a bit of care or use a protective sleeve. For year round coastal enthusiasts who don’t want to clean rugs in the rain every weekend, a cover or storage on a sunny but dry day can keep the rug looking its best. In short: storage is straightforward, maintenance is manageable, and your investment pays off in the vibe you create rather than constant repair work.
