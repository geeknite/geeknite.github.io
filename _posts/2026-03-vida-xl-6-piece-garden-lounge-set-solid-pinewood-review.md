## Durability and Weather Resistance

Pinewood is not teak. It’s more accessible and easier to care for, but it does require a bit of maintenance to keep it looking sharp. VidaXL-treated pinewood typically receives a protective finish that resists some degree of UV exposure and moisture. The set survived multiple sunny days and a few unexpected downpours in testing. The cushions dried relatively quickly, and the wood showed minimal signs of warping after hydration. Of course, if you live in a climate with extreme humidity or heavy snowfall, you’ll want to consider winter protection and periodic re-sealing.

Key weather considerations:

- Moisture can affect pinewood if left damp; ensure cushions are dry and consider a breathable cover during rain.
- Wind load matters; heavier pieces stay put, lighter cushions may blow around. Anchoring cushions with ties is a nice touch.

## Maintenance and Cleaning Tips

To keep the pinewood looking its best and prolong the life of the cushions, consider the following routine:
- Clean wood annually with a mild soap solution and a soft brush. Rinse thoroughly and let dry completely before applying any protective finish.
- Re-seal or re-oil the wood every 1-2 years, depending on exposure. A penetrating sealant helps ward off moisture and UV damage.
- Cushion care: spot-clean spills promptly. For deeper cleaning, check the fabric’s care label. A fabric protector spray can be a wise investment.
- Store or cover during long periods of rain or winter months to minimize moisture uptake and extend aesthetics.

If you enjoy tinkering, you could try a DIY-sealant recipe using a light oil mix with a UV blocker. Just remember to test on a hidden spot first and keep a bottle of the product safe for future touch-ups.
