## Collectibility and Market Insight
### NM US Condition: What It Means
Near Mint (NM) in the US market carries a precise connotation: the card looks almost perfect under casual inspection. Edges are clean, corners show little to no whitening, and the surface sparkle is robust but not blasted with micro-scratches. For glossy AR cards, NM is a higher bar because the holo finish is more sensitive to handling. If you plan to grade or sell the card in the future, you’ll want to confirm consistent centering (often 60/40 or 55/45 depends on print lot), pristine edges, and a surface free of scuffs and micro-folds.

### Price Dynamics and Collectibility
AR cards from SV3 have seen a steady, if not meteoric, appreciation curve in the last year. The Gloom AR 109/108 SV3 is front-and-center in the “eye-catching art” niche—great for binder display, tasteful for shelf art, and surprisingly resilient in price when supplied in NM condition. Expect NM US copies to command a premium over non-AR variants and standard art cards, given the combination of rarity (Alternate Art) and desirability (Gloom with a regal black flame motif).

### Grading and Preservation
If you plan to have this card graded, you’ll want slab-ready edges and vibrant holo. The AR finish can sometimes show “edge wear” more readily under grading scrutiny, so store it in a proper binder page or top-loader with a solid inner sleeve. The general rule of thumb: the more you baby it, the more you earn back when you sell or trade. If you’re a long-term collector, consider storing it in a UV-protected display case or a climate-controlled environment to guard against fade or gloss delamination over many years.
