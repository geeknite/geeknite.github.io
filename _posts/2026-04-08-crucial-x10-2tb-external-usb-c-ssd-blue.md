## Specs and What They Actually Do for You
Here’s the boring-but-important part. Specs matter, but the practical reality matters more. The Crucial X10 is an external NVMe SSD in a USB-C shell, designed for modern laptops and desktops with USB-C. That means you get fast data transfer while staying flexible about where you use it (coffee shop, coworking space, or your couch fort).

- Capacities: 2 TB in our blue-tinted specimen, plenty for 4K video projects, large game libraries, and the casual backup of a life’s worth of memes. 
- Interface: USB-C. Ideally paired with USB 3.1 Gen 2 or USB 3.2 Gen 2 hosts for best results; you’ll notice the difference compared to USB 3.0 asymmetry when moving large files.
- Controller and NAND inside: NVMe-based, which explains why it doesn’t feel like a slow external drive. It behaves more like a compact flavor of NVMe you can plug into a laptop than a parking lot of old-school spinning disks.
- Endurance and warranty: Critical for those who treat backups like diaries you actually regret losing. The X10 carries the usual Crucial warranty and endurance expectations, which means peace of mind for your long-running projects.

In practice, you are looking at speeds that are competitive with other modern USB-C external SSDs. You won’t mistake it for a PCIe inside a desktop, but you’ll notice the drop-off-from-internal-NVMe gap is real—yet perfectly acceptable for most everyday workflows. If you’re transferring big video files, editing on the road, or backing up your entire game library, the X10 is a sensible, familiar upgrade compared to older external drives.
