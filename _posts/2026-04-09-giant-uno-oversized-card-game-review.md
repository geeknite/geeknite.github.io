## Setup and How to Play
### Quick setup guide
1) Find a large, flat surface. The bigger the better. If you’re playing on a dining room table that doubles as the family buffet, consider moving the buffet and giving UNO its own stage. 2) Shuffle the giant deck thoroughly. Yes, you will shuffle slower than a regular deck; yes, the dramatic effect is worth it. 3) Deal 7 cards to each player as you would in standard UNO. 4) Place the remaining deck face down as the draw pile and flip the top card to start the discard pile. 5) Decide who goes first and prepare for glassware chaos and color-coded glory.

In terms of rules, Giant UNO follows the same backbone as classic UNO: you must play a card that matches either the color or the number of the top discard. If you can’t play, you draw from the draw pile. Special cards—Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four—work just as you remember, but their impact feels bigger when you slam them down with a card the size of a small novel.

### The turn flow, amplified
On a typical turn, you announce your move, then place a card on the discard pile with a satisfying thump. The oversized cards make the “uno!” moment more theatrical, especially when a player shouts it and the whole table groans in good-natured chaos because the game’s tempo just doubled in size. Expect a little more time to reflect on your choice, a bit more negotiation about whether a color switch is worth it, and a lot more dramatic face-offs if two players are racing to reach seven cards left while the rest of the table cheers, boos, or quietly rearranges seating to minimize card-kicking risk.
