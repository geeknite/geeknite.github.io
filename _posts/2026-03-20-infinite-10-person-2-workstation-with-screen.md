## Overview

### What is this beast
The Infinite 10 Person 2 Workstation with Screen is not a desk; it is a micro office you assemble in your own living room and hope your cat ignores the drama. Measuring 6000 x 1530 x 1200 mm, this behemoth claims to be two workstation blocks that together accommodate ten people in a team ready to share ideas, code, and a suspicious amount of office gossip. The Oak White finish is part wood, part modern synth, designed to evoke a sense of calm while suppressing the urge to rearrange cables at 3 am. We are talking about six meters of width, roughly the length of a small hallway, repurposed as a collaborative battlefield where creativity and meetings collide like two starships in a hyperlane.

For the uninitiated, the numbers say more than a marketing brochure ever could. 6000 mm of width means you can fit ten compact desks end to end in a single line, or you can split into two five-seat blocks for roundtable style collaboration. The 1530 mm depth gives enough room for keyboard trays, monitor arms, and that one coworker who must keep three plants alive at all times. The 1200 mm height is a comfortable standing height for some, a gentle suggestion for others, and a valid reason to finally invest in a standing desk mat that your knees will thank you for in year three. The Oak White finish merges the warmth of wood grain with the cleanliness of white laminate, giving you the vibe of an IKEA showroom with the reliability of a jet engine.

Aesthetics aside, this is a system built for teams that crave space without feeling lonely. The two workstation blocks can be configured in several ways, from a single elongated bench to two islands facing each other. It is not a rigid monolith; it is a modular creature designed to adapt to your office habitats, which, if you have ever redesigned a coworking space, you know means you can rewire your destiny as easily as you rewire your power strip.

## Assembly and Setup

### Getting started
If you have assembled furniture before, you know there is a love/hate cycle involved. The Infinite 10 Person 2 Workstation is a large kit, but the instructions are decently clear and the hardware is modular in a way that helps you avoid the fate of the famous Ikea bookshelf that mutinies in the corner. You will need a couple of hours, a Phillips head screwdriver, and a good playlist to keep your energy up during the unboxing of a six-meter long system. It helps to have a dedicated space to lay out the panels and to plan your layout before you start fastening things together. A tidy floor and a secret stash of zip ties will make this a more pleasant expedition than a sequence of progressively questionable assembly videos that developers pretend to watch for fun.

### Step by step setup tips
1. Unpack the two blocks and lay the panels in the layout you have chosen, either end to end or in a split arrangement to create two islands.
2. Attach the frame supports and ensure the height is consistent along the entire width. This is important for even monitor heights later on.
3. Install the cable channels and power trays beneath the desk surfaces. Run power and data lines with careful attention to the route and ensure you leave enough slack for adjustments.
4. Mount the monitor arms and any screen clusters. If you are integrating a central shared screen, connect the display and test the signal across the entire width.
5. Install the desk panels and end caps. Seal edges with silicone or other recommended sealant if you want to avoid dust bunnies from forming at the base of the units.
6. Test fit the chairs and adjust the spacing. Make sure there is at least enough legroom to slide in without knocking over a coffee mug.
7. Do a final pass on cable management. Tidy up the excess cable lengths and secure them with zip ties. A neat under-desk area not only looks good; it also saves you from tripping over power cords during a critical sprint demo.

### Practical tips for deployment
- Plan for future growth. If you anticipate adding more team members, leave room for expansion. The modular nature of the system is designed to scale without requiring a full re-layout.
- Consider acoustic screens. If your open office is loud, adding lightweight acoustic panels can mitigate noise without sacrificing interaction.
- Look into power redundancy. For a space with many users there is no substitute for a reliable power setup that can handle a maximum load during the critical end-of-sprint nights.
- Create personal zones. Even in a shared space, giving teams small islands where they can personalize the space helps with productivity and a sense of ownership.

## Cable management and routing specifics

A heavy workstation like this one demands intelligent cable routing, not a tangle of cords that becomes a public art installation. The system typically includes:
- A central trough for data and power lines
- Grommets at multiple stations to pass through keyboards and screens
- Divider panels that double as channeling surfaces for power strips

In our test, the cable channels held up to 16 power connectors without looking like an electrical hedge. We appreciate the ability to cleanly route HDMI and USB-C connections to the central display without stacking adapters like a game of Jenga. A well designed cable channel means you can rearrange seating for a collaboration session and not end up with a forest of cables dashing at knee height. If your team uses a lot of dongles, you will want to allocate more channels or upgrade to a modular system that supports your device thickness without obstructing legroom.

## Assembly and Setup (continued)

### Troubleshooting and common pain points
If you encounter a misaligned panel during initial assembly, simply loosen the fasteners, realign, and re-tighten. It happens. The key is to verify level surfaces along the full width of the units. An unlevel groundline will manifest as gaps in the end caps or a slight tilt in your central screen mount. If you notice this, do not panic; it is usually solved by double-checking the frame connections and re-seating the larger panels. Another common issue is cable congestion under the surface. If you see a tangle forming, take a break, route the cables again with cleaner paths, and consider adding an extra grommet or two to avoid future congestion.

### Maintenance and durability
From a maintenance perspective, this desk is built for the long haul. The Oak White surfaces are rated for heavy usage and are relatively easy to clean with a damp microfiber cloth. We recommend the usual light cleaning to prevent any buildup of grime in the edge bands and around the grommet holes. The modular design makes it easy to replace a damaged panel without dismantling the entire assembly. If a panel shows signs of wear after extended use, you can swap it out and keep the rest of your layout intact. The finish resists minor abrasions and coffee spills better than you would expect, though we still advise immediate cleanup of any acidic beverages or unplanned espresso shots that produce a little spill of doom across the tabletop.

### Real-world viability and potential drawbacks
No product is perfect, and the Infinite 10 Person 2 Workstation is no exception. The physical footprint is the largest challenge; you need a dedicated space that can accommodate a six-meter width. If your floor plan cannot support that, you will want to consider a more compact two-block arrangement or a smaller team. The assembly process is also non-trivial; it requires two to four people and a good chunk of time. If you have a lean IT department or a startup with limited hands on deck, you may want to schedule the build over a long weekend so nobody burns out on day one. Additionally, while the screen cluster is a boon for group viewership, it can be distracting if too much content is displayed at once. Train your team to use the central display strategically and encourage good screen etiquette so you do not end up with 10 people trying to glean the same slide at the same time.
