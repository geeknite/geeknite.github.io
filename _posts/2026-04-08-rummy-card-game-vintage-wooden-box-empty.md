## Restoration and Care: How to Treat Your Box for Generations
If you decide to care for the box and perhaps restore a deck to accompany it, here are practical ways to approach the project while preserving the spirit:

1) Surface care: dust with a soft cloth; avoid abrasive polishes that could erase the grain’s character. 2) Lubricate the hinges sparingly if they creak, using a tiny drop of a mild lubricant. 3) Decide on interior lining: velvet for a royal vibe, suede for a softer, more modern touch, or keep the plain finish for authenticity. 4) Deck selection: vintage reproductions lend a period vibe; modern permutations can give you a crisp, refreshed look. 5) Display and storage: a small glass-front display keeps the box safe while letting its patina do the talking.

In the end, the care you give it says more about you than about the box. It says you value the tactile experience, the memory of games past, and the possibility of many more nights of laughter around a single wooden vessel.

### A tiny, practical restoration checklist
- Gently clean the exterior with a microfiber cloth.
- Inspect for loose joints and tighten if needed with a careful hand.
- Apply a light coat of mineral oil to the wood if it looks dry (test in a hidden spot first).
- Choose an appropriate deck replacement and test fit in the interior slots.
- Decide whether to add a liner or to leave the interior raw for a more rustic vibe.
- Create a small ritual to accompany the first use of the restored deck—perhaps someone shuffles while telling a faux origin story for the box.
