## Care and cleaning: keeping your cloud pristine

### Routine Care
Vacuuming with a gentle setting is your friend. Use a vacuum with a beater bar turned off or on a low setting to avoid lifting the pile. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and avoid impression lines from heavy furniture. Periodic professional cleaning is recommended every 12–24 months, depending on traffic and spill frequency. Wool can be forgiving, but it isn’t invincible—quick action after spills saves you from long-term staining and color bleed.

### Stain Removal and Spills
Blot, don’t rub. Act fast when there’s a spill, especially with liquids like wine or coffee. Blot from the outside toward the center to prevent spreading. For most water-based spills, plain water plus a dab of mild detergent should do the trick. For stubborn stains, test any cleaning solution in a hidden corner first, which is the geeky equivalent of “do not press the big red button.” If you need stronger options, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in wool.

### Long-term Maintenance Plan
Every rug needs a maintenance plan, and wool rugs are no exception. Consider a quarterly check for loose fibers, edge integrity, and shedding. If you notice continued shedding beyond the first few months, you may want to consult a professional to rebind or tidy up the pile. The goal is to maintain that plush cloud aesthetic without letting the rug lose its character due to neglect.
