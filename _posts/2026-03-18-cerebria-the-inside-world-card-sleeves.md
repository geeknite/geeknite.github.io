## Card sleeve sizing for Cerebria

### What size do Cerebria cards use?

Most Cerebria cards fall into the standard board-game card size range: roughly 63.5 x 88 mm (2.5 x 3.46 inches). This is the most common size in the hobby, which means there are a ton of sleeve options that fit snugly without adding bulk that unbalances the feel of the deck. When you’re choosing sleeves, you’ll want 2.5 mil or thicker sleeves (often 96–110 microns) for durability, and you’ll want to confirm that the sleeves are listed as “standard size” rather than “custom” if you have a special edition with a slightly odd border.

If Cerebria includes any oversized tokens or special card art in expansions, you may want to test a sample sleeve with a handful of base cards plus one expansion card to ensure a comfortable fit. The goal is not a pancake-thick deck but a smooth glide that doesn’t snag on the sleeve’s inner seams when you shuffle like a gamer who has watched too many magic tricks on Twitch.

### Distinguishing between sleeve types

- Clear sleeves (no tint): Best for preserving the original art, color accuracy, and contrast. They look like you borrowed the cards from an art museum and then protective-glassed them for good measure.
- Matte sleeves: Slightly textured finish to reduce glare, which can be very helpful when you’re streaming or playing under bright room light. Matte sleeves also tend to resist fingerprint smudges a tad better than shiny plastics, which is handy if you’mainly play with snacks and not with spotless hands.
- Glossy sleeves: Aesthetically sharp and slick, but can reflect light in a way that makes reading the icons at table height a tiny brain teaser. If your gaming area has a lot of reflective surfaces, you might want to skip glossy and go for matte or velvet-textured options.
- Premium/archival sleeves: Thicker options that emphasize durability and long-term storage. These are great if you aim to keep Cerebria cards pristine for years, or if you run a dedicated game library where cards need to survive the occasional climate change or card-slinging duel.

### Material matters

Most standard sleeves are made from polypropylene. They’re inexpensive, flexible, and reasonably durable. Some premium sleeves use a more rigid or thicker formulation with a slightly different feel; the trade-off is that the deck becomes a bit stiffer, which some players actually enjoy for shuffling control. Acid-free, archival-safe sleeves are always a safe bet if you’re building a long-term collection or want to future-proof your art under long-term display conditions. If you plan to sleeve overlays or ambidextrous puzzle cards, consider slightly larger thickness than standard to avoid resistance when shuffling.

### Fit and function: a practical guide

- Fit should be snug but not tight. You should be able to shuffle without fighting the sleeve or bending the card corners.
- The card back should remain fully visible without reflection glare, given Cerebria’s artwork and iconography.
- Sleeves should protect both sides of the card, as Cerebria cards carry both rich art and important information that you’ll rely on during crucial turns.
- Sorting and sleeving should be a calm ritual, not a grueling puzzle game in itself. If your sleeves feel like a chore to work with, consider trying a different brand or thickness.

## Sleeving strategy for base game and expansions

### Base game first, then the expansions

If you’re new to Cerebria and want to start with the base game, sleeve those cards first to get a feel for the size and finish. Once you’re comfortable with the base, you can extend sleeves to any expansions that introduce new card types or larger card counts. The practical approach is to sleeve a few base cards first and shuffle to test the fit, then sleeve a representative sample of expansion cards to ensure uniform handling.

### Handling factions and mood cards

Cerebria’s mood and faction cards often have different border treatments or text density. You might find that some sleeves fit mood cards perfectly but leave a little extra room for the more text-heavy faction cards. If that’s the case, you may want to segregate by card type in your storage solution and choose sleeves accordingly so you minimize bulk while keeping readability high for both card groups.

### Card sleeves for tokens and other components

Some players sleeve only the action and card-backed components while leaving tokens un-sleeved for tactile reasons. If you sleeve tokens or small boards, make sure your storage solution accommodates the mix without causing a chaotic jumble when you reach for a mood card during a tense moment. You want the table to feel smooth, not like a chaotic craft table from a DIY show.

## How to sleeve Cerebria: a quick, sane workflow

1) Sort the cards by type (emotions, roles, actions, events, etc.). 2) Decide on a sleeve brand and thickness (start with 90–100 microns for a durable feel). 3) Sleeve a small sample to test fit, back-to-back with a few reference cards. 4) Once you’re happy with the feel, sleeve the rest and label containers to reflect card types for easy organization. 5) Update your play area with a designated sleeve-friendly zone to speed up setup and pack-away. 

Tip: If you’re setting aside time for a livestream or a photo shoot, sleeves can reduce glare and help your camera pick up the artwork without boosting your post-processing time. Your future self will thank you.
