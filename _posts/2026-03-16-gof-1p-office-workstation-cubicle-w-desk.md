## Design and Build

### Materials

The GOF 1P uses a sturdy metal frame with laminated panels that mimic wood tones. Artisan Gray is a durable, wipe-clean laminate. It isn’t real wood, which means fewer splinters and more chances to brag about “low maintenance.” The finish resists coffee rings and accidental doodles, which is basically the office equivalent of a tiny miracle.

### Cable Management

Built-in channels, grommets, and clips attempt to corral your cable spaghetti. In reality, you’ll still want to do a little cable wrangling, but the GOF 1P gives you a spotless baseline to start with. You won’t squeeze every USB-C cable into a neat little sausage, but you’ll certainly reduce the visual chaos enough to pretend you’re in charge of an organized data center.

### Aesthetic and Finish

Artisan Gray, as the name suggests, lands in a neutral-but-not-sterile zone. It’s the color you want when you’re trying to look professional and not like you just opened a sci‑fi shelf from a shipping crate. It pairs well with most office palettes and plays nicely with plants, desk lamps, and the occasional desk companion—like a tiny, non-threathening desk buddy.

## Functionality and Ergonomics

The GOF 1P is designed for a single user. The desk surface accommodates a monitor, keyboard, laptop, notebook, and a surprising number of coffee mugs. Height options (4', 5', 6' H) give flexibility for ergonomic setups. If you’re tall, you’ll appreciate the extra inches for eye-level screens; if you’re short, you’ll appreciate not having to stoop to see the taskbar. The system is modular enough that you can adjust later if your job shifts from “solo desk jockey” to “solo desk jockey with occasional tiny tasks from the corner cubicle.”

## Assembly and Setup

### Tools and Time

A basic toolkit will get you through. You’ll need a screwdriver, an Allen wrench (usually included), and a willingness to negotiate with hardware that occasionally hides behind a bracket you forgot to align. Expect about 60–120 minutes for a straightforward setup if you stay focused and avoid the dramatic self-talk that comes with assembling furniture after a long day. If you have a helper who occasionally phases in and out of assembly mode, you’ll appreciate the extra hands.

### Steps at a Glance

1) Lay out the desk base and main panels. 2) Attach the cubicle panels to form the privacy wall. 3) Insert the desk surface and align. 4) Route cables through the built-in channels and grommets. 5) Tighten screws to a snug, not strangling, degree. 6) Do a quick test plug-in and adjust monitor height as needed.

### Common Pitfalls

- Misaligned panels causing wiggle when you push the chair back.  
- Over-tightening screws that cause minor warping.  
- Cable routing that looks neat until you realize you can’t reconfigure without a tool avalanche. 
These are normal. The GOF 1P is not a showroom sculpture; it’s a piece of furniture designed for real-world use, which means some wobble and some triumphs.

The desk surface is wide enough for a 24-inch monitor and a laptop, with room for a notebook and a mug or two. If you’re a power user with two large monitors, you’ll still have room for a docking station and a legal pad—when you actually use one of those again. ### Privacy and Focus

The cubicle panels provide a wall of visual separation that helps you focus, especially during sprint planning or video calls where you want to project “serious work” while your brain is mostly thinking about lunch. If your office provides power inside the cubicle, you’ll be able to set up a clean power path to your desk without crossing the streams. If not, you’ll thank yourself for the extra zip ties and cable sleeves you bought during that “essential cable organization” impulse buy.

### Storage and Organization

The modular design allows for modest overhead storage or side panels, depending on the exact GOF 1P configuration you purchase. It isn’t a full filing system, but for everyday work—the laptop, a notebook, chargers, a few reference books—it holds up well enough.
