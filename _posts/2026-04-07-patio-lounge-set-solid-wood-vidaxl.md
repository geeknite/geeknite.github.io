## Assembly tips and practical setup advice

## Maintenance, weather, and longevity: how to keep wood smiling
Outdoor wood loves sunlight and rain roughly equally, but not equally in the sense that you want to neglect it and pretend it will endure. Regular maintenance is the best way to extend the life of your solid wood set. Wipe away dirt and debris with a soft brush or cloth; wash with mild soap and water when needed. If you live in an area with heavy rain or intense sun, you may want to seal or oil the wood every season or two to preserve color and protect against moisture ingress. Be mindful of humidity and changing weather; wood expands and contracts with moisture, which can alter alignment over time. A periodic check of joints and fasteners can prevent hardware from loosening and keep everything feeling solid and secure.

Cushions deserve a bit of love too. Store cushions indoors or in a dry place during prolonged rain or snow. If your climate is milder, consider a breathable outdoor cover to keep the cushions dry while still allowing moisture to escape. Quick airouts on sunny days help reduce moisture buildup and prevent mildew. A good practice is to bring cushions inside during the coldest winter months or when the patio is not in use for an extended period.
