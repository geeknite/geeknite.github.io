### Installation walk through (no rocket science required)
1. Power down the printer and wait for any residual gyroscopic vibes to settle. You want to avoid a sudden jolt to the internal gears when the cartridge is changed.
2. Open the front cover and locate the toner bay. The CP405D CM405Df design makes this obvious enough that a mildly tuned-in office plant could direct you to it.
3. Remove the old cartridge with a careful tilt and a slow pull. Do not yank, you are not extracting a stubborn plug from a slow-loading Tupperware lid. If there is any resistance, double-check the latches rather than applying brute force.
4. Gently shake the new cartridge to distribute the toner inside. This helps avoid print density variation at the top of early pages. Do not shake vigorously; you do not want to spray toner on your keyboard like snow on a Christmas morning.
5. Insert the cartridge until it seats with a satisfying click. Close the cover and power on the printer.
6. Run a test page or a few sample prints. If you see light areas or banding, run the printer’s built-in cleaning cycle and, if necessary, perform a second calibration pass.

The installation is intentionally simple because the folks at Fuji Xerox, or their supplier, want to reduce the number of times your desk turns into a DIY lab. If you have previously struggled with cartridge changes that felt like a puzzle, this experience should be less dramatic and mostly predictable.

### Common early issues and how to handle them
- Light print or faint text: Run a couple of cleaning cycles. Sometimes a new cartridge needs a quiet moment to bed in and release a few stray toner particles that drift away like shy party guests.
- Ghosting or faint dark areas: It could be a drum issue or a misalignment in the toner layer. Check the toner cartridge seating and ensure there is no protective strip left in the cartridge path. If the problem persists, a calibration print may help.
- Leakage or powder on the toner bay: This is typically a storage or handling issue. Seal the packaging correctly and wipe up any powder with a dry microfiber cloth. Do not use water or solvents that could smear the toner like a bad witness on the stand.
