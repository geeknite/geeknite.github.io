## Performance and Battery Life

### Battery life and charging
Battery life is the lifeblood of any ride-on. Expect a practical window of 45 minutes to an hour of play on a fresh charge for typical usage. The battery is rechargeable and ships with a standard charger; you can plug in after play and let the car breathe until the next session. Real-world battery longevity depends on weight (kid plus gear), terrain (hardwood vs carpet), and how aggressively your child taps the go pedal. A quick tip: charge fully before first use and avoid leaving it uncharged for extended periods. If the battery shows signs of aging after a couple of seasons, you may find it more economical to swap in a similar capacity unit rather than stretching the life of a tired battery beyond its comfort zone.

## Maintenance and Longevity
### Cleaning and upkeep
Plastic shells age gracefully when kept out of direct sunlight for extended periods. A damp microfiber cloth and mild soap will handle most dirt. Avoid aggressive solvents that can degrade decals or fade the paint. If you live in a dusty area, periodic wipe-downs will help maintain the look and feel of the ride-on. Keep the wheels clean to avoid grit buildup that could reduce traction, especially on wooden floors where friction is your enemy and your best friend.

### Parts availability and repairs
Because this is a licensed product with a decently standardized build, replacement parts are more likely to be available than for off-brand toys.
