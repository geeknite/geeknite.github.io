## Care, cleaning, and a few hacks worth knowing

### Routine care

- Vacuum regularly to remove dust and pet hair. The ribbed texture hides hair better than a flat weave, which is a small victory in most households.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Do not rub; you’ll push the stain deeper and perhaps turn it into a rumor instead of a stain.
- For light stains, a mild detergent solution works. Always test in an inconspicuous area first.
- If outdoors, bring the rug inside after heavy rain to dry, or elevate it to let air circulate. Wet rugs are magnets for mildew if left damp too long.

### Deep cleaning strategies
If you’re up for a more thorough clean, a gentle wash with mild soap and water can do wonders. Do not use bleach or harsh solvents; this rug isn’t a chemistry experiment gone wrong, and you don’t want to test the limits of fiber resilience. For stubborn marks, consider a stain remover labeled safe for synthetic fibers. Always air-dry completely before returning it to its spot to avoid mold growth.

### Storage tips
When seasonal decor is housed away, roll the rug rather than folding it to prevent creases. Store in a dry place away from direct sunlight. A simple cotton bag or a breathable container will do, but avoid plastic wraps that trap moisture and invite wrinkles.
