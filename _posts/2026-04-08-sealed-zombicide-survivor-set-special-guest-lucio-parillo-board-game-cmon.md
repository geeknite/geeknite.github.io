## Artwork and Miniatures: A Closer Look
The miniatures in this set benefit from Parillo’s signature approach: dramatic posing, the sense that a figure’s presence dominates the space even before you roll a die. The sculpts carry a painterly texture, as if each piece has been through a comic-book inker and never fully let go of its inked lines. The faces carry character—eyes that seem to be calculating the next move and mouths that hint at conversations you know you’ll never hear because the world has ended and only planning sessions remain.

Color choices matter here. In undead-heavy environments, contrast is a friend. The survivors often wear darker tones with accent colors that pop, whether that accent is a crimson scarf, a neon glove, or a shard of luminous armor. The zombies, conversely, tend to drift into a grayscale that’s occasionally punctured by well-chosen gore tones or a splash of bile-green to convey mutational effects. The overall palette reinforces the sense of danger and desperation while still looking cinematic on the table.

For painting hobbyists, the set is a gold mine. The sculpts offer good edge geometry to practice edge highlighting, subtle dry brushing on fabric textures, and careful wash work to bring out creases and scars without losing the figure’s readability in low-light gaming sessions. If you’re a content creator who likes to produce painting tutorials, this set provides clear, teachable examples of how to translate a Parillo-inspired aesthetic into tabletop-ready finishes.
