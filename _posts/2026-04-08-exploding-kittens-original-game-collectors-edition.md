## Strategies, tips, and how to win (without turning the table into a war zone)
### Quick tips for beginners
- Learn the core risk-to-reward ratio: if you are on a hot streak and others are queued up, defend aggressively and hoard Defuses to stay in the game longer.
- Use skip and see-the-future strategically: if you suspect a chain of detonations, skip to reset the draw order or to set up a future reveal that can turn the tide.
- Don’t overdo the taunts: banter is fun, but excessive chatter can reveal your hand. The goal is to sow doubt, not to torch friendships.

### Advanced tactics for the brave
- Target the second-to-last draw: in larger groups, guiding the rhythm can yield a late-game edge. Force a few Defuses to burn in a chaotic moment and leave yourself with fewer sure deficits.
- Card economy matters: time the attack card to stretch defenses and give you a window to survive later turns, especially if you manage a quiet Defuse stash.
- Read the table after a few rounds: timing reveals personality—who bluffs, who holds, who quietly rearranges the deck. Use those tells to calibrate risk tolerance.
