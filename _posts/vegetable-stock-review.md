## What is Vegetable Stock?

Vegetable stock is a liquid made by simmering vegetables, herbs, and spices in water. It’s like the love child of soup and potion-making—incredibly versatile, flavorful, and magical in the kitchen.

## The Good

### Flavor Explosion

Vegetable stock is like a party in your mouth, where all the cool vegetables get together and dance. The onions bring the tears (of joy), the carrots add sweetness, and the celery brings the crunch. Add some garlic and herbs, and you've got a recipe for deliciousness.

### Health Benefits

Low in calories and high in nutrients, vegetable stock is the unsung hero of healthy eating. It’s packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. Plus, it’s vegan and gluten-free, making it inclusive enough for the whole squad.

### Versatility

Vegetable stock is the Swiss Army knife of the kitchen. Use it as a base for soups, stews, sauces, and even risottos. It’s like the duct tape of cooking—fixes everything!

While store-bought vegetable stock is convenient, homemade stock is the real MVP. You control the ingredients, the flavor, and the amount of love that goes into it. Plus, it’s free from preservatives and additives.

## Recipe: Classic Vegetable Stock

### Ingredients

- 4 cups chopped onions (2 large onions)
- 2 cups chopped carrots (2 large carrots)
- 2 cups chopped celery (4 large stalks)
- 1 head garlic, halved
- 1 bunch fresh parsley
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
- 16 cups water (4 quarts)

### Instructions

1. **Heat**: Heat a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions, carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables start to soften, about 10 minutes.
2. **Simmer**: Add the water, parsley, bay leaves, thyme, and peppercorns. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for at least 1 hour.
3. **Strain**: Strain the stock through a fine-mesh strainer, discarding the solids. Let cool.
4. **Store**: Store in airtight containers in the fridge for up to a week or in the freezer for up to three months.
