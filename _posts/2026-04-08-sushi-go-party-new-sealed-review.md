## Overview: What is Sushi Go Party!?
Sushi Go Party! is a card-drafting, set-collection party game that lets players assemble a rotating menu of sushi dishes. The core mechanic is deliciously simple: pass a hand of cards around the table, pick one, and pass the rest. Repeat across multiple rounds, scoring points for the right combinations of cards. The “Party” edition adds a menu-building twist: players select a subset of cards to populate a communal menu for that round, varying the scoring conditions and increasing replayability. The result is a game that feels both cozy and surprisingly spicy, depending on how merciless your dining table choices become.

The box I’m reviewing is brand-new and sealed, which means it’s as fresh as a cucumber scooter ride through a brilliantly lit kitchen. ## How to play: Setup, rounds, and scoring in digestible bites
Setup is straightforward, ideally performed while a minor chorus of sizzling rice plays in the background in your head. Shuffle the deck, lay out the selected menu cards (the “Party” twist), deal hands to players, and let the rounds begin.

- Each round consists of drafting several cards from a hand, selecting one, and passing the rest to the left. The round ends when all cards have been drafted.
- After each round, scores are tallied according to the current menu cards, which will be different every round depending on the menu mix. This is the heart of the “party” mechanism: each round feels unique, even if you’re playing with familiar cards.
- At the end of a preset number of rounds, the total score determines the winner. Simple in theory, yet deliciously tricky in execution.

The rulebook keeps the language approachable, avoiding the kind of jargon that makes modern board games feel like a physics lecture. You’ll be up and drafting within minutes, and you’ll be confused for exactly one or two rounds as you try to remember which card combos are worth the most points in this particular menu configuration.

From a practical standpoint, the game shines with four players, but it scales nicely with 2–8 players depending on your appetite for chaos. The more players, the more unpredictable the table becomes; the fewer, the more you’ll feel like a culinary mastermind calculating the perfect sequence of maki and tempura in real-time.

### Round structure: A closer look
Each round begins with menu drafting. You’ll see the current selection of cards on the table, each card representing a type of sushi or a supporting dish (like tea or dessert). The menu cards add a scoring twist: instead of chasing a single objective, you’re chasing a collection of potential objectives, many of which can conflict with each other. This tension is what elevates Sushi Go Party! from a simple “collect the best cards” mechanic to a tactical puzzle about resource management and risk assessment.

As you draft, you’ll be juggling a few mental bets:
- Do I secure a high-score card early, risking that others will block me or pivot to a completely different strategy?
- Can I assemble a solid combination across several categories to outpace other players with more specialized, high-scoring lines?
- How aggressively should I block opponents when a particular dish holds the promise of a big payoff in this round? (This is where the table-talk begins, and the fun of party games emerges wholesale.)

### Endgame and scoring: The moments that matter
Scoring in Sushi Go Party! is a modular craft. Depending on the menu for that round, you’ll tally points for different patterns. Common targets include:
- Collecting a set of specific sushi types (nigiri variants, maki rolls, gyoza, etc.)
- Achieving a high count for a single category (e.g., most maki or most nigiri of a certain kind)
- Special scoring rules on certain cards that trigger when combined with others (like pairing a high-value dish with a complementary item from the same row of the menu)

Because the menu changes, the sometimes chaotic endgames feel balanced rather than arbitrary. You don’t always know what you’re aiming for until you see the menu drawn for that round, which adds a little theatre to the table as players adjust their plans on the fly.

## Strategy notes for the hungry strategist
No game review would be complete without a little edible wisdom. Here are a few tempered tips that might keep you from turning your sushi into wasabi-level misplays:

- Read the menu, not just the cards. The menu cards are where the round-specific scoring lives. If your strategy hinges on a particular dish, identify early whether it will be a viable path given the other players’ likely choices.
- Diversify early, then optimize later. In the early rounds, it’s often wise to pick a card that secures you a broad pattern rather than going all-in on a single, fragile strategy. The later rounds reward consolidation, so you’ll want to maintain options.
- Be mindful of your neighbors. A big part of this game’s charm is the social dance: you pass cards, you snatch back the ones you need, you wink at the person who just blocked your dream dessert. The best players learn to balance personal scoring with the art of disruption.
- Don’t underestimate the power of mid-tier cards. A neat trick is collecting several mid-value cards that align with a couple of round-specific scoring quirks. The sum can beat a single high-value card you chased all game long.
- The luck factor remains real, but skill can tilt outcomes. You’re never guaranteed victory, but you can significantly tilt the odds by menu planning and timing.
