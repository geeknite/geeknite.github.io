## Condition and Grading Considerations

Here’s where the suspense gets real. A lot like this is often sold “as is,” which means you should assume a mix of lightly played and near-mint cards, with some that could show signs of wear if they survived the packaging, the trip, and your 2 a.m. card-topped midnight snack ritual.

## Care and Storage: Keeping The Good Cards Good

Card care is a tiny ritual that pays big dividends. Here are practical steps you can adopt right away:

- Sleeves: Start with clear protective sleeves (standard 2.5 x 3.5 inches for most Dragon Ball Super cards) to prevent fingerprints and minor surface scratches.
- Top-loaders: For the top-tier cards or promos, consider rigid acrylic top-loaders for extra protection when you’re moving cards around or displaying them.
- Binders: Use a binder with 9-pocket pages. This makes it easy to flip through your collection, compare cards, and avoid that dreaded “card sliding adventure” that ends with a card under your couch cushion.
- Storage environment: Keep them away from direct sunlight, heat fluctuations, and humidity. Cards dislike being baked in a sunny window as much as your coffee dislikes being reheated in the microwave.
- Handling: Clean hands or cotton gloves if you’re dealing with extremely valuable pieces. If nothing else, wash your hands and dry them well—especially when you’re streaming your unboxing to the world.

Maintenance is not just about preserving value; it’s about preserving your sanity during a long unboxing session. A messy desk leads to lost cards, corners bent the wrong way, and the occasional “wait, I thought this card was rarer than it is” moment that hurts your brain a little.
