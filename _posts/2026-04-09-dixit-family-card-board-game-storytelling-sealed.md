## What is Dixit
Dixit is a storytelling game from Libellud, designed to spark imagination through dreamy art and a simple rule set. Each round a storyteller selects a card from their hand and gives a clue that relates to the image. Other players choose a card from their own hand that best matches the clue, and pass it face down. The cards are revealed and players vote on which card they think is the storyteller card.

If your card is identified, you gain points; if you can guess the storyteller card while others do not, you gain points as well. The core idea is to tell a story that resonates but not too obvious. The sealed edition preserves the beauty while offering mint in box vibes.

## Components and Setup
Dixit uses art cards as the main currency of wonder. The deck is full of surreal, dreamlike illustrations that look like they were painted by a sleepy Salvador Dali after a late-night pastry binge. The sealed edition keeps these cards pristine, and the box art is often upgraded or refreshed with limited edition prints that collectors may want to show off on the shelf. In addition to the cards you get six voting tokens in different colors, a scoring track, and simple player aids that remind you of the flow.

Setup is delightfully quick. Shuffle the 84 art cards and deal a number of them to each player, usually six. The storyteller rotates clockwise, and the chosen card for the round becomes the canvas for your clue. The democracy of the box works because no one has to memorize complex systems; the rules are as friendly as a golden retriever in sunglasses.

Visuals matter in Dixit, and the sealed edition preserves the card stock and gloss, which means the artworks pop and the tactile experience is satisfying. The art is the hook. You can spend hours staring at a single image and still find new details.

## Gameplay Overview
Dixit moves through a loop of three core actions:
1) Storyteller offers a clue that relates to one of their cards.
2) Players select a card from their hand that best matches the clue and pass it face down.
3) The cards are revealed and players vote on which card they think is the storyteller card.

If your card is identified, you gain points; if you can guess the storyteller card while others do not, you gain points as well. The exact scoring shifts with player count, but the core remains: tell a story, match the story, outwit your friends, and avoid turning the room into an academic seminar.

Two things to love here are the social dynamics and the art cues. The storyteller exercises a narrative muscle that many games never ask you to flex. The players must listen, interpret, and sometimes lean into misunderstandings for the fun of it.

Dixit scales nicely but shines with a balanced mix of players. With too many, the storytelling might tip into chaos; with too few, the guessing may become trivial. The sealed edition does not alter this essential dynamic; it only keeps the card art crisp and the packaging from showing its age after a couple of game nights.
