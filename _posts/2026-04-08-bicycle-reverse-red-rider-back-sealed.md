## Practical Tips for Owners
- Storage and care: The most boring but most effective tip is to keep the deck in a dry, cool place and away from direct sunlight. The red dye can fade over extended sun exposure, which isn’t a tragedy unless you’re a collector who obsesses over exact color matching in dozens of decks.
- Cleaning and handling: If you accidentally drop a card or two with rough edges, you can lightly rub the corner on your finger to re-seat fibers and restore a smoother edge. For stubborn stains, a soft microfiber cloth with a touch of gentle cleaning solution can do wonders, but avoid soaking or scrubbing aggressively.
- Rotation strategy: Don’t keep the same sealed deck for every single routine. Rotate with a few other Rider Back variants to prevent back pattern fatigue, which can flatten the magical impact when the same design becomes too familiar to your audience.
- Camera-ready tips: When filming, use a light with a gentle angle to maximize the red color’s punch. A slightly warm white balance helps keep the red from looking too orange or too crimson, depending on your lighting setup.
