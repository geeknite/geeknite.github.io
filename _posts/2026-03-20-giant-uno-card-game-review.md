## How to play with oversized cards
The core UNO rules still apply: match by color or number, draw cards, skip turns, reverse direction, draw two, wilds, and the game-ending "UNO" shout. But there are differences:

- Handling: You physically hold fewer cards at a time; you spread a stack across the table when declaring UNO to ensure everyone knows you mean business. 
- Visibility: The numbers are large, the colors crisp. It’s not just for kids; adults will enjoy new vantage points for their favorite games. 
- Pace: It’s slower at first because you physically lift bigger cards, but once players adapt, the energy level surges as you pass around a giant UNO card that’s basically a slip-and-slide of color.

The rulebook covers standard UNO rules with expansions: who gets to call UNO when your hand is 2 cards, what happens if you forget to yell UNO, and the rare "we just invented Octoberfest Uno" variant where you add a two-minute break for snacks. The included official rules are straightforward, but we also tried some house rules.

## Setup and playthrough
Initial setup is simple: lay out the draw pile, the discard pile, and the big UNO card tray. Shuffle the deck either by a quick thrash-of-the-wrist shuffle (recommended for the dramatic effect) or a more civilized method if your audience includes kids who still think "shuffle" means "dance." The first player is determined by the usual method (youngest player picks a card; highest face value wins; or the adult with the loudest laugh). The game proceeds with players taking turns, drawing to bring their hands back to a sane size, and playing cards to either match color or number. When a Wild card is played, the player chooses the color for the next round, and the table erupts into a mini-confetti moment or at least a chorus of "yay" and "oh-no" from the spectators.

### Example round explained
- Round starter: a red 2 hits the table.

## Variants and house rules
The standard rules apply, but many households enjoy extra variants:
- Color flood: after a Wild per round, the color must be used by all players within three turns, to encourage quick color choices.
- Team UNO: pairs of players share one oversized hand and must coordinate their plays with whispered strategizing.
- Speed UNO: play reduces the time for each turn; the timer hums in the background as players dash to decide their next move.
- Snack-laden UNO: players take a bite of snacks when a Wild card is played. This slows the game down but amps the fun.
