## What you actually get in a 4000+ card lot
### The long tail: commons
Commons are the unsung heroes of your binder. They are not glamorous, but they are genuinely functional. In a big bag of commons you will find the following opportunities:

### The spicy backup: uncommons

## Sorting, inventory, and the workflow
### Step one: inventory after unboxing
Do not dump everything into a single pile and call it a day. You want to split the initial batch into color bins and by rarity. The order of operations matters:
- Separate bulk from potential playables
- Organize by color first, then by type (creature, instant, sorcery, enchantment, artifact, planeswalker, land)
- Sort by mana cost within each subcategory

### Step two: quick valuation tricks
Commons and uncommons are not supposed to be your retirement plan. However, you can extract real value by:
- Pulling a handful of commonly used removal spells that are underrated in your local market
- Checking for iconic uncommons that still see play in budget decks
- Identifying land cycles that improve mana bases for your cube or casual game night

Pro tip: keep a running list of “maybe” cards. If you can identify 5 to 10 cards that could see play in a deck you own, you are already turning the haul into a playable experience rather than a flea market fiasco.

### Step three: storage and care
Once sorted, you need proper storage. Use binders with enough pages for your color categories and consider card sleeves for long-term storage. The goal is to protect the cards from the ravages of gravity, humidity, and that pesky coffee you spilled during a late night drafting session.
