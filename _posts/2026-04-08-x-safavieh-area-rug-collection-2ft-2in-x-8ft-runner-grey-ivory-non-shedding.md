## Maintenance playbook: keeping it fresh for longer

### Cleaning routine

1. Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting to prevent fiber pull and edge curling. 2. Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth, then clean with a mild detergent solution if needed. 3. For sticky residues, use a gentle cleaner and avoid saturating the fibers. 4. Rotate the rug occasionally if you want to ensure even wear and avoid visual flight paths where sun exposure might fade color.

### Spot cleaning and stain removal

A little caution goes a long way here. Start with water, then a mild soap, and avoid aggressive scrubbing. If you’re dealing with red wine or tea, a light blot with a hydrogen peroxide solution (only if the stain persists) can help; test first in a hidden corner. For pet messes, enzyme cleaners can be effective, breaking down proteins and odors rather than simply masking them. Always dry the area thoroughly after cleaning to avoid moisture pockets and potential odor development.
