## Care and Longevity

Handmade or not, the longevity of a Kilim Dhurrie rests on two things: routine care and smart placement. Here are some practical tips to keep the rug looking fresh for years.

### Cleaning and Maintenance

Regular vacuuming with a low-suction setting helps prevent dirt from embedding into the weave. Use a vacuum with a brushless head or turn off the beater bar to protect the fibers. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones or near windows where sunlight can fade colors unevenly. For spills, blot immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fiber. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution tested on an inconspicuous corner can be used. Always air dry and avoid heat that could shrink or distort the weave.

### Longevity Tips

Rotate, reverse if feasible, and place rug pads under the rug to prevent slipping and extend wear. If your space has pets, trim nails regularly and provide designated nap zones away from the most delicate sections of the rug. For seasonal refreshes, flip the rug to expose the less-worn side for a quick once-over. Small irregularities that you may notice are part of the handcrafted charm and should be celebrated rather than corrected with a sledgehammer of modern manufacturing expectations.

### Kilim vs Dhurrie vs Flatweave

Kilim and Dhurrie are sometimes used interchangeably; in practice, Kilim refers to a weaving technique where the pattern is woven into the fabric without a pile, while Dhurrie typically indicates a flatweave commonly produced in India and surrounding regions. The rug we are discussing leans into both identities: a Kilim-inspired pattern with the Dhurrie flatweave. The outcome is a lightweight, easy-to-move piece that still delivers a robust, tactile surface and a vibrant palette. If you are chasing a fluffy, high-pile rug, this is not it. If you want something that hides footprints and dog hair better than a black cat hides in a chandelier, this flatweave is your ally.
