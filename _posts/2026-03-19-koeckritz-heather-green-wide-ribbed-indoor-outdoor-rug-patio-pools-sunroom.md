## Maintenance and Care: Keeping It Fresh

No rug review would be complete without a realistic look at cleaning. Outdoor rugs are meant to be lived on, spilled on, tracked on, and occasionally dragged through mud. The Koeckritz Heather Green Wide-Ribbed Rug shoulders these duties with a sensible approach:

- Routine cleaning: Vacuum or shake out the rug to remove loose dirt and leaves. The ribbed texture means a simple shake often does the job for everyday debris.
- Stain management: For spills, blotting is your friend. Rinse with water and a mild soap if necessary. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the fibers or fade the color.
- Deep clean: Hose down the surface with a gentle spray, give it a scrub with a soft-bristle brush, rinse, and air dry. Do not use a high-pressure washer close to the backing, as excess moisture can sneak into seams.
- Drying: Ensure the rug is completely dry before placing it back in position. A damp rug can develop a musty odor or, worse, help mold form in hidden corners.
- Seasonal storage: If you live in a climate with all four seasons, consider storing the rug during the harshest months. Rolling it up in a dry space reduces the chance of edge curling and extends life.

### Why the Ribbing Matters for Maintenance
The wide rib pattern is not just for aesthetics. It acts like a built-in landing strip for dirt and moisture, guiding grime toward the gaps so you can whisk it away easily. It’s the little design flourish that makes a big difference in cleaning ease over time.

## Maintenance Myths Debunked

- Myth: Outdoor rugs get mildew no matter what. Fact: A polypropylene rug with a tight weave and proper drying rarely mildews if you avoid leaving it soaked. It’s all about moisture management and air flow.
- Myth: UV-stable colors never fade. Fact: They fade, but the color decay is typically slower than non-UV-stabilized options. You’ll still see some hue shift after years of sun, but it won’t look like a different rug.
- Myth: The rug is slippery when wet. Fact: A good backing and textured surface provide grip. If you’re worried, add rug grippers or a memory-foam underlayment in the highest-traffic areas.
