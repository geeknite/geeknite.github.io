### Cleaning and maintenance
Maintenance for sisal is straightforward but slightly more deliberate than a flat-weave synthetic rug. Here are the basics:
- Vacuum regularly with a vacuum without a beater bar to avoid fiber snags.
- Address spills promptly by blotting with a dry cloth; avoid rubbing as it can push stains deeper into the fibers.
- For stubborn spots, use a mild, pH-balanced cleaner recommended for natural fibers. Always test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Professional cleaning is recommended for significant stains or if the rug is in a high-traffic area.

### Durability and long-term expectations
Sisal rugs are known for their sturdiness and a longer life in active spaces. ## A note on installation and room dynamics

Installing a rug, especially a custom size, is a mix of art and math. You’re balancing furniture geometry, walking space, and the rug’s proportion to your room. It’s less about trigonometry and more about which edges are visible from the entryway and which furniture legs will enjoy a soft, even retreat after you stand up from that gaming chair marathon. In practical terms, consider:
- Rug pad thickness: a thicker pad adds comfort but can affect door clearance. Measure clearance if you have to roll the rug into place.
- Edge curling: some natural fiber rugs may curl at the edges when new. A steam or light weight pressing can help, but if you have a strong preference for perfectly flat edges, you might opt for a professional installation step.
- Room lighting: the warmer hue of honey quartz shines best with warm LEDs or amber-toned lighting. If you’re all about white-light vibes, the rug will still look good, but the color warmth will be less pronounced.
