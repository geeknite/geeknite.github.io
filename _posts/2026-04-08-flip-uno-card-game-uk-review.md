## What is UNO Flip Card Game

UNO Flip Card Game is a variant of UNO that doubles down on the chaos by flipping the entire draw pile to reveal a dark side. Essentially, you start with light colored cards on one side, and when a flip card is played, the rules, color palettes, and action cards flip as well. The game becomes a cardio routine for your brain as you manage two color ecosystems, two sets of action cards, and a growing pile of near misses. The core objective remains the same: be the first to shed all cards and shout UNO to win. In practice, this means planning not only your color matching but also predicting when your opponent will flip the deck and reveal a new cascade of penalties. It is UNO, but with a theatrical plot twist that would make a Marvel movie jealous.

## How to Play UNO Flip Card Game

1. Shuffle the deck, place the draw pile face down, and deal a hand of seven cards to each player. The rest of the deck forms the draw pile in the center. The game begins with the top card of the draw pile turned over to start the discard pile. 
2. On your turn, match a card from your hand to the card on the discard pile by either number, color, or symbol. When a flip card is drawn or played, flip the entire deck to reveal the dark side and switch the flow of the game. 
3. The dark side introduces new action cards that can dramatically alter who is winning and who should consider a career in diplomacy instead of direct competition. The game continues until a player runs out of cards. UNO is not only a game; it is a test of patience for the family who insists on rules that are flexible enough to justify a second round. 
4. Shout UNO when you have one card left. The rules are strict here, because if you forget and your opponent calls you out, penalty cards may serialize into your fate. 
5. Victory is as much about speed as it is about holding cards that create the most dramatic flips at the perfect time.
