## Introduction to the Storyteller's Screen

The Storyteller's Screen is not just a pretty piece of cardboard with gloss coating and a neon border. It is a tactical tool, a ritual artifact for the GM that promises faster setup, fewer glossed over rule checks, and more time for the important thing: making your players feel like they are living in a comic panel where every panel transition is a sigh-worthy reveal.

In the City of Mist setting, players embody characters who have mythic roots and modern day lives. The Screen acknowledges this duality by placing quick references that cover both the investigative instincts and the mythic turns that can occur during a session. It is designed to be practical at the table, but it also looks the part: the art direction leans into slick noir vibes with a dash of graffiti, as if a superhero storyboard whispered to an alleyway and the alleyway replied with neon glow and rain-slick pavement.

The Screen's design philosophy is not to replace the core book but to complement it. The Screen gives you cheat sheets for common actions, quick reminders for the kinds of tests you will roll, and a few ready-made prompts that can inject momentum when the night starts to lag. If your group enjoys the feel of a cinematic crime drama, this tool keeps pace with your storytelling without forcing you to flip through pages while a player looks at you with expectant eyes that say, Why do you hate fun?

The three-panel design is a classic GM screen moveset: a sturdy exterior, a quick-glance reference inside, and a couple of extra notes that you can customize over time. The material quality is practical but not premium museum-grade. It’s the kind of screen that stands up to a regular gaming night without turning into a creaky relic after a few dozen sessions. The art direction keeps the neon noir vibe intact, with bold typography that remains legible under candlelight or LED strips. It is not designed to be a centerpiece of a gallery wall, but it is definitely designed to look good behind the table and be practical when you shoulder-charge into a tense scene.

For the GM, the layout typically features quick references for:
- Scene structure and pacing prompts
- Clue and investigation prompts
- Power tags and character aspects
- Combat or action prompts that aren’t cumbersome to access mid-scene

### The quick reference content

The Screen is designed to minimize rule-check gymnastics. You will find condensed reminders for: how Logos and Mythos interact, how Distinctions shape a scene, the basics of invoking and tagging, and the way that one can escalate a scene with a few well-timed prompts. Reading this, you get a sense that the Screen is less about teaching you the rules and more about giving you a fast lane to execution. The intention is that you never reach for the core book in the middle of a crucial beat unless you want to. If you do want to, it should be in a moment where you are comfortable with the rules, and the Screen is there as a safety net rather than a lifebuoy.

If your group loves to riff on the idea that a myth speaks through a frontline detective who wears a hoodie and an unwieldy metal badge, the Screen supports that vibe with prompts that feel flavorful rather than filler.

## How to Use the Screen in Play

### Start of session mindset

Place the Screen where everyone can see it, ideally within arm's reach of the GM. A good rule of thumb is to set it up before players arrive, so you are not rummaging for pages while your players debate whether a streetlamp is a clue or a red herring. The Screen helps you maintain a quick tempo by letting you reference the core mechanics in seconds rather than minutes. The moment you realize you can avoid rifling through a stack of PDFs or the core book to answer a single question, you have entered the sacred zone of GM productivity.

### Scene pacing and prompts

City of Mist is built around a structure that rewards momentum. The Screen encourages you to lean into scene prompts when the action slows. It includes prompts for arena-scale actions, scene transitions, and the kinds of test checks you will run most often. The prompts are not a replacement for your GM creativity; they are a menu that you can pull from whenever you want to keep the scene moving, and to avoid that awkward five-minute silence where no one knows whether the detective can push through a door or simply roll a die to see if the door yields.

### Handling the mythic and the mundane

The setting thrives on a balance between the everyday and the extraordinary. The Screen reinforces this balance by providing quick guidance for when a clue comes from a mundane object that might reveal a mythic truth, or when a hero moment reveals something about a city that no one anticipated. It is a reminder that the best moments in City of Mist often come from the intersection of two different kinds of knowledge: the grounded investigative logic and the elevated, symbolic meaning that characters attach to the world around them.

### Tips for GMs and Storytellers

- Use the prompt suggestions as a warm-up for new players. It reduces the first-session nerves and helps people find their voice as investigators or mythic beings.
- Pair the Screen with a few index cards detailing player personas and a small map of a few key city districts. It will speed up scene setup and give you a quick visual reference that complements the text on the Screen.
- Keep a small notebook handy for on-the-fly mythic revelations. The Screen is a guide, but the best moments are unique to your table. The ability to record a quick, memorable revelation on a sticky note can be the difference between a one-shot and a campaign that players beg to continue.
- If your group uses miniatures or tokens, consider a simple table for positioning and a separate deck for clue cards. The Screen can remind you of these accessories without forcing you to rummage through a pile of props.
- For tonal consistency, revisit the theme notes in your core book periodically. The City of Mist setting rewards a consistent mood, and the Screen is your friend in keeping that mood intact across multiple sessions.
