### Setup and Gameplay
Sushi Go! is a drafting game. Each round, players pass a hand of cards around the table, choosing one card to keep and then passing the rest. The goal is to assemble the best combination of dishes to score points at the end of each round. The 2-5 players variant on this tin scales nicely, with simple table talk and a few quick scoring tokens to keep things tidy.

- Setup: Shuffle, deal, and lay out a few cards; done.
- Play: Pick a card, pass the rest, repeat.
- End of round: Reveal cards, score, and set up for the next round.

You’ll quickly learn that Sushi Go! rewards memory as much as it rewards luck. If you’ve got a memory like a goldfish but a heart of gold, you’ll still have a shot at winning. The Collaborators at Geeknite tested this with a group that included a polyglot grandma who can pronounce “nigiri” in three languages and still scored the most points because she remembered who took the egg nigiri last.

### Strategy, Light and Bright
Sushi Go! is not a heavy strategy game, but don’t let that fool you into thinking it’s fluff. There’s texture and choice: which dumplings do you go after? A line of maki rolls? A stack of desserts? A miso soup that might anchor your score or sink it? The balancing act comes from choosing a card that helps your future hands while denying others a card that would screw them later. The tin makes this feel tactile; you can flip, sort, and show your collection in a way that feels milestone-y rather than a stack of paper.
