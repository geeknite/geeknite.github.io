## Overview
The Eternal 10 Person Oak and Black Workstation is designed for teams that need a unified, scalable work surface without sacrificing personal space. Think a row of desks with a continuous oak top mounted on a black powder-coated steel frame. The look is bold: warm wood meets industrial backbone, like a Viking shield made of oak and steel. It isn’t subtle, and that is part of its charm. This is not a single-desk setup; this is a modular command center for squads who code, brainstorm, and occasionally debate whiteboard etiquette in the same breath.

Core idea: a shared surface that can expand or contract as projects demand. The Eternal system offers modular add-ons—monitor arms, storage cabinets, cable trays, under-desk drawers—so you can tailor the setup to your team’s workflow. If your team is distributed across rooms, you can split the line into clusters and still claim you’re maintaining a cohesive culture rather than operating a maze of separate desks.

The oak top is real wood with a tactile grain that smiles at coffee rings and marker doodles. The finish is resilient, with a top layer that fights the inevitable spill test. Weight capacity is the real-world conversation here. In our tests, we loaded monsters of monitors and keyboards and still had room for a tiny desk plant that pretended to improve morale. It felt anchored and stable, not top-heavy—exactly what you want when a sprint pivots and someone upgrades to a 4K monitor that eats desk space for breakfast.

## Assembly Experience
If you’ve assembled furniture before, this will feel familiar with a dash of theater. The kit arrives in multiple boxes, color-coded fasteners, and straightforward instructions that make sense after a second cup of coffee. Aligning the oak top to the steel frame requires patience, but the design is forgiving: pre-drilled holes click into place, and there’s enough wiggle room to accommodate minor misalignment after a long day of cable tangles.

Two people is optimal: one for frame alignment, one for top alignment and cable management. You’re looking at a couple of hours for a full kit with all add-ons. Pro tip: lay everything out, assign roles, and avoid the holy chasm of missing M6 bolts that seems to swallow dowel rods in a demilitarized zone.

## Cable Management and Tech Readiness
Cables are the silent mass that can derail a team’s vibe. The Eternal system includes integrated channels and optional trays to route power, data, and HDMI without turning the desk into a tangle sculpture. A practical plan is to pre-wire power distribution with a trunk line and detachable branches, so you can remove a module or reconfigure the line without a dramatic unplugging montage.

Access to cabling is straightforward. You can slip modular trays in and out, making maintenance and upgrades painless. The payoff is real: quicker reconfigurations, less maintenance, and fewer headaches from cords that refuse to stay organized in the modern cloud-powered world.

## Maintenance, Durability, and Longevity
Oak tops demand a little care. Wipe with a soft cloth, use coasters for beverages, and address minor scratches with a suitable wood conditioner. The finish resists everyday wear, but avoid placing hot items directly on the surface. If marker marks persist or coffee rings appear, a gentle cleaner designed for wood surfaces will usually do the trick. The powder-coated steel is forgiving; avoid dragging heavy furniture across floors to prevent scuffs.

Longevity is where this desk shines. A well-maintained Eternal can anchor a team for many years. It’s not disposable furniture; it’s a backbone that can adapt to growth, reorgs, and new product lines.
