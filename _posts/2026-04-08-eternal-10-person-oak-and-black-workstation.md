## Overview
The Eternal 10 Person Oak and Black Workstation is explicitly designed for teams that crave a unified work surface without sacrificing personal space. Picture a long row of desks with a continuous oak top supported by a black powder-coated steel frame. The result looks like a corporate outdoors meets a woodland vampire aesthetic. It is not subtle, and that is part of its charm. This is not a single desk meant for one person who demands minimalism. This is a networked battlestation meant for squads who code, brainstorm, and occasionally argue about whiteboard marker etiquette in the same breath.

The core idea here is a shared, scalable surface with modular modules that you can slide in and out as projects demand. At Geeknite we love modularity that does not require a forklift to rearrange. The Eternal system aims to tick that box with options for appendages like monitor arms, storage cabinets, cable trays, and under-desk drawers. If your team is distributed across a few rooms, you can even split the line into smaller clusters and still claim you’re a rock-solid office family rather than a lineup of people staring at a single screen until someone breaks coffee mugs in solidarity.

## Assembly Experience
If you have assembled furniture before, this will feel familiar yet slightly more dramatic. The kit arrives in multiple boxes, each labeled with color-coded fasteners and simple instructions that most of us can decipher after a second coffee. The major trick with assembly is aligning the oak top to the steel frame without taking a heavy sigh and asking for a fourth pair of hands. The good news is that the design is forgiving: pre-drilled holes align with a satisfying click, and there is enough room to adjust a little if the pieces want to be a touch misaligned after a long day of dealing with cable tangles.

We recommend a two-person assembly team for best results, with one person focusing on frame alignment and the other on top alignment and cable management. Expect a couple of hours if you have a full kit with all the add-ons. If you want to go full efficiency mode, clear a space, lay out all hardware, and assign roles. You will thank yourself later when you are not hunting for the missing M6 bolt in a box that is basically a black hole.

## Ergonomics and Comfort
Size matters when you are dealing with 10 people who all believe they deserve their own screen real estate. The Eternal workstation provides generous legroom and enough under-desk space to store a rolling cart or a compact filing system for those who still pretend to file things in physical form. The key ergonomic benefits include:

- Sufficient knee clearance for tall teammates and shorter colleagues who insist on their own corner as a personal refuge
- A comfortable monitor height range with optional monitor arms that can be adjusted without tools
- Cable management that minimizes the chaos of power bricks and USB hubs while allowing easy access for maintenance
- Aesthetic balance that reduces visual clutter while keeping a professional vibe

One of the most underrated aspects of a large shared desk is the social ergonomics. When queues form around a whiteboard, you want people to be able to move their chairs without bumping into the next person. The Eternal desk accomplishes this by offering a wide central surface with clear demarcations for each workstation. It is not a privacy fortress, but it does give individuals a sense of space, which reduces the midday email drama that can erupt when someone sits too close to your keyboard and claims your personal sprint backlog.

## Cable Management and Tech Readiness
The tech life loves cables as much as humans love coffee. The Eternal system comes with integrated cable channels and optional trays that make it possible to route power, data, and HDMI cables without turning the entire desk into a nest of spaghetti. A practical tip is to pre-plan your power distribution. The last thing you want is to discover that a single power strip is insufficient for a row of 8–10 workstations plus occasional guest laptops. Our test setup used a central trunk line with detachable branches so you can remove a module or reconfigure the line without unplugging everything in a theatrical fit of frustration.

Access to cabling is reasonably straightforward. You can slip modular trays in and out, which helps during maintenance and upgrades. In the long run, the cable management system pays for itself in the time saved during reconfigurations and in the fewer headaches from tangled cords that somehow manage to survive even in a modern cloud-powered world.

## Real World Use Case: A Sprint in a Shared Row
To understand how the Eternal 10 Person desk works in practice, we staged a 2-day sprint with a team of ten. The group included developers, a product designer, a project manager, a QA specialist, and two remote contributors joining over a shared video call. The goal was to simulate creative sprints where everyone can jot notes, drop ideas on sticky notes on a whiteboard, and then pivot to a more focused coding session.

What happened was a surprisingly smooth flow. The space allowed people to gather for quick standups along the central line and break into smaller groups for deep work. The oak surface handled marker traces and occasional coffee drips without showing a lot of wear. The hardware held up to the heavy laptop rigs and large monitors without warping or flexing under pressure. The cable management system kept the floor clean, reducing the risk of tripping during moments when someone would leap into the sprint to present a new concept. The only challenge we encountered was the need for a couple of quiet corners to accommodate remote participants who preferred to tune in with a dedicated camera angle. The Eternal system gracefully accommodated this via a modular arrangement that allowed us to create small pods along the line, each with its own small monitor and microphone cluster.

All in all, the sprint proved that a large shared desk can foster collaboration without sacrificing the personal space that each team member requires to do deep work. It is not a magic pill, but it is a solid tool that can help teams function more cohesively when they are physically in the same room and trying to land on a concept quickly.

## Maintenance, Durability, and Longevity
Wood tops require a little care, and the oak used on the Eternal line is no exception. Routine care means wiping with a soft cloth, using coasters for beverages, and addressing minor scratches with a suitable wood conditioner. The finish is designed to be resistant to everyday wear and tear, but you should still avoid placing hot items directly on the surface. If a marker marks the top or a coffee cup leaves a ring, you can usually remove it with a gentle cleaner designed for wood surfaces. The powder-coated steel is more forgiving than a bare metal frame; it resists chips and scratches, though you should still avoid dragging heavy furniture across the floor to prevent scuffing the legs.

Longevity is where this desk shines. A well-maintained Eternal can be a backbone of a team for many years. It is not a disposable chunk of furniture you replace every two seasons, nor is it a prestige display that makes you feel obligated to hire a team of carpenters for maintenance. It sits in the middle ground: solid, reliable, and adaptable to your changing needs.
