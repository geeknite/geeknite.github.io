Materials, construction, and durability
The rug is machine woven from a modern synthetic blend that feels soft underfoot and resilient under the daily onslaught of cat claws, children, and the occasional dramatic tutu twirl during a home photoshoot. The fibers are tight enough to resist snagging, yet plush enough to cushion an accidental gaming marathon on carpeted floors. Maintenance is surprisingly reasonable: weekly vacuuming on a low suction setting and a yearly deep clean to keep colors from fading into a polite beige. Practicality and care
Vacuum regularly, preferably with a beater bar set to a gentle setting, to avoid pulling fibers or turning the rug into a static-charged cape. If you have pets, expect a daily procession of fur confetti; a quick pass with a vacuum is usually enough, but you might want to run a lint roller along the edges to collect stubborn pet hair that clings to every petal as if it has a loyalty card. Spills are manageable; blot, don’t rub, and treat with a mild stain remover if necessary. Avoid harsh cleaners that could fade the color or alter the pile height.

Maintenance, care, and longevity
- Vacuum weekly with a low suction setting; avoid aggressive beater bars that might threaten the yield and tuft of the fibers.
- Spills: blot gently; avoid rubbing; treat with mild detergent if necessary.
- Sun exposure: periodic rotation helps prevent uneven fading; rotate every few months to keep colors even.
- Deep clean: schedule a professional carpet cleaning every 12–18 months if you want to preserve that showroom glow.
