## Materials and Craftsmanship
The Rachelle Pearl is described as wool on a cotton backing. In practice that means a sturdy, durable surface with a comfortable tactile feel. The pile height is mid-range, giving you cushion without sacrificing the sense of a tidy floor line. The backing is solid and helps prevent curling along the edges, a common problem with slightly lighter rugs.

From a cleaning and maintenance angle, wool fibers tend to resist dirt and can shed the occasional crumb more gracefully than synthetic fibers. But this does not mean you can ignore it; regular vacuuming, rotation, and occasional professional cleaning will keep the colors vibrant and the fibers aligned.

## Distressing and Authenticity
Distressing is the star here. The look is crafted to appear aged, with subtle faded patches that mimic sun exposure and foot traffic. The edges appear sheened with wear but are still intact and well finished. The result is a rug that carries a vibe of vintage charm without turning your space into a museum exhibit.

If you are chasing authenticity, you should know that this is a modern production with an intentional distressed finish. For many buyers, that is exactly the point: the rug looks older than it is, not one that pretends to be older than it is.

### How to spot quality distress
- Consistent wear patterns that feel deliberate rather than random
- Edges that show soft fraying but remain firmly attached
- Color variation that appears natural rather than printed

## Care and Maintenance
We tested a few common scenarios to see how the rug would handle daily life. Vacuuming at low to medium suction with a soft brush attachment is recommended. Rotation every few months helps prevent uneven fading in sunlight. Spills are best addressed with prompt blotting using a clean cloth, followed by a manufacturer recommended cleaner if needed.

For deeper cleanings, plan to call in professionals if the rug is large or heavily soiled. The wool fiber responds well to gentle cleaning methods, and regular maintenance will extend the life of the distressed finish without compromising the look.

## Durability and Everyday Use

Pet-friendly? Yes, with the caveat that claws can disturb any wool surface, though the distressed look helps hide mild snags. It is not a barrier rug for households with pets; rather, it is a rug that can adapt to an active home while staying stylish.
