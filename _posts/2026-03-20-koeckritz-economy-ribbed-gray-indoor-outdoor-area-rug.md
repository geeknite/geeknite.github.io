## Cleaning and Maintenance
### Everyday Care
Maintenance should be simple, which is the expectation with budget friendly outdoor rugs. A quick sweep to remove leaves and debris followed by a rinse with a hose is usually enough to bring back the freshness. The rug dries quickly in sun and breeze, so you can roll it out again within the same day after a light rinse. For stubborn stains, mild soap and a soft brush do the trick without harming the fibers. Avoid abrasive scrubbers as they can fray edges and dull the ribbed texture over time.

### Stain Management
Red wine on the deck is a universal tragedy, but with a quick blot and a gentle wash, the rug will survive to tell the tale. Food and oil-based stains respond to a gentle cleaning solution and a rinse. The synthetic blend resists staining better than natural fibers, which means you can host a BBQ without necessarily sacrificing your rug hero status. If you know you are a heavy user, consider spot cleaning and immediate drying to extend the life of the rug and maintain its color fidelity.

### Storage and Seasonal Care
If you live in a climate with harsh winters, you might want to roll the rug up and store it away during the off season. The lightweight nature of the rug makes storage painless. Roll it, secure with a strap, and place it in a dry area to avoid mildew. When you unroll it again, you will find minimal odor and reduced stiffness from the previous season. If you want to keep a near permanent outdoor look, you can even leave it out through mild winters, though be mindful of snow and freezing temperatures that may eventually degrade the fibers.
