## A quick profile: how Cipresso di Toscana works in the real world

Like many Acqua di Parma fragrances, Cipresso di Toscana is built to be observed by others rather than to punch you in the nose from a mile away. It’s not a wallflower; it’s a confident eucalyptus whisper that you’ll catch on the skin after the initial spray settles.

## Performance: sillage, longevity, and “how long will this last?”

Sillage (how far the scent projection travels) sits in the “present, but not overbearing” zone. You’ll have a noticeable trail within the first 30 minutes to an hour, and then Cipresso di Toscana settles into a more intimate projection that’s easy to catch when you lean in for a conversation. It’s not going to fill a room on a crowded train, but it will be noticed by a person who leans toward your side of the desk or your shoulder in the elevator. The trick is to spray once or twice—more if you’re a fragrance enthusiast who loves a bold scent presence, but not if you prefer subtlety.

Longevity-wise, you’re looking at roughly 6–8 hours on average with moderate to low layering. On hot days, you might see a shorter window due to faster evaporation, while in cooler weather or with mid-day air-conditioned environments, you could comfortably cross into an 8–10 hour range. The base notes tend to linger longer than the top notes, so you’ll still smell a hint of that evergreen warmth as you wind down your day. It’s not a monster of longevity, but it’s steady and reliable—the kind of fragrance that doesn’t vanish after your afternoon meeting, then reappears with a subtle, inviting skin scent in the evening.
