## Painting and Display Tips

If you’re the kind of player who enjoys turning your minis into tiny works of art, Edouard’s sculpts invite some bold but practical painting strategies. The armor and gear are designed with distinct color blocking possibilities, which makes it easier to create a cohesive palette across the box without slipping into a chaotic rainbow.

- Primer choice matters: a grey or white primer works best for Edouard’s contrast-heavy styles. A black primer can be used for moody zombie shades, but you’ll need to push highlights more later.
- Base colors: start with a strong, saturated base for the armor and fabrics to ensure the sculpt’s edges pop. The skin tones for survivors should be slightly desaturated to keep the undead feeling alive in the opposite sense.
- Shading and highlights: use midtone washes to bring out recesses around armor plates, belts, and concealed weapons. Then apply brighter edges to the raised areas to catch the table’s lighting.
- Basing: Edouard’s aesthetic benefits from a cohesive base design. A few wet basing materials—like dirt and small pebbles—mixed with static grass produces a natural battlefield texture that’s both cinematic and practical for display.
- Varnishing: a matte finish typically works best for table play; if you’re aiming for a “gallery piece,” a satin or low-gloss topcoat can bring out slightly more depth.

If you’re new to painting or want a quick path to tabletop duty, the included miniatures do well with ready-to-run schemes. The emphasis on clear silhouette details means you won’t be fighting to identify a piece under a heavy wash; instead, you can dial in a few contrast layers to bring the character’s personality to the surface.
