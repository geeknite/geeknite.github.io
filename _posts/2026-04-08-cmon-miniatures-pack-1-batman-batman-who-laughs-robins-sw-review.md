## Overview

CMON Pack 1 is pitched as the starter slice of a larger scoping suite—think of it as the cinematic trailer to the DLC pack you didn’t know you needed. If you like battles that look cinematic, a hero who never stops flexing, and a villain who makes a hobby out of your unfortunate dice rolls, you’re in for a treat. The pack centers around the Dark Knight in two flavors of chaos: canonical Batman and the Batman-Who-Laughs, a mash-up that fuses Batman’s gear with the moral dumpster fire of the Joker. Surrounding them are Robins with a SW twist—special weapons that feel like they were designed by a Robin who binge-watched every Bond film and then asked for more gadgets.

A quick note on scope: this is not a full DC story arc disguised as a board game. It’s a skirmish-friendly kit augmented by CMON’s usual focus on pose, base design, and “do we have to paint this?” scale. If you are into deep narrative campaigns with heavy rules, you’ll want to pair Pack 1 with the other CMON entries in the line or borrow some narrative frameworks from your favorite indie RPG to create a story arc around these miniatures.

### What’s included
The box promises a dynamic mix: elite Batman, a chaotic takes on Batman-Who-Laughs, and a squad of Robins equipped with specialized weapons and clear personality quips. The sculpts are crisp, the bases are cinematic, and the little tactical bits—tokens, counters, and standees—are there to remind you you’re rolling dice for a battle that matters to you and nobody else.

If you’re the sort who loves the “unboxing” moment, Pack 1 does not disappoint. The miniatures come in glossy plastic with clean mold lines and faces that radiate character. Batman’s cowl has that dramatic silhouette that begs to be highlighted with a wash that makes the eyes look alive (and, yes, bats can be expressive when you put them in the right light). The Robins are a study in how to pack energy into a 28mm figure without them looking like tiny action figures—these little guys feel like they’d steal the night if you left them unsupervised on a kitchen table.

### The cast: Batman, Batman-Who-Laughs, Robins SW
- Batman: The classic centerpiece, a versatile fighter with a toolkit that covers everything from brawling to tactical moves.

Pack 1 is designed to feel balanced in the sense that you can build a tiered team without needing a full rulebook of exceptions. You’ll have enough tools to create a couple of dynamic squads that can threaten a Batman-Who-Laughs in close quarters or snipe him at a distance with surgical precision.

## Build, painting, and presentation

### Assembly tips
The miniatures are mostly straightforward to assemble, with a few pieces that require attention to shoulder joints and cape alignment. The Batman-Who-Laughs figure has more intricate blade-work on the cape, so plan for a minute more effort to align them perfectly. If you’re new to assembling CMON minis, a tiny dab of plastic cement and patience will reward you with a pose that looks natural rather than glued in a mid-sneeze. A quick note on bases: they’re designed to read as a cohesive squad on display, so consider painting the bases in a unified tone—urban grit, Gotham browns, or a slick midnight blue finish to push the figures forward visually.

### Painting approach
Painting tips come down to two simple ideas: contrast and focus. Batman’s suit begs for a matte black base with highlights on edges where you want the silhouette to pop. Batman-Who-Laughs benefits from a darker palette with a few touches of sickly purple or green to hint at his warped morality. The Robins SW require brighter palettes—think red, green, and yellow accents with metallic touches on weapons—so you’ve got an obvious color separation when the board is a moving blur of fight cards.

If you’re new to painting miniatures, start with a base coat, apply a wash to bring out details, then pick three highlight points to keep the finish cohesive. You’ll achieve a cinematic look without spending three months on it. If you’re a veteran painter, you’ll enjoy the challenge of layering, edge highlights, and subtle weathering on the costumes to give each character a distinct personality on the table.

## Gameplay and mechanics

### Core rules in Pack 1
The pack leans on CMON’s familiar “move, shoot, fight, special ability” loop but with new twists tailored to the Batman/Who-Laughs dynamic. The base squad design allows you to gather one heavy hitter (Batman or Batman-Who-Laughs) and a few nimble Robins to manage objectives, with the Robins’ SW gear adding a different type of burst damage or defensive utility. Expect standard action economy: move, attack, special actions, and limited-use cards or tokens that provide a spice-level mechanic, like edge-case stealth or a fear aura for your opponents.

The Batman-Who-Laughs mechanic marries Batman’s tactical accuracy with the unpredictable nature of the Clown Prince of Crime—so you’ll see players balancing patient positioning with sudden, chaotic plays. The Robins SW can be used to swing objective captures, guard flanks, or create openings for your heavy hitters to pounce. The mix encourages a dynamic playstyle that rewards planning while not punishing you for a misstep.

### Batman vs Batman-Who-Laughs dynamic
The two Batman variants are designed to create a compelling “mirror match” orchestration. Batman plays the straight-hitting, gadget-laden commander who thrives on calculated moves and resource management. Batman-Who-Laughs embodies the mischief, exploiting your misplays and punishing sloppy positioning. The match-up is not just about who hits harder; it’s about who can force the other to reveal their cards first and then flip the battlefield to their own advantage.

### Robins SW synergy
The Robins add a layer of tactical depth that makes the pack more than a two-boss fight with a few goons. Their SW gear provides buffer actions, trap-like effects, or short-range damage bursts that help control threats before they get to Batman’s flank. The synergy shines when you coordinate a Robin distraction with a Batman heavy strike—the classic “hit, retreat, repeat” with a flair that’s distinctly CAPED class. If your group enjoys chain-activations and tempo games, Pack 1 will scratch that itch in a satisfying way.

## Scenarios and mission variety
