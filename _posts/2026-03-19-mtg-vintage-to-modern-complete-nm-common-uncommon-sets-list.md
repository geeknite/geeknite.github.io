## Quality control: what to look for when you receive your NM bulk
Because you are dealing with a bulk NM common/uncommon bundle at a flat shipping rate, you should approach with sensible checks. Here is a practical checklist you can use when you open your package:

- Card borders and corners: look for 1 or 2 very minor dings in big stacks; a few edge whitening marks are common in bulk but should not ruin the majority of cards. If you see widespread whitening or noticeable creases, that’s a red flag. Do a quick flick through several random cards to gauge the overall condition.
- Centering and surface: many commons and uncommons will have imperfect centering. A fair NM bundle will not have heavy surface scratches. If you can see through the card or if the surface shows significant dings in the light, consider raising an issue with the seller.
- Edge wear and back texture: edge wear is expected in bulk, but if you see rounding, heavy whitening, or back misprints you may be dealing with a lower grade. A handful of misprints are part of the charm; too many, and the set stops being NM.
- Photographic transparency: reputable sellers will include clear photos from multiple angles. If the listing lacks photos or the photos look suspiciously staged, proceed with caution. You want to see real counts, not a carefully curated angle.
- Packaging: ensure there is proper protection (top loader, semi-rigid sleeves, binder pages or sealed packaging). A well-protected bulk shipment reduces the risk of damage in transit, especially if your shipping address is not in a climate-controlled environment.

If you are new to bulk NM, don’t expect perfection. If one in every 40 cards is a dinged common, you’re still fine for casual play, but if half your stack is borderline, you might want to renegotiate, request replacement for damaged cards, or consider a different solicitation. The seller’s willingness to address quality concerns quickly is a better signal than a flawless photo. The world of bulk means you will see variance, and the novelty lies in discovering usable cards that slip into your deck-building puzzle in surprising ways.
