### Rules and how to play with the Bakugo set

If you know UNO Flip, you know the general flow. If you’re new to UNO Flip, here is a quick refresher with a Bakugo-tinged twist: each player gets seven cards. On your turn, you play a card that matches either color or number. If you don’t have a playable card, you draw. When you flip to the dark side, the action cards take effect; you may have to draw more cards or skip multiple turns. The Bakugo edition uses the same triggers but labels them with explosive-themed names. To avoid confusion, keep the color-coded sides visible and remind players to flip at the right moment. A lot of the joy comes from watching someone realize they misread a card, then watch them scramble to salvage a situation with a well-timed Bakugo punchline. In practice, the Bakugo cards add a touch of personality without delaying the game’s pace; they are the spice, not the main course.
