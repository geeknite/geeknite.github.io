## The Basics

### What is None But Heroes?

None But Heroes is a strategic board game that simulates historic battles with a focus on tactical decision-making and resource management. With beautifully designed components, this game promises to transport players to the heart of the action.

### Components

The game comes with an array of miniatures, cards, and tokens that are meticulously designed. From the detailed maps to the finely crafted soldier miniatures, these components are a collector's dream.

## The Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setting up None But Heroes feels like preparing for a grand battle. It's time to lay out the maps, assemble the miniatures, and try not to get distracted by the sheer awesomeness of the components.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, players draw cards and use them to perform actions. These could range from moving troops and attacking to fortifying positions or managing resources. The synergy between strategies and the unpredictability of war is where None But Heroes shines.

### Combat

Combat is straightforward but strategic. Players must coordinate their attacks and defenses to outmaneuver and defeat their opponents. Remember, thoughtful planning and tactical genius are the keys to victory.

## The Story

### Scenarios

None But Heroes introduces various scenarios inspired by historic battles. From the Battle of Gettysburg to the Siege of Yorktown, each scenario offers unique challenges and requires different strategies.

### Characters

This game allows players to command legendary historical figures such as Ulysses S. Grant, Robert E. Lee, and George Washington. Each character has distinct abilities and strategies that keep the gameplay fresh and engaging.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Immersive Gameplay**: The historical theme adds depth and excitement, making you feel like you're part of an epic saga.
- **Replayability**: With different scenarios and characters, no two games are ever the same.
- **Beautiful Components**: The miniatures and maps are a feast for the eyes.
- **Strategic Depth**: Coordinating attacks and defenses adds a layer of strategy that keeps players engaged.

### Cons

- **Complex Setup**: Be prepared to spend a good amount of time setting up each scenario.
- **Punishing Difficulty**: Some scenarios can be overwhelmingly tough, which might frustrate newer players.
- **Expensive**: Collecting all expansions could be a hit to your wallet.
