## Design and Ergonomics: How it actually works for humans

When you build a workspace for ten, you must think about human dynamics. Open plans love a good energy spike, but they hate awkward knee angles, cramped hips, and the never-ending question of who is responsible for chair adjustments. This Infinite workstation design leans into collaboration by providing ample table surface, but it also pushes ergonomics into the spotlight. Here is what matters in practice:

- Spacing: At 9000 mm, you can fit ten chairs with room to spare for a whiteboard or two. However, without screens, you might need to rely on body language, sticky notes, and interpretive dance to convey a point. If your team loves to crowd-surf ideas, the space could be a dream. If you savor quiet, personal focus, you may feel like a medieval scribe in a library of shared surfaces.
- Height: The 730 mm height is generous for standing discussions and seated work. It is tall enough to ensure chair wheels glide without causing a minor earthquake in the floor. This is not a desk for tiny children. It is a desk for professionals with long legs and longer to-do lists.
- Surface: The oak veneer gives a pleasant tactile feel. It is not overly slippery or sticky, which means you can slide a laptop with relative ease. The surface is wide enough to spread out a dozen papers, a couple of laptops, and a snack plate that will probably survive an apocalypse of coffee spills.
- Collaboration vs focus: Ten-person benches are inherently social. If your team thrives on spontaneous brainstorming, this is a stage worthy of a TED talk. If your workflow depends on deep concentration, you will either need a throwback solution like privacy pods or a few good headphones to create personal soundscapes.

### Ergonomics Tactics for Real-World Use
To maximize comfort, you might want to pair the Infinite with adjustable chairs that offer proper lumbar support and synchronized reclining features. Consider also providing footrests for longer people and foot tappers for those who like to keep a rhythm while discussing sprint goals. A simple rule of thumb: if your team needs to discuss something fragile, designate a two-person focus zone near the ends of the bench and rotate the talk baton to avoid head bobbing in unison.

## Cable Management and Screen Considerations

Since this is a no screen model, there is a built-in opportunity to rethink connectivity and cable management. You won’t find a monster tangle of monitor arms on this bench, which is a win for the eyes. However, you still need order for power, docking stations, and perhaps a handful of USB-C hubs for the occasional laptop charge frenzy. A smart plan might include:

- Centralized power hubs tucked under the work surface with cable channels running along the underside of the bench to keep cables off the tabletop.
- Clip-in grommets at strategic points so people can route cables without performing interpretive judo on their cords.
- Optional accessory trays for pens, sticky notes, and those wooden pencils that somehow survive every reshuffle.

A screen-free environment invites creative collaboration but demands discipline. It is the Lego block of office setups: you have unlimited potential as long as you do not step on a brick at 3 p.m. on payday.

## Assembly and Maintenance: Two Modules, One Dream

The concept of two modules means you are not building a single wall street fortress on your own. You assemble two five-seat blocks and then join them like a long seam in one of those artisanal jeans. Expect a few hours for a thorough setup, especially if you do not have an assistant named Siri to guide you through the manual. The good news is that modularity is your friend: you can disassemble and reconfigure into a different shape if your office layout changes or if an executive decree calls for a new collaboration workflow.

Maintenance is straightforward. The oak veneer handles daily life with aplomb, but you still want to wipe away coffee rings before they set into the grain. For scratches and abrasions, a light furniture polish will help keep the surface looking sharp. If your team treats the surface like a canvas for creative doodles, provide whiteboard markers and a dedicated wipe-friendly area so the desk does not become a public gallery of doodles.
