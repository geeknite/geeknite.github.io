## Corner The Market: what it actually adds

The core concept of Pit is chaotic trading under pressure. You’re shouting, fingers twitching, and suddenly someone yells a number that collapses the stock market of your party in one glorious five-second sequence. Corner The Market adds a twist that attempts to introduce some strategic gravity to the chaos. It’s like turning a mosh pit into a chess tournament, but with more confetti and less silence.

### How Corner The Market plays out

- The market cards create micro-goals: but can you corner a single commodity before your neighbor does something even more devious with the same card set?
- The bell serves a secondary role: it’s a reminder that you’re in a “deluxe” edition and the universe is receptive to loud noises.
- The strategy layer comes from recognizing when to hoard versus when to scream for trades. It’s a puzzle that turns into a party trick when you realize you’ve cornered the market in Pork Bellies and the table erupts in chaos.

Experienced Pit players will appreciate the density of the decision points, while newcomers will enjoy the social friction of trying to explain why you just yelled “RICE!” while someone else claimed “CORN” to their own dismay. The rules are approachable enough for a light night, but there’s enough nuance to support a longer campaign of smiling misery.

## How to play (quick primer)

If you’re familiar with Pit, you’ll feel right at home. If you’re new, you’ll be there within a round or two.

1) Setup: Everyone gets a hand of commodity cards, a few market cards are placed face-up, and the bell sits ready for action.
2) Objective: Trade aggressively to collect sets, with Corner The Market introducing a micro-goal to corner a particular commodity.
3) Turn order: There isn’t a traditional turn order in Pit; it’s a free-for-all of shouts. The Bell signals a round end, and everyone must settle on a trade before the next chaos wave begins.
4) Trading: You yell, you wave your cards, you try to out-banter your friends (and sometimes bluff). It’s a social blustery cloud of trading.
5) Corner The Market twist: The market cards create a temporary goal. If you manage to assemble a complete set that matches a Corner The Market objective, you trigger a bonus effect that can swing the entire session—usually in the most dramatic fashion imaginable.
6) Endgame: The round ends when an agreed trigger happens (often the bell hooping), and players tally points based on sets and objective bonuses.

Tips for success:
- Don’t be afraid to bluff with a straight face. The moment you smile, you reveal your hand’s true intentions.
- Use the bell strategically. Ring it a beat earlier to seize the turn order advantage or late in a round to force a rushed decision.
- Communicate with your table with witty banter. It’s not cheating; it’s performance art.

### A few sample strategies
- Early aggression: Push for rapid trades early on to secure a few key commodities before the table locks down.
- Diversification: Don’t chase a single commodity; use Corner The Market objectives to create synergy among several sets.
- Timing the bell: Save the bell for the moment you’re confident you’re about to swing into a dominant position. If you ring too early, you might invite chaos that undermines your plan.
