## What is Throw Throw Burrito?

Throw Throw Burrito is a party game created by Smirk & Laughter. It comes in a heavy, color-splashed box that promises chaos, laughter, and mild injuries. The core premise is simple: collect matching sets of cards by grabbing flying burritos and dodging foam projectiles launched by your friends. It’s a blend of a card game and a dodgeball duel—without the actual gym socks. In a used edition, you also get the joy of patina and potential sticker residue—quirks that become talking points around the table.

### Components and Setup

The box typically includes:

- A deck of Cards – whimsical, sometimes punny, sometimes aggressively obscure.
- Two Foam Burritos – soft to the touch, slightly itchy if you scratch your nose mid-throw.
- Token markers, players’ boards, and a scoring pad.
- A “burro bomb” tile and other silly accessories that remind you that physics is not your friend in this game.

In a used copy, you might discover:

- A burrito that’s seen a bit more life than the average salad, with creases and faint juice marks (don’t worry, it’s just paint, not actual meat). 
- A slightly worn rulebook with coffee rings and a page marked with doodles that indicate someone’s strategy for life, not just the game.
- A few stickers or marginal notes on the inside cover—these are tiny time capsules of past players’ strategies and jokes.

The setup is quick. Shuffle the deck, place burritos at the table’s center, and shuffle. Explain the rules: collect sets or avoid the burrito tosses until your fingers are too slow or too excited to catch a card. It’s a high-energy experience, and in the used market, the tactile warmth of a box that’s traveled is part of the fun.

### A Quick Look at the Mechanics

- Players work to collect approved card sets by grabbing burritos when they appear. The catch: when a burrito is thrown, everyone scrambles to dodge.
- If you catch the burrito during a throw, you trigger a mini-challenge that often involves discarding or exchanging cards, muttering a pun under your breath, and trying not to inhale the burrito’s cloud of (likely harmless) foam aroma.
- The scoring system rewards both speed and risk, meaning you’ll oscillate between sprinting for a set and cowering behind a chair while the burritos fly. It’s chaos, but it’s also a symphony of everyone realizing that their friends are not good at throwing. In a good way.

## The Components: Quality and Quirk

### The Burritos

The foam burritos are the stars of the show. In new copies, they feel fresh—soft enough to catch but firm enough to avoid collapsing mid-air. In used copies, you might notice a slight flattening, a few permanent creases, or a tiny squeak that happens when you squeeze them. The squeak, frankly, adds a comedic layer: you’ll catch yourself counting seconds before you twist your wrist to flick it away. The importance of the burritos in this game can’t be overstated—the entire chase revolves around these fake fast-food icons. If these things are comfortable in your hands, the game works as intended.

### Cards and Rulebook

Card stock is decent in most releases. A used copy might show slight edge wear on the top corners, which is purely cosmetic but also a badge of honor. The rulebook is small, easy to skim, and filled with witty remarks that celebrate the absurdity of the game. Don’t worry if you’ve never played a game that requires you to shout “Burrito dodge!” while your friend pretends to be a living shield. The rules walk you through it with clarity and a few jokes that remind you that game night is supposed to be unpredictable.

### The Playing Surface and Table Talk

The table becomes a stage when Throw Throw Burrito enters the room. ## Gameplay Experience: A Sincere Chaos Review

### The Pace

If you love fast rounds and loud laughter, you’ll adore the tempo. A typical match has 2–4 rounds of escalating chaos until someone forms a predictable pattern and the whole pack tries something new to outsmart the others. The pace is punishing in the best possible way: you’re sprinting, dodging, then suddenly you’re handing someone a card you don’t want to give up, while a burrito sails toward your face. It’s ridiculous, and that’s the point. A used copy often has more character in the box than a pristine one because the players who used it have left their fingerprints on the dune of cardboard dust and paint splatters, which can be endearing or alarming depending on your penchant for grimy counters.

### Strategy vs. Chaos

There’s very little in the way of strategic depth, which is fine. The joy of Throw Throw Burrito is not in long-term planning, but in micro-decisions: do you risk grabbing a risky set, or do you wait and hope your friends misplay? Do you duck behind the couch or fly across the room to punch a card into alignment? The chaos is the strategy, and in a used copy, you’ll find that the vibe encourages improvisation. If you’re a strategic gamer who worships at the altar of “math and synergy,” you’ll still have a great time, just be prepared to abandon your spreadsheets and embrace the ridiculous. If you’re a social gamer who thrives on quick, silly rounds, you’re in heaven.

## The Used Copy Checklist

Before you toss your money at a used copy, run through this quick checklist:

- Box condition: Are there rips, tears, or missing stickers? Are the burritos still intact and squishy or have they degraded into a rock-hard shell that feels more like a stress toy?
- Foam burritos: Do they retain their bounce? Are there sharp edges? Do they squeak in a way that’s endearing rather than alarming?
- Rulebook: Is it legible, with no pages missing? Are the player aids intact and readable? Are there coffee stains that might double as a memory of a late-night game?
- Tokens and boards: Are the tokens intact? Is there any warping of the cardboard or war-torn edges of your favorite counters?
- Personal touches: If the box contains doodles or notes, decide if they add value or simply remind you of the person who previously owned it. Either way, they contribute to the story.

If these checks pass, you’re probably looking at a fantastic bargain that will deliver many nights of laughter and mild chaos. If not, you might want to negotiate for a discount or look for a seller who’s willing to swap out a few components for shinier parts. It’s a retail game, after all.
