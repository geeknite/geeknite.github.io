## Card design and mechanics: what actually changes in Cross Worlds
BT03 introduces a few twists that feel like they were designed by someone who plays a lot of card games and fell in love with dimensional travel as a concept. Here are practical takeaways for players who approach this set from both collector and player perspectives:

- Dimension Link mechanics: A set of cards that reference other world attributes and grant cross world synergy bonuses. The idea is to reward players who build around a broader alliance rather than focusing on a single universe. This can lead to early pressure if you manage your energy economy well.
- Cross World foils: Foil treatment on certain rarities that accentuate the dimensional motif. The foil quality is decent and durable under normal play and casual sleeve abrasion, so your shiny art survives the table slap test.
- New character variants: Expect a mix of alternate art versions and reprint potential, but with a twist that signals their cross world role. These are the cards collectors chase, especially if the art team nails the portal aesthetic.
- Deck-building implications: The set nudges players toward mixed world archetypes rather than pure mono world domination. If your local scene enjoys offbeat ideas, Cross Worlds pays off with creative synergies. If you prefer rigid tier lists, you may need to adjust expectations and embrace the occasional off meta pick that becomes a hidden gem after a few rounds.

In practice, BT03 rewards experimentation without punishing players who like a slower plan. The balance leans toward exciting openings and mid to late game board states that feel earned rather than gifted by a single power card.
