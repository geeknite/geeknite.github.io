## Materials and Build Quality
### The Material Story: Neoprene vs. Cloth
The Green Horde mat is marketed as a neoprene surface, which has a few practical benefits for a living-room battleground. Neoprene tends to be more resistant to warping, slightly more forgiving under heavy tokens, and easier to wipe down than cloth mats. It also tends to resist fraying around the edges, assuming the edges are heat-sealed or roughly finished. If you’ve ever had a cloth mat that curled at the edges after a few scenarios, you’ll appreciate the more robust feel of neoprene.

That said, neoprene isn’t magic. It can attract dust and fine hair more readily than some matte cloths, which means you’ll want to give it a quick wipe-down before a big session. A damp microfiber cloth with a touch of mild soap is enough for most scenarios. Avoid harsh chemicals; you’re not cleaning a car seat, you’re cleaning a tiny swamp that needs to stay pristine for the next wave of survivors. After a few sessions, you’ll notice the mat holds up—dimensional stability remains solid, colors stay vibrant, and the surface doesn’t feel sticky, even after you’ve spilled the occasional coffee or energy drink on it.

### Edges, Curl, and Longevity
Edge integrity matters. A lot of mats suffer from curling at the edges; a rogue curl can throw off line-of-sight calculations and create “wiggle room” that your fellow players will notice before you roll a single die. The Green Horde mat features clean, likely heat-sealed edges designed to minimize curling. It’s not a miracle cure, but it’s a noticeable improvement over cheaper, untreated surfaces. In my testing, the mat laid flat within a few minutes of unrolling, with only minimal air-bubble distortion that settled after a gentle press.

Longevity is another factor. If you’re a hardcore gamer who plays weekly, you’ll want to consider protection against scuffs from minis and dice. The surface takes minor scuff marks well, and the printed countryside continues to look good after repeated wipe-downs. It isn’t a “wipe-and-forget” product; occasional light scanning with a microfiber and a non-abrasive cleaner will keep it looking sharp for longer, which is exactly what you want if you’ve got a campaign that’s been running since the dawn of your group.

## Maintenance and Durability
### Cleaning and Care
The mat’s surface is resilient enough to survive regular gaming nights.
