## Care guide and maintenance routines
- Regular vacuuming with a beater bar on a low setting keeps the pile crisp and avoids flat spots.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic entryways or hallway intersections.
- Blot spills immediately rather than rubbing, to avoid color transfer and pile distortion.
- For deeper cleanings, follow manufacturer guidelines; many owners can manage gentle cycles or hand wash with care if the product page permits.
- A rug pad is a smart investment: it reduces slipping, adds cushioning, and extends the rug’s life by distributing weight more evenly.

### Spot cleaning tips that actually work
Spill happens. The key is acting fast and using mild cleaners. For common culprits like coffee, wine, or tomato sauce, a quick blot and a dab of dish soap water will usually restore the surface. For more stubborn stains, a gentle cleaner recommended by the manufacturer is ideal. Always test on a hidden corner first to ensure colorfastness.

## How to choose the right size and color (revisited for 8x10)
- Measure with care: for a typical living room, an 8x10 rug under the seating area helps anchor the sofa and chairs without crowding the space.
- Proportion matters: a larger rug should still leave a border of floor visible around the perimeter, ensuring you’re not “hugging the furniture in” in a way that makes the room feel smaller.
- Color logic: if your walls are light, a slightly darker or textured neutral can create depth. If your walls are bold, a calm mid-tone helps prevent color clashes.
- Lighting matters: consider the room’s exposure to natural light. In rooms with bright sun, cooler neutrals stay crisper; in dim rooms, warmer neutrals keep the space from feeling gloomy.

## Rug care and maintenance (expanded tips)
- Vacuuming: weekly is the general rule, but in high-traffic areas you might push to 2–3 times per week.
- Rotation: rotate every 3–6 months to ensure even wear, especially if the rug sits in one position long-term under traffic lanes.
- Sun protection: keep direct sunlight off the rug for long periods to preserve color integrity.
- Edge care: check edges for curling; if you see lift, consider a non-slip pad or a light seam adhesive (per manufacturer guidelines) to keep the edges laying flat.
