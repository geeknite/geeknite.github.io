### Cleaning, maintenance, and everyday care
One of the big selling points for synthetic rugs is ease of maintenance. Here’s a practical care checklist you can actually use:
- Regular vacuuming: Use a low to medium suction to avoid pulling fibers. Vacuum in different directions to remove loose dirt from the pile.
- Blot, don’t rub: For spills, blot with a clean cloth from the outside in to avoid spreading. Avoid vigorous rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers.
- Treat stains promptly: Mild soap and water or a gentle carpet cleaner designed for synthetic fibers usually do the trick. Always test on a small hidden area first.
- Quick-dry: If the rug gets very wet, allow it to dry thoroughly before walking on it to prevent mold or odor buildup.
- Rug pad: A non-slip pad underneath isn’t just for safety—it also reduces wear and helps fibers ‘bounce back’ after foot traffic or kids’ play sessions.

For deeper cleaning, a gentle hand-clean or machine wash (if the manufacturer’s care label allows) can rejuvenate the pile, but always follow the product’s official care instructions to avoid voiding warranties. The practical upshot: you won’t be in a constant cycle of “professionals or bust.” The Quarry Oyster is designed to be manageable for real people with real life messes.
