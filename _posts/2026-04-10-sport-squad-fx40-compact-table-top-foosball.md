## Setup and assembly
Assembling the FX40 is a surprisingly straightforward process. You’ll likely spend more time choosing a color scheme for your coffee than actually assembling this thing. On average, expect 15-25 minutes, depending on your familiarity with small screws and the speed at which your cat decides that the instruction manual is a new toy.

Tips:
- Clear a flat space and lay out all screws before you start; you don’t want to realize you’ve misplaced a key nut halfway through.
- Attach the legs and frame first, then mount the playing surface. This helps you avoid the “gravity says no” moment when the full weight of the unit tries to slide off the table while you tighten the last screw.
- Make sure the rods are aligned properly and that the handles move smoothly from one side to the other. If they feel gritty, a light wipe-down and a touch of silicone spray can help.

## Maintenance and care
The FX40 wants to be treated with respect, not with a weekly maintenance regime that would require a professional sports car mechanic. A quick wipe-down after use to remove fingerprints and dust is enough. If you notice the rods becoming a little less silky after a few sessions, a light application of silicone-based spray is a gentle upgrade that keeps everything smooth without changing the feel of the play.

Keep balls in a separate container when not in use to avoid mischief from pets or curious human hands. If you have kids around, you can even assign ball retrieval as part of a mini-game—reward the child with extra screen time if they help you find the stray ball under the couch.

## Common issues and quick fixes
- Balls sticking on the play surface: wipe with a dry microfiber cloth, then test the ball’s movement. If it sticks again, check for surface debris and clean accordingly.
- Rods feeling gritty: apply a small amount of silicone spray to the rods and work them back and forth until they feel smooth.
- Feet wobble: ensure all legs are firmly attached and the surface is level. A quick readjustment can solve a lot of wobbly problems.
