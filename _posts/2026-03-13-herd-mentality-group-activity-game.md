## What is Herd Mentality?
Herd Mentality is a party game where players respond to prompts with single word answers or short phrases. The core mechanic is straightforward: reveal all answers, then collectively identify the herd—the most common answer—without letting ego ruin the moment. If your answer is in the herd, you score points. The more people who picked the herd answer, the more points you get. If you somehow picked an answer that nobody else did, you still get a reaction, which is valuable in a social game night because laughter is a legitimate currency.

### The setup
- Players: 4–12 is a sweet spot; more than 12 can become unwieldy without a strict timebox.
- Time: 20–40 minutes for a full game night with 5–6 rounds, including socializing and snack breaks.
- What you need: a deck of prompts, writing surface (paper slips or a whiteboard), pens, a timer, and a moderator who is comfortable with laughter that might be louder than the voice of reason.
- Space: enough room for people to write without looking over each other’s shoulders. Bonus points for ambient lighting and a playlist that says let’s do this without becoming a full on karaoke session.

### The core rules (simple version)
1. The prompt goes up. Everyone writes an answer privately.
2. All answers are revealed and grouped by identical responses.
3. The herd is identified as the largest group of identical answers. Those in the herd score points.
4. If you picked a unique answer, you can still get team sympathy points if your answer is entertaining and well-timed but the strictly speaking scoring margins tend to favor the herd.
5. Play continues with a new prompt and a new round. After a set number of rounds, the player with the most points wins.

> Note: In many households, players also secretly assign a role like Herd Whisperer or Quiet Rebel to add flavor. It is optional but extremely fun if your group enjoys that sort of RPG-lite vibe.

### Optional house rules for extra flavor
- Silence the herd: no speaking during the answer phase. This forces a pure reading of the room during reveal.
- The inverse round: one round where the goal is to pick the least common answer for a big twist in scoring.
- Crowd favorite: add a rule where players vote on the most entertaining answer, awarding bonus points to the storyteller.
- Thematic prompts: tailor prompts to a theme like sci-fi, retro gaming, or a specific fandom to increase the giggles and the references.

## How to run a round smoothly: a step-by-step guide
### Step 1: The prompt drop
The moderator reads a prompt and ensures everyone understands the boundaries. Prompts should be broad but not insulting or too personal. Good prompts have plausible answers ranging from the obvious to the absurd. Examples: What is the ultimate web browser you would trust with your life? What is a hobby that sounds impressive but is actually extremely low effort? If you could replace your job with one cartoon character, who would it be?

### Step 2: The writing sprint
Players write their answers privately. In a standard 60-second round, you can push for speed while still letting players conjure something witty. The key is to avoid giving away the herd by asking everyone to write the same thing—variations spark the real magic.

### Step 3: The reveal and herd counting
Answers are revealed and grouped. The herd is the largest group of identical answers. The moment you spot that one clever person wrote something that somehow matches the majority and the rest of the table erupts, you’ve found a classic Herd Mentality moment.

### Step 4: Scoring and storytelling
Players who chose the herd answer score points. If a round produces multiple identical answers, everyone who picked those gets points, often creating a mini-studded chorus of cheers. If you chose a unique answer that happens to be funny, you still get social points for the performance. The moderator often adds bonus applause for the best pun or the most cinematic delivery.

### Step 5: Move to the next round
Rotate prompts, or rotate the role of host if you want to share the load. The game continues for the planned rounds until someone hits the target score or until the snacks have vanished into a powerful vortex of chips and dry soda.

## Variants and twists to keep the board fresh
Herd Mentality thrives on clever prompts and a little roleplay. Here are some veteran twists that keep sessions fresh without breaking the vibe:
- Thematic deep dive: base prompts on a specific theme like space opera, 80s arcade, or anime tropes. This nudges the herd toward shared cultural signposts.
- Rival herd mode: designate two competing herd factions and score based on which herd the room collectively follows for a given round.
- Turbo rounds: shorten answer time to 30 seconds and crank up the rapid-fire jokes. Great for high-energy crowds.
- Quiet mode aftermath: after a few rounds, switch to silent writing and only allow vocal reactions during the reveal. It changes the pacing dramatically and can result in a different comedic payoff.

### Caveats for smooth sailing
- Avoid prompts that touch delicate personal topics. The goal is inclusive fun, not awkwardness.
- Keep clips of humor non-personal and kind. The best moments come from shared experiences, not from someone feeling targeted.
- The host should control the pace. If a round flops, switch up prompts or introduce a fun prompt that invites a quick, uncontroversial laugh.
- Consider accessibility: ensure prompts are legible and answers can be easily written and read by players with different levels of literacy or language comfort.

## Round-by-round example: a mini-session
- Prompt: What is your superhero side hustle?
- Answers: The room fields a surprising mix of entries like Night Chef, Super Librarian, Gadget Gardener, and Procrastination Man.
- Reveal: The herd forms around Night Chef, because food powers the team. A few misfits go for the equally valid Gadget Gardener. The crowd roars at a punny Publius-ian note: Super Librarian becomes the crowd favorite for its bibliographic flair.
- Scoring: Night Chef and Gadget Gardener both score, while Procrastination Man earns hearty applause for its honesty.
- Aftermath: The host announces a snack break and displays the next prompt on a whiteboard: If your pet could join a sports team, what would it be? The room enters full brainstorming mode.

This is the vibe you want: quick rounds, high energy, and a genuine sense of curiosity about your friends. If people are new, the game will quickly reveal who is quietly observing things, who is ready with a pun in the pocket, and who is silently plotting for a dramatic reveal in the next round.

## Reading the room: humor, pacing, and consent
Humor is the oxygen of Herd Mentality. It thrives when people feel safe taking creative risks. Here are some practical tips to keep the mood buoyant:
- Lead with a warm joke and an inclusive prompt to set the tone.
- If someone seems hesitant, encourage them with a supportive comment or a gentle prompt tweak. The goal is participation, not spotlight shaming.
- For new players, reserve a couple of easier prompts to build confidence before unleashing the truly wild ones.
- If a moment becomes too chaotic or awkward, do a quick reset: cheer for the person who attempted something bold, then move to a lighter prompt.

## Accessibility and inclusivity in practice
Herd Mentality travels well across different groups, but to keep it inclusive you should:
- Use prompts with broad appeal and avoid niche jokes that exclude newcomers.
- Offer options for non-verbal responses, like writing or drawing if someone is more comfortable that way.
- Provide clear, gentle guidance on the scoring to reduce confusion and friction during the reveal.
- Respect boundaries: if someone expresses discomfort with a prompt, skip it and move to a milder alternative.

## Post-game: reflection and social payoff
After the last round, you will find the room more talkative than a conference lounge after the keynote. The real payoff of Herd Mentality is the social data you get from the group dynamic: what jokes land, who knows how to time their delivery, and which prompts anchor lasting inside jokes. For a Geeknite crew, the post-game conversations can go full recommend-your-favorite-nerd-franchise mode, where people riff on who would win in a crossover battle between two poll results and a cat meme.

### Pro tips for moderators
- Time management is your friend. Keep rounds crisp; one extra round can turn a high-energy moment into a lull.
- If you want to escalate the fun, introduce a theme for prompts like retro tech, sci-fi tropes, or video game lore. It gives people a north star for their answers.
- Use the herd concept as a gentle social experiment: watch how people choose to express themselves and how the group responds to humor that lands differently with different players.
- Build in a few palate-cleanser prompts—something light about food or pets—to reset the room after a big laugh or a tense moment.
