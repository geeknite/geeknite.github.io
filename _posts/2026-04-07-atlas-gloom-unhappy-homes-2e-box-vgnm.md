## Box condition and first impressions
 VG+ NM is a funny pairing of abbreviations that tells you two things at once. VG+ (Very Good Plus) means the box has seen some action. Maybe a small corner ding, a surface scuff, or a shelf tumble that didn’t quite become dramatic. NM (Near Mint) suggests the interior elements are in excellent shape, with just the faintest whisper of wear on the outer edges. Put together, VG+ NM on a Gloom expansion is usually a good sign that you’re not buying a damaged time capsule but rather a well-loved jewel with a little extra character.

### The box itself

The bottom line: the exterior tells you the box has spent time in a living room that smells faintly of cinnamon and printer ink, which is very Gloom in spirit.

### The interior layout
 Inside, you should expect a tidy pile of gloom‑tinted components: rule sheets, scenario cards, gloom cards specific to Unhappy Homes, and the usual tokens that will eventually migrate under sofa cushions and the occasional cat toy. In a 2nd Edition pack, you may see improved card stock compared to the original run, with a slightly more durable feel and perhaps a gloss that makes the cards look like they’ve spent years in a Victorian glass case—pretending they are art rather than cardboard with a villains’ laugh printed on the back.

## Components and usability in VG+ NM condition

### Cards
 Card quality is crucial in a game like Gloom. In a VG+ NM box, you expect cards to be clean, with no major scratches or edge wear that would make shuffling unpleasant or damage the finish. The Unhappy Homes set should have cards that shuffle smoothly, without sticking to the sleeves you probably earnestly forgot to take off your shelves after the last game night. If you find glossy surfaces with micro-tears along the edges or a bit of whitening on the corners, that’s typical and not a deal-breaker for a well-loved box. For collectors, such wear can even add a certain “battle-scarred veteran” aura.

### Tokens and markers
 Tokens in gloom games tend to be small plastic coins or cardboard shapes that serve as punctuation marks for misery. ## Practical care and maintenance tips

Even a box described as VG+ NM deserves a little TLC. The following guidelines help preserve the moodiness of your Unhappy Homes set for many campaigns to come:
- Keep the box away from direct sunlight to prevent fading and cardboard brittleness. Gloom art wants to glow, not fade into sunburnt nostalgia.
- Store sleeved cards upright or flat in a dedicated organizer. Sleeves add protection against the inevitable drama that comes when someone drops a goblin card on the floor and it skitters into the cat’s hideout.
- Use a soft cloth to remove any dust on the card faces and edges. Avoid harsh cleaners that could affect the ink or the back design—this is not a car finish; it’s a mood board.
- Consider a basic card saver for those enthusiasts who want to preserve the sharpness of the gloom‑cards in perpetuity. If you’re not sure, a simple protective sleeve often does the trick.
- If the corners show real wear, you can carefully use a corner protector that is reversible, so you don’t affect resale value for future collectors.
