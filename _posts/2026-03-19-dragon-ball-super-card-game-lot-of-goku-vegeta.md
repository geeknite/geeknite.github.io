## Household Play: Sorting, Sleeving, and Storage Tips
### Sorting tips for a big lot
- Start by rarity and type: Leaders, Battle Cards, and Extra Cards. This makes it easy to sort through the pile without surrendering your sanity.
- Group Goku and Vegeta cards into two piles first, then by form or set; it helps you identify pairings and potential synergy more quickly.
- Separate foil from non-foil. It’s not just about aesthetics; foils become your party favors for casual tournaments, and they also help you spot potential heavy hitters when you’re building a deck on a budget.

### Sleeving and binder organization
- Use standard-sleeve protection for most cards; consider premium sleeves for foils or rare cards you plan to showcase. A binder with dividers labeled by form or by set helps you find that one love-at-first-sight card you pulled while your buddy was busy drafting a different plan.
- Consider a two-tier binder: core Goku vs Vegeta and a legendary or foil section for the showpiece cards. It gives your play area a sense of order amid the chaos of a dragon ball emoji-storm.

### Card care and maintenance
- Keep sheets away from humidity. Dragon Ball cards don’t dissolve in rain, but they don’t enjoy becoming wavy either. The same goes for direct sunlight, which will fry your eyes and your foil shine.
- Avoid deep edge wear from aggressive shuffling. If you’re prone to “shuffle like a deity,” consider a card-shuffler or at least a more careful hand.
