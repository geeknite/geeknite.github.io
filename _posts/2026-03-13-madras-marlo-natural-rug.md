## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh

Natural fiber rugs require a little more TLC than their synthetic cousins, but the payoff is a distinctive, durable presence that ages gracefully. Here’s a practical maintenance cheat sheet:

- Regular vacuuming: Use a vacuum with a gentle setting and avoid the beater bar on high. Natural fibers can be sensitive to intense agitation.
- Spot cleaning: Blot spills with a clean cloth as soon as possible. Avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution or specialized rug cleaner can help—test in an inconspicuous spot first.
- Rotation: Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and prevent uneven fading, especially if sunlight floods one side of the room.
- Protector considerations: Some natural fiber rugs benefit from a protective spray designed for natural fibers. If you have pets, consider a pet-friendly protector that won’t ruin the texture.
- Professional cleaning: Schedule a professional cleaning every 12–24 months, depending on traffic. A good pro can refresh the fibers and restore texture without over-wetting the rug.

### Potential Pitfalls
- Stains on natural fibers can be more challenging than on synthetic blends. Quick action is your friend.
- Some natural fibers are more prone to sun fading. If your rug sits near large windows, consider UV-blocking drapes or rotating placement to minimize color shift.
- Wrinkles and resting folds can occur in new wool or jute rugs. Placing the rug flat and giving it time to settle may eliminate waviness.
