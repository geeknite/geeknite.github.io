What is Zombicide Black Plague?
-------------------------------

First, a quick primer for anyone new to the party: Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative board game from CMON that drops you into a medieval zombie apocalypse. Think grimdark, but with more melee weapons and less existential dread about taxes. The core mechanic centers on cooperative play where 2-6 survivors work together to complete objectives while hacking, slashing, and dodging hordes of undead modeled as zombies. The dice determine both injury and outcomes of combat, and the board tiles form a modular cityscape of doom, with secret doors, traps, and loot chests that can swing the tide of battle in an instant. The “Black Plague” edition adds more serious-looking minis, a more brutal dice pool, and, yes, a few more ways to fail spectacularly. All in all, it’s a polished, chaotic, and surprisingly tactile experience: lights go out, swords collide with bone, and the table erupts with cheers and groans depending on how much blood is spilled on a single turn.

The Missing Dice Crisis: A Short, Frantic Tale
----------------------------------------------

Let’s not beat around the brazier: the demo unit arrived with the dice for the attack and defense mechanics missing. MIA. Absent. Zilch. The red, white, and black pips of destiny were not in the box. The moment we opened the lid and started counting, the situation shifted from “this is going to be a fun, cinematic run” to “we might need to improvise a deus ex dice.” The immediate questions were obvious:

- How many dice are required for a basic setup?
- Can you run a full session without the exact dice in the box?
- What are workable, fair, and not-too-crazy house rules for replacements?
- Will this color-coded apocalypse be workable at a gaming table without a roll of the true dice gods?

In practice, ZBP uses several types of dice with different outcomes: red combat dice for fighting, black or gray dice for special events, and white dice for certain modifiers. The absence of the dice doesn’t ruin the game; it creates an opportunity to lean into the improvisational heart of tabletop gaming. Our approach was to treat the missing dice as a prompt rather than a disaster. We pulled out spares from other games, pulled some artisan dice from the shelf, and even used a few tokens to track the outcomes when the dice were not present. It was not glamorous, but it worked well enough to test the core experience.

This side quest became a surprising source of humor rather than a pure frustration: the survivors sprinted across the board while a single player used a handful of tokens to track hit points, and a few dice rolls later, we had a narrative of cunning and hazard that felt in the spirit of the game—dramatic, a little silly, and entirely memorable. It’s a grim reminder that the best gaming nights are rarely perfect. They’re messy. They’re loud. They’re imperfect in the most charming ways. If the dice gods refuse you, you improvise; the table becomes your new altar of chaos, and you find a way to make it work. 

Setup, Components, and Aesthetics
----------------------------------

ZBP is a feast for the eyes. The miniatures look like they wandered straight out of a heavy metal riff and landed in a medieval tavern painting, while the tiles form a ruined cityscape that makes you feel like you’re defending a keep against a tide of rotting death. The art direction is sharp, the text on the cards readable, and the overall presentation of the box and its contents is premium CMON—glossy, tactile, and a little bit dramatic in the best possible way.

- Miniatures: The survivors and the zombies are well-sculpted, with enough variation to encourage hero worship from the hobbyist who can spot a quality sculpt from ten paces away.
- Tiles and board: The modular tiles slot together with satisfying click-clack and create lanes, corners, and chokepoints that can be exploited by clever players or, alternately, exploited by bad dice luck.
- Cards and loot: The loot cards and zombie behavior rules add flavor and tactical nuance; you’ll want to rummage through them mid-game to see what new advantages or traps turn up. 

Jekyll and Hyde: The Dice Also Do Things
--------------------------------------

With the original dice missing, we explored using community dice, other game dice, and even digital dice apps to simulate outcomes. The moral of the story: the game can survive the loss of specific physical dice, but you should be prepared to adapt. The core mechanic—combat resolution via dice—still functions if you replicate the dice type in a way that preserves the probability distribution. If you substitute too aggressively (think random number ranges or non-standard pips), you risk unbalancing the system and destroying the tension. If you’re forced into substitution, keep a few guardrails:

- Use a standard D6 if possible, with a clear mapping to the original red dice (e.g., 5-6 = hit, 3-4 = wound, etc.).
- Assign tokens or markers for misses and criticals to preserve the arc of the narrative.
- Communicate to players what you’re doing in the moment; transparency is your friend.

We found that the story remained intact as long as everyone understood the substitution rules. It’s a testament to the game design that the central experiences—the moral choices, the near-misses, the dramatic boss fights—still shine through even when you’re substituting a wheel of fortune with a wheel of improvisation. If you’re reading this and you’re about to embark on a CMON adventure with a missing dice situation, breathe. The game isn’t broken; it’s a chance to test your table’s improvisational chops and to see your friends become tactical philosophers under pressure.

The Core Gameplay: How It Feels to Be a Survivor
-------------------------------------------------

Zombicide Black Plague emphasizes cooperative play in which players control survivors trying to escape or survive a set of objective tasks on a zombie-infested map. The action is a blend of strategic movement, resource management, and cinematic combat. You’ll coordinate to rescue civilians, secure doors, and push toward the endgame while the board swells with a growing threat that can overwhelm even the most seasoned player.
