### Care and Cleaning
Cleaning is where the practical geek in us shines. Routine maintenance includes vacuuming to remove dust and debris. For spills, blot the area with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent solution if necessary. Avoid harsh chemicals that may discolor or degrade the fibers. Quick response is your friend here, as letting a spill sit can lead to staining or odor accumulation. For tougher stains, a fabric-safe cleaner and a gentle scrub can help lift the mark without harming the pile. If you want to be extra savvy, test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous corner first.

A few pro tips: place a mat under the rug in high-traffic entryways to catch grit before it becomes a chore. Use a simple rotating routine—the rug, not your houseplant—can cycle in and out of the main living space to keep wear even. And if you’re dealing with pet hair, a rubber brush or a pet-friendly vacuum attachment works wonders without having to subject the fibers to a heavy hand.

## Cleaning and Maintenance Tips
- Vacuum regularly to prevent dirt from embedding itself in the fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to avoid uneven wear from foot traffic and furniture movement.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth; do not rub, as rubbing can push stains deeper.
- Use a mild detergent solution for more persistent stains, testing it on a hidden corner first.
- For pet hair, a gentle brush or a vacuum attachment designed for pet hair can help remove fur without pulling the fibers.
- If necessary, consider professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months, especially in high-traffic zones. This keeps colors vibrant and fibers resilient over time.
- Consider a rug pad to prevent slipping and to extend the life of the fibers, especially under a coffee table where daily traffic concentrates.
- Air out the rug after cleaning to prevent damp odors—no one needs a perfume of damp wool when you’re binge-watching your favorite show.
