## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh
### Routine care that preserves color and texture
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers. Use a handheld vacuum for corners and transitions to prevent fraying at the edges.
- Rotate every 3–6 months to even out wear, especially in rooms with direct sunlight that can fade color gradients over time.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slippage and improve underfoot comfort. The pad also dampens noise and protects floor finishes.
- Spot clean spills promptly with a clean, dry cloth. Blot rather than rub to prevent embedding stains deeper into the fibers.

### Deep cleaning and stain removal
For stubborn stains, test a mild detergent solution on an inconspicuous corner first. If the stain persists, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in natural fiber rugs. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners, bleach, and high heat, which can degrade wool fibers and alter the rug’s color and texture. Sunlight fading is a real risk; consider window treatments or periodic repositioning to keep color even across the surface.

### Protective tips for longevity
- Keep it away from direct heat sources and intense, prolonged sunlight to prevent fiber degradation and color fade.
- Avoid placing heavy furniture directly on the rug’s center, since concentrated weight can cause indentations and uneven wear.
- Consider seasonal cleaning routines as part of your maintenance calendar, not as emergency repairs.
