## How to Play: Quick refresher with the Deluxe touch
### Setup and turn structure
Setup remains straightforward: deal each player a base number of cards, lay out the draw deck, and decide who will be the brave soul to risk a kitten sneeze first. The Deluxe Edition comes with a slightly tightened rulebook, clearer diagrams, and a few new card icons that reduce the dreaded moment of, “Wait, does anyone remember what this card does?” On your turn you can draw, play a card to alter the course of the game, or attempt to Defuse when a kitten lands in your pocket of doom. The core loop remains friendly to newcomers but adds micro-nuance for veteran players who enjoy the spicy feeling of a high-stakes, high-laughter punt.

### Card types and core mechanics
- Exploding Kitten: draw this, and you’re in the danger zone unless you can Defuse.
- Defuse: the saving grace cards that let you re-enter the game after an explosion.
- Attack, Skip, See the Future, Shuffle, Nope: these action cards let you twist order, momentum, and fate at the table.
- Exclusive kittens: a handful of new kitten cards that inject fresh “oh no” moments into the risk pool.

The Deluxe Edition’s big win is legible iconography and art that makes the consequences feel bigger, which translates into bigger reactions. The table erupts in laughs or groans at just the right moments, which is exactly what you want when you’re trying to coax chaos from a group of friends who swore they only came over for a quiet game night.

### Strategy notes
This is not a game of brute luck; it’s a social engineering exercise. You want to massage risk so that someone else draws, while keeping your Defuse toolkit ready for the inevitable last-minute miracle. The Deluxe Edition nudges this through clearer card clarity and a couple of new ritualistic vibes—think of a See the Future moment shrouded in theatrical flair that lets you peek one or two future cards with a flourish. The smarter you play, the less you reveal about your own hand and the more you enjoy the orchestra of chaos that follows.

Top tactics to consider:
- Save a few Defuses for the final rounds; last-second salvage is a comedy’s lifeblood.
- Stage your Attack sequences in two- to three-step micro-arcs to maximize table drama.
- Use Nope cards to derail overly confident players’ plans without tipping your own hand.
- Read the table energy: when the laughs start to crest, you’re probably about to see a big defuse or a spectacular explosion.

If you can master these micro-maneuvers, you’ll be the person who brings the party together and somehow also ends up wearing the crown of “Most Dramatic Defuse.”

### Variant rules and house rules
The Deluxe Edition invites surgical house-rule spicing. A few quick ideas to spark early chaos:
- World’s Hottest Cat App: players must moo a token before playing certain cards (yes, chaos is allowed).
- Catwalk mini-rounds: players perform a silly action before continuing, providing a dramatic pause for suspense and judgment at the table.

The core remains immutable: assess risk versus social leverage and avoid getting buried in cat-traction. The kit is designed to adapt, not to trap you in a maze of micro-rules that require a PhD in feline diplomacy.
