## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Geometry Sharp
### Cleaning Basics
- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting. Be mindful of fringe or edges if present; flatweaves can have a delicate edge line that you’ll want to protect.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth—avoid rubbing, which can drive stains deeper into the wool.
- For nice long-term care, rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and sunlight exposure.

### Stains, Odors, and Pets
- Most common spills (coffee, juice, wine) can be treated with a mild solution of water and a tiny bit of dish soap. Test on a small inconspicuous corner first.
- If pet hair is an issue, a textured rug brush can make your life easier in addition to a vacuum.
- Sunlight can fade colors over time. If your sunlit window is unforgiving, consider rotating the rug or using window treatments to protect vibrant blues.

### Storage and Seasonal Care
If you’re rotating seasons or moving homes, roll (don’t fold) the rug with the pile facing inward and store in a dry, climate-controlled space. Avoid plastic bags for long-term storage because wool breathes and the rug may thank you later by preserving its shape.
