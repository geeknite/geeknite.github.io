## Design and Build
### Form factor and aesthetics
Each bay is roughly 220x60x15 mm and weighs about 450 grams. The shell uses brushed aluminum with a protective polymer overlay. The connectors are recessed and magnetically aligned, so you don’t end up jamming the plug halfway into your table leg like a freshman trying to plug in a bulky USB-C cable.

The design screams “professional hobbyist” in a controlled fashion: minimal branding, but enough blue accents to remind you that this is a modern gadget and not your father’s old school parade battery.

### Battery chemistry and performance
The bays house pouch cells in a multi-bay arrangement. The pack energy per bay is in the ballpark of 120–150 Wh, depending on model and ambient temperature. The BMS monitors cell voltages, temperatures, and charge/discharge cycles to prevent your job from turning into a science drama.

Charging supports PD-friendly power sources, with per-bay charging rates up to 60–100 W. The combination of fast charging and high capacity is ideal for on-the-go workflows that demand speed and endurance.

### Connectivity and daisy-chaining
Daisy chaining works using the bay connectors on the sides. The BMS coordinates balancing across bays to keep everything in harmony. Pass-through charging keeps the laptop alive while the battery pack itself charges, making it a useful companion for long streams, travel days, or spontaneous marathons of code.

### Install and setup
1) Align the bay connectors with the base unit.
2) Press firmly until you hear the click.
3) Use the management app to calibrate the bays and enable pass-through if desired.
4) If prompted, update firmware. It’s usually a minor patch, but it can fix a bug that makes the LED blink in Morse code.

## Field Notes and Pro Tips
- Keep bays aligned and secured—the click is a friend, not an adversary.
- Use recommended chargers per bay to avoid heat spikes. If you must mix chargers, monitor temps.

- Label bays to track age or wear; mark newer vs older to simplify field swaps.
- For rapid-fire changes in shoots, practice a quick swap routine to minimize downtime.
