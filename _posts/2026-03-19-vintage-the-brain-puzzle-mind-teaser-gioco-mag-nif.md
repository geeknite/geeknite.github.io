### How to play and solve
Here’s a practical guide to getting your brain wearing its thinking cap (preferably the one with the magnets held in by memory of physics class).

- Start with the base frame. The base anchors your puzzle and gives you a solid reference point.
- Sort the pieces by shape and magnet orientation. Group identical shapes and note which magnets attract versus repel.
- Read the challenge (if you’ve got the booklet). If you’re lucky, the challenge texts are enigmatic and witty; if not, you’ll rely on the visual hints, which is still fun in a cryptic way.
- Build a core silhouette. The brain shape usually wants a symmetrical spine; start there by placing the central pieces first.
- Iterate: rotate, rearrange, and reorient. The magnets will click into place when you’ve hit a valid configuration. If you hear a soft click and feel tension release, you’re likely close to a solution.
- Validate the solution by comparing it to the challenge’s constraints. Some configurations emphasize symmetry; others aim for an asymmetrical but balanced look.
- Reflect on the process. The journey matters as much as the destination. You’ve learned something about spatial reasoning, about how you think, and about how a magnet can gently nudge your brain toward a pattern.

If you prefer video, you’ll find plenty of demonstration clips online; but there is something uniquely satisfying about the tactile feedback of real magnets in your hands. ### The science of magnets in puzzles (without going full lab coat)
Magnets add two dimensions to a puzzle: orientation and force. It’s not enough for pieces to fit; they must align with the magnets in specific directions. This means solving requires a mental model of how magnets behave: attraction, repulsion, and the effect of adjacent magnets on a chain of connections. The experience is akin to pattern discovery with the extra layer of constraint that magnets impose. For many puzzlers, this constraint is the sweet spot: enough difficulty to require strategy but not so much that it becomes a chaotic tangle. The magnets guide you toward a solution with tactile affirmation rather than a guessing game powered by trial and error.

### Practical care and display tips
- Display on a clean, dry surface away from other magnets or metal objects to prevent unwanted rearrangements.
- Wipe pieces with a soft, dry cloth to remove fingerprints and dust. Avoid soaking in water, which can affect magnets over time.
- If you store the kit, keep the pieces in a small container or pouch to prevent stray magnets from wandering.
- If magnets become misaligned, take a slow, patient approach and re-seat pieces rather than forcing them into place. Patience is a virtue for any good puzzle solver—and magnets respect that humble virtue.
