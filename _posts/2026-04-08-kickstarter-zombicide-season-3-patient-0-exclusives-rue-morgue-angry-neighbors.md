Geeknite returns with a field report from the Kickstarter trenches, where cardboard dreams sometimes come with miniature blood and a sense of dread about your dining room table after dark.

In this episode we dive into Zombicide Season 3 Patient 0 Exclusives, a pair of add ons that has backers buzzing like a twitchy zombie in a jar of preserves. On one side we have Rue Morgue, a notorious horror mag that has long haunted bookstores and late night TV. On the other side we have Angry Neighbors, a scenario module that promises suburban dread with a side of existential dread about the neighbor who borrows your power strip and forgets to return it.

![Patient Zero Box Art](./assets/images/zombicide-s3-patient0.jpg)

What are the Patient 0 Exclusives

The Rue Morgue tie in

Rue Morgue brings a flavor of pulp horror to the table. The exclusive mini is a zombie with a trench coat and a pocket copy of a Rue Morgue issue clutched in its rotting hand. The model is not just a new sculpt; it is a small narrative moment that can be used to kick off a session with an atmosphere check rather than a stats check. The exclusive card set adds a new encounter deck defined by investigative clues, deciphered handwriting, and a howling effect that triggers in the middle of a quiet periphery. The magazines serve as tokens that remind players that the undead in this universe know a thing or two about magazine racks and late returns.

Angry Neighbors

The Angry Neighbors module plays like a slice of suburban horror. You start with a block of houses rather than a single mansion. The neighbors are not mindless brute force but cunning, loud, and a little petty. Their cards twist the standard Zombicide flow by adding noise markers, an urban rumor track, and a mechanic where a neighbor can ring your alarm and bring in reinforcements if you do not clear a location fast enough. It is a playful, slightly hostile add on that injects tempo into an otherwise methodical game.

How it plays

The core gameplay remains intact. Season 3 introduces new tiles, new zombie variants, and new gear you can pick up along the way. The Patient 0 Exclusives do not rewrite the rules, but they give you hooks to build a session around a story instead of simply grinding every mission to a pulp. Rue Morgue introduces investigative flavor, allowing backers to read a clue that leads to a horror reveal. Angry Neighbors increases tension by making noise an important resource and by introducing a miscalculation risk at the edge of a cul de sac.

Miniatures and components

The Rue Morgue exclusive comes with a unique zombie model and a printed Rue Morgue issue that acts as a scenario prompt. The Angry Neighbors adds several new survivor cards with unique lines that reflect their mischief. The quality is typical CMON style: solid sculpting, clean molding, no paint job required for display, and a nice balance of scale with existing zombies.

Artwork and flavor

The Rue Morgue tie in is not just a gimmick; it is a sort of cultural wink at horror fans. The packaging art uses a moody palette and a hint of late 70s horror magazine design. The Angry Neighbors uses a crisp suburban street map style that feels like it was ripped from a crime doc and then filtered through a retro video look. The overall visual cohesion with Season 3 is good; the set feels like a cohesive add on rather than an afterthought.

Value and pricing

Backer value is always a balancing act. The exclusives are priced to reward dedicated fans but not to lock out casual players. The Rue Morgue model and the accompanying promo cards add a narrative layer that can enrich a campaign. If you are a player who picks the core box up for play, you may find these exclusives optional. But for collectors or players who want to run a horror influenced campaign set in a decaying suburb, the exclusives can be a strong driver to open the box sooner rather than later.

Comparison to Season 2

Season 2 had its own set of exclusives that leaned toward heavy combat and table presence. Season 3, with Patient 0, tries to broaden the thematic palette by integrating magazines, clues, and a narrative track. Rue Morgue feels like a natural extension of the horror vibe, while Angry Neighbors adds a human, possibly more personal threat level. The net effect is that Season 3 feels a little more story driven than Season 2, while maintaining the crunchy tactical core that Zombicide is known for.

Integration into campaigns

To use these exclusives in a night of play, start with a quick atmospheric briefing. Read a short blurb from the Rue Morgue issue and have players decide if their survivors are willing to investigate a rumor or skip the clue in favor of broader objectives. The Angry Neighbors can be introduced after a few rounds of standard play as a side track that grows or declines depending on how many houses you defend. In practice, these modules encourage a more flexible pacing than pure objective chasing.

Notes on table presence

If you display the Rue Morgue exclusive on the shelf, it will look glorious. The model is visually distinct enough to act as a centerpiece during a session. The Angry Neighbors, with its urban layout, tends to prompt players to spread out, pay attention to line of sight, and keep an eye on the perimeter.

Community reactions

Backers have shared mixed feedback. Some praise the thematic richness and the way the two exclusives complement Season 3’s flavor. Others wish for a more robust balancing approach or perhaps more scenarios to keep the fever going after the first few sessions. The consensus seems to be that this is a desire for more narrative injection rather than a criticism of the core functionality.

Pros and cons

Pros:
- Thematic expansion adds narrative hooks
- Unique mini and scenario cards provide replay value
- Strong visual cohesion with Season 3

Cons:
- Optional content may feel pricey to some backers
- The narrative focus might not appeal to players who only want pure tactics
- Some backers hoped for more content than two exclusives

Final verdict

For horror fans, the Rue Morgue and Angry Neighbors exclusives bring a welcome layer of atmosphere to Zombicide Season 3. They feel like a carefully curated mini campaign rather than just a set of tokens. If you want a campaign experience that leans into the mood and story of your zombie outbreak, these exclusives are worth adding to your collection. If your playgroup prioritizes raw numbers and raw power over ambiance, you may choose to skip or to acquire only the components you truly value.

Final Recommendation
