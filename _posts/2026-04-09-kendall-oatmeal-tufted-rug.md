## Cleaning & Maintenance

Maintenance is where the Kendall shines as a practical choice for busy households. Vacuuming regularly with a vacuum that has a low-beater bar setting keeps the pile looking tidy and reduces the risk of pulling fibers. For spills, act quickly: blot with a clean cloth or paper towel; avoid rubbing, which pushes the liquid deeper into the fiber. For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution is usually sufficient. Rinse with a clean cloth to remove any soap residue, and blot dry. Let it air dry fully before you set heavy furniture back on it to avoid warping or puckering.

If you spill a colored beverage, test a small area first, since we all know coffee is basically a test of life choices: sometimes you win, sometimes the stain wins. The rug’s backing is designed to resist wrinkling and to reduce slipping, but you still should pair it with a quality non-slip pad if you have hardwood or tile floors.

Worried about pet messes? No problem. Polypropylene fibers resist odors better than natural fibers, but you’ll still want to address any accident quickly. A mild enzymatic cleaner designed for carpets is fine if needed, followed by a rinse and thorough drying.

Stain removal quick tips:
- Blot, don’t rub
- Use cold water for protein-based stains (milk, sweat)
- Use a gentle solution for wine or coffee stains
- Dry thoroughly to prevent mold
