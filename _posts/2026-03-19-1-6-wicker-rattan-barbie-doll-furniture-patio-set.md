### Wicker vs rattan: a mini material debate
- Wicker is not a material in and of itself; it describes the weaving technique. Rattan is a natural vine that often supplies the structural backbone. In high-end vintage sets, you may see actual hand-woven wicker with a rattan frame. In more budget-conscious pieces, you’ll get a wicker-looking plastic craft that mimics the real thing. The difference is not just romantic; it affects durability and how the set ages.
- Vintage sets often use natural fibers and a slightly uneven weave that adds character. This unevenness is not a flaw; it signals a time before mass-produced perfection. If you value the quirks, you’re in the right club. If you crave perfectly uniform patterns that look like they were manufactured by robots in a glossy factory, you might prefer modern resin or plastic recreations.

## Condition, wear, and restoration tips
Vintage sets live by a code of care. If you plan to display rather than play, you might want to preserve the look of age while preventing further deterioration. Here are practical tips:
- Cleaning: use a soft brush or microfiber cloth to remove dust from the wicker weave. Avoid harsh solvents that can degrade the surface. For stubborn grime, a damp cloth with mild soap can help; dry thoroughly to prevent moisture-related warping.
- Sun exposure: prolonged direct sunlight can cause fading in painted details. If you display near a window, consider a UV-filtering curtain or a small, indirect-light setup to preserve color fidelity.
- Repair considerations: tiny loose weaving strands can be carefully tucked back into place with a fine needle and a dab of clear craft glue. If the table leg becomes wobbly, you may stabilize it with a small bead of glue at the joint; avoid excessive glue that might ooze into the weave.
- Teaset touch-ups: chipping paint on the teapot or cups can be touched up with enamel paint in a matching shade. Keep the touch-ups minimal and precise to maintain the vintage feel.

## Care, storage, and display tips for longevity
Keep your tiny patio pristine with these practical habits:
- Climate control: store in a cool, dry place. Excess humidity can promote mold on fabrics and weaken wicker fibers.
- Handling: when moving the set, lift rather than drag to protect joints and the wicker weave. A gentle touch goes a long way in preserving the product’s life.
- Display positioning: avoid high-traffic shelves where curious fingers or pets can interfere. A light dusting every few months keeps it looking its best without becoming a full-on restoration project.
- Photography care: if you photograph the set, use a macro-friendly lens and keep your light soft to avoid harsh reflections on glossy teacups and the parasol canopy.
