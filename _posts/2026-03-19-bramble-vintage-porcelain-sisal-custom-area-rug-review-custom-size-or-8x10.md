## Construction and Care: How It Handles Real Life
Sisal rugs have a reputation for being sturdy, but not invincible. The Bramble piece likely uses a natural sisal fiber woven tightly, with a backing that helps stabilize the weave. The beauty of natural fiber is its subtle imperfections—fibers may vary in thickness, colors can present slight variations, and that’s the design’s charm rather than a flaw. If you’re expecting a flawless, machine-perfect surface, you may be chasing a unicorn; if you want material character and authenticity, you’re on the right track.

### Cleaning rough-and-tumble environments
- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only vacuum (no beater bars). Loose dirt on natural fibers can grind into the weave over time, which reduces its life and its ability to politely ignore the coffee spill.
- For spills: blot, don’t rub. Avoid saturating the fiber with water; patchy moisture can lead to swelling or warping. If you spill something sticky (think red wine or suspicious smoothie), gently blot and use a mild cleaning solution recommended for natural fiber rugs. Always test in a hidden area first.
- Dry pilling and fiber snags can occur. A soft-bristle brush can help restore the surface’s texture; if you’re dealing with snagging, you might need a professional to tidy the warp and weft without unravelling the weave.
- For pet owners: nails can scratch or snag sisal; keep nails trimmed and consider a rug pad to reduce friction under heavy-use zones. If the family pet turns the rug into an “adventure trail,” you’ll be thankful for the durability but remind yourself to rotate the rug periodically to equalize wear.

### Color care and light exposure
Porcelain, being a pale neutral, can show wear from sun exposure over time. If your space has large windows, you may want to position the rug away from direct sun for hours to minimize color fading. Use window treatments or UV-protective coatings on windows to preserve the color integrity of the rug. As with all light-sensitive textiles, a well-positioned rug can still glow after years of use, but you’ll want to manage expectations regarding ceaseless pristine lighting.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity: Your Bramble Will Thank You
A good rug deserves good habits. Here’s a concise care plan to keep your Bramble looking sharp for years to come.

- Vacuum regularly, but avoid harsh suction that could pull fibers loose. A canister vacuum with a gentle beater bar on low is ideal.
- Address spills promptly. Blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth. For more stubborn stains, consult a professional who handles natural fiber rugs for best results.
- Rotate seasonally. If your sunroom tends to cook in summer sun, move the rug to a less intense spot every few months to reduce color fading and fiber fatigue.
- Use a rug pad. A quality pad prevents slipping, protects floors, and minimizes wear along the most walked routes.
- Professionally clean every 12–24 months (depending on traffic). A professional can refresh the fibers, restore sheen, and extend the rug’s life.
