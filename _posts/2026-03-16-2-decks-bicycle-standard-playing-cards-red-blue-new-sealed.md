## Practical tips for maximizing the two-deck experience
- Rotate decks between sessions: This helps evenly distribute wear and keeps the feel consistent across multiple game nights.
- Use color-coded rules: When teaching, assign red for one player or team and blue for the other. It helps avoid confusion and makes the session look organized even if your scoreboard is chaos in disguise.
- Protect the finish: A small card protector or a dedicated game-table cloth can preserve the stock and finish for longer. Avoid leaving cards on rough surfaces or near pets who consider cards as chew toys.
- Clean up properly: If you spill something sticky, wipe gently with a dry cloth and let the surface dry before resuming play. Card surfaces are not made to be disinfected aggressively, so light cleaning is best to preserve longevity.
- Practice basic shuffles first: Before attempting advanced flourishes, master the fan, the break, and the basic riffle. Then you can experiment with color changes, and a little misdirection, all while staying calm and collected.
