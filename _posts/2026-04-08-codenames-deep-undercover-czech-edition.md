## Strategy depth: tips and tricks for smarter (and funnier) plays
Like any good word game, there’s a sweet spot between risk and reward. Here are some practical tactics to maximize your rounds while preserving camaraderie:

- Build a mental dictionary: During setup, quickly scan the grid for words that seem likely to connect. An early plan helps you decide when to risk a bold clue. 
- Think “umbrella” clues: Clue words that group several target words under a common umbrella can yield big point swings. But beware of trap words that could easily lead your team astray.
- Read your audience: If your team laughs at risqué wordplay, lean into the humor. If someone cringes at innuendo, adapt with cleaner connections and save the spicy ones for later rounds.
- Balance speed and accuracy: The timer creates pressure, but don’t rush to guess. A well-chosen clue with a precise set of words beats a hurried play that opens the door to a catastrophe.
- Use the right amount of risk: In a best-of-n scenario, you’ll want to take some calculated bets to tilt the round in your favor. Don’t be afraid to intentionally miss a few words if it means capturing several more important targets.
