## Design notes: what makes Edouard Guiton stand out
Art direction matters, especially in a line that sits on a shelf next to a stack of plastic miniatures. Edouard tends to emphasize three elements that resonate with tabletop players:

1) Bold silhouettes with clear reading at a distance. When you are staring at a wall from across the room during a late night clutch, you want the characters and zombies to read instantly. Edouard nails that with well-defined edges and high contrast.
2) Color storytelling. The color language is punchy but balanced, often using a limited palette to enforce a mood rather than overwhelm the viewer. It translates well in frames and camera angles for streaming or photo shoots.
3) Narrative in a single frame. Each print seems to tell a micro-story that invites a second look. It is not just a hero posing; there is a moment, a consequence, a hint of what happens next. That cinematic feel elevates a wall piece from decorative to conversation starter.

Collectors who adore micro-narratives in art will recognize the discipline here: the image composition guides your eye through the scene, while the zombies do not simply fill space; they create energy and potential danger for the survivors. The result is a picture that begs to be paired with a game night anecdote.

## Print quality and materials
In the world of collectible prints, the physical spec matters as much as the design. Edouard Guiton prints in this lineup lean toward premium materials and careful finishing. Here is what you typically find:
- Paper stock: acid-free archival-grade stock that holds color for decades without yellowing
- Finish: matte or satin, chosen to minimize glare under room lighting and to provide a clean edge for framing
- Inks: color accurate with a good gamut that captures the vibrancy without oversaturating the scene
- Certificate: a small card or stamp of authenticity that assigns a limited edition number

The attention to detail shows in the print edges—no brittle corners, no misalignment where color meets border, and a consistent bleed that ensures the image goes edge-to-edge when framed properly. If you have ever had the misfortune of a poster printed on cheap stock, you know the value of a well made print. Edouard’s lineup proves that premium materials are not optional accessories; they are part of the art’s personality.

Care tips from the pros are simple: keep prints out of direct sunlight, frame them under UV-protective glass if possible, and store any extras in acid-free sleeves when not on display. The goal is to keep the prints looking as fresh as the first day you opened the package, not as worn as a zombie’s fashion sense after a windy night on the boardwalk.

## How to display and care for your prints
Display choices matter almost as much as the art itself. Here are practical tips to show Edouard Guiton prints off to their best advantage:
- Frame selection: go for archival frames with UV protective glass to preserve color integrity over time
- Lighting: use soft, indirect lighting to avoid hotspots and glare; consider warm white LEDs to bring out the warmth in reds and ambers
- Placement: give the prints breathing room; avoid overcrowding the wall and let the art be the star of the display
- Rotation: if space is limited, rotate a set of prints to keep the display feeling fresh

If you want to take it a step further, create a mini gallery wall that tells a story across multiple pieces. You can pair the Edouard Guiton prints with other Zombicide related art or with snippets of tabletop imagery that capture the mood of your campaigns. The goal is to create a cohesive visual narrative that reminds you why you fell in love with the game in the first place.
