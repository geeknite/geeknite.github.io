### Materials: Poly Rattan and Cushions
Poly rattan fairs well in sunlight and rain, resisting fading and cracking better than traditional wicker. It is lightweight enough to move around yet sturdy enough to hold up to daily use. Cushions in these sets are typically covered in a water resistant fabric with zippable removable covers for cleaning. Expect a density that offers comfortable support without turning you into a beach ball. The cushion color typically stays within the grey palette, giving you a clean, contemporary look that can pair with many other color accents you might throw into the mix.

Maintenance wise, poly rattan requires minimal upkeep. A light wipe down with mild soap and water can keep it looking fresh. Avoid harsh chemicals that could wear down the weave or degrade the frame coating. And if you live in a region with strong sun or heavy rain, consider occasional UV protection checks for the weave just to be safe. A small amount of preventative care goes a long way toward keeping the set looking sharp through seasons of sun and shade.

## Assembly and Setup
If you have a love-hate relationship with flat-pack furniture, this set should play to your love side. Assembly typically involves snapping together the modular pieces, attaching legs, and lining up cushions. Most kits take roughly 60 to 120 minutes depending on your DIY enthusiasm and whether you have a helping hand. A two-person setup helps because you can manage awkward angles, especially with corner units, without cursing at the bolts in unison.

Top tips for a smoother assembly:
- Lay out all pieces and hardware before you start. It sounds obvious, but it reduces the odds of discovering you’re missing a single screwdriver half-way through.
- Use a gentle hand with the screws; you want snug fits, not overtightened bolts that strip the threading.
- Have the cushions nearby but keep them off the frame while you assemble to prevent scuff marks.

## Weather Resistance and Maintenance
Outdoor furniture lives a rugged life: sun, rain, wind, BBQ sauce, and the occasional rainstorm that decides to move in unannounced. Poly rattan fares well against UV exposure and moisture, but it is not a superhero cape. It benefits from regular care and protection during heavy weather seasons.

The cushions are the more delicate component. They are designed to withstand outdoor use, but the covers should be removable for washing. A mild detergent with a gentle cycle works nicely. You want to avoid bleach or strong solvents that can degrade the fabric or fade the color unevenly. A simple rinse and line dry keeps the cushions looking crisp and reduces mold risk in damp climates.

If you anticipate heavy winter weather, consider storing the cushions indoors or using high-quality weatherproof covers. This reduces the need for seasonal washing and extends the life of both the cushions and the weave. In coastal areas, rinsing off salt spray after dry conditions can prevent long-term corrosion on any exposed metal parts.
