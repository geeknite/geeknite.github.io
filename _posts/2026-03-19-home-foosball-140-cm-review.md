## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping It Rolling

Like all good machines, foosball tables deserve some care. A few minutes of maintenance after every few sessions will keep your table performing well for years:

- Wipe down surfaces with a dry or lightly damp cloth to remove fingerprints and sticky fingers after late-night snacks.
- Lubricate rods periodically with a light machine oil or the product recommended by the manufacturer. Don’t overdo it; you’re not oiling a vintage motorcycle.
- Check the screws and bolts for looseness. A loose screw is an annoying squeak that becomes every game-night villain.
- Inspect ball return paths for obstructions. Crumbs, pens, or stray game-night snacks can derail a perfect volley.
- Keep children’s fingers away from the ball return machinery during setup. It’s a minor hazard, but you’ll thank me later when you’re not fishing a tiny ball out of the coffee mug again.

Maintenance means longevity. A 140 cm table that’s well-kept can easily outlive a couple of living-room furniture cycles, and it will still be punchy enough to host your next family tournament.
