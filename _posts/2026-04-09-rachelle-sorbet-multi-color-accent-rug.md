## Maintenance and day-to-day care
### Cleaning basics
The practical virtues of the Sorbet rug revolve around easy maintenance. Vacuum regularly on a low setting to avoid pulling fibers, especially in high-traffic zones where the pile can pick up dust and pet hair. For spills, act quickly: blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb as much liquid as possible. Mild detergent mixed with water can handle most common stains; test a small, inconspicuous patch first to ensure color stability. Avoid harsh chemicals that could affect the color balance—this rug isn’t a chemistry experiment; it’s a party of color, not a lab accident.

For more stubborn stains, consider a professional cleaning every 12-18 months depending on wear. If you have pets, a quick weekly flick of the vacuum can prevent claw reveals in the pile, though the density of the weave usually makes this rug fare well against light pet traffic written in the stars and not on the warranty documents.

### Care tips to keep colors vibrant
- Rotate the rug every few months to prevent uneven fading in areas that receive more sunlight.
- Use sun-blocking window treatments or UV-protective films to slow color shift.
- Consider a rug pad to extend life and reduce wear on high-traffic zones, which also helps with noise dampening in multi-use rooms.
- If you live in a bright environment with lots of window light, monitor color consistency across seasons; while the Sorbet is designed for visual balance, short-term color variation is normal with natural light.
