## Setup and quick rules refresher

For those who haven’t played Exploding Kittens before, the core concept is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten unless you can Defuse it. The Defuse cards are your only safety net, and they’re precious, since you’ll want to hold onto them for when fate decides you’re the star of a cat-bomb demonstration.

In the two-pack setup, you’ll duplicate each deck’s core components and shuffle the two piles together, unless you prefer to keep them separate for a more segmented experience. The rules are largely the same, with a few practical tweaks to keep play smooth across two decks:

- Shuffle both decks and deal a set number of cards to each player depending on player count (typical ranges are 4-7 cards to start). If you’re using two decks, you may reduce the initial hand size slightly to maintain tension.
- Remove additional Exploding Kitten cards to adapt to your player count if you’ve added more participants beyond the recommended 2-5. The exact number isn’t critical, but you want to avoid an instant meltdown where everyone dies on the first draw.
- Defuse cards behave identically across decks; if you draw an Exploding Kitten and you have a Defuse, you should be able to play it and continue, which is basically a small victory dance for the table.
- Card effects stack in interesting ways. Attack cards force the next player to take multiple turns in a row, which becomes hilariously chaotic when the deck is doubled and the tension multiplies in slow-motion.
