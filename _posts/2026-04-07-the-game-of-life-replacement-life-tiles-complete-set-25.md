## Storage, Care, and Longevity
Good storage is the unsung hero of every board-game component. Replacement LIFE Tiles benefit from thoughtful storage because it reduces the chances of bending, warping, or misplacing them during the next session. Here are some practical tips:

- Keep tiles in their own tray: Use a shallow compartment or a dedicated tile tray to prevent sliding and friction. The goal is to limit movement during transportation, which reduces edge wear and keeps printing legible.
- Consider protective sleeves for the tile area: Some players sleeve the tiles similarly to cards, particularly if their tableboard is frequently shuffled or stacked with multiple sets. This keeps the print crisp and reduces the chance of smudges.
- Store in a cool, dry place: You don’t need a climate-controlled vault, but extremes of humidity or heat can warp cardboard or laminate finishes over time. A simple closet shelf or gaming cabinet works perfectly.
