Official dimensions place the pod at about 6.5 x 4.5 x 7.2 ft (L x W x H). It seats up to four people for a casual chat, but in practice three is the sweet spot for conversation and not knocking elbows on the backlog.

## Assembly and Setup
Two humans can assemble the pod in under an hour with a snack break. The hardware is labeled well, the instructions are clear enough to avoid a meltdown, and the bag of screws is sprinkled with color-coded parts to prevent mixing a panel with a chair back. The floor mounting kit is robust, designed to handle office traffic and occasional wheel misadventures from office chairs.

## Interior and Ergonomics
Inside, the pod uses dense acoustic foam panels and a ceiling panel. The seating is modular and adjustable to fit different team layouts. There is a pocket for chargers and cables to avoid cable spaghetti. The LED lighting is adjustable, and the ventilation is quiet enough to ignore in everyday use.

## Acoustic Performance
The core selling point is the acoustic performance. The double-layer walls and internal foam matrix reduce ambient noise dramatically. In our tests, the pod cut down office chatter by a meaningful margin, making calls and focused work far more pleasant.

## Connectivity and Features
Power and data are essential. The built-in power module provides outlets and USB-C charging. The optional Bluetooth speaker with mic passthrough can integrate with corporate comms apps. Lighting can switch color temperatures for different times of day, and ventilation can be controlled to keep things comfortable.
