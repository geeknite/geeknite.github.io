## Introduction
City of Mist fans, assemble. Tonight we dive into a mysterious bundle that promises to turn your roleplaying nights into a living urban myth: the Fortune Row Dice Set in its Deluxe Edition. You know the drill. City of Mist is all about archetypes, noir shadows, and a detective's love affair with a good rulebook. ## Unboxing and Presentation
Fortune Row comes in a sturdy presentation box that whispers of leather-bound notebooks and late-night stakeouts. The outer sleeve features foil stamping and a city skyline silhouette that would make Batman feel underdressed. Open it, and you find a snug foam insert that holds the dice, a small velvet pouch, and a little instruction card that tells you how to use the dice in the City of Mist dice mechanic. The Deluxe Edition part shows up here with flair: a metal tin for rolling, a leather-like dice tray, and a magnetic clasp that suggests the box can double as a prop in your next urban thriller campaign.

- Components overview
Inside the tin you get:
1. A set of seven dice in a smoky resin, tinted to evoke fog over a neon-lit alley.
2. A leather dice tray with the City of Mist emblem embossed on it.
3. A card deck with quick prompts to help you spin tales when your players stall at a crossroads.
4. A fold-out map that hints at the Fortune Row district, a fictional area brimming with neon myths and shady probabilities.
5. A microfiber cloth to buff the glaze on your dice and keep your dice from gathering rogue fog.

### Aesthetics and Build Quality
The dice themselves are the star here. Each die has a crisp edge, a satisfying weight, and a glaze that catches light like a streetlamp in a rainstorm. The seven dice include standard colors for City of Mist roles but with a twist: the numbering is clean and the artistry on the face designs is subtle enough to look sleek on a table, yet legible enough to be read by a half-awake GM at 3 am.

- Color palette
The hues range from deep indigo to smoky gray, with flecks of emerald that pop under certain angles. The effect is deliberate: you want this dice set to feel like you found a crate behind an abandoned club in the Fortune Row district, not something you found in a clearance sale at a big-box store. The color scheme is cohesive, which matters when you roll a critical and the group glances at the dice as if to ask permission to believe in magic.

- Glyphs and marks
A common complaint with kits like this is that the faces look neat but turn out to be hard to read during a tense scene. Fortune Row avoids this trap with clean numeral font and discreet glyphs that nod to the mystery of City of Mist. The glyphs are etched, not painted, which means they tend to wear gracefully with time rather than chip or flake off. The effect is elegant rather than garish, which is essential when you play a game about legends living in the shadows.

- But is it functional?
Yes, the dice roll true. The distribution of results is fair, and the edges are consistent across all seven dice. The resin has a comfortable grip; it’s not sticky or slick, which means in a dimly lit cafe you can still feel your roll in your palm like a slow heartbeat. One caveat: the semi-translucent property can reveal the cores of some dice if you hold them up to light in a window; a nerdy detail that will delight some players and annoy others who fear the existence of a rogue spacer die hiding in the set.

## How It Plays in City of Mist
City of Mist is not just about rolling dice, it is about rolling into a mythic city where you are half myth and half person. The Fortune Row dice set is designed to complement that vibe. It doesn’t try to replace the standard d6 approach used in City of Mist; it augments it, giving players a tactile sense of the mythic metropolis. The numbers are clean, but the real value is in the way the dice feel and the way the color tells a story before you even read the result.

- Mechanics alignment
In City of Mist you are the myth, the urban legend, and the detective pinging on a forgotten crime. The Fortune Row dice set is designed to be used with the core mechanic in a way that doesn’t disrupt the flow. You can imagine a dice pool being built with the seven dice to represent a crew of personas stepping forward as you navigate a tense scene.

- The mood booster
There is something about rolling a pair of dice in a fancy tin that makes you feel like you are about to uncover a truth that the city would rather keep hidden. The Deluxe Edition adds a sense of ceremony: you lay out the dice, you admire the finish, and the moment of rolling becomes a small ritual that signals the start of your next dramatic reveal.

- Interaction with the Fortune Row setting
Fortune Row as a district in City of Mist is all about secrets that twist the night into something you would want to write on a napkin in a coffee shop at 2 am. The dice set’s aesthetic echoes that with a smoky translucence and a neon glow that would not feel out of place in a comic panel. If your table is into lore and world-building, the dice set doubles as a prop, giving players something tangible to anchor the city in their minds.

## In-Play Experience
Rolling dice mid-game in a noir fantasy setting can be a lot of fun when the dice feel like they belong to the world. Fortune Row Deluxe Edition makes you want to roll the dice more often, which is both good and dangerous. The dice roll with a satisfying click on each hit, and you can tell when a dice has a bit more weight behind it—the kind of weight that makes you feel as if the city itself is leaning in to hear your plan.

- Feel and handling
The dice have a nice uniform weight; they don’t feel cheap, and you won’t need to worry about dice that chip after a few sessions. The edges are precisely rounded, which means that even with sweaty palms the dice still glide out smoothly after a spin. If you are a person who likes to cup the dice in your hands for a moment before rolling, this set rewards that ritual.

- The tray and storage experience
During a long session, you want a place to rest your dice that doesn’t feel like a stage prop you found in a thrift store. The Deluxe Edition tray feels premium; it is sturdy and the lid closes with a soft magnetic snap that gives a sense of this is mine now. It’s not just storage; it’s a character moment for your table.

- Player feedback
My group had a mixed reaction to the seven-die setup. Some players loved the variety and the sense that each die represented a different facet of a persona. Others found the seven-die pool a bit much for early sessions and suggested starting with a subset. The good news is that the set is versatile enough to adapt: you can use a subset of dice to represent a small crew or escalate the pool as players gain new personas.

## Longevity and Maintenance
A weaponized set of dice is only as good as its maintenance routine. Fortune Row Deluxe Edition holds up well under regular use, but there are a few care tips that help prolong its life and preserve its mythic aura.

- Wear and tear
Expect minor cosmetic wear on the glyphs after heavy weekly sessions. The etched glyphs are resilient, but like any artful accessory, there is a small risk of fading if you are particularly merciless with your rolling session and your storage routine.

- Cleaning and storage tips
Keep the dice in the included tray when not in use. Use a microfiber cloth to wipe fingerprints and light grime. Avoid exposing the dice to extreme heat, which can affect the resin and cause warping in rare cases. If you store the dice in a cool, dry place, you will keep the glow that makes them feel like you just discovered a neon bottle in a backroom of Fortune Row.
