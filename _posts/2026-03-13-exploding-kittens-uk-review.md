## How to play (the quick and the quirky)
If you want a terse version that fits into a busy schedule, here is the gist: you take turns drawing cards, playing action cards when you can, and trying not to draw an exploding kitten. The Defuse card buys you another round of life, your friends think you are a chess genius because you used a See the Future card to avoid disaster, and the last person standing is crowned with a smug grin and perhaps a sarcastic trophy made from a paper cup.

### Setup
- Each player gets a starting hand of seven cards (in most playthroughs).
- Shuffle the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck, with a few Defuse cards mixed in per player guidelines. The number of Defuse cards differs depending on how many players you have, but the balance is designed to keep the game fast and fair.
- The rest of the deck goes face down; the top card is flipped to start the discard pile. The tension begins now.

### Turn flow
On a player’s turn, you may do several possible actions using your cards. Most importantly, you will decide whether to draw from the deck or use an action card to shift the odds. If you draw and land on an Exploding Kitten, you must either defuse it or exit the round. If you defuse it, the kitten goes to your discard pile and you reshuffle the deck, keeping the suspense alive for everyone else. The see the future cards let you peek, the attack card can force other players to take multiple turns, and the skip card lets you dodge a hand where the odds feel unfriendly. The balance is light on strategy and heavy on luck, which is exactly the vibe most families want when a game night arrives with pizza in the air.

### Endgame and victory conditions
The game ends when the last Exploding Kitten is drawn or otherwise removed from circulation, and the remaining players count up their status. In practice, this means the table erupts into a chorus of cheers, groans, and maybe a discussion about who should have used a certain Defuse card earlier. The winner is the player who survives the final explosion without losing their nerve, or more precisely, the last person able to play a card to stave off a catastrophic reveal. It is simple on paper and delightfully chaotic in person.

## Strategy tips for both kids and grownups
While Exploding Kittens looks silly, there is a method to the madness. Here are some practical strategies you can actually use at a family game night without turning it into a calculus seminar:

- Defuse sparingly, use wisely: The Defuse cards are currency in the survival market. Don’t blow all your Defuses on early rounds unless you are sure the odds are in your favor. Save at least one for a dramatic late run when the table starts to feel like a thriller and everyone is counting you as the most likely to explode.
- See the Future for safety planning: If you have a See the Future card, peek and plan. Not every peek should become a play, but a well-timed reveal can save you and force others into awkward choices about whether to risk a draw.
- Attack and skip for tempo control: Attack cards can force others to draw more often than they want, which is delicious chaos when the table is already on edge. Skip cards let you dodge a fate you do not deserve. The trick is not to overplay; immediate doom can ruin a night faster than a burnt pizza.
- Cat cards as social leverage: Cat cards are mostly there for humor and momentum. If you collect too many, you become a moving target for jokes. Use them to nudge conversations, not just to cause a pile of laughter. A well-timed cat moment can derail a serious strategy and turn the table into a giggle factory.

Pro tip: The UK crowd tends to appreciate light sarcasm and quick banter. So while playing, sprinkle your turns with light jokes about the weather or the merits of tea over coffee. It keeps the mood buoyant and prevents anyone from getting too salty when fate slaps them with an Exploding Kitten.
