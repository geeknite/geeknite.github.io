## Size, Fit, and Layout: Getting the Geometry Right
One of the biggest debates in rug shopping is size. The Kendall Oatmeal Braided Rug comes in a handful of standard dimensions: 4x6, 6x9, and 8x10. Each size has its own role in the canvas of a room. A 4x6 is perfect for under a coffee table or at the foot of a bed; it creates a focal area without dominating the space. Placement is as important as size. For example, in a living room with a sofa and loveseat, you’ll want the rug to anchor the seating group. The front legs of the sofa and chairs should be on the rug to unify the arrangement; otherwise, you’ll end up with a floating carpet that looks nice but doesn’t feel integrated. The Kendall’s braided edges make it easy to align with furniture lines, and the color helps disguise slight misalignment that can occur during initial setup. If your room has an awkward alcove or a non-standard layout, consider a larger rug to create an intentional border around the seating area, or pair two smaller rugs with a careful gap to separate zones without creating an island effect.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh Without Losing Your Mind
Maintenance is where a rug earns or loses points with everyday users. The Kendall Oatmeal Braided Rug is relatively low-maintenance. Regular vacuuming on a low pile setting helps keep the fibers from compressing and prevents dirt from embedding. A quick weekly pass should be enough to maintain the plush look; more frequent vacuuming may be necessary in households with heavy foot traffic, a crunchy crew of kids, or a furry family member who sheds in more colors than a rainbow machine.

Spills should be addressed promptly. Blot, do not rub, to avoid pushing the stain deeper into the fibers. For most common spills like coffee, juice, or a splash of sauce, a light dab with a clean cloth and a bit of plain water is sufficient. For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution is acceptable; always test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Avoid bleach or harsh chemicals that could fade the oatmeal hue or damage the fibers.

If you’re dealing with pet odors, a quick spray with a pet-safe neutralizer and a thorough airing-out can help. Additionally, rotating the rug every few months helps distribute wear evenly. If you’re moving the rug or storing it for a season, roll it rather than folding to prevent creases that may set in and impact the appearance once you unfurl it again.

Sustainability varies by the manufacturer’s sourcing and processing practices. The Kendall’s blend is designed to be durable and stain-resistant, which reduces waste through longer product life.
