## Quick Refresher: How CODENAMES Works (If you somehow forgot after your last 12 game nights)

CODENAMES pits two teams against each other. One player from each team is the spymaster; the rest are field operatives trying to find their words on a 5x5 grid of cards. The spymaster gives one-word clues followed by a number that indicates how many cards on the grid relate to the clue. The catch: you must avoid the assassin word. If any team guesses the assassin card, they lose immediately. If a teammate guesses a card belonging to the other team, control passes after that guess. A round ends when all of one team’s words are found or the assassin has sabotaged the plan.

Why does this work so well in the CGE edition, aside from the obvious wordplay potential? Because the game rewards concise, clever clueing and dramatic tension. It’s a social game that’s not afraid to lean into goofy humor or strategic depth, depending on the group. The new box won’t suddenly turn a dull table into word geniuses, but it does make the experience a little smoother and more satisfying to execute.

## The Box Experience: Setup, First Turn, and Flow
