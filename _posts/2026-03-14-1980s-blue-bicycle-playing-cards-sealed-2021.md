### Authenticity and how to spot a faithful reproduction
The world of collectible card decks is a curious blend of nostalgia and modern manufacturing. If you’re chasing the true 1980s blue Bicycle look, here are a few signposts:
- Back color should be a deep, slightly desaturated blue with a subtle gloss under a bright light.
- The Bicycle shield back should be crisp, with clean lines and no faint misalignment where the pattern repeats.
- Face cards should reflect the classic 1980s Bicycle typography and coloring, with the proper heart, spade, club, and diamond symbols.
- The tuck box should reproduce the logos and legal copy you’d expect from an official Bicycle product of that era, not a modern reinterpretation.

If you’re shopping, one reliable tactic is comparing close-up photos of the seal and the tuck. A genuine sealed deck will show a consistent seam along the top lid and a clean, crisp notch on the seal. If the seal looks suspiciously pristine or exhibits odd labeling, you may be looking at a reproduction.
