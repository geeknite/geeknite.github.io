## Maintenance and Cleaning

Maintenance is where most rugs either shine or crumble under the weight of your life. The Avenue rug is designed to be forgiving. Regular vacuuming with a standard vacuum head works well, and you can opt for a rug pad beneath to improve comfort and prevent creeping. For spills, the quick response matters more than the fancy cleaner you own. Blot the spill with a clean cloth, work from the outside in to avoid spreading, and consider a mild detergent if the stain lingers. Do not rub aggressively; you are not polishing a gemstone, you are coaxing a fiber to behave.

If you have pets, you will appreciate the color fastness of the palette. Darker accents hide pet hair and dust more gracefully than pale tones, and the subtle texture helps disguise minor footprints that accumulate after a long workday. For deeper cleans, you can schedule a professional cleaning if the rug requires it, but typical maintenance is well within the reach of a weekend warrior with a vacuum and a bottle of all-purpose cleaner.

### Rug padding and placement

A note on pads: a good rug pad under this size makes a world of difference. It prevents slipping on hard floors, adds a touch more cushioning, and helps extend the rug’s life by reducing friction against the floor. Select a pad that matches or slightly undercuts the rug dimensions so you get even coverage without edge curling. If you’re placing the rug under dining furniture, consider a pad that can absorb some of the movement induced by chair legs. It is not a gadget; it is a safety feature that helps keep your chair wheels from becoming miniature bulldozers.

## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity Tips

- Vacuum weekly to keep the pile looking sharp. Don’t use a brush with a hard edge; a standard vacuum with a soft brush will do.
- Blot stains immediately. Do not rub as that drives the stain deeper into the fibers.
- For stubborn stains, a solution of mild detergent and water can be used. Test a small inconspicuous area first.
- Protect high traffic zones with furniture coasters to minimize indentations that can occur under heavy chairs.
- Consider rotating the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color exposure if your room gets a lot of sunlight.

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it is the adult version of keeping a virtual pet alive. You feed it, you groom it, you vacuum it, and in return it elevates your living space from acceptable to wow enough to make your in-laws nod approvingly.
