## Installation notes and safety considerations
Like any piece of vehicle hardware, proper installation is essential. Here are practical tips to minimize headaches:
- Use the correct mounting plate and ensure you have solid attachment points. A loose mount is not just a failure mode; it’s a potential safety hazard.
- Align the rope line with the fairlead to prevent rope damage. Misalignment can cause abnormal rope wear, leading to a shorter rope life.
- Set proper winching angles. If you’re pulling from an awkward angle, use a suitable anchor point to avoid bending forces that could overload the drum or the mount.
- Regular maintenance: rinse and inspect after each tough recovery, especially if you’ve been through mud, water, or salt spray. Dry off the unit and check for any visible wear on the rope and fairlead.
- Safety first: stand clear of the line’s path when the winch is in operation. A misbehaving rope or a sudden snag can snap back and cause injuries. No dramatic close-ups while you’re filming the exact moment you lose your dignity—trust us, we’ve learned this one the hard way.
