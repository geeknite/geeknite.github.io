### Setup and rules in five minutes (or less)
UNO is famously approachable, and the Tin version nails the onboarding curve. Here’s the quick gist:

- Shuffle the deck inside the tin. If you’re feeling fancy, you can rate the shuffling on a scale of 1 to “I can’t believe this is happening.”
- Deal seven cards to each player. The rest becomes a draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Players take turns matching the top card by either number or color. If you can’t match, you draw a card. If that card can be played, you can chain it—sometimes—and chaos ensues.
- Action cards shake up the flow: Skip makes the next player miss a turn, Reverse flips the direction, Draw Two forces the next player to draw two, Wild changes the color in play, and Wild Draw Four adds four to the next player’s misery while you pick a color to continue the chaos.
- When a player has one card left, they must yell Uno. Silence is golden, unless you’re playing with a rules-light group where someone forgets—then chaos unfolds.

For those who want a little extra spice, the rule sheet includes optional house rules to tailor the game to your group. You can add a “Stacking Draw” rule for nail-biting comebacks or a “Crazy Colors” variant where colors rotate in a manic rhythm. House rules are the spice rack of UNO; use them judiciously, or your group will devolve into a color-based allegory for office gossip.
