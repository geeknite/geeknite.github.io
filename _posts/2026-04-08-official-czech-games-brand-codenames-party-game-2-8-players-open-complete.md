## How to play in a nutshell
If you are coming to Codenames Party from a different part of the gaming multiverse, here is a quick primer to get you up to speed. The game is built on the same skeleton as the base Codenames and adds more players to the table, but not more rules to memorize.

- The host shuffles the word cards and lays out a 5 by 5 grid of words on the table.
- Each team has a spymaster who sees the key card and knows which words belong to their color, which words are neutral, and which word is the assassin.
- The spymaster gives a one-word clue followed by a number indicating how many words on the grid relate to that clue.
- The team discusses and points to words on the grid to make their guesses.
- Guesses must be limited to one word per clue, with up to the number indicated on the clue. If a word guessed belongs to your team, that card is covered with your color. If you guess an opponent word, your turn ends and the opponent takes the card. If you reveal a neutral word, nothing else happens except you wasted a guess. If you reveal the assassin, the game ends immediately in favor of the other team.
- Play continues with alternate turns until one team anchors five words in a standard 5x5 grid or in a variant you might be running.

For the party edition, there is more chatter, more back-and-forth, and more opportunities for a wild misinterpretation that ultimately proves to be the most entertaining moment of the night. It is why we keep returning to this format with new groups who think they can outplay the table banter.

## House rules and variants for longer nights
A common practice in Geeknite circles is to spin up house rules that add a little extra spice to the gameplay. Here are a few ideas that work well with Codenames Party open complete edition:

- Timed rounds: Give each team a strict time limit per clue to accelerate the pace and keep folks from overthinking. A 60-second timer can generate intense but hilarious pressure.
- Double-trick clues: Allow spymasters to give two one-word clues per turn but with a higher risk. If a team guesses a wrong word, the turn ends immediately as a penalty for over-ambition.
- Thematic weekends: Pick a theme such as retro tech, fantasy realms, or famous movie quotes, and curate the word deck to favor words that fit the theme. It adds coherence and laughs as players hunt for thematically linked guesses.
- Spectator mode: For larger groups, designate a panel of non-active players to shout out alternative clues after the primary clue is given. The spymaster can choose to accept or reject the panel’s suggestions, a dynamic that invites playful debates and keeps everyone engaged.
