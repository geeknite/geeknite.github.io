### Care and Maintenance
Cotton rugs require a light touch for maintenance. Regular vacuuming on a low setting helps prevent dirt from embedding in the fibers. It is best to avoid high suction on a delicate weave to prevent stretching or pulling at the edges. If you spill something, address it quickly with a blotting technique rather than rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fiber. For tougher spots, a mild detergent solution and a soft cloth can do the trick, followed by a gentle rinse and thorough air drying.

Rotating the rug every few months can distribute wear more evenly, especially in high traffic zones. If you have pets, a light brushing of fur with a soft brush can keep the surface looking fresh rather than matted. Do not soak the rug in water for extended periods; cotton can retain moisture, which may lead to mildew if no adequate drying is possible. For deeper maintenance, consider professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months depending on usage.

### Care Tips for Longevity
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting; avoid beater bars that can fray the fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing which can push stains deeper.
- Rotate every few months to promote even wear across the surface.
- Treat stains with a mild soap solution and rinse lightly, then air dry completely before placing back on the floor.
- Use a rug pad to minimize slipping and to extend the rug life by reducing friction and wear at contact points.
