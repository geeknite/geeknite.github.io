## How to use the cards in gameplay
### Quick setup tips
- Shuffle the ten two-sided cards thoroughly and place them within easy reach for both teams. The faces should be accessible so you can flip a card between rounds if you decide to remix the clues mid-game.
- Establish a quick ritual for flipping sides between rounds. A simple flip after each successful turn keeps the energy high and reduces the temptation to memorize the same prompts round after round.
- Decide in advance which sides to use for a given round. If you are playing a tournament bracket or a casual ladder night, you can designate certain sides as the main track and others as the wildcard track to keep the pace lively.

### Strategic implications
Two-sided cards inject a level of variability that can reward flexible clue-giving. If the sides differ enough in their hinting direction, you can tailor your clues to the current on-table vocabulary, which keeps players engaged rather than drifting toward a single dominant strategy. This can be especially helpful for mixed-age groups or players with uneven experience, because the dual prompts reduce the cognitive gap between seasoned players and newcomers.

However, with more options comes more potential for misinterpretation. A clue that seems crystal clear to you may feel obtuse to your partner, particularly when the two sides emphasize different semantic angles. As with any promo pack, a light touch and good communication with your teammates will save you from the dreaded blank stares and mid-round debates about whether the clue was too risky or perfectly safe in a specific context.
