### Setup and learning curve
Setup is straightforward for a gamer who has dealt with medium-heavy euro-lair experiences before. You will lay out the board, place the pork-on-a-plate tokens, organize the talismans, and decide which scenario you want to tackle. The Deluxe Version does a nice job at offering a gentle ramp: the first scenario is a friendly introduction with a few hints for new players, while the later chapters throw a gauntlet of undead politics that requires you to coordinate with teammates without tipping your hand too early.

Learning the rules feels like reading a well-rendered graphic novel rather than memorizing a spreadsheet. There are terms that matter, like blood pool management, ritual completion, and zhong kui style defense lines, but the guide does a good job of mapping these to actions you can physically perform on your turn. The inclusion of a detailed glossary and quick-reference sheets helps you avoid the classic mistake of misreading a card and accidentally starting a boss fight before you have prepared your tea and snacks.

### Gameplay loop and pacing
The core loop blends worker-placement with set-collection and light hidden-information moments. You assign your agents to different banquet rooms to perform actions: recruit a Jiangshi, gather blood or ritual items, recruit allies, or set traps for the banquet guests who are not exactly who they appear to be. The game scales across players by adjusting the number of rooms and enemies that spawn per round, keeping it tense but manageable for the table size you have chosen.

Pacing is where the Deluxe Version shines and sometimes bites back. The mechanical tempo can feel like a symphony conducted by a timer on a dish drier. When you hit the middle stage of a scenario, you get a flow where all players are engaged, but you also feel the pressure to optimize your choices. This is not a game that rewards pure chaos vacuum; you will want to plan your turns a couple of steps ahead, and the extra components in the Deluxe Version help you visualize those plans with nice tactile feedback.
