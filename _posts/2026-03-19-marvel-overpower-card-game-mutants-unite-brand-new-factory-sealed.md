## Care, storage, and long-term preservation

If you’ve decided you’ll actually keep the item sealed for a while (which is a perfectly reasonable choice in a world where your shelves are basically real estate for memories), here are a few practical tips:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV light can degrade color-sensitive artwork over time.
- Keep the deck in its original shelf or a protective outer box to minimize edge wear even within the shrink wrap.
- If you do eventually open the deck for play, consider investing in standard card sleeves and a protective binder to maintain card integrity.
- Humidity control helps: a steady humidity around 40-50% is generally safe for paper-based ephemera like this.
- Avoid magnetic card storage or anything that might chemically interact with card coatings.

For those looking to display the deck, consider a display case with a neutral climate and avoid stacking heavy objects on top of the sealed package. It’s a quiet, nerdy form of armor for your youth’s most cherished gaming relics.
