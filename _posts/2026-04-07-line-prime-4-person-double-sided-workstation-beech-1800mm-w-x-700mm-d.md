## Assembly experience
### How hard is it to assemble, really?
As with many multi-person desks, Line Prime benefits from some planning. Our setup involved four teammates: two at each end, passing parts along like a relay team. The assembly manual is straightforward, with numbered steps guiding you through attaching leg frames to the central spine, aligning top panels, and tidying the underside with the cable trays.

Two people handling the base and two assembling the top panels is the recommended approach. The panels are large enough to require careful alignment, but not so heavy that you’d need a forklift. The alignment pins help with stacking top panels, making the process smoother than expected. If you’ve built flat-pack furniture before, you’ll recognize the familiar rhythm of bolts, washers, and the occasional “where does this spare clip go again?” moment.

### Time investment and caveats
Expect roughly 60 to 90 minutes for a two-person setup, and a couple of hours if you’re going full team-mode with four people and a few breaks for coffee. The size can make you clear some space in the room, demote a few items from the desk and assign a helper to pass tools. The documentation is helpful, and the hardware is standard metric fare, with locking nuts that keep things from wobbling during a productive meeting that turns into a group stretch break.

## Ergonomics and comfort for four
The tricky part of any shared desk is ergonomics. Line Prime addresses comfort by offering a broad surface that lets four people spread out their gear without feeling pinched. But ergonomics aren’t magic; they rely on chair choice, monitor height, and seating posture. Here are some practical guidelines to maximize comfort:

- Align height for all users: With four users, chairs will vary. Favor chairs with strong height adjustment and monitor stands to maintain a neutral neck position.
- Depth for monitor placement: The 700 mm depth is ample for standard dual-monitor setups plus a mug warmer. Aim for a comfortable line of sight with the top of the monitors at or slightly below eye level.
- Space for collaboration without chaos: The double-sided layout encourages discussion, but be mindful of elbows bumping during quick stand-ups. Consider slightly angled monitor arms or inward-facing stands to reduce unintentional contact.

The finish and uniform height across the surface contribute to a comfortable baseline. It’s unrealistic to expect a miracle ergonomic revolution from a single desk, but the Line Prime sets up a solid canvas for good posture to flourish across a four-person team.

## Build material, finish, and maintenance
The beech veneer finish delivers a warm, professional look that pairs well with modern office decor. It’s not solid wood, but it’s engineered for durability with a natural aesthetic.
