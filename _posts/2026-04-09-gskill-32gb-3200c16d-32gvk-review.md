## Memory tuning and DOCP guidance

If you want to minimize the fiddling and maximize the chance of success, follow these general steps for a smooth ride with the F4-3200C16D-32GVK kit:

1) Install the modules in the recommended slots on your motherboard for dual channel operation. 
2) Enter the BIOS and locate the memory profile section. Enable XMP if you are on Intel or DOCP if you are on AMD. 
3) Select the 3200 CL16 profile and apply. The system will reboot and test stability. 
4) If stability issues arise, reduce to a lower speed around 3000 or 2933 with CL16 or CL18 and then test stability again. 
5) If you want to push a bit further, small voltage nudges up to 1.35 V are generally safe on many boards, but monitor system stability with a memory stress test. If you encounter boot loops, revert to the default profile and lower the speed. 
6) Always keep your system temperatures in check and do not push voltage beyond recommended safe ranges. 

DOCP profiles simplify the process for AMD boards but a note of caution remains: every motherboard design is different, and some boards may not perfectly align with the rated profile. If you encounter instability, try enabling a slightly looser timing like CL18 or bump a little slower speed to 3066 or 3200 with a more forgiving timing. Patience and small adjustments are the name of the game here.
