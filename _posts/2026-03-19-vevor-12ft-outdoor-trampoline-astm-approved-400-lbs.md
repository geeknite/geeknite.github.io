Maintenance and care
---------------------
- Cleaning: Wipe down the mat and frame with mild soap and water. Dry completely to prevent rust on hidden seams.
- Inspect regularly: Check springs for rust or loss of tension. Replace worn springs promptly; it’s cheaper than a chiropractor, though not as dramatic.
- Cover when not in use: A cover helps keep dirt off the mat and reduces sun damage to the surface.
- Storage: If you plan to mothball it for winter, disassemble and store parts in a dry place to prevent rust and to keep neighborhood squirrels away from your hardware stash.

Safety culture and responsible use
----------------------------------
The 12FT Trampoline is a fantastic backyard gadget, but it’s not a toy for reckless experimentation. Treat it with a mix of curiosity and caution. Here are some best practices:
- Supervision: Kids should always be supervised by a responsible adult who can perform a quick safety check before a bounce session.
- One jumper at a time when attempting tricks: This reduces the risk of collisions and mid-air entanglements. Friends can line up for the next jump and heckle with comedic timing instead of a pile-up.
- No flips without training: Aerial maneuvers require skill and practice. Start with safe, basic bounces and progress gradually.
- Clutter-free zone: Keep toys, pets, and garden furniture away from the trampoline area to prevent unexpected collisions.
- Helmet and protective gear: Not strictly required, but if you’ve got a daredevil tween who insists on trying backward flips on the first day, a helmet might be a good idea.
