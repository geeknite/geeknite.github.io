Maintenance and care: keeping the vibe without turning it into a chore
A common question about handmade woven rugs is maintenance. The good news is that this rug typically responds well to routine care. A simple vacuum on a gentle setting, done regularly, helps prevent the deeper fibers from compacting and collecting dust. The solid color helps hide minor mishaps—think a stray pet hair tumble or a wandering crumb from a snack attack while binge-watching the latest streaming fantasy—but you’ll want to address spills promptly. The best practice is to blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing (which can push the stain deeper), and treat with a mild cleaner appropriate for the fiber blend. For more stubborn stains, you might consider professionals who specialize in wool or cotton care rather than a DIY cure that could turn your rug into a science experiment. If your space is prone to high traffic, consider rotating the rug periodically to ensure even wear and to keep the pile from flattening in the same corners.

Care and maintenance expanded: practical tips for real homes
To ensure your rug maintains its appeal, consider these practical steps:
- Vacuum weekly with a brush attachment, on a low setting to avoid pulling on fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and to add underfoot comfort; this is particularly important if the backing is not inherently non-slip.
- Address spills promptly; blot rather than rub, and treat stains with recommended cleaners suitable for the fiber blend.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12–24 months, depending on traffic and household type.

Durability and long-term expectations: does handmade equal fragile?
Durability depends on the fiber blend and weave density. Higher-density weaves generally hold up better in busy spaces. Maintaining color integrity over time: colorfastness and sunlight
Solid color dyes can fade if exposed to direct sunlight for long periods. If your rug sits in a bright window, rotate exposure by occasionally moving furniture or using sheer curtains to reduce direct sun. A well-made solid color rug tends to show fading uniformly rather than patchily, which helps preserve the intended aesthetic rather than turning the rug into a blotchy souvenir from a sun-drenched vacation. If you’re concerned about color shift, test a small inconspicuous area with a mild cleaner and observe for a few weeks before committing to a longer-term color plan.
