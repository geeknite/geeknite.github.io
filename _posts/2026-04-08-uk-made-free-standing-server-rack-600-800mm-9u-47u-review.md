## Airflow, cooling, and efficiency
Airflow is the silent killer of IT gear. The moment you suffocate a switch with poor cabling or block a front-to-back airflow path, you pay in latency and heat. This rack design addresses airflow with:

- Perforated front doors (for intake) and properly vented rear sections to allow heat to escape without sticking around like a bad rumor.
- The internal spacing and venting are designed to support front-to-back cooling, a standard in most enterprise deployments. If you’re into hot-aisle/cold-aisle configurations, this cabinet should slot into your plan with minor adjustments.
- Cable management features that don’t block the airflow. The rear cable channels are a small but important detail—they keep cables out of the main airflow path, reducing turbulence that would otherwise lead to hotspots behind the rack.

For labs and home deployments where you’ve got a handful of devices with different thermal profiles, this rack’s approach to airflow is a solid starting point.
