## What is Herd Mentality, Exactly? (A Refresher for the Herd)
Herd Mentality is a party game designed around group dynamics and social guessing. Each round revolves around a prompt—quick-fire scenarios, silly opinions, or pop culture snapshots. A prompt card is revealed, and players secretly choose answers from a small set of options. After everyone has chosen, the cards are flipped and revealed. The fun is in the reveal: players earn points when their chosen answer matches the majority. It is not about crafting the perfect argument; it is about reading the room, leaning into the herd vibe, and surviving the occasional misread with dignity or with a ridiculous victory lap.

The core mechanic is simple, but the social chemistry is rich. Will you align with the loudest voice in the room, or will you risk a brave misalignment and earn bragging rights for being the oddball who stood out? The latter is not always rewarded, but it is always entertaining.

## Gameplay Deep Dive: How the Herd Really Moves
### Round Flow: Breath In, Breath Out, Then Laugh
- Each round begins with a prompt card designed to provoke a quick, instinctive response rather than a careful essay. Speed is your ally; hesitation is your foe.
- Players secretly select an answer card that they think represents the majority response. The confidence with which you present your choice matters as much as the choice itself.
- All answers are revealed simultaneously. The herd index is calculated by how many players picked the most popular response. The more you match the majority, the more points you snag.
- Rounds rotate in larger groups; everyone gets a turn to be the oracle, which keeps the game from turning into a one-man show.

The result is a frenzied mix of social guessing and gentle manipulation (in the most wholesome sense). The timer’s presence is crucial; it keeps the pace brisk and the pressure manageable. Let the clock tick or you risk a philosophical debate about the nature of the herd, which can be fun, but sometimes you just want the punchline.

### Strategy and Social Chemistry: Reading the Room, Not Just the Card
A big part of Herd Mentality’s appeal is the social meta-game. You are not just guessing a majority; you are trying to read who thinks what others will choose. People with stage energy might lean into loud, obvious choices; more introverted players may hide in plain sight with safe, mass-appeal answers.

The real joy comes when the herd norm shifts mid-game: the quiet player suddenly becomes the most persuasive vote-wrangler by timing and personality. This is not a game about the best answer; it is a game about social amplification: the most popular response is often the one that resonates with the table at that moment. Your role is a mash-up of mind-reading, improv, and a dash of ego management (keeping a straight face is a separate sport).
