## The Stage: Malta in the 1560s
Malta in the mid 16th century was a crossroads in the Mediterranean. The Order of the Knights Hospitaller had settled there after being displaced from Rhodes. They were a motley bunch of knights who could drill, argue, and toast a glass of wine with equal gusto. Across the sea, the Ottoman Empire under Suleiman the Magnificent had designs on control of the central Mediterranean, which meant Malta was a useful pin in their grand game of geopolitics. Add to this the Spanish empire's appetite for expansion and you have a combustible mix.

### The Players
#### The Knights
The Grand Master of the Knights, Jean Parisot de La Valette, is often described as a portrait of calm and iron will. He faced not only a massive assault but also the inevitable questions about supply lines, morale, and the etiquette of retreat. He is a collision of leadership and luck with a dash of stubborn heroism.

#### The Ottomans
The Ottoman force arrives with numbers, ships, and a sense of inevitability. The siege was a marathon not a sprint; it was a siege of months with thousands of rounds fired and a decision to press, push, and press again.

### The Turning Points
As weeks pass, there are moments when the balance shifts. The Ottoman lines recalibrate; storms disrupt supply; disease gnaws at morale. ### The Final Days
The closing acts of the siege include bold sallies, stubborn holds, and a retreat that never quite ended. Malta survives because of an act of stubborn resilience that looks a lot like stubborn optimism wearing armor.
