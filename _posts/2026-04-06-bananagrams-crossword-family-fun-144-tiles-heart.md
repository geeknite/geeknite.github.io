## Strategy tips for the quick-thinking crowd
- Prioritize flexibility: build a grid that allows you to add words later, rather than locking yourself into a static structure. The goal is to be able to peel or dump strategically as tiles flow in, not to lock yourself into a dead end early.
- Keep vowels and common consonants handy: E, A, R, T, N, S, L, O — these will be your bread and butter. You’ll thank yourself for not overstocking on Qs and Zs in the early rounds.
- Create cross-connection opportunities: as soon as you can, place a few cross-connecting short words these will act as scaffolding for bigger word combos in later rounds.
- Be mindful of other players: part of the fun is watching your opponents’ grids collapse in comical fashion as you steal a critical cross-lane tile with a well-timed dump.
- Endgame optimization: the last couple of rounds are where you refine your lattice, prune questionable words, and squeeze out every last tile.

A hallmark of Bananagrams is its high replay value: the randomness of tile draws guarantees that no two games are exactly alike.
