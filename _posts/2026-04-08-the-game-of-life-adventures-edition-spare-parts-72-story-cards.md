## How to Use the Spare Parts: Integrating the Cards into Your Session

Adding 72 new prompts to a life-sim board game can feel like installing a high-speed rail system into a pedestrian-friendly pavement. You want smooth lines, not a chaotic traffic jam. Here are practical strategies to weave these prompts into sessions without derailing the ride.

### Baseline Rules and Game Flow Adjustments

- Start Small: Use 6–12 cards for a standard session. This keeps pace manageable while letting you gauge how players handle narrative prompts.
- When to Draw: Decide on universal triggers for drawing a card. For example, every time a player lands on a milestone space, they draw a card and weave that prompt into their ongoing story arc.
- Resolve and Reflect: After a card resolves, give players a moment to reflect. A quick why-is-this-relevant debrief helps anchor the narrative and reduces confusion.

### Card-Play Examples: How the Cards Reshape the Table

- Milestone prompts can trigger flash-forwards or flashbacks that shift how a player narrates their life path. Some players will try to time-travel a few turns to correct a misstep, which is perfect material for humor and chaos.
- What-If prompts drive small collaborative storylines. You may end up with one player recalling a family legend about a legendary vacation that spirals into a multi-episode arc, complete with recurring jokes about the family dog who ran the world’s longest courier route.
- Fortune/Misfortune cards add dice-charmed elements to the session: windfalls arriving via a mustached postman, bureaucratic hurdles that require negotiation with a fictional council of neighbors. These moments inject a healthy dose of risk and comedy into otherwise predictable turns.

The actual playtime impact varies with your group. If you run with improvisers, expect higher tempo and wilder twists. If your table leans toward strategy and less into storytelling, you’ll still get value from the prompts but at a slower narrative pace. Either way, the deck pays off in characterful moments you’ll be retelling for years—or at least until someone spills soda on the box and the nostalgia warranty kicks in.
