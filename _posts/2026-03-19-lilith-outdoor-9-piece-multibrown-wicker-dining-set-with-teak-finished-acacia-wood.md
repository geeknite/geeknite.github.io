## Materials, Weather, and Durability
Outdoor furniture is a battle-tested arena. The Lilith set has to endure sun, rain, wind, and the occasional stubborn sprinkler. Here’s how it fares from a durability perspective.

### Weather Resistance
Sun exposure can discolor wicker and dry out wood, turning a romance novel into an old encyclopedia of cracks. The Lilith set’s wicker is designed to resist UV-induced fading, while the teak-finished acacia wood provides a protective barrier against moisture and warping. The combination is a practical compromise: you get the warmth and texture of natural materials without needing to commit to the high-maintenance routine of solid teak or untreated hardwood.

In wet climates, the acacia wood needs a little care to prevent water marks and staining. The teak finish helps, but a routine rinse and a light oil or sealant a couple of times per season can extend the life of the wood. The wicker, being synthetic, is more forgiving, but if you’re in a seaside locale with salty air, giving the set a periodic rinse removes salt buildup that could compromise the weave over time.

### Maintenance and Care
Maintenance is the adult version of “charging your lightsaber.” It doesn’t have to be dramatic, but it helps if you do it regularly. For Lilith, a light wipe-down with mild soapy water and a soft cloth after outdoor use is usually enough to remove dust, pollen, and the occasional jam-stain from a s’more mishap. Avoid harsh chemicals that attack the finish; think of it as applying a gentle disinfectant for a starship—gentle is better for long-term performance.

Cushion care is straightforward: when possible, store cushions in a dry space during heavy rain or winter months. If you can’t store them, consider removable, water-resistant covers. It’s not just about aesthetics; it prevents mold and mildew from turning your patio into a tiny rainforest without your consent.
