## Comparisons: orange vs other dice sets and colorways
If you’ve spent time with Zombicide dice before, you know colorways can vary in function and flare. Here’s how the orange 6pc stacks up against common alternatives.

### Orange vs white dice
White dice are classic, clean, and versatile. They disappear into the table when you’re trying not to draw attention, which is great for stealthy planning sessions but can be a nightmare in a dim basement when you need quick reads. Orange dice solve that problem with a splash of color that reads instantly at a glance. If you value speed of interpretation during heavy combat, orange has the advantage. If you’re aiming for a minimalist aesthetic and a quiet vibe, maybe white is your friend.

### Orange vs red dice
Red dice scream danger. They’re great for high-intensity moments, but sometimes you want to avoid hinting at every blood-curdling result from a distance. Orange sits in a comfortable middle ground: high visibility without the aggressive heat of red. It’s a playful tone that fits the zombie vibe without shouting at your senses.

### Orange vs multi-color or translucent variants
Multi-color dice look fantastic on Instagram, but while they can be a feast for the eyes, they often carry a higher price tag and occasionally inconsistent readability under certain lighting. The orange set sticks to a solid, gamer-friendly palette that performs well under a variety of lighting conditions and wood-toned tables. If you’re playing for the long haul and want a reliable, no-drama option, orange wins in readability and accessibility.
