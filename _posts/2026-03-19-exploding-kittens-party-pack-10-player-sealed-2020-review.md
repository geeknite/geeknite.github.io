## How to Play with 10 Players

### Core mechanics and what changes with larger groups

Exploding Kittens relies on a simple core mechanic: you draw a card, you risk explosions if you draw the wrong card, and you use a mix of defense and misdirection to survive. In a ten player setup, the dynamics shift from one on one duels to group pressure. When the deck is breezing through cards and the table is roaring with laughter, every turn feels like a small victory or a small disaster depending on which side of the table you sit. The added card pool from the Party Pack introduces more ways to manipulate the pace and to bait opponents into bad decisions. The balance remains accessible to newcomers, because the cost of failure in the early rounds is fun and not punitive, which is exactly the energy a party game should bring.

### Rounds and tempo

Rounds evaporate quickly when ten players are engaged. The tempo swings on a dime: one player might methodically pick a safe route and then the next player trumps them with a well timed counter move. The charm here is that even a marginally unlucky draw can pivot the entire table into chaotic hilarity. For groups that like to lean into social bluffing, keep an eye on the timing of your plays. The moment you decide to press your luck with a risky move or to bluff your odds with a clever play, you inject a little theater into the proceedings. The game rewards nerve and humor in roughly equal measure, which is exactly the vibe a party game should offer when the room is full of friends who all want to be the star for a moment.

## Hosting Tips for a 10 Player Night

### Space, seating, and timing

Hosting ten players requires a little planning. You want a table wide enough to allow everyone to comfortably reach their cards, or you will want a couple of end tables to help with card access. Seating should be arranged to maximize visibility of the action for all players, which often means a little extra space around the table and perhaps a standing option for late comers to observe the chaos. Timing is important because this is a party game, and the mood can shift quickly from carnival to chaos if you let it run too long. Plan for two to three hours, but be prepared to stretch to four if the group is mid laugh and you want to milk the moment for every last cat pun. A short pizza break can help re-energize the room and rebuild the energy for the second half of the night.

### Hosting etiquette and house rules

A few simple house rules can keep things moving smoothly. Rotate the role of timer, use a soft tone when the table erupts in laughter, and remind guests that banter is welcome but mean spirited jokes should be kept in check. The beauty of a big group is that the chaos can be inclusive; ensure that everyone gets a fair chance to react and that no one hogs the spotlight for too long. A healthy dose of lighthearted ribbing goes a long way in keeping everyone engaged and willing to jump back into the next round.
