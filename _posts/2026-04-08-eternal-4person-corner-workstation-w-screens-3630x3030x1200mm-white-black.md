## Cable management, heat, and power planning

In any multi-user desk, power strips and cable routing become a minor art form. The Eternal desk’s cable management is designed to be modular, which means you should be able to route power and data cables through dedicated channels without sacrificing legroom or tripping over a stray HDMI. The trick is to plan the path of the cables before you install the screens and the devices. A few practical tips:

- Use labeled cable ties to identify each seat’s inputs and outputs. It saves you from the headache of not knowing which cable goes where during a Friday afternoon sprint.
- Place power modules within easy reach of each user without creating a trip hazard or a spaghetti avalanche that blocks legroom.
- Consider a small fan or vent solution if your room tends to trap heat around the workstation. Four people under lights can generate a surprising amount of warmth, especially when they’re all staring at screens with intense focus.

## Maintenance, care, and longevity

Durability is a long game. If you invest in a four-person corner workstation, you want it to survive the long haul. Cleaning should be straightforward: wipe the desk surface with a mild cleaner; avoid harsh solvents; and regularly check the frame bolts to prevent looseness. The privacy screens should be inspected for scuffs or cracks, and if you have pets or small children in the house, consider corner guards to protect the edges from curious paws or inadvertently shared snacks.

To maximize longevity, here are a few routine maintenance tips:

- Vacuum or wipe the desk surface weekly to remove dust and debris.
- Tighten allen screws every few months to maintain stability.
- Inspect grommets and cable channels for wear, and replace as needed to preserve a clean look.
- Clean screens with manufacturer-approved products to avoid scratching lens coatings or reducing privacy functionality.
