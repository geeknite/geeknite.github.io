## Design and Build Quality
This is where the romance begins and the dents of reality start to show up. A good travel case should feel premium in the hands and be capable of withstanding a rough TSA pat-down or a particularly enthusiastic airport conveyor belt run. Look for these design cues:

- Exterior shell: Should feel rigid yet slightly forgiving. A good case resists deformation; a bad one looks like it survived a wind tunnel test and a curious toddler with a stick.
- Latches: Prefer dual closures or a combination of a latch plus a secondary locking mechanism. The goal is “one-hand access” without turning the case into a stealthy escape pod.
- Hinges: If your case has hinges, they should operate smoothly and not squeak like a haunted door. A loose hinge invites a future crack along the line between the top and bottom halves.
- Exterior texture: A matte finish tends to resist fingerprints; a glossy finish might win you compliments but will collect smudges as if it were the grumpy cat of travel gear.
- Seals and water resistance: A simple gasket is a nice-to-have. If you can get a case that claims some moisture resistance, you’ll thank the universe when your cards survive a spilled drink or a sudden ocean-braced drizzle in the airport bar.

In practice, the cases I tested felt sturdy without being overly heavy.
