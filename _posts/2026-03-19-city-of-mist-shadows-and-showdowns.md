## What is Shadows and Showdowns?

Shadows and Showdowns is an expansion packet for the City of Mist system that prioritizes two things: tense, shadowy intrigue and climactic, cinematic confrontations. It doesn’t throw away what the core game does best—fashioning mythic implications from everyday life—but it does tilt the camera toward the crescendo, toward the moment when a character with a mask and a motive faces the consequences of their choices in the most literal sense. In practice, that means more structured showdown encounters, a stronger emphasis on location as a factor in outcomes, and a handful of tools to keep the action moving at the kind of pace where a single mistake can destroy a carefully built plan.

The book assumes you’ve got a copy of the base game. You’ll thumb through its pages and recognize the familiar dual-track system: Mythos versus Logos, Masks versus Motivations, and the constant tug-of-war between heroism and the city’s shadowy underbelly. Shadows and Showdowns doesn’t pretend to reinvent the wheel; instead it adds a few shiny spokes and gives you a more satisfying crank to turn when you need that big, neon-lit moment to land. If the core game is about discovering your myth, this supplement is about presenting you with the stage where that myth looks back at you and asks for a performance that could change the neighborhood forever.

The material is organized into a handful of long-form investigations with built-in showdowns, plus a suite of optional encounters that can be slotted into existing campaigns or spun up as standalone one-shots. The investigations are built around two or three central shadow figures—the kinds of antagonists who make sense as symbols in a city that never sleeps—and each arc threads through a local hotspot, a ribald corner of the city where the noir vibes are thick enough to cut with a switchblade. The idea is to give players and GMs a rhythm: a slow, ominous build, a reveal of a deeper scheme, and then a showdown where consequences finally meet reality.

To kick things off, Shadows and Showdowns opens with a short primer on mood and pacing. The author is explicit about the tone: avoid the lazy trope of a sudden “boss fight” when the story wants something more personal and meaningful. Instead, expect sequences that leverage the city’s environment, the players’ masks, and the narrative clock the book calls its Showdown Track. The Showdown Track is a new mechanic that acts as a heat meter for the climactic moment. As the story progresses, the track advances, and the closer you get to the finish line, the more consequences the city imposes on hero and villain alike. It’s a clever way to simulate a showdown while forcing players to make meaningful choices under pressure.


## Core mechanics and theme: what’s new under the neon?

### Showdown Track and Showdown Cards

The centerpiece new tool is the Showdown Track. It’s a coin-toss of tension: as scenes unfold, players contribute a little momentum to push the track forward. When the track hits certain milestones, the GM triggers a dramatic mechanic that makes the upcoming showdown both more dangerous and more cinematic. The result is less of a “you win or lose” moment and more of a “how do we narrate this last act without breaking the myth we’ve built?” approach. It’s not radical if you know City of Mist, but it’s a very welcome push toward narrative closure.

Coordinated with the track are Showdown Cards, a deck of one-page prompts you can draw at the critical moment. These aren’t just plot hooks—they’re mood modifiers: a crowd erupts, a symbol appears, a key location changes hands, or a power flips from ally to liability in a heartbeat. The cards help keep the finale feeling fresh, even if you’ve played the same core antagonists before. If you enjoy the idea of a showdown that isn’t just a fight but a performance, these cards will sing to you.

### Shadowy factions and civic mythos

Shadows and Showdowns is at its best when it foregrounds the city as a character. The book introduces a couple of new factions—shadow brokers, late-night previews of corruption, and a “Public Eye” faction that threads through multiple investigations. These groups aren’t just enemies; they’re ecosystem builders. They influence which masks appeal most to the situation, what motives seem noble to some and monstrous to others, and how the public narrative can shift overnight with a single broadcast or scandal.

The mythos in this supplement feels kin to the core game’s approach: your characters aren’t just punching above their weight; they’re performing a mythic role for a crowd that misreads their actions as fate. Shadows and Showdowns gives you an extra layer of mythic motifs to work with—a handful of theme cards that players can deploy to emphasize a trait the party wants to emphasize at the moment. If your table loves the idea of a mask that becomes a mirror, you’ll find a lot to adore here.

### New investigations and optional play modes

The investigations in Shadows and Showdowns are structured rather than wandering. You’ll find a few extended arcs that are designed to be resolved in 4–6 sessions, with a clear “season finale” vibe. The book also includes a few shorter, self-contained adventures that can slot into an ongoing campaign when you want a quick pivot to something that feels elevated and a little theatrical. The recommended pacing is on the slower side at first, building atmosphere and character stakes, then ramps into a high-energy final act with a proper showdown that justifies the time you’ve invested.

There’s a nice nod to players who love roleplay heavy sessions and to GMs who want more mechanical scaffolding for set pieces. The investigations provide both narrative beats and mechanical cues to facilitate action-heavy scenes without sacrificing story depth. If you’ve ever wished for an official toolkit to run a “neon-noir opera” with ensemble cast, this part of the book comes closest to delivering that fantasy with a satisfyingly crisp rhythm.

You get more masks that emphasize internal conflict, more motifs that flip power dynamics in a scene, and a handful of new relationships that can spawn red herrings, alliances, and fragile loyalties. The masks are designed to be played against the backdrop of a specific showdown, so you’ll notice that your character’s strengths and weaknesses are placed in a context that’s more dramatic than typical. If you’ve built a character who thrives on ambiguity and moral compromise, you’ll find Shadows and Showdowns to be a strong match for your playstyle.

One of the neat touches is how the new masks interact with the existing public perception rules. It’s not world-shaking, but it does give you more plausible ways for a character to be both celebrated and hunted at the same time. The book also includes a few ready-to-run character prompts that are specifically tailored to the investigations included in the package. ## Campaign structure: arcs, locations, and the rhythm of a finale

### Arc design and pacing

Each investigation is built around a three-act rhythm that aligns with the Showdown Track. Act One establishes the shadow figure and the city’s mood; Act Two complicates the plan with a revelation that reframes the stakes; Act Three culminates in a Showdown where the players’ decisions lead to a visible consequence for the city and its myths. The pacing is deliberate to support roleplaying and investigation work, but it also gives you a reliable engine to push toward a climactic resolution when your table wants a big moment. The book provides pacing tips for various group sizes and play styles, which is a thoughtful addition for new GM teams who might otherwise struggle to hold a tense finale together.

### Locations as characters

The setting pieces in Shadows and Showdowns are designed to feel like living stages. You’ll visit a nightclub that doubles as a signal tower, a mural-filled alley that acts as a map of the city’s memory, and a courthouse where the law and myth clash in a way that reveals the city’s deeper contradictions. The book provides ready-to-use stat blocks for key locations and a set of environmental effects that trigger during the Showdown. This makes scenes feel cinematic without making the GM flip into “boss fight mode” every time a clock ticks toward the end.

### Showdowns as narrative force multipliers

Showdowns aren’t simply fights; they are narrative turning points. The mechanics behind them reward players who leverage their masks in unconventional ways and who engage with the environment rather than just rolling dice. If your group tends to rush to the tabletop’s most mechanical solution, Shadows and Showdowns nudges you to think in terms of spectacle and consequence. If your group loves to roleplay the moment when a plan collapses under its own ambition, you’ll likely savor the Showdown phase. In that sense, the supplement does what it intends: it makes finales feel earned rather than inevitable.

## GM tips: running Shadows and Showdowns effectively

- Start with the mood, not the map. The showdowns need atmosphere and audience. Use the Showdown Track as a storytelling compass and let the city react to the players’ decisions.
- Use environments as active participants. The showdowns benefit from a few environmental effects that can tilt the odds or change the stakes in a heartbeat. Don’t be afraid to reuse locations in different ways across sessions; the city is big enough for that reuse to feel purposeful.
- Let the masks do the talking. In City of Mist, the mask is a lens. Encourage players to narrate not just what their heroes do, but what the mask reveals about their character’s inner contradictions in the moment of crisis.
- Don’t force the fight. If a showdown could be resolved through negotiation, leverage, or a clever misdirection, give the players space to pursue that path. The best finales in this book feel earned because they were chosen, not forced by a GM who “needs” a fight scene.
- Pace the reveals. The investigations in this supplement reward patience. Build a narrative rhythm where a major clue reframes a character’s motive, which then leads to a critical choice. The Showdown should feel like the natural consequence of those choices, not a deus ex machina.
- Prepare a quick anchor for each player. The book provides a handful of prompts for each mask that you can drop into a session to reconnect players with their core themes. These anchors help maintain player engagement during longer investigations and make the final act feel like a culmination rather than an exit ramp.
