## Grading and condition talk: what does Excellent mean here?
### What “Excellent Condition” usually entails
In the grading world, “Excellent” means the card shows very little wear for its age. You’ll typically see:
- Bright, clean artwork with minimal, if any, surface scratches or whitening along edges
- Corners that are sharp with only minor touches of white if any
- Centering that’s close to 60/40 or better (top-to-bottom and left-to-right) depending on the manufacturer’s tolerance for that particular run
- No creases or major print defects on the front; back should be clean with even color distribution

Of course, the exact threshold can vary by grader (CGC, PSA, and BGS all have their own scales and quirks). The Gym Heroes print run had its own quirks—some copies show slight print-shine in the holo area, others present a flatter finish. An Excellent card is a dream for binders, not just an investment piece but a conversation starter; you’ll be asked politely by fellow collectors to show it off, and you’ll respond with a proud, nerdy grin that says, “Yes, the nostalgia is real, and I own it with a stamp from a bygone era.”

### Visual checks you can perform at home
If you’re evaluating a copy yourself, here are quick checks that align with general excellent-grade expectations:
- Surface: Look for uniform gloss in printed areas and assess for fingerprints, smudges, or fingerprints that could indicate handling damage. A clean surface means you’re not paying extra for a glossy trap.
- Edges and corners: Check for faint whitening or tiny nicks. Remember, a near-imperceptible edge touch can still be a deal-breaker at certain grades.
- Color integrity: Back of the card should have consistent color saturation and no significant fading.
- Centering: While not always critical, better-centered copies hold more value and present better in a display.

If you want a formal grade, you’ll want to bring the card to a reputable grading service. In our market, PSA, BGS, and CGC are common names. ## Care, display, and preservation tips
### Immediate steps for excellent-condition copies
- Store in a humidity-controlled environment (ideally 40-50% relative humidity) to prevent warping or edge whitening.
- Use a top-loaded sleeve with protective inner sleeves (soft sleeves) and then a rigid binder page or a merchandise-safe display to minimize contact.
- Avoid direct sunlight—UV exposure can fade colors over time, even on older print runs.
- Keep away from magnetized spaces and metal objects that could scratch or dent the surface.

### Long-term display ideas
If you’re brave enough to mount your Erika’s Gloom, consider a display approach that celebrates the Gym Heroes era without risking damage:
- A matte-finish display frame with UV-filtering acrylic to protect the card while you admire the art.
- A binder with acid-free pages and 2-pocket sleeves that allow for flip-through without repeated exposure to air exposure.
- A dedicated “Gym Heroes Corner” in your display, with a light dusting of nostalgia and a small bio card about Erika and the Celadon City gym for context.

### Cleaning and handling etiquette
- Handle with clean hands or wear cotton gloves if you’re going for that professional grader vibe.
- Avoid touching the surface; hold the card by the edges to reduce skin oils and residues from transferring.
- If you accidentally smudge the card, do not attempt DIY cleaning. Seek professional restoration advice or consider a professional-grade cleaning service that specializes in vintage trading cards.
