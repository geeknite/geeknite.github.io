## Unboxing and First Impressions
Opening a box of modular panels is a little like unwrapping a sci-fi building kit. You expect it to be simple, but you also expect the manual to be written by a cryptic AI that learned three languages from IKEA manuals and a motivational TED Talk. The GOF system ships in multiple cartons, each labeled with a bold plan that only makes sense when you line up nods with your team lead and pretend you’re following a treasure map. The metal frames feel sturdy—solid enough to survive a couple of coffee-fueled standups without bending into a melodramatic shape. The panels themselves are a mix of fabric-taced boards and acoustic felt—quiet and squinted-eyed when you test them with a finger tap.

The color options presented a small but charming dilemma: coffee-bean brown, storm-gray, and something GOF cheekily calls “industrial sunrise.” If your office has a wall color you pretend to like, aim for the storm-gray or industrial sunrise to mask the inevitable coffee spill fingerprints. Assembly is where the magic (and the minor curses) begin. You’ll need basic tools, maybe a power drill, and the ability to pretend you understand a two-page diagram written by someone who clearly drew the panels upside down. The included hardware is plentiful and clearly labeled—trust me, there’s nothing more satisfying than hearing the satisfying clack of bolt meets bracket and realizing you’ve built something that might survive a minor earthquake.

## Design and Layout
Ten-person openness is a tricky proposition. You want privacy, but you don’t want to feel like you’ve been walled in by a fortress of productivity. GOF’s approach is to create ten modular pods with shared panels between adjacent units and tall screens that provide a reasonable amount of acoustic separation without imprisoning creativity.

The 48-inch width gives you decent desk surface per person, with enough space for monitor arms, small document stands, or a compact plant that is likely to become a coworker’s personal jungle. The 30-inch depth is sensible for keyboard-and-mouse setups, with enough room to pull back and enforce a proper posture. At 48 inches high, the panels provide a sense of privacy while not giving the feeling of being in a separate cabin. It’s a good balance between openness and seclusion, a compromise that many workplaces claim to seek but rarely achieve without resorting to opaque glass walls or cubicle barns that would make a sheep believe it’s in a pasture.

The modular design shines when you need to reconfigure for a project team, a new hire cohort, or a top-secret internal hackathon that only needs to borrow a few desks for a week. The inter-panel connections click into place with a quiet confidence, and you can slide the panels to merge pods for larger collaboration sessions while keeping the “zones” concept intact. It’s a compromise that feels deliberate rather than arbitrary—an important distinction when you’re assembling a workspace during a lunch break that would otherwise involve a lot of stomach growling and questionable pizza choices.

## Assembly Experience
If you’re expecting a “drawers-and-foort” experience, you’ll get a more grown-up experience—like assembling a high-tech bookshelf that happens to be a desk at the same time. The instruction manual is straightforward enough for someone who isn’t allergic to hardware. We found the included labeling and hardware packets helpful, with color-coded screws that prevent you from mixing up a bracket meant for Panel A with one meant for Panel J. If you’re hosting a family BBQ at the office, this is the moment you realize your colleagues are quietly judging you for reading assembly diagrams with the seriousness of a diplomatic treaty.

A good spirit and a bubble level do more than you’d expect for a kit that is designed to travel across ten people aboard a single system.

## Acoustic Performance and Privacy
Acoustics matter when you’re trying to focus on that tricky spreadsheet or a programming bug that refuses to be fixed. The GOF panels incorporate acoustic materials that dampen the general hubbub. It won’t turn your office into a library, but it will help reduce those moments when your neighbor’s call about a “quick chat” becomes the soundtrack to your afternoon.

## Cable Management and Power
Cable management is one of those things that looks easy in diagrams and harder in real life. GOF’s system provides under-desk trays, grommets, and channels that help you route cords out of sight while staying accessible. We recommend routing power and data lines along the back edge of the workstation to minimize the chance of someone catching a chair leg and sending a cascade of cables down the aisle like a low-budget Rube Goldberg machine.

Keep in mind power density and heat. Ten workstations will draw a surprising amount of current if you’re running eight monitors, multiple docking stations, and a server-level coffee maker in the break room. If you’re worried about your electrical load, consult a licensed electrician and a GOF representative to ensure your office does not trip a breaker every time someone unplugs the printer to do a dramatic power move.
