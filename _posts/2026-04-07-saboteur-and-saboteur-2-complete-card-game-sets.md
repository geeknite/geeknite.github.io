## How to play in a nutshell

Saboteur and Saboteur 2 share a DNA: you assign secret roles, you build tunnels with a mixture of path cards, and you try to reach treasure before the other team or before you are exposed as a saboteur. The round structure tends to be fast, which is perfect for casual game nights, streams, or chaotic family gatherings.

- Setup: deal role cards to players; each player receives a secret assignment (good dwarf or saboteur). The treasure card is placed at the bottom of a row of path cards that players will try to reveal.
- Turn structure: draw a card, play a path or action card, and pass the turn. Action cards can block an opponent, peek at another player’s card, or rearrange the tunnels in some clever fashion.
- Endgame: once a sequence of path cards reveals a treasure, the round ends and points are tallied according to who collected the treasure or successfully sabotaged other players.

Saboteur 2 adds more complexity with new roles and new action card dynamics. The core tension remains the same: do you cooperate with your neighbors to crack the tunnel code, or do you push a trap in their path to claim the treasure yourself?
