Assembly: Step by Step-ish

We honest-to-goodness tested assembly with basic tools, and it felt like the right balance of straightforward and forgiving. Here is the condensed play-by-play:

- Lay out the parts and confirm you have all the screws, washers, and the allen wrench or screwdriver included in the package. The frustration of discovering a missing bolt is real but manageable if you have a spare from a prior IKEA expedition.
- Attach the legs to the frame using the included hardware. Be mindful of alignment; wobbly legs will make your coffee nervous and your printer suspicious of gravity.
- Mount the lower shelf first, then the upper shelf. The order matters if you want the shelves to be level; otherwise you might end up with a tilt that makes everyone in your Zoom call think you are running a beta version of a tilt-a-desk.
- Tighten all screws to snug, not T-REX grip levels. You want stability, not a metal song that plays every time you bump the desk.
- Place items on the shelves and adjust for balance. If items slide, consider adding a small non-slip mat or a tiny strip of shelf liner to keep things tidy.

A few assembly tips from the field: keep an extra self-contained screwdriver in the desk drawer; use a battery-powered driver if you have one; and don t over-tighten the screws, or you risk stripping the threads. The goal is rigidity with enough give to avoid the sensation of a legal-binding furniture contract cracking when you lean on it.
