### Gameplay: what is the core loop and how does the Collector's Edition influence it?
Suburbia is a tile-laying city building game with a focus on balance and optimization. On your turn you draw a district card, you buy a tile and place it on your board, and you try to maximize your population while meeting the various scoring conditions. The Collector's Edition doesn't rewrite the mechanics; it clarifies them and gives you a slightly more tactile experience. The core loop remains:
1) Acquire resources and population. 
2) Place new districts to unlock better income and more population. 
3) Score points via end-game scoring cards and city growth metrics. 
4) Repeat until the city reaches a pleasing or terrifying size.

The English edition helps by presenting the icons clearly and providing a consistent font that reduces eye fatigue. The upgrades in the Collector's Edition make the late game less punishing to read, which is a win because the late game is where you realize your entire suburban dream hinges on a single misread symbol that costs you a circulation of income or a crucial population point.
