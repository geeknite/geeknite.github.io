## How the game actually works

Bananagrams functionality rests on a few simple principles: you flip tiles, you shout “go” (or pretend you didn’t hear the timer and hope no one notices), and you assemble a crossword-like grid in record time. The New Bananagrams edition sticks to this core mechanic but adjusts the tilt with a couple of design choices.

### Core gameplay loop
- Each player grabs a certain number of tiles from the bag (the exact number varies with household rules, but a typical setup is enough tiles to fuel a whirlwind of word-building for 2–4 players).
- When the game starts, everyone races to form their own grid of connected words. There are no turns; it’s raw speed, vocabulary, and a little strategy about letter distribution.
- When a player finishes, they shout “peel” and all players take one more tile. The round continues until there are no more tiles left, or someone yells “BANANAGRAMS!” with a triumphant grin.
- The first player to properly use all their tiles wins. That sounds simple, but it’s a glorious chaos of crosswords, bluffs, and the occasional existential moment when you realize you’ve made a grid that looks more like a barcode than a word search.
