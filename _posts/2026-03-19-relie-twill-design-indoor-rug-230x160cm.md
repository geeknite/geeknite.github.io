## Care and Maintenance
### Cleaning Regimen
Maintenance is where a rug like the Relie Twill shines. Routine vacuuming at least once a week keeps surface dust and pet hair at bay. For spills, blot the area promptly rather than rubbing — rubbing tends to push the liquid deeper into the fibers. For more stubborn stains, a gentle solution of water and a mild detergent works well; always test a small, inconspicuous spot first to ensure colorfastness.

Deep-cleaning a rug of this size is a two-person job. You can roll it up, take it outside, and give the back a shake to dislodge compact dirt. If your space allows, a gentle steam clean can refresh the pile without shrinking or deforming the weave. It’s not necessary every month, but a periodic refresh is beneficial, especially in homes with kids and pets.

### Durability Across Floors and Environments
On wood, tile, or laminate, the rug offers steady, non-slip performance when paired with a good underlay. On carpet, the rug’s weight helps anchor the space and reduces excessive shifting. The edge stitching tends to hold up well under normal foot traffic; fringing or edge loosening is rare, but if you notice any pull, a quick seam repair or patching is straightforward for a home DIYer.

## Buying Guide: What to Look For When You Shop
### Key Factors to Consider
- Size and Shape: Ensure the rug fits the space with room to spare for chairs and door clearance.

## FAQ
### How do I know if this rug will fit under my sofa?
Measure the footprint of your sofa and add a 6–12 inch buffer to each side. If you’re unsure, lay out painter’s tape to simulate the rug’s dimensions before purchasing.

### Can this rug be used under a dining table?
Yes, provided the table seat clearance is compatible with the rug’s dimensions. A larger rug helps prevent chair legs from catching on the edge, and the twill texture helps hide scuffs from chair legs.

### Is it pet-proof?
The fibers are generally stain resistant and vacuum-friendly. For pets that love to scratch or chew, consider an underlay and keep an eye on the edges; some animals may pick at loose fibers if left unchecked.

### How do I remove pet odors?
A quick spray with a mild enzymatic cleaner designed for fabrics can help, followed by a thorough air-out and vacuum. Always test a small patch first.
