Pine wood, even when kiln-dried, requires routine maintenance. The protective coating matters; if you leave it unsealed, moisture can cause warping, swelling, and micro cracking. The recommended approach is to apply a sealant, maybe a wood oil or varnish, every year or two depending on climate. The kit may include self-assembly; expect some parts to require alignment.

## Care and Maintenance
A quick care routine goes a long way. Brush off leaves, wipe down frames with a mild soap solution, and ensure cushions are fully dry before storing. For fabrics, removable cushion covers should be washed as per label; avoid high heat; air-dry recommended. For wooden parts, treat with a wood conditioner or exterior-grade sealant every year to maintain color and prevent cracking. If you live in a rainy region, use covers during storms or store inside during the off-season.
