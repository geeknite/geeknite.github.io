## Care and maintenance: keeping your Comet Blue in peak cosmic condition

### Routine care

- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting to lift particles without pulling at the fibers. Wool loves a good vacuum, but it does not love being abused by a spoiled, overpowered behemoth vacuum with a brush bannister.
- Rotate the rug every 3-6 months to ensure even wear. Your rug wants to see all parts of the room in due time; don’t make it a biased resident that only stares at the coffee table.
- Address spills immediately. Blot with a clean, dry cloth—avoid rubbing, which can work the liquid deeper into the fibers. If you’re dealing with red wine or coffee, a mild wool-friendly cleaner can help, but test on a hidden corner first.
- Sun exposure matters. Wool can fade under direct sun, so consider moving the rug occasionally or using window treatments to preserve the color story.

### Deep cleaning and maintenance

If a seasonal deep clean is warranted, consider professional cleaning. A wool-specific cleaning service will know how to treat delicate fibers without causing shrinkage or color bleed. For DIY enthusiasts, do a bit of homework; not all home cleaners are created equal for wool fibers. You can consult trusted sources for recommended methods, but again, avoid making the rug a science experiment.

### Dealing with common wool woes

- Shedding: Expect some initial shedding in the first few weeks; this calms down with regular vacuuming.
- Pilling: A wool rug may develop minor pilling with heavy usage. Gently remove pills with a fabric shaver and maintain a tidy surface.
- Dents and flattening: Move furniture occasionally and use rug pads to prevent sinking. A good pad reduces wear and protects floors, giving your Comet Blue a stable gravity well to anchor the room.

## Quality checks before you click the purchase button

- Look for tight weave and uniform pile height. Fold the rug and inspect the edge finishing; you don’t want frays or loose threads.
- Check backing: a sturdy backing reduces shedding and extends rug life. If the rug moves on the floor, you’ll want a non-slip pad underneath.
- Color consistency: natural wool dyes can vary slightly between batches. If you’re ordering a custom size across a wide area, plan for minor shade variation. It can be a feature, not a flaw, especially if you love a handmade vibe.
- Weight and thickness: heavy wool rugs often denote quality. Pick a weight that balances feel with practicality in your space.

### How to order and what to measure

1) Measure the room: length and width; account for furniture; decide how much space you want to extend beyond the furniture legs. A typical setup often places the front legs of sofas on the rug to unify the seating area.
2) Decide on pile height: a lower pile is easier to clean and better for high-traffic areas, while a higher pile adds coziness and insulation.
3) Choose custom vs standard: if you have an unusual space, go with a custom size. If you want a quick upgrade, pick the 8x10.
4) Confirm colors in your lighting: test color samples or swatches under your lighting for a more realistic expectation.
5) Place your order and plan for delivery times. Custom orders may take longer, but the payoff is a perfect fit.
