### Durability and Care

- Wool fibers naturally resist staining, but spills should be blotted, not rubbed, to avoid embedding liquids into the fiber.
- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting; avoid baring down on the fibers with a beater brush that could loosen the weave.
- Professional cleaning is recommended every couple of years depending on traffic.
- Rotate occasionally to promote even wear and prevent heavy traffic patterns.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Beauty Alive

A rug like this is not a one-night stand with your floors; it’s a long-term relationship with your interior life. Here are practical care steps to keep the Ivory Grey Hand-Woven rug looking new-ish for the foreseeable future (and beyond).

- Vacuum monthly or as needed using a vacuum with adjustable height; avoid high suction that could pull on long fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the weave.
- Treat stains promptly with mild wool-safe cleaners; test on a hidden corner first.
- Use a rug pad to reduce movement, extend life, and protect flooring underneath.
- Have it professionally cleaned as needed; wool can handle gentle cleaning cycles, but avoid aggressive detergents that could strip natural oils.

### A Quick Supply Checklist

If you want to maintain color depth and texture, you might appreciate a few care items: a gentle wool-safe cleaner, a pad, a mild stain remover, and a lint roller for incidental fuzz. The result is a rug that remains a conversation piece without turning your living room into a science experiment.
