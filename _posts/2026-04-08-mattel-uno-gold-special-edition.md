## Quick rules refresher for the swift fans
If you want a quick look at the core UNO mechanics before the drama begins, here is a compact refresher. UNO is a shedding card game where you aim to be the first to empty your hand. You match either color or number on the top card of the discard pile. Action cards include Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four. The key twist is that when you have one card left, you must shout UNO to avoid a penalty if someone catches you. In UNO GOLD, these rules still apply. The gold elements do not change what you can do on a turn; they simply make the moment you call UNO a little more ceremonial. Feel free to add house rules that lean into the gold vibe, such as awarding a tiny prize to the person who shouts UNO with the most flourish, or granting an extra draw for dramatic, over the top color change commits.

### Card actions at a glance
- Skip makes the next player miss a turn. 
- Reverse flips the direction of play. 
- Draw Two forces the next player to draw two cards. 
- Wild lets you choose a new color. 
- Wild Draw Four combines color choice with a five card penalty (use per official rules). 

Shouting UNO remains a social contract that binds players to a moment of shared laughter or mild chaos. A good rule of thumb is to shout UNO with a sense of timing and a pinch of theatrical flair. It makes the moment feel earned and not forced.
