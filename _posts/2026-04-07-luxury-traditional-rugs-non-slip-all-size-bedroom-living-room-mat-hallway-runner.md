### Non-slip fundamentals

The best non-slip performance comes from a combination of backings and padding:
- a latex or rubber backing that grips hard floors
- a felt or fabric secondary backing for added friction
- optional anti-slip pads that create a friction barrier between rug and floor

Now, before you go shopping, here’s how to approach sizing and room fit.

## Materials and construction: what to expect

A luxury rug is a marriage of material, loom, dye, and finish. Here are the big considerations:

### Materials

- 100% wool: soft, resilient, and excellent for foot warmth
- Wool blends: balance softness, durability, and cost
- Silk or viscose accents: add sheen and fine detail
- Blended synthetic fibers: for cost-conscious shoppers seeking durability

### Backing and grip

Backings can make or break the non-slip claim. Look for:
- latex or rubber backings that grip hard floors
- textile backings with non-slip compounds
- integrated anti-slip underlays for stairwells and hall runners

### Size matters: all sizes for all spaces

- Mats 60 x 90 cm
- Bedside 60 x 120 cm
- Small to medium area rugs 5 x 8 ft
- Large living room rugs 8 x 10 ft
- Hallway runners 2 x 8 ft or longer
- Dining room rugs 9 x 12 ft

In general, ensure there is 6-12 inches of exposed floor around the rug.

## Room-by-room: a practical guide

### Bedroom comfort underfoot

Choose a plush to mid-pile rug for bedtime coziness. Place under the bed so that there is at least 18 inches of rug extending on each side. This makes cold mornings a little less dramatic.

### Living room: anchor and accent

In a living room, the rug should underlay the seating area. The rug size should be large enough to sit under at least the front legs of all major furniture. A traditional rug with a bold medallion becomes the focal point of the space.

### Hallways and corridors: quiet luxury

Hallway runners should balance with the width of the corridor and provide a comfortable walking width. A non-slip back keeps it in place.

### Runners: length matters

Runners are all about proportion. Measure your hallway and choose a rug that leaves 2-3 inches from the wall on each side, so the runner feels integrated rather than squeezed.

## Design and color palettes

Traditional rugs come in deep jewel tones or soft neutrals. A classic combination like navy, ivory, and burgundy offers contrast; a muted palette with a pop of red or teal can work with modern furnishings.

- Medallion centers
- Floral borders
- Persian and classic European patterns
- Color blocking with borders to anchor furniture

### Pattern integrity and scale

Scale matters: large rooms can swallow bold patterns; small rooms benefit from more refined details. If you’re unsure, start with a central motif and pull color from the surrounding decor.

## Care, maintenance, and longevity

A luxurious rug is an investment; treat it like a fine leather jacket, but for floors.

### Vacuuming and dust control

- Vacuum with a beater bar on low suction to avoid fiber damage
- Vacuum along the grain of the pile, not against
- Rotate periodically to ensure even wear

### Stain management

- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth
- Use a mild detergent and water for stains
- Avoid rubbing; work from the outside in to prevent spreading

### Professional cleaning intervals

Most households benefit from professional cleaning every 12-24 months, depending on traffic and pets.

## Durability and value

Quality wool rugs and well-made backings age well. Silk accents require more care but deliver incredible luster.
