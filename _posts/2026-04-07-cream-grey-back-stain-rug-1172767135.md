## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance
Daily maintenance is your friend in rug ownership. Here are practical care tips to keep this runner looking fresh without turning your life into a professional cleaning schedule.

- Vacuum regularly: A light, frequent pass with a vacuum helps prevent dirt from embedding into the fibers. Use a brush roller setting if available, but avoid aggressive agitation that can shed fibers over time.
- Spot cleaning: For spills, blot, don’t rub. Rubbing can push stains deeper and create a longer road to recovery. Mild soap or detergent mixed with water is typically sufficient for most household mishaps.
- Deep cleaning: If you ever decide to do a deeper clean, follow the manufacturer’s guidance. In general, a gentle wash or steam cleaning with a rug-safe solution is advisable. Do not soak the rug; the backing and fibers may not appreciate being submerged for long periods.
- Drying: Allow the rug to dry completely before walking on it. A damp rug can attract more dirt and may emit smells you’d rather not discuss online.
- Pet hair and dander: A lint roller or a vacuum attachment designed for pet hair can help when furry friends leave their mark. The light color shows fur more easily, but it also makes the cleanup moment rewarding because you can visibly see your progress.
