## Cleaning and maintenance tips
### Routine care
- Vacuum regularly to remove dirt and grit that can act like sandpaper on the fibers.
- Shake or beat the rug to dislodge loose debris if you have access to a balcony railing or a big backyard tree limb.
- Spot clean stains with mild detergent and a soft brush. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the color or backing over time.

### Deep cleaning when life gets extra dramatic
If you host epic outdoor gatherings with messy food, you might need a deeper clean. A gentle hand-wash with a soft brush and lukewarm water can do wonders. For drying, lay it flat in shade if possible to avoid color bleed and avoid direct sunlight on hot surfaces that could warp the backing. If you have a hose, a gentle rinse is perfectly fine, but avoid blasting it with high pressure that could loosen fibers.

### Storage for winter or off-season months
When you store the rug, roll rather than fold to prevent creases. Store in a cool, dry place to protect from humidity. If you are a true space pirate about it, you can even use a breathable fabric cover to guard against dust while the rug lounges in retirement until spring.
