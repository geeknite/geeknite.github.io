## Rules and Gameplay: How It Plays in a Tiny World
Phase 10’s core mechanic—complete ten specific phases by collecting sets, runs, or other patterns—remains the heart of Miniature Phase 10 GO!, but the delivery is different. The game leans into speed and accessibility, designed for quick rounds that you can squeeze into a lunch break or between episodes of your favorite streaming series.

### Setup and Turn Order
- Each player starts with a small hand of cards, enough to begin planning a phase but not so many that you feel overwhelmed by options.
- The goal of each round is to complete a phase. In classic Phase 10, players progress through ten explicit phases; in the GO! version, you still chase phases, but the pace is adjusted for compact play. The exact composition of each phase remains faithful to the original spirit: a mix of sets (like trips of the same number) and runs (sequences of consecutive numbers).
- On your turn, you draw a card from the draw pile and then discard one. If you’re feeling bold, you might hold onto a GO! card or a strategic handful to try for a phase on your opponent’s turn, because the GO! edition often rewards bold, quick moves and a dash of risk.

### GO! Cards and Turn Dynamics
One of the standout design tricks in Miniature Phase 10 GO! is the GO! card mechanic. It’s designed to inject moments of momentum into the round. A GO! card in play can do one of several fast-paced things, depending on the local rules you use:
- A GO! card could let you immediately advance to the next phase, bypass a slower one, or let you take an extra draw when the table is humming with tension.
- It could also serve as a last-minute disruptor, forcing opponents to redraw or reshuffle a minimal portion of their hands as you clamor for dominance.

The exact rules surrounding GO! cards can vary by the edition, so be sure to skim the quick-start guide before you play. The joy, however, comes from the sense that every GO! moment feels like a small surprise party in your living room: you did not expect to gain precious ground through a tiny card, and suddenly you’re in a fight you didn’t know you wanted to win.

### Round Flow and Winning the Phase
Rounds in Miniature Phase 10 GO! typically operate like this:
- Each player tries to complete the designated phase they’re aiming for in that round. The game continues across rounds as players accumulate success markers or score points based on how efficiently they completed phases.
- Once a player completes a phase, they can move to the next phase in the sequence. In a travel-friendly edition, the progression might be a little more modular, with phases that are easier to grasp on a crowded bus than a full-size table.
- The winner is usually the player who completes the required number of phases first or who achieves the highest score after a fixed number of rounds. In some home rules, you can cap the game after a certain number of rounds, ensuring a complete arc to your micro-adventure.
