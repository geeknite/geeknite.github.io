## How to play this edition (brief refresher)
If you’ve played CODENAMES before, you know the drill: two teams, one spymaster per team, a grid of 25 word cards, and a single word clue that must connect as many of your agents as possible in one turn. The Marvel Edition sticks to that backbone but sprinkles in a few tweaks that are worth noting:

- Clues can reference Marvel characters, places, tech, and events as long as they don’t directly copy a code word on the board. This is basically the same rule as the classic game but with more opportunities for nerdy wink-winks.
- The hero cards add different boosts or constraints. For example, flipping a card might reveal a “Power Up” mode that can grant an extra guess for a turn, or impose a new constraint to keep teams on their toes.
- Some cards tilt the risk/reward meter: a few words carry bigger associations, but they also come with a higher chance of blowing up your comms if your team isn’t precise. It’s like wielding Thor’s hammer—immense power, but you still need to aim.

If you’ve never played CODENAMES, here’s the distilled version: the spymaster gives a one-word clue plus a number. The number tells you how many cards on the board are connected to that clue. Your teammates then discuss and try to guess which cards those are. If you hit all your cards, you lock in another turn; if you hit a neutral card, the turn ends; if you hit the assassin card, you lose immediately. Simple, tense, and deliciously cruel—perfect for a gamer’s heart and a villain’s ego.

In Marvel Edition, the theming makes the process feel more cinematic without altering the core rules. The changes are mostly cosmetic and a few card effects that lean into hero vibes rather than game rules. It’s a clever blend of license flavor and solid game design that doesn’t interrupt the flow when you’re in the middle of a long string of clues.
