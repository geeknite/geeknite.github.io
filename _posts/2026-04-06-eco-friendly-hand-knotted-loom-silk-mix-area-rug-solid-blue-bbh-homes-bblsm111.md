## Weaving Technique: The Art Behind the Pile
### From Loom to Living Room
Hand-knotted loom weaving is a traditional technique that requires time, patience, and a stitch rhythm that might remind you of a well-practiced drumline. Each knot contributes to the rug’s overall density, resilience, and ability to stay aligned with minimal wrinkling. The process is roughly akin to knitting a forest of little fiber triangles that lock together into a sturdy surface. It’s slower than machine weaving, but the result typically shows more depth, more texture, and fewer visible machine-made “uniformities.”

### The Fringe Factor
Fringe is not just a decorative flourish; in many handmade rugs, fringe is the last step that ensures the edge is clean and secure. BBLSM111’s fringes likely reflect the weaving technique rather than a decorative afterthought. That means you get a rug whose edges resist unraveling after a few years of vacuuming and a lifetime of foot traffic.

### Cleaning and Maintenance
Maintenance is the less glamorous side of rug ownership. Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid pulling on the fibers; you’ll want to avoid beater bars that can snag delicate silk blends. If you spill coffee or red wine, blot—don’t rub—immediately to minimize staining. For deeper cleaning, call in a professional rug cleaner who specializes in hand-knotted pieces. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the dyes or the silk blend. With proper care, your BBLSM111 should keep its color and feel for years, aging like a fine wine if the wine wasn’t spilled on it in the first place.

## Care and Longevity: Keeping the Blue Brilliant
### Routine Care
- Vacuum on low-suction with a gentle no-beater setting
- Rotate every 6 to 12 months to even out wear patterns
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and protect floors
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, white cloth; avoid rubbing
- Seek professional cleaning for tough stains or deep cleaning every few years

### When to Reconsider Size or Placement
If you notice excessive matting in high-traffic areas or feel the blue tone has shifted unusually, it may be time to assess rotation or even a re-cut edge if needed. Silk blends respond well to careful handling; they don’t forgive rough treatment like a dog with a chew toy. But with a little care, you can expect the rug to age gracefully, developing character rather than looking worn out.
