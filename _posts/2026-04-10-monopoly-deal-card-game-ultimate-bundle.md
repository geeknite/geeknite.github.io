The objective is simple and merciless: collect three complete property sets of different colors. The cards do the talking; you accumulate money into your bank, place property cards to build sets, and unleash action cards to disrupt opponents. ## Mechanics Deep Dive

### Card types explained
- Money cards: used to fund rent payments, keep your house of cards from collapsing, and quietly pretend you are not broke.
- Property cards: form sets that you need to complete, color-coded to help you pretend you know what you are doing.
- Action cards: rent charges, steal cards, demand deals, swap properties, or force other players to do your bidding. Some of these are cheeky, some are brutal, and all of them are perfectly designed to trigger a loud reaction from a table.
- Wild property cards: they can slot into any color set, saving you from color mismatches and maybe teaching you a little geometry along the way.

The synergy here is the real magic. Money funds your defense, property builds your objective, and action cards provide the tools to derail plans that someone just announced with a straight face.

### Rent and timing
Rent cards feel like the pressure cooker of the game. If you play them too early, you might waste them; if you hold them too late, someone else will drain your cash pool and leave you staring at a rainbow of color cards that you cannot complete. Timing is everything: the best players can sense two steps ahead, like chess players who also know how to roll a dice and wield a rubber chicken as a prop. The Ultimate Bundle gives you more rent cards to deploy, which is great for longer sessions, but it also increases the risk that your opponents will coordinate a counterstrike of epic proportions.

### Strategy tips
- Reserve a handful of money cards early. It pays to be able to pay rents and avoid giving away your property in contested wars.
- Use wild property to fill urgent gaps in your color sets as soon as you draw them. It buys you time and reduces the pressure to perform a miracle draw.
- Don’t overcommit to a single color set if the table is gunning for it. Diversify, then collapse the table with a well-placed rent that cripples the top player financially.
- Watch for the moment your opponents reveal a large stash of money. If you anticipate a rent spike, you can stage a careful defense or go full tilt with a steal or swap play that changes the entire board.
