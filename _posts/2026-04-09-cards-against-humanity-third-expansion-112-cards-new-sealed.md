## Gameplay and Replayability
Gameplay remains straightforward: players use white cards to fill in the blanks of black prompts. The Third Expansion adds a set of new prompts to pair with existing white cards, creating novel interactions.

- Setup: The setup remains the same, with players drawing a hand of white cards and a black card being played each round. The speed of rounds depends on your group’s familiarity with the material and frankly how quickly you recover from a failed pun.
- Card interaction: Some of the new prompts lean into topical humor and contemporary references. In practice, you’ll find that some rounds feel crisp and witty, while others feel forced. That’s not a failure of the expansion; it’s a reflection of the unpredictable nature of humor and group dynamics.
- Rounds and pacing: In a typical game, you’ll cycle through multiple rounds with the new cards. You’ll find yourself rediscovering old jokes and finding fresh angles on familiar topics. A word of caution: heavy-speed rounds can devolve into a caption contest where players shout out lines in a frenzy—this can be chaotic but also hilariously entertaining.

One helpful tip to maintain game quality: curate a moment of gold by allowing players to veto or reroll particularly weak prompts. In a few sessions, we found that a small amount of gatekeeping (without killing the vibe) improved the experience and cut down on stalling.

Additionally, the expansion works best with a diverse mix of players. It shines when people feel comfortable riffing off each other and when there’s a shared context in the room. CAH excels in that social layer the moment when someone drops a perfect line and the whole table erupts in that delicious mix of laughter and disbelief that CAH fans crave.

## The Social Experience
A CAH session is as much about the people as the cards. The Third Expansion assumes you’ve got a crew that enjoys pushing boundaries with a wink and a nod. If your party includes players who are conservative about jokes or who struggle with the concept of humor as edginess you might want to establish a few ground rules.
- Consent and comfort: Before you start consider a quick lighthearted agreement about what is off limits. No jokes that target a person identity can be a helpful baseline for many groups.
- Moderation: The expansion is best enjoyed when the players aren’t constantly scanning the deck for the most offensive option. Let the funniest card win, but don’t let the game derail into a negativity spiral.
- Turn-taking: In a larger party designate a round captain to ensure everyone gets a chance to play a card and keep the flow moving. CAH can become chaotic fast the captain helps maintain pace.

The social dynamic of CAH is what makes or breaks a night. For many, it’s a high energy late night ritual that becomes a memory anchor like a ridiculous inside joke your future self will laugh about with a tinge of embarrassment.

Here are practical tips to ensure your CAH night is memorable for all the right reasons:
