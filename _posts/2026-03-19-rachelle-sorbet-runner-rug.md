## Performance: How It Actually Performs

### Traction and Safety
In the world of runner rugs, one of the biggest concerns is curling edges and sliding legs. Sorbet uses a grippy backing and a dense pile, which reduces movement along the length of the hall. If you have a particularly boisterous dog who thinks the hallway is a sprint track, you’ll appreciate that the rug doesn’t slide when you sprint past the cat on two legs wearing slippers. ### Cleaning and Maintenance
Maintenance is the boring part that matters, so here it is: vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting; if you’re using a high-suction vacuum, be mindful of the fiber tension and the possibility of fuzz shedding. The Sorbet’s colorway helps hide the inevitable dust accumulation; a quick wipe with a damp cloth on accidental spills is usually enough to prevent permanent staining. For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution and a gentle blot rather than scrubbing will keep the colors vibrant without turning the rug into a color-swap experiment.

### Durability in Daily Life
The runner is designed for high-traffic zones in a typical home. If you’re a commuter who steps into the hallway with muddy boots after hiking trips, the Sorbet Runner will show wear over time but not in a catastrophic mess. ## Cleaning Chronicles: A Quick Reference

- Vacuum weekly with a soft-bristle attachment to avoid fuzz shedding
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent friction that can push the stain deeper
- For stubborn stains, mix a mild detergent with cold water and blot; never use hot water on color-sensitive fibers
- If you have pets: consider using a protective spray on top to reduce odors and protect color
- Consider rotating the rug every few months to distribute wear evenly if your layout allows it
