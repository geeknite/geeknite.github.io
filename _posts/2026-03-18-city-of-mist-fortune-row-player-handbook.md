## Introduction
If City of Mist is the noir-drenched jam session of myth and modernity, Fortune Row is the streetlit alleyway between two skyscrapers—the part where you realize the city has more secrets than your aunt’s attic and more secrets than your deck of character sheets can hold. In short: Fortune Row – Player's Handbook is not just a supplement; it’s a gateway drug for your evening sessions, a toolkit for explicit character mangling and glorious, chaotic storytelling. If the core rulebook gave you a mythic backbone, Fortune Row hands you a whole skeleton crew of urban legends, neon-lit factions, and plot hooks so tempting you’ll want to reroll your Shadewalker just to see how many new power dynamics you can juggle in a single session.

As with all City of Mist products, Fortune Row leans into mood, texture, and narrative weight. It isn’t a simple add-on where you get extra monster stats or a handful of new powers. No, this is a full extension that invites you to lean into the city’s past and present with more robust options for Masks, Mythos, and the kind of player-driven intrigue that makes your table feel like a late-night brainstorming session with a coffee-fueled invisibility cloak. So, grab a snack, strap in, and let’s dive into the alleyways, rooftop terraces, and haunted backrooms of Fortune Row.

## What Fortune Row Is Trying to Do
Fortune Row aims to expand the City of Mist toolkit by giving players more intimate, street-level stories—think districts within a city as micro-settings with their own moods, factions, and legend-gravitas. It’s not just more powers or more Masks; it’s braiding the city’s history into a living, breathing playground where the line between myth and memory becomes a living thread you can pull during play. The book emphasizes:

- A dense, living setting for urban legends and conspiracies.
- A suite of new Masks, Oneiroi, and Mythos hooks tailored for street-level intrigue.
- Guidance for GMs on running investigations, faction politics, and epic revelations without losing the human-scale drama.
- Mechanically integrated tools to balance speed-of-play with narrative depth—so games don’t stall while you hunt down a clue in a neon-lit back alley.

If you’ve ever wished your city had an interior designer who could also summon a thunderstorm in the middle of a stakeout, Fortune Row is basically your dream apartment building with a very loud elevator. It’s designed for groups who want their investigations to feel personal, their factions to feel alive, and their players to come away with the sense that their choices have real consequences that echo through the city’s arteries.

## Layout, Design, and Aesthetic
Fortune Row keeps the familiar City of Mist visual language intact—gloved noir, glassy skyscrapers, and the soft glow of neon that suggests a world just beyond the everyday. The physical product is well laid out, with generous margins for notes, pull quotes that tempt you to steal them for your GM screen, and a design sensibility that makes you want to annotate every page like a philosophical scavenger hunt. The art direction leans into moody street photography, with characters who could be your neighbor’s receptionist or the alley’s most persistent rumor. The result is a readable, immersive book that invites you to slow down and soak in the atmosphere before you start dissecting masks and myths.

From a practical standpoint, Fortune Row doesn’t sacrifice clarity for mood. The sections are clearly delineated, with quick-start templates for new players and a GM’s kit that makes it easy to slot in the new material without a full rehaul of your existing City of Mist campaign. If you’ve built a mundane city that occasionally erupts into mythic chaos, Fortune Row gives you the tools to do that eruption with style—and without the GM needing a drawer full of sticky notes and a metaphorical map of the city’s emotional weather.

## New Masks, Mythos, and Oneiroi
A big chunk of any Fortune Row review should focus on what it adds to the Masks system. The core conceit of City of Mist—a world where legends walk alongside ordinary people—rests on Masks and Mythos. Fortune Row expands both, with a curated lineup of local Masks that feel at home in a city-within-a-city context. Expect new limitations and benefits that are thematically tied to Fortune Row’s districts, hustlers, and underbelly myths. It also includes refinements to the Oneiroi (the dreamlike powers that connect emblems to mythic potential), giving players new lenses through which to view their powers and their origin stories. The net effect is a balance: more flavor and options, but with a design that respects the game’s rhythm so sessions don’t stall while someone explains the latest mythic taxonomy.

One of the book’s clever design moves is to present Masks that are both aspirational and dangerous. They invite players to stretch their character concepts into morally fraught territory without defaulting into “this is just the power to break locks.” Instead, Fortune Row emphasizes consequences, relationships, and the city’s moral economy. The result is a set of Masks that feel like you’ve discovered a new neighborhood in your own city—the kind that changes how you walk home at night because you know a rumor could become a clue, a clue could be a villain, and a villain could become a legend you’ll tell around a table for years.

## Investigations, Clues, and the Momentum Engine
The heart of City of Mist remains its Momentum mechanic—the GM’s push-and-pull with narrative momentum and the players’ ability to manipulate clues to reveal the city’s hidden truths. Fortune Row leans into this, giving you district-specific clue tracks, faction cues, and scenario seeds that can accelerate from a quiet stakeout to a neon-lit confrontation before you know it. It’s not about more random tables; it’s about meaningful, repeatable narrative structures you can lean on when your table demands something with a little extra bite.

The book provides a toolbox for weaving these tracks together. You’ll find templates for single-session investigations, as well as longer arcs that allow players to uncover a city-wide conspiracy that could collapse the Fortune Row district—or even the entire metropolis—under the weight of a single, carefully timed reveal. The GM guidance helps you maintain tension without twisting too hard in the direction of melodrama. It’s about balance: letting the players feel the city’s pressure while keeping the story flexible enough to accommodate unexpected choices.

## The Fortune Row Setting: Mood, Factions, and Local Color
Fortune Row isn’t a generic city district; it’s a character in its own right. The book offers a vivid palette of neighborhoods—think rain-slick alleys, rooftop markets, and a jazz club that never sleeps and always knows your name. Each locale feels distinct, with factions that feel like real players in a sprawling urban carnival of legends. The factions aren’t just obstacles; they’re potential allies, rivals, and sources of rumors that can change the course of a session in delightful ways.

As a GM, you’ll appreciate the way Fortune Row gives you both macro and micro-level hooks. On a macro scale, there are overarching themes and conspiracies that connect across the district. On a micro scale, you get micro-productions: micro-dramas, micro-scandals, and micro-relationships that make the city feel alive. It’s easy to slot these into ongoing campaigns or to drop them into one-shot adventures when your table wants a quick, mood-rich game with immediate payoff.

The aesthetic is essential here. The book leans hard into sensory detail—the smell of rain on neon, the metallic tang of a city that never truly sleeps, the texture of graffiti that tells you a story before anyone speaks. It’s not just about what happens; it’s about how it feels when it happens. If you’re a GM who loves to lounge in mood boards and soundtracks, Fortune Row gives you a treasure trove of inspiration that you can mine for weeks.

## Character Creation in Fortune Row
Character creation in Fortune Row remains faithful to City of Mist’s ethos: collaborative, myth-infused, and led by the idea that a character is the intersection of a Mask, a Myth, and the real-world identity the player wants to explore. Fortune Row adds district-level flavor to this process, offering prompts and templates that encourage players to root their Masks in the Fortuna Row setting. The results feel organic: a detective whose Mask is tied to an ancient myth about bargains, or a street artist who channels a figure from local folklore with the power to bend light.

The new options support diverse playstyles. For players who love puzzle-solving and clue-chasing, Fortune Row gives you Masks and abilities that reward careful observation, pattern recognition, and social maneuvering. For those who prefer action, the book’s suggested encounters let your characters make bold moves with high-stakes consequences. Even for players who lean toward dramatic introspection, there are pathways to explore grief, memory, and the way the city shapes you, not just the other way around.

Balance remains a design priority. While there are many delicious power fantasies here, the book also provides clear guidance on keeping play fair and fun for everyone at the table. It’s easy to slide into power-wanking if you’re not careful, but Fortune Row’s structure—immersive district vibes, meaningful choices, and consequences attached to each reveal—helps keep the play grounded and narrative-forward.

## GMing in Fortune Row: Tools and Tips
If you’re a GM who enjoys running City of Mist sessions, Fortune Row will feel like coming home to a studio apartment you always wanted: compact, thoughtfully designed, and with enough character to keep you entertained for years. The GM-facing sections are robust without being heavy. You’ll find ready-to-run arcs, seed ideas, and a structured approach to city politics that lets you scale the drama up or down depending on your table’s appetite.

One standout feature is how the book handles factions. You’ll get a credible sense of who holds power in Fortune Row, why they hold it, and what they fear most. This fuels your sessions with political tension that doesn’t rely on the GM pulling a deus ex machina out of a hat. Instead, you get a living ecosystem—one that can shift on a single setback, an whispered rumor, or an accidental discovery. The factions aren’t merely obstacles; they’re potential allies, rivals, and sources of rumor that your players can chase, negotiate with, or betray—and that choice should feel meaningful.

The session pacing guidance is another win. Fortune Row emphasizes a rhythm that preserves mystery while pushing toward a satisfying climax. It’s not about stretching investigations into eight sessions if your players prefer a brisk, tense ride; and it’s not about gaslighting your group into a 30-minute sprint if they love the slow burn. The balance is what makes the GMing approach versatile. If you’ve got a table that loves long arcs, you can lean into multi-session conspiracies. If you’re in the mood for a one-shot, you’ll find analyzable, modular chunks that slot into a single evening with grace.

## Art, Layout, and Accessibility
Beyond the narrative and mechanical contributions, Fortune Row shines in accessibility. It recognizes that City of Mist has a reputation for being crunchy and sometimes opaque to newcomers. The layout is clean, with callouts for new players, quick-start character concepts, and easy-to-dread-but-fun plot hooks. The art direction keeps the mood while avoiding feel-bad complexity: it’s evocative, not overwritten. If you’re sharing this with a new GM who’s curious about the system, Fortune Row is approachable without sacrificing the depth seasoned players crave.

Importantly, the book’s examples are varied and inclusive. It’s refreshing to see a supplement that embraces a spectrum of identities and experiences without resorting to tokenism. It feels like the designers listened to a wide community and reflected those voices in the material, which makes your table safer and more imaginative at the same time.

## Playability: Does It Add Up?
Short answer: yes. Fortune Row adds value without bloating your game. There are enough new mechanics to feel fresh, but not so many that you’ll need a mathematician to run a single session. The district-focused Masks and Mythos give you a modular toolkit that can be inserted into existing campaigns with minimal friction. If your City of Mist games have started to feel a bit same-y, Fortune Row is a thoughtful invitation to reframe what your city can be and what your characters can become within its neon-lit, myth-haunted streets.

The pacing benefits are particularly noteworthy. The book helps you orchestrate investigations that flow like a good crime novel: setup, misdirection, a critical clue, and a reveal that changes what your players believed about the city—and themselves. The momentum engine remains the spine of the experience, but Fortune Row adds new flesh and function to that skeleton, letting you twist, turn, and pivot with confidence.

## Case Studies: Scenarios You Might Run
To give you a feel for the kind of play Fortune Row enables, here are a few quick, GM-friendly scenario seeds you can lift straight into your table. They’re designed to be adaptable for both short one-shots and longer arcs, depending on how much you want to lean into the district’s myths.

### 1) The Neon Bargain
A series of disappearances trace back to a late-night pawn shop that doubles as a rumor mill. The shopkeeper claims a “price” has to be paid for each mythic favor granted—an idea that leads to morally gray choices about who at Fortune Row is willing to bargain with, and what they’re willing to give up to get what they want.

### 2) Echoes in the Rain
A flood of memories from long-dead residents starts surfacing in a district plaza after a sudden storm. The players must decide whether these echoes are traps, clues, or something more sinister—each memory revealing a responsibility the modern city owes its past, and a debt that might come due tonight.

### 3) The Clockmaker’s Guild
An ancient clock shop becomes a hub for factions, each manipulating time-related effects to suit their aims. The PCs must navigate a web of allies and rivals who swear they’re preserving the city’s future, while secretly profiting from its past misdeeds.

### 4) The Velvet Underground Rising
A theatre troupe that performs in an abandoned transit tunnel begins to reveal performances that predict real events. The show’s “prophecies” drag the party into a conspiracy that ties back to Fortune Row’s most infamous legend and the people who profit from keeping it quiet.

These seeds are not meant to be rigid. They’re designed to spark your creativity and show how Fortune Row’s materials can bloom into a living, breathing campaign with minimal setup.

## Final Verdict and Recommendation
Fortune Row is the kind of supplement that earns its keep by delivering mood, mechanics, and meaning in equal measure. It’s not just “more content”; it’s a thoughtfully designed expansion that deepens the city’s myths, enriches the Masks system with district-specific flavor, and gives GMs a reliable toolkit for weaving complex investigations with human stakes. If your table enjoys noir vibes braided with mythic commentary on modern life, Fortune Row will feel like a welcome arrival rather than a detour.

Pros:
- Rich, district-driven setting that invites personal investment and collective storytelling
- Balanced, modular additions to Masks, Mythos, and Oneiroi
- Strong GM tools for pacing, factions, and long-form arcs
- Accessible layout with clear guidance for new and experienced players alike

Cons:
- Some players expecting a simple power upgrade might feel the book leans toward narrative complexity
- A few scenarios assume a GM’s comfort with political intrigue and non-binary loyalties

If you’re on the fence, consider this: Fortune Row makes your city feel earned, not given. It gives your players a sense that the legend they’re chasing has weathered every alley and every neon reflection and that their choices will echo in the city’s myths long after the session ends. It’s not a magic wand; it’s a carefully tuned instrument that lets you compose a score for a city that doesn’t sleep.
