## Assembly: how painful is pain, really?

If you’ve assembled a desk from a flat-pack catalog before, you’ll feel right at home. The instruction manual is clear enough for a sane person to follow, but there’s always a moment of existential dread when you realize you’ve reached step 7 of 12 and you’re pretty sure you’ve already mixed up left and right rods. Don’t panic — the table is designed to be forgiving. A few tips for smoother sailing:

- Group screws by size before you start. It saves time and reduces the risk of mixing a 5mm with a 7mm head like a culinary disaster in a hardware store.
- Don’t overtighten the screws. The wood can crack if you treat it like you’re building an orbital sculpture, and nobody wants a sudden “crack heard round the living room.”
- Use a friend as a spotter to catch the rods during insertion. Gravity is not your friend here.
- Dry-fit the rods first to ensure the players aren’t playing “Where’s the ball?” all night. A little pre-check goes a long way.

## Maintenance and longevity: keep the spin alive

A foosball table is a kid and adult magnet for crumbs, soda spills, and occasional coffee misfires. Here’s how to keep your 140 cm marvel playing nicely for years:

- Wipe the playing surface regularly with a soft microfiber cloth. A quick wipe keeps the sheen and the play feel aligned, like a tiny car showroom for your desk champions.
- Lubricate the rods sparingly if they start to squeak, using a silicone spray or a light machine oil. Too much oil invites dust and a suspicious glitter on your playing surface.
- Check screws every few months. If a rod becomes a tad loose, tighten gently. This is not a medieval siege engine you’re assembling; you’re dealing with a friendly table that deserves respect.
- Use soft balls or ensure the balls aren’t bouncing into fragile objects. If your table becomes a study in kinetic chaos, you might want to move the furniture away first before a goal celebration turns into an accidental living-room demolition.

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it’s the quiet hero that keeps your foosball nights from turning into an impromptu kooky science experiment about leverage and gravity.
