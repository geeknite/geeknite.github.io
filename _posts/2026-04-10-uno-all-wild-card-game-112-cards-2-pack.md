## In the wild: setup, flow, and the All Wild twist

### Setup and quickstart

To start, shuffle the two decks together, then deal seven cards to each player. Place the remaining cards face down as the draw pile. Flip the top card to begin the discard pile. The game follows standard UNO logic with color matching and number matching; when you can’t play, you draw. The twist with All Wild is that there are additional cards and effects you’ll encounter along the way that can complicate the simple strategy you had in mind, which is part of the fun and part of the chaos.

A key recommendation for setup is to decide ahead of time whether to enable all All Wild effects from the start or to trigger them in a staggered fashion as players adjust to the new dynamics. We found that gradually introducing the All Wild effects keeps the table in smiles rather than tears during the first few rounds. ## Strategy tips and pro moves

- Keep an eye on color distribution and card flow. If you notice that your opponents are holding onto a lot of a single color, use a Wild card to shift the color to where you have stronger hand management.
- Learn the All Wild cues. They’re not all created equal, and some require players to reveal information you might be better off keeping to yourself. Reading the room is half the game, and with All Wild, it’s more literal than ever.
- Don’t reveal your last card before you’re ready. UNO is as much a social game as it is a math puzzle. If you telegraph your last move, you’ll be the target of more than your fair share of banter.
- Use house rules for pace. A clock on turns helps prevent the table from drifting into a long, contemplative debate about whether to play a Wild or a Wild Draw Four. A little time pressure can speed up rounds without killing the fun.
