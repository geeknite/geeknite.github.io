## Overview

### What is Skip-Bo, anyway?
Skip-Bo is a card game where the aim is to shed your hand faster than you can mutter the word “Uno” under your breath. You are dealt a stack of cards, and the core mechanic is simple in concept: play cards in ascending order (1 through 12) onto a build pile. You can also use wild cards (Skip-Bo cards) to stand in for any number. The thrill comes from carefully timing when to drop a 6 instead of a 9, or when to unleash a wild that wrecks your opponent’s orderly plan. ## Care, Cleaning, and Storage

### How to preserve a near-complete deck
- Keep it away from direct sunlight to prevent fading of those vivid primary colors.
- Use soft sleeves or acid-free card protectors to minimize edge wear during display or play.
- Store in a cool, dry place; humidity can warp boxes and cause the cards to stick together.
- Consider a labeled storage box with a clear window so you can admire the deck without constantly handling it (you know you’re going to crave a flip through at 2 a.m.).

### Cleaning tips for a vintage stack
Gentle is the motto. If you must remove surface dust, use a soft brush or microfiber cloth. Do not soak the cards; moisture can warp the stock or remove printed color. If a card is slightly sticky from age or handling, a light wipe with a barely damp cloth followed by air-drying can help, but you should avoid aggressive cleaning that could strip away the print. In other words: treat your 161-card wonder with the tenderness of a cat video you’ve saved for a rainy binge-watch.

## Buying Guide: How to Authenticate and Acquire

### Red flags to watch for
