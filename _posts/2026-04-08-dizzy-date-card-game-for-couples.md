## How to Play: A Quick Start Guide
### Setup
- Shuffle the 100 cards and place the deck within easy reach.
- Decide the tone for the evening: Cozy Chill, Spicy Sparks, or a mix of both.
- Each player should be comfortable with the idea that cards may spark conversation, laughter, or both.
- If you want a timer, grab a kitchen clock or your favorite timer app. Timeboxing can help keep things flowing rather than turning date night into a TED Talk.

### Turn Structure
1. On your turn, draw a card from the top of the deck.
2. Read the prompt aloud and present the card type to your partner.
3. You and your partner negotiate who answers, who does the dare, or how you will respond to the prompt.
4. Complete the prompt and move the card to the discard pile.
5. The first to 5 wins? Not necessary. The goal is 20 minutes of genuine connection and 10 minutes of laughter. If you end with a kiss on the cheek, a giggling hug, or a high five, you have achieved the minimum viability threshold.

### Card Play Variations
- Two Card Roulette: Each person draws two cards on their turn and chooses one to play. This adds a strategic element without introducing heavy gameplay complexity.
- Minute to Win: Set a two minute timer; you must complete the card in that time or swap it for a new card. The pressure adds a goofy adrenaline boost.
- Whisper Mode: Some prompts work better when whispered to a partner; try whispering a prompt and see their reaction. The point is not to make your neighbors think you are plotting a bank heist, but to enjoy the moment.
