## How to play in Spanish mode: a quick refresher

Monopoly Deal is a quick playing card game where players aim to collect a full array of property sets in different colors. The Spanish edition keeps the same structure:

- You start with a hand of five cards and draw up to five cards at the start of your turn
- On your turn you may play up to three cards from your hand, with some cards forcing plays by other players
- The rent mechanic is central; you collect rent by playing the corresponding rent cards and charging opponents to fill your sets
- You can trade, steal, or block, depending on the action cards in your hand.

The pace is brisk. Roughly 15 to 20 minutes can determine the winner in a standard two to four player game, and up to 30 minutes for larger groups. The Spanish text adds a light layer of challenge for non-fluent players, but the mechanics are explained in a way that makes sense visually on each card. It is easy to translate the core flow into a language you understand while you cunningly stall your opponents with a well-timed action card.

### How to set up and run a quick game

1) Shuffle the deck and deal five cards to each player. 2) Place the remaining deck face down as the draw pile. 3) Decide who goes first. 4) On your turn, you may play up to three cards from your hand and draw one card at the end of your turn to replenish to five cards. 5) The first player to complete a full color set captures the game victory. 

In practice, you will often start with a few cash or money cards to feed your early rent charges while you quietly plot your multi-color strategy. The Spanish edition makes it easy to say rent in the tone of a street market vendor, which surprisingly adds a layer of performance credit if you have a winning scowl ready for the final rounds.
