## Introduction
City of Mist has always been a game that wears a trench coat and tells you a joke in a smoky bar. Its neon-noir city is a playground where myth and mundane collide, where your character wears a mask to hide your true self and a secret to light up the night. Mysteries of the City is the latest expansion that purports to deepen the intrigue, broaden the toolbox for GMs, and give players more reasons to say, I know this city like the back of my hand, and also who stabbed the season's hottest weapon-wielding goblin in a bar? In this review we will tease apart what the book actually offers, what it promises, and what it mostly delivers with a wink and a grin.

## What City of Mist Is
If you have dipped a toe into the neon-tinted water of City of Mist, you know the core vibe: you are a living meme in a living city, a hero whose powers are defined by myths and legends, but your day-to-day life is a coffee-stain on the timeline of the city. The game uses a dual-truth mechanic: you navigate the world with your "Mask" (the heroic persona) and the "Mishap" (the mundane life). The interplay of mythic possibilities and the ordinary world is the engine that drives both the mystery and the comedy.

## Mysteries of the City: What's Inside
Mysteries of the City is pitched as a toolbox for GMs to run more structured investigations while still letting players improvise wildly. The book is not a strictly linear campaign; it presents a catalog of mysteries, each built around a signature urban legend, a curious crime, or a city-wide secret that threatens to spill coffee on the asphalt and ruin everyone's investigators' cover.

The product is generous with ideas: there are a dozen or so standalone mysteries, each with a short setup, a list of leads, a few sample clues, and a minimal map of locations. There are also "mystery arcs" that loop back into existing campaign threads, allowing players to pick up threads that feel personal rather than institutional. For groups that enjoy the episodic approach, Mysteries of the City is a dream; for groups that prefer a long, twisting campaign, it offers the scaffolding to string multiple cases into a city-wide conspiracy.

## Masks and Characters
### New Masks
One of the riskiest parts of expanding a game like City of Mist is risk of dilution. Mysteries of the City handles this with care, offering a handful of new masks that slot neatly into the existing character framework. They are not a radical rework; they are more like new color palettes for the same set of powers you already love. The result is a fresh wave of character possibilities without triggering the dreaded “everyone must be the same memory of the city” problem.

### Integration with Existing Characters
The expansion respects the people you already built. It includes quick-start prompts so players can retrofit their current masks and keep the narrative momentum going without a heroic graffiti-tag level of rework. If you enjoy character-driven stories, you will feel right at home; if you prefer a leaner approach, you can cherry-pick a few masks and keep your party lean and mean.

## Mechanics and Play Flow
### Investigations and Clue Types
One of the trickiest parts of City of Mist is balancing mystery momentum with character spotlights. Mysteries of the City acknowledges this and tries to fix the pacing by providing three things: tempo guidelines, scene goal cards, and GM notes on when to hand out key clues. The tempo guidelines are the most welcome addition, as they give novice GMs a built-in sense of when a scene should escalate and when it should let a character shine.

### Tempo and Scene Goals
The new scene goal cards function as little prompts that push players toward a narrative beat. You can use them to orchestrate tense family dinners, smoky noir reveals, or a rooftop chase, depending on the vibe you want to chase. They also help new GMs avoid the trap of letting-the-player-solve-it-first in a way that undercuts the suspense.

### New Clue Types
The expansion also introduces a small set of new clues that are easier to tie to narrative beats. The clues range from character-driven to location-driven to phenomenon-driven, providing a well-rounded set of hooks that can be embedded in nearly any city. If your players adore hunting for a cryptic symbol, you’ll be happy to find more of those in this book.

## Art, Layout, and Accessibility
The book continues the City of Mist tradition of high-contrast neon art and moody, rainy-city photography. The color palette is cinematic; the layout favors readable type and modular sections so GMs can thumb through quickly while juggling a dozen NPCs and a rogue's gallery of conspirators. The art doesn’t merely decorate; it helps sell mood, and Mysteries of the City leans into that with cover pieces, spot illustrations for key locations, and a handful of two-page spreads that look great at a table or in a streaming backdrop.

Accessibility is a mixed bag. The new content is well-organized, and there are many micro-scenarios that contextualize all the clues in universally understandable ways. Some of the language, however, can be a little dense for beginners, and there are a few sections where the authors assume you have a working knowledge of previous City of Mist expansion materials. If you are stepping into the city fresh, you might want to skim the author notes and then read the mysteries in reverse order to maintain momentum.

## GM Tools and Running Tips
A standout feature of Mysteries of the City is its GM toolbox. It includes ready-to-run mystery seeds, a session zero checklist tailored to noir investigations, and a "how to run a reveal without feeling like you're bending spoonfuls of light" section that is surprisingly practical.

- Mystery seeds: short prompts that can be used to seed an investigation even if you’re short on time. Perfect for one-shots or interrupted campaigns.
- Session zero: clear you up-front expectations for tone, content, and safety. The book gives you a script to check in with players about what triggers their nightmare fuel and what is deemed off-limits.
- Reveal choreography: a step-by-step approach to bringing a mystery to its satisfying culmination without deus ex machina non-sense.

In terms of balance, Mysteries of the City does not pretend to “solve” every issue with a single product. It provides tools that allow GMs to stage a well-paced arc without having to improvise from scratch every week. If your table loves long-form investigations that still leave room for character moments, this is a crown jewel; if you’re here for quick, episodic one-shots, you still can, but you might need to trim some of the longer sections.

### Sample Mystery: The Neon Locket
To illustrate flavor, we’ll walk through a sample mystery that appears in the book, The Neon Locket. This case centers on a citywide rumor about a locket that reveals the wearer’s true mythic identity under neon lights. The mystery unfolds across a handful of scenes: a street market with an enigmatic vendor, a barfight that reveals an old feud between two masks, a rooftop chase under a storm of rain and neon, and a final reveal at a mausoleum that doubles as a city archive. The puzzle lies in connecting the clues that seem disparate at first: a torn photograph, a music box that only plays in reverse, and a cryptic rhyme found in a burned-out transit station.

What you can do with this case: you can run it as a stand-alone mystery or thread it into a larger storyline about mythic factions and their control over a particular neighborhood. The case is designed to be modular: you can place clues in the locations that your party visits, and you can adjust the stakes to suit the players’ level of engagement. If your players enjoy social intrigue as much as action scenes, this mystery will feel especially satisfying because the reveal hinges on social maneuvering as much as on fight choreography.

## Design Notes and Aesthetic
Mysteries of the City benefits from a thoughtful layout that respects the player’s time. The text is broken into bite-sized sections with sidebars that provide quick-case notes, GM tips, and alternative takes for different tones. The book also includes a handful of ready-made city location cards that can be printed or used digitally, which means you can set up a session in under an hour if your players insist on riding the neon-lit chaos.

The cover art shows a silhouette masked by a shimmering veil of rain, while the interior art emphasizes the contrast between the mythic and the mundane. The aesthetic makes sense for this expansion, and it helps new players feel the mood from the first page. If you are color-sensitive or prone to dizzying layout choices, you’ll want to take your time with the PDF to avoid table glare during a late-night session.

## Comparisons and Campaign Integration
If you have a handful of City of Mist expansions under your belt, Mysteries of the City will feel both familiar and refreshingly new. It doesn’t rewrite the underlying systems, but it reframes the toolkits around the concept of investigation with more structure and more personality choices for players. In terms of value, the book sits at a reasonable price for what you get: a solid dozen long-form mysteries, a suite of micro-scenarios, new masks, and a handful of GM aids. It is not a replacement for the core rules but rather a robust extension that can be dropped into a campaign with minimal friction.

If you compare Mysteries of the City to other noir RPG expansions, the standout is how Gotham-y the content feels without leaning into trademark content. It leans into city-specific flavor and local legends rather than generic crime tropes. The result is a product that feels fresh even if you have played City of Mist for a few campaigns.

## Campaign Integration and Ongoing Play
The expansion is designed to be integrated into the existing city world rather than as a separate sandbox. The master document allows GMs to slot new mysteries into any neighborhood, so your players don’t have to travel to a new dimension to feel the thrill of a major reveal. For groups who prefer to weave in political intrigue and mythic power plays, Mysteries of the City provides enough hooks to support a long arc, with recurring factions that can become recurring nemeses or ally-fallen mentors.

If you are the GM who loves handouts, you’ll appreciate the printable location cards and the “investigation flow” cheat sheets. If your players want more direct control over the mythic identities, the new masks offer more expressiveness without requiring a major rules overhaul.

## Final Verdict
Mysteries of the City nails the balance between shape and shadow. It gives you enough structure to keep the game moving while still allowing your players to improvise, to reveal, to misdirect, and to laugh at themselves. If you want a set of mysteries that are ready to run but flexible enough to adapt to your city’s particular legends, this is the pick for you. It slots neatly between the core rules and your campaign’s big payoffs, giving you a toolbox that will keep sessions feeling fresh.
