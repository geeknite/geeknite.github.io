## How to Play: The Core Flow Without Getting Lost in the Tiny Details
The core loop remains familiar: players draw a card, decide whether to keep a card, and play cards to complete a phase. The difference with W4729 is that the path to success is a little less linear and a lot more playful. You are encouraged to think on your feet, re-evaluate your plan after each turn, and occasionally take a small risk that could yield a big payoff.

1) Draw phase: You start each turn by drawing a card from the deck or the discard pile. The discard pile adds a layer of tactical choice because sifting through the top few discarded cards can reveal a hidden opportunity or blow your current plan apart in a spectacular fashion.

2) Decide phase: You assess whether your current hand can fulfill the phase on the table. If you can, you lay down the required set or run. If you cannot, you hold and wait for new draws to jostle your situation into something workable.

3) Twist phase: When you unlock the twist cards, you gain additional options that may allow completing a phase in more than one way. This is the heart of the excitement. It rewards flexible thinking and punishes stubbornness with equal enthusiasm.

4) End of turn: Your goal is to minimize the cards you have left at the end of your turn. The more efficiently you navigate your hand, the closer you come to the finish line. The ever-present risk is a pile of cards that refuses to cooperate, causing you to slip behind a rival who seems to have drawn a memory palace full of perfect combinations.

The general strategy leans on balancing speed with reliability. If you chase high-value, complex phases too early, you may stall when the twist cards shuffle the board, giving others a clear path to victory. Conversely, if you focus solely on small, safe phases, you may let others lap you while you meander toward a slower finish. The sweet spot is a dynamic approach where you read the table, anticipate opponents’ moves, and leverage the twists to keep your options open.

## Strategy Tips: Reading the Table Like a Cool Detective
- Watch the discard pile. It tells you what your opponents are hunting for and what limbs of the phase still need to be assembled. If you spot a card that would complete a critical phase for someone, you can consider drawing from the deck to disrupt their plan, or you can simply hold back to deny them a quick win.
- Use twist cards to create multiple routes to a phase. The twist is a tool, not a trap. The best players use twists to unlock alternative completions that keep their opponents guessing and their own consistency intact.
- Plan a couple of flexible routes per phase. If one route becomes blocked by an opponent or by a lucky draw, you want an alternate path ready to deploy. This is the Markov chain of your hand, moving toward victory with style and a touch of improvisation.
- Endgame awareness matters. When a few players are within striking distance, you might want to accelerate, even if it means taking a riskier phase route. Or you can play conservatively to secure a comfortable position. The twist rewards those who can switch gears on a dime.
