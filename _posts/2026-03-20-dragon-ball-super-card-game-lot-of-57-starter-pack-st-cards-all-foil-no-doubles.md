## Care, Maintenance, and Storage Tips
A good collection starts with good storage. Here are Geeknite-approved tips to keep your 57-foil stash pristine:
- Use high-quality foil-safe sleeves and a sturdy binder with dividers to separate foil cards by archetype, rarity, or color. Foils are more prone to showing scratches, so sleeves aren’t optional.
- Store away from heat and humidity. Foil cards can warp or delaminate if exposed to sweaty tournament tables or sunlit shelves.
- Consider top-loading toploaders for your most valuable pieces if you plan to display them on a shelf or stream overlay. This adds an extra layer of protection against curious paws and cat-attack photo shoots.
- Keep a small, labeled case to hold the “extras” that don’t make it into your main deck but could be swapped in for event play or casual games.
- Periodically check the binder for edge wear. Early detection means you can replace compromised cards before their resale value erodes.

## Authenticity, Dupes, and Red Flags
With any card lot, there’s always the caveat of counterfeit concerns. Here are practical checks to keep you grounded:
- Compare foil patterns. Real foils in DBS lots typically have consistent foil distribution and color shifts. A patchy or muddy foil can be a red flag.
- Check the card stock and print quality. Look for crisp printing edges, proper alignment, and no miscuts. A blurry or off-centered print is a red flag.
- Look for misprint anomalies unique to counterfeit cards, such as inconsistent back designs or unnatural gloss.
- If the seller provides photos, compare the lot“layout” to official starter deck references. If it looks dramatically different, ask for proof of authenticity or a return policy.
