## What is Exploding Kittens: Unicorns Edition?

Exploding Kittens (original) is a dexterity of nerve and luck, where players draw cards while trying to avoid drawing an exploding kitten. The Unicorns Edition keeps the core mechanic—don’t explode, or you’re out—but adds unicorn-themed twists, new power cards, and a rainbow-colored aura that makes even the most tense draw feel like a magical pony parade. The rules are largely familiar to EK veterans: draw, skip, attack, see the future, and defuse—except now there are unicorn upgrades that can shift the tide in spectacular, sparkly ways.

Some of the standout additions include: 
- A set of “unicorn power” cards that trigger when you utter a unicorn-related pun or when a rainbow token is produced. 
- A new “glitter bomb” mechanic that changes the pace of the game, occasionally turning a tense moment into a loud, giggly disaster in the best possible way. 
- Thematic art that leans heavily into pastel gradients, starbursts, and munchkin-tier humor that caters to superfans of fantasy and puns alike.

For newcomers, the Unicorns Edition preserves the accessibility of the base game: it’s quick to learn, quick to play, and quick to forget. For veterans who’ve worn out more sleeves than a magician’s hat, the new mechanics offer fresh micro-goals, new bluffing opportunities, and the kind of visual spectacle that makes a misdraw feel like a tiny victory or a tiny catastrophe—depending on how you spin it.

If you want a digestible comparison: think of EK Unicorns as EK with a glittery side quest. It’s still about pushing your luck, but the unicorns add a layer of thematic justification for dramatic reveals and ridiculous verbal jabs. In short, this edition doesn’t replace the original; it supplements it with a level of whimsy that’s hard to resist at a party where “one more round” usually means you’re late for something important like a TV show finale or a snack sting operation.

## Rules, Gameplay, and Pacing

If you’ve played Exploding Kittens, Unicorns Edition slides into your play cadence with minimal friction. The core loop remains: draw cards until you explode or survive. The unicorn twist comes from new cards that encourage punny interactions, timely defuses, and a few “oh no you didn’t” moments that only work if you commit to a little theatricality.

Key rule changes and additions include:
- Unicorn Power Cards: These are special abilities that trigger when certain conditions are met. They can let you swap cards, peek at future draws, or force other players to reveal a hand, all under the banner of whimsy.
- Glitter Tokens: Earn or lose tokens as you play. Collect enough tokens and you unlock a “glitter cascade” moment that adds dramatic flair to the board—great for social media clips and unforgettable game nights.
- The Rainbow Deck: Some cards alter the deck composition mid-game. This keeps players on their toes and prevents the match from falling into a predictable rhythm.

Pacing is the big variable here. Unicorns Edition tends to swing on the unicorn-boosting cards more than the standard EK, which creates a different arc: a handful of quiet turns, then a burst of chaos when someone lands a unicorn power. The unpredictability is the charm, but it can also lead to moments where a single lucky draw dictates the entire table’s mood and, some nights, the entire dinner conversation. If you’re hosting a multi-hour game night, you’ll want to decide beforehand how many rounds you’re comfortable with and whether you’ll allow rewriting the rules to accommodate fun over function.

The unicorn theme is the spine of this edition. If you’re worried about theme fatigue, the Unicorns Edition manages to keep things fresh by rotating new power cards across runs, so even if you’ve played a dozen rounds, you’ll encounter something new enough to justify another session. Thematic consistency is the secret sauce here: unicorns aren’t just a cosmetic layer; they’re woven into the mechanics in ways that feel natural rather than tacked on for groan-worthy laughs.

## Strategy and Player Psychology

As with any EK iteration, strategy isn’t only about which card you play when—it’s about how you read the room and manage risk. Unicorns Edition adds a social layer you’ll either love or tolerate depending on your tolerance for theater.

- Reading the room: The unicorn tokens and power cards are as much about bluffing as they are about raw luck. You’ll notice players reacting to near-misses with a sense of destiny. If you can time your punny declaration to coincide with a risky draw, you can tilt the mood in your favor even when the odds are stacked against you.
- Card economy: The new deck elements create micro-alliances and micro-backstabs. Some players will hoard certain cards to block a specific player’s unicorn power, while others will unleash a cascade of effects to swing momentum back in their direction.
- End-game pressure: In standard EK, the last few draws can feel like a siege. In Unicorns Edition, those last few draws are often punctuated by a dramatic unicorn reveal or a glitter explosion that shifts who’s in the “last man standing” position. If you enjoy tense finales with a splash of carnival energy, this edition nails it.

Pro-tip: use the theater value of your own moves. Even a suboptimal draw can become a success if you time your commentary to maximize panic and laughter. The more you lean into the show, the longer the table stays engaged—even when the odds aren’t in your favor.

## Cards, Mechanics, and Noteworthy Interactions

The Unicorns Edition introduces several card types that alter how you approach a draw. Here are a few highlight interactions that frequently surprise players:
- Unicorn Power Cards: Triggered by pun-based or timing-based criteria, these cards can bypass a draw entirely, peek at the next few cards, or force another player to replay a hand with a twist. - Glitter Bomb Moments: When a glitter bomb occurs, the entire table experiences a shared moment of chaos. We’re talking about a cascade of silly sounds, glitter-like visuals (in your imagination, if nothing else), and a sudden shift in momentum. It’s ridiculous in the best possible way.
- Liability Cards: Some cards impose penalties or forced actions on players who previously looked too confident. This creates a feedback loop of tension and hilarity as players adjust their strategies on the fly.

If you love this kind of crunchy interaction, you’ll be delighted by how the unicorns turn everyday card effects into dramatic, narrative-rich sequences. If you dislike interruptions, you may want to embrace a stricter interpretation of the rules or implement a house rule to curb back-and-forth chaos.
