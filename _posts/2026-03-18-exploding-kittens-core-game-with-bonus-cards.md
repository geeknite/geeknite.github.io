What is Exploding Kittens Core Game with Bonus Cards
  
Exploding Kittens is a fast paced, multiplayer card game that leans into silly art, goofy puns, and a simple but brutally effective premise. You and your friends take turns drawing cards from a face down deck. Some cards are friendly, most are not. The key objective is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you draw one and you do not have a Defuse card, you are out of the round. If you do have a Defuse, you secretly plant your Defuse in your hand and continue playing. It sounds simple, and in practice it is, until folks start stacking the chaos with wild cards that can skip turns, shuffle the deck, peek at future draws, steal cards, or tell a friend to do something hilariously unfair.

In this Core Game with Bonus Cards edition, the standard deck is joined by a curated set of Bonus Cards that spice up the typical flow. These Bonus Cards tilt the odds, inject new rhythms into turns, and occasionally escalate the stakes to a glorious level of ridiculousness. If you have played the base game and are hungry for more mayhem, the Bonus Cards are a small addon that feels like a sparkly cherry on a chaotic sundae. They are not mandatory, but if you are hosting a party where chaos is the feature, they will shine.

Setup and first impressions
  
Setting up Exploding Kittens Core Game with Bonus Cards is simple enough to enjoy on a lazy evening with snacks. Shuffle the deck, deal a number of cards to each player, and place the rest in a draw pile. A few small decisions can influence the vibe of the session: do you play with the Bonus Cards enabled from the start, or do you flag a few as late game unlocks? The game supports 2 to 5 players in the core experience and can stretch to 6 or more with certain variants. The recommended player count keeps the tension high without dragging the game out into the wilderness of endless turns. The rulebook suggests a lightest version for newcomers, which is the vibe I endorse if you have people at the party who are still deciding whether choices matter at all in a game night.

The core loop: draw, react, and bluff with style
  
On a typical turn you draw a card from the deck and hope you escape an Exploding Kitten draw. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you need a Defuse card to survive. The Defuse card is not a guaranteed safe escape; you must use it with the right timing. The interplay between defusing on your own turn and bluffing on other players turns creates the core tension. A classic moment in the core game happens when someone dodges a direct threat by cunningly using an Attack or See the Future card to reshuffle the deck or peek ahead and shift the odds. The Bonus Cards amplify these moments. They may grant extra defuses, force someone else to draw, or create mini puzzles within a round. The result is energy that swings between chaotic and surprisingly clever.

Card by card: what you actually get in play
  
- Exploding Kitten: the ultimate bait card that ends your round unless you are armed with a Defuse. Drawing it feels like stepping on a Lego while wearing boxing gloves.
- Defuse: the lifeboat that saves you when the kitten makes a surprise entrance. The Defuse card can be stored in hand until needed, but you cannot use it after the kitten detonates; timing is everything.
- Skip: a simple card that lets you skip your turn, which can be valuable when you want to avoid a risky draw or force someone else into a hot seat.
- See the Future: a peek card that lets you look at the top few cards of the deck. It is a strategic tool that allows for careful planning or reckless bravado depending on the mood.
- Attack: makes the next player take two turns in a row. This card is the classic chaos catalyst, forcing someone else to bear the consequences while you enjoy a breather.
- Shuffle: forces the deck to reshuffle, changing all the odds and enabling dramatic comebacks or dramatic misreads.
- Favor: force another player to give you a card from their hand. Expect whispered negotiations and dramatic standoffs.
- Nope: the flexible counter card that can cunningly stop any action. The Nope card thrives in social deduction moments when someone tries to push a zany plan over the line.
- Cat cards: pairs and combos that yield unique effects when used together with matching cards. Cat cards are the social glue of the game that rewards memory and collaboration, even if collaboration means backstabbing in good humor.

Bonus Cards: what the expansion adds to the table
  
The Bonus Cards are the secret sauce of this edition. They inject fresh wrinkles into the core loop, like a recipe that adds a dash of hot sauce to every decision. Bonus Cards come with a few recurring themes: extra safety nets, high risk rewards, and sometimes risky penalties that are only fun if players are willing to lean into chaos. Think of them as mini side quests that appear mid game and force players to recalculate risk on the fly. They may include effects such as drawing an extra card before your turn ends, granting a temporary immunity against Exploding Kitten, or swapping tasks and hands with other players. These cards are optional, but they are easy to integrate with very little rule overhead. If you like your games with a little extra bite, you will appreciate how these bonus options subtly alter strategy and social dynamics.

Mechanics and strategy: how the game really plays out
  
Exploding Kittens thrives on timing, misdirection, and the social rapport of the table. The core decisions center around whether to play an action card on your turn or hold it for a critical moment later. The interplay between cat pairs, defuses, and the occasional bonus card shapes how you plan ahead. A few practical tips help you get more wins and fewer grudges:

- Balance aggression with patience. It is tempting to go all out and unload a flurry of actions, but your best fates often come from waiting for the right moment to strike with a well timed Attack or a counter Nope.
- Use See the Future to map the deck. When you can peek ahead, you can identify whether a big threat is near and whether you should defend, defuse, or simply bail out of the current chase.
- Manage your Defuse cards like life vests on a sinking ship. You want to hold them back for the most dangerous detonations while not completely hoarding them away from the table. A misread here can cost you two or three rounds of elimination if you run out at an inopportune moment.
- Cat card strategy matters. Cat cards reward memory and strategic pairing. If you can coordinate a pair with a friend for a beneficial effect, do it early while the table is still loose with trust. Later, you might be swapping cat cards with the same player who just stole your last good card, and the room erupts in loud laughter or mock despair.
- Bonus Card dynamics demand quick adaptation. When the Bonus Cards are in play, you must pay attention to new win conditions. Sometimes it is better to pass on an attack to cause a cascade that favors you later, other times you simply need to survive a single draw and hope the table engine breaks in your favor.
