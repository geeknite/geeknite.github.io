## How to store, protect, and display your collection

Storing and protecting MTG cards is as important as the pulls themselves because good storage extends the life of your cards, preserves your art, and keeps your investment intact. Here are some practical tips tailored to new and moderate seasoned players:

- Use solid deck boxes or 9-pocket binders for regular playing cards. This keeps your cards safe from bending and helps you organize your deck lists.
- Invest in protective sleeves for playable decks. A good sleeve can reduce wear and keeps your cards looking new after many games.
- Keep your high-value cards in a separate binder or a top loader to prevent scuffs during trades and storage.
- Label your binders by format or by the theme of the cards. This makes it easier to locate specific types of cards when you want to build something quick for a game night.
- Maintain a quick-reference list of your best cards, especially if you like to trade with friends. A little note about the card’s uses and counterplay can speed up trades and prevent miscommunication.
