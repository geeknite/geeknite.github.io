## Connectivity and Ports
Here’s where the dock earns its keep. The HP Thunderbolt Dock 120W G4 is a port-hungry device designed to consolidate a lot of what you’d otherwise connect individually. The upstream connection is Thunderbolt-based, which is the true ninja move here: one cable to handle video, data, and power delivery (PD) in a neat, single-wire experience.

- Upstream: 1 Thunderbolt 3/4 port (for the host laptop). That one port is the highway, and your laptop will be the vehicle.
- Video outputs: The dock typically presents multiple video outputs—DisplayPort and HDMI options—to drive one or two displays depending on your host capabilities. In practice, you can drive two 4K displays at 60 Hz or a single high-refresh 4K monitor, often with a fallback to 1080p if you need it. The exact combination of HDMI/DisplayPort ports is designed to accommodate common professional setups without forcing you into a single display lane.
- USB ports: Expect several USB-A 3.2 Gen 2 ports and at least one USB-C data port. The USB-C downstream port is handy for external SSDs or other peripherals that love USB-C’s speed without stepping into the USB-A lane. The exact count is generous enough to keep your keyboard, mouse, webcam, and a couple of flash drives happy at the same time.
- Ethernet: A gigabit Ethernet jack is not optional in a real-world desk scenario. Cable-laden offices and coworking spaces still need reliable network performance, and HP hasn’t forgotten to tuck Ethernet in for stable, high-speed connectivity.
- Audio: A 3.5mm combo audio jack or line-out for headsets ensures you can jump into calls or jam along with your favorite productivity playlist without hunting for adapters.
- Power delivery: The 120W PD is not just about keeping your laptop alive; it’s about maintaining your productivity rhythm. If you are running a typical ultrabook or workstation-class laptop, 120W is usually enough to charge while you work, even with a few peripherals pulling power.
