## What is City of Mist and why a Storyteller's Guide PDF?
City of Mist is a tabletop RPG built around the idea that ordinary people become living legends through mythic alter-egos called Mysts. Those Mysts mingle with mundane life in a modern city that is part crime-noir, part pulp fantasy, and totally soaked in neon vibes. The core rulebook gives you the mechanics to resolve actions, but the Storyteller's Guide is where the game-specific GM (Game Master) tools live. It’s the “how to make a game about legends feeling like a city you could walk to on your lunch break.” The PDF edition consolidates guidance that might otherwise have lived across forum posts, scattered PDFs, and your own sticky-notes on a napkin in a coffee shop; it’s the one-stop shop for running a consistent, mythically infused campaign.

What you get in a nutshell:
- A clear framework for Storyteller duties: pacing, danger, and the balance between mystery and myth.
- Mechanic-oriented tips tailored to City of Mist’s “mythic identity vs. mundane world” dichotomy.
- Tools for building scenes, encounters, and investigations that feel cinematic but still playable.
- Ready-to-use NPCs, factions, and mythos hooks to seed your sessions without hours of prep.
- Advice on voice, tone, and safety in a game about power, identity, and manipulation by the Mist itself.

If you’re coming from a more traditional RPG background, you’ll notice a few differences: the Guide leans into story over crunch, emphasizes investigation and character-driven drama, and expects you to juggle multiple myths colliding in everyday spaces. It’s not about adding more rules to memorize; it’s about giving you robust high-level tools to steer the ship when your players start arguing whether a villain is a god, a person, or a combination of both.

## What’s inside: storytelling tools and GM techniques
Here’s where the Guide earns its keep. City of Mist thrives on the tension between the Mist’s mythic pull and the players’ everyday choices. The Storyteller's Guide treats that tension as a toolkit rather than as a mystery you must solve in the dark of night.

### Identity, Masks, and the city as a character
A central concept in City of Mist is that people carry two identities: their mundane self and their Mythos-driven self. The Guide provides exercises to help you translate that dichotomy into scene objectives. For example, a scene can begin with the detective’s ordinary goal (find a missing person) and then pivot toward a mythic constraint (the person’s mythic identity compels them to resist being found, because discovery threatens their role in a larger story).

In practice, this means you’ll want to design encounters where a character’s Mist-driven impulse coincides with the investigation’s needs. The Guide offers practical prompts that push both players and GM to think in terms of identity conflict rather than purely mechanical outcomes. It’s not about giving every character a flashy power; it’s about making their inner myths drive the story in interesting, surprising ways.

### Scene design and pacing
One of the trickier aspects of City of Mist is keeping the pacing tight while still letting mysteries breathe. The Guide provides a library of scene templates you can reuse and adapt. You’ll find structured beats—for example, a scene can have a Hook, a Revelation, a Challenge, and a Consequence—and you’ll also find more flexible templates for investigations whose outcomes hinge on a single choice. The balance here is to maintain forward momentum while giving players choices that genuinely matter.

The PDF also gives you guidance on how to escalate tension: how to introduce complications without derailing the session, how to misdirect players without feeling unfair, and how to reveal twists that feel inevitable in hindsight rather than forced in the moment.

### Encounters, NPCs, and factions
NPCs in City of Mist are often more than they appear. The Guide includes archetypes you can customize: a corrupt city official, a street artist with a secret, a corporation villain, or a mythic watcher who exists just out of sight. Each template is designed to be seeded into a session and then adapted as the story unfolds. Factions get similar treatment with vibe, goals, and complications that can clash or align with players’ interests.

This is useful because it helps you avoid NPCs that feel like “one-note plot devices.” When you have maintainable templates, you can generate meaningful interactions on the fly, which is perfect for improv nights or sessions where players decide to chase a rumor that wasn’t on your original outline.

### Tools for safety, tone, and player experience
City of Mist often deals with mature themes—identity, power, corruption, mythic manipulation. The Guide’s safety tools are practical: lines and veils, a clear consent approach, and guidance on setting a tone that suits the table. It’s not moralizing; it’s about making sure everyone has a good time while exploring heavy topics. That combination—ambition with empathy—defines the best moments of misty storytelling, and the Guide does a credible job scaffolding it.

## From pages to play: practical advice for sessions
If you want to translate theory into practice, here are the key takeaways you’ll actually use at the table.

- Start with a cityscape that feels alive: a place where every street corner could hide a myth or a memory of a ruined legend.
- Anchor investigations with a mythic prompt: what does the city need to learn about itself, and which Mask is obstructing that truth?
- Use identity-driven obstacles: not every obstacle should be defeated by brute force; sometimes the obstacle is a choice you must make that reveals who you really are.
- Build scenes around the Mist’s influence: the Mist isn’t a passively ambient fog; it can grant opportunities or distort information in a way that reveals a character’s true motive.
- Prepare flexible encounter seeds: have a few generic seeds that can be matched to different investigators’ Mythos, which lets you respond to player actions in real time.
- Prioritize tone consistency: City of Mist thrives on noir with a mythic underlayer. The Guide helps you keep this balance even when PCs push boundaries or when chaos erupts mid-session.

### Sample scene seeds
The Guide sprinkles ready-to-run seeds you can drop into your campaign. Here are a couple reimagined for a modern city where every myth breathes through street art and subway whispers:
- The Painted Oracle: A mural seems to whisper advice to anyone who looks at it long enough. The advice sometimes helps, sometimes warns of a looming disaster. The mystery is whether the mural is a sentient artifact or a social phenomenon amplified by the Mist.
- The Quiet Architect: A construction site becomes the stage for a mythic conflict as a god of ruin tries to reconstruct a city block into a sacred ruin. The investigators must navigate corporate interests, city zoning laws, and ancient charisma to stop a catastrophe that would upend the city’s identity.
- The Masked Choir: A group of performers with hidden identities uses a public ritual to draw out the city’s biggest secrets. The investigators need to uncover who they are, why they’re attracting attention, and whether their ritual is a force for healing or a weapon of chaos.

Each seed is designed to be adaptable. You can push the theme toward mystery, action, or tragedy depending on your table’s preferences, and the Guide nudges you toward a satisfying convergence of character arcs and plot developments.

## The art of tone and safety
If there’s one area where the Guide shines, it’s the emphasis on tone and player safety without feeling like you’re reading HR guidelines during a game night. The intent is not to sanitize the darker aspects but to keep a boundary where players feel free to explore dramatic material without crossing into discomfort. That said, the Guide doesn’t pretend to solve every ethical debate you’ll encounter at the table. It simply gives you the language and structure to navigate those waters—so when the Mist tempts a character with power or a difficult choice, you can handle the moment with clarity and care.
