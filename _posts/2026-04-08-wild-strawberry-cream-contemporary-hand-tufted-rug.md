### Materials and construction: what’s under the surface

This rug is a hand tufted piece, which typically means a skilled artisan uses a tufting gun to push yarns through a fabric backing, creating a dense, durable pile. Hand tufting is a middle ground between hand-knotted luxury and machine-forged practicality. It offers a robust construction with the potential for sizable pile height variations and a strong sense of texture. In the case of the Wild Strawberry Cream, you’ll enjoy a pile that reads plush underfoot but remains easy to clean—an important factor in spaces with foot traffic, pets, and occasional snack mishaps during game night.

The base material is designed to be durable enough for daily living, with a backing that helps the rug maintain its shape over time. While you should still rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear, the tufted construction tends to resist crushing better than lower-pile, low-friction varieties. ## Cleaning and care: keeping the dream pristine

Cleaning a hand tufted rug doesn’t have to feel like a black belt in domestic warfare. The general approach is straightforward: regular vacuuming with a soft-bristle attachment, prompt blotting of spills, and periodic professional cleaning for deep maintenance. For daily care, avoid aggressive vacuum settings that could snag the pile or pull the yarns at the tufted seams. A gentle pass or two across the surface is usually enough to lift surface dust and keep the fibers looking fresh.

Spill management is where you’ll want to react quickly. Blot, don’t rub, with a clean cloth or paper towel to prevent liquids from wicking deeper into the pile. If the spill is something stubborn—think red wine or coffee—consider a mild, rug-safe cleaning solution following the manufacturer’s guidelines, or consult a professional cleaner who specializes in tufted textiles. Always test any cleaning method on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.

From a maintenance perspective, rotating the rug every few months helps even wear and color distribution, especially in high-traffic areas. If you’ll be moving furniture around or rearranging the room, lift items rather than dragging them across the surface to minimize edge fraying and pile deformation.
