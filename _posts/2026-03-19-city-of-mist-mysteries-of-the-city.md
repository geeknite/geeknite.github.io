City of Mist: Mysteries of the City is not just a supplement, it's a mood. It's the kind of thing that makes you want to wear a trench coat indoors, sip coffee with a straw, and mutter about private eyes who are also gods. In this Geeknite review, we dive into what Mysteries of the City adds to the core City of Mist system, how it handles noir vibes, and whether it actually helps your group tell a story. If your last game felt like a sprint that's missing the finish line, Mysteries of the City invites you to slow down and savor the reveal.

## Introduction
If City of Mist is about legends in the modern city, Mysteries of the City leans into investigations rather than splashy fight scenes. The book pushes players toward clue-chasing, motive spotting, and the idea that the city itself has motives. It rewards players who listen to street corners, coffee cups, and neon signs more than those who carry a big gun in broad daylight.

## What you get
Mysteries of the City comes with numerous investigations, new masks, ready-to-run mysteries, GM tips, and a toolbox of assets to keep players guessing. The art direction continues with neon noir and surreal myth. New assets include investigation templates, scene prompts, and a playlist of prompts that can complement your mood.

### Visuals and layout
The layout is clean, typography is legible, and the interior art adds flavor. The cover image evokes rain-slick streets, and the interior art shows enigmatic runes in coffee stains and masked figures swapping hats. It’s a package that rewards reading slowly and allowing clues to sink in.

### Mechanically speaking
In City of Mist, you play Masks—public personas—paired with Logos and Mythos. Mysteries of the City adds new Masks and power interactions that fit the core system. New mechanics emphasize investigation, social cues, and the clever use of the city as a character. Combat is not the default focus; the fun comes from connecting clues to the citys legends and watching the mythic structure unfold.

## System overview
If you are new to the system, think of City of Mist as jazz noir: familiar building blocks with surprising timing. Masks define how others see you; Logos power your abilities with a cost; Mythos are the forces driving the plot.

### Masks, Logos, and Mythos
- Masks define public persona
- Logos are cities myths realized through powers
- Mythos are the citys forces that push the story

Mysteries of the City introduces more Masks and more prompts, letting you mix and match with existing options for a blended experience.

## Thematic fit: noir and myth
Mysteries of the City is built for players who want myths embedded into everyday life. The subway becomes a temple; a laundromat becomes a sanctuary; neon signs are street-level omens. The tone guide encourages personal stakes and city-driven myth, not huge, world-shaking battles.

### Word on pacing
The book favors investigation and discovery over constant combat. If you love the moment of catching a thread that leads to an emotional reveal, you will enjoy the pacing. If you want a page filled with goblins and magic swords, you may be disappointed but perhaps you should try a different system because this one is not about that.

## Play experience: running a session
Running this book feels like conducting a small ensemble of noir-fueled mythic investigators. The GM kit provides prompts, NPC hooks, and scene anchors. The players bring their Masks and Logos and step into a city that responds to their choices. We ran a three-session arc with two intertwined mysteries. The clues encouraged players to discuss, debate, and sometimes roleplay the emotion behind the motives. The result was not a solve-everything sprint but a believable unraveling of a myth that had crept into ordinary life.

### GM tips
- Treat the city as a character with a voice. Describe weather, smells, and street music to set tone.
- Tie clues to Masks. People react to your public persona in ways that can create dramatic irony.
- Use the Logos as recurring motifs. A logo that reappears can signal a looming revelation.
- Keep it flexible. The book offers modular blocks you can rearrange to suit your table.

### Scene prompts
The chapter includes ready-to-run scenes with maps and NPCs. One scenario involves a subway tunnel that doubles as a corridor for a mythic under-city. Another has a mysterious cafe where coffee beans contain whispers from the citys past. Use these prompts as a starting point and then improvise.

## Artwork and production quality
The art direction stays on brand: moody lighting, stylized portraits, and panels that feel like a graphic novel. The layout is readable, with clear section breaks and helpful callouts. If you gather glossaries or a playlist, they will feel at home here. The production value makes it a book you’ll want on your shelf as a reminder of the mood it creates.

## Pros and cons
Pros:
- Thematic coherence that nails noir-myth hybrid
- Flexible investigations that scale to one-shot or long arcs
- Strong artwork and design

Cons:
- Dense reading; newcomers may need slow skimming or time to digest
- If you prioritize combat, the book nudges you toward other options

## Final verdict
Mysteries of the City succeeds in exporting the mood and the toolkit for mythic investigation. It isn’t a single ready-to-run adventure, but a blueprint for urban myth storytelling. If you want a game that rewards players for asking the right questions and paying attention to the citys hints, this book is for you. If you crave constant combat or a tightly scripted campaign, you may want to borrow a page from another game.

If you’re newer to the system, you’ll find the book a bit intimidating at first glance—but that intimidation is a feature, not a flaw. It invites you to lean into the city’s textures, to think about how a mythic power might influence a run-in at a coffee joint, and to accept that some clues lead to more questions than answers. The narrative payoff comes from the players’ ability to connect the clues, their masks, and the citys mottled past into a resolution that feels earned rather than handed to them. Publication quality is high; layout remains reader-friendly, and the design language is consistent with other City of Mist products. If you’ve enjoyed previous City of Mist supplements, you’ll feel right at home; if you’re new, prepare for a delicious learning curve.

## Recommendation
- Best for groups that enjoy noir-inspired mythic investigations, character-driven stories, and a city that feels alive with myth
- Good for players who want to build characters with complex public personas
- Great for GMs who enjoy modular design and a toolbox rather than a scripted path
