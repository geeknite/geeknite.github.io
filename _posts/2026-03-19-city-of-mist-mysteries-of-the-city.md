## Introduction
Mysteries of the City isn’t selling action figures; it’s selling a mood—the kind of mood that makes you consider wearing a trench coat indoors, sip coffee through a straw, and mutter about private eyes who are gods in discount disguise. The book leans into investigations, secrets buried in everyday spaces, and the feeling that the city itself is a living, talking suspect. The review that follows treats this supplement as both toolbox and invitation: a guide to weaving urban myth into mundane errands, and a toolkit for turning a coffee run into a doorway to a legend.

Mysteries of the City also comes with a generous helping of GM guidance, ready-to-run investigations, and a healthy array of prompts that can jump-start a session. If your group tends to treat the city as background texture, this book dares you to listen closer—the streetlight tick, the subway rumble, the murmur of a city that remembers every mistake you’ve ever made.

## What you get
Mysteries of the City is part toolbox, part mood board, and part manifesto for investigators who believe that every corner holds a story. Here’s the expanded breakdown of what’s inside, how it fits with the core City of Mist system, and why you might want to slot it into your next table:

### New Masks and power interactions
The supplement broadens the public personas players can embody, introducing a handful of Masks that feel at home in noir-drenched settings. These Masks aren’t just cosmetic labels; they bring structural changes to how a character interacts with Logos and Mythos. Expect tighter links between a Mask’s public personae and the city’s hidden legends, plus some fresh power stunts that reward clever social play and clue-chasing over brute force.

### Investigations as the spine
Where the original book leaned toward mythic framing and mood, Mysteries of the City leans into investigations as the spine of play. You’ll find ready-to-run investigations that emphasize clues, motives, and the city’s own influence on outcomes. The investigations are modular, allowing groups to braid multiple cases into a single arc or run them as tight one-shots with a noir-tinged finish.

### GM tools and scene prompts
GM-facing material is the backbone here: scene prompts, encounter hooks, and “city-as-entity” prompts to keep players aware of the world’s response to their actions. The book nudges GMs to describe weather, sounds, and little city cues that make the urban environment feel alive rather than decorative.

### Assets for mood and pacing

## Mechanically speaking
City of Mist runs on Masks, Logos, and Mythos. Mysteries of the City expands these pillars with new options and refined interactions that support investigation-driven play.

### Masks, Logos, and Mythos, revisiting core ideas
- Masks: Public personas that shape social responses and narrative threads.
- Logos: Powers tied to city myths, now with new angles for investigative play.
- Mythos: City-drawn forces that push the plot forward and trigger thematic revelations.

Mysteries of the City adds more of each, designed to braid together with existing Masks and Logos so that players can weave a richer web of relationships and motives around the city itself.

### A note on combat and emphasis
The system still favors investigation, deduction, and social maneuvering over endless combat. You’ll find more opportunities to leverage environment, symbolism, and public perception as you close in on the city’s mysteries. If your taste leans toward punchy fight scenes, this supplement still offers satisfying moments—but the joy lies in following clues rather than stacking punch cards.

### How the city becomes a character
City-as-character design remains a highlight. The city has moods, textures, and contradictions that players can exploit or misread. When you describe a setting—say, a laundromat that doubles as a shrine—you give players a foothold to infer meaning from mundane details. Mysteries of the City doubles down on this approach by offering prompts that link physical spaces to myths and public perceptions.

## System overview
If you’re new to City of Mist, picture jazz noir in a modern city: familiar building blocks with surprising timing. Masks define how others see you; Logos empower your abilities with costs that encourage clever storytelling; Mythos are the city’s underlying forces, pushing the plot in unexpected directions.

### Masks, Logos, and Mythos (expanded)
- Masks: Public personas with distinct social responses and narrative hooks.
- Logos: City-myth powers that propagate through scenes, often with price paid in narrative or resource costs.
- Mythos: The city’s narrative gravity, drawing players toward revelations and ironic outcomes.

Mysteries of the City adds more Masks that fit naturally into noir investigations, along with new prompts that let you mix and match with existing options for a blended experience.

## Thematic fit: noir and myth
Mysteries of the City is built for players who want myths threaded through everyday life. The subway becomes a temple, a laundromat a sanctuary, neon signs street-level omens. The tone guide nudges you toward personal stakes and city-driven myth rather than big, world-shaking battles. It’s a playground for players who want to feel like their choices matter in a city that remembers every rumor and every misstep.

### Word on pacing
The book favors investigation and discovery over constant combat. If you savor the moment of catching a thread that unravels a deeper truth, you’ll love the pacing. If you’re hunting goblins and magic swords, you may be disappointed—but that’s a feature here, not a bug. The city’s rhythms determine the tempo, and the best runs ride those rhythms to a satisfying emotional close.

## Play experience: running a session
Running Mysteries of the City is like conducting a small ensemble of noir-fueled mythic investigators. The GM kit includes prompts, NPC hooks, and scene anchors that help you orchestrate a narrative without forcing it. Players bring their Masks and Logos, and the city responds—sometimes with sarcasm, sometimes with a whisper of inevitability.

### A three-session arc, reimagined
For our test run, we braided two investigations into a thematic arc: a rumor about a missing courier who delivered messages that changed minds, and a second thread about a cafe whose beans carry memories of forgotten deals. The first session established the detectives’ public personas and the city’s mood; the second spun the clues into a web connecting a streetcorner prophet, a bank heiress, and a subway tunnel with a mythic under-city. By the end, players didn’t just solve a mystery; they confronted a myth that had insinuated itself into daily life. The pacing rewarded discussion, deductions, and emotional resonance over bluster.

### GM tips (expanded)
- Treat the city as a character with a voice. Describe weather, smells, street musicians, and the way a passerby looks at your Mask. Small sensory details create a chorus that supports the mystery.
- Tie clues to Masks. Public personas shape how information is offered or withheld; a well-timed reveal can tilt a social encounter into dramatic irony.
- Use the Logos as recurring motifs. A logo that reappears can signal a looming revelation, a misdirection, or a twist you didn’t anticipate.
- Let Mythos influence pacing. The city’s legends aren’t just backdrop; they can activate in response to player choices, pushing the team toward a narrative payoff at just the right moment.
- Maintain flexibility. The modular blocks in the supplement let you rearrange scenes, swap motives, or substitute NPCs without losing momentum.

### Scene prompts (sample)
- The Whispering Café: A coffee joint where beans whisper fragments of the city’s past to those who listen closely. A regular claims the beans taste like a memory that isn’t theirs. The players must determine whether this is a harmless performance or a breadcrumb to a larger truth.
- Subway Under-City: A tunnel beneath the city becomes a corridor to a mythic underworld, where commuters vanish into legend. The party must discern which myths are genuine warnings and which are red herrings designed to derail them.

Use these prompts as a starting point, then improvise. The joy is in how players react to the city’s nudges and how their Masks color those reactions.

## Case studies: mini-arc outlines
- Arc A: The Memory Market
  - Hook: An underground market trades in memories, and a familiar face offers to buy a memory that doesn’t belong to them.
  - Clues: A playlist of songs that unlock a forgotten neighborhood; a masked vendor who knows too many city legends; a ledger that tracks memories, not money.
  - Revelation: The city’s memory network was manipulated to suppress a rival faction’s uprising.
- Arc B: The Café with Whisper Beans
  - Hook: A café’s beans whisper to patrons about their regrets—some memories come true, others dissolve.
  - Clues: A barista with a face that isn’t their own; a label on a bag that points to a mythic origin; a customer who is both deceased and present in the room.
  - Revelation: A living myth uses the café as a sanctuary to rewrite the past, and the players must decide whether changing history is worth the price.

## House rules and optional tweaks
- Calm vs. Tension Mode: Introduce a setting pair where investigators can switch between social investigation and mythic tension without breaking pace.
- Memory Economy: If you want a more resource-driven feel, treat Logos points like memory credits you spend to reveal a clue or alter a scene’s mood.
- City Agendas: Give the city a short-term agenda each session that can align or clash with the players’ goals, forcing them to negotiate with the urban environment.

## Final thoughts and recommendations
Mysteries of the City succeeds in exporting the mood and toolkit for mythic investigation. It isn’t a single ready-to-run adventure, but a blueprint for urban myth storytelling that invites groups to slow down, listen closely to the city, and follow the clues where they lead. If you want a game that rewards careful questions, patient roleplay, and a city that feels alive enough to gossip about your party, this book is a strong addition to your shelf. If you crave constant, bone-crunching combat or a highly scripted campaign, you may want to borrow a page from another game or stick with the core system and add these mysteries as flavor rather than backbone.

If you’re new to the City of Mist ecosystem, take your time with Mysteries of the City. It’s designed to reward a slower burn, a careful ear for the city’s whispers, and a willingness to let clues compound into a satisfying, earned revelation. For veteran Mist players, you’ll find new masks, sharper prompts, and a revitalized sense of how to tell a story of people, masks, and the city’s own mythic heartbeat.

- Great for players who want to craft and live with complex public personas
