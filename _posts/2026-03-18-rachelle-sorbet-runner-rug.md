## Cleaning and maintenance
A quick vacuum once or twice a week keeps the surface free of pet hair and dust. Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth, then cleaned with a mild detergent solution. Do not rub vigorously, and avoid hot water that could shrink fibers slightly. For deeper cleaning, you can remove the rug and wash on a gentle cycle if the manufacturer guidelines permit; otherwise, spot cleaning is the safe route. Because polypropylene dries quickly, you will seldom be waiting for the rug to air out.

### Stain resistance
The polypropylene pile resists most common kitchen spills and pet accidents. Ink, tomato sauce, coffee—these are all manageable with prompt action. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade fibers or alter color.

### Colorfastness and fade
In typical indoor daylight, Sorbet hues hold well for several years with standard exposure. Direct long-term sun exposure can lead to slight color fading; rotating the rug or using window treatments can mitigate this.

## Durability and performance
The 2 ft by 6 ft size is a classic hallway runner. If your hallway is slightly longer or shorter, you can customize via a cut-to-fit approach with a reputable rug installer or by pairing with a non-slip pad to adjust the length visually.

The backing's micro-grip helps keep it in place on smooth floors; on rough surfaces, you may need an additional pad for extra security.
