## The Basics

### What is Atari Centipede Board Game?

The Atari Centipede Board Game is a cooperative strategy game based on the iconic 1981 arcade game, Centipede. Designed by Jon Gilmour and Nicole Kline, this game brings the fast-paced action of the original to a new medium. Players team up to defend their garden from the relentless march of centipedes, spiders, and other creepy crawlies.

### Components

The game comes with a beautifully designed board depicting a garden under siege, detailed miniatures of centipedes, spiders, and other pests, and various cards and tokens to manage the game's mechanics. The artwork is a delightful throwback to the original game's pixel art, and the components are sturdy and well-made, ensuring the game will withstand many intense sessions.

## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setup is straightforward and quick. Players choose their characters, set up the board with the initial wave of pests, and draw their starting hands of action cards. The objective is to work together to eliminate the pests and protect the garden.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, players can move, attack pests, and use special abilities to clear the garden. The pests move automatically, following simple AI rules that mimic the behavior of their digital counterparts. The centipedes move in a zigzag pattern, while spiders and other pests add unpredictability to the game.

### Coordination and Strategy

The game emphasizes teamwork and strategic planning. Players must coordinate their actions to effectively manage the swarm of pests, protect key areas of the garden, and avoid getting overwhelmed. Communication is key, and the game provides plenty of opportunities for clever tactics and epic saves.

### Replayability

The game boasts high replayability, thanks to its variety of characters, random pest movements, and different board setups. Each session presents new challenges and requires fresh strategies, ensuring that players will return to the Atari Centipede Board Game time and time again for more action-packed fun.
