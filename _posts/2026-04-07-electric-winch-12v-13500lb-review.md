### Safety first
Before anything else, disconnect the vehicle battery. I know, I know, it’s the internet, and the phrase “safety” is a vibe killer, but this is a high-kinetic-energy device. Gloves, eye protection, and a clear work area are non-negotiable. If you’ve never cranked something that could literally drag a friend out of a ditch, you’re in for a learning curve.

### Setup tips
- Mounting: Most 4-bolt bumpers or winch plates will do, but verify the bolt pattern and chassis rating. This beast isn’t shy about weight or torque, so you want a solid platform.
- Wiring: Use a sturdy power source; heavy-gauge cables are your best friend. Don’t pretend the picture of a thin wire will do the job.
- Remote pairing: The wireless remote is convenient, but test it from various angles to ensure you’re not playing the stuck-in-mud version of “Where’s Waldo?”
- Rope maintenance: Steel rope looks tough until you snag a branch and realize you have a thousand sharp little friends. Inspect for kinks, broken strands, or corrosion.

## What to look for when buying a winch
- Mounting compatibility: Make sure your bumper or plate is rated for the weight and mounting pattern.
- Gear ratio and line pull: Higher line pull with a reasonable ratio ensures a strong torque-to-load relation.
- Rope material: Steel vs synthetic rope trade-offs—steel is durable but heavier; synthetic is lighter but needs more careful handling.
- Waterproofing and closing: A good IP rating and sealed components matter when you’re using the winch in rain or mud.
- Remote reliability: Wireless remotes are handy, but always have a cable backup in case the remote battery dies.
