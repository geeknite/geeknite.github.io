## Practical Painting and Hobby Tips
If you’re a hobbyist who enjoys the process as much as the outcome, here are a few tips to make the most of this sealed box once you decide to crack it open (or if you’re collecting for display):

- Prepare a painting plan: identify which miniatures you want to foreground and what color schemes feel thematically cohesive with the Black Plague aesthetic. Medieval armor tones, weathered leathers, and subtle blood-wine red accents can create striking silhouettes on the table.
- Check for mold lines and flash: older gold standard CMON sculpts often have fine mold lines. A gentle file or hobby knife can take care of them without sacrificing detail.
- Primer choice matters: spraying from a reasonable distance with light coats preserves surface detail and avoids primer pooling in recesses. A gray or black primer typically works well for metallic finishes.
- Base basing ideas: add a splash of narrative by basing bases with swampy textures, cracked stone, or ash-like ground to echo the plague ambiance. It’s amazing what a little texture paste can do to a hero’s presence on the board.
- Keep your tokens organized: since this is a sealed set, you’ll want a tidy method to store the tokens so you don’t spend half your session hunting for the correct one. A resealable tackle box or an inkjet-created tray can save you precious seconds and reduce table chaos.
