## Performance and gaming in the wild

Let’s crack open the numbers, but with the caveat that your mileage may vary based on your cooling solution, firmware version, and whether you’re using the box to its fullest or simply as a daily driver for office tasks.

### CPU heavy tasks

In multithreaded workloads, the i9-8950HK can deliver respectable results for a laptop-class chip inside a mini PC. In Cinebench-like workloads, you’ll see multi-core scores in the neighborhood of a few thousand points, with single-core around the low hundreds. Those numbers translate into snappy app launches, quick encoding for light editing, and a surprisingly capable experience when you’re running multiple applications at once. If you’re into content creation on the go, this mini PC won’t replace a high-end workstation, but it will handle 1080p video editing and basic 4K transcoding without complaining. It’s the kind of machine you could bring to a coffee shop and pretend you are a wily software artisan rather than a parent who forgets to charge the laptop.

### Gaming tests at 1080p and 1440p

Out of the box, the integrated UHD 630 is not a gaming juggernaut. It can handle light esports titles at 1080p with frame rates that are respectable, but to play the newest triple A games at high settings you’ll want to attach a discrete GPU or rely on an external GPU enclosure.

In our tests with a midrange discrete GPU attached, we observed 1080p gaming at high detail in a handful of titles, averaging 60-100 FPS depending on the title. Lighter games ran well at even higher frame rates, while some demanding titles required lowering settings to maintain comfortable temperatures and noise levels. The 1 TB NVMe drive makes loading times feel breezy, which is crucial when you’re chasing opponents or simply loading a big map. If your game library includes more indie titles or well-optimized hits from a few years ago, this mini PC becomes a formidable living room companion that can handle a coffee table, a big screen, and your questionable skill at precision aiming.

## Thermals, acoustics, and living room practicality

The i9-8950HK is designed for performance, not for whisper-quiet operation in a 1-foot-wide chassis. In our testing scenarios, the fans spin up when you push the CPU past comfortable thresholds, especially during gaming or long encode tasks. The noise is not a shriek; it’s a steady spaceship hum that you quickly forget after you switch to headphones. In everyday use—web browsing, video streaming, and office tasks—the machine tends to run cool and almost silent. This dual personality is exactly what you want in a mini PC you’ll place under your TV or on a desk in a small apartment. It offers enough cooling to keep the CPU from choking on its own ambition, while not turning your living room into a wind tunnel.

If you plan to game for longer sessions, consider a better cooling plan (a fanless mode is possible in some models, but you’ll want the fans for sustained performance). If the venue is a living room, you'll appreciate the ability to tuck the mini PC away behind a monitor or on a shelf.
