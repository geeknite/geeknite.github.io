Fortune Row strides into City of Mist like a mug of coffee at 6 am on a Monday: a little too much swagger, a little too much risk, and a suspiciously heavy deck of cards. If you want a playbook that makes luck feel tangible, this is your lane. Welcome to a deep dive into the Fortune Row playbook, a package deal that leans into the chaos of chance and the glamour of street-level mysticism. This is not your typical hero story; this is what happens when the city’s luck table is rigged by a cabal of grifters and mystic shopkeepers who know that fate has a sense of humor (and a sharp eye for your mistakes).

## Overview

City of Mist, if you’re not already aware, situates superheroes as myths in the modern world. The city is a living comic book, and ordinary folks with extraordinary stories become the Wind, the Ink, and the Glass when the Mythos leans in and whispers, “You, friend, have a story to tell.” Fortune Row fits into that architecture as a playbook that capitalizes on narrative leverage, social ingenuity, and a risk-reward approach that makes you feel like you’re playing with fate itself. If you’ve ever wanted to be a streetwise con artist who also has a mystic superstition as a partner in crime, the Fortune Row playbook is your dream job.

In short: Fortune Row is the character who treats the city as a giant roulette wheel, with the right tells, the right numbers, and a sprinkling of mythic luck sprinkled on top. It’s not about brute force; it’s about reading people, knowing the right moment to take a chance, and making sure the payoff is worth the risk.

## The Concept: Luck as a Product and a Weapon

Fortune Row comes from the idea that luck is a marketable commodity. The playbook members trade in secrets, coins, and glimpses of possible futures. They can bend the odds in a scene and extract value from the situation with a smile that says, I’ve read your cards and I know your next move, champ. The power of Fortune Row lies in the tension between optimism and danger: the more you push luck, the more dangerous the city becomes, and the more your own fate becomes entangled with the city’s ever-shifting mythic structures.

A few quick talking points about the vibe:

- The aesthetic is a mashup of speakeasy glamour, neon noir, and a dash of carnival mysticism.
- The character types typically skew social, investigative, and cunning rather than physically robust.
- The mechanics lean into risking something valuable for a dramatic payoff, and the payoff often ripples outward in surprising ways.

If you’re building a party around mystery, misdirection, and charm that could outsell a city, Fortune Row is a natural fit.

## Archetypes Within the Playbook

Fortune Row isn’t monolithic. It’s a toolkit with a few archetypes that you can slot into your City of Mist campaign. Each archetype has its own angle on fortune, a signature move that shows up in play, and a few prompts to nudge your storytelling. Here are some of the archetypes you might see in a typical Fortune Row run:

- The Grifter: A master of social engineering, the Grifter uses charm as a weapon and a shield. They’re the face of fortune, the person who can switch between a con and a confession in the space of one scene.
- The Cardsharp: This archetype lives in the margins of luck. They have moves tied to decks of cards, lucky talismans, and visual misdirection. The Cardsharp makes the impossible look easy—until it isn’t.
- The Soothsayer’s Agent: An intermediary who translates mystic signs into practical outcomes. They’ve got a network of informants and a knack for turning cryptic prophecies into workable plans.
- The Gambler-Prophet: Half gambler, half mystic, they push their luck in the direction that benefits them most—and often, the city itself gets a little extra drama as a side effect.

Each archetype is a lens on Fortune Row’s core concept: reading the room, deciding what to gamble on, and learning how to move from “hunch” to “outcome.”

If you want a sample background for your PC, try: you were drawn to Fortune Row after a close call at a poker table with a mysterious stranger who whispered a fate you could not shake. It’s not just luck; it’s a debt owed to something bigger than yourself. And that debt is what keeps you playing the game.

## Core Moves and Signature Abilities

A big part of any City of Mist playbook is the list of moves that define a character’s capabilities. Fortune Row has moves that emphasize social leverage, cunning, and the occasional glint of mystical luck. While the exact wording may vary from the exact book you’re using, the flavor typically includes the following kinds of abilities:

- Fortune’s Edge: When you leverage a stroke of luck in a scene, you gain a narrative edge that might tilt the odds in your favor. It’s not guaranteed success, but it creates a moment where the odds tilt and you feel the city’s eyes on your back.
- The Crowd’s Pulse: You read a room or a crowd—ledgers, glances, and whispers—allowing you to anticipate the next move, and perhaps steer a social encounter toward your desired outcome. This is your social advantage track.
- Card Sharp Gambit: If you deal with a deck of cards, secrets of fortune, or a wallet full of old debts, you can turn a mundane moment into a meaningful turn of events. The card or coin becomes a symbol that unlocks a dramatic beat.
- Fate’s Breadcrumbs: A move that rewards you for leaving behind a clue or fragment of a prophecy that another PC can follow. It’s a way to echo a mythic thread without hogging the spotlight.

These moves are designed to feel cinematic: you’re not solving a mystery so much as you’re guiding a mystery to unfold in a way that reflects your character’s approach to risk, luck, and human interaction.

In addition to these signature moves, Fortune Row characters usually pick a few “routine” moves that reflect daily life in a city where luck runs just beneath the surface: a quick hustle to pay rent, a nightly ritual to appease the luck gods, an informant who owes you a favor, and a talisman you never take off.

## Roleplay and Tone

Fortune Row characters are all about a vibe: “safe with a risk,” the kind of person who looks both dangerous and charming in equal measure. The tone is witty and sarcastic, with hints of carnival barker energy. The GM should lean into the sense that Fortune Row is both a service and a risk: you can offer a glimpse of a brighter future, but there’s always a cost.

If you want to push the tone, try these flavor lines in your sessions:

- A whispered promise from a stranger who might be the city’s fate embodied as a man in a trench coat.
- A deck of cards that seems to shuffle itself when the room’s lights flicker.
- A talisman that hums when you approach a location where luck is about to change.

The key in roleplaying Fortune Row is to let the dice tell a story of balance: the more you lean into luck, the more you risk drawing attention from forces you don’t fully understand.

## Mechanics and Playstyle Tips for GMs

As the GM, you’ll be balancing two forces: the players’ ambition to push luck and the city’s mythic understructure that might punish reckless gambits. Here are some practical tips to run Fortune Row smoothly:

- Plan table moments: Fortune Row works best when there are explicit beats tied to luck-based outcomes. Plan a few moments where luck shifts are painfully obvious, then let a PC edge the outcome with a daring move.
- Use fate threads: Have prophecies or fates appear as breadcrumbs (Fate’s Breadcrumbs) and let the players chase them. It’s a good way to introduce a recurring antagonist who’s also in the “fortune business.”
- Be prepared for consequences: Luck isn’t a free pass. When players push their edge, the city might reflect that risk in a cost—perhaps a favored contact disappears, or a debt comes due from a mysterious patron.
- Collaborative storytelling: Fortune Row thrives when players contribute their own myths. Encourage players to weave their PCs into the clues and prophecies that drive the case.

GM tips also include how to model the city’s myths visually in the session: you can have a “Mythic Board” with cards or tokens representing different trends in fortune—good, bad, and mixed—and let players move those tokens with their choices.

## Play Scenarios and Hooks

To show how Fortune Row can function in a campaign, here are some fresh hooks you can drop into your next City of Mist session:

- The Debt of a Card: A gambler owes a debt to a shadowy patron who can bend the city’s luck. The team is tasked with retrieving a card that can cancel the debt, but the card is hidden at a casino where the house always wins.
- The Prophecy in Neon: A neon sign flickers a prophecy that only Fortune Row can read. The players must figure out what the prophecy means in the real world and how to intervene in a plan that could alter many people’s destinies.
- The Grift That Saved a Life: A misfired con leads to someone’s life being saved in an unexpected way. The players realize that their luck saved a person who now has the power to tip the city in the wrong direction.
- The Dice That Decide the World: A mysterious set of dice lands in a crowd, and whoever rolls them shapes the next day’s events. The players are drawn into a race to secure the dice before a rival gang does—and the dice might require a debt to be paid to claim their power.

These hooks are designed to be flexible enough to slot into your ongoing story arcs while letting Fortune Row’s focus on luck and social leverage play a central role.

## Progression and XP Footing

In City of Mist, advancement comes in waves as you accomplish goals, reveal secrets, and build your mythology. Fortune Row’s progression path emphasizes:

- Mythic development: Expand your charter to include new fates and connections within the city’s underworld and mythic networks.
- Social capital: Accumulate debt and favors; each new contact can alter how you leverage luck in future scenes.
- Risk-reward milestones: When you push your edge and net a major payoff, you get a narrative reward that cascades outward, affecting other players and NPCs.

XP should be spent to unlock new Moves or to strengthen existing ones: Fortune’s Edge becomes more reliable, Fate’s Breadcrumbs become a wider network, and Card Sharp Gambit can slice through scenes with greater precision. The progression should feel like a carnival ride—exciting, a little dangerous, and finally satisfying when the city’s lights flicker and you realize you controlled the moment.

## Strengths and Weaknesses of Fortune Row

Strengths:

- Socially dynamic: Fortune Row excels in scenes that require negotiation, manipulation, and social quick-thinking. It’s a party-lubricant that can smooth over tense interactions with charm.
- Mystically flavored without being overbearing: You can dip into the supernatural side without leaning into full-blown sorcery or heavy mythos nerd energy.
- Narrative leverage: Fortune Row makes it easy for the GM to give you a reason to chase a lead or a clue. The “luck” angle provides a flexible mechanism to guide a scene.

Weaknesses:

- Consumable luck risk: If your table overuses the luck mechanic, you risk the city punishing you a little too harshly. It’s easy to burn a scene if players lean entirely on luck without supporting their plan with actual investigative work.
- Potential overemphasis on social encounters: If the party is heavy on combat-focused players, Fortune Row may require some adaptation to keep everyone engaged.
- Vulnerability to stagnation: If the GM doesn’t bring dynamic fate-based consequences, Fortune Row can feel like a casino night that never ends. Spice things up with a recurring patron who makes sure the odds aren’t in your favor forever.

Tip: pair Fortune Row with a more action-oriented playbook to keep the table balanced. Or, if you’re feeling bold, embrace the orbit of risk in every scene and let the entire party chase after the next big payoff.

## How to Integrate Fortune Row with Other Playbooks

Fortune Row shines when paired with other City of Mist archetypes. Here are a few synergy ideas:

- Thinkers and Investigators: A Grifter + a Thinker makes for a perfect duo: the Thinker frames the problem; the Grifter uses luck and social leverage to sculpt a path to a solution.
- The Brute and The Gambler: A Balance of brawn and risk-taking. The Brute breaks down barriers while Fortune Row manipulates the odds to cover the team’s retreat, or pushes the team toward a climactic moment.
- The Mystic and The Gambler-Prophet: A duo that reads the city’s signs and then wagers on the outcome. The mystic’s signs give the group an edge, while the gambler-prophet ensures the outcome lands in a dramatic, narrative way.

If you’re writing a one-shot or a mini-campaign, Fortune Row can be a central hub around which your players’ stories revolve. A single arc can reveal a larger conspiracy behind the city’s luck—the “house” that’s been underwriting the failures and successes of ordinary people for ages.

## Sample Run: Neon Carousel Heist
Setup: The Neon Carousel is a midtown carnival turned casino with a rotating floor and a never-ending night. Fortune Row gets a tip that the Carousel holds a fortune-teller’s die that can tilt outcomes. The problem: a rival crew wants the die badly enough to torch the city’s mood with chaos.

Scenes:
- Scene 1: The Meet-Cute con at a pinball arcade: your Grifter chats up a clerk who secretly knows the Carousel’s schedule. The players leverage Fortune’s Edge to secure a reservation in the Carousel club, a place where luck itself seems to hum in the background.
- Scene 2: The Cardsharp’s test: a high-stakes poker game that hides a vault key in plain sight. The Cardsharp uses a deck of fortune cards to reveal the correct play and steal a glance at the vault’s schedule.
- Scene 3: The Outcome Gate: Fate’s Breadcrumbs: a cryptic tour of old prophecies that leads you to the die’s resting place behind the Carousel’s illusions.

Twists: The die cannot be used lightly; it requires a partner to activate a ritual, and that partner’s fate becomes a second debt one of the PCs owes to Fortune Row’s patrons.

Climax: The dice roll in a dramatic moment when the city’s chorus of neon lights flickers in time with a decision the players make about whether to keep the die or destroy it to prevent a catastrophe.

Payoff: You either walk away with the die and a new contact who may supply future luck, or you break the device, altering the city’s luck in a permanent but unknown way.

Character Spotlight: Three Sample Fortune Row PCs

- The Grifter: Moves: Fortune’s Edge, Crowd’s Pulse, Quick on the Table; Background: con artist who learned to read people by studying street markets and fortune-tellers.
- The Cardsharp: Moves: Card Sharp Gambit, Fate’s Breadcrumbs, Deck of Fays; Background: gambler who believes every “lucky” card hides a memory of a debt owed to the city.
- The Gambler-Prophet: Moves: Fortune’s Edge, Fate’s Breadcrumbs, Prophetic Tilt; Background: a mystic who wagers on outcomes and collects on the city’s string of prophecies.

Each PC has a different path to push luck and misfortune, offering varied routes to your party’s ongoing campaign arc.

## Progression and XP Footing (Expanded)

Fortune Row’s progression isn’t a flat ladder; it’s a tapestry of interconnected threads. Here are extra notes on advancement:

- Each new Fate you unlock should feel like you’ve earned a city-wide rumor. It’s not just a power; it’s a social upgrade with consequences.
- You can gain access to new “Luck Decks”—specialized items that grant micro-benefits in particular situations (negotiations, theft, information gathering).
- Gaining relationships with underworld patrons should be tracked, since those patrons can demand favors when the party tries to leverage luck in a scene.
- Special milestone moves: after a major heist or a major misfortune the team absorbs, the players get a special, temporary boost that helps them push the next story arc forward.

## Verdict and Recommendations (Revisited)

Fortune Row is a stylish, pulse-quickening entry point for campaigns that want to lean into the city’s luck as a narrative engine. It’s not a raw engine of brute force; it’s a toolkit for social maneuvering, cunning, and the occasional high-risk high-reward moment that can redefine a scene. If your table loves con games, neon-noir vibes, and the idea of luck as a bargaining chip, Fortune Row is a strong choice. The archetypes give you a clear direction for character creation, while the signature moves ensure you’re never short on dramatic beats.

That said, Fortune Row works best when your GM is ready to lean into consequences and to weave a web of fate that isn’t solved by a single die roll. It’s about how the city’s luck shifts over the course of a campaign, and how your team learns to read those shifts and ride them without getting shredded by the city’s more aggressive mythic patrons. If you want a playbook that makes the players feel like professional risk-takers in a living, breathing comic book city, Fortune Row will deliver.

The City of Mist toolkit is best used as a living, evolving design problem; Fortune Row offers a lyrical, stylish, and highly social approach that fits well with mystery-driven arcs and heists.

## Final Word

In the end, Fortune Row isn’t just a playbook; it’s a philosophy of storytelling. It invites you to gamble on the unknown and to craft a story where luck, mischance, and underworld networks interact in dramatic, surprising ways. If you’re itching for a new way to structure a session around character-driven social play with a hint of mystic carnival, Fortune Row is a perfect fit. It will scratch that itch for a heist-like vibe while letting you push for mythic development and personal growth.

It’s the kind of playbook that invites you to plan a plan that plans another plan, and then watch as fate executes the punchline in neon, rain, and city noise.
