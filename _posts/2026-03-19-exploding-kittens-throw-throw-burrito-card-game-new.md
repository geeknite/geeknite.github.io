## Setup: setting the arena
Time to create the arena. The setup is short, sweet, and designed to be completed during the intermission of your favorite snack run. Steps include:

## How to play: the chaos in 60 seconds
Here’s the essence, with enough detail to start you off while leaving room for ridiculous improvisation:
- On your turn, you draw two cards, aim to complete a set of three matching cards, and—if you’re bold—play an action card. Action cards can modify targets, force dodges, or trigger burrito defenses; they feel like power-ups you’d expect in a wacky video game.
- When a set is formed, claim it and place it face up in front of you. You need three such sets to win.
- The burritos are the heartbeat. If you fail to dodge in time, the consequences ripple through your hand, options, or even the pace of the game itself. It’s a simple risk-reward system: push your luck, but know that your luck might be thrown at you in a fluffy brown blur.

The rules aren’t overly formal and the momentum is the main feature. You aren’t playing a ritual of precise sequence; you’re playing a social sport with cards, laughter, and literal burrito near-misses.

### Quick rules digest
For the first round, keep this short cheat sheet in mind:
- Draw two cards per turn and work toward three-of-a-kind sets.
- You may play one action card to influence the board or to boost your own position.
- Dodge the burrito with your best superhero-nerd reflexes and enjoy the chaos.

### The physicality and safety notes
This game thrives on safe chaos. Plush burritos are designed to be soft and forgiving, but safety still matters:
- Define a clear safe-throw zone before you start.
- Use gentle, fun throws and avoid aiming at faces or electronics.
- If someone wants to opt out of the physical tosses, you can switch to a purely card-driven round and keep the humor alive.
