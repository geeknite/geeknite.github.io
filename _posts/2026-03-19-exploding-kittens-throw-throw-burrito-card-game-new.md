## What is Throw Throw Burrito?
Throw Throw Burrito is a party card game by Exploding Kittens. It combines the whimsy of collectible card antics with the physical comedy of throwing plush burritos at your opponents. The core mechanic is to collect sets of matching cards while simultaneously attempting to avoid getting hit by burritos. Each burrito is a 7-inch long fluffy weapon of giggles; the idea is that if you turn the game into a dodgeball match with edible-ish weapons, you are not far off.

The packaging promises chaos with a smile. It is bright, bold, and unapologetically cartoonish. The burritos—two or three included in the box—are soft enough to be safe but sturdy enough to deliver a funny thud when you miss your target, which, given the speed and the chaotic environment, is an ever-present risk.

## Setup: setting the arena
The setup is quick enough to fetch a snack, but not so fast that you miss your window to buy snacks. Shuffle the deck, place the burritos in the center, and deal each player a starter hand. The rest of the cards go into a draw pile, with the goal of forming a three-card set on your path to victory. The board is mostly optional; you can play on a coffee table, a floor rug, or even a couch cushion battlefield. The key is space; space is your friend.

## How to play: the chaos in 60 seconds
Here's a rough, no-nonsense overview of the flow:
- On your turn, you draw cards, attempt to form a single set (three cards of the same rank), and—if you’re bold—play an action card. Action cards may cause someone to grab a burrito and toss it in your direction; the target is to be quick and dodge at the right moment.
- When a set is formed, the player claims it, and the three-card set is placed face up in front of them. The goal is to collect three such sets to win the game.
- The burrito mechanic is the heartbeat of the game. If you fail to dodge a burrito, your hand may be reduced or you may be forced to discard, adding a layer of risk to every toss.

The actual rules are easier to absorb than a Jell-O shot on a hot day, but the flavor is what sells the experience: you’re not just playing a card game; you’re playing a chaotic, physical, comedic sport.

### Quick rules digest
If you want to survive your first round with minimal chaos, remember:
- Draw two cards on your turn and attempt to complete a set of three.
- Play one action card if you’ve got the nerve. Actions can swap cards, force dodges, or trigger burrito defenses.
- Dodge—and hope your friends’ aim remains merciful.

### The physicality and safety notes
This game is all about safe chaos. The plush burritos are soft and designed for light contact. Still, safety rules apply:
- Define a safe throwing zone before you start.
- Use soft throws and avoid aiming at faces.
- If someone is uncomfortable with physical play, switch to a purely card-driven round.
