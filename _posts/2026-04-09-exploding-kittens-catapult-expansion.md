## What's in the Catapult Expansion?
The Catapult Expansion adds a handful of objects and cards that radically alter how you approach Exploding Kittens. The core idea is simple: add a little more physical humor, a bit more randomness, and a handful of new cards that interact with both your cunning and your willingness to nod, smile, and pretend you didn’t see that weird spring-loaded device on the table.

### The Catapult Launcher
At the heart of this expansion is the catapult launcher itself, a compact tool designed to fling small tokens—like kitten cards or explosive tokens—across the table. It’s goofy, it’s loud, and yes, it’s a little bit Tetris-adjacent as you arrange tokens in a way that ensures maximal chaos. The launcher is a prop, but in the spirit of the game, pretend you are a rocket scientist who studied the delicate physics of “paws and precision.”

The launcher requires calibration. It’s a tiny device that comes with a brief manual that reads more like a comedy script than a technical specification. You’ll spend the first few rounds figuring out the angle, the distance, and how to avoid launching a token into the nearest beer. The good news: the more you practice, the funnier it becomes, especially when you witness a kitten token sail across the room to land in the sand castle formed by the chips.

### New Cards and Card Interactions
The expansion adds a handful of new cards, each designed to interact with the launch mechanic in interesting ways. Some cards are direct threats—“Launch a Token at Any Player”—while others are clever manipulations of the game state, like “Catapult Interception,” which can be used to redirect the token mid-flight if you have the right angle and a good sense of dramatic timing.

One of my favorite additions is a card that rewards players for mischief. It gives you extra turns or a second shot at a failed catapult toss. There’s also a “calibration” card that forces everyone to reposition tokens before the catapult can fire again, ensuring that you all pause for a moment and appreciate the ridiculous scenario you’ve created.

### Tokens, Cards, and Tokens on Tokens
The expansion includes a new token type that interacts with both the base kit and the catapult. Tokens can be stacked, launched, intercepted, or redirected depending on the card in play. The art continues to deliver the same mix of adorable cats and cheeky humor. The tokens are sturdy enough to survive a few table bounces, which is essential given how aggressively you’ll be throwing pieces around when the catapult is engaged.

### Rules and Clarifications
No expansion is perfect without a clarifying FAQ. The Catapult Expansion includes a short set of clarifications that keep players from arguing about whether a launched token counts as a “strike” or a “hit.” The key points:
- Token launches count as an action that can be blocked or reflected by certain cards.
- If a token leaves the table, you should pause and retrieve it; pretend you’re a helpful referee who has a better sense of humor than the rules.
- The catapult cannot be used in a way that would cause damage to the players or the table, obviously; this is still a family-friendly game night, not a physics class in a laboratory.

## How Catapult Changes Gameplay
This is where the expansion shows its teeth—not in a brutal sense, but in the way it intentionally injects mischief into routine turns. You’ll find yourself giggling at the ridiculousness as you realize that a single launched token can swing the momentum of the entire round.

### Turn Flow and Decision Making
Base Exploding Kittens focuses on risk and bluffing; Catapult adds a fourth axis: physical risk. Suddenly, your choices aren’t just about “do I risk drawing the exploding kitten?” but also “do I want to sacrifice a perfectly good card to push a token closer to that unlucky neighbor?” The tension skyrockets when someone catches the token mid-air by sheer luck and then uses that momentum to rob you of your precious plus-one card.

### Interaction with Existing Cards
Catapult interacts with the classic set with surprising elegance. A “nope” style counter is now joined by a set of cards that can influence the flight path of a token. That means you’ll get a lot more “you had one job” scenarios, where your own strategy is undone not by the opponent’s cunning but by an unexpectedly aerodynamic token trajectory.

### Metering Luck vs. Skill
As with any expansion that introduces physical elements, there’s a natural tension between luck and skill. You’ll have more opportunities for big plays, but you’ll also have more opportunities to lob a token into your own soup. The trick is to embrace the chaos without blaming it for your subpar dice roll.

## Strategy and Tips
If you want to maximize your fun while minimizing feuds, here are a few practical tips.

- Practice the catapult: get a sense for distance and angle. Start with smaller throws and gradually challenge your friends to more ambitious launches.
- Assign roles: designate a “launch captain” who handles the catapult, a “scorekeeper” who tracks tokens, and a “guardian” who keeps the table free of stray missiles.
- Communicate clearly: a well-timed wink or a dramatic reading of a card can turn a tense moment into a hysterical one.
- Use space wisely: clear the area around the launch zone so you aren’t launching tokens into someone’s drink.
- Manage expectations: remind players that this is a game night, not a physics demo on the kitchen table.

## Solo Play and Social Dynamics
Exploding Kittens shines as a social game; Catapult expands that social dynamic by adding a physical hilarity factor. Some players will thrive in the chaos; others will want to politely request fewer kitten-related projectiles. If you’re playing with a mixed group (newbies and veterans), the expansion can serve as a great equalizer: the more you tilt the catapult, the more unpredictable the match becomes.

There’s also a nice balance to be found in group size. Too many players and the catapult might feel like a chaotic circus; too few and it can devolve into a very loud and very funny chess match of token tossing. The sweet spot seems to be around four to six players—people who know each other’s swearing tolerance and who have the reflexes to catch or dodge a token launched at them.

## Comparisons with Other Expansions
If you’ve explored Exploding Kittens beyond the base game, you’ll know there are a few other expansions with different vibes: the party vibe, the hyper-chaos vibe, and the “let’s pretend we’re in a competitive sport” vibe. Catapult sits in a sweet middle ground: enough novelty to feel fresh, but not so much complexity that you lose the humor in the chaos.

Compared to other expansions, Catapult leans heavily on physical humor without tipping over into the realm of “this is no longer a card game.” The rules are approachable for new players, and the novelty keeps a game night lively even for groups that have played Exploding Kittens a hundred times. You can pair Catapult with other expansions to create a stack of modules that feel cohesive, rather than a pile of mismatched gimmicks.

It’s not going to replace your favorite deck-building or strategy-heavy games; it is a party extension, a laugh machine, and a strong reminder that sometimes the simplest words—plus, avoid, catapult—are all you need to generate memorable moments.

For seasoned players who crave depth, Catapult offers enough micro-interactions to keep rounds interesting without bogging you down in analysis paralysis. For families with kids who can count to ten and appreciate slapstick, Catapult becomes a safe, ridiculous, and surprisingly cooperative chaos engine where the goal is to have fun first and complain about the rules second.

If you’re unsure whether to pick it up, consider your group’s tolerance for physical humor and occasional shrapnel-free chaos. If your answer is “yes, yes, yes, and more yes,” then dive in. The Catapult Expansion is a playful upgrade that respects the base game’s spirit: you’re still chasing the exploding kitten, you’re just doing it with more laughter, more noise, and, perhaps, more applause for a perfect token launch.
