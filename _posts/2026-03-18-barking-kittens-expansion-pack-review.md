Setup and Game Flow: How Barking Kittens Changes Things
-----------------------------------------------------

Baseline Exploding Kittens is a fast game where players draw cards and try not to explode. Barking Kittens adds a handful of new mechanics that subtly shift the rhythm without turning the core feel into a completely different game. The expansion is designed so that you can mix and match with the base rules—there is no need to commit to a full expansion-only night if you are unsure whether your group will tolerate more chaotic chaos.

New Mechanics and Interactions
- Bark Tokens: A set of tokens introduced to reward or punish players based on their interactions. Think of them as a tiny, square reminder that the universe has a sense of humor about your ability to hold onto your sanity.
- Dog and Cat Cards: The expansion adds both dog and cat cards that interact with existing Exploding Kittens cards in new ways. Some cards might force you to bark at the wrong moment or to purr away a risk that you would otherwise take, which adds a layer of misdirection to every turn.
- Interaction Chains: The new cards enable the possibility of longer chains or combos that can swing a round from near-automatic disaster to a hair’s breadth of victory. The key is reading the board and predicting when someone will unleash a pun-based countermeasure.
- Paws and Claws Rules: A lightweight rule variant that allows players to use paw or claw actions to defend against explosions in an unpredictable way. This can create moments of glorious near-misses that will have everyone at the table shouting out a cat pun before the next draw.

Note that Barking Kittens does not reinvent the wheel. It purposefully adds depth without forcing players to re-learn the rules from scratch. If you are a purist, you can simply ignore the new mechanics, shift the focus back onto the core strategy, and still enjoy the expansion as a booster pack of humor and occasional plot twist fuel.

The Card Spotlight: Examples of New Interactions
- The Howling Fool: A card that forces a player to reveal their hand; if they hesitate, they must draw two extra cards. A classic meta-game move that can produce memorable “did you just bark at me?” moments.
- Purr-ception: A defensive card that lets you redirect an incoming attack to another player who has not incurred a catastrophe yet. This creates a fun, chaotic chain where players start blaming each other for letting a cat out of the bag.
- Bark-O-Matic: A special action that triggers if a dog-themed card is played in a specific sequence. It can either help your position or explode someone else’s hopes—depending on where you stand during the turn order.

These examples illustrate the playful and chaotic flavor Barking Kittens brings to Exploding Kittens: more ways to bluff, more opportunities to misread a situation, and more chances to laugh at your own misfortune.
