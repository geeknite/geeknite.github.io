## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity Tips

Keeping a synthetic rug healthy is about regular maintenance and mindful handling of spills. Here are practical tips that work in real life, not just in glossy catalog shots.

- Vacuum weekly to remove dust and prevent grit from wearing down the fibers. Use a vacuum with a brush attachment on the lowest setting to avoid pulling fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth or paper towel. Avoid rubbing, which pushes the stain deeper and can create a dull patch.
- For tougher stains, use a mild detergent solution and rinse with a clean damp cloth. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the fiber or affect color.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic areas. This is a small ritual that pays off in a long-term sense of balance and style.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slippage, provide extra cushioning, and extend the life of the rug by absorbing impact and reducing friction with the floor underneath.

### Longevity expectations

A well-chosen synthetic rug with good density and a sturdy backing can last many years under normal living conditions. It will typically withstand routine vacuuming and light cleaning without showing significant shedding or matting. If you have a pet with a dramatic tail or a toddler who treats the living room like a playground, expect to invest a little more time in spot cleaning and occasional maintenance, just as you would with any durable, life-in-charge home accessory.
