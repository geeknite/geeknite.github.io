## Condition and water mark on the lid
The water mark on the lid is a small but meaningful detail. It may be a stamp, embossing, or printed mark that has withstood the passing of time without wearing away. For collectors, this watermark can be a sign of an unused or lightly used set, especially if the rest of the deck shows minimal wear. When evaluating condition, look at the edge wear, corner dents, and the overall gloss of the card faces. A mint or near mint set will have clean edges, smooth corners, and very little creasing. ## Care and restoration
Vintage card sets deserve respectful care. Store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. A box with silica gel is not mandatory but helpful for keeping humidity in check. If you notice minor surface scuffs on the card backs, a soft cloth can remove some dust, but avoid any liquids. For the lid water mark, if it is an embossed seal, you should not try to erase or cover it up; it is part of the unit's charm. If the box shows the original label, do not attempt to reprint or replace it with a modern sticker; the value lies in the patina of time.
