## Wave 1: Setting the Stage

### The Roster and Why It Matters

In any wave, the roster is the first handshake with a collector. Wave 1 of Marvel Legends Series 2022 pulled its weight by balancing nostalgia picks with modern reinterpretations, a combo that can feel like a curated playlist rather than a shopping list. The goal here is to give you both a fan-favorite moment and a fresh look at a character you might have only seen in a panel from a comic that existed before your junior high friends learned to drive. The line-up is designed to appeal to long-time collectors who remember the first appearances, and newer fans who discovered these characters through MCU popcorn cinema, video games, or a thick stack of back-issues.

From a design standpoint, the wave leaned into two main strategies: (1) display-ready sculpts with dynamic action poses, and (2) deco that honors the character’s most iconic outfits while offering enough variants to differentiate each figure as a standalone piece on a shelf. This isn’t a party designed for one hero; it’s a roomful where each statue has a reason to stand in the same room with the rest and still feel distinct.

### Packaging: A Small But Mighty Update

Packaging is often the unsung hero (or villain) of the collecting world. Wave 1 in 2022 brought a few subtle updates to packaging that mattered less to the casual browser and more to the obsessive display curator. The artwork on the card art remains bold and cinematic, with bright accents that pop under neon lighting. The window box gives you a clear look at the figure and its accessories, while still maintaining that classic Hasbro packaging silhouette that is instantly recognizable to long-time fans.

Display-wise, the packaging is a prologue to what you’ll do with the figure once you unbox it. There’s always a moment of drama as you slide a figure out, and the packaging often hints at the figure’s era—what era of the character’s costume this deco is trying to evoke, and how that choice will impact your display later on. If you’re a photo-leaning collector, the packaging becomes a light backdrop when you snap a shot of the figure framed against a clean tabletop surface.

### Build-A-Figure: A Note on the Concept (and Why It Might Not Be Front and Center)

Build-A-Figure (BAF) pieces are one of the more polarizing components of the Legends line. Some waves center a BAF character and make the rest of the figures feel like supporting cast. Other waves skip the BAF entirely in some regions and instead rely on richer deco and extra accessories to fill the box. Wave 1 of 2022 followed the latter approach in certain markets, focusing on solid sculpting, better paint apps, and a variety of accessories that can turn a shelf into a mini diorama. It’s not a crime to admit you prefer a BAF piece—it’s a collector impulse to assemble something large and impressive. If your version of this wave included a BAF component, expect a chunk of the torso or limbs to be the glue that ties the lineup together in the worst possible way (and the best possible way when you finally set up that display). We’ll keep this section universal to honor the reality that not every region has the same BAF experience, and that is part of the charm and chaos of Legends collecting.

## Sculpt, Paint, and Overall Aesthetics

### Sculpt Quality: A Mixed Bag with Plenty of Wins

The sculpting in Wave 1 is a showcase of modern tooling. The figures carry a mix of featherlight lines and chunky, heroic silhouettes—each character reinterpreted to feel fresh yet familiar. Some sculpts lean heavily into hyper-detailed anatomy and micro-sculpting like micro-sculpted fabric folds that only a true geometry-geek would notice. Others strike a balance that makes posing simple without compromising the silhouette that makes the hero instantly recognizable from across the room. In practice, this means you’ll see some characters with jawlines sharp enough to slice through cardboard, and others with softer lines that remind you of classic comic panels. The goal is to give each figure presence, whether displayed solo on a shelf or in a diorama with the rest of the lineup.

### Paint Application: Two Notes, One Truth

Paint apps in 2022 Wave 1 hover between precise and painterly, depending on the hero. There are moments where a hand-painted highlight on a gauntlet catches the light just right, and other moments where a subtle wash reveals the under-sculpt shading as if the figure walked off a comic panel. The risk with high-detail paint work is the occasional misalignment—tiny gaps, slightly misaligned eyes, or a misapplied wash here and there. But for the most part, the results are sturdy enough for display, photography, and the occasional accidental stand-up contest with your enamel paints and a set of action-digging cat claws. In a world where QC is not always predictable, Wave 1 tends to lean toward consistency: a good sign for new collectors building their display, and a nice baseline for veterans looking for a reliable line.

### Articulation: The Joints You Want, the Balance You Need

Articulation in Marvel Legends has come a long way from the days when a figure could pull off a decent pose but would wobble if you looked at it sideways. Wave 1 demonstrates how Hasbro has refined the balance between dynamic posing and stability. Expect a healthy range of articulation: ball joints in the shoulders and hips, double joints at elbows and knees, and ankle articulation that helps a figure plant a stable hero stance on a sloped shelf. The result is a lineup that can hold a mid-air kick pose, a classic punching stance, or a reflective, cape-swatting pose without looking like the figure is about to topple itself in polite social settings. The one caveat is that a couple of figures in this wave carry accessories with peg points that don’t always align perfectly with traditional hand sockets. It’s not a disaster, but it’s something to keep in mind when you’re trying to pin a lightning effect or a back-peg to that figure’s wrist.

### Accessories: Why You Buy the Figure in the First Place

Accessories can make or break a figure’s shelf presence. Wave 1 brings a well-curated set of extras: removable masks, interchangeable hands, dramatic weapons, and effect parts that add a little extra gloss to action poses. The key is modularity. The accessories aren’t just thrown into the box as a distraction; they’re designed to extend the figure’s playability and display options. Some sets include display stands, a welcome addition for folks who want to stage dynamic action shots or create a miniature battle scene on a glass shelf. The designs tend to respect the character’s canon while giving you a few clever deviations that keep things visually fresh.

## Display, Photography, and Collection Strategy

### Display Tips: Making the Most of a 6-Inch Scale Shelf

The Marvel Legends standard 6-inch scale sits nicely on most bookshelf displays, but if you want to go all-in on a curated display, you’ll want a few tricks. Start with a clean backdrop, a light source that isn’t blasting directly from above (angle lighting is your friend for bringing out sculpt details), and a consistent shelving height to maintain visual rhythm. Consider grouping heroes by theme (Avengers, Spider-Verse, X-Men) or by year of release to create a retro-futurist timeline on your wall. The figures are tall enough to command attention but not so tall they overwhelm other collectibles in the same space.

### Photography: Capture the Decals, Not Just the Silhouettes

For those who enjoy toy photography, Wave 1 provides plenty of opportunities. The skin tones, textured fabrics, and metallic deco on armor translate well under natural light with a touch of gentle fill flash. If you want to experiment with color grading, try a cool-warm gradient background and a light-angle that brings the metallic finishes to life. A tripod becomes valuable here, particularly for hero poses that demand multiple frames to convey motion. The bottom line: if you can capture the figure’s character in a single shot, you’re doing something right; if you can stage a mini action scene with three characters and a dramatic explosion effect, you’ve earned a cape and a photo award in the world of mid-size figure photography.

### Collecting in 2022: Market Trends and Practical Advice

The 2022 wave landscape wasn’t just about the plastic figures—it was about how collectors navigated variants, reissues, and perceived value. The market response to Wave 1 was generally positive, with a balance between new sculpts and reissued classics that kept long-time fans engaged while inviting new collectors to join the party. If you’re hunting for Wave 1, here are a few practical tips:

- Keep an eye on retailer exclusives in your region. Some stores switch up the lineup with region-specific variants that offer slight deco differences.
- Consider the potential value of late-stage reissues or variant colors. The nostalgia premium often drives resell prices up, but it’s not a guaranteed win if you’re primarily a display collector.
- Inspect QC in-store or on first online delivery. A quick test pose in the box can reveal loose joints or brittle joints that become annoying on day two when you attempt a dramatic action pose.

## A Quick Comparison: Wave 1 vs Earlier Waves

Compared to early Marvel Legends waves, the 2022 Wave 1 release leans into more refined deco and improved articulation joints. The earlier waves had a certain charm and nostalgia factor, but they could be fiddly with joints that didn’t always hold a pose as well as you’d hope. Wave 1 provides a smoother transition for posing—less brittle feel, more robust locks—and while it doesn’t reinvent the wheel, it refines the wheel in a way that feels like a natural progression rather than a jump-cut jump-cut. If you’ve got shelves filled with 2020 and 2021 Legends, Wave 1 sits nicely as a bridge between the era of chunky suits and the era of micro-decals that reward a microscope-level observation on your coffee table.

## Final Thoughts: Is Wave 1 Worth Your Time and Shelf Space?

In the world of collectible action figures, value can be a tricky concept. Is it about the long-term investment, or is it about the joy of display and the thrill of unboxing? Wave 1 of Marvel Legends Series 2022 offers a balanced package: a mix of notable characters, solid sculpt discipline, dependable articulation, and a design approach that respects fans without turning the line into a modding project. If you are a new collector looking to start a diverse shelf, Wave 1 serves as a solid backbone to your display—standalone figures with enough character variety to prevent your shelf from turning into a monochrome corridor. If you are a veteran who has followed Legends for years, Wave 1 is a reminder that Hasbro’s best moments aren’t always about the biggest and flashiest releases; they’re about the steady, dependable figures that you’ll reach for when you’re staging a weekend photo set with three friends and a neon backdrop.

In terms of collectability, the wave is not the single most expensive or the most limited run in a given year—but it is a dependable entry point with a demonstrated commitment to poseability and character fidelity. You’ll likely find your favorite hero or villain represented here in a form that you can display proudly, even if you have to negotiate a bit with a friend to avoid a joint that stubbornly refuses to rotate.

### Final Recommendation

- If you want a reliable, well-made wave that offers a genuine balance of nostalgia and modern design, this is a strong pick. It’s not revolutionary, but it is solid. You’ll be comfortable buying one or two figures for display and you’ll enjoy photographing them in a variety of action poses without fighting with misaligned joints. It’s a good baseline; it’s a good step forward for the line; and it’s likely to bring a few new fans into the Marvel Legends fold with its accessible price point and approachable build quality.

If you’re just starting out, pick up at least one figure from Wave 1 and a couple of friends to form a basic team. You’ll quickly realize that the fun comes in mixing and matching across waves to see which armor deco, which head sculpt, and which accessory set best suits your display goals. And if you’re a veteran collector with a shelf that can tell stories, Wave 1 can be the connective tissue, the bridge to your next set of showpiece figures—especially if you decide to assemble the Build-A-Figure (where available) to create a centerpiece that anchors your entire display.
