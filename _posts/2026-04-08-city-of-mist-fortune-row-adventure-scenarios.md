## Fortune Row: The Neighborhood That Grins When You Blink
Fortune Row is not a map so much as a mood board. It’s a district where fortune-telling meets clockwork, where vending machines spit out prophecies instead of snacks, and alleyways bend around memory like a kid bending a straw to drink from the future. The area is rich with potential masks, motifs, and misdirection—the core Fuel of City of Mist. In Fortune Row, players can explore themes of reputation, consequence, and the myth of control. The villains aren’t monsters from another time; they’re the kinds of people who think they can rewrite a life by rewriting the rules.

In terms of playstyle, Fortune Row rewards curiosity, clever tagging, and collaboration. The more your table leans into the bizarre but grounded vibe, the more you’ll feel the street pulse under your feet as you push toward the truth: what do these myths want from us, and what do we want from them?

### Thematic Lenses You’ll Bring to Fortune Row
- Neon-noir myth: A city that glows with potential and danger.
- Fate as a fragile thing: Fortune Row test-beds how much we believe in luck when the odds are rigged against us.
- Power with strings attached: Each major clue tug pulls a thread that might unravel someone’s life.

If you’re familiar with the City of Mist core book, Fortune Row plays nicely with the idea that Masks are not just costumes but social arsenals. Expect the usual mix of Investigator and Iconic Archetypes to flex within a world where every choice is a hinge and every hinge leads to another mystery.

## The Adventure Set: Scenarios in Fortune Row
Below is a curated selection of adventure scenarios designed to be run as a one-shot or integrated into a longer arc. Each scenario includes a hook, a short beat outline, major NPCs, a few clue types, and GM tips to keep the pace snappy and the tone just right.

### 1) The Velvet Ledger
Hook: A legendary ledger that records not just events but the emotional weight of those events has resurfaced in Fortune Row. Whoever possesses it can alter the memory of a night out in the district, effectively altering public perception.
Beat Outline:
- Act I: The Ledger returns to public view through a charity auction hosted at a forgotten theater.
- Act II: A memory glitch shows up on billboards; the players must identify who paid to erase a scandal and why.
- Act III: The reveal: someone is trying to rewrite a quiet tragedy into a triumph for a political rival.
Key NPCs: The Auctioneer (a Mask who manipulates perception), The Grieved Parent (a memory-weighted backer), The Ledger Keeper (a spirit bound to the ledger).
Clues: A torn ticket stub, a reflected memory in a shop window, an endorsement speech that never happened.
GM Tips: Use memory shifts as a mechanic to challenge players to track truth over slick appearances. This scenario loves misdirection and the reliability of eyewitness accounts.

Run Notes: This scenario is excellent for teaching players to trust or distrust their own senses. It’s also a great springboard for introducing Fortune Row’s more powerful Memory Masks.

### 2) The Neon Clockmaker’s Trial
Hook: The clockmaker who can bend time in Fortune Row has been accused of selling a cursed timepiece that resets a person’s life back to a moment before a crime.
Beat Outline:
- Act I: A time-tick in a local analog clock shop causes residents to relive a pivotal moment for a minute at a time.
- Act II: The Clockmaker must be proven innocent by finding a hidden gear that could only be forged by a specific mythic craftsperson.
- Act III: A choice to preserve the truth or reset a day for everyone in Fortune Row.
Key NPCs: The Clockmaker, The Gearwright (an artisan with a knack for impossible alloys), The Prosecutor (masked avenger).
Clues: A gear bearing a symbol that matches a long-lost manufacturer, an empty watchcase that somehow contains a memory, a customer’s confession written in a diary that disappears when read aloud.
GM Tips: Time-based puzzles can be tense but extremely satisfying if you lean into the clockwork aesthetic and the idea of options that “roll back the day.”

### 3) The Fortune Row Heist
Hook: A ransom note written in fortune-cookie fortunes demands something only Fortunes Row can give—a whispered future. The target is a fragile myth that sits at the edge of a crime lord’s empire.
Beat Outline:
- Act I: The team uncovers a delivery network that circles around a fortune-teller’s parlor.
- Act II: A chase through the district’s hidden tunnels beneath the clocktower.
- Act III: The reveal: the “victim” is a trap meant to lure the group into a political trap.
Key NPCs: The Fortune Courier (shadow courier who can vanish in a crowd), The Oracle of Tabloids (a figure who can make or break reputations with a single headline).
Clues: Missing courier routes, a fortune from a cookie that predicts a crime that hasn’t happened yet, a map etched into a wall that redraws when touched by a Mask.
GM Tips: The heist leans into planning, misdirection, and how a single rumor can topple a city block’s power balance.

### 4) Ghosts at the Docks
Hook: A haunting that tears at the edge of Fortune Row. Literally a haunting—voices carried by the fog that only the tuned ears of certain Masks can hear.
Beat Outline:
- Act I: A ship that never arrives, a dock that never ends, and a chorus of echoes that repeat a tragic night.
- Act II: Investigating reveals the haunting is anchored to a mythic accident that happened years ago.
- Act III: The truth requires a sacrifice that benefits the living but costs the dead their cotillion in memory.
Key NPCs: The Harbor Warden (a masked guardian of the docks), The Echo Choir (spirits bound to the location), The Shipwright (the man who built the ghost ship).
Clues: A bell that tolls without a clock, an old cargo manifest with names erased, a lullaby sung by a storm.
GM Tips: Use sensory hazards—sound, fog, and cold—to create fear without gore. Ghosts thrive on unfinished business, so give players a door to close, even if it’s a doorway back into their own pasts.

### 5) The Masquerade at the Clocktower
Hook: Fortune Row hosts an annual masquerade where masks reveal what people want most—except this year, the masks reveal a secret everyone wants to forget.
Beat Outline:
- Act I: Invitations arrive with a cryptic riddle and a promise of a revelation.
- Act II: The party is a showcase of allegiances; as the night goes on, allegiances shift and the room grows electric with lies.
- Act III: The truth is a trap—someone will die by the mask’s reveal if the players don’t intervene with clever social cunning.
Key NPCs: The Host (a Mask who thrives on spectacle), The Confessor (a Reporter who never lies), The Dagger Merchant (makes masks that reveal truths).
Clues: A mask that refuses to be worn, a confession hidden in a prop book, a stage cue that acts like a threat.
GM Tips: Focus on social dynamics, leverage the one-ring motif of “the mask reveals who you are.” Encourage players to use social powers and misdirection as weapons.

## Running Fortune Row: Mechanics, Tone, and Table Dynamics
City of Mist runs on Masks, Tag-based abilities, and a mystery-solving vibe that rewards collaborative storytelling. In Fortune Row, you want a rhythm that swings between noir humor and mythic awe. Here are practical tips to help you run these scenarios smoothly:
- Start with a strong hook: A rumor, a glitch in memory, or a challenge that only a specific Mask can solve.
- Build clues that intertwine with fortune and misfortune: the more a clue feels like fate, the more it anchors players in the theme.
- Use social challenges as combat: not every conflict ends with a punch; sometimes the best victory is a clever lie that becomes a truth.
- Manage the tone: fortune and tragedy sit on the same chair; keep your table stocked with jokes, and don’t let the mood spiral into despair.
- Safety tools first: use a consent and safety check, respect player boundaries, and have a plan to wrap up if a scene gets too intense.

## Clues, Masks, and the Fortunes We Fold
City of Mist uses the concept of Masks to empower players and the idea of Clues to drive the narrative forward. Fortune Row’s scenarios are designed to maximize this loop:
- Masks give you a pool of tags you can use to describe actions, influence outcomes, or bend time and space within the dreamlike logic of the city.
- Clues are the breadcrumbs that connect a mystery to the myth the players must face. In Fortune Row, clues often come in twinned forms: a physical artifact and a memory resonance.
- The setting slipstream—Fortune Row—lets clues morph as players explore. This is where the GM can introduce twists that feel both inevitable and surprising, a hallmark of good noir with mythic backing.

Examples you can drop into play:
- A memory-weighted badge that weighs more than its wearer realizes.
- A carny fortune that predicts not the future but the price of attempting to change it.
- A clock face that advances only when someone in the party acts morally ambiguous.

## Sample Session Flow: Pace and Play
A well-paced Fortune Row session often follows a three-act arc with a touch of a twist ending. Here is a practical flow you can reuse:
- Opening Act: Establish the hook and the mood. Let players tag scenes with Masks to reveal what each character wants most at the moment.
- Middle Act: Introduce 2–3 clues that complicate the initial assumption. Use a social conflict or a time-bending event to test how players use their Masks to shape outcomes.
- Final Act: A choice with real consequences. The players decide what truths to reveal and what realities to accept as true. The ending should feel earned, not preordained.

## World-Building in Fortune Row: Safety, Tone, and Thematic Confidence
Fortune Row thrives on a blend of humor and dread. Your table’s ability to laugh at a difficult clue while still treating the theme with gravity is what makes the arc shine. Here are a few tips to keep the vibe on track:
- Keep humor sourced: Let jokes come from character voice and humorous NPC quirks rather than random gags that break the mood.
- Respect the line between fate and choice: Remind players that their choices alter a myth’s course; even a small decision can ripple into a bigger change.
- Prepare content warnings: Fortune Row can touch on memory loss, coercion, and loss; prepare your players and have exit ramps for uncomfortable topics.
