### Phase Mechanics and Core Rules

Phase 10 is, at its core, a rummy-type game with a twist. Each player starts with a hand of cards and must progress through a predetermined sequence of phases. The interplay of discards, draws, and the order of phase completion creates a dynamic where luck and strategy share the stage.

- Turn sequence: Draw a card, either lay down a completed phase or discard a card.
- Phase progression: Each phase must be completed in order. Once you complete a phase, you advance to the next phase in the sequence.
- Wild and skip cards: Wild cards can substitute for any card within a phase. Skip cards can disrupt opponents by forcing them to skip turns.
- Scoring: After each round ends, players tally points based on the cards remaining in their hands. The player with the lowest total at the end of the final phase claims victory or at least the bragging rights.

This Fundex edition is a faithful take on the original concept. The artwork and this particular deck layout reflect the late 2000s aesthetic, which some players find nostalgic and others might simply find quaint.
