## Care, Maintenance, and Practicality
Wool is relatively low maintenance compared to many synthetic fibers, but it still requires routine care to retain its look. Vacuuming is your primary weapon here. Use a vacuum with a gentle setting or a beater bar that you can switch off to avoid rough treatment of the fibers. Regular vacuuming prevents grit from acting like tiny sandbags that weigh down the pile and flatten the lush surface. For spills, blot, do not rub. Wool fibers have a natural resilience, but rubbing can push stains deeper or cause the dye to migrate. The recommended approach is to blot with a clean, dry cloth and use a mild wool-safe cleaner if needed. Always test cleaner on a small inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability. If you want to go the extra mile, a professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months will keep the fibers looking crisp while you suspect the rug has earned tenure as an iconic piece of the room’s personality.

The rug is easy to rotate to even out wear. Rotating every few months is a good habit not just for even wear, but to ensure that any vacuumed wool fibers do not develop a directional bias in how they lay. If you have pets, this is particularly important. The wool is relatively pet friendly in terms of durability; however you will want to be mindful of any potential pilling in high traffic areas. A gentle pet hair removal tool can help; some folks even use a soft brush to stimulate a refreshed pile look between cleanings. Overall maintenance is straightforward, and the wool keeps its feel and color relatively well with proper care.
