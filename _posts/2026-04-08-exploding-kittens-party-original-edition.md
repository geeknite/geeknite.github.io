## Gameplay Mechanics

The feel of this edition is similar to the original kitten caper, but with a faster tempo and more social interaction at the table. The goal is simple on the surface: be the last player standing. The deck contains action cards that force players to draw more cards, steal cards, or alter the rhythm of the round. There are cards that cancel actions, cards that skip your draw, and of course the infamous Exploding Kitten that can wipe a player in an instant unless they defuse the catastrophe.

Turn order is straightforward. On your turn you draw a card, then you may play one or more action cards to shift chances, steal, or dramatically alter the deck. The best games here feel like a chaotic dance where someone plays a nope at just the right moment, a defuse is saved for the right time, and a rogue attack card lands when you least expect it. The strategy is less about advanced planning and more about reading the table, bluffing effectively, and knowing when to push your luck.

Defuse cards are the lifeblood of the game. They let you survive an Exploding Kitten, but you can only use them once per issue (the edition uses them as one-time life lines). The tension comes from deciding whether to risk pulling a second kitten during a round or to conserve resources for a potential late-game swing. In larger groups, a momentary misplay can cascade into a chain reaction that ends a round in spectacular fashion. 

In practice, the edition facilitates quick sessions. Expect a couple of rounds if you are playing with six to eight players. If you want more playtime, simply rotate players or add a house rule to keep the deck cycling. The pacing rewards social play more than deep card-skill mastery, which is exactly the point of a party game.
