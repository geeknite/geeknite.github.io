## Edouard Guiton: The Artist Behind the Art

### Style and Inspirations

Edouard Guiton has a knack for turning the grotesque into something tangible, almost tactile enough to pass as a character you’d invite to a cozy Sunday game night—though you wouldn’t invite a zombie without warning. His work across the Zombicide universe tends toward bold silhouettes, moody lighting, and a surprising flair for dynamic composition. You’ll notice how he experiments with negative space to create tension on the page; you’ll also notice how some panels feel like cinematic storyboards begging to be realized as a diorama or a tabletop vignette.

The influence of classic horror artists, modern concept art, and a dash of vintage pulp is evident. It’s not just a gallery of monsters—it’s a curated narrative where each piece nudges you toward speculating about the survivors, their gear, and the little human moments that survive amid the chaos. It’s art that makes you lean closer, squint at the details, and whisper, “Yes, I know what you’re trying to convey.”

Edouard’s relationship with Zombicide is less “assigned task” and more “shared obsession.” The art in this Collector’s Edition feels like a love letter to the game’s atmosphere: the clatter of metal on concrete, the soft glow of streetlights through broken glass, the quiet tension of a survivor counting their ammo while a lurking silhouette suggests something worse than a snack.
