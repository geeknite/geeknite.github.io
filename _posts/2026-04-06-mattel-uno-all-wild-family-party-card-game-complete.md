## Gameplay Mechanics
### Turn structure and flow
Gameplay mirrors classic UNO with a few key deviations depending on whether you’re in standard mode or All Wild mode. On your turn, you must either play a card that matches the color or number or play a Wild card to switch colors. If you can’t play, you draw one card. The Wild Party cards up the ante by adding optional actions or forced interactions that require players to perform a silly task or swap hands under certain conditions. The flow remains fast, which keeps the energy high and the table lively.
### Special rules and variants
The party variants are where the game shines for groups that love a little extra chaos. You can enable a few combinations:
- Quick-Draw Round: Everyone reveals a draw card together, and the highest draw card wins control for the next round.
- Costume Turn: A lighthearted rule that requires players to briefly “dress” their play with a humorous gesture or prop.
- Whisper Rule: The current player must describe their move in a whisper over a loud room, encouraging hilarity and chaos. 
The book suggests starting with a minimal set of twists and increasing complexity as players warm up. For families with younger players, start slow and skip the most punishing twists until you’re comfortable.
### House rules that actually improve the night
Some groups love to modify UNO so it ends in a dramatic, story-like fashion. If you’re considering house rules, try these:
- The “One-Timely Rule”: If someone accidentally breaks a rule, the turn passes to the left, and they draw a penalty card—timing is everything.
- The “Group Win”: A player wins not just by emptying their hand, but by completing the last twist clause—this can extend the game and add a theatrical finish.
- The “Swap-and-Trade”: Swapping hands becomes a dramatic moment with a time limit, adding tension and humor as players attempt to barter for a better hand.
