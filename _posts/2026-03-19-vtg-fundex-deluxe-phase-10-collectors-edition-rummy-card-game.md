## How Phase 10 actually happens: the core loop
The core loop of Phase 10 is simple on the surface and gloriously chaotic in practice:
- Draw card
- Play cards to complete the current phase (a set, a run, a combination, etc.)
- If you complete the phase, you advance to the next phase; if you can''t, you stay and draw again
- The first player to complete all ten phases wins

The VTG Fundex Deluxe Edition preserves this loop while offering a few ergonomic tweaks: a more legible card layout, visible color bands for each phase, and that tray for quick reference during the hectic mid-round sprints. The gameplay remains familiar if you''ve played Phase 10 before, but the packaging adds a little tactile pleasure to the process. It''s the kind of edition you pull out at a game night and hear the approving murmurs of “oh nice, this is the fancy one” from the crowd.

Phase-specific notes (to set expectations):

You''re balancing the need to hit the current phase with the long-term trend of your hand, which means you sometimes need to pass on the “optimal” move in the short term to set up a smoother run in rounds to come.
