Okay, enough about the serious bits—let’s talk care. The general care routine for a hand tufted wool rug is straightforward:

- Regular vacuuming on a low setting to avoid pulling up fibers. Use a vacuum without a beater bar in high-traffic zones, or use a rug beater if you’re old-school but careful.
- Treat spills immediately. Blot, don’t rub, with a clean cloth. Mild detergent for stains recommended by the manufacturer.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and to avoid color fade from sun exposure.
- Consider a rug pad to reduce slipping and provide extra cushioning. It also helps prolong the pile life by reducing friction against the floor.
- Avoid direct exposure to moisture; quick action is your friend.

A final tip: with a geometric scalloped rug, the edges can present a bit more risk of curling if left in direct sunlight for long periods or if the rug is jostled by heavy furniture. A properly placed rug pad and occasional readjustment can help maintain a crisp edge and prevent the scallops from curling in on themselves.

Moving toward the practicalities of space planning, there are a few recommended guidelines to ensure the rug does its best job in your home:

- In a living room with a sofa and chairs, aim for the rug to extend beyond the front legs of the furniture; this anchors seating and defines the space. If your space is long and narrow, you can place the rug under the front legs of the sofa and still have enough surrounding floor visible to frame the arrangement.
- In a dining room, the rug should be large enough so that chairs can be pulled out without catching on the edge. This ensures comfortable movement and avoids constant tugging at the corners.
- In a bedroom, consider laying the rug under the bed and extending toward each side at least 18-24 inches. The rug will peek out from beneath the bed at the sides, providing a soft landing for feet at night.
