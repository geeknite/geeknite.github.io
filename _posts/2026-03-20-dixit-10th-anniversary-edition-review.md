What is Dixit? A quick refresher for the uninitiated, or for those who found this review while deep in a rabbit hole of art appreciation:

- Dixit is a party game where players use dreamlike images on illustrated cards to tell stories. The goal is to be clever enough that your story is guessed by some, but not so obvious that everyone nails it in one go. It’s a game about suggestion, imagination, and the chaos of interpreting art while your friends pretend to be decision-makers.
- The base game contains 84 cards, each one more surreal than the last, with art that ranges from “storybook whimsy” to “that looks like a dream I had after watching a documentary about mushrooms.”
- Gameplay centers on one player giving a clue to the other players, who then choose cards from their hands that best fit the clue. The cards are revealed, and players bet on which card matches the storyteller’s clue. If everyone guesses the same card or if no one does, the storyteller gets fewer points. The moral of the story: words over pictures, unless the pictures suck and you have to rely on your words alone.

Dixit 10th Anniversary Edition: What’s New, What’s Old, and What the Heck is That New Sparkle?

The 10th Anniversary Edition arrives with the intent of both celebrating a decade of dreamlike misadventure and enticing a new wave of players who might be scared off by the sheer number of disparate art styles in earlier versions. In practice, you’ll find these components:

- A deluxe insert and upgraded components: thicker player boards, embossed tokens, a snazzy storage tray, and a rulebook that looks like it was printed on recycled star-dust. The packaging might be a little sturdier, which is nice if you’re the type who carries their games like precious inheritance. 
- The board layout has been adjusted in some editions; you’ll notice more space for player doodles and a few design tweaks that make table talk feel a touch more cinematic. The goal is to encourage storytelling and reduce table chaos, but we all know chaos loves a good stage.
- New artwork and layout tweaks across the card deck. Expect cards that look familiar enough to trigger nostalgia while presenting new angles that have you squinting and nodding like a drunk art critic who accidentally walked into a children’s party.

Packaging is glossy, art is inviting, and the overall vibe is “we took the dream you forgot you had and printed it on card stock.” The experience can feel like stepping into a gallery where someone silently whispers, “Make up a story about these pictures,” and the rest of your party responds with a chorus of ridiculous interpretations.

Gameplay Rhythm: The Dance of Clues, Cards, and Laughter

The core loop of Dixit remains elegant in this edition: the active storyteller gives a clue that is designed to be clever but not too revealing. Other players select a card from their hands that best matches the clue, a reveal happens, and everyone votes on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. The scoring is where the tension triumphs: you want some but not all players to guess your card. If your clue is too obvious, you’ll get punished with low points; if it’s obscure, you risk nobody guessing and losing your turn in the spotlight. It’s a delicate balance between “Another round!” and “We’ll never talk to you again.”

Solid social design, this is not. Dixit thrums with the social electricity of a party: the banter, the moments of “that wasn’t my card but it could’ve been,” and the way someone spins a clue into a story that makes everyone laugh or groan in equal measure. The 10th Anniversary edition doesn’t reinvent the wheel here; it polishes the wheel and gives it a fresh set of spokes. The result is a game that can feel like a cozy social ritual or a raucous improv exercise depending on the mood of your group.
