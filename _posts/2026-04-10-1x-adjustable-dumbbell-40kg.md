## Maintenance and Longevity
Maintenance is the unglamorous but essential part of owning any mechanical gym gear. A few simple habits will extend the life of your 1X:
- Wipe down after use: Sweat and salt can corrode metal parts, so wipe the handle and the weight plates after sessions.
- Check the locking mechanism periodically: A quick test before every workout can save you from an embarrassing mid-rep failure.
- Inspect for loose screws or any unusual play: If you notice looseness, tighten according to the manual. Tiny looseness can become a bigger issue if ignored.
- Keep it dry: Avoid leaving it in damp basements or humid rooms that could compromise the coating or lock mechanism.

Warranty details are generally included with the product. A robust warranty is a strong signal that the brand stands behind its build quality. If you’re pushing heavy loads frequently, take note of what the warranty covers (parts, mechanical failure, and wear-and-tear allowances) and how long it lasts. It’s not the sexiest part of gear talk, but it’s the thing that saves your budget when the universe decides to test your “home gym” dream.
