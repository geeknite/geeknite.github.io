## How to verify this “complete set” claim before you click buy

We all know that deals can shine brighter than a holo foil, only to reveal gaps once you’ve paid. Here’s a practical checklist to validate the claim:

- Confirm the count: Request a photo or a quick inventory list that enumerates each card in the set. If the seller has not cataloged the 151, that should be a red flag. You want to see a concise checklist with the card names in a readable order.
- Look for duplicates: If you notice duplicates in the photo, ask for clarification. A truly complete set should have 151 unique cards, not 150 plus a second Raichu that somehow counts twice.
- Examine holo quality: Reverse holo typically means a holofoil on the back or in the artwork itself. Ask for a close-up image of at least two or three cards to gauge holo consistency, surface wear, and centering.
- Condition notes: Inquire about any surface scratches, edge wear, or whitening. If the listing is silent on condition, push for specifics or a return policy.
- Return policy: A seller with a clear return policy gives you room to assess the set after arrival. If there’s no return window, that’s a risk—particularly with vintage items.

Pro tip: treat the listing as a starting point rather than a final word. Use your own photos to confirm your expectations. If you’re uncertain, consider negotiating for a smaller partial set first, then upgrade to the full collection once you’re satisfied with the quality.

## Storage, protection, and presentation: what to do after you buy

Assuming you win the bid or purchase the set, how you store and present the collection can dramatically affect your enjoyment and potential future value. Here are a few practical steps:

- Invest in sleeves and top loaders. You want a solid barrier against dust and minor handling wear for 151 cards. Use acid-free sleeves and solid top loaders to protect edges and holo surfaces.
- Consider a binder with a full page for each card. A large capacity binder keeps the entire set together and shows off the holo effect more than loose cards would.
- Use labeled dividers. Group the team cards, evolutions, and legendarys in a way that makes sense to you and helps you locate favorites quickly.
- Keep a digital catalog. Take photos of your cards, create a simple inventory, and track any damage or improvements in condition over time.
