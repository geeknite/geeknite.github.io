## Storage, Protection, and Care Tips
A thousand cards demand more than a shoebox and a prayer. The storage plan should consider:

- The climate: keep away from humidity and extreme heat. Foil surfaces can warp and lose their luster if exposed to heat or moisture for extended periods.
- The sleeves: use quality card sleeves with consistent thickness and avoid double-sleeving unless necessary. Double-sleeved cards can create bulk that makes your binder look like a science experiment.
- The binders: oversized binder pages or specialized MTG binders help you organize by rarity and set. If your collection eventually becomes a small museum, you will be proud of your careful curation.
- The cat factor: if you have a feline friend, ensure that they cannot treat your collection as a scratching post. The glitter glitter is a magnet for curious paws and the consequence is scuffed foil. It’s better to have a separate storage zone your cat cannot access, or at least teach your cat to respect the shiny shiny.

We have tested various storage strategies and found that climate-controlled rooms with consistent humidity and light exposure yield the best long-term results. It is not glamorous, but it works.
