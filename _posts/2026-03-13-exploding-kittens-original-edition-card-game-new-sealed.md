### What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition?
Exploding Kittens is a party card game created by Elan Lee, Shane Small, and The Oatmeal. It is a lightweight, fast paced game that leans into humor and misdirection, with a simple objective: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten card. The original edition includes a mix of Exploding Kitten cards, Defuse cards, and various action cards such as Attack, See the Future, Shuffle, Skip, and Favor that create a back and forth that is equal parts strategy and chaos.

### What's in the Box
A new sealed Original Edition box is a compact bundle of chaos. Inside you will usually find:

### Gameplay and Mechanics
The core loop is straightforward but allows for surprising depth. On a turn you will usually draw a card, but a large portion of the variability comes from the cards you decide to play. Defuse cards are the lifeline here; using one at the right moment can turn the tide. Some cards allow you to attack other players, forcing them to take extra draws or skip their next turn. There are see the future cards that let you peek at upcoming draws so you can calculate risk.

The end condition is dramatic enough to be memorable: the table removes a player when the explode card lands on them and they cannot Defuse. The moment of deciding to risk a draw, or to protect yourself, or to push someone else into the danger zone is where the social element shines. It is also where a new sealed copy feels like a fresh canvas, ready to be loaded with inside jokes and shared laughter.

### Setup and Playthrough Flow
Setting up Exploding Kittens is a quick affair. Shuffle the deck, deal a handful of cards to each player (the exact number is defined in the rule book), and place the remaining deck in the center. The Defuse cards go somewhere accessible for those who might get lucky enough to draw one early. Then you declare the start and everyone takes a turn.

The play flow is designed to be intuitive. New players can be brought into the loop after a brief explanation of the action cards and the explosion mechanic. The pace tends to be rapid; a table of 4 to 6 players can cycle through rounds quickly, with laughter punctuating the chaos at every turn. The See the Future cards contribute to a sense of control even at times when you feel like chaos is the only reliable constant.

### Strategy and Playstyle
While this is a party game, there is a surprising amount of strategy if you lean into it. Here are a few practical tips:
- Pay attention to other players' tells and the flow of the deck. If people are discarding many defuses early, you have a window.
- Use See the Future to map out a few draws ahead, especially if you suspect a dangerous card could be coming up.
- Attack and favor cards can disrupt the rhythm of your opponents. Use them to prolong the game or shift momentum when you see a vulnerable player.
- Saving Defuse cards for the critical moment is often the difference between survival and elimination.
- Keep track of who has already used defuses and who is likely to be in trouble.

### Expansions, Variants, and Alternatives
Original Edition is a good baseline, but the fun can be extended with expansions. Imploding Kittens, Streaking Kittens, and Attack on Titan crossovers are part of the ecosystem that expands the chaos by adding new card types and the opportunity for even wilder shenanigans. If you’re buying a sealed New Copy, you might consider whether you want to stick with the original wild ride or step into a larger deck with more cards and more risk.

It’s worth noting that the core mechanics stay intact across expansions, but the card count grows, and the probability of a big swing increase. If you find yourself repeatedly drawing Exploding Kitten cards early in the game, you might want to pick up an expansion that gives you more ways to influence the draw pile or to defuse a scent of impending doom.
