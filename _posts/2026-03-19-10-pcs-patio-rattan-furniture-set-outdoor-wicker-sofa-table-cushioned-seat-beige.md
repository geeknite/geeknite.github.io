### Assembly and maintenance

Assembly is the part where reality meets the brochure. The instruction manual will tell you to lay out the modules, connect the corner pieces, and then tighten all the bolts until they feel like they will not budge. In practice, you can expect about an hour or two of assembly for one person, depending on your patience level and whether you took snacks during the process. The hardware should be straightforward: standard bolts, washers, and an Allen wrench. If you are unlucky, you will spend an extra 20 minutes searching for the missing bolt because your cat decided to use it as a toy. That is not a misprint; cats do have opinions on furniture assembly.

Maintenance-wise, a simple wipe-down after outdoor use will go a long way. Use a mild soap solution for the frame if you notice dirt buildup, and let the cushions dry completely before storing them away for the winter. The cover fabric, if it is removable, should be laundered according to the care label. If you have a rainy climate, a light rain should not doom the set; you can hose it down after a thunderstorm and let it air dry in the sun. Just don’t leave the cushions out in a downpour for days on end, or you may discover that beige loses its bite more quickly than your sense of fashion on a rainy Tuesday.

### Weather resilience

Outdoor furniture lives or dies by its ability to withstand sun, rain, wind, and the occasional child’s juice box explosion. The PE rattan weave is designed for UV resistance, so color fade should be slower than your houseplant’s growth cycle. The cushions’ fabric score is important here: choose high-density cushions with quick-dry fill and resilient covers; you want to avoid a soggy beanbag situation when the first storm of the season hits. If you live in a tropical climate with heavy rain, invest in a breathable cover or plan to store the cushions indoors during heavy downpours. If you forget, you may wake up to a mildew party you did not RSVP to.
