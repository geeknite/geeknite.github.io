### Assembly and maintenance

Assembly is where reality visits the brochure and politely asks for a cup of tea. The manual will guide you to lay out modules, pair corner pieces, and tighten bolts until they feel like they’re in a committed relationship with their new home. In practice, budget about an hour to two for one person, factoring in snack breaks and your willingness to negotiate with a stubbornAllen wrench. The hardware uses standard bolts, washers, and an Allen key. If you’re unlucky, you’ll spend an extra 15–20 minutes hunting for a missing bolt because your cat decided it was a ball and a mystery. Pro tip: designate a tiny assembly assistant (even if it’s your dog’s paw) to supervise the process.

Maintenance-wise, a simple wipe-down after outdoor use does wonders. Use a mild soap solution on the frame if dirt builds up, and let the cushions dry completely before storing for winter. If the cushions are removable, launder according to care labels. In wetter climates, a light rain shouldn’t doom the set; you can hose it down after a thunderstorm and let it air dry in sun. Just don’t leave cushions out in a downpour for days; beige loses its crispness faster than your fashion sense on a rainy Tuesday.

Outdoor furniture lives and dies by how well it handles sun, rain, and wind force. The weave’s UV resistance aims to preserve color, while the cushions’ fabric seeks to resist stains and dry quickly. If you forget, a mildew party may RSVP to your patio and you weren’t even on the guest list.
