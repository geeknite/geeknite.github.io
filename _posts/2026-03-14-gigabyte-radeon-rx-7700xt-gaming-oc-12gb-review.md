### Power Delivery and Connectors

This card ships with a standard 8-pin + 8-pin (sometimes 8+8 with a modern orientation, depending on bin) power design, which is enough to feed most games at high clocks without requiring an industrial-strength PSU you’d feed a small city. It supports PCIe 4.0, which keeps it future-friendly enough for the next couple of GPU generations, or at least until the next gen arrives bearing its own DLC for compute shaders and ray tracing.

I/O options include three DisplayPort 2.1 and one HDMI 2.1, which is the mix you want for multi-monitor setups and ultra-high-refresh-rate gaming on supported panels. If you’re still rocking a single 1080p monitor, this card will still feel like a king because it can generate frames with enough swagger to make your older PC components jealous.

## Thermals, Noise, and Cooling Performance

A GPU’s core identity is its cooling story. If the fans sing like a choir of angry espresso machines, you know you have a problem. Fortunately, the RX 7700 XT GAMING OC treads a more civilized path. In my testing, the triple-fan setup kept the GPU in comfortable thermal territory even when we banged through synthetic workloads and a few hours of modern gaming at 1440p and 4K with high to max settings.

During extended sessions, GPU temperatures hovered in the mid-70s Celsius range under heavy load in a well-ventilated case. That’s not record-breaking, but it’s solid and practical. The cooling solution is quiet enough for a living room gaming rig when you’re in quieter titles, and it ramps up with a vengeance when you push the engine to its limits. If you like turning your gaming into a symphony of fan noise, you’ll want a social area away from the PC because the fans do sing when you start pushing beyond 2x to 3x stock boost clocks.

Noise-wise, at 50% fan speed or below, the card stays relatively hushed, perfect for streaming sessions or late-night raids where you want to hear your teammates over the engine roar but still hear your own thoughts occasionally. At full tilt, it’s a form of white noise—useful for blocking out the neighbor’s lawn mower or for sleeping guests who need a reason to stay in the guest room with the door closed.
