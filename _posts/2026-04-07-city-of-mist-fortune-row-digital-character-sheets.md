## What is Fortune Row, and why digital sheets? 
Fortune Row is a specific story arc within City of Mist that leans hard into the noir vibe while letting the player characters embody mythic identities with a modern twist. The digital character sheets take the classic City of Mist character-creation flow—roles, powers, icons, and the ever-present Beginnings of a Hero—and repackages it as a living document you can edit during a session. The goal is to reduce cognitive load during play so you can focus on describing a shadowy alleyway while not accidentally assigning your own power to the wrong side of your sheet.

The design philosophy behind these sheets is simple: readability, quick editing, and the ability to share notes with co-players without emailing a PDF back and forth until the sun comes up. If your table runs on sticky notes and pencil margins, these sheets may feel like moving from a battered notebook to a sleek dashboard. If your table runs on habit and ritual, the sheets should still feel familiar enough to not derail your immersion.

### How the sheets map to City of Mist concepts
City of Mist uses a few key abstractions: Traits (Rouses), Icons, and the overarching Mythic Identity. Fortune Row’s digital sheets try to mirror that structure with sections for the character’s Drive (what motivates them), Identity (the mythic persona), and their Skills/Tags (the mechanical bits that determine success or failure). The result is a framework that supports both the noir detective mood and the larger-than-life heroism that City of Mist encourages.


## UI and usability: clicking with purpose
One of the biggest questions with digital character sheets is: can this thing stay out of the way? Fortune Row’s sheets lean into a clean, modular layout that resembles a well-organized notebook more than a spreadsheet maze. The editors allow you to add, remove, and rearrange sections as your Fortune Row plot evolves. This flexibility is excellent for groups who love to re-flavor a Spark of Inspiration at the drop of a hat or when a GM suddenly introduces a new antagonist who wants to steal your amethyst badge of office.

In practice, you’ll see sections labeled with intuitive prompts—Motives, Skills, Incarnations, and Look/Feel (the aesthetic knobs that keep your player-characters from looking like normal humans at a cosplay event). The interface supports both quick, on-the-fly edits and more deliberate, paper-trail-like note-taking. A nice touch is the live preview of how a change to your Icon’s power might ripple into your mighty deeds or dramatic missteps. It’s like watching a tiny magic show unfold in real time, with your sheet as the stage manager.

The built-in editing tools respect the City of Mist vibe: you can tag edges, tweak limits, and adjust the balance between your Power Level and your Street Smarts. For long campaigns, this matters because you’re balancing mythic potential with the real-world constraints of a detective’s day job. If you’re playing Fortune Row as a one-shot, you’ll enjoy how quickly you can spin up a character, select a few mythic “Rouses,” and be ready to roll in under ten minutes.

### Accessibility and cross-platform concerns
Fortune Row’s digital sheets don’t pretend to reinvent the wheel; they gently improve it. The UI scales reasonably on desktop and tablet, but you’ll want to keep an eye on mobile: small screens can make the more verbose sections feel crowded. The design team has done a decent job of ensuring high-contrast labels and legible typography, but if you wear gamer-fatigued eyeballs like badges, consider exporting or printing a quick reference sheet for table talk. It’s not perfect on tiny devices, but it’s practical enough to keep your game moving without shouting at the screen.

If your group relies heavily on accessibility features, you’ll be glad that the sheets support screen-reader friendly labeling and keyboard navigation for core actions. It’s not a full-fledged accessibility overhaul, but it’s a thoughtful inclusion that makes Fortune Row more inclusive than a lot of digital tools hiding behind fancy fonts.

Compared to a traditional printed sheet, Fortune Row’s digital version wins in the speed department and in giving you a living, mutable document. Against other digital tools, Fortune Row’s strength lies in its clarity and city-appropriate flavor. ## Crafting your character: a quick-start workflow
1) Pick your Icon and Identity. The sheets encourage you to lock in your mythic motif early so you can see how it threads through Actions and Consequences.
2) Outline a Drive and a Personal Tag. These act as narrative hooks and mechanical modifiers. The digital sheet visually ties these choices to your Skill checks, making the correlation between story and system obvious and accessible.
3) Allocate a small set of Rouses. Think of Rouses as the triggers that push your identity into action when the moment calls for it. The sheet’s prompts help you decide when a Rouse makes sense and how it should shape your next scene.
4) Fill in your Look and your Connections. This gives your GM something concrete to reference for mood setting and world-building when Fortune Row’s neon fog thickens.
5) Save and share. The real magic happens when your group uses the share features to keep everyone aligned—no more “I forgot to tell you I changed this power” chaos mid-session.
