## Capacity and layout

Capacity is the headline act here. The Pro 1000+ Altered Edition claims space for over a thousand cards, and in practice you can likely fit a little more when you bend the space-time continuum with careful stacking and half-sleeved duplicates. For someone who runs a serious collection or a game night that features sprawling card-based titles, the interior dividers accommodate various deck sizes and sleeve thicknesses. You can separate games by title and expansion, or go full chaos mode and create a hybrid system for all the of-age, non-card items you stash in there for the inevitable post-game snack run.

One neat feature is the ability to adjust the interior trays without tools. The trays click into place and stay put, which is essential when your gaming buddy decides to slam the lid shut with the theatrical flourish of a magician completing a trick. The layout is forgiving enough to handle both standard 2.5 x 3.5 inch cards and slightly oversized or trimmed variants, provided you accept a little more snugness in the compartments.

There are also optional inserts aimed at specific game families, which is the sort of thing that makes you feel seen as a collector. If you own a lot of Gamegenic sleeves, you can expect a snug fit that reduces rattling. If you have a mixed bag of sleeves from various manufacturers, there is a minor chance you will hear a soft creak as the box settles into its preferred arrangement on your shelf. It is not loud, but it is a small reminder that even premium storage has opinions about your life choices.

## Practical setup tips

- Plan your layout before you open the box. Sketch a rough map of where you want base decks, expansions, and accessory trays to live. A little planning now saves you from a rework later that could trigger a minor crisis of organization.
- Label your sections with durable, legible labels. You will thank yourself after a year when you forget exactly which expansion belongs where.
- Use sleeves that fit snugly but allow easy edge access. This reduces wear on your cards during retrieval, and reduces the risk of card edge damage when you slide a deck back into the tray.
- If you travel with your set, consider a protective sleeve or bag for the entire box. The portability of this model is good, but you do not want a scenario where a rolling suitcase meets your gaming box at a silly angle.
- Keep a small rulebook or cheat sheet handy inside the lid. It is not strictly necessary, but it is a nice touch that saves you from explaining to your group that you cannot find the base deck because you used the wrong lid compartment for the dice again.
