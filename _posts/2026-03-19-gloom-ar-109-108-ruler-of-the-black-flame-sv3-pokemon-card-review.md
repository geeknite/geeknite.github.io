### Print quality and condition notes for NM status
When you see NM on a card, you picture a tiny, nearly invisible edge wear and a perfectly centered image. NM is not a myth; it is a discipline. For a Gloom AR from SV3, NM means the following: the holofoil is crisp with no obvious scratches; the centering is close to perfect (but not miracle-perfect); and there is no whitening on the edges. If you flip the card over and see any black smudges from a factory misprint, you are flirting with lightly played territory — not NM. In the Japanese market, the card stock tends to be a bit firmer than some other regions, so misprints are rarer to spot, but the real tell is how the holo light catches the flame motif. In a sense, NM is a decision you make after weighing the card’s condition in your own mind, the way a cat contemplates a laser pointer on the carpet.

## Collectibility and the NM condition: practical advice

### How to protect and present this card
If you want NM status to last, you need to protect the edge wear and to keep sunlight away from the holo surface. Sleeves, top-loaders, and a rigid binder page are your friends. Avoid stacking the card in a binder where it can press against other cards; that is how you wake up one morning with a fingerprint in the holo. For long-term storage, consider a desiccant packet and a climate-controlled room if you are truly serious about preserving the AR glare. Do not attempt to clean the card with household cleaners; that is a one-way ticket to a damaged aura and a disappointed collection.
