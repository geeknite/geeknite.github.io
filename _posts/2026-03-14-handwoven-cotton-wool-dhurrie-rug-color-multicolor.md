## Materials and Craft: Why Cotton-Wool and Why It Matters
Most handwoven rugs face a critical decision: what fiber contracts or expands with time, and how many pet rotations can it survive before the loom gets jealous. The cotton-wool dhurrie solves this with a clever blend. Cotton brings structure, softness, and easy washability; wool adds warmth, resilience, and a tiny bit of woolly drama when the vacuum cleaner sneaks up behind it like a stealth ninja. The result is a rug that remains comfortable under foot, resists crushing, and recovers relatively well after furniture rearrangements—the floor equivalent of a good reboot.

From a textile nerd’s perspective, this blend is also a practical choice for dye uptake. Cotton’s smoother surface helps the dye cling evenly, while wool’s natural scales can lock in color in ways that look richer over time. In multicolor designs, this means the hues aren’t just flat patches of pigment; they mingle like a well-executed bevel on a 3D-printed coffee mug—bold, but not loud enough to require earplugs.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Rainbow Bright
Maintenance is where many rugs either shine or crack. For cotton-wool dhurries, the cleaning playbook is forgiving enough for a busy household but still demands some common-sense rules. Here are practical tips you can actually implement:

- Vacuum on a low setting with a brush attachment to avoid pulling fibers. Do not use a beater bar on delicate woven rugs.
- Address spills quickly with blotting, not rubbing. Rubbing can push stains deeper and puff up the weave in ways that ruin the flat finish.
- For spot cleaning, use a mild detergent mixed with water. Test on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear. If you have pets, consider a routine to reduce nail marks and claw-induced pilling.
- Avoid direct sun exposure for extended periods to minimize color fading. If your room faces a sunny window, rotate the rug or deploy window treatments to maintain color integrity.

If something dramatic happens (think red wine, coffee, or experimental beet smoothie art), act fast. Blot, rinse with clean water, and repeat. Persistent stains may require professional cleaning, especially if the dye is not fully colorfast. In many cases, however, a well-timed blot and a mild cleaner will salvage the rug’s appearance without the need for specialized services.

## Practical Buying Tips: What to Look For in a Handwoven Dhurrie
Before you click the buy button, here are practical pointers that can save you from rug-regret:
- Size matters: measure your space, leaving 6-10 inches of floor visible around the rug when it’s a living room, or align edges with the bed for bedrooms for a cohesive look.
- Pattern scale: larger rooms benefit from larger patterns to avoid visual overcrowding; smaller rooms gain texture with medium-scale patterns.
- Color intent: pick colors that either complement or provide a deliberate contrast with your dominant furniture hues.
- Finish and weave knowledge: flatter weaves are easier to clean and less prone to trapping dust but may show footprints more readily, while slightly denser weaves hide footprints better but require more careful cleaning.
- Craftsmanship cues: straight edges, consistent weave density, and clean transitions between color blocks are signs of quality workmanship.
