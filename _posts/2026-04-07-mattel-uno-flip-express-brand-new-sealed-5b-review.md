## How to Play UNO Flip Express (A Quick Guide)
If you’ve never played UNO before, do not panic. This is the version that will teach you the basics and then some, with a few twists to keep you on your toes. Here’s a concise guide to get you started:

- Setup: Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player. Place the remaining deck in the center as the draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile. On your turn, you must play a card that matches the color or number or symbol of the top card on the discard pile, or play a Wild card to change color.
- Express twist: In Express play, you’ll encounter lighter rule-checking, faster turn sequence, and sometimes an emphasis on rapid color changes or quick action cards. The exact text on the cards will guide you, but the spirit is simple: keep the pace up and the color changes smooth.
- Action cards: Skip means the next player loses a turn, Reverse flips play order, Draw Two forces the next player to draw two cards, and Wild cards let you choose the color. The key here is to stay attuned to rapid shifts as people start chaining effects to squeeze out a victory before you realize what happened.
- Winning: The first player to discard all their cards wins the round. In some variants, you’ll need to call “UNO” when you have one card left. If you forget, other players can remind you with a playful penalty, which is a social lubricant that never gets old.

Tips for a smoother experience:
- Have a dedicated area for the draw pile and discard pile so play can flow without constant card hunting.
- Decide whether you want to enforce UNO calls strictly or waive the penalty if someone forgets. It’s a social choice, not a law of physics.
- Keep drinks away from the play area. If someone knocks over a glass, your tidy rhythm ends in chaos and you’ll be searching for a red card in a sea of blue. This is not a mimic of a lab strewn with test tubes; this is a living room rush-hour experiment.
