### Safety and maintenance tips (to keep the fun from turning into a trip to the ER)

- Regularly inspect the springs and mat for wear. Replace any frayed springs or torn sections before a mis-timed jump becomes a medical concern.
- Check the enclosure net for sagging sections. A sagging net can be a gateway to “oh no” moments when the kids go airborne.
- Anchor the trampoline where possible to prevent movement during windy days. A little extra security goes a long way toward keeping neighbors calm and your trampoline upright.
- Keep the surface clean. Debris can cause slips or tears in the mat, and nobody wants to be the family clown who trips on a rogue leaf.

Tips for a smooth build:

## Maintenance and care hacks to extend the life of your trampoline

- Inspect monthly for rust on frame components and signs of wear on the jumping mat and safety padding.
- Wipe down the mat and frame with a gentle cleaner to remove dirt and grime accumulated during outdoor use. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the mat.
- Store or cover the trampoline during long periods of non-use or severe weather to reduce UV exposure and wind damage.
- Check the springs for signs of stretching or corrosion; replace springs in matched sets to keep the bounce even.
- Keep the enclosure’s zipper clean and lubricated if the mechanism starts sticking. A small dab of silicone lubricant can do wonders.
