Collectibility: grading and preservation tips
-----------------------------------------------
Collecting Carddass cards is as much about preservation as it is about acquisition. Here are a few practical suggestions to protect your No. 262 and keep it in prime shape:
- Sleeving: Use soft sleeves that are sized for standard Japanese cards to avoid edge wear. Avoid over-tight sleeves that crimp the corners.
- Top loaders: For display, a rigid top loader with moderate snugness can protect the card from bending while still allowing you to present it in a binder.
- Handling: Wash hands before handling to avoid oils and fingerprints. A microfiber cloth can be used for light cleaning when you’re adjusting the display.
- Environment: Keep away from direct sunlight and humid spaces. The yellowing and warping that comes from sun exposure is the enemy of any printed collectible.
- Grading: If you ever decide to submit your No. 262 for grading, document its condition with high-resolution photos and note any micro scratches or edge wear. A well-documented submission improves the odds of a favorable grade.
