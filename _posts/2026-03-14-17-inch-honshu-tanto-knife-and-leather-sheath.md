## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
Durability is partly about design and partly about maintenance. Stainless steel is forgiving, but all knives require care to last. Here are a few tips that align with the design ethos of this Honshu Tanto:

- Cleaning: After use, wipe the blade clean and dry. If you’ve used it in damp conditions, apply a thin layer of oil to the blade to prevent rust and keep the surface from drying out. The TPR handle cleans easily and dries quickly, which is exactly what you want when you’re in the field.
- Sharpening: For sharpening, you’ll want to pick a comfortable angle and use a suitable sharpening stone or tool designed for stainless blades. The Tanto geometry means you’ll preserve a strong edge at the tip, which is critical for piercing tasks. Don’t try to mysteriously perfect the edge in one go—consistent, gentle strokes over time will yield the best results.
- Sheath care: Leather needs love too. If you want to keep the sheath supple and attractive, treat it with a leather conditioner every so often. This preserves the look and reduces the risk of cracking when the weather is dry or when the sheath is pressed against your gear for weeks on end.
- Storage: Store in a dry place to avoid moisture getting trapped near the blade or within the sheath. A little humidity never hurt a blade, but sustained moisture can lead to unnecessary wear over long periods.

By following these steps, you’ll keep the blade performing well for years and ensure that the leather sheath remains a reliable companion with that classic patina that shows you’ve lived a few adventures.
