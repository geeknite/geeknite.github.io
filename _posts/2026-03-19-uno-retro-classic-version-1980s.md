## How to Play: The Core Rules, With Retro Flair
If you already know UNO, you’re halfway home. The core concept remains: match the color or number to play, act on action cards to alter the flow, and be the first to shout “UNO” when you have one card left. The retro edition adds a few packaging flourishes and a slight emphasis on quick rounds that keep the energy high while preserving the classic feel.

- Objective: Get rid of all your cards. The first player to discard all cards wins the round.
- Setup: Shuffle, deal seven cards to each player, place the remaining deck in the center as the draw pile, and flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Gameplay: On your turn, you must play a card that matches either color or number (or symbol, if you want to get fancy with the wilds). If you cannot play, draw one card from the draw pile. If you draw and can play, you may still play it immediately as part of a single turn if your rules allow the casual house variation.
- Special Cards: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four perform as you’d expect. Some variations in the 1980s packaging tweak the art on the back of the cards to hint at old-school target markets (board-game cafes of the era, you know who you are).
- Winning: The moment you have one card left, you must call “UNO” to avoid a penalty. If another player calls it first, you’ll need to draw a couple of extra cards as the comedic penalty, making your victory feel more earned and your flex less embarrassing.

The retro version doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it buffets the wheel with a bit of nostalgia and lighter competition that makes a casual gathering feel like a time-traveling party rather than a tournament. If you’re expecting the full modern UNO deck with app integrations and online matchmaking, you’ll be surprised by how refreshing the simplicity feels when paired with whoopee cushion-level laughter around the table.
