## How to play: Express in a hurry
If you’ve played UNO before, you already know the basics: match color or number, play action cards to mix things up, and keep an eye on the person who has one card left, because that person is about to become the most popular human in the room for a solid 2 minutes or until chaos ensues.

What Express adds (or trims) to the classic flow:

- Shorter rounds: expect fewer turns per player before someone yells UNO, which means you might actually finish a full game during a coffee break. If you hate waiting for your turn to arrive like a distant relative at Thanksgiving, Express is your friend.
- Simpler setup: less fluff, more rapid-fire card action. The decks are tuned for quick-start games, so there’s less time spent arguing about whether you should shuffle again or just flip the table and start over.
- Speed-friendly rules: some editions swap out lengthy rule clarifications for quick, one-line bullet points. The goal is to keep the table moving and the jokes flowing.

Here is a basic playthrough outline to set expectations:

1) The dealer shuffles and deals a handful of cards to each player. In Express, that hand is intentionally lean to speed things up.
2) The top card from the draw pile is flipped and begins the action. If it’s a flip-worthy card, chaos ensues in the most delightful possible way.
3) On your turn, you can either play a card that matches color or number or use an action card to shift the flow (skip, reverse, draw two, or the express variants included in your deck).
4) When you’re down to one card, shout UNO. If someone else catches you or you fail to shout UNO in time, penalty rules can be mercilessly enforced by your crew.
5) The first player to get rid of all their cards wins. The rest of the table shrugs and contemplates whether they should start a serious gym routine or adopt a new hobby that doesn’t involve tiny rulers and counting points.

A few tips for victory and sanity:
- Pay attention to color shifts on the flip side. In UNO Flip, the “dark side” cards can appear and flip the current wind direction—keep an eye on the back of your own cards and your opponents’ faces.
- Don’t be afraid to use quick-start rules if time is short. The express mode is designed for a quick victory lap, not a multi-hour epic saga.
- Use the rule sheet as a safety net but rely on your group’s social rules for how loudly you celebrate victory. A well-timed victory dance is half the fun.
