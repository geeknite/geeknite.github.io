Gameplay overview and why it works

Dixit is marketed as a party game, but that description barely scratches the surface. It is a storytelling game that also tests your ability to read a room and to read art in unexpected ways. The basic flow is simple enough for new players: one player takes on the role of the storyteller and picks a card from their hand, then gives a clue—this clue can be a phrase, a word, a sentence, or something that evokes the card’s mood. Other players pick a card from their own hands that best matches the storyteller’s clue. All chosen cards are shuffled and revealed, and players vote on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. Points are awarded when the storyteller’s clue is guessed by some but not all players, rewarding both creativity and a little cunning. The storyteller gains more points if their clue is not too obvious, but not so obscure that nobody gets it. It’s a delicate balance between being evocative and being obscure, which is precisely why Dixit thrives in a party setting where people feel comfortable being both dramatic and silly.

The new sealed copy preserves this dynamic perfectly. You get the same broad-spectrum creativity with a slightly different flavor depending on the cards within your set. Some players love the art’s dreamlike ambiguity; others love the social puzzle of figuring out what clues will resonate with their friends without giving away the answer too easily. The scoring system remains intuitive: if at least one but not all players guess the storyteller card, the storyteller gets a handful of points; if everyone or no one guesses it, the storyteller gets fewer or no points. The other players gain points by matching the most convincing choice for the card. In practice, that means you will celebrate when your clue defeats the obvious choices and groan when your own hints reveal your exact intention to your friends in a room full of sharp-eyed cynics.

Even though you might leave the plastic wrap on for a few weeks, you should still take care of the components.
