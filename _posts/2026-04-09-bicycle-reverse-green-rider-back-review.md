## Design and Artwork
### The reverse color philosophy
The “Reverse” label isn’t just a keyword for marketing; it signals a deliberate swap in the back color palette while leaving the suit icons, pips, and aces intact. In practice, that means a deck that catches the eye under bright lights but still looks like the familiar friend you’ve known at card tables and coffee-shop magic shows. Green on the back brings a certain calm-energy to the table—less jarring than red in the middle of a long practice session, more “you’re here for the tricks, not a fireworks display.” It’s a color choice that invites different light interactions, with the back pattern becoming slightly more legible under certain angles. If you’re a casual player who enjoys the look of a deck but can’t stomach the loudness of lime, the green Rider Back is a nice compromise.

Bicycle’s Rider Back pattern is a simple, geometric, almost retro motif. There’s a classic nice-workmanship vibe—edges straight, print registration solid, and the tuck case remains the display-case counterpart to a well-built instrument.
