## Setup and Quick Start
UNO has a rhythm: deal seven cards to each player, shuffle the deck, and let the chaos begin. The BTS edition keeps that rhythm but layers on a little extra tempo. Here’s a practical, step-by-step setup you can perform in less time than it takes to pick a playlist for a party:

1) Shuffle the BTS UNO deck thoroughly. Pro tip: the face cards with BTS styling look cooler when you shuffle aggressively; pretend you’re bringing down the lights at a rehearsal—your friends will nod in impressed approval. 
2) Deal seven cards to each player. And yes, the “Uno” rule still matters: when you have one card left, you must shout “Uno!” before your turn ends. If you forget, a quick penalty dance might ensue (you and the nearest player get to choreograph a literal quick-draw of your mood).
3) Place the deck in the center and flip the top card to start the discard pile. If the first card is a BTS action card, prepare for a surprising turn of events right out of the gate.
4) Review the BTS-specific action cards. They function like standard UNO cards with some pop-cultural flair. The exact effects are in the small rule card, but expect things like “Dance Break” or “Glow-Up” type modifiers on rare occasions—these cards inject party vibes without derailing the core UNO rules.
5) Determine who goes first. The dynamic is the same as any UNO game, but the BTS aesthetic makes the first draw feel like a rehearsal call.

The quick-start includes no complicated learning curves. If you’ve played UNO at any point in your life, you’ll be fine. If you haven’t, you’ll still catch on because the turns are simple: match color or number or play a BTS twist card and watch the table react with exclamations and mild chaos.

- Thematic actions: Some of the BTS-specific cards pull the group into a moment of shared reaction (like a “Dance Break” or “Stage Lights” moment). These moments aren’t weighted in the scoring—so they’re about social energy rather than strategic advantage.
