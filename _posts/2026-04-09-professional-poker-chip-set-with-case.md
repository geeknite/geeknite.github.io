### Materials and Denominations
Chips come in a few different material families:
- Ceramic: Durable, premium feel, crisp edges; tends to be heavier and more expensive

- Pure clay: The premium classic; very expensive but beloved by enthusiasts

Denominations are color-coded for quick reads. Common schemes include:
- White = 1
- Red = 5
- Blue = 10
- Green = 25
- Black = 100
- Purple = 500

## Build Quality and Design
### Chips: Feel and Longevity
The feel of a chip is more important than most players admit. A good chip sits in your hand with a satisfying weight and a texture that doesn’t slip when your coffee-kissed hands are slightly damp from sweating out a bluff. Chips should maintain their integrity over time; you don’t want a chip that chips away at the first tournament or one with fading numbers.
- Ceramic chips: Pros — durable, premium feel, crisp edges; Cons — can be pricey and slippery
- Clay composite: Pros — traditional feel, durable, affordable; Cons — edges may wear over time
- Pure clay: Pros — classic vibe; Cons — expensive and delicate

Case: Fit, padding, and security
The interior padding is what keeps your chips safe during transport. A well-designed case keeps chips in their designated slots and protects delicate edge designs. A robust latch or locking mechanism adds a sense of security; you’re not just carrying chips, you’re carrying confidence. Some cases include a dedicated deck sleeve or card holder; some offer extra space for dealer buttons and duplicate dice if you misplace them.

Ergonomics and usability
A well-made case will be easy to grab and comfortably carry. The interior layout should be intuitive, not a jigsaw puzzle every time you open the lid. The ability to quickly load chips into stacks and then count them without fidgeting reduces the “table talk” time and helps you get to the game faster.

## Practicality and Table Performance

Portability matters for those who like to spread their game nights across friends’ houses or clubs. A case should be easy to carry, with a handle that won’t snap off after a few trips. ### On-Table Performance
The right chips read well to the eye and the hand—denominations visible from across the table and a weight that feels consistent at every stack. A good set reduces confusion and speeds up betting decisions. The chips should shuffle easily and stack neatly without tipping or slipping.

## Comparisons to Other Gear
### Ceramic vs Composite vs Clay
- Ceramic: Crisp edges, longer-life, premium feel. Higher price, but often worth it for frequent players.
- Clay: Traditional, good weight and feel, a bit more susceptible to wear on edges.
- Composite: Budget-friendly, durable, widely available.

### Durability and Longevity
Ceramic chips generally win for longevity, but proper care matters for all types. A good case extends life by protecting chips from jostling and moisture.

## Maintenance and Longevity
