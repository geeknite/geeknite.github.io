## Care and maintenance: keeping the texture alive

### Regular upkeep
Vacuuming with a beater bar on low suction is generally recommended for wool rugs, but you may want to avoid aggressive scrubbing along the weave. The aim is to remove surface dust and debris without fraying the fibers. A gentle approach goes a long way here. Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear, especially if you have a heavy foot traffic zone in front of a couch or entryway.

### Spot cleaning strategies
Spills happen. For most liquids, blotting with a clean, absorbent cloth is your first move. If you need a cleaner, use a wool-safe solution that won’t strip the natural oils from the fibers. Avoid harsh bleaches and industrial cleaners that can dull the fibers or alter color. If you’re dealing with stubborn stains, test in a small, inconspicuous area first and consider seeking professional cleaning advice.

### Long-term storage tips
If you must store the rug, roll it rather than folding it to prevent creases. Wrap in breathable fabric or paper to avoid moisture buildup. Storing in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight helps preserve color integrity over the years.
