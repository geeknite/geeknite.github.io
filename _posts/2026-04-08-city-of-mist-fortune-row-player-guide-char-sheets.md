## What Fortune Row is in City of Mist
Fortune Row is not just a location; it is a mood. A place where the masks you wear are as important as the powers you wield, and where the line between legend and hustler blurs with every late night espresso. The district is packed with a mix of established fashion houses and run-down storefronts that cook up trouble with equal parts spice and danger. In a game where Mythos and Logos are the two main currencies, Fortune Row serves as a perfect playground for archetypes that hatch in a dimly lit club and morph into a plan when the neon sign flickers 3am.

For players, Fortune Row offers a dense network of hooks, factions, and micro mysteries. You can play the glamorously doomed photographer chasing a mythic subject, the fixer who knows a contact on every block, or the mythic who blends in by holding a real job at a coffee shop that never seems to close. The player guide for Fortune Row should give you a sense of tone, a toolkit for building a pocket myth, and a clear path to integrate your character into the ongoing narrative without stepping on the GM’s toes.

## Elements of the Fortune Row Player Guide
The Fortune Row guide is designed to feel like a friendly manual handed over at a late night after party — thorough but not suffocating. Here are the core elements you should expect to find, and how to leverage each one for maximum dramatic effect.

### Tag and Concept Framework
City of Mist is built on tags, power descriptors, and mythic resonances. Fortune Row emphasizes tags tied to the location — glamour, crime, art, and rumor. A solid character concept in Fortune Row should include:

- A strong Theme Tag: something that signals both your mythic identity and the role you play in Fortune Row. Think refined misunderstandings, a magical social circle, or a rumor that won’t die.
- A Motif: a recurring symbol or action that threads through your scenes. It could be a signature way you speak, a recurring prop, or a ritual you perform before a big reveal.
- A Stress Track or Fate Levers: how you respond when the heat comes, and how your story might bend toward a mythic payoff rather than a quick escalation.

### Character Creation Flow for Fortune Row
The guide outlines a flow that keeps you in the neon river rather than on the sidewalk.

1) Pick a core archetype that fits Fortune Row: the Glamourist, the Insider, the Ringmaster, the Street Poet, the Quick Dyer of Truth, etc. These archetypes exist in the core book, but Fortune Row lends itself to more specialized variants. 2) Attach a local hook: a shop, a club, a specific event that ties your motive to Fortune Row. 3) Define three core tags: a mythic power tag, a social tag, and a vulnerability tag. 4) Map a relationship network: who owes you a favor, who you owe favors to, and the rival who wants your spotlight. 5) Choose a personal quest: a mythic desire that drives you beyond mere survival.

### Character Sheets: Layout and Usability
Fortune Row character sheets should be legible at a glance and flexible enough to capture shifting identities. The sheets typically include space for:

- Identity header: name, real name, and public mask details
- Mythic tags: the powers, limits, and resonances you carry
- Clues and connections: hooks that link you to the city and its factions
- Stress and Fate: a compact way to track how close you are to tipping into a mythic event
- Gear and assets: items that carry narrative weight and mechanical utility

The guide insists on a clean typographic hierarchy and consistent iconography. It helps if there is a dedicated space for narrative moments that align with Fortune Row themes — a tiny panel where you jot down a rumor you heard on the street, or an event that might become a turning point in a scene.

## The Character Sheets: What Works and What to Tweak
Fortune Row character sheets should feel both elegant and functional. Here is a breakdown of what to look for, and how to tweak it for your table.

### Cover and Identity Section
The cover should display a silhouette or emblem that hints at your mythic identity. A bold name, a mask type, and a location tie keep your concept anchored. If the sheet allows, add a short line about your public alteration or disguise. A good practice is to include a line for a signature look — the thing your mask wears that makes you recognizable in a crowd.

### Tags and Powers
Power tags in Fortune Row need to be flexible and narrative. Instead of a long powers list that resembles a spreadsheet, aim for 5 to 7 core tags with two or three resonant effects. For example, a tag like Shadow Carousel might grant you stealth in social arenas, plus a dramatic reveal when you narrate a scene that twists perception. The key is to ensure tags have both a mechanical outcome and a story payoff.

### Clues, Connections, and Factions
Fortune Row thrives on networks. The sheet should have dedicated sections to track clues you have gathered, allies and enemies in the city, and any faction you either belong to or are trying to infiltrate. A good sheet makes it easy to swap a connection in and out as new rumors surface. This is where Fortune Row becomes a living map rather than a static profile.

### Personal Quests and Consequences
A solid Fortune Row sheet includes a place to write your personal quest and the visible consequences of failure. The more you push your character toward the mythic end of the spectrum, the more likely you are to slip into a memorable climax. Use this space to record what a failed attempt would reveal, which rival would celebrate, and what new myth you might unlock as a result.

## How to Use Fortune Row Sheets at the Table
A sheet is not a prison, it is a launchpad. Here are tips to maximize your Fortune Row experience:

- Make your tags breathe. Each tag should have at least one mechanical effect and one narrative hook. If a tag only gives you a plus, you might be missing the dramatic potential.
- Narrate first, roll later. In City of Mist, your narration triggers what the dice try to resolve. Start with a vivid description and then lean on your tags to shape the outcomes.
- Use clues as plot seeds. Clues on your sheet should be the starting point for scene ideas rather than trophies to collect. The GM should be able to spin a scene quickly from a clue you’ve left behind.
- Keep relationships dynamic. A fortune row table thrives on shifting loyalties and rivalries. Update connections as the story evolves; a sheet that remains static feels like a painting in a gallery — nice to look at but not alive.

## Gameplay Flow and Fortune Row Timing
Fortune Row sessions tend to swing between two modes: social intrigue and mythic escalation. The player guide encourages you to prepare for both.

### Social Intrigue
- Scenarios centered around reputations, rumors, and the power of perception.
- Scenes often revolve around a reveal, a party, or a club where masks are worn literally and metaphorically.
- Your role as a player is to navigate alliances, extract information, and decide when to reveal or conceal your power tag.

### Mythic Escalation
- The city pushes back when a myth breaks the surface. A tag may escalate the scene, forcing a dramatic shift in narrative.
- You may trigger a temporary boon or consequence that tests your control of your own myth.
- The climax often involves a choice: save a friend, reveal a truth, or seal a secret forever.

## GM Tips for Fortune Row Players
A good GM for Fortune Row is a curator of moments. Here are practical tips for facilitating memorable sessions:

- Lean into faction dynamics. Fortunes Row thrives on the interplay between power centers. Let players negotiate with factions that claim different visions of the city.
- Use environmental storytelling. Neon signs, music cues, and crowd dynamics can be used to cue a shift in the mood. A club’s lighting can reflect your city’s mood; a red glow can signal danger, a blue glow a moment of truth.
- Encourage character-driven reveals. The setting invites secrets to break the surface. When a secret is revealed, reward the player with a new tag or a powerful narrative option.
- Keep the power balance dynamic. It is easy to tilt toward excess in a city of masks. Your best sessions come when the players are forced to recalibrate their plan after a dramatic misstep.
