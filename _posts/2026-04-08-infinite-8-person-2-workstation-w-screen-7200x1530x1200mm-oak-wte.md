## Ergonomics and Comfort
### Seating and Posture Considerations
With eight workstations side-by-side, comfort is not merely a feature; it’s a requirement. Each station can be configured with height-adjustable legs, allowing for standing or sitting preferences. The engineering team behind the Infinite 8-Person pays particular attention to leg clearance, knee room, and the ability to slide a chair in and out without provoking a chain reaction of “excuse me”s from adjacent users.

For teams that alternate between tasks—coding, design, QA, and the occasional sprint planning—the ability to switch to a standing posture for longer brainstorm sessions is a real productivity booster. Just be mindful that a tall person near the end of the bench may need a longer reach to adjust their own height; thankfully, the modular nature means you can position the central screen and power hubs to minimize awkward stretches.

One of the standout features is the central display system. The screen acts as a shared focal point without stealing personal desk space. A well-implemented cable management system reduces desk clutter, which is crucial when you’ve got eight people navigating around a few thousand dollars’ worth of monitors and peripherals.
