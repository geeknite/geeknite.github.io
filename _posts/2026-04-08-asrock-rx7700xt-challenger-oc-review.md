## Overclocking guide: how far can you push a Challenger OC?

If you enjoy fiddling with clocks as much as you enjoy the thrill of a perfect headshot, the Challenger OC offers a reasonable playground. Here are practical tips to coax a little more horsepower without turning your rig into a furnace.

- Start with a modest core clock bump: +50 to +100 MHz increments and test stability with a couple of game sessions. Don’t chase a big jump without stability testing; otherwise you’ll spend more time rebooting than enjoying your games.
- Tweak memory subtly: RAM speed can give you a tangible bump in texture-heavy scenes. A +50 to +100 MHz memory offset can help with FEAT pixel-pushing, but binding memory too aggressively can cause instability in some titles.
- Adjust the power limit: a small bump in the PL is often enough to keep your overclock from throttling under load, which translates to more consistent frames when you push high textures with RT on.
- Optimize fan curves: aim for a curve that stays quiet at idle but ramps up predictably under load. You want to avoid the “okay, it’s loud now” scenario that ruins your ambient gaming vibe.
- Stress tests and stability: use trusted tools like 3DMark or a long gaming session with your usual titles to ensure there’s no artifacting or crashes. If you see artifacts or sudden frame drops, dial back a notch. The goal is comfortable stability, not heroic overclock bravado.

This is a card that rewards careful tuning, not reckless overclocking. If you’re new to overclocking, treat it like a hobby that takes patience, not a sprint—because a sprint often ends with a blue screen and a stressed-out hero’s journey.
