## UNO Flip Cards: quick primer for non fans

If you have not played UNO Flip Cards before, you are in for a ride. The core mechanic is a two-sided deck: the light side is the standard UNO experience; when someone plays a Flip card, the entire deck flips to the dark side, where new action cards come into play and the color rules can suddenly become chaotic. It is a little bit like training your brain to adapt to two different game states on the fly, which is exactly the kind of skill you want in a universe where hero training includes not just fight skills but also social deduction and resource management.

In the McDonalds Canada promo deck, expect a playful integration where the hero art does not interfere with core UNO playability. You still draw, you still play a color, you still shout UNO when you are down to one card. The flip mechanic remains the star of the show, giving players a moment of suspense as the game toggles from bright and bubbly to dark and strategic. For kids, this is a perfect introduction to game rules in a non-intimidating way. For adults, it is a handy excuse to justify grabbing a second round of fries while plotting a lighthearted sabotage on your sibling’s turn order.
