### What is Throw Throw Burrito?
Throw Throw Burrito is a real-life dodgeball-meets-card-game that uses soft foam burritos as projectiles. The premise is delightfully ridiculous: players collect matching pairs from a deck while simultaneously doing their best impression of a chaotic youth camp activity organizer. The goal is simple: grab points by collecting cards, dodge burritos, and avoid earning the infamous “fury of the burrito throw” penalties when you’re not paying attention. The game’s humor comes from its contrasts: premium-looking cards paired with kid-sized foam weapons; bright colors that scream “family game night” but the gameplay leans closer to an adrenaline-fueled dodgeball tournament for adults who forgot to grow up.

The core experience is short, punchy rounds with big energy. It’s the kind of game that shines in a living room turned into a impromptu battlefield, where everyone is shouting about a stolen pair and the burrito is still somehow used as a safe weapon. The design leans into the chaotic vibe: the cards are sturdy, the burritos are soft, and the instructions are hilariously breezy. The result is a party game that feels like a chaotic festival where every booth is giving out prizes that might explode in your face (figuratively, we hope).

## How to Play in 30 Seconds (Overview)
If you want the gist, here it is: you’re collecting pairs while throwing foam burritos at each other. Sounds simple, right? It’s not simple, but it’s wonderfully simple to explain, and that’s a big part of the charm.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a subset of cards to each player. The exact number varies with the player count, but you should expect chaos by about the second turn.
- Place the burritos in the middle, cluster them like a tiny comically dangerous bouquet of soft projectiles.
- Decide the scoring target and the burn-the-burrito penalties if you’re into dramatic risk management.

### Turns
On your turn, you may do the following, in any order that seems thematically appropriate:
- Attempt to collect a match by drawing from the deck and trying to pair cards.
- If you draw a burrito card, you have the option to throw one of the foam burritos at another player to steal a card, to dodge, or to risk a chaos moment that could flip the entire table of fortune.
- If you successfully avoid getting hit while you’re in a draw phase that involves a burrito, you may get a bonus point or a surprise card that changes how the rest of the round plays out.

### Endgame and scoring
The endgame is when someone hits the target score or empties their hand. Scoring is typically quick and dramatic: you tally the matches, add any bonuses from special cards, and optionally award extra points for style (like landing the most dramatic dodge or performing a ridiculous victory pose). The excitement comes from the near-constant threat of a burrito-flurry that can swing the score in a satisfying and silly way.

## Strategy and Tips for Winning (or at Least Avoiding a Cringe Moment)
- Communicate clearly but loudly. Part of the charm of Throw Throw Burrito is the energy you generate. The more you shout about your moves, the more the table buys into the chaos.
- Focus on matches early, but don’t neglect the dodge opportunities. If you can Time Your Throw with the right moment, you’ll maximize your chances of reducing your own risk while forcing opponents to react.
- Protect your backhand. If you’re throwing, keep your aim balanced and avoid flinging with your elbow like you’re auditioning for a sports movie. It’s more fun when everyone can still keep playing after round one.
- Use the deck’s special cards wisely. Some cards give you bonuses for a round or penalties for others. Paying attention to these can swing momentum when the table is hot.
- Don’t underestimate the social penalty of being the burrito-magnet. If you keep getting hit, consider pivoting to a defensive style and using the mismatch to your advantage by grabbing more favorable matches during your draws.
