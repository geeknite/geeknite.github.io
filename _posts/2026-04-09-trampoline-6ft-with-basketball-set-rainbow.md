## Safety and Maintenance
Backyard play means you should plan for supervision, maintenance, and the occasional parent flex when the kids start inventing new moves that look suspiciously like legendary video-game boss battles.

### Safety Tips
- Always supervise children during use.
- Check frame bolts and padding regularly for wear.
- Ensure the enclosure net zipper is fully closed during play.
- Keep a clear zone around the trampoline to prevent collisions with garden gnomes or stray lawn furniture.
- Use inside-out sneakers or bare feet if your floor surface encourages grip, but never wear shoes that will scratch mats or the protective padding.
- Avoid roughhousing that could cause a fall onto the hard edge;
- Follow weight limits and recommended ages in the instruction sheet.

### Cleaning and Longevity
Wipe down the mat and padding with a mild soap solution and a soft cloth every few weeks, especially after muddy adventures or summer popsicle incidents. Dry promptly to avoid any moisture-related rust or potential slippery patches on the mat. Inspect the springs for rust or fatigue and replace padding if it becomes compressed or cracked. A little routine care goes a long way toward extending the life of your rainbow playground and keeping the colors popping for seasons to come.
