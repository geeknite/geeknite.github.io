~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
The heavy-duty leg levelers are the real stars here. A good pair of levelers can turn a so-so table into a fortress of accuracy. Maintenance and Longevity
-------------------------
Once you’ve found the right spot in your game room, you’ll want the table to last. Here are practical maintenance tips:

- Keep the surface clean: Wipe with a soft microfiber cloth and a gentle, non-abrasive cleaner to avoid smudges and sticky fingers.
- Check the rods and bearings: Periodically inspect for wobble and lubricate as needed with a silicone-based lubricant. Avoid petroleum-based oils that can degrade the plastics.
- Tighten hardware: After a few heavy matches, check screws and end-caps. You don’t want a loose end-cap causing a wayward ball to take a detour onto your rug’s destiny.
- Leg levelers: If you notice wobble after months, re-check the leg levelers. They can settle a bit, especially on carpeted floors. A little twist brings back the championship posture.

Maintenance simplicity is a big part of why a full-size table remains appealing for family games and weekend tournaments. A well-maintained unit stays visually clean, plays consistently, and resists the creeping suspicion that you bought a prop from a sci-fi movie rather than a real game table.

Playing Tips and Tricks
-----------------------
- Start with core passing drills: short passes across the 5-1 formation to develop quick-twitch reflexes and improve your team’s ball control. 
- Defense counts: practice blocking shots with your defenders in the 3-4 lines. A practiced defense reduces the number of counter-attacks you face.
- Shooting drills: practice bank shots off the side rails to add variety to your game. It’s not cheating; it’s geometry with flair.
- Warm-up routines: don’t jump straight into a tournament when your hands are stiff from scrolling phones. Do a short warm-up; your wrists will thank you and your top-spin shots will be more accurate.
