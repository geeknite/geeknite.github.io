## What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition?

### A bite-sized refresher for the uninitiated
Exploding Kittens is a party card game designed by Elan Lee, Shane Small, and Matthew Inman that aims to mix chaos, strategy, and very quick decision-making into a chaotic circus of a playmat. The original edition (also affectionately referred to as the OG) came with a deck of quirky cards, a handful of instruction pages, and a single mission: avoid drawing an exploding kitten card unless you’re extremely confident you’ve got a Defuse card tucked away like a secret weapon.

### What you actually get in the box
- A deck of custom art cards featuring a range of hilarious and slightly questionable illustrations.
- A handful of Defuse cards that protect you from digital what-if scenarios and, in this game, from exploding panic.
- Exploding Kitten cards that spell doom for the unprepared—I mean, unless you’ve got a Defuse handy.
- A compact rule sheet that somehow feels both simple and fiendishly clever in the same breath.
- A little stack of other token cards that determine actions like skip, attack, or shuffle (the rules are simple, but the chaos is not).

If you’re into the collectible angle, this edition is a certified classic. It’s not a glossy, multi-pocket, fully illustrated deluxe set, but it isn’t shy about delivering a punchline you’ll remember long after the last card is discarded. The original art leans into the internet’s love of cheeky cats and absurd scenarios, which makes the set a time capsule of early-2010s meme culture with a friendly, kid-unfriendly wink.

## How to play: quick refresher for the curious

### Core mechanics distilled
- The aim: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten, unless you can Defuse it. Defuse cards are your lifelines, but you only have a finite supply.
- Turn order and actions: you draw a card after performing any playing action. You can play action cards to influence the deck or your opponents, then draw. It’s a push-your-luck mechanic with a humorous twist that makes even losing feel like a punchline.
- Key cards to know: Defuse, Exploding Kitten, Skip, Attack, Shuffle, See the Future, and various other category cards that generate chaos without requiring a heavy rulebook.

### Strategy tips for a sealed OG edition
- Keep Defuses close: they’re not just protection; they’re morale boosters. If you can save a Defuse until the late game, you’ll feel like a tactical mastermind with a flair for dramatic reveals.
- Timing is everything: don’t blow your Defuse early unless you’re absolutely certain you’ve got the upper hand. It’s a currency of risk and reward.
- Read the room: in younger groups, the humor may cut through the suspense; in a more serious crowd, the chaos can be a welcome relief from heavier strategy games.

### Practicalities of keeping it pristine
- Storage: keep the box in a cool, dry place away from sunlight to prevent fading or warping of the box. A dedicated shelf or a high-quality storage solution can keep things tidy.
- Handling: hands should be clean; oils and grime can transfer to the box or cards over time. Gloves aren’t strictly necessary, but if you’re chasing perfection, they’re not wrong.
- Documentation: keep any receipts or proofs of purchase in case you want to authenticate and resell later. It’s not a trophy case, but it’s a paperwork wins game.
