## What is UNO Attack and how does it differ from classic UNO?
UNO Attack is still UNO at its core. You have the same goal: be the first player to shed your cards, either by matching color or number or by laying down wild variants when the moment is right. The big difference lies in the mechanism for the draw. Instead of simply drawing one card when you’re forced to, Attack scales up the drama by introducing a card launcher device that can churn out a random number of cards. The thriller is that you never know how many you will draw until the launcher finishes its job, adding a roulette-wheel vibe to every draw two or skip card.

In practice, UNO Attack can turn a casual game night into a caffeine-fueled sprint where strategy and luck collide. The original 2005 version also includes a deck of Attack-specific cards alongside the classic UNO roster. These cards can alter how many cards are dumped from the launcher or how the turn order shifts. The result is a brisk, highly social game that rewards quick thinking and teamwork — or at least quick dodges when it becomes your turn and the launcher coughs up a handful of surprise cards.
