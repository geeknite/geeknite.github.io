## Screens and Privacy Features
The W Screens in the name aren’t just for drama; they’re the practical reason this thing exists. Privacy screens serve multiple purposes: reduce visual distraction, provide a per-person canvas for private notes, and give you a degree of acoustic isolation from your neighbors’ emails and ringtones. The typical setup uses frosted or lightly tinted panels that offer enough opacity to shield your monitor from a curious coworker while still allowing you to see the person next to you when you want to say something witty like, “Yes, I already archived that file.”

From a micro-ergonomics perspective, screens on a four-person station help, because each user has their own “attention zone.” This means you won’t have to lean towards someone else to point out a line item or hover with a mouse over the shared contract window. The panels also create a sense of personal space that can be surprisingly comforting in a room where the coffee smells like a competition.

In terms of reflection and glare, white surfaces can bounce a surprising amount of light. If your room is punchy with natural light, you may want to consider adjustable lighting or even anti-glare screen protectors. If you’re into streaming or recording, a subtle matte finish on screens and the desk surface can reduce the “my desk is reflecting my face at every angle” issue.

## Maintenance and Cleaning Tips
White surfaces scream for regular cleaning. The last thing you want is a desk that looks like it’s a crime scene after a gaming marathon. Here are practical tips:
- Wipe down daily with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner suitable for melamine or laminate.
- Use a soft brush attachment for cable channels to dislodge dust without scratching.
- Keep a small can of compressed air for monitor mounting points and vent openings.
- Establish a weekly cleaning ritual that includes wiping screens and checking for loose screws.

If you host occasional meetings in this space, you’ll appreciate how easy it is to wipe away coffee splatters and marker ink from the screens and desk surfaces. Just don’t attempt greasy fingerprints with solvent-based cleaners—they’re not friends of quality finishes.
