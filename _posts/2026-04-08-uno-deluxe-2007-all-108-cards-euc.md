## What exactly is in the box? The 108-card reality
The hallmark of the 2007 Uno Deluxe edition is its completeness. A standard UNO deck is 108 cards, and this Deluxe variant kept that ambition intact. In practical terms, that means you’re dealing with four suits (red, blue, green, yellow) packed with 0–9 numbers, action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two), and the wild cards (Wild and Wild Draw Four). If you’re counting, that’s a lot of copies of the same numbers, a few surprising quirks of color balance, and just enough wilds to keep the chaos humane rather than chaotic-chaotic.

What does “108 cards” actually mean in day-to-day play?
- You’re not likely to run out of Draw Twos in a casual game night unless you’re playing with a small brigade of bantering teenagers who somehow manage to draw the entire pile in one hand. The Wild Draw Four remains the ultimate showstopper. ## Maintenance and storage tips for keeping EUC cards pristine
- Keep the deck in its original box when not in use. A little dust in the box is far better than a climate-controlled dungeon for your cards.
- Avoid direct sunlight. This is not a vinyl record; color fade on cards in sunlight looks more like a sad painting than a game.
- Use card sleeves only if you’re a serious collector who wants to protect resale value. For regular play, sleeves can feel odd with a Deluxe deck, but they do reduce edge wear over time.
- If you need to clean sticky residue, use a very light wipe with a microfiber cloth. Don’t soak the deck; water and gluey sweetness do not mix well with glossy stock.
