## Rules: Quick Rules Recap (Because You’re Curious If You Need a Lawyer)
UNO rules are famously straightforward, but Disney-tinted editions often introduce a handful of flavor twists. Here’s a concise refresher to reduce the time you spend arguing about whether a “Mouse Wild” counts as a color or a character.

- Objective: Be the first to get rid of all your cards.
- Setup: Shuffle, deal 7 cards to each player, place the rest in a draw pile, flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Card types: Number cards in four colors, Action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two) in colors, Wild, and Wild Draw Four.
- Play: Match either the color or the number of the top card on the discard pile, or play an action card when possible.
- Special Disney twists: The deck features Mickey-themed wilds and character-flavor cards that don’t alter the core mechanics but add thematic twists that make the experience feel younger and more wholesome.
- Draw rules: If you can’t play, you draw one card. If you draw a playable card, you may play it immediately in the same turn (depending on house rules).
- UNO shout: As usual, you must shout “UNO” when you have only one card left; otherwise, you face a penalty in the form of a fresh card from the draw pile—legitimate high-stakes theater for family audiences.

Variants and house rules are where the real spice lives. We’ll get to that in a later section, but the essence: this version sticks to the foundational UNO rhythm with gentle Disney seasoning rather than a dramatic rewrite.
