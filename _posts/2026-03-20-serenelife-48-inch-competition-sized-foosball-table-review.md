## Assembly Tips and Practical Setup Advice
- Read the manual before you begin; it will save you from assembling a part you don’t actually need yet. The diagrams are there for a reason, and the step numbering is not a villain—it's a helpful guide, not a scavenger hunt.
- Have a dedicated friend or two to hold pieces in place while you align screws. Foosball tables are not amateur-level furniture when it comes to alignment; you want straight lines and stable legs, not a wonky battlefield that shakes every time someone scores.
- Use a level to check the tabletop. The balance is more important than you think; a tiny tilt can make your best shot look spectacularly sloppy and ruin your post-game bragging rights.
- Tighten all screws gradually and in a cross pattern to ensure even pressure. The last thing you want is a panel that bows under the weight of your ambitions.
- Attach the cup holders early if space allows. You’ll appreciate them once a late-night victory drink becomes a reality and your friend exclaims that the table deserves a standing ovation.

## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
The best way to extend the life of any foosball table is to treat it like a tiny arena where you occasionally allow yourself to forget you are not a pro. Here are practical tips:
- Wipe the playing surface after use to prevent dust buildup that can affect ball spin. A soft microfiber cloth is your friend here.
- Keep the rods clean and lightly oiled. Use a silicone-based lubricant designed for metal, and avoid petroleum-based products that can degrade plastics over time.
- Check screws and end caps monthly and re-tighten if you notice any looseness. Nothing kills the vibe of a dramatic goal like a wobbly table.
- Protect the surface when you’re not playing. A lightweight cover can keep dust away and preserve the finish between sessions.
- If you’re a heavy user, consider rotating ball positions to reduce wear on a single set of players. It’s not cheating, it’s strategy—and maybe a little science.
