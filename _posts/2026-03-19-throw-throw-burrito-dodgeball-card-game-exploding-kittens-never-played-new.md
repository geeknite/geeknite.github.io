## How to Play: The Core Mechanics Explained
If you have previously attempted to teach a friend to play a complex strategy game with a two-hour timer and a five-page FAQ, Throw Throw Burrito will feel like a spa day in comparison. It is designed to be played with a group, and the rules are simple enough to be explained in one breath between burrito tosses.

### Setup
- Shuffle the Treasure Puffy and Challenge decks separately
- Place the burritos within arm's reach of each player or at least within the radius of your couch fort
- Deal a small number of Treasure cards to each player usually three These are your points your beacons of glory and your potential to turn your friends into cornered throw targets
- Decide who goes first by a dramatic burrito toss or by the age-old method: the host makes everyone stand up and pretend to be a burrito roll

### The Turn Structure
On your turn you perform two steps
1) Draw two cards from either the Treasure deck or the Puffy deck
2) If you draw a Puffy card you must perform the action indicated usually something like swap treasures with another player or discard a card If you draw a Treasure card you add it to your stash which increases your score but there is a catch you must have added enough Puffy cards to balance your loot otherwise the burritos will come for you in the next round

The essence is simple draw collect and survive The real joy comes from playing the Puffy cards strategically to mess with your friends while dodging incoming burrito artillery

### Burrito Toss and Dodge Mechanics
This is where the chaos meter shoots into the red When a Treasure card is at stake players may throw burritos at each other to steal treasures The burritos function as literal dodgeballs you swing aim and brace for the inevitable squeak of fabric hitting fabric Hitting another player scores a point for the attacker and reduces the target's Treasure cards by one It is not about accuracy it is about timing chaos and the ability to keep your balance on a furniture mount

It is not unusual for a game to devolve into a frenzy of fumbles and giggles as someone attempts a mid-table toss and ends up hitting a lamp a plant or the cat with a burrito that has the physical presence of a small pillow The modular nature of the game means the play area quickly becomes a dynamic battlefield with players repositioning chairs cushions and heroic limbs to avoid a burrito storm that could erupt at any moment

### Endgame and Winning the Round
The round ends when a player has collected enough Treasure cards to meet a target score The Puffy cards continue to circulate keeping the pressure on and ensuring that no one leaves the table with a clean ego The game typically rewards bold plays and pun-based banter with the occasional insane toss that somehow finds its mark The endgame is where the real comedy happens youll see players diving for cover behind a couch performing a dramatic burrito dodge that would make a seasoned dodgeball player jealous and a chorus of laughter that drowns out the neighbors

## Techniques for Beginners: How to Survive and Thrive
If your last party game experience was a chaotic disaster of rulebooks here is a friendly starter kit of tips to help you navigate Throw Throw Burrito with grace or at least with minimal furniture damage
- Communicate clearly but not aggressively Let your teammates know when you intend to throw and where you aim A calm measured approach reduces the risk of collateral chaos
- Target the toy not the person The punishments feel harsher when you accidentally bean your mom your cat or your grandmother’s favorite plant
- Time the throws with the music If you have a background playlist use the tempo to your advantage a catchy chorus can help coordinate a team-friendly dodge
- Use the Puffy cards strategically Sometimes it is better to trigger a defensive action before you go for a treasure than to wait and let chaos overwhelm you Remember you want the points but you also want to leave the table with your dignity intact
- Keep the burritos clean A damp burrito is a dramatic game changer it becomes harder to throw and increases the likelihood of slipping A dry burrito is the blessing you want
