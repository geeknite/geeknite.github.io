## Maintenance, durability, and care

Wood surfaces require a light touch when cleaning. A damp microfiber cloth works well for removing fingerprints and minor smudges. Avoid harsh cleaners that could strip the wood finish or dull the color. Periodically check the screw joints on the legs and the rods; loose joints will not ruin a session but can dampen the vibe and cause wobble that makes you swear the ball grew legs and ran away. The included tool kit is useful for quick tuneups, but you will likely only reach for it every few months unless your kids treat the table like a pet project workshop.

Durability wise, the wood cabinet and hardware hold up well with typical family use. The plastic player figures are sturdy enough for enthusiastic play but not designed for heavy rough play. If you anticipate robust, tournament-level abuse, you may want to explore additional upgrades such as stronger ball bearings or alternative player figures, but for most homes, the stock setup is more than adequate. The finish resists minor scratches, which is essential for a piece of furniture that will see finger smudges and marker doodles from kids who love to draw on everything except the wall. A simple wipe-down after intense sessions keeps the table looking fresh and ready for the next round.
