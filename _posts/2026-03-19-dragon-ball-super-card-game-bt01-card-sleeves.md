## Fit and feel: do these sleeves fit your deck?

### Standard size considerations
Dragon Ball Super Card Game uses standard playing-card size. The BT01 sleeves are designed to be compatible with standard size cards, including the DBSCG card dimension range. The fit should be snug but not so tight that you’re tugging on the cards during a mid-game draw. If you plan to sleeve all 60 cards with double sleeves (two layers), you should consider slightly larger sleeves; otherwise, you’ll get card-glue issues when you try to insert. Our recommendation: pair BT01 sleeves with a fitting deck box; you don’t want the cards to slide out when you tilt the box to show a friend your UR Super Saiyan quick draw.

### Thickness and feel
The middle ground of 60–80 microns is a good standard for many DBS players; it balances protection with shuffle ease. A deck that feels like a heavy stone tablet in your hands is not fun to shuffle; you will feel your ability to perform complex combos slipping away as you fight with the deck. The BT01 sleeves we tested felt light enough to not hamper performance but sturdy enough to resist the occasional accidental snag.

### In-hand experience
In the cockpit of a tabletop duel, your fingers appreciate a sleeve that does not catch on the edges or rub the adjacent card too aggressively. The BT01 sleeves we tried offered a good glide between cards; you could quickly pull a card, slide it into the next, and keep the table from erupting into a dramatic Dragon Ball Z moment. The texture was smooth with a subtle grip that helps keep cards from sliding all over the place when you’re grabbing a handful during a heated sequence.

## Durability and long-term use: what happens after months of play?

### Long-term wear
We tested the BT01 sleeves over a 6- to 12-month window while active in weekly board game nights and occasional tournament sessions. The result? They held their color well, with little yellowing or surface wear on the top layer. The area that receives the most abuse—near the deck’s edge—showed minimal abrasion.

### Cleaning and maintenance
Decks can collect dust and fingerprints; cleaning sleeves is straightforward. A soft microfiber cloth can be used to wipe the outer surface. Avoid aggressive cleaners that could degrade the material. For deeper cleaning, you can remove the cards and rinse the sleeves with mild soap and warm water, though be sure to dry them thoroughly before re-inserting your deck.

### Re-sleeving and compatibility
If a new BT01 edition comes out and you want to upgrade, you’ll want to re-sleeve the cards, which is always a little dream-splitting for the collector who wants uniformity. In some cases, switching to a higher thickness sleeve, such as 90–100 microns, may be necessary if you are combining double-sleeving with a tight deck box. If you are a streamer or a content creator, keep spare sleeves around for swapping out during recording sessions.
