## How to display and use on the table
- Display the art cards prominently at the edge of your play area so everyone gets the vibe instantly.
- Use the new survivor as your go-to hero when you want a dramatic entrance and a heroic pose that matches the art.
- Place the monstrous sculpts in the first or second waves to emphasize Smiths signature menace and scale differences.
- Mix the glossy art prints into a binder or a wall display so your hobby room feels like a small museum of tabletop storytelling.

## Quick painting tips for the Adrian Smith set
- Start with a strong base: deep midnight blue or black for shadows, a chill base for metallics, and a bone or off-white for highlights.
- Build contrast with a mid-tone glaze that pulls the blues into the armor and flesh tones toward the monstrous aspects of the zombies.
- Use a thin glaze of teal or ultraviolet on the edges to mimic the glow effect often present in Smiths pieces.
- Dry brush the cape or cloth areas to pick up the light from your desk lamp; the dramatic surfaces will sing with a little dry brushing magic.
- Seal with a matte finish to avoid glare during gaming; you want the mini to hold up to table lights, not reflect them like a mirror ball.

## Gallery-ready display and maintenance
If you want to photograph the set for social media, set up a simple lightbox with a neutral background and a single strong key light from the top-right. That crisp diagonal highlight is the hallmark of sci-fi visuals and helps the sculpt details pop. A lot of the appeal of this set is in the art fidelity, so a clean background with minimal clutter helps the models speak for themselves. When you’re done, carefully remove the assembly grease and dust, and you’ll preserve the crisp lines and the dramatic silhouette for years of coffee-stained hobby sessions.
