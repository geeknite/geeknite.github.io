## A Quick refresher: What are Saboteur and Saboteur 2 Anyway?

If you somehow missed the saga, here’s the capsule version: Saboteur is a card game where players take on the roles of dwarves tasked with finding gold by laying down path cards to connect a starting point to a precious treasure. The twist? Some dwarves are secretly saboteurs who want to derail the path and cause everyone else to despair at their unfortunate routing choices. Saboteur 2 introduces new twists, more action cards, and additional layers of misdirection while keeping the core vibe intact.

- Core mechanics: Each round, players draft and lay down path cards, placing their miners’ roles on the table. The true identity of each player remains hidden for the majority of the game, which fuels tension and dramatic reveals during scoring.
- Win condition: If your team (minors vs saboteurs) can successfully build a path to the treasure, the miners win; if saboteurs derail the route, sabotage success is celebrated, and the gold remains stubbornly uncollected.
- Pace: A typical session breathes in and out within 20-30 minutes, which makes it a perfect “one more round” title for game nights without becoming a marathon commitment.

In Saboteur 2, you’ll notice a handful of tweaks and fresh card types that keep things interesting even for folks who’ve played the original a dozen times. We won’t spoil every card, but expect new trick cards, more role-pool variety, and some extra ways for saboteurs to play dirty without obvious telegraphs.

## The VG Condition Verdict: What to Expect

VG stands for Very Good, a grading common in the world of used games. Here’s what that means for this bundle:

- Cards: Likely no major creases, clean fronts, and edges with minor wear. You might see light scuffing or very small whitening on occasional corners. Nothing that impedes shuffle or readability.
- Boxes: Expect some edge wear, small scuffs, and possible minor storage creases. The boxes should still open and close without the risk of a sudden avalanche of cards escaping.
- Components: Any tokens, markers, and rule sheets should be intact, with minor signs of use. If you’re a perfectionist, you may want to sleeve the cards for long-term protection, which is a cheap add-on that pays dividends in shelf-life.

If you’re buying this lot for casual play with friends or family, VG is usually more than enough. If you’re a collector who needs “near-mint” for display, you might want to negotiate a small discount or request additional photos prior to purchase.

## How Saboteur and Saboteur 2 Play (A Friendly Rundown)

### Setup and Roles (Saboteur basics)

- Players: The exact number depends on the edition, but you’ll generally be in a range where a dozen dwarves are too many and a handful of players feels too lonely. The more you have, the more chaotic the suspicion becomes.
- Roles: Each player receives a secret role card: Miner (good) or Saboteur (bad). The miners are trying to reach the treasure by building a valid path; saboteurs aim to derail and misdirect. The secret-keeping element is what makes Saboteur sing.
- Objective: Build a correct path from the start to the treasure card. If the path is blocked or the saboteur successfully misroutes, the saboteurs win the round.

### Path-building vs. Sabotage (core tension)
The game is a built-in tug-of-war of trust. Players lay down path cards to extend a tunnel toward the treasure. Each card can be a straight section, a corner, or a junction, and some cards have special symbols that let you peek at the layout or force other players to move cards around. Saboteurs can insert mismatched cards, flip the order of play, or force a discarding spree that disrupts the miners’ carefully calculated routes.

### Endings and Scoring (two-lane chaos)
When a tunnel reaches the treasure card, the round ends and the mining crew scores based on who contributed to the successful path and who caused the derailments. Hidden roles are revealed at the end of the round, and players who guessed correctly about others’ loyalties gain extra points. The tone tends to swing between “we’re in this together” and “who sabotaged whom this time?”—in other words, a perfect microcosm of office politics but with beards and lanterns.

### Saboteur 2: What’s New Without Spoiling the Fun
Saboteur 2 expands the sandbox with additional card types and alternate victory conditions. Expect more bluffing, new sabotage tools, and some clever balancing tweaks that make it feel familiar yet fresh. The packaging for Saboteur 2 remains approachable, and the learning curve is gentle enough that you can pick up the rules in a single, enthusiastic round with friends who have never touched the series before.
