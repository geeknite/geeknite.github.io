## How to run a smooth game night with this edition
Large groups require a little extra planning, or else the chaos will live up to its name and swallow the sofa cushions. Here are practical tips to ensure everyone has fun and no one is left staring at an empty hand while the rest of the table negotiates color changes like diplomats at a peace summit:

- Start with a quick 2-3 minute rules overview for newcomers. The All Wild cards should be explained in the context of their impact on the game’s pace rather than the entire library of possible effects.
- Use a single deck if possible. In most setups, the All Wild deck is integrated with the standard UNO cards, but some retailers provide two decks that combine into a bigger pool of cards. Decide early how you want to manage the draw pile and ensure there is enough space for all players to see the top card at a glance.
- Assign a moderator. A calm voice who can remind players when a wild is placed down and help resolve disputes about the interpretation of a card can prevent chaos from turning into a meltdown.
- Consider rotating who starts each round. The player who starts may influence the round’s pace significantly, and rotating starts helps distribute this advantage and keeps things fair.
- Establish a soft rule to manage shouting. UNO nights can be loud. Encourage friendly banter but set a limit to avoid hurting relationships around the table. A little noise is fine; a shouting match, not so much.

In a big room with a lot of noise, you may also consider using a timer to keep things moving. A minute per turn is more than enough to preserve tempo while still giving players time to think. If you want to add a fun twist, award a tiny trophy or a silly title to the winner of each round, turning the game into a little ritual that people remember long after the deck is back in the box.
