## Components and setup
### The prompt deck
This is the heart of the game. Prompts set up scenarios ranging from workplace satire to dating misadventures to family dinners that spiral into meme territory. Every prompt is crafted to invite a humorous or savage response, and some of them can be surprisingly specific, which is where the magic happens.

### The answer deck
These are the lines you will lay down in response to prompts. They are the comedic punch, roasts, and sometimes surprisingly elegant wordplay that anchors a good moment. The more you know your friends, the more you will feel the power of the perfect punchline.

### The roast-master role
One player becomes the roast master for the round. This person reads the prompt and decides which answer card best fits the moment. The roast master rotates, so the chaos stays fair and lively. A good roast master keeps the room engaged, moderate the energy, and maybe keeps a straight face long enough for the killer punchline to land.

### The setup ritual
Shuffle decks, deal seven cards to each player, decide the roast master, and prepare the space for quick rounds. If you're playing in a larger group, you can add a few house rules to keep energy high and avoid a lull.

## How to play: rounds, scoring, and roast dynamics
### Round flow
- The roast master draws a prompt and reads it aloud.
- Players select one or more answer cards from their hand to respond.
- The roast master reads the submitted responses aloud and selects a winner for the round.
- The winner gains a point; play passes to the next roast master.

### Scoring
Most groups use one point per round, but you can customize: a two-point round for the best clever wordplay, or a loser's mantle for the night to add drama. The key is to set a target score before starting.

### Quick tips for beginners
- Read the room: if the group is into warm roasts, lean into sharper lines; if people prefer light humor, temper the bite.
- Save big cards for later rounds. The payoff often comes when a prompt asks for a bold punchline or a misread that lands perfectly.

### Example round
Prompt: When your coworker says we value your contributions, you know they mean:
Player A: a line about Slack emoji usage
Player B: a savage roast about long meetings
Player C: a pun on corporate jargon
Roast Master selects the winner, and the room erupts.
