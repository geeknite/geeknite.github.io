## Assembly, installation, and setup

### Unboxing and base prep
Unboxing is straightforward. You’ll see the main frame, the rails, doors, side panels, and the mounting hardware. The most fun part is aligning the rails with the correct spacing and ensuring the rack sits flush against the wall or on a clean, level floor. The 600x600 base helps with stability, but if your floor is a little uneven, you’ll want to deploy the included leveling feet. Having level feet is like giving the rack a tiny pair of spacers that say, I am not wobbly and I demand polite handling.

### Rail installation and alignment
Rail alignment is a two-person job for best results, especially if you’re mounting 42U of heavy gear. The rails slide into place with a satisfying click, and you can secure them with the included cage nuts. Do your best to align the front and back rails so that your 19 in devices don’t look like a modern art sculpture once installed. If you’re working with mixed equipment heights, plan ahead for clearance in the back and a little extra elbow room for the PDU and cable management accessories.

### Doors, panels, and finishing touches
Attach the front and rear doors, fix the side panels if included, and do a quick check to ensure the doors close without rubbing any gear. A quick once-over for grounding connections is worth it; you want to avoid a situation where a chassis is grounded to metal and the entire rack forms a tiny Faraday cage for the entire closet. While testing, you can power up a light-weight switch in the middle of the rack and watch the fan drones hum with regulatory compliance vibes. If all is well, your rack is ready for a full load test.

### Cable management during install
Now comes the part where you pretend you are a magician and the cables are your rabbits. Route power and data separately where possible to reduce crosstalk and EMI. Use the rear channels for data and the top for power when feasible, avoiding sharp bends and kinks in critical lines. The AVIT 42 RU supports some decent cable management options; the real trick is discipline and planning before you start fastening zip ties like a hamster on a caffeine spree. A clean install makes future upgrades so much easier and reduces the post-install screams during a maintenance window.

### First boot and post-install checks
Power on the equipment and verify that each device reports proper network status and that the fans run smoothly without rattling. Listen for irregularities—if you hear a metallic ping, check for loose screws, misaligned rails, or a particularly judgmental tape measure left in the rack by the last technician. A quiet, consistent hum is a sign that your cooling and airflow are doing their jobs, while a cascade of beeps usually means someone turned a screw too far and rebranded a cable tie as an emergency anchor.
