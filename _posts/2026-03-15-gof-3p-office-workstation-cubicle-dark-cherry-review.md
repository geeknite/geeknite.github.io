The GOF 3P combines two pieces of furniture into a cohesive workstation: a long desk surface that serves as the command center for monitors, keyboards, and epic snack breaks, and a cubicle component that creates a bit of privacy without locking you into a cave. ## Dimensions and ergonomics

Here are the numbers that matter for real-world layout: the GOF 3P is described as 5'D x 18'W x 4', 5', 6' H. That reads like a cryptic riddle, but it translates in practice to a desk depth of about 5 feet, a desk width of 18 inches (a reasonable surface area for a concise workstation), and height options of 4 feet, 5 feet, or 6 feet to accommodate different cubicle configurations and user heights. The mixed height options are great for standing desk enthusiasts or for setups where the cubicle needs to scale to the surrounding furniture. If you want to go fully ergonomic, a 4' to 6' height range gives you flexibility to adjust standing and seated positions across the day, which can help with posture and focus on those long video calls.

The 18 inch width is the tricky part for some power users who want multiple large monitors side-by-side. It is not the smallest desk in the world, but it is not a sprawling aristocrat with leg room for your draft of a novel either. You’ll likely pair this with one or two external monitors depending on your use case, plus a keyboard tray or an external keyboard on the desk surface. If you’re juggling more than a couple of screens and a printer, you may need to rethink the layout or opt for the deeper variant if available. For most typical office tasks, the arrangement works well and doesn’t visually overwhelm a compact cubicle—an important factor when you’re trying to maintain an aura of productivity rather than “I must vamp for the next meeting.”

The dark cherry finish pairs well with modern office palettes: grays, charcoals, and neutrals; it can also pop against lighter colors if you want to highlight the wood vibe without feeling like you are adding a rustic cabin to a glass-walled skyscraper. It’s a safe choice for open offices that want a touch of warmth without making the space feel too heavy or too fussy.

Like most modular furniture, the assembly requires time and the right tool kit. The GOF 3P ships with the usual set of Allen wrenches and a handful of screws that seem straightforward until you realize you are trying to assemble a floating cable channel while your coffee grows cold. It’s not exactly a midnight puzzle, but you’ll want to pace yourself. A decent partner or a good streaming snack can speed up the process and make the long screws less menacing. In our experience, expect a couple of hours to assemble the core desk and cubicle module, plus time for cable organization once you realize that you want the dream setup without the mess of a mid-move jam session.

If you’ve assembled basic desks before, the process will feel familiar. If you’re new to this, take your time—read the instructions, lay out the hardware, and test-fit pieces before you tighten screws. The result is solid, and once in place, the unit feels stable and resistant to wobble during vigorous typing or the occasional door-slam from the break room in the next cubicle. The modular design also means you can reconfigure in the future, which is a nice feature if your office space evolves or your team grows from one person with a laptop to a team of caffeinated wizards with more monitors than you thought possible.
