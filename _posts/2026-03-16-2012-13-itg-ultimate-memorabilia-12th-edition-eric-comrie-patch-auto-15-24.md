### Grading and authenticity considerations
If you ever plan to grade a patch auto, you should consider the two main variables: the autograph condition and the patch integrity. For patch autographs, professionals will often check for authentic autograph placement, ink consistency, and patch-to-card alignment. Patch quality is also evaluated for its integration into the card. Some cards will be designated as authentic and ungraded, while others might gain a grade based on the patch condition and autographic clarity. In this era, ITG products typically did not rely on a modern grading template with universal grade labels, but third-party grading services still apply to these cards for many collectors. If you plan to pursue grading, ask for a professional evaluation of both the autograph and the patch. Make sure to request high-resolution photos from the seller and consider a reputable grading service that frequently handles patch autos.

## Practical care and preservation
Preservation is not glamorous, but it is essential. The first rule is to avoid direct sunlight, which can fade auto ink and patch colors over time. Temperature and humidity matter too; aim for a stable room environment rather than a chaotic hobby den that doubles as a workshop for future cosplay props. Here are a few quick care tips:
- Store in a cool, dry place with consistent humidity. Extreme conditions can compromise the patch fabric and autograph.
- Handle minimally. When you do handle, use clean hands or nitrile gloves to reduce oils and fingerprints on the autograph surface.
- Use archival-quality storage materials that are acid-free and designed for trading cards. Avoid flimsy plastic sleeves that twist and bend over time.
- Consider occasional inspection. Even well-protected cards can shift or settle in their sleeves, so a periodic check helps catching minor issues early.

## Buying tips and red flags
If you are hunting for this specific card, here are practical checks to avoid pitfalls:
- Verify edition and numbering. 15/24 should be printed on the back or front depending on the printing process. Confirm this number is visible when you receive the card.
- Inspect the autograph legibility. A signature that reads clearly is a strong sign of well-preserved condition. If the autograph looks smeared or washed out, it may indicate handling or grading issues.
- Check the patch alignment. The patch should align with the card edges and not appear detached or shifting. A patch that seems misaligned is a red flag for possible damage or poor manufacturing.
- Seek clear seller photos. High-resolution images from multiple angles help you evaluate surface wear, corner integrity, and potential creases.
- Ask about provenance. A good seller will provide a brief history of the card, including any previous grading or encapsulation, to help you assess authenticity and condition.
