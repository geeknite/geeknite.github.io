## How to play: Setup and basic rules
Phase 10 is a phase-based card game where players aim to complete ten predefined phases before anyone else. The table stakes are simple: draw a card, discard a card, and assemble the required combination on your hand to advance to the next phase. The exact phases vary a little from edition to edition, but expect a mix of runs (consecutive numbers) and sets (cards of the same value) that get progressively trickier as you push through the ten phases.

Setup is straightforward. Each player draws a starting hand from the deck, a process that takes roughly a minute. The remaining deck sits in the center, with the top card flipped up to begin the discard pile. On your turn you draw one card, then decide to keep it or discard it, aiming to assemble the phase you are currently working on. When you believe you have the right combination to complete your phase, you declare that you have completed the phase and move on to the next one. The moment you finish the tenth phase and empty your hand, you win that round. The rest tally up points for their remaining cards, and you roll into another round if the family is up for it.

The core rules are not complicated. What makes Phase 10 sing is the interplay between personal strategy and the social dynamics around the table. It is a game that rewards memory and planning, but you will also use a little luck to determine whether the card you need will show up or whether someone else’s discard helps you out in a pinch. The joy comes from watching players pivot on a dime as phase requirements shift and cards switch hands in wild ways. The kids will love the dramatic turns, and the adults will appreciate the moments of shared relief when a difficult phase finally lands.

## Phases, strategy, and the social engine
The ten phases are designed to ramp up in complexity. Phase one might be a simple pair or run, while later phases demand multiple elements aligned in a precise way. The addition of wild cards offers flexibility when a hand is not cooperating, which keeps the experience accessible. Strategy emerges from several angles:

- Card management: Which cards should you hold back for a potential future phase, and which should you discard to minimize your opponents’ chances?
- Timing: When is the right moment to attempt a phase, and when should you wait a turn to improve your hand?
- Reading the table: Are your opponents signaling that they are close to a phase? Can you disrupt their momentum by drawing or discarding strategically?
- Luck management: How much do you rely on luck to fill gaps when your own cards are stubborn? The wild card can swing a game in dramatic fashion, which is part of Phase 10’s charm.

In a two-player format, the pace is brisk and the decision tree is always present. In larger groups, the social energy becomes the real show. You get more banter, more dramatic near-misses, and more opportunities for mischief as players try to force favorable discards or patiently wait for the phase they need. The gameplay shines when people lean into the silliness of the moment and let the rules drive the fun rather than the other way around.
