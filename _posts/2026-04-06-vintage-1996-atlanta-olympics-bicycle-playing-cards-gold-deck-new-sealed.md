## Authenticity and preservation: how to verify you’re getting the real deal
There’s no substitute for a legit, sealed deck when you’re building a collection. Here are practical checks and tips to help you avoid fakes or damaged goods:

- Seal integrity: Examine the outer plastic and cellophane. Look for uniform sealing with no tears or resealing marks. A compromised seal is a red flag for potential tampering or re-packaging.
- Box weight and dimension: Compare the weight to a typical Bicycle deck. The gold edition box should feel slightly heftier than a standard deck but not so heavy as to be suspicious. If it feels unusually light or bulky, suspect a swap or counterfeit.
- Print quality and foil: Inspect the foil on the lettering and emblem. Genuine editions will show crisp foil work with minimal bleed. Any misalignment or fuzzy embossing suggests lower quality production.
- Jokers and interior prints: The jokers and interior card backs should align perfectly, with consistent margins. A misprint or misalignment can indicate a misprinted batch or a counterfeit.
- Tariff and licensing marks: Some Olympic-branded cards carry licensing marks; if those are missing or mismatched, proceed with caution.

If you’re new to the world of vintage cards, you might consider consulting a trusted dealer or joining a collector’s forum to learn the year-specific quirks and production runs. The common-sense approach is to weigh provenance and packaging quality more than one-off savings. It’s a small investment for a piece that will likely outlive you in a display case.
