## How to install: step-by-step (no NASA training required)
1. Place the panel on your desk where you want the primary privacy zone. Ensure there’s a little room for the clamps to grip without hitting the keyboard tray or a monitor arm.
2. Position the two C-clamps at opposite ends of the panel. The goal is to have even pressure spread across the desk edge for stable support.
3. Tighten the clamps gradually. Don’t overtighten; you’re not mounting a satellite dish. The silicone pads should make even contact with the desk surface without slipping. If you hear squeaks, you’ve over-tightened; back off a notch.
4. Check for wobble. If there’s flex, adjust clamp placement or tighten slightly more. If the panel still wobbles, consider mounting one clamp closer to the center to reduce tilt.
5. Peel off the protective film if present, and admire your new mini sanctuary.

Optional: For deeper privacy, consider pairing with a soft desk light aimed at the user side. It helps to reduce glare on your screen while keeping your face softly lit, which improves your Zoom meeting presence—your coworkers get the vibe that you’re managing world domination with a latte in hand.

## Maintenance and care
Maintenance is minimal but not optional if you want the panel to look snazzy for longer than a single quarter. Wipe down with a damp microfiber cloth, and avoid harsh detergents that could dull the surface. If you notice micro-scratches after months of use, you can buff the surface lightly with a plastic-safe cleaner; it won’t restore perfect like-new clarity, but it can reduce the appearance of wear.

The C-clamps should be checked occasionally for looseness. If you hear a soft squeak or feel movement in the panel, re-tighten the clamps a notch and verify the contact points on the desk are clean. Avoid gripping over edges where the desk surface is already damaged; you don’t want to turn a small impression into a big sad watermark on your desk.
