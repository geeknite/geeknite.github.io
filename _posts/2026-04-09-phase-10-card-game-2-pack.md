## How to Play (A Quick, Friendly Refresher)

If you know Phase 10 already, you’ll walk into this with a wince and a grin. If you don’t, you’re about to learn a card game that blends set-building with a match-your-phase mechanic. Here’s the quick refresher, adapted to the 2 Pack:

- Objective: Be the first player to complete all ten phases across the two decks. Each phase has its own requirements (e.g., two sets and two runs). The catch is that players must complete them in order, from Phase 1 to Phase 10, while also contending with the occasional “Phase 10 wilds” that can throw a wrench into even the most carefully laid plans.
- Turn structure: On your turn, you draw a card, decide whether to keep or discard, and then you may lay down a completed phase if you can. If you can’t complete a phase, you keep working on it across turns. The interplay between two decks means more opportunities to complete your phase, but also more cards to keep track of—which can mean more mental math and more friendly heckling.
- Wild cards and skips: The wild cards in Phase 10 let you substitute for any card in a phase, which is the perfect speed-running tool when you just need a card that fits precisely into your gleefully chaotic plan. Skips can hamper others’ progress or be used strategically to stall a rival who is flirting with that perfect hand. It’s a delicate dance between helping your own strategy and sabotaging your friends—and that’s where the game shines.

In the two-pack format, those moments scale up. You’ll experience more variability because you’re effectively merging two slightly different card sets into one table. This isn’t a “dual-wield phase” it’s more like a “two-stage rocket that sometimes still lands in your lap.” It’s messy, but in a good way.
