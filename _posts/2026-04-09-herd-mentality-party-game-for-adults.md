### What is Herd Mentality?
Herd Mentality is a party game about guessing what the majority of players will pick for a given prompt. Each round, a prompt card appears—think “Name something you wouldn’t want to find in your fridge at 2 AM” or “Choose a fictional character who would probably survive a zombie apocalypse.” Each player secretly writes an answer. Then everyone reveals their answers simultaneously. The twist: players vote on which answer they think the majority of players will choose. If your answer is chosen by the herd, you score points. If your answer is alone or unique, you get fewer or no points, depending on the round. The herd wins when the cumulative points tilt in the crowd’s favor. It’s a social meta-game about conformity, fear of missing out, and occasionally realizing you’re a living meme.

For the adult crowd, this is not just about going with the flow; it’s about reading the room, trolling the room with a perfect blind item, and discovering that the human brain is a surprisingly excellent predictive machine when everyone’s slightly tipsy and highly opinionated.

### Setup in Five Easy Steps
1. Shuffle the prompt deck.
2. Give each player a writing surface (narrow sticky notes work well) and a pen or pencil.
3. Decide on a maximum number of rounds per game (recommended: 6–8 for a standard game night, more if you’re chasing a marathon session).
4. Pick a starting player (the “herder” of the moment) and let the prompts begin.
5. Place the score track where everyone can see it, because nothing fuels conversation like watching your points disappear when your friends conspire to outthink you.

Setup is intentionally light. Herd Mentality is designed to be a party game where the biggest barrier to entry is not knowing your friends’ weird tendencies. If you’ve played a lot of micro-games, this will feel like a familiar, friendly warm-up that invites you to talk, tease, and occasionally reveal a surprisingly dark sense of humor.

## How to Play: The Skeleton of the Trickster’s Parade
### The Core Cycle
- A prompt card is drawn and read aloud.
- Every player secretly writes an answer on their own sheet.
- All answers are revealed and read aloud for the table to consider.
- Players secretly vote for the answer they think will be the herd’s pick.
- Points are awarded based on how many players chose your answer. Every round ends with a little celebration or a sarcastic groan depending on the result.

The key tension comes from balancing your own quirky idea against what you think the herd will actually pick. You’re playing social clairvoyant more than puzzle-solver, and the humor emerges from the misreads and conflicting perceptions of your friends.

### Scoring Usually Goes Like This
- If your answer is chosen by the herd (i.e., the majority of votes aligns with your response), you earn points. The exact number of points might vary by edition, but the spirit remains: blending in is often lucky, but sometimes standout originality can pay off—if you’re bold enough to risk it.
- If your answer isn’t chosen by the herd, you might still score depending on the house rules. Some groups award a consolation for coming close, others simply deny your points and let you brood in a humorous way while refilling drinks.
- The game tends to reward both big cackles and quiet strategy. Your mileage will vary based on how well you understand your circle’s collective taste.

### The Social Mechanics That Make It Sing
The beauty of Herd Mentality is not in the complexity of the rules, but in the social feedback loop it creates. ## Why It Works for Adults (And When It Might Not)
Herd Mentality shines with adult groups for a few reasons:
- Shared reference frames: Adults bring in personal memes, pop culture references, and inside jokes that can create surprisingly strong herd signals.
- Social risk-reward: There’s room for bold, offbeat answers that pay off if enough people think like you, but there’s also a stigma to “wrong” answers in the eyes of your friends—creating delicious tension.
- Flexible length: The rounds can be tuned to the table; you can do quick 3-minute bursts or take a full-blown game night approach.

On the downside, the game can feel repetitive after a handful of sessions if your group leans too heavily into the same style of prompt responses. It can also become loud and chaotic—great for a party, not so great if you’re trying to host a more intimate or quiet evening. If you’re the type to discuss game mechanics in depth or demand a strategic, puzzle-like experience, Herd Mentality may feel like a breezy escape rather than a deep dive.

## Strategies to Win (Without Selling Your Soul)
Here are some practical tips to maximize your herd-tapping potential without becoming a menace to your friends:
- Observe, then echo lightly: If you know the room’s vibe, lean into the most likely herd answers, but don’t overdo it. A touch of individuality in a sea of conformity can pay off in later rounds.
- Use shared experience: Pull from common experiences you know most players will recognize. Nostalgia is a powerful herd magnet.
- Mix predictability with surprises: Alternate between “obvious” answers and a subtle, clever spin. The herd loves the obvious pick, but the clever twist earns you bonus points when they decide your twist is “the move.”
- Read the room when voting: If the table is split between two safe options, maybe experiment with a third choice that still feels like it could be the herd’s pick. Calculated audacity can turn the tide.
- Don’t overthink silly prompts: Some prompts are designed to be silly on purpose. Embrace the silliness rather than analyzing every possible outcome; you’re playing a social game, not composing a dissertation.

A practical approach is to seed your mind with a few “go-to” answers that align with common experiences while keeping a few wild cards on standby. It’s the exact same strategy you’d use in trying to blend into a dance floor—look like you’re enjoying yourself, and you’ll be surprised how often the crowd follows.

## Variations and House Rules
If you want to keep Herd Mentality lively across multiple game nights, here are a few house-rule ideas that won’t ruin the core experience:
- The Celebrity Herd: In one round, players choose an “influence” card that grants extra points to the herd’s chosen answer if they can predict who played it. This adds an element of social bluffing to the mix.
- The Whisper Round: A silent round where players write a single, bold answer on a single card. Then you reveal and vote as usual, but the tension is multiplied because no one’s saying a word about why they chose what they chose.
- The Rapid Fire Variant: Shorten the writing phase to 15 seconds, push the penalty of wrong picks, and keep the pace brisk. Great for high-energy crowds.
- The $5 Rule: If you want to emphasize the “adult” vibe, establish a reward pool for the round with small stakes (snacks, dares, trivia challenges). Just keep it friendly and consensual.

House rules can extend the lifespan of the game in a way that keeps the humor fresh and the table engaged. The key is to ensure everyone consents to the added rules and has a clear sense of what counts as a fair round.
