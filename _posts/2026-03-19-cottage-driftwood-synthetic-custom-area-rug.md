## Cleaning, maintenance, and care
### Routine care
Vacuuming on a regular basis is your best defense against the creeping dust bunnies that haunt every tech lounge and living space. A vacuum with a gentle beater brush setting is ideal; you don’t want to unfold your rug into a modern art piece of shredded carpet fibers. A quick daily pass and a deeper weekly clean should keep the rug looking fresh and ready for a film night or a tabletop gaming session.

### Spot cleaning and stain removal
For spills, act fast. Blot up as much liquid as possible with a clean cloth or paper towel, then use a mild detergent solution to treat the area. Avoid soaking the pile—excess moisture can seep into the backing and potentially cause odor or mildew issues. For stubborn stains, a gentle carpet cleaner or a solution designed for synthetic fibers will usually do the trick. The key is to address the stain while it’s still a memory rather than a souvenir crammed into your rug’s fabric. If you live with coffee lovers or tea enthusiasts, you know exactly what that means.

### Dealing with pet accidents
Pet accidents require a bit more attention. Clean thoroughly, then consider a neutral-pH cleaning solution to prevent odor build-up. If your furry friend has a favorite spot, you may want to rotate the rug occasionally to ensure it ages evenly and doesn’t develop a commanding “this is my spot” vibe that shouts dominance to your eight-foot-tall bookshelf.

### Maintenance checklist
- Vacuum weekly
- Flip or rotate every few months if you notice wear patterns
- Blot spills immediately
- Treat stains promptly with appropriate cleaners
- Avoid direct heat or sunlight for extended periods to preserve color
