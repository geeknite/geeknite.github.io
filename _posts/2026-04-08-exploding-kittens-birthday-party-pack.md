## What is the Birthday Party Pack?

The Birthday Party Pack is an expansion kit designed to slot into Exploding Kittens and crank up the birthday chaos. Expect birthday-themed cards, new card types, and a pinch of extra mischief that makes the core mechanics feel fresher without turning the game into a full-blown new title. Thematically, it leans hard into cake, candles, and the delightful chaos of party planning—where party hats become potential projectiles, and cake slices double as strategic tools.

### A quick overview
- Adds a handful of birthday themed cards to the deck
- Introduces new effects that interact with the base game cards in surprising ways
- Keeps the playtime short, the humor loud, and the tension deliciously ridiculous
- Designed to scale with 2 to 6 players, or more if you like chaotic diplomacy and cake envy

In short, if Exploding Kittens is chaos with a cute whiskers, the Birthday Party Pack is chaos with a party favor and a confetti cannon. The mood it sets is perfect for birthday celebrations, game nights, or any event where you want to feel like a party mascot but with a much higher risk of accidental laughter-induced missteps.

## How to play with the Birthday Party Pack
If you already know Exploding Kittens, you’ll recognize the core flow, but the Birthday Party Pack adds its own twists. Here is a straightforward run-through to get you from setup to chaos in under ten minutes.

### Setup
- Shuffle the base deck together with the Birthday Party Pack cards. Some groups may opt to play exclusively with the Birthday cards for a themed night, but mixing tends to yield the best results.
- Remove any cards not compatible with your house rules if you have established a preferred variant.
- Deal cards as you normally would in Exploding Kittens, with the venue selection of the cake table and the party playlist ready to blast at the first fizzle of the first turn.

### Turn structure
The Birthday Party Pack does not reinvent the wheel; it adds new options that appear when certain conditions are met or certain cards are drawn. In practice, you will see:
- Birthday draw effects that trigger optional celebrations or cake-themed misfortunes
- Cards that reward players for keeping a calm demeanor around cake knives (or in this context, around exploding kittens)
- A few items that add synergy with existing cards, so you are not learning an entirely new system on the same night

A typical round still looks familiar if you have played Exploding Kittens before: players take turns drawing cards, playing as needed, avoiding the exploding kitten, and hoping the cake holds together long enough for the fireworks to go off at the right moment.

### Birthday card effects in practice
- The Party Popper card forces everyone to show their cards and reveal any trick cards in their hand, which increases tension and helps the next player plan a prank.
- The Cake Slice card acts as a shield for that moment when the deck is about to collapse, giving you an extra option to avoid an explosion—though not without some risk attached.
- The Confetti Bomb card triggers a chaotic sequence that can flip the table (metaphorically, of course) in a good-natured, chaotic way that makes players groan and giggle simultaneously.

These effects are designed to be thematic (birthday cake, candles, and confetti) while preserving the core risk-reward dynamic that Exploding Kittens fans crave.

### How it interacts with the base deck
The new cards are carefully balanced to feel like they were always meant to be in the same universe as the base game. They do not overshadow the core mechanics but instead provide spicy new combos that reward memory, timing, and a little bit of party host cunning. The result is a game that feels fresh without requiring an advanced degree in card-ology.
