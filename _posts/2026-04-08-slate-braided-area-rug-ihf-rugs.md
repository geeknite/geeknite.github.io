## Cleaning and Care Guide

A practical rug should be easy to maintain. Here’s a reliable routine:

- Vacuum regularly with a non-beater setting to avoid fuzzing the braid.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, absorbent cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper and distort the braid.
- For spot cleaning, use a mild detergent solution and test on a small area first. Do not saturate the rug; braided rugs dislike soaking.
- Rotate occasionally to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones or under heavy furniture.
- For deeper cleaning, consult a professional who specializes in braided textiles. They can refresh the fibers without compromising the weave.

If you want to minimize maintenance, placing the rug on a rug pad that provides cushion and grip is a small investment with surprisingly large returns. Your feet and your furniture will thank you.
