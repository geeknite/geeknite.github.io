## How Skip-Bo Express works in this edition
Express variants emphasize speed, minimal setup, and quick rounds. The core idea remains the same: you manage a hand of cards and try to play them in numerical order onto building piles. The wild cards give you liberties when the numbers just refuse to cooperate. The express edition trims away some of the fussy rules that can bog down a regular round, leaving you with an immediate sense of momentum. It is the gaming equivalent of a shot of espresso for a card game night.

### Core mechanics in a nutshell
- You draw a small handful of cards each turn
- You attempt to play cards onto building piles in ascending order
- Wild or skip cards help you fill gaps and keep the piles flowing
- The first player to clear their hand or a designated goal wins
- The brisk pacing makes it ideal for 15 to 25 minute sessions

If you have ever played a speed round of a classic card game, you know the drill. If not, imagine a world where your memory can juggle a few numbers at once while a helpful jokester sits on your shoulder whispering the right move just as you think you have a plan ready to deploy.
