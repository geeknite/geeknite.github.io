## Overview
Herd Mentality is a party game that revels in the thrill of guessing and lying within a crowd. It's the kind of game that turns a living room into a small theater where everyone tries to predict what the majority would say, and sometimes tries to mislead them with a bold, ridiculous answer. The core idea: you want to pick or craft an answer that the herd would pick, but you also want to present something that stands out just enough to catch the eye of your fellow players.

Quick facts: 4-12 players, about 20-30 minutes per session, ages 14+. Great for game nights, college dorms, and awkward family gatherings when strategically deployed.

## How to Play (the short version)
1. Setup: Shuffle the prompts. Each round, a prompt is revealed and every player writes down one answer that they think the herd would choose. You can use your own brain's wisdom or dive into pop culture logic or pure nonsense; the choice is yours.

2. Reveal and vote: All answers are revealed. The players secretly vote for the answer they think the majority would pick. This is where the herd starts to appear as a living organism rather than a bunch of strangers with snack stains.

3. Score: If your answer is selected as the herd's pick by the votes, you earn points. If you manage to predict which answer the herd would pick on your own (for example by trusting your own sense of how others will think), you can earn bonus points. The exact scoring scheme can vary by edition, but the core principle remains: alignment with the herd means reward.

4. End conditions: After a set number of rounds, tally points. The person with the highest score at the end wins. In practice, the game tends to devolve into a chorus of laughter and mild existential dread as people realize how predictably unpredictable we all are.

## Strategy and Tips
- Play to what you think the herd would say, not what you personally would write. The game's physics reward social prediction more than personal wit.
- Use approachable, recognizable answers. Niche references can be funny but may fail to capture the herd’s heart.
- Balance your risk. If you have an obvious answer, you might be safely in the herd; if you have a wild answer, you must make the herd think there is a rational reason behind it.
- Observe your players. Herd Mentality rewards social sensing as much as clever writing. Track the voting patterns of your friends to predict how they might respond to future prompts.
- Consider a few house rules for a better experience: allow players to veto or reinterpret some prompts if the game stalls, or implement a “joker” option for a round to avoid dead air.

## Variants and Extensions

The herd’s dynamics change with the makeup of players, the time of day, and the number of laughs you’ve already had that night. If you enjoy games where the social moment matters as much as the mechanics, you will likely keep this on rotation.
