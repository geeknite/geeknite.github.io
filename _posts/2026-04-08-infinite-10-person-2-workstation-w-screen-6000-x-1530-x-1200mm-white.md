## Ergonomics and Comfort
Comfort is the currency here. If you’re going to be sharing a long bench with ten colleagues, you want personal space, good seat height, and monitor placement that won’t force you into the “ledge of doom” pose where your neck migrates toward your shoulder.

We recommend a seating arrangement that emphasizes adjustable chairs with lumbar support and armrests that can slide in and out to accommodate different arm lengths. The screen height should be adjustable so that eye level sits around 1.2 to 1.4 times the user’s height, depending on posture. On a large bench like this, you’ll also want to ensure that someone isn’t repeatedly leaning forward to read a screen while others can’t see. A small screen riser in the center or an anti-glare finish on the screens can prevent neck strain from staring at bright reflections.

We also tested the “sit-stand” potential of the bench. The height is comfortable for seated work for long sessions, but if you need to encourage movement across shifts, you will benefit from a sit-stand option or integration with power-adjustable desks. The ergonomic payoff is worth it: more energy, better focus, and fewer spells of “my workstation is haunted by a cable ghost.”
