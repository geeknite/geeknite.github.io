## Safety Considerations and Best Practices

### Setup area and ground rules

Choose a flat, level surface free of obstacles, sharp objects, and suspiciously suspicious neighbor dogs. A stable base is crucial; wobbly ground makes the frame work harder and increases the risk of tipping. The enclosure net should stand tall and secure with anchors. Keep a little play area around the trampoline to avoid collisions with garden furniture or the barbecue—safety first, party later.

### Weight limits and supervision

Respect the manufacturer’s weight limits. If the guideline says 250 pounds, that includes the total user weight, not your cousin’s extra luggage. Supervision is mandatory for kids, especially during first weeks of use. For adults, use common sense: avoid complex flips unless you are trained, and avoid multiple athletes on the jumping surface if you’re not prepared for a gravity remix.

### Maintenance and upkeep

Periodically inspect springs for wear, check the net for tears, and tighten any loose hardware. Replace worn parts promptly. A tiny rattle can be a harbinger of more painful noises later if ignored. Finally, store the product in a dry place when not in use to prolong its life and keep it looking presentable for neighbors who might borrow a cup of sugar and end up with a backyard fortress of joy.
