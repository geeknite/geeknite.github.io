## Painting & Hobby Tips
If you’re reading this, you probably want to paint at least one of these before your next session so your players can go ooh instead of eww. Here are practical tips to bring out those undead greens and rotten leather.

### Basecoat & Color Scheme
- Base: A neutral dark green or olive for the skin; use a midtone to prevent a flat look.
- Cloth and Armor: Leather browns and muted metallics; avoid chrome-shiny to keep the undead vibe.
- Flesh Wash: A blue-tinted or purple wash to push shadows into recesses while preserving the green hue.

### Shading, Highlights & Depth
- Layering technique: A few thin layers of higher-value greens toward raised surfaces to simulate light catching on bones and armor bars.
- Edge highlights: Very light gray or bone white on edges of armor and tusk tips for a crisp look.
- Flesh shading: Build contrast with a darker base tone in deeper crevices; lighten toward knuckles to emphasize knobby structure.

### Weathering & Gore Effects
- Subtle blood splatter on armor and teeth evokes recent skirmishes; use a tiny brush and a dab of red with brown wash.
- Gore: A light darker wash on exposed flesh and bone can read as rotting tissue without becoming obscene on the table.

### Quick Paint Scheme for Fast Table-Ready Minis
If you’re pressed for time, you can push a speed paint approach: basecoat, wash, drybrush highlight, then apply a dry pigment to the base. Your players will be grateful for the visible undead presence on the field even if the paint job isn’t museum-worthy.

> Tip: Paint in batches. You’ll thank me when you’re varnishing the entire box at 1 AM and your coffee still tastes like bean optimism.

## Painting, Storage & Maintenance
A big part of owning real minis is not just painting, but keeping them intact through a few heavy sessions.

### Storage Tips
- Use a padded tray box with foam inserts to prevent chipping.
- Place the four orc zombies in different sections of the tray so you can pull a deadly quartet on demand.

### Maintenance
- If you notice creaking joints after a handful of battles, consider a quick drop of plastic-safe lubricant on joints; a dab of glue can also fix loose limbs.
- Keep the figures away from heat; plastic can warp under high temperature, especially if you’re a heavy-handed painter with a hairdryer to speed drying.
