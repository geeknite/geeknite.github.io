## Durability and Cleaning: How Resilient Is It?

### Daily Wear and Tear
In real-life tests, the rug stood up to ordinary wear from sandals, pet paws, and the occasional ceremonial foot-stomping during a backyard game of cornhole. It’s not a fitness mat; it’s a surface meant to withstand routine outdoor life without complaining.

### Cleaning: The Easy Route
One of the biggest selling points for polypropylene is how easy it is to clean. A quick vacuum can remove dust and crumbs; a hose-down can remove mud, pollen, and that suspicious tick-mark of a leaf that decided to become a permanent feature. For tricky stains like red wine or sauce splashes, a mild detergent mixed with water and a light scrubbing usually does the job without dissolving the fiber. It’s not a miracle cleaner, but it’s a reliable maintenance routine that doesn’t require you to own a steam cleaner the size of a microwave.

### Longevity: How Long Will It Last?
A rug in this category is designed for seasons, not centuries. It will hold up for several summers with reasonable care, and its color will remain in the ochre family rather than turning into the color of an overcooked fried egg. If you treat it well—avoid dragging heavy furniture, don’t yank at the edges, and store it properly when not in use—it can be a long-lived garden-side companion rather than a seasonal fling.
