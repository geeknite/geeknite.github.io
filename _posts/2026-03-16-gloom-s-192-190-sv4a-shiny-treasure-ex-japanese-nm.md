### Condition Signals for NM
Near Mint isn’t a single, rigid number; it’s a suite of indicators. In this Gloom’s case, you’re looking for: crisp corners (no whitening), flat surface with even holo—no obvious scratches or scuffs, and no crease lines or edge wear along the foil. The card should lie flat and glide into a sleeve with little resistance. In high-grade NM variants, you’ll also notice the text remains sharp without any ghosting of color blur on the printed details. When you inspect, a trusted method is to compare against a known NM reference card from the same print run, if you’re lucky enough to have one in the neighborhood.

### Handling and Presentation Tips
- Use soft sleeves with inner liners to minimize corner wear. 
- Place the card in a top loader before tool-free storage to protect from accidental jostling.
- Avoid long exposure to direct sunlight; UV can subtly alter holo sheen over time.
- Keep humidity steady; extremes can affect the card’s flex and surface quality.
- If you’re photographing for listing or hobby content, use diffuse light to avoid hotspot glare on the holo.

## Authenticity and Verification: How to Protect Your Investment
### Quick Authentication Guide
- Verify the card’s set symbol and number (S-192/190 in this case) against official or trusted reference sheets for the SV4a subset.
- Inspect holo foiling quality under proper lighting to ensure alignment with the art; any misalignment can be a red flag.
- Check the card’s back for consistent color and font printing—janky ink or misprints on the back can indicate improper reprinting or counterfeits.
- Compare edge bevels, corner rounding, and surface gloss to reputable NM samples from the same print run.

### Preservation as Insurance
- Store NM cards in a climate-controlled environment; avoid humidity spikes and direct sun.
- Use UV-filtering sleeves or cases if you intend on exhibiting or photographing frequently.
- Maintain a catalog with serial-like notes for each card’s print run and any distinguishing marks—this helps if you’re ever asked to verify provenance.
