## Collecting tips and display strategies
This Gloom AR isn’t just a card; it’s a small sculpture you can carry in your pocket and display on a coffee table without lighting candles. Here are practical, field-tested tips to maximize enjoyment and minimize the drama:

- Sleeve the card with a high-quality inner sleeve and pair it with a rigid top loader. This combo protects the holo surface during transport to shows and keeps corners pristine during casual flipping in your daily life.
- Use a binder with pages sized for ARs—9 to 12 per page is a sweet spot for visual cohesion. Keeping related ARs together lets you compare textures, borders, and holo depth side-by-side without hunting through a dozen binders.
- Climate control matters. Extreme heat can degrade holo and degrade the gentle caress of light ripple on the surface. A cool, dry shelf is your friend.
- Document and photograph. Back up a high-quality photoset you can share for trades and listings. The better your photos, the more trustworthy your listing—and fewer headaches when someone asks for macro shots of the corners.
- Track serial notes if your card carries any. Some Japanese prints include internal codes or batch hints that can help you verify authenticity and catch misprints before you spread the word to suspicious buyers.
