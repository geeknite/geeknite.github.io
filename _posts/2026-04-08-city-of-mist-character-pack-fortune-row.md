## Fortune Row Character Spotlight

### Fortuna Dea The Gambler
Archetype: Gambler of fate
Motif: Luck and risk
Mythos: Fortuna and Tyche
Logos: Streetwise, Deception, Athletics
Powers: Lucky Break, Fate's Edge, Glass Ceiling
Backstory: Fortuna Dea grew up on crowded casino floors where the house always wins unless you learn to outplay the odds. She treats luck like a currency and keeps enough backups in her pockets to pay for the consequences of living in neon shadows.
Role at the table: The undeniable nerve center; she can swing an investigation with a well-timed bluff or a risky bet that pays off in unexpected ways.
Hooks: The big game at a secret venue where a misdeal could topple a power broker; a rival gambit that forces Fortuna to choose between personal luck and loyalty to her crew.

### Velvet Rook The Social Engineer
Archetype: Social navigator
Motif: Influence, control of the conversation
Mythos: Aphrodite and The Host
Logos: Persuasion, Subterfuge, Rapport
Powers: Velvet Gaze, Spin Doctor, Curtain Call
Backstory: Velvet Rook runs a prestige club where secrets are the currency and the guest list reads like a who is who of the city. Her network is a living thing that breathes and shuffles influence like a dealer shuffles cards.
Role at the table: The master of information flow; Velvet can defuse a volatile scene or escalate one into a carefully choreographed reveal.
Hooks: A gala that becomes a stakeout; a whispered rumor about a secret treaty that could tilt a citywide feud; Velvet’s own past catching up in the form of a former ally turned rival.

### Ace Card The Trickster
Archetype: Master of misdirection
Motif: Masks and illusion
Mythos: Loki and Hermes
Logos: Stealth, Deception, Acrobatics
Powers: Cardsharp Mirage, Escape Plan, Blackjack
Backstory: Ace Card used to perform in a street theatre and now uses those tricks to pull off heists that feel impossibly stylish. He lives for moments when the audience believes the con but forgets the act at the last breath of a reveal.
Role at the table: The classic wildcard; Ace Card keeps the table guessing, providing both the spark of misdirection and the keystone for dramatic confrontations.
Hooks: A masked ball where the trick is not the trick but the reveal; a rival who wants to steal Ace Card’s signature move and weaponize it against him.

### Cassian The Croupier The Handler
Archetype: Networker and operator
Motif: Doors and access
Mythos: Janus and Hermes
Logos: Contacts, Investigation, Streetwise
Powers: Open Doors, Gatekeeper, Knuckle Bump

Hooks: A backroom power play that requires crossing paths with a mysterious benefactor or a brutal creditor; a door that should be closed but refuses to stay shut.

## How to Run Fortune Row at the Table

Fortune Row shines when you lean into its vibe: neon grit, social leverage, and the constant tension between luck and responsibility. The four characters can be used as a compact team or as independent leads for separate threads that eventually converge.

### Team Play vs Individual Spotlight
If your table enjoys ensemble play, Fortune Row gives you a reason to run all four as a coordinated crew. Each member has a distinct lane, but there are plenty of cross-links that make the team feel like a living organism rather than four archetypes pasted together. If you prefer a leaner table, you can run two or three members at a time and still have enough narrative momentum to carry you through a couple of sessions.

### Plot Seeds and How They Unite
The included plot seeds are written to allow you to chain them into a larger arc or run as standalones. A recurring motif is the tension between private luck and public consequence. The crew can garden a citywide rumor that spirals into real trouble, or use a high profile event as cover for a more intimate caper. Either way, Fortune Row asks a simple question at each turn: what does luck cost, and who pays the price?

### Scenes and Set Pieces
Fortune Row is optimized for location-based scenes that are heavy on atmosphere. A swank nightclub with a guarded back room, a hidden casino beneath a glass tower, and a rooftop rendezvous with a view of a city that never sleeps all work well as recurring anchors. The GM can stage confrontations here that feel big and cinematic without requiring a battlefield-scale fight="$scenes". Instead, expect conversations to shift power, reveal secrets, or lay down the gauntlet for a dramatic showdown later in the arc.

### How to Use Existing Material in Your Campaign
If your campaign already has a villain or a faction that aligns with luck or deception, Fortune Row can slot into that subplot with minimal tweaks. The characters can be reinterpreted as agents of the same syndicate, former allies now ambiguous, or rivals who exist at the margins of the primary conflict. The goal is to leverage the pack as a narrative bridge between your ongoing investigations and a glamorous, dangerous side road that your players will want to explore.

## Narrative Tips and GM Guidance

- Let luck be a character. In a game about myths in the modern world, luck can be shaped, bribed, or cursed. Use Fortune Dea as a catalyst whose luck-drenched successes carry collateral consequences that ripple across the arc.
- Run social scenes as meaningful puzzles. Velvet Rook shines in dialogue-heavy scenes. Give her lines that force players to reveal motives, negotiate terms, and decide what they are willing to sacrifice for a win.
- Use misdirection as a plot engine. Ace Card is not just a flashy trickster; he is a device that pushes players to question what they see, what they believe, and what they choose to do when the sleight of hand becomes a moral test.
- Tie outcomes to mythic motifs. Each character’s mythic identity should have a direct line to a scene outcome. If Fortuna Dea makes a lucky break, what does the city owe her in return? If Velvet spins a truth, who gets misled and who gets the truth?
- Build personal stakes. The best Fortune Row games connect the pack’s secrets to the players’ own backstories. If a player once walked away from a casino job in shame, let Fortune Row force them to face that choice again with consequences they cannot ignore.
