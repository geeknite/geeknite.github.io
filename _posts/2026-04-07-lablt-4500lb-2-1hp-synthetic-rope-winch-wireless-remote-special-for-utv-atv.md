### Safety first: a running manual for the wild
Winching is one of those activities that sounds like a simple hoist but can become dangerous very quickly. The LABLT unit itself is well-built, but you still need to practice basic safety rules:
- Always wear gloves when handling synthetic rope; it can chafe and can deteriorate if abused.
- Inspect the rope for frays and cuts before each use. If you see damage, retire the rope and replace it; don’t risk a sudden failure mid-recovery.
- Keep bystanders clear of the pull line. A rope under tension can cause injuries or unintended movement if something in the line shifts unexpectedly.
- Never stand in the line of pull; the best place to observe is behind a solid obstruction or behind your vehicle, where you’re less likely to suffer an unplanned hug from the rope.
- Test the system with a light load before committing to a heavier pull. This helps verify the remote response, wiring, and general coordination of the winch with your rig.

In short: treat it seriously, keep your wits about you, and you’ll have a tool that’s as dependable as your favorite snack during a long ride.

### Installation tips and tricks
- Read the user manual and plan your wiring path before you start. A little planning saves a lot of head-scratching later.
- Use the proper mounting bolts and spacers. Don’t improvise with random hardware you found in the junk drawer; this is one place where improvisation can become a long-term problem.
- Clean the mounting area and inspect your anchor points. A dirty pallet or a weak anchor is a poor substitute for strength and safety.
- When mounting, align the winch so the drum feeds rope smoothly. A misaligned drum can lead to rope snagging and performance hiccups you don’t want to explain to your mechanic.
- Verify the remote’s range by walking a few paces away from the vehicle in different directions. If you lose control or hear static, re-check the wiring or distance restrictions.
