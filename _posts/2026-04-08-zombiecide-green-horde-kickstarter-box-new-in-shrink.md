## Gameplay: The Core Loop and How It Feels Different
At its heart, Zombicide remains a dice-charged, tile-destroying, cooperative experience. The Green Horde package injects a new flavor into that base recipe. Here are the big changes you’ll notice.

- Orcish movement and attack patterns: The horde doesn’t simply walk in a straight line. They maneuver around obstacles, which introduces a tactical challenge for players who tend to run through the map like a pinball. You’ll sometimes be forced to cut a path and retreat to fight another day, which is a refreshing change from the “let’s sprint into the center of the board” pattern.

- New encounter deck: The event and encounter cards bring varied effects that can disrupt your best-laid plans, sometimes producing dramatic results in a good way and other times forcing you to improvise mid-round. It keeps players honest and engaged, and it’s a great way to keep multiplayer sessions from becoming predictable.

- Green fog and line-of-sight dynamics: Time to re-check your LoS calculations, because the green fog can obscure sightlines in dramatic fashion. This mechanic incentivizes careful planning and better teamwork, and you’ll find yourself using more terrain features than in a normal Zombicide game.

- The synergy between survivors and horde: The synergy is what really sells the expansion. The survivors have specialized roles that mesh with orcs’ aggressive tempo, so you’ll need to coordinate your moves and plan ahead, rather than just pressing the kill button in the most direct route possible. It’s a little more strategic and a lot more fun.

### Combat and Dice
Combat remains the core thrill: you roll dice, assign hits, and manage wounds. The Green Horde adds some. The numbers aren’t the same in every scenario, but the general principle remains: you want to maximize your dice results, protect your most valuable survivors, and avoid missteps that will ruin your round. The new units alter the dice pool in a way that makes you adjust your tactics mid-game. It’s less predictable and more satisfying when your plan comes together.
