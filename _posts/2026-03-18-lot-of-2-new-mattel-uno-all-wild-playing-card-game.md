## All Wild mechanics explained
### How it differs from standard UNO
The core objective remains the same: shed your cards first. The difference is that this version gives you extra Wild cards and action effects that can swing the momentum in a single turn. Expect more color changes, more forced draws, and more opportunities to dunk on your friends with a well-timed All Wild card.

### The two deck dynamic
Mixing two decks doubles the action. You can chain combos more effectively, but you also risk running into more confusion if players are not paying attention. The extra decks create longer games, which can be a pro or a con depending on your group.

## Two-player vs party mode
### Small group play
Two players is a brisk duel with a lot of back-and-forth banter. The All Wild cards still exist, meaning you can lean on tactical draws to pressure your opponent.

### Large groups
With bigger groups, the chaos escalates. The table becomes a stage, and the players become improv comedians with a focus on color and wordplay. The All Wild aspect fosters social interaction and lighthearted chaos.

## Strategy and tips
- Keep an eye on the color pool: if you hold onto the wrong color too long, you set yourself up for a color shift that could ruin the plan
- Use wilds strategically: they are your best chance to turn the game in your favor, but if you misplay them, you’ll hand victory to someone else
- Pair the two decks thoughtfully to maximize options without losing track of what’s in the draw pile
- For newcomers, start with classic UNO to learn rhythm, then graduate to All Wild
