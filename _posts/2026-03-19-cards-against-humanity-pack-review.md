## Practical Tips for Hosts and Players

### Setting expectations before you flip the first card

- Do a quick vibe check with players about humor boundaries.
- Decide on a few house rules for this session if needed (no offensive prompts without consent, etc).
- Have a contingency plan for quiet nights: a different game might be a better fit if the room is not up for edgy humor.

### Handling awkward moments with grace

- If a card lands poorly, laugh it off and move on. A well-timed cringe is part of the charm, not a personal attack.
- Have a couple of go-to lighter prompts ready so you can pivot when needed.
- Remember that humor is subjective; what lands for some might miss for others. Respect the space of your fellow players.

### Maximizing replays without fatigue

- Mix prompts with personal inside jokes that only your crew understands.
- Create a running tally of favorite lines and award a light-hearted prize at the end of the night.
- Rotate judges to keep the game fresh and to spread hosting duties evenly.
