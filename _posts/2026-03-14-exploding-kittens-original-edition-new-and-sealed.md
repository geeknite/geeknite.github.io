## Overview of the Original Edition

Exploding Kittens is a party card game designed to be quick to learn and even quicker to spark laughter. The original edition came with a core deck featuring Exploding Kitten cards, Defuse cards that save you from the inevitable blast, and a variety of Cat cards that unleash memes and mischief. The premise is simple: draw cards to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten. If you draw one and you have a Defuse, you can save yourself and reshuffle the danger into the bottom of the deck. If not, you are out of the round and the remaining players keep the chaos going. The last kitten standing, or the last player alive in a given round, wins the round. Repeat until someone has a preposterous number of victory points, or more realistically, until the pizza arrives.

The charm of the original edition lies not in revolutionary mechanics but in the execution: crisp art, playful cat memes, and a sense of chaos that emerges organically when six adults try to outpace a ticking time bomb drawn from a deck. In a sealed copy, the experience is elevated by anticipation — the squeak of shrink wrap, the glossy texture of the cards, and the knowledge that your first draw will either set you up for glory or doom you to the fate of a meme that dies in a single, glorious moment.

## Rules at a Glance: What to Expect When You Play

The rules are deliberately simple, which is a perk for newcomers and a relief for veteran players who want to jump straight into the chaos without flipping through a thousand pages of clarifications.
- On your turn you may play any number of action cards and then draw from the deck.
- Exploding Kitten cards are bombs. If you draw one and you lack a Defuse, you are out of the round.
- Defuse cards cancel an Exploding Kitten draw. After using a Defuse, the Exploding Kitten is reshuffled somewhere into the deck, keeping players guessing.
- Cat cards grant unique actions or special effects when played, often changing who draws next or who is forced to take an extra card.

The pacing is brisk. Rounds typically last only 10-15 minutes, which makes it perfect for a session between longer games or for a party where attention spans are measured in memes per minute. The social dynamics matter more than the mechanics; the fun emerges from the way players react to near misses, dramatic defuses, and the inevitable moment when someone whispers, I probably should have saved that Defuse card for the end.
