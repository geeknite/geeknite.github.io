## Performance and Durability

### Foot traffic and wear

Wool offers resilience, while jute supplies a sturdy backbone that can handle typical living room use. This rug is best suited for living rooms, dining rooms with care, bedrooms, and sunlit nooks where you want texture to be part of the scenery without worrying about immediate shedding or fuzzing. In dining rooms with heavy daily use, it is wise to pair the rug with a non-slip rug pad to keep it flat and to protect the fibers from chair legs.

### Stain resilience and cleaning

Wool fibers can repel small spills if treated quickly, and a quick blot with a clean cloth goes a long way. For more stubborn spots, a mild detergent solution and a gentle blotting approach can help. Avoid aggressive scrubbing, which can disrupt the weave and flatten the texture you love. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting keeps dirt from embedding into the fibers. A hand bush or a suede brush can help lift the nap and maintain the rug’s plush feel.

### Longevity expectations

With proper care, this rug can be a fixture in your home for years. The wool adds a softness that ages gracefully, while the jute keeps the structure intact. If you routinely move furniture across it, consider rotating the rug occasionally to even out wear. The true test of a rug is not how it looks on day one but how well it performs after daddy long legs visits for holiday photos.

## Care and Maintenance

### Routine care

- Vacuum regularly with a low-power setting, preferably with a wand attachment to avoid mats forming at the rug edges.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fade from sunlight.
- Brush the surface gently with a soft-bristled brush to lift the fibers and maintain texture.

### Cleaning spots

- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing drives stains deeper into the fibers.
- For tougher stains, test a mild solution of water and a small amount of gentle detergent on a discreet area first.
- Allow the rug to air dry away from direct heat sources to avoid fiber damage.

### Sunlight and fading

Natural materials can fade with prolonged sun exposure. If your space has a strong afternoon sun, consider rotating the rug or using window treatments to protect the fibers from UV exposure while still enjoying the rug’s warm presence.
