### Design and craftsmanship

The look you buy with a Kilim Dhurrie is not only texture; it is geometry in motion. Flat weave means the pattern is created by interlacing the weft and warp threads, not by looping knots. That makes the rug exceptionally flat, which in turn reduces bulk and makes it easier to align with furniture legs, track lighting, and that one noisy chair you forgot to tighten. Cotton as a fiber brings a balance of softness and resilience. The sky blue color in this piece is achieved with dye that resists quick fading under moderate sun exposure, though you should still be mindful of direct sun that can gently bleach color over months or years. In a bright living room, rotate the rug every few months to avoid uneven wear and color drift.

In terms of thickness, flat weaves like this rug sit closer to the floor than a plush, high-pile rug, but not so thin that you nick your shins on moving furniture. The edges are finished with a clean hem that holds shape under vacuum and foot traffic. If you want to maximize longevity, consider a rug pad; it reduces movement, provides a little additional cushioning, and protects flooring from scratches when you rearrange your furniture for a game night or a streaming binge.

### Size and placement guidance

Rug sizes matter. A small rug in a large space can look like a carpet swatch that forgot its courage. A good rule of thumb: the rug should accommodate the fronts legs of two to three main seating pieces, or define a space in an open plan. For a standard living room, a 5x7 or 6x9 rug is a solid starting point when paired with a sofa set that sits around 8 feet in length. In bedrooms, a rug that sits under the lower two-thirds of the bed can look balanced, but you should ensure that there is about 18 to 24 inches of rug visible beyond the bed on at least two sides. The Kilim Dhurrie you are considering is typically available in a range of sizes from small runners to larger area rug dimensions. Consider measuring your room in advance and bringing a mock-up or painter tape outline to visualize the space before purchase.

Maintenance note: flat weaves are less likely to shed fibers than looped pile rugs, but cotton fibers can pill or show wear in high-traffic corridors. Regular vacuuming with a low-suction setting or a hand-push attachment helps. Avoid heavy beater bars that tug at the weave. If a spill happens, blot gently with a clean cloth and avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the weave. For tough stains, test a small inconspicuous area first and consult a professional cleaner if needed. A quick tip from our care guide: rotate the rug seasonally to equalize wear and produce a more uniform look over time.

### Care and maintenance

Cotton kilims should be cleaned with care. Use a vacuum with an adjustable height and avoid aggressive suction that can pull on the weave. If you must shampoo, use a mild detergent and a soft brush, and avoid saturating the rug. Dry it flat in a well-ventilated area, away from direct heat sources. Colorfastness can vary by dye lot, so test a corner before heavy cleaning. Because this rug is flat woven, it dries quickly, which reduces the risk of mildew in humid climates.
