## Introduction
City of Mist is a game about myths wearing masks in neon city alleys. The Fortune Row Special Character Set dives into that vibe by offering a curated roster of Special Characters that are not your standard heroes. They are fated, quirky, and somewhat chaotic, in a way that makes every session feel like a neon sign flickering in a storm. In this review we examine what Fortune Row adds to the base game, how it fits with the mood, and whether this set is a creative leap or just another pretty box on a shelf.

## What is Fortune Row Special Character Set
Fortune Row is a themed subset of City of Mist that gives players access to a curated roster of Special Characters. These are not your common capes; they are symbols of fate, chance, and the city itself. This set includes new character sheets, new power tags, new story hooks, and a chunk of flavor text that reads like a late night talkshow monologue written by a mysterious oracle.

## Mechanics and design philosophy
Fortune Row emphasizes dice pools that reflect fate rather than brute force. The Special Characters come with unique tags like Fate, Coin, Echo, and Crowd, and these interact with the world in intriguing ways. The design encourages players to lean into misfortune as a narrative engine; bad rolls become story seeds rather than obstacles.

## Character creation experience
The process is quick, with a handful of prompts to choose from. You can mix and match backgrounds and motivations, which makes it easy to slot into a table that already has running arcs. The prompts push players toward themes of chance and perception, which aligns with the City of Mist aesthetic.

## Story hooks and themes
Fortune Row yields arcs about luck cycles, heists, and illusions. A session may begin with a fortune that seems to go wrong in the moment but delivers a better outcome later. The GM is given ready made hooks, but room to improvise if the party insists on a different fate.

## Artwork and presentation
The art direction stays consistent with City of Mist while offering its own flavor. The color palette leans into neon pinks and midnight blue greens. The character portraits carry mood rather than explicit action, which suits a game that centers on masks and rumors. The layout is clean, with readable fonts and clear symbol keys. If you are running on a dim lamp, you will appreciate that the icons remain legible.

## How to run a session with Fortune Row
Plan to weave the Special Characters into the existing Fate and Mythos framework. Keep a few memorable prompts handy and let the players decide how the fate dice tilt. The recommended setup is a short session in which the Fortune Row characters reveal a city secret, followed by a twist that makes the group rethink who the heroes are.

## Balance and pacing
As with most City of Mist expansions, Fortune Row does not turn the game into a power contest. It adds flavor and narrative tools that can accelerate or slow down pacing depending on how the GM uses them. The key is to lean into misdirection, not to force a big set piece every encounter. If your group enjoys cinematic vibes with moral ambiguity, this set will sing.

## Accessibility and player experience
The set tries to stay accessible; the new tags are not overwhelming and there is a logical progression from base rules. For players new to City of Mist, the extra layers may feel heavy at first, but the payoff comes as soon as the team grasps the pattern of fate that the Special Characters bring to scenes.
