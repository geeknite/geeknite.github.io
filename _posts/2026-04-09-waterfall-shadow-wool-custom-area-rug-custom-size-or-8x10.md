## Custom Sizing vs 8x10
A central question is whether to lean into a custom size or rely on the trusty 8x10. Custom sizing is ideal when you have a unique space, unusual furniture layouts, or an architectural feature demanding a precise footprint. You can tailor length and width so the rug anchors a seating area exactly where you want it or frames a reading nook with surgical accuracy. The tradeoffs are longer lead times and a price bump—though not always a deal-breaker if the geometry is your friend.

An 8x10 remains a solid default for many living rooms and open-plan spaces. It pairs well with a standard sofa set or a couple of accent chairs and still has enough surface area to avoid looking dwarfed in larger rooms. If you have a large coffee table impulse or a wall of open floor around the rug, 8x10 can feel a touch undersized, like bringing a tiny knife to a dragon battle. Our approach remains to map your furniture footprint first, then pick the size that preserves traffic flow around the rug rather than creating a traffic jam in your living space.

### How to decide
- Measure the intended area twice, then add a 1 to 2 foot buffer around each side for walking space.
- Consider the rug as a foundation for your seating arrangement. The edges should align with the front legs of sofas and chairs when possible.
- For open floor plans, a larger rug can unify the space, while a smaller rug creates cozy zones within a larger room.
- Think about rug pads. They matter for stability, noise dampening, and overall feel underfoot.

If you want a hard rule: for a typical 12x14 living room, an 8x10 rug can anchor the main seating area if the sofa sits on the rug with room to spare around it. For more generous rooms or rooms with elongated furniture, a custom size lets you sculpt the footprint to your exact dimension.

## How it wears and how to care
Wool is naturally resilient, but like any beloved artifact, it benefits from a little care. The Waterfall Shadow wool rug is no exception. Routine maintenance is straightforward, and you can extend its life with a few good habits.

### Cleaning basics
- Vacuum regularly with a suction head that does not have a rotating beater. A gentle pass twice a week keeps fibers liberated from dust without causing pilling.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing can push the liquid deeper into the pile. Work from the outside in to prevent ring stains.
- For common household spills like coffee or red wine, a mild wool-friendly detergent with water can be used in small amounts. Always test a hidden corner before applying to a larger area.
- For stubborn stains, consider a professional rug cleaning service. Wool can respond well to careful cleaning, but aggressive treatments can damage the fibers.

### Longevity and performance
The gradient design helps hide everyday wear by breaking up uniform traffic patterns. The wool fibers have inherent resilience that helps retain shape over time, especially when supported by a good rug pad. A high quality wool rug will not flatten as quickly as synthetic blends, provided you rotate it periodically and avoid heavy furniture staying in one position for prolonged periods.

### Maintenance checklist
1. Vacuum weekly
2. Rotate every 3 to 6 months
3. Lift and reposition heavy furniture occasionally
4. Address spills immediately with blotting
5. Periodic professional clean if needed
- All of these steps help keep the Waterfall Shadow looking sharp for years.

### Stain solutions and avoidance
- Blot, do not rub stains.
- Use a wool friendly stain remover if needed, following the product directions.
- For oily stains like makeup or grease, sprinkle a small amount of baking soda to absorb the oil before vacuuming.
