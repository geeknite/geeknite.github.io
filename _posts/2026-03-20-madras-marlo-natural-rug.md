## Maintenance and care without turning your life into a laboratory experiment
Maintenance is where a rug earns its keep or reveals its rebellious side. The Madras Marlo Natural Rug is straightforward in cleaning. Regular vacuuming with a vacuum that has a beater bar turned off is recommended to prevent fiber frizz. A rotating schedule helps reduce wear patterns, especially in front of the couch where feet accumulate most frequently. For spills, blot up liquid immediately with a clean cloth, then treat with a mild soap solution. Never rub aggressively; you’ll end up with a frayed drama queen. If you deal with stubborn stains, you can consider a gentle upholstery cleaner or a professional cleaning service with experience in natural fiber rugs. Always test a small area before full application.

To extend life, use a rug pad. This not only prevents slipping but also creates a small air cushion beneath the rug that reduces wear and adds to comfort. The pad also protects your floor from scratches and reduces noise—great for late-night gaming marathons or stealthy pizza deliveries that somehow become family feasts.

External thought on care: different fibers may react differently to cleaning products. A quick consult with the product’s care instructions or the manufacturer’s guidelines can save you from accidental discoloration or a rug color rebellion. While we won’t quote old textbooks here, a general rule of thumb is to stay on the mild side and avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade natural fibers.

## Sizing up the fit: room types and layout magic
Size matters, but more importantly, placement matters. The 4x6 is perfect for compact living rooms or under a coffee table in smaller apartments, while 5x8 and 6x9 pull their weight in larger spaces like open-plan living areas, home studios, or gaming dens where a little extra surface area helps the cables disappear into the rug’s embrace. A common approach is to have the rug under the front legs of a sofa to anchor the seating area, or to place it in a dining zone with chairs that maintain balance, not wobbly knee-caps from moving table legs. The key is to ensure at least a portion of each piece of furniture rests on the rug to create a cohesive space rather than a jagged carpet island in the middle of a room.

Style-wise, go for a rug pad with a little extra grip if you have polished wooden floors or slippery tiles, so your paintings don’t take a tumble when you stand up to fetch a snack during a tense movie night.
