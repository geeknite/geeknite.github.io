## Comparisons: how it stacks up against alternatives
- Open frame vs closed frame: Open frame racks like this StarTech model are great for airflow and rapid access. If you prioritize security and physical protection for your gear, a closed frame or cabinet with a door might be a better match. The decision is a balance between airflow, accessibility, security, and space constraints.
- Four-post vs two-post: A four-post design adds stability for heavier equipment, particularly where the rack will be moved around or subject to vibrations. The extra posts reduce side-to-side sway when you power up a high-RPM NAS or a row of switches. If your gear is especially light and you want something ultra-portable, a two-post option could be enough, but the StarTech four-post construction makes this model feel roomier and more robust.
- Shelves included: Shelves are a practical inclusion, especially for non-rack devices or temporarily storage of components that you need handy. If you need more shelves, verify if additional shelf kits are available and at what price, so you can expand capacity without sacrificing stability.
