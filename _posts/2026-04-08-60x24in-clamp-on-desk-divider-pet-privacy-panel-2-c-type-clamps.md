## Installation and fit: how easy is this to set up?
### Quick install overview
The essence of clamp-on design is obvious: hang the panel where you want privacy and let gravity and friction hold it in place. The two C-Type clamps are the key to mounting. The general steps are:

1) Place the panel near the edge of your desk where you want the privacy to begin. 2) Open the clamps wide enough to grip the desk without crushing the surface. 3) Position the clamps on the edge and tighten until the pads press firmly against the desk top and under the lip of the panel. 4) Check for wobble. 5) If wobble exists, adjust the clamp alignment and retighten. 6) Clean the panel and admire your new private corner.

### Desk compatibility and edge cases
Clamps typically work on desks with a thickness range. If your desk edge is extremely rounded, or if you have an unusual lip depth, you may want to test the clamp bite on a small, inconspicuous area first. Some desks have a beveled edge or a lip that makes clamp alignment a little trickier. The good news is that a tiny bit of patience and a second attempt can salvage the setup without requiring you to call the desk manufacturer to ask about a warranty for your ego.

### No drilling, no damage
One of the major selling points is that there is no drilling or permanent modification. If you move offices, upgrade desks, or simply want to reposition your privacy panel for a different coworking pod, you can remove the clamps and reuse the same panel elsewhere. The lack of drilling means less risk to your desk warranty and less guilt when you decide that the panel looked better in the other corner of the room.

## Durability, maintenance, and care
### Cleaning and upkeep
PET surfaces are generally easy to clean. A quick wipe with a mild soap solution or a disinfectant wipe should remove fingerprints, coffee splashes, and the occasional paint swatch from a DIY project. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could scratch the surface. If you do notice scuffs, a soft microfiber cloth with a gentle cleaner usually does the trick without leaving ring marks or discoloration.

### Longevity and wear
A well-installed panel with proper clamps should last a long time in normal office use. The plastic may accumulate micro-scratches over time, particularly if you lean your chair against it or reposition the panel frequently. If you do notice signs of wear, consider rotating the panel to a different edge or removing it temporarily to inspect the clamps for any loosening. A tight clamp bite is essential for keeping the panel flush and unobtrusive.

### Safety considerations
Always ensure the edges are smooth and not sharp. A properly beveled edge reduces the risk of nicks to wrists, arms, or laptop lids during quick desk swirls. If you have small children around, a quick check to ensure there are no loose clamps or dangling cables is a good habit. The last thing you want is a paperweight disguised as a clamp to slide into the realm of chaos during a busy workday.
