## Size, Fit, and Room Planning
Choosing the right size is less about math and more about the vibe you want. The Cambridge Contemporary Grey Rug is available in sizes from 4x6 to 9x12, with more options likely in regional assortments. Here are quick planning tips:

- Small rooms (4x6 or 5x8): Place the rug so that the walls peek out from under the rug’s edges by about 8–12 inches on all sides. This creates the illusion of space and prevents the rug from feeling like an afterthought.
- Medium spaces (6x9 or 8x10): Position the rug under the main seating group, with the front legs of the sofa resting on the rug. The goal is to unify furniture into a coherent cluster while leaving enough floor visible to avoid a “hugged-by-carpeting” effect.
- Large living rooms (9x12 and up): Layer with a larger second rug or run the Cambridge rug under the central seating area, ensuring at least the front legs of the sofa rest on the rug for cohesion. Large rooms benefit from a hair more negative space around the edges to prevent the rug from feeling anchor-like.

If you want numbers, we recommend aiming for at least a 2–3-foot border from the rug’s edges in smaller spaces and a 1–2-foot border in larger rooms—unless you’re going for a bold, cover-the-floor-with-grey-ambition look, in which case, go big or go home.

## Comfort, Maintenance, and Practicality
### Comfort underfoot
People often equate “soft” with “fragile.” The Cambridge Contemporary Grey Rug breaks that stereotype. It offers a cushioned feeling that’s friendly to bare feet, pets, and the occasional sprint from the living room to the kitchen, all without feeling like you’re padding across a cloud that’s about to devour you. If you own a chilly floor and enjoy boot-clad walks across the living space, you’ll appreciate the mid-range density that prevents toes from turning into popsicles during winter mornings.

### Cleaning and upkeep
Maintenance is where the Cambridge rug earns extra brownie points. Vacuum regularly on a low-suction setting to lift dust without pulling at the fibers. For spills, act quickly with a blotting motion—don’t rub, as rubbing tends to spread liquids and invites the carpet-gremlin into your fibers. For stained or high-traffic patches, an in-between solution of mild detergent and water works well. Test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness; given the neutral tone, most household stains play nice, but there are always exceptions.

### Stains and pet-friendly notes
If your home features four legs with fur and a curious appetite for the unknown, you’ll appreciate the rug’s resilience. Polypropylene blends tend to resist staining and are relatively easy to spot-clean. For stubborn spots, use a gentle carpet-cleaning solution and avoid harsh bleach that can alter the grey tone over time. The rug’s construction also helps hide the occasional pet paw print, which is a feature that makes life with a furry friend a little less dramatic.

### Longevity and warranty
Colorfastness and wear tolerance are commendable, especially for a mid-range product. While warranties vary by retailer and region, your best bet is to look for coverage that addresses manufacturing defects and significant color loss within the first year. If you’re the approach-with-hope and wash-don’t-worry type, you’ll be rewarded with a rug that remains relevant in your room’s design narrative for years to come.

## Styling Tips: Making It Your Own
### Pairing with color palettes
Grey acts as a chameleon in design. It complements cool blues, deep charcoals, and even warm terracotta accents. For a contemporary/cozy hybrid look, layer a smaller textured runner on top of a larger Cambridge rug in a high-traffic area. ## Buying Guide: What to Look For During Purchase
- Fiber composition: A polypropylene backbone with a polyester or polypropylene pile offers stain resistance and softness. The Cambridge rug aims for a good balance between comfort and practicality.
- Pile height: Think about foot traffic in your space. If you’ve got kids or pets, a low-to-mid pile is safer for squishy humans and adventurous paws.
- Backing and grip: A rug pad is not optional—it's a life choice. It keeps the rug from sliding, reduces wear, and adds a little warmth to your feet.
- Colorfastness: Especially in sunny rooms, you want a rug that resists fading and maintains its cool grey tone after months of exposure to sunlight.
- Return policy: It’s rugs—things can happen during shipping or setup. A retailer with a friendly return policy can save you from a design decision you’ll regret at 2 a.m.
