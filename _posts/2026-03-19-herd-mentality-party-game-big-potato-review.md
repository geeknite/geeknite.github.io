## How to Play (Quick Start Guide)
If you’re ruthless with your time, you’re going to want a quick-start version. Here’s a concise guide to get you from “box” to “everybody argue about the same thing” without needing a PhD in social dynamics.

### Setup
- Gather 4–8 players for maximum herd energy. If you’ve got more, you can adapt by forming sub-herds or negotiating with the neighbor’s cat to join in for dramatic effect.
- Shuffle the prompt cards and place them in the middle of the table.
- Distribute a set of response cards to each player. If you’re playing with kids, choose prompts that won’t traumatize your offspring or your family’s delicate sense of humor.
- Decide who will be the first reader—the person who will shepherd the crowd into making a choice (metaphorically, not literally the person who must drink the milk first).

### Round Play
- A prompt card is revealed. Everyone secretly writes down an answer on their response cards.
- Each player places their answer face-down in a central pile. The reader then reads the prompt out loud and gathers all the submitted responses.
- The herd votes on which answer they think the majority will choose or, alternatively, which answer they personally agree with most. The exact rules can be clarified in the rulebook, but the gist is to align with the herd or stand out from it, depending on your strategy.
- Points are awarded based on how well your answer aligns with the herd’s vote. If your answer matches the majority, you’re in good standing. If you’re the lone ranger, you either get a big boost for boldness or a quiet note about “standing out” in the most awkward way possible. The rulebook explains the scoring in detail, but the fun is mostly in the laughter and the groans when the crowd proves you were dead wrong.

### End of Round and Next Round
- The role of the reader rotates to keep everyone involved. The herd remains the center of attention and the group morale hinges on how well you can ride the wave of social consensus.
- Play continues for a set number of rounds or until the group loses the remaining shred of self-control—whichever comes first.

If you want to see a more formal breakdown, the official pages and a few community rules exist, but the essence remains the same: the joy is in predicting, reacting, and bonding through the hilarity of shared opinions.

## The Humor and Social Dynamics
Herd Mentality leans into social psychology in the same way a cat leans into sunbeams: it’s irresistible, a little bit chaotic, and you can’t help but bask in the warmth of collective agreement. Here are the threads that tie this game together and the moments that make it worth dragging out for a full night of laughter.

### The Core Mechanic: Align or Flop
The central thrill is watching the herd drift toward certain answers. It’s a perfect playground for inside jokes, groupthink humor, and those bold “I’m with you, but I’m not you” moments. When someone nails the majority, the room explodes with a chorus of, “We’re so in sync!” Conversely, when a player confidently chooses a fringe answer that somehow becomes the majority, you’ll hear a surprised murmur that sounds halfway like a mic drop and halfway like a confession of gambling addiction—but in a good way.

### The Best Moments Are the Small Details
Herd Mentality thrives on the tiny misreads—the way a seemingly innocent prompt triggers a cascade of personal associations, or how the same word can mean something completely different to two players who share almost everything except the one thing that matters: their perspective. Some of the best laughs come from people misinterpreting prompts, or from the quiet moments when someone tries to explain their answer with the solemn gravity of a courtroom verdict.

### Group Size and Energy
Four players can generate a surprisingly tight game with a lot of energy, while eight players bring a festival-like vibe where the laughter lingers long after the cards are forgotten. The box is portable enough for a living room, kitchen, or a chaotic campsite where someone inevitably yells, “We’re a team, right?” and the rest of the herd mutters, “The herd is a concept that exists only in our minds.” The point is: Herd Mentality scales well with group energy, which is precisely what you want in a party game with the title that implies the presence of a crowd.

## Tips and Tricks for Hosts
- Keep the room comfortable: a tidy space, chairs in a circle, and good lighting are your best bets to keep every member actively engaged.
- Pre-game warm-up: spend a few minutes with a silly prompt example so players get into the rhythm and aren’t paralyzed by the first card.
- Mix smug with humility: everyone loves seeing a bold, confident response, but be ready to celebrate the unexpectedly perfect match or the hilarious misalignment.
- Time management matters: a quick timer helps maintain momentum. You don’t want a single prompt dragging on while the herd debates the interpretation of “cozy.”
- Set a light boundary for jokes: humor should elevate the moment, not put someone on a hot seat for a long time. A friendly, ready-to-laugh vibe is the backbone of a solid session.
- Varied skill levels: rotate the role of reader to give everyone a moment in the spotlight, including newer players who might need a bit more confidence building.

For gamers who want even more efficiency, we’ve found that using a separate timer app and a rotating host schedule makes for smoother, faster rounds. It’s a minor investment of time for a big payoff in energy and flow.

### Important Note on Accessibility
If any players have accessibility needs or require accommodations for reading prompts or writing responses, you can adapt the game with larger cards, screen readers, or alternative response methods. The core experience remains the same: you’re asking people to share ideas and to read the room’s energy. A few thoughtful tweaks can ensure that everyone has a seat at the table and a voice in the herd.
