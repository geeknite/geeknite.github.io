### Ergonomics, privacy, and acoustics: does it feel right?

Gear heads in the office furniture space love to discuss ergonomics as if it’s a superpower. The VE 5x5 triple-station is designed to be a compromise between collaboration and focus. Here’s how it breaks down in real-world terms.

- Ergonomic fundamentals: Task chairs, adjustable monitor arms, and a keyboard tray that’s within a comfortable reach radius — all pieces that matter more than the color of the glass. - Privacy vs openness: The glazed panels offer visibility, which reduces the sense of isolation common in drab cubicles, but the upper glass sections typically block direct line-of-sight to your neighbor’s face during intense focus periods. For many offices, adding fabric panels or acoustic tiles on the ceiling and walls around the cluster will help dampen noise more effectively than repainting with “quiet white.” If your space is already a symphony of ambient noise, the glass might be less forgiving; if you’ve got a quiet floor, it can be surprisingly workable.

## Where does it fit in your office ecosystem?

To help conquer the planning phase, here are a few guidance notes and best practices for integrating a VE 5x5 U-shaped glazed workstation into your office floor plan:

- Pick a lighting plan that minimizes glare. Natural light is golden, but glare on glass panels can be a real mood killer for screen-based tasks. Consider adjustable blinds or diffusive overhead lighting to even out the brightness.
- Pair with ergonomic accessories. Monitor arms, footrests, and keyboard trays can profoundly impact comfort. A thoughtful combination helps maintain posture without sacrificing speed.
- Create zones for collaboration. A 5x5 U-shaped cluster is excellent for quick stand-ups and shared screens, but you’ll still want floating zones for privacy tasks. Use the natural flow of your floor plan to separate these activities, ensuring space for people who need quiet time.
- Establish a cable-discipline rule. Cable chaos is the enemy of an elegant workspace. Use anchored grommets, cable trays, and labeled outlets to reduce trips and tangles.
