The city never sleeps, and apparently it also never stops collecting loot boxes of myth. City of Mist: The Mythos Pack is the latest expansion to the noir-tinged RPG/card-game hybrid, and if you thought you had enough runekind puns in your life, strap in—the Mythos Pack brings a fresh wave of archetypes, hashtags, and plot hooks that will make your investigators feel like walking, talking legends who just got a spiritual upgrade via a celestial UPS truck.

If you want a quick TL;DR: this pack is more myth, more chaos, more ways to pull a mythic rabbit out of your trench coat, and it mostly works. It doesn’t fix every nitpick the base game had, but it does give GMs and players excellent material to spin into hours of moody, character-driven mystery. Now, let’s dive into the mythos without stepping on a coffee cup with a voice that says, nicely but firmly, “you should reconsider your life choices.”

## Overview
City of Mist invites players to inhabit a city where legends walk among the ordinary, and where the power of a person’s story shapes reality. The Mythos Pack expands that conceit by adding new mythos, new templates for hero and antihero builds, and a fresh set of cards and story seeds that feel like a cinematic mixtape rather than a textbook. The pack leans into the game’s core concept: you are what your myth makes you, and your city has plenty of myths to borrow lines from.

The design goal here is not simply to throw more cards at the table; it’s to deepen the pool of possible mythic identities while giving GMs a toolbox for running campaigns that feel both personal and epic. In practice, Mythos Pack delivers new powers, more conditions, more locations, and a handful of “mythic moments” that can flip a scene in a single card flip. If you’ve ever thought “this city needs more legends,” the Mythos Pack is your new favorite street preacher.

As a reviewer who has suffered under many a lockbox expansion, I approached Mythos Pack with cautious optimism. The base game was clever and stylish but sometimes fragile under the weight of too many mythic options. Mythos Pack strikes a relatively healthy balance: it broadens the playground without turning the city into a mosaic of overbearing Uber-heroes. The result is a set that feels like it belongs to City of Mist’s vibe, while still leaving room for your group to carve their own absurd, heartfelt legend into the fog.

## What’s Inside The Mythos Pack
This pack contains three main pillars: new mythos cards, new power cards, and a handful of story seeds that practically beg to be adapted into your ongoing mystery arc. Here’s the breakdown:

- New mythos and factions: Think of fresh divine or occult influences that can embellish a party’s identity or complicate their public image. The mythos are designed to mesh with existing city factions, so your vigilante with a celestial sponsor won’t feel like they’ve wandered into a different game, but rather a different wing of the same theater.
- New masks and powers: Mythos Pack adds more mask archetypes—the persona that a character adopts when they walk the street. These masks come with new abilities, limitations, and interesting ways to trigger narrative moments, which is the heartbeat of City of Mist.
- Story seeds and encounters: A well-placed seed can transform a routine investigation into a mythic caper. The seeds are written with a GM’s mindset: hook, complication, payoff, and a couple of optional twists if you want to turn a one-session case into a mini-arc.
- Refined components: The pack also improves some of the creature features, location cards, and tokens, making it easier to stage morally gray interrogations or mythic reveals without shuffling through a dozen decks.

If you’re a completionist who loves a shiny new pile of cards, you’ll be happy. If you’re a GM who wants practical tools for running a more thematic session, you’ll be even happier. The Mythos Pack doesn’t just throw more bells and whistles at you; it gives you additional means of telling personal, messy, mythic stories in a city that already feels alive with myth and smoke.

## How It Changes City of Mist
The base game’s core hook is “your story is the myth that powers you, and the city punishes you accordingly.” The Mythos Pack expands this concept in three directions: breadth of mythic archetypes, depth of the narrative hooks, and a pinch more mechanical texture to push scenes toward dramatic moments.

- Mythos breadth: You’ll recognize familiar mythic inspirations—gods, ancient powers, modernist imprints—but with a fresh twist. The pack avoids re-skinning old concepts into new wrappers. Instead, it adds nuanced flavors that can coexist with the existing mythos without erasing what makes your current party unique.
- Narrative texture: Story seeds are designed to thread into ongoing campaigns. They’re not one-off adventures so much as potential story engines: seeds that sprout complications, reveal secrets, and force players to revisit how their legend intersects with the city’s underbelly.
- Mechanics and pacing: The new powers and masks introduce opportunities for dramatic reveals and narrative pivots. Some of the most satisfying moments come when a Mythos Pack card triggers a chain reaction that culminates in a pivotal scene, ideally with a cost that feels weighty rather than cosmetic.

In practice, this means your players will feel a little closer to “myth” while still grounded in noir-ish reality. It’s a tricky balance, but Mythos Pack handles it with a light, confident touch. The risk is subtle: if you go too heavy on the mythic, you can tip the game toward cinematic excess; go too light, and you miss the opportunity to push a scene into a genuine “oh wow” beat. The pack leans toward the former in the most satisfying way possible when used with care.

## Thematic Flavor and Story Seeds
One of the standout strengths of City of Mist is its thematic flexibility. Mythos Pack doubles down on that. It offers masks, motifs, and powers that feel like they belong to someone who’s both a hero and a consequence of the city’s myths. If your table enjoys moral ambiguity, you’ll savor the way this pack invites players to wrestle with the responsibilities their powers entail and the public image they’re expected to maintain.

Here are a few examples of the flavor you’ll encounter:

- The Mask as Identity: A new mask archetype explores how a hero conceals a fragile truth beneath a confident facade. The mask comes with a set of conditions that push players to decide if they want to protect the myth or the person behind it.
- Mythic Symbiosis: Some cards create a bond between the investigator and a mythic force, offering a narrative loop where the city’s myths respond to the party’s success or failure. It’s a clever engine for long-running campaigns because it creates recurring and evolving stakes.
- The Mythos Network: A web of hidden influences—fixers, occultists, vigilantism-aligned factions—serves as a playground for intrigue. It’s not just “who did it?” but “who benefits from this myth becoming widely recognized?”

If you enjoy your mysteries with a heavy atmosphere and a sprinkle of the metaphysical, Mythos Pack feeds that appetite nicely. The seeds are written with a GM’s pragmatism, focusing on what’s buyable in a single session while still being expansive enough to seed longer arcs if you want them.

## Mechanics and Card Interactions
City of Mist uses a mix of dice, cards, and narrative prompts. Mythos Pack adds more moving parts to this ecosystem. Here’s how the new content typically influences play:

- New masks provide fresh triggers for competence checks and dramatic reveals. They encourage players to think about how their legend is performed on the street, not just what it can do on paper.
- Power cards interact with the existing mechanics in a way that can speed up scene transitions. A well-timed power reveal can escalate a chase, a confrontation, or a courtroom scene with a single card flip.
- Story seeds often come with recommended scene structures. They’re not rigid adventures; they’re scaffolds that you hang your own characters and city on. Expect the GM to improvise a lot, but with stronger rails for getting to the thematic payoff.

If your playgroup enjoys fast setup and high payoff moments, you’ll appreciate the pacing options this pack offers. If you prefer a slower burn with more planning, you’ll still appreciate the seeds for the inspiration they provide, even if you end up adapting them to your own tempo.

## Artwork, Layout, and Production Value
Production quality matters in a game that aims to convey noir mood and mythic gravitas. Mythos Pack keeps City of Mist’s signature aesthetic: dark, smoky visuals with neon glints and stylized character portraits. The cards use a readable font, with enough visual flair to sustain the setting without becoming a gimmick. Card backs maintain consistency with the core game, which helps when you’re dealing with a large table and need to keep the stacks visually coherent.

The art direction deserves a nod for its balance between “mythic” and “street-level.” You’ll see heroes who look like they belong in a comic panel while still appearing approachable enough to be your neighbor or your barista—if your barista happens to whisper about cosmic resonance and the price of truth.

In addition to visuals, the physical design holds up under table abuse. The pack uses sturdy cards and reasonable packaging, which matters because you’ll likely tote this expansion around and shuffle it repeatedly. If you’re a reviewer who hates fragile minis or flimsy sleeves, you’ll be happy here—the Mythos Pack is built to survive Modeled-deck shuffles and the occasional table flip in a fit of dramatic rage when a clue finally lands.

## Narrative Playability and Balance
Balance in City of Mist is less about “who’s stronger” and more about “who has the right leverage at the right moment.” Mythos Pack does a commendable job of expanding options without letting any single build steal the show. There are a few carry-through ideas you’ll want to consider:

- Avoiding power creep: The new masks and powers are designed to complement existing options. They create interesting asymmetries rather than simply boosting every character’s power ceiling.
- Encouraging party dynamics: Several seeds explicitly leverage inter-character relationships. This is City of Mist’s strength—the way personal legends intersect with the city’s mythic undercurrent. These moments require the GM to lean into character backstories, which rewards players who have put in the narrative work.
- Scaling for campaigns: The seeds are resilient enough to seed short sessions or grow into arcs with some GM improvisation. If you’re planning a longer arc, you’ll find hooks that can weave into ongoing storylines without feeling out of place.

The bottom line is that Mythos Pack respects the soft rules that make City of Mist sing: constraints create creativity. The new material gives you crisp prompts and a structured way to escalate tension, but it never imposes a forced beat or a melodramatic twist that would break the mood of a serious noir mystery.

## GM Tips for Running The Mythos Pack
If you’re stepping into the city with Mythos Pack as your companion, here are practical tips that should help you maximize the experience:

- Start with one seed per session: Pick a seed that speaks to a current character arc and use it as the anchor for your session. The rest can fill in as optional flavor or complications.
- Build around the city’s mythic economy: The Mythos Pack’s new factions and powers tend to shine when you connect them to the city’s power dynamics. Think about who benefits from a given myth becoming public and who’s losing control as a result.
- Use moral tension as a lever: City of Mist thrives when players wrestle with what their legend requires of them. If a player’s mask demands a difficult choice, lean into it; it’s where the game earns its noir flavor.
- Keep the stakes personal: While grand, mythic stakes are satisfying, the most memorable moments come from how the myth affects the investigators’ lives—relationships, reputations, and the moral price of being a legend.
- Have a few “mythic moment” prompts ready: You’ll want a handful of dramatic triggers for when a seed pays off. A reveal, a betrayal, a public spectacle, or a choice that flips the investigators’ public image are reliable options.

## Value and Production Value: Is It Worth It?
From a strictly monetary perspective, Mythos Pack sits in that “more content for more stories” sweet spot. You’re paying for a handful of cards, a few new masks, and some story seeds that can catalyze sessions for months if you use them well. If you’re a GM who already runs City of Mist with any regularity, the incremental value is solid.

The content is thematically rich enough to feel substantial but not so overbearing that new players feel overwhelmed. It’s approachable for a mid-range veteran and accessible for slightly newer groups who have acclimated themselves to the vibe. The design encourages you to dip in and out of the pack without needing to commit to a full campaign arc, which is crucial for a game that thrives on mood and character nuance.

From a collectability standpoint, the Mythos Pack is one of those expansions that adds personality as much as it adds mechanics. If you love the idea of your investigators wearing a myth like a coat or a scythe, you’ll want these new pieces on the table. If you’re indifferent to stylistic upgrades, you may still find the seeds useful enough to justify a purchase for the sessions that follow.

## The Final Verdict
City of Mist: The Mythos Pack is a confident, stylish expansion that respects the core game’s strengths while offering a curated set of new mythic options. It’s not a revolution, but it’s a valuable evolution—like discovering a new department in a city you thought you already understood. It gives GMs more tools to craft personal legends, and it gives players more things to intrigue about their own mythic identities. If you love the vibe, you’ll love what Mythos Pack brings to your table.

Pros:
- Fresh mythos that feel integrated, not tacked on.
- Strong seeds and flexible triggers for dramatic sessions.
- Good balance between new options and existing mechanics.
- High production quality that fits the mood and aesthetic.

Cons:
- Some players may crave more mechanical power; this pack leans narrative rather than min-max optimization.
- A few seeds assume a GM is willing to improvise, which may require some player buy-in for optimal effect.

Overall, Mythos Pack is a recommended pickup for groups who want more personal mythic moments and a richer city noir atmosphere. It’s not essential for every table, but if you’re cooking a story about legends wearing human skin, you’ll be reaching for this box again and again.
