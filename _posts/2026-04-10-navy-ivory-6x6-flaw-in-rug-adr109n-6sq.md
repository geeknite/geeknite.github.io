## Cleaning, Care, and Real-World Maintenance

This rug is forgiving. Its fiber blend dries relatively quickly after spills, and you can use a mild cleaning solution for the occasional stain. It is not a miracle cure for every mess, but it makes most everyday incidents manageable without requiring a PhD in rug chemistry. Pro tip: treat spills promptly, blot rather than rub, and keep a spare cleaning cloth handy in the living room for those moments when a dramatic mug drop occurs during a favorite show.

Vacuuming frequency depends on foot traffic. In a typical living room, once or twice a week should keep the surface looking fresh without causing fiber fatigue. Do not use aggressive brushing tools on the pile, especially near the ivory flecks, as you want to preserve that crisp contrast rather than turning the rug into a single blue blur. If you plan to move the rug between rooms or to a different floor, vacuum both sides before you relocate; a dry, fluffy rug is easier to reposition than a damp one that wants to stick to the floor like a stubborn boss fight.
