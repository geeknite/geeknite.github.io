## Overview: What Fortune Row is trying to do
Fortune Row isn’t just a collection of encounter seeds and stat blocks. It’s a blueprint for turning a city block into a living, breathing character with its own secrets, allegiances, and rules about who can own what corner of the night. The toolkit provides templates for scenes, NPCs with agendas, and a set of GM aids that help you run sessions without doing the same old improv every time. It leans into the core City of Mist concept that legends live in the everyday—barbershops, street corners, and the little coffee shops where the truth might be served with a shot of espresso and a side of danger.

The vibe of Fortune Row is unapologetically glamorous and dangerous, with a color palette that leans toward midnight blues, gold accents, and the occasional chrome glow. It is the kind of setting where a detective might flirt with a mythic figure and the mythic figure responds with a bargain that involves the city’s future and a certain character’s past. It’s not a toolkit for “easy mode” campaigns; it’s a toolkit for campaigns that want to feel cinematic, stylish, and ever so slightly chaotic in the best possible ways.

If you’re new to the City of Mist approach, think mythic archetypes in the real world: mortals who carry legends in their veins, and legends who are, in turn, hiding behind ordinary masks. Fortune Row doubles down on this by giving you a microcosm a GM can lean on—the Fortune Row district—where every shopfront tells a story, every alley is a rumor, and every NPC holds a secret that could change the city if told at the right moment.

## Inside the Toolkit: What you actually get
Fortune Row ships with a curated set of components designed to save a GM time and increase table fun. Here’s what you can expect, with some quick commentary on how it translates into real play.

### NPC cards and faction sheets
The toolkit includes printable NPC cards and faction sheets that help you manage loyalties, schemes, and relationships without constant flipping through a rulebook. The cards are clean, legible, and an absolute dream for players who want to skim a page or two and know exactly who is allied with whom and why someone cares about a given corner of Fortune Row.

### Scene seeds and adventure hooks
There is no shortage of hooks here. The seeds are designed to scale—perfect for a one-shot night, a two-session arc, or an ongoing city-wide mystery. They’re not rigid scripts; they’re prompts that encourage improvisation while keeping momentum. The seeds also include cross-links to mythos and logos-driven dilemmas so you can weave ideas into the larger city canvas rather than dropping in isolated episodes.

### GM screen inserts and cheat sheets
A small but mighty addition, the GM screen inserts summarize rules interactions you’ll actually need during a session. They’re a godsend for players who want to ask 17 questions before any roll happens and for GMs who don’t want to interrupt the mood by flipping to the rulebook mid-scene.

### Map tokens and encounter mats
The toolkit includes printable maps and token shapes that help you visualize scenes without spending an hour drawing forced perspectives. The tokens are visually distinct, with color-coded factions and mythic cues that make it easy to understand who’s in play and what their priorities are. If you’re a digital GM, the printable assets also translate well into virtual table setups.

### A reference guide to the Fortune Row district
There’s a lore primer that acts as a reference for the district’s history, major actors, and current hotspots. It’s the sort of thing you want open on a second monitor while your players wander, detective-style, through the streets.

### A short bestiary of mythic forces
You’ll find a handful of archetypal mythic levers—agents of power, hidden patrons, and legendary echoes—that you can drop into scenes without needing to create everything from scratch. These are pitched as adaptable templates rather than rigid stat blocks, which is exactly how City of Mist should feel: flexible, cinematic, and a little dangerous to lean on.

### Artwork and presentation
Fortune Row is a visual treat. The art direction emphasizes mood and atmosphere, with sharply colored neon signs, rain-slicked sidewalks, and fashion-forward legends. It’s not just eye candy; the art makes you, the GM, feel the world you’re trying to evoke. The layout is clean enough that you can access the relevant piece of information in seconds, which is essential during a tense scene where the players are driving toward a cross street you didn’t anticipate.

## How to use Fortune Row at your table
If you want to maximize the toolkit’s value, you’ll want to treat it as a modular layer on top of your existing City of Mist campaign. Here are practical approaches that work well in practice:

### Quick-start sessions
Choose one or two scene seeds and map a handful of districts within Fortune Row. Run a single evening where players uncover a mystery, cross paths with three NPCs, and encounter a mythic force that threatens the district’s delicate balance. The toolkit’s seeds are designed for that crisp, cinematic sense of payoff you get in a well-timed montage scene.

### Long arcs with a daily rhythm
If you’re running a longer arc, Fortune Row helps you maintain momentum by giving you recurring factions and a few red-thread plot lines that connect across sessions. You can drop in a new seed when a player reaches a breaking point, or you can have a local mythic force reappear with a different bargain to offer—the city loves a good repeat performance.

### NPC-driven sessions
One of the toolkit’s strengths is how well it supports NPC-centric play. The NPC cards are easy to skim, and the faction sheets help you track shifting loyalties as the players push back against a corrupt system or decide to form a temporary alliance with a more powerful mythic agent. If you enjoy social intrigue, Fortune Row rewards careful listening, calculated bribes, and the occasional last-second bluff.

### Action and investigation balance
The toolkit encourages a balance between action-packed moments and investigative beats. You’ll find scene seeds that mix chase sequences with clues, interrogations with leverage, and barroom brawls that end with a surprising bargain rather than a knockout blow. If you’re worried about fights bogging down the pace, the GM inserts and cheat sheets are designed to keep things moving without sacrificing flavor.

## Design, usability, and accessibility
Fortune Row stands out for its thoughtful design choices. The typography is legible, the color scheme communicates mood without interfering with readability, and the modular structure means you can chunk a session into manageable units without losing track of continuity. The printable assets and the digital-friendly layout are a boon for both in-person tables and virtual tabletops. It’s easy to adapt the content for shorter sessions or for campaigns that aim to be a little more theatrical and a little less grimdark.

Some potential caveats worth mentioning:
- The vibe is thick and stylish, which can feel intimidating to new players. If you’re running a table with newcomers, consider a toned-down intro arc that lets people get comfortable with the rules and with the mythic logic of the world before diving into Fortune Row’s more flamboyant elements.
- The district lore can be dense if you treat it as a single monolith. It pays off to stage the reveal in layers, giving players the sense that Fortune Row is alive and full of secrets worth chasing.
- If you’re using digital maps, you may want to supplement with your own overlays. The stock assets are solid, but you’ll likely want to customize tokens and terrain to match your table’s pacing and your players’ expectations.
