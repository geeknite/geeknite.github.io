## Weather, Maintenance, and Practical Care: A Real-World Running Commentary
### Weather resistance
The set is designed to handle sun exposure and occasional rain, with wicker that’s weather-resistant and cushions that are, ideally, water-repellent. If you live in a climate with heavy downpours, you’ll want to invest in cushion covers or store the cushions during storms. If you forget to bring them in, don’t panic: a day or two of sun can help dry the cushions, though you risk color fading if you neglect proper shade protection. The real hero here is the frame: metal construction provides a stable base even on slightly uneven ground, and the weave adds a level of all-weather resilience that’s pleasant to the eye and practical.

### Cleaning and upkeep
Removal of cushion covers for washing is straightforward in most models. Spot-cleaning the wicker with mild soap and water is the recommended method. ## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity: Keeping It Fresh Like a New Episode
### Routine care
- Regular wiping with a damp cloth keeps the wicker from looking dull and dusty.
- Removable cushion covers should be washed according to the label—usually cold water and gentle cycles.
- Protect from prolonged direct sun with an umbrella or shade sail to preserve color integrity.
- Store cushions during heavy rain or winter to maximize cushion life.

### Common issues and quick fixes
- Cushion sagging: Rotate cushions and consider a light foam refresh if needed.
- Faded weave: It’s cosmetic; a sun hat over the set isn’t a fashion statement so much as a longevity strategy.
- Loose hardware: Periodically check bolts and hardware and tighten as needed; a tiny bit of torque can save you from a wobble that becomes a Friday night comedy routine.
