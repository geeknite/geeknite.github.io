## Rules and Gameplay: How the Huntsmen Change the Field
### Core Changes, Big Flavor
The Huntsmen Box doesn’t rewrite Black Plague’s fundamentals; it expands them. The new cards introduce events and effects that emphasize space control, line-of-sight for ranged threats, and the occasional “the woods bite back” trap that punishes poorly chosen moves. Mortimer’s rules interplay adds a nice layer: perhaps his special action allows you to pull a survivor out of harm’s way with a risk-and-reward mechanic reminiscent of a sprint to safety that leaves your group sweating with the dice in their hands.

### Turn-by-Turn Feel
In practice, you’ll find battles with Huntsmen feel like a balance of chess and quasi-archery. You’ll want to position units to maximize Mortimer’s capabilities, protect flanks, and occasionally lure the Hunters into traps that your board layout cunningly suggests. The net effect is a campaign that rewards smarter planning rather than pure brute force. It’s also worth noting that the Huntsmen’s ranged ability creates tension for groups that like to cluster around a single survivor—suddenly, spread out and let Mortimer shine becomes a very real tactical choice.

### Difficulty Curve and Session Tempo
If you’re playing with a standard Black Plague setup (4–6 survivors, a handful of starting zombies), the Huntsmen box stretches the pacing in an engaging way without tipping into “unfun difficulty spike.” It’s not an automatic win button; Mortimer doesn’t conjure miracles every turn. Instead, he provides a structured way to swing momentum when you’re slightly behind, giving players a path to resilience and continuation rather than doom-spiraling failure.

### Strategy for Victory: Tips and Tricks
- Map control is king: with Huntsmen in the mix, encouraging early map control is more valuable than ever. Use Mortimer to position survivors in safe zones while the Huntsmen create pressure elsewhere. 
- Watch your lines: with ranged threats in play, you’ll want to avoid over-reliance on a single choke point. Open lines give your party a chance to maneuver without getting roasted by archers.
- Timing matters: Mortimer’s best moments come when you can sync actions across multiple survivors. Don’t waste his turns; treat them like a resource that can swing a critical round.
- Don’t overcommit to arrow-dodging: sometimes the best defense is a strong offense—using Mortimer for a quick, precise strike to break the enemy’s rhythm can pay off more than a defensive stall.
