### Grading considerations
When you send SV4a to be graded, you’re paying for a moment of validation and a stamp that says, yes, this card has been handled by humans with adequate humidity control. Grading factors for a shiny Gloom include surface condition, centering, edges, corners, and the presence of any printing defects or scuff marks. The holo surface can show whitening along the edges if the card has been stored roughly. The best practice: store in sleeves, top loaders, or graded slabs if you’re serious about display and resale. If you’re populating a casual binder and you’re not chasing a pristine 10, you can still smile at a strong 8 or 9 and enjoy the aesthetics without the anxiety ambulance chasing.

### Authenticity checkpoints
Authenticity is the ultimate boogeyman for shiny print runs. Check for consistent foil pattern across the card, legible font, correct set symbols, alignment of text, and a clean back with standard Pokémon card design cues. For Japanese prints, you’ll notice the text is often fully in Japanese on the front while sector-specific information on the back remains consistent with the rest of the range. If you suspect a fake, a quick cross-check with a trusted database and a close look at the holo foil’s uniformity can save you from a regretful purchase.

## Authenticity and Care
### How to store and protect your shiny treasure
- Use high-quality sleeves and thick outer toploaders. Avoid overstuffing the binder; let the card breathe—jk, it doesn’t breathe, but it deserves space.
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV light is not your friend; it ages holo foils like a bad morning coffee ages a runway model.
- Consider graded slabs for display if you’re building a gallery of shinies. The glass shelf deserves a star turn and your Gloom wants to shine without your fingerprints marking the foil.
