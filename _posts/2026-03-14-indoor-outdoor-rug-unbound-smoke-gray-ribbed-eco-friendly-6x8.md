### Practical placement rules
- Place the rug on a flat, dry surface that is swept clean to prevent dust from kicking up underfoot.
- Use a non slip rug pad to prevent curling at the edges in high traffic areas. A pad is the difference between a rug that stays flat and a rug that behaves like a lazy cat.
- For outdoor use, ensure the rug is in a covered area or a protected corner to minimize direct sun fading; even eco friendly fibers can fade if left in sun for months on end.
- Pair with a water resistant furniture set; this was a wise choice in our latest patio setup and reduces the chance of moisture pooling on the rug.

## Maintenance and care tips
Maintaining an indoor outdoor rug is not about setting a high bar; it is about not letting it become a science project in the corner of your living space. The Unbound rug cleans up with ease: a quick vacuum to lift loose debris, a wipe down with a mild soapy solution for spills, and a thorough air dry for any damp spots. The ribbed texture helps trap debris which can be a good thing because you can vacuum more effectively. Some users report that the rug dries fairly quickly after washing, which is essential for outdoor use where rain may leave a damp residue that invites mold if not addressed.

### Cleaning schedule and best practices
- Regular vacuuming to remove dust and pet hair; a soft bristle attachment works well without catching fibers.
- Treat spills immediately with a damp cloth, then blot rather than rub; rubbing can push moisture deeper into the fibers and you may end up with a weird patch that looks intentionally distressed.
- For deeper cleaning, a light rinse with water and mild detergent, followed by air drying; avoid high heat drying as it can cause shrinkage or warping over time.
- Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the fiber, especially if the rug is used outdoors where sunlight and moisture can accelerate deterioration.
