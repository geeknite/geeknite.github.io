### Character creation in Fortune Row: steps and tips
Creating a fortune-teller, risk-taker, or glam-lord in Fortune Row follows the City of Mist framework, but with a few crisp refinements that fit the district’s vibe. Here’s a practical walkthrough you can adapt for a one-shot or a longer arc:

- Step 1: Pick your Fate and your Mythos. Fortune Row leans into a handful of district-specific myths—think local legends, hood ornaments of fortune tellers, and mythic archetypes tied to late-night cabarets. The guide helps you map these to the classic City of Mist schemes so your concept remains legible to other players.
- Step 2: Define your Street-level role. The Fortune Row guide nudges you to connect your character to the neighborhood’s power centers: a cabaret line, a late-night arcade, or a pawnshop with a vault where every debt is marked. This step aligns your background with your present influence, making your choices feel consequential rather than cosmetic.
- Step 3: Choose your Power tags and Glamour. The product introduces new tags that trigger under different conditions, letting you leverage luck to shift the odds. The Glamour track is especially satisfying in scenes where fashion, music, or nightlife mood-setters influence outcomes. It also encourages players to narrate their glamour moments in a way that feels cinematic rather than just a roll result.
- Step 4: Generate a few micro-story seeds. Fortune Row supplies short prompts that can be dropped into a session or used as a pre-session “warm-up.” These seeds help players anchor their characters to the locale and start improvising dialogue with the city itself—an essential tool for games that emphasize mood and myth over raw math.
- Step 5: Build your starting gambit. The starting gambit is where you declare your first dramatic move in Fortune Row, which often sets up a bid between the player and the GM. Expect a mix of stylish banter, clever misdirection, and a reveal that reframes the scene. If you’re new to City of Mist, this is a perfect primer for how the game handles negotiation, risk, and consequence.

As you see, the Fortune Row guide is not a rigid recipe; it’s a design toolkit. The intent is to empower players to make strong, story-forward choices without forcing them into a single “right” path. The result is a campaign mood that feels both cinematic and intimate—exactly the kind of vibe you want when you’re chasing a clue through a neon-lit alleyway or arguing with a fortune teller about a debt you never paid.
