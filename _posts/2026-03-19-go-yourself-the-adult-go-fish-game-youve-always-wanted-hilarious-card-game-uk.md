## How to play: a quick guide
### Setup and the onboarding experience
Deal five cards to each player and place the draw pile in the center. Each player starts with a handful of fish tokens, which come into play as you draw and complete sets. The first few rounds are a gentle onboarding process where players learn how the dares are triggered and how power cards can alter the flow of play. The pacing is friendly; you want to avoid bogging down the table with too many rules at once. The goal is to have fun and keep the energy high; complexity can creep in if you insist on a no-mistakes approach to each hand.

### Turn structure and flow
On your turn you ask another player for a card rank. If they have it, they hand it over and you continue your turn. If not, you draw from the pile and your turn ends. When you collect four of a kind, you lay them out and draw a challenge card from the challenge deck. Challenges vary from cheeky prompts to short actions that can influence the rest of the round. The challenges are the heart of the game, injecting unpredictability and humor into the standard run of Go Fish. The round ends when someone reaches the target score or after a predetermined number of rounds, depending on how long you want the session to last.
