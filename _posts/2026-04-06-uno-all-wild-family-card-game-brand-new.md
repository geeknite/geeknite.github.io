## How to Play: Quick Rules Overview

Here is a concise walkthrough to get you started without pulling out the official rules every time you forget a detail:

- Each player starts with seven cards. The rest becomes the draw pile.
- The top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile.
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either color or number of the top discard. If you cannot play, you draw one card and pass.
- All Wild cards allow you to change the active color. These are your tactical shots in the chaos arena.
- The action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, etc.) behave as in classic UNO, but the All Wilds can inject an extra layer of decision-making.
- When you have one card left, you must say Uno. If you forget, the penalty is often a light-hearted nudge from other players and perhaps a short laugh-at-yourself moment.
- The game ends when a player runs out of cards. Scorekeeping can be simple (who got rid of their hand first) or more elaborate with point values for cards left in opponents’ hands.

The rules remain accessible, but the All Wild mechanics make everything feel a touch more cinematic. It is the kind of game where a casually heated debate about color choices is halfway to a family tradition.
