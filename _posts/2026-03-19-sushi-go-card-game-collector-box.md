## Gameplay: Quick Refresher and Box-Specific Considerations
### How Sushi Go! Works (a quick refresher)
In case you’re new to the concept, Sushi Go! is a card-drafting game where players pass cards around the table in a circle. Each draft, you select a card, place it into your scoring area, and pass the rest on. The goal is to assemble the best combination of dishes over three rounds (or more, depending on variant rules). Each dish type scores differently: maki totals, nigiri cards, sashimi combos, dumplings, tempura, and desserts all contribute different points, and some cards interact in surprising (and sometimes hilariously punny) ways.

The core challenge is predicting other players’ picks; you want to balance short-term gains (a low-variance, immediate score) with long-term strategy (which cards will pay off in the end). In practice, the game feels brisk, with turns that advertise “fast and fun” rather than heavyweight strategy. The Collector’s Box does not change the core gameplay; it’s purely about how you manage your deck and enjoy the physical packaging while you play.

### Does the Collector’s Box Change Gameplay?
Short answer: not really. The Collector’s Box is a packaging and organizational upgrade. It does not alter the mechanics, scoring rules, or card interactions. It’s designed for a smoother setup, quicker rule reference, and a satisfying sensory experience as you handle premium cards. If you’re hoping the box adds new strategies or more players, you’ll be disappointed. If you’re here for improved tactile feel and better storage, you’ll probably smile as you remove the lid and hear that satisfying thunk of a well-constructed insert.
