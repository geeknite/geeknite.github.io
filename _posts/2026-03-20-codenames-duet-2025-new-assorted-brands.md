## How to Play: A Quick Refresher with a Twist

If you already know the standard Codenames Duet rules, you’ll recognize the core rhythm: two players coordinate to reveal a set of target cards on a shared grid, guided by one player’s clue-giving and the other’s guessing. In New Assorted Brands, the procedure remains similar but with brand-flavored flavors that affect your cognitive snack choices:

1. The clue-giver provides a one-word clue followed by a number, aiming to point out multiple brand cards on the grid. Example: “Tech 2” might point to two brand cards associated with technology.
2. The guesser selects cards from the grid, trying to hit all the target brand cards without hitting any of the “dead” or “opponent” brands. You fail the round if you hit the wrong cards, and you get to try again with a new clue once the round resets.
3. Brand Family Tokens can be spent to unlock small bonuses that help connect obscure cards to the clue. These tokens are finite, so you must decide when a well-timed brand token is worth the cost.
4. The game ends when you’ve connected all target cards or when the clock (or a set number of rounds) runs out.

The learning curve remains gentle. The first few games are about pattern recognition and shared memory with your partner. You’ll quickly pick up how your partner tends to clue “tech” differently than you do, and you’ll learn to adapt your approach to maximize the synergy tokens without becoming unhinged by a barrage of near-misses.

A few practical tips as you begin:
- Start with a mild difficulty level. The base game plus a couple of tokens is enough to teach the mechanics without forcing a marathon session.
- Communicate your thinking. The beauty of a two-player co-op is the mental dance between clueing and guessing. Don’t be afraid to explain your mental leaps, even if it reveals your private thought process.
- Don’t over-clue. One well-placed clue is often better than three half-baked ones. The tokens exist to compensate for creative leaps, not to replace thoughtful clueing.

In addition to the core play, there are optional modes that extend the life of the game. The solo mode can be surprisingly challenging, turning your brain into a one-two punch of guessing and self-critique. Tandem mode adds a third player for limited guidance, which can be a recipe for hilarious chaos—great for social situations but not ideal when you’re aiming for a quiet night of brain bonding.


## Brand Mechanics: Why Brand Tokens Matter

The standout mechanic in New Assorted Brands is the brand synergy token system. These tokens act as a bridge between the one-word clues and the longer, more nuanced relationships among the brand cards. Here’s how they affect your strategy:

- Token Economy: Tokens are not unlimited. You’ll need to decide when a token can stretch a single clue beyond the standard two-card payoff, or when it’s worth spending a token to reveal a colleague brand’s stronger connection. Knowing when to hold tokens and when to spend them is the difference between victory and a near-miss. The balance feels deliberate and satisfying, a nod to good game design rather than a gimmick.
- Brand Families: Cards are grouped into families that share a common theme. When you clue a family, you’re hinting at multiple cards at once, which makes tokens exceptionally valuable if your partner can confirm those brand family connections with confident guesses.
- Risk vs Reward: As with any cooperative puzzle, there’s a risk you’ll misinterpret a clue and stumble into a “dead card” trap. Tokens don’t make this risk disappear; they simply tilt the odds toward success if you’re precise in your connections.

If you’re a fan of deduction games that reward careful thinking and collaborative planning, this brand-centric twist should feel like a refreshing calibration—not a radical detour from what you already enjoy in Duet.


## Strategy and Tips for Duo Domination

Here are a few mental notes and practical tactics to help you maximize your wins in New Assorted Brands:

- Build a map, not a list: Treat each brand family as a mini-map. The goal is to connect multiple cards with one clue rather than chasing one perfect card at a time.
- Talk through your thought process: In two-player co-ops, transparency is your friend. A quick, shared explanation of why a certain brand feels like a match helps your partner fit the puzzle pieces together faster.
- Use tokens at pivotal moments: Save tokens for rounds where you’re stuck on a tough cluster or when you’re attempting to chain multiple cards with a single clever clue. Don’t blow them all on a single high-variance clue.
- Calibrate your risk: If you’re just one card away from victory, a slightly riskier brand clue may be worth it. If you’re far from the target, a safe, obvious clue is often the smarter path.
- Learn from misses: When you spark a cascade of wrong guesses, analyze which brand associations were misread. The board will reward your adaptation in the next round.
