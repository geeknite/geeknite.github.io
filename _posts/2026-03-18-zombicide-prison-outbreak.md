## How to Play (The High-Level Salsa)
Zombicide remains a cooperative engine built on a simple premise: survivors move through a grid, fight zombies with a mix of improvised weapons and dice, and manage limited resources to stay alive. Prison Outbreak sticks to that core but adds a prison-specific spice rack: lockdowns, sightline management through narrow corridors, and a map that can morph into a strategic fortress rather than a mere stage for fights.

The flow is familiar but sharpened: players take actions in sequence, then event cards push the narrative forward and spawn zombies toward the loudest target. Actions are precious resources you’ll weigh carefully—move, attack, search, barricade, and those glorious “wild” actions that let you pull off something beyond the obvious. The zombie AI remains relentlessly consistent: chase the loudest noise, break down doors with zeal, and spawn reinforcements in ways that feel crafted for your group’s skill level rather than random chance alone.

### Setup and first impressions
Setup is a careful ritual and not a sprint. You lay out the prison map, divide spawn piles, shuffle encounter and objective decks, and assign survivors. The modular board is a puzzle in itself, inviting you to brainstorm the night as you test the layout for chokepoints, line-of-sight options, and where to place alarms that might doom your escape plan or save your bacon at the last moment. The tone is clear: this is a facility where escape is not the primary objective; survival and puzzle-solving are the main event.

What’s new in this revisit is recognition of how the lockdowns alter the sense of urgency. You’re not just holding rooms; you’re governing an entire block. A corridor can swing from safety to danger with a single door toggle, and that means the clock is always ticking in the background as you weigh risk and reward.

### Turn structure in a nut shell
Each round, people act in order, then you flip an event card that nudges the narrative forward while continuing to pull the horde toward the most exciting target. Actions are limited but potent: move to location, attack, search for tools, barricade to slow the undead, and those wild actions that let you attempt something clever or dangerously fun. The zombie AI still chases the loudest target, but Prison Outbreak adds predictable storytelling: a hallway becomes an evolving trap as doors seal, alarms blare, and the map shifts from open arena to tactical labyrinth.

### The new wrinkles in Prison Outbreak
The prison setting injects a cascade of constraints that can trigger heroic improvisation or panic-induced blunders. Lockdown mechanics force you to rethink pathways—doors may seal, alarms may trigger, and the map breathes as a living puzzle rather than a static stage. The corridors, cell blocks, and yard provide distinct tactical spaces with different lines of sight and cover challenges. Mission cards lean toward heist-theater rather than dungeon crawl, nudging players toward deliberate planning with purple-hued moments of cinematic gambles.

If you relish plotting a route through a prison while the zombie choir inches toward your flank, you’ll feel the thematic kick here. ### Strategy tips and best practices
- Communication is king. In a cramped prison setting, a poorly timed zombie reveal can ruin a run faster than a broken flashlight. Use concise callouts and avoid becoming a chorus of panic.
- Quick barricades are powerful, but don’t overcommit to defense at the expense of objective progress. Balance defense with exploration to keep rewards flowing.
- Prioritize survivors with mobility and access to strong weapons early. You want speed and positioning to prevent the horde from reaching a critical mass before you set up your plan.
- Don’t neglect morale. A well-timed joke about a zombie taking a literacy exam can keep the team from mutiny when the board looks like a mood lighting test gone wrong.
- Learn spawn patterns. The better you understand how the horde grows, the more you can anticipate bottlenecks and identify doors that can be sealed with minimal effort.
- Use the yard’s open sightlines, but beware the temptation of wide-open spaces that zombies love to flood. Funnel them into chokepoints, then unleash dramatic combos when you can.
- Mix a non-combat survivor into the roster. A character focused on search and resources can open critical tools without becoming a liability when the horde advances.
- Plan your ladder climbs and locksmith moments. Sometimes the best move is not to fight your way out but to outsmart the entry point with a clever distraction.
