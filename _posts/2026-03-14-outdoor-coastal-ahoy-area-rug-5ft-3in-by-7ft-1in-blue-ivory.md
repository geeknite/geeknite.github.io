## Cleaning and Maintenance
### Routine care
Outdoor rugs need a light touch, not a heavy metal concert. For regular upkeep, do a quick shake or gentle vacuum to remove loose dirt. A quick weekly sweep is enough to keep the surface looking presentable and ready for a podcast interview with your neighbor about new porch furniture.

### Deep cleaning
When the stain parade shows up (and yes, the parade will eventually arrive with sunscreen and spilled drinks), a mild detergent mixed with water will usually do the trick. Use a soft brush to lift dirt, then rinse with a hose and allow it to air dry completely. Avoid high-pressure washers that might bend fibers or dislodge the weave. In a pinch, you can spot-clean with a damp cloth for minor spills and let nature do the drying work.

### Stain resistance and upkeep
The fiber is designed to resist staining from typical outdoor use, but nothing is truly stain-proof. If you have a red wine incident during a sunset soiree, act fast and blot—don’t rub—to minimize set-in stains. Regular cleaning and sun exposure will help keep colors vibrant, but don’t stress if a little patina develops over time; that is just the rug aging like a fine, salt-kissed bottle of rum.
