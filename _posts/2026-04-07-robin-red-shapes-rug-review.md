## Cleaning and Maintenance Deep Dive
### Routine upkeep
Vacuum regularly to prevent soil deposition from turning into a grimy tunnel of colorless drama. Blot spills quickly and avoid rubbing, which can spread stains and lift fibers. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent mixed with water should do the trick; test on a small inconspicuous area first. If you have pets, you might encounter occasional pet hair buildup that’s easy to fetch with a quick roll of the vacuum rather than a full on hair-attack session.

### Stain scenarios and strategies
- Juice spill: blot, then a gentle cleanse with mild soap; avoid alcohol-based cleaners that could leach dye. 
- Mud from a rainy day: dry brush to remove loose soil, then wipe with damp cloth and let dry.
- Ink or dye transfer: act fast, dab with a salon-approved cleaner or seek professional help if the stain resists.

### Long-term care tips
Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color distribution, particularly if your space has a consistent sun exposure pattern. Direct sunlight can fade pigment over time, so consider window treatments or rotating the rug to a less sunlit zone to extend its vibrancy.
