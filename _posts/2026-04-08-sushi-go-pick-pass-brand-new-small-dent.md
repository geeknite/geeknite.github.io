## What is Sushi Go?
Sushi Go is a delightful, quick-paced card-drafting game where players assemble the best combination of sushi dishes to earn points. The core mechanic is famously simple: pick one card from your hand, then pass the rest to the next player. Rinse and repeat until the round ends. Score cards at the end of each round to accumulate points across four rounds. The Box of Fun includes a dozen characters with as much personality as a cartoon food truck and a compact playing surface that fits neatly on any table. The game is designed for 2–5 players and plays in about 15 minutes, making it an ideal filler between heavier games or a late-night brunch session with grandma who still insists on dessert first.

## How to play: a step-by-step guide
### Setup
- Shuffle all sushi cards and deal 4 cards to each player.
- The remaining cards form a draw pile in the center.
- Each player looks at their hand privately and decides which card to keep.

### Turn sequence
- Simultaneously, reveal the chosen card from each player.
- All players pass the remaining 3 cards to the left.
- In the next round, players choose again and pass, repeating until all cards are played.

### Scoring end of rounds
- After each round, tally the points for each player according to the card types: nigiri, maki, sashimi, tempura, and dumplings. Wasabi doubles the next nigiri’s points, if timed correctly.
- Wasabi cards do not score themselves; their power lies in the next nigiri you claim.

### End of game
- After four rounds, add up all points from each round. The player with the highest total wins.

### Strategies for different player counts
- Two players: The passing becomes twice as important because each pass carries more weight. You’ll need to anticipate more precisely and block your opponent more aggressively to gain the edge.
- Three players: The pace is lively but not chaotic. You’ll often see a mix of safe picks and strategic blocking to prevent another player from completing a sashimi or maki set.
- Four or five players: The game stays breezy because more cards are involved in each round. Scouting the board and forcing suboptimal choices on opponents becomes a key skill.

### A closer look at the cards
- Nigiri: The backbone of point-chasing. If you’re lucky enough to snag multiple nigiri types in a round, you can rack up a surprising number of points. Wasabi helps if you can string it with a high-value nigiri.
- Maki: The race to the most Maki cards is a simple mechanism but a strong source of bragging rights. If you’re behind, you can try to grab Maki cards to catch up in the final scoring.
- Tempura and Sashimi: The two-card combos require some planning. Missing a key card might doom your chase for a big round score, but you can still salvage points with other cards.
- Dumplings: The more you collect, the more points you get. We’ve seen players go from 1 to 12 in a dramatic swing, blurring the line between luck and strategy.
- Wasabi: The hidden gem of the deck. It rewards timing and reading your opponents, turning a modest first pick into a potential win with a perfect second card.
