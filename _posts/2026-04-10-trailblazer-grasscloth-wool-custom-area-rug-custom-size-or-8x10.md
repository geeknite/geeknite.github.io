## Care, Durability, and Maintenance: Keeping Wool Warm Without the Drama
### Daily care basics
Wool fibers are generally more forgiving than cotton in high-traffic areas. They naturally resist staining, and the grain structure in grasscloth helps disguise minor marks. A simple vacuum with a brush attachment on a gentle setting will usually keep the rug in good shape. Avoid aggressive beater bars or rotating brush heads that can flatten the texture. Think of it as a spa day for your rug: gentle, mindful, and a little indulgent.

### Spill handling and quick fixes
Accidents happen. For most beverages and sauces, blot the spill with a clean, dry cloth as soon as possible. Try not to rub, which can push the liquid deeper into the fibers and disturb the weave. For stubborn stains, you can consider a wool-safe cleaner recommended by rug manufacturers, but test a small inconspicuous area first. The goal is not to erase the stain completely but to prevent it from becoming the rug’s new personality trait.

### Cleaning frequency and professional options
Deep cleaning should be scheduled based on foot traffic, pets, and household activity. A professional rug cleaning for wool, particularly in a custom weave, can help maintain color integrity and fiber resilience. If you live in a dry climate, you might notice wool shedding a little in the first few months as the fibers settle; this is normal and typically fades with the first professional cleaning or consistent vacuuming.

### Edge finishing and durability
Custom edge finishing adds to the rug's longevity by preventing fraying and helping maintain a crisp silhouette. A well-finished edge avoids the dreaded curling at the corners, which, if left unchecked, can lead to a slippery or uneven feel underfoot. If you’re coordinating with a deck or tile floor, consider a non-slip rug pad to keep the piece anchored and to preserve the edges.
