## Assembly and painting notes: getting the most out of your miniatures

- Prep work matters: soak pre-assembled miniatures in warm soapy water, rinse, and prime. The more the details show through the primer, the more the Parrillo/Guiton magic will translate on the tabletop. If you’re aiming for a painterly finish, you’ll want to have your brush selection ready and a palette of thin layers. The key is to avoid a heavy hand; the best painters know that less is more, especially with highly detailed sculpts.
- Speedpaint vs. showpiece: if you’re painting for display, you’ll want to invest in a highlight-and-glaze approach. If you’re painting to play, you can lean into faster shading and dry-brushing that still preserves the sculpt’s character without becoming a time sink. The artist’s flair is in the faces and the cloth folds; keep those lines crisp and you’ll have something that reads well from across the table.
- Assembly tips: some of these miniatures may require pinning for stability, especially if the limbs are designed to look dynamic. Have a pinning kit on hand, and don’t rush the dry-fit stage. The moment you force a misaligned shoulder joint, you’ll know you’ve stepped into “regret-town” and the only exit is patient surgery or a lot more glue.
