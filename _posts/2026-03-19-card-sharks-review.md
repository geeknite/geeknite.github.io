## Setup and Quick Start Guide

Here is a practical play outline that you can memorize before your first game night without needing a nap in the middle:

- Players: Card Sharks works best with 4 to 12 players. For larger groups, create teams or run rotating mini rounds to keep everyone in the orbit of the action.
- Table layout: The central space houses the deck, the shark mat, and the scoring tokens. Ensure everyone can see the mat and hear the game master.
- Setup steps: Shuffle the deck, lay out the mat, place tokens within reach of all players, and appoint a game master who can keep track of rounds while still telling a few good jokes.

### Turn Structure and Scoring

- The turn: A round consists of players drawing a card, revealing it, and then deciding on a bet or a challenge according to the current round rules. The shark mat will trigger action prompts that can send the round into a silly task or a quick team challenge.
- Betting and bluffing: Bets are placed on outcomes such as whether the next card will be higher or lower, or whether a team can accomplish a humorous objective. Bluffs are encouraged and misdirection is celebrated as long as it makes the table smile.
- Scoring: Tokens are awarded for successful bets, style points for performances, and bonus tokens for clever teamwork. The scoring system stays light and forgiving so that a big mood swing does not erase the fun you had in the previous minute.
- Endgame: When a fixed number of rounds is reached or a team hits a target score, the night ends. The winner is determined by tokens and the memorable moments you created while playing.

### House Rules and Variants

Card Sharks invites customization like a kid opening a big present. Try these to tailor the chaos:

- Lazy runner: If you want a slower pace, extend rounds with extra prompts. If you want faster play, cut down on optional actions and lean into rapid reveals.
- Thematic rounds: Theme each round with categories that fit the mood. Space nights could include alien impressions or rocket trivia.
- Team battle mode: Divide players into teams with a rotating captain who calls the final bet. Keeps leadership fresh and energy high.
- Accessibility friendly: Use larger print, slower pacing for reading prompts, and optional nonverbal signals to include players with different abilities.
- Audio optional: Encourage short chants or sound effects to accompany actions for extra flavor.

## Getting It to the Table

Getting a party game onto the table is half art, half science. Here are practical tips that help Card Sharks shine in your own space:

- Create a welcoming moment: three minutes of goofy warm up before play begins can be a super powerful mood setter. Let the shark mascot lead a silly chant or a mechanical wave of applause when a round ends.
- Use a short demo: have one round played as a quick demonstration for the curious bystander. Show how bets work, how prompts trigger tasks, and how scoring tokens accumulate. Seeing it in action makes the table feel confident about jumping in.
- Embrace the chaos: the tension in party games is often the moment you realize you cannot quite read the room. The trick is to lean into chaos with grace and to celebrate the creative misfires as part of the show.
