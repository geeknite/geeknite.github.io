### How to play a quick refresher
Skip-Bo is a race with a philosophy. The goal is to discard all cards from your stock pile by playing them to the build piles in ascending order from 1 to 12. You may draw from the draw pile, and you can use your hand to help you advance your build piles and discard portions of your hand through the process. The wild cards provide a flexible mechanism to bridge gaps or to accelerate your play when the luck of the draw smiles on you. The neat thing about this 1992 edition is that the rules are preserved in their original form. There are no new expansions to complicate matters; there are just you, your opponents, and a table that suddenly becomes a battleground of logistics and memory. It is a small, cozy game that still has enough tension to keep the talk lively and the play brisk.

## Preservation Tips for Vintage Card Games
If you want to keep your sealed vintage deck in mint condition, you should consider a few practical steps. Storing the deck away from direct light is essential to prevent color fading on the box and any printed art. A stable humidity level keeps the seal from weakening or the cardboard from warping. A dedicated display shelf out of direct sunlight can transform a plain room into a small museum of gaming history. For collectors who want to create a premium display, using a glass cabinet with UV-protective glass adds another layer of protection and allows you to enjoy your artifact daily without compromising its integrity. You should also examine the interior content every few years to ensure no moisture has penetrated the seal. If you find minor signs of aging, consult a professional restorer who specializes in paper goods and card games to get advice about preservation options.
