## How the rules bend without breaking the game
### Core UNO remains the backbone
UNO remains a game of sprinting improvisation, with players trying to discard cards by color or number while using action cards to block, skip, reverse, or spring confusion on opponents. The Family Guy Special Edition keeps the essential mechanics intact. This is important because it preserves the familiar pace you love, ensuring seasoned players are not derailed by a completely new rule set. The base game remains accessible to newcomers who just want something light, quick, and silly after a long day of adult responsibilities.

### Twists and turns: show-inspired actions
Where the edition shines is in the flavor cards. Instead of standard Plus 2 or Skip cards, you get show-themed variants that adapt the same effect to fit the character personality on the card.
