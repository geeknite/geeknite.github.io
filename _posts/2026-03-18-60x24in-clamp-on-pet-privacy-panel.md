## Installation: Clamp-On, Not Church, But Still Sacred
The installation is the best part because it’s the closest you get to a DIY win without actually building a shelf or drilling into the desk. Here’s the play-by-play:

- Step 1: Decide where you want your privacy panel. The 60x24 size works well on many desks but you should measure to be sure you won’t muffle a monitor stand or a lamp with too much width.
- Step 2: Position the panel so that the clamps align with the desk’s edge. Make sure you’re not clamping directly onto a soft wood edge that could compress; if you’ve got a metal desk you’re golden. If you have an extra-thick desk, don’t sweat it—the clamps still bite as long as there’s some material for them to grip.
- Step 3: Tighten the two C-clamps evenly. The idea is to create a stable anchor without bending the desk edge or warping the panel. I recommend a little test wiggle after you tighten. If it wobbles, give the clamps another quarter turn; if the desk starts to groan, maybe back off a notch.
- Step 4: Slide the panel into position and affix the divider overlay if you want extra privacy at the sides. The overlay is slim, so it doesn’t turn your desk into a suspicious black box.
- Step 5: Adjust for angle and height. The beauty of clamp-on privacy is the flexibility; you can tilt the panel slightly to reduce glare or align it so you’re not staring at your own reflection in a meeting while sipping coffee and silently judging slides.

The result is a neat, quasi-permanent addition that can be removed without tools in seconds. This is particularly handy for renters, students, or anyone who likes to remix their desk layout on a whim.

### Quick tips for installation success
- Ensure your clamp pads are clean and free of grit before clamping. Sand on the desk edge is a no-go, but a clean surface is a friend.
- If your desk edge is rounded, consider a small pad or gasket (not included) to distribute pressure and reduce the chance of slippage.
- Don’t over-tighten. You’re aiming for stability, not a dented margin in your Velcro cable ties.
- If you share a desk, talk it out with your teammate. There’s something satisfying about mutually agreeing to a privacy shield rather than silently seething while your coworker uses your screen as a mirror.
