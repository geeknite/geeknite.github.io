## Overclocking and cooling headroom

### Factory OC versus manual OC

The Gaming OC variant includes a modest factory overclock that yields tangible gains in many scenarios while staying largely quiet. If you enjoy pushing hardware, manual overclocking can give you a few more frames per second in sustained loads. However, the returns begin to taper as you chase ultra-high clocks, and the power budget and temperature headroom become the gatekeepers. We recommend incremental bumps and stress testing with a reliable stability tool before pushing the envelope. The result is a more responsive gaming experience with a mild improvement in frame times rather than a miracle jump in every title.

### Cooling strategy in practice

The cooler is the heart and lungs of this setup. Gigabyte has configured the fans to ramp up proportionally as temperatures rise, which means you should not expect silence in the heat of battle. When you start synthetic benchmarking with full overclocking, you will hear a more pronounced fan cadence, but the thermals usually stay within reasonable limits.
