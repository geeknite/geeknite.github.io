Overview
--------
Sushi Go Party! is a standalone, multi-menu version of the beloved Sushi Go concept. It uses the classic pick-and-pass drafting mechanic, but instead of a single fixed menu per game, you cycle through a rotating roster of menus that you unlock and draft from for each round. The result is a chaotic, delightful blend of strategy and guesswork: which cards will your neighbors take, and which combinations will actually score you points? The deluxe package typically features more menu options than the base game, offering a wider variety of scoring opportunities and a higher degree of customization for groups of different sizes and tastes.

How to Play: The Flow of Pick & Pass
---------------------------------
If you’ve played Sushi Go!, you’ll feel at home with Sushi Go Party! The core mechanic remains the same: you draft one card from your hand and pass the rest to the left, then you reveal your chosen card at the end of the round to score according to the current menus. The twist is the rotating menu system: instead of playing with one fixed scoring sheet, you’ll encounter a variety of menus across rounds. This means you’ll need to adapt on the fly, balance short-term grabs with long-term combos, and read the table like a librarian with a fondness for spicy tuna.

Turn structure is straightforward:

1. Each player is dealt a hand of cards from the current menu set.
2. You choose one card to keep and place it face down in front of you, then pass the rest to the neighbor on your left.
3. Simultaneously, players reveal their chosen cards and place them in a personal display to be scored later.
4. After a round is scored according to the active menus, the next round begins with a new hand from the next menu set.

The fun (and panic) ramps up because the variety of menus can introduce conflicting objectives. One round might reward a specific line of nigiri, while the next pushes you toward building a large sashimi set. The unpredictability is where the party vibe shines—no two games feel the same, even if you’re playing with the same group of friends who swear they’re “not lucky” and then proceed to roll a straight flush of luck after three sake shots.

Menus, Scoring, and Strategy
-----------------------------
The heart of Sushi Go Party! is the menus. Each menu is a scattershot of scoring objectives, which means you’re always juggling several potential routes to victory. Some menus emphasize fast scoring through small sets of common cards, while others reward you for rare, strategic combos that require you to plan several rounds ahead. Here’s a rough breakdown of how to read these menus and a few strategy tips to survive the dinner table battlefield:

- Nigiri and Sushi Types: Traditional favorites like Nigiri and various sushi types continue to be scoring staples. Nigiri cards tend to be simple but potent when paired with the right side bonuses, while other cards may grant points for specific combinations or leave-overs you can collect gradually.
- Maki and Dumplings: These cards typically reward you for collecting multiple types in your own deck. The more you go all-in on a single category, the bigger your payoff, which can create a dramatic swing if you’re the one who ends up with a perfect little pile.
- Sashimi and Dumplings: Sashimi is a high-risk, high-reward card that can suddenly vault your score if you land the right triplet. Dumplings scale with how many you gather, so sometimes it’s worth intentionally not chasing a big payoff right away to build for a later crescendo.
- Puddings: These are the “end-of-game” bonus cards that often determine the winner in close scores. Puddings are great because they create tension that lingers long after the last turn has been drafted. If you ignore them, you’ll wake up in the middle of the night thinking about the five pudding points you left on the table.

Strategy-wise, there are a few universal truths you can rely on when the group dynamic becomes a heat-seeking sushi knife:

- Don’t overcommit early. The rotating menus mean you might pass up a safe point path for a riskier but higher-reward route a couple of rounds later. The key is to read the table: who’s chasing which combo, and who seems to be in a good position to steal a critical card from you just when you thought you had it locked up?
- Track what’s being passed. It’s easy to focus on your own hand and forget that the person to your left is quietly assembling a perfect three-card tuna-take combo while you’re fiddling with your pick of the day.
- Balance speed with setup. If you’re ahead early, you might want to lean into quick point-generating combos. If you’re behind, you may need to swing for more valuable sets that can move the needle in a single turn.

Community and house rules often shape the experience as much as the cards themselves. If you’re playing with a group that loves longevity, you can implement a rule where last-minute pudding bonuses become more valuable as the game tapes a dramatic, multi-round arc. If you’re playing with impatient folks who measure every second aloud, you can enforce shorter rounds or a fixed menu set for a streamlined experience. The beauty of Sushi Go Party! is that it invites adaptation without breaking its core identity.

Playtest Tales and House Rules
--------------------------------
Here are a few tips from the trenches, based on nights of friendly chaos and the occasional whiff of soy sauce:

- Use a timer if your table numbers exceed six. It keeps the energy up and prevents one player from dragging the round with every decision like a sushi chef contemplating the precise thickness of a nigiri slice.
- Rotate menus intentionally. If your group loves a particular theme, build a mini-season where certain menus appear more frequently, letting you chase specific combos while still leaving room for surprise.
- Try a “hidden scoring” variant where players total points privately for the last round to add a spice of suspense. It’s a fun twist that keeps everyone on their toes—especially the person who bluffs about their pudding strategy.
