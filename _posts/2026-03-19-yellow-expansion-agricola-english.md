## Overview

The Yellow Expansion for Agricola arrives with more sun than a solar-powered scarecrow. It introduces a new color coded layer that sits atop the classic orchard of decisions: your familia, your fields, your wood, your family planning. In Geeknite fashion this review will test its teeth and tell you if you should invest in more boards or more candy corn.

## Whats in the Yellow Expansion

### Components

- Yellow action tiles (40)
- Yellow resource tokens (20)
- Yellow family cards (12)
- Yellow accommodations (6)
- Yellow wooden discs (18)
- Rulebook with color-coded diagrams

### Rules changes at a glance

- The yellow overlay adds a new action economy: each round players may choose to activate one yellow action as a bonus if they want to push a resource chain.
- Yellow actions require a sacrifice of one standard action per round? This line will be clarified in the actual rule set, but for our purposes, think of it as a spicy optional ping.
- When a yellow action tile is used, it creates a yellow marker on your board; at the end of round, those markers convert to additional resources.

### Setup adjustments

- Shuffle the yellow deck and place it face down near the main board. At the start of the game, reveal one yellow action.

### How to incorporate into base game

- The recommended approach is to play Agricola with yellow mode on only after all players are comfortable with base. It scales from a gentle nudge to a full on sunburst depending on how you select actions.

### Visuals

- The expansion uses bright yellow border on components to help you locate the sunniest choices across the board.

## How the Yellow Expansion changes the game

The core idea behind the Yellow Expansion is tempo, not chaos. It tries to reward forward planning while giving you dramatic rash of choices that make you feel like a wizard with 12 different spell slots. Here's how it shakes out in practice:

- Golden tempo: Yellow actions tend to yield bigger payoff in the midgame if you commit early. But committing early costs you early actions elsewhere. It is a classic coin flip in a golden cloak.
- Resource chaining: Some yellow actions unlock resource generation in a chain reaction. If you time it right, you can unlock a self-sustaining loop that powers your farm without heavy investment in fields.
- Risk and reward: The yellow deck introduces cards that reward quick expansion but penalize slow starts with fewer resource returns.

### Round structure and yellow actions

During each round you may decide to spend a standard action to activate a yellow action tile. The tile may give you immediate resources, a free build, or a temporary boost on your pasture. The catch is that every activated yellow action also nudges your yellow marker forward, increasing the risk of overextending when the round ends. It is a balancing act and one that makes the midgame feel like a high-stakes puzzle rather than a gentle farm stroll.

### Thematic implications

Color as a mechanic is tricky in a historical farming game. The Yellow Expansion leans into a sunny mood; the wood grains glow with a sun-baked finish and the yellow tokens look like tiny suns. The art direction is playful but surprisingly readable under candlelight. If you are a fan of the base game aesthetics, you will find the yellow-laden pieces a welcome, if occasionally blinding, addition.

## Playstyle and strategy tips

If you are new to the Yellow Expansion, the best way to approach it is to treat it as a supplementary engine that can accelerate your progress but not substitute for the core. Here are some actionable tips:

### For beginners

- Start slow with light yellow actions that grant extra clay or wood rather than going for ambitious early exploitation. This helps you warm up into the tempo without risking famine.
- Pair yellow actions with stable base strategies like pasture expansion and cattle management. The synergy is real, especially in later rounds.

### For seasoned Agricola veterans

- Plan your yellow actions around a midgame pivot. The deck houses marginal but powerful actions that create a snowball effect when chained with base actions.
- Use yellow markers to track your progress, but do not let them push you into a resource trap. The worst is having too many markers and too few resources to feed your family.

## Interactions with base game and other expansions

The Yellow Expansion interacts with base Agricola in both additive and optional ways. It can be used as a standalone variant set, or integrated into a full campaign mixing in other expansions. A few cross-over notes:

- When used with the core game, you should avoid stacking too many expansions at once. The board can start to look like a technicolor maze and you will forget what your actual goal is.
- If you pair it with a small expansion like the Moor or the Farmers of the North Sea crossovers, the yellow actions interact with new resources and new occupations in a surprisingly harmonious way.

## House rules and optional variants

To keep games fresh, here are some house rules that feel like they belong in a sun hat:

- Double yellow: players may once per game take two yellow actions in a single round, with the second action costing double the standard resources for balancing.
- Sunset drafting: at the start of round, reveal two yellow cards and choose one to add to your hand for that round; you may use only that card this round.
- Yellow lottery: at the end of the game, players discard unused yellow markers to gain extra Encore cards or extra end game bonuses.

The expansion includes many micro-encounters that encourage different paths; however, board designers might adopt a meta where players keep track of which yellow actions are most potent.
