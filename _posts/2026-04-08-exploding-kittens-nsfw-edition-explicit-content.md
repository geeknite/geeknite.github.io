## How to play: quick setup and flow

If you already know Exploding Kittens, you know the vibe. Draw a card, try not to explode, use a defuse card if you have one, and keep the explosions away from your own head while setting up a chain of silly plays that traps your friends in the drama you create. The NSFW Edition adds a few structural tweaks, but the core cycle is familiar:

- Each player starts with a small hand and one last line of defense against the exploding kitten. You will likely have to manage risk with each draw.
- On your turn, you may play one or more action cards to influence the deck, other players, or the draw pile itself.
- When a kitten explodes, the player who drew it is out unless they can defend themselves with a defuse card or some equally clever play that the card text allows.
- The game continues until only one player remains un exploded, who claims victory, usually with a smug grin and a story about how they outplayed everyone with a single card that changed the entire table dynamic.

The NSFW Edition keeps setup simple and session length short. You can burn through a round in about 15 to 20 minutes, depending on how spicy your cards are and how quickly your players catch on to the in-jokes. This is not a marathon RPG session; it is a coffee break with a punchline, and it benefits from a casual group where everyone is there to have a good time rather than overanalyze the card text line by line.
