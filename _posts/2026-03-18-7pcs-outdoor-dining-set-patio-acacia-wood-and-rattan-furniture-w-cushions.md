## Assembly and Setup
### What to Expect When You Open the Box
Like many multi-piece outdoor sets, the 7PCS package arrives in several boxes with hardware, feet caps, and assembly screws. The table typically requires attaching legs to the tabletop, while chairs involve fastening backrests and seat frames. The process is usually straightforward but can be a puzzle if you rush through it; take your time to align bolts and washers. A second pair of hands helps, but one capable DIYer with a little patience can do it.

What you’ll need:
- A standard Phillips screwdriver or a drill/driver with a Phillips bit
- A bit of patience and a bit more patience
- Optional: furniture oil or sealant for post-assembly protection

Estimated time:
- Table: 20–40 minutes
- Each chair: 8–12 minutes
- Total assembly session: about 1 to 2 hours for a lone ranger, slightly less with a helper and a snack break

### Assembly Tips for a Smooth Ride
- Unpack and sort hardware before you start. A tidy workstation prevents mystery fasteners from turning your project into a numeric riddle.
- Lightly grease any bolt threads with a dab of oil to help insertion; avoid over-lubing, which can attract dirt.
- Do not overtighten during the first pass; snug is good, then go back with a torque-friendly approach to ensure a firm, not warped, frame.
- Check alignment between the chair seat and backrest before tightening fully. A misalignment can cause wobble that adds a suspenseful ambience to every meal.

## Durability and Weather Resistance
### Wood Care and Longevity
Acacia wood is strong but not miracle wood. Without proper sealing, it can darken or weather unevenly. The set typically ships with a protective coating or a recommended sealant. Reapply periodic coats as the manufacturer suggests and you’ll prolong the life of the wood significantly. Seasonal maintenance includes cleaning, sanding any rough spots, and reapplying finish when the wood begins to look dry or faded. Doing so helps maintain the warm tones and prevents small cracks from turning into bigger splits.

### Rattan and Cushions in Outdoor Conditions
Rattan weaving adds aesthetic charm but can be vulnerable to moisture if untreated. Outdoor storage or cover during heavy rain or winter seasons is recommended. Cushions, when left out in rain, can take on moisture and mildew if neglected. Use cushion storage boxes or waterproof covers to protect the cushions during inclement weather. Quick-drying cushion fill and moisture-resistant covers are a practical pairing for a busy household that does not want to cage its outdoor life.

### Real-World Weather Considerations
If you’re in a climate with heavy humidity or salt air, you’ll want to reseal wood more frequently and inspect the weave for any looseness. Light rain shouldn’t instantly ruin the set; the key is to dry off the surfaces and inspect joints for looseness after any major weather event. A little proactive maintenance goes a long way toward keeping the set looking fresh year after year.

## Maintenance and Care
### Daily and Seasonal Care Checklist
- Wipe down with a damp cloth after meals to remove crumbs and sticky residues.
- Use a mild soap solution for tougher stains; avoid harsh chemicals that might erode finishes.
- Dry all surfaces thoroughly to prevent water damage or sap buildup on the wood.
- Store cushions in a dry area when not in use for extended periods or use weatherproof covers.
- Periodically check screws and bolts and re-tighten as needed.

### Cleaning the Cushions
Machine washing is not always recommended; consult label instructions. If fabric is not removable, spot clean with a fabric-safe cleaner and let air dry. If removable, wash according to fabric care guidelines and ensure they are completely dry before reassembly. Quick maintenance hacks include using a lint roller to collect pet hair and a fabric refresher spray to keep things smelling fresh after a long outdoor session.

### Long-Term Storage Tips
For winter months or heavy rain seasons, consider storing cushions in a dry space or using weatherproof covers. While the set is designed for outdoor use, a little extra protection keeps the vibrancy of the wood and the weave intact, saving you money in the long run by postponing replacements.
