## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Longevity Tips

- Cleaning: use a soft, dry cloth to wipe the chips occasionally. If they get a little sticky from condensation or spills, a quick wipe with a damp cloth and then a thorough dry-off should do. Avoid harsh cleaners that could degrade the edge markings or the surface texture.
- Handling: avoid throwing chips or stacking too aggressively. Nice, controlled shuffles preserve the edges and reduce the risk of nicks that can accumulate and make chip recognition harder over time.
- Storage: keep them in a cool, dry place to prevent warping or edge wear. A small acrylic rack or dedicated bag can reduce chaos and keep your table area tidy between games.
- Wear and tear: chips are resilient but not invincible. If you set them down with too much force, the edge texture can wear down in high-use areas. A light touch when stacking and unstacking helps them last longer.

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it pays off with smoother games and fewer complaining players about chip miscounts when the pot reaches double-digit thousands of chips-worth sizes in friendly tournaments.
