Overview
## What is the Red All-Out Dice Set
### The concept behind All-Out dice
Zombicide leans hard into cinematic action. The All-Out Dice Set locates that moment where the survivors go full tilt, trying to push through a horde in a blaze of glory. The red dice are designed to emphasize success outcomes when players take aggressive, multi-action turns. In practice, when you roll these dice, the probability of favorable outcomes tilts toward high-impact results — but it comes with a cost in the form of higher risks or the potential for misfires if the wrong faces appear.

The set CMON markets as a compact, color-coded upgrade. In the standard presentation, you get a stack of red color dice with bold white icons that pop on the table. The kit includes:

Gameplay and mechanics
## How the All-Out Dice Change the Game
### Dice faces and probabilities
The red dice come with a modified face distribution designed to push players toward decisive moments. The faces typically emphasize higher-yield results such as extra damage, extra movement, or auto-kills depending on the official rules for All-Out actions. The trade-off is that misfires or critical failures can loom larger when you bank too much on a single roll. This is one of those design choices that makes you feel like you are risking a lot for a trophy that might not be within reach.

If you enjoy crunchier probability math, the All-Out dice provide a fertile ground for discussions. A particular tactic might rely on chaining All-Out dice to wipe a cluster of zombies; the statistical cliff edge becomes the centerpiece of many table talk moments, which in turn fuels the social energy of the session.

### How the red dice interact with base zombies and survivors
In Zombicide 2nd Ed core rules, you use the same set of dice for events and zombie-handling tasks. The red dice amplify certain outcomes during All-Out actions while keeping the rest of the turn’s results aligned with existing rules. The interaction is not so much a rewrite as a spice rack addition; you still need to plan lines of sight, cover, and the ever-important supply of ammo and blades.

From a practical standpoint, you want to plan two turns ahead. If your survival squad has one big last push on the board, the All-Out dice can help you realize that vision. If you miscalculate, it can also yield a brutal cascade where you end up with the dice doing more harm than good. That duality is part of the charm.

### Time to roll and time to reflect
In social terms, rolling red dice can trigger a wave of exclamations. The players lean into the drama: red dice flashing on a critical hit, a survivor teetering on the edge of a pool of zombies, and the board’s atmosphere improving because the dice do not just lie there quietly — they assert themselves.
