## The Draft/Sealed Experience
Sealed play is a different beast from constructed formats. In sealed, you bring fewer decisions to the table but with more on-card consequences condensed into a single deck. The Starter Factory Sealed bundles excel for players who enjoy social play and experimentation rather than tight optimization. Here are some practical takeaways for the sealed experience:

- Team up or play in small groups. A 15-pack pool can be perfect for a three-to-four player mini-draft that consolidates the best pieces into a cohesive deck. If you are playing one-on-one, you may want to use a partner draft or a pre-release style format to ensure the result remains competitive.
- Build with color balance in mind. It is easy to get pulled into a two-color deck while ignoring the third color’s potential. In sealed, a three-color deck is ambitious but doable if you kept your options open during the pack-opening phase.
- Don’t overboard on rares. A common mistake is to chase a single top-tier rare at the expense of the curve and early game pressure. The real power of sealed is consistency. A deck that runs out of gas in the midgame is a deck that wants more mana or more early-game plays.
- Learn from your mistakes. Each sealed night is a classroom where you can observe what cards you missed or overvalued, which teaches you how pulls translate into deck construction in a way that pre-planned crafted decks do not.

## Build Tips and Practical Advice
A few practical tips to enhance your Starter Factory Sealed night:

- Start with a quick scan: Open the packs and do a light color-within-accuracy scan to see what colors you are drawn to. It saves you time during deck construction and reduces mana-fixing stress.
- Focus on mana curves: A reliable mana base is the backbone of any sealed deck. If you face a mana-screw moment, you will have a bad time. Draft cards that help you hit your early turns consistently. A smooth curve often outperforms raw big-drop power later in the game.
- Don’t be afraid to splash colors: If you have a couple of powerful cards that can function with a third color, consider a light splash. Sealed formats can tolerate a splash if the mana and card synergy support it.
- Keep sideboarding in mind: If you have a group that plays multiple rounds, build a lightweight sideboard. A few well-chosen cards can convert a bad draw into a win when it matters.
- Track your own learning: If you are new to sealed, keep notes about which strategies you found most effective and which cards surprised you. The act of reflection itself is a critical part of becoming a better player.
