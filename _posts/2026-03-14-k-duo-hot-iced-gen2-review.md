The Tech Behind MultiStream

Gen 2 introduces MultiStream Technology to the brewing equation. In plain English, multiple streams means the water is delivered in parallel through several vertical channels rather than a single stream. The hoped-for benefits are even extraction, reduced hot spots, and the sense that you are not drinking water-turned-coffee. For our taste buds, this translates to more uniform flavor in both single-serve cups and carafes. The science here matters because uneven flow on a warm mug can turn a good roast into a muddled memory. With MultiStream, you should expect consistent temperature bands and more even extraction across the coffee bed. It may not create flavor that grows an extra beard, but it does help your morning cup avoid the dull edge of a poorly distributed brew.

Maintenance and Cleaning Tips

- Regularly descale using a mild cleaning solution or a Keurig-approved descaler. This helps maintain flow and flavor. - Empty the carafe and rinse promptly after use to prevent coffee residue from building up on the warming plate. - Wipe the exterior with a damp cloth; avoid harsh cleaners that may degrade the finish. - Clean the brew head occasionally to avoid flavor drift. - Use filtered water if possible; minerals in hard water can impact taste and scale buildup. The goal is to minimize guesswork and maximize morning coffee vibes.
