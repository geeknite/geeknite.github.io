## How to Play With the Expansion
The mechanical changes are not overly complicated. You add a handful of cat cards and a couple of new action cards, and the rest of the base game remains the same. Setup time increases slightly, because you must sort a few extra piles and decide which cat powers will be live in the current round. Here is a practical walkthrough to get you started.

### Step by Step Setup
1. Shuffle the base deck as usual and place it in the center. 2. Add a subset of expansion cards according to your chosen game mode. 3. Each player draws their starting hand as per base rules. 4. Decide if the round includes all cat powers or a curated subset. 5. Begin the round and prepare for glorious chaos, with occasional meows for dramatic effect.

### Example Round Outline
- Player A draws a card and triggers a cat power that forces a swap with player B. The crowd gasps, the cat on the card stares into your soul from across the room, and somewhere a dog sighs in the background.
- Player B counters with a defense card as Player C tries to bluff their way into a cat-assisted victory. Cat cards do not forget; they simply observe and wait for their moment to pounce on your misplay.
- The round ends when the explosion deck finally reveals itself in a spectacular puff of theatrical smoke, and everyone pretends not to cry about losing their favorite card to a stubborn whisker.

## Strategy and Playstyle Notes
Cat cards add nuance to decision making. It is not enough to simply hope for a good draw; you need to read the room, predict your opponents moves, and be ready to pivot once the cat powers come into play. Strategy in this expansion is a blend of classic bluffing, cat-like reflexes, and a surprising amount of memory work as you recall who burned which card at which moment.

### Early Round Strategy
- Focus on building a flexible hand that can respond to cat powers without overcommitting to a single plan.
- Use cat interactions to force other players into suboptimal plays. If someone else surrenders a strong card for a cat in play, you have earned a small victory that is priceless in this game world.
- Don t be afraid to pass on strong cards when you know a cat is set to pounce in the next draw — saving your defuse for the late game is often worth more than a flashy early play.

### Mid to Late Game Strategy
- When the explosion deck grows thinner, cat powers can flip the balance quickly. Keep a mental tally of who has defuses left and which players have shown a willingness to risk it all for a cat card.
- Use cat cards to bait a misplay. If you can coax your favorite frenemies into a hasty decision, your moment to strike will arrive with fewer consequences.
- Remember the social contract: this is a party game; you are allowed to be dramatic. The audience loves it, and the game benefits from the energy you bring to the table.

### Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
- Overplanning a single move leads to tunnel vision. Allow space for chaos; you will thank yourself later.
- Underestimating the cat cards because they look cute. Cute is a trap door to disaster, and disaster is a fun color on a sofa cushion.
- Forgetting to reshuffle after a big play. Your play area will thank you if you take a second to tidy the deck, even if your ego does not.
