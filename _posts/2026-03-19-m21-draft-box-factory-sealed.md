## Box Contents and Layout: What You’re Actually Getting

A standard M21 Draft Booster Box contains 24 draft boosters. Each draft booster is designed to maximize drafting fun: five cards of basic quality per pack, with at least one rare or mythic per pack, three uncommons, ten commons, a basic land or a non-land card in the incidental slot, and a foil slot that sometimes yields a spicy foil. The total experience is built to feel like a graceful waltz through a mana library, except you’re wearing sneakers that squeak every time you pass wind through your sleeves. And yes, there are basic land cards in every pack; those lands do not go into the “graveyard” the way myths do, but they do go into your binder to remind you that you could have treated yourself to something shinier.

A note on the factory-sealed experience: factory-sealed means you’re buying the product in its original, untouched packaging. That’s important if you’re chasing the “never opened” collector vibe or if your garage has a small, squealing demon called FOMO. The seal also acts as a guard against the dark arts of pre-opened packs and the ancient practice of box-slinging that the internet pretends happens with alarming frequency. In short: sealed is a good baseline if you want a clean slate for your draft night or if you enjoy the ritual of unsealing more than any particular card pull.

## Box Quality, Security, and the Factory-Sealed Dilemma

Factory sealing preserves the original packaging integrity, which matters if you’re chasing condition-sensitive collectors’ cards or simply want to avoid the thrill-killing moment of discovering a compromised box. In practice, the sealed product you purchase should be enjoyed for what it is—a fresh start for your draft night and a tangible artifact of a hobby that stays popular, even when your wallet screams for mercy.
