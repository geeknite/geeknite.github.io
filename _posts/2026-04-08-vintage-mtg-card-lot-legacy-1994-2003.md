## Storage, Handling, and Care
Bulk buys require care to preserve the value and enjoyability of your collection. Here are practical tips to protect the 1200+ card treasure you’ve acquired:
- Sleeve all rares first, ideally with near mint protection to preserve edges and corners.
- Use deck boxes and acid-free binders with proper page protection to prevent warping.
- Keep humidity and temperature stable; avoid direct sunlight, which can fade colors and edges.
- Consider separating the rares from the bulk to prevent frequent handling of the high-value cards.

## Condition and Valuation: A Reality Check
A 1200+ card lot is not a certified portfolio of fortune; it’s a chance, a garden of possibilities. The value is highly sensitive to the condition of the key rares, the presence of heavy play damage on the standout cards, and the overall distribution of sets. Here are a few practical approaches to thinking about value:
- Scan for condition of rares first; the majority of bulk cards will be commons and uncommons. The exact ratio can drastically affect how much value you derive from the lot. 
- Separate damaged cards; some buyers are interested in damaged copies for non-collectible uses or for parts to salvage edges.
- Check for foil versions; foil variants can add a small premium if they are in good condition.
- Compare with recent sales of similar lots on marketplaces; use multiple sources for a balanced view.
