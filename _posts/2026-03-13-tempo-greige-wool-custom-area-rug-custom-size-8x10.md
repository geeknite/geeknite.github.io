## Durability and Performance in Real Life
Wool is naturally resilient, which helps with foot traffic in high-use rooms. Tempo Greige Wool should hold up well to daily wear in most households, including those with one or two pets and at least three different temperature zones in the home. As with any natural fiber rug, there is a housekeeping cost: regular vacuuming (a gentle setting) and immediate attention to spills to avoid staining.

A few practical notes:
- Flattening and shedding: New wool rugs can shed a little at first. Regular vacuuming helps. A soft brush can be used to lift the pile and encourage balance in the surface.
- Stains: Treat spills promptly with a blot-and-dab approach using a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper and work in more into the fibers. For stubborn areas, a wool-safe cleaner is recommended.
- Foot traffic pattern: Expect some flattening in high-traffic zones. Periodic rotation (if practical) helps distribute wear more evenly and extend the rug’s life in a busy space.

In essence, Tempo Greige Wool is not a disposable product. It’s designed to endure, while still looking like a high-design piece that belongs in a magazine spread or your own living room that you actually use for living in.

## Care and Maintenance
Careful care is the unsung hero of keeping wool rugs looking great for years. Here’s a concise guide:
- Vacuum regularly with a vacuum that has a suction only setting and a brushless beater bar to avoid a snowstorm of fibers. Rotate occasional areas to even out wear.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing; you’ll only spread the mess and fluff the carpet in a way that makes you cry when you see the footprint pattern.
- For stains, use a wool-safe cleaner in a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. If in doubt, consult the retailer’s care guide or a professional cleaner who specializes in natural fibers.
- Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods, which can fade colors over time. Ladder the drama by placing the rug away from windows or using curtains during peak sun hours.

The care routine isn’t glamorous, but it’s predictable. And in geek terms, predictable care equals sustainable performance, which is the hero move in the RPG of home decor.
