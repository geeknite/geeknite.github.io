## Assembly tips and maintenance
### Assembly tips
- Pre-sort: Separate screws and washers by size before you begin; it saves a lot of head-scratching and lets you feel like a real DIY hero.
- Two-person rule: A second pair of hands dramatically reduces assembly time and mistakes, especially when aligning the table top or tightening the last few bolts.
- Patience with cushions: If cushions are included, allow a few hours for the foam to settle after unboxing. Don’t be surprised if you find a little foam indentation that resolves after a few days of use.
- Tighten in stages: Don’t crank every bolt to max on the first go. Tighten gradually in a cross pattern to ensure even stress distribution.

### Cleaning and maintenance
- Regular wipe-down: A damp cloth with mild soap is enough for most days. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could wear down the protective coating.
- Cushion care: If cushions are machine-washable, follow the care label. For non-washable fabrics, spot clean with a gentle soap solution and air-dry away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade.
- Seasonal storage: Store cushions indoors or in a dry shed during off-season. For metal frames, wipe down with a dry cloth to prevent moisture buildup that could cause rust over years.

### Outdoor storage notes
If you live in a climate with harsh winters, consider using a patio storage box or a tarp cover. This is not a fashion thing; it’s a tiny insurance policy against mold, mildew, and the cat who thinks the table is a scratching post for its laser pointer adventures.
