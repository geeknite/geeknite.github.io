## Foil and holo quality: does it shine in real life?

Foil fidelity can make or break a set’s perceived value, especially when you’re chasing those dramatic character-issue foils that pop in photos and play sessions alike. ## Sorting, storage, and staying sane with 150 cards

Sorting a pile this big is half the fun and half the chore. Here are a few practical tips to keep you from drowning in a sea of dragon-energy counters:

- Invest in a basic card sorter with dividers by rarity, color, or character. It makes deck-building nights productive instead of a chaotic scavenger hunt.
- Keep a separate bin for duplicates you don’t plan to use in decks. You’ll thank yourself later when you’re juggling multiple theme ideas and need spare copies.
- Use soft sleeves for display while keeping play-ready sleeves for tournaments. It preserves the holo shine and reduces wear during casual bar-top duels.
- Label your foil-heavy sets for easy retrieval. A simple color-coded sticker system on the storage box can save you dozens of minutes when you’re in a rush.
