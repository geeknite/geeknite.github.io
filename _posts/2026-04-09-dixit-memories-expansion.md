## Introduction
Welcome, fellow raconteurs, to the wild, whimsical, slightly chaotic world of Dixit Memories Expansion. Dixit, for the uninitiated, is a party game about storytelling through abstract, dreamlike imagery. Players take turns being the storyteller, giving a clue that matches exactly one or more of the 6–8 cards in players’ hands. Everyone else picks a card from their own hand that best matches the clue, and then all cards are revealed for players to guess which card belonged to the storyteller. The beauty (and chaos) of the game is that the clue should be specific enough to make people pick your card, but not so obvious that everyone’s waving their hands and shouting, “Yes, that’s clearly the card!” The Memories expansion slides into this delightful chaos by adding 84 new cards to the pot, expanding the pool of imagery to an even more sprawling landscape of dreams.

## How It Plays with Base Dixit
If you’re a Dixit veteran, you know the drill: you tell a clue that could describe multiple cards, then you lure the table into guessing which one is yours. The Memories expansion doesn’t overwrite that engine; it expands it. But the impact varies depending on your group and play style. Here’s what to expect.

### How the Mechanics Change (Or Don’t)
- More imagery means more potential signals. If your group tends to be a quick guess crew, you might find the added cards keep the tension just right; if you love gambling on cryptic clues, you’ll be fed more fuel for obscure prompts.
- The supply of 84 new cards makes it easier to craft a consistent “tone” for a round or an entire game night. You can chase a specific mood—whimsical, eerie, cozy—without recycling the same handful of prompts for every session.
- Because the base game’s objective remains the same, the expansion doesn’t introduce a new victory condition; it simply broadens the creative surface you’re painting on.

### Strategy and Brainstorming: Tips for Getting More Lols and Fewer Blank Stares
- Start with a theme. Pick a mood for the round (e.g., “dreamlike ocean” or “surreal library”) and then sift the 84 cards for potential matches. You’ll train your brain to map clues to images more quickly.
- Use ambiguity sparingly. The risk of an overly obscure clue is that no one gets it; the risk of an obvious clue is giving away your card. The new cards provide a wider spectrum, which can help you calibrate this balance in real-time.
- Mix your prompt with a story beat. A good Dixit clue often nods to a mini-narrative. The Memories expansion gives you extra cards that can act as “plot twists” in a tale you’re trying to tell.
- Lean on card art as a co-narrator. Sometimes a card’s imagery is so evocative that you can squat on it and let the scene tell most of your story. The expansion’s artistry helps with those moments when you want to sound poetically lazy in the best possible sense.

### The Social Score: How It Affects Group Dynamics
With 84 new prompts, expect a few players to lock into a groove and produce sublime clues; others will still improvise off the cuff. Dixit thrives on creative synergy, and Memories supplies more raw material for that energy. If you play with a rotating cast of friends who each bring different storytelling senses, the expansion can act as a leveler, offering prompts that speak to various sensibilities. If you’re after a more robust solo Dixit experience, you might want to pair Memories with a dedicated solo variant or a fan-made scoring system. ## Quick Start Guide for Your First Session with Memories
- Set up as you would for base Dixit: the storyteller clue, voting, reveal, and scoring steps remain the heart of play.
- Shuffle the 84-card deck into the existing deck you’re using (or use it as a standalone memories deck for a special night).
- Decide on a mood or theme for the round to help players craft clues that feel cohesive with the chosen dreamscape.
- Remind players to avoid overly obvious clues. A well-chosen card with a dream-like quality will reveal itself as the winner with the right hint.
- Use a timer if you’re prone to long soliloquies. The game doesn’t demand speed, but keeping rounds moving helps maintain the party energy.

## Variants and House Rules
- Thematic rounds: declare a theme at the start of a round (e.g., “Underwater Memories,” “Skyward Village,” “Midnight Circus”) and only allow cards that fit the theme for the storyteller’s hand.
- Team storytelling: split into teams; each team crafts a single clue, then everyone votes. This can democratize the balancing of clues and bring new dynamics to the table.
- Prompt rotation: after a few sessions, rotate which cards you draw from the Memories deck to ensure variety and prevent predictable prompt patterns.

## Accessibility: Language and Cognitive Load

If you have players who might feel overwhelmed by abstract prompts, you can offset this by choosing to weave together a couple of easier prompts before diving into the more cryptic ones.
