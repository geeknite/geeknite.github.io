## Installation and fit

### Tools you need

To install this housing, you will need basic bicycle maintenance tools: a cable cutter, a cable fine adjuster, cable end caps, ferrules, and a good set of cable ferrule pliers. If you are replacing a long run through the frame, you might also want a cable liner to ensure the inner wire does not snag on corners or near transitions. The good news is that a lot of the work can be done in a single afternoon, especially if you already have a bike shed that looks like a mad scientist has been tinkering there for years.

### Step by step install guide

1. Remove the old housing and inner cable from the brakes and shifters. Inspect the inner cable for wear and replace if there are visible kinks or fraying. 2. Measure the new housing length you need by tracing the path from lever to brake caliper. Add a couple of extra inches for trimming and trimming again. 3. Cut the new housing to length with a proper cable cutter. 4. Insert ferrules on both ends of the housing. 5. Thread the inner cable through the housing from lever side to brake side. 6. Install end caps, adjust tension, and test the lever feel. 7. Reassemble any components you removed and take a short test ride. 

### Common mistakes to avoid

One common mistake is buying a very long piece of housing and then trying to cram it into a tight frame path with lots of curves. The extra length becomes a liability if you cannot route it cleanly, resulting in a tug or snag that will ruin your day. Another common error is failing to trim the inner cable and leaving a long tail that binds when you pull the lever. Always ensure you have a clean end before finalizing the setup. Finally, avoid overtightening the ferrules, which can crush the housing and cause a frayed edge.

## Compatibility and fit considerations

### Frame and brake types

This housing is compatible with most frames that require a standard 5 mm outer diameter housing. It works well with cantilever and linear pull brakes and can also be used with some road brake configurations that have a wide enough ferrule. Always check your brake type and the maximum housing diameter your levers and calipers can accommodate before purchasing.

### Inner cable compatibility

A 5 mm housing expects 1.1 to 1.2 mm inner wire for standard braking. If your inner cable is thicker or has a different termination (for example, a wire that ends in a barrel or a specific connector), you will need to verify compatibility or consider a different housing. In most everyday bikes, standard 1.2 mm wires fit easily and work well with this kind of housing.
