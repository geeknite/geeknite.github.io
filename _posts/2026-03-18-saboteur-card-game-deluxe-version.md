## How to Play: The Core Loop in Plain Talk
Saboteur is a social deduction and light strategy experience wrapped in a dwarven mining theme. The deluxe version preserves the skeleton of the system while increasing the potential for misdirection and narrative play.

### Setup and Roles
- Each player receives a face-down role card, which designates you as a miner or saboteur. The number of saboteurs is chosen to maintain tension without crushing the miners under the boots of doom.
- The path layout is created with a few map cards, laying the groundwork for a mine that might either lead to treasure or derail into chaos. You will be tempted to accelerate progress with a well-timed play, or accidentally sabotage your own team in a moment of dramatic flair.

### The Turn Structure
- On your turn you draw a card and decide whether to place a path card, fix a blocker with a tool, or use a sabotage card to hinder others. The path cards wind their way across the table, gradually forming a tunnel with opportunities for multiple detours.
- Saboteurs use their special cards to hamper miners and force new routes to be chosen. Miners respond by coordinating efforts to push toward the treasure while keeping track of who might be pulling the strings behind their backs.

### End of Round and Scoring
- The round ends when the treasure is reached or when the deck runs out. Treasure tokens are distributed to the miner team, while saboteurs accumulate points for creative opposition.
- A new round begins with new roles, fresh ambitions, and a renewed sense that the underworld is a stage for misdirection and teamwork.
