### Painting and Hobby Notes
Painting a Wulfsburg Wolf Abomination is a joy but not without its caveats. The scale and fur ridges invite a painter to practice layering, dry brushing, and perhaps some stippling to create a dynamic fur look. A few tips for best results:
- Start with a solid undercoat in a mid tone to anchor shadows and highlights. A neutral gray or brown works well depending on your color plan.
- Use a subtle contrast wash along the fur to bring out texture without pooling in every needle of fur detail.
- Dry brush lighter tones along the front-facing ridges to simulate moonlit fur catching light.
- Paint the eyes a piercing color to give the beast a characterful stare that can break the monotony of a long evening of gaming.

If you are new to painting, you can experiment with a two or three color palette and still achieve a visually striking result. The fur texture tends to hide a lot of minor paint inconsistencies, which is good news for hobbyists who are either impatient or perpetually late to the painting table.
