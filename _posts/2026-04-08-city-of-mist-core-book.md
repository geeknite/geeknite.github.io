City of Mist is one of those games that makes you lean back in your chair and realize you are not merely playing a hero, you are conjuring a myth wearing a raincoat. The core book wears its neon noir influences on its sleeve and invites you to step into a city that feels alive, corrupt, and wonderfully ridiculous all at once. It is not a game that pretends to solve crime with a single roll of the dice; it is a celebration of stories that unfold as you walk the streets, light a cigarette, and realize that your Mask has a life of its own. If you are tired of swords and dragons staring at you from the page, City of Mist gives you something fresher and more urban: legends that live where the bus stops, the laundromat hums, and a rusted sign proclaims the legends are true, even if no one admits it in polite company.

## Introduction

City of Mist asks a simple question with a sly grin: what if myths and legends could be hiding in plain sight, attached to real people in a real city, bending the everyday world into something extraordinary? The core book delivers a toolkit for building Masks, Mythos, and Enigmas that collide in a way that feels intimate, cinematic, and a little bit silly in the best possible way. The tone sits between a moody graphic novel and a late night detective show, with enough humor to keep things from getting too heavy and enough texture to stay interesting after several sessions.

### What City of Mist Core Book tries to do
The book wants you to craft three things at the same time: a living city that breathes through myth, a set of characters whose public faces mask private legends, and a system that supports cinematic storytelling rather than crunch-heavy simulation. It leans into collaboration, letting players and GMs shape the world as they play. In practice, this means lots of space to explore how a Mask can cover a real fear, how a Mythos can grant a powerful moment, and how a Tag can tilt a scene toward a surprising outcome. The result is a game that rewards long term character arcs as much as one shot spectacle.

## What you get in the core book

The core book is a compact, art-forward manual that wears its heart on its neon sleeve. It contains sections on setting, character creation, the mechanics, and a handful of ready to run adventures. The layout is friendly, the art is evocative, and the tone invites you to lean into the mood without turning away from real emotional stakes.

### The city as a character
City of Mist treats the city as a living, breathing partner in the story. It has moods, neighborhoods, rumors, and a mythology that bleeds through the concrete. The book provides a framework for sketching districts with their own legends and tensions, plus a set of factions and figures who pull the strings behind the scenes. The city is not just a backdrop; it is a force that can push players toward mythic moments even when they are chasing a petty clue. This design choice makes every session feel like it matters beyond the individual character sheet.

### Masks, Mythos, and Enigmas
This is the core concept that keeps the book grounded and immensely replayable. Masks are the public personas that players perform in the city. They determine how others see the character and what kind of personal flaws are likely to surface under pressure. Mythos is the mythic power the Mask taps into, the source of strength that can drive dramatic scenes. Enigmas are the personal life issues that justify why the Mask exists and what it costs to maintain the masquerade. Together, these three elements create a triangle of personal myth, public reputation, and the street level reality the team is trying to protect or disrupt.

### Tags as the engine
Tags are short descriptors that define a character or a scene. They are the primary engine for the game, used to justify actions, modify dice results, and shape the narrative. Tags can be anything from a mundane skill to a mythic capability witnessed in a single moment. The stronger the Tag, the more it can color the story, giving players and GMs a vocabulary to push scenes into new directions. The core book offers dozens of example Tags and a method for creating bespoke Tags that fit your Mask and your Mythos. This is where the game truly becomes personal, because your Tags are the fingerprints of your myth made tangible in the city.

### Mechanic overview and play feel
The system leans toward narrative momentum rather than crunchy mechanical precision. You roll a dice pool that combines your base capabilities with active Tags and any situational bonuses. The outcomes promote cinematic storytelling: a 7-9 invites a twist or complication, a 10+ gives a strong effect, and a miss opens a lane for a clever failure that can propel the scene in unexpected directions. It is the kind of system where a failed stakeout might reveal a deeper conspiracy or where a misread clue triggers a sudden blast of mythic action. The balance between control and chance makes play feel dynamic, and the emphasis on story over perfect math keeps newcomers from drowning in charts while rewarding players who lean into dramatic moments.

### Character creation and play rhythm
Creating a Mask is intentionally evocative. You pick a public face, a personal myth, and the life that you juggle behind the scenes. You craft your Mythos as a power source, a symbol that gives you access to extraordinary effects at the cost of attention and consequences. You assemble Tags that reflect your strengths and vulnerabilities and the ways your Mask interacts with the city. The rhythm of play then becomes a back-and-forth between stakeouts and illusions, between pulling a thread and watching the city bend around it. It is not a game that rushes you toward a final boss; it invites you to savor the reveals and let the myth come alive slowly, one scene at a time.

## The GM toolkit and play style
For GMs who love strong mood, City of Mist is a gift. The book offers practical advice on how to frame scenes, manage clues, and build threads that feel personal to each player. It emphasizes collaboration, giving players agency to shape the city through their Masks and to drive the story through their myths. The GM tools cover pacing, clue distribution, and the gentle art of escalation, so even if you are new to storytelling heavy RPGs, you can still run sessions that feel cohesive and earned.

### Adventures and seeds
The core book includes ready to run adventures that range from one-shot mysteries to multi-session arcs. These seeds are designed to be adaptable to your table tone, whether you prefer gritty noir or caper level hijinks. They provide scene ideas, sample locations, and a set of hooks you can drop into the city to spark the mythic moment. You can treat them as stand-alone stories or as connecting arcs that reveal more about a District, a faction, or a hidden legend that binds many Masks together.

### Artwork and accessibility
The production values are strong, with striking art and a clean, readable layout. The art helps set the mood and gives players quick visual cues about the kinds of stories the game encourages. The writing is accessible without being simplistic, striking a balance between evocative prose and practical guidance. For new players, the core book provides a gentle introduction with concrete steps for creating a Mask and starting a campaign. For experienced players, there are enough examples and tips to keep things fresh and interesting.

## A closer look at Masks with sample ideas
Here are a few illustrative Masks that show the flavor the book encourages. These are not just names but prompts you can flesh out during session prep:

- The Velvet Oracle: a public face that reads signals in the city lights and knows when a prime moment is about to occur. Mythos might be a prophecy in the form of a recurring symbol, while Enigma is the fear of being forgotten. Tags include high social manipulation and a knack for reading people under pressure.
- Iron Maiden of the Grid: a tech savvy Mask who uses digital myths to bend the city toward rescue or ruin. Mythos could be a digital deity that speaks through circuits, while Enigma is a past betrayal by a mentor. Tags emphasize hacking, surveillance, and a ruthless streak.
- The Concierge: a city insider who knows every doorway and back alley. Mythos as an oath to protect a neighborhood and its secrets; Enigma as the burden of keeping promises at any cost. Tags center on social leverage, streetwise knowledge, and a mentor figure who might still be listening in.

These examples illustrate how Masks can be both emblematic and deeply personal. The Mythos and Enigma layers ensure that even a shared concept can evolve in wildly different directions depending on who plays it and which myths the group chooses to amplify.

## Campaign seeds and play tips
To help you get started, here are a few seed ideas that you can drop into a game night. They are designed to spark both investigation and mythic escalation, and they work nicely with the core book guidance on pacing and mood:

- Seed 1: The City Sleepwalking. People in a district are experiencing dreams that bleed into waking life. The team must trace a mythic thread back to a neglected shrine beneath an abandoned subway tunnel before the dreams erase a neighborhood home by home.
- Seed 2: The Sunken Theater. A closed down theater starts showing live performances that are rumored to reveal a character s deepest secrets to the audience. The team must decide if knowing the truth is a blessing or a trap, and whether to reveal the ultimate myth behind the performances or to crush it before it goes public.
- Seed 3: The Stamp Collector. A villain who collects symbolic stamps from people s lives appears, and each stamp unlocks moments from a myth the person embodies. The players must navigate a network of personal legends while trying to stop a ritual that could rewrite the city s history.

If you are a GM looking for quick starts, these seeds are designed to be flexible. The city should react to the players, and the Masks should feel like believable people with real fears and ambitions. The best sessions emerge when the players start to see themselves as the characters in legends rather than mere readers of a myth.

## What the book does well and where it might need tweaks
City of Mist excels at mood and personal storytelling. It gives you a framework that makes the city feel alive and the Masks feel meaningful. It rewards players who invest in their myths and in the relationships they have with other Masks and with the city itself. The cooperative storytelling approach is a real strength, particularly for groups who want to experiment with tone and pacing.

If you prefer heavy combat crunch or puzzle solving with strict rules, you may need to adjust the expectations or bring in houserules. The book does not try to cram the city into a single mold; it invites you to sculpt it. You may find that the narrative engine shines most when you let the Maskss have their own agendas and let the Mythos surface in moments of decision rather than in violent showdowns. The flexibility is a strength, but it requires a table that is comfortable with a collaborative, evolving story rather than a rigid plan.

## Final verdict and recommendations
City of Mist Core Book offers a distinctive and highly enjoyable experience. It cabbages the noir mood into a mythic urban fantasy that is both approachable for newcomers and deeply satisfying for veterans who want to explore personal legends and city level mysteries. The art, layout, and writing are strong, and the mechanics support cinematic storytelling without weighting you down with extraneous rules. For groups that enjoy a blend of investigation, mythic moments, and character driven drama, this book is a must add to the shelf. It is not the right tool for every table, but for the right table it feels almost inevitable in how it elevates ordinary life into mythic possibility.

## Final thoughts on play experience
The joy of City of Mist comes from seeing the city respond to the players as a living partner in the story. The masks begin as a concept and grow into a living myth that the city itself seems to push forward. The dynamic between public identity and private fear keeps every scene tense and emotionally engaging. You will find yourself laughing at the absurdity of a mythic twist in a brutal stakeout and leaning forward when a reveal lands with the weight of inevitability. It is a game that plays best when players consent to a vibe that oscillates between noir grit and mythic wonder, and where every clue is a thread that unravels another myth.
