## The Rules: A Quick Refresher (With Add-Ons)
Million Dollars, But... The Game is built around prompts that start with a tantalizing premise plus a twist. In the base game, players propose an amount of money and then the panel negotiates outcomes based on the premise. The Community Pack introduces more aggressive prompts, some of which are explicitly designed for longer sessions and more intense negotiations. The general cycle remains the same: reveal a prompt, players argue why their offered scenario would be the most devastatingly funny or morally acceptable, and the judge or the group decides who has the best pitch.

The Community Pack adds a few optional rules that can be toggled on to tilt the balance toward speed or toward theatricality. These include:

- Rapid-Fire Rounds: Shorten thinking time and encourage impulsive pitches.
- Champion of Chaos: A rotating chair figure who can veto or empower a particular type of prompt mid-round.
- Thematic Bundles: Pre-assembled mini-sets of prompts built around themes like pop culture, workplace, or fantasy realms.

In practice, these rules do what expansions should do: make the game feel fresh without turning it into a completely new beast. If you’re bringing this to a game night with strangers, I’d suggest sticking to Rapid-Fire for the first hour to set expectations, then unlock Thematic Bundles for the later rounds when people have already loosened up.
