## Strategy and Tactics: From Black Hats to White Lies
### For the Black Team
Black players thrive on calculating risk and seizing moments when the room’s attention is elsewhere. Use the expanded card pool to bait opponents into committing to half-truths that you can later twist. Remember: confidence is a weapon; misdirection is a shield. In practice, you’ll want to seed a few high-stakes plays early, force others into over-commitment, and watch the room fracture into competing narratives.

### For the White Team
White players should embrace steadiness, misdirection via calm negotiation, and controlled reveals. The aim is to build a quiet fortress of logic around your plans, while others chase tangents. With two more players, you’ll need to be mindful of dead-end negotiations: don’t chase every shiny card if it drags you away from a solid endgame strategy. The White side rewards patience, clarity, and a well-timed bluff that sells as a harmless datapoint rather than a calculated trap.

### The bargaining tier: who to trust, who to tease, who to vanquish
The social algebra becomes more complex with two extra players. You’ll want to establish microalliances that can be easily dissolved when a new card or revelation surfaces. The key is to balance trust with suspicion: give away just enough to keep people talking, but not so much that your own plan becomes obvious. And yes, it’s totally appropriate to roast your friends mid-negotiation, as long as you keep it on-brand and fun.
