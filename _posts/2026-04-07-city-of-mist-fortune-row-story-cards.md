## Overview
City of Mist: Fortune Row – Story Cards takes the moody, neon-noir vibe of the City of Mist RPG and distills it into a deck of prompts, hooks, and short scene beats that a GM can drop into a city already brimming with mythic identity. If the base game is a slow-burning detective novel with vigilantes and gods masquerading as ordinary people, Fortune Row is the handbook that hands you a pocketful of plot twists and optional side quests you can toss into the air like a handful of confetti at a villain’s reveal.

In this review, we’ll unpack what Fortune Row brings to your table, how it fits with the core City of Mist engine, and whether the cards will spice up your sessions without turning your city into a chaotic thunderclap of miscellany. Spoiler: it’s not perfect, but it’s surprisingly useful and genuinely fun when you lean into its strengths.

## What is Fortune Row, exactly?
Fortune Row is a deck of Story Cards designed to be used as a supplemental prompt generator for sessions focused on a particular area of the city—the so-called Fortune Row itself. The card set leans into the game’s signature blend of myth, rumor, and grit, presenting players with miniature micro-arcs, NPC beats, and situational prompts that can be slotted into a long-running arc or used to spark a one-off scenario.

Think of Fortune Row as a box of fast-burning ideas you can thread into: a lead that always seems to curve away from the protagonist, a mysterious benefactor with a questionable moral compass, or a rumor that folds the city’s past into its present with a neat “you thought this was about you, but it’s about the city” twist.

As with many City of Mist products, the deck expects you to be comfortable with ambiguity and a little chaos. The cards don’t spackle over the city’s underbelly; they invite you to poke it with a data stick, listen to the echo, and decide what myth you want to pull into the light.

## What’s in the deck?
Fortune Row’s card lineup breaks down into several overlapping archetypes, each designed to slot into a game session with minimal friction:

- Story Seeds: Short prompts that can launch a scene or a micro-arc. They often hinge on a local rumor, a memorable locale, or a minor myth that’s grown large enough to affect daily life.
- NPC Sparks: Vignettes and personality hooks for key figures who could become allies, antagonists, or mirrors to the PCs. These are useful when you need a new face to bounce off a PC without committing to a full character write-up.
- Plot Twists: Cards that nudge the session toward unexpected revelations or reversals. They’re most effective when you’ve got momentum but want a dramatic jolt rather than a full rewrite.
- Relationship Beats: Tiny prompts that tease connections between PCs and NPCs—perfect for weaving backstory into the current run without forced exposition.
- Mythic Ties: Cards that anchor events to the game’s mythos, helping you thread the “Powered by Myth” motif into the current scene rather than letting it drift away in a detective’s coffee cup.

In short, Fortune Row is not a campaign bible; it’s a toolbox. You’ll grab a few tools at a time, depending on whether your table is hungry for a new mystery, a street-level complication, or a rooftop monologue that turns the whole arc on its axis.

## Mechanics and how to use them
The deck is designed to be low-luss, high-utility. Here’s a practical framework for dropping Fortune Row into sessions:

1) Prep light, play heavy. Before play, shuffle the deck and keep it near the GM screen. You’re not forced to pull a card every session, but when you need a spark, a single draw can yield a complete micro-scene beat.
2) Draw with purpose. Use a Story Seed to start a scene. If the seed immediately spawns a plot twist, that’s a win—your players are now following a path that feels organic rather than “handed to them.”
3) Tie to mythos. If a card references a Mythos moment or a city’s legend, you’ve got a ready-made hook to bring a divine or spectral angle into a standard investigation.
4) Use NPC Sparks to flavor a scene. Rather than rewriting a full NPC, grab a Spark and drop it into the scene as a conversation catalyst or a conflicting motive.
5) Respect the tone. Fortune Row shines when you lean into the noir atmosphere. Cards that feel a little too “hand-wavy” for a given night can be reserved for a future session.

The deck plays nicely with standard City of Mist mechanics. Because Fortune Row is designed to be additive rather than replacement, it won’t radically alter the base rules or dice economy. Instead, it fills in gaps and provides quick, thematically coherent prompts that remind players why the city feels alive when watching it through the glow of a streetlamp and a pair of rain-slicked shoes.

## Design quality: art, layout, and production
From a design standpoint, Fortune Row strikes a balance between readability and flavor. The cards use the familiar noir-ish color palette—the teal and purple of city myths colliding with a rainy night motif—so the deck looks coherent on the table and doesn’t demand an art history degree to decipher. Card typography prioritizes legibility: the title is prominent, with the description set in a lighter weight, airy enough to be scanned in mid-scene without losing concentration.

The card stock feels sturdy, with enough flex to survive a few table tosses during a heated moment but not so stiff that shuffling becomes a chore. The finish is standard, not glossy enough to cause glare in studio lighting, which is a small merciful mercy for streamers and in-person players alike. If you’ve got a preference for a particular aesthetic, Fortune Row’s art leans into that signature City of Mist vibe rather than chasing a brand-new look, which is a smart move for cohesion across the product line.

Layout-wise, the deck does its job: each card has a succinct prompt, a couple of keywords to jog memory, and a single sentence that orients you toward a scene. It’s not a room-scale novel; it’s a cue card deck, and the designers understand that constraint well. If you’re a GM who likes to improvise around a tight set of prompts, you’ll feel right at home here.

## Thematic fit with City of Mist
Fortune Row slots neatly into the game’s mold: urban myth meets detective fiction, with secrets dressed in masks and the city as a character with a splash of cosmic weather. The deck’s prompts aren’t a “complete arc” so much as a “nudge toward a thematic moment.” That design decision mirrors the game’s core structure: players chase legends while the city itself exerts pressure, shaping outcomes in ways players won’t expect.

A frequent pitfall when borrowing micro-arcs is that they can feel out of scale—too big for a single session or too small to matter after two plots twist. Fortune Row does a fair job avoiding both extremes. Most prompts are lean enough to fit into a single session’s tempo, but robust enough to ripple into future scenes if a GM wants to plant a seed that grows in Week 3 or Week 5. If your group likes city-level threads (gossip chains, power brokers, and mythic factions) rather than isolated investigations, Fortune Row pays off handsomely.

## Tactics and practical tips for using Fortune Row
- Build around a single anchor. Pick one or two Story Seeds that feel thematically compelling for your party. Use them to drive multiple scenes, letting the players discover connections as they progress.
- Use NPC Sparks as party accelerants. If a player forms a strong attachment to a spark, lean into it. Sparks are ideal for loosening the table’s dynamic—someone suddenly has a motive that aligns or collides with the PCs’ goals.
- Layer Mythos for payoffs. When a Mythos tie shows up, don’t treat it as a one-off. Plant a little mythic residue in the city’s architecture, signage, or rumors. The payoff will feel earned when the party starts tracing more than a string of clues.
- Let a Plot Twist reset expectations. If the session starts turning predictable, consult a Plot Twist card to reorient the stakes, perhaps revealing a hidden ally or a betrayal that shifts the scene’s even balance.
- Use Fortune Row to flavor downtime. The deck isn’t only for action scenes; it can generate rumor mills during investigations, social encounters, or even in-between sessions, when the city’s mood thickens like fog at dawn.

If you’re navigating a long arc, Fortune Row’s prompts can function as memory nodes—fixtures you revisit, tweak, and remix as the city evolves. That adaptability is the deck’s secret weapon: it’s not a fixed script, it’s a palette of ideas you can repaint night by night.

## Value, pricing, and accessibility
Price-wise, Fortune Row sits in that sweet spot where a GM can justify purchasing a deck for ongoing use without breaking the party’s budget. It isn’t a “one-time special edition” splurge; it’s a tool that can be drawn upon across multiple sessions. If you’re a group that runs weekly campaigns or if you GM for a convention table, Fortune Row pays for itself in the first handful of sessions—if you actively employ it, that is.

Accessibility is decent. The prompts are short enough to grasp in the middle of a scene, yet evocative enough to spark discussion and creative thinking. The real test is whether you’ll remember to use the deck after the first two sessions. If your table tends to devolve into “talking heads with watches,” Fortune Row can serve as a reminder to switch gears, refresh the tempo, and re-ignite the city’s energy.

## Comparisons: where Fortune Row sits among City of Mist supplements
Compared to a full campaign supplement, Fortune Row is a lighter lift—less world-building, more spark-laden prompts. It works well alongside other City of Mist products like arc guides, faction sheets, and neighborhood rosters. If you own multiple decks or modules, Fortune Row stacks nicely with the others by providing city-specific micro-arcs that can weave into a broader, multi-arc experience.

If you’re deciding between Fortune Row and a more narrative-heavy supplement, ask yourself how your group plays. Do you prefer “scene prompts that allow player choice to drive the investigation”? Fortune Row leans toward that, with plenty of room for players to interpret and react. If you want more explicit beat-by-beat structure, you might pair it with a longer arc supplement and treat Fortune Row as the “tangent threads” that enrich the central plot.

## Final verdict
Fortune Row is not a revolutionary expansion, but it is a well-executed one. The prompts feel thematically consistent with City of Mist’s noir-mythic vibe and are crafted to be both flexible and immediately useful at the table. It rewards a GM who leans into improvisation—someone who’s comfortable weaving a street rumor into a decked-out scene rather than trying to force a blockbuster into a single night.

The card stock and production quality are solid enough to justify the purchase on tactile grounds alone, and the art style sits in harmony with the game’s aesthetic. It’s not the kind of supplement that will remake your city, but it can help you magnify its mood, push characters toward meaningful decisions, and accelerate scene pacing without losing the GM’s voice.

If you’re running a City of Mist campaign and want a reliable, quick-hit source of inspiration that won’t derail your narrative momentum, Fortune Row is a strong candidate. It’s particularly friendly to GMs who favor urban legends as anchors and who like to pepper sessions with small, character-driven breakthroughs rather than all-out action climaxes.

### Final recommendation
- Use Fortune Row as a monthly injection of atmosphere and momentum.
- Pair it with your favorite neighborhood fluff and let the city’s myths spill into the PCs’ lives.
- Do not treat the cards as a script—treat them as a set of prompts that invites exploration and improvisation.
- If the party wants a brisk, engaging session with a noir bite and a myth twist, Fortune Row delivers.
