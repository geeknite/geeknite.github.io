## Maintenance and Care: Keeping the Two-Sided Magic Alive

Maintenance is the boring but essential part of any rug romance. The great news is that these rugs don’t require a PhD in textile science to keep looking fresh. Here’s the practical care plan:

- Regular Vacuuming: Use a low-suction setting and a brushless attachment to avoid tangling or pulling the fibers. Vacuuming removes the everyday dirt and pet hair that can dull the color on either side.
- Spot Cleaning: Treat spills promptly with a mild detergent and cold water. Blot rather than rub to avoid pushing stains deeper into the weave. For stubborn spots, test any cleaning solution on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Stain Management: For oily stains, a light dusting of baking soda before vacuuming can help break down oils. Remember: minimal moisture is your friend with woven textiles.
- Airing and Rotation: If you notice a faint “one-side-only” bias in your room’s lighting, rotate the rug every so often to even out wear and keep both sides looking vibrant.
- Washing: Most woven scatter rugs of this size aren’t meant for machine washing, but always check the care label. If machine washing is allowed, follow the manufacturer’s instructions precisely to prevent fiber distortion or color bleed.

Maintenance is basically adult-level common sense dressed up as a slightly boring chore—worth it for the payoff of a room that feels deliberate rather than ad-hoc. And since you can flip sides, you effectively double the life of this tiny decor investment without sacrificing style.
