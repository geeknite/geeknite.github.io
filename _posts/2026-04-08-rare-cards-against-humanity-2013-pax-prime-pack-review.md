## Background: Cards Against Humanity and Event Packs
CAH began its life as a party game that thrived on shock value and quick wit. The core mechanic is simple: players fill in blanks with outrageous cards to maximize ridiculousness. The game exploded in popularity because it turned the social awkwardness of punchlines into a collaborative performance. The 2013 PAX Prime Pack sits squarely in the latter category: a single event with a footprint so small that many collectors treat it like a fossil.

## Authenticity and Verification: How to spot a legit gem
With rarity comes responsibility. Here are the practical tips you’ll want to keep in mind if you ever get your hands on a 2013 PAX Prime Pack:

- Serial or Certificate: If your edition includes a certificate of authenticity or a serial number, compare it with other known examples. Consistency matters more than hitting an exact number; if you see a mismatch in a serial scheme that’s widely believed, you’re probably looking at a repro or a boot.
- Packaging Cohesion: The box, interior padding, and any seals should appear cohesive. A modern reprint would likely have differences in weight, finish, and font usage. If something looks off, approach with a healthy dose of skepticism.
- Card Stock and Finish: Prototypes or limited prints often use a slightly different gloss or texture. If your cards feel significantly different from typical CAH stock, that’s a clue, not a red flag—just a clue worth noting for authenticity checks.
- Community Verification: The CAH community is fantastic at cross-checking subtle markers. Look for forum discussions, collector groups, or posts by reputable bloggers (including the elusive secret handshake of our own Geeknite posts) to validate your copy’s provenance.

The lesson here is simple: rarity is a feature, not a guarantee of value. Collectors today are as excited by the story behind the pack as by the ledger price it commands.
