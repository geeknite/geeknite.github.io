## Cleaning, Care, and Long-Term Love
Caring for a rug is a dance between routine maintenance and the occasional spa day. Here’s the practical, no-nonsense routine you can adopt:

- Vacuum weekly with a standard setting. The pile holds up well to regular suction, and the subtle pattern helps hide incoming dust until it’s time for a refresh.
- For spills: blot, don’t rub. The goal is to lift the liquid without pressing it further into the fibers. A mild detergent solution can be used on minor stains; follow with a rinse and blot dry.
- Deep cleaning: If you want to give the rug a refresh, you can machine wash on a delicate cycle with cold water and a gentle detergent. Air-dry flat away from direct heat until fully dry.
- Drying: Do not tumble-dry. The heat can warp the pile and you’ll end up with a warped sense of fashion for your floor.

### Stain removal playbook
- Fresh stains: act quickly, blot gently, and apply a mild cleaning solution if needed.
- Red wine: dab, blot, treat with a mild detergent solution, then rinse—rinse is the key to preventing a wine-stain love story from forming.
- Mud: let it dry, brush off the crust, then vacuum. Your shoes appreciate a dry floor after rain; your rug appreciates the same with more fashion.
