## Practicalities: care, placement, and daily life with the rug
A rug is a practical investment, not a pet project. Here are the realities of living with a 25x49 cotton rug in a busy home:

### Cleaning and maintenance
Cotton rugs are approachable when it comes to upkeep. Here are sane cleaning guidelines:
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to remove loose dirt and pet hair. Avoid aggressive beater bars that can pull threads.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Don’t rub; you want to avoid pushing liquid deeper into the fibers.
- For light stains, a mild detergent mixed with water works well. Test in a small inconspicuous area first to ensure color retention.
- For deep cleaning, many cotton rugs can be hand washed or machine washed on a gentle cycle, depending on the construction. Always check the rug's care label if available and consider air-drying flat to avoid distortion.
- Avoid direct, prolonged sunlight to prevent color fading. If possible, rotate periodically to keep wear even.

### Durability and pet-friendly notes
Cotton stands up well to daily use in most households. It isn’t a heavy-duty option for a muddy entryway, but in a normal living room or studio, it’s resilient enough for kids, cats, and the occasional dog misstep. If you have a particularly rumbustious pet, consider a rug pad to reduce slippage and add a touch of cushion underfoot.

### Placement do’s and don’ts
- Do align the rug with your furniture’s dominant lines. A rug that mirrors the room’s layout looks cohesive and intentional.
- Don’t cram the rug beneath heavy furniture so that it wrinkles or curls at the edges. Give it room to breathe.
- Do consider a rug pad if you have hardwood or tile floors. It improves grip and extends the rug’s life, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Don’t forget the fringe. A floating fringe can degrade faster if stepped on repeatedly; keep it clean and untangled with occasional gentle brushing.

## Maintenance mindset: four weeks to a more confident living space
A rug is not a set-and-forget purchase. It requires attention to keep looking fresh and feeling comfortable. Here is a practical maintenance mindset that fits a busy schedule:
- Week 1: vacuum gently, smooth out any wrinkles or folds, and assess whether the fringe needs grooming.
- Week 2: address a small stain with a gentle blot and a mild detergent. If the stain persists, consider a spot treatment that matches the rug’s fabric color.
- Week 3: rotate the rug’s position if possible. A little rotation keeps wear even and prevents one area from fading faster due to sunlight or traffic.
- Week 4: inspect the edges for fraying and trim stray threads with scissors; avoid pulling on loose fabrics to prevent unraveling.
