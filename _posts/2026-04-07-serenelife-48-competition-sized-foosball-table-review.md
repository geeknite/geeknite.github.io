### Maintenance and Durability: Keeping the Table in Peak Form
Cleaning is straightforward. Wipe down the playing surface after games to remove fingerprints and smudges. If you spill something sticky, a damp cloth with mild soap will do; nothing fancy needed. Periodically, you might want to check the screws and ensure the legs remain firmly attached. If you have kids or enthusiastic guests, you’ll appreciate the absence of any plastic parts that feel flimsy. So far, the build quality holds up to enthusiastic play and occasional clumsy dives for the ball.

Lubrication of rods is optional for most households, but if you’re chasing the most buttery-smooth action, you can apply a light silicone-based lubricant sparingly to the rod sleeves. Do not overdo it; you do not want a slip-and-fall scenario that ends in a dramatic fall over the coffee table. Moderation is the friend of foosball players.

Storage and maintenance in a home environment is a non-issue for most spaces, though if you live in an area with extreme humidity, keep the table away from doors or windows that introduce drafts and moisture. The surface is resilient, but like all wooden/plastic composites, it’s best to keep it out of direct sunlight for extended periods to prevent warping or color fading over many years of use.
