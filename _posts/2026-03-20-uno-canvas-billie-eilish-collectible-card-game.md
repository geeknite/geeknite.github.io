## How to Play: Quick Start
If you know UNO, the Quick Start is friendly. If you are new to UNO, you can learn quickly and still appreciate the art. The rules are basically the same:

- Each player starts with seven cards.
- On your turn you must match the card by color or number, or play a Wild card to choose a color.
- If you cannot play, you draw a card.
- The first player to get rid of all their cards wins the round, shouting UNO at the top of their lungs if they can pronounce the last color.

The canvas edition does not drastically alter the core rules. The changes you will notice are minor and mostly affect the social experience rather than the fundamental mathematics of the deck. In practice, you will spend more time admiring the art before you proceed with your plan to win. If you want to tweak the game for a more salon-style gaming experience, you can add a small set of house rules that lean into the mood. For example, you might introduce a rule that when a player goes out with a hand of all one color, they must describe the mood of a Billie Eilish track in a short paragraph. It is silly and fun, and it keeps the energy high without turning UNO into a debate club.
