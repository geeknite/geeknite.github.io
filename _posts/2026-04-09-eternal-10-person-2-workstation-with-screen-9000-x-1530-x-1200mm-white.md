Dimensions and What They Mean

- Length: 9,000 mm (that’s 29.5 feet for those who still think in feet and inches). Yes, you could legally park a small sedan along this thing if you had a parking-lot-sized office.
- Depth: 1,530 mm (~5 feet). This isn’t a coffee table; it’s a battlefield where cable management meets gravity and wins.
- Height: 1,200 mm (~3.9 feet). The top surface is at a comfortable working height for most people, assuming your knees don’t mind a tiny cardio routine every time you reach for a USB-C dongle.

As a design note, the white finish is not purely white in the sense of paper pristine. It’s more like a white that has learned to forgive coffee stains and marker doodles with grace. The edges are chamfered to reduce snag hazards, but if you’re the type who tests things with sledgehammers, you should probably start with lower-impact tests.

Cable Management and Maintenance

The cable management system is where the Eternal earns extra stars. It’s not perfect—no system is—but it’s among the best you’ll find in a desk this large. There are multiple channels, trunking options, and a spine that gracefully routes data and power to 10 stations without turning your floor into a tangle of ethernet spaghetti. Expect some cable fatigue after a couple of months as you reconfigure teams and devices; you’ll still be able to salvage a surprisingly clean look with a bit of planning.

There’s a small maintenance cost: dust and debris can accumulate in the screen housing, so a quick quarterly wipe-down is recommended. And yes, you’ll want to test for heat buildup if you’ve got the screen on full brightness for eight hours; the last thing you need is a thermal meltdown that logs into your Slack and starts boasting about my fans are cool.

Assembly and Maintenance: The Real-World Grind

This is where the rubber meets the road. It’s one thing to talk about the aesthetics; it’s another to actually assemble a 9-meter desk. The good news is that most assembly tasks are straightforward; the floor plan for the module aligns with pretty standard L and T joints. The bad news is that you will need help; it’s not a one-person job unless you have the muscle mass of a small mascot and the patience of a monk. The manufacturer provides step-by-step instructions, with color-coded parts and a helpful YouTube video that demonstrates the assembly process, along with helpful tips for cable routing and bracket alignment. Expect a day or two of physical labor and a few moments of I swear I saw a missing screw panic before you realize it’s a misplacement of a spacer.

Maintenance is straightforward: wipe-downs, check for loose bolts, and confirm that the integrated screen’s brightness is tuned for the room’s lighting. If a panel starts to squeak, apply a touch of silicone lubricant to the joints and you’ll be back to normal. The key thing is to schedule regular checks so nothing falls apart during your company’s most important quarterly review.

Connectivity, Software, and the Human Factor
