## Care and Longevity: Keeping It Fresh
### Vacuuming Routine
A large surface like this benefits from a two-pass approach across different axes. If your vacuum has a beater bar, keep it on a gentle setting to avoid abrasive wear on fibers. The goal is to lift debris without flattening plush zones. A weekly zigzag pattern works nicely for most homes; you can also do a more scientific “grid pass” to ensure even coverage in high-traffic lanes.

### Stain Management
With big rugs come big stain zones. Treat spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing. Quick action minimizes set-in challenges, especially with synthetic fibers. For kids or pets, a color-safe stain remover designed for synthetic fibers is best. Always spot-test in an inconspicuous corner to verify colorfastness before committing to a full clean. When in doubt, a professional cleaning every 12–18 months can extend life without sacrificing texture or color.

### Pet and Hair Hurdles
Furry cohabitants love large flat surfaces for lounging and shedding. The Martha Rug is light enough to move for a deep clean and resilient enough to handle a spray of fur with minimal effort. A lint roller or a vacuum with a brush head makes hair removal straightforward. In homes with long-haired pets, you may find yourself vacuuming more often, but the payoff is a consistently fresh room feeling rather than tumbleweed fuzz.

### Seasonal and Climate Considerations
Humidity and temperature can subtly influence fiber feel and appearance. A well-made rug will retain shape better than cheaper options amid humidity swings. In warm, dry climates, airflow helps fibers breathe and maintain crisp edges. In humid regions, a dehumidifier can help maintain fiber health and reduce the risk of musty corners. Martha is designed to be resilient, but proactive care keeps it looking sharp year-round.
