## What is Fortune Row, and why does it matter?

Fortune Row is a district-forward expansion that treats a single urban zone as the character to beat, bless, or break. In City of Mist terms, Fortune Row is a living tapestry of masks, myths, and human fragility—where storefronts aren’t simply a front for a crime boss, but potentially a front for a god who pretends to own that corner shop. The supplement emphasizes how place shapes power and how small decisions ripple into legendary consequences.

- A compact, highly playable district with its own vibe and tone.
- New factions that can align with or oppose a player’s Mask—your core identity that drives the mystery solving and combat style.
- A set of “scene seeds” and “investigation threads” designed to be dropped into ongoing campaigns or used to bootstrap one-night affairs with maximum noir impact.
- Expanded GM tools: encounter guides, pacing chips, and cross-linking mechanics that keep the mythic influence consistent without devolving into a random myth soup.

There’s a clear design throughline: Fortune Row wants you to feel like you’re walking one foot in a crime caper and the other in a mythic masquerade. It’s not a single myth or a single crime; it’s a city block that hums with multiple patterns at once, a place where a single lead could turn into a season-long arc or into a character-defining moment for a player whose Mask just realized it’s now wearing a different face.

### The cover-to-cover experience
The art direction stays true to City of Mist’ s hallmark noir-glam aesthetic, mixing moody shadows, glowing sigils, and everyday props that become portals to myth. The layout is clean and readable, with plenty of space for GM notes and player reminders. It’s designed to be thumb-table friendly: you can skim the page, grab an idea, and get back to the table without getting lost in the aesthetics.

The book starts by anchoring Fortune Row in the city’s larger mythic framework, ensuring you don’t have to re-learn the core rules to use the supplement. If you’re flipping directly from the Core Book, you’ll find several cross-references that feel organic rather than forced: the new factions echo the standalone factions from the core, and the new Masks echo—but don’t parody—the existing archetypes. In short, Fortune Row respects the spine of City of Mist while giving it a fresh spine-tingle.

### What fortune-row adds to the core system
Fortune Row introduces a handful of substantive additions that feel both practical and flavorful.

- New district stat blocks and a local calendar: The district has its own rhythm, traffic patterns, and myths that cycle with the seasons. This gives you a ready-made backdrop for investigations and a natural cadence for episodes.
- Factions and loyalties: You’ll meet rival groups that pull at players’ Masks in different directions. These factions provide natural antagonists and potential allies, plus a reliable source of hooks for long-form campaigns.
- Tools for the Keeper: Scene templates, pacing advice, and GM tips for weaving urban intrigue with mythic threat. It’s the difference between “you meet a monster” and “you realize the city itself has learned your name.”
- Player options and new Gear: Several new assets and relationships for PCs who want to expand their social reach in Fortune Row, including a few “favored spots” that can serve as safe havens or dangerous traps.
- Narrative prompts and hooks: A library of micro-scenarios designed to slot into a bigger arc or to run as a stand-alone session with minimal prep.

The emphasis is on punchy, narrative-driven content rather than dry exposition. Fortune Row presents you with a living map, where each street corner offers a hint, a hazard, or a doorway to mythic mischief. It’s not about adding more pages of rules; it’s about giving you more ways to tell a story that feels personal and large at the same time.

## The district: Fortune Row as a character

If you’re familiar with the genre, you’ll recognize Fortune Row’s goal: make the city itself feel like a character with opinions, moods, and a grudge against your party. The supplement treats the district as a composite hero, with its own myths, power dynamics, and a cast of locals who may become either your allies or your obstacles. It’s a common trick in City of Mist to see the city as a living partner in the story; Fortune Row leans into that, giving the district a robust personality that players can sense on both a macro and micro level.

- Local color with mythic resonance: Each block or storefront carries a vibe that can be leveraged in scenes. When a character interacts with a café, the place isn’t merely atmospheric—it’s a node in a mythic network, with clues winking at the players when you least expect it.
- Characters that breathe: NPCs aren’t cardboard cutouts of mobsters and mystics. They’re people who reflect, distort, and magnify the Masks players wear. This makes social dynamics feel meaningful, not just cosmetic.
- A grid for mysteries: The district provides a modular approach to mysteries. You can start with a single clue that spirals into an interwoven set of plot threads, all connected through Fortune Row’s mythic texture.

The payoff here is a game that’s more than “investigation + fight.” It’s a living, breathing setting where a wrong choice in a scene can tilt the entire neighborhood toward salvation or ruin. Fortune Row wants you to feel the city has agency—and that you’ve got a chance to influence it without having your character lose the ground they’re built on.

### Mechanics: how the new content hooks into Core Book rules
City of Mist uses a mechanics framework built around two core stats—Mythos and Logos—and a pool of six-sided dice used to resolve actions and clashes with a bit of narrative flair. Fortune Row fits into this scheme primarily by expanding the scope of scenes and adding locale-based modifiers that can tilt dice results, raise stakes, or alter the conditions of a confrontation.

- Locale modifiers: Fortune Row introduces district-based modifiers that can enhance or hinder certain actions depending on where you are. For example, a nightclub with mythic undercurrents might give a temporary edge to a character with a Charm Mask but make it harder for someone relying on a Steely Resolve Mask to act unobtrusively.
- Relationship mechanics: New “relation” tokens track the player’s ties to local factions, informants, or patrons. These relationships can be spent to gain clues, pull favors, or navigate bureaucratic labyrinths that block the way to the truth.
- Scene seeds and escalation ladders: The book provides ready-to-run scenes with suggested escalation steps. It’s a practical approach for GMs who want a clear structure: Hook > Complication > Reveal > Decision > Consequence.
- Encounter pacing: The supplement includes pacing guidelines tailored to Fortune Row’s rhythm. You’ll get cues for when to shift from noir tension to mythic reveals and back, a balance that helps a session feel cinematic rather than chopped up into discrete acts.

If you’re meticulous about keeping track of Masks and who owns which corner of the city, Fortune Row gives you more levers to pull without forcing a new, heavy rulebook into your table. The practical effect is clearer scenes, richer NPCs, and a city that responds with a sense of inevitability instead of a shrug.

### How to run Fortune Row with existing campaigns
- Start with the district’s mood: If your current arc leans toward gritty crime, Fortune Row’s mythic layer can add a satisfying twist without derailing the tone. If your group loves mythic intrigue, Fortune Row helps escalate that energy with location-based mechanics.
- Tie Masks to the locale: Consider which Masks feel natural in Fortune Row’s contexts. A character who embodies a “Commerce Mask” might thrive in Fortune Row’s marketplace of secrets, while a “Rogue Mask” may find the district’s alleyways and backrooms perfect for clandestine plots.
- Use faction dynamics as plot engines: The factions aren’t just antagonists; they’re engines for long-form storytelling. A faction’s bargaining power can ebb and flow across sessions, driving player choices and reconfiguring alliances.
- Keep the scale human: Fortune Row shines when you keep the stakes personal—someone’s favorite diner could be at risk, a beloved informant might be compromised, or a mythic force could threaten a neighborly bond that players value.

### Design decisions and potential caveats
No supplement is perfect for every table, and Fortune Row has its trade-offs. Here are a few considerations to keep in mind:
- Pacing risk: The district’s layer of myths can tempt a Keeper to stack escalating reveals. The best fortune is to pace it like a tasteful melody—not a drum solo that drowns out the players’ voices.
- Information density: Fortune Row gives you a lot to latch onto. If your group prefers lean sessions, pick and choose seeds and NPCs to avoid information overload.
- Compatibility: If you’re using house rules or a heavily customized Core Book, some of Fortune Row’s assumptions may feel a touch inconsistent. The remedy is to treat it as a modular toolkit rather than a mandate; borrow what you love and adapt what you don’t.

## The big picture: is Fortune Row worth your time and table space?

In Geeknite’s estimation, Fortune Row is a strong addition to City of Mist’s Core Book for groups who want a more anchored, place-based experience. It delivers a vivid district with its own rules, conflicts, and mythic underpinnings, while still speaking the same language as the core book. The supplement shines when used to punctuate a campaign’s high-stakes moments with a city-wide texture that feels real and mythic at the same time.
