## Maintenance and Longevity: Cleaning, Care, and Carefree Moments

Wood finishes love to be cleaned with a microfiber cloth, and perhaps a damp wipe now and then—just not a soaking bath for the entire unit. Spills should be blotted rather than wiped with a drenched cloth; the goal is to avoid warping or swelling of the veneer. For long-term longevity, avoid direct sunlight for extended periods, which could cause color fading in a way that makes your desk look less like a design choice and more like a neglected time capsule.

If you’re worried about stains, a quick application of furniture-safe cleaner can help preserve the finish. The result is a piece that continues to look good after months of daily use. In addition, the flexibility of the wood-color finish means you won’t worry about it clashing with future desk upgrades or a redecoration binge that involves moving to a different wall color.
