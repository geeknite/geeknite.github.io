## Practical tips for setup
- Plan cable runs before you fully mount the desktop. It saves you from redoing the channels after you realize you forgot the power brick you need for the second monitor.
- Use silicone cable clips along the underside of the desk to keep cables from snagging on your chair wheels.
- Consider adding a monitor arm with a heavy-duty clamp if you want to maximize desk space without sacrificing stability.
- Don’t forget a good anti fatigue mat if you plan to stand for long coding sessions; the math of standing while coding is real and the desk isn’t going to do your calves any favors by itself.

## Buying guide: what to check before you buy
Before you press that buy button, here are practical checks to save you regret later:
- Measure your space: check the width and the door clearance so you don’t get a beautiful desk stuck in the hallway. Also consider how you’ll move it into place.
- Weigh your gear: ensure the desk’s load rating covers your monitor setup, desktop PC, and a few extraneous items. If you run a studio space with audio gear, factor in the additional weight of studio monitors.
- Confirm the height standard: 29-30 inches works for most people, but if you’re unusually tall or short, you may want an adjustable option.
- Check cable management options: if you have a lot of power bricks, a dedicated under-desk tray or a vertical cable management system will keep the surface clean and accessible.
- Consider accessories: monitor arms, keyboard trays, drawer modules, and LED lighting all pair well with this frame, but ensure your mounting points won’t conflict with the desk’s bracing.

## Troubleshooting and common issues
- A slight wobble on carpet? This is usually solved by tightening the leveling feet or adding a small plywood shim under the desk foot. 
- Cable clutter behind the desk? Reassess your cable routes and switch to a slimmer power strip. 
- Surface not perfectly flat? If your floor is warped, you can adjust the leg height slightly or add a small under-surface pad in the problem corner.
- Monitor alignment drifting? Re-check the monitor arms or ensure you’ve tightened all the mounting points securely.
