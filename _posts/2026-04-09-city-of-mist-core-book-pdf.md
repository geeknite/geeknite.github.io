## The core system and what makes City of Mist tick

City of Mist runs on a blended engine that the book (and subsequent supplements) calls Mythos, Logos, and Masks. The three strands fuse into a system that supports heroic, noir, and investigative play without forcing you into a single genre silo. In practice, that means your investigator isn’t just a “fighter with a cool name” or a “mage who loves libraries.” They are a complex collage of legends, personal beliefs, and a stubborn refusal to stop until the truth is messy enough to be interesting.

### Mythos, Logos, and Masks explained, with practical flavor
- Mythos: The inner storytelling engine. This is what your character believes in—faith, legends, obsessions. The PDF explains how to translate a belief into mechanical benefits, pushing your character’s actions toward thematic outcomes rather than just “I roll a die to hit.” The trick is to let your Mythos guide your decisions and to let it collide with the city’s realpolitik in dramatic, story-fueled ways.
- Logos: The rational counterpoint to Mythos. Logos is about the character’s personal philosophy, the means by which they justify actions, and the logical structure that makes them believable. The PDF does a decent job of keeping Logos from turning into a contrived expository dump by tying it to character goals and narrative payoffs.
- Masks: The power toggle switch that shifts your character from ordinary person to a legendary persona. Masks are the social layer—the outward identity—and the Core Book’s treatment of Masks helps you frame scenes where the disguise matters more than the punch. This isn’t about “gimmicks” as much as it is about how others perceive and react to your presence in a city full of rumors.

The PDF gives you enough rules to keep the game moving without forcing a reader to consult a hundred separate supplements. It’s not a light rules-light game, but it’s not a dense gravity well either. It sits in a comfortable middle ground that invites both new players and veterans who crave a system that supports heavy storytelling without mutating into a math-heavy nightmare.

For GMs, this is a feature, not a bug. The structure of Mythos and Masks makes it easy to seed scenes with moral ambiguity, while Logos ensures there’s a throughline for the mystery that won’t collapse under pressure. If you’ve played urban fantasy or investigative fiction, you’ll recognize the design intent: give players freedom to be extraordinary while ensuring the game can still exist in a city that feels dangerous, alive, and suspicious of magic. The PDF leans into that tension, and that tension is precisely where City of Mist shines.

## Character creation and the joy of “we can be gods and street rats at the same time”

Character creation in City of Mist is one of those systems that sounds simple on the surface but becomes deliciously complicated when you start layering choices. The PDF outlines a streamlined path: pick a Mythos, define a Logos belief, and craft a Mask that makes your presence in the city feel inevitable. The result is a character who feels big, but not in a way that requires a megaphone to convey what they’re about.

- Mythos gives you a theme. It might be a long-form obsession like “justice for the neglected” or a cultural myth that you draw on for style and power. In practice, you’ll find that Mythos is best used as a compass rather than a checklist. The PDF advises you to build scenes around this core concept rather than forcing it into a binary success/failure model.
- Logos ties to your problem-solving approach. This is how your character rationalizes their actions when the city throws a curveball. The PDF’s examples help you see how Logos can justify both investigative breakthroughs and personal compromises.
- Masks define your social presence. In play, Masks are what your character wears to get through the day. The PDF does a solid job of making Mask choices feel meaningful without turning into a cosplay ledger. You’ll see how a character might switch masks mid-session to align with the mood of the scene, a natural fit for the game’s theatrical roots.

A highlight in the PDF is its emphasis on collaboration. Character creation isn’t just about “What cool power do I want?” It’s about building a team whose myths interlock in interesting ways. The book provides prompts, example silhouettes, and a few “what-if” scenarios that help players workshop a party that feels coherent and capable of carrying a city-wide mystery. If you’re GMing a one-shot or a longer campaign, you’ll appreciate the way the PDF suggests starting with a strong hook and then letting the players fill in the mythic scaffolding with their own twists.

## Practical tips for running a session from the PDF

Here are a few quick and practical tips to get the most out of the Core Book PDF when you’re running a session:

- Start with one myth, one belief, and one mask. Don’t try to juggle five at once. Give players a simple starting framework, then let the city’s rumors complicate their paths.
- Use the city as a partner in the story. Treat the urban setting as a living, breathing entity that responds to players’ actions. The PDF encourages this by providing prompts that connect personal legends to the city’s evolving landscape.
- Lean into improvisation. Many of City of Mist’s most memorable moments come from players reacting to the unexpected. The PDF’s structure supports these moments by design, so be prepared to let the narrative pivot when a rumor spirals into a scene that changes everything.
- Keep scenes modular. The IO (input/output) of the game thrives when you can swap in or out scenes without derailing the overarching mystery. The PDF’s scene templates are perfect for this, whether you’re running a one-shot or a longer campaign.
- Use the reference materials. The PDF’s index and cross-references save time during play. If you’re a GM who loves a clean desk and a short stack of cards that you can flip with confidence, you’ll appreciate how the book nudges you toward the next beat rather than forcing you to rummage through your bookshelf.
