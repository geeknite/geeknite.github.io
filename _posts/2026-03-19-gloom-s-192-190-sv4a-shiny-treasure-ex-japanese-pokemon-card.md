## Condition and Grading Reality Check
NM, or Near Mint, is a slippery standard in the world of Pokemon cards, especially with shiny reflective surfaces involved. Here are practical guardrails to understand what NM might mean for this Gloom:

- Corners: likely sharp with minimal if any whitening along the tips; avoid sleeves that abrade the foil finish
- Edges: may show faint micro-wear from handling, but nothing glaring that would scream casualty at a glance
- Surface: holo pattern should be crystal clear with stable color; any fingerprint smudge should be minor and easily cleaned with a soft microfiber
- Centering: often not perfect in promo runs; a slight tilt can still be NM if the glare distracts more than the border alignment does

A good rule of thumb: NM means you can display the card and still wave it around in a circle to show off the shine without feeling a twinge of guilt for not keeping it in the freezer-grade vacuum press. Real graders will have their own litmus tests, but for us mortals, NM is a state of happiness that you can tolerate while nerding out about micro-scratches you cannot see with the naked eye until the light catches just right.

## How to Authenticate and Verify NM Condition (Non-Sale Ordeal, Just Wisdom)
- Inspect corners for white nicks; if none are visible under good light, you’re likely in the safe zone.
- Check holo surface for scratches; a single minor scratch isn’t the end of the world, but multiple marks could downgrade the surface state.
- Compare the centering with edges; a perfectly centered card often commands appreciation, even if the holo can obscure micro-centering quirks.
- Confirm language and set marks match the promo description; mislabelled cards do happen in casual marketplaces and can lead to confusion later on.

If you’re unsure, seek a second opinion from a trusted friend or a local card shop with a reputation for sharp eyes and honest evaluations. A 15-minute look-over can save you from a regrettable purchase and a memory of squinting at a rainbow on cardboard.
