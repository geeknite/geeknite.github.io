## Overview: what is this pack really for?

The Zombie Miniatures Pack for Zombicide: Black Plague is not a gimmick. It’s a supplement that expands the pool of undead you’ll encounter during a campaign, letting you dial up the chaos without changing the core rules. In a game where you conquer dungeons and defend keep gates from waves of rotters, abominations, and the occasional knight-turned-zombie, more zombies means more crunch for the player who loves to manage risk and resources while shouting at the board in a mock-British accent.

If you’ve played the base game and you’ve enjoyed the pacing, the Zombie Miniatures Pack drops in like a fresh wave of brain-stealers—slightly different in sculpt, a touch more variety in poses, and a few new zombie units that make the board feel alive… or, well, undead. It’s not a must-have, but it’s a very tasty add-on for teams who want to push the difficulty, the chaos, and the occasional dice-rolling disaster into the red.

A good zombie pack should feel like a snack you can’t resist when you’re a little hungry for more undead. This pack delivers that vibe with:

## Quality and design: sculpt, scale, and paintability

One of the big questions with any zombie pack is how well the minis scale with the core game components. The answer here is a confident yes. The zombies in Black Plague already share a certain medieval-grungy charm with a dash of “this is a tabletop battlefield.” The new minis slot in with a similar footprint and stance, which makes group painting and basing feel cohesive rather than a patchwork of different artists.

From a painting perspective, the minis are a joy to work with. The textures range from rotting flesh to burlap tunics to splattered armor—great for dry brushing and layering. If you’re not excited about airbrushing a whole army, you’ll still find the surface details readable under a standard brush. The plastic is not so brittle that you’ll fear snapping a toddler’s grip when you handle them; you can be a little vigorous with your hobby knife and sanding sticks without turning your miniatures into abstract modern art.

Design-wise, the pack doesn’t reinvent the wheel. It does the classic Zombicide thing: zombies with a readable silhouette, easy to identify with your eyes in a cluster, and enough variety to prevent the board from turning into a green-gray blur on a busy turn. The added variants help players recognize different threat types at a glance. You’ll have the standard shufflers, the more aggressive crawlers, and perhaps a couple of flankers who insist on loitering near stairwells just to punctuate the doom.

## Painting and basing: tips to maximize the experience

If you’re into painting, you’ll appreciate the variety here. The sculpts offer different texture levels that respond well to dry brushing and washes. If you’re new to painting, this pack gives you a nice palette of surfaces to try without overwhelming your brush control. A simple two-step approach works wonders: base coat in a muted, desaturated tone, then a wash to pull out the textures. Dry brush with a lighter shade on raised surfaces to highlight bone, cloth, and dead flesh. If you’ve got a favorite color for decayed armor, you’ll have new opportunities to apply it without clashing with your existing unit colors.

Basing is where you can get creative. In Zombicide, bases matter for readability. The new minis offer nice base geometry that aligns with the old minis, which means you won’t lose your alignment during a furious melee. Add a touch of basing grit—sand, small pebbles, or static grass—and you’re golden. Pro-tip: before glueing, dry-fit a few minis into their stand spots on your board to check lines of sight and blocking. It saves you from a last-minute reshuffle that looks like a defensive line dance gone wrong.

## Narratives in practice: a short scenario guide

To give you a concrete flavor, here’s a quick scenario outline you can try with the Zombie Miniatures Pack. It’s not a hard rulebook scenario, just a template to spark your imagination:

- Setup: Place the board as per base game instructions. Add a small “breach” objective at the far end to simulate a gate that’s starting to give way.
- Spawn: On round one, spawn a modest wave of standard zombies. From round three onward, begin introducing the more dynamic variants from the pack. This staggered release creates a rising tempo without overwhelming players immediately.
- Objective: The party must escort a caravan to a safe room while fending off the undead. If the caravan survives rounds X, you win; if a hero is bitten and transformed, you lose a point of narrative momentum. The outcome should depend on planning, positioning, and a splash of luck.

This is not the official mission design from the pack, but it captures the vibe: more zombies, more decisions, and more storytelling opportunities for your group.
