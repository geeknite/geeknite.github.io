## Overview

The GOF 1P Office Workstation Cubicle is a one person portable workspace that ships in a flat-pack dream and assembles into something that looks like a professional's lair, only with lower height clearance for entering the fantasy world of cubicle life. The Artisan Gray color is a matte neutral that refuses to steal the spotlight from your monitor. It is designed for single person use, with privacy panels available for those who enjoy the feeling of being surrounded by walls while pretending to be in a cave streaming triple-A games at lunch.

### Design and Build

The build quality appears sturdy enough for regular office use, and the framing is comprised of metal and laminate panels that resist daily wear and tear, along with a scratch resistant finish that makes your coffee rings less obvious. The Artisan Gray finish is not glossy, which means fewer fingerprints to chase after on those long days when you are debugging a complex Excel spreadsheet that somehow turned into a polygon mesh.

The panels are designed to minimize sounds from keyboard chatter and printer beep anxiety. It is not soundproof, but it helps with a low hum vibe that reduces the echo of your coworker's mic when a meeting goes from professional to chaos.

The cubicle's design includes a modest privacy screen option, which is perfect for those who want to feel like they are on a solo quest while still being accessible to your colleagues for water cooler philosophy.

### Size and Fit

This is where the GOF 1P shines and fights with the reality of real office spaces. The measurements given are 5.5 feet deep by 6.5 feet wide; height options either 4 feet, 5 feet, or 6 feet tall. Or to translate into inches for the metric curious: about 66 inches deep, 78 inches wide, and available heights of 48, 60, or 72 inches. That is a footprint that will dominate a corner but fit in most standard office spaces with careful planning. If you plan for a 6 feet tall panel, you might want to check the ceiling height to ensure there is no existential crisis about space above your head.

The depth is generous enough to accommodate a dual monitor setup, plus a keyboard tray that refuses to recoil when you push too hard. The width is ample for a single user to spread out papers, perhaps a second screen, and a small plant that will likely die but will look fancy on the first week. The height options let you tailor the configuration to your ergonomics: either a compact 4 foot high setup that is easier to sit under, or the full 6 foot tall privacy barrier for those who take their secret agent vibe seriously.

### Assembly and Setup

Building this thing should feel like assembling a transformer, but with less risk of ending up with a rogue arm that ends up in your keyboard. The flat-pack approach means you bring panels to the room, lay them out like a puzzle, and hope the instruction manual uses pictures and actual words. The included hardware and the setup sequence is straightforward, but be prepared for a few minutes of mental gymnastics as you figure out the correct orientation of the panels. The good news is that tools required are minimal: a screwdriver, perhaps a power drill if you like to live dangerously, and a friend to hold the panel while you align the holes.

Cable management is an area GOF has not ignored. There are cable channels and grommets that allow you to route power and data lines in an unobtrusive way. You can create a tidy run along the underside of the work surface, which will help keep the wires out of sight yet within reach when you need to plug in a monitor or a pixel-laden external drive.

We tested the GOF 1P with a 27 inch monitor, a mid-sized keyboard tray, and a small desk lamp. The result was a comfortable user experience that did not require extra structural changes to the room.
