## Ergonomics and Comfort

Ergonomics is the real stage for this show, not the paint job. A ten person workspace by itself could turn into a seesaw of posture issues if properly managed. The Eternal 10 Person 2 Workstation attempts to address this with adjustable surfaces and a central screen zone that encourages a collaborative posture rather than a crowding of corners. Let us break down what you get in terms of ergonomic features and how they land in real life.

- Height flexibility: The main desks offer height adjustability to accommodate standing or sitting phases of the day. In practice, you can switch from a case of buzzing energy to a calm breathing exercise as you stand and stretch during long planning sessions. The key is to ensure the switch is smooth and the transition does not disrupt your colleagues who are mid keyboard sprint.
- Monitor alignment: The central screen modules are designed to provide a shared focal point without anyone having to crane their necks. The ideal scenario is one where each person can find a comfortable stance while still benefiting from the large screen workspace. If your team has a heavy reliance on dual monitors, the screen modules should be positioned to minimize neck strain and glare. A poor placement is as bad as a bad coffee choice at 9 am; it ruins the entire day.
- Keyboard and mouse zones: The surface depth provides enough room for peripherals, but you still want to set up a reasonable anchor zone for each team member. If you pack too much hardware into each workstation you risk a tactile avalanche of keyboards clacking in unison. Instead, invest in cable-managed trays and simple anti slip mats to keep devices in their lanes.

From a practical standpoint, you will want to run a quick ergonomics test after installation. A simple test: have your ten team members sit, stand, and adjust their chairs in a coordinated fashion. If you end up in a silent comedy routine with each person trying to find a comfortable angle, you have a sign that your layout needs a small tweak. The goal is a baseline that reduces neck strain, lowers shoulder tension, and keeps wrists in a neutral position during most tasks.

## Cable Management and Power Delivery

A large shared workstation is a magnet for cables, power strips, and a small forest of USB cords. The Eternal design includes a cable management spine that runs along the back. The promise here is a neater surface with all the cords tucked away, which reduces pinch points and keeps the entire front edge free for comfortable keyboard use.

What we found in practice is a workable, not miraculous, cable system. It handles the typical thicket of USB hubs, HDMI/DisplayPort cables, plus a handful of power outlets. If your organization loves to push the envelope with additional devices like network switches, printers, and a shared 3D printer or two, you may need to add extra conduit or a separate under desk power unit. The important aspect is to designate a single power source per segment, which reduces tripping hazards and helps with class 1 vs class 2 electrical considerations.

If you are setting this up in a space that has existing cable clutter, plan a pre wiring pass. This means you route things behind panels, measure clearance for the spine, and test a few cable lengths to avoid a tug of war between ten people trying to retrieve a misplaced micro HDMI lead during a crucial call. The hardware is robust enough to handle a steady stream of devices, but it will not magically reorganize itself. Plan ahead and give your IT folks a seat at the table during the layout phase.

White surfaces demand care. They show dust, fingerprints, and the occasional coffee spill with alarming fidelity.
