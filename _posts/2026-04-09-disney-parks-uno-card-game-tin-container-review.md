## How to play: a quick refresher with Disney flair

UNO basics are timeless: matching color or number, playing Draw Two or Skip to disrupt the progress of your rivals, and using Wilds to switch things up. The Disney Parks edition doesn’t rewrite the rules; instead, it glazes them with park-themed art and a couple of light twists that won’t derail a seasoned UNO player but can create a few laughs for beginners.

Here’s a quick refresher for newcomers and a reminder for veterans:

- Each player starts with seven cards. The rest forms a draw pile. The top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile.
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the top card on the discard pile. If you can’t play, you draw one card. If you draw and can play, you may do so immediately.
- Special cards do classic things: Skip makes the next player lose their turn, Reverse flips play direction, Draw Two forces the next player to draw two cards, Wild can change the color, and Wild Draw Four makes the next player draw four cards (and you get to choose the color).
- In a Disney-tinged edition, the artwork may include subtle nods to popular park experiences; for example, you might see a silhouette of Space Mountain or a cartoon version of the Castle as part of the art on the back of the cards. It’s more about vibe than a new mechanic.
- The first player to run out of cards yells “UNO!” and then wins once they’ve shed all their cards; if necessary, you can agree on a few extra laps around the park-themed vibe for a longer session.

The Disney flavor adds a social cue rather than a strategic overhaul. It’s a safe, family-friendly addition that keeps the core tension and excitement of UNO intact while giving players something to smile about beyond the standard card colors. If you’ve got a family of Disney fans, expect a touch more chatter about their favorite park icons between rounds. If you’re just here for the cards and not the theming, you’ll still get a solid UNO experience in a neat tin.
