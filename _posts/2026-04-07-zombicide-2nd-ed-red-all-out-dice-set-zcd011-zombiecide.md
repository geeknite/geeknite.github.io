## Maintenance, Longevity, and Care
The best way to preserve any dice set is to keep them clean and dry. Red dice aren’t inherently fragile, but the faces can accumulate grime after many table nights—especially if you have snacks that involve powdered cheese or sticky soda. A quick wipe with a damp cloth between sessions is usually enough to maintain readability. If you’re a serial tabletop photographer, you’ll appreciate how well these dice hold up to the camera lens: the color stays bold, and the pips remain legible even in macro shots.

Storage is simple: keep them in their original tray or a dice bag, away from direct sunlight to avoid any potential fading. If you’re someone who frequently travels to conventions or meetups, consider a padded case to protect them from accidental crushing or the occasional rogue d20 that sneaks into your tote bag.
