## Practicality: Care, Cleaning, and Longevity

### Cleaning basics

- Regular vacuuming (with a gentle setting) helps remove dust and loose fibers without pulling the pile. Use a suction head designed for delicate fibers.
- Spills: blot, don’t rub. Alpaca fibers can tip into staining if you rub, so act quickly with a clean cloth and a mild cleaning solution recommended for wool.
- Professional cleaning: depending on soil load and foot traffic, professional cleaning every couple of years is a good baseline; ensure the cleaner is approved for alpaca wool or delicate fibers.

### Stain management

Spills and pet accidents are inevitable. The key is to catch them early, blot, and consider a gentle stain remover designed for wool blends. If you’re dealing with pet accidents, you may want to store a small spot-cleaning kit for quick action—no one should have to delay a game-night for rug reconditioning.

### Environment and care tips

- Avoid direct, prolonged sunlight to minimize fading, especially in rooms with large windows. Alpaca fibers can darken subtly with sun exposure, much like a favorite chair that has seen better days but still remains the throne of your streaming empire.
- Humidity management helps prevent fiber brittleness. A balanced room environment reduces the risk of static and keeps fiber resilience high.
