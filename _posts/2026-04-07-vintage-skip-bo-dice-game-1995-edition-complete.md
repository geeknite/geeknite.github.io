## How to Play: The Core Loop of Chaotic Fun

The core loop of Skip-Bo Dice Game is simple on the surface but has enough subtle wrinkles to keep evenings interesting. Here’s a practical walkthrough that will read like a stage play for a group of friends who owe each other a lot of games after the holidays:

1) Setup
- Each player takes a set of markers and sits around the board track. The dice are shuffled like a deck of mentos in a soda. The quick-start guide might say you place the markers on the Start space, but the real rule is: start where your stomach tells you it’s the best place to begin.

2) Turn Structure
- On your turn you roll the dice and draw a number of action cards from the deck. The action cards dictate how you can advance, whether you can block others, or whether you should unleash friendly sarcasm on your opponents. The goal is to move your marker along the track, accumulate points, and avoid the dreaded “snake eyes” that curses you to a fate of mockery at the table.

3) Scoring and Winning
- Points are awarded when you reach or pass the finish line on the track, with special bonuses for completing certain sequences. The exact scoring can feel like a D&D treasure table — a mix of small gains and the occasional big payoff that makes you forget the last three failed rolls.

4) Endgame
- When one player crosses the finish line, the round ends and scoring is tallied. The high score wins. In the 1995 edition, there’s always a moment where someone forgets whether they’re supposed to keep rolling or pass; this leads to a dramatic shrug and a chorus of, You know what, we’re going to pretend that was intentional.
