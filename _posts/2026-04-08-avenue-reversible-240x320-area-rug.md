## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity
Caring for this rug is mostly about quick reflexes and a good vacuum. Here’s a practical care plan:
- Daily/weekly: Vacuum with a beater bar low setting to avoid fiber snags. Use a slow pass method rather than aggressive up-and-down strokes that can fray the pile.
- Spills: Blot promptly with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper. For liquids, start at the spill and work outward to limit spread.
- Stains: For tougher stains, a mild soap solution or a manufacturer-recommended cleaner is best. Pretest on a small area to avoid color bleed. If you’re dealing with pet accidents, an enzymatic cleaner can help neutralize odors instead of just masking them.
- Drying: Allow the rug to air-dry completely before flipping or rolling. A damp rug can develop a musty odor and may attract more dirt if used while damp.

Maintenance aside, the fibers are designed to resist pilling and wear. If you have high-traffic zones or rambunctious pets, you’ll appreciate that the face you see today can still look good months later after flipping to the other side. Think of it as a conceal-and-reveal magic trick for your floor.
