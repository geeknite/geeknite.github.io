## Step-by-step: building a Fortune Row character in 1-2-3-4

This is the practical core of the kit. Below is a distilled workflow you can actually run during a game night, rather than a sacred sermon you need to attend to in a quiet cathedral of dice rolls.

- Step 1: Pick a Mask and an Echo
  - Start with a Mask archetype that resonates with your table’s vibe — something like The Night Clerk, a connoisseur of secrets who works the crowd at the margins of the city. Then choose an Echo, a recurring motif drawn from the Fortune Row’s legends (the river, the neon, the backrooms). The Echo acts as a narrative beacon, a fault line you’ll revisit as your legend unfolds.
- Step 2: Choose an Origin bucket
  - The origin answers: what did your character do before the city’s mysteries pulled them into Fortune Row? Were they a courier for a shadowy union, a failed artist chasing inspiration, or a gambler who learned to read the city like a library card catalog?
- Step 3: Establish a Key Relationship
  - You’ll define one anchor relationship that feels essential to your character’s current arc. A former mentor? A rival who knows your secrets? A shopkeeper who keeps your gear running? This relationship should be something you’re willing to drop random breadcrumbs about in scenes.
- Step 4: Assign a Clarity and a Flaw
  - Clarity is the part of you that seeks truth in the chaos; Flaws are the things that will get you into trouble if you lean on them too much. Fortune Row pushes you to embrace both sides rather than pretend you’re a flawless hero — because in City of Mist, even heroes have a messy history.
- Step 5: Equip a Signature Item and a Debts/Boons ledger
  - The Signature Item is a memorable prop that your character uses to access their legend. Debts/Boons are the city’s currency of favors and obligations. Fortune Row nudges you to play with these as narrative levers, not as mere stat sticks.
- Step 6: Seed a Scene Seed
  - Choose one of the kit’s prebuilt scene seeds to kick off your first session. You don’t need to be a master improviser to run with it; you just need a character who looks good against a noir-lit backdrop.

If you stick to these steps, you’ll have a playable concept within a single session and enough momentum to carry you through a few more. The kit’s design makes it easy to move beyond a single neon-lit moment into a series of escalating choices that feel personal and consequential.

## Balancing color with balance: keeping City of Mist on the rails

A common pitfall with any character creation kit is letting the vibe outrun the mechanics. In City of Mist, you want the Masks to shine without swallowing the evidence, and you want the relationships to push drama without turning the table into a soap opera. Fortune Row leans into tonal balance by providing prompts that are vivid but not prescriptive. The GM retains control of the dramatic economy, ensuring that Masks’ power scales with the player’s creative input.

Some practical tips:

- Use scene seeds to anchor sessions, but let sessions depart from those seeds when the story deserves it. A good seed is a jump-off point, not a rails map.
- Treat Echoes as character-driven hooks rather than external constraints. Echoes should be ignite points for scenes, not stubborn anchors that break the drama.
- Track the Debt/Boons economy with a small, shared sheet. It helps players see how favors accumulate and how risks compound, which is the engine of tension in City of Mist.
- Encourage players to articulate what their Mask’s power looks like in the real world. The more concrete the manifestation, the easier it is to weave scenes that feel personal and cinematic.

The kit doesn’t pretend to solve every narrative problem. What it does do is give you a strong, immediately usable starting point that respects the game’s core: mythic vibes layered over urban grit, with characters who feel both iconic and fallible.
