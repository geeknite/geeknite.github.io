## Craftsmanship and Techniques
### The hand tufting process explained (in friendly terms)
Hand tufting involves pulling loops of yarn through a fabric backing to create a dense, plush pile. ## Maintenance and Care
### Cleaning basics
Regular vacuuming with a gentle, low-suction setting is your rug’s best friend. Use a vacuum without a beater bar or with a soft brush attachment to avoid pulling on the wool fibers. Address spills quickly by blotting with a clean, dry cloth. For tougher stains, test a gentle wool-safe cleaner in an inconspicuous area before applying to the entire surface. Avoid harsh chemical bleaches that can degrade wool fibers and dull the color.

### Avoiding common pitfalls
- Don’t rub stains; it pushes the liquid deeper and abrades the pile. Blot, then clean.
- Avoid prolonged direct sunlight on the rug to prevent fading in your favorite geeky cosplay poster-lit room.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear, especially in high-traffic zones near doors or gaming chairs.

### When to call for professional care
If you’ve got a stubborn stain, a strong odor, or you suspect the backing is loosening, bring in a professional rug cleaner. They can assess fiber content and backing and treat the rug with industry-grade tools that aren’t available in your local grocery store aisle. Think of it as sending your woolen buddy to a spa day—with less cucumber water and more fiber-safe solvents.
