## Sizing Options: Custom Size or 8x10
Two options are what set Crown White apart: a standard 8x10 and a fully configurable custom size. The standard 8x10 is a safe bet for many rooms, offering generous coverage under seating and a pleasing border around your furniture.

Custom sizing is the real showstopper. If your room has nonstandard dimensions, odd corners, or a unique furniture arrangement, you can request a rug that fits perfectly. The process typically starts with precise room measurements and an on-paper plan for the edge binding and pile orientation. You get the satisfaction of a rug that was made to be part of your floor plan, rather than forcing your floor plan to fit a cookie-cutter rug.

In practice, custom options may carry a longer lead time than stock sizes, but the payoff is a footprint that aligns with your layout and viewing angles. You’ll avoid awkward gaps and under-furniture zones that look like a glitch in a video game.

## Care and Maintenance
Maintenance is a ritual rather than a chore. Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting and a suction head designed for wool. Avoid aggressive agitation or beater bars that can pull fibers. Immediate blotting of spills is crucial; avoid rubbing, which can drive stains deeper.

For tougher stains, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in wool. - White fibers can gray over time if exposed to strong sunlight; consider room layout and blinds to minimize sun exposure. If sun rays are unavoidable, you can rotate the rug periodically to even out any fading patterns.

## Room Setup and Practicality
White is a strong color choice but demands practical planning. In high-traffic rooms, you might consider the rug next to a sitting area or along the perimeter where it’s less likely to accumulate stains. Use a high-quality rug pad to limit movement, reduce wear on the edges, and add a touch of cushion underfoot.

If your living area includes kids or pets, establish a “rug zone” that stays clean via a near-permanent cleaning routine and careful placement away from the kitchen or play areas. It’s a practical approach to keep your rug looking pristine longer.
