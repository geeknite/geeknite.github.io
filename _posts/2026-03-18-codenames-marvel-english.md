## How to play (the Marvel twist)
If you have played Codenames before, Marvel Edition will feel familiar but with two notable enhancements: a larger pool of theme specific cues and a few clever rule tweaks that push storytelling and pop culture vibes to the foreground. If you are new to Codenames, fear not—the learning curve remains approachable and the Marvel theming adds a layer of accessibility and humor that can help new players dive in.

### Setup and team roles
- The board is arranged with 25 word cards in a 5x5 grid.
- Two teams, red and blue, each nominate a Spymaster who gives clues that are one word plus a number, such as Stark 3 or Shield 2. The rest are Field Operatives who try to guess their team words.
- Marvel edition widens the pool of possible cues by including recurring Marvel themes or characters in the clues, giving Spymasters more expressive options without requiring a master list of obscure names.

### Clue giving and guessing (the core loop)
- The Spymaster provides a one word clue and a number. The clue should connect as many of the team words as possible while avoiding the opposing team words and the assassin word.
- The Field Operatives discuss and point toward cards they believe match the clue. The more accurate the guess, the more control they gain.
- A correct guess allows additional guesses up to the number plus one optional extra if another card seems apt. Hitting an opponent word ends the turn for that team. Revealing the assassin ends the round immediately and can swing momentum dramatically.

The Marvel edge offers thematic cues that can be a blessing or a trap. A clue like Stark 3, for example, might point toward tech or a specific character, but it can be misread as something outside the Marvel sphere. The key is balance: Spymasters should aim for Marvel flavored connections while avoiding overreach that could mislead both teams. It is a delicate balance between clever wordplay and pop culture overload, and Marvel Edition leans into that balance with a wink and a nod rather than a full on trivia sprint.

## Strategy, tips, and best practices
A robust strategy exists for Codenames in general, and Marvel Edition adds its own cheat code sheet courtesy of the Marvel theme. Here are practical tips to improve play for beginners and veterans alike.

### For beginners: building a safe foundation
- Start with safe obvious connections that unambiguously link a few words without pulling in too many traps.
- Lean on Marvel tropes and cinematic shorthand: tech, teams, and widely known events can be effective if they stay clear of ambiguity.
- Read your teammates: the same clue can click for one squad and fall flat for another. Adapt your clues to the shared knowledge of your group.
- Manage risk: if you are uncertain, limit your guesses to two or three cards to avoid triggering the assassin or giving away points to the other team.

### For veterans: weaving complexity with clarity
- Try multi target clues that touch several own words at once, but avoid cornering yourself into a trap. Example: a clue like Avengers 3 could link to three words if the layout cooperates, but a poor grid alignment could make it a disaster.
- Use cross category cues sparingly. The Marvel deck supports cross-links like New York or S H I E L D, but you should ensure those words are likely to be claimed by your team.
- Exploit the assassin with care. A high stake clue might swing the turn in your favor, but a bad guess can end the game in a single move.
- Balance theme with pace. A well chosen Marvel cue can keep everyone engaged, but overusing lore heavy references can bog down the table.

### Common missteps and how to avoid them
- Too specific references: a clue tied to a single character risks leaving teammates behind if their Marvel knowledge varies.
- Over reliance on pop culture knowledge: the most fun moments come when players can pivot to safer clues and let the team laugh off a close miss rather than chasing a perfect read.
- Ignoring the grid: pay attention to the spatial pattern of the 5x5 board. A well placed spatial cue can guide multiple teammates in one turn.
