### Card Quality: what near mint and lightly played actually means

In MTG trading circles, condition is a language. Near mint (NM) typically means cards show minimal signs of wear: clean fronts, sharp corners, and no noticeable scratches or scuffs that affect readability or play. Lightly played (LP) means there might be some minor wear on the edges or corners, maybe a tiny scratch you can barely see under a lamp. The combination is common in collection lots because it’s a buyer-friendly compromise: you get visually appealing cards that still have a playable sheen. The risk, of course, is that some entries may be more LP than NM and could vary within the same lot. We recommend a quick run-through:

- Check centering: most NM cards will be nicely centered; LP cards might be a hair skewed. A 2-3 mm off-center can be a deal-breaker for collector-grade, but fine for a budget deck.
- Inspect corners and edges: a tiny ding on LP cards is expected; if it’s on NM cards, you’ve got yourself a potential discrepancy to ask the seller about.
- Look for sleeve wear: if the lot was stored in sleeves, you might see some sleeve marks that read as LP rather than NM. Ask the seller for photos if you can.

If you’re into grading vibes, you may want the more expensive items physically inspected, but for a bulk 50-card lot, your primary decision is whether the perceived condition aligns with your tolerance for edge wear and surface marks.

### Conditioner and buyer tips: what to ask the seller and how to handle the deal

- Ask for the exact list if possible. A simple card-by-card inventory helps you verify what you’re buying and prevents a misunderstanding about condition.
- Request high-resolution photos or a video walkthrough. It’s the old truth: you don’t know what you’ve got until you’ve seen the edges up close.
- Clarify whether the cards come with sleeves or top-loaders. If the lot was stored in sleeves, you’ll generally see less edge wear — but not always.
- Confirm shipping details and return policy. If the seller offers free returns within a reasonable window, that’s a good signal they’re confident in their listing.
