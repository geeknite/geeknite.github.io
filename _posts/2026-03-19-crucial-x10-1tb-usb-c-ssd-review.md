Benchmarks matter, but they tell only part of the story. Here is what we observed in a typical test rig consisting of a modern Windows 11 laptop with a USB-C 3.2 Gen 2 port and a high quality USB-C cable: 
- Large sequential transfers: about 900–1050 MB/s read, similar writes when the drive is cool.

### Cable choices and power: what to use with the X10
The X10 ships with a USB-C to USB-C cable that should be sufficient for most uses. ### Durability, throttling, and thermal behavior
The aluminum shell helps dissipate heat but under sustained writes you can expect some throttling as the drive warms. This is normal for most NVMe based portable SSDs. In a typical office or home setup with good airflow, these drives usually stay in the comfortable zone. If you are traveling on a hot day or in a vehicle where the sun is bottling up the interior, plan for occasional slowdowns rather than a sudden meltdown.
