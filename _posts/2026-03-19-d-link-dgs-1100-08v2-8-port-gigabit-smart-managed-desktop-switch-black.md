## Features and Management
The 8 port variant markets itself as a smart managed desktop switch. If you have ever tried to segment a network with a dozen VLANs using a consumer router that is allergic to configuration, you will appreciate the clarity of a proper switch language.

You can manage the DGS-1100-08V2 via a web GUI that you access by pointing a browser to the switch IP. The UI is functional rather than glamorous. ### VLANs and QoS
A key reason to pick an eight-port smart switch is the ability to separate traffic with VLANs. You can place printers, guests, and your personal laptop on separate VLAN segments so that your old printer stops lashing out whenever someone starts a video call. QoS capabilities allow you to prioritize critical traffic such as VoIP or video conferencing, ensuring your meetings do not become a chorus of dropped packets. You can implement VLAN tagging as needed and set port based or policy based rules. It is not the same as a full enterprise fabric, but for a small office or a dedicated home lab, it is enough to feel like you have a grown up network.
