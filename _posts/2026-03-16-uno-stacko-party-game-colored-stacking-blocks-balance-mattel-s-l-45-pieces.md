## How to Play
This is where the game either sings or crashes like a failed attempt at a gravity-defying trick on a trampoline.
- Set up the tower at the center of the table. Make sure the surface is flat, because a wonky table equals a wonky tower.
- Shuffle the UNO-inspired block cards and deal or place them as per the instructions. The deck determines color or number to go after on the next move.
- The youngest player goes first. On your turn, you draw the top UNO action card and then select a block from the tower that matches the color or the number shown on the card. You remove that block carefully without knocking the tower dollhouse to the ground.
- After removing, you place the block on top of the stack or return it to its designated position following the game variant you choose.
- If the tower falls, you typically draw a penalty card or lose your turn depending on the variant; if you successfully remove a block without topple, you pass the turn to the next player.
- The first player to run out of blocks or reach a designated point in your variant wins. The rules come with a few variants to change the tempo, so you can keep the chaos fresh.

Tip: communicate clearly with your teammates about which color or number you are targeting. It reduces the chaos in the chaotic process that is UNO Stacko.

## Design and Build Quality
Block feel matters in a stacking game. The blocks should be uniform in size, with clean prints of color numbers. You want a balance of friction and smoothness so that blocks slide out with control but do not slip due to slick surfaces. If the edges are too sharp, they can cause fingers to sting or blocks to become misaligned. If the colors are vibrant and consistent across blocks, that is a big win for a game that is played in a chaotic, loud environment.

The base, the tower stand, and card holder should be sturdy enough to absorb the inevitable wobbles. If the base is light and the tower keeps tipping even on a normal table, your players might have more drama than focus. The packaging should reflect the quality of the product inside; a well-produced box that protects the blocks during shipping is a good sign that the manufacturer cares about your experience.

## Variants and House Rules
One of the best parts of UNO Stacko Party Game is the potential for variants. If you want to move away from the UNO card mechanic, you can implement house rules like:
- All players must remove a block of a specific color on their turn. If you fail, you pass the turn.
- A color chain challenge: as blocks are removed, the color must correlate with a color chain drawn from a separate deck.
- A timed variant: players must remove a block within a certain time limit to avoid penalty.

These variants keep the game from becoming repetitive and boring. They also enable you to tailor the chaos level to your group.
