## Sorting and organization
If you are the type who dreams of one day sorting a thousand cards into a perfect color-coded system, you are in for a treat and a workout. The best approach is to create a system before you touch the stack. You should decide whether you want to divide by set, by character, by energy cost, or by card type. For most folks, a combo of set and card type works best.

We present a recommended sorting scheme:

1) Separate the main character cards from support and energy and items.
2) Within the character pile, subdivide by set and by rarity, so you can pull foils or rares more quickly.
3) Create a “needs sleeves now” pile for the cards you want to use in the next couple of decks.
4) Create a “binder fillers” pile for everything else.

The core question: is bulk good for actual play? In short: bulk is a mixed bag. If you want to build a precise, tournament-ready deck, bulk is not your best friend. ### Practical storage and care
If you are going to own 1000+ cards, you owe it to your future self to store them properly. Here are practical suggestions:
- Invest in good sleeves for the main play cards. Thick sleeves reduce shuffle fatigue and protect the cards during play.
- Use a large, sturdy storage binder with regular page pages (not the cheap ones that tear after a month).
- Consider color-coded dividers so you can quickly find cards by type or by set.
- Create a simple inventory system on a spreadsheet to avoid losing track of rare cards.

### Quality and English language considerations

That said, English versions are typically well-supported in the DBS community and are easier to trade and play with than foreign language exports.
