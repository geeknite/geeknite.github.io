## Authenticity checks and verification (how to confirm you have the real deal)
If you are parting with money for a sealed NOS deck, you owe it to yourself to verify authenticity. Here are practical steps:
- Inspect the seal: Look for intact shrink wrap with no holes, rips, or evidence of tampering. A few minor wrinkles are normal, but large gaps can be a red flag.
- Review the box art and logos: The 1985 International Games edition will typically show the International Games logo along with a copyright stamp from that era. Cross-reference the imprint with archived images or catalogs if you have access.
- Review the interior contents: If the inner deck is still sealed as well, confirm the standard UNO deck has 108 cards with four colors and the usual action cards. Depending on the run, there might be a small insert—note its presence or absence and compare with known examples.
- Examine the card stock and color consistency: The cards should have a consistent back pattern with no obvious discoloration or edge wear on the sealed unit. If you can peek under the shrink wrap safely, check for the weight and feel of the cards themselves; modern reprints tend to be glossier and lighter.
- Look for a legitimate manufacturing clue: Some editions include small print on the bottom edge of the box or a label that confirms the manufacturing location. This can help distinguish original packaging from later fakes or misprints.

## Display, storage, and care
A sealed UNO deck is a treasure not just for its gameplay potential but for the display value. If you plan to show it off on a shelf or in a dedicated display case, consider the following:
- Light exposure: Avoid direct sunlight, which can fade box art and, over time, degrade the wrap. If you want a sunlit display, use UV-protective glass or keep it in a shaded area.
- Humidity and temperature: Keep the item in a stable environment. High humidity can cause cardboard to warp, and temperature swings can exacerbate cardboard shrink wrap aging. A climate-controlled room is ideal.
- Handling: When you decide to handle the deck, do so with clean hands or cotton gloves to minimize fingerprints or oils on the plastic wrap. Remember, you are a steward of a relic.
- Display options: For a truly enthusiastic presentation, place the box in a shallow display case that allows viewers to see the wrapper while you preserve the sealed state. Some collectors also use acrylic risers to lift the box for a more dramatic shelf presence.
- Photography tips: Use diffuse lighting, a neutral background, and a shallow depth of field to create a good product shot. Show the back of the box clearly, especially if the imprint is a line of information that might indicate the year and edition.
