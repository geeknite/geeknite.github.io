## Host Setup, Safety, and Accessibility
A great game night is as much about the host’s energy as the cards in play. Absurd Box scales well from intimate gatherings to larger party settings, but a few host-focused tips can keep things comfortable for everyone:

- Set expectations at the start: briefly clarify the tone and permit opt-outs if a prompt touches a nerve. A simple “we play for laughs, not to offend” line can set a positive baseline.
- Create a rotating judge system if your group likes to experiment with rules. A rotating judge adds structure while maintaining the spontaneous vibe that makes Absurd Box shine.
- Use a timer to keep rounds moving. A fun, but strict, 60-second window per round? It adds pressure and hurries clever clumping into one glorious moment.
- Use physical boundaries and break points. If a particular theme becomes too heavy, pivot to lighter prompts for a round or two.
- Accessibility matters. Ensure players with visual or cognitive differences can participate meaningfully. This can mean providing larger print cards, clearer contrast, or a reduced speed version of the rounds.
