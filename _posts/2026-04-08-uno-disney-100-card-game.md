## How to play (Disney 100 flavor with UNO bones)
If you’ve played UNO before, you’ll feel right at home. If you’re new, here’s a quick primer, with the Disney 100 twist aimed at keeping things brisk and entertaining:

- Objective: Be the first player to discard all your cards. When you’re down to one card, you must shout UNO. Failing to call UNO can cost you in a later round, as the penalty often involves drawing more cards or performing a goofy forfeit—think of it as a family-friendly comedy reprieve rather than a harsh penalty.
- Setup: Each player gets seven cards. The remaining deck becomes the draw pile, and the top card flips over to start the discard pile. Disney artwork is visible from the start, but you’re here to play, not to stare at a gallery.
- Turn order: Move clockwise (unless you flip a reverse card that’s probably in pink and teal because Disney loves to keep things visually interesting). On your turn, you either play a card that matches either the color or the number/action of the top discard, or you draw from the deck if you don’t have a playable card.
- Action cards: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two—and in this set, some Disney-flavored twists on those actions. These cards function as you’d expect, but with minor visual changes and occasional flavor text that makes kids giggle and adults eye-roll with affection.
- Wild cards: The wild and wild draw four remain standard, allowing you to choose the next color. The Disney 100 set may add small flavor differences on the back of the card or in the narration when you play a wild—little touches that keep the world cohesive without changing the core rules.
- Special Mystery Foil Card: This card changes the course of the game in some rounds. It could be a re-roll of a draw, a swap of hands, a local rule tweak, or a temporary “everyone draws until the foil is revealed” rule (you get the vibe). The exact effect varies by set, but the thrill remains: you don’t know what this foil card will do until it matters, which is exactly the kind of suspense you want during a family game night.

In practice, the Disney 100 edition nudges the pace to stay quick. It’s not a heavy strategic conquest; it’s a social, laugh-forward experience with enough strategic choices to feel like you’re not just flipping cards aimlessly. If you’re a purist who wants strictly classic UNO rules all the time, this edition is a light departure. If you’re after a cheerful, kid-friendly version that still scratches the itch for competitive shenanigans, this is a solid pick.
