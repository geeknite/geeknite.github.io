## Gloom AR 109/108: Ruler of the Black Flame — a SV3 japonés gem with extra bling

In the dim glow of a playmat lamp, the Gloom AR card slides into frame like a misfit candle at a goth wedding. Yes, it's still a Gloom card, but this time the alternate art is less “sad-sack graveyard” and more “mysterioso botanist with a barbecue grill.” Let me walk you through why this particular AR Wonder from the SV3 set might just become your next obsession or your next casual conversation starter at your local card shop.

![Gloom AR SV3 Card]({{ '/assets/images/cards/gloom-ar-sv3-ja.jpg' | relative_url }})

### Overview

#### Card Identity
This Gloom is from the SV3 line in the Japanese release, and as a curious oddity in a language you may not read easily, the AR stands for Alternate Art — a fan favorite that swaps the watercolor melancholy for something a little more flamboyant and fanservice-y. The card number is rumored by collectors as 109/108, a math problem that only increases when you try to trade with your friend who insists the AR print is a secret 109/109. Regardless, it's a Gloom card with a silhouette that glows and a frame that shouts “Japanese set, proper glare.”

#### Artwork and Flavor
Alternate Art cards are like the director’s cut of a film: you already know the script, but someone in the art department decided to crank the color saturation by two notches and give Gloom a crown made of shadow. The artwork leans into high-contrast purples, neon greens, and a glow that would probably trigger a photoluminescent alarm if you hung this card on your wall at night. If you’re into Pokemon TCG art, you know that ARs are where the set’s personality sometimes roams free like a caffeinated Charizard; this Gloom wears the crown with a certain regal menace that feels almost vaporwave in 2020s Japan.

### Mechanics and Playability

#### Stats
- HP: around 60-70
- Type: Grass/Poison
- Weakness: Fire (as one would expect from a plant that has not learned the art of self-control)
- Retreat Cost: 1 or 2, depending on which panel you’re looking at, because alternate art edits love drama

The SV3 Gloom AR keeps with Gloom’s classic flavor: a bit shy in raw speed, but with a couple of flavorful moves that make you want to run a deck that lets you hoard special conditions and spread gloom like a pool party at dusk.

#### Attacks
- Stinky Gas (or Putrid Mist) – 20 damage, may Poison; players often use it to stall for time and force the opponent to manage board state. The art makes this feel more like a soft, aromatic cloud than a choking gas—while still being a valid mechanic in the game.
- Bloom Burst or Black Flame Finale – 60-80 damage, with a side effect like “your opponent’s active Pokemon is knocked Back” or “discard a card from their hand.” In AR form, this attack’s flavor text suggests the Black Flame is not just look; it’s a narrative device that makes your opponent think about flame, shadows, and balance. The reach is decent for a Gloom-typical line and it gives you a reason to build a trainer-driven deck around status conditions.

Note: The exact numbers vary by print run and region; the important bit is that this card offers two distinct options: slowing the opponent with a toxic aura or slamming with a mid-range punch. In a meta where every deck tries to accelerate, a Gloom AR offers a small but meaningful variable.

#### Abilities and Special Qualities
Gloom AR’s special ability, if we call it that in a tongue-in-cheek sense, is the aura around it. The alternate art frame is designed to create a certain “board presence” from the moment you lift it. It’s not just about the numbers; it’s about the vibe. If you’ve ever read a card like this and thought, “I want this in a deck that leans into disruption and draw synergy,” you’ll appreciate the AR’s flavor synergy with other Gloom variants and Grass/Poison types.

### Collectibility and Market

#### Print run and Rarity
AR variants tend to have somewhat limited print runs and are often distributed among vaults, shops, or promotional bundles in Japan. The label 109/108 adds to the mystique, though it might be a misprint in some catalogs. Whether you chase the chase or you simply enjoy the aesthetic, this card’s rarity sits in that sweet spot where collectors look for something distinct but not absolutely unattainable at the entry-level price point.

#### Grading and Condition
As with any AR card, grading quality is a major factor. The AR artwork’s gloss and the border finish will be scrutinized by PSA/BGS or your local grading service. Because AR cards often have heavier contrast and color saturation, minor edge wear may show more dramatically than on standard prints. The price delta for graded copies can be significant, especially when the AR print is highly sought after by fans of Japanese sets and Gloom’s broader lineage.

#### Market Snapshot (Non-Official)
In the world of online marketplaces and price tracking (which we won’t link to—this is a hands-off approach, folks), expect the AR Gloom to command a premium above base Gloom cards from SV3. However, the exact value can swing based on grade, the card’s condition, and whether it comes with a pristine holo that the buyer wants to frame for display. If you’re hunting for an investment that looks stellar on a shelf and acts as a conversation starter in a deck-building session, this card ticks both boxes.

### Display and Protection

#### Display Setup
If you’re into gallery setups, consider a clean display with a soft LED backlight to highlight the glow around the Flame crown. A UV-protective sleeve or binder page helps preserve the corner integrity and prevents the glossy finish from sticking to the sleeve.

#### Storage Tips
- Keep at stable room temperature
- Use sleeves designed for modern cards
- Store away from direct sunlight to preserve color saturation
- Use a binder with soft protection so the card doesn’t press too hard into the page

### Japanese Context and AR Culture

In Japan, alternate art variants also exist in a distinct market, often with more intense color palettes and a packaging style that underscores the “collector’s item” vibe. The SV3 set has its own fanbase, and AR cards from this set are commonly discussed in Japanese collectors’ circles for their art direction and print quality. If you’re chasing AR in a non-Japanese market, you might see slightly different color balance due to printer variants. The Black Flame theme might resonate with a modern anime aesthetic, where power and mystery are fused with a little bit of mischief.

### Comparisons: Gloom AR vs Other Gloom Cards

- Traditional Gloom from earlier sets tends to emphasize the “stinky gas” and the “curse of the snail” vibe (okay maybe not snail, but you get the idea). AR variants shift the emphasis toward color, glare, and a more dramatic aura—perfect for display—and sometimes it translates to a stronger presence on the battlefield in the right deck.
- Compared to other AR cards in SV3, Gloom AR holds its own: the art is distinct, the rarity is desirable, and the deck synergy can be built around disruption and support rather than raw power.

### Playstyle Reflections

If you’re a player who loves control and attrition, you might appreciate how Gloom AR can pressure opponents into poor resource management. If you’re a collector who loves a visually striking card, AR is a forever lock on your display shelf. The synergy between the card’s flavor text and the deck you’re building matters: a green-tinged theme that leans into poison and forest vibes makes sense. But if you’re chasing hyper-meta speed, Gloom AR might feel a little slower off the line and more about the narrative of your board state.

### Community Opinions

In Geeknite’s circles, this card is often discussed as a standout AR for its bold art and the way it pairs with other ARs. Some folks love the intense neon glow, while others worry about the card’s long-term top-of-mind appeal as new ARs drop in future sets. Overall, the sentiment is positive: visually impressive, reasonably playable for casual play, and a solid addition for display collection.

### Care and Handling

- Handle with clean hands to avoid fingerprint smudges on the glossy holo.
- Use top loaders or penny sleeves for protection when you transport the card.
- Avoid rough sleeves that catch on the holo edges. If you want to preserve resale value, avoid sharp bends or creases.

### Final Verdict

Gloom AR 109/108 from the SV3 Japanese release is a vibrant, collector-focused addition that merges the gloom from its namesake with a sense of aristocratic Dark Flame. It’s not just a card you flip for battle; it’s a piece of art that can anchor a shelf, catch the light, and deliver a conversation starter in every trade. If you’re a Japanese set enthusiast, an AR collector, or a Gloom connoisseur looking to expand a theme, this card offers a balanced blend of aesthetics and potential utility.
