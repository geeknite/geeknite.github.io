## How to play: core mechanics in the outdoors

The game maintains the core mechanic of “collect cards to score points while avoiding being hit by burritos,” but with a few notable twists that take advantage of outdoor space:

- Turn order and action resolution remain fast-paced. Expect a typical match to run 15-25 minutes, though the pace can accelerate if players start treating the lawn like a tiny football field of chaos.
- Outdoor prompts introduce environmental hazards that affect strategy. For example, a wind gust card might force you to throw at a different angle, while a sprinkler card can create a moment of slippery drama where a burrito nearly slides into a friend’s shoe.
- Zone-defend mechanics with mats provide a more tangible way to block or catch burritos. You’re not just relying on forearms and reflexes; you’re defending “territory” with a foam shield, which somehow makes you feel like a miniature gladiator wearing sunscreen.
- Card-based scoring remains intact, but there are special outdoor bonuses. For instance, a “sunburnt luck” card might grant extra points if you still manage to stay upright after the burrito bounce off the ground, which is basically a physics experiment you volunteered to test after your third lemonade.

The result is an experience that evokes a party game while still giving you enough physicality to scratch that dodgeball itch. The key is to balance aggression with laughter: you don’t want to injure a friend, you want to injure their pride in the most friendly, ridiculous way possible.

## A quick note on safety and etiquette

Because you’re outdoors and you’re likely to be more animated than usual, here are some safety tips that will save you from the embarrassment of a real-life blooper reel:

- Clear the play area of fragile objects, glass, pets that can’t appreciate foam projectiles, and obviously dangerous plants. Your garden gnomes can take a back seat for a few minutes, but not all of them.
- Establish a soft-landing zone. If your lawn has a slope or jelly-like grass, choose a flatter patch or risk burrito misadventures that end with a dramatic tumble. The goal is laughter, not hospital visits.
- Keep a cap or sunscreen handy. You’ll be squinting into the sun as you track a high-speed burrito arc, and no zombie-eyed glare should dampen your dopamine.
- Encourage good sportsmanship. You want the energy to stay playful rather than devolve into petty feuds. If someone steals a win with a hilarious trick, celebrate their creativity and move on.

Outdoor play comes with a certain unpredictability that is the point of the edition. Embrace it as a feature, not a flaw, and you’ll be rewarded with stories worth telling long after you’ve tucked the burrito away in the bag and called it a successful day of mischief.
