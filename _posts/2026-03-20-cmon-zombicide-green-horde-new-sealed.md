## Gameplay overview
Zombicide is a cooperative experience where you and your teammates coordinate to survive a zombie-infested night. Green Horde adds a forest-centric twist that emphasizes tactical movement, terrain usage, and a crowd-control approach that makes you feel like you are orchestrating a siege rather than simply blasting through a city.

Turn structure and flow
A typical round features activation of survivors, movement, item usage, and then the response of zombies. The deck-driven spawn system means you never quite know what is coming next, which is both thrilling and a little terrifying. The Green Horde introduces a few new mechanics that lean into melee engagement and strategic positioning, encouraging teams to funnel zombies into chokepoints and utilize the environment to their advantage.

New faction mechanics
Greenskins are not your usual mindless mobs. They have a penchant for aggressive moves, aggressive positioning, and a certain theatrical flair that makes your table feel like a forest stage where every turn could become a mini-drama. The faction’s rules encourage you to plan offensively and react adaptively, which is exactly the kind of dynamic that keeps a co-op night engaging rather than procedural.

Co-op dynamics and player roles
This edition shines when your group communicates well. The best nights happen when you coordinate tasks, allocate items efficiently, and don’t cry over dice that betrayed you in the final stretch. The game rewards teamwork, careful risk assessment, and the willingness to pivot strategies on a dime when a tree-line ambush appears or a stat line suddenly shifts due to a well-timed zombie surge.

Solo and two-player configurations
If you want to play solo, Green Horde includes AI-driven enemy behavior that can give you a fair challenge. The solo experience scales very differently from the group format, but it is a fine way to test new tactics and keep your brain sharp when your friends have other life commitments. Two players can still enjoy the co-op rhythm but might find the pacing slightly faster as decisions become more compact.
