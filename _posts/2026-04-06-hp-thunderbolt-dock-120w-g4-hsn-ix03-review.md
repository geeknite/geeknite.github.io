## Ports, Connectivity, and The Great Port Parade

This is where the dock earns some serious points or at least avoids major letdowns. The HP TB Dock G4 is built to handle multiple simultaneous tasks without making you pick favorites. Here’s a general port map you’ll likely encounter:

- 1 x Thunderbolt 4 upstream port (for the laptop connection) and power delivery passthrough (up to 120W total)
- 2 x DisplayPort 1.4 (for multi-monitor setups, depending on your GPU capabilities and TB bandwidth)
- 1 x HDMI 2.0 (great for a secondary display or a home theater mode at your desk)
- 4 x USB-A 3.2 Gen 2 (snappy data transfer for external drives, dongles, and that one stubborn USB-C mini hub)
- 2 x USB-C (Gen 2, for faster data and charging of smaller devices)
- 1 x 2.5Gb Ethernet or 1Gb Ethernet (depending on the exact SKU; some revisions may vary) – for solid wired network reliability
- 1 x Audio jack (for headsets or a devious plan to listen to ambient desk sounds when you’re designing graphics)

In practice, this means you can drive a multi-monitor setup, connect high-speed storage, attach peripherals, and keep the laptop charged without juggling a nest of adapters. The two DisplayPort outputs are particularly nice if you’re a power user with GPU-accelerated apps or you simply want to keep both a 4K monitor and a high-refresh-rate display humming along at the same time.

For photographers, video editors, and software developers who keep a million windows open, this port parade is a godsend. If you ever needed an excuse to upgrade your workstation to a TB4-friendly setup, this is a strong “yes, please” moment.

### Setup Tips and Troubleshooting (Because Nothing Is Perfect)

- Update firmware: Always check for the latest firmware for the dock before heavy usage. This can resolve minor connectivity hiccups and display-handling oddities.
- Cable management: Route TB4, HDMI, DisplayPort, and USB cables neatly to avoid tugging on ports during docking/undocking.
- Monitor order: If you’re running multiple displays, verify the order in the display settings to ensure the right monitor becomes the primary display.
- Sleep/wake issues: If the laptop refuses to wake reliably from sleep when connected to the dock, try toggling the “prefer external display” option in the display settings or re-plugging the TB4 cable after wake.
- Charging edge cases: Some laptops may momentarily dip below their optimum charging rate if a lot of peripherals are drawing power through the dock. In extreme cases, you can unplug a high-draw peripheral temporarily to give the laptop a moment of breathing room.
