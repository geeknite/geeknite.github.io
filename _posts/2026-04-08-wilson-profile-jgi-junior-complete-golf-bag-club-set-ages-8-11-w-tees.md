## Setup, Comfort, and Maintenance Tips

Getting started with a junior set should be a fun, stress-free experience. Here are practical steps for your first session and beyond:

- Build a quick-fit setup: Adjust the bag to a comfortable height, ensure the strap sits on the stronger shoulder, and test the stand on a flat surface before a practice session.
- Grip and stance check: Have your child stand with feet shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent, and arms relaxed. The club should meet the ball in a natural position with a slight forward lean.
- Short practice wins: Start with short chip shots and putting to build confidence before moving to longer drives. Junior sets encourage this progressive approach.
- Maintenance routines: Wipe clubs after rain, dry the bag if it gets soaked, and inspect the shafts for any dings after a couple of seasons. Keep tees in a separate pocket away from moisture. If the grip starts to feel slick or worn, replace the grip or the club as needed.
- Safety basics: Teach your child to retrieve clubs carefully, watch for stray balls, and keep the set away from stairs or sharp edges to avoid accidental drops.

Jekyll and Hyde tasks like bag care, occasional cleaning of grips, and seasonal checkups will keep the set happy and extend its lifespan. Remember: the aim is consistent practice, not a weekend demolition derby on the driving range.
