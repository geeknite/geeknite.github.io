## Gameplay Impact

### How It Interacts With Core Gloom Mechanics
Gloom is famous for its handedness at doom: you play cards representing your family’s misfortune, and the total gloom on everyone’s table influences who will win (or lose) at the end. A promo like Went Dark in the Night would typically interact with this system by either modifying a family’s gloom counters, altering endgame scoring, or triggering a special condition when the card is revealed. We’ll outline a few plausible interactions while keeping the spirit of the game intact:

- Endgame Shift: The card could grant a small endgame bonus to a chosen family, effectively pushing them into the spotlight of misery and making it more rewarding (or punishing) to push certain families into the darkness.
- Temporary Alteration: The card might apply a temporary gloom modifier to a family for the remainder of the round, influencing decisions about which families to target for misfortune first.
- Narrative Twist: In line with Gloom’s storytelling nature, the card could introduce a mini-scenario or a “twist” stamp on the score track, changing how players narrate their doom for that round.

These options aren’t mutually exclusive in the wild world of fan rules or official promotions; promo cards often exist to spark new strategies, not to enforce a single specific rule. The joy comes from the variety of possible interactions and the moment you realize your “beloved” family’s tragedy has just become a little darker, and your opponents’ tables are suddenly more dramatic than a soap opera season finale.

### Strategy and Combos
Smart players will treat Went Dark in the Night as a catalyst rather than a single action. Here are a few fictional-but-plausible play patterns that fit the Gloom vibe:

- Dark Tempo: Play the promo early to establish a tempo of doom. By nudging a family’s gloom early, you can steer endgame scoring toward your preferred protagonist—your own quirks of narrative that you’ve been cultivating since the first card was drawn.
- Targeted Gloom: Pair Went Dark in the Night with other promo or base cards that amplify gloom counters on specific families. The synergy is about maximizing a single family’s misery and then watching your rivals scramble to outdo each other with even gloomier tales.
- Narrative Tactics: Use the card to craft an endgame story that makes it appear you orchestrated the final tragedy for dramatic flair. The table’s social scoring can sometimes outpace the numerical scoring, so the promo becomes a social instrument as much as a mechanical one.

In the end, the real strategy is to embrace the storytelling. Gloom is a game about weaving a tapestry of tragedy while keeping the table engaged and entertained.
