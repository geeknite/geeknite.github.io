## The Collector’s Guide: Storage, Care, and Surfacing Value
### Storage and Handling
- Invest in clean, acid-free sleeves and a binder with divider pages labeled by year or theme. The 1990s can be a maze of different print runs, so organization pays off when you’re trying to track a set.
- Keep cards out of direct sunlight to prevent color fading and brittle edges. A simple display case with UV protection does wonders for long-term preservation.
- Avoid humid environments; moisture is the enemy of cardboard romance. A small desiccant pack in your storage box helps keep things dry during the occasional monsoon of a climate-controlled room.

### Cleanliness and Condition Checks
- Inspect edges for wear, corners for dings, and centers for print defects. A well-centered card is a mark of care, and it’s one of the few times where OCD can become an advantage in collecting.
- If you’re serious, consider grading. A professional grade can boost value, but be mindful of the cost-to-value ratio. Some cards are more meaningful as memory triggers than as investment vehicles.

### Display Tips
- Create a thematic display featuring Cosby Show relics, including the Malcolm-Jamal Warner card, framed with other memorabilia from the era. A glass frame with a white matte can help the colors pop without making it feel like a museum piece.
- Pair the card with a short curated playlist of 90s-themed TV show tunes and a few copies of the show’s most memorable quotes. It’s surprising how a well-timed line can enrich the storytelling around a physical artifact.
