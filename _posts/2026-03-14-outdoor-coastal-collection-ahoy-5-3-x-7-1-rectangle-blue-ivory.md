## Installation, Placement, and Maintenance
### Placement Ideas That Work
- Center a seating cluster on the rug to define a conversation zone with a cohesive anchor for chairs and a coffee table.
- Use the rug as the dining anchor for a bistro set; this creates a defined dining nook that feels like a seaside cafe rather than a random outdoor arrangement.
- Pair with a shade solution—whether a canopy, pergola, or simple umbrella—to create a sun-kissed or shade-swept stage for your afternoon rituals.
- If space is long and narrow, place the rug perpendicular to the railing; it visually expands the area and creates an illusion of depth in a small footprint.

### Cleaning and Basic Upkeep
Outdoor rugs love air and water—just not at the same moment. A brisk sweep removes surface debris, followed by a gentle rinse with a garden hose to conquer wind-ruffled sand. Let dry completely before bringing cushions back into place or rolling it up for storage. For stains, a mild soap solution and a soft brush are your best friends. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade fibers or alter color balance.

In real-world life, the rug will occasionally bear the brunt of splashes from food, pets, or enthusiastic sea-breeze wanders. The Ahoy is designed to withstand these adventures, but a proactive approach to cleaning will keep it looking shipshape across seasons.

### Seasonal Care and Longevity
Rotate or flip the rug seasonally if you want even wear. Use a rug pad or underlay to reduce slippage and extend the life of the fibers. If you’re storing it, ensure it’s completely dry and clean to prevent mildew. And if you’re in a climate with freezing winters, consider securing and storing the rug indoors or in a covered storage area during the cold months to preserve color and texture.
