Play sessions: setup, flow, and how to maximize the chaos

Setting up UNO Express is straightforward. Shuffle the deck, deal five cards to each player as a starting baseline, place the draw pile in the center, and flip the top card to start the discard pile. The timer is set, and the round begins with the youngest player or the organizer's chosen lead. From there, players take turns matching either color or number or playing an action card that modifies the flow of turns. The key in UNO Express is to keep the pace brisk: there is less time to overthink, more pressure to act, and more opportunities for small bluffs to mislead the person on your left about your real color intent. If you enjoy mind games, you will find that this mode rewards quick reading of opponents and the willingness to risk a strategic play even when you are down to your last card.
