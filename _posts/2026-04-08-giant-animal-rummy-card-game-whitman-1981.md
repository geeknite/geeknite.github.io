## How to Play Giant Animal Rummy

If you’re familiar with standard Rummy, you’ll be pleasantly at home here, with a few animal-themed twists to keep it fresh.

### Setup

- Shuffle the jumbo deck and deal a fixed number of cards to each player (usually 7–10, depending on the house rules found in the rule sheet). 
- The rest of the deck becomes the draw pile, with the top card flipped to start the discard pile.

### Objective

The goal remains to form valid sets and runs: three or four-of-a-kind with animal-themed ranks (e.g., three cards showing the same animal) and sequences of consecutive ranks within the same suit. In Giant Animal Rummy, the “suits” are more like thematic groups rather than traditional hearts, diamonds, clubs, and spades, but the structure is familiar enough to avoid confusion.

### Rules and Strategy

- Sets: Three or four cards showing the same animal (e.g., three lions) from anywhere in the deck. 
- Runs: A sequence of consecutive ranks of the same suit. In the animal deck, runs are typically built within each animal group or across a color-coded subset if a color system exists. 
- Wildcards or Jokers: Some editions include a couple of jokers or wild cards; if your sealed box doesn’t mention them, you can optionally assign wild status to a designated animal (like the chameleon or octopus) to spice things up. 
- Lays and Melds: Players can lay down valid sets/runs on the table as they form them.

Important note: In real life, the exact rules can vary by edition. Since your Whitman 1981 edition may have its own variation, check the included instruction sheet for any unique twists.
