Why Pick Denominations? A Practical Guide
-----------------------------------------
Choosing denominational makeup is both a science and an art. The practical goal is to keep play smooth, minimize counting, and preserve the drama of chip stacks without turning a simple pot into a financial audit. Here are a few tips to optimize your pick-denomination setup:

- Start with a base of mid-range chips (e.g., 25s and 100s) for most pots. These are the chips that will get you through the majority of mid-game decisions without needing to pull out a calculator.
- Place smaller denomination chips (1s and 5s) in easy reach for quick bets and small pots. You don’t want to fumble while your opponents are watching the board—trust me, they notice everything.
- Add a handful of large denomination chips (500s and 1000s) for late-stage blinds or tournament-like action. You’ll thank yourself when you don’t have to count out a mountain of 25s just to bet a big pot.
- Color code consistently. The human brain likes patterns, so keep your colors intuitive. If you’re using a color scheme you’re used to, you’ll speed up decision-making and keep the table vibe playful rather than chaotic.
- Keep a spare chip rack or tray. It helps when you’re re-banking chips during a break and can be a nice visual cue that the game is on pause and not just a chaotic blender of chips.
