## Assembly Tips and Maintenance

A few practical tips to help you enjoy the rack without wrestling it into place:
- Lay out all parts before you start. The last thing you want is to realize you are missing a shelf after you have already dispensed with your coffee and begun to tighten bolts.
- Use a level during setup. The leveling feet or casters should let you achieve a plumb, which helps with door alignment and cable routing.
- Install equipment in a top-to-bottom sequence that makes sense for airflow. Consider placing heat-generating devices lower down to minimize hot-air rising into delicate components at eye level.
- Label cables as you go. A simple labeling scheme saves hours of frustration when you need to replace a failed NIC or patch a misrouted fiber later.
- Clean the interior surfaces when you are done. A quick wipe with a microfiber cloth helps maintain the finish and reduces dust buildup that can hamper cooling efficiency.

Maintenance is mostly preventive: check mounting rails for looseness, inspect door latches, and make sure vented shelves are free of debris. A little routine care goes a long way toward preserving performance and aesthetics.
