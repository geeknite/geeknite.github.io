## Setup and Play Flow
Setup is a ritual that sets the mood more than it sets your expectations. You lay out tiles in a modular fashion, shuffle objective and event decks, assign investigators, and decide who among you is going to pretend to be calm for the next 60 to 90 minutes. The actual play flow is tidy: each round has a phase structure that lets you do a few key things—gather clues, confront suspects, and make a skill check that could either push the investigation forward or provoke chaos in the town square.

### Core Mechanics in Simple-ish Terms
- Cooperative play with a whisper of betrayal flavor: you want the town solved, but someone might be hiding a motive that makes the night wobble with tension.
- Skill checks that rely on a handful of attributes like perception, intellect, and endurance. Rolling fails often lead to narrative consequences that can screw or save your night depending on how you handle the aftermath.
- Clue tokens and event cards that create a pressure cooker atmosphere. The more you play, the more the town begins to feel like a living, slightly annoyed character rather than a backdrop.

The Special Edition does not rewrite the wheel; it tunes it. The rules remain accessible, which is a blessing because if a game demands a glossary before you even begin, you are probably chasing a niche that pretends to be deep but ends up punishing your social life. Here, you get a well-balanced challenge plus the spice of exclusive content that adds flavor without burying you in neon footnotes.
