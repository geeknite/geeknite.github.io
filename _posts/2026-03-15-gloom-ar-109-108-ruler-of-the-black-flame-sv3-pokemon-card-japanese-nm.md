## Grading near mint Japanese prints
Grading is a ritual, and for Japanese NM ARs it’s especially precise. Here’s what graders look for when evaluating Gloom AR 109-108:
- Centering: ideally 60/40 or closer to perfect; anything wider than ~65/35 starts to risk not being NM.
- Corners: sharp, with minimal whitening at the tips.
- Surface: free from scratches, scuffs, or micro-bumps that catch the light.
- Edges: no whitening along foil edges; the foil pattern should be vibrant and evenly embossed.
- Print quality: color fidelity should be intact; no fading or misregistration of the foil.
Japanese buyers tend to lean toward strict grading, which means borderline examples become more affordable in the short term but less attractive long‑term buys if you’re chasing a pristine set.

## Display, care, and storage strategies
Protecting a Japanese NM AR is more than a matter of pride; it’s a practical decision that protects your investment:
- Use a top loader, then a rigid binder sleeve with archival materials.
- Store away from direct sun, heat, and humidity.
- Keep separated from heavy usage areas; a mousetrap is less dangerous than a sticky finger.
- Include silica gel packs in long-term storage to prevent moisture-related issues.
- For display, consider two-piece cases that allow for 360-degree viewing without touching the foil surface.
