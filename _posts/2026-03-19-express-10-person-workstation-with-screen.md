H2: What is the Express 10 Person Workstation with Screen

This product is billed as a 10-person collaborative workstation with built-in screens, a shared cable management backbone, and a family of software features designed to orchestrate group work. The pitch is simple: replace rows of individual desks with a connected, ergonomic, screen-dense hub that encourages everyone to talk and share ideas without the usual latency of asking someone on the other side of the room to unmute. It sounds like a futuristic classroom setup, but with more power strips and fewer chalkboards.

The core idea is a long, modular desk array equipped with ten displays (or a combination of displays and large shared displays) and a central control hub that helps route video, audio, and data streams across the team. The system is designed for teams that do a lot of synchronized tasks: software architecture sprints, data analysis scrums, hardware debugging labs, and maybe even group streaming events where the audience is just the team watching a live demo. What makes this product unique is not the screens themselves but the way the entire desk is built around software glue that tries to keep everyone in the loop without turning your IT admin into a full-stack systems engineer.

Form follows function in this beast. The Express 10PWS is a long, sturdy unit with a modular feel. The cable management system is the unsung hero here; a good cable plan means fewer mock epic sagging cables on a Tuesday morning when someone is trying to slide in a new HDMI cable during a standup.

Ergonomics matter for a long day of collaboration. The desks are height-adjustable and support a wide range of postures.
