## Assembly and Ergonomics
### Setup Time and Process
Let’s be real: this is not a do-it-yourself IKEA weekend project. Expect professional installation with a small crew, especially if you’ve ordered the full screens and a smattering of under-desk drawers. The process typically unfolds as a staged ballet: base frames first, then the single long top panels, then the screen risers, then cable management. If you’re lucky, you’ll finish before your organization’s quarterly planning meeting, which is to say: you won’t. Pro tip: label components during unpacking and map your cable routes as you go. It saves you from a future-day drama where you find a USB-C port that decided to be a fashion designer and hide behind a panel.

### Ergonomics and Comfort
A 10-person bench needs thoughtful ergonomics. Adjustable monitor heights, keyboard tray options, and seat adjustments are non-negotiable. The idea is to prevent a chorus of neck strains, back pains, and indoor-panic-fainting when someone’s screen is inexplicably three inches too high. We advocate: per-desk adjustable monitor arms, a central height-adjustable row for shared tasks, and privacy panels that allow for personal space without turning the bench into a claustrophobic sardine can. Footrests, chair ergonomic support, and a system of quick-release cable ties become your best friends when ten humans all decide to work at the same time. 

### Cable Management and Cleanliness
Cable management is not glamorous, but it is essential. The Express 10P 2WS typically ships with a central channel and optional under-desk trays. You’ll want to label every cable with color-coding for power, data, and “this is not a charging station for the coffee maker.” The goal is a sea of orderly cables that makes you feel like you could land a spaceship in a spotless hangar rather than fight gravity with a power strip and an elaborate knot of USB-C roses.
