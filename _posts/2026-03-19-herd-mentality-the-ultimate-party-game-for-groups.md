## How to Play: A Quick Refresher
Herd Mentality is all about predicting what the majority of players will pick. The joy comes from the tension between your personal choice and what you assume others will pick. The game shines when the table is large enough to be loud but not so large that a single rogue voice can derail the herd’s consensus.

### Setup
- Shuffle the question deck and place it face-down.
- Each player draws an answer card from the drawn set. The number of cards you draw may vary by edition; the gist is to have enough options so that no answer feels obviously forced.
- The current round's question is revealed to everyone.
- A dealer (or rotating voter) will select a player to read their chosen answer aloud, or if your edition supports it, each player reveals their chosen answer face-down and then all answers are revealed simultaneously.
- A majority or the most common answer wins the round, depending on your house rules.

### The Core Mechanic
The core mechanic is simple: pick an answer you think others will pick, but also a little bit of your own taste to avoid being the odd sheep who shouts pineapple on pizza and gets left behind. The fun comes from the social pressure and the willingness (or unwillingness) to reveal your inner herd-mentality. The moment when someone calmly states a non-obvious answer that ends up being the majority choice is the moment that makes the table go wild with laughter and mild existential dread about your own predictability.

### Scoring and Endgame
Scores are awarded for aligning with the herd or for being the oddball who ends up in the minority but has the boldness to own it. Endgame triggers vary by edition, but the general arc is simple: play a number of rounds until someone hits a target score, then celebrate with the kind of raucous applause usually reserved for a halftime show or a particularly ferocious meme.

## Why Herd Mentality Works (and When It Doesn’t)
Herd Mentality is not trying to be a deep strategic beast. It is a social glue game. It thrives in groups where everyone is loud enough to influence and receptive enough to laugh at themselves. It works best in a casual setting where you want to lower the stakes and raise the smiles rather than chase a perfect tactic. Here are the situational pros and cons you should consider:

### The Pros
- Easy to teach and quick to pick up. A round can wrap in about 5 to 15 minutes, depending on how many plot twists your friends bring to the table.
- Flexible player counts. It scales well from small gatherings to larger groups, provided you avoid corralling too many quiet folks into a single corner.
- Strong social payoff. The real joy lies in shared expectations and the humor when the herd diverges into surprisingly accurate predictions or comically wrong ones.
- Replayability comes from the variety of prompts. Even the same crowd can have new experiences as the prompts skew toward different pop culture moments, inside jokes, or the weird little quirks your group has developed.

### The Cons
- It is possible to get stuck in a runaway consensus, which can feel a bit anticlimactic if there is no tension around the answers.
- In very small groups, the herd effect may be less pronounced and more like a polite crowd at a lecture.
- The humor is very group-specific. If your table doesn’t share a sense of humor on the same wavelength, some rounds can land flat.

If you want a more intense, head-to-head experience, you may want to pair Herd Mentality with another party game as a “pre-game” or “after the herd” activity. Speaking of pairings, a widely enjoyed combo is to follow up with a quick word game to keep the energy high after a round where everyone chose the same answer and then discovered they all said the opposite in a mirror round.

## Strategy and Social Dynamics
There is not a lot of deep strategy here, which is part of the charm. But there are a few practical tips to improve your odds of not ending up as the awkward outlier who believed the world would clap for your obsession with cheese puns.

### Read the Room
Pay attention to which players tend to dominate the herd and which ones love to zig when everyone else zags. If you can identify who tends to push for more mainstream answers, you can calibrate your own picks to align with their preferences while still staying semi-faithful to your own sensibilities.

### Lean into Shared Jokes
The best rounds are the ones where your answers tap into common experiences among the group. If your crew has a running joke about a certain show, meme, or hobby, lean into it. The herd loves predictable familiarity—just don’t over-predict to the point you become a walking meme yourself.

### Don’t Be Afraid to Risk It
Sometimes the most entertaining rounds come from bold picks that seem wildly out of step with the crowd, only to reveal that a surprising portion of players secretly agrees. The social risk is part of the thrill, and if you fail, you still get laughs, which is basically a win in the modern comedy economy.

### Time Management
If your group struggles with round timing, assign a friendly timekeeper. Short rounds keep energy high and prevent the game from devolving into a philosophical debate about the nature of happiness. The herd wants speed, not sermons.

## Expansions and Variants

If you’re the kind of player who enjoys collecting shiny expansions, this is where Herd Mentality shines. If you want to maintain a more streamlined experience, skip most expansions and keep the prompts in a tight rotation. The core set is strong enough for many game nights, and you won’t drown in a sea of extra cards and rulings that require a new Venn diagram to explain.

## Comparisons: Herd Mentality vs Other Party Games
- vs. Apples to Apples: Both games rely on social consensus, but Herd Mentality puts you in the driver seat of predicting the herd rather than just matching a judge’s taste. It adds a more dynamic social pressure.
- vs. Codenames: Herd Mentality is less about wordplay and more about social perception. If you want a game that makes people scream “Not it!” while pointing at someone else, Codenames can scratch that itch; if you want a game night that explodes with laughter, Herd Mentality often wins.
- vs. Telestrations: Both are great for big groups, but Herd Mentality tends to produce faster rounds and sharper social payoffs in a casual setting.

Herd Mentality is a party game built for people who enjoy laughing at themselves as much as at others.
