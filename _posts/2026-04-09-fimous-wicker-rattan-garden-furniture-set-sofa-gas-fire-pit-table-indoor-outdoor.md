## Assembly and Maintenance

Assembly is a mixed bag, as with many modular outdoor sets. The good news is that the instructions are clear and the parts are labeled. The not-so-good news is that you will want a second pair of hands for the larger sofa sections and a bit of patience for aligning all the screws and caps. Expect 45 to 90 minutes depending on your dexterity and how loudly your playlist screams at you about bolt alignment. A standard drill helps, but be sure to use it at a low torque to avoid snapping any of the lighter parts.

Maintenance-wise, you are basically managing four things: clean cushions, wipe down wicker, ensure the fire pit components stay dry when not in use, and store the protective cover during winter. Cushions are removable and machine washable where possible; for covers that resist washing, a gentle wipe with mild soap and water should do. Wicker is designed to resist UV damage, but you can still extend its life by avoiding prolonged direct sun exposure on the cushions. If you live somewhere with heavy rainfall, a quick seasonal cover makes sense to preserve the fabric and the fire pit components.

Cleaning tips from Geeknite gurus: use a soft brush to remove surface dust, then a mild soap solution on the wicker with a soft cloth. Rinse thoroughly and air dry. For the cushions, spot clean stains as soon as they appear; if you can wash the cushion covers, do so in cold water and air dry to maintain shape. If you store the set for the off-season, make sure the fire pit is fully cooled and that all propane components are stored according to local safety guidelines. A dry, well-ventilated shed is ideal for long term storage.
