## Setup and Rules (A Gentle, Slightly Flippant Tutorial)

If you’re familiar with UNO Flip, you already know the drill. The game starts with each player receiving seven cards. The goal is to be the first to get rid of all cards, but the twist is that the back side of each card can turn your color world upside down when someone plays a Flip card. In this version, the UNO Guy Launcher adds a little theater to the proceedings. It doesn’t replace the rules, but it does replace your ability to pretend you’re calm as your cousin plays a Wild Draw Four and you start counting to a regression of angry thoughts.

Setup steps in simple form:

1) Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player. 2) Place the remaining deck in the center and flip the first card to start the discard pile. 3) Decide if you’re playing with the launcher or not, because this is a rules-light moment that will define the night’s tone.

Launcher usage: The UNO Guy Launcher is designed to pop out or land on the table with a little flair when specific actions occur (for example, when someone plays a Skip card, or when someone yells “UNO!” with enough drama to fill the room). It’s theatrical, not deadly. Use it judiciously. The launcher can be triggered by a card play, or you can opt for a manual activation if the room needs a little more pep. Don’t aim at people’s eyes, and don’t let small children operate the launcher without supervision—this is a game, not a physics demonstration.

You’ll encounter the usual UNO Flip rules: matching color or number, wild cards, and the ever-popular Flip card that switches the color palette and flips up the rules. The twist this time is that the launcher is part of the ritual; the moment a “Uno!” is declared, a small prop or figure (UNO Guy) might soar toward the center of the table, providing a moment of chaos that only a well-placed stamp of laughter can tame.
