## Space planning and layout tips
The L Shape configuration is the real creative friend here. You can pair two 5x5 bays to form an L that can wrap around a corner or tuck neatly against a wall. This gives you an opportunity to create a collaborative nook on one side and a private stretch on the other. Here are some practical layout tips:

- Start with a central corridor for circulation and plug access. Place the most private bays along the outer edges or near screens to reduce glare and keep the team focused.
- Use the L corner as a natural break point for teams that require quick standups. The corner can host a shared monitor or a small display for project tracking.
- Plan for power and data distribution early. Mark the outlets and network drops on your floor plan and overlay them on the cubicle cluster to avoid patchwork improvisations later.
- Consider acoustic density versus openness. If the space is loud, you might want denser fabric panels or even add ceiling tiles and soft furnishings to reduce echo. If the space is quiet already, lighter fabric options can create a cleaner, more open look while preserving privacy.

Pro tip: map your future growth. If you anticipate teams expanding, choose a setup that can be easily reconfigured without disassembling the entire system. The goal is to preserve purchase value and minimize downtime during reconfigurations.
