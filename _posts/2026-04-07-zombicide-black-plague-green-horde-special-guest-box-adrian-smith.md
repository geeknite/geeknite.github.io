## What is the Green Horde Special Guest Box?

The Green Horde Special Guest Box is the kind of product that makes a hyper-focused hobbyist grin like a goblin who just found a coffer full of gold coins. It is an offshoot of the Zombicide Black Plague line that leans deeply into the green tide of orcs, trolls, and other marauders that give you that satisfying sense of “we are the chaos, and you signed up for it.” This particular Special Guest Box features artwork and design input from Adrian Smith, the artist whose name alone brings a certain gravitas to any fantasy project. It’s not just a “new faction pack” or a set of re-hash miniatures; it’s a curated experience with a strong narrative spine, a handful of unique sculpts, and a packaging that practically shouts, “You are about to invade a world that breathes orc-stomped prophecy.”

Inside the box you’ll find a carefully balanced mix of creature types and elite units designed to work both as a standalone scenario pack and as a complementary expansion to existing Black Plague tables. The goal is to give players a fresh engine for the game’s core loop—explore, fight, scavenge, upgrade—while injecting a new rhythm, a new cadence, and, yes, new tactical dilemmas that will make you curse your dice and then smile at the memory of that moment when everything clicked.

### A quick overview of contents (what you get, in practical terms)
- A cadre of Green Horde miniatures including Orcs, Shamans, and a pair of mid-tier leaders with unique abilities.
- A small scenario book that threads the Green Horde into your Black Plague campaigns with new maps and objective cards.
- Cards, tokens, and stat sheets curated to ensure the new units interact cleanly with your existing sets.
- A couple of art cards and a fold-out visual guide featuring Adrian Smith’s distinctive style.
- A “Special Guest” cover insert that makes the box visually sing on your shelf, even when you’re single-handedly deciding which table behaves and which table gets humiliated by goblins with stubborn resolve.

The design intent here is to balance showpiece miniatures with a workable gameplay loadout. You aren’t just buying a pretty display; you’re arming your table with a new threat that can scale from a one-off scenario to a short campaign. It’s the difference between bringing a decorative axe to a dungeon crawl and bringing a functional, well-balanced tool that you will actually use to cut through a wall of orcs instead of smacking the minis and hoping for luck to do the rest.

When you pull the miniatures from the plastic, the first thing you notice is the weight of the green-tinted ambition. The sculpts are sharp, with clean lines and a sense of motion that translates well to a tabletop, even for players who aren’t part-time geometry professors. Adrian Smith’s influence shows in the stylized proportions and the dramatic expressions—the kind of faces that would feel at home on a book cover next to a dragon that wears armor made of moonlight and questionable policy decisions.

The Orc Warriors strike a fine balance between bulk and agility. They look capable of shoulder-checking a ziggurat and still posing for a victory photo. Aesthetics aside, how do these minis actually perform on the table? ### A note on assembly and painting

Some players prefer to assemble their minis straight-out-of-box for a quick game night. That’s perfectly valid here; the parts fit together without excessive trimming, though you’ll find a few egress points where a careful nipper is worth its price. If you’re painting along with this expansion, the greens benefit from a layered approach: base coat, mid-tone, then a wash with a deep green or brown for the recesses. Edge highlighting with a pale lime or jade makes the armor pop. The Adrian Smith pieces especially reward careful painting—the linework around the faces and the musculature reads cleanly when you apply careful highlights.

## Strategy and Tips: Getting the Most from the Green Horde

- Prioritize target selection. The Orc Boss is a high-value target, but don’t forget about the supporting elites who can destabilize your formations. Balance your attention between the big fish and the smaller predators.
- Use terrain to your advantage. The box’s maps encourage you to funnel the Horde into chokepoints where your squad’s synergy can shine. If you can force a multi-phase engagement, you’ll maximize your damage output while minimizing your own casualties.
- Manage your resources. The new cards can tilt the odds, but you’ll still need to manage your items and morale. Don’t blow your powerful tools too early on a wave that you could survive with a bit of patience.
- Play to your crew’s strengths. If you have players who prefer heavy infantry, lean into front-line pressure. If you have a sharpshooter in the group, stage a precise, long-range harassment plan that chips away at the Horde while the rest of the squad holds the line.
- Learn the cards by heart. The new objective cards are not simply “kill x, collect y.” They require sequencing and timing, which means a little rehearsal goes a long way before you step into a live session with surprise guests—by which I mean your friends, not the humans who occasionally pop by your table to observe the chaos.
