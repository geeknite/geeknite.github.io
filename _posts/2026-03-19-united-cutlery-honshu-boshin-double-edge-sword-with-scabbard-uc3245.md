## Care and maintenance

This is not a razor blade care guide, and you shouldn’t treat it like you’re shaving your face with a kitchen knife. However, a little maintenance goes a long way to preserving the blade’s finish and the scabbard’s gloss:

- Dust regularly with a soft microfiber cloth. A quick wipe keeps fingerprints and smudges from building up on the blade’s surface.
- If the blade looks a bit tacky, use a light coat of corrosion-inhibiting oil or a blade-specific cleaner. Wipe it off and buff to restore the shine. Avoid heavy oils that attract dust.
- Check the fitting of the scabbard and the handle periodically. If you notice a loose piece, address it sooner rather than later to prevent wobble or rattling.
- Store away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading or plasticizer migration on finishes. Yes, UV light is the villain here, so give your wall-hanger some shade.

A good rule of thumb: treat it as a display prop first and a conversation piece second. If you’re rough with it, you’ll degrade its visual appeal long before you’d use it for anything practical.
