## Assembly, installation, and day-one sanity
### It arrives in modular stages
If you’re expecting a one-piece hero that slides into place like a space shuttle, you’ll be pleasantly surprised by how modular the Altis counter is. The typical delivery includes a few large panels, a handful of screws, and a dose of assembly instructions that won’t require a mechanical engineering degree to decipher. The overall process is straightforward: attach the legs to the base, secure the countertop to the skirting, and bolt everything into the wall if you’ve chosen a wall-mounted scenario. The result is a counter that feels solid and ready for action rather than something still curing in a warehouse corner.

### Time and tools
For the average office team, plan about 2–3 hours for two people who know the difference between a Phillips head and a flat-head, plus a level and a tape measure. The good news is that no special power tools are required; most of the assembly is about aligning panels, tightening a few bolts, and double-checking that the top is perfectly level so your cash register doesn’t roll away like a tiny, caffeinated sailboat. If you’re installing in a small lobby, you’ll want a helper to maneuver the countertop into place and a spotter for the edge—nobody wants a chipped corner because someone tried to bend physical laws in the coffee line.

### Maintenance and daily care
In daily operation, wipe-down with a damp cloth, then buff with a dry microfiber. The oak veneer enjoys a little moment of rest after a busy day, like a musician stepping off stage to sip water and survey the crowd. The black base responds well to routine cleaning but doesn’t appreciate abrasive polishes that could dull its satin finish. If you’re concerned about water marks or heat transfer from mugs and hot beverages, place a coaster or pad near the edge of the counter to preserve the surface’s pristine vibe. The end result is a desk that looks good now and ages with a dignified patina as years pass—assuming you resist the temptation to use the counter as a giant cutting board during a post-pandemic bake-off.
