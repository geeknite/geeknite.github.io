## What is the FDM Zombicide Black Plague Character Dashboard?
### The concept
The Concept: Think of it as a modular, printable sheet that consolidates a hero’s vital information—Health, XP, Weapons, Skills, and Status Effects—into a single, readable plate. The design is inspired by the need to reduce table clutter and speed up decision-making during combat. Instead of flipping through multiple pages or sorting through a forest of tokens, players get a snapshot of their character at a glance. The dashboard is designed to pair with Zombicide Black Plague, but its flexible layout makes it adaptable to various expansions and house rules. The design intent is to reduce cognitive load so you can focus on the fun of the game rather than hunting for the right token under the board.
### Core components
- A compact hero block with core attributes, health track, and XP progress.
- An equipment grid for weapons, armor, and items that can be checked off or moved as you equip/unequip.
- A status column for wounds, immobilized conditions, and special abilities that trigger under specific circumstances.
- A quick-turn section for actions, moves, and combat modifiers to keep you from digging through rulebooks mid-game.
- A notes area where players can jot quick reminders about hero traits, favorite items, or house-rule quirks.

## Build quality and components
### Plateaux de Personnage design
The heart of this dashboard is its Plateaux de Personnage. The design emphasizes legibility and speed. The layout uses a bold header with the hero name, followed by a segmented health bar and a compact XP strip. The color palette is a tasteful nod to the Black Plague aesthetic: muted earth tones with accents of sickly green and crimson that signal danger without screaming “special effects!”. The typography is clean enough to be read from across the table, which is crucial when you’re rolling a 12-sided die that looks suspiciously like it could double as a prop from a medieval sitcom.
### Printing and materials
From a printing perspective, the dashboard seems designed for standard A4 or Letter templates with either a glossy or matte finish. The lines are crisp, and the blocks are sized to fit common miniatures and token tokens used in ZBP setups. If you’re printing at home, consider a light cardstock for durability or a slightly heavier stock for long-term use. A protective lamination or a clear sleeve can extend life, especially if your gaming group runs campaigns where you reuse hero sheets every night. A minor caveat: if you’re using a laser printer, you’ll want to watch for warping on thicker pages when you apply moisture from a dry dice roll (you know who you are). Overall, the physical aspect aligns well with the “show up, play, and forget you even printed it” vibe that good gaming accessories aspire to.

## Design and usability
### UI/UX on the board
There’s a philosophy in good board-game accessories: readability first, flourish later. The ZBP dashboard nails this balance. The segmented blocks reduce the mental gymnastics of “where did I put my HP again?” to a simple color-coded glance. The hero’s health track is often the loudest element, using a stacked approach that changes color as damage piles up. This makes it immediately obvious whether you’re in the green, in the danger zone, or flirting with the brink of a grim finale.
The equipment grid is a small-but-mighty feature. It presents weapon categories, armor slots, and miscellaneous items in a grid that’s easy to scan. If you’ve ever played with the standard sheets where you have to scribble item names on post-its because there isn’t enough room, you’ll appreciate the layout’s efficiency. The quick-turn section is also a godsend; it helps you map your actions into a sequence you can repeat without rifling through cards or the rulebook.
### Customization options
One of the dashboard’s biggest strengths is customization without chaos. It’s built with modular sections so you can adapt to your group’s house rules. If your table runs a house rule that changes XP thresholds or modifies the way wounds are tracked, you can print extra pages or replace certain blocks without tearing apart the entire sheet. The design invites personalization—choose your hero color accents, print out optional trait blocks, or even add a tiny sticker for that rare “boss kill” moment. This adaptability means the dashboard isn’t a one-trick pony; it’s a toolkit that grows with your campaigns.

## Real-world gameplay impact
### Setup time
We tested the dashboard with a couple of different groups: the speed-running Saturday crew and the more meticulous Sunday squad. For the former, the dashboard shaved precious minutes off setup because players didn’t have to fumble with dozens of tokens. A quick glance told you whether a hero was battered, what they were carrying, and what their next action could be. The latter group appreciated the tactile satisfaction of sliding equipment cards into the grid. Some players even laminated their sheets and used dry-erase markers to track changes during a session. Overall, setup time dropped noticeably without sacrificing information density.
### In-game clarity
During play, the dashboard’s clarity shines. Combat decisions often hinge on a couple of numbers: attack modifier, defense, and potential skills that can swing a round. The dashboard consolidates these into visible blocks. Because the information is grouped by category, players don’t need to reference multiple areas of the table or flip to a separate sheet to find modifiers. For new players, this is especially valuable; a well-laid-out card reduces rule-desans—the temporary confusion that arises when you try to juggle multiple rules simultaneously. For veterans, it’s a satisfying tactile upgrade; the dashboard makes hero sheets feel more like a control panel rather than a homework assignment.

## Tips and tricks
### How to integrate with expansions
Zombicide Black Plague expansions often introduce new gear and traits. The dashboard’s grid can usually accommodate extra slots or mini-grids as add-ons. If you’re printing from a fan kit, print a blank “Expansion” row that you can fill in as new items are introduced. You can also create sticky notes for expansion-specific rules and place them in the notes area so you’re not constantly flipping to the expansion card.
### House rules and personal tweaks
House rules are part of the game’s life. The dashboard’s modular sections invite you to tweak XP thresholds, morale checks, or wound mechanics. If your group uses a different pace of leveling, you can reprint XP markers or adjust the XP strip locally. For example, you can color-code XP thresholds by tier and keep a legend at the bottom of the page so new players don’t have to memorize a dozen numbers. The more you tailor it, the more you’ll feel like the field commander of your own little armada of survivors.
