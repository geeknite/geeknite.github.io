## Ergonomics and Comfort: When Ten Heads Need Ten Comforts
### Seating and posture per seat
The bench design encourages active participation. Each seat is slightly contoured to provide seat support; you’ll still want cushions or small disposable seat mats if you plan to sit for a marathon sprint review. Yet the real magic happens in adjustability. You get armrests, optional footrests, and a seating arrangement that can be reconfigured in minutes to accommodate a design sprint or a last-minute “we need to brainstorm a feature we forgot to mention in the backlog.” Ergonomics isn’t just about your spine; it’s about social ergonomics: reducing the need for elbow wars when someone insists on open laptop usage while others want whiteboard space.

### The screen height conundrum
With ten people, everyone wants a good view. The Eternal 10 PWS addresses this with an adjustable central screen stand and tiltable display so the front-row attendees don’t feel like they’re watching a film in a life-size iPad. There are also alternate mounting options for peripherals: you can push laptops to the sides, place tablets in a small cradling track, or simply do the “roundtable-with-chalk” technique—everyone writes notes on the whiteboard while a few valiantly pretend to be recording the meeting’s minutes on the main monitor.

## Collaboration, Productivity, and the Culture Shock of Shared Space

A 10-person workstation forces you to rethink meeting etiquette.
