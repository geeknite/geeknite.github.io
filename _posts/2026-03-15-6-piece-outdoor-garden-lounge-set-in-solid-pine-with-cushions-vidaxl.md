Maintenance and weather resistance
Pine is prone to swelling in humidity and warping if left in rain without protection. So: seal the wood properly before setup, avoid prolonged exposure to water, and use breathable, water-resistant cushion covers. If you live in a climate with heavy rain or heavy sun, consider using a weatherproof cover when not in use. Cushions should be stored indoors or in a dry place to extend their life. If you want to maximize longevity, treat the wood annually with a sealant or oil to maintain color and reduce risk of cracking.

Durability and weather: what to expect
Pine is not teak, and that’s intentional. It’s lighter on the wallet and easier to work with, which is perfect for weekend warriors. Expect some seasonal maintenance: sealing the wood before long sun exposure, checking for small cracks, and ensuring cushions stay dry between use. The cushions are the weak link in extreme conditions; if they stay damp, mold can become a party of one—so store them indoors when you know a rainstorm is coming or at least cover them with breathable fabric.

Maintenance routine that won’t bore you
- Seal wood annually or biannually depending on climate
- Wipe cushions with mild soap and water; avoid harsh chemicals
- Use a breathable cover during shoulder seasons and storms
- Store cushions indoors or in a dry place when not in use for extended periods
- Periodically inspect screws and joints and tighten as necessary
