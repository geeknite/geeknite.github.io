## Authenticity and care tips
Authenticity starts with a careful inspection of hallmarks and the quality of the setting. Look for: 
- A clear metal stamp indicating 14K (or 585 in some markets) on the inner shank. 
- A consistent, tight setting with uniform gaps between stones (if it’s micro-pavé or bead-set, the uniformity is a big tell).
- Absence of obvious repairs or mismatched metal tones, which can indicate re-fitting or re-sizing.

Care-wise, vintage rings benefit from gentle cleaning and mindful handling. Use a mild soap solution and a soft brush to remove debris, then rinse thoroughly and pat dry with a lint-free cloth. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can wear down the metal or dull the diamonds’ sparkle. For tougher grime or more valuable stones, a professional cleaning every couple of years is a reasonable investment.

If you’re ever unsure about authenticity, consider a professional appraisal. An appraisal can confirm metal purity, diamond grades, and overall condition.
