## What is All Out Dice?
All Out Dice is, in plain nerdy terms, a dice pack that accompanies Zombicide Second Edition and introduces a suite of specialized dice designed to speed up action economies and push players into riskier, more cinematic plays. The core concept is simple: you get more dice to roll, but each die carries a higher variance, creating moments of glorious improbability. The color palette in this set is unapologetically 'end-of-the-world chic'—think bold reds, oranges, and maybe a dash of neon green for those zombies who clearly went to the rave of doom.

In the box you’ll find a handful of dice that feel distinct from the standard core-set dice. They’re not just “extra dice”; they’re a design that explicitly nods to cinematic zombie might: louder, bolder, and with faces that imply both glory and ridiculousness. If you like your apocalypse stories to swing between heroic saves and spectacular misadventures, All Out Dice is basically upbeat Monty Python meets Romero in tabletop form.

## The All Out Dial: what the dice do
Let’s get to the heart of the matter: what are these dice actually doing? The All Out Dice change your risk calculus in a few meaningful, table-flipping ways:

- More dice, more options, more chaos: You’ll typically roll extra dice for certain actions, which means you’ll see a wider spread of results. That range is where the drama lives.
- All Out results as a feature, not a bug: When an All Out result shows up, you either feel like a genius or you’re ejected from the table to the soundtrack of a collapsing building. Either way, you’ll remember the moment.
- Special faces with unique icons: These aren’t just numbers; some faces trigger chain reactions, allow re-rolls, or enable temporary bonuses for the next turn. The “feel” of these faces contributes to the sense of momentum in the game, which is what you want when you’re fighting a crowd of undead that can’t seem to unsubscribe from your feed.
- Interaction with core mechanics: The expansion doesn’t break the base rules; it folds in. It’s designed to complement rather than complicate, smoothing you toward climactic outcomes instead of stalling at “you miss again.”

If you’ve played any zombie horde game where you feel like that one hero who always rolls doubles on the bad days, the All Out Dice promise relief: fewer turns spent staring at a deck of misses and more turns spent staring down the maw of doom with renewed bravado.

## Mechanics in play: how it actually feels
From the table’s edge, the All Out Dice feel like a cinematic upgrade rather than a mere component swap. The first playthrough often produces a cascade of “aha” moments: a dice face that turns a near-disaster into a triumphant save, or a poorly-timed bad roll that collapses a plan you were certain would work. The thrill of narrative control—where your fate was previously tethered to a single dice pool—gets blessed with a bit more chaos, which, paradoxically, can increase the sense of agency as you roll with the punches.

In co-op play, this energy translates into more banter and more strategic negotiation. Do you spend All Out dice on a desperate rescue or hold them for a potentially larger payoff later? The dice don’t force your hand, but they do push you toward big, movie-like decisions. It’s not all swagger, though: there’s a cost to risk-taking, and the All Out faces remind you of that with occasional spectacular failures that are cathartic in their own right.

For solo play, All Out Dice can function as a dramatic pacing tool. If your solo scenario is built around a ticking clock, these dice can accelerate the sense of urgency. The downside is that you can quickly spiral into a cycle of “one more turn” if you’re not careful, which is exactly the kind of origami-die-fate we love to unfold at midnight.

## Tabletop realities: components quality and setup time
We’ve mentioned that the dice look good and feel sturdy; now the practical bits. The setup time for All Out Dice is minimal. You don’t need to reprint new character boards or memorize a dozen new acronyms. You add the dice, perhaps shuffle in a quick rule refresher, and your group slides into the new tempo within a single session’s preamble. The instruction booklet is fairly concise, which is welcome because in games of this size, nobody wants a three-hour errata lecture before the first roll.

Component quality extends beyond the dice themselves. The storage solutions offered by the box (if you’re lucky enough to have that edition) keep dice from rolling away between turns. The dice have a clean finish that resists chipping and doesn’t feel “cheaply hasty,” a common complaint in budget expansions. If you value tactile joy in your hobby, you’ll appreciate picking up a die and hearing that satisfying click of a solid face landing on a pocked table.

## How it interacts with the core game rules
All Out Dice are designed to slot into Zombicide Second Edition without requiring a new campaign structure. They change the tempo but not the destination: you still aim to clear zones, rescue survivors, and keep the zombie tide from spilling into your safe house. The expansion doesn’t erase the tension of the original rules; it compounds it in a way that makes the tension feel earned rather than engineered for drama’s sake.

As you’ll see in play, some missions are friendlier to All Out dice than others. If you’re playing a scenario with a tight turn-count or a fragile escape route, the All Out dice can feel like a misfit—too unpredictable for a fragile plan. Conversely, scenarios designed around big, cinematic swings benefit enormously: a bad roll can still push toward an epic save if the dice align, but even when they don’t, the failure produces the sort of dramatic misfortune that fuels good stories around a table.

## Comparisons: how does All Out Dice stack up against other dice expansions?
- Versus straightforward dice packs: All Out Dice stands out because it deliberately tries to alter the decision space, not just add more outcomes. It’s less about more dice for the sake of it and more about smarter moments of risk and reward.
- Versus other Zombicide expansions: The language of the dice is consistent with the vibe of Second Edition; you’ll feel aligned with other updates, but the All Out Dice offer a more narrative engine than some of the more mechanically dense addons.
- Versus retrofits from fan communities: There are fan-made variants that replace or reinterpret dice, but All Out Dice give you a sanctioned, balanced approach that respects the base game’s design while pushing it into a more cinematic space.
