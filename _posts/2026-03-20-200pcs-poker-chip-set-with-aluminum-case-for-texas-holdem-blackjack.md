The chips in this kit are typically a clay composite with resin, which yields that satisfying “clack” when you slam the pile down, and a tactile heft that makes a good rattle on surrender. Weight distribution matters: you don’t want the same weight in every chip or chips that feel hollow.

Color distribution is important for quick recognition during a fast-paced hand. The standard distribution is something like:
- White or gray for the smallest denomination
- Red for the mid-range
- Blue and green for mid-to-high
- Black or purple for the highest

## Durability and Maintenance
Chips are tough, but they’re not invincible. A few rules of maintenance will keep your set on the table for longer than your average Friday night:
- Keep the case dry and away from moisture. Chips don’t like humidity; it makes them swell a notch and the edge spots can misalign.
- Avoid harsh chemicals for cleaning; a damp cloth is enough to wipe away coffee rings or stray smudges.
- When not in use, store in a cool, dry place. Temperature swings can slightly alter the case’s fit and chip feel over long periods.

If you’ve got kids or pets, consider adding a small “no food or drink near the chips” sign to your setup. The only bite you want is from the chips, not from a curious pooch who might think they’re edible.

You might also want to occasional dry the case and the foam with a soft brush to keep dust and lint from influencing the chips’ longevity.
