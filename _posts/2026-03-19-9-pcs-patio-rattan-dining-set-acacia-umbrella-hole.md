## Maintenance myths vs reality: keeping it pristine
Reality check: outdoor furniture is a long-term investment in your outdoor living happiness. A quick maintenance routine goes a long way. Here are practical tips that won’t bore your neighbor who keeps a bird feeder from the 1980s:

- Clean cushions regularly with a mild soap solution and let air dry completely. Do not machine wash the cushion foam; it will degrade faster than a rebooted console.
- Wipe the Acacia table with a damp cloth and reapply a sealant every season or as needed. If you notice water beading on the surface, you’re on the right track; if it soaks in, it’s time to reseal.
- Use a cover during the off-season. It’s the difference between a furniture investment and a furniture sun-soaker that looks like a relic after year one.
- Check for loose screws and tighten gently; overtightening can strip threads and reduce structural integrity. If you’re new to DIY hardware, remember: good things come to those who use a gentle touch.

Weather considerations: the set holds up well in sun and rain, but prolonged exposure to salty air or heavy winds can take its toll. In such cases, store cushions indoors when not in use and consider a shade sail to reduce UV exposure over time. Keeping the umbrella covered when not in use also helps preserve the mechanism for tilt and adjustment.
