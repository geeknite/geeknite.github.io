## Performance: Speeds, Tests, and Reality

Speed is the crux of any portable SSD review. The X8 touts up to around 1,000 MB/s in both read and write under ideal conditions. In the real world, your performance will depend on several variables:

- Host device (USB-C port, chipset, and firmware)
- Cable quality and length
- File type and size (large sequential transfers behave differently than many small random writes)
- Whether the drive is thermally constrained during long transfers

In our testing matrix, we ran a handful of representative scenarios to simulate real-use cases. You’ll see big differences between sustained large-file transfers and random-access workloads. The important part is: the X8 performs consistently well in typical consumer workloads, and it doesn’t require you to run a lab in order to enjoy fast copies.

Test Scenario A: Large sequential copy (movies, ISOs). On a USB-C Gen 2 port with a good cable, the X8 hovered around 900–980 MB/s for reads and similar for writes, with occasional dips into the 700s when the drive throttled due to heat after sustained long copies. This is still substantially faster than typical HDD-based externals and most older USB 3.0 drives.

Test Scenario B: Mixed media (photos, RAW images, and 8K QuickTime proxies). Here you’ll see more variance: reads in the 600–900 MB/s range and writes trending a bit lower, depending on file fragmentation and metadata. It’s a reminder that real-world performance isn’t a single number; it’s a waveform of user activity.

Test Scenario C: Random I/O (photos, game assets, app data). Random performance tends to be the Achilles heel of consumer SSDs, but the X8 still demonstrates decent IOPS for a portable drive, which translates into snappy game asset loads and quicker app launches when you’re away from your desktop.

If you want to compare to a known competitor, watch for how the X8 stacks up against similar drivers like the Samsung T7/T7 Shield or SanDisk/WD My Passport SSD series. You’ll find mixed results depending on the exact firmware and host accelerators involved.
