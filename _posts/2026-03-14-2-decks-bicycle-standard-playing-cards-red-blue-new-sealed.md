## Maintenance and Longevity

### Cleaning and Care

A simple wipe-down after sessions helps keep the finish intact and your moves crisp. Avoid exposing the cards to prolonged moisture; water and cardstock do not mix well. If you spill something sugary or sticky, a quick wipe and a gentle air-dry will prevent sugar-coated misfires in future performances. Store the decks in their box when not in use, and consider using a card shuffler or a soft cloth to reduce friction when you’re deep in thought about your next big reveal.

### Storage and Longevity

Two decks means more surface area to maintain, but it also means you can rotate decks to reduce wear on any single set. If you’re stacking decks on a shelf, place them upright to prevent warping and keep them away from direct sunlight, which can fade the color backings over time. With careful handling, these decks can remain reliable for many games, tricks, and after-hours card-flinging experiments.
