## Performance and Real World Tests
### Sequential Read and Write Speeds
The official numbers claim up to 1050 MB s read and up to 1000 MB s write on USB 3.2 Gen 2. In our lab friendly tests on a capable USB-C host, the X8 hit the upper echelons of its spec. Copying a 20 GB video folder from the X8 to the host hovered around the 90 to 100 seconds mark for the larger contiguous blocks. A 50 GB asset pack transfer landed in the 4 to 6 minute range depending on file fragmentation and the host bus speed. In practical terms this performance translates to a workflow where moving large media or project files between machines no longer feels like a waiting game.

Small file performance, which is often the pain point for portable drives, remained respectable. The drive handled batches of thousands of little items without the typical stutter you sometimes see on budget options. This makes the X8 a capable tool for cataloging photo shoots, moving design assets, or shuttling a handful of game mods between machines for a LAN party.
