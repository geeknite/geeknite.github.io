## Durability and Maintenance: Built to Weather the Storm(s)
Outdoor rugs have two jobs: 1) handle the weather, and 2) stay presentable while your neighbor’s dog treats your yard like a parkour course. The Unbound rug is designed with that dual purpose in mind. Here’s how it holds up in real-life scenarios:

- Weather Resistance: It’s UV-stable and water-resistant, which means you won’t have to panic if the sun decides to flamboyantly bake your floor into a souvenir of summer. Rain beads up, dries quickly, and doesn’t leave a suspiciously soggy patch that looks like a bad art project.
- Stain Resistance: Accidents happen. Spills? Yes. Red wine? Maybe. Tracking-in-dirt that looks suspiciously like a mud-colored philosophical debate? Probably. But the polypropylene fibers are designed to resist staining and to be easily cleaned with a hose, mild detergent, or a quick wipe-down with a damp cloth.
- Durability: This rug isn’t going to be offended by foot traffic, pet claws, or the occasional dropped brunch plate. It’s low-pile in a way that favors practicality over pretension, which means fewer worries when you’re hosting the neighborhood barbecue or just trying to lounge like a majestically lazy spaceman.

Maintenance-wise, the recommended approach is simple: regular sweeping to remove grit, occasional shakedown for dust, and a quick rinse when life hands you a stubborn spill. If you’re really into “set-it-and-forget-it,” you can also use a mild carpet cleaner, but always test a small area first to avoid any color surprises.

### Practical Cleaning Tips from a Rug-Addicted Geek
1) Treat stains promptly with a mild detergent and a soft cloth. 2) For outdoors, a quick hose-off after a heavy rain will do wonders. 3) Avoid harsh scrubbers that can fray the fibers; you’re aiming for a spa day, not a crypto-rebelling scrubbing party. 4) Let it dry completely before heavy use to prevent mold returns and the suspenseful suspense about under-rug fungus.
