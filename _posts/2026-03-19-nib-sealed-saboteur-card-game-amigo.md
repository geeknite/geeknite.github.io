## Gameplay Flow: How a Round Feels
A typical round in Saboteur Card Game is fast and spicy:
1) Each player is assigned a role, kept secret from others. There should be at least one Saboteur and several Prospector roles.
2) Players draw a hand of cards from the deck. The starting position on the table is often a corridor or entry point, depending on the edition.
3) On your turn you may play a path card to extend the tunnel, or use an action card to hinder others, or draw new cards to keep your options open.
4) The round continues until the treasure card is uncovered or a path to the treasure is blocked and someone claims victory by a different route (or you reach a natural stalemate, which is rare but glorious when it happens).

The dynamic of deception is the charm. The Saboteur in your group will likely be the one who keeps suggesting that a given route is perfectly safe—only for someone else to realize that the path curves back into a trap. The balance between risk and reward is a spicy spice that makes the game a delight for groups who enjoy a quick pivot, a bluff, and a little drama.
