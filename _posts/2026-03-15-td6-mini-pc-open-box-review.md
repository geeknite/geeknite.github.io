## Upgrades and Expandability: What Can You Change, What Should You Expect

Open-box or not, it’s worth considering what you can or cannot upgrade. The TD6’s appeal often lies in its simplicity: a compact, no-nonsense system that does the basics with grace. On many minis at this price point, RAM is soldered or paired with a socket that isn’t always user-upgrade friendly. Storage sometimes hides behind a replaceable 2.5-inch SATA bay or an M.2 slot; other times you’re dealing with a soldered SSD and a tiny SSD that you can’t easily swap without disassembly and, you know, a tiny Spudger of Courage. Here’s how to think about upgradeability in general terms for a TD6-like unit:

- RAM: If it’s 8GB DDR4 soldered, you’ll be limited to what you currently have. If there’s an accessible RAM slot, you might be able to upgrade to 16GB or more, depending on the board. Check the model’s manual or product page for a clear RAM path.
- Storage: A 128GB SSD is a light footprint. If there’s an M.2 slot or a 2.5-inch bay, you can add larger storage or a secondary drive. If not, you’ll be relying on USB-based external storage for more capacity.
- Expansion: Mini PCs of this genre usually don’t hide PCIe lanes behind a thousand-socket sandwich. Expect limited PCIe expansion beyond USB and video outputs. If your goal is a “tiny server” or “compact family PC,” that’s typically enough, especially when you couple it with network sharing and cloud storage.

If you’re hoping to play the upgrade game, you’ll want to confirm whether your specific TD6 unit supports upgrading RAM and/or storage—Open-box units may vary in this respect since some components could have been swapped in the return or re-shelved. Always check the seller’s notes and the hardware ID to avoid wasting time on a box that’s already maxed out elsewhere.

## Comparisons: How Does It Stack Up Against the Big Guys and the Tiny Ones?

- vs. Raspberry Pi 4/400: The Raspberry Pi is a different animal—cheaper, more hobbyist, and not designed for Windows support out of the box (though Linux is very doable). The TD6 has more CPU muscle, more RAM headroom, and the ability to run a full desktop OS with a more traditional app ecosystem.
