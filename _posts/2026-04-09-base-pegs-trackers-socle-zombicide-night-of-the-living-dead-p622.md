## The Building Blocks: Base, Pegs, Trackers, and Socle

### Base: The stage of your undead opera
The base is the stage on which the entire night will unfold. It’s robust enough to survive a reasonable amount of dice chucking and the occasional topple that happens when your cat decides it’s the perfect time to audit your game night. The surface is grippier than it looks, with a texture that doesn’t encourage slippage while still letting your minis sit where you want them. It’s not a glossy studio floor—this is a battlefield with character. If you’ve ever had to stage a tiny cityscape on a kitchen table, you’ll appreciate a base that doesn’t slide around like a cat on a polished floor during a thunderstorm.

### Pegs: The brave little pegholders of fate
Pegs are the tiny, pointy plastic heroes that hold everything together (figuratively and literally, depending on your playstyle). They slot into holes with a satisfying click that says, “Congrats, you now officially own a system that can cause a very mild existential crisis.” They come in a few colors—usually neutrals with a splash here and there. Their job is to support terrain and zombie tokens while never smirking when you forget to count a plus one modifier. If you’re a collector, you’ll want to organize by color and capacity so you can narrate your own internal Highlander-style sales pitch: There can be only… accurate track-keeping.

### Trackers: The little numerical gods of survival
Trackers are your scorecards, initiative counters, wound trackers, and general barometers of “how bad is it going to get before I stop pretending I’m a responsible adult and instead pretend I’m a wily necromancer?” They’re compact, readable, and satisfyingly tactile. The tactile joy is real: sliding a tracker along a rail or fitting it into a notch provides tiny dopamine hits that say, yes, you are in charge of a slowly escalating doomsday scenario and you’re doing fine, probably. The downside: they’re small, so if you’re aggressively clumsy, they’ll audition for the role of “missing item #3.” Pro-tip: store them in a tray with lipped edges so you don’t wake up with a midnight swarming of hidden trackers auditioning for a sequel.

### Socle: The quiet support staff you forgot you needed
Socle, or the base’s base, is the foundation that keeps everything from wobbling into your beverage and becoming a temporary zombie obstacle. It’s the invisible hero—like a stand mixer in a world of hand-whisks. It stabilizes terrain tiles, helps with stacking scenarios, and ensures you don’t spend a third of your session chasing the one tile that decided it wants to be an independent island. The socle’s presence is a gently persuasive reminder that sometimes the strongest asset in your post-apocalyptic toolkit is not the hammer or the shotgun, but the furniture-grade support beam that keeps it all from collapsing into chaos.

## Maintenance, durability, and care tips

- Keep pieces dry and away from hot sun to prevent warping of the plastic. Sunlight is the slow zombie that fades color over time.
- Store trackers separately in a soft pouch to avoid scratches on the surface.
- Use a non-slip placemat or tray to keep the entire setup anchored on the table—especially handy on wobbly surfaces.
- If you misplace a small peg, consider ordering a spare set from the same batch to mitigate mix-and-match color confusion during multi-player nights.
