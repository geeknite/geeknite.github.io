## How to play: quick refresher
If you’re familiar with meme-based party games, you’ll adapt quickly here. The general flow goes like this:

- A judge (Grumper) changes each round. The Grumper draws an image card from the Disney-image deck and lays it face up.
- The other players select a caption card from their hands that they think best matches the image—ideally a witty or surprising line that nudges at a Disney meme moment (think “The moment you realize your side quest is actually your main quest” but adapted to a Disney twist).
- The caption cards are handed to the Grumper face down. The Grumper shuffles, then reads them aloud, or displays them in a way that minimizes bias.
- The Grumper picks the funniest or most on-brand pairing and awards the image card to the caption-holder. Points are tallied and the next round begins with a new Grumper.

This cycle continues until you run out of cards, or you hit the agreed-upon end condition (usually a certain score or a time cap). The Disney edition tends to reward clever references to Disney films, character quirks, inside jokes about parades, princess moments that intersect with modern memes, and lighthearted pop culture nods. It’s not about “punch-down” humor or edgy content; it’s about playful recognition of iconic scenes, easily understood by mixed-age groups.

Pro tip: in a long family game night, decide early whether you’ll try to keep your captions clean or lean into cheeky humor. Disney prompts tend to be friendlier than adult-focused meme decks, but there’s still room for sly quips that adults will appreciate more than little ones—and that’s where the real charm comes from.

If you’re curious about the underlying design philosophy, the game designers clearly aimed for accessibility and re-playability. ## Strategy tips for maximizing fun (and points)
- Know your audience: If you’re playing with kids or non-native speakers, aim for captions that rely on simple wordplay or widely recognized Disney memes. Teens and adults can push for subtler humor, but you’ll earn more points by reading the room.
- Use character knowledge to your advantage: If someone in your group adores a certain Disney franchise, tailor captions to align with that love. Inside jokes can pay off big—but only if the Grumper is in on the reference.
- Don’t overthink: The joy of meme games is in quick, clever punches. If your caption seems forced, redraw and keep the tempo up. A well-timed half-smile beats a long-winded pun that lands with a thud.
- Play the long game: In rounds where the Grumper is unyielding, keep notes mentally about which players are great at certain styles of humor and try to draft captions that accentuate their strengths in future rounds.
- Mix it up with post-round rituals: Quick post-game chats about favorite moments can be a great bonding exercise and help you tailor future sessions to your group’s taste.
