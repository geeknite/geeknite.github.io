## How to Play (Brief Overview)

Sushi Go! is a fast drafting game. Each player starts with a hand of five cards, picks one, passes the rest, and repeats until all cards have been drafted. You score the cards based on type: nigiri, maki, dumpling, sashimi, and the various rolls that make your kitchen look like a satirical version of a Michelin star buffet.

In the Travel Size, the core rules stay intact; the main difference is the deck size and the simplified score track. The pace remains brisk, usually around 10-15 minutes per round, and the rulebook emphasizes quick setup, quick drafting, quick clearing. The travel edition typically supports 2-4 players; sometimes a variant for 5 emerges if you have extra sleeves and a patient bartender.

Key tips for the travel format: keep the decisions simple; don’t overthink the dumplings; and always remember to laugh when you catapult a chopstick into someone’s cup, which is a vibe you’ll encounter in more serious group trips.

## Strategy and Tactics

- When playing with 2 players, the table feels intimate and cruel in a way that only a tiny card game can deliver. You will be reading your opponent like a sushi menu, trying to anticipate what they’ll draft next.

- With 4 players you have more shenanigans; you’ll watch the table snap up cards you hoped to land, and you’ll curse the RNG gods for granting your neighbor the perfect sashimi sequence.

- Pay attention to the endgame: you can bank a few points from small cards, but your big swings are often built around the big synergy combos. The Travel Size version doesn’t dilute the joy; it compresses it into a perfect bite-sized experience.

- Don’t forget the power of fake combos: sometimes taking a sushi roll you don’t want can block your rival from finishing their own high-value set. It’s like chess, if chess used seaweed and wasp-like wasabi.
