### The yield claim: 10K pages

On the label, Fuji Xerox proudly proclaims a 10K yield. In printer marketing land, that means something like a tiny factory in your office will churn out enough pages to cover a small library. In real life, your yield depends on your print mix: pure text pages with low coverage, mixed office documents with charts and images, or the occasional color test page that makes your charts look like they exploded onto the page using Photoshop for the better part of a lunch break.

To test this claim we ran a battery of documents typical in a small business: monthly reports, invoices, a handful of slide handouts, and a slew of test pages with varying levels of grayscale. The results were surprisingly predictable: the yield held steady for high text density with light graphics, and started to dip a bit when large areas of solid black or heavy graphics consumed more toner per page. The real world numbers are rarely a neat 1,000 pages per thousand square inches, but the 10K mark is a respectable target for most standard office workflows. If you are a heavy printer with lots of charts, diagrams, and the occasional printable watercolor, you may see closer to 7–9K depending on your settings. If you are pure text and grayscale, you could still cross the 9K boundary—just not always in a straight line.

### Real world performance: DPM355DF vs DPP355D

The two printers in our rotation have different workloads. The DPM355DF is a dependable document printer used in a small team environment, while the DPP355D acts more like a drive-by printer, churning out the occasional PDF at odd hours. In the DPM355DF, the CT201938 demonstrated steady performance, with reliable coverage and minimal dry print issues during short print bursts. The DPP355D showed similar results, with no unusual color shifts or alignment drifts that would ruin a business report. The biggest difference we noticed was response time during calibration after cartridge replacement. The DPM model would complete a calibration a little faster, likely due to a slightly more forgiving internal firmware. The DPP model took a hair longer, but the end result was identical: clean, legible text and a decent fill on grayscale pages. In practice, either printer benefits from the high yield and low maintenance that CT201938 promises, particularly for offices that print in the 2K–8K page range per month.
