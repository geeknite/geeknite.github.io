### Maintenance and durability: care without anxiety
Wool is inherently more stain resistant and durable than many synthetics. This rug benefits from straightforward care: regular vacuuming with a vacuum that has a soft brush attachment, rotation every few months to avoid uneven wear in high traffic areas, and quick blotting of spills when they happen. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners as they can degrade wool fibers; instead, use a mild detergent solution and dry thoroughly. If you suspect a spill is persistent, consider gentle professional cleaning. This is not a throwaway decor piece; it is an investment that continues to add value as it ages gracefully. The edge finish is robust, and the hand finishing gives you a sense that someone cared about small details. Over time you may discover pet hair and lint collect more easily on lighter colors, so a quick lint roller or fabric brush becomes a good daily habit rather than a chore.
