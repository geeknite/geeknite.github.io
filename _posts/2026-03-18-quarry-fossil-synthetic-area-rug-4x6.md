## Durability and Maintenance

Durability is where synthetic fibers often shine, and the Quarry Rug leans into that reputation. The blend is designed to resist wear in high traffic zones, which is essential for family rooms, entryways, and those spaces where your life happens in a constant loop of footfalls. The anti-slip backing helps keep things in place, a feature that reduces the hazards of toddlers and adults who pretend they are on a slippery dance floor—nobody wants a rug skidding away like a runaway asteroid during a moment of gravity induced drama.

Maintenance is straightforward. Regular vacuuming keeps surface dust and pet hair from becoming a fossil in its own right. For spills, the recommended approach is to blot gently and use mild soap with water if needed. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade color or backing. The rug is not typically machine washable; that is the case for most area rugs with patterns that risk color migration if soaked for too long. In a pinch, spot cleaning with a damp cloth and a little elbow grease will keep the fossil imprints looking crisp and avoid that worn look that happens when you spill a latte and pretend it never happened. Real fossil beds can tolerate erosion; your rug should tolerate a few spills and a heroic vacuum pass.

If you have pets, the little claws can create a little texture in the carpet over time. That adds to the character of a fossil-like surface, but you may wish to rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear across the pattern. The good news is that the fiber structure tends to maintain its shape well after multiple vacuum cycles, which means fewer wonky edges or ripples that make your cat laugh at your geometry calculations.
