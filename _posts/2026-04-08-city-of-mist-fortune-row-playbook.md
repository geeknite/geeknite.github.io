City of Mist is a game that wears a trench coat and a fedoras like it is still 2014, which is to say it knows how to have a noir-y good time while also watching your back for a metaphorical alleyway full of metaphorical hail marys. Fortune Row, as a playbook option, pushes into a space where luck, risk, and the mechanics of fate collide with the street level chrome of a modern city. It invites players to lean into glamour and grit with equal vigor, to chase luck as a resource, and to decide whether fortune is a friend or a trap. In this review we will dive into what makes Fortune Row shine, where it may stumble, and how to weave it into your sessions without turning your table into a chaotic carnival of dice and drama.

## What is Fortune Row in City of Mist?
Fortune Row is a playbook that slots into the broader City of Mist engine as a character type rooted in luck, probability, and the flexible, sometimes slippery, moral choices that luck affords. This is the kind of playbook that can feel like a chorus of whispers, a gambler at the edges of a neon night who can bend outcomes through wit, will, and a bit of courage. It is not a brute tank or a pure investigator; it is someone who can tilt the odds, manipulate narrative time, and, with the right angle, turn a bad roll into a story worth telling at the after party of the city.

Fortune Row shines when you want a character who embodies the tension between potential and consequence. They can be bold about chasing opportunities and cunning about avoiding the consequences of those choices. This creates opportunities for dynamic scenes where players can decide between cashing in a risky bet and hedging for a safer outcome. The playbook also has a flavor that fits the City of Mist aesthetic — a modern mythic vibe — while still offering grounded, street-level hooks that GMs can use to anchor sessions in a city that feels both familiar and larger-than-life.

## Core Concepts and Theme Feel
### Luck as a Narrative Tool
Fortune Row treats luck as a resource that can be spent or traded like a currency. The playbook gives you moves and triggers that reward daring choices, clever misdirection, and timely restraint. The way luck interacts with the mechanics tends to produce scenes where small decisions compound into big consequences. It mirrors the idea that in a city of masks and myths, your fate is rarely sealed by a single heroic moment; it is crafted by a sequence of choices that feel fated but aren’t strictly predetermined.

### The Mask and the Row
As with other City of Mist playbooks, Fortune Row blends a modern persona with a mythic identity. The “Row” aspect suggests a crew, a project, or a social thread the character is pursuing — a gang, a business venture, or a cause—something that gives the luck-driven narrative a macroscopic goal. This gives the GM and players a natural entry point to weave personal stakes into the larger mythic fabric of the city. The twofold identity — the everyday and the mythic — provides fertile ground for scenes where Fortune Row must choose which version of themselves to reveal to the city at large.

## Mechanics at a Glance
### Stats, Moves, and the Rhythm of Action
Fortune Row builds on City of Mist standard mechanics: you have shadows (or stats) tied to your traits and a set of moves that trigger under specific conditions. What makes Fortune Row interesting is how it nudges players to create opportunities rather than rely on brute force. The playbook typically emphasizes improvisation, timing, and leverage — you want to be in the right place at the right time, with enough luck to determine the outcome without making the rest of the table feel like passive spectators.

A typical Fortune Row arc uses moves that let you do things like flip a risky situation into a favorable one, swap the odds in your favor through social maneuvering, and dramatically reveal a moment of luck that changes the trajectory of a scene. Because luck is a resource, there are built-in prompts for when to roll and when to conserve, which keeps the pacing lively without devolving into a pure dice slog.

### Abilities and Customization
Fortune Row provides a toolkit rather than a rigid ladder. You’ll see a mix of social prowess, quick thinking, and a hint of mythic utility. The customization comes through choosing a mythic identity that best aligns with your idea of luck: a patron deity, a legendary gambler, a trickster spirit, or a cultural archetype that embodies chance and change.

This flexibility supports both theory crafting and practical play: you can shape a Fortune Row into a suave con artist who manipulates perceptions, or a street-smart survivor who reads micro-signals that others miss. The balance between cunning and risk is what makes the playbook feel alive at the table, and the narrative payoff tends to be bigger when the player leans into the ambiguity of luck rather than claiming absolute control over outcomes.

### Progression and Growth
Fortune Row is designed to reward storytelling over crunchy optimization. The character grows through story beats, personal revelations, and the escalating stakes of their chosen Row. You might start by chasing a simple score and end up confronting a mythic power that demands you weigh the cost of your luck against the lives of those you care about. The sense of growth here is not about leveling up a stat line but about the character becoming a more complicated agent within the city’s mythic ecosystem.

This is where the GM’s guidance becomes crucial. If the table leans too hard into deterministic luck, the session can feel manipulative; if it leans too far into chaos, Fortune Row may become a luck cannon that fires unpredictably with little narrative payoff. A good balance cultivates scenes where luck emerges as a character in its own right — giving players something to chase, something to fear, and something to celebrate when the dice align with a satisfying story beat.

## Visuals, Feel, and Table Presence
The aesthetic of Fortune Row benefits from a few well-chosen touches. If you’re running this playbook, consider props or micro-scenarios that evoke neon rain, a city at once glamorous and dangerous, and the sense that luck is both a privilege and a responsibility. The art direction can play with juxtaposition: a glamorous, well-dressed figure in a rain-soaked alley, a cashmere glove reaching for a coin while a crowd moves like a tide in the background. Small, tactile cues help players inhabit the mythic vibe without turning the session into a cosplay parade.

## Story Seeds and Session Ideas
To help you drop Fortune Row into your campaign, here are a handful of narrative spine ideas you can customize to fit your table and setting:

- The Lucky Break Disconnect: Fortune Row receives a string of improbable wins that seem too convenient. The GM hints that a hidden adversary is manipulating the odds. The player must decide whether to chase the streak or uncover the truth behind the pattern before a costly payoff arrives.
- The Row's Old Debt: A debt to a mythic patron comes due. The city demands a sacrifice of luck in exchange for a major boon, and Fortune Row must determine who benefits and who pays the price.
- The Masked Benefactor: A benefactor who never loses money asks for a favor that would tilt the city’s power balance. This forces the Fortune Row player into a moral calculus about the nature of luck and who deserves to wield it.
- The Gambler’s Choice: A scenario where the player can trade a personal secret for a guaranteed outcome. The choice reveals a hidden vulnerability that could redefine the character and the Row’s ambitions.

These seeds are designed to be modular and scalable. You can compress them into single-session arcs or expand them into a multi-session arc that culminates in a dramatic clash between luck and consequence. The key is to give Fortune Row chances to monetize luck, push for meaningful outcomes, and then force a reckoning that reshapes the character and the Row’s place in the city.

## GM Guidance: Running Fortune Row Smoothly
### Framing the Stakes
Fortune Row thrives when the GM frames stakes as both personal and systemic. Luck can tilt outcomes, but the city itself will push back in unexpected ways. It’s a good practice to layer consequences that feel cosmic but still tethered to the players’ choices. For example, a crowd favorite might receive a lucky break that saves a life at a cost that only becomes apparent later, or a glamorous score could unlock a new enemy who has been watching from the wings.

### Keeping Pace with Luck
A common hazard is letting the luck engine run on autopilot. The GM should interject with narrative prompts that remind players that luck is a shared credit. If a player uses a Lucky Move, the GM can offer a consequence in the next scene that heightens tension. This keeps the table from entering a loop where the same outcome repeats and makes each best luck feel earned rather than preordained.

### Integrating with Other Playbooks
Fortune Row is most fun when it interacts with the other City of Mist playbooks. A Fortune Row character can be the hinge that pulls multiple mythic threads toward a single grand reveal. Encourage party composition where one member’s skill set complements another’s, so that the combination of luck and competence yields dramatic, cinematic scenes rather than a string of solo victories.

## Sample Scenes and Narrative Techniques
### Scene 1: A City That Believes in Coincidences
The Fortune Row player finds themselves in a crowded public square where a string of improbable coincidences aligns perfectly with the city’s pulse. A rumor begins that someone controls the odds in the city. The move here is to read the room, identify who benefits from these coincidences, and decide whether to reveal the truth or let the pattern flourish. The thrill comes from watching the crowd react as a single favorable event cascades into a chain reaction of smart choices made by the player and their allies.

### Scene 2: The Edge of a Deal
A deal is proposed that promises one glorious outcome, but the price is steep and the terms are murky. Fortune Row must decide whether to take the bet, negotiate a safer version, or honor a higher moral code and walk away. The tension is not only about the outcome but also about how the decision changes the Row’s relationship with power in the city.

### Scene 3: The Unraveling of a Mythic Patron
A patron who has been guiding the Row’s luck reveals a hidden clause in the agreement. The player must confront the possibility that the patron’s own luck is a weapon, and that it may turn on the Row if misused. This can be a powerful pivot point that shifts the direction of the campaign, potentially elevating a one-off character to a true mythic figure in the city’s ongoing saga.

## Practical Tips for Players
- Lean into the ambiguity of luck. Instead of forcing a perfect outcome, frame your choices as high-stakes bets with meaningful tradeoffs.
- Use social leverage as a core mechanic. Fortune Row shines when you turn conversations and impressions into advantages, not just dice results.
- Embrace thematic identity. The mythic side of Fortune Row should feel like a reflection of real-world aspirations and anxieties — fame, wealth, reputation, or a personal code worth guarding.
- Collaborate with your GM. Ask for prompts that force you to decide how much you’re willing to risk for a favorable outcome and what happens when that risk backfires.

- Encourages dynamic scene pacing with built-in prompts for consequences

## Final Verdict
Fortune Row is a confident, stylish addition to City of Mist that invites players to explore the seductive and dangerous side of luck. It rewards creativity, roleplay, and narrative risk-taking while giving the GM ample hooks to propel a campaign toward a climactic reveal. The playbook shines in sessions where luck is treated as a character itself — with a personality, quirks, and a price tag. If your table loves noir vibes, mythic urban fantasy, and a dash of magical gambling, Fortune Row will likely become a favorite in your rotation.

If you are aiming for a character who can bend moments into opportunities without turning every scene into a dice roller, Fortune Row gives you that sweet spot. It can be a little slippery if the table is not aligned around the idea that luck has costs, but with some good GM guidance and thoughtful scene design, it becomes a source of powerful storytelling rather than a mechanical cheat code.
