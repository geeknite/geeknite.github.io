### Screens, privacy, and acoustics
The W Screens are designed to dampen ambient spill and provide a measure of acoustic separation. In a four-person setup, conversations will inevitably overlap. The screens help by absorbing higher-frequency chatter and reducing cross-talk, which is a polite way of saying you won’t learn about your teammate’s weekend plans from the next desk, unless they’re shouting over the white noise of a printer that’s just learned to scream again.

Privacy panels aren’t purely about secrecy; they’re about control of distraction. When one person needs to focus, they can slide a screen into a more closed position. When the team needs to have a quick brainstorm, they can pivot screens to open side-by-side, enabling a sense of shared space without total chaos.

### Maintenance, durability, and long-term care
The veneer is designed to withstand daily office life: coffee rings, pen marks, and the occasional sticker label that didn’t want to leave the wood. Clean with a soft cloth and mild soap solution; avoid abrasive scrubbers that could dull the finish. The metal frame benefits from occasional wipe-downs to keep fingerprints in check and to maintain that showroom shine.

For high-traffic offices, you’ll want to schedule periodic inspections of the screen rails and joints. A small amount of wobble is normal over time, but you don’t want a creak to become your team’s unofficial meeting soundtrack. Tightening screws and realigning panels during routine maintenance will extend the product’s life and keep it feeling solid rather than flimsy after the third sprint review.
