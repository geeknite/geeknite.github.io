### Maintenance and Cleaning
Care is fairly straightforward, which makes this rug friendly to busy households. Here are practical tips:
- Vacuum regularly with a vacuum that has adjustable height, to avoid loosening fibers or creating “wadge” patterns.
- Blot spills quickly with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing; birthing coffee stains is a terrible idea for anyone trying to be stylish and not a coffee sponge.
- For deeper cleaning, use a mild detergent solution and a damp cloth, then air dry. Avoid soaking, as excessive moisture can cause the backing to loosen and the texture to misbehave.
- For pet owners, the natural color helps disguise minor accidents, but quick action is still recommended to prevent set-in odors.

## Custom Sizing: Why Custom Sizes Matter
Custom sizing is the superpower here. Many rooms in real life refuse to be.iched into tidy 6x9 or 8x10 boxes. The Linden Honey Quartz Sisal rug comes in a custom size option that lets you tailor the rug to fit the exact dimensions of your space. Whether you re trying to fill an awkward alcove, align with a sectional in a way that creates a visually balanced room, or simply want a rug that makes your space feel intentional, custom sizing helps you avoid the common pitfalls of mismatched borders and awkward gaps.

If you need an 8x10, that s a classic configuration that suits many living rooms, bedrooms, and home offices. An 8x10 rug gives you a comfortable buffer around furniture and creates a cohesive anchor for your layout. But if your room has corners that refuse to cooperate with orthogonal geometry, you can request a custom size that lets the rug bend and follow your room s peculiar personality.

### How to Order Custom Sizes
Ordering a custom size typically involves sharing your room dimensions with the retailer or manufacturer, selecting the base color and finish, choosing the rug thickness or pile height, and providing a few measurements for edge finishing. The Linden line is marketed as flexible, but remember that custom orders may take a bit longer to produce and ship. Here are practical tips:
- Measure twice, cry once. You want to be sure you re not sending a rug that is a quarter inch too long or too short.
- Plan the layout before ordering. Sketch the room, note door swing, and consider underlay or rug pad options for extra stability.
- Ask about return policies on custom sizes. Some retailers offer written assurances but the reality might involve a longer wait or a different policy than standard sizes.
