## What is Herd Mentality?

### The premise
Herd Mentality is a fast-paced party guessing game. Each round, a prompt card is revealed. Everyone writes down an answer, but here’s the twist: you want to align your answer with what you think the majority will choose. The more you match the majority, the more points you earn. The humor comes from how wrong you can be—yet how often you nail it when you least expect to. It’s a delightful social paradox: you want to think like the herd, but you also want to react against the herd just enough to claim victory. The game thrives on social dynamics: the fear of standing out, the joy of being “in the know,” and the occasional delicious sting when your brilliant-but-rare answer wins you nothing because no one expects it.

### Why it’s addictive
If you’ve ever watched a room full of people shout “Of course the majority will pick X!” and then watched the minority win because they picked the counterintuitive choice, you’ve felt the Herd Mentality pull. It’s not just about right or wrong; it’s about social alignment. The scoring reward keeps you rooting for the misfit option that somehow becomes fashionable mid-round. The gameplay loops on itself: you guess what others will guess, you reveal, your friends react, then you laugh, then you do it again with some new prompts. The repeatability comes from the endless variety of prompts and the fact that no two game nights ever play exactly the same way. And yes, this means you’ll sometimes find yourself arguing passionately about whether “pineapple on pizza” is a clever joke or a timeless truth—the debate is half the entertainment.

## How to play (quick-start guide)

### Setup
1. Shuffle the prompt cards and place the deck in the center.
2. Give each player a number of tokens or slips of paper for secret answers. If you’re playing with kids or newcomers, you can simply use sticky notes.
3. Decide on a starting player. This is often whoever owns the snack closest to the couch, which is a surprisingly reliable host cue.

### The rounds
1. The active player draws a prompt card and reads it aloud to everyone. Prompts range from silly to provocative—think “My dream superpower is___” or “The best snack at a party is___” or “The most likely to forget their own birthday is___.”
2. Each player writes down their answer privately. If you’re playing in person with kids, you can share answers aloud; if you’re playing online, use a private chat to submit your response.
3. After everyone has written an answer, all responses are revealed simultaneously. Round up the votes: players point at or shout out the answer they think is the majority. In some variants, players rank the answers by popularity.
4. Scoring: players earn points for matching the majority. The bigger the majority’s size, the more points you’ll earn for being in sync with the crowd. However, you can also earn bonus points for clever, unexpected, or especially funny responses that the group loved but didn’t want to admit would be popular.
5. The role of the moderator rotates, and the next round begins with a new prompt. The round continues until a predetermined number of rounds are played or a target score is reached.

### Endgame and scoring
The simplest path to victory is to set a target score (e.g., 40–60 points for a 60–90 minute session). If you want a more cinematic finale, you can run a “finale round” where the last round grants extra points for the winner’s guesses. The key is to keep it fun and flexible rather than rigid. The beauty of Herd Mentality is that even when you’re losing, you’re not really losing—because the specter of social embarrassment has been replaced by laughter, bonding, and ridiculous stories about why someone wrote “my pet hamster” for “the most unexpected hero of the week.”

## Why Herd Mentality shines on game nights

### Social dynamics
The heart of Herd Mentality is social chemistry. It thrives in rooms where people feel comfortable laughing at themselves and with one another. The scoring rewards alignment with the crowd, which encourages quiet participants to speak up and share. The energy of a room where everyone is trying to forecast where the majority will land is contagious: it creates a rhythm of anticipation, exhaled relief, and the occasional triumphant “I told you so” that is, frankly, part of the fun. The game also scales to different personalities. For extroverts who love being in the spotlight, it gives space to lead the room with bold answers. For introverts, it offers a chance to participate through a quick, low-stakes vote and observe the dramatic reactions of others.

### Laughter and chaos
Let’s be honest: many prompts will lead to choices that are funny in the_context of your group. You’ll see a cascade of reactions as players process which answers align with the majority, and which players will claim “we all knew that” even when the truth was hilariously different. This is where Herd Mentality earns its stripes as a party game: the laughter isn’t just about the answers themselves, but about the social moment—the shared realization that you and your friends think in surprisingly similar ways, or in gloriously different ways and are not afraid to show it.

### The group size and who should host
Herd Mentality works best with 4–8 players for robust interaction, but you can push beyond that with a larger group by dividing into subgroups for quicker rounds or running parallel tables. The host should steer the energy rather than police it. A good moderator keeps things moving, calls out particularly funny responses, and ensures everyone gets a chance to contribute. If you’re hosting a mixed-age crowd, consider prompting age-appropriate questions and pre-teaching a few rules to keep younger players from feeling overwhelmed by the pace.

## Variants and house rules
If you’re a rule tinkerer (we know you are), here are a few ways to customize Herd Mentality for your crew.

### Quick-fire prompts
For a high-energy night, introduce a timer for each round (e.g., 60 seconds) and require players to write fast, fierce answers. The pressure often yields gut-punch honesty and even bigger laughter. It also reduces the risk of overthinking, which is where a lot of fun gets drained.

### More inclusive prompts
Want to reduce a few awkward moments? Use prompts that are inclusive rather than divisive. For example, “The best snack at a party is___” tends to generate universal interest, while “Who in this room would survive the zombie apocalypse by eating the most questionable thing” might trigger a riot of personal jokes. You can curate prompts from a separate deck or supplement with your own prompts to suit your group’s sensibilities.

### For remote game nights
Herd Mentality translates surprisingly well to online play. Use a shared whiteboard or chat for answers, and rely on “poll vote” style reactions for the majority. A longer video call with a rotating host can mimic the in-person moderator, and you can sprinkle in “round-robin” rounds where each participant must contribute one answer per round. The key is to keep the pace steady and the prompts varied enough to prevent tech fatigue.

### Thematic prompts and expansions
If you want to keep the party fresh over many sessions, consider adding fan-made prompt cards with a specific theme: ’80s nostalgia, sci-fi eruptions, or foodie fables. The expansion can be as simple as printing your own prompts on cards or purchasing a small add-on deck if you’re feeling fancy. The thematic prompts tend to fuel stronger group memories and—bonus—great inside jokes for future game nights.

## A few practical tips for a great Herd Mentality night

- Start with a warm-up round that includes a couple of “soft prompts” to get the room comfortable with sharing. You don’t want the first prompt to feel like a trial by water-cooler judgment.
- Mix prompt difficulties. Some prompts should be easy for everyone, while others should be a little spicy or bizarre. The contrast keeps the energy high and prevents fatigue.
- Consider a “penalty-free” round where every player’s answers are deemed perfectly valid; this gives people permission to be honest without fear of embarrassment, which is especially helpful for newer players.
- Use a timer for prompts if your group tends to overthink. The timer encourages gut responses, which typically yield the most entertaining reveals.
- After the night, capture a few memorable quotes and share them as a post-event recap. The best way to grow a game-night culture is to turn inside jokes into a shared tradition.
