## Maintenance and care

Caring for a Honshu Quilan dagger is straightforward for the most part. Stainless steel means less rust risk than plain carbon steel, but that does not mean you can ignore it entirely. Here are a few practical tips:

- Wipe down after handling. Oils from your skin can contribute to staining or dulling the finish over time.
- Store in a dry place. Humidity is the enemy of any steel, even stainless varieties, if there are other metals involved in the guard or fittings.
- Use a light oil occasionally to maintain the blade’s surface. A simple mineral oil or a dedicated blade oil will suffice. Wipe off excess to avoid a film.
- Inspect the sheath regularly. Ensure the blade sits securely and that the belt loop or strap is intact. A loose sheath can lead to accidental draws and unwanted scratches on the blade or your display surface.
- For display, consider a soft microfiber cloth surrounding the blade in the sheath during long-term storage to avoid contact with dust and other particulates.

If you plan to use the dagger in photos, consider a minimal, reversible cleaning approach. You want the blade to read as polished, not as a brand-new mirror. A little patina is acceptable on props if you like the character it projects, but avoid aggressive polishing that could remove the blade’s aging look that gives it personality.
