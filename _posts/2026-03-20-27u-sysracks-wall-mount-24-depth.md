## Installation and Setup
Setting up a wall-mounted rack is a two-stage affair: install, then populate. Here’s a practical walkthrough:

1) Choose the wall mount location with a stud layout in mind. You’re not hanging this on drywall alone unless you enjoy emergency phone calls to your IT guy.
2) Attach mounting rails to the cabinet and align with the wall studs. Use the provided screws and cage nuts. Double-check alignment; misaligned rails can turn a five-minute install into a frustration montage.
3) Mount the cabinet to the wall. Ensure it’s level. If you have a laser level, now’s the time to flex it a little.
4) Install power distribution and cable management components first, then begin populating with your devices. Start with the heaviest items at the bottom for stability.
5) Route cables with a thoughtful plan. Use Velcro ties to allow for future upgrades and avoid the “cable lemonade” effect where you squeeze the life out of your patch cords.
6) Power up and test. Check for heat pockets and verify that cooling paths are clear. If your gear whines about airflow, you’ve got work to do.

### Maintenance Tips
- Periodic inspection of cage nuts and rails can prevent drama during a firmware update or a hardware swap.
- Label ports at both ends and keep a simple rack map. It saves you hours when someone asks, Where does this patch cable go again?
- Clean the front filter (if present) and ensure the door seals properly against dust and light. You can’t protect against all gremlins, but you can deter a few with a well-sealed front.
