How to Play a Quick Look

If you already know Exploding Kittens, you’ll notice that the Mini Version keeps the core mechanic intact: don’t explode, or you’re out. The way you avoid blowing up is through card plays that influence draw piles, skip turns, or pass the hot potato of doom to someone else. The only difference is the tempo and the space you’re playing in. The mini format shines in two modes:

- Quick rounds: Shorter cycles. You’ll go from setup to dramatic near-misses in under 15 minutes. Perfect for coffee breaks, or for when you plan to do three back-to-back sessions and pretend it’s “character-building.”
- Party chaos: This version scales well for small gatherings. It’s the kind of game that invites trash talk and negotiations that end up with someone being mercilessly teased about their card-shuffling technique.

The Setup and Turn Flow

1) Shuffle the deck and deal a hand to each player. The exact hand size is smaller than the standard version but still enough to feel like you’re assembling an orchestra of tiny doom.
2) Place the draw pile in the middle. The top card will determine who carries the ticking heart of doom next.
3) On your turn, draw a card and optionally play a kitten or a taunt card. The taunt cards add chaotic modifiers that push the game toward an inevitable, absurd conclusion.
4) If you draw the Exploding Kitten, you are in trouble unless you have a defuse card. The defuse cards are the cheapest superpowers you never asked for but secretly always wanted.
5) The last player remaining wins. It’s simple, fast, and frequently ridiculous.

Strategy Tips and House Rules

- Memorize the draw patterns: In the mini version, the speed means you won’t memorize everything, but you can still get a feel for when someone is about to explode. Use that to your advantage.
- Use defuse cards wisely: They’re the safety net that keeps you in the game longer. Don’t waste them on low-stakes moments; save them for the big swings.
- Create mini alliances: In short games, temporary alliances can help you survive until someone else triggers the chaos. Just make sure you both remember that you might be betraying each other next turn.
- House rules for family friendly chaos: If you’re playing with kids or office colleagues, consider muting the more risqué jokes and swapping them with kid-friendly puns. It’s amazing how a pun can defuse a tense moment without defusing the tension altogether.
