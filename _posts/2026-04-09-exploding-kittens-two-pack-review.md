## how to play in a nutshell

exploding kittens is a light, fast party game built on risk and rapid decision making. the two-pack does not change the core rules, only the number of cards and the number of players you can accommodate. here is a quick refresher on the core flow:

- each player starts with a Defuse card or two, depending on your house rule
- players take turns drawing from the draw pile
- if you draw an Exploding Kitten you must defuse (if you have a Defuse card)
- if you cannot defuse, you're out of the round
- action cards change the flow: you can skip turns, attack others, shuffle the deck, or peek at future draws
- Nope cards can cancel other cards, leading to hilarious counterplays and table talk
- the last player standing takes the crown and the wildly earned bragging rights

playing with two decks simply gives you more of everything: more Defuse cards, more opportunities to slow down or accelerate the flow, and more chances to squeeze out a victory with a well-timed defuse or a well-timed attack on a friend who forgot to keep track of the draw pile.

## strategy and tips

while Exploding Kittens is not a deep strategy title, there are still some moves that can improve your odds and add to the drama:

- manage your Defuse cards: you want to hold a Defuse for the moments when the draw pile gets crowded with Exploding Kitten cards
- use See the Future strategically: peeking ahead can save you from bad draws or allow you to plan a killer attack
- timing of Attack cards: a well-timed attack can force an opponent to draw multiple cards, increasing their risk of exploding
- the power of Nope: cancel a potentially catastrophic move at just the right moment and swing momentum in your favor
- dawn of the big moment: in two-deck play, don’t be afraid to accelerate time with a Shuffle or a See the Future when the table tension spikes

for parents trying to maintain a safe and fun environment, establish a few ground rules up front. ensure everyone is comfortable with the humor and the pace. remind players that this is a family-friendly party game, but it is also a comedy show where the main stars are kittens, not rocket scientists.
