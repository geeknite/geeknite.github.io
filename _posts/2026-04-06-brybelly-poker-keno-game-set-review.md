- Step 1: Set up. Layout the board, shuffle the deck, place chips for betting, and decide whether you’re playing Keno-style draws or poker-style hands.
- Step 2: The draw. In a Keno fashion, numbers 1–80 are potential draws. Use a random method (spinning the wheel if you have one, or a quick dice trick, or simply random card draws) to select a set of numbers. The excitement is in the suspense: will your numbers show up?
- Step 3: Betting. Players can place bets with their colorful chips on the numbers or on cards that align with numbers drawn. If you’re doing pure poker as well, you can assign bets to card outcomes or hand strength.
- Step 4: Showdown. Reveal the drawn numbers and compare them with players’ bets. In a party setting, this results in a lot of dramatic groans and surprising celebrations when someone hits a tiny win.
- Step 5: Payouts. This is where your house rules come in. Do you pay out chips or assign points? Either way, the goal is to keep the energy up and the laughter rolling.

If you want to spice up the experience, you can lean into the adult-oriented theme and add a few cheeky bets.
