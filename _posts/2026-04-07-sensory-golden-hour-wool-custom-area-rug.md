Care and maintenance expectations
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Wool is naturally resilient and self-cleaning to an extent, but it isn’t magic. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting is recommended to avoid matting and thread-pull on busy traffic paths. Spills should be blotted quickly rather than rubbed in, as friction can encourage fibers to either shrink or pill in stubborn patches. For tougher stains, a mild wool-safe cleaner is advised; always test on a small area first. Professional cleaning every few years can help maintain luster and texture, especially in high-traffic areas like entryways and living room walk zones. The rug’s natural fiber means it loves air, not a vacuum canister tsunami; give it space to breathe if you want to preserve that plump feel.

Sizing and installation tips
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If you are opting for a custom size, measure with multiple angles and consider the door swing. A rug that is slightly too large under a doorway can become a stumbling hazard. If you have a room with multiple zones, consider two smaller rugs instead of one large piece to achieve a layered look. For those who worry about slipping tiles or hardwoods, add a rug pad beneath the rug to improve stability and cushioning. A pad can help prevent sculpting under heavy furniture and reduce wear in high-traffic zones.

Care routines for a long-lasting glow
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Vacuum regularly on a low setting to avoid rough brushes tugging on the fibers. Rotate the rug every few months if it sits in a sunny corner to minimize uneven fading. For spills, blot gently and avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the pile. If you notice pills forming along the surface due to heavy chair movements, you can trim them with small scissors carefully. It helps to have a fabric shaver on hand for occasional touch-ups. In short, treat the rug with a light touch and a bit of respect for the material, and it will reward you with years of warmth.
