## What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition

The original edition is a fast paced party game built around a central risk mechanic: survive as long as possible without pulling an exploding kitten card. Instead of the usual strategic grind, this is a nod to chaotic humor and cat shaped chaos. The cards bring a mix of mischief, interaction, and irresistibly silly art. The 2020 reprint keeps that chaos in a neat, brightly colored deck that is easy to teach and hard to resist playing again and again.

### Core components

In this box you will find a deck of cards stuffed with a variety of card types. The majority are action cards that let you alter the flow of the game, cat cards that add personality to the table, a handful of defuse cards that act as lifelines, a few Exploding Kitten cards that end the fun for someone, and a rulebook that explains the clear and simple rules. The art is playful, the humor is accessible, and the mismatch of cute kittens with dangerous bombs creates a satisfying mismatch that makes people laugh even when they lose.

### Setup and first impressions

The setup is refreshingly quick. Shuffle the deck, adjust by removing a few Exploding Kitten cards if you are playing with more players, deal a starter hand to each player, and ensure a few Defuse cards are in play. Place the Exploding Kitten cards back into the deck and distribute any starter defuses. The rules emphasize speed and simplicity, which is absolutely the point. There is no long manual to memorize, just a few basic steps and then you are off to the table. The pacing invites you to lean into chaos and see how long you can survive the turn that almost always ends with a chorus of laughter.

### Turn structure

On your turn you perform a sequence of actions that depends on how many players are left and what cards you hold. You might draw a card if you do not end your turn with an action that changes the draw, or you might use an action card to shift the flow of rounds. You can also play a cat card to boost the vibe or to set up subtle combos that make the late game feel like a tiny comedy of errors. The interplay between action cards and the chance of pulling an Exploding Kitten keeps everyone on their toes. The game rewards timing and quick thinking more than heavy calculation, which is part of its charm.

### Key card types

Exploding Kitten cards are the cliff hanger of the deck. If you draw one and you lack a Defuse card, your run is over and the fun continues with someone else. Defuse cards are lifelines; they buy you precious seconds and possibly a bigger chance to turn the table. Action cards let you manipulate the deck and the draw order. See the Future lets you peek at the top few cards, building a little treetop view of the doom stack. Shuffle reorders the deck to keep the risk fresh and unpredictable. Attack forces the next player to draw even more cards, spreading chaos across the table. Nope is the ultimate comeback card, canceling an action just when the moment seems perfect for your rival to explode into laughter. And then there are the cat cards, which create small but powerful synergies when collected in groups and used to trigger lighthearted power plays.

### Images, humor, and style

The art style is bright and cartoony, with a wink that says the game is here to entertain rather than to threaten your friendships. The humor lands through playful captions and silly visuals, balancing lighthearted silliness with a sense of playful danger. The aesthetic is accessible for a wide age range, which helps Exploding Kittens stay a staple for family game nights and casual gatherings alike. The box shape, the card stock, and the included insert all contribute to a tidy package that travels well for a game night on the go.

## How it plays in practice

We tested Exploding Kittens Original Edition with groups of different sizes and ages. The core experience stays the same: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten while leveraging your Defuse cards and the various action abilities to influence who draws next. In practice this yields a dynamic where the table becomes a stage and each turn is a mini performance. The energy is high, the laughter immediate, and the tension genuinely fun. The simplicity of the rules makes it easy for newcomers to join late in the night without feeling behind, while the quick rounds keep veterans from tuning out between longer games.

### A closer look at strategy

Do not hoard Defuse cards forever; you want to use them at moments that maximize your odds of staying in the game or shifting risk onto someone else. See the Future can be a game changer when the top of the deck holds a lot of Exploding Kitten triggers; planning a safe draw through the future view can buy you a crucial turn. The Attack card, when timed right, can extend the chaos by forcing the next player to draw additional cards, but you need to read the room and count the players left in the game. In the end, the strategy is less about airtight math and more about reading the energy at the table and deciding when to take a risk that might pay off in the next round.

### Cat cards and deck synergy

Cat cards add personality and a subtle strategic tilt. Collecting enough cat cards does not guarantee victory, but it opens up opportunities for small combos and psychological pressure. The synergy comes from keeping a few card types in hand to threaten the next moves in the round, while maintaining a flexible approach that allows you to pivot if the deck tilts toward doom. The humor remains light, making it easy to laugh at your own peril or at the misfortune of a friend who just drew a second Exploding Kitten in a row.

## Where the original edition shines

The 2020 edition preserves the tight core mechanics and the bright humor that defined the early days of Exploding Kittens. It teaches quickly, plays fast, and scales well from a quick 15 minute round to an even faster burst during a party night. It shines in mixed groups where players range in experience and tolerance for silly humor, because the game expects little from you beyond paying attention and joining in the fun. The art and voice of the cards hit the right balance of cute and chaotic, which increases the joy factor even when your hand is a mess.

## Where it might miss the mark

As with many party games that lean into humor, Exploding Kittens can feel repetitive after several rounds. If you crave deeper strategy and long term planning, this might not scratch that itch. The pace can also feel too chaotic for players who prefer precise control over every move. And if the humor style is not your thing, the experience might feel one note after a while. Having said that, most groups find that the laughter wins out and the chaos is part of the charm rather than a flaw.
