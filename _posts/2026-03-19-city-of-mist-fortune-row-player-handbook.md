## What is Fortune Row - Player's Handbook?
Fortune Row - Player's Handbook is a supplemental tome designed to expand the City of Mist universe by focusing on the eponymous district, a place where luck is a tangible resource and where the mythic world often collides with the touch-and-go reality of organized chaos. The book positions Fortune Row as a playground of whispered deals, glamorous disguises, and the trickster patrons who pivot the gravity of the city from a safe distance behind a velvet curtain.

The core concept is simple but deliciously large: power in Fortune Row is not just what you swing with your mask, but what you bluff, what you borrow, and what you persuade the city to owe you back later. It is a setting where street-level loyalties collide with mythic-level obligations, and where a single chair can become a throne when the right rumor circles the right ears.

### What you get in the box (well, the PDF)
- A tight overview of Fortune Row's districts, factions, and iconic locales, all filtered through the City of Mist lens of myth and coincidence.
- Expanded playbooks that lean into fortune-based mechanics, which are a natural fit for a city built on reputations and grudges as currencies.
- New powers, drives, and affiliations that broaden the character arc beyond the original core toolkit.
- Location-based micro-scenarios and hook seeds tailored to a district that thrives on rumor and risk.
- GM-facing guidelines with tone notes, pacing tips, and cautionary tales about pushing players into ethically murky territory—because the city loves drama as much as it loves its mugs of neon rain.

If you were hoping for more masks that feel like arcs in an indie film, Fortune Row delivers. If you were hoping for a clean, tidy, perfectly balanced system without moral ambiguity, you might want to bring a nap and a leash for your sense of doom.

## Thematic Core: Fortune Row as a Narrative Engine
Fortune Row operates on a simple, delicious paradox: luck is a resource you actively manage, and misfortune can be a clever trap or a spectacular pivot. The book leans into the vibe of a district where the glamorous and the grimy exist on a handshake's distance, and every choice feels like it could unlock a fountain of favors or a vault of consequences.

From a narrative perspective, Fortune Row provides several new axes for the story to tilt around. If you love city-wide puzzles that hinge on a single whispered name, you will feel right at home. If you prefer hack-and-slash belts and direct mythic brawls, you will still enjoy the tension, but the emphasis shifts toward negotiation, misdirection, and the clever use of limited resources.

## New Playbooks and How They Fit
The Player's Handbook introduces several new playbooks (which in City of Mist terms means new Ways and Tasks you can weave into a character's mythic identity). Here is a taste of what you can expect:
- The Fortuner: A patron-driven trickster whose luck-based powers are fueled by the city’s whispers. The Fortuner thrives in social scenes, where a well-timed compliment can alter a plan’s trajectory more than a brutal display of power.
- The Ledger: A mathy, risk-friendly mystic who reads the city’s ledger of probabilities. Their power grows when they track chances and convert risk into leverage. They might debit a debt with a favor or rewrite an outcome with a masterful calculation.
- The Velvet Hand: An infiltrator who manipulates the physical and social networks around Fortune Row. Their gifts revolve around misdirection, disguise, and a knack for turning fragile plans into glorious success—or spectacular disaster.
- The Charmed: A pact-maker whose bonds with patrons in Fortune Row shape both personal fate and city-wide intrigues. They do not simply gain power; they harvest stories, turning gossip into a potent resource.

For each new playbook, expect a few mechanical twists that emphasize social capital, rumor economy, and the district’s unique flavor of luck. If your table enjoys long-term character storytelling, these playbooks reward careful play and narrative development rather than one-off skirmishes.

### Playbook Mechanics: Luck, Debt, and the City’s Echo
Fortune Row leans into a three-legged stool: Luck Points, Debt, and Echoes. Luck Points are a resource you accrue by leaning into the story’s coincidences. Debts are how the city keeps players honest; each debt incurred has a trigger that grows in scope as the story unfolds. Echoes, meanwhile, are the narrative memory of the city—the intangible weight of the rumor trail that follows players like a perfume badge and a few well-timed misdirections.

These mechanics are not just counters; they are narrative levers. A good GM can structure a scene so that spending Luck boosts an upcoming rumor payoff, which then creates a debt that must be settled through a choice that reveals more about a character’s weakness or a district’s political tide. It is not a system that forces players to chase numbers; it nudges players toward telling stories that matter to the world they inhabit.

## In the Field: Hooks and Scenarios
The book offers a handful of ready-to-play hooks that map nicely onto Fortune Row's vibe. Here are a few to spark your session planning:
- The Whispering Lottery: A rumored festival where fortunes are literally auctioned off. The players must decide which whispers to monetize, which patrons to trust, and which debt to swallow when a rumor goes wrong.
- The Velvet Coup: A faction within Fortune Row tries to topple a rival by weaponizing a rumor that will tilt law enforcement attention toward the rival’s criminal network. The players must decide whether to let the coup unfold or intervene without burning their own reputations.
- The Glass Theater Heist: A live show where the masks on stage are the city’s myths in disguise. In the chaos of acrobatics and illusions, a precious artifact moves between hands—maybe into the players', maybe into someone else’s.
- A Patron’s Request: A wealthy patron from Fortune Row asks the players to retrieve a rumor that was accidentally published in the city’s most trusted sources. The hunt for the truth will reveal a deeper conspiracy that links multiple districts.

Each hook includes scene-building guidelines, NPC snippets, and a suggested progression that respects the district’s tone. The GM toolkit is rich enough to adapt to one-shot sessions or longer campaigns, but fortifies the sense that Fortune Row is not just another neighborhood; it is a living rumor you can influence with a clever choice.

## Art Direction, Tone, and Accessibility
Fortune Row's art direction aligns with City of Mist in terms of mood, color palette, and the elegance of ruin. Expect neon reflections, blurred lines between truth and lie, and a visual language that rewards attention to detail. The book describes the district’s couture as a form of mythic armor; players don these garments (figuratively and literally in some playbooks) to reveal or hide aspects of their legends.

One of the book's strengths is its accessibility. The prose aims for evocative, not opaque, and the layout strikes a balance between GM guidance and player-facing mechanics. There are reward paths that feel earned rather than handed out, and the book doesn’t shy away from thorny ethical questions. If your group enjoys moral gray areas and the weight of reputation, Fortune Row supplies a steady stream of prompts for tough decisions.

On the other hand, the density can be a challenge for very new players. The district-specific vocabulary and the nuance of flashes, echoes, and debt triggers require a patient intro session. My suggestion is to run a short introductory arc with a couple of the new playbooks to help the group acclimate to the district’s rhythm before throwing in complex debt chains and Fortune Row’s more delicate rumor economy.

## GM Tips: Running Fortune Row at the Table
- Embrace ambiguity: Fortune Row thrives on rumors. Don’t be afraid to let players own half-truths and conflicting loyalties; the tension will pay off in dramatic reveals.
- Seed subtle consequences: Spending Luck should eventually trigger consequences that feel personal. A debt might surface in a conversation with a patron who has a long memory.
- Use the district as a character: Fortune Row should feel like a living thing with its own rules of flirtation and punishment. Treat locations as NPCs with agendas.
- Be mindful of tone: The best moments come from a balance of glamour and grit. Don’t swing too deeply into grimdark without relief in the form of wit, fashion, or a ridiculous rumor that pays off later.

## Mechanics Deep Dive: The Lucky Deck and Its Ramifications
The book introduces a few power-words and prompt-systems that act like a deck of fortune. The key idea is that luck isn't a universal resource; it’s a currency retrieved from the environment: a painting’s reflection, a bartender’s remembered line, or a street vendor’s whispered rumor. When properly used at the table, these prompts become windows into the city’s hidden architecture.

One particularly delightful mechanic is the “Debt Exchange.” Players can trade a debt to alter a scene’s stakes, but each exchange nudges the table toward a consequence that can echo into future sessions. It’s a neat way to tether episodic play to a longer arc while preserving the sense that Fortune Row’s nycturnal economy has a memory and a price tag.

There are also new Mist powers that feel like they belong standing next to existing titles rather than replacing them. The powers emphasize social manipulation, misdirection, and the city’s ability to turn a rumor into a weapon, a shield, or a bridge to a better fate—depending on your players' appetite for risk.

## Narrative Arc Possibilities: Long-form and Short-form Play
Fortune Row shines in long-form campaigns where threads from a single rumor can spiral into city-shaking events. The district’s web of patrons and rival factions creates built-in antagonists and potential allies, letting players choose where to invest their limited Luck and which debts to honor. For one-shots, the hooks are crafted to deliver a strong payoff within a single session while leaving a few tantalizing threads dangling for future sessions.

If your group enjoys weaving personal stakes into grander schemes, Fortune Row is a natural fit. The playbooks encourage you to develop patron relationships that evolve as players interact with the district’s power structure. It’s about who owes whom a favor and how that debt shifts as the city’s rumor mill churns.

## Pros and Cons: A Quick Take
Pros:
- Strong mood and district concept that fits the City of Mist ethos.
- Compelling new playbooks with mechanics centered on luck and debt.
- Flexible hooks that scale from one-shots to multi-session arcs.
- Clear GM guidance on tone, pacing, and ethical complexity.

Cons:
- A dense read that can overwhelm newcomers if you push straight into the debt mechanics without a warm-up.
- Some playbooks lean toward social intrigue at the expense of combat options, which may not fit every table’s taste.
- The rules-light vibe can veer into meta-narrative complexity if players over-index on rumor manipulation.

If you crave a setting that feels more noir-glitz than dungeon-crawl, Fortune Row delivers in spades. If you prefer a stricter, more tactical fight night, you’ll need to counterbalance with other supplements or house rules.

## Visuals, Layout, and Accessibility Review
Visually, Fortune Row stays coherent with the City of Mist brand: noir silhouettes, bold neon, and a fashion-forward sense of danger. The layout makes it easy to pull quick references during play, though the table might appreciate more in-book examples of rumor chains and debt triggers. The PDF is accessible with good contrast, but some of the table spreads may require a second pass to fully absorb the mechanics.

As for accessibility in terms of content, the book does a good job of presenting ethical dilemmas and consent-aware play prompts. The district’s themes can lead to morally gray situations, so the book includes clear notes to keep the table comfortable for players with different boundaries. Overall, Fortune Row respects its audience while offering a chance to push creative boundaries in a controlled, narratively satisfying way.

## Final Verdict and Recommendation
Fortune Row - Player's Handbook is a worthy addition to the City of Mist library for groups who enjoy the glamorous grit of myth and rumor colliding with street-level scheming. If your table thrives on social maneuvering, character-driven plots, and the thrill of turning chance into fate, Fortune Row gives you a treasure map with the occasional cursed coin. It does demand a bit of patience as you grow comfortable with its debt economy and rumor dynamics, but the payoff can be deeply satisfying and memorable.

If you are a GM who loves district-level campaigns that feel like a living, breathing ecosystem—where a single whispered rumor can reroute a city’s fate—Fortune Row is a strong, thematically coherent pick. The new playbooks expand the core toolkit in a way that invites long-term character development without derailing the game’s core identity. It’s not a universal fit for every table, but it is hard to dismiss for players who want a richer mythic layer stitched into the fabric of Fortune Row.

For new players, plan a gentle onboarding: start with one or two of the new playbooks, run a couple of short arcs, and let the debt and luck systems breathe. For veterans, Fortune Row provides a polished codebook for building intricate schemes and testing your players with moral questions wrapped in velvet and chrome.
