Maintenance is straightforward:
- Vacuum regularly to preserve nap and color integrity. Use a standard vacuum without a beater bar on delicate piles to prolong life.

## Care, Naps, and a Gentle Nudge Toward Better Choices
If you’re new to the rug world, you’ll notice a simple truth: rugs are not furniture. They’re stage sets for life happening around them. You’ll spill coffee, track in grass from the outdoors, and wander the room barefoot to test the foot feel. Your cleaning approach should be consistent and practical rather than perfectionist. Here are quick care tips tailored for this specific rug:
- Vacuum weekly with a standard suction setting; avoid a high-powered, aggressive brush setting that can pull fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to minimize uneven wear patterns, especially if your living room has one favorite seating configuration that never moves.
- Treat spills the moment they occur. Blot—do not rub—so you don’t push the stain deeper or drive color migration.
- Use a rug pad to reduce wear along the edges and to provide a buffer against cold hardwood floors. It also helps prevent sliding when you shininess-test your new remote-control habits in front of the TV.

If you’re more technically inclined, you can think of rug maintenance as a software update: regular patches, a periodic reboot (vacuums count as reboots), and user feedback for future releases. We’re all beta testers in the living room of life, after all.
