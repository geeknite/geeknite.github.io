## Care and maintenance
### Cleaning and stain resistance
Wool tends to handle everyday mishaps well when treated promptly. For spills, blot rather than scrub to avoid pushing the liquid deeper into the fibers. A mild detergent mixed with cold water works for spot cleaning. Avoid bleach and harsh alkalis, which can dull the color and damage the fiber’s natural oils. Regular vacuuming with a soft brush is recommended, and a professional cleaning every so often helps preserve the rug’s luster.

### Longevity and rotation
Rotating the rug every few months helps ensure even wear, particularly if you have high-traffic areas. Wool’s resilience plus a sturdy weave means you can enjoy many years of use if you treat it with care. Edge fraying is a common concern for woven rugs; check edges occasionally and consider a rug pad to minimize movement, which reduces wear along the border. A good pad can also help keep the rug in place on hard floors, preventing trips and accidental mid-room slides.

### Color stability and sunlight
If your rug sits in a sunny corner, colorfastness becomes a consideration. The coastline palette is forgiving, but prolonged sunlight can cause fading over long durations. Indoor UV-blocking curtains or lamps with low-UV settings can help preserve color integrity. If you notice slight fading over time, you can rotate the rug to keep color balance across rooms.

## Durability and longevity
### Weave and backing
The rug’s material and weave pattern contribute to its durability. Hand-loomed wool rugs tend to be more robust than machine-made equivalents because the knots are typically tighter and less prone to shedding. A solid backing supports the wool fibers, reducing edge wear and enabling better performance on busy floors. The rug’s longevity makes it a smart investment for households that want a long-term floor solution rather than something you replace every few years.

### Shedding and maintenance
New wool rugs can experience a mild shedding phase as the loose fibers settle. This is normal and usually short-lived. Regular vacuuming can mitigate this issue and help the rug reach its full potential for softness and appearance. If shedding persists beyond a few months, consider consulting the retailer for care instructions or possible wool-specific maintenance.
