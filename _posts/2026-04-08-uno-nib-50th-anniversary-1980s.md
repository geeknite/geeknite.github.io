## The Gameplay Experience
UNO is simple by design: match color or number, and throw in a Wild or Wild Draw Four to twist the current trajectory of play. The 1980s edition does not rewrite the fundamental rules; rather, it nudges them with a retro persona. You’ll still skip turns, draw cards, reverse the play, and try to shout UNO before your opponents count to two. The NIB 2020 edition adds some flavor by including a small nod to house rules that were popular in the era, but nothing too spicy to spark a formal rules war in your living room.

### Are there any rule changes or additions?
There are not radical overhauls to UNO rules here. Think of it as a faithful classic deck with a curated 1980s personality cameo. You’ll see a few cards with slightly updated artwork and a couple of design-led reminders on the back to cue players into the era aesthetic. The official rules remain the same: match color or number, play a Wild card to change color, or draw cards if you cannot play. If you’re a purist, you’ll appreciate the crisp return to a familiar rhythm with a nostalgic filter applied.
