## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping Your Table in Top Form
Maintenance is not glamorous, but it’s the quiet hero of extended tabletop life. A few simple rituals go a long way:
- Clean both surfaces regularly with a soft, dry microfiber cloth to remove dust and chalk residues that can accumulate in pockets and along rails.
- Check the swivel mechanism every few months. If you notice unusual wobble, tighten the mounting bolts with the included wrench, but do not over-tighten; this is a balance between stability and longevity.
- Replace or refresh the felt or pool surface when you notice persistent wear. A worn surface will affect ball roll consistency and your pride in sinking a simple shot.
- Keep the paddles and rods clean. If the rods begin to stick, a light lubrication with a silicone-based product (applied sparingly) can restore smooth motion. Do not oil or grease aggressively; the goal is a controlled, predictable feel.
- Use protective covers when not in use to prevent accidental spills and sunlight-induced fading. The table deserves a good night’s sleep too.
