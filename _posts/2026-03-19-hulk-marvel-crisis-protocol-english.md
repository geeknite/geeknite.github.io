## Hulk in Marvel Crisis Protocol: Overview

### Character Concept

The Hulk in MCP embodies the archetype of a front-line bruiser who can dictate the tempo of a game by occupying critical space on the board. He excels when he can threaten multiple angles of attack and force opponents to split their attention. The design encourages players to push into the enemy lines, grapple with the choice of committing to a powerful single strike or building up a sequence that edges toward a board-wide advantage. The Hulk is the kind of model that makes your opponent pause mid-move and reassess their entire plan, which is exactly the kind of theater MCP thrives on.

### Kit and Components

A Hulk blister typically includes the large hulking mini, a few objective and power tokens, a move template set, and a selection of upgrade tokens that represent gamma-powered upgrades. The miniature itself is a satisfying centerpiece: broad shoulders, a sculpt that reads well under any level of painting, and a dynamic pose that communicates movement even when the model is standing still. The components are designed to maximize table presence, from the footprint of the base to the dramatic silhouette the model cuts when you place it down.

## Playstyle: How to Use Hulk Effectively

### Core Play Pattern

Hulk thrives when he can threaten the center of the board and dictate the tempo of the engagement. A typical pattern starts with Hulk stepping into a threatened zone, using his early activations to apply pressure and draw attention away from more fragile teammates. A carefully timed attack, followed by a push or a maneuver that repositions him to cover multiple angles, can create a domino effect that compels your opponent to split their defenses. The key is not to overcommit in a single moment; rather, you want to chain actions that build safety nets for yourself while maintaining the aggression you need to win the initiative.

### Movement and Positioning

Positioning is everything for a Hulk-led list. His presence occupies space and changes how the opponent must respond. You want to create lanes where Hulk can charge through without getting trapped by enemy melee lines, while ensuring your ranged or support pieces can exploit the openings you create. The trick is to balance aggression with caution: you are not trying to solo your way through an entire enemy roster unless the moment is truly right. Many players miss the mark by chasing an isolated hero kill; the true win often comes from forcing the opponent to abandon a plan they were counting on.

### Combos and Sequence Timing

MCP rewards practice and planning. A Hulk player should be mindful of the power economy and how each activation bleeds into the next. A classic sequence might involve advancing Hulk into a frontline unit, delivering a heavy strike to induce an overextension in the opponent’s formation, and then leveraging a well-timed push or throw to shape the board for your second wave. It is rare that you score multiple knockout blows in a single turn; what you are aiming for is a chain of threats that keeps your opponent guessing about where the next wave will break through.

### Terrain and Board Design

Hulk loves terrain that can serve as a springboard or a trap. Elevated positions provide angles of attack, while obstacles offer cover to mitigate counterattacks after a bold charge. If you have a dedicated play space, think about how to arrange cover, debris, and lines of fire to funnel opponents into a corridor where Hulk can press their placement advantage. In a sense, Hulk is a maze runner on the board; your job is to design the maze in a way that makes his presence feel inevitable.

## List Building and Battle Plans

### Core List Concepts

To maximize Hulk's impact, most players build around a central theme: Hulk as a threat anchor supported by specialists who can manage objectives, counter-fire, and manipulate the enemy's movement. A typical approach pairs Hulk with a flexible bruiser or a ranged controller who can threaten backline targets, plus a support element such as healing or defensive buffs that keep Hulk on the board longer. A well-rounded list lets Hulk do the heavy lifting while other pieces handle the finer points of objective control and line disruption.

### Synergy and Pairings

Simplicity often beats complexity in list design. Hulk benefits from teammates who can create openings or absorb attention, freeing him to close distances and punish misplays. Pair him with a mobile skirmisher who can threaten the backline and force the opponent to split their focus, or with a ranged threat who can threaten the defensive lines Hulk wants to breach. The best lists deliver not just raw power but a coherent strategy: movement synergy, objective pressure, and the occasional game-changing moment that only Hulk can deliver when everything clicks.

### Counterplay and How to Exploit Weaknesses

No model is perfect, and Hulk has specific counters. If you face a patient, long-range team, the winner often hinges on your ability to penetrate the backline indirectly or force a tempo change through terrain. A good counter to Hulk is to keep him on the periphery where he cannot easily reach the most valuable targets, then punish any overextension with a precise, safe attack. Players who can read the flow of a game and adjust their approach—shifting emphasis from offense to defense or vice versa—will find Hulk a lot more forgiving and a lot more lethal in the late game.
