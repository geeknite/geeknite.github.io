### Real-World Sizing Tips: Nail It Before You Buy
- Measure twice, order once. This is the old carpenter’s wisdom updated for interior textiles. Map out width and length with clearance around furniture. A popular guideline is to leave 6–12 inches of rug visible beyond the furniture legs. If your room is a touch tight, aim for a footprint that underplays dramatic scale and relies on color to anchor the space instead.
- Consider traffic patterns. A sofa-forward rug should support the seating arc; for dining rooms, include chair clearance when chairs are pulled out. Bedrooms benefit from rugs that extend beyond the bed’s edge to soften the morning steps.
- Pile height and pet-friendly spaces. Higher piles trap fur and dirt; lower piles vacuum more easily and stay visually fresh even with furry roommates. If you’ve got pets, a mid-range pile height tends to be a practical sweet spot.
- Tailoring for non-standard rooms. Hallways and oddly shaped spaces can benefit from long, narrow runs or tailored corner pieces to avoid wasted floor space and awkward gaps around furniture. Your tailor (AKA the rug) will thank you.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping Seafoam Fresh Without Turning Cleaning Into a Full-Time Job
### Routine Care: Vacuuming, Rotation, and Sunlight
Regular vacuuming with a soft-brush setting is recommended to avoid fiber pulling and to keep the pile aligned. Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially in rooms with uneven sun exposure or heavy furniture blocks. Sunlight is a natural color shifter—gentle and gradual—and can cause mild fading over many years. If your room basks in a particular window, a simple rotation schedule can help maintain color consistency across the rug’s surface.

### Stain Management: Quick Blot, Gentle Clean, Gentle Dry
Spills happen, and wool’s forgiving nature helps recovery, but time matters. Blot spills promptly with a clean, dry cloth. For more stubborn spots, use a wool-safe cleaner (test in an inconspicuous area first). Avoid aggressive scrubbing, which can push stains deeper and distort pile height. For tough stains or lingering odors, consider a professional cleaning as part of a periodic maintenance plan. A professional cleaning every 12–24 months is common in busy households; if you’ve got kids or pets, you might opt for the more frequent end of that range.

### Deep Cleaning and Refresh Cycles
Even with routine care, wool rugs benefit from a periodic refresh. A gentle steam-cleaning option can remove deep-set dirt while preserving fiber integrity. If you’re feeling particularly bold, you can refresh the surface with a light, color-safe rug shampoo—just ensure complete thorough drying to prevent wool elasticity loss or mold risk.
