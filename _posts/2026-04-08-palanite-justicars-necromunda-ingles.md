## Introduction
Necromunda’s Underhive isn’t just a board with a sprinkling of paint on it; it’s a mood. A mood fueled by rust, plasma burn marks, and the distant screaming of a Goliath who forgot to pay his gang dues. In this wonderfully grim sandbox, the Palanite Justicars arrive as the enforcement wing of the Magistratum—the faction that embodies law, order, and the occasional bureaucratic nightmare that makes your local DMV look like a spa day. If you’ve ever wished your Necromunda games came with a badge that actually counts your fines, the Palanite Justicars are your squad. They are the police procedural with a bolt pistol, the security detail that knows every corner of the map because they coded it themselves in a previous life.

In this review, we’ll explore what makes the Palanite Justicars tick: the lore, the models, the paint schemes, and, most importantly, how they actually perform on tabletop. We’ll mix strategy with a dash of sarcasm, because if the Underhive isn’t laughing at your roll, you’re not playing it right.

> Quick note for the new players: Palanites aren’t a “kill everything” faction. They’re a “control the board, pin your opponent, and deliver a very calculated dose of law-and-order” faction. Think of them as the slow-burn marshals of Necromunda—the kind you respect after you’ve taken notes from their playbook, preferably while crying into a codex page.

## Who are the Palanite Justicars?
The Palanite Justicars are the Magistratum’s front line in the ongoing hive cleanup operation. In-universe lore, they’re the disciplined, if sometimes bureaucratic, enforcers who patrol the Underhive with a mix of steel nerves and swagger that says, “Yes, we absolutely believe in due process—just not for you.” The aesthetic is unmistakable: clean lines, heavy armor, and a vibe that communicates they’d rather file a report than throw a punch—unless the punch is necessary, decisive, and delivered with a perfectly tidied boot on the enemy’s torso.

From a gameplay standpoint, Palanites bring a blend of resilience and controlled aggression. They aren’t built to out-muscle a Juggernaut gang in a bare-knuckle brawl, but they excel at punishing mistakes. Their mobility lets them advance into critical lanes, support their allies with disciplined fire, and create zones where enemies think twice before stepping into a corridor you’ve converted into a choke point. They shine in missions that reward map control and objective denial, which is the Necromunda way of saying: “Make the enemy stumble into your plan and then pour on the fire.”

## The Kit and How to Build Them
Games Workshop released Palanite Justicars as a multi-model kit that fits a very specific vibe: disciplined enforcers with a stubborn sense of duty. The typical squad composition includes a Justicar leader and a mix of Palanite Gangers in roles like gunners, specialists, and interceptors. If you’re into kitbashing, there’s also ample opportunity to customize shields, shoulder plates, and equipment to give your unit a unique personality that screams “I’m here to file this report and also to look cool while doing it.”

### Building tips
- Start with a clear leadership model. The Justicar should look like someone who has conducted the hive’s most tedious audits and somehow still has time to shoot a bolt pistol with precision.
- Use a couple of heavier weapons for counterpunch duty. Palanites carry tools that can lay down covering fire and pinning shots, so lines of sight matter as much as a good courtroom argument.
- Mix in specialists who can debuff, buff, or repair as the mission dictates. The more you can make the squad adaptable to different scenarios, the easier a win becomes.
- Don’t forget the conversion potential. The Palanite look works well with a few “bureaucracy in action” touches—snazzy pouches, a satchel full of paperwork that doubles as a shield, or a baton that looks like it was designed by committee.

### Painting and tabletop aesthetics
Palanite color schemes tend toward cool blues, steel grays, and white insignias with a dash of shocking red for rank. The contrast makes them pop on the board and reinforces their “lawful suspect” vibe. Here are a few tips to get that crisp look:
- Undercoat with a neutral gray, then build up to a blue-tinished scheme. A soft white for the insignia helps them stand out from the hive’s grime.
- Use dry brushing to accent edges on armor plates. A metallic silver followed by a glaze of blue wash gives a weathered, utilitarian look that reads well from across the table.
- Apply subtle warband or gang insignia as part of the hobby project. You want to tell a small narrative about each model—like which precinct they defend or which infamous inmate they’ve had to “negotiate” with.

## Gameplay and Tactics: How Do They Play?
The Palanites aren’t the flashy “run-and-gun” choice; they’re the calculated, patient option. They excel at controlling space, trading shots with a stable pace, and punishing mistakes while maintaining enough resilience to weather a rough round.

### Core strengths
- Fire discipline: The Palanites have reliable ranged output that can pressure enemy moves and force bad decisions. They’re particularly effective at picking off skirmishers who try to cross open lanes.
- Objective denial: If you’re playing missions where you’re defending or cutting off routes, Palanites can stand in the way and hold key lines while your more aggressive gangs do the heavy lifting elsewhere.
- Survivability: With solid armor and steady wounds, they aren’t easily removed in a single volley. They can take hits while still delivering steady returns on the damage front.

### Weaknesses to keep in mind
- Mobility can be slower than you expect. You’re looking for calculated pushes, not dynamic sprints across the table.
- They aren’t the best at chasing down a fleeing enemy. If your opponent plans to kite you, you’ll need to rely on your board control and luck more than you’d hope.
- They can rely heavily on good positioning. If you fail to secure key lanes early, you’ll be fighting uphill from a defensive stance.

### Tactics and synergy with the board
- Lane control and zone denial: Use Palanites to lock down narrow corridors and doors. This denies enemy movement and funnels them into your firing lines, where your other squads can contribute with supplementary support.
- Support fire discipline: Move as a unit to maintain overlapping targets. The more angles you can maintain on a target, the more likely you are to wear them down or force a retreat.
- Reserve the heavy hitters: Don’t feel compelled to unload every grenade in one turn. Preserve some punch for the late rounds, when the enemy is least prepared for a hard counter.
- Board awareness: The Underhive rewards players who understand line of sight, elevation, and cover. Palanites benefit from every little advantage—wooden crates, scaffolding, or even a collapsed wall—so learn the terrain as if you’re the city planner who also happens to be a warlord.

### A sample matchup logic
If you’re facing a fast, melee-heavy gang, the Palanites can soften the approach with precise shots and careful spacing, then pivot to a defensive hold while a second squad hits the enemy’s flank. If you’re up against a ranged-heavy opponent, you’ll want to maximize your own range advantage, forcing them into a position where their shots become less effective and your patrol can advance with impunity. The key is to treat each turn as a micro-decision about space, tempo, and who’s paying the next set of fines.

## Lore Deep Dive: The Magistratum, the Justicars, and the Hive
Palanite Justicars aren’t just a collection of miniatures with good paint jobs; they’re a narrative engine. They represent the hive’s attempt to balance chaos with order in a place where chaos tends to win if you blink. In the fiction, you’ll see them auditing gangs, escorting prisoners through plausibly terrifying corridors, and occasionally clashing with corrupt officials who think the law should be more like “flexible guidelines” than enforceable rules. This tension between order and entropy is what makes Necromunda’s Underhive so compelling, and the Palanites are the perfect instrument of that tension.

If you’re a lore nerd, you’ll enjoy imagining the palanite leader presenting a stack of requisition papers to a Goliath who tries to bluff his way through a checkpoint. You’ll picture venting steam and the inevitable moment when the Justicar’s baton comes up as if to say, in very polite terms: “Sir, please step away from the eddies of chaos.” It’s a playful but meaningful theme: even in the hive’s brutal world, law and order are not merely decorative—they are a system that actually tries to work. And that’s a nice nod to a certain grounded reality in the midst of all the plasma and piracy.

## Painting and Display Tips for Maximum Impact
- Contrasting tones: Use a crisp white or pale blue to highlight insignia and armor details. It makes the model’s lines pop in photos and at the table.
- Bases with story: A base can tell a small tale. A little rubble, some decorative crackle texture, and a few stray bolts can say, “This precinct has seen a lot of action.”
- Light weathering: Subtle weathering on armor edges or weapons can suggest overtime shifts in the hive’s harsh environment. Don’t overdo it—this isn’t a diorama; it’s a tabletop squad that has to survive a few minutes of laser fire.
- Gloss vs matte: A matte finish on armor with a gloss on the visor can make the figure look more futuristic and crisp. It’s a small trick that reads well on camera and in real life.

## Battle Strategy: Against Other Factions
Against gangs that rely on speed and melee, Palanites stall the approach, hold the line, and then pivot to a wider engagement that punishes every misstep your opponent makes. In a game with heavy ranged power, Palanites keep a measured pace, using cover to survive long enough to get a higher pulse in the later rounds. They’re the type of unit that appreciates patience, discipline, and a little bit of luck—three things every Necromunda player loves to claim they have in abundance when the dice decide to temper their ego.

Comparing Palanites to other factions is a reminder that Necromunda is less about who can roll the best dice and more about who can enforce a plan through terrain control, discipline, and timing. If you come to the table with a plan and a clear sense of where your lanes are, the Palanites will reward you with steady progress and the occasional dramatic moment when a well-timed volley takes a key target out of the game.

## How to Use Them in Your List: Loadouts and Combos
- Leader: The Justicar should anchor your squad, delivering leadership and a reliable presence. A well-equipped leader can carry the squad through rough rounds and provide stable offensive pressure.
- Heavy weapon options: Include at least one heavy or heavier option to break through tough armor or contested zones. The goal is to make the enemy consider retreat instead of staying put.
- Support pieces: A couple of Palanites with mobility or special shooting options can keep the squad vital for longer. You want them to be flexible enough to meet the mission’s demands rather than a purely one-note unit.
- Synergy with other factions: Palanites play well with mid-field support from other factions. If you’re building a mixed list, you’ll want to consider how your other gangs can complement the Palanites’ control game, whether through shielding fire or drawing enemy attention away from your main strike force.

## Final Thoughts and Recommendation
Palanite Justicars are a strong, thematic choice for Necromunda players who value discipline, space control, and a little bit of old-school police work flavor on the table. They aren’t the loudest or flashiest faction on the board, but they are reliable, which in the Hive is basically a superpower. If you enjoy planning a chess-like game where each move is deliberate and the payoff is measured rather than explosive, then the Palanites will fit your playstyle perfectly. They reward you for patience and precision, and when you pull off a long-range pin into a winning melee swing, you’ll feel like a tactician who finally managed to file a win under “Lawful Good” in the most demon-haunted of hives.

If you’re new to Necromunda, start with a small Palanite detachment and a friend’s gang to practice the rhythm. If you’re a seasoned grimdark veteran, you’ll find the same vibes here with an added layer of official enforcement that makes you feel like a tiny municipal hero in a world where buses never arrive and the gangers keep moving the goalposts for you. Either way, the Palanite Justicars bring a distinct, satisfying flavor to the game’s dynamic: a disciplined approach to a wonderfully chaotic underworld.
