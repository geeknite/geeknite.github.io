## The Two Stars: Gilbert and Mortimer

Gilbert and Mortimer are the kind of pair you do not know you needed until the moment you realize that your party has been suffering from tragic coordination all along. Gilbert is the stubborn optimist, the type who brings a medical kit and insists the plague can be negotiated with a polite bow and a better spreadsheet. Mortimer is the schemer, the guy who brings a spark of ridiculous luck to the table and then proceeds to roll a critical on a plastic zombie that is basically screaming for mercy. Together they form a synergy that shines when your party is facing a dense wave of walkers.

The miniatures are characterful without being cartoonish. Gilbert stands tall with a staff that doubles as a makeshift spear; his pose conveys leadership without tipping into alpha-mroar territory. Mortimer curves into the edge of chaos with a sly grin that might translate to an ability that lets you reroll a failed action once per round. The use of color on both sculpts feels deliberate and balanced; nothing pops too loud enough to clash with the rest of the Black Plague palette, but both minis are easy to identify at a glance, which matters when you are trying to decide who gets the heroic last stand.

Their character boards carry a few new trait tiles that feel both thematic and practical. You do not get overwhelmed with new complexity; you get a handful of options that actually affect how you play. The ability design leans into the plague theme while offering meaningful choices that reward good planning and a little bit of risk-taking. This is not a gimmick pack; it is a strategic nudge that feels like a natural extension rather than a forced add-on.
