## The System: How It Feels to Play

At the heart of Batman Who Laughs SW New is a familiar Monolith engine with a twist: action dice pools, board-driven challenges, and a villain who can flip the entire battlefield in a single card draw. The Shadow War mode introduces a dynamic where players aren’t just racing to complete objectives; they’re contending with a narrative clock that compels cooperation, cunning, and occasional sacrifices for the greater good (or at least the greater narrative suspense).

### Core mechanics in plain language
- Action dice pools: You roll a set of dice to perform moves, attacks, and special actions. Your character’s stats and the card-driven modifiers shape the pool’s distribution, making planning feel meaningful rather than simply “hope the dice don’t hate you.”
- Villain disruptors: Batman Who Laughs isn’t a passive boss. He drips with ability cards and “twist” effects that can alter the board state mid-round—forcing you to adapt on the fly rather than rigidly execute a plan.
- Objective-driven play: Each mission has a central objective, but the path to victory is littered with side tasks, contingencies, and choices that matter long after the final combat die is rolled.
- Cooperative tension: In co-op, you’ll need to coordinate your powers, crowd-control options, and timing of critical strikes. One mis-turn can cascade into a tense scramble where you’re both outsmarting and out-pacing your own team’s plans.
- Solo mode: The box shines in solo play too, with a competent AI that scales the challenge. The feeling is very much like fighting a cunning villain who has seen every one of your tricks and still finds a way to surprise you.

All of this comes with a clockwork clarity that avoids the dreaded “one more page” fatigue. The rules teach themselves in a handful of sessions, leaving you with a compact, elegant toolkit that rewards careful planning and resilient improvisation.
