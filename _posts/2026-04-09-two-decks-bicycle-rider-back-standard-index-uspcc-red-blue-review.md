No product is perfect, and two evenly matched decks are no exception. The Rider Back prints can vary slightly from batch to batch, and color consistency between red and blue can differ by a few shade steps depending on the production run. If you’re a perfectionist, you’ll want to check that both decks feel similar in their snap and spring, particularly if you plan to perform a routine that requires a precise switch between colors. Another small caveat: while the tuck boxes are sturdy, they are still vulnerable to creasing in a bag if you squash them under heavy items. If you travel with your decks, consider a protective sleeve or a small card protector to keep them looking fresh. 

In terms of maintenance, you’ll want to avoid exposing the decks to moisture. ## Care and Longevity: Keeping the Decks Fresh

A little care goes a long way. After a few sessions, you might notice fingerprints or minor smudges on the faces. A quick wipe with a dry microfiber cloth is usually enough to restore that pristine look. If you’re dealing with stubborn smudges, a very light wipe with a barely damp cloth can help—just don’t soak the cards. Storage matters as well. For cardists who travel, a protective pouch is worth its weight in flourishes, because it helps prevent edge creases and corner wear that can otherwise creep up after a few weeks of intense practice. 

The two-deck pairing also means you can rotate use so that one deck is your “practice deck” and the other is your “performing deck.” This separation can help you preserve the crisp edge feel of the performing deck while you work through your drill routines on the practice deck.
