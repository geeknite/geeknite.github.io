## Size, fit, and room planning
### Sizing options and footprint psychology
The Kendall Oatmeal rug is available in a handful of standard sizes, typically including 5x8, 6x9, and 8x10. The choice of size influences not just the physical footprint but the perceived scale of the room. In smaller living spaces, aim for a rug that sits under the front legs of your sofa and chairs to anchor the seating area; in larger rooms, you can go a bit bigger to create a defined lounge zone within an open layout.

Layout tip: place a subtle coffee table across the rug’s center to unify the seating arrangement. If you’re unsure, a good rule of thumb is to extend the rug under at least the front legs of your main seating. That little trick makes your space feel more intentional and less like you grabbed a rug at the last minute from an online flash sale.

### Pile height and daily life compatibility
A mid-pile rug offers a balance between plushness and ease of maintenance. It’s comfortable for bare feet and doesn’t fight your vacuum cleaner. If you have kids or pets, a mid-pile option is often more forgiving than a low-pile rug that shows footprints and a high-pile rug that swallows small objects. The Kendall rug sits in that sweet spot where you can enjoy a soft underfoot experience without needing a rug pad the size of a dining table.

If you want to add extra bounce or noise damping, consider pairing it with a non-slip rug pad. The pad helps prevent curling edges and reduces slipping risk in high-traffic areas, especially for households with little ones who think the rug is a performance stage.

## Color, care, and upkeep
### Cleaning basics and stain resistance
Stain resistance is a big win for any rug in a busy home. Quick blotting is recommended for spills, followed by a gentle cleaning with a mild detergent solution if necessary. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fiber balance or dim the geometric lines. For everyday dirt, a standard vacuum with a brushless attachment on a low setting should keep the surface clean without turning the pattern into a blur.

We recommend treating spills sooner rather than later. Coffee and tea are particularly prone to staining on light backgrounds, so a quick blot and a mild cleaning solution will help keep the pattern crisp. If you’re dealing with something more stubborn, test any cleaning product on a hidden edge first to ensure no color bleed occurs.

### Maintenance rhythm and seasonal care
Establish a simple maintenance rhythm: vacuum weekly, rotate the rug every 3 to 6 months to ensure even wear, and pull back to check the edges for curling. A rotating schedule helps the pattern wear evenly and prevents certain sections from looking tired before others. If you have a large living area with multiple seating zones, rotating a couple of times a year can keep the overall look fresh without needing a full rerun of the decor.

## Practical buying tips
### How to measure and plan for your space
Measure the room carefully before buying. Use painter’s tape on the floor to visualize where the rug will sit and to ensure the size aligns with your furniture layout. Keep in mind door clearance and traffic lanes. You don’t want to be the person who has to pull a chair out of a rug seam every time someone slides across the floor. A little planning goes a long way toward avoiding post-purchase rearrangements.

### Returns, exchanges, and customer service
Check the retailer’s return policy before you buy. A rug can look perfect online and then read the room differently once it’s in place. A flexible return policy helps you test the look and feel in your actual space without the guilt of a poor decoration decision. If you do encounter issues, reach out to customer service promptly; most retailers will offer exchanges or returns if there are manufacturing defects or damage in transit.
