### How to Play: A Quick-Start Guide

Is there a single sentence that can capture Exploding Kittens Party Pack? Yes: On your turn, draw a card, and if you’re lucky you’ll avoid a kitten explosion. If not, you’ll either defuse or you’re out. That said, the real joy is in the way you combine cards to delay, dodge, or strictly ruin someone else’s day.

- Setup: Shuffle the deck and deal a starter hand to each player. In the Party Pack, the number of starting cards is designed to support plays with 2-10 players. The rule sheet suggests a baseline, but in Geeknite fashion we always find fun ways to tweak.
- Turn structure: On your turn you may play zero or more action cards from your hand to manipulate the deck or threaten your neighbors. The core action is that you draw one card at the end of your turn. If you end up drawing an Exploding Kitten, you must Defuse it. If you can Defuse, you place the Exploding Kitten back into the deck (often facedown) and continue; if you can’t Defuse, you’re out.
- Action cards: Attack forces the next player to take two turns; Skip ends your turn and passes the turn; See the Future lets you peek at the top three cards of the deck; Shuffle shuffles the deck; Nope cancels the previous action. The variety of actions is what makes every round feel unpredictable. The new Party Pack cards add fresh sneaky moves that you’ll want to test on your least-lucky friend.
- End of game: The last player standing wins. In the most ridiculous nights, you may declare victory with a flourish that involves a triumphant "defuse" dance.

### Strategy and Player Experience: No Master Class, Just Fun

This is not about deep planning. It’s about micro-decisions that change the probability curve in a single moment. You’re balancing the safe route (holding Defuses, retaining See the Future cards for late-game planning) with the risk-taking that makes you the captain of chaos.

- 2 players: The pace is fast and the read of the other player’s hand becomes a game of reading their tells. There’s still bluffing and the occasional moment where you pass a Defuse back into the deck in a shocking reversal.
- 3-4 players: The table dynamic becomes more collaborative and more combustible. You feel a stronger incentive to use Nope strategically because the moment is right for a dramatic cancellation.
- 5-10 players: The energy is at max. The new cards in the Party Pack ensure the pace remains brisk and unpredictable. You get more near-misses, more dramatic "I thought you had it" moments, and more opportunities for the group to erupt in laughter.

### Variant Rules and House Rules

There are several optional rules you can use to tailor the experience:

- Round limit: Play a fixed number of rounds (say 5-7) and declare the winner by most points or most successful Defuses.
- Meme mode: Allow stacking of multiple Nope cards or implement your own silly restrictions. The more creative your group is, the more fun you’ll have.
- Card removal for younger players: You can remove some of the more mature jokes or complicated consequences for younger players, though the base game is designed to be family-friendly.
- Team play: If your group is large, you can create temporary teams and alternate turns within teams to keep things lively while you chat. This variant can be surprisingly fun.
