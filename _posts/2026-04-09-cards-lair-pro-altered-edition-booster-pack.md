### Card Distribution and Odds
The typical distribution leans toward a classic booster pack layout: a rare or higher at the top of the pack, a handful of uncommons, and the majority of the pack comprised of commons. The Altered Edition twist adds a wrinkle in the form of altered art or altered mechanics that only show up in a subset of cards. For players who chase buzzwords like synergy, tempo, and control lines, this can be a delightful surprise or a frustrating detour from the deck you had in mind.

From a value perspective, the Altered Edition component is meant to push collectors toward certain subsets, potentially increasing the long game value if those altered cards become sought-after or serve as strong proxies in casual play. Of course, value in booster packs is a fickle beast; it can roar loudly on release day and then quietly retire to the graveyard of “remember when this was hype.” The thrill is part of the sport here, and the Altered Edition twist adds a little extra adrenaline to the daily ritual of opening trashy plastic and hoping for the one perfect pull.

### The Altered Edition Mechanics in Practice
In practice, Altered Edition cards can tweak a few parameters: altered power/toughness, altered costs, or altered activate abilities. The Altered Edition cards are meant to spark conversation at the table, to encourage new memes around deck-building, and to remind players that there is more than one way to win a game without resorting to a single tried-and-true deck style.
