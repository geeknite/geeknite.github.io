## The Social Dynamics: Hosts, Guests, and Moderators
Hosting a CAH session with the 600-card foil expansion requires a few social adjustments. Here are some pro tips to maximize laughs and minimize awkward silences:

- Setup is half the battle: a neat table, good lighting, and a comfortable chair for the card czar (the person responsible for explaining rules and handing out black cards) can transform an ordinary game into a well-rehearsed improv session.
- Establish ground rules: define what’s off-limits early on, and decide how strict you want to be with “punch up” vs. “punch down” humor. The foil finish doesn’t change sensitivity, it just makes the jokes shine a little brighter.
- Rotate the dealer: keep the moderator role rotating among players to ensure everyone has a fair shot at curating the table’s humor. It also prevents the night from orbiting around a single comedic voice.
