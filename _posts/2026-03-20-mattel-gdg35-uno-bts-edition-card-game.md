## How to Play the GDG35 Twist: Rules, Rhythm, and a Little Bit of BTS Flair
### The Core Twist: BTS Mode and New Action Cards
UNO basics are the same at the surface: match color or number, play a skip/reverse/draw-two, try not to draw a whole hand of misery. The BTS Edition spices things up with eight BTS-themed action cards that replace or augment some of the standard actions. Some hypothetical examples you might encounter (these are designed to capture the vibe and could vary by batch):
- Mic Drop: Skip the next player—because the mic drop moment is essential in any pop-concert encore. 
- Dynamite Draw: The player who draws this card can force a small group to draw a total of four cards over the next two turns, like a quirky ring-light moment in a live performance.
- DNA Shuffle: Reverse the play direction and change the color, as if the beat suddenly dropped into a new key.
- Idol Sprint: The next player must play color-mame-free to reset the tempo of the round (effectively a wild draw). 

The exact wording varies by print run, but the general idea is simple: you replace a couple of UNO’s classic actions with BTS-inspired “moment” cards that ship you into pop-culture territory while preserving competitive tempo. The intent is clear: more planned chaos, less predictability, more shouting of the iconic lines “UNO!” when someone misreads a color.

### The Arcade-Lite Experience: How to Run a GDG35 Round
- Setup: Separate the eight BTS action cards, shuffle, and deal. The BTS-themed cards go into a separate stack for the duration of that round. 
- Turn Structure: A normal UNO turn is: match, draw if you can’t, play if you can, pass. In GDG35, when a BTS card is drawn, the effect triggers immediately or at the player’s discretion depending on the house rules. The “house rules” formula is optional but recommended for families who like to customize chaos levels.
- Victory Condition: Be the first to empty your hand. But with the GDG35 edition, you might be challenged with a “playlist ending” moment: if you shout “Uno!” with perfect timing, you earn a bonus point from the dealer. It’s cute, silly, and a little odd to count, but it’s part of the charm.

### The Interaction Layer: How It Feels in Real-Life Play
