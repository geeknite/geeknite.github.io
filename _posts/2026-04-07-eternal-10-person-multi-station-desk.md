### Cable management and power distribution
Cable management is the unsung hero of any multi-seat desk. Eternal uses a two-tier approach: a central raceway for data cables and a separate power rail that houses surge protection and power strips. Each seat can access a shallow cable channel that keeps the cords out of the main surface area, which means you can wipe up spills and coffee with confidence. We tested the setup with a dozen PCs, several monitors, and a few USB hubs, and we can report that the surface stayed relatively clean with minimal wandering computer cords crossing the path of the starship crew.

The power rail features a centralized power distribution unit (PDU) with surge protection and overload protection. If you’re running a very dense setup with high-wattage GPUs, make sure you calculate your total draw and assign circuits accordingly. The last thing you want is a party that ends with the lights flickering and a referee declaring a safety timeout because of a suspiciously enthusiastic surge. In this department, the Eternal is comfortably competent, though it’s not exactly a substitute for a dedicated electrical plan in a high-demand lab environment.

In a real test, we used the Eternal in a room that saw multiple simultaneous online gaming sessions and a few late-night editing sessions.
