## Materials and Craftsmanship
### Hand Tufted Quality
The rug is described as hand tufted, which means skilled artisans used tufting needles to insert fibers through a backing material, creating a dense, plush surface. The hand tufting method typically yields a robust weave, with good resistance to shedding and a strong yarn alignment. It is not the same as hand-knotted or machine-tufted, but it sits in a sweet spot between durability and warmth. The result is a rug that can handle daily traffic in a living room or family space while maintaining a refined edge that suits midcentury and contemporary décor alike.

## Maintenance and Cleaning
### Routine Care
A practical rug requires a practical care routine. For the Estella Submarine rug, regular vacuuming is your best friend. Use a vacuum with a gentle beater bar setting or switch to a suction-only mode to avoid pulling at the tufts. Vacuum in multiple directions to lift debris that may settle into the pile rather than brushing it to the surface. A weekly maintenance sweep keeps dirt from embedding itself into the fibers and helps preserve color integrity over time.

### Spot Cleaning and Stains
Spills happen, especially in living spaces where coffee inevitably collides with a good story. For most liquid spills, blot gently with a clean cloth or paper towel, working from the outside inward to prevent spreading. If you need to treat a stain, use a mild detergent mixed with water and test a small, inconspicuous area first. Avoid bleach or harsh solvents that can fade the blue and yellow accents or degrade the pile. For stubborn stains, consider a professional cleaning service that specializes in area rugs. Remember that patience is a virtue here; aggressive scrubbers will only morph a spa day into a rug spa day for your fibers.

## Durability and Longevity
### How This Rug Holds Up Over Time
The durability of a hand tufted rug comes down to fiber quality, backing, and construction. The Estella Submarine rug, with its dense pile and sturdy backing, is designed to resist flattening under regular foot traffic, which is a nice way of saying you should not expect a perfect plane after a year of daily use. The color saturation is another factor. If the dyes are well-impregnated, you should see minimal fading in response to normal sun exposure over several seasons. In crowded family rooms with pets, you can expect the rug to show some wear in high-traffic zones, but the overall structure should remain sound, preserving the color and texture profile that makes it feel premium rather than worn out.

### Rotation and Placement Tips
To maximize longevity, consider rotating the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if sunlight exposure is uneven or if a particular seating area commands most of the attention. If you place heavy furniture on the rug, use furniture coasters to distribute weight and avoid crushing the nap. The goal is to keep the rug looking new and lively, not to transform it into a flattened, horizon-scarred landscape.
