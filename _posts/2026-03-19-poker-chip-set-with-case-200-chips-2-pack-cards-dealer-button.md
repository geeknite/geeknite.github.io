### Setup tips and tricks
- Lay out the box, open the case, and inspect the chips for any edge chipping. If you find more than a couple, contact the vendor for a replacement – you don’t want a chipped chip stealing the show.
- Sort chips by color before the first deal. Yes, it’s a ritual, but it looks cool and reduces confusion when you start stacking in 3-d, 4-d, or 5-d denomination increments.
- Check that both decks are shuffled and presentable before you begin. There’s nothing more dramatic than someone flipping a card that should have been thoroughly riffled but wasn’t.
