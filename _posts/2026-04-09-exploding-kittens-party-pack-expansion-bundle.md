## How the Party Pack expands the base game
### Core mechanics: what stays the same, what changes

The base game of Exploding Kittens is a fast and furious card drawing exercise where your goal is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. Defuse cards save you in a pinch, while other sabotage cards keep you guessing about what will happen next. The Party Pack keeps the central engine intact: draw cards, avoid explosions, use defuse cards when you can, and avoid being the last kitten standing.

What the Party Pack brings to this familiar dance is a curated assortment of expansion content that increases the number of players, adds new card types, and sometimes introduces new mechanical loops that alter how you approach the endgame. The Expansion Bundle part of the kit typically includes extra cards that interact with the base deck in new and surprising ways. Some examples include cards that alter the draw order, force a re-draw, or create temporary alliances that end in glorious betrayal when an explosion finally lands. The exact balance of these new cards is always a matter of taste; some groups adore the chaotic energy, others prefer a more strategic rhythm. In this sense, the Party Pack is a modular experience: you can lean into the chaos or dial it back depending on how much you want to up the ante.

## Tips and tricks for hosting a Party Pack session
### House rules and pacing adjustments

To maximize your experience with the Party Pack, consider a few simple rules to keep the game moving and the energy high:

- Warm-up round: conduct a quick 2 card draw to illustrate the defuse mechanic and the concept of an exploding kitten, then go around with a normal first round. This helps new players get the hang of the pace without downtime.
- Endgame cue: when there are three or fewer cards left in the draw pile, switch to a countdown style where players know the round will end soon. This increases tension without dragging the game on forever.
- Chaos quota: if your group loves chaos, allow a few expansions to run together but cap the number of new card types to keep the game approachable.
- Gentle reminders: a quick, friendly reminder about safe table behavior helps avoid elbows and aggressive laughter turning into accidental table flips. The chaotic energy should feel fun, not hostile.

### Strategic tips for the main players

While the luck factor is real, there are a few small strategies you can lean into:

- Focus on Defuses early: getting a couple of Defuse cards early can be the difference between a quick round and a multi explosion disaster.
- Read the room: use the expansion cards to create opportunities for social manipulation. The best players are the ones who manage to coax a betrayal when the card that triggers an explosion lands in someone unexpectedly.
- Manage your energy: know when to push for a round or when to slow down and observe. In a party ring setting, the player with the best timing often wins social currency even if they do not win the game itself.
