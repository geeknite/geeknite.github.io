## Assembly and Setup Time
### Step-by-Step Overview
As with many foldable tables, assembly is mostly about aligning components and tightening a few screws. The process is straightforward:
- Unfold the table halves and lock the legs into position
- Attach the crossbars and ensure the frame is square
- Install the net using the posts and tighten until taut, but not so tight that you hear wood creak in protest
- Test for wobble and adjust feet as needed

On a good day, an experienced hobbyist could assemble in under 15 minutes. For first-timers, give yourself a relaxed 30 minutes to read the manual, verify hardware, and curse a few times in satisfaction when you finally get to the first serve.

### Routine Maintenance
A quick wipe-down after use is recommended to keep the surface pristine. If you are lucky enough to play outdoors, you’ll want to bring a cover or store the table indoors after your session to prevent moisture impact on the frame and the surface.

## Practical Considerations: Maintenance, Longevity, and Common Pitfalls
### Maintenance Tips
- Clean the surface after use to prevent sticky residue and chalky marks from impacting ball bounce.
- Inspect the clamps and legs for looseness after heavy sessions and re-tighten if necessary.
- Use a cover if you plan to leave the table outdoors for extended periods to protect against moisture and sun exposure.

### Longevity Expectations
A well-taken-care-of unit can handle months to years of casual play. The folding mechanism is designed for repeated use, but like any mechanical component, it appreciates gentle handling and periodic checks. Don’t slam the halves together or force it to fold in ways it’s not designed to; treat it as you would a reliable but slightly mischievous robot: well-loved and gently coaxed into action.

### Potential Pitfalls to Watch For
- Outdoor use in heavy rain or wind can affect playability and frame integrity over time.
- A very uneven playing surface may cause wobble in the frame; ensure your base is level before play. A small mat or rug under the table can help mitigate this.
- The accessory paddles and balls included are serviceable but may not satisfy a serious tournament player. If you’re chasing professional-grade gear, consider upgrading to your preferred paddles and balls.
