## Sizing options: custom size or a classic 8x10—and why the choice matters
One of the biggest questions consumers ask about any rug that brags about “custom size” is: will it actually fit, and what’s the trade-off?

### Custom sizing: when your room has quirks and your tools are a tape measure and a dream
Custom sizing is a magic phrase that often translates to “we can get you something tailored to your space.” It’s ideal if you’re dealing with unusual room dimensions, a wide doorway, or a configuration where a standard 8x10 would either leave dead space or require a chain of rugs to cover the floor like a patchwork quilt. Here are some practical considerations:

- Fit and proportion: custom sizing helps you maintain the correct rug-to-furniture ratio, ensuring your sofa legs sit comfortably on the rug or just off it as you prefer.
- Logistics and lead time: custom orders typically involve a lead time for production. You’ll be waiting, during which you can perfect your “cozy minimalist” mood board in your head.
- Price impact: custom sizes are often more expensive than off-the-shelf options. If you’re budget-conscious, you may decide to compromise on size but keep the same vibe.
- Edge behavior: larger or tailored shapes may require careful measuring to ensure the rug doesn’t look like a random rectangle floating in a sea of wood; it should feel integrated, not accidental.

### The classic 8x10: the reference size that makes everyone nod in interior design seminars
The 8x10 footprint is the industry’s favorite default because it’s a “doable” size for most living rooms. It offers predictable coverage and a familiar anchoring effect that helps define a seating area. Pros:

- Predictable fit in standard living rooms and open-plan spaces.
- Easier to source in multiple retailers, often with faster shipping.
- Usually more cost-effective than bespoke orders.
- Easy to layer with other textiles like throws and cushions for a cozy vibe.

Cons:

- In some rooms, an 8x10 can feel “small” if you have an expansive floor plan or high ceilings—resulting in a design scape that looks more about the rug than the furniture.
- If your furniture layout is non-linear, you may end up with awkward gaps or overhangs.

If your floor plan is a friendly rectangle and your furniture groups sit with a tidy rhythm, the 8x10 can be your best friend. If your space is quirky, you’re a friend of big personality, or you’re chasing a specific artful layering effect, a custom size might be your better bet.

## Ordering and installation: what to expect and how to avoid drama
Ordering a rug, especially one with custom sizing, is a little like ordering a pizza with extra toppings: you want it perfect, but you also want it delivered on time and without extra charges that ruin the mood.

### How to order: steps that won’t make you cry
1. Measure precisely: length, width, clearance around furniture, and door clearances. Don’t rely on “roughly” here; accuracy matters for a product that’s supposed to tie a room together.
2. Decide on the dimension strategy: custom size vs 8x10. If you’re uncertain, start with the 8x10 and note where you’d like changes for future rooms.
3. Confirm fiber and weave expectations: confirm that the leg-minimal surface is what you want, especially if you have pets or small children who test the materials.
4. Review care basics: sisal weaves can be a little stingy with moisture; you’ll want to know cleaning methods before you spill coffee on a Tuesday.
5. Confirm backing and protection: consider a rug pad for added stability and to protect your flooring.

### Installation and care: keeping the vibe alive
- Placement: align the rug so it anchors the seating area; the front legs of seating should ideally rest on the rug for grounding. If you have a coffee table on top of the rug, ensure the rug is large enough so all chairs can slide in and out without catching the edge.
- Cleaning: vacuum regularly with a gentle setting; avoid aggressive brushing that can loosen fibers. If you spill something, blot, don’t rub, to minimize fiber damage. For deeper stains, consult the care guide—though with natural fibers, speed is your best friend.
- Maintenance: rotate periodically to even out wear from sunlight and foot traffic. Natural fiber rugs can show patina over time, which is designer speak for “it looks lived-in but in a good way.”

The fiber’s argument here is simple: it’s organic, sustainable, and reasonably forgiving—just not a spa for the fluffiest of paws.
