## Painting, assembly, and conversion notes

### Assembly and materials

Most Zombicide Black Plague minis are pre-muglified into sturdiness; Ariane tends to come in a few pieces: the main body, cloak, lute, and an optional base insert or extra accessory. The usual caveat applies: clean mold lines, assemble with plain plastic cement, and let it cure. If you’re a hobbyist who loves a perfectly seamless look, you’ll want to do some green stuff work around joints. If you’re a gamer who just wants to throw the miniature into a battle, a quick dry brush and a matte seal will suffice.

### Painting strategy for Ariane

Ariane’s color palette is ideal for a bardic aesthetic: muted earth tones, a splash of metallics on the lute, and a cloak that benefits from a cool wash. Here’s a quick approach:
- Prime in gray or white to keep details sharp.
- Basecoat flesh tones and browns for the cloak, with a deep blue or emerald as the secondary color to evoke a “bard with a secret” vibe.
- Layer highlights on raised edges with a lighter shade; wash the recesses with a black wash for depth.
- Paint the lute with wood tones or a metallic highlight if you want it to glow with a magical aura.
- Seal with a matte finish to avoid glare in dim gaming rooms.

You’ll end up with a miniature that looks good under standard indoor lighting and doesn’t demand a forced perspective to read at a gaming table. If you’re into tabletop photography, Ariane does photograph well, especially when you position her against the cathedral’s silhouette or a trap-ridden alley. A quick tip: use a neutral backdrop with a light from the side to bring out the textures on the cloak and the lute’s wood grain.

### Alternatives and conversions

If you’re not entirely satisfied with the stock pose, consider a reversible pose or alternative arms/weapon swaps that are common in the Zombicide community. Some players swap the lute for a more aggressive instrument, such as a sword-shaped club or a staff to imply magical augmentation. Some hobbyists go further and convert Ariane into a different class entirely for homebrew campaigns. If you’re into conversions, you’ll find a host of tutorials online that show how to attach a lute-toting arm without sacrificing stability or pose integrity. Remember: conversions can void warranty if you’re buying from a store with strict return policies, so proceed with caution if you’re aiming for a strict_out-of-the-box experience.
