## What makes this special guest edition stand out

If you have followed Zombicide for a while, you know that the base game offers co op chaos and a satisfying array of undead and survivors. The Green Horde expansion brings goblins, orcs, and a different flavor of chaos, while Black Plague DE is the classic zombie virus reimagined with a medieval twist and, in the German edition, snappy translation touches and unique card art. The collaboration of Hewelin and Edouard Guiton adds a designer touch that fans have long craved in the Zombicide line: more personality inside the rules without sacrificing the frantic, pick-and-bash gameplay the series is famous for.

From a distance, two things pop out about this set. First, the production quality. Second, the balance between two very distinct directions: the goblin onslaught of Green Horde and the plague-choked, grimdark vibe of Black Plague DE. It sounds like a recipe for a confusing kitchen, but the designers manage to serve a banquet that feels cohesive, with each component playing nicely with the others. The DE edition adds a local flavor that reminds you this is a game designed for a specific audience, while still being accessible to newcomers.

To borrow a line from our own archives, if you want to know how a strong guest designer can inject energy into a long running line, this set is your case study. Hewelin brings a lighter touch and a knack for quirky interactions, while Edouard Guiton leans into tactical clarity and crunchy decision points. The result is a product that feels incremental in a good way: you don’t need to relearn the wheel, but you do get a few new knobs to twist.

## Box contents, components, and visual design

The box weight tells you this is not a starter set. It’s a midtier expansion that rewards players who like to spread boards and cover them in plastic goblin teeth. The miniatures are sharp and detailed. The Green Horde goblins have a character all their own: spiky helmets, twig-like bows, and a tendency to swarm more than some other factions. The Black Plague DE components lean into the medieval plague doctor vibe, with a palette that leans toward muted browns and mossy greens, as if your board has wandered into a damp dungeon and decided to stay for a nap.

The overall art direction is cohesive with the main Zombicide line but adds a dash of regional flavor with the DE edition. You’ll find new survivor cards and new enemy cards, each with clear icons and practical layout. The rulebook is thorough but accessible, with a quick start insert that’s worth printing for a real table-ready experience. And yes, there are those occasional designer notes you expect from a guest edition that tell you the designers had a good time, but the notes never break the gameplay rhythm.

One thing worth highlighting is the quality of the tokens and boards. The Goblin tokens are punchy and easy to read, which matters when your goblins start sprinting in from multiple corners at the same time. The Black Plague DE boards carry a tactile, slightly rough finish that makes tracking wounds and tiles a little more satisfying than a slick, glossy surface. It’s the small touches that separate a good expansion from a great one, and in this pack, those touches are plentiful.

## How Hewelin and Edouard Guiton influence the gameplay experience

Hewelin’s design voice tends to lean toward modular, bite-sized decisions that still feel impactful. In this set, you’ll notice scenarios where a goblin ambush can be neutralized not just by killing the goblin, but by exploiting terrain or ally timing in ways you might not expect. That’s a nod to Hewelin’s penchant for tactical nuance rather than brute force. The result is a more dynamic early game where new players won’t simply rush the closest zombie, and veterans won’t feel like they’re spinning their wheels in the mud.

Edouard Guiton brings a temperament for clarity and flow. If you’ve ever sat down to a game with a pile of rules that somehow never quite click, you’ll appreciate his contributions here. The synergy rules, initiative order, and objective tracking land with a certain elegance. The learning curve remains gentle enough for a few friends who show up with coffee and questionable dice sets, but there’s enough density to keep long-running campaigns interesting.

The guest fusion also means you’ll encounter fresh scenario objectives that push players to think beyond the maximize-two-tiles-and-horde approach. It encourages cooperative play that feels natural rather than forced. And because we’re dealing with two disparate IP flavors — goblin chaos and plague era ambiance — the resulting game tempo benefits from alternating bouts of skirmish and strategy. You’ll feel like you’re juggling both a siege and a rescue mission at the same time, which in board game logic terms equals entertainment with escalating tension.

For players who crave a dice-tossing adrenaline burst, there are moments where the goblin swarms become a ridiculous puzzle you can solve with team positioning and timely card play. For those who prefer a slower burn, the plague decks puzzle out ways to corral your survivors and make every single decision feel weighty. The duality is a feature, not a bug, and it’s a credit to how Hewelin and Edouard balance the two halves of this package.

## Scenarios, campaign feel, and replayability

Green Horde adds a chunky new roster of scenarios. Some highlight fast action and multi-boarding plays, while others emphasize careful planning with brief windows of opportunity. In Black Plague DE, you’ll see mission design that nods to classic siege and plague doctor tropes, but with a modern twist that keeps everything feeling fresh on repeat plays. The marriage of the two in one box gives you a surprisingly broad sandbox to explore.

Campaign play feels surprisingly coherent here. The special guest designers managed to thread a narrative through the scenario cards that doesn’t feel forced. You’ll have moments where your crew nearly loses a survivor to a misstep, only to recover and push forward with a clever card combo. If you enjoy lore pockets and small narrative wins, this is a nice canvas for your group’s inside jokes and house rules.

In terms of balance, there are a few tricky interactions where the Green Horde can snowball if you misread the terrain or mismanage the activation phase. That is not a flaw; it’s a feature that rewards attentive co op play. If you love a good puzzle where the win state requires team coordination under pressure, you’ll adore the tension here. If you prefer pure brute force and unambiguous paths to victory, you might want to approach with caution and a backup plan.

We also appreciate how well this integrates with other Zombicide lineups. If you own the base game plus other expansions, you’ll find that the card and token ecosystems mesh in surprisingly clean ways. It’s not a universal fit, of course, but the design ensures you can mix and match scenarios across boxes without a spreadsheet of errata on your lap.

## Components and durability under table abuse

The minis are crisp and hold up to the occasional coffee spill conditioned by a shaky table and a cat who thinks your dice are snacks. The plastic quality is durable enough for regular play without the fear of snapping under moderate pressure. The cardboard punch boards feel sturdy, with clean scoring and minimal burrs. The dice, you ask? They feel satisfying to roll, with a tactile heft that makes a long play session feel a little more epic than it should be.

We should mention the scenario boards and terrain tiles. They are sturdy enough to stand up to a few accidental flips and some enthusiastic table flips after a particularly tense round. If you play with a dry-erase marker to track certain status effects or board state, you’ll appreciate the smooth surface tolerant to occasional marker smudges and quick erasing.

In short: this is not a fragile collector’s edition where you must keep the shrink wrap on forever. It’s designed for regular play, which is exactly what a party game should be in the first place.

## Practical play tips and start-to-finish flow

- Start with the quick start guide included in the box. It’s your best friend for your first session and helps you avoid the dreaded long setup.
- Separate Goblin miniatures by faction. It reduces table chaos and speeds up the setup time between rounds.
- Use the quick reference sheets for status effects. They’re a small investment that saves you a lot of repeated rule-checking and table talk.
- Allocate a rotating table host to manage spawn tables and objective cards. Keeps the chaos contained and ensures fairness, especially with new players.
- If you are streaming or recording a play-session, keep the tempo high. The games in this box reward momentum, and pausing too long breaks the dramatic tension of the Zombicide encounter.

## Final thoughts and recommendation

The Zombicide Special Guest Hewelin Edouard Guiton Green Horde Black Plague DE package arrives as a rare creature in the world of expansions: it respects the core engine while injecting a personality with two designers who clearly had a good time. The balance between goblin chaos and plague era drama creates a tempo that challenges both new players and veterans. The German edition touches add flavor without complicating mechanics for non German players, which demonstrates thoughtful localization rather than token inclusion.

If you own the base game and at least one of the big expansions, this set is a strong candidate for your next purchase. It brings new tactical questions, a handful of memorable scenarios, and a playful energy that keeps Zombicide feeling fresh rather than repetitive. If you are a completionist, you’ll want it on your shelf for the novelty alone. If you’re a practical gamer who values replayability, you’ll appreciate the variety and the way it slots into your existing library without demanding an entire reorganization of your board game closet.

For fans of the series, this expansion proves one thing quite clearly: designers who know how to mix two distinct vibes can produce something that feels both familiar and excitingly new. Hewelin and Edouard Guiton have delivered a package that respects the past while inviting players to explore new corners of the Zombicide universe. It doesn’t replace existing favorites; it expands them with a new rhythm and a wink to the fans who have followed the series for years.

If you are in the mood for a session that alternates between siege tactics and swarm management, this is your box. If on a rare day you want to combine a little storytelling with fast-paced skirmishes, you will find plenty of that here as well. And if you happen to be playing in DE with friends who prefer their undead with a side of lederhosen, rejoice — the DE edition is ready to roll.

In short: it’s a solid buy, a fun design collaboration, and a worthy addition to the Zombicide pantheon. Now go grab your dice, rally your survivors, and let the memes commence.
