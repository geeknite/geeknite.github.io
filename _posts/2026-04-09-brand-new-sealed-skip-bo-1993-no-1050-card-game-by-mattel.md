### What is Skip-Bo and what makes this edition special?

Skip-Bo is a fast moving, family friendly card game about sorting and racing to build sequential stacks. The goal is to get rid of all your stock cards by playing them in numerical order in a central building pile. It is not a heavy strategy game—this is not a chess tournament—but it does reward players with a tiny sense of victory when a three card combo lines up like a tiny, very loud waterfall of numbers. The 1993 No.1050 edition by Mattel sits in a class of its own. The packaging screams early 90s optimism, with bold primary colors and friendly character design. The no-nonsense box art promises endless afternoons of family fun, plus the subtle vibe that your aunt may or may not remember how to count to twelve.
