### Assembly: a tiny quest that ends with a feast

Two hands are nicer than one, but one determined human with a teaspoon of patience and a playlist that builds confidence can do it. Expect to assemble chair frames, attach backrests, and secure the table crossbars. It’s not a marathon, but it’s a good workout for your screwdrivers and your mental image of “semi-assembled furniture.”

Pro tip: lay out all parts before starting; it saves time and reduces the drama of finding a misplaced bolt mid-assembly. After you’ve done the initial setup, you’ll discover the folding mechanism is a delightful blend of simplicity and reliability. The chairs fold with a reassuring snap, and the table folds down into a compact footprint that you can slide behind a planter or stash under a deck railing when the party ends and you want to pretend you’ll host a “tiny dinner” next week.

The wood’s natural warmth pairs well with nearly any décor—from modern to rustic to coastal—the table’s quiet presence letting your dishes and conversation take the spotlight.

### Weather resistance and durability: how it holds up to the elements

Acacia wood stands up well to outdoor life, especially when treated with a protective oil and kept out of constant rainfall or direct-edge sun exposure. In climates with heavy rain, you’ll want to cover or store the set during the worst of the season to preserve color and reduce potential warping. If you forget to oil the wood every so often, you’ll notice a slightly drier, more crack-prone surface over time—think of it as the wood’s way of showing you it needs a spa day. The stainless hardware should resist rust under normal use, but you’ll maximize longevity by ensuring water isn’t sitting on the surface for extended periods and by cleaning salt spray if you’re near the coast.

### Maintenance tips: keeping the glow without turning it into a project

- Clean with a mild soap and warm water; avoid harsh detergents that strip the finish.
- Oil or seal the wood every 6–12 months depending on exposure. Lightly sand first if you notice a rough feel or uneven color.
- Use a breathable cover in winter or during heavy rain. Do not seal with polyurethane if you want the natural look to stay—this is wood furniture, not a spaceship hull.
- Use cushions to increase comfort and reduce wear on the seating surface.
