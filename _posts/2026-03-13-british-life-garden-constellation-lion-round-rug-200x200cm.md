## Materials and Build Quality

Durability in rugs is mostly about fibers, backing, and the technique used to apply the motif. In this product, you’ll typically find a synthetic fiber blend (often polypropylene and polyester) chosen for stain resistance, durability, and that coveted “soft underfoot” feel. The backing is usually a non-slip or lightly textured base to help the rug stay put on smooth floors; this is essential in high-traffic zones, where a round rug might otherwise politely roll away during a heated debate about which is the superior sci-fi franchise.

Softness versus resilience is a balance. Expect a plush surface that feels lush but not so plush that it’s a magnet for every trampoline bounce your kids or pets attempt in the living room. The fibrous blend tends to hold up well to everyday wear—foot traffic, pet nails, and spilled tea—but it’s not a be-all end-all. If you’re planning to stage a regular, rowdy game night with three rival clans and a referee who insists on a rug-runner mat, you might want to consider a protective rug pad underneath for longevity.

## Maintenance and Cleaning

Maintenance is the boring-but-important part of rug ownership. The good news: this rug is designed with everyday life in mind. Here are practical guidelines to keep your lion constellation looking majestic:

- Regular vacuuming on a low setting to prevent fiber damage and to keep the starry night sky shimmering.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing; you don’t want to smear the celestial map into a meteor shower on the surface.
- For tougher stains, use a gentle detergent solution and test on an inconspicuous spot first. Most synthetic blends resist common household stains, which is exactly what you want when a brave cup of tea encounters a hungry puppy.
- If pet hair becomes a nuisance, a rubber brush or a lint roller helps. The textured surface means some fur may cling, but the overall look remains cleaner than you’d expect.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear and sun exposure. Some fade can occur with prolonged direct sunlight, but a responsible rotation helps preserve the constellation’s glow.

Keep in mind that heavy accumulation of dirt or spills could require professional cleaning over time. It’s not a “set it and forget it” piece, but it’s also not so high-maintenance that you’ll need a degree in textile chemistry to keep it presentable.
