## Ventilation, cooling, and airflow considerations

Keeping equipment cool is as important as keeping a clean desk. The 22U chassis uses perforated doors and panels that enable airflow to reach rear-mounted fans and hot-swappable drives. If you’ve got dense packs in the rack and you’re running high-RPM gear, you’ll want to pair this cabinet with appropriate cooling—whether that’s a couple of quiet fans or an entire microdata-center strategy. In smaller installations, you might rely on passive cooling with adequate front-to-back airflow. The cabinet’s construction minimizes gaps that would allow hot air to take a scenic route around your cables, and the doors close with a solid seal that reduces air recirculation.

If you’re planning a high-performance cluster, you’ll want to consider adding blanking panels and ensuring the front and rear doors have sufficient airflow. The cabinet’s depth and width are friendly to typical 19-inch gear, but you’ll want to verify clearance for any large PSU exhausts or rear-side cables. The goal is a consistent airflow: air enters from the front, travels through the devices, and exits out the back without getting stuck along the rails or behind a patch panel.
