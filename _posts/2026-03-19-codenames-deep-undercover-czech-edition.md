## How to Play: The Core Mechanics (Spoiler-Free)
### The turn structure
On your turn, the spymaster gives a single-word clue along with a number, indicating how many cards on the grid relate to that clue. The catch: the clue must be a word that ties to as many of the target cards as possible, while avoiding the assassin card. The rest of the team tries to guess which words match the clue. You can stop guessing at any time, but if you guess an assassin card, your team loses instantly. The tension is exquisite: you either ride the clue to glory or crash into a red mist of bad bets.

### Strategy and risk management
The adult nature of the content introduces new angles to strategy. You’ll want to gen up on your pop culture references, your double entendres, and your ability to read the room. Some players will nail a perfect string of guesses; others will misinterpret a pun and unleash a cascade of hilariously wrong choices. This is where the game shines: it rewards social reading, humor timing, and the willingness to accept a bad pun as a valuable learning experience for the table.

## Practical Tips, Tricks, and House Rules
### Pro tips for the spymasters
- Build a library of flexible clues. One-word answers that can apply to multiple cards are gold; single-card hints that only fit one card are risky unless you’re sure your team will catch it.
- Use color and context; tie clues to shared experiences, inside jokes, or universal pop culture frames. The better your clue maps multiple cards, the more momentum you gain.
- Read the room. If your teammates are groaning at a pun, pivot to something lighter before the round ends. The best spymasters know when to switch gears.

### House rules for smoother nights
- Quick-deck swap: keep the adult deck handy but swap in a clean deck for younger players or mixed groups.
- Safety off-switch: designate a “pause” rule if a clue crosses a line that makes someone uncomfortable. A quick, respectful pause can save a night from becoming awkward.
- Time pressure toggle: adjust the timer to loosen or tighten the pace. A shorter timer increases the adrenaline; a longer timer improves thinking time but can slow momentum.

### Variants and clever implementations
- Cooperative variant: both teams share a single deck and work to connect all red and blue keywords together in a single narrative thread. Adds a storytelling layer and shifts the vibe away from competition.
- Team-switch rounds: mid-game swap teams for a few rounds to mix players and refresh the dynamic.
- Thematic rounds: pick a single theme (e.g., 80s movies, sci-fi jargon) and exclusively use clue words aligned to that theme. It’s nerdy in the best possible way.
