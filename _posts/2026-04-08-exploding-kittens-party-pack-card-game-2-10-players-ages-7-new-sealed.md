## How to Play: Quick Setup and Movement
The Party Pack plays fast, and setup time should be measured in minutes rather than in the era of long, complicated board games. To start, shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand to each player. The goal remains simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you draw one, you better have a Defuse card or some other trick up your sleeve to mitigate the blowback.

Turn flow is straightforward:
- Draw a card at the start of your turn
- Play any number of action cards from your hand to influence the turn
- End your turn by passing the play to the next player
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten and cannot Defuse, you are out of the round

The party-friendly twist is the presence of a wider pool of action cards that encourage more aggressive plays and more opportunities for mischief. The Attack cards, for instance, push a back-and-forth tempo where players chain actions, creating opportunities for witty banter and dramatic misreads of your rivals’ facial expressions. The Nope card remains a fan favorite; it allows you to interrupt someone else’s move, which leads to hilarious moments that feel like a courtroom drama but with rubber chickens instead of subpoenas.

Setup notes for big groups: you can adjust the starting hand size or the number of Defuse cards in circulation to throttle or accelerate the chaos. It is a design decision that should be made with the vibe of the night in mind. If you are hosting a particularly messy game night (which is to say, the best kind of night), you might want more Nope cards to keep everyone honest and to encourage generous trash talk. If you want a longer game for a smaller group, trim the deck or give more Defuse cards to keep players around the table longer.

## The Core Mechanics: Luck, Skill, and Cat-Based Shenanigans
Exploding Kittens as a concept thrives on a simple mechanism: drawing cards and hoping you’re not about to explode into a cloud of animated fur. The Party Pack extends that concept to a bigger party, but it keeps the heartbeat intact: draw, risk, react, survive, repeat. There is luck, there is social manipulation, and there is the unavoidable reality that some players will overplay their power to compress the game’s timeline into a single, glorious moment of chaos.

Strategically, you can do a few things: (1) estimate the probability of drawing an Exploding Kitten given the number of Defuse cards left; (2) deploy cards that maximize your survival odds, such as Shuffle or See the Future-like effects (if included in your variant); (3) manipulate the social dynamic by pressing opponents with threats of an emotional or comedic “blow-up” that is more terrifying to them than any actual explosion. The game rewards players who balance risk and humor. If you go too conservative, you’ll get sniped by a more reckless neighbor—like a party where one person insists on playing a literal “fun” theme and somehow makes everyone else feel attacked by their good mood.

The social component deserves emphasis.
