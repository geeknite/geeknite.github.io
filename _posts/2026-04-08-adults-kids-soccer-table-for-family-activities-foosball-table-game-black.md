### Assembly tips and common pitfalls
A lot of assembly drama is just misreading the parts list. The most common pitfall is forgetting to align the rods with the correct holes, which leads to a wobble that makes even the most confident adult question their life choices. Pro-tip: lay out all hardware before you start, and use a small magnetic tray to keep screws from fleeing under the sofa mid-assembly. You’ll also want to ensure the table is on a flat surface; a carpet jungle can turn your perfectly leveled playfield into an undulating sea of chaos—great for dramatic comebacks, terrible for accurate passes.

If you have kids helping, assign roles like “ball handler” and “scorekeeper” rather than letting them wield a screwdriver. It keeps the process fun and reduces the risk of accidental repairs that require a trip to the hardware store. It’s also a good opportunity to teach them the value of patience and teamwork, because nothing accelerates a group learning curve like a looming, bookshelf-straightening level.
