## Care, Display, and Long-Term Preservation

### Sleeves, Top Loaders, and Display Stands

When you own a Gloom Holo AR SV3, care is a ritual. Use sleeves that match your preferred protection level—hard sleeves for display, semi-rigid for travel, and ultra-clean anti-glare sleeves during storage. A top loader or binder with moisture- and UV-protective properties will keep the holo pop intact without dulling the flame aura. If you’re the type who likes a dedicated display, consider a light stand with a soft beam to avoid direct sunlight, which can cause color shift in certain holo patterns over many years.

### Handling and Cleaning Tips

Always wash hands before handling, avoid touching the holo surface directly, and keep pets away, because nothing ruins a collector’s mood like a curious puppy nose on a shiny card. If fingerprints appear, a gentle microfiber cloth with a tiny amount of legitimate card-cleaning solution (specifically designed for trading cards) will do the trick. Don’t scrub like you’re buffing a car; you want to preserve the card’s micro-texture and prevent micro-abrasions on the holo layer.

### Storage Environment and Humidity

Aim for a cool, dry place with stable temperature. High humidity can encourage warping, and heat can alter the adhesive in packs over time. If you’re serious about long-term preservation, consider climate-controlled storage and avoid stacking too many cards on top of one another; pressure can lead to edge wear on holo corners that collectors fear more than a bad meme.
