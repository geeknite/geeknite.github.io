## Sizes and Customization: Custom Size or 8x10
One of the big selling points here is the flexibility around sizing. The Eden rug is pitched as a custom-size option, with a popular fallback to the classic 8x10 measurement for users who want a reliable, proven footprint. Here’s how to think about it:
- Custom sizes: You can tailor the rug to fit a specific space, from a cozy reading nook to a sprawling living room. If your room has odd dimensions, a custom rug helps avoid the “is it too small?” or “is it too big?” dilemma that makes interior design feel like a minefield in socks.
- 8x10 standard: A safe, versatile size that works under most coffee tables and centers in medium-to-large living spaces. It’s also the size most readers ask us to test because it’s a sweet spot for shared spaces and photography alike.

Sizing considerations:
- Room dynamics: Measure the room, consider furniture footprint, and leave a border of 8–24 inches around for visual balance. A rug that’s too small will make a room feel disjointed; a rug that’s too large can swallow your furniture’s character.
- Traffic and wear: If your space is high-traffic (entryways, kids, pets), the wool’s resilience and pile height will matter. A deeper pile feels luxurious but can show wear quicker in heavily used zones; a flatter weave looks cleaner longer.
- Room function: Under dining tables or desks, a flat-wold or low-pile option avoids tripod-legged chair leg drama and carpet snagging.

## Materials, Durability, and Maintenance
Wool is inherently a durable fiber, with natural elasticity that helps rugs bounce back after foot traffic and heavy chair swivels. The Eden toast coconut wool rug benefits from that elasticity, which reduces early pile crush compared to cheaper synthetic blends. Let’s break down the practical aspects:
- Softness underfoot: The wool fibers feel cuddly rather than scratchy, even after months of daily use. It’s the kind of rug your kids and pets will claim as their own playground—within reason.
- Durability: Wool’s resilience means the rug should resist crushing and wear in high-traffic zones. Expect years of service with proper care.
- Stain resistance: Wool has natural moisture-wicking properties and tends to resist staining better than some synthetics, but it’s not invincible. Quick blotting is your friend. For tough stains, consult the care label or professional cleaning guidance.
- Cleaning guidance: Regular vacuuming with a suction setting that doesn’t pull or stretch the fibers will keep the rug looking crisp. For spills, blot—don’t rub—and use a wool-friendly cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that can alter color or texture.
- Odor control: Wool fibers are naturally odor-resistant to a degree, which is a nice perk for busy households with pets and the occasional late-night pizza run.

### Handling and care tips
- Use a non-slip pad on hard floors to prevent creeping and add cushioning.
- Rotate your rug every 6–12 months to ensure even wear and color balance.
- Address spills promptly with blotting and appropriate cleaners suited for wool.
- Consider a professional cleaning every 12–24 months for deeper refreshment.

External readers often ask if wool rugs shed. In the Eden line, you might observe minor shedding initially, which tends to settle down as fibers relax with use. Regular vacuuming (no beater bar) helps minimize this and keeps the surface looking cohesive.

## Installation, Placement, and Real-world Use Cases
Installing a rug isn’t rocket science, but there are some best practices that help you avoid the “rug appears smaller than the room” syndrome or, worse, excessive curling at the edges:
- Underlay and padding: Use a high-quality rug pad to prevent slipping and add a touch of cushion. This makes the Eden rug feel more luxurious under bare feet and keeps it in place on hard floors.
- Table layouts: Under coffee tables, ensure that at least the front legs sit on the rug to anchor the space; you can extend the rug beyond the immediate table footprint for a more generous footprint.
- Door clearance: If a door opens onto the rug, verify there’s enough clearance to avoid tripping or catching the edge.

Placement ideas:
- The living room hero: Center under the seating arrangement with even margins, allowing the rug to visually “hug” the furniture. 
- The reading nook: Create a secluded corner by pairing the Eden rug with a plush chair and a side table. A warm lamp completes the ambiance.
- The work-from-home zone: A wool rug under your desk with a slightly larger footprint to accommodate chair movement and foot comfort.

## Ordering, Customization Process, and Guarantees
The Eden rug is positioned as a product with flexible sizing options. Here’s a typical flow you might expect when ordering a custom size or the standard 8x10:
- Measurements: Have your room dimensions handy, plus consider furniture footprint and running measurements. Decide whether you want the rug to sit under the front legs only or extend beyond the furniture footprint.
- Material and finish: Confirm wool grade, color consistency, and edge finishing. Ask about dye lot variance and whether the color you receive will match the advertised toasted coconut hue.
- Customization details: For custom sizes, provide exact width and length. If a particular border treatment or edge binding is desired, specify in advance.
- Lead times and shipping: Custom sizes typically require longer production times. Confirm the estimated delivery window and any return policy specifics for custom orders.
- Care instructions: Request care guidelines tailored to your rug’s size and intended use. A smaller rug in a high-traffic area might require more frequent cleaning.
