## Sizing and Compatibility

### Common sizes and hallway fits

The Sorbet Runner is available in standard runner lengths, typically ranging from 6 ft to 8 ft with a narrow width that suits foyers and narrow corridors. The footpath length matters because a too-short rug can visually chop up the space; a too-long rug may collide with door thresholds. If your hallway is exactly 6 ft long with a 31-inch width, you are in the Goldilocks zone—the rug will feel just right. For longer hallways, consider pairing two runners in a staggered arrangement or using an additional runner to create a visual rhythm between spaces.

### Placement tips

Place the rug with at least 6 inches of floor visible on both ends if you want a framed look. If you have a console table or a coat rack at one end, ensure you maintain at least 2 inches of clearance for door movement. And if you have a radiant heating system under the floor, double-check the rug backing's heat resistance to avoid curling edges when the thermostat goes on a rampage.

## Practicality: Cleaning, Care, and Long-Term Use

### Stain resistance and colorfastness

Sorbet hues are generally forgiving, but color bleeding can happen when you scrub aggressively or rub the wrong way. We tested a coffee spill and a red wine incident, and both stories ended with minimal staining after immediate blotting and following cleaning guidelines. The key is to act fast and never rub, because friction is the enemy of color fidelity.

### Vacuuming and maintenance schedule

We recommend light vacuuming two to three times a week during heavy traffic seasons. Use a vacuum with a soft brush attachment to prevent snagging. If you notice tiny loops in the weave, resist the urge to yank; instead, trim carefully with small scissors to prevent further unraveling. For stubborn spots, a mild carpet cleaning solution is usually sufficient.

### Pets and kids friendly?

Yes, the rug can handle kid mischief and a friendly canine shreddy afternoon. It’s not a chew toy, so we cannot guarantee that a determined puppy won’t leave a tug-of-war mark, but the color blocks help hide a multitude of small paw prints. If your pet tends to drag in mud, you’ll want to consider a larger size to reduce the grass-stain parade across the living space.
