## Introduction
Herd Mentality is a party game that thrives on the chaos of a big group and pretends to be a deep social experiment. It asks you to reveal both what you would say and what you think the herd will say. It is the kind of game that turns a simple living room into a stadium of opinions where everyone cheers for the same wrong answer, and somehow that is exactly the point. If you love fast paced rounds, inside jokes that land and miss by inches, and a little friendly roast of your best friend for picking a totally insane option, this is your jam.

## How to play in a sentence
Herd Mentality is a social experiment in which players try to predict the crowd plus a dash of self aware humor about what we think others will think. A round begins with a prompt card, each player writes down their answer, and then everyone reveals simultaneously. The trick is that you want to be in the majority, but you also want to avoid being the first one to reveal the wrong option.

### Setup
- Pick the first reader
- Shuffle and draw a prompt card
- Each player writes an answer on their paper
- The group reveals the answers
- Points are awarded to players whose answers match the herd

## The core mechanic
### The herd mentality
The heart of the game is the tension between being right and being popular. The herd chooses a single answer and that choice becomes the target everyone wants to hit. The more players who pick the same answer, the bigger the reward. This simple dynamic creates a rapid feedback loop of guessing and reacting that fuels the party atmosphere.

### Why it works for large groups
When you jam 10 or 12 people into a room and tell them to predict what the crowd will do, you get a buzz that is impossible to fake. The energy grows with each round as new people realize that their gut instinct is often as silly as the latest meme. With 20 players, you get a chorus of opinions, a tapestry of accents and catchphrases, and more inside jokes than a year of stand up specials.

## The tone and humor
Humor in Herd Mentality is social and situational. The prompts often hinge on everyday behavior, pop culture, and the small decisions we pretend to keep secret from the group. The joy comes less from clever wordplay and more from watching the room react to the outcomes. The prompts invite self mockery and friendly roasting, which is exactly the spice that makes a game night memorable.

## Gameplay depth and replayability
The depth lies not in rules but in the social dynamics you cultivate. In different groups you will see different crowds form around different prompts, and the results become stories you might retell later. The prompts can be rotated or swapped with expansions to refresh the night, yet the core experience remains satisfying in its simplicity. Replayability shines when you pair Herd Mentality with various groups, from families to college friends to coworker teams, each bringing a new flavor to the table.

## Accessibility and inclusivity
The game is approachable for players with varying reading abilities and ages. Short prompts and simple scoring mean you can bring in players who rarely play long strategy games but love social silliness. Still, there is a social pressure element that some players may find uncomfortable. Hosts should set a light tone and be mindful of prompts that might hit sensitive topics. The more inclusive the hosting, the more people will feel comfortable and participate in the laughs rather than fading to the back of the room.

## Host tips and party night strategies
- Start with a light prompt to warm up the room and ease players into the vibe
- Rotate the reader so everyone gets a chance to steer the questions
- Keep the scoring light and celebrate the funniest moments rather than the math
- Use household rules for sensitive topics to keep the mood positive
- Consider a mini tournament format to add friendly competition without keeping score for hours

## House rules and variations
If you want to push things into a different direction, try a team mode where players pair up and discuss before answering. Another option is a rapid fire format where prompts appear in quick succession and players must improvise answers on the fly. You can also run a one night tournament with a rotating host and a small final round to crown the winner.

## My take on the 4 to 20 player format
The range on the box is not marketing; it reflects how the game shines with different group sizes. With four players the rounds are still lively if the players are vocal and engaged. With eight to twelve players you get a broader spectrum of opinions and more opportunities for surprising results. ## Score and conclusion
The scoring system is straightforward and designed to keep everyone involved. If your answer matches the herd, you earn points; if you do not, your chance at winning remains intact as you chase the next round. The real magic is in the reveal moment when the majority pops up and you see who nailed the social read and who loved a totally wrong but utterly hilarious choice. It is a party game that rewards social perception and a willingness to embrace the chaos of the room.
