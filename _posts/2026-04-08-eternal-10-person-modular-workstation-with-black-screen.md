## Concept and Design

### Modular Philosophy

The Eternal is built on the idea that a workspace can be decomposed into modules that snap together with the ease of LEGO bricks but with fewer unhappy cousins when you step on them barefoot. Each module is a 120-degree wedge that can be rotated to form a long row, a U shape, or a circle around that Black Screen in the middle. The whole system can be reconfigured in under an hour by a determined team of three interns and two cable ties. It is not a product for people who secretly hate humans and prefer solitude; it is a product for people who want to borrow the best of both worlds: social collaboration and personal space.

### Black Screen: The Mystery Panel

Here we meet the star of the show, the Black Screen. In practice, this panel is a large cohesive surface that absorbs glare and unifies the aesthetic. It is not a one trick pony; it serves as a whiteboard area when needed, a backdrop for product demos, and frankly a convenient place to hide the worst headphones. The Black Screen helps to reduce the feeling that your workspace is a chaotic nest of spaghetti cables. It also provides the creative illusion that you are staring into a black hole of productivity, which is motivational in a nerdy way.

## Setup and Cable Management

### Power Distribution Module

A key feature is the integrated power distribution dock, which serves ten stations at once without turning the room into a tangle of power cords. Cable management is where the Eternal earns its keep.
