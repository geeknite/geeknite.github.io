## Assembly Experience
### Unboxing and Setup
Unboxing is straightforward: hardware in one bag, shelf panels in another, and the inevitable instruction sheet that looks like it was designed by a stressed software engineer. The good news is the assembly typically requires only basic tools (often included) and a calm afternoon away from the urge to over-tighten screws and strip threads. A little patience goes a long way; misaligned holes are an existential crisis in dowel form, but it’s nothing a gentle re-seat can’t fix.

### Step-by-Step Assembly (High-Level)
1) Layout the frame pieces and hardware. 2) Attach uprights to the base. 3) Slide in the lower shelf and secure with crossbars. 4) Add the middle shelf, then the top shelf. 5) Tighten all screws evenly and test for wobble. 6) Place on the desk and admire your new orderliness.

Pro-tip: don’t overtighten. It’s tempting to crimp those screws like you’re building a spaceship, but a little give helps prevent warping and keeps everything aligned. If something feels off, backtrack one step and reseat the shelf panels. It’s not a mea culpa, just good engineering practice.

## Performance and Stability
### Stability on Different Surfaces
On a flat desk, the shelf feels solid, with no dramatic wobble unless you intentionally shake the desk. If your desk is uneven, the adjustable feet or a small rubber pad under one leg can help level the unit. The real test comes when you add a printer and a few heavy notebooks on the top shelf. In most cases, the frame remains sturdy; the top shelf may bow slightly under the heaviest loads, but that’s within the expected tolerance for consumer-grade furniture.

### Weight Distribution and Safety
Distribute weight evenly and avoid mounting a pile of heavy paper reams on the top shelf. A printer in the bottom tier with a stack of paper and a coffee mug on the middle tier is a classic configuration that remains stable. If you ever hear a creak when you reach for a paper tray, pause, re-check screws, and give it a light test wobble to ensure everything’s snug. It’s not a scare tactic; it’s simple maintenance for long-term happiness.
