### Cable management and labeling
Cable management in a 27U wall mount is all about planning and labeling. The enclosure makes this task easier by offering several vertical channels and multiple hooks along the rails for gently coiling cables and keeping the patch panel region tidy. A practical approach is to separate data cables from power, use color coding for clarity, and route cables along the rails with zip ties or Velcro straps at regular intervals. The more you invest in labeling and planning before you mount gear, the less you will curse when you realize you forgot to route a fiber patch and end up needing a backend rework.

A common question is how many 1U devices fit in 27U given the depth. The rough counting method goes like this: plan for at least three to four 1U devices in the first half of the rails, a small 2U or 4U switch in the middle, and space for a patch panel in the upper half. The exact number depends on the thickness of the rails you are using, the thickness of your patch panels, and whether you run cabling behind or in front of the rails. It is possible to achieve a clean layout that minimizes airflow interference and makes maintenance a breeze, but you will need to invest time in the initial organization and labeling.
