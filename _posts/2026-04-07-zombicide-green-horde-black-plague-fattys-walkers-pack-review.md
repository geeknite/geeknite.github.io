## What's in the Fattys & Walkers Pack
The Fattys & Walkers Pack is a compact but chaotic bundle designed to refresh two core Zombicide ecosystems: Green Horde and Black Plague. In practical terms, that means you don’t just get additional zombies; you get new mechanical wrinkles that force you to rethink your entire approach to each game’s map, objectives, and morale. The kit typically contains:

- A handful of Fatty zombie miniatures designed to loom over your survivors like bloated, slow-motion threats—think big, think hungry, think elevator music at a dungeon party.
- A slate of Walker variants—quicker, sneakier, and sometimes more numerous than your typical zed cast. These are not just “more of the same”; they’re the kind of trouble that makes you reconsider your preferred risk profile.
- A set of new zombie cards that reintroduce Fatty and Walker interactions with the existing decks. Expect some nasty combos that are the game’s equivalent of “surprise, it’s not over yet.”
- A couple of thematic mission/scenario sheets that let you weave the new monsters into a narrative arc rather than just blasting your way through a tile farm.
- A quick-start reference sheet to prevent you from spending half a round muttering “What the heck do I do with a Fatty again?” while your team bleeds morale on turn three.

What this pack does beautifully is to preserve the core rhythm of Zombicide while injecting a new tempo. The Fatty miniatures are not simply bigger versions of existing zombies; they come with special rules that create strategic choke points, forcing you to split your attention and balance your team’s strengths against a clock that literally ticks louder when a Fatty shuffles into range.

## How it integrates with Green Horde and Black Plague
This is where things get deliciously nerdy. Zombicide Green Horde is a fantasy eco-system with orcs, goblins, and magic-y bits that turn your survivors into resourceful, sometimes ridiculous heroes. Black Plague, on the other hand, leans into grim fantasy with heavier, often more brutal encounters. The Fattys & Walkers Pack respects both game lines by including rules that adapt to the tone of each universe rather than forcing a one-size-fits-all approach.

- In Green Horde, Fattys tend to be more of a “siege engine” type threat. They slow the pace but force your heroes to coordinate those special abilities and terrain advantages. The walkers, while still dangerous, operate within a tribal-swarm dynamic that the orc world handles with a certain brutal practicality. Your survivors learn to leverage chokepoints, to trap Fattys with terrain features, and to use the greenskin chaos to their advantage rather than fight it head-on.
- In Black Plague, Fattys feel like the kind of apex-zombie that makes you rethink the value of your best survivors. They require focusing fire and often trigger “boss-like” moments where one or two Fattys become a narrative centerpiece. Walkers remain the bread-and-butter threat, but with an added layer of mechanic shenanigans that keep you on your toes—think secondary goals that suddenly become primary if the Fatty is in play.

In short: this pack respects the core engine of both games. It doesn’t feel tacked on; it feels like a natural extension that would have existed if the designers had decided to throw a few extra pounds onto the portable zombie gymnasium. If you rotate between Green Horde and Black Plague, you’ll appreciate how well these additions scale from map to map, from run-and-gun to slow-burn tactics.

## Setup, playtime, and rule tweaks
Before you lay out the robber-barons of cardboard that is your dungeon, here is the practical bit:

- Setup time: Slightly longer than base games but not absurd. If you’re a two-player team, budget an extra five to ten minutes for deployment and card shuffles. If you have four players, you’ll be fine as the Fattys show up as bigger-than-average blips in the early game and become a persistent headache in the mid-to-late game.
- Playtime: Expect 60–120 minutes for a standard mission with Fattys and Walkers in the mix. If you attempt a longer campaign, you’ll want to plan a second session or two in advance; your calendar will become a mausoleum of GMT commitments—great for story, not so much for restful evenings.
- Rule tweaks: The Fatty’s special abilities often include a mix of defense-ignoring brawls and a tendency to reshape the board with a well-timed attack. The Walkers bring swarm dynamics that reward coordination and movement planning. The net effect is a richer tactical layer that rewards players who bring plan-A, plan-B, and plan-C to the table.

If you want a crisp summary of the rules changes without spelunking in the manual, the quick-start reference sheet included in the pack is your friend. ## Strategy tips: surviving the Fatty onslaught
- Don’t underestimate the Fatty’s reach. They may be slow, but their threat range is deceptively long. Use terrain to your advantage, choke points to funnel the horde, and don’t ignore the space your survivors occupy. Sometimes the best tactic is not the most aggressive one but the most strategic.
- Coordinate your team roles. Assign someone to hold the line, another to support with ranged weapons or magic, and a third to pursue objective-based goals while the Fatty is distracted. The key is to keep a balanced party who can adapt as Fatty and Walker waves change the battlefield.
- Don’t ignore the walkers. The classic zombie trap is building up numbers while your survivors handle the bigger threats. A smart Walker deck can overwhelm unprepared teams, so keep a portion of your squad in reserve to handle reinforcement waves.
- Pawn the Fatty with environment. If your map includes doors, pits, or elevation changes, use them to restrict the Fatty’s movement as a line of defense. It’s not always possible, but when it works, it’s glorious in its efficiency.
- Set up a kill cadence. The best way to handle Fattys is to avoid giving them multiple chances to attack by ensuring that your team’s actions grant you control over the initiative. It’s not just about hitting hard; it’s about hitting at the right times.

## Final verdict and rating
What you get with Fattys & Walkers Pack is a carefully tuned recalibration of two classic Zombicide worlds. It doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it refines the ride, offering a fresh tempo and a more engaging strategic layer. If you are a fan of either Green Horde or Black Plague and you crave more tactical depth and memorable moments, this pack will likely become a mainstay on your table. If you value speed over choreography, or if your gaming group prefers a lighter, more pickup-style experience, you may want to approach this with a bit more caution and perhaps a school of thought that favors bite-sized sessions.

- Strengths: thoughtful integration with both games, solid miniatures, engaging new mechanics, good narrative potential, scalable challenge.
- Weaknesses: rule-splosion risk for first-timers, setup time increases slightly, potential for “fatigue” if your group relies on the same tactic repeatedly.

Overall: 8.5 out of 10. A standout expansion that respects the DNA of both Green Horde and Black Plague while giving players a new reason to glare at their table during a zombie assault. It’s a recommended pick for serious Zombicide fans and a solid buy-in for players looking to deepen the table’s strategic heartbeat.
