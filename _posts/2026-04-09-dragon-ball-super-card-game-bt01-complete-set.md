## What BT01 actually is
BT01 stands for Booster Test 01, or at least that is how some of us memory-hoarders like to pretend the acronym means. In practical terms, BT01 is the first big booster wave that introduced a host of new cards, a lot of flavor text, and a couple of sprinkles of power cards that could shake up early game metas. The complete set, by design, is a curated slice of the booster contents that ensures you can build at least a few viable decks right out of the box without chasing down dozens of random pulls from separate boosters.

The 100 cards are not just random; they are a curated mix of common, rare, and more powerful rarities. This mix makes BT01 a solid entry point for players who are new to the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, as well as for veterans looking to fill in gaps in a growing collection. The distribution typically aims to provide enough support for a couple of tier-1 archetypes, alongside a handful of one-off tech picks that can surprise your local meta. The end result is a box that feels like a complete starter experience, but with enough complexity to keep an experienced player from yawning in boredom.

## Playability: building decks with BT01
BT01 is not a one-trick pony. It supports a variety of deck-building philosophies, from raw aggression to measured control and tempo-based plays. Here are a few practical tips for getting the most out of the complete BT01 set:

- Build around a core engine. Look for cards that accelerate energy management and card draw, so you can stay aggressive without burning your resources. A strong core engine helps you press the advantage when your opponent is reactive.
- Identify your win conditions. Some cards tilt the game toward aggressive finishes, while others enable counterplay or stall tactics that extend the match into a favorable endgame. BT01 offers a balanced mix to explore these paths.
- Tech for meta chips. The set includes several “tech” cards designed to counter common strategies. Don’t be afraid to include a couple of counter-picks in your sideboard or as flexible one-offs that you swap in between games.
- Practice timing, not just power. In Dragon Ball Super Card Game, when you play a card often matters more than what the card does. Qualify the moment to maximize impact, and you’ll notice a real difference in your win rate over multiple sessions.
