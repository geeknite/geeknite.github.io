## Installation and Compatibility
### Desk Compatibility
This panel is built to work with standard desk edges (the kind you see in most home offices and open-plan workspaces). If your desk uses a thick solid wood or metal edge, you’re still likely fine, but the clamp’s bite and the distribution of pressure matter. If you have an irregular edge shape, add the extra caution that you might need a shim or a slightly different clamp variant. If you’re not sure, the manufacturer’s spec sheet usually lists the compatible thickness range; if you’re between numbers, err on the side of caution and test the clamp on a scrap piece of wood first.

### Setup Time and Tools
The setup is designed to be quick and painless. Most users will need only the included tool (or a standard Allen key) to tighten the clamps. You’ll set the panel height, ensure it’s level, then tighten both clamps evenly to avoid lateral tilt. A helpful trick: place the panel in position before you tighten the clamps fully to check for any interference with drawers, monitor stands, or your favorite desk plant. If your desk has a cable tray underneath, make sure the clamps don’t collide with it; you don’t want to discover mid-assembly that your cables now have better plans than you do.

### How It Affects Desk Layout

If your desk is part of a larger run in an open-plan office, the panel can act as a privacy corridor for video calls, noting that it isn’t a cure-all for acoustic privacy. ## In-Use Performance
### Privacy, Focus, and Screen Real Estate
The essential promise of any desk privacy panel is to create a private micro-environment. This panel does a decent job of cutting line-of-sight from the sides, which helps with focus when coworkers lean in to discuss the color of the stapler or the meaning of life. It won’t magically remove all ambient noise or turn your desk into a soundproof booth, but it does create a visible boundary that reinforces a focus-first mindset. If you’ve ever tried to work with a neighbor who insists on narrating their day like a sports announcer, this panel may be your best friend.

### Stability and Movement
Clamps are designed to hold, and the 60x24 panel generally stays put as long as the desk edge is gripped evenly. If you’re the kind of desk occupant who likes to lean back and bounce in your chair, you might notice slight movement or a tiny wobble if the clamps aren’t perfectly aligned. The trick here is to level the panel and tighten in a criss-cross pattern so the pressure is distributed. If your desk is on carpet and you don’t have a level surface, consider a little anti-slip pad under one clamp to stabilize the setup. If you do a lot of standing desk transitions, you’ll want to recheck the clamps after you’ve reconfigured the height, just to be safe.

### Cleaning and Maintenance
Cleaning is straightforward: a damp microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner are all you need. The fabric veneer is resistant to coffee splashes, marker doodles, and the occasional rogue dust bunny that tries to hold a meeting on your panel. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fabric cover or the rubber padding on the clamps. If you notice the fabric developing a snag or pilling after heavy use, you can follow up with a fabric-friendly refresher or replace the panel if it’s part of a modular system.
