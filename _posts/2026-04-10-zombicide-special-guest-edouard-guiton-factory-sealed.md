## The Guest Interview: Edouard Guiton — Design Philosophy and Quirks (Fictional)
### A Quick Q and A with Edouard
Q: What does factory sealed mean to you as a designer?
A: It means the product arrives with care that makes the first playthrough feel like a reward for time well spent. It is about preserving the tactile joy of components, as if every card is whispering that the action is about to begin.
Q: Any favorite component you want players to notice?
A: The inserts. They are the unsung heroes that keep chaos from turning into disassembly at 2 am. They keep the table tidy so you can focus on the drama of surviving the last stand.
Q: If you could design a new Zombicide expansion, what would it be?
A: A snow map. Zombies in the snow mean footprints, slippery edges, and a fresh set of tactical misfires. It would be a chilly test of nerves and dice control.

While this is a playful interview, Edouard is clearly serious about component quality, and his candor underscores a design ethic that respects players from the very first touch of the box through the final turn of a chaotic campaign.
