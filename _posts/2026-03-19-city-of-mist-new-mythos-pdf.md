## What New Mythos Brings to the Table

### New Mythos Archetypes
New Mythos introduces a cadre of fresh archetypes designed to slot into the existing framework without demanding a full rewrite of your character concepts. Among the new faces you will encounter are:
- The Whisperer: a mythic presence that thrives on secrets and suggestion, bending conversations with a silent nudge
- The Archivist: keeper of hidden histories, turning every clue into a thread that leads to a larger tapestry
- The Ember: a force of passion and reaction that fuels impulsive choices and dramatic turns
- The Veil: master of misdirection and subtext, turning perception against protagonists in clever, cinematic ways
- The Oracle: prophecy as a tool for foreshadowing, adding a new layer of fate to your scenes
- The Warden: enforcer of a personal or broader moral code, imposing structure on chaotic urban legends

Each archetype is written to feel distinct, with its own flavor of narrative leverage and mechanical hooks. You can pair any of these with a classic City of Mist hero to produce fresh conflicts, revelations, and dramatic irony. The result is a wider palette without requiring players to abandon the vibe that makes the game feel unique.

### New Power Tags and Play Tools
The core system rests on the interplay of Tags, Power Tags, and Story Points. New Mythos adds several new Power Tags that emphasize the new archetypes, along with a handful of situational Tags to describe mood, motif, or thematic pressure in a scene. Expect lines like Whisper Influence, Archive of Hidden Truths, Veil of Remorse, and Oracle’s Farsight to be the recurring motifs when players describe their actions.

A neat addition is the Mythic Thread mechanic, which helps GMs connect multiple player arcs through a single mythic seed. It provides a natural way to escalate tension when several characters appear to be tugging on the same mythical thread, without forcing everyone to chase the same objective. The tooling feels intuitive: you plant a seed, players attach their arcs to it with Tags, and the GM uses the thread to craft a throughline that makes sense across sessions rather than in a single scene.

### Interaction with the Core Rules
The New Mythos material does not rewrite the core rulebook; instead, it expands and deepens the existing mechanics. You still declare Tags, spend Story Points to activate effects, and react to the GM’s scene framing. The expansion provides a handful of new archetypes and scene-framing options that can slot into existing narratives with minimal prep. If your group already enjoys the improvisational energy of City of Mist, you will likely find the New Mythos content to be a natural extension rather than a detour.

## Mechanics, Balance, and Play Experience

### How the New Mythos Plays With Your Sessions
The new archetypes come with their own micro-mrules to keep play consistent with the core engine. The balance is sensible: the new options give you additional ways to describe and direct a scene, but they don’t make or break a resolution for your dice pool. The rules feel additive rather than disruptive, which is exactly what a good expansion should do: grant new angles without forcing a new set of meta-choices every session.

In practical terms, you’ll notice that the Mythos seeds can be dropped into a preexisting arc without creating odd power gaps or narrative clumps. The new Tags and the Mythic Thread mechanism provide opportunities to weave personal stakes into a larger city-wide myth has-been or legend-in-the-making. A GM who likes to thread personal arcs through a frame narrative will find the book particularly rewarding.

### The Flow of a Session with New Mythos
When running a session, the flow remains familiar: players declare Tags that reflect their current mythic influence, the GM frames a scene, and you escalate with Scenes and Clocks. New Mythos feeds into this structure by offering scene templates and a few encounter prompts that feel cinematic rather than procedural. The prompts are designed to be toggled up or down in intensity, so you can calibrate them to a two-hour one-shot or a multi-session arc.

If you are a fan of the noir-mythic vibe, you’ll appreciate the emphasis on characterization and motif. The archetypes are built to support dramatic reveals that feel earned, rather than gimmicky. The Oracle seed, for example, provides a natural conversation pivot—players discover a truth that reframes the entire scene, forcing new decisions about who to trust and what counts as a legitimate myth

## Thematic Fit and Campaign Integration

### How New Mythos Enhances a City of Mist Campaign
If your table enjoys a sprawling, mythic narrative with personal stakes, New Mythos offers a natural extension. The new archetypes provide fresh angles for character identity and conflict, and the Mythic Thread element gives you a practical tool to maintain continuity across sessions. The feel remains true to City of Mist: a modern world where legends are visible in the iconic mask and the whispered rumor on a rain-soaked street. The expansion invites groups to explore how a hero’s myth originates and evolves under the pressure of city-scale conflicts, conspiracies, and personal choices.

### Potential Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
As with any expansion, there is a potential risk of over-dusing the new mechanics. If your group is prone to meta-linguistic analysis or if you tend to push for complex combinatorics in every scene, you could end up with more rules friction than narrative payoff. To avoid this, start with a single Mythos seed in a one-shot to feel out how the new elements influence your session rhythm. The included GM tips emphasize pacing and restraint, which is exactly what you want when you are trying to preserve the mood rather than bog everyone down in parsing.

### Quick-Start Guide for a Two-Session Arc
- Session 1: Introduce a mythic seed and allow players to declare Tags that reflect their emerging mythos connection.
- Scene: Use one of the starter seeds to generate a noir-mythic scene that ends with a tabletop reveal, not a cinematic smackdown.
- Session 2: Bring in the Mythic Thread to link the PCs, escalate tension with a clause of the seed’s influence, and close with a decision that reframes the city’s mythology for the players.
- Optional: integrate the Encounter Cards to streamline scene pacing and keep the focus on story beats rather than mechanics.
