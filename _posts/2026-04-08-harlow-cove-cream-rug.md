## Cleaning and Care
Cream rugs are not royalty; they are participants in daily life. They require routine attention, not a miracle, which is good because miracles are expensive and you probably spilled coffee again anyway. For day-to-day care, vacuum regularly with a vacuum that has a gentle suction setting and a brush attachment that won’t snag the fibers. A weekly or biweekly shakeout outside can help dislodge hidden debris that you can hear the quilting needles trying to stitch back into shape during the night. For spills, the first move is to blot, not rub, with a clean cloth. Rubbing tends to push the stain deeper into the fibers and makes the rug look like it has an uninvited tie-dye moment. Immediate blotting reduces the odds of lasting discoloration. Mild detergent mixed with water can help tackle stubborn marks, but test in an inconspicuous corner first to ensure there is no color shift. Avoid chlorine bleach and high heat, both of which can degrade the fibers and ruin the cream aesthetic you paid for.

For deeper maintenance, a professional cleaning every year or two can be a smart investment. If your life is a recurring episode of messy meals and pet antics, consider a rotating cleaning schedule that aligns with seasonal changes. The key is consistency rather than heroic cleaning stunts, because a rug that stays clean simply behaves like the good software update you actually want to install without drama.
