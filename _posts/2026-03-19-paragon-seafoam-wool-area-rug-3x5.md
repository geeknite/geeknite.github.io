## Rug Pad and Footing: Security and Longevity
Rug pads are the unsung heroes of rug setup. They prevent slips, soften footfalls, and extend the life of the rug by distributing weight more evenly. For hardwood or tile floors, a medium-density felt or rubber pad is a solid choice. If your space is carpeted, a thinner pad reduces the risk of the rug catching on furniture legs or folding awkwardly under daily movement.

- Slippage prevention: a good pad keeps sofas and chairs from creeping as you push them back into place after a gaming marathon or a party planning session.

## Maintenance: How to Keep Seafoam Beautiful Without Turning It Into a Science Experiment
Care for wool the smart way. Regular vacuuming is your best friend, ideally with the brush attachment on a low setting and moving with the pile direction to avoid fuzzing. Periodic rotation helps to reduce visible wear and color fading in heavy traffic zones. Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. For tougher stains, opt for a wool-safe cleaner and test in a hidden corner first to ensure no color drama erupts. Avoid harsh solvents that can damage the wool fibers.

- Vacuum weekly: keep the pile lively, avoid over-aggressive passes that pull at fibers.
- Rotate every few months: a little turn can make a big difference in wear patterns and color consistency.
- Address spills fast: the sooner you blot, the less chance a stain has to become a memory.
- Sun exposure: direct sun can fade color over time. If your rug sits in a sunny spot, consider window treatments or periodic rotation to keep seafoam vibrant.
