## Condition, pricing, and long-term value
Let’s be clear: 48-card raw singles are a niche fixation. They’re not for the casual buyer who wants a minty showroom display. They’re for people who enjoy the tactile ritual of handling older cards and who relish the detective work of price estimation based on condition grades you don’t physically own yet.

Condition matters more than most modern collectors admit. A corner ding can be a deal-breaker for some and a badge of honor for others who see the card as a veteran of many play sessions. Because these are raw singles, expect a broad spread of prices. Some cards may fetch a modest sum if they’re of rare characters or artworks that evoke peak 90s style. Others, more common or with art that’s widely duplicated across the era, will settle into affordable territory that still makes sense as a nostalgic indulgence.

From a long-term perspective, the value of raw singles often tracks the overall interest in the game’s historical footprint. If OverPower experiences a renaissance or a retro-collecting wave, the 48-card subset could become a gateway for new collectors to dip a toe into the scene without committing to a full set or a sealed box. Of course, the reverse is also true: if interest wanes, these cards become charming but practical curiosities rather than investments. That honesty is part of the charm—they’re imperfect little time capsules, not financial instruments.

### Pricing tips for the prospective buyer
- Compare condition grades informally: edge wear and corner dings are part of the story for raw singles.
- Look for the iconic characters and artstyle that scream 1990s Marvel supremacy; these tend to be more desirable.
- Consider how you’ll display the cards: binders, page protectors, and UV-safe sleeves can help preserve the look while you stare at them during long game nights.
- Don’t overlook the value of bundle deals: sometimes a multi-card listing yields more satisfaction (and more storytelling potential) than a single prized card.

## Display, storage, and the tangibility factor
The physicality of raw singles is what makes them uniquely satisfying in a digital age. The cardboard smell, the glossy finishes, and the weight of a 90s card in your hand: it’s a sensory trip you can’t replicate with a PDF printout of a checklist. If you’re keeping these 48 cards, you’ll want to consider:

- Proper sleeves and top-loaders to protect edges and corners without warping the card’s natural curvature.
- A sturdy binder with acid-free pages so the art remains vibrant for decades rather than fading into a memory of your youth.
- A dry, dark storage space to prevent yellowing or moisture damage—basic but essential for any physical collection.
- Clear labeling so you can quickly flip to your favorite character or villain without turning your living room into a chaotic card avalanche.
