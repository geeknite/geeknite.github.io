## How to Play in the Most Common Setup

The setup is straightforward: each player takes a small board or token track, shuffles a deck of emotion cards, and places fatigue tokens on the districts they plan to contest. The game then moves in rounds where players draft cards, deploy mood infused actions, and resolve conflicts by comparing the powers of the emotions on the table. The exact numbers depend on the edition, but the overall design uses a tempo-based bargaining style where you jockey for position, commit resources, and hope your opponent misreads the emotional weather of the room.

The turn structure usually looks like this: draw, draft, play, resolve, and refresh. You draw into a hand, you draft or retrofit the mood you want to play this turn, you spend your influence to push into a district, you resolve conflicts by comparing values on emotion cards, you adjust the board state, and you pass to the next player. The flow is clean, quick, and allows for significant strategic depth without requiring a rulebook to be your constant companion. The result is a game that can be played quickly to a satisfying conclusion or stretched into a longer session if your group wants a more epic mood story.

### Card types and synergy

Emotion cards form the backbone of Cerebria. They come in a few flavors:

- Core emotions that define the baseline interactions. They usually offer consistent value and are safer to play early but can lose their shine if you chase them too long.
- Tactical emotions that create strong effects but require careful timing to be credible. These are the kinds of cards that win you games if you use them right, but they punish misreads harshly.
- Hybrid cards that combine two moods into a single action and require you to chain plays for maximum effect. The payoff is worth the pol, but you must plan ahead to keep the chain alive.

The synergy between these cards creates a deck that is not just a stack of numbers. Each card carries a mood that changes how it interacts with other cards, the board, and your opponents. The emotional engine is where Cerebria shines, giving you a reason to keep playing even when luck is not on your side.
