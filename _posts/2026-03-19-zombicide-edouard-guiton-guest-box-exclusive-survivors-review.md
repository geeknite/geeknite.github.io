## Materials and Assembly
Zombicide plastic miniatures are generally easy to assemble, and these two survivors fit that mold. They come in multi part configurations that allow you to adjust poses without excessive glue drama. The bases are standard, so you can drop them into any mission map without needing extra conversion work. The miniature quality holds up under basic tabletop lighting and moderate table scuffs, which is a nice reassurance for players who only own a table that is a little weary after years of fantasy battles and kitchen experiments.

### Assembly tips
- Use a sharp hobby knife to snap off any mold lines; go slow and check edges for rough seams.
- A quick dunk in warm soapy water helps remove release agent before priming.
- Prime in light spray and build color gradually; avoid heavy layers that obscure details.
- If you want extra durability, base coat dark and dry brush lighter tones to emphasize texture without needing thick paint.

## Painting and Display Tips
If you enjoy a showpiece table, these two minis respond well to contrasting color schemes. Consider one wearing a bright accent color to pop on the board and the other in muted tones that blend with ruined urban backdrops. A little weathering on armor and a splash of blood-free gore on the edges can emphasize a survivor who has lived through more than a few close calls. Here are quick tips to get a strong tabletop result:

- Prime in gray or white for cleaner color application. 
- Use a mid tone as a base and build up highlights to bring out the sculpted edges.
- Dry brush metallics on any metal parts and apply a wash to bring out armor details.
- Seal with a matte finish to prevent chipping on busy play nights.
