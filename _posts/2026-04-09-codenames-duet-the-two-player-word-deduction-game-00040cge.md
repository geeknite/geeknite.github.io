## What is Codenames Duet? (From a Distance)
Codenames Duet is a cooperative word deduction game played by two people. The core mechanic mirrors the original Codenames: a clue giver offers a single one word clue accompanied by a number, signaling how many words on the board relate to that clue. The other person uses that hint to guess which words on the 5x5 grid are the target words. The twist here is collaboration. There is no opposing team to crush; there is only the clock, the possibility of misdirection, and the shared joy when a clue unlocks multiple correct words. There is also the looming risk that a guess lands on the assassin word, which ends the round with a dramatic thud and a chorus of groans that only puzzle lovers can truly appreciate.

Duet is designed with a two player rhythm in mind, but you can bring in a partner for a practice round, a quick round while your coffee cools, or a late night session with a friend who is too clever for their own good. The game provides a structure that keeps the two players in a dance rather than letting one person dominate the guesswork. That balance is the secret sauce here: you feel clever for the right reasons, not because you cheated by peeking at a cheat sheet. It is a game about language and memory and the jokes you make when you realize you and your partner are hearing the same clue in vastly different ways.

For the curious, a quick look at the official notes: Duet uses a 5x5 word grid, a deck of clue cards, a set of prediction tokens, and a single assassin marker. The objective is to reveal all target words by using cues that connect several of them in a single linguistic leap, all while avoiding traps. The experience scales with your duo dynamic; a pair that communicates well will glide through rounds with elegance, while a duo that thrives on sarcasm and misdirection will enjoy the ride just as much, as the game invites those moments of mutual misinterpretation that turn into inside jokes.

### The Board and Grid
The 5x5 grid is the heart of the game. Each word card sits on a single side, ready to be revealed or left in play. The layout is tidy, and the font choice is accessible, which matters when you are juggling memory and language under pressure. The 5x5 format balances abundance with focus: you have enough words to create satisfying linkages, but not so many that the board becomes a sprawling labyrinth you fear solving. The grid card is double‑sided, which subtly breathes a little extra life into replays by letting you shuffle arrangements and try new setups without collecting extra modules.

### The Clue Cards and Clues
The clue master uses a one word clue plus a number to signal how many grid words are related. The elegance here is in the restraint: you cannot throw out a clue that directly ties to every safe word; you must calibrate your hint to the actual board state and to your partner's memory. This mechanic rewards creativity and careful word choice. The clue cards also subtly enforce safety: clues that veer too close to the assassin or neutrals carry a weighty risk. Your best bets are clues that tie two or three target words together in a way that your partner is likely to recognize, a mental leap that feels earned rather than forced.

## How to Play
If you have played Codenames or any classic clue game, you will feel at home quickly. If you are new to this world, fear not: the entry barrier is low, and the payoffs are high in terms of shared laughs and eureka moments.

### The Clue Giver
As the clue giver, your job is to translate a mental map of the board into a single word and a number. The number indicates how many words on the grid relate to your clue. The trick is to avoid tipping off neutrals and to steer your partner toward the target words without guiding them into a trap. It is a delicate balance between being clever and being compassionate towards your partner, because a well chosen clue can unlock multiple words at once, while a poorly chosen one can crash the run in spectacular fashion.

### The Guessers
The guesser listens, weighs the relationships, and marks words on the grid accordingly. The process is social deduction in a notebook, with a splash of memory and a dash of cultural knowledge. Good guessing comes from aligning your partner’s mental map with yours, which often means trading jokes about shared experiences for precise word associations. The tension peaks when you land on a target word and you both share the tiny moment of victory that makes the rest of the game feel reachable.

### Turn Structure and Rounds
A round unfolds with the clue giver presenting a clue, then the guesser attempting to reveal words up to the number indicated. If a guess hits a target word, that card is revealed and you continue until you either run out of guesses or you hit a neutral or assassin word. Revealing a neutral word is a small sting; revealing the assassin ends the round immediately in a dramatic puff of suspense. The rounds continue until all target words are revealed or the clock and cards declare a mission failure. The pacing is smooth, with enough cadence to keep the session from stalling while still letting you savor the right moments of insight.

### Endgame and Victory Conditions
The victory in Duet is simple in theory and surprisingly satisfying in practice: reveal all target words before running out of safe guesses or hitting the assassin. The sense of accomplishment comes not just from discovering the words but from solving the language puzzle together. It feels like completing a two‑person crossword with a friend who finally gets that you were aiming for the same shared image all along.
