## Capacity, Organization and Sleeving
1000+ cards is a big number, and the real-world capacity depends on how you sleeve and organize. In standard sleeves, you can expect about 900 to 1100 cards, depending on how aggressively you stack the trays and how many double sleeves you slap onto your decks. The included dividers are adjustable and allow you to segment your collection by color, archetype, or mood — call it deck taxonomy, a noble science.

A practical note: if you go full double-sleeve on every deck, your capacity slides toward the lower end of that range. If you run a lean single-sleeve approach, you will likely crest toward the higher end. The trays are accessible, with a shallow depth that makes it easy to slide a card in and out without a loud prayer for mercy. The dividers feature a gentle lip to keep cards from wandering from tray to tray during a commute or table flip moment.

Sleeves matter here. If your life is a symphony of sleeving experiments, you know that sleeves interact with a container in unpredictable ways. The Lair accommodates thick sleeves and standard sleeves with comparable ease, though you will want to measure your preferred sleeves before assuming you can fill it to the brim with two dozen different deck types. The interior lining reduces static and fealty to the foil gods, helping to protect your unicorns, dragons, and foil legends from micro-scratches and the occasional dramatic shuffles that would drive a normal person to anxiety therapy.

Compatibility notes: this unit targets standard size TCG cards. Oversized promos or oversized promo cards may require a separate solution. Altered Ed New does not magically amplify capacity; it merely optimizes the layout so you can access your biggest hits quickly without performing a scavenger hunt.

A quick maintenance routine can keep your Lair looking fresh for years. Wipe the exterior with a soft microfiber cloth to remove fingerprints and dust.
