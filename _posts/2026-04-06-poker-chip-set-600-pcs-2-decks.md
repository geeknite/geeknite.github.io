## Maintenance and care
Keeping chips clean is easier than you’d think: a quick wipe with a damp cloth is usually enough to remove fingerprints or slight smudges. Avoid soaking them, as moisture can cause discoloration or swelling in some plastics, which could ruin the edge spots. For the cards, basic care applies: avoid bending, don’t leave them near a high-heat source, and consider a card shuffler if you’re spinning through hands at a furious pace.

The felt mat mid-table will collect crumbs in the creases, especially if you’re a snack-forward table host. A regular vacuum or a gentle brush will help keep it pristine. If a spill happens, you can lift the mat and clean the surface underneath. In a pinch, you can use a damp cloth and mild soap, but make sure it dries fully before you resume play—nobody wants slippery chips creating slippery logic errors during a critical bluff.
