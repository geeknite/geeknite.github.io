## Mechanics, Gameplay, and How It Integrates

Don’t expect a revolution here; expect a refinement. The two new survivors have abilities that emphasize risk-taking and strategic positioning. One might perform a risky action that yields extra loot or a vital card if you succeed, but punishes you with a tougher survival check if you fail. The other survivor excels in reconnaissance, helping your party anticipate hazards and plan safer routes through the board.

The two exclusive zombie types are designed to push you toward delegation and planning. They’ll encourage you to split your party to handle multiple threats or to fortify a single stronghold while the rest of the team handles the coming wave. It’s not a game-breaking shift; rather, it’s a nudge toward a slightly different tactical posture that can yield satisfying “aha!” moments when you pull off a clever trap or coordinate a multi-turn plan.

Scenario-wise, the box is designed to slot into your existing Zombicide campaigns with minimal friction. The missions scale with player count and can be added to your ongoing campaigns to refresh the feel of a long-running session. If you’re new to Zombicide, these missions provide a gentle entry point by introducing new mechanics gradually rather than tossing you into a completely new ruleset.
