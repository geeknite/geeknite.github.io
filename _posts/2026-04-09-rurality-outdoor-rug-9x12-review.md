Durability and Cleaning
-----------------------
Durability is the third act of our show. Plastic-based outdoor rugs tend to be resilient against sunlight and abrasion, but they can still show wear after massive foot traffic or dragging heavy furniture. The RURALITY rug typically features reinforced edges and a dense weave that helps it resist fraying. Cleaning is straightforward: remove loose debris with a broom or leaf blower, hose it down if it’s especially dirty, and let it air dry. Avoid high-heat drying since it’s plastic at heart, not a spa robe. If you’re dealing with stubborn stains, a mild soap solution and a light scrub with a soft-bristle brush will usually do the trick without dissolving your rug’s structural integrity. One pro tip: ensure it’s completely dry before rolling up or storing to prevent any mildew from joining the party uninvited.

Setup, Placement, and Maintenance Tips
---------------------------------------
- Roll out on a clean, dry surface to maximize the rug’s crease-free appearance. If you’re impatient, you can lay it over a patio that’s still damp, but don’t blame the rug when your furniture discovers a new wobble.
- Use rug grippers or non-slip pads if your patio gets a lot of foot traffic or if you’re hosting a dance-off under the stars. The last thing you want is a slippery surprise during a sweaty kitchen-to-outdoor transition.
- When cleaning, start with a broad sweep to remove dirt, then apply water for a rinse. For stubborn marks, stick to mild cleaners and a soft-bristle brush to avoid dulling the weave. Always air-dry completely before storing.
- Storing: if you’re seasonal and shrink-wrapping your outdoor space, roll the rug tightly and keep it in a dry, ventilated place. A humid closet might turn this hero into a reluctant roommate for mold, and nobody wants that.

Maintenance Routine: A Geeky, Yet Realistic Approach
---------------------------------------------------
- Quick daily: shake out debris, wipe spills as they happen, and run a quick sweep if leaves accumulate.

- Seasonally: wipe down, dry, and store; if you’re not storing, you’ll want to rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and sun exposure.
