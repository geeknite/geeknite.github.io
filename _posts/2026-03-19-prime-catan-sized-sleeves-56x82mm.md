## The 56 x 82 mm Standard: Why Size Actually Matters
### Why size matters in a game like Catan
Catan cards aren’t gigantic, but they aren’t tiny post-its either. They live somewhere in the Goldilocks zone where a standard sleeve still allows comfortable shuffling, edge protection, and easy picking when you need to swap a card mid-trade negotiation. If you opt for sleeves that are too small, you fight every shuffle. If you go too large, slides and slumps ruin the table vibe. Prime sleeves target the sweet spot: they hug the card with a snug fit that resists popping out during furious table flipping, yet they aren’t so tight that you’re forced to perform card-surgery every time you draw a card.

### Material matters: thickness and texture
Sleeve material comes in a spectrum from ultra-thin to battle-ready. Thickness is typically measured in mils or microns. Prime sleeves lean toward a middle ground that supports aromatic table talk and robust shuffling without turning your cards into a squeaky, warehouse-grade playlist. Matte finishes cut glare under strong ceiling lights and are kinder to the eyes during long, tense settlements, while glossy finishes make art pop and your shields shinier. Your mileage may vary depending on whether you favor cinematic glare control or show-stopping reflectivity during a dramatic dice off.
