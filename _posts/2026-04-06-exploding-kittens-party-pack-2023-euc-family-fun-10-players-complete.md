House rules and tips
---------------------

- Keep turns snappy. In large groups, a few seconds per turn adds up fast.
- Don’t let one player monopolize the action with a big chain. Rotate responsibilities or set a timer for each turn.
- Encourage participation. A big table is more fun when every person has a moment to react to a big play.
- Use house rules to reduce downtime. For example, allow a shared See the Future into the deck rather than each player peeking individually.

Strategy for large groups
-------------------------

Even in a purely chaotic game, you can practice mild strategy:

- Save a Defuse for the later rounds. It is the single card that can save your life and your bragging rights.
- Use Nope intentionally to disrupt a big plan. The right Nope can ruin someone’s carefully-laid plan and spark a cascade of reactions that is pure comedy.
- Develop loose alliances. Friendly bargaining, even if temporary, adds social stakes that elevate the experience.
- Don’t be afraid to intentionally misdirect. People love to guess what you are about to play, and misdirection creates laughter.
