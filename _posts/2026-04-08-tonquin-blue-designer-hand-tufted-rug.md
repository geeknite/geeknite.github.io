## The craft behind the comfort: tufting technique and durability

Hand tufting is a multi-step dance: a row of knots, a careful shaping of loops, and a finishing pass that secures the fibers with a sturdy backing. Tonquin Blue uses a robust tufting foundation and high-grade wool, which means you’re getting more texture per square inch and better resilience under foot traffic. The Tuft density is optimized to reduce shedding while delivering a plush feel—think walking on a soft carpeted cloud that has its own policy on leaving fuzz behind. 

Careful machine-like precision meets artisanal quirks. The result is an interior item that reads as handcrafted without requiring you to learn the difference between purl and garter stitches. The rug is designed to hold its shape under typical living-room traffic, so if your family includes a small canine with a questionable appetite for area rugs, you’re in safer territory than with a cheaper, machine-made alternative. 

For those curious about the science of fibers, wool fibers rebound when compressed, which helps the pile recover after foot traffic. This rebound reduces the appearance of permanent flattening in high-traffic zones and keeps the rug looking fresh longer. This doesn’t mean you can skip vacuuming, but it does mean you’ll probably vacuum less aggressively than you think you need to—your back will thank you.

## Size, placement, and layout considerations

Choosing the right size is never as exciting as choosing the rug’s color, but it matters. A rug that’s too small will “float” in the room awkwardly; one that’s too big can feel like the rug is hosting the furniture. A common rule of thumb for a standard sofa setup is to aim for a rug that fits under all four feet of the front legs of the sofa and at least the front legs of any coffee tables. For larger living rooms, a bold option is to place Tonquin Blue under the entire seating area to unify the space. For smaller rooms, a smaller rectangular or round version can still anchor the zone without overpowering it. Always measure your space, then add a few inches as a cushion for future rearrangements. 

### Room-by-room ideas
- Living room: anchor seating with Tonquin Blue under the sofa and coffee table, leaving at least 18–24 inches of bare floor between the rug edge and the walls to create a generous frame. 
- Bedroom: place the rug under the bed so that about 12–18 inches peek out on all sides; this gives you a soft landing when you climb out of bed. 
- Home office: use the rug under the desk to reduce echo and create a tactile boundary between work and rest of the room. 
- Kids’ play area: keep the rug where kids gather during playtime to add a warm surface for building blocks, with color that hides the occasional marker stain.

## Care, maintenance, and long-term durability

Caring for Tonquin Blue is not a ritual that requires a degree in fiber theology. A regular vacuum with a soft beater bar (or without the beater bar if you’re worried about fiber pull) is usually sufficient for weekly upkeep. Address spills promptly with blotting and a light dry-clean solvent if recommended by the manufacturer. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade wool fibers or alter the dye. Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear, especially if your layout has a high-traffic corridor. 

If you’re worried about shedding in the first weeks, a quick tip from rug nerds: vacuum slowly and frequently, shake out the excess if you’re moving it, and consider a professional cleaning every year or two if you want to preserve the pile’s loft. The goal is a balance between routine care and long-term beauty, not a ritual that requires a deadline and a sacrificial coffee mug.

## FAQs and practical tips

Q: Is Tonquin Blue suitable for underfloor heating? 
A: Yes, as long as the rug is rated for compatibility with your underfloor heating system and the fiber choice remains intact under heat. Always check the manufacturer’s guidance. 

Q: Can Tonquin Blue be used in a kitchen or dining area? 
A: It can, but you’ll want to consider accidental spills and heavier traffic. A lower pile height is easier to clean, and a backing with non-slip properties helps prevent slipping. 

Q: How does light exposure affect the color over time? 
A: Wool dyes tend to hold up well, but continuous, intense sunlight can cause subtle fading. Consider UV-resistant window treatments if your dining room basks in sun for hours each day.
