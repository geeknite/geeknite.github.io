## How to Play: A Gentle Refresh (Or a Neon Sprint If You’re Hungry)
If you know how to play Sushi Go!, you know most of what you need here: draft, collect, score. If you’re new, here’s a brisk refresher that focuses on the deluxe flavor rather than reinventing the wheel.

### The Core Mechanic: Draft and Resolve
Each round, a hand of five cards is passed around the table. You select one card to keep, and pass the rest to the left. You repeat this process for a predetermined number of rounds, usually around eight to ten in total for the standard game. When you take a card, you place it in front of you to form your personal sushi menu. The trick is to balance the cards you want now with the cards your neighbors might pass next, forcing a little strategic guessing and a lot of quick glances at what others are likely collecting.

### Scoring: The Tasty Bits That Matter
Scores come from a few familiar sushi combos:
- Nigiri: Each nigiri card is worth a fixed point value; the more you collect, the better you do, but you’re competing with your friends for those runs.
- Maki rolls: The largest set of Maki cards earns bonus points at the end of the round. This is the classic head-to-head tug-of-war—who will grab those wrappers first and risk the other types?
- Tempura and Sashimi: These often require pairs or triples to score, rewarding players who plan a little ahead rather than grabbing calories willy-nilly.
- Puddings (sometimes included in the Deluxe editions as a tie-break or endgame reward): They often swing endgame points when you’ve tallied everything else.

The deluxe variant may add a few new card categories or tweak point values slightly, but the essential math remains approachable. The moment you accidentally draft a card you’ll never use, you realize why the game rewards careful drafting and seat-location awareness. You’ll also understand why your friends insist on designing “drafting v2.0” variants when they lose a single round by a single point.

### Game Pace and Player Interaction
Sushi Go! thrives on short rounds and quick turns. A typical round lasts less than a minute per player, with the whole session sprinting along in under 30 minutes for a five-player game. The interaction is mostly indirect: you’re watching who’s collecting which types and then pivoting your strategy to deny them or to pounce on a new opportunity that has just opened up. The Deluxe Edition improves the tactile and visual cues—scoring is easier to track, and the card stock feels more authoritative in hand—making the pace even more satisfying. This isn’t a heavy strategy game; it’s a fast, cheerful exercise in pattern recognition and risk management, like a micro-optimised speed-dating event for your brain.

## Strategy Tips for the Deluxified Digest
No matter the edition, a few universal tips help you maximize your score in Sushi Go! Deluxe Edition:
- Early game focus: In the first few rounds, try to draft a balanced mix of categories that give you multiple scoring opportunities. This lowers risk if your neighbors start to target one type aggressively.
- Track your neighbors: If you notice someone chasing a specific type, pivot toward a complementary set that denies them bonus points and still yields you positive value.
- Plan for pairs and triples: If a card set requires pairs or triples (like tempura or sashimi variants), hold onto a couple of cards to secure the big scoring payoff later rounds.
- Don’t over-commit to a single type: It’s tempting to chase a big set, but flexibility is your friend. If the board shifts, you’ll regret having locked into one path.
- Use the deluxe extras strategically: The extra card variants and improved scoring visuals help you see opportunities more clearly. Pay attention to how those upgraded elements align with your current hand and the likely passes around the table.
