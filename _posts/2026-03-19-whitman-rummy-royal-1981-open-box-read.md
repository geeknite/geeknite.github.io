## Care, preservation, and practical tips
### How to maintain open-box vintage games
- Keep them away from direct sunlight to avoid fade on boxes and rule sheets.
- Store in a cool, dry environment; humidity can compromise cards and paper.
- If you want to display the interior, consider a simple acrylic display case that protects while still letting the components be visible.
- For the card deck, sleeves are optional; in many cases they are unnecessary for play, but if you’re planning long-term display, sleeves can help preserve card faces.
- Gently dust with a microfiber cloth and avoid chemical cleaners that could react with paper stock.

### Simple restoration ideas that don’t kill value
- If corners are creased, consider corner protectors internally to slow further damage.
- If the rule sheet is crinkled, scan it and keep a digital copy; print a clean, store-bought copy if you want a readable, useable version without risking the original.
- If the box is scuffed, consider a light refurbishment with archival-grade tape or a professional restoration service—though this can affect value, so talk to a pro before you proceed.

Preservation isn’t glamorous, but it pays in the long term. The joy of owning a vintage Whitman item is not just in playing the game; it’s in knowing that your copy has weathered time with grace and that you, as curator, are keeping its story alive.
