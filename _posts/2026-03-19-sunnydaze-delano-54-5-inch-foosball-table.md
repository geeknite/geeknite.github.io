## Setup tips and quick-start guide

- Clear a solid, level surface. The worst enemy of a good foosball game is a slanted playing field that morphs into a chaotic vertical battlefield.
- Have a friend help you with the assembly. Two hands are better than one, especially when aligning legs and ensuring the rods slide smoothly.
- Don’t over-tighten cam locks. A little give is good for movement; too-tight means you’ll end up with a table that won’t hold alignment for more than two passes.
- Calibrate your goals before you start playing. It’s nice to have eye-hand coordination, but it’s even nicer to know what you’re aiming for when you’re aiming for the top corner.
- Regular maintenance is your friend. A clean surface and occasional rod lubrication keep the drama at bay and your victory dances less likely to jolt the neighbors awake at 3 AM.
