## How to play this edition: a quick-start guide

The core gameplay of Monopoly remains intact, but several details have been retuned to fit Fortnite theming. Here’s a practical starter guide to get you from “what the heck are we doing?” to “oh wow, I actually want to play again.”

### Setup and first turns
- Place the game board in the center. Shuffle the Battle Pass and Loot Llama cards, and deal a starting stash of V-Bucks to each player.
- Each player picks one of the Fortnite-themed tokens and places it on the GO-like space on the board.
- Roll to determine who goes first. The highest roll starts the first round, and the game proceeds clockwise.
- On your turn, you may roll the dice and move. If you land on an unowned property, you may purchase it for the listed price. If you skip purchasing, the property goes to auction, just like classic Monopoly, with a Fortnite twist: you’ll often find yourself bargaining over locations you barely recognize from the in-game map.
- If you land on a property owned by another player, you pay rent according to the rent table for that property, one of the many moments where you realize your in-game skills don’t translate into real-world capital.
- If you land on Battle Pass or Loot Llama cards, you draw a card and follow its instruction. Expect a mix of “collect extra cash,” “pay taxes,” or “exchange currencies.” It’s chaotic, yes, but the chaos is part of the fun.

### Building and rent escalation
- As you acquire entire property sets (Fortress clusters, if you will), you can build Fortress houses and Fortress hotels to increase rent. The rules are friendly, but the effects on the game’s pacing can be dramatic. The more you own, the more you want to block others, which is basically the game’s core geopolitical dream.
- Special Fortnite flavor cards occasionally alter rent or grant a one-time bonus. It’s not always perfectly balanced, but balance isn’t the goal here; entertainment is.

### Banking, trading, and endgame
- You’ll need a capable banker. In households where someone’s phone has a bank app open all the time, this role can be surprisingly fun as you watch a real person try to keep track of “real money” and “in-game currency” without breaking into a wallet-sized mini meltdown.
- Trading is encouraged and often the best route to victory. Pro tip: over-trading early can lead to a glorious comeback narrative or a dramatic last-minute collapse. This is Monopoly, after all; the drama writes itself.
- The game ends when one player goes bankrupt, or when everyone has decided they’ve had enough of the chaos and agrees to a draw. It’s a flexible finish for a flexible game night.
