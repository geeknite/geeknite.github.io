## Weather, Maintenance, and Longevity: Keeping Your Pinewood Proud
Outdoor furniture earns its keep by surviving the weather and not looking bedraggled after a season. Here are practical tips to maximize longevity:
- Treat the wood: Apply a sealant or outdoor varnish at least once a year to prevent moisture penetration and wood swelling. A small “home improvement quest” that pays off in fewer creaks and cracks.
- Cushions care: Remove covers for washing per manufacturer instructions. Store cushions indoors during heavy rain or winter to protect the fabric from mildew and sun damage.
- Cleaning: Wipe with a mild soap solution for the wood. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the finish. A quick wipe-down every few weeks keeps dirt and pollen from embedding itself into the grain.
- Coverage: When not in use, keep the set under a cover or in a shaded area to prolong the wood and fabric life. If you have a storage shed or garage, that’s the fancy superhero hideout for your furniture.

In regions with heavy winters, a seasonal ritual of inspecting joints, tightening screws, and reapplying sealant will help you avoid the rattly-snake syndrome when you sit down after a long storage period. The hardware is usually corrosion-resistant, but a little preventative care goes a long way.
