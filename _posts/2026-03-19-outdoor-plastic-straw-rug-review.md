## Weather resistance and care
### Rain, mud, and sun
The plastic fibers shrug off rain and mud without absorbing moisture, which means less chance of mold and a quicker dry time. UV exposure won’t instantly turn it into a brittle mess, but prolonged, direct sun will gradually fade color and soften certain frays. Rotate sides over time to minimize even fading or consider bringing it indoors during the harshest sun months if you’re going for longevity.

### Cleaning basics
- Quick clean: hose it down, scrub with a mild detergent if needed, and hang to dry.
- Tough stains: a soft brush and a bit of dish soap can lift greasy spots without harming the fibers.
- Storage: roll it up when not in use to prevent creasing and extend life. If you’re storing for the off-season, keep it in a dry spot away from sharp objects that could snag the fibers.

## How to choose the right outdoor rug for you
### Size matters
Measure the space and give yourself a little breathing room on all sides for a framed look. If you’re placing under a dining set, ensure there’s enough room for chairs to slide out without catching the edges.
### Color and pattern
Choose a color that either blends with your existing furniture or provides a pop for your outdoor area. The reversible design is a bonus when you want two looks in one season.
### Material trade-offs
Plastic straw-like fibers are durable and easy to clean, but they’re not as soft as textile rugs. If you want barefoot luxury, pair it with a memory-foam outdoor mat pad or alternate with a softer indoor rug for lounging zones.
### Weight and portability
If you’ll move the rug around for gatherings, lighter is better. For wind-prone spaces, consider heavier underlays or outdoor rug grippers to keep it put.

## Care tips for longer life
- Regularly sweep or shake off debris to prevent buildup in the weave.
- Clean with mild soap solution and rinse thoroughly.
- Allow full air-dry before rolling or storing.
- Rotate sides annually to keep color even and prevent excessive wear on one face.
