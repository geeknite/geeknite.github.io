- Easy assembly: The four-pack is a puzzle, but a solvable one. If you can assemble a coffee table from an IKEA manual, you can assemble these desks with more structural confidence and less existential dread.

### The 6’ x 2’ dimension in practice
In practice, the 2-foot depth is a sweet spot: enough room for a keyboard, mouse, and a neatly arranged notebook; not so large that you need a forklift to reposition the desk when the office layout changes. ### Locking drawers and security
Locked drawers in a desk environment are not a security fortress, but they offer reasonable peace of mind. In a shared office, employees store laptops, small personal gadgets, and important documents in drawers with a simple tumbler lock. The draw configuration matters: if one desk has two drawers with locks and the other two have a single lockable file, you can design a feel of controlled access across the team. Some tests reveal that keys can be a small, extra thing to misplace, join a minor scavenger hunt, and eventually reappear in the most improbable places—like under a plant pot or inside a monitor stand.

### Assembly and setup
Here are practical steps for assembling the Hon 4-pack L-shaped desks with partitions:
- Clear a wide space: The four desks will require some floor real estate during assembly.
- Identify hardware bags: There are multiple small bags with screws; keep them organized by size.
- Attach legs and frames: Start with the main legs, then secure the frames to prevent wobble.
- Install partitions: Partitions mount on rails; ensure they align with the desk surface for stability.
- Secure locking drawers: Install the lock hardware following the manual; test the locks.
- Cable management: Thread power strips through the channels for clean lines.
