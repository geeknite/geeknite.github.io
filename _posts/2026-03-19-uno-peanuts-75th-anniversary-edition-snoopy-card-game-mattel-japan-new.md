## Rules and Gameplay: A Cheerful Return to Form

UNO is a simple game that rewards color recognition, memory (a little bit), and the willingness to roast your friends for their terrible color choices. The Pee-nuts edition remains faithful to the core mechanic: you want to be the first player to discard all your cards. A player can play a card if it matches the color or the number or the symbol of the top card on the discard pile. If you can’t play, you draw a card. The first to color clear wins the round.

What changes in the Snoopy edition is the presentation of the action cards and some decorative twists that keep the energy high without complicating the math. The wild cards occasionally carry Snoopy themed prompts that might trigger a side quest or a playful group vote, but nothing so elaborate that you need a PhD in Peanuts studies to interpret it. The overall flow remains familiar: you want to call UNO and avoid getting stuck with that ominous Draw Four when your hand has one good card left and you’re counting on a color shift to save you.

For players who want to anchor their understanding in a familiar foundation, here are the core steps you’ll be executing on any given turn:

- If you have a card that matches either color or number (or symbol), you play it.
- You can use a Wild to change colors or the Wild Draw Four to force the next player to pick up four cards (and lose their moment of glory if they were about to declare UNO).
- The game continues until someone empties their hand.

The Snoopy edition adds a few twists, mostly as flavor. Expect some cards that incorporate Snoopy’s world in a gentle way—enough to elicit a smile but not enough to derail the game’s core tempo. The effect is a lighter, more family-friendly vibe that still retains the competitive spark when the table tightens toward the endgame.
