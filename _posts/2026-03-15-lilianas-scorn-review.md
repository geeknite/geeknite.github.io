## What Liliana's Scorn Actually Is
Liliana's Scorn is a black spell from Core Set 2021 that gives you two usable options on a single card. I won’t pretend to regurgitate the exact word-for-word Oracle text here (because you can read it on the card or in your deck builder), but the essence is: a cheap, flexible instant that helps you deal with a threat now or a threat lurking in the graveyard later.

The “two-for-one” nature of the card is the bread and butter of black removal in this era of MTG. You can pressure a creature now, or you can threaten graveyard disruption later, depending on what the board needs. The vibe is: I have a card that can either answer a creature or answer something in a graveyard—your choice, your tempo, your fate. In practice, that means you’re rarely stuck with a card that’s strictly dead in certain matchups. That kind of flexibility is a big deal when the days of “one role per spell” feel ancient and quaint.

## Mechanics and Flavor: The Card in Your Deck, Not in Your Hand Waving at You
### How the two modes play out
The card’s two options give you distinct lines of play:
- Mode A often serves as a removal or answer to a creature, helping you stabilize the board.
- Mode B leans into graveyard interaction, which is a fun and increasingly important theme in MTG, especially with sets that flirt with reanimation, delve, or graveyard shenanigans.

The decision point is crucial. Do you need to trade with their big threat right now, or do you want to set up graveyard hate for a later turn? The answer usually depends on the life total, the board state, and your long-term plan. If you’ve ever played a game where your opponent’s graveyard plan threatens to snowball and you wish you had an answer you could still cast in a single mana, Liliana's Scorn is basically your spirit animal.

### Flavor: Liliana’s presence on the card
The name and flavor text tie the spell to the iconic planeswalker Liliana Vess. Black has always been about choices, secrets, and the art of making an enemy overthink their life decisions. Liliana’s Scorn embraces that ethos: a scornful, clever spell that punishes greedy boards while hinting at the darker corners of necromantic strategy. If you enjoy the mood of dark salons of magic, this card will give you a little extra swagger every time you cast it.

## Limited Play: Drafting and Sealed Scenarios
In limited formats, Liliana's Scorn shines when you build around flexibility. A two-mode spell gives you a safety net in many color-fixated drafts. Here are the dos and don’ts:

- Do value the correct mode for the current board: if there’s a deadly board stall, take the removal route. If the opponent’s graveyard is a ticking time bomb, the graveyard option becomes a late-game savings account.
- Don’t overvalue the graveyard mode in a format overloaded with graveyard-based strategies. If you’re up against aggro or do-nothing control builds, your graveyard targets might never matter, and you’ll be left staring at a card you can’t cast without dying before you get there.
- Do consider mana stability. In a three-color deck, a sorcery-speed answer is less valuable than a flexible instant that can be cast on your opponent’s end step when you’re tapped out. Liliana's Scorn fills that role nicely.

Deck-building tip for limited: pair Liliana's Scorn with creatures that can survive a removal spell or with a curve that allows you to pressure the opponent while also threatening to disrupt their graveyard on the very next turn. If you find yourself in a late-game stall, Scorn can turn the tides when combined with cheap removal or evasion creatures.

## Constructed Play: Where to Slot This Card
In constructed formats, Liliana's Scorn is not always a slam-dunk, but it’s a versatile puzzle piece. Its success depends on your color identity, your other removals, and your graveyard strategy. Here are common takeaways:

- Black midrange and control shells love a flexible answer. If you’re playing a deck that leans into threat-light, removal-heavy lines, Scorn acts as a catch-all piece that can answer early aggro or late-game graveyard threats.
- Graveyard-hate oriented decks benefit from the graveyard-targeting mode more often than not. If your meta features a lot of reanimator builds or graveyard recursion strategies, Liliana's Scorn becomes a proactive discard-and-exile tool rather than a reactive one.
- In fast formats where your life total matters, the removal mode is the safer pick. It buys a tempo win if you land it on the right turn and keeps you in the driver's seat when your opponent is pressuring you with efficient creatures.

If you’re brewing, consider how Scorn fits with cards that enable you to lean on your own graveyard as a resource. Cards with delve, graveyard recursion, or self-macabre synergy can create interesting two-card combos where Scorn acts as the catalyst to grind you into a salty victory.

It’s also a reminder that modern MTG design often rewards players who think a few turns ahead and plan around multiple possible futures rather than locking into a single line.

## Strategies and Build Ideas: How to Use Liliana's Scorn Best
Here are some practical build ideas and play patterns to consider:
- Mono-black aggro-control hybrids: Add Liliana's Scorn to your list of affordable removal spells while staying lean on threats. This creates a resilient plan that can respond to early aggression and still threaten a late-game preservation plan.
- Graveyard-aware decks: If your meta is full of graveyard-based strategies, Lean into the graveyard mode more heavily. The ability to hit graveyards at instant speed can derail opponents who assume their engine is safe there.
- Midrange synergy: A midrange shell can benefit from Scorn as a flexible card that helps you find the right line for the moment. Its two modes let you adapt to the state of the game without over-committing to a single plan.

If you’re serious about building around this card, consider partnering with other spells that either accelerate or slow down the battlefield. Cards that stabilize early, or cards that punish reanimation or graveyard recursion, can create a deck that uses Scorn as a hinge piece for a wider net of interaction.
