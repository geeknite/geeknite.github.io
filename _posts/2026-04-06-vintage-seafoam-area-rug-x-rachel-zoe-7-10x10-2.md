## Performance: Durability, Maintenance, and Comfort
### Everyday Durability
If your life includes kids, pets, and the occasional art-project disaster, you’ll appreciate the rug’s ability to resist obvious wear. The fibers are designed to bounce back after foot traffic; the pattern maintains its integrity without looking worn after a few months of daily living. That said, no rug is invincible. If you have a heavy-exposure area (think kid-friendly play zone or a high-traffic entry), a quick rotation schedule and a yearly professional cleaning can extend its life and keep the tonal balance intact.

### Cleaning and Care
Spills happen. Your best friend here is prompt action: blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth, then treat according to the fiber content guidance from the manufacturer. Most spills respond well to a gentle detergent solution. Avoid harsh chemicals that could alter the color or texture. A weekly light vacuum, with a beater bar set to a gentle setting or a hand-vac for delicate areas, keeps the pile looking fresh and the surface free of crumbs—an important feature when you have a weekly reminder that you should exercise and you haven’t yet.

### Rug-Proofing Tips
- Use a non-slip rug pad for stability and extended life.
- Consider rotating the rug seasonally to promote even wear.
- Protect high-traffic zones with furniture coasters or felt pads.
- If you have pets, keep an eye on litter and hair; the colorway helps hide minor fur shedding but a quick weekly wipe-down helps maintain its pristine look.
