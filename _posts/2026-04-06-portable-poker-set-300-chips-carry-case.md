### Chips: weight, feel, and denominations
The 300 chips usually come in a tri-color layout, often with a mix of 1, 5, 25, and 100 denomination chips. Common color conventions lean toward red for 5, white for 1, blue for 25, green for 100, and sometimes black for the high rollers at 500 or 1000—depending on whether the set is designed for low-stakes family nights or backyard tournaments with side bets that require a legal pad for tracking. The make of chips tends to be ABS plastic for cost effectiveness, but a decent set uses a slightly thicker thickness that yields a satisfying “thunk” when you drop them on a table. That sound is the gaming equivalent of a victory drumline; it makes you feel like you actually scored a critical hit in a video game. If you are a chip aficionado who eye-balls the weight per chip to gauge how much you should tip a dealer, you may be surprised how close you can get to casino-like heft in a compact kit. Expect a chip diameter around 39 to 40 mm, a standard in many sets, with a balance of smooth edges and a firm edge-snap when you push a stack together for a quick stake.

Being able to stack 300 chips in a carry-friendly box means the set probably uses a simple but effective chip rack or tray inside the case. A typical portable set might come with a standard deck of poker-sized playing cards, a couple of spare jokers, and a small deck protector or two. ## Travel, storage, and maintenance
Storage and maintenance are not sexy topics, but they are essential. A well-kept portable set lasts longer and looks better in photos. Here are some tips to keep that set looking sharp.

- Store chips and cards in their dedicated slots. Do not cram everything into the case so tight that you end up fighting with the lid to close. The best travel is one that does not require a wrestling match with a box lid.
- Avoid direct sunlight for long periods. UV rays can fade chip colors and dry out leather or fabrics if you have a leather strap or soft padding. If you are going to leave the case in a car on a sunny day, consider a compact sun shade that keeps the interior from becoming a miniature oven of regret.
- Keep the chips away from moisture. A small silica packet can help, but ensure it does not become a choking hazard to curious players who love to snack on tiny packet crinkles mid-game.
- Perform a quick weekly check on the latches and hinges. If they feel loose, a tiny drop of lubricant on the hinge can keep them from squeaking under the pressure of a dramatic all-in bluff that goes on for five minutes longer than it should.
- Replacements are a reality. Chips wear down after extended use, and the case may accumulate scuffs. Track the wear and consider replacement chips or a new case if the wear starts to affect the playing experience. A well maintained kit remains a joy to play with and helps you maintain your poker night legend status without the catastrophe of a broken buckle.
