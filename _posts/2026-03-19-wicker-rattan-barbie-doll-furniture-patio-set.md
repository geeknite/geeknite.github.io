## The Set in Context
### Vintage Vibes: When Wicker Was the It Material
Wicker and rattan have a long historical run as the upgrade from “toys made of whatever local wood we could afford.” In 1:6 scale, wicker sets evoke that mid-century sunroom dream that makes you wish you could sip tea with your pinky raised—except your pinky is a Barbie-sized pinky and the teapot is so tiny you could fit it inside a thimble. The charm of wicker lies in its texture: weave patterns that catch the light in a way that plastic simply cannot. The trade-off? Wicker and rattan can be fragile, especially in a miniaturized form where joints and joins are more joints and less metal. The set we’re discussing today captures that catch: a weave dense enough to feel real, yet relaxed enough to handle with the delicate care a collector would give a vintage comic book from the same era.

## Care, Maintenance, and Restoration Tips
### How to Keep the Weave from Fading, and Other Tiny Triumphs
- Dusting: Use a soft brush to avoid snagging the weave or disturbing the parasol’s fabric.
- Cleaning wicker: A gentle wipe with a damp microfiber cloth, followed by air-drying, helps prevent mildew on the wicker. Do not soak it; wicker hates soaked patience.
- Tea set care: If the porcelain-like pieces get sticky, wash them with mild dish soap and water, then pat dry with a lint-free microfiber. Avoid harsh solvents that could crack or discolor the tiny finish.
- Parasol care: If the fabric shows wear, a tiny brush can help remove dust. If color has faded, consider a light, non-permanent fabric dye applied with caution. Remember, tiny things take tiny risks.
- Storage: Keep the set away from direct sunlight to preserve color, and store it in a cushioned box to avoid warping or bending the parasol’s frame.

### Common Issues and Quick Fixes
- Loose parts: A dab of clear super glue can solve a wobble in the table leg or join. Use sparingly and test on a hidden area first.
- Warped parasol frame: If the frame is bent, very gentle adjustment with needle-nose pliers can straighten it. Do not force it—tiny breakage is permanent and sad.
- Faded color: For collectors, patina is part of the story. If you can live with it, leave as-is. If not, a light protective spray designed for miniatures can help slow further fading.
