## Setup and rules
Setting up is refreshingly breezy. Shuffle the main deck, deal five cards to each player (or adjust for your group size), and place the draw pile within easy reach. The core mechanic mirrors Go Fish: players ask for specific cards from opponents to complete sets. The twist is the infusion of dare and confession prompts that can swing the current player’s advantage in moments, not minutes.

Key rule shifts you’ll encounter:
- Prompt cards can require a quick performance, a small truth, or a playful action that keeps energy high.
- If you call for a card that your opponent does not have, you draw a card from the deck and might trigger a light penalty—think mild social consequence rather than a crushing blow.
- Team play is optional; you can compete as individuals or pair up for a cooperative twist on the standard flow.

House-rule possibilities abound. Here are a few you might consider:
- Mild mode: replace explicit prompts with G-rated equivalents.
- Double-dare: allow two dares per round for extra chaos.
- Prompt swap: participants can swap a prompt card with a neutral prompt to keep things fair.

The design respects pace. You won’t need a lecturer or a lawyer to understand the flow, and you won’t be stuck staring at a long manual either. It’s a game-night accelerator, not a legal brief.
