- Shuffle the core deck and deal a hand to each player (the number of cards per player is outlined in the rule sheet, but you’ll typically start with 7).
- Place the draw pile in the center and flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Decide on a requested mode (standard UNO-like play or the two alternate modes included in the booklet).
- Set any scoring tokens in reach of every player. The gold standard is “play to 50” or your preferred target distance for a competitive-but-fun night.

### Core rules (Uno-like with a Schitt''s Creek flavor)
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the top card on the discard pile.
- Special cards unleash either a beneficial twist or a playful calamity. The exact effects vary by mode—make sure to consult the included cheat sheet so no one argues with a card that clearly says, “Sass it up.”
- If you cannot play, you draw a card from the deck. If that card can be played immediately, you may do so; otherwise your turn passes.
- When you’re down to one card, you must shout “Schitt’s out!” (or ignore the rule if you’re playing in a particularly cautious household). The rules caution: you’re allowed to mock your own last-card anxiety, but keep it good-natured, folks.
- The round ends when a player empties their hand. Score is tallied using the tokens or the score sheet. The exact scoring method depends on the mode, but you’ll find a few variations in the booklet for variety across play sessions.
