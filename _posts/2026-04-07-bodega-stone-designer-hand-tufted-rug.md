## Tufting technique explained
What exactly is hand tufting? It is a method where yarn is inserted through a backing fabric by a needle to create loops or cut pile. The result is a dense, plush surface that feels responsive yet sturdy. Hand tufted rugs typically take less time than hand knotted ones, which makes them more affordable while still feeling premium. A note for the curious geek: the process shares DNA with 3D printing in the sense that a design is translated into layers of material, albeit with yarn rather than molten plastic. For die-hard craft watchers, you can explore more on Instructables or design articles about tufting.
