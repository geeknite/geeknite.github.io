## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping Nero in Peak Form
Natural fibers require a balance between elegance and practicality. The Nero is no exception. Here’s a methodical approach to keeping it in tip-top shape.

### Daily and Weekly Routine
- Vacuum with a soft brush attachment at least once a week to prevent particulate buildup.
- If you have a rolling desk chair or heavy furniture on top, consider a hard-backed mat under those zones to minimize indentation.
- Rotate the rug twice a year to ensure even wear, especially if sunlight hits one corner more than others.
- Brush or shake out the rug outdoors if you can, to dislodge loose debris before bringing it back inside.

### Stain Management
- For liquid spills, blot immediately with a clean, dry cloth—don’t rub, or you’ll push the liquid deeper into the fibers.
- Use a mild detergent mixed with water to spot-clean, then blot again to remove excess moisture. Avoid harsh chemicals that can discolor natural fibers.
- For tougher stains, consider a professional cleaning service familiar with natural fiber rugs. If you’re a DIY nerd, test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous corner first.

### High-Traffic Scenarios
If your living room doubles as a gaming station and a snack arena, you’ll want to be vigilant about crumbs and oils. The Nero’s dark color helps mask typical grime, but it’s not a superhero cape. A weekly vacuum is your best friend, and a quick brush-down after long marathons will keep the surface looking coherent rather than “I slept in a cave.”

## Sizing, Placement, and Styling Tips
One of the most fun (and nerdy) parts of rug shopping is the math of fit. Rugs can anchor a room, define a seating area, or softly delineate zones within an open-plan space. The Nero rug comes in a handful of standard sizes, but the principles remain the same.

### Size Matters, But Not Like You Think
- 5x8 feet: Great for small living rooms, under a sofa and coffee table combo, or a work desk zone.
- 6x9 feet: The classic middle-ground size that supports a larger seating area without overpowering the space.
- 8x10 feet: If you want a generous underfoot sweep that can hold the furniture group in one plane, this is your move.

When planning, remember to leave about 18–24 inches of exposed floor around the rug to create a floating, runway-like effect that makes the room feel larger. If you’re a gamer with a desk setup in a corner, consider a runner rug along your desk path to minimize noise and provide a consistent feel underfoot as you swap between keyboard and controller.

### Placement and Layering

- Under furniture: Place the front legs of sofas and chairs on the rug to anchor the seating area. This creates a unified space and prevents the furniture from feeling like it’s floating in a void.
- In front of the bed: A sizeable rug at the foot of the bed adds warmth and a “soft landing” when you wake up from a raid boss defeat at 3 AM.
