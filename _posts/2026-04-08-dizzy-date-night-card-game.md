## How to set up and start playing

Setting up is painless and designed to be almost instantaneous, which is exactly what you want on a date night when your intention is to avoid arguments about who left the dishwasher open for the hundredth time. Here is a quick setup guide:

1) Clear a comfortable surface where both players can sit close enough to whisper and laugh without feeling exposed. A couch, a bed with ample pillows, or a picnic blanket on the floor all work well.
2) Shuffle both decks separately and place them face down within easy reach. The Dizzy deck stays separate from the standard prompt deck, at least for the initial rounds.
3) Decide who goes first. The spinner or token can help, or you can just flip a coin. If you are feeling dramatic, flip the coin while wearing a silly hat; the internet approves silly hats.
4) Establish a drink policy that works for both of you. The game encourages drinking, but we slap on a gentle reminder here: know your limits, hydrate, and if anyone wants to pause, you pause. No one should feel pressured to overdo it in the name of romance.
5) Place the prompt deck face down and keep the Dizzy deck within reach. You are ready to play.

During a turn, the active player draws a prompt card and reads it aloud. The prompt might require the other player to share a memory, reveal a preference, or perform a light, playful action. The key word is playful; this means no forced discomfort, and any prompt that lands in a cramped zone should be modified or skipped if necessary. The Dizzy deck adds a twist: when a Dizzy card is drawn, the current round gets a shake-up, which could be a rapid-fire round of questions, a quick challenge that must be completed within a time limit, or a small penalty that leads to a sip from the designated drinks, again all with consent and safety in mind.

The pace of the game should feel like a date-night remix — a blend of sweet moments and goofy detours. If you are not in the mood for heavy banter, you can turn on a more relaxed mode of play by skipping the Dizzy cards or by setting a limit on the number of drinks per round. The point is to enjoy the moment together, not to turn a cozy evening into a test of endurance.
