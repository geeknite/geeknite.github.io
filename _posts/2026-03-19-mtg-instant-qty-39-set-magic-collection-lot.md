## Sorting, sleeving, and storage: practical guidance for real-life use

If you’re going to play with or trade these, you’ll want a sane process. Here’s a straightforward approach:

1) Separate playable from likely-trade and likely-store-in-a-binder cards. A simple gauge: does the card offer immediate utility in at least one deck you own or plan to build?
2) Sleeve everything. Even if it’s not pristine, protection saves your investment and future trade leverage.
3) Sort by rarity, set, and color. You’ll quickly discover patterns: some sets produce a lot of similar effects; others are wild. Sorting by color helps you assemble theme-based partial decks faster.
4) Create a quick inventory. A simple spreadsheet with columns for Card Name, Set, Rarity, Condition, and Potential Use can save you countless headaches later.
5) Trade with intent. Don’t just dump entire piles on your local group. Propose specific swaps and be honest about condition and expectations.

Storage tricks: use labeled boxes or dividers to separate burn, utility, and counterspells. A magnetized or zip-top binder with dividers can make it easy to flip through cards at game night without turning your kitchen into a landfill of tiny cardboard rectangles.
