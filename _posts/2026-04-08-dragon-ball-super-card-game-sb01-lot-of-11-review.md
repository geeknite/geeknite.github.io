## What SB01 Lot Of 11 Is and Isn’t
SB01, short for Starter Box 01, is a set that has appealed to new players and collectors alike. When someone lists a lot as SB01 Lot Of 11, they typically mean you will receive 11 cards plucked from the SB01 pool — not an official prebuilt deck, not a guaranteed foil festival, and not a treasure map to the perfect meta deck. If you are expecting 11 cards that make your opponents quiver on sight, you might be in for a cultural disappointment; if you are hoping for a quick entry into the DBS CCG world with a few sweet pulls and a story to tell at the next local tournament, well, you are in good shape.

That distinction matters because it affects price, risk, and the way you should evaluate the lot. In a 11-card purchase, you should look for a few absolutes: balance (not all commons), some variety (a couple of rares or foils, or at least some useful strategies), and the absence of fake burn cards that say you own a valued card on trend but actually you own a stack of tiny nothings that will get you benchwarmers in the next match.

## What’s In The Lot: Card Quality, Print, and Reality Checks
A real SB01 Lot Of 11 can include a mix of common, uncommon, rare and possibly a foil. The exact composition will vary, but the expected things you hope to see include:

- A mix of cards from the SB01 assortment, ideally with at least a couple of mid-rarity cards that can slot into a deck without requiring a years long quest to trade with a dragon made of cardboard.
- Good print quality for the card stock typical of DBS: clean borders, legible text, and proper holo if a foil is included. Some lots may include non foil prints that still look sharp; others might show slight centering issues or edge chipping depending on the batch. The important thing is playability; a card with warping or misprint can ruin a turn in a pinch, but not your entire self confidence in life.
- Diversity in color and character: you want a few different units from a variety of tribes and colors, as this gives you a sense of strategy rather than a single cookie cutter build.

If you open the lot and see a stack of mostly identical commons, try not to panic. It happens. If you see a rare or two that look modern and enticing, consider that your mood might swing in the opposite direction of the local meta, which is exactly where we geeks like to be — a little chaotic, a little hopeful, and a lot dramatic.

### Print Quality Notes

## Playing the Lot: Deck Building and Meta Considerations
Tabletop card games are as much about story and mood as they are about numbers. The SB01 Lot Of 11 can be a catalyst for a playful wandering into deck building or a pragmatic jump into a budget build. Here are a few guidelines to get you from card pile to actual play:

- Start with a theme: pick a character, color, or mechanic you enjoy and search for synergy among your 11 cards. DBS cards often reward synergy between specific techniques, such as use of energy under a given color, or the clever timing of a counter move.
- Balance the costs: ensure you have a mix of energy costs to avoid getting stuck with a mouthful of high cost cards you cannot play in round 1. If your 11 card lot is heavy with high cost swings, you risk stalling your own pace and letting the game slip away.
- Consider speed vs. power: some DBS strategies rely on speed and aggression; others rely on defense, control, and resource denial. A well rounded 11 card lot may support a hybrid that can pivot when needed, rather than forcing a single path that the opponent can easily predict and counter.
- Playtesting matters: do not assume the lot will be immediately tournament ready. Set up a few friendly games with friends or local players to see how your 11 cards perform in practice. You will learn more about what you actually own than by staring at spreadsheets all night.

### Sample Deck Ideas You Might Build From SB01 Lot Of 11
- Aggro color mix: a fast curve with cheap early drops, especially if the lot contains a few low cost cards with direct damage or disruption.
- Midrange control: mid-range cards with mid cost that support stalling or fatigue strategies, letting you outlast opponents who lean into raw speed.
- Theme synergy: if you get a set of cards that share a common mechanic or tag, you can craft a deck built around a coherent plan rather than a handful of individual cards that do not talk to each other.
