History and origin

The Kashan region, perched in a historically important corridor of Iran, has long produced high quality rugs that many call the kings of Persian carpets. The Mohtashame (sometimes spelled Mohtashami) Kashane style brings together intricate floral motifs, balanced medallions, and a refined palette that often includes soft blues, ivory creams, and warm terracotta notes. In the pre 1900 category, you will find a transition from the more geometric, tribal influences of older cohorts to a garden like composition that seems to invite a stroll through a painterly courtyard. The present rug uses the standard hand knotted method, a mark of authenticity that is not just decorative but structural. The wool is a natural hero here, dense and springy, with a surface that shimmers under light as if each fiber has a tiny battery inside.

Care and appraisal tips

Proper care helps preserve a rug like this for future collectors. Keep it out of direct sun to prevent color fade, rotate every six months to promote even wear, and use a rug pad to reduce friction with the floorboards beneath. Professional vacuuming with a gentle suction is recommended; avoid aggressive scraping or beater bars that can raise the pile. If you plan to have it stored for long periods, roll the rug rather than folding to minimize crease lines. When it comes to appraisal, seek a certified professional who has experience with Persian antique pieces and a good sense of judgment about regional variations in Kashan production. Document any known restorations, repairs, or re-dye work as these factors influence value and historic integrity.
