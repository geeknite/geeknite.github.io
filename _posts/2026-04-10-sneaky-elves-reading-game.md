## What is Sneaky Elves Reading Game?
Sneaky Elves Reading Game is a social, semi-cooperative-to-competitive card-and-board game that uses deduction, set collection, and a dash of bluffing to drive players toward victory. The elf cabinet—yes, you’ll encounter a cabinet, not a cupboard, because you’re dealing with bibliophile woodland creatures—holds a mix of rare titles, blank slips of paper, and power-ups that feel suspiciously like enchanted bookmarks. Cards grant you entrances into different reading rooms, allow you to swap clues, or nudge your opponents toward the wrong shelf entirely.

### Core idea
The core mechanic revolves around claiming sets of books by solving clues whispered by the elf librarians. Each clue points toward a specific book or a class of books. The trick is that clues are partial, sometimes misdirection-laden, and occasionally downright punny. The more you play, the more you realize the true objective isn’t merely collecting books; it’s collecting the story of how you got those books. It’s a narrative engine disguised as a risk-reward puzzle with a charming, evergreen fantasy vibe.

### Design and tone
The game exudes a cozy, mischievous vibe. The illustrations lean into a warm woodcut aesthetic with cheeky details—tiny elves peeking out from under stacks of tomes, candlelight glinting off brass compasses, and a librarian owl who speaks to you only in footnotes. The tone errs on the side of lighthearted chaos. It doesn’t demand solemn reverence for lore; it rewards you for leaning into whimsy and gleefully misreading clues as soon as you notice the tiny red herrings among the shelves.

## Components and Setup
### What’s in the box
- A compact board depicting a stylized woodland library with branching alcoves and shelves—perfect for those who enjoy flipping a map and pretending you’re a fantasy cartographer.
- A deck of clue cards with riddles, puns, and occasionally haiku-level poetry.
- A set of book tokens representing different genres and rarities.
- Elf pawn minis, each unique with its own tiny personality (one wears oversized spectacles, another sports a cape that promptly falls off when counting points).
- A supply of “crowns” (the scoring tokens) and a handful of special action tokens that grant temporary power-ups.
- A small rulebook that reads like a cheerful librarian’s note card, complete with as-you-need-it glossary entries.

### Quick setup guide
1) Place the board in the center. Each player chooses an elf pawn and receives a starting hand of clue cards.
2) Shuffle the book deck and lay out a few visible books on the shelves. The rest goes face-down as the draw pile.
3) Randomly assign a librarian mood card to the table. This mood shifts how clues are interpreted during the round.
4) Decide whether you’re playing a straight race to X points or a “collect the rarest library” end condition. Then, begin.

The setup is intentionally approachable, designed to minimize boring setup time while maximizing the inevitable chaotic laughter as players cram more clues into their pockets than a fantasy-grade archaeologist would ever admit to having.

## How to Play: Turn Structure and Core Mechanics
### Turn structure (in brief)
- Draw phase: draw a clue card or receive a special action token depending on the librarian mood.
- Action phase: move your elf, collect a book token, solve a clue, or perform a cunning swap with another player. Each action has a cost measured in movement points or clue fragments, so you’ll be juggling resource management with social legerdemain.
- Clue resolution: if you think you matched the current clues to a book, you can declare a match. If correct, you claim the book token and score points. If wrong, you face a minor penalty but gain a clue-driven twist that can tilt the next round.
- End of round: check for end conditions; if met, tally scores and reset or push into overtime with new moods for the librarian.

The cadence feels reminiscent of a friendly scavenger hunt with occasional roulette spins—every turn has the potential to swing your position dramatically, either by securing a critical book or by forcing you to reveal your hand and take the heat from the other elves.

### Core mechanics in plain language
- Set collection: Build the best library by grabbing book tokens that match suit-like categories (mystery, fantasy, science, folklore, etc.). Some cards require you to collect multiple items across categories, which adds a “multicolor collection puzzle” flavor to the game.
- Pattern recognition and clues: Clues link to specific books or categories. The more you study the clue stream, the less you rely on sheer luck and the more you rely on pattern memory. Expect friendly debates about what a clue actually points to. It’s all part of the fun.
- Bluffing and misdirection: You can bluff about which clue you think points to which book. The other players may call your bluff; if you’re caught, you pay a minor penalty. If you’re not, you gain a small advantage. It’s a gentle nudge toward social play rather than a cutthroat competition.
- Spatial movement and positioning: The board’s shelves are modular in many setups, so where your elf stands can influence what you can reach next. It’s not about being the fastest drawer, but about choosing the right shelf and the right moment to sprint toward a coveted token.

### The rhythm of play
Sneaky Elves Reading Game thrives on a tempo that scales with player count. With three players, you’ll find it delightfully snappy—handfuls of clues, quick trades, and a flurry of “I think I see it” declarations. With five or more players, the game blooms into a lively kaleidoscope of guesses, near-misses, and theatrical reveals. The mood card system (librarian mood) ensures that the same core rules feel fresh with repeated plays. It’s a clever way to avoid dethroning the excitement of discovery while still offering a reliable framework for strategy.

## Strategies, Tips, and House Rules
### Foundational tips
- Start with a map of moods: understanding how each librarian mood shifts clue interpretation will help you plan your moves rather than react to every whim. Treat mood cards like weather forecasts for your intellect.
- Balance tempo: don’t sprint to the nearest rare book if you’re still two clues away from a safe, high-scoring set. A patient approach often yields bigger score returns and reduces the risk of being caught with your metaphorical bookmark down.
- Use the swapping tokens strategically: sometimes a well-timed swap can unlock two or three points you wouldn’t reach otherwise. Don’t be shy about testing the waters—just be ready to pivot if your plan hits a cliff face of misinterpreted clues.

### Variant ideas for veteran players
- Elvish Pact: allow players to form temporary alliances to complete a multi-player set with shared scoring. It adds a layer of diplomacy and negotiation to a game that already rewards social dexterity.
- Time-Pressure Rounds: introduce a timer for each turn. It ratchets up tension and mimics the feeling of elves racing to assemble a legendary library before the moonlight fades.
- Expanded library: add a few more genres to the board and include “artifact” cards that grant rare abilities. It increases diversity and gives experimental players more opportunities to shine.
