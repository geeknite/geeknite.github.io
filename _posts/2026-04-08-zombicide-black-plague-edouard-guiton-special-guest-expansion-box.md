## How does it alter the box gameplay? A practical breakdown
This is the heart of the matter. The Special Guest Box piggybacks on the base system and adds depth rather than chaos. Here are the major levers it pulls:

### New survivors with distinct playstyles
The expansion introduces a handful of survivors who each carry a signature mechanic. Some might offer better crowd control, others excel in scavenging or healing teams, and a few are built around risky but rewarding gambits. The upshot is that your tactical options multiply without exploding the complexity. You’ll find that certain combinations of survivor powers interact with the new enemy types to create dynamic turning points in mid game. The design intention is to encourage you to experiment rather than brute-force your way through a room full of screaming undead.

### Fresh zombie archetypes and encounter cards
New zombie types act as mini bosses or specialized threats. They may impose new debuffs, alter movement patterns, or change how objectives are scored. The variety is what keeps Black Plague from feeling stale, and this box pushes that envelope without tipping into “the box needs a manual the size of a novella.” In practice, you’ll experience moments where a normal zombie becomes a test of patience, or where a skirmish with a duo of specials forces you to rethink your plan on the fly.

### Objective cards and mission variety
If you enjoy the slog of collecting points and surviving until the last round, this expansion spices it up with new objectives. Some reward precise routing through perilous terrain, others reward patience and careful extraction of survivors. The net effect is a fresh challenge that makes replayability feel less like a grind and more like an ongoing puzzle to be solved with friends who are equally invested in the chaos.

### Thematic, not tacked-on flavor
One of the common complaint tropes about expansions is that they add shiny new things without tying them to the world. Edouard Guiton’s box avoids that by weaving thematic hooks into the new mechanics. The zombies feel different not just because their stat cards say so, but because the art direction, board-tile layouts, and survivor text reflect a distinct tone that sits well within Black Plague’s grim, darkly comical atmosphere.
