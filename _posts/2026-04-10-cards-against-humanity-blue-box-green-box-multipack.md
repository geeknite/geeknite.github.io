## How It Plays
A standard CAH round is simple to learn. The narrator (the player who is reading the black card) selects a question card and slides it to the center of the table. The other players choose one white card from their hand that best answers the prompt. The narrator then reads the chosen cards aloud, often with a tone that sells the absurdity of the selection. The winner is designated by the narrator, and the winning cards go to the discard pile. The game continues with a new prompt card, a new narrator, and the same cycle of improvised humor.

The Blue Box and Green Box combination adds more depth to this simple loop. With more cards in the pool, players have a richer set of options for their punchlines. More cards also mean more chances for a misclicked or misinterpreted meaning to arise, which is often where the best laughs live. It is not unusual for a room to pivot from family friendly humor to something more adult and provocative as the game progresses, especially when the Green Box expansions are in play. In this sense the multi pack is a tool for customizing your party’s tone and speed.

One note on pacing: Cards Against Humanity can be a marathon or a sprint, depending on the group.
