## How to play
The Mind is deceptively simple, especially in the Black Edition. You’re not reshaping the rules here; you’re sharpening a familiar blade. The goal is to place cards in ascending order without uttering a word. Silence is your ally, not your kryptonite.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a hand to each player according to the rules of the variant you’re playing.
- Decide on the number of lives or mistakes you’ll allow in each round. In the base game this is often a small fixed number; in premium editions you might see slightly different variants to adjust the tension.
- Decide on a pace you’re aiming for and set the mood. If your group tends toward dramatic irony, you’ll probably lean into a slower, more deliberate tempo; if you’re all about quick bursts, you’ll chase a brisk rhythm.

### Round flow
- When the round starts, each player selects a card from their hand and places it face-down on the table in a single moment.
- All players flip their cards at once. If the cards are in ascending order, you continue. If a card breaks the order, you lose a life and the round ends.
- The objective is to complete the entire deck or come as close as your lives allow, all while never speaking a word.

### Scoring and lives
Most plays are gentle and forgiving, but missteps can accumulate. In The Mind, your success is measured by how well you coordinate across a series of rounds, not by a point tally. The Black Edition’s premium components can encourage longer play sessions and a more immersive feel, but the core risk remains: a single misread moment can cascade into tense, funny chaos.
