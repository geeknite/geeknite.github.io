## Gameplay overview
Here is a quick tour for new players and for those who need a refresher between snack breaks.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal 10 cards to each player (rule variants exist for larger tables or shorter play sessions).
- Place the remaining cards in a draw pile and flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Each player tracks their progress through the classic ten phases (for this game, think of them as levels you unlock by meeting specific card combinations).

### Turn structure
On your turn you typically draw one or two cards, then attempt to lay down cards that match the current phase. If you complete a phase, you advance to the next phase and declare your success. If you can’t complete a phase, you discard a card and end your turn. The game continues until a player completes all ten phases, or until the agreed-upon playtime elapses.

A few twists that keep things lively include Wild cards that substitute any color or value to complete a phase, Wild Draws that punish your friends a little for their ambition, and occasional Skip cards that force a temporary timeout on someone else’s plan. The pacing of these moments is what makes Phase 10 more than a simple “match-of-the-collection” exercise.

### Special cards and phase details
- Wild cards can stand in for any number or color to help you complete a phase. Use them wisely; they’re the best tool you have for clutch plays—like using a cheat code in a kid-friendly game.
- Skip cards create short delays for opponents, which can be the difference between hot victory and a tepid loss. Timing is everything here. Don’t waste a Skip on someone who’s a hair away from finishing their phase; you’ll regret it at the next table turn, or at the very least during dessert.
- Phase completion requires a careful balance of risk and timing. Sometimes you’ll be forced to take a risk on a Wild card. Other times you’ll play it safe and wait for the perfect moment. The joy comes from the tiny celebrations when someone swoops in to finish a phase right after you thought you had them cornered.

## Tips and strategies for players of all ages
- Play for multiple phases, not just the immediate one. Keep an eye on what you still need for future phases and try to optimize your deck draws to set up final goals.
- Use Wild cards strategically. It’s tempting to chase the next phase, but a well-timed Wild can turn a near-miss into a victory.
- Don’t be afraid to team up with the table to sabotage a genius plan. The social energy is part of the fun, and a well-timed Skip can disrupt a rival who is clearly about to seal the deal.
- For younger players, keep the rules simple and focus on celebrating each achievement. The goal is to keep the night light and joyful, not to exhaust everyone with math and misinterpretations.
- If you want to keep a longer session engaging, rotate the lead role and let everyone experience the decision-making pressure. It’s amazing how a simple shift in responsibility alters table dynamics.
