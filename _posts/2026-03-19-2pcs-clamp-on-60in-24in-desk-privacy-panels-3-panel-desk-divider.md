## Setup and Installation: No Drills, No Drama

The whole point of clamp-on privacy panels is to avoid drilling holes in your desk and to be movable when the office decides to rearrange the floor plan like a chessboard. Setup is meant to be intuitive, with adjustable clamps that can fit a range of desk thicknesses. The installation steps usually go something like this:
1) Place the long 60-inch panel across the desk edge where you want the privacy barrier.2) Open the clamps, nestle them onto the desk edge, and tighten until they hold; don’t overtighten and risk marring the desk.3) Attach the three-panel partition to the base panels using the included connectors.4) Adjust the angle and height to fit your needs—some desks are square specials; others are the kind of rectangle you only see in gymnasiums or conference rooms.

In practice, you’ll appreciate the ease of installation. There’s no need to haul out a drill, a level, or a stranger named Bob who insists on giving a TED Talk while you try to assemble your privacy fortress. The clamps can be tightened by hand, and some designs offer a quick-release feature if you need to temporarily remove the panels for cleaning, rearranging, or performing that afternoon dance break you forgot you needed.

A common pitfall to watch for is desk lip variation. If your desk edge has a pronounced bevel or a deep edge, you may need to adjust the clamps to get a snug fit. Some users report that the long 60-inch panel can sag a little in the middle if the desk is extremely long or if you lean on the panel while standing or stretching toward the monitor. If sag is a concern, consider adding a small central support or angling the three-panel partition to create a slight arch rather than a flat, stubborn wall. This approach preserves privacy while maintaining ability to peek at your own reflection in the monitor as you pretend to be productive.

## Practical Tips and Tricks: Getting the Most Out of Your Panels

- Angle matters: Don’t lock the panels into a flat plane across your desk. Try a slight angling to create a private nook while still keeping your monitor visible. A subtle V-shape can do wonders for reducing head-turn fatigue during long video calls.
- Cable management: Plan where your cables will run. The panel setup can obstruct some cable pathways, so design a small tunnel behind the edge where you can pass the power and HDMI cables without bending them. A tidy desk is a happy desk.
- Lighting: Good lighting is essential for video calls. If your room has overhead lighting that makes you look like you’re in witness protection, consider a small LED lamp or a ring light to balance shadows. The privacy panels won’t fix lighting alone, but they’ll give you a better canvas on which to paint your face with confidence.
- Cleaning routine: Wipe down surfaces weekly. A quick wipe not only keeps things looking sharp but also reduces the buildup of smudges that make the panel look permanently scratched even when it’s not.
- Compatibility notes: If you have a monitor stand or a brutal, heavy monitor arm perched on your desk, ensure the clamps don’t collide with those components. Sometimes you’ll want to move the panel away from the edge to avoid interference. The modular design should give you enough flexibility to do this without retooling your entire desk setup.
