## Care and Preservation: Keeping the Sparkling Auspices Intact
Preservation is a dance between display and storage. Here are a few geeky tips to ensure your rare sealed deck remains a shining beacon of color and metal-inspired mystique:

- Store in a cool, dry place. Cards hate humidity; humidity makes the edges warp and the colors bleed. If your home has a basement that likes to collect moisture like a needy software update, consider a climate-controlled display case.
- Keep the seal intact if you intend to hold on to the deck forever. The seal is part of the rarity story, and opening it reduces the deck’s collectible value, even if you get to flip the cards gracefully at game night.
- Avoid direct sunlight. UV rays are the silent killer of colored inks. A sunny windowsill is a collector’s worst nightmare—better to keep the deck tucked away in a cabinet with a little ambient lighting for your display shelf.
- If you do open it for use, store the cards in a box or a soft case to protect them from scuffs and oils on your fingers. Remember, these are not disposable. They’re a small art piece with a geometry that loves a gentle touch.
- Handling with care doesn’t mean you can’t enjoy the deck. A well-shuffled deck can still look marvelous in your hands, and showing off a perfect fan is a moment where the card gods smile at your level of nerdy dedication.

If you’re the kind of person who keeps a small log of every new deck you acquire, consider a simple notebook entry with notes about weight, feel, color saturation, and whether the back shows signs of reflective lighting. You’ll thank your past self when you’re deciding which deck to bring to a magic show or a poker night with the in-laws.
