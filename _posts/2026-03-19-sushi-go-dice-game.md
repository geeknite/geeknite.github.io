## Components and Setup
### What's in the box
- A colorful set of dice, each etched with sushi icons: nigiri, maki, gunkan, and a few cheeky side-dishes.
- A compact score pad and reference card for tallying points at light speed.
- A handful of food-tongue-inspired tokens and a tiny discard tray for dice that didn’t quite land in your favor.
- A tight rules booklet that could have been written by a sushi chef who survived a busy Saturday rush.

The design is deceptively simple: roll dice, draft a few, pass the rest, and try not to let your rivals steal your favorite combos. Some dice scream big scores; others offer steady, cozy gains. It’s the culinary equivalent of a dungeon crawl where your weapon is a spicy tuna and your armor is a cucumber roll with a grin.

### Setup notes
Setup clocks in at about a minute or so. Shuffle the dice, lay them within reach, hand each player a tiny score tracker, and elect the starting chef (the dice roller). The first player rolls a pool to reveal a face-up tableau; everyone secretly picks a die to keep and passes the rest to the left. Repeat a couple more times until all dice have been drafted. Then comes scoring, where you tally points from dice faces and the scoring cards that dictate bonuses for combos, color variety, and special patterns.

### Table presence
Visually, the game keeps the same bright, cartoony vibe as the card version, but with a tactile twist: dice. Watching a sushi dice tumble toward your napkin is oddly satisfying — like landing a satisfying crit, but in edible form. The table becomes a little sushi buffet of possibility, the kind of scene that makes you grin even if your roll is simply a cucumber roll with a dramatic sigh of fate.

### Accessibility considerations
Sushi Go! Dice is approachable for kids and adults alike, and its drafting mechanic rewards pattern recognition and risk assessment more than heavy strategy. It’s not a megadungeon of rules; it’s a breezy, digestible quick-play that wears a goofy apron and smiles at you while you attempt a tiny chess match with a piece of wasabi as your signal flare.

## How to Play: A Walkthrough
### Turn structure and flow
Each round consists of a drafting sequence followed by a scoring phase. On your turn, you roll a subset of your dice, reveal faces, pick a die to keep from the face-up pool, and pass the rest to the left. The aim is to assemble a menu of faces that aligns with the scoring cards. The number of rounds isn’t infinite, but the micro-decisions feel endless when you’re hungry for a win (and hungry for snacks).

### The faces and what they do
- Nigiri faces yield points based on type and sometimes require combinations for maximum effect.
- Maki faces grant points if you collect enough copies; more of these means more bragging rights later.
- Side dish faces (edamame, gyoza, etc.) provide smaller bonuses and can be the tiebreakers that decide a tense scoreline.
- A few rare faces trigger special bonuses, rewarding you for variety or for matching patterns across rounds.

The real joy appears in the scoring cards, where math and whimsy mingle. You’ll craft a tiny multi-move plan: should I chase the big Nigiri payoff now, or grab several smaller gains and hope the late rounds tilt the table in my favor? It’s a delicious dilemma that makes you snicker as you try to outsmart your tablemates without stealing all the fun from your own piece of the menu.

### Example round
Imagine a quick hypothetical: six dice in the pool. A flashy Nigiri with a rich point value sits next to two Makis, a small Gunkan, and a duo of side-dish symbols. You grab the Nigiri, pass the rest. The next player does the same, and so on. By the time the dice circle the table twice, your choices shift from “grab the big payoff now” to “secure the set that will crush the tiebreakers.” The tension simmers, the table laughs, and you realize you’ve spent more mental calories on sushi than you did on your lunch.

### Scoring and end-of-round tally
At the end of each round, you tally points from your dice and the scoring cards. The rules give you shortcuts for combos and sets; with a bit of luck, you snag the coolest bonuses for assembling a rainbow of sushi types. The final tally across rounds crowns the winner in sashimi-grade bragging rights. The scoring isn’t rocket science, but it rewards careful drafting and a touch of chaos — the perfect pairing for snack-driven competition.

## Strategy and Tips
### Early game focus
In the opening rounds, think of yourself as laying breadcrumbs toward a future feast. Favor flexible, low-risk gains that leave multiple paths open. Don’t become married to one sushi type too early; the dice hate commitment and will happily betray you with a surprise eel roll if you blink.

### Mid-game pivoting
As dice whirl, start reading the table. If you notice others chasing a specific combo, you can try to block by snagging a die that disrupts their rhythm. It’s not purely antagonistic; it’s about adjusting to the energy of the table. Some rounds reward risk: do you gamble on a high-scoring path requiring two perfect rolls, or do you bank steady points from smaller sets?

### Endgame clinchers
The last rounds often decide the winner. It’s tempting to chase a flashy objective, but a quiet, consistent approach can win out. Track opponents’ totals and their potential to explode on one strong roll. If you deny a single critical die to them while stacking small gains for yourself, you’ll earn table-cred and a smug grin that says, I came, I rolled, I conquered.

### House rules and variants
Some groups like to remix the rules for extra chaos: faster rounds, hidden scoring cards, alternate die faces for extra chaos. If you adopt house rules, keep the vibe faithful to the game: speed, silliness, and snacks. The joy of Sushi Go! Dice is its nimble design — any tweak should accelerate the pace and not choke the laughter out of the room.
