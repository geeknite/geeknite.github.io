## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity Tips
Like all gear meant to last through a growing phase, this set rewards a bit of maintenance. Wipe clubs after use to minimize chalky residue on the faces, check that the grips aren’t peeling by age 6, and store the bag in a dry place to prevent rust on the hardware. If your child leaves the set out in the rain, don’t panic—the steel parts won’t instantly rust, but they’ll appreciate a quick wipe-down and a light oil on high-wear surfaces every few months.

A practical tip: keep a small maintenance kit in the garage with a microfiber cloth, a soft brush, and a tiny bottle of CLP or equivalent for the metal surfaces. The goal is simple: keep it looking sharp and feeling good so your kid feels like a pro every time they pick up a club.
