## Ergonomics and daily use

Let’s talk about ergonomics, that science-y word people throw around when their backs hurt after six hours of keyboard combat. The GOF 3P is designed so that you can achieve a reasonable seating position with your chair at standard desk height. The keyboard tray is a bit of an odd duck because this particular unit is not a separate keyboard tray; instead, the keyboard sits on the desk surface, which is fine as long as you’re mindful of wrist position. The monitor shelf is slightly elevated, which reduces neck strain compared to a ground‑level monitor, and the edge of the desk is rounded to give your forearms a comfortable resting place.

Cable management matters: the included grommets help keep the power strip and data cables from running into your legs, but you’ll still want cable ties and clips to keep things tidy. A neat desk is a productive desk—at least that’s what productivity gurus keep telling us as they publish more videos about “keyboard posture.”

For taller users, there’s a separate riser option that raises the entire monitor stack by a few inches. For short or mid-height folks, the standard height is comfortable for minutes, hours, or endless Zoom calls where your eyes are fighting a constant battle against the glare of a ceiling light.

If you’re a dual-monitor warrior or a triple-monitor overlord, the GOF 3P can accommodate two monitors on the main surface with a monitor arm. The space behind the monitor is not cavernous, but it’s enough for a small speaker or a USB hub, which is all you need to create your own little streaming setup in a pinch.

## Setup tips and tricks

- Plan your layout before you start mounting. A simple floor plan on a piece of paper can save you a lot of time when you’re trying to decide where to place the file cabinet, monitor, and lamp.
- Use the cable management grommets to route power cables and data cables to the back. You’ll minimize trip hazards and keep the whole thing looking sleek.
- If you’re adding a monitor arm, ensure your desk supports the additional torque on the surface. Some arms require additional reinforcement. If you’re not sure, a simple test with a towel under the monitor can help you gauge whether the desk will flex under the added load.
- Don’t over-tighten screws. It’s easy to strip a panel if you push too hard. Tighten just enough to seat the piece and avoid overtightening which can cause warping.
- Consider a small monitor riser or a keyboard tray if you want to optimize elbow height. A little extra investment here can make a big difference in long-term comfort.
