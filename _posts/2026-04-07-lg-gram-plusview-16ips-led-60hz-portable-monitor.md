## Connectivity and Versatility
### USB-C and power delivery: the bread and butter
The big advantage of the Gram +View lies in its USB-C connectivity. The monitor is designed to work with laptops and devices supporting USB-C with DisplayPort Alternate Mode (and ideally power delivery). In practical terms: you plug one USB-C cable into your laptop, and the monitor both displays content and draws power (within the PD budget). In behavioral terms, you cut down on cable clutter—one cable to rule them all. If your laptop can deliver around 30-60W through USB-C, that’s often enough to run the monitor without needing a separate power brick. If your device can’t deliver enough power, you’ll see the monitor’s built-in power usage from the USB-C connection and you may need to provide the extra oomph via a separate charger. In day-to-day terms: plug, play, and pretend you are the manager of a tiny, perfectly organized control room.

### Other ports and accessories
Unlike some portable monitors that advertise a wide array of ports (HDMI, USB-A, micro-USB, etc.), the LG Gram +View’s strength is its streamlined USB-C experience. Some versions may include a 3.5mm audio jack for headsets when you’re in a pinch, and a micro-HDMI or HDMI port is less common on this line. It’s best to verify the exact port configuration you’re buying, but in the common USB-C-centric SKU, you’ll want to bring a USB-C to USB-C cable that supports DP Alt Mode. If you want a multi-device setup, you can still get creative: use a USB-C hub to add HDMI or DisplayPort outputs, and still use the USB-C connection to the laptop to keep the single-cable convenience. The key is to keep your expectations aligned with the product’s intended use: portable productivity, not a desk-bound hub monster.
