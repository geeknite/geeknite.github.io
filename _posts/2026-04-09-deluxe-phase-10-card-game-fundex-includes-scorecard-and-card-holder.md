### How to play Phase 10 (brief refresher)
Without turning this into a full tertiary literature on Phase 10 rules, here’s the quick gist with the Deluxe upgrades in mind:
- The game is played in two main parts: drawing and discarding, with players attempting to complete the ten phases in order. Each phase has a specific requirement (e.g., two sets of three, a run of four, etc.).
- Each player takes turns picking a card, discarding, and trying to build the required hand to complete the current phase. Completing a phase advances you to the next one, while lateness and penalties can drag souls to the bottom of the scoreboard.
- The Deluxe edition’s scorecard helps track completion and penalties, while the card holder keeps your melds organized for quick reference in the heat of competition.
- The game ends when someone completes Phase 10, and then you tally up the scores for the final winner.

If you’re new to Phase 10, this is a game about drafting, memory, and pattern recognition with a time-management vibe—perfect for people who treat an evening in as a festival of color-coded strategies rather than a stoic grind.
