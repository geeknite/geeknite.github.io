## Overview

UNO Flip Attack is Mattel's latest attempt to remix the iconic color-and-number game with a few toyish flourishes and a high-energy twist. The core mechanics still revolve around matching colors or numbers to the discard pile, but two big additions promise to spice things up: the Attack cards and the Flip mechanic. The Attack cards are designed to escalate the tension by forcing other players to draw cards or face other inconvenient penalties. The Flip mechanic swaps the entire color palette and deck orientation mid-game, flipping the board from the light side to the dark side (and sometimes back again), depending on how you or fate decides to play.

If you enjoyed UNO Flip for its two-sided deck and the occasional mental math race, UNO Flip Attack takes that concept and dials it up to 11. The mood is unmistakably fast-paced, chaotic in the best sense, and ideally suited to a group that thrives on banter, bluffing about rules, and the possibility of someone dramatically slamming a draw pile onto their own forehead as a result of a poorly timed UNO call.

## How to Play: Quickstart Guide

Here’s a streamlined approach to getting UNO Flip Attack on the table without consulting the Rule Book for the 15th time. These steps assume a standard 2–6 player layout, with the usual deck and a standard 7-card hand.

### Setup
1. Shuffle the UNO Flip Attack deck.
2. Each player draws seven cards to start.
3. Place the remainder of the deck face down to form the draw pile.
4. Flip the top card to begin the discard pile. If the first card is an Attack or Flip card, you follow its effect right away to avoid a boring opening.

### Turn Sequence
On your turn, you have several options:
- Play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol on the discard pile.
- Play a Flip card to switch sides Light to Dark or vice versa. This changes which color palette is active and may introduce different Attack or penalty options.
- Play an Attack card to affect the next player (most Attack cards cause that player to draw additional cards, while others skip or reverse).
- If you cannot play, draw a card from the draw pile. You may be able to play the drawn card immediately if it matches.

If you have one card left, you must shout UNO. If another player catches you forgetting, you will face a penalty, typically drawing additional cards. This classic rule remains intact, though some Attack cards can alter the consequences.

### Special Cards
- Flip: The most thematic card. It flips the current color palette on the table and can dramatically alter what cards are legal to play on your next turn.
- Attack: There are several variants within the Attack family. Some cause the next player to draw two or four cards; others force the next player to perform a small action, or to flip the deck to reveal an alternate target. The exact effects are listed in the Rule Book, but they all add a sense of urgency to a game night.
- Draw-to-Say Uno: A variation included in the new deck where certain cards require you to perform a social action before you can play.

### Winning
The first player to shed all of their cards wins the round. Points are tallied according to the values of the cards left in opponents’ hands, as in standard UNO. The game ends when a player reaches the target score or after a predetermined number of rounds.

### House Rules
Some households enjoy adding house rules for extra chaos. A popular one is Skip the Penalty where an Attack card can be canceled by playing a Flip card immediately after it, though that rule shortens the game and reduces some of the drama. If you want to keep it simple, skip most house rules and keep the structure as a fast-paced family game.

## Strategy and Tips

- Play aggressively early, but don’t throw away your draw pile right away. You’ll want a fallback plan once the Attack cards start flying.

- Watch your UNO calls. One incorrect call can reset your progress and turn a win into a draw, gladly.
