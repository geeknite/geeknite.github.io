## Overview

Fortune Row is a district-focused expansion rather than a sprawling city-wide add-on. Think of it as a heist-meets-myth-meet-microcosm. Fortune Row leans into the street-level hustle that City of Mist has always whispered about but rarely shoved into the foreground. It isn’t about multi-city conspiracies or higher-order gods carving continents out of mist. It is about the alleyways that lead to those conspiracies, the street-level risks that pull a mere mortal into the orbit of gods, and the fortune that smiles just long enough to reveal your next big mistake.

The book is, in many ways, a love letter to the craft of urban noir. The heroes will be drawn to Fortune Row with its promise of diamonds in day-old coffee and a rumor that someone owes a debt to someone who owes a debt to the wrong god. Fortune Row asks a core question: in a city where everything has a mythic alias, how do you navigate the social contracts that hold a neighborhood together when every face on the street could be a mask waiting to be swapped? The answer is not “punch harder” but “think smarter, listen closer, cheat the dice with wit and charm, then perhaps cheat fate itself a little more.”

From a design standpoint, Fortune Row keeps the City of Mist spirit intact while leaning into two mechanical themes that resonate with a lot of players: fate manipulation and social intrigue. You’ll find a suite of tools designed to keep your investigations moving without requiring you to conjure a dozen new charts for the night. If the base game is a jazz quartet, Fortune Row is the saxophone solo with a little extra reverb: familiar air, but it hits you in a new, satisfying way.

A note on tone: Fortune Row preserves the game’s noir grit, but it leans into a more gambling-and-luck vibe. Thematically, it’s not sunshine and ponies; it’s late-night ramen on payday, a street performance that could be a con, and a myth that wants to borrow your name to walk the world a little differently. The expansion invites a GM to stage more social escalation scenes, longer cons, and moral gray zones where luck might tilt the odds but never absolve you of consequences.

## Contents and physical product

If you’ve opened a recent City of Mist expansion, you’ll feel right at home with Fortune Row’s physical and layout language. The book comes in a compact format that travels well, with a cover that shimmers in the light the way a neon sign does when you’re parked at a red light waiting for a bus that may or may not arrive. The art direction keeps the city’s blur of rain and chrome intact, with character pieces that look as if they were sketched in a back booth while a judge argued with a streetlight.

What’s inside the pages? In broad strokes, Fortune Row includes:

- Several new investigator playbooks or at least modifications to existing archetypes tuned to Fortune Row’s vibe. These aren’t mindless reskins; they shift a few playstyle levers toward crafting social entanglements and manipulating luck in the city’s social networks.
- A handful of new logos or mythoi—these are the mythic identities you can embody or encounter. Expect a few that tilt toward fortune, chance manipulation, and the street’s own deity myths.
- A collection of assets, including new Keepsakes, Contacts, and possibly a couple of “Row Cards” designed to trigger flashbacks during investigations or illuminate social dynamics at the table.
- Investigation prompts and scenario seeds tailored to Fortune Row’s districts: a bank with a secret vault of luck, a theater troupe that performs outcomes instead of plays, and a bar where the drinks reveal a person’s hidden truth after you buy a round.
- A GM toolkit: tips, hooks, pacing guides, and a few ready-to-run plots that show how the district’s environment can derail even well-laid plans in the most entertaining ways.
- A map page and an abbreviated GM screen or quick-reference card to help keep the game moving without constant flipping through pages.

The book does a good job of aligning its components into a cohesive whole. You’ll notice the district’s microcosm approach helps you run a single session or a short arc without needing a galaxy-spanning conspiracy for a night’s entertainment. If your table loves puzzle-box investigations and social drama, you’ll feel right at home here.

## New mechanics and how they feel at the table

Fortune Row introduces a handful of mechanics designed to reinforce its focus on luck, social maneuvering, and the moral ambiguity of noir. The exact mechanics can vary by table, but the core experience remains approachable for veteran City of Mist players while offering enough nuance for newcomers who want to dip a toe into something more than a straight chase sequence.

- Fortune points or luck tokens: These are not foreign concepts in City of Mist, but Fortune Row converges on them with a slightly more tactile approach. Players can spend luck to nudge outcomes, plus the GM might stage narrative debt that can push for a dramatic payoff later. The risk is that luck becomes too cheap and resolves a scene too quickly; the fix is to have meaningful costs and consequences tied into the district’s economy and social stakes.
- Row Cards and flashbacks: The expansion uses a row of card-based prompts that trigger flashbacks or reveal hidden relationships between suspects and allies. These cards provide a quick-reference means to pivot a scene toward revelation without grinding the game to a halt. They also serve as a storytelling courtesy, allowing players to leverage knowledge they already possess in fresh ways.
- Social fabric and masks: The district design pushes players to explore masks not as abstract ideas but as social currencies within Fortune Row. Masks function as both identity and leverage—how you present yourself in Fortune Row can unlock advantages but may cost you authenticity or invite new conflicts. This is a thematic win for players who enjoy roleplaying nuance.
- Investigative pacing: The toolkit supports a rhythm where players chase leads, gain a new clue, face a complication, and then pivot toward a social showdown or a daring extraction. The pacing encourages a blend of voice-driven scenes and action pieces, keeping the session lively without becoming a sprint or a slog.

If you have a table that loves the “cut to the chase after a clue” feeling, Fortune Row’s mechanics reward clever negotiation and careful exposure of secrets. If you prefer straight-ahead combat or outlandish set-piece chases, you can still enjoy this expansion, but you’ll want to lean into the social intrigue portions and let the Storyteller drive the action toward a timely reveal rather than a heavy fight scene.

## Play experience: tone, pacing, and table energy

Fortune Row’s tone sits squarely in the noir-adjacent lane with a wink. The district feels lived-in, with locals who have names, debts, and agendas. The city’s rain-slick streets are a metaphor you can touch, and Fortune Row makes you feel its weight without forcing you to chase a villain across three different neighborhoods. The pacing supports a session where each scene builds toward a consensus that the players have crafted a plan, only to realize that their plan was never as good as their ability to improvise when the fortune column flips to a new page.

In terms of table energy, Fortune Row tends to favor social scenes, delicate negotiations, and the kind of table talk that makes players lean in because someone just tipped their hand and now everyone must decide whether to call that bluff or press their luck. If your group loves the “one more hit of tension” moment—where a line of dialogue reveals a link between two suspects—you’ll savor Fortune Row’s ability to turn a quiet conversation into a turning point. The GM’s job, now more than ever, is to listen to the room and time reveals for maximum dramatic effect: don’t overplay the twist, let the room’s energy guide when to reveal a character’s lie or a god’s subtle nudge.

Visual design and layout help with pace too. The art direction leans into moody street scenes, billboards that shimmer with misdirection, and a color palette that makes neon signifiers feel like a language you can read. The placement of sidebars, hooks, and prompts in the book makes it easier to pick up and run a scene in a pinch, which is essential for a system that depends on quick social reads and intuitive dice outcomes.

If you’re a GM who enjoys crafting a city that feels alive but is also cheeky about its own myths, Fortune Row gives you a playground where you can experiment with social leverage and narrative misdirection. If you’re a player who loves acting, deception, and the thrill of a perfect bluff, the new playbooks and tools offer plenty of opportunities to shine without turning you into a one-trick pony.

## Thematic fit and safety considerations

City of Mist has always thrived on mythic meta-narrative. Fortune Row stays true to that promise while sharpening the focus on district-specific stories that feel intimate and personal. The if-then-else lines of Fate and Fortune give both players and GMs permission to lean into moral ambiguity—your fortune can open doors, but it might implicate someone you care about.

As with any expansion that leans into noir and crime-inspired material, you’ll want to consider safety and consent markers. The book presents noir-level stakes that can touch on theft, deception, crime, and the social harm that accompanies these themes. The recommended approach for a happy table is to have clear lines and veils about what is and isn’t on the table, and to encourage players to communicate when a line is being crossed or when a scene has gone too far. If your group runs with the safety tools you already use for other games, you’ll likely find Fortune Row plays well with your existing practices. If you’re new to the system, now is the time to adopt a simple safety framework so everyone can enjoy the mood without a single player feeling blindsided.

The district’s emphasis on social maneuvering also invites a healthy diversity of character concepts. As with any City of Mist supplement, the playbooks and myths should feel inclusive and allow players to represent a variety of backgrounds and experiences. Fortune Row doesn’t automatically fix every narrative blind spot, but it does invite players to tell more nuanced stories about how power, luck, and masks intersect in a single night’s work.

## Value, price, and production quality

Fortune Row sits in a familiar price range for City of Mist expansions. If you’re in it for the long haul, it’s a worthwhile purchase for the additional playbooks, new myths, and the district-focused tools that make a one-shot feel like a mini-campaign. The production quality aligns with City of Mist’s standard: sturdy book, crisp art, readable layout, and a confident screen presence on the table. It’s not a lavish coffee-table edition, but it’s not a card-sleeved pamphlet either. It’s a sturdy investment in a campaign that you intend to run for multiple sessions.

As with any RPG supplement, the question of value comes down to your table’s appetite for the expansion. If you relish a setting that invites more social ingenuity and more “what would the Mask do in this bar” moments, Fortune Row pays back its price in memorable scenes and character-driven revelations. If your table just wants more gunplay and chase sequences, you might find Fortune Row a slower burn—yet a burn that can still sing when paired with the right crew and the right narration tempo.

## How it compares to other City of Mist expansions

Fortune Row shares DNA with other City of Mist expansions in terms of tone, mood, and the core mechanic marriage of myth and modern crime. Compared to other district-focused or character-centric add-ons, Fortune Row leans harder into the social chess game than into pure action. It’s the kind of expansion that rewards players who enjoy a slow-burn investigation with a few key moments that reframe the entire night. If you’ve played other expansions that emphasize a new city block or a new generic myth, Fortune Row stands out for how quickly it makes a single neighborhood feel like a character with ambitions and debts.

If you’re evaluating Fortune Row against a more combat-focused expansion, you’ll likely see a trade-off: the new tools may give you fewer direct fight mechanics, but they reward you with richer dialogue, sharper misdirection, and more satisfying “aha” moments that come from social reveals and carefully staged lies.

For fans who have enjoyed previous City of Mist core rules and expansion packs, Fortune Row slots into the catalog as a natural, stylish progression that doesn’t rewrite the game but enhances the table’s ability to tell morally gray stories with flair. It’s not a modular overhaul; it’s a refinement of what the game can do when you tilt the scene toward fortune, status, and the street-level angle of mythic influence.

## Who should buy Fortune Row

- GMs who want a ready-to-run district with a strong theme and a toolkit for social intrigue.
- Players who adore misdirection, clever dialogue, and the satisfaction of a well-timed reveal that shifts the story’s axis.
- Groups that like to weave micro-arcs into a larger campaign without needing a full city-wide conspiracy every session.
- Teams that enjoy a noir vibe and want to lean into the moral complexity of masks, debts, and the side-effects of luck in a living city.

Fortune Row may not be the best fit for groups seeking pure action, but it is excellent for teams who value storytelling turns, character-driven negotiation, and the thrill of watching a plan crumble under the weight of a bad decision—then watching those bad decisions become the night’s best moments.

## Scenarios and seed ideas to kick off Fortune Row nights

To help you hit the table running, here are a few seeds you can drop into any Fortune Row session. Feel free to mix and match, twist them with your players, and let the dice do the rest.

- The Bank of Luck heist: A local bank vault houses a mythic coin that amplifies any beneficiary’s fortune for one night. The crew has to navigate a maze of family debts and a security system that reads like a fortune-teller’s horoscope. Will your team steal the coin, or will the coin steal the team’s luck?
- The Velvet Curtain Gambit: A theater troupe sells glimpses of possible futures to the audience through surreal stagecraft. The players must decide which futures to reveal—and which to bury—before a rival faction twitches the performance into a deadly reality.
- The Bar That Keeps Time: A neon bar promises a memory-altering drink that lets patrons recall a single moment with perfect clarity. The players must uncover who is behind the bar’s business and why they have a vested interest in rewriting a night that never fully happened.
- The Mask Auction: A secret auction gathers the city’s most dangerous masks for sale. Each mask carries a debt or a favor owed to a mythic power. The team could walk away with a priceless artifact or awaken an entity that remembers every lie spoken in Fortune Row.
- The Debt Collector: A debt owed by a powerful figure to a god of luck creates a cascade of consequences on Fortune Row. The players must either settle the debt, exploit it for personal gain, or find a way to sever the city’s tug-of-war with fate.

Each seed is designed to be modular: you can drop it into a longer arc or run it as a stand-alone night. The important part is to let the district’s mood drive your pacing and to use the Fortune Row tools to heighten tension at exact moments when a reveal or a betrayal would feel most satisfying.

## Production notes and conclusion

Fortune Row is a voice that City of Mist can use to say something new about luck, power, and the stories ordinary people tell when they’re caught between myth and modern life. It is not a game-changer in terms of core rules, but it is a reminder that the city itself is a living character—one that you can tease into revealing its secrets under the glow of a neon sign. The materials are well-produced, accessible to both new players and veterans, and the expansion leans into what makes City of Mist appealing: a blend of myth, noir, and a creative, improvisational approach to storytelling.

If you’re seeking a supplement that rewards careful world-building, social cunning, and a few well-placed dives into the darker corners of your city, Fortune Row is worth adding to your shelf. It gives you a concentrated dose of mood, myth, and misdirection that can anchor a campaign or spark a long-running arc focused on a single, unforgettable district.

## Final verdict

Fortune Row captures the spirit of neon-lit noir that fans of City of Mist crave, while delivering a practical toolkit that makes district-focused storytelling a joy rather than a chore. It’s a compact expansion with a lot of character, designed for groups that love social manipulation, moral ambiguity, and the thrill of watching luck tilt the odds in a way that feels earned. If you want more texture, more intrigue, and more moments that make your players grin with the realization that the city itself is their ally and their adversary in equal measure, Fortune Row delivers. 

If you want a recommendation in one sentence: buy Fortune Row if you want to deepen your City of Mist table’s mood, character drama, and narrative fireworks; skip it if your group is allergic to social intrigue and only wants the occasional chase scene. Overall, it’s a stylish, well-crafted addition that will spark conversations around the table long after the credits roll.
