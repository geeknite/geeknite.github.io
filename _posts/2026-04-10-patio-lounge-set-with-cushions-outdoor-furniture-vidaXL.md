### Care and maintenance: practical tips you can actually use
Caring for a wooden outdoor set is not a secret society of endless oiling and buffing, though you might feel like you joined one after finishing a weekend maintenance session. The basic recipe is simple: keep the wood clean with mild soap and water, dry thoroughly, and apply a protective finish at regular intervals. Cushions usually benefit from spot-cleaning and occasional machine washing per the care label. A breathable cushion cover helps prevent moisture buildup that can lead to mold or mildew; if your covers are machine washable, use a gentle cycle and air dry to preserve fabric integrity.

A few practical rituals that help extend the life of any outdoor wood set include:

- Regularly wipe off dust and debris to prevent surface abrasion.
- Inspect joints for looseness and re-tighten as needed.
- Use coasters or placemats to protect wood from water rings and heat damage.
- Store cushions during heavy rain or winter; use a breathable cover when possible.
- Periodically rotate cushions so wear distributes evenly across all seats.

These small habits add up, allowing your patio furniture to stay stylish without resembling a weathered museum piece before its time.
