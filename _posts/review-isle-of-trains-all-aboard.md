## Introduction: All Aboard for Fun!

Hello, train enthusiasts and board game fans! Today, we're diving into **Isle of Trains: All Aboard**, the latest addition to the beloved Isle of Trains series. Whether you're a seasoned conductor or a newbie to train-themed board games, this review will provide you with everything you need to know about this exciting game.

Isle of Trains: All Aboard is designed by Dan Kesling and Seth Jaffee and published by Dr. Finn's Games. It's a competitive, engine-building game that revolves around delivering passengers and cargo while upgrading your train to become the most efficient railway tycoon.

---

## Game Components

Let's start with the components. Isle of Trains: All Aboard comes with a beautifully designed board, train cards, cargo tokens, and passenger meeples. The artwork is charming and captures the essence of a bustling train station.

### Train Cards

Players use train cards to build their locomotives, adding carriages and upgrades as the game progresses. These cards are the heart of the game, determining your train's capabilities and scoring potential.

### Cargo Tokens

Cargo tokens represent the goods your train will deliver throughout the game. Managing these tokens efficiently is key to maximizing your score.

### Passenger Meeples

Passenger meeples add an extra layer of strategy, as players must decide when to prioritize cargo or passengers to gain the most points.

**Criticism Alert:** While the components are high quality, some players might find the small tokens a bit fiddly to manage during intense gameplay. Keep a steady hand, or you might derail your plans!

---

## Gameplay Mechanics

### Building Your Train

At the start of the game, each player receives a basic locomotive and a few train cards. As the game progresses, players can add carriages and upgrades to their train, increasing its capacity and efficiency.

**Pro Tip:** Focus on building a balanced train with a mix of cargo and passenger carriages. This will give you more flexibility in scoring points.

### Delivering Cargo and Passengers

The core of Isle of Trains: All Aboard is delivering cargo and passengers to various destinations on the board. Players must carefully plan their routes and manage their resources to maximize their deliveries.

**Funny Moment:** During one game, a player loaded up their train with passengers but forgot to leave room for cargo. Their train looked like a clown car, and the resulting laughter was only matched by their frantic attempts to rectify the situation.

### Upgrading Your Train

Upgrading your train is essential for success. Players can add new carriages, improve their locomotive's speed, and gain special abilities to outmaneuver their opponents.

**Criticism Alert:** Some players might find the upgrade system a bit overwhelming at first. Don't worry; you'll get the hang of it after a few rounds of trial and error.

---

## Strategic Depth

Isle of Trains: All Aboard offers a rich strategic experience. Players must balance their resources, plan their deliveries, and make tactical decisions to outsmart their opponents.

### Resource Management

Efficiently managing your cargo and passengers is crucial. Players must decide when to prioritize deliveries and upgrades, constantly adapting their strategy based on the game state.

### Route Planning

Planning your routes is another key aspect. The board features various destinations, each with different rewards and challenges. Players must navigate these routes to optimize their deliveries.

**Pro Tip:** Always keep an eye on your opponents' routes and deliveries. A well-timed disruption can throw a wrench in their plans while giving you a competitive edge.

## Comparison with Other Train Games

### [Ticket to Ride]({{ site.constants.wsib }}ticket-to-ride)

Ticket to Ride is a classic train game known for its simple mechanics and engaging gameplay. While Isle of Trains: All Aboard offers more strategic depth, Ticket to Ride is perfect for those looking for a lighter, more accessible experience.

### [Railroad Ink]({{ site.constants.wsib }}railroad-ink)

Railroad Ink focuses on route-building and dice rolling, offering a different kind of challenge compared to Isle of Trains: All Aboard. Both games share the train theme but cater to different types of players.

### [Trains]({{ site.constants.wsib }}trains)

Trains combines deck-building with route-building, offering a unique twist on the genre. Isle of Trains: All Aboard provides a more traditional train experience while still incorporating strategic elements.
