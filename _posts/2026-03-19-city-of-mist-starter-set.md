## Introduction
City of Mist is a game that makes you feel like you are stepping into a neon-lit alley where myths, legends, and questionable life choices collide. The Starter Set is the entry point, a compact bundle that promises the mood of the full game without requiring a mortgage on your bookshelf. In this review we will look at what the Starter Set actually contains, how it plays, and whether it scratches the neon-noir itch without turning your gaming group into a detective agency with caffeine addiction. Expect jokes, a few odes to old pulps, and a balanced dose of practical advice for GMs and players alike.

### What makes City of Mist special?
The core conceit is simple and delicious: ordinary people with superhuman legends living in the city, each person a walking contradiction of power and vulnerability. You are not chasing a defined hero archetype so much as you are stepping into a myth setting that uses modern life as its stage. The Starter Set codifies this by giving you ready-to-go myths and the tools to build new ones on the fly. It is a game about identity, about how the city shapes you and how you shape the city back with clues, improvisation, and a little bit of dramatic flair.

### What you should know going into a City of Mist game
If you love moody lighting, clever dialogue, and the feeling that every rooftop has a backstory, City of Mist is likely to be your jam. It leans into the idea that myths are not just in long-forgotten tomes but in the people walking through your streets every day. The Starter Set leans into that identity with a focus on quick play, a guided setup, and a few pre-generated heroes so you can start rolling dice before you finish your first round of coffee. The system favors collaborative storytelling and cinematic pacing over crunchy math, which means you will be trading tactical precision for narrative momentum. If you want a game you can run with minimal prep and a group that thrives on conspiracy vibes, this is worth a closer look.

## Box contents and first impressions
### What's inside the Starter Set
The Starter Set is designed to be a sampler tray rather than a full banquet. You should expect a compact rule booklet that covers the core mechanics in a friendly, nonspecialist voice, a handful of pre-generated detectives and mythic figures, a city map, clue tokens, and a selection of cards or sheets that support quick setup for investigators. It also often includes ready-to-play adventures that steer you through a one-shot or a short arc. The aim is to get you into the mood quickly, not to overwhelm you with dozens of advanced options on day one.

### Components: looks and feel
The production value is solid without being overly flashy. The boxes, cards, and sheets use a consistent neon-noir aesthetic: bold typography, moody blues and pinks, and an art style that nods to comic book visuals without feeling derivative. The art direction matters here because a lot of how you experience City of Mist comes from the vibe—how the art sells the idea that a city is haunted by legends as much as by crime. The larger point is that you immediately feel the difference between a typical fantasy RPG starter kit and something that wants to be a living, breathing modern mythos.

### Card and token design
Pre-gen cards bring a lot of personality to the table. They are not just stat blocks; they are personality prompts. The clues and power tokens support the investigative flow, making it easy to run through a scene while preserving the sense that you are uncovering a bigger truth beneath the city’s glitter. The Starter Set includes a reasonable number of tokens for quick play, which means you can stage a few noir scenes without rummaging through a dozen bags of tokens left over from a previous campaign. If you like organization, you will appreciate how easy it is to reset a scene and pass the baton to the next player with minimal chaos.

## How City of Mist plays in the Starter Set
### Core mechanic: the mythic-lien dice pool, explained simply
The fundamental rhythm of City of Mist is about suspense, not about raw numeric advantage. Players describe actions in terms of legends and personas that intersect with modern life. The dice mechanic is designed to be approachable: you roll a pool of six-sided dice, counting successes to determine outcomes. You will be asked to describe how your mythic identity interacts with the mundane world, and the GM will respond with a complication or a clue. The emphasis is on storytelling momentum rather than maximizing a single roll. This makes the Starter Set approachable for new players and gives a strong baseline for a scene that starts small and grows into something bigger.

### The investigation flow: clues, masks, and revelations
One of City of Mist core strengths is how it structures investigation without becoming a boardroom meeting of stats. Clues are the circulating currency that moves a group from discovery to danger to revelation. In the Starter Set you will find a system for tracking clues, experiencing a few flashbacks, and letting your characters confront the tension between their public mask and their private legend. The flow tends to be episodic, which makes one-shot games easy and a solid foundation for longer campaigns if your group wants to explore the city in more depth. The set provides a framework for this flow without forcing you into a single, rigid structure.

### Character creation and pre generated options
The Starter Set gives you a few ready-to-play heroes and villains that demonstrate how the mythic framework can be exploited for dramatic effect. These not only illustrate the rules but also reveal the tone of the game: a detective story where every clue amplifies the sense that the city itself is a living character. If you enjoy choosing a pre-gen and then personalizing it through roleplaying, you will get a satisfying entry point. If you prefer fully bespoke characters from the ground up, you will be nudged toward the core book later, but the starter materials still offer a strong sense of how the mythic identity interacts with everyday life.

### Box timing: how long before you want a variant or an expansion
In my experience, a typical starter session runs smoothly in 3 to 5 hours, depending on your group and how deeply you lean into roleplay or action. If you run it as a weekend mini-campaign, the Starter Set provides enough structure to keep you moving while you decide which longer city arc you want to chase. The pacing is built into the design, which means your table can move from urban mystery to mythic confrontation without long setup breaks. If you want a sense of a longer arc, you can slot in supplementary adventures and use the same mythic framework to connect them.

## Art, tone, and accessibility
### Mood and style
The neon-noir vibe is not just cosmetic; it informs the tone of scenes and the kinds of choices players make. The city is a character with its own moods, secrets, and temptations. The rules support scenes where mood and theme carry weight, not just fights and skill checks. If you enjoy a game that treats dialogue as currency and the city as a living antagonist, City of Mist delivers.

### Accessibility for new players
The Starter Set is designed to help players who are new to tabletop RPGs. The rulebook focuses on storytelling, with plenty of examples and prompts. The learning curve is gentle, but the game rewards players who lean into character psychology and investigative pacing. If your group loves long, sprawling worlds, you will want to supplement with the core book to access deeper mechanics. If you prefer a compact, high-velocity session, the Starter Set can stand strongly on its own.

### Artwork and layout
The layout is readable and stylish, with a focus on readability during tense scenes. The fonts are legible, the color palette a good balance of dark and vibrant. The art supports the mood without getting in the way of the game text. Overall, it is a win for players who want a product that looks like a proper modern RPG, not a throwback or a board-game aesthetic that insists on neon for the sake of neon.

## Replayability and campaign potential
### Short-form play
For a one-shot or a weekend one-shot arc, City of Mist works well. The clue economy and mythic identities encourage players to improvise, which keeps sessions feeling fresh. You can run multiple sessions with the same core characters and adjust the investigation focus as the group grows more confident in the system.

### Long-form play
If you intend to build a longer campaign, the Starter Set shows you the materials and the doors you need to open. You will want to reach for the full core book and possibly additional supplements to keep the mythic options expanding in interesting directions. The beauty of the system is that you can quickly pivot from a local city mystery to a larger city-wide conspiracy, while staying under a single mythic umbrella that your players already understand.

### Diversity of themes
The mythic framework supports stories across a wide range of themes, from personal tragedy to heroic sacrifice to social commentary. The setting is flexible enough to accommodate urban fantasy, detective fiction, or modern mythic thriller. This flexibility is one of the strongest reasons to pick up the Starter Set even if you do not intend to stay with the game forever. It gives you a sandbox with a clearly defined mood and a set of tools to carve your own path through the city.

## Quick-start play plan
### Setup and first session flow
1) Gather your players and pick a starter identity from the pre-gen set. 2) Lay out the city map and place initial clues. 3) Run the first scene focusing on atmospheric narration and character dialogue. 4) Use clue tokens to drive the investigation toward a major revelation. 5) Finish with a cliffhanger and set up the next session. The flow emphasizes mood, character choices, and the guiding question of what the city wants from its citizens.

### GM tips for smoother nights
- Start with a strong opening scene that establishes the city as a character. Create a heartbeat moment that hints at a larger conspiracy or mythic pressure.
- Use the masks as a conversation tool. Encourage players to discuss not just what their character can do, but what they tell others about themselves and why.
- Let clues accumulate and escalate. A good revelation should feel inevitable in hindsight, not stitched together by pure chance.
- Balance action with roleplay. City of Mist shines when dialogue and tension carry you through the scene, not when the room becomes a dice factory.
