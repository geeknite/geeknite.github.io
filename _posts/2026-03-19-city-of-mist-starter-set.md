## What makes City of Mist special?
City of Mist trades typical fantasy power cards for legends wearing human skin. Ordinary people become conduits for mythic identity, and the city itself feels like a living entity with moods, secrets, and the occasional inconvenient prophecy. The Starter Set distills that atmosphere into a handful of pre-gen heroes, a few mythic frameworks, and a toolkit to spark improvisation rather than memorize a stat block. At its core, the game is about identity, perception, and how the city politely heckles you into becoming a letter in a larger mythic alphabet.

- Myths in modern life: your power comes from a legend you carry, not a hero you pretend to be. 
- Masks and clues: what people see and what the city masks you as. 
- Collaborative storytelling: narrative momentum is currency, not the sum of modifiers. 
- Cinematic pacing: scenes build toward revelations that feel earned, not tacked on for flavor.

If you love moody ambiance, witty banter, and the feeling that every rooftop hides a backstory, City of Mist is your jam. If you crave crunchy combat math, you’ll want to look elsewhere or at least keep the core book at arm’s length from the punch bowl of your first sessions.

## What you should know going into a City of Mist game
If you are chasing neon glow and pulp vibes, you’ll find a friendly learning curve here. The Starter Set foregrounds mood over maxing your dice pool, and that’s by design. You’ll be rewarded for storytelling choices, character psychology, and the way you link clues to mythic identity. The system leans into collaborative storytelling with a light touch on crunchy mechanics, which makes it approachable for newcomers and a delight for players who want to improvise with a strong emotional throughline.

- Myths in the city: legends aren’t tucked away in dusty tomes; they walk among rowhouses, black coffee, and rain-slick alleys.
- Masks and identity: every detective has a public mask and a private legend; the tension between the two drives scenes.
- Clues as currency: you collect clues to unlock revelations, not to push a single character past a DPR threshold.
- Quick-start vibes: you can start rolling with pre-generated identities so you’re not assembling a character sheet during coffee breaks.

For deeper world-building, the official site and fan lore can be a gold mine, but the Starter Set stands on its own as a mood-first starter. ## Box contents and first impressions
### What’s inside the Starter Set
Think sampler platter, not banquet. The Starter Set is designed to get you into the mood fast and avoid overwhelming new players with dozens of choices. Expect a compact rule booklet, several pre-generated detectives and mythic figures, a city map, clue tokens, and quick-setup sheets that help investigators hit the ground running. There are ready-to-play mini-arcs that guide a one-shot or a short campaign arc. The emphasis is on vibe, pacing, and social play rather than exhaustive customization.

### Components: looks and feel
Production value remains consistent with City of Mist’s neon-noir aesthetic: bold typography, electric blues, hot pink accents, and a comic-book sensibility that signals modern myth rather than medieval fantasy. The art direction matters—this isn’t just decoration; it’s a guide to how the city feels as you explore it. The Starter Set nails the balance between style and readability so players aren’t squinting at diagrams while trying to remember the names of their own myths.

### Card and token design
Pre-generated cards are far from dry stat blocks; they are prompts to roleplay. Clues and power tokens encourage investigative momentum and scene pacing. The tokens are compact and functional, letting you stage noir moments without rummaging through a bag of mismatched bits. It’s simple enough for new players, yet expressive enough for a GM to nudge a scene toward a satisfying payoff.

### The architecture of a starter session
The Starter Set isn’t trying to solve a puzzle so much as to provide a mood and a map for improvisation. You get a breezy opening that treats the city as a living partner, a middle act that ratchets tension, and a final beat where revelations align with the city’s mythic heartbeat. You can adapt it to a straightforward mystery, or pivot to personal arcs for each investigator—so long as the vibe stays intact.

## How City of Mist plays in the Starter Set
### Core mechanic: the mythic-lien dice pool, explained simply
The heart of City of Mist is suspense, not raw numbers. Players describe actions through legends and personas that intersect with everyday life. The dice pool is a handful of six-sided dice; successes determine outcomes, but the emphasis is on narrative momentum. Describe how your mythic identity interacts with the mundane world, and the GM serves a complication or a clue. The result is a scene that feels cinematic rather than a grind through modifiers. The Starter Set uses this to a tee, giving beginners a clean route from idea to dramatic moment.

### The investigation flow: clues, masks, and revelations
Clues are the lifeblood of investigation in this game. They move the group from discovery to danger to revelation. The Starter Set provides a compact framework for tracking clues, weaving in flashbacks, and letting players confront the tension between the public mask and the private legend. The episodic flow makes one-shots straightforward and long-form play flexible: you can expand a city-wide conspiracy without abandoning the mythic core that your players already know.

### Character creation and pre-generated options
The set includes a handful of ready-to-play heroes and villains that illustrate how the mythic framework can be exploited for dramatic effect. This is a strength if you want quick-start roleplay anchored in strong character voice. If you crave fully bespoke characters right away, you’ll likely migrate to the core book later, but the starter materials still provide a solid sense of how mythic identities intersect with ordinary life.

### Box timing: how long before you want a variant or an expansion
In my experience, a typical starter session glides along nicely for 3 to 5 hours, with variance depending on how much players lean into roleplay or action. A weekend mini-campaign is absolutely feasible with the Starter Set, especially if you drop in a couple of supplementary adventures to bridge sessions. The pacing is embedded in the design, so the transition from urban mystery to mythic confrontation feels natural rather than forced.

## Quick-start play plan
### Setup and first session flow
1) Gather players and pick a starter identity from the pre-gen set. 2) Lay out the city map and place initial clues. 3) Run the first scene focusing on atmospheric narration and character dialogue. 4) Use clue tokens to drive the investigation toward a major revelation. 5) Finish with a cliffhanger and set up the next session. The flow emphasizes mood, character choices, and the guiding question of what the city wants from its citizens.

### GM tips for smoother nights
- Start with a strong opening scene that establishes the city as a character. Create a heartbeat moment that hints at a larger conspiracy or mythic pressure.
- Use the masks as a conversation tool. Encourage players to discuss not just what their character can do, but what they tell others about themselves and why.
- Let clues accumulate and escalate. A good revelation should feel inevitable in hindsight, not stitched together by pure chance.
- Balance action with roleplay. City of Mist shines when dialogue and tension carry you through the scene, not when the room becomes a dice factory.
