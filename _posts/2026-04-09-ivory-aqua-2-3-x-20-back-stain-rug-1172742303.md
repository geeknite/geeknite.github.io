## Maintenance and Cleaning: What It Takes to Keep It Looking Sharp
### Daily care and routine practice
Vacuuming this rug is a pleasant routine rather than a chore. The medium-density pile accepts regular vacuums without showing immediate wear or losing shape. Avoid aggressive pile-height settings that can cause the fibers to fluff up in one spot or even become frayed around the edges. A standard low-to-medium suction setting works best for most household vacuum cleaners. If you’ve got a pet that sheds in dramatic waves, you’ll appreciate the rug’s tendency to mask fur shafts between cleanings.

For minor mishaps, the standard approach works well: blot, don’t rub, with a clean white cloth and a mild detergent solution. The key is to address spills early and avoid letting liquids seep into the backing. If you find yourself dealing with a stubborn stain, test a small inconspicuous area first, then proceed with a gentle spot-cleaning method. Our internal testing summary suggests you favor enzymatic cleaners for organic stains and a vinegar-water mix for mineral-based spots, always following the product’s care guide.

### Deep cleaning and professional care
Every few months, a light, professional-cleaning pass helps maintain the rug’s color integrity and pile height. Given the rug’s backing and fiber blend, it tolerates professional low-moisture cleaning methods without shrinking or warping. The “Back Stain” label is part marketing, part reality. The backing is designed to resist typical staining that traces from the underside due to spills and moisture wicking.
