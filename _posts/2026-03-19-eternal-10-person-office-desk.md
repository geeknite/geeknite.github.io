## Ergonomics and Comfort

Let’s talk about the elephant (or maybe the keyboard) in the room: comfort. Ten people does not inherently guarantee ergonomic nirvana, but the Eternal desk aims to be adjustable and accommodating. Each workstation can be tuned within a reasonable range for height, monitor tilt, and arm reach. The monitor arms are sturdy without turning the user’s wrists into percussion instruments during a heavy typing session. The chair compatibility is, thankfully, a separate problem, because pushing a chair ten units down the desk line is not a feat to be attempted with only one person.

The desk offers an integrated cable management trough that runs along the back, which is a revolution for the modern knowledge worker who believes that cable spaghetti should be kept away from the plane of reality. In practice, you can route power, data, and that mysterious USB-C dongle that everyone suspects is a micro-USB parasite into the trough, out of sight until someone asks, where did my laptop charger go? Then you show them the glorious gullet of the trough and the room cheers (or maybe only the IT guy does, but that’s a victory).

Pro-tip: if you’re designing an office with ten people, you’ll want to define a sit-stand rhythm. The Eternal desk supports height variation, but if you intend to alternate between four standing people and six seated folks across a shift, you’ll need to coordinate with the schedule gods. The moral: coordinate, coordinate, coordinate.

- Always map your team’s typical workflows before finalizing spacing. If most people need two screens, ensure they can access them without cranking their necks.

- Consider acoustic options if your team is prone to long, unmuted conversations that drift into conference-room territory.
