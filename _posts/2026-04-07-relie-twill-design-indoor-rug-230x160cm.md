## Durability and Maintenance: Keeping It Looking Sharp
### Durability in Daily Life
Durability is the word of the day. The Relie Twill rug is designed to withstand heavy foot traffic without losing its luster. ### Cleaning and Care Tips
Cleaning is where rugs either earn their hero cape or reveal their villain origin story. The Relie Twill rug is designed for straightforward maintenance. Vacuum regularly to remove surface dust and debris. For spills, blot with a clean, absorbent cloth rather than rubbing, which can drive stains deeper into the fibers. If stains do appear, treat them promptly with a mild detergent solution; test on an inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. For more stubborn stains or semi-frequent cleaning, a professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months can help maintain the rug’s appearance without sacrificing its weave. We also recommend avoiding direct sunlight for long periods, as prolonged exposure can cause slight fading over time—think of it as sun aging, but with less drama and more responsible adult behavior.

If you’re a gadget person who loves to automate everything, you might wonder about smart cleaning devices. A robot vacuum can handle the daily grime, while a targeted spot-cleaner can address spillage edges that venture toward the boundary between couch legs and coffee table. The rug’s weave handles occasional snagging from stray toy cars or the odd charging cable with surprising restraint; just remind the kids that cables are for tech, not for dramatic rug wrestling.

### Maintenance as a Lifestyle Choice
Maintenance is not sexy, but it’s necessary. The Relie Twill rug invites you to adopt a routine without turning your home into a cleaning dojo. A simple weekly vacuum, a quarterly deep-clean routine if you’re in a high-traffic home, and a quick spot-clean as soon as you notice something spill-worthy will keep the rug looking fresh for years. A well-maintained rug can outlive a few trend cycles, which is nice if you like the idea of stability in a world full of moving furniture and ever-changing color palettes.
