## Overview and specs you actually care about

The DGS-1100-26MP slides into the category of smart managed switches that are not flashy like a gaming router but are arguably more useful in a real office environment. Here are the highlights you should know:

- 26 ports total: typically 24 PoE+ ports plus 2 uplink ports (gigabit) that can be used as copper or SFP depending on the model revision. This makes it ideal for placing IP cameras, VoIP phones, and wireless access points where you actually need power.
- PoE budget: generous enough to power a practical set of devices without hunting for extra adapters. In the ballpark of several hundred watts, though exact figures depend on port usage and device power draw. Always check the official spec sheet for the precise budget before you start sketching your power plan on the whiteboard.
- Layer 2 feature set: VLANs, QoS (including port-based and 802.1p), LACP/port-channel, IGMP snooping, storm control, and basic security features. It is not a Layer 3 router, but it is not shy about keeping your traffic organized at the edge.
- Management: Web GUI accessible from a browser, plus support for CLI for power users. It also includes features typical of the Smart Managed line such as SNMP, RMON, and syslog for keeping your IT diary honest.
- Physical form factor: rack-mountable and/or desktop-friendly, with a sturdy feel and a front panel that promises not to judge your cable management choices—at least not out loud.

## Setup tips and best practices

- Plan your VLANs before you plug everything in. It saves time and preserves your mental health. A clean segmentation map is worth its weight in labeling tape.
- Use LACP for uplinks if you have more than one core path. It prevents single points of failure and gives you the feeling of running a real enterprise network without the expense of one.
- Enable QoS for critical services (voice, video conferencing) to ensure bandwidth is reserved when the network gets busy.
- Regularly check PoE budget usage. If you see a bunch of devices near the budget limit, consider upgrading the uplink or rebalancing devices across ports.
- Keep firmware up to date. It’s not glamorous, but it reduces the risk of vulnerabilities and improves stability in feature-rich hardware like this.
