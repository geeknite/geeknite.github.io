## Age groups and sizing: 5 age brackets in one bag

The Aspire Junior Plus set is marketed as accommodating five age groups. In practice, that often means club length and weight tuning within the same family of clubs. Here is a typical allocation you might encounter:

- Age Group 1: Ages 4-6 (height roughly 3'7" to 3'9"), length tuned for short, enthusiastic swings.
- Age Group 2: Ages 7-9 (about 3'9" to 4'2"), balance and grip size adjusted for growing hands.
- Age Group 3: Ages 9-11 (about 4'2" to 4'8"), mid-length clubs with a little more weight.
- Age Group 4: Ages 12-14 (about 4'9" to 5'1"), longer clubs and slightly stiffer shafts.
- Age Group 5: Ages 15-18 (about 5'1" to 6'0"), near-adult feel with premium components.

Note that the actual height and swing speed vary, so the best practice is to measure from wrist to floor when standing naturally and compare to the club length chart in your owner's manual. If your child falls between sizes, it's usually better to err on the shorter side and gradually move up as they demonstrate control and speed.

A quick aside on what you should be measuring: the child’s height could be misleading; the main driver is swing speed and control. A taller kid who still has a baby guard can benefit from shorter clubs that promote contact rather than raw distance.
