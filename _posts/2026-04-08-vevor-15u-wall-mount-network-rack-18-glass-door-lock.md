## Installation and Setup
Setting up the VEVOR 15U wall mount cabinet is approachable even if you are not a professional network engineer. The wall mounting process is the critical step here; you want a secure anchor into studs or robust wall anchors depending on your wall type. The kit typically includes mounting screws and brackets to simplify the process. Here are the typical steps you would follow, along with Geeknite style tips to keep things fun and safe:

1. Locate studs and plan the mounting height. You want the cabinet at a comfortable eye level to avoid excessive bending while plugging in devices. If you have a ceiling light that likes to flicker when you install things, you may want to do the mounting on a dry, well-lit day, preferably after coffee.
2. Use a level to ensure the cabinet sits straight. A skewed rack is more than a cosmetic issue; it can cause doors to rub and cables to irritate your sense of order.
3. Attach the wall brackets to the studs using the included hardware. Do not skip this step; a wobbly rack is not a badge of character, it is a hazard.
4. Slide in your 19 inch equipment rails and fasten with screws. Make sure you leave a bit of space for cable management and air flow. Overpacking the rack is a common mistake that leads to heat buildup and the occasional heroic sigh when you realize you forgot the patch cables you just bought.
5. Mount the glass door and ensure the lock engages properly. Test the door operation before loading gear to avoid having to disassemble and remount after discovering the door won’t close.
6. Route cables with care. Use cable ties or Velcro straps to keep power and data lines separate where possible. The goal is clean airflow and easy future maintenance rather than a spaghetti sculpture that would make a medieval torturer blush.
