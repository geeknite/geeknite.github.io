Design and Quality
The sculpts are bold, the textures are dramatic, and the paintwork carries the moody vibe you expect from a Green Horde faction—an ominous mix of fungus, moss, and green fire. The Abomination is the star here: a hulking mass of limbs that makes your survivors feel teeny-tiny and your table feel temporarily hazardous. It’s the kind of mini that begs to be placed in a dramatic pose, preferably while someone says something heroic and totally-not-reckless.

The other bosses bring variety: each has a unique silhouette to help players track threats at a glance. The base sizes are consistent with Zombicide standards, so you won’t have to awkwardly wedge them into standard map templates. Assembly is straightforward for players who enjoy a little hobby time; the connection points are generous, and the fit tends to be snug enough to avoid accidental disassembly mid-game. If you’re new to painting, you’ll find this line forgiving—the greens read well in layers, and the cracks and runes aren’t overly crisp to require a microbrush. If you’re a veteran painter, you’ll appreciate the opportunity to push texture and weather onto armor and flesh without feeling like you’re painting a model from a different decade.

Cards, Rules, and How It Changes the Game
The Boss Cards introduce new goals, second winds, and the occasional you forgot to bring a ranged weapon moment. The Abomination’s cards add chaos, making it harder to predict the encounter, which is exactly what boss fights should feel like: chaotic, cinematic, and occasionally a little unfair if the dice decide to stage a riot. The overall effect is to make the players coordinate more deliberately, drawing on each others’ strengths and, crucially, avoiding the temptation to go all-in with a single heroic push.

Gameplay Integration
If you’ve built your Black Plague nights around the safe rhythm of zombie waves, this pack shakes things up in a delightful, terrifying way. Bosses require you to pivot from kill everything that moves to control the space, manage hazards, and watch for special boss triggers. It’s less about speed and more about tempo—how you pace the team’s moves across the map, when to commit to a room, and how to corner a monstrous foe without getting stuck in a corner yourself.

Paint and Assembly Tips
- Plan your colors before you touch the brush. Greens that lean toward moss and olive read as Green Horde, while a dash of teal or blue can give the Abomination a more otherworldly feel.
- Dry brushing is your best friend for quick texture; use a light gray for bones and stone, then a touch of green wash to pull the recesses into shadow.
- Use a sturdy plinth or base for the Abomination. It’s top-heavy, and the last thing you want is a dramatic tabletop fall during a heated moment of the game.
- If you want to keep the figures safe during the next move, magnetize the base or use a foam tray to transport them between games.

Table Rules and Thematic Scenarios
The pack includes scenario cards or references that vary the experience. Some scenarios emphasize escape routes and siege dynamics, while others lean into a narrative where a single well-placed trap can tilt the odds in your favor. Difficulties and Balancing
Like any expansion that adds big monsters to a co-op game, you’ll see a shift toward team coordination and resource management. Boss encounters are less forgiving than basic zombie waves; you’ll need to allocate survivors to roles (tank, shooter, medic) and coordinate movement between rooms to minimize the risk of a catastrophic boss attack. The Green Horde’s presence also invites players to lean into terrain features: walls, doors, and chokepoints become critical decision points rather than mere obstacles.

Comparison with Other Boss Expansions
If you’ve got other Zombicide expansions, you’ll likely notice a distinct flavor with this pack. The Abomination is a classic thrill, but the Green Horde’s boss rogues bring a different vibe than the City or Dark World boss sets. In terms of scale, the Abomination sits in a similar weight class to other large bosses in the Black Plague lineup, which means it can be a satisfying capstone to a session that has earned a victory coffee and a nap.

Strategy Recipes and Example Turns

Common Issues and Troubleshooting

If a mini topple happens mid-play, declare a pause, reset the piece, and resume with the group’s permission. The important thing is to keep the noise down, not to break the image of a heroic team on the brink of collapse.
