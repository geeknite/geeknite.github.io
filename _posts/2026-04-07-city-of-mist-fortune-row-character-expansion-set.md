## The new characters and their vibe

Fortune Row adds a small crew of characters designed to be flavorful blends of street wisdom and mythic resonance. Each new persona provides a distinct approach to the core activities of the game, from social maneuvering to up close and personal interactions with the cities hidden currents. Here is a quick tour of the cast and what they bring to a party that has already learned to talk and argue with the best of them.

### Velvet Oracle

A character who thrives on ambiguous signals and uses fortune as both shield and blade. The Velvet Oracle turns luck into a resource and uses it to shape outcomes in scenes where the stakes feel personal and immediate. This is a character that rewards players who enjoy weaving rumors into actual scenes, letting the group feel like an urban myth is waking up in the room. The Oracle challenges you to think in terms of probability, symbolism, and narrative timing rather than pure raw power.

### Gutter Knight

A street level hero who is just as comfortable negotiating a truce as they are throwing a punch. The Gutter Knight excels in close quarters and can accelerate the pace of a scene by creating opportunities for dramatic reveals. If your group enjoys action with a moral twist, this character gives you a strong backbone for high stakes scenarios that still feel grounded in the cities messy reality.

### Silk Syndicate Liaison

A master of information and influence, the Silk Syndicate Liaison is built for social play and careful planning. This character thrives on building networks and manipulating narrative levers through careful relationships. If you enjoy the political thriller vibe of City of Mist, this persona expands your options for scene framing and hostage negotiations that feel cinematic rather than contrived.

### Night Clerk

An unassuming presence with a knack for turning quiet moments into dramatic shifts in momentum. The Night Clerk specializes in time based mechanics and can pull off story flips most players would not expect. This is a subtle but potent addition that rewards players who plan several steps ahead while maintaining a sense of humor about their own plans failing spectacularly when fate intervenes.

Together these new characters provide a broad spectrum of approaches. They invite players to experiment with masks and arcs that might not have fit neatly into existing character rosters, opening up new angles for a group that likes to chase a slowly unspooling mystery rather than a series of fast wins.

## Fortune Row mechanics in practice

If you already know the core Fortune engine from City of Mist, Fortune Row slides into the same rhythm with a measured ease. If you are new to Fortune, the concept is simple but potent: a deck of events and modifiers that interacts with the narrative of the scene and the overall campaign. A Fortune Row card usually offers a concrete reward for a successful test and a consequence that can ripple across future scenes. The goal is to create a sense that the city is actively shaping your character while you shape the city back in return.

This expansion does not overwhelm you with new rules. It instead adds a suite of cards and a handful of tokens that feel integrated rather than tacked on. The new mechanics often reward players who lean into story first and mechanic second. If you prefer a game night where your goals are driven by cinematic moments and character development, Fortune Row will feel like a natural extension of your preferences. If you prefer to optimize a single character and push for the most efficient path to victory every session, you may need to adjust your expectations accordingly.

The balance approach here is thoughtful. The new effects are potent enough to matter without becoming game breaking. In practice, you will see scenes hinge on a fortune event or two, creating moments that are memorable but not unfair. The design team has done a good job of calibrating the power level so that new options feel meaningful but not game overshadowing. The result is a product that can sustain long campaigns without making the base set feel stale.

## Sample night outline and GM tips

If you want to run a memorable Fortune Row night, a simple three act structure works well. Start with a brief scene that introduces a local complication tied to a Fortune Row card. The second act dives into a confrontation or a tense negotiation fueled by a Fortune Row event. The final act resolves the immediate plot while planting a seed for a longer arc that will carry into future sessions. The key is to let the new cards alter the tempo and to keep the NPCs polygonal rather than one note. The new characters invite players to improvise and adapt, which is exactly what makes City of Mist sing.

GM tips to smooth the curve:
- Keep a quick reference sheet handy for the new card types. The faster you can tell players what a card does, the more fluid the table stays.
- If a Fortune Row card creates a major shift, give players a couple of minutes to mark up their character arcs. A brief overlap of narration helps everyone stay aligned and engaged.
- Don’t overdo it. Fortune Row shines when used sparingly. A few well placed fortune moments in a session can feel like a season finale in miniature.
