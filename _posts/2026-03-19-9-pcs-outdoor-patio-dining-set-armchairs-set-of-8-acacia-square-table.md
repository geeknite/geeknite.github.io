## Setup, Assembly, and Initial Maintenance

### Maintenance 101
Outdoor wood needs a little upkeep to keep that glow alive. After your first season, consider applying a protective sealant or light oil to rehydrate the surface and fend off water marks. It’s not a chemistry experiment; it’s more like a spa treatment for wood. Wipe spills promptly, and use coasters or placemats for hot dishes. If you live in a climate with harsh sun, you may want to cover the set when not in use to prevent color fading. The Acacia wood will naturally darken with time, which is part of its character—like aging a fine character in a video game knight. If you store cushions separately, you’ll reduce moisture risks and prolong their life.

As for cushions (if your kit includes or has optional ones), ensure they’re weather-resistant and quick-drying. If you add cushions later, choose ones with UV-stable fabrics to prevent the fabric from turning into a sunburnt mesh of colors after a couple of summers.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity Tips

- Clean regularly: A damp microfiber cloth is often enough for routine cleaning. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the wood finish.
- Protect the surface: Consider applying a thin sealant every year or two (depending on exposure) to keep the color rich and protect against moisture.
- Cushion care: If cushions are included, store them indoors during heavy rain or when not in use for extended periods. Choose cushions with UV-stable fabrics to minimize fading.
- Storage strategy: In winter or off-season, store the furniture in a shed or garage if possible. If space is tight, use waterproof covers designed for outdoor furniture to extend life.

By following these steps, you’ll maximize the set’s lifespan and keep your patio looking like a tasteful getaway rather than a DIY project that forgot to hit the “delete” button on time.
