## How to choose the right junior set for your 11 to 13 year old

Here are quick tips to ensure you pick the right kit for your child:

- Measure height and wrist-to-floor for each potential club length. A good fit is when the child can stand upright and still make contact near the ball without overreaching.
- Prioritize forgiveness for early learners. A slightly longer bag and a driver with a larger face can accelerate confidence on the course.
- Check grip size. Smaller hands need tighter and more controlled grips to avoid fatigue and poor club control.
- Consider future growth. If your child is in the middle of a growth spurt, you might choose a kit with a little extra length that can adjust downward as they grow.
- Look for light overall weight. Lighter clubs reduce fatigue during practice and make the learning process more enjoyable.

## Maintenance and care tips for longevity

- Clean clubs after practice: wipe the clubface and grooves to keep spin consistency intact.
- Dry and store properly: ensure humidity is controlled and keep the bag off carpet to prevent musky smells.
- Protect the finish: use headcovers and avoid dragging clubs across rough pavement.
- Check for loose hardware: tighten screws, verify grip tape, and keep the stand functioning smoothly.

Following these tips will extend the life of the set and keep your child swinging with enthusiasm rather than frustration.
