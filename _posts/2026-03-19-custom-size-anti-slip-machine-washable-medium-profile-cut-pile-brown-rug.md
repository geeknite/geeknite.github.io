## Maintenance and care

Care guidelines: machine wash cold on gentle cycle; avoid chlorine bleach; tumble dry low or air dry.

Detailed steps:
- Before washing, shake out loose debris.
- Pretreat any heavy stains with a stain remover that is safe for synthetic fibers.
- Use a mild detergent, no fabric softener.
- Wash on cold, gentle cycle; do not use hot water as it may shrink or warp the backing.
- Dry on a low heat setting; remove promptly to prevent wrinkles. For best results, lay flat to dry to preserve shape.
- Do not dry in direct sunlight for too long to prevent color fade.

Edge care: If edges begin to curl, you can trim them and re-sew with a simple seam. The backing adhesives are designed to maintain adhesion after multiple washes.

## Tech and science behind anti-slip backing

Static friction vs. dynamic friction: the rug's backing aims to maximize static friction against smooth floor surfaces. The material choice (rubberized or latex film) creates a micro-rough interface that grips floors when stationary and resists sliding when force is applied, like a tiny, silent handshake between rug and floor. Real-world results vary with floor texture, humidity, and the presence of spills. In lab-ish terms, the more surface area the backing has in contact with the floor, the higher the initial resistance to movement. In practical terms, this means fewer oops moments when you race to grab the last slice of pizza.
