## The characters: Carson, Gilly, Igor & Matt
### Carson
Carson, the team’s improvised medic, arrives with a belt full of bandages and a calm that says, “I’ve seen worse than a pile of undead pastry.” In game terms, Carson’s healing options encourage mightier turns where you balance pulling teammates back from the brink with continuing pressure on the horde. The art depicts Carson as the kind of person who patches up even a zombie with the same care you’d give a goldfish. The humor lands in both the visual and text flavor, giving the character a memorable personality without breaking the game’s balance.

### Gilly
Gilly is the wily scavenger who would rather flip a zombie in a losing skirmish than risk losing a minute in the supply run. The artwork shows a scarf, goggles, and a look that says, “If I survive this, I deserve a cookie.” In terms of gameplay, Gilly’s vibe translates to reconnaissance and loot-drops, with light risk-reward that makes low-stakes gambles feel like cinematic popcorn moments rather than reckless chaos.

### Igor
Igor feels like the group’s practical scientist, a thinker who fumbles through a lab of improvised devices while the world outside stumbles into chaos. The Kovalic style gives Igor a scientist’s swagger and a cartoonish spark in the eye, the kind that says, “If this gadget doesn’t explode, I’ll pretend it’s part of the plan.” Igor’s gadget-focused cards introduce clever traps and device-based effects that reward clever placement and timing rather than brute force alone.

### Matt
Matt is the sturdy, stubborn, and affectionate frontline presence—the kind of hero who makes a stand and then asks, “What’s next?” The art leans into a classic action-hero vibe, and the rules text supports simple, reliable decisions: push into the closest zombie, shield your teammates, and somehow look cool while doing it.

## Artwork and theme
### Visual language
Kovalic’s signature style—a bold line, exaggerated expressions, and a wink of humor—shines through these four survivors. The cards’ layout and the characters’ poses make it easy to identify each survivor’s vibe at a glance, which is a big win when the action cards are dense and you’re juggling multiple turns. The miniatures carry the same energy; they’re not hyper-realistic, and that’s exactly the point. The cartoonish dynamic adds flavor without sacrificing proportionality or tabletop readability.

### How art informs gameplay
In Zombicide, the link between art, flavor text, and mechanics matters more than you’d expect. Pair Carson’s healing focus with Matt’s frontline presence, and you’ll find your party’s resilience increases without having to rely on brute force alone. Gilly’s scavenging options create an incentive to move across the board, which in turn invites you to consider line-of-sight and terrain dynamics more carefully. Igor’s gadgets invite experimentation with trap layouts and device-assisted tactics. This set leans into player creativity while preserving the core loop of clearing space, collecting gear, and surviving the onslaught.

## Gameplay impact and replayability
### Do these characters change your strategy?
The survivors bring a distinctive flavor to the table. Carson’s emphasis on healing nudges teams toward slightly longer turns as players decide whether to push for a kill or pull someone back for recovery. Gilly’s scavenging increases loot variety, injecting a bit more risk-reward into resource management. Igor’s gadgets encourage experimentation with trap layouts and device-based strategies, expanding the tactical toolbox beyond the base game. Matt’s frontline role pairs nicely with a shield-wall approach, enabling you to hold territory when the horde pushes with renewed vigor.

From a pure tactical standpoint, it doesn’t overhaul Zombicide’s engine, but it adds enough variety to keep mid-to-late-game decisions fresh. The new survivor cards are flavorful and offer micro-synergies with existing gear, which means your campaigns can spin off into small side quests that feel organic rather than tacked-on expansions.

Balance is a moving target depending on which base game you’re running.
