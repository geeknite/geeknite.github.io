Design and construction: materials and geometry

Overall length and blade width aren’t just numbers; they shape your leverage and control in field scenarios. The tanto design is optimized more for piercing than slicing, a trait that can be advantageous in certain scenarios where the blade needs to breach thick materials. Durability and maintenance

Because we’re dealing with stainless steel and a rubbery handle, you’ll want to avoid the corrosion trap known to plague cheaper stainless mixes: salty environments, wet uniforms, and prolonged exposure to acids. We recommend dry storage after use, occasional oiling of the blade (food-grade mineral oil is fine if you’re worried about food contact during prep tasks), and a simple wipe-down after heavy wet tasks. The handle’s flexibility is a feature during use; it provides grip, but you’ll want to inspect the integral tang for any signs of loosening if you’ve been putting this blade through the full-bore test suite.

Maintenance tips:

- Keep the blade clean and dry after use.
- Lightly oil stainless surfaces to avoid micro-corrosion (especially near the edge).
- Routine stropping will help retain edge integrity during extended use.
- Check screws and fittings during periodic maintenance to ensure nothing has loosened with use.
