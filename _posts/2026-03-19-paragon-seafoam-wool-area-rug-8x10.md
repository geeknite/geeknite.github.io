## Performance and Maintenance
### Everyday Use and Comfort

### Cleaning and Stain Management
Wool is naturally resilient but not invincible. Spills should be tackled promptly by blotting with a clean, dry cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible. Avoid rubbing, which can push the spill deeper into the fibers. For organic stains, a mild detergent solution and a light blotting can clear up many issues; avoid harsh solvents that can degrade wool. For more stubborn spots, consider professional cleaning at the recommended interval. The goal with wool is to maintain the integrity of the fibers while keeping the color intact. A quick weekly vacuum (with a suction that does not pull the pile flat) helps keep dust and grit from dulling the rug’s texture. 

Rotation is your friend. By rotating the rug 180 degrees every few months, you even out wear patterns caused by sun exposure and furniture blocks. This little ritual keeps the rug looking vibrant longer and avoids the dreaded diagonal wear lines that scream when guests step into the room. 

### Sunlight, Pets, and Human Factors
Direct sunlight will gradually lighten any color, including seafoam, so if your lounging spot basks in a lot of sun, consider window treatments or a strategic room layout to reduce fading. Pets can leave hair and occasional claws, but wool handles claw marks relatively well compared to more delicate fibers. If your pet spends a lot of time on the rug, keep a basic trim of loose hair and a regular vacuum schedule to maintain the plushness.

## Rugs, Pads, and Longevity
A quality rug pad is often the unsung hero of rug longevity. It reduces bunching, protects the rug backing, and provides extra cushioning. For carpeted rooms, choose a pad with a slightly lower profile to prevent a lumpy transition between rug and carpet.
