### What’s in the pack
The Special Anniversary 2020 sleeves are designed to celebrate a milestone while still being practical enough for daily play. A standard pack contains 100 sleeves sized to fit the DBS card dimensions (the usual 2.5 inch by 3.5 inch format). The outer art features the anniversary emblem in gold foil, a splash of Dragon Ball flair, and a neon glow that would make a Dragon Ball hero look like a walking trophy. The back of the sleeve usually bears a simple but sturdy surface to keep your cards protected, with a slightly textured finish that helps your fingers grip and shuffle without turning your deck into a squeaky ball of chaos.

Inside, you’ll find a reinforced inner layer designed to reduce friction and minimize surface wear on card fronts. The material is a familiar polypropylene blend that sits somewhere between “solid and flexible” and “not so slippery you’ll lose your cards in a vacuum.” On the practical side, they’re designed to slide smoothly in and out of standard deck boxes, even during the heat of a tournament sprint. If you’re the kind of player who likes to keep a tight, professional-looking table, the 100-count supply is usually enough to sleeve a few decks, with a few packs left for experimenting with alternate art and favorite characters.

As with many official sleeves, there’s a design choice here: the outer surface is richly printed with a blend of gradients and metallic accents, while the inner layer remains plain enough to let the card’s face read clearly. The result is a balance between “wow factor” and “deck readability.” This matters when you’re trying to glance at your hand quickly while your opponent is contemplating whether your plan includes a sudden Spirit Bomb or a well-timed counter.

### Design and Artwork
This is where the product earns its stripes. The outer sleeve demonstrates the anniversary branding with a gold foil accent that catches the light the moment you tilt the sleeve toward the play area. The color palette leans into red, gold, and a dash of electric blue—classic Dragon Ball energy vibes that still feel contemporary. The artwork doesn’t try to reproduce a specific card image; instead, it crafts a mood: the feeling of a milestone moment you can carry to every match rather than a mere decoration.

The sleeve’s surface is crisp and clean; you’ll notice it immediately if you’ve ever shuffled with less premium sleeves and felt the drag. The edges are nice and square, which reduces the risk of corner curling and misalignment as you flip cards. This matters in high-pressure environments where a slight bend in the sleeve can be enough to reveal a telltale arc of foil or a weak spot in the back. The net effect is that the sleeves look purposeful and feel sturdy enough to survive the occasional accidental spill or a rogue dice throw.

As for the art on the back—the less visible side, if you’re not one of those players who study back designs for “hidden tells”—it remains visually cohesive with the front. There’s no jarring mismatch between what you see when you pull a card and what you see when you’re staring at your opponent’s face across the table. This is a nice little design alignment that adds to the overall premium feel.

### Materials and Build Quality
In terms of build, these sleeves land in the mid-to-upper range of standard protective sleeves. They’re not the thickest out there, but they’re not the flimsiest either. Expect roughly 100 to 120 microns of thickness—a sweet spot that offers protection without making the deck prohibitively stiff to shuffle. The outer surface has a moderate gloss that catches the light nicely, but not so much that it blinds you during a critical reveal. The inner surface is smooth, designed to reduce friction against card fronts as you cycle through your deck.

One practical note for players who frequently arrange their cards in color-coordinated stacks: the inner surface is optimized to minimize sticking and to reduce wear, so your foil accents won’t reveal themselves to the world after the 20th shuffle. The sleeves maintain their shape after repeated use, resisting the common “warped corner” problem that plagues some budget sleeves. If you’ve ever seen a poorly stored sleeve with a persistent bend, you’ll appreciate the design here: it’s a simple, functional solution that doesn’t rely on gimmicks to do the job.

### Practicality in Play
In real-life play, the sleeves perform as intended: they protect the cards, don’t add excessive bulk to the deck, and offer a smooth shuffle experience. The deck’s overall thickness remains comfortable within a standard 60-card build, and you won’t feel like you’ve strapped a small brick to your cards. For players who like to triple-sleeve—one on top of another for extra protection—these sleeves are compatible with the common inner sleeves you might already be using. The inner surface reduces friction, so those outer sleeves don’t cause excessive resistance during long games.

One minor caveat: if you’re the type who likes to play with very glossy sleeves in an extremely bright play area, you might notice a little more glare on certain points of the front art as you rotate the deck. It’s an aesthetic thing rather than a functional flaw, but it’s worth noting if your play area relies on precise lighting for card recognition. Most players won’t notice, though, once the match begins and the energy from the table drowns out the glare anyway.

### How to Care and Store
Maintenance is simple. Keep sleeves out of direct sunlight for extended periods to avoid color fading. The gold foil, while striking, can be susceptible to micro-abrasions if you rub the surface aggressively against rough objects. For storage, a simple deck box or binder designed for sleeves will keep them organized and protected, and a silica gel packet in the storage area can help maintain dryness. If you’re traveling to a tournament, consider a padded bag to protect the sleeves from bending in transit, as well as any dice or other accessories that could press into the sleeves.
