## Gameplay Dynamics: When the Cardboard War Breaks Out

The Third Expansion tends to slow down or speed up games depending on the players, the cards drawn, and the chaos multiplier of your group. For new players, expect a couple of rounds to feel chaotic but manageable; for veterans, the new prompts are a trap door to a whole new reservoir of punchlines, so you will enjoy the freedom to improvise more elaborate jests.

### House rules and recommended setups

A lot of groups love to implement house rules to maximize banter. Here are a few popular options that align well with this expansion:
- The Meme Freeze: when the fastest reader is uncertain about a black card, the opposing team gets to propose a replacement prompt for comedic effect. It adds a little strategic tension to the pure randomness.
- The Context Rule: players can provide quick, non-spoiler context behind their white cards if it helps set up a pun. This keeps a few players from just dumping an off-kilter white card and walking away.
- The Pop Culture Indicator: place special tokens on certain cards that reference specific franchises; when triggered, players must explain the context to non-fans at the table. This creates an educational moment, with humor as the main outcome.

## Critiques and Limitations

No expansion is perfect for every group. The Third Expansion doubles down on meme culture, which means it can feel niche if your audience lacks familiarity with certain references. It also means that some prompts may rely on a shared context that newer players don’t yet have. A few players may also push the boundaries in a way that makes others uncomfortable. The best remedy is to use pre-game boundaries and a quick check-in, then let the comedic machine roll.

Also, as the line between jokes and offensiveness blurs more often in CAH territory, it is wise to have a policy. The policy can be something like a soft boundary: you are allowed to joke, but you respect the comfort levels of your friends and set a safe limit. If you want a more formal approach, discuss with your players what is off-limits before you begin. The expansion then becomes a tool to build laughs rather than a source of tension.
