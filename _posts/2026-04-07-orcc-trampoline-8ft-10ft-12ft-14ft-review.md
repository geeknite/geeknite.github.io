## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping the Jump Bouncy
Your trampoline will be in the sun, rain, and occasional windstorm. A little maintenance goes a long way toward keeping it in good shape. Here are practical tips to maximize lifespan and minimize the post-punishment look.

- Regular inspection: Check the frame for rust, the springs for signs of wear, and the net for tears. Small issues today prevent big problems tomorrow.
- Clean routine: Wipe down the frame and mat with a mild soap solution and water. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade padding or mats. Rinse and let it air dry before stowing away or covering to prevent mildew.
- Weather protection: If you’re in a climate with heavy rainfall, consider a weather cover during the off-season. If you leave it uncovered, a gentle tarp and proper anchoring can prevent wind from turning the unit into a kite with a trampoline addiction.
- Regular tightening: Springs can loosen over time. Check for even tension and adjust as needed. A loosely tensioned mat can cause unbalanced bounce that ruins both the experience and your knees.
- Storage considerations: If you store the unit for an extended period, detach and store components properly to prevent rust and warping. For many households, this is not practical in a suburban yard, so make peace with a set it-and-forget it approach—just check it seasonally.
