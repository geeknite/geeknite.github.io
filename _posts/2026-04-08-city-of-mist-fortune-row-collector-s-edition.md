City of Mist Fortune Row Collector's Edition arrives like a caped crusader stepping onto a neon street. You can feel the city breathe through the packaging, a plush slipcase hugging a sturdy box, gold foil catching the light with a wink that says you know exactly what you are doing. This is not a casual add on; this is a statement piece. The base game is a clever blend of noir detective storytelling and mythic power scaling, and Fortune Row pushes that concept into a more structured, narrative heavy expansion with enough new bits to keep your table from getting bored during the third session.

What is Fortune Row really adding? If you know the base game, you know that you control a Mask, a persona that hides a real identity beneath a dramatic vanity plate of a name, a power, and a role in the city. Fortune Row expands the city map to include a new district with a row of storefronts that carry secrets as heavy as a thundercloud. It introduces new identity arcs around luck, fate, and the choice to bend reality using your mask. It also offers a curated punch list of encounters that can be slotted into existing campaigns or run as a mini campaign arc of its own.

Below we will break down the experience from unboxing to closing credits, with a detour into the story potential and a verdict on value for money.

Fortune Row gameplay: what is new and how it affects your table

If you are new to City of Mist, the core engine is a dice pool mechanic where you roll a number of six-sided dice based on your Masks abilities and traits; you succeed on a certain threshold and you use these successes to resolve outcomes and unlock story beats. Fortune Row adds a few focused improvements and a couple of new levers that the storyteller can pull to heighten tension.

- A fortune track that runs through the district and that players can influence with their actions. This introduces a light resource management layer that can reward savvy players who time their exploits to catch the attention of powerful patrons. The fortune track does not overwhelm the game but it gives a sense of momentum when a hero successfully threads multiple acts together.
- Four new Masks with unique power sets, each built around a specific flavor of luck and fate. These are not just reskins; they each come with a tailored set of encounter prompts and a recommended arc to help players discover their identity in the Fortune Row environment.
- A new assets deck and a liabilities deck. These cards are designed to create tension and opportunity in equal measure. Assets give you leverage when you push into a dice pool, while liabilities introduce complications you must resolve to avoid paying a price in the city's eyes.
- New villain types who exploit fortune related weaknesses. These antagonists bring a different flavor to the city drama, forcing players to rethink their approach.
- A special district map with locations that feel cinematic and interactive. The map encourages dynamic movement and encourages players to pivot their plans when a situation in Fortune Row spirals in unexpected directions.
- Expanded rules for solo and cooperative play. Fortune Row scales nicely from two players to a full table, and there are optional mechanics that help one player pilot a whole crew of Masks with the storyteller rotating as GM.

A few concrete mechanics worth calling out

Fortune Row introduces a new flow when a scene reaches a turning point. The players can push the fortune track to tilt outcomes, trading short term relief for long term cost if they do not manage the consequences well. This creates a risk model that matches the noir vibe: every big moment has a cost attached to it.

The new Masks feel distinct but compatible with the core game. One deck is built around a character who exploits luck to bend outcomes; another is about someone who embodies fate and has to reconcile feelings of inevitability with personal choice. The third focuses on a trickster who can weave misdirection into the story, and the fourth presents a guardian who protects a secret that can unlock a whole chain of events. Each Mask brings a unique style of play and a new narrative lens through which to view the city.

Narrative potential and campaign hooks

Fortune Row shines when you lean into the storytelling aspect of City of Mist. The district map is not just a pretty picture; it is a playground for scenes that demand your attention. The encounters are structured to weave into an ongoing arc, but they also stand alone — perfect for a one shot that wants to feel cinematic and complete in a single session.

In practice, a Fortune Row run can feel like a mini arc inside the larger city. You pick a villain who has a foothold in Fortune Row, set a set of goals for the Mask crew, and then let the scene prompts generate a string of consequences that keeps players on their toes. The narrative flow is well supported by the cards that guide you toward dramatic reveals and emotional beats. The result is a campaign feel without requiring you to commit to a long campaign if time is scarce.

Solo and group play

If you are a solo player who likes to test out new mechanics, Fortune Row includes rules for one player to manage multiple Masks against a small rogues gallery. The solo mode is not a gimmick; it is a compact but complete experience that demonstrates the flavor of the new district without asking you to pretend you are playing with ghosts. For groups, Fortune Row scales to fit a mid sized table with the right storyteller pacing. You can run the new arc as a dedicated session or weave your existing Masks into the new location for fresh flavors.

How it compares to the base game and earlier expansions

Fortune Row stands on the shoulders of the base game while carving out its own niche. The base game established the neon noir mood and the concept of overlapping identities that feel larger than life yet intimately personal. Fortune Row amplifies the emotional stakes by turning luck and fate into tangible components of the engine. The Collector's Edition adds polish and a curated experience, but the core gameplay remains accessible to newcomers.

For players who already own other City of Mist content, Fortune Row acts as a gateway to a more strategic style of play without becoming a heavy grind fest. If you enjoyed the base game but found some scenarios a bit predictable, Fortune Row offers surprises that will keep your table guessing what happens next. The new assets and liabilities cards spawn more interesting choices at the table, and the new Masks provide fresh roleplaying opportunities without requiring a wholesale shift in how you approach a scene.

Accessibility and learning curve

City of Mist has always rewarded players who lean into the roleplay and storycraft, possibly more than the number of dice you roll. Fortune Row is no exception. The rulebook is clear and well organized, with color coding and quick reference cards that speed up setup. The new mechanics are introduced gradually, and there are ample examples of play to illustrate how a scene should unfold. If you are already comfortable with the base game, Fortune Row will feel like a natural extension. If you are new to City of Mist, you can still enjoy a robust experience by focusing on the story prompts first and then circling back to the mechanic layer as you gain confidence.

Notes on length, tone, and pacing

Fortune Row can be a talky, cinematic experience if you lean into the roleplay aspect. Do not expect a brisk Euro style engine with tight turns and 60 minute games. This is a narrative playground with moments of fast action and slower, character driven beats. Good pacing comes from the storyteller embracing the tone and guiding players toward the dramatic reveals that the art and cards imply. If your table defaults to short sessions, consider running a two to three act arc instead of a long campaign to maximize the impact of each session.

The final verdict

Fortune Row Collector's Edition is a celebration of the City of Mist system. It doubles down on the noir mood, the mythic flavor, and the storytelling engine. It offers a fresh district to explore, new Masks with their own arcs, and a well designed set of cards and tokens that make the table feel both cinematic and tangible. It is a product that rewards players who value immersion, narrative pacing, and tactile components. It may not blow up your follower count with a sudden surge in players, but it will absolutely elevate your local game nights.

If you already own the base game and you crave a stronger sense of place and character, Fortune Row is a strong candidate.
