## What’s in the Box and What Makes it “Customizable”
In classic UNO fashion, the base deck brings the familiar numbers, four action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild), and a Wild Draw Four for those glorious dramatic moments. The customizable variant cranks the dial by including:
- Blank or semi-blank action cards with a sticker sheet to label your own effects. Yes, you can create Double Tap Draw Four or Reverse with a Costume Change if your inner game designer wants a show.
- A small booklet of templates with suggested home rules and balance tips. Balance is what you bring to the table when someone declares Uno after your second Draw Four.
- A set of removable face stickers you can apply to individual cards without permanently altering official cards. No permanent ink crimes here.
- A few card sleeves and a cardboard divider to carve out house rules for your living room arena.
- A mini notepad for drafting house rules or comedic disclaimers about why your mischief is legally supervised by the neighborhood cat.

The big idea: you are not just playing UNO; you are designing a micro-game within a game. The sticker sheets let you annotate blank cards with your own effects, but you still can revert to the classic UNO experience whenever you want. It is a clever, low-stakes way to experiment with multiplayer dynamics while keeping the original rules accessible for newcomers.
