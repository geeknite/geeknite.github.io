## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Long-Term Care

Cleanup is simple: wipe with a damp cloth and let air dry. If you’re dealing with stubborn residue (think sticky soda spill), a mild soap and water wash will do. Avoid bleach or harsher solvents, as they might degrade the printed ink over time. After cleaning, ensure the deck is completely dry before returning it to storage. A small silica gel packet in your storage tin or plastic case helps with moisture control, especially in humid environments.

Storage matters too. Use a rigid case if you’re traveling, and keep the deck away from extreme heat or direct sunlight for extended periods. Prolonged exposure to heat can affect the plastic’s rigidity, though a casual sunny deck on a porch table isn’t likely to cause drama.

If you’re building a pool-side game station, consider dedicating a rainproof container that’s easy to carry. It makes setup faster and reduces the “is that water on the table?” panic that often accompanies poolside game nights.
