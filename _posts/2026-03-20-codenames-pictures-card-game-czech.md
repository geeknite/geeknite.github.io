## Setup and rules: getting the game going without a PhD in clueing

Codenames Pictures follows the same general blueprint as the word game. Two teams, a spymaster on each side, a grid of picture cards spread face up. The spymaster gives a one-word clue that relates to multiple pictures on the grid, and the team tries to guess which cards correspond to those hints. Each turn, players can guess multiple cards until they either hit a wrong card or run out of guesses for that turn. The goal is to reveal all of your team’s agents first while avoiding the opposing team’s agents and the dreaded assassin card.

Key differences from the word version include the reliance on imagery rather than vocabulary. This often reduces language barriers and increases the emphasis on shared cultural cues and visual interpretation. It also creates opportunities for wilder, more cinematic clues. Our group has had nights where a single clue triggers a cascade of connections that feels like a group improv session and a group therapy session wrapped into one. It is chaotic, delightful, and occasionally ridiculous in a way that only a good party game night can deliver.

Rule variations exist, such as a time-limited round or alternative scoring schemes. ## Rules variations and house rules you can actually use

You can also add a light penalties layer such as awarding extra points for creative clues that reference a team member’s inside joke.
