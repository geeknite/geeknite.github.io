## Gameplay and rules

If you’re familiar with UNO, you’ll be home in minutes. Standard UNO mechanics apply: match color or number, utilize action cards to alter turns, and declare UNO when you’re down to one card. The Puerto Rico edition preserves this familiarity, which is a big plus for players who want a themed deck without relearning the entire playbook.

What’s new:

- Gift cards: four cards that add island-flavored objectives or social interactions. They’re optional and can be left out if you want pure UNO.
- Thematic Wilds: the Wild drawings include a compass motif and a small lighthouse.

Example Gift card ideas:
- Trade Card: The active player chooses one card from another player’s hand to swap with their own (not a steal—just a swapped card, with consent of players).
- Isla Moment: The next player must tell a Puerto Rico landmark before drawing or skip if impossible due to hand size.

Isla Rule (house rule variant): If you cannot play a card, you must announce a Puerto Rico landmark; if you do, you pass until you can lay a card; otherwise, you draw one card. It’s a light educational moment that’s also funny if you pretend you’re on a guided tour.

Using Gift cards can stretch the game out a bit, but it’s a good way to break tension when you’ve got a big party or a room full of guests who want to tell their own travel anecdotes.
