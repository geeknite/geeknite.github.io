## What is Fairytale Gloom?

Fairytale Gloom is a stand-alone or combinable entry in the Gloom family that leans into fairy tale vibes while preserving the signature goal of the series: make your family the gloomiest by assembling a tableau of misfortunes and calamities for your characters, and then minimize the gloom for everyone else. The box code ATG 1332 marks it as one of Atlas Games’ contemporary entries designed to be accessible to casual players while still rewarding savvy card play from players who like to tease, trick, and narrate their way through a story.

If you’ve never played Gloom before, the concept is simple but deliciously devious: players have a hand of transparent, illustrated cards that you place to create a family line, each card adding (or subtracting) gloom points. The trick is that you want your own family to accumulate as many miserable things as possible while ensuring your opponents’ families accumulate as few as possible. Because the gloom is transparent, you can see all the misfortunes stacked on the other players’ families—unless you have a villainous sense of mercy and decide to let them suffer just a smidge more for the sake of dramatic storytelling.

Fairytale Gloom, however, replaces some of the darker spectral vibes with a fairy tale lens, letting you stage your misfortunes in a storybook world full of witches, talking wolves, and cursed shoes. The result is a game that can feel delightfully cheeky rather than outright grim, with enough storytelling room to make the table crack up or groan in just the right way.

## How to Play: Quick Start Guide

Fairytale Gloom is designed for quick rounds and easy introduction. Here’s a compact run-through, broken into bite-sized pieces to get you from zero to storyteller in under 10 minutes, even if your own story involves being late to your own bedtime:

### Setup
- Each player chooses a family silhouette and draws a starting hand (the exact number varies by the edition; in many Gloom variants, 4-5 is typical).
- Place the gloom deck within reach, along with any story or curse decks included in the set.
- Each player looks over their initial cards and can mulligan or swap a card, if your group allows it. The aim is to feel accepted by the table while quietly plotting everyone’s doom.

### On Your Turn
1. Draw a card: pick from the gloom deck or from a story deck if your version uses them. This is where you glean either a new misfortune or a tool to weave into your narrative.
2. Play a card: place a card into your tableau or onto another player’s family to claim a parable of tragedy. The lame wizard, the cursed slippers, or the misfit prince all show up here with style.
3. Narrate a moment: storytelling matters. The best tables aren’t just playing cards; they’re crafting a fairy tale with a punchline. You’ll want to weave a quick mini-story that makes the gloom feel earned rather than arbitrary.

### End of Round and End of Game
- Most Gloom games include a trigger for the end of the round or game, often when a player reaches a certain gloom threshold or when a familial line is fully revealed with misfortune completed.
- Calculate gloom for each family, add up the points, and determine the winner (usually the player with the lowest gloom wins, though your table can adopt a light-hearted variant if your group wants to tilt toward alternates).
- The Fairy Tale variant often adds bonus tallies for storytelling quality, group cooperation, and theme fidelity, which helps players who aren’t card-perfect but excel at spinning a yarn.

If you’re curious about how the scoring actually shakes out in practice, you’ll want to compare your notes with a trusted Ace Narrator or your group’s preferred house rules. The fun in Fairytale Gloom comes when your table negotiates the rules as you tell your story, not when you memorize a 12-step process.

### Quick Tips for New Players
- Focus on synergy between your cards: some cards combo for bigger negative values if you can pair them with a narrative that ties the two misfortunes together.
- Don’t be afraid to tell a silly story. The table wants to laugh and groan in equal measure; your job is to guide them there with a memorable tale.
- Keep track of other players’ families. You don’t need to memorize exact values, but you should know who’s trailing and who’s about to flip the table with a dramatic plot twist.
- Use the fairy tale motif to your advantage: a well-timed twist or a reference to a familiar story can turn a mediocre turn into a legendary moment.
