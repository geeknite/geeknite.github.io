## Specifications you can actually use

### Materials

- Face: 100 percent polypropylene with a subtle twist to create depth in the color. Polypropylene is known for stain resistance and resilience under daily traffic, which means this rug is not going to shrink after a grape juice incident. It also dries quickly after a spill, which is a definite win when you have kids, roommates, or the occasional clumsy dragon visiting your apartment.
- Backing: cotton canvas backing for extra grip and a little bit of support over low-pile surfaces. The backing is not a replacement for rug grippers, but it helps reduce the occasional shifting that leads to embarrassing rug curls around the coffee table.
- Dye: color fast, with a deep blue that avoids the notorious navy vibe of a midnight snack at 2 am. The hue plays well with both warm and cool lighting and tends to make neutrals pop without clashing with bolder accents.

### Dimensions

- Typical size options include 5 by 8 feet and 8 by 10 feet. If your room is shaped like a rectangle and your life is shaped like a chaos coordinator, choose the 8 by 10 for a more expansive, cinema-like floor presence. The 5 by 8 is ideal for smaller living rooms, apartments, or those bold attempts at a yoga mat that doubles as a statement piece.
- Pile height sits around 1/2 inch to 3/4 inch, giving you a plush feel without triggering a mortgage on the cleaning service. It balances cushion and practicality—enough to feel nice underfoot while not turning every landing into a miniature trampoline for pets and toddlers.

### Care and maintenance

- Vacuum weekly using a low suction setting and a brushless attachment to minimize fiber frizz. A standard vacuum handle works best, and you should rotate the rug occasionally to even out wear patterns.
- For spills, blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and treat with a mild detergent solution if needed. The polypropylene base stands up to moisture better than natural fiber options, so you are less likely to end up with a soggy, sad carpet after a coffee accident.
- Professional cleaning is recommended every 12 to 18 months for heavy traffic areas. The goal is to maintain color vibrancy and structural integrity without over-wetting.

## Practical care cheat sheet

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid fiber frizz and flattening of the pile.
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber damage and color migration.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear patterns in high-traffic zones.
- For stubborn stains, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in synthetic fibers and does not vacuum with a forklift.
- Use rug grippers or a non-slip pad if the rug slides on slick floors or if you have curious pets that invent new ways to rearrange living spaces.
