## How to Play: Mechanics in a Nutshell

CAH does not reinvent the wheel; it rotates it, sometimes with a grind that will remind you how tricky social boundaries can be. Here is the nutshell version:

- Each round, players draw a hand of white cards. The number varies by house rules; typical is 10 cards per player.
- The Card Czar draws a black card and reads it aloud. The prompts vary from witty to vile and back to witty again.
- Players pick one white card from their hand that best completes the prompt and submit it face down.
- The Card Czar shuffles or collects the submissions, reads them aloud, and selects a winner.
- The winner keeps the black card as a point or token, and the round continues with a new Card Czar.

The humor often comes from the juxtaposition of innocent prompts with savage responses. The 2024 update tries to preserve that tension while updating some of the references to keep non-stop surprise lurking in the margins. The mechanics remain accessible: you can teach the game in a few minutes, and then unleash chaos for the next hour or two.
