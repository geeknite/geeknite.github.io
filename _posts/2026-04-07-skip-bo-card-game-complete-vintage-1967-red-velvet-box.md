## How Skip-Bo Works: A Refresher for the New and Nostalgic

Skip-Bo is a race against chaos. The rules are simple, which is part of the charm—and also where you’ll find the most hilarious mishaps. Here’s a quick refresher with a Geeknite spin:

- Objectives: Be the first to play all the cards from your stock pile by building sequential runs from 1 to 12 on shared build piles. The wilds act as wildcards, substituting any number, and they can be recreated by players later—not unlike your childhood dreams, which keep resurfacing as you clean out the garage.
- Setup: Shuffle the 162-card deck. Deal stock piles to each player; the exact number depends on how many folks are playing. The rest becomes the draw pile. Build piles start empty; your goal is to fill them with rapidly blossoming numbers.
- Turn structure: On your turn, you draw to have five cards in hand (or as many as needed to play), then you attempt to play cards from your hand or from your stock pile onto any of the build piles in ascending order. You can skip around with wilds, but you can’t skip too far ahead—Skip-Bo is about micro-optimizations, not teleportation. You’ll quickly learn that social deduction is less about who’s lying and more about who’s about to overfill a build pile with an entire batch of 2s.
- Ending a round: The moment a player empties their stock pile, the round ends and points are tallied. The accumulation of rounds determines the winner. The joy of victory is equal parts smug and ridiculous, especially if you managed to win by exploiting a last-minute card swap that you totally saw coming but pretended you didn’t.
