## What is the ZCD001 Tiles Set
### What's in the box
The ZCD001 Tiles Set of 9 is marketed as a modular add-on pack designed to expand and diversify the surface you use for the Zombicide 2nd Edition board. The nine tiles are intended to slot into the standard board layout, giving you more corners, doorways, and corridors to trap your survivors in a cinematic, zombie-yarn of a scenario. Each tile is distinct but thematically consistent with the Zombicide aesthetic: ruined flooring, cracked tiles, scattered debris, and a touch of moody lighting that makes the undead look suitably hungry on a tabletop.

On packaging and feel, CMON leans into the premium vibe. The surface texture is matte, not glossy, which reduces glare under lamp lighting and helps camera phones focus on your minis instead of your own reflection. The edges are clean and designed to align with other CMON terrain products, which means less fiddling with misaligned corners and more time to scream “Zombicide!” at your own reflection.

### Build and materials
The Nine Tile Set uses a sturdy core that feels more rigid than plain cardstock but lighter than metal or thick resin. The material choice is typical for CMON’s modular terrain: a heavier cardboard core with a coated finish. This results in reasonable durability for normal table play and occasional mishandling. Scratches are unlikely to ruin the look if you handle tiles with patient, careful hands. If you’re the spill-and-crumble type, you’ll want to consider a protective sleeve or a light coat of matte sealant to lock down the dust and keep the edges sharp.

The finish is not a glossy, premium resin gloss, but it carries a tactical advantage: it doesn’t glare under LED lighting, which means your survivors can be seen clearly without washing out the walkways. It also absorbs markers well; you can place a few sticky notes or dry-erase marks for quick scenario notes without fear of smearing across the tile art.

## How the tiles fit into gameplay
### Layout and compatibility
The biggest value proposition here is modularity. ZCD001 is designed to slot into the standard Zombicide 2nd Edition map, allowing players to reconfigure the board mid-session for newly generated scenarios or to create barrier-rich urban labyrinths. The nine tiles act as flexible building blocks — you can chain them into long corridors, cluster them into rooms, or scatter them to create open spaces that still feel tense due to mid-run zombie spawns.

If you’ve used fan-made or third-party tiles before, you’ll appreciate the consistency in these as you won’t need to rescale minis or reprint rule sheets to fit the new geometry. ## Maintenance and durability tips
- Store tiles flat in a protective sleeve or a dedicated tray to prevent edge chipping. The last thing you want is a loose zombie to wedgie a corner and make your board unusable.
- Keep away from moisture and humidity. If you spill something, wipe gently with a soft cloth and let them dry before stacking again.
- Consider a matte sealant for long-term durability if your game nights are particularly rough on terrain or you frequently lend your copy to enthusiastic friends who think a tile is a snack.
