## What this PDF is and isn’t (a quick refresher with extra depth)

The Book of Legends is a sourcebook, not a standalone game. If you already love City of Mist, this PDF is the edition that feels like a collector’s edition that someone left a treasure map in your mailbox: more lore, more legends, more tools, and more opportunities to turn a noir crime scene into a mythic stage.

## Content overview: legends, mythos, and GM tools (expanded breakdown)

### The Legends section (new archetypes, same vibe)

The Book of Legends adds a robust roster of player-ready options. You’ll find cinematic archetypes that let mythic power slip into modern crime with style. Think armored gods arriving at a nightclub in a rainstorm, or a detective whose patron is a bureaucrat deity with a fondness for red tape and reform. These legends remain faithful to City of Mist’s core conceit: power arises from belief, and belief is a social contract as much as it is a set of mechanics.

The legends are designed for drop-in use or upgrade paths for existing arcs. You’ll get guidance on variant power sources, social hooks, and how legends evolve as players make choices—your god-heuristic doesn’t have to remain static. The emphasis is squarely on narrative flexibility: your legend can be tuned to fit a campaign’s tone without turning the table into a rules puzzle.

### The world and mythos (factions, threads, and concrete anchors)

City of Mist’s appeal is its mythos-driven premise, and The Book of Legends doubles down. Expect expanded factions, city-wide threads, and motifs that tie back to real locations around your city. The document provides prompts for mythic factions that can be woven into long-form campaigns or improvised on the fly when players hijack a subway car for a monologue about destiny.

The book makes it easier for a GM to present a legend as more than a set of powers; it’s a story waiting to unfold, with the city acting as a co-author.

### Narrative tools and GM tips (applied theater for your table)

GM tools in this PDF include pacing prompts, social conflict guidelines, and scenario charts that nudge players toward dramatic decisions. You’ll find tips for running action scenes that balance mythic spectacle with the mundane texture of a city block: the moment when a racer’s engine roars past a mural of a fallen god, and a clue about the city’s heart finally clicks for the players.

The result is a nimble toolkit: tell a story first, mechanics second, and let the legend-driven momentum carry you toward a satisfying finale.

The PDF includes micro-scenarios designed to be dropped into ongoing campaigns or used as quick-start explorations when players need a nudge.
