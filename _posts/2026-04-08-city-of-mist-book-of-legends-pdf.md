## What this PDF is and isn’t

The Book of Legends is a sourcebook rather than a stand-alone game. If you already love City of Mist, this PDF is the version of the game that someone left a treasure map in your mailbox. It expands the mythos, gives you more character arcs, additional factions, and a battalion of legends that your players can slot into their backstories. It is also designed to be GM-friendly, which is a nice way of saying it will help you explain why your players suddenly resemble a walking Greek chorus at a crime scene.

If you are completely new to City of Mist, you might want to pair this with a core rulebook or another starter pack. The Book of Legends does not reinvent the wheel; it gives you more wheels, gears, and a map to the city so you can drive faster without running out of pathos. The PDF layout makes sense for both quick reference on a laptop and a coffee-stained tabletop printout of essential pages.

## Content overview: legends, mythos, and GM tools

### The Legends section

At its core, The Book of Legends is a buffet of player-ready options. It expands the roster of legends with fresh, cinematic archetypes and twists that let you weave mythic power into modern crime. Think armored gods slipping into a noir club, or a detective with a mythic patron who is more bureaucrat than deity—still terrifying, still human, and still capable of turning the city’s skyline into a set-piece for a chase scene.

The legends are designed to be drop-in replacements or upgrades for existing arcs. The book offers guidance on variant power sources, social hooks, and guidelines for how legends evolve over time in response to player choices. If your table loves the concept that every hero happens to be a rumor with a practical ability, you’ll feel right at home here. The emphasis is on narrative flexibility: your god-heuristic can morph into a legend that fits your campaign’s tone without forcing a map of quantum possibilities onto your players’ feet.

### The world and mythos

One of City of Mist' s appeal points is its mythos-driven premise. The Book of Legends doubles down on this by offering expanded factions, city-wide threads, and mythic motifs that tie back to concrete locations in the city. The document provides prompts for mythic factions that can be integrated into long-term campaigns or improvised on the fly when players decide to hijack a subway car with a running monologue about destiny.

These additions aren’t just flavor; they’re practical hooks for sessions that destabilize status quo and force players to question what is real, what is myth, and what the heck is a hint of orange juice doing in a crime scene photo. The book makes it easier for a GM to say, this legend isn’t just power; it’s a story waiting to be told, and the city itself shapes the narrative as much as the players do.

### Narrative tools and GM tips

The Book of Legends includes useful GM tools: pacing prompts, social conflict guidelines, and a few “what if” charts that push players toward dramatic decisions. You’ll find tips on running action scenes that balance mythic spectacle with mundane setting details—because the most memorable moments in City of Mist are often the ones where a racer’s engine roars past a mural of a fallen god and the players realize they just rolled a critical truth about the city’s heart.

There are also rules-of-thumb for power scaling, which are essential when you’re juggling multiple legends in a single session. The PDF nudges you toward balancing narrative stakes with mechanical clarity so players don’t have to spend fifteen minutes debating whether a superpower would break the page difference between a minor and major mythic feat. In practice, you’ll discover how to lean into the story while keeping the table on track for a satisfying finale.
