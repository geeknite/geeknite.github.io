## Synthetic rope vs steel rope: the great rope debate (revisited)

Let’s get this out of the way again, because it’s a frequent forum favorite: synthetic rope or steel cable? For most off-road, ATV, and UTV trailer recoveries, synthetic rope wins for handling, safety, and ease of use.

- Safety: Synthetic rope floats and remains flexible under load, with fewer sharp burrs if the line breaks. If it does snap, synthetic fibers tend to fray instead of whipping with the ferocity of a titanium katana. Obviously, you should never rely on a broken line, but the immediate hazard profile is lower with synthetic rope than with steel.
- Handling: Lighter rope means easier management around a hitch, a drum, and a tree anchor. You can coil and store it with less muscle and more sanity.
- Durability: While steel is notoriously strong, synthetic rope handles abrasion and heat differently. It wants to live on a drum, glide through a fairlead, and resist kinks. It’s forgiving in real-world, imperfect setups.

On the flip side, steel rope can be tougher in terms of heat resistance and longevity under heavy abuse (think long, continuous pulls in rocky environments). If you’re doing heavy, industrial style towing with demanding loads, steel still has a place. For a mid-range, all-around recovery tool for trailers, ATVs, and UTVs, synthetic rope tends to be the sensible default—and that’s why Stealth ships with it in this line.

## Installation and compatibility: mounting without drama

If you’re handy with a wrench, you’re good. If you’re a hobbyist with a vague recollection of school shop class, you’ll still be fine as long as you follow the manual and resist the urge to improvise a universal mount using bungee cords and hope. Here are practical notes to skip the drama:

### Mounting pattern and bolt circle

Most 4,500 lb winches share a standard footprint—often around a 10 x 4.5 inch bolt pattern. Make sure your bumper, bumper plate, or trailer winch mount aligns with those holes. If you’re mounting on a trailer, check stability under load and ensure bolts are rated for the expected loads. If it doesn’t align with pre-cut holes, you may need to fabricate an adapter plate. Planning ahead yields happier recoveries and less muggy chaos when you finally stop spinning your wheels.

### Wiring and switch options

A clean installation means tidy wiring. Run a dedicated power line with a proper fuse and a switch. The wireless remote is handy, but you’ll still want a safe, easily accessible switch inside your vehicle or on the bumper so you can engage the winch without squinting through spray. Use heavy-gauge wiring, ensure solid ground, and remember that a good ground is half the victory. For trailers, consider a separate battery or dual-battery setup for longer outings, especially if your trailer sits unused for weeks and then sulks in the cold.

### Accessories and mounting add-ons

Many packages include a protective cover, a rope dampener, and sometimes a mounting kit with extra hardware. A dampener can be a nice safety-minded addition to prevent rope whip during snatch recoveries. Safety first, even when you’re laughing at your own ploys for victory.

## Maintenance, care, and longevity: do the simple things well

To keep a winch like this performing well across seasons, you won’t need a full-blown workshop. Just a few sensible habits:

- Keep it dry when possible. If you’re in a rain-heavy environment, wipe down the motor housing and external components after use to prevent corrosion.
- Use a protective cover when stored outside. The synthetic rope benefits from storage in a dry, cool place away from direct UV exposure.
- Inspect the rope for frays or nicks. Synthetic fibers can hide damage; bright white fibers or fraying near the hook are a sign to replace or address wear with a professional.
- Lubricate moving parts as recommended by the manufacturer. The goal is smooth operation and minimal drag during winching.
- Check mounting bolts and hardware periodically. Loose bolts are the silent killers of recoveries.

A quick quarterly check can save you from an embarrassing failure at the exact moment you need it most.
