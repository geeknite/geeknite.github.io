### Thermals and sustained performance

During long gaming sessions or sustained copy runs, the integrated heatsink will keep the SSD from hitting high peak temperatures. In our testing, the drives hovered around mid 50s to low 60s Celsius under heavy load in a well-ventilated case. In a cramped thermally-challenged chassis, you might see 70s. A good rule of thumb is to have airflow around the drive and not rely on the heatsink alone to do all the cooling. The result is less thermal throttling and steadier speeds.

## Installation and Compatibility

Installing an M.2 NVMe drive is one of those tasks that feels like magic the first time you do it and a crime against physics the next. Fortunately the CT4000T705SSD5 is pretty friendly. It uses the standard M.2 form factor and will slide into most motherboards with a compatible M.2 slot. The integrated heatsink reduces the risk of clearance issues, but you still want to verify a few things before you press the power button.

- Check your motherboard M.2 slot compatibility and any cooling restrictions
- Confirm your BIOS is up to date to ensure the PCIe 5.0 lane is recognized
- If your case has limited clearance around the motherboard, verify the height of the heatsink does not collide with the GPU or RAM modules
- Prepare a backup of critical files before doing any major drive changes, because you never know when Windows decides to panic and reinstall itself
