What is inside the Folio Pack

The Folio Pack centers around a set of character folios, prompt cards, and quick reference aids that are designed to slot neatly into City of Mist sessions. The concept is simple: give players a ready-to-use visual and narrative scaffold that can be pulled into a scene with minimal setup. The goal is to move from concept to scene in as few minutes as possible, without sacrificing the thematic weight that City of Mist fans crave. To achieve that, Son of Oak has packed a cohesive mix of elements that speak the language of the game. - A dozen or so character folios, each one a compact portrait of a potential Mist child or mythic avatar, with sections for backstory, aliases, and a few key traits. Each folio is designed to capture a character arc in a few lines of text and a couple of prompt images that you can reference during play. The folios are color coded and laid out with a consistent typographic backbone that helps the GM scan for the right prompts without losing track of the scene’s mood. The content alignment with the mythic layer of City of Mist is solid without feeling forced; you get a mix of urban legend type archetypes and modern day tragedy that sits well with the game’s core approach.
- A set of prompt cards that function as quick hooks or scene prompts. These prompts are like micro-scenarios that you can drop into an ongoing scene to drive action or to push players toward a new angle. The prompts are well balanced between outward action and inner drama, which is essential for keeping a Mist session grounded while not stalling the pace. The prompts also include some reliability checks for the players so you know when to lean into the mythic dimension and when to pull back to the character drama.
- Location cards that give you immediate settings to populate the scene with a sense of place. City of Mist is as much about place as it is about people, and those location cards help you hit the mood quickly. You can attach a location to a folio to create a micro-scene that invites the players to explore clues and interact with the environment rather than just narrating events. The best use of these location cards is as a tool to anchor scenes in a location that feels plausible yet suggestive of larger mythic stakes.

The potential pitfalls and caveats

No product is perfect and the Folio Pack is no exception. Here are a few things to watch out for:
- It is not a replacement for the core rulebook. The Folio Pack is best used in conjunction with your existing City of Mist materials. It fills in the mood and prompts but leaves the essential mechanics and world-building where they belong: in the core rules, your imagination, and your GM discipline.
- It can feel a little limited if you are running a very big city plot. If your campaign demands sprawling detective epics with dozens of factions and a heavy reliance on city scale, you might outgrow the scope of what the Folio Pack provides and need to supplement with your own notes or other accessories.
- The art style is strong but not universal. The mood may resonate with some players immediately while others prefer a different vibe. If your table is on the edge of a lighter or darker mood than the pack assumes, you may want to curate the visuals or choose to mix in some alternate art from other sources.

How to integrate with your existing sessions

Integration is the practical test. Here are several recommended approaches to make the Folio Pack sing at your table:
- Use folios as session anchors. Start by handing a folio to a player who will lead a scene, and use the character prompts to frame the negotiation, the catch, and the decision that will drive the next minutes. Having a clear focal point helps maintain the noir mood and gives the scene a through line for the players.
- Rotate prompts to generate evolution. Every session, pull a different prompt and adapt it to the ongoing story. The same prompt can morph across episodes as a character’s secrets surface, so you get a sense of thematic continuity rather than one off ideas.
- Use location cards as recurring sets. If you place a particular location at the center of a plot arc, you can repeatedly surface clues and tensions from the same physical space, which deepens the sense that the city itself is complicit in the story rather than a static backdrop.
- Create a table of mythic clues. The city of Mist uses a mythic layer as a lens for ordinary events. The Folio Pack is a tool to surface those clues and then deconstruct them with players. A simple approach is to have a shared list of clues that come from folio prompts, then weave them into the role play as the players chase a mythic thread through the urban maze.
