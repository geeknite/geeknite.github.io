## Practical Tips for Collectors and Bargain Hunters

### How to evaluate a 46-card lot before buying

- Request high-resolution photos of each card or, at minimum, a collage showing a few representative samples.
- Ask about the total count, whether any cards are duplicates, and whether the lot includes any mission or support cards beyond the core hero/villain mix.
- Inquire about the back design and card stock color consistency. A noticeable back color variance can indicate reprint or non-original stock, which may affect value for strict collectors.
- Check for watermark or printing errors that are sometimes valued by specific sub-communities.
- If possible, request a quick video or live stream of the card’s surface under bright light to reveal surface scratches or gloss issues that aren’t visible in stills.

### Storage and care tips

To preserve this 46-card lot, keep a stable environment. Card pages or sleeves with acid-free materials are a good start; if you plan to handle the cards, wear gloves to minimize fingerprints. Humidity should be controlled to prevent warping or edge whitening. A simple display approach can be to mount the cards in a shadowbox or frame them as a running collage. If you’re more of a trader than a display-type collector, store the cards in labeled bags with brief notes about each card’s hero/villain role, power level, and the card’s place in your personal OverPower chronology. The goal is to maintain readability and a sense of the story the lot tells as a whole.

## The Historical Context: Fleer, Hero Mission, and the 1990s CCG Boom

The 1990s saw a tidal wave of collectible card games flooding the market, each trying to outdo the last in artwork, ambitious card text, and cross-promotional potential.
