- Enhanced Power Mechanics: The PDF introduces refined moves that let players push their mythos’s limits in flavorful ways. - Narrative-Driven Combat: Don’t expect a D&D-like combat ledger. City of Mist remains a game where fights are cinematic and interpretive. The New Mythos content leans into this design: you’ll see mechanics that reward clever storytelling, misdirection, and using the environment. It’s a reminder that the city’s mythic ecosystem isn’t a peaceful garden; it’s a living, breathing ecosystem where legends collide and the streetlight catches the clash of ideas.

In short: New Mythos respects the core system’s strengths—story-driven choices, mood over math, and a playful sense of mythic improvisation—while offering enough new material to feel fresh without requiring a complete reboot of your existing campaign.

## A few practical tips for running New Mythos at your table
- Start small: pick one new mythos that matches your party’s vibe and weave it into one session. If things go well, add another in the next arc. You won’t need to deploy the entire batch all at once.
- Use the seed encounters as a pacing tool: they’re designed to be slotted in between major plot twists or as a bridge between acts. They’re perfect for a game night that’s equal parts mystery and performance.
- Embrace the misdirection: players love a twist that forces them to confront a myth in a new light. Let the environment or the city’s lore tilt toward interpretation. The goal isn’t to prove the myth’s “truth” but to experience the myth’s impact on the characters’ lives.
- Balance narrative and action: plan a few scenes that reward roleplay and a few that reward quick decisiveness. The best sessions juggle both mood and momentum, and New Mythos provides tools to help you achieve that balance without feeling like you’re juggling flaming swords with one hand tied behind your back.
- Keep track of the city’s truth budget: City of Mist thrives on the idea that myths cost something to keep alive. Make sure the group is mindful of what each myth demands and what it returns in turn. This is where the pacing and player buy-in shine.
