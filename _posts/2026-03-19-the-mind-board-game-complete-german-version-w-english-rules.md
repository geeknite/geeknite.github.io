## Rules and Setup: A Gentle Reintroduction to Silence

The Mind’s core mechanic is deceptively simple: players receive a hand of cards that correspond to the current round, and they must play their cards in ascending order without speaking. Communication is reduced to timing, a wink to the table, or a gentle nod. In the German edition, the English rules are there to supplement any translation hiccups and ensure no player gets stuck thinking the numbers correspond to the colors in some arcane code.

Setup is quick. Shuffle the deck, deal a number of cards to each player equal to the current round (level 1 means one card each, level 2 means two, and so on). The objective is to play cards in ascending order across the team without verbal instruction. If a player plays a card out of order, the team loses a life or a token. If you deplete the team’s lives, the round ends in disaster and you conspire to blame the one who “misplaced” their brain at the last moment. If you make it through all 12 rounds, congratulations—you have achieved a degree of synchronized telepathy that should probably be illegal in several jurisdictions.

What the German edition highlights is the importance of the bilingual rules. You’ll find the core mechanics explained in German, with the English version provided for reference. This is a practical choice because it reduces confusion for players who rotate between German and English-speaking groups. It also reduces the friction that sometimes occurs when color palettes or number sequences are misread under game night lighting. The end result is a setup process that is swift and smooth, letting you jump straight into the headspace of quiet cooperation.
