## Painting journey: recommended color schemes and practical tips
Painting minis is a blend of art, science, and therapy—especially when your table is negotiating with a zombie horde at 7:30 PM on a Friday. Here are practical tips if you’re aiming for cinema-ready Ultimate Survivors without a week per figure:

- Start with a strong base coat. A mid-tone undercoat provides soil, rust, and leather contrasts and gives your highlights something to bounce off.
- Use a three-step shading approach: base → wash → dry brush. Washes (brown or black inks) settle into crevices for depth, while a light dry brush on raised edges highlights detail without turning the model into a plastic postcard.
- Leather and fabric deserve separation. Warm browns or olive greens for leather give a gritty vibe; for fabric, hero blue or burgundy can pop against metal armor and bone accents.
- Metallics deserve their due. A silver/steel undercoat with a blue-black wash provides a cold, edge-lit look. For warmer metal, brass or copper highlights on worn edges read nicely under torchlight.
- Blood and grime: used tastefully, blood streaks and dirt on knee plates can lift a figure from “clean knight” to “battle-seasoned veteran.” Keep it subtle unless you’re chasing a specific aesthetic.
- Shield and emblem work: emblems deserve emphasis. A saturated color with a sharp edge highlight helps them pop on a darker base.

If you crave concrete color ideas, here’s a starter palette that works on most Ultimate Survivors without clashing:
- Leather: Saddle brown with burnt umber washes; edge highlight with tan.
- Cloth: Deep steel blue with a midnight wash; pale edge highlight.
- Armor: Feldgrau base, dark recess wash, light gray edge highlight.
- Wood: Walnut base, sepia wash, light dry-brush to simulate wear.
- Metallics: Dark iron base, blue-black wash, silver edge highlight to pop.

For a visual guide, the community is your friend. Check out painting albums on BoardGameGeek or Instagram with terms like Zombicide painted survivors to see a spectrum of choices. The most important palette, though, is the one that stirs your own heart every time you tilt the model and declare, This is mine.

## How to display and store your painted Ultimate Survivors
Display is a personal choice, but a few practical tips can keep your minis looking pristine for years:

## Display, storage, and long-term care: practical wrap-up
To keep the painted minis looking their best over time:
- Gentle cleaning with a soft brush to remove dust after a few sessions.
- Avoid handling by weapons or thin blades; edges can chip with careless handling.
- Use a topcoat to seal the paint if you’re playing frequently. A matte sealant provides a grim, realistic finish; a satin finish can reduce glare under bright lighting.
- If you want to repurpose parts or add customization later, keep the original sprues and part numbers in labeled storage so you can reconstruct the exact kit later.
