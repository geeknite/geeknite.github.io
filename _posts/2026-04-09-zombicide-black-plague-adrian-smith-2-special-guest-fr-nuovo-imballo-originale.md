## What is Zombicide Black Plague, and why a Special Guest edition matters

Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative, zombie-horde strategy game where you and up to six friends (in practice, 2–4 is optimal for table space and sanity) fight through a city overrun by undead. It’s the spiritual successor to the classic zombie-battle archetypes, with a modern, modular system that scales nicely as you add more board tiles and more miniatures to the battlefield. The core appeal is simple: you equip survivors, you roll dice for actions, you unlock better tools, and you try not to become a snack for the walking dead.

Now, what makes a Special Guest edition stand out? The big draw is the art and sometimes the components. Adrian Smith’s involvement signals a strong emphasis on cover artwork and potentially some limited edition miniatures or alternate-card art. This FR edition leans into that with a consistent, high-contrast palette, detailed zombie sculpts, and a packaging approach that feels more premium than your average plastic-wrapped card box.

If you’re a fan of his work, you’re likely here for the aesthetic mood as much as the gameplay. And if you’re new to the artist’s universe, consider this edition a portal into a particular flavor of fantasy grimdark, served with a side of tactical, chaotic co-op play.

For the curious mind: Adrian Smith’s involvement with board game art isn’t just window-dressing. The art environment influences how players feel about the setting, the stakes, and even the perceived value of the physical pieces. A good cover can transform a simple box into a heroic artifact on your shelf, which in turn makes you more likely to actually open the box and play the game instead of just letting it collect dust next to your Souls-lack-of-time-sleep pile.
