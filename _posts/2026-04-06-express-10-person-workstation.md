## What is the Express 10-Person Workstation?

The Express 10-Person Workstation is a modular desk ecosystem designed to seat ten teammates in a shared, lightly partitioned grid. Each “station” includes a height-adjustable desk surface, a primary monitor arm, a personal cable channel, and a small, neighbor-friendly power hub that politely refuses to be a fire hazard at 2 a.m. on a Thursday. The whole setup is designed to minimize the amount of time teams spend fighting the Great Cable Mess of 2020 while giving everyone enough personal space to pretend they’re in a sci-fi command center rather than a bustling open-plan office.

Design goal: create a collaborative environment without forcing group hugs around the same keyboard. The Express system aims to deliver:

- A consistent line of sight across rows so screen glare and tilt shenanigans stay to a minimum.
- Shared access to power and USB-C charging without balancing on chair legs and praying to the cable gods.
- A modular footprint that scales with your team as if you’re playing a long, corporate game of Tetris.
- Accessibility in every sense: adjustable heights, easy cable routing, and surfaces that tolerate coffee spills without becoming a landscape of brown regret.

## Ergonomics and Comfort: Can Ten People Sit Comfortably?

The Express system centers ergonomic flexibility. Each station uses a height-adjustable surface with a comfortable range that covers both sitting and standing configurations. The key ergonomic knobs are easy to reach from a seated position, and they feel durable enough to withstand the occasional coworker who tries to “adjust once and forget” by lifting with their hips instead of their knees. It’s a small thing, but ergonomics is the small thing that keeps you from becoming a coffee-addled pat of churned crankiness by 3 p.m.

### Monitor Arms and Keyboard Trays
Monitor arms offer enough range to accommodate a variety of monitor sizes, from the classic 24-inch to the glorious behemoth that makes your IT team glare with suspicion. The arms lock into place without requiring a PhD in mechanical engineering, which means you can avoid the post-assembly circle of doom where someone says, “If you tighten the wrong bolt, the monitor becomes a satellite dish.” Spoiler: it doesn’t. The keyboard trays, when present, glide smoothly and hide away when not needed, helping to reduce desk clutter and the occasional elbow-to-monitor incident that occurs during intense meetings.

### Seating and Space Allocation
With ten seats, space allocation is as important as the seat itself. Express overlaps service zones allow for comfortable elbow room while still enabling a social atmosphere. If your team includes tall individuals and vamos-of-agile-types who believe “standing desk” equals “standing desk for everyone else,” this system offers enough space to respect personal lines without entering the realm of social awkwardness. Ergonomic chairs (your mileage may vary) pair nicely with the setup and will be the celebrities of your office if you invite chairs that actually move without sounding like a distressed dolphin.

## Power, Cable Management, and the Joy of Not Tripping Over a Surge Protector

Cable chaos is the true dragon under the desk. The Express 10-Person Workstation attempts to tame that dragon with a two-pronged approach: centralized power hubs and clever cable channels for each row. The result is a workspace where you can plug in the work laptop, a secondary monitor, and your beloved, slightly overbearing office lamp without playing a game of “Which cable belongs to which device?” with a sticky note as your only compass.

### Power Delivery and Safety
Each pod comes with a dedicated power hub, surge protection, and a clean ability to route cables through a discreet channel. The hubs are rated for the typical blend of devices found in a modern team environment: laptops, external hard drives, some USB-C-powered accessories, and a suspiciously large number of charging cables that somehow always appear when a quarterly review is in the air. The safety features include automatic shutoff for overloaded rails and a sensible labeling system that makes the IT team murmur “we’ve trained for this.”

### Cable Channel Design
The cable channels are not just a cosmetic addition; they’re the quiet heroes of the setup. They keep power bricks from becoming abstract sculptures and reduce the wear on USB-C cables by offering a gentle slope for the wires to habitually return to their rightful places after a fight with a moved monitor arm. And yes, there are adhesive clips for those who enjoy micro-organization as a form of personal meditation.

You can opt for different finishes (dark ash to match your office vibe, or a brighter laminate if you’re into the “the future is shiny” aesthetic). If your team grows, you can add a wing; if your team decides to pivot to a 100 percent remote mode, you can partially disassemble for a smaller office refresh and still keep the DNA intact.
