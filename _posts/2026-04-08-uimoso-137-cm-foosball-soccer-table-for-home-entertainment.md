## Assembly and Setup

### Unpacking and parts
When you open the box, you will be greeted by a chorus of screws, allen keys, and small plastic spacers that look like tiny Lego pieces with a higher purpose. Sort everything into labeled piles, or at least into piles that look equally organized to you.

### Tools included
The kit includes the standard allen keys and a small wrench; you may want a ratchet to speed things up, but that would be overkill unless you own a mechanized workshop in your living room.

### Step by step assembly
- Attach the legs to the frame using the supplied bolts. Tighten until snug, but not to the level that you fear you might strip the wood or bend reality.
- Mount the rods and passive players to the side rails. Ensure the end caps are secure; you do not want to hear the ball rattle into your socks during a tense late-game moment.
- Install the playing surface and attach end ball returns. Make sure the ball retrieval mouth is aligned with the table so the balls do not go wandering into the land of lost socks.
- Level the table using the built-in feet until the surface is perfectly flat. If you have balanced it on a bubble of air, you did not complete the leveling process.

### Setup tips
Take a break halfway; your hands will thank you. Use a little wax on the rod joints if you want smoother action, but do not apply wax to the ball or your cat will start chasing it like a tiny terminator.

## Maintenance and Durability

### Cleaning and care
Regular dusting and wiping with a soft cloth is enough for most households. For sticky game nights, a damp cloth with mild soap can do wonders. Do not soak the playing surface, and avoid abrasive cleaners that might wear down the green finish.

### Waxing and rod maintenance
If the action begins to feel gritty, a light application of rod lubricant or silicone spray on the rod joints can restore smooth play. Avoid over-lubricating; you do not want a slippery ball to become a hyperbola of chaos.

### Longevity and warranty
With proper care, this foosball table should last for many years of weekend showdowns. Warranties vary by seller, but a standard one-year warranty is common for most home models. Check the manual for the details.

### Customer support and replacement parts
If part wear becomes an issue, you should be able to source replacement rods or end caps from the manufacturer or from third-party suppliers. The world of parts is often surprisingly friendly to foosball fans.
