## What is Herd Mentality?
Herd Mentality is a party game about predicting what the majority of your friends will say. A prompt card is drawn and read aloud. Each player writes a response on a dry-erase card or slip (depending on your edition). Once everyone has submitted, the answers are revealed. The goal is simple: you want your answer to match the most popular answer in that round. If your answer aligns with the majority, you score points; if you nailed the exact most-popular choice, you score extra points. It is basically a social science experiment with snacks and a lot of high-fiving and sarcastic laughter.

The beauty lies in the social psychology flavor: you are not always right. You are right if the herd thinks like you do (or if you can out-predict them). It is a game of collective memory, biases, and the occasional stubborn denial that yes, people can be as basic as the rest of us. The humor comes from watching people defend their choices with missionary-level zeal, which is exactly what makes family game night feel alive again rather than a recursive cycle of “we played this last week.”

## Setup
Setting up Herd Mentality is the adult equivalent of being handed a smoothie with a random garnish and told to pretend it’s fine. Here is a typical setup flow:
1. Choose your reader for the first round. The reader is responsible for reading the prompt aloud and ensuring everyone understands the rules for that round.
2. Shuffle the prompt deck and the Bonus Cards deck separately (if you are using them).
3. Deal an answering slip or dry-erase board to each player. Everyone should have a writing surface or space to jot down an answer.
4. Place the scoring tokens within easy reach. You’re going to want to grab those like a dragon guards gold.
5. Decide on the scoring threshold for the game, or simply play a few rounds and tally as you go. The beauty of party games is that you can adjust the pace to match your crew’s energy level.
6. If you are using Bonus Cards, shuffle them into their dedicated deck. They typically kick in after a couple of rounds or when someone lands on a specific icon on the board.

Pro tip: have a few snack breaks. Herd Mentality moves faster than a caffeinated raccoon on a skateboard, and you’ll want to refuel before you start arguing about the correct definition of popular in 2026.


## How to Play
The core loop is deliciously simple but can yield surprising results:

### Round flow (base game)
- A prompt card is read aloud by the current reader.
- Each player writes down a response privately on their answer slip.
- The slips are revealed simultaneously.
- Players vote (or the reader determines) which answer is the most common. The twist: you do not vote on your own answer unless you want to give away your strategy.
- Points are awarded to players whose answers match the majority. If your answer is the most common among all players, you receive bonus points.
- The role of reader passes to the next player, and the cycle continues.

In practice, what happens is a rapid-fire game about social alignment. You will find yourself leaning into the herd’s mindset, watching your own opinions shift like a chameleon with a PhD in social psychology. It is a light strategy game, not a heavy brain teaser; the emphasis is on fun and interaction rather than winning by a mile with a flawless plan.

### Round flow (with Bonus Cards)
- Before the prompt is read, a Bonus Card may be drawn (depending on your house rules). Bonus Cards can require players to alter their responses in predictable or absurd ways.
- Some Bonus Cards may require that you switch your answer with another player, or force you to pick a second answer that you must defend publicly. Others might add a time limit, pushing you to juggle your thoughts under pressure.
- The scoring remains similar, but the presence of the Bonus Card often creates unexpected alignments that can swing the round from predictable to ridiculous.

This dynamic creates moments where your aunt’s pride-for-pierogi-opinions collide with your cousin’s meme-driven reasoning, and suddenly you’re all arguing about a prompt that your smartphone could have answered with a mere emoji. It is exactly that kind of chaos that makes game nights memorable.

## Bonus Cards: flavors, types, and how they spice things up
The Bonus Cards pack introduces new scenarios that disrupt standard expectations. Here are typical card archetypes you might encounter:
- Time pressure cards: You must write your answer within a shorter time frame, increasing stress and silly mistakes.
- Forced pairing: You must pair your answer with another player; if that pair is the majority, both get points. If not, both may lose points depending on house rules.
- Meme mode: Your response must reference a popular meme. Expect groans, eye-rolls, and surprising bursts of laughter.
- Reversal cards: You must defend a minority stance, which can create dramatic reversals and surprising crowd sympathy for your underdog opinion.
- Bonus multiplier: A round where the majority’s points are multiplied by a factor, creating a dramatic swing in the final tally.

The outcome is a more chaotic and cinematic game experience. Bonus Cards can tilt the map, force collaboration, and ensure that no one is permanently in the driver’s seat. It’s a playground for improv and improvisation, which is part of the reason it shines in mixed-age groups.


## Gameplay tips and strategies
While Herd Mentality is designed to be accessible, there are some helpful heuristics that can improve your odds without ruining the fun:
- Observe patterns: People often respond in ways tied to common cultural references, shared experiences, or recent media you’ve all discussed. If you notice a trend, you can ride it to the most common answer.
- Embrace the crowd’s energy: If you feel the herd leaning in a certain direction, consider aligning with it rather than fighting it for the sake of “being original.” Sometimes the most valuable move is to predict the room well.
- Use the Bonus Cards strategically: Don’t save them for the late game only. They can reshape the current round and throw the other players off balance, yielding more points if used at the right moment.
- Read the room, not just the card: If the room is a chaotic mix of kids and adults, you may expect a broad answer range. In more serious gatherings, you can gameplay with more precise targeting of popular sentiments.
- Manage your expectations: Some rounds will feel unwinnable if you and your group diverge dramatically. That is part of the fun; the key is to enjoy the ride and not take every round as a life-defining moment.
