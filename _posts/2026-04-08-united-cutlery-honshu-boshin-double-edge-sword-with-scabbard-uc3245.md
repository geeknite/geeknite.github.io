## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
The longevity of any blade depends on how well you treat it after purchase. UC3245 is designed for longer shelf life with minimal rust risk if you keep it dry and clean. A routine maintenance regimen will include cleaning the blade after exposure to moisture, applying a light protective oil, and avoiding aggressive sharpening that would alter its display geometry.

### Cleaning and Care
Begin by wiping down the blade with a soft cloth to remove fingerprints and dust. A gentle pulse of silicone or mineral oil can help keep the edge and the fuller (the channel along the blade) from drying out. For the wrap, check for loose threads and address them with light tension adjustments or consult a professional if you notice fraying. Scabbard care is equally important: avoid moisture buildup inside the sheath and occasionally wipe the interior with a dry cloth. If you plan to store the sword for long periods, consider placing a silica gel pack inside the scabbard to reduce humidity.

### Display and Storage
If you display UC3245, keep it away from direct sunlight to preserve the finish and guard details. A dedicated sword mount helps prevent accidental knocks and keeps the blade in pristine condition during those long, dramatic cosplay photo sessions. A glass case is ideal for display, offering both dust protection and a sense of archival importance for your collection. Remember: the more you treat it like a piece in a museum, the longer it will keep its dramatic edge in photographs and show venues.
