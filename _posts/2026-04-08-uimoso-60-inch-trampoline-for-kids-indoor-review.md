### Assembly Tips and Longevity Best Practices
A few practical assembly tips can save you hours of head scratching: lay out all parts first, check the hardware against the parts list, and keep a magnetic tray handy for tiny fasteners. Use the correct tools and avoid overtightening bolts, which can strip threads or warp the frame. If you encounter a stubborn spring, try a gentle wiggle rather than forcing. Remember to work with a second person to keep the mat taut as you attach springs. This prevents the mat from slipping and reduces the likelihood of pinched fingers.

Longevity is about maintenance. Regular checks for loose bolts, signs of wear on the net, and the condition of padding are essential. If you notice the padding has started to peel or the net shows signs of fraying, replace parts or contact the manufacturer for assistance. Regular cleaning is also important; wipe down the frame with a mild cleaning solution and dry thoroughly. A quick wipe-down after each play session can keep rust and grime at bay, preserving the look and feel of the unit for years to come.
