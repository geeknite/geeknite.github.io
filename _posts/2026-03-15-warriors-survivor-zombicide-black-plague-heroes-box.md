### Painting and assembly tips for loose minis

One of the nicest things about a loose minis set is that there s minimal risk of spoiling a perfect factory finish. These figures usually arrive unassembled on the sprue or in a loose plastic bag; you will need to clip, clean, and assemble if you want to mount them onto bases. Here s a quick guide to get you from box to tabletop without losing your mind:

- Preparation matters: Use a sharp hobby knife to remove the excess plastic, then gently file any burrs. A little bit of trimming goes a long way toward clean lines and better paint adhesion.
- Primer choice: A matte white or gray primer gives you a clean base to build layer color on. If you re rushed, a light wash can help you see lines and details more clearly during the first pass.
- Base coating strategy: Aim for mid tone colors for most of the armor and leather areas. You can layer metallics for the armor plates and your base skin tones with a slightly thinned paint to keep the shadows crisp.
- Washes and highlights: A simple black or brown wash can settle into the recesses to pull out details. Dry brushing lighter tones on raised edges can bring out hair, capes, and cloth textures.
- Basing ideas: Since these minis are loose, you might want to mount them on standard 25 mm or 32 mm bases with a few bits of cork or texture paint to create a small dungeon or ruins vibe. This helps with stability on the table and improves the overall silhouette on the battlefield.
- Gluing vs not gluing: If you re not comfortable with glue, you can opt to pin the weapons and hands with a dab of strong glue. If you re painting and want swap-out options, you can leave some parts unglued and use tack to reposition them during the painting process.

### Care, maintenance, and storage tips

Loose minis can be fragile during transit, so store them in a dedicated mini cabinet or a soft foam case with a few separators. After painting, keep them away from direct sunlight to avoid fading. If you use a matte sealant to protect your paint, apply in light coats from a distance to avoid pooling around the joints. For players who like to transport their mission boards, consider a simple carry tray with foam inserts to keep the five survivors from tumbling together with other components.
