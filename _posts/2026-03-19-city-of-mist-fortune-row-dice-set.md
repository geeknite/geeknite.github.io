## Design and Build Quality

### Materials and Finish
The Fortune Row dice set leans into a classic resin look with a modern finish. The resin feel is precise and the edges are sharp enough to catch the light but not so sharp that you worry about chipping when you flick the dice across your gaming surface. The pips pop in a way that makes important numbers easy to read, which is crucial during sessions where you are juggling multiple aspects, boons, and countless dramatic twists at once.

### Aesthetics and Thematic Tie-In
The Fortune Row aesthetic leans noir meets myth, which is a sweet spot for City of Mist players. The color palette and glyphs evoke street-level mysticism: luck as currency, danger as a currency exchange, and secrets as your daily bread. The symbols on the dice are designed to hint at fortune, misfortune, and the subtle manipulation of outcomes that your players will chase as they try to navigate a city that rewards cunning more than brute force.

### Face Design and Legibility
The dice include the standard array of dice you would expect in a classic 7-die set: d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, and a percentile die. The numbers are clean, the font is readable, and there is enough contrast for rapid recognition under tense lighting. This matters because a true noir table can be hard on eyes with lots of shadows and a row of tokens casting long reflections on the table. The Fortune Row set mitigates that with clear numerals and a consistent design language across faces.

### Thematic Icons and Symbols
Similarly themed dice sets often overload the glyphs with too much detail. This set does not go overboard. The glyphs serve as visual cues for story beats rather than as intricate art projects. That balance matters because you want the dice to be evocative without slowing down the game while players try to read them in the moment of a tense roll. The Fortune Row style manages this balance with a restrained approach that still feels luxurious.

## Roleplay Integration: Plots and Hooks

### Fortune Row Inspired Prompts
Fortune Row in City of Mist evokes a blend of street-level crime, glamour, and rumor-driven power. With this dice set on the table, you can prompt the players with small, noir-flavored questions that thread through the session:
- Who among the Fortune Row stands to gain the most from a rumored artifact, and what does that loss look like to the people who trust them?
- A roll of a certain die triggers a flashback that lets a PC lay out a piece of a past myth that alters the present moment. What truth about the city does the roll unearth?
- A critical success or failure reveals a new informant or a dangerous enemy. How does the Fortune Row bewitchery work into the plan you are building?

These prompts help maintain momentum and give players a reason to lean into the themes rather than just looking for mechanical success. The dice are the physical metaphor for luck, leverage, and limited time to master the city’s secrets.

### Using Dice to Track Boons, Curses, and Clout
In City of Mist, your power often comes from a mixture of myths and social leverage. You can use your Fortune Row dice to track boons, curses, and clout in the following simple way:
- Boons: Assign a color to a beneficial consequence that players can spend to gain advantage on a check. The dice pool reads as a resource that can be invested for a better outcome.
- Curses: When a player falls under a minor curse or drawback, map a die to a negative effect. It adds narrative pressure while providing a mechanical reminder of the price of performance.
- Clout: Use a die to represent influence within the Fortune Row and beyond. When a character gains or loses influence, shift the pool accordingly.

This approach ties the dice to the campaign’s politics and social maneuvering, which is where City of Mist often shines. The Fortune Row dice set becomes not just a tool for numbers but a ledger of the characters' reputations and the city’s mood.

## How to Use in a Campaign

### GM Tips
- Start with mood, not mechanics. Present a rumor or a street-level mystery and let the dice color guide how the plot twists unfold.
- Use dice as a pacing mechanism. If the session is dragging, roll a Fortune Row prompt and let it steer a new scene. The dice are your narrative sledgehammer for building momentum.
- Tie dice outcomes to local legends. If a PC triggers a mythic response, have a visible city myth react, reinforcing the idea that the city notices their actions.

### Player Tips
- Treat the dice as story prompts. If you roll a number that feels lucky, describe a narrative beat that foreshadows the next move your character makes.
- Use the color cues to coordinate with team dynamics. Assign roles or capabilities to different dice colors, so the party can speak in a shared shorthand.
- Don’t be afraid to narrate the world’s reaction. In City of Mist, the city itself is a character. Let the dice results ripple through the streets and alleyways as if the city is whispering back to you.

Like any good noir prop, you want your dice to last as long as your character’s ambition. The Fortune Row dice set is reasonably durable if you treat it with care:
