## Buying, Shipping, and Caring for Your Dark Gloom
A practical section for the shop-savvy collector. The world of vintage cards is full of tiny bureaucracies—grading, shipping, and protecting your card from the tyranny of humidity and caffeine spills.

- Sleeves and top-loaders: Use acid-free sleeves and sturdy top-loaders to preserve the card’s surface. A well-protected card ages better and looks better on display.
- Grading prep: If you plan to grade, don’t attempt to rub a hidden scratch away with a sleeve; that’s how you ruin a potential gem. Instead, store it in a pro-grade binder and get it evaluated by a reputable grader.
- Shipping: For fragile shipments, consider insured, tracked shipping. Vintage cards are precious, and you want to minimize the risk of transit drama.

Where to buy: Specialty vendors, reputable online marketplaces with strong seller ratings, and fan communities offer the best chances for legitimate copies, including those no. 044. Compare prices, view photos, and always request high-resolution scans to check for surface wear.
