The rug is advertised as eco-friendly and made from recycled cotton. In practice, that means fewer new crops and less water wasted on growing cotton for this particular square yard of floor art.

The texture is soft enough to sit on barefoot and sturdy enough to resist the kind of vacuum cleaner love you only give to the area near the sofa. There is an ongoing debate about the full life cycle of recycled cotton products, and we won’t pretend to have a perfect carbon score here. ### Care and Maintenance Without Losing Your Mind
Care is where the geek in us shines, because the more rational you are about maintenance, the longer your sofa wins the style lottery. Here are practical tips we tested:

- Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting to avoid pulling threads. Think of it as a firmware update for your living space.
- Treat spills promptly. Blot, don’t rub. Rubbing is just a polite way to turn a spill into a full-on disaster scenario.
- For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution works fine. Test on a hidden corner first, because even recycled cotton appreciates a good privacy screen.
- When washing, use a gentle cycle with cold water. Avoid hot water that could cause the fibers to shrink like a hoodie in a dryer vent.
- Air dry flat to maintain the knit’s shape. The goal is to avoid warping or unwanted curling at the edges.

The result is a rug that asks for minimal ritual care but rewards you with maximum comfort. It’s not a designer-only maintenance regime; it’s a practical routine fit for busy humans, apartment life, and the occasional late-night coding sprint where you forgot to close the window before the coffee spilled.
