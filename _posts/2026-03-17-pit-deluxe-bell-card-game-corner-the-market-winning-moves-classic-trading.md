### A quick note on what Pit is
Pit is a real time commodity trading game where players shout bids and offers for sealed sets of cards representing commodities like wheat, corn, sugar, and more exotic items such as coffee, cocoa, and silk. The goal is to be the first to collect a set of four different commodities, or in some variants, to keep trading until someone hoards enough to win. Pit Deluxe takes the base concept and adds variants and better components. The bell variant in Pit Deluxe with Bell is designed to speed up games and provide audible feedback to players who may be missing a rapid set of trades.

### How to set up
1. Shuffle the commodity deck and lay it in the center as the bank. 
2. Each player takes four trading markers.
3. Decide the dealer as the first ring-the-bell player and set the timer of sorts by agreeing on how long you will shout before a trade ends in victory or humiliation.
4. Everyone closer to the bell is allowed to shout first.

The setup is straightforward; there is not much to fear beyond the possibility of your neighbors discovering a secret talent for rapid-fire, high-stakes offers that somehow involve turning a bag of corn into a summer sale on the street.

To be fair, the initial setup is a small ritual of its own. You arrange your markers, you mutter a small prayer to the gods of probability, and you try not to cry when you realize that the first exchange will be for something you thought you had a strong chance to acquire.

### How the gameplay works
Pit Deluxe with Bell uses real-time trading where players simultaneously bid on commodities by shouting the number of units they want to buy or sell. The twist is a bell that signals a new round of trading, and sometimes a little extra time to process the current deals. The player who shouts the largest trade wins that round, buys or sells as agreed upon, and then passes the bell to the next player.

The core objective is to collect four different commodities, either by trading or acquiring them through the large scale shouting match. The person who completes the set wins. In this edition, the bell adds a fun level of mechanical feedback, telling you loud and clear when you have reached the apex of trading chaos.

This is real-time auction meets party game. You need to keep your cool, you need to talk your way into trades without insulting the other players, and you need to be careful about confusion because the table might start to look like a busy stock ticker from a late night sci-fi broadcast.

### The market mechanics and what makes this edition special
The Market is a thematic framing in Pit. In Pit Deluxe with Bell, the concept of the market exists as the board of commodities in front of every player and as the entire dynamic of language, supply, and demand. The more you shout, the more likely you are to make silly mistakes, but the faster you go, the more likely you are to secure a set that wins the game.

The market mechanic relies on the idea that all players are trying to trade one or more commodities to collect a full set. The ring of the bell signals that a trade window is open, and you can shout your offers to all participants. The chaos of fast trading makes it nearly impossible to keep track of every player's offers; this is where the clacking of the markers on the table becomes a rhythm section.

The advantage of the bell is obvious: it creates a rhythm that players can respond to. If you are a player who hates silence, Pit Deluxe with Bell is your dream game. The trade window becomes a performance, and you are the lead actor delivering the lines of negotiation.

### Strategy tips and how to approach Pit Deluxe with Bell
- Start with simple sets. The more you rush, the more likely you are to make silly mistakes, but the simpler sets are easier to assemble with the bell ringing on every other turn.
- Keep your shouting clear. Do not attempt to compress three words into a single phrase; the more you speak clearly, the more likely you will be able to close a deal. 
- Use misdirection. The speed of the game means you can mislead your opponents with a simple double-check to see if they are paying attention.
- Practice your counting. The game is about sets, but you also need to count accurately how many of each commodity you already own to avoid over-trading or under-trading.
