## How to Play with the Batman Deck: Rule tweaks and play feel
UNO is UNO, right? The Batsuit edition doesn’t reinvent the wheel but adds a few flourishes to ensure the theme remains integrated into the rules rather than added as a forced afterthought.

### Core rules you’ll notice right away
- Standard UNO rules apply: match by color or number, draw until you can play, use action cards to disrupt your friends’ progress, and declare UNO when you’re down to one card.
- If your group loves flavored variants, you’ll appreciate the Batman flavor cards that visually cue the role-cards and actions. The deck uses stylized action icons that mimic hero versus villain encounters, giving players a quick visual read on who’s delivering a penalty or a power play.
- Collectible “signal” cards can be used as silly table markers. They’re not necessary for the game to function, but they add a playful component to keep the table engaged.

### Possible rule tweaks for a smoother night
Some groups want light house rules for the Batman deck to balance speed and interaction. Here are a few optional tweaks you can consider:
- Bat-Signal nudge: If a player flips the Bat-Signal card, everyone must reveal one card from their hand. The player with the most Batman-related cards (as judged by the majority) might get to choose who draws two cards or who loses a turn.
- Robin’s help: If a player has a Robin card (thematically a helper), they can swap hands with a player of their choice once per game. This is obviously optional and keeps the vibe playful rather than punitive.
- Crisis mode: On any Turn About Batman event (a theme-specific name for when someone plays a wild card), you can add a mini-dare like telling a bat-junior pun or sharing a silly memory from the show. It’s not a formal rule, but it creates a social mood that matches the deck’s tone.
