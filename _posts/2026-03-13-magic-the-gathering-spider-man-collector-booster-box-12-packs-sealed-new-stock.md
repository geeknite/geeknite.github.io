## Build and Draft Experience: Sealed Play with a Twist
Sealed play in a collector booster box is different from standard draft. In sealed, you’re building a 40-card deck from a pool of cards, with a focus on synergy and consistency rather than raw bomb power. The collector boosters, however, tilt that balance toward premium cards and flashy foils. The 12 packs give you a larger sample size of cards to comb through, which can be thrilling for new players and seasoned collectors alike.

In practice, you’ll want to pull creatures and spells that can anchor your deck while the premium cards act as the finishers or the big swing turns. If you’re lucky and your pulls align with a Spider-Man themed engine or a synergy with web-slinging tricks, you could assemble a sealed deck that feels both thematic and surprisingly competitive in casual games.

A few tips for sealed with this product: prioritize consistency over flash. Foil reprints can be gorgeous but may be thin on straightforward synergy. Look for cards that offer removal, blockers, or card draw that can function in a variety of matchups. The collector aspect is nice, but don’t rely on a single lottery ticket card to carry your whole deck.
