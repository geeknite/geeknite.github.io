## Materials and construction: cotton meets wool
This dhurrie rug pairs cotton and wool in a way that isn’t just about aesthetics. It’s about performance. Cotton fibers give you a smooth surface underfoot and a sheen that catches light in a friendly, non-sparky way. Wool adds resilience and a bit of insulation that makes a chilly morning feel less like stepping onto a glacier. The stripes themselves are created by tightly interlacing the warp and weft in alternating color runs, resulting in a flat, dense weave that holds up to daily life and the occasional toddler, dog, or coffee spill.

From a maintenance perspective, cotton is relatively straightforward to clean—though you still want to avoid soaking it. Wool brings a little more resilience to abrasion, though you should treat it with the same care you would extend to your favorite wool sweater: vacuum regularly (on a low setting) and address spills promptly. A simple rule of thumb: the sooner you blot, the less likely a stain will set. For those who want to treat themselves to a deeper clean, a gentle rug shampoo or a product designed for delicate fibers can keep the weave looking vibrant without turning your living room into a chemistry lab.

## Care and durability: making it last
Care is not glamorous, but it matters. The hybrid cotton-wool blend used in this rug is designed to stand up to everyday life, but it isn’t indestructible. Here are some practical care guidelines to keep the stripes bright and the fibers happy:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting. Avoid beater bars that can loosen fibers; a simple suction is enough to lift dust without fraying the weave.
- Clean spills promptly. Blot with a clean, dry cloth rather than rubbing, which can push the spill deeper into the fibers. For liquids, a mild detergent solution can help, but test on a small area first.
- Rotate periodically. This helps even out wear in high-traffic zones and keeps the stripe pattern looking balanced from all angles.
- Avoid direct sun for extended periods. Prolonged exposure can fade colors; if you have strong sun in the living area, consider rotating the rug occasionally or using window treatments to soften the sun’s impact.
- Professional cleaning: if you need a deeper refresh, consult a rug cleaning service experienced with flatwoven fabrics. They can refresh the fibers without compromising the weave.
