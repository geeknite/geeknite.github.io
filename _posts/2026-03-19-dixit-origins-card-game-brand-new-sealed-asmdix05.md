## How to Play (A Gentle Refresher)

Dixit Origins uses the core mechanism that has kept this series alive for more than a decade: a storyteller picks a card from their hand and gives a clue about that card. The clue should be evocative but not so obvious that everyone immediately knows which card is the storyteller s. The other players pick a card from their own hands that best matches the clue and all chosen cards are laid face up. The group then votes on which card they think is the storyteller s. If everyone guesses correctly or if no one guesses correctly, the storyteller loses points; if only some players guess correctly, the storyteller gains points for being clever and the guessers score points for accuracy. It is a simple core mechanic, but the elegance comes from how it invites iteration and misdirection without ever crossing into cryptic frustration.

In Origins, the rules are presented in a way that welcomes newcomers. The pace is comfortable, the turns flow, and the tension grows as players watch the board accumulate points (or, more often, watch the score remain suspiciously close because of your own questionable clue choices). The adaptive nature of storytelling means that you can scale the game to your group s vibe. If you have a table full of jokesters, you will lean into witty clues; if you play with a family crowd, you may prefer gentler hints that encourage collaboration and shared smiles.

### Round Flow: A Typical Turn
1. The storyteller chooses a card from their hand and says a clue that hints at that card s content without rendering it obvious.
2. Each other player selects a card from their hand that could plausibly fit the clue, trying to misdirect while staying relevant.
3. Cards are revealed and pooled; players vote on the card that matches the storyteller s clue most closely.
4. Points are awarded according to how many players correctly guessed and whether the storyteller s card was selected as the winner by the majority.

The social dynamics are what make Dixit Origins sing. You will hear players describe a card as a memory, a feeling, or a silly inside joke from a previous night of gaming. The best moments happen when someone nails a clue that perfectly aligns with a card s image in a way that is unexpected and deeply personal, yet universally understandable. You can tell a group is having a good time when the table roars with laughter and someone mentions that the clue is so close to a real life moment that you must have a secret telepathic connection to the artist who drew the card. It is chess in a dream laboratory, with a dash of improv and a sprinkle of party game chaos. The key is to read the room: some groups crave precise connections, others enjoy ambiguous clues that require creative interpretation. The game rewards risk and imagination, but never punishes players for thinking outside the box too much. The box in your hands is a passport to a shared moment of imagination.
