Maintenance and care:
- Vacuum regularly: Use a low-suction setting or a soft roller to avoid crushing the fibers. If you’re a fan of robotic vacuum cleaners, you’ll be pleasantly surprised at how well many roombas handle the rug’s surface when guided by a straightforward boundary.
- Spot clean stains promptly: The sooner you treat spills, the better the results. Use a mild detergent and water solution; blot—don’t rub hard—as rubbing can push the stain deeper and weaken the fibers.
- Avoid machine washing: Most area rugs of this type aren’t designed for machine washing. If you have a stubborn stain or require a truly deep clean, consult a professional rug cleaning service.
- Rotate occasionally: Rotating the rug every so often helps even out wear and helps the floor underneath stay consistent in color and finish.
- Pet hair management: A pet hair magnet like this rug plus a good vacuum-polisher approach can help. You’ll find the fur clinging less to the surface than to a rougher fiber rug.
