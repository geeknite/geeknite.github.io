## Strategy tips and how to use the new tools

The new cards aren’t just flavor; they shift the game’s decision tree. Here are practical tips to integrate the expansion without turning your table into a math lab:

- Manage defuses like a magician handles a rabbit: The expansion introduces pressure to defuse more thoughtfully. Don’t just spam defuses; consider timing, risk, and the potential for other players to bluff their way into a safe zone.
- Read your table, not just your hand: The added card types create richer misdirection. If you can sell a believable read that you’re out of danger while secretly plotting a counter move, you’ll keep others guessing and yourselves in the game longer.
- Don’t overcommit early: The new tools reward late engagement. A lean early hand paired with a clever late-game combo can turn the table’s momentum in your favor when the final few cards come into play.
- Balance is king: The more ways to influence turns, the easier it is to swing too far into aggressiveness. Those who pace themselves, observe others’ tells, and deploy a measured risk strategy will outperform the sprint-and-bluff crowd over the long night.
- Diversify your threats: Because there are more ways to manipulate turns, building a mixed strategy-—bluff, defuse pressure, and calculated gambits—tends to yield better results than going all-in on one move type.

### House rules to consider
If your group enjoys tinkering, here are a few ideas that play nicely with the expansion while keeping the chaos light:
- Extra defuse, extra risk: Allow one extra defuse per round in exchange for shuffling one additional risk card into the deck. It forces deliberate choices and raises the stakes slightly.
- Cat chorus finale: If a specific combo with the new cards is pulled off, everyone at the table must sing a line from a cat-themed jingle. It’s silly, it’s silly, and it spices up the table talk—especially if that combo triggers a near-miss moment.
- Time-boxed rounds: For fast-paced games, enforce a round timer and allow the last player to take a final action before the timer hits zero. It preserves momentum for larger groups while giving latecomers a fair chance.
- The “purr-fect defuse” negotiation: Allow players a brief, one-minute negotiation window per round where they can chat about potential defuses with table mates. It increases interaction at the risk of over-analysis, which is part of the fun.
