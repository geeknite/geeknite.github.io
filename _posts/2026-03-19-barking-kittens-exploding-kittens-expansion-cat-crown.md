## How to Play with Barking Kittens

If you are a veteran Exploding Kittens player, the addition of Barking Kittens should feel like you upgraded from a bicycle to a motorcycle with training wheels. Here is a simple framework to get you into the chaos quickly:

1. Shuffle Barking Kittens into your base deck or keep it as a separate mini deck for a modular experience.
2. Decide who gets to wear the Cat Crown when the timer hits a critical moment. The crown upgrades a basic action into a power move that may save you or wreck your evening.
3. Play as usual, but pay attention to the bark mechanic. Bark cards replace some of the traditional meows with bark like actions that push the game into unpredictable territory.
4. When the Cat Crown is Activated, the wearer gets to alter one rule around the table for a single turn. Expect sneaky influences like forced draws, swapped hands, or a random effect that benefits the crown holder. Yes, the crown is cheeky.
5. End the round with the final explosion token, which now has an extra paw print on it that indicates where the chaos landed.

If you have a sense of humor about losing, Barking Kittens will make you feel like you are the star of a tiny chaotic show for just long enough to give you another round of applause and another round of drinks.

## Cat Crown Mechanic Deep Dive

The Cat Crown is the centerpiece of Barking Kittens. It is a token that can be acquired at specific moments in the game, or perhaps traded for a paw print counter in the middle of a heated argument about which meme is the most powerful. The exact rules can vary depending on house rules, but the gist is this: the crown empowers the wearer to alter the course of one action per turn. This moment can be used to force a redraw on a risky player, swap hands, or convert a safe draw into an explosive dansing moment. The crown is very powerful, but not omnipotent. Its power is limited by the chaos around the table and by the willingness of players to cooperate or sabotage the crown holder.

The crown turn introduces a new dynamic into the endgame. The final explosion can be triggered with extra pomp and circumstance as the crown holder uses the final turn to shift the last few cards and decide who stays in the game and who exits with a dramatic flip of the table. The purple glow of the crown in the art is a nice flourish, a small wink from the designer that says this expansion knows it is a silly product and loves it anyway.

## Strategy Tips for Barking Kittens

- Embrace chaos: Do not fight it. The Cat Crown thrives when players attempt to control the inevitable randomness. Let the crown do the heavy lifting while you practice your victory dance.
- Coordinate with the bark cards: The bark actions can disrupt traditional strategies. Use them to push rivals into making bad decisions that you can exploit later in the round.
- Keep track of timing: The crown is most valuable in the last few turns. It is not a magic wand that saves you on Turn 2. Save your moves for the moment when the table is hot and everyone is itching for a dramatic finish.
- Manage your table talk: Part of the fun is the social aspect. Do not be shy about taunting rivals and boasting about your cat crown. A little swagger goes a long way toward keeping the game lively.
