### Seating posture and ergonomic notes
Ergonomically, the design invites a neutral posture if you align your chair correctly and keep monitor heights appropriate. The unit’s screens can be positioned to avoid glare and to keep neck strain at bay. If you’re sharing the space, you’ll want a quick, low-friction approach to monitor alignment so no one’s screen is in someone else’s conversation. We recommend standard ergonomic checks: monitor top line at or slightly below eye level, elbows at ~90 degrees, wrists neutral, and feet flat on the floor or on a footrest. If your team tends to lean into collaborative huddles, consider adding adjustable monitor arms to fine-tune heights when folks trade tasks mid-project.

### Step-by-step assembly tips
Take a deep breath. Here’s a pragmatic assembly rhythm that won’t break your back or your sense of humor:
- Start with the corner module. It’s the spine of the house; assemble it and secure it to the floor if you’re dealing with a high-traffic environment.
- Attach the peripheral panels evenly. This ensures a uniform look and reduces wiggling when someone slides a chair across the surface.
- Route cables early. Pointing them now is much easier than rerouting later when your team discovers a playlist of work-in-progress projects you forgot to save.
- Install the screens and ensure tilt/height adjustments don’t collide with the desk edges. If you’re using monitors, mock up a height plan that you can share with teammates before you commit to a hole-saw moment.
- Check for stability. A four-person workspace should feel anchored, not spasmodic; tighten screws and confirm that there’s no creak or wobble during a “soft ping” test (placing a lightweight object and nudging it lightly).

If you’re new to assembly, a friendly pro tip: keep a playlist of your favorite celebratory songs, because you’ll be listening to them while you make sense of the instruction diagrams. It makes the whole process feel like a mini-adventure rather than a chore. ## Maintenance, care, and longevity

The Eternal is built to last, but nothing is immortal in a busy office. Regular checks for loose screws, cleaning of dust from under-desk troughs, and periodic re-tensioning of panels will keep the unit looking pristine. For cleaning, a microfiber cloth with a mild cleaner is enough for the laminated surfaces. Avoid harsh abrasives that could degrade the finish. If you’ve got screens, a gentle microfiber to wipe fingerprints keeps things readable and civilized. And yes, those integrated cable channels collect dust; a quick once-every-two-months pass with a vacuum attachment will ensure your under-desk area remains breathable for the tiny dust goblins that live there.
