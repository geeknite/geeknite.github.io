## Go Fuc Yourself: The Must-Have Game for Adult Game Nights

In Geeknite style we celebrate the brave moments when a group breaks into spontaneous roast battles and still pretends to be mostly civilized. Go Fuc Yourself is the kind of game that turns a quiet dinner into an all you can laugh buffet. The premise is simple: two decks, a charismatic host, and a table full of opinions.

### What is Go Fuc Yourself?

#### The core concept
Go Fuc Yourself is built around two decks: the prompt deck and the response deck. Each round one player acts as host and draws a prompt card with a provocative statement or question. The other players secretly select a response card from their hands that best matches the prompt. The host then reads the match aloud and everyone votes on which response is the most savage, witty, or cringe worthy. The winning player gets a point, typically by taking the prompt card into their victory pile. The game continues until someone reaches the target score, usually five or seven.

#### Why it works for adult game nights
This design blends improv energy with card game rhythm. You get the spontaneity of a roast without needing a stand up mic, and you avoid the long tail of a joke that never lands. The prompts provide a scaffold; the responses supply the punch lines. The best part is watching alliances form and crumble in real time as someone ruthlessly counters an absurd prompt with a perfectly delivered pun or a cringe worthy comeback.

### Setup and components

#### What you should expect in the box
- A two deck system that you will treat like a chaotic set of witty missiles
- A host marker or a small die to decide who roasts first
- A timer for crisp rounds (optional but highly encouraged)
- A referee card with the house rules
- A compact score pad to track who dominates the table

#### The decks and the vibe
The prompt deck is heavy on spicy scenarios, pop culture nods, and the occasional inside joke. The response deck is where your crowd control radar goes off; there are responses that land, a few that miss, and some that become inside jokes you carry for years. Good card quality matters because you will shuffle, riff, and throw them around the table with a level of drama that would impress a theater troupe.

### How to play

#### The basic flow
1. Shuffle both decks.
2. Decide the host using the method you agree on, perhaps a dramatic spin of the dice or rock paper scissors.
3. The host draws a prompt card and reads it aloud to set the vibe.
4. Each of the other players secretly selects one response card from their hand that best matches the prompt.
5. The host reveals the chosen responses one by one, reads the best pair aloud, and leads a vote for the round winner.
6. The winning player takes the prompt card as a point; discard used cards to a waste pile.
7. The next round begins with a new host.

#### Round remix and pacing tips
In actual game nights you will experience varying degrees of humor: some rounds will feel like a well timed roast, others might be cringe worthy. The important thing is that the group stays in the same vibe and nobody crosses a boundary that makes someone uncomfortable. If a round feels too spicy, switch to a lighter prompt deck or pause to re align the energy.

### Strategy and tips

#### Reading the room
The most valuable skill here is social radar. If you can sense how far your table is willing to go, you will dodge the awkward silence and find the sweet spot where jokes land and stay on the right side of friendly competition. Try to tailor your responses not just to the prompt but to the likely reaction of the host and players.

#### Mixing decks for extra flavor
Expansions or home grown prompts can add a lot of flavor. You can mix in new prompts with older responses to create fresh mismatches that surprise the table. Always leave room for a wild card moment; the table will remember the moment when a random third option destroyed the entire room.

### Variations and house rules
#### Quick variants for instant chaos
- The Roast Round: designate a moment to roast a chosen target and let the table decide the best roast.
- The Double Tap: players can play a second response if the first misses, increasing the chaotic potential.
- The Audience Choice: if you have a bigger group, include an audience vote with a phone app to decide the winner.

#### House rules that actually improve things
- PG mode: remove prompts that cross a personal line; you can slowly reintroduce them as the group grows more comfortable.
- Time champ: set a shorter timer to keep energy high and attention sharp.
- Ambient roasts: require every player to deliver a supportive compliment before a roast to keep things from going too sour.
