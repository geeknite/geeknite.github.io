## How Exploding Kittens 2015 plays on the table (the quick and the chaotic)
Exploding Kittens thrives on a blend of luck and bluff, with a heavy emphasis on social interaction. The game is designed for quick rounds—perfect for those nights when you want to sprint through a few rounds and still have the energy to talk about memes. The basic flow goes something like this:

- Setup: Players receive a hand of cards and the deck is shuffled. The first to draw an exploding kitten without a defuse loses the round. The tension starts immediately because even the slowest draw can trigger chaos.
- Turn structure: On your turn you draw a card. You can also play action cards to alter your fate or to sabotage someone else. Action cards include skipping draws, peeking into the future, or causing someone else to shuffle the deck. The presence of combined action cards creates a sense that every choice matters—even if it does not always pan out as planned.
- Card variety: Defuse cards provide a lifeline. See the Future cards give you glimpses into one or more upcoming draws. Nope cards let you declare that you are not fooled by another player’s attempt to save or steal your fate. The interaction among these cards is where the game’s humor and tension reside.
- Endgame: The round ends when someone draws an exploding kitten and cannot defuse it. The winner keeps playing, while the loser sits the round out and waits for the next match to dawn, armed with a new strategy and perhaps a healthier respect for cats that genuinely want to ruin your day.

Yes, it is simple on the surface, and yes, that is the point. The elegance lies in how small decisions can snowball into ridiculous outcomes. You will discover your friends and family developing signature strategies, like hoarding see-the-future cards until someone dares to talk smack, or attempting to bluff with a defuse in hand while pretending you have a rescue plan ready if the kitten explosion comes. It’s a house of cards built on a light foundation of chaos—and it works brilliantly.
