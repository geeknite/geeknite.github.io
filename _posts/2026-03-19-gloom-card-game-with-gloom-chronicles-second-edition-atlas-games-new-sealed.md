In the Gloom system, the goal is to make your family of ill-fated characters as miserable as possible before they die, while trying to ensure your opponents’ families meet a happier ending (or at least a less glorious funeral). In Chronicles, Atlas Games adds new arcs and revised rules to streamline the storytelling and reduce the “rules overhead” for the sake of more table talk and fewer referees. The joy of Gloom is not in perfect math but in the narrative chaos you and your friends create together.

How to play: a walk-through of the core loop

Setup: getting started quickly

To begin, you shuffle the deck of gloom and story cards and deal out a starting set for each player. Each player receives a starting character and perhaps a couple of supporting characters—enough to establish a personal narrative thread at the table. The rules guide you through a simple sequence: (1) draw, (2) play gloom and story cards, (3) react to opponents’ interventions, (4) narrate, (5) resolve end-of-round scoring. The Chronicles edition’s goal is to make this loop feel natural rather than a weaponized set of rules. The result is something you can learn in a single practice round, then lean into for the rest of the night.

Turn structure and momentum

In each round, players take turns playing gloom cards to worsen their own characters or to intervene on others’ stories in clever, often theatrical ways. Story cards introduce narrative prompts that push players to narrate beyond the card text, while arc mechanics reward players for weaving a longer, more coherent tale. The balance between “tell the story” and “score the gloom” is deliberately tuned so that table talk remains king. You’ll hear everyone’s voices rise as the drama escalates, you’ll witness someone try a pun that earns groans and laughs in equal measure, and you’ll feel the room tighten as a beloved character teeters on the brink of a grim finale.

Gloom mechanics and the storytelling engine

The gloom cards function as a punishment or misfortune for characters, with each card carrying a text blurb and a numeric weight that affects scoring. The more gloom a character has when the round ends, the higher their final score—until the last card is played and the tally is made. The interplay with story cards and arc prompts is what makes Chronicles shine: you can seize a moment to craft a character’s tragedy with a punny line or a dramatic pause, then watch it ripple through the table as others either attempt a rescue or, more likely, a new catastrophe.

Arc-driven narrative: what’s new in Chronicles Second Edition

The Chronicles edition pushes you toward longer narrative arcs. Arc cards provide a structure for your game night, offering thematic goals and new scoring opportunities tied to your group’s storytelling. The arcs aren’t arbitrary; they’re designed to create a sense of forward momentum, a “season” of gloom rather than a one-off tragedy. This is a meaningful addition for players who want to revisit the same families across multiple sessions, watching their misfortunes compound in often ridiculous, always entertaining ways. The arc system also helps organize meta-narratives within the table—what you’re trying to achieve across multiple rounds becomes clear, and that clarity helps players commit to more ambitious storytelling.
