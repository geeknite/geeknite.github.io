## Gameplay Tips: Getting the Most Out of the Expansion
### Setup, pacing, and party dynamics
- Start with a light rotation: designate a rotating Card Czar so the same player doesn’t always shoulder the burden of choosing the black card.
- Use a “no repeats” rule within a session to keep the tone fresh and the cards from becoming stale too quickly.
- Balance the game’s energy by mixing in lighter rounds between heavy, edgy rounds. This helps maintain group morale and ensures everyone gets a chance to shine.
- Keep a few safe-word triggers in mind; it’s okay to pause and acknowledge when a line has crossed a boundary. The goal is to keep the room laughing, not uncomfortable.

### Card-craft: maximizing the combos
- Look for white cards that can be read in multiple ways. The expansion often rewards clever delivery, so practice your timing and emphasis.
- Don’t be afraid to reframe a joke. Sometimes a white card appears too perfect for one black card but hits perfectly with another. The room will tell you what works.
- If you’re playing with a larger group, consider team-based rounds. Teams can discuss and craft a more layered punchline, which often yields bigger laughs than solo efforts.

### Safety and consent in humor
- Humor can be a social lubricant or a trigger depending on the context. Always ensure that participants are comfortable with the level of edge. If someone is quietly asking for a gentler round, honor that request. It’s still CAH at the end of the day.
- If you’re streaming or sharing content from a CAH night, be mindful of your audience and potential sensitive material. You can still capture the chaos and the laughs without revealing explicit content that could be misinterpreted by a broader audience.
