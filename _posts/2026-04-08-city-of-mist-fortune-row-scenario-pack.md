## Overview

City of Mist: Fortune Row is the latest scenario pack for the game that pretends to be modern-day mythic noir and sometimes forgets it's a role-playing game about masks, myths, and mistaken coffee orders. Fortune Row drops you into a neon-drenched district where luck, crime, and a few too many fortune cookies decide who lives and who gets to blame destiny. This review is not just a puff piece to justify buying a book, it's a careful, occasionally-snarky assessment of how this drop-in pack slots into your table and whether it makes the Word of Mist feel fresh, fun, and not entirely chaotic.

### What’s in Fortune Row?

- 3 linked adventures that can be run back-to-back or as standalone vignettes in a single session
- 1 GM screen featuring quick prompts, scene hooks, and timers for chases
- 40 NPC cards that include relationships, secrets, and misdirections
- 5 new Masks to explore
- 2 location maps with encounter tables
- 1 prep sheet to help you plan your session in under an episode of your favorite streaming show
- 1 player handout with a bite-sized fortune mechanic that can unlock a twist at the table

The box also contains a neat set of handouts designed to be printed and cut, because nothing says “we value your time” like letting players cut out their own destiny.

### Who is Fortune Row for?

If your table loves neon-noir, myth-punk vibes, and the sensation that fate itself is an unreliable co-writer, this pack is for you. It’s especially friendly to:

- GMs who want a plug-and-play arc with adjustable length
- Players who enjoy multi-NPC threads, skill challenges, and social intricacies
- Tables that want to dip into the City of Mist mythos without a deep dive into the core rulebook
- Groups that appreciate a strong urban setting with a moral gray zone

Fortune Row also serves as a gentle entry point for new players who can lean on the pack’s structured scene prompts rather than trying to spin together an entire city’s worth of subplots from scratch.

### Why Geeknite loves this pack

We like Fortune Row because it respects the core strengths of City of Mist: the masks, the undercity secrets, and the sense that ordinary storefronts hide extraordinary legends. It also dares to lean into a very specific vibe—slick, satirical, and a little bit cruel—without turning the whole thing into a grimdark slog. It’s a pack that gives you both a mood and a mechanism, which is a delicate balance to strike.

## Design and Mechanics

City of Mist uses the Masks mechanic as a way to represent a character’s emergent powers and the way society labels them. Fortune Row expands that vocabulary with new Masks, more social scenes, and a handful of new tags that alter how actions are resolved. Here’s what stands out:

### New Masks and Mythic Tools

Fortune Row introduces five new Masks, each with a twist on the usual power set. They’re not just “cool names with cool coats”; they come with:
- unique compels that fit the Fortune Row setting
- thematic links to local factions
- recommended scene hooks that map well to the three adventure modules

If you’re used to tiered arcs in City of Mist, these Masks feel like a natural extension rather than a forced novelty.

### The GM Tools That Don’t Get In The Way

GM tools matter in City of Mist because the GM is juggling multiple secret factions, a handful of player-driven mysteries, and a clock that sometimes runs faster than your pizza delivery. Fortune Row provides:
- a clean, printable GM screen with quick prompts
- encounter tables aligned to the three chapters
- a “scene flow” sheet that helps you keep track of when a scene should escalate or pivot
- ready-to-run stat blocks for NPCs that aren’t overcomplicated, but have enough bite to be memorable

This is not a lazy recitation of stats; it’s a toolkit designed to save you table time, which is the actual currency of most gaming sessions.

### Pacing, Tone, and Session Length

One of the trickiest things with any City of Mist product is getting the pacing right. Fortune Row gives you a recommended arc length that scales from one-shot to a short campaign, with an eye toward keeping scenes punchy. The three adventures are designed to move with a rhythm: hook, investigation, confrontation, then reveal. The “investigation” phase can feel a little long if you treat it as a pure puzzle hunt, but the pack’s prompts and NPC threads help you keep momentum without forcing a sprint.

### Locations, NPCs, and Life in Fortune Row

The maps cover a couple of district-scale locales and a handful of interior spaces that get used again and again. The NPC cards are well-thought-out, with secrets that are easy to remember but tricky to piece together in the players’ minds. The best NPCs in Fortune Row have motivations that feel big enough to matter across sessions, but small enough to fit into a single night.

If you like interwoven threads—where a crime boss’s right-hand has a crush on a detective while the fortune-teller at the corner stall knows more than she’s saying—Fortune Row gives you plenty of relational hooks. The pack’s writing makes it easy to improvise: you’ll find a lot of “this could happen next” prompts that the GM can drop into a scene when players stray off-script.

### Thematic Lore and Arcana

Fortune Row leans into the mythos of fate as something that can be negotiated, bargained with, or broken. The arcana (City of Mist’s version of magic) get new twists here, and the new Masks seem to have a natural synergy with the way fortunes and prophecies operate within the city. If you like the idea that everyone has a “story,” Fortune Row gives you a batch of micro-magasins (okay, micro-story devices) that you can slot into scenes to keep the themes front-and-center.

## In-Box Experience and Presentation

From a physical perspective, Fortune Row is a solid entry in the City of Mist line. The layout is clean, the art direction nails the neon noir vibe, and the tone stays consistent across the three adventures. If you’ve previously bought other City of Mist products, you’ll feel at home with the typography, the card stock, and the iconography used for Masks and factions.

The artwork has a balance of style and readability that makes it easy to describe a scene to players without getting bogged down in long-winded prose. The cover art screams “Fortune Row” in a way that’s hard to miss on a shelf, and the internal art pieces do a good job of illustrating mood rather than crowding players with extraneous detail.

Jekyll and Hyde moment: there’s a small but welcome nod to meta-narrative devices—the pack uses a few card prompts that feel like fortune cookies with consequences, and you can use them as quick improv beats to anchor a scene when players try to push a plot thread in a different direction.

## How to Use Fortune Row at Your Table

- Step 1: Pick your chairperson. The GM uses the Fortune Row materials to set a provisional arc, then invites players to lock in their Masks and relationships.
- Step 2: Read the scenario hooks aloud, but don’t read the whole book to the table in one go. Let players discover secrets at their own pace.
- Step 3: Use the NPC cards as a relational map. If a player asks, “Who is this person to you?” you can pull a prebuilt micro-mystery from the card to spur a scene.
- Step 4: Keep a clock visible. The pack’s pacing suggestions are helpful, but you’ll still need to manage time in a way that fits your table’s rhythm.
- Step 5: Wrap with a twist. Fortune Row leans into a few twists that feel earned, not forced. The “fortune cookie” mechanic is not overused, and when it lands it lands hard.

### Running the three adventures

Each module has its own flavor, but they share a spine: investigation, underworld politics, and a climactic confrontation that tests both Masks and loyalties. The arcs can be stitched together for a longer campaign or split into one-shot sessions, depending on your cadence.

### Telegraphed expectations for players

Fortune Row isn’t about “solve the puzzle” style play; it’s about “navigate people, motives, and rules of a city where fortune can be gamed.” Players should expect social complexity, a bit of detective work, and moments where a single choice can ripple through both the city and the mythic fabric of their Mask.

## Final Verdict

Fortune Row is not a radical overhaul of City of Mist, but it is a robust, well-paced, and thematically coherent addition to the line. If your table enjoys a city where luck, crime, and myth collide, this pack offers a clean path from first contact to dramatic ending. It’s particularly effective for groups that want a curated arc with strong NPC dynamics and a tight pacing plan. The new Masks feel distinct enough to warrant trying at least one session even if you’re already satisfied with the base Masks in the core rulebook. The GM tools and the well-considered scene prompts are the star here; they take the pressure off you as the facilitator and let you focus on table banter and clutch decisions.

Pro: Great mood, cohesive design, strong support for GMs, adaptable length, good NPCs and new Masks
Con: The pacing can dip if players insist on exhaustive investigations without a plan to escalate, and the fortune-cookie prompts may feel gimmicky in the wrong table

### Final recommendation

If you’re on the fence about whether Fortune Row is worth picking up for your City of Mist table, the answer is yes—with caveats. It shines when you want a polished, mood-heavy urban mystery with strong NPCs and a tight pacing plan. If you’re after a sandboxy mega-campaign, you might be happier with other packs or with the core system and your own homebrew content. But if you want a confidence-boosted GM toolkit and a comic-noir ride that respects the mythic mechanics that already exist in City of Mist, Fortune Row will likely become a regular feature on your shelf.
