The most distinctive feature of Fortune Row is its set of custom faces. The symbols give the dice a personality, which is essential in a game built on myth-making and layered identities.

## Mechanics and Play Experience
### Integrating Fortune Row with City of Mist rules
City of Mist uses a system that emphasizes narrative momentum, with the GM setting the stakes and the players rolling for outcomes that shape the story. Fortune Row can be slotted into this framework in a couple of intuitive ways:

- Symbol-driven action economy: Each symbol on a die can correspond to a tier of action. For example, a mask could grant a social advantage (stealth, deception, or misdirection), a clock might govern time-limited actions (you’ve got one turn before a trap triggers), a key could unlock a new option (a door, a choice, or a leverage point), and a spark could represent a bold, spectacular move (a stunt or a dramatic reveal).
- Monumental dice pool shifts: In sessions where players lean into fate and myth, Fortune Row can act as a focal point for temporary dice pools. When fate seems broad and uncertain, a subset of Fortune Row dice can be rolled to generate “fortune tokens” that influence scene beats later in the session. This signals a shift from micro-actions to macro-story momentum.

From a mechanical standpoint, the biggest potential pitfall is overloading players with too many symbolic options to track. Fortune Row succeeds best when the GM and players commit to a shared vocabulary around symbols. A short glossary on a reference card can go a long way toward keeping the table aligned and avoiding “symbol confusion” during critical moments.

These beats show how Fortune Row can turn a mechanical roll into a storytelling hinge, a moment that characterizes the mythic noir vibe City of Mist loves to chase.

## Practical Notes: Setup, Playability, and Longevity
### Setup and table time
Unboxing Fortune Row doesn’t require a long setup ritual. You can crack the lid, pop the dice into your hand, and you’re ready to roll within a few minutes. The more important variable is how you introduce the symbols to your players. In one session, we used a quick reference sheet that mapped each symbol to a narrative cue. It saved time, kept everyone on the same page, and prevented the table from spending 15 minutes debating what a symbol actually means in-game. A simple one-pager with symbol meanings and a couple of example prompts is all you need to keep momentum.

### Playability across scenarios
Fortune Row scales nicely from one-shot mysteries to longer arcs. For one-shots, you’ll want to predefine a handful of symbol-driven prompts that you can recycle and remix to stay within the session’s tempo. For longer campaigns, you’ll likely create a small icon-mapping glossary tailored to your party’s themes. The more you customize, the more the dice begin to feel “your” dice—the ones your group earned by surviving a brutal chase through Neon City.

### Durability and care
Like most premium resin dice, Fortune Row appreciates a little care. Avoid exposing the set to extreme heat (it can warp the edges) and keep it away from direct sunlight for extended periods to prevent fading of the symbols. When not in use, storing them in a padded tray helps prevent chips on the edges. If your table tends toward clumsy players, a soft felt mat underneath can prevent accidental roll-offs and other tabletop calamities that lead to dramatic, but not narrative, table damage.
