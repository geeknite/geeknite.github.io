## Seal status and value retention

Sealed boxes carry a particular kind of magic mixed with risk. The physical seal guards against dust and accidental wear but can also add a premium on the resale market. The value proposition of a sealed Reboot Box hinges on several factors:

- Availability and demand among collectors who want exclusive CMON content without hunting for backer updates.
- The perceived rarity of the content, which can heighten interest for completists who want every piece of the Zombicide universe in a near mint state.
- The condition of the outer packaging and the integrity of the shrink wrap. A damaged seal can generally deflate value more than a small dent on the box itself because collectors see the box as a time capsule rather than a game piece.

For many players, the sealed status is not purely about investment. It also offers the fun of discovery without spoilers, a sense of anticipation, and the joy of a future resell or gift. If you plan to play the game heavily, keeping the box sealed could be a strategic choice if you are contemplating long term collection goals. If you are primarily after gameplay, you may be happier cracking it open, assembling the minis, and diving into the new content sooner rather than later.
