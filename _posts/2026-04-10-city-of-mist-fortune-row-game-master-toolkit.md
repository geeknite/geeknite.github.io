## What Fortune Row is
Fortune Row is a compact but mighty GM toolkit designed for the City of Mist universe. It provides a set of practical tools to help you run investigations on the street level while keeping the mythic energy alive. The kit acknowledges that a city full of logos and myths can be a handful for a GM to manage and offers a structured approach that still leaves room for improvisation.

Inside the kit you will find a foldable GM screen a deck of clue cards a set of scene seeds a collection of rival NPCs and a small assortment of pacing tools. The goal is to give you a quick reference for common NPC reactions clue types and location based twists so you can keep the narrative moving even when the dice roll poorly.

## How the kit feels to use
The GM screen is the heart of the kit. The left side sports a compact narrative agenda that helps you quickly frame the scene and identify the rising action. The right side contains quick references to logos powers and relevant mythos. The screen reads like a friendly map that guides you through a tense moment rather than a dry reference book.

The clue cards are a clever tool. Each card carries a compact prompt that links a location a motive and a potential obstacle. You can draw a card in the middle of a scene and suddenly the room you thought was safe becomes a pressure cooker. The cards support both investigative style play and more theatrical scenes depending on how you like to run the city.

## Scene seeds and micro arcs
Fortune Row offers a library of scene seeds that are deliberately modular. You can drop a seed into a session and watch it radiate into a multi step arc. The seeds include a location hook a character motivation a potential misdirection and a final reveal that ties back to a larger mystery in Fortune Row. The design encourages non linear play so players can chase different threads and still converge toward a satisfying payoff.

## NPCs and keepers notes
The kit introduces a small cast of rival NPCs who are thematically linked to the Fortune Row area. Each NPC comes with a short stat block a list of influence moves and a few signature phrases that help you improvise during a session. These rivals are not stand ins for major villains but they act as street level forces that push the investigation forward. Keepers notes provide quick prompts for each NPC including potential alliances and betrayals that can shift when new clues come to light.

## Pacing tools and how to pace a session
A pacing clock is included in the toolkit to help track suspense and reveal timing. You set the clock to four ticks for a compact session or extend it for a longer arc. Each tick signals a shift in the information landscape for the players. The pacing tools work hand in hand with the clue cards and scene seeds to maintain momentum while avoiding the common trap of endless exposition.

## Adaptations and preferred play styles
Fortune Row is designed to be adaptable. If your table enjoys a gritty noir vibe you can lean into the noir grammar and use the mythos language to deepen the mood. If you prefer a more cinematic style you can accelerate the pace and emphasize the action beats. The toolkit does not force a single approach but instead gives you a menu of options to tailor to your table.
