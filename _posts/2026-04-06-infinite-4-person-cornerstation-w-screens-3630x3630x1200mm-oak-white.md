## Ergonomics and Comfort

Ergonomics in this setup is less about a single adjustable feature and more about a symphony of coordinated components. The desk depth, height adjustability, and the privacy screens work together to create an environment that encourages good posture without turning your workstation into a science project. If you opt for a sit-stand module to complement the main surface, you won’t be disappointed—the four-person layout accommodates a modular approach to height versatility that many teams crave but rarely achieve with a single-desk solution.

Ground rules for comfort:
- Ensure each station has at least 70–75 cm of knee clearance. The corner geometry helps keep you from feeling boxed in when you lean in to grab a document or slide a sketched mockup across the surface.
- Pair with ergonomic chairs that offer lumbar support and adjustable seat depth. The best combination here is a mid-range chair that sustains comfort during long sessions without forcing you to break the bank.
- For keyboard and mouse setups, maintain a 10–12 cm space in front of the keyboard to allow for forearm rest and avoid wrist extension fatigue. The surface area makes this feasible without crowding the space.
- Monitor height should align with your eye line, with the top of the monitor roughly at or slightly below eye level for optimal neck posture. This is easier with dual-monitor setups, as you can place the primary display on the main surface and the secondary display at a comfortable angle.
