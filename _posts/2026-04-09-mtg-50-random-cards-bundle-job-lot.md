## Care, storage, and keeping your chaos organized
MTG cards love a good home as much as a good joke loves a punchline. Here are a few practical tips to make your random cards stay in fighting shape and to keep your brewing space sane:

- Sleeves: If you’re serious about playing, sleeve the cards you intend to keep and route duplicates into a separate box. It keeps edge wear at bay and your future self from crying when you accidentally shuffle a miscut into a tournament deck.
- Deck boxes: Use labeled storage for different themes or colors. It helps you brainstorm a builder’s way through the chaos and makes your future self more efficient at constructing a new list.
- Binders: If you’re a collector, a binder with the appropriate card pages can showcase a subset of your random pool as an exhibit of chaotic art.
- Sorting: For the best mental health, sort by color and rarity first, then by set date. It’s not a perfect system, but it’s a reasonable way to find the cards you actually want to play with during a frantic draft night.
