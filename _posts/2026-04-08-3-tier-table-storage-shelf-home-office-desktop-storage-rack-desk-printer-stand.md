## Assembly and maintenance tips
Assembly is straightforward: attach the legs to the base, attach middle and top panels, tighten the screws with reasonable torque, and then place your favorite items on top. It helps to have a friend who can hold a panel while you align holes and avoid the existential crisis of misaligned holes. If you are alone, the patience of a monk and the grip strength of a gym rat will be enough. Maintenance is minimal: wipe with a damp cloth, avoid harsh chemicals on the veneer, and keep snacks away from the top shelf or you will learn a new meaning of the word crumbed.

### Cable management options
One of the more delightful features of this shelf is the ability to mock up a simple cable management routine. The back edge has a shallow lip you can route power cables through, so your desk remains presentable even when you have a forest of chargers behind the machine. A small cable sleeve makes it look like you care about organization, even when you are pretending to be a secret agent on a fail-safe mission to hide the cable spaghetti.

## Comparable options and buying advice
If you are not convinced yet, you should compare this unit with simpler two-tier models or with rolling cart options. The 3-tier design offers more vertical storage but can take up more horizontal space than a compact rolling cart. For bigger spaces, the three levels allow you to designate a dedicated printer station, a filing area, and a light-weight decorative zone. If you require more mobility, a rolling cart may be a better option; if you want more stability and less moving parts, the 3-tier shelf is the clear winner. You may also want to consider alternatives with adjustable shelves if you plan to reconfigure height depending on equipment, but for most home offices this unit strikes a pleasing balance between flexibility and simplicity.
