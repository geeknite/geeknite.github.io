## Display, Storage, and Preservation: Keeping the Aesthetic Alive
Display can be as simple as a glass-tane box or a shallow acrylic case. The goal is to protect from humidity, sunlight, and the occasional curious pet that believes every deck is edible. Humidity and heat aren’t your friend; they can cause warping and color fade. If you’re serious about long-term preservation, store cards upright—or flat, if you’re protecting against warping—with a silica gel packet nearby. Some collectors invest in archival sleeves for the individual cards, a method that keeps the edges pristine and reduces the likelihood of paste or adhesive issues over time.

Cleaning is another consideration. Wipe surfaces gently with a microfiber cloth; avoid harsh solvents. The goal is to maintain the finish’s glide and keep the faces legible. If you spot a crease on a card, you can carefully flatten it under a stack of books for a few hours, then re-sleeve. Patience is a virtue in card preservation, and the 1885 Cyclist deck will reward you with years of service if you’re mindful of the environment and your handling.
