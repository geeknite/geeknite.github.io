## What is Exploding Kittens?
Exploding Kittens is a fast-paced, light strategy card game for 2 to 5 players in its base form. The objective is simple and deliciously suspenseful: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten. You don’t want to be the last one standing with a bomb in your hand; that would be awkward at family dinners. The deck blends Exploding Kitten cards, Defuse cards, and a crowd of action and cat cards that create chains of misfortune, clever plays, and moments where a single card can change the entire table dynamic. The humor is central to its appeal—bright, goofy illustrations, cat puns, and a wink at pop culture that says, We know this is silly, and we own it.

### Components
The base game is compact, portable, and built for quick, chaotic rounds:

- A succinct rulebook that fits inside a pocket for those on-the-go game nights

- Art that breathes personality into every card, making you care about your next draw even when you know it’s probably a joke

The quality of the card stock and the bright ink give the game a premium feel for a party game, while remaining accessible to younger players with parental supervision. The art by The Oatmeal style is playful without being fussy, which is exactly the vibe you want in a social game where everyone is trying to blink away the next big misplay.

### Setup
Getting started is a five-minute operation:
- Separate the Exploding Kitten cards and Defuse cards from the rest of the deck.
- Shuffle the remaining deck and deal 7 cards to each player (adjust the number if you are playing with very large groups).
- Place the Exploding Kitten deck face down in the middle, ensuring there are enough kittens for the number of players minus one.
- Return the Defuse cards to the pool or give them to players according to your chosen variant.
- Shuffle the deck one last time and flip the top card to start the draw pile.

Pro tip: if you are gaming with kids or first-timers, consider giving each player a Defuse card in advance so no one feels like they need to survive a mysterious explosion from the get-go.

### How to Play (Turn Overview)
On your turn you will typically perform a sequence like this:
1) Play one or more cards from your hand to trigger effects such as attacking the next player, peeking at future cards, or shuffling the deck. You can chain certain actions together, but you have to respect the order and the action limits.
2) Draw a card from the draw pile. If it is an Exploding Kitten and you do not have a Defuse, you are out of the round. If you have a Defuse, you can use it, then reinsert the Exploding Kitten anywhere in the draw pile—an opportunity to sow chaos for others or to plant your own path to victory.
3) End your turn and pass to the next player. The rounds move fast, and before you know it the table is roaring with laughter and disbelief at the cards that have landed in the wrong hands.

A few cardinal rules to keep the table lively:
- Attack cards accelerate the pace by forcing the next player to take extra turns.
- See the Future allows you to look at the top three cards to plan your fate or misdirection.
- Nope can cancel another player’s action, but its use is an art form: timing matters almost as much as the move itself.
- Cat cards form combos that let you steal or force exchanges—these are your strategic levers when the deck grows unpredictable.

### Card Types in Brief
- Exploding Kitten: If drawn and not defused, you are eliminated from the round.
- Defuse: A lifeline; lets you survive the boom and reinsert the kitten anywhere in the draw pile.
- Attack: Pushes a new round of turns onto another player, increasing chaos.
- Skip: Lets you skip drawing and pass turn.
- See the Future: Lets you peek at the top three cards so you can plan your fate.
- Shuffle: Resets the order of the deck.
- Nope: A sneaky counter card that can stop another player's action. Use it with timing and a grin.
- Cat Cards: Pairs and triples trigger special effects that encourage stealing and strategic exchanges.

### Strategy and Tips
- Defuse sparingly: you want to keep your Defuse for the worst moments, not the mild misdraws. This card is your lifeline and your sword in the same breath.
- See the Future as a planning tool. It lets you anticipate danger and position your defuses more effectively.
- Save some Nope energy for the big plays. The biggest misplays come from using Nope in the wrong moment.
- Cat cards teach patience. You need to collect pairs and understand the value of stealing opponents' cards while still protecting your own hand.
- The number of players matters: in smaller groups you will see more direct confrontations; in larger groups you will experience more misdirection and longer rounds.

### Variants and House Rules
- Short rounds: reduce the number of cards dealt or remove some high risk cards.
- Team play: two teams share a pool of Defuse cards.
- Narrative objectives: players draw secret prompts and try to complete small missions while avoiding elimination.

### Expansions and Why You Might Consider Them
There are expansions and party packs that add new cards, new kittens, and even new mechanics. They are great if you want to push the chaos factor higher. Just be mindful that expansions can significantly increase round length and table chatter, which may be perfect for a long game night or a chaotic weekend session, but less ideal for a quick intro night.
