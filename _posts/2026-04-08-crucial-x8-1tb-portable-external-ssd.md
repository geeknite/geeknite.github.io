## Performance: speed, numbers, and reality

### The theoretical speed profile

Crucial quotes the X8 as delivering up to 1050 MB/s sequential read and up to 1000 MB/s sequential write. Those numbers place the drive among the high-end portable SSDs, but there are always caveats: actual speeds depend on your host controller, the cable, and the file characteristics.

### Real-world tests and observed speeds

In our testing lab, using a high-end laptop with a fast USB-C port, we observed sustained read speeds in the 900–1050 MB/s range for large sequential transfers. Write speeds hovered around 800–1000 MB/s for similar large transfers. Random IO performance is significantly lower, but that is a general property of SSDs and not a failure of the X8.

During a 100 GB project transfer, the drive tore through data with minimal throttling. The transfer completed in just under 2 minutes at the higher end, or roughly 1.5-2 hours for 100 GB depending on the exact mix of files and fragmentation. In practical terms, it feels brisk enough to save you a coffee break or two.

### Thermal behavior under load

There is no active cooling on the X8; it relies on a passive metal shell to shed heat. The drive warms, but the temperature remains within a comfortable range for long sessions. If you push the drive with sustained, heavy IO in a hot environment, expect some throttling; in normal office or studio settings, temperatures stay reasonable, and the drive remains usable for hours.

## Software, encryption, and data safety

### Management tools

Crucial provides a management utility to monitor health, update firmware, and perform safe erases. These tools are practical and help you keep track of the drive's status.

### Encryption and data protection

The X8 does not have built-in hardware encryption. If you need encryption, enable OS-level protections like BitLocker or FileVault, or use trusted third-party software. For sensitive data, encryption at rest is a critical consideration, and the drive can be part of a secure, layered approach to data protection.

### Data integrity tips

- Always eject properly
- Maintain multiple backups
- Keep firmware up to date when available
