## What is City of Mist: The Gilded Age

The expansion slots into the core City of Mist system, which means you don't have to learn a brand-new engine to get your gilded, gas-lit hands dirty. The Gilded Age gives you:

- A meticulously researched, fictionalized version of late 19th-century New York, with neighborhoods that feel real, not generic. You will walk through the Bowery and Wall Street as if they are intersections of mythic energy rather than just urban planning.
- A suite of new Masks and Motifs tailored to the era. Think financiers whose motives are made tangible as magical currency, a reporter who can bend a headline into truth or its opposite, a dancer who uses music to control the crowd's mood, and a tinkerer who coexists with a familiar — like a tiny electric gremlin inside a brass clockwork toy.
- A selection of ready-to-run scenes and longer arc hooks. The adventures feel cinematic and replayable, but they respect the city's tempo: fast dialogue, quick reveals, and a risk of escalation that can spark a multi-session campaign.
- GM aids that help you set the mood, pace the action, and keep track of the city's shifting politics. The book includes scene prompts, NPC profiles with quick hooks, and a few how the era changes the myth sidebars that are gold during table time.

## Setting and Theme

The Gilded Age is a love letter to atmosphere. The book leans into Manhattan as a living organism rather than a static backdrop: the rivers hiss with steam, the streets are arteries of opportunity, and every set piece is a stage with a different audience. The tone blends noir grit with mythic flourish—the way a jazz standard can feel ancient when played in a dimly lit room by a musician with warts and wisdom.

In terms of themes, The Gilded Age asks players to consider who profits from myth and who pays the price for it. The era's real-world power players—industrialists, reformers, labor agitators, and criminals—become archetypes that can be repurposed as Masks or as influential NPCs. The book doesn't shy away from the era's harsh realities: class disparity, political corruption, and social friction all present opportunities for dramatic tension at the table.

Of course, there's romance too: clandestine salons, improvised orchestra performances, and whispered confidences in backroom offices. The romance here isn't just about love; it's about longing, belonging, and the way a myth can fill a political passport with perks while hollowing out the human.

For players and GMs who adore visual storytelling, the setting provides a perfect canvas to stage moments that feel both epic and intimate — a hero making a stand on a rooftop as a circus of illuminated marquees glows below, or a quiet conversation in a candlelit club that reveals that the greatest enemy may be the man you call ally.

## Mechanics and Play Experience

The Gilded Age keeps the core City of Mist engine, which means you won't be learning a wholly new dice system. If you've played the base game, you'll feel at home here. If you haven't, the book does a stellar job of introducing you to the essential terms: Masks, Tags, and Clues, all swinging together to create narrative tension.

### Masks, Motifs, and the Play Rhythm

Masks are the identity you inhabit during a session. The Gilded Age expands this concept with era-appropriate Masks that feel both fresh and familiar. You’ll discover Masks built around the world's spectacle: the financier who negotiates with mythic currency, the journalist who channels rumor into truth or truth into rumor, the inventor who harnesses steam, the dancer who bends the room to her mood. These Masks are not only powers; they’re social instruments, enabling scenes in which a single line of dialogue can alter a city block or a family fortune.

Motifs provide the thematic glue that binds a Mask's power to the moment. In The Gilded Age, motifs draw from things like currency, reputation, and technology. The interaction between a Mask's abilities and its motif can create moments of glorious chaos: a negotiation turned ritual, a chase that becomes a performance piece, a revelation that comes wrapped in a moral conundrum rather than a bullet.

The campaign arcs are structured to scale to your group's appetite, with levers to turn from one-shot to long-form epic without a reconfiguration ritual.

## Campaign Ideas and Thematic Arcs

Here are a few seeds to spark your imagination:

- The Brass Ledger: A near-anarchic network of financiers uses a mythic ledger to tilt politics. The party must decide whether to rewrite the ledger or burn it, all while a clockwork-night carnival roams the city, pulling the strings behind the scenes.
- The Gilded Ball: An annual gala becomes a proving ground for Masks. But this year, a subtle allergic reaction to myth threatens to reveal everyone's true face. The players must navigate the dance floor while a tide of rumor threatens to break the evening apart.
- The Shadow Postal Service: A rogue postal route carries messages that reveal secrets of the era. The agents discover that the post is not just moving letters but moving myths from person to person. Their mission is to piece together what the myths want from the city's political future.

## Districts and Locales

The Gilded Age maps a city that hums with life in several districts, each with its own myths and social tempo.

- The Club Quarter: velvet ropes, private clubs, and conversations that can alter fortunes in a single handshake. The Masks here lean toward influence, charm, and debt to patrons.
- The Docks and Waterfront: ships, warehouses, and a labyrinth of smugglers. The myth here feeds on fog, rope, and rumor, and the daylight rarely reaches every shadow.
- Wall Street and the Financial Row: where numbers are more than numbers and reputations are currency. Masks here often negotiate power by controlling perception as much as assets.
- The Five Points: a frontier of street life, art, music, and the human stories of people who live on the edge of polite society. Secrets here move through optimism and necessity, not just fear.
- The Theatre District: stage magic, literal stagecraft, and the politics of spectacle. The Masks thrive on performance as a form of sovereignty.
- The Laboratories and Garages: early robots and ether tech, steam-powered contraptions, and experimental devices that flirt with myth.
- The Sanctuaries: churches and holy spaces where belief and myth cross paths, sometimes peacefully, sometimes contentiously.

Each locale includes tailored scene prompts, local NPCs, and hooks that can be dropped into a session with minimal prep. The goal is not to overwhelm you with options but to give you a city that feels lived-in and dynamic.

## Final Thoughts and Quick Verdict

City of Mist: The Gilded Age is a strong addition to the City of Mist line. It captures the era in a way that feels both faithful and playful, giving you a playground where myth and history can collide with style and humor. It’s not a replacement for the core book; it’s a complement that shines when you want to run something that looks like a cinematic period piece while still letting the players improvise at the speed of a spark in a dark ballroom.

If you’re into mythic noir, social intrigue, and campaigns that want to explore power, money, and myth as intertwined forces, you’ll feel right at home. The book respects your desire to tell stories that feel big and personal at the same time. If your table loves to lean into showmanship—dramatic entrances, stylized fights, and the thrill of a reveal that changes everything—The Gilded Age is your go-to expansion.

Pros:
- Rich setting with strong thematic hooks
- Strong Masks and Motifs that fit the era
- Flexible adventure seeds and reliable GM tools
- Excellent production values and art direction

Cons:
- If you’re not into urban myth or political intrigue, the emphasis on factions might feel a little heavy
- Requires the core City of Mist book for full system context

Bottom line: The Gilded Age is a stylish, thoughtful, and entertaining addition that pushes the core gameplay into a period that is visually exciting and narratively satisfying. It’s a recommended buy for groups who relish period ambience and myth-driven storytelling, and a welcome invitation to step into a city that glitters with myth and breathes with secrets.
