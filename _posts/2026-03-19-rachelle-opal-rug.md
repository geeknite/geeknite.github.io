## Build and Materials: Weave, Backing, and the Science of Not Pretending to Be a Rug Monster

The Opal rug typically features a low-pile construction. You’re not strolling ankle-deep through a plush valley, but you’re not stepping on crunchy aquarium gravel either. This is a practical choice for high-traffic spaces, commercial settings, and the occasional chaotic toddler playdate where the main event is not tripping over a loose thread. The weave feels dense enough to withstand vacuuming with a standard vacuum without showering lint like a confetti cannon at a robot wedding.

### Backing and Durability
The backing is a cotton-blend that adds some bite to prevent shifting on hard floors. In a world where area rugs are known to kiss your coffee table goodbye with a dramatic slide, the Opal keeps itself in its lane—anchored enough to survive the occasional tantrum from a rug-rugged puppy or a very excited dance routine. The fabric treatment on the surface is designed to resist everyday wear and tear, including light stains and the occasional enthusiastically spilled water bottle from a kitchen-prone YouTube star in your life.
