## Materials, build quality, and durability
Most 4-seater rattan sets use a synthetic resin weave (PE rattan) around a lightweight, corrosion-resistant aluminum frame. Benefits include light weight, weather resistance, and low maintenance. A good set uses:
- Woven wicker with a tight, uniform pattern: less snagging, better abrasion resistance.
- An aluminum frame with a powder-coated finish for scratch and UV protection.
- Cushions with water-repellent fabric and removable covers.

## Comfort, ergonomics, and space planning
The seating depth and back height influence long-term comfort. Look for:
- Seat depth around 18-20 inches (46-50 cm) and back height around 14-16 inches (35-40 cm).
- Cushion density around 25-35 kg/m3 for supportive seating.
- Round table diameter typically 90-110 cm.

## Design and aesthetics
Rattan sets come in hues from natural light to espresso to cool gray. The weave pattern should feel even and comfortable against skin; the color should be fade-resistant and easy to coordinate with outdoor decor.

## Weather resistance and maintenance
- Cushion covers should be removable and machine washable where possible.
- Clean wicker with a mild soap solution and a soft brush; avoid harsh solvents.
- Use storage or covers to protect cushions during heavy rain or winter.

## Real-world usage: space, flow, and practical tips
A round table fosters sociable conversations; ensure 80-100 cm clearance around the table for easy movement. If your space is tight, you might prefer a smaller diameter table; if you do a lot of hosting, you might want more space around seating.

## Round vs rectangular: pros and cons
Round tables suit intimate gatherings and banish corner crowding, while rectangular tables accommodate long dining sessions with more seat adaptability. For a family of four, round often wins for conversation and comfort.

## Value, warranty, and where to buy
Prices vary, but a well-built set with a high-quality weave and cushions should be a good investment. Warranty length typically ranges from 1-3 years on the frame and 1-2 years on cushions.

## Quick buyer’s guide
- Always measure your space and ensure the clearance is adequate.
- Check the cushion fabric: UV and moisture resistance matter.
- Look for a table that can hold a typical meal plate and has a stable base.
- Test the chairs for comfort and stability.
