## Overview
Exploding Kittens is a fast-paced, humorous take on the traditional Russian roulette-style card game, with a lot more cats and a lot less existential dread, unless your friends take this game a bit too seriously. The original edition was designed by Elan Lee, Shane Small, and Matthew Inman (of The Oatmeal fame) and has a vibe that says: “We love cats, we love chaos, and we love short, ridiculous rounds.” The concept is simple: you draw cards, you try to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten, and you use various Skill and Defuse cards to keep yourself alive while pushing your luck and your friends’ fates. It’s chaotic, it’s silly, and it’s surprisingly strategic if you treat it like a high-stakes game of keep-away with imaginary kitty lives on the line.

## How to Play: The Quick Start Guide
If you’ve played any card-draw game in your life, you’ll pick up Exploding Kittens in about five minutes. The core flow is simple:

1) Shuffle the deck. 2) Each player draws a card. 3) If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re in trouble unless you can Defuse it. 4) If you Defuse, you keep playing; if not, you’re out unless someone uses a Defuse they saved earlier. 5) Rinse and repeat until one player remains.

What adds depth is the variety of Defuse cards and the strategic power of Attack or See the Future cards. Defuse cards let you defuse an Exploding Kitten with a little improvisation, while See the Future gives you a glimpse of what’s coming, granting a few seconds of luxury to plan your next move. The Attack cards force the next player to take two turns in a row, creating moments of panic that are equal parts hilarious and terrifying.

The game pace tends to range from breezy to chaotic depending on how many Defuses your group has, how early someone clutch-carries an Ace of Bananas (that would be a Defuse card with a shiny name), and how willing you are to bluff your way through a few rounds. In practice, you’ll often see a handful of players get knocked out early with explosive endings, while one clever strategist lurks in the wings, defusing, skipping, and peeking into futures until they’re on the verge of victory.

## Strategy Tips and House Rules
While Exploding Kittens thrives on luck and chaos, there are a few strategic moves worth keeping in mind:

- Save Defuse cards for early overconfidence: If you blow your Defuse early, you’ll find yourself in a jam later. It’s usually better to keep some Defuses for the inevitable late-game Exploding Kitten draws.
- Read the table: See the Future cards can reveal what’s coming, but the timing matters. If you know there are two Exploding Kittens left and three Defuses in play, use that information to calibrate your risk tolerance.
- Bluff with occasional risk: If you’re holding a Skip card, you can create the illusion of certainty and push others into risky plays. The opposite strategy can work too: pretend you’re fragile to coax an opponent into a misplay.
- Don’t forget the timing of Attack cards: Forcing the next player to take two turns can be a lifesaver if you’re low on Defuses. If you suspect a player ahead of you is about to win, a well-timed Attack can save you from elimination by stealing momentum.
- Don’t underestimate See the Future: This one costs a few cards, but it can be a clutch investment when the board is chaotic. Knowing what’s coming lets you plan a counterplay or a protective move for your next draw.

House rules: If you want even more chaos, try a rapid-fire variant where players draw additional cards to end a round after an Exploding Kitten is defused. Or add a “cat-lovers unite” rule where players who draw a non-exploding kitten gain a bonus to the next round’s defusal success. Just be sure to set expectations about what counts as a “bonus.”
