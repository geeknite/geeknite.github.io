## Maintenance Made Simple (Because None of Us Have Time for Complexity)
### Vacuuming and care routines
- Vacuum weekly on a low setting to avoid sucking out the fluff and the soul of the rug. A beater brush on low helps keep the pile fluffy but not frayed.
- Grooming with a carpet rake or a hand‑held brush can help maintain the loft if the rug starts to look a little smooshed after long marathons of foot traffic.
- Spot-clean spills immediately with a blot-and-dab method and a mild detergent. Avoid rubbing—the fibers will matt, and you’ll end up with a tiny fiber monster in your living room.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear; this is especially helpful in rooms with strong sunlight that can fade the fibers in a chosen corner.

### Cleaning caveats you’ll want to memorize (and pretend you remember later)
- Avoid harsh chemical cleaners or bleach (obvious, but necessary to say out loud). The goal is to preserve color depth and fiber integrity.
- If you’ve got pets, consider a routine that minimizes pet hair tangling. The plush fibers are more comfortable when not full of hair confetti. A quick sticky roller on the surface can help before vacuuming if hair becomes a daily theme.
- For larger stains, consider a professional cleaning every couple of years. You want to preserve the pile’s bounce, not transform it into a museum exhibit of ills and spills.
