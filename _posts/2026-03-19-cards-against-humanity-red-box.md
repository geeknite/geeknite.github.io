## How to Play the Red Box

### Setup

Set the box in the middle. Each player draws a hand of white cards the exact number depends on your agreed house rules typically around 10. One player is designated as the Card Czar for the round and the rest of the players prepare to submit their best line. The Card Czar shuffles a fresh batch of black cards in a separate pile to pull from and to avoid giving away the prompt before it is read. The first round begins once the Czar reads the black card aloud. The pace is quick and the game rewards improvisation more than long buffering thinking.

### Rounds and Card Czar

On each round every non-Czar player submits a white card anonymously. The Czar then reads all the submitted lines combined with the prompt and selects the winner. The chosen player resets into the Czar role for the next round. The simple structure is the reason the game remains accessible to new players even when the group just started drinking coffee or tea and decided to test the waters of social gaming. The rules emphasize flow over complexity and this is exactly where Red Box shines.
