## Quick take

This is a large card driven expansion for City of Mist that adds a Fortune Row mythos character set. It blends noir urban myth with caper style storytelling and it brings six new mythos playbooks, several location cards, and a batch of story seeds that are spicy enough to set a table on fire in a good way. If you are into heists in a city where myths walk among us you will feel right at home. If you are a GM who likes to pivot from chase sequences to slow burn mystery this set gives you many options.

## What this set is and is not

The Fortune Row Mythos Character Set is a compact bundle designed to slot into the core game with ease. It is not a full game, nor is it a mere add on that makes your life harder. It sits in the middle as a toolbox for the GM and a playground for players who want to sprint down the glamorous lane with a midnight cape. On the table it reads as a premium set with sturdy cards, crisp art, and a vibe that screams late night city bus in a rainstorm.

### The roster

The six mythos characters in this set are built around a central motif of luck and consequence. Each character feels like a small epic on its own but also fits neatly into a crew that chases big scores while dodging bigger fates. Here is a quick tour of the six mythos profiles you will find inside the box

#### The Gambler

The Gambler is a mythos figure who lives by the turn of a card and the fall of a dice. On the table this build plays with a high risk, high reward style that folds nicely into a crew based caper. Mechanically the Gambler leans into masks that affect risk taking and the dice pool dynamic that City of Mist fans love. On the fiction side this is a character who can forecast danger by reading the room, a true card shark and a dapper trouble magnet. Best fit is with masks of fate and cunning that let the player stack the odds in your favor when the moment demands a bold move.

#### The Oracle

The Oracle is a fate obsessed watcher who sees patterns where others see noise. In play the Oracle provides guidance and clues, with a pool of divination style prompts that push the team toward themes of destiny and consequence. This profile shines in mystery focused sessions and can be a powerful driver for a long campaign arc. The Oracle uses a set of mythos cards that reward reading the city and the players for spotting patterns that others miss.

#### The Clockmaker

The Clockmaker is a master of timing and infrastructure. Their power centers around manipulating urban systems and the rhythm of the city. On the table this translates into a mechanical minded approach to planning, sabotaging delayed returns, and orchestrating complex set pieces. The Clockmaker is a great pick for players who enjoy puzzle like play and for GMs who want to weave time based traps into scenes.

#### The Siren

The Siren uses allure and truth bending to pull others toward a chosen outcome. The siren is a social character with the ability to sway crowds with voice and presence. In game terms this class excels in social mechanics and in scenes that end with a dramatic confession or a gleaming reveal. The Siren can break tension in the right moment and turn a potentially failed conversation into a pivotal turning point.

#### The Shadow Broker

The Shadow Broker is the money and information engine of the crew. They gather secrets, monetize them, and use that currency to nudge outcomes without signing their own name in permanent ink. On the table this is a seductively tactical role that thrives on misdirection and leverage. The Shadow Broker is not a frontline bruiser but a strategist who can tilt the scales when the crew faces a wall of misfortune.

#### The Velvet Hand

The Velvet Hand is the finesse artist of the crew, a character whose grace hides a deadly precision. Their role is to smooth over rough moments and to broker silent deals that keep the plan together when the heat rises. The Velvet Hand can salvage a failure with style and can pull a last second victory from a rapidly failing situation. On the table this is a character for players who want a strong presence in scenes with social conflict and delicate negotiation.


> Note on flavor and switchability
> The roster is designed so you can mix and match these six mythos with other city factions and with standard masks. You can pull any combination of two or three and still feel that you have a coherent crew. If you love a fast glassy noir vibe you can shuffle the cards to create a heist caper. If you love slow burn mystery you can lean into the Oracle and Clockmaker to keep the tension high.

## Mechanic overview and how it fits the core game

City of Mist is built around the idea that a city is powered by masks and myths. The Fortune Row set leans into this design by giving you six new masks that slot into the existing Masks and Power system. Each mythos character comes with a small set of story seeds and a handful of location and event cards. The cards are designed to be replayable and to create different outcomes each time you run a session. One of the pleasures of this set is that it does not try to teach you a new system. It rather adds flavor and structural hooks that you can drop into a not yet known city of fog and neon.

In practice this means that during a session you can anchor scenes around the fate of a single card from the fortune deck or you can run a multi scene caper that uses several mythos cards as triggers. The dice mechanics in City of Mist remain the same but this set gives you more ways to trigger outcomes. The six characters are designed to produce moments that feel cinematic yet grounded in the urban myth vibe that fans crave. If you have run a number of City of Mist campaigns you will notice a few new shorthand moves that feel both familiar and fresh.

### Build ideas and tips for players

The following build ideas are offered to spark your imagination and not to be taken as a one size fits all spec. You can adapt any build to your table by swapping masks and by adjusting the story seeds you want to emphasize.

#### Build 1: the daring heist crew chief

This build leans into the Gambler and Shadow Broker with a dash of Velvet Hand. Your plan is to run a small but flashy caper that ends with a twist that makes the city talk. Focus on masks that enhance risk assessment, insight into other players motives, and the ability to turn a setback into a comeback. You want friends and enemies to remember your crew long after the last bottle has cracked open.

#### Build 2: the mystery at the edge of time

The Oracle and Clockmaker combine to drive a mystery story that feels like a clockwork riddle. You want to plant seeds for a longer arc and to deliver a few poetic reveals that haunt the player characters. This is a campaign style that rewards patience and careful listening. You should plan for a season of sessions where the clues grow into a pattern that leads to a grand reveal.

#### Build 3: the social storm leader

Combine the Siren with Velvet Hand to create a charismatic leader who can hold a crowd in the palm of their hand. The emphasis is on social mechanics, negotiation, and influence that goes beyond street cred. In the right hands this can be a runway of dramatic scenes where the city itself seems to lean in to hear a single truth.

## Play experience and pacing

When you put Fortune Row on the table you will notice a certain glow in the room. The art style is moody, with neon shards, raindrop textures, and a vibe that says we are in a city that runs on secrets. The cards are sturdy, the text is legible, and the design makes it easy to pick up a scenario and go. Pacing benefits come from the new location cards which allow you to shift settings quickly. One moment you are in a rooftop market and the next you are in a back alley auction for secrets. The tempo oscillates in a way that fits both tabletop noir and a fast moving heist plot.

### Artwork and production values

The artwork is bold but restrained, with character portraits that look like they could walk out of a late night movie. The color palette leans into blues, magentas, and golds that evoke both danger and possibility. If you are a fan of good design this is a kit you will enjoy sorting through. The production quality is solid and the card stock feels premium in hand, which matters in a game that relies on tactile feedback as much as on abstract mechanics.

## Scenarios and quick starts

To help you drop into game night fast here are two quick start scenarios built around the Fortune Row set. Each scenario is designed to be run in about two hours with room for improvisation if the city decides to misbehave.

Scenario 1: a night market caper

Premise is a night market that sells secrets rather than heat. The crew is asked to recover a passed secret that can ruin a political ambition. The location cards push players into a maze of stalls, neon signs, and whispered bets. The session is driven by a mix of the Gambler and Shadow Broker to shape the plan, while the Siren manipulates social scenes that help or hinder the team. Expect a twist that the secret is a curse and that the party must decide how to break it without breaking the city further.

Scenario 2: the clockwork harvest

This one happens in a month where time itself is stretched. The Clockmaker builds a plan that requires precise timing which the city does not tolerate. The Oracle casts light on inevitable patterns while The Velvet Hand negotiates safe passage through a hostile corporate zone. Your villain is a rival crew that wants to seize the same secret in a different way. The tension comes from a clock like countdown in the turn sequence and a final reveal that redefines what counts as a win.

## Design notes and balance considerations

Fortune Row is not a set that tries to overpower the core game. It is a complementary tool that aims to expand storytelling options without upsetting the fundamental balance. The characters are designed to be flexible and easy to slot into existing campaigns. This means you can use a single new mythos character as a guest for a single arc or bring the entire roster onto a long campaign with a few tweaks to the story seeds. The set acknowledges that players like to improvise around set pieces and story prompts and it gives you a few more levers to pull when the session leans into drama rather than action.

If you are a GM who values player agency you will likely appreciate that each mythos character comes with a helpful list of prompts that invite players to contribute ideas. The prompts avoid the trap of over specifying the narrative and instead offer a scaffold that the table can fill with humor, danger, and whimsy. This is the kind of design that makes for memorable nights rather than hours of staring at the table while everyone pretends to be focused.

## Why this set might be a must have for your collection

For the veteran City of Mist table Fortune Row adds depth and texture without shackling you to a single meta plot. It gives you a ready to play crew that you can drop into a session at short notice and still feel fresh. The six mythos profiles offer enough variety to spark conversations that lead to odd alliances, surprising betrayals and city shifting revelations. If you like the idea of a crew that floats between high risk caper moments and intimate character driven scenes this is the kind of set that keeps your nights from becoming a routine grind. On a practical level the cards are clear, the deck building options are intuitive, and the recommended scenes are robust enough to stand on their own if you need a quick session on a week night.

## Final verdict and rating

The Fortune Row Mythos Character Set hits a sweet spot for players who want a strong vibe with a dash of glamour and danger. It is not for every table if you prefer bare bones chaos and minimal thematic prompts. For the right crew this set becomes a conversation piece that keeps players engaged between sessions and gives the GM reliable hooks to pull into moments that feel epic and intimate at the same time. The strengths lie in the cohesive vibe, the sturdy production, and the way it integrates with standard city mechanics. The potential weaknesses are the risk of over the top glamour that might clash with a darker campaign and the need for the table to enjoy a deck of prompts rather than raw data. If your group thrives on witty banter and cinematic flourishes you will likely get 9 out of 10 on your own internal scale. If you want a cleaner minimalistic approach you may find the lush texture a bit much for a light night of gaming.

## Our final recommendation

If you have a running City of Mist table that loves a strong vibe and a set of ready made roles you should consider the Fortune Row Mythos Character Set as a high value add. It brings six distinct masks, a collection of location and event cards, and enough prompts to run multiple sessions without repeating the same beats. The design is polished, the art is striking, and the tone is on point for the noir magic megacity. The only thing you might want to prepare is a calendar to fit in the many session ideas that can emerge from this set. If you are new to City of Mist this set can still be a great onboarding tool because it shows the core idea of the game through a strong ensemble that embodies the blend of myth and modern life that makes the system shine.
