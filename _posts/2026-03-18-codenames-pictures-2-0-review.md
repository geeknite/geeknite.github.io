## What is Codenames Pictures 2.0
Codenames Pictures 2.0 is a revision of the original concept, focusing on image based clues instead of words. ## How to play in 10 seconds or less
Two teams, one spymaster per team, a grid of image cards on the table, and one word or short phrase clue from the spymaster that links multiple cards. Teammates then discuss and select cards that fit the clue. If they select correct images, their progress marker advances. If they select a wrong image, the turn ends. If they step on an opposing agent card, the turn ends and control passes to the other team. First team to reach the end wins. It is a simple premise, but the storytelling potential is huge.

## Turn structure and rounds
A typical round starts with the spymaster giving a one word clue and a number indicating how many cards the clue applies to. The team then discusses and selects cards that they believe fit the clue. The team reveals whether each chosen card is a correct image, a neutral, or an enemy card. Correct guesses move the team toward victory; neutral cards are safe but stop the turn if a limit is reached; enemy cards end the turn and pass control to the other team. The 2.0 edition has tunes to keep the tempo brisk and reduces the guesswork, making rounds feel more focused and less chaotic than earlier sessions.

## Strategy and tips
A few practical tips to get the most from your sessions:
- Build image families: Track recurring motifs such as animals in motion, vehicles in motion, or everyday objects in unusual environments. A well mapped theme makes it easier to see connections across multiple cards.
- Clue breadth versus depth: Short clues can be effective if you can link several images, but longer clues that loosely tie a larger cluster can pay off in big rounds.
- Read the table: The room is part of the clues; observe players reactions, pauses, and guesses. A tense room can reveal a lot about which images they interpret as related.
- Team balance matters: The spymaster is the conductor, but the team must actively discuss, and ideally a few players should feel comfortable taking on the role of the interpreter. The more voices, the more creative the connections become.
- Practice patience: The abstract nature means there will be misreads. Treat these as talking points rather than failures; the art is designed to spark discussion as much as to signal answers.
