### What is BT01?
BT01 is the game’s first big sprinkling of new mechanics and new tools. Expect to see a mix of familiar faces from the core set and a few surprising newcomers who show up just often enough to remind you that the set designers are watching you. The full BT01 set of ten packs includes:
- A mix of Common and Uncommon cards that fill out your deck-building options with reliable options.
- Rare cards that push you toward a few specific archetypes—some are straightforward aggro, others are heavier control or combo-based strategies.
- A handful of Super Rare (SR) and possibly one or two Secret Rare (SPR) or other chase cards that make opening a pack feel like a lottery win without the risk of the actual lottery.

Note: you won’t necessarily pull one of every card when you buy a full ten-pack set; that is as likely as getting all four Dragon Balls in a single cosmic wish. However, the odds are favorable enough that you can complete some quick archetypes without selling your kidney to a local stadium vendor.

## Strategy primer: building with BT01
A lot of folks treat booster sets as “just more cards.” Geeknite takes the opposite view: BT01 is a sandbox that reveals the core design identity of the game. If your aim is to craft a credible, terminal set that you can actually pilot, here are a few practical pointers.

- Decide on an archetype early: BT01 has something for everyone, but the real power is unlocked when you decide whether you want to flood the board with early threats, slow the game down with control tools, or punch through with a finisher.
- Synergy matters: Look for cards that increase your consistency, not just big stat lines. A single card that helps you draw an extra card, search for a key piece, or recycle a combo piece can be more valuable than a flashy rare that sits in your deck like a decorative coin.
- Energy management: Dragon Ball games often reward efficient energy use. BT01’s cards lean into this philosophy with options that accelerate or optimize your energy curve. If you’re a new player, keep a simple energy plan in mind: don’t spend energy you don’t absolutely need to, and always ensure you have a plan to spend what you have.
- Defensive options aren’t optional: Many BT01 cards provide blocks or removals in the hero-plot sense, allowing you to survive the early aggression and set up your own winning condition. Don’t forget to allocate a portion of your deck to defense; nothing ruins a good plan like getting stuffed by a well-timed attack.

### Archetype ideas
- Idea 1: Blue Control with Card Advantage: Use draw-and-filtering tools to sculpt your hand and then drop a big finisher while your opponent watches the rainbow glitter fade into the night.
- Idea 2: Red Aggro with Early Pressure: Leverage tempo cards to push pressure in the first few turns. Your objective is to force a suboptimal play from your opponent and capitalize with a strong secondary finisher.
- Idea 3: Hybrid archetype: Mix a little of multiple colors or factions to create a flexible engine that can adapt to a wide range of opponents. BT01 supports this approach by providing a robust toolkit, including engines for drawing, searching, and repeating key combos.

- Practical tip: keep a written “deck skeleton” as you draft from the ten packs. Start by identifying your core engine, then add disruption and removal to handle threats, and finally add a few cards that simply win if you can maintain a stable resource curve.

## Value and practical buying considerations
If you’re budget-conscious, BT01 is a relatively accessible entry point to the Dragon Ball Super Card Game, especially when sold as a full ten-pack set. The ten-pack bundle delivers more consistent value than a handful of single boosters, because you’re guaranteed a larger pool of cards to fuel your deck-building experiments. And on the collector side, you’ve got plausible odds of pulling the rarer cards that can fetch a respectable price on the market or serve as the centerpiece of a personal display.

- Budget-trim approach: If price is a concern, consider starting with one BT01 ten-pack set and supplementing with a couple of well-chosen singles or a small side order of boosters from other sets to fill in gaps. This approach gives you a strong foundation without an overwhelming outlay.
- Collectors’ route: If you’re chasing SPRs or specific SRs, you may want to pair BT01 with additional booster boxes or trade with community members. The card economy is dynamic, and BT01 cards can be both a gateway into new archetypes and a gateway into trading for your dream foil.
