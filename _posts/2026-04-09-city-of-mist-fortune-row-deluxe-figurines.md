## Introduction
City of Mist is all about identity, glamour, and the uneasy alliance between myth and alleyway. It’s a game where players become ordinary people who discover they’re more than their ordinary selves, a noir-fantasy melting pot where dreams get messy and cards butting heads with fate. The Fortune Row Deluxe Character Figurines are a recent expansion that promises to bring the underworld’s glam squad to your tabletop in resin form. The big questions: do these miniatures look as sharp as the cover art promises? Do they actually enhance (or at least not ruin) your storytelling? And, most importantly for this review, do they justify the price tag with enough “wow” factor to make your die-hard players shout, “Take my money, but only if you’ve got enough space for display dioramas.”

## Overview
### The Fortune Row premise
Fortune Row brings together a quintet (or more, depending on the edition) of archetypes that slip into the shadows of City of Mist’s sprawling mythos: the glamorous mastermind, the streetwise rogue with a heart of gold, the enigmatic mystic who knows more than they admit, the mercurial socialite who manipulates destinies from behind a smoky curtain, and the draped figure who wears fate on a sleeve. The deluxe figurines aim to capture the essence of these archetypes with stylish sculpts, dynamic poses, and bases that tempt you to build small dioramas—because nothing says “we’re serious about mood” like a diorama that makes a crime scene look like a studio photography set.

### Box contents (what you actually get)
- 4–6 high-detail resin miniatures, each with a unique pose intended to reflect the character’s defining trait.
- A handful of themed bases, some with cobblestones, some with velvet banquettes, all designed to snap into place with satisfying magnetic or peg-based attachment. 
- Optional character cards or stat slips (depending on the batch) to keep your glam squad in sync with your Glamour, Mythos, and tags. 
- A small diorama-friendly sheet of backdrop elements—think about a Boston-black velvet curtain and a streetlamp silhouette that you can position behind the figures for that extra “night-sky noir” feel.

If you’re expecting a glossy pre-painted set that looks like it came straight from a boutique, you’ll need to temper expectations. These are well-crafted, but they’re not prepainted. The deluxe label here means more about the packaging, the sculpts’ fidelity, and the bases’ potential to tell a story rather than a guarantee of ready-to-use miniatures out of the box. You’ll want to bring your brushes, and maybe a sense of mischief about whether your modeling skills will ever live up to the packaging’s promise.

### A quick note on scale and compatibility
City of Mist figures, in most official and fan-made ecosystems, sit in the 28mm-ish realm—roughly the scale of many standard RPG line miniatures. The Fortune Row set nods to that, offering silhouettes and silhouettes-adjacent silhouettes that sit well beside other resin or metal minis. If your table runs a hybrid: your City of Mist cards, a handful of D&D or other 28mm miniatures, and a couple of fingered dice, these should fit in without turning your game into a scale-warped cosplay contest. If you’re into heavy-duty diorama setups, you’ll appreciate the risk-reward of creating a small, curated corner on the table where the characters get to live their best velvet lives.

## Design and Craftsmanship
### Materials and finish
The resin used is on the stiffer side—less likely to warp under heat, more likely to hold fine lines in the feathers of a cape or the glint of a polished weapon. You’ll notice good edge definition and crisp detailing on faces, hair, and accessories. The cape folds, the jewelry glints, and the tattoos or runic motifs that are so iconic to Fortune Row pop with just enough subtlety to be intriguing but not so sharp they look like a lithography print.

### Poses and personality capture
One of the true tests for any figure set is whether the poses convey narrative immediately. Fortune Row nails it, at least in the broad strokes. You get dynamic stances—someone launching into a sudden street-charmed stride, another counting coins with fingers that suggest more than counting. The problem with many “deluxe” lines is that the poses look fantastic on the box but fall apart in play when you’re trying to place a figure precisely for a tableau. Here, the designers have balanced dramatic gesture with tabletop practicality. The figurines aren’t so anfractuous that you’ll constantly have to re-assemble after a single sneeze from the dog. They’re expressive, with a cinematic angle that invites you to craft a micro-scene around them during a session.

### Details and readability
The faces are expressive but not overly busy, which helps with readability during sessions. Even in poor lighting, the eyes do their job—the signature “I know something you don’t” glimmer that makes you want to lean in and ask, “What did you see on Fortune Row that I didn’t?” The clothing textures—think silk, velvet, and metal buckles—are crisp without feeling toy-like. If you’re planning to display these on a shelf, you’ll notice how the glint from the plastic/ resin plays with room lighting and creates a moody, cinematic aura. If you’re planning to game with them, you’ll be glad that the faces keep their angles at a distance, so you can identify who’s who at a glance without squinting through the glow of a dramatic neon sign.

### Paintability and assembly
If you’re a painter who loves to prime, basecoat, and wash with intent, you’ll relish the challenge. If you’re someone who expects pre-primed, color-mcoded masterpieces, you’ll need to slow your roll. The set is very paint-friendly: fine lines and tiny details invite layering, and the separate bases give you opportunities to craft a tiny street-vignette or stage a dramatic reveal for your table. The resin takes paint well, though you’ll want to keep an eye on flash and mold lines—these aren’t absent-minimal pieces; there will be a little bit of cleanup. Expect to spend additional minutes per figure to achieve a couture look rather than a “box-painted” vibe, and you’ll be rewarded with a much more striking overall presentation.

## Narrative integration: playing with Fortune Row
### How these minis slot into City of Mist campaigns
City of Mist thrives on mythos and identity struggling against the real world’s constraints. The Fortune Row figures—by their nature—offer easy hooks for your sessions:
- The glam mastermind can drive social combat in the city’s corridors of influence; a party member might be tasked with extracting a confession from a figure who knows how to stage a flawless heist.
- The streetwise rogue offers a counterpoint to the glamour, grounding the action in street-level stakes while being the character most likely to have a plan for escape routes or decoys.
- The mystic brings a dash of the arcane, weaving glamour with fate and adding a texture of prophecy to your scenes.
- The socialite’s influence can be the key to opening doors that would otherwise be locked, a reminder that in Fortune Row, social capital can be as dangerous as physical weaponry.
- The figure with fate on their sleeve can operate as a wild-card, challenging players to reckon with what destiny has in store for them and how their choices ripple through the city.

These figurines are not just prizes for the aisle of shelves; they’re a practical toolkit for weaving scenes where glamor and danger collide. A table that’s heavy on character-driven storytelling will find these minis a plus, especially if you’re trying to evoke the sense that Fortune Row isn’t just a location—it’s a living, breathing personality with its own code of glamour and menace.

### Using the minis as narrative prompts
Think of the figures as prompts rather than just tokens. Place a couple on the table during the prelude to a scene and watch players lean in: “Who is this figure?” “What’s their relationship to Fortune Row?” “What would they risk to secure this item?” The miniatures can help anchor your descriptions, enabling you to move from abstract mood to concrete action in a way that cards alone sometimes struggle to achieve. The set invites you to stage a moment: a secret rendezvous on a rain-streaked rooftop, a velvet-draped backroom of a speakeasy, a ballroom where a chorus of whispers tells a fortune you can’t quite trust.

### Interplay with cards and glamor mechanics
City of Mist uses Arcanum cards, tags, and glamor scores to resolve a lot of the drama. The Deluxe Figurines don’t replace those mechanics, but they can serve as tangible reminders of which character is currently soaking up glamor or influencing a surrounding scene. You can assign each figure a set of tags that reflect their role in the campaign and use their poses (as you describe the scene) to cue players about potential interactions. In short: these figures aren’t just pretty props; they’re metaphors you can physically rotate and reposition as the story tilts toward fortune or misfortune.

## Value, pricing, and value proposition
### Price versus perceived value
Deluxe sets live on a fine line between “practical tool for storytelling” and “expensive collectible.” If you approach Fortune Row as a painting for your table that you’ll curate and display, you’ll probably feel satisfied with the investment. Those who prefer to rely on cards and token stands alone may want to wait for a sale or look for a companion add-on with a lower price point.

### Longevity and collectability
The appeal of Fortune Row’s figures isn’t just their immediate use. The sculpts carry a degree of collectability that makes them a fun long-term upgrade for a dedicated City of Mist table. If you’re playing a long-term campaign that weaves Fortune Row more deeply into the plot, you’ll get more mileage out of the set as the story arc unfolds. As with any collectible line, price and availability will trickle—so if you’re interested, you’ll want to track announcements and pre-order windows to avoid missing your chance.

## How to use Fortune Row on your table
### Setup tips for a dramatic tableau
- Create a vignette: use a small, velvet-textured base or a darker felt underlay to deepen shadows around each figure.
- Move the camera, not the figures: for photos or screenshots, adjust lighting to accent creases, jewelry, and the glint of metal, not just the silhouette.
- Mix media thoughtfully: pair the resin minis with acrylic markers for signage or street elements to hint at Fortune Row’s environment without overcrowding the space.

### Campaign integration can be as easy as a single scene hook
Use Fortune Row to anchor a campaign arc around influence, power, and betrayal in the city’s elite circles. A few scenes of social maneuvering at a gala or a dangerous corner backroom can bloom into a larger mystery: who truly controls Fortune Row, and what secrets lie behind that velvet curtain? ## Comparisons and alternatives
### How does Fortune Row stack against other deluxe lines?
Compared to generic fantasy miniatures or non-themed premium sets, Fortune Row offers a strong narrative integration. Other deluxe lines might deliver similar quality in sculpting, but Fortune Row’s identity as a faction within City of Mist makes it easier to justify its existence as a storytelling tool. If you’re leaning toward a more generic aesthetic, you might want to compare this set to a broader noir or urban-cape miniatures lineup. The key difference is storytelling leverage: Fortune Row’s vibe is tailor-made to your City of Mist table, which can make it feel more justified than a broader but less cohesive collection.

### Where it shines and where it trips
- Shines: mood, storytelling potential, table presence, and a tangible anchor for Fortune Row scenes.
- Trips: price, assembly time, and the fact that it’s not a plug-and-play replacement for card-based mechanics. It’s a supplementary experience that requires your willingness to paint, display, and stage.

## Packaging, care, and display tips
### Storage and transportation
- Use dedicated miniatures cases with foam to guard against base detritus and keep the velvet photography-ready edges intact.
- Keep a small microfiber cloth to wipe away fingerprints and to maintain the figure’s sheen after handling.
- If you’re a frequent traveler with your campaign, consider a display-friendly travel case that doubles as a storytelling prop—this way you can stage a quick Fortune Row scene even on a hotel desk.

### Cleaning and maintenance
- Lightly dust with a soft brush before each game night.
- For deeper cleaning, use a mild soap solution and a soft brush; avoid soaking the resin for too long to prevent base detachment.
- If you plan to repaint, a primer coat (gray or white) will help your colors stick better and give you a smooth base for shading and highlighting.
