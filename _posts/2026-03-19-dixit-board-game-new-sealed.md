Dixit is back in a new sealed box, a dreamlike invitation to storytellers everywhere. ### Setup
Unpacking is the easy part. The real magic happens when you lay out the rules in a way that even non-gamers can understand. In Dixit, setup is quick: each player draws six cards from the deck, the storyteller for the round selects a clue from their mind, and the rest of the players select a card that best matches that clue. All chosen cards are shuffled and revealed face up. Players then vote on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. Scoring comes after the votes are tallied, and the storyteller rotates to the next player. That is it — a handful of steps, a big heap of storytelling, and a table full of reactions.

### The Turn
Every round follows the same rhythm, with the same core tension. The storyteller might tell a clue that is subtle and poetic, perhaps a phrase that evokes a mood rather than a literal description. The other players weigh their options, choosing a card that could plausibly fit the clue without giving away the entire game. When all cards are revealed, the voting begins. The moment the votes land, everything becomes a little chaotic and wonderfully human. People start to argue about the best clue, the most obvious card, or the one that perfectly misleads everyone. The joy of Dixit is in these micro-alliances and rivalries that form around the table as you try to outsmart your friends with language, memory, and a healthy sense of humor.

### Scoring
The scoring in Dixit is the quiet backbone that keeps rounds interesting. The storyteller earns points if some players guess their card but not all. If everyone guesses the storyteller card, the storyteller loses some points; if no one guesses, the storyteller also loses points. In practice, this yields a delightful balance: clever clues that are neither too easy nor too obscure, and players who learn to read the room and the kind of humor that resonates with their group.

The exact point values can vary slightly across printings, but the spirit stays the same. The base game rewards a blend of imagination and social reading. It is not enough to be clever; you must be perceptive about what your friends find evocative. This is a game built for conversation and laughter as much as for vinyl-level misdirection. If you are in a mood to argue with your friends about whether a clue was too on the nose or beautifully obscure, Dixit has a way of rewarding that energy without turning into a vicious competition.

## Artwork, Theme, and Accessibility
The star of Dixit remains the artwork. The new sealed edition retains the distinctive style — soft edges, dreamlike color palettes, and scenes that feel both fantastical and emotionally tangible. The images invite interpretation; players craft clues that echo themes found within the art, or perhaps memories that the imagery stimulates. This is where Dixit shines: it is less about victory and more about shared storytelling and the wonder of seeing familiar images reinterpreted in surprising ways.

The accessibility of the game makes it a welcoming option for different age groups and skill levels. The rules are straightforward, with a shallow learning curve, allowing younger players to participate meaningfully. Yet the depth of interpretation and the social dynamics provide enough nuance to engage adults who love a good storytelling session. The base game supports 3 to 6 players, which makes it an excellent social option for small gatherings or family game nights. For larger groups or longer sessions, you can explore expansions that add more players and more artwork to fuel ideas.

## Expansions and Variants
Dixit has many expansions, each with its own visual flavor and potential changes to the storytelling dynamic. Some expansions offer new card sets that reinvent the tone of clues you can create, while others introduce variant rules or scoring tweaks that can refresh the experience without turning it into an entirely different game. If you enjoy Dixit and want to keep the rounds feeling fresh, expansions are worth exploring. However, for a first-time buyer, the base game is more than enough to produce countless evenings of imaginative play, while expansions can be added gradually as your group warms up to the core mechanics.
