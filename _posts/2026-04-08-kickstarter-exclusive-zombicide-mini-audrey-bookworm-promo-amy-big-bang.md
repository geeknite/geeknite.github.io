## The Promos in Question: Audrey Bookworm and Amy Big Bang
### Audrey Bookworm: The Scholar with a Sword… and a Script
Audrey is the kind of survivor who brings a library card to a knife fight and somehow makes it work. ### Amy Big Bang: The Quantum Hacker with Explosions of Style
Amy Big Bang is the polar opposite of Audrey in aesthetic, but not in intent. Where Audrey whispers wisdom, Amy shouts velocity. The Big Bang motif translates into a dynamic pose—coils, cables, a wrench, and perhaps a tiny rocket or spark effect—giving Amy the feeling that every move could trigger a mini explosion of turn-order efficiency. Both minis come with rider dice on the base (for those who keep a collector’s log of every accessory), and both are tuned for tabletop photography—be it close-ups of tiny goggles or the satisfying snap of a book spine that can double as a shield in a pinch.

## Painting and Display Tips: Getting the Most Bang for Your Brush
### Basecoating and Shading
- Start with a neutral base coat for Audrey’s clothing—midnight blue or charcoal gray works to highlight the book’s old-leather vibe.
- For Amy, apply a light gray or white undercoat to make the gadgetry pop when you add metallics and enamel glazes.
- Use a brown wash on Audrey to age the cardigan and a blue wash on Amy’s lab coat to bring out the folds.
- Dry brushing: pick out the book spine texture on Audrey with a lighter shade to catch the light; on Amy, dry brush the cables with a faint copper or brass tone to give the toolset a functional metallic gleam.

### Freehand and Details
- Audrey’s label on the spine or a tiny bookmark detail can be painted with a thin brush to add personality without overshadowing the main sculpt.
- Amy’s gadget cluster benefits from tiny dots of white or silver to simulate sparks and reflections. A gloss finish on the tool ends helps them look actively polished.
- The bases can be treated with a splash of texture paint or tiny debris to suggest urban wasteland vibes, which matches most Zombicide settings.

### Display and Diorama Ideas
- Stage Audrey with a small shelf-like backdrop that mimics a library corner or study alcove; a few fake book spines leaning in the background can elevate the storytelling aspect without crowdsourcing the scene.
- For Amy, create a diorama of a ruined lab: scattered tools, beakers, and a safe glow from an “experimental” crack in the base. The result is a dynamic center of attention that photographs well under artificial light.
