## Durability, Maintenance, and Longevity
No deck lasts forever, especially when you’re pushing the boundaries of flourishes and playing in all sorts of venues. Here are practical tips to maximize the lifespan of your two-deck set:

- Storage: keep decks sealed until use. Once opened, store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The finish can fade with UV exposure, more a concern for collectors than for casual players.
- Handling: avoid repeated extreme bending. If you’re learning something new, practice with your non-dominant hand occasionally to reduce wear on your primary deck.
- Cleaning: use a soft microfiber cloth for fingerprints and light dust. Avoid moisture and harsh cleaners—card stock is not a sponge.
- Travel care: consider a card guard or simple sleeve when in transit. The two decks are robust, but rough handling can still leave tuck creases or tiny edge wear on the faces.

With some care, your two-deck set can perform reliably for many sessions, letting you practice your craft and look cool while you do it.
