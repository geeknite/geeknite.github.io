## Durability, Maintenance, and Longevity
No deck lasts forever, especially when you’re practicing advanced flourishes or playing in dusty bookstores, coffee shops, and kitchen tables that occasionally double as a craps table. Here are practical tips to help you maximize the lifespan of your two-deck set:

- Storage: keep the decks sealed until you’re ready to use them. Once opened, store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The finish can fade minimally with constant exposure to UV; this is more a concern for collectors than for daily players.
- Handling: avoid bending the cards excessively. If you’re learning a new flourish, practice with a non-dominant hand sometimes to reduce wear on your primary deck. The goal is to preserve the stock’s texture for as long as possible.
- Cleaning: a soft microfiber cloth can remove fingerprints and light dust. Don’t soak or use harsh cleaners—card stock isn’t a sponge, and moisture is the enemy of a crisp finish.
- Travel care: in transit, consider using a card guard or a simple box sleeve to prevent warping. The two decks in a sealed box are relatively robust, but rough handling can still leave marks on the tuck or tiny creases in the card faces.

With a little care, your two-deck set should perform reliably for many sessions, let you practice your craft, and avoid that infamous “deck of shame” look after a brutal poker night.
