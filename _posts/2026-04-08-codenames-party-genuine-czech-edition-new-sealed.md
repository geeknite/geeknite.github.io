## How to Play: The Core Loop
If you have played Codenames before, you already know the core loop. If not, here is a condensed refresher tailored to party scale:

- Teams: players are divided into two sides. Each team designates a clue giver who will be responsible for providing one word clues and a number indicating how many words on the table the clue applies to. The rest of the team attempts to guess those words based on the clue given.
- Word table: spread a set number of word cards face up on the table. In the party edition this may be a larger spread than standard, which adds to the social tension and the comedic value if someone shouts out a clearly unrelated word.
- Clue giving: the clue giver must give a single word clue that relates to multiple target words on the board. The number after the clue indicates how many words the clue relates to, to guide the guessing pace. A bad clue can derail a round, a brilliant clue can turn a single turn into a flash mob victory.
- Guessing: team members discuss and select one card at a time that they believe matches the clue. Each guess that hits a target word reduces the pool. If a team guesses an opposing word or a neutral word, it ends their turn or halts their progress, depending on the rules you are playing by. If they hit the assassin word, the round ends immediately and the other team wins.
- Victory: the first team to correctly identify all their words wins the game. With party style play, the pacing is quick and the mood light, but the strategic spark is still there. A well judged clue can lead to a triumphant cheer or a dramatic moment of collective realization that someone has misread a synonym and inadvertently summoned a dungeon monster of a word.

For the Genuine Czech Editions, there is often a slight polish on the wording of the clue deck and perhaps small modifications to ensure players across different languages can play together with minimal friction. The core mechanics stay faithful to the original concept, which is a big part of the charm of this edition.
