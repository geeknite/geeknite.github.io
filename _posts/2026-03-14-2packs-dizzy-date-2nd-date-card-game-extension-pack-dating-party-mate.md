## How to play: quick start and pacing tips

If you are new to these games, a quick start is essential to avoid analysis paralysis and the dreaded silence that can creep in when players stare at a row of similarly themed prompts. Here is a compact starter guide that works well for a first night:

1) Set expectations with a quick 2 minute orientation. Explain that the goal is to have fun, not win with perfect answers. This helps reduce performance pressure and increases participation.
2) Choose a lead chair or judge for the first round. The judge rotates each round to keep energy balanced.
3) Draw a prompt and respond. Other players give feedback or vote according to the edition rules. The judge awards points or awards votes as per the chosen variant.
4) After a few rounds, switch to The 2nd Date mechanics to introduce narrative prompts that encourage storytelling. Keep the pace brisk with a timer or a natural time limit for responses.
5) If using Dating Party Mate, sprinkle in a crowd challenge every few rounds to break up the rhythm and keep the energy high.

Decluttering tips for smoother sessions

- Use a timer for quick responses. A crisp 30 to 60 seconds per prompt keeps the energy high. 
- Keep a small rule sheet handy. It saves time and prevents arguments about what constitutes a valid answer.
- Have a lighthearted warning about boundaries before starting. It avoids uncomfortable moments and preserves the party vibe.
