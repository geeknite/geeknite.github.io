## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

As with any natural-fiber piece, care matters more than you would expect. The Relie Twill rug does well with routine cleaning that respects its hand-woven nature. Vacuum with a low suction setting and a gentle hand; avoid harsh beater bars that could pull fibers. If you notice small stains, treat them quickly with a mild detergent and water solution; blot rather than rub to avoid pushing the stain deeper into the weave. For persistent marks, a professional cleaning is a good option to maintain the rug s finish without compromising the weave. Do not expose the rug to direct sunlight for prolonged periods, as this can cause colors to fade unevenly; use curtains or blinds to protect your investment and keep your colors vibrant, like a painter s palette that forgot to take a break.

Keep in mind that natural fibers shed a bit during the first months. This is normal and does not indicate a manufacturing flaw. Regular vacuuming will gradually reduce visible shedding as the weave settles. You may also encounter small fibers on the surface after heavy vacuuming; this is just stray fibers that will disappear after a few uses. If you have pets with long hair, a weekly brushing with a soft brush can help reduce pet hair buildup.
