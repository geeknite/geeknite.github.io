### Compatibility and setup ease
The T705 is designed to plug into modern systems that support M.2 NVMe drives. It works with a wide range of motherboards and laptops that support PCIe 3.0 or 4.0 interfaces. The setup process is standard fare: insert the drive, boot, format if needed, and you are good to go. In BIOS/UEFI you may need to set it as a boot drive if you want to install the OS on it, but in practice the experience is straightforward and user friendly. No special drivers are typically required on Windows 10/11 or modern Linux distributions, which makes life easier for folks who just want their system to start running fast without a stack of install steps.

For those curious about compatibility with your current rig, note that laptops with compact heat profiles or older PCIe versions may see differences in peak throughput, but you should still experience a meaningful speed bump over your old SATA or small NVMe drive. Always verify that your motherboard firmware is up to date and that your M.2 slot supports NVMe in addition to the physical fit. If you are upgrading from a smaller NVMe or from SATA, the upgrade path is typically straightforward and worth the time.
