## Overview
The Infinite 10-Person 2-Workstation is a modular bench system designed to accommodate ten people, split across two longer rows that can be configured to suit a mid-size team. No screens are included, which means you can pick your own monitors, your own keyboards, and your own drama about whether your colleagues actually use the shared cable trays or pretend they do from across the room.

If you’re evaluating this for a New York loft conversion or a campus-style open-plan office, the long length could be both a benefit and a curse. The oak finish is attractive and durable, but be prepared to dust it regularly and to seal the transition between wood and steel with your favorite artisanal elbow grease.

## Design and ergonomics
### Ergonomics basics in a mega bench
In a perfect world, every workstation would be designed around the ideal math problem: space per person, reach envelopes, and the ability to adjust seat height without performing an Olympic-level stretch. This product adheres to a pragmatic approach to ergonomics: keep the central work surfaces at a comfortable height, provide enough legroom and knee clearance, and design the system so that each user has a reasonable path to the shared cable trays and power access points. It’s not a posture gadget, but it is a posture-friendly power station if you install the right chairs.

### Space planning for ten
The length is the star here. At 9000 mm, you can fit a whole team of ten in a single continuous line. It’s the kind of bench that makes productivity feel like a relay race, with each person passing along ideas (and occasional snacks) to the person on the next station. You’ll want to plan for aisle space along both sides to keep circulation smooth, and you’ll probably want acoustic treatment to keep the meeting room from becoming a blue screen of doom. The width of 1530 mm is generous enough for shared monitor arms, but since this version is no screen, you’ll still want to consider the relative width for comfortable chair placement, legroom, and a little wiggle room for people who “just need to stretch.”

## Cable management and power
A long bench is only as good as its cable management, and this system acknowledges that by offering trays and channels along the underside to route power, data, and the occasional USB-C bulldozer of doom. The two long rows are designed to minimize tangling by offering centralized channels. You’ll find that the cable runs are straightforward, with clearly labeled access points at the ends. If you’re a systems admin or a frustrated intern who has to manage a dozen different power adapters, you’ll appreciate how simple it is to route cables neatly to the ends rather than try to thread a spaghetti bowl around a coworker’s chair. The no-screen variant means you’ll need to install your own power strips at the ends, along with your preferred surge protection, which is a good reminder that power management is important even if you aren’t playing a giant version of Tetris with your cables.

Durability is a key selling point in a piece of furniture of this scale. The oak veneer is resistant to minor nicks, and the black powder coating on the frame hides fingerprints well enough that you can pretend you’re a minimalist if no one visits your office. ## Maintenance and care
Caring for a large bench like this is a ritual. Dust the surface with a microfiber cloth, wipe down with a mild wood-safe cleaner as needed, and avoid placing hot items directly on the veneer. A common-sense approach to maintenance keeps it looking sharp and reduces the chance of stickiness from late-night snack raids. If you have a particularly thirsty team, you may need to reapply a veneer-safe finish after several months to maintain the oak coloring. The black powder coating on the frame should be cleaned with a damp cloth; avoid abrasive cleaners that could scratch the finish. The result is furniture that ages gracefully, with a patina that tells the story of all the standups, sprint demos, and the occasional all-night hackathon.
