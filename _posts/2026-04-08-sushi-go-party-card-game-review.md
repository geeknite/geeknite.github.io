## What is Sushi Go! Party!?

Sushi Go! Party! is not merely a reprint or a tiny expansion; it is a standalone party edition that preserves the core mechanic of drafting cards from a selection of menus. Each round you draft a hand of cards, pass the rest, and score points based on combinations of sushi items and side dishes. The Party edition turbocharges the concept by offering a rotating pool of predefined menu cards that players print from the included booklet or mix and match to taste. In practice, you build a custom menu before the game starts, choosing from a wide array of dining options like Nigiri, Sashimi, Tempura, Dumpling, Maki, and more. The result is a tailored scoring puzzle for every session rather than a fixed, one-size-fits-all scoring sheet.

This edition shines when you want variety without changing the base rules. ## How to Play (Overview)

Sushi Go! Party! is designed to be learned quickly and played quickly. Here’s a high-level walkthrough, with emphasis on the bits that make or break the fun:

### Setup

- Each player gets a score sheet and a pencil, though many groups prefer the handy dandy token counters or a mobile counter app. If you want to feel like a real scorekeeper, you can track your points on a whiteboard and shout out numbers like you’re calling a ballgame. 
- Select a menu set from the included options according to the number of players. The menus define the scoring cards you will be chasing this round. The number of active menus scales with player count, so it stays balanced when the table is large or small.
- Shuffle the deck(s) and deal a hand to each player. In a standard round, you’ll be drafting from one to a handful of cards depending on the count chosen for that game. The rest sits in the middle, ignored by your hungry brain for the moment.

### Turn structure

- Each round, you pick one card from your hand to keep. Then you pass the remaining cards to the next player. This continues until all cards are claimed. The drafting mechanic is classic and satisfying—watching someone pass a card you wanted and realizing you’ve just backstabbed yourself with a delightful miscalculation is part of the fun.
- After all cards are claimed, you reveal your cards and score according to the current menu’s rules. The goal is to assemble the best combos of sushi types, side dishes, and special scoring cards within each round.
- The round completes, and you begin another round with a new hand and possibly a new menu set. In Party mode, the menus rotate or switch out, creating a fresh scoring puzzle without changing the core rules.

### Scoring basics
- Common scoring tracks include sets (e.g., three Nigiri of different varieties), food pairs (like Maki in combination with other items), and specialty cards that grant points when certain conditions are met.
- The wild cards or special scoring cards can swing a round, letting players rethink their strategy mid-game rather than committing to a single plan from the first draft.
- Some menus penalize if you ignore certain types of cards, encouraging you to diversify even while trying to chase a big scorer.

### End-of-round and end-of-game
- After each round, tally up points for the cards collected that round. The total across rounds determines the winner. In eight-player games, the pressure to keep pace grows as everyone tries to out-draft each other with a mix of luck and cunning.
- Depending on the number of players, you’ll likely play 2-3 rounds per game session, or more if your group really wants to suffer for the sake of bragging rights.

### Player interaction
- Interaction is mostly indirect: you influence what others can take by what you draft and pass. Rarely does one trading hand become a chaotic brawl; instead, it’s a genteel, competitive dance of who gets the perfect second choice and who ends up with an array of just OK cards.
- The fun of party-mode play comes from the variability in menu cards and the social exchange rather than from pure algorithmic optimization. It’s a game where you can boast about your sushi knowledge while not being judged for taking the last dumpling.

## Menu Variation and Customization

A core feature that sets Sushi Go! Party! apart from the base Sushi Go! is the ability to customize the menu. You can pick from a library of scoring cards to assemble a menu that fits your group’s vibe. The customization yields a different strategic flavor each time you play. The process of building a menu is part of the game’s charm. It’s a social puzzle you can tune to your group’s mood, from light and silly to surprisingly serious.

## Strategy and Tactics

Sushi Go! Party! rewards both micro-decisions and macro planning. Here are some practical gears to help you navigate the drafting wheel:

### Micro decisions
- Card recognition: As cards stack up in the deck, recognizing scoring patterns becomes a quick process. You’ll learn which cards are worth chasing and which ones are filler that only dilute your strategic focus.
- Opponent behavior: Pay attention to what others are collecting. If someone is clearly chasing a particular set, you can pivot to a counter-strategy that disrupts their flow or blocks a crucial card.
- Timing: The moment you decide you can no longer pursue your chosen scoring track is the moment you pivot. In Party mode, this pivot becomes more important as the menu changes between rounds.

### Macro decisions
- Menu curation: The choice of which scoring cards are enabled in a given round heavily influences your risk tolerance. You can design menus that reward early aggression or late, careful accumulation.
- Table dynamics: In larger groups, you’ll rely more on reads of what others might draft next and adjust accordingly. In smaller groups, you have more control to push a personal agenda.
- Resource management: While not a traditional resource game, the concept of managing your card flow — what you keep and what you pass — is central. If you hoard too many cards, you risk getting stuck with poorly aligned scoring cards at the end of the round.

### Path to victory nuance
- The best players in Sushi Go! Party! aren’t the ones who grab the most points from a single card; they’re the ones who consistently build flexible portfolios across multiple rounds with menus that force opponents to choose suboptimal options.
- A common pitfall is fixating on a single recipe or combination and missing out on the micro-shifts that the menu changes each round. Flexibility is the secret sauce here.
