## Safety Concerns and Maintenance

Like any piece of kid gear, safety is a moving target. Regular checks are essential to maintain the trampoline’s safe status. Inspect the springs or tension cords for signs of wear, look for any tears in the net, and ensure the padding remains intact. If you spot loose bolts or wobbling legs, stop bouncing and tighten or replace components as needed. Cleaning is simple: a damp cloth and a mild detergent will handle everyday grime; avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade padding or netting. Position the trampoline away from walls, shelves, or windows to reduce the risk of injury during enthusiastic play. If you’ve got carpet, a non-slip mat underneath can help keep the unit stable and reduce shifting on busy bounce days.

Maintenance wise, it’s smart to schedule a quarterly inspection. Tighten fittings, re-check the door zipper, and ensure the enclosure poles remain firmly attached. A few minutes of proactive care will extend the life of the unit and keep playtime chaos to a minimum.
