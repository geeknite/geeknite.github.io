## Strategy and tips for surviving the chaos

If you want to actually win something other than the bragging rights that come with a well-executed taunt, you will need a small bag of tricks. The 20 Card Expansion adds new variables, which means you should recalibrate your approach to risk. Here are some practical tips to make the most of the expansion without trading your soul for a perfect strategy:

- Learn the new cards by heart, not by memory alone. The better you understand the interactions, the less you will be surprised when a new card hits the table.
- Track the deck flow. The expansion increases the card count in play, which means you can gain an edge by watching for patterns in when the two or three most disruptive cards tend to appear.
- Use social pressure to your advantage. In a party game, influence matters as much as luck. A well-timed taunt or misdirection can influence decisions more than a perfect draw.
- Embrace the long game when it makes sense. The expansion has moments that reward patience and moments that reward bold action. Read the room, and adjust your strategy accordingly.
- Don’t overthink the art. Some players will suddenly overanalyze a card’s flavor text and forget the practical effect. Keep a quick mental note of what you can actually do with a card, and enjoy the ride.

### Solo and cooperative play

The base game is primarily a competitive party game, and this expansion continues in that vein. There are few, if any, explicit cooperative elements introduced by the 20 cards, but the shared chaos can create interesting group dynamics where everyone is rooting for someone else to fail. If you are hoping for a formal solo mode, this expansion is not the answer; for a cooperative or semi-cooperative vibe, you can improvise by mutual banter and shared story beats that make the experience memorable rather than solitary. The fun here is in the tension, not in a solo puzzle to solve.
