## Build quality and materials
### Solid pine wood characteristics
Pine is a friend of the budget-conscious woodworker. It’s renewable, widely available, and easier to machine than hardwoods. It does, however, have a propensity to move a little with humidity, which means you might see slight shifts in boards if you live in a humidity rollercoaster. The solid pine used in this vidaXL set is treated for outdoor life and assembled with stainless-steel hardware that resists corrosion. The result is a frame that’s light on your shoulders and heavy on the “this could outlast that plastic set by a season or two” test.

### Finish, weather resistance, and potential issues
The finish appears to a be a protective sealant designed to resist UV and moisture. Sun exposure can gradually lighten the color, emphasizing the wood’s natural grain. Rain may raise the grains slightly, but that’s not a fatal flaw—just a sign you might want to top the furniture with a breathable outdoor sealer every season or so. One potential caveat: pine, when left untreated, can absorb moisture and swell. VidaXL’s finish helps, but if you’re in a coastal environment with salt spray, you may want to retreat the entire set after a couple of seasons to keep things aligned and avoid squeaks.

## Maintenance tips
### Cleaning
Regular cleaning keeps the wood looking fresh. A gentle soap solution and water wipe typically does the trick. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the protective finish. For cushions, follow the care label—usually a removable cover that can be laundered in cold water.

### Seasoning and sealants
If you want to extend the life of your pine, apply an outdoor sealant every season or two. This helps slow moisture absorption and reduce swelling. If you’re the kind who enjoys DIY challenges, you can treat the wood with an oil or varnish that suits your climate. Just remember to test a small area first to avoid any surprises.
