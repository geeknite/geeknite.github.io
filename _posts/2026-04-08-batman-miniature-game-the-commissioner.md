## Introduction
In the neon-lit alleys of Gotham, even a civil servant can become a force of nature when paired with a catapult of dice and a heroic backstory. The Commissioner, a.k.a. Commissioner Gordon in the Batman Miniature Game, is not your typical piece on the table. He is a walking, talking policy memo with a badge and a very loud megaphone that in game terms becomes a morale aura, a leadership rule, and a reason to declare the bat signal twice per round.

### Box contents and sculpt
The miniature base is 40-50mm, depending on edition; the sculpt shows Gordon in his classic suit with a badge and a trench coat. The pose exudes that no-nonsense vibe you need when your plan A is to coordinate a city-wide manhunt with a coffee mug in your hand. The box includes a stat card, a punchy little token set, a stand base, and the standard neighborhood of token markers. The paint job to illustrate the figure is crisp; the details are crisp enough that you can pretend to draft zoning ordinances as you base coat.

### Rules and role in the game
In Batman Miniature Game, The Commissioner operates as a support and control piece rather than a glass cannon. He uses morale boosting auras, command actions that allow you to reposition your forces, and some unique control rules that let you manipulate the tempo of a round. He is not the quickest piece on the board, but he can pave the way for your Batman to strike by setting up lanes of fire or blocking lines with the authority of the law.

- Leadership aura that increases squad cohesion
- Special actions to reposition allies
- A calm, cool, collected set of orders that can outthink the criminals

The big thing here is how the Commissioner interacts with Batman and the GCPD units. While Batman shoves his fists through brick walls, Gordon uses policy to push the rest of your army through those same walls. It is a chess match with a cape.

### Painting and modeling tips
If you want your Commissioner to look the part, aim for the classic navy suit with a crisp white shirt and a badge on the belt. Dry brushing silver for the badge and a subtle trench coat wash adds a weathered look that says I have seen 20 good precinct coffee beans and two good precinct coffee crimes. If you want to add a Gotham‑specific touch, a small cane or a neat tie clip can be added to indicate the character's authority without turning him into a steampunk villain.

### Thematic flavor and narrative
The Commissioner is the voice of order in a city that has more exclamations than a subscription box of surprises. His card in BMG should include a line like the law is the law and the law will have your back when the crows of crime start to gather. His presence on the board is not about flashy heroics; it is about enabling your squad and reminding you that sometimes the best plan is to wait and let the criminals trip over the rope of your own defense, while you call the precinct to bring in a few extra units.

### Battle scenarios and campaign potential
As a campaign anchor, The Commissioner shines in missions that require control of zones, or in scenario trees that rely on keeping a secure perimeter while Batman disassembles the plan of the criminals. If you are the GM, you can weave a narrative that starts with a routine city ordinance vote and ends with a dramatic rooftop chase, with Gordon calling the shots in the middle of it all.

### Field notes on playstyle
If you want to be the cool kid on the block, pair The Commissioner with a Batman who wants to push through lanes and a Robbery Team that knows how to move around the board with surgical precision. The key is to maximize movement for your allied models while using The Commissioner to cover your angles with his morale aura. The synergy is not about raw power but about tempo and control.

### Comparison with other games and figures
If you have dabbled in other Batman games or other DC Universe skirmish games, you will notice that this piece is quite different from the tanks you get in other lines. In some games, you have big hitters; in Batman Miniature Game, you have a city official who can call out the cops and reorganize your forces in real time. The Commissioner is the anti-mastiff on the board — a leader who you can trust to keep your squad from wandering into the dark alleyway of defeat. He is also a great counter to heavy-handed villains because he can slow the tempo and force the criminals to respond to your initiative rather than the other way around.

### Painting tips and care
A neat painting approach uses a base coat of navy blue, followed by a charcoal wash to bring out the folds of the coat. A highlight on the edges with a lighter blue helps to make him pop on the board. A little silver on the badge and a accent of red for the tie gives the figure that comic strip vibe that the fans adore. Do not forget to glaze the face to capture that worn, world-weary look that Gordon has in the year 6,000 of Gotham time.

### Final thoughts and verdict
The Commissioner is not a flashy, kill-streak machine; he is the master of tempo, a conductor who can coax your entire army to do more than its stats would suggest. He is a must-have for players who love the game style and want a sense of narrative for their campaigns. He pairs well with Batman and his allies, giving you extra tools to control the board. If you are short of space or money, there are cheaper ways to pick him up in the used market or as a digital alternative, but you will be missing that Game Night energy that Gordon brings to your table.

Pros:
- Great tempo controller
- Useful aura that benefits nearby units
- Thematic and iconic for Gotham fans
- Good sculpt and board presence

Cons:
- Not a glass cannon; can be outpaced by fast attackers
- Some players may overlook his support role
- He is an expensive addition for a squad

The bottom line: If you want a game that balances strategy and story, The Commissioner is a strong pick. He helps you direct the game rather than simply running headlong into the enemy.
