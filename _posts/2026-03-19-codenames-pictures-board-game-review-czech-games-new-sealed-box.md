## How to Play: A Quick Primer (If You’ve Been Locked in a Box for a Year)

Here’s the down-low in plain terms, with the trademark Geeknite humor intact:

- Setup: lay out the grid of image cards. Each grid location shows a unique image. The spymasters receive a key that marks which images belong to their team. The number in the clue tells your team how many images are tied to the clue.
- Clue-giving: the spymaster gives a single-word clue plus a number, e.g., Earth 3. That clue must relate to some of the images in the grid without giving away too much to the other team or the assassin.
- Guessing: the team discusses and decides which images match the clue. Each chosen image is revealed. If it’s a red agent, your team adds a peg to your track; if it’s blue, the other team benefits (and so on). If the assassin is revealed, the round ends immediately and your team loses that turn.
- Endgame: the first team to reveal all its agents wins, but the game is designed to deliver a story—sometimes a chaotic, hilarious, and almost lyrical story—before someone claims victory.

Key differences you’ll feel right away: the path to victory is less about exact wordplay and more about your team’s shared associations, inside jokes, and willingness to interpret the absurd. If you love a good pun, you’ll need to reframe your brain as a picture-hound, which is a fun challenge in itself.
