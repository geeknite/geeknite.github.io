## Layout and workflow
Fortune Row is designed to speed up character creation and keep everyone aligned during play. The main sheet centers around a few core blocks:
- Identity and distinctions
- Mask and backstory
- Mythos and Logos tags in a tidy grid
- Power tags with space for flavor notes
- Trouble lane to capture persistent narrative complications
- A compact notes area for fate points and session events

This structure lets players fill in the important bits quickly and gives the GM consistent cues to drive scenes. The layout reduces page flipping and makes it easy to reference player info during tense moments. The alternate NPC sheets extend the same logic to non player characters adding cohesion to the world while keeping the players in the spotlight.

### Digital and print considerations
The deluxe set is offered as a high quality print and as a PDF with interactive fields. If you play online you can use the PDF to fill out fields and share with the group. If you prefer physical sheets you can print them on standard letter size paper and sleeve them for durability. The digital option is especially useful for campaigns that run across multiple devices or for players who want to experiment with different font sizes and color schemes. The print version remains an aesthetic highlight on the table a kind of tactile reward for choosing to embody a myth.

## In practice at the table
We tested Fortune Row Deluxe sheets with a party of four. Each player used a main sheet plus a few NPC sheets during a short session. The speed of setup and the clarity of the layout impressed the group. We observed quicker consensus around a hero s disposition and fewer interruptions caused by rule lookups. The compact tag grid allowed players to quickly assemble a pool of possible actions and to narrate how those actions align with their character s mythic identity. The notes area proved invaluable during downtime when players planned future moves and refined their masks. In short the sheets worked as intended and then some.

### Tips for getting the most from Fortune Row
- Start with the mask and the mythos logos pairs to establish the hero s thematic core. This sets the tone for the entire session.
- Use the trouble lane as a narrative lever. Let the conflict drive the next scene and reward players who lean into their Trouble with additional story beats.
- Keep a small reference sheet on the back for common terms. This reduces the initial friction for new players and makes the first session smoother.
- If possible print a backup set for players who attend late or for con style games where quick handouts are a plus.
