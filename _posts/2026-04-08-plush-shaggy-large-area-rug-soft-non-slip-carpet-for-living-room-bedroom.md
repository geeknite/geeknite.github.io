The rug is built from a dense ply of polyester fibers designed for durability, bounce-back, and color retention. The rug fiber is engineered to resist flattening, with a pile height typically in the 1.5 to 2.0 inch range. That makes it plush enough to sink your toes in, but not so long that vacuuming becomes an adventure.

### Size and Room Fit

Size matters, especially when you are trying to anchor a seating area without turning the room into a rug island. The Plush Shaggy rug typically comes in several common dimensions:

- 5x8 feet: great for small living rooms or under a coffee table with a single seating cluster.
- 6x9 feet: the sweet spot for most living rooms with a couch and a couple of chairs.
- 8x10 feet: designed for open layouts, larger seating groups, or under a bed in a master suite.

When selecting size, imagine foot traffic and door paths. A rug should extend beyond furniture by at least 12 inches on each side for a cohesive look that anchors the space. It also helps reduce the visual noise of a room with many textures.

Underfoot notes: the thicker the rug, the more cushion you get, but the more footprint you see in the pile after a few weeks. If you have pets, a shorter pile may be easier to clean and less prone to hair tangling.

### Non-Slip Backing and Safety

One of the rug’s critical features is its backing, designed to prevent slips on hard floors. Latex-backed options offer grip, while some products use silicone-based adhesives or a blend of fibers to improve grip. A non-slip backing increases safety for households with kids and pets and reduces the risk of tripping when walking across the room in sock feet at midnight.

Pro tip: Use a rug pad for maximum grip and to extend the rug’s life. A pad creates an extra barrier against wrinkling, which can happen if you fold the rug in half to move it.

### Cleaning and Care

Care is straightforward but not magical. The long fibers can trap dust and pet hair, so regular grooming with a vacuum designed for high-pile carpets is recommended. For stains, spot clean with a mild detergent and water; test in a hidden area first to prevent color migration. Deep cleaning every year or two, depending on traffic, can keep the rug looking fresh.

- Vacuuming: Use low-suction or a vacuum with a high-pile setting to avoid fiber damage.
- Stains: Blot, do not rub, to prevent fiber damage.
- Drying: Lay flat to dry and avoid direct sun for color longevity.
