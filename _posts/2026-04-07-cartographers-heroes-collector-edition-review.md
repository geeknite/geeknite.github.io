## How to play with the Collector's Edition
Cartographers is one of those games that feels intimidating until you dive in and realize the core loop is simple, elegant, and surprisingly forgiving. The Collector's Edition preserves that loop but enriches it with the Heroes content and premium packaging.

- Setup is straightforward. You lay out the map grid, distribute the hero tokens, and prepare your set of scoring cards. The new cards add a few dynamic targets that can shift the momentum mid-game, which keeps things interesting and stops players from becoming too predictable.
- Each round, you roll a set of dice to determine where you can place terrain. You then choose a placement from your tableau that both fits the dice results and advances your map’s layout according to the scoring text on the cards. The scoring objectives can be devious in a delightful way; you might find yourself chasing a chain of connected settlements or zoning clumps of forests for bonus points.
- The Heroes expansion adds asymmetrical incentives. Some rounds favor certain hero combos while punishing lazy placement of roads. This makes each game feel a little different, which is a win for long-term replayability.
- Endgame scoring is quiet drama. You tally points from a mix of map coverage, adjacency bonuses, and hero-specified bonuses. The tension builds as you add up points while watching opponents flirt with bold, high-risk layouts that may or may not pay off.
- Play time tends to hover around 30 to 60 minutes for a standard map, depending on group size and how ruthless your group’s penmanship is. The Collector's Edition is best enjoyed with a regular group: it gives you more material to work with and a few more levers to pull when a few players are racing ahead with clever territory patterns.

The box and its contents are designed to be accessible to newcomers while offering a bit more depth to veteran Cartographers players.
