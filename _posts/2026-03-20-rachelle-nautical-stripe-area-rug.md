## Materials, durability, and care
### Everyday durability
Rugs labeled as machine woven with a medium pile generally hold up well to typical family life. The key is to avoid aggressive dragging of heavy furniture that can snag fibers and cause pulls. If you have high traffic, consider a non slip rug pad and rotate the rug from time to time to achieve even wear. While we are at it, a regular vacuum with a soft brush attachment keeps dust from dulling the stripes. 

### Cleaning and stain management
For spills, blot promptly with a clean cloth to minimize saturation. Mild deteregents or rug-safe cleaning solutions work in most cases; test in an inconspicuous area first. For tougher stains, consult the rug care instructions, but generally avoid harsh chemicals that could fade the stripes or alter the fiber’s integrity. If a pet accident occurs, act quickly but refrain from over-wetting, as too much moisture can lead to stretching or warping that ruins the crisp stripe geometry. 

### Maintenance pattern to keep stripes crisp
- Vacuum regularly on a low to medium setting.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color exposure.
- Use a rug pad to prevent slippage and to extend the life of the fibers.
- Address spills within 24 hours for best results.

## Size, placement, and living with the rug
### Spatial planning with stripes
Stripes can visually alter the dimensions of a room. In small spaces, horizontal stripes can make walls appear longer, whereas in larger rooms they can add a sense of rhythm and structure. The nautical stripe also has a psychological aspect: it suggests a clean, ordered space that lends itself to a calm, methodical approach to decorating. If you love symmetry, align the stripes with architectural lines or furniture edges to create a sense of harmony. 

### Underlay and safety considerations
A quality rug pad is not optional; it is the unsung hero of rug life. It reduces slippage on tile or hardwood and softens the impact during foot traffic. Additionally, consider edge preservation: if the rug is in a doorway or high-traffic corridor, ensure the pad is thick enough to prevent curling at the edges. If you have area rugs that are frequently moved, invest in a pad with non-slip grip for both practicality and safety.
