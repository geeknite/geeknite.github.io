## Durability and maintenance
Durability is the backbone of an outdoor rug. This Koeckritz ribbed rug is designed to take foot traffic from loungers and deck chairs, plus the occasional heavy water bottle that accidentally takes a tumble. The polypropylene fiber resists moisture and dries fast. The rib texture may require a periodic shake-out to remove sand and grit that will inevitably accumulate near pools and docks, but the rug resists mold and mildew as long as you give it a proper rinse every now and then.

Maintenance tips:
- Regular shaking or vacuuming removes loose debris.
- A light rinse with a garden hose helps flush out salt and pool chemicals that can dull a surface over time.
- For stubborn stains, use a mild detergent and a brush with soft bristles. Do not use harsh chemicals that can degrade the fiber.
- Let the rug dry completely before placing furniture back on it to avoid trapping moisture and creating a damp smell.

## Cleaning and care for long life
Outdoor rugs do not require the same TLC as indoor premium options, but a little love goes a long way. Here is a straightforward care routine that keeps your Koeckritz rug looking good season after season:
- Water down after heavy use to remove sand and salts. A quick spray-down is enough most days.
- If you notice a spill, address it promptly with a mild soap solution and a gentle scrub. Rinse and dry thoroughly.
- Periodic deep clean: use a gentle detergent and a soft brush to lift dirt from the ribs. Rinse and let dry in the sun.
- Store away during extreme weather if you have a harsh climate, but the rug is designed to withstand a lot, so you will likely only need to roll it up for seasonal storage rather than full winter packing.

Real world wearability is influenced by foot traffic and the intensity of sun. The ribbed texture remains intact after a season of use, while the color holds up with typical outdoor exposure. You do not get a brand new look every week, but you get a rug that grows with your space and continues to do its job without turning into a soggy, faded blotch on the deck.
