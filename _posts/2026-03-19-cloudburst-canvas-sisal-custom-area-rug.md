## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Cleaning and routine care
Regular vacuuming with a soft brush attachment is essential for sisal to prevent dirt from embedding into the fibers. The positive side is that the texture tends to disguise dirt in high-traffic areas more than a plush pile, but patience is required. Avoid using heavy water or soaking the rug, especially if you have a natural fiber rug with a non-labric backing. For more on sisal care, see [Sisal rug care guide](https://www.thespruce.com/articles/sisal-rugs-care-4689591).

### Stains and spills
Sisal can resist some stains but is not a miracle fabric. Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and consider a mild, pH-balanced cleaner if necessary. For more persistent stains, consult a professional rug cleaner who understands natural fiber rugs. Do not steam-clean or soak the rug; excessive moisture can cause fiber swelling and potential warp in the backing.

### Preventive maintenance
Rotate the rug every 6-12 months to promote even wear and prevent persistent foot-traffic patterns. Use a rug pad to improve traction and cushion the edge, especially on hardwood floors.

### Seasonal care
In humid climates, you might see slight expansion or contraction with seasonal changes. This is normal for natural fibers; simply keep the room at a stable humidity level and avoid direct moisture exposure. If you’re ever unsure, a professional can take a look without you needing to cry into your yarn stash.
