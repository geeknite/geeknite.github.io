### Cleaning and stain response
Like most polypropylene-based rugs, Sorbet is reasonably stain-resistant. Spills should be blotted (not rubbed) promptly with a clean cloth. For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution or a specialized rug cleaner can help, but always test a small area first to avoid unexpected color shifts. The color palette doesn’t scream for a meticulous maintenance routine; it tolerates everyday care while still looking presentable. ### Maintenance rhythm
Vacuuming once or twice a week is usually sufficient for average wear. Avoid using highly aggressive suction on delicate areas, and consider a low-power setting for the initial pass to prevent fiber fuzzing. Rotating the rug every few months helps distribute wear more evenly and keeps furniture footprints from becoming too pronounced in one area. If you have sun exposure on one side of the room, rotating can also help prevent uneven fading over time.

## Care and Maintenance
### Routine care
- Vacuum at least weekly, using a suction setting that’s appropriate for a medium-pile rug.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth to prevent staining from setting in.
- Avoid harsh chemical cleaners unless tested on a hidden area first.

### Stain management
- For common spills (coffee, juice, pet mess), start with blotting then use a mild soap-and-water solution, rinsing lightly and ensuring the area dries completely.
- For more stubborn stains, consider a specialized carpet cleaner that is safe for polypropylene. Always test on an inconspicuous patch first.

### Rotation and sun exposure
- Rotate every 3–4 months to distribute wear evenly and to prevent noticeable sun fading on one side.
- If your room gets long sun exposure, you may notice uneven coloring over time. Rotating helps mitigate this risk and keeps the rug looking balanced.

### Cleaning cadence in busy homes
If you have kids or pets, you might need a more frequent care cadence. A quick weekly vacuum plus monthly spot treatment can keep Sorbet looking fresh without requiring a full deep-clean every few months. When it’s time for a deeper refresh, you can use a manufacturer-approved cleaner and follow the care instructions on the label to preserve color and texture.
