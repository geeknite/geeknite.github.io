## Handling, safety, and display etiquette
Display swords aren’t juggling clubs, folks. They demand respect, a little space, and an arrangement that suits their purpose as a conversation piece. Here are a few practical tips to get the most out of your Honshu Wakizashi Tactical 31" without turning your living room into a drama club:

- Display, don’t swing: The best way to showcase this blade is to place it in a glass cabinet, on a wall mount, or on a stand. The drama is in the silhouette, not in the swing. If you must handle it, do so slowly and with both hands, keeping the blade away from your body. Remember: cosplay moves are best left to the professionals—or at least to people wearing protective gloves—when real-world props are involved.
- Clean and maintain lightly: Wipe with a soft cloth to remove fingerprints and fingerprints-only oils. If you feel the need to go deeper, use a product designed for high-carbon stainless steel and avoid abrasive cleaners that could mess with the coating.
- Keep away from kids and pets: Not because this thing is a danger in itself, but because kids and cats can turn any beautiful display piece into a modern art sculpture with a few curious sniff-and-nudge moments. Respect the “fragile” sign your inner museum curator dreamed up for this shelf.
- Lighting matters: A subtle LED strip along the shelf can highlight the blade’s silhouette and cast dramatic shadows on the wall. It’s amazing how lighting can transform a display piece into your personal piece of theater.
