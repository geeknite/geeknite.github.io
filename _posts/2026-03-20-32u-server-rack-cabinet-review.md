### Wiring and Cable Lab Safety
The key to a clean rack is thoughtful cable management. Use color-coded cables and label both ends of each run. Use cable ties only as needed and avoid tight bundles that compress the airflow. If you have a lot of high-speed cables, consider adding a dedicated cable management arm to keep them out of the airflow path. Keep power and data runs separated where possible to prevent electromagnetic interference. If you follow these guidelines you will be rewarded with a tidy data center and fewer headaches during maintenance windows.

### Floor Plan and Placement
If your space has multiple racks, imagine a grid where air moves from front to back. Place the 32U rack to optimize the front intake and ensure there is a clear path behind the rack for hot air to escape. Avoid placing the rack directly against a wall if possible, since that creates recirculation zones that hamper airflow. If you must place it in a corner, ensure there is at least a few inches of clearance so fans and intakes can draw air from the room.
