## How to play: quick start for kids and families

### Setup

- Shuffle the deck thoroughly. If you suspect a rival is dealing a cheat code, shuffle again while they are not looking.
- Deal seven cards to each player. The remaining cards form the draw pile; flip the top card to start the discard pile. Pro tip: don’t flip a Wild Draw Four on your own head; you’ll regret it when four extra cards appear on your lap like confetti at a party that never ends.
- The game proceeds with players taking turns matching either color or number. If you can’t match, draw a card. If that drawn card can be played, you may do so immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.

### Cards and effects explained

- Number cards: basic building blocks. Match by color or number to stay in the rhythm of chaos.
- Action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two): these add momentum shifts. Skip makes the next player miss a turn; Reverse flips the play direction; Draw Two forces the next player to draw two cards.
- Wild card: changes the current color to any color you declare. It’s your dramatic moment, your chance to pivot strategy on a dime.
- Wild Draw Four: the classic power move that makes the next player draw four and skip their turn, but in this edition you’ll sometimes see additional twists attached to the wilds for flavor and strategic mischief.
- Wild variants in this edition: there are extra wilds that can swap colors, force a reshuffle, or trigger a mini-challenge between players. The exact effects can vary slightly by print run, which is perfect for keeping everyone on their toes.

## House rules and variants

Uno Night is a living thing. Some families love to tinker, others prefer the classic rules with a dash of chaos. Here are a few widely shared variants you can try:

- Stacking Draw Cards: if someone plays a Draw Two, the next player can stack another Draw Two to pass the burden along. This accelerates chaos and gives everyone a chance to scream in surprise—perfect for a quick punch of adrenaline.
- Wild Swap: a Wild Swap card lets you switch hands with any player. It’s like musical chairs meets a scavenger hunt, and it always produces at least one dramatic hand gesture.
- Color-blind accessibility: if you or someone at the table experiences colorblindness, consider printing a quick cheat sheet with large color icons and symbols. UNO’s core is color-focused, so a tiny adaptation can make the night more inclusive without sacrificing the energy of the game.
- Tempo rounds: set a timer and see who can deplete their hand fastest. It’s a great option when you want a shorter session that still feels like a victory lap for someone.
- Cooperative mode: for younger players or longer family events, you can switch to a team-based mode that emphasizes collaboration to reach a shared goal before the deck runs out.

- Kids: prioritize rapid discards and color variety. If you can get rid of a lot of cards in quick succession, you’ll be the hero of snack time rather than the person who forgot to shout UNO at the right moment.
