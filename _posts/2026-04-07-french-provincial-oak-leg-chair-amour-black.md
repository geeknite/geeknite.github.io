## Materials and build quality

### Oak legs and seat: a commitment to sturdiness

Oak is not a trend; it is a veteran. The Amour Black chair uses kiln-dried oak for the legs and frame, a choice that translates into a sturdy ritualistic durability. The legs are slightly tapered, which adds grace but does not compromise the sense that this chair can take a solid weekend of use without complaining. The seat is also oak with a thin cushion option—if you choose to add one—and a covering that complements the dark finish with a touch of contrast. The cushion, when equipped, uses a density that strikes a fine balance between seat-time comfort and the desire to avoid that “hot-sauce-on-metal” sensation after an hour of sitting.

The joinery is a nod to traditional methods without sacrificing modern reliability. Mortise and tenon joints meet modern adhesives and a careful sanding regime to produce a seat that feels anchored rather than ephemeral. The result is a chair that doesn’t squeak unless you forget to tighten the screws after a wine night. When you give it a wiggle test, there’s a reassuring absence of wiggly anxiety—the kind of wobble you notice only when your roommate’s cat decides to use your chair as a launchpad for high-altitude zoomies.

Beyond the oak, the hardware is chosen to blend in rather than shout. The connectors are unobtrusive, and the protective feet are made to glide over wood floors without leaving a trace.
