## Assembly Steps: A Quick How-To
If you have built furniture from flat-pack boxes before, you’ll feel right at home. The steps are straightforward, and there are only a handful of fasteners to secure. Here’s a quick overview:

1. Unpack and verify all components against the included parts list.
2. Spread the table halves on a clean surface with the playing side up. 
3. Attach the legs to each half using the provided bolts and washers. Tighten with the included hex keys until snug but not so tight you strip the metal.
4. Install the net posts on the long edge of the table and thread the net into place. Adjust the tension to your preferred height, usually around 15.5 to 16.5 cm for a standard setup.
5. If you chose wheels, attach them to the leg assemblies following the manufacturer’s guidance. 
6. Align the two halves, connect the folding mechanism, and test the lock pins to ensure the halves stay stable during play.
7. Do a quick bounce test with a few paddles to confirm the surface feels balanced and even.

## Maintenance and Care
Maintenance is the boring but essential part of any gear review. The good news is that this table is straightforward to care for. Wipe the surface with a damp cloth after gaming to remove chalk dust from the balls and natural oils from hands. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the surface finish. For outdoor use, keep the table dry and cover it when not in use. If your area experiences strong sun or heavy weather, a dedicated cover is worth its weight in sunscreen for your table’s lifespan. 

In terms of storage, avoid stacking heavy items atop the folded halves. The weight distribution is designed for balance, but we want to prevent any accidental bending or warping. If you notice consistent wobble after a while, check the leg joints and tighten the bolts as needed. Regular maintenance will extend the life of the folding mechanism and keep the play experience consistent across seasons.
