- Enclosure net: A full-height safety net reduces the risk of a tumble off the edge. The zipper is sturdy enough for repeated use and the net attaches securely to the frame, keeping those little experimental jumpers from discovering that gravity is real.

H3: Setup tips from the trenches

- Place the trampoline on a flat, level surface to prevent wobble that makes your kid feel like they’re on a wave.
- Keep the area around the trampoline clear of furniture, sharp corners, and loose toys. We know, this sounds like your living room is about to be a safety drill, but trust us.
- Use the enclosure net consistently. The more the kid bounces with a net around, the less likely they are to wander toward the danger zones like bookshelf forts or sibling wrestling rings.
- Regularly inspect the net, padding, and frame connections. Loose clips or sagging nets are not just cosmetic issues—they increase risk during vigorous bounce sessions.

Maintenance and Cleaning

Cleaning is a surprisingly simple affair. The mat and pad surfaces can be wiped with a mild soap solution and a damp cloth. The net can be gently brushed to remove dust, and the balls can be cleaned separately if they start to pick up a chalky playground vibe. Inspecting the springs and frame every few months is a good habit; tiny cracks or rust spots on the frame should be addressed early. If you notice any significant wobble, it’s wise to tighten connections or re-check the floor surface. Short of that, a little routine care goes a long way in keeping this toy safe and functional for the next child who calls it “the best thing ever.”
