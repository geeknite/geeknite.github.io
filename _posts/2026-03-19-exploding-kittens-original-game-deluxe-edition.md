## Setup and gameplay: a refresher
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you know the rhythm. If you haven’t, here’s the quick gist:

- Each player starts with a small hand of Defuse cards
- The deck is shuffled and placed in the center
- On your turn you can either play a card from your hand to influence the board or draw a card
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten and have no Defuse, you’re out
- Defuse cards can banish an Exploding Kitten by using a bit of cunning, luck, and possibly a cat pun

In the Deluxe Edition, the mechanism is unchanged; the upgrade is in feel. The cards shuffle more smoothly, and the larger cards reduce the number of awkward face-down glimpses that sometimes happen with standard-size cards.

### Turn structure and pacing
A typical game moves along in brisk fashion. You’ll have a few rounds where players try to dodge the dreaded kitten, interspersed by moments of strategic card play. The pacing is influenced by player count: more players means more chaos, more misdirection, and more opportunities for glory when someone survives the round by a sliver.

### The role of misdirection
This is where the joke meets the game’s heart. The suits of cards, art, and descriptions often lead players to misdirection about what’s in the deck. The Deluxe Edition’s nicer print and bigger cards make these misdirections more vivid, turning the state of the board into a small performance.

## House rules, variations, and how to mix things up
No game-night is complete without some house rules. Here are a few you’ll see at Geeknite events:

- Speed rounds: limit each player to a single action per turn to speed up the game.
- Team play: pair players and let Defuse cards double as exchange opportunities between teammates.
- No-peek: players must keep their hand face down while drawing to reduce the temptation to memorize what’s been played.

The Deluxe Edition’s stronger printing helps these variants because it reduces misreads, which means less arguing about whether a card was blue or purple.
