### Assembly and installation

Assembling this desk is a group sport. The weighted bulk is delivered in segments, with predrilled holes and labeled components. Expect a couple of hours of hands-on assembly if you have one or two helpers. If you are a lone ranger, be prepared to recruit a friend or two and caffeinate heavily. The process is straightforward: attach the legs, connect crossbars, mount cable channels, and align the top panels. Tools are typically included, but you may want a torque driver to ensure consistent tightening. The Beech finish is robust, but like any furniture, it can show fingerprints and minor scuffs if you mishandle it with a red wine sneeze.

### Ergonomics and space planning

A six person setup demands planning. Ensure each workspace has a comfortable reach to monitors, keyboards, and a shared power strip. We tested multiple monitor configurations with 24 inch to 32 inch displays. The central channel can host a minority of power cords. Use cable ties to maintain a tidy appearance. For height alignment, add adjustable monitor arms for ergonomic posture. It is wise to consider a footrest and a chair with adjustable lumbar support for prolonged sessions. For more on ergonomics, OSHA provides a practical primer at [ergonomics basics](https://www.osha.gov/ergonomics).
