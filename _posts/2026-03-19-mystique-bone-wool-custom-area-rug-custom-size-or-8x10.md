Wool is the hero of many home textiles, and for good reason. The Mystique Bone Wool rug uses high-quality wool fibers that deliver a soft texture underfoot without that synthetic squeak that makes some rugs feel more like kitchen towels than a cozy floor companion.

- Texture: Expect a plush, mid-pile feel with a gentle bounce. It’s not a shag, but it’s not a flat weave either. If you like walking around barefoot and feeling like you’re stepping on a cloud that also doubles as an ergonomic mat, you’ll enjoy this.
- Density: The weave density matters for durability and a sense of substance under your toes. Wool fibers don’t just lay down and quit; they stand up to traffic and still look refined after a good vacuum—though you should vacuum regularly to keep the natural oils balanced and the pile upright.

## Maintenance and care: do not fear the lint monster

Wool rugs require a touch more respect than their synthetic cousins, but the payoff is a longer-lasting, more luxurious feel. Here’s the practical maintenance playbook for Mystique Bone Wool:

- Regular vacuuming: Use a low-suction setting and a beater brush if your vacuum offers it. Gentle, consistent passes keep the pile upright and the fibers from becoming matted.
- Spot cleaning: For spills, blot—do not rub—with a clean cloth. Use a mild detergent solution and test a small area first to ensure colorfastness. For tougher stains, treat as you would with any wool fabric—gentle, patient, and with a quick test on a hidden corner.
- Rotation: Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and exposure to sunlight. Wool loves variety, and your floor deserves a chance to show off its personality equally.
- Protection: If you have pets or kids, consider a rug pad and perhaps a protective barrier under dining chairs. The goal is to avoid constant friction that could distort the pile or scratch the floor beneath.

Maintenance is a ritual, not a chore. Embrace the small joys: the way a clean sip of tea reflects on the wool’s surface, or how your toes sink in during a lazy Sunday morning. The Mystique Bone Wool rug invites you to slow down and enjoy the texture—yes, it’s basically textile therapy with a soft edge.

Here is a practical, no-nonsense guide to getting your Mystique Bone Wool rug from order form to floor romance:

- Step 3: Select color tones and pile height. If you’re naming your colorway, keep it simple and descriptive—this isn’t a paint color chart; it’s footwear for your feet.
