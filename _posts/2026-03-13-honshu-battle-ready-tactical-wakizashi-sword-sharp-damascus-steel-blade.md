## Maintenance and Care
### Everyday Care Routine
No blade is a set it and forget it wonder. Damascus steel benefits from a simple routine. After each use, wipe the blade clean to remove fingerprints, oils, and any residue from your practice session. Apply a thin layer of quality mineral oil to the blade to prevent oxidation. Do not soak the blade in water or leave it wet for extended periods; moisture is a blade s least favorite cousin. Store the blade in its sheath when not in use, and check the alignment of the guard and tang to ensure nothing has shifted during handling.

Regularly inspect the edge for nicks and micro-chips. If you find any imperfections, consult a professional bladesmith for a proper sharpening. Do not attempt aggressive sharpening at home if you do not have experience; Damascus blades require attention to grind geometry to preserve the pattern and ensure edge geometry remains consistent. A careful approach to maintenance will extend the life of the blade and keep it looking sharp enough to date a poster boy for medieval lore.

### Safety First
Safety is not a buzzword here; it is the moral of the story. Always handle a blade with attention and respect. Use proper protective equipment during training or demonstration. Keep the blade out of reach of children and curious pets. When carrying the blade, ensure the saya is properly fastened and that your grip is secure. The last thing you want is a dramatic swing that ends with the blade in an unrelated object or a dramatic sigh from a bystander who looked away for a second.
