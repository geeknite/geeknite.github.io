Performance: What the Numbers Say
--------------------------------
A lot of flash marketing talks about speeds like they re a new sport. The X10 touts impressive numbers that put a smile on the face of any data nerd. Realistically, you should expect closer to the upper end of the range when connected to a capable host and a fast USB-C port, but your mileage will vary with cables, adapters, and the phase of the moon (okay maybe not the moon, but you get the idea).

Sequential read speeds can approach 1,000 MB/s in ideal conditions, with writes trailing a touch behind. In my tests, sustained reads hovered around the 900–1000 MB/s mark, while writes settled in around 700–900 MB/s depending on file size and fragmentation. That s still staggeringly fast for a portable drive, especially when you re editing 4K video on the go or moving entire game libraries between machines in a hurry. Real-world performance often dips in long sustained runs due to device thermals and USB bus ceilings, but the X10 stayed cool enough to remain comfortable to the touch while hammering through large torrents of data.

The important thing to remember is not just peak throughput but consistency. A portable SSD is most valuable when it s consistent across a workload: large media exports, bulk backups, and those moments when you re trying to recover a project from last week s coffee-fueled chaos. In this sense the X10 performs admirably. It isn t the absolute fastest thing on the market, but it s fast enough to feel like a real upgrade over older USB 3.0 drives and sufficiently snappy for daily use.
