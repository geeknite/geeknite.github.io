## Unboxing and assembly: a quick reality check
If you love the ritual of IKEA-style flat-pack theatre, you will probably enjoy assembling this unit. If you hate it, you’ll still survive, but you may need a buddy who brings more humor than muscles.

- Prep work: clear a large flat area, ideally with space for one extra person to fetch snacks while you curse the hex bolts at step four.
- Tools: you’ll need a basic set of Allen keys, a screwdriver, and a measuring tape. No specialized power tools required, though a drill with a clutch can speed up the process.
- Process: expect a two-phase assembly. Phase one is the base assembly, where you connect the frame pieces and anchor the cable trays. Phase two is the top surfaces and the screen mounts. The system is designed to be intuitive, but it helps to label parts as you go and keep the little screws in their own tiny jars so you don’t mistake a foot of wire for a decorative accent.
- Time: plan for a couple of hours if you’re doing it solo and want the desk to stay in the living room while you attempt to explain your life choices to a box of Allen bolts. With a friend or colleague, you’ll shave the time down noticeably.

We did a light mock-up to check the layout before diving into the final assembly. The result felt like a blueprint for efficient teamwork—everyone has a dedicated space but shares a central surface. If you’ve ever seen a sports team huddle, you’ll understand the energy this system aims to replicate: communication, synergy, and the occasional friendly mockery when someone forgets to align the screens.

## Ergonomics and space planning: breathing room for brains
Space planning is where a lot of good product design earns its keep. The Express unit creates a strong baseline for ergonomic alignment while offering enough flexibility to adapt to different body types and work styles.

- Seating alignment: Each workstation block aligns with the central shared surface so teammates can maintain eye contact and still have personal space for elbows and keyboard wrists. The 2-person screen mounts help reduce neck strain by keeping monitor tops near eye level for the average adult. If you have taller team members, you can swap out or adjust the screen heights without compromising structural integrity.
- Posture-friendly design: The surface height at 1200 mm is within typical ranges for standing and seated tasks, with ample leg clearance. If your team toggles between sprints and standup rituals, you’ll appreciate the ease with which you can switch from seated to standing tasks without a dramatic desk migration.
- Cable management: The system hides wires away neatly with integrated trays and clips. It’s not magic, but it’s close. You’ll still want to label power outlets and keep a few spare Velcro ties around for the sprint where every cable aligns as if guided by a benevolent tech god.
- Personal space and collaboration: The long, shared panel layout fosters collaboration while still preserving pockets of privacy for writing, planning, or the occasional video call that requires a moment of quiet between teammates. If your team relies on deep work, you can add optional privacy screens that clip into the top rails without breaking the aesthetic.
