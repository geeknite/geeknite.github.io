### Tufting technique: the practical magic behind the fluff

Tufting involves a needle and a backing fabric. In handmade tufted rugs, each loop or cut creates a tuft that contributes to the rug’s texture and resilience. ## Care and maintenance: machine washability in real life

One of the standout features of a rug like this is its machine washability. In a world where many area rugs demand delicate handling and professional cleaning, a machine washable tufted rug is a breath of fresh laundry air. Here are some practical care tips that align with real-life usage:

- Washing: Use a gentle cycle with cool water to minimize color bleeding and textile stress. Use a mild detergent that won’t strip the fibers.
- Drying: Air dry flat to prevent stretching or warping. Avoid high heat in tumble dryers; it can distort the backing and flatten the tufting.
- Spot cleaning: For small stains, a quick dab with a mild soap solution can prevent more stubborn marks; remember, the goal is to keep the design crisp and the pile plush.
- Frequency: For high-traffic areas, consider rotating the rug every few months to ensure even wear and maintain a uniform look.
