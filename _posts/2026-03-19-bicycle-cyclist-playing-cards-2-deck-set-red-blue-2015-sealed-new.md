## Practicalities: Stock, Seals, and Storage
### The Seal and What It Means for Value
A sealed two-deck set is a dual threat and a safety net: it preserves the exact moment of purchase and the pristine condition that matters to collectors. If you plan to resell or swap later, a sealed set typically commands a premium over open decks, provided the seal is intact. The flip side: sealed decks can occasionally harbor subtle moisture or humidity changes over long storage. If you intend to tuck them away for a decade, consider climate control, humidity control, and perhaps a small, villainous anti-mold plan. If you detect foggy plastic or an odor that suggests a lab experiment, you should air them out before you call them collector-grade.

### Storage Tips for Your Two-Deck Time Capsule
Keep the set away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade on the backs, especially the red deck. A dark shelf or a display box is ideal. If you have a fancy cabinet, ensure vibrations from doors or nearby windows won’t rattles your two decks into disarray. A dust-free environment helps; dust is the silent saboteur of collector stock. If your cards live near a computer or a coffee machine, place them in a secure spot that isn’t a caffeine-scented hazard or a magnet for stray USB drives. And if you’re paranoid about temperature swings, consider a small protective climate-friendly sleeve, just to keep those inks from whispering, “remember the year 2015?” every time you reach for a card.
