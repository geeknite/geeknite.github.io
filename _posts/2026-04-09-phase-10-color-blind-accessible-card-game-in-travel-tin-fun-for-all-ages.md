## How to Play a Color Blind Friendly Phase 10 (A Quick Setup and Run-Through)
### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a set number of cards to each player depending on the variant you’re playing. In a typical Phase 10 setup for 2–6 players, you deal ten cards per person and lay out the rest as the draw pile.
- Each player starts with a score of zero. The goal is to complete certain phases, moving through phase stacks as you go. The travel tin makes it easy to generate a quick setup anywhere—on a park bench, in a hotel lobby, or inside a starship’s conference room if you happen to have one.
- The color blind cues are visible when you lay out your cards. If you need a quick reminder of which color code corresponds to which color group, the underside of the tin includes a reference card with the symbols for quick glances while you’re trying to focus on your hand without a dramatic sigh.

### Turn sequence
- Draw a card from the draw pile or the discard pile. It’s standard Phase 10 tempo: quick, strategic, then chaos again as your opponents snag something you need.
- Attempt to lay down a phase. In color blind mode, you will also be listening for the cues: can you complete the set or run? If you complete a phase, you lay cards down in front of you and continue building toward the next one. If not, you discard a card and wait for your next turn.
- The round ends when one player completes all phases or when the deck is exhausted. Scoring happens, and the next round begins with shuffled cards.

### Color blind accessibility in play
- The primary benefit comes from the added cues and, in some designs, textual labels. If you have a color blind player in the group, these cues reduce the cognitive overhead of color matching so the game stays fast and fun instead of turning into a color-differentiation marathon.

If you’re someone who enjoys mixing the rule set with your own house variants, this edition tends to be friendlier to improvisation because you can point to a symbol or a number without a frantic pantomime of color names.
