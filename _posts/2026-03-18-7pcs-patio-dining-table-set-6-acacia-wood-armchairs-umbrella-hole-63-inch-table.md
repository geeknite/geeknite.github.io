## Care, maintenance, and weather readiness

Wood loves a routine, not a brute force approach. Routine cleaning with mild soap and water keeps the surface from tasting like last year’s grill leftovers. Avoid harsh cleansers that strip the natural oils. After washing, dry thoroughly to prevent water spots or swelling; a well-dried surface makes the finish last longer and the wood more honest about its age.

Seasonal maintenance matters. Depending on climate, you might oil or seal every 6 to 12 months. This preserves the color and helps minimize cracking. In cold or humid environments, the wood can appreciate a little extra attention before winter arrives. A cover is a good idea during heavy rain spells or winter storage. Remember, outdoor furniture loves consistent care as much as it loves gatherings of friends and family.
