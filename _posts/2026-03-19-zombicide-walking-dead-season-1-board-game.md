## How to play in a nutshell
Zombicide operates on a cooperative design: each player controls a single survivor, and together you strive to complete a mission while the walkers close in. Turn structure is straightforward, with a handful of steps that feel intuitive after a couple of plays but still reward smart planning.

- Setup: Lay down map tiles, choose a scenario, deploy walkers, and assign survivors to players. Each survivor has a unique starting kit and a few special abilities on their character card.
- Turn order: A standard round runs plan actions, perform actions, draw and resolve event cards, and resolve walkers. You’ll manage movement, melee and ranged attacks, searches for loot, and team coordination to cover angles and bottlenecks.
- Actions: Each survivor typically has 3 action points per turn. Actions include Move, Search, Attack, Open Doors, or a special Skill. The tactical joy is deciding when to go loud (risk attracting more walkers) and when to go quiet (risk burning actions on critical tasks).
- Combat: When you attack, you roll dice that scale with weapon and range. Zombies aren’t polite dinner guests; they crowd you, but they don’t ignore your best attempts to stop them. The thrill comes from dice misfortune and chaining actions to maximize hits while keeping allies alive.
- Objectives: Each Season 1 scenario has a win condition — escaping, retrieving an item, or surviving a number of rounds. Some scenarios reward stealth and map control; others reward a rapid, noisy push toward a final objective. You’ll often weigh the risk of moving into a new zone against the long-term payoff of a precious tool.

The game threads nerve-wracking tension with satisfying mechanical precision. It’s not a timed sprint; it’s a careful dance where a single misstep can cascade into a compromised turn that ends with half the crew sprinting down an alley while the rest hold the line with a pistol and a prayer.

### The bitey bits: combat and morale
The walking dead aren’t mere obstacles; they’re pressure points. Each encounter forces you to decide how many actions you’re willing to spend to clear a room and whether chasing a loot crate is worth the risk of encountering a mass you can’t handle in a single turn. The system rewards group planning: you’ll often coordinate to block walkers from reaching a vulnerable ally while another survivor searches for an essential tool.

Morale in Season 1 is a light touch but meaningful. Injuries don’t just reduce hit points; they affect your ability to search or move. If your character takes too many injuries, their effectiveness drops, and you’ll feel the consequences across the board. This creates fatigue that echoes the show’s mood without melodrama, reminding you that survival depends as much on prudence as on bravado.

### Scenarios and replayability in Season 1
Season 1 is built for variability. You’ll get a handful of distinct scenarios, each with its own map layout, win condition, and a few twist rules that appear as you advance. Replayability hinges on two factors: modular map tiles and scenario variety. You’re not consistently playing the same layout, and each scenario imposes different constraints, so your group won’t hit boredom quickly.

For players who crave deeper strategic challenges, Season 1 also offers optional “hard mode” variants and house rules to crank up the tension. Introduce stricter time limits, alter starting toughness for walkers, or tweak loot distribution. The result is a game that scales with your gaming group and provides new pain-inflicting joy on every re-run.

## Strategy tips and common pitfalls
- Early game isolation vs. early expansion: In the first few turns, it’s tempting to rush for loot. Resist sprinting into the unknown; it’s usually better to secure a safe corner where you can plan your next moves with fewer walkers spilling onto your turf.
- Manage noise and attention: Zombicide builds tension through potential noise. If you overcommit to noisy actions in a crowded zone, you’ll invite a skirmish you may not finish in a single turn. Use quieter searches to locate critical items and rely on adjacent teammates to handle the melee if the odds tilt unfavorably.
- Team synergy matters: Positioning is everything. A strong plan often involves one survivor drawing walkers toward a chokepoint while another player works behind them to complete an objective. If your table tends to scatter, you’ll miss the synergy that makes the game feel cinematic rather than chaotic.
- Resource management: The inventory system rewards careful planning. Don’t waste weapons with limited durability on trivial encounters. Save your best tools for the moments when a single misstep could end the run.
- Learn the scenarios: Each Season 1 scenario has its own rhythm. Read the win condition, note any timer or environmental effects, and tailor your opening moves to set up your plan rather than reacting to board chaos as it unfolds.
