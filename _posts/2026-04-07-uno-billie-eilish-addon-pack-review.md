## How to play with the add on pack

### Quick start guide

Getting started is surprisingly painless. The add on mix-in is designed to slot into the base UNO deck without requiring a new rules bible. Here is a quick route to a smooth setup:
- Separate the new mood and action cards from the rest of the pack and shuffle them into a common deck. The exact percentages vary, but a good approach is to have roughly the same number of new cards as your base deck size allows so the additions feel organic.
- Keep the standard UNO rules in place for the core game. The mood and action cards introduce optional twists that you can toggle on or off depending on the vibe of the night.
- Decide on a variant before you start. You can keep it simple by declaring the mood cards active for the entire game, or you can trigger a mood reset after a certain number of rounds for a dynamic shift in play.

### Variant options

If you want to experiment, here are two common variants that work well with this pack:
- Mood shift: When a mood card is drawn, players swap a card with the player to their left. This creates a social moment and shakes up what you thought you held.
- Tempo boost: An action card that speeds the game by forcing a two card play on the next player if a certain color is played. It adds pressure without breaking the core UNO logic.

### Example play flow

In a typical round you will see the familiar back-and-forth: match color or number, draw cards, shout UNO when you are one away. With the add on pack active you may toss a mood card mid-round that requires players to draw not just one card but two if they cannot answer a question or complete a tiny skill test included on the card. The social moment is real, and it often results in a surge of laughter as players attempt to maintain strategy while the mood card sways the odds.
