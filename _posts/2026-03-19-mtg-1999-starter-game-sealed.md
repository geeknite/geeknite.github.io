What is inside a starter set

- A set of basic land cards to keep the games moving without needing to source external resources. In practice this means enough land to fill out the mana base for the two starter decks, so you can slam into early game plans without a vendor run on your hands.
- A simple guide that introduces the core rules of MTG at a pace a newcomer can appreciate. The guidance is practical, focusing on the flow of a turn, how to tap mana, how combat works, and how the phases fit together.
- A small cheat sheet of common keywords and a few strategic tips to give new players a solid sense of how to pilot their deck.

Storage, preservation, and handling tips
Sealed MTG items are designed to withstand a bit of neglect, but that does not mean you should store them in a hot attic and forget about them. Here are some practical tips to keep your sealed 1999 Starter Game in good shape:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV light can degrade colors and may yellow the cardboard over years.
- Maintain a stable humidity level. Excess moisture can lead to warping of the box and potential ring marks on the surface.
- Avoid extreme temperature fluctuations. A steady environment is kinder to the box and the product inside.
- Keep the outer wrap intact if your goal is preservation. If you need to inspect the product, do so carefully and consider resealing if possible to maintain the sealed status.
- When handling, touch only the edges to avoid bending the box corners. The collector world loves pristine edges as much as the gameplay itself.
