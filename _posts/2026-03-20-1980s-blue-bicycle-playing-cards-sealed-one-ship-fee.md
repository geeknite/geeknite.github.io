## Authenticity: how to verify a vintage Bicycle deck from the era

Authenticity matters almost as much as aesthetic. Here are some quick checks you can perform to verify that you’re dealing with a genuine 1980s blue Bicycle deck, sealed and ready for your collection:

- Inspect the tuck box for font fidelity and back design alignment. Vintage boxes typically show slight variances in alignment and ink spread, which is normal; perfect alignment is rarer and historically more expensive.
- Examine the seal. A modern counterfeit might mimic the look but will use different shrink-wrap texture or edge finishing. If the wrap feels unusually thin or the seal shows modern labeling such as a holographic sticker, you should proceed with caution.
- Look for the barcodes and the Vintage Bicycle logo. Some reproductions will attempt to replicate these, but tiny inconsistencies in the typeface can be a giveaway.
- Compare the card stock feel to known references; while not practical for everyone, a side-by-side with a known-good vintage deck is a great method to confirm authenticity in person.
