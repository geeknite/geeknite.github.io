Ports, Specs, and What They Mean for You

Here’s the heart-to-heart about the Blaze Hub’s port dragon. The exact port counts can vary by revision, but a typical Blaze TB4 hub typically includes the following real-world expectations:

- Upstream: 1x Thunderbolt 4 (40 Gbps) that also delivers PCIe data and up to substantial power delivery to charge laptops.
- Video: 1–2x HDMI (up to 4K60 per port, depending on TB4 bandwidth sharing and host capabilities). Some revisions provide a DisplayPort alternative or unique passthrough; check your model if you plan to run multiple high-res displays at once.
- USB: A mix of USB-C and USB-A downstream ports—often a handful of USB 3.x with high data throughputs (up to 10 Gbps+), enough for external SSDs, mice, keyboards, and the occasional nunchuck of peripherals.
- Networking: Gigabit ethernet is common, with some boards offering 2.5 Gbps Ethernet for serious network enthusiasts.
- Media and card slots: Some Blaze Hub variants sport SD/microSD card readers for quick media transfers, which is a godsend for creators.
- Power Delivery (PD): A substantial PD profile that can pass through significant wattage (enough to top up most laptops during use; exact wattage depends on the host and cable). This is the sort of feature that turns a dock into a lifecycle-saving buddy for your laptop.

In practical terms, that means you can run a high-refresh-rate workflow with an external monitor, attach high-speed storage, plug in a couple of USB peripherals, and keep the laptop charging—without swapping between ports like a drunk octopus. The Blaze Hub aims to be the central nervous system of a compact creative setup, and in that mission, it mostly delivers.
