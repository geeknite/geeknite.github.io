## Interactions and synergies: Notable combos and potential traps
- Soldier tokens + buffs: The more soldiers you have, the more impact your buff spells and emblems will have. This is a classic synergy that makes every single token count.
- High-power removal synergy: Your -3 is most effective when you’ve already got a few big threats on the other side or you’ve lined up a few high-value targets that your opponents rely on. If you can time it right after your table has dumped a big threat, you might save your own board while wiping out theirs.
- Combos with token-centric stax pieces: Some cards slow the table in interesting ways, enabling you to restructure the battlefield to your advantage. Elspeth works well in decks that lean into both resilience and a patient build-up toward a big endgame.
- Pitfalls: Beware overcommitting into a lockdown that doesn’t actually win you the game. You can out-token your opponents, but if you don’t have a plan to close, you’ll risk helping others catch up or giving someone a window to pivot around your defenses.

## Collecting angles: care, storage, and card-grading tips
- Storage: Use rigid sleeves, then place in a solid 100-count or larger box to prevent warping. Foils can warp over time if they’re exposed to heat and moisture, so keep them climate-controlled.
- Handling: Hold cards by the edges when you can; avoid touching the face of the card to maintain the foil sheen and art. Foil cards are more prone to fingerprints that show up under light.
- Grading: If you’re into grading for value, keep your minty borderless foils in top shape; any wear can reduce the grade. You’ll want to preserve the borderless art’s integrity, as it’s a large portion of the card’s appeal.
- Deck considerations: If you keep a display binder, add a label about this card’s borderless foil status to help others understand its collectibility and why it’s worth the extra shelf-space.
