## Unboxing and box art reveal
### Box art and presentation
The box art is a bright, punchy affair, with Edouard’s likeness front and center, flanked by zombies that seem to be whispering, "Please don’t paint us like that." The back of the box promises a few guest-themed missions and perhaps a nod to Edouard’s own design sensibilities. The packaging feels premium, with reinforced corners and thick cardboard that makes you feel like you’re unboxing a collector’s item rather than a subscription box for coffee.

### Contents list — what you actually get
Inside, you’ll find the usual Zombicide components: cards, tokens, dice, and a board that folds into a compact, soccer-field-friendly footprint. But in this Special Guest edition, there’s a special guest sheet card featuring Edouard, a new character with unique abilities, and a few mission scenarios designed around his playstyle. We’ll get into balance later, but the quick tease: Edouard brings a little extra strategy spice to the table.

### Miniatures and sculpts
The miniatures are where CMON often shines, and this edition is no exception. Edouard’s figure is the star, a character mini with a confident stride and a brain-as-weapon vibe that matches his real-world reputation for thoughtful design. The zombies are standard CMON quality — clean lines, good poses, and a sense of menace that telegraphs well on the tabletop. If you’ve handled the regular Zombicide range, the Special Guest set slots in as a natural upgrade rather than a complete divergence.

### Ask the experts? No, just edges
One thing you notice right away is the edge craftsmanship: matte cards with crisp typography, resilient tokens that survive the occasional slam of a tabletop, and a rulebook that doesn’t assume you’ve memorized every CMON product you’ve ever touched. But the real question is: does Edouard’s cameo alter the fundamental Zombicide experience, or is it just a stylish afterthought?

### Board layout and scenarios
Zombicide boards are iconic for their modular tiles. In this edition, the scenario design leans into Edouard’s personality: more planning, a touch of humanitarian crisis on the rooftops, and a little puzzle-solving to unlock certain narrative beats. If you’re a fan of the base game’s cookie-cutter run-and-gun approach, this edition will surprise you with a few well-placed puzzle moments that slow things down in a satisfying way — as long as you’re not hoping for a pure bullet-hell session every time.

### Gameplay depth and balance
Edouard’s special abilities add a layer of tactical nuance that can feel like a “boss battle” in miniature form. The new character comes with unique actions that encourage you to think a step or two ahead, which is a refreshing change from the standard zombie sprint mechanic. However, balance is the tricky part: if the opponent spawn rate remains aggressive, the new rules risk becoming a gimmick rather than a meaningful variation. We tested a handful of scenarios, and the results were encouraging, with Edouard’s kit providing viable paths to victory without bending the core game beyond recognition.

### Rulebook notes and clarity
If you’ve spent any time with CMON’s rulebooks, you know the style: dense, peppered with icons, and occasionally cryptic unless you cross-reference a few supplements. The Special Guest Edouard edition keeps the language approachable but still expects you to be comfortable with the zoning systems and action economy that CMON games adore. The quick-start guide included in the box is a godsend for players who are new to Zombicide or who want to run a one-off session with minimal planning.

### Artwork and production quality
The production quality deserves a full paragraph of praise. CMON’s art direction is strong, with Edouard’s portrait capturing the calm, almost professor-like vibe the guest embodies. The color palette is consistent with CMON’s current range, with moody, underground blues and sickly greens for the zombie hordes that feel cohesive rather than slapped-on. The tokens are sturdy, the boards lay flat, and the card stock is thick enough to survive repeated play sessions without showing wear in the areas that most matter (the action icons, the health bars, and the mission goals).

### Would you get this if you already own Zombicide?
If you’re collecting Edouard Guiton’s appearances or you simply enjoy the variation in mission design, the Special Guest edition is a nice-to-have. If you’re a casual gamer who loves zombies with a strong narrative tilt, you’ll likely find that the guest’s presence nudges the experience into something closer to an adventure than a pure “kill everything” session. For completionists, this is a must; for casual players, it’s a convenient way to refresh a game they already love.

### Price and value
Pricing for CMON products fluctuates, especially for special guest editions. The box you’re looking at today sits at a premium tier, reflecting the guest concept, the new cards, and the additional narrative content. The question is not simply “can I afford it?” but “does this add enough meaningful content to justify the cost?” Our take: if you adore Edouard Guiton’s design sensibility and you’re the type who wants a new narrative arc every few months, the answer is yes, especially if you’re already invested in Zombicide as a long-term gaming project.

### Comparison with standard Zombicide
In standard Zombicide, you get a reliable zombie-slaying engine: chaotic, fast-paced, and a little punishing in the right dose. The Special Guest Edouard edition preserves the core, but with Edouard’s heuristics, it adds a layer of planning and strategic choice to the early rounds. The difficulty curve remains approachable for veteran players who enjoy a mental workout but could be a touch stiff for newcomers. The hero/guest mechanic introduces a new lens: a designer’s touch on a beloved game. It’s not a radical departure, but it’s a noticeable difference that can please players who crave closer moments of decision making.

### Table talk: clans, fandom, and the guest vibe
If there’s a recurring topic in our community threads, it’s whether special guests add flavor without overshadowing the game’s foundations. ### A note on accessories and paintability
One feature fans appreciate in CMON products is paintability. The Special Guest Edouard minis are clean enough to be painted at a reasonable speed, but not so intricate that you’ll need a full dedicated painting night. If you’re a weekend painter, you’ll be happy; if you’re a pro-painter, you’ll likely see it as a charming project that doesn’t demand the entire hobby room. The other miniatures in the set respond well to standard basing, dry brushing, and a wash or two to bring them to life.

### Player experience and how it plays out in practice
What matters most in a board game is how the experience flows at the table. If you’ve played enough Zombicide to know when the dice gods are going to misbehave, you’ll appreciate the added design caution in this edition. The guest scenario sequences encourage a more deliberate approach to exploration and escape, which is a breath of fresh air in a series that can feel relentlessly breezy in its standard mode. The pacing becomes a dance: you’ll want to take a moment to study the map, plan a route, and then commit to a daring move that pays off in the next round.

### Session length and replayability
With the new scenarios included in the Special Guest edition, replayability is improved by a margin. You’ll likely get three to five distinct sessions out of the box, if you’re the kind who likes to rotate through guests and keep the narrative momentum going. If you’re a single-session gamer, you might appreciate the bite-sized, guest-inspired arcs that don’t require a long campaign to deliver satisfaction.

### Durability and long-term shelf life
CMON tends to make durable components, and this edition stands up to regular play. The board is robust, the cards can take a few scrapes if you aren’t vigilant, and the dice are consistent. If you’re a neat freak who likes to keep your gaming space pristine, you’ll be pleased with the packaging that protects the pieces during storage and transport.

### User experience and accessibility
There’s a lot to like for first-time Zombicide players, but the Special Guest edition is best enjoyed by those who already know the base game. The rule density isn’t insurmountable, but it’s not the simplest starter experience. That’s the nature of a product that tries to be both a standalone game and a modular expansion at the same time. In short, this is a game for players who love a little crunchy design and a dash of narrative fireworks.

### Final recommendation
If you’re a Zombicide devotee who collects special editions, or if Edouard Guiton’s design fingerprints excite you, the Special Guest edition is an appealing add-on that pays homage to the CMON ethos of pushing a familiar skeleton into more ambitious shapes. It doesn’t reinvent Zombicide, but it does reimagine a handful of sessions with a designer’s perspective, and that can be a lot of fun when you’re in the mood for something a little more thoughtful than pure sprint mechanics. For players who only occasionally reach for Zombicide, you’ll probably be happier sticking to the base game or a standard expansion, because the guest content requires time and willingness to engage with the new mechanics.
