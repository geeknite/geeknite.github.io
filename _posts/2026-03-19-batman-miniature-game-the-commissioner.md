## Overview
The Commissioner is a figure in Batman Miniature Game that focuses on leadership and tactical support rather than raw brawling. He offers command actions and buffs that help nearby models operate more efficiently. He is best used as the steadying hand that keeps your list cohesive in Gotham.

### Role on the table
On the board the Commissioner tends to sit at mid range, delivering orders to a squad of cops or technicians. He does not normally charge into combat, yet his presence improves activation efficiency and line of sight planning for the rest of the force.

## Unboxing and Components
The miniature comes in a standard Knight Models blister. The sculpt has clean lines, a badge detail on the chest, a trench coat that reads well under heavy wash, and a hat that suggests head of city operations. The kit includes the base, a stat card, a couple of tokens, and a small scenario card that covers one or two official rules.

### Painting guidance
Begin with a base coat of deep navy, then apply a midtone wash into the folds. Dry brush the edges for sharp definition, and finish with a bright badge and subtle highlights on the tie. A touch of gloss on the badge makes it read as metal in photos. For a gritty Gotham vibe, add a little grime to the coat cuffs and shoes.

## The Commissioner on the table
### Abilities and playstyle
The core value of the Commissioner is leadership and command actions. He grants nearby models extra activations or improved timing in a turn, which helps your skirmish line maintain cohesion. His presence works best when you set up a protected lane where a squad can exploit a buffered turn or two to move into a better position. Do not rely on him for heavy melee; his best tricks are about timing and control.

### List building and synergy
Pair the Commissioner with a mix of cops and a couple of detectives or medics. The combination creates a small but durable force that can hold a zone while Batman or Nightwing engages from a distance. The leadership tokens help your team act more efficiently when the enemy is trying to force a breach.

## Painting and modeling notes
Piece by piece, the Commissioner shines on the tabletop when you spin up a narrative scene. The badge reads well, the trench coat is dramatic, and the overall silhouette says field leader rather than street enforcer. Use a base that hints at a rooftop or street scene to help fix the mood.

### Alternate pose ideas
If you want to push your Commissioner toward a more cinematic moment, consider swapping the head or arm to simulate him giving orders or pointing toward a threat. Simple conversions can add a lot of personality without breaking the model or the budget.

## Narrative and atmosphere
The Commissioner adds a human dimension to Gotham warfare. The city is not only about heroes and villains; it is a stage for municipal governance under duress. The figure can be used to frame small stories about restraint, procedure, and discipline in a law enforcement response to crime.

## Rules in practice
### Early game
Start by securing a safe route for your specialists. Use the Commissioner to place a buffer zone and keep your key threats within suitable range for your heavy hitters to react.

### Mid game
Push for control of a central objective while your officers hold a line. The command actions should be used to ensure that your upgrades or additional units can come online without exposing the entire formation to risk.

### Late game
When the enemy tries a last push, the Commissioner can orchestrate a final defense by shoring up your flank and keeping the squad alive for one or two more activations. This is where the leadership payoff tends to peak.

## Value and alternatives
The Commissioner sits in the mid tier for cost and utility. He excels in leadership roles and in missions that reward careful planning. If your playstyle leans toward patient, thinky warfare, he is likely to pay off. If you prefer a punch up, look at a stronger frontline leader or a hero determined to go toe to toe with the criminals.

## Community feedback and closing thoughts

If you play Gotham as a city where there is a plan beyond the next roll of the dice, you will likely enjoy The Commissioner.
