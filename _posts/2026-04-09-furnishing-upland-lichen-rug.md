## Cleaning and Maintenance
### Everyday upkeep
The rug responds well to regular vacuuming. Its fibers resist tangling and shedding, which means you will not find yourself manually detangling a nest of threads every other week. For spills, blotting is recommended rather than aggressive scrubbing, which could disrupt the pattern. The goal is to maintain the look of a mossy patch rather than erode into a color riot.
### Stain handling and accidents
For stains, a mild detergent solution works well. It is best to test a small inconspicuous area first, because even forest floors get irritated by misuse. If you have a pet with an accidental scent story to tell, you may want to consider a carpet cleaner or a professional service after a heavy party night. Overall, the rug performs like a decent field shirt: practical, not perfect, and forgiving enough to survive the occasional spillage that nature itself would politely overlook.
### Longevity and care cycles
With proper care, the rug should maintain its look for years. It is not indestructible, but it is designed to age gracefully rather than crumble into a pile of fibers that look suspiciously like a decayed log. The key is to rotate the rug occasionally, avoiding prolonged sun exposure on one patch, and giving the fibers a little shake now and then to reset the pile. In our experience, the rug handles daily life with the resilience you would expect from a product designed to echo nature without requiring you to become a forest ranger.

## Frequently Asked Questions
Q: Is the upland lichen rug suitable for all floor types?
A: It performs well on most hard surfaces and low pile carpets. On very shaggy or plush carpets, you might want to consider a larger rug underlay to prevent bunching.
Q: Can you machine wash this rug?
A: No, machine washing is generally not recommended. A light vacuum and spot cleaning are the suggested routine to maintain the pattern and fibers.
Q: Does the rug shed a lot?
A: It sheds less than you might fear for a patterned rug, especially after the initial unrolling. A quick vacuum cycle after installation helps set the fibers in place.
Q: How does it react to sunlight?
A: Prolonged direct sunlight can fade the greens over time. If the rug sits near a sunlit window, consider rotating it every few months to preserve color balance.
