## Care, Cleaning, and Everyday Maintenance
### Cleaning Basics
Wool rugs benefit from gentle care. Regular vacuuming with a brush attachment helps lift surface dust without breaking the fiber’s natural spring. We recommend a light touch—think cat-nap rather than a full-on vacuum rampage. For small spills, blot gently with a clean cloth and avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper. If you’re a coffee-enthusiast and deep into late-night caffeine-fueled gaming, keep a stash of stain-removal solution suitable for wool, tested in a discreet corner first. Always refer to care instructions on the rug’s tag and, if in doubt, call a pro.

### Stain Management
Wine spills? Ketchup? The heroic heck of life? Act quickly. Blot, don’t rub, and if you have a wool-safe spot cleaner, test it on a hidden edge first. Wool has natural oils that resist some stuff, but not all. The sooner you nip stains in the bud, the less heroic your cleaning saga will be. For tough stains, combinations of club soda and mild detergent can help lift the mark without collapsing fiber resilience—but again, test first. The goal is to keep the texture crisp and the color true.

### Maintenance Rituals
Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear—particularly if you have a high-traffic gaming chair or a desk stool that enjoys rolling. A quick fluff with your vacuum’s low-suction setting can restore some bounce after long sessions. Consider a rug pad beneath for extra cushioning and to reduce slippage for those dramatic stand-up moments when you realize the boss is about to respawn in your screen. A pad improves longevity and keeps the rug from sliding away from you during heated table flips.

### Eco-Factor and Longevity
Wool is a renewable resource and has a smaller carbon profile than many synthetic fibers when responsibly sourced. It’s also naturally flame-resistant, which means your cozy streaming nook gets an extra layer of safety without you having to install metal barriers around the coffee table. A wool rug like this tends to age gracefully, developing a patina that reads as “seasoned gamer” rather than “tired room.” If you’re worried about pilling, wool does pill, but with proper care and occasional gentle brushing, you can minimize it and keep the surface looking sharp.
