## Weather resistance and maintenance schedule
Outdoor furniture is a commitment. It’s not a throw pillow—it's more like a plant you’re allowed to sit on. The key to longevity is consistent care.
- Seasonal cleaning: wipe down the rattan with a damp cloth, check for loose screws, and inspect the cushion zippers for fabric wear. A gentle soap solution can help remove grime without breaking down the weave.
- Storage: if you live in a climate with heavy winter weather, consider storing cushions indoors or under a protective cover. While the set is designed to handle sun and rain, prolonged exposure to freezing temperatures can be hard on fabrics and the wood’s moisture content.
- Quick care: for red wine or coffee spills, blot (don’t rub) and use a mild fabric cleaner after removing cushion covers. Dry thoroughly before reassembling. Remember, tiny messes today become epic tales of “I spill again” tomorrow if you don’t take care of them properly.

Materials-wise, Acacia wood weathers gracefully when oiled or sealed occasionally. If you’re in an area with heavy rain or humidity, you might want to re-seal the wood every year or so to keep color consistency and avoid water peeking into any microscopic cracks. It’s not a big pain, just a small ritual that makes you feel like a responsible homeowner instead of a casual lawn ornament.
