## What is Exploding Kittens in 2026?
Exploding Kittens began as a card game designed by Elan Lee and Matt Inman, with a quirky sense of humor that leans hard into meme culture and cat-derived peril. The objective is simple: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten card, or defuse it using a defuse card or clever play. The deck is a mix of cute kittens, bizarre cat-people, and a laundry list of “bombs” that create a domino effect of near-misses and dramatic reveals. The style is not just about luck; it’s about timing, misdirection, and reading the room. A handful of defuses can ruin the dramatic tension you built up, and that, dear reader, is where the game becomes deliciously chaotic.

The brand-new sealed edition promises pristine cardboard, crisp art, and the authenticity of a product straight from the factory line. The box might boast a few splashy cat-laser eyes on the cover, but the real magic is in how the game transforms a quiet evening into a laughing, talking, finger-snapping competition where the winner likely owes the losing players a round of snacks.

## How to play in 10 steps (summary)
Exploding Kittens is a take-that card game with a few moving parts. Here’s the gist to help you decide if you want to dive deeper:
1) Shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand. You’ll be balancing the need to draw with the risk that your next card could end the game for you. 
2) Keep Defuse cards handy. They are your safety net, and you’ll want them when the inevitability of a kitten explosion lands on your lap. 
3) On your turn, you may play as many action cards as you like, then draw one card to end your turn. 
4) The Exploding Kitten card ends your run—unless you’ve saved a Defuse, or have a hero card that mocks the fates and lets you cheat the rules in a moment of chaos. 
5) Defuses are flexible; if you can’t use them on your own Exploding Kitten, you could attempt to use one to ruin someone else’s draw with a little misdirection. 
6) The game ends when only one person remains with their head above water and not staring into the void of a bomb-free horizon. 
7) Courage, deception, and a plan to redirect the deck’s chaos will carry you further than brute luck. 
8) House rules are common: you can adjust draw counts, insert extra Defuse cards, or create so-called “friendly fire” rounds to shake things up. 
9) Expand with expansion packs if you want bigger nights and more cat-tastrophes. 
10) Most nights, this equals a quick 15- to 20-minute sprint. And that means you’ll probably play again—immediately.

Note: The simplicity is the charm. If you’re exploring with younger players, you’ll want to be mindful of some of the card art’s darker humor, which can be a run-time giggle or a minor eye-roll, depending on your group’s vibe. The baseline experience remains straightforward: you’re trying not to explode, you’re using clever defusing devices, and you’re laughing at dicey plays that go off the rails.
