## How Monopoly Deal Works (A Quick Refresher)

If you’re entirely new to Monopoly Deal, here’s a concise refresher. The game is built around collecting sets of property cards. Each player starts with a hand of six cards and a turn consists of drawing two cards, then performing up to three actions. Actions can include playing a property card to your collection, playing a rent card to charge opponents for rent, or using debt/steal/wild cards to disrupt other players’ plans. When someone collects a complete color set (e.g., all the green houses), they win the round. In the Ultimate Bundle, you’ll find more ways to push someone toward that victory, with additional action cards and sometimes even different scoring variants depending on the module you’re running.

### Card Types in the Bundle
- Property cards: The core build assets. They come in colors that match sets; some cards are rainbow-property or multi-color, offering flexible options.
- Rent cards: The lifeblood for collecting money from opponents. They come in standard and variant versions that affect how the rent is charged or how you can dodge it.
- Action cards: The bombs and boons of the game. Play them to steal, swap, or force more deals. They’re the spice, and sometimes the pepper spray, of your turn. 
- Debt cards: A mirror of chaos, these cards force opponents to pay up, often in ways that hurt your own pipeline if misused.
- Wild cards: Flexible, chaotic, and magnificent. They act as either a property or a rent card depending on what the table needs in that moment.
- House and Hotel tokens: These step up the prestige of your property portfolio and add a physical layer to the game’s tension.

### Scoring and Win Condition
The standard win condition is to collect a complete property set of any color. In the Ultimate Bundle, there are optional modes and expansions that tweak the number of sets required, the pace of play, and the way you can score points or steal victory away from the table. The mathematics of “who owes whom how much” is part of the charm and the occasional headache, but this is where the social manipulation shines. You’ll often hear players whispering, “Just one more turn,” while counting how many wilds they have left in the deck. And yes, a bold but honest truth: in a room full of gamers, someone will try to bluff their way into a win—or bluff you into shuffling a card you didn’t want to see again.
