## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

Cotton is relatively easy to care for, but hand-woven textiles always benefit from a gentle approach. Here is a practical guide to keeping your rug looking fresh without turning it into a science experiment:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting. Avoid aggressive beater bars that can loosen fibers over time.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fading, especially if sunlight hits one side more than the other.
- If a spill occurs, blot gently with a clean cloth rather than rubbing. Blotting helps prevent the stain from migrating and weaving its way into the fibers.
- For stains that won’t quit, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in cotton and hand-woven textiles. Do not try to toss this rug in the washing machine unless you enjoy the laundry version of risky business.
- Keep the rug out of direct sunlight for extended periods to minimize color fading. If you must place it in a sunlit area, consider rotating it more frequently or using UV-blocking curtains during peak sun hours.

## Buyer’s Guide and Comparisons

If you are shopping for a hand-woven cotton dhurrie with earth tones, there are several factors to compare across options: size, weave density, border complexity, and the tonal range of blues and browns. A border that feels crisp and well-defined is generally a sign of careful weaving; if the border looks wobbly or uneven, that could indicate a looser weave or less attention to finishing details. Consider how the rug will interact with your lighting throughout the day. Natural daylight can make the blue appear cooler, while warm indoor lighting can soften the blue and bring the earth tones forward.

When comparing similar rugs, check the weave density and pile height. A denser weave typically means better durability and a more uniform surface. A slightly lower pile height is easier to clean and can be more forgiving in high-traffic areas. If you have pets, a mid-to-high density weave and a tight, compact knot pattern will be more resistant to snagging. Always compare the edge finishing; neat, reinforced edges prevent fraying and extend the life of the rug.
