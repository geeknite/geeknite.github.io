## How to Play This Deck Properly
### UNO Rules Revisited with a Hero Twist
The default UNO rules apply: match color or number, stack on draw twos, and push a Wild Draw Four if your mood is to cause some scandal at game night. The MHA twist adds two meaningful, non-disruptive adjustments:
- Quirk Cards: When you play a Quirk card, you trigger a temporary effect inspired by MHA quirks, such as forcing the next player to skip a turn, or reversing the play order with a little hero swing.
- Hero Cards: These cards can be used as Wilds in addition to their hero-themed actions, giving players an opportunity to declare a color that matches their favorite UA character’s theme.

The result is UNO that feels slightly more theatrical—like a board game that decided to audition for a stage production. It\'s still UNO at heart, but the crowd goes wild when a Hero Card hits the table and suddenly your dog-eared strategy sheet looks less like a plan and more like a storyboard.

### House Rules: The Geeknite Edit
If your group likes extra chaos, consider these optional house rules:
- Hero Rally: If a player gets down to one card, all players chant a quick, silly hero line (think \'Plus Ultra!\' or your own variant). It adds drama, not rule confusion.
- Quirk Combos: If two players simultaneously play Quirk cards, the next player must declare a hero trait before playing (e.g., \'I\'m going with resourceful and brave\'). It\'s silly, but it keeps players on their toes.
- Promo-Only Shuffles: When shuffling, a player with two or more Hero Cards must shuffle with flair—one-handed spins, or an air punch. It keeps the energy high and the table a little warmer.

### The Experience: Casual Play vs. Tournament Mode
For casual game nights, the deck shines because it breaks up routine UNO sessions with bright visuals and small but meaningful tweaks.
