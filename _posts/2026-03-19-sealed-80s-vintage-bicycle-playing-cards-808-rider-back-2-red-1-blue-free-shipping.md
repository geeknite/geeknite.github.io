## Maintenance, display, and storage tips

- Keep it sealed: If your goal is display rather than use, keep the packaging intact to preserve the provenance. The moment you open a sealed deck, you alter its status from “time capsule” to “use artifact,” which is a valid path for some collectors but not all.
- Temperature and humidity: Store in a cool, dry place. Vintage stock is less forgiving of humidity fluctuations than modern synthetic stock, so avoid basements and hot attics that can flex the cardboard over time.
- Handling: When you do handle sealed decks, use clean hands. If you plan to test one deck, consider doing so with a single red or blue deck so you preserve at least one deck’s sealed status for display. If you’re a magician who wants to actually perform with them, you may practice shuffles on the non-sealed or a test deck to avoid affecting your display copy.
- Display ideas: A small acrylic case or a shadow box with the tri-pack displayed open or closed offers a nice narrative of “collectible art meets street magic.” You can position the color-coded decks to evoke a color story in your room or studio while keeping the third deck as backup for quiet practice.
