## Overview

### What is City of Mist Core Book?
City of Mist is an urban fantasy detective RPG where players are ordinary humans who discover they are more than human—each one wearing a Mask that taps into a mythic truth. The world is a modern city overshadowed by legends, conspiracies, and the constant tension between who you appear to be and who you truly are. The core book is the rulebook, the setting guide, and the toolkit rolled into one glossy, neon-drenched package. It provides a flexible system designed to support focused mystery play, character-driven drama, and high-energy action without forcing you into the corners of any single genre.

The book’s backbone is a systems approach designed to keep momentum and storytelling at the forefront. It’s not about stacking bonuses or crunch for the sake of crunch; it’s about enabling dramatic questions, moral choices, and the moment when a hero realizes their power doesn’t only save people, it reveals another layer of the city’s mythic fabric. The City of Mist core book invites you to treat the city as a character—bright enough to glare at you in the night, shadowy enough to conceal a thousand secrets, and patient enough to let your group shape its myths in real time.

> For fans of noir, urban fantasy, and character-driven storytelling, the core book is the red-lipped invitation you didn’t know you were waiting for.

### The Theme and Tone
City of Mist thrives on mythic realism. It strips gods and monsters down to intimate, human-scale stakes. The Masks—your public persona—give you a lens to interpret your mythic power in everyday life. Want to be a detective who channels the trickster archetype? You can. Would you rather be an artist haunted by a god of storms? Also possible. The book is not shy about letting you juggle light and shade: your hero can do something spectacular, but there’s always a price, a consequence, or a mystery that echoes through the next scene. The tone can swing from wry humor to earnest tragedy in a page flip, and that tonal flexibility is one of the book’s strongest selling points.

The city itself breathes with lore. The book provides a scaffold for building a setting that feels lived-in rather than handwaved. It’s the kind of game where you can headline a caper about a subway heist and a legend who calls themselves the “Mother of Signals” in the same session, and your players will insist you go for round two because you left a thread dangling about a mysterious symbol etched into a brick wall near a coffee shop.

## System and Mechanics (High-Level, No Spreadsheet Spills)

City of Mist uses a modular, story-forward system that emphasizes narrative agency over mechanical bloat. The core rules are designed to support collaboration and brevity, especially when you’re running a session that hinges on dialogue, clues, and moments of revelation. Rather than bog down players with a hundred tactical options, the book leans into a pair of crisp dice-based mechanics that reward creativity and risk.

- Dice and Resolution: Players roll a pool of dice to determine the success of their actions. The goal is not merely “succeed” or “fail” but to generate narrative momentum—ties, edging toward a reveal, or a twist that propels the story into the next beat. The outcomes are designed to be evocative: a successful action can still carry costs, while a messy success can open doors to interesting complications.
- The Masks: Masks are more than cosmetic gimmicks. They define how a character presents themselves to the world, what myths resonate with their identity, and what vulnerabilities bite in the wake of using power. Each Mask has a built-in concept toolkit—hooks for roleplay, a few signature traits, and specific avenues for power or influence. The Masks are both character identity and plot levers, weaving a deterministic thread into a very flexible narrative fabric.
- Powers and Mythic Truths: The Power is the engine that drives action; the Mythic Truths are the city’s myths realized in a human story. This pairing keeps every moment meaningful—an action isn’t just a checkmark on a table; it is a story beat that resonates with the myth the character embodies. The core book offers a menu of power concepts that can be paired with different Masks to create dozens, if not hundreds, of unique characters.
- Clues, Climax, and Conflicts: The book emphasizes structured mystery play. Clues are not simply “items” but narrative breadcrumbs that push toward a culminating conflict. Conflicts are designed to scale with the hero’s Masks, allowing intimate personal stakes to intersect with public-city dangers in satisfying ways.

In short, City of Mist aims to be a streamlined engine for storytelling with a strong thematic spine. It doesn’t pretend to be a war-game or a calculus-heavy RPG; it’s a toolset for crafting mythic personal sagas within the fog of a living, breathing city.

## Characters: Masks, Personalities, and the Art of the Reveal

### Masks as Character Design
Masks are the core personality conceits of your character. They are what your hero chooses to wear to the world each day, their public face that helps them blend into the urban crowd or stand out like a neon beacon. Each Mask carries a flavor of myth and a practical avenue for play: what kind of power they draw on, how they approach problems, and how the city sees them. The core book provides a handful of example Masks, and the rules enable players to craft new ones with relative ease. The result is a roster that can feel mythic without ever tipping into cliche.

The best Masks feel like characters you could meet in a late-night diner. They aren’t perfect heroes; they are imperfect people grappling with the moral fog of a city that has opinions about legends. The Masks also serve as a storytelling contract: what the character wants and how their myth intersects with reality become the engine of scene framing. Expect moments where a Mask’s power flips from a boon to a temptation, or where the hero’s public image clashes with a private truth that refuses to stay buried.

### Powers, Distinctions, and the Human Scale
Power in City of Mist is not a scoreboard; it’s a narrative instrument. You don’t simply add a number; you add a way to describe your character’s intervention in the world. The book provides a palette of power concepts—elements drawn from myth that translate into modern abilities. When you pair these powers with Masks, you get a distinctive voice for your hero: the way they affect the city, the way the city affects them, and the cost of their legend.

Distinctions are the micro-beats of a scene. They’re not just mechanical buffs; they’re prompts for roleplay. What does your Mask think about power? How does their daily life conflict with the myth they embody? The core book incentivizes players to develop these distinctions through play, rewarding thoughtful characterization with narrative traction.

### Supporting Cast and Relationships
No detective saga survives on a lone hero. City of Mist emphasizes relationships—mentors, rivals, confidants, and city factions. The core book gives you tools to sketch the social web around your Mask, which is where a lot of the city’s flavor hides. You’ll build allies who see through facades, enemies who know your true name, and shady organizations who want to plant their symbols in the city’s skull of neon. This social layer isn’t optional—it’s a design feature that turns a single mystery into an ongoing web of consequences.

## The City: Tone, Setting, and World-Building

The city in City of Mist is more than a backdrop; it’s the engine that powers the truth behind every Mask. The neon glow, rain-slick streets, and piano bar secrets create a cinematic vibe that makes you feel like you’re starring in a modern mythic noir. The core book offers guidance for building your own city or adapting a real-world metropolis into the City of Mist. It provides mapping tools, tone-setting advice, and encounter seeds that can unlock sessions with minimal prep.

A strong strength of the book is its approach to mysteries as layered puzzles. The city’s myths aren’t abstract legends; they echo in street signs, underground clubs, and family lore. A single clue can feel mundane on the surface but bloom into a revelation that reframes the entire city. The core book’s design nudges groups toward collaborative world-building—players and GM shaping a shared mythos rather than passively consuming a pre-packaged story.

Layout-wise, the book delivers art that blends noir charm with mythic symbols. It’s not just pretty; it’s purposeful. The visual language helps you read a scene at a glance—colors signaling mood, iconography hinting at mythic themes, and callouts that remind players of their Masks’ ethical lines. The result is a book that invites you to open it with a sense of possibility rather than dread over page counts and rule interactions.

## Layout, Artwork, and Accessibility

Geeks love good layout, and City of Mist delivers. The core book uses a clean, modular layout with generous margins, sidebars for quick-reference rules, and artwork that sells the mood without becoming indulgent. The typography keeps readability high, and the use of color is deliberate—neon blues and pinks to evoke that late-night city glow, tempered by darker tones to remind you that legends have shadows.

Accessibility is a mixed bag in some indie RPGs, but City of Mist makes a reasonable effort here. The book provides clear character creation steps, a gentle ramp from basic concepts to more advanced play, and plenty of examples. That said, the system remains a narrative engine rather than a tactical toolkit. If you’re the kind of player who lives for stat sheets and hit tables, you’ll need to lean into the storytelling side of things and let the Masks and Mythic Truths carry the weight.

For new players, there’s a gentle onboarding path: start with one Mask, a single mystery, and a few practice clues. It’s an approachable way to dip your toes in the city’s lore without drowning in options. For veteran groups, the core book still offers enough optional rules and setting variants to keep campaigns fresh across a long arc of play.

## GM Tools, Adventure Seeds, and Campaign Design

The GM portion of City of Mist is where the book becomes a practical play aid rather than a theoretical treatise. It includes example mysteries, structured clues, and scene templates that help you stitch an arc together without resorting to click-bait adventure PDFs. The case-file approach makes sessions feel episodic but connected, which is perfect for groups that enjoy a series of interconnected mini-mysteries rather than one sprawling epic.

Campaign design in City of Mist benefits from its emphasis on theme and character arcs. Rather than forcing a single “boss fight” endgame, the book encourages you to build a city-wide Mystery that every Mask contributes to reveal or redefine. This keeps the investment high for players who care about their characters’ M asks and personal stakes. You can drift between street-level investigations and mythic confrontations with ease, and the pacing tools help you maintain momentum even when a scene slows to a conversation in a coffee shop or a heated exchange in a seedy club.

## Pros and Cons: What Works, What Not

Pros:
- Strong thematic focus that blends noir sensibility with mythic undercurrents.
- Flexible system that supports both mystery-driven and character-driven play.
- Masks-as-design-tools create memorable, roleplay-rich characters quickly.
- Beautiful layout and art that reinforce the tone without slowing down play.
- Practical GM tools and encounter seeds that help run sessions with minimal prep.

Cons:
- The resolution mechanics can feel abstract to players who prefer crunchy, tactical combat or precise numeric outcomes.
- Some readers may crave more explicit power-by-power progression paths, as the system rewards narrative movement over numeric scaling.
- The city-as-myth concept can require a collaborative world-building commitment; if your group isn’t into shared world-building, sessions may feel less anchored.

If you’re a GM who loves pulling threads from a neon-drenched tapestry and watching players tug at the yarn until a myth unfurls, City of Mist is likely to become a favorite. If you’re looking for a rigid, math-forward game with a tight combat loop, this isn’t your stop—at least not without transforming how you run it.

## Narrative Experience: Session Flow and Player Agency

The book does a commendable job of balancing player agency with a guided narrative structure. You’ll likely see entire sessions built around a single clue that grows into a sprawling truth about a city-wide scheme tied to a mythic origin story. The pacing advice is practical: begin with a strong premise, insert a few rising actions that force a character choice about their Mask’s public persona, and then pay off with a revelation that changes the rules of the city for everyone involved.

That approach rewards players who think in terms of character motivation and mythic symbolism. The Masks provide a lens through which action can feel meaningful, not just accomplished. You’ll often see players question whether a bold action aligns with who their Mask wants to be—this friction is where the game shines, because it keeps the noir mood intact while letting mythic forces complicate even the simplest pickpocket caper.

In practice, a City of Mist session can swing from a tense stake-out to a mind-bending revelation about a city-wide truth that reframes earlier scenes. The core book’s guidance helps the GM handle those tonal shifts gracefully, and the players usually rise to the challenge with improv that lands more often than not.

## Art interlude: The Visual Language of the City

Art direction matters in a game about myths in modern clothes, and City of Mist understands this instinctively. The interior art leans into neon-lit silhouettes, reflective surfaces, and mythic symbols that feel ancient yet current. This isn’t just decorative; it’s a storytelling tool. When you show a page with a symbol etched into a subway pillar, the players’ imaginations fill the room with possibility. When you drop an illustration of a character whose face remains half-hidden in shadow, you’re nudging players to ask, “What are they hiding, and why?” The book doesn’t just present a city—it invents a visual vocabulary for a world where legends wear trench coats.

Jekyll-and-Hyde scenes occur naturally here: public perception vs. private truth, the hero’s public Mask vs. their real vulnerabilities. The imagery reinforces that core tension, giving players a shared lexicon for “what this myth is doing to the city” in every scene.

- Teams who enjoy heavy roleplay and character-driven narratives.
- GMs who like a flexible setting with lots of opportunities for cooperative world-building.
