## Overview

### What is Codenames Duet?

Codenames Duet is a cooperative reimagining designed for two players. The core concept is the same as the original: you give one-word clues that connect multiple words on the table, and your partner guesses those words. The difference? Instead of two teams competing for points, the two of you work as a unit toward a shared victory. The grid is still 5x5, and the tension remains: a wrong choice can end your turn or even blow your cover in a worst-case scenario. The design is lean, the rules are approachable, and the pacing rewards careful communication.

For readers new to the Codename universe, think of it as a spy briefing that doesn’t require you to narrate your entire life story. The two-player format forces you to trust a partner and to articulate clues clearly enough for them to translate into meaningful guesses.

### How Gameplay Flows

#### The Core Loop

In each round, one player is the clue-giver and the other is the guesser. The clue-giver provides a one-word cue plus a number indicating how many words on the board relate to that clue. The guesser then selects words. The catch: you’re working toward a dual objective—covering your words while avoiding the assassin and your partner’s words. The goalkeeper here is the shared goal: if you flush enough of your colored words, you win; if you hit the assassin or too many of your partner’s words, you lose.

The game uses a single shared goal and a finite number of turns, making each clue feel like a tactical decision rather than a casual throw of the dice. It’s a thought experiment you’re performing in parallel with your teammate, which is what makes the game feel collaborative rather than combative.

#### The Clue Dilemma: Staying Sharp Together

The one-word clue is an art form here. Short, dense, and loaded with interpretation, clues require you to anticipate your partner’s thought processes. It’s less about cleverness than about empathy: can you predict how your partner will map a word to a meaning you had in mind? The tension of potentially hitting the assassin makes even the simplest clue feel high-stakes. It’s the kind of dynamic that makes you laugh, groan, and high-five your teammate when a risky move pays off.

### Strategies for Two

#### The Early-Clue Strategy
If you’re new to Duet, start by giving clues that reference common themes (colors, categories, or everyday objects) with conservative numbers. This builds trust and reduces the risk of a misstep on your first few rounds. The more you play, the more you’ll learn to calibrate a two-digit number for more ambitious reveals.

#### The Safe vs. Risky Tradeoff
Duet rewards balance. A conservative clue might lead to a slower but safer victory, while a daring clue can yield a rapid but risky win. The best players know when to push and when to pull back, like a chess player who’s also a linguist with a passion for puns.
