## Care and Maintenance: Cleaning, Rotation, and Longevity
Maintenance is where a rug either earns a standing ovation or a sad trombone sound. The Harlow Cove Cream Rug is straightforward to maintain. Vacuuming once or twice a week keeps dust from becoming a visible crust; rotating the rug every few months helps prevent uneven wear, especially in rooms with one constant sunbeam that makes a tiny desert of one corner.

Spot cleaning is your friend. For spills, blot the area with a clean, absorbent cloth or towel. Do not rub aggressively, as that can embed stains deeper into the fibers. For stubborn spots, a mild detergent solution (a few drops of dish soap in warm water) works well; test in a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability. Let the area dry completely before walking on it again to avoid matting or wrinkling the fibers. If you live in a climate with high humidity, ensure you dry the rug thoroughly after cleaning and avoid prolonged dampness that could encourage mildew or that suspicious look of a science project drying on the patio.

Some consumers swear by baking soda as a deodorizer; sprinkling a light layer and vacuumming it up later can help with mild odors. If you are dealing with heavy pet smells, you may need to use a dedicated pet stain remover or consult a professional carpet cleaner for a more thorough refresh. The key is to be proactive, not panicky, and to treat your rug like a living, breathing character in your home rather than a decorative prop that only exists for photos.
