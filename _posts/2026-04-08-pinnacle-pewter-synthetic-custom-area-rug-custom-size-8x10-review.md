## H2: Size Options: Custom Size vs 8x10

This is where the Pinnacle Pewter tries to be your home’s personal tailor. You can opt for a custom size—great for non-standard rooms or oddly-shaped meditation dens—or the ever-popular 8x10 for those who want a classic footprint that still leaves some floor visible for dramatic lighting and those dramatic overhead cat stares.

- Custom Size: Pros include precise fit, fewer awkward edges, and the satisfaction of bragging about your exact measurements to your in-laws. Cons may include longer lead times and slightly higher price, since you’re asking a rug to be as particular as your AVL playlist. If you’ve got a space that’s 9x6 or 7x11, this is your hero move. You’ll probably still want to confirm thickness and edge finishing to ensure your floor transition looks seamless.

It’s the safe option with a hint of elegance—enough to justify a coffee table, but not so much that your dog decides it’s the new throne room.

In practice, the decision usually comes down to space accuracy and how much you enjoy measuring. If you dread rulers and laser measures, the 8x10 is a solid bet. If you’re playing interior designer and enjoy the occasional spreadsheet, the custom size option makes you feel like you’re running your own boutique rug shop from the comfort of your living room.

When you’re deciding between sizes, a quick pro tip: lay out painter’s tape on the floor to visualize the rug’s footprint before you commit. It’s cheaper than returning a rug and pretending it was “the dog’s plan all along.”

## H2: Cleaning and Maintenance — Keeping It Fresh

- Regular vacuuming with a light touch keeps dust and grit from embedding. Avoid aggressive vacuum settings that can snag fibers over time.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing as it can push stains deeper.
- For tougher spots, a mild detergent solution (water + gentle soap) works, followed by thorough rinsing to remove soap residue.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear, especially in high-traffic zones.
- A quick damp cloth wipe on the back can help if the rug has a tendency to shed a little; if shedding persists, you might be dealing with a short-term season of “new rug fuzzies” which should settle after a few cleanings.
