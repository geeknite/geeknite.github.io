## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Cleaning basics
Vacuum regularly on a low setting, preferably with a beater bar turned off or on the gentle mode to preserve the pile. Quick daily vacuuming helps prevent grit from wearing down the fibers over time. For spills, blot, don’t rub, and use a mild detergent solution if needed. Avoid strong chemicals that could alter color or fiber integrity. If you ever run into tough stains, treat promptly but lovingly; this rug is resilient, not invincible.

### Sun exposure and color retention
Ivory hues tend to show sun exposure more readily than darker tones. If your window line bakes in the afternoon sun, consider rotating the rug periodically to prevent uneven fading. A simple method is to rotate 180 degrees every few months so the central seating zone experiences light differently over time, keeping the color even and calm.

### Rotation and long-term care tips
Rotate every 3 to 6 months in high-traffic rooms to maintain wear balance. Fluff the fibers with a gentle hand after vacuuming to keep the pile looking plush rather than compressed. A professional clean can be considered every couple of years for deeper refreshment, especially if your space welcomes pets, kids, or a recurring climate where humidity shifts across seasons.
