### Grading and preservation tips
If you’re serious about preserving the card’s condition, here are practical tips that won’t require a scientific degree in card science:
- Sleeves first, then binder. Thin scratch-resistant sleeves help keep the surface pristine. If you want to go premium, consider UV-protected sleeves to slow down color fade from sun exposure.
- Backing matters. Use a sturdy binder with a decent number of pages so the card isn’t constantly flipping around like it just discovered instant ramen at 3 a.m.
- Temperature and humidity control. Cardboard hates humidity; a cool, dry environment keeps the edges crisp and reduces curling.
- Handle with care. It’s a cliche, but true: the fewer fingerprints and oils on the surface, the happier your collector heart.
