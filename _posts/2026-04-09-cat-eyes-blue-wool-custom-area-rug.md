## Materials, construction, and the long game
Wool has a reputation for dignity, durability, and the ability to resist turning into a felt sculpture when spilled-on. The Cat Eyes Blue Wool rug takes advantage of a wool blend or pure wool (depending on the batch) and uses a weave that's designed to resist crushing in the high-traffic zones of a modern home.

Key points to consider about wool rugs and care:
- Wool is inherently resilient, with natural elasticity that helps it bounce back after foot traffic.
- A tight weave reduces snagging on pet claws and helps your vacuum not eat the fibers. If you’ve ever wrestled with a flat-woven monstrosity that looks like it survived a small lava flow, you’ll appreciate the compact pile of this rug.
- Wool’s moisture management means fewer scent traps than a cotton rug, provided you don’t spill coffee, red wine, or your mom’s lasagna—though guidance suggests spot-cleaning rather than waiting for the apocalypse.

Care basics: vacuum with a brush attachment, rotate the rug every few months to even out wear, and avoid direct sun for extended periods to prevent fading. For spills, blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth. If your home has a pet with legendary shedding skills, that color will help conceal hair between cleanings and give your guests a quick sense that the rug is alive in a stylish, non-anthropomorphized way.

A note on dyes: blue dyes can vary, and the color you see online might shift in your lighting. If you’re chasing a specific hue (think midnight blue vs. sky blue), you might request a swatch before committing. The cat-eye motif can also come across as more or less pronounced depending on the dye lot and the fiber’s nap. ## Maintenance magic: keeping blue wool vibrant
The trick to preserving the blue wool’s luster over the years is simple but not glamorous: regular care, smart placement, and a little mindful restraint during cleaning sessions.

- Routine vacuuming: a gentle pass every week is enough in most rooms; you’re not fighting a dirt uprising, you’re managing a patina that evolves with your living space.
- Sun exposure: direct sunlight can fade the hue over time. If possible, position the rug away from large windows, or use sheer curtains to soften the beam. If your home enjoys a sunlit epic, consider rotating the rug to different windows across the seasons.
- Spot cleaning: act quickly on spills. Blot, don’t rub. For stubborn stains, test a small hidden patch with a mild wool-safe cleaner, then proceed if the test passes your internal quality check.
- Professional cleaning: every couple of years, or when the rug feels like it’s earned its war stories, a professional cleaning can refresh the fibers and bring back the original luster. If you’ve got a spot cleaner you trust, you can audition that first, but wool is finicky, so treat it with care.

One more pro-tip: rotate your rug 180 degrees every few months if you’re dealing with heavy traffic. The fiber orientation and the way your feet press down can create a subtle, pleasing texture variance that keeps the rug looking less like a static painting and more like a living piece of floor art.
