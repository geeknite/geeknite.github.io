### House rules and variants: keep it fresh without losing your mind
Part of the UNO charm is that you can bend the rules without breaking the game. The 112-card deck invites a few popular house rules that can spark more laughs and more shared guilt for bending the deck to your will:

- Speed rounds: every player gets a very short hand (three cards) and the goal is to empty faster than everyone else. Chaos, glory, and a sudden sense of strategic panic ensue.
- Stacking draws: if someone plays a Draw Two and the next player has another Draw Two, they stack; the total draw pile grows, and everyone gets dramatic about not revealing how many cards they hold.
- Wild color pick: when a Wild is played, the player choosing the color can also demand a silly challenge (name a movie star who matches the color or sing a quick jingle). Keeps the energy up and the table in stitches.

### How to store and care for your UNO deck
A good UNO deck deserves respect. Here are a few practical tips to keep the cards in good shape for years:

- Keep the deck in its original box or a slim organizer to prevent them from tangling with other games.
