## How to use this deck: practical tips for magic, cardistry, and gaming
- For cardistry: start with simple fans and slides to test how the gold foil interacts with light. You will likely notice a small amount of foil friction at the outset; with a little practice, you can minimize this and achieve clean, cinematic displays.
- For magic: consider using the Gold Edition for reveals where you want a moment of bling without going over the top. The foil and black background can make a single card pop during a color change or a transformation routine.
- For games: this deck is still a standard Bicycle deck at heart. You can use it in any card game, though you might want to reserve it for special rounds or to simply boost the mood of the evening. It is not designed to be the most economical choice for long sessions, but it excels in moments that call for style.
- Maintenance: keep the deck away from moisture and heat. The foil can be sensitive to humidity over long periods, and you want the tuck to stay pristine if you plan to store the deck for a show or photo shoot. A card guard or a clean, dry box is your friend here.

## Care and maintenance: keeping the glow intact
The key to preserving the Gold Edition is environment and handling discipline. Avoid exposure to direct sun for extended periods, as the foil can fade or dull with prolonged UV exposure. Use a cool, dry storage area and consider a display case if you own more than one deck from this line. If the deck binds or sticks after long storage, a gentle flood of air or a light fan across the surface can re-invigorate the stock, but avoid aggressive rubbing or forced opening to prevent edge creases on the foil portions. In short, treat the deck with respect, and it will reward you with a striking presence on the table.
