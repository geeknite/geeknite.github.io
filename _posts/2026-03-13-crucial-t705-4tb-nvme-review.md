## Thermal, Power, and Sustained Performance
Thermals matter more than you might think when you push a 4 TB NVMe to its limits. The T705’s thermal regime is friendly to most cases with ample airflow. In a typical mid-tower with a solid intake and exhaust, you’ll see robust sustained performance without hitting a thermal throttle. In compact builds or laptops with less aggressive cooling, you’ll want some form of passive cooling or a small heatsink where available. The last thing you want is stutters during a long render or a late-night gaming session that somehow turns into a slideshow.

Power draw is reasonable for a Gen4 NVMe. It isn’t a vacuous power-robbing goblin, but it isn’t a model of frugality either. Light usage sips power; heavy workloads demand more, as expected. The practical takeaway is simple: ensure good cooling and airflow, and the T705 will reward you with steadier performance and less thermal throttling.

## Compatibility and Installation
The T705 is an M.2 2280 drive compatible with most modern desktops and laptops that support PCIe Gen4 NVMe. On a Gen4 system, you’ll realize the full speed potential; on Gen3, you’ll still see a meaningful uplift over SATA-based storage, just not the peak Gen4 numbers.

Installation tips:
- Ensure you have an available M.2 slot and the PCIe lane configuration to maximize speed.
- Use OS cloning tools if you’re migrating your OS and data; or set up a fresh OS install and move data later.
- Consider enabling NVMe optimization in the BIOS for smoother operation.
- Maintain tidy cables and good airflow; a well-ventilated case yields better sustained performance and less thermal throttling.
