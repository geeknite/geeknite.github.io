## Compatibility and Installation: A Quick Guide

Before you buy, make sure the laptop supports DDR5 SODIMM and confirm the maximum memory per slot and total memory capacity. If your laptop has two SODIMM slots and you currently have 16GB or 8GB in each slot, you can add or replace modules to reach 32GB. If you currently have 32GB installed in a single module and a second slot open, you might upgrade by adding another 32GB to reach 64GB, if the laptop supports it. But many mainstream laptops cap total memory at 32GB or 64GB, depending on the model. Always verify.

To install:

1) Power down the laptop, unplug, and remove the battery if possible.
2) Open the service panel or bottom cover to access the memory slots.
3) Gently release the module latch if present, align the notch, and insert at a 45-degree angle, then press down until it clicks.
4) Return the cover and boot. Enter BIOS to verify the memory shows up in full.

If your laptop has only one slot and you currently have a smaller capacity, you’ll need to remove the existing module and replace with 32GB. If you’re lucky and you can pair with another 32GB stick in a two-slot system, you could reach 64GB total.
