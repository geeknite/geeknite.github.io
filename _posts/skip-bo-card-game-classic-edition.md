## The Basics

### What is Skip-Bo?

Skip-Bo is a classic card game that combines elements of luck, strategy, and a sprinkle of chaos. The objective is simple: be the first player to play all of the cards in your stockpile. Sounds easy, right? Well, prepare yourself for a wild ride as you navigate through your hand, discard piles, and the unpredictable Skip-Bo cards themselves. Think of it as Uno's mischievous cousin who likes to turn the tables - literally.

### Components

The game includes a deck of **162 cards** numbered 1 to 12, along with **18 Skip-Bo wild cards** that can substitute for any number. The instructions are so straightforward even your pet hamster could understand them (though teaching it to play might be a different challenge).

## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setting up Skip-Bo is a breeze. Each player is dealt a **stockpile** of 30 cards (20 for shorter games), and the remaining deck forms the draw pile. The goal is to create build piles in numerical order from 1 to 12 using cards from your hand, draw pile, and stockpile.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, players draw up to five cards from the draw pile and use them to create build piles. You can play cards from your hand, the draw pile, or the top card of your stockpile. The wild Skip-Bo cards can be used as any number, adding a twist of unpredictability. When you can't play any more cards, you discard one into your personal discard pile and pass the turn. The first player to empty their stockpile wins!

### Strategy and Fun Factor

While luck plays a role, strategy is key. Do you hold onto a Skip-Bo card for later or play it now to disrupt your opponent's flow? Decisions, decisions! The game offers a balance of tactical planning and spontaneous moves, making each round exciting and unique.
