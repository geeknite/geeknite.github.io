## How It Plays: Rules and Flow

### Core UNO Mechanics Refreshed

If you know UNO, you know the flow: match color or number, play action cards to shift the tempo, and scream UNO when you’re down to one card. The BTS Edition preserves that core: you can play a card that matches color or number, you can stack or play special action cards, and you still have to yell UNO when you have one card left. The BTS specific twist introduces a few new action cards inspired by the merch or the DMs that come with fandom energy, not by the neutral logic of the UNO core. The objective remains the same: get rid of all cards before your friends, ideally while keeping a straight face long enough to avoid suspicion.

### The Special Cards and Their Abilities

In addition to the standard UNO action cards, BTS Edition introduces a handful of branded actions. One or two cards trigger a mini BTS-themed interaction, like a small prompt to sing a line or to perform a quick dopamine-heavy reaction, if your group is into that kind of vibe. These additions are not intrusive; they are optional, giving the game a party-level flavor without forcing everyone to perform a karaoke challenge if they want to keep the game moving. The overall effect is to maintain pace while inviting a little social fun that aligns with BTS fandom. If your group is not into fan activities, you can simply ignore the prompts and use the underlying UNO mechanics as usual.

### Playing with Different Group Sizes

The BTS Edition scales nicely from a tight 3-player session to a larger party setting. In smaller groups, the rhythms are snappier, and the effect of BTS-card twists is felt more strongly because there are fewer players to soak up the chaos. In bigger groups, the energy becomes contagious, and the deck’s art and little card-splashes of fandom act as social lubricants. One potential caveat is the additional cards may slow down some rounds slightly due to the added prompts; this is not a problem if your crew enjoys a friendly, social gaming experience, but if you are aiming for a pure speed-run UNO vibe, you might feel the extra flavor cards a tad distracting.
