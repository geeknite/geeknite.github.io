## Handling, Grading, and Slabbing Considerations
### Grading Realities
If you are a purist who wants to push this card into a PSA, BGS, or SGC slab, you should be aware that the grade will hinge on the patch’s integrity, the autograph’s legibility, and the overall centering and surface condition of the card. The patch should be clean with sharp edges and no missing color blocks. The autograph should display a clean trace of ink without heavy bleeding. It’s not unusual for older ITG items to show some micro-scratches or edge wear, especially if the card has lived in a binder or a slightly too-tight toploader. A mint 9 or 9.5 grade could be a sweet spot for a card of this era, provided the patch and autographs aren’t compromised by handling, humidity, or sticky fingers from a snack mishap during a card night.

### Slabbing and Display Options
Display is a key factor for these pieces. If you choose to slab the card, a clear, well-protected slab will keep the patch color pop from fading and the autograph from smudging. If you prefer a more vintage vibe, you can opt for a custom display binder with acid-free sleeves and a label card that lists the patch colorway, serial info, and date of issue. The goal is not only to protect the card but to present it in a way that makes its story legible at a glance, because the story is a big part of the value here. In Geeknite’s opinion, a nicely designed display helps you tell the tale of a card that quietly traveled from a factory in 2012 to a shelf in your home, gathering dust and glory in equal measure.

### Storage Best Practices
Humidity is the silent villain of anything that uses paper, ink, and foil. Keep this card away from damp basements, hot attics, and the kind of sheet music that smells like 1980s lemonade stands. Store in a cool, dry place, ideally in a high-quality top loader and a semi-rigid card sleeve. If you are the kind of collector who rotates displays, consider swapping in a few other Bo Horvat cards to keep the vibe fresh and the rotation interesting. And yes, you can still geek out about a 24/24 patch auto while being perfectly practical about humidity control.

## Display, Care, and Nerdy Display Hacks
### Showmanship Matters
The display of a 24/24 patch auto should feel like presenting a trophy in a grand hall. The patch should be visible, not buried under a pile of other relics. If you’re using a binder, jot down a small note about the year and set so a future you can recall the moment you added the Horvat to your collection. ### Cleaning and Maintenance
Don’t overdo the cleaning. Card collecting is not meant to be a micro-scarab cleaning service. Wipe released dust with a dry microfiber cloth if needed and keep fingers off the surface. Oils from skin slowly degrade surface quality, so handle with care. If you’re particularly hands-on with tactile collecting, use cotton gloves for long sessions to protect the surface and the color fidelity of the patch and autograph.
