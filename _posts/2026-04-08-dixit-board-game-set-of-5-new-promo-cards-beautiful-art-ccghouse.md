## How to Use the Promo Cards in Your Dixit Games
### Quick Start: Add Them to the Deck
In most setups, you can slip the five promo cards into your regular Dixit deck and treat them as a standard subset. There are two common approaches:
- Integrated Promos: Mix the promo cards evenly with the regular Dixit cards. The round’s storyteller can choose from the whole deck, including promos, for their prompt.
- Promo-Only Rounds: Run a few rounds where the storyteller only uses the promo cards, with the rest of the deck kept for future rounds. This can yield a fresh rhythm and a slightly different balancing of risk vs. storytelling risk-taking.

Whichever route you pick, the key is to make sure everyone understands that the promo cards are part of the same “storytelling language” as the standard cards. The goal is to avoid confusion and promote creativity, not to spark a minor civil war about card legality.

### Crafting Prompts: Storytelling Techniques
The art aesthetics often lead to the following prompt styles:
- Abstract resonance: Let the card evoke a mood or feeling rather than a concrete image. The storyteller describes the sensation and relies on the voting audience to map their own experiences to the card.
- Thematic threads: Tie the card into a mini-theme that runs through the round (e.g., “dreams of travel,” “city after midnight,” or “forgotten toys”).
- Character-driven prompts: Build a micro-narrative around a figure or creature in the image. Even if the card is not a literal prompt, your description can give players a path to follow.

These tactics help keep the round dynamic. The promos are effective because they don’t dictate “the one thing you must say”—they encourage imaginative leaps, much like the core Dixit experience.

### Balancing with New Players
New players can find Dixit intimidating—the sheer volume of imagery can feel overwhelming. Promo cards with strong visual hooks can provide a bridge. The five cards in this set offer memorable anchors; their art tends to be vivid enough to spark quick associations, which is helpful for people who are still learning to translate visuals into words. For veteran players, they provide new texture and a fresh set of jumping-off points that can reinvigorate a long-running game night.

If you’re hosting a game night with mixed experience levels, consider a two-round “promo feature” to help newcomers warm up. One storyteller uses the promos; the rest of the table participates in a short explanation of how their prompts were formed. It’s a tiny investment in social glue that pays off with smoother rounds and more laughs.
