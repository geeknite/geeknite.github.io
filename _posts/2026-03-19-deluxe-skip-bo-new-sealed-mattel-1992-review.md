## How to play the core mechanics (refresh for new players)
Even a sealed Deluxe Skip-Bo is a delight to study for those who remember the rules but want to re-enter the rhythm of the game. Skip-Bo is a shedding-style card game where players try to be the first to empty their stock piles by playing numbered sequences in ascending order from 1 to 12. The catch is the wild nature of Skip-Bo cards themselves, which can act as wilds to fill in gaps or force a strategic pivot when you least expect it.

Here is a quick refresher without spoilers from a sealed artifact perspective:

- Each player draws five cards from a stock pile and uses a common discard pile to build sequences from 1 to 12. The first player to discard all their stock pile wins.
- Skip-Bo cards serve as wilds and can stand in for any number in a sequence. The trick is managing your wilds so that you can keep pushing forward rather than stalling on a stubborn card.
- On your turn, you can play from your hand to the building piles, or move cards from the draw pile to your hand to unlock new plays. The rhythm is deliberately simple but invites clever planning and misdirection.
- The game scales well for 2 to 6 players, which makes the Deluxe edition ideal for family nights and neighborhood game days alike. The more players, the more chaotic the sequences, the more potential for dramatic come-from-behind wins.

If you’re lucky enough to own a sealed 1992 Deluxe Skip-Bo, a few care tips will help preserve it for decades more:
