## How to play (quick rules and the adult twist)

If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll recognize the bones: players draw cards, play Defuse cards to avoid exploding, and use a variety of strategic, goofy cards to manipulate the deck and other players. The NSFW Edition keeps the core loop but adds a few spicy wrinkle cards that require you to decide whether to risk it or back away while someone yells something that could get you politely escorted from the table.

But let’s break it down step by step, with the giggles turned up to eleven:

1) Setup: Shuffle the Exploding Kitten deck with the Defuse cards. Deal a hand to each player (usually 7 cards for 2-5 players, though you can tweak). Remove enough Defuses so that each player has a fair chance to defuse if they draw the bomb later.
2) Turn order: The game proceeds in clockwise fashion, with each player drawing one card at end of their turn. On your turn, you can play as many cards as you want from your hand that are legal, finishing by drawing a card.
3) Defuse: If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you can use a Defuse card to save yourself. If you don’t have one or can’t defuse, you’re out. The Twists: NSFW-themed cards can stretch the rules in ridiculous ways: cards that force others to wear imaginary "adult-rated" blindfolds, or cause someone to swap hands with the person on their left, etc. The exact text is risky, but the gist is: the more cheeky the card, the more strategic chaos you’ll unleash.
4) Endgame: The last player standing wins. Yes, the rules are simple. The chaos is not.

Important note: In the NSFW Edition, some cards rely on innuendo, jokes that play on adult situations, and humor that may be lost on younger players. Use your best judgment about who you’re playing with. If your group is squeamish about mature content, you have two options: tone it down or pick a different table for tonight’s adventures.
