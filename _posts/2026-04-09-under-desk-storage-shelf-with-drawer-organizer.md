## Setup, Ergonomics, and Desk Compatibility

The product is designed to work with most desks that have a little underbelly space. The mounting area requires a certain clearance for legroom and kneading your chair into place. The good news is that the mounting hardware is forgiving; you won’t need a PhD in mechanical engineering to get this right. A simple eyeballing alignment followed by minor tweaks will get you there.

### Ergonomics and reachability

With the shelf under your desk, the items you use most—pens, sticky notes, USB adapters—are closer to your dominant hand than your monitor. It changes your workflow in subtle but real ways. When you don’t have to lean forward and rummage through a top desk drawer, your posture stays a little more upright, a little less stressed. This shelf works best with minimalist or modern desks, but it doesn’t scream “I only suit metal-and-wood aesthetics.” It plays nice with light wood tones, darker laminates, and even some painted surfaces. There are a few different flavors: metal rails with trays, hard-plastic modular boxes, or a combination unit with a hinged lid.
