## Overview

Zombicide: Black Plague is the medieval cousin of the zombie apocalypse. It's all giggles and gore until your survivors are knee-deep in undead and one of them forgets to reload and you realize your dice are the life support for your own obsession. The Wulfsburg expansion adds more urban-area tones to the mix: bigger maps, new zombies with cunning, and a few new survivors who immediately claim the throne of the table. The Feldherr Sortiereinsatz is a foam insert intended to slot into the Feldherr cases (and some other brands) to organize minis, tokens, cards, and dice more efficiently than your own brain can track your car keys.

In this review, we test not just if the foam fits, but if it makes the table feel less like a chaotic battlefield where puppies get trampled by dice. Spoiler: it mostly does. Also: we will tell you where the foam could have used a pinch more creativity and a dash of humor.

### Unboxing and First Impressions

Opening the box is like discovering a well-curated tool chest that smells faintly of cardboard and potential. The Wulfsburg expansion comes with a modest amount of plastic, cardboard, and plastic again—because everything is plastic in this world. The Feldherr Sortiereinsatz, when it finally arrives, resembles a high-tech aquarium with compartments instead of fish. The foam inserts arrive neatly pre-cut, and the process of removing them from their plastic wrap is almost as exciting as pulling a legendary card in a loot-driven RPG: you know something good is about to happen, but the actual moment of triumph is a small pop and a lot of relief.

The pre-cut trays feel substantial—closed-cell foam with a satisfying density that refuses to collapse under the weight of your zombie miniatures. You can press, slide, and wedgie your minis into place, and the foam smiles back with just the right amount of resistance. The color is a neutral grey, designed to hide glue marks and dust, rather than to scream “look at me, I’m premium foam!” This is a design choice we appreciate because our gaming table already has enough color drama with the tableside napkins that have somehow become a permanent part of the battlefield.

### Fit and Finish of the Sortiereinsatz

The heart of this review is the Sortiereinsatz, a foam insert that claims to sort minis, tokens, cards, and dice with surgical precision. The call-and-response moment goes like this:
- Will your Zombicide minis fit without a fight? Yes, mostly, depending on base size and pose.
- Will the tokens stay in their little wells without migrating into the dungeon of your dice bag? They will, with a little careful placement.
- Will the cards remain upright and accessible during a heated moment of the game? Yes, if you avoid tipping the tray while screaming “Zombicide!” at the top of your lungs.

In practice, the Sortiereinsatz does a decent job of dividing the content by type and by faction. The larger wells hold surviving minis and the big boss types, while the smaller slots keep the tokens from turning into a card-pile mash. There’s even a dedicated space for the mini-rulebook and the mission cards—though you may need to adjust if you own the 2nd edition or various special editions, as the card sizes sometimes diverge by a millimeter or two.

One note: the foam is dense enough that it doesn’t compress easily, which is great for protection but can make it a little stiff when you’re trying to pull a particularly stubborn miniature out of its slot. If you’re not careful, you’ll do the zombie-ninja trick where you tilt the tray and let gravity do the work. Proceed with caution and maybe practice a little before you start a critical campaign night.

### Compatibility and Modularity

A major concern with any insert is compatibility: with multiple expansions, can it keep up without needing a spreadsheet, scale model, or a small choir singing backup? The Feldherr Sortiereinsatz is designed to fit Feldherr cases, and it’s compatible with many of the standard Zombicide: Black Plague components. The Wulfsburg minis are a bit of a challenge due to their unique poses and sculpts, but the insert handles them with a bit of panache—enough to feel like you’re in the control room rather than wrestling a sleeping dragon.

If you own additional expansions beyond Wulfsburg, there’s a good chance the insert will accommodate them, but you’ll want to check the exact tray layout. The test case here had a) core game minis, b) Wulfsburg zombies, c) Wulfsburg survivors, d) a non-trivial pile of tokens, and e) a couple of extra dice that somehow multiply when you blink. In all of these, the Sortiereinsatz preserved order long enough to allow a moment of calm before the next wave of undead stormed the table.

For those who want to expand beyond Feldherr, there are alternative brands and “universal” foam trays. In a future post we will do a cross-brand foam-off featuring popular competitors like Waaagh! Foam, Ultra-Pro, and a mysterious vendor who swears their foam is made from recycled submarine ping-pong balls. Until then, this Feldherr insert is a good baseline.

### Real-world Playtest: Setup to Showdown

We ran a full practice game with a small cadre of survivors, a handful of Wulfsburg villains, and a deck of mission cards. The sorting tray kept cards in easy reach and minimized the rummaging that usually ends with a stray goblin in your coffee mug. The minis popped into their slots with a satisfying snap that felt like a microwave-safe moment of validation: yes, you measured twice, cut once, and now you have a table that won’t trigger a minor existential crisis every time you pick up a zombie mini.

The tray’s design equally shines in the post-game cleanup. You can slide everything back into the tray with a single pass rather than performing the heroic relay race of shifting foam and complaining players. We did notice that some of the taller, multi-part models require slightly angling the foam and twisting them gently—like performing a small yoga sequence for your figures. It’s not a nightmare, just a reminder that borders exist for a reason, and your minis have feelings too.

### Storage, Durability, and Everyday Use

Storage-wise, the Sortiereinsatz pays for itself in fewer minutes spent cramming loose minis back into a box after a violent, glorious match. The insert reduces the risk of damage from contact with other boxes, which is a thing that happens in the wild world of tabletop storage when your cat decides the shelf is a perfect launchpad for a projectile that looks suspiciously like a goblin import card.

Durability-wise, the foam stands up to repeated use. It’s not a rubber-like behemoth that will bounce back after every removal, but it doesn’t crumble to dust after a single week of intense gaming either. The cutting lines are consistent, and the wells stay aligned even when the box is tipped sideways, which is good because absolutely nothing in tabletop gaming remains perfectly aligned after a raid night.

### Value for Money and Practicality

But let’s be honest: you’re buying this insert not only for the “fun” of sorting (which is genuinely satisfying) but for saving your back and your sanity. The Feldherr Sortiereinsatz is not the cheapest piece of the puzzle, but it does deliver perceived value by keeping the miniatures intact, reducing setup time, and giving you one more reason to pretend you’re a sophisticated grown-up who organizes his hobby like a responsible adult.

If you currently have a handful of different organizers, the cost may feel steeper. You’ll be trading dollars for minutes saved and fewer existential crises about accidentally shattering a mini into two or three half-broken pieces. In our test, the time saved on setup and cleanup roughly paid for itself after a couple of sessions, especially if you’re the kind of player who loves to perfect the “start-of-game ritual” with ritualistic token placement and dramatic stacking of mini-cards at the edge of the tray.

### Alternatives and Comparisons

If you’re curious about other options, you can explore foam inserts from brands like Waaagh! Foam, Ultimate Guard, or the smaller, life-loving elves that populate the indie insert market. Our general take: Feldherr is a good middle-ground between price, quality, and compatibility. Waaagh! Foam tends to be flexible and a bit cheaper, which is great if you’re on a tight budget but may require more careful handling to avoid compression of the minis. Ultimate Guard has its own strengths, but their inserts sometimes lean more toward general gaming kits rather than highly tailored shapes for specific games.

For a more premium feel, there are black-mouthed inserts that claim to be “hand-sculpted by wizards” and guaranteed to “transcend your gaming experience.” We’ll leave the dragons to the imagination and judge by what you can actually fit into the box and retrieve without copying a PhD in cardboard engineering.

### First-hand Takeaways

- Pros:
  - Keeps minis safe and organized, with dedicated spaces for important tokens and cards.
  - Durable foam that resists crushing under normal use.
  - Improves setup and teardown times by offering quick access to essential components.
  - Compatible with standard Feldherr cases and a number of Zombicide expansions.

- Cons:
  - May require some rearrangement if you own a lot of expansions with unusual card or token sizes.
  - A bit pricey compared to generic foam trays, but the layout and cut quality compensate for this.
  - Taller models may require gentle manipulation to slot in and out.

### Final Verdict

If you’re someone who treats Zombicide: Black Plague and its Wulfsburg expansion like a hobby that deserves more than being dumped into a bucket, the Feldherr Sortiereinsatz is a solid upgrade. It won’t magically turn your dice into perfect teenagers who clean their room, but it will help you keep the minis unbroken, the cards in order, and your hobby table looking crisp enough to warrant a little bragging on social media. It’s a practical investment for players who want to minimize table chaos and maximize drama-free play.

TL;DR: The Sortiereinsatz is a well-designed, durable, and practical foam insert that makes setup and storage smoother, with a few caveats for larger campaigns and expansions. It’s not the cheapest solution, but you’ll likely appreciate the extra peace of mind and the time saved during setup. If you own Zombicide: Black Plague with Wulfsburg and you’re prone to box chaos, this insert is worth considering.
