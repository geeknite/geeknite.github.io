## How to assess authenticity and condition at a distance

For non-graded copies, you’re often relying on your own close inspection. Here are practical tips to evaluate a Bo Horvat UM12 Silver with a patch auto from 2012, even if you can’t hold it in your hands:

- Inspect the autograph: look for uniform ink flow, consistent color, and absence of pen skips. A clean, fully legible autograph usually signals a higher-quality autographed card.
- Check the patch window: rare patches should appear authentic in color and stitching. If the patch looks like a uniform stripe that might be reproduced, it could be a red flag.
- Edges and corners: ensure there’s no unusual whitening, chipping, or edge damage that would indicate mishandling.
- Surface texture: the Silver parallel should have a distinct gloss. Watch out for surface scratches that could affect grading; if you can’t see them easily, request multiple high-res images from the seller.
- Centering: many collectors emphasize centering as a key factor in grading. If the card is notably off-center, it can reduce the grade’s potential, even if the autograph and patch are pristine.

If you decide to go down the grading route, remember that some sellers intentionally seek to minimize the appearance of flaws in photos. Request confirmation photos and, if possible, a direct video walk-through showing the card under proper lighting. A little diligence goes a long way when the price tag reflects a short print with strong market chatter.

## Display, storage, and preservation tips for a scarce gem

Preservation is as important as acquisition when you’re talking about a 24/24 patch-auto. Here are practical, Geeknite-approved tips for keeping your card in prime condition:

- Use a top-loader and a semi-rigid card holder to prevent flexing and to stop moisture exposure.
- Store away from direct sunlight; the Silver parallel can glare under strong sunlight, which can cause color shifts or surface damage over time.
- Temperature and humidity control: keep the display area around 65–70°F (18–21°C) and around 40–50% humidity if possible. This reduces paper and ink degradation that can affect autographs.
- Avoid handling with bare hands; oils on skin can transfer to the card and degrade surface quality over time. Use clean cotton gloves if you’re serious about preservation.
- Frame with UV-blocking glass if you want the card to be part of a larger display. A good frame can turn a hobby piece into a wall-worthy centerpiece.
