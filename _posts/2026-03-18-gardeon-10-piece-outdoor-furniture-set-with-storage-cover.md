## Assembly and setup: how hard can it be?

### Time, tools, and tips

Assembling a 10-piece set is not exactly a lab experiment, but it isn’t a casual Saturday morning puzzle either. Expect a moderate challenge. You’ll typically need basic tools like a screwdriver and a wrench, plus a little bit of patience to align frames and fasten screws without cross-threading. The good news: most kits come with labeled parts and a clear hardware layout, sometimes with a little pictogram boasting that the instructions exist somewhere in the box. The assembly flow usually follows a straightforward path: build the table frame, attach glass top or top inserts, assemble seating frames, add cushions, and finally fit the cover to test your “future-proof” confidence.

If you’re new to outdoor furniture assembly, here are a few pro tips:

- Dry-fit the frames first, then loosely screw in hardware to allow adjustments. Tighten only when everything aligns.
- Use the included assembly hardware in the intended sequence. It’s easy to mix up washers, but the spacers you’ll need to create proper gaps are usually color-coded or have tiny marks.
- Check that the cushions aren’t upside down or inside out. It happens to the best of us, especially after a few extra cups of lemonade.
- Take breaks. This isn’t a sprint; it’s a marathon that ends with you lounging and nodding at your own ingenuity.
