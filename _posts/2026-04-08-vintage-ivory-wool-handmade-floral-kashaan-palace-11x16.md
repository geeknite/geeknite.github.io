## Material, craft, and the handmade ethos

This rug is described as wool and handmade, which in rug-speak signals a few important things. Wool brings natural resilience and a bite against wear that synthetics often struggle to mimic. It also handles foot traffic with a forgiving softness, which means you can walk across it in the morning with bare feet—no cold tile, no shocking carpet sting. Handmade rugs typically carry character in the form of slight variations that speak to the human hand behind the loom. If you’re into authenticity, those little quirks are features, not bugs.

Wool fibers deserve a quick science aside: natural wool is crimped, giving it bounce and the ability to spring back after compression. That matters for sofas that scoot over time and kids who attempt to turn living rooms into obstacle courses. A hand-knotted or hand-woven Kashan rug tends to have tight knots and sturdy weft work, contributing to a long life if cared for properly. ## Care and maintenance: longevity hacks

Care isn’t glamorous, but it’s the difference between a rug that lasts a decade and one that lasts a generation. Here are practical tips for this Kashan Palace 11x16:

- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting. Use a vacuum without a beater bar on high carpet mode to avoid pulling fibers.
- Rotate the rug every 6 months to ensure even wear across all zones (especially under heavy furniture).
- Address spills promptly with blotting technique—blot, don’t rub. For minor stains, a mild detergent diluted in water can work; test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods to minimize color fading. If your room has large sun exposure, consider UV-blocking window treatments or strategic rug placement to protect the palette.
- For deeper cleaning, hire a professional rug cleaning service familiar with handmade wool pieces. That’s not a cop-out; it’s a smart investment in preserving the weave integrity and color vibrancy.
- Periodically check the rug’s edges and fringe. Loose threads or unraveling can be repaired by a professional or a careful DIY with matching thread; don’t ignore small fabric issues, as they can propagate if left unchecked.

## Practical buying notes and setup tips

- Measure before you buy. Ensure your seating area fits comfortably with the rug accounting for all major furniture legs within the rug’s boundary. If you’re unsure, bring a tape measure to visualize the layout or use painter’s tape on the floor to map out the rug’s footprint.
- Consider a rug pad. A high-quality pad under the rug increases stability, prevents slipping, and adds a touch more underfoot cushion. It also helps protect the rug from wear and reduces friction under heavy furniture.
- Check care recommendations with the seller. Handmade wool rugs may have specific cleaning guidelines; verify that you’re aligned on approved cleaning methods and any professional services recommended for this particular piece.
- Factor in lighting. Warm lighting can enhance the rug’s ivory base and floral hues, while cooler lighting can change the feel. If you can, test the rug under a few lighting scenarios to see how the colors shift through the day.
- Budget for maintenance. Although wool is durable, professional cleaning every few years is a wise investment for preserving color and fibre integrity. This is a long-term relationship with your floor’s backdrop, not a one-and-done purchase.
