## Authenticity and Preservation: Keeping It Real
If you are a stickler for authenticity, there are a few angles to consider. First, verify the tuck art and the spine typography lines up with known 2015 Cyclist variants. Look for any deviation in the red accents or in the Bicycle logo alignment on the tuck. Sealed decks typically carry a sticker or seal that matches the factory pattern of that era. The presence of any tampering should raise flags; if you see an imperfect seal, treat it as a red flag that invites deeper inspection or a test cut that you probably should avoid on a sealed deck.

Preservation is not only about the seal but also the environment. Store the decks away from direct sunlight, humidity fluctuations, and maphon accidental liquid exposures (a coffee spill can ruin a lot more than a mood at a game night). A dry, dark space with stable temperature is ideal. The cards will thank you by remaining pliable for years and by not warping into warped memes that your online circle could not unsee for days.
