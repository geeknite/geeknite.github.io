## Performance and Compatibility
Compatibility is the bread and butter of a dock when you buy used. You want confidence that the system will work with your laptop, your existing monitors, and your everyday workflow. The HP 120W G4 is a solid performer in this space, especially when paired with laptops that embrace TB4 or USB-C alt mode. Yet there are a few caveats worth noting up front.

### Windows ecosystem vs macOS diplomacy
- On Windows machines with TB4, docking tends to feel seamless: displays pop up, USB devices wake from sleep, and power delivery remains stable through the workday. You can typically run two 4K displays or a robust single 8K setup if your host GPU and cables are up to the task.
- macOS users sometimes encounter slight quirks around display detection or sleep/wake transitions. This is largely dependent on the exact MacBook model and macOS version. If your setup is heavily macOS oriented, check compatibility notes for your laptop before purchase.
- Linux users often get a straightforward experience, provided the kernel supports Thunderbolt docking and the graphics stack is well configured. In practice this means some command line dance may be involved for perfect display alignment, but the dock itself remains a reliable hub once configured.

### Display and bandwidth realities
Thunderbolt 4 is superb when connected right. The dual display scenario is straightforward on a host that supports it. If you push two 4K monitors, you will be using substantial bandwidth; ensure you use a high quality TB4 cable and robust DisplayPort/HDMI cables. For day to day productivity this is a non issue; for high end video editing with multiple streams, you might want to test your exact configuration before committing.
