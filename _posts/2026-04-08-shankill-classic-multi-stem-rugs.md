## Care and longevity tips

### Cleaning basics

- Spill first aid: blot, don’t rub. Blot gently with a clean cloth to prevent staining from seeping deeper.
- Stains: mild detergent mixed with water can lift most common spills. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the fiber sheen.
- Vacuuming: use a vacuum with adjustable height. Avoid beater bars on delicate settings to preserve pile height.

### Rug pads and placement

A quality rug pad helps prevent slipping and adds extra cushioning. The pad also reduces wear on the rug’s underside by providing a more uniform surface for foot traffic. Choose a pad sized slightly smaller than the rug to avoid edges catching on doorways or furniture.

### Longevity and routine checks

Even with daily life, the rug holds up nicely. If you notice flattening in high traffic zones, flip the approach by rotating the rug or repositioning furniture to distribute wear more evenly. This little maintenance habit can extend the life of the textile and keep the stems looking crisp year after year.
