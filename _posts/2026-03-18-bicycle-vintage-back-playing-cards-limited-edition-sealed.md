## Care, storage, and display tips
### How to store sealed decks
Keep sealed decks in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The goal is to minimize any gradual, unseen effects of humidity and heat on the tuck’s glue and the stock itself. If you have a display cabinet, a soft-lined drawer, or a purpose-built card case, those are ideal. The sealed deck will perform best when it remains in its original environment, a little away from drafts and temperature swings.

### If you ever decide to open it
If you choose to crack the seal (we won’t tell), handle gently. Use a clean, dry surface and avoid fanning the cards in a way that could create creases on the edges. For archival quality, you might consider placing the deck in a protective sleeve or an archival-safe container after you open it. This preserves both the appearance and the physical integrity for future generations of curious collectors and Discord card-flair videos.

### Display ideas for the geeky collector
A clear display case with a recessed stand can make the deck feel like a museum piece without making it inaccessible. Add a small card that explains the edition, the year, and a note on the design influences. If you have a rotating display, set the Vintage Back deck to a nice, slow turning angle so viewers get to appreciate the back motif from every maybe-facing angle. The old-world charm deserves a moment of limelight in every enthusiast’s living room or game room.

### Verifying a sealed deck
The best way to verify is to check the seal integrity, the box’s print quality, and the serial or edition information printed discreetly on the tuck.
