## Mint condition and grading signals
### What to look for in NM/M foil cards
- Surface integrity: A smooth surface with no visible scratches when held at typical play angles.
- Edge wear: Minimal whitening or chipping along the edges; if you control the card’s handling, this should be a non-issue.
- Centering: Many players prize a perfectly centered card; foil finishes can accentuate slight misalignment, making it more noticeable.
- Foil consistency: A uniform holo pattern across the card’s area; patchiness or inconsistent reflection can be a sign of lower quality or heavy use.

### How to care for your foil BT3-105
- Sleeves with UV protection are a good idea, especially if you keep the card on display. The foil’s shimmering can attract attention from the sunbeam in your living room, which is cute until it becomes a permanent fixture on your ceiling.
- Internal storage in soft sleeves and extra card pages helps maintain the surface. Avoid stacking foils in a way that could cause edge kinking or scratches from contact with other cards.
- If you trade or sell, photograph the card under neutral light with a light source at a 45-degree angle—this ensures the foil’s quality and any small flaws are visible to buyers. It reduces the chance of surprises when someone opens the package.
