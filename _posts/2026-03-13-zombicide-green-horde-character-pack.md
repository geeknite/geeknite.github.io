### What’s in the Pack
The Green Horde Character Pack adds a set of five to six survivors, each with a distinct flavor, ability arc, and a couple of extra gear cards to kickstart their story arcs. The vibe leans toward nature magic, skirmish survival, and a dash of goblin-friendly engineering—think improvised weapons, totem-based defense stances, and a surprising amount of patience for waiting out a pit trap while you hold the line with a staff that mutters under its breath.

- A brand-new roster of survivors with narrative prompts and unique ability trees
- A handful of new equipment cards that align with the Green Horde aesthetic
- Mini-story clues and flavor text that reference the forest, the totems, and the tribal kin that refuse to let a single day go by without a new plan
- A couple of new terrain interactions that can tilt the board in your favor—if you’re cunning enough to use them

If you’ve played other Zombicide packs, the design language will feel familiar: clear stat lines, a handful of action tokens, and a synergy design that rewards tactical positioning and cooperative planning. If you’re new to Zombicide, the pack is a gentle onboarding that doesn’t break the sweaty pace of a Friday night game, but it does push you to consider new angles rather than just “kill all the zombies, somehow.” The sheer variety of the characters helps avoid the classic “cookie-cutter survivor” syndrome that can creep into co-op games when players are tired of the same two or three builds.

The mechanics encourage players to communicate, assign roles, and then adapt when the board throws a curveball—like a cluster of fast attackers sneaking up in the blind spot or a zombie herd suddenly changing the rhythm of the game with a well-timed surprise move.

If you’ve played other Zombicide packs, you’ll recognize the core tempo: a sprint of action, a probability check, and a moment of relief when the last zombie in a cluster is eliminated. ### Strategy Tips and Playstyle Variants
Here are a handful of practical tips to help you get the most out of the pack on your next session:
- Plan your turns 2–3 ahead. The new survivors reward multi-move setups that optimize terrain control. If you can create a chain of totem buffs that keep your frontline safe for an extra round, you’re already ahead.
- Combine immobilizing terrain with your heavy hitters. The forest markers aren’t just decor; they can funnel zombies into kill zones where you can use your strongest survivor to clear a path without spreading your squad too thin.
- Balance risk and reward. The pack introduces high-risk, high-reward options—like stepping into a trap to trigger a powerful effect—but use them with a group-wide risk assessment. A reckless use of a big ability can backfire just as quickly as a well-timed one can save you.
- Upgrade economy. Save some resource for late-game buffs. The pack’s equipment options tend to scale with the threat level, so ensuring you have a few extra tools for that mid-to-late game crunch is worth the sacrifice earlier on.
- Communicate. The best rounds are the ones where you all talk through the board state, assign roles, and watch the horde collapse in a choreographed way rather than chaotic chaos. This is a game that rewards teamwork more than solo heroics, and the character pack nudges you toward sharing leadership tasks without turning it into a political drama.

For the more analytically minded players, a mini meta-game emerges: you’ll want to identify which survivor pairings offer the most durable frontline, or which combination lets you trigger chain reactions that thin out overwhelming hordes before they reach your line. The joy of the pack lies in these small, crunchy decisions that accumulate into a satisfying, near-solo-level of table problem-solving for a good chunk of the session.
