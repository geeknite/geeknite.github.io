## How to Play (Kid-Friendly Breakdown)
### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal 10 cards to each player for a standard 2-6 player game. In some house rules you can accommodate more players if your table is big enough, but the core experience shines with the standard setup.
- Place the remainder of the deck face down as the draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile. The phase outlines are separate, but you’ll use those as the guideline to win before your opponents.
- Decide who goes first—this is usually the youngest player or the one with the best goofy victory dance. It’s important to keep it fun, not punitive.

### Phases Overview
Phase 10 requires you to complete 10 different phases in order. Each phase is basically a specific hand combination you must collect and lay down to advance. Common phases include sets (three of a kind), runs (five consecutive numbers), and mixed sets. The plastic edition doesn’t change the core logic: you must complete the current phase before moving to the next. If you complete a phase, you advance; if you don’t, you draw and discard, trying again next turn.

Here’s a quick snapshot of typical phases you might encounter:
- Phase 1: 2 sets of 3
- Phase 2: 1 set of 3 + 1 run of 4
- Phase 3: 1 set of 4 + 1 run of 4
- Phase 4: 1 run of 5
- Phase 5: 1 run of 6
- Phase 6: 1 run of 7
- Phase 7: 1 run of 8
- Phase 8: 1 run of 9
- Phase 9: 2 sets of 4
- Phase 10: 7 cards of one color (or other color-based variation depending on the edition)
Note: the exact phase structure can vary slightly by edition, so quickly skim the included rule sheet before you attempt your first phase sprint on game night.

### Turn Flow
- Draw: On your turn, draw one card from either the draw pile or the discard pile.
- Phase Submission: If you’ve completed the current phase with your hand (before you end your turn), you can lay down the completed phase on the table. This is where the “win” tempo begins to clip into place. A well-timed laydown can give you the lead you need, while a mis-timed one might invite a dramatic table-side groan from your siblings.
- Discard: End your turn by discarding one card. When your hand becomes smaller, your brain becomes louder, which is a perfect recipe for family bonding, right?

The flow is straightforward, but the fun comes from watching players juggle high-demand cards and the occasional “why do I have six red 2s?” moment. The plastic edition’s durability keeps the visuals clean as you throw your cards down with gusto, even if your child’s victory dance involves spinning the chair and yelling “Phase 10!” at maximum decibels.
