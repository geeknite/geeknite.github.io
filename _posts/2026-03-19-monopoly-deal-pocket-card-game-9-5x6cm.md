## How to play in a nutshell
Here is the quick-fire primer you can memorize during a layover or while you are pretending to work at the airport lounge.

- Setup: Shuffle the deck; deal five cards to each player; place the remaining deck as a draw pile. It is a simple start that invites frenetic energy rather than solemn ceremony.
- Objective: Build three full property sets, with at least one color set. The color sets in monopoly are a big deal, and in this pocket game they still demand attention, even when they are miniature.
- On your turn: Draw two cards, then play up to three actions. The actions can be money to your bank, property to your collection, or various effects from action cards.
- Rent and payments: When you collect rent, you can charge opponents for using your properties. Your opponents pay with money cards either from their hand or from their own stash. The interplay of rent values and property sets creates a fun economic tug-of-war.
- Just say no: The game has protective mechanics that feel like a subtle jab at the idea that nothing is sacred in a family gathering. A "Just Say No" card can cancel someone else’s action—until it’s your turn and you decide to throw one back at someone else.

The pocket format remains faithful to the vibe of the larger game. The pace is quick, and you will often cycle through hands rapidly. It is an excellent social icebreaker because you will be laughing at the ridiculous combos that emerge and groaning when someone swipes a property you were counting on.
