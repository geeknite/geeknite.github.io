## How to play with the 90s Nostalgia Pack Torn

Playing CAH with an expansion is about balancing fast wit with social reading. Torn adds flavor but does not change the basic structure of the game: one player acts as the Card Czar and reads a prompt card; the other players submit best matchups using their white cards. The judge selects the funniest or most fitting combination, and so the game cycles continuing to the next round.

### Best practices for optimal laughs
- Build your hand with a few flexible responses that can suit multiple prompts. Nostalgia is often about associations, so cards that can pair with a range of prompts tend to perform well.
- Pay attention to the room. Nostalgia is powerful when the audience shares context. If your group is a mix of 90s natives and younger players, lean into the more universal pop culture lines while saving inside jokes for later rounds.
- Keep the energy in check. The Torn edition is sharp but not mean. The goal is to keep the humor flowing and inclusive, so avoid punching down and instead lean into lighthearted absurdity.
- Use the 90s lens for a few rounds. A good strategic move is to open the game with nostalgia-driven prompts to set the vibe and then pivot to more general humor as the night progresses.

### Variants and house rules
If your group enjoys customizing, consider these light variants:
- Memory lane mode: encourage players to tell a quick personal memory related to their chosen card before submitting the response.
- Power nights: give a small advantage to players who can weave two or more nostalgic references into a single response. Just keep it fun and not too long.
- Clean cut version: if some players want to keep things on the tame side, declare one round as clean humor only and see how that changes the dynamic.
