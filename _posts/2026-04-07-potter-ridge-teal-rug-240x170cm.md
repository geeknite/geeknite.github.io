## Maintenance and Durability

Durability is a big deal for rugs, especially if you’re putting it in living rooms or hallways that see a lot of foot traffic. The 240x170 cm Potter Ridge has a machine-made construction that’s designed to resist shedding, a common issue with budget fabrics. You’ll notice the fibers hold shape over time, and the rug doesn’t begin to pill or fuzz excessively with everyday use. Vacuuming is straightforward; you’ll want to avoid high-velocity suction on a daily basis to minimize any potential friction that could cause wear on the fibers. A standard weekly vacuum is usually enough to keep the rug looking sharp.

Stain resistance is another big argument in favor of polypropylene. Spills tend to sit on the surface, and with a quick blot and mild cleaning solution, most common stains disappear. It’s not a miracle cure for red wine or coffee disasters if you pour with reckless abandon, but it’s significantly more forgiving than natural fiber rugs. For tougher stains, treat promptly and avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the weave.

Maintenance routine tips:
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; don’t rub, or you’ll push the stain deeper and risk fiber distortion.
- Use a mild detergent solution for tougher spots; test on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Vacuum regularly but avoid a high heat setting if your vacuum has a beater brush; a gentle setting is usually enough.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear and color exposure to sunlight.
