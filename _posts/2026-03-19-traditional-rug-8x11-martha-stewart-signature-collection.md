## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Cleaning basics and routine care
Traditionally styled rugs like this one reward consistent care. Regular vacuuming on a low setting helps keep the pile dense and free of surface dirt without pulling fibers. It’s wise to rotate the rug every few months if you notice uneven wear—this prevents one side from looking more tired than the other. Spills should be blotted quickly with a clean cloth rather than scrubbed, which can push stain deeper into the fibers. While this rug is designed to resist many common stains, no rug is truly stainproof; the key is prompt action and a backup plan for stubborn spots.

### Stain resistance and dye stability
If you’re worried about colorfastness, you’ll be pleased to know that the dye stability in this model appears solid in the tests I performed. Color migration is minimal, especially with water-based spills. For more persistent stains, you’ll want to follow manufacturer-approved cleaning methods, ideally tested on a small inconspicuous area first. Remember, what looks good in the showroom can sometimes reveal surprises in a real home, so always test before committing to a full cleaning cycle.

### Longevity and value over time
Durability is one of the rug’s strongest selling points. The combination of a dense weave, quality backing, and a traditional pattern ensures it remains a staple in your decor for years. If you count the number of seasons your furniture arrangement will stay relevant, this rug earns a few extra brownie points for aging gracefully as your room evolves.
