## What is Quest - Dixit 2?
Quest - Dixit 2 is a tabletop game for 3 to 6 players that blends storytelling with light deduction in a structure that leans into a light campaign flavor. Each round, players receive a set of art cards and a quest prompt that pushes them to craft a clue that fits the quest theme but remains just obscure enough for the rest of the group to chase after the right cards. The twist here is that the quest prompts are not just static phrases but modular, multi part challenges that can be combined or chained across rounds. This design lets the game scale from casual, chaos filled sessions to more structured storytelling nights where you want a narrative spine to cling to.

If you are familiar with Dixit, you will notice the DNA remains intact: evocative art, a friendly but sly scoring system, and the social engine that makes a room feel collaborative and a little competitive all at once. What Quest - Dixit 2 does differently is to lean into the quest mechanic as a driver of interaction. Clues are not only about signaling to your friends what the art means but about guiding them through a small narrative puzzle. This can be delightful when players lean into it and a bit chaotic when folks just want to show off their painting interpretations. The tone is more adventurous, the art more mission oriented, and the vibe overall is a wittier, more theatrical version of the original game.

For fans of expansions or variants, Quest - Dixit 2 sits somewhere between a tightened party game and a light campaign romp. It can be played as a one shot where everyone tries to maximize points on a handful of rounds, or you can string a few Quest sets into a longer arc featuring recurring characters, evolving clues, and a shared story arc. The rules encourage collaboration and misdirection in equal measure, and that tension is the breezy backbone of the entire experience.

## How to Play: Setup and Turn Structure
We will keep this section approachable for new players while not stripping away the quirks that make Quest - Dixit 2 special.

### Setup
1. Each player selects a storyteller token and receives a hand of art cards. In a 3 or 4 player game, you may want to reduce the hand size to keep things brisk; with 5 or 6 players, the standard hand size helps maintain the social tension.
2. Shuffle the quest deck and place it within reach. You may choose to draft a few quests at the start to seed early rounds with accessible, punchy prompts or to let the deck surprise you as the game builds momentum.
3. Place the clue board in the center and lay out the scoring tokens and influence tokens within reach. Make sure everyone has a clean view of the quest prompts and the cards on the table.
4. Each round, the storyteller for that round picks one quest from the deck or from a preselected pool, depending on your house rules. The storyteller then looks at their hand and selects a card that best embodies the quest while staying reasonably cryptic.

### Turn Overview
A typical round flows like this:
- The storyteller chooses a card and reveals it. The other players select one card from their hand that they feel best matches the quest or that would mislead others in a delightful way.
- Players submit their cards face down. The storyteller shuffles the submissions and reveals them alongside their own chosen card.
- Players vote on which card they think is the storyteller card. The more players who correctly identify the storyteller card, the greater the reward, but there is a catch. If everyone or no one guesses correctly, the storyteller receives no points for that round, and instead all players receive a small consolation at the end of the round.
- Scoring phase: Points are awarded based on whether players correctly guessed the storyteller card and how many people guessed your card. In addition, influence tokens can be spent to tilt outcomes in certain directions, which adds a light strategic layer without turning the game into a numbers game.
- The storyteller marker passes clockwise and the next round begins with a refreshed set of cards if you are playing with a larger hand.

This rhythm is the core of Quest - Dixit 2. It rewards precise, evocative clues that are just cryptic enough to spark debate, but also rewards a playful misdirection that makes sense within the quest framework. It is a game that wants you to talk, argue, and laugh together, which is where the magic lives.

## Scoring and Progression
The scoring in Quest - Dixit 2 is straightforward at face value, but the nuance shows up in the influence tokens and minor arc bonuses. You earn points when your card is guessed by others and more points when your clue convinces the table without tipping the entire room off. The influence tokens allow players to tweak outcomes in subtle ways, which can be a source of strategic fun without tipping into power creep. The game rewards cleverness and collaboration in equal measure. ## Quick Start Guide for New Players
If you want to wring a fast, fun session out of Quest - Dixit 2, here is a concise starter plan:
- Gather a group of 4 to 6 players for a balanced dynamic. If you have 3 players, drop a hand size a touch for quicker rounds. If you have 5 or 6, consider two quest decks to mix the pacing.
- Pick a storyteller for the first round. The storyteller selects a quest and then a card that embodies it without revealing too much.
- Each other player picks a card that they think fits the quest, not too well or too obvious. Place cards face down and reveal them together with the storyteller card.
- Everyone votes on which card they believe is the storyteller card. Score points as described in the rulebook and track your progress on the quest arc board.
- Rotate the storyteller and repeat for a pre agreed number of rounds. When you reach the end of the arc, tally final scores and declare a champion of imagination.

With those steps, you can run a clean, satisfying game night that ends with everyone leaving with a grin and a few new inside jokes about portals, goblins, and a painting of a cat wearing a wizard hat.
