## Painting and Assembly Tips
Painting these runes into the night is half the joy. Here are a few practical tips to get you from blank model to posed horror in as little time as possible:

- Prep is king: a quick wash with a simple soap-and-water bath followed by a good scrub with a soft brush will reveal detail without much effort. Dry thoroughly before painting.
- Prime with a neutral color: a light gray primer helps you see those crevices and gives you a solid base for skin tones and leathers.
- Flesh and rot palettes: use a three-tone approach for skin: base tone, mid-tone for highlights, and a thin glaze of a purplish-brown to simulate cold rot in creases.
- Clothes and leather: use a higher-contrast dark base with lighter highlights along raised seams. Wash with a dark brown or black to settle the recesses.
- Dry brushing for texture: a light gray or bone highlight on raised areas reveals bone fragments or tattered cloth edges without requiring a heavy hand.
- Bases matter: base the runners with a little texture paint or sand to convey the B-Movie vibe of a haunted alley or ruined fortress courtyard.

If you are a busy gamer, you can do speed-paint sessions where you focus on one or two units per night. The rest can be kept in their blister packs awaiting a future painting session. Pro-tip for tabletop aesthetics: apply a subtle matte varnish to keep the color from rubbing off during heroic dice-dodging moments.

For those who enjoy a bit of experimentation, try basing some runners with small bits of cork or mixed basing materials to create the illusion of speed trails. The effect is not only visually striking but also fun for photo sessions that you might post for your gaming club with the hashtags you know and love.
