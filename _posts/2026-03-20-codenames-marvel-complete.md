## How to play: the core loop and the Marvel twists

At its core, Codenames is a word association game. You have a grid of words, a pool of hints, and two teams trying to uncover their own color words before the other team finds theirs. The spymaster gives a one-word clue and a number. Each clue must connect to as many of their team's words as possible. The number tells the team how many words on the grid are connected to the clue. The challenge is that you might hit words belonging to the other team, neutral words, or the dreaded assassin. The Marvel Complete edition layers on a Marvel flavored mission deck and hero-specific bonuses that can tilt a round in unexpected ways.

Here is the typical round flow:
- Spymaster consults the mission deck and hero cards to understand the thematic constraints for the round. These constraints might encourage your hints to be more sciencey, more hero-team oriented, or more villain focused. 
- Spymaster gives a one-word clue plus a number. The number indicates how many words on the grid are connected to the clue for that round. The clue can be anything as long as it relates to the words, and the one word twist is where the fun begins. 
- Field agents confer and decide which words to guess. A correct guess reveals a red or blue word; a wrong guess reveals a neutral word or, in a worst case, the assassin. If you guess all your team words, you win the round. If you step on the assassin, you lose immediately. If you hit the enemy words, you give them a little more rope to snatch victory. 
- The round ends when the team hits their own count or runs out of guesses. Then the mat is rotated to the other team with a new set of clues, and the cycle continues.

The Marvel twist adds a few complications that feel natural rather than gimmicky. Some hero cards offer special advantages such as a second clue or a safe go-around after a near miss. The mission cards can require you to cover certain themes, like technology or space, which pushes the vocabulary in a new direction. The assassin remains a ruthless reminder that success requires precision and creative thinking. The Complete edition ensures you can toggle between standard Codenames and Marvel flavored modes with minimal friction, so you can adapt the pace to your group without spending 15 minutes redefining the rules at the table.

## Strategy and tips: making the most of the Marvel Complete deck

Like any good party game with a license, the real fun often comes from the social dynamics rather than raw mechanical depth. Here are some practical tips to level up your play experience when you dive into Codenames Marvel Complete:

- Build a shared Marvel vocabulary: Encourage players to bring up Marvel characters or terms that have clear but not obvious connections to common words on the board. The more your team can connect to the same hero imagery, the easier the hints will be. If your group is more casual, lean into obvious connections that are still safe and avoid overthinking.
- Use hero perks strategically: The Complete edition gives a few hero-based advantages. Use those perks to create a temporary advantage that aligns with your team’s words, but don t hinge the entire round on a single perk. The best rounds feel earned through clever hints, not loopholes in the rules.
- Play the long game with caution: When you sense a tight match, avoid spicy hints that require you to stretch too far. A good hint should connect two or three clear words without stepping on the enemy or the assassin. If you can keep your hints concise and precise, you will outlast your opponents even in a longer session.
- Balance speed and accuracy: The Marvel deck rewards quick thinking, but speed without accuracy is a recipe for disaster. Encourage your team to discuss a potential path briefly and then commit. The Keepers of Time (your denizens of the couch) will appreciate it when you don t stall to the point of a dramatic monologue.
- Manage the tempo: The Complete edition supports a lively tempo, which is great for party nights but taxing on slower groups. If you have players who like to talk through every possibility, set a round time limit or add a house rule that limits debate to a fixed window. The point is to keep the energy high without losing the thread of the game.

A few tactical candids often crop up in Marvel Complete games. Some players treat the HTML of a clue as a puzzle to be solved in the same moment as the words. Others lean into a storytelling approach by connecting a constellation of words to a hero mythos that they then weave into their hint. Both approaches have value, and the balance between wordplay and thematic railroading is what keeps the game fresh round after round.
