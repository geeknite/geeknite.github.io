## Maintenance and care
### Everyday care routine
Regular vacuuming is your friend. Use a soft brush attachment and avoid power head vacuums that may distort the pile over time. Address spills promptly with a clean, colorless cloth. For stubborn stains, treat with a mild detergent solution, testing a small inconspicuous area first. Do not rub aggressively; instead, blot gently to lift the stain and minimize fiber disruption. A quick rotation every few months can help the wear patterns distribute evenly, especially in high-traffic areas where the floor plan starts to resemble a chaotic chessboard after a few weeks of weekday chaos.

### Cleaning methods and professional help
For deeper cleaning, you can hire a professional rug cleaner or rent a rug cleaning machine. If you opt for DIY, follow the machine’s instructions and consider a low moisture approach to avoid mold growth and long drying times. The key is to keep moisture to a minimum and to avoid soaking the rug for extended periods. In all cases, ensure thorough drying before reintroducing the rug to the space to prevent that dreaded musty smell that says I am a rug with a personality complex.
