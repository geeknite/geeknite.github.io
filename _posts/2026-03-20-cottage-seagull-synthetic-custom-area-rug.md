## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity: A Practical Guide

Synthetic rugs shine in the cleaning department. The Cottage Seagull rug is designed to be more forgiving than natural fibers, especially when faced with red wine spills, coffee rings, muddy paw prints, or the occasional runaway glitter project from a weekend craft session. Here are some practical care tips:

- Quick cleanups: blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the fibers.
- Stain treatment: for tougher stains, use a mild detergent solution or a stain remover recommended for synthetic fibers. Always test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.
- Vacuuming: vacuum regularly to prevent dirt buildup. Use a suction setting that’s gentle on the fibers to avoid excessive wear.
- Drying: if the rug gets soaked, air dry thoroughly before placing it back on the floor. Synthetic fibers dry faster than natural wool, but you still want to avoid mold or mildew, especially in damp spaces.
- Outdoor exposure: if you have a sunlit patio or a sunroom, be mindful of UV exposure. Prolonged direct sunlight can fade colors over time, even on synthetics. Consider rotating rugs or using window coverings to minimize sun exposure.

In practice, the synthetic formula means you won’t dread cleaning day as much as you might with a wool rug. It’s not a license to spill red sauces with reckless abandon, but it is a practical reminder that life happens and you don’t have to schedule a rug-cleaning appointment every weekend.
