## Cleaning, maintenance, and longevity

Cleaning is simple. A quick sweep to remove leaves and dust, followed by a rinse with a hose or a bucket of warm soapy water, should be enough to keep it looking fresh. For stubborn stains like wine or marinades that have seen a little too much of your kitchen, a gentle scrub with a soft brush does the trick. Avoid harsh chemicals that might degrade the plastic over time, and protect the rug from flames or high heat sources, which is common sense but worth repeating for safety and longevity. The rug is lightweight enough to be rolled up and stored during winter or severe weather, which is a big advantage for those who get into seasonal storage. The ability to roll up also helps with pressure washing or deep cleaning when the patio wood needs extra care.

## Setup and installation tips

For a clean look, consider laying the rug on a flat, dry surface. If your deck has a slight slope or uneven boards, use a few non slip mats or rug grippers to keep the edges in place. Since the rug is not attached to the ground, it can move slightly if there is heavy foot traffic or a gust of wind. In a multi day party scenario, you may want to anchor the corners with deck clips or heavy furniture to prevent edges from curling. When placing furniture, the rug should be large enough to accommodate a seating arrangement with a coffee table without limiting leg room. If you have a grill or a fire pit, keeping at least a small border of clearance around the open flame is recommended.
