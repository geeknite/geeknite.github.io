## Strategy: Reading the Menu Without Drooling on the Table
If your goal is to win more than you crave revenge on your opponents’ misreads, consider these actionable tips:

- Don’t chase the big payoff in round one. Early momentum is valuable, but the real work happens in the last couple of rounds when the new cards’ interactions start to sing.
- Learn the new synergies quickly. A minute of glance-time before a round can pay off with several extra points by the end of the game.
- Observe neighbors closely. Drafting is as much social deduction as it is math. If your neighbor is visibly chasing a particular line, think about whether you can block or redirect their efforts.
- Embrace variability. The expansion trades some predictability for more “aha” moments. Those moments are what make gatherings memorable, even when someone misreads the timing and you all end up laughing through tasty, chaotic chaos.

If you want to treat this like a tiny puzzle you solve with friends rather than a war of attrition, you’ll get more joy from it. The best moments come when a single card alignment suddenly unlocks a neat chain of totals that makes everyone cheer (or groan, depending on how your group handles public scoreboard anxiety).
