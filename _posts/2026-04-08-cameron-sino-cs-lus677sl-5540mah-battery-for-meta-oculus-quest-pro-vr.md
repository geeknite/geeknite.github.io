## Installation guide: a safe, sane path to power
If you’re comfortable with minor electronics work, you can swap the CS-LUS677SL in a few careful steps. If you’re nervous, consider professional service—this is not a space where nerves should be shot by the first spark of doubt. Here’s a practical guide to a clean, safe install.

### Step-by-step outline
1. Power down and unplug. Remove the Quest Pro from any accessories. If you’ve been gaming in the last hour, give it a minute to cool and avoid thermal shock during disassembly.
2. Open the casing with the appropriate tools. Preserve the clips and screws; you’ll want them in their rightful place during reassembly.
3. Locate the old battery and carefully disconnect the connector. Do not tug on wires; use the proper connector release tools and gently disengage. If you feel resistance, recheck alignment and avoid forcing a stubborn plug.
4. Remove the old battery. Keep it away from your workstation’s cables in case it still holds some charge—you don’t want to inadvertently bridge circuits with metal objects.
5. Prepare the new CS-LUS677SL. Inspect the new battery for any shipping damage and ensure connectors align with the original orientation.
6. Connect the new battery’s plug to the motherboard connector. Confirm a secure fit—no wiggle, no half-insertions.
7. Re-seat the battery into the housing with the adhesive and mounting brackets as provided. Take a moment to visualize how the original battery sat; you want a snug fit that doesn’t bend the casing.
8. Close the casing, screw everything back in, and perform a careful power-on test. If the device boots and you can access the home screen, you’re probably good to go.
9. Run a short calibration charge cycle and allow the battery to settle in. This helps the protective circuitry learn the new pack’s behavior and ensures early accurate readings on charge levels.

### Safety reminders during install
- Work on a non-conductive surface to minimize static risk. A wooden desk or anti-static mat works well.
- Use proper tools designed for electronics. Avoid metal prying tools that could scratch the casing or damage nearby components.
- Don’t puncture or crush the battery. If you see swelling or unusual warmth during test runs, stop immediately and seek professional help.
- Dispose of the old battery responsibly. Most electronics recyclers will accept spent cells; do not toss them in the trash where they can pose a hazard.
