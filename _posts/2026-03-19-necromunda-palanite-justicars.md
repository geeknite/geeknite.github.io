## Overview: Who are the Palanite Justicars?

The Palanite Justicars are a police-like contingent in the Necromunda setting. In a city where crime is a constant, the Palanites position themselves as the thin blue line between the outlaws and the slow, psychotropic doom of bureaucratic eternity. They arrive equipped with a mix of pistols, melee tools, and the sort of goofily heroic swagger that makes you want to roll a narrative dice and declare them the heroes of the hour—until the dice betray you and remind you that you’re still playing Necromunda, a game where good intentions often collide with grenade launchers and questionable cover saves.

From a flavor perspective, the Palanites are a refreshing change of pace. They’re not the berserkers of Goliath, nor the streetwise hustlers of Escher. They’re your compliant, law-abiding, paperwork-obsessed squad who believe that a well-timed citation can solve most problems—provided you survive the firefight long enough to fill out the form. They bring a different rhythm to your table: you’ll be balancing the urge to enforce with the urge to survive, which, in Necromunda, is basically the same thing with a different color of blood.

### The Box and the Miniatures

The Palanite box is a character study in “less is more” when it comes to ornamentation. The sculpts tend toward the practical: body armor that looks plausible under a neon haze, shields that scream “we mean business,” and weapons that look like they could double as law-punching devices in a pinch. The range typically includes a mix of enforcers, specialists, and maybe a couple of commanders who look suspiciously like they would rather be wearing a suit and solving crimes by inference rather than shooting a bolter in a hallway. The plastics are clean, streamlined, and paint-friendly, which makes them an excellent entry point for new Necromunda players who want a confident, table-ready army without a months-long painting marathon.

If you’re into conversion potential, you’ll find plenty of room to play. The Palanites aren’t so specialized that you’re locked into one look. You can tailor their armor and insignia to reflect a district-wide style, a particular precinct, or a corporate sponsor that somehow ended up funding the enforcement program in the underhive. The aesthetic leans into that “civil servant who moonlights as lawgiver” vibe, which is perfect for the grimdark mood of Necromunda while still giving you something you can look at on a rainy weekend without needing to consult a PhD in cryptic flavor text.

### In-Game Identity and Playstyle

Mechanically, the Palanites skew toward a discipline-focused list: steady, reliable firepower, controlled movement, and a fondness for sealing doors behind them like a not-very-friendly security system. The archetype tends to reward players who plan routes, control lines of sight, and use terrain to shield their enforcement squads while they slowly, methodically push forward. If you’ve ever played a board game where you’re told to “move slowly and secure your objectives,” you’ll feel right at home with the Palanites, except in Necromunda, where the floor can swallow you in a single sweep of a grenade template.

What they bring to the table that other gangs don’t is a kind of lawful-done-right attitude. They’re not just about blasting bad guys; they’re about the doctrine, the order, the sense that every action has a bureaucratic consequence. This can be both endearing and maddening, depending on whether your opponent is a fan of lead flying, or you’re the GM who loves narrating a tense standoff with a lot of “you need three more favors from the precinct to proceed.” The Palanites thrive in objective-based play, where you’re not just trying to outgun your opponent but out-schedule them. It’s a refreshing change from pure slugfests, and it gives you a reason to bring along the extra clipboard you’ve been hiding in your backpack since middle school.

## Building a Palanite List: Practical Advice

### Core Units and Roles

A typical Palanite list balances three core ideas: frontline stability, controlled fire support, and meaningful close-quarters options. Here are the common roles you’ll see in most builds:

- Enforcers: The workhorses. Durable, decently armed, and capable of threatening the front line with steady fire.
- Judges/Commanders: The leadership piece. They carry the aura of order and can buff the squad while dodging a bit of danger, making them excellent anchors for your formation.
- Specialists: A bit lighter on raw firepower but heavy on utility. Think personnel who can handle puzzles, disable devices, or provide meaningful buffs to the squad’s survivability or effect range.

The trick is to design your list so your shooters aren’t left babysitting a pair of stubborn heavies who refuse to die. A good Palanite squad has someone who can perform a quick, precise action—like taking a long-range shot, providing a cover fire, or delivering a morale-boosting speech through a megaphone—while the rest of the gang rearranges the boarding action to maintain a defensible line.

### Points and Pace

Necromunda is a game about risk versus reward. Palanites reward careful mobility and disciplined fire, but that discipline can translate into a fragile tempo if you misjudge the flow of the game. If you’re new to scaling your list, start with a small cadre: 3–4 Enforcers, a Judge, and one or two specialists. This keeps you nimble enough to adapt while letting you experience both the law’s heft and the law’s paperwork backlog—yes, the power of penmanship can still swing a battle. As you gain experience, you can add more bodies, more rules interactions, and more “we know who you are” moments that will make your opponents glare at their own dice with a mix of fear and begrudging respect.

### Tactics: Positioning, Control, and When to Push

The Palanite playstyle tends to reward deliberate positioning. You want to establish lines of fire without exposing your flanks to flankers who would eat your lunch and then charge for dessert. Your best moves feel like a chess game played under bright neon, where every advance, retreat, and pivot is a small negotiation with gravity and fate. Here are a few practical tips:

- Use terrain to create choke points. The hallway with a vending machine can be a formidable defense when you have a line of sight on your opponent’s advance.
- Divide and disrupt. A sharp, well-timed push can draw your opponent into the open or force them to split their attention between two lanes of fire.
- Time your charges. Unlike other factions that sprint headlong into danger, Palanites excel when they strike as a coordinated unit after you’ve softened up the target with suppressive fire.

If you like narrative flavor, you can frame each skirmish as a lawful intervention gone slightly awry—like a jury trial where the verdict is still being drafted and the bailiffs are juggling plasma pistols. The result is a campaign-friendly experience where you can show the world that even the best intentions require a red stamp and a few carefully placed grenades.

### Painting and Personalization

Painting Palanites is a dream if you love clean lines and the glow of a neon sign. The armor plates are angular enough to look striking on the tabletop, and the insignia can be accented in your preferred precinct colors. For a quick-start paint scheme, you can try:

- Baseline: Leadbelcher for armor, a wash of Nuln Oil to bring out crevices, and Sarekh Green for cloth accents (to evoke that “city-stained, badge-wearing” vibe).
- Highlights: Layer Mist Grey or Ulthuan Grey on edges to catch the light in a way that screams “enforcer chrome.”
- Badges and details: Pick a color that pops—electric blue, crimson, or a bright copper—to mirror your district’s identity.

If you’re into conversions, Palanites are a forgiving canvas. A few spare pieces from other kits—like a helmet crest from a fantasy soldier, or a slightly larger pistol from a different faction—can turn a standard enforcer into a character who looks like they’ve issued a couple of thousand warrants this week alone.

## Thematic Coherence: Narratives You Can Believe In (Or At Least Pretend To)

The Palanites shine when you lean into the ideas of law, order, and the consequences of overzealous bureaucracy in a place where law is more of a suggestion than a reality. A campaign with Palanites can explore themes like:

- What happens when the law collides with outlaw culture? Does paperwork protect you from a plasma blast, or only from the retribution of a bored magistrate?
- How do you enforce a jurisdiction that spans multiple domes, each with its own flavor of corruption and its own gravitational pull? The Palanites can be the thread that ties disparate districts together, even if that thread is literally a chain of legal warrants.
- The moral grey area of policing: are Palanites heroes when they save a life, or villains when they suppress a riot with excessive force? Necromunda happily muddles the lines, and Palanites give you a respectable, slightly chaotic lens to view that muddle through.

If you want to run a narrative arc, consider missions that require collecting warrants, securing evidence, or transporting a “high-value criminal” to the precinct. The tension between moving quickly and obeying protocol can create memorable moments where every decision feels weightier than the last, and you get to dramatize the small, human moments in a world where a badge can buy you a little time but not a full cure for chaos.

## In-Game Interactions: How Palanites Meet the World

Necromunda isn’t a friendly party game, and Palanites don’t pretend to be. They will clash with gangs that move through the underhive without the same level of impulse control, and that clash can lead to some delightful table moments. Here are a few scenarios you’ll likely encounter:

- Ambush in a narrow corridor: The Palanites rely on discipline, so you’ll be tempted to break formation and improvise. Resist the urge; a well-placed volley through a closed doorway can save your skins—and your campaign’s reputation.
- The precinct vs the underhive: Some missions require you to secure an evidence cache, while your opponent tries to keep it out of your reach. The Palanites’ ability to assert control can tilt the balance, especially if you’ve brought along a specialist who can disable those pesky minefields.
- The slow burn capture: You might corner a target who begs for escape routes. Do you apply a surgical approach to take them in, or do you flood their position with suppressive fire and hope the law is kind enough to forgive your impatience?

The Palanites are at their best when the table leans into narrative tension and you’re watching every die roll as a vote cast in a grand, messy trial. It’s not always elegant, but it is intensely satisfying when a well-timed action ends up feeling heroic and a little ridiculous at the same time.

## List-Building Tips: Getting A Palanite Army On The Table

- Start small, grow slowly: Build a core squad of 3–4 Enforcers with a Judge as the anchor. Add a specialist or two to provide utility. Only then expand to a full complement.
- Balance firepower and control: You want rifles that can reach across a board, but you also want someone who can handle devices, trap doors, or light a fuse under the enemy’s plans.
- Think about upgrades: Gear like longer-range weapons, anti-armor tools, or specialized equipment can give you the edge in particular missions. Don’t be afraid to tilt your loadout to the campaign’s theme—space out your upgrades to preserve your squad’s flexibility.
- Terrain awareness: The Palanites’ effectiveness often rises in constrained environments where they can pin enemies behind cover and use line-of-sight discipline to your advantage.
- Practice the patience: Your instinct might be to sprint forward like a squad of sprinters in a race, but Necromunda rewards the patient planner. Let your enemies reveal themselves through their own missteps before you commit to your next, decisive move.

## Painting, Modeling, and Table Presence

From a hobby perspective, Palanites deliver a clean, crisp tabletop presence. The armor lines are sharp, the insignia readable from a good distance, and the miniatures balance well on typical Necromunda boards. If you’re a painter who enjoys a fast, satisfying slog, these kits reward you with satisfying results in a relatively short time. If you’re a conversion enthusiast, the model’s ample available space allows you to try a few edge-case combinations without losing readability or the essence of the squad.

I personally enjoyed giving a few of my Palanites a slightly more urban vibe by adding bits of scrap metal, little neon decals, and a couple of “district-specific” insignia that tell the story of their patrol route. It’s amazing how much character you can add to a squad by selecting a distinct color palette for the armor and a contrasting color for the badges.

For those who want a sense of scale: Palanites aren’t oversized monsters like some House Goliath conversions. They’re closer to the human scale in Necromunda, which keeps the game feeling gritty and plausible. You won’t be tempted to model them with absurdly oversized props or ridiculous spikes; instead you’ll lean into the practical elegance of a squad that embodies restraint without losing the sense of menace that a city with a gun problem deserves.

## Narrative Suggestions: Campaign Ideas Featuring Palanite Justicars

- The Clearance Mission: Your squad is tasked with clearing a warehouse district of informants who’ve hidden a data cache. Complications arise when the criminals start to show you mercy, and you must decide whether to proceed with a heavy-handed show of force or risk losing the trail.
- The Case of the Missing Warrant: A crucial warrant vanishes in transit. The Palanites must reconstruct the route, interview suspects, and balance speed with due process. The tension is delicious because you know the clock is always ticking in Necromunda.
- The District Collage: A festival of law and order goes wrong when a rival gang uses the Palanites’ presence as a cover to stage a breach. Your objective is to restore order while keeping your own leadership structure intact and not letting a minor riot become a full-blown city-wide calamity.
- The Substation Gambit: An underground substation is hosting illicit experiments that threaten the whole precinct. Your squad needs to secure the area, arrest key figures, and avoid a cascade of catastrophes that could escalate into a full-blown inferno.

These campaign seeds aren’t just a way to pad your game night; they’re a chance to explore the Palanites’ moral compass in a setting that rewards creativity and quick, disciplined thinking. You’ll emerge with stories that feel plausible in a universe where every arrest has a story and every story has a price.

## The Competitive Edge: How Palanites Stand Up in a Meta World

In the broader Necromunda scene, Palanites present a solid option for players who enjoy a methodical, control-oriented approach. They may not always deliver the flash of a heavy melee unit, but they have a dependable rhythm that can exploit an opponent’s missteps when you’re playing the long game. In a meta where speed and brutality often decide early rounds, Palanites offer a counterpoint: a faction that can tee off with controlled aggression rather than all-out assault. If you’re tired of risk-heavy gambits and want a more measured, narrative-friendly playstyle, Palanites deserve serious consideration.

That said, a few caveats apply. They can feel fragile against high-velocity, multi-shot weaponry or against enemies with mobility that outflanks their disciplined lines. The key is to leverage terrain, timing, and the occasional well-placed sabotage to keep those risks in check. The beauty of Palanites is that your win condition isn’t necessarily kinetic supremacy; it’s a story you can claim by the end of the game, even if the scrappy underdog managed to steal the moral high ground—and perhaps a victory biscuit or two.

## Final Verdict: Should You Build Palanite Justicars?

In a world where every faction brings its own flavor of apocalypse, the Palanite Justicars offer something grounded, a touch of bureaucratic charm in a place where charm is a scarce resource. They’re not the loudest or the flashiest in the short term, but they reward careful planning, patient execution, and a storytelling approach to warfare that can be incredibly satisfying. If you’re seeking a faction that lets you explore themes of law, order, and the cost of a long chain of warrants, Palanites will scratch that itch while giving you plenty of opportunities for characterful conversions and satisfying board presence.

Pros:
- Clear, cohesive theme and strong narrative potential
- Solid, reliable performance with a disciplined playstyle
- Great base kits for beginners and hobbyists who want an approachable project
- Excellent room for conversions and precinct-inspired customization

Cons:
- Can feel slower and less explosive than some other factions
- Stricter list-building constraints may frustrate players who want maximal crunch immediately
- Some players might struggle with the “bureaucracy” flavor that’s heavier than pure adrenaline

If you’re unsure where to start, pick up a small Palanite squad, test a couple of tactical combos, and let the list-building evolve as you play. The Palanites aren’t the loudest revolution in Necromunda’s underhive, but they are a patient, stubborn, and endearingly stubborn faction that can deliver surprise victories when you least expect it. They’re the kind of gang you want on your side when the city’s power grid flickers and the night’s mystery deepens—precisely the kind of moment Necromunda wants to give you.
