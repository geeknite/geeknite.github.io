## Overview: Palanite Justicars at a Glance

The Palanites are a police-like contingent in the Necromunda setting. In a city where crime is a constant, the Palanites position themselves as the thin blue line between the outlaws and the slow, psychotropic doom of bureaucratic eternity. They arrive equipped with a mix of pistols, melee tools, and the sort of goofily heroic swagger that invites a narrative dice roll to declare them the heroes of the hour—until the dice betray you and remind you that you are still playing Necromunda, a game where good intentions often collide with grenade launchers and questionable cover saves.

From a flavor perspective, the Palanites bring a refreshing change of pace. They are not the berserkers of Goliath, nor the streetwise hustlers of Escher. They are your compliant, law-abiding, paperwork-obsessed squad who believe that a well-timed citation can solve most problems—provided you survive the firefight long enough to fill out the form. The Palanites bring a different rhythm to your table: you balance the urge to enforce with the urge to survive, which in Necromunda basically amounts to the same thing with a different color of blood.

### In-Game Identity and Playstyle

Mechanically, the Palanites skew toward a discipline-focused list: steady, reliable firepower, controlled movement, and a fondness for sealing doors behind them like a not very friendly security system. The archetype rewards players who plan routes, control lines of sight, and use terrain to shield their enforcement squads while they slowly, methodically push forward. If you have ever played a game where you are told to move slowly and secure objectives, you will feel right at home with the Palanites, except in Necromunda where the floor can swallow you in a single sweep of a grenade template.

What they bring to the table that other gangs do not is a kind of lawful done right attitude. They are not just about blasting bad guys; they are about doctrine, order, and the notion that every action has a bureaucratic consequence. This can be endearing and maddening depending on whether your opponent loves lead flying or you are the GM who enjoys narrating a tense standoff with a lot of you need three more favors from the precinct to proceed. The Palanites thrive in objective-based play, where you are not just trying to outgun your opponent but out-schedule them. It is a refreshing change from pure slugfests and gives you a clean excuse to drag out an extra clipboard you have been hiding in your bag since middle school.

## Building a Palanite List: Practical Advice

### Core Units and Roles

A typical Palanite list balances frontline stability, controlled fire support, and meaningful close-quarters options. Core roles you will see in most builds:

- Enforcers: The workhorses. Durable, decently armed, and capable of threatening the front line with steady fire.
- Judges/Commanders: The leadership piece. They carry the aura of order and can buff the squad while dodging a bit of danger, making them excellent anchors for your formation.
- Specialists: Lighter on raw firepower but heavy on utility. Think investigators who can handle puzzles, disable devices, or provide meaningful buffs to survivability or range.

The trick is to design your list so your shooters are not babysitting a pair of stubborn heavies who refuse to die. A good Palanite squad has someone who can perform a quick, precise action—long-range shots, cover fire, or morale boosts—while the rest of the gang rearranges the boarding action to maintain a defensible line.

### Points and Pace

Necromunda rewards careful mobility and disciplined fire and can translate into a fragile tempo if you misjudge the flow of the game. If you are new to scaling your list, start with a small cadre: three to four Enforcers, a Judge as anchor, and one or two specialists. This keeps you nimble enough to adapt while letting you experience both the law has heft and the law has paperwork backlog—yes, penmanship can still swing a battle. As you gain experience, you can add more bodies, more rules interactions, and more moments that make opponents glare at their dice with fear and begrudging respect.

### Tactics: Positioning, Control, and When to Push

The Palanite playstyle rewards deliberate positioning. You want to establish lines of fire without exposing your flanks to flankers who would eat your lunch and dessert together. Your best moves feel like a chess game played under neon, where every advance, retreat, and pivot is a negotiation with gravity and fate. Practical tips:

- Use terrain to create choke points. A hallway with a vending machine can be a formidable defense when you have line of sight on your opponent’s advance.
- Divide and disrupt. A sharp, well-timed push can draw your opponent into the open or force them to split their attention between two lanes of fire.
- Time your charges. Unlike other factions that sprint headlong into danger, Palanites shine when they strike as a coordinated unit after you have softened up the target with suppressive fire.

If you like narrative flavor, frame each skirmish as a lawful intervention gone slightly awry, like a jury trial where the verdict is still being drafted and the bailiffs juggle plasma pistols. The result is a campaign-friendly experience where you can show the world that even the best intentions require a red stamp and a few carefully placed grenades.

### Painting and Personalization

Painting Palanites is a dream if you love clean lines and the glow of a neon sign. The armor plates are angular enough to look striking on the tabletop, and insignia can be accented in precinct colors. Quick-start paint scheme ideas:

- Baseline: Leadbelcher armor, a wash to bring out crevices, cloth accents in Sarekh Green (city-stained badge vibe).
- Highlights: Edge highlights with Mist Grey or Ulthuan Grey to catch the light and scream enforcer chrome.
- Badges and details: Pick a color that pops—electric blue, crimson, or copper—to mirror your district identity.

Conversions are a breeze. Spare bits from other kits can turn a standard enforcer into a character who looks like they have issued a couple thousand warrants this week alone. Palanites are not oversized monsters; they stay within a believable human scale, preserving the gritty, plausible feel of Necromunda.

## Thematic Coherence: Narratives You Can Believe In

The Palanites shine when you lean into ideas of law, order, and the consequences of overzealous bureaucracy in a place where law is more a suggestion than a reality. Campaign ideas that fit well with Palanites include:

- What happens when the law collides with outlaw culture? Does paperwork protect you from a plasma blast or only from a magistrate's retribution?
- How do you enforce a jurisdiction that spans multiple domes, each with its own flavor of corruption and gravity? The Palanites can be the thread that ties disparate districts together, even if that thread is a chain of warrants.
- The moral grey area of policing: are Palanites heroes when they save a life, or villains when they suppress a riot with excessive force? Necromunda loves muddling the lines, and Palanites give you a lens to explore that muddle with style.

If you want to run a narrative arc, consider missions that require collecting warrants, securing evidence, or transporting a high-value criminal to the precinct. The tension between moving quickly and obeying protocol can create memorable moments where every decision feels weightier than the last.

## In-Game Interactions: How Palanites Meet the World

Necromunda is not a friendly party game, and Palanites do not pretend to be. They will clash with gangs that move through the underhive with less impulse control, and that clash can lead to delightful table moments. Scenarios you may encounter:

- Ambush in a narrow corridor: The Palanites rely on discipline, so you will be tempted to break formation. Resist. A well-placed volley through a closed doorway can save your skins and your campaign narrative.
- The precinct vs the underhive: Some missions require you to secure an evidence cache while the opponent tries to keep it out of your reach. The Palanites' control ability can tilt the balance, especially with a specialist who can disable minefields.
- The slow burn capture: You corner a target who begs for escape routes. Do you apply surgical restraint or flood the position with suppressive fire and hope the law is kind? The table loves this tension when it unfolds as a lawful standoff rather than a chaotic firefight.

The Palanites work best when the table leans into narrative tension and you treat every die roll as a vote in a grand, messy trial. It may not be elegant, but it is intensely satisfying when a well-timed action ends up heroic and a little ridiculous at the same time.

## List-Building Tips: Getting a Palanite Army on the Table

- Start small, grow slowly: Build a core squad of three to four Enforcers with a Judge as the anchor. Add a specialist or two for utility. Only then expand to a full squad.
- Balance firepower and control: You want rifles with reach, plus someone who can handle devices, traps, or lock doors.
- Think upgrades: Gear like longer-range weapons, anti-armor tools, and specialist equipment can tilt the match in particular missions. Don’t be afraid to tailor loadouts to a campaign theme, spreading upgrades to keep flexibility.
- Terrain awareness: The Palanites shine in constrained environments where they can pin enemies and use line-of-sight discipline to your advantage.
- Practice patience: Necromunda rewards deliberate planning. Let enemies reveal themselves through missteps before you commit to a decisive move.

## Narrative Suggestions: Campaign Ideas Featuring Palanite Justicars

- The Clearance Mission: Your squad clears a warehouse district of informants who have hidden a data cache. Complications arise when criminals begin to show mercy, forcing you to choose between a heavy-handed show of force or risking losing the trail.
- The Case of the Missing Warrant: A crucial warrant vanishes in transit. The Palanites reconstruct the route, interview suspects, and balance speed with due process. Time pressure makes for great dramatic tension.
- The District Collage: A festival of law goes wrong when a rival gang uses the Palanites presence as cover to breach defenses. Your objective is to restore order while keeping leadership intact and preventing a riot from turning into a city-wide calamity.
- The Substation Gambit: An underground substation hosts illicit experiments that threaten the precinct. Your squad secures the area, arrests key figures, and avoids a cascading catastrophe that could spiral into disaster.

These seeds are not just padding for game nights. They are opportunities to explore Palanites moral compass and to produce stories that feel plausible in a universe where every arrest has a story and every story has a price.

## The Competitive Edge: How Palanites Stand Up in a Meta World

In the broader Necromunda scene, Palanites offer a robust option for players who enjoy a methodical, control-oriented approach. They might not always deliver the radiant speed of a gunline or the brute force of a melee monster, but they have a dependable rhythm that can punish missteps over the long game. In a meta dominated by speed and brutal engagements, the Palanites provide a counterpoint: a faction that can press a win through patient play and selective aggression rather than all-out sprinting

That said, there are caveats. They can feel fragile against high-velocity multi-shot weapons or enemies who out-maneuver their disciplined lines. The trick is to leverage terrain, timing, and the occasional well-placed sabotage to compensate for those weaknesses. The true beauty of Palanites is that your win condition is not simply raw kinetic dominance but a story you can claim at the end of the game, even if the underdog steals the moral high ground and a victory biscuit or two.
