## How to play: setup and flow
Playing UNO Canvas Billie Eilish is, at its heart, still UNO. The steps below reflect the canonical flow with a few optional flair tips for those who want to embrace the Canvas vibe.
- Setup: Shuffle the 108 cards, deal seven to each player, and place the remainder as a draw pile. Flip the top card onto the discard pile. If the first card is a Wild or special card, reshuffle and flip again to avoid starting with chaos.
- Turn flow: On your turn you must match either the color or the number. If you cannot, you draw a card from the draw pile and your turn ends. If you have one card left, you must shout UNO to signal impending victory.
- Special cards: Standard UNO action cards remain in play, offering Draw Two, Skip, Rewind, and Wild. The Billie Canvas variant adds decorative touches to certain cards but does not alter these mechanics. A few cards feature canvas motifs that can encourage fun storytelling moments, but the function stays the same: rule breathing room that does not get in the way of fast play.
- Collectible art cards: Optional. You can remove them entirely if you want a pure UNO experience, or you can cycle them in as a side display or a mini rhythm cue to spark conversation during quiet moments of the game.

The pacing remains brisk. Sessions feel short enough to fit into a lunch break or a coffee run, but long enough to let players settle into a rhythm and start trying goofy strategies that leverage the new visuals. The mood is upbeat and energetic, with a touch of pop culture flair that makes the table feel alive rather than like a formal board game night.
