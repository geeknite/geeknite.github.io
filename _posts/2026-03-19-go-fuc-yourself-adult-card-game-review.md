## Gameplay and Mechanics

### Card types and their roles

There are two major card types: prompt cards and action cards. Prompt cards set the scenario; action cards modify the round in unpredictable ways. The interaction between the two creates a dynamic that keeps the rounds fresh. Sometimes you have to pair a prompt that involves embarrassing yourself with an action that makes you perform a silly task. The combination is where the magic happens.

### How scoring works

The scoring system is intentionally simple to avoid turning your living room into a accounting office. Players earn points by completing prompts successfully and playing cards that fit the moment. There are usually a few house rules you can adopt to keep the scoring fair for your group.

### House rules and variants

The beauty of this game lies in its adaptability. You can adjust the length of rounds, decide how to handle near-misses, and even create your own custom prompts. Some groups introduce a 'round-robin punishment' for the worst-told joke, which can be a fun test of your ability to defend your sense of humor while the rest of the table rolls their eyes in unison.
