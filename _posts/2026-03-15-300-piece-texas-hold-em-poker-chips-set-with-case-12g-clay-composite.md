## Maintenance and care

### Cleaning and minor upkeep

Chips can collect fingerprints and dust after intense games. A simple wipe with a soft, dry cloth is usually enough to maintain their appearance. If you must clean, avoid soaking or submerging the chips; moisture can dull the edge texture and potentially affect color consistency over time. Store the chips in the case when not in use to minimize exposure to sunlight, which can cause slight fading in some color hues. For the avid collectors among us, a mild wipe with a damp microfiber cloth is a safe option, followed by air-drying away from direct heat sources. If you want to go the extra mile, you can treat the case interior with a light dusting of anti-static spray to prevent dust from clinging to the chips during high-energy sessions.

### Long-term care tips

- Keep the case closed between sessions to minimize stray chips wandering into exile under the sofa.
- Use chip racks or trays to avoid clumping when you are dealing with a full house of players.
- Re-check denominational labeling and edge designs every few months to ensure you haven’t developed a blind spot for certain colors.

## Comparisons and alternatives

### Clay composite vs ceramic vs plastic chips

Clay composite chips are often pitched as the middle ground between ceramic and plastic. Ceramic chips offer a very premium feel and exceptional durability but at a higher price point. Plastic chips are lighter and cheaper, often lacking the tactile feedback and heft you expect in a “serious” game. Clay composites aim to deliver a satisfying heft and a robust feel without the steep price of the pure ceramic varieties. If you want the best of both worlds — price and feel — a clay composite set like this one is a sensible choice for home gamers who want a table that looks and sounds like a pro’s table without the casino’s price tag.
