## What is Saboteur

Saboteur is a hidden roles card game that nests bluff and collaboration in a gold mining theme. Players take on the roles of dwarves digging a tunnel from a starting point toward treasure. Some players are treasuring seekers, others are saboteurs trying to derail the tunnel and steal glory for themselves. The twist is that the roles are secret, and everyone must read the table as the cards come out. The game unfolds in short rounds with a brisk tempo that keeps the energy high and the banter flowing.

The base game supports a range of player counts and adapts quickly to the mood of the table. You might find yourself laughing at a well placed rockfall card or gasping as someone lobs a last second path card that redirects the tunnel toward disaster. The Anniversary Edition preserves all of that while giving you improved visuals to go with your improved table talk. If you have ever tried to pretend to be supportive while playing a role you know is a disaster, Saboteur lets you live that dream without risking your actual social life.

The setup is simple enough for newcomers to grasp in a single turn and fast enough that veteran groups do not feel bogged down. ## Roles and bluff mechanics

Saboteur thrives on hidden roles and bluffing. The treasure seekers form a loose alliance to reach the treasure, but inside that alliance lie saboteurs who have their own secret agenda. The problem with secret agendas is that they inevitably collide with your own desire to win. The joy of Saboteur comes from carefully balancing cooperation and suspicion while you lay down path cards and decide when to play the all powerful action cards.

Path cards come in various shapes and connect to form a tunnel. The trick is that the tunnel must be connected between the start card and a treasure card to win the round. Any tangling misalignment shifts the ability to win and can doom an otherwise solid plan. Action cards serve as both tools and traps. A rockfall card can block a portion of the tunnel, stalling progress and creating room for misdirection. A tunnel repair card approximates a fix that can salvage a doomed run. Some action cards hinder others, including reveals of a players role at a critical moment. In practice this means the table becomes a subtle chess match of masking intent while sliding cards to shape the path toward or away from glory.

The number of saboteurs and treasure seekers scales with player count, ensuring that the table always feels precarious. With fewer players the game becomes a tight alignment of bluff and deduction, while with more players the table erupts in a chorus of hasty decisions and suspicious glances. The Anniversary Edition shines here by keeping the balance tight while giving players enough intelligence to read the table with minimal friction. The result is a satisfying blend of teamwork and treachery that can tilt in a dozen different directions in a single round.


## How to play in practice

The game unfolds in a series of short rounds that keep the table engaged without wearing out the welcome. Each round begins with role assignment, then the treasure seekers start their effort to connect the tunnels. The players take turns adding path cards to extend the tunnel. At any point in the round players may play action cards from their hand to influence the tunnel or to sabotage opponents. The first time a tunnel connects start to treasure, the treasure seekers reveal their cards and tally the treasure. If sabotaged, the round ends with the saboteurs scoring in other ways or the treasure seekers regrouping to try again in the next round.

Turn order moves fast with minimal downtime. The thrill of Saboteur comes from the subtle detection of who is bluffing and who is simply playing their cards close to the chest. The Anniversary Edition keeps this tempo by ensuring the pace remains quick and the options for players are easily digestible. You can expect rounds to run roughly 15 to 30 minutes depending on the size of the table, which means you can squeeze in several rounds in a single gaming session before attention spans drift toward snack breaks and memes.

If you want to maximize your odds as a treasure seeker, you will need to coordinate with your teammates while maintaining the secrecy of your own identity. In practice this means praising early collaboration, but never revealing too much of your strategy to one person at the table. If you want to maximize the chaos as a saboteur, you will lean into misdirection and cunning, buying time to lay down a strategic card that turns a potential win into a near miss. The Anniversary Edition rewards clever misdirection with crisp feedback and a tangible sense of progress as the tunnel slowly takes shape and then potentially collapses in a satisfying crunch of card play.


## Strategy tips for readers at every level

- For treasure seekers start with a plan but stay adaptable. Do not invest all trust in a single ally who may be a saboteur. Early cooperation can create momentum, but the moment you sense a diverging goal you must pivot to a safer path and prepare for a pivot card that may reset your progress.
- As a saboteur your best tool is timing. Do not reveal your role too early. The night you bluff your way into a blocked path is the night you win. The key is to create a narrative through card placement that makes others question their allies rather than you directly leaning into suspicion.
- Use rockfall cards to deliberately stall a path you know will be crucial for a win. This creates tension and buys you time for a more cunning play later in the round. Remember that stalling is a tactic, not a habit; you still need to deliver an ending that looks plausible to the table.
- Pay attention to the number of treasure cards in play and the visible progress of the tunnel. If the path seems too long for the given cards, you may need a bold move that changes the entire approach of the round. Confidence and calm humor in the face of a potential misplay often wins you the social game even when the path fails.
- In a crowded table, leverage the social element. A well timed joke or a casual aside can destabilize important reads from other players. Saboteur thrives on the social dynamic as much as the card play, so keep the mood lively and the jokes friendly. This is a game of shared chaos as much as private strategy.

Replayability in the Anniversary Edition is strong thanks to the hidden role mechanic and the variety of action cards. The new art and revised components also help with legibility and ease of play, reducing the time spent squinting at small icons and enabling more time for table talk and laughter.

## Variants and house rules

Geeks are notorious for tinkering, and Saboteur is no exception. The Anniversary Edition provides a solid canvas for custom rules that can stretch the life of the game. Here are a few variants you might enjoy experimenting with at your table:

- Double the treasure seekers: increase the number of treasure hunt rounds by adding a second treasure pool and a slightly longer tunnel. This variant invites more players to collaborate and increases the potential for dramatic swings.
- Quiet round variant: remove certain public cues to elevate misdirection. This can heighten tension and force players to rely more on reads and social cues rather than visible progress.
- Cooperative finale: in the last round, allow teams to pool information and discuss broad strategy while keeping individual roles hidden. This variant emphasizes social deception and conversation as the main engine of play.

As with any house rule, start with a small tweak and gauge player enjoyment before expanding to larger changes. The pastoral humor and communal vibe of Saboteur tend to shine when players are given just enough structure to keep everyone engaged while leaving room for creative bluffing.
