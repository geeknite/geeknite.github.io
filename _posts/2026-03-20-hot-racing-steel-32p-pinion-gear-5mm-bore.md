## What is a 32P pinion gear and why steel

### The basics
32P refers to the gear pitch—32 teeth per inch if you want to think in imperial units. In RC world, you measure with pins and bitches (just kidding about the last one). The point is: 32P is a common size for 1/10 and 1/8 scale transmissions, especially when you are chasing efficiency and reliability rather than bling-bling. A 5mm bore means the gear attaches to a 5 mm shaft or motor spline, which is a common spec for many RC motors and spurs.

### Steel vs aluminum vs titanium
The trade-off is simple: steel is heavier, harder, and less forgiving when it comes to friction heat. It resists wear, defies deformation, and if you drop your truck on an unearthly boulder, steel will still be standing with a groan. Aluminum spins more freely, runs cooler, and is friendlier to your wallet. Titanium is basically a myth—until you win the lottery and build a nuclear-powered pit crew. The steel 32P is the no-nonsense option for those who demand longevity under high torque loads. If you are running brushless with a 4S or 6S setup, this 32P steel pinion might be the anchor that steadies your drivetrain from screaming like a banshee on a hillside.

## Installation tips and maintenance

### Easy install steps
1) Remove the old pinion, if present
2) Clean the shaft and bore area to remove any burrs or dust
3) Slide the new 5 mm bore pinion onto the shaft
4) Tighten the set screw with a proper driver; do not over-tighten (or you will strip the thread and cry a little)
5) Check mesh with the corresponding spur gear; adjust as needed

### Mesh tuning tips
- Start with a light mesh and gradually tighten until you feel a slight resistance in turning by hand. If you hear a whine or see signs of rubbing, back off a touch. The goal is a quiet, consistent mesh.
- Use thread lock if your driving style is aggressive; this will prevent the pinion from walking off the shaft under load.
- If you notice rapid wear on the base of the teeth, re-check your spur gear alignment and ensure you have the correct tooth count for your gearing ratio.

### Maintenance routine
- After every session, visually inspect the teeth for chips.
- Wipe off dust and re-lubricate with a light pinion-specific oil; avoid heavy greases that attract debris.
- Check the bore and ensure the set screw remains snug. Do not ride the edge of the valley; you know the drill.

## Comparisons and alternatives

### Aluminum vs steel 32P pins
If your emphasis is lightweight performance and cost, aluminum is appealing. It dissipates heat faster and is easier to replace when you crash and bend the gear you wish you never attempted to spin. However, aluminum can wear faster under heavy torque and aggressive driving. The steel version offers better long-term durability, less wear, and higher resistance to deformation. The trade-off is weight and cost. For many drivers, the steel 32P is a fantastic upgrade that doesn’t require you to redo your entire drivetrain.

### Other brands
We also tested a few other brands in the same 32P bore class to evaluate performance. The conclusion: the geometry matters, and as long as the pitch and bore match your spur, you should be good. Your mileage may vary, and your local track might have its own quirks that make one brand feel better than another.

### 32P vs 48P vs 64P in general
- 32P is for fine torque control and smooth acceleration at a mid-high speed; it sits between power and efficiency.
- 48P is more common in smaller scale packs but translates to more teeth and more contact surface, leading to better wear resistance in heavy torque tasks but higher friction and weight.
- 64P is for micro machines and requires very precise minor tolerances and offsets; enthusiasts only, please, and hats off to you if you run this.
