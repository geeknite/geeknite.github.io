## Materials and construction: flat-woven realness
### Flat weave fundamentals
Flat-woven rugs are like the un-killjoy cousins of pile rugs: they lay flat, show patterns clearly, and refuse to puff up into a plush marshmallow whenever you step on them. The Sierra Diamonds rug uses a flat-weave construction that emphasizes the geometric motif with clean edges and crisp lines. The result is a rug that sits close to the floor, reducing the chance of tripping on a tuft or catching a sock on a stray loop. If you crave that “museum floor” look with a pragmatic, kid-pet-friendly backbone, flat weave is often the hero you’re looking for.

## Cleaning, care, and maintenance: keeping the diamonds crisp
### Routine maintenance
Regular vacuuming with a brushless attachment is usually enough to keep a flat weave tidy. Avoid rotating too aggressively around tight corners, as repeated sharp turns can push fibers out of alignment over time. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth rather than rubbing; this reduces the chance of pigment migration. If something stubborn sticks to the weave, use a mild detergent solution and a soft cloth, then blot dry. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability.

### Stain strategy by fiber type (general guidance)
- Water-based spills: blot, then clean with a mild soap solution.
- Oil-based stains: a gentle solvent recommended by rug care guides; always spot test first.
- Pet accidents: act quickly; neutral cleaners and enzyme-based solutions work well on many synthetic blends.

### Long-term care tips
Rotate the rug every 6–12 months to even out wear patterns and prevent any one edge from becoming a visual hotspot. If your space has heavy sunlight exposure, consider UV-protective window treatments to minimize color fade over the years. A rug pad not only reduces slip risk but also minimizes movement and wear, which means less maintenance in the long run and more time for you to nerd out about other interior-design conundrums.
