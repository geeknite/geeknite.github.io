## Cleaning, care, and maintenance: keeping the diamonds crisp
### Routine maintenance
Regular vacuuming with a brushless attachment is usually enough to keep a flat weave tidy. Avoid aggressive, sharp turns around tight corners, as repeated motion can disturb the fiber alignment over time. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth rather than rubbing, which can push pigment or fibers out of place. If something stubborn sticks to the weave, use a mild detergent solution and a soft cloth, then blot dry. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability and to avoid surprises.

### Stain strategy by fiber type (general guidance)
- Water-based spills: blot, then clean with a mild soap solution.
- Oil-based stains: use a gentle solvent recommended by rug care guides; always spot test first.
- Pet accidents: act quickly; neutral cleaners and enzyme-based solutions work well on many synthetic blends.

### Long-term care tips
Rotate the rug every 6–12 months to even out wear patterns and prevent a single edge from becoming a visual hotspot. If your space receives a lot of sunlight, consider UV-protective window treatments to slow color fade. A rug pad reduces slip risk and minimizes movement, which translates to longer-lasting pattern integrity and easier upkeep. Additionally, a rotating schedule helps you notice slight shifts in how light interacts with the blue-grey tones as seasons change.
