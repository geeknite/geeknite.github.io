### Building the Tower

Setup is straightforward: you build a solid tower by stacking layers of three blocks each, alternating direction with each layer, just like the classic Jenga. The rotating base sits beneath the entire structure, so as soon as you remove a block, the tower is always engaged with the platform to deliver that extra wobble you crave. The baseline is gentle wobble; the rules of gravity still apply, but there is a new variable to manage in every turn: will the base tilt toward your friend, your pet, or that one plant that acts as your good luck charm?

## How to Play

### Core Rules (H2)

- Players take turns removing one block from any level of the tower, excluding the topmost completed layer. The goal is to push your luck without triggering the tumble.
- After removing a block, place it on the top of the tower in the same orientation as the block above. The rotation base may cause the stack to shift subtly, increasing or decreasing the current level of risk.
- Once a block is placed on top, the turn ends and the next player begins. If the tower falls, the player who caused the tumble loses the round.
- The game continues until one player has all of the blocks toppled or you declare a winner by reaching a preset score, depending on house rules.

The presence of the rotating base introduces a delightful tension. It is not just about choosing the right block; it is also about calibrating the timing of your block removal to align with the micro-tilt of the base. The platform adds a kinetic layer that makes the decision tree slightly more chaotic, which is exactly what makes the game entertaining in a noisy, lively setting.

### Variants (H3)

- Solo Challenge: Play against a pretend opponent by forcing yourself to complete a self-imposed challenge, such as removing only from edges for the entire round. This variant tests your patience and discipline when the tower politely says no.
- Team Tumble: Pair up players, with alternating turns inside a team. Teams strategize to maximize risk on certain blocks, which can spark hilarious negotiations and friendly banter.
- Time-Limit Tumble: Introduce a timer that forces quick decisions, increasing pressure and laughter as players frantically decide which block to pluck under a ticking clock.
- Expert Mode: Remove a few blocks from the lower levels first; this simulates real-life Jenga tournaments where the base becomes more treacherous as the tower grows taller.

### Strategies and Tactics (H3)

- Block Type Awareness: Middle blocks are often safer to remove than outer ones, but removing from the middle can sometimes backfire faster as the top becomes more unstable. Learning to read the subtle cues from the base tilt is a crucial skill.
- Sequence Planning: Don t plan too far ahead. The tower has a memory of gravity, but the platform adds a new layer of unpredictability. A flexible plan outperforms a rigid one because the base will betray your expectations at least once per game.
- Rhythm and Tempo: Some players thrive by playing quickly to keep the other participants off-balance. Others savor a long, slow, methodical style to savor the drama. Both tempos can work; it just depends on your group dynamic.
- Edge vs Center Dilemma: Edges give you a preventive mindset but may produce a more dramatic tumble; centers offer a robust match that tests your bravery and precision. The trick is to know which mood your opponents are in and choose blocks accordingly.
