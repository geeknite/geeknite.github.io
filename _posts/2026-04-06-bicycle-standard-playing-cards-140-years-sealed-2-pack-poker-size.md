## Maintenance, Longevity, and Real-World Use Tips
- Storage: Keep the decks in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The ink and stock hold up better when not exposed to extreme heat, humidity, or sunlight for extended periods.
- Handling: Do not aggressively bend the cards back and forth, especially when new. Give them a gentle warm-up with a few fan flourishes before you go full-throttle in a social setting.
- Cleaning: If you need to clean fingerprints or smudges, use a very light microfiber cloth and a tiny amount of water. Do not soak the cards or use harsh solvents; that will ruin the finish and void the seal. It’s not a spa day for decks, but a clean deck still shuffles a little nicer.
- Maintenance reminders: Rotate your backups. If you’re a serious collector, you’ll want to keep a couple of backups in pristine condition so you can protect your pride-and-joy deck for the long run.
