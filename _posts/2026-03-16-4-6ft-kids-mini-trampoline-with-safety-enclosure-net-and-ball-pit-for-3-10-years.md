## Safety first: how to maximize safety without becoming a safety police maître d'

Safety is the magic word here. The enclosure net is designed to prevent those “surprised astronaut” moments when a bounce goes off-script. Key safety considerations include:

- Net height and door access: The net should be tall enough to contain a child during vigorous bouncing, with a door that latches securely. Teach your kid that the door is not a toy to swing on like a grappling hook.
- Padding: Foam padding around the frame and rails helps prevent injuries if a child slips or exits the trampoline unexpectedly.
- Jump mat tension: A well-tensioned mat should be taut but not so tight that it strains the fabric. Too loose, you lose bounce; too tight, and you risk tears from exuberant jumping.
- Weight limits and age guidance: Manufacturers specify a max user weight and a recommended age range. Always follow guidelines and supervise kids during use. If a tiny tornado tries to out-bounce an eight-year-old, remind them that physics class starts in the living room and safety comes first.

## Safety tips and best practices for sustained fun

- Supervise at all times. This isn’t a self-guided amusement ride; adults present ensure the plot twist remains child-safe.
- Learn the door etiquette. Use the door for entry and exit, not a swinging grappling hook.
- Check the structure regularly. Inspect bolts, springs, padding, and the net for wear. If you see a loose bolt, fix it before it becomes a gravity experiment.
- Use appropriate footwear or go barefoot depending on flooring and preference for squeakiness. The important thing is grip and comfort.
- Set limits. Older kids might feel the urge to attempt gravity-defying stunts. Consider limiting advanced maneuvers to reduce injuries.
- Keep the area clear. Remove sharp or heavy furniture nearby to avoid accidental collisions during enthusiastic maneuvers.
