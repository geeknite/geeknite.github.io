### Maintenance, cleaning, and durability
Cleaning the chair is straightforward: use a damp cloth to wipe the leather surface and a soft brush to clean dust from crevices. The footrest and relief nodes should be kept free of hair and debris to prevent blockages and ensure smooth motion. It is not a good idea to spill drinks directly onto the chair, as this will lead to sticky surfaces and potential electrical issues—basic common sense applies.

Durability-wise, most consumer-grade massage chairs are built to last several years with regular use. The most common points of failure tend to be motor wear and minor electronics in the remote or control panel. If you are buying with a longer-term investment mindset, consider models with a longer warranty window and accessible service options. ### Safety notes and best practices
Massage chairs can be very safe when used correctly, but there are some caveats:
- If you have a history of back surgery or acute back pain, consult a physician before using the chair on high-intensity settings.
- Do not use the heating function if you have impaired sensation, as you may not notice overheating.
- Always be mindful of posture: the chair is a tool to help align the body, not a substitute for a good sitting habit. If you feel numbness or unusual pain, stop and adjust.
- Keep the chair away from water and ensure proper electrical grounding. If you live in a home with flaky electrical storms, unplug during heavy weather to avoid any unfortunate surprises.
