## Safety and maintenance tips
Winches are heavy, powerful devices with the potential to cause serious injuries if misused. Always wear gloves when dealing with rope and wire, keep bystanders at a safe distance, and never stand in the line of the rope when it's under tension. Regular maintenance includes checking the rope for frays, ensuring the drum is properly aligned, and keeping the electrical connections clean and dry. Synthetic rope loves to absorb moisture, so you might want to dry it after a wet excursion. Lubrication on the drum and gear train helps reduce wear, but avoid over-sealing; moisture evaporates, rust returns, and your neighbors will hear your curses across the street.

## Cleaning, storage, and longevity
After a day on the trail, give the winch a quick inspection. Wipe away mud, check the rope for kinks, and inspect the wiring for corrosion. The remote, being a small electronics device that loves to be left on the ground, should be tucked away in a dry place. If the weather gets gnarly, spray a little rust inhibitor on exposed metal surfaces, though be mindful not to drown the electrical components in lubricants that suck up dust like a black hole. The key to longevity is consistent maintenance and a modest amount of common sense: if water pooling around the mount looks suspicious, stop, dry, and test in a dry environment before charging back into the wilderness.
