## Setup and play: the mood for two

Setting up for a couple is basically the same as a party game with two players, but with a more intimate pace. Here is a quick recipe:

1) Unfold the table, preferably in a cozy corner that has a soft light or a lava lamp—whatever screams date night vibes.
2) Shuffle the word cards and lay out a 5x5 grid as instructed by the rulebook. In a sealed edition, this ensures you are starting fresh, not dealing from a deck that has seen more parties than your favorite fan convention.
3) Randomly assign spymasters and the partner roles. In a two player setup, you can switch roles mid game to keep things lively, or keep a running tally of how many clues did we need to win for bragging rights.
4) The spymaster gives a one word clue followed by a number, indicating how many words on the grid relate to the clue. The other player tries to guess; if they guess a word belonging to the opposing team, it counts as a mistake. The catch here is that there are also neutral words and the dreaded assassin word, which ends the round immediately. This is where the tension and the humor happen, particularly if you both end up choosing something wildly off base and then get a vote of confidence from your partner that you are still a genius despite everything.

This is where the adult angle comes into play. The clues can be flirt y, light-hearted, or inside jokes that only you two share. The goal is to read your partner s mind with a single word, which is basically a trust exercise with a dash of improv. If your partner snorts with laughter at your clue, you know you have a keeper relationship. If they wince, well, it makes for memorable storytelling at future game nights.

## Practical tips for date night success

- Start with a light clue: For example, one of you might say clue words that evoke a memory or shared interest. You will be surprised at how often simple cues beat a long-winded clue.
