## Maintenance Tips and Pro Hacks

- Vacuum regularly to prevent the pile from flattening and to keep the color looking crisp. A low-suction setting can help avoid pulling fibers loose over time.
- Address spills quickly. Blot—do not rub—with a clean, dry cloth to lift liquids before they set. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution tested on a small area first is a safe approach.
- Rotate the rug periodically. This helps it wear evenly and prevents any obvious asymmetry from high-traffic zones.
- Use a rug pad or underlay for extra grip and more even wear. It also softens the bottom loud thud when your cat leaps onto the couch. Your floors get protection, and your cats get a dramatic stage for their own dramatic entrances.
- Groom fibers occasionally with a brush or comb to restore fluff and prevent matting. This step is particularly helpful after heavy use or after a game night with extra snacks and prying hands.

If you want to save time, you can also schedule a quarterly deep clean and use a professional service once a year if your budget allows. The rug will thank you in the form of consistent plushness and a floor that no longer yells at you every time you walk into the room.

## Care and Longevity: A Quick Reference

- Regular vacuuming is a must. Use a vacuum with adjustable pile height and gentle suction to avoid fiber pull.
- Spot clean promptly. For tougher stains, a mild detergent and water solution tends to work well on most synthetic blends used in shag rugs.
- Rotate the rug every few months. This helps even out wear and ensures no one side looks dramatically more worn than another.
- Consider a rug pad underlay for grip and added comfort. It reduces bunching and helps with anti-slip properties on slick floors.
