## The Game in a Nutshell

Exploding Kittens is a fast-paced, player-elimination style card game designed for 2-5 players in its original form, with expansions boosting the headcount to larger groups. The core mechanic is superhero-simple and devilishly effective: don’t draw the Exploding Kitten card. If you do draw one and you can’t defuse it with a Defuse card (or some wild, house-rule workaround), you’re out of the round. The tension builds with each draw pile reshuffle, each strategic hand play, and each dramatic last-minute defusal attempt. The result is a game that feels like a chaotic party game but often reveals surprising depth as players mind game their opponents, bluff their way through a bluffing round, and gamble on whether the cat is bluffing or genuinely ready to detonate.

The start is simple: a few passes around the table and a quick read-through to ensure everyone knows what a Defuse does (and when to pretend to be heroic rather than stealthy).

As with many party games, the moment you reveal that everyone’s trying to outplay one another is when the table starts to hum. ## How to Play: The Core Loop

### Basic Turn Structure

- The active player draws a card from the draw deck. If they draw an Exploding Kitten and have a Defuse, they can use the Defuse to survive and place the Kitten back into the deck at a location of their choosing (for strategic mind games). If they do not have a Defuse, they’re out and the remaining players continue.
- After drawing, the player may play any number of action cards to affect the order of the deck, steal cards from others, or skip their turn. This is where the bluffing, misdirection, and occasional chaos all start to glow.
- The round continues with players attempting to avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten while thumbing their noses at opponents with witty plays and clever deflections.

### The App’s Take on the Core Loop

The free app version tends to reproduce the basic mechanics with extra touches: a virtual hand that’s readable on a phone screen, automated deck management, and optional animations that illustrate explosions with silly cat art. The app also adds features such as pass-and-play modes for multi-device table setups, which can be convenient when some players aren’t fully bought into the “almost certain doom” vibe yet want to join in.

### House Rules and Fine Print

One of the joys of Exploding Kittens is its flexibility. The official rules cover a standard game, but many groups introduce small house rules that shift the balance—such as allowing more Defuses early on, or adding a “no repeats after you play an effect” clause to prevent endless cycles of card reuse. The app can sometimes feel overbearing with its automation, but it’s surprisingly good at respecting simple house-rule variants, so long as you keep the card-counting faithful to the spirit of the deck.
