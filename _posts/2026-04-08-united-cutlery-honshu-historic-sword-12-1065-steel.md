## Maintenance and Care
### Keeping your Honshu blade happy
Maintenance for a decorative blade like this is modest but worthwhile. Wipe the blade with a soft, dry cloth after any handling to prevent fingerprints and potential corrosion. If the blade has a protective coating, you may use a light, food-safe oil to maintain the finish and prevent oxidation over time. If you’re storing the blade, ensure it’s in a dry environment and away from direct sunlight, which can degrade wooden handles and finishes over extended periods.

Cleaning the wood handle should be a gentle affair—avoid soaking or using harsh solvents. A little wood-safe conditioner or a dry microfiber cloth can keep the grip looking rich and natural. For display stands, consider a soft velvet or felt pad to protect the blade and handle from abrasions that could accumulate over months of sitting on a shelf.

### Longevity expectations
This Honshu model is built to be a reliable display piece for years. It won’t retain showroom sharpness or stand up to heavy-duty use, but it will remain visually striking and a conversation starter. If your collection centers on aesthetics and nostalgia rather than practical blade work, this is a sensible addition that won’t demand constant maintenance or specialized care.
