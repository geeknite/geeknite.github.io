## Durability, Maintenance, and Long-Term Use

### Simple Maintenance Routine
A belt that’s well-lubricated lasts longer and performs more consistently. Here’s a quick maintenance routine you can perform about every 3–6 months or after every 60 hours of use, whichever comes first:
- Wipe down the deck and belt after workouts to remove sweat and oils.
- Lubricate the belt per manufacturer instructions if the belt feels dry or creaky.
- Check belt tension and alignment; adjust if the belt starts to drift to one side.
- Inspect the folding mechanism for any looseness or squeaks and tighten screws as needed.

### Wear and Tear Considerations
With 300 lb capacity and a foldable design, the frame and joints should hold up well with regular maintenance. The key is to avoid dropping the treadmill or slamming the deck shut without engaging the locking mechanism. If you store the unit in a high-traffic area, consider a floor mat to dampen vibrations and prolong the life of the belt and deck.

## Safety First: Keeping It Safe in a House Full of Gadgets

Safety features on folding treadmills commonly include:
- A magnetic safety key that cuts power if detached.
- A stop/reset button for quick interrupts in case of wobble or distraction.
- Non-slip feet that keep the base planted on hard floors and protect your floors from scuffing.

If you’ve got kids or pets, place the treadmill in a room with a door you can close, and keep the safety key within arm’s reach. It’s not just a fitness device; it’s a small piece of your routines you should respect as you would any other appliance with moving parts.

## Practical Tips for Getting the Most from This Treadmill
