## Maintenance and care tips
- Clean after use: wipe down with a dry cloth and lightly oil the blade to prevent corrosion.
- Dry the leather sheath after exposure to moisture to prolong its life and preserve its patina.
- For the handle, a gentle wipe to remove dirt or debris keeps your grip feeling fresh rather than gritty.
- Sharpening: a routine maintenance schedule includes occasional touch-ups to maintain edge geometry without overdoing it. A coarse stone for reshaping, followed by a finer stone for finish work, will help keep the edge ready for action without eroding the profile of the blade.
- Storage: keep the blade dry and away from humid environments when not in use to minimize rust risk on any exposed metal areas.
