### What is Exploding Kittens?

Exploding Kittens is a 2-5 player card game built for quick learning, quick rounds, and quick emotional swings. The core idea is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card unless you can Defuse it. The Defuse cards save you from certain doom, and the rest of the deck is filled with various action cards that let you peek at the deck, attack others, skip turns, or force someone else to draw more. The art is gloriously over-the-top, the explosions are cartoonishly cute, and the whole vibe is intentionally chaotic in the best possible way. It’s a game that thrives on the human element—the jokes, the glares, the triumphant grins when a Defuse lands at just the right moment.

### Components and Setup

- A deck of Exploding Kitten cards
- A deck of Defuse cards
- A handful of tokens (for defusing fun and bragging rights)
- A compact rules cheat sheet for the forgetful among us

Setup is a breeze: shuffle the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck (one fewer than the number of players), shuffle the Defuse cards with the rest, deal two cards to each player, and place the remainder in the middle. The goal is simple: survive longer than everyone else until you’re the last kitten standing. But the beauty of setup is how little it asks for: a flat table, a curious mind, and a willingness to surrender a bit of pride to the table gods of chaos.

### How to Play (2-5 Players)

Exploding Kittens rewards speed and social cunning. On your turn you can play one of several action cards or simply draw from the deck. The Exploding Kitten card ends your run unless you can Defuse it. Defuse cards typically let you defuse a potential disaster and reposition the kitten in a way that influences the timing of future draws. The Nope mechanic adds a delicious layer of cat-chase diplomacy: any player can shout Nope to cancel an action, which often leads to an uproarious back-and-forth about whether a move counts as a legitimate action.

Two core mechanics drive the experience:
- Defuse: Your lifeline. It buys you a moment to strategize and buy time to survive the next draw.
- Exploding Kitten: The ultimate “oh no” moment. If you can’t Defuse, you’re out of the game.
- Nope: The counterspell that makes every turn feel like a courtroom drama in a living room.

With two players, it becomes a tense duel where every draw counts and the fate of your defuse headcount is a hot topic. With three to five players, the table transforms into a chaotic social catastrophe where alliances form and dissolve in the blink of an eye. The humor and pace stay intact across player counts, which is a big win for a family game night.

### Strategy and Social Dynamics

Sure, it’s a party game, but you’ll still see players thinking: which card should I defend against? Should I push for a risky move or pass the turn back into the deck’s chaotic maw? Here are practical angles:
- Track Defuses: If you suspect Defuse cards are running low, you can apply pressure with riskier plays, knowing the window to defuse is narrowing.
- Use Nope judiciously: Timing is everything. A well-placed Nope can save you, sink a rival, or spark a hilarious meltdown when mis-timed.
- Read table psychology: People will bluff, over-assert, and celebrate falsely—watch their body language, or at least the way they lean into the table after drawing a card they’re sure is a Defuse.
- Card economy: Some actions are one-time uses; others float between rounds. Don’t waste a powerful card on a small likelihood of success.

### Playtime, Pacing, and House Rules

Base rounds run about 15-20 minutes, depending on the group. If you’re hosting a mini-tournament, aim for three rounds with a short break in between to maintain energy. House rules can be as light or as strict as your crew desires:
- Limit the Nope to after the initial draw, to keep the early turns moving.
- Introduce a 30-second timer per turn to keep pace and reduce analysis paralysis.
- Allow a one-time “peek” option per player per round for a dramatic, almost cinematic reveal of the next few cards.

### Accessibility and Inclusion
