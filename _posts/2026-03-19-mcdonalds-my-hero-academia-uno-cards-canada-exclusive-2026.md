## Practical tips for extended life and fun
- Use protective sleeves on the cards if you plan to play frequently with friends who are a little rough with their elbows. The sleeves protect both the deck and the surface you play on. 
- Create a house rule that allows a hero card to be used on a stash of two or more cards that you want to discard. It is a fun way to escalate tension without breaking the core rules. 
- Keep a small organizer with the deck to store extra cards, tokens, and any rule emblems that come with the set. A neat layout makes all the difference on a busy game night. 
- Play a themed marathon with bite sized anime episodes. It is a great way to keep energy high and conversation lively while you rotate rounds. 
- Add a timer to speed up play so you can fit in multiple rounds in a single session without dragging the night. 
- Use the show theme to create a team mode where you pair up and coordinate hero power cards for dramatic finishes. 

### House rule experiments
If you want a more dramatic tweak, try a rules variant where hero swaps are allowed only when the active player has at most 3 cards. That creates thrilling comeback moments that feel like arc climaxes rather than routine resets. Another option is to add a redraw option for players who draw three cards, allowing a swap with any player instead of just a partner. The key is to keep things light and fun while preserving the core UNO flow.
