### Cable management: the art of not launching your monitor into orbit

The Eternal desk ships with channeling options that could host a small orchestra of power strips, HDMI extenders, and USB hubs. ## Ergonomics, comfort, and the five‑person reality check

### Ergonomic challenges with a long, shared desk

Five people, one surface, and a single screen means you’ll encounter the classic ergonomic trap: the shared space creates conflicts about monitor eye level, chair height, and typing distance. The solution is a mix of personal monitor arms, adjustable chairs, and a shared agreement about “zone ownership.” Each seat should have a clearly marked micro‑workspace, with a designated place for laptop stands, notepads, and the mysterious half‑eaten energy bar you found in the bottom of your backpack.

If you’re concerned about neck strain during long meetings, you’ll want to install monitor arms that offer at least +30 degrees of tilt, and ensure the screen is not perched too high. The center seat will inevitably become the de facto “captain’s chair,” and the captain is not always the most ergonomic person in the room. Good luck to you, Captain Clean Desk.

### Comfort in a sprawling shared setup

Comfort isn’t an afterthought here; it’s the entire premise. The Oak Blck finish adds a tactile warmth that makes the desk feel premium enough to justify the effort. However, with a 9‑meter surface at play, comfort also means foot placement, leg room across multiple users, and a shared habit of turning the heat up too high during winter meetings. The result is a space that begs for a rotating cast of chairs and a rotating schedule for who sits where. If you can implement a fair seating plan, you’ll maximize productivity; if not, you’ll discover a surprising spectrum of personal space boundaries and a few well‑timed glances when someone encroaches on another person’s keyboard angle.
