# City of Mist: Core Book — A Velvet-Noir, Myth-Mashed Metropolis

City of Mist (CoM) is the kind of RPG that makes you lean in and say, “Wait, what if Batman fought Zeus in a subway tunnel and their capes were patterns in the fog?” It’s a game that wears a detective’s coat with confidence, but the lining is stitched from myths and myths’ mischief. The Core Book is the ticket to stepping into a city where every alley could be a legend-in-waiting and every citizen is defined by a mask—one you can take off and reveal a different motif of power underneath. If you like your mystery served with a side of cosmic swagger and your heroes as much myth as man, City of Mist welcomes you with open trench-coats.

## Overview: What City of Mist Is Trying to Do
City of Mist is not your standard superhero RPG. It’s not about capes or speed-rushing zaps through the skyline. It’s about the mythic underpinnings of modern life—the ideas, symbols, and stories that feel bigger than a single person can contain. The Core Book codifies a setting where the city is a character, the myths are real, and the people who live in between are the ones who make the myth real in the first place.

- The game frames power as two layers: a personal identity (your day-to-day mask) and a mythic identity (the Power beneath the mask). Think Batman and the bat-logos are not just gadgets but a living emblem of who you are becoming.
- The rules system is queerer than a standard RPG: a heavy emphasis on narrative prompts, tags, and a flexible dice mechanic that favors storytelling momentum over raw mechanical perfection. It’s Powered by the Myth, not strictly Powered by the Apocalypse, though it shares a sibling vibe with the indie RPG scene—emphasis on character voice, theme, and the city as a living, breathing antagonist.
- The art direction leans into noir elegance and mythic flare. It’s not a glossy blockbuster aesthetic; it’s a tasteful, slightly grainy homage to crime novels, street photography, and mythic propaganda posters. The pages invite you to scribble, crank, and doodle logos and clues in your own margins.

If you’re here for a crunchy power-structure spreadsheet, this isn’t the book for you. If you’re here to weave a city where every clue is a fragment of a legend, and your player’s real-world life folds into a fantastical pattern, you’ll feel right at home.

## The Core Mechanics: Powered by the Myth (Sort Of)
In City of Mist, the core system isn’t about who has the bigger die pool or who rolls more tens. It’s about how you apply your two layers of identity to the obstacles in front of you. The “Mythos” framework is the engine that drives action, while “Tags” represent the bits of story you can lean on to tilt the narrative toward a heroic or tragic outcome.

- Tags: Think of Tags as your character’s kit—skills, resources, and associations that you can invoke to gain an advantage, create complications, or bend the rules of reality. They come in a few flavors: Real World Tags (your day job, street-smarts, social networks) and Mythos Tags (the symbolic power of your masked identity—your Logos, your signature weapon or sigil, etc.). The art of the game is knowing when to lean on which tag and how to mix them for narrative effect.
- Dice: The actual dice mechanic is approachable and forgiving. You roll the number of dice equal to your relevant Tags and compare the results to a target number that represents the scene’s difficulty and the GM’s plan. A success is not a binary pass; it’s a chance to shape the story: you attain a consequence, reveal a clue, or reveal a larger mythic truth.
- Clues and Logos: Clues are how you peel back the mystery layer by layer. Logos are city-wide symbols that imbue characters with mythic potential and reveal the greater pattern to the GM. The book does a nice job of teaching you to interlace personal stakes with city-wide legends so the players feel like active participants in a living myth, rather than simply solving a puzzle.

The book’s design encourages “soft rules” that can bend to the story’s demands. If your group loves on-the-fly storytelling, CoM’s system rewards you with narrative leverage rather than punishing you with procedural rigidity. The result is a game that feels cinematic—think crime-noir meets mythic fantasy, with the city as the co-conspirator.

If you’re a GM who loves resource books with a strong visual language, the Core Book’s physicality sells the game before you even crack the spine. And if you’re a player who wants the flavor first and the mechanics second, you’ll find the beauty of the book an invitation to dive into the world that’s being built around you.

## Character Creation: The Dual Identity Playground
One of CoM’s signature joys is the character creation ritual. It’s not just “pick a class, then pick a power.” It’s building two legible identities that are layered and sometimes at odds with each other, then threading them through the city’s myths.

- The Real World: Your character’s day-to-day identity. Occupation, relationships, and ordinary goals. This is where the grit lives—the mundane textures that make the masks plausible.
- The Mythos: Your character’s secret identity, the Logos that defines their mythic power, and the symbol they embody in the city’s lore. This is not just a power set; it’s a narrative wellspring from which you draw your actions and your moral questions.
- The Masks: Your public-facing persona—the way your character presents themselves to the world. Masks are not deception in CoM; they’re essential tools that reveal the character’s deepest fears, desires, and loyalties. Choosing and changing masks is how you chart character arc across a campaign.
- The Powers and Clues: The book provides a toolkit of powers that feel mythic and thematic rather than “do this one thing really well.” You’ll pick a set of powers that align with your Masks and Logos and then use Clues to steer investigations toward meaningful discoveries.

The process is collaborative and often a little ridiculous—in the best possible way. It’s a moment when the table leans in, and everyone suddenly understands that the city itself has a dream you’re about to wake.

Players who adore character-centric RPGs with a strong theme will revel here. The Core Book’s methods encourage you to think about who your character is when the mask is on, and who they are when the mask is off, and how the city’s stories pull them back into the mythic fold.

## Thematic Experience: Noir, Myth, and the City Itself
If you want a game that feels like stepping into a crime novel edited by gods, City of Mist delivers. The tone is not about heroism in the grandiose sense; it’s about moral ambiguity, personal cost, and the tension between everyday life and legendary possibility.

- Noir core: The city is fogged in, the streets are slippery with secrets, and even the “good guys” carry flaws that make you squint and ask, “Who’s really watching whom?” CoM treats danger as a mood, not a stat block. Suspense rises from character choices and the consequences that spill from them rather than from a purely mechanical threat level.
- Mythic overlay: The Logos injects a mythic energy into the city’s fabric. Symbols aren’t just aesthetic; they are engines. They reframe ordinary events so that the reader and the players ask: What story is the city trying to tell, through you?
- The city as a partner: The GM is guided to treat the city as a character with its own desires and rhythms. Clues, motif hints, and city-wide “events” push the scene toward a larger pattern, weaving a tapestry where the endgame isn’t “defeat the villain” but “discover how the city has chosen to reveal its truth.”

The result is a game that treads a careful line between grounded investigation and mythic upheaval. It’s possible to run a grounded noir mystery where people have real consequences, and it’s equally possible to stage a mythic crescendo in which symbols come alive and shape the city’s fate. The Core Book gives you a toolkit to blend those modes without forcing a single answer on the table.

## GM Tools: Making the Myth Talk Back
A good GM book is a map and a weather report. CoM’s Core Book excels in both, offering practical guidance while inviting improvisation.

- Session scaffolding: The book walks GMs through session pacing that leans into investigation, escalation, and revelation. There’s a rhythm to the scenes that keeps players engaged without ever feeling like the game is constraining them to a prewritten script.
- City-building prompts: There are prompts and mechanics for creating a city with unique Logos and Mythoi. You can populate a district with a specific corporate emblem and a mythic undercurrent, which becomes a natural well for plot hooks and faction tensions.
- Clue flow and investigation structure: Clues aren’t simply “hints” to the player; they’re nodes in a mythic chain. When players pick up one clue, it ripples through the Logos and the city’s patterns, pulling threads that connect to other clues, NPCs, and even future episodes.
- NPCs and factions: The Core Book presents archetypal factions with motives and symbolic hooks, giving GMs ready-made antagonists and allies in the city’s mythic ecosystem. You’re not forced to improvise every character from scratch; you can customize the existing templates to fit your campaign’s vibe.

If you’re a GM who loves a flexible framework with generous room for improvisation, CoM’s GM guidance is a treat. It respects your players’ voices and gives you a robust playground to choreograph scenes that feel both personal and epic.

## Strengths, Trade-offs, and What It Feels Like to Play
- Strengths:
  - Thematic coherence: The interplay of masks, Logos, and real-world identities gives each scene a clear through-line and a sense of inevitability about the city’s design.
  - Narrative leverage: Players aren’t constrained by purely numerical success; they unlock consequences that push the story forward, which keeps sessions feeling cinematic and meaningful.
  - Aesthetics that inform play: The book’s art and layout aren’t mere decoration; they’re prompts and mood-setters that help players inhabit the mythic city.
  - Accessibility for newcomers: While there are rules, the core experience is about storytelling, not surgical rule clarity. New players can jump in with the vibe and learn as they go.
- Considerations:
  - The system is more about storytelling synergy than crunchy optimization. If you crave the most granular combat simulations, you’ll find CoM lighter on that brass-tin detail and heavier on narrative outcomes.
  - Campaign pacing leans toward mystery arcs rather than long, power-scaling campaigns. It shines most in episodic play and one-shot arcs with a mythic spine.

For groups who love noir, mystery, and myth in equal measure, City of Mist’s Core Book is a treasure chest. It invites players to participate in shaping a living city where legends are not a backdrop but a driver of the action. It rewards players who intertwine character growth with the city’s mythic cycles and punishes no one by forcing a single “correct” path. Instead, it gently nudges everyone toward a story where the masks matter—and where the mystery of the city is the true star.

## Comparisons: Why CoM Stands Out (And Where It Sits Among Its Peers)
- City of Mist vs Masks (Powered by the Apocalypse family): Both games celebrate character-driven storytelling, but CoM leans into the mythic layer more overtly. Masks is more about the evolving teen superhero vibe; CoM is noir with a cosmic tilt. If you liked Masks’ vibe but wanted more city-scale symbolism and a noir atmosphere, CoM scratches that itch more directly.
- CoM vs World of Darkness: WoD has tone and texture, but CoM emphasizes a city’s symbol-driven power and the hero’s dual identities in a more accessible way. If you want to explore awe without giving up the gritty, CoM’s balance often feels more approachable for a mixed table (new players and veteran storytellers alike).
- CoM vs other indie games in the vein of narrative tumblers: It sits well in a hall of games that prize story momentum and thematic clarity. It’s not a math puzzle; it’s a map of legends you and your group decide to draw in the air. The Core Book’s guidance helps you stay on the rails without cramping your creativity.

If you’re deciding between this and a few other titles, ask yourself: Do I want a city that speaks to me as a myth? Do I want dual identities to drive character arcs? Do I want a toolset that invites collaboration with the GM to craft an evolving, mythic metropolis? If yes, the Core Book will feel like a launchpad rather than a manual.

## What You Get (In Practice) and How It Feels at the Table
With the Core Book in hand, you’ll soon discover that CoM isn’t just “a game about masks.” It’s a design philosophy: that myth and city life aren’t separate layers but two strings of the same guitar, played together to sing a louder story. The players strum the strings through their two identities, and the GM strums the city’s extra chords—the Logos, the Clues, the city-wide tensions—that create harmonies or dissonances depending on the group’s rhythm.

This is a game that rewards talking and listening as much as rolling dice. The mechanics exist to support conversation: a way for a player to coax a scene into revealing a deeper truth; a way for a GM to pivot away from a dead end by leaning on a Logos or a clue that reframes the problem. You’re not “fighting the system”; you’re dancing with the system and letting the music of the city guide your steps.

The Core Book’s structure—concept, mechanics, GM guidance, and ready-to-play seeds—makes it a strong purchase for both new players and veterans. If you’re a GM starting a campaign, the book offers seed ideas and a framework you can adapt across arcs. If you’re a player who wants to dive into a dual-identity character that feels like a living postcard from a mythic city, this is the book that helps you find your voice.

In terms of content density, it’s surprisingly generous.
