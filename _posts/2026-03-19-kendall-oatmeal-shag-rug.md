## Practicality: Cleaning, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Cleaning basics
One of the most important aspects of rug ownership is maintenance. The Kendall rug cleans relatively easily for a shag. Regular vacuuming, preferably with a beater brush turned off, helps collect dust and hair without tangling the fibers. For spills, the standard approach is to blot rather than rub, using a clean cloth to prevent staining. Yet life being life, you will still encounter coffee mishaps or juice storms on board game night. In those moments, a mild detergent solution and quick blotting will help restore the fibers to their previous fluffiness.

### Dealing with shedding and fuzz
Shag rugs in general shed a little during the first weeks. The Kendall is not dramatic in shedding, but a persistent, light shedding is normal as the fibers settle into the backing. Regular vacuuming and occasional fluffing with your hands or a gentle brush will help maintain a consistent look. If you discover a stray fiber that seems particularly rebellious, use a fabric comb or a lint brush to coax it back into line. It is a little rebellion, but a friendly one.

### Longevity and wear patterns
The quality of the fibers and the backing means this rug can withstand everyday life for years. It is not a one-season wonder; it has the stamina to keep up with kids, pets, and the occasional gaming marathon. Over time, you will notice a softening in high-traffic paths, which adds to the cozy atmosphere rather than detracting from it. If you are planning a big move or a renovation, the Kendall rug is portable enough to be rolled and carried, yet sturdy enough to survive the rigors of a new home.

### Deep cleaning considerations
Every rug benefits from periodic deeper cleaning. A professional cleaning every couple of years or a thorough DIY approach with a rug shampooer can refresh the fibers and return the plush feel. The key is to avoid over-wetting the backing, which can lead to moisture retention issues. If you opt for DIY cleaning, test a small area first and follow the manufacturer guidelines. The result should be a refreshed surface rather than a risk to the rug or the floor underneath.

### Ethical considerations
