## PS5 compatibility and installation guide

PS5 compatibility is the key reason a storage upgrade exists in the first place. The PS5 M.2 slot supports PCIe 4.0 NVMe drives with a certain height, and not all drives fit into the slot without a tune up at the edge of your patience. Here is a concise guide to installing the WD SN850P into your PS5:

- Power down the PS5 and unplug it. You do not want a dramatic unplugging moment while a game is loading in the background. 
- Remove the side panel to access the M.2 slot. This is typically the step where your cat stares at you like you are trying to reorganize the galaxy using a screwdriver. 
- Remove the screw holding the M.2 cover, slide out the old drive if present, and insert the SN850P at a slight angle, then press flat until it sits securely. 
- Reinsert the screw, reattach the cover, and reinstall the side panel. 
- Boot the PS5 and go to settings to format the new drive for expanded storage. The PS5 will guide you through the formatting process, which is a one time operation, after which your new library can start to show up in the storage settings.

There are a few caveats worth noting. If your PS5 has been running hot or you have an older version of the console, you might want to double check that the heatsink is properly seated and that there is adequate airflow inside the chassis. A good fan setup and a slightly elevated console can prevent throttling and keep those speeds humming. The PS5 compatibility is strong with the 2 TB SN850P, and the drive typically shows up as a straightforward expansion option in the PS5 UI.
