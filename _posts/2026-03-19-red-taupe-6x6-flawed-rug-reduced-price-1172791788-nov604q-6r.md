## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity

### Routine Care
Regular vacuuming at a gentle setting keeps dirt from embedding in the fibers. Do not use a high-suction setting that might felt or pull on the fibers, especially near edges. If you have pets, a pet-hair removal tool can be helpful as it concentrates on a short-pile surface.

### Stain Management
For common spills, blot gently with a clean cloth. Use a mild detergent and warm water (test on an inconspicuous area first). Avoid aggressive scrubbing that can push the stain deeper. If the stain is ink, wine, or dye-rich foods, treat promptly and rinse thoroughly.

### The Rug Pad: Not Optional
A rug pad prevents sliding on hard floors, adds thickness for foot comfort, and reduces wear by absorbing some friction. For a 6x6 rug, a pad should be slightly smaller than the rug footprint to prevent curling. If you have hardwood or tile, a pad with a non-slip latex underside is a good match for this kind of rug.
