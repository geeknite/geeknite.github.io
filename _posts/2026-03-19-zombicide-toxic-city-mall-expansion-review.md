## New mechanics and what they change about gameplay
### Toxic zones and the environmental hazard system
Toxic City Mall introduces a living hazard ecology that interacts with movement, line of sight, and item use. Central to this are toxic zones that can spread or cluster across mall tiles. End your move on a toxic tile, and you might face movement penalties, forced card discard, or other constraints designed to nudge you into live hazard management rather than pure firepower.

Practically speaking, this crafts tension on every turn. A misstep can turn a hero into a liability just when you need two survivors to escort an injured ally or recover a critical tool from a remote corner. The designers strike a balance: sometimes sprinting through a toxin corridor hits a vital objective, other times retreating to a safer route becomes the prudent but nerve-wracking choice.

### Mall-specific objectives and progression
Scenario cards push you into objectives that feel appropriately mall-adjacent: rescue a worker from a maintenance closet, restore power to a security booth, reclaim a satellite signal from a rooftop antenna. These aren’t decorative prompts; they demand group coordination and provide explicit retreat options when things go pear-shaped. The sense of momentum builds as you shift from “clear the floor” to “secure the entire mall,” and you’ll feel each mini-win as you claim another wing without turning your team into an anxious grayscale smear.

### Survivor diversity and tactical depth
New survivor tokens bring distinctive abilities that nudge players toward cooperative play. Mobility-focused survivors trade raw weapon power for utility and speed; others offer sensors or board-control options that let your team choreograph the movement of the undead like a well-rehearsed stage fight. The net effect is a more dynamic turn order and those glorious moments when someone shouts, “We need a scout!” or “Medic in this wing, now!” If your group thrives on synergy, Toxic City Mall rewards you with meaningful teamwork opportunities rather than rewarding brute strength alone.

### Zombies with a toxic twist
The undead roster gets a smart upgrade that leans toward terrain manipulation and tactical pressure rather than simply more brute force. Expect zombies that can pinch a route, block lines of attack, or force the party to rethink approach vectors. It’s not an avalanche of new rules; it’s a handful of well-placed twists that keep combat tense in the mall’s tight corridors and escalator shafts.

## Strategy tips and tactics: surviving the mall with style
- Plan your routes around two or three safe zones early on. Toxic zones punish reckless wandering, so you’ll want a plan that keeps at least two survivors moving toward key objectives without stepping into the worst clusters.
- Balance offense with mitigation. A few survivors who can slow or neutralize the toughest zombies will be worth their weight in ammo and first aid kits.
- Use the environment to your advantage. The mall layout rewards baiting zombies into chokepoints or funneling them into hazard zones where you can manage them more predictably.
- Don’t underestimate rescue missions. Extracting a survivor or retrieving an objective from the far corner of the mall can be the difference between victory and a spectacular collapse. Communicate, coordinate, and time your actions to maximize your team’s synergy.
- Learn the new zombie types quickly. Understanding their special abilities is often the key to turning a punishing round into a well-executed plan.
- Think in layers. Treat the mall like a level-based game mission with sub-goals: secure power, hold a corridor, and then push to a final objective in a multi-stage crescendo.
