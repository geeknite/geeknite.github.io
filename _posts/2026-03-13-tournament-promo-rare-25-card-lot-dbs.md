## Handling, storage, and general care

Caring for a 25 card lot is easy if you apply the same care you give to your favorite figurine, your most beloved plushie, or your experimental soldering project. The main steps are:

- Use sleeves that match your color scheme and protect both front and back when possible
- Maintain a clean storage box with dividers so you don’t mix promos with regular rares or uncommons
- Store in a cool, dry place to prevent warping or foil degradation
- Keep a quick-reference guide for each promo so you remember its release context and any errata that might exist

If you’re serious about preserving value, consider placing each card in a graded sleeve for archival purposes. It’s a little extra cost, but it can add a layer of security when you’re moving from tabletop play to a display shelf. And yes, some collectors enjoy arranging these cards by color gradient, by character arcs, or by the emotional arc of the Ginyu Force. You do you.

### Jekyll-and-Hyde moment: condition vs. rarity

Promo rares often ride the fine line between excellent condition and near-mint. You’ll want to check for surface scratches and edge nicks, especially around the foil borders that catch the light so aggressively it could be used to signal in a dark parking lot. Rarities with a limited print run may fetch premium if they’re truly pristine. The trade-off is that some promos show wear faster when played in a tournament or demo environment, so expect a personal style of patina on your prized cards.

If your goal is collection value, keep a ledger of which promos you have and their approximate market value. If your goal is to build a viable deck, consider how the card functions in-game, including its mana cost, attack power, and any interaction quirks that game designers might not have anticipated at the time of printing.
