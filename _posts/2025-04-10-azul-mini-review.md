## What is Azul Mini?

Azul Mini is a condensed version of the original Azul game, which means you get all the tile-placement strategy goodness, but in a bite-sized format. Imagine Azul got hit by a shrink ray—voilà, you have Azul Mini! It's perfect for travel, quick games, or pretending to be a giant while playing.

## Components

### The Tiles

The tiles are just as colorful and vibrant as in the original game, but now they're so small you might mistake them for candy. Just remember: **these tiles are NOT edible**. Trust me on this one.

### The Board

The board is compact, folding neatly into the game box. It's like the board went on a diet and lost some inches, but it's still functional and slick.

### The Bag

The bag is also mini-sized, which means it's cuter and more likely to get lost under your couch. But fret not, it holds all the tiles perfectly, with room to spare for your hopes and dreams.

### The Scoring Track

The scoring track is integrated into the board, ensuring you can keep track of those precious points without needing a magnifying glass. No need to squint—unless you want to look extra dramatic.

## Gameplay

The gameplay remains true to Azul's roots, with players strategically placing tiles to score points. It's the same strategic depth, but you get to feel like a dexterous genius playing with mini components. Here's a quick breakdown:

### Setup

1. Lay out the mini game board.
2. Draw tiles from the mini bag.
3. Pretend you're assembling a masterpiece.

### Turns

Players take turns drawing tiles and placing them on their board, aiming to complete rows and columns to score points. The game ends when one player completes a row, and the final scores are tallied.

### Strategy

Azul Mini requires the same strategic planning as the original. It's all about blocking your opponents while completing your own patterns. Evil laughter is optional but highly recommended.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Portability**: Take it anywhere—it's like Azul on the go.
- **Quick Gameplay**: Perfect for those moments when you have just 20 minutes to spare.
- **Adorable Components**: Who doesn't love tiny, colorful tiles?
- **Great for Beginners**: Easy to learn and teach.

### Cons

- **Small Pieces**: Easy to lose if you're not careful (or have butterfingers).
- **Limited Player Interaction**: Not as immersive as the full-sized game.
- **Mini Bag**: More likely to get lost in the abyss that is your furniture.
