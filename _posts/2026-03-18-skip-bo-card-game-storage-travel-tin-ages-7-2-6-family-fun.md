## Setup and Rules (in Plain English, with a Dash of Sass)
Skip-Bo is a sequencing/carding game where players try to be the first to play all of their cards in sequential order on building piles. The travel tin version keeps the core mechanics intact but streamlines setup so you can start playing within minutes of unboxing. Here’s the gist:

- Each player is dealt a stock pile (the bottom of the deck you won’t peek at unless you want to feel despair in technicolor).
- The goal is to be the first to play all the cards from your stock pile by building up builds of 1 through 12. Skip-Bo cards (wild cards) can stand in for any number.
- On your turn, you draw to a set number of cards, play from your hand onto the building piles, and discard excess cards into your personal discard piles.
- When a building pile reaches 12, it’s claimed and kept, and a new pile can start. First one to clear their stock pile wins.

If you’re worried about the rules dog-piling your brain, fear not: the included rule sheet has a kid-friendly version with illustrations and “play-by-play” steps. We prefer starting with the 3-minute crash course—hand out cards, set stock piles, and declare “game on.” The rest is improvisation and a healthy dose of strategic mischief.

### How to Teach Kids Without Losing Your Voice
Rule-heavy games tend to devolve into “kids yelling at the rules, adults tearing hair.” To keep things friendly:

- Start with a live demo: show a couple of successful plays before anyone touches a card.
- Emphasize not yelling at your siblings when a wild card sabotages your plan.
- Use a timer to keep rounds breezy; a 10-minute game goes far better than a 90-minute epic that ends with someone crying over a misaligned stock pile.
- Set up a “rule of cool” moment: if a player lays down three consecutive 1-2-3s and a wild, applaud. It’s not just luck; it’s theater.
