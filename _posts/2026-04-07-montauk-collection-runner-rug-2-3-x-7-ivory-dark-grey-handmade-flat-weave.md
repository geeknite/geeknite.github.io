## Care, stain removal, and longevity: practical tips for the long haul

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it’s the backbone of a rug that should age gracefully. Here’s a practical playbook:

- Regular vacuuming: keep the surface clear of dust and debris; plan a routine that fits your household tempo.
- Spot cleaning: blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing as that can push the liquid deeper into the weave. Use a mild detergent solution or a rug-safe cleaner; test on a small area first.
- Avoid harsh chemicals: stay away from strong bleaches and alkalis that can degrade fibers and fade pigments.
- Rotation: periodically rotate the rug to ensure even wear and to prevent dominant foot-traffic patterns from forming.
- Professional care: every few years, consider a professional cleaning to refresh the appearance and extend the rug’s life.

If you have pets, keep a small stain kit handy and use a pet-safe cleanser for accidents. A little proactive care goes a long way toward keeping the ivory looking bright and the grey accents crisp.
