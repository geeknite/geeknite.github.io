## Painting and assembly tips for best results

Even if you’re not a master painter, you can get a striking look with a handful of simple steps. The Edouard Guiton figure benefits from a restrained palette, focusing on contrast and clean edge lines rather than a thousand micro-shades. Here’s a quick, friendly workflow:

- Prime in white or gray to keep the minis looking sharp under lighting.
- Block in main colors with basic tones that reflect the character you envision. For Edouard, a designer vibe often translates well to a palette with muted earth tones contrasted by a bold highlight color on accessories or a single focal point.
- Apply a couple of quick washes to deepen the recesses. A low-contrast wash can bring out the details without smearing the sculpt into a muddy mess.
- Dry brush the edges lightly to bring back the raised lines. This step makes the figure pop on the table and makes it easier to spot during frantic moments on the board.
- Seal with a matte varnish to prevent chipping during tabletop shenanigans. If you’re feeling fancy, a light satin finish can give the figure a premium look without turning it glossy.

The other zombies respond well to basic painting techniques too. You don’t need to become a master painter to make the set look cohesive on the table. The key is consistency: use a unifying color theme and keep your highlights balanced so nobody looks like they’re wearing two different costumes in the same faction.
