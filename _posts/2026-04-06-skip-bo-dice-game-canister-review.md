## How to play: a traveler-friendly quick start
If you’ve played Skip-Bo or any quick-draw card game, you’ll have the general vibe. If not, here’s the quick-start idea: roll dice to claim, extend, or move cards in a personal stock pile toward completion. You win by finishing your stock pile first, which might sound simple, but the dice add an element of luck that keeps the game unpredictable and entertaining.

Setup:
- Each player starts with a stock pile of cards (as per the included rules or your preferred variant).
- Each player has a personal dice pool; you’ll roll a subset of the dice on your turn.
- The center is often a shared discard area or a scoring track depending on the variant you’re playing.

Turn sequence:
- On your turn, roll a subset of dice from your pool.
- Use the results to move cards from your stock pile toward completion, following the basic sequences or sets laid out in the variant you choose.
- Some dice faces act as wilds or allow you to swap, steal, or block an opponent’s progress, depending on the house rules you opt into.
- After resolving, pass the turn to the next player.

Scoring:
- In many play modes, scoring happens as you complete stock piles, with additional points awarded for efficient turns or successful blocks. In cooperative variants (if you decide to mix things up with family or kids), you can even set a cooperative target where players collectively chase a shared victory while still having a little friendly competition.

The key here is that the rules are designed to be learned quickly and used flexibly. You’ll notice the pacing naturally encourages quick turns and upsets, which is exactly the vibe you want for portable gaming on a plane or in a hotel room.

## House rules and variants: spicing it up
The real charm of portable games often lies in the variants you can adopt. For Skip-Bo Dice Canister, you can experiment with several house rules to tailor the pace and strategy:
- Quick-fire rounds: Set a timer (e.g., 10 minutes) and see who finishes first. Classic for travel time.
- Limited re-rolls: Limit to two re-rolls per turn to keep things snappy and reduce dice fatigue.
- Wilds as power-ups: Define one or two faces as wilds with unique effects (swap a card, steal a die from another player, etc.).
- Cooperative mode: Players work together to complete a target stock pile within a set time, encouraging teamwork and shared celebration.

These variants can be a great way to keep the game fresh, especially if you’re packing it for multiple trips where you can rotate between modes.
