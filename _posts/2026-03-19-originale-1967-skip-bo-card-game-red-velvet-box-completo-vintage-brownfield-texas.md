## H2: Condition, Grading, and The Care It Deserves

### H3: What to look for when you buy
- Box integrity: Are the corners sharp or tired? Is the lid snappy, or does it sag? Velvet wearing thin around the edges adds character, but outright tears are a red flag that demands negotiation.
- Card stock: Are there creases, stains, or heavy wear? Is there any warping, humidity damage, or color bleed on the backs?
- Rulebook: Is it legible? Are pages intact? Small font can be charming until you realize you can’t decipher the text in dim light.
- Inserts and extras: Are there factory inserts, price tags, distributor stamps, or date codes? These are the breadcrumbs that help confirm provenance and originality.

### H3: Grading reality vs. fantasy
Vintage grading is as much about story as it is about physical condition. A box with a perfectly preserved lid that has a few scuffs might be more valuable to a collector than a minty box that lacks a sense of history. In Geeknite reality, we celebrate boxes that tell a tale—edges that show use, a velvet surface that still shines, and a deck that doesn’t pretend it’s a brand-new product. If your item has a consistent story, you’ve got a better-than-average chance at appreciating its value not just in dollars, but in the stories it carries into your living room.

## H2: Care, Restoration, and Storage — Keeping the Velvet Alive
### H3: Basic care moves
- Humidity control: vintage card stock and velvet both feel happier in steady humidity. Avoid extreme swings, and store the box away from direct sunlight, which will lighten the colors and fade the print at a rate you’ll regret later.
- Gentle handling: wash hands, avoid oily fingerprints on the card faces; use a clean, dry surface, and consider a light breath of air over the deck to remove dust rather than a heavy dusting.
- Velvet care: if the velvet shows wear, a very gentle approach with a soft brush can lift dust without damaging the nap. Avoid water, oils, or abrasive cleaners; velvet is not a fan of fast fashion or casual solvent experiments.
- Storage: a flat, cool, dry shelf or a display case where temperature variations are minimal is your friend. Avoid high heat and moisture; treat this as a delicate artifact rather than a random box of randomness.

### H3: Restoration considerations
If you’re considering any restoration, approach with caution. The goal is not to reinvent the box but to preserve its story. Subtle stabilization of a slightly bent card or a minor tear on an insert is often acceptable, but heavy restoration can erode value by removing the aura of originality. When in doubt, consult a professional conservator who specializes in gaming artifacts or, at the very least, connect with a community of collectors who can offer a second opinion before you risk a leap into “improvement.”
