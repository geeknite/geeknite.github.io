## Burn Performance and Longevity

### Wick Quality, Burn Time, and Even Melting

The wick is a standard cotton rope that glows with a consistent flame, producing a stable melt pool after a few minutes of initial wax reverie. Meadow Mist burns evenly across a respectable radius, avoiding the dreaded tunneling that makes you feel like you are lighting a stick of wax with a tiny, disappointed rocket. Expect a burn time in the range of 40 to 50 hours for a mid-sized tin, depending on room drafts and whether you obsessively trim the wick or not. Trim the wick to about ¼ inch before each burn to extend life and reduce smoke; this is candle care 101, but hey, some of us learn the hard way that untrimmed wicks can produce a smoky, uneven curtain of fragrance that is less meadow and more wildfire metaphor.

### Soot, Smoke, and Air Quality

Meadow Mist produces minimal soot in normal use. If you are running a coffee shop of a living room (i.e., ambient, not actual coffee shop), you should be fine. If you like to burn candles in a poorly ventilated space while simultaneously running a power-hungry computer, you may notice a slight haze—nothing dramatic, but a gentle reminder that candles are not air filters. In general, the air remains pleasant and the scent persists after the flame dies, lingering like a good memory rather than a clingy ex. For sensitive households or people with fragrance allergies, always test with a small burn to gauge impact before committing to long sessions.

## Usage Tips, Care, and Practicalities

### Wick Care and Burn Hygiene

- Always trim the wick to about ¼ inch before lighting. This reduces soot and ensures an even burn.
- If you notice a mushroom-like wick tip forming, extinguish, trim, and relight. It saves you wax and reduces smoke.
- Keep the wax pool clear of wick trimmings or debris; this helps the scent throw stay consistent.

### Storage and Longevity

Store the candle in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Prolonged exposure to heat can cause the wax to discolor or the fragrance to fade in a less vibrant way, which would be a tragedy for meadow vibes. If you have multiple Meadow Mist candles, you can rotate them to extend scent memory across spaces and seasons.

### Cleaning Up after the Burn

When done, wipe the tin and lid with a dry microfiber cloth. If the tin has collected a bit of wax residue around the rim, a warm, damp cloth will remove it without scratching the finish. Recycle the tin if possible and save the lid for small trinkets or a micro-scented storage jar for future candle experiments.
