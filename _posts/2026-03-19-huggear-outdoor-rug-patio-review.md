## How to pick the right size and set it up like a pro

### Measuring your space properly
Rule of thumb: your rug should anchor your seating area with a bit of space to spare. If you’ve got a dining set, you want the rug to extend beyond the chairs when they’re pulled out. ### Placement ideas that maximize feng shui and airflow
Place your rug to create a natural “room” on the patio. The dining set can anchor the rug’s longer side, with chairs placed around it. A secondary seating area can sit on a smaller rug nearby, creating multiple zones without all feeling shoved into one corner. ## Maintenance routines that won’t ruin your weekend

### Quick daily care
- Sweep or vacuum to remove dust and debris.
- Shake out light winter debris if you store it outside.
- Hose down if needed and let air dry.

### Periodic deep clean
- Mix mild soap with warm water and scrub with a soft-bristled brush.
- Rinse thoroughly and hang to dry fully before laying it back down.
- Store rolled in a dry place when not in use for extended periods.

### When to replace versus repair
If the rug starts showing visible fraying, odd odors, or has significant color fade that makes it look worn rather than well-loved, it’s probably time to consider replacement. On the other hand, if the rug still offers a solid foundation, is cleanable, and fits your space aesthetically, you can squeeze a few more seasons of life out of it with regular care.
