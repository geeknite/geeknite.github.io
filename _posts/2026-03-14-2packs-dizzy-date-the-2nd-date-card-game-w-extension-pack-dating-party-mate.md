## How to Play: The Core Flow
The core game is built around short, fast rounds that emphasize social interaction over heavy planning. Here is the typical flow you can expect:
1) Each player assumes a character with a light backstory. No need for a full novella to start; a few sentences will do. The goal is to give yourself a playful lens through which to approach the prompts.
2) A player draws a Date Card, which sets up the dating scenario, and a Challenge Card, which adds a constraint or twist to the scene.
3) The player then crafts a short scene that weaves together the Date Card and the Challenge Card. The rest of the table observes and rates the performance based on a simple, shared scoring method.
4) Mood Meter tracks the atmosphere: enthusiasm, humor, or awkward energy. The Mood Meter helps pace rounds and gives a tactile way to reward or encourage certain play styles.
5) Rounds continue around the table until a pre-agreed score limit or time limit is reached. The player with the highest mood points or the most laughs tends to win by the end of the session.

The elegance of the system lies in its simplicity. There are no heavy math calculations, no need to memorize dozens of rules, and no fear of getting boxed out by a rules lawyer who insists on the exact interpretation of a word. It’s a party game built to be inclusive and fast-moving, letting the story lead and the gags pile up in a natural, organic way.
