## What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition?

Exploding Kittens is a light, fast-paced card game designed to be accessible and chaotic in equal measure. The premise is simple: you draw cards from a single deck, avoid the exploding kitten card, and use a handful of action and defuse cards to survive long enough to outlast the other players. The defuse card is your lifeboat, a lifebuoy you can play to prevent an explosion, typically by disarming the kitten in some cartoonish way. The deck also includes action cards that tilt the odds in your favor, including ones that let you skip turns, force other players to draw more, or peek into the near future so you can anticipate the path your fate will take on the next few draws.

The original edition centers on a playful collision between strategy and luck, where you might carefully playing a defuse while the table laughs off a near miss, only to be blindsided by a well-timed attack that shuffles the deck into a shape nobody anticipated. The core mechanic is straightforward enough for a first game night, yet there are enough little decisions and moments of social mischief to keep even folks who aren’t into card games entertained for a few rounds.

## How to Play: The Core Loop

If you want a quick primer for teaching a new group, here is the short version. On your turn, you can play any number of cards from your hand that fit the card’s rules, then you must draw one card from the draw deck. If you draw the Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card, you’re eliminated from the round; if you do have a Defuse, you can play it to avoid the explosion and reinsert the Exploding Kitten somewhere back into the draw pile. The trick is to balance risk and defense while keeping an eye on how many Defuses remain and how many Exploding Kittens might still lurk in the deck.

Action cards let you manipulate the flow of the game. See the Future lets you peek at the top few cards, letting you plan your next few draws. Attack forces the next player to take two consecutive turns, which can create a cascading chain of near misses and dramatic stalls. Skip ends your turn without drawing, Shuffle messes with the deck order, and Defuse cards are there to save you when fate tries to bite back.

Cat cards bring a social sub-game into the mix. Collect two of the same Cat Card to steal a card from another player’s hand. This mechanic introduces a light competitive layer that rewards memory and timing more than raw luck. You’ll see players feinting and bluffing with their cat cards, and the room will find themselves laughing at the ridiculous possibilities that come from a two-cat combo against a desperate poor soul who forgot to count cards.
