The B-side variant is a running joke among card folks: more image, more color, more reasons to debate whether this card qualifies as a “rookie.” In some cases, B-sides were deliberately printed in smaller runs, creating an instant scarcity that fuels bidding frenzies. The 1991 Emilio Estevez B-side is no exception: it adds a second visual element and a slightly different aesthetic that makes binder pages look more dynamic.

## Collectibility, Value, and How to Store Your Treasure

Let’s talk shop: if you’ve got a specimen in fair to excellent condition, with minimal corner wear and an unbroken gloss, you’re looking at a card that could graze the margins of mid-range market interest—enough to justify a careful purchase but not so high that it requires a mortgage on your comic book shelf. Grading could push it into more serious territory, particularly if the card’s surface is pristine and the back text remains legible. PSA and BGS grades will matter to the right crowd, but don’t forget that the heart of the hobby lies in the story you tell around the card. The good story can be a stronger pull than a pure numerical grade.

Storage is simple enough but worth doing properly. Use a pencil-free, acid-free binder sleeve (double-sleeve if you’re extra cautious), place the card within a sturdy binder page that has adequate spacing to prevent sleeve-to-sleeve contact, and consider a top-loader for rare variants. Keep the binder away from direct sunlight, humidity, and the occasional curious cat that thinks your cards are a tasty snack—cats are weirdly attracted to the scent of cardboard and pastels, and we cannot pretend otherwise.

If you’re trying to understand the market today, start with a watchful eye on major auction sites and the occasional local card shop that still stocks oddball items. The key is to compare similar “rookie/cameo hybrid” cards from the same era. Look for appearances of the Canada Games connection in listings and the presence (or absence) of a B-side variant. The more you compare, the clearer the price range becomes. And yes, you’ll find listings that describe the card as a “classic” or a “cult favorite”—and you’ll have to decide whether you agree with that sentiment yourself. In the end, authenticity and your own enjoyment matter more than a numerical rank on a grading slip.
