Instead of shouting across a wood table, players trade and swap with a deck that represents classic commodities—Wheat, Corn, Oats, Coffee, Sugar, Cocoa, and more. The objective remains deliciously simple: corner a market by collecting a full set of a chosen commodity. The variability of trades makes each session feel like a new season of a chaotic trading drama, minus the stock tips and quarterly reports.\n\n## Corner the Market: The Variant That Changes the Game\nCorner the Market is the feature that gives Pit Card Game its distinctive tilt. Here’s what it adds beyond the core Pit vibes:\n\n- Target market focus: Each round, players chase a specific commodity market. Achieving a complete set for that target triggers special actions and momentary advantages that can upend even the most confident lead.\n- Corner mechanics with twists: The corner condition isn’t just “finish your set.” It’s a moment that can trigger additional trades, forced reveals, or temporary protections. The exact flavor depends on the variant ruleset you’re using, but the intent is consistent: reward planning and social maneuvering as much as random luck.\n- Disruption opportunities: You can nudge others with swaps, forced trades, or peek-like glimpses at hands (depending on how you and your group decide to house-rule this).
