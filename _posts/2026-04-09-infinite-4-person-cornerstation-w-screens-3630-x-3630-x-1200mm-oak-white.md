## Ergonomics and user experience: comfort as a feature
A desk this size invites long hours, but ergonomics is where you don’t want to cut corners. Each user needs adequate legroom, adjustable chair heights, and monitor ergonomics that meet the recommended 20–40 degree viewing angle. The height range on many corner stations supports both seated and standing configurations, which is a big win for teams that insist on alternating between deep thought and deep breaths.

Context matters: if you’re 6’4” and your teammates are 5’6”, the seating geometry should be adjustable enough to accommodate different torso lengths without creating clumsy stretches to reach a keyboard. The corner design can help unify posture across four people, but you should still plan for proper chair depth, foot support, and monitor arms that allow tilting to minimize neck strain. Remember, a 2,000-word review won’t save you from a poorly set up workstation after a full day of debugging and sprint planning.

## Assembly experience: from flat-pack to usable workspace
Here comes the moment of truth: how arduous is it to convert a flat-pack dream into a four-person operational weapon? Expectations should align with the reality that a corner station this size will require some planning and a couple of helpful hands. Common assembly steps include: unboxing the modular frames, attaching cross supports, mounting the desktops, aligning the corner arcs, and finally routing the cables through the internal channels.

If your team has done any IKEA-level furniture assembly in the past, you’ll recognize a familiar rhythm: get the main frame up, ensure square alignment, fasten the major panels, then do the fine-tuning. The finishing touches—drawer runners (if included), cable trays, and optionally cable clamps—often determine whether the space feels like a high-function workspace or a workshop that hosts a weekend hackathon. If you’re not a fan of assembly kinesthetic puzzles, consider hiring a local professional or bundling in a pre-assembly service from the vendor.
