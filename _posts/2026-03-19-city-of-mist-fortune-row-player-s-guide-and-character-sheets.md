## Fortune Row: setting the vibe
Fortune Row isn’t a place to wander aimlessly. It’s a corridor of neon secrets running along the edge of the city’s mythic fringe. Think illuminated signs, a chorus of whispers, and businesses that look ordinary until you notice the subtle tilt—like a painting that knows you better than you know yourself. The Row is a point of contact where myths rub elbows with the everyday, where the Mythos can be borrowed, bargained for, or exploited in the right moment. It’s a sandbox with edges, and those edges can cut in interesting ways.

In terms of game design, Fortune Row offers a curated set of locations, NPCs, and hooks that provide both mechanical incentives and narrative prompts. You’ll find places to exchange favors, secrets, and power; you’ll encounter patrons who know more than they admit and enemies who prefer to keep their suits pressed and their intentions sharper. The Row isn’t just a backdrop; it’s a living, breathing engine that nudges players toward drama while still letting them choose their own mythic path.

### Core themes and motifs
- Identity under pressure: who are you when the myth you worship looks back from a cracked storefront mirror?
- Fortune and fate as social currency: who owns luck, and how do you cash it in?
- The blur between the seen and the unseen: everyday life glimmering with myth and mischief.
- Community and consequence: choices here ripple through friends, rivals, and the people who clean up after you.

## The Player's Guide: character creation boot camp
Fortune Row’s player guide is designed to be practical, punchy, and a little bit cheeky. You’ll find a blend of tools, prompts, and templates that help you translate a vibe into a sheet you can read at the table without needing a librarian’s degree in mythic linguistics.

### Step 1: Define your identity in three layers
1) Who is your “you” in the mundane world: a barista with a memory like a sieve, a courier who knows the city’s heartbeat, a student who edits old myths into social media memes. 2) What is your Masks-identity: the face you present to the world, and the one your Mythos uses when you’re in action. 3) What rumor do you want people to whisper about you after you leave the scene: an unsolved mystery, a spectacular failure, or a glorious win that smells faintly like trouble.

### Step 2: Pick a Mythos and an Avatar
In City of Mist terms, your Mythos is the persona your powers embody, while your Avatar is the figure behind the mask that you channel during scenes. Fortune Row narrows these down with a Row-specific flavor: myths tied to luck, choice, trade, and the clockwork of fate. Your choices will influence how you approach scenes, the hooks you chase, and the relationships you cultivate.

### Step 3: Tags, Traits, and Ties
Fortune Row adds a curated set of tags that help you slot into groups, factions, and story threads. You’ll fill out traits that guide your actions and define how your character interacts with the Row’s patrons and pitfalls. Ties to other players and to NPCs keep the table connected; the fascinations and resentments you list here become fuel for future scenes.

### Step 4: Clues, Gear, and Gambits
The items and clues you collect along Fortune Row aren’t just inventory; they’re narrative accelerants. Clues connect scenes, gear unlocks moments of mystery or misdirection, and gambits serve as aggressive or clever ways to push a scene toward payoff. This isn’t just bookkeeping; it’s a toolkit for keeping the story tempo high and the table engaged.

### Step 5: Relationships and Entanglements
Fortune Row is a social ecosystem. Your sheet should reflect who owes you, who you owe, and what you’ve promised to keep quiet. The Row rewards trust—but with a price tag. By mapping relationships, you create potential triggers for personal arcs and group tension that can escalate or resolve with satisfying gravity.

### Player sheets: formatting tips for printables
We recommend compact, legible sheets that fit on a single page front-and-back. If you’re printing, consider a light gray grid for the clue boxes and a color-coding scheme for Mythos, Gear, and Relationships so you can skim a character’s strengths in a glance.

## The Fortune Row toolkit: mechanics and vibe
Fortune Row brings a few tweaks and additions to the City of Mist toolkit to support its flavor. Here’s what you’ll typically encounter and how it feels in play.

### The Row’s currency: Fortunes, Fates, and Favor
Fortunes are the social capital you can exchange to influence scenes. They’re not just money; they’re a measure of how deep your ties to Fortune Row’s power brokers really run. Fates act as boons or boulders—moments where luck tilts in your direction but at a cost you’ll grin and attempt to justify later. Favor is the personal currency you trade to friends, rivals, and patrons, creating ongoing plot threads rather than one-off wins.

### Clues and revelations
Fortune Row players chase clues that reveal the city’s hidden markings. These clues aren’t just items on a shelf; they’re keys to unlock scenes, to shift a mask’s power, or to force a confrontation at the precise moment when a protagonist’s plan hinges on timing. Effectively using clues creates momentum and a sense of causality you can track across sessions.

### Scenes that feel cinematic
Fortune Row emphasizes scene construction that feels cinematic without devolving into film school vibes. Each scene should have a clear question, a rising tension, and a cost at the end. Will your character reveal their true mythos to save a friend? Will a misdirection lead to a betrayal that redefines a relationship? These arcs should feel earned, not handed to you on a neon-dusted platter.

### A note on balance
The Row is generous in its emphasis on character, mood, and narrative payoff. It’s not a guaranteed board-wreak for every table. GMs should manage pacing, avoid driving players into corner cases, and remember that the best moments often come from the tension between a character’s mask and their choices in the face of a mythic prompt. If you’re feeling stuck, lean into character-driven prompts and let the Row provide the spark rather than dictating every beat.

## Scenes, prompts, and example encounters
To give you a sense of how a Fortune Row arc might unfold, here are a few scene seeds you can drop into a session. Use them as-is or as inspiration to tailor to your table’s vibe.

1) The Neon Confessional: A public confession booth in Fortune Row reveals a character’s deepest fear, but the confessor is a trickster who flips the script on the crowd. The players must decide whether to intervene, use the confession to their advantage, or walk away with more questions than answers.
2) The Trade with a Patron: A patron offers a life-altering fortune in exchange for a favor that will disrupt a rival faction. The choice is complex: do you seize the fortune for your own path or protect the larger balance of power in Fortune Row?
3) The Clock at Midnight: Kael Jinx’s chronometer is counting down to a “perfect moment” that could rewrite a relationship or erase a memory. The party discovers the moment is tied to a ritual that could fracture the Row or seal a dangerous secret away forever.
4) The Lost Map of Murmurs: A missing map shows routes through the city’s myths. Finding it means stepping through doors that weren’t meant to be open, each door a test of your character’s true nature.

## How to use this guide with your table
If you’re playing in a mixed group of veterans and new players, Fortune Row serves as a gentle onboarding path. Start with one or two character sheets, a small set of clues, and a single arc—The Neon Confessional, for example. Once your players feel comfortable with the rules and the vibe, you can layer in more complex mechanics: additional Fortune tokens, layered relationships, and longer-term arcs that unfold over multiple sessions.

Here are a few practical onboarding tips:
- Start with vibe, not complexity. Let players pick a Mythos and Avatar that feel personally thrilling rather than mechanically optimal.
- Use cards and prompts to spark scenes quickly. A well-timed prompt can turn a trading post into a doorway to a mythic memory.
- Keep track of your row’s power brokers. A simple grid with the Patron, Rival, and Protector can give you immediate hooks for social scenes and fights alike.
- Rotate who drives the table. If someone’s been GMing for a while, don’t be afraid to switch roles to maintain energy and creativity.
- Remember the cost. Fortune Row isn’t just about wins; it’s about consequences and the way friendships, secrets, and loyalties fracture under pressure.
