## Weather resistance, care, and longevity: what happens when the skies open

Weather resistance is not the sexy feature; it is the quiet hero that ensures your set remains intact after you forget to bring cushions inside during that sudden downpour. Synthetic wicker resists UV damage and moisture far better than natural wicker, which tends to sigh dramatically in the sun. Cushions' fabric is usually water-repellent and quick-drying, aiding in spills and rain plot twists. A seasonal check is wise: inspect the frame for loosened screws, clean the weave gently with a soft brush, and store cushions indoors or under a weather cover when storms loom. Coastal locales benefit from rinsing off salt residues occasionally to preserve finish.

Practical care tips include a mild soap solution for stubborn grime and a soft sponge to avoid dulling the wicker’s sheen. The fabric choice matters: breathable, quick-drying, and mildew-resistant fabrics will extend the life of your cushions without turning your patio into a moisture museum.
