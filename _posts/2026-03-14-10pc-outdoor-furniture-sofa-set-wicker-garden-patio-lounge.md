## Weather resistance, care, and longevity: what happens when the skies open

Weather resistance is not a sexy feature; it’s the quiet hero that ensures your set remains in one piece after you forget to bring cushions inside during an unexpected rainstorm. Synthetic wicker frameworks resist UV damage and moisture better than natural wicker, which tends to crack and sigh dramatically when exposed to the sun. The cushions’ fabric is often treated to be water-repellent and quick-drying, which helps with accidental spills and that summer rain that arrives like a sneaky plot twist. Still, you’ll want to do a seasonal check: inspect the frame for any loosening screws, clean the weave gently with a soft brush, and consider storing the cushions indoors or under a weather cover when storms are on the horizon.

All that said, this set can handle the outdoors reasonably well if given the right care. A little routine maintenance goes a long way: wipe the frame with a damp cloth, brush away dirt from the weave, and keep cushions dry until you’re ready to reuse them. If you live in a coastal area, you’ll want to rinse off salt residues periodically to avoid corrosion and preserve the finish. Pro tip: a simple capful of mild soap in warm water and a soft sponge works wonders for removing stubborn grime without dulling the wicker’s sheen.
