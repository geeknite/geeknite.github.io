## Grading and Preservation
The best way to keep No.262 in pristine condition is proactive care. Invest in a solid binder, acid-free sleeves, and a storage system that protects from humidity and sunlight. Carddas’ smaller format can be a friendly entry point for younger collectors who are just learning how to handle cards with care, while still challenging older collectors who want to keep their holdings in optimal condition.

If you decide to pursue graded options, be mindful that grading services differ in how they treat non-Western card formats. Some graders are comfortable evaluating Carddas prints using general grading criteria for corners, edges, centering, and surface gloss; others have a more specialized rubric for language editions. When in doubt, look for a grader with prior experience in Japanese card lines. A slabbed copy can add a layer of resale confidence, but the up-front cost and turnaround time should be weighed against your long-term goals.
