City of Mist Fortune Row arrives like a slick cigarette smoke cloud rolling through a neon alleyway, promising luck, lurkers, and a roster of new identities that can make a city slide into your control with nothing but a smirk and a well-timed misstep. Welcome to Fortune Row, the character expansion set that taps into the noir dream of a city where destiny is rented by the hour and the next roll of fate might be the only thing standing between you and the gutter. If you thought City of Mist was about icons, masks, and mythic power colliding with everyday life, get ready for a street level remix that leans into the ridiculous glow of fortune, risk, and the occasional spectacular fail that somehow still looks stylish on camera.

In this review I will lift the lid on what Fortune Row adds to the core game, how it feels in actual play, and whether it earns a slot on your table or simply collects dust next to the old subway map you swore you would frame. And yes, we will reference some classic City of Mist flavor while staying focused on the expansion itself, because this game is at its best when your players feel the aura of a living city, not just a set of numbers in a file card.


## Overview and mood

Fortune Row is built around a nightlife powered by luck, shifty deals, and the kind of street-level mythography that makes a great one shot sing and a long campaign shimmer. The pack leans into a vibe where characters are both guardians and tricksters of the city, wearing a mask that invites chaos and a suit that looks ready to negotiate fate face to face with a mob boss or a forgotten god. If the City of Mist core book is your noir engine, Fortune Row is the after-hours bar where the bartender leans in and says, you are about to be paid in coins that might still be spinning when you wake up.

The theme is tight and well executed: a cohort of new PCs who survive on wits, luck, and a willingness to bend rules that no one ever wrote down. These are not the grand mythic demigods of a city wide mythos; instead, they are the foot soldiers and edge players who can push a scene from tense confrontation into a brilliant disaster with the right roll and an even more precise misplay of timing. Fortune Row wants you to lean into human scale magic, and it rewards tables that enjoy riffing on the luck versus consequence dynamic rather than pretending every problem can be fixed with a single clever move.

As with many City of Mist expansions, the flavor shines in the presentation and the hooks it gives the GM to guide the session. The new archetypes feel distinct and useful, the new assets are flavorful without being one trick ponies, and the setting details easily slot into the street corners you already know. If you enjoy the idea of a city where a gambler can tilt a district, a fixer can grease a wheel in seconds, and a mythic alias can alter a plan at the last possible moment, Fortune Row is a hook you will be happy to pull again and again.


## What is in the box

Fortune Row brings a compact but competence packed spread of content designed to slot into a typical City of Mist table without requiring a rework of your existing crews. You can expect to find:

- Several new character cards with distinct prompts, masks, and power themes centered around luck, risk, and streetwise influence.
- A handful of new assets and props that characters can leverage to influence scenes, negotiate with factions, or flip a situation in their favor through clever use and misdirection.
- A compact set of encounter seeds that can be dropped into an ongoing campaign or used to populate a nighttime session with minimal prep.
- A small GM aid that helps track fortune tokens, risk thresholds, and the evolving mythic weight of a scene as luck shifts from player to player.
- A double sided map or scene handouts that reinforce the urban geography of Fortune Row and the niche corners your players might choose as their turf.

The box is designed for quick setup. For groups that love to dive straight into a scene with minimal book flipping, Fortune Row does a great job of presenting clear prompts and concise stat blocks. It is not trying to overhaul the system; it is trying to augment the flavor of a city where luck is a practical consideration in every plan, every heist, and every negotiation.


## New characters and archetypes

The core of Fortune Row is a cast of new archetypes that feel distinct from the base game but fit neatly into city scale mythics. They are designed to play well with existing City of Mist characters and to offer fresh levers for your group to push on a scene:

- The Gambler: a rogue with a talent for reading odds, skilled in turning near misses into opportunities when fate looks the other way. The Gambler comes with a small package of luck based tricks, often with probabilities that swing the outcome of a scene more than raw power would.
- The Kindred Dealer: a mid level broker who can link people, information, and favors through a network of contacts. This archetype shines when you lean into social maneuvering, fencing, and the art of making promises that are not quite promises but feel binding enough to carry a scene forward.
- The Lucky Charm: a supporting figure who adds a layer of mystic luck to the team. They can influence dice pools, grab a needed asset at the last moment, or create openings in the facade of a rival to let the crew slip through.
- The Night Runner: a fast mover who excels at escape, pursuit, and applying pressure in the urban maze. Their powers hinge on terrain awareness and timing, turning ambient chaos into a route to safety or extraction.
- The Silk Banker: this archetype weaves influence in the financial and social markets. They can buy time, tilt a deal, or pivot a conversation by reading the room and guiding others toward a preferred outcome.
- The Ink Traveler: a user of stories and myths as a resource, someone who can recast a moment with narrative weight and bend perception using rumors and the city’s collective imagination.

Each figure has clear mechanics, a unique mask concept, and a few signature actions that map cleanly onto a typical City of Mist session. They all emphasize the tension between luck and consequence, which runs as a thread through Fortune Row. You will be rewarded for daring decisions, for leaning into the ambiguity of the city, and for accepting that sometimes a plan collapses into a better chaos that saves the day.


## Assets, gear, and street-level tools

Fortune Row expands the toolbox with a range of assets you can deploy during scenes. These are not simply cut and dried items; they are narrative tokens designed to tilt dice pools, grant minor boons, or create a stage for a dramatic reveal. Expect assets that cover:

- Street gear that makes a difference in social or chase scenes
- Evidence and leverage assets that can be used to curry favor or extract information
- Small physical props that players can claim to narrate a moment with more color and specificity
- A few cursed or lucky items that carry a price tag in the form of a consequence or risk

The art direction for these assets matches the core book’s moody vibe while pushing a bit more into noir neon. The items feel tangible on the table, and this translates to fast on tap roleplay in sessions where players want to lean into the world without heavy crunch.


## Mechanics and risk versus reward

City of Mist uses a blend of dice based mechanics and narrative prompts to resolve scenes. Fortune Row leans into the same core structure but injects a handful of twists that encourage risk taking and playstyle experimentation. Here are several aspects you will notice:

- Fortune tokens or luck points that can be spent to influence outcomes, with diminishing returns or escalating costs as the scene progresses. This creates a natural pacing arc for a session and a reason to keep the tension rising toward a big payoff.
- A risk meter that helps GMs calibrate how dangerous a scenario feels. As luck shifts, so does the stakes, pushing players toward bigger choices.
- Social maneuvering and misdirection become more viable options with new masks that provide specialized abilities in conversations, interrogations, or negotiations. The cards emphasize talk as much as action, which suits a game that rewards narrative cleverness.
- A compact engine for scene bootstrapping. If you are running a one shot or a short arc, Fortune Row provides reliable seeds that can be dropped into an existing city map with only a few adjustments.

If your table loves the interplay of luck and strategy, this set pays off quickly. It gives players perceptible incentives to lean into clever plays rather than brute force, and it provides enough mechanical texture to make a session feel personal and reactive to the people at your table.


## Theme, tone, and GM guidance

Fortune Row nails a noir tone that is consistent with City of Mist but spices it with a carnival of luck and risk. The writing evokes seedy after-hours vibe without losing the sense of mythic possibility. It is easy to present a night market scene where every deal could be a trap or a doorway to a larger destiny, and the GM can lean on the new seeds to generate atmosphere quickly.

GM guidance in Fortune Row emphasises pace and mood. It includes tips on how to scale danger with the luck mechanic, how to use the new archetypes to create a dynamic party rhythm, and how to balance the line between cunning and overreach so sessions stay tense but fair. If you enjoy flexible pacing and a game that rewards roleplay that feels both grounded and slightly magical, Fortune Row will feel like a natural extension rather than a separate system.


## Artwork and presentation

The art direction continues the stylish look of the base game with a neon noir palette, moody silhouettes, and exaggerated typography that communicates a sense of urban legends in motion. The character cards, asset cards, and encounter sheets sit well on the table and are designed for quick scanning. The layout supports a GM who needs to flip a page and call out a consequence, a line of dialogue, or a new twist in the scene. While not every piece of art will be a showstopper, the overall cohesion feels intentional and polished, which matters when you are tearing through a session and want visuals to reinforce the storytelling rather than distract from it.


## Design quality and balance

Fortune Row is not the kind of expansion that shakes the table with radical overhauls. Instead, it provides a measured increase in flavor, mechanical options, and narrative tools. The designers appear to aim for two goals at once: keep the core system intact so veterans can slot these options in without a second rulebook, and offer enough variety so a GM can craft a session or arc that is distinctly their own. In practice this translates to:

- Archetypes that feel different from base game options yet are immediately usable in a scene
- Assets that drive social and investigative play, not just combat
- A risk and luck framework that scales to different table sizes and play durations

If your group loves a tight, theme forward addition that enhances the mood rather than complicating the math, you will feel rewarded by Fortune Row. If you prefer a heavy mechanical overhaul or more large scale mythic play, this might feel like a flavorful accessory rather than a core reimagining.


## Play sessions and table fit

Fortune Row shines in a few particular table formats:

- Short arcs and one shots where quick setup and mood are paramount. The new seeds are ideal for hooking players into a night of lurid adventures without heavy prep.
- Mid to long campaigns that want to introduce a street level faction dynamic. The assets and archetypes provide new angles for ongoing plots as you shift emphasis between front page myth and back alley rumor.
- Tables that enjoy a strong social layer. The emphasis on negotiation, information control, and leverage makes Fortune Row a natural fit for players who enjoy talking through problems and turning scenes with clever dialogue rather than pure action.

For groups that lean into heavy combat or expansive world-scale myth, Fortune Row will still work but may require a lighter touch to integrate the new mechanics with the existing power progression. The content is approachable and flexible, but it is not a replacement for the core rules or a complete rewrite of the game’s core spine.


## How to integrate with existing City of Mist material

Because Fortune Row is designed as an expansion, it integrates smoothly with the core game. Here are practical tips for adopting Fortune Row without losing momentum:

- Mix archetypes gradually. Introduce one new character option at a time and allow the table to acclimate to the new dynamics. This helps avoid overload and ensures players understand how the character interacts with the other game elements.
- Use the new assets as payoff tokens. If a session reaches a crucial moment, a well timed asset play can actually shift a negotiation or a chase in a surprising direction.
- Leverage the seeds for quick setup. When you need a scene that feels fresh, Fortune Row seeds give you a ready to deploy scenario with minimal prep. This is especially useful for weekly games where you want variety without hours of planning.
- Maintain an eye on balance. If your table pushes a couple of archetypes into most sessions, consider creating a house rule to diversify opportunities for other players. The best city microcosms require balance among masks, not just power.
