## Connectivity, USB-C, and Power Play
The promise of a single USB-C cable powering both data and video plus charging is what makes portable USB-C monitors appealing. The LG Gram 16MR70 View delivers on that promise with a USB-C interface that supports display data, device power delivery, and a robust data channel. This means you can connect your laptop, push the screen content to the monitor, and keep your laptop charged—potentially even letting the monitor sip a little power while you work away.

### Ports and power delivery
- USB-C with power delivery: The monitor can be powered by your laptop or a USB-C power source, depending on your setup. If your laptop can spare juice, the monitor can be powered passively or with a small boost from a dedicated charger.
- No extra power brick needed in many setups: Depending on your power adapter and the capabilities of your USB-C port, you may not need to carry an extra wall wart. The beauty of USB-C PD is that it keeps your backpack neat and your energy supply simple.
- Data and display over USB-C: You get video output as well as fast data transfer, which is handy for sensor data, cables you may be using for peripherals, or just a snappiness boost when working with larger documents and codebases.

### Compatibility and setup tips
- Laptop compatibility: Works nicely with Windows, macOS, and many Linux setups. If you are a heavy Linux desktop user, you’ll appreciate the plug-and-play simplicity when your kernel recognizes the monitor as a standard display.
- Desktop setups: If you prefer a standing desk, you can pair the monitor with a lightweight arm or stand. The slim profile ensures you won’t tip the whole desk weight balance trying to attach a heavy arm.
- Cable choice: Using a quality USB-C cable with power delivery improves stability, especially if you’re dealing with higher brightness or more aggressive color modes. The cable choice can sometimes influence whether the monitor draws enough power from the laptop to be comfortable for long sessions.

### Practicality caveats
- USB-C power sources vary in performance. If you run a resource-hungry workload, you may want to rely on a dedicated power source to ensure consistent brightness and color performance.
- Some users may desire additional ports (HDMI, DisplayPort, etc.). This monitor is designed around USB-C, so if you require legacy connections, you’ll need a small hub or adapter.
