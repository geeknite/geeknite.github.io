## How to Play: Quick Start Guide

Phase 10 is a meld-building game with a cycle of phases you must complete to win. The Go! edition condenses this into a streamlined experience. Here’s a lightweight quick-start guide to help you jump in fast:

- Objective: Be the first to complete the required number of phases or accumulate the fewest points after a set number of rounds. The exact winning condition depends on how you decide to play, but the spirit remains the same: plan, adapt, and don’t forget to laugh at your own mistakes.
- Setup: Shuffle the compact deck and deal a small hand to each player. The quick reference guide shows you the phases at a glance.
- Phases: Typical phases include sets (same rank), runs (consecutive numbers of the same color), and mixed combos. The GO! edition keeps the essence but trims extraneous details.
- Turn flow: On your turn, draw a card, decide whether to lay down a phase, and then discard. The pace is brisk; you’ll be surprised how many turns pass in a single breath when you’re waiting for your coffee to cool.
- Scoring: You’ll accumulate points for cards not yet laid down and for uncompleted phases. In a travel setting, shorter rounds mean scoring stays tight and easy to track.

If you want the full official rules, the Mattel product page is a reliable destination for any edge cases or clarifications. [Mattel official page](https://www.mattel.com/en-us/products/mattel-mini-phase-10-go-card-game)

- Quick-start tip: Don’t overcommit to one Phase early. The thrill of completing an early Phase is real, but it can lock you into a suboptimal hand for the next rounds. Flexibility is your superpower here.
