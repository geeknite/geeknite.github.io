## What is Codenames Pictures, and how does it differ from the classic?

Codenames Pictures is CGE’s visual spin-off to the original Codenames formula. Instead of words on blues and reds, you’re presented with a grid of illustrated pictures. Each picture card depicts a scene with multiple objects, people, or actions. If you’ve played Codenames before, you already know the core mechanics: two teams race to contact their agents by giving clever clues that point to multiple cards at once without tipping off the opposing team or the assassin card.

In Pictures, the shared goal remains the same: be the first team to locate all your agents. The twist is that each card is a mini-puzzle because the pictures can encode multiple meanings. A single image might evoke a cluster of ideas: a pirate ship, a clock, and a piano in a single frame. The clue-givers think in broader, more visual terms: not just “dangerous animal” and “zoo” but “danger, time, and music—together.” The guessers, in turn, have to triangulate the intended meaning from their team’s perspective, the current card layout, and the vibes emitted by the artwork.

This is not a mere palette swap from the word version; it’s a creaking-open door into a different category of social deduction and teamwork. The art style—vibrant, sometimes surreal, often humorous—lends itself to storytelling and inside jokes among players. It’s a playground for interpretation, which is exactly what you want when you’re trying to herd a crew of friends into a productive guessing spree before someone shouts “We’ve got it!” with the confidence of a magician revealing his tricks.

For those wondering about the blocky questions and tension: the game keeps the same tense tempo as the original. The board setup, number of agents per player, and the turn structure are familiar enough that you’ll slip into play fairly quickly after a short read-through. The major change is the decoding method: instead of linking words to a single meaning, you connect pictures to multiple meanings, themes, or vibes. If you’ve got a crew that loves visual storytelling, Pictures is your new jam. If your group prefers crisp wordplay with precise definitions, you might still enjoy Codenames, but Pictures will scratch a different itch.

## How to play: a quick primer

If you know Codenames by heart, you’ll be at home here. If not, fear not—the learning curve is gentle, and the theme provides natural talking points that keep players engaged.

- Setup: A grid of 5x4 (or 5x5, depending on player count and preferred variant) is laid out with picture cards facing up. Each card shows a unique illustration; there’s no need to decode letters or words—you’re reading visuals.
- Teams: Two teams, red and blue, each with a clue-giver. The other players are guessers on their team.
- The clue-giver’s turn: The clue-giver selects a single clue that ties together multiple pictures. The clue should be a word or phrase that hints at several cards on the board without being too vague or overly obvious. The classic pitfall here is misalignment: giving a clue that points too clearly to the opposing team’s cards or to the assassin card.
- Guessing: The guessers discuss and point at cards they think align with the clue. They can guess multiple cards until they either decide to stop or hit the limit set by a turn or the number of cards the clue-giver ties to the clue. The tension peaks when you’re creeping toward the assassin card, which ends your team’s turn immediately, or when you successfully uncover several of your team’s agents—delivering a triumphant, cinematic moment.
- Winning: First team to locate all their agents wins. The interplay between clue-giver interpretation and guesser inferences creates social drama that’s perfect for a night of storytelling, banter, and laughter.

Key differences from the original word-based version include a larger emphasis on shared interpretation and the fun of misreadings. The pictures allow for more ambiguous clues and a greater possibility of surprising connections, which can yield some of the most memorable moments of the night.
