# Mayfair Games Saboteur 2 Expansion: Subterfuge and Glitter in the Mine

If you thought your mining crew was a wholesome bunch of good egg miners singing campfire songs, the Saboteur 2 expansion has something that will shatter your dreams and replace them with gold coins of questionable shine. Mayfair Games drops a second expansion that promises more chaos, more secret objectives, and more opportunities to sabotage your teammates while pretending to be a productive dwarf.

In this guide, we dive into what Saboteur 2 actually adds to the base game, how it plays with different player counts, and whether the flourishes are worth your precious playtime. If you came for gold and giggles, you are in the right tunnel. If you came for quiet, well, bring earplugs and a loyal alibi.

Here at Geeknite, we love a good subterfuge mechanic almost as much as we love puns about axes and pickaxes. So whether you are a Saboteur veteran or a curious caver who has never mined for glory without immediate backstabbing, this review should help you decide if Saboteur 2 belongs in your collection. 

## Overview

Saboteur 2 is an expansion that winks at you from the corner of the table and says, hey, want more chaos? It adds new roles, new treasure objectives, and a handful of fresh tunnel cards that upgrade the classic path-building premise to something spicier, tighter, and a little more devious. It does not replace the base game; it piggybacks on it, like a tiny goblin hitchhiker clutching a pickaxe. The result is a game that can feel both exhilarating and nerve-wracking, depending on which team you are on and how suspicious your fellow miners appear after drawing a particular card.

But does Saboteur 2 actually improve the experience, or is it a cash grab dressed in a glittery helmet? In this review we examine the contents, the rules, and the emotional roller coaster that proceeds after the first handful of misplays. 

## What’s in the Box

The physical components in Saboteur 2 are a step up from the core game in terms of variety and heft. You get a new deck of role cards, new obstacle cards, new treasure cards, and a handful of alternate victory conditions that encourage players to adapt their strategy on the fly. There are more tunnel cards than you might expect, and several of them feature clever twists that can turn a straightforward mine into a labyrinthine death trap of your own making.

Key items you’ll encounter include:

- New role cards that introduce mid game pivots and potential double-crossers
- Additional treasure and gold tokens with alternative win conditions
- Fresh tunnel cards with unique effects that reward misdirection
- A small handful of rules clarifications to keep the chaos contained (mostly) 

If you’re a completionist, you might want to sleeve everything and declare your love for the glittery chaos. If you’re a casual gamer, you’ll be happy to know Saboteur 2 does not completely derail your weeknight; it simply nudges the road toward a different melodrama.

### The Components Up Close

The art style remains faithful to the original Saboteur—tongue-in-cheek dwarven caricatures, humorous treasure icons, and bright color-coded suits—yet Saboteur 2 adds a dash of modern polish: crisper icons, cleaner symbols, and a better-defined symbol system for the new roles. The physical quality is solid, with sturdy cards and nicely sized tokens. You’ll be tossing a lot of these things around the table, and you want to feel confident that they won’t warp into a muddy mess after a couple of sessions.

In short: the components feel designed for repeat play and quick identification during heated moments. Nobody wants to fumble through a treasure card while a saboteur reveals their secret objective and you realize you misread the entire table. Again.

## How to Play with Saboteur 2

Saboteur 2 builds on the base game’s core premise: miners dig tunnels to reach treasure cards while saboteurs try to derail them without tipping off the miners. The new expansion adds layers of depth without overburdening the rhythm of the game. Here is a rough guide to the core changes you’ll encounter during setup and play:

- Setup: You still deal role cards randomly, except now there are additional roles with more nuanced objectives. The number of treasure cards in the deck remains the same, but the distribution of victory conditions shifts depending on the number of players. This means that even in your regular 4-player game you may encounter alternates like rescue missions, or decoy treasures that award victory points to the wrong team.
- Turn order: The turn structure remains familiar—play a tunnel card, place a worker card, possibly play an action card, and then draw. The new action cards give players more ways to influence the board state or flip the objective in mid-game.
- Objectives: The treasure cards now come with alternate victory conditions. Instead of simply collecting the most gold, you might need to collect a set of special treasures or complete a path that passes through a particular sequence of tunnels. This fosters both cooperation and betrayal in new ways.
- Roles: The new roles add more subtle ways to cast suspicion. Some roles reveal themselves only when the path reaches a particular card, while others provide limited protection for a single round. The complexity scales with player count, so groups that enjoy more tabletop soap opera will find Saboteur 2 particularly rewarding.

If you are new to Saboteur, you may want to start with the base game first before diving into Saboteur 2. The early rounds can feel chaotic enough; the expansion should not be the first time you encounter a hidden alliance at the table. On the other hand, seasoned Saboteurs will appreciate the extra levers and the way new roles weave into the existing strategy.

## Thematic and Aesthetic Notes

Humor remains a strong through-line in Saboteur 2. The dwarven artwork leans into cartoonish expressions that exaggerate emotions in a way that makes the sabotage feel almost playful, even when someone is actively sabotaging your work. The new treasure and identity cards come with bold icons and cheeky flavor text that evoke a sense of mischief without tipping into mean-spirited territory. This balance is important for groups who like a bit of shade but still want to laugh together rather than at one another.

If you care about the tone, Saboteur 2 nails it. The humor is a guest you want at your table rather than a distracting clown. You won’t mistake this expansion for a grim strategic simulator; it remains a party game at heart, with enough tactical depth to satisfy people who enjoy reading the room like a game of poker and who also enjoy the occasional pun about gold and goblins.

## Strategy Tips for Saboteur 2

- Gauge the room before you draw: The expansion’s new roles reward those who can read the table and identify early pressure points. If you see people eyeing the exit, you might be dealing with a saboteur more concerned with the pot than the path to treasure.
- Diversify your paths: With more routes and alternate objectives, you can sometimes create two plausible paths to victory. This makes it harder for saboteurs to land a fatal blow and easier for you to claim a win by retreating into a safe route.
- Use the new role cards strategically: Some roles offer only limited protection or only trigger under certain conditions. Save these for critical turns, like when the board is about to reveal a devastating obstacle or a potential “checkmate” treasure card.
- Communicate, but don’t reveal: The social aspect is the lifeblood of Saboteur 2. Use mild insinuations, misdirection, and well-timed compliments to keep your opponents off balance. But remember: you’re playing a game about trust, which is ironically the least trustworthy thing at the table.
- Endgame pressure: As with any expansion that adds complexity, you’ll want to manage the late-game momentum. The adversaries may have momentum, but you have a secret weapon: refereed misdirection and a surprising coin flip that makes the table erupt in laughter.

## Comparisons: Base Game vs Saboteur 2

The base Saboteur is a lean, speedy card game that thrives on social dynamics and the tension of potential backstabbing. Saboteur 2 does not pretend to replace that formula. Instead, it gilds the existing framework with more subtlety and more possible outcomes. In practice, you’ll find:

- More strategic depth without turning into a heavy Euro: The new objectives shift the win conditions and keep players thinking about long-term goals rather than simply maximizing gold collection on every turn.
- Greater variety, higher replayability: The additional roles and cards ensure that no two games feel identical, which is crucial when you want your group to keep showing up for a monthly Saboteur night.
- Fragile balance depends on player group: If your table has a lot of veteran Saboteurs, Saboteur 2 will sing. If you’re still teaching the base rules and someone is constantly misreading the card flips, Saboteur 2 can feel a bit fiddly at first. Give it a few sessions to settle into rhythm.

## How to Play with the Expansion: Quick Setup and Rules Primer

To get Saboteur 2 on the table, you don’t need to shred your preexisting approach to the base game. Here is a concise setup guide:

- Shuffle the base deck with the Saboteur 2 expansion cards: this creates a combined pool that accommodates the expanded role set.
- Deal role cards: with the expansion, you’ll have more possible identities in the pool. The number of roles depends on the player count; check the included setup guide for exact numbers.
- Lay out the starting tunnel: set up the initial board according to the usual instructions, but with the new tunnel cards available. You’ll see new paths, new obstacles, and new opportunities to misdirect your fellow players.
- Draw and play: the turn order remains familiar. Use action cards to influence the board, and remember that the treasure cards now come with alternate paths to victory. 

The key rule to remember is: sabotage is allowed, but keep it in good spirits. If your friend flips a card and says, that is not what I meant, you can reply with your best dwarven grin and a well-timed pun about gold. It is a game after all, and the best sabotage is the kind that makes everyone laugh instead of sulking in their seat.

## Replayability and Longevity

One of the most significant tests for any expansion is whether it sustains interest after multiple plays. Saboteur 2 nails this to a point. The new roles keep things unpredictable; the alternate winning conditions require players to rethink their usual approach; and the larger pool of cards means that even after dozens of games, you still might encounter a path you haven’t seen before. It’s not a mandatory purchase for casual players who only want light entertainment; but for groups who like to tinker with rules and push social dynamics to the edge, Saboteur 2 becomes a staple that you’ll reach for when you want something a little more unpredictable.

There are occasional rough edges. The expanded rule set can be a little intimidating for new players, and the tension between cooperation and betrayal can escalate quickly in late rounds. If you are bringing a large group, consider splitting into two tables for a while to keep the mood upbeat while you learn the intricacies. The last thing you want is a game that lingers longer than the jokes at the table.

## Artwork, Design, and Production Quality

The production quality remains high. The cards are thick enough to survive a few hands and the artwork maintains that playful dwarven aesthetic. The treasure tokens are visually distinct, which helps prevent misreads during fast-paced decision moments. Some players note that the new icons can be a little busy at first glance, but with a single game or two, you’ll be interpreting them with ease and speed. The designer’s intent is to present a lighthearted theme that still lets you sink into the tactical decisions that define Saboteur, and Saboteur 2 achieves this with a mix of clever art and thoughtful layout.

For those who care about components: the expansion integrates with the base game charm without feeling tacked on. Everything you loved about Saboteur is still present, and the expansion adds enough new color to justify its inclusion in your gaming nights. 

## Which Groups Should Buy Saboteur 2?

- Groups who have exhausted the base game: If you crave new twists and a fresh puzzle, Saboteur 2 delivers without forcing you to relearn the entire system.
- Social players who enjoy the theatre of suspicion: The new roles and objectives provide a stronger stage for players who love whispering and strategic misdirection.
- Family-friendly gaming groups who want a way to inject quick chaos into a typically cozy game night: The humor remains accessible, and the rules are transferable between adult players and older teens.
- People who like a challenge but don’t want a heavy upgrade: It’s not a complex expansion, but it does demand more attention to the table dynamics than the base game.

If you frequently host game nights with a rotating cast of players, Saboteur 2 is likely to become a go-to option for those evenings when you want something familiar yet novel.

## The Geeknite Verdict and Final Recommendation

Saboteur 2 Expansion is a solid choice for players who adore the base Saboteur and crave a richer, more dynamic social puzzle.
