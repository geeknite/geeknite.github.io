## Build and paint: tips to bring out Audrey’s character

- Priming and base coat: Start with a clean model to highlight the crisp lines on the book and the coat. A gray primer can help emphasize the texture on fabric and leather, making the book’s cover pop in your chosen color palette.
- Color choices: Lean into earthy tones for the outfit (dark greens, browns, and muted blues) and give the book a special treatment—perhaps a worn parchment color with subtle red or gold lettering to evoke a weathered tome. A touch of metallic for any accessories completes the look without overpowering Audrey’s scholarly vibe.
- Shading and highlights: Use a mid-tone wash to settle the recesses, then pick out edges with a fine brush for a crisp finish. The goal is to keep Audrey looking polished and purposeful rather than battle-scarred; a few light scratches on the book’s spine can hint at the many stories it’s endured.
- Basing: Whether you’re aiming for a library corner, a ruined marketplace, or a shelved executive study, basing can increase the mini’s storytelling value. Small book stacks, broken desk items, or a breadcrumb trail of pages can all add personality.
