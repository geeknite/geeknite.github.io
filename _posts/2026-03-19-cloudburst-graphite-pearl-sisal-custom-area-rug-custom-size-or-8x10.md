## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity

Natural sisal is relatively easy to maintain with regular vacuuming. Avoid aggressive vacuuming with a beater bar on carpet mode; it can wear the fiber over time. A gentle pass every few days keeps the pile from looking tired and helps prevent buildup that can attract more dust than a black hole in a sci-fi probeware lab.

Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth. Avoid soaking; water on sisal is not your friend. For stains that stand up to a fight, you can use mild detergent on a small, inconspicuous area or rely on professional rug cleaning if the stain seems too dramatic for home theatrics. The caveat with natural fibers is that heavy moisture can cause the color to shift at the edges, so keep it dry and calm.

Rotation is recommended for larger spaces to even out wear and sun exposure. If you have a sunny living room, rotate the rug every few months to avoid a persistent sun fade on the graphite side.
