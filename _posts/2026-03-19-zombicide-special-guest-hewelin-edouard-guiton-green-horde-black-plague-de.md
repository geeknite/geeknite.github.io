## Painting and Hobby: Tips to Bring Hewelin to Life
If you’re the kind of hobbyist who loves to push contrast and drama, the Hewelin model is a gift that keeps on giving. Here are practical tips to take your painting from “ok tabletop” to “how did you do that”:

- Base layering: Start with a deep undercoat to preserve the cool greens in the recesses. A mid‑tone green on the armor and cloak will keep the surface readable even under tabletop lighting.
- Contrast and edge: Use a bright edge highlight on the blade and a slightly lighter green on raised surfaces to make silhouette lines pop on camera.
- Leather and metal weathering: A thin wash of brown in the leather belt and a light dry brush on metal accents will sell age and character without turning Hewelin into a sci‑fi prop.
- Base storytelling: Add little basing details (stones, moss, a discarded coin) to give the miniature a micro‑story that you can reference during banter at game night.

The box invites experimentation, whether you want a vivid, comic‑book look or a gritty, battle‑worn aesthetic. The sculpt supports both directions with bold shapes and clear lines that reward careful brushwork as much as quick, fun painting sessions.
