## Gameplay Overview: How It Feels to Play Card Sharks in a Living Room
Card Sharks as a board game attempts to translate the tension of real-time card guessing into a social event with friends and family. The core is simple enough that it can be learned in a single afternoon, yet nuanced enough to produce dramatic comebacks, surprising strategic moves, and the occasional “I was this close!” moment that makes you reconsider every risky decision you’ve ever made in a board game.

### Core Mechanisms
- Card Guessing Rounds: Players draw cards and attempt to predict whether the next card will be higher or lower, with points awarded for accurate predictions. The interplay between risk and reward creates tense moments that can escalate quickly from casual chat to “we all just witnessed a small miracle.”
- The Board Challenge: A board section introduces a visual element that resembles the show’s famous “survey” or “card reveal” moments, presenting players with the chance to stake chips on outcomes tied to the next card.
- Host Commentary: The game includes guide cards or modules that simulate host commentary, providing flavor text that mimics John McHale’s signature cadence. It’s basically stand-up comedian energy translated into table stakes.
- Scoring and Endgame: The scoring system rewards accurate predictions and clever play while keeping the pace brisk enough to maintain excitement through multiple rounds. Endgame often hinges on a final-room drama moment where a single correct guess can swing the entire board.

### House Rules and Family Tuning
One of the joys of retro board games is the opportunity to tweak rules to fit your group. Card Sharks invites you to do just that: adjust the number of rounds, tweak the high/low thresholds, or insert a silly penalty for overconfident players who keep shouting “higher” but end up with a “lower” disaster. If someone’s eye-rolling becomes a running gag, you’ve achieved classic Card Sharks status.
