## Material & Construction
### Cotton is King, But Not a Spoiled Prince
Cotton fibers are the backbone of this flat weave, and you can tell. Cotton is breathable, washable to a degree, and has a pleasant “soft-but-resilient” feel that punches above its weight in the durability department. The flat weave means there’s less pile to flatten under your boots, your desk chair, or your D&D dice tray—perfect for busy hallways or coffee nooks that are always getting foot traffic from your cat who swears it’s a dragon and demands a throne.

### The Flat Weave Advantage
Flat weave rugs are created by weaving weft and warp threads at the same level, resulting in a lean, low-profile surface. This isn’t the fluffy shag you see in a 70s movie; it’s a sleek, efficient workhorse that cleans up faster than you can say “lint roller.” The absence of a thick pile reduces the chance of tripping, which in Geeknite terms is a win for both humans and tiny robots that wobble around your house collecting dust like stat-boosts in a JRPG.

## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance
### Everyday Care
Cotton is forgiving but not invincible. Light vacuuming with a brush setting helps keep dust from embedding. Do not use a beater bar on carpeted vacuums; the flat weave is more delicate than a space shuttle’s paint job. If a spill occurs, blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth. Rubbing can push stains deeper into the fiber and you’ll be sad when you discover a galaxy of coffee ring patterns forming across the motif.

### Stains and Cleaning Dilemmas
For minor stains, a mild detergent solution (a few drops of dish soap in warm water) can work wonders. Dab, don’t soak. Remember: cotton loves air, not soaking baths. For more stubborn stains, consider a professional cleaning on a periodic basis. Quick tip: test any cleaning solution on a small inconspicuous area first to avoid weird color shifts that resemble a failed code patch.

### Rotating and Sun Exposure
If your rug sits in a sunny west-facing window, rotate it every few months to ensure color evenness and reduce sun-bleeding. This is the rug equivalent of defragmenting your hard drive—annoying in the moment, but you’ll thank yourself later when the color stays even across the surface.
