## Assembly and Maintenance
Let’s be real for a moment: this isn’t a furniture kit you slap together during a Sunday afternoon. The Infinite Cornerstation is a serious piece of equipment, meant to handle four workstations with all their cables, monitors, and personal keyboards like a well-choreographed orchestra. Expect a multi-hour assembly process, especially if you’re doing it with a team rather than a solo assembly hacker with a coffee addiction. The instruction set is thorough but not overly verbose; think of it as “read once, assemble with friends, then re-check three times” level of guidance.

A good rule of thumb: designate a primary assembler who becomes the project manager of the day. You’ll want at least two spare hands to help with lifting, aligning, and securing the panels. The modular design helps; you can assemble in sections and then join them, which makes the process more forgiving than trying to wrestle a single, giant block into place. As with any sizeable furniture, you’ll hit a few “adjust this” moments where you realize you should have measured one more time and maybe brought a level for the final alignment. A good practice is to install the privacy panels after the main frame is stable; it avoids misalignment that could make the panels creak or sit wonky when people lean back in their chairs.

Maintenance is straightforward: wipe with a damp cloth for most everyday grime; the Oak White veneer is forgiving but not invincible to hardcore scrubbing. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the finish. For cable channels, a periodic reorganization helps prevent the dreaded “every cable has a different connector” cascade. If you’re using the panels to run a lot of power strips, consider adding a modest surge protector to the base and securing it with the provided clips so you don’t have a gravity-powered spaghetti avalanche whenever someone moves their chair more enthusiastically than necessary.
