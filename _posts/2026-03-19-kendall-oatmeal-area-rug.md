This rug is not just a pretty face; it’s a robust underfoot warrior against typical family chaos. ### Vacuuming and Maintenance

A regular vacuum is all it takes to keep Kendall looking its best. The low pile minimizes snagging and makes daily vacuuming an unremarkable chore rather than a full-blown cleaning crusade. If you have a pet with long hair or a dust-happy toddler with a fascination for glitter, you will still need to address hair tumbleweeds and glitter trails like you would with any other rug. The key is consistency: a quick run every few days prevents the accumulation of the weird dust bunnies that herd together and plot your next furniture rearrangement.

### Cleaning Tips and Tricks

- Blot spills quickly to prevent staining; do not rub, as friction is the enemy of fabric serenity. 
- For most stains, a mild detergent solution on a clean cloth works best; test on a small corner to ensure colorfastness. 
- If you have a stubborn spot, a gentle scrub with a soft-bristle brush can help, but avoid aggressive motion that could loosen fibers. 
- For deeper cleanings, consider professional cleaning options that are compatible with synthetic low-pile rugs. 
- Use a non-slip rug pad to keep Kendall in place and to create a little frictionless runway for your feet during late-night snack runs.
