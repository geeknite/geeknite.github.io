## How to play at a glance
Exploding Kittens is a game of nerve and timing. On your turn you may play one or more action cards to manipulate the draw, skip your turn, peek at upcoming cards, or steal a card from another player. The goal is to avoid drawing an exploding kitten card unless you want to be spectacularly eliminated from the round. Defuse cards can be used to neutralize an exploding kitten if you’re lucky enough to draw one with your defuse handy.

Key mechanics to know:
- Turn structure: draw a card, then optionally play action cards in your hand to stall, defend, or sabotage.
- Counterplay: many cards allow you to force other players to draw, steal cards, or skip turns—classic “I’m not mad, I’m just strategic” vibes.
- Exploding kitten cards: if you draw one and you don’t have a defuse, you’re out for that round. If you do have a defuse, you can use it and continue playing—but you’ll have used up a precious defuse card in your hand.
- Endgame: rounds continue until only one player remains. Then you reset, shuffle, and start again if you’re playing multiple rounds.

Pro tip: the UK edition’s pacing is friendlier when you keep actions to a handful per turn. It’s easy to overwhelm people with too many options, especially when there are kids in the room who will point out that a pizza break is now a legal requirement for “just one more round.”
