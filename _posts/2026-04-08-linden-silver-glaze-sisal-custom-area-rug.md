## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity

### Daily use and vacuuming habits

Sisal fibers are sturdy but not invincible. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps keep surface dirt at bay. Avoid high-powered vacuums that could rough up the fibers. If possible, use a brushless or soft-brush attachment and rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear, especially near doorways and main walkways.

### Cleaning spills and stains

Sisal does not forgive moisture easily. If you spill coffee, red wine, or soy sauce, blot immediately with a clean cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible. Rubbing can push the stain deeper into the fibers. For stubborn spots, use a mild detergent solution and dab rather than saturate. Always test any cleaning solution on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness and avoid ring marks. If stains persist, professional cleaning is a wise choice.

### Sunlight and fading

Natural fibers can fade subtly with prolonged sun exposure. If your Linden rug sits near a big window, rotate furniture or use shades during peak sun hours to preserve color balance between glaze and fiber. The glaze provides a bit of reflective interest as light shifts through the day, which helps the rug feel alive without losing its natural warmth.

### Spills, pets, and snagging

Pets are adorable until they discover the thrill of dragging the rug. If you have claws, train them to avoid snagging the edges and consider a quality rug pad to reduce friction. In spaces with kids and pets, keep a spot-cleaning kit on hand and address accidents promptly to prevent staining and wear.

## Put It in Practice: Room Measurements and Layout Tips

Before you commit, map the room and measure the floor space. A common dining room guideline is to leave at least 24 inches of rug beyond the chair backs so chairs won t snag on the edge when people pull out their seats. For living rooms, allow 18 to 24 inches of bare floor around the rug to help room scale feel balanced. When working with custom sizes, you can tailor the rug to snugly fit an alcove or define a seating arrangement with surgical precision.

To visualize the intended layout, painters tape can outline the footprint on the floor. Stand on the taped area with your table and chairs in place and test traffic flow. If you can navigate around the tape zone without elbowing coffee tables or tripping over rug edges, you likely have a winner. Remember, this is a practical design exercise that blends art with geometry.
