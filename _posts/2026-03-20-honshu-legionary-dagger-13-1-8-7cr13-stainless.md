## Maintenance Insights

### Cleaning and storage

After handling a dagger of this type, a careful wipe down with a soft cloth is a good habit. If you use it, clean the blade to remove fingerprints and oils, then dry it thoroughly. Storing the dagger in a dry place prevents rust and helps maintain the leather sheath integrity.

### Sharpening routine

Sharpening a budget blade for good results is a skill that rewards patience. A light honing on a ceramic or fine file and careful maintenance can keep the edge serviceable for a long period. Because 7cr13 isnt the hardest steel, you will want to avoid aggressive over sharpening that can create micro chipping and reduce edge stability.
