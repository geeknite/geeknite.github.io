## Strategy and Tactics

### Early game: establishing doom momentum

In the early turns, you’re building your doom engine. You’ll want to balance card draw with doom deployment so you don’t flood your own hand with too many negatives too early. Remember, in Gloom-lite, doom cards aren’t just negative—they unlock the board’s potential for bigger catastrophes later. A well-timed doom can push a rival’s family from widow to woe in a single breath, while you quietly keep your own family from combusting in a spectacularly wrong way.

### Midgame: card synergies and misfortune timing

The sweet spot for most players is finding synergies between cards that amplify each other. A doom card that triples the effect of a later card can swing momentum from neutral to catastrophic in a single round. Keep an eye on the timing, because sometimes delaying a doom is more valuable than getting it out immediately. You’re not just flipping cards—you’re orchestrating a little cosmic cabaret where the punchline lands at just the right moment.

### Endgame: closing the narrative

In the late game, the scoreboard becomes a narrative. It’s not just who wins or loses; it’s whose tragedy has the most dramatic arc.
