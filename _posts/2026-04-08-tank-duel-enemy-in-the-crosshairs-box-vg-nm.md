## How to Play: A Quick Run-Through

Tank Duel is designed for two players who want a brisk duel rather than a marathon. The core loop can be summarized in three beats: setup, maneuver, and impact resolution. The setup phase is all about selecting a starting position, deploying tanks, and setting the initial fog of war. The maneuver phase lets players move units across the grid, manage terrain advantages, and line up shots. The resolution phase calculates damage, checks for critical hits, and updates the map status. The winner is the player who reduces the opponent’s decisive points to zero or who achieves a victory condition defined by the mission card in play.

What makes this particular box sing is the balance between accessibility and depth. The rules do not require a priest-level ceremony to understand, yet the flywheel of tactics feels substantial enough to reward experimentation. You can learn the core rules in a single sitting, and then your subsequent sessions will reveal a more nuanced interplay of terrain, line of sight, and armor value. The game rewards careful positioning, risk calculation, and the occasional bluff—like pretending to retreat so your opponent overcommits and leaves an exposed flank.

## Maintenance, Storage, and Care Tips

To keep your VG+ NM copy humming for years, treat it with a touch of care. Store the box in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to avoid map sheet yellowing and insert warping. Use a shallow tray to separate counters by type, which makes setup faster and reduces the risk of misplacing pieces. If a counter edge begins to fray, a tiny drop of archival glue can stabilize it; just be careful not to overdo it. Finally, consider a small humidification solution in the storage area if you live in a very dry climate—your map sheet will thank you, and you will avoid the dreaded brittle edges that make every move feel like a nerve-wracking puzzle.
