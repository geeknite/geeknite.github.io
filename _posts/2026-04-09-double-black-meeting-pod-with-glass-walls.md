Acoustics, privacy, and glass realities

Glass walls are a double edged sword for acoustics. They look elegant and provide visibility, but sound travels differently in a glass enclosure. The Double Black solves this with a layered approach: tempered glass, bonded sound dampening panels on the interior, and a ceiling treatment designed to absorb reflections. In our test, spoken word was clear, but there was a noticeable drop in ambient noise compared to an open plan. If your meetings routinely involve sensitive information, a glass enclosure does not automatically guarantee privacy from adjacent spaces. You want to couple the glass with a solid privacy policy inside the pod, and you may consider adding acoustic curtains or sealing gaskets around the door to reduce leakage. The manufacturer offers optional acoustic upgrades that boost the STC rating by a few points, which can make a tangible difference in open office environments. 

For teams that rely on video calls, the glass can present a challenge if callers are sensitive to reflections. A remote participant may see office activity behind your team, which is not ideal if someone is scouring for snacks on the break room shelf. The remedy is simple: use the built in timing options to modulate lighting and glass tint for calls, and keep the door closed during important video conferences.

Field test highlights and lessons learned

We put the pod through a two week test cycle with a mixed team of engineers, designers, and a sales rep who forgets to mute his video mic. The experience yielded a handful of useful insights. First, the visibility of glass walls improved collaboration by inviting random face to face interactions during a design sprint. People outside the pod would often wave and share quick notes through the glass, which kept the energy high and the hallway interruptions low. Second, the acoustics were effective for normal speech but not perfect for very loud post it note tapping or keyboard clacking. For tech teams, this is acceptable as long as participants keep voices at a normal level. Finally, the modular seating configuration mattered a lot. A slightly different arrangement unlocked a much more comfortable flow for longer meetings and reduced the need for people to rearrange chairs mid session.
