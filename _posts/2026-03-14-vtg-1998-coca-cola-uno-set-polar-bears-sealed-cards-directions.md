## Condition considerations: graded mint versus display friendly

Mint condition sealed sets are the gold standard for collectors. A mint sealed set preserves the pristine edge wear, the cardboard resilience, and the original gloss on the card faces. For sealed sets, look for:
- A tight, unbroken seal with no tears or lifting on the edges.
- A box that shows no signs of moisture damage, mold, or warping.
- Card decks that are crisp, with no yellowing on the cards themselves.
- A directions sheet that has not been crumpled and still sits flat inside the box.

If you decide to open a sealed set for play or display, you trade some market value for the joy of experiencing the nostalgia firsthand. Once opened, you can still maintain value by preserving the cards in soft sleeves and storing them away from direct sunlight.

## How to store and care for this edition when you are not playing

Storage is the unsung hero of preserving any vintage game set. Here are some practical guidelines to keep your Coca-Cola UNO in top shape:
- Keep the box in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fader from the box art and card backs.
- If you own a sealed set, avoid opening it unless you are prepared to rehouse the box in a protective display case or a poly sleeve for the box.
- For opened decks, use card sleeves and a card holder to reduce wear on the edges and corners.
- Avoid storing in an attic or basement where temperature and humidity swings can degrade the cardboard and card stock over time.
- If you plan to display the box, consider a UV-protective frame or a protective acrylic case. This keeps dust off and prevents accidental moisture exposure.
