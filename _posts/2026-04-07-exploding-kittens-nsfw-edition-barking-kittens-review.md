- The art and jokes are more daring, which means more moments that are laugh-out-loud and, in some circles, cringe-worthy in a delightful way. You’ll still be fighting to limit your own hand while poking at opponents’ vulnerabilities. The NSFW additions are designed to be memorable rather than overpowered, which means it remains accessible to casual players while providing a little extra spark for veteran table-toppers.

The NSFW edition does not transform Exploding Kittens into a different genre. It enhances the laff-o-meter and raises the stakes of social interaction. ## How to play quickly with NSFW and Barking Kittens

If you already know Exploding Kittens, the transition to NSFW Edition plus Barking Kittens is mostly about adding a few cards and a dash of personality. Here’s a quick-start guide to get you into the chaos within minutes:

1) Set up. Shuffle the NSFW deck with the Barking Kittens expansion cards. Deal a starting hand to each player. Decide on a starting player and place the deck in the center.
2) Learn the new faces. Spend a few minutes going over the new dog cards and the specific interactions they have with cat and explode cards. The rulebook does a decent job outlining the new edges without burying you in jargon.
3) Start the rounds. On your turn you draw a card and may play any combination of your cards to advance the group toward another Expanding Cat catastrophe. The NSFW additions give you more ways to be cheeky while the Barking Kittens adds a new dimension to who is likely to draw next.
4) Manage the risk. As with the base game, the goal is to avoid exploding. Defuse cards still matter, but you’ll notice the occasional random twist that makes a defusal feel like winning the lottery or surviving a very awkward family dinner.
5) Endgame and laughs. When the last Exploding Kitten is drawn or used in a way that ends the round, the winner collects a small bragging-right trophy and the group drinks in the glow of victory or consoles each other for the epic fail. The NSFW jokes will pop at predictable times, and the Barking Kittens expansions will ensure there’s never a dull moment when a dog card interrupts a plan you spent two anxious minutes defending.

Tip: use house rules if your group requests them—perhaps a “gentlemanly” or “lady-like” pause rule before the final draw to stretch the suspense. Or keep it purely chaotic and let the dogs and cats argue for the mic. Either way, you’ll end with stories that will get retold at future game nights.
