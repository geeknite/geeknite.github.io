## Installation and alignment notes
Installing the Mount Kit AU is a straightforward process if you approach it with a plan. Here is a pragmatic walkthrough that reflects common field practices, not a rigid requirement list from a manual you will read once and forget.

### Step 1: Pre install checks
- Verify you have the correct PTP 550 unit and the matching AU Mount Kit variant. Mismatch can mean non‑fit hardware or functional issues.
- Inspect the mounting surface. If it is a pole, measure the diameter and ensure clamps will fit. If it is a wall or a roof gable, assess whether you need additional brackets or an alternative mounting option.
- Check the line cord and power outlet availability. Australia uses specific plug types and safety standards; ensure the line cord is rated for outdoor use and fit to the power inlet on the device or inline power supply.
- Confirm the weather readiness. If you are in a coastal region or an area with high humidity, you may want extra sealing around entry points and weatherproofing grease on threads.

### Step 2: Attach the mount to the base end
- Open the mounting bracket and align the holes with the pole clamps.
- Fasten clamps with appropriate bolts. Use thread lock if recommended by the kit for vibration resistance.
- Verify that the bracket is square to the pole axis. A tiny misalignment is easy to correct before you mount the radio unit on the bracket.

### Step 3: Prepare the PTP 550 unit for mounting
- Remove any protective covers from the radio unit and from the mount interface area.
- Check the orientation marks on the PTP 550 base. The alignment will be easier if you reference a known direction or a pre‑drilled alignment mark on the mount.
- If your kit includes a temporary alignment guide, use it to stage the radio near the mount before final tightening.

### Step 4: Mount the radio and secure cabling
- Attach the PTP 550 to the mount using the provided screws or clamps. Do not over tighten; torque values exist to prevent warping the chassis or damaging the mounting surface.
- Route the line cord through glands or weatherproof conduits as designed by the kit. Keep the line cord away from any protruding connectors to reduce wear.
- Attach the line cord to the radio or inline power supply, depending on your configuration. If you use PoE, ensure the PoE injector or switch is placed in a suitable indoor or sheltered location to minimize exposure.
- If your kit includes a grounding option, attach the grounding strap to the appropriate point and secure to your building or tower grounding network.

### Step 5: Alignment and final checks
- Power up the PTP 550 and begin the alignment process. Use a standard signal strength and SNR readout to adjust azimuth, elevation, and tilt. A good practice is to start with coarse steps and then tighten to fine movements as you approach the sweet spot.
- Once you have a strong link, recheck the mechanical fixtures for any movement. A quick check at the end of a wind gust can catch issues that only show up in natural conditions.
- Confirm the line cord and cable entries remain sealed after the final assembly. Re-seal any compromised entry points to maintain the IP66 or higher rating your kit claims to support outdoor use.

### Step 6: Functional validation
- Run a basic throughput test if possible and compare against your expected backhaul requirements. If you see surprising drops, revisit the alignment and ensure there is no obstruction on the line of sight.
- Check the power stability. A flicker in the light or occasional reset can indicate a power integrity issue that may be resolved by adjusting the line cord or moving to a more robust power source.

If you follow these steps, you should experience a clean, repeatable mounting process with fewer surprises during the critical post‑install commissioning window.
