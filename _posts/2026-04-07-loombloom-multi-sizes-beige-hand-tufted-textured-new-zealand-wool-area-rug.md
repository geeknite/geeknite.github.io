### Hand tufting explained
Hand tufting is the process of inserting yarns into a fabric backing to build up the pile. It creates depth and texture that a flat machine weave cannot easily replicate. You can think of it as a tiny needle artist at work, weaving a landscape of loops and twists. The result is a tactile surface that rewards foot traffic and casual strolls across the rug with micro textures that catch the light from different angles.

### Dye and colorfastness notes

## Sizing and layout guidance
LoomBloom offers several size options, typically including common choices such as 5x8, 6x9, 8x10, and sometimes larger. When selecting size, consider the following:

- Living room: The largest rug should extend beyond the front legs of all seating, creating a unified area. A 8x10 works well with a standard mid to large sectional, while a 6x9 can be a good fit for a larger sofa layout against a wall. If the rug is too small, you risk creating a disjointed look that makes your furniture seem to be floating without a carpet halo.
- Dining room: A dining rug should extend at least 24 inches beyond the table on all sides to accommodate chairs pulled out. For a standard 6 to 8 seat table, a 9x12 or 8x10 is a good rule of thumb. The hand tufted construction ensures the pile remains comfortable under chairs, even when someone forgets to scoot the chair in properly.
- Bedroom: A rug under the bed should be large enough to extend beyond the sides and foot of the bed by at least 18 inches for a balanced footprint. A 8x10 under a queen bed typically works nicely.

## Comfort and daily performance
Foot traffic is the real stress test for any rug. The LoomBloom rug with its hand tufted construction has a plush pile height that offers a comfortable lounging surface while still being easy to vacuum. If you are used to synthetic, low pile carpets, transition may feel like stepping onto a cloud that has earned a PhD in physics. The friction of wool fibers adds a natural grip, preventing slips on slick floors, though area rugs alone cannot replace non slip pads. We recommend pairing the rug with a non slip rug pad suitable for the chosen size. Also consider the pad thickness; too thick and you lose the plush feel, too thin and the rug may slide around or bunch up under heavy traffic.

Another practical aspect is cleaning. Wool is relatively stain resistant, especially when treated early. The natural oils in wool help repel moisture to a degree, and the fibers hold up well under regular vacuuming. Spills should be blotted promptly with a clean towel; avoid rubbing, which pushes the stain deeper. If a more stubborn stain arises, consult a light wool safe cleaner recommended by rug care guides. ## Durability and edge behavior
Durability for hand tufted wool rugs depends on fiber density, pile height, and backing quality. The LoomBloom rug claims to offer a robust pile that stands up to everyday wear while maintaining a refined look. In real world testing, expect minor shedding in the first few weeks as the loose fibers work themselves out. A gentle vacuum with a brush head (high pile setting) is recommended to avoid pulling and to keep the tufts tidy. Over time you may notice slight flattening in high traffic portions, which is normal for plush wool carpets. Regular rotation helps even out wear and preserves the rug's character. The backing should lie flat after initial installation, and any curling edges can be eased with light weight books or furniture blocks under corners during the first week.

### Edge care tips
Pay attention to the edges, especially if you have active kids or pets. Some looms produce slightly wavy edges early on. A good rug pad with grippy edges can help keep the boundary line clean and prevent curling from becoming a permanent feature. If you notice curling persist beyond a couple of weeks, consult a professional or your rug retailer for advice on re-stretching or edge sealing options.

## Care and maintenance basics
Caring for a wool area rug requires patience and routine. The steps below summarize an approach that balances performance with ease:

- Vacuum regularly with a suction only attachment; avoid stiff brushes that can fray the fibers.
- Rotate the rug every 3 to 6 months to ensure even wear and fading.
- Address spills promptly by blotting with a clean colorfast cloth; avoid rubbing.
- Use a wool safe cleaner for tough stains, following the product instructions.
- Consider a high quality rug pad to improve comfort, extend life, and reduce slipping.
