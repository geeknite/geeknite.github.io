## What is the Infinite 4 Person Cornerstation W Screens
At its core, this product is a large, L-shaped corner desk configuration designed to seat four people around a central space. Each “seat” has access to its own section of laminated desk surface, with a shared central corner that expands the usable area for peripherals, power strips, and personal devices. The “W Screens” are privacy panels integrated into the periphery—think of it as a lightweight run of white acrylic that provides a gentle boundary between workstations without completely isolating teammates from the group energy.

The total footprint is generous: 3630 mm in one axis and 3030 mm in the other, with a height of 1200 mm. That translates to roughly 11.9 feet by 9.9 feet, and about 3.9 feet tall. If you live in a tiny apartment and you’re trying to squeeze in four full-sized monitors with their stands, this desk isn’t going to apologize for its footprint. It is what it is: a boundary-friendly, four-person arena for collaboration and concentration at scale.

I should also note the white colorway. White surfaces tend to read clean and modern, but they can reveal dust and fingerprints the way a cat reveals a sleeping pattern: relentlessly. You’ll want to wipe this thing down with a microfiber cloth with some mild cleaner every week, and perhaps consider protective desk mats for the days you’re juggling hot coffee mugs and hot takes.

## Setup, Assembly, and Quick Tips
### Unboxing Experience
Expect a multi-box delivery with a lot of hardware. The product is modular, which helps with transport, but it also means you’ll be sorting screws into a dozen zip bags and cross-referencing assembly diagrams. The upside is that if you’re a person who enjoys DIY puzzles, this is your moment: you’ll feel like an interior architect with a budget that doesn’t involve a real architectural firm.

### Assembly Timeline
If you’ve done flat-pack furniture before, you’ll recognize the flow: frame assembly, panel alignment, screen mounting, cable management hardware, and finally the finishing touches. A typical two-person crew can set this up in a day, depending on room space and the level of precision you demand for cable control. If you have a lot of peripherals to mount, you’ll want to budget additional time for cable routing behind the screens and along the internal runners. Remember: patience is a virtue, and a level is your best friend here. A wonky surface will magnify every minor misalignment across four person stations.

### Cable Management and Organization
One of the nicest practical touches is the meal-kit-like approach to cable routing. Expect cable grommets at each workstation and a central channel that can accommodate four power strips, USB hubs, and a handful of HDMI or DisplayPort cables. The goal is to keep the surface free of cable chaos, because a clean surface looks expensive and works better when your hands aren’t playing Twister with a power strip every time someone taps a key.

### Maintenance Tips
To keep the white finish pristine, wipe down surfaces weekly with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaning solution. Avoid abrasive materials that could scratch the laminated surface. For the screens, a gentle wipe to remove dust and fingerprints will preserve their clarity. If you’re a red-eye worker who tends to snack on your desk, consider a dedicated desk mat to protect the surface from spills and crumbs that might attract office ants or the moral equivalent of a tiny cybernetic rodent.
