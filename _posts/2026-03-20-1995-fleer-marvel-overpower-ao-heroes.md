## What is OverPower anyway
OverPower is a two player collectible card game that blends the cinematic flair of marvel comics with card driven battles
think of it as a tabletop skirmish where the hero team you assemble is your army and the villain team on the other side is their opposing force
cards represent heroes, allies, locations, artifacts, and events that shape the battlefield
the goal is simple yet glorious reduce the opponents life points to zero while guarding your own and ideally pulling off a fancy synergy that makes you feel like a genius
the A-O slice of this set focuses on heroes whose names begin with A through O so if you grew up with ant man or ororo munroe this is your nostalgic lane

## How the cards actually work
this is where a lot of nostalgia and some honest critique intersect
overpower uses a mix of stats and abilities on each card that indicate attack power defense resilience and special moves
you will see a few recurring patterns across the A-O heroes
- energy or power cost: most heroic abilities require spending a resource to execute a move
- attack and defense values: a standard measure of how much you can hit and how hard you can be hit
- special abilities or powers: these are usually flavor plus mechanical effects that can alter the board state such as extra draws extra moves or board wide effects
- team buffs: some cards provide benefits when paired with specific allies which encourages deck building and strategy planning
- limited effects like one time boosts or situational bonuses that reward good timing and board state awareness
what this means in practice is you can play a defensive game with a strong lead on the board by using location cards and allies to protect your heroes or dive into an aggressive sprint that aims to overwhelm in a couple of brutal turns
for players who love micro decision making OverPower rewards you for optimizing turn orders and counting power tokens so if you enjoy the puzzle of a well constructed turn window this set rewards that approach

## Card mechanics and deck building notes

- power economy matters: you have to manage your resource pool carefully or you will be left with stalling turns while your opponent moves forward
- synergy matters: certain heroes complement each other with boosts or shared effects you can build a small engine that outperforms raw punch power
- location and artifact cards: these can tilt the board by altering line of sight or granting temporary boosts and those cards often decide the mid game
- hero layering: you can pair a fast moving hero with a sturdier ally to cover two fronts at once which keeps your options open
- villain interactions: not every card is a pure hero card the adversaries and villains on the other side push back with their own tactics creating a tug of war dynamic

if you want to dive into quick deck ideas we recommend starting with a core of three to five reliable heroes from the A-O range and add a couple of supporting allies and locations to shore up weaknesses
this gives you a balanced approach that can handle both control and tempo strategies without folding to a single trick

## How to collect and what to chase in the a-o window
if you are rebuilding a vintage marvel deck or just starting out here are some practical tips on what to hunt for
- aim for a balanced ratio of heroes to support cards not every deck needs a full set of artifacts but a couple of well chosen locations can dramatically shift the odds

## Quick starter guide for new players
if you are approaching this set with fresh eyes here is a simple path to a fun first game
1) pick three core heroes from the a-o roster that offer a mix of offense defense and utility
2) add two location or artifact cards that buff your preferred playstyle
3) choose a couple of allies that can extend your board presence and provide resilience
