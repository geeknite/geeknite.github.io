## Maintenance and cleaning
Cleaning is straightforward. Wipe the mat and frame with a damp cloth and avoid harsh cleaners that might degrade padding. Regular inspection is wise: check bolts and joints for looseness and re-tighten as needed. A wobble mid-hop can be dramatic, especially if it collides with a couch shaped like a toppled fortress. A quick wipe-down after muddy adventures keeps surfaces hygienic and ready for the next round of hop-based chaos.

If you notice loose components, stop using the unit and address the issue. A safe, well-maintained trampoline is a joyful trampoline. Your future self will thank you for the extra minutes of peace that come after you fix the wobble rather than pretending it’s not there and hoping the problem goes away.

## Troubleshooting and tips
- If the bounce feels uneven, check the leg height on each corner and make sure the feet are all firmly planted on a flat surface.
- If the mat looks stretched or saggy, consult the user manual for recalibration steps or consider replacing a worn mat due to intense use.
- To minimize scuffs near edges, place a soft foam mat around the perimeter. It also protects little toes when the energy levels spike and jumping becomes a game of leapfrog obstacle course.
- Keep a small maintenance kit handy: adjustable wrench, lubricant for moving parts, and a spare pad in case of unexpected wear.
