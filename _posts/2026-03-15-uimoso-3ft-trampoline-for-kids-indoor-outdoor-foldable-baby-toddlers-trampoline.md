## Safety first: Guard rails, mats, and safe play routines
The safety plan includes a padded edge and a sturdy frame. The safety net is not always included in the budget version, depending on the model. If you do get a version with a safety net, ensure you secure it properly and remind kids that the net is for containment and not a launch pad. Always supervise, especially with kids who are new to bouncing and curious about launching themselves into the world like tiny astronauts.

It is also wise to check the floor surface where you place the trampoline. A soft rug on a hard floor will help reduce the risk of slipping while the kids figure out their new bouncing rhythm. If you have a slippery surface, consider placing a non-slip mat underneath to keep the trampoline anchored during the epic bounce sessions.
