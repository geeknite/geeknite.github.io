## Overview

A board game insert is basically a fancy tray system that lives inside the base game box. The idea is simple: compartments that hold all components separately and, ideally, help you set up and tear down faster. The Zombicide Black Plague insert organizer we are reviewing today is a laser-cut, MDF or wood-based marvel that claims to fit the base game and a selection of expansions with a sensible layout. The promise is loud and clear: more duct-tape-free storage and less rummaging through zombie mutt-packs every time you start a mission.

To appreciate the marvel, you first need to understand the tangle it aims to solve. Zombicide Black Plague ships with multiple zombie spawns, token decks, survivant miniatures, cards for equipment and missions, and a plethora of cards for each scenario. ## Design and Craftsmanship

The design philosophy here is modularity and category separation. Each component gets a home. Your cards go in one tile-like zone, your tokens in another, and your miniatures in a third, padded, sometimes cradled, compartment. The layout is meant to speed up setup because players can pull out entire stacks rather than juggling loose piles of chits. In practice, the fit can be perfect or gracefully imperfect depending on expansions and the box’s internal tolerances. If you own a few extras or alternative game boards, there is a non-zero chance you will need to reconfigure or omit a couple of sections to avoid overcrowding.

The engineering behind these inserts is all about tolerances. A few millimeters here and there can cascade into a box that closes with a sigh rather than a satisfying snap. A well-made insert uses precise grooves that allow trays to nest without rubbing cards or causing tokens to slide around during transport. The good news is that most reputable inserts have adjustable or flexible compartments. You can swap out foam pads for a bit more protection or rework a slot to accommodate a slightly different component size after a couple of campaigns. The downside? If you force-fit expansions that push components into non-designated slots, you risk a regressive cascade that ends with you hating your own shelf organization.

## Setup, Assembly, and Maintenance

Assembly typically follows a simple pattern:
- Open the box, lay out the trays, and identify each component.
- Clean any release residues from laser cuts with a soft brush.
- Dry-fit the trays to ensure a snug fit; do not glue yet if you want to reconfigure later.
- Apply glue to joints if required by the model; clamp and let dry according to the glue’s instructions.
- Place the trays back into the base box, perform a quick fit check, and close the lid.

Pro-tip: store a small pouch of desiccant in the box if the humidity swings are wild in your gaming closet. It helps prevent warping and keeps those token sleeves from growing a mysterious moisture aura.

Maintenance is mostly cleaning and occasional re-gluing if you travel frequently with your games. You don’t need a maintenance routine as intense as a chess grandmaster, but a quick check every few months to make sure the trays haven’t shifted is a good habit. If the insert uses lightweight veneer, avoid standing on the box during storage; gravity is unkind to precision cutouts and you will regret your life choices when you find your zombie tokens under the sofa cushions because the box rolled off the shelf during a hurricane-level gust of air.

## How to Choose Your Insert

If you are shopping for an insert for Zombicide Black Plague, here are a few practical tips:
- Measure the box: some inserts assume a tight box plus expansions; a few fractional misalignments can prevent the lid from closing properly.
- Check expansion compatibility: if you own Green Horde or Jaws of the Underworld variants, ensure the insert accommodates their tokens and cards without overcrowding.

- Plan for maintenance: a design that makes replacement parts easy to source is a nice bonus when a piece wears out.
