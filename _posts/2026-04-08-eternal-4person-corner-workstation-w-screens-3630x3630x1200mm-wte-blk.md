## Design and Ergonomics: Comfort, Space, and Subtle WiFi Interference

### Ergonomic fit for four
The ergonomic ambition here is noble: a shared corner workspace that preserves personal leg and knee clearance while maintaining a sense of communal focus. Each user has a dedicated desk surface with generous depth, allowing keyboards to sit at a natural angle and wrists to rest in a neutral position. The four-person layout encourages posture variety—some folks sit higher to match their screen angle, others prefer a more reclined, backward-leaning stance while they brainstorm. It’s not a perfect ergonomic utopia, but it’s a dramatic step up from a row of clashing wrists and an accidental elbow-to-monitor collision at 3 PM.

### Privacy panels: a gentle barrier to social sprinting
The corner design includes optional privacy panels that separate each user’s field of view without creating a fortress vibe. These panels are light enough to rearrange on a whim but sturdy enough to avoid rattling every time someone sighs dramatically after a meeting invite. If you want maximum focus, install the panels and pretend you’re in a glass-walled studio, minus the fame and with more cables.

### Monitor arms and screen alignment
The screens (or screen walls) can be mounted on adjustable arms with multi-axis tilt. For four users, you’ll want to set up symmetrical alignment so no one’s monitor blocks another’s camera during those all-hands checks. The trick is to spend a few minutes dialing tilt, distance, and height to keep eyes from wandering into the coworker’s coffee cup. If you’re new to ergonomics, think “neutral neck” and avoid the temptation to crane for a better vantage point on the office memes feed.
