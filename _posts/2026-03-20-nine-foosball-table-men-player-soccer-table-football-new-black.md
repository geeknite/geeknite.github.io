## Setup, Maintenance, and Longevity

Setting up the Black Knight is a weekend warrior’s rite of passage. It’s not the easiest assembly, but it’s not the end of the world either. The instructions are clear enough, the hardware pieces are labeled, and your patience can be stretched like taffy if you’re not careful with the alignment.

### Setup Timeline and Tips
- Pre-assembly check: Make sure you have a clean workspace, a level floor, and a playlist that won’t judge you for yelling “Goooaaal!” at a 500-point noise when you miss the easiest shot.
- Assembly pace: If you’re methodical, set aside a good block of time and don’t rush the rod installation. If you’re a midnight assembler, you’ll likely end up with a few extra nerves and a sense of accomplishment when you resolve the “sticky bolt” puzzle.
- Post-assembly calibration: Level the table, test a few shots, adjust as needed, and celebrate with a victory lap. You earned it.

### Maintenance Routine
- Wipe down surfaces after play to prevent sticky residue from coffee, juice, or sweaty hands. A mild soap solution works for most surfaces, followed by a quick wipe with a dry cloth.
- Check rod alignment and tighten screws if you hear creaks or feel wobble. A little maintenance goes a long way toward extending the life of your table.
- Lubricate the bearings periodically if you feel resistance. A small amount of silicone-based lubricant is usually enough; avoid oil-based products that could attract dust or create a slime-like film.
- Cover when not in use to preserve finish and keep curious pets at bay. A simple table cover is your best friend here.


### Durability and Longevity in Real-Life Scenarios
The Black Knight is built to withstand the chaos of family gaming sessions, casual tournaments, and late-night brain games that involve shouting two-letter strategies at the tabletop. While not a professional-grade tournament table, it’s sturdy enough to survive rambunctious children, enthusiastic friends, and the occasional spilled beverage with grace. If you’re a true power user who plans to burn the midnight oil practicing scoring patterns, you’ll want to treat it with the respect you’d give a gaming chair: regular maintenance and occasional upgrades.
