### Weather behavior

Weather resistance is a big selling point here. The wicker is designed to be resilient to moisture, and the metal frame is coated to resist rust. Still, we recommend keeping the set under a patio cover or moving it inside during heavy storms to prolong its life. Occasional rainfall is fine, but heavy winds can rearrange your cushions as if a tiny gusty orchestra conducted a chair-based minuet. If you live in a coastal climate with salt spray, bring the cushions inside after use to prevent salt abrasion on the fabric over time.

### Cleaning and maintenance

Cleaning is straightforward: wipe down with a damp cloth, then a quick dry to prevent any moisture from lurking in the weave. Cushions can be spot-cleaned with mild soap and water, and it’s a good habit to fluff them periodically to prevent clumping. A light protective spray on the wicker can help maintain its luster and prevent minor color changes from sun exposure. If you’re serious about keeping the set pristine, consider storage solutions or breathable covers for off-season use. If you forget to cover it, you’ll still be fine; just expect the occasional weathered patina that some folks actually pay for in vintage lines.

## Maintenance tips to extend life

- Use a breathable outdoor cover during off-season or prolonged rain exposure.
- Bring cushions indoors when heavy rain or long periods of sun are forecast to prevent seam wear.
- Clean the wicker gently with mild soap and water; avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the finish.
- Rotate cushions and cushions’ covers to even out wear and color fading.
- Check and tighten screws if you notice wiggle after a season of use.

Following these steps can help you preserve the aesthetic and function of the set for several seasons without turning your patio into a DIY project that consumes weekends.
