### Maintenance and care tips
- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting; avoid beater bars on delicate wool. A gentle pass keeps loose fibers in check and maintains the tufted look.
- Blot spills promptly with a clean, white cloth. Do not rub; wool fibers can behave like tiny springs and push the stain deeper if you rub aggressively.
- For stubborn spots, test a mild wool-safe cleaner in an inconspicuous area first. Always blot, never scrub, and let the rug dry completely before walking on it again.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and reduce noticeable sun fading in one area. If you’re in a sunny climate, consider window treatments to minimize sun exposure over years of use.

### Durability and wool health
Wool fibers are tough, resilient, and naturally flame-retardant to a degree. A pure wool rug tends to recover well from compression, meaning moments of cross-legged sitting or occasional yoga attempts in front of the TV won’t leave lasting creases. The natural elasticity helps the rug snap back after vacuuming and chair movement. If you have pets, wool’s lanolin content can provide a light self-cleaning effect, and the dual-tone colorway helps confine fur tumbleweeds to a visually manageable area—that is, until your cat discovers a sunny spot to lounge on this rug.
