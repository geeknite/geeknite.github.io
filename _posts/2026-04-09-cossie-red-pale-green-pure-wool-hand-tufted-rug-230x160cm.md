### Maintenance and care tips

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only mode (avoid beater bars on delicate wool). A gentle pass across the surface will pull loose fibers and maintain the uniform look of the tufted pile.
- Blot spills promptly with a clean, white cloth. Do not rub; wool fibers can work like tiny springs and push the stain deeper if you rub aggressively.
- For stubborn spots, test a mild wool-safe cleaner in an inconspicuous area first. Always blot, never scrub, and let the rug dry completely before walking on it again.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and avoid summer sun exposure causing uneven fading. If you’re in a sunny climate, consider a window treatment to minimize sun damage over years of use.

### Durability and wool health

Wool fibers are tough, resilient, and naturally flame-retardant to an extent. A pure wool rug stands up to everyday life and recovers well from compression, which means those moments when you sit cross-legged or pretend to do a yoga pose in front of the TV are less likely to permanently imprint an unwanted crease. The natural elasticity of wool fibers helps the rug snap back into shape after vacuuming or chair-scarcity drama. If you own pets, wool’s natural lanolin residue can provide a light self-cleaning property, and the colorway helps confine the occasional fur tumbleweed to a less noticeable area, at least visually speaking.
