## What makes a marquise cut special?

The marquise cut is a geometry lesson dressed in elegance. It’s essentially an elongated brilliant cut that aims to maximize visible carat weight while preserving a sharp, regal silhouette. Historically associated with the French aristocracy and the “pompadour” era’s glamour, the marquise shape can visually stretch a finger, creating the illusion of length and grace. In practical terms, this cut delivers a lot of sparkle, because the marquise is a brilliant cut with many facets—often around 58 or more depending on the pavilion and maker.

Pros of the marquise cut in a pendant:

- Maximizes perceived size: the longer silhouette makes the diamond look more substantial than its actual carat weight.
- Brilliance and fire: when well cut, marquise diamonds throw light in a way that’s both lively and sophisticated.
- Odd-but-not-awkward finger-length illusion: great on shorter necklines or when you want a touch of drama without overpowering your face.

Cons to beware:

- It can be sensitive to cupping or bowing if the cutter isn’t meticulous. You want a brilliant finish, not a boat hull with misaligned facets.
- The length-to-width ratio matters. A too-tall marquise can look skinny, a too-wide one can appear stumpy. Your best bet: trust the certificate and the jeweler’s inspection to confirm a flattering ratio (typically around 1.75 to 2.25 for a pendant).

## Understanding the GIA certification

This particular piece arrives with a GIA certificate—that’s the gold standard, pun fully intended, in diamond authentication. The GIA report will typically cover:

- Carat weight: the actual mass of the diamond. Even small fractions matter when you’re calculating value and appearance.
- Color grade: how colorless the diamond is. In a classic pendant, you’ll often see near-colorless to colorless ranges that please the eye without drawing attention to color zoning.
- Clarity: presence of inclusions or blemishes. A well-cut marquise can mask some inclusions, but a certificate helps you understand what you’re looking at under 10x magnification.
- Cut: the cut grade, which is especially crucial for a marquise because it impacts brilliance and symmetry.
- Polish and symmetry: ensure facets align cleanly for maximum light return.

## Care and maintenance: keeping the sparkle permanent

Natural diamonds are surprisingly hardy, but they’re not indestructible. Here are practical care tips to ensure your pendant remains radiant:

- Cleaning: a gentle soak in warm soapy water and a soft brush works well. Rinse thoroughly and pat dry with a lint-free cloth. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could affect metal patina over time.
- Storage: keep the pendant in a fabric-lined jewelry box or a dedicated pouch to prevent scratching and avoid tangling with other jewelry.
- Wear habits: remove the pendant during heavy physical activities or swimming to avoid exposure to chlorine and hard knocks. The chain and the bail will thank you for that small mercy.
- Regular inspection: check the bail and setting periodically for loose stones or wear in the metal. A quick professional inspection every 12–24 months can prevent gemstone loss and ensure continued peace of mind.
