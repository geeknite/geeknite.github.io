Maintenance is not glamorous, but it is essential. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps remove surface dust and hair. ## Care and Maintenance Deep Dive
### Routine Care
- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only attachment to avoid catching the fibers in a rotary brush.
- Flip and rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear, especially if you have a monolithic light source that creates patterns on the fibers.
- Use a rug pad for additional support and to minimize edges curling over time.

### Stain Wizardry (Mild Cleaners First)
- For most everyday spills, blot, do not rub, and apply a mild detergent solution. Rinse with clean water, blot dry.
- For more stubborn stains, consult the fabric care label. Avoid bleach unless the label specifically approves it.
- Odors? A sprinkle of baking soda after cleaning can help neutralize residual scents; vacuum after an hour or two.

### Maintenance Milestones
- Every 6–12 months, check for edge fraying. If present, trim or secure with a rug grip to prevent further unraveling.
- If you have pets, consider a routine schedule that minimizes pet-related accidents by using triggers like lidded litter boxes farther away from the hallway when feasible.
