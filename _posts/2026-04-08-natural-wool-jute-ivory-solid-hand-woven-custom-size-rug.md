## Durability and Maintenance
Durability is the practical heart of any rug decision. Wool fibers tend to outperform many synthetics in resilience because they have elasticity and memory. Jute, while a robust fiber, requires a bit more care to avoid fraying in high-traffic areas, especially at edges. The combination in this ivory rug yields a surface that handles daily life with relative ease, while still inviting you to walk barefoot and not feel like you’re stepping on a cobblestone.

### Cleaning Steps
- Vacuum regularly with the vacuum head at a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing can push liquid deeper into the pile.
- For spots, a mild detergent solution can be used; test in an unobtrusive area first. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can erode natural fibers.
- For deeper maintenance, professional cleaning every 12-24 months is recommended for high-traffic spaces. This helps to rejuvenate the fibers and preserve the pile’s bounce.

### Wear and Tear
- Over time, wool will show light creasing where foot traffic is high. This is normal and part of its natural transient character.
- The ivory color will reveal dirt less readily than a pure white, but you should not assume it’s maintenance-free. A routine maintenance schedule keeps it looking crisp.
