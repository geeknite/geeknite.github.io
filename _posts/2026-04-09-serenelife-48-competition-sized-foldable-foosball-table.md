## Maintenance and longevity

Maintenance is the boring-but-important adulting you must do to keep the foosball magic alive. Here are practical tips from a gamer who’s spilled more soda on gaming gear than a chaotic LAN party:

- Clean the surface regularly with a soft microfiber cloth to prevent dust buildup that could affect ball roll. A quick wipe-down after gaming sessions keeps the surface looking crisp.
- Check the leg locks and folding pins every few weeks if you move the table around. A loose pin is not scenic; it’s a hazard for matches that require rapid reactions.
- Lubricate the joints sparingly if you hear squeaks that persist after cleaning. A tiny amount goes a long way — think of it as whispering to the table rather than shouting at it.
- Replace balls if they become misshapen or heavily dented. Worn balls can disrupt consistent play and ruin your bank-shot swagger.
- Store in a dry environment to prevent warping of the MDF frame. Damp spaces are the arch-nemesis of wooden furniture, even the foldable variety.

With proper care, this table will be your gaming companion for years. It’s not built to survive a medieval siege of roughhousing, but it’s more than capable of withstanding typical family game-night chaos and the occasional tournament bracket you accidentally created in the living room.

## Tips for setup and getting the most from your setup

- Leveling is your friend: Place the table on a relatively flat surface and use a spirit level to calibrate the feet. A few millimeters of tilt can impact ball trajectories in surprising ways.
- Tweak the players if you must: Some players prefer slightly tighter or looser rods. If you’re not hitting your shots consistently, experiment with rod tension to find your sweet spot. Remember: small adjustments can have big impacts.
- Organize a mini-tournament: Family matches are not just games; they’re memories in the making. Create a bracket, assign team names, and let the trash talk flow. You’ll be surprised how much fun chaos can generate when the stakes are as low as a snack budget.
- Use a dedicated ball cleaning scheme: A quick wipe of the ball before a match reduces inconsistencies. If you’re playing in a dusty living room, you’ll thank yourself later when the ball rolls true instead of bouncing unpredictably off a ghost of a crumb.
- Keep a spare ball stash handy: The two included balls are great, but everyone knows you’ll lose one to a curious cat, a mischievous child, or a rogue sock. A couple of backups means you won’t skip a match on account of a ball’s dramatic exit from the field.
