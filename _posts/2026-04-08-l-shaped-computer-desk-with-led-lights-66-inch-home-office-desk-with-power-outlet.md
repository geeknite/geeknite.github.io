## Power outlets and cable management

The built in power outlet is the star of the show. A discreet power strip with surge protection and multiple outlets means you have room for a PC, a monitor, a printer, and still have a bit of wiggle room for a charging hub. USB ports are a nice touch on some models for quick phone charging or powering on a headset. Cable management features are the unsung heroes here: grommets, clips, and a modest cable tray help keep the ground from becoming a knot of seething cords. A clean desk is a happy desk, and a happy desk reduces the chance you trip over a hissing power strip on a late night hardware reboot.

### Practical cable management tips

- Route cables through the grommets and keep the cords grouped by function.
- Use zip ties or Velcro straps to reduce cable chaos behind the desk.
- Keep the power supply close to the desk so that you can upgrade your PC without relocating the entire room.

## Assembly, durability, and maintenance

The assembly process is straightforward but not instantaneous. Expect a solid hour or two depending on how aggressively you handle included screws and whether you read the instruction sheet like a sacred scripture. The most common pitfall is misaligning the legs or the CPU stand. Take your time and use the diagrams. Durability is quite respectable for its price range; the frame feels sturdy enough to support a heavy monitor array and a small library of mugs, if your coffee addiction reaches heroic levels. The LED strip is mounted with double sided tape that sticks well but can lose adhesion over time if you try to reposition the desk too often. If you plan to move the desk around often, consider a model with hardware mountedLEDs and fewer adhesives.

Maintenance is minimal: wipe with a damp cloth, avoid harsh solvents, and periodically check the screws for tightness. The surface is not scratch proof, but it resists typical wear and tear, and the edges are finished to prevent a casual snag of clothing.
