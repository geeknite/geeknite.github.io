## Care and maintenance: keeping it fresh without turning your life into a detergent lab
Cotton rugs are relatively straightforward to maintain. For everyday care, a routine vacuum at a gentle setting will do most of the heavy lifting, especially in rooms with foot traffic. If a spill occurs, act quickly. Blot the liquid with a clean cloth rather than rubbing, which can push the stain deeper or cause the fibers to fray. For more stubborn spots, a mild detergent mixed with water on a soft cloth can help lift the stain, followed by blotting again to remove moisture. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade cotton fibers or fade the color.

Rotation can also help with even wear. Consider rotating the rug every few months if traffic is uneven, particularly in a high-traffic doorway or under a frequently used chair. This is a small practice, but it helps prevent high spots that make your floor plan look like a bad video game map where the hero kept stepping on the same tile over and over.

To keep the colors vibrant, avoid direct sunlight for extended periods. UV exposure can lead to color fading over the long term, a not-so-subtle reminder that even decorative items have a shelf life in the sun. If you have pets, use a protective pad underneath in areas where nails could snag the surface; cotton is durable but not invincible against claws and rough play.
