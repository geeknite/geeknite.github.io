## What is the Herd Mentality Expansion Pack? (H2)
Herd Mentality has always been about collective guessing, social cues, and subtly sabotaging your friends’ confidence while you pretend you know exactly what the room is thinking. The Expansion Pack leans into that vibe with a few new goals: broaden the range of prompts, introduce new card types that tilt the balance between guesswork and bluffing, and nudge the group toward longer, more hilariously chaotic rounds.

### The core idea amplified (H3)
The expansion’s central premise is to intensify the social pressure cooker. It introduces prompts that require players to think not just about what they would say, but how their peers would misread them. It’s a deliciously cruel mechanism that rewards confident falsehoods and punishes careful hedging—exactly the kind of behavior that makes that one friend’s face turn a shade of red previously reserved for spicy sauces.

### The new components in a sentence (H3)
- New category of prompt cards designed for longer rounds and higher cringe potential. 
- A small pile of “policy” cards that alter how players may reveal information. 
- A handful of “wildcard” pieces that can change the rider on any given prompt. 
- A fresh set of tokens that influence scoring and tension in mid-rounds.

### How it fits with the base game (H3)
If the base game relied on a delicate balance of deception and crowd-sourced interpretation, the Expansion Pack adds more levers than a vintage arcade cabinet. It does not replace the original rules; it re-weights them. You’ll still flip cards, you’ll still guess, and you’ll still judge the room by the color of its collective confidence. In short: it’s an additive upgrade, not a reckless reboot.

## What's Inside the Expansion Pack (H2)
Let’s break down the actual contents and what they do for your game nights.

### Card types and new prompts (H3)
The expansion introduces several new prompt archetypes:
- Identity prompts that require players to align with a persona rather than a fact. 
- Team prompts that pit two teams against each other, amplifying the group dynamics. 
- Abstract prompts meant to provoke wild interpretation rather than factual guessing.

The practical effect is that the game space becomes a little less predictable, a little messier in the best possible way, and a lot more likely to produce that glorious “wait, what did we just decide?” moment.

### Rule-modifiers and meta-tokens (H3)
- Truth-tilt tokens: once per round, a player may force the group to reveal a tiny truth they would normally hide. This can be a blessing or a trap depending on who’s in the hot seat.
- Bluff boost cards: these allow a player to embellish an answer without penalty—great for those who consider lying a sport.
- Time-squeeze mechanic: a timer that accelerates prompts mid-round, turning polite discourse into a sprint of wild hunches.

### Thematic and aesthetic changes (H3)
The design team clearly had a sense of humor about the franchise. The art style remains recognizable, with slightly wittier captions, more tongue-in-cheek doodles, and a few Easter eggs that only the longest-running players will appreciate. It’s a small but meaningful upgrade that signals the designers were listening to the community’s requests for a more “theater of the absurd” vibe.

### Components quality and physical feel (H3)
The cards are sturdy, the tokens are tactile, and the box feel has a little more weight to it—like it’s trying to justify the extra shelf space you’ll allocate to it. If you are a meticulous collector who values production quality, you’ll be pleased; if you’re simply here for the laughs, you’ll still be pleased because the physical product doesn’t get in the way of the joke. ## Rules and How It Changes Gameplay (H2)
A lot of expansions feel like a bunch of random bits tossed into a bag and left to the players to figure out. The Herd Mentality Expansion Pack, however, wants you to feel the difference without requiring a trek through the manual that’s longer than a college semester.

### Base rules refresher (H3)
If you’ve played Herd Mentality before, you know the rhythm: someone reads a prompt, everyone writes down their answer, then you all guess which answer is the majority’s. The pack doesn’t erase that rhythm; it slightly bends it by introducing new prompts and meta-tools that influence what counts as “the majority” and how risky you want to be with your interpretation.

### Expansion-specific rules (H3)
- Start-of-round stage: you may draw a “policy” card which affects how many answers count for the majority and whether hints can be used in this round.
- Mid-round pivot: at a signal, players may swap or modify one of their own responses based on a wildcard token, creating dynamic shifts mid-flow.
- Scoring adjustment: the scoring rubric becomes a little more elastic. You’ll see pockets where bold moves pay off, and lanes where hesitation costs you more than usual.

### Example scenario (H3)
Pretend you’re in a round with a prompt about a bizarre hobby. The room’s stance is predictable until someone drops a truth-tilt card that reveals a quirky backstory. Suddenly, the majority’s “most reasonable” response looks like a counterfeit coin, and players scramble to reinterpret the blame for the hilariously wrong answer that still somehow nails the vibe of the moment. In that moment, you discover that the expansion is less a tool and more a stagehands’ crew: you don’t notice them until the curtain drops and you’re laughing so hard you forget what the original prompt even was.

The humor vein in this expansion is sharper, pokier, and more affectionate than punitive. It doesn’t go for mean-spirited roast; it leans into playful exaggeration and situational comedy. The prompts lean into pop culture, personal anecdotes, and silly hypotheticals that force players to reveal not just what they think, but how they think. There is a certain joy in watching a room of friends realize they’ve all misread the same thing in opposite directions, and the sense of shared embarrassment is contagious in a way that few party games manage to achieve without crossing into pure cringe.
