## Overview: What this guide is and isn’t

This book is not a “how to be a superhero” manual in a breezy cape-and-spandex sense. City of Mist treats heroism as a combination of mythic identity, narrative tags, and believable incompetence in the face of existential chaos. The Player's Guide focuses on the player side of the equation:

- How to craft a Mythic Identity that feels fresh, not a cosplay catalog.
- How to assemble a set of Power Tags that you can actually use in play, not just in the backstory notes you’ll never mention.
- How to collaborate with a Keeper (the GM analogue in City of Mist) to weave a city that breathes with myths and modern clutter.
- How to keep your character consistent, interesting, and capable of making a terrible life choice on a Tuesday.

If you’ve previously skimmed the Core Rulebook or the starter materials, the Player's Guide is the seasoning salt that makes your stew taste like ancient prophecy and late-night fridge raiders. It isn’t a new system overhaul; it’s a refinement pass with better examples, more player-centric tips, and a few memes about crime syndicates with really good tailor shops.

## The core idea: identity, myth, and the street-level mythos

City of Mist isn’t about flying or teleportation or cosmic power-ups. It’s about you and the story you tell when you put on a mask and declare you’re the hero in a city that pretends not to care. The Player's Guide doesn’t rewrite the game’s premise; it helps you inhabit it with more confidence and fewer awkward pauses during the table talk.

The book emphasizes three pillars: identity, power, and narrative stance. Identity is how you see yourself and how the city sees you: a detective who’s also the god of lost wallets, or a barista who’s secretly the whispering shade of a forgotten goddess. Power Tags are the mechanics that let your character lean into their myth without becoming a walking spellbook. Narrative stance is the GM-facing concept that guides how scenes unfold so they feel cinematic, but not like a superhero blockbuster—more like a caper with philosophical undertones and a soundtrack you can’t quite hear in your head.

In practical terms, you’ll be constructing a Mythic Identity with a set of core tags, then selecting additional Tags that shape how you interact with the world. Tags are not just “abilities” or “skills.” They’re the spark that gives you both gravity and a side-eye toward danger. The guide shines when it gives example Tag combinations that make sense and then shows how those Tags interact with the city’s factions, clues, and daily grind.

## Character creation: building a myth you can actually sustain

### Identity first: who are you in the city's glare?

The Player's Guide walks you through designing a Mythic Identity that isn’t just a wish-list of powers. The trick is to create a person whose personal myth resonates with a local myth or a contemporary city issue. You might be a “Goddess of Night Bus Routes” who appears to passengers as a comforting rumor, or a “Shadow Librarian” who guards a forgotten catalog of city sins. The book gives prompts to remix myths with modernity: what would the ancient hero’s weakness look like in a city that never turns off the streetlights? What does your identity say about your ethics when every choice has a social consequence?

### Tags: the engine of your legend

Tags are the bread and butter of City of Mist. They are descriptors that tell the city how you operate and how the world might react to you. In the Player's Guide, you’ll find structured guidance on choosing Tags that interact well with your identity and with the city’s current story. The guide emphasizes cohesion: your Tags should inform your actions, your dialogue, and your relationships with allies and antagonists. The best Tag combinations create moments where a single line or a single look in a scene can flip the entire outcome—without requiring a heroic sprint across rooftops.

### Gimmicks, Complications, and the human factor

A great Mythic Identity isn’t a one-trick pony. It’s a living, breathing person with a favorite coffee, an unresolved want, and a pattern of making questionable decisions under pressure. The Player's Guide dedicates space to how your character’s gimmicks—quirks, catchphrases, or signature methods—shape not only combat or challenges but also social interactions and investigative beats. Complications, those narrative rocks that trip you up, are not punishments; they’re opportunities for storytelling. The guide provides examples where a character’s reliance on a Tag creates room for dramatic irony or a moral dilemma that reveals more about the city than about their own power level.

### The power balance: avoiding the “too-much-magic” trap

A common pitfall in any mythic game is the temptation to stack Tags into a one-way steamroller. City of Mist intentionally limits power by embedding Tags within a story world that can harness them but doesn’t always forgive cleverness. The Player's Guide helps you calibrate your character so that your Tags grant you narrative leverage rather than simply guaranteeing success. The recommended practice is to pair a strong Identity with a set of Tags that produce interesting consequences when used creatively. The guide provides tableaus and scenarios that show how two or three well-chosen Tags can create a surprising array of outcomes—usually with a side of noir irony.

### Example builds and walk-throughs

The guide is peppered with sample builds that illustrate the philosophy above. You’ll see how a “Mercantile Oracle” blends economic intuition with prophetic insight, or how a “Night-Shift Alchemist” trades in everyday miracles that don’t always pay their tab. The examples aren’t static; they’re designed to be deconstructed and rebuilt, so you can see how your choices ripple through sessions and campaigns. If you’re a player who loves world-building, you’ll appreciate the way the guide nudges you toward narratives that feel personal yet universal.

## Rules and mechanics: what to roll, what to narrate, and what to argue about at the table

City of Mist uses a streamlined system that emphasizes storytelling over dice gymnastics. The Player's Guide clarifies common questions and fills in the gaps left by the core book. Here’s a quick tour of what you’ll encounter and why it matters at the table.

### Dice and resolution: keeping it cinematic

The dice mechanic in City of Mist is designed to be intuitive. You roll a pool of dice that corresponds to your Tags in a scene, looking for results that reveal the city’s mood and your character’s fate. The guide offers tips for pacing: know when to narrate a cool moment and when to lean on the Keeper to present a consequence that moves the story forward. You’ll learn how to interpret mixed results—those 7–9s that provide a partial success—and turn them into meaningful narrative beats rather than awkward “fake outs.” The guide’s commentary on success thresholds is practical and, more importantly, adaptable for one-shots and long campaigns alike.

### Clues, scenes, and the city as a character

One of City of Mist’s strengths is treating the city as a living, breathing character. The Player's Guide doubles down on that concept, showing you how to structure scenes so that clues emerge organically, and the city’s face changes with the players’ choices. You’ll learn to design scenes that hinge on identity reveal moments, where a tag triggers a reveal that reframes the entire investigation. This is not a video game boss fight in a polluted alley; it’s a literary scene you’re authoring in real time, with dice as punctuation marks rather than bullets.

### Keepers and players: collaboration as the core mechanic

City of Mist is famously collaborative. The Keeper and players co-create the story, with the guide offering tools to maintain balance, tension, and momentum. The Player's Guide includes a section on session zero rituals, consent-based content warnings, and methods for resolving disagreements about how a scene should unfold. It’s not a dry policy page; it’s a playbook that helps you keep trust intact while you generate the city’s mythic tension. If you’ve ever worried that your table would dissolve into a power struggle or creative chaos, this guide gives you practical governance without killing the vibe.

## Tips for players: getting the most out of your mythic identity

- Start with a single, strong image. A city-savvy hero who channels a myth of travel and loss is often more compelling than an overstuffed power roster.
- Embrace the moral ambiguity. City of Mist thrives on gray areas; use Tags to explore conflicts between personal loyalty and public good.
- Use the city’s factions as partners in your story, not obstacles to your power fantasies. Alignments should feel like choices rather than mandates.
- Practice your dialogue. A well-crafted line can reveal a Tag, a backstory, or a motive faster than any stat sheet.
- Don’t fear failure. Clues that go wrong are often the most interesting parts of a session. The guide provides examples where failed plans lead to character growth and city-wide consequences.

## How to get the most out of your play sessions

- Run a shared-session zero. Have all players discuss what myths they want to explore and how their identities will interact with the city’s ongoing mysteries. The guide provides a simple framework for this that is both quick and meaningful.
- Build a city-facing arc. Start with a few major shadowy factions and one or two city-wide problems. Let the player characters’ myths reshape those problems rather than each player being a solo star.
- Use the Tag economy deliberately. Tags should create leverage, not guarantee success. The guide’s examples show how to leverage a single Tag in multiple ways across scenes.
- Schedule careful catch-ups. After a session, revisit your identity and Tag choices. The city changes; your myth should adapt, too.
