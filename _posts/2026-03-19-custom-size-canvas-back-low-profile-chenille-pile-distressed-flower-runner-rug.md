### Why canvas backing matters
Canvas backings are the unsung heroes of rugs. They provide stiffness, reduce curling around the edges, and often help with slip resistance on wood, tile, or laminate floors. It’s not a miracle, but it’s a well-behaved miracle that doesn’t complain when vacuumed on a Tuesday.

Chenille is that fluffy middle ground between plush and practical. The pile height on this piece is deliberately kept low to avoid the “puddle of socks after a long day” impression that higher-pile rugs can accidentally create in busy spaces. It’s soft enough to enjoy barefoot, durable enough to survive hallway traffic, and forgiving when you drop a snack on it during your latest Netflix binge-western marathon.

### Cleaning and maintenance
- Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting is your best friend. The low pile makes it easier to keep clean without needing industrial-grade equipment.
- Blot spills promptly with a clean cloth. Chenille can trap moisture if you’re not careful, so avoid soaking.
- For spot cleaning, a mild detergent dissolved in water and a soft cloth usually does the trick. Test a small area first to ensure there’s no color bleed.
- Rotate the rug every few months if you notice uneven wear in high-traffic zones.
- Professional cleaning is recommended if there are stubborn stains or if the runner covers a heavy-traffic hallway. Pro tip: schedule it during a rainstorm so you can pretend the rug learned to “weather” like a true adventurer.

## About Care and Longevity
A well-maintained runner rug can last years. Here are quick longevity tips:
- Keep it dry: moisture is the natural enemy of many textiles. If your entryway collects melted snow, consider a quick dry-mat solution under the runner during winter.
- Avoid direct sunlight on the dye-heavy sections to prevent fading. If your space has a sunlit corner, rotate the rug to extend color life.
- Vacuum regularly with a vacuum that has a brush attachment on a low setting to avoid fuzzing the chenille. If you hear a loud noise, turn down the vacuum, not your expectations.
