How to set up and run a 2 to 5 player game
------------------------------------------
Setting up is a breeze for experienced players and a harmless ritual for newcomers. Shuffle the base deck, lay out the Gloom overlay cards, and deal a starting hand. The transparent cards come into play by stacking them with standard cards to create layered effects. The tempo is immediate: someone draws a card, someone else swears under their breath (in the friendliest possible manner), and the table erupts into laughter as the overlays reveal a cascade of consequences.

Play pacing varies by number of players. In a 2-player game, you’ll trade quick curses and faster-than-expected plot twists, which can feel like a humorous duel where both contestants are in on the joke. In a 5-player setup, expect the chaos to triple as alliances form and collapse in a heartbeat. The game scales with energy levels: you can push a brisk 20-minute session or slow-burn it into a longer comedy of errors if your group is in the mood for a longer storytelling arc.
