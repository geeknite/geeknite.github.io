## Performance in Different Weather Scenarios

### Sun and heat

The resin wicker helps with UV resistance, and the color remains relatively consistent under bright sun. The cushions may feel a bit warm if you lounge directly under noon sun, so a shade canopy or umbrella can significantly enhance the experience. If you’re living in a sunny region, consider the cushion cover fabric’s heat tolerance and perhaps rotate cushions to distribute wear evenly.

### Rain and humidity

Water resistance is a practical feature here. The cushions are designed to dry relatively quickly, and the wicker isn’t prone to water creep and warping as long as you cover or store cushions during heavy rain or long humidity spells. That doesn’t mean you should skip maintenance; wipe down, check for water pooling, and make sure to store cushions inside if you anticipate a storm. A little maintenance goes a long way toward extending the set’s life and keeping the color and texture looking fresh.

### Cold weather and storage

Winter storage isn’t mandatory in many regions, but if you’re in a climate with freezing temperatures, it’s wise to store cushions indoors or use protective covers to prevent moisture build-up and potential mildew. The frame itself should be fine in cold weather, but cushions are the sensitive part—think of them as the fabrics of your outdoor life, not the hard steel skeleton. Rotate and store properly to extend cushion life and reduce the risk of cracking or fiber damage over seasons.
