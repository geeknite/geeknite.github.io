### Practical tips for flipping

When you flip the rug, make sure to lay it flat and walk on it evenly to avoid wrinkles. If you notice a curl along the edges, a quick press with a warm iron or heavy object overnight can help coax it into lying flat. Because this is jute, avoid excessive moisture to prevent warping. A thorough vacuuming with a gentle setting keeps the fibers clean and prevents dirt from taking up residence in the braid. With proper care, the reversible face remains crisp and attractive for years to come.

## Care and maintenance

### Cleaning basics

Jute is relatively easy to care for, but it is not a universal spill remedy. Regular vacuuming on a low setting will pull out loose dirt before it becomes deep and stubborn. For spills, blot—do not rub—with a clean cloth to lift moisture quickly. If the mess is stubborn, a mild soap solution and a damp cloth can help, but avoid saturating the fibers. After cleaning, allow the rug to air dry completely before placing it back in position. The goal is to keep the fibers from getting mildewed or permanently flattened by moisture.

### Rotation and wear patterns

To keep wear even, rotate the rug every few months, especially if you have a strong traffic flow in and out of the space. The circular shape means that movement is noticeable, so a gentle rotation helps prevent uneven color shifts or flattening along the same path. If you notice fraying at the edges, trim loose threads with small scissors to prevent further unraveling. In general, treat this rug as a reliable sidekick: practical, stylish, and low maintenance.
