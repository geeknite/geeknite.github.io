## What is Monopoly Deal Refresh?
Monopoly Deal Refresh is a deck based card game that compresses the Monopoly property collecting into rapid rounds. The deck includes property cards, rent cards, action cards, and a new category called refresh cards. The function of refresh cards is to adjust the pace of the game by allowing draws, hand reshuffles, or controlled card exchanges. The playing surface remains simple: you try to complete one or more color sets to win, while denying opponents access to those sets.

## How It Plays: The Rules in a Nutshell
The core loop is simple and friendly for new players:
- Draw five cards at the start of your turn.
- Play up to three cards.
- Use rent cards to demand payments from opponents or to collect property sets.
- Use action cards to disrupt opponents or to protect your own holdings.
- The goal is to complete a color set or sets to achieve the win condition.

With Refresh, a new set of cards introduces mild shifts in pacing. Some refresh cards trigger extra draws, others let you peek at or swap one card from an opponent. The net effect is a smoother ramp to the endgame and more opportunities to turn the tables when you are behind.

Turn order remains intuitive. Each player cycles through draw, play, and resolve steps, keeping the club dynamic and interactive. The pace tends to be brisk, making it easy to play multiple rounds in a single gaming session without losing track of what is happening.

## Card Types in Refresh
### Property Cards
Property cards mirror classic Monopoly sets but with refreshed art and sometimes revised color assignments. Completing a full color set yields victory points equivalent to the win condition. Some versions of the rules demand particular color counts for a set, while others allow modular sets that still reach the target.

### Rent Cards
Rent cards allow you to charge opponents based on what color sets you own. The numbers on the rent cards indicate the amount owed, and players can respond with defense cards or with a combination of their own sets.

### Action Cards
Action cards include familiar pieces such as Deal, Deal Breaker like effects, and Just Say No style cards. In Refresh these cards may come with adjusted costs, more flexible cancel cards, or single step combos that yield stronger plays when used in conjunction with refresh cards.

### Refresh Cards
Refresh cards are the new element. They enable performance on the draw phase or hand management. They can let you redraw, exchange a card with an opponent under certain conditions, or temporarily extend the number of cards you can play in a turn. The exact text will vary by print run, but the design intent is to accelerate the end game and reward shrewd card management.

## Strategy and Play Style Archetypes
Monopoly Deal Refresh supports a range of play styles. Here are a few archetypes and how to approach them in this edition:
- The Quick Builder: you focus on fast color sets and early pressure. You tolerate a few risk cards to accelerate a race to win.
- The Negotiator: you leverage action cards to extract deals from opponents, often trading away your own early gains for mid game abundance.
- The Blocker: you focus on defense, using Just Say No style effects to protect your most valuable cards until you secure a closing run.
- The Gambler: you chase high rent totals by stacking reliance on a single large set, even if it means you accelerate the end of the game for everyone else.

With Refresh these archetypes get a bit of a facelift. The hand refresh and exchange options mean you can be more flexible without being chaotic. The key is to read the table and know when to pivot your plan mid turn.
