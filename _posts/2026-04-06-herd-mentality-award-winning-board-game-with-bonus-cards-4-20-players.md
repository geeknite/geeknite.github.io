## What is Herd Mentality, and why should you care about the crowd?
Herd Mentality is a party game that puts a spotlight on groupthink, not on individual brilliance. In a world where everyone wants to be the funniest person in the room, Herd Mentality gives you a simple, elegant premise: guess what the majority will pick, and then try to steer your own answer toward that majority without turning into a full-blown hype-man or a complete wrecking ball. The result is a social experiment you can own without spending a semester studying behavioral psychology.

The core idea is delightfully straightforward: players answer prompts by writing down an option that they think the majority will choose. After everyone has written, everyone reveals their answers, and players who matched the majority score points. The twist? Bonus cards can alter the flow, add extra scoring opportunities, or flip expectations in ways that force even the quietest member of the group into the spotlight.

## How the gameplay unfolds: step by step
Herd Mentality runs in rounds, with different prompts and a rotating lead player. Here’s a typical round in digestible bites:
1) The prompt is read aloud by the round’s leader (or drawn from the deck if you’re keeping things anonymous).
2) Each player writes down a single answer that they think will be the majority choice. The answers stay secret until the reveal.
3) All answers are revealed at once. If your guess matches the actual majority—the answer that got the most votes—you score. If you’re tied with the majority, you still score; if not, you shrug and wait for the Bonus Card magic to show up.
4) Bonus cards surface, either giving you extra points for alignment with the majority, or flipping the expected outcome in hilarious ways. Cards might say things like “Switcheroo: everyone must switch their answer with another player,” or “Disagree and Die Laughing: you earn bonus points for the most outlandish non-majority answer.”
5) The scorekeeper tallies up, and the next round begins.

One of the most charming aspects is how quickly a round can become wonderfully awkward. The moment someone reveals a self-justifying answer that perfectly matches the majority, you hear the chorus in the room: “Aww, we’re all the same brain.” The moment the Bonus Card hits and someone tries to justify their bizarre choice with an elaborate, seven-step justification—well, that’s when the room erupts into laughter and the occasional debate about whether banana-flavored items “count” as favorites.

The flow is intentionally gentle, designed to accommodate people who might be wary of complicated rules or long setup times. The emphasis is on social play rather than strategy per se, which makes it a good fit for mixed-age groups and for folks who only pretend to be serious gamers on holidays. If you’ve ever played a game night where someone tries to nerd out about victory-point optimization, you’ll appreciate that Herd Mentality doesn’t pretend to be that kind of game. It’s about belonging to the group and enjoying the ride, even when your answers reveal more about your inner snack preference than your ability to solve a puzzle.

## The “Bonus Cards” twist: why extra cards matter
Bonus cards are the zing on top of the vanilla Herd Mentality experience. They can be played as part of the normal round, or saved to unleash mayhem at just the right moment. Here are the archetypes you’re likely to encounter:
- Alignment Boosters: cards that reward players for sticking with the majority or aligning with a surprising sub-group. They incentivize social alignment in a playful, non-judgmental way.
- Wildcards: cards that inject chaos, such as forcing everyone to reveal one extra answer, or allowing a “double-dip” where you can earn points for two different rounds in a single turn.
- Switch and Swap: cards that swap answers between players, creating a glorious mess that may or may not resemble your actual preferences.
- Penalty Cards: light penalties for the boldest or quirkiest responses, designed to keep things moving and prevent a single player from hogging the spotlight for too long.

The net effect is that Bonus Cards reduce the predictability of rounds and reward a sense of humor more than a steady hand. They’re also a clever way to tailor the experience for different groups. Want a gentler, more cooperative vibe? Use fewer bonus cards or forego the harsh penalties. Want a high-velocity, chaotic night? Deck out the table with a few cards that reward outrageous honesty and silly self-presentation. The design team clearly anticipated parties with different vibes, which is a big plus in the era of hyper-specific gaming experiences.

## Rules, tips, and light-house rules to smooth the chaos
- Timebox every round: a minute per round is plenty for 6–10 players; with 15–20, you can extend to 90 seconds or two minutes if you want a more relaxed pace. The timer is your friend, not your jailer.
- Encourage a single answer per player: multiple answers can derail the majorities and slow the momentum; a single, confident guess tends to deliver bigger laughs.
- Use house rules for new players: if a guest is shy, allow them to pass once per game. It reduces anxiety and helps maintain energy.
- Rotate the lead: changing who reads the prompt each round keeps people from coasting on a single style of humor. A good rotation helps everyone feel involved rather than merely spectators.
- Integrate a no-judgment policy: one of the best parts of Herd Mentality is the social glue of shared humor. Emphasize that the goal is not to “win” every round but to enjoy the moment and celebrate the silliest explanations.

## Strategies for maximizing fun (not just points)
- Read the room before you commit: in a big group, your response should consider what the majority might actually say, but there’s room to push a little humor in a way that’s inclusive rather than insulting.
- Use humor to bridge gaps: a clever, self-deprecating justification often lands better than a showy, contrived pitch. People remember the moment they got to cross their arms and declare, “Yep, I’m part of the herd now.”
- Pair up for clever pairings: in mixed groups, sit next to someone who shares a similar sense of humor. The synergy can elevate the whole round and prevent a single loud voice from dominating.
- Don’t fear the loudest voice: sometimes the most entertaining rounds show up when someone stares down a social dilemma with ridiculous confidence. Let them steer the ship for a moment; it’s part of the spectacle.
- Save a few Bonus Cards for late rounds: you don’t want the finale to feel like a preordained process. A single well-timed switcheroo can reframe the entire game night narrative.
