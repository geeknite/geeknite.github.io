## Assembly and first impressions
Assembly is the real test for most backyard gadgets and trampolines have a reputation for turning into a puzzle devised by a cranky scientist. Galactica tries to keep things straightforward by color coded hardware numbered steps and clear pictograms. In practice you will want two adults at minimum for the larger sizes the 14 ft in particular benefits from having a second set of hands to anchor the frame while the other person threads springs.

### Step by step walkthrough (high level)
- Unpack the box and inventory all parts. If you bought the 14 ft you will have a sizable pile of parts including the frame sections the legs the springs the mat the enclosure net and the ladder.
- Assemble the base frame into a circular or near circular shape depending on the size. Use the instruction manual as your map it is not a treasure map but you will feel a lot more confident once the corners start to align.
- Attach the springs to the frame first then connect the jump mat. This order keeps the mat from slipping while you work. The springs may arrive with a light coating of protective oil which you should wipe away before assembly to prevent transferring grease onto the mat.
- Install the enclosure net around the frame. Make sure the zipper is accessible and that the net corners are secured to the corresponding frame poles. The zipper should function smoothly with a tug test before all the way up.
- Attach the ladder and ensure all bolts and connections are tight. Check all anchor points and, if you have wind in your area, install the included ground anchors to prevent the whole structure from moonwalking across the yard.
- Do a final safety check bounce test with a careful adult and then allow the kids to take the first spins once you recheck for any loose hardware or wobbly legs.

Typical setup times can vary from 2 to 6 hours depending on the size and your familiarity with assembly. The 6 ft and 8 ft versions generally go faster while the 12 ft and 14 ft can test your patience but offer a satisfying sense of accomplishment when the last bolt is tightened and you hear the first properly judged bounce.

## Safety first the queue of practical tips
Safety is the prime directive for backyard trampolines. No product can replace good judgment but the Galactica line includes several features designed to help reduce the risk of mishaps.

### Enclosure and netting
- The enclosure height provides a tall barrier that limits the chance of accidental ejection. However it is not a substitute for supervision; kids should always be watched during play and rough housing should be discouraged.
- The zipper entry should be closed when anyone is bouncing. Teach kids to use the zipper instead of squeezing through the mesh which can compromise the net integrity over time.

### Ladder safety
- The ladder should be placed on a stable surface and strapped if possible to prevent it from slipping. Teach kids to climb and descend facing the trampoline to minimize awkward slips.
- Shoes off during play to avoid wearing in the mat or loosening the ladder attachment points.

### Ground anchors and weather considerations
- If you live in a windy area consider anchor kits. A windy afternoon can turn a bounce session into a mobile aerodynamics test, possibly relocating the trampoline to a neighbor s yard and triggering a neighborhood emergency bounce plan. Not ideal.
- A weather cover can extend the life of the mat and the enclosure. It also prevents sun damage that can cause the mat to weaken over time.

### Age and weight guidance
While Galactica trampolines are sold with age ranges on the packaging the best approach is to assess each child s size and bounce ability. A rough rule of thumb is to match the size of the trampoline to the child s age but always supervise and adjust based on the child s confidence and experience. For twins or siblings of different ages the larger 12 ft or 14 ft may be a better option to reduce fight over space and to provide more room for safer play.

## Maintenance and care
- Regularly inspect springs for signs of loosening or rust and replace as needed. A single loose spring can cause uneven bouncing and stress surrounding frame points.
- Clean off any mud or debris on the mat and net after use. This prevents grime from compacting into fibers and can extend the life of the enclosure.
- Lubricate moving connections as indicated in the user manual to maintain proper operation over the long term.
- Keep the area around the trampoline clear of toys and pets to prevent accidental collisions that can lead to damage.
