## Maintenance and Care: Simple Routines for Long-Term Looks
We’ve established that this rug is tough; now we’ll cover the actual routine that keeps it looking decent for years. You don’t need a full cleaning fleet—just a few per-season checks and a couple of quick cleaning rituals.

### Vacuuming: Frequency and Technique
- Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a gentle brush setting. Heavy-duty beater bars can cause wear over time, so a low-power setting is ideal.
- For heavy traffic areas, focus on the ribs to lift debris from the crevices rather than trying to drag the whole pile with a single pass.

### Spot Cleaning: Spill Protocols
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Don’t rub; you’ll push liquid deeper into fibers and increase stain risk.
- Use a mild, pH-neutral detergent solution if needed. Avoid bleach and ammonia-based cleaners that can strip color or degrade fibers.
- After cleaning, rinse with a small amount of clean water and blot dry to speed up evaporation.

### Deep Cleaning: When to Break Out the Big Guns
- For outdoor rugs, seasonal deep cleaning with a gentle wash and air-drying can refresh the fibers. A hose-down or gentle scrub with a soft brush works well.
- Allow the rug to dry completely before returning it to service to prevent mold or mildew growth underneath.

### Storage: Off-Season Care
- If you’re storing the rug during winter months or for a trip away from home, roll it tight (don’t fold) and keep it in a dry, well-ventilated space. Storing flat can invite creases that are hard to shake out.

### Against High-Pile Shag Rugs

### Against Ultra-Budget PVC or Poly-Backed Options
Some cheaper outdoor rugs use PVC or thin synthetic backs that wear out quickly when exposed to sun and rain. The Unbound rug’s ribbed texture and polypropylene fibers strike a balance between durability and cost, offering longer-term value than bargain-bin options.

### Against Natural Fiber Rugs Outdoors
Natural fibers like jute or sisal aren’t always ideal for weather-exposed spaces. They stain more easily and take longer to dry. If you’re after an outdoor-friendly option with reliable maintenance, the Unbound carpet’s synthetic fiber approach wins hands down for most households.
