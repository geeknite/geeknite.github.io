### What is included?

- A suite of Player Character Sheets. The pack includes multiple variants: Basic, Extended, and a “No-Nonsense GM Companion” for those who want to hand something to a player that doesn’t require a marathon of page-flipping.
- NPC Sheets. For GMs who want to keep a few archetypes on hand without scribbling in a notebook that smells faintly of coffee and doom.
- Arcana Quick-Reference Pages. In City of Mist, Arcana define a character's mythic power. The quick reference should save you from flipping through the core book to remember the exact flavor of “The Trickster” or “The Enforcer.” This is especially handy during tense moments when you want the Arcana name to feel like a thunderbolt rather than a footnote.
- Fate/Power Tracking. A dedicated area to track the number of Fate Points, Modifications, and the times the character has dodged a fatal blow with style.
- Condition and Status Sheets. Because you can never have too many ways to mark that your character is "Sunglasses Moment" or "Under Pressure" or "Wings-of-Morose"—okay maybe the last one is a stretch, but you get the vibe.
- GM Reference and Cheat Sheets. Quick rules reminders, GM tips, scene prompts, and plot hooks that you can glance at without losing the vibe of the night.
- Printable Player Aids and GM Aids. Physical or digital, you can choose your poison: fillable PDFs or print-ready sheets.
- Editable PDFs. For groups that enjoy customizing the field names to reflect their unique setting, there is likely a fillable format to keep the chaos from spreading.
- Color Coding and Indexing. A small magic spell that helps players find the right page quickly, saving your group from a minor existential crisis at the table.

### Usage with City of Mist core rules

The Fortune Row pack is designed to slot into the City of Mist system without requiring a complete rework of your current game flow. Arcana remain the central concept, so the sheets reflect that structure: a place to write the character’s Arcana, their Fates, and the way their allied myths manifest in the real world. The sheets are careful not to interrupt the core rules; they provide additional scaffolding for managing the things you were already doing in the core book—tracking Fate Points, recording Moments (your dramatic, city-altering, fate-laden scenes), and keeping track of "mythic debt" or “moral alignment” or whatever your group calls it. The risk with any character sheet pack is turning a session into a spreadsheet, but this pack seems to strike a balance: it looks great, it’s clearly organized, and it doesn’t insist you spend the night inputting fields rather than telling a story.

In terms of compatibility, the pack is designed around the standard City of Mist character framework: the protagonist (the Detective), the Arcana that give them supernatural flavor, and the ever-present threat of the city’s underbelly. If you’ve played City of Mist for a while, you’ll recognize the language, the categories, and the way Mists (mythic intuitions) push the narrative forward. If you’re new, the pack may help you get started quickly by pre- filling many of the common fields, letting you focus on roleplaying from minute one rather than on form-filling.

#### Where it shines

#### Where it could improve

- The art density could overwhelm players who prefer more white space or who want to customize the look to fit their group's vibe. While the pack offers a strong identity, groups that want to customize heavily might find themselves printing extra sheets or editing PDFs.
- If you rely heavily on digital tablets for character sheets, you may want to check that the editable PDFs carry over nicely to your preferred app; some fillable fields can look a bit cramped on smaller devices.
- A few minor labeling tweaks would help: in some variants, certain fields share a label header that could be mistaken as something else on first glance. A touch more iconography or tooltips could solve that, but it’s not a deal-breaker.

#### Printing and production notes

If you’re planning to print these sheets, consider using a sturdy paper stock (around 24-28 lb or light cardstock) for the NPC cards and a slightly heavier stock for the player sheets. A clean-cut, two-per-page layout helps you keep a compact binder, and laminated quick-reference tabs can speed up the action when the city starts to glow and the plot starts to writhe. If you’re digital-only, the fillable PDFs should render well on modern tablets and desktops, though you may want to verify the fields on smaller screens for legibility. A quick rule of thumb: print in color if you want to enjoy the noir palette; otherwise grayscale can sometimes look sharper in low-light gaming rooms.
