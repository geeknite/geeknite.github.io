### The mind behind the bling: the GIA certificate
GIA stands for the Gemological Institute of America, a name you’ll hear a lot in diamond circles. A GIA Diamond Grading Report or Diamond Dossier is the DNA of a diamond. For a marquise cut, you’ll see data on carat weight, color, clarity, cut grade, polish, symmetry, fluorescence, and size proportions. The GIA report also confirms the stone is natural (no lab-grown shenanigans) and not merely enhanced.

For this pendant, the GIA certificate means you’re paying for authentic natural diamond material, with a recognized standard of measurement. The 4Cs matter. The carat weight influences size and price; color indicates how warm or cool the diamond appears; clarity tells you about inclusions; and cut grade tells you how well the stone has been shaped and faceted.

### The cut: marquise geometry and optical performance
The marquise cut is an elongated oval with two pointed ends. The shape can elongate the finger and creates a visual line that draws the eye across the neckline. The marquise is particularly forgiving of small imperfect facets because the long sides can disguise minor inclusions at a distance. But for a GIA-certified stone, the cut is measured with careful attention to symmetry, table diameter, crown height, girdle thickness, and culet size. A well-cut marquise will exhibit a lively fire and brightness, with light bouncing from one facet to the next as you tilt your head in a dramatic fashion while you’re in a Zoom meeting.

### Care and maintenance
Diamonds are strong, but their sparkle depends on how you care for them. Here are practical tips:
- Clean with a mild dish soap solution and a soft toothbrush to reach the crevices of the marquise shape.
- Dry with a lint-free cloth; avoid paper towels that can scratch the gold.
- Inspect the prongs every few months to ensure the stone is secure.
- Avoid harsh chemicals that can discolor the gold or degrade the metal over time.
- Store separately from other jewelry to prevent scratching.

### Note on authenticity and verification
Careful readers may wonder about authenticity and what to verify when you purchase a GIA-certified piece. Always request the actual GIA report number and match it to the stone’s information. The report number is usually inscribed on the girdle or the setting and is referenced in the certificate. That’s your route to confirm that the piece in your hands matches what the certificate says. If you’re buying online, ask for close-up photos of the prongs and the girdle to confirm secure setting and absence of chips or damage.
