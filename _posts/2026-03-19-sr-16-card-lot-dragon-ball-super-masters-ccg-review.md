## Value, grading, and condition considerations
Grading and condition are the two levers that determine the financial aspect of any card lot. In this context, we’re talking about near mint, lightly played, and moderately played grades. Here’s a quick mental model to help you calibrate what you might expect:

- Near mint (NM): Sharp corners, clean surfaces, minimal to no edge wear. The card looks as if it just left a glossy sheet in the printer. A NM SS4 Gohan++ is the kind of card you can confidently pull out at a local game night and show to friends while not cringing at every light reflection.
- Lightly played (LP): Small edge wear, possible faint scratches, but no creases. The card still looks remarkably good for its age and can be slipped into a deck with minimal impact on performance. LP SRs are typically valued at a discount relative to NM, but still carry life in play and trade value.
- Moderately played (MP) and below: Visible handling marks, more wear on the corners, and perhaps slight surface scratches. These cards are the ones you put into a budget deck or use for drafting nights where you are less worried about pristine aesthetics.

For collectors, the best scenario is to land NM to LP SRs with the SS4 Gohan++ in similarly nice condition. For players, a sturdy mix of SRs that fit a variety of archetypes, including potential synergy with SS4 characters, is the sweet spot. The value proposition hinges on your goals: display bragging rights, playable potential, or a bit of both.

If you plan to get the lot graded, you will need to decide a grading service you trust and understand their turnaround times and pricing. Grading can increase the value of a handful of SR cards, particularly if you hold a standout piece like SS4 Gohan++ in NM condition. However, grading costs can eat into profits for a multi-card lot, so you should evaluate the math carefully. A practical approach is to grade only the candidates with the highest potential uplift and keep the rest in their raw form if they still add value to your collection.
