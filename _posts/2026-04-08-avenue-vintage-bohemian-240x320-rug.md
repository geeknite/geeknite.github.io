## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Quick Fixes
### Everyday Care
Vacuuming regularly will keep the fibers looking vibrant and prevent the “pet hair sculpture” effect from taking over. Use a vacuum with a gentle to moderate setting to avoid pulling threads, especially if you’ve got a high-traffic area. For spills, blot—don’t rub—using a clean, dry cloth. Most minor stains can be treated with a mild detergent solution, but test a small hidden area first to ensure colors don’t waltz away. The synthetic blend makes spot cleaning more forgiving than natural fibers, but it’s not a cape you can wear out in a rainstorm of red wine and grape juice.

### Quick-Fix Hacks
If you notice a slight curl at the edges after shipping, a light steam session and a few hours of flat-surface weight can coax the rug into lying flat again. For pet-related odors, a splash of baking soda before vacuuming can help—just don’t rely on it as a long-term deodorizer. Over time, you may also want to rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in sunlit rooms where fabrics bleed their colors into the sunlight like fashion models returning from a tropical run-way show.
