## Build quality and craftsmanship

### Handmade flat-weave — what that really means

The claim of handmade is a reminder that there is more to these textiles than a machine blink-and-you-move. Handmade flat-weave typically implies a dense, sturdy construction with a low profile. It’s less prone to fraying at the edges than higher-piled options and tends to wear well under traffic. The Montauk appears to embrace this ethos with clean edges and a compact weave that sits close to the floor—perfect for doorways, high-traffic surfaces, or spaces where a fluffy rug would fight for center stage.

### Sensible expectations for wear and care

With a rug of this type, you can expect a few realities:
- A flat-weave will show footprints and vacuum marks a little more readily than a shag or plush rug, but the ivory/dark grey contrast helps hide minor scuffs.
- Handmade items often have tiny irregularities—color variation or weave density differences—that many buyers actually appreciate as character rather than defects.
- Edges should lie flat, but with any woven piece, you may see slight wave or curl at the corners if it’s still new. A bit of patience and a light steam from a clothes steamer can help settle the fibers.

## Performance in daily life

### Foot traffic, pets, and the Montauk in action

Let’s get practical. Hallways carry a lot of foot traffic, from shoes to wheeled office chairs, to the occasional pet sprint after a toy. The Montauk’s low profile means it won’t trip you up or catch on chair legs. If you’ve ever had a shag rug in a hallway, you know the pain of sweeping pile into vents; with a flat-weave like this, you’ll have a much easier time keeping the surface relatively clean. A quick vacuum on a gentle setting should do the trick. For high-traffic areas, you may opt for a vacuum with adjustable height so you don’t stress the fibers or the weave.

### Maintenance rituals that won’t ruin your weekend

- Vacuum regularly to prevent dirt from lodging in the weave. Use a light touch and avoid aggressive beater bars that could loosen or fray the fibers over time.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers.
- For stubborn stains, treat with a fiber-safe cleaner and test on a inconspicuous spot first.
- If the rug ever needs a little refresh, rotate it a few degrees to even out wear, and flip it if your weave allows (some flat-weaves are reversible, though the Montauk’s reversible capability would depend on the exact construction).

### Cleaning beyond the basics

General maintenance for a rug like this keeps it looking sharp without professional cleaning. For more stubborn grime, you can consider a light steam treatment or professional cleaning on a periodic basis, especially if you have pets or kids who test the boundaries of “clean” with markers, ink, or glitter glue.
