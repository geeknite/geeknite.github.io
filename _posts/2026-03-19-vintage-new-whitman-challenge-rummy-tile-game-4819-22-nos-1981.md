## Rules, setup, and the thrill of tile strategy
If you’re expecting a pure, modern-day Rummikub clone, this Whitman edition will surprise you with its personality. The rules are similar enough to be recognizable, but the emphasis, timing, and occasional red-optical-challenge to memory create a unique rhythm.

Setup:
- Each player draws a certain number of tiles (the exact count depends on the edition and the number of players).
- The rest becomes a draw pile. The first player starts by trying to lay down a valid hand or reducing their rack to a solvable state by forming runs and sets.
- If no one can play, players draw from the stack until someone can show a valid combination. This sometimes leads to dramatic last tile moments that would make a spoiler technician cry with joy.

Basic gameplay:
- A run is a sequence of consecutive numbers in the same color, such as 3-4-5 in blue.
- A set is a group of the same number across different colors, like 7 in red, green, and yellow.
- The goal is to be the first to empty your rack or to minimize your score when the game ends, depending on the house rules.

House rules and variations:
- Some tables like to deal with jokers or wild tiles; others keep it simple and rule-bound for maximum nerdy bliss.
- The NOS Whitman flavor tends to encourage longer sessions with a little more negotiation and banter. It’s not just about playing quickly; it’s about savoring the tactile sensation of tiles clicking into place while your friend calculates the odds of a perfect run.

## Care, preservation, and display without turning into a museum piece
Careful handling is essential when you’re dealing with plastics from the early 80s. Here are some practical tips:
- Cleaning: Use a microfiber cloth lightly dampened with water or a mild soap solution to wipe down tiles and box surfaces. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade color or the cardboard components.
- Avoid direct sunlight: Long-term exposure can fade the vibrant colors and weaken cardboard. If you’re displaying, opt for indirect light or UV-protective display setups.
- Storage: Keep the box sealed or placed in a breathable, dust-free area. A small climate-stable cabinet is ideal for preserving the components, avoiding humidity swings that could warp tile racks or disturb glue on printed materials.
- Display ideas: Build a small vignette featuring the box, a few exemplars of the tiles, and a couple of wooden racks. It’s not just about showing off; it’s about telling a story of 1981’s game-night fantasies.
