## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity
### Routine maintenance
- Vacuum once a week, moving in multiple directions to lift dirt from the fibers.
- Rotate the rug every three to four months to ensure even wear and color retention across the surface.
- Address spills promptly with blotting technique and a mild cleaning solution appropriate for the rug’s material.
- Consider a rug pad beneath to reduce wear, add insulation, and improve overall stability.

### Cleaning tips for busy people
For light stains, try a mixture of lukewarm water and a gentle detergent. Gently blot; avoid scrubbing, which can push stains deeper and create a scuff-fest on the fibers. For more serious issues, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in large area rugs rather than trusting a DIY method that could accidentally transform your carpet into a blotchy piece of abstract art.

### Longevity and colorfastness
Colorfastness often hinges on dye quality and fiber type. If you’re concerned about color bleeding, do a small test patch in an inconspicuous area before fully cleaning. With proper care, this rug should retain its look and texture for years of use, gaming marathons, and family gatherings—without needing an authentic medieval rug-repair montage.
