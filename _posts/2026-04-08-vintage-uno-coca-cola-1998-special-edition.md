## Authenticity and Verification

### How to verify this is the legit Coca Cola edition
Start by cross checking the year, edition name, and branding marks against documented references. Look for a Coca Cola logo that is crisp, not smeared. The UNO brand should be on the front and back, with the 1998 copyright year visible somewhere that can be photographed and displayed to your friend group. If the box features an odd watermark or a foil stamp that looks suspicious, contact the seller for additional photos. A legitimate edition will have packaging that matches period design trends of the late 90s. If you can, ask for a photo of the seal and the inside box lid to confirm the content is present and undisturbed. A good seller will provide close up shots of the cards and a shot of the rulebook for authenticity checks.

### The risk of fakes and how to spot them
Fake vintage items exist, just like bread that pretends to be a baguette. Look for consistent fonts, alignment, and color balance. If the price is suspiciously low, that is a red flag. Fakes often skip the careful printer alignment or produce a slightly off shade on the red background that makes the box look fake from a distance. The packaging should feel substantial; if it feels lighter than expected, it could be a rewrap or a non original packaging of a later reissue. Always request additional photos of the bottom of the box that shows copyright and manufacturing details. In the age of high res smartphone photography, there is no excuse for not verifying.
