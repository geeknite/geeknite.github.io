## How to setup and how it flows
Setup is refreshingly straightforward. Shuffle the life event deck, deal out a starter hand to each player, and determine the order of play. The table presence is minimal, which is perfect for a kitchen table battle or a couch-based game night where you’re trying to avoid the heroic dust level of a true board-meets-tabletop experience.

On each turn you’ll perform a light sequence: draw a card, resolve the card’s effect, adjust your life track or score, and pass to the next player. The cards themselves do the heavy lifting; you’ll rely on your memory of the rules for a couple of turns until the rhythm locks in. The game clips along at a brisk tempo, which makes it ideal for families, roommates, or a group of friends who want to squeeze a session into a small window between chores or episodes of your favorite streaming service.

## Strategy tips and do/don’t suggestions
- Manage your risk: Some cards reward bold choices, but don’t overspend your momentum on a single swing. Balanced decisions yield sustainable points rather than flashy, one-card miracles.
- Watch the pacing: Because the game is designed for quick sessions, you can adapt your play to suit your group. If people are new, keep turns short and confirm card effects out loud to maintain transparency.
- Deck awareness: Shuffling matters. If you’ve noticed a few life event cards stacking in one player's hand, you might want to adjust your strategy to mitigate the effect of a potential cluster of luck in a single turn.
- Timing matters: The moment you feel you’re close to a milestone, consider your card portfolio and whether you should push for a high-value event or conservatively secure small gains for a more predictable finish.
- Player interaction: Don’t underestimate the social dynamics. This is a social game, and sometimes the best tactic is to nudge a friend toward a less favorable path with a timely comment or a playful taunt. Remember, the table should be a playground of laughs rather than a battlefield of bitterness.
