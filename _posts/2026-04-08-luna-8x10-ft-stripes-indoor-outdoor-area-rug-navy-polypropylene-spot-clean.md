## Cleaning and Maintenance: Simple, Sanity-Preserving Techniques

### Spot Cleaning: The First Line of Defense
Spills and accidents happen. The Luna rug responds well to spot cleaning. The recommended approach is to blot the liquid first with a clean cloth, then use a mild detergent solution and a soft-bristled brush. Rinse with clean water and allow air drying. The polypropylene fibers dry quickly, so you won’t be left with a soggy floor for days. For stubborn stains, a little elbow grease goes a long way, but you don’t need to transform this into a semester-long research project. It’s a rug, not a laboratory experiment.

### Deep Cleaning and Frequency
In general, a light overall cleaning every few months keeps it looking fresh. You can use a gentle carpet cleaning solution if you want to go beyond the surface, but do not over-wet the rug as it can take longer to dry. If you’re someone who loves to give your outdoor space an annual deep-clean, consider taking the rug outdoors to shake off loose dirt and debris, then washing with a garden hose at a gentle spray setting. The goal is to avoid mold and mildew, which is the eternal nemesis of any fabric that dares to pretend it’s weatherproof.

### Drying Time and Storage Tips
Air-drying in a shaded area is preferred to sun exposure, which can accelerate fading over a long period. If you’re short on space, roll the rug (don’t fold it, to prevent creases) and store it in a dry place when not in use for extended periods. For seasonal storage, consider stacking with a thin layer of silica gel or moisture-absorbing packs to avoid any musty vibes that could ruin your geometric-chic mood.

## Practical Tips for Owners: Getting the Most from Your Luna Rug

- Place it on a clean, flat surface to prevent the edges from curling. A few furniture legs nudging the rug downward can keep it looking neat.
