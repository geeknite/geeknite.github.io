## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Look Fresh

### Everyday care

Vacuum regularly with a low-power setting to avoid fuzzing the pile. For most spills, blotting is enough—avoid scrubbing, which can push the stain deeper or damage the print. If you need a deeper clean, check the label for washability. Many rugs in this category tolerate light machine washing or professional cleaning when needed. Always air dry; heat can shrink fibers and distort patterns.

### Spot cleaning and stain removal tips

Treat spots promptly. Use a mild detergent mixed with water, apply to a white cloth, and blot from the outside in to prevent spreading. After cleaning, rinse the area with a damp cloth to remove soap residue and blot dry. Avoid harsh solvents that can damage colorfastness or lead to a chemical odor that doesn’t go away for days. If you have persistent stains, a specialized rug cleaner designed for synthetic fibers is a good next step. As with all fabrics, test any cleaner on a hidden corner first.

### Longevity strategies

Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially if your room has a frequent layout change or if your furniture tends to creep during the night. Use a rug pad to prevent slipping, protect the underside, and extend the rug’s lifespan. A pad also reduces friction with hardwoods, which helps prevent scuffs on those precious floors you’ve spent hours buffing to perfection.
