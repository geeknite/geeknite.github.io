# Infinite 5-Person Workstation W Screen 9000 x 780 x 730mm White: A Geeknite Review

Spoiler alert: if you ever wanted a desk so sprawling that a small village could set up shop on it, you found your holy grail. The Infinite 5-Person Workstation with W Screen in bright white is basically the Swiss Army Knife of corporate furniture—if the Swiss Army Knife came with a power strip, USB-C docks, and a partition that doubles as a screen wall. In this review, we will explore whether this behemoth desk is just marketing hype or a genuinely useful centerpiece for collaborative spaces. So buckle up your cable ties, and let’s dive into 9 meters of white, semi-mature desk goodness.

## The Grand Unboxing: A Desk But Also a Lifestyle

Unboxing is not as dramatic as a new console release, but it has the charm of a small sci‑fi gadget kit. The packaging promises “modular, scalable, and ready for integration with your team’s chaos.” The assembly manual arrives a bit like a treasure map: lots of arrows, some insistence on aligning with the infinity symbols (which are not included), and a sticker that says Designed in Norway or something equally Nordic and cool. The first impression? The material feels solid but not uncanny valley solid; it’s just heavy enough to let you know you’re assembling something that will survive a few reorganizations of an open-plan office.

> Quick note: this is a long workstation meant for shared spaces. If you’re a solo coder who just wants a throne of white lacquer, you might feel slightly underdressed.

### Design and Materials: White, Bright, and Slightly Unreal

The finish is a gleaming white laminate over a robust core. Think of it as the architectural equivalent of a white Jedi robe—clean, luminous, and possibly dangerous if you spill coffee on it. The edge banding is tight, minimal gaps, and there’s a quiet confidence to the way the panels line up. The texture is smooth enough to be a tactile pleasure, yet not so slick that your mouse slips to the floor every two minutes. The W Screen partitions are mounted with adjustable rails that allow the screen walls to flex with the room’s layout. It’s not latex-free, but the white color helps keep the space feeling bright, not a sterile hospital corridor.

The “9,000 mm” dimension promises room for five, plus a couple of overhead monitor arms for good measure. In practice, you’ll likely configure three to four workstations along the main run, with the remaining space dedicated to a collaborative whiteboard zone or a snack station. The white finish can show reflections—so you’ll either love the glare, or you’ll install extra desk lamps to convert those reflections into nifty light shows during presentations.

## Why a 9-Meter Desk Might Save Your Team (Or Your Budget)

Before you commit the better part of your budget to a single desk, let’s talk space. A 9000 mm desk is essentially a small conference table with legs. It encourages collaboration, reduces the distance between team members, and makes social distancing a retro fashion statement. The length also poses practical questions: how do you route cables? Where do you store power strips? Will there be five different users who each claim “my laptop needs two 4K displays and a docking station that looks like a spaceship?” The answer is: the Infinite system is designed with integrated cable channels, modest grommets, and modular add-ons to help manage the chaos. If you dislike cable spaghetti, you’ll appreciate the deliberate routing options and color-coded channels. If you love cable spaghetti, you’ll still be able to make it look like modern sculpture.

### The W Screen: Privacy, Partition, and Panache

Let’s talk about the star feature: the W Screen. It isn’t just a partition; it’s a personality. The W Screen sections provide adjustable privacy without completely sealing you off from your colleagues—the most civilized approach to collaboration since the open office was invented and immediately burned in a ceremonial bonfire. The panels can be adjusted in height and angle, which helps with glare control during the inevitable 3 PM sunbeam that hits the leftmost workstation. It’s not going to block every sound; you’ll still hear colleagues brainstorming about the best font for dashboards, but it does dampen the “HEY, ARE YOU ON MUTE?” chorus that plagues the modern office.

The white panels themselves have a clean finish, and the joints are designed to minimize visible seams. This matters not only for aesthetics but for sanitation: fewer seams means fewer places for crumbs and stray markers to hide, which matters when you’re trying to maintain a professional space with an occasional coffee spill.

## Ergonomics for a Five-Person Setup

Space for five is one thing; comfortable ergonomics are another. The Infinite 5-Person workstation offers adjustable monitor arms, keyboard trays, and the ability to set desk heights across the length of the run. The height adjustability is highlight-worthy: you can align everyone to a comfortable 72–75 cm desk height range, which is the sweet spot for most people for typing posture. The integrated cable channels help with leg clearance and allow people to slide their chairs in and out without bumping into stubborn power strips. It’s the difference between a collaborative environment and a chaotic, elbows-first battlefield over space.

But there are caveats. With five adults, you’ll need a plan for personal space. The widths of each workstation work out to a few feet per person—enough for a laptop, a notebook, and maybe a small plant. If you’re used to gazing at a single monitor, you’ll discover the fun in sharing a 4K display across two people, which is a surprisingly entertaining way to procrastinate together while pretending to be productive. And yes, the W Screen does help reduce the intimidation factor of the main display; you can create a sense of “this is mine” without resorting to a punitive moat of cable ties.

### Reclaiming Cable Real Estate: Power, Ports, and Pizzazz

Cable management is not optional on this scale. The desk includes integrated channels and some built-in power solutions. You’ll want to map your devices ahead of time: which ports are accessible to which user? The last thing you want is a “sharing is caring” moment that ends with someone tripping over power cords during a status meeting. The design encourages you to structure a clean topology: per-user outlets on the inside lip of their station, shared power banks at the end of the row, and a central trunk line for high-draw devices like docking stations.

For those who care about sustainability, the channel design minimizes friction and reduces friction heat—because nothing kills a meeting like a coil of black, semi-melted cable hugging the leg of your chair. The dynamic cable channels also help with maintenance: you can pop a panel to replace a faulty USB-C hub without disassembling the entire workstation.

### Aesthetics and Tiny Cruelty-free Rays of Light

We’ve already touched on glare, and yes, there will be glare. White surfaces reflect. A lot. The trick is to pair the desk with appropriate ambient lighting and task lights. The result is a workspace that looks bright and modern, not like a sterile lab where your only friend is the lab monitor that refuses to age. If your office has big windows, you’ll want to position the desk so that the W Screen doesn’t cast awkward shadows on your colleagues’ faces during video calls. In practice, this is a balance between natural light, the wall color, and your LED go-to’s.

Maintenance is simple: wipe with a mild cleaner and microfiber cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners and harsh solvents that might dull the white finish. It’s not a museum piece, but treat it with the respect you’d give to a high-end gaming rig and you’ll be rewarded with years of clean lines and minimal squeaks.

### Real-World Footprint and Room Planning

Let’s do some math, because math loves office furniture almost as much as we love memes. A 9,000 mm desk equals 9 meters. In a standard office, that would occupy roughly an entire wall space in a large conference area. In a penned-in environment, it creates a strong social contract: if you’re at the far end, you’re likely the designated “scout for solutions” because you physically out-range the rest of the team with your workstation’s long reach. It’s kind of glorious in a sci‑fi boardroom way, but you’ll need to consider doorways, path-of-travel, and whether you can assemble this thing in your space without taking down two walls.

The depth of 730 mm is enough for a comfortable keyboard setup, mouse, a laptop, and a coffee mug that always seems to vanish into oblivion. The 780 mm height ensures that you’re not craning your neck while standing—assuming you’re using it at its default height. It’s worth noting that if your space typically runs with shorter ceilings or a slightly lower than standard desk height, you may want to adjust the legs or pair it with a standing mat to preserve joint health across a five-person crew.

### Maintenance and Durability: The Desk That Plays It Safe

The materials are chosen for durability. The white laminate can resist scuffs, though it’s not immune to the occasional scratch if you’re a heavy-handed marker user or you host a post‑its thunderstorm. But the core is solid: metal frame, robust joints, and a modular architecture that invites upgrades or changes as your team evolves. The product is designed to last through the typical corporate lifecycle: reorganization, mergers, office allergies to natural light, and the existential dread of another all-hands meeting. With reasonable care, you’ll be swapping monitors, upgrading the docking station, or reconfiguring partitions without the entire desk needing replacement. It’s not a lifetime investment like a mountain bunker, but it’s closer than most desks in the wild.

### Subsystems: How It Plays With Other Gear

One question we’re asked: does it integrate with standard monitor arms, trackballs, CPU holders, and cable drops? The answer is yes, with caveats. The rails and mounting points align with common VESA standards, and there are aftermarket adapters for oddball shapes. If you’re the kind of person who attaches a 32-inch curved monitor to their keyboard stand, you’ll be pleased to discover that the long surface can accommodate two 27–32 inch displays per two-person module with some careful arrangement. If you want to put five 27-inch displays into a single run, you’ll want to check weight limits and ensure your monitor arms aren’t bending the rails. It’s a matryoshka doll of compatibility, and you’ll discover that “compatible” is a spectrum rather than a single boolean.

Let’s address the elephant in the room: price. A 9-meter white behemoth is not a budget purchase.
