### Condition Spec and Verification Tips
A sealed box with a corner ding will typically present the following realities:
- The shrink wrap is intact, which means the seal is unbroken and the game contents have not been opened.
- The outer box shows a small dent or crease along one of the corners or edges. The severity can vary from a tiny crease to a noticeable indent. In most cases, it should not affect the interior components.
- The interior tray and insert should be undisturbed, with tokens, tiles, and miniatures still in their factory bags or protective sleeves as shipped.

To verify condition when you receive the box:
- Inspect the exact corner that shows the ding. If you can see light through the seam or the cardboard shows exposed fibers, you might question the shelf life of the interior components.
- Gently press the corners to ensure nothing inside is shifting, though with sealed boxes you should not expect movement beyond the anti-tilt inserts.
- Look for tamper-evidence on the shrink wrap. A broken seal or resealing tape can indicate a previously opened box. The latter is a red flag for a sealed listing.

If you are a reader who loves the mechanical thrill of the unboxing, you may enjoy the drama of a sealed shell showing a tiny scar, a reminder that even legends have their rough days on the battlefield. If you are a critical collector, you will want clear photos of the ding itself and a credible seller note about its size and location. The presence of a corner ding does not automatically ruin the value; it often depends on the interest of the buyer and the desire to keep the box pristine for future display or resale.

### Components and Quality
The core components in Zombicide Black Plague include a heap of plastic miniatures, heavy-duty cardstock cards, durable double-sided tiles for the board, dice, and a robust rulebook that covers both co op play and the occasional chaos of expansions. A sealed copy aims to preserve the exact texture of the cards, the exact number of miniatures, and the correct color balance across the factions. The corner ding on the outer shell should not affect the plastic sprues or the texture of the tokens, which means you can expect the same tactile feel on the minis as in any opened copy.

In practice, here is what you should anticipate when you slice open the shrink wrap only to discover that the box has survived the shelf with nothing but a cosmetic blemish:
- No bent or crushed miniatures in the interior packs. If you see a miniature that has shifted due to a mispacked insert, that is more of a packaging error than a sign of interior degradation.
- Cards that lay flat and feel the same as any opened pack. The cardstock should be sturdy, with the same glossy finish that helps keep the artwork lively through years of play.
- Tokens and tiles that lie flat, with no warping or moisture effect. If your sealed copy shows any warping, that is a sign of humidity exposure rather than a structural defect introduced by the ding.

If you are a player who values clutching onto every fine detail, you will appreciate the reliability of the interior.
