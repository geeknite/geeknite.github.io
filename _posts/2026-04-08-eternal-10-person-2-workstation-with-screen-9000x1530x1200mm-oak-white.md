If you’ve ever tried to fit ten monitors on a single, standard desk, you know sag is not just a design flaw; it’s a philosophical impairment. If you love the satisfaction of a neatly routed spaghetti project, you’ll be in your element here. If you hate cable jungles, you will become a matters-of-order zealot by day two.

## Ergonomics and Comfort: Surviving a Full-Day in the Land of Ten
A big desk doesn’t automatically mean big comfort. Ergonomics are still king, even when you have space for a small library in your workspace. Here’s how Eternal attempts to keep humans from transmogrifying into neck-cranial sculptures after a 8-hour shift:

- Seating and knee clearance: Expect ample knee room under the desk. The 1.2-meter height is friendly to most chairs, including some of those ergonomic beasts with sculpted lumbar supports. If you’re tall, you’ll want to test chair compatibility and make sure your knees don’t complain about the excursion toward the desk edge.
- Arm reach and typing posture: With 9 meters of length to play with, you can orient your three main zones to optimize typing posture and keyboard reach. The central area remains unobstructed for quick sprints to the whiteboard while keeping your forearms in a neutral position for long tasks. If you’re a heavy typist, consider keyboards with split ergonomics to maintain comfortable alignment across the row.
- Monitor distance and neck strain: The two screen blocks offer flexibility. You can position displays at eye level and tilt them to reduce glare. If your lighting creates hotspots on the screens, you’ll want to adjust desk height or angle slightly to maintain a comfortable viewing angle and avoid a future trip to the ophthalmologist.
- Breakroom considerations: Big desks attract big coffee cups. Ensure your cup holders are not just decorative while also providing enough space for water, snacks, and those prototype doodads your team keeps leaving behind after caffeine-fueled sprint days.

E-Pi and ergonomic nerds alike would approve of the fact that you can spread your posture experiments across ten people without all of you having to huddle around a single, tiny laptop screen like starved explorers in a desert of spreadsheets.

A 9-meter long desk is not a one-size-fits-all solution. If your space or budget doesn’t justify a single mega-desk, consider these alternatives:

- Modular sub-assemblies: Instead of a single continuous unit, look at modular desk blocks that you can reconfigure as projects scale up or down. You retain the flexibility to re-allocate space depending on team size.
- Dual 3–4 meter desks joined by a connector: This gives you similar team dynamics with a little more flexibility in space planning and room for privacy partitions.

If you are chasing a wow-factor with a smaller footprint, you might also consider a dedicated monitor array and a series of small-but-hostile-in-a-good-way desks for the ten-person team. It all depends on your budget, space, and the level of drama you want in your open-plan theater.
