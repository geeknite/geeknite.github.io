## Practical Setup Guide
### Step 1: Unbox and Inspect
Carefully remove all parts and lay them out. Check for obvious dings, missing bolts, and the corner protectors that keep your floor from becoming a display of shattered pride. If something is missing, do not panic. The free accessories usually include more than enough to replace tiny items that vanish into the void of a cable management arsenal.

### Step 2: Attach Rails
Install the universal rails with the provided hardware. The rails should be adjustable to accommodate different equipment depths. If you have a strict deadline, do not rush the alignment. A misaligned rail leads to a tilt or a creak that will haunt your IT dreams for weeks.

### Step 3: Mount Your Gear
Begin with heavier items at the bottom to maintain center of gravity. This helps reduce the risk of tip overs and ensures you do not need a forklift to stage a switch. Use hardware like cage nuts and screws recommended by the manufacturer. If you use alternative fasteners, you may void the warranty or trigger a small existential crisis during a quarterly audit.

### Step 4: Cable Routing and Management
Group data cables and power cables separately. Use the included cable management accessories to create neat channels. Label cables for future you and future you will thank you for your organized life. Route power cables through the bottom or the rear to minimize heat coupling with data lines as your rack hums along at full tilt.

### Step 5: Close Doors and Test
Close the doors and lock them securely. Power on the equipment gradually, observing for any unusual noise or heat buildup. If you notice hotspots, pause, re-check your airflow, and consider repositioning devices or adding a fan rail kit if necessary.
