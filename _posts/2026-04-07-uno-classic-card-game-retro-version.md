## Cards and Rules: what remains classic, what gets a retro wink
UNO classics that stay intact include the number cards, Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four. The Retro Version preserves those core mechanics, but it spices up the experience with a few thoughtful tweaks that align with the retro motif. Here are the key components and how they land in a modern night of play.

### Card types at a glance
- Number cards: 0 through 9 in four primary colors. They still match colors or numbers to reduce the pile and push players toward quickly shedding cards.
- Skip: The next player loses a turn. Classic and reliable, great for dramatic plot twists.
- Reverse: In a four-player game, this changes the direction of play; in a two-player game, it acts like a Skip. The Retro Version uses a slightly thicker symbol to ensure readability on quick hands.
- Draw Two: The next player draws two cards and loses a turn. This utility card remains the backbone of UNO’s tension.
- Wild: Change color to any you desire. The retro treatment keeps the bold color wheel and adds a subtle glow on the card edge to make it standout.
- Wild Draw Four: Forces color change and the next player draws four cards. It’s the big swing card you want in your back pocket when the table collapses into chaos.
