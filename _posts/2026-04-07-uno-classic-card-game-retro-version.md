## Cards and Rules: what remains classic, what gets a retro wink
UNO classics that stay intact include the number cards 0–9 in four primary colors, plus the standard action cards: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four. The Retro Version preserves those core mechanics but spices up the experience with a few thoughtful tweaks that align with the retro motif. Here are the key components and how they land in a modern night of play.

### Card types at a glance
- Number cards: 0 through 9 in four primary colors. They still match colors or numbers to reduce the pile and push players toward quickly shedding cards.
- Skip: The next player loses a turn. Classic and reliable—great for dramatic plot twists and for that moment when you wish you could skip your in-laws’ third anecdote about the “good old days.”
- Reverse: In a four-player game, this changes the direction of play; in a two-player game, it acts like a Skip. The Retro Version uses a slightly thicker symbol to ensure readability on quick hands. If you’ve got long nails or quick reflexes, you’ll appreciate the tactile cues; if you’re playing at a neon-lit bar table, you’ll appreciate the big, bold marks that scream, “READ ME NOW.”
- Draw Two: The next player draws two cards and loses a turn. The backbone of the tension—this card has caused more table-murderous gasps than any dramatic cliffhanger in a sci-fi binge-watch.
- Wild: Change color to any you desire. The retro treatment keeps the bold color wheel and adds a subtle glow on the card edge to make it stand out during a chaotic game-night reveal.
- Wild Draw Four: Forces color change and the next player draws four cards. It’s the big swing card you want in your back pocket when the table descends into glorious chaos.
