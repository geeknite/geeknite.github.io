Beige is your best friend for small spaces. It expands, reflecting light and creating the illusion of more air. Pair the NAT801B-6SQ with a compact, sleek coffee table and light-toned furniture. ### Medium to large rooms
In larger rooms, beige gains a subtle presence rather than a bold statement. Use the rug as a base for a seating area, then introduce color through cushions, throws, and artwork. ### The lighting equation
Lighting can dramatically alter how beige reads in a room. Warm lighting will emphasize the rug’s soft, cozy vibe, while cool lighting may reveal its color variance more clearly. If your space has big daylight windows, that gorgeous natural light can reduce the perception of the flaw entirely—until 3 p.m. on a sunny day when life resets to reality and you remember you bought a beige rug with a minor imperfection.

## Practical care: keep it looking respectable for longer

### Cleaning schedule
- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting to avoid rough abrasion of the fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent embedding.
- Treat stains with a mild detergent solution as needed; test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear.

### Long-term maintenance
Consider a rug pad to reduce slipping and add a touch of cushion underfoot. If the room experiences a lot of sun, you may notice slight fading in the beige area over time; that’s normal and can be managed with occasional repositioning of furniture or the rug itself. If you enjoy the hellscape of maximalist decor for a season, a well-chosen accent rug could also pair nicely with the beige base and hidden flaw—like the secret sidekick in your furniture justice league.
