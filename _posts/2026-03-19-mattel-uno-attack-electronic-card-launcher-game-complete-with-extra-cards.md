## What is UNO Attack?

UNO Attack is not your grandmother’s card game. It’s a high-tech twist on a timeless favorite: a 2–10 player game that marries the classic UNO rules with a spring-loaded card launcher that blinks to life and spits out cards when you least expect it. In the Complete edition, you get the base UNO Attack experience plus extra cards that expand the possibilities, making it a birthday party, a dorm-room saga, and a chaotic family game night all rolled into one combustible plastic-wrapped package.

The core gimmick is simple: instead of drawing cards by counting, you press a button and wait for the launcher to decide your fate. Sometimes you win; sometimes you lose an entire hand because the machine found its inner dragon and gobbles up the deck like a hungry goblin. The unpredictability is the heart of the game—if you crave strategy and calm, UNO Attack is not for you. If you crave adrenaline, sneeze-free chaos, and the occasional moment of glorious luck that makes you the hero of your own living room, you’ve found your jam.

## The Launcher: How It Eats Cards (And Why It Matters)

The central gadget is the electronic launcher. It is a small, square-ish hopper connected to a motorized mechanism that pushes cards into a feeding chute and, on a whim, spits one out into the table or across the room. The mechanism runs on batteries (or a rechargeable pack in some editions), and it’s designed to be reloaded with the included deck at the start of a game. It’s not a perfect machine, but it doesn’t pretend to be. It’s honest about its chaotic nature: sometimes it spits out a single card; sometimes it spews an entire handful; sometimes it remains stubbornly silent, which is a kind of mercy, really.

The unpredictable nature is what gives UNO Attack its repeated charm. You never know when the launcher will eat your fate whole, and that unpredictability keeps you on your toes. If you’re the sort who wants to plan every move, UNO Attack will frustrate you in a deeply satisfying way. If you’re the social type who thrives on surprise and silliness, you’ll adore the energy this thing brings to a party.

One note on safety: the cards are standard size, with plastic coating that’s easy to grip, but the launcher can fling them with surprising speed. A light punch to the elbow or a near miss to the glass table is not unusual in a heated match. It’s all part of the theater, but do exercise common sense and keep a clear playing surface so nobody ends up playing real-life whack-a-card and losing more than a few nerves.

## How to Play: A Quick Start Guide

- Set up the launcher in a central, clear space. Ensure you have fresh batteries or power if your edition supports it.
- Shuffle the UNO Attack deck and deal seven cards to each player as usual.
- When it’s your turn, you can either play a card that matches the color or number or play an action card. If you can’t play, you must draw from the stock deck. The twist: you may also trigger the launcher on certain action cards, or when the game reaches a threshold you can define.
- Keep the momentum by passing the launcher to the next player, or by letting the game decide who gets the next turn via the trigger rules in the extra cards.
- The winner is the first player to reach 500 points or to empty their hand, depending on how you set your house rules. The launcher’s unpredictability adds a strategic dimension: do you gamble on a big risk when you’re close to victory, or do you play it safe and hope the machine cooperates?
