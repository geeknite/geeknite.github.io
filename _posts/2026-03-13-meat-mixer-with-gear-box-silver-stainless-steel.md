## Cleaning and Maintenance
Cleaning is not the most glamorous part of kitchen gadget ownership, but it is essential for health and longevity. Start by stripping off the attachments and rinsing with warm water. The gear box is sealed, so you can wipe it down without fear of grease explosions. For deeper cleaning, disassemble the mixing head according to the manual and soak the parts in warm soapy water, then air dry. Do not submerge the motor housing; it is not a submarine and will not appreciate that.

### Best Practices
- Regular lubrication of moving parts is important; use food grade grease as recommended by the manufacturer. 
- Ensure all seals are intact before each use to prevent leaks. 
- Wipe down with a soft cloth to preserve the finish.

### Longevity Tips
Treat the gear box to a yearly ritual of inspection. Check belt tension, inspect gears for micro cracks, and listen for odd noises during the first 5 minutes of operation. A little preventative care goes a long way toward keeping your kitchen singing instead of groaning.
