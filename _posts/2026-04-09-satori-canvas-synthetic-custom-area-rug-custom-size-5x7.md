## Practical usage and care
### Cleaning basics
- Vacuum regularly to keep dirt from embedding. Use a vacuum with a gentle brush setting to avoid pushing fibers around. 
- For spills, blot and clean with a mild detergent solution. Avoid soaking the rug, which could take longer to dry and potentially affect the backing. 
- For stubborn stains, a diluted cleaner suitable for synthetic fibers can help. Always test a small corner first. 
- Drying is best done with air flow and away from direct heat sources to prevent shrinkage or warping.

### Maintenance tips
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and prevent a single area from looking distinctly older than the rest. 
- Use a rug pad to improve durability, especially if you have hardwood floors or tiles. A pad reduces bunching and adds a touch of comfort underfoot.
- Mind the edges. If you have a busy household, consider a corner protector or furniture coasters that won’t snag the edges when rearranging layouts for game nights.
