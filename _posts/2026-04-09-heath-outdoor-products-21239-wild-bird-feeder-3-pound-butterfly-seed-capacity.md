## Capacity and seed compatibility
### The 3-pound “butterfly” seed capacity
Yes, the model name’s butterfly seed capacity is a mouthful, but the practical takeaway is simple: you can feed a surprisingly large number of small to mid-sized birds before you need to refill. The capacity threshold is useful for folks who want to minimize maintenance on busy weekends or during bird-friendly autumns when seed consumption spikes. The 3-pound capacity translates into longer intervals between refills, which is perfect for folks who want birding without a full-on seed procurement sprint every other day.

### Seed blends that work well
Butterfly seed blends are a broad category that includes nyjer, white proso millet, and a mix of hulled or shelled seeds that attract finches, sparrows, and a handful of cardinals and tufted titmice. When you’re choosing blends, consider your local feathered population. If you’re in a feathered metropolis, nyjer (thistle) is a clear winner for goldfinches, pine siskins, and certain warblers at certain times of the year. If you’re dealing with more generalists, a mixed seed blend that includes millet can help diversify the visitors. If you ever consider a seed cake or suet, you’ll want to place it away from the main feeder to avoid the heavy traffic turning the area into a bird alley where the ping-pong of beaks competes with the weather forecast.

### Real-world refill strategy
A practical refill strategy is the name of the game. With a 3-pound capacity, you’ll want to check the feed level occasionally, especially if you have a yard that’s a bird magnet. In our tests, we found that refilling every 3–5 days in peak feeding seasons keeps the birds happy and prevents seeds from getting stale or attracting pests. It’s not a “set it and forget it” device—there’s a little bit of stewardship involved—but that’s part of the joy of feeding wildlife, isn’t it?
