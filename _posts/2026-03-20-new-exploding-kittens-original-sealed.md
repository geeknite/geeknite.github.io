## Why the original still matters
Exploding Kittens burst into the gaming scene with a minimalist premise that somehow managed to flip the switch on party gaming norms. The essence is simple: a take on the classic Russian roulette concept, but with humor, surprise, and a dash of strategic misdirection. In a landscape of heavy euro games and sprawling campaigns, the original card game is a reminder that fun can be small, fast, and hilariously unpredictable.

### Core mechanics in plain language
- Each turn you draw cards, trying to avoid drawing an exploding kitten card. If you draw one, you might be out of the round unless you have a defuse card to save yourself.
- Defuse cards offer heroic power ups that let you live to see another turn, often at the expense of someone else who misreads the table dynamics.
- Other cards shave options from opponents, steal cards, or even nudge everyone toward ridiculous outcomes. The result is a social puzzle: who bluffs the best, who remembers the order of the deck, and who is brave enough to press their luck.

The simplicity of the mechanics is the secret sauce. You do not need a rulebook to start playing, and that is the best compliment a party game can get. It invites spontaneity and invites you to rely on your social read of the group rather than a complex scoring system. That is a rare blend in a market saturated with ever more complicated rule sets.
