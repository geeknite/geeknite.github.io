## Handling and preservation: keeping a relic safe for decades to come
Caring for a vintage game is part art, part habit, and part science. Here are the practical steps you can take to preserve your 1967 Skip-Bo treasure without turning it into a fragile exhibit:

- Store the box upright on a shelf away from direct sunlight. The Texas sun has a way of aging cardboard that is not entirely kind.
- Keep humidity moderate. A dry storage environment helps prevent warping and keeps the card stock from becoming brittle.
- Use acid-free sleeves for individual cards if you plan on handling them frequently. This helps preserve the print and reduces the chance of unwanted yellowing.
- Avoid aggressive cleaning. A soft brush or cloth is enough to remove dust. Avoid solvents or cleaners that could affect the ink or the velvet lining.
- Maintain a consistent temperature. Large swings cause materials to expand and contract, which can lead to cracking and edge wear faster than a chaotic game night.

If you care for it properly, your Skip-Bo relic can survive for another generation of tabletop enthusiasts who value a bit of theater with their strategy.
