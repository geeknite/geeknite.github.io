### What you get in the Fortune Row Dice Set

The Fortune Row kit ships with a thoughtful spread of dice and a few accessories that feel tailored to a city noir vibe. Here is a rundown of what arrives in the box:

- 6 standard d6s in a three color palette: shadow black, dusk gray, and fate gold
- 2 symbol dice, both d6s but with stylized icons instead of pips, designed to prompt narrative beats
- 1 d20 for dramatic checks and scene escalations
- 1 d8 and 1 d4 themed around fortune and misfortune motifs
- A compact velvet bag for on the go sessions
- A quick-start guide that focuses on using dice to evoke mood rather than just hitting numbers
- A small fate tracker card that you can slip into your GM screen for gentle reminders of the scene’s tempo

The dice themselves are well weighted and feel substantial in the hand. They are not the cheap acrylic you might expect from a budget bundle; these have a polished finish with a subtle sheen that catches light in a way that says you mean business when the city is watching. The symbols on the two narrative dice are tasteful without being cartoonish, hinting at the occult and the underworld without tipping into cheesiness.

### Design and aesthetics

The Fortune Row dice set leans into the noir aesthetic City of Mist fans know well. The color palette is a deep, cinematic mix of black, charcoal, and gold. The pips on the standard d6s are crisp and easy to read, while the symbol dice carry motifs that feel intentional rather than decorative. The symbols are not random doodles but rather cues that you can weave into the fiction you are building with your players. If your table appreciates a little theater, Fortune Row delivers that in spades.

The packaging is a small win in itself. The tin is sturdy enough to survive a backpack raid from a curious cat or a fridge magnet that wants to steal your dice. The velvet lining is a nice touch, though it does pick up dust easily; a quick wipe down is all that’s needed before you roll again. The quick-start guide is concise and friendly, avoiding the trap of jargon while still giving you enough to run a lively session with just this set.

One nice surprise is the optional image inlay that comes with the set, a glossy print you can place on the table to set the mood. It isn’t essential, but it does help frame the fiction around the dice you are rolling. If you like to stage your sessions with a little art, you will find this a useful addition.

### Build quality and durability

In terms of materials, the Fortune Row dice feel robust. The dice are made from a quality resin blend that resists chipping better than cheaper plastics, and the finish holds up well with normal play. The edge rounding on the dice is comfortable, making them glide across the table without catching on fabric or play mats. The symbols on the narrative dice are etched cleanly and filled with enamel, which should resist fading with light use. I wouldn’t churn through them like a gamer who camps at the table all weekend, but for regular weekly sessions, they hold up just fine.

If you have a particularly energetic playgroup, consider a soft dice tray to reduce the occasional tumble onto the floor. A small incident with a stray die is not the end of the world, but it does break immersion when the rally goes from noir to chaos in a single bounce.

### How it feels in play

City of Mist is a game about atmosphere, character, and the tension between the everyday and the mythic. The Fortune Row dice set aligns with that vibe by offering dice that feel tangible and thematically consistent with the setting. The two symbol dice act as prompts that your players can roll to generate a scene beat or a twist in the investigation. The presence of a d20 is a pleasant nod to classic RPG structure, giving you a reliable tool for dramatic checks when you need to push the story toward a cliffhanger.

In actual play, you will likely use the standard d6s for regular checks, while the symbol dice act as catalysts for scene shifts. The d20 is reserved for moments when the city stands still and watches as fate decides the next turn. The d8 and d4, both aligned with fortune motifs, provide a quick way to incorporate fortune-based re-rolls or narrative boosts without complicating the core mechanic.

One potential learning curve: if you are used to a strict numeric system, you might initially treat the symbol dice as a novelty. But once you embrace their storytelling function, they become powerful tools for collaborative world-building. A player can roll a symbol die and instantly propose a twist that aligns with the arc of their masked alter ego. The rest of the table then builds on that beat, weaving a more personal and emotionally resonant scene than a straight numeric success/failure would allow.

The Fortune Row set shines when used as a storytelling catalyst. The symbol dice provide a tactile way to prompt backstories, secrets, and emotional stakes. ### How to use with City of Mist campaigns

Fortune Row works best when you want to shift from linear investigation to character-driven scenes. Use the symbol dice to trigger flashbacks, secret reveals, or moral quandaries that push a character toward a masked identity moment. Use the standard d6s for skill checks and to represent class or persona dice in the City of Mist framework. The d20 is your emergency brake for turning a good scene into a pivotal moment that changes the direction of the campaign.

If you want a quick method to integrate Fortune Row into your sessions, try this: at the end of every major scene, roll the symbol dice and pick a prompt that your players must address in the next scene. This creates a natural continuity and gives players a rhythm to anticipate. Pair this with a Fate Clock or 4x6 scene tracker that you can adapt mid game; the Fate Tracker in the Fortune Row kit is a good starting point, but feel free to substitute your own method if your table favors a more mechanical approach.
