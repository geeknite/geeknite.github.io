The funny thing is that once you begin to play, the game reveals a core mechanic that you can replicate with minimal guidance: you match colors, you try to shed cards, and you sprint toward a finish line while encountering occasional wildcards that bend the rules in amusing ways. - Turn structure: On your turn, you draw a card from the deck, play cards from your hand that match color or sequence criteria, and then discard if needed. The exact ordering and stacking rules are intentionally flexible to allow improvisation.

If you want to formalize these ideas for your family, consider implementing a few house rules that retain the spirit of the original while providing predictable structure.
