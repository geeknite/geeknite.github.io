## Safety, side effects, and caveats

### Safety profile
Quality dewormers in the 100-tablet class are generally considered safe when used as directed. Still, nothing in the world of medicine is 100% risk-free for all dogs. Some dogs may experience mild GI upset, changes in appetite, vomiting, or loose stools after dosing. More severe adverse reactions are rare but can occur, particularly in dogs with preexisting conditions or those taking other medications. If you observe unusual symptoms after administration, contact your veterinarian promptly.

### Contraindications and interactions
Avoid giving a dewormer to dogs known to be hypersensitive to any of the active ingredients. Some products may interact with other meds, including certain heartworm preventives or anticonvulsants, so a quick vet check-in is always prudent if your dog is on multiple medications. If you are breeding dogs or if your dog is pregnant, seek veterinary guidance before administering any dewormer.

### Special populations
Puppies, senior dogs, or dogs with chronic illnesses deserve special attention. Dosing for puppies is typically different, and certain formulations may be recommended or avoided based on age or weight. Similarly, senior dogs with liver or kidney concerns may require dose adjustments or alternative products. Again, this is where your veterinarian should be your guiding star rather than the nearest online forum. No one should replace veterinary advice with memes and cute dog pictures, no matter how persuasive the memes are.
