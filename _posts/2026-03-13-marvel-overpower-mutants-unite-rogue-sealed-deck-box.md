Care and preservation

Preserving 1990s cardboard and plastic is a little like trying to maintain a pet dinosaur. It requires the right environment, a little common sense, and a dash of patience. Temperature control matters. Avoid direct sunlight. Keep humidity in check to prevent warping or color bleed on the box art. If you plan to display the box, consider mounting it away from bright windows and invest in a display stand that supports the heavier end of the box without warping the edges. For the cards themselves, consider storage sleeves or top loaders if you want to keep the deck pristine but still accessible for demonstration. The aim is to keep the surface gloss and the internal deck in as close to mint condition as possible.

The Rogue character deck and the tokens inside are, ideally, preserved by minimal handling. If you are the type who must touch the cards, wash your hands first, avoid greasy fingerprints, and consider a light anti static approach when transporting them. We know this is not always practical, but it is a good habit.
