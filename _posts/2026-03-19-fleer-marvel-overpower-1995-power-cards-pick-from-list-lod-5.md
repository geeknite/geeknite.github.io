## What is OverPower 1995 and why Lod 5 matters
OverPower started as a collectible card game by Fleer with Marvel licenses, glossy art, and a ruleset that rewarded clever drafting and synergy. In plain terms: instead of drawing a random power that might be great or mediocre for your hero, you get five options on the table and you choose one to shape your next few turns. It is a mini draft within each duel. The mechanic rewards foresight, risk assessment, and a little bit of bluffing: can you sell the idea that your five options are all terrible to coax the opponent into a counter that actually isn t optimal?

## The Lod 5 mechanic explained
### How it works
LOD 5 presents players with a curated list of five Power Cards. Each card represents a unique ability, buff, or effect that can alter the course of a duel. The player selects one card from the list to add to their hero’s arsenal for the upcoming turns. The rest of the cards stay out of circulation for that round, making the selection a valuable strategic resource rather than a throwaway choice.

The design intent is straightforward but deliciously devious: you want something that complements your current board state, your recent card draws, and your opponent’s likely plan. It also means that even if you are behind, you can still flip a match by choosing the perfect card from the five shown to you. The five-card pool makes every decision feel weighty, every choice a potential swing.

### Why five cards and not more or less
Five is not a random flourish; it is the sweet spot in game design. Too many options overwhelm players, turning the pick into a gut-check lottery. Too few options reduce meaningful choice, making the mechanic a binary coin toss. Five cards strike a balance between variety and manageability. It invites strategic thinking without descending into paralysis by analysis. And the five-card hand aesthetic is a comforting nod to classic card games without drowning in probability math.

### Interaction with the rest of the deck
Power Cards exist to create synergy opportunities. A Lod 5 pick might interact with your hero powers, your team plan, or your opponent s likely counters. The savvy player looks for triggers: cards that boost damage when combined with a particular hero power, or effects that chain into a defensive shield when you sense an aggression window forming. Lod 5 rewards players who can read the table, not just their own card catalog.
