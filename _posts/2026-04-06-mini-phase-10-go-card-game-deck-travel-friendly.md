## How to play on the go
Travel games require a balance: enough rules to be fun, not so many rules you need a pocket-sized jurisprudence degree. The Mini Phase 10 GO! deck delivers a streamlined GO! version that keeps the Core Phase 10 rhythm while removing some of the long setup and long breaths between turns. Here’s how to get rolling in real-world microtime.

### Quick setup
- Shuffle the compact deck.
- Deal a small number of cards per player (the GO! format typically uses a reduced hand size, which speeds things up and keeps the table from turning into a full-on strategy summit).
- Place the reference sheet within easy reach. It is not a sacred text, but it helps if you momentarily forget whether a Skip counts as a wild or a moral obligation.
- Each player keeps score rough and ready on the included pad. If you lose the pad midflight, you can improvise with receipts, napkins, or the back of the pilot’s license (just kidding, don’t do that).

### The GO! flow in travel mode
- On your turn, you draw a card and either lay down a completed phase or add to your hand. The GO! rules cut down the multi-phase marathon into several quick micro-goals, so you crash through a phase or two before the seat belt sign goes on.
- If you complete a phase, you call it and advance. If you miss your chance, you still get to play a card and keep the mini drama alive. It’s the casino of your living room, but with less sweat and more laughter.
- The game ends when a player completes all required phases or when the tour group decides you have entertained them enough for one flight.

### Rules tweaks for travel reality
- Shorter rounds, fewer players: it’s amazing how dramatically a four-crowd game turns into a mudslide of cards after a delayed arrival.
- Scoring is simplified: you gain points for finishing a phase earlier, lose points for holding too many cards, and get bonus points for flair with the GO! extras. The exact math is less important than the glow of accomplishment on your fellow traveler’s face as you celebrate finishing a Phase while the seat neighbor is mid-snore.
- Optional house rules: you can banish the high drama of massive combos by leaning into quick penalties for misfires, or you can allow a tiny “shuffle mercy” if someone’s bag practically exploded all over the aisle.

### Strategy for tiny tables
On a coffee shop table or a tray table, speed wins. The GO! version rewards clean, decisive turns, not marathon sessions of “I’ll just redraw six times and hope for a miracle.” It’s a game that respects your time, your space, and your neighbor’s personal bubble.
