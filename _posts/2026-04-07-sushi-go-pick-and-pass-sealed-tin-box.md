## What is Sushi Go! and what is this tin box edition?

Sushi Go! is a fast-paced drafting card game where players pass a hand of cards around the table, choosing one and passing the rest to the next player. The selected card stays with you, while the rest wander to their doom in someone else’s hand. It’s quick, it’s cute, and yes, the score sheet has more sushi varieties than a trendy restaurant menu.

The tin box edition is exactly what it sounds like: a sturdy metal tin that holds all the standard cards, plus some extras or maybe just a premium packaging upgrade. ## How to play in a tin nutshell

Sushi Go! is a 2-5 player game where you draft one card from a hand of three and pass the rest to the left. You keep the chosen card and play rounds until the deck is exhausted. The catch? The cards you pass are the same cards everyone else will covet, so your strategy is a delicate dance between greed and graciousness, kind of like a family dinner where grandma\'s meatloaf is on the line.

Here\'s a quick outline:
1) Each player begins with a hand of three cards.
2) Choose one card to keep and place it face-down in front of you.
3) Pass the remaining two cards to the left.
4) Reveal all chosen cards and tally points according to the sushi combinations on the cards.
5) Repeat until all cards are used. The player with the most points wins.

The scoring is where Sushi Go! shines in its simplicity. For example, making a set of Nigiri cards (salmon, squid, egg) yields points if you pair them with Wasabi or Pudding cards that boost or alter your totals. The Pudding cards tend to be a late-game kicker that can swing the final numbers in surprising ways. It\'s a tidy little system that makes you think in flavors rather than just numbers.

## Scoring and dish-by-dish etiquette

The standard Sushi Go! scoring revolves around the cards you collect in each round. Here\'s a quick cheat-sheet for the common dishes you\'ll see in the tin box edition:
- Nigiri: Each card gives you a base score (2, 3, or 5 points depending on the type). If you pair Nigiri with Wasabi, the points multiply – but you must commit to Wasabi on the same turn you pick the Nigiri card.
- Maki Rolls: Most players love to collect as many Maki as possible because the person with the most at the end gets bonus points.
- Sashimi: Collect three Sashimi cards to score points; missing a card is heartbreak in a rice-ball universe.
- Puddings: These are late-game boosts that can grant victory points and can swing the final tally if you happen to be behind. They add a nice strategic twist without complicating the core drafting.

The exact values can vary slightly with editions, but the general principle remains: balance immediate benefits against potential future returns; greed rarely pays in this game, except when it does, in which case you quietly nod at your own audacity.

## Maintenance, care, and longevity of your tin box

- Keep the tin dry and clean; use a simple cloth to wipe it after play. Paper towels or a quick drink spill are friend, not foe.
- Avoid dropping the tin on hard surfaces; the paint and edges can show wear, which is a shame if you like your game to look pristine for Instagram posts, though real life often proves authenticity is in the wear and tear now more than ever.
- Store the deck inside the tin with the original insert. It keeps the cards in order and reduces the risk of misplacing any few crucial cards (we all misplace at least one card per game night; there are therapy jokes there).
