## Strategy, Luck, and Psychology at the Table

Exploding Kittens is a game where luck and psychology do a slow dance while you pretend to be strategic about your Defuse card placements. The Party Pack Edition pushes this into the realm of social strategy. Here are a few mental models to keep in mind when you sit down with a group of friends who may or may not have a competitive streak:

- Reading the room beats reading the rulebook: In large groups, the most effective strategy often involves reading your opponents’ tells. Who looks stressed when a particular card is revealed? Who grins nervously after someone plays a steal card? These subtle cues can be more valuable than any card you hold.
- Card counting is cute but dangerous: Counting cards in this deck is not a reliable long-term strategy, because the deck is designed to keep a surprising amount of chaos in flux. Use your memory to track pivotal cards, but don t let it become a compulsion that ruins the mood.
- TheDefuse economy matters: In party mode, every Defuse card is a precious resource. The decision to use a Defuse card is a small negotiation with the table about how much you value your own safety versus keeping the deck unpredictable for others. Sometimes the bold move to save a Defuse for a later round will win you the night; other times, it will yield nothing but poor air quality and a loud sigh from your friends.
- Bluffing and misdirection flourish in larger groups: The Party Pack Edition rewards those who can paint a compelling narrative around a seemingly useless card. If you can sell the idea that a less-than-ideal card is actually a game-changing artifact, you have already won part of the night’s social battle, even if you end up losing the round.

As with any party game, your mileage will vary based on your group. If you have a table full of people who treat the game as a serious competition, you may find yourself with tense high-stakes drama.
