## Setup, Maintenance, and Longevity
A good foosball table becomes a family heirloom if you treat it with respect and minimal neglect. Here are some maintenance routines that will keep your 55 inch table kicking for years:

- Cleaning: Wipe the surface with a microfiber cloth after use. This removes dust, fingerprints, and the occasional leg-jarring snack crumb that somehow lands on the field—because the table is level and the food is not.
- Rods care: Periodically check for wobble and lubricate the rods if the model uses non-wobble bearings. A dry rod is a sad rod. A lightly oiled rod glides like a skater on ice, yielding faster reaction times and fewer elbow dents when your friend performs a victory celebration that would make a Viking jealous.
- Leveling: Ensure your legs have proper leveling. Uneven surfaces can warp the ball’s behavior and make the player figures lean like they’re posing on a runway. If your floor is warped, adjust the leg levelers and check after each move to keep things fair.
- Ball care: The balls should be kept clean and swapped when they become scuffed or burrowed with marks of many a lost game. A well-spun ball is a happy ball, and a happy ball translates into a happy match.
- Accessories and storage: The 55 inch model ships with a handful of spare screws and a wrench or two. Keep these in a labeled container, ideally near your toolbox, so you don’t have to hunt for the right size when you suddenly realize your scorekeeper is drifting into the 80s with a loose end.

Longevity is also about material choices. A well-made 55 inch table uses high-quality wood or composites with durable veneers, robust hardware, and reinforced rods. The cabinet should be able to resist a couple of enthusiastic “we’re still playing” sessions without warping. The payoff is obvious: you’ll have a centerpiece that looks sharp in photos, plays smoothly in battles, and ages with character rather than falling apart with a shrug after the second year.
