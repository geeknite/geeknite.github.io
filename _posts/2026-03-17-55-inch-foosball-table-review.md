## Setup, Maintenance, and Longevity
A foosball table is a family heirloom in the making if you treat it with respect and regular care. Here are maintenance routines that will keep your 55 inch table kicking for years:

- Cleaning: Wipe the playing surface with a microfiber cloth after use. Dust, fingerprints, and stray snack crumbs love a good tabletop drama, but a quick wipe keeps bounce honest.
- Rods care: Check for wobble and lubricate the rods if your model uses bearings. A dry rod is a sad rod; a lightly oiled rod glides like a skater, delivering faster reaction times and fewer elbow dents during celebratory sprees.
- Leveling: Ensure legs are properly leveled. An uneven table can warp ball behavior and turn “home” ends into sideshow attractions. Adjust leg levelers and recheck after heavy play sessions.
- Ball care: Keep balls clean and replace when they become scuffed. A well-spun ball is a happy ball, and happy balls translate into happier games.
- Accessories and storage: The 55 inch model ships with spare screws and wrenches. Store these in labeled containers so you don’t need a treasure map when a tiny part goes missing.

Longevity depends on materials and build quality. A well-made 55 inch table uses sturdy wood or composites, reinforced rails, and robust hardware. It should survive boisterous celebrations, accidental bumps, and the occasional “we’re still playing” encore without showing its age.
