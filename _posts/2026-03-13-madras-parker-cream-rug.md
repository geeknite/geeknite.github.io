## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping It Fresh Without Killing Your Weekend
### Routine care
- Vacuum once or twice a week with a gentle, slow pass to avoid fiber wear.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even exposure to sunlight and foot traffic.
- Address spills quickly with blotting; avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into fibers.
- For mild stains, use a water-based cleaner or a solution of warm water and a small amount of gentle detergent. Always test a hidden area first.

### Stain guide
- Food stains: Blot, clean, and repeat. Most food stains respond well to a mild detergent and a fresh cloth.
- Beverage spills: Blot, then use a mild cleaning solution. For stubborn marks, a mix of white vinegar and water can help break up residue; rinse and blot dry.
- Pet accidents: Blot up as much as possible, then clean with an enzymatic cleaner to neutralize odors and mark residues.

### Long-term care tips
- Consider a professional cleaning every 12–18 months for deeper maintenance, especially if the rug sees high traffic or family pets.
- Store or cover the rug properly when not in use for long periods to avoid sun fade and dust buildup.
- Inspect for wear and tear along edges; edging attention prevents fraying and gives a more polished look.
