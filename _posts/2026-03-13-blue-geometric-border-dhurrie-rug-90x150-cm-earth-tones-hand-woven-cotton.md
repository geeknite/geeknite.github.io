## Materials, Build, and Practicality

The rug is described as hand woven cotton, which typically means a few practical realities:
- Comfort: Cotton fibers deliver a comfortable underfoot feel that’s softer than many sisal or jute options, yet firmer than plush wool. It’s a nice middle ground for bare feet, kids, and the occasional pet who believes the rug is a personal runway.
- Durability: Flat-weave cotton rugs are generally durable for living spaces with moderate traffic. They don’t have heavy pile to snag on zippers or snag threads as easily as tufted textiles, but they aren’t as stain-resistant as synthetics. A good routine of quick blotting and light rotation will keep color distribution even.
- Maintenance: Regular vacuuming with the beater bar off helps prevent dirt from embedding. For spills, act fast with a clean cloth and mild detergent; avoid soaking the fibers entirely. Drying is essential to prevent water marks or color bleeding, especially with the lighter earth tones.
- Longevity: Cotton’s natural resiliance means the rug will likely wear in gracefully if you give it a little love—rotate every few months, especially if sunlight hits it through a window that acts like a sunbeam straight from a sci-fi laser.

Careful note on shedding: Expect tiny amounts of cotton fiber debris during the first few months. It’s not a sign of poor quality; it’s the rug shedding its pre-sheddability. A firm, regular vacuuming will reduce pile fibers in traffic zones and help you maintain a crisp appearance.

Care tips you’ll thank us for later:
- Vacuum with a light touch and a low suction setting. The flat weave doesn’t need aggressive suction; you don’t want to pull fibers loose.
- Spot-clean stains promptly using a mild solution of water and a gentle detergent. Avoid bleach or harsh solvents; they can alter color and weaken fiber.
- Rotate the rug 90 degrees a few times a year to ensure even exposure to sunlight and furniture traffic.
- Consider a rug pad for slipped edges and extra comfort. A pad also extends the rug’s life by reducing wear on the backing.
