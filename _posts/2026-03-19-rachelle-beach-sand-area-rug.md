## Cleaning and Maintenance

### Routine Cleaning
Vacuuming every couple of days is recommended to keep the pile looking sharp and to remove dust that can accumulate quickly on light colors. Use a vacuum with adjustable height and a gentle setting to avoid pulling on the fibers. A high-suction vacuum is fine, but if you have a delicate tuft, you might want to switch to a brush attachment or a low-suction setting to prevent fiber pull.

### Spills and Stains
Blot spills quickly with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can spread the stain and push it deeper into the fibers. For most coffee, juice, or sauce spills, a mild detergent solution followed by thorough blotting works well. For stubborn stains, consider a gentle foam cleaner designed for synthetic fibers. Always test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. If a pet accident happens, act fast, clean up, then rinse the area lightly to prevent a lingering odor.

### Drying Time
Expect a moderate drying time; this rug isn’t a marine rescue mission, but it’s not a swamp either. If you’re cleaning a large area, you might prop a fan nearby to accelerate drying. Pro tip: avoid direct sunlight on sensitive synthetic dyes for extended periods, as prolonged exposure can cause subtle color shifts over time.

### Sunlight and Fading
The beach sand color is resilient to light fading but not immune. If your space has a lot of sun, consider rotating the rug seasonally or pairing with curtains that reduce UV exposure. A little sunlight is good for keeping white balance in check, but too much of it can lead to slight color drift after years of basking.
