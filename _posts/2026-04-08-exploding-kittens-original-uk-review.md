### How to play: a quick tour for beginners (and totally re-derivation for seasoned players)
Note: The base game rules are simple enough to pick up in one sitting, but there’s enough room for misdirection and clever misplays that you’ll still enjoy watching your friends pretend they know what they’re doing.

1) Setup: Remove all Exploding Kitten cards and Defuse cards from the deck. Shuffle the remaining cards, then deal 7 cards to each player. Place the Defuse cards back into the deck and add enough Exploding Kitten cards to match the number of players minus one. Shuffle the deck, then place it facedown in the center.
2) The goal: Be the last player remaining who hasn’t exploded. If you draw an Exploding Kitten card and you don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out of the round.
3) On your turn: You draw a card, then you can play any number of cards from your hand to either manipulate the deck, protect yourself, or meddle with others. The See the Future card lets you peek at the top three cards; the Attack card makes the next player take two turns; the Defuse card (definitely not in the deck when you start) is your lifesaver in a near-miss scenario.
4) Exploding Kittens: If you draw one and you can’t Defuse it, you’re out. The fun twist is that later rounds continue with fewer players until only one remains.
5) End of game: The last player standing wins. Small, fast, and deliciously chaotic.

To keep things spicy, you’ll want to layer in the See the Future and Shuffle cards to create moments of “squarely gambled fate” suspense. The risk of the deck becomes part of the delight, and the social dynamics—the way players bluff or celebrate—are where Exploding Kittens earns its keep.
