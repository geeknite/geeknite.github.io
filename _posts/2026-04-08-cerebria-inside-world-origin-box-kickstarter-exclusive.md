## Setup, Play Time, and Accessibility: Realistic Expectations

One of the recurring challenges in Cerebria is the learning curve. The base game is deep; the Origin Box adds layers on top. Here are some practical takeaways for a smooth experience:

- Setup time: Expect a longer setup if you go all-in on the Origin Box components. The upgraded boards, extra tokens, and more complex mood tracks contribute to a more thorough pre-game routine. We recommend a 15–25 minute setup window for your first game after you’ve got the hang of the base game.
- Play time: Typical games run 60–120 minutes depending on player count and how sparingly you use the new content. If you’re playing four or five players with all the extras, be prepared for the long haul. Cerebria is not a light evening; it’s a cinematic experience with strategic rhythm.
- Rule accessibility: The Origin Box sometimes comes with an updated rulebook or laminated quick-reference sheets. Take advantage of them. A few crisp reference sheets can save you many minutes of flipping through rulebook pages, especially under the pressure of a closing turn.
- Player count and balance: The base game scales well, and the Origin Box generally preserves that balance while offering more variations. The risk here is that with more content, some combos become dominant if not properly nerfed or balanced by the rule tweaks that accompany the upgrade.
