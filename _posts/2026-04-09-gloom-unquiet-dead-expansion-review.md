## Introduction
In the dim glow of a candle that should have gone out hours ago, Gloom sits there like a moody teenager with a tragic backstory and a black cape. Keith Baker handed the world base gloom with a wink, a bottle of stage lights, and the promise that storytelling could be a pure mechanic rather than a mindless dice roll. Then came Unquiet Dead, the expansion that asks a simple question with the gravity of a graveyard at midnight: what happens when your ghosts start contributing to the family drama? Spoiler: it is glorious, chaotic, and surprisingly humane for a table full of undead overachievers. Here at Geeknite we dug into the deck, checked the ectoplasm, and found that this expansion slides into the base game with the ease of a ghost through a hollow door. No heavy rules overhaul, just more ways to twist a tale and multiply the moans in ways that make you snort with laughter while pretending to cry softly into your sleeve.

## What Gloom is and who Keith Baker is
Gloom is a card game about telling miserable mini stories about a family, with the goal of having your own characters end up the most sadly illuminated while others end up tragically cheerful. The beauty is in the theatrical cards and the art that makes you feel like you are watching a lurid but affectionate soap opera on an overcast night. Keith Baker designed the original concept and setting, and the game has since become a staple for players who like their humor black and their tragedy a little delightful. Unquiet Dead keeps the core premise intact while offering new undead inspired twists that invite you to exaggerate the gloom even further, which, if you ask us, is the exact mood you want when you cannot stop the giggles at a funeral. 

### Core ideas carried into the expansion
Unquiet Dead introduces a fresh deck of cards that slots into your gloom stack with minimal fuss and maximal mischief. The new cards add undead relatives, spectral modifiers, and a handful of artifacts that bend fate in surprising ways. The tone remains playful, with a respectful wink at Gothic vibes rather than a full on horror makeover. If you enjoy the base game but crave more opportunities to tell terrifyingly funny tales, this expansion is basically a love letter to that impulse. 

## Components and quality
### Card stock and artwork
The card stock in Unquiet Dead matches the sturdy feel you expect from Gloom. It shuffles smoothly, resists warping after a night of enthusiastic play, and the edges hold up to both aggressive table talk and aggressive fan theories about the afterlife. The artwork maintains the same charmingly macabre aesthetic that makes the base game a joy to read aloud. It leans into slightly more spectral imagery without losing the goofy energy that makes gloom so endearing. 

### Tokens and extras
In addition to new character and event cards, you may encounter tokens that track curses and ghostly modifiers. The tokens are tactile enough to feel real on the table, which matters when you are trying to manage a tense moment while someone else grins about the sheer dramatic irony of your situation. The added physicality helps reinforce the theme and keeps the action feeling kinetic rather than ceremonial. 

### Packaging and storage
The expansion slots into the base game neatly, and you can keep the undead extras in a small tray with little friction. If you are the sort who loves dedicated storage boxes, you may want to plan for a slightly larger tin or a vendor tray to keep the corpses organized. The packaging is compact and practical, designed for repeated nights of gloom rather than one-off novelty. 

## How to set up and play a round with Unquiet Dead
### Setup basics
Setup is a quick ritual that takes only a few minutes. You lay out the base gloom deck as usual, shuffle in the Unquiet Dead cards, and then deal out gloom for each player. The table mood shifts the moment the expansion cards are introduced; you will notice a subtle shift toward the spectral and a more frequent rate of dramatic reveals. If you are new to gloom, you should not be discouraged by the added variety; the rules remain easy and accessible. If you are a veteran, you will appreciate the extra levers and the way they let you sculpt tension in a more nuanced fashion. 

### Playing through a round
In a typical round you will assign gloom to your characters, play events, and then reveal the dramatic turns as you place more gloom on the board. The undead twist adds new targets for negative modifiers and a few cards that reward clever timing. The pacing remains snappy, and the humor emerges not only from the misfortunes but from the way the cards encourage offbeat storytelling. There is a rhythm here similar to a well-timed punchline in a stand-up set: you want the setup to land, then the reveal to land harder. Unquiet Dead gives you more setups and more hard landings without making the game feel bloated.

### Deck management and strategy tips
Because there are more cards in play, you will want to keep a mental map of what cards are likely to appear and when. The expansion rewards players who track not only the gloom on their own family but also the spectral interference hovering over others. A good rule of thumb is to save a couple of undead influenced cards for late rounds, where they can disrupt the closing arcs and force opponents to improvise a new ending to their stories. If you are new to gloom, start with the basic strategies you learned from the base game and gradually weave in the new undead flavors as you feel more comfortable. The expansion does not demand you rewrite your entire strategy; it invites you to adapt and enjoy the new texture of the gloom night. 

## Theme, tone, and humor balance
Gloom has always lived in a weird space between tragedy and comedy. Unquiet Dead pushes that balance a bit toward the spectral without losing the playful core. The undead elements are not grim for grim sake; they provide a narrative flavor that spices up the jokes and lets players riff on ghostly antics. If your group thrives on dark humor and theatrical storytelling, you will want to embrace Unquiet Dead as a new engine for table drama. If, however, you prefer a purely lighthearted or purely grim tone, you can calibrate by choosing which undead cards you allow at the table and which you veto. The expansion respects your table dynamic and gives you control over how intense the gloom night becomes. 

## Artwork and flavor notes
The art direction continues to work in harmony with the mood of gloom: a mix of Gothic charm, cheeky humor, and a dash of the absurd. The undead figures are not overbearing; they are characterful, with backstories that feel both clever and a little silly. The flavor text reads like a silver screen dialogue written by a ghost who had too much coffee and a sharp sense of irony. This keeps the table engaged and encourages players to lean into performance, not just card counting. If you enjoy the way gloom invites you to perform a tragic mini scene, Unquiet Dead will feel like a natural extension rather than a forced addition. 

## How Unquiet Dead compares to other gloom expansions
When stacked against other gloom expansions, Unquiet Dead sits comfortably as a cohesive extension that respects the base game while introducing fresh narrative opportunities. It avoids becoming a separate sub game or bloated with rule citations. Instead, it provides a walk-on character arc that can be slotted into any gloom night with ease. Some expansions lean toward complexity or new win conditions; Unquiet Dead keeps the focus on storytelling and mood, which is precisely what many players love about gloom. If you want a well integrated addition that amplifies the core experience without making it feel heavier, this expansion is a strong contender.
