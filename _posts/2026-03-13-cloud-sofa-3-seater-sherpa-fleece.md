## Maintenance and Cleaning
No furniture is perfect in perpetuity; there will be days when you spill a beverage or epic mom-moji of coffee appears on the cushion. The key is maintenance and practical care.

### Cleaning Sherpa Fleece
The sherpa fleece is relatively easy to maintain. Regular vacuuming reduces dust and hair buildup, while occasional spot-cleaning with mild detergent works for minor spills. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fleece or produce unusual odors. It’s important to act quickly on spills; the longer you wait, the more the stain will integrate into the plush surface like a coffee painting in a museum.

### Stain Removal
For minor stains, a cloth dampened with water and a dab of mild soap is enough to lift the stain after a few gentle blotting moves. For more stubborn marks, you might consider a fabric-safe cleaner. Always do a small test on an inconspicuous area.

### Long-term Care
Rotate cushions occasionally to ensure even wear. If you have a pet that sheds, keep a lint roller handy and schedule a regular cleaning plan. In the long term, the goal is to maintain fluffiness rather than letting the cushions flatten into a pancake. The sofa is built to recover some of its plushness but not infinite bounce; treat it well and it will repay you with extra years of cuddle-worthy comfort.
