## Assembly and Painting
### Tools and Tips
If you’re a veteran painter, you’ll zip through these with your regular toolkit: hobby knife, file, super glue or plastic glue, primer, brushes, and your favorite necrotic color palette. If you’re new to the hobby, take a breath—the runners are forgiving. Here are a few practical tips:
- Trim minimal flash and remove mold lines with care. You don’t want to shave down essential details.
- Use primer to give the surface something for paint to grip. A matte black primer can help define the shadows for a more terrifying silhouette.
- Start with a base coat of muted flesh tones or earthy grays, depending on your theme. Then wash with a dark shade to bring out the crevices, followed by dry brushing to highlight raised surfaces.
- For a more cinematic effect, consider a subtle purple or green tint on the wounds or exposed bones to imply disease and rot.

### Painting Style Options
- Classic Necrotic: Muted browns and olives with deep red accents on mouths and exposed flesh. Perfect for grimdark campaigns.
- Gory Flash: High-contrast colors with bright reds and bone whites to make the runners pop on a busy board.
- Gothic Mono: A single monochrome scheme with bone-white highlights, which looks striking under game lighting and keeps your table from looking like a candy shop after a dragon’s sneeze.

### Display and Basing
Basing is more than just a place to stand the minis. Consider a textured base to imply a dungeon floor, a ruined courtyard, or a swampy alleyway. A few tufts of grass or tufts of tufty moss can transform a dull tile into an environment that tells a story as your party tears through the horde. If you’re playing D&D or another RPG system, basing can help the group contextualize the scene—your players will remember that swamp zone where the runners hounded the party like hungry geese on a picnic.

## Care, Maintenance, and Storage
### Handling and Cleaning
After trimming mold lines, you’ll want to wash the minis in warm soapy water and rinse well. Dry thoroughly before priming. Resin-based or epoxy-based washes can reveal deeper textures; plastics generally tolerate gentle handling without chipping if you use proper varnish layers.

### Storage Tips
Store them in a labeled container with separators for transport to gaming nights. A small foam-lined case prevents paint chipping and keeps the runners from mashing into one another on the way to the table. If you’re a collector who rotates armies, consider basing options that are quick to swap between sets while preserving paint integrity.

### Longevity Pointers
- Keep humidity in a moderate range to avoid skin-splitting on older plastics.
- Apply a matte sealant to reduce wear from tabletop friction and miniature handling.
- Avoid direct sunlight to preserve color vibrancy over time.
