## Durability and Longevity

When you buy two decks, you’re effectively funding a small army of future card nights. Durability depends largely on handling: misfires happen, sleeves happen, Velcro pockets exist in every gamer’s life. Here’s how to maximize longevity:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The colors can fade faster than you’d think if left in a sunny window (and no one wants a red deck that looks pink).
- Use a proper deck box or tuck-case. This protects corners and reduces the risk of creases along the edges after heavy shuffles.
- Avoid eating while shuffling. We all know the coffee-and-card-balance is a delicate thing; keep liquids away from the cards to prevent sticky catastrophes.
- Clean your hands before handling. Oils from skin can subtly affect the finish over time, especially if you’re a heavy user who shuffles after every party trick.

With sane handling, these two decks will provide you with months of service, and the color variety keeps your games feeling fresh. If you’re a collector or a performance artist, you’ll appreciate that both decks age similarly, so your practice sessions don’t produce jarring wear patterns that betray which deck you’re using mid-routine.

## Care and Maintenance Tips

Simple, practical maintenance will extend the life of your cards far beyond a single season of gaming:

- Dry storage and a cool environment. Moisture is the silent killer of card stock; keep humidity moderate.
- Regular dusting, but no abrasive cleaners. If your cards get a little dusty, a gentle brush or microfiber cloth is enough.
- Proper handling. Avoid cramming decks into tight spaces; let them breathe a little and avoid bending the corners.
- Use a protective case. This isn’t just about looks; it’s about preventing creases and maintaining a consistent feel across sessions.

If you do these things, your 108 cards will yield a consistent experience worth returning to again and again. And who knows? You might even get a brag-worthy ping from the table when you pull out the two-deck setup and everyone yells “Two; the sequel!”
