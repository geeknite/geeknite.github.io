## Mechanics and Gameplay Feel
### Core mechanics at a glance
Mutants Unite aims for quick rounds with minimal setup. You’ll likely have a central action economy revolving around playing cards to perform attacks, build combos, or trigger mutant synergies. Expect a deck-building layer, a few resource-management decisions, and a battle tempo that can swing dramatically from turn to turn.

If you’re coming from longer-form games, this will feel brisk, with a focus on tempo and momentum rather than a marathon of long planning phases. It’s less about who has the perfect combo on turn seven and more about who navigates the current board state with the most flair.

### Tactical decisions in early rounds
Early-round decisions typically center on your opening deck choices: do you pursue speed, card draw, or direct power boosts? The design nudges you toward a playable rhythm rather than a wildly punishing planning phase. This can be a relief for casual players, but it also invites a degree of strategic depth for those who want to optimize their synergies.

We saw a few memorable sequences where a well-timed event card turned a board state from “okay” to “we need a miracle,” which is the sweet spot for many card-players who crave a dash of drama without drowning in numbers.

The framework supports both competitive and cooperative vibes depending on house rules. You’ll appreciate the moments of back-and-forth banter as cards hit the table in dramatic fashion.
