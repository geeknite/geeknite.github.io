## How to Play: A Quick Refresher for Veterans and Newbies Alike
Skip-Bo is deceptively simple on the surface: players try to play cards in numeric order from 1 to 12, using stock piles and discard piles to build out runs. The wild Skip-Bo cards provide flexible options when you’re staring down a stubborn lock in your own stock pile. The goal is to be the first player to play all cards from your stock pile. It’s a race, but not the kind that ends with someone sprinting and then collapsing in the cereal aisle at a grocery store back-to-back with a snack. It’s a race that unfolds across a tabletop with a blend of luck, memory, and strategy—especially when you’re forced to use wilds to rescue a failing run or to block an opponent who seems to have all the right cards at the exact wrong moment.

Here’s a high-level overview of the core rules, focused for players who want to jump in quickly:
- Each player has a stock pile and several discard piles.
- Turn order rotates clockwise; on your turn, you may play cards from your hand to the building piles in the center, following the 1–12 sequence.
- Skip-Bo wild cards can represent any number, which makes them incredibly powerful for bridging gaps.
- When you empty your stock pile, you win the round (and often the table sighs with a mix of relief and nostalgia).
- If you get stuck, you can draw from the draw pile to replenish your hand.

The Accessories Set doesn’t modify the core rules, but it does make the experience of setting up and tearing down a lot faster. The organizers keep your piles tidy, so you’re never three rounds into a game before you realize you’ve confused your 2s with your 7s. A tidy play area is a happier play area, and that’s not just sentimentality speaking; it translates into smoother rounds and a lower chance of accidental “one more round” benders that last until you wake up with a glow-in-the-dark card on your cheek.
