## How it fits with the base game

If you know Exploding Kittens, you know the rhythm: draw, play, explode, defuse, repeat. Streaking Kittens adds a turbocharger to that rhythm without making you relearn the entire track. The expansion is designed to slot into the existing ruleset, offering new opportunities for mischief, bluffing, and strategic misfires. It doesn’t remove the core tension of not drawing the exploding kitten; it reframes the tension so you’re worried about who will race to a streak instead of who will end up with the exploding card.

### Setup and flow

1. Shuffle the Streaking Kittens deck into the base Exploding Kittens deck, or use a lighter setup by encountering a subset of the new cards. The setup should feel like you’re adding a second layer to a familiar cake, not replacing the cake entirely.
2. Each player draws their standard starting hand, with the new Streak cards shuffled into the mix.
3. Decide on any house rules you plan to use (we cover popular variants below).

Turn flow remains the same at a high level, but with new options that can change the order of operations and who gets to declare the next streak. If you’ve mastered the base game, you’ll pick up the expansion in a couple of rounds, and you’ll be laughing while you figure out the exact moment to trigger a streak that will derail your friends’ carefully planned strategies.

### The core new mechanic: Streak mode

The headline addition is Streak mode. A Streak Kitten card can trigger a temporary mode where players gain access to some extra options and face new costs. Think of it as a temporary power-up: not always worth it, but when it lines up with your hand, it can swing momentum in a single turn. The exact rules around streaks are intentionally flexible to keep the chaos flowing and to encourage improvisation, which is exactly the kind of chaotic improvisation that this game thrives on.

#### Timing and risk

The expansion rewards players who read the table: who is itching for danger, who wants to fish for a payoff, and who is simply clinging to their defuse with a white-knuckled grip. When you decide to embrace a streak, you’re leaning into a moment of uncertainty where one wrong reveal, one “Nope” at the wrong moment, can flip the table. It’s chaotic, but that’s precisely the point.

## Card types and new interactions

### Streaking Kitten cards
These are the titular cards that drive the new mode. When played (or activated, depending on the variant you’re using), they open a short window of “streak time” in which additional actions become available. The exact actions vary from card to card, but the core idea is clear: you create a temporary state where you can influence the flow of the game beyond the standard play order.

### Streak counters and supports
A subset of cards helps track or sustain streaks across turns. Think of these as little in-game incentives that push players toward riskier plays if they want to milk the most value out of a streak. These counters aren’t mandatory; they’re optional ways to increase the chaos level for players who want to tilt the scale toward adrenaline-fueled play.

### Alignments and combos
The expansion introduces a few combo-oriented interactions with existing cards. You’ll encounter moments where a streak aligns with a specific card’s effect, creating a chain that can be extremely satisfying when it pays off and hilariously painful when it backfires in spectacular fashion.

Throughout all of this, the humor remains the north star: the kitteh-based gags, the cheeky flavor text, and the relentless puns keep morale high even when the dice (or the cards) are against you.

## Strategy and practical tips

Here are some practical pointers to help you approach Streaking Kittens with a plan instead of pure instinct:

- Timing is everything. The best streak moments come when you can maximize impact without exposing yourself to a brutal counter-blow. Watch the table, assess who’s low on defense, and consider starting a streak only if you know you can leverage the turn order.
- Use distraction to your advantage. The comedic chaos of the expansion can make other players distracted and more likely to overcommit on a decision. If you can keep a straight face while others flip out, you’ve already gained a little edge.
- Manage your hand. You’ll want to balance risk and defense. Don’t hold too many high-value, streak-activating cards in your hand unless you’re ready to commit to a bold move.
- Read the room, not just the deck. People often get tunnel vision on defuses, but the Streak mode rewards social sensing: who’s likely to chase the most risky option, and who would rather clutch a safer card and watch the chaos unfold from the sidelines?

### Advanced tactics
- Pair streak cards with “nudge” or “Nope” interactions to maximize the chaos on a single turn. The number of players who will attempt to counter your streak usually peaks right after you announce it, which makes the player-to-player dynamics a huge part of the fun.
- If you’re new to the expansion, start with a light variant (see Variants) to learn how streaks interact with the base rules. Once you’ve got the feel for it, you can ramp up the intensity for a night of pure mayhem.

## Variants and house rules

If your group wants to tailor the chaos level, here are a few popular variants you can try:

- Easy streaks: reduce the amount of streak time or the number of cards that can trigger a streak in a single round.
- Team streaks: pair players into teams; streaks impact both teammates, turning a single risky decision into a collaborative spectacle.
- Time-boxed rounds: limit each round to a set number of minutes to encourage snappier decision-making and keep chaos loops from spiraling out of control.

These variants are designed to be friendly for newcomers and veterans alike, letting you tune the adrenaline to your group’s tolerance.
