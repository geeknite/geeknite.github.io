## Display, storage, and care

### Display strategies
If you’re displaying this card, a standard 35-point or 100-point penny sleeve in a binder may not cut it for a high-visibility shelf. A slab or a top-loader with a sturdy acrylic display case is a better match for the 12th Edition Silver line. The goal is to protect the patch window from UV light (which can cause discoloration over many years) and from environmental humidity. A small riser or stand can position the card with the patch in the best lighting angle, maximizing the reflective pop without bam-bam-blinding glare.

### Storage tips
Keep the card in a climate-controlled space and avoid extreme temperature swings. A simple, acid-free binder with archival-safe plastic pages will do the trick. If you own multiple ITG UM patches, consider a dedicated display cabinet or a protective sleeve system that’s designed for thicker cards. The goal is to prevent edge chipping and to maintain the patch’s surface texture.

### Cleaning and handling
Always handle patches with clean hands or cotton gloves. Oil from fingers can tarnish the surface over time, especially on glossy finishes. Avoid laminated top layers that trap moisture; instead, choose breathable displays that still protect the patch from dust and accidental moisture exposure.

To maintain a balanced approach to care, we recommend periodically checking the patch window for any loose edges, ensuring the card sits comfortably without pressure on the corners. A quick inspection every six months can help catch minor issues before they escalate.
