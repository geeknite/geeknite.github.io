# Zombicide: Special Guest Edouard Guiton

Intro: There are two kinds of board game moments that make a reviewer smile in a goofy way. The first is when you open a box and find a perfect blend of components and chaos. The second is when a guest designer signs off on a project and you can actually feel their fingerprints in the rules, the scenarios, and the tiny miniatures that seem to have opinions about what constitutes a viable strategy. Today we dive into Zombicide: Special Guest Edouard Guiton, a collaboration that feels like a crossover episode between a zombie apocalypse and a surprisingly tense bake-off. Strap in, gather your survivors, and try not to roll a crit fail on turn one.

If you are new to Zombicide, this is basically a cooperative board game where you and your crew team up to survive waves of walkers, all while rummaging through drawers for loot and scribbling survival plans that would look more at home in a detective noir than a kitchen spattered apocalypse. If you are a seasoned Zombicide veteran, you want to know how Edouard s guest stamp changes pacing, risk, and table banter. Spoiler alert: there is more chatter at the barricades than a group chat on a bad Halloween.

## The Guest: Who is Edouard Guiton?

Edouard Guiton is one of those designers whose name turns up on a lot of crowd favorite tabletop projects. If you have played several Zombicide iterations, you will notice his influence is not subtle. He brings a flair for story driven scenarios, a preference for tight turn structure, and a sense of humor that does not get in the way of the dice grab. In this special edition crossover, Edouard leans into the tension between buy everything you can loot and dont pull every zombie into the same corridor. The result is a box that sometimes feels like a heist movie in a morgue a little dangerous and absolutely obsessed with loot tables.

### Edouard s design fingerprints

What stands out is the way the guest designer shapes momentum. You will feel the emphasis on teamwork without losing the spark of a quick turn that keeps players on their toes. The scenarios reject a simple go kill everything approach in favor of mini narratives and stagey moments. Each encounter hints at a backstory instead of a dry objective dump. The art direction leans toward moody blues and muted greens, with zombies that look like they would rather discuss the plan than simply eat your brains. It is a tone that makes a co op session feel like a tight caper rather than a straight line of combat. The loot feels earned and not just randomly dropped by a machine that says how many zombies to spawn in a corridor.

## Unboxing and Components: a quick lay of the land

### What s inside the box

- 1 rulebook with Edouard s design notes and a few jokes about keeping zombies fed after midnight
- 5 survivor miniatures with alternate sculpts that nod to Edouard s signature aesthetic a blend of sleek angular heroism and suspicious vibes
- 40 zombie miniatures with varied poses and sculpts
- 1 set of brand new tiles designed for Edouard s scenarios
- 6 scenario cards that Edouard clearly marks as story driven not just kill zombies and loot
- A handful of tokens for loot traps and a mysterious guest mood dial that nudges difficulty as you play

### Component quality

The miniatures are cleanly sculpted and the production copy feels sturdy rather than flashy. The tiles are thick and resistant to the table hopping chaos that accompanies a good zombie charge. Edouard s scenarios are crafted to reward careful planning without turning into a spreadsheet Induced path calculus. The little details matter from survivor cards to the way loot tokens feel like hand me down gear from a well meaning mercenary union. The mood is more cinema than factory line, and that matters when you want to feel like a team with a mission rather than just a collection of dice throwers.

### The guest s imprint on art and flavor

Edouard s design taste is all about atmosphere. You will notice it in the scenario text where encounters hint at a backstory rather than a dry objective. The art direction leans into mood and the zombies showing personalities that say I would like to talk about this but I am also a zombie. This tone helps the cooperative experience feel like a caper rather than a slog. The hunt for loot remains a rhythm, but the loot itself feels earned rather than randomly dropped by a box that wants you to max out your kill count.

## How Edouard s mechanisms reshape the game s tempo

### Mechanisms you will notice
- A guest mode turn timer that keeps pace brisk without forcing reckless rampages
- Mood tokens that shift difficulty in small story driven nudges rather than blunt numeric changes
- Loot ladders that require specific actions to unlock, encouraging players to work together rather than all sprinting for a single big payoff
- Scenario arcs that stitch the six adventures into a narrative through line instead of six separate mini quests

### Pacing and risk: a delicate balance

One common critique of Zombicide is mood volatility when a single player latches onto a critical tool. Edouard s guest content solves this with scenario arcs that reward team planning and communication. Mood tokens create rising tension that makes failures feel like dramatic missteps rather than mission ending catastrophes. It s a design choice that harmonizes teamwork with the fear of a real time clock ticking away your options. You feel the need to discuss turn order before the wall of zombies becomes a ring of fire at your back.

### Survivor customization and synergy

The survivor cards in this edition lean into synergy. Some characters unlock special actions after achieving objectives, while others gain access to unique loot sets that push you to diversify your approach rather than stick to a cookie cutter formation. It stays faithful to classic Zombicide while weaving a richer cooperative tapestry. The synergy threads connect to a shared story rather than a series of isolated wins.

## Thematic depth: tone, story, and feel

### Story beats you will remember

Guest friendly scenarios feel like episodes of a zombie TV show rather than a grind through a game table. Consequences stack across sessions, nudging you toward a preferred playstyle without turning every path into a guaranteed win or a rng trap clap trap. You get a sense of progression rather than a one off objective sprint every time.

### Flavorful encounter design

Edouard s scenarios include set piece moments a passing the loot sequence where the team must physically transfer a package as zombies close in and a trap laden warehouse where line of sight becomes a story hazard as much as a tactical requirement. These are not flavor filler moments. They push you to coordinate actions under pressure and to talk through turn order with the energy of a high stakes escape room.

## Artwork and production values

The artistic imprint brings a shelf worthy aesthetic with a vibe that pairs well with a few well chosen sci fi or noir novels. It s not just eye candy; it s readability under pressure. Icons are clear card backs are consistent and tile design keeps orientation intact when the room heats up. Zombie bases feature varied poses that help differentiate threats at a glance a small quality of life touch that saves time and reduces table side confusion.

## Tactics and tips for surviving Edouard Guiton s edition

- Communicate early and often Mood tokens reward early information sharing. Don t wait until the last second before rotating a survivor to a new door
- Don t overcommit to one plan Flexibility matters because tokens flip the balance and you will need to pivot on a dime
- Use loot ladders strategically If you can unlock a high tier item for a critical moment do so when timing matters most even if it means a detour
- Scout corners with caution Some doors hide ambush variant tokens opening with one survivor and letting others support can be the difference between a clean sweep and a total mollusk situation

## How it stacks up against other Zombicide editions

Compared to the base game and other expansions this edition emphasizes narrative coherence and pacing. The additional tokens and arc based scenarios reduce the risk of the got to loot all the things tunnel vision that can plague a co op session. If you have played a lot of Zombicide and want more storytelling without sacrificing the core fight loot revive loop this edition scratches that itch very satisfyingly.

However if you are a pure numbers person who tracks odds and returns to the table solely for stat chasing damage math you might find the mood tokens and scenario arcs a touch fluffy. It is not a deal breaker just a design preference that tilts the game toward shared narrative rather than pure optimization. Still the balance feels purposeful and the fun factor remains high enough to justify a second or third playthrough in a month if your group is into recurring campaigns.

For veteran players the content adds enough nuance to justify revisiting the base game with a fresh coat of design luster The table presence is solid the miniatures hold their own on a mid sized play area and the tile art remains readable in dim ambient lighting a couple of sessions we ran ended up in a candle lit kitchen which was oddly atmospheric rather than chaotic.

## Replayability and longevity

Replaying Edouard s six scenarios is not simply running a checklist Mood tokens and guest mode create a dynamic that evolves with each playthrough especially if you mix and match scenarios The added survivor cards offer different starting conditions which means you can tailor your team s approach repeatedly without feeling like you are pulling the same lever over and over

A practical note the early game tends to be calmer and if you push too hard for loot you might pay later as zombies flood the board The strategic joy is watching that balance unfold and adjusting your plan mid session rather than waiting until the campaign ends

## Final verdict: should you buy this edition

If you are a Zombicide devotee who craves a touch more story a pinch less brute force chaos and a design sensibility that makes you feel like you are participating in a well choreographed zombie heist Edouard Guiton s special guest edition is a solid purchase. It is not a radical overhaul of the base game but rather a refinement that adds flavor pacing and narrative cohesion The new mechanics slot neatly into the existing framework providing fresh decisions without overheating the decision tree The risk reward curve remains satisfying the loot feels meaningful and the scenarios give your group a sense of progression rather than a buffet of isolated challenges
