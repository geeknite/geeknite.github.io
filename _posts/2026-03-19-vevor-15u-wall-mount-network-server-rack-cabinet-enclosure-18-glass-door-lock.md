### Setup Guide for New Users

If you are new to wall-mount racks, here is the quick, practical guide to get you from packaging to productive:

- Step 1: Pick a wall and locate studs. A stud finder is your friend here. You want solid anchors that won’t shy away from the burden of your hardware. If you live in a rental with questionable walls, consider a wall plate and anchors that are rated for the weight you plan to mount.
- Step 2: Plan your layout. Decide what goes on top, middle, and bottom. Heavier items go lower to maintain center of gravity, but you should also consider cable routing. Mark the mounting holes on the wall.
- Step 3: Attach the rails. Most racks come with adjustable rails to align with your device widths. Make sure they are level and secure. A wobbly rack is the antagonist in many IT tales.
- Step 4: Mount the cabinet to the wall. Start with the top screws to hold alignment, then insert the bottom screws. Check for plumb and level as you go. You want a stable frame before you place any devices inside.
- Step 5: Manage cables and devices. Install a patch panel, switch, firewall, NAS, and any additional devices. Route cables along the rear channels, and keep power and data separate when possible to minimize interference. Remember to leave some slack for future changes.
- Step 6: Close the glass door and lock. Give the door a test open and close to ensure the locking mechanism works smoothly. If the door drags or binds, check the alignment of the rails and the mounting screws.
- Step 7: Power and test. Plug in the devices and test connectivity. A few LED indicators becoming the choir of your lab is the moment you realize you have achieved IT adulthood.
