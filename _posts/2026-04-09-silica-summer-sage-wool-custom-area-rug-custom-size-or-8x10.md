## Durability and care
### Durability
Wool rugs are known for resilience, and Silica has put a lot of care into the weave. Expect the rug to resist everyday wear and tear and maintain its shape after daily traffic. The natural elasticity means fewer permanent creases, though you should rotate occasionally to ensure even wear across the surface.

### Cleaning and maintenance
Regular vacuuming is recommended, with the beater brush on a gentle setting or off depending on your vacuum. Treat spills promptly by blotting — never rub — to prevent fiber intrusion. For stubborn stains or deeper spills, a mild wool-safe cleaner with water can do the trick. Wool has natural stain resistance but not stain immunity, so treat it with care and a little grace.

### Care tips
- Vacuum weekly, with the wand or brush attachment rather than the beater bar for delicate wool.
- Rotate every 3-6 months to promote even wear.
- Use a rug pad to reduce movement and protect wood floors.
- Spot cleanse with mild soap and water for minor stains; test on an inconspicuous corner first.

## Sizing and placement tips
### Measuring your space
Before you commit to a size, measure your space two times; measure the wall-to-wall, the floor, and the furniture footprint. For the custom size, you should measure the exact area where you want the rug to live. The 8x10 is a good rule of thumb for most living rooms, but if your sofa is oversized or you have a reading nook that begs for a smaller rug, consider customizing.

### Underlay and padding
A good rug pad is essential for wool. It adds grip, prevents curling at the edges, and provides extra cushioning. For wood floors, a felt pad helps protect the finish. If you live in a high-traffic area, choose a denser pad to reduce wear and make cleaning easier.

### Room-by-room guidance
- Living room: A larger rug like 8x10 can anchor a seating arrangement and reduce noise in open-plan spaces.
- Bedroom: A smaller custom size that hits under the lower third of the bed adds a touch of luxury without crowding the room.
- Home office: A rug that sits under the desk and chair can improve acoustics and provide a comfortable foot rest.

## Maintenance myths and realities
### Myth: Wool rugs are high maintenance
Reality: With proper care, wool rugs can be the least high maintenance of all floor coverings. They resist static, repel pet hair better than synthetic options in many cases, and clean up nicely with the right cleaners. They also don’t shed as vigorously as some shaggy synthetics.

### Myth: You cannot place a wool rug in high traffic areas
Reality: If you select a dense weft and add a good pad, a wool rug can survive a busy living room with regular vacuuming and rotating.
