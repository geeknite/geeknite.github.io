## FAQ
### Is a lab-created diamond real?
Yes. Lab-created diamonds are real diamonds with the same chemical, physical, and optical properties as mined diamonds. The only difference is their origin. They’re produced in controlled environments, which often yields lower prices and more consistent quality.

### How are lab-created diamonds made?
Most lab-grown diamonds are produced using either high-pressure, high-temperature (HPHT) or chemical vapor deposition (CVD). HPHT simulates the natural geological process by applying intense pressure and heat, while CVD builds a diamond layer by layer from carbon-rich gases. Either method results in a stone that is chemically and physically identical to a mined diamond.

### Will the ring hold up to daily wear?
Yes, with proper care and a sturdy setting. Look for a setting with secure prongs and a comfortable inner channel if you’re active. A 2.0–2.4 mm band width is a practical balance between presence and wearability for daily life.

### Are lab-grown diamonds ethically produced?
Generally, lab-grown diamonds offer improved traceability and often lower environmental impact than traditional mining. However, ethics depend on the production facilities and supply chain, so it’s wise to ask for certifications and third-party verification when available.

### Does fluorescence impact price or beauty?
Fluorescence can influence price and appearance depending on the stone and lighting. A faint fluorescence (FN) is usually a minor factor in most environments and rarely affects the stone’s beauty in daylight. If you’re very flash-sensitive or under strong UV lighting, you may want to discuss fluorescence with the jeweler.

## The science behind the sparkle
### Growth methods: HPHT and CVD
Lab-grown diamonds begin as carbon arranged into a crystal lattice. HPHT applies intense pressure and heat to an initially small seed crystal, encouraging growth in all directions. CVD, on the other hand, uses chemical reactions to deposit carbon atoms onto a seed to build up a diamond layer by layer. Both processes yield stones with identical physical properties. The differences you’ll notice as a buyer typically show up in growth marks or minor color and clarity variations, which are often managed by stringent quality control and certification.

### How this affects color and clarity
Color grading in lab-grown diamonds tends to be in the same spectrum as mined ones. A 1.5 ct center stone in the G-H range makes for a near-colorless appearance that remains beautiful under most lighting conditions. Clarity grades of VS1-VS2 are common for stones in this size category, offering a clean appearance with minimal inclusions visible to the naked eye. The cut grade is what makes the stone come alive; an Excellent or Ideal cut ensures the facets work synergistically to maximize brightness and dispersion.
