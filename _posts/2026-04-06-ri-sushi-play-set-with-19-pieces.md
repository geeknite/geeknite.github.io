## Practical Tips for Parents and Playtime Routines
- Set a timer for 10–15 minutes of “restaurant service.” Short, focused sessions work better for younger kids and reduce the chance of fatigue.
- Create a simple storyline. Example: The chef is preparing a weekend special and must arrange the plate in a specific order before the guests arrive. This encourages narrative thinking and sequencing.
- Combine with a real meal: let the child help arrange a few real veggies or fruit slices on a kid-friendly plate. It blends real-world nutrition with imagination and keeps the table from turning into a chaotic food fight.
- Use storytelling prompts: What does each piece say about flavor, color, or texture? This can help with language development and sensory description skills.
