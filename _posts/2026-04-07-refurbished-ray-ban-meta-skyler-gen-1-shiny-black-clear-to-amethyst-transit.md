If you’re buying refurbished, ask your seller about the calibration checks they performed and whether a test AR session was part of the package. A little extra assurance goes a long way here.

## Battery life and charging: when you actually use it

Battery life on AR glasses is a tricky subject because it’s highly dependent on use-case. When you’re actively rendering overlays, mapping the environment, and using the camera for pass-through, you’re drawing on power in a way that is far from a simple Bluetooth earbud scenario. Our refurbished Skyler Gen 1 unit offered a decent endurance profile for casual daily use: a day of intermittent AR overlays, occasional calls, and a handful of quick-note captures seemed to land around 3.5–5 hours of active AR time. That’s not spectacular when you compare to true standalone devices with larger batteries, but it’s not unusable either if your day includes a couple of walk-and-work sessions or short commutes.

Charging is straightforward with USB-C. The refurb unit we tested came with a solid charging puck and a cable that didn’t fray after a few twists, which is a small but significant detail when you’re juggling a morning routine. Users who rely on continuous AR sessions may want to consider a backup battery solution or a quick-desktop recharge during a long day. The benefit of refurbished stock is you can often pick up a spare or two from the same seller without burning through budget awe—if you’re comfortable with the procurement risk and the potential for slightly different battery health across units.

The interplay between battery health and software optimization is real here. If you’re only using the glasses for occasional AR prompts, you’ll likely be happy with your endurance. If you’re a power-user who expects the device to be a constant companion (maps overlay while you bikes-and-hights etc.), you might want to temper expectations and plan recharge stops.

Use cases that aren’t ideal:

## Maintenance tips for long-term happiness

- Calibration: Run a quick calibration when you first set up and after updates. It helps anchors overlays to your real-world view so you don’t end up with a parallax nightmare.
- Cleaning: Clean the lenses with microfiber to avoid micro-scratches. Keep the frame clean to preserve the glossy finish; a little polish goes a long way.
- Battery expectations: If you’re planning a long day, consider scheduled recharges to maintain performance—don’t push the battery to the absolute limit if you can help it.
- Software care: Keep firmware updated, but if you encounter stability issues after an update, try a factory reset or reapply the latest patch from Ray-Ban Meta’s app to re-stabilize overlays.
