## The Set Mechanics in Practice
Zendikar Rising leans into a land-forward identity. Landfall, a classic Zendikar mechanic, triggers when a land enters the battlefield under your control. The returning theme creates a sense of exploration and risk, as you draft and play around terrain that interacts with your plan in interesting ways. It is the kind of mechanic that rewards careful sequencing: a well-timed land drop can unlock a cascade of value that multiplies as you add more lands to the battlefield.

Modal Double-Faced Cards (MDFCs) are a feature that introduces a subtle strategic layer to the drafting and deck-building process. Each MDFC offers two faces with different abilities, and you choose which face to play or reveal depending on the state of the game. The design encourages you to think about your deck as a dynamic system rather than a static list of cards. MDFCs can anchor a deck by providing two modes of value on a single card, or they can serve as flexible mana sources that adapt to the board.

One of the delightful consequences of this design is how it invites players to experiment with color pairings and tempo. You might find yourself building a deck that straddles two colors and uses MDFCs to cover angles of attack or defense that were not obvious at first glance. The set rewards players who practice patience and who value resource efficiency over brute force.
