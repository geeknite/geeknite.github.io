Audrey arrives in Zombicide as the survivor who treats the library as a field hospital and the book as a shield. - Bookish Tactics: Audrey’s abilities emphasize reading the situation, predicting zombie waves, and leveraging quiet spaces. In practical terms, she shines when you’re trying to secure a library, a bookstore, or any tile with shelves that can be repurposed into cover. This translates to a reasonable chance to influence spawn patterns and set up safe chits for your squad.
- Zone Control: Audrey’s kit includes a few actions that let you “claim” a shelf or wall as a defensive position. It’s not overpowered, but it’s flavorful and surprisingly functional in mid-game skirmishes. You’ll often find yourself using Audrey to funnel zombies toward a choke point where your team can use area-of-effect clears without spreading your group too thin.
- Card Flavor: The card text reads as if a librarian sprinkled in tactical notes between cataloging and Dewey Decimal jokes. Expect lines that imply misplacing zombies on the wrong shelf and a cheat code for “checking out” the last breath of the undead. It’s silly in the best, most endearing way.

Audrey’s narrative arc in-game feels like a nod to the classic “underdog scholar outsmarts the chaos” trope. Her synergy with other survivors is decent, especially with those who can capitalize on precise positioning and controlled, small-area clears. If you like the idea of a quieter, more cerebral playstyle that still delivers satisfying zone control, Audrey is your person.
