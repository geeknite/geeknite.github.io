### Maintenance: keep the tin and cards happy
A vintage UNO tin will appreciate a little care. Here are some practical tips to keep the set in good shape:
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent card colors from fading.
- Clean the tray with a soft, dry cloth to remove dust without scratching the surface.
- If the cards show any war-torn edges, consider a light card-shaping with a gentle card shuffler; overzealous shuffling can wear down corners, but careful shuffles preserve the deck’s longevity.
- Don’t force the lid; if the hinges or the lid latch feels stiff, a tiny amount of graphite (pencil lead) on the hinge area can ease movement—gently, please.

If you’re a collector, consider a display case that fits the tin like a glove. The aesthetic isn’t just about looking nice; it’s about preserving a memory in a way that future generations can flip through and feel that same spark you did when you first “UNO!”ed with friends and family.
