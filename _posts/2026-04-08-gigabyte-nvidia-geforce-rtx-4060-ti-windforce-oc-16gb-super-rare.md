### Cooling and acoustics

The WindForce OC is all about keeping things cool while you binge. The three fans move serious air through a multi-section heatsink, designed to keep the hotter components around the GPU die from becoming a tiny volcanic island of heat. In testing, idle noise stays low and fans ramp up with a satisfyingly quiet whoosh under load. Under sustained gaming, the audible signature is more of a distant whisper than a brake-bleeding scream, which is exactly what you want when you are trying to hear your character shouting yolo during a boss fight.

### Power delivery and thermals

This variant uses a single 8-pin PCIe power connector, which is typical for a card in this performance segment. The PCIe slot on the motherboard remains the bottleneck less often than your old 4:00 a.m. coffee habit. Thermals stay in check thanks to the WindForce OC design; the arms-length heatpipes and the fin array keep the thermals from becoming villainous. If you like to push overclocking, you should keep a close eye on the power envelope and your chassis airflow, because even a well-cooled card can suffer if your case has the airflow of a closet.
