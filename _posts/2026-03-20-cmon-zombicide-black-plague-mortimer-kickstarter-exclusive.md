## The Practicalities: Assembly, Base, and Table Presence
If you insist on the practical side, here is the recommended process for integrating Mortimer into your Zombicide Black Plague table:

- Inspect the miniature for any flash or loose sprue remains. Remove with a hobby knife and a fine file if necessary. Clean edges improve paint adhesion and prevent snags during handling.
- Decide whether to mount on a standard 25 mm or 32 mm base, depending on your preferred level of table presence. Mortimer's stance often benefits from a slightly larger base to prevent the silhouette from looking overwhelmed by taller zombies on the board.
- Prime with a neutral spray to ensure paint adheres correctly. A light gray or brown primer works well as a neutral canvas for grimy tones.
- Paint with emphasis on contrast. In a table full of earth-toned grays and battle-wattered armor, high-contrast edge highlights on the cape and the weapon can dramatically improve readability at distance.
- Seal with a matte varnish to protect the paintwork during weeks of game nights and casual snacking during long campaigns. You will thank yourself for the longevity and the ease of cleaning the figure after the final battle of a scenario that felt like a marathon.

Table presence matters in a game like Zombicide. A well-painted Mortimer draws the eye and invites admiration from table mates.
