## Rules cheat sheet: the gist in under 250 words
- Objective: be the first player to empty your stock pile by playing numbered sequences in ascending order.
- Play: on your turn, you draw to reach a playable hand; you can place cards on the build piles in the center if they fit the sequence. If you play a series or single card, you may get to draw again depending on rules variations you agreed upon before starting.
- Skip-Bo wilds and blocking strategies: skip-bo cards function as wilds and can fill in gaps, depending on the official micro rules included in your edition. It\'s the kind of twist that makes you feel clever and means you occasionally blame the tiny deck for villainy when the plan falls apart.

## Tips and tricks: maximizing tiny glory
- Use a shallow tray or shallow dish to keep the micro deck in order. Tiny cards have a habit of wandering off like mischievous sprites, and a magnetic board or a shallow dish can keep them in line.
- Pre-cut a small play area with a ruler or a thin tape line to define your center build piles. You will thank yourself when you don\'t have to spend five seconds looking for a single card that rolled under the sofa.
- The wild Skip-Bo cards are your late-game heroes and sometimes your panic-inducing maniacs. Don\'t be afraid to deploy them in a way that seals the round, but don\'t reveal your entire strategy before the last turn—let suspense be your micro edge.
- If you are teaching kids, swap in a short reward for a good move. Tiny games are about momentum and confidence as much as they are about cards.
- Photograph your tiny wins. The moment when you manage a perfect sequence on a tiny stage can translate nicely into a loop video that your friends will either adore or mock with good-humored sincerity.
