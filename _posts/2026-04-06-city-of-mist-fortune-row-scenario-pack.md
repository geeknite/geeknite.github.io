## Overview

### What Fortune Row Is
Fortune Row is City of Mist's answer to the question: what does a street-level myth look like when the coffee is bad and the neon never sleeps? It is a scenario pack meant to slot into your City of Mist campaign as a multi-part arc or run as a series of one-shots with varying tones—think heist thriller meets urban fantasy with a dash of boutique noir. The design intent is clear: give you a handful of episodes that feel distinct but thread together through a shared street name, a set of recurring NPCs, and the ever-present possibility that luck, lucks darker cousin, will tilt the scales.

### What You Get

Fortune Row ships with a curated set of tools for both GM and players. The package includes:
- Four linked adventures that can be run one after another or extracted for stand-alone sessions.
- A refreshed pool of investigator-templates, each designed to slot into the Fortunes Row arc without feeling forced into a specific mythos.
- Handouts for players, including cryptic notes, wish-lists, and a few puzzle pieces that require collaborative problem-solving rather than solo heroics.
- GM aids: pacing tips, mood-setting prompts, and a few fortune cards that add a layer of fate and consequence to key choices.
- A fold-out map or map-like asset to help ground the action in the citys geography (or in your head, if you prefer to run with the lights off and a playlist on).

Below, a quick note on the contents, not a shopping list but a culinary guide. If you’ve got the City of Mist core book, Fortune Row slots neatly into the existing tags-and-mythos framework, expanding what your players can do with a minimum of train-wreck-level prep.

### Why it Works for City of Mist

City of Mist is built on a core idea: myth and reality overlap as players narrate the world through a lens of legends and personal identities. Fortune Row doubles down on that by weaving mythic propositions into everyday settings—a broken elevator becomes the threshold to a divine bargain; a coffee shop can be the front for a pantheon’s petty feuding; a debt collector might be a herald of a fallen deity. The writing is crisp, with a GM-facing style that promises you can run it with a weekend's prep or a late-night sprint between chores.

## Narrative Design and Tone

Fortune Row is at its best when it leans into mood over mechanical complexity. The scenarios are set in a city that feels alive yet bruised; it assumes players will improvise and that the GM will lean into the unpredictability of a world where people have both mortal troubles and mythic legacies.

### Thematic Consistency

The pack uses a consistent color palette of urban myth: neon signs, rain, haunted billboards, and a grid of mundane tasks that become ladders into larger questions about power and luck. The tone is witty, self-aware, with a sense of humor that fits the Geeknite vibe: clever lines, pop-culture allusions without getting too precious about them.

### Mechanics and System Notes

Here we are in a love-letter to City of Mists tag-based system. Fortune Row respects the core momentum: your investigators mythic identity is still a resource, not a prison; your Hope is real, even if it costs a little something later on. The pack introduces a handful of new tags and a small set of fortune-moment triggers to layer into scenes. Expect some additional GM guidance on running multi-episode arcs, including advice on pacing reveals and letting players co-create the mythic texture of the city.

## Play Experience

Running Fortune Row is a special kind of tabletop theatre. The sessions feel cinematic but not in a way that requires a cinema budget. The scenarios provide a structured skeleton, yet they encourage improvisation as players uncover clues that tie back to their identities and the mythical undercurrent of the city.

### Episode Structure

Each episode typically begins with a hook—a public spectacle, a whispered rumor, or a suspiciously convenient coincidence. The investigators then pursue a trace that leads to a decision point where the fortune theme enters: do you align with luck, cheat fate, or attempt to rewrite it? The choices feel meaningful; the consequences are not always immediate but often resonant two sessions later.

### NPCs and Factions

Fortune Row populates the city with a few recurring factions and memorable NPCs. Some are morally ambiguous; some are corrupted by power; a few are just tired and want a nap in peace. The pack does a good job of giving you vivid character hooks so you can spin scenes quickly even if you didn’t write a novel about each NPC.

### Player Experience

Players will appreciate how Fortune Row respects their character's mythic identity while forcing them to reckon with the consequences of their choices. In some moments, you’ll see players lean into dramatic reveals; in others, they’ll opt for quiet, character-driven scenes that push the story forward in subtle but satisfying ways.

## How to Run Fortune Row

### Preparation Time

Fortune Row is designed to minimize prep, which is exactly what many busy GMs want. If you’ve run City of Mist before, you’ll recognize the flow: identify the core mythos for the session, map the clues to the investigators identities, and let the players improvise. A couple of hours of quick prep can yield a satisfying arc. If you’re new to the system, you can lean on the GM tips and the built-in prompts to find your footing.

### Session Flow

Fortune Row works well as a multi-session arc or a set of stand-alone adventures. The recommended approach is to start with the Hook, then move into Investigation, followed by a Confrontation, and finally a Denouement that ties up the arc and leaves room for a follow-up if your players want to continue.

### GM Tips
- Let players define mythic implications; ask, what does your legacy mean in a world where myth can be bought or borrowed?
- Use fortune-moment triggers sparingly but intentionally; they should feel consequential, not random.
- Lean into urban texture: music, ambient sound cues, and a couple of props can ground scenes in the street-level mood.
- Recognize when a scene is stalling and pivot to a new angle: a different location, a new NPC, or a sudden shift in allegiance.

### Accessibility

The pack is fairly accessible in terms of language and structure. It doesn’t require you to memorize a huge codex; it gives you a script you can improvise from. It also avoids overly dark content that might alienate players who enjoy a lighter approach, while still delivering thematic depth.

## Comparisons with Other City of Mist Products

Fortune Row sits in between the core rulebook's structural clarity and the more elaborate expansions that push the city’s scale. It’s not as sprawling as some of the larger campaigns, but it isn’t a bare-bones two-session module either. If you’re deciding between Fortune Row and other scenario packs, consider what you want from your city: do you crave a long, fog-drenched arc, or do you want a set of discrete, highly-encoded encounters that you can weave into any ongoing campaign with minimal fuss? Fortune Row gives you both the thematic density and modular structure.
