## Leftovers, Storage, and Reheating Realities

Let us be practical for a moment. Sushi is best enjoyed fresh, but leftovers can be a blessing if handled sensibly. The two-box set can become your next day hero when you implement simple steps: keep it chilled promptly, keep the soy sauce packet away from the rice in a separate compartment to prevent sogginess, and consider a quick refreshing drizzle of citrus or a light sesame dressing to reinvigorate any piece that tastes a touch tired. Some pieces can be revived with a very brief toast in a dry skillet to reacquire a hint of warmth and aroma, though you must be cautious not to overdo the warmth and turn sushi into a fried rice experiment that never gets asked for at the table.

Leftovers are not a save-all; they are a utility.
