## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance Routine

Maintenance becomes a lot more straightforward when you adopt a routine that you can actually keep. Here is a practical maintenance plan that helps you keep the Bahama Classic looking fresh without turning your weekend into a DIY marathon:

- Weekly: vacuum or sweep away debris to prevent grit from grinding into the fibers.
- Monthly: perform a light spot-clean if you notice stains or spills. Use a mild soap solution and a soft-bristle brush; avoid harsh chemicals.
- Seasonally: perform a thorough check for fading, fraying, or wear along the edges. If you notice issues, address them before they worsen.
- After heavy rain or muddy events: hose off the rug and allow it to dry completely in a shaded area before placing it back on the floor.
- Storage: if you plan to store it for winter or a long period, clean it first, roll it up, and store in a dry, cool place. Do not fold the rug to avoid creases that may become permanent.

Be mindful of the underside. If the rug is on a wood deck, ensure air circulation so moisture doesn’t become trapped. A rug pad designed for outdoor use adds grip and reduces wear on both rug and deck.
