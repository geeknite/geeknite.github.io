## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Glow Alive

Maintenance is where rug owners become part-time meteorologists predicting the next stain pattern. For this rug, the recommended routine is simple:

- Vacuum on a low setting at least once a week to prevent dirt from embedding in the pile. 
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth, then clean with a mild detergent if needed. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could alter the color balance or the metallic threads’ luster.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and avoid high-traffic patterns from becoming too pronounced.
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and to reduce wear on the backing. A pad improves comfort and helps preserve the rug’s shape over time.

This is the kind of care regimen that fits into a busy modern life. You don’t need a full-time textile technician to handle it; you need a good vacuum, a damp cloth, and a couple of minutes of routine time on a Sunday afternoon. If you’re the type who forgets to rotate or who treats their rug like a living creature that needs attention, consider setting calendar reminders. Your future self will thank you when the rug still looks chic and the living room doesn’t resemble a chalkboard of random wear patterns.
