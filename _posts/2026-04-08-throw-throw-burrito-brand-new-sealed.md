## Strategy, House Rules, and Practical Tips
Here’s where we shift from “unboxing hype” into “real-life play guidance.” If you want to maximize your fun without sacrificing too much personal space, consider the following tips.

- Communication is key. This is a game where trash talk can be as effective as a dodge, but you need to read the table. If you have a friend who’s laser-focused on precise play, you might want to encourage a more relaxed style for the night. The social facet is the engine of this game.
- Positioning matters. Don’t cluster in a single corner; the best chaos happens when players have a bit of space to react and bounce burritos unpredictably. You’ll want a room with clear floor space, soft furnishings, and the least risk of furniture suffering collateral damage.
- Manage your own “hit” tokens. If your table uses a token-tracking system, you want to balance risk with defense. Don’t hoard your tokens, but don’t dump them all at once to prove some dramatic show. The humor is in governance, not in stampeding the balance.
- Use house rules to tailor the chaos. For example, you can designate a “no-throw” zone to keep the game safe around kids or pets. You can also introduce a “bonus round” where a lucky player gets to choose a card with a special effect that’s silly but relatively harmless.
- Thematic alignment. If your group loves nerdy crossovers, you can weave in references to your favorite fantasy or sci-fi worlds. The burritos become a running gag—imagine a “Drogor Burrito” and a “Pulse Fryer” duel for the ultimate snack-toss victory.

These tips aren’t about breaking the rules; they’re about bending the room’s energy into something that feels uniquely yours.
