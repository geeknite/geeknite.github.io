## The Miniatures: Chauncey and Beauregard

### Chauncey: the Debonair Doombringer
Chauncey is the kind of survivor you invite to your victory party and then promptly regret because he insists on narrating every zombie encounter in a dramatic monologue. The sculpt captures a perfectly over-the-top aristocrat vibe: tailored coat, monocle, a cane that doubles as a blunt instrument, and a hat that says I am too fashionable to die, but not too stupid to survive. On the tabletop, Chauncey brings a set of abilities that revolve around control and disruption. He has a flair for manipulating zombie movements, forcing undead lines to collide with each other, and creating crowd-control opportunities that would make even a battle mage jealous. His sheet is not just a power list; it reads like a short story in which Chauncey becomes the protagonist of your own chaotic survival scenario.

### Beauregard: the Brute With Brains and Baritone
Beauregard is the counterpoint to Chauncey, a hulking, loud, and lovable powerhouse who pairs brute force with a surprisingly strategic edge. The sculpt conveys a sense of towering confidence, with an oversized weapon, a smirk that implies he knows more than he lets on, and a presence on the board that often demands the attention of more than a couple of zombies at a time. Beauregard shifts the balance toward aggressive tactics: more actions in the heat of battle, more opportunities to breach zombie lines, and a knack for soaking up damage that would derail a less sturdy recruit. The pairing of Chauncey and Beauregard feels like a duo from a buddy cop movie replaced with knights of the living dead and a dash of high society sarcasm.

### The dynamic duo on the table
The two minis are designed to play off each other, not just as a duo but as a narrative engine for your game night. Chauncey and Beauregard create opportunities for synergy where Chauncey controls the crowd and Beauregard breaks through it. The sculpt quality is detailed enough to please the painting crowd but robust enough to survive a table tumble during a particularly tense moment of the session. The paint schemes lean toward opulent color palettes with metallic accents, which helps them pop on the gaming table against a sea of grays and earthy zombie tones. If you enjoy painting miniatures, these are satisfying but not impossible to bring to a showpiece level; they are characterful without requiring you to become a professional mini painter.

## How the Duo Changes Gameplay

### Chauncey the Controller
Chauncey excels at manipulating the flow of the board. His abilities revolve around bamboozling the undead with crowd control in ways that feel both cinematic and practical. Expect options to shard zombie density, pull threats into chokepoints, and set up ideal bomb-lane opportunities for Beauregard to smash through. In practice, Chauncey lets you plan a turn with a cinematic beat, then execute the beat with surgical precision. It is a rare moment when the weight of strategy lines up with the drama of the moment, and it feels good every single time you pull it off.

### Beauregard the Breaker
Beauregard is all about converting Chauncey’s setups into progress. His mechanics reward you for aggressive plays, with additional actions or enhanced attack efficiency when he pushes through crowds. The synergy is something like a buddy comedy crossed with a tactical skirmish: Chauncey creates the setup, Beauregard delivers the punchline and the punch. The practical upshot is more momentum in the mid game and better odds of breaking through a stubborn siege using a mix of brute force and well-timed distractions.

### Scenarios and replayability
The included exclusive scenario ties the two of them into a narrative thread that encourages players to revisit this content in future sessions. The scenario design leans into the theme that survival may require a certain degree of arrogance plus teamwork. We find that these new interactions scale well from small squad games to mid-size campaigns. The level progression of this duo can feel satisfying without forcing you into a heavy campaign mode if you prefer short sessions. If you enjoy long campaigns, the duo remains a reliable engine for a string of interconnected missions that escalate both in danger and in the opportunity to nudge your table toward chaotic spectacle.

### Difficulty and balance considerations
As with many Kickstarter extras, the question is always about balance. Chauncey adds a lot of control, which can make some scenarios feel friendly to players who enjoy planning. Beauregard adds raw power, which can tilt the board toward more aggressive outcomes. The key to balance in practice is to read the room: if your table loves planful play, Chauncey can shine as a strategic anchor; if your group values big moments and heroic saves, Beauregard can be the star that steals the scene. In both cases, the duo requires you to adapt your team composition and your approach to mission pacing. In short, they are not a one trick pony: they offer multiple ways to engage the game depending on your group’s preferences.
