## Performance: Limited play and Constructed viability
Liliana's Scorn shines brightest when you can connect the right timing with your game plan. Let’s break it down into two lanes: Limited and Constructed.

### Limited (Draft / Sealed) implications
In Limited environments, the efficiency of a spell often equals its flexibility and the density of playable targets on both sides of the board. Liliana's Scorn brings a few important advantages here:
- Price of entry: The mana value is accessible enough for early game control without stalling your development.
- Versatility: If you are racing a faster deck, having a removal spell that can answer a specific threat without over-committing is gold. If your opponent is slow-rolling bigger threats, the Scorn can be the tempo swing you need to push for damage or to steady the board as you transition to mid-game threats.
- Flexibility: In many Limited queues, you’ll find that removing a mid-curve target keeps you from being forced into awkward blocks or trades. Liliana's Scorn helps you avoid the all-too-common situation where you spend a card to remove a small threat, only to regret not having something more substantial to answer a later, bigger bomb.

Deckbuilding note for Limited players: lean into curving your mana so that you can cast Liliana's Scorn on turn 3 or turn 4, depending on the format's speed. It pairs with a black leaner that wants to trade resources carefully, and in sideboard games you can use it to answer a variety of problems without overextending your own plan.

### Constructed viability
In Constructed formats, Liliana's Scorn does not appear in every black deck, but it has a niche where it truly shines:
- Tempo control: In midrange or tempo-focused black decks, Scorn helps you maintain pressure while keeping your own mana curve healthy.
- Flexibility with removal suites: It slots into a suite of removal options where you want a mix of speed and efficiency. If your deck features a couple of bigger threats, Scorn provides the right number of answers without overloading your deck with removal spells that clog late game.
- Synergy with other black staples: The card often benefits from other black résumés that punish card advantage or force your opponent to waste resources, enabling your own board to stabilize and take over.

However, the card is not a universal fix-all. If you are playing against a tribal shell or a deck that relies on a single emblem-level threat, Scorn might feel underwhelming due to its limited scope. The key to success is to run it as part of a cohesive plan rather than as a stand-alone fix.

## How to use Liliana's Scorn effectively
When you pull out Liliana's Scorn in a game, you should think in terms of tempo and value. Here are some practical tips to get the most out of this card:
- Identify the right target: Early on, forcing trades on 2-for-1 tokens or small creatures is a safe play. In the mid-to-late game, you can use Scorn to clear a path around bigger threats that are threatening to swing the board heavily.
- Pair with pressure: If you have a plan that involves constant pressure or inevitability, Scorn helps you maintain the edge. Don’t waste it on a threat you can’t profitably remove without losing tempo elsewhere.
- Time your plays with your curve: Don’t stall your mana too much just to cast Scorn; if you pass a turn with undeveloped board presence because you want to hold Scorn for a larger pay-off, you might give your opponent the window they need to take control.
- Remember your role in the match: Is your deck trying to race, or is it trying to outlast? Liliana's Scorn can be a bridge between the two approaches if used honestly and with a little dash of cunning.

### Matchups and game plans
- Versus aggro: Expect to use Scorn as a reactionary tool to keep the opposing board manageable while you stabilize behind life totals and card advantage. You don’t want to waste this removal on a blocker with a plan—save it for the bigger clear or the most troublesome threat.
- Versus midrange: The card becomes a tempo engine, letting you shave away a midrange threat and continue building your own clock. You want to be careful about over-committing, though; midrange decks sometimes have inevitability built in, and you don’t want to give them the exact tempo swing they need.
- Versus control: In control-heavy matches, Scorn can be an efficient way to poke at a late-game plan without inflating your own risk. It is less dramatic here but can still be an essential piece of resource management when other removal is taxed.
