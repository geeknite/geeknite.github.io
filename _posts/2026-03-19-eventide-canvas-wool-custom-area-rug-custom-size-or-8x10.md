## Durability and maintenance: The practical care playbook
Durability in a wool rug is often about resilience to everyday life and responsible maintenance that keeps it looking fresh for years. Here’s how Eventide’s canvas-wool duo stacks up in real-world usage.

- **Wear resistance:** Wool fibers are naturally resilient and can bounce back from foot traffic. A well-made weave reduces the risk of crushing under heavy furniture and keeps the surface looking evenly plush even in high-traffic areas.
- **Stain resistance and cleaning:** Wool is relatively stain-resistant, but not miracle-proof. Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth to prevent staining. For most common spills (coffee, red wine, tomato sauce), a mild detergent solution or a wool-safe cleaner works well. Avoid harsh solvents that can damage the fibers or fade the color. Always test a small hidden area first.
- **Shedding and break-in period:** New wool rugs can shed a bit. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps remove loose fibers without pulling at the pile. After the first few months, shedding typically decreases significantly as the fibers settle in.
- **Rotation:** To maintain even wear, rotate the rug every few months, especially if one side faces heavier foot traffic or sun exposure that can cause uneven fading.

Care advice from the pros often includes a simple routine: vacuum weekly, spot-clean promptly, and schedule a professional cleaning every 12–24 months depending on usage. If you have pets, consider a wool rug pad to reduce staining risk and extend the life of the backing.

### Caring for wool in a modern home
- Use a gentle, pH-balanced detergent designed for wool.
- Avoid bleach or ammonia-based cleaners, which can degrade the fibers.
- Blot rather than rub to prevent fiber fraying.
- Keep away from direct sunlight if possible to prevent fading; if sun is unavoidable, rotate or use window treatments to minimize exposure.
