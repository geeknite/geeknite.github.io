## Sorting, Storing, and Setup Tips
Owning six booster boxes means you’ll be sorting and storing for a while. Here are practical ideas to keep the process from turning into a chaotic card avalanche.

### Quick-start sorting approach
- Separate by rarity first: commons, uncommons, rares, mythics, foils. This gives you a quick map of what you’ve got and what you might want to trade.
- Within rarity, color-code and align by set. This makes future pulls easier to evaluate for draft and trade potential.
- Create a “bulk” bucket for commons and less-in-demand uncommons. You’ll come back to this later when you’re hustling in trades.

### Storage solutions
- Resealing and stacking: invest in a few card-storage boxes with dividers. It makes the ecosystem of your collection feel like a tiny sports archive rather than a random jumble of cardboard. Label by set and year so you don’t forget which card came from which universe.
- Sleeving strategy: sleeves are your friend here. A bulk haul benefits from basic protective sleeves on the entire stack, especially for rares and foils you intend to trade or play with. It reduces edge wear and helps preserve clarity for foils—because nothing screams mid-life crisis like a wilted foil with a smudged holo stamp.
- Binding and display: if you’re into collectors’ vibes, consider a simple binder or a display wakening your inner curator. Even a few well-labeled binders can turn a project into a sustainable hobby rather than a garage-scale loot operation.

### Handling foils and misprints
Foils are the unicorns of bulk. They pop up occasionally, and when they do, your day gets a little brighter. Misprints are rarer, but they add an extra layer of charm for the obsessive cataloger in you. If you’re serious about grading, a subset of your six-box haul could become the core of a future investment, but remember that MTG cardboard often forgives the casual collector more than the competitive grader expects.
