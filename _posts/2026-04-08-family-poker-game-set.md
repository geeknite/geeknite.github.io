### Practical Tips for Using This Set at Home
- Establish a simple rhythm: small blind, big blind, deal, flop, turn, river, showdown. Use a timer if needed to keep pace; kids respond to predictable cycles.
- Use the cheat sheet as a teaching tool, not a crutch. Encourage players to verbalize why a hand is strong or weak rather than just following the card values.
- Keep snacks nearby but not on the table. A snack-savvy player tends to forget their chips, and a spilt drink on a deck is the fastest way to end a game night in tragedy and laughter.
- Celebrate learning moments. If a kid learns a concept like fold equity or hand rankings, highlight the moment with a quick shout-out so they feel clever and capable.
- Rotate the dealer role. A fair rotation reduces tension and gives everyone a chance to be in the hot seat, especially if they want to learn table management.
- Use house rules for gentle enforcement. If someone attempts a board wipe of chips across the table in frustration, remind them that we call that a ‘poker rainstorm’ and it’s not how we handle emotions.
