## How to Play
If you’re familiar with classic UNO, you’ll recognize the core rhythm: players take turns playing cards that match color or number, with special cards introducing draw penalties, skips, or reversals. The All Wild Card version tweaks this flow to be friendlier for larger groups while injecting new twist cards that offer both advantage and mischief.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck(s) thoroughly. A good rule of thumb is to give the deck a proper, ritualistic slap-skim to wake it from its cardboard dreams.
- Deal a hand of cards to each player. In large groups, you’ll likely deal eight to ten cards per person, depending on the exact variant you’re running.
- Place the top card of the draw pile face up to start the discard pile. If a Wild card appears, that’s your cue for the first dramatic turn of the night.

### Turn structure
On your turn, you may play a card that matches either the color or the number of the top discard. If you cannot play, you draw a card and your turn ends. The All Wild edition adds a couple of flavor choices for your turn, including the option to trigger a group action that can affect several players at once. The exact wording in your rulebook is important, but the gist is straightforward: chaos remains your friend, but chaos with rules is better chaos.

### Special cards
- Wild cards: The classic escape hatch. You can choose the color to continue play, which can dramatically tilt the balance late in the round.
- Wild Draw Four: The iconic chaos engine. In a large group, it becomes a social weapon with consequences that ripple through the table.
- Group action cards: These are the edition’s signature twist. They can force everyone to swap hands, skip a player, or reset the color. The effect can be mild or manic, depending on when you deploy it and who’s on the receiving end.

Remember, the exact text of each card matters for the precise effect. The rulebook is your friend here, especially when you’re trying to explain to a roomful of friends why a Group Swap isn’t just a party trick but a strategic element that can reverse a round’s fortunes in a dramatic fashion.

## Strategy and Tips
Here are some practical tips for getting the most out of Uno All Wild Card for large groups, without turning your living room into a tense strategy bunker.

### Beginners tips
- Watch the flow: In a large group, it’s easy to lose track of what cards remain in play. Keep an eye on color distribution and try to anticipate who might be sitting on a strong hand.

- Save Wild Draw Fours for late rounds when a single draw could swing the balance. It’s better to invest in late-game disruption than to waste a big move early when your table is still learning the scoring rhythm.
