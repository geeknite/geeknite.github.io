## Care and Maintenance
### Keeping Your Decks Fresh
Two decks mean twice the responsibility, but also twice the joy. Here are practical tips to keep your blue Rider Backs in prime condition:
- Store upright in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading.
- Avoid jamming them into tight spaces; they’re not a cheese grater, but they do have delicate edges that don’t appreciate being bruised by the corner of a box in your backpack.
- Use a card case for trips to the park or a friend’s apartment; the case minimizes edge wear and keeps them from mingling with stray gum and lost chapsticks.
- Avoid moisture and humidity—cards hate water almost as much as your laptop hates coffee, and that is an existential commentary we won’t explore here.

### Cleaning and Maintenance Routines
Light dusting with a soft brush or cloth is fine. If you notice ink rubbing off after a heavy practice session, it might be that you’ve got a particularly enthusiastic deck, but that would be unusual. In most cases, you’ll not see significant color transfer during normal use. If you’re a perfectionist, you can run a gentle cleaning method: wipe the surface with a barely damp cloth and let it dry completely before the next shuffle. This is the kind of mild maintenance a responsible card enthusiast performs after a particularly dramatic flourish.
