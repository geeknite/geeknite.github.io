## What the Expansion Packs Into the Herd
### What’s inside
The box copy promises more of what you love about Herd Mentality, plus a handful of new twists that are tailor-made for people who enjoy a little extra spice with their conformity. Inside you'll typically find:
- A set of new Prompt Cards that push boundaries in ways you’ll either celebrate or regret.
- A batch of New Response Cards designed to tease out different social dynamics.
- Special Mechanic Cards that introduce temporary rules or game modes mid-session.
- A small booklet clarifying how to weave the expansion with the base game without needing a PhD in sociolinguistics.
- Additional scorekeeping tokens and a slightly redesigned player aid that mentions memes and inside jokes—perfect for adding a few wink-wink moments to the table.

The card art keeps the same playful aesthetic as the base game, which is a relief for anyone worried about a sudden tonal shift from cheeky chaos to “serious boardroom vibes.” The creators understand their audience: you want humor that lands, not a glossy coexistence of sarcasm and design that looks like it belongs in a corporate wellness brochure.

### New mechanics and how they feel at the table
The expansion introduces a handful of mechanical shuffles that don’t reinvent the wheel, but they do alter the wheel’s spin. Expect a few new twists:
- The Herd Mode toggle: an optional rule that nudges players to align or intentionally misalign with the majority for bonus points or social penalties. It’s basically “What would the herd do?” but with the option to throw the herd into a tailspin for a dramatic finish.
- Dare cards that require players to perform quick challenges or pose outrageous questions. They inject active participation rather than passive guessing.
- Meme-style prompt cards that invite players to lean into contemporary internet culture rather than merely producing clever textual responses. If you’re into memes, you’ll feel right at home.
- Random event cards that can shift the objective mid-game, creating a mini-crisis of “do we pivot or endure?” that’s perfect for chaotic group dynamics.

All of these mechanics sit atop the base rules rather than replacing them. The expansion respects the flow of the original, which helps with adoption—no one wants to memorize a new set of rules that requires a student loan to understand.

## How setup and play change with the expansion
### Setup basics with the new bits
Setting up Herd Mentality Expansion Pack is largely the same as the base game with a few additional steps:
- Shuffle the new Prompt and New Response cards into their respective decks. The game’s AI assistant in your brain will tell you this is a routine task; your group will tell you it’s a chaotic but lovable one.
- Place the special mechanic token within reach for mid-game triggers. You won’t use this on every round, but when it hits, you’ll know it.
- Keep the score track visible, with the same token system. The expansion’s additional round types can stretch a session, so you may want to set a timebox or a flexible victory condition.

### Turn structure with expansion rules
A typical round goes like this with the expansion in tow:
1) A Prompt Card is revealed. Players write their responses privately.
2) Responses are read aloud, and players vote as usual for the one that best represents the crowd sentiment.
3) If a New Mechanical Card is in play, a mid-round event can alter the voting dynamic, such as rotating which players are allowed to vote, or introducing a “choose-your-own-adventure” style prompt.
4) In the optional Herd Mode, players align or resist the majority outcome for points, adding an extra strategic layer rather than pure comedy.
5) Score is tallied and the next player starts the new round.

The key difference is that rounds don’t just rely on the original prompt-and-vote dynamic; they sometimes require players to perform a light task or to pivot based on a mid-round event. The pacing stays brisk, which is essential for party games—the longer it takes to resolve a single moment, the more likely the table will drift away to other conversations, memes, and snack preferences.

The physical components hold up well. The cards are sturdy and easy to shuffle, and the little mechanic tokens feel satisfying in hand. If you’re worried about wear and tear from heavy-handed players or accidental spills, you’ll be happy to know the card stock is robust enough to survive a few table-slams and enthusiastic high-fives after a particularly clever answer.

## Strategy and tips: getting the most from your herd
### General tips
- Read the room before playing the new mechanic cards. If your group is more comfortable with low-key jokes, you’ll want to lean into the prompts rather than the dare cards.
- Use the memory trick: the best way to maximize points is to predict what the majority will pick, but occasionally taking a bold, unexpected stance with a well-timed joke can swing the round in your favor.
- Don’t be afraid to “lose with style.” If your response is genuinely silly and someone else’s is slightly more reasonable, the humor can outshine the points. In party games, vibe often wins the day.

### Adapting to different player counts
The expansion scales reasonably well with different group sizes. In smaller games (4-5 players), you’ll feel the impact of mid-round events more acutely, which can speed up rounds but also intensify the social pressure. In larger groups (6-8+), the herd dynamics become more pronounced, and the laugh density rises. The expansion’s extra rotating elements can mitigate some of the chaos by introducing structured moments within a sprawling party.

### Common pitfalls and how to avoid them
- Overthinking prompts: the expansion rewards quick, witty responses more than perfectly crafted essays. If you stall, you steal the fun from others and you risk a dull round.
- Domination by one player: sometimes a single clever maverick will hog the spotlight. Try to coax quieter players into the spotlight with well-timed prompts or by saving your funniest responses for when they’re most likely to be heard.
- Inconsistent tone: some prompts land as wholesome jokes; others slide into edgy territory. Know your table and skip prompts that might push people outside comfort zones unless you have explicit consent.
