## Care, storage, and preservation: how to keep a shielded time capsule healthy
The practical wisdom for sealed items is simple: keep it away from direct sunlight, maintain a stable humidity level, and avoid drastic temperature swings. Sunlight can fade box art over the decades, humidity can cause warping or muffled noises when you open the shrink (yes, even the shrink feels the mood of the room), and temperature spikes can stress the cardboard edges.

- Handling: if you’re planning to move a sealed copy, handle with care. The goal is to avoid sleeve wear or any strain on the corners, which can be more fragile than the glossy surface of a newly minted sleeve on a modern card set.
- Storage environment: a cool, dry place is your friend. A climate-controlled shelf away from damp basements or attic heat waves is ideal. If you’re lucky enough to have a display case, that’s even better—just ensure it’s not catching too much UV light.
- Inventory mindset: consider noting the edition year, box art notes, and any identifying marks that might help you recall later why this particular version felt special. The more you write, the longer you’ll remember, even if your memory fogs up around the edges of the 2001 printing date.
