### Care and maintenance
- Vacuum weekly using a low-suction setting; avoid beater bars that can pull fibers.
- Rotate every 3-6 months to even out wear from chairs and foot traffic.
- Spot-clean spills immediately with a blotting technique; do not rub (think of your rug as a polite friend who hates being rubbed the wrong way).
- Protect with a quality underlay to prevent slipping and to reduce wear on the underside.

## Size options: Custom size vs 8x10
Here’s where the Orchard Seafoam rug earns its nerdy stripes: you can order a custom size tailored to your space, or you can grab the standard 8x10. The 8x10 is the classic living room choice—big enough to anchor a seating arrangement but not so large it requires a calendar to rearrange your furniture. Custom sizing is the keyword if your room has odd dimensions, a fireplace hearth, or a seating area that demands exact proportions. The trade-off: lead times tend to be longer for custom sizes, and price goes up with the increase in scale. If you live in a space with perfect rectangle geometry, the 8x10 often offers the best balance of price, availability, and ease of cleaning.

Pros and cons:
- Custom size pros: exact fit, reduced risk of “rug-too-small” syndrome, better room flow for unusual layouts.
- Custom size cons: longer wait times, higher price, possible color and texture variation between batches.
- 8x10 pros: immediate availability in many markets, lower price, easier to find matching pad and furniture layout.
- 8x10 cons: risk of underutilized space if your room is wider or longer, potential for awkward gaps if furniture arrangement isn’t ideal.

If you want to think in tangible numbers: a typical custom wool rug might cost more per square foot than a standard 8x10, but you’re paying for exact space optimization and bespoke texture. The rug’s performance will likely justify the price if your room’s geometry is the main obstacle to a cohesive look.

## Durability and care
Wool fibers are naturally resilient, and wool rugs like this typically resist crushing better than many synthetic alternatives. The Dye lot’s stability matters; you’ll want to see if the rug has been treated to resist fading under strong natural light or UV lamps from your gaming rig. For a busy household (kids, pets, the occasional pizza delivery), the rug should wear well for years with proper care.

Care ideas:
- Regular vacuuming on a low-suction setting.
- Immediate blotting of spills with a clean, dry cloth.
- Avoid rubbing; friction can push the stain deeper and encourage pilling.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slipping and protect the underside.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12-24 months depending on traffic.
