### How the game plays in practice: a sample round, expanded
Here’s a longer walk-through of a typical round to give you the flavor without spoiling every surprise:

1) Deal: Each player gets a hand of cards (start with 5, adjust for your group). The kids lean in with the curiosity of tiny critics who have opinions on every rice grain.
2) Draft and pass: You pick one card you want to keep, then pass the rest to the player on your left. Your neighbors are either your best friends or your fiercest rivals depending on which cards they’re eyeing and how loudly they celebrate a successful theft.
3) Resolve: Once everyone has chosen a card, you reveal and begin a new round by passing the next hand to the left. The energy in the room climbs as cards disappear and new threats—er, opportunities—appear.
4) Score: After several rounds, you tally points from your collected sets. Most often it’s about completing pairs or triples, maximizing longer collections, and the occasional big-score hero card that changes the math mid-game. The scoring is designed to be teachable in minutes but with enough depth to reward the quick-eyed player.

The speed is the real magic here. A round can wrap before the joke about mispronouncing a sushi type lands, and you’ll be shouting for another round before the last plate disappears. The rapid-fire pace makes it ideal for family nights, casual gatherings, or even a quick break during a long work-from-home day when you need a palate cleanser that doesn’t require a nap afterward.

### Quick-start tips for your first session
- Start with 2 rounds of a small hand size to teach the flow, then expand to standard rounds for the main experience.
- Encourage players to narrate their reasoning aloud during the draft—it heightens entertainment value and reduces conflicts.
- Use a timer to cap rounds if your group tends to stretch the moment into a TED Talk about cuisine philosophy.
- Don’t overthink early picks; the fun is in the late-round steals and surprising scoring turns.
- Have a quick snack break ready between rounds; the palate cleanser keeps energy high and table chatter friendly.

### House rules and variants
- Lightning mode: deal 4 cards per hand, and play two passes per round. This accelerates the chaos and makes every decision feel under pressure.
- Cooperative twist: add a “team scoring” card that sets a shared target score for everyone to chase in a single round, then compare individual paths instead of direct competition.
- Memory variant: after each round, players reveal their own score cards to discuss why certain choices paid off, which adds a light debrief that can be surprisingly educational and funny.
