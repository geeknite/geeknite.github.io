## How to Play

At its core, Card Sharks is a risk management exercise with a social layer. It asks you to decide how confident you are about a card outcome, but it asks you to do it in real time with friends who occasionally forget the concept of “moderation.” Here is a distilled version of the rules, designed to be accessible but still flavorful:

1) Setup: Shuffle the deck. Place the table mat in the center, and give each player a few betting tiles. Decide who goes first. If you have a big group, you might want to establish turn order with a brisk rock-paper-scissors to avoid early chaos.
2) The Card Draw: A card is revealed face up. Players bet on whether the next card will be higher or lower, or on a specific value or suit if your group wants to layer complexity. The app can handle the odds, but if you want to keep it tactile, you can run the odds in your head like a ridiculous, overconfident mathematician.
3) Betting Phase: Each player places their bets with their tiles. Bets usually scale with the risk—low bets for safe predictions, high bets for bold plays. This is where personalities emerge, because some players treat the table like a stock market and others treat it like a weekend garage sale.
4) The Flip: The next card is revealed. If your bet matches the result, you gather points; if not, your points dwindle. The thrill comes from the discrepancy between what you thought would happen and what actually happened; it’s the dramatic irony of card games, and it never gets old.
5) End of Round: After a fixed number of rounds, or when a player hits a target score, the round ends. The leaderboard resets, but the grudges stay. And yes, there are built-in penalties for dramatic flair that overshoots the line—though we all know the only penalty we truly respect is the penalty of losing with style.

For players who crave deeper mechanics, you can add optional twists: wild bid rounds where a single tile can double or halve the score, or a “locked-in” round where players must lock in decisions before the card is revealed, leading to tense, almost ceremonial reveals.

If you want to see how a more formal set of rules looks, the official product booklet is your friend, but the beauty of Card Sharks is that you can start playing with the minimum viable rules and then layer on complexity as your table desire increases. The game scales well from a casual evening to a full-blown tournament where bragging rights are as important as victory snacks.

This is not merely a game; it’s social theater stitched into a deck of cards.

## Tips for Getting the Most Laughs and Wont-Drive-Your Friend Crazy

Card Sharks thrives on social energy, not strict mathematical perfection. Here are a few practical tips to maximize the fun:

- Embrace the chaos: Encourage players to comment on probabilities with theatrical flair. The game rewards commentary as much as correct bets.
- Create micro-stakes: Use small bets for early rounds and ramp up to bigger plays later. It keeps everyone engaged without anyone feeling crushed by a bad first move.
- Use the wild cards strategically: If your edition includes wild cards, decide early whether they are universally beneficial or risk-risky, and stick to that rule so the table isn’t constantly renegotiating the same thing.
- Rotate host duties: Let different players take a turn to explain the odds and narrate the reveals. The fun is in the storytelling, not in who happened to win the last hand.
- Keep snacks handy: The best kind of distraction is delicious nourishment. It helps diffuse tension after a brutal bluff or a surprising reversal.
