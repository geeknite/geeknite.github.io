## Maintenance and Care: Keeping It Looking Sharp (and Not Rusty)
Carbon steel benefits from maintenance, and the Midnight Forge is no exception. Here’s a practical, non-pretentious maintenance plan:
- Wipe after handling: oils from your hands can promote rust over time. Wipe with a clean, dry cloth.
- Oil the blade: light mineral oil or camellia oil is enough to ward off humidity-induced rust. Do so after you’re done handling it or if you plan to store it for a while.
- Store dry: avoid leaving the blade in a damp environment. A dry closet or safe with a desiccant works nicely.
- Clean the scabbard occasionally: dust and moisture can find their way in; a quick wipe and a dry cloth can help.
- Regular checks: inspect the habaki, fit, and tsuka wrap from time to time to ensure there are no loosened parts.

If you’re new to blades, consider a basic maintenance kit that includes oil, a cleaning cloth, and a ceramic rod for occasional alignment checks. It doesn’t have to be fancy to do the job well.
