## How to Play: A Short Rule Primer

Codenames is a social deduction game at heart. The spymaster gives a one-word clue followed by a number. The clue should connect several words on the board that relate to the hidden HP theme. The number indicates how many words on the board the clue refers to. The operatives discuss and guess those words. If a guessed card belongs to the team, it is revealed and stays under that team's control. If a neutral card is revealed, the turn ends and play passes. If the assassin card is revealed, the team immediately loses the round.

The HP edition preserves this rhythm, which means even if you are new to word games, you will catch on quickly. The HP flavor gives you material for the occasional wink or gasp, but the fundamental rules remain as approachable as ever.

## Strategy and Tips

- Start with broad, safe hints. A well-chosen hint can pull in several cards without inviting trouble. Think of it as a surgical strike rather than a battlefield gambit.
- Use multi-card hints when possible, but avoid hints that would pull in too many non-target words. The more cards you connect with a single clue, the higher the payoff—yet the risk of misfires grows.
- Be mindful of the assassin. The moment you risk too many connections, you also risk triggering a wrong move that could hand the round to your opponents.
- Leverage HP flavor for clever connections, but stay grounded. The best hints are ones that others can infer with a shared base of knowledge without spoiling the entire board for everyone else.
- For smaller groups, rotate spymasters more frequently to keep the energy high and to prevent any one player from becoming the oracle of all HP knowledge.
