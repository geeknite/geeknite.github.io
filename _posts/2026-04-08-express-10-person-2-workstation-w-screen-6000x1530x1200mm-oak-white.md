## Assembly, Installation, and the Reality Check

Like any large-scale modular system, the Express desk set benefits from planning. If you’ve ever built a PC late at night using a manual that seems written in runic, you know the drill: lay out parts, verify hardware, then assemble in a stepwise fashion. The 6000 mm length means a bit of forethought. We recommend two adults, a coffee thermos, and a blueprint that doesn’t vanish into the “things to do later” abyss.

- Pre-assembly planning: measure your doorways, confirm ceiling height, and ensure there’s a path to bring the panels through without disassembling the floors. A little planning saves a lot of frustration.
- Panels and frames: the framing pieces usually click into place. Keep an eye on alignment so you don’t end up with a skewed surface that would make a pirate ship jealous.
- Screens and power: route cables with care. If you’re integrating multiple screens, label cables and test each connection before you commit to a final position. It’s like wiring a spaceship, but with less risk of catastrophic failure and more risk of a minor coffee spill.
- Final setup: level the surfaces, adjust any legs or feet, and tighten everything to the recommended torque. If you skip this, you’ll know it when you run your finger along the desk and discover you’ve created a new form of voice-activated squeak.

For those who want a more precise playbook, consult the vendor’s assembly guide and pair it with a floor plan from your space-planning software. If you’re a fan of DIY sensibilities, you’ll enjoy the challenge; if you’d rather be watching a movie about heroic software developers, hire a pro to assemble and calibrate your screens so you can claim that you, too, are participating in the “space optimization” movement.

## Ergonomics, Comfort, and the Human Element

Ten humans sharing a two-block layout means you’ll have to consider ergonomics at scale. The height of 1200 mm is comfortable for many users, balancing legroom and screen visibility. The key ergonomic points to verify before finalizing the order:

- Chair compatibility: ensure chairs with adjustable height and lumbar support align with the desk height to maintain neutral postures for extended periods.
- Screen height: plan for screen mounts or arms that position display tops at or slightly below eye level to prevent neck strain during long screen time.
- Keyboard and mouse placement: ensure enough depth for comfortable arm reach and under-desk space for movement.
- Lighting: the glare factor can ruin even the best desk setup. Consider overhead lighting that minimizes reflections on monitor surfaces.

If you want to go beyond the basics, you can add small personal work zones with subtle dividers or color-coded mats to reduce cognitive fatigue. The design encourages collaboration but doesn’t force it; individual comfort remains a priority, because the hardest part of a long day is convincing your inner self to stop binging spreadsheets and start creating magic in your own corner of the desk universe.
