## How to Care for a Large Rug: A Practical Guide
Care is not glamorous, but it’s essential. For a rug this size, a regular vacuum is your best friend. Use a gentle setting and avoid aggressive passes that could prematurely wear the fibers. Rotating the rug every few months helps ensure even wear and fading. Spills should be blotted immediately; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers. For stubborn stains, a specialized rug cleaner that’s safe for synthetic fibers is recommended. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to confirm that the cleaner won’t alter the color.

Pro tip: If you have pets, a quick wipe-down with a damp cloth for minor accidents can buy you precious time before you break out the washing machine. If you plan on machine washing, make sure to follow the manufacturer’s wash instructions to avoid any swelling or distortion. We found that air drying keeps the rug’s shape intact better than a hot tumble dry, but both methods work well depending on your living space and dryer types.
