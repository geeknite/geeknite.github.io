## How Dixit Origins Plays: Setup and Rounds
Dixit Origins follows a familiar rhythm for the Dixit family but with a few deliberate tweaks that keep veteran players engaged while remaining approachable for newcomers. Here is a compact breakdown of setup and round flow:

- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a set number of cards to each player, and place the scoring tokens within easy reach. The storyteller role rotates clockwise, and the game begins with the storyteller selecting a clue that describes one of their own cards without revealing it. The clue can be a word, a phrase, a sound, or a short piece of poetry—whatever gets the imagination buzzing. The other players select a card from their own hands that best matches the clue and pass it face-down to the storyteller.
- Reveal and vote: The storyteller reveals their chosen card along with the other players' cards. Everyone secretly votes on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. If players correctly identify the storyteller's card, the storyteller gains fewer points; if not, the players who guessed the storyteller's card gain points. The exact scoring rules can vary slightly depending on the variant you play, and Origins includes some slight tweaks to keep the scoring interesting and fair.
- Rounds and pacing: The round proceeds with new cards dealt to refill hands. The storyteller role passes to the next player, and the game progresses until someone reaches the target score. The balance of storytelling, deduction, and a little luck makes the rounds brisk and unpredictable, with unexpected stories and memory triggers popping up in every session.

What makes Origins feel distinct is the synergy between the card art and the storytelling cue you muster. The images are so evocative that a single card can spawn dozens of interpretations. The challenge is not to be too obvious or too obscure with your clue. You want your clue to be memorable without giving away the exact card you have chosen. The better you calibrate your clue to the group, the more satisfaction you gain when others fail to identify your card while still appreciating your narrative approach.
