## Assembly and installation: how hard is it, really?
Like most modular office systems, the assembly depends on your tolerance for reading installation guides and your team’s willingness to split duties (you know, the heroic “one person holds, one person tightens” dynamic). Expect a two- to four-hour setup window for a six-person layout if you’re doing it with minimal interruptions. If you’re a one-person heroic tinkerer who enjoys a little DIY test of stamina, it might take longer—but the results are worth it.

Before you start, inventory all the pieces and check for any transit damage. The last thing you want is a missing panel halfway through assembly. It’s not the end of the world, but it will require a bit of interpretive choreography with the hardware store if you don’t want mismatched gaps to appear on the front row.

During assembly, you’ll be aligning tables and attaching supporting brackets. While it’s not rocket science, it does require a bit of patience. A helpful tip: have a second person assist with alignments and a third nearby to manage screws and small parts so you don’t have a micro-disassembly on your hands when you realize you’ve used the wrong bolt length at the wrong place.
