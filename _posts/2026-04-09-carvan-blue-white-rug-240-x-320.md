## Cleaning, care, and spill strategy: a practical guide
Spills happen; this is a universal truth known to humans, dogs, and the occasional cat who believes their tail is the remote control. For the Carvan rug, your best friend is a quick blot with a dry cloth or paper towel to absorb as much liquid as possible. Avoid rubbing, which only drives the liquid deeper into the fibers. After blotting, you can use a mild detergent solution or a carpet cleaner designed for synthetic fibers. Test in a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.

For organic stains like coffee, juice, or red wine, you’re going to want to treat promptly. A gentle solution of water and a small amount of mild detergent can help lift the stain. Blot again and rinse with clean water to prevent soap residue, which can attract more dirt over time. If you’re dealing with stubborn stains, consider enzyme cleaners designed for carpets—these can help break down organic residues—but always test first to avoid discoloration.

Vacuuming is your rug’s best friend. Regular vacuuming prevents dirt from becoming embedded in the fibers and keeps the pile looking crisp. For high-traffic areas, vacuum more frequently, rotating cleaning patterns so that the rug wears evenly. A vacuum with adjustable height is helpful; you don’t want to press the beater bar into the fibers with the enthusiasm of a vacuum-driven rocket.

Seasonal care can help as well. If you live in a climate with heavy dust, consider shaking the rug outside (gently) or using a soft brush to loosen surface dirt. In humid environments, dry the rug thoroughly after cleaning to prevent mold and mildew from staging a coup on your living room floor. A well-ventilated room is your ally here, as is a good rug-touring schedule that doesn’t involve poor decision-making in the middle of the night when you’re tired and craving pizza.

## Installation tips and layout hacks
Installing this rug is less about heavy-lifting and more about planning. If you have a large living room, start by placing the rug roughly where the furniture will anchor the room. Then measure the distance from walls, ensuring about 8–24 inches of exposed floor around the rug’s edges for a floating, airy look. In smaller spaces, you may want the rug to sit more centrally so that the furniture sits proportionately on the rug without feeling overwhelmed by the room’s boundaries.

Layering is a trend that works surprisingly well with blue-and-white designs. Consider placing a smaller, solid-colored rug under a coffee table to ground the center, then add a larger Carvan rug on top if your space allows. The layered look can create depth and texture while preserving the rug’s visual impact. Remember to use a rug pad between layers to prevent slip-offs and bunching.

If you have a holiday or seasonal decor plan, the rug can be a flexible anchor. You can swap pillow colors, throws, and wall art to reflect the season without changing the rug—which is a win for your wallet and your sense of humor when your in-laws arrive with a sudden need for a “level-up” in interior design.
