## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Looking Its Geekiest
### Routine Cleaning
For routine upkeep, a simple vacuum to remove loose dirt and a quick water rinse are often enough. After a rain, you’ll probably want to shake out the rug to remove excess water before it dries. If you’re dealing with stubborn stains, use a mild detergent mixed with warm water and a soft-bristle brush. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fibers or affect the color. Polypropylene is tolerant, but it’s not a super-hero; treat it with respect, and it will treat your patio well.

### Stain Removal Strategies
- For organic stains like leaves or pollen: light brushing and rinse.
- For food stains: a gentle microfiber pad with warm soapy water.
- For oil-based stains: a small amount of degreaser; test in a hidden spot first to avoid color changes.
- For mildew or mold patches (rare in dry climates): a mild solution of water and white vinegar can help, followed by thorough rinsing.

### Storage and Seasonal Care
If you live in a climate with seasonal swings, you may want to roll or fold the rug and store it in a dry place during heavy winters or monsoon seasons. The goal is to avoid long-term moisture retention and to prevent any color changes due to prolonged sun exposure on the same spot. The rug’s 350x250cm size makes storage a bit of a puzzle, but with a little clever folding and a sturdy storage bag, you can tuck it away without turning your garage into a textile labyrinth.
