## What City of Mist is, in a sentence and a city block

City of Mist isn't a typical system where you roll to succeed and advance your character with numeric progression. It’s a game about identities—how a person becomes a hero (or a villain, or something deliciously messy in between) when real life complexities collide with mythic potential. Players pick a Concept, which is your core identity thread, and then attach a Mask—a social and personal role you wear in the city. The magic is not about how physically powerful you are; it’s about how you narrate your power, how your mythic self interacts with ordinary people, and how your backstory nudges the city’s fate toward a particular outcome.

The Core Rulebook leans into a storytelling philosophy that treats the city as a living, breathing character. The city isn’t just a backdrop; it’s a co-author. The rules exist to serve the story, not to overpower it with dice gymnastics. That means the best sessions feel like a conversation in a dim diner where two people realize they’re telling the same myth from different angles, and the truth is somewhere in the space between the two voices.

## Core mechanics in a nutshell (enough to start, not to bore)

City of Mist uses a relatively light set of mechanics designed to keep the focus on story rather than on table-slaying math. The system centers around three big ideas: Concept, Mask, and Power, all operating through a pool of dice and a simple resolution framework. The exact numbers aren’t the point; what matters is how you narrate and how the dice outcomes coordinates with your story.

### Concept, Masks, and Tags
- Concept: The character's core identity. Think of it as the living memory of who you are when the city turns the lights on at 2 a.m. Concept defines your motivations, your moral dilemmas, and where your heart tends to lead in a pinch.
- Mask: The social role your character plays in the city. The Mask can be heroic, shady, or gloriously ambiguous. It’s the persona you reveal to the world when you perform your role under pressure.
- Tag: The world’s tools—figures, clues, locations, or personality traits that can be invoked to change how scenes unfold. Tags are where the game gets spicy: you can flag a location as “lethal fog” or call upon a character’s “deadly charisma” to bend the scene in your favor. Tags also serve as the primary vehicle for narrative control and cost management.

The trio—Concept, Mask, and Tags—creates a flexible engine: you can use tags to twist outcomes, heighten drama, or pivot the scene toward a thematic beat you want to explore. This isn’t crunchy for crunch’s sake; it’s crunchy for storytelling’s sake. And that’s the intent the book leans into with gusto.

### Verve, Dice, and Resolution (light on math, heavy on momentum)
The system uses a dice pool to resolve actions. The number of dice you roll is driven by your current Verve and the tag modifiers that apply to the action you’re attempting. Most scenes demand instant, cinematic results—where a pool of dice either confirms your plan, complicates it, or reveals entirely new twists. The resolution framework rewards clever narration: you win not only by rolling well but by framing your successes as meaningful beats in the story.

One of the most refreshing aspects is how the book treats misfortune. Failure isn’t a dead end; it’s an opportunity for a twist that can reframe the entire scene. The “mist” of the city—its myths leaking into the everyday—creates a constant potential for surprise. This keeps the game from ever feeling predictable, even when you’re playing through a familiar trope (disciplined vigilante vs. a corrupt corporation, city-wide conspiracy vs. personal redemption, etc.).

### Story-first playstyle advantages
City of Mist isn’t about wiping out a stat block with a perfect combo. It’s about drafting a narrative arc and then letting the dice catch up to it. The book emphasizes collaborative storytelling: the GM and players are co-authors of the city’s mythos, not just a referee and numerically-coded heroes. This approach makes sessions feel more like a cinematic montage than an actuarial spreadsheet. If your group loves character-driven plots, you’ll find the Core Rulebook an almost-too-friendly playground for dramatic reveals, plot twists, and characters who talk in the rhythm of a double-percussion track—one beat for intimacy, one for danger.

## World-building: setting your myth in a modern metropolis

City of Mist’s setting isn’t a static world you flip through in a single chapter. It’s more like a sandbox of neon alleychats and mythic rumors, built to be populated by your players. The Core Rulebook offers modular guidance on creating districts, factions, and mythic phenomena—the kinds of ideas you can lift and drop into your city as if it were a living, breathing map you’re constantly annotating. It’s the kind of book that rewards GMs who enjoy cocktail napkin worldbuilding: note a few distinctive landmarks, a couple of recurring NPCs, and a handful of urban legends that the players can taunt or confront.

The tone the book fosters is deliberately flexible. You can run a city-wide noir epic, a micro-campaign in a single district, or a string of episodic vignettes that feel like episodes of a mythic detective show. That flexibility is valuable for groups with varying play styles: some players want a long-running arc; others crave one-shot sessions that still feel complete, thematically satisfying, and meaningful.

## Character creation: building a myth in bits and pieces

Character creation in City of Mist is one of the book’s most fun and relaxing aspects. It encourages starting with a vivid concept, then layering on the Mask and Tags to define the character’s unique voice. The process is less about grinding a stat block and more about discovering who your masked self is and why the myth matters in the city’s grand scheme.

### The step-by-step vibe
- Pick a Concept: This is your character’s core identity. It should be a strong, resonant idea that can drive scenes without needing a dozen pages of backstory. Pro tip: pick a flaw or contradiction to create immediate drama.
- Choose a Mask: What social role does your character play in the city? The mask should interact with the concept in interesting ways—perhaps your hero is a well-liked community figure who struggles with a powerful secret.
- Select Tags: Tags are where you define the tools, connections, and limitations that push you toward story-critical moments. They’re also a bankroll of narrative hooks for the GM.
- Decide on Verve: Verve is the resource that scales how bravely you push into the story. It recharges in interesting ways as the city’s myths surface in your life. Building Verve involves both risk and reward, so you’ll learn quickly to calibrate your approach to scenes.

The result is a character who feels like a myth you can hold in your hands—a person who can discuss ethics with a mob boss and still have the courage to stare down the unknown when the power goes out.

### Play expectations for new players
For newcomers, the Core Rulebook does a terrific job of lowering entry barriers. The core concepts are straightforward, and the rules are designed to support storytelling rather than stall it with page-long clarifications. There’s a healthy amount of “GM quick-start” guidance that helps you bootstrap sessions quickly, which is a godsend for groups who want to get from “idea” to “play” without a long pre-game ritual. If you’ve previously found modern RPGs too crunchy, City of Mist offers a palate-cleanser where you can enjoy character reveals and scene-framing without wrestling with a thousand edge cases on every turn.

## Worldbuilding for GMs: running the Myth City smoothly

### Session Zero and player buy-in
The Core Rulebook strongly advocates a collaborative Session Zero. The group agrees on the city’s tone, the kinds of myths you’ll explore, and how gritty or hopeful the city should feel. The book provides prompts to help you weave player concepts into the city’s fabric, ensuring that everyone gets a moment to shine and that no one’s origin sits on a final boss pedestal for three sessions in a row.

### Plot hooks, arcs, and the GM’s toolkit
City of Mist presents a toolkit rather than a fixed adventure structure. You’ll find ready-made arcs and a library of antagonists ready to slot into your city. The GM’s toolkit emphasizes flexibility: if a scene heads toward a different mythic beat, the book suggests ways to pivot without losing narrative momentum. This makes it easier to respond to players’ clever choices and to reward bold narrative moves.

### Pacing and escalation without crunch
Because the system favors storytelling over spreadsheet-warfare, pacing is handled through narrative momentum, not rule-chasing. The Core Rulebook provides pacing tips: keep reveals tight, let one big mythic moment land per act, and let the city itself push the stakes higher through environment and social leverage. You’ll learn to read the room: if players are leaning into a moral gray area, tilt the next scene toward ethical tension; if they’re chasing a fast, kinetic beat, keep the tempo high and the stakes personal.

## Accessibility, inclusivity, and representation
City of Mist, at its best, invites a spectrum of identities into its mythic, neon-lit world. By design, the game centers on character concepts and storytelling beats rather than on a single canonical setting. The Core Rulebook makes an effort to present inclusive examples and to encourage GM discretion when it comes to sensitive themes. It’s not a flawless blueprint, but it’s a solid foundation for a table that wants to tell diverse stories under a shared umbrella of myth and memory.

The text is mindful of triggering content and provides guidance on handling sensitive topics, but as with any game that leans into noir and myth, it’s up to the GM to facilitate a respectful environment. The best sessions feel like a mutual agreement among players: we’re telling a story that respects our boundaries while still leaning into the city’s dangerous beauty.

Pros:

Cons:

- Some players may want more explicit examples of how tags interact in complex scenes

If your group loves mythic fiction integrated with detective vibes and you want a game that encourages talking through the beats rather than calculating outcomes, City of Mist’s Core Rulebook is a strong candidate. If you thrive on deep optimization and meticulously defined combat systems, you’ll need to lean on house rules or supplementary materials.
