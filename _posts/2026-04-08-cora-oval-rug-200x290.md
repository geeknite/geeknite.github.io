### Care and Maintenance

Maintenance is where many rugs either earn their keep or start collecting dust (figuratively and literally). Cora's synthetic construction simplifies care. Regular vacuuming is your friend, ideally with a low to mid-power setting to avoid pulling fibers loose. For spills, blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth; the goal is to lift the liquid rather than grind it deeper into the fibers.

Stain removal should be approached with gentle solutions, starting with a mixture of mild detergent and warm water. For tougher stains, a dedicated rug stain remover designed for synthetic fibers can save you the heartbreak of a dull spot. Always perform a spot test in an inconspicuous area before applying any cleaner to the main field.

If you’re dealing with pet hair, a bristle brush or a vacuum with a pet-hair setting can help remove fine fibers. Pro-tip: rotating the rug every few months can help even out wear and give a fresh look to the high-traffic zones. Avoid placing the rug in direct sunlight for extended periods if you’re worried about color fading—the sun is a fickle designer that loves to pick favorites.

### Placement Tips and Styling Ideas

Placement is part science, part art. Start by centering the rug under your primary seating arrangement and adjust so that the longest edge aligns with the longest wall or piece of furniture. If your room has an asymmetrical layout, use the rug to visually balance the space. A common trick is to have at least the front legs of a sofa resting on the rug, with chairs on or near the edge. The result is a cohesive area that signals, “this is where the conversation happens.”

Another styling approach is to use the rug as a color anchor. Pull the rug’s accent color into cushions, throws, and small decor pieces to weave a consistent narrative without overloading the room with competing patterns. Remember: less is often more when you’re dealing with large textiles in a visible space.

For those who love interior design adventures, integrating the rug with a second layering piece can produce dynamic textures and depth. A thinner jute or sisal runner near an entryway can create a transitional look between rooms, while a plush, lighter-toned rug beneath a different seating zone offers a multi-dimensional floor story.
