## Setup and Installation: Making It Work Without Turning Your Garage into a Lab
### Packing and Prep
The box arrives with the usual assortment of brackets, bolts, and a user guide that may remind you of a mission briefing from a low-budget sci-fi film. Take your time to sort the hardware, check for missing pieces, and ensure your mounting plate is prepped for installation. A clean and dry workspace helps a lot here. If you are one of those people who treat a manual like a sacred text, you will enjoy the explicit step-by-step approach. If you are more of a “figure it out as you go” type, you might take longer, but you will still end up with a functioning system.

### Installation Tips
- Verify bumper clearance. If your bumper has unusual shapes or extra protrusions, you might need an adapter plate or some patience with the drill.
- Set the fairlead alignment early. A misaligned fairlead causes side-loading and unnecessary wear on the rope.
- Use proper wiring and fuse protection. A 12V system can be fussy when you push it near its limit, so secure the wiring and protect it from moisture.
- Re-check everything after the first test pull. It is common to discover a loose bolt or a misrouted cable once the drum starts spinning under load.

### Tuning and Fine-Tuning
Once installed, take a moment to dial in the line length, the braking behavior, and the remote's response. The winch will not require constant babysitting once you’ve got the hang of it. But for the first few pulls, keep a safe distance, wear eye protection, and remind yourself that you are operating heavy machinery rather than trying to impress your neighbors with a never-ending pulley trick montage.
