## How to Play and What This Edition Adds (or Subtracts)

### Core Rules Quick Recap
- Each player starts with seven cards. The goal is to be the first to get rid of all your cards.
- On your turn, you can play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the top card on the discard pile.
- Action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two) shake up the flow to make the game feel less like a slow parade and more like a chaotic sprint.
- Wild and Wild Draw Four cards allow you to change color and stack more chaos on your opponents, with the standard cautions about challenges and penalties if you’re cheating or misplaying.
- When you’re down to one card, you must yell “UNO!” If you forget, other players can call you out and make you draw; a classic moment that never gets old at family gatherings.

### Family Fun Twist and House Rules
The 2012 Family Fun edition—like many party-friendly re-packagings—encourages a little extra flavor. The exact rule variations vary by batch and country, but the spirit is consistent: more laughs, fewer “I’m bored” moments, and more opportunities for you to unleash your inner chaos-agent without breaking the bank or the table. Common twists you might see or invent include:
- Quick-Draw for everyone at the end of a round: throw a Wild card and pick a color that everyone must follow for the next two turns. It’s as friendly as it sounds—until someone forgets and accidentally triggers a friendly fire wild.
- Team UNO: pairs or trios team up against the other teams; you still play cards but tally scores in rounds rather than individual wins.
- Double-take rounds: if a draw-two is played and the next player also has a draw-two, they can chain it, passing the burden along as a comedic “cards-for-coffee” chain. This is optional and often leads to dangerous levels of laughter or chaos.
