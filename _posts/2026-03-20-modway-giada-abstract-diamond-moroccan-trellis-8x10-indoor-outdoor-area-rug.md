### Durability and UV Resistance
Outdoor life means UV exposure—yep, those sun rays are not going to be kind to you if you’re made of something delicate. Giada rugs are designed with color fastness in mind. The colorways are stabilized to resist fading under typical sunlight exposure. This makes Giada a good candidate for patios, sunrooms, or poolside seating areas where you want to maintain a crisp geometric vibe without repainting your floor every summer.

### Weather Resilience
If you live in a climate with wet seasons or salty air, you want something that doesn’t absorb moisture like a sponge. The polypropylene fiber of Giada dries relatively quickly and won’t hold onto mold or mildew with typical indoor-outdoor use. That being said, if you spill red wine or miso soup, you should still clean promptly. Prolonged moisture can lead to issues with any rug, outdoor or indoor, even if the fiber itself is water-resistant. So yes, drink responsibly near your Giada rug and you’ll thank us later.

## Performance and Maintenance
### Cleaning Hacks that Actually Work
Cleaning is the boring part, but it’s also the part that keeps the space feeling fresh. For an outdoor indoor rug like Giada, routine maintenance tends to involve vacuuming to remove loose dirt and quick spot-cleaning for spills. For tougher stains, a mild detergent mixed with water, a soft-bristled brush, and a good rinse with a hose are often enough to revive the pattern. Avoid bleach since this can degrade the color and shorten the rug’s life. If you live in a dusty area, a periodic gentle wash with a garden hose helps prevent dirt from embedding, especially in the crevices of the trellis pattern. And yes, it dries faster than a cat on a hot tin roof, but it’s still wise to let it air dry completely before you roll up and stash it away.

### Longevity and Everyday Use

- When placing Giada in a high-traffic area, consider a rug pad to minimize slipping and to increase life expectancy. A low-profile pad helps preserve the clean edge lines while providing a bit of extra bite under furniture feet.
