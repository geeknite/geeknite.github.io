### Card Composition and Rank: How a Baraja Española Is Built

A quick primer for newcomers: the Baraja Española is traditionally organized in four suits—Oros (gold coins), Copas (cups), Espadas (swords), and Bastos (clubs). Each suit usually runs from 1 (As) through 7, then the court cards Sota (jack), Caballo (knight), and Rey (king). That’s 10 cards per suit, totaling 40 cards in the classic setup. Some variants expand to 48 or even 50 for game-specific tweaks. The three 50-card decks in this bundle likely include a couple of extra cards for teaching or tarot-themed play. The important bit is that you’ll have a robust set for learning, practicing, and playing.

If you’re curious how the distribution breaks down, the insert inside the box should outline the exact roster. But the practical takeaway stands: you’ve got enough cards to run multiple tables without reshuffling a single deck to the brink.
