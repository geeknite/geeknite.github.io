## Overview

Streaking Kittens is the first expansion for Exploding Kittens, a game that already thrives on chaotic card play, dramatic cat puns, and the existential dread of not drawing the Defuse card when you need it most. The expansion adds 15 new cards that slot into the base deck and rewire some of the tempo and tactical options available to players. The theme stays true to Exploding Kittens: goofy art, fast turns, and moments where everyone suddenly realizes that the deck is conspiring against them in the most adorable way possible.

For players who have never played Exploding Kittens, the base game is a kitty-infused version of a loot-and-dodge card game where players try to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. The kicker is that you’re allowed to defuse explosions—if you have a Defuse card and you manage to bluff your way out of a near-death experience, congratulations, you just won a small victory against fate and a group of roving cats. The Streaking Kittens expansion expands that universe by injecting new card types, new win conditions, and new ways to misdirect your friends into thinking they’re about to win, only to be cat-flung into a different trajectory entirely.

## Card by card: what the 15 cards bring to the table

The expansion introduces a handful of new mechanics that feel like they were designed by a bored but benevolent lab assistant in a wizard’s tower. Here’s a practical look at what you actually get in the 15 cards:

- Streaking Kitten cards: These are the marquee cards that subtly alter the endgame tempo. When played, they nudge the cat chaos in a new direction and may force players to recalibrate their strategy midstream. They’re not just flavor; they alter risk assessment and turn ordering in surprisingly elegant ways.
- New action cards: A handful of new actions spice up the midgame, offering players more opportunities to alter turn order, steal cards, or shuffle the deck in ways that no one predicted. These cards reward clever timing and risk-taking in equal measure.
- Defensive options: The expansion includes cards that give players more protection or ways to defuse an imminent blowout moment. The balance here is important — too many defenses and you dull the chaotic edge; too few and it’s a sprint to the finish with everyone sprinting toward a meltdown.
- Cat and anti-cat synergies: You’ll notice new interactions with base game cards, which create surprising combos that are satisfying to execute and, even more, fun to witness. Expect some new cat memes to become permanently etched into your group’s memory after a single game night.
- Quick victory nudge cards: A couple of cards exist to provide a slightly higher probability of dramatic early wins, if the stars align and you’re feeling lucky (or unluckily heroic).

All told, the 15 cards are not a complete metamorphosis of Exploding Kittens but rather a well-judged expansion that slots into the core experience without demanding a rulebook-reading marathon on a Tuesday night. The design keeps the humor intact while injecting additional decision points that can swing momentum in delightful ways.

## How to play with the expansion: a practical guide

- Set up: Mix the 15 new cards into the base Exploding Kittens deck. Shuffle everything well. The expansion doesn’t require a full rule rework; you’ll still use the standard Defuse cards, your Exploding Kitten pile, and the general turn-taking mechanism.
- Turn order and flow: Expect more back-and-forth than in the base game. The new action cards tempt players to make bold moves or to play it safe, depending on how the current status of the game looks. The Streaking Kitten cards add a new layer to when the end-game will actually begin, which is perfect for groups that like to stretch a single session into a saga.
- Endgame dynamics: The endgame can feel unpredictable, but in a fun way. The new cards encourage a quick pivot from “let’s stall and see what happens” to “let’s push for the big finish,” which can lead to dramatic comebacks and even more dramatic heartbreaks.
- Group size considerations: The expansion scales well from 3 players up to the typical max group size Exploding Kittens supports. With smaller groups, the pacing remains brisk, and with larger groups, the chaos unfolds in a wonderfully chaotic spectacle that your table will talk about for weeks.
