## Installation and setup tips

### Step by step
1. Remove the old housing from the bike and measure the path from shifter to derailleur.
2. Cut the new 4 mm housing to the measured length, adding a little extra for safe alignment.
3. Fit ferrules at frame entry points if your frame uses them, then slide the housing into place.
4. Thread the inner cable, then test by shifting through all gears while the bike stands or is supported on a stand.
5. If you notice any snagging or stiffness, inspect for burrs on ferrules or sharp edges on frame guides.

### Tools you might need
- Cable cutters with clean blades
- Ferrules or end caps
- A sharp knife for cleanly cutting the housing to length
- A light lubricant safe for cables may help in very cold climates

## Maintenance and care

Cables and housing are not set it and forget it components. After a few long rides, it is wise to inspect the length, ensure there are no obvious kinks and re-lubricate if needed. Clean the exterior with a damp cloth and avoid aggressive solvents that could degrade the outer jacket. If you live in a wet climate, consider drying the housing after a ride to prevent rust in the end caps and ferrules.

## Thoughtful tips from the Geeknite lab

- Always cut the housing cleanly; jagged ends invite frustration and burrs that catch cables.
