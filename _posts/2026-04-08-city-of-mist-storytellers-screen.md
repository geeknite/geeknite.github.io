## City of Mist Storyteller's Screen: A Neon Noir Lens on Your Tabletop Campaigns

If you were hoping for a bland, businesslike GM screen that quietly holds your dice and your sanity, City of Mist Storyteller's Screen smirks and slides a neon-inked card across the table, saying, welcome to the actual city. City of Mist is never purely serious and it is never purely silly; it lives in that delicious comic-book-noir space where hard choices are made and tiny gods argue with ordinary people about their own identities. The Storyteller's Screen leans into that vibe with a design that shouts I know what you’re here for: speed, flavor, and a handful of quick references that feel like they were minted in an underpass after midnight. In a genre that can feel like a spreadsheet with capes, this screen tries to be characterful where it matters and utilitarian where it helps the table stay in the moment.

### What you get in the box
The Storyteller's Screen ships as a compact three-panel GM screen with a foldout, double-sided set of quick-reference sheets. On the outside, you get art that nods toward the mythic-graffiti vibe of City of Mist: a silhouette against a rain-slicked alley, a hint of a mask catching the streetlamp, and a color palette that makes you want to lean in closer to understand the story hidden in the shadows. On the inside, you’ll find a mix of rules reminders and setting-flavored prompts designed to keep the table buzzing without forcing you to flip to the core book every ten seconds.

To kick things off, here are some highlights of what the product includes:

- Three panels of quick references: Scene structure, MacGuffin hooks, and a cheat sheet for dice and checks. The layout showcases a balance between the system’s trademark flexibility and the GM’s need for a fast, frictionless turn.
- A foldout quick-reference sheet: This is where City of Mist’s more esoteric terms get written in bold, but readable, shorthand that you can literally glance at mid-scene and still remember what you meant to do.
- A couple of small add-ons: one more page in the booklet with optional house rules, and a few ready-to-use prompts that feel like they could be dropped into almost any campaign—if your campaign is City-of-Mist-shaped, of course.

The packaging feels sturdy enough to survive a small-apocalypse-table run or a particularly enthusiastic player flipping it closed with a hammer fist when a twist lands. The stock is not premium coffee-table card-stock, but it’s not flimsy pulp either. The backers who care about durability will appreciate the fold lines that stay intact after a few games. It’s not indestructible; it’s a tool, not a shield, and it behaves as such.

### Design and layout: a balance between flavor and function
City of Mist is all about blending mythic identity with ordinary life. The Storyteller's Screen translates that into a printed artifact that feels like a piece of the city itself: weathered edges, a glow-in-the-dark border (subtle, not overdone), and typography that screams noir without making your brain do cartwheels to read what’s on offer.

- Panels: The three-panel setup is clean and readable from a typical table distance. The panels present—concept, Distinctions, and Scenes—are organized so that you can navigate between them with a gentle glance rather than a full arm-swing to reach your notes. A lot of RPG GM screens go heavy on generic “combat modifiers” and “skills,” which makes you memorize a handful of static numbers. City of Mist, in contrast, prioritizes the GM’s narrative toolkit; the screen reduces cognitive load when you’re juggling multiple threads at the same table.
- Icons and typography: The icons lean into neon-line art, with color coding that’s helpful at a glance. The typeface is readable; the contrast is good; the lines break in all the right places so you never feel like you’re fighting the page to find a single line of vital information.
- Flavor without flinch: The interior keeps the city’s vibe intact with short, evocative prompts. It’s not a coffee-table book; it’s a working screen that makes you want to lean forward and start riffing with your players instead of rifling through the core rules.

If there’s a small area where some players might grumble, it’s that City of Mist’s entire brand relies on a flexible, sometimes sprawling script for “Scenes” and “Motivations.” The screen mirrors this: it gives you a framework, but you’ll still need to rely on your memory or your own note cards for the specifics of your ongoing epic. That’s not a flaw so much as a reminder that City of Mist is designed to invite improvisation, not constrain it with a one-size-fits-all cheat sheet.

### Inside the engine: what the screen actually helps you do
Let’s get practical. The GM screen isn’t just a pretty face; it’s the city’s reflection—faint, a little smoky, and always one step ahead of you. The quick references support the game’s rhythm: setting up a scene, acknowledging a Distinction, and triggering the story’s next big beat without breaking the mood.

- Scene flow reference: The screen provides a compact guide to how scenes are structured in City of Mist. This can help new GMs establish a reliable tempo and help veterans avoid the “narrative drift” where a session meanders without advancing the core mystery.
- Distinctions and Motivations: These core mechanics are the bones of a player’s identity in the game. The screen’s prompts serve as memory jogs for the GM when a player leans on a Distinction to pivot the scene toward a thematic payoff. You’ll find yourself nudging players toward the story’s larger scales without having to dig through the core book’s chapters.
- Powers and Tags: The game’s power mechanics revolve around the interplay of human folklore and modern-day cunning. The screen helps you track tiered effects and the conditions under which a power might escalate or fizzle. The optimization payoff is not purely mechanical; you’ll hear players say, “That’s what my Distinction was trying to tell us” in a way that makes the table feel alive.

The practical result is a GM screen that supports City of Mist’s storytelling engine rather than obstructing it. It’s not a cheat sheet for power gaming or a one-stop cheat for every possible NPC you’ll ever cram into a session; it’s a prompt-rich companion that helps you stay in the moment while still honoring the game’s design philosophy.

If you’re a GM who loves the game’s flavor but hates flipping through the book in the heat of a scene, the Storyteller's Screen is doing some useful heavy lifting.
