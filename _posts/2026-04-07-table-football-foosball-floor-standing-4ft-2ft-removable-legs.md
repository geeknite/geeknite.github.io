## Maintenance and Longevity

### Cleaning routine

Wipe the playing surface with a soft damp cloth after use to remove fingerprints, dust, and the occasional stray snack crumb from tournament night. Avoid harsh cleaners that might degrade the surface coating. If you notice any plastic chips or rough spots on the players, treat them with a light polish to restore smoothness.

### Components care

Inspect the rods for any looseness or irregular movement. Tighten any loose bolts and check the leg joints periodically. For the ball returns, clear any clogs and ensure the chute flows freely. A well-maintained table is a table you will reach for on a rainy Sunday rather than a table you will hide away in a closet for fear of it falling apart.

### Replacement parts

Spare rods or player pieces are not always available in the same model family, so keep a few spare screws and washers in a labeled container. This reduces downtime and preserves your reputation as the resident table tactician who refuses to concede a shot because a tiny part disappeared into the couch cushions.
