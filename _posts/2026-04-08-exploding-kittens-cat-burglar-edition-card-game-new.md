## What is the Cat Burglar Edition?

Exploding Kittens Cat Burglar Edition is a spin-off in the Exploding Kittens family that leans into the caper vibe: think artfully drawn cats in burglar masks, tiny bags of loot, and booby-trapped playhouses. The aim remains simple at its core: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten card, use the various action cards to delay or redirect danger, and—importantly—steal victory points through clever play and a dash of audacity.

This edition adds a few thematic twists: the loot cards replace some of the standard draw-and-discard dynamics, the burglar-themed art gives the cards a cohesive story, and a handful of new action cards tilt the balance toward more aggressive, take-that style gameplay. If you loved the chaotic charm of the base game, Cat Burglar leans into it with extra glitz, more mischief, and a stronger sense of “we’re all in this together, until someone swipes your loot.”

In terms of tone, the Cat Burglar Edition keeps the same jokey, self-aware humor that Exploding Kittens fans cherish. The humor lands best when you’re not playing for a Michelin-star strategy award but rather for the glory of catching a thief who tried to swipe your loot and then accidentally exploded their own ego. It’s silly, fast, and very much a party game, designed to be enjoyed with a group that appreciates punny captions, cat-themed chaos, and the occasional dramatic gasp when a player finally triggers the cat-curse of doom.

## How to play (a quick overview)

If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll find a familiar rhythm here, but with a few fresh beats that change the tempo of the game—sometimes for the better, sometimes as a cautionary reminder to breathe between heists.

### Setup

- Shuffle the loot deck and the Exploding Kitten deck separately.
- Deal a hand of cards to each player (the exact number varies by player count and edition rules; you’ll find the current math in the rulebook). The loot cards populate hands with resources and sneaky options.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile, interspersed as per the rules. You’ll want to keep others guessing about when doom arrives and who gets to blow up their own moment of glory.
- The first player is chosen randomly, or by a goofy cat-related ritual we won’t disclose to avoid family disputes at the table.

### Turn structure
On your turn, you typically do the following:
- Play one or more action cards (if rules allow)—these can force draws, swap loot, or change who’s on the hook.
- Draw a card from the draw pile. If you draw an Exploding Kitten card, you’re either saved by a defuse-like action or you’re out of the round, depending on how many lives you’ve got with this edition’s lifelines.
- If you finish your turn without blowing up, you pass the cat-curtain to the next player.

### The Cat Burglar twist
The Cat Burglar Edition introduces loot as a resource that interacts with action cards in new ways. Some loot cards can be used to cancel an attack, reveal a hidden set of cards, or unlock “safe cracks” that give you a second chance when doom looms. The thieves on the cards sometimes carry small kit-bag icons that unlock extra moves if you’ve collected enough loot. This creates a mini-economy within the game: do you save loot for a big play, or spend it to disrupt a rival during a critical moment?

This twist makes each decision feel a little heavier, even though the game’s pace remains brisk. It also introduces a social layer: players who hoard loot can feel tempted to protect their stash, while others may decide to stage a strategic heist that sacrifices immediate safety for long-term payoff. If you enjoy a game that blends hot potato mechanics with a cat-robbed treasure hunt, you’ll feel right at home here.

### Endgame and win conditions
The round ends when all players but one have exploded (metaphorically or literally, depending on your group’s tolerance for chaos). The surviving player collects a victory trophy in the form of bragging rights, loot cards, or a special rule that the group has agreed to adopt. As with many party games, the winner isn’t always the one who plays perfectly; sometimes it’s the person who reads the table just right, or who manages to get a few players to overcommit to a plan that collapses in glorious fashion.

## Strategy and tips (for the cunning cat burglars)

- Balance speed with planning. If you sprint through your turns, you’re likely to miss a key loot card that could swing the game in your favor.
- Read the room—social deduction is part of the chaos. If someone looks too calm, they’re probably plotting something deliciously devious.
- Use loot cards defensively. Some loot can cancel or delay attacks. Save those for when you’re about to get blindsided by a chain of draws.
- Don’t be afraid to sacrifice loot to save a larger objective. A single well-timed steal can turn a near-defeat into a victory lap.
- Pay attention to the art and iconography. The game uses visual cues to signal what cards do; learning them helps you play faster and avoid embarrassing misreads on a crucial turn.

If you’re the sort who enjoys constructing a plan that relies on reading your friends’ tells as much as the card in your hand, Cat Burglar Edition gives you a robust sandbox for developing those instincts. If, on the other hand, you prefer tight, deterministic strategies with a fixed win condition, you might find the chaos a bit much—but even then, you’ll appreciate the game’s pacing and the laughs it engenders.
