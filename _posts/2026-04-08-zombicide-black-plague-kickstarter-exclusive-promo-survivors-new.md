## Assembly, storage, and painting tips

Before you dive into the hobbyist deep end, here are a few practical tips to maximize your experience with the promo survivors:

- Assembly basics: Remove mold lines with a sharp hobby knife, wash minis in warm soapy water to remove mold release, and prime with a light coat for better paint adhesion. If the promo has multiple parts, assemble in stages and test-fit before glue to avoid awkward gaps.
- Storage: Use padded trays or foam for transport. Promo minis with unusual bases may require a custom foam insert so they don’t rub against each other during shipping or storage. A little extra care now saves a lot of frustration later.
- Painting: Start with a base coat that matches the intended color palette, then apply washes to bring out details. Dry brushing can highlight raised details on armor or cloak edges. If you’re less confident in freehand, don’t worry—these minis aren’t going to require a Renaissance-style mural; bold, readable colors work best on the battlefield lights.
- Safety first: If you’re playing with kids or clumsy adults who enjoy dramatic table flips, consider a separate, protected play surface for the minis. A small plastic tray is a small price to pay for a clean, uninterrupted game turn.
