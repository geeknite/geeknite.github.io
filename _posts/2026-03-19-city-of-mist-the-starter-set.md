## Overview
City of Mist is a game about ordinary people who carry extraordinary legends. The Starter Set is the elevator pitch: a complete, playable, narrative-heavy RPG in a box, designed to drop you into the neon-soaked alleys of a city where the impossible is normal and the normal is suspicious. The set aims to be accessible to new players while still delivering the mythic punch that fans of the core game crave. It’s not a replacement for the full rules, but it’s a terrific doorway that invites you to step through with a group of friends who are more into vibes than valkyries.

### What’s in the Box
- A compact Quickstart Rulebook
- 4 pre-generated characters, each with evocative Tags
- A mix of scene prompts and mini-scenarios
- Dice, tokens, and a fold-out city map
- A handful of ready-to-run encounters that illustrate the Tag system in action
- Printable sheets for Relationships, Clues, and Evidence

The quality of components is a step above "cheap but charming." The dice feel satisfyingly heavy; the cards have a nice thickness; the map is perfectly serviceable for a city of mist and metaphor. The art direction nails the mood: neon rain, steam, and silhouettes that would make a noir film jealous.

### The Core Mechanic Behind the Magic: Tags
In City of Mist, your character's power comes from Tags. Tags are prompts that describe who you are, what you can do, or what you symbolize in the city. When you attempt something risky, you lean on your Tags to justify outcomes. The Starter Set demonstrates this concept with pre-made Tags and prompts you can drop into a scene. The interplay between mundane identity and mythic potential is the engine of the game; the Quickstart rules give you a condensed version of this engine so you can start playing in minutes rather than hours.

### The Premade Characters
The Starter Set ships with four ready-to-play protagonists, each representing a different facet of the city: a detective who hides a superheroic secret, a scholar whose knowledge is a weapon, a crime boss who thinks they're a savior, and a street artist who paints the truth into reality. You don’t have to pick one of these, but they serve as a friendly nudge toward how to balance personal stakes with mythic power. The character sheets are clean and readable, which is essential for pickup games. They also show off the visual identity of the game—bold typography, stylized icons, and evocative "legend" prompts that spark instant roleplay.

### The City as a Character
One of City of Mist's strengths is that the city itself feels like a living character with secrets, factions, and rumors. The Starter Set includes prompts and map elements you can use to sketch out districts, neighborhoods, and recurring locations that will reappear in future sessions. The GM can seed clues that tie a character's personal legend to a city-wide mystery, creating a sense of place that matters more than any single fight sequence.

## Theme and Tone
### Mythic Noir in Practice
The "mythic noir" mix is not for everyone, but those who love it will feel at home quickly. The Starter Set does a good job of balancing mood with playability: it leans into moody lighting and concept-heavy prompts, but it doesn’t demand you to memorize dozens of charts before you roll the dice. If you’ve ever wanted a game where heroes walk past a street preacher who is a living oracle and a corporation executive who’s secretly a hydra, City of Mist will feel like home.

### The Urban Legend Angle
City of Mist thrives on the tension between what the city knows and what its citizens pretend to believe. The Starter Set emphasizes this tension through prompts that require players to reveal hidden identities at the right moment, without spoiling the fun. The city becomes part of the story’s texture, a backdrop that threatens to swallow heroes whole if they stop listening.

## Mechanics and Play
### Tag System Explained
Tags are the backbone of City of Mist. Each character has Tags that describe literally who they are and what they can do. During play, you’ll call on Tags to justify actions or to trigger special effects granted by the narrative. The Starter Set uses a streamlined approach that keeps the process intuitive. Instead of heavy math, you lean on your imagination and your ability to describe scenes that connect personal stakes with mythic power. The result is a game that feels cinematic and collaborative rather than a series of dice checks.

### Resolution and Scenes
When a scene ends, the game asks: what matters now? The outcomes are not just success or failure; they are narrative beats that push the story toward a mythic conclusion. In the Starter Set, you’ll see how a single scene can reveal a character’s legend while also nudging the city toward a bigger crisis. The emphasis on storytelling means you’ll spend less time counting successes and more time crafting the myth you want to tell.

### Player Agency and Collaboration
City of Mist invites players to co-create the story. The Starter Set encourages this by providing prompts, relationship tracks, and a flexible scene structure. It’s not a solo RPG; it’s a group exercise in mythmaking—something Geeknite loves when the group clicks.

## Components and Artwork
### Card Quality and Print
The pre-generated characters wear their art with pride, and the iconography on the cards is clear and attractive. The scarcity of overly busy art pays off; you can actually read the crucial bits without needing a magnifying glass. The dice feel good in the hand, which matters because you’ll be rolling about a billion times in a city where every corner hides a story.

### Visual Design and Layout
The layout is a win for readability and mood. When you open the Quickstart Guide, you’ll find the hierarchy of information is clear: what you can do, what your Tags mean, and what someone might want to do next. The color palette and typography reinforce the noir-no-myth vibe, and the dreamlike icons help you feel the mythic weight behind each choice.

### The Map and Props
The fold-out city map is adequate for a starter session. It’s not a full-blown campaign map, but it isn’t meant to be. It’s enough to establish the setting and give you an anchor for the scene prompts. Tokens and evidence sheets provide tactile reinforcement for players, which is something many modern RPGs forget to include.

## Accessibility and Replayability
### For Beginners
If you’ve never played a tabletop RPG, the Starter Set is a strong entry point. You won’t get lost in a sea of stat blocks or nested formulas. The rules are kept light, and the prompts are designed to spark storytelling rather than requiring you to memorize a dozen edge cases. You’ll likely feel confident building a scene after a few run-throughs.

### For Veterans
If you’ve played the full City of Mist before, the Starter Set serves as a sandbox for experiments. Try running the quick-start scenarios with a different tone, or swap in a few player-chosen mythic prompts to explore new themes. The flexibility of the Tag system means you can nudge the story into directions you wouldn’t expect.

### Replayability and Longevity
The city-as-character approach means there are countless mythologies to mine. The Starter Set gives you the scaffolding to begin, but your table will define the rest. If you’re the kind of group that loves to revisit a location again and again, you’ll enjoy building a recurring cast of legends in the same district.

## GMing Tips and Session Planning
### Quickstart Session Outline (60 minutes)
- Setup and introductions: 5 minutes
- Scene 1: The incident in District X, with a mythic prompt: 15 minutes
- Scene 2: A confrontation with a mythic figure in a back alley: 15 minutes
- Scene 3: The reveal that links the personal legend to a city-wide threat: 10 minutes
- Debrief and wrap-up: 15 minutes
The aim is to end with a strong cliffhanger that invites a follow-up session or a continuation next week.

### Scene Seeds and Plot Seeds
The Starter Set includes seeds that can be dropped into any city. You can adapt them to your group’s vibe, whether you want a darker mood, a more comedic tone, or something in-between. Use the seeds as a jumping-off point for scenes that reveal the characters’ legends while also driving toward a city-wide revelation.

### Handling Player-Led Moments
As a GM, you’ll want to stay flexible and listen to players’ ideas. The Tag system is built to accommodate improvisation; when a player says, “My Tag allows me to turn this statue into a shield,” you’ll want to lean into that and see where it leads. That’s when the mythic vibe truly shines.

## Comparisons to Core Game
### Starter Set vs Core Rules
The Starter Set is a compact, beginner-friendly introduction to the City of Mist system. It keeps the essence of the core rules while removing a lot of the heavier details that might overwhelm someone new. The core game is where you’ll find deeper mechanics, more complex city-building options, and a broader range of playstyles. If you want to explore the full potential of the system, the Starter Set is a stepping stone rather than the final destination; if you want to run a quick, story-driven session with friends who may be skeptical of complicated rules, the Starter Set is your best friend.

### Interplay with Other City of Mist Contents
Note how the Starter Set can be integrated with the core rules; you don’t have to abandon the Starter Set if you’re playing a longer campaign. It can inform your approach and provide ready-to-use prompts to bootstrap scenes.

## Final Verdict and Recommendation
If you love mythic storytelling and noir mood music, you’ll find a lot to like about City of Mist: The Starter Set. It’s approachable for newcomers, forgiving for groups that aren’t sure what they’re doing, and rich in potential for heroism and mythic drama. If you require crunchy mechanics or prefer more traditional dice-rolling, you might need to temper your expectations or pair this with the core rules.

City of Mist: The Starter Set is not a perfect product, but it is one of the most accessible entry points for a mythic-noir RPG. It gives you all the tools you need to start telling stories right away, while still offering a path to deeper exploration in the core rules. If you’re someone who wants to tell stories about legends hiding in plain sight, the Starter Set is your gateway.
