## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Longevity: How to Keep Alma Fresh

Long-term satisfaction with Alma depends on routine care. Here are practical tips to maximize life and appearance:

- Vacuum weekly with a low-to-medium suction setting. A gentle pass across the rug will remove surface dust without pulling fibers. If you notice flattening in high-traffic zones, give those areas extra attention.
- Blot stains immediately with a clean cloth. For stubborn spills, use a mild cleaner designed for synthetic fibers. Avoid aggressive scrubbing that can fray the surface.
- Rotate the rug every few months to prevent uneven wear. This is especially important for rooms with high sun exposure or extreme furniture placement changes.
- Use a quality rug pad to prevent curling edges and slide. Pads also provide extra underfoot cushioning for foot traffic and pets.

## Materials Deep Dive: Fiber Science and What It Means for You

Here’s a quick primer on why Alma feels the way it does and why you won’t be chasing a certain cleaning tool every other week:

- Fiber blend: A nylon or polypropylene mix balances softness, stain resistance, and durability. It’s not the plushest option available, but it’s designed to hold up under daily life and cleans up more easily than high-pile natural fibers.
- Pile height and density: Shorter piles are easier to clean and more resistant to crushing in high-traffic areas. A dense weave improves structural integrity, making the rug bounce back after chairs roll away or a table gets bumped.
- Backing and padding: A stable backing plus a good pad reduces movement, prevents edge curling, and supports underfoot comfort. This pairing is essential if you have slick floors or a lively household where furniture is frequently rearranged.
