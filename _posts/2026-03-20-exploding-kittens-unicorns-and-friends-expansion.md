## Introduction
Exploding Kittens has always thrived on chaos, cute art, and the kind of humor that makes you question your life choices while giggling at a kitten that looks suspiciously like your ex. The Unicorns & Friends expansion leans into the whimsy, giving fans of the base game a reason to invite their least responsible friends over again—and then again after that. In this review, we’ll break down what Unicorns & Friends adds, how it plays, and whether it’s worth your precious gaming hours. If you’ve ever wondered what happens when glitter meets existential risk, you’ve found your field study.

### Quick taste test
If the base game is a sharp cheddar slice of doom, Unicorns & Friends is a sparkly cheese puff: a distraction that somehow tastes like victory and regret at the same time. It’s the kind of expansion that makes you grin like an emoji cartoon while your friends groan at the price of a unicorn-shaped bookmark in the rulebook. The humor lands gently at first, then crescendos as the table begins to believe in unicorn diplomacy more than any real-life mediator we know.

## What’s in the Unicorns & Friends Expansion
The Unicorns & Friends expansion is not merely a deco kit; it’s a playful re-tuning of the base engine. It adds new life to the table without turning Exploding Kittens into a family-board-game-with-therapy-sessions simulator. Here’s what you’re likely to encounter.

### Components
The box promises whimsy, and it delivers with a membership card to the unicorn club: no, you didn’t join a fantasy cult, you’re playing Exploding Kittens. The expansion typically includes:
- A handful of new action cards featuring unicorns and friends
- A handsome set of Cat Cards designed to pair with unicorn shenanigans
- A handful of Unicorn-themed tokens that track “friendships” and “temporary immunity”
- A compact reference sheet that explains the new rules without turning your brain into soup
- Bonus art cards that make the board look like a festival poster rather than a war map

The exact counts vary by edition, but you should expect roughly 20-30 new cards and 5-10 tokens. The components feel sturdy and well-made; the art direction is bright, fluffy, and a little chaotic—in other words, perfect for a party night when you want chaos with a smile.

### Card Spotlight
Let’s peek at a few representative cards you’ll encounter, so you know what you’re buying a ticket to:
- Unicorn Card — The headline act. When you play a Unicorn card, you may choose to exchange a hand card with the deck’s top card or gain a one-time shield against a single Exploding Kitten draw. The moment you realize you’ve saved your own chair from disaster, you’ll feel like a cosmic magician.
- Rainbow Blast — A wild action that can be used to force all other players to draw a card or skip an action, depending on your mood and your level of mercy toward friends. It’s a dramatic tool that can reset momentum in a way that makes the table look at you like you’re a benevolent sorcerer who learned magic at summer camp.
- Friendship Token — Not a classic card, but a mechanic that lets you “link” with another player for a single turn, granting them a temporary safe zone. It’s mostly used to concoct dramatic showdowns later, when that ally forgets they’ve got your back and you’ve got their number in a not-so-nice way.
- Mischief Managed — A cheeky card that lets you swap two cards between players. It’s the expansion’s equivalent of suggesting a group costume and then handing out glitter bombs for added authenticity.
- Glitter Bomb — A fun, optional insertion that rewards players with extra points for style but punishes those who underestimate the glamour of unicorns.

The exact card texts vary with print runs, but the feel stays consistent: unicorns as benevolent mischief-makers rather than glittery chaos engines. If you’re the kind of player who enjoys dramatic reveals, you’ll find Unicorns & Friends to be in your wheelhouse.

### Gameplay Changes
The expansion doesn’t rewrite the base rules; it adds a playful overlay that intensifies social interaction. The notable shifts include:
- Unicorn mechanics bring a quasi-ally system into play. You can pair with a friendly player for a single turn, trading risk for a moment of comfort—though “comfort” in Exploding Kittens is relative and may only be a temporary truce.
- New “friendship” tokens add a lightweight alliance layer to the table dynamics. These tokens encourage bluffing, pawing for sympathy, and the occasional suspicious wink that signals a betrayal coming at turn two.
- The new Cat Cards amplify the chaos. Some require you to perform additional actions, others force opponents to reveal hands, sparking the inevitable debate about sharing is caring or sharing is just showing off your card collections.

If your group enjoys the base game’s risk-reward loop, Unicorns & Friends adds a fresh flavor without altering the core pacing. It’s quick to learn, quick to play, and quick to forget… in a good way.

### Artwork & Theme
The unicorn motif is not merely cosmetic; it threads through the entire expansion—from card borders to the box art and token shapes. The pastel palette sits comfortably beside Exploding Kittens’ pre-existing aesthetic while adding a dash of glitter that makes you want to pose for a victory photo after a win. It’s bright, sweet, and cheeky—the exact vibe a party game should aspire to when the group is fueled by snacks and questionable life choices.

The art direction also nods to internet culture without turning into a meme parade. It’s accessible, playful, and designed to spark immediate group conversation—sometimes about strategy, often about who in the room looks most ridiculous wearing a unicorn horn hat.

### Interactions with the Base Game
Compatibility is high, provided you treat the expansion as a layer of chaos rather than a replacement. If you love the base game’s tension, Unicorns & Friends amplifies it with some social dynamics that keep players engaged between draws. Here are a few practical takeaways:
- You’ll want to leverage Unicorn cards to protect players who are on the cusp of elimination, especially if they owe you a future gaming favor (which they almost certainly will forget but you’ll remember).
- The Rainbow Blast serves as a comeback mechanism—when you’re behind, it can swing momentum in your direction, turning the board into a carnival of cries and cheers.
- Introducing friend tokens encourages misdirection and negotiation. The best moments happen when two players pretend to be allies, only to realize they’ve been playing each other all along.

New players can enjoy Unicorns & Friends as a friendly entry point, while veterans can mine for deeper synergies and jokes that still land even after repeated plays.

### Setup & Play Time
- Setup: Open the base box, pull the expansion deck, shuffle both together, and lay out the tokens and reference sheet within easy reach of all players.
- Setup time: 2-5 minutes for experienced players; 5-10 minutes if you’re teaching new folks and letting them squint at the tiny text.
- Playtime: 15-30 minutes depending on player count and how aggressively people lean into unicorn-induced chaos.

If you’re teaching new players, take a moment to break down a few example turns using the unicorns as living examples. Let them feel the tension between “saving a life” and “stoking a friendly revolution” before you attempt to explain any scoring nuance.

### Strategy & Humor
- Unicorns present two modes: defensive and offensive. A unicorn shield can save you from a brutal draw, but it’s often better used to lure an opponent into a trap that will explode in their face a moment later. The subtlety matters; you’ll learn to time your protection when a cat card threatens to flip the table.
- Timing is everything with Rainbow Blast. Using it too early can feel like a tease; using it too late may leave you with an embarrassing silence as you stare at your dwindling hand.
- Friendship tokens are where social deduction comes into play. You’ll quickly notice patterns: who brags about alliances, who quietly sabotages, who tries to keep the peace while pretending not to notice the chaos unfolding around them. The fun comes from reading the table as you would a group chat after a meme goes too far.

Humor is the driving force here. The card text leans into playful absurdity, and the unicorn motif gives you something to point at when you need to deflect blame for a catastrophic bad draw. It’s lighthearted, not mean-spirited, and that balance makes it easy to enjoy even when luck is doing that cruel, cosmic joke thing where you draw three Exploding Kittens in a row.

### Value & Replayability
Expansions are a question of whether you’re after new content or new chaos. Unicorns & Friends adds a solid 20-30 cards and some tokens that deliver enough variety and social dynamics to justify a re-purchase, especially if your group rotates games on a regular basis. Here’s what you should consider:
- Solo play: Not the intended mode; the novelty is in the social banter and the chaotic saves, not in a solo puzzle.
- Group play: Best with 3-6 players. Fewer players can compress the social dynamics into a few rounds, while more players expand the chaos to a level that makes you wonder whether you’re playing a game or running a tiny improv theater.

The expansion won’t make you forget the base game, but it will remind you why you love Exploding Kittens in the first place: silly art, ridiculous turns, and that slight adrenaline rush that makes you yell something you’ll regret later in the group chat.

### Quick Setup & Start Guide
1) Remove any cards you don’t want to keep for your gaming group. 2) Shuffle the expansion with the base deck. 3) Lay out the unicorn tokens and the reference sheet in a central spot. 4) Deal the standard number of cards. 5) Decide how strict you’ll be with the alliance mechanic. 6) Start the round and pretend you’re not sweating bullets under the unicorn gaze.

Pro-tip: Start with a shorter first game to let everyone habituate to the new mechanics. If they come back for a second game, you’ve basically formed a small cult of unicorn lovers. Welcome to the club.

### Final Recommendation & Scoring
Final verdict: Unicorns & Friends is a solid, playful enhancement for Exploding Kittens that keeps the core tension intact while injecting a buoyant social layer. It’s not a must-have for every player—especially if you’re allergic to chaotic social games—but for groups that enjoy laughter, competition, and a little glitter in their life, this expansion pays off in spades.

- Fun factor: High. The humor lands frequently, and the unicorn theme remains endearing rather than grating.
- Accessibility: Easy to teach; the new rules are introduced through the reference sheet and a few example turns.
- Longevity: Good; adds enough replay value to justify a few nights of play without turning into a monster to learn.
- Value: Reasonable for what you get; you’re not paying for a completely new system, just a flavorful expansion.
