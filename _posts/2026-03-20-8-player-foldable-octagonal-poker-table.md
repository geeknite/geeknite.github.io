## Materials, Durability, and Maintenance
The table top will often be made of a laminated core with a durable felt surface on top. The edge rails are usually plastic or wood with a foam padding for comfort. The legs might be steel or alloy with anti-slip feet to keep the table stable on uneven surfaces. A well-made foldable table uses corner brackets that stay aligned after repeated folding and unfolding. The gains here are less about flash and more about the long arc of use: you want a table that will stand up to a few years of weekly games or a few dozen weekend warriors who like to shuffle cards as if auditioning for a thriller.

Maintenance is straightforward. Wipe the surface with a damp cloth after a long night and avoid soaking the surface; avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the felt or the coating. If chips or scratches appear in the wood edges, you can fill them with a designated wood filler or cover them with a table edge trim. If you spill a drink on the surface, blot immediately and avoid pushing the liquid to the seams, where it could creep into the frame. In general, treat the table with the same care you would treat a beloved gaming chair: keep it clean, keep it dry, and keep it ready for the next session.

If you want to extend the life of the table, you can use a table cover or a felt protector when the table is in storage. This is especially helpful if you have pets or small children who sometimes treat the living room like a tiny casino and a tiny tornado at the same time.
