## House rules and variants: keeping it fresh
No board game night is complete without a couple of house rules, and the NSFW edition is no exception. Here are a few ideas that work well in practice:
- The Whisper Rule: permit players to whisper a single hint about a card in their hand to one other person per round. This adds a layer of social deduction that doesn’t exist in standard editions, and it’s hilarious when misinterpreted.
- The Elevator Pitch: when a player plays a particularly spicy Da Bomb or Dare card, they must deliver a one-sentence pitch as to why their action is completely justified. Bonus points for dramatic pauses and overacting.
- Content Boundary Card: create a neutral card that marks the moment when the game crosses a line for someone. It’s a playful safety valve that reduces awkward moments and helps keep the room comfortable.
- Quick Defuse: allow players to hold onto Defuse cards from hand for an additional small privilege later in the game, such as drawing an extra card before play or swapping a card with another player. It changes late-game calculus in a satisfying way.
