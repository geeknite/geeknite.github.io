## How It Plays with Big Groups
The essence of UNO becomes more deliciously chaotic with 6-16 players. The rules remain familiar at the core: color-matching and card-number sequencing, with wild cards removing the need to match color at crucial moments. The hallmark twist of this edition lies in the extra party cards that can affect several players at once.

### Setup
- Gather players around a wide table or two coffee tables pushed together. If space is tight, you can lay the deck out in the middle of the floor as long as everyone agrees to respect the sacred shuffle.
- Shuffle the full 224 cards and deal a starting hand (number of cards per player follows house rules; most house rules would be 7 to start).
- Place the rest of the deck facedown as the draw pile; flip the top card to begin the discard pile.

### The Rules in a Nutshell
- On your turn, you must either play a card that matches the color or number of the top discard, or play a wild card.
- If you don’t have a playable card, you draw one or more cards, depending on the house rules.
- When a party card is played, follow its effect. Party cards can force one or more players to draw, swap hands, reveal cards, or alter turn order in dramatic fashion.

### House Rules and Clarifications
Because you’re playing with a large group, having a few default house rules helps:
- UNO shout rule: Does saying UNO require an actual shout, or is a friendly tap on the shoulder enough? Decide before you start.
- Stacking: Are you allowed to stack Draw Two cards or Wild Draw Four cards? Some groups love stacking; others avoid it to prevent chaos from overflowing.
- End game: What constitutes “go out” and how is the winner determined when the deck itself is huge? A simple rule is “first to get rid of all cards wins.”
