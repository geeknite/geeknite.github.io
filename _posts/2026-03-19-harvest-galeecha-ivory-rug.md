## Care, maintenance, and longevity
No rug review would be honest without a plan for care. The Harvest Galeecha Ivory Rug is not a maintenance monster, but it benefits from sensible treatment.

### Cleaning spills and stains
Act quickly with spills — a good rule of thumb is to blot rather than rub, because rubbing pushes the stain within the fibers and invites a longer struggle. For organic spills, start with a blot and a touch of cold water, followed by a mild detergent solution if needed. For tougher spots, consider a gentle rug cleaner designed for wool or a professional cleaning every couple of years if you live in a high traffic household. Energy and budget permitting, you can also consult a professional cleaning service that understands wool fibers and color retention. Quick action will help keep the ivory from showing wear and the geometric pattern from fading.

### Vacuuming cadence
Vacuuming once or twice a week in high traffic zones is a reasonable rhythm for most households. A vacuum with a gentle beater bar or a brushless setting will keep the pile from fraying. Pay attention to the edges and backing to avoid loose fibers that could unravel over time. Rotate the rug occasionally if your space has a lot of directional sunlight; this helps prevent color unevenness in areas that see more sun exposure.

### Rugs pads and protection
A non slip rug pad is a must for almost any rug placed on hard floors. It reduces bunching, evens out the surface, and protects the rug backing from friction that can lead to wear. If you have hardwood floors, look for a pad with a non-slip grip and a thickness that works with your furniture placement. In rooms with underfloor heating, ensure the pad you choose is rated for heat and is compatible with wool fibers. A good pad also helps mitigate curling at the edges, which can happen with humidity changes or heavy foot traffic.
