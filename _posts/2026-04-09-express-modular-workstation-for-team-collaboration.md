## Setup: from zero to productive in roughly three and a half coffee breaks

Setting up the Express is a straightforward dance of alignment, attachment, and a small dose of patience. The kit is optimized for three common scenarios: 2-person micro-teams, 4-person squads, and expanding open-plan configurations. Here’s a practical walkthrough:
- Lay out the core modules and establish a rough footprint. It’s less “jenga tower” and more “fleet of ships aligning at warp speed.”
- Attach rails and brackets in your preferred orientation. The modular design means you can flip things around if you realize you’re a left-handed desk dweller or if your teammate insists on a different monitor layout.
- Install the decking surfaces. Heights are adjustable within a comfortable window, which means you can switch from sitting to standing without staging a full-blown desk-replacement operation.
- Route cables and secure hardware in the back with the integrated trays and grommets. The goal is to minimize snag hazards while keeping a clean, accessible system for future rewiring or expansion.

During our test, a 4-person configuration took roughly two hours, including a brief interlude where someone declared the central monitor wall a “team sermon” and needed a quick reboot-break. In practice, it’s quite forgiving. The connectors feel sturdy, the finish resists the normal desk-grind, and the whole setup holds its own even when a couple of teammates lean in for a closer look during a design critique.

### Space planning and measurement tips
If you’re considering Express for an existing office, here are practical planning tips:
- Measure the room’s width and the door clearance. A modular system ships in pieces that need to be brought through doors; trust me on this one.
- Leave enough space for movement. You don’t want to feel like you’re stepping through a narrow gallery whenever someone needs to adjust a panel.
- Map out electrical outlets and network jacks before you place the central island. You don’t want to chase a breakout cable through a wall during your first demo.
- Plan for future growth. If you anticipate expanding beyond 6 concurrent users, think in islands and hubs rather than a single massive block.

## Ergonomics, comfort, and healthy habits

Ergonomics is the quiet backbone of any desk evaluation. The Express system offers adjustable decks and versatile monitor arms so you can align devices with your body and your tasks. A few practical tips we tested:
- Set your elbows to roughly 90 degrees and keep wrists in a natural, neutral position. The goal is comfortable typing, not a medieval keyboard-fighting pose.
- Position monitors at eye level to reduce neck strain. For multi-monitor setups, consider a monitor wall or a curved arm array to keep everything readable without craning.
- Use short standing bursts. A 15- to 20-minute standing stint every couple of hours helps with posture and digestion—yes, we’re lifting the office morale as well as the keyboard.

The Express makes micro-ergonomic improvements possible without buying a separate ergonomic chair for every team member. You get flexibility without adding another piece of furniture to the backlog.
