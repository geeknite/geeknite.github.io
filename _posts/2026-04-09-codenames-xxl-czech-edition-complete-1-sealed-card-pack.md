## Setup and rules quick guide
For those who want a refresher on how Codenames works, here is a quick map of the essential steps, focusing on what matters most when you are dealing with Go big or go home word cards.

1) Place the grid of word cards on the table and ensure all players can see them clearly. XXL helps with readability, which reduces the number of times someone shouts the wrong card because they misread a letter that looks like a slightly different letter from across the table.
2) Choose spymasters for each team. Spymasters give one-word clues that point to multiple words on the board. The rest of the team guesses the words that match the clue. The tension and the humor typically peak here as clever clues collide with stubborn word associations.
3) Start with a color determined by the key card, and alternate turns until either team hits all of their words, or the assassin word is revealed. The assassin is the dramatic mic drop; hit it and you end your team’s run, which is a memorable moment that either leads to boasting or humble pie depending on who is at the table.
4) Theme your clues to keep the pace organic. You want clues that are witty, sharp, and land well with the players. XXL makes the risk-reward dance more forgiving because legibility reduces misreads, which means you can lean into humor and creativity without sacrificing speed.
