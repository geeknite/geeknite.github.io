## What is Herd Mentality Family Edition?
Herd Mentality is a party game with a social psychology twist. Players submit answers to prompts, and then everyone votes for the most popular response. The thrill comes from predicting what the majority will pick, which somehow reveals the herd in all of us. The Family Edition preserves the core mechanic but streamlines prompts, reduces the emphasis on niche knowledge, and adds kid-friendly touches. In practice, this means you can play with your eight-year-old without fearing that the humor will crash into a wall of sarcasm or sarcasm-in-disguise.

## How to play
This section breaks down setup, the turn structure, and the core mechanic in kid-friendly terms, but with enough flavor to keep adults engaged. The Family Edition emphasizes quick rounds and social interaction rather than deep strategy or trivia mastery.

### Setup
1. Shuffle the prompt cards and place them within easy reach of everyone. 2. Give each player a handful of voting tokens. 3. Choose a judge for the first round; the role rotates to keep things fair. 4. Decide how many rounds you want to play before you start the first game so the kiddos aren’t stuck waiting for a mysterious end.

### Turn structure
On a turn, the judge draws a prompt card and reads it aloud. Everyone writes down an answer that they think the majority will pick. Answers are revealed simultaneously. The judge reads all answers aloud and selects the top picks. Players score if their answer matches the selected popular answers, and if enough players follow a single sensible, or hilariously offbeat, path.

### Variations and family rules
The Family Edition includes kid-friendly prompts and a simplified scoring track to prevent confusion for younger players. If you have toddlers or early elementary kids, consider a version where the judge explains prompts in plain language and there are fewer rounds. You can also introduce a rule where adults have to phrase their answers in a way that kids won’t only find confusing but also funny.

### The core mechanic explained
The main thrill is social inference rather than trivia knowledge. Predicting the herd dynamics is a form of performance art. The better you read the room, the more you align with the crowd, and the higher your score climbs. It becomes a dance of anticipation and group humor, where the win is less about being right and more about being liked by the table—an oddly wholesome version of social strategy.

## Strategy tips
- Read the room before you write: in many rounds, the safest move is to align with the majority if the prompt leans toward common experiences.
- Embrace the weird: some prompts reward those who take a mild risk with humor that resonates with many people.
- Short rounds, big smiles: keep momentum by avoiding overthinking; the goal is laughter and shared experience, not a perfect algorithm.
- Use prompts to invite participation: if you notice a quieter player, craft an answer that invites them to respond, creating a moment of inclusion that scores extra points.
