## Safety Considerations
Safety is not optional in the modern pool ecosystem. Always supervise children around water, install a fence or a barrier if your yard is also a playground for your neighbor's toddlers, and keep rescue equipment handy. The rectangular shape can create narrower sections that can be slippery, so non-slip mats around the pool perimeter are recommended. If you have pets, teach them that the pool is for humans first; it’s surprising how many dogs interpret a pool as a giant water bowl and try to drink the entire contents in a single gulp.

For nights or days when the pool is not in use, secure the cover to keep debris out and to deter curious critters who think the pool is a new form of bathtub with a shallow end. Always follow the manufacturer’s safety guidance; this includes drain and fill instructions, which, in practice, means you should be patient and avoid the urge to speed-run your setup with an electric drill and dreams of a time-lapse.

## Comparisons: Rectangular vs Round
If you’re on the fence about the rectangular shape, consider the use-case. Rectangles fit more neatly into backyards with space constraints and can double as a temporary event stage for children’s birthday parties, small concerts, or the occasional gaming LAN party near the pool. Round pools have the advantage of less edge and more uniform waves, which means fewer sharp corners to bump your shin on while sprinting from the deep end to the shallow end to grab a Nerf gun. Ultimately, the shape choice is a lifestyle decision: do you want more efficient space usage or more aerodynamic splashes?
