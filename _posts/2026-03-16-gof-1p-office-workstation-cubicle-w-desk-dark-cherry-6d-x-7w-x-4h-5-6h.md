## Assembly and Setup
### Getting It from Box to Brilliance
As with most modular furniture, your GOF 1P Cubicle arrives in a few flat-packed boxes that require a small amount of patience and a larger amount of swearing in a language that only a DIYer could love. Here are practical steps to minimize frustration and maximize your productivity:

1) Inventory: Lay out all components on a soft surface to avoid scratching the veneer. Check for missing screws, brackets, or panel corners. A quick inventory saves you from that dreaded moment when you realize you’re missing a key piece at hour three of assembly.
2) Tools: You’ll typically need a Phillips-head screwdriver and a rubber mallet. Some folks like to use a cordless drill with a low-torque setting, but be careful not to over-tighten—GOF hardware is designed for manual control.
3) Base and frame: Start with the base panels. Align the frames, attach cross-bracing, and secure with the provided screws. The key here is to tighten in a star pattern so you don’t skew the frame as you go. If the base feels a little wobbly, temporarily brace it with a couple of boxes to maintain alignment while you finish the next steps.
4) Desk surface: Attach the work surface using the screws provided. Don’t force the desk into place; if a hole doesn’t line up, double-check which piece is supposed to go where. Most failures come from swapping a panel that looks similar but isn’t meant for that position.
5) Panels and privacy screens: Attach vertical panels with the included brackets and ensure the panels sit flush against the desk frame. Privacy screens clip in with a satisfying little click that reminds you you are indeed making progress.
6) Cable management: Finally install grommets and cable channels as needed. Route power and data cables through the channels to avoid creating a forest of cables under the desk surface.

For first-time builders, the manual’s assembly steps are clear enough to prevent a comedy of errors. Most folks will complete the build within a couple of hours, with a comfortable break in the middle to stretch, refill coffee, and prepare for the next round of “how do I arrange my monitors?” decisions.

### Tips for a Smooth Setup
- Lay out everything on the floor before you start. A clean workspace decreases the cognitive load while assembling.
- Use a level during assembly to ensure that the surface is perfectly flat. A tiny tilt can spiral into shoulder and neck discomfort after a long day.
- Consider pre-placing a power strip or a surge protector under the desk before attaching the keyboard tray to reduce clamp points later.
- If you’re integrating a monitor arm, verify the weight limits on both the desk surface and the arm to avoid nasty bending of the veneer over time.

## Maintenance and Care
### Keeping Dark Cherry Looking Sharp
The key to keeping a dark cherry finish looking vibrant is a simple care routine. Wipe the surface with a soft, slightly damp microfiber cloth and avoid harsh solvents that could wear down the veneer over time. If you’re prone to coffee splashes or pen mishaps, a mild cleaner designed for wood laminates is usually enough to lift the scuffs without affecting the finish. Door-slam free, the GOF desk face has a smooth texture that resists oil-based grime better than rougher finishes, which means you’ll spend less time rubbing out stubborn marks and more time enjoying your productivity.

Cable channels benefit from an occasional wipe-down to remove dust that can accumulate in the small crevices. You won’t need special tools for this; a soft brush or a vacuum with a brush attachment is enough to keep the channels clear and prevent overheating from dust buildup.
