## 1st edition vs 2nd edition core differences for Josh
Let us break down the key differences you are likely to notice between the 1st and 2nd edition Josh survivor cards during a game night. The list below focuses on practical outcomes you will feel at the table rather than memorizing a handful of obscure numbers.

- Readability and typography: The 2nd edition uses larger numbers and bolder section headings. This makes it easier to scan for action costs and effect text when you are navigating a zombie scrum that has a life of its own.
- Color coding: The 2nd edition often tightens color coding for gear slots and ability types. This helps players quickly identify at a glance whether an item is a weapon, a tool, or a consumable, which speeds up decision making in tight moments.
- Iconography: Updated icons in the 2nd edition reduce the reliance on long lines of text. That means fewer players needing to lean in to read while the rest of the team is dodging a horde. If you like to run a fast table, the 2nd edition is your friend.
- Card border and art alignment: The art and border alignment in the 2nd edition tends to be crisper, with more consistent margins. The net effect is a card that feels more modern and less prone to misreads when your group is deep in the fog of war.
- Starting equipment and trait text: Some small wording changes adjust how starting equipment interacts with other cards. These tweaks can alter which combos you prioritize, especially if you like to build Josh into a particular theme such as stealthy salvager or a frontline charger.

These are not radical changes, but in Zombicide, small readability improvements can shave minutes off a game session and save you from a misplayed action that would have made your team scream louder than the zombies. If you are building a Josh-focused deck across both editions, the 2nd edition cards often feel like an upgrade that brings your hero into the modern era of card design while preserving the quirky personality that makes Josh so endearing.
