## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity
### Cleaning routine
The stain resistance claim holds up in everyday life unless you spill something truly dramatic like a red wine avalanche. For minor spills, blot gently with a clean cloth, and use a mild detergent if necessary. For more stubborn stains, you’ll want to follow an actual rug cleaning guideline rather than hoping magic will do the work. Quick tip: avoid rubbing, which tends to push stains deeper and cause the pile to fray.

### Maintenance tips
- Vacuum regularly with a beater bar on a gentle setting to avoid fuzzing the fibers.
- Rotate every few months to ensure even wear and to prevent solar fading in one corner if you have direct sunlight.
- Consider a rug pad for added cushioning and to prevent slippage.
- Protect from sunlight to avoid color fade—your red rug is busy enough with attention without turning pink after a year.

### Longevity expectations
With proper care, you’re likely looking at a rug that lasts several years with minimal attention beyond routine maintenance. If you’re the kind of person who changes your room’s vibe with the seasons, you might rotate this rug into a guest room or office to preserve its bold color in the main living area.

## Cleaning and maintenance: advanced tips
### Handling tough stains
Colorful rugs test your stain-fighting reflexes. For tough stains, you may need to test a small area with a color-safe cleaner. Always blot instead of rubbing, and use a clean white cloth to avoid transferring color. If the stain persists, consider consulting a professional service to avoid permanent damage to the pile.

### Protecting the rug over time
Rotating the rug every few months not only evens out wear and tear but also prevents hot sun from bleaching the color in one corner. A padded underlay helps reduce friction against hard floors and can improve overall foot comfort.
