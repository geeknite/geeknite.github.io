### Material and Diamonds
The core material is 14K white gold. If you want to get nerdy about it, 14K gold is 58.3 percent gold by mass, with the remainder silver and copper. This alloy strikes a balance between durability and that elegant pale sheen that happily resists the yellowing tendencies of pure gold. Diamonds on eternity stack bands vary in size, cut, color, and clarity. In vintage pieces, expect a mix that favors sparkle and restraint over modern, perfectly matched rounds. If you re chasing a brain teaser of specs, this ring s size 9.5 and the total weight imply a ring that was designed with a focus on wearing comfort and traceable, if not exact, budgeting.

### Care, maintenance, and longevity
Vintage rings deserve a little extra TLC. Cleaning is simple: a gentle soak in warm, mild soap, a soft brush, rinse, and pat dry. Avoid ultrasonic cleaners if you re unsure about the prongs or settings; vintage settings aren t always built to endure high frequency vibrations. If you plan to stack, consider a soft cloth or microfiber sleeve for your other bands to reduce friction. The 14K white gold plating, if present in the piece you re evaluating, may fade over time; however, the underlying alloy will remain durable with proper care.
