## What’s in the Fortune Row Adventure Pack

Fortune Row is designed as a compact, high-velocity addition, but it isn’t a throwaway module. It comes with a curated set of locations, factions, and a handful of compact mysteries that can be threaded into a longer arc or spun as stand-alone episodes for a single session table. The physical product emphasizes production value, with clean layout, durable card stock for the investigator cards, and evocative art that reinforces the mood of a city forever on the knife-edge between myth and traffic jam.

Key components typically include:

- A district map and location sheets for Fortune Row sites (clubs, a gallery, a tech startup hub, and a couple of underground spots).
- PC aids and investigator cards that you can drop into your ongoing characters or re-skin for new player-characters.
- A cluster of adventures and scenes: a mystery with interwoven threads, a caper, and a twist that reframes the ordinary city as a living myth.
- GM’s toolkit: encounter tables, plot hooks, and optional rules that gently push the players toward the mythos without turning the table into a lore dump.
- Faction and NPC stat blocks, with hooks for alliances and betrayals, as well as a short bestiary of “mythic consequences” that can escalate tension quickly.

The pack doesn’t pretend to replace the core book. Instead, it leans into the addictive pull of a specific area, letting the GM lean into recurring themes: corruption, secrets, and the way glamour works as currency in a city where the truth is a rumor and the rumor is a soliloquy.

## How Fortune Row Tightens the City of Mist Formula

City of Mist runs on a balance between investigation, action, and mythic rhetoric. Fortune Row tightens that formula by providing a localized sandbox where investigators can chase glamour, uncover hidden histories, and face antagonists who operate in the gray zones of power — the kind of people who own a club but also the zoning rights to a river of secrets. The Adventure Pack invites players to move from “what happened” to “what myth is this town trying to tell us?” with a more explicit emphasis on the procedural: clues, leads, and consequences. It’s not a heavy-handed rewrite; it’s a well-curated expansion that respects the game’s core identity while offering a more defined toolkit for running a city within a city.

Mechanically, the pack preserves the game’s “mythos vs. logos” tension while giving GMs a few new levers to pull: tailored glamour sources tied to Fortune Row, new masks and powers that reflect the district’s vibe, and a handful of story beats designed to keep players engaged without chasing infinite plot threads. In practice, that translates to sessions that feel meaningful, not merely episodic; players can watch their choices ripple through the city in ways that feel tangible and cinematic.

## Adventure Design: Structure, Scenes, and Flow

Fortune Row doesn’t bury the lead in a wall of lore. It provides a modular arc structure that typically follows a three-act rhythm, with hooks that can be dropped into an existing campaign or used to frame a new campaign concept. The recommended flow is something like this:

- Act I: Introduce the district, set the stakes, and hand players a moral dilemma that ties into the glamour economy. Audience: investigators who like to poke at the seams of power.
- Act II: The mystery deepens. A key location or NPC becomes a liability and a lifeline at once. The players must navigate competing factions and make a choice that reveals their own mythic alignment.
- Act III: Consequences and reveal. The mythos behind Fortune Row shifts the city’s balance, and players walk away with new long-term narratives or a clean slate for the next arc.

Within each act, Fortune Row offers: scene prompts, rival disguises, and a few red herrings that feel off-kilter enough to be memorable but not so obtuse that players will give up. The GM toolkit helps with pacing, offering templates for “cool-down” scenes and “climactic” showdowns that feel like cinematic micro-mests rather than a straight-up dungeon crawl.

In one memorable sequence, you can watch a roleplayer’s face light up as they realize that glamour is more than a power source — it’s currency, influence, and a kind of social currency that can buy you information you didn’t know you needed. Fortune Row leans into that dynamic with scenes that require both deduction and social maneuvering, which is deliciously City of Mist in vibe.

The pack emphasizes relationships over pure combat, which suits City of Mist’s emphasis on narrative leverage and dramatic flair.

## How to Run Fortune Row: GM Tips and Pitfalls to Avoid

A few best practices emerge from actually running Fortune Row:

- Start with a single focal location. Don’t try to map every street in Fortune Row on the first session. Pick one or two sites, seed the rest with hints, and let the players discover the rest through action.
- Tie glamour to player masks. The best moments happen when a reveal about someone’s mask reframes a choice the players just made. If you can orchestrate a payoff where a mask-skill unlocks a scene or a bargaining edge, you’ve nailed the tone.
- Use faction dynamics to create pressure. The “who controls the glamour economy” question should echo in every scene. If a faction feels like a dead end, you’ve lost momentum.
- Allow downtime as a weapon. In City of Mist, character development often comes from little, messy personal revelations between sessions. Fortune Row lends itself to those micro-dramas by giving you intimate scenes and personal stakes to toggle with.
- Prepare a couple of “what-if” branches. A good GM can pivot when players make unexpected choices. Fortune Row’s structure supports improvisation by offering alternative outcomes in a few ready-to-run branches.

Pitfalls include overcomplicating the mythos, turning the arc into a lore dump, or putting too much emphasis on a single “boss” figure whose defeat feels hollow if the city’s balance doesn’t shift meaningfully. The pack works best when you let the city breathe and respond to the players’ actions rather than forcing a dramatic monologue from a villain. Remember: City of Mist shines when the myth and the street collide, not when the myth gets to lecture the street for an hour.

Thematically, Fortune Row asks you to consider: what happens when the glamour economy clashes with ethical complexity, whenMask and power intersect, and when ordinary people discover they are the proof that myths exist?

For players who crave moral ambiguity and social intrigue, Fortune Row is a treat. For GM’s who enjoy a well-paced, cinematic arc with a strong sense of place, Fortune Row provides both a stage and a script that can be adapted to many ongoing campaigns. It’s not just an add-on; it’s a focused engine for stories that feel intimate and consequential at the same time.

In the pantheon of City of Mist expansions, Fortune Row sits somewhere between a well-crafted location-centric supplement and a compact campaign companion. It’s smaller than a full campaign arc but denser than a handful of one-shots. Compared to other city-focused packs, Fortune Row’s strongest card is its emphasis on narrative payoffs tied to actual locations. Other packs sometimes lean into new rules or new archetypes; Fortune Row gives you a strong sense of place and a handful of scenes that feel quotable and iconic.

That said, it doesn’t reinvent the wheel. It’s a refinement and an intensification of what makes City of Mist appealing: a neon-noir sandbox where the myths you embody at the table matter in the moment-to-moment drama. If you want more of that vibe with a tighter, district-centered scope, Fortune Row is a worthy addition. Fortune Row is a confident, thematically rich addition to City of Mist that excels at delivering a sense of place and a compact, replayable arc to slot into a current campaign or run as a standalone mini-campaign. If your table loves mythic noir, if you crave a district-focused playground with a handful of memorable scenes, and if you want to deepen your city’s mythos without wading through hundreds of pages of lore, Fortune Row earns a solid recommendation.
