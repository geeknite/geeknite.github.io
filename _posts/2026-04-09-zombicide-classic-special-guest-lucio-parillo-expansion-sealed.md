## Overview
Zombicide Classic welcomes a special guest star: Lucio Parillo. This sealed expansion from CMON adds more than just extra zombies and shiny cards; it delivers a hefty dose of narrative flair, premium artwork, and enough tactical twists to make your next zombified night feel like a blockbuster about to go off-script. If you’ve ever found yourself admiring zombie armies not only for their crunching mechanics but for their dramatic silhouettes, you’re in for a treat. Parillo’s art elevates the entire presentation—from survivor cards to the undead that threaten your safe haven—without turning the game into a fashion show. It is possible to appreciate the visuals and still ruthlessly shoot your way to victory, but here you’ll get both.

The expansion arrives sealed in CMON’s characteristic glossy packaging. The window on the box offers a teasing glimpse of the new minis and artwork, while the interior insert keeps cards, tokens, and figurines organized enough to survive a tabletop earthquake or a toddler’s accidental one-minute-mined destructo-fest. The overall quality feels premium without requiring a second mortgage to enjoy it. If you’ve chased limited editions before, you’ll recognize the familiar charm of a product that signals attention to detail without giving you two left hands in the process.

A quick note on the star of the show: Lucio Parillo’s art direction gives the survivors a sense of cinematic presence. You don’t just see heroic silhouettes; you feel like you’re peering into a moment where fate could pivot on a single decision. The set isn’t merely a color splash; it’s a curated aesthetic that invites painting, display, and, yes, a bit of heroic daydreaming during a wall-to-wall zombie assault.

### Lucio Parillo's Vision
Parillo’s work on this expansion is unmistakable. It blends high fantasy drama with the gritty, fast-paced tone of Zombicide. If you’re into art books masquerading as board games, this box is your coffee table dream; if you’re here for the board, you’ll still be satisfied by the crisp line work and readable cards that don’t force you to squint while debating which zombie is coming next.

## Tips and Tricks for a Fantastic Run
- Plan your turn order around the new survivors’ abilities. The environmental manipulation card shines when used early to carve optimal lines of attack and block potential ambush routes.
- Save trap cards for moments when the room is at the brink of collapse. Delayed triggers can turn a near-loss into a dramatic comeback.
- Coordinate equipment choices so that one survivor’s strength aligns with the team’s approach to the map layout. It’s easy to get distracted by fancy weapons; the smart teams win by synergy, not sheer firepower.
- Use the campaign’s focus on three interconnected missions to your advantage. Treat each mission as a chessboard move that preps the next session for success instead of a one-off fight.

## Solo Play and Table Talk
