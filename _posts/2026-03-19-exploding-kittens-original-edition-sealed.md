## What’s Inside the Original Edition Deck
### Card Types and Roles (H3)
The Exploding Kittens Original Edition deck is a playful medley of card types designed to sabotage, defend, and amuse. Here’s a broad brushstroke of what you’ll be dealing with:
- Exploding Kitten cards: the doom within the deck. If you draw one and don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out—kaboom, kitten confetti everywhere.
- Defuse cards: your emergency repair kit. They allow you to diffuse an exploding kitten with the least dramatic pop.
- Action/Attack cards: these spice things up. Some force the next player to draw more, others skip turns, and some let you peek into the future to plan your chaos.
- See the Future cards: you get to peek at the next few cards in the deck, which is a surprisingly powerful way to feel like a mastermind for a moment.
- Nope cards (the cheeky veto): these let you veto someone else’s move, creating glorious moments of denial and dramatic stares.
- Shuffle and Skip cards: shuffle the deck or skip your own turn, depending on how polite or ferocious you’re feeling.

The Original Edition’s art remains a defining feature. The Oatmeal’s style is not subtle; it’s a comedic badge that tells your players, “We’re here to have a ridiculous time.” This isn’t a game to forge deep strategic insights; it’s about timing, bravado, and the occasional miscalculation that becomes legendary when recounted the next day.

## How to Play (In a Nutshell)
This section is intentionally concise because the joy of Exploding Kittens is brevity—someone draws, either they’re saved by a Defuse card, or chaos ensues and someone else takes a dramatic turn. Here’s a simplified flow:

- Setup: Shuffle the deck. Each player starts with a hand of cards (the exact count varies with players, but you’ll typically draw a few to begin). Place the deck face down.
- Turns: On your turn, you play a card or pass, depending on your strategy. The most interesting moves come from attacks, defuses, and the occasional “See the Future” peek.
- Exploding Kitten: If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you have a Defuse card, you can use it to diffuse the bomb and continue playing. If you don’t have a Defuse, you’re out.
- Endgame: The last player standing wins. It’s chaotic, it’s unpredictable, and it’s often accompanied by a chorus of accusations about who set off the last catnado.

Important tips for beginners:
- Don’t be afraid to use Nope cards defensively. They’re not just for the player who’s about to win; they’re a psychological weapon that can turn the game on a dime.
- See the Future cards are powerful, but they reveal information that others can exploit. Use them when you’re ready to commit to a plan that will either delight or devastate your table.
- The box says “family-friendly,” but the logic around exploding kittens is anything but. It’s friendly to people with a good sense of humor and tolerance for table-shaking laughter.
