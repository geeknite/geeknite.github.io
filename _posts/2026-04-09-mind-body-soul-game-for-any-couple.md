## How to Play: A Simple Setup
Here is a practical, no‑frills setup you can start tonight:

1) Choose your space. A couch, a floor cushion fight, a kitchen table, or a bed—wherever you can sit close, breathe, and not trip over a charging cable.
2) Set a loose boundary. Agree on a soft limit for topics or disclosure time. Consent is sexy, even in a game. A quick phrase to kick things off: I am choosing to listen with curiosity and without judgment.
3) Shuffle the deck and place chakra tokens within easy reach. If you are gaming with a partner who also uses their own tokens, you can share or trade for a day.
4) Pick a starting color or chakra vibe. The sacral chakra is great for intimacy, playfulness, and emotional honesty. The heart chakra works wonders for vulnerability and trust.
5) Decide on a time cap. A 20‑ to 30‑minute session works well for a first run; longer sessions can be rewarding but require extra stamina and snacks.

From there, follow the prompts on the cards. Each prompt typically has three modes:

- Talk mode: a guided question or prompt that invites you to share a feeling, memory, or wish.
- Do mode: a small activity that involves touch, posture, eye contact, or a shared task.
- Reflect mode: a brief moment to acknowledge, thank, or reframe what was just shared.

A few practical tips to keep things smooth:

- Speak in the first person and use I statements. This keeps criticism out of the mix and makes it easier to own your own feelings.
- Use the timer with joy, not judgment. If the bell rings, keep it gentle and honor your pace, not your ego.
- Normalize pauses. Silence is not a crime; it’s data. Use the pause to breathe together and check in on each other’s energy.
- Embrace imperfect momentum. You will stumble; that is not a failure, it is a feature of authentic connection.
