## Maintenance, care, and longevity of the blade

Carbon steel blades require maintenance to resist rust and corrosion. Here’s a practical care plan that will help you keep your dagger in fighting shape without becoming a full-time maintenance hobby.

- Clean and dry after use: Moisture is your enemy with carbon steel. Wipe the blade down after any use, especially if you’ve used it outdoors or in humid environments. A dry cloth is your first line of defense.
- Oil regularly: Apply a light coat of mineral oil or a blade-specific protective oil to the blade surface after cleaning. This helps prevent microscopic rust pits from forming and keeps the edge from oxidizing.
- Inspect the guard and handle fit: Check that the guard remains secure and the handle scales are not loose. A loose piece is a recipe for blade misalignment during use.
- Store properly: When not in use, keep the dagger in its sheath in a dry environment. Avoid leaving it in a sheath that traps moisture against the blade for extended periods.
- Sharpen with intention: Carbon steel sharpens cleanly with a fine stone or a sharpening system. Start with a medium grit to reset the edge, then move to a finer stone for a refined edge. If you’re new to sharpening, practice on a lesser blade or a spare piece of steel first. This is not a kitchen knife; respect the edge geometry and maintain consistent angles for best results.

With care, the Honshu Crusader can maintain a respectable edge and appearance for years, which is the kind of longevity a practical blade nerd can appreciate.
