## Condition, Grading, and Authenticity
Condition is king in card collecting, and Carddas are no exception. Here is how to approach it when you pick up a No 261:
- Check corners for whitening or chipping. In pocket cards, corners are easy to bend and easy to notice.
- Inspect surface for scratches and scuffs. A mint surface makes the card pop in pocket or binder.
- Look for centering and edge wear. Carddas may have slightly off center designs, but extreme misalignment can indicate prints done in haste.
- Verify the card with known print run identifiers if you can, but be aware that some very rare cards do not have widely published identifiers.
- Compare with reliable community photos. The card community often has image galleries that let you verify your card against chips of the same print run.

Counterfeit awareness is part of the game. If you suspect a knockoff, try to compare the card against known print run variations and consult trusted collectors. Remember that a real No 261 is not just a piece of plastic; it is a memory encoded in pigment and a story you share with others in the hobby.

## Display and Storage
Holding a No 261 card is not just about possession; it is about display as well. Here are practical tips:
- Use acid free sleeves that are sized for standard Carddas cards. They protect from oils and environment without harming the surface.
- Store in a binder with a sturdy sleeve or in a protective slip case. Avoid stacking under heavy objects that could bend the card.
- Keep away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade. The bright DBZ color scheme looks better when the hues stay vibrant.
- Consider a UV protective display case if you plan to show off the card at conventions or meetups. A well lit display case can turn a simple card into a mini work of art.

## Collecting Tips and Pitfalls
Here are some practical tips to help you navigate the No 261 market:
- Start with a baseline of near mint or mint and work your way toward pristine copies if budget allows.
- Watch for misprints and color wash anomalies; rare prints often carry unique characteristics that set them apart from standard copies.
- Compare prices across reputable marketplaces and community groups. Do not be surprised if you see wildly different numbers in different regions; shipping and listing fees can influence price perception.
- Beware of resellers that offer steep discounts on obviously rare cards; if something sounds too good to be true, it probably is.
