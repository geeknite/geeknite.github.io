## Maintenance and Cleaning Tips
### Routine Cleaning
Vacuuming is your best friend here. Use a standard vacuum, preferably with a beater bar set to an appropriate height so you don’t over-drag fibers. Empty the canister or bag regularly to prevent dust buildup that can dull the texture. Quick weekly sessions will keep the fibers crisp and the pattern legible, even after the kids build a fortress out of cushions in the living room.

### Spills and Stains
Spills happen. For most household accidents, blot the area gently with a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb as much liquid as possible. Then clean with a mild detergent solution and rinse if needed. Avoid harsh bleach or solvent-based cleaners that can degrade color or leave a halo where you cleaned. If you’re dealing with a stubborn stain, treat it as you would a stubborn boss in a video game: with a patient, methodical approach, and maybe a little patience.

### Deep Cleaning and Refresh
Periodically, you may want to deep clean or refresh the fibers. Some folks opt for a gentle machine wash if the rug’s care instructions permit it. If you’re uncertain, a professional cleaning service can give you a safe, thorough refresh. A refresh keeps the patterns from fading, the fibers from looking threadbare, and the overall vibe from slipping into “well-loved comfy disaster” territory.
