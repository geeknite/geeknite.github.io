Unboxing and Setup
------------------

Brand new CAH packs feel like a treasure map in your hands. The crisp edges of the cards, the slight scent of fresh cardstock, and that satisfying crackle as you peel away the plastic wrap. This is the tactile joy that fans of tabletop games crave. In each expansion you typically find a large stack of white cards and black prompts, which will be used by players to construct outrageous responses with maximum impact. The packaging is standard CAH: compact, efficient, and designed to withstand the chaos of a night where the box will likely be used as a prop in the photos you post the next day.

Before you begin, a few setup tips that help your first session go smoother:

- Create a comfortable space: CAH nights can be loud and boisterous. Make sure there is room to spread out, a table to hold the decks, and a place for players to set down the cards they re not currently using.
- Organize your cards: With three expansions, you will accumulate a lot of white cards. Sort them by theme or simply into a pile that is easy to shuffle. If you are worried about wear, consider sleeves to keep everything mint for longer.
- Establish ground rules: The humor in CAH can be biting. For some groups, a light consent guideline is essential. A simple approach is to declare that if a prompt or card crosses a line that feels personal, the card is set aside and the group moves forward with a different prompt.
