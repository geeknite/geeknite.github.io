## Care and Maintenance: Keeping Cliffwood Green Looking Sharp

Wool is forgiving but not invincible. Regular vacuuming with a soft brush (no aggressive beaters) helps prevent fuzz and pile compression. Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean, white cloth to absorb liquid rather than rub in the stain, which only makes matters worse. For tougher stains, a wool-safe cleaner or a mild detergent solution can be dabbed gently on the area—test on a small patch first to ensure color retention.

Rotation every few months is a sneaky great habit. It promotes even wear and helps you notice if any foot traffic patterns form—like a stealth route your cat uses to claim the sunbeam throne. Using a quality rug pad beneath the rug reduces movement, protects floors, and adds a touch of cushion that makes standing at your standing desk feel a little more like walking on a cloud while saving your ankles from the “office chair wheel-meets-rug” roulette.

Sunlight can gradually lighten colors, so consider UV-blocking drapes for rooms that get a lot of sun. The good news is that Cliffwood Green tends to ages gracefully, becoming a little richer and more nuanced as the years roll by.
