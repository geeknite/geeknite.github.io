## Assembly Experience: The Good, The Bad, and The Hex Allen Keys
### Unboxing and initial impressions
Unboxing is a ritual. The pieces come in clearly labeled boxes, but you’ll still spend a good chunk of time patting, aligning, and swearing to the whittle-down instructions. The good news: the pieces are modular, which means you can adapt to room shape and doorways without needing a structural engineer or a small crane.

### Assembly timeline
- Phase 1: Core frame assembly (2–3 people, ~45–60 minutes).
- Phase 2: Central spine and screens (er, a little more patience, ~30–45 minutes).
- Phase 3: Cable management and final alignment (20–30 minutes).

Pro tip: secure the frame loosely first, then tighten after you’ve confirmed all alignment points. It saves you from the “let’s pretend we know what we’re doing” panic at 3 a.m. on a Tuesday.

### Common pain points (and how to dodge them)
- Alignment headaches: Use a laser level or a trusted hotel‑level app to verify corners are square before locking screws.
- Screen height: If your team is a mix of short and tall folks, plan for adjustable screen heights or adopt an alternate seat height for certain users.
- Cable chaos: Start with a dedicated run for power, then add data lines. Label cables with color-coded tags to avoid the “which wire goes where?” game.

### Aesthetics post‑assembly
Once assembled, the piece looks less like a product and more like a purpose-built collaborative machine. The privacy screens do a decent job of dampening visual noise while still letting light through, something that’s crucial in a shared space where sunlight is a finite resource.

## Cable Management, Power, and Connectivity: Keeping the Flow Clean
### Power rails and data paths
The built-in power rail is a real boon in crowded spaces. It minimizes countertop clutter by providing centralized access to outlets and USB‑C pass‑throughs.
