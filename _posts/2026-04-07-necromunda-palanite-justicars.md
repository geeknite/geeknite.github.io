## Who are the Palanite Justicars?

In the grimy, fluorescent-lit corridors of Necromunda, the Palanite Justicars are the enforcement arm of the Palanite Enforcers. Think Adeptus Arbites meets a district attorney who brought a power hammer to a board meeting. They operate as a mobile, disciplined squad tasked with investigating crimes, rounding up troublemakers, and maintaining a sense of order in a place where the only thing more dangerous than a ganger is a bad decision made under pressure.

Fluff-wise, they don't chase warlords so much as paperwork. They carry out arrests, issue warrants, and – when absolutely necessary – shoot a bolt pistol with a righteous fury that would make any librarian proud. Their presence on the board adds a very specific flavor to Necromunda games: the orderly, procedural side of the law clashing with the hive's chaotic, greasy reality. Your gang might be trying to bash its way to the loot, and the Justicars will be there to remind everyone that there are rules. And that someone has to file the report afterwards.

## In-game rules and playstyle

When you assemble a Palanite Justicar squad, you are signing up for a very particular playstyle: methodical, disciplined, and with a strong sense of jurisdiction. They tend to operate best as a mobile objective-secure unit that can project moderate firepower while controlling the pace of the game through discipline-themed rules. In Necromunda, a good Justicar player will use range firepower to suppress threats while keeping their own fighters in good positions to pounce when a target is flagged or an objective becomes contested.

The core appeal of Palanite Justicars is their thematic value and the way they force you to think about action economy. They are not necessarily the fastest or most brutally efficient fighters in the Underhive, but they make up for it with reliable shooting, solid leadership, and a deep flirtation with the concept of lawful restraint. If your opponent has a lot of mobility or melee threats, the Justicars offer a counterplay option: use cover, control space, and compel the enemy to move into your preferred firing arcs.

### Common loadouts and how they feel on the table

- Mid-range specialists: Autogun with a bolt pistol for backup, supported by a Justicar with a short melee option. This build gives you a credible shooting platform and a flexible melee reach for when someone tries to close the gap.
- Defensive dogs of the law: A heavier melee option, like a baton or a light power weapon, paired with good armor and a leadership role. This is the chunk of the squad you want near a contested objective to deter reckless charge attempts.
- For the roleplay crowd: A pair of Justicars with dramatized inscriptions and minimal extra gear, so you can narrate your enforcement arc with flair while still keeping your budget in check.

The rules are designed to reward careful positioning and crisp use of line of sight. You’ll want to maximize cover and use the gang’s discipline to maintain a stable firing lane while your more aggressive gangs try to bluff their way into your lines. It is a chess match with gunmetal and grit, and there are a lot of moments where a single well-placed shot or a strategic retreat can flip a game from a scrape to a victory.

For those who enjoy table-flipping synergy, Palanite Justicars pair nicely with house-specific rules that reward control and defensive play. If your campaign emphasizes holding ground, policing districts, or investigating crimes, the Justicars are a natural thematic fit. If your campaign rewards sack-the-scarcity-bullets-all-day, they’ll still hold their own but you’ll want to supplement them with more aggressive, high-odd units.

## Painting and modeling tips

A good Palanite paint job should emphasize their law-enforcement vibe without turning them into a sterile police show. Here are practical ideas that help you achieve that look without wringing every pigment out of your brush.

### Quick color theory and palette

- Armor: Cold steel or gunmetal base with blue-gray shading. The aim is something that reads as practical armor, not ceremonial platemail. A blue-gray shade in the recesses gives you depth without looking dirty, which is important when you want them to feel like functional law enforcers rather than space-bouncers with a badge.
- Trim and insignia: Brass or gold trim with a bright enamel highlight. It catches the light nicely and reads as official insignia on the battlefield. It also provides a nice contrast against the darker armor.
- Fabric and leather: Deep black or leather brown, with weathering to imply long shifts and frequent kneeling to report on crimes in the hive. Leather tones work well with the brass trim and provide a nice natural contrast.
- Accents: Red or amber hazard stripes on shoulder pads and a small emblem of the law. This helps with quick identification during games and adds a bit of personality to each model.

### Step-by-step painting workflow

1) Basecoat the armor with a mid-tone gray, then apply a blue-gray wash to bring out the depth in the recesses.  
2) Paint the trim in brass/gold and pick out insignia with a bright color.  
3) Highlight edges with a lighter gray and a touch of white on the most raised edges.  
4) Paint the weapons in a consistent metal or dark gunmetal, adding a few chips and scratches along the edges for realism.  
5) Add the leather fabrics with a deep brown and highlight with lighter brown to bring out seams and folds.  
6) Apply a reach of weathering powder to simulate dust, grime, and hive fallout to tie everything together.  
7) Finish with a matte varnish to keep your work from shining like a siren in a neon alley.

### Freehand insignia and banner ideas

If you want to push the theme further, consider a small freehand insignia on the shoulder pad or a banner that reads a short legal maxim. You might do something witty here like a small icon that signals jurisdiction or law enforcement, but not so much that it reads as a bumper sticker from a spaceport. The goal is to create a recognizably Palanite look without making the models feel like a marching parade.

### Quick conversions for flair

If you are a convert-friendly hobbyist, the Palanite Justicars tolerate a bit of creativity. You can swap weapons to reflect different enforcement roles, add a small data-pad or arrest warrant frame on one model, or re-position an arm for a more dynamic arrest pose. The flexibility in the kit is a nice surprise and makes the squad a good candidate for fast conversions, especially if you want to narrate a dramatic capture scene on the tabletop.

## Fluff and campaign integration

The Palanite Justicars are not just a pretty face for your display shelf. They have a distinct narrative flavor that can drive campaigns and quests beyond the standard loot-focused games. Their presence in a campaign can be used as a plot hook, a catalyst for a grand law and order arc, or even a reason to split your gangs along ideological lines. Here are some campaign ideas and hooks to help you weave them into your Necromunda sessions without feeling like you are shoehorning them into random scenarios.

### Campaign hooks and narrative ideas

- Case of the Missing Hydroponics: A district’s hydroponics supply goes missing, and the Palanite Justicars lead a cross-district investigation to trace the supply chain. Gangs that rely on water-rich resources become suspects, and you have multiple factions vying for control of the case.
- The Arrest Warrant Gambit: A high-profile gang leader has a standing arrest warrant, but the Justicars cannot capture him without causing a riot. The campaign revolves around building the legal case, collecting evidence, and planning a precise, surgical capture.
- The Bureaucrat’s Web: An old bureaucrat in the district office orchestrates a plan to use the Justicars as pawns in a larger scheme. The players have to navigate a maze of red tape, false reports, and shifting loyalties to ensure justice or to expose corruption.

### How to incorporate them with other houses

Palanite Justicars work well with other houses that appreciate a slower, more calculated approach to the board. If your table has a mix of fast strike gangs like Goliaths or Orlocks, the Justicars provide a counterpoint by controlling space and forcing your opponent to respect objectives rather than simply smash their way through. They also pair nicely with a house that values stealth and infiltration, where the Justicars can provide reconnaissance and surveillance support that helps your team strike its own objectives before your opponents have a chance to respond.

## Scenarios and missions ready for the Palanite touch

If you want ready-to-run content, you can borrow some of the Palanite flavor for your own campaigns. Here are a handful of scenario ideas that play to the strengths of a disciplined squad like the Justicars:

- The Legal Challenge: A contested area requires the Justicars to hold ground for a set number of turns while other gangs attempt to break through. The reward is not just loot but a case file that can grant you a campaign advantage.
- The Rally to Jurisdiction: The Justicars must escort a group of essential civilians or a legal document convoy through a dangerous zone protected by rival gangs. You win by keeping the convoy safe and delivering it to a courthouse objective on the map.
- The Warrant Hunt: The Justicars receive a region-wide warrant; your goal is to locate the target and bring them in for question. It demands careful reading of the board, careful pacing, and some cunning micro-decisions.
