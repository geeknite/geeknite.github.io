## Care, display, and preservation

- Storage: keep cards in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The glare of a sunlit window can fade blues faster than you can say Blue Marvel. Use archival sleeves and an appropriate binder with acid-free pages to keep edges crisp.
- Handling: wash hands or wear cotton gloves when you handle the card to prevent oils from your fingers from dulling the surface over time. This is part of the ritual that makes you feel like a genuine collector instead of a casual window shopper.
- Display: a simple acrylic stand or a small shadow box can showcase the Mind Games card without burying it behind glass. The 90s vibe benefits from protective display options that let you see the art while keeping it safe from cat scratches and accidental coffee spills.
- Environment: avoid high humidity and extreme temperature fluctuations. The card’s color vibrancy is happy in a stable climate, not in a fighting game where a rogue thermostat becomes the villain.

## How to verify authenticity

Authenticity is the friend of the collector; fakery is the friend of the scammer. Here are some quick checks you can do if you find a Mind Games card at a show or in a pile of thrift-store treasures:

- Check corners and edges: original cards usually have clean, crisp edges with uniform rounding. A sharp misalignment or an odd edge bevel may indicate a reprint or counterfeit.
- Examine print quality: the 1990s print world had a distinctive texture and color saturation. If the card looks too shiny or the colors appear off in suspicious ways, take a closer look.
- Compare to known references: study known authentic examples from reputable sources and look for subtle differences in border width, font weight, and back-of-card layout.
- Consider packaging: rare or foil variants should be corroborated by provenance. If you found a foil Mind Games card in a limit-admitted lot, there should be other indicators of authenticity.

If you intend to invest or complete a set, you might want to consider professional grading for the truly precious pieces. It might cost a little upfront, but it can yield a safer long-run value and a cleaner display, both of which are worth their weight in bubblegum and nostalgia.
