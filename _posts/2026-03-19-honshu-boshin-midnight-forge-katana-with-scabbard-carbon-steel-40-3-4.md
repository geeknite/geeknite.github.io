## Scabbard and Maintenance: Keeping the Midnight Glow
### Scabbard practicality
The scabbard is a functional companion to the blade. It’s designed to protect the blade from moisture and surface damage while giving you a secure draw. A well-fitting saya reduces the risk of accidental snagging or catching on fabric and gear, which is exactly what you want when you’re maneuvering around a crowded dojo or a cosplay booth. The lacquered finish offers a degree of moisture resistance, but you should still wipe the blade after use and store it with a light coating of oil to prevent rust and pitting.

### Maintenance routine you can actually keep
Carbon steel requires a simple maintenance ritual that, frankly, makes you feel like a responsible adult. After every practice, wipe the blade clean, apply a light coat of mineral oil or specialized sword oil, and store it in a dry environment. Avoid exposing the blade to humidity or corrosive solvents, which is a fancy way of saying don’t leave this blade in a humid bathroom or an unventilated closet in the middle of a tropical storm. Sharpening, if needed, should be done with care—start with a basic stones-and-oil approach and progress to more advanced methods as you gain confidence. The goal is to maintain a sharp edge without removing too much material or introducing micro-wobbles along the edge. If you’re new to maintenance, consider a lesson or a guided sharpening kit that’s appropriate for carbon steel blades.

### Safety reminders
This katana is a tool for sport and display, not a toy. Treat it with the respect you’d give a high-end kitchen knife and a respectful approach to any weapon. Always use a proper training surface, wear protective gear as needed, and ensure you have ample space around you so you don’t accidentally behead your own bookshelf or the neighbor’s fancy flamingo statue. If you’re new to swords, seek instruction from a qualified professional or join a reputable dojo that emphasizes safety fundamentals. Remember, a blade this long is a project in the making, not a quick fix for weekend warrior vibes.
