## How to Use the Expansion during Gameplay

This expansion folds neatly into the core Dixit experience. You don’t have to overhaul your rules; you simply add the 84 new cards to your existing Dixit deck and let the storytelling magic happen. Here are practical ways to deploy the expansion to maximize fun:

### 1) Freshen up storytelling rounds
With 84 new cards, every round can feel like a mini-art gallery stroll. The clue-giver has more material to pull from, and the guessers are faced with fresh imagery that defies predictable interpretations. The risk is that some cards might be a touch more ambiguous than the base set, but that’s part of the charm. The best clues will straddle the line between obvious enough to be guessed and obscure enough to be debated for minutes after the round ends.

### 2) Theme nights and narrative arcs
If you’re into long-form storytelling, you can choreograph a theme night (e.g., “Dreams of a Quantum Bakery” or “Intergalactic Tea Party”). Use a subset of the 84 cards to avoid analysis paralysis, then craft a narrative arc by weaving clues that connect across rounds. You’ll end up with a shared story that makes less sense than a fever dream but more memorable than any night you spent arguing about who left the fridge open.

### 3) Variants and house rules
Some groups like to mix expansions with different rule tweaks. A popular approach is to require the clue-giver to provide a one-sentence story that must reference at least two different cards from the expansion. It’s a delicious constraint that pushes players to think outside the box and collaborate in surprising ways. If you’re feeling spicy, add a “wild card” card from the base deck that can be used as a universal clue—just be careful not to unleash chaos that makes the entire table throw their hands up in surrender.

## Custom Scenarios, House Rules, and Strategy Notes

Here are a few quick ideas to get more mileage from the expansion:

- Thematic Shuffles: Create a mini-event where each round must adhere to a theme (e.g., “Nostalgic 90s Tech” or “Mythical Marketplace”). The cards are the inspiration; the clues are the challenge.
- Clue Confidence Meter: After each round, players rate how confident they were about their own clue. This adds a playful meta-game about storytelling style and self-awareness.
- Partner Clue Day: Pair up players and have the clue-giver describe their card as if they’re collaborating with a teammate. The partner must guess which card was chosen while the rest of the table listens for the story’s co-authored vibe.
- The Quiet Round: For one round, the clue-giver must say nothing but a single emoji. The guessing players must interpret the emoji in the context of the new cards. This is surprisingly challenging and incredibly funny when someone tries to explain a potato emoji as “existential hero.”
