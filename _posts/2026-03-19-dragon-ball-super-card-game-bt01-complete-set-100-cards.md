## Deep dive: mechanics and strategic building with BT01
### Core mechanics introduced by BT01
BT01 emphasizes three core mechanics that recur throughout DBS: life management, energy efficiency, and card advantage. You’ll become comfortable with the rhythm of tapping energy to cast spells, trading one card for several advantages on the board, and sequencing your moves to threaten a win while denying your opponent the chance to respond in time.

### Leader cards: your on-board plan and personality
Leaders define your deck’s trajectory. They act as the face of your strategy, often providing a unique effect that can set the tempo of a game. Some Leaders reward aggressive acceleration, while others require patience and careful resource management. Learning a Leader’s timing is half the art of playing BT01 well.

### Battle cards: offense, defense, and everything in between
Battle Cards are your primary weapons, blockers, and sometimes your best answers to opposing threats. The BT01 set variety lets you mix early pressure with mid-game stabilization, and occasionally you’ll stumble into a card that unexpectedly wins a game with a single, well-timed attack—or a perfectly timed counterspell of your opponent’s plan.

### Resource management: energy and life as currencies
In DBS, energy and life are currencies you spend to perform actions. BT01 helps illustrate the cost curves: some of the most powerful combos require you to juggle energy from early turns and preserve life to avoid a late-game collapse. The challenge is to find the sweet spot where you maximize output without letting your opponent run away with the momentum.

### Building archetypes from BT01
- Aggro/Tempo: fast starts designed to overwhelm opponents before they can stabilize.
- Midrange control: a balanced approach emphasizing card advantage and answers, trading one-for-one with occasional explosive plays.
- Combo-based: a discipline that aims to trigger a victory condition through a sequence of precise plays. It’s satisfying when it comes together and brutal when it fails due to a misstep or a perfect counter from your opponent.

### Practical deck construction tips
- Start with a coherent core: pick a Leader and 8–12 Battle Cards that serve a consistent game plan.
- Fill gaps with flexible tools: add 4–6 removal and 4–6 cards that can adapt to several matchups.
- Don’t go all-in on foils or showy cards. A well-tuned, budget-friendly BT01 deck can outperform a shiny but poorly integrated one.
- Protect your key pieces: sleeves, binders, and a tidy play area reduce the cognitive load during stressful moments.

## Glossary of terms (BT01 edition)
- Leader Card: The deck’s figurehead, dictating some of your early game plans.
- Battle Card: The tool you use to attack, defend, and manipulate tempo.
- Energy: The resource you spend to play cards. Mastery of energy is the difference between a loud plan and a well-timed shutdown.
- Token: Small, often non-card representations of effects or counters that matter in timing and strategy.
- Foil: A shiny variant that catches the eye and can cause jealousy, awe, or both in your playgroup.
