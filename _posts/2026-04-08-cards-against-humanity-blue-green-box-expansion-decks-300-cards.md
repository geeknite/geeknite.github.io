## What exactly are these Blue Box and Green Box expansions?

The Blue Box and Green Box are not mysterious cult artifacts hidden behind a secret door in a thrift store. They are two separate 300 card expansions for Cards Against Humanity designed to blend with the base game and with each other. Each expansion adds a large stash of new prompts for the black cards and a fresh crop of possible punchlines for the white cards. The exact balance between white and black cards may vary, but the intent is clear: more prompts, more responses, and more opportunities to watch your party devolve into chaotic laughter or embarrassed silence, depending on the crowd.

Blue Box versus Green Box is less a philosophical debate and more a matter of vibe. The Blue Box leans into pop culture references, current events, and edgy humor that will hit well with a media-saturated audience. The Green Box tends to skew toward absurd, surreal, and offbeat humor that can topple the sanity of even the most jaded players. Put simply, Blue Box is your culturally tuned guitar solo, Green Box is your experimental improv piece that might end with a smoke machine and a confused cat.

### Inside each 300 card deck
Each box advertises 300 cards and delivers a mixture of new black prompts and white answer cards. The exact composition can vary by print run, but you can expect a healthy ratio of prompts that ask players to fill in blanks and prompts that skew toward bold, provocative, or delightfully awkward situations. The cards are designed to slot into your existing base game without requiring you to shuffle everything into a single pile. In practice, you can mix the expansions with your current set and the base game so your rounds have a bigger risk and bigger reward — or at least bigger cringe when your friends pick the card you secretly regret writing on a Tuesday afternoon.

## How to play with the expansions in practice

### Mixing with the base game
Mixing expansions with the base Cards Against Humanity deck is straightforward. The general approach is to blend and shuffle, then draw from the combined pool. Some groups prefer to pull a small number of cards from the expansions to avoid overwhelming new players with too much material at once. Others go full send and replace the base cards after a certain number of rounds so the table feels fresh even for veteran players.

If your group loves themed games, you can use Blue Box for one night and Green Box for another so your players experience two distinct moods. This approach keeps the game nights varied and helps you avoid the fatigue that can set in when the same handful of prompts returns again and again.

### House rules and moderation
Because CAH can veer into territory that some players find unpalatable, a few light house rules can help the night stay fun rather than accidentally toxic. A few ideas that worked well in our testing sessions:
- Establish a quick consent check for players who want to opt out of particularly edgy prompts. This can be as simple as a whiteboard vote or a thumbs up/thumbs down rule at the start.
- Use a rotation system for judges so no single player bears the burden for the entire night. A rotating host can keep energy high and ensure everyone gets equal opportunities to shine.
- Create a quick ban list of cards that the group has decided to skip for personal or professional reasons. This keeps humor inclusive and ensures the game stays comfortable for everyone involved.
- Try a speed round variant where prompts are read faster and players have a limited time to submit a response. This adds energy and helps mitigate the monotony of long, drawn-out rounds.

### Quick tips to maximize the experience
- Separate the deck by mood: keep Blue Box prompts in one stack and Green Box prompts in another. When you want a more upbeat night, pull from Blue Box; for a WTF vibe, pull from Green Box.
- Use a timer for each round to keep things moving. A fast pace makes even cringe-worthy prompts land with a punchline instead of hanging in the air like a bad joke you regret telling three days later.
- Bring a few friend groups together. The best CAH nights happen when you can pivot humor from one group to another; what is cringe to one person may be pure gold to another.
- Keep a sense of humor about the inevitable repetition. The expansions bring many new cards, but you will still encounter the occasional old favorite resurfacing. Embrace the chaos and keep the energy up.
