## How to play a quick refresher (for seasoned players and first-timers alike)
Sushi Go! is a drafting game where you pick one card from your hand and pass the rest to the next player. Each round occurs in a loop until all cards are gone, then you tally points for set collections and special combos. The rules are famously simple, which is part of the charm: you can explain the core loop in under two minutes, after which the table erupts into a chorus of “Wait, what counts as a set again?” and “I’m going for the pudding card this time.”

- Draft phase: Everyone looks at their hand, selects a card, and passes the rest to the left. You do this simultaneously, so there’s a moment of frantic glancing and micro-psychology as you try to predict what your neighbor needs.
- Scoring phase: After all rounds (usually three to four, depending on player count), you count points from sets like Nigiri, Sashimi, Dumplings, Maki Rolls, and Puddings. The Special Edition may reweight some scoring, but the core is still about picking the most valuable tiny plate combos while denying others access to their ideal ones.
- Endgame: The pudding card is a quirky endgame tiebreaker—if you’ve never cared about pudding, you probably haven’t fallen in love with a game that rewards you for finishing strong with a dessert that doesn’t exist in the real world. The endgame scoring is fast and cinematic, which makes the last few rounds feel like a tiny sports finale at a sushi bar.

## Strategy and depth: how to angle your plates
Here are some opportunistic tips you’ll likely employ once you’re comfortable with the flow of the game:

- Read the table, not just your hand. The moment you glimpse that your neighbor is after a conflicting pork nan—uh, nigiri line, you have a chance to pivot into a different combo that still scores well but blocks their plan.
- Favor flexible cards early. You want choices that keep your options open as the hand you pass around changes. In the Special Edition, the new promo cards sometimes offer swingy scoring that rewards you for planning multiple turns ahead.
- Don’t forget pudding scoring. It’s a quiet, sneaky endgame power-up that can flip a second-place finish into a one-point victory if you’ve kept a pudding card unscored until the right moment.
- Balance speed with calculation. The thrill of Sushi Go is in deciding when to commit to a set and when to keep your options open. In high player counts, speed matters as much as accuracy; the Special Edition’s improved visuals help you parse the table faster, which is a surprising advantage when you’re in a race to the final round.
