## Maintenance: Keeping the Table in Prime Form
Foosball is a sport that loves to show up in new homes with a little bit of dust and a lot of fingerprints. A quick wipe-down after a session will do wonders for longevity. The playing surface should be kept clean to avoid any grit that might alter the ball’s path. The rods and handles benefit from the occasional light oiling or silicone-based spray to keep them from squeaking during dramatic saves. This is not the moment for WD-40 shenanigans; remember, you are playing foosball, not rebuilding a vintage motorcycle.

Check the leg attachments periodically. If any wobble creeps in, retighten with the included hardware. A stable table turns chaotic chaos into choreographed chaos, which is exactly the vibe you want for the grand finale of a family tournament. If you’re storing the table long-term, ensure the surface is clean and dry to prevent warping or mold in humid climates. Basic maintenance is a small price to pay for long, laughter-filled evenings with friends and relatives who suddenly realize they had a hidden talent for “kick save” and “bank shot into family glory.”
