## Assembly and maintenance tips

Assembling two workstations in one frame is not a daily sport, but it’s not a myth either. Here are practical tips to avoid the “I built a shelf, not a desk” moment:

- Unpack everything and lay out parts first. The instruction manual reads like a treasure map, and you’ll thank yourself later for not mixing bolts from the coffee table-era with those from the desk era.
- Use a magnetic tray to keep small pieces from disappearing into the carpet’s black hole. You’ll thank me when you don’t spend twenty minutes searching for a rogue washer.
- Tighten bolts in a star pattern to distribute pressure evenly. Over-tightening can warp the surface and ruin your perfectly flat primary workstation.
- Route cables through the built-in channels and secure with the provided covers. If you don’t, you’ll have a cable spaghetti monster that resembles a techno-organic sculpture.
- Periodic maintenance: wipe with a microfiber cloth to remove dust and coffee splashes. Avoid harsh cleaners on the laminate; a mild solution works best to preserve the finish.

### Quick assembly checklists
- Confirm all hardware is present and identify the left/right sides before you begin.
- Pre-assemble monitor arms off the desk if possible to avoid fighting gravity while balancing two monitors.
- If your room has a bright window, consider glare management; angle the monitors to minimize reflective hotspots on your eyes.
