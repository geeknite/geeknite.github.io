## Installation, Use, and Care Tips
Rolling out a rug should be a ceremony, not a chore. Here are some practical tips I found helpful during the test runs:

- Prepare theSpace: clear the area of ends of furniture and ensure the backing sits flat with the pile direction aligned properly.
- Unroll and Settle: give it a day or two for any wrinkles to relax; in a busy living room, you might not notice a small curl until after vacuuming several times.
- Vacuuming Routine: for the first week, vacuum it every few days to lift loose fibers and help the color settle in. Use a gentle setting and avoid aggressive suction on the edges to prevent fraying.
- Stain Response: Blot with mild detergent and water for most spills. For tougher stains, test a small area first and consider a professional cleaning if needed.
- Kids and Pets: assign a dedicated play zone with a rug that’s easy to wash; the desert palette helps camouflage occasional cereal crunches and muddy paw prints.
- Rotation: rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear if you have heavy traffic in one corner.
