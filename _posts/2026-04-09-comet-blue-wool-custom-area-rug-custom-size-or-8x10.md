## Materials, Craft, and Care

### Wool quality and texture

The rug is described as 100 wool in most product sheets. That means a fiber with natural crush resistance and a tendency to look better with a little wear. A high-quality wool rug should feel soft to the touch, with a resilience that makes your feet forget the cold tile life. Expect a medium to medium-high pile, enough to cushion but not so tall that you’re auditioning for a carpet championship. The surface should feel plush under bare feet, yet not trap every crumb like a black hole in a galaxy far, far away.

### Construction notes that matter

Whether hand-tufted or machine-loomed, the method affects durability and feel. A wool rug tends to shed a little at first while the fibers settle into their place, then stops. Don’t panic if a few loose fibers show up in the first week—this is common with new wool. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting should keep surface fibers aligned without pulling them out. You’ll want to avoid heavy traffic from sharp-edged furniture and be mindful of pets’ claws; we are aiming for longevity, not a shredded meteor shower.

### Color stability and sun exposure

Blue dyes can fade if left in direct sunlight for extended periods. If your window line points toward the sun for most of the day, you’ll want to pair the rug with curtains or UV-protective window film. The Comet Blue shade might shift slightly over time, but that can be part of its character, like a constellation becoming more defined with age. Rotate the rug every so often to promote even wear and avoid the “one-footprint galaxy” effect where your favorite sofa leaves an imprint that looks suspiciously like a starship’s outline.

### Care and maintenance basics

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting; avoid beater bars on high power to reduce pilling. 
- Address spills immediately with blotting rather than rubbing; rags or paper towels work in a pinch. For stubborn stains, a mild wool-safe detergent with cold water is your friend. (Test in a hidden corner first.)
- Rotate every few months to promote even wear and color consistency across the surface.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12–24 months if you have heavy foot traffic or a penchant for accidental art projects on the floor.

A note on shedding: a small amount of wool shedding is normal in the first weeks. It will taper off as the fibers settle in. It’s not a maintenance fever dream; it’s the rug finding its footing in your apartment’s gravity well.

## Sizing Options: Custom Size or 8x10

### Why size matters

A rug is not a decorative throw pillow for your floor. It defines zones, warms a seating arrangement, and anchors a room’s color story. The Comet Blue allows for two practical paths: a precise custom size or a standard 8x10. The custom size option means you can tailor length and width to your floor plan, leaving no awkward gaps under the sofa or in that perfect reading nook. The 8x10 option is a timeless staple that suits many layouts, offering a balance of coverage and proportion in rooms of average or moderate size.

### How to choose between custom size and 8x10

- Room dimensions: If you have an unusually shaped living area or peculiar offsets around furniture, a custom size helps you avoid negative space that eats your visual harmony.
- Furniture layout: Consider the footprint of coffee tables, sofas, and media consoles. A rug that underlays all key pieces can unify the space, while a rug that stops short can create a layered, modern effect if done intentionally.
- Aesthetic goals: The 8x10 is reliable and versatile, but a custom size can emphasize a focal point such as a coffee table cluster or an accent seating arrangement.
- Budget and lead times: Custom sizes may come with longer production times and slightly higher pricing; plan accordingly if you’re redecorating on a tight schedule.

### Ordering process for custom size or 8x10

The ordering process is designed to be friendly to both indecisive researchers and decisive space captains. Start with measurements in inches or centimeters, double-check your door and hallway clearance, and summarizing your layout in a simple diagram. Then select your desired size in the product options: the standard 8x10 or your precise custom dimensions. If you’re going with a custom size, you’ll usually specify length and width, choose edge binding style (fringe or clean edge), and you might also select padding thickness. The rug maker will confirm the color consistency and dye batch for Comet Blue, ensuring your rug aligns with your space’s lighting and the other blues in the room.

## Durability, Usability, and Practicality

### How the rug holds up under real-life use

Durability is the honest truth behind wool rugs. In a busy household, a wool rug tends to hold up better than many synthetics, resisting crushing and maintaining shape better than synthetic fibers over time. The Comet Blue is meant to handle daily foot traffic without looking worn after a few weeks, which is a blessing for families and individuals who treat their living spaces like a mission control center. If you have kids or pets, the rug’s fiber structure helps prevent pilling and fraying from normal play and routine activities. But, like all things beloved, it will benefit from mindful care.

### Spill management and stain resistance

Wool has some inherent stain resistance due to its scaly, waxy surface, which repels some liquids. Quick blotting remains your best defense against stains. For tougher spots, a mild wool-safe cleaner with cold water will help break down the mess without compromising the fiber. Always test a hidden corner first and avoid harsh chemicals that could strip the dye. External stain guides are helpful references, but your first line of defense should be timely action and gentle cleaning.

### Longevity expectations

A well-made wool rug with proper care can last a decade or more with daily life. The color will mellow slightly over time as dyes settle, and the texture will soften in a way that makes your feet feel like they’re stepping on a cloud factory that took a field trip to a space station.
