## Condition, Authenticity, and Value: A Collector’s Perspective
Factory-sealed status is the ultimate green light for many collectors. It promises that the contents are as close to “as-new” as a 31-year-old item can realistically be. But with sealed product, there’s a paradox: sealed decks can appreciate in value, yet you’re also buying into the possibility that the contents may not align perfectly with modern expectations for balance, print quality, or presentation.

### What to Look For When Assessing a Factory-Sealed Deck
- Seal integrity: Check for tears, reseals, or obvious attempts at tampering. A clean, intact seal is a strong signal of authenticity.
- Box condition: If you’re into display, the exterior matters. Dents, creases, or sticker residue can impact the shelf appeal.
- Card stock and print fidelity: Once you crack it, you’ll want to compare the color saturation and card gloss with other sets from the era. Some batches show better color calibration than others, but the retro charm tends to be forgiving.
- Completeness: Ensure all inserts, rules sheets, and token sheets are present. A missing rule sheet can make a sealed deck feel less valuable from a gameplay perspective—even if the cards themselves are pristine.
