### Safety and Outdoor Use: Wind, Ground, and Gentle Caution
Safety first, theatrically second: Jenga Giant 3ft is a tall wooden tower, and gravity is a gambler with a friendly smile. Here are some practical safety notes:
- Choose a flat, open surface. Grass is fine, but avoid slopes that could tilt the tower uphill toward chaos. A hard surface like a deck or patio is ideal when you want maximum balance control.
- Keep pets and small children at a safe distance during play. The blocks are sizable and heavy enough to cause a hurt toe if a careless step happens—remedial supervision is advised.
- Weather matters. Wind is the wild card in a game meant for careful needle-threading. If a breeze is consistent or gusty, consider moving the set indoors or under a sheltered area.
- Regular checks help. If the base shows any looseness or wobble, fix it before continuing. A gentle nudge with a level can help you realign and regain confidence.
- Cleaning is easy. A quick wipe with a damp cloth and a light sealant reapplication every season can help preserve the wood’s appearance and feel. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the wood’s smooth surface.

The safety caveats aren’t meant to scare you away; they’re practical guidelines to keep the fun intact while avoiding that cringe-worthy moment when a block flies into a neighbor’s yard and you are suddenly the star of a low-budget epic film about gravity and ambition.
