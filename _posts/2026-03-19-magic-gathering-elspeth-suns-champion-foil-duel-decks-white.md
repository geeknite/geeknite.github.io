## Aesthetics and handling: does foil still matter in 2026?
Yes, foil still matters. The modern foiling process yields cards with shimmering edges and a surface that can catch the light in a variety of angles. The Elspeth foil in the Duel Deck is particularly satisfying as a display piece, and if you’re the kind of player who enjoys shuffling a binder with a little extra flair, the foil version is a welcome addition. However, with great shine comes a bit more risk: foils are more prone to edge wear if you shuffle aggressively, and sunlight can nudge the color-saturation of the art in unexpected directions. If you’re a player who frequently plays in the sunlit dining room, consider a mat or a card display stand to keep glare at a minimum and the aesthetics intact.

From a purely practical standpoint, if you want to play with Elspeth foil at your table, plan for a slightly different grip when you handle the card. Foils are slicker than non-foils, and that can be a plus or a minus depending on your card-handling mojo. If you’re going to put this card into a deck, think about your deck’s theme and whether the foil is a thematic highlight or simply a decorative perk that makes you smile every time a token is born on the board.
