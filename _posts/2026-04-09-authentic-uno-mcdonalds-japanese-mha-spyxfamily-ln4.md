## The Gameplay Twist: How LN4 Changes the Game

- Quirk Draw Four (Wild Draw Four variant): When played, the next player must draw four cards unless they can bluff a counter-quirk that matches a hero’s power. The meta-game here is subtle: can you call out a valid counter-quirk without tipping off your own strategy?
- Espionage Skip (Skip card): Loid’s eye-poking glare is enough to make your opponent skip their turn. It’s built to feel satisfying, because you’re basically staring someone into a lost turn.
- Family Bond Reverse (Reverse): A nod to Anya’s psychic hijinks. Reverses the flow of play, much to everyone’s delight and slight confusion.
- Hero’s Might (Draw2 with a twist): If played by a red card, you can demand two cards down the stack with a hero-chanting line of your choice. It’s dramatic and a little ridiculous—in the best way.

Beyond the new card names and slight rule tweaks, the LN4 deck retains Uno’s core footprint.
