The listing calls this rug a “flaw in rug,” paired with a reduced price. That phrase can spark a spectrum of interpretations—from a minor cosmetic imperfection to a functional limitation. In our experience, a rug that’s labeled flawed often delivers one of three scenarios:

## Practical care and maintenance notes

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to prevent fiber disturbance. A quick weekly pass keeps dirt and pet hair from building up. For high-traffic areas, a slightly more robust vacuum is fine, but avoid aggressive agitation that could loosen fibers.
- For spills, blot do not rub. Use a mild detergent or rug cleaner following the product instructions, and test on a small invisible area first. Quick action minimizes staining risk and preserves color integrity.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear and color exposure. This is especially helpful in rooms with a single strong light source that could cause subtle shading differences over time.
- The rug pad you pair with this piece matters: choose a pad that offers adequate grip and cushioning. A good pad will prolong the life of the rug and reduce curling edges, which are not only unsightly but also a trip hazard if you’re not careful.
