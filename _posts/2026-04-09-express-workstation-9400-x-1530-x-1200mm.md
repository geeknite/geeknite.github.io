## Assembly: Time, Tools, and Trials
Assembling the Express Workstation is not a weekend project for beginners. You’ll want a devoted afternoon, a second pair of hands, and a mild tolerance for swear words. The modular panels click into the frame with a satisfying “thunk,” and the instruction manual is straightforward enough to avoid a meltdown—unless you’re me at 2 a.m. after three coffees and a deadline.

Step one is layout: clear a space large enough for the main frame. Step two is assemble the frame sections with the provided brackets and bolts. Step three is align the surface panels and secure them with screws. Step four is route your cables into the channels. Step five is test-load and adjust. The process is well-documented, but you’ll still find yourself juggling screws and wondering if you could have earned a degree in advanced hardware tantrums. In the end, the desk stands like a proud behemoth in your room, a steel-and-laminate monument to productivity—and a warning sign to your inner procrastination demon.

For those who love precision, the leveling feet ensure the desk sits flat on imperfect floors. You’ll want to use a spirit level and a friend with a good balance to check evenness across the entire length. Expect a few degrees of tilt if your room’s floor is more historical ruin than modern substrate, but the included shims make fine-tuning feasible.

If you’re dealing with an older apartment or a rental that fights back, the assembly process also becomes a stealth exercise in “where can I hide this monster when the landlord comes by?” The answer is probably under the surface panels, in a neat stack of spare screws, or behind the mighty shade of a wall-mounted monitor arm. Pro tip: label every bracket the moment you detach it. It saves you from a sequel assembly where you’re chasing a rogue bolt down the hall like a tiny metallic bear.
