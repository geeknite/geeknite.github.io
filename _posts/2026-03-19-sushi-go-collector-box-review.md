## How to Play: Core Mechanics and Round Flow
Sushi Go is a compact engine of risk and reward. The Collector's Box does not rewrite the rules; it refines the experience by making the drafting itself easier to handle mid-session. If you know Sushi Go, you know the drill: each round you pick one card from your hand, then pass the rest to the left. You accumulate a hand of various card types that score differently at the end of the round. Games typically wrap in about 15 to 20 minutes, which means a handful of rounds, a few bold moves, and a lot of light banter as you realize your scoring trajectory is either cooking or flopping.

### Core Mechanics
- Card drafting: This is the moment where probability meets social psychology. You want to block others from grabbing a valuable card, but you also want to secure a set that pays off later. The Collector's Box helps here by providing quick visual cues and a clean layout that makes scanning the board a breeze even when the table erupts in laughter.
- Set collection: Each sushi type has its own scoring rule. Nigiri cards grant raw point values, sashimi rewards points for collecting two or three, tempura rewards points for pairs, and maki puts pressure on the lead at round end. The pudding remains the wildcard that can derail a plan if misread. The improved readability in the Box helps you track sets forming without needing a quiet epiphany moment.
- End game scoring: The final tally is the moment of truth. The Box helps you keep track of numbers with ease, reducing cognitive friction and letting you savor the moment when the orange glow of victory blooms across the table or when a surprising pudding blips into the lead.

### Round Flow
- Start with a hand of cards, typically seven in the base game. The Collector's Box layout ensures suits and types are easily distinguished so you can scan for your target in seconds.
- Pick one card, pass the rest. This is where restraint shows its teeth: resist the temptation to snatch a card your neighbor wants, and instead lock in a safe pick that maintains a solvable arc for the round.
- Reveal and score the round as needed. Scoring is straightforward, but the real entertainment comes from bold moves that trigger cascading point bonuses and table-wide reactions.
