## Maintenance and Care

### Keeping your Rider Back in mint condition
Humidity is the sworn nemesis of cardboard. Keep your sealed deck away from damp, heat, or direct sunlight to prevent warping or color fade. If you’ve already opened the deck, treat it like a living thing: rotate your usage to keep the stock evenly worn, and consider re-hydrating slightly by fanning gently after a long session. Cards enjoy a little break between heavy practice days; give them a night off in a dry box. Dust it off occasionally with a soft brush if you’re displaying your collection; you don’t want dust motes interfering with your ability to track the back design under bright stage lights.

### Cleaning and accidental spills
In the unlikely event of a spill, don’t freak out. Wipe carefully with a dry cloth and let the deck air-dry completely before use. Avoid solvents that could degrade the finish. If the finish begins to rub off in odd places after heavy practice, you might be using the deck as a prop for longer than intended. It’s not a mission failure; it’s a badge of honor indicating you’ve spent time with this deck in the trenches of practice and performance.
