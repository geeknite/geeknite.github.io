## Mythbusting and common questions
- Myth: 10k gold is too soft for daily wear. Truth: 10k gold is an alloy designed for durability; it’s generally tougher than higher-karat gold in terms of scratch resistance and everyday wear, though it may show wear a bit more over time because of its alloy mix.
- Myth: Bigger carat always equals better sparkle. Truth: Carat is weight; brilliance comes from cut, light performance, and clarity. You can have a large stone that looks dull if the cut isn’t right. This band emphasizes a balanced cut and a setting that maximizes light return while maintaining security.
- Myth: Vintage-inspired rings are fragile. Truth: Modern manufacturing merges vintage aesthetics with robust engineering. You’ll get the visual charm without the fragility that older techniques sometimes implied.
- FAQ: Is a 1.8 ct center enough to stand out in photographs? In most lighting, yes. The true test is how it looks on day-to-day wear and how your partner feels about the presence of the ring. Happy partners make for even happier photos.
