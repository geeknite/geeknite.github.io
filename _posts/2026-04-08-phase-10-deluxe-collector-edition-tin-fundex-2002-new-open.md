## The Rules and Deluxe adjustments

### Core mechanics preserved

Phase 10 is a race against the clock and your fellow players to complete ten phases. Each phase is a set of requirements, typically a combination of sets and runs, and your goal each round is to complete the current phase and move on to the next one. The basic loop is simple: draw, discard, hope your opponents trip over their own discarded cards, and try not to get stuck with dead weight at the end of a round.

### Deluxe changes and mischief

The Deluxe Collector Tin does not reinvent Phase 10; it enhances the presentation and storage. The rules remain faithful to the original design, with any edition specific clarifications explained in the included sheet. The extra aesthetic touches in the Deluxe edition can make a round feel more ceremonial, which some players adore and others simply tolerate. The biggest practical impact is how the set looks on the table and how satisfying it is to physically hold a glossy deck with a nice tin backing you up when you declare victory.

## Storage, care, and longevity

### Keeping it pristine

Storage in a dry place away from direct sunlight helps preserve the colors and card stock. If the tin has a glossy finish, light exposure can cause some slight fading over multi-year periods. The cards, however, are relatively forgiving if the tin is stored properly. A quick check before a night of play is a good idea; you want to make sure no cards have slipped and that the deck is in good order for shuffling.

### Card care tips

If you want to extend the life of the cards, consider using a standard card sleeve set or a light cleaning approach. Wipe the surface with a soft microfiber cloth if you see fingerprints or smudges accumulate over time. Avoid harsh cleaners that could degrade the glossy finish or alter the color balance of the artwork.
