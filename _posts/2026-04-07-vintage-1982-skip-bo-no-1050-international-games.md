## Collectibility, value, and authenticity
### Sealed vs opened: why sealed rocks a little harder
A sealed edition is a silent, time-traveling wallet. While an opened copy can still be a cherished item, the sealed version confers premium status for collectors who love the assurance that every corner of the packaging has remained untouched. The tactile feel of the seal, the crispness of the cards, and the faint scent of aged cardboard combine into a sensory memory of game night that never actually leaves the room.

### Authentication tips
- Look for the International Games branding in the box art and the back of the cards; consistency here is a good sign.
- Check the card stock for a slightly glossy finish typical of early 80s manufacturer standards. 
- Inspect the edges of the box and the seal; a re-seal is possible but not guaranteed, and sometimes you’ll spot a telltale line where a sticker-slit was resealed.
- Compare the artwork and typography to known originals; minor deviations can signal a misalignment in reproduction, though some variance is part of the charm of vintage items.
