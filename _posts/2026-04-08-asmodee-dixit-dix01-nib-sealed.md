## How to play in a few minutes

Dixit is a party style game built around a storyteller each round. The steps are simple, but the magic happens in how you connect clues to the cards and how others interpret those clues. The basic flow looks like this:

- A storyteller selects a card from their hand and gives a clue that relates to that card. The clue can be a word, a phrase, a sound, or something oddly specific or cryptic. The key is that the clue should be clever enough to evoke the chosen card but not so obvious that everyone can guess it immediately.
- The other players pick a card from their own hand that best matches the storyteller's clue. They submit their chosen cards face down.
- The storyteller shuffles the selected cards with their own and reveals them. Everyone then votes on which card they think belongs to the storyteller’s clue card.
- Scoring occurs. If everyone or no one guesses the storyteller’s card, the storyteller gets few to no points; otherwise, players who guessed correctly get points along with the storyteller if their guess was aligned with the majority.

The rules are compact, but the real challenge is how you craft the clue so that it is neither too obvious nor too obtuse. You want to tease, not shout. You want to inspire a chorus of whispered interpretations rather than a chorus of loud accusations that someone is sabotaging the round with a subpar clue. This is where Dixit shines: the social dynamics, the creative maneuvering, and the shared laughter that comes with misinterpretation.
