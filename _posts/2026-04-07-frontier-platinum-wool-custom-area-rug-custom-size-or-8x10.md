### Maintenance, Cleaning, and Longevity: Wool as a Friend, Not an Foe
Wool is famously resilient, but it’s not invincible. The Frontier Platinum Wool rug can be a long-lasting investment if you follow sensible care practices:

- Vacuuming cadence: Regular, gentle vacuuming with a wand attachment helps remove surface dust and prevents soil from grinding into the fibers. Avoid using a beater bar on high settings, especially on a wool rug, to minimize fiber agitation that can cause pilling or fuzzing.
- Spills and stains: Blot liquids immediately with a clean, white cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing can push liquid deeper into the pile. For persistent stains, test a small inconspicuous area with a wool-safe cleaner or consult a professional cleaner.
- Rotation and aging: Rotate the rug every 6–12 months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic rooms. Wool tends to wear gracefully, but you’ll thank yourself for rotating to prevent visible traffic patterns from forming on one section.
- Professional cleaning: A professional rug cleaning every 12–24 months (depending on foot traffic and pets) can refresh dyes, maintain pile integrity, and extend the rug’s life. Look for cleaners who specify wool-safe techniques to ensure you don’t accidentally shrink or distort the weave.

### Rug Pads: The Quiet Vitality Underfoot
A padding layer isn’t optional; it’s the unsung hero of rug longevity and comfort. A suitable rug pad increases underfoot cushioning, reduces slippage, and helps the rug resist edges curling. For wool rugs, a memory-foam or felt/latex pad can offer a stable, non-slip base that protects against wear. Consider the room’s needs:

- In high-traffic living rooms, a denser pad helps the rug retain its shape in the most-used zones.
- For rooms with hardwood floors, a good pad minimizes friction and protects both rug and flooring. 
- If you have radiant heating or moisture concerns, verify pad compatibility and moisture resistance with the retailer or a rug specialist.
