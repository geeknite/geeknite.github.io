## Gameplay: the core loop, refined

The core loop is deceptively simple: move through a network of interconnected rooms, dodge hazards, blast enemies, collect shards, and use those shards to unlock upgrades that push you into new combat styles. The game wears its roguelike influences lightly, offering a few permadeath stairs that tempt you into a one-and-done run, but it also provides safe checkpoints that keep you moving without grinding you into the, well, ground.

The movement feels responsive and precise, an essential trait for a title that demands both speed and accuracy. The dash mechanic is satisfying, offering a burst that can be timed for escapes or for risky, adrenaline-fueled maneuvers through a tight corridor. Combat blends laser shots, shotgun bursts, and a few exotic weapons that feel surprisingly distinct from each other. You’ll switch between weapons not out of necessity alone but out of a desire to experience the rhythm of each tool’s cadence in different encounter sequences.

Upgrade paths are the heart of the strategic layer. Shards collected from defeated foes serve as currency for enhancing your speed, shields, fire rate, and weapon versatility. The upgrade tree is well structured: you can pursue a linear progression to maximize reliability, or you can branch out into risky but potentially reward-heavy lines that push you toward creative builds. The game nudges experimentation by offering multiple viable routes through each stage and by presenting optional encounters that reward curiosity with extra loot or a cheeky easter egg.
