## How to Play: Setup and Rules
Dizzy Date keeps setup minimal, which is the essential ingredient in a successful date-night game: less setup, more chaos. Here’s how to get rolling:

### Setup
- Clear a comfortable space where you won’t spill sushi sauce on your favorite sweater.
- Shuffle the 100 cards (the official method is to give them a crisp shuffle; the unofficial method is to pretend you’re building a tiny black hole in the middle of your coffee table).
- Decide who goes first. If you’re in a loving, symmetrical relationship, you can flip a coin; if you’re in the “let’s see who gets to blame the other person” phase, just improvise.
- Place a small stack face-down within reach.

### The Turn
- The active player draws a card and reads it aloud.
- The prompt is followed to the letter, or you add a personal twist if you’re feeling spicy.
- The other player responds in turn, and a quick discussion follows. There’s no timed motor; you’re allowed to take a moment to appreciate the ridiculous or mushy moment that may emerge.
- After both players respond, the next player draws and the cycle continues.

### The Optional Twists
If you want to take things up a notch, use the included twist bookmark. These are optional rules you can blend in:
- The Silent Round: no talking for one prompt (you’ll communicate with eyebrow raises and nods, which is somehow more intense than talking).
- The Gift Card: a card that instructs you to write a tiny compliment for your partner on the back and exchange it.
- The Dare-to-Kiss Round: a playful, non-intrusive dare that ends with a kiss on the cheek or lips, depending on your comfort level.

## Variations and House Rules
If you want to customize your experience, here are a few house rules you can try:
- Time-lock Round: set a timer for each prompt to keep a brisk pace and end on a high-energy moment.
- Compliment Chain: after each card, both players give three quick compliments to the other, fostering positive vibes and a little ego boost.
- Thematic Weeks: dedicate a date-night to a theme (romance, travel, nerdy hobbies). Pull only cards that fit the theme to deepen the mood.
- Card Drafting: if you have a small group or a slightly larger crowd, you can draft cards into a personal “date kit” to keep things fresh across weeks.
