## Maintenance and Care: Keeping Potter Folly Glamorous
### Daily Care
Regular vacuuming is the sword you wield against the dust goblins. The rug’s dense pile means you should use a vacuum with adjustable height settings to avoid pulling fibers. A quick weekly sweep should keep surface dust at bay, and you can rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear across the surface. Spills happen. The key is speed: blot with a clean cloth rather than rubbing; that action tends to push the liquid deeper into the fibers, which results in a living art piece that smells faintly like yesterday’s coffee and a questionable snack choice.

### Deep Cleaning and Stain Treatment
For stubborn stains, a gentle carpet cleaner or diluted dish soap with water can help break down the mess. Always test a small, inconspicuous spot first to ensure colorfastness; cream rugs can be a little dramatic and may show color transfer more readily than darker options. If you have a pet drama in your home, keep a mopped floor nearby to handle accidents—because even magic carpets need a safety net. The manufacturer’s care guide recommends professional cleaning every 6-12 months if heavy use is anticipated; this is a good plan for households with kids, dogs, or a habit of hosting spontaneous pizza nights that end with a flour-dusted living room floor.

### Longevity and Wear
The rug’s construction aims for durability in high-traffic zones. The pile density and edge finishing help resist flattening in central pathways. If you have heavy furniture that never moves, consider using furniture coasters to distribute weight and avoid indentations. The reality is you will likely notice some flattening in the areas most walked on; that is not a failure of the rug but a sign that life happens. The trick is to rotate furniture occasionally to distribute wear and recover the uniformity of the look.
