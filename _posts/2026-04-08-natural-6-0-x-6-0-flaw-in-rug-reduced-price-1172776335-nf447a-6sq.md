## Care, Longevity, and Practical Tips
Care is where many rugs either prove their worth or reveal their Achilles’ heel. Here are practical care tips that align with the rug’s natural fibers and the “flaw-as-feature” philosophy:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to avoid pulling fibers. Use a beater brush sparingly if your vacuum has one. The goal is to keep the weave intact while removing dust and hair.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push a stain deeper into the fibers and create a “rug stain volcano” that takes months to extinguish.
- Rotate the rug every few months if possible. This helps maintain even wear and prevents certain areas from looking more sun-bleached than others.
- For stains that won’t budge, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in natural fibers. The wrong chemical can ruin the integrity of the weave and the rug’s personality. As a rule of thumb, test any cleaning solution on a small corner first.
- Shield from the harsh midday sun if possible. The color may shift gently over time, which is a normal aging process that some people actually find appealing. If you prefer consistent color, think about UV-blocking window treatments or rotating the rug’s placement when sunlight is strongest.
