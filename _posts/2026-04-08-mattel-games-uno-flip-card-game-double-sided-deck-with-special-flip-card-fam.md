## How UNO FLIP! differs from classic UNO
If classic UNO is a bright, friendly neighborhood, UNO FLIP! is the masked cousin who shows up with a flashlight and a plan. The key differences are simple but meaningful:

- Double-sided deck: Light side versus dark side. The color palette shifts from sunny to something with a little more mystery, which affects how you prioritize cards.

What is delightful is that UNO FLIP! preserves the quick pace that makes UNO accessible to all ages while injecting a strategic angle that keeps adults engaged. ## Gameplay: Quick start and strategic tips
Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal 7 cards to each player. Place the remaining deck face down as the draw pile on the table. The top card of the draw pile is flipped to start the discard pile. If that card is a special card, follow its instruction on the light side before flipping to the dark side later in the game.

Turn flow
- On your turn you must play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the top card on the discard pile. If you cannot play, you draw one card. If you draw and can play, you may do so immediately.
- If you play a Flip Card, the entire deck and all players flip to the opposite color side. The active color on the top of the discard pile becomes the opposite side, and the game continues with the new set of rules on that side.
- On the dark side you find new action cards: Draw Five, Skip, Reverse, Wild on the dark side. These cards mix up the pace and create opportunities for dramatic comebacks even when you are staring at a handful of two’s and four’s.

Strategy considerations
- Counting colors on both sides matters. A good UNO FLIP! player tracks which colors are still in the mix on both sides and uses flips to force opponents into unfavorable colors or draw sequences.
- Hand management is different here. Because you will experience a flip mid-game, you want to avoid having a disaster of a hand once the dark side comes into play. Keeping a balanced mix on both sides helps you adapt to the flip with fewer nerves.
- Timing the flip can be a game winner. If you can trigger a Flip Card when the opponent is poised to call UNO on your turn, you can swing momentum dramatically. But be aware that you might also flip the table on yourself if you miscalculate.

House rules and variations
- Two-path UNO: Play rounds on light side only for a casual night, then rotate to the dark side for a more intense session. This keeps the game accessible for first-timers while offering a more strategic challenge for veterans.
- Team UNO FLIP: Pair up players and switch sides after a Flip Card, making team communication critical and chaotic at the same time.
- Stackable Flip: Some groups enjoy stacking Flip Cards, effectively triggering multiple flips in a single round. This is not official, but it is hilarious chaos that can be a great source of late-night entertainment.
