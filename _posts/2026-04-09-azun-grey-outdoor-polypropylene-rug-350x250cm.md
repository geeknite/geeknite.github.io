## Cleaning and maintenance: keeping it fresh without a full-blown spa day

One of polypropylene’s biggest selling points is the cleaning ease. Here’s a practical regimen you can actually follow without needing a professional cleaner or an extenstive course on rug therapy:

- Regular vacuuming: use a vacuum with a wide head. The rug’s low pile means you can pick up dust and pet hair without fighting the fibers.
- Spot cleaning: for spills, blot (don’t rub) with a mild detergent solution and a soft brush. Rinse with clean water and air-dry. For stubborn stains, a gentle mix of water and white vinegar can help without altering the color too aggressively.
- Deep clean (occasionally): a light hose wash can refresh the fibers. Avoid high-pressure jets that might pull fibers loose or force water between gaps in decking.
- Drying: air-dry completely to prevent mold and mildew. Polypropylene dries fast, but you still want to ensure it’s thoroughly dry before heavy use.

Maintenance-wise, this rug is low-friction: you can move furniture around, hose it down, and store it without crazy expertise. It’s basically “set it and forget it,” but with more style and fewer expensive electronics involved.
