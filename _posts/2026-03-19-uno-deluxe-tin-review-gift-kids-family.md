## How to Play: A Quickstart for Busy Nerds and Newbies
UNO has a simple premise: match the color or the number of the card on the discard pile. The Deluxe Tin version sticks to that core, with a couple of nice upgrades that brighten the game-night experience.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player.
- Place the remaining cards in the draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- Decide on players and turn order. UNO is famously friendly to large groups, so you can easily swing from a 2-person game to a chaotic 8-person chaos session without breaking a sweat.

### Basic Play Rules (Let’s keep it simple for the first round)
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the top card on the discard pile.
- If you don’t have a playable card, you draw one card from the draw pile. If that card is playable, you may put it down immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.
- Action cards add spice: Skip (the next player loses a turn), Reverse (changes the play direction), Draw Two (the next player draws two), Wild (choose a new color), and Wild Draw Four (wild card plus four cards for the next player) are your friends when you want to establish a little strategic chaos.

### Endgame and Victory
The first player to shout UNO (if you have one card left) wins. If another player catches you with a one-card blunder and calls you out, you might be forced to draw a penalty card. It’s the drama we all secretly crave.

### House Rules and Variations
For families, house rules are sacred. Here are a few that play nicely with UNO Deluxe Tin:
- Stacking Draw Twos: If a player places a Draw Two, the next player can place another Draw Two instead of drawing, passing the penalty along to the subsequent player. This one keeps the table buzzing with loud counting sounds.
- UNO Uno: When a Wild is played, the color chosen must be announced loudly. The louder, the better the victory dance.
- Skip Dilemma: If two players in a row get skipped, the one who played the first Skip card gets to perform a silly “victory pose” before the next round begins.
