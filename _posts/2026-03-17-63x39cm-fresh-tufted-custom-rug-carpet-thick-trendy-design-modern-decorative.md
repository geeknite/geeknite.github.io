## Care and Maintenance
Caring for a tufted rug is not a punishment, it is a ritual. With a little routine, you can extend its life and keep it looking fresh even in a high-usage room. Here are some practical care steps that work well in real apartments and home offices:

- Vacuum lightly at least once a week. Use a brush attachment or a low-suction setting to avoid crushing the tufted fibers. Let the vacuum do the heavy lifting, not your rug’s personality.
- Blot spills immediately. Use a clean white cloth or paper towel to blot—do not rub—as rubbing can push the stain deeper into the fibers. If you must, a bit of mild detergent diluted with water can help lift the stain. Always test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- Consider a professional clean every 12-18 months if the rug experiences heavy use or if stains accumulate in a visible area. A professional can refresh tufted fibers and restore that just-cut-pile look.
- Rotate occasionally. A small rug may seem to settle in one place, but rotation can help evenly distribute wear and prevent obvious depressions from forming under heavy desk chairs.

A few quick tips to keep your rug looking brand-new include avoiding direct heat sources, using a coaster under drinks when the rug is in a coffee zone, and keeping pets’ claws trimmed to reduce snagging risk. If you suspect color transfer from clothing or furniture, a quick spot-clean is often enough to keep the palette intact.
