## Card quality, condition, and how to spot red flags
Condition matters, especially when you’re dealing with a bulk lot. Here are practical checks you or a friend with a keen eye can perform when you receive the lot:

- Edges and corners: The corner wear on Dragon Ball Super cards is common in bulk lots. Look for whitening on the edges and soft corners. A few cards may be borderline, but if a large percentage looks borderline-to-poor, that reduces resale value or playability.
- Surface and foil: Foil or holo cards should have consistent shine. If foil looks patchy or peeling, that’s not ideal for display; for play, it may be acceptable depending on your tolerance for cosmetic warts.
- Printing anomalies: Occasionally you’ll see misprints, miscuts, or misaligned borders. Some players collect these quirks as cosmetic “earned badges”; others see them as blemishes to avoid in their main deck.
- Backs and sleeves: If you’re buying a lot, you’ll often encounter random back variants. Some sets switch the back design mid-run—this can impact your ability to play casually with others who demand uniform card backs. If you’re buying for play, make sure most cards share the same back design or you’re comfortable sorting in binder pages that hide the variety.
- Authenticity cues: Compare the signature font, logo placement, and set identifiers with an official checklist from a trusted source. If a top-tier rare looks like it was printed in a basement at 3 AM, that triggers alarm bells.

To help with confidence, remember this quick rule of thumb: more than a handful of misprints or miscuts across a large portion of the stack is a red flag. If you’re unsure, ask for detailed close-ups of the border areas and the backs of several cards. A seller worth their salt should provide clear photos and be open to questions.

## Care, storage, and handling tips
Bulk buys are excellent, but only if you treat them well after arrival. Here are practical steps to protect your investment and maximize joy:

- Inspect on arrival: Do a quick surface check as soon as the package lands. Look for bent corners, scuffs, water damage, or any signs of mishandling.
- Sleeve immediately: Put your battle cards into standard dragon ball sleeves, ideally with matte inner sleeves to reduce glare for your display photos and to protect against wear.
- Binder organization: Sort by set, rarity, and color scheme. Having a clear plan saves you time later and reduces the temptation to turn every card into a dramatic 90s montage of mis-sorted chaos.
- Dubbed backups: Create a small “trades” pile for duplicates you’re willing to swap away. The swap economy is alive and well in most local shops, and you’ll thank yourself later for sending a few cards to new homes.
- Storage environment: Keep cards away from humidity, heat, and direct sunlight. The worst enemies of cardboard are rust, heat waves, and the occasional dragon breathing down your neck while you’re sorting cards at 2 am.

## Authenticity, returns, and seller reliability
In the wild, you’ll encounter a range of seller experiences. Here are red flags and green flags to help you decide if a listing is player-friendly or a fairy tale:

- Red flags: Vague counts, no photos, suspiciously perfect conditions, or packs that look like they rearranged themselves after shipping. If a seller cannot provide clear close-ups of the top, middle, and bottom of your cards, proceed with caution.
- Green flags: Clear photos of multiple cards, a timestamp showing actual packaging, a transparent return policy, and a reasonable description of what’s included in the lot. A seller who is responsive with quick messages earns trust points.
- Returns and protections: A seller who offers a return window, buyer protection, or a money-back guarantee is your best friend for bulk purchases. It’s not about being paranoid; bulk buys are inherently riskier than buying a single sealed booster.

Before you pull the trigger, consider asking for a short video or a few animated GIFs showing card backs and edges from several cards. The extra effort shows they care about their reputation, and you’ll be happier with the purchase when you have a better sense of what you’re getting.
