The W Screen is the star of the show here. It is not a flimsy privacy panel but a well engineered barrier that stands up to daily use. The panel also helps reduce glare from overhead lighting and prevent the occasional ghost of a coworker from flashing across your screen during a meeting. ### Dimensions and Layout

This desk is built to host six people in a layout that fosters collaboration without turning into a carnival of elbows. The total length of 5400 mm means you can add six primary work zones, each with enough space for a monitor, keyboard, mouse, and personal accoutrements like a water bottle or a lucky plant. The width of 1530 mm gives a comfortable lane for chair movements and quick stand up stretches without bumping into a neighbor. The 1200 mm height is a comfortable average for most chairs; to get the best ergonomics, you want to align your monitor arms so that the top of the screen sits at eye level when you are seated and a little above when you are leaning back. Remember that the aim of this setup is not to crank your neck to the maximum angle every five minutes but to create a stable, healthy posture that can survive long days and late-night debugging sessions.

### Maintenance and Care

Caring for a large desk is not rocket science. The oak veneer should be wiped down with a soft microfiber cloth to remove dust and fingerprints on a weekly basis. If there are small spills, wipe them promptly with a damp cloth and dry the area to prevent moisture from seeping into the veneer. The black metal frame benefits from periodic dusting and a light wipe of a gentle metal cleaner if you notice any smudges or fingerprints. The W Screen can be adjusted or swapped with minimal effort; just ensure that the support arms remain securely attached. If you notice any wobble, check the anchor bolts and tighten as needed. Regular checks will keep this desk sturdy for many years and many stand up meetings.
