## Setup and assembly
Setup is a ritual: you align the legs, attach the crossbars, bolt the side rails, and then fight the urge to use the Allen key as a weight for a catapult when you realize you’ve got one too many screws left over. In practice, expect the process to take a single-player 60–75 minutes if you’re focused; add a friend who offers counterproductive “help” every eight minutes, and you’ll be closer to the two-hour mark.

Key tips for a smoother build:
- Lay out all hardware before starting. It’s shocking how many bits will look identical but aren’t.
- Use a light oil or dry silicone on the rods once they’re installed to reduce friction and preserve the feel of the turnover. You don’t want a stiffer, squeaky experience every time you try a spin pass.
- Check levelness at multiple points along the cabinet. Uneven floors will skew shots and trigger conscious-uncoupling thoughts about your life choices.

With the legs attached and the rails sitting true, you’ll feel the table begin to earn its “competitor” badge. The assembly is not glamorous, but it pays dividends in reliability and long-term playability.
