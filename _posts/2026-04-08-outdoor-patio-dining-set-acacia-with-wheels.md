### Durability, weather resistance, and care
Acacia is known for being hardy in outdoor environments, partly thanks to natural oils that resist moisture and insects better than many softwoods. It’s not indestructible, but with sensible care it ages with character rather than cracking into a sad museum piece. Here are practical maintenance steps:

- Keep the set out of standing water when possible; use a waterproof cover during heavy rain or snow seasons. Avoid constant exposure to soaking conditions, which can lead to warping over time.
- Clean using mild soap and water. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that strip the wood’s natural oils.
- Reapply a protective sealant (oil-based or waterborne finish) every season or per manufacturer guidelines to maintain color and moisture resistance.
- Use coasters for hot dishes and placemats to protect the surface from moisture and heat.
- If cushions are included, store them indoors when not in use, especially during wet weather, to prolong lifespan.

In field trials, the set held up well under sun, light to moderate rain, and routine neighborhood chaos (read: kids running around with plastic swords and a pretend dragon). ### Maintenance tips and tricks
- For a longer-lasting finish, apply a penetrating oil or sealant designed for outdoor wood at least annually. The goal is to keep the wood’s natural oils in play, not to seal it into a plastic-like shell.
- Clean occasionally with a soft brush and mild soap; avoid pressure washing on high settings as that can erode edges and open pores.
- Use the wheel locks to prevent unintended movement; this is especially helpful on sloped surfaces or when kids are playing around the table.
- If you notice slight color changes over time, that’s normal; you can bring back some vibrancy with a light re-oiling routine.
