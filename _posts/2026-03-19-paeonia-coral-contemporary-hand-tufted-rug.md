## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Bloom Fresh
### Everyday Care
Regular vacuuming with a suction head (no beater bar on high) helps keep the pile from looking matted. Spot-clean spills as soon as you detect them. For stubborn stains, a mild, pH-balanced cleaner is recommended. Avoid harsh solvents that could compromise fiber integrity or color vibrancy. If you’ve got kids or pets, you’ll want to implement a quick “snack zone” rule to reduce crumb migration into the bloom of your coral dream.

### Rotation and Shape Retention
Rotate the rug every 3–6 months to ensure even wear across high-traffic zones, dramatically improving its long-term life. This also helps prevent carpet patterns from forming due to sun exposure—yes, even with modern synthetic dyes the sun can become a cruel artist.

### Drying and Airing
If you spill something, air-dry the rug thoroughly after cleaning. Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods to prevent color fading. If you’re in a humid climate, ensure the rug dries completely to prevent mildew growth; a sunny day or a well-ventilated room will work wonders here.

### Careful Cautions
- Avoid abrasive cleaners and bleach.
- Keep sharp objects away from the surface to prevent snagging.
- Use furniture coasters to distribute weight and avoid dent marks.
- Consider a rug pad for underfoot comfort, insulation, and to reduce slippage.
