## Rules and Setup
UNO’s core premise is simple: match color or number, use special cards to shift the table’s mood, don’t forget to say “UNO” when you’re down to one card. With the All Wild Card 112-card decks, the devil is in the extra wilds. The two-pack invites you to either merge the two decks into one colossal deck or keep them separate for parallel tracks of chaos.

Setup is straightforward:
- Shuffle each deck separately at first, then decide whether you’re merging or playing in parallel.
- If you’re merging, combine the decks and shuffle thoroughly. Expect more shuffle-induced chaos and, if you’re lucky, a chance to derail someone’s color choice with an all-new Wild variant.
- Deal seven cards to each player for standard rounds. If you love long sessions, go for eight to nine cards and bask in the extra mulligan potential. If you’re feeling ruthless, start with five cards and watch the chaos erupt quickly.

Key rules to keep in mind with All Wild Card flavor:
- Wild cards can typically be played on any color. In this set, some wild variants bend or twist color matching in new ways, so be ready to enforce or ignore new rules sweetly and firmly, depending on your table.
- The additional wild types are designed to introduce actions that affect all players, a team-up vibe that older UNO challengers may not have expected at a cozy game night.
- When someone calls UNO, everyone pays attention. In this two-pack, calling UNO may feel more like a ceremonial command because the wilds can swing momentum in surprising ways.

To help you navigate, a neat piece of advice: establish a quick policy about “house rules” before you start. ### Card variants inside the 112-card deck
The All Wild Card edition emphasizes interaction over silent rounds. While standard UNO focuses on color matching and strategic plays, the wild card roster adds several new flavors:
- Swap Hands Wild: allows a sudden hand swap with any opponent. Yes, this one sparks mid-round identity crises and a few dramatic rail passes across the table.
- Draw Everyone Wild: a wild that compels every other player to draw two or more cards depending on the variant in play. It’s a dramatic reveal that lights up the table with groans and excited whispers about the next round.
- Color Lock Wild: forces a color lock for a single color for the next turn chain; it’s a mind-game that makes players suddenly question color choices and why they ever believed yellow was a good idea.
- Skip Everyone: a wild option that can skip every player except the caller. This is the chaos in a bottle—one moment you’re waiting for a color, the next you’re staring at a moonlight of emptiness as everyone gasps in disbelief.

The practical effect is that rounds can swing wildly, which makes for incredibly fun memory stones of game-night chaos. It also means you’ll need to be consistent with house rules so that the chaos doesn’t become random chaos for the sake of chaos. The trick is to find a balance that amplifies laughter without turning every game into a drawn-out, rule-riddled ping-pong.
