## The Sorting Strategy How to Approach 1000 Cards

### Step 1 Quick scan
You will do a fast pass to identify the obvious keepers cards with immediate appeal nice foiling sets with good reprint potential and anything that stands out with unusual printing. You will also zero in on edge damage misprints and any unusual corners.

### Step 2 Rarity stratification
Sort into mythics rares uncommons and commons. This is your macro level pass that helps you understand the distribution. It is a good practice to create separate stacks foils and non foils with duplicates tracked in a quick run list.

### Step 3 Condition notes
Add a quick note for each cards condition and any obvious defects. The goal is to make a rough ROI estimate that can be refined later it will save you time when you actually price check.

### Step 4 The sample sizing trick
If you are dealing with a time sink use the 10 card sample approach. This is a quick practice pick 10 cards from the lot randomly and check price ranges. This will give you both a sanity check and a baseline for estimating the full value.

### Step 5 Documentation
Create a basic inventory with fields like card name set rarity foil status condition and notes. Its not fun but its the backbone of any bulk buy. You will want this both for your own clarity and for any resellers or potential trades.

### Step 6 Slotted storage
Set aside space to store the foils separately the high value rares in top loaders and the rest in a binder or deck box. The risk of shipping damage or humidity damage increases if you shove everything into a single tote.

## Design and Condition The Fine Print

### Packaging
The packaging is the first indicator of a sellers approach. A robust well padded packaging approach reduces the risk of bent corners and crushed foils. If the box is heavy and the padding dense you are in safer territory if the package looks flimsy expect more edge wear Its not just aesthetics it affects the long term value of the collection.

### Card edge wear
Look for white edge lines especially on borderless or foiled cards. This is often the telltale sign of wear that reduces resale value. Edge wear on foils can be particularly obvious sometimes revealing the underlying card surface.

### Surface scratches
Foils are most vulnerable to surface scratches some might be visible only under bright light. However certain scratches can be devastating to the value of a foil.

### Centering and printing
Centering is a big deal in MTG. In a bulk lot you will see cards with good centering and others with off center prints. The distribution will affect playability if you plan to use these cards in deck building.

### Subtlety in the grind
In a bulk pool there is a certain grind to discovering that one card you truly want. It often comes when you find a sleeper rare that has both playability and price stability. The fun is the chase the thrill of hunting that one perfect card that makes the whole pile worth it.
