### Care and maintenance: keeping the fluff under control

We all know spilled coffee is not a crime when you own a rug like this, but it does require a plan. Wool fibers are relatively easy to care for, and a routine should keep them looking bright and fresh. For routine cleaning, vacuum with a gentle, no-beater setting. Avoid high suction on edge seams to minimize wear. Blots are your friend when you have spills; the sooner you blot the area, the better your chances of avoiding a stain. For more serious marks, a mild wool-safe cleaner applied following label instructions is recommended. Always test a small, inconspicuous area before treating a stain to confirm that the color and texture will not be compromised.

Rotation helps too. If you have strong sunlight in one corner, consider rotating the rug occasionally to distribute fading evenly and keep the color uniform. Wool holds dye well, but sunlight can still take a toll over long periods. If you live in a dry climate, you might need to adjust humidity or use a rug pad to maintain loft and shape. A good rug pad not only extends the life of the rug but also improves underfoot comfort and reduces slipping on hard floors.

### Durability and performance under real world conditions

Durability is a big concern for any rug that occupies high-traffic zones. Wool has a natural elasticity that helps resist flattening but that does not mean it is invincible. The Crown White Wool Rug is typically built to handle everyday living—kids bouncing around with toy cars, pets running loops around the coffee table, and you stepping on it barefoot at 7 in the morning while mumbling about Mondays. A higher density weave and proper backing can reduce wear, maintain shape, and keep the edges neat after vacuuming and cleaning.

In terms of stain resistance, wool fibers can resist some liquids and often respond well to blotting. However, there is no free pass for red wine or curry sauce. Quick action is key; the sooner you blot and treat with a wool-safe cleaner, the more likely you will preserve the rug’s whiteness and texture. Let us be clear: this rug is not a magical stain shield, but with proper care it can look pristine for many years.

### How to order and what to expect

Ordering the Crown White Wool Rug is a multi-step process, largely dependent on whether you go custom or standard. Here is a practical rundown of what to expect:

- Step 1: Measure your space carefully. Note the length and width in inches and determine if you want a border around the seating area. A paper template on the floor is a good way to visualize the space.
- Step 2: Decide on pile height and density. A higher pile is cosier but can be harder to clean; a low pile looks sleeker and is easier to maintain. If you share your space with kids or pets, you might favor a shorter pile for practicality.
- Step 3: Choose the rug backing and any border or edge details. A sturdy backing helps prevent slipping and edges from curling.
- Step 4: Place your order with the retailer or maker. For custom sizes, you will typically provide precise dimensions and confirm the production options. For an 8x10, you select this size from the catalog and proceed to checkout.
- Step 5: Lead times vary. Custom orders often take longer than standard options, so you might anticipate several weeks of waiting. The upside is that the rug will be crafted to your exact specifications, making it a truly unique piece for your space.
- Step 6: Shipping and delivery. Most reputable sellers provide tracking, and many will offer white glove delivery or in-home setup. If you are in a high-traffic habitat, you might want to arrange delivery when you or a friend are around to receive and place the rug precisely where you want it.
