## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping It Bright and White
White surfaces can show dust or smudges more readily than darker tones. The good news is that maintenance is simple: a damp microfiber cloth, a mild cleaner, and a moment of patient wipe-downs when you want the desk to look showroom-fresh again. The matte finish resists stubborn marks better than a glossy top, which means you can avoid the “mirror effect” when you lean in for a design sketch. If you accidentally scratch the surface with a hobby knife, don’t panic. Minor scratches can be sanitized with a gentle polisher and then treated with a protective wax that doesn’t yellow over time.

For ongoing upkeep, consider adding a small desk caddy with a blotter for spills, a shelf for manuals, and a corner organizer for cables. The goal is a desk that ages gracefully, like a fine cheese, not a stubborn block of plastic that refuses to yield to a sponge.
