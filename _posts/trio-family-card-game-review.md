## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setup is a breeze. Shuffle the deck, deal three cards to each player, and place the rest in a draw pile. That's it. You're ready to go.

### Turns and Actions

On your turn, you draw a card from the deck and try to form sets of three matching cards (hence the name Trio). If you manage to collect a set, you place it face-up in front of you. The player with the most sets at the end of the game wins. Sounds simple, right? Well, there's a twist. Players can steal sets from each other by remembering what's already been played and calling out the card they need.

### Strategy and Memory

Trio is all about strategy and memory. You need to keep track of which cards have already been played and anticipate which cards your opponents might have. It's like playing poker with cartoon characters.
