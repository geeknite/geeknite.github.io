Setup and Play Overview

Phase 10 is famously straightforward to learn but deceptively tricky to master. In standard Phase 10, players attempt to complete ten specific phases in order, with the caveat that you can only advance to the next phase once you’ve completed the current one. You draw, discard, and, if you’re lucky or strategic, you glide through a series of hands that look suspiciously like a subset of Rummy meets a lighthearted puzzle.

The Deluxe Edition doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it refinements the wheel. Setup is quick: open tin, sort decks, deal cards, and explain the ten phases (each player begins on Phase 1 and works upward). The rulebook is surprisingly clear for a game that is really about memorizing and manipulating a handful of patterns. The ten phases, to recap, are typically a mixture of sets and runs: three 3-of-a-kind, a run of four, a full house, and so on. The specifics can vary slightly by edition, but the core idea remains the same: finish a phase to unlock the next, and hope you don’t end up stuck on Phase 7 while everyone else is sprinting toward Phase 10.
