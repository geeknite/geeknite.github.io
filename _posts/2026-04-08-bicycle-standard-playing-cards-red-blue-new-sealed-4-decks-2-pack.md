## Cares and Maintenance Tips
### Keeping your decks fresh
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading and stock warping.
- Avoid bending or stamping the tuck boxes; they’re designed to protect the decks rather than serve as stress toys.
- If you’re a magic enthusiast, consider a card scrub: a light fan-out test and a gentle wipe of the backs to maintain grip without transferring oils from your skin.
- For cardists, a gentle cleaning routine with a microfiber cloth can help keep faces crisp and pips legible under stage lighting.

### When to retire a deck
If you notice excessive whitening on edges, a persistent bend that won’t resolve after a few shuffles, or if the color on the back starts transferring onto the faces, it’s time to retire that deck. Even the best decks hit their limit some day; the trick is knowing when to swap in a fresh set so your game or routine isn’t sabotaged by a stubborn deck quitter.
