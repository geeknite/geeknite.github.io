## Cares and Maintenance Tips
### Keeping your decks fresh
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading and stock warping.
- Avoid bending or mistreating tuck boxes; they’re protective shells, not stress toys.
- For magicians, a light card scrub—gentle fan-out tests and a quick wipe of the back surfaces—can help maintain grip without transferring oils from your skin.
- For cardists, a microfiber cloth in between sessions can help keep faces crisp and pips legible under stage lighting.

### When to retire a deck
If you notice excessive whitening on edges, persistent bending that won’t recover with shuffles, or back color transfer onto the faces, it’s time to retire that deck. Even the best decks have a breaking point; the trick is to swap in a fresh set so your game or routine isn’t sabotaged by a stubborn deck quitter.
