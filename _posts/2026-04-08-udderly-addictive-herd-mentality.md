## How to Play: Setup and Core Mechanics
Udderly Addictive follows a simple loop that even non-gamers can grok after a single practice round. The goal is not to out-logic your friends but to ride the herd—after all, social momentum is a powerful thing.

Setup is quick: shuffle the Prompt Cards, deal a hand of Answer Cards to each player, and place the Herd Meter within easy reach. The player who most recently watched a documentary about cows becomes the first “Herder” for the round. The Hearder (yes, we’re intentionally misspelling to moo your attention) picks a Prompt Card and reads it aloud without showing it to the others. This prompt is the seed of the round’s chaos: a statement or question that invites a variety of responses.

Each player secretly chooses an Answer Card from their hand that would be a plausible audience-pleaser for the given prompt. The kicker is that these answers are not read aloud yet. Instead, they’re collected and revealed—either all at once or in a dramatic mini-drama where you pretend to be a game show host.

Here’s where the herdiness comes in: once all answers are laid out, players vote on which answer they think is the most popular among the group. The twist? You can’t vote for your own card. The most votes earns points and possibly the coveted Herd Meter boost for that round, while the remaining players shuffle their cards with the dignity of squeeze bottles at a picnic.

What makes this section shine is the social feedback loop. People see which answers are trending, which prompts are reliably gut-busters, and which players consistently nail the “mass preference” vibe. It’s not about being clever in a vacuum; it’s about tapping into the shared mental shortcuts of the room. That, friends, is the essence of the herd.

If your group loves competitive banter, this is a playground. If your group hates banter, prepare for the occasional silent recalibration as someone tries to game the system with ultra-predictable answers. Either way, the game is a compact social experiment: you learn a little about your friends, you laugh a lot, and you realize that sometimes the simplest ideas become the most entertaining experiences.
