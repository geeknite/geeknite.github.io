## Overclocking Guide: Quick Tips for the Curious
- Start with XMP: Enable the 3600 MT/s profile in your BIOS. This is often the simplest, safest path to good performance.
- If stability requires it: Increase DRAM voltage very gradually, in 0.02V steps, while watching temperatures and system stability tests. Do not exceed 1.40V unless you know what you’re doing and you have adequate cooling.
- Tuning subtimings: If you want to experiment, tighten CL a step or two (e.g., CL15) while ensuring primary timings stay stable. Remember that not all chips scale evenly; some kits love tighter CL with looser other timings, while others respond best to tighter overall timings with modest CL.
- Monitor with care: Use a memory benchmark tool and a stress test to ensure stability across sustained workloads. Take a few hours of real-world use (gaming, rendering, or VM workloads) to confirm reliability before pushing into production.
