## Durability and Care: How long before your denims retire?

Durability is the unsung hero of any rug review. Quarry Denim promises a resilient face with a backing designed for longevity. The pile surface is designed to resist flattening under typical family life, but like any textile, it will show wear in high-traffic zones over time. The key to longevity is routine maintenance: vacuum regularly with a gentle setting, rotate the rug every 3-6 months to avoid permanent traffic patterns, and address spills promptly with a blot-and-clean approach. If you’re one of those people who neglect cleaning until the dog starts considering the rug a chew toy, you’ll want to treat this rug like a semi-sentient friend: give it a quick once-over weekly and a deeper clean every few months.

### Spill management and stain strategies

Spills happen. The denim-inspired palette can hide minor stains better than a solid black rug, but avoid letting substances soak in for too long. For most household spills, blot gently with a clean cloth, then use a mild solution as needed. Avoid harsh bleach-based cleaners that can dull the color or damage the fibers. For more stubborn spots, consider a fabric-safe stain remover and always test in a hidden corner first. The goal is to preserve the denim character while keeping the surface pleasant to walk on and visually appealing.

### Cleaning routines you can actually keep

A straightforward routine works best: weekly vacuum, rotate, and spot-clean. If you have pets, you’ll appreciate a rug that doesn’t immediately show every hair or footprint—though you may still see a few carbon paw prints from adventurous kitties. In the long run, the Quarry Denim’s color and texture tend to hide minor daily abuse better than lighter-toned options, which makes it a pragmatic choice for busy households with a sense of fashion.
