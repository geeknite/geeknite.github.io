H2: Maintenance and Care

Taking care of stainless steel blades is not rocket science, but it is a ritual. Wipe the blade after use to remove moisture and acids that can cause corrosion. If you are using the knife in salt air or near oceans, a quick rinse and thorough dry will help keep the coating from dulling and prevent staining. The TPR handle responds well to light cleaning with mild soap and water; avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the grip texture over time. If you want to extend the life of the edge, a routine light sharpening with a medium grit stone or a basic ceramic rod will keep the bevels performing without removing too much material. Storage is a good habit for any fixed blade; keep the sheath clean and dry, and avoid storing the knife in extremely humid places for prolonged periods. A well-cared-for Honshu Sub Hilt Tanto will serve you through a camping trip or a weekend cosplay with equal reliability.
