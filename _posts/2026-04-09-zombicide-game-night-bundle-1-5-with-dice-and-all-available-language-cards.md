## Language Cards: A Tiny, Mighty Helper

Language cards are not flashy, but they’re the unsung heroes of a good Zombicide evening. They help new players jump into the action without a crash course in academic gobbledygook. They also prevent you from spending ten minutes arguing about whether a line on a card is “you can” or “you cannot” in an alternate reality where you misread a font weight. The Game Night Bundle 1-5 includes a wide range of language cards, usually English, French, German, Spanish, Italian, and Portuguese packs. This means you won’t need a bilingual dictionary the size of a small novel to get your turn order right. If you’re playing with a multilingual crew, you’ll be grateful for the consistency across titles. If you aren’t, well, you’ll still enjoy the crisp iconography and clear text that keeps the brain-dead hordes from winning by default.

One tiny caveat: language cards, while abundant, do take up space and attention. It’s not a problem if you’re a rulebook-obsessed savant, but if your crew is more “hammer-and-dice” than “read-and-reflect,” you might need a quick-start cheat sheet to speed things up. The bundle does a good job of including a concise set of reminders for each game, which is a win in my book. You’ll be able to tell at a glance who’s carrying the medic’s kit, who’s got the shotgun, and which survivor’s special ability actually gets you a better result than pure brute force.
