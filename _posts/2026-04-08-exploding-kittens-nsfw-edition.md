## How to Play (A Quick Rundown for Those Recovering from a Snack Break)

If you’ve played standard Exploding Kittens, you know the vibe: you want to avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten. If you draw one, you’re out unless you can Defuse with a Defuse card or somehow outsmart the deck with a strategic misdirection. The NSFW edition follows the same core rhythm but with more adult humor and a few rules tweaks to accommodate the extra spice. Here’s a brisk refresher for those returning to the battlefield after a long snack break:

### Setup

- Shuffle the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck according to the number of players (the more players, the more kittens are in the abyss).
- Each player gets a hand of seven cards, including at least one Defuse card if possible. If you’re playing with a lightly used deck, you may find one Defuse card tucked under a favorite prom-posal meme card—this is normal and adds to the memory lane.
- Place the remaining Defuse cards back into the deck. The Exploding Kitten cards go into the main draw pile. The rest of the action cards weave into the deck so that every draw could be a mini-heart attack or a strategic escape hatch.

### Core Mechanics
- On your turn, you can play as many cards as you want, as long as you have the resources. The goal is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten, or to survive if you do.
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten, immediately reveal it. Unless you have a Defuse card, you’re out of the round. If you do have a Defuse card, you play it and secretly place the Exploding Kitten back into the draw pile somewhere you can’t survive the next few rounds (or you can be devilishly clever and place it where your opponents will get excited for their own misfortune).
- Attack, See the Future, Shuffle, Skip—these are your tools for misdirection, predicting, or repositioning doom. In NSFW editions, some of these cards have names and art that lean into meme culture and adult humor, which is part of the charm (and potential awkwardness at family gatherings).

### What Differentiates NSFW Edition from the Base Game

- Hand management is your best friend. You’ll want to balance fear with tempo—playing a See the Future card to peek at upcoming cards can be massively strategic, especially if you’re not the loudest voice in the room.
- Defuse timing is everything. If you draw a kitten and don’t have a Defuse, you’re out. If you do, you’ve got to place the kitten back in the deck in the most inconvenient place possible for your friends. This is where sarcasm and misdirection come into play.
- Bluff and callouts: in NSFW edition, players often call “Stop bragging about your Defuse!” which means you’re doing something right. Use the humor to keep the energy high and the tension palpable.
