## Setup and Rules: Quick Start Guide

If you already know how to play the base Dizzy Date, you’re basically halfway there. The Deeper Questions Expansion adds a few new layers of decision-making and social nuance. Here’s how to get started without needing a two-hour tutorial:

1) Gather players: Ideally 4–8 adults, though more is fine if you have the stamina and a supervising adult sense of humor about it. 
2) Prepare drinks (or mocktails): This is a drinking game, not a hydration seminar. If you’re playing with minors or folks who don’t drink, there are non-alcoholic alternatives and some prompt cards that can be used in non-alcoholic mode.
3) Combine decks: Separate the base deck from the Deeper Questions deck. You can shuffle them together for a mixed experience or draw from each deck in alternating turns for a two-layer itinerary of fun.
4) Decide on consent rules: The expansion includes more intimate prompts. Before you start, set expectations. In practice, you can opt for a “skip/alternate” rule on any card that feels too personal or uncomfortable. The spirit here is fun, not policing boundaries.
5) Start playing: The person who drew the card determines who answers or who performs the action (or both, depending on your group’s vibe). Cards may require players to share, guess, or perform a small task. 

The flow remains familiar: draw a card, read aloud, respond as a group, and accumulate points (or not—many groups I’ve observed care more about laughs than numeric tallies). The Deeper Questions deception-free approach means you’ll likely hear stories you never expected to hear, delivered with a wink and a grimace that says, “I regret nothing.”

## Safety, Consent, and Inclusivity

I’m not going to pretend the Deeper Questions Expansion is a safety manual, but it does include thoughtful steps that are worth repeating:
- Clear consent reminders at the start and optional “skip” cards on cards that probe topics some players might find too personal.
- Language that’s mostly respectful. There are awkwardly funny prompts, but you won’t see it slipping into mean-spirited territory the way some rehearsed party games can when played by far too many drinkers in a single room.
- An explicit emphasis on inclusivity. The prompts are crafted to avoid gendered assumptions and to invite a wide range of experiences.

If you’re running a game night with brand-new friends, these safety-first touches can be acids-tested by the room’s energy. The “skip” mechanic is crucial for preventing the vibe from tipping into something unfunny or invasive. The best guidance I can offer is to model the behavior you want to see: be the first to demonstrate consent, show appreciation for honesty, and be ready to gracefully bow out of a prompt when the moment demands it. The box is a guide, but you’re the real game master here.

## Tips for Maximizing Fun

- Set a loose rhythm: 2–3 questions per round, then switch to a lighter prompt to reset energy. You don’t want one heavy topic to derail the entire night.
- Use the base game as a palate cleanser between deeper prompts. A few quick, silly rounds help maintain balance.
- Create “themes” ahead of time: one round could be about favorite fictional characters, another about worst dating disasters, etc. The expansion lends itself to these playful arcs.
- Acknowledge great moments: someone’s unexpectedly heartfelt response or a surprising admission deserves a clap or a toast. It reinforces trust and makes future revelations feel welcome.
- For new players: designate a “mentor” at the table who can explain prompts with calm humor instead of letting new players drown in a wall of questions.
