### Art style and design critique
CCGHouse leans into what Dixit fans adore: soft color palettes, surreal but approachable imagery, and a color language that screams mood rather than slogan. The five cards in this set sit comfortably alongside the original artwork, sharing textures that feel like they belong to the same family portrait but with a wild cousin who wore a hat made of stars to the party.

Pros:
- Cohesive aesthetic: The new promos feel like they were painted in the same studio as the base deck rather than sculpted in a lab somewhere. This helps keep a unified table presence when shuffled together.
- Mood-forward prompts: The imagery invites storytelling angles tied to memory, dream logic, and myth, which is the bread and butter of Dixit sessions.
- Strong color and contrast: Even in dim lighting, the art retains readability and a sense of depth, which matters for quick card peeking and guesswork.

Cons:

- A handful of images may share overlapping motifs, which could feel repetitive if you’re using promos in every round for an extended campaign.

In practice, the art feels like a celebration of Dixit’s essence: the line between “pretty” and “meaningful” is negotiated with care, and these five cards don’t dominate the session; they elevate the vibe.

## How to use the promos in Dixit
Dixit’s genius lies in the tension between a storyteller’s clue and a set of image cards that could plausibly fit or betray that clue. The promos are a tool to tilt that tension toward fresh interpretations, not to erase the core balance.

### Quick integration guide
1) Decide on the ratio: base deck only, base deck plus promos, or a dedicated promo-only round. For most groups, a 1:4 or 1:5 mix works nicely; you’ll get the new imagery without overwhelming the known cards.
2) Shuffle with care: a well-seeded shuffle ensures these five random images don’t land in a predictable pattern. The goal is to surprise, not to create a predictable “promo cadence.”
3) Plan your storyteller rotation: with new art, it’s often best to let different players introduce prompts that align with the promos’ themes. This reduces the risk of one player monopolizing the “best match” angle.

### Story prompts and synergy
Each promo card is designed to invite a particular storytelling texture. Here are hypothetical prompts you might encounter (and how they might shape play):
- “Echoes of a forgotten carnival”: encourages memory-based interpretations and playful misdirection.

- “The train of parallel moments”: nudges players toward a chain of memories or a sequence of events that feel connected yet disparate.
- “A garden of impossible doors”: ripe for metaphorical clues about choices, opportunities, and hidden identities.
- “The creature who learned to dream in daylight”: invites a surreal, personified approach—animals, beings, or objects with an inner life.

### Balancing and pacing
The main risk with promos is the temptation to lean into abstract art so hard that players stop recognizing the prompt’s emotional beat. The antidote is to use a few rounds with explicit prompts in the early game, then mix in rounds where the storyteller is encouraged to craft a clue from the promo’s mood rather than a literal interpretation. When used thoughtfully, promos never derail Dixit; they expand its expressive vocabulary.
