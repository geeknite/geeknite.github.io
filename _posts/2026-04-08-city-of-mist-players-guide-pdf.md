## Overview

City of Mist is a game about legends wearing ordinary clothes, dreams stitched into the urban night, and the city itself as a living, breathing antagonist. The Players Guide PDF is a player facing companion that sits alongside the core rulebook and offers a deep dive into how to inhabit a mask, how to pick your mythic identity, and how to navigate the strange blend of noir city intrigue and supernatural traffic. If you thought the citys graffiti was weird in the base game, wait until you see the tips, prompts, and new options inside this PDF. It is not a replacement for the core rules, but an expansion pack with a tone that fits the game without turning it into a different system.

## What the PDF covers

The PDF is divided into three broad areas: expanded character options, toolkit resources for players, and run ready prompts and sandbox ideas for sessions. Youll find new masks, new relationships options, and refined advice on how to bring your mythic identity to life at the table. The writing is breezy, patient, and slightly witty; it understands that players want to feel legendary while also not getting crushed by the weight of the city.

### Masks and identity

A big chunk of the guide is dedicated to Masks, the core conceit that every PC in City of Mist has a mask a mythic identity they play to the hilt when the city pushes on them. The Players Guide expands this with a handful of new Masks, each with a suggested backstory sketch, a few example prompts, and sample power options. The benefit here is twofold: players can lean into a theme they love, and GMs get ready to use hooks that feel organic rather than tacked on.

### Relics, clues, and powers

In addition to Masks, the PDF adds more relics and a few new power concepts. This helps players tailor their hero vibe without bloating the sheet with arcane, hard to remember mechanics. The relics are designed to feel like borrowed myths rather than magical items with numeric envelopes that derail narrative flow. It is a style that fits City of Mist because everything in this setting should feel mythic and slightly ephemeral.

### Relationships and story scaffolding

One of the bookish staples the PDF improves is how players collaborate with each other and with the city through relationships. There are ready made relationship prompts, including rivalries, loyalties, and the occasional tragic link that makes a scene pop with emotional weight. If you run a table that thrives on character drama, this section is a gold mine. It helps players build a shared city of myths that is bigger than any single hero.

### Mechanics and clarity

City of Mist uses a light, abstract mechanics scheme that emphasizes narrative beats over crunchy math. The Players Guide respects that design ethos and adds clarity where players tend to stall for example, at the point where a character tries to invoke a power or a mythic identity. The guide offers clear phrasing for power usage, a few ready to run prompts for failed rolls, and a handful of example scenes that show rather than tell. The aim is to keep the momentum up and avoid the dreaded table stalling lull.

### Art, layout, and accessibility

Visually, the PDF continues City of Mists moody noir aesthetic with glossy panels, stylized fonts, and art that announces mood without overshadowing readability. The layout feels designed for quick scanning during a game session. Important sections use callouts, boxed tips, and sidebars that you can actually read when the lights are dim and the city sirens are wailing outside. The accessibility note is also welcome; there are suggestions for players who are new to the system, along with a few tips for screen readers and color blind friendly color choices.

## How to use this PDF at the table

### Session prep

If you run a weekly city run, you can use the PDF as a session zero companion to align players on their masks and establish a handful of mythic threads. The prompts inside function as a warm up exercise that helps players articulate their character voice and finally decide how their hero will integrate with the citys ongoing mysteries. The prompts are also a good fit for one shot sessions, where you need quick anchors to get players into their masks fast.

### Play by post and online sessions

For online games, the PDF provides concise guidance on describing action in a smaller screen space. The prompts are easy to paste into chat windows, and the relationship questions are ideal for asynchronous play where you want to keep every character lane flowing.

### GM and player collaboration

The guide also emphasizes collaboration; it suggests that players bring strong ideas about their backstory, then the GM builds the city around those ideas rather than forcing a pre baked set of events. The city should feel alive and reactive, not a fixed stage where players only walk through scripted beats. The PDF includes a short exercise that helps players and GMs align on tone before play begins.

### Design, tone, and writing style

If you love the vibe of City of Mist but crave a touch more elegance in the prose, you will enjoy the writing approach here. It is accessible, playful, and confident but not arrogant. The authors know when to crack a joke and when to let a moment breathe. The humor lands in the right places without clobbering you with winks and nods, which matters during tense scenes.

### Artwork, layout, and production values

The cover image and internal art maintain the book's brand, with silhouettes and street level imagery that echo the core rulebook. The layout uses smaller blocks and pulse color to guide your eyes through a page, and the typography feels intentional rather than arbitrary. If you have ever skipped a supplement because the page count looked intimidating, this PDF is friendlier than many. It reads like something you want to leave on the table rather than in a drawer.

## Value for players and GMs

The Players Guide shines when you are trying to expand the roster of Masks while maintaining the themes that City of Mist does best myth meets detective noir in a city that feels larger than life. For players who are new to the setting, this guide provides a safe entry point with well crafted examples and prompts. For veterans, it is a treasure trove of ideas that can spark a fresh arc with minimal prep. It is not the core rulebook, so you do not get a full rules overhaul; instead it offers a curated set of enhancements, with an eye toward reliability at the table.

## Comparison to the core rulebook and other supplements

The core rulebook is a toolbox; this PDF is a guided tour through a particular room of that toolbox. It does not rewrite the foundational mechanics; it builds upon them with more options and more approach angles to the mythic modern genre. If you own other supplements, you will recognize a familiar voice here, but the content feels tuned for players who want more texture around identity rather than chase new combat options or power scaling.

## Running a campaign that nods to the PDF

If you are running a campaign that uses citywide mysteries, the new prompts can quickly yield a few memorable scenes. You can drop a Masks driven scene into an episode with minimal prep a single relationship trigger, a relic that changes the status quo, and a tight scene description that pushes the characters toward a moral or mythic decision. The PDF also includes a few ready made set pieces that you can lift directly into your session with only minor adjustments for tone.

## Using the PDF with other City of Mist materials

For players and GMs who want a coherent experience across multiple books, treat the Players Guide as a bridge between the core rules and the setting specific supplements. The prompts in the guide complement the City of Mist wiki and other free resources that help keep the world consistent. If you like to cross check ideas with a familiar list of themes, this PDF will feel like a natural extension rather than a strange bolt on.

## Tips for first time players

If you are new to City of Mist, here are a few quick tips to get the most out of the PDF

- Start with a single mask theme that resonates with your personal myth. Let the city do the heavy lifting of developing the rest of the identity as you play.
- Use the prompts to build your relationship network early; it pays off in in game tension and narrative momentum.
- Don’t worry about fully enumerating all relics on day one. Pick a few you love and let others emerge from play.

## A few notes on accessibility and inclusivity

The PDF acknowledges that players come from diverse backgrounds and experiences. It offers inclusive language suggestions, options for players who prefer to keep some things vague, and tips on creating NPCs and scenes that avoid harmful stereotypes. It also includes quick adjustments for people who prefer lighter or heavier mechanical weight in their sessions. If your table has different accessibility needs, consider using the prompts and visuals in a way that works for your group rather than forcing a one size fits all approach.
