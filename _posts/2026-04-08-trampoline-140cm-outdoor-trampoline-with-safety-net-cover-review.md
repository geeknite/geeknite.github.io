## Accessories, Setup Tips, and Longevity Hacks
### Assembly Do’s and Don ts
- Lay out all parts and confirm nothing is missing before you begin. A clean workspace makes assembly faster and less frustrating. 
- Follow the sequence in the manual rather than improvising. It saves you the broken hardware heartbreak and needless swearing. 
- Check the frame squarely on level ground; a skewed base leads to wobble and misalignment of the net enclosure.
- Tighten fasteners in a systematic pattern to avoid uneven stress on the frame.

### Safety Add-ons and Maintenance Tricks
- Use the padding around springs consistently; a small bump against an exposed spring is not the kind of science experiment you want to repeat.
- Periodically inspect the net for tears; small rips can become larger with use, turning a safe bounce into a dangerous one. 
- Keep the jumping area clear of obstacles. The last thing you want is a rogue garden tool interrupting a perfect mid-air twist.

### Cleaning and Longevity
Wipe down the pads with a mild soap solution and let them dry completely. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners which can degrade the material. This is not a spaceship hull; you don t want to risk materials breaking down under repeated exposure to sun and water. A light annual maintenance cycle will help you get multiple seasons of joy out of the setup.
