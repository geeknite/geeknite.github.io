## Safety First: Standards, Care, and Real-World Use

Safety is more than padding; it’s a system. The Morgan mat is designed to address common household training risks: inadequate padding, unexpected hard surfaces, and the occasional “I swear I didn’t jump from the coffee table” moment.

- Layered protection: The 20 cm depth provides a robust buffer for a range of moves, from gentle bridge kickovers to more ambitious tucked landings.
- Maintenance: The vinyl cover is wipeable and relatively stain resistant. It loves a good disinfectant wipe after a sweaty session and appreciates being dried properly to prevent mold growth.
- Floor compatibility: Works on hardwood, laminate, tile, and low-pile carpet. It may be less ideal on extremely uneven floors unless you place a sturdy underlayment or a thin sub-floor mat under it to prevent wobbles.

Usage safety reminders:
- Always ensure the mat is placed on a flat, non-slip surface before training.
- Use a clear area around the mat with no hard obstacles on the landing zone.
- For kids and beginners, set the complexity level gradually to avoid overambitious leaps.
- Check seams and covers regularly for wear and tear. If you see cracking or exposed foam, retire the mat from high-impact routines and replace.

## Setup, Storage, and Maintenance

Setting up the Morgan 20cm Crash Mat is less about engineering and more about common sense:

- Placement: Find a spacious area with plenty of room to roll out and completely b-r-e-a-t-h-e. A cleared living room or garage is ideal.
- Anchoring: The mat’s weight helps keep it in place, but in super windy garage sessions, some people use a few non-slip mats or gym tape along the perimeter to reduce movement.
- Cleaning: Wipe with mild detergent or a vinyl-safe cleaner. Avoid harsh solvents that might degrade the vinyl over time.
- Storage: When not in use, store it flat in a cool, dry place. If you have to roll it, roll with the surface facing outward to minimize creases in the vinyl cover.

Maintenance tips:
- Periodically check the seams for loose threads and re-stitch if you have the know-how or seek professional repair for larger issues.
- Clean up spills quickly to prevent staining; a quick wipe-down after a sweaty session goes a long way toward preserving the cover’s appearance.

## Cleaning, Longevity, and Care

A crash mat is not forever, but it can last a while with proper care. Here’s how to keep your Morgan mat looking and feeling solid:

- Regular cleaning: Wipe after use to remove sweat and oils that accumulate over time. A mild soap solution works well.
- Odor control: If you notice an odor over time, a light spray with a vinyl-safe deodorizer can help. Let it dry completely before your next session.
- Stains: Most stains wipe away with a damp cloth and mild detergent. For stubborn spots, a soft brush works wonders without harming the vinyl.
- Storage: Keep it flat when possible. If you must roll it, avoid rolling too tightly to minimize creases. Rolled, store in a dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent vinyl fading.
