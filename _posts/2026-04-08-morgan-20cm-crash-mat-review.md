## Safety First: Standards, Care, and Real-World Use (Updated Guidance)

Safety isn’t padding; it’s a system. The Morgan mat is designed to address common household training risks: inadequate padding, unexpected hard surfaces, and the occasional “I swear I didn’t jump from the coffee table” moment.

- Layered protection: The 20 cm depth provides a robust buffer for a range of moves, from gentle bridge kickovers to more ambitious tucked landings.
- Maintenance: The vinyl cover is wipeable and stain-resistant. It appreciates a quick wipe after sweaty sessions and thorough drying to prevent mold or funky gym socks vibes.
- Floor compatibility: Works on hardwood, laminate, tile, and low-pile carpet. Extremely uneven floors may require underlayment; otherwise, you risk wobbly landings that make your cat start a second career as a referee.

Usage safety reminders:
- Always ensure the mat is on a flat, non-slip surface before training.
- Clear a landing zone with no hard obstacles to mitigate ricochet risk.
- For kids and beginners, escalate complexity gradually to avoid chasing the dream of a 720 before breakfast.
- Inspect seams and covers regularly for wear and tear. If you see cracking or exposed foam, retire the mat from high-impact routines and consider replacement.

## Setup, Storage, and Maintenance (Operational Wisdom for the Home Gym)

Setting up the Morgan 20cm Crash Mat is mostly about human factors:

- Placement: Pick a spacious area with room to roll out and breathe. A cleared living room or garage is ideal; you’ll be surprised how much space tiny humans need for air and awkward poses.
- Anchoring: The mat’s own weight helps hold it in place, but in breezy garage sessions, some gym-savvy folks use a few non-slip mats or athletic tape along the edges to cut down on wanderlust.
- Cleaning: Wipe with mild detergent or a vinyl-safe cleaner. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade vinyl or trigger a mini-melting meltdown of your mood.
- Storage: Store flat when possible. If you must roll it, roll with the surface outward to minimize creases. Keep away from direct sunlight to preserve color and texture over time.

Maintenance tips:
- Periodically check seams for loose threads and re-stitch if you have the skills; otherwise, seek a professional for major issues.
- Clean up spills quickly to prevent staining. A quick wipe after sessions goes a long way toward preserving the cover’s aesthetic and longevity.
