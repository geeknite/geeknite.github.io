Maintenance and Longevity Tips

- Regularly inspect screws and braces to prevent wobble. A loose bolt is not a dramatic, cinematic moment; it’s a real problem when you’re mid-pass.
- Wipe the rods with a dry cloth after heavy play to minimize residue and keep things smooth. If you want to go extra, a tiny dab of silicone lubricant can help the rods glide with minimal resistance. Don’t go overboard—slippery rods lead to wild shots and more laughter than precision.
- Keep the playing surface clean. Dust can slow down the ball or create unpredictable bounces. A microfiber cloth and a little patience go a long way.
- Consider placing a soft rug or mat underneath to reduce floor noise and protect both the table and your floor from the occasional epic slide.
