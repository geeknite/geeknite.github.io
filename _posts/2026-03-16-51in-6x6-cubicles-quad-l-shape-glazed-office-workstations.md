## Build Quality and Materials

### Materials and Finish
The frame is built to withstand the daily grind of people knocking their knees into the edge while thinking about quarterly goals. The steel is usually heavy gauge enough to feel substantial but light enough to ship without an industrial fork. The panels are laminated particleboard or MDF, with edge banding to reduce wear. The glaze panels, typically tempered glass or high-quality acrylic, offer a clear view of your team while keeping your privacy within reach. The finish on the metal can resist chafing and fingerprints, and the wood/laminate surfaces hold up to coffee rings and the occasional lunch spill—assuming you wipe them promptly with a mild cleaner.

### Glass vs Acrylic Options
Glazed options are often a selling point for these quartets. Tempered glass looks premium and reflects a modern aesthetic, but it can be more prone to smudges and requires gentle cleaning. Acrylic panels are lighter and less prone to shattering but can scratch more easily. Depending on your office vibe, you might prefer glass for a high-end look or acrylic for a safer, lighter alternative. Either way, regular cleaning with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner is enough to keep things gleaming without inducing a superhero-level glare during video calls.

### Acoustics and Privacy
Acoustics in cubicle walls vary widely depending on panel thickness, core material, and whether you have fabric screens in the interior. The 51in height helps with the eye-to-eye contact while reducing the chance of your colleagues having a full-on open office moment. Expect the poly panels to dampen some noise, but not to make the space a private oracle. If your team needs whisper-quiet focus, consider adding acoustic panels or fabric screens to the inside of the cubicles, or define the quiet zones with rugs and plants to absorb some reflections.

## Assembly, Maintenance, and Durability

### Assembly Time and Tips
Assemble with a partner if you can; the four modules are not exactly light beach balls. A careful approach reduces misalignment and the risk of crossing threads. A typical four-person assembly can take between 2 and 4 hours, depending on your workspace and the chance that you drop a screw into the carpet abyss. Pro tip: lay out all parts in the order of assembly, label panels, and test-fit connections before fully tightening hardware. This saves you from the fate of a partially assembled unit while your coworkers pretend to be building a spaceship on a lunch break.

### Maintenance and Care
Maintenance is mostly a routine cleaning job. Glass panels: wipe with a microfiber cloth and a mild glass cleaner. Frames and wood surfaces: wipe with a damp cloth, then dry. For stubborn marks, a slightly damp microfiber with a tiny amount of dish soap works wonders without loosening the finish. If you encounter wobble due to loose bolts, do a quick torque check on the frame corners and adjust the levelers on the feet. The longer you own the system, the more you realize that a well-maintained setup lasts longer than a hasty, cheap alternative that collapses under a phone call about the quarterly budget.

### Durability and Warranty
Durability is where modular office furniture shines and sometimes hides a cheap trick. The quad configuration is designed to handle daily use, with reinforced corners and robust panel connections. Warranty terms vary by vendor, but you can typically expect several years of coverage on frame components and a separate warranty for panels. Always verify what is covered under the warranty, especially for the glass panels and the gaskets that seal the edges.
