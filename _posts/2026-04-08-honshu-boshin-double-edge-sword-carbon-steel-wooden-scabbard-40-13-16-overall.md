## Maintenance and care

Care is straightforward and sensible. Wipe the blade with a soft, dry cloth after handling, especially if your hands are oily or you’ve just wrapped a long cosplay session. A light coat of oil is a good habit to deter rust and preserve the blade’s appearance. Carbon steel hates prolonged moisture exposure without protection, so store it in a dry, cool place. The wooden scabbard should stay dry to prevent warping or cracking over time. If you notice looseness in fittings, a small amount of wood glue or careful tightening of screws can restore a snug fit, but avoid overtightening, which could damage the scabbard finish.

For cosplay maintenance, a quick polish before storage helps keep the blade photo ready. If you want a bit more gleam for dramatic lighting, a light oiling can create a subtle glow that reads well in studio photography. These little touches make a big difference when your goal is cinematic impact rather than battlefield durability.

## Practical buying tips

- Check the actual measurements listed by the seller. The 40-13-16 range indicates batch variation, so verify the exact overall length and blade length if you are constrained by space or display needs.
- Inspect the scabbard for fit with your blade. A loose scabbard can lead to rattling or cosmetic damage during photos and exposure to handling.
- Consider your storage environment. A dry, cool place is ideal for carbon steel and a wooden scabbard. Avoid damp basements and direct sunlight for long periods as both can affect wood and finish over time.
- If you plan to use it for cosplay, think about accessories such as a display stand or wall mount. A well chosen stand can elevate your presentation and prevent accidental tipping.
- Be mindful of safety with younger cosplayers. Even as a prop, a double edge look deserves respectful handling and supervision during photoshoots.

## A note on safety and responsibility
