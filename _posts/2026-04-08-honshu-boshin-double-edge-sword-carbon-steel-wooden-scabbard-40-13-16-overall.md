## Maintenance and care

Maintenance is straightforward and sensible. Wipe the blade with a soft cloth after handling, especially if you have oily hands or if you have just finished a photo shoot in a humid environment. Apply a light coat of oil to prevent rust and to maintain the blade’s appearance. Do not leave the blade exposed to moisture for extended periods; carbon steel does not enjoy prolonged exposure to damp air without some protection. The scabbard should be kept dry and free from moisture to avoid issues with warping or cracking over time. If you notice any looseness in the fittings, a small amount of wood glue or a light tightening of screws can restore a snug fit, but avoid overtightening, which can damage the scabbard and ruin the aesthetics you paid for.

For cosplay maintenance, a quick polish and a wipe down before storage will keep the blade looking sharp in photos. If you want to push the photo quality a bit further, you can experiment with light oiling to create a subtle gleam that reads well under studio lighting. This is especially effective when you want to capture a dramatic sword glow that sells the character you are portraying.

## Practical buying tips

- Check the actual measurements listed by the seller. The 40-13-16 range suggests variation between batches, so confirm the exact overall length and blade length if you have strict display space or cosplay constraints.
- Inspect the scabbard for fit with your blade. A loose scabbard can lead to rattling or accidental wiggling that ruins photos and increases the chance of minor cosmetic damage.
- Consider your storage environment. A dry, cool place is ideal for a carbon steel blade and wooden scabbard. Avoid damp basements and direct sunlight for long periods as both can affect wood and finish over time.
- If you plan to use it in cosplay, think about accessories such as a display stand or wall mount. A properly chosen stand can elevate your presentation and prevent accidental tipping.
