## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Pearl Pearl-ier

### Cleaning basics
The rug is marketed as machine-washable, which is the dream of many busy households. In practice, you’ll want to check your care label, but the general approach is cold-water wash on a gentle cycle, light detergent, and air-dry. A tumble-dryer option is typically discouraged or limited to low heat with careful monitoring because microfiber fibers can flatten or shed if abused by heat. The great thing is that spot cleaning works well for spills—blot—not rub—to prevent fiber damage. In Geeknite terms, this rug can outlive a few coffee spills without requiring a belt sander.

### Pet and kid-friendly care
If you’ve got a furry roommate or tiny humans with crayons, you’ll appreciate the ease of cleaning and the resilience of the fiber. Pet hair tends to release with a quick vacuum pass, and the light color hides a surprising amount of dust motes before you actually need to launder. For small skinprints and pen marks, a damp cloth and mild cleaner usually do the trick. The risk with light colors is visible staining, but the Pearl’s shade and fiber depth do a good job disguising the occasional misstep.

### Long-term maintenance schedule
We recommend a quarterly rotation to avoid wear patterns—just like rotating your tires but with more coziness and fewer road hazards. A light vacuum on low suction will keep the pile fluffy, and a gentle shake-out will restore loft when you’ve had guests that brought along a sudden wave of “DIY project” vibes. If you’re a meticulous cleaner, you can schedule professional cleaning every 12–18 months to refresh the fibers without dissolving the sheen.
