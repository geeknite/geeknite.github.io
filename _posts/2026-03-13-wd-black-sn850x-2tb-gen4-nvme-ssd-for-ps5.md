## Heatsink, PS5 compatibility, and the installation mindset
PS5 installation guidance insists on proper heat dissipation. The SN850X 2TB comes in variants with and without a heatsink. If you’re riding the stock PS5 cooling system, a drive that has a heatsink pre-installed is your best buddy. If you end up with the non-heatsink version, you’ll want to pick up a compatible heatsink that fits within Sony’s M.2 slot space constraints. The PS5’s internal slot has a finite amount of room; misalignment means you’ll fight with screws and potentially misaligned airflow. Take your time here; the last thing you want is a rattling memory module echoing through your living room when you’re in blackout mode while trying to finish a boss run.

One practical tip: set aside a few extra minutes to update the PS5’s firmware after you install the drive. ## Setup, installation, and first boot in the living room arena
Installation workflow typically looks like this:

- Power down the PS5 completely and unplug it.
- Open the side panel to reveal the expansion slot.
- If you’re using a heatsinked SN850X variant, ensure the heatsink sits flush and the thermal pad doesn’t obstruct the slot.
- Slide the drive into the M.2 slot at the correct angle (the notch alignment matters; you’ll see a small guide in the slot).
- Secure the screw to hold the drive in place. Do not over-tighten; you want a snug fit, not a winch scene.
- Reattach the cover, plug in the PS5, and power up.
- On first boot, you’ll be prompted to format the drive as extended storage. Proceed with formatting, and your PS5 will recognize the space as a new library for games, updates, and that one indie title you almost forgot you bought during a Steam sale.

If you’ve never installed an M.2 SSD in a console, you’ll be pleased to learn that Sony’s system software handles the heavy lifting. The real game here is ensuring the drive is properly seated and cooled. A badly seated drive can trigger read/write errors or, worse, make you do that awkward “is it plugged in?” dance while the room stares.
