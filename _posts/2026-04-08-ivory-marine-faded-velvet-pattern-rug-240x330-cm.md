### Cleaning and Care Basics
- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to prevent the pile from flattening too aggressively. If you’re the type who uses a beater bar, switch to a delicate mode or a brush attachment.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing can embed the stain deeper into velvet fibers.
- For liquid spills, start from the outside and work toward the center to minimize spreading.
- Consider a rug pad to prevent slipping and to add a layer of comfort underfoot. A pad also reduces wear on the velvet by letting it glide rather than drag across hard floors.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and to keep the faded pattern looking balanced instead of patchy.
- Seasonal deep cleaning may be needed for heavy dirt or odors. A professional cleaning every 12–18 months is a nice insurance policy if you’re worried about persistence of stains.

## Care and Maintenance: A Quick-Start Guide
To keep the Ivory Marine rug looking fresh, you’ll want a routine that suits velvet without turning your living room into a science experiment. Velvet can show wear if you neglect it, but with a few simple steps, you’ll maximize its life and beauty:

- Vacuum weekly with a soft-bristle attachment on a low setting.
- Treat spills immediately. Blot, don’t rub. Rubbing can embed particles and create a more stubborn stain.
- Consider rotating the rug every 3–6 months to ensure even color exposure and wear.
- Use a rug pad to reduce curling and sliding, and to protect the underlying flooring.
- For deeper cleaning, consult a professional rug cleaner who has experience with velvet fibers. DIY steam cleaning can be risky for velvet; moisture can change the pile’s feel and the color’s saturation.
- Protect edges. Velvet is luxurious, but frayed edges are not. Use corners or edge protectors if you have heavy traffic or if you drag furniture across the rug.
