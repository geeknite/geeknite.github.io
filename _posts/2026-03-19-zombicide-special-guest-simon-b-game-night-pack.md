## Overview of the Special Guest: Simon B. Pack

If you’ve played Zombicide at any volume, you know that the heart of the game is not the number of walkers you bamboozle with a machete; it’s the vibe—how ridiculous you feel while rolling dice, how you brag about your cleanest kitchen floor, and how you somehow manage to misplace a cardboard token in the same location every single time. The Special Guest — Simon B. Pack is meant to tilt the vibe from “labyrinthine city of the undead” to “you accidentally found a celebrity at a bar that also happens to be a safehouse.”

Inside you’ll find a handful of components designed to inject personality into your run through the city of the shambling. The pack includes:

- A Simon B. figure (or figures) with a unique ability and personality that influences encounters during scenarios.

- New survivor and zombie cards featuring guest quotes, signature gear, and “Simon B.” flavor text.

## Simon B.: The Guest Star and How He Changes the Stage

Simon B. is not a random NPC that shows up to drop a one-liner and vanish. No, he’s a full-blown cameo that impacts how you approach the game. The designers clearly wanted a spectacle, not a gimmick, and in that regard they delivered a guest who brings thematic spice without overshadowing core mechanics.

Here’s what Simon B. changes on the tabletop:

- A signature ability that triggers under certain conditions, encouraging aggressive, risk-taking play from your survivors. If you’re the cautious type, you’ll feel the seat slightly burn as you consider a celebration risk.
- Signature gear that seems tailor-made for dramatic turnarounds. You’ll be tempted to press your luck, and your dice will respond with a chorus of groans—never trust a d6 that aims to keep a straight face.
- Thematic dialogue cues: you’ll have an opportunity to shout clever lines inspired by Simon B.’s persona, which adds to the table culture in a way that sometimes matters more than the number of extra zombies you wipe out.

The result is a more dynamic game-night rhythm. You’ll experience moments where fate briefly smiles upon your team, only to laugh as a zombie pile drives its way through your carefully laid plan. It’s a reminder that zombie apocalypse is as much about performance as it is about probability.

## Setup: Getting Ready for the Spotlight

Setting up the Simon B. pack is a touch more complex than a standard Zombicide setup, but not by much. The user manual clearly indicates where the new tiles go, where the new survivor tokens sit, and how to integrate the signature gear into your survivors. The setup flow is designed to take roughly 5–15 minutes depending on your group size and how many layers of snacks you’ve decided to place on the table. The more snacks, the more the table becomes a hazard zone for game pieces, so beware the culinary terrain.

A nice touch is how the pack respects the base game’s flow. You don’t need to dismantle the core rules to enjoy the expansion. It slides into the existing SPS (Survivor Power System) without causing a coup d’état in your play group. If you’re a person who loves “just a little more complexity,” the Simon B. pack gives you a handful of options that can be toggled on or off as your group’s tolerance for chaos evolves.

For those who enjoy the social dimension of gaming, you’ll appreciate how Simon B. adds prompts and shout-outs you can use in between turns. It’s not a replacement for banter; it’s a spark plug for banter, which is exactly what you want when the hero gets overwhelmed by a horde of undead and you scream with glee because someone else is taking a poorly timed risk.

## Mechanics and Balance: Does the Spotlight Outshine the Core Game?

Zombicide thrives on heavy automation, where the math of dice and the order of activation drives the tension. The Simon B. pack adds a handful of new cards and tokens that layer on top of these mechanics. The risk is that expansions can overcomplicate the core game or tilt balance in a way that makes base-game strategies obsolete. In the case of the Special Guest pack, I found the balance to be thoughtful, not oppressive.

- The Simon B. ability is powerful but not game-breaking; it nudges players to consider new tactics without invalidating survival strategies that you already rely on. If anything, it rewards initiative and risk-taking in a manner that aligns with the “showtime” theme.
- The new gear cards provide equipment with flashy names and flavorful text. The flavor text is where the pack shines; it makes you want to roleplay, which can slow down a party not used to theatre in a tabletop fight, but in a good way.
- The scenario design encourages player interaction. You’ll find tasks that require you to plan together, coordinate style, and maybe groan together when a misstep leads to a cascade of zombie greed.

If you’re worried about complexity creep, fear not. The pack scales well with the base game, and you can decide whether to enable a “hard mode” by turning on additional encounter decks. The design allows you to calibrate the fun-to-chaos ratio to fit your group’s mood—whether you’re running a late-night marathon or a casual afternoon with friends who just discovered they love survival horror games more than they hate visa bills.

## Scenarios: A Pair of Thematic, Replayable Adventures

The pack ships with a couple of ready-to-play scenarios that lean into Simon B.’s persona. They’re designed to be modular and easy to slot into an existing campaign or run as a standalone game night with minimal prep.

### Scenario 1: Simon’s Showdown
In this scenario you’ll find that the host’s badge is more than a prop; it becomes a game mechanic. Simon B. triggers additional waves when the survivors cross certain thresholds, forcing players to decide whether to push toward a difficult objective or retreat to stage a more controlled, intimate last stand. The tension ramps up quickly, and the group dynamic shifts as some players decide to throw their dice into the blaze while others attempt a surgical extraction of the objectives.

The map design supports mid-range skirmishes, with a few chokepoints that reward good positioning and careful use of terrain. If you’re the type who enjoys big, cinematic set pieces in your zombie games, this scenario delivers that vibe without requiring you to memorize a dozen new rules.

### Scenario 2: Guest-Star Gauntlet
The second scenario is more of a gauntlet run, designed to test both individual skill and team coordination. Simon B. acts as a kind of “event” that can trigger a crescendo of complications: special zombie types entering the board, new modifiers on survivor actions, and a finale where you must secure a final objective before the last wave of walkers collapses your party. It’s a good test of planning and nerve—two things you’ll have in spades if you’ve played through several base campaigns and want to test your luck against higher-stakes choices.

Both scenarios reward memory and camaraderie: you’ll want to remember which player delivered the most dramatic line, which survivor muscles through with a heroic, risky move, and which snack kept everyone’s morale from dwindling into a muttered, sleep-deprived doomscrolling session.

## Value, Replayability, and Longevity

A big question with any expansion is whether it adds enough value to justify the cost. The Simon B. pack offers a handful of new content, with enough variety to feel like a fresh experience rather than a repeat of the base game with a new boss and some flair. The replay value comes from the two scenarios, the different survivor nods, and the dynamic of playing with a guest-like figure whose presence alters the game’s tempo in memorable ways.

If your usual game nights run a campaign or semi-campaign with a rotating cast, this pack slots in nicely and can serve as a “special event” night that you rotate in every few months. It gives you a reason to gather the same friends with a narrative hook—almost like a miniseries within your weekly sessions. In practical terms, you’ll see a few nights get more memorable just because you pulled out this expansion and embraced the guest-appearance theatrics.

Some players might wish for more guest content in future expansions as a way to keep the novelty fresh. That’s not a flaw in this pack, but a reminder that with Zombicide, you’ll probably want to add more if you live for the heightened drama of “celebrity survivors” and want every session to feel like a theatrical performance.

## Accessibility and Learning Curve

For the newbies who are just stepping into Zombicide, the Special Guest pack is not a hard barrier to entry. It layers on top of existing rules and gives you a selection of new cards and tokens that you can ignore if you want to. It’s a gentle introduction to how expansions can shift your playing style without forcing a full rules audit. The components are clearly labeled, and the scenario sheets include brief summary rules to expedite setup and reference those crucial moments during play.

Some players will want to read the entire rulebook before diving in. That’s okay, and this pack can comfortably accommodate curious minds who want to deeply understand the interactions introduced by Simon B. But you don’t need to memorize everything to start having fun. The pack invites you to lean into theater and pacing, which for many players is the best entry point into a zombie apocalypse.
