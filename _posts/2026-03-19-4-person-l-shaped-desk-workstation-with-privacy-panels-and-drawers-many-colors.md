Ergonomics and comfort: making space for human bodies

Good ergonomics isn’t just about a chair back or a monitor height — it’s about the whole ecosystem. The workstation supports adjustable monitor arms, allowing each user to place their display at eye level without the dreaded “neck tilt.” Keyboard trays or a flat keyboard surface are both supported, and there’s enough leg room for comfortable posture shifts during longer sessions.

Pro tip: if you’re pairing these with sit-stand desks, make sure the end users’ chairs can glide along the panel line without scraping the privacy panels. A little planning goes a long way toward preventing panel dings and mood-damping noise when someone tries to adjust their chair and accidentally nudges the panel into someone else’s space.

Assembly and installation: the long, satisfying stretch

Let’s be honest: this is not a “dump it in a box and hope for the best” product. It’s a modular, multi-user system that benefits from planning. Expect a chunk of time for aligning rails, anchoring panels, and setting the drawers with care. The instructions walk you through a roomful of components with labeled hardware, polybags of screws, and tiny Allen wrenches that magically multiply when you aren’t looking.

A practical approach to assembly:
- Clear the space and lay out the panels, rails, and pedestals in the room where it will live.
- Start with the main frame, secure the central spine, and then attach the corner panels to ensure proper alignment.
- Install drawers on the pedestals and verify smooth operation before sliding them into place.
- Attach privacy panels and adjust height to suit each worker.
- Run cables and tidy, then perform a final check for wobble and alignment.
