## Overview
### What is City of Mist Storyteller Screen
In City of Mist the GM is a conductor of mood; the Storyteller Screen is a physical tool that tries to keep the mood while giving you at a glance access to essential mechanics and quick reference tables. The three panel screen is a fold out piece of cardboard designed to sit on the table and block a glimpse of the secrets that the players are about to reveal while still providing the GM with access to the most commonly used rules.

## Build and Materials
The screen is typically thick cardboard, foldable into three panels, with a noir tinted art direction. The cover art features a cityscape at night blurred by rain; the inside panels present arranged references. The build quality is sturdy enough for regular game nights and after game nights; you can reliably knock it against a chair without a loud crack. The gradient art is not laminated to death; it has a matte finish that resists fingerprints.

## Layout and Quick Reference Content
This is the heart of the product. The City of Mist rules revolve around two forces Mythos and Logos, as well as the idea of power tags that can be invoked to influence outcomes. The Storyteller Screen condenses the essential charts into a quick reference that you can read without tilting your head to the neon sign above the bar.

The reference panels typically include:
- Quick checks for typical actions like Investigate, Create, Attack, Defend.
- A compact listing of the generic conflicts and how to resolve them.
- A small glossary of terms used in the game so you do not have to flip pages every time.
- A cheat sheet for NPCs and major antagonists, including their power tags and how they can be used in scenes.

It is not a substitute for the core rulebook, but it is a useful card to accelerate the pace of scenes. City of Mist can be heavy on storytelling and mood, and the screen helps you maintain control of the pace without losing the vibe.

## Practical Use in Sessions
If you frequently run City of Mist the screen is a helpful addition. It keeps the essential actions visible and reduces the number of times you reach for a book. It allows you to maintain the noir vibe by always having the relevant prompts at hand. If you run multiple campaigns in the same evening the screen helps you adapt quickly; you can switch scenes with fewer interruptions.

A few practical tips:
- Place the screen at the center so you can glance at it without leaning too far over the table.
- Pair the screen with quick notes about current NPCs and faction relationships to maximize the gain from a single page.
- Use the screen to enforce pacing by prompting a dramatic question at the end of each scene.

## Content Coverage and Gaps
The screen covers the core GM actions and essential references, but it cannot replace the nuance of the City of Mist core manual. It may not include every possible action or outcome for those long sessions with players who love to push every boundary. It is also worth noting that this screen is more style than content, which is exactly the vibe the game wants to achieve.

### Comparing with Other Screens
When compared to screens for other RPG systems, City of Mist Storyteller Screen emphasizes mood and narrative prompts over exhaustive tables. Other screens might deliver more numeric convenience for combat sequences, but City of Mist is about storytelling tempo and atmosphere. The screen leans into that identity by presenting evocative prompts alongside the basic rules.

## How to Set Up and Use
Set up the screen on the table where it is visible to you but not easily peeked at by the players. The idea is to keep the players oriented in the world while giving you the controls you need in a glance. A few minutes of arrangement before play can save you 10 minutes of rummaging during a tense moment. The result is faster pacing and more time for the players to react to the noir beat of the scene.
