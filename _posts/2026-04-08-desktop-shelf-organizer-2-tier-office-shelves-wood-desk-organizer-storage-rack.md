## Maintenance, Durability, and Upgrades

### Cleaning and Care
Clean with a soft, dry cloth to maintain the veneer’s luster. If you spill coffee or tea, wipe promptly and dry. A damp cloth followed by a thorough dry is enough to prevent staining. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could strip the veneer finish. If your desk organizer begins to show signs of wear, a light refinishing with a wood polish can help restore its glow—though in a busy home office, you’re usually too busy to refinish the furniture anyway, so let’s be honest about your priorities.

### Durability Over Time
Two-tier wood desk organizers like this typically last several years with moderate use. The more you load the shelves with heavy objects, the more you’ll want to check for loose screws or slight warping. If you frequently move your desk to redecorate or to chase the sun across your apartment, you may want to disassemble and re-tighten the hardware every few months.

### Potential Upgrades
If you’re into DIY vibes, you can upgrade the look with decorative risers, rubberized feet for floor protection, or add-on brackets to expand storage. You could also pair it with a small cable tray behind to hide the wire spaghetti—because cables are the real nemesis of any tidy desk setup. For those with a green thumb, a tiny herb planter on the upper shelf could turn your workspace into an “office garden”—just don’t overwater or attempt photosynthesis experiments in the middle of a deadline.
