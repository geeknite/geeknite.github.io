## Gameplay Mechanics

The core loop is straightforward enough to teach in a few minutes, but the strategic depth reveals itself after a handful of rounds.

### Card deck and objective

A standard 52-card deck powers the action, with suits, values, and sometimes jokers depending on the variant. The objective is to form valid sets and runs and to declare before the clock ticks down. The sense of progress comes from seeing your hand morph as you plan ahead—grouping three of a kind, sequencing runs across suits, and slotting in deadwood cards that might later turn into perfect melds.

### Turn structure and discarding

Turns follow a familiar rhythm: draw a card, decide whether to meld, then discard. The tension emerges in whether you should risk melding early to reduce your deadwood or hold back, hoping your next draw supplies the perfect piece. The discarding decision is a micro-drama: you weigh the risk of helping your opponent against the value of keeping a flexible hand. United Games adds optional hints and a few rule variants that can nudge you toward a more aggressive or more cautious path, depending on how you like to play.

### Scoring and variants

Scoring in rummy boils down to minimizing deadwood and successfully declaring. In this version, you will encounter standard scoring plus occasional event modifiers that tilt the odds during time-limited sessions. Early declarations can earn bonus points, while unused cards at game end carry penalties that encourage efficient planning. If you enjoy a little volatility, event modifiers can spice things up by rewarding bold plays or punishing overcautious ones.
