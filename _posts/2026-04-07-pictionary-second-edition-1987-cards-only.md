## The “Cards-Only” Experience: How to Play Without the Board
### Setup and Rules
Playing with cards-only means reducing the ritual to its most essential elements: a deck of prompts, a timer, and, ideally, a scorekeeper. The rules are simple: someone picks a card and has a shortened time window to get their team to guess what they’re drawing. The rest of the players provide guesses; if the team gets it right within the timer, they score a point. If not, the turn passes. That might sound straightforward, but the real trick is managing the tempo and keeping the drawing legible under pressure — which is precisely why the bold, clean lines on the 1987 cards work so well.

A crucial tip for cards-only players: designate a common drawing surface that’s large enough for fast sketching. A whiteboard or a large notepad works better than a small card drawn in a spiral-bound notebook. The goal isn’t perfect renditions; it’s clear silhouettes and quickly read shapes. You’ll end up with a room full of comedians and artists alike, each trying to interpret the clues through a fog of laughter.
