## Assembly and maintenance: a quick how-to and a few tips
Most 5-piece sets arrive as modular components that slot together with screws, end caps, and the occasional Allen wrench. The assembly, while not riveting, isn’t a full-blown engineering project either. Plan for roughly 60–90 minutes for a single person; time yourself, and you’ll discover you can still make a decent espresso while you work.

Maintenance is a two-part ritual: routine cleaning and protective storage. Routine cleaning means wiping the wicker with a damp cloth, removing cushion covers for washing when possible, and keeping the cushions dry when there’s no sun. The wicker should be kept away from harsh chemicals—soaps and light sprays on a gentle setting are fine. For long-term outdoor living, consider seasonal storage or a sturdy furniture cover. The cushions, if removable and machine-washable, should follow label instructions; if not, spot-cleaning with mild detergent and water works just fine. A little care goes a long way toward maintaining color and structure.

## Weather resistance: rain, sun, and the occasional pollen sneeze
Outdoor furniture loves rain—when it’s designed to handle it. The synthetic wicker in this set resists water absorption and shrinkage, so it won’t crack or warp with a misting shower. Cushions are a different story; water-repellent finishes help, but heavy rain can saturate cushions if covers aren’t water-resistant or if left out too long. A hooded storage solution or a breathable furniture cover helps a lot. UV exposure is real: even the sturdiest fabrics fade slowly with months of sun. If fading concerns you, rotate cushions or bring them inside during peak sun hours.
