## What is the Hero Box 1? An Overview of Contents and Intent

The Zombicide Black Plague Hero Box 1 (GUF005, 2015) is one of CMON’s early, glossy expansions designed to extend the base game with new survivors, new weapons, and fresh strategic options. In practice, these Hero Boxes were a way to keep the core experience feeling new without requiring a full-blown new campaign. They offered players a handful of unique survivors—each with their own special abilities—alongside a smattering of new equipment and status cards that could alter how you approach a wave of undead, or, frankly, the number of times you roll the dice to either save your friends or watch them become undead-flavored memory foam for the next wave.

If you’re a long-time reader of Geeknite, you know we love what these boxes do: they expand the “what you can be” rather than simply “what you can do.” Hero boxes are about flavor and personal stories: “I am Sir Hammerspark, the Knight who learned to enjoy loot and still hates fog.” That kind of narrative potential is why we keep telling you that the true value of a small add-on isn’t the extra zombies—it’s the extra story you’ll tell during and after the last card is discarded.

For those who care about the timetable, this box was released during a period when Zombicide was exploding in popularity and CMON was riding the wave with accessible, well-produced miniatures, bright cards, and a packaging aesthetic that screamed “you want me on your shelf.” Factory sealed and 100% fresh from 2015, this box sits proudly with its original plastic wrap, its characterful sculpts still looking sharp enough to make your taste buds paranoid about the quality of your own painting job.

Here’s the clean list of what you typically get in a Hero Box 1 of this era (paraphrased for readability):

For lovers of production value, there’s a small but notable attention to the art direction. ## Integration into Your Campaign: How Box 1 Feels on the Table

When you’re integrating Hero Box 1 into your ongoing Black Plague campaign, a few practical tips help you maximize the experience:

- Introduce heroes gradually: don’t throw all six new survivors into the party at once. Let each player claim one hero, and run a short one-shot session to help them grok the new abilities. It’s a smarter onboarding approach that also makes room for storytelling moments.
- Align equipment with your party’s playstyle: if your crew likes heavy martial combat, prioritize weapons that boost melee output. If your group enjoys skirmish tactics and objective control, select items that enhance movement or range control. You’ll find the synergy is less about brute force and more about clever positioning.
- Use the new cards as “cooldown” rewards: if your party achieves a tough objective, reward them with a special equipment card—this adds a sense of progress and keeps players motivated between waves.
- Consider terrain synergy: the Black Plague settings shine when terrain interacts with your heroes’ abilities. If you’re using a dungeon-like board with chokepoints, the new hero cards can influence which chokepoints you protect and which you abandon.
