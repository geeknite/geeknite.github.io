## Practical tips for owners
### How to store your deck
Keep your Rider Back decks in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The sun can fade the color on the card backs over time, especially if you are leaving them in a sunlit room while you are streaming a tutorial about how to shuffle cards like a pro. A simple deck box or a felt-lined case is perfect. Some players even keep a dedicated deck for cardistry and another for games, which sounds intense but makes sense when you are chasing perfect stacks during a long game night.

### Cleaning and maintenance
If your deck starts to show fingerprints or smudges, wipe the back gently with a microfiber cloth. Do not use water or cleaning solvents. Card stock is not a sponge. If you are really into keeping the deck pristine, wrap the card box in a sleeve that protects the tuck and the back design from occasional coffee spillages during late-night gaming sessions.

### When to replace a deck
A deck should last for dozens of games if handled with care. Replace it when the stock begins to show obvious wear, the corners start to peel, or you notice frequent misreads in your group’s gameplay due to sticky fingers. Another signal is the color looking dull under your favorite lamp; a fresh deck can rekindle the fun.
