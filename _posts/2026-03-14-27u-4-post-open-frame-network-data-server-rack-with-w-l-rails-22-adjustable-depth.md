## Tips for getting the most out of this rack

- Plan the layout before you mount. Sketch where devices will go and how cables will travel. It saves time and nerves.
- Use vertical cable managers if you can. They dramatically improve patch-panel access and cleanliness.
- Level the rack early. It saves you from the annoying wobble while connecting the last patch cables.
- Label as you go. A little labeling goes a long way in a space where every cable matters.
- Keep a spare set of feet or anti-slip pads for the bottom to prevent skidding if you place the rack on a slick surface.

## Quick do nots

- Do not overload the rack with heavy devices on one side. Even with four posts, weight distribution matters.
- Do not ignore ventilation. If you seal the open frame, it ceases to be open frame and you lose the airflow advantage.
- Do not forget to secure all rails properly before loading devices. It saves you from surprising UPS adventures.
