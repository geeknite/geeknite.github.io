## How UNO Flip Works: The Core Mechanics (A Quick Refresher)
If you’re new to UNO Flip, here’s the quick primer before we dive into the Boruto twist: UNO Flip uses two sides of the deck—the Light Side, which behaves like classic UNO (color-match, number-match, and action cards), and the Dark Side, which introduces more punishing actions after a Flip card is played. When a Flip card appears, players flip their entire hands to the opposite side. Play continues with the new, harsher Dark Side cards in effect until the round ends.

Key actions on the Light Side include standard color-matching, Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four. On the Dark Side, expect more chaotic moves like Draw Five, Skip Everyone, and a few Wild Flip cards that flip both decks simultaneously. In this Boruto edition, the mechanic remains intact; the theme merely provides flavor explosions around the actions rather than complicating the physics of the game.

The BorutoNaruto theme adds a layer of narrative in the card art and a few flavor rules that reference signature moves, shinobi terms, and a sprinkle of ramen talk. It’s enough to spark a few laughs without devolving into a heavy anime trivia match, which is precisely what most party players want on a relaxed game night.
