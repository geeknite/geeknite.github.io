## Setup and rules overview

Setting up Angry Neighbors is not radically different from base Zombicide. You will still be sorting survivors by color, shuffling new zombie spawns, and laying out the scenario deck. The neighbor mechanic introduces a social dynamics layer that can influence whose turn feels most tense. The general rhythm is familiar to veterans but with enough new twists that it remains fresh.

Key things to watch during setup:

- Identify the new neighbor factions and their activation rules. They tend to operate with their own agenda, so keep track of threat levels carefully.
- Integrate new mission cards with existing boards. Some missions may lean into the neighbor tension, which means you will be balancing escape routes with power outages and neighbor interactions.
- Keep the new objective tokens close at hand. Objectives in Angry Neighbors tend to be a little more narrative and a little less straightforward than your standard zombie kill fest.

The rule changes are not a radical overhaul. If you are comfortable with Season 1 or Season 2, you should feel at home. The neighbor layer adds nuance, not a new language. The game remains accessible, with the learning curve gently climbing as you incorporate the social mechanics into your tactical planning.

## Gameplay deep dive: the turn structure and neighbor chaos

Angry Neighbors keeps the core turn structure simple enough for casual evenings and deep enough for long sessions. The typical flow remains familiar:

- Players plan and perform two actions per survivor, usually moving, searching, fighting, or using an item.
- Enemies (zombies or other threats) turn in a manner dictated by the scenario and the neighbor cards.
- Cards get drawn, threats get assigned, and the board state evolves as the lockstep of survival becomes a chaotic dance.

What makes Angry Neighbors interesting is the neighbor activation. They don’t always follow the same bold rules as the zombies. Some neighbors have access to special actions that can disrupt your plans in clever ways. They might open doors for zombies, create ambush angles, or trigger chain reactions that turn your tight corridor into a trap. It is this unpredictability that injects a sense of urban hazard into the suburban mythos.

From a design perspective, the neighbor system is well-balanced. It nudges players toward cooperative coordination without breaking the sense of inevitability in a zombie apocalypse. The added layer rewards careful planning and risk assessment. Do you clear the nearest threat, or do you push toward the objective while balancing the neighbor tension? The answers often come down to your group dynamic and your tolerance for chaos.

Thermometer test: how much chaos can a group tolerate before it stops being fun? Angry Neighbors leans toward the manageable end of the spectrum, provided your table values fun and narrative as much as victory conditions. If your playgroup loves pure optimization with minimal randomness, you might find the neighbor system a bit spicy. If you crave stories about suburban legends turning into field reports from the front lines, you will likely adore it.

## Tips to get the most out of Angry Neighbors

- Start with a gentle mission to acclimate to neighbor mechanics. A gradual ramp helps everyone, including new players, to absorb the additional rules without feeling overwhelmed.
