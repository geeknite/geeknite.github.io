## How to Play: go mini, go fast, go chaotic
Phase 10 GO! Mini distills the core loop of Phase 10 into a compact, portable experience. The basic rhythm remains familiar: you draw, you discard, you try to complete a phase, and you move on. The mini edition trims down the deck and simplifies the setup so you can jump straight into the chaos in a few seconds.

Setup is quick. Each player receives a small starting hand, and the phases you’ll chase are laid out clearly in the rule sheet. Because the deck is smaller, rounds tend to move quicker. If you’re a fan of long, dramatic lead-ins and 30-minute planning sessions, you might need to recalibrate expectations for a game that’s designed to fit into a single coffee break.

Turn flow is straightforward. On your turn, you draw a card from the deck or the discard pile, then discard a single card. The goal is to complete the currently active phase, which you can lay down in front of you once you’ve collected the required cards. Completing a phase advances you to the next, tougher one or returns you back to the start if you’re playing a more forgiving variant. The mini rules preserve the essence of “phase first, last man standing wins,” with emphasis on the readable wins and the nerve-wracking near-misses.

The pace is brisk in the best possible way. There’s something deeply satisfying about watching a hand cluster into two sets of three or a run of four while the rest of the table sweats over a near miss. Because the deck is smaller, you’ll see more cycles per game, which translates to momentum swings that are perfect for travel nights when the airport lounge needs a little more personality.

## Strategy notes: how to maximize a tiny deck
- Play the long game, but with a fast clock: mini decks mean you’ll cycle through phases more rapidly. Be prepared to pivot if the cards don’t cooperate on the current phase.
- Prioritize flexible cards: cards that can help with multiple phase types (short runs and varied sets) give you options when the wind shifts. When in doubt, hold onto a middle card that could bridge two possible runs.
- Watch the discard pile like a hawk: if you see a lot of the same color or a particular sequence being discarded, you can anticipate what others are chasing and adjust your plan accordingly.
- Don’t be shy about breaking plans: the compact format rewards improvisation. If you’re stuck on a dead-end run, switch to a different phase that matches the cards you’re likely to draw. The goal is continual progress, not perfection in a single phase.
