### What is Cerebria: The Inside World?

Inside the brain lies a surreal plane where emotions act as agents with agency and budgets. Players lead Spirits who command emotional domains and guide the body through tracks, regions, and rituals. The aim is to accumulate points by controlling regions, performing rituals, and maintaining a balance of influence across districts.

From afar, Cerebria might look like a Euro with a creative twist. Up close, it is a mood machine with bold color, evocative art, and a suite of tokens that feel like they were carved by a chorus of serious artists in a quiet studio. The Inside World is weird, wonderful, and just chaotic enough to feel alive when your strategy gets thwarted by a rogue event.

### Core Mechanics and Gameplay Loop

Cerebria blends area control, resource management, and asymmetric power into a playable symphony. The core loop has players building momentum with mood tokens, claiming regions, and triggering rituals that bend the rules in dramatic ways.

- Turn structure: Each round features a planning phase where players choose actions via cards and mood moves, followed by a resolution phase where actions resolve and influence tallies update. It rewards forward thinking, but rewards midgame improvisation as well.
- Mood tracks and energy economy: Mood is the heartbeat. Tracking mood on multiple tracks generates energy. Energy powers more actions, rituals, or defenses. The trick is that mood can be disrupted by events, so you must decide when to push a reliable engine and when to disrupt others.
- Regions and influence: Regions grant points and opportunities for rituals. Controlling a region is not the end all; it is a strategic foothold that shapes late game movement and scoring. The dynamic invites you to weigh the value of risk against the comfort of defense as the board shifts.
- Special abilities and asymmetry: Each Spirit faction has unique powers that tilt the table during certain phases. Asymmetry rewards diverse strategies and increases replayability, but it can overwhelm new players with the sheer number of options.

The result is a game that rewards planning and risk-taking in roughly equal measure. You become both author and editor of your own mood narrative, writing a script with moments of triumph and inevitable mood-aligned chaos.

### Turn Structure in Detail

To give you a sense of how a typical round unfolds, here is a skeleton you will memorize after a few sessions:

- Planning Phase: Choose a set of actions and mood moves, laying the groundwork for your next turns. The mood economy opens up opportunities you may or may not exploit in the same round.
- Activation Phase: Resolve your actions. Resolve conflicts in regions, run rituals, and generate energy for the next cycle. The tempo may swing between deliberate, careful plays and sudden disruptions as mood tiles flip and event cards surprise you.
- End of Round: Tally points, refresh resources, return cards to hand, and prepare for planning again. The end game pressure mounts as regions near cap values and rituals become more valuable.

This cadence creates drama without devolving into a dry Euro static. Cerebria respects your time yet lets momentum build and shift.

### Strategy and Player Interaction

If you enjoy long-term planning with a dash of political negotiation, Cerebria fits nicely. The interaction level sits between gentle negotiation and political theater.

- Symmetry and asymmetry: Two factions create a dynamic where each run feels unique. Use your faction niche to outmaneuver the other side, and watch how your opponent tries to counter your anti-advantage moves.
- Timing the rituals: Rituals are powerful but require precise timing. Trigger too early and you waste momentum; trigger too late and you surrender leading positions.
- Region contest dynamics: Not all regions are equal. The value of a region grows as the game nears its end. You can force misdirection by challenging the wrong zones, creating openings elsewhere.
- Hand management and tempo: Cards are your tempo. The right card at the right moment can turn fatigue into momentum, the one big swing that turns the board.

Cerebria remains approachable enough to teach in one sitting if your group indulges the mood theme and agrees to suspend disbelief about mood tokens purchasing regions or triggering rituals.
