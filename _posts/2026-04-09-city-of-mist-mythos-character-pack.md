## What is the Mythos Character Pack?
The Mythos Character Pack is all about expanding the mythos layer—the way the City of Mist game lets players fuse ordinary lives with heroic legends. The core idea remains: you are a detective who has access to masks or mythoi that give you powers, weaknesses, and an inner monologue suitable for a late-night monologue party. The Mythos Pack nudges this concept into new directions with several components:

- New Mythos Cards: fresh embodiments of gods, demigods, and legendary influences you can merge with a character. Think of it as adding more flavors to the neon noodle soup.
- Additional Character Sheets: more templates to guide your players into complex identities—because one vampire detective wasn’t enough, apparently.
- Expanded Arcs and Story Seeds: prompts that help your MC (mysterious Commissioner, not the car) push your players into morally ambiguous crossroads where the city itself is a character.
- Aesthetic and Flavor Components: art, flavor text, and table-ready prompts that make the decision between chasing a clue or chasing a legend feel like a choice between a cigarette and a comet.

If you loved tinkering with masks and mythos in the base game, you’ll feel right at home here. If you’re new to City of Mist and you bought this pack thinking it’s a starter kit for a park ranger cult, you might want to pause and read the rest of this review—and perhaps the core rulebook first.

## Mechanics Deep Dive: Do We Need More Masks?
Let’s talk a little shop about mechanics because that’s where the game eats or grows on you. City of Mist uses a dice system built around two things: the mundane you bring to the table and the mythic moves you trigger through masks and mythoi. The Mythos Pack’s new material slots neatly into this ecosystem without violently destabilizing it. In some cases, the pack offers alternative paths to the same results—good news when your table has three players who tend to collude in clever ways and one who wants to “do a bigger spell” every round.

One practical effect is that new masks expand the pool of options for edge-case scenarios: you’re not constantly forcing a square peg into a round hole when the story requires a god’s permission slip or a demigod-level bluff. Players get more choices, DMs get more opportunities for dramatic irony, and the city itself gains a few new sarcastic voices to tease the players with.

That said, there is a point to consider: more masks means more rules interactions to track. If your group tends toward high-speed action or you’re juggling a six-person table, you might feel the pack adds a layer of complexity. The cure is simple: lean into mood and pacing. Use fewer masks at a time, keep arcs modular, and let the mytho moments ride on the back of the scene rather than turning every turn into a math problem. The pack rewards players who enjoy roleplay and revel in the mythos vibe. It’s not the best pick for groups that primarily want a tight, tactical dungeon crawl under fluorescent lighting—but that’s not City of Mist’s lane anyway.

## How to Use the Mythos Pack: Practical Play Tips
If you’re new to City of Mist or this particular expansion, here are practical tips to integrate the Mythos Pack smoothly:

- Start with a single mythos mask per session to avoid overwhelming players. Rotate masks as needed to heighten tension.
- Use the story seeds as “openers” rather than rigid scripts. Your players will surprise you with their own interpretations, and that’s the point.
- Pair two players’ masks to create a duo arc. That dynamic can deliver dramatic interplay and a few very loud, very heartfelt hero moments.
- Embrace the city as a character. Let the environment enforce mood, not just backstory. A rainy night can reveal a deity’s favor or cynicism.
- Balance is your friend. The new content adds options, not a requirement to use every feature at once. Pick what resonates with your group’s vibe.

If you want a concrete session structure, try this quick flow:
1) Open with a weather motif and a clue that screams mythic significance.
2) Introduce a mythos encounter that tests the players’ masks in a morally gray scenario.
3) Let the city react to their choices. The neon sign outside the detective’s office flickers, offering a symbolic token that ties into a future arc.
4) Close with a choice: reveal the mythic truth, or preserve the mystery for a future session.

This approach keeps momentum high and the mood on-brand for City of Mist fans.

## Balance and Power: Are We Pushing Power Creep?
Power creep is a valid concern in any expansion, especially when the new content adds more masks and more mythoi. The Mythos Pack, however, stays aware of balance by framing new options as thematic enhancements with narrative emphasis rather than raw mechanical superiority. In practice, that means: yes, you can take on more dramatic angles, but your success remains a function of clever storytelling and party dynamics rather than just stacking more dice.

If your group uses a house-rule for tighter balance, start by sandboxing two or three new masks per session. If you find that a particular mythos is too strong, curb its use in upcoming sessions or create a counter-charm that gives other players a chance to shine. The best part about City of Mist is that group taste governs the table, not the expansion’s marketing copy.
