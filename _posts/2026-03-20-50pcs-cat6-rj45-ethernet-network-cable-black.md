### Jacket and Shielding
Most bulk Cat6 patch cables come as unshielded twisted pair (UTP). That’s fine for typical residential and small office environments where interference is minimal and the distance isn’t creeping toward long-run territory. If you happen to be in a factory, near heavy electrical gear, or you’re running cables through metal conduits with a lot of RF noise, you might consider shielded variants (FTP/STP) for extra protection. This kit leans toward the practical, cost-efficient side—great for everyday use and great for teaching your kids how networks work by letting them unplug and replug cables without fear of catastrophic EM interference.

### Connectors and Impedance
RJ45 connectors in Cat6 cables are designed to maintain impedance consistency across the run, which reduces reflections and helps support higher frequencies. In Cat6, you’re aiming at bandwidth up to 250 MHz, which is a nice upgrade over Cat5e’s 100 MHz. In practical terms, that translates to cleaner performance at higher speeds and less crosstalk when several devices are hammering the network at once. The gold-plated or tin-plated contacts (depending on the batch) add a touch of reliability, and the plug’s latching mechanism is usually dependable enough to survive a desk job’s daily hustle.

## Installation and Cable Management Tips
- Plan your topology: Sketch a quick map of where each device sits and which cable lengths you’ll use. It saves headaches when you implement a new device or relocate equipment.
- Label as you go: A simple label near the connector or a small tag about the device makes future upgrades a lot easier. It also helps you keep track of which port on a switch corresponds to which device.
- Use cable management tools: Velcro ties, cable trays, and labeled cable combs help keep your desk neat. The less your cables poke out, the less dust you attract in the long run.
- Avoid excessive bending: Patch cables have a bend radius; avoid kinking by bending them too tightly. The general rule is a gentle arc rather than a sharp bend.
- Watch routing: When routing cables behind a desk or around a corner, keep longer runs off the ground and away from power cables to minimize EMI (electromagnetic interference).
