Introduction

If you thought Exploding Kittens was chaotic enough, wait until you meet the Cat Tower, the tall whiskered centerpiece that now graces the table rather than just the margins of a joke on the internet. The Cat Tower is not a required component; it’s a bold statement piece that says, We are here to escalate the chaos and to remind you that cats are in charge during game night. In this review, we’ll examine the Cat Tower from every angle — design, gameplay integration, durability, value, and whether it belongs in your next session.

What is Exploding Kittens: Cat Tower?

The Cat Tower is a modular stacking centerpiece that sits on the table and interacts with the Exploding Kittens deck in ways that only a cat-themed card game could imagine. It is comprised of base plates, interlocking columns, and top caps. The goal is to build a vertical structure that remains stable during the highs and lows of card draws, defuses, and misplays, while also giving players a stage for their inner theater kid to shine.

Design and Components

As with many premium expansions, the Cat Tower aims for a mix of function and flair. The pieces snap together with a satisfying click that makes a small sound you can pretend is your cat purring in the other room. It’s light enough to glide across a table if someone bangs it, but sturdy enough to survive a night of laughter, boisterous card flips, and the occasional topple that ends up as an accidental treasure hunt for the tokens that may have fallen underneath.

Aesthetically, the Cat Tower borrows heavily from Exploding Kittens art. It uses a bold color scheme and stylized cat motifs that feel both familiar and delightfully offbeat. You’ll notice paw prints on the edges of the tiles and a general vibe that says, This was drawn by someone who loves cats, who also happens to enjoy causing a little mischief at the table.

Cat Tower in Gameplay

Setup and Integration

If you’re the kind of player who values efficient setup, the Cat Tower is a slow-burn starter. It takes moments to assemble, but those moments contribute to the atmosphere of your game night. The base plates go down, the columns click into place, and the top caps are fitted. The process intentionally slows things a touch, letting players bask in the anticipation of what the Cat Tower might do when the action shifts into high gear.

Once assembled, the Cat Tower sits at the edge of the play area, ready to be poked, prodded, knocked, or adored by the cat who chooses this night to make an appearance. It’s designed to be stable but not immovable, which means you get a constant reminder that chaos can be beautiful if you steer it correctly.

Interaction with Cards and Strategy

Sure, the tower can be a mere spectacle, but it’s not without function. In many variants of the Cat Tower, certain cards interact with the tower itself. The tower might rotate or reveal a hidden token when a particular card is drawn, affecting the defuse odds or the likelihood of an explosion in the next turn. The exact mechanical interactions depend on the edition you own, but the spirit remains consistent: the Cat Tower is a dynamic element that adds an element of “physical risk” to a game that is already built on risk management and bluffing.

Aesthetics and Thematic Vibe

The Cat Tower is more than a toy; it’s a storytelling catalyst. It invites players to narrate a little theater around their table. The cat stalking across the tile, the tiny paw prints catching the light, the occasional topple that reveals a token you didn’t know existed — all of these moments turn card-flipping into a live script.

Practicality and Use Cases

Solo Play vs Party Nights

The Cat Tower shines in group settings. It is a social piece that encourages storytelling, collaboration, and a little healthy chaos. It’s less practical for a solo game where you’re zeroing in on odds, probabilities, and defusal rates, but if you’re the kind of person who enjoys talking to yourself while playing, the Cat Tower can provide dramatic pauses and a built-in audience of characters who aren’t there but you pretend are.

Storage and Care

The Cat Tower is tall and a little delicate by design. If you’re short on shelf space, you may need to dedicate a portion of a closet to storing the modular pieces. The good news is that the parts are modular and can be disassembled relatively quickly. A soft cloth to wipe down the pieces after a few gaming nights should be enough to keep them looking sharp. Avoid placing the tower in direct sunlight for extended periods to prevent fading of the printed motifs.

Value and Pricing

This is where things get spicy and a lot more subjective. The Cat Tower is a premium accessory. If you’re chasing only the bare minimum for your Exploding Kittens sessions, this piece may feel extravagant. But if you think of your game nights as an event and you’re willing to invest in a centerpiece that doubles as a conversation starter and a photo backdrop, the Cat Tower offers tangible value beyond its functional use. It’s the difference between a card game and a performance piece, and in a culture that loves sharing memes and stories about failed strategies, the Cat Tower pays dividends in laughter and nostalgia.

House Rules and Variants

- The stack height determines both lines of sight and the potential risk of a topple. Tall configurations raise tension but demand steadier hands.
- Rotate or reposition rules that allow players to re-contextualize a top segment after a decisive moment might also be introduced.
- A brief ritual where players acknowledge the tower before each turn can be fun for some groups.

Comparisons with Other Accessories

Compared with dice towers, card holders, or neon sleeves, the Cat Tower offers a different kind of value: it’s more about theatre than utility. It’s not a necessary upgrade; it’s a lifestyle upgrade. If you’re a collector or a streamer who wants stable props for content creation, the Cat Tower is excellent; if you’re someone who travels light and plays in small spaces, you may prefer to skip the extra bulk.

Photography and Social Potential

A good photo of the Cat Tower can anchor your social posts. Stage a shot with good lighting and a playful caption, and you’ve got a piece of content that looks intentional rather than random. We’ve found that a mid-range smartphone with natural light produces the best results, with a backdrop that shows your game table in action rather than a pure studio shot. For social explorers, the Cat Tower is a gift that keeps on giving.

Gifting and Collectibility

If you know someone who collects Exploding Kittens or who adores quirky game pieces, the Cat Tower is a well-placed gift. It’s not a universal candy bar you hand out to everyone, but it’s the kind of present that inspires a “this is insane and I love it” reaction, which, let’s be honest, is the reaction you want when you’re giving someone a new piece for a game night.
