### How Skip-Bo Works: A Quick Refresher

Skip-Bo is a race to deplete your stock pile by building up to 12 in ascending order across multiple build piles. The wild cards act as flexible placeholders that can substitute any number. During a turn, you draw to refill your hand and attempt to play cards from your hand or from the top of your stock pile to the running build piles. The first player to empty their stock pile wins the round. It is one of those games that feels easier than it is to master, which is why it becomes entertaining for months, then years, then decades when played in the right company.

### Setup: Step by Step

1) Each player receives a stock pile face down, with the top card flipped face up. The rest of the deck becomes the draw pile.
2) Players draw up to five cards in hand (depending on the exact edition and house rules).
3) The play area should contain a few empty build piles in the center where players will attempt to place ascending runs from 1 to 12.
4) Decide who goes first by a quick shuffle-off or by the classic rock-paper-scissors that somehow always ends in a truce.

### Turn Structure: What Happens on Your Turn

- You may play cards from your hand or from the top of your stock pile to the build piles in ascending order. You can also add to existing builds if your card fits naturally into the sequence.
- Wild Skip-Bo cards can be used as any number. The timing of using them is a strategic choice that can either accelerate your own progress or hinder someone else’s momentum, depending on your mood and the room temperature.
- When you finish all feasible plays, you draw up to five cards and pass the turn to the next player.
- The round ends when a player has emptied their stock pile. The table erupts in a mix of cheers and groans, which is basically the emotional currency of 90s gaming nostalgia.
