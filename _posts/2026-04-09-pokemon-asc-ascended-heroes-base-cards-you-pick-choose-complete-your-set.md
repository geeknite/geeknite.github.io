## What is ASC Ascended Heroes?

## You Pick Choose Complete Your Set

The tagline You Pick Choose Complete Your Set captures the core philosophy of ASC Ascended Heroes: you are an active participant in shaping your destiny. Here’s how this works in practice and why it matters to both casual players and completionists.

### The selection process

When you start a new ASC Ascended Heroes run, you’ll be faced with a curated pool of BASE Cards from which you select your core lineup. The number of picks depends on your play style and the size of the active rotation, but the principle remains the same: you deliberately choose a handful of BASE Cards that will anchor your deckbuilding decisions for the session. ### Completing your set: the long game

Completion isn’t about finishing the entire game in a single night. It’s about the long arc: collecting, trading, and playing matches that gradually fill in the gaps. The simpler your base, the quicker you’ll feel confident to complete the set in the context of ongoing campaigns or seasonal leagues. For some players, part of the joy is tracking progress across weeks and feeling the incremental thrill of adding a missing BASE Card that suddenly elevates a previously mediocre pairing into a potent combo.

### Set-building strategies for you and your group

- Start with a simple core: pick two or three BASE Cards that have obvious synergy and one flexible option that can adapt to various matchups.
- Plan for coverage: identify two or three potential counters to your own deck, and choose BASE Cards that help you navigate those threats.
- Embrace diversity within unity: don’t force a single archetype if you see a few complementary BASE Cards that can be mixed into different lines. The best players learn to pivot—without losing their founding identity.
- Trade smart, not fast: if your local scene supports trading, make a plan to complete your set through thoughtful exchanges rather than mass grabbing. The long game rewards patience and negotiation skills almost as much as it does raw power.

If you want a couple of concrete examples, consider a pair of base cards that share an energy synergy and a third that smooths card draw—suddenly you can chain plays, keep a steady tempo, and outlast opponents who are chasing big, swingy turns.
