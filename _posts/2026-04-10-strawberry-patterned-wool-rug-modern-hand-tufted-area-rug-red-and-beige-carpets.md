### Sourcing and materials
This rug uses high-quality wool that's been dyed with color-fast pigments designed to resist fading in typical living-room lighting. Wool is a renewable resource, and hand-tufted production is typically more efficient than fully hand-knotted rugs, giving you a product that's both premium and reasonably timely to deliver. ### Craftsmanship: Hand-Tufted vs Hand-Knotted
In the rug world, a hand-knotted rug is often considered the pinnacle of craftsmanship but also the most expensive and time-consuming. The hand-tufted process, by contrast, uses a tufting gun to insert wool into a backing fabric, producing a dense pile with a modern vibe. It's the modern hacker approach to rug-making: fast, precise, and adaptable for complex patterns. If you've ever seen a technique video on YouTube, you'll know what I'm talking about: a conveyor belt of yarn being punched into a canvas, the mechanical rhythm almost like a drum solo for interior design. The result: a rug that looks intricate without the premium price of a hand-knotted piece.

### Materials and Care: Practical and Realistic
Wool is naturally stain-resistant and resists water to a degree, which helps in a room where beverages may be consumed near dramatic desk lamps. Still, protection is essential. The recommended care involves regular vacuuming with a gentle setting, quick blotting of spills with a clean cloth, and avoidance of harsh chemical cleaners that can break down wool fibers. For more stubborn stains, consult the manufacturer's care guide; if that fails, a professional cleaner who specializes in wool rugs is worth the investment.

## Care and Maintenance

### Regular care
- Vacuum the rug weekly with a beater bar on a low setting to keep the pile fluffy and to remove dust that would otherwise dull the color over time.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color distribution. This is especially important in rooms with direct sunlight or high-traffic zones.

### Stain removal
- Blot, don't rub. Rubbing can push the stain deeper into the pile.
- For water-based stains, blot gently with a clean cloth and use mild detergent if necessary. For more stubborn stains, a wool-safe stain remover can help; always test in an inconspicuous corner first.

### Sunlight and fading
- Prolonged sun exposure can fade the colors. Use window treatments to protect the rug from direct sun for long periods.

### Cleaning schedule
- Have the rug professionally cleaned every 12 to 18 months, depending on traffic. This keeps the wool resilient and the pattern crisp.
