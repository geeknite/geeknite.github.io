## Practicality and Longevity
A counter that shines today may look dull tomorrow if it’s not practical. Here’s what to expect in daily life over months to years of service.

### Cleaning Routine and Maintenance
Gloss surfaces require light, routine maintenance. A quick wipe at the end of the day with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner is usually enough. For stubborn smudges, a dedicated gloss cleaner helps restore the shine without leaving streaks. The key is consistency: a little daily routine beats a marathon session of elbow grease on Sunday. If you neglect it, you’ll soon live in a world where fingerprints look like murals and coffee rings become permanent artifacts in the lobby’s microclimate.

### Durability Against Daily Use
The counter’s durability depends on its core materials and how cleverly you treat it in real life. It’s built to handle greeting, credit-card swipes, the occasional emergency printer jam, and the occasional misrouted courier who tries to sign for a package that’s clearly not theirs. Expect a few cosmetic scratches over time, which can be minimized with a proper screen protector for the glassy surfaces or a topcoat refresh if available through your supplier. For high-traffic environments, consider adding protective edge guards to reduce edge chipping.

### Environmental Considerations
If your lobby sits in a bright, hot space, the gloss finish can be more susceptible to yellowing or dulling in the longer term. This is not unusual for glossy laminates, and you’ll want to take care with UV exposure. Using window films and perhaps a strategic plant layout can help keep the surface looking crisp without turning the lobby into a sun-drenched sauna.

## Maintenance and Cleaning
Let’s get practical. People buy things because they look good, but they stay good because you clean them. A glossy white counter asks for two things: a gentle touch and a weekly date with a microfiber cloth.

### Daily Care Tips
- Wipe spills promptly to prevent staining and ghosting from sugary drinks.
- Use a microfiber cloth to avoid micro-scratches.
- Avoid ammonia-based cleaners on gloss surfaces, as they can dull the shine over time.
- Don’t use sharp objects as stand-ins for a stylus—your screen backbone will thank you.

### Dealing with Tough Stains
For stubborn marks, a non-abrasive cleaner and a soft sponge usually does the trick. If you’re dealing with ink or permanent marker, test a small area first. You might need a specialized plastic cleaner to avoid removing the gloss on the surface. In a pinch, a touch of mild dish soap and water can loosen grime without turning your desk into an accidental science experiment.

### Long-Term Care
Consider scheduling a yearly refresh of the surface by your supplier. A surface that’s professionally resurfaced will look nearly new and extend its life without requiring you to pretend the crackle is intentional—the “artisanal crack” that you say is deliberate to match your “rustic industrial” vibe. If you’re in a high-traffic area, you might benefit from protective edge guards or mats in front of the counter to reduce abrasion where you walk and talk and sign a dozen forms per hour.
