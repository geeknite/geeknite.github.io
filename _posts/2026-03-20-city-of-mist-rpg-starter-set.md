City of Mist loves a good neon noir vibe so hard it practically glows on caffeine. If you enjoy superheroes, detective fiction, and the occasional existential crisis about whether your character is more memory than muscle, City of Mist is probably already whispering in your ear. The Starter Set is not the full game, but it is the red solo cup you pour your first taste into. In this review, we’re going to crack open the box, poke at the dice, salute the glossy art, and figure out whether this set is a gateway drug for new players or a trapdoor for veterans seeking a faster one-shot.

What’s in the box
- A compact core booklet that markets itself as quick start rules and then proceeds to explain a system that is not as quick as a kettle on a Windows PC.
- Several pre-generated templates you can spin up in about five minutes of table time, plus guidance for making your own Mist-powered hero in roughly the same interval.
- A city setting that frames the noir vibe and gives your GM a map of trouble to drop into the players’ laps.
- A handful of tokens and character sheets; maybe a few dice, likely a small deck of cards or similar mechanics for the masks and logos.
- A GM guide with scene prompts, mood guidance, and a few ready-to-run encounters that show how the city’s mood shifts as players push against it.

Box quality and production values: City of Mist Starter Set is not a budget throwaway. It leans into the neon aesthetic with well-printed art, a sturdy cover, and a compact book size that’s easy to carry into a coffee shop with pretensions of “serious gaming.” The pages lay flat enough for quick reference, and the art does a lot of heavy lifting in communicating the vibe: think rain-streaked skylines, glowing logos in the margins, and character portraits that look like they came from a vintage comic cover illustrated by someone who secretly loves midnight snack recipes.

System overview
City of Mist positions itself as a roleplaying game about ordinary people who realize they are more than human versions of what they seem. The core concept is myth meets modern city, where detectives and criminals aren’t simply people with a hardboiled attitude—they’re embodiments of mythic powers grafted onto the city’s grid. The Starter Set introduces you to two key ideas: Masks and Logos. Masks are the identities players inhabit—your character’s public face, beliefs, motivations, and the things they’re trying to protect. Logos are the powers that give them an edge—think the symbolic, mythic, or cosmic aspects that let you bend reality in clever ways when the story demands it.

Rules in a nutshell:
- Characters are built by balancing two axes: Masks and Logos. The more you lean into your Masks, the more you influence how the city sees you; the more you lean into your Logos, the more dramatic power you can unleash in the right moment.
- Scenes focus on social and investigative challenges more than brute force. The system rewards clever roleplay, planning, and narrative gambits.
- The dice are used to resolve conflicts with a thematic flavor rather than heavy crunch. You’ll roll to see if the city buys your dramatic entry or if it tosses a neon sign in your face.

The tone and the GM toolkit
City of Mist is a mood game. If you crave hyper-specific combat math, you’ll be disappointed; if you crave atmosphere and character-driven drama, you’ll feel right at home. The Starter Set includes GM guidance on how to frame scenes, evoke the city’s mood, and manage the pace of drama. The advice is practical and spicy: treat the city as a living character, seed your scenes with moral choices, and let players’ Actions ripple through both their micro arcs and the city’s larger story.

Character creation and play flow
- Pre-generated templates in the Starter Set give players a fast entry point. They arrive with a city-infused identity and a ready-to-go Logos ability, which helps you start playing within two or three pages of setup.
- Creating your own Mist-powered hero is a two-step dance: first pick a Masks concept, then attach a Logos capability. The pairing should feel like a myth stepping into a modern body—dramatic, surprising, and a little ridiculous—in the best possible way.
- The session flow tends to favor shorter scenes that accumulate into a coherent arc. The Starter Set’s examples demonstrate how a case can arise from a simple social encounter and escalate into a city-spanning revelation.

Art direction and mood
The art in City of Mist leans into neon-streaked nights and the silhouette of a city that never sleeps. The style sells the tone before you even open the rules. The mood guidance helps you stage a scene with a minimal setup: dim corner booths, rain on the neon, and a line of dialogue that hints at a larger secret. If you’re worried about “gamemastering in the rain,” this book gives you a mental toolkit: mood music suggestions, prop prompts, and micro-scene prompts you can drop in during play.

Where the Starter Set shines
- Accessibility for new players: The Starter Set lowers the barrier to entry with quick-start templates and a digestible rules frame.
- Quick-start scenarios: The included adventures demonstrate how Masks and Logos play off one another in a compact, cinematic way.
- GM tools: The book includes practical guidance for managing the city’s mood, pacing encounters, and guiding players toward meaningful choices.

Where it falls short
- Crunch versus flavor: The game leans heavily toward storytelling, which isn’t the best fit for players who want a heavy tactical experience.
- Power balance: Some Logos combos can feel stronger in certain scenes, while others require creative problem-solving to shine. The Starter Set demonstrates a few good examples, but it’s not a perfect balance out of the box.
- Content depth: If you want a deeper dive into the mechanics and more options for Masks and Logos, you’ll want the core rulebook.

Worked-play ideas
A sample session outline could look like this:
- Hook: The protagonist’s city is vibrating with a new Logos rumor. A bank heist becomes a cover for a superstition turned fact.
- Rising action: The Masks debate whether to reveal their secret identity to the city, while the Logos power manipulates crowd imagery to reveal the villains’ hubris.
- Climax: The hero confronts a choice that could either restore balance to the city or burn it to the ground as a final truth.
- Denouement: The city’s mood shifts, revealing a new set of consequences that will ripple into future sessions.

Campaign seeds
- The Neon Cathedral: A Logos-driven arc that ties a religious symbol into a criminal empire ruling a district.
- The Waking Street: A case where an ordinary street becomes a living mirror of the city’s collective memory, forcing Masks to confront what they truly want from the city.

Final thoughts
City of Mist Starter Set stands as a capable gateway into a modern mythic noir RPG. It won’t satisfy players who crave crunchy combat rules, but for groups that want to tell stories about people who become legends through the choices they make in a city that literally hums with possibility, this starter gives you a powerful nudge in the right direction. The interplay between Masks and Logos creates a thinking-person’s superhero vibe, where your power comes from the identity you build and the stories you’re eager to tell.

Do you buy it? If you’re curious about the neon-noir vibe and want to test the waters without a huge up-front investment, yes. If you’re chasing a long, crunchy tactical RPG with dozens of pages of equipment variants, City of Mist is probably not your jam—at least not at this moment.

Final recommendation
- Best for players who want mood, story, and a hero’s journey built around identity rather than brute force.
- Great as a one-shot or a short arc to test the system and vibe before diving into the full core rulebook.
- A green light for groups that enjoy collaborative storytelling and a city that feels alive.
