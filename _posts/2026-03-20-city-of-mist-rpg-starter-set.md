Myth meets a modern metropolis, where detectives and criminals are embodiments of mythic powers grafted onto the city’s grid. The Starter Set introduces two central ideas: Masks and Logos. Masks are the personas your heroes wear—the public face, beliefs, motivations, and the things they’re trying to protect. Logos are the powers that grant your edge—the symbolic, mythic, or cosmic aspects that let you bend reality when the story asks for it.\n\nRules in a nutshell:\n- Characters are built by balancing two axes: Masks and Logos. The more you lean into Masks, the more the city recognizes and responds to you; the more you lean into Logos, the more dramatic, narrative leverage you can unleash when it matters most.\n- Scenes emphasize social and investigative challenges rather than brute force. The system rewards clever roleplay, careful planning, and narrative gambits that let players outthink the setting as much as outmuscle it.\n- The dice are used to resolve conflicts with a thematic flavor rather than heavy crunch. Expect moments where a roll answers a question like: Will the city buy your entry, or will it retaliate with a glowing billboard of doom?\n\n### Masks and Logos: A Two-Character Dance\nMasks shape how the city sees you, what you’re willing to reveal, and what you’re fighting for. Logos shape how your powers manifest, how you twist the city’s rules to your advantage, and how your abilities align with your overarching narrative purpose. The interplay between Masks and Logos is where the system truly hums: it invites you to build a hero who feels inevitable—like a myth finding a human shoulder to ride on.\n\n### The Rules Light, Not Light Rules\nThe Starter Set aims to be approachable, not a workbook of every possible edge case.
