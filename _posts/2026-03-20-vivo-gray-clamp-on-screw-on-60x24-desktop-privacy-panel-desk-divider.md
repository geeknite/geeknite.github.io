## Installation and compatibility

### Clamp-on vs screw-on operation
The clamp-on portion is designed for quick setup. You place the panel's bracket over the desk edge and tighten the clamp until it feels secure and confident. You should be able to slide the panel along the length of the desk to find the best privacy angle and the least interference with a keyboard tray or a monitor arm.

If you’re the kind of person who loves permanent modifications, you can opt for the screw-on approach. With a few anchors, your panel becomes a fixed landmark on your desk—a reminder to take your breaks away from the screen and to pretend you’ve got a real office vibe.

### Desk compatibility and mounting tips
- Minimum desk thickness: Check the manufacturer specs. If your desk edge is thinner than the clamp can handle, you’ll want to rely on the screw-on method to ensure stability.
- Desk depth: A panel this size benefits from some extra depth on the desk to avoid the panel interfering with your monitor stands or keyboard tray.
- Cable management: The panel can influence cable routing. Plan your cables in advance; you may want to route them under your panel or behind it to keep the workspace clean.

### Step-by-step quick setup
1) Position the panel over the desk edge. 2) Clip the clamp and tighten until the bracket hugs the desk edge. 3) For screw-on, mark pre-drill points, insert anchors, then tighten the screws. 4) Adjust the panel to the desired height and angle. 5) Check for movement. If it wiggles like a caffeinated jelly bean, tighten the screws or readjust the clamps.

### Practical notes
- Weight: The panel adds some heft to the desk boundary, but not enough to crash a coffee mug. It’s light enough to reposition but sturdy enough to stay put during intense typing sessions.
- Surface protection: Some clamp hardware includes protective pads or bumpers to avoid marring the desk edge. If yours doesn’t, consider adding felt pads or a thin protective strip.
