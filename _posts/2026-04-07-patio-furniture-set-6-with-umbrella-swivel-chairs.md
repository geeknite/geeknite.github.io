## Unboxing and setup: the moment of truth
Patio furniture is half about the packaging drama and half about actual installation. The six swivel chairs come flat-packed with bolts, washers, and a tiny hex key that asks more questions than your last ex. The good news: the hardware is standardized, which means you won’t spend an entire afternoon hunting for the same size Allen key that you used to assemble that bookshelf from the 90s.

The assembly involves a straightforward sequence: attach legs to the table, secure chairs to the foundation frame, and then slide in the cushions. The worst part is not the manual labor but the suspenseful moment when you realize the umbrella base is heavier than a small child’s backpack and you’re already wearing sunscreen like a vampire who forgot their cape. A second pair of hands is helpful, but not required. If you are solo building, plan for a measurable coffee-and-acoustic-guitar break halfway through.

A little tip from our lab: lay out all the hardware in a grid before you start. It’s not a makeshift ouija board, but it will save you from three minutes of frantic searching while muttering to the couch cushions that the bolts have gone rogue.

## Real-world usage and maintenance tips
- Cushions: Bring the cushions indoors during heavy rain or winter. If you can’t store them, use a breathable outdoor storage bag to prevent mildew.
- Cleaning: A quick rinse or wipe-down with mild soap keeps the surfaces looking fresh. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the glass or dull the aluminum finish.
- Umbrella care: Clean the canopy with mild soap and water; let it dry completely before folding to avoid moisture buildup that leads to mold.
- Storage: When not in use for extended periods, consider a furniture cover that fits the entire set. It’s not glamorous, but it’s cheaper than buying new cushions every other spring.
