## Practical Considerations: Cleaning, Care, and Long-Term Love

Wool rugs deserve gentle handling and a little love. Here are pragmatic care tips to keep your Mystique Bone rug looking sharp for seasons to come:
- Vacuuming: Use a low-suction setting or a beater bar on the lowest setting. Frequent light vacuums keep dirt from embedding into the fibers.
- Spills: Act quickly. Blot with a clean white cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push a spill deeper. For stubborn liquids, use a mild wool-safe cleaner and test on a hidden corner first.
- Rotating: Periodically rotate the rug to ensure even wear and fading—especially if your space gets strong sun in the afternoon.
- Underlay: A quality rug pad not only adds comfort but reduces movement and protects the flooring beneath from dents.
- Professional cleaning: For deeper cleans, a professional rug cleaning service is worth it. Wool holds onto fibers, so you don’t want to risk a DIY attempt that leaves your rug looking less than pristine.

What about pets? Wool is surprisingly resilient to pet traffic, and many wool blends resist odors and staining better than synthetics. That said, pets like to claim the rug as their throne for naps and the occasional dramatic QOL cut-scene (think feline Judges Judy with a tail). If you have an energetic dog with muddy paws, a quick broom sweep or a damp cloth will help, followed by a careful vacuum to fluff the fibers back to life.
