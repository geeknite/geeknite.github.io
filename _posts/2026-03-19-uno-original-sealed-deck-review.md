## How UNO Works: A Quick Refresher for New Players
If you’re new to UNO or you’ve spent the last decade using UNO as a sleep aid, here’s a quick refresher on how the game flows. The rules are simple enough to teach in under five minutes but strategic enough to keep things interesting for the rest of the night.

- Objective: Be the first player to score 500 points. Points are earned by the cards your opponents are left with after someone goes out. If you want a shorter game, you can target a smaller number of points.
- Setup: Each player starts with seven cards. The remaining deck becomes the draw pile, and the top card of the deck starts the discard pile.
- Turn structure: On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number/symbol of the card on the discard pile. If you don’t have a matching card, you draw from the draw pile.
- Special cards: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four cards add chaos to the table. Skip and Draw Two cause the next player to miss a turn or draw cards, Reverse changes the direction of play, Wild lets you pick the color, and Wild Draw Four makes the next player draw four cards and also lets you declare the new color. Remember the rule about Wild Draw Four: play it only if you have no card that can be played in the current color; it’s a rule that’s ignored by rebels and, occasionally, by the referee in friendly matches.
- UNO shout: When you’re down to your last card, you must shout UNO. Forget and get called out with a penalty, and your opponents will remind you with glee and maybe a dramatic eye roll.
- Winning: The round ends when a player no longer has cards. The scoring works as follows: number cards are worth their face value, special cards carry different values, wild cards carry high value. The deck’s ultimate goal is to bankrupt everyone else and win the bragging rights for a week or two.

This is the core structure. The official sealed deck doesn’t change the rules; it simply provides you a pristine, unplayed set that will make your nostalgia sing and your dinner table feel like a tiny arcade where adult supervision is optional.
