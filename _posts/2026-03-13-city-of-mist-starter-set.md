## Introduction

City of Mist is neon noir you can play with friends without needing a trench coat and a leveraged knowledge of crime cinema. The Starter Set is the gateway drug to City of Mist energy: moody city vibes, Masks that feel like cinematic personas, and a storytelling engine built for collaboration more than cogs in a rulebook. If the core game is a long-form novel, the Starter Set is a well-edited short story with a satisfying twist ending.

In this review we explore what you actually get inside the box, how it plays at the table, the GM experience, art and production values, and whether this starter pack earns your dice, or your space, or both.

## What City of Mist is trying to do

If you have not met City of Mist before, imagine a city where mythology wears a modern coat and the legends are stitched into everyday life. Ordinary people become icons because they have a story worth telling, and the city itself is a character that nudges, teases, and sometimes shouts ideas to the players. The Starter Set distills that mood into a package designed for a first session, not a two-year campaign. It is not a cheat sheet for a cinematic epic; it is a map to a neon-noir night where decisions matter and the night remembers.

## What's in the Starter Set

The Starter Set is designed to be played with a group that is new to City of Mist or to role-playing in general. It leans on a quick-start structure so you can go from unboxing to table in under an hour, while still weaving a story that feels like it belongs to the City and its Masks. Here is a guide to what you typically find inside, with notes on how it feels in actual play:

- A concise GM guide and a starter scenario to run your first session
- A set of ready-made Masks with narrative hooks and powers
- Character sheets that are readable and quick to fill out
- Tokens or counters for tracks, conditions, and clues
- A compact map or scene tiles to set locations quickly
- A handful of story seeds and case hooks to kick off the session
- A rules reference card and a quick-start dice guide

Note: contents can vary by edition and print run, but the intention is clear: you can run a session without needing to rummage through a giant core rulebook.

### Box contents (a closer look)

The real charm of a Starter Set is not the sum of the pieces, but how they combine to create momentum at the table. The City of Mist Starter Set emphasizes three things: atmosphere, accessibility, and momentum. The artistically saturated cards, the readable stat blocks, and the simple dice mechanic all contribute to a feeling that you can "begin the story now" without a dozen prep sessions.

## How it plays: quickstart rules and the Masks in action

City of Mist uses a dice pool system where you roll D6s equal to the number of factors you have, and you add or subtract dice based on the situation and the Masks you embody. The Starter Set keeps this approachable by giving you a limited but expressive set of Masks, each with its own flavor and mechanical triggers. The result is a game that rewards you for thinking narratively while still giving you concrete ways to resolve actions.

### Masks as character and mechanics

Masks are more than cosmetic identity. They are your engine for telling the story. Each Mask carries three dimensions: Identity, Power, and an associated set of Traits that will influence the dice you roll and the narrative outcomes you narrate. When your Mask’s Power fires, you can gain a controlled advantage, but often at a cost that pushes the scene toward a dramatic moment. The Starter Set’s Masks carefully balance this trade-off so that new players feel the catharsis of a power turn without an overwhelming rules burden.

### The dice and narrative resolution

In operation, you roll a pool of six-sided dice. A successful outcome may require a certain number of successes, but the engine also rewards narrative description and cooperative storytelling. If a Cue or a Clue is triggered by the action, you can add a die or create a narrative detail that influences future scenes. This loop—act, respond, narrate—drives momentum and helps players feel that the city is watching, and that the city has opinions about their decisions.

## The GM experience

A good starter kit should make the GM feel supported rather than overwhelmed. The Starter Set does this by providing ready-to-run scenes, simple procedures for running encounters, and clear guidance on how to escalate tension without turning every session into a resource management exercise. The GM is not alone in this city; the book supplies prompts, hooks, and a few GM-only tips that help you breathe life into the block you invented five minutes ago.

If you are a first-time GM, you will appreciate how the system reduces the friction of running a mystery. If you are a veteran GM, you will appreciate having a compact toolkit you can carry to a convention table, with a quick-scene generator, a few ready-made antagonists, and a set of narrative prompts that require minimal prep.

## Art, design, and production values

City of Mist’s art direction has always walked the line between moody noir and fantastical fable. The Starter Set continues that tradition with evocative visuals, bold typography, and a packaging that is sturdy enough for trips to and from the gaming club. The aesthetic makes a difference: you are more likely to lean into the mood when the visuals reflect that neon-lit city you want to inhabit for a couple of hours.

The components are practical and well-constructed. Cards are cleanly laid out, the dice are readable, and the map is big enough to be functional but small enough to be used in a cafe or a small dining room. The tactile experience matters when you want a game to feel special, and the Starter Set nails that without turning the experience into a production number.

## Value, audience, and longevity

The Starter Set is a gateway product. It’s priced to be accessible for curious players and curious GMs who want to test-drive the vibe before committing to a larger rulebook purchase. For a group that values narrative clarity over mechanical depth, this set provides a robust entry point. For players who enjoy the texture of world-building and the thrill of a mystery solved through character-driven action, the Starter Set offers a durable foundation for sessions that can scale up as you accumulate more Masks and more case files.

In terms of replayability, the set excels at one-shots and short arcs. If your playgroup thrives on long campaigns with sprawling city-wide conspiracies, you’ll want to expand beyond the Starter Set sooner rather than later. However, if you prefer succinct, cinema-style sessions with a tight crew, the Starter Set is a perfect repeated-use starter kit that you can rotate with other neon-noir one-shot formats.

## Play tips for your first City of Mist session

- Start with a single Mask and a single clue: the simplicity helps players focus on character voice and the city vibe rather than the mechanics.
- Keep the city as a co-creative partner: ask players what the city wants, and let their choices shape the location and the next scene. The city should feel like a living, breathing antagonist as much as any villain.
- Use short scene prompts and quick-cut flashbacks to reveal backstory. Flashbacks are a powerful tool for this game’s theme and don’t require an intricate rules framework.
- Embrace the genre mash: you can mix film noir, urban fantasy, and mythic archetypes in a single session. The Starter Set gives you enough leeway to experiment without turning into a lore encyclopaedia.

## Final verdict and recommendation

The City of Mist Starter Set is not a full, inexhaustible dungeon of rules; it is a mood box. It is a toolkit that empowers a GM to get from concept to session quickly while keeping the vibe intact. It rewards players who enjoy character-driven drama, scene-changing reveals, and a city where moral complexity is the stock-in-trade.
