## Painting and Assembly

One of the joys of collecting miniatures for both DnD and Zombicide is deciding whether you want to finish the model in a museum-grade paint job or go full chaos goblin with a wash and a brave brush. The Orc Zombie lends itself to both paths. The sculpt holds enough texture to respond nicely to dry brushing and layered shading, letting you bring out the armor, the decayed skin, and those telltale orc scratches without turning the piece into a miniature painting marathon.

### Assembly Tips

- Start by affixing the head and torso separately if the fit feels tight. A tiny dab of plastic cement will help the parts settle without warping or stress marks.
- Use a lightweight cement on the base to keep the mini from wobbling during painting, especially if you’re doing a tabletop fight where you need to know if you can actually bend the arc of a spell in midair.
- Prime with a neutral gray or bone color to ensure shadows and highlights pop. If you’re into bright, comic-like colors, a white primer can help the greens and metallics punch through more effectively.

### Painting Strategy for a Cinematic Look
If you’re new to painting minis, start with a two-step approach: base color plus shade. For an Orc Zombie, you’ll want to emphasize the undead pallor of decayed skin in greenish-tinged hues, while keeping a sense of their orc heritage through stronger greens in areas like exposed muscle or bone plates. A couple of metallics for armor pieces add contrast; you don’t need to go overboard. Remember: a zombie does not respond to the concept of “shimmer.” It responds to “effort.”

Guiding steps for a clean finish:
- Base coat the skin with a desaturated green, add a wash of brown-green to bring out the recesses.
- Layer in lighter greens on raised surfaces for dimension.
- Dry brush bone or ivory tones on exposed skulls or bone armor to give a weathered, ancient feel.
- Paint the eyes with a small dab of red to give a haunted look that will pop on the gaming table.
- Finish with a matte seal to reduce shine and keep the undead vibe believable on camera or in person.
