## Material science: what’s under the fibers?

The Quarry Oyster is marketed as synthetic. What does that mean in practical terms? You’re looking at a blend (often polypropylene or a similar synthetic fiber) designed for durability, stain resistance, and ease of cleaning. These are the quarterback fibers for households with kids, spills, and the occasional “I want pizza on the rug, but I also want to wear socks” moment. Synthetic fibers typically resist crushing better than natural fibers, which helps in high-traffic areas and under heavy desk wheels—the kind of wheels that squeak if you look at them funny. 

Maintenance is where the Quarry Oyster shines. Vacuuming is straightforward; a quick pass with a standard vacuum is usually enough for daily upkeep. For spills, most synthetic rugs like this one respond well to blotting with a clean cloth and a mild detergent solution. The key is to avoid saturating the fiber—think of it like training your friend to spill only small, manageable lagoons rather than full-on monsoon floods. The fiber composition helps resist staining from common culprits: coffee, red wine (a villain we all fear), pet accidents, and the occasional drop of soy sauce from a questionable cooking experiment. 

One thing to note is that with synthetic fibers, you’ll want to avoid harsh solvents and aggressive scrubbing. The goal is to preserve the texture and color, not to strip the rug like you’re de-greasing an engine block. A gentle approach plus a quick rinse if needed should bring you back to near-new glory.
