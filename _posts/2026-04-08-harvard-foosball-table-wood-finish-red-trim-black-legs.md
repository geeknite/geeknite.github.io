## Setup and Maintenance

Unpacking a foosball table is part puzzle, part unboxing ritual, and entirely a test of your velocity toward adulthood. Here’s what you need to know about getting the Harvard table ready for prime time.

### Assembly Time and Tools
The box contains a reasonable amount of hardware and a compact instruction sheet that looks like it was written by someone who learned English as a second language and then refined it into slightly amusing bureaucratic poetry. Expect two to three hours of assembly time if you’re starting from scratch and you insist on aligning every screw with laser-perfect precision. A power drill helps, but you’ll thank yourself later for taking your time and not water-spritzing the finish to speed things up. The clip-on legs and straightforward rail system make the process less soul-crushing than some other tables on the market, which is a nice bonus when you’re assembling after a long workday.

### Maintenance and Upkeep
Wood finishes on foosball tables can be sensitive to humidity and temperature, so keep the room climate-friendly. Wipe the cabinet with a soft microfiber to avoid scratch marks and use a mild cleaner designed for wood or furniture-grade surfaces. The red trim is painted; avoid aggressive solvents that could dull or chip the color over time. For the playing surface, a quick dusting and occasional wipe-down with a damp cloth will keep the balls rolling smoothly. If a ball becomes misshapen or the rods get squeaky, a little WD-40 on the joints (applied sparingly) will bring back the glide. Remember, you’re not building immune system for a spaceship—just a foosball table—so be gentle with the finishes.

### Longevity and Durability
The Harvard table is designed for home use, and that means durability is robust enough for family game nights and friendly tournaments among roommates. It’s not designed to withstand industrial arcade abuse, but for the price, you’re getting a long-lasting piece that can endure a few year’s worth of enthusiastic league play, with occasional moves that could involve a friend’s back as a courtesy stretch.
