## Care, Cleaning, and Durability (Expanded)
Durability depends largely on fiber density and loom quality, which in value-line rugs can be hit-or-miss. The polypropylene fibers ensure easy cleaning and stain resistance to an extent. Routine vacuuming will keep surface dirt from settling into the fibers. For spills, act quickly: blot with a clean cloth rather than rub, or the stain might spread like a glitch in the matrix. For tougher stains, a mild detergent diluted in water can be used; test on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Do not use bleach or harsh solvents. If you have kids or pets that treat the rug like a playground, consider a rug pad to reduce wear and to add some extra cushioning under footfall. A quality pad also helps to keep the rug in place, preventing sliding when you’re pushing a chair out to reach a keyboard or a snack plate.

The smell of a brand-new rug is not dangerous; it’s the scent of new goods. If the odor bothers you, air the rug out for a day or two in a well-ventilated area. A light breeze and a sunny day can do wonders. If you notice a persistent odor after a week, consider returning to the vendor if you’re within the return policy window or exploring a fabric-safe air freshener that neutralizes odors without leaving a chemical residue on the fibers.
