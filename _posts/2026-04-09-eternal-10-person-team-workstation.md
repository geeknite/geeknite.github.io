## Ergonomics and Comfort for Ten People

### Spacing and seating arrangement

The table geometry is designed to avoid the classic “group hug” problem where elbows collide and coffee sloshes onto the keyboard. There are slight arcuate curves in the bench edges to create a natural flow for movement, so you can slide into your seat without performing a tactical maneuver that would make a gymnast blush. Seating positions are intended to be neutral with the possibility to step into a more specialized chair for those who need extra lumbar support. The aim is to keep posture in check and mood in checkmate.

### Visual and cognitive load management

With ten screens and a wall of monitors, the cognitive load can become a rave if you’re not careful. The Eternal system helps by allowing independent zones and a central shared view when the team is aligned on a single objective. Lighting is a big deal for a team of ten. The system supports adjustable task lighting to prevent those blue-tinted faces from turning into digital totems of Monday. The ambient lighting is designed to reduce eye strain and help maintain a comfortable temperature along the bench. If you tend to overheat while making a perfect feature flip, the design anticipates this with airflow channels and heat-dissipation-friendly materials.


## Setup and Installation: A Practical Roadmap

### Step-by-step plan

1. Measure the room and confirm the floor plan can accommodate a ten-seat bench with comfortable circulation. 2. Unbox the modules and lay out the components in a staged manner. 3. Install the central hub and secure the backbone to the floor or supported frame. 4. Mount the monitor arms and connect the docking stations. 5. Route cables through the channels and secure cable ties. 6. Adjust each seat for height, chair type, and personal preference. 7. Run a test session and adjust lighting and acoustics as needed. 8. Train the team on the new workflow and the etiquette of “you can pass the keyboard without a ceremonial bow.”

### Required space and layout

The footprint of the Eternal system will depend on the chosen configuration. A ten-seat axis typically requires a wider footprint than a regular desk cluster but is more space-efficient than a dozen individual desks crammed into a similar area. The layout should allow for at least 1.5 meters of walking space behind the bench, with a buffer zone for the central hub to allow tech maintenance without a stealth operation.

### Cable management tips

Cable management is not glamorous, but it is essential. Use the internal channels to keep power and data separate, label every major cable pair, and include quick-release ties for reconfiguration. If your team is prone to cable art projects, add a removable cable cover panel that doubles as a tiny runway for dramatic cable reveals during sprint demos. Pro-tip: test the setup with multiple devices and ensure that the central hub does not become a magnet for every charger sold by a barista who forgot to drink his own coffee.
