## What Fortune Row Adds to City of Mist

Fortune Row is not just more tables; it’s a re-centering of the game’s vibe toward a specific kind of urban myth machine—a casino as a city of legends. The book leans into two core ideas: the Fortune Row as a series of micro-societies within the city, and a set of new rules that give players more hands-on control of their fate. You’ll find: new Masks (the core concept of City of Mist), new Modules (mini-arcs with their own beats and villains), and a suite of GM tools designed to help you pivot from “investigating a myth” to “managing a myth in progress.” It’s the type of content that makes your table lean closer, whisper conspiratorially, and say, “Okay, now you’ve got my attention.”

What that actually means in play is a mix of punchy mechanical shorthand and vivid, cinematic scenes. Masks remain the flavor of choice for characters, but Fortune Row crystallizes how those Masks intersect with a Row’s defined territories and power plays. If you’ve ever wanted a city section where the local god of luck runs a speakeasy that only appears on the 7th of every month, Fortune Row gives you the framework to run it without a GM’s notes-drawer full of scribbles. The rulebook’s tone is confident, a little cheeky, and very much in the spirit of City of Mist’s almost mythic noir. It’s not attempting to reinvent the wheel so much as it is giving the wheel a velvet rope and a back-alley valet.

## Mechanics: How Fortune Row Changes the Cart

### Mask, Power, and Row Interplay
The core mechanic of City of Mist—dice pools, Masked Abilities, and the hows of your mythic power in a modern setting—remains intact. Fortune Row’s contribution is to push Masks toward more pronounced affiliations with Row-level outcomes. Think of each Fortune Row as a political microclimate where certain Masks naturally lean into or against the Row’s power structures. The book provides clear examples of these alignments, plus quick-start tables for creating Row-specific complications that feel inevitable rather than contrived.

The dice system stays familiar: two six-sided dice for the action, with successes determining outcomes and seven to nine results adding complications or twists. But Fortune Row adds a handful of Row-focused tags, which are small mechanical adjectives that give you more control over stakes, motives, and consequences. It’s a smart addition that reduces the need for improvisation on the fly—your players won’t have to negotiate whether something constitutes a success or a complication, because the Row provides built-in placeholders for those moments.

### New Assets, New Tools
Fortune Row introduces a handful of new play assets: 
- New encounter aids designed for fast pacing during social scenes or heist-like sequences. 
- Quick-start adversary sketches that slot into Row-centric neighborhoods rather than your generic noir alleys. 
- A small but potent set of “Row Cards” you can print and lay on the table, each card offering a thematic beat, a minor complication, or a twist you can deploy when the party thinks they’re in control.

These tools are intentionally compact. The book understands that GMs don’t need a novella to run a Fortune Row scenario; they need crisp prompts, colored dice, and a few backup options if the players do something delightfully unexpected. The design philosophy here is not to overwhelm but to empower. If the core rules already feel like a city guide written by a bard with a badge, Fortune Row is the well-organized appendix that finally includes a map, a wallet, and a flashlight for every alley you’ll stroll through.

### Tone and Pace
Fortune Row leans toward a brisk tempo. Several segments are explicitly designed for one-session arcs or quick arcs that can crescendo in a single long table session. The pacing advice is practical, including tips on escalating stakes, how to weave in a Row’s power to cause consequences without forcing long exposition dumps, and how to end with a satisfying mythic beat rather than a neat bow. It’s the kind of book that makes you feel like you’ve got a toolkit that actually fits in your pocket—the pocket is a little bigger, sure, but you can still carry it into a coffee shop and pretend you’re doing surveillance for a god of luck without looking too suspicious.

## Layout, Artwork, and Accessibility

Fortune Row’s layout is familiar if you’ve spent time with City of Mist books: clean two-column spreads that balance rules text with narrative prompts and a generous splash of noir-inflected art. The art direction nails the vibe—neon reflections on rain-slick pavement, a sly wink from a statue that’s probably not just a statue, and posters that promise power if you can crack the code. The book isn’t just pretty; it’s practical. Sidebars, quick-reference boxes, and callout quotes appear where you’d expect them, which means you can thumb to a needed section during a tense moment without spelunking through a labyrinth of page numbers.

From an accessibility angle, Fortune Row remains approachable. The language is punchy but not inaccessible, and the examples walk you through edge cases without requiring a law degree in myth-law. The layout does a good job of separating concept from execution, which is crucial when you’re standing in a dimly lit gaming shop corner trying to interpret a Row’s political mechanics before the dice start rolling.

If you’re curious about the physical product, the book uses standard stock with enough thickness to survive a few glares from the dice and a stray napkin. The binding holds, the pages lay flat, and there’s a satisfying weight to the physical artifact that mirrors how it feels to finally crack a tricky Row to your side during a session.

## Play Experience: One-Shot to Campaign Arc

In our playtests, Fortune Row shone as a bridge between the micro-plot and the longer mythic arc. A single session of Fortune Row can feel like a speed-run through a myth-making day in a city that never sleeps. A longer arc can use Row-based factions to create a living web of loyalties, double-crosses, and urban legends that mutate as the players interact with the Row’s power brokers. The real joy is watching players realize that their Masks aren’t just costumes; they’re tickets to influence in a Row’s domain. And the GM gets to stage scenes that feel more intimate than a city-wide conspiracy while still carrying the same dramatic weight.

The dice feel good here. The successes aren’t narrative fluff; they carry mechanical weight with meaningful consequences. Failures sting, but Fortune Row provides options for “the fate flip” where a setback forces the team to improvise a new path toward a mythic conclusion. It’s not about guaranteeing a sparkly finish; it’s about the possibility that you can twist the neon night into a shape that reveals a heartbeat beneath the glitz.

Humor has a place in Fortune Row, too. The book leans into the absurdity of legends operating within a real-world ecosystem of casinos, cabarets, and crime-lord towers. You’ll find moments where a Mask’s grand plan collides with a Row’s petty politics in a way that is both ridiculous and deeply satisfying. It’s the same vibe that makes a good noir story punchy: the stakes stay high, but the table’s laughter lightens the tension just enough to keep the tension from snapping.

## Thematic Fit and Group Dynamics

Fortune Row excels at helping players explore the gray areas of motive. The Row’s power structures encourage factions to negotiate, ally, and betray with a clean, rule-supported logic that still feels chaotic enough to mimic human whim. If your group loves puzzles, power grabs, and morally ambiguous decisions, Fortune Row is a stout compass that points toward those storms. If you prefer a brighter, more straightforward hero’s journey, you can still leverage Fortune Row—just lean into the social intrigue and let the masks do the heavy lifting in other scenes.

Group dynamics benefit from the added maneuvering room. The new tools let players discuss what they want their legends to do in a more concrete way: do you want your Mask to be a genius who manipulates probability? Do you want your Row to be the quiet puppet master whose influence is felt more than seen? Fortune Row provides language and systems to have those conversations at the table without turning the session into a masterclass in exposition.

## Campaign Seeds and Quick-Start Scenarios

If you’re itching to run a Fortune Row story right away, the rulebook includes a handful of ready-to-run seeds. They revolve around the Row’s core locales—think a glamorous casino nightclub, a back-alley data vault controlled by a cryptic figure, and a transportation hub where legends come and go like trains in a storm. Each seed includes a Beat Sheet (the sequence of events to hit), notable NPCs (with motives that tie to Masks and Row power), and recommended Scene Hooks for session zero through the big finale.

For example, one seed might place the players in a Row-controlled district where a masked performer’s comeback threatens to expose the Row’s illegal influence. The players must decide whether to intervene directly, stage a counter-myth that blindsides a rival faction, or gamble on a literal risk-for-reward plan that could tip the balance in a way that makes the city remember their names—good or bad.

The seeds are not fragile. They’re adaptable, which is essential for a game about luck, legends, and the messy business of human connection. A GM can take a seed, swap in different NPCs, or shift the Row’s focus to the party’s preferred style—be it heist, social intrigue, or chasing down a myth that refuses to die under the neon glare.

## Accessibility, Replayability, and Longevity

Fortune Row is designed with replayability in mind. The Row system’s modular nature means you can run a one-shot where every scene is a compact micro-arc, or you can weave Fortune Row into a longer campaign with ongoing factions that respond to the players’ actions. The new tools encourage variety in session structure: social scenes can be crunchy in a good way, combat can stay lean, and investigative beats can be light on math as long as you lean into clues that feel mythic rather than mundane.

In terms of accessibility, the book remains readable for newcomers while offering deeper options for veterans. There’s a gentle ramp from core rules to Fortune Row’s concepts, and the quick-start scenes help new groups warm up to the Row’s cadence without needing a full orientation seminar. If you’re a GM who loves deck-building games or modular storytelling, you’ll feel right at home. If you’re more of a mapper and world-builder, Fortune Row still pays off with rich world-building hooks and a clear sense of place.

## Pros and Cons at a Glance

Pros
- Strong thematic focus on mythic urban power and the politics of luck.
- Crisp, usable tools for GMs to generate tension and plot at the table.
- Flexible for one-shot or extended campaigns.
- High-quality layout and evocative artwork that reinforce mood.

Cons

- A few seeds assume a certain level of table familiarity with City of Mist’s broader mythic framework.

## Final Verdict

Fortune Row lands as a confident, stylish expansion that respects the core City of Mist system while delivering enough new knobs to spin a dozen different Row-based adventures before you need a breather. It’s not a radical rewrite; it’s a precision-tuned instrument that helps GMs orchestrate a city where legends literally collide with the everyday. If you enjoy social intrigue, mythic politics, and the fun of watching a party’s Masks ripple through a carefully described underworld, Fortune Row is a must-add to your collection. It’s not about turning City of Mist into a high-concept ballet; it’s about giving you a better stage, a bigger orchestra, and a few more dramatic pauses for the audience to gasp.

If you’re new to the City of Mist line, Fortune Row is a robust invitation to dive into the broader mythic noir playground. If you’re a seasoned Mistmonger, you’ll detect the care with which the designers handled balance and pacing, and you’ll appreciate how the Row’s stories feel both intimate and epic in scale.
