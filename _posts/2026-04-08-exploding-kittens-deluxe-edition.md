#### How to Play (Deluxe Flavor) – The Expanded Take

- The deck is shuffled and each player starts with a hand commonly seven cards, with room for house rules that reflect your table tolerance for chaos. The exact starting hand can be adjusted depending on how much chaos you want to foster at the outset.
- The Exploding Kitten card is mixed into the draw pile. Draw it and you are out unless you can Defuse.
- If you have a Defuse, you must place the Exploding Kitten back into the draw pile in a spot of your choosing. This is where strategy and cowardice collide in glorious fashion.
- On your turn you may play as many cards as you want from your hand or end your turn and draw a card. The real puzzle is deciding when to drop an Attack or a See the Future card to tilt the odds in your favor.
- The last player standing wins. Easy to say, harder to pull off when your table mutes themselves in anticipation of chaos.

### Strategy and Tips

Strategic play in Exploding Kittens Deluxe is less about long term planning and more about micro plays and misdirection. Here are deeper tips that actually help your odds without turning you into a spreadsheet wizard.

Defuse cards are the currency that matters most. Deluxe adds more Defuse style tools that raise survival chances, but you should spend them when they will save you not just for that moment but for future rounds. Don t hoard them like dragon treasure; the goal is to stay alive long enough to become the last kitten standing.

Smart Defuse usage often means reading the draw pile with your brain slightly on fire. If you have seen two See the Future cards and suspect a big Exploding Kitten run is coming, a well timed Defuse can be worth more than a flashy card with a meme on it.

See the Future lets you peek into upcoming draws. Deluxe expands this pool, allowing you to map out a safer few turns.
