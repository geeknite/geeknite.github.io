## Assembly Tips and Troubleshooting

- Before you start, lay out all parts and hardware; a quick inventory helps avoid the classic missing-screw mid-build situation.
- Use a magnetic screwdriver if you have one; it makes small screws feel less adversarial.
- Keep a level handy; a wonky surface can cause headaches when you realize your monitor is not perfectly aligned. A minor tilt can make you feel like you are in a funhouse rather than a modern workspace.
- Don’t over-tighten fasteners; a light hand goes a long way. You want rigidity, not a bent metal sculpture.
- Plan the cable routes ahead of time. It saves you the embarrassment of a tangle tango when you finally plug everything in.
- If you want to add a monitor arm, ensure the desk surface can bear the weight and that you have adequate VESA compatibility with your monitor pair.

## Maintenance and Longevity

A mahogany finish, when properly cared for, ages gracefully. Regular dusting with a soft cloth and occasional mild cleaner will keep the surface looking rich without stripping or dulling the color. Avoid harsh chemicals that can erode the veneer or degrade the panel edges. If you happen to spill a strong coffee, act quickly: blot, then wipe with a damp cloth and let it dry. A wax or furniture polish every few months can help preserve the gloss and extend the life of the veneer. The frame, if treated with a basic anti-corrosion wipe-down, should stay sturdy against the typical wear of daily use.

One last tip: keep a small hand vacuum or lint roller handy for clearing dust from the panel edges. The GOF 1P is a stage on which your desktop life can perform, and keeping it clean ensures the performance remains crisp and distraction-free.
