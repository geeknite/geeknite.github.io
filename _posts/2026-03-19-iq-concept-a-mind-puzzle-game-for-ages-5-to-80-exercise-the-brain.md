### The Core Challenge (What You’re Actually Doing)
The crux of IQ Concept is simple in theory: arrange or connect pieces to satisfy a rule printed on the challenge card. But the devil is in the details, which means your brain will throw a mini party every time you spot a subtle pattern after staring at the same arrangement for a minute.

Common challenge archetypes include:
- Spatial fit: Can you rotate and place pieces to complete a picture or silhouette?
- Sequence logic: Do the tiles line up in a logical progression or follow a numerical pattern?
- Connectivity: Can you connect points or shapes to create a single continuous path?
- Pattern replication: Do you mirror, rotate, or transform a target shape using the given pieces?

The rule on the card is not merely decorative text; it’s a constraint that your brain must respect while you explore the possible moves. Some cards have a single solution; others reward multiple valid approaches, which is a blessing for people who hate “one right answer” situations.
