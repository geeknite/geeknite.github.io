## Real-World Performance

### Foot Traffic and Durability
In a space with kids, pets, or a standing desk, the rug should hold up. The material is designed for everyday wear, with a pile height that won’t trip you up or swallow your toes when you pass by with a laundry basket. The dye-fastness will depend on the fiber mix and the cleaning routine you adopt, but for most households, it will resist the daily antics you typically throw at it.

### Cleaning and Maintenance
- Vacuum regularly to prevent particle buildup. If you have a pet, invest in a pet-specific vacuum head to catch hair before it becomes a mood ring on the rug.
- Blot spills as soon as possible with a clean white cloth. Avoid rubbing to prevent fiber distortion. For stubborn stains, use a mild detergent and test in a hidden corner.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear across high-traffic zones. The large dimension (20 feet long) provides plenty of opportunities to catch sunlight in a photogenic way; rotating helps minimize color fade in one direction.

### Care Tips
- Use a rug pad to prevent slippage and to extend life. A pad adds cushioning and reduces wear; it’s like adding a little extra HP to your rug’s armor.
- Keep away from direct sunlight for extended periods to reduce color fading. If your space has windows that glare like a starship’s viewports, consider UV-blocking window treatments.
