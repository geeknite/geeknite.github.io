## Who are the Palanite Justicars?
The Palanites are an interesting mashup: think about a squad of disciplined officers who trade flashbangs for legal briefs and productivity software. They operate under a theme of order, procedure, and a dash of righteous indignation—perfect for a campaign where you want to feel like you’re enforcing the law while also rolling dice like a caffeinated magistrate.

### The roster at a glance
- Leaders who can slice through nonsense with a well-timed speech check and a bolt pistol.
- Gangers who carry a mix of weapons, from straightforward autoguns to specialized tools that can pin, stun, or humiliate a foe.
- Specialists who bring a bit of law-and-order flair to the table: medics, scouts, and technicians who can keep the squad stable during a skirmish.
- A toolkit of gear options—grapnel shots, stoppers, and utility items that make the Justicars a versatile force on the necromundan streets.

This is not a gang built for flamboyant personal brandings of chaos; it’s a well-provisioned squad with a mission: bring the hem of the law to the underhive, but keep it stylish. And yes, you’ll be tempted to paint them in the classic blue, but the best Palanites—like good bureaucracy—look good in gray as well.

### Why the Palanite aesthetic sells
There’s a reason the Palanites look different on the tabletop: their sculpting emphasizes practical gear, protective vests, and the look of a squad that’s seen paperwork turn into last-minute decisions in a firefight. The models often feature clean lines, reinforced armor plates, and a sense of “we only touch you with a badge if you’re within the law.” It’s a strong visual that says: we are here to maintain order, even if the hive refuses to play ball.


## The Miniatures: Design, Conversion, Painting
If you’re into painting, Palanites are a dream or a nightmare depending on your tolerance for crisp lines and small accessories. The miniatures typically come with a variety of heads and torsos so you can customize your squad to reflect different precincts, precincts that might have a vestige of tone-deaf humor about “the paperwork on page 42.” Here are the big points:

### Design, scale, and silhouette
- Clean, blocky armor that reads well from table distance.
- Distinctive helmets and faceplates, giving each model a slightly different personality while keeping the squad cohesive.
- Some models include capes or cape-like elements, which are always fun to paint if you’re into dramatic flair (though cape maintenance is another hobby).

### Painting tips for the Civics Club look
- Start with a base of city-gray or ash-blue to evoke urban enforcement gear.
- Shade with a cool gray wash to bring out the crevices of armor and pouches—this helps the lines stay readable under game lighting.
- Highlight with a lighter gray or steel to make edges sing on the tabletop. If you fancy extra drama, a subtle edge highlight with white can make “engineered regulation” look pop.
- For logos and insignia, use a fine brush and a steady hand. The Palanite insignia is a great place to practice edge highlights and tiny glyphs without needing a magnifying glass.
- Optional: a scuffing layer to simulate wear and tear from hive dust, rain, and bureaucratic red tape. It looks great and doesn’t require you to repaint the entire model if you’re pressed for time.

Visual note: the Palanites photograph well in neutral lighting. If you want to push the dramatic narrative, photograph them with a backdrop that echoes harsh, industrial lighting—think overhead fluorescents and the occasional flicker from a ceiling vent.


## In-Game Mechanics: How They Play in Necromunda
Let’s get to the heart of the matter: how do Palanite Justicars perform on the board? The core strength of this gang is its combination of disciplined close-range firepower and a few control tools to shape engagements. They’re not the glass cannon archetype; they’re the precision rifle squad—accurate, sturdy, and a bit punishing when you mishandle their tempo.

### Gangers and leaders
- The leader typically has access to a mix of reliable ranged options and a few personal upgrades that improve command and control on the table.
- Gangers bring a balance of range and melee threat. They’re designed to hold ground, press objectives, and punish misplayed man-to-man engagement.
- Specialists are where the Palanites earn their keep: a medic for the vital taps of healing, a technician for mapper-level control, and a scout for reconnaissance and first-strike repositioning.

### Gear and loadouts
Palanites shine when you combine gear that gives you both staying power and objective control. A typical setup might be:
- Autoguns for core firepower, with a mix of rapid-fire options to keep enemies honest.
- Special weapons that tilt the balance in urban corridors where cover is king.
- Utility gear like grapnel shots to reach rooftops or topside vantage points, enabling drop-down confusion and flanking maneuvers.

### Rules and playstyle notes
- They often reward careful positioning and synergy with specialists who can keep the squad healthy and functioning during a skirmish.
- The synergy with terrain and tactical objectives is vital; Palanites tend to excel in map-based or objective-based scenarios where movement and control wins the day.
- Don’t overlook the indirect control tools—stun attacks or immobilizing gear can force a turn in the opponent’s plan, giving you that precious moment to swing the momentum.

## Tactics and Synergy in a Necromunda Campaign
If you’re stitching together a campaign, Palanites are excellent at solidifying a front while you gather intel. Here are some practical approaches:

- Positioning: use lines of sight to force the enemy into chokepoints where your Gangers can pin them down with controlled fire. The idea is to reduce the opponent’s options by steering engagement ranges.
- Objective control: Palanites are at their best when you can threaten multiple objectives with a compact formation. You move as a pixel-perfect unit; your opponent must choose between taking a risk and falling behind.
- Resource management: your Medic can prolong the life of your squad when a fight goes sideways. This is where the “bureaucracy meets battlefield” vibe sells itself—turn the medically sound into a strategic advantage.
- Micro-moves: in tight corridors, small repositioning moves can make the difference between a clean win and a messy retreat. Remember, a single reposition can break your opponent’s timing, especially when you combine it with a tactical discipline card or a trap using your Specialist.

## Painting and Model Tips: Getting the Most from Your Palanites
- Focus on clean edge highlighting to emphasize the armor lines. The armor plate edges are where your contrast will sell the rider’s seriousness.
- Use a cool-tone wash (ultra black or blue-grey) to push depth. Palanites look best when the recesses catch a hint of cold light—like a sterile lab, but with more grit.
- For insignia, choose a bold color that stands out against the primary armor. A bright blue or amber accent can be striking on a grey base.
- Base them with urban grit: pebbles, dust, and subtle hints of rust will evoke a hive city vibe. A gloss seal can make metal look fresh, but a matte or satin finish is often more realistic for urban warfare.
- For conversions, small tweaks like swapping heads for a more stern expression or giving a different gun variant can help you differentiate squads and reflect different precinct backgrounds.
