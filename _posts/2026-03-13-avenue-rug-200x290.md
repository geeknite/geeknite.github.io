## Size, Placement, and Room Fit
A 200x290 cm rug = roughly 78.7" x 114.2", or in feet, about 6.5 ft by 9.5 ft. That’s big enough to anchor a living room with a couch and two chairs, or to cover a substantial swath of a studio apartment floor. The challenge is in placement: you don’t want the rug to swallow a space or make the room feel shorter than it is. A general rule of thumb is to place the rug under the front legs of larger furniture or to create a defined conversation area. In an open-plan room, you can use the rug to define the seating zone, while leaving walkways clear to keep the place from feeling like a carpet maze.

Placement tip: measure your furniture first. The last thing you want is a rug that’s perfect in theory but pins you into a corner with a TV stand that always sits on the rug’s edge.

Underlay: a good rug pad is essential. Without it, you’ll have slipped-around vibes, plus you’ll lose some of the cushion. A felt or rubber rug pad will do the trick; if your floor is wooden, make sure the pad has enough grip to prevent scuffs.

## Maintenance and Care
Spills happen. This rug is designed to handle spills better than a whiteboard after a hackathon. Clean spills quickly with a blotting motion—don’t rub, or you’ll push color around like a bad UI. For tougher stains, a mild detergent with water and a soft cloth usually handles most things (coffee, wine, the occasional cranberry sauce). For pet accidents, neutral pH cleaners are best; avoid bleach or high-alkaline products.

Vacuuming is straightforward. Use a low- to medium-suction setting and avoid the beater bar on delicate fibers. Go in multiple directions to lift the pile and prevent a shiny wear pattern. If you have the occasional shedding issue, a gentle lint roller or a vacuum with a carpet attachment helps.

Seasonal storage tip: roll the rug rather than folding, to minimize creases. If you’re storing long-term, wrap in breathable fabric to avoid moisture buildup and possible mold in high humidity climates.

A note on odor: new rugs can have that new carpet smell. It’s not a hazard, but you might want to ventilate the room for a day or two.

### Maintenance tips and common issues
- Protect edges with a heavy furniture pad to deter curling.
- Rotate every few weeks to ensure even wear if you have a high-traffic area.
- Address spills promptly and blot—don’t rub.
- Check the rug pad compatibility with your floor type.
