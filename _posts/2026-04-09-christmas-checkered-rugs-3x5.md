## Maintenance Tips: Keeping the Pattern Sharp Between Washes

- Vacuum regularly to remove loose dirt and prevent grit from grinding into the fibers. A gentle pass is usually enough; you do not want to prematurely flatten the pile.
- Spot-clean spills promptly with a mild detergent and cold water. Blot, don’t rub, to prevent color migration.
- When machine washing, use a gentle cycle with cold water. Avoid hot water that could cause shrinking or color bleed.
- Air-dry flat or hang to dry. If you use a dryer, choose a low heat setting and remove promptly once dry to minimize static and maintain the backing’s grip.
- Keep the backing free of lint. A quick shake or vacuum beneath the rug can help maintain grip and extend the backing’s life.
