## Introduction
When I say Fortune Row, you might picture a neon-soaked street in a city where myth and asphalt rub elbows with coffee-stained crime files. If that image doesn’t quite match the usual RPG cover aesthetic you’re used to, good news: Fortune Row is here to speed-run your brain into a noir-wrapped mythic heart. This core book for City of Mist leans into the glittering danger of a district where fortunes are made, lost, and occasionally unmade by people who may or may not be legends in disguise. It’s less a supplement and more a portable stage set where the city’s sexiest secrets dare your players to spill them—one dramatic reveal at a time.

But enough poetry. Fortune Row is a tool, a sandbox, and a love letter to pitch-black alleys where a single tag can tilt the entire scene. Think myth + mystery + mobsters + magic, all rolled into a thick, glossy volume you’ll want to lick like a candy bar before you realize you just touched a plastic wrapper. In this review we’ll explore what makes Fortune Row tick, what it brings to your City of Mist table, and whether this book deserves a place on your bookshelf or your campaign schedule.

## What Fortune Row Is and Why It Works
Fortune Row isn’t just a location on a map; it’s a living organism in the City of Mist universe. It’s the kind of district that makes you lean forward in your chair: a place where fortune tellers hawk fortunes with a wink, a crook pays in favors, and a hero’s past keeps showing up wearing a hat with two too many feathers. The book invites you to play within a single, consequential zone rather than a broad metropolis, and that tight focus pays off in heightened mood and consistent tone.

- Atmosphere first, mechanics second: Fortune Row uses a compact set of rules that supports cinematic play rather than heavy crunch. The book leans on the core City of Mist framework—a blend of investigative storytelling, mythic tags, and narrative-driven outcomes—to deliver noir vibes with mythic edge.
- Playbook diversity: Expect a range of archetypes that fit Fortune Row’s vibe. You’ll see investigators, street-level mystics, crime-lighting avengers, and a handful of eccentric NPCs who feel like they’ve wandered out of a Lecției of neon and shadow. The playbooks feel tailored to a district that lives and breathes its own myths, which makes it easy to slot into a larger City of Mist campaign or run as a self-contained arc.
- World-building tools: This book doesn’t just drop you into Fortune Row; it hands you the tools to sculpt it. It includes location templates, faction dynamics, and rumor mills that can fuel sessions for weeks. The GM will appreciate the ready-to-use hooks, while players will enjoy the variety of threads to tug on when they need a new scene.

If you’re used to City of Mist core books that swing big (big myths, big sets, big city), Fortune Row gives you a high-gloss microcosm that still feels like it belongs to the same living, breathing city. It’s a great example of how you can distill a sprawling setting into a single, punchy zone and still have enough material to sustain campaigns for a long time.

## What’s in the Book: Inside Fortune Row
Let’s break down the main sections you’ll encounter and how each one serves both GM and player. TL;DR: this is a toolbox with prebuilt masks and a sandbox with enough room to improvise without losing the mood.

### Overview and Tone
Fortune Row sets the tone with a few pages of art-direction notes, mood boards, and a “city blocks and backroom deals” vibe. You’ll read about neon-lit alleys, the smell of rain on asphalt, and the kind of people who can sell you a miracle as long as you’re willing to pay in something you’ll regret later. The tone is indebted to classic noir but refuses to sacrifrice whimsy—mythic beings in flannel shirts and trench coats feel right at home here.

### Location Catalogs
The district becomes a character by itself through a catalog of places: a gambling parlor that doubles as a forge for counterfeit myths, a rooftop temple of a forgotten patron deity, an abandoned theatre where stage magic isn’t just acting, it’s alchemy. Each location comes with description prompts, potential conflicts, and a few “tags” you can assign in your scenes. The result is a toolkit that makes it easy to string a session together without needing to riff from scratch every time.

### Factions and the Power Dynamics of Fortune Row
There’s more to Fortune Row than slick men in fedoras and girls with eyes like polished marbles. Factions—corporate patrons, underworld syndicates, mystic cabals, and vigilante collectives—line up along the street like a chessboard. Each faction comes with motive, leverage, and a handful of tag ideas to trigger when you want the table to snap into action. The book emphasizes how power flows through the district, and how a single decision can tilt the balance for both the players and the non-player characters.

### Playbooks and Masks
One of City of Mist’s signature ideas is the concept of masks—the mythic identity your character dons to perform their feats. Fortune Row expands on the masking with new or tweaked playbooks that fit the district’s aesthetics. Expect a mixture of classic archetypes (the investigator, the dealer, the mystic) and some more unusual twists that feel like they wandered off a retro-future poster and fell into a noir postcard. The playbooks come with built-in “mythic tags” you can lean on during play, plus suggested story ideas tailored to each mask.

### Rules Lightness and Narrative Levers
The system remains deliberately light in numeric weight, which is a blessing for groups that want to talk more than roll. The core mechanics encourage collaborative storytelling, with players leaning on tags to influence outcomes and the GM providing consequences and reveals that push the noir mood. Fortune Row doesn’t attempt to reinvent the wheel; instead, it refines the wheel’s spokes to spin faster in a single district’s direction.

### GM Tools: Adventure Seeds, Encounters, and Troubleshooting
Every GM loves a capsule of pre-baked hooks. Fortune Row offers a curated set of adventure seeds—each one designed to be dropped into a session with little prep. You’ll also get encounter tables that feel both thematic and useful, a strip of “Troubles” prompts to escalate tension, and advice for ramping up the stakes when the case hits a wall. It’s a thoughtful package for GMs who want to run a campaign in one dense city block without losing the charm of the rest of the world.

### Artwork, Layout, and Accessibility
The art direction leans noir and neon with a modern edge. The typography is legible, the layout clean, and the chapter breaks make it easy to flip to what you need in the moment. The book isn’t shy about its character portraits; you’ll see a lot of silhouettes and close-ups of eyes that seem to know the city’s deepest secrets. It’s not a coffee-table-book aesthetic; it’s a practical GM’s companion that will survive repeated cockpit tests (the sessions that run long and across multiple nights).

## Mechanics: How Fortune Row Feels at the Table
If you’ve spent hours with City of Mist, you know the drill: you use tags to shape the narrative, the GM reveals the consequences, and the group negotiates outcomes in a chorus of noir wit. Fortune Row stays true to that core but focuses it around the district’s atmosphere.

- Tag-casting as a narrative engine: Players call on tags—things like “Street-Wore Resolve,” “Fortune-Teller’s Insight,” or “Gambling Den’s Shadow”—to influence actions. Tag usage feels like a conversation with the city itself. The mechanic rewards clever tagging and character consistency.
- Scene construction: Scenes in Fortune Row are bite-sized and cinematic. You’ll move quickly from investigation to confrontation to a final reveal, all under the glow of neon punctuations. The pacing guide helps keep sessions from dragging (or from sprinting past mood-setting moments).
- Consequences that matter: The core City of Mist approach is to make outcomes resonate. Fortune Row emphasizes lasting consequences for both heroes and the district itself. Your character might earn a reputation in Fortune Row, good or bad, that will ripple through future sessions.
- Syndicate dynamics and personal stakes: The district-level factions aren’t just backdrop; they’re lever points you can pull to raise stakes and deepen character arcs. Fortune Row nudges players toward personal stakes (what your mask costs you) and societal stakes (how your district changes when secrets come out). It’s a nice balance for campaigns that want both personal drama and city-wide impact.

If you’re new to the system, Fortune Row is a fine entry point because it keeps the math unobtrusive while delivering a strong, cinematic vibe. If you’re a veteran, you’ll appreciate the batch of district-specific tools that make set pieces feel more earned and less improvised.

## Thematic Spotlight: Noir, Myth, and the City That Never Sleeps
Fortune Row is less about grand mythic battles and more about the intimate, secretive power that myths hold in the everyday. It’s about what happens when a mythic identity collides with a real person’s messy life. The book leans into a few classic noir motifs—dilemma-laden decisions, unreliable narrators, double-crosses—but it layers them with mythic resonance: a detective who doubles as a pseudonym for a god, a crime boss who channels a forgotten deity’s will, a street preacher who reads destinies in street-light shadows.

This blend works because it doesn’t require players to pretend to be gods in battle armor. It invites them to test the tension between who they are and who they pretend to be for the city’s sake. The result is a game-night vibe that’s equal parts sunlit neon and midnight confession: the sort of mood that makes players lean in closer, whispering over pizza about the secrets they’ve promised never to tell the city’s ghostly patrons.

## GM Tools, Encounters, and How to Use Fortune Row at Your Table
GM-facing sections are often skimmed by players; Fortune Row asks them to do the opposite. Here’s what you get and how to wield it effectively:

- Ready-to-run locations: If you don’t want to build Fortune Row from scratch, you can drop in a couple of pre-built blocks and let the players do the rest. The locations come with tips on NPCs, conflict hooks, and “fortune-tag” prompts that push scenes toward dramatic reveals.
- Faction dossiers: Short, spicy intros to the major players and their goals. They’re especially useful for your long-form campaigns where alliances shift and a single betrayal can realign power in the district.
- Theme and tone anchors: The book provides a mood checklist you can use to calibrate the table—soundtrack suggestions, prop ideas, and quick prompts for players who want to go a little more cinematic in their narration.
- Quick-start session ideas: For groups starting Fortune Row as a first campaign, there are ready-made arcs with escalating stakes, perfect for a four-to-six-week arc. You’ll be bumping into myths you thought you understood, while your players navigate a city block that’s always watching.

If you want to see how Fortune Row can expand a one-shot into a longer arc, look at how the district’s evolving power games echo character-driven plots. The book encourages you to let locations and factions reflect your players’ choices, rather than forcing the players to bend to arbitrary plot rails. It’s a refreshing approach for a system that can feel a little too meta at times.

## Art Direction, Layout, and Accessibility Notes
Fortune Row’s art remains faithful to City of Mist’s signature aesthetic: crisp silhouettes, glow effects, and typography that feels both modern and retro. The layout is clean, with generous margins for GM notes, sidebars for optional rules, and a consistent flow from “Setting” to “Play” to “GM Tools.” Accessibility isn’t ignored; the book includes a clear glossary and a few sidebar explainers that help new players get oriented without wading through pages of jargon.

If you value art direction as part of the gaming experience, Fortune Row earns extra brownie points. The district’s mood is reinforced through visual motifs—neon signs in different languages, rain-slick pavement reflecting city lights, and character portraits that convey a lot with a single glance. It’s the kind of production polish that makes you want to flip through the pages even when you’re not planning a session.

## Play Tip: A Sample Scene to Try This Weekend
To give you a concrete sense of how Fortune Row plays, here’s a compact scene outline you can throw into your next session. It requires minimal prep and leans on the book’s tools rather than your improvisational muscle alone.

- Setup: The players’ masks converge on Fortune Row when a rumor surfaces that a missing fortune has begun to reveal the hidden truth of the district’s power brokers.
- Inciting Incident: A street vendor dies in a suspicious blaze of neon light that throws a glow over a critical clue—an engraved coin that resembles a mythic symbol.
- Rising Action: The party must navigate the district’s factions, interrogate a shadowy artifact dealer, and confront the “fortune-teller” who sells glimpses of possible futures.
- Climax: The truth about Fortune Row’s fortunes is revealed—one of the city’s legends used the missing coin to rewrite the district’s fate, but the price is steep for everyone involved.
- Payoff: The party must decide: take control of Fortune Row’s future, or save the district from a fate of perpetual subjugation to the mythic patrons. Either choice reshapes the city and their characters’ relationships to their own masks.

This structure emphasizes mood and choice over brute force, which aligns with Fortune Row’s design philosophy. If you want to see more ready-made arcs, revisit our post on “City of Mist: The Basics” and then dip into Fortune Row for the mood and pacing you crave.

## Final Thoughts and Recommendation
Fortune Row stands out as a focused, well-crafted addition to the City of Mist line. It embraces the core mechanics—tag-based storytelling, mythic masks, and a city that feels alive—and uses them to craft a district that is both tangible and mythically charged. If you’re looking for a campaign high in mood and story potential, Fortune Row delivers with a confident, stylish stride. It’s an ideal purchase for groups who love noir aesthetics wrapped around legendary beings and for GMs who want a ready-made playground that still invites improvisation and surprise.

If your table thrives on atmosphere, character-driven scenes, and the gentle nudge of fate as a GM tool, this book will be your friend for months. If you prefer sprawling city-wide campaigns from the jump or crave heavy crunch, you might want to supplement with other materials or approach Fortune Row as a modular piece rather than the main campaign backbone.

In a city of myths and rain-slick streets, Fortune Row gives you a doorway into stories that feel inevitable and electrifying at the same time. It’s not flawless, but it’s the kind of book that makes you grin during a session and then sulk when the lights come back on and reality demands you go to bed.

If you’re new to the system, give Fortune Row a look as your entrée into noir-myth storytelling. If you’re a veteran, you’ll likely find it a refreshing, high-gloss augmentation that respects your time and your table’s mood.
