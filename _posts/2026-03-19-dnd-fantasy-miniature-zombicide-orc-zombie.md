## Painting and basing: tips to make this mini sing on your table

- Primer and base coats: start with a matte black or gray primer to give the rotten tones something to bite into. Then block in the armor with a desaturated steel color, adding a wash to bring out the ribbing and wear marks.
- Decay palette: use greens and browns for the rot, with bone or ivory highlights to accent exposed bone. A subtle purple wash in the crevices can make the undead look cold and unfeeling, which is exactly what your party wants when the dice say “initiative.”
- Highlight strategy: dry brush bone color on raised edges of armor plates and skulls. Focus on teeth and fangs to punch up that undead menace look without losing the model’s silhouette on a dim map.
- Base theming: add sand, small corpse bits (cleaned and fully sanitized, of course), and tufts of dead grass to show a battlefield littered with the remains of battles long forgotten. If you’re feeling fancy, you can use a clear resin water effect around the base to imply a swampy battleground where this creature might dwell.
- Sealing: a matte seal keeps the moaning anatomy intact under your table’s pizza heat and the occasional spilled coffee. If you’re feeling heroic, a gloss wash on the jawline can mimic a gore‑slick sheen—just be mindful that too much gloss can end up looking like a sequin from a 90s con‑cosplay hobbyist’s kit.
