### Authenticity Tips
If you’re considering resales or just want peace of mind before you drop serious cash, here are practical authenticity checks:
- Compare the signature placement against the official product photos. While some variation is natural, a wildly inconsistent autograph location can be a red flag.
- Check the back of the mat for a stamping or a card-stock certificate that notes the edition number and artist credits. Some KS editions include a small card; others rely on the printed metadata.
- Inspect the edge finish. A clean, uniform edge—without chipping or warping—usually signals a well-maintained product.
- If possible, verify the mat’s dimensions against published specs. A hyphenated decimal off by a few millimeters is nothing to panic over, but a large mismatch warrants closer scrutiny.

## Authenticity, Care, and Longevity
### Caring for Your Limited Run Playmat
To maximize longevity, store the mat in a cool, dry place away from direct sun. Use a soft cloth for gentle cleaning, avoiding harsh chemicals that could fade the ink or degrade the surface. If you plan to display the mat with other items (frames or acrylics), ensure there’s enough airflow to prevent moisture buildup. When rolling and storing, use the original protective sleeve or a similar tube to avoid creases. If you’re on the move, a dedicated playmat sleeve or a padded case helps preserve both the artwork and the edge integrity.

### Handling Signature Preservation
Since the artist’s signature is a feature, avoid repeated rubbing over the signature area. If accidental smudging occurs, a light, non-abrasive cleaning approach with a microfiber cloth is usually enough. Avoid water contact around the signature zone, and if you’re using card sleeves that are glossy, ensure they don’t catch on the ink and pull the signature. In general, treat the signature area with the same care you’d give a signed card—delicate, but not unavailable.
