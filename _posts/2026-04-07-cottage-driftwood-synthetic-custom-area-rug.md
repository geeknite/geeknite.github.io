## Cleaning and maintenance: keeping driftwood looking tranquil
Maintenance is the boring but essential part of rug ownership. The Cottage Driftwood rug is designed to be low-maintenance, which is exactly what we geeks want from a living space artifact:

- Regular vacuuming: A weekly pass with an upright or handheld vacuum helps keep dirt from marring the surface and matting the fibers.
- Spot cleaning: For spills, blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent solution. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade fibers or alter colors.
- Deep cleaning: If you go for professional cleaning, ensure they follow synthetic-fiber guidelines to avoid damage or color bleed. A good rule of thumb is to treat it like a sturdy, stain-resistant sweatshirt: clean when needed, not every week, and you’ll extend its life.
- Pet care: If you’ve got claws, trim nails regularly and consider a protective backing to reduce snagging. For drool and minor accidents, quick blotting usually does the trick.

What to avoid: bleach, high heat, and rough scrubbing. This rug is not a science experiment; it’s a practical home accessory. Respect the fibers, and they will respect your budget.
