## Compatibility with your gear
This shelf is designed for standard 19 inch racks, the universal language of data centers and home labs alike. If you are using heavy switches or servers with deep mounting feet, double-check the depth; some devices have protruding feet or cable management arms that can extend beyond 10 inches. Consider the cabling clearance behind the shelf as well; you will want at least a few inches of free space to bend cables without bending the shelf itself. Also, consider airflow: if your rack hosts hot devices on top, the vented shelf below can help draw air from the front, across the devices, and up toward the exhaust fans.

## How to install
- Measure your rack height and confirm your device height is 1U.
- Place the shelf into the rack and align the holes with your cage nuts.
- Insert M6 screws and tighten; avoid over-tightening, which can warp the shelf or strip the holes.
- Install your devices with front feet supported by the shelf; avoid hanging weight from the shelf edges.
- Cable management: plan your cables to avoid blocking the vent holes; use zip ties or velcro to compress loose cabling.
- Test fit before installing heavy gear; a two-shelf test run helps you visualize spacing.
