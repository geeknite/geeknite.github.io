## Maintenance and Cleaning: DIY-Friendly, Not Medieval

Caring for this rug is within reach of the average homeowner. Here’s a practical routine:

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting. Avoid a beater bar or brush, which can pull fibers and flatten the pile over time.
- Address spills promptly by blotting with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper into the fibers.
- For spot cleaning, use a wool-safe cleaner following the manufacturer’s instructions. Test in an inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially if you have a sunny window that bleaches edges more than the center.
- Use rug pads or non-slip underlay to prevent shifting and reduce wear in high-traffic zones.
- Annual professional cleaning can refresh the pile and remove deeply embedded grime, helping the color pop again.

If you’re dealing with stubborn odors or heavy staining, a professional cleaner with experience in wool blends is your best bet. They can tailor the cleaning method to preserve color when necessary and avoid harsh solvents that may compromise the texture.
