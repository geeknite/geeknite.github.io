## Practical Buying Considerations
- Space requirements: A ten‑seat configuration demands a substantial footprint. Measure your room height, width, and traffic pathways before purchasing. Ensure there is enough clearance at entry doors and around corners for chairs and people to move without collision.
- Screen configuration: Decide whether you want a single central display wall or distributed screens. Consider the distance to the farthest seat and the types of content your team will display most often.
- Power and data needs: Confirm availability of robust power supply and data outlets. A dedicated circuit or surge protection may be prudent if you plan to run multiple displays and docking stations.
- Maintenance plan: Look for a warranty that covers the tabletop finish, frame integrity, and screen components. For a large investment, a service plan that includes on‑site maintenance can save headaches later.
- Aesthetics and interior style: The Oak Black finish pairs well with modern, industrial, and mid‑century spaces. If your office decor leans toward bright colors or minimalism, this desk will either anchor the room or clash depending on execution. It’s best to view a sample or mockup in person if possible.
