## Overview

Dhurries are flat-woven, low-pile rugs with a history that would make archaeologists jealous. They’re typically produced in parts of India and Pakistan and are prized for their durability, crisp patterns, and the ability to withstand the chaos of daily life (pets, kids, glitter glue experiment lovers, you name it). ### Care and Maintenance

Cotton Dhurries are relatively forgiving, but nothing is maintenance-free. Here’s how to keep this camel-brown beauty looking crisp:
- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting. A hand-held nozzle helps avoid snagging threads. Avoid beater bars that could loosen the weave.
- Spot-clean spills promptly with a mild detergent and cool water. Blot—don’t rub—to prevent color migration. For stubborn stains, test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and fading. This is especially helpful in rooms with sun exposure that alternates by season.
- Keep away from direct, prolonged sun when possible. If the rug fades, you’ll still have the mid-century chic named “patina” rather than “stain.”
- Professional cleaning is recommended every couple of years, particularly if the rug is in high-traffic zones or households with kids and pets.

A note on dirt and pets: cotton is pretty forgiving, but it’s not a miracle fabric. A well-timed vacuum, a quick wipe after spills, and a good deterrent for muddy paws will keep the geometric lines sharp. If you have shedding pets, consider a rug pad to minimize sliding and to reduce the amount of fur that hides between the fibers.
