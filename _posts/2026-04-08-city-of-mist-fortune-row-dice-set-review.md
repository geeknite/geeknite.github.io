## What is the Fortune Row Dice Set for City of Mist?

Fortune Row is one of those brands that understands that dice aren’t just tools to generate numbers; they’re tactile mood lighting for your tabletop mood boards. The Fortune Row Dice Set for City of Mist is designed to slot into the game’s vibe: crime-noir meets mythic prophecy, a city that blurs the line between the mundane and the legendary. The set typically arrives with a curated assortment of dice that fit the game’s dice-leaning mechanics and the shared narrative language players use when they’re riffing on a big arc while sitting at a coffee-stained table.

In this review we’ll treat the Fortune Row set as a complete package: what’s in the box, how it feels in your hand, how it looks on the table, and how well it actually helps or hinders your storytelling. We’ll also compare it to the broader ecosystem of City of Mist accessories—because in a game this flavor-forward, the right tchotchkes can either elevate a scene or derail it with a glittering distraction.

### Contents and packaging

First impressions matter. Fortune Row tends to ship in packaging that leans into the noir aesthetic: a sturdy box with debossed logos, a magnetic flap (if you’re lucky), and a small art insert that hints at the role you’re about to play in the city’s sprawling myth-and-hood. Inside, you’ll usually find a standard dice set (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20, and a percentile or two) and sometimes a small velvet pouch for carry. Some editions throw in a quick, one-page guide on assigning dice for different facets of the Mist—Power, Legend, and Logos—depending on how you want to narrate your character’s arc that night.

The design language leans into smoke-gray resin with flecks of metallic gold or silver that catch the light as you roll. It’s not just dice; it’s a subtle costume accessory for your character. If your table aesthetic is “secret city that eats streetlights,” you’ll appreciate the way Fortune Row makes your dice feel like a lucky talisman rather than a pedantic prop.

### The design and aesthetics

Look and feel matter in this space more than you’d think. The Fortune Row set exudes an Art Deco meets alleyway vibe: crisp edges, clean pips, and a sobriety of color that matches City of Mist’s tone. The dice themselves are comfortable in the grip, with a satisfying click when you land a roll. The pips are crisp, not shallow; you’ll see your numbers without having to squint. If you like your dice to double as conversation starters, the etched motifs on certain faces—tiny cryptic runes or city-scene silhouettes—give players something to comment on while you’re negotiating outcomes from the shadows.

The d6 and d8 are particularly lovely, with slightly bevelled edges that give a tactile feedback that’s easy to read when your dice are rolling in a dim tavern. The D20 is balanced and clack-free enough that it doesn’t dominate the table’s soundscape, which matters if you’re playing a tense scene where whispers matter more than thunderclaps.

### Build quality and durability

Is the set built for a long life or a few dramatic nights and then a quiet retirement? The Fortune Row dice are a step above budget plastic but not a luxury abyss. They’re robust enough to survive a couple of accidental tumbles, a few coffee spills, and the occasional dramatic “I kid you not, the crucial roll happened under a napkin.” The resin is solid, without obvious air bubbles or defects, and the color saturation remains pretty steady across the set. None of the faces feel mis-mated or mis-angled, which is the kind of thing you notice only when you’ve had to re-roll a critical failure because a die landed in a gap between the table top and a coaster.

If you’re a gamer who keeps your dice in a pouch, the Fortune Row set isn’t so heavy it becomes a wrist workout; similarly, if you like a bit of stiffness so your dice don’t rattle loose in transit, you’ll appreciate the overall snugness.

## In play: how it actually fits City of Mist

City of Mist uses a narrative-driven system that invites players to narrate outcomes and lean into the city’s mythic texture. Dice are the tool that helps translate impossible choices into something the table can see, feel, and react to. How does the Fortune Row Dice Set help or hinder that? Let’s break it down.

### The dice feel and speed

In a game that rewards quick actions and cinematic reveals, you don’t want your dice to feel fussy. The Fortune Row set reads cleanly at a glance; the pips are large enough to confirm a result without stopping to squint. The weight is satisfying but not exhausting; you can flick them across the table with a confident arc or drop them into a hand and roll with a flourish. The tactile feedback matters when you’re describing “the Moment” where a single die result shifts a scene’s tempo. A clacky, heavy die can speed up a tense moment just as a light, breezy roll can lighten a heavy scene. Fortune Row makes a balanced choice somewhere in the middle: not so heavy that it steals focus, not so light that you question the durability after a few rollercoaster sessions.

### Color coding and narrative alignment

A lot of City of Mist players use color-coded dice to map to different aspects of a character. Fortune Row’s palette helps you create a quick mental map of the scene’s tension. If you and your group don’t assign dice to specific narrative beats, you can still leverage the set’s aesthetics to guide the storytelling: a darker gray die for noir tension, gold-speckled dice for mythic sparks during a scene, and a clear-dominant die for moments where truth cuts through the fog. The kit’s look encourages a ritual around the roll—a little moment of focus before the outcome lands—and in City of Mist, that ritual is part of the game’s vibe.

### Handling failed rolls and consequences

City of Mist thrives on consequence and dramatic narrative turns. The Fortune Row dice don’t change your probabilities; they augment the storytelling. If your party is fighting a Midwestern thunderstorm of secrets or chasing a hint through a neon underpass, the dice help you convey uncertainty in a concrete, visible way. When you roll a spectacular miss or a near-miss, the dice’s presentation—weight, color, and the visual of the result—helps players and Narrators lean into the scene’s mood rather than staring at a tiny chart on a mismatched card.

### Compatibility with the game’s engines

City of Mist is a game that can be run with a wide array of house rules and varying GM preferences. Fortune Row sits well within that spectrum. It doesn’t enforce a particular mechanical gimmick; it supplies a physical tool that can be integrated with any set of house rules you’re using. If your table uses a dice pool or a pair of three-die mechanisms to resolve actions, the Fortune Row dice can slot into those systems with little to no friction. If you’re a purist who sticks to a standard d6/d20 ecosystem, the set still reads clearly enough to serve as thematic proxies for “Mist-lore dice” or “Power dice” in your narrative, which is a nice value-add.

## Value, pricing, and why you might buy or skip

Let’s talk money. Dice sets aren’t exactly cheap toys, but in a game like City of Mist where mood and aesthetics contribute to the storytelling, there’s value in a set that communicates tone as well as numbers. Fortune Row sits in the mid-to-upper mid-range price bracket. You’re paying for the materials, the finish, and the consistent quality across a full set. If you’re someone who treats your dice like a character prop—one you’ll handle, talk to the city’s secrets with, and carry across sessions—then the durability and the way the set’s visuals reinforce the vibe matter a lot.

For daily or weekly players, the question is whether the purchase improves the table’s speed and clarity. Do you find that you’re always rifling through dice to find the right color or shape? The Fortune Row set’s clear separation between die types reduces search time and reduces the cognitive load of determining which die to roll next. That, in turn, can speed up sessions and keep momentum high, which is particularly valuable in a game like City of Mist where the pace of discovery matters as much as the results themselves.

If you’re someone who values minimalism and simplicity, you might feel the price is steep for what you get. But if you crave a table-scape that screams “this is a city where everything is a myth and nothing is quite what it seems,” the extra cost buys you a lot of atmosphere. And atmosphere is a currency in tabletop RPGs—sometimes more valuable than any rule tweak.

Remember: you don’t buy dice for raw sturdiness alone; you buy them for what they do to your storytelling experience. Fortune Row’s dice deliver a particular flavor that can shift tone in a scene, which is arguably worth the investment if your group leans into the cinematic texture of City of Mist.
