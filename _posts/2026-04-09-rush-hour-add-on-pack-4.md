## New mechanics and how they feel in play
The brilliance of Rush Hour has always been the elegance of a simple constraint — blocks can slide forward or backward along their lanes, but only in a single axis — paired with the thrill of discovering an unexpected route to freedom. Pack 4 leans into this elegance and adds three clear innovations that we found compelling without turning the game into a rocket science lab.

### New car silhouettes and paths
The 5 new silhouettes each bring a subtle deviation in how you block and unblock. Some are longer, some are shorter, and a couple have unique shapes that force you to rethink the approach to a lane clearing. The result is not chaos but refreshing friction that prevents you from sliding into old habits. You will still feel that moment of breakthrough when the exit car slides clear, but the path there is now a bit of a wilder ride. If you enjoyed the minimalist geometry of the base game, you will appreciate how Pack 4 nudges the palette without losing the core geometry.

### Variants that stretch thinking
Pack 4 introduces 3 gameplay variants that can be toggled on or off to customize difficulty. The first variant tightens the number of moves allowed for each solve, turning a clever solve into a tight time trial. The second variant adds a few extra cars to the board, creating more potential choke points and a longer path to the exit. The third variant formally introduces a rule where one car may briefly be teleported to a different lane after a successful slide, creating a mini misdirection puzzle that tests your memory and spatial foresight. These variants are optional but well balanced; they provide a gentle ramp from base puzzle solving toward more structured endurance challenges.

### Time attack mode
Time pressure has a way of sharpening focus that is very Rush Hour. The time attack mode in Pack 4 rewards quick thinking and punishes hesitation. It is not a pack only for speed demons; it rewards planning and shuffle management as much as it does brute speed. If you are new to Rush Hour, skip time attack until you have a few comfortable solves under your belt. If you want the adrenaline of a timed challenge while keeping the game accessible for casual sessions, this variant is a solid addition.

## Tips and strategies for Rush Hour Add-on Pack 4
- Start with the new silhouettes in the quick solve mode to familiarize yourself with the new blocking patterns before diving into the more complex layouts.
- Use the time attack mode as a training tool. Set a mental goal for each solve and try to trim one or two moves from your best time on the card before moving to the next.
- When a puzzle feels stuck, focus on a single lane and map each car movement in that lane before you attempt any shifts in other areas. This helps you avoid backtracking that wastes precious seconds in time attack mode.
- Create a habit of naming the key blocking sequences in your head. It sounds silly, but it makes it easier to recall the path you want to carve out when the grid becomes crowded with cars.
- If you are sharing the game, rotate who takes the lead on the first solve. A fresh perspective helps avoid the trap of replicating the same mistaken approach.
