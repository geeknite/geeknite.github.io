## Maintenance and care: simple routines that preserve the look
### Cushions care and color preservation
Cushions are the heart of comfort in outdoor seating. The fabric used on these cushions is meant to resist staining and resist UV fade. However, no fabric is truly impregnable; sun exposure and rain can degrade the color or compress foams over time. We recommend rotating cushions periodically so that wear is evenly distributed. If you notice flattening in high-use areas, consider flipping cushions or switching seat positions to allow the foam to recover.

Cleaning is straightforward. Remove cushion covers if possible and machine wash on a gentle cycle with mild detergent. Air dry completely before returning covers. If machine washing is not preferred, a simple wipe-down with a mild soap solution and a hose rinse can do wonders. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the fabric over time. If you notice any staining from spilled sauces or coffee, a gentle stain remover suitable for outdoor fabrics can help. The goal is to keep cushions looking fresh rather than letting stubborn stains accumulate and become a memory you wish to wipe away.

### Wicker and frame care
The wicker weave benefits from occasional dusting with a soft brush or cloth. For stubborn dirt, a solution of mild soap and water works best. Rinse with a light spray to avoid forcing water into joints. If you encounter any loose weaving or wobble, the modular nature of the set means you can reposition pieces or adjust anchors to regain stability. In the unlikely event of a frame issue, check for loose screws and retighten as needed. The secret to longevity is a light touch and regular maintenance rather than aggressive wrestling matches with gravity.
