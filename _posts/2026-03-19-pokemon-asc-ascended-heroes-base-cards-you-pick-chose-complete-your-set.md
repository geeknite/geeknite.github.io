## H2: The You Pick mechanic: choose your own destiny

You Pick is the first, most existential decision you will face in Ascended Heroes. It asks one simple question: which BASE card should headline your strategy today? The You Pick mechanic allows players to select from a pool of BASE cards, each with its own micro-synergy, power curve, and flavor text that will make your spice rack feel underappreciated.

This design choice is not arbitrary. The You Pick mechanic introduces meaningful variety into each session. You can, for example, choose a BASE card that ramps energy early on, enabling a rapid mid-game push, or you can choose a fat, scaling base that rewards long-term planning and patience (two virtues many of us struggle to maintain while watching a plant grow). The key is to understand the tempo you want to set for a given match and the card's potential to influence future turns.

In practice, You Pick creates a meta-game before the game actually begins. You are staring at a handful of possibilities, debating whether to lean into aggression or grind for card advantage. It is the warmup act that makes the main show feel like a real sport, not a board-game version of arcade dodgeball with slightly nicer card art.

### H3: Strategies for You Pick
- Early tempo pick: Choose BASE cards that offer cheap early plays with immediate payoff. This helps you establish board presence and keeps your opponent off balance.
- Mid-game pivot: Pick a BASE card that unlocks combo potential in the next two to three turns. The mind games here are delicious: you present an option, and your opponent guesses which path you are likely to take.
- Long-view pick: Some BASE cards scale dramatically if left for a couple of rounds. This can be risky, but the payoff can be spectacular if your opponent misreads your intent.

## H2: The Chose mechanic: committing to a path

Chose is the companion mechanic to You Pick. After you have selected your BASE card through You Pick, Chose forces you to commit to a path. This mechanic is where the strategy thickens. It isn’t enough to pick a card; you must align your subsequent plays to maximize the chosen path’s potential.

The Chose decision affects your draw order, resource generation, and how you interact with your opponent’s board. It creates a neat tension: do you commit to a reliable, steady plan or do you gamble on a flashy, high-risk-high-reward line? The answers vary by deck, by mood, and by your willingness to explain your playstyle at the dinner table after the match.

Balancing You Pick and Chose is the art here. If you are too conservative in You Pick, you might hate your Chose outcomes. If you go for the high-wattage option in You Pick, your Chose commitment better be robust, or you will fade into the background faster than a character in a JRPG who forgot to equip their sword.

## H2: Completing Your Set: the collector’s itch you can’t deny

Completion is where ASC Ascended Heroes earns a second wind. Completing Your Set means you gather a defined set of BASE cards that unlock a crescendo of abilities, abilities that play well with your You Pick and Chose choices and give you momentum as you trundle toward the endgame. It is not just about having the most cards; it is about having the right cards at the right time, arranged in a sequence that makes your opponent question their life choices.

This mechanic can produce a satisfying feedback loop: the more you complete, the more powerful your board becomes, the more your opponents fear you, and the more your post-match bragging becomes a matter of honor. It is not all glory—there is a real puzzle here, a puzzle that rewards memory, pattern recognition, and a penchant for making risky plays when you should be playing it safe.

The thrill of completion is often enhanced by supporting cards that reduce the cost to acquire more BASE cards or that enable better cycles of draw and resource generation. It creates a space where you can build a near-perfect engine if you plan with enough foresight.

A note on value: completing Your Set does not always guarantee victory, but it does guarantee a more interesting game experience—one that feels like a slow-blooming flower rather than a quick sprinkler of petals.
