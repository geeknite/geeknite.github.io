## How to Play: Setup, Turns, and Endgame

### Setup
Setup is designed to be a breath of fresh air rather than a logistics nightmare. Shuffle the kitten-zombie deck, deal a starting hand to each player, place some brains on the table as a shared resource, and arrange tokens in accessible piles. If you can assemble a coffee table and a cat at the same time, you’ve basically got this.

### Turn Structure
A typical turn features quick decisions: draw a card, decide whether to play a card from your hand, and possibly trigger one of the game’s goofy, chaotic effects. These effects often involve forced ya-whoop moments where players may have to sacrifice kittens, swap tokens, or unleash a mini-zombie horde that mostly exists to remind you that you should have bought more snacks. The turns are intentionally light and breezy, making them ideal for groups with mixed experience levels. The emphasis on luck over deep strategy keeps it accessible and, sometimes, painfully relatable to real-life game-night chaos.

### Endgame and Scoring
The endgame logic is straightforward: as you accumulate certain sets of kittens, brains, and token combos, you edge toward a victory condition. Scoring is concise, and the winner is determined quickly, which keeps the table balanced and gives latecomers a fair chance to break the glass ceiling of dominance if luck smiles on them. “Close but no brain” is a mantra you’ll hear a lot in the last few rounds, which is exactly the flavor of humor Geeknite loves.
