## Tokens, Pig Tokens, and the Pig Plus Variant: How They Fit In
Tokens in Herd Mentality serve as the memory of who just said what and who owes the last round of snacks. They can be used for tracking points, rounds, or any house rule you adore. Replacement token sheets or individual tokens give you two main benefits: longevity and creative customization.

- Step up the look: Replacements that feature higher contrast colors help players quickly locate their tokens on a busy table. This is especially helpful during the heat of a heated debate about whether a potato is a vegetable or a concept.
- Material quality matters: Wooden, acrylic, or thick cardboard tokens each come with a different tactile experience. If you often spill drinks or knock tokens over, a heavier material with a good static resistance will be your friend.
- Pig tokens and Pig Plus: The pig is the friendly mascot of the herd, and the Pig Plus variant adds a slightly larger piece or a different finish to differentiate rounds or player statuses. If you want to lean into the theme, swapping in Pig Plus tokens can be a fun, visually cohesive upgrade.

When you choose replacement tokens, consider how they will age with your table. A glossy surface can be gorgeous but might glare under stage lighting; a matte finish reduces glare but can collect fingerprints. The sweet spot is a finish that remains legible after a dozen sessions and still looks new enough to post on social media with a wink.

### Practical tips for token handling
- Store tokens in a small box or pouch so they don’t scatter when you bump the table during a heroic explanation of why the herd selected a non-sensical option.
- Label colors if you have a multi-color set; a tiny color legend helps new players jump in without a five-minute lore lesson.
- Use magnetic storage for metal or magnetic-backed tokens to keep them anchored during loud bouts of agreement and accidental table flips.

## Materials, Quality, and What to Expect
The 2020 You Pick Cards and their replacement pieces typically come in a few common packaging styles: blister packs of cards, token sheets in resealable sleeves, and sometimes a small plastic tray to keep everything organized. In the replacement world, you want pieces that perform well under casual neglect. Here are the top reliability signals to watch for:

- Card stock: A durable, matte-finish stock is easier to read under overhead lights and doesn’t reflect glare when you are trying to read prompts in a dimly lit living room.
- Ink quality: Crisp typography and well-contrasted color schemes prevent misreads during rapid-fire rounds. It is amazing how a single letter can turn a confident answer into a garbled giggle spree if the ink is muddy.
- Token rigidity: Tokens should not warp easily. If they feel flimsy or bend at a gentle press, you are going to lose them to the sofa’s underworld faster than a misplaced keychain.
- Fit with sleeves: If you use card sleeves, ensure the replacement cards fit as snugly as the originals. Too-tight can crimp the corners; too-loose can lead to shuffled chaos.

Quality often correlates with price, but not always. A mid-range set can outshine a premium option if it has better legibility and sturdier tokens. The goal is a cohesive experience where the new pieces look like they belong with the original art style and humor of Herd Mentality, not a random assortment rescued from a dollar bin.

## Fit, Form, and Functional Humor: Do They Play Nice With Your Setup?
A replacement kit should slot into your game night without turning a simple setup into a scavenger hunt. Here is how to assess fit and function in real-world terms:

- Card dimensions vs sleeve compatibility: If the replacement You Pick Cards are too large for your existing sleeves, you might end up with bent corners or cards that won’t stack reliably. Measure the card width and height (roughly 63 x 88 mm for many standard playing cards) and compare to the replacement’s spec. If you own a fancy, heavy card stock sleeve, you may require a slightly tighter fit.
- Token scale: Tokens should sit comfortably on the board without tipping over when someone leans in to make a dramatic argument about why the herd chose a potato as a definitive solution to a prompt. A good replacement token will fit inside standard punchboard holes or designated spots without friction.
- Visual cohesion: If you care about color and style, the replacement kit should harmonize with the base artwork. A clashing palette can be funny, but it can also be a distraction during a close vote moment where the audience should be focused on the punchline rather than the palette shift.

If you are maximizing humor, you can flip the original economy on its head by choosing tokens in neon or metallic finishes. A bold finish can underline the comedic stakes of each round — but remember, too much gloss can reflect your own giggles into your players' eyes, making it harder to read the prompts quickly.

## DIY and Quick Hacks: Making It Your Own Without Breaking the Herd
Not everyone wants to purchase full replacement packs. If you enjoy DIY, here are some quick hacks to stretch existing pieces and tastefully customize your Herd Mentality set:
- Print-your-own You Pick Cards: Design simple, bold replacement prompts with a program you like and print them on standard card stock. Lamination is optional, but a light lamination can help with spill resistance.
- Homemade tokens: Use colorful disc stickers on wooden or plastic tokens to differentiate rounds or players. If you are careful about weight balance, you can create a satisfying tactile experience without buying new stock.

DIY comes with a couple of caveats: durability and resale value. If you craft replacements by hand, they might not hold up to heavy weekly use, and their resale value on the secondary market may be negligible.
