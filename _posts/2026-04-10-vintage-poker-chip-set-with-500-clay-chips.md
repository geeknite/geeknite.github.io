Clay vs ceramic vs composite: why 500 is not a random number

Clay chips are traditional, heavier, and often more durable than their plastic cousins. The term clay is a bit of a misnomer here; most modern poker chips labeled as clay are actually composition chips made from a clay composite and color layers. You will often see a lot of talk online about the difference between clay and ceramic chips. In practice, the feel matters more than the exact material. A well-made clay or clay-composite chip should feel substantial, with a clean edge and a satisfying clink when you drop it on the table. Ceramic chips may hold a sharper edge and a more precise inlay, but the retro vibe of a genuine clay chip often wins the room.

### Handling and room setup tips

- Use a felt mat or a dedicated poker table: the right surface enhances the chips' color and reduces noise on the table.

- Avoid mixing chips with significantly different weights inside the same set; it can disrupt counting and chip tracking.

## Maintenance, storage, and care tips

- Keep chips in their case when not in use to prevent dust and damage. A microfiber lining can help.
- Avoid exposing chips to direct sunlight for extended periods; long exposure can fade colors.
- Clean using a mild damp cloth; avoid harsh cleansers that can degrade the surface.
- Test denominations by stacking a few chips and ensuring they align cleanly. Misaligned inlays can indicate a cheaper build that will fail under tournament pressure.
