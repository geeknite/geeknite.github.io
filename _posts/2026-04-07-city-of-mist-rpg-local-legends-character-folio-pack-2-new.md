### What counts as a folio pack
A folio pack is not a full campaign supplement. Think of it as a curated set of character folios, quick plot hooks, NPC dialogue prompts, image templates, and a handful of bespoke assets that help you drop new legends into sessions without needing to craft every beat from scratch. Pack 2 in particular leans into neighborhoods you might actually recognize — the corner cafe, the abandoned rail yard, the graffiti tunnel near the bridge — places that feel ordinary until the legends start to surface.

The packaging emphasizes portability and modularity. The design aims to be GM friendly first, player friendly second, and collectable third. You will see a lot of the same consistent art direction and layout from Pack 1, which is a relief if you kept Pack 1 on your shelf. If you enjoyed the visual language of Pack 1, Pack 2 will feel like a natural evolution rather than a radical rebrand.

If you are new to City of Mist, the folio packs are not a complete game in themselves. They augment a campaign you already run or want to start with a clean slate using the City of Mist core book as your backbone. The Local Legends line is particularly good for injecting a sense of place and recurring NPCs that feel like they could have lived in your city for decades, even if you just set them down yesterday.

## How to run Pack 2 at the table
This is where the practical value really shows up. Pack 2 is designed to reduce prep time while maximizing mood and player agency. Here are some concrete ways to run it effectively:

- Start with a focal legend and a single hook that ties to a local hotspot. Use the included prompts to generate the first scene in under ten minutes.
- Use the micro arcs to establish a campaign rhythm. A two session arc can evolve into a longer thread, while a longer arc can be divided into five or six sessions with breaks in between to keep tension from burning out.
- Use the handouts as a tactile prop for your players. A handout with a fragment of a rumor can be a strong motivator, especially if it hints at a larger conspiracy that is not yet fully revealed.

## Playtesting impressions and balance concerns
Pack 2 is designed to feel nimble rather than heavy on rules friction. The legends are built so that players can shine through role play and character interaction rather than brute force. The design intent is to reward clever use of the city as a resource — social leverage, rumors, and environmental storytelling are your best tools. In practice, this means some legends can become over or under balanced depending on your party composition. If your table skews toward social manipulation, Pack 2 feels nearly optimal; if you lean toward direct conflict, you may want to prune a couple of prompts to maintain pace.

In a test run with a five player table, the pack delivered a satisfying spread of stories across two sessions. A couple of legends sparked great role play moments, with the GM leveraging the available prompts to nudge players toward uncovering the larger city mythology behind the local legends. There were moments when a legend felt a touch too reliant on a single trope, but the included variation prompts helped to pivot quickly and keep the narrative fresh.
