## Assembly, Maintenance, and Troubleshooting: A Simple Manual, A Complex Sense of Humor
### Routine Maintenance Checklist
- Inspect the enclosure net and zipper for tears after heavy use. Small rips can turn into dramatic escape routes if ignored.
- Check bolts and frame connections monthly. A little tightening goes a long way toward keeping the bounce predictable.
- Clean the jumping surface and padding to remove grass clippings and pollen. A tidy surface equals fewer slips and a longer life for the pad.
- If you notice squeaks, identify the source. It’s usually the springs when dry or the frame where a bolt needs a touch of grease or re-tightening.

### Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
- Over-pumping the springs can push the frame out of alignment and shorten its lifespan. Let the springs do their job, but don’t force them into heroic feats beyond their intended range.
- Rugged jumping in high wind is a bad idea. Wind can tilt the enclosure, causing a less-than-ideal bounce. Always postpone heavy jumping on windy days.
- The zipper should be fully closed when bouncing. A zipper that’s half-open is like opening a parachute during a freefall — dramatic but unwise.
