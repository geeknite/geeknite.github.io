## What is Disney Beat the Parents
Disney Beat the Parents is a head-to-head, kids vs adults trivia game crafted by Spin Master. The core premise is simple: teams answer Disney-related questions faster or more accurately than the other team to advance on the score track. The Disney edition adds a theme filter that rotates around Disney classics, modern Disney, Pixar, Marvel, and a sprinkle of Disney Channel nostalgia. The tension comes from two factors: the pace of the timer and the ever-present risk that the other team will horn in on your answer before the timer runs out.

### Why this edition exists
Beat the Parents already has a strong track record as a party game that benefits from easy-to-learn rules and quick rounds. The Disney twist is designed to widen the appeal beyond the trivia nerds by leaning into beloved IPs that mix high memory with a dash of nostalgia. It’s the kind of game that invites storytelling: the kid will recall the line from the movie that the parent forgot, and the parent will remind the kids of a plot twist from a movie they watched together as a family 10 years ago.

## Setup and game flow
### Setup
- Split into two teams: Kids and Adults. If you’re a multi-generational group, you can create a rotating team dynamic so everyone gets a crack at playing both sides.
- Shuffle the Disney trivia deck. Place the timer within easy reach of both teams.
- Decide if you want a single path to the win or a race to a certain number of correct answers. The rulebook covers both variants, so you can tailor it to your crowd.

### How to play
1) On a team’s turn, one player draws a card and reads the question aloud. The clock starts on the timer.
2) The team has 60 seconds to agree on an answer and state it. If you’re playing in a high-stakes room of competitive bragging, consider a quick buzzer or raised-hand method to prevent argument over who spoke first.
3) If the answer is correct, the team slides their marker forward on the score track. If incorrect, the other team gains a chance to steal by answering correctly.
4) After a correct answer or a steal, play passes to the opposing team. Rounds continue until a team reaches the target score.

The pace is brisk, which is a design feature meant to keep energy high and the prequel sequin-level questions from dragging the night into a land of yawns. The 60-second timer ensures even slower players don’t anchor the ship for an entire evening, but it also creates tasty pressure that makes even the simplest Disney fact feel like a dramatic reveal.

## Strategy tips for max fun and wins
### For kids
- Focus on common Disney knowledge: princess lineages, classic songs, and well-known Disney channel era trivia. Those handshakes with memory usually land you faster than you expect.
- Use a team approach: another kid might know the lyrics you forgot, and that synergy is the real power of kids teams.
- Don’t fear the risk: some questions seem risky but are actually straightforward in hindsight. If you don’t know the answer, a bold guess with good rationale can work surprisingly well.

### For adults
- Lean on the memory map you built across years of family movie nights. Recalling plot arcs and character relationships can be a strong advantage on categories spanning Disney classics and Pixar.
- Don’t underestimate speed. If you can volley a correct answer within the 60-second window, you may steal a turn and swing momentum.
- Value the steals. The opponent’s question might be easy for you but the scoring system could reward a tactical steal more than a clean win on your own turn.

### General tips for a great night
- Create a light scoring system. You can award two points for a clean hit and three for a steal to make steals feel worth the risk.
- Keep a light-hearted atmosphere. If you push for perfection, you risk the vibe going dry. The charm of Disney trivia is in the laughter and goofy misremembered facts, not in flawless accuracy.
- Use visual aids. A large family room or a projector showing posters of Disney movie posters can add to the atmosphere and keep younger players engaged between questions.
