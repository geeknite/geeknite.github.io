## How to play with the MHA twist

UNO is a simple game that has aged well because you can memorize it in a couple of minutes and still lose spectacularly in the last two turns. The MHA crossover adds a few hero-flavored twists without removing the core rules. Here is a practical guide to playing with the Canada exclusive deck without turning your living room into a karaoke bar for chaos.

### Core rules unchanged
- The objective remains to be the first player to get rid of all your cards.
- Color-matched cards and number cards still require you to match either color or number.
- Action cards like Skip, Reverse, Draw Two stay in play, with their standard effects.

### The MHA twists
- Hero cards: When you play a hero card, you trigger a one-time power that lets you skip the next player or force a reshuffle of the discard pile. This is used sparingly to keep games fun rather than hyper-competitive.
- Quirk power cards: A small subset of special cards grant temporary abilities such as drawing extra cards if you are behind in the game, or swapping two cards with another player. These river-like twists create occasional moments of dramatic comeback.
- Thematic art cards: Instead of standard art, some cards carry character art that links to a familiar scene from the show. These cards do not alter play but add to the immersion, which is perfect for fans who want to feel like a hero during a silly, chaotic game night.
