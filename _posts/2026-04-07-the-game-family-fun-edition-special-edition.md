## How to Play (Spoiler-Free): A Friendly Elevator Pitch

If you want to pretend you’re a calm game-night host who exudes confidence while your dog steals the dice, this is your game. The general idea: players cooperate to play cards in ascending order into two piles, all while avoiding mismatches that cause a metaphorical earthquake in your living room. The Special Edition adds a handful of twists that can flip a quiet game night into a carnival of chaos without plunging you into a debt spiral of rulebooks.

### Setup and Turn Structure

- Shuffle the deck and deal out a handful of cards to each player. The exact number depends on the number of players; in our family, it’s always too many cards and too many opinions about how to stack them.
- Place two piles—one for ascending action, one for descending action—on the table. These piles will wobble with the gravity of your decisions, much like the moral weight of choosing what movie to watch.
- On your turn, you draw a card, you consider its potential, you decide whether to place it into one of the piles or discard. The goal is to place as many cards as possible before someone triggers the endgame conditions.

What makes it approachable is that the rules stay gentle but still offer you a moment of strategic satisfaction. You’ll feel like a genius when you manage to squeeze a card in without collapsing the entire system—then immediately fail on your next move and blame the dog for stepping on the rulebook.

### Special Edition Twists: Extra Tools for Chaos

The Special Edition introduces two new twists that can flip a quiet game night into a chaotic carnival:

- The Parade of Tokens: Every time you hit a milestone, you collect a token that can be exchanged for a one-time effect. These effects are delightfully unpredictable and can turn the tide without requiring you to memorize a 17-page card encyclopedia.
- The Family Rulebook: A couple of optional house rules that allow players to reinterpret a card’s intent in a fun, sometimes questionable way. This is where you’ll discover who among your relatives is willing to commit to a family-friendly interpretation of ascending order.

Keep in mind: these twists add replayability, but they also add risk. You’ll laugh as you realize that your sister’s interpretation of ascending means the card you just drew belongs in the pet aisle of the local board game store. The Special Edition invites chaos as a friendly reminder that family game night should be memorable, not a solemn tournament with a spreadsheet.
