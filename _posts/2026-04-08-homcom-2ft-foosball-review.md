### Quick setup tips
- Read the manual first, or at least skim the diagrams. The worst thing is discovering you’ve mounted the rails in the wrong direction and have to take it apart in a dramatic fashion.
- Have a friend help with the leg attachments. Two hands are better than one; one person can hold while the other tightens.

- Lubrication is optional for a tabletop surface, but if you want silky-smooth shots, a tiny dab of silicone-based lubricant on the rods can reduce resistance. Wipe away any excess to avoid attracting dirt.

## Maintenance and care: keeping the vibe alive

### Cleaning routine
- Wipe the playing surface with a soft microfiber cloth after intense games to remove fingerprints and smudges.
- Dust the rails and the rods regularly to prevent grit build-up that could impede ball movement.
- If you’re worried about sticky fingers, consider a light wipe with a gentle cleaner that won’t damage the laminate.

### Longevity and parts
A compact foosball table doesn’t have the same endurance as a full-sized model, but it should last through multiple gaming sessions if cared for. The most fragile components tend to be the rod end caps and the ball-return assembly. If you notice looseness or wobble, tighten screws and replace any bent rods promptly to avoid more serious misalignments.
