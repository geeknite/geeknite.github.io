## Blade Performance and Edge Retention
D2 steel is famous for edge retention, which means fewer trips to the whetstone for maintenance. But keep in mind: D2 can rust if neglected in damp conditions. Regular cleaning, a light oil coating, and a proper storage routine will maximize this blade’s longevity. In practical terms, you’ll be doing light maintenance after use: wipe, dry, oil, and store. That’s the small price you pay for long-lasting performance with a steel composition that resists wear well under typical outdoor tasks.

Edge geometry matters: the Honshu Black’s edge is designed to be sharp out of the box, with enough bite to handle rope, cardboard, plastic, and light wood. If your project requires heavy prying or batoning, expect to re-sharpen sooner rather than later. The number of times you will actually need to re-sharpen depends on how aggressively you slice and how well you maintain the blade under field conditions.

## Maintenance and Care
- Clean after use: wipe the blade with a dry cloth; avoid leaving moisture on the steel to prevent corrosion. 
- Oil the blade lightly after cleaning to maintain rust resistance. 
- Wipe the handle area to keep grip texture effective. 
- Store in the provided sheath away from extreme humidity.

As a general rule, treat D2 like an adult knife: it can take a lot, but it does best when you give it some routine care. If you forget, you’ll notice a duller edge and a slower-than-expected draw. If you remember, you’ll get many months (or years with moderate use) of serviceable performance.
