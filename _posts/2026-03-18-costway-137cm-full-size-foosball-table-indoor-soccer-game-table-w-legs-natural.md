## Maintenance and care: keep it in fighting shape
### Cleaning routines
Wipe the playing surface with a soft microfiber cloth after use to remove fingerprints and smudges. If you spill a beverage, wipe up promptly—foosball tables and sticky residues don’t mix well, especially when you’re mid-tournament and your focus is on pure athletic artistry rather than sticky drama.

### Lubrication and wear
Rods don’t require frequent lubrication, but if you notice a bit of roughness or a squeak, a light dab of silicone grease on the rod joints can restore smoothness. Avoid over-lubrication; you’re not maintaining race car parts, you’re maintaining a table that’s going to be involved in hours of fun and occasional chaos.

### Long-term storage and protection
If you’re storing it away for an off-season, consider covering it to protect from dust. The natural finish over time will benefit from occasional conditioning that preserves grain and minimizes surface wear. The key here is consistent care rather than heroic one-off attempts to restore a lifetime of memories with a single product.
