## Micro Strategy: Quick Tips for Pocket Play
- Prioritize early fragments of phase sets that are likely to appear as you draw. Small decks mean the likelihood of chain plays is higher if you know what to look for.
- Consider discarding cards that are likely to be blockers or set oppositions for your opponents. In a pocket game, you can disrupt others without sacrificing your own rhythm.
- Use the compact tray to quickly organize your sets and runs. A tidy hand tends to translate into faster decisions and fewer misfires.
- Pay attention to the phase progression. With three decks in play, you can coordinate a multi-front assault or create a shared target for your group to chase.
- Practice quick shuffles. The mini cards can bend if you handle them like a rigid pro, so a light shuffle trick helps you keep the game moving without the dreaded card meltdown.
