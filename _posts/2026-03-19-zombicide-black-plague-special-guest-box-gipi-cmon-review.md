## Painting and hobby potential
If painting miniatures is part of your hobby joy, then you’ll love the GIPI figure as a canvas. The armor and cape offer different surfaces that can yield varied results with metallics, leathers, and cloth washes. A few tips for getting the most from this figure:
- Start with a black primer to maximize shadow depth in the armor’s crevices.
- Use contrast paints for quick base coloring on the cloak, then layer thin highlights to make the edges pop.
- A medium wash over the armor can bring out rivets and plates; a brighter edge highlight will give you the edge on the blade and weapon details.
- Consider dry brushing on the base textures to heighten the rocky terrain around the feet, which helps the miniature feel grounded on a medieval battlefield.

Even if painting isn’t your thing, you can use the new mini as a centerpiece on your display shelf. It’s a reminder of the night you decided to brave the abomination-filled night with style, swagger, and a cape that probably costs more than your entry fee to the game night.
