## Care and maintenance
Caring for a hand woven floral rug with linen backing is not a big mystery, but it does require a little respect for the craft. The first rule of rug care is to vacuum regularly, preferably with a gentle setting that lifts dust without pulling on the pile. Vacuuming helps maintain the rug’s texture and prevents soil from embedding, which makes later cleaning a lot less dramatic.

Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing drives stains deeper into the fibers. For most minor stains, a mixture of mild detergent and water works well; test on a hidden area first to ensure colorfastness. Avoid harsh chemicals or bleach that could weaken fibers or alter the dye tones. If you encounter a tough stain on the floral pattern, consider enlisting a professional rug cleaner who specializes in hand woven pieces. The goal is to preserve the weave integrity and color nuance rather than turning the rug into a still life of chemical reactions.

The linen backing adds moisture sensitivity considerations. While linen is relatively resilient, you want to avoid soaking the backing for extended periods. If a deep cleaning is necessary, consider spot cleaning and air drying rather than machine soaking. A thorough dry cleaning or professional rug cleaning is recommended for substantial soiling or when encountering stubborn smells.

Rotation is another helpful habit. If you have strong sun exposure in parts of the room, rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even fading. This practice helps maintain color uniformity across the entire surface and prevents one side from looking like it survived a solar apocalypse while the other side remains perfectly pristine.
