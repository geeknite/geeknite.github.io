## Overview
City of Mist has always been a game about legends in rain-slick streets, a neon-drenched playground where myths wear trench coats and players pretend to be human and heroic at the same time. Fortune Row, the scenario book in question, steps into that same alley and shouts, Look here, a whole row of fortunes waiting to be gambled away or saved with equal enthusiasm. This is not a single adventure but a curated set of connected scenarios that spin out of Fortune Row, a district within the City of Mist universe that feels like a cross between a crooked casino, a mythic fairground, and a late-night noodle shop run by a nosy oracle named Mirtha who never forgets a face.

Fortune Row aims to give the GM a toolkit that can be dropped into a campaign at many levels. You can run a tight one-shot that introduces the core vibes, or you can weave these into a longer arc where the city spirals around a central heist, a cursed remnant from a long-ago myth, and a decision that could flip a currency of luck into something much louder and more dangerous. The production values lean into the neon noir aesthetic City of Mist is famous for, with crisp layouts, punchy hooks, and a generous number of prompts that help a GM improvise without losing the thread. The tone oscillates between witty and creeping, a balance that suits both mystery lovers and myth nerds who like their legends with a side of street-level grit.

Fortune Row isn’t a sequel so much as a remix of the CoM formula. It gives you a pocket-sized city block where myth and money rub shoulders, and every fortune has a cost that isn’t always measured in dollars. If you’ve run City of Mist before, you’ve already tasted the vibe; Fortune Row pours a larger drink, adds a few more puzzle box components, and invites you to lean into the luck economy with gusto.

## The Fortune Row Experience
Fortune Row functions as a mini-city inside the larger city. It is designed to be rolled into a campaign at varying scales: a tight one-shot that captures the neon-drenched mood in under four hours, or a longer arc stitched together with recurring NPCs, district-specific flavor, and a handful of persistent mysteries you can pick up session after session.

The book leans hard into City of Mist’s core mechanic: mythic identities clashing with ordinary life. The adventures present hooks that can be resolved in multiple ways, encouraging GM improvisation and player agency. The prompts aren’t flavor flourishes so much as a toolbox—enough to spark ideas and enough structure to keep a thread from unraveling. The result is a sandbox that respects the GM’s time and the players’ curiosity, while still delivering the signature neon-noir atmosphere that makes CoM sing.

Fortune Row also nudges players toward themes of luck, fate, and the cost of leaning into one’s destiny. If you like your CoM campaigns to feel like a high-stakes game of poker where every hand hides a myth, Fortune Row feels like coming home to a familiar but refreshed casino floor.

## Why Fortune Row Works for Different Play Styles
- For the one-shot purists: The book provides tight, well-paced arcs with clear hooks, a few twists, and enough atmosphere to keep players invested without demanding a long-term commitment.
- For the campaign crowd: The scenarios slot into a larger arc smoothly. They offer connective tissue through recurring NPCs and location-based threads, so you can weave a longer tapestry without rewriting the wheel each session.
- For the GM who loves flavor: The prompts, mood-setting cues, and lanterns of myth that pop up during play offer vibrant materials to fuel improv without collapsing into chaos. This is a book that respects the GM as a co-creator rather than a mere event planner.

### Narrative Design and Mechanics
#### The Setting and Tone
Fortune Row sits at the intersection of myth and metropolis. The book embraces City of Mist’s signature vibe—mythic power wrapped in human problems—while injecting fresh flavors designed to be aspirational and playable. The scenarios nod to noir cinema, urban fantasy, and carnival magic. You’ll encounter legends who operate under parlor-trick logic: fortunes change not only with decisions but with conversations, rumors, and the way a city scales its own myths.

#### Structure and Flow
Each scenario tends to follow a consistent rhythm: hook, escalation, complication, choice, and consequence. Fortune Row embraces the fractal nature of CoM adventures—moments of micro-mystery that spiral into macro-decisions. The design encourages multiple entry points. If a table wants to explore a mystery from a villain’s perspective, or wants to watch the same thread unfold through an ally’s lens, Fortune Row has cues to support either path. The pacing is deliberately modular, which is ideal for groups that like to switch focus without losing momentum.

#### The Mechanics Paraphrased
Fortune Row adapts and expands on the core City of Mist framework. You’ll see more prompts that invite players to lean into their secret identities and the city’s mythic economy. Expect new flavor cards or prompts that accentuate the luck-based currency of the setting, as well as scenes that hinge on choices about what to reveal, conceal, or gamble with. The book doesn’t reinvent the wheel so much as it adds new gears that fit snugly with the existing mechanism, making it easier for a GM to orchestrate a multi-threaded narrative without becoming a wizard of spreadsheets.

#### Encounters and NPCs
If you enjoy a gallery of memorable NPCs who feel mythic yet human, Fortune Row delivers. The antagonists aren’t mere obstacles; they’re mirrors pressed against the snake-bitten glass of Fortune Row’s windows. Some are dazzlers with charisma who mask crimes in charm, others are tragic figures who believed their own legends for too long. There are mentors who push players to confront the costs of their choices, and there are tricksters who test the boundaries of truth, rumor, and consent in a game that already tilts toward moral ambiguity. The writing gives you enough to improvise with confidence, but not so much that you’re carried away from the players’ agency.

#### Accessibility for New Players
Fortune Row does a commendable job of respecting newcomers while offering enough “weight” for experienced CoM players. The scenarios are introduced with clear stakes and accessible hooks. The GM guidance is practical but not prescriptive, encouraging you to adapt the material to fit your table’s rhythm. Of course, the best sessions still require preparation. If you’re the kind of GM who thrives on a loose outline, Fortune Row will feel like a sandbox the players can sprint through or stroll across depending on their mood.

## Art, Layout, and Production Value
The visual language mirrors City of Mist’s core aesthetic: neon, memory-smeared color, and typography that shouts secrecy in a good way. The art direction channels late-night cityscapes, with illustrations that feel like posters torn from a mythic detective agency. The layout strikes a balance between flavor and practicality. You’ll find clean sections for hooks, NPC stats, scene prompts, and GM tips, interspersed with evocative full-color images that actually aid immersion rather than merely decorate.

Jekyll-style image blocks are used to give you quick visual anchors for each scene. The book also includes handy callouts for potential content warnings and accessibility notes. This last bit is appreciated; it signals a respect for varied playstyles and the real-life considerations of a diverse gamer community. If you are a GM who likes to pepper your table with mood, Fortune Row’s visuals do a lot of the heavy lifting without forcing you into a particular aesthetic.

### How It Fits with City of Mist Core Rules
Fortune Row slots into the CoM ecosystem without feeling like a stand-alone spinoff. It leverages the idea that mythic energies inhabit the ordinary city and that the line between hero and hazard is often a matter of perspective. The scenarios assume a working knowledge of the core rules, but they don’t require a strict adherence to every mechanical nuance. If you are bringing new players to the table, Fortune Row can function as a gateway, offering a contained sandbox where players can discover how their legends influence the world around them.

Compatibility notes: the book is most effective when your group uses a relatively stable Fate economy and a consistent set of mythic identities. If you like to mix and match house rules widely, you may want to read through Fortune Row with an eye toward what will need adaptation to your table. In practice, though, the material is flexible enough that a GM can tailor it to a wide range of play styles without losing the core feel that makes City of Mist distinctive.

## Run-Through: Sample Hooks and How They Play
To give you a taste, here are a few sample hooks that appear across the chapters. These are not meant to be complete adventures but rather entry points that demonstrate tone, pacing, and player engagement.

- The Fortune in the Neon Bank: A legendary safe holds a coin that can flip the outcome of any conflict in the city. But every flip costs a memory, and players must decide which memories they’re willing to surrender to win the next confrontation. This hook invites moral choices and great table talk about what players value more: victory or identity.
- The Lottery of Lovers: A mythic romance broker claims to be able to guide lovers toward their perfect fate, but every match comes with a price. Characters are drawn into a web of relationships that may spark as much chaos as fortune, and the GM gets to test the party's ability to navigate consent, desire, and responsibility under pressure.
- The Carnival of Blind Fortune: A traveling carnival reveals itself as a nexus of luck and risk. The players must decide which fortunes to take and which to refuse, all while a troupe of performers sees through the heroes’ secrets with unsettling clarity. Expect misdirection, props, and a few moments that feel like living in a stage play where the audience is always watching.

### Practical GM Tips
- Treat Fortune Row as a living document in your campaign log. Keep a running list of introduced fortune nodes and how players interacted with each one. It will help you avoid repetition and identify opportunities for future mischief.
- Use the districts as both a stage and a character. Fortune Row has texture; let that texture influence NPC behavior and the players’ decisions. A good GM leans into place as a character rather than a backdrop.
- Keep a balance between clue-gathering and action. The best moments in Fortune Row come when players have to decide whether to chase a lead or press forward based on their own myths. Provide a mix of investigation, social intrigue, and combat staples so the table doesn’t stall at any one pillar for too long.

## The Verdict and Final Recommendations
Fortune Row lands as a strong, mood-forward addition to the City of Mist library. It does not pretend to be a complete campaign in a single volume; rather, it offers a toolkit, a mood, and a set of threads that a GM can weave into a broader tapestry with ease. The scenarios are well-paced, clearly written, and generous with prompts that support improvisation while still guiding less-experienced GMs toward satisfying outcomes. The art and layout reinforce the atmospheric goals without getting in the way of play. The material strikes a nice balance between being evocative and practical, which is a hallmark of quality RPG writing.

If your table loves the idea of mythic power tangled up with ordinary life, Fortune Row will feel like a weekend in a city that never sleeps—but the sleeping is purely mythic, and the waking is where the real risk and reward live. There are a few paths through the book that can feel a little dense for new players, but the book remains accessible with a patient GM who sets a comfortable pace and offers clear anchors. The quality of the writing shines in the way it nudges players toward the moral questions at the heart of a myth-haunted city. It is not just a series of encounters; it is a curated journey through a place where fortunes are earned, traded, and sometimes bought at a heavy price.

If you run City of Mist regularly and want to enrich your Fortune Row sessions with robust hooks, memorable NPCs, and a structure that supports both single-session thrills and longer arcs, this book is a strong candidate for your next purchase. It is not a magic solution to every campaign problem, but it is an excellent toolkit for a GM who wants to push the table into a more mythic, more personal, and more neon-night reality.

## Final Thoughts on Value and Fit
- For GMs who crave texture and flexible pacing: Fortune Row delivers.
- For groups who want a jump-start into a multi-session arc: Fortune Row can be transformative.
- For players who enjoy moral complexity and mythic identity: Fortune Row offers plenty to chew on, plus some satisfying twists.
- For those who want a quick, plug-and-play one-shot: Fortune Row still works, but you may want to cherry-pick the tightest hooks to respect session length.

Overall, Fortune Row is a recommended buy for City of Mist fans who want more of the neon-lit mythic grit that makes the game sing. It respects the system and its players while delivering a batch of vivid, playable moments that can expand a campaign rather than merely filling a shelf with beautiful pages.
