## Durability and maintenance
Wool is inherently durable, and the Cambridge Myrtis benefits from this natural resilience. Regular vacuuming keeps dirt from embedding into the fibers. A good strategy is to vacuum in different directions to lift the fibers and reduce the appearance of wear patterns. If you have a pet with a fondness for couch-based naps, you will appreciate the rug’s ability to handle daily life without looking worn after a year. Use a vacuum with adjustable height and avoid aggressive suction on wool fibers to prevent flattening the pile.

Spill management is straightforward for common household messes. Blot the spill immediately with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing to prevent fiber damage, and use a mild detergent with cold water for spot cleaning. Avoid saturating the rug, as wool can absorb moisture and may take longer to dry, particularly if the backing is dense. For stubborn stains or odors, professional cleaning is recommended rather than home steam cleaning, which can push stains deeper or warp the backing over time.

Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear, especially in high traffic areas. A well-placed rug pad also extends the rug’s life by reducing friction against the floor and absorbing some of the foot traffic. Kept in good shape, this rug can age gracefully and look just as stylish five years on as it does today.

## Care and cleaning quick tips
- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting; avoid high suction on wool fibers.
- Rotate the rug every six to twelve months to promote even wear.
- Spot clean with a mild detergent and cold water; blot, don’t rub.
- Use a quality rug pad to prevent slipping and to cushion the fibers.
- Avoid direct sun for extended periods to minimize color fade.
- For deep cleaning or persistent stains, hire a professional rug cleaning service.
