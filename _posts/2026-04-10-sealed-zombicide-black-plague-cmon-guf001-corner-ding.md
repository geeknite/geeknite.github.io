If your intent is to actually play the game, you’re safe—the contents are intact and the gameplay experience is unaffected.

## Storage, Maintenance, and Long-Term Care
Storing a sealed Zombicide set is relatively straightforward. Keep it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, ideally with a shelf or cabinet that minimizes the risk of accidental knocks. If you want to maximize shelf stability, consider a storage solution that keeps boxes perfectly upright and provides extra padding around the corners to prevent further cosmetic damage. If you ever decide to transport the box, use a protective sleeve and ensure the corner ding is cushioned to avoid magnifying the dent during transit.

Inside the box, there are a lot of smaller bags and cards. When you finally open it, you’ll want a clean workspace so that you don’t misplace tiny components. A ziploc bag for the punch boards, a small labeled container for tokens, and a clean surface for tracking figure placement will help your first playthrough go smoothly. In general, treat the sealed set as both a potential display piece and a future playset. The practical balance is to protect the exterior and track interior contents once you decide to unseal.
