## Care and Maintenance
No rug is maintenance-free, but a wool rug of this kind is generally forgiving. Here’s a pragmatic plan for keeping it looking fresh:

### Regular vacuuming
Use a low-suction setting or a vacuum without a beater bar to avoid pulling on the wool. Vacuum in the direction of the pile to minimize fiber lift. A weekly regimen should suffice in most households.

### Spot treatment
For small spills, blot gently with a clean cloth or paper towel. Avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper and create a longer-lasting stain. If needed, use a mild wool-safe cleaner and test in an inconspicuous corner first. Blot again to remove any residue.

### Deep cleaning
If your rug requires a thorough cleaning, consider hiring a professional who specializes in wool. A gentle, low-moisture cleaning is often the best option for wool carpets, helping maintain their texture and color integrity.

### Care for the color and pile
- Rotate periodically to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic areas.
- Use a rug pad to cut down on shifting and to protect the rug’s underside from friction with the floor.
- If you live in a dry climate, consider occasional light humidification to prevent wool from becoming too brittle.
