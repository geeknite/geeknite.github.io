## How to play this bundle: quick primer
If you are already familiar with the base Cards Against Humanity rules, you will slip into this bundle with ease. Here is a quick primer to get you started without reading a novel about scoring rules:

- Each round, a judge picks a black card or the equivalent prompt from the Nerd Bundle deck.
- The other players select one of their white answer cards to fill in the blank or respond to the prompt in the most ridiculous way possible.
- The judge chooses the funniest or most fitting response, and that player scores a point.
- Rotation of the judge ensures everyone gets a turn to steer the humor ship, preferably with a dramatic flourish that would make a tabletop RPG narrator nod in approval.

Pro tips for maximum chaos and laughs:
- Create a house rule to allow a two-card submission for especially ridiculous prompts, but restrict two-card submissions to a single round per person to keep the chaos manageable.
- Try theme nights. Pick a fandom per round and ask players to only use answer cards that align with that theme. This tends to produce surprising and surprisingly clever combos.
- If the room includes non gamers, keep a handful of simpler prompts for them to enjoy. The bundle shines when everyone participates, but it also shines a little less when you leave non players out of the punchline loop.

## The experience with different group sizes
Party dynamics matter for CAH style games. The Nerd Bundle NEUF scales reasonably well from a tight 4-person table to a rowdy 12-person session at a con hall or a dorm common room. With larger groups, you start to see the social physics of the room take over. In both cases, the bundle succeeds if the players are patient with the inevitable long pauses as someone kinks out a joke and the rest unfold the chorus of groans and cheers.

If you want a safe baseline, aim for a 6 to 8 player count. It gives you enough competing lines to spark a few memorable zingers, without overwhelming the table with too many competing jokes at once. A quick word about younger players or people who are not into the heavy humor: you can adapt the prompts by swapping in lighter categories or by using a timer to keep rounds moving. The point is to keep the energy up while avoiding moments that feel like a nonconsensual roast party. The Nerd Bundle NEUF does not pretend to solve every social dynamic, but it does a pretty solid job of providing a scaffold for fun.
