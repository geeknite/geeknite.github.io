## Authenticity and Verification
NOS NEW sealed Woolworth adds a particular flavor to the authenticity check. Here’s how to verify you’re looking at the real deal rather than a lovingly aged reproduction:
- Box feel: The cardboard should be firm, with crisp edges and a box lid that sits snugly. Fading and wear around the edges are normal, but you shouldn’t see obvious modern printing inconsistencies or mismatched color tones that scream reprint.
- Tape and seal: If the shrink wrap is intact and the original seal remains unbroken, you’re in the sealed territory. If the seal shows modern adhesive patterns or the box is resealed with irregular tape, proceed with caution.
- Card stock: Hold the deck in your hands and feel the texture. A genuine 1980s stock will have a certain stiffness and a faint gloss that modern reissues rarely replicate perfectly. You should be able to see the color wear on the corners after repeated handling, a sign of age rather than cloning.
- Rule sheet typography: The font and layout of the rule sheet are a fingerprint of the era. If the text looks like modern digital typesetting with pixel-perfect margins, you may be encountering a reproduction rather than an original.

## Care, Storage, and Display Tips
A well-preserved UNO deck deserves a respectful micro-climate. Here are some practical tips to keep your 1985 UNO in fighting shape for future nostalgia sessions:
- Environment: Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The sun is not your friend when you want bold colors to remain bold.
- Handling: When handling the cards, wash your hands first to avoid transferring oils to the card faces. This is especially important if you want to preserve the back designs and prevent color transfer.
- Display: If you want to display the box, consider a UV-protected display case. A simple shelf with glass doors can keep dust out while letting your inner collector bask in the glory of a sealed Woolworth lot.
- Cleaning: If you ever need to examine the card edges, do so with a soft microfiber cloth. Avoid harsh cleaners, which can degrade the card stock and dull the hues.
- Rotation: If you actually play the game (which is totally fine and encouraged), rotate the deck with a modern UNO set to keep the vintage pieces pristine for display or resale. It’s a little time traveler’s compromise.
