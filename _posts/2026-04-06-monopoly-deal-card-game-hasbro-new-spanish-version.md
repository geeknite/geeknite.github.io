## How to play the Spanish edition: quick refresher

If you’ve played Monopoly Deal in English, you know the rhythm: players collect property cards by acquiring full sets; you use money cards to pay rents; you play action cards to steal, swap, or protect your assets; you win by completing three full property sets. The Spanish edition does not reinvent the wheel. It keeps the structure intact, but the language becomes the driver that makes the wheel spin smoother for Spanish-speaking players.

Turn structure remains straightforward:
- Draw two cards at the start of your turn (or one, depending on house rules you’ve adopted).
- Play as many cards as you want from your hand during your turn, but you must pay rent to opponents when required using money or property cards.
- Use action cards to steal, force trades, or force rent payments. The emphasis is on building up property sets while disrupting others.
- The first player to complete three full property sets wins.

Key action cards include deals, rents, and steal cards. In the Spanish edition, the names may be localized, but the effect remains the same: you can force someone to pay rent, swap cards under certain conditions, or steal a property. Mastering the timing of these spells is where the game tips from casual pastime to dramatic spectacle.
