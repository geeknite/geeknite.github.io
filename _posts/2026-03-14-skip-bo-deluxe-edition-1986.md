## How to authenticate a sealed old edition without losing your mind

Authenticating a sealed vintage edition is a mix of practical checks and nerdy intuition. Here are some guidelines that may help you avoid opening a time capsule you will later regret opening for the sake of a photo:

- Examine the seal: A clean, intact seal with no signs of tampering is a good indicator. Look for air bubbles or unusual creases that could indicate the seal has been broken and resealed. In a 1986 edition, you should expect a robust seal that feels like a sealed fruit crate from a bygone era. If the seal looks suspect, proceed with extra caution.
- Inspect the box corners: Boxes from the era often show slight wear, but major crushing is a red flag. If you are chasing mint condition, minor corner dings are acceptable, but be honest with yourself about the condition when you plan to resell or display.
- Check the insert and manual: The rulebook should be legible, with minimal yellowing but not brittle edges. The presence of a manual in good condition adds to the collectible value. If you can confirm the correct edition and the deluxe components, you are one step closer to accurate valuation.
- Compare with known team references: Check vintage catalogs or collector guides to verify the edition specifics. Those references help you confirm that the packaging matches the Deluxe edition as released in your target year. If you see discrepancies, treat it as a red flag or a story for the comments section of your blog post about the journey to authentication.

## Collecting tips and care for the long haul

- Store in a cool, dry place: The classic cardboard and lacquered box combination does not love humidity. A stable environment helps prevent warping and keeps the seal intact longer.
- Keep the box away from direct sunlight: UV light can fade packaging art even if the cards themselves stay pristine.
- Avoid aggressive card sleeves on a sealed collectible: if you sleeve cards, you risk introducing friction that could affect resale value or, in the worst case, damage to the box integrity if you attempt to remove sleeves later without care.
- Document provenance: a simple note about where and when you acquired the item can add a layer of story for future collectors. It makes the item feel more alive than simply a product on a shelf.
- Consider optional accessories with care: you might find deluxe editions bundled with vintage storage tins or inserts. If you pursue these, ensure that they match the original edition to maintain authenticity.
