## Salvage operations: how to proceed without manual
### DIY house rules to get you through the night
When the instruction sheet is missing, you can adopt these simple house rules to keep things fair and fun:
- Rule of Three: If a move would cause the tower to fall within three moves, forgo the move and take a penalty sip of beverage. That keeps the game from turning into a single catastrophic show.
- The Balancing Rule: If you cannot find a stable block, you must switch sides and try a different column. It forces players to be flexible and to acknowledge that gravity sometimes plays favorites.
- The Gentle Placement Rule: Place blocks with the lightest touch possible. The gentler approach reduces the risk of catastrophic collapse and allows for more dramatic slow-burn suspense.
- The Quick Scoring Rule: Use a simple tally — one point for every block removed without tower collapse, extra points if the tower stays upright after a particularly close call. Keeping score helps maintain competitive energy without turning the game into a meltdown.

### Quick-start guidelines for a 5-block session
If you want to run a quick session with this missing-block scenario, try a five-minute warm-up to get the energy up, then ramp into a 10-minute main round using the House Rules above. If you keep the session light and humorous, you will find that the missing blocks become a springboard for witty banter and clever improvisation rather than a reason to rage-quit. The aim is not to maximize height but to maximize fun and collaboration, with a little geek bravado thrown in for good measure.
