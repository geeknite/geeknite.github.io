### Card quality and design notes
From a tactile standpoint, Cards Against Humanity cards typically feature sturdy card stock with a glossy finish that makes shuffling feel satisfying rather than slippery. The Fourth Expansion continues this tradition, and you can expect the colors to pop when sunlight hits the deck at a dramatic angle. The text remains legible, which matters when you are trying to salvage a joke on the first attempt rather than after a chaotic cascade of laughter leads to a misread. In terms of art direction, CAH cards are not trying to win any award for fancy illustration. The charm lies in the simplicity: bold black and white with concise prompts that invite a wide range of comedic interpretations. This edition stays true to that ethos, delivering punchlines that are often more about the clever juxtaposition than the ornate artwork.

If you are the kind of player who loves to annotate your cards or customize the deck to your own in-jokes, you will appreciate how well CAH cards hold up to a bit of wear and tear. The quality allows you to scribble, cross out, and add your own comments on the margins if that is your style. But be warned: once you start marking up the cards, you might have a harder time pretending you are not fully committed to the irreverent humor you signed up for in the first place.

### How to run an epic CAH night with this expansion
- Start with a light warm-up round using a subset of the new white cards to acclimate players to the tone of the expansion.
