## Practical Usage and Session Tips
- Establish a color-to-status map early: red for danger, blue for clues, green for investigation progress, purple for hold or influence. A quick one-line recap at the end of the session helps new players catch up on token language.
- Use tokens to mark allies and enemies on a practical backdrop to visualize pursuit or confrontation. Tokens can serve as a stand-in map showing who has the narrative edge or who is pressing the action pool.
- Consider using Fortune Row dice as fate dice for narrative prompts. A roll might unlock a twist; the group describes a short beat and then you drop a token to anchor the moment for later callbacks.
- Transition scenes by sorting tokens by location or by who will carry the next thread. A tidy transition feels cinematic and preserves momentum.
