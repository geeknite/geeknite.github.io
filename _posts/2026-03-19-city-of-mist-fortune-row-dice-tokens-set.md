## Practical Usage and Session Tips
- Establish a color-to-status map early: red for danger, blue for clues, green for investigation progress, purple for hold or influence. A quick one-line post-session recap can help new players catch up with the token language.
- Use tokens to mark allies and enemies on a simple backdrop to visualize the chase or confrontation in a crowded scene. The tokens can act as a proxy map to show who has the advantage or who is pressing the action pool.
- Consider using Fortune Row dice as fate dice for narrative prompts. If a roll unlocks a twist, let the group describe a short narrative beat and then assign a token that captures the moment for future reference.
- When transitioning between scenes, arrange tokens by location or by who holds the next plot thread. A tidy transition feels cinematic and gives players a sense of momentum rather than chaos.
