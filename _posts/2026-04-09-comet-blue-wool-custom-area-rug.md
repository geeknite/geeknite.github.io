## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity: Keeping Your Rug Kissed by the Cosmos
### Cleaning and Spot-Treatments
Wool can be resilient to stains if treated promptly. A gentle blot, not rubbing, is typically recommended for most common spills. For more stubborn stains, a mild wool-safe cleaner will do the trick, ideally tested on a small corner first. Avoid harsh solvents that could weaken the fibers or alter the color. If you’re into the science of stain resistance, you’ll appreciate how wool’s natural properties can resist some liquid infiltration better than synthetic fibers. For hobbyists who want a deeper dive, check your rug’s care guide (often provided by the manufacturer) and compare it to external wool-care resources for best practices.
### Vacuuming and General Upkeep
Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps remove dirt before it becomes embedded in the fiber. A slow, deliberate pass, rather than a fast sprint, will preserve the pile’s loft and prevent bead-like agglomerations that can occur with aggressive vacuuming. If you own a pet, you’ll want to schedule deeper cleanings a little less frequently and use an upright vacuum with a rotating brush that’s not too aggressive for the rug. The goal is to maintain vitality in the wool without matting the surface into a tumbleweed of fibers.
### Longevity and Wear Patterns
Wool tends to age gracefully if cared for properly. A 2- to 5-year horizon is common for high-use rugs in busy households; many owners find that a well-kept wool rug can outlast plastic synthetics by a surprising margin. The Comet Blue rug, with its sturdy weave and colorfast dye, should endure daily life and the occasional space-battle with furniture legs. If you’re the kind of person who updates decor every season, you’ll appreciate the rug’s versatility in coordinating with different color palettes and textures over time.
