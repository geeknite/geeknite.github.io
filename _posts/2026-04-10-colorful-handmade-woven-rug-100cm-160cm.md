## Care and Maintenance
### Cleaning basics
Regular maintenance is straightforward. Vacuum with a soft brush attachment on a low setting to avoid pulling fibers. Rotate every few months to equalize wear, especially if you have a high traffic zone or if the rug sits under a frequently used chair. In case of spills, blot rather than rub; this helps preserve the weave and prevent piling that can occur if you aggressively scrub the surface. For stubborn stains, test a small inconspicuous area first, then consider a mild non toxic cleaning solution recommended for natural fiber rugs.

### Color preservation tips
Natural dyes are beautiful but can fade with prolonged sun exposure. If your rug is in a sunlit area, consider rotating it periodically or using blinds/curtains during peak sun hours to prolong color intensity. A UV protective window treatment can also help. If color migration becomes a concern due to heavy wear or heat, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in hand woven textiles. They can advise on color sequestering and fiber care without compromising the integrity of the weave.

### Longevity tricks
Investing in a rug pad is a small but mighty move for longevity. A good pad reduces friction, prevents curling at the edges, and adds a layer of comfort underfoot. It also helps in keeping the rug in place if you have a busy household with kids or pets. The pad acts as a silent partner that extends the rug life and reduces the need for frequent repositioning, which means less wrangling and more lounging.

## Ethical Considerations and Sustainability
### The ethics of handmade goods
Ethical sourcing is a major talking point in the handmade world. Good rugs often come with transparency about materials and labor. If you are shopping with a conscience, ask the seller for information on fiber origin and the weaving community involved. Many artisan groups rely on fair trade practices, which means the people making the rug are compensated justly for their craft. ### Environmental footprint
Natural fibers and hand weaving are generally more sustainable than mass produced synthetics, particularly when dyes are natural or low impact. The environmental footprint tends to be lower per unit area, especially when compared with rug production methods that require heavy energy inputs. If you are an eco minded consumer, look for rugs that come with certifications or artisan group endorsements that highlight sustainable practices. You can also consider the end of life of the rug; natural fibers are more likely to be recycled or repurposed than synthetic blends.
