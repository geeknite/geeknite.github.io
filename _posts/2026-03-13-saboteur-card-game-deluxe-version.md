## What is Saboteur? A quick primer
Saboteur is a light to medium-weight card game wrapped in a mining fantasy. Players take on the roles of dwarves mining for treasure. Some players are honest miners who want to reach the treasure cards, while others are saboteurs whose goal is to derail the mine’s path to the gold. Each round, players lay down path cards to connect a starting point to one of several treasure cards while the saboteurs quietly insert roadblocks, forcing detours, misdirections, and a chorus of accusations that would shame a medieval tribunal.

The base game emphasizes deduction, negotiation, and a dash of luck. The Deluxe Version arrives with higher-quality components, a handful of new cards to add spice, and some refined rules that smooth the rough edges without dulling the tremors of chaos that make Saboteur so entertaining.

## Setup, flow, and the dance of deception
Saboteur Deluxe follows a familiar loop:

1) Each player takes a role card (secretly). Some players are dwarves who want the treasure—others are saboteurs who want chaos or misdirection.
2) A starting point is laid, followed by a row of tunnel and path cards. The objective is to connect the tunnel from the start to a treasure card.
3) Players take turns laying cards and sometimes causing a little mischief to derail others. When a treasure is reached, the round ends and scoring happens.

What makes the Deluxe Version shine here is the smoother setup. The upgraded components mean fewer confusion moments and quicker recognition of the card you’re about to play or block. That might sound like small potatoes, but on game night, quick starts are the difference between “this was a good idea” and “this table is officially a cave.”

A few tips to maximize the experience:
- Read the room before you lay your first path card. If you have a table full of chaos-loving players, lean into the sabotage flavor early; let the distrust simmer.
- Use the treasure goals as foils. The way you connect to different treasure cards creates varying routes and dramatic reveals that keep people engaged rather than staring at their phones.
- Don’t be afraid to bluff. Saboteur thrives on misdirection; a confident claim that “I’m definitely not the saboteur” is often enough to trigger a cascade of paranoia.

Key strategic touches in the deluxe edition include:

- The role reveal cadence. In many rounds, players rely on social timing and a few well-placed claims to guide the group toward the treasure or toward a spectacular collapse of the tunnel.
