## Build Quality: Pine That Means Business (Or at Least Not-Creaking-While-Sipping-Lemonade)

## Weather and Maintenance Real Talk: Pine Isn’t Forever, But It Can Be Very Durable

Outdoor wood requires some care. Pine is a softwood relative to cedar or teak, which means it’s more prone to dents and scratches from things like lawn chairs sliding across a deck. With proper care, it can last several seasons, especially if you apply sealant or varnish annually and store cushions under cover during heavy rain or winter months.

Maintenance tips:
- Regularly check for loose bolts and tighten as needed. The last thing you want is a wobble that converts your happy hour into a mid-patio demolition derby.
- Clean with mild soap and water; avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the wood finish.
- Reapply sealant or protective coating every 1-2 years depending on climate. Coastal regions with salty breezes may require more frequent care.
- Cushions should be stored indoors or in a dry, ventilated space when not in use for extended periods. Mold and mildew love damp cushions like raccoons love trash can mysteries.

Pro golfer tip: if you want to preserve color and reduce sun fading, consider placing the furniture under a shade sail or moving it to a spot with partial shade during peak sun hours.
