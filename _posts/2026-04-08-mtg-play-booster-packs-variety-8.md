## Build Quality of the Lot and Safety Considerations
Here’s the practical reality you must consider before dropping the cash: packaging integrity and authenticity. With any sealed lot, you want to ensure the product is legitimate and not counterfeit or resealed. The eight-pack model tends to come from reputable distributors, but it’s not immune to oddball packaging anomalies. Here are a few checklists to keep you safe:

- Check the outer wrap: The seal should be intact and free of tampering. A crumbly or suspiciously loose wrap should raise a red flag.
- Inspect the packs: The inside wrappers should be evenly wrapped with no obvious signs of resealing or resealing marks. If you can peep inside the pack and you see old or mismatched card backs, consider returning the package.
- Compare with official bagging: If you’ve opened similar packs before, you’ll know the general feel and weight differences. A drastic deviation in weight or texture can be a sign of a counterfeit or nonstandard batch.
- Beware of fakes that mimic foil and texture: Some fakes attempt to replicate the foil haze. The real thing usually has a consistent finish and a proper set symbol.

If you’re buying from an official retailer or a reputable marketplace, your risk is lower, but it’s still wise to examine the packaging upon arrival. If you’re unsure about authenticity, reach out to customer service and request proof of origin. The Magic the Gathering community thrives on trust, but a little due diligence never hurts.
