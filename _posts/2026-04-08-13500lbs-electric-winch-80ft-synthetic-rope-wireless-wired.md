## Rope: Synthetic vs Steel
Out in the wild, many people debate synthetic rope against steel. The rope in this unit is a modern synthetic fiber that balances safety and performance. It is lighter, easier to handle, and less likely to cause severe injury if the line does snap. It does require proper maintenance and inspection for heat and abrasion, but this is a good trade-off for most hobbyists. The synthetic rope is also less prone to kinking and fraying if you spool it correctly.

## Wireless and Wired Remote: Convenience vs Reliability

### Rescue a Stuck Trailer
Same principle, different attitude: the line thread and block angle are key. Use a snatch block to re-route the line, avoid sharp angles, and keep loads aligned with the drum to reduce friction and heat. The synthetic rope’s stretch helps spread dynamic loads across the line, reducing peak stress and making the recovery safer for the rope and the anchor.

## How to Use and Maintenance
- Inspect rope and hardware after every use
- Re-spool rope to maintain even layers; avoid hot spots
- Check remote batteries and connectivity
- Keep the winch and its motor clean and free of mud build-up

- Store in a dry place when not in use
