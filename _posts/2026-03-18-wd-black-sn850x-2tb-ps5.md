Sustained heavy writes can cause temps to rise, which can trigger throttling if the heatsink isn’t doing its job. The SN850X is not immune to this: in a closed PS5 interior with a tiny heatsink, you’ll likely see some throttling in sustained heavy loads. That’s not a deal-breaker, but it’s a reason to ensure you have a good heatsink or at least adequate cooling. ## Installation in PS5
### Prerequisites
- PS5 with an M.2 slot that accepts Gen4 drives (most models do).
- A heatsink that fits within 15–25 mm height (or a bundle that includes one).
- A small screwdriver and a little patience for the install ritual.

### Steps
1. Power down and unplug the PS5.
2. Remove the side panel and locate the M.2 slot cover.
3. Remove the screw backing and the M.2 shield; install the SN850X into the slot.
4. Attach the heatsink (if not already integrated with the drive).
5. Secure the heatsink with its screw, reassemble the panel, and power on.
6. Format the drive in the PS5 UI; decide which games to move first.

### After install
You’ll be greeted by a faster-boot experience and snappier game loads.
