## 3D printing with custom colors

Custom colors are where this project shines. Whether you have a multi material printer or you print in stages, the ability to differentiate parts by color helps teams stay organized and makes the box look like a product rather than a hobby project. Two common paths:
- Multi filament printing: print the lid and inner tray in contrasting materials to get a bold two-tone effect. You can also add small color accents to highlight the token tray or the phase dividers.
- Single filament with post processing: print the entire assembly in a single color, then use vinyl wraps, acrylic paint, or even colored resin to add the accent colors. This is excellent if you do not own a multi extruder or if you want to maintain a minimal printing footprint.

If you want to push the color game even further, you can print inserts for the phase slots in several colors and slide them in after printing. A tiny spacer in a bright color helps you quickly identify which phase is up next without reading tiny numbers. The interior can also incorporate a soft gasket or foam padding to protect the deck during rough travel. Use a light film of silicone grease or spray to keep the cards from sticking to the slide if your tolerance is a bit tight.

### Print settings and tips

Across printers, there are a few baseline settings that consistently deliver good results:
- Layer height: 0.2 mm for a crisp edge on the external surfaces; 0.12 mm if you want extra detail.
- Wall thickness: at least 1.2 mm with 3 to 4 perimeters for rigidity.
- Infill: 15 to 25 percent is usually sufficient for a sturdy box while keeping print times reasonable.
- Bed adhesion: use a brim if you expect warping, particularly with larger lid pieces.
- Tolerances: allow 0.2 to 0.4 mm extra in the slot areas to facilitate easy card insertion and removal.
- Post processing: a light sanding around the card slots helps cards slide in smoothly; a small fine file can soften any sharp corner inside the tray where cards might snag.

If you have a multi color printer, you can insert color changes at the exact border where the lid meets the base, or you can opt for a crisp two tone by printing the base and the lid separately in distinct colors.

## Troubleshooting and tips

If you experience fit issues or the lid is too snug, try these checks:
- Reassess the slot width and adjust by a few tenths of a millimeter if necessary.
- Confirm the outer shell dimensions align with lid geometry. Slight misalignment can cause binding.
- For a persistent lid issue, print a slightly larger lip or add a small shim to the lid edge to improve alignment.
- If your cards rub when slid into the slots, consider adding a micro bevel to the card edges or slightly rounding the inner slot corners for smoother travel.

For color changes in a multi material printer, keep a consistent raft strategy so you avoid color bleed into the adjacent parts. If you are printing in stages, separate the components and assemble after printing to ensure clean color boundaries.
