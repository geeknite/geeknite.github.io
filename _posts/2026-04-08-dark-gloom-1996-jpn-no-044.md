## Care and Display: Preserving Old Cards
### Physical Care
Vintage cards like the Dark Gloom require a gentle touch. Use archival-grade sleeves and top-loaded plastic protectors when you’re handling and displaying. Keep them away from direct sunlight, which is the arch-nemesis of color and gloss, and store them in a cool, dry place to avoid warping and curling. If you opt for display sheets in a binder, choose pages that lay flat and don’t stress the card at the corners. And yes, keep a dry microfiber cloth handy for the occasional fingerprint you didn’t notice until you tilted the card and admired the light-catching details.

### Storage and Long-Term Preservation
If you’re serious about long-term preservation, consider a climate-controlled display case with UV-filtering glass. While you don’t need a lab-grade environment, a stable humidity level reduces the risk of paper shrinkage and edge wear. For collectors who want to go the extra mile, inert storage boxes with desiccants can help maintain an ideal environment during long periods of inactivity. The idea is simple: treat the Dark Gloom card as a little piece of history that deserves the same care you’d give to a rare manuscript or a vintage console.
