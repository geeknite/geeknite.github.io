## Maintenance and Care

To keep these desks looking sharp and performing well for years to come, you’ll need a regular light maintenance routine. Here are a few practical tips:
- Wipe surfaces with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner. Avoid harsh solvents that can dull the finish or remove the matte coating.
- Tighten bolts every few months. The weight distribution of three desks means the occasional squeak might happen due to slightly loose screws.
- Check the screen dividers for alignment and movement. If they start to wobble, re-tighten the mounting brackets; you don’t want to recreate a chaotic art installation of privacy panels during a video call.
- Use cable trays and grommets to minimize dust buildup. A quick vacuum around the desk legs helps more than you’d expect.

A few maintenance misadventures to consider:
- If you drop a bottle of coffee on the desk, do not panic. Wipe it up quickly and enjoy the caffeinated memory of your accidental splurge. The surface should recover if you clean promptly.
- If a screw clanks when you lean on the desk, it’s probably fine but torque-check the area. If you hear a metallic whisper following a move, stop and re-tighten before things get dramatic.
