### Paintability and detail
Let’s talk about the elephant-sized brush stroke in the room: Edouard’s contrast-driven aesthetic benefits from crisp line work. These minis respond nicely to a layering approach that starts with a mid-tone base, followed by bold edge highlights and selective washes in the recesses. ## Painting Tips for Edouard Guiton-Style Survivors
If you’re inspired by the art direction and want to approach these survivors with a simple, repeatable workflow, here are a few practical tips:
- Start with safe base colors. Pick two base tones per model (armor and skin) and keep the rest neutral. This reduces decision fatigue and keeps your tabletop looking cohesive.
- Edge highlighting is your friend. A light edge highlight on armor and weapons makes sculpt details pop from a distance, which is handy in a busy scenario where you need to recognize friends from foes quickly.
- Use selective washes. A controlled wash in the recesses will deepen the shadows and give you that crisp, dramatic look Edouard is known for without flattening the contrast.
- Dry brushing for texture. A subtle dry brush on leather or fabric textures can add a tactile depth that photographs well and looks more convincing on camera than it does in person if you push too hard.
- Clear coats matter. A matte spray will preserve the painted details and reduce the glare on glossy highlights. If you’re planning a display piece, you might experiment with a satin or gloss finish for a dramatic sheen on the metallics.

To borrow a line from a famous necromancer: knowledge is power, but primers and paints are the power tools. The Survivor Set rewards a little extra effort on the paint desk, especially if you’re chasing a unique character vibe rather than a uniform army aesthetic.
