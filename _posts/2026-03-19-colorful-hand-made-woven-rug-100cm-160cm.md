## Materials, Crafting, and Durability: What Keeps It Together

### Fiber Content: Wool, Cotton, or a Friendly Hybrid?

Hand-made rugs often come in a few common fiber families: wool, cotton, jute, or blends. Wool is the most common for durability and softness, offering natural elasticity and resilience to foot traffic. Cotton provides a lighter feel and easier cleaning but can be less durable under heavy use. Some modern hand-made rugs use a wool-cotton blend or a wool-synthetic mix to balance softness, resilience, and price. ### Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Rainbow Bright

Maintenance is the practical superpower of any textile. For a hand-made rug, a regular vacuum with a brush head set to a gentle setting is ideal. Avoid aggressive beater bars that can untwist fibers over time. Rotate the rug every few months to distribute wear evenly, especially if sunlight hits it unevenly. For stains, treat promptly with a gentle, fiber-safe cleaner. Blot rather than rub to avoid pushing stains deeper into the fibers. A quick spot test on a tiny corner is always wise before applying any cleaner to a visible area.

If you have pets, you’ll want to address the occasional shedding or loose fibers promptly. A fine-toothed comb or a fabric shaver can help maintain a tidy surface, and a quick lint roller pass can remove pet hair without requiring a deep clean every week. Pro tip: if you have a child who loves color, keep a light, washable area near the rug for art projects; it’s far easier than removing marker ink from wool.
