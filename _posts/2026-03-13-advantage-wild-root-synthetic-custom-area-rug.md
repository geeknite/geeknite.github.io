## Practical Tips for Setup and Care
### Placement Ideas
- Center the rug under a coffee table with at least 18 inches of exposed floor on all sides for a balanced look. 
- In a lounge with a sectional, consider a custom length that reaches under the chaise and tucks neatly beneath the seating; this creates a cohesive anchor for the entire space.
- A rug under a bed can pull a bedroom together without fighting with the bed legs. If you have a king-size bed, a slightly larger custom rug might be the right call to ensure you wake up to a soft landing every morning.

### Padding and Comfort
A rug pad is the unsung hero of comfortable living. It keeps the rug in place, adds underfoot cushioning, and reduces wrinkling at the edges. For a custom size, you can choose a pad that matches the exact dimensions or select a slightly larger pad with a trim to fit. Either way, the result is better traction, less bunching, and a more luxurious feel underfoot when you step out of bed for that early-morning laptop sprint.

### Cleaning and Spot Treatment
Spills happen. A quick blot with a dry cloth to lift the liquid is your first move. For more stubborn stains, a mild detergent mixed with water can be dabbed on gently. Avoid aggressive scrubbing, which can push stains deeper or damage the pile. The synthetic fibers typically dry quickly, so you won’t be standing on a damp rug for long. For pet accidents, a quick clean and a light deodorizing spray after the fact keeps smells at bay and your carpet from feeling like it’s the dog’s personal gym.

### Seasonal and Light Considerations
Direct sunlight can fade colors over time, even with colorfast synthetics. If possible, rotate furniture placement seasonally to maintain a balanced look and minimize sun exposure in one area. If you have a bright sunspot that you can’t avoid, consider window treatments that reduce UV exposure and keep the rug vibrant for longer.
