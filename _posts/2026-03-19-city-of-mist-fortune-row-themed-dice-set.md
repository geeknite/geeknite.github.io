## How to incorporate Fortune Row into your campaigns
### Campaign planning and session pacing
If you want the dice to contribute to storytelling rather than merely recording outcomes, create a ritual around them. Before a crucial scene, you can declare that Fortune Row’s “fortune” is drawn from a specific subset of faces or symbols. For example, in a tense negotiation, you might say that rolling a particular icon means the GM reveals a concealed motive, while a plain number indicates a more straightforward success. ### House rules and adaptations
To avoid slowing down the table, consider these house-rule ideas:
- Narrative prompts: On certain icons, grant a brief narrative prompt to the player describing a clue or motive.
- Readability buffer: If someone misreads a face due to lighting, allow a quick secondary roll or a “reroll” once per session for that die.
- Focused dice pool: If you’re running a big investigation arc, designate Fortune Row dice as “story dice” that you only use for narratively significant checks.
