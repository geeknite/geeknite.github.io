## Strategy and Tactical Play: Don’t Let the Feline Bombs Win

Even though Exploding Kittens is often described as chaotic and chaotic by design, there’s a surprising amount of strategy if you’re paying attention to the table’s rhythms. Here are a few tips to help you maximize your odds without becoming the pariah at the table:

- Manage your Defuse cards like rare tech for a space mission; you’ll want to hold onto them for the later rounds when the risk ramps up. If you use them early, you dramatically increase the likelihood that someone else will survive long enough to cause you trouble later.
- Read the room: different groups treat the “cat” cards differently. Some players treat Cat cards as mere placeholders; others use them to force card draws or to draw in a chaotic rhythm—play to your audience but not at their expense.
- Timing is everything: pulling a strategic move when the deck is thinning can decide who gets exploded and who thrives. Expect a few rounds of misplays, accidental taunts, and dramatic “oops, that was not supposed to happen” moments.
- Don’t overthink the art; some Cat cards are functionally more valuable than others because they enable combinations that keep you alive longer or disrupt others more effectively.
- The “Attack” and “Skip” cards can be used to stall or accelerate; using them at the right moment can ruin someone else’s plan while protecting your own butt. Use them with flair, like you’re staging a tiny fireworks show but with paper and ink.

A lot of this comes down to social dynamics more than raw math; your friends’ risk appetite is the true hidden variable, and you’ll be adjusting your strategy on the fly as players misinterpret the board state.
