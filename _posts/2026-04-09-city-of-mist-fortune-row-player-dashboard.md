## What is Fortune Row and what is the dashboard for
### The core idea
Fortune Row centers around the idea that every hero in City of Mist has a thread that leads toward a larger, city-spanning myth. The dashboard in question translates that thread into a playable interface: clues, relationships, masks, and fateful moves live in a visible, accessible place. The intent is not to overwhelm with data, but to offer a choreographed surface where narrative decisions can be tracked with pace and flair.

### Who is it for
- Players who want a tactile way to visualize their arc, their clues, and their evolving relationship web.
- GMs who want a structured yet flexible scaffold to propel mystery threads across sessions without the dreaded sudden forgetfulness of a memory-challenged city.
- Tables that lean into the noir-meets-mythic vibe and enjoy a little dashboard theater during downtime and revelations.

### What you get on the dashboard
- A central space for Masks, Powers, and Mythos connections tailored to Fortune Row’s setting.
- Clue and Evidence trackers that encourage forward momentum without forcing the pace.
- A Relationship grid to map ties between characters, factions, and NPCs that populate Fortune Row and beyond.
- A Fate/Fortune mechanic tracker that hints at the weight of choices and the speed of consequences.
- A clean, stylish aesthetic designed to be both readable at the table and lovely in the hands of a player who appreciates visual storytelling.

For the curious mind, this is the narrative equivalent of a mood board that also handles game mechanics with a little pocket calculator tucked behind a velvet sleeve. It is not a complete system refresh; it is a targeted tool designed to enhance situational awareness and give players something tangible to hold onto as the city mutates.

## The anatomy of the Player Dashboard
### The layout and flow
The dashboard is laid out to mimic a noir storyboard—rooms of the city interconnected by clues and masked identities. The top layer is typically occupied by the player character’s core identity blocks and the mask they own. Beneath that, you’ll find the known Powers and Relationships that shape how the character approaches Fortune Row’s puzzles and the city at large. Then there are the Clues and Evidence sections, which serve as both a memory aid and a storytelling prompt. Finally, the Fate and Consequences areas remind players that in City of Mist, every action echoes along the alleyways and across the rooftops.

The beauty of the arrangement is in its tactile nature. You can physically move cards, post-its, or tokens across the dashboard to reflect revelations, betrayals, or changing loyalties. The dashboard is deliberately modular, meaning you can add or remove sections to fit your table size and the tone of your campaign. If a table loves the dynamic of dynamic secrets being surfaced gradually, Fortune Row can be your mechanical partner in that dance.

### Masks, powers, and mythos integration
City of Mist uses a mask mechanic to separate who the character is from who they want to become in the city. Fortune Row’s dashboard honors that with crisp, accessible sections that map mask-theme alignment to in-game choices. It helps players visualize how a reveal might shift their Mask's boundaries and how a new Power might tilt the balance of a scene. The integration is not merely cosmetic. When a player places a clue or shifts a relationship on the dashboard, it often correlates to a dramatic Freeplay moment or a Mythos-tied event that the GM can leverage for a dramatic beat.

### Clues, evidence, and narrative momentum
Fortune Row emphasizes narrative momentum by encouraging players to collect clues that can later coalesce into significant plot pivots. The dashboard’s Clues section is designed to avoid bloat while ensuring each clue has a potential narrative payoff. Evidence links can be dynamic rather than static, allowing players to connect the dots in ways that feel personal and consequential rather than database-like. The goal is to make clues feel like real-world breadcrumbs in a mythic city, with enough ambiguity to fuel discussion and enough clarity to avoid stalemate.

### Relationships and factions
A city as layered as Fortune Row requires a robust network map. The dashboard integrates a Relationship grid and a Faction section, so players can see how their choices ripple across different groups and players. The design encourages players to think about alliances, rivalries, and the price of keeping secrets from the wrong people. It also provides a convenient high-level view for the GM to weave social dynamics into the next session rather than pulling champs out of a hat mid-scene.

## Mechanics and balance: what to expect at the table
### Tracking fortune and fate
Fortune Row introduces a refined focus on Fate and Fortune as guiding forces behind choices. The dashboard offers a tangible home for these concepts, giving players a sense of agency and narrative consequence. The balance lies in ensuring the Fortune Points or Fate tracks do not become a numbing resource race. A well-calibrated session uses the dashboard to highlight significant decisions rather than mundane actions. The GM can nudge the pace by spotlighting a high-stakes choice that aligns with a mythic beat or an escalating tension in Fortune Row’s setting.

### Clues and their narrative weight
Clues carry narrative weight because they can lead to meaningful discoveries or red herrings. The dashboard helps by categorizing clues by significance and tying them to relationships and masks. This synergy makes sessions feel purposeful. It also invites players to debate which clue matters most, a healthy form of table-side collaboration and friendly contention.

### Relationship mapping and faction dynamics
A robust relationship network makes Fortune Row a living city. The dashboard’s mapping tools help ensure no NPC or faction feels like a prop; every actor has motive, history, and potential consequences tied to the players’ actions. In terms of balance, the GM should monitor the pace of relationship development to prevent a scenario where the board becomes an unwinnable knot. A good rule is to pause and summarize the table’s momentum every few scenes, using the dashboard as the memory anchor to remind everyone who cares about what and why.

## Session prep and practical workflow
### Pre-session setup
- Review the current Mask alignments and the players' declared goals. If a character’s Mask clashes with Fortune Row’s core mystery, highlight those tensions on the dashboard.
- Populate at least three clues that can plausibly connect to a larger secret. One should be a misdirect to keep players honest and curious.
- Prepare a short list of NPCs or factions with a single driving objective. Give yourself a couple of lines of dialogue to drop during downtime.
- Decide on one dramatic reveal that will create a turning point. The dashboard should be ready to accommodate the wave of consequences that follow.

### In-session workflow
- Start with a quick status check: who knows what, who has a new clue, who is pursuing a lead. Update the dashboard as you go. This keeps the table aligned and reduces confusion.
- Use the dashboard as a visual prop during narration. A player can point to a clue and say, this is what I want to pursue, and the GM can respond by enriching the scene with micro-beats that reflect the player’s choice.
- When a reveal happens, update the Relationships and Faction sections to show the shifting allegiances. Reward narrative breakthroughs with a small mechanical payoff on the Fate track.

### Post-session wrap-up
- Do a quick debrief: which clues moved forward, which masks gained new nuance, and what the next thread will be. The dashboard acts as a memory palace here, helping players recall where their story went and where it might go next.
- If someone had a particularly dramatic moment, record it as a narrative highlight and consider whether a new clue or complication should arise in the following session.

## Alternatives and comparisons
If you are weighing Fortune Row against other City of Mist supplements or third-party tools, consider how you want to balance narrative clarity with mechanical depth.

- Quickstart sheets: If you want something lean for a one-shot, an alternative is a compact, pre-filled sheet focusing on a single mystery. It will save you setup time but might not offer the same era-defining player experience.
- Full cast dashboards: Some groups prefer a large, creature-of-the-city dashboard that tracks dozens of clues and dozens of NPCs. Fortune Row’s approach is more moderate and table-friendly, especially for intermediate campaigns.
- Digital toolkits: If your table is digital-first, a dashboard in a shareable app can replicate the experience with fewer border constraints. The risk here is missing the tactile punch that a printed dashboard offers.

### A note on expectations
Fortune Row is designed as a narrative instrument, not a hard game engine. It works best when players lean into mood and character through their decisions rather than grinding to optimize a score. If you crave mathematical granularity, you may want to complement the dashboard with additional GM tools to maintain a comfortable pace.
