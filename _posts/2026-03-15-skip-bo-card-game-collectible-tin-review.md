## How to Play (A Quick Setup and Run-Through)

### Setup

1) Shuffle the entire Skip-Bo deck and deal a stock pile to each player. The exact number of cards for the stock pile can vary based on the number of players, but in most 2-6 player games, you’ll have a reasonable stack to chase down rather than a hero’s long march into the night. 2) Each player draws five cards into their hand from the draw pile. 3) In the center, four build piles are available for everyone to contribute to. These piles start at 1 and must be built in ascending order up to 12, using either from your hand or from your stock pile when you can play a card. Wild Skip-Bo cards can stand in for any number in a sequence. 4) The first player to empty their stock pile wins.

### Turn Structure
On your turn, you’ll typically do two things in this order:
- Draw back up to five cards in your hand from the draw pile.
- Play as many valid cards as you can from your hand or from your stock pile onto the build piles. You can use a Skip-Bo wild card as any number to bridge gaps or to finish a pile. If you play an entire stock pile, you instantly win the round.

If you can’t play a card, you discard one card to your own discard piles and your turn ends. The rule-skim is simple, but there’s enough flexibility to create some fun “what-if” moments as you string together a chain of plays.

### Endgame and Runners-Up
The moment one player clears their stock pile, the round ends and you tally scores based on the cards left in other players’ stock piles. In a casual setting, you can opt for quick “best of three” rounds or play for a single-round bragging right. The quick tempo is a big part of Skip-Bo’s appeal, and the Collectible Tin edition amplifies that by making setup friction near zero.

## Strategy and Tactics (For When the Tin Isn’t Just for Show)

### For Beginners: Simple Wins, Big Smiles
- Play to your build piles as soon as you can safely do so. The more piles you can advance, the more options you create for future turns.
- Use wild Skip-Bo cards strategically. They’re your “wait-until-you-need-it” lifelines; preserve them for high-value sequences or when you’re close to finishing a stock pile.
- Watch opponents’ discard piles. If someone has a visible preference for a particular suit or number, you can often guess what they might be holding and steal a march by timing your plays accordingly.

### For Intermediate Players: Timing and Reading the Table
- Control the pace by pressuring opponents with your own build pile choices. Sometimes you want to force a tiny tank to stall if you’re on the cusp of victory and need to burn a few cards from your hand first.
- Use your draws to fill gaps in your own stock pile while blocking others from finishing. It’s not just about your own cards; it’s a chess match in the form of a lighthearted card game.

### For Social-Group Play: Chaos, Camaraderie, and Rules-Quick-Reference
- In larger groups (5-6 players), you’ll get more wild swings. That’s part of the charm, but you’ll also need to be mindful about keeping the table engaged and not letting one lone player monopolize the table talk. A quick rules refresh between rounds keeps the vibe friendly and fun.
