### How to verify condition when buying used titles

If you’re the type who wants to do a quick field check before checkout, here are practical steps you can take:

- Inspect the box corners and edges for crushing, especially around the spine and lid. A few furls and dents are normal; major creases are not.
- Look at the rulebook for excessive creases, water damage, or staining. A clean rulebook with legible text is a good sign.
- Check components: do you see all tokens, hallmarks, dice, boards, and player mats? If something is missing, ask if replacements are available or if the seller can supply a partial refund.
- Spin the box. A light shake that results in loose pieces clinking around is totally normal for some games, but a box that rattles with every move might indicate missing inserts or loose boards.
- Play length alignment: ensure the target playtime ~60-90 minutes for mid-weight titles if you’re booking a family night; longer games deserve a dedicated slot or a separate evening.

## Practical shopping tips: maximizing value and minimizing headaches

- Know your room. If your play area is tight, measure your shelves and box dimensions before you buy. Some boxes are deceptive: they look compact on a photo but are big enough to displace your cat when opened.
- Check the return policy. If you’re buying from a private seller or an online marketplace, confirm if returns are possible for unused or lightly used items. A seller who stands by their stock will offer a reasonable window for inspection.
- Ask about missing pieces early. If there’s a chance of missing tokens or card sleeves, ask for replacements or a discount to cover buying replacements. It’s totally fair to want a complete, playable experience.
- Build a playlist for your first month of play. With 27 titles, you’ll likely have enough content for multiple weekends. Create a rough schedule that alternates between light, medium, and heavier titles so you don’t burn out on a single vibe.
- Create a “rotation mood board” on your wall or board. Use sticky notes to designate the tone of each game (party, strategy, cooperative, abstract) to help guests navigate the shelf and pick what’s right for the moment.
