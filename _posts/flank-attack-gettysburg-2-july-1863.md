## Setting the Stage

The Battle of Gettysburg was already in full swing when the Confederate General **James Longstreet** decided to execute a bold maneuver. His goal? To outflank the Union Army and secure a decisive victory for the South. Sounds simple, right? Well, hold your horses (or cannons), because Longstreet's plan was anything but straightforward.

### The Plan

Longstreet's strategy was to launch a flank attack on the Union's left, targeting the key positions at **Little Round Top** and **Devil's Den**. If successful, this maneuver would disrupt the Union line and potentially force a retreat. But as any seasoned war gamer knows, even the best-laid plans can go awry.
