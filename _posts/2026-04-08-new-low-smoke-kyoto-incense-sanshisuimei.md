For those who enjoy a more granular breakdown, imagine the notes arranged in a gentle pyramid: top notes of citrusy brightness and green bergamot, heart notes of sandalwood and cedar, and base notes of amber and musk that give the scent persistence without turning it into a perfume fog. ## Safety, Care, and Practical Tips

Always burn in a well-ventilated area. Even low-smoke blends release particulates, so if you’re sensitive to smoke or have respiratory concerns, keep a window open or use a small fan to direct air away from your face. Place sticks upright in a stable holder, away from papers, fabrics, or electronics that could scorch or catch. Extinguish sticks fully when you’re done; they should cool completely in the tray before you discard them.

If you’re burning multiple sticks, give each one its own moment. A quick rotation prevents scent from colliding and becoming one flat note the next morning. And if you’re streaming or recording, consider doing a quick test flame to avoid the risk of smoke appearing in your video feed by surprise. It’s better to test with a sample stick than to broadcast a sneeze live to your audience.
