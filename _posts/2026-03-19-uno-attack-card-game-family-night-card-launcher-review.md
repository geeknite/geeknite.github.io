### How UNO Attack Works: Rules on a Lightning Bolt

UNO Attack twists the classic UNO rules in three core ways:

1) The launcher adds a randomness layer. Instead of drawing a single card or an amount you can predict, sometimes the launcher burps, spits out a handful of cards, or triggers a cascade that tests your reflexes and your ability to count while laughing.
2) The Draw Pile is a live, unpredictable beast. It can run out quickly, forcing players to rely on Action cards and the occasional luck-based miracle.
3) The Attack cards cause some of the most dramatic moments in the game. When you’re handed an Attack card, you either suffer a penalty or become the target of a wave of random cards that may change the entire pace of the match.

What you’ll notice: the core UNO rules are still there — match color or number, play special cards to change the flow, and aim to be the first to run out of cards. The launcher adds a layer of “holy wow, what did I just draw?” that can swing momentum in the blink of an eye.

### Variants and House Rules

UNO Attack is flexible enough to support a few fan-favorite house rules that keep the energy high while avoiding the never-ending cascade trap. Here are a few ideas you can try:

- The Quick-Draw Round: For the first round, require players to draw only one card when failing to respond to a challenge. If you trigger a cascade, you go to the back of the line, which adds a strategic nerve to the draw.
- The Team Shuffle: Divide players into two teams and require alternating turns within a team. It creates funny dynamics when teammates coordinate with the launcher’s chaos to complete a comeback.
- The No-Launcher Round: To calm the chaos after a few hectic rounds, declare a “sunset” period where the launcher is temporarily disabled and the game reverts to traditional UNO rules. It gives players a chance to reset and re-evaluate their strategies without the beeps and blips constantly demanding your attention.
