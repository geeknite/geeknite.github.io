### Cable Management
The rear cable channel is a welcome feature in a world where cables love to form frantic tangles the moment you close the door. The guides, grommets, and brush inserts help keep the front of the rack presentable while your cabling crew tries to maintain order in the back. ### Ventilation and Cooling
Ventilation is a perennial pain point for many racks. The Avit 37RU includes front ventilation and the option to add side panels for cross-ventilation. In practice, if your equipment runs hot, you’ll want to pair this rack with a modest cooling strategy—think a small AC unit or a smart fan controller—to avoid turning your data room into an improvised sauna. If you’re running high-density servers, consider additional fans, ducting, or a chimney for hot air. The key is airflow: cold air in the front, hot air out the back, with minimal resealing of the room to keep energy bills from turning into a sci-fi novel.

## Real-World Installation Tips
- Measure your space twice; check the door swing and clearance for the rack doors when open. A 600mm square footprint is compact but not invincible against a closing office door.
- Plan your cable routes before you rack gear. Label cables at the outset; the moment you forget to label is the moment you’ll create a tangle worthy of an epic myth.
- Consider the room’s airflow. If you’re in a hot environment, close the doors only after you have proper intake and exhaust strategies. A hot room is a happy place for nothing except fans and frustration.
- If you’re using casters, keep a levelling foot kit handy for corners and uneven floors. It’s one of those small accessories that dramatically changes how stable your setup feels when you start adding weight.
