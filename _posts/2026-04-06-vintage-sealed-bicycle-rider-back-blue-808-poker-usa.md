## Authenticity, Provenance, and the Value Dance
Authenticity matters, especially if you’re chasing a specific variant. The blue 808 is not the rarest Bicycle variant in the wild, but it’s a desirable one, particularly for those who want a sealed, display-worthy deck that still feels robust enough to use during a casual game night.

- Seal authenticity: Look for a factory seal that is intact with the correct alignment and typography from USPCC’s packaging. Any tampering, resealing, or obvious wear can be a red flag.
- Print run knowledge: With vintage decks, the confusion often becomes a game of “which run is this exactly?” The 808 label can refer to specific stock or year ranges. Do your due diligence, compare seal details, and consult collectors’ guides or forums for cross-checks.
- Pro provenance: If you’re buying from a reputable dealer, request clear photos of the seal and the tuck. A credible seller will provide close-ups of the seal, the bottom of the tuck, and any date codes that might be present.

If you’re new to the game, a cautious approach is best: buy from trusted sources, avoid deals that look too good, and use a grading rubric for sealed decks (seal integrity, box wear, and the presence of any moisture or odor that suggests age or improper storage).

## Care and Preservation: Because Great Things Deserve Gentle Treatment
If you’re serious about preserving this particular deck’s value and visual impact, a simple care regimen goes a long way:

- Climate control: Keep the deck out of direct sunlight; UV rays can fade inks and alter color perception. A stable humidity around 40-60% is a comfortable range for most card stocks.
- Handling: When handling sealed decks meant for later opening, avoid brute force. Gentle pressure and proper support help maintain seal integrity.
- Display: If you plan to show this deck off, store it in a display case with a glass cover that blocks dust and minimizes accidental handling.
- Documentation: Maintain a small note about where you acquired the deck, approximate year, and current market conditions. This makes future resale or trades easier and more credible.
