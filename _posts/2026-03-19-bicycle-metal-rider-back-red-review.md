## Care, storage, and presentation tips

- Store sealed if you want maximum preservation, as the foil and metallic pigments respond best to controlled humidity and temperature. 
- If you plan to use the deck, a simple card case is enough. Avoid exposing cards to direct sunlight for long periods; while the dark chrome look is cool, prolonged sun exposure can lead to color fading of the red accents.
- Use a microfiber cloth to wipe any dust or fingerprints from the tuck box. The box has that metallic sheen that loves a gentle wipe down with a dry cloth—no harsh cleaners; you are not buffing a vehicle, you’re caring for a work of art.
- When displaying, a glass cabinet with LED lighting can highlight the red glow and metallic finish. If you don’t have a cabinet, a well-lit shelf will still do justice to the deck’s design. The goal is to show off the back pattern and red hue without glare that makes it hard to appreciate details.
- For performance, break in the deck with a few shuffles before a show. You want that familiar Bicycle feel, but you also want to experience how the added weight and texture interact with your technique. Slightly dampen your practice time if you’re learning advanced flourishes—your future self will thank you when your cards don’t cling together at the worst possible moment.
