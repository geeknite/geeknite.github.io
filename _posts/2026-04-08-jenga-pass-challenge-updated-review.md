### How to play

The general arc of Jenga remains intact: remove one block from the tower and stack it on top. The twist is the pass mechanic and the social pressure that comes with it. The rules are intentionally lean, making it accessible to kids while still offering avenues for cunning adults to flex.

#### Setup
- Build the tower with all 54 blocks, placing each layer of 3 blocks perpendicular to the previous layer. The box base is built to cradle the tower so it can be passed with a bit of dignity.
- Decide the play order. In practice, the game becomes a rapid-fire relay, where players must be mentally prepared to react fast as the tower leans toward their neighbor.

#### Your turn
- On your turn, you remove one block from anywhere below the highest completed layer and place it on top, completing the current layer as you go.
- If the block removal results in a tower that topples, you lose the round. In the Pass Challenge variant, your loss is often less about the block and more about your neighbors' laughter and taunting.

#### The pass twist
- After your move, the tower must be passed to the next player in a manner that is safe and friendly. The passing is a built-in time pressure; players must guide the tower to the next seat without letting gravity do the heavy lifting for them.
- If you drop the block or fumble the pass, penalties can be added. The penalties are mostly good-natured, such as singing a silly song or telling a quick joke about your own gaming anxieties.

#### Endgame
- The round ends when the tower falls and a player is declared the loser or the hero depending on your mood. In family settings, the goal is to have fun more than to win. The thrill of a tower that barely clings to the top while a chorus of oohs and ahs erupts around the circle is what the game is all about.

### The geeky side: why the tower behaves
If you are the kind of person who imagines Reynolds numbers and center of gravity while assembling LEGO, you will enjoy the physics of Jenga. The tower is a delicate system where removing blocks changes the center of gravity with every turn. A slight miscalculation can lead to an elegant collapse — a moment that feels like a tiny earthquake at the scale of a coffee table. The Jenga Pass Challenge adds another layer to this physics mimicking a social dynamical system where additional random variables such as passer timing and spectator laughter influence the outcome.

### Age range and accessibility

This edition is marketed for ages 6 and up, which is a sensible range given the coarse-grained motor skills involved in block removal. For younger players, a cooperative house rule can be implemented: players take turns removing blocks with the consensus of the table and pass the tower in a careful manner to minimize accidental chaos.

If someone in the group has a hearing impairment or prefers nonverbal communication, you can adjust the pass rules to focus on gestures rather than voice feedback. The goal is to create a game that is inclusive and fun for all participants.

### Strategy tips for max fun

- Read the rhythm of the table: If the room grows quiet after a block is pulled, that is your cue to pause and anticipate the pass. The tempo matters in this variant; a hurried pass often leads to a dramatic tumble.
- Block selection isn't about power moves; it s about finding blocks that are easy to remove with minimal disturbance to the rest of the tower. In the updated edition, the feel of each block is more consistent; use this to your advantage.
- Pass etiquette matters: Remember to communicate clearly as you pass. A good-natured comment about your own performance tends to soften the blow of a near collapse.
- Use your surroundings: If you are playing on a couch or a coffee table, the surrounding noise can contribute to the humor. Lean into it.
