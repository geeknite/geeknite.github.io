## Material & Craft: Why New Zealand Wool Matters
New Zealand wool is the MVP of the textile field—soft, resilient, and stubborn in all the right ways. It’s the kind of fiber that can survive a kid’s scooter antics in the living room and still come out looking fresh as a daisy. In this rug, the wool is hand-tufted rather than machine-loomed, which is a statement in a world of speed-run production lines.

### The Science of Softness
Why does hand-tufting matter? Because it places each tufts’ height and density with human judgment rather than an autopilot. The process creates a texture that feels plush under bare feet but still responds to everyday wear. NZ wool’s natural crimp gives resilience; you’ll get a surface that springs back after compression rather than looking like a wrinkled coffee cup as soon as you stand up. Think of it as a gym routine for fibers: repeatable, controlled, and a little bit dramatic.

### A Studio-Grade Backing
Behind every tuft is a sturdy base that keeps the arcs crisp and the fibers aligned. The backing in this rug is designed to handle traffic without pilling or wobbling. It’s not a flimsy mat; it’s a structure—like a small architectural element for your floor, ensuring the arcs retain their geometry even after a few seasons of living-room shenanigans.

### Colorfastness & Longevity
New Zealand wool tends to take dye well, which translates into colorways that stay bold for longer. In the right lighting, those arcs glow like a vector graphic you didn’t know you needed in your life. The colorfastness also means fewer worries about sun glare and room lighting washing out the contrast between arcs. If you’re chasing a modern palette that pops without shouting, NZ wool is your friend.

Rug size matters, and this hand-tufted NZ wool rug comes in multiple dimensions to fit different rooms. Here are practical guidelines for choosing and placing:

### Vacuuming and Cleaning
- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only attachment to avoid loosening the tufts.
- Attend spills quickly; blot, don’t rub, to prevent fiber distortion. NZ wool resists staining better than you’d expect, but it’s not miracle-stain-proof.
- For deeper cleaning, professional rug cleaners familiar with hand-tufted wool can help keep the fibers looking sharp.

## Care: Keeping the Arcs Sharp Over Time
- Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting; avoid aggressive bristle attachments.
- Rotate the rug 180 degrees every few months to distribute wear evenly across arcs.

- Protect from prolonged direct sun to minimize color fading in sensitive tones.
