### Painting strategy: tips from the Geeknite tub
- Prime with a neutral gray or black to maximize the depth of crevices. Mortimer’s details are fine; you’ll want the shadows to do some work for you.
- Use a midtone midtone wash (Nuln Oil, Agrax Earthshade, etc.) to bring out cloth folds and sculpted muscle ridges. Let gravity do the work for you; don’t drown the whole thing.
- Layer highlights on edges of cloak and armor—think thin lines, not surfboard-white edges. The goal is subtle, not neon signal flags.
- Pick a kitchen-sink color for Mortimer’s accent: a rusty metal for the blade or a sickly green for a magical aura works wonders. Mortimer is the kind of model where your color palette choice sets the mood: grimdark or “seasoned gamer with a coffee addiction.”
- Matte seal once dry. The last thing you want is a gloss over Mortimer that makes him look like a plastic moose on a display shelf.
