## How Phase 10 Works: A Quick Refresher

Phase 10 is a rummy-like game where each player tries to complete a set of phases in order. A phase is a specific arrangement of cards you must lay down to advance to the next stage. A phase is achieved when your cards meet the required pattern, such as two sets of three cards and a run of four, and you lay them down in front of you. The catch: you don’t repeat phases until you finish the current one. That means you might march through Phase 1 twice if you’re unlucky or incredibly entertaining (depending on your friend group). The classic deck includes 108 cards, four colors, multiple phase cards, wild cards that can be used almost anywhere, and skip cards that prevent your arch-nemesis from acting. The Deluxe tin version does not alter the core mechanics; it simply gives you a more elegant way to stash your chaos.

Here is a micro-guide to how your nights typically unfold:

- Draw a card at the start of your turn, then discard one. The standard cycle.
- Try to assemble your current phase: for example, Phase 1 might be two sets of three, or two runs of four cards each (depending on the edition you are using).
- When you complete the phase, you lay it down and advance to the next one. If you can’t complete it, you keep digging and occasionally cursing at your own bad luck.
- The game ends when a player completes Phase 10, or when the supply depletes. The winner is the one with the lowest score after the closing hand.

## Strategy and Play Styles: Getting the Most from Phase 10

Phase 10 is not a complex game to learn, but like any strategy blend, you’ll get more out of it as you play more rounds.
