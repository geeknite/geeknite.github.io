## Maintenance and care

### Cleaning and storage tips

Plastic chips are easy to clean: a quick wipe with a damp cloth, a gentle dry, and they’re as good as new for the next night. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the color or the printed denominations. If you notice any chips developing chips themselves, that’s a sign you might be stacking a bit too aggressively at 11 PM after a long day at work. Gentle handling, stacked properly, will keep the colors bright and the edges intact.

For cards, a light wipe and proper air drying help prevent sticking or warping. Don’t leave decks out in direct sun or in a hot car; it’s amazing how quickly the cards will turn a pancake-shape and refuse to stay square during a serious hand.

### Long-term care tips

Rotation is your friend. If you’re playing weekly, rotate chips in the pot so wear is evenly distributed, and you won’t get a handful of chips that feel significantly lighter than others. This is not just about aesthetics; it helps maintain a fair feel for players who may be very tuned to the weight and balance of their chips.
