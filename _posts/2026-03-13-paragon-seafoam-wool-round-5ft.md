## Care, maintenance, and longevity
 Wool rugs demand a bit more respect than their synthetic cousins, but the payoff is longevity and character. Here’s a practical care plan:

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only attachment. Avoid beater bars that can pull wool fibers and create fuzz. A gentle pass every week or two keeps dirt from embedding deep into the pile.
- Rotate occasionally. Wool fibers don’t wear evenly in high-traffic paths. A gentle quarterly rotation helps the pile wear more evenly and extends the life of the rug.
- Spot cleaning: blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Use a mild detergent and water for stubborn spots, then blot again to remove moisture. Avoid harsh solvents that could affect color or fiber integrity.
- Sun exposure: wool can fade over time with strong sun, so position the rug away from direct bright light when possible or use drapes and UV-protective coatings.
- Underlay: use a non-slip rug pad to keep the rug in place and protect your hardwood or tile from scratches. A good pad also reduces bunching and helps with longevity by distributing foot traffic more evenly.
