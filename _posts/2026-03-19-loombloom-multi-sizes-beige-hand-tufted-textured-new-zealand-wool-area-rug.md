## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Cleaning Essentials
- Regular vacuuming with a beater brush set to a gentle setting helps keep dirt from embedding in the tufted texture.
- Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing pushes the liquid deeper into the fibers.
- For minor stains, use a wool-safe detergent diluted with water. Test on an inconspicuous area first.
- Professional cleaning every 12–18 months (depending on foot traffic) can preserve luster and texture. If you have pets or children, consider more frequent cleaning.

### Spot-Care Pitfalls to Avoid
- Avoid bleach or solvents that can breakdown wool fibers.
- Avoid hot water on wool rugs; it can cause wool to felt and lose its texture.
- Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods to minimize potential fading of the beige tone.

### Underlay and Placement Tips
- A quality rug pad is worth the investment. It helps with stability, reduces wear, and keeps the rug in place on slippery flooring.
- If your space has doorways, ensure the rug doesn’t interfere with opening and closing. Multi-size options can help tailor placement precisely to traffic patterns.
