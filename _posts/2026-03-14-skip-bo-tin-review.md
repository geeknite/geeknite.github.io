## How to Play: A Quick Primer (for People with Short Attention Spans)

Skip-Bo is a race to empty your stock pile by building stacks in ascending order. Each turn you may play cards from your hand to the four build piles, or use wild cards to replace missing numbers in a sequence. The strategic complexity comes from the timing of when to play from your stock pile versus building piles and when to throw away cards that the other player might steal from you in the next move. In two-player mode, the tempo is intimate, and the stakes feel personal, even if the deck is just a pile of cardboard and your imagination.

- Objective: Be the first to move all cards from your stock pile to the four build piles in order from 1 to 12, using any sequence of cards from your hand and from the discard piles as needed.
- Turn flow: Draw to five cards, then play any card to a build pile. You can also move from your stock pile if the opportunity presents itself. If you cannot play, discard from your hand and pass to your opponent—setting up future drama and the possibility of a silly brag when you finally triumph.
- Wilds: Skip-Bo wild cards can stand in for any number. They are invaluable for bridging awkward gaps, but use them with restraint. Saving the last wild for the moment you actually win is a pro move, while wasting it on a near-miss can be a heartbreaker.

### Variant: The Two-Player Tightrope

In two-player games, consider these tactics:
- Timing of wilds: When to use a wild to complete a build vs saving it for a future turn is a constant mental exercise. You want to balance aggression with risk management.
- Build pile tempo: Four build piles on the table create a constant rhythm. If you can predict your opponent’s moves, you can manipulate the tempo to force suboptimal plays.
- Stock pile discipline: Your stock pile is your lifeblood. Don’t unleash the entire reservoir at once unless you’ve calculated the optimal moment. The joy of victory comes not from dumping a bunch of cards but from the satisfying feeling of cleanly moving the last card from your stock pile to a build pile.
