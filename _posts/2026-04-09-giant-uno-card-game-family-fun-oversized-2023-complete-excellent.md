## How to Play: The Basics and Giant Card Quirks
At its core, this is UNO. You match color or number and play action cards to disrupt the flow. The giant format modifies a few steps to accommodate the scale.
- Setup: Deal out the appropriate number of cards to each player. With oversized cards, shuffling becomes a bit of a performance, but it is a fun one.
- On your turn: You’ll aim to match color or number, play a Skip, Reverse, or Draw Two to tilt the balance, or throw a Wild into the mix to swing the game toward a dramatic finish.
- Wilds and color strategy: Wild cards in giant UNO still permit color changes, but the bigger format means you’ll often plan several moves ahead, and your color forecasting might involve your entire family guessing what color you are likely to switch to next.
