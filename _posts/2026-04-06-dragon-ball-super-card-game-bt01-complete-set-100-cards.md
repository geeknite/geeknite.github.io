## BT01 Overview

BT01 stands for... technically, 'Booster Set 01'—the first big content dump after the base game. In Dragon Ball Super Card Game, these boosters introduce new characters, 'leaders', and a flurry of synergy mechanics that can break your deck if you blink. The BT01 Complete Set brings 100 cards to your table: a mixed bag of staples, new boss units, keyword-crazy cards, and — importantly — some of the most meme-worthy art in the DBSCG catalog.

### What’s included

- 100 distinct cards across a handful of rarities, with a few chase cards that will make your local meta scream.
- A couple of 'leader' cards that restructure the way you swing the battlefield.
- A soundtrack of new mechanics: awakening costs, keyword interactions, and some sneaky counters to your favorite archetypes.

> Quick note: if you’re literally chasing every single card in BT01, this box is your starting point. If you’re building a specific deck, you might want to cherry-pick pieces rather than loot-crate your pockets with all 100 cards. More on that in a bit.

### Card types you’ll see

- Leaders and Battle Cards: the bread and butter of DBSCG. Leaders shape your deck’s strategy and provide a lead-in to enormous plays.
- Extra and Secret Rares: they spice up the board state and can be clutch in the late game.
- Common and Uncommon: the soldiers that fill your early game and enable your power spikes.

## Gameplay implications: how BT01 changes the meta

If you’ve been playing with the base set or earlier boosters, BT01 will feel like stepping into a new dimension where the gravity is heavier and your opponent’s best card is always two moves ahead. The BT01 set rewards players who plan two or three turns ahead and who can pivot when the board state shifts unexpectedly.

- Early game tempo: BT01 provides cards that help you squeeze speed into the early turns without losing your guard. You’ll see a lot of micro-decisions about when to draw, when to play a blocker, and when to push for a lethal swing.
- Mid to late game: awakened leaders begin to show their true faces, turning otherwise ordinary turns into potential momentum swings. The cards here often scale with the board, turning a small lead into a roaring victory—if you don’t misread the counter-play.
- Counterplay: all that power comes with answers. The set includes counters that can mitigate heavy beatdown and force your opponent into suboptimal plays. But beware—the more synergy you rely on, the more you risk getting blown up by a single well-timed counter.

### Deck-building tips with BT01

- Start with a core strategy: decide if you want to go aggro, control, or a hybrid. BT01 supports several archetypes, but you’ll maximize your win probability if you pick a plan and stack your 60-70 cards around it.
- Balance your curve: mix early-game pressure with mid-to-late-game power cards. Avoid a mono-curve deck that bricks in the second phase of the game.
- Protect your leaders: with new awakening cards, you’ll want to schedule your resources so you can awaken on your terms, not your opponent’s.
- Budget vs. power: not every card is a must-pick. Some of the best BT01 plays come from a handful of cards that enable multiple lines of play. If you’re price-conscious, identify a core core and fill the gaps with flexible options.
