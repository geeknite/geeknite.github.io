## Collectibility, Grading, and Condition

As with any rare holo, the central question is: is it worth the investment? Here are some practical guidelines:

- Condition matters more than you think. Holo AR variants reflect light in unpredictable ways; the surface scuffs, edge nicks, and centering will influence grading outcomes.
- Grading agencies look for an authentic print line and proper card stock shimmer. A card with perfect centering and an even holo pattern often scores higher than a card with minor scratches but a stunning flame glow.
- The Japanese edition may command a premium, especially for SV3 holo variants with unique back-of-card text or limited-run print marks.

### Authentication Tips
- Check for crisp Japanese typography and accurate alignment of the flavor text lines. If the text is smeared or misaligned, that’s a red flag.
- Inspect the holo foil. A true AR pattern returns in the same light each time but shifts with tilt; fake foils are often inconsistent in the way the sheen scans across the surface.
- Compare the card’s weight and thickness to a known authentic sample; fakes are often a tiny bit off in mass.

## How to Store, Care, and Display

Since you’ve already read this far, you’ll want to protect the card from the villain of every collector’s life: sunlight. UV light will fade the vivid purples and dangerous-lovely golden flecks. Invest in a high-quality binder with inner pages or toploaders with UV protection. A soft microfiber cloth helps you wipe away any accidental fingerprints from the holo pattern, and a silica gel packet keeps the binder dry in case of humidity.

If you’re feeling fancy, display the card under a lightbox to emphasize the AR holo’s shimmer. You’ll be the envy of your gaming group when you can show a subtle purple-to-copper shift as you flip to the page.
