## Why an Oval Shape Matters
The geometry of a poker table is not just aesthetic; it changes reach, pacing, and social dynamics. The oval shape lets players slide chips and cards toward the center without one player acting as a traffic cone in the middle of the action. You get a more inclusive feel since no one has to lean across the table like they are negotiating a treaty with the salad server. In practical terms, you can reach everyone more easily and you cut down on the dreaded "table edges advantage" that sometimes crops up on long rectangular tables.

For those who enjoy a sci fi metaphor, the oval is basically the starship bridge of poker tables. It keeps the action in the center while giving each crew member a comfortable seat and a place to rest your elbow that does not double as a temporary garbage bin for chips you promptly lose under the seat cushions.

## Setup and Maintenance Tips
- Unpack the table and lay it on a flat surface. Avoid carpeted floors unless you are okay with wobble noises that sound suspiciously like a bored audience at a late-night show.
- Attach the folding legs in a cross pattern so the table sits evenly. Use a flat head screwdriver to snug the screws, but do not over tighten as that can crack the rail or the top.
- If your model includes a felt insert or replaceable top sheet, install it before you play. It helps keep chips from rolling off and blocks the inevitable stray cards from wandering into the kitchen.
- Clean spills quickly with a damp cloth. Avoid using harsh chemicals that can degrade the surface finish. In case of stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution works well.

Long term maintenance includes vacuuming around the legs to keep chips from getting stuck, and periodically checking the leg anchors for looseness. If you hear wobble or see the table tilt when you lean, tighten the leg screws a little more. It is not a good sign when your table becomes a pinball machine during the river.
