## Preservation, Storage, and Display
Original packaging is both treasure and trap. It looks fantastic under glass, but it also demands discipline. Here are practical tips for preserving your Abomination Pack while still making it accessible when you want to admire it or pull it out for a game night.

### Climate control and humidity
Humidity is the silent killer of board game boxes. Excess moisture can warp cardboard, bend inserts, and degrade artwork. Keep the box in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. If you live in a humid climate, consider a climate controlled storage area or a dehumidifier. Not cool in the literal sense, but it will save you from a warped cardboard nightmare.

### Handling and display options
If you are a display shelf person, consider a protective case or a dedicated display box. A UV-protective acrylic case is ideal for preserving the outer art while still allowing you to show off the centerpiece and the special insert. If you prefer to actually use the contents, consider removing the inner tray to prevent stress on the plastic and the card stock. But if your aim is to keep the original packaging pristine, resist the urge to rebox everything unless you plan to unpack and rebox with care.

### Display vs store: the geek balance
There is a difference between a display piece and a functional game asset. A sealed original packaging is a display piece with a performance torque. If you plan to play with the contents occasionally, you should open the outer box, inspect the contents, and then decide if you want to rehouse them for transport. The Abomination pack can still be a playable addition, but you might want to decant the components into a more robust storage system to preserve the original box.
