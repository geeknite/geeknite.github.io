## Estimating Value: What Can You Reasonably Expect?
Price is a moving target in bulk land. Here’s a grounded way to think about it without turning your living room into a currency exchange:
- Commons and uncommons: typically low per-card value when bought in bulk, but they have utility. If you’re just sorting for play, these are the bread and butter, the glue that keeps decks together. If you’re price-chasing, you’ll likely pass on most of these unless you’re bagging a rare gem among them.
- Rares: the big swing cards in bulk. Some may be worth a few dollars apiece, especially if they are modern or sought-after in popular formats. A handful can dramatically tilt the overall value of the batch.
- Mythics and foils: the unpredictable wildcard. A modern mythic that sees play in top-tier EDH or standard can fetch a surprising sum, but these are the exceptions rather than the rule.
- Condition and edition matter: a near-mint rare from a legacy set can be far more valuable than a heavily played modern card with identical artwork. Condition often matters more in bulk scenarios than you’d think.

A practical approach is to set a target return you would be happy with and then adjust your expectations as you sort. If you want a rough framework, aim for a modest return on bulk that covers your initial outlay, then view any bigger hits as bonus. It’s not a get-rich-quick scheme, but it can be a satisfying way to turn unexpected finds into playable decks or trade fodder.

## Sorting, Storage, and Salvaging Value
Sorting bulk into recognizable, usable piles is where the magic happens. Here’s a practical, comedic approach you can actually follow at home:

### Stage 1: The Quick Visual Pass
- Flag obvious gems: modern rares, mythics, and any obvious foils. Put these into a special hit pile that you’ll price first.
- Separate obvious junk: heavily damaged cards, water-damaged cards, or obviously counterfeit-looking items. If it looks questionable, set it aside for a second opinion.
- Create a bulk bucket: the rest goes here. You’ll return to this later with a more discerning eye.

### Stage 2: Set-by-Set, Then Color-by-Color
Sorting by set helps with price checks, but organizing by color can be more fun for deck-building aspirations. If you’re into commander or cube creation, color-coded stacks will save you hours of rummaging later.

### Stage 3: Value-Ready Sorting
This is where you separate potential singles from pure bulk. Create a keep-for-trade or sell pile and a separate bulk pile for games, charity drives, or donation. Label clearly so you don’t confuse a $2 uncommon with a $0.02 common you’re ready to toss back in the bin.

### Tools You’ll Need
- Stackable storage bins with lids to keep stacks organized
- Card sleeves and top loaders for value hits
- A scale if you decide to price by bulk weight
- A binder or card boxes for easy access to sorted piles
- A durable workspace that doesn’t fold under a pile of 1000+ cards

### Handling and Condition Tips
- Separate heavily played from near mint. Condition matters for price, especially for rares and foils.
- Protect the cards from moisture and direct sunlight. Humidity can warp lines and curling can ruin edges. Your future back will thank you.
- Keep track of your inventory as you sort. A simple spreadsheet or a dedicated app can pay for itself in saved hours and miscounts.
