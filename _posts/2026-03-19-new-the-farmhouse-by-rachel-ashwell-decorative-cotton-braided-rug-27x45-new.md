Practicality, Durability, and Care

H2: Everyday Wear and Tear: What You Should Expect
The Farmhouse rug is designed for real life, which means it should handle the usual suspects: coffee rings, pet footprints, and a stray remote that somehow migrates to the far corner of the couch. H3: Cleaning and Maintenance: A Practical Guide
Cleaning a cotton braided rug is more “mindful bath” than “full spa day.” Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps prevent dirt from burying itself into the braided crevices. If you spill something substantial, blot—don’t rub!—with a clean cloth to avoid pushing liquids deeper into the weave. For tougher stains, test a mild detergent solution in a small inconspicuous area before committing to the entire surface. The Farmhouse responds well to a light, regular refresh rather than aggressive scrubbing. And if you own a pet with a gravity-challenged tail, you’ll be grateful that the braids won’t instantly turn into a tangled rope when a dramatic wag happens nearby.
