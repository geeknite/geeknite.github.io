## What you actually get in the 2013 Holiday Expansion Pack

The packaging promises 30 sealed cards. In practice, that means a tidy little chunk of content you can shuffle into your existing CAH deck with minimal fuss. The exact distribution isn’t widely advertised in a single product page, but you can expect a mix of prompts and responses that lean into holiday memes, seasonal sarcasm, and the kind of humor that CAH fans either adore or defend with a shrug at family reunions.

What makes a holiday expansion feel special is not just the novelty factor but how it blends with the tone of standard CAH gameplay. You want lines that hit the sweet spot between clever and savage, with enough pop culture reference to be instantly recognizable but not so obscure that players spend half the night with a glossary of memes. The 2013 set aims for that balance with a handful of new bits that reference winter vibes, gift-giving horrors, and the joy of an office party you wish you could uninvite yourself from.

The holiday pack adds new flavors to both sides: a few fresh black cards that set up holiday-specific prompts, and a set of white cards with one-liner punchlines that work well against the prompts you’ll encounter during a regular game.

## How to integrate the Holiday Expansion into your play style

### Quick setup and play flow

1. Shuffle the 30 sealed cards into your existing CAH deck. If you’re playing with a group that loves to hoard the best lines, you can keep the pack separate and only draw a few cards per round to preserve the surprise.
2. Decide whether you want to keep the full pool of cards in rotation or rotate them in only during the holiday season. The latter keeps the novelty fresh without cluttering your deck permanently.
3. Introduce the new prompts and responses naturally. The holiday flavor works best when it’s not forced—let the moment and the group dynamics dictate when to pull from the holiday pack.
4. If your table tends to swing between edgy and offbeat humor, try pairing some of the holiday prompts with standard white cards that reflect the group’s vibe. You’ll be surprised how a holiday mischief line can pair with a more normalization-based white card to yield unexpectedly clever outcomes.
