## Custom size versus 8x10: two routes to rug enlightenment

### Why choose a custom size?
Custom sizing is the holy grail for rooms that don’t conform to standard dimensions. You have an awkwardly shaped living area? A long, narrow hallway? A dining nook that must accommodate a rectangular table without turning into a “pull the chair in and out of the carpet trench” situation? Custom size lets you tailor the rug to fit precisely, which can make a room feel larger and more cohesive. It also reduces the risk of that awkward “too much carpet, not enough furniture” look where the rug becomes a floating plane of despair in the center of the room.

The downsides? Time and price. Custom orders typically require additional production time and, yes, a premium. You’ll also want to measure with the precision of a NASA engineer: length, width, and every corner a tiny bit imperfect but in a good way, because real rooms aren’t perfectly square. If your space has built-ins or a fireplace hearth that juts out like a moody ex, custom sizing helps you align rug edges with architectural features so the space feels intentional rather than accidental.

### When 8x10 makes sense
The 8x10 standard is the classic go-to for most living rooms and large bedrooms. It hits a lot of common layouts with architectural ease: under a coffee table, peeking out beneath a couch, and leaving enough room for a couple of accent chairs without creating a zebra-stripe border around the furniture legs. The 8x10 is also typically faster to ship and easier on the budget, which means you can spend more on snacks for your next rug photoshoot or, you know, a new lamp you actually need.

With Eden Shadow, the 8x10 option provides a reliable anchor for room proportions. It gives the space a grounded, grown-up feel while still leaving you room to breathe. If your space is unusually shaped, you’ll likely lean toward custom sizing; if your room is fairly standard, the 8x10 can be the worry-free workhorse you didn’t know you needed.

### Practical considerations for choosing between both routes
- Room function: high-traffic living rooms benefit from durability of wool paired with a size that underpins seating groups. A too-small rug can visually shrink a room; a rug that’s too big can swallow a space.
- Furniture layout: a rug should “sit” under at least the front legs of major furniture or be large enough to anchor the seating area. Custom sizing helps if you want a perfect underlay for an asymmetric couch or a sectional with unusual dimensions.
- Maintenance footprint: heavier rugs can be harder to move for cleaning, but wool tends to be resilient and easier to lift with proper care.
- Budget and timeline: custom orders carry a longer wait. If you’re staging a room for a move or an event, the 8x10 option is probably the pragmatic pick.

To get the best of both worlds, some shoppers order the 8x10 and then augment with custom runners in adjacent hallways or entryways. It’s like wearing a tux with sneakers: stylish, functional, and a tiny bit rebellious.

## Care and maintenance: keep the shadows sharp

### Vacuuming routine
Vacuum once or twice a week in high-traffic zones, and as needed elsewhere. Use a vacuum with a good beater bar setting or a gentle suction mode to avoid pulling on fibers. Wool’s resilience shines when you keep shedding under control rather than letting it accumulate like a cozy tumbleweed.

### Spills and stains
Act quickly. Blot with a clean, dry cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible, then use a mild cleaning solution if needed. Avoid harsh chemicals; they can affect the wool’s integrity and color depth. If the stain persists, consider a professional cleaning service with experience in wool rugs.

### Rotation and reshaping
Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if your sunlit window spots cause uneven fading. This keeps the Eden Shadow looking consistent from season to season.

### Long-term care notes
Wool rugs can bloom over time, meaning the fibers may rise slightly with use. A light grooming with a soft brush can help smooth the surface after heavy traffic or after pet play. If you’re concerned about shedding, a low-pile vacuum is less likely to grip loose fibers while still refreshing the surface texture.
