## Maintenance and Cleaning
### Daily Care
A quick weekly vacuum with a soft attachment keeps dust at bay without disturbing the weave. Because this is a flat weave product, you do not have to chase a fluffy pile around the room; instead you maintain a simple routine that respects the fiber orientation. For minor spills, blot with a clean cloth and use a mild cleaner designed for delicate textiles. Do not rub vigorously, as that can misalign fibers over time.

### Stains and Deep Cleaning
Stains should be treated as soon as possible. Blot to remove excess liquid and use a gentle solvent if needed, testing in a hidden spot first. For stubborn marks, a short cycle of professional cleaning is wise, especially in high traffic zones. After cleaning, allow the rug to dry completely before resuming use to prevent odor development or mold growth in damp fibers. With proper care, this rug will stay looking calm and clean through seasons of foot traffic and pet patrols.

## The Geeky Tech of Rug Weave
### Fiber and Weave 101
Flat weave is all about structure. The technique uses a simple over under pattern that yields a flat surface free of piling. The fibers remain close to the ground which reduces the accumulation of dirt and gives the rug a crisp, refined finish. Flat weave rugs wear differently from pile rugs; they show wear in a more linear pattern that often reads as character rather than damage. The Montauk tests well in terms of durability while maintaining a comfortable feel underfoot.

### Underfoot Comfort and Padding
If you want extra stability, pair this rug with a non slip pad to prevent movement on hard floors. The underlay also reduces noise such as footfall in small spaces. In workspaces or studio apartments this little trick can feel like a small victory every morning, especially when the coffee machine is whirring in the next room.
