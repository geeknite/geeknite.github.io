## Handling and playability in practice
Handling a pair of sealed 808 Rider Back decks isn’t about auditioning a new fairness protocol; it’s about evaluating how the stock and finish behave in real-world scenarios: shuffles, fans, and the ever-ubiquitous card trick.

### Shuffle test
- Overhand shuffle: smooth with each pass, a little resistance when you want a tight break, which is ideal for controlled dealing in magic routines.
- Riffle shuffle: the classic tester. The 808 stock accepts a good riffling without warping or giving you spongy feel. It’s not as glassy as some premium lines, but it’s reliable and predictable. Your fans will appreciate the ease of motion rather than fighting the deck.
- Bridge and hydraulic shuffles: if you’re into fancy cardistry, these decks respond well to flourish-oriented moves. Not a miracle material, but enough snap and spring-back to look impressive on camera.

### Fan and grip
The combined effect of finish and weight makes for a dependable fan. The edges aren’t razor-sharp, which is a plus for new players who worry about tearing corners. Pros with quick-fire fan sequences will enjoy the stable return and the crisp feel at the fingertips.

### Durability in the wild
Durability is one of those topics that sounds boring until you’ve had a deck for a few weeks of weekly games. The 808 finish handles shuffling wear and minor handling scuffs with reasonable resilience. You’ll notice the usual edge wear after a couple dozen reveals, but the interior card life remains comfortable, with pips and indexes staying legible.

## Durability and maintenance
Durability matters when you’re buying in bulk for a game night or a trick repertoire. The two decks in this package should hold up well under moderate use. Here are practical tips to maximize life:

- Keep the wrap; sealing the decks after use protects against humidity and helps keep the stock stable during transport.
