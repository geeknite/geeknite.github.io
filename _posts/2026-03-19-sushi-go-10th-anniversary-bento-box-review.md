## How to Play with the Anniversary Edition
### A quick refresher for new players
Sushi Go is a drafting game. Each round, you choose a card from your hand and pass the rest to your neighbor. Keep the cards that score you points according to a simple yet surprisingly strategic scoring system. The 10th Anniversary edition does not alter core rules, but a few new cards add more ways to chain combos. Expect a couple of new tricks that reward you for planning multiple rounds ahead rather than simply snapping up all the Nigiri you can see.

### Sample round explained
- You draw a hand of five cards
- You pick one and pass the rest
- The round ends, and you score by categories: Nigiri, Tempura, Sashimi, Dumplings, Maki, Pudding
- The player with the most points wins the round or, in a tournament setting, the series of rounds.

### The learning curve
If you have never played Sushi Go, expect a gentle ramp. If you have played once or twice, you will notice the new cards add optional complexity that rewards memory and prediction more than raw luck. If you bring this to a group with kids or non-gamers, you will find that the quick rounds and cute art make it a friendly introduction to drafting games without scaring off your grandmother.
