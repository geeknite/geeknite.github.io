## Care and maintenance: keeping the blue baby bright without turning it into a science experiment
Caring for a vintage hand-knotted rug is a ritual, not a chore. Here are practical guidelines that respect the fibers and the story they tell:
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting and a low-suction hand attachment. Avoid beater bars that attack the pile. Pro tip: vacuum in the direction of the nap to minimize wear in a given area.
- Rotate seasonally to ensure even exposure to sunlight and foot traffic. This helps prevent concentrated fading or wear in any one zone.
- Spot clean with a mild detergent and cold water. Blot gently; do not rub, which can fray fibers and alter the pile direction.
- Professional cleaning every 1 to 3 decades, depending on use and exposure. Vintage pieces deserve a spa day, not a cat-scratching session.
- Use rug pads to reduce slippage and extend the life of the fibers. A good pad can dramatically improve the rug’s stability and reduce wear on a single high-traffic line.

## How to buy and what to watch for in a vintage Kashmar rug
When you are shopping for a vintage Kashmar rug, you are shopping for history and a tactile experience. Here are practical signals to help you evaluate profitability, authenticity, and fit:
- Inspect the pile in multiple lighting conditions. A good rug should reveal pattern details in daylight and lamplight alike.
- Look for uniformity with the rug’s field and motifs. Some variation is expected in vintage pieces, but glaring misalignments can be a sign of later alterations.
- Check the back for knot consistency. If you can trace the design cleanly on the reverse side, you are likely looking at a well-made piece. If the back resembles a tangle, you may be dealing with a mass-produced reproduction.
- Ask about the rug’s origin and era. A reputable seller should be able to provide provenance or at least a credible origin story.
- Inspect the wool for softness, resilience, and color bleed. A well-tended wool should feel lush and responsive to touch, not rough and dry.

A vintage rug is not a disposable streaming service; it is a long-term investment in character. Caring for it is not a fear test but a tenured friendship.
