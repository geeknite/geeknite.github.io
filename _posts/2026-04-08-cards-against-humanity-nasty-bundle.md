## Gameplay Experience: How It Fits Into Your Night
### Speed, Rhythm, and the First Few Rounds
A CAH night typically hinges on the rhythm of prompt-read, guess-the-judge, and two or three strong punchlines, in roughly 25-minute bursts. The Nasty Bundle doesn’t require a radical change to that flow; instead, it introduces heavier prompts that reward risk and clever social alignment. The prompts can be longer, which means the judge’s timing matters more than ever. You’ll want the room to be comfortable with a bit of improv; if you rely on a quick, punchy line, some prompts will demand a half-second of setup before the payoff lands. That can be glorious if your group loves the extended build; it can slow the tempo if your party is a “throw it and see” crowd. In practice, the best rounds became the ones where someone pivoted to a misdirection that turned a card you’d expect to be average into a rising-banana of a moment—everybody gasping and then cackling as the table erupts.

### The Balance Question: More Cards, More Power? 
One common concern with any expansion is whether the new cards drown out the classic humor that people actually love. The Nasty Bundle manages to balance this by not replacing the core deck but augmenting it. The base prompts still carry weight; they remain your reliable entry point for the night. The new prompts fuel the higher-intensity moments, which means you can mix-and-match according to mood. In practice, you’ll likely rotate the newly added cards with some time-tested staples, letting veterans show off their “unhinged but brilliant” riffs while newer players have a chance to find their comedic voice within the chaos. The result is a game that scales with your crowd: more edgy lines with a seasoned table, or a milder entry in a smaller, mixed group.

### The Judge’s Burden: Read-Alouds and Delivery
Delivery is king in CAH—how you Present the line matters almost as much as the line itself. The Nasty Bundle nudges players toward more dramatic readings, which can be part of the fun or a drag if your group’s public speaking is more “squeaky wheel” than “improv genius.” You’ll notice that the worst cards shine when delivered with bold confidence and a pinch of theatricality. Some players can turn a single card into an entire short performance piece; others might prefer a quick one-liner. The bundle supports both modes, which makes it a versatile choice for some groups and a potential speed-bump for others depending on your judge rotation and the mood of the night.
