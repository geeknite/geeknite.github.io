## Overview

Fortune Row is billed as a scenario pack that leans into the glamorous grit of a city that never sleeps, while slinging mythic undercurrents that remind you the city is a myth wearing a designer suit. It wants to be a self-contained campaign arc, with a tight first act, a mid-session escalation, and a finale that can tilt based on the choices players make. The vibe is noir, a pinch of caper, and a splash of legend—the kind of mix City of Mist fans crave when they want a night that feels cinematic rather than procedural.

At its heart, Fortune Row presents a central mystery that revolves around a string of seemingly coincidental events that are, in truth, connected by a single mythic thread. The threads tie to a handful of Masks the players can embody or encounter—every scene looks to test player agency about what it means to be a hero with a secret, and what it costs to keep that secret when the city is whispering that it already knows who you are.

### What is Fortune Row exactly?

Fortune Row is a compact but richly textured set of scenes, NPCs, clues, and handouts designed for a City of Mist table. It typically includes: a scenario book with step-by-step beats, NPC stat blocks that fit the Masks system, clue cards and location sheets, printable handouts, and a handful of GM aids such as a mini map and a scene flow chart. The pack aims to be run over a single long session or split into two shorter sessions, with enough branching to reward players for thinking outside the box while still giving the GM a clear path to steer and stage-set the mood.

If you are a GM who loves giving players enough breadcrumbs to theorize, Fortune Row will feel like a delicious puzzle box. If you prefer a strictly linear rail, you may find yourself itching for a few more rails or a stricter escalation ladder. The design intent is definitely to empower improvisation while keeping the mystery coherent enough to avoid fatigue or bewilderment.

## How Fortune Row fits in City of Mist lore

City of Mist situates a city as a paradox: a place where myths wear human faces and ordinary people collide with extraordinary powers. Fortune Row leans into this by presenting a case that isn’t just about beating a villain; it’s about understanding how myth operates in a modern metropolis and what people are willing to sacrifice to protect a secret or realize a dream. The scenarios explore themes such as:

- The cost of glory versus the cost of anonymity
- The tension between public identity and private life
- How legends can become curses when misinterpreted by crowds and media
- The fragility of networks that look too powerful to fail

In terms of canon, Fortune Row is not a complete reboot of a city district; it uses well-known City of Mist archetypes—masks, glimmers, crime rings, and mythic totems—while giving a fresh spin on what happens when fortune itself seems to be gaming the city. The plot threads are designed to feel contemporary: power brokers who monetize fear, trusted friends who carry long shadows, and a mystery that could threaten both the city’s soul and the people who call it home.

If you have read our City of Mist primer, you’ll notice the same emphasis on duality and symbolism. Fortune Row reiterates the three-part rhythm of the game: you investigate, you react with your Mask, and you uncover a piece of the city’s myth that compels you to decide what you stand for. ## Mechanics and balance in Fortune Row

City of Mist uses a tag-based resolution system that favors narrative control over raw numbers. Fortune Row is designed to work within that system, offering scenes that flex with the players’ Tag choices and the GM’s cues. You’ll find:

- Scene prompts that invite players to apply a Tag creatively rather than just rolling for a flat success
- A curated set of Vamps, Fools, and other city archetypes that feel both familiar and fresh
- Clue distribution designed to encourage discussion, theorizing, and occasional misdirection

Balance-wise, Fortune Row leans toward a cinematic feel rather than a grim, ultra-realistic indictment of crime. It leans into style and mood—the world’s dangerous beauty—without turning every encounter into a chore. There are moments where players may want more agency than the scenario traditionally grants; the best remedy is to remind yourself that the city rewards clever interpretation of a clue more than heavy-handed action sequences. If a moment feels stuck, try shifting a similar clue to a different location and observe how the party reinterprets the mystery.

From a GM perspective, Fortune Row is forgiving but not hand-holding. It provides optional tracks for players who want to chase a red herring or pursue a more resilient, long-term payoff. The plot is designed to accommodate different numbers of players, but as with many City of Mist packs, a table of five to six players tends to yield the liveliest exchange with a good mix of Masks active at the table.

## Narrative spine: structure, scenes, and progression

Fortune Row is built around a central mystery that evolves as players uncover clues, interview NPCs, and test their beliefs about fortune, power, and identity. The structure typically follows a three-act arc:

- Act I: The spark. An incident or rumor triggers a chain of events: a vanished benefactor, a misread fortune, or a performative display that hides something deeper. NPCs offer conflicting testimonies, and players must decide what to trust.
- Act II: The entanglement. Clues point to a set of interlocking schemes that involve crime, media, and myth. Relationships among NPCs become testy; players juggle their Mask’s public expectations with what they actually believe about the city. The stakes feel personal because fortune itself seems to tilt at their choices.
- Act III: The reveal and the choice. The climactic scene compresses the whole arc into a choice: protect a secret that saves the city or reveal it in a way that might save the city but ruin someone’s life. The ending is designed to ripple into future sessions, providing hooks for a follow-up or a callback.

The pacing is the real star here. It’s easy to get lost in the mood and lose track of the mystery, but Fortune Row includes pacing cues and optional beats to help the GM keep the thread from slipping. If you enjoy a mystery that rewards listening and inference as much as bravado, Fortune Row hits the sweet spot.

### Scenes and flow

The pack often includes a suggested sequence of scenes with beat-by-beat triggers. Some scenes feel like stand-alone set pieces, while others are micro-contained vignettes that can be dropped in whenever a player needs a hint or a push. You’ll see a mix of social encounters, urban exploration, and mythic revelations. The GM has the option to place a single, decisive clue early on or spread several subtle hints to create a sense of growing inevitability.

If you prefer a more improvisational approach, Fortune Row can function as a launch pad for unscripted chaos. The included scene prompts are robust enough to spark a GM’s imagination and give players a clear sense of direction without forcing them down a rigid track. The result is a game that feels both cinematic and personal—the city watching you while you decide who you are under its gaze.

## Accessibility and replay value

A great benefit of Fortune Row is its replay potential. Because the mystery involves social dynamics and mythic undercurrents rather than a single binary foe, the same pack can play out very differently at another table. The Masks system allows players to reinterpret clues through the lens of their alter-ego, which means your party can revisit Fortune Row to explore alternate outcomes, different alliances, or new interpretations of the mythic elements.

In terms of accessibility, the pack does a decent job of guiding new GMs through the arc while offering richer notes for seasoned players. The handouts are legible, the cues are clear, and the pacing aids help a GM stay on track without feeling micromanaged. A potential pitfall is that players who love heavy combat or puzzle boxes may feel the experience leans more toward mood and social intrigue than traditional dungeon-like action. If your table thrives on banter and deduction, Fortune Row is likely to become a favorite. If you crave high-tempo action, you might want to augment it with a couple of quick encounter sketches.

## How to run Fortune Row: GM tips

- Start with a mood check. Spend a few minutes at the table to calibrate tone—are we glam, noir, or something in-between? A shared understanding of mood helps players lean into the Masks and roleplay more naturally.
- Use the clue cards actively. Treat clues as red herrings and real threads at the same time. Let players test each thread, then reward them with a truth that changes how they approach a location or NPC.
- Lean into social dynamics. Fortune Row thrives on relationships—between NPCs, factions, and the ensemble of Masks. Encourage players to explore alliances and conflicts rather than just chase the culprit.
- Prepare alternate endings. Even if you don’t plan to diverge, you’ll feel more confident late in the game if you have two or three plausible endings lined up and ready to reveal depending on player choices.
- Bring a little theater. The city glitters; the myth looms. A small prop, a meaningful prop handout, or an on-table visual cue can dramatically elevate a scene without adding prep time.

## Final verdict

Fortune Row is a strong entry in the City of Mist catalog. It delivers a well-orchestrated mystery with memorable settings, interesting NPCs, and enough variety to keep a table engaged across multiple sessions. The tone hits that delicious middle-ground of glittering decadence and shadowy danger, a vibe City of Mist does exceptionally well when the writers remember that mystery, not Mayhem, drives the story. It’s not a perfect pick for every table—if you crave non-stop action or if your group wants a harsher, grimmer tone, you might want to tweak the emphasis—but for players who enjoy roleplay, intrigue, and mythic flavor, Fortune Row shines.

In terms of value, you’re getting a solid scenario book with polished assets, flexible structure, and a lot of room for creative interpretation. It’s the sort of pack you’ll reach for again when you want a night that feels cinematic, character-driven, and distinctly City of Mist in flavor.
