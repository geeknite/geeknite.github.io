## How to Play: The Pass Challenge Twist
Classic Jenga operates on a simple premise: remove a block from anywhere in the tower and place it on top, trying to keep the structure upright. The Pass Challenge adds a social layer: after removing a block, you must pass the tower to the next player within a time limit or a cue, then perform a move that aligns with a requirement from the rules deck.

Common elements you’ll encounter include:
- Quick pass: pass the tower promptly after a move to keep the pressure on.
- Thematic moves: a round might require you to move a block in a specific direction or from a certain region of the tower.
- Consequence options: you may assign a silly consequence to the next player if the tower falls or if a move fails a deck prompt.

The objective remains the same as classic Jenga: avoid toppling the tower. The social layer is what makes this variant feel fresh and a bit spicy. It’s Jenga with a dash of improv theater, where timing, communication, and a dash of bluffing become integral to survival.

## Setup, Rhythm, and Global Flow: Strategy for Surviving the Shake
A successful Jenga Pass Challenge night isn’t just about pulling blocks; it’s about orchestrating a rhythm that makes the tower’s fate feel like a foregone conclusion only to witness it buckle at the last second anyway.

- Start with safer blocks: early rounds benefit from lower-risk removals closer to the center of the tower.
- Read the deck quickly: skim for quick wins and avoid high-risk moves when you’re near the top. If the deck asks for a dramatic gesture, consider delaying it until you’re more secure or choosing a safer alternative when possible.
- Communicate clearly: pre-passing a quick plan helps everyone coordinate. You don’t need a full monologue, but two to three words can set expectations and prevent chaotic misreads.
- Manage pass rhythm: pace matters. Too fast, and you sacrifice control; too slow, and nerves spike. Find a tempo that keeps energy high without tipping toward gridlock.
- Risk management with upper blocks: top layers are where the tension lives. If unsure, favor safer lower blocks and reserve higher-stakes moves for later rounds when the pressure has built to a manageable level.

## Variants and House Rules: Crafting Your Own Chaotic Symphony
The charm of Jenga Pass Challenge is its flexibility. Editions vary, and you can customize the experience with house rules that feel more like improv theatre than a strict board game:
- Time-bank twists: lengthen or shorten the timer to tune the chaos.
- Consequence coins: small, silly penalties for mistakes, such as singing a short song or performing a humorous pantomime.
- Cooperative mode: team up to keep the tower upright for a set number of rounds, passing only after unanimous block moves.
- Blindfold challenge: for the bravest players, add a safety variant to test spatial awareness (and furniture safety).

House rules help keep sessions fresh across multiple nights and groups. They also reduce the risk of a repetitive cycle that can dull the excitement after a few runs.
