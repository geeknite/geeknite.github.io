## Maintenance: Keeping It Running Like a Well-Oiled Tournament

### Cleaning and Upkeep

Foosball tables, like any playground for competitive spirits, reward routine maintenance. Wipe the rods and surfaces after heavy use to prevent stickiness and ensure consistent ball roll. If your space becomes a magnet for dust or pet hair, you’ll want to vacuum around the legs and occasionally wipe down the cabinet with a dry microfiber cloth. Lubricating the rods is a nuanced decision; many players prefer a silicone spray or light graphite to maintain smooth rotation. Avoid heavy oils that can attract dust and create a sticky, slow mo atmosphere, especially if you like fast breaks and rapid-fire shots.

### Ball Replacement and Spares

Keep a spare ball handy. Foosball balls can vanish into tiny black holes after intense sessions or during a dramatic breakaway moment that becomes your new party trick. An extra ball helps you avoid the dreaded “one ball left in the corner” scenario—trust me, that is a mood killer you won’t want on your best night.

### Longevity and Care

If you’re investing in a compact competition table, you’re likely in it for the long haul. The key to longevity lies in addressing wobble early, tightening screws after the first 20–30 uses, and treating your table like a cherished vintage console you want to keep pristine. Regular checks on leg levelers, rail alignment, and the playing surface will save you from a cascade of micro-problems that would otherwise ruin a close game.

## User Tips: Getting the Most Out of Your Table

- Level the table and ensure the playing surface is true; a few millimeters of tilt can dramatically change ball trajectories.
