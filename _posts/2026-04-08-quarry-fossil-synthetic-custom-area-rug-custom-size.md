## Care and maintenance: keeping the fossil looking fossil-astic

A key selling point here is maintenance friendly design. Cleaning is mostly straightforward:

- Spot cleaning: treat spills with a mild detergent and water, blot—don’t rub—to avoid pushing the stain deeper into the pile.
- Vacuuming: use a standard vacuum with a brush attachment. Avoid high-suction settings on delicate fibers to minimize fraying or snagging.
- Deep cleaning: if you ever need a deeper refresh, most synthetic rugs can be professionally cleaned or machine-washed on gentle cycles depending on backing. Always check the manufacturer’s care instructions first.
- Odor control: if you’re dealing with new-rug scent, air out in a well-ventilated area for a day or two before placing it in a high-traffic room.

For ongoing care, a rug pad not only helps with slip resistance but also increases longevity by reducing friction between the rug and the floor. It’s a small accessory with a big impact.

Durability in synthetic rugs is generally strong, but it’s not magic. Expect the rug to hold up well to foot traffic and light spills over time.
