## Painting tips that actually help you finish the army

- Start with a solid primer and not too heavy a coat; you want detail preserved, not an oversized blob of color.
- Use a mid-tone base color on skin tones, then build shadows with a brown or blue wash. It helps bring out texture without overcomplicating the process.
- Dry brushing works wonders on armor, rust, and bone textures. Lightly run a dry brush over raised edges to catch highlights and make the minis pop under standard gaming lighting.
- For a grimy look, mix a tiny drop of blue into a brown wash for shadows; the greenish-rot vibe is a classic, but keep it restrained to avoid making your minis look like walking cucumbers.
- Basestep with a neutral earth tone and apply a gel varnish for a durable finish that survives accidental dice throws and enthusiastic table flips.

## Assembly reality check and tips to survive the glue fog

Assembly remains straightforward for the bulk of the figures, but a few pieces may require a touch of patience. We recommend a slow-paced approach with a thin bead of glue and a lot of clamps or tacky tackers to hold those sculpted limbs in place while the glue cures. Do not force joints; if you encounter resistance, take a breath, back off, and re-check the part alignment. A little extra time here prevents the horrifying moment when a lovingly painted zombie detaches from its base on the first glow of the lamp.

If you are new to the hobby, you can also adopt a modular approach: assemble a few minis each night. This reduces the monster of backlog anxiety and helps you savor the process rather than sprint to the finish line. The community often recommends starting with a core escape scenario to learn the ropes with Season 2 quickly. It is less about speed and more about enjoying the miniature storytelling that unfolds on the table.
