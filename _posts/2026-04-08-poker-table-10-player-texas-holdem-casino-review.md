## Durability, Maintenance, and Longevity

### Cleaning and Care
A high-traffic poker table undergoes many daily rituals: chip swapping, card shuffles, occasional spills, and the occasional curious pet or child who wants to “help” by licking the felt to see what it tastes like. The table’s top should be easy to clean with a damp cloth and mild soap. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the felt or the leather rail. If you spill something sticky, act fast: wipe the surface, rinse with a small amount of water, and dry thoroughly. The more you treat the table like a living room and less like a battlefield, the longer it stays looking premium.

### Metal Legs and Stability
The metal legs are the real backbone of this unit. If you engage in heavy-handed shuffling or aggressive “read-your-strong-hand” bluffs, the legs should hold steady. We evaluated this by applying moderate lateral pressure while players leaned in. The table remained solid, with only a minor creak audible when a player rose to double-check their outs. If you expect to host high-stakes events, consider adding anti-slip floor caps to the feet to dampen vibrations on wooden floors and to prevent inadvertent scuffs.

### Maintenance of Folding Components
Fold mechanisms require occasional lubrication and tightening. We recommend a light machine oil or silicone-based spray on hinges, with a quarterly check to ensure nothing has loosened. If you’re planning frequent disassembly, keep a small toolbox with a screwdriver set and a few spare bolts handy—particularly if you’ve selected a model with a complex folding pattern. A wobbly table edge spoils the vibe faster than a bad tell on a bluff card.
