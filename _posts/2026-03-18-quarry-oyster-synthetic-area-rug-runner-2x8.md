### Cleaning and Maintenance Quick Guide
- Vacuum regularly with a soft-beater or brushless attachment to avoid fiber pull.
- For spots, use a mild detergent solution and blot, never rub (rubbing spreads stains and can warp fibers).
- Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade color or fiber integrity.
- If you decide to deep-clean, follow the manufacturer’s guidance; many synthetics allow gentle machine washing, but verify the backing and care label first.
- Let the rug dry fully before placing it back on the floor to prevent mold growth.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh
Maintaining a rug is about anticipating life, not hunkering down into paranoia. The Quarry Oyster Runner is designed with low maintenance in mind, which means you don’t need to become a rug-care nerd to keep it looking decent after year one. Here are practical tips to maximize its lifespan:

- Use a rug pad for stability and added cushioning. A pad reduces matting in high-traffic lines and extends the rug’s life by distributing weight and preventing curling at the edges.
- Vacuum weekly or as needed. In busy homes, a quick pass every few days keeps dirt from embedding into the fibers, preserving color and texture.
- Clean spills promptly. Even though synthetic fibers are relatively stain-resistant, liquids can set if left to soak. Blot, don’t rub, and treat with a mild cleaning solution if necessary.
- Address sun exposure. Direct sunlight can fade colors over time. If possible, rotate the rug or shade the sun with curtains to maintain the oyster hue over longer periods.
- Edge care. Keep an eye on edges and corners; if you notice curling, consider re-stretching or using a longer pad under the rug’s edges to maintain a seamless look.

## Comparisons: Quarry Oyster vs Other Rugs in the Same Class
### Polypropylene vs Nylon vs Polyester
- Polypropylene (PP): The most common for affordable runners. Durable, stain-resistant, and colorfast; it’s the backbone of many budget-friendly options. It’s also the fiber you’ll find in the Quarry Oyster. The upside is value; the downside is that ultra-tortured feet might prefer a slightly plusher feel.
- Nylon: More resilient and springy, with better crush resistance. Nylon fibers can feel nicer underfoot and hold up well under heavy traffic. However, nylon can be pricier than PP, which is a consideration for hallways that require long-term durability without blowing your budget.
- Polyester: Great color clarity and softness. It’s often used for mid-range rugs. Polyester can be more prone to flattening in high-traffic areas, though modern constructions have narrowed the gap. If you’re chasing a plush feel, polyester might be a better choice, but it may require more upkeep.
