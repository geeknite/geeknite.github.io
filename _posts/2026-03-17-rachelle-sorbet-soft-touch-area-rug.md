The science of squish:

If you are curious about why soft-touch fabrics feel so adorably cushiony, there is a small universe of fiber science behind it. While I am not enrolled in a formal textile engineering program, I can share that polymer blends and pile heights influence how the rug compresses under pressure and how it recovers after you step away. A higher pile can feel more luxurious but invites more dirt to hide in the fibers, while a shorter pile is easier to clean but may feel more utilitarian. The Sorbet tries to strike a balance: enough fluff to be comfortable, but not so much that it becomes a magnet for every crumb in your apartment.

Cleaning and maintenance:

Spill management is straightforward: blot, blot again, and then blot once more with a clean towel. The goal is to keep the stain from sinking into the fibers rather than saturating the entire surface. A mild detergent solution can be used for stubborn marks, then rinse with clean water and blot dry. Do not machine wash or tumble dry unless the manufacturer instructs you to do so; this rug thrives on life as a non-machine-washed hero. For routine cleaning, a standard vacuum on a low or gentle setting does well, and you can use a brush attachment to lift any matting that may have settled in high-traffic pockets.

Care tips and room-life:

Rotate the rug seasonally to promote even wear. This is less about fashion cycles and more about making sure every corner gets equal screen time in your lighting setup. Use a rug pad to prevent sliding when placed on wooden floors and to improve foot comfort. If you have kids or pets, you will appreciate a rug that cleans up relatively quickly and does not require specialized cleaning solutions.
