## How to Play Skip-Bo (A Quick Refresher for Busy Families)
Skip-Bo is a race against chaos where players try to empty their stock pile by playing cards in numerical order into building piles that ascend from 1 to 12. The big trick is that you can only play cards that match the next number in the sequence, and wild Skip-Bo cards can substitute for any number you need to complete a pile. The first player to deplete their stock pile wins. Sounds simple, and yet the moment when you nail a sequence while your sibling groans in mock despair is pure family-game-night gold.

### Setup
- Each player gets a stock pile: a face-down stack of cards that you will flip to reveal the next card to play.
- Players usually draw a hand of five cards (or as per the exact house rules) to start the first turn.
- There are one or more discard piles available for strategic plays and quick access to certain numbers when a play is needed in a pinch.
- The central goal is to be the first to empty your stock pile, with the rest of the table chasing to finish as well.

### Basic rules in plain language
- On your turn, you draw up to your hand limit, then you try to play any number cards from your hand or stock onto the building piles in ascending order (1 through 12).
- You can use wild Skip-Bo cards as any number to help you complete a sequence. The key is to keep your options open without overextending your hand.
- If you cannot make a move, you end your turn and you draw to replenish your hand, setting up for the next round.
- The game continues until someone empties their stock pile; that player is declared the winner, potentially followed by some dramatic table-side celebrations and taunts.
