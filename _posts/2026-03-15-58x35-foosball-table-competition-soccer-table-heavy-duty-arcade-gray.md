## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
### Cleaning and Care
- Wipe the playing surface with a soft microfiber cloth after use to remove fingerprints and dust.
- Periodically check screw tightness on the feet and crossbars to avoid wobble drift.
- If you notice a squeak, apply a small amount of silicone-based lubricant to the rods’ joints (do not overdo it; you don’t want your players sliding around the board like ice skaters).

### Longevity and Resale Value
A well-maintained competition table can last well beyond a decade of casual use. The Gray Edition’s rugged construction means even heavy user groups—teenagers, post-work-night owls, and the occasional party host—can expect years of service before anything requires replacement. The gray aesthetic also tends to hold up visually, maintaining a premium look that’s less prone to showing every fingerprint or minor scuff.

### Common Issues and Fixes
- Rods sticking: wipe down the rod ends and check for debris. A light lubricant on the ends can help, but avoid applying in moderation; too much slickness can cause misalignment.
- Ball bounce variability: ensure the playing surface remains clean; some tables may benefit from a gentle re-sealing of the laminated surface if you notice drift in bounce consistency.
- Leveling drift: re-check leg levelers periodically. Floors aren’t perfectly flat; the table will thank you for occasional readjustments.
