## Stain resistance, cleaning, and maintenance

### What to expect in daily life

Back stains, coffee spills, pet paw prints, and the occasional glitter explosion from a craft project are the realities of modern living. The MAD611C-220’s stain-resistance claims are a strong selling point, but let’s be real: nothing beats quick blotting and a proper cleaning routine. The surface material is designed to resist many liquids, and the color choice can help hide minor spills between cleanings. For stubborn stains, you’ll want to treat promptly and use mild cleaners suitable for synthetic fibers, following the product care guidelines.

### Cleaning tips that actually work

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting to avoid snagging the fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing, which can grind mess into the fibers and smear the stain.
- For tougher spots, use a mild solution of water and dish soap, apply to a cloth, and work from the outside of the stain toward the center. Rinse the cloth and repeat if necessary.
- Allow the rug to air-dry completely before walking on it; moisture can contribute to odor or mildew in poorly ventilated spaces.
- For deeper cleaning, consider a professional cleaning service that handles synthetic fibers, or a machine-washable option if your rug is certified for such care.
