## Safety and Maintenance

### Safety considerations
The surface is free from sharp edges, and the underlying foam is low-odor, low-toxicity, and kid-friendly. It’s not a toy in the strict sense, so supervision is recommended for younger children who tend to discover gravity in dramatic ways. The colorfast dyes used in the print are designed to resist heavy cleaning, and the surface remains comfortable to touch even after months of high-frequency play. If your robots produce static electricity, a quick wipe with a damp cloth should dissipate any stubborn charges and keep your play sessions calm and friendly.

### Cleaning routine
A gentle wipe-down after play is all you need. For stubborn syrup or marker pen incidents, you can use a mild detergent and warm water, then air-dry completely before the next session. It’s recommended to store it flat or loosely rolled to avoid warping, especially in rooms with dramatic shifts in humidity. The more you treat it like a living room rug rather than a science experiment, the longer it will look bright and cheerful.
