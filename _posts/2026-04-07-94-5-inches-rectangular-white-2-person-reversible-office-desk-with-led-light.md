## Assembly and Unboxing

Unboxing the 94.5-inch desk is a two-person job by design. The box is long enough to carry a toddler—and that’s not an exaggeration. The parts are dense but well labeled, and the hardware bags make you feel like you’re opening a high-budget piece of modular furniture rather than a flat-pack metric experiment. The instructions are readable if you’re comfortable with icons that resemble a small starship hull diagram, but you’ll probably want a second set of hands. Here are some practical tips to keep your sanity intact:

- Clear a wide space and pre-sort hardware. Trust me, you’ll thank your past self when you’re not hunting for a 5mm hex key in a pile of packaging shreds.
- Leveling is your friend. The long span of the desktop means any wobble gets amplified visually. Use the adjustable feet to dial in a firm stance on uneven floors.
- Cable management channels are your best friend and worst enemy. They’re designed to keep wires in line; they’ll also tempt you to route everything under the desk via a single maze-like conduit. Resist that impulse unless you enjoy spiral-cut spaghetti under your chair.

The LED lighting module is typically installed as a separate sub-assembly. It’s not complicated, but it does require care to avoid pinching any cables during final assembly. The power supply is compact and discreet, and the remote control (if included) sits somewhere convenient for quick adjustments during late-night work sessions or creative YouTube binge-watches.

After you’ve anchored the frame, placed the desktop panels, and routed the cables through the channels, you’ll stand back and say, Okay, this is big. And you’ll be right. It’s big in a good way—offering two distinct work zones, generous legroom, and a presence that says, I mean business, even if your business is doodling color-coded post-it notes.

## Space, Ergonomics, and Two-Person Usability

Two-person desks can either be a masterpiece of spatial planning or a cramped two-seater on the bus. The 94.5-inch width is designed to minimize those awkward two-person moments, like someone reclaiming space with a sudden laptop shuffle or a debate over which monitor is the main one. In practice, each user gets roughly 47 inches of bench space, which is plenty for two laptops, extra keyboards, mice, a notebook, two mugs, and a small plant that pretends to be a coworker.

Ergonomics are not entirely forgiving if you attempt to model a cinema seating arrangement with chairs behind the desk. The key is to keep elbow spacing comfortable and to arrange monitors in a way that doesn’t cause neck strains. A little tilt on the monitors can help reduce reflections and eye strain. The height of the desk is adjustable if there are any height mismatches between chairs, which is common when you’re sharing with a friend who insists on a human couch posture during long writing sessions.

From a space-planning perspective, this desk shines in medium to large rooms. It demands a dedicated area with a clean edge and a cable-friendly corner. It’s not a hidden work desk for small apartments; it’s a bold piece of furniture that tells visitors that you mean business about productivity, organization, and possibly more plants than any sane person would keep.

### Monitor placement
A two-person setup works best with side-by-side monitor placement and equal desk space. If monitors are too wide apart, you risk neck strain from constant head turning. If too close, you’ll have a shared keyboard and you might as well start a duo project. Balance, people, balance.
