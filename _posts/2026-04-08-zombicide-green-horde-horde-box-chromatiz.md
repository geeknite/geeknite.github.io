## What is the Horde Box Chromatiz?

The Horde Box Chromatiz is an expansive add on to Zombicide Green Horde that bundles together several small expansions and a new mechanic built around Chromatiz. The idea is to intensify the late game by introducing chromatic zombie forms that shift color and power as the game progresses. It plays nicely with the Green Horde baseline rules but introduces a few adjustments that keep the board fresh without turning the whole thing into a math seminar. In practice, Chromatiz adds:

- A set of new zombie variants that feature color based traits
- New survivors with unique starting gear
- A mission book with five new scenarios designed to showcase Chromatiz
- A handful of tokens and cards to enable Chromatiz interactions, including trait boosters and risk cards

These are not entirely new rules, but they are layered on top of the classic Zombicide experience. If you have previously mastered the base game or the Green Horde expansion, Chromatiz will feel familiar yet unpredictable enough to earn a place on your table. The look of Chromatiz is deliberate. The miniatures in this box lean into bright, almost neon color motifs that reflect the idea of chromatic corruption spreading through the zombie swarm. The art direction remains faithful to Zombicide's gritty humor, with zombie sculpts that feel both threatening and cartoonish in a way that invites you to lean into the chaos rather than retreat. Chromatiz does not erase the grim tone; it adds a layer of color coded chaos that is surprisingly thematic.

## Chromatiz mechanics explained

Chromatiz is the new mechanic that adds a dynamic color system to the zombie swarm. It is not a replacement for the standard Zombicide rules, but a parallel track that interacts with them in predictable ways. Here is a practical breakdown:

- Chromatiz zombies have color profiles that shift as you re-roll, fight or survive. Each color indicates a minor stat boost or a slight penalty to a survivor action.
- On certain turns, a color event or Chromatiz card flips a zombie into a more dangerous variant. The effect is not game breaking, but the moment you see a pink tinted zombie lumbering toward your survivors, you feel a little surge of adrenaline.
- Survivors equipped with Chromatiz gear can actually influence the color shifts in their favor. For example, a Chromatiz blade may be more effective against a red tinted zombie, creating a small but satisfying tactical decision point.
- The number of Chromatiz zombies on the board increases with time. It is a subtle mechanism to push players toward decisive plays rather than waiting passively for the tide to turn.

The Chromatiz cards are the real star here. They give you a toolkit to respond to the evolving threats. Some cards push you to split your group, others reward you for sticking together, and a few punish you for being too optimistic about your odds. It is not a revolutionary change to the core design of Zombicide, but it is a well executed addition that will feel familiar to fans while providing enough novelty to keep a few games fresh.

### Deeper dive: color cycle and timing

This is where the box earns additional love. The color cycle in Chromatiz is not a random flourish; it is a careful design decision that ensures tension builds as you move through the board. In practical terms, you will notice that late game cycles tilt the balance in favor of color heavy variants. This creates a sense of inevitability that keeps players on their toes. It also means that early decisions matter more than in many other expansions, because you do not want to be caught with weak survivors and a bad color roll when the board is suddenly painted with chromatic menace.

### Surviving Chromatiz: tips for players

- Stage a plan for color shifts. Do not run one lone survivor around the board hoping for a miracle. You will likely regret it when a cluster of Chromatiz zombies converge on their chosen target.
- Use the new survivors to anchor critical zones. They provide safer routes for the rest of the team.
- Prioritize objectives that require you to close a score or complete a mission quickly, before the color cycle becomes overwhelming.
- Balance risk and defense. Chromatiz naturally invites bolder plays, but you will still need to hold the line if you want to see the end of the match.
