## How to Play: Core Rules Revisited
### Setup and Goal
Each player receives a stock pile of face-down cards, a hand of draw cards, and several build piles placed in the center of the table. The objective is to be the first to empty your stock pile by playing cards onto the central build piles in ascending order, from 1 to 12. Skip-Bo wilds can take the place of any number, acting as flexible puzzle pieces that keep everyone engaged as the table shifts with each hand and each new reveal.

### Turn Structure and Decision Points
On your turn you draw cards to refill your hand, then you play as many cards as you can onto the build piles. You can use cards from your hand or from your stock pile, but the stock pile is the real prize. Once you run out of stock cards, you win that round. The tension arises from timing — knowing when to push a long run and when to hold back a key card to block an opponent or to complete your own sequence just as the clock is ticking down.

### Variations and House Rules
Skip-Bo is ripe for house rules. Some groups prefer shorter rounds with a running tally, others pursue marathon sessions that test memory and endurance. The Deluxe edition accommodates these customs with a simple, forgiving rule sheet and a deck that remains readable after many games. The result is a game that scales well for kids, parents, and board-game veterans who enjoy a fast-paced, social, and slightly theatrical experience.
