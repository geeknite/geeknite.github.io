## What is OverPower, Really?
OverPower is Fleer’s ambitious bid to map the superhero pantheon onto a card-game framework with a crunchy-yet-snappy rules set. The game emphasizes synergy over sheer brute force, with powers that can complement each other, stern villains to oppose your heroes, and a drafting mechanic that makes every booster pack feel like a lottery ticket. It’s not perfect—90s card games borrowed design brains from every popular trend and produced a mosaic rather than a single clean path—but it has personality in spades.

From a design perspective, OverPower leans into large-art aesthetics, punchy keywords, and a sense of epic scale that makes you feel like you’re guiding a team that could save or crash a city block in a single turn. The Superheroes line, in particular, dives into iconic figures and their thematic power sets. The P-Promo Pick From List sits inside that vibe, offering a twist that rewards players who read the board and respect the synergy of their lineup.

## The Superheroes P-Promo: Pick From List — What Is It?
Let’s place the P-Promo in its proper niche. Promo cards in the 90s were the clever little hooks publishers used to encourage magazine subscriptions, convention attendance, or simply to reward the most loyal fans who kept a binder full of foil legends from every set. The P-Promo, standing for something like Premium-Promo (the exact nickname can vary by collector), is a card that isn’t part of the standard booster drop. It’s a special edition with a distinctive art touch, and often a unique in-game effect or a restricted-use ability that makes it stand out from the crowd.

In the case of the Pick From List, you get an option—an in-game mechanism that literally asks you to pick a target or an option from a curated list that’s printed on the card, or from a known set of eligible choices. The flavor text leans into the sense that you’re directing your hero to focus on a lane, a task, or a moment of heroism that pushes you toward a particular strategy. It’s a playful, almost “choose your own adventure” in a CCG form.

The potential power of such a promo card can vary wildly, depending on your local meta and how your friends interpret “promo.” Some collectors regard P-Promo cards as the holy grail: scarce, with a print run that’s far from transparent, often accompanied by glossy stock or a distinctive foil treatment. Others simply adore them for the historical record they carry—memories of the time you pulled a hero you love on a rainy afternoon and immediately tried to make that one card float an entire deck.
