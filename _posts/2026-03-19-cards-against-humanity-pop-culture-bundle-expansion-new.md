- Early rounds tend to center on safe, crowd-pleasing prompts that establish the group’s humor baseline.
- Mid game, the prompts become sharper and more specific to pop culture in your circle, which sometimes leads to clever micro-jokes that fly over the heads of non-fans.

## The party kit: play setup and timing

Setting up the expansion is straightforward, but the way you run the game can elevate it from casual pastime to a legendary group activity. Here is a quick play guide to help you maximize your experience without burning out your jokes.

### Setup tips
- Separate the expansions from the base deck for a few rounds to allow new prompts to shine without overshadowing classic favorites.
- Shuffle thoroughly; with big decks, it is possible to have a run of nearly identical prompts if you do not mix properly.
- Designate a rotating Card Counter to ensure fairness in who gets which white cards. The Card Counter should be the person who most recently made a pun in the group chat.

### Running the rounds
- Start with light prompts that set a friendly tone, then gradually escalate to more niche memes.
- Encourage players to explain why a certain white card works for a given black card. A short justification often makes the round more entertaining than the actual answer.
- End with a recap round where the winner of each round has the honor of describing the joke in one sentence, while everyone else nods and pretends they understood the reference.
