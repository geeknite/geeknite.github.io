## Gameplay and Mechanics

### How Elite Core Differs from Classic UNO

The Elite Core line aims to preserve the core UNO experience while refining the physical and visual design. The basic rules remain: match color or number, play action cards to disrupt opponents, and be the first to empty your hand. Where this edition diverges is in the following areas:

- A refined action card set with improved iconography. The symbols are larger and more intuitive, which helps new players jump in without a manual.
- A subtle rule-smoothing adjustment that reduces “range-of-interpretation” in chaotic group games. For example, the “Draw Two” effect is summarized on the card with a tiny, readable blurb to speed up early learning.
- Enhanced color separation. The Yellow Foil isn’t just aesthetic; it makes yellow cards feel bolder in a crowded sea of hues, reducing misreads and accidental plays.
- A limited-run signature edition approach. The Barnes artwork and foil finish place this deck into a collector’s category without forcing you to hunt for a rare chase card to complete your set.
