## Maintenance, Longevity, and Care Tips
### Routine Cleaning
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to remove surface dust and crumbs. Avoid high-suction vacuum modes that can pull fibers out of place over time.
- For small spills, blot, don’t rub. Rubbing can push stains deeper and encourage pile distortion.
- Address pet hair with a brush attachment or a low-suction setting. The rug’s fibers don’t cling to fur in a dramatic fashion, but a quick pass doesn’t hurt.

### Deep Cleaning and Stain Treatments
If you need a more thorough cleanse, consider a mild carpet shampoo or a diluted cleaning solution—test a small patch first to ensure color stability. Avoid bleach or harsh solvents, which can dull the grey and dull your day. Air-drying is ideal; avoid direct heat that can cause shrinkage or texture changes.

### Longevity and Wear Patterns
With normal rotation and a bit of furniture management, you can expect the rug to age gracefully. The grey colour tends to mask minor wear and tear better than light creams or beige, which is a practical advantage for high-traffic rooms. If you’re worried about flattening in the center, consider a secondary protective pad under the main pad to distribute weight evenly.
