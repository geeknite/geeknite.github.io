## Dhurrie 101
### What is a dhurrie
Dhurrie is a flat weave rug technique that originated in India. Unlike thick pile rugs, a dhurrie is essentially a tightly woven cloth that lies flat on the floor. It is the minimalist avatar of the rug world: no fuzz, no fluff, just crisp lines and honest materials. Modern dhurries retain traditional loom work but adapt to current aesthetics with sharper patterns and more refined color palettes.

### Why cotton matters
Most dhurries are made with cotton warp and weft, sometimes with a touch of wool for warmth. Cotton offers a breathable, lightweight feel that makes daily vacuuming and moving easier. It dries quickly after a spill and resists the catastrophic shedding that you get with some woolen rugs. The result is a rug that is easy to maintain and pleasant to step on barefoot after a long day in front of a laptop.

### Colorfastness and care for blue dyes
Quality blue dyes in cotton rugs are typically fast to light, meaning their hues hold up nicely against sun exposure. But be mindful: direct sun on a blue dye can still fade over years. Rotating the rug every few months helps even wear and keeps the color from fading unequally. A good rug pad also reduces shifting that can cause the blue to rub and fade along the edges.

## Handcrafted Craftsmanship
### The loom to living room journey
A handmade cotton dhurrie is a product of careful human craft. The process generally looks like this:
- Design and color mixing: artisans plan color blocks and test swatches
- Warping: setup of warp threads that define the rug width
- Weaving: the actual weaving, a rhythmic process that converts yarn into a flat, durable fabric
- Finishing: trimming and edge binding to prevent unraveling
- Washing and drying: a gentle wash to set colors and remove excess fibers

This sequence results in a rug that is not just a floor covering but a story spun in cotton threads. You can almost hear the loom singing when you walk across the finished piece, which in Geeknite terms is a victory chorus for textile engineering.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Everyday care
Cotton dhurries are easy to care for but need routine attention:
- Vacuum regularly with a beater bar on low suction, or use a hand vacuum for delicate areas
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber distortion
- For tough stains, test a mild detergent on a hidden area first; avoid harsh chemicals
- Rotate the rug to even wear and prevent hotspots

### Deep cleaning and protection
Periodically, you can give the rug a gentle hand wash or professional clean. Ensure the rug is completely dry before placing it back to avoid mold growth. A rug pad not only cushions but also reduces shifting that creates creases and wear lines. Protect the rug from direct sunlight to minimize color fading over the long term.

## Size, Shape, and Layout Considerations
### Choosing the right size for your space
A blue cotton dhurrie comes in various options, from slender runners to large area rugs. When choosing size, measure the room and account for furniture placement. For a living room, leave roughly 18 inches of exposed floor around the rug edges to visually anchor the space. A hallway rug should be long enough to cover the traffic path with a small amount of floor visible at both ends.

### Shapes and edge finishes
Most dhurries favor rectangle shapes, but rounds or irregular shapes can be used for dramatic effect in modern dens or studios. Edge finishes range from classic fringe to clean binding. If you want a modern, edge-to-edge look, opt for no fringe with a tight binding and a seamless transition to the floor.
