## H2: Care, Restoration, and Maintaining Charm

Care for vintage ceramics isn’t magic, but it’s close. Here are practical steps to keep your knee hugger looking its best for years:

- Cleaning: Use a soft, dry microfiber cloth or a gentle brush to remove dust. If you need a damp wipe, use a barely damp cloth with a tiny amount of mild dish soap, then dry immediately. Avoid soaking or submerging the ornament, which could cause glaze cracking or paint bleed.
- Handling: Hands should be clean and dry. The glaze often has delicate high-spot edges that can chip with rough handling. When moving, cradle the piece in the palm where possible and avoid gripping with a single finger for extended periods.
- Repairs: If you notice a hairline crack or tiny paint loss, consult a professional conservator if you’re aiming for museum-grade preservation. If you’re simply displaying and appreciating, a dab of acrylic paint in a color-matched tone can sometimes be touched up by careful amateur artists, but proceed with caution—stock photos are deceptive and your paint-matching skills might be, too.
- Storage: When not on display, store in acid-free tissue and place your box with silica gel to reduce moisture. The goal is to avoid mold, not the joy of turning on a bag of crystals for ambiance.

Maintenance is less about fixing something broken and more about preserving the story these pieces tell. Each chip or glaze nuance is a chapter, and you’re the archivist who gets to choose which chapters stay in the foreground of your display.
