## Setup and Assembly
### Prep and planning
As with any modular desk setup, a little planning goes a long way. Before you open the instructions, measure your space. The 6D by 7W footprint is generous, so verify you have room for a stand-up zone if you plan to switch to a standing posture frequently. A good practice is to mock-place the cubicle components on the floor to confirm alignment, then map out where the grommets and channels will lie once assembled.

A note on tool selection: the included Allen wrenches are perfectly adequate for the job, but you might want to have a drill with a low torque setting handy if you’re assembling late at night and your hands have entered the no-coffee zone. A gentle touch is advised; you don’t want to strip a bolt head because you were pretending you’re a robotics engineer during assembly.

### Step-by-step assembly
A typical assembly flow looks like this:
- Attach the desk surface to the frame using the provided bolts and washers.
- Secure the cubicle panels to the base frame. Double-check alignment; you don’t want to discover that your side panel is slightly askew after you’ve installed the monitors.
- Run cables through the channels and through the desk pass-through grommets.
- Install any optional accessories such as monitor arms or shelf units.
- Tidy up and perform a final shake-down to verify stability.

Common pitfalls include overtightening bolts (which can strip threads) and underestimating the weight of the assembled structure. Take breaks if you start to feel the fatigue monster creeping in. Trust me, your back will thank you on day two when you’re not wrestling a stubborn corner panel into place.

### Common pitfalls and tips
- Do a dry-fit before commitment. Place pieces without fasteners to ensure alignment.
- Don’t rush the cable assembly. It’s easier to route once you’re calm and have a plan for each cable type.
- Have a buddy for the stubborn panels. A second pair of hands is worth a lot more than you’d expect when a panel is heavy and a little stubborn.
- Use a level during assembly. You want your desk surface to be flat so monitors aren’t hovering in mid-air in the wrong dimension.

## Ergonomics and Comfort
### Desk height range and leg clearance
The height range offers both seated and standing options, which is increasingly essential for ergonomic health. A properly used adjustable desk can reduce prolonged static postures, which are a known villain of the modern workday. The leg clearance appears generous; there’s enough space for a comfortable chair and some knee wiggle room that might be useful for mid-workstanding stretches. If you’re tall or unusually long-legged, you’ll want to measure your seat height against the desk to confirm you can achieve a natural elbow angle without squeezing under the monitor.

### Monitor placement
With a 7 ft width, there’s ample room for a dual-monitor or single ultra-wide setup. The cubicle panels provide some rumor-proof privacy that helps virtual meetings look less chaotic from far away, but you’ll still want a proper monitor stand or VESA mount to optimize viewing angles.
