Hand weaving brings a human cadence to the rug’s surface. Expect slight irregularities in weave density that translate into a charming, organic look. In bohemian design, edges matter for both safety and style. You’ll likely see clean but sturdy borders, designed to maintain the rug’s shape over time. ## Performance and Durability: How It Holds Up in Real Life

### Daily Wear and Tear
Wool holds up well in many settings, but the performance depends on traffic. In living rooms with kids, pets, or enthusiastic coffee cups, a wool rug can shed tiny fibers and show footprints. The soft ivory color can show dirt more readily than darker palettes, so routine vacuuming and spot-cleaning habits become essential. The upside is that wool fibers have natural resilience; with proper care, you can extend the rug’s life well beyond a single season of fashion cycles.

### Cleaning and Maintenance
Maintenance is the boring but essential part of rug ownership. For wool, you’ll typically want to vacuum on a low setting with a beater brush turned off to prevent fiber damage. Spills should be blotted rather than rubbed, and a mild wool-safe cleaner can handle most stains. For deeper cleaning, a professional rug cleaning service familiar with natural fiber rugs is often worth the investment. Given the ivory color, I’d assign this rug a regular maintenance plan rather than a “set-it-and-forget-it” approach.

### Odor and Colorfastness
Natural wool can carry a subtle animal-scent memory when new, which fades with time and airing. Colorfastness is generally good, but always check for a dye migration policy if you’re mixing light and dark furniture. If you’re worried about color transfer, consider a layered approach: a neutral underlay and a reinforcing rug pad can help stabilize the surface and reduce movement that could irritate the fibers over time.
