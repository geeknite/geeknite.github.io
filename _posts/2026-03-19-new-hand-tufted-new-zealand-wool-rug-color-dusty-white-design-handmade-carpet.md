## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

### Daily Care: Vacuuming and Foot Traffic

Wool fibers are naturally resilient and tend to rebound well after compression, which helps with high-traffic zones. A regular vacuum with a gentle, low-suction setting is ideal. Avoid aggressive beater bars that can fray the pile over time. The key is consistent care: vacuum frequently enough to prevent dirt from embedding, but not so aggressively that you flay the surface into a shaggy version of a furry dream. Rotate the rug every three to six months if you can, especially in rooms with direct sun exposure or heavy foot traffic. Rotation helps distribute wear evenly so no one corner looks brand-new while the rest looks retired.

### Stain Care: Quick Response Is Your Friend

Accidents happen. Spilling red wine during a cozy evening? Tomato sauce while you attempt a new pasta recipe? The immediate plan is simple: blot, don’t rub. Blot with a clean cloth or paper towel, start from the outside of the spill and work inward to prevent spreading. For most organic stains, a gentle wool-safe cleaner with a mild detergent is enough. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. If you’re unsure about a stain or want professional guidance, consult a local rug care specialist or reference our Rug Cleaning 101 post for a step-by-step approach. For more on stain remedies and best practices, you can also explore industry care guides linked in this article or the Wool Care basics on reputable care sites.

### Seasonal and Long-Term Care

Rotate, vacuum, and watch sunlight exposure. Direct sun can gradually lighten wool over years, even if the color is durable by design. Use window treatments or UV-filtering films if your space gets intense sun at certain hours. A simple underlay or rug pad helps keep the pile from creeping under furniture and reduces wear by providing an even surface for foot traffic. It’s not just about underfoot comfort; it’s about protecting your investment from the daily drama of chairs being moved, pets zooming across the room, and your occasional leap of faith when you attempt a dramatic vacuum dodge.

### Cleaning Myth-Busting

People ask if wool rugs can be machine cleaned. The short answer is usually no for the rug as a whole; many manufacturers and care guides discourage machine washing or using high-heat cycles. Deep cleaning is typically done by professionals or through careful, manual methods that retain the rug’s integrity.
