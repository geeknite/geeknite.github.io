- Hero Power Cards: Each deck includes limited-use hero power cards that grant players special abilities for a single round. You’re not only playing Uno; you’re collecting a mini-gallery of Midoriya, Bakugo, Todoroki, and friends, all reimagined as UNO-facing avatars. - The blend of UNO mechanics with anime hero powers creates approachable depth for a light strategy game.
- The visual design is vibrant, bold, and instantly recognizable to fans of the show, without feeling overly busy.
- The Canada-exclusive approach adds a sense of urgency and community buzz that typically accompanies collectible drops.
- The promo supports family-friendly play and socializing around a shared hobby, rather than isolating players to screens.

Cons:
- Exclusive regional drops can frustrate fans in other regions and may encourage scalping in the secondary market. 
- The hero power deck, while exciting, isn’t deeply balanced against classic UNO rules, so competitive play might feel a bit tilted toward those who draw the luckiest powers.
- Storage and wear on small cards can degrade quickly if the set is pulled out on a chaotic kitchen table with spilled ketchup and crayons.

What would improve the experience? A more robust rulebook with clear examples and a “quick-start” version for new players would help families get to the table faster. A small zip pouch or card holder included with the deck would also enhance durability and display value. Additionally, a few more hero cards with alternate art, perhaps featuring fan-favorite moments from MHA, would be a delightful “spin-off” for collectors who want to show off their dedication.
