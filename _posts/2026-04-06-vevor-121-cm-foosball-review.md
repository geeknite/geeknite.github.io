## Assembly and setup
### Box contents and prep
Do not panic when you see the hardware bag and the stack of slightly bent cardboard. This is normal. The first step is to lay everything out and verify you have all pieces listed in the manual. Missing a bolt is not catastrophic, but it can derail your Friday night theme. It helps to have a clean space, a measuring tape for levelling, and a quick plan for tile-adjacent furniture. In many cases, the legs come with pre-installed brackets; others require you to attach them with the included bolts. The general flow is to assemble the legs, attach the frame, mount the end panels, install the rods, and finally drop in the playing figures.
### Step by step tips
Here is a straightforward sequence that works well for most people who can follow simple diagrams:
- Attach the legs and levelers to the main frame. Use the included bolts and washers. Tighten in a crisscross pattern to avoid warping the frame.
- Secure the end panels to enclose the playing field. Ensure the screws do not protrude into the playing surface; we want clean contact with the ball not a napkin-fold of wood.
- Install the rods and the counterbalanced foosball figures if included in your kit. Make sure the rods rotate smoothly before seating the players. Lubrication is optional; most people benefit from a little light silicone spray on the rod tracks.
- Drop in the ball return assembly, then test a few practice shots to confirm the alignment. If you hear squeaking or feel binding, backtrack to the leg levelers and the anodized surfaces where the rods pass through the end plates.
- Finally, calibrate the players. If your players lean or wiggle on contact, you might need to adjust the mounting screws for a stable stance. The goal is to avoid any wobble that disrupts the rhythm of the ball.
### Common issues and fixes
- Ball gets stuck near the goal: Check if the goal mouth is aligned and the ball return tubes are clear. A quick realignment often fixes this.
- Rods feel stiff at first use: Give them a gentle run with the handles to loosen up. A tiny amount of lubricant on the rod tracks can help; avoid over-lubricating as this can cause dust to accumulate.
- Uneven playing surface: Revisit leg levelers and ensure the frame sits squarely on the floor. A slight tilt can drastically alter ball trajectories.

## Tips and tricks for optimizing your experience
### Accessorizing for better play
Consider adding a few accessories to enhance your enjoyment. A set of extra balls can reduce downtime between rounds, and a quality ball rack helps keep the playing field tidy. ### Tactics to win more games
For a home table, you want to master a few reliable tactics. Focus on quick short passes along the back line to pull the goalie out of position, then execute a center shot with a strong follow-through to catch the opposing defense off balance. Keep your wrists loose, use controlled flicks, and avoid over-swinging as it often leads to misdirected shots. Practicing with a partner who plays different styles helps you adapt and improves your own game. The more you play, the more you learn the table quirks and the less the ball seems to have a mind of its own.
### Maintenance routines that pay off
In addition to cleaning, regularly check the alignment of players and ensure that the rods rotate without binding. A light wipe with a PTFE-based lubricant on moving parts can help; just avoid heavy greases that attract dust and gum up the bearings. If you hear strange noises, inspect the joints and bearings for looseness. A simple tighten here and there goes a long way toward a quiet and smooth playing experience.
