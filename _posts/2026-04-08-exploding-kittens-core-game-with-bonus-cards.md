## What is Exploding Kittens Core Game with Bonus Cards
The core of this product is a deck built around the central hazard exploding kitten card. If you draw an exploding kitten, you blow up and the round ends badly for you — unless you play a defuse card that saves your bacon. Defuse cards give you a lifeline, often including extra items like cat cards or other items that can disrupt the chain of events. The bonus cards in this edition add new flavors to the mix — extra misdirection, quirky effects, and wild interactions that can turn a safe draw into a teeth grinding moment of hilarity. The goal is simple: stay alive while you press your luck and try to pass the hot potato of doom to someone else. See the future or laugh at the chaos; either way you win if you survive to the next round.

## How to play in 5 minutes
The rules are intentionally lightweight. On your turn you do a few things in order: draw a card from the pile, or play a card from your hand to cause effect, or pass. If you draw an exploding kitten you are out unless you have a defuse card that saves you. Defuse cards let you reinsert the kitten into the deck and continue playing. The See the Future card lets you peek at the next few cards, providing a moment of calm before the storm. The Attack and Skip cards create pressure by changing who takes the next draw, while Shuffle mixes the order and adds a little chaos to the plan. The core loop is quick and friendly to new players, but the timing of when to use a card can feel like a mini game within the game.

## Card types explained
### Base kit
The base kit contains a handful of action cards that shape the flow of the round. Defuse gives you a lifeline, See the Future promises a peek at the top cards, Attack forces the next player to deal with extra cards, Skip lets you pass, and Shuffle can turn the deck order into a roulette wheel. Then there are Cat Cards which may look cute but require partner play to unleash their power. Exploding Kitten is the danger at the center of the deck, a bomb with a furry face that ruins your day.

### Bonus cards explained
The bonus cards you get with this edition add new twists. Some bonus cards let you force an extra draw on another player, some transfer control of the deck, and others create chain reactions that can snowball into a dramatic comeback or a dramatic meltdown. These cards are designed to be aspirational and a little cheeky, in keeping with the overall tone of the game. They increase the chaos, which is exactly what a party game is supposed to do after one round of normal play.

## Strategy and player dynamics
If you want to win in Exploding Kittens you need to fuse luck with what you can control. You can influence how the deck flows by using See the Future early on to map the next few cards and time your Defuse holds for moments when the pressure climbs. You can bluff by playing a card that signals you know more than you actually do, or by pretending you forgot a card you actually hold. The presence of bonus cards adds a new layer; their effects tend to reward players who track who has what in their hand and who is likely to be unlucky when the next draw lands. Remember to observe your opponents; a tell can reveal when someone is holding a Defuse or a life saving card that could alter the outcome of the round. The core idea is that you are managing risk, not chasing perfect information, and the chaos makes the game feel fresh with every round.

## Strategy tips for beginners
- Save Defuse cards for the late rounds when the risk is highest. 
- Use See the Future at just the right moment to avoid running into a bad sequence. 
- Dont be afraid to pass or cancel your own draw with an appropriate card, even if it means delaying your doom by a turn or two. 
- Cat Cards pair best when you can chain cat effects together. 
- In the bonus deck, look for effects that shift the card flow or force the next player to draw extra cards. 
- Keep track of who has what type of card and adjust your guesses accordingly.
