## Maintenance and care: keeping the dream alive

Caring for this rug is surprisingly straightforward. Regular vacuuming on a low setting is recommended to avoid rough agitation of the fibers. The low pile means you won’t have to battle with a roller brush that keeps getting stuck on stray threads. When accidents happen, blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid harsh chemicals—if it’s a fancy stain in your life, a mild soap + water solution is usually sufficient. Always test any cleaning solution on a small inconspicuous corner first, because even your floor deserves respect.

Rotation is a good habit for long-term color and wear balance. Rotating every 6 to 12 months helps ensure consistent wear across high-traffic zones. And yes, you can absolutely move this rug around like a deer in a sitcom, though you might want a friend to lend a hand if you’ve got a larger space or a stubborn coffee table core that insists on staying in place.
