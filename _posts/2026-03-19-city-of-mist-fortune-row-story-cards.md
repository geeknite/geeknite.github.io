## What are Story Cards?

Story Cards in Fortune Row are a supplementary toolkit designed to spark scenes, twist plots, and provide narrative prompts that feel personal to the city’s mythic underworld. Each card carries modular prompts you can mix and match with your current arc: a character vulnerability, a local rumor, a supernatural thread, a moral dilemma, or a consequence card that pushes a scene into a new gear. The core premise is simple: instead of rigid plot hooks, you’ve got a living city that negotiates with the players. These cards are not just random prompts; they are designed to dovetail with the City of Mist core mechanics, particularly the way GMs describe the city and how players declare their masks’ actions and consequences. The design intent is to give the GM something tactile to run with, and to give players a sense that Fortune Row is a map with moving parts, not a static set of bullet points.

If you’re used to deck-building prompts or table-aided adventures, you’ll recognize the value proposition: reduce dead air, increase character-driven drama, and give everyone a moment in the spotlight that feels as if the city itself is leaning in to eavesdrop.

As with any such tool, the payoff hinges on how you use it. The cards are not a safety net for bad sessions; they are a mood amplifier. They may nudge a hero toward a conflict you were planning and still leave you the freedom to improvise. If your table enjoys communal storytelling that ricochets between myth and street-level grit, this is a card deck that will probably find a home on the table and in the GM's hand like a trusted cigarette lighter—easy to flick open, bright enough to illuminate a moment, and a little smoky at the edges.

## Fortune Row Overview: The Card System at a Glance

Fortune Row Story Cards are built around a few core categories, each designed to evoke a different kind of story beat:

- Local Secrets: Clues tied to a specific area in Fortune Row that can twist a scene toward a neighborhood drama or a hidden conspiracy.
- Character Threads: Prompts that pull on a player’s mask, a personal vow, or a weakness that begs to be tested.
- Mythic Echoes: Vintage City of Mist flavor that ties the urban fantasy vibe to mythic archetypes—cards that remind you that the myth behind the mask has a life of its own.
- Consequences: When a decision leads to fallout, these cards seed the fallout with nuanced, believable outcomes rather than vague punishments.
- Twist and Turn: Cards that introduce an unexpected pivot mid-scene, forcing players to adapt their plans on the fly.

The card art is thematically consistent with the game’s mood: smoky, stylized, and rich with texture. The physical cards feel sturdy enough to survive a table fight over who gets to draw first but light enough to shuffle without choreographing a windstorm across the room. The typography is legible, and the prompts are written in a way that invites interpretation rather than dictating outcomes.

## How to Use Fortune Row Story Cards at the Table

Using Story Cards well is a game in itself. Here are practical guidelines to get the most out of Fortune Row without turning your table into a card-ticking exercise:

### For the GM: Framing and Flow

- Begin with a quick mood setup. Tell the players the emotional baseline you want for the session segment and then drop a card that aligns with that mood. The players will adapt and lean into the atmosphere.
- Use a tiered approach. Start with Local Secrets to ground the scene in Fortune Row, then bring in Mythic Echoes to elevate the stakes. Finish with Consequences to close the arc with meaningful outcomes.
- Allow cards to steer, not to force. If a card suggests a twist that doesn’t fit the current storyline, reframe it as a foreshadow or a hint rather than a hard break. The city has many doors; you don’t need to slam them all.

### For the Players: Owning the Moment

- Treat each Card as a signature beat. If your hero has a vow, a card that touches that vow should be a moment to declare what the vow costs in this city. The payoff is character growth, not just plot progress.
- Use cards to reveal the city’s mythology. When a Mythic Echo card lands, lean into the legend behind your mask. The city loves myths, and your character might be the vessel through which one emerges in Fortune Row.
- Don’t rush. Allow a card to breathe. A good card doesn’t just push the scene forward; it invites players to interpret, negotiate, and decide together how to respond to the city’s ambiguous guidance.

## DIY and House Rules: Making the Cards Your Own

Fortune Row is friendly to house rules. Here are a few ideas to customize the deck for your table without losing the product’s core benefits:

- Create a Deck of Two: Split the deck into two piles for session pacing — one for local secrets that ground the scene, and one for echoes that raise the mythic stakes. Draw one from each during pivotal moments.
- Animate a Card: When a card is drawn, the GM can describe a lingering effect that persists into the next scene. This gives you a tangible thread to carry forward rather than resetting every session.
- Tighten the Text: If your table prefers concise prompts, nominate a single sentence to summarize each card and remove any extra wording. The type of prompts remains the same; you’re just trimming the fat to fit quicker play.
