## What is Dixit and why does it feel like sorcery in a box?

Dixit is a game about storytelling with artwork that belongs in a surreal art gallery and on a fever dream playlist. Each round, one player becomes the storyteller and picks a clue — a word, a sentence, a mood, or even a fragment of a song lyric. The rest of the players select a card from their hand whose artwork best matches the storyteller’s clue. The cards are shuffled and revealed, and players secretly vote on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. Points are awarded to the storyteller if at least one but not all players pick their card, while the players who guessed the storyteller’s card also score, and everyone else gets nothing. It’s a game about interpretation, empathy, and the delicate balance between giving too obvious a clue and too cryptic a one.

Dixit shines because no two rounds feel quite the same. The variety of illustrations, by a rotating stable of artists, makes strategy feel less about brute force and more about vibe, mood, and shared cultural references you all pretend you didn’t notice in the last round. It’s a game that begs for storytelling tricky enough to require a mind palace, but forgiving enough that a chaotic brunch crew can still join the fun without needing a PhD in metaphor. If you’ve ever tried to describe your dream to a friend and watched them misinterpret it as a wet dachshund riding a unicycle, you have a taste of what Dixit asks of you.

## How Dixit 2019 plays: the nuts and bolts without the boring bits

### Setup and round flow

To begin, shuffle the storytelling deck and deal six cards to each player. Each player keeps their cards secret from others but visible to themself. A storyteller for the round is chosen (often the last storyteller rotates or is selected by some whimsy like the flip of a Lemur card, if you’re into silly rules). The storyteller thinks up a clue — this could be a phrase, a single word, a line from a song, a mood, or a quirky idea that ties to one of their cards. The trick is to be evocative enough for those who know you, yet obscure enough to keep others from guessing with the confidence of a GPS.

Each other player selects a card from their hand that best matches the storyteller’s clue and places it face-down. All cards are then revealed, and everyone votes for which card they think is the storyteller’s. Scoring is where the dance gets spicy: if no one or everyone guesses the storyteller’s card, the storyteller gets nothing, and the round fizzles. If exactly some, but not all, players guess correctly, the storyteller and those who picked the correct card each score points. This creates a wonderful tension: you want to be clever but not too obscure; you want to reward players who understand your vibe, but you don’t want to alienate the table with your hyper-niche humor.

### Turn structure and pacing

Play proceeds around the table with a storyteller each round. The pacing hinges on the participants’ familiarity with each other and their ability to channel a shared sense of whimsy. Dixit is not a power-game; it’s a social game where the art and phrasing do the heavy lifting. You’ll see table talk that oscillates between a ridiculous interpretation of a card and a sharp, almost scholarly deduction about why that interpretation makes sense given the storyteller’s earlier clues. In practice, rounds tend to accelerate into a chorus of groans and giggles as someone nails a punchline, someone else misreads the pun, and the rest of the room slides into that delicious middle ground where everyone is pretending to be a literary critic while secretly planning the next round’s clue around a pop culture reference the group last watched at 3 a.m.

### Scoring and strategy without spreadsheets

The scoring system in Dixit is beautifully simple, yet it rewards cleverness. A few core ideas tend to emerge in experienced groups:

- The brave but reasonable clue: it should be evocative enough that it could apply to the storyteller’s card but not so obvious that every player would select it. If you find yourself shouting your clue with the confidence of a lecturer delivering a thesis, you’re probably leaning towards overkill. Style matters as much as substance in Dixit.
- Card selection as a social signal: your vote isn’t just about matching a card; it’s about signaling your relationship with the storyteller’s persona. This makes it a fantastic social exercise in reading people and their shared in-jokes.
- The risk-reward of guessing: if you think you know the storyteller’s card, your best move is often to vote that way and risk giving away the farm. If you’re wrong or indifferent, you’ve still learned something about the group dynamics for the next round.

In the 2019 edition, you’ll likely notice that experienced players lean into a rhythm: a few easier clues early on to warm everyone up, followed by more nuanced clues as players get a vibe for the storyteller’s style. It’s a subtle social negotiation that benefits from a comfortable group and a willingness to embrace the absurd.
