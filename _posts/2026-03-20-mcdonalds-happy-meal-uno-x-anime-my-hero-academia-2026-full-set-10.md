## What’s in the 2026 Set?

The full set is 10 cards, each one a fusion of UNO’s familiar shapes and My Hero Academia aesthetics. The characters represented here aren’t just fan service; they’re deliberate design choices that nod to the hero hierarchy in the anime. If you’ve watched Deku go 0 to 100 in 3 episodes, you’ll recognize the familiar faces on each card—Deku, Bakugo, Todoroki, Uravity, and more—each reimagined as color-coded UNO action cards. - Red Draw Two: Bakugo, because explosions? Just like his personality, this card forces your opponent to draw and groan. The art twist is that Bakugo’s quirk-ified explosions are illustrated as a winged blast behind the number. - Green Reverse: Midoriya’s hero growth arc gets translated into a reverse that changes the tempo of the table. The green hues feel like a fresh growth spurt—literally. He’s not just turning the flow; he’s turning your expectations about who wins family game night.
- Yellow Wild Card (Hero Power): All Might, because what’s a cross-promotional set without a big, smiling tyrant of good vibes who can change the color of the board with a wink? The Wild Card with All Might reminds you that leadership isn’t about power so much as the ability to cheerfully enforce your own set of rules for the night.
- Purple Draw Four: Uravity, a nod to her gravity-based abilities, with a four-card draw consequence. It’s a reminder that sometimes helping your friend isn’t about mercy but about seeing who survives the gravity of your kitchen table drama.
- Orange Skip (Plus 1): Shoto Todoroki again (we like to keep the design cohesive, okay?), but this time the orange is a sunrise of new matches and a reminder that you’ve got one more turn to show your most dramatic UNO face.
- Pink Card (Color Change): MHA’s best ensemble moment with a color-change twist. The pink hue feels like a punchy hero moment in the rainy evening, a mood change button for when your family refuses to accept your last draw as “strategic genius.”
- Light Blue Reverse 2 (Double Take): A quick-draw reversal that seems to say, “Are you sure you want to take that?” while Deku’s eyes look mischievous in the corner. It’s a gentle reminder that sequencing matters and sometimes the table needs a reset.
- Green +2: Froppy (Tsuyu) or another favorite hero depending on the card batch. The concept stays consistent: when you play this, you’re not just adding points; you’re adding a storyline to the pile.
- Red Wild Draw Four (Promo variant): The ultimate chaos card with a hero’s grin. Use it wisely; it’s the weapon of choice when you want to end a back-and-forth with a dramatic flourish that would make a class president proud.

Each card is designed with bold, glossy art that looks at home on a collector’s shelf and as a display on your coffee table when you’re not playing. The design choice is purposeful: the set balances the familiar UNO color palette with the My Hero Academia aesthetic in a way that doesn’t scream “merch promo” but rather invites you to pretend you’re a hero on a debt-ridden night off from class and hero work. In practical play, the 10-card set behaves like a regular UNO deck with a stylish skin.
