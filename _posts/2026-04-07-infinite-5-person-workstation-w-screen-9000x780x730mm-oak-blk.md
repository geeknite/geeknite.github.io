## Overview

If you ever dreamed of a piece of furniture that looks like it could double as a starship console and a group study hub at the same time, the Infinite 5 Person Workstation with Screen in oak blk is probably your dream-come-true. This beast clocks in at a whopping 9000x780x730 mm, which translates to roughly the length of a small asteroid, the width of a velvet rope at a tech convention, and the height of a very ambitious mountain of paperwork. It is marketed as a five person workstation with integrated screen capability, designed for open offices, studios, or that one coworking space where every employee pretends to be busy while quietly live-streaming their cat’s latest thunderstorm video to the team chat.

In this review, we will explore what makes the Infinite workstation both a gift and a curse, depending on your perspective, your space, and your tolerance for the sound of a thousand keyboards clacking in perfect harmony. Spoiler: if you have a small office or a mind for minimalism, this is likely not the desk for you. If you crave epic scale and modularity, you might have found your ceremonial throne.

### Design philosophy and first impressions

From the moment you unbox this thing (which takes a crane, a small team of movers, and perhaps a ceremonial drumroll), you get the sense that the designers wanted to build a fortress for five colleagues who never agree on printer placement. The oak blk finish has a stubbornly premium vibe: deep wood tones meet a matte black frame, like a lumberjack meeting a cyberpunk hacker in a mutual admiration society. It looks heavy, it feels heavy, and it weighs heavy with the weight of expectations. This is less a desk and more a platform for collaborative warfare and impromptu stand-up routines about sprint planning.

### Build quality and materials

Material choice is where this product shines or whines depending on your standards. The primary panels are solid oak with a veneer that resists fingerprints and coffee spills just enough to stay practical. The blk components are powder-coated aluminum or steel, depending on the batch, with a finish that hides the occasional scratch like a ninja hides in plain sight. The joints are boastful in their solidity; you can rely on the thing to remain level even if your team treats it like a drumkit during a product strategy session.

One thing to note: at dimensions of 9000x780x730 mm, this is not a desk you slide under a standard ceiling. It demands real estate, ceiling height, and an architectural plan that would normally be drafted by an architect with a strong dependency on coffee. If your office layout is anything less than open-plan cathedral, you may need to rethink the space planning with your facilities manager. Still, if space is your sandbox and collaboration your game, this fortress can become the centerpiece of a modern team setup.

### The layout and ergonomic considerations

The Infinite is designed with five workstations in a shared environment, each with its own screen module mounted at a comfortable eye level. The layout is essentially a long spine with five seating pods or stations branching off. It attempts to strike a balance between personal space and team visibility, which is a fancy way to say you can see your coworkers’ screens without getting promoted to a shrine of passive-aggressive post-its.

Ergonomically, the design tries to support neutral posture with adjustable monitor arms, cable management channels, and a knee-friendly under-desk space. The monitor screens are integrated into the desk or mounted as modular units depending on the model you choose. The result is a workspace that encourages conversation, but also comes with the caveat that it may encourage everyone to shout across the desk rather than step away for a private call.

### Setup, assembly, and real-world usability

Now comes the fun part—assembly. If you enjoy a puzzle that would frustrate a quantum computer, you’ll feel right at home here. The assembly process, depending on the kit, is not glamorous. It involves aligning panels, securing industrial fasteners, and then performing the classic desk self-check: is everything perfectly level? If you’re lucky, you’ll finish with a sense of accomplishment and a few extra screws that somehow look like decorative accents until you realize you left them out of the keyboard tray hinge. It’s not impossible, but it is not a weekend IKEA project either. It’s a project that requires planning, space, and a crew who won’t mind carrying this behemoth through doors that could double as submarine hatchways.

If you are installing this in a real office, plan for a workday or two of getting the space unpacked, laid out, and wired with power, data, and the right cable conduits. The built-in screen modules are a saving grace for teams that want shared visibility: you can run a single conference feed for the whole team or deploy multiple screens for each user. If you are writing code, designing graphics, or playing the latest VR productivity game, this setup can do it all without strain—assuming you’ve budgeted the power supplies and cooling properly.

### Connectivity, screens, and multimedia integration

The screen integration is the star here. The Infinite 5 Person Workstation often ships with integrated display modules that can be configured per user. If you opted for external panels, you’ll want to check the height adjustability and arm reach to avoid the classic desk crunch—where your monitor sits two inches too high and your eyes start sending you gentle but persistent reminders about posture.

In practice, you’ll be able to display code editors, dashboards, online meetings, and, if you are a multitasker, a streaming panel for the occasional productivity meme. The connectivity is robust but not magical; expect to handle HDMI, USB-C, and Ethernet through the concealed channels. Wireless devices pair up just fine, and if you are a gamer in the office (which some teams secretly are), you may have enough latency headroom to pretend this is a land-locked arcade cabinet. Just don’t attempt competitive speedrunning the Kanban board, or you might trigger a cascade of urgent status updates that could resemble an emergency meeting in a sci-fi movie.

### Aesthetic, finishes, and maintenance

Oak blk is not just a finish; it is a lifestyle choice. The oak veneer feels warm and natural, while the black accents give a sleek, futuristic edge. It treads the line between rustic and cyberpunk with surprising grace. The surface is wipe-cleanable, assuming you avoid the classic culinary experiments your team keeps planning for lunch and learn sessions. The finish hides minor scratches well, but a serious oops with a hot coffee mug will demand some patching or refinishing. You’ll want to set a policy: no mugs on the main work surface, and if you must, place them on the designated coffee capture pad that comes with the kit. In the long run, the maintenance becomes a ritual: wipe, polish, marvel at the grain, repeat.

Dirt and dust collect in the cable management troughs, but that is a fair trade for the tidy look and the sense that you can perform a clean desk deployment without sacrificing the shared space. If you care about longevity, you’ll appreciate that the oak tones will age gracefully, developing a patina that makes the unit feel more like a veteran part of the office team rather than a sleek celebrity guest.

### Five-person ergonomics in practice

Let us imagine a typical day with five humans, five laptops, and five monitors. The Infinite 5 Person Workstation is designed to make this feasible without sacrificing the ability to walk around and discuss project updates without shouting. The arrangement keeps everyone in the loop and reduces the need for everyone to crowd around a single screen—one of the universal productivity sins of older open-plan offices. It also invites the gentle murmurs of collaboration: someone points at a screen from across the table and a cascade of ideas flows, like a well-choregraphed dance of productivity. That said, there is a trade-off. The sheer footprint means you must be mindful about doorways, lifts, and the ability for maintenance staff to access the rear panels for cable management after month three of a long project.

For teams that thrive on collaboration but hate the chaos of an open-plan cavern, this bench can become a central hub. For teams that prefer quiet, isolated work, the shared surface could feel too open, and you might end up with the fourth wall as your only silent partner. It is all a matter of fit, and the Infinite is best approached as a modular hub rather than a fixed throne. If you want to reconfigure, you can often add or remove seats, swap screen arms, or migrate to a slightly different layout as your team evolves. The modular mindset is a nice touch, one of those practical touches that makes you feel like your furniture is playing nice with your workflow rather than fighting it.

### Assembly of the full kit and potential pitfalls

A real-world warning about assembly: do not attempt this alone unless you enjoy long, meditative puzzle solving with an audience. It is a project that benefits from teamwork, good lighting, and a plan. I recommend assigning a lead installer, a cable magician to coax the cables into neat channels, and a safety officer to remind everyone to wear gloves during the heavy lifting. The upside of a complicated assembly is the sense of accomplishment and the satisfaction of a workspace that truly feels built for five. The downside is the potential for a few days of temporary chaos and a few extra trips to the hardware store for assortments of bolts and washers that somehow always go missing, only to reappear after the fifth cycle of this review and a lot of head-scratching.

In terms of long-term reliability, the system holds up well under normal office conditions. The finish is resilient enough for daily use, and the structural elements show little flex under typical office loads. If your office has a particularly aggressive use-case—think hands-on prototypes, heavy equipment maintenance, or a crowd of software developers who insist on pulling moveable work surfaces around like a convoy—you might want to invest in an extended warranty and a maintenance plan. Otherwise, you can expect a long and useful life out of this unit, so long as you respect its proportions and give it space to breathe.

As always, compare with alternatives that offer similar modularity and screen options, and consider if you truly need an entire five-person configuration in one continuous surface or if a pair of smaller pods would be the better fit for your space.

### Aesthetic and maintenance notes

The oak blk finish holds up well to daily office life. You will get natural warmth from the wood with a contemporary edge from the black accents. It’s a combination that rarely fails to impress clients who walk through the space and notice that the desk looks as serious as a board meeting but as welcoming as a coffee bar. Keep the surface clean with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner designed for wood finishes. Don’t go full technology rebel and use harsh solvents; you will regret it when the surface begins to show wear that looks less like patina and more like a science experiment gone wrong.

For cable management, the troughs are adequate and can be configured to keep most of the tangle out of sight. The goal is to maintain a clean, linear look while preserving enough space for five people to reach their gear with minimal fuss. If you maintain discipline about cable routing during setup, you will be rewarded with a desk that not only looks good but also reduces the usual desk-lawnmower clutter that tends to plague large open offices after a year of use.

The Infinite 5 Person Workstation with Screen in oak blk is a bold piece of furniture that proves you can marry monumental scale with modern ergonomics.
