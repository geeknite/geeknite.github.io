## Care and maintenance: keeping the seafoam serene

Caring for a wool rug isn’t a mystery, but it’s not something you can ignore like a gym membership you’ll cancel later. Here’s a practical care plan:

- vacuum regularly: A weekly pass with a gentle suction helps keep wool fibers free of dust that can dull the color and texture. Use a vacuum without a strong beater bar on the highest setting to avoid fiber damage.
- blot spills immediately: If coffee or juice spills happen, blot with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which pushes spill into the fibers and can create a lasting stain. For stubborn stains, consider a wool-safe cleaner or consult a professional cleaner.
- rotation: Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and prevent obvious traffic patterns forming in your living room. This is particularly useful if you have a high-traffic area or a gaming chair that does more footwork than a squat rack.
- professional cleaning: A yearly or bi-yearly professional cleaning can extend the rug’s life. Wool benefits from proper detergent and rinse methods that only a specialist can deliver without leaving behind residue.
