## Materials and Craftsmanship

### The fiber story

The silk mix is chosen for softness, luster, and a lighter ecological footprint than pure viscose or heavy synthetics. Silk is a renewable fiber with surprisingly robust properties when blended properly. A well-executed loom-knotted rug distributes weight evenly across its surface, reducing wear on the highest-traffic areas of a room. The result is a rug that feels luxurious underfoot during those long evenings of streaming and snack loops.

### The knot density and looms

Hand knotting is a labour of love. Knot density is the main influence on durability and texture. Higher knot counts usually translate to more precise patterns and longer wear life. In a solid blue rug, knot density matters less for pattern fidelity and more for even color along the fiber. The loom is set by skilled artisans who adjust tension, ensuring the pile remains uniform. The result is a rug that looks almost machine-made, but you know it is not, which is the best kind of deception – the ethical kind.

### Dye and colorfastness

Solid blue can go sideways if dyes bleed. A reputable rug like BBLSM111 should pass colorfastness tests, becoming resistant to color transfer from UV exposure and everyday wear. Expect a shade that deepens slightly with age and light exposure; that posits a patina of personality rather than fading to a questionable denim shade.

## Installation and Care

### Installation notes

Install the BBLSM111 by unrolling it in place, letting it relax for a few hours, and flattening any curling edges with light weight. If your floor has a reflective surface, a non-slip rug pad can help prevent creeping and keep the rug in place during those intense sofa-surfing sessions.

### Cleaning and maintenance

Regular vacuuming is your best friend. Use a vacuum without a beater bar on high-pile settings to avoid pulling fibers. Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber fraying. For spot cleaning, use a mild detergent diluted with water and test on a small inconspicuous area first. Avoid harsh chemicals—this is not a chemistry lab, though you can pretend for the drama.

## Durability and Longevity

A well-crafted hand-knotted rug should last decades with proper care. Silk blends benefit from the cream of durability and the velvet of resilience. Expect slight natural variability in pile height, which is normal in handmade pieces and contributes to its character rather than being a defect. If you’re worried about heavy furniture marks, consider furniture coasters or small felt pads to distribute weight more evenly.

## Comparisons: Why this over 100% Wool or Polyester Rugs

- 100% wool rugs bring warmth and natural resilience but may require more careful dye handling and can be heavier. The silk mix can achieve a similar feel with less weight and a shimmer that holds up well in modern interiors.
- Polyester or synthetic blends often win on price and stain resistance but sacrifice the tactile softness and longevity that come with natural fibers. If sustainability is your priority, the silk mix with careful production practices is a middle path that offers compromise rather than sacrifice.
