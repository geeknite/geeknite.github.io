## What is All Out Dice? A quick overview
All Out Dice is exactly what it sounds like: more dice, more chaos, more opportunities to fail gloriously (and occasionally succeed at the last second). The kit typically includes a set of new survivor dice, new zombie dice, and a handful of symbol tokens that modify outcomes in interesting ways. The philosophy behind it is to simulate the feeling of a squad in a tight spot, where every decision carries the weight of a scream and a clatter of plastic.

From a design perspective, the dice are crafted to be readable at a glance. The faces are optimized for clarity in the heat of a box-lunch-sized skirmish: bold icons, high-contrast colors, and a design language that says you’re playing a modern board game, not a math textbook with a bite habit.

## Unboxing and components
### What’s in the box
The All Out Dice box typically includes:

- A handful of zombie dice with their own twist mechanics

The quality of the plastic is solid, the colors pop, and the symbols are large enough to read under the glow of a post-apocalyptic basement light. ### Dice design and readability
The dice themselves carry a two-pronged advantage over typical base-game dice: better readability and more expressive symbols. This matters a lot when you’re sprinting through a hive of walkers and you desperately need to know if your roll equals a critical hit, a disaster, or a momentary lull in the apocalypse. The symbols are designed to be distinguishable from a distance, which is essential when you’re trying to plan two turns ahead while a zombie with a garish grin is closing in.

The color palette is bold but not gratuitous; it helps separate factions and actions without triggering any color-confusion-induced panic. Some players will appreciate the extra contrast when playing in dim corners of a gaming night—an environment many of us know too well from late-night campaigns.

## How All Out Dice changes gameplay dynamics
### The core mechanic shift
With All Out Dice, you’re not just rolling for hits and misses; you’re rolling for outcomes that can chain into more actions, create opportunities, or trigger malfunctions that put your plan in jeopardy. The new dice introduce symbols that interact with existing rules in clever ways: some symbols grant extra actions, some convert a failed attempt into a rescue operation, and others add risk-reward elements that keep everyone honest about the odds.

This shift changes the pacing. The base game rewards careful planning and solid execution, but All Out Dice rewards players who can read the table, predict zombie behavior, and lean into a bit of reckless optimism when the odds look terrifying. It’s a design choice that adds spice without destroying the core feel of Zombicide: teamwork in the face of a tide of undead cardboard misery.

### Symbol ecology and synergy
If there’s one thing All Out Dice does well, it’s creating an ecosystem of symbols that interact with the board in meaningful ways. You’ll see combinations where a symbol on one die enables a second die to re-roll, or where a token on the table changes the fate of an entire corridor sweep. The design intent is clear: players should feel like their choices matter in every phase, not just during their big finishing blow.

Such a system can create dramatic swings. A single favorable roll can cascade into a sequence where multiple enemies fall, doors open, and the voice chat erupts with triumphant memes and groans. It’s this cadence—the glorious, chaotic rhythm—that makes All Out Dice so entertaining on the table.

## Pros and cons in typical Geeknite fashion
### Pros
- Enhanced action economy: more chances to act, and more moments where you feel like you’re steering the apocalypse rather than simply reacting to it.
- Clear symbol language: you don’t have to memorize a dozen obscure rules to understand what’s happening on your turn.
- Durable components: you’ll beat this set up and tear it down multiple times without worry.
- Replayability: more dice means more combinations, which keeps sessions fresh longer than you’d expect from a single expansion.

### Cons
- All that extra chaos can overwhelm casual players: if you’re new to Zombicide, the learning curve gets steeper with All Out Dice in the mix.
- Balance trade-offs: some tables report certain symbol combos leaning a bit strong in high-stakes scenarios, which can skew early campaigns if you’re not careful with house rules.
- Table talk required: to maximize value, players need to coordinate and communicate effectively, which is great until someone claims the dice are cursed.

## Solo and co-op viability
All Out Dice scales surprisingly well with different player counts. In a tight 2-player game, the extra dice can accelerate tension, producing bursts of simultaneous action that feel cinematic. In larger groups, the chaos multiplies, but the shared narrative benefits still shine through as players cheer each other on and improvise strategies on the fly.

If you’re a solo player, the expansion can provide a more dynamic auto-play feel—depending on the rules variant you adopt. The extra dice can simulate a more aggressive zombie environment, giving the solo player more opportunities to flex planning skills and manage risk across multiple “counter-moves” in a single turn. Just be aware that a little self-imposed chaos goes a long way in solo modes; you’ll want to predefine a few house rules so you don’t burn out on the climbing pile of dice after the first act.

## Campaign integration and scalability
All Out Dice shines brightest when integrated into ongoing campaigns. It introduces a natural hook for narrative arcs: a new resource pool, a set of encounters that become more or less deadly depending on how the dice align, and the possibility of temporary allies who join the survivors for a limited arc.

One practical tip for campaign play is to rotate who handles the “special symbols” on a per-session basis. It keeps the table engaged and prevents anyone from dominating the dice narrative. If your group is into modular campaigns, you can design scenarios around specific symbol clusters, encouraging players to adapt on the fly rather than blindly following a fixed plan.

For players who maintain a living campaign log, All Out Dice offers fertile ground for plot twists: a cluster of favorable rolls might unlock a new survivor’s backstory or reveal a hidden safehouse that becomes a recurring haunt for future sessions. In short, it’s not just a game upgrade; it’s a storytelling engine that runs alongside the dice cannot-quit enthusiasm of your table.
