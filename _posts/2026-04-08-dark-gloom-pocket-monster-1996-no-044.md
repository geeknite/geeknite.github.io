## Preservation, Storage, and Care
Vintage cards deserve more than a casual sleeve. The best way to preserve Dark Gloom is with patience and a plan. Here are practical tips that combine care with geeky joy:

- Keep away from direct sunlight. The sun is a mischievous force that makes colors fade and corners bend in the worst possible way. If your display shelf happens to double as a window ledge, consider UV-protective glass or a cloth shield that keeps the card’s personality intact.
- Use rigid card protectors and a proper binder. The combination of a top-loader and a binder insert keeps your No. 044 in shape while you debate whether it’s a 1996 masterpiece or a time-travel device.
- Mind the humidity. Vintage paper loves a stable climate. A small dehumidifier in your card room a few times a year helps maintain the edges and prevents that dreaded “warped corner” look that becomes a conversation starter for all the wrong reasons.
- Don’t over-clean. Dust is a friend that helps you appreciate the card’s texture. But avoid aggressive cleaning or solvent use; you want the natural patina of time, not the crackling residue of a failed attempt at restoration.
- Store with a narrative. If you’re organizing a display, pair Dark Gloom with a little blurb about its Japanese 1996 context. The collector who reads the backstory while gazing at the glossy surface will thank you with a nod of respect and probably a selfie with their binder in frame.
