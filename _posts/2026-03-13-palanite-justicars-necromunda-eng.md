## Painting and Assembly Tips
Here is a practical painting path that keeps things fast, cinematic, and not too precious for a table that fights with dice, not with friendly competition only:

- Assemble in small squads. Start with the torsos and legs to get good stability on the base. Leave the arms and gear off until the torso is dry to avoid glued awkwardness.
- Prime with a neutral gray or black spray. This ensures you can achieve quick shade and highlight transitions without ending up with weird color shifts from the plastic.
- Base coat metallics first, then block in cloth and armor. This reduces the number of layers you must retrace once you go into washes and highlights.
- Washes are your best friend. A mid tone wash (think a strong gray-brown) will bring out panel lines and insignia without making everything look muddy. A second, darker wash can be used to add depth in creases for dramatic contrast.
- Edge highlighting works wonders on Palanite armor. Use a lighter version of the base metal on the edges to make the models pop from the game board. Do not overdo it. A thin line on edges along major plates is enough to make the model read from across the table.
- The badges and insignia deserve a touch of care. A tiny dot of gold or brass on shoulder pads, and a crisp white or bright enamel for the badges adds character without complicating the painting process.
- Bases should tell a story. Consider a quarry tile texture if you want to evoke an urban hive path, or a rough road texture with rubble to indicate a district that is in a constant state of construction and danger.

## Rules Vibe and Gameplay Philosophy
Even though this is a review that leans into the storytelling side, the Palanite Justicars have a compelling mechanical identity. They are designed to be mobile, authoritative, and capable of influencing the pacing of a game without behaving like a generic power unit. The vibe is less about raw brutality and more about strategic pressure: the ability to control space, threaten key targets with measured firepower, and pressure your opponent into tough decisions.

From a gameplay perspective, you can expect a mix of reliable shooting, decent melee when necessary, and some interesting tools that help the squad manage objectives and map control. The aesthetic of a disciplined force means you will want to pair Palanite Justicars with leadership units who can provide buffs, making them a core piece for midrange control rather than a pure glass cannon or spammy meltagun armadas we sometimes see in the hive. If your goal is to lawfully impose order with style, the Palanite Justicars do deliver that satisfying sense of enforcement and tactical nuance.

In narratives, they are a dream. You can run campaigns where the governorship keeps sending them to enforce a curfew that the gang wants to extend for its own ends. The Justicars do not just shoot; they are a story engine. A well placed Palanite moment can pivot a whole game narrative, especially if you lean into the underhive politics and the tension between the enforcement arm and the more chaotic factions that exploit loopholes in the cratered law.

### Loadouts and Tactics
While the exact weapons and profiles can vary with the edition, the general playstyle centers on controlled aggression and objective locking. A common approach is to use the Justicars as a mobile firing line that can threaten multiple angles and force the opponent to react. They shine when used to protect an objective marker while your other units maneuver for better survivability. They also benefit from terrain that blocks line of sight in tight corridors, turning the game into a chess match where every move is a calculated risk.

A practical tip is to use Palanite Justicars to threaten two objectives at once. If you can force your opponent to split focus, you can handle the rest of your squad to grind out a win. The key is not to over commit; keep the Justicars as a reliable presence, rather than sending them forward to take on a fortified position alone. In a campaign setting, the Justicars make excellent mentors or enforcers for new players who want to learn how to control zones and play around cover without getting overwhelmed by heavier violence options from other gangs.
