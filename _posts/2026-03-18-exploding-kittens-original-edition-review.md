What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition: The game started as a crowd funded project that exploded into a real life beverage of chaos in a box. The core concept is simple and brilliant: draw cards and avoid drawing the exploding kitten card, unless you have a defuse card to save yourself. The deck is a combination of kitten themed bombs, defuse counters, and a variety of action cards that allow you to skip turns, force others to draw, shuffle the deck, and more. The humor comes from the art that features kittens doing questionable things and the light hearted yet savage declarations on each card. The experience is casual but addictive, with enough tension to keep the table grinning through the meltdown.

Gameplay overview: The game starts with a shuffled deck and a target of avoiding explosions. Each player receives a number of defuse cards and the rest of the deck goes into the center. On your turn you can play action cards to alter the course of the draw, or simply draw from the deck, risking the fate of pulling an exploding kitten. If you pull an exploding kitten, you must use a defuse card if you have one, which typically saves you from immediate elimination, after which the damaged deck is reshuffled. The objective is to be the last player standing or the last person not to draw the exploding kitten after the final round. The rules are intentionally simple, and the game is designed to move quickly. You can finish a session in 10 to 20 minutes, depending on how many times the deck decides to mock you. The real magic is in the table talk and the scattergun humor of the art.

Turn structure and card types: On your turn you can either play a card with a special effect or draw a card from the deck. The fun begins when you realize that the deck is not just a stack of random cards but a living entity that can be manipulated by the players. The main categories of cards are exploding kitten cards, defuse cards, and action cards. Exploding kitten cards are bad news; defuse cards are your lifelines; action cards do things like skip your own turn, attack the next player, shuffle the deck, or peek at the top cards. The diversity of action cards keeps the game fresh and ensures no two turns feel the same.

Strategy and risk assessment: The smartest players treat defuse cards as sacred lifelines rather than disposable resources. The decision to play a defuse card early can mean the difference between a meme worthy meltdown and a dignified victory. If you have multiple defuses, you can build a protection plan and attempt to keep the tension on other players. The unpredictable nature of the deck makes bluffing and misdirection crucial. It is not a game about deep strategy; it is a game about reading your table and knowing when to hold the line and when to press your luck. As you gain experience, you will learn to gauge the risk of each draw and to time your defuse plays for maximum clowning.

Run-through of a typical session: The session begins with a shuffling of cards and a quick explanation that invites everyone to ignore the math and focus on the fun. If you want to add an extra layer of comedy, you can set a timer and declare a mini challenge for whoever draws the second exploding kitten.
