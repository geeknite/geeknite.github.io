## What Is Herd Mentality?

Herd Mentality is a party game designed for adults that leans into groupthink, peer pressure, and a good old-fashioned gag reflex when the responses get ridiculous. The basic premise: players write answers to prompts, then everyone simultaneously votes on which answer they think most of the other players will choose. The catch? The goal isn’t to be right; the goal is to ride the wave of what the “herd” is thinking. It’s a game about social deduction, but the clues are often nonsense, making it perfect for mixed groups of friends, families with teenagers (under supervision, obviously), roommates who think they’re hilarious, and that one cousin who always DM’s you memes at 2 a.m.

The game typically comes with a deck of prompt cards, response cards, voting tokens, and a simple scoring system. The prompts skewer everyday life, pop culture, and the little obsessions we pretend aren’t a big deal. You’ll read a prompt, everyone writes a supposed response, then you reveal the responses and try to guess which one most aligns with the rest of the group. The hilarity comes not from your own answer but from how wildly the herd disagrees with you—or agrees with you, depending on how persuasive your bland-but-sincere take is.

## How to Play: The Quick-and-Dirty Guideline

Herd Mentality thrives on quick rounds, spontaneous humor, and collective chaos. Here’s the streamlined flow, so you can start a round in under five minutes.

### Setup
1. Pick a referee for the round (often the person who drew the first prompt card).
2. Each player writes down one answer to the prompt on the prompt cards deck. This is your personal take, your inner voice, your most embarrassing predilection, or whatever silly thing you want to commit to in public.
3. Everyone places their responses into a central pile (or hands them to the referee, depending on your edition and table style).
4. The referee draws a prompt card and reads it aloud, without showing the prompt’s answer options yet.

### The Round
1. Each player selects one of their own response cards that they think best matches what the herd will pick as the most popular answer.
2. Players play their chosen card face-down and the referee shuffles them to ensure confidentiality.
3. All responses are revealed simultaneously. The herd then votes on which response they think will be the most popular among the other players.
4. Points are awarded. If your answer is the herd’s chosen top option, you earn points. If your guess about the herd’s choice is correct, you also earn points. The scoring keeps a delicate balance between predicting the crowd and predicting your friends’ shenanigans.

The rules vary slightly by edition—some include a twist where a player can 'steal' points by challenging an obvious but terrible option—yet the core experience remains: prompt, write, reveal, vote, laugh, repeat.

## Tips, Tricks and House Rules for Maximum Laugh Factor

- Lean into bold options: When in doubt, pick the most outrageous answer you can defend with a straight face. The herd respects audacity, even if it’s nonsense.
- Encourage playful trash-talk: Gentle ribbing can elevate the energy. A few well-timed zingers can turn a dull round into a riot.
- Rotate the ‘herd’: Switch the role of predictor to different players across rounds to keep everyone engaged and prevent the same two or three people from hogging the podium.
- Tailor prompts to your crowd: If your group includes non-native speakers or younger players, choose prompts that rely on universal experiences instead of niche pop-culture references.
- Use themed prompts for variety: Do a 80s nostalgia round, a tech-obsessed round, or a “deserted island with terrible Wi-Fi” round to experiment with mood and context.
- Keep the pace snappy: Timebox the rounds so no one overthinks their answer. The charm is in the quick, often ridiculous, decision-making.
