## Displaying and Caring for Your Card

Protecting a vintage card is part art, part science. Here are a few practical tips for the modern collector who wants to keep the Malcolm-Jamal Warner face looking as fresh as a new pack opening:

- Use a soft sleeve and a rigid top loader to prevent bending. The goal is to keep it flat, not fold it into a tiny origami crane of regret.
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV light is a tiny villain with a cape that loves to fade colors and erase memories of the purple hoodie from the front cover.
- Consider a binder with acid-free pages if you are completing a set. It keeps everything organized and makes it easier to show off to friends without fear that a sneeze could launch your precious cards into another dimension.
- Avoid laminating. The 90s loved a good transformation, but once laminated, your card becomes a permanent member of a museum exhibit called Mistakes You Made in a Moment of Excitement. Not ideal for future resale value or your mom’s sense of decorating taste.

Display tips include creating a small Cosby Show nostalgia corner, complete with a mini shelf, a few extra cards from the set, and a playlist of 90s sitcom intro tunes. The goal is to treat the card like a friend you want to keep around for a long time—someone who offers a smile when you flip the card over and see the memory you were hoping to recall in the first place.
