In-game tips and tricks
------------------------
- Serve variety: mix flat serves with gentle topspin to keep your opponent guessing. The small table rewards quick adjustment more than heavy spins.
- Footwork basics for micro-courts: stay light on your feet, don’t overcommit. Your arms can do most of the work; your legs should keep them balanced.
- Practice drills that don’t require a partner: bounce returns, target zones on the table edges, or solo rallying with the wall to build consistency for those chaotic two-player sessions.
- Battery of warm-up routines: a quick 60-second stretch, then a handful of practice serves, and you’re ready to roll. It’s a gym routine disguised as a game—highly efficient for busy geeks.

Maintenance and care
---------------------
- Wipe the surface after use to remove dust and any sticky residue from snacks and drinks. This helps maintain consistent bounce and prolongs the life of the finish.
- Dry storage is your friend: humidity and moisture are not co-pilots you want in your back-pocket ping-pong adventure.
- Check the net tension occasionally. If it sags, a quick readjustment is usually enough to bring back that satisfying “snap” on returns.
- Keep spare paddles and balls in a small bag for portability. It’s not only practical but also a good way to maintain order when your gaming circle expands beyond two players.
