## Durability and Maintenance: The Real World Test
Durability is a big part of the appeal here. Polypropylene and similar synthetics resist moisture and stains better than natural fibers. Spills are not punishing; most common stains like coffee, sauce, or juice can be blotted up with a cloth and a mild cleaner. It is not a guarantee that you can spill red wine on it and forget about it, but you can show it some respect and the rug will return to its original color much quicker than you expect.

Maintenance tips:
- Vacuum regularly to prevent grit from grinding the fibers into a dull beige. The shorter pile collects dust, but a quick pass with a standard vacuum often suffices.
- Spill management: Blot, do not rub. Rubbing can push the liquid deeper and set stains, particularly with dyes that might bleed.
- Cleaning: For stubborn stains, use a mild solution of water and a gentle detergent, then blot. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade synthetic fibers.
- Rug pad recommended: A quality pad under the rug increases traction and extends the life by cushioning foot traffic and furniture movement. It also helps reduce bending and curling at the edges. If you have wood floors, a pad reduces wear and noise on the surface.
- Sunlight: The Blue Creme geometry is color-stable, but prolonged direct sun exposure can fade a little bit over time. Rotate the rug every so often to distribute wear evenly.

## Cleaning and Care: A Quickstart Guide for Busy Humans
- Routine vacuuming: Use a standard vacuum with adjustable height. Low-pile rugs benefit from more frequent passes to prevent dirt from embedding.
- Spill response: For liquids, blot immediately with a clean cloth. If the spill is sticky, you can use a mild detergent solution on a small area and blot again.
- Stain removal: For common stains, a mixture of water and mild detergent with a gentle scrub is effective. Avoid bleach unless you want a white-blue creme omelette on your rug.
- Rug padding: A good pad reduces wear and keeps edges flat, which reduces curling and tripping hazards. If you want to go hardcore about comfort, tack on a memory-foam pad under the rug for a cloud-like experience.
- Rotation: Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, particularly if your room gets more sun on one side than the other.
