## Setup and assembly
A common question is how long does it take to assemble this behemoth. The answer depends on your level of ambition and your ability to coax a hex wrench into submission. Expect a few hours if you are building solo, but with a friend and a snack break you can likely finish within 2-3 hours. You will need basic tools: screwdrivers, wrenches, a bit of patience, and the mysterious art of interpreting a diagram that looks like it was drawn by someone who does not speak your language.

The good news is that many parts ship pre-assembled, reducing the number of loose pieces to a minimum. The bad news is that the parts are heavy and the alignment must be precise. A level is your best friend in this process. The first step is to attach the legs and stabilize the cabinet. Then you mount the playing rods, which can feel like hoisting a small spaceship. Next comes aligning the players and adding the end caps. Finally, you screw in the scoring units and test a few shots. The initial setup can feel like a mini puzzle, but once you see the finished result you will appreciate the engineering behind it.

In the world of assembly, there is always a trick. Do not rush the alignment of the rods. The players are designed to be perfectly aligned only after you tighten the screws to a t. If you end up with one rod being slightly higher, your shots will travel on a diagonal path that will frustrate you and cause your ego to deteriorate just a little bit.

## Maintenance and care
To keep this table in top shape you will want to maintain the moving parts. Lubricate the rods and bearings periodically using a silicone based spray. Clean the surface regularly with a soft cloth and mild cleaner. Do not use harsh chemicals that can wear the finish. If you spill something sticky on the surface you can wipe it away quickly to prevent staining. A light protective cover when the table is not in use will extend the life of the cabinet and keep dust at bay.

Leather gloves are optional but recommended if you want to treat the rods with extra care and you have the patience of a saint. If your foam hand grips start to wear, you can replace them without needing to disassemble the entire table, depending on your skill. The key here is to keep the play experience consistent; irregular shots and misaligned players will break the rhythm of the game and the vibe of your living room.
