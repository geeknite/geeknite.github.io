## How to Play: A Quick Start Guide

If you already know UNO, you are halfway there. The Spy X Family edition preserves the core structure: match color or number, play action cards to twist turns, and work toward discarding all your cards first. The flavor cards add subtle twists that don’t derail the core rules. Here is a compact guide to get you started:

1) Setup: Shuffle, deal seven cards to each player, place the remainder as the draw pile, and flip the top card to start the discard pile.
2) On your turn: Play a card that matches the color or number of the top discard. If you cannot play, draw one card. Some of the themed cards allow chaining or add new effects; read the booklet for specifics.
3) The Spy X Family twist: The character-themed cards introduce flavor effects that influence the pace and social dynamics. They are designed to be intuitive and quick to apply, so your game night stays breezy.
4) Declare UNO: When you have one card left, you must yell UNO. If you forget and another player catches you, you draw two more cards as penalty. Dramatic, but fair.
5) Winning: The first player to discard all their cards wins the round. The rest tally points from cards left in hand; the typical target is 500 points across multiple rounds, but you can set your own victory condition for a faster or longer night.

The edition remains faithful to UNO while adding character-driven moments that can influence decision-making in unpredictable but entertaining ways. It is approachable for new players and still engaging for veteran UNO lovers who crave a thematic twist.

- Yor Precision: A Yor card lets you skip the next player if you can justify a spy scenario on the fly. This works brilliantly in groups with a good sense of humor and a willingness to role-play a little.
