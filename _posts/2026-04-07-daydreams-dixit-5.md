## Daydreams at a Glance
What exactly is Daydreams? If you’ve played Dixit before, you already know the core: a storyteller picks a clue, everyone else chooses a card from hand that best matches that clue, and then players try to guess which card was the storyteller’s. Daydreams leans into the dreamy, surreal, and wonder-filled side of storytelling without throwing your bluffing instincts to the wolves. The expansion includes new dream-themed artwork and a handful of optional rules designed to encourage more imaginative prompts, more risky clues, and more opportunities for players to shine or hilariously misfire.

### What's new in Daydreams for Dixit 5?
First up, the deck. Daydreams adds a full set of 84 brand-new dream cards. These aren’t just “weird pictures”—though they are delightfully weird—they’re calibrated for storytelling momentum. Some cards feel like visual riddles, others like notes from a dream journal you’d find in a time capsule, and a few would be right at home in a fantasy novel’s dream sequence chapter. The art direction leans more toward surrealism and whimsy than grimdark, which makes it a very friendly expansion for players who love color, texture, and imaginative leaps.

Second, the game flow gets a small but meaningful nudge. The designers toss in a handful of “Dream Prompts” that players can draw when it’s their turn to give a clue. These prompts act like thematic levers: think “A memory you forgot,” “A scene from a lullaby,” or “An object that doesn’t belong in this world.” The prompts don’t replace the core rules; they augment them, nudging players toward more narrative and fewer literal associations. If you’ve ever felt stuck trying to craft a one-liner clue, Daydreams offers a fresh toolkit to get your brain out of the rut and into the clouds.

Third, a couple of small but clever balancing tweaks. The scoring margins get a tiny reshuffle to better reward both the clever storyteller and the players who read the room correctly. It’s nothing earth-shattering, but in practice you’ll notice that Daydreams plays a little longer, a little more investigative, and a little more forgiving of honest misreads—assuming your table isn’t prone to monstrous chaos at every round.

## How Daydreams Changes the Dixit 5 Experience
Daydreams doesn’t attempt to reinvent Dixit’s wheel; it tweaks the feedback loop between clue-givers and guessers to promote richer storytelling without destroying the core mechanic. Here are the practical changes you’ll notice at the table:

### Rule Variants and Flow
- Dream Prompt prompts: On your turn (as storyteller), you can draw a Dream Prompt that can influence the clue you give. The prompt asks you to frame your clue around a dream logic idea—something non-literal, more suggestive. This helps players who struggle with making a concise yet evocative clue.
- Aimed-for balance: The prompts are designed so the storyteller can optionally lean into the surreal, but they aren’t mandatory. If you prefer straight-faced clues, you can ignore them; if you want to push the bounds of imagination, Daydreams gives you a structured path to do just that.

### Scoring Adjustments
The scoring tweaks are subtle but meaningful. The gap between a successful guess and a failed guess is tuned to reward a storyteller who builds a convincing dream-y narrative while still challenging guessers to interpret the dream. In practical terms, you’ll see more round-to-round variability in scores, which keeps late-night sessions from becoming a predictable exercise in memory and recall.

### Card Rhythm and Pacing
With 84 new images, players sometimes need to swap cards mid-game to refresh the pool in longer sessions. The expansion respects Dixit’s pace and doesn’t force you into a long card-drawing break; instead, it provides a natural rhythm that sustains energy across three, four, or five players. If you’re new to Dixit, Daydreams might feel like a lot of new cues to juggle; if you’re a veteran, you’ll appreciate the extra texture and nuance.

## Gameplay Tips: Getting the Most from Daydreams
As with any strong expansion, Daydreams rewards players who lean into its strengths while avoiding common pitfalls. Here are some practical strategies to get more value from your sessions:

- Embrace the dream logic. Don’t be afraid to frame a clue that nods to a dreamlike association rather than a literal match. The more you trust the audience’s imagination, the richer the round becomes.
- Use the prompts as a bridge. If you’re new to Daydreams, try using one Dream Prompt per game to build a habit of weaving narratives that are more suggestive than explicit.
- Balance your crowd. In a table with a wide range of storytelling styles, Daydreams shines when players are comfortable with both poetic clues and wry, literal cues. If your group tends toward the literal, lean into the prompts to push the tone toward surrealism.
- The storyteller’s risk, the group’s reward. Daydreams nudges you to take a risk with your clue. If your group enjoys the thrill of near-misses and clever misdirections, Daydreams will feel like a playground; if your table prizes stone-cold accuracy, you might need to nudge the prompts toward the more grounded spectrum.

## Setup, House Rules, and Practical Play Session Notes
A typical Daydreams night plays as follows: set up the usual Dixit layout, split into teams if you’re playing with mixed experience, and shuffle the 84 new cards into the existing deck or keep them separate for a themed round. The Dream Prompts can be used at the storyteller’s discretion, and you can choose to start with a “gentle dream” mode for first-timers or go full surreal from Round 1 if your group is itching to float away on a story.

As with any expansions, it’s worth keeping a handful of lightweight house rules handy:
- Timer rule: Use a 60-second timer if you’re new to Dream Prompts; it helps keep rounds brisk.
- Prompt rotation: If a player uses a Dream Prompt, rotate to the next player to give everyone a fair shot at creative clues.
- Card refresh for longer sessions: If you’re playing with five players or more, consider a quick refresh after every two rounds to keep the card pool fresh.

These micro-changes aren’t essential, but they smooth out the rhythm and help you get more mileage out of your Daydreams nights.
