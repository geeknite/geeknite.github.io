### Cleaning and maintenance
Maintenance prescriptions for these rugs are refreshingly practical:
- Vacuum regularly with a beater bar on a low setting or use a suction-only attachment to avoid agitation that can fray fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the pile.
- For stains, use a mild detergent solution or a rug-specific cleaner. Do a test patch first in a hidden corner.
- If you have pets, consider a pet-specific odor neutralizer and a quick weekly vacuum to prevent grime buildup.

Avoid harsh chemicals and chlorine-based cleaners, which can dull the pattern or alter the color slightly. The aim is deterrence of stains, not a full-blown chemical war on your floor.

## Maintenance Magic: Quick Tips to Keep the Wave Looking Fresh

- Vacuum weekly to prevent dirt from embedding; a simple routine keeps the fibers looking crisp and the colors true.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color exposure across the surface. This helps the wave pattern stay balanced rather than forming a single, flattened path.
- Place a high-quality rug pad underneath to reduce sliding and provide a bit of extra cushioning. A pad also helps with acoustics, which is always a nice side benefit when you have kids or a roommate who enjoys loud playlists.
