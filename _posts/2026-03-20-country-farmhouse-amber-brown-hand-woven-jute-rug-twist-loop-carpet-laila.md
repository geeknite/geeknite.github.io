## Performance and Comfort

### Feel underfoot

Jute isn’t going to give you the plush-pillow experience of a shag or a high-pile wool rug. The feel is more of a grounded, natural fiber with a low profile that’s ideal for kitchens, living rooms, or home offices where you’d like to stand up without pretending you’re walking on a cloud. It’s firm enough to provide support for standing tasks and soft enough to be forgiving when you shuffle barefoot after a long day. If you’re a barefoot enthusiast or have kids who love to sprint across the room, Laila offers a sturdy canvas that won’t pancake under you after a few weeks of use.

### Durability

Jute fibers are strong, but they do respond to moisture and heavy abrasion with a slower recovery than synthetic options. Expect natural shedding in the first few weeks—this is normal and a sign of honest hand weaving. Regular vacuuming will manage loose fibers, and rotating the rug every few months helps prevent uneven wear in high-traffic zones. If you’ve got pets with claws or a curious cat who enjoys the rug’s texture, you’ll want to trim any frayed loops before they become a habit.

### Underlay and Pads

A good rug pad is your best friend here. It not only keeps the rug in place on slick floors but also provides a little extra cushioning and a barrier against moisture wicking from hardwoods. Look for natural-fiber pads or microfleece pads that won’t harm the jute’s weave. If you’ve got radiant floor heating, confirm that the pad is compatible with heat; you don’t want your amber brown dream sweating under your toes in July.

## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning Guidelines

- Vacuum regularly with a vacuum that’s set to a low suction, and avoid aggressive beater bars that could snag the loops.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Do not rub; that forces the liquid deeper into the fibers and makes the spill look heroic only to your vacuum cleaner later.
- For stubborn spots, use a mild detergent diluted with water. Test on a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Do not soak the rug—jute doesn’t love water so much as a dramatic soap opera.
- Dry thoroughly. Avoid drying in direct sunlight for extended periods; color variation could occur, and we don’t want the amber to burn to a starship glow.
- Professional cleaning is a good idea for large areas or tough stains. Look for a cleaner experienced with natural fibers and handwoven textile care.

### Common Issues and Quick Fixes

- Shedding: Normal in the initial weeks. Regular vacuuming helps. A light shake outdoors can also reduce surface fibers.
- Fading risk: Prolonged sun exposure can alter color. Use curtains or blinds during peak sun hours, or rotate the rug periodically to even out any color shifts.
- Edge curl: Some rugs may have gently curled edges due to the weave. A touch of rug tape or a non-slip pad beneath can help keep the edges down and flat.
