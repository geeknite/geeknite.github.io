## Durability and installation

### How tough are these strips?

Durability depends on the surface, the adhesive, and how often the desk gets cleaned with solvent-based cleaners. In typical office environments, the strips hold up against daily wipe-downs, a few coffee rings, and the occasional enthusiastic handshake. They are not designed to survive a full-on table-dancing performance, but they shrug off normal wear and tear with grace. If you rotate the counter layout with any regularity, you may want to choose strips with a slightly stronger adhesive and a longer install window before they cure into permanent adhesive happiness.

### Installation steps in a nutshell

1) Clean the surface thoroughly and dry it. A clean slate equals better clingage. 2) Measure and mark where you want each strip to sit. 3) Peel back the protective liner a little at a time while pressing the strip down to avoid air bubbles. 4) Use a soft cloth or a credit card to smooth out any bubbles. 5) Wait a few hours for the adhesive to settle before loading the counter with the usual human artillery (laptop, notepad, barcode scanner, etc.).

## Practical tips and pro hacks

### Start with a mock layout

Before you commit, lay out the strips on the desk using painter’s tape to visualize the final arrangement. This helps you tweak spacing and alignment without lifting adhesive multiple times. It also lets you test how signage looks in relation to the new color boundary.

### Use the contrast to guide guests

Place the darker strip where guests are most likely to pivot their gaze toward a sign, while the lighter strip can frame the service edge. This creates a natural line of sight and reduces confusion during peak times.

### Combine with a branding block

If your brand uses a two-tone palette, you can incorporate a small logo panel on the adjacent wall or on a sign next to the counter. The strips then act as a frame that makes the branding pop rather than compete for attention.

### Maintenance tips

Wipe the strips with a soft microfiber cloth dampened with a mild cleaner. Avoid abrasive cleaners that might wear down the finish. Regular light cleaning keeps the colors crisp and the lines sharp. If you notice lifting at the edges after a few months, reapply pressure and check that the surface remains clean and dry before you reseat the edge.
