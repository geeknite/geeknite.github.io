## How the game plays

Skip-Bo is a race to empty your stock pile. In the center are Build piles that you fill in ascending order from 1 to 12. Cards come from your hand, your stock pile and your discard piles. Wild Skip-Bo cards can substitute for any number when building piles. The first player to deplete their stock pile wins the round.

Turn flow is simple:

1. Draw to five cards (or replenish to five if needed) 
2. Play cards from your hand or from your discard piles onto Build piles in order
3. If you cannot play, discard one card onto one of your own discard piles
4. End turn and draw back up to five

The game features moments of rapid play, surprising reversals, and a wholesome level of chaos that makes it ideal for mixed age groups. Kids will enjoy the sequencing and the simple math, while adults can savor the little strategic choices about when to burn a Skip-Bo wild card or when to hold onto it for a bigger move later in the round.

### Opening moves and mid game

A good opening is often about setting up a flow that will produce Build piles quickly and give you more options on future turns. This means paying attention to which numbers you already have in your stock and which numbers you are most likely to draw from the deck. Mid-game is where things get spicy; you are juggling your own Build piles with the pressure to assist your neighbors by creating opportunities for yourself without helping too much. It is a delicate dance of building momentum and denying others the path to victory.
