## Safety reminders and parental guidelines
A trampoline is a toy, but it is also a small risk volcano if not used correctly. Here are practical safety reminders tailored for 3- to 6-year-olds:

- Always supervise during play. Even the most docile bouncer can misread the law of gravity during a dramatic leap.
- Keep the number of jumpers to one at a time when practicing new moves. Sharing is great, gravity is not a fan of two-person synchronization routines.
- Shoes off, socks on. Some families prefer bare feet for grip; others like non-slip socks. Either way, remove jewelry and sharp accessories to prevent snags.
- Check the enclosure around the frame regularly. If you notice fraying or loose springs, pause jumping and perform maintenance.
- Use the trampoline on a flat surface; if you must place it on a lawn, ensure the ground is even and there is no hidden hole beneath the mat.
- Weather matters. Do not leave the trampoline outdoors during storms or heavy winds. A gust can turn your backyard into a trampoline rodeo, which is not recommended for beginners.

## Maintenance, cleaning, and durability tips
Taking care of a trampoline is mostly common sense with a few nerdy reliability habits:

- Wipe down the frame with a mild soap solution and a soft cloth every few weeks to prevent rust and grime buildup. Don’t flood it with abrasive cleaners; the goal is a long, elegant life, not a chemical pyre.
- Check the net and zipper monthly. Don’t wait for a sudden zip malfunction; catch it while it’s still small and easy to repair.
- Inspect springs for signs of wear. If you see stretched springs or an odd gap, consult the manual or the manufacturer’s support channel. Replacement parts are cheaper than hospital bills from accidental landings.
- Keep the jumping surface taut. A loose mat can create extra bounce height that might surprise a kid who isn’t ready for dramatic airtime.

Durability wise, expect the interior components to hold up well through a season of daily use, with outdoor exposure gradually whitening the frame and potentially fading the mat. A yearly checkup is a good idea; treat it like a small car maintenance routine, not a glamorous spa day for your trampoline.
