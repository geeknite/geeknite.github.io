### Vacuum test

Vacuuming is straightforward. The rug’s fiber is not fluffy like a plush carpet; it’s more like a robust tapestry that rewards consistent, gentle passes with a standard vacuum. The repeated passes help keep the surface clean without pulling fibers or showing excessive wear at the high-traffic zones. If you own a vacuum with adjustable suction, you’ll want to set it to medium, then fine-tune to your fiber’s tolerance. The result is a floor cover that looks clean between cleanings and does not require constant deep maintenance.

### Spill test

Spills occur. The Natural Terra rug’s fiber holds up against tea, coffee, and water. The dye’s earth tones help, as stains are less likely to pop in the beige spectrum. The key to keeping it pristine is immediate action: blot, blot again, then blot some more. A mild detergent with water can help if the spill is stubborn, but do not saturate; you want to avoid any chance of dye migration. This is especially important in natural fiber rugs, where moisture can travel along the weave and cause warping if left to soak.

### Wear and edge life

Foot traffic is the true test. A rug that looks good on day one might not hold up to daily life. The Natural Terra shows a little more wear along the edges where feet leave the room and along the center where the coffee table sits. This is to be expected, given the natural fiber’s durability rather than the rug’s price. The pattern and color help hide minor scuffs, and the flat-weave texture helps with a crisp look even after daily use.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

Natural fiber rugs are robust but require a little more care than synthetic pile. Here are practical guidelines:
- Vacuum regularly with suction only, on a low-to-medium setting to avoid fiber damage.
- Spills: blot, blot, blot. Use a mild detergent if needed, but don’t soak.
- Rotation: flip or rotate every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic areas.
- Padding: use a high-quality rug pad to keep the rug in place, reduce sliding, and extend life.
- Cleaning: plan for a professional cleaning every 12-18 months, depending on traffic, to refresh color and texture.
