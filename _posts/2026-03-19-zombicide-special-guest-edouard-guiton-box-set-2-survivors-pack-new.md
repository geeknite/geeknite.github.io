## Meet the two survivors: how they feel on the table

The two exclusive survivors bring distinct personalities—one leaning into defense and teamwork, the other into mobility and risk-taking. If you’re a fan of “glass cannon” archetypes where one character dodges a bad dice roll by being in the right place, you’ll probably enjoy how these two work together. Their abilities blend with core mechanics without trampling over existing balance, which is a tricky tightrope to walk when you’re adding new heroes to a well-loved system.

### Survivor A: the strategist-commando
This survivor excels at planning, using a set of actions that encourage careful movement and protection of teammates. They reward you for clever positioning and taking a beat to prep. The practical upshot is that, with good table presence and a bit of foresight, you can clear rooms more efficiently than sprinting headlong into the zed swarm. This is the kind of character that rewards mid-game adjustments and zen-like dice management—great for players who like to think through the map rather than simply start swinging.

### Survivor B: the sprint-tactician
In contrast, Survivor B thrives on mobility and quick engagement. They’re the character who can shepherd you around the board, create flanking opportunities, and get you into and out of danger with a minimum of fuss. Their cards emphasize turn-order management and the integration of terrain as a resource. If you like the idea of orchestrating “cover-fire” moments where one or two survivors draw attention while another slides into a better position, this is the one to try.

In actual play, these two survivors feel like a balanced duo: one is a safety net that helps the party avoid risky hasty decisions, and the other is a spear that goes on the offensive when the moment is right. They don’t reinvent the wheel, but they do bring enough personality and tactical depth to justify adding them to a mid-to-late campaign without dragging the table into a rabbit hole of complicated rules.
