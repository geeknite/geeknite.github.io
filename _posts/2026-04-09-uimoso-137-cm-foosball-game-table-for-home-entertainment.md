## Assembly Tips and Common Pitfalls

- Take your time with levelers. A slightly uneven table will ruin the game’s fairness quickly. Use a carpenter’s level to verify the tabletop and adjust the legs accordingly.
- Tighten hardware gradually. It’s tempting to crank everything down in a rush, but a little patience yields sturdier construct and a better playing feel.
- Calibrate the rods after assembly. A quick pass with a light oil or lubricant on the joints can reduce stickiness and improve overall feel, but avoid over-lubing; you don’t want the table to become an oil slick during a dramatic comeback shot.
- Practice on a clean surface. Debris on the field can affect ball bounce and lead to erratic plays. A quick vacuum or wipe-down before a session is worth the effort.
