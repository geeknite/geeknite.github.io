## Maintenance and longevity: care tips to maximize your investment
The reality is: chips are pretty forgiving. A gentle wipe-down with a dry microfiber cloth will take care of dust and the vague fingerprints that accumulate during intense hands. If you spill something sticky, a light wipe with a damp cloth and mild soap will do, followed by a quick dry. Do not soak the chips in water to avoid color bleed or softening the edge. And as much as you want to, please don’t attempt to repair edge wear with magic markers—the result is never as crisp as the factory print and looks odd under pressure.

Storage is straightforward: keep the aluminum case closed when not in use and avoid exposing chips to extreme temperatures for prolonged periods. Extreme heat can cause some materials to warp or discolor over time, and extreme cold may make the chips brittle. In a nutshell: treat your set like a pet dragon—keep it safe, fed with a clean environment, and don’t leave it near open flames or your microwave.
