### Care and maintenance: keep it looking sharp

Maintenance is the practical art of keeping a large rug in good shape. Here are the key routines that make the Wilma rug behave nicely season after season:

- Vacuum regularly with a vacuum designed for hard floors or low-pile rugs. Avoid aggressive beater bars that can pull at fibers; a gentle pass is often enough to remove loose crumbs and pet hair.
- Clean up spills quickly by blotting (not rubbing) with a clean cloth. Rinse with a bit of water if needed and blot again. For sticky substances, a mild detergent diluted in water is usually safe for synthetic fibers; always test a small area first to confirm colorfastness.
- For tougher stains, a standard carpet cleaner labeled safe for synthetic fibers is your friend. Pre-test on a hidden corner to ensure no color bleed occurs.
- For daily maintenance, a quick vacuum and occasional flattening with your hand on the edges helps the rug keep its crisp look.
- If you have pets that shed, consider a lint roller or a pet hair remover between big vacuum sessions.

Edge care matters because, in the long run, fraying edges ruin the clean line that makes a large rug so visually appealing. If your entry path or furniture legs tend to drag across the border, the binding is your best friend.

### Cleaning mythbusting: what not to do

- Don’t rub spills aggressively. It can push the stain deeper and create an unsightly ring around the area.
- Don’t use harsh chemicals unless the manufacturer explicitly approves them for synthetic fibers. Mild detergents are usually enough.
- Don’t vacuum with the rug on very high pile settings. You want a gentle pass that lifts debris rather than dragging it through the fibers.
- Don’t neglect a rug pad if your floor is slippery. A good pad makes moving around feel like a glide rather than a stumble.
