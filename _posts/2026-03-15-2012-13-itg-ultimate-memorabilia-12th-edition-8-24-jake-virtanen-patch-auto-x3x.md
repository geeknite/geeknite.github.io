### Authenticity, Grading, and Verification

Authenticity in this genre comes down to three pillars: on-card autograph authenticity, patch provenance, and the card’s overall condition. A lot of ITG-era material relied on standard witness signatures and patch authenticity that the manufacturer stands behind. If you’re serious about long-term investment, consider sending the card to a professional grading service. Grading can help you later position the card in auctions or sales, especially if you’re aiming for a high grade on a triple patch auto where surface wear can be a subtle villain. Remember: graders pay close attention to surface scratches, corner wear, and any lifting of the patches at the edges. A clean surface and intact patches can push your grade from a 7.5 to an 8 or 8.5, which can significantly impact resale value.

Pro-tip: if you’re at a card show and the price feels “friendly,” consider a quick, discreet inspection—turn the card under bright light, tilt to observe the patch translucence, and inspect the autographs for consistency. The devil is in the details, and the details here are the patches and the autographs themselves.
