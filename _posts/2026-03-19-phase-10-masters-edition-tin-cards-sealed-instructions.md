## How to Play with Masters Edition (Sealed)
Playing Phase 10 Masters Edition reframes your standard playthrough with a focus on the master level rules and the collectible presentation. Below is a practical guide to get you from package to competitive chaos.

### Setup and Components
- Shuffle the Masters Edition deck and place it face down. The tin acts as a perfect reset container.
- Each player draws 10 cards. The rest of the deck forms the draw pile, and the top card becomes the discard pile starter.
- Decide which player will be the first to go by a fun house rule or a spin of the game tokens, if you have them. The first player then attempts to complete the first phase.

### Phase Gameplay
Phase 10 is a phase progression game. Each phase has a unique set of requirements. A typical setup includes a mix of runs (sequential cards), sets (cards of the same value), and occasionally special cards that shake up the symmetry.
- Phase 1 usually involves a simple set of cards like a pair or a trio. Phase 2 might require two runs and a pair, and so on. The Masters Edition keeps the same logic but sometimes overlays extra trackable tasks or special cards.
- Completing a phase allows the player to advance to the next phase and pass the turn to the next player. The first player to complete all 10 phases wins the game.
- If you cannot complete a phase on your turn, you must draw and discard as in standard Phase 10. Strategic decision making matters because a single discarded card can open up other players’ opportunities.

### Special Rules for Masters Edition
The Masters Edition introduces a glossy surface to the familiar rules, often including:
- Additional wild cards or jokers that function as wilds for completing phases
- Special action cards that can force another player to redraw or skip a turn
- A slightly more generous burn of the deck for longer game nights or when you accidentally go all in on a tricky phase
Be mindful of any variant rules listed in your specific tin. Collectors and family groups might prefer strict adherence, while casual players may enjoy the extra spice that Masters Edition provides.

### Timings and Variants
A standard Phase 10 session can range from 30 minutes to an hour depending on player count and how aggressively you chase those coveted phases. Masters Edition variants can adjust the pace by tweaking how quickly phases are cleared or how many wild cards appear in the deck. If your group likes speed runs, consider a timer or a house rule that accelerates the early phases. If your group leans into strategy, allow for longer planning windows and more deliberate discards.
