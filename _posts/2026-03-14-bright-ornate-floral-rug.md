## Maintenance and Cleaning

Vacuum the rug regularly using a gentle setting to avoid rough agitation that could wear the fibers. Rotate the rug a quarter turn every few months to promote even wear and prevent permanent impressions from heavy furniture.

Direct sunlight can fade colors over the years, so if your room has strong sun exposure, consider rotating furniture or using UV-blocking window treatments to preserve the vibrancy.

For spills and stains, act quickly: blot the area with a clean cloth rather than rubbing, which can push the stain deeper. For tough spots, consult a carpet cleaning professional or try a mild rug shampoo following the manufacturer's instructions. Always test any cleaning method on a small, inconspicuous area first to prevent unexpected color changes.

A note on pets: the pattern can hide minor shedding, but regular vacuuming remains essential. If a dog decides the rug is a chew toy or a cat uses it as a scratching post, consider a patch repair technique and a quick edge reinforcement if needed.

### Quick Care Tips
- Use a soft-bristle vacuum to minimize fiber wear
- Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade color and backing
- When possible, rotate the rug to avoid high-traffic hotspots
- Schedule periodic professional cleanings every 12-24 months depending on use
