### Design and materials
The rug is described as cotton blend rope. That implies a yarn that mimics the rustic charm of cotton with a touch more durability than pure cotton, which can feel a tad fragile when you roll it out for the first time. Rope textures are often forgiving under foot because the loops compress and spring, giving a tactile surface that is more interesting than a flat weave. The blend should resist wrinkling and flatten more quickly than a boucle or shag, though rope construction can shed a bit when new. The cotton blend makes cleaning easier than a wool rug, and you can spot clean with mild detergent without summoning a cleaning crew.

Carefully constructed, the fabric is meant to endure light to moderate traffic while maintaining its striped identity. The fringe adds a playful boho note without pushing into the territory of maximalism. In this setup, the rug marries function with aesthetic charm, giving you a piece that looks good in photos and feels comfortable underfoot.

### Care and maintenance
- Regular vacuuming with low suction to prevent fiber pull
- Rotate every few weeks to avoid wearing in sun-exposed areas
- Spot clean with mild detergent for stains; blot not rub to maintain texture
- Dry flat; avoid a dryer
- Fringes: straighten after cleaning and avoid pulling

### Practical durability and longevity notes
Cotton blends are easy to wash, though they do not tolerate heavy traffic as well as synthetic blends. If you have a high traffic area, consider a larger rug to distribute footfall more evenly, plus a rug pad for slip resistance and improved longevity. In rooms with pets or kids, examine the rug regularly for pulled threads and frayed edges, and address them early to prevent more extensive damage.

### Care for color and fade resistance
Direct sunlight will fade blues and beiges over time. If your rug sits in a sun drenched corner, rotate it to reduce uneven wear. A UV protective window treatment can also prolong color life. If you notice color running during a spill, rinse promptly and dry thoroughly to minimize color migration.
