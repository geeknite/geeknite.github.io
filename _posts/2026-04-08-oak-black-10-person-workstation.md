## Setup and Assembly

### Tools and Time Investment

Let us be honest: a ten person bench is not a one person weekend project. The initial assembly will take a solid afternoon plus a helper. The package typically includes major components, a hardware kit, and clear instructions. Expect a few trips to the hardware store for extra screws or spacers if your floor plan requires nonstandard adjustments. If you have a small team, you can split assembly into two phases: base frame and top surface, followed by integration of cable trays and privacy panels. It is a satisfying puzzle with a tangible payoff once every workstation is aligned and the cable jungle has been tamed.

### The Realities of a Large-Scale Install

Installing a ten seat bench means coordinating a tiny build project. Floor leveling, wall clearance, and the path for transporting large panels all matter. The modular design helps adapt to uneven spaces, but you will want to test your workflow before you commit. A little planning goes a long way, especially if your office has a policy about moving furniture after a remodel. The worst case is discovering mid install that one corner is off and the entire line has to be re-leveled. The best case is finishing early, doing a victory lap around the workspace, and avoiding chat drama about velocity delays.

### Practical Tips for a Smooth Setup

- Assign a lead installer who can read instruction diagrams without turning them into garage wall art
- Group small parts into labeled bins for quick access during assembly
- Use a level and a tape measure to confirm straight lines after you place the first few stations
- Plan for cable management from day one rather than pretending you can jerry-rig the power later
- Allow time for the first week of usage and adjust as needed after real workflows emerge
