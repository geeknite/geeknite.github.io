## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping It Fresh Without Rituals

Caring for a natural wool and jute rug requires a practical, low-maintenance routine rather than a kitchen-sink approach. Here are the essentials: 
- Vacuum regularly, with a gentle beater-bar setting or a handheld vacuum that won’t pull threads. Avoid aggressive agitation that can loosen fibers over time.
- Rotate periodically to promote even wear and prevent obvious traffic patterns.
- Spot clean spills promptly using blotting, not rubbing. Use a mild detergent solution or a cleanser designed for natural fiber rugs. Always test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Address stains with patience. Natural fibers can be sensitive to certain chemicals, so using a pH-balanced cleaner is a safe bet. For tough stains, consider enlisting a professional rug cleaner familiar with wool and jute blends.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12–24 months for heavy-use rooms. The cost-to-benefit ratio often pays off in long-term appearance and fiber health.

Maintenance also means being mindful of moisture. Wool loves to stay dry, and jute loves to stay honest about humidity. If you’re in a humid climate, ensure the rug dries fully after cleaning or accidental moisture exposure. In damp spaces, consider a dehumidifier or improved air flow to prevent mold or odor buildup.

## Underlay, Padding, and Floor Health

A rug pad isn’t optional; it’s a life choice for longevity and comfort. A well-chosen pad reduces slippage, protects your floor from abrasion, and cushions the felt of the wool-weave’s fibers. For a natural fiber rug like this, look for a pad with a non-slip grip and a thickness that suits your room’s traffic. A pad made from polyurethane or felt can work, but for sustainability-minded shoppers, look for pads with recycled materials or those designed specifically for natural fiber rugs.

Additionally, consider the floor type. On hard surfaces like hardwood or tile, a pad adds comfort and protects the floor. Over carpet, a pad can help reduce bunching and maintain a clean edge along the rug’s border.
