The first thing you will notice is the absence of superfluous chrome and the presence of purposeful sturdy lines. The Artisan Gray finish is not a flashy chrome or a glossy black; it sits somewhere in the middle, like the dependable friend who is not trying to outshine anyone but is always ready with a wrench when you need help. The materials feel robust yet not industrial-club heavy. Expect a combination of particleboard or MDF with a protective laminate that resists scratches and the occasional pencil-dust disaster. The hardware is stainless-ish where it needs to be and plastic-tinged in areas that should flex a little under assembly pressure. In short, it is the kind of build you can trust to survive a couple of office relocations, or at least a relocation from the desk to the trash can when the quarterly performance review lands on your desk like a friendly but unwanted reminder.

- Measure your space carefully and map out door swing clearance before committing. A 6 D x 7 W footprint is spacious, but the arrow of your room layout can hit the wall if you aren’t careful.
- Consider your power needs. If you rely heavily on USB-C hubs and high-wattage peripherals, plan for extra outlets or a small power strip with surge protection.
- Reflect on noise levels. If you are in a busy office or a shared environment, you may want to pair this cubicle with a light sound-dampening setup to preserve focus during crunch times.
- Check the installation path for panels and height settings. Some taller possibilities require more space to adjust comfortably without bumping into ceiling lights or shelving units.
