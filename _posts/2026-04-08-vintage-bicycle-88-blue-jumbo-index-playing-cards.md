## The care and playing life: maintenance tips for longevity
### Handling tips for the collector in you
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve the blue back color and prevent color fade. Sunlight can be surprisingly merciless to vintage inks, and you do not want your jumbo index to become a faint memory.
- Use a protective case or a display lid when not in use. Yes, the cards deserve to be treated with reverence, especially when they are the kind that evokes a sense of old-world charm in a modern world.
- Avoid over-wetting the surfaces during cleaning; a dry dusting is usually enough. If you must wipe, use a soft microfiber and a whisper of a cleaning aid designed for card stock. Do not soak the deck in promise or despair.

## Authenticity and provenance: keeping it real in the age of reprints
With vintage decks, there is always a curiosity about authenticity. The Ohio USPCC lineage adds a layer of credibility, but you still want to watch for reissues and counterfeit packaging. Look for consistency in the color saturation, the exact alignment of the back pattern, and the presence of a genuine USPCC seal if that is part of the original packaging. The market for vintage decks can be a rollercoaster ride of price fluctuations, so caution is wise. A well-photographed listing with close-ups of the tuck seal, the face typography, and the back pattern is worth a thousand guesses. If you are new to the hobby, consider starting with a certified dealer who can provide authentication notes and a warranty, then graduate toward direct-from-collector sales as you gain confidence.
