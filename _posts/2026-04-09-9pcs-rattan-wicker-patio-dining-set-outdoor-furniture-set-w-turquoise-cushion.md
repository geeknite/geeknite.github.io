## Care and maintenance: keeping the set looking fresh

To maximize longevity, commit to a light maintenance routine. Dust the wicker weekly and wipe away any spills immediately to prevent staining. For deeper cleaning, use a gentle soap solution and a soft brush, then rinse with clean water and allow to dry completely before covering or storing cushions. Cushions should be laundered according to the fabric care instructions. In many cases, cushion covers are removable and machine-washable; if not, spot-cleaning with a mild detergent works well. If you live in a climate with heavy rain, consider bringing the cushions indoors during storms or using waterproof covers to protect the fabric. Routine checks should include inspecting the frame for loose screws and ensuring the legs are level. A wobble is a warning signal; fix it early to prevent more serious wear and tear.

For those who want to dive into long-term care, a light sealant or protective finish on the wicker can help resist moisture penetration and maintain the weave’s appearance. Always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines for any protective products to avoid harming the wicker fibers or cushions.
