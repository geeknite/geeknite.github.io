## Maintenance and cleaning

One of the biggest selling points for a synthetic rug is maintenance. Spills are less frightening when you know you can clean them without panic. For everyday spills, blot with a clean cloth and a mild cleaning solution. Avoid soaking the rug; you’re aiming for damp, not swamp. Most stains lift cleanly with a gentle approach.

Compact vacuuming works well for daily upkeep. If you’re dealing with pet fur, it may take a couple of passes to remove all the hair, but you’ll find the surface tends to resist dirt accumulation better than many natural fiber rugs. A weekly vacuum is usually enough to keep it looking vibrant. For tougher stains, a carpet cleaner designed for synthetic fibers can be your best friend, but be mindful of the product’s instructions and colorfastness tests first.

If you’re buying custom sizes, you’ll want to confirm what backing or padding comes with the rug. A pad dramatically reduces slippage and extends the rug’s life by distributing foot traffic more evenly. Pads are particularly helpful in high-traffic living spaces or entries where doorways create extra friction. Rubber or latex backing can provide good grip, though you’ll want to ensure it won’t react with certain floor finishes.

## How to choose the right rug size (for 8x10 and beyond)

Choosing the right rug size is a design responsibility that often gets skipped until after you’ve bought the sofa. A common pitfall is buying a rug that’s too small for the space, which creates that “floating furniture” feeling and leaves naked floor as a design statement. Here are a few guidelines to help you pick the right size when you’re eyeing the custom-size option or the 8x10 standard:

- Measure your furniture footprint: The rule of thumb is to have at least 18 inches of rug exposed beyond the edges of the furniture, but in high-traffic rooms you may want more. For a living room with a sofa and loveseat, an 8x10 often works well if the furniture sits on the rug. If the rug ends under the front legs only, consider a larger rug or a different arrangement.
- Consider traffic patterns: If your space has clear walking paths, you may want the rug to extend into those zones to avoid stepping onto bare floor. A custom size can help you tailor the rug to the flow of movement.
- Balance with the room: The rug should help unify disparate furniture pieces and define zones. In a small space, a well-placed 6x9 or 8x10 can anchor a seating area without overwhelming the room.
