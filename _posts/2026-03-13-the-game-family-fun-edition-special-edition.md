## How to play: the rules in friendly language
If you know how to pass the remote around a room, you know how to play this game. The objective is simple: accumulate points by correctly matching prompts with turns while your fellow players attempt to derail you with witty input and questionable improvisation. The Special Edition keeps the core loop intact, but it sprinkles in extra prompts and a few social challenges that push players to describe actions rather than just fill in the blanks.

### Turn structure
- The active player draws a prompt card and reads it aloud. The prompt usually invites a short story, a quick description, or a silly answer that fits the color-coded category on the board.
- Each player crafts a response, aiming for humor, accuracy, and a dash of eccentricity. The active player occasionally acts as judge for the best response and awards points accordingly.
- The round ends when everyone has contributed, after which the points are tallied and the board shifts to highlight the current leader. If chaos grows, you know the Special Edition is doing its job.

### Strategy for different ages
- For younger players, focus on imaginative storytelling and cheerleading. Let them steer the moment with goofy ideas. The color tokens provide a gentle structure that keeps things accessible.
- For older players, lean into witty riffs, inside jokes, and timing. The Special Edition is a playground for quick wits, but be mindful of lines and keep the jokes inclusive so the table remains friendly rather than feral.
