## Cleaning, care, and the long-game maintenance
Cleanliness is the unsung hero of any rug review. A good rug should be easy to care for, not a cryptic puzzle that requires a weekend expedition to decode. Kendall scores well on routine maintenance. A standard vacuum does its job without the fear that you’re going to pull up a thread that resembles a plot twist. We recommended using a parquet or upholstery attachment for best results; a beater brush can be too aggressive for the longer fibers, potentially causing a modest shedding event, which, let’s be honest, is everyone’s least favorite unplanned party.

For spills, act fast. Blot gently with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing (because you’ll push the stain deeper and also because this is how you unlock a rogue side quest). If you’re dealing with a stubborn spill, a mild detergent solution can help—test on an inconspicuous edge first. Let it dry completely before stepping back onto the fiber to preserve its plush aura. The rug dries reasonably quickly in normal room conditions, but if your home is a sauna in August, you’ll want to give it a dry day or two, because damp shag is not the vibe we’re aiming for.

A note on shedding: new shag rugs shed a little at first. Kendall’s shedding is minimal and tends to subside after a handful of vacuum sessions. If you’re particularly sensitive to lint, you may want to keep a lint roller handy for stubborn stray fibers that decide to take up residence on your couch cushions. It’s a small price for the long-term luxury of a surface that feels like you’ve embraced the concept of “soft mode” for your feet.
