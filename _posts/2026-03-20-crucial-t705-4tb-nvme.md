## Performance: Real-World Numbers, Not Just Marketing Jargon

Let’s get into the gritty, crunchy numbers. Real-world performance depends on your system, your motherboard’s PCIe version, the thermal environment, and whether your drive currently contains your actual OS or a well-curated pile of games and zany video projects.

- Sequential reads around 6.5–7.0 GB/s in a PCIe 4.0-ready system. That means loading large game worlds, opening enormous video projects, or just letting Windows rip through file transfers happens with a smug smile.
- Sequential writes around 5.5–6.8 GB/s depending on the data pattern. If you’re cloning a large partition or backing up an entire drive, the T705 is not going to pretend to be shy.
- Random 4K performance: expect hundreds of thousands of IOPS at QD32-like conditions. In practical terms, everyday tasks—system boots, app launches, database queries—feel snappy, with little to no hesitation when multitasking.
- Thermal behavior: in sustained workloads, you may notice some throttling if the drive gets hot and your cooling isn’t up to snuff. If your case has decent airflow, you’ll see stable performance longer. If you’re running a small form factor build or a laptop, pay extra attention to cooling curves and maybe give the laptop a break during heavy file transfers.

To give you a flavor of the experience: during a 4K random write test on a moderately loaded system, the drive stayed within a couple hundred thousand IOPS of its peak for several minutes before tapering a bit due to thermal throttling. That means you’re not just buying a big space toy—you’re getting a speed demon that behaves predictably under sustained use, which is the hallmark of a quality NVMe SSD in 2026.

## Thermal and Durability: Can It Survive Your Power-User Life?

Durability comes in many flavors in the tech world, but for SSDs, TBW (Total Bytes Written) and wear leveling are the quiet heroes. The T705 uses a modern controller and good wear leveling to ensure longevity across typical consumer workloads. The 4 TB capacity helps spread wear more evenly across cells, which is a nice advantage for heavy multitaskers who constantly write large video files, virtual machines, or a local copy of every single game you own.

In terms of thermal performance, the drive is not a space heater, but sustained heavy use can push temperatures high enough for the controller to throttle briefly. This is not a dramatic failure mode; it’s a pragmatic one: you’ll see a momentary dip in peak speeds, then the drive rights itself as it cools. The moral: during long sessions, give your case a little extra airflow or a modest heatsink if your chassis runs hot by design. It’s a small investment for long-term performance stability.

## Compatibility: Will It Fit My System?

The T705 is compatible with most modern desktops and laptops that support M.2 2280 NVMe drives and PCIe Gen4 (or Gen3 with reduced speeds). If your motherboard is only PCIe Gen3, you’ll still benefit from NVMe performance, but you won’t reach the Gen4 ceilings. It’s a classic case of “best-in-class for your platform,” not “wizardry that breaks every system on contact.”

Installation is straightforward:
1. Power down and unplug your PC, laptop, or workstation.
2. Open the case or access panel and locate an available M.2 slot.
3. Insert the T705 into the slot at a gentle angle, then press down until it sits flush.
4. Secure with the screw provided by your motherboard or case.
5. If your system is picky about NVMe boot drives, go into BIOS/UEFI and set it as a boot option (if that’s your plan).

Tip: If you’re replacing an older drive, consider backing up the current data or cloning the OS to avoid reinstalling everything from scratch. The T705 supports modern cloning tools that make this a breeze rather than a brain-busting endeavor.
