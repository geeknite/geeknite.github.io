## Weather, durability, and care tips
Geeks love numbers, so here are the stats you actually want. The aluminum frame resists rust and is light enough to be repositioned without requiring a forklift, which is nice for those times you want to create a new social area in your yard. The wicker weave is designed to withstand sun exposure and a little mist from sprinklers, but long periods of heavy rain and humidity will eventually demand a cover or storage solution. The cushions use outdoor-rated fabric that resists fading, minor water absorption, and the occasional accidental sunscreen spill.

Care tips to extend life beyond your neighbor's expectations:
- Use a breathable seat cushion cover that is easy to remove and wash
- Store cushions in a dry place during prolonged rain or winter months
- Regularly check and tighten hardware to prevent wobble and squeaks
- Consider using a furniture cover in extreme weather to extend the life of the weave
- Clean the weave with a soft brush to remove dirt and debris rather than harsh scrubbing

We also looked into weather resilience across different climates. In a dry climate with strong sun, the cushions will resist color fade better with a UV-rated fabric. In humid regions, the weave remains looking good provided you avoid standing water and keep cushions dry when not in use. In coastal environments, salt air can accelerate wear, so cleaning and protective storage become more important for long-term beauty. ## Cleaning, storage, and longevity tips
- Regularly wipe the frame with a damp cloth to remove dust and pollen

- Use fabric protectant spray that is safe for outdoor fabrics
- When possible, store cushions indoors during extreme weather or use breathable covers designed for outdoor furniture

If you want a simple routine, set aside 10 minutes every month to wipe down the frames, check the hardware, and fluff the cushions. You will be amazed at how this small habit preserves the look and feel of the set for years to come.

## Maintenance mindset and longevity myths
There is a common belief that outdoor furniture is only for people who enjoy constant maintenance. Not true. With a honest approach to cleaning and storage, you can enjoy a long-term relationship with your furniture without calling in a maintenance crew every season. Here are some myths dispelled:
- Myth: Outdoor furniture should be replaced every year. Reality: With basic care, many sets last multiple seasons, and if the fabric is still in good condition, replacing cushions is cheaper than buying a whole new set.
- Myth: All wicker weaves are fragile. Reality: Modern resin wicker is designed for outdoor life and can resist fading, cracking, and weathering when properly maintained.
- Myth: Cleaning is complicated. Reality: A simple soap solution and a soft brush will do the trick most times; you do not need a heavy-duty cleaner or a magic wand.
