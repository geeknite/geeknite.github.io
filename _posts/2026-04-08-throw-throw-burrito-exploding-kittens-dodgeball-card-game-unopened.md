## How to Play: The Quick Rundown

Throw Throw Burrito is a chaotic mix of set collection, matching pairs, and dodge action. Each player draws cards and tries to collect sets of matching items or burrito cards, all while dodging the burrito by playing dodge cards. If a burrito card is involved in a set or when a certain card triggers a toss, players physically pass a burrito arcing toward someone else, butterfly styles, in slow motion with dramatic sound effects. The goal is to be the first to collect three burrito cards or be the last one standing when the round ends, depending on the house rules you adopt. The exact scoring can vary by edition and house rules, but the core idea remains: you want to collect burrito cards while avoiding getting bopped by a plush squeeze of deliciously safe chaos.

### Step-by-step flow

- Start with each player drawing cards to begin a hand
- You take turns playing cards to form sets or trigger dodge actions
- When a burrito card is involved in a match or when a lure card is drawn, a plush burrito may be thrown
- Opponents must dodge or risk losing points
- Sets that include burrito cards grant you points or move you closer to a win
- The game ends after a fixed number of rounds or when a target score is reached, with the lead player crowned by the board game gods

The mechanical heart of the game is not the strategy but the timing. You time your actions, watch others try to dodge, and revel in the chaos as the burritos glide through the air like soft projectiles of destiny. The experience rewards social awareness and quick reflexes more than resource management or long-term planning.
