## How to play: quick start
If you want a quick explainer you can share with your friends, here’s the gist (as condensed as a cat’s attention span): shuffle the deck, deal a small number of cards to each player, and place the rest in the center as the draw pile. On your turn you may play an action card or use a Defuse to avert an exploding kitten. When an Exploding Kitten is drawn, you either rely on a Defuse or you’re out of the game. The objective remains delightfully simple in its complexity: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten while manipulating the deck with a blend of cunning and luck, and pray that your friends’ nerves don’t crack before your nerves crack.

### A pocket cheat sheet (for internal use only)
- Setup: Shuffle and deal a small hand to each player; place the rest in the middle as a draw pile.
- Turn order: On your turn you may play one or more action cards, then draw to fill your hand.
- Bombs: Exploding Kitten cards must be avoided. If you draw one you either have a Defuse or you are out.
- Defuse cards: Use them wisely—or hoard them like a dragon guarding its hoard and setting up future chaos.
- Action cards: Look at cards, steal cards, skip turns, reshuffle the discard pile, force another player to pick up extra cards. The exact mix is part of the party pack flavor, and also a test of your friendships.

The Exploding Kittens Party Pack shines not through deep strategy but through social dynamics. It’s a game where the energy of the group matters more than crystal-clear optimal play. If your crew loves banter, cheap theatrics, and a little risk, you’ll see a vibrant dynamic: someone dramatically narrating a card as if auditioning for a stage adaptation of a bad pun, another person insisting that a rule change violates the space-time continuum, and a few players peeking at cards with a smug little grin that only appears when you trust your friends to be honest about their mischief. The pace is brisk, rounds are short, and the laughter climbs quickly. It’s the kind of game you reach for after a longer, heavier title, or as a palate cleanser between deeper experiences.

## Strategy and tips
- Don’t hoard all the Defuses; you’ll need a few late-game counters when the drama hits peak hour.
- Use your action cards strategically; timing is not only about what you reveal but when you reveal it. A well-timed Peek or Skip can be worth more than a handful of guaranteed cards.
- Pay attention to table talk; banter is not just for fun—it’s a data stream. If a rival keeps insisting a card is “definitely not coming up,” they might be trying to lull you into a false sense of security.
- Two-player mode works, but it’s not the main highlight; expect more chaos with more players and a higher chance someone will break a leg in laughter while trying to keep a straight face.
- Do not forget to keep the game moving; a good party game thrives on momentum, even when the group is howling with laughter and chaos in equal measure.

The Party Pack embraces the familiar art style of Exploding Kittens while dialing up the playful color palette and cheekier humor. The cards feel solid, with cards that slide smoothly from hand to deck and back, and the Defuse cards are clearly distinguished so you never have to squint at a crucial moment. The game remains accessible to beginners while still offering enough chaos for more seasoned party gamers to enjoy a few rounds without getting bored.

### Art and humor
The kittens remain the star of the show. ## How many players, and is it worth it for two?
The Party Pack is built for 2 to 10 players. In practice, you’ll notice a bigger laugh payload with a larger crew. With more players, the social dynamics tilt toward bluffing, misdirection, and dramatic reveals. Two-player games still work, but they lack the same friction of social chaos that defines the party experience. If you’re grabbing this for a weekly two-player game night, you might want to pair it with a more strategic head-to-head or co-op title to balance the evening. That said, even in a two-player scenario, the “party” flavor isn’t stripped away; you can still enjoy banter, card-reading theater, and those satisfying misplays that make a night memorable.
