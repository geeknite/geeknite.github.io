## Materials, construction, and quality
### Hand-tufted craftsmanship
This rug uses hand-tufted techniques, a process that sits between machine-made precision and artisanal charm. Tufting involves pushing yarn through a backing fabric with a needle, resulting in a lush pile and a resilient surface. Hand-tufted rugs typically offer higher character and warmth than machine-loomed counterparts, thanks to the human touch that shows up in slight irregularities—deliberate features that remind you that someone poured time into making this piece rather than it being churned out by a robot in an overly air-conditioned studio.

### Materials and pile height

The rug density—how many knots per square centimeter you’re dealing with—plays a huge role in how long the colors stay vibrant and how well the fibers resist flattening under heavy furniture. A well-built hand-tufted rug in this size generally promises years of service if you treat it with the right care. Expect good resilience against flattening around sofa legs and chair edges, which is the real test of any living-room rug that’s also a workout partner for your feet.

## Maintenance, care, and longevity
### Cleaning basics
- Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting prevents grit from embedding in the fibers. Use a vacuum with a brush roller that’s not too aggressive to avoid fiber pull.
- Spills? Blot—don’t rub. A light blotting with a clean, dry cloth helps absorb moisture. For tougher stains, a mild detergent solution tested on a small area is your friend.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and to keep color fading from longevity issues to a minimum. This is especially important if you have direct sunlight streaming through a window of doom-and-gloom brightness.

### Stain resistance and longevity
While no handmade rug is a magical stain-proof cape, if you treat spills quickly and keep pets’ nails trimmed, you’ll preserve the color richness and pile height for years. Rotating furniture and employing a stain protector before major messes occur can help, though nothing beats a good, quick response when life decides to spill the tea (or coffee) on your new teal runway.

### Shedding and seasonal care
Expect a little shedding in the early months—common for wool-based hand-tufted pieces. Regular vacuuming reduces it, and over time, as the fibers settle, shedding typically tapers off. A seasonal deep clean or professional cleaning every couple of years can refresh the look and prolong the rug’s life.
