## Box contents and build quality
### What sits inside a Set Booster Box
A standard Set Booster Box in this set usually ships with 24 booster packs. Each booster pack is designed to maximize the thrill: a premium slot, potential borderless or extended art variants, and a foil treatment that catches the eye on a crowded battlefield. You can expect a mix of rares, uncommons, uncommons and mythics, and the occasional commander or legendary creature that ties the whole theme together. Beyond that, you get the satisfying tactile feel of opening a sealed product with a promise of something shiny and potentially spicy inside.

In the past we have asked the question: does the premium treatment justify the price? The answer often depends on how much you love chase cards, how deep your card valuation goes, and whether your playgroup enjoys the art and lore that Battle for Baldur's Gate packs offer. ## Deck building prompt: turning pulls into plays
### Strategies that shine in Baldur's Gate
The commander ecosystem gains new support, and Set Boosters sometimes help you edge into niche archetypes that you might not have explored otherwise. Here are a few play patterns to consider when assembling a Baldur's Gate themed deck:

- Multicolor ramp and card draw that align with the Gate's broader mana base. Think of commanders who enjoy a healthy mana curve plus card velocity.
- Politics and commander interaction. In a format where everyone fights for the throne, a strong and reliable commander who can stabilize or swing a game with proper timing is invaluable.
- The artifact and tribal synergies that show up in cross-set interactions. While not the focus of every pull, you can still weave a deck that takes advantage of strong artifact support or a compelling creature type synergy.

### A couple of example shells
- A six to seven colorless or low color cost ramp approach, enabling a mid to late game explosion. This is a classic Baldur's Gate style of deck building: you accumulate value, your opponents stall, and you drop a crescendo of threats when the window opens.
- A control variant that uses removal and defensive counterplay to manage the board. The set's bigger cards can complement a plan that buys time to deploy a commander with a win condition that aligns with the table's pace.
