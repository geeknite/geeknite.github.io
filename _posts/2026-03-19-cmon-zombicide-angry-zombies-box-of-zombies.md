## How It Plays: Core Mechanics Revisited

If you played Zombicide before, you already know the core loop: players manage survivors, scrounge for gear, and face waves of zombies until a mission objective is met or the table erupts in thematic chaos. Angry Zombies adds spice to that already potent mix with several notable tweaks.

### The Zombie Cadence

The additional zombie variants bring fresh threats that require rethinking your turn order and your risk tolerance. Some variants chase relentlessly; others ambush from unexpected angles, turning a simple corridor into a death trap scenario. The key is to anticipate not just the nearest threat but the most likely multi-step envelopment, which is a fancy way of telling you to stop sprinting into the open with your shotgun blazing and consider some cover and synergy with your teammates.

### Survivor Roles and Team Synergy

The survivors gain new skill cards and equipment options that encourage team combos. The synergy feels like a pickup basketball game where everyone has a role, but the roles morph as the board state shifts. There are moments where you will plan a multi-step trap and then watch the dice throw a curveball that makes your plan look hilariously optimistic. The laughter is deliberate; the tension in the room is powerful, and that is what makes cooperative games so compelling.

### Campaign and Scenario Flow

Angry Zombies shines when you run a linked set of scenarios or a short campaign. The Box of Zombies provides more narrative hooks than a soap opera season finale, and the mission design rewards memory and bookkeeping discipline without requiring you to sprint through a thousand-page rulebook. Expect the occasional rule-clarification moment that devolves into an enthusiastic debate about whether a particular modifier applies to a specific zombie type on the current turn. This is normal, and the community will likely produce an FAQ that is longer than the rulebook itself within a few weeks of release.

If you enjoy progressive campaigns, you will appreciate the sense of escalation and the way new equipment and zombies unlock as you complete objectives. The box encourages you to revisit older scenarios with new constraints, which keeps your play sessions fresh rather than cyclical.
