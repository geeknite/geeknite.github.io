## How to play in bread loaf size sections

The core loop of Sushi Go Party is simple: in each round, you draft one card from your hand and pass the rest to your left. After everyone has drafted, you score using the current menu. Then you reset the board, reshuffle, and draft again with a new menu, if your session uses multiple rounds.

More specifically:

- Each player starts with a hand of cards (size depends on player count)
- On your turn, pick one card to keep
- Pass the remaining cards to the next player
- After all cards are drafted, score the cards according to the active menu
- Rounds repeat until you’ve exhausted the deck or you decide to stop

The official rules provide a crisp structure, but what makes this game shine is the interplay between what you take and what you pass. You have to think about your current hand, the likely cards your neighbors will keep, and how to set up your own eventual collection of items to maximize points.

In practice, you’ll be surprised by how much decision-making exists in a lightweight game. It feels like learning to juggle while someone is passing you sushi on a conveyor belt—fun, frantic, and perfectly manageable.

## Strategy slice: how to optimize decisions

- Early planning matters, but not too early. A strong opening that aligns with your upcoming menu can set you up, but you should be flexible; menus change play style every round.
- The social wedge: use the passing scheme to influence opponents. Sometimes you take a card not because you need it but because you want to stop someone from building a perfect row.
- Learn the menus. Some rely on sets of identical items, others on a mix of unique cards. Don’t chase a strategy that your table cannot support; adapt to what the current menu invites.
- Card counting on the fly helps. If you notice a handful of cards that will appear in multiple rounds, you can plan to monopolize or deny those items accordingly.
- Don’t ignore timing. In some rounds you will need to prioritize speed over perfection so you can get maximum rounds in before fatigue sets in.
