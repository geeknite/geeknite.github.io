## What is Go F Yourselves?

Go F Yourselves is a fast-paced, cheeky riff on the classic Go Fish mechanic. Instead of asking your friend for a card by number or suit, you’re now probing for a level of adult humor, innuendo, or wildly inappropriate theme that may or may not align with your group’s dynamic. The game blends standard card mechanics—draws, matches, and sets—with a deck built around risqué prompts, cheeky one-liners, and occasionally questionable prompts that make you question your life choices the moment you read them aloud with your poker face intact.

The core loop is simple enough: you draw a card, you ask someone for a set, you brag a little about your “fishing technique,” and you pass the turn when the group decides you’ve exhausted your bravado for the moment. The result is a game that can be played in roughly 15 to 40 minutes depending on the crowd. It’s great for a party night when you want to wake up your group chat with audible laughter and the kind of eye contact that says, I am now morally compromised but we are friends. And yes, you can play with a mixed age group if you’ve decided to turn the dial down for the occasional adult in the room; just be mindful that the risqué prompts are not your grandma’s shopping list.

## How to Play: The Quickstart Guide

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a hand to each player (the traditional Go Fish approach works here). The number of cards per player scales with the number of players, but a standard 5-6 cards per person is a solid baseline.
- Place the remaining deck face down in the center. This becomes the draw pile.
- Decide if you’re playing with “mature mode” or “light mode.” Mature mode unlocks the full spectrum of prompts; light mode trims down the content so you can still laugh without fear of your in-laws dropping by unannounced.

### Turn flow
- On your turn, you ask another player for a specific card rank that matches one in your hand. If they have it, they give you all copies of that rank, and you get another go. If not, you draw from the pile and the turn passes.
- The aim is to collect complete sets of five (or whatever the deck specifies) of each rank, which in Go F Yourselves means you’ve collected the ultimate gag payload for that category.
- The first player to collect the required sets wins, but as any good adult game should teach you, the real win is the laughter you share along the way.

The humor lands better with groups that know each other’s boundaries and are ready to push them for a night of memory-making stories and blushing for the right reasons. You don’t need a PhD in probability to enjoy Go F Yourselves, but a few strategic ideas can push your experience from decent to legendary:

- Read the room: the best players know when to switch to light mode or to pause for a collective chuckle rather than push a risky prompt that could derail the vibe. A well-timed pause can be more valuable than the most brilliant card-trade in your hand.
