## What is NSFW Edition and how does it differ from the original?
NSFW Edition is basically a remix of the core Exploding Kittens concept with a more mature, raunchier flavor. The mechanics remain largely recognizable: you are drawing cards, trying to avoid triggering the dreaded Exploding Kitten, while using various action cards to alter outcomes, steal cards, or mess with opponents. The big shifts are in the flavor text, the card art, and a handful of card effects that lean into adult humor without stepping over a line that would force a parental advisory sticker on your gaming nights. If you enjoyed the chaotic energy of the original but wanted a little more edge, this edition is designed with you in mind—assuming you’re not playing with children who prize moral clarity over snickering at puns about feline chaos.

## How to play: quick overview
If you’ve played the original Exploding Kittens, the NSFW Edition won’t reinvent the wheel. The goal remains simple: don’t draw an Exploding Kitten card, or suffer the consequences dictated by other cards—usually a quick snicker from the table and a dramatic outsized punishment that would be illegal in a few jurisdictions of the cosmos, but perfectly allowed here on your living room battlefield.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a few cards to each player (usually around 5 to 7, depending on the number of players and how much chaos you want).
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards back into the deck in a way that ensures a few safe rounds before the inevitable catastrophe.
- Decide who goes first by some silly measure (rock-paper-scissors, best cat video on the internet, or the age-old “the host says so” method).

### Rules quick overview
- On your turn, you draw a card. If it’s an Exploding Kitten, you’re in trouble unless you have a Defuse card or some other effect lets you skip the explosion.
- Use action cards to manipulate turns, swap cards, steal cards, or add new rules that warp the flow of play in exactly the way a cat would if it could issue edicts from an ethereal litter box throne.
- The game ends when the last Exploding Kitten is defused or dodged in a glorious chain of bad decisions and even worse puns.

The actual rulebook is short but surprisingly robust. The NSFW edition leans into self-contained humor and references that a group of friends who have known each other for years will recognize instantly. It’s the kind of game that makes a three-hour session feel like a late-night party that ends with everyone wearing goofy hats and a trust exercise that basically boils down to, “Did you just draw a card with a cat pun?” If the table is cooperative about embracing the absurd, the experience lands with the same merciless laughter as the original, but with a sharper, wittier edge.

### Strategy, luck, and table dynamics
Exploding Kittens thrives on a balance between luck and strategic card use. The NSFW Edition is no different in that regard, but the added humor creates a different social dynamic. You’ll notice players become increasingly strategic about when to deliver a zinger, because a well-timed joke can deflate a tense moment as skillfully as a well-timed Defense card can avert disaster. ## The experience: a session by session breakdown

From a design standpoint, the NSFW edition is a refined variant, not a complete reinvention. It makes sense for groups who want a familiar dynamics but a different flavor. If you’re the type who enjoys collecting multiple iterations of the same base game to keep the shelf interesting, this edition offers enough variation to justify a separate purchase.
