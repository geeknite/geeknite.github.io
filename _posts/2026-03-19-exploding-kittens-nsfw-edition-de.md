## How to play: quick overview
If you know Exploding Kittens, you know the rhythm here. The goal is simple: don’t draw an Exploding Kitten card, or suffer the consequences dictated by other cards—usually a shared laugh and a theatrical punishment that would be questionable in a courtroom full of cats, but totally acceptable on your living room floor.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a handful of cards to each player (typical 5–7, depending on player count and how chaotic you want the table to be).
- Return Exploding Kitten cards to the deck in a way that guarantees a few safe rounds before the inevitable catastrophe. Because suspense builds better with a little preposterous optimism.
- Decide who goes first using something silly and distinctly gamer-y (rock-paper-scissors, a best cat video on the internet, or the host’s commanding decree).

### Rules quick overview
- On your turn, you draw a card. If it’s an Exploding Kitten, you’re in trouble unless you have a Defuse card or another effect that lets you dodge the blast.
- Use action cards to manipulate turns, swap cards, steal cards, or craft new rules that warp the flow of play in exactly the way a mischievous cat would if it could type a haiku from within a litter box.
- The game ends when the last Exploding Kitten is defused or dodged in a glorious chain of questionable decisions and even worse puns.
