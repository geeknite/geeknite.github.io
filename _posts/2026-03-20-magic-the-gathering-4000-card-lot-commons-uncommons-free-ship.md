## Sorting, Sorting, and Sorting Again

### Practical Sorting Techniques

Sort the cards by color first to get a quick sense of the distribution. Then sort by rarity (commons vs. uncommons) and by set where possible. A humble, well-organized sorting approach will save you hours when you start building decks, cubes, or new player bundles. Here are a few practical steps to tame the bulk:

- Start with a “playable” pass. Pull out common removal, efficient two-drops, and cards that have clear, modern-value in budget decks.
- Create a separate pile for uncommons with known payoffs: pump spells, efficient fixers, and single-card combos that players actually run in cheaper builds.
- Consider color balance per deck you want to run. If you’re drafting a two-color deck, separate cards by color to see how many solid two-color options you truly have.
- Declutter duplicates that are clearly duplicates of the same card without any foil or unique art variant. They’re not worthless, but they aren’t the focus for a basic deck either.

### Protection, Storage, and Card Care

Bulks can be a bit rough on the sleeves department. If you intend to keep the bulk long-term, invest in a sturdy storage solution: Apollo-type binders or deck boxes with acid-free sleeves. It’s not glamorous, but it’s a life-saver for card longevity. Card sleeves, even for commons, help reduce surface wear from shuffle friction and air exposure. If you’re planning to loan or lend these cards, proper sleeving keeps the color identity readable and reduces the risk of accidental damage during games.
