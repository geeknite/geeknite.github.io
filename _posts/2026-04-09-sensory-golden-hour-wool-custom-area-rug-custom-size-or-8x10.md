## Size Matters: Custom Size vs 8x10 — What Should You Pick?

The rug’s two primary sizing options are custom sizes (special-order, often tailored to exact room dimensions) and a standard 8x10 footprint. Both have their charms, and both play nicely with the Golden Hour color story. Here’s how to decide:

- Custom Size: If you have a room with odd dimensions, or you’re trying to anchor a sectional or a bed with a specific floor plan, a custom size ensures you don’t have to “make do” with an awkward leftover space. Custom orders typically require a bit more lead time, but the payoff is a perfect fit. Pro tip: measure twice, cut once, and then measure again to confirm you measured correctly. If your space is a standard rectangle with a couch and a coffee table, 8x10 typically covers the core area while leaving room to breathe. It’s also a sensible choice if you’re layering other textiles like a separate accent rug or a large rug under a dining table.

## Durability and Care: Keeping the Golden Hour Golden

Wool rugs are known for durability, but every fiber has its Achilles’ heel. Here’s how the Sensory Golden Hour holds up under typical domestic conditions and how to care for it to maximize longevity:

- Vacuuming: Use a low-suction setting or a vacuum without a beater bar to avoid friction on the fibers. Regular gentle vacuuming keeps dirt from embedding deep into the pile. Rotate the rug every few months if your space has uneven foot traffic to prevent distinctive wear patterns.
- Spills and stains: Blot with a clean cloth—don’t rub, don’t scrub. Wool can resist a surprising amount of liquid absorption if addressed promptly. For oily stains, a mild wool-safe cleaner or a mix of water and a touch of white vinegar can help; test on a corner first.
- Cleaning frequency: A light refresh every 6–12 months is usually enough for typical living areas. If you have pets, you might need touch-ups more frequently, but wool fibers tend to hide hair and lint better than many synthetics.
- Rotation and sun exposure: Wool is relatively stable to sunlight compared to natural fibers like jute, but long, direct sun exposure can fade the color over time. If your rug sits in a sunlit corner, rotate it periodically and use curtains to protect the hue.

## Maintenance FAQs: Quick Answers for Busy Humans

- How often should I vacuum? Regular light vacuuming once a week is a good baseline; deeper cleanings can be scheduled every 6–12 months depending on foot traffic.
- Can I machine-wash wool? No. Wool rugs should not go through a typical home washing machine. Spot cleaning and professional cleaning are recommended for major stains.
- Is it OK to use a rug pad? Yes. A rug pad helps with stability, reduces wear, and can improve insulation under the rug. Choose a pad that matches the rug’s size and your floor type.
- Will it shed forever? Wool tends to shed a bit at first, especially in the initial weeks. Regular vacuuming typically reduces shedding quickly. If shedding persists, contact customer support for guidance.
