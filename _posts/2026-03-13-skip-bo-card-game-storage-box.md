## Size, Fit, and Card Compatibility
Skip-Bo decks come in variations that range from standard packs to “grand conga line” collections if you’ve mixed in an expansion or two. When evaluating a storage box, consider:

If you’re a purist who hates any extra fiddling, go for a box with fewer but sturdier dividers. If you’re a connoisseur of “never too much order,” a box with lots of adjustable compartments is your best friend.

## Maintenance Tips: Keeping Your Box in Top Shape
- Regular quick wipe-downs: A microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner will keep fingerprints and juice stains away. Don’t soak the box; moisture is the nemesis of cardboard and some plastics.
- Check the dividers: If they’re removable, ensure they sit flat and don’t tilt. A crooked divider means you’ll be chasing a rogue stock pile every other game.
- Store upright: When possible, keep the box upright to minimize the risk of cards slipping out and migrating into the couch cushions. The couch is a black hole; we don’t need it to become your card’s permanent residence.
- label maintenance: Revisit your labels after a few months. If you’ve added an expansion, update the labeling so new players aren’t left behind in the labeling labyrinth.

In short, a little upkeep goes a long way. It’s less “box maintenance” and more “maintaining the vibe of your game nights.”
