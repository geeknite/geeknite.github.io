## What Fortune Row is really about
Fortune Row places the party in the middle of a chain of interconnected favors, debts, and whispered promises that have tangible effects on the city. It escalates quickly from a routine investigation into a layered challenge where every action has a reaction, and the reaction might be kindness from a mythic patron or a snarl from a rival faction that views the player characters as pawns with a schedule. The module plays with three core threads that City of Mist players tend to love: investigative play anchored by clue collection, supernatural icons with moral weight, and a social-political subtext that gives the investigation consequences beyond a simple victory or defeat.

In terms of GM tooling, Fortune Row offers a robust set of mechanics to keep track of Influence, Clues, and the ever-present risk of a Verity flip. You will have opportunities for chase scenes, social bargaining, and a few key set-piece moments that reward preparation and flexible pacing. The design expects you to lean into the city as a living character rather than treating it as a stage. If you enjoy city-building games in miniature, Fortune Row provides you a playground where rumors are currency and the rumor mill is as dangerous as any gunfight.

## Structure and pacing: what to expect from the book
Fortune Row is built around a sequence of investigative beats that culminate in a major reveal and a climactic choice. It tracks well for a two to four-session arc, depending on how aggressively your table engages side threads. The pacing is designed to reward careful note-taking and attention to the city as a character. You’ll find a balance between dialogue-heavy scenes that let players lean into the masks they wear and action beats that keep the adrenaline up without turning every encounter into a shootout. The GM guidance is explicit about reading room for improvisation: the city does not always follow the plan, but it does provide lip service to the plan’s existence, which is almost as useful.

To help with pacing, the module provides scripted transitions between scenes, an explicit countdown in the form of time-based complications, and a few red herrings that are convincing enough to misdirect players without feeling like cheap tricks. The result is a table that feels like it’s chasing a moving target rather than a static blueprint. If you like your investigative mysteries to feel alive rather than museum-like, Fortune Row scratches that itch nicely.

## Core mechanics and GM notes
City of Mist runs on a system of iconic power, where each PC has a mythic identity you can lean on during scenes. Fortune Row embraces this structure and ties it to a regional hub within the city’s underworld: a district where risk and reward dance in the same elevator. Expect opportunities to Earn Clues, collect Influence with various factions, and run into Verities that push players to reveal who they truly are beyond their street-level persona.

GM notes here are particularly valuable. Fortune Row does not require you to memorize a long list of NPC stats. Instead, it gives you recognizable archetypes and clear motivations, which makes it easier to improvise. If you like to improvise NPCs on the fly, you’ll find the book supportive rather than restrictive. If you prefer a tight, pre-defined cast, you can lean on the prebuilt profiles while still improvising dialogue options that fit your players in the moment.

Hands-on tips for GM prep:

- Do a quick city map walkthrough to identify two or three streets where action could plausibly intersect. This helps with anchoring scenes and gives players sense of place.
- Prepare two to three alternate consequences for every major decision. The city is a living organism; if players pick a thread, make sure the other threads react in believable ways, even if believability means a slightly chaotic outcome.
- Use Verities as a narrative pressure valve. Let the players question what they know, and let the Verities push them toward a truth that is hard to swallow.

## Scene-by-scene breakdown and how to run it
Fortune Row unfolds as a sequence of linked scenes that challenge players to balance their personal myths with public consequences. The book provides a recommended flow, but you can reshuffle as needed while preserving the core tension. Here is a high-level breakdown that captures the spirit of the adventure while offering a framework you can drop into your table.

### Scene 1: The Whispering Ledger
In the opening scene, the PCs stumble onto a ledger filled with numbers that look innocent until you realize they map to people, debts, and whispered promises. The investigators must determine who is owed, who owes, and what happens if the numbers stop moving. The scene emphasizes clue gathering, social beating, and a small puzzle that reveals a protected Benefactor signature.

### Scene 2: The Mask Parade
A street festival celebrating a legendary local myth erupts into a tense negotiation between factions. The players navigate a social scene with embedded power plays, collecting allies or enemies depending on their approach. You’ll want to lean heavily into the bits where masks fall away and true intentions become visible. This scene tests your players’ ability to bluff, bargain, and swap narratives on the fly.

### Scene 3: The Neon Heist
The party attempts a high-stakes heist that uses both physical skill and mythic leverage. The obstacles mix environmental challenges with storytelling, keeping the tension high without tipping into cinematic chaos. It’s a great moment to show how the city rewards clever thinking over brute force.

### Scene 4: The Price of Fortune
The final act centers on a choice that will ripple through the city. The PCs must decide whether to use their powers to fix a systemic problem or to protect the status quo for the sake of personal gain. The consequences are not binary; expect moral complexity and a few uncomfortable but satisfying resolutions.

This structure is designed to be scalable. If your table prefers longer investigation arcs, you can lengthen Scene 1 or 2 with extra clues and side NPCs. If your players crave a leaner experience, you can compress Scene 3 into a single dramatic confrontation and move directly to Scene 4.

### Example NPCs you might meet
- The Dealer of Daylight, a mythic banker who can turn favors into collateral.

Each NPC is designed to have a motive, a vulnerability, and a couple of lines of dialogue that can be flipped depending on the players choices. Use them as anchors for your improvisations or as springboards for longer threads that could justify a sequel or a spin-off.

## Strengths and potential pitfalls
Strengths
- Strong mood and city voice that fit the City of Mist brand while offering fresh mythic hooks.
- Balanced pacing with a clear escalation path, plus room for improvisation.
- Solid GM tooling: explicit scene transitions, manageable number of NPCs, and versatile encounter design.
- High replay value thanks to modular structure and multiple endings.

Potential pitfalls
- The wealth of clues and twists can overwhelm new GMs if you don’t pace yourself. Start with two or three threads and gradually introduce others as players gain confidence.
- Some players may want more combat or action anchors; while the module has action, it leans toward investigation and negotiation. If your group loves pure dungeon crawls, you might need to bolt on a couple of extra fight-focused scenes.
- The city-as-character concept is fantastic but can feel abstract if you don’t lean into the mood cues and props during play. A little theater work goes a long way here.

## How to run Fortune Row at your table
- Read the opening threads aloud and ask players which identity they want to foreground early in the session. The answers will shape your early clues and the tone for the rest of the adventure.
- Prepare two alternate paths around each major choice. A city like Fortune Row rewards flexibility; you will want your options ready so you can pivot without breaking immersion.
- Use prop hints to anchor memory. A single token or a fragment can unlock a cascade of roleplay opportunities as players try to interpret its meaning within the mythic economy of the city.
- Encourage players to lean into the Relationship system. The bonds between PCs and the NPCs are a powerful engine for both suspense and humor. Don’t shy away from letting a few relationships deliver consequences that feel personal and dramatic.
- If you plan a longer arc, seed a follow-up thread during the final scenes. A single, well-placed hint can set the stage for a second adventure that feels inevitable and exciting at the same time.
