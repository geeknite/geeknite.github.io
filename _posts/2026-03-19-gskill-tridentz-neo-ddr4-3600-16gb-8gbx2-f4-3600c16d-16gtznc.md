### XMP/DOCP and platform notes

- For Ryzen (Zen 3/Zen 4 depending on your motherboard), enabling DOCP/XMP typically yields the 3600 CL16 timing with one caveat: the memory controller’s calibration can be picky on higher sub-timings. Some boards love it; some boards require a touch more voltage or a gentle timing tweak to 16-18-18-38 at 1.35V.
- For Intel platforms (e.g., 12th/13th gen with Z690/Z790), the same DOCP/XMP approach usually lands you at the advertised 3600 CL16. However, you might see slight panics if your kit isn’t binned for the exact speed on your silicon. ## Platform compatibility and build tips

The Trident Z Neo DDR4-3600 kit is a versatile option for both AMD and Intel builds. Here are practical tips to squeeze the most out of your RAM without the drama:

- Ensure your motherboard BIOS is up to date. Memory compatibility and DOCP/XMP profiles are often refined in BIOS updates.
- Use DOCP/XMP first. If you encounter instability, consider manually setting 3600 MT/s with CL16-18-18-38 at 1.35V, then run a memory stability test.
- For Ryzen users, enable Infinity Fabric (FCLK) around 1:1 with memory speed if your motherboard allows it. This can yield noticeable gains in certain workloads due to improved memory latency.
- If you’re on a tight budget, consider reducing DRAM voltage slightly to 1.25–1.30V while preserving stability for light workloads. In practice, the sweet spot varies by motherboard, so test carefully.
- When shopping for RAM, always ensure you have adequate clearance for your CPU cooler. The Neo’s height is modest, but if you’re using a big air cooler or a large AIO, double-check clearance on the first try.
