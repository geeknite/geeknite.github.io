### How the Flux Cards Work

Flux cards aren’t just “Draw Two” or “Skip.” They modify the rules in small, chaotic ways that feel like a design brief written by someone who binge-watched a full NFL game and then sketched a dozen color palettes on a napkin. Here are a few sample effects:

- Flux Skip: The next player is skipped unless they can counter with another Flux card of the same foil family.
- Flux Draw: A modified Draw Two that can occasionally force a Draw Four when the mood hits the designer.
- Flux Reverse: Reverses order and sometimes swaps color requirements for a single turn.

If you’re a control freak, Flux is both a headache and a lifebuoy. It’s the card-game equivalent of a sports highlight reel: exhilarating, loud, and full of improbable plays you’ll tell your friends about for months.

### House Rules Variants

If Flux becomes a staple for your group, you can adopt house rules to tame or turbocharge the chaos:

These variants can keep things fresh for long-term fans and prevent the game from devolving into an incoherent sneeze of color and chaos.
