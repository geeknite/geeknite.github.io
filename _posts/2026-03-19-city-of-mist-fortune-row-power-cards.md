## Introduction: what is Fortune Row power cards anyway?

Fortune Row is a thematic add-on that tilts a well lit city toward a neighborhood full of risk, reward, and very bad decisions. The Power Cards are a deck designed to slot into the City of Mist core system, giving players new ways to push tags, trigger powers, and set up dramatic gambits. If the core game is a black and white film with neon smoke, Fortune Row adds a third dimension: a deck of glimmering d20 adjacent possibilities that reminds you that every hero in this city is also a gambler at heart.

## What you get in Fortune Row a quick contents overview

Fortune Row power cards come in a compact suit of categories, each one feeding a different flavor of scene: gambits, fates, and factions that tie into the street level noir vibe of the district. The cards are designed to be drawn when the GM wants to shift momentum, or when a PC wants to pull a stunt that relies on social leverage rather than brute force. The visuals lean toward art deco neon, with a little graffiti and a lot of attitude. Each card carries a compact mechanic that can be integrated into the existing powers and tags of a character, usually by prompting a tag boost, a reframe of a scene, or a free minor action on a successful roll.

If you’re familiar with City of Mist card design, you’ll notice a familiar vocabulary: tags that represent your mystery, powers that align with the mythos, and a few move options that push the narrative rather than the dice. Fortunes Row adds a few new tag types that feel unique to the district: hustle, crew loyalty, and street-level leverage. The aim is to give your City of Mist sessions a tactile layer of physical cards that players can hold, trade, or bluff with. And yes, the art direction gives you that cinematic polish you want when a fog machine is involved.

## Card design and flavor how the art and text carry the mood

### Thematic alignment
Fortune Row cards are crafted to feel like windows into a crooked arcade of choices. They lean into noir tropes with a modern twist clever lines, a little bravado, and the sense that every advantage is paid for in something else usually a secret or a debt. The text on the cards pairs concise mechanical prompts with evocative flavor lines. This pairing is crucial it lets a GM describe a scene while players quickly map their options to a real in world consequence. In practical terms, you’re getting a deck that nudges the narrative toward the kind of dramatic reveal you’d expect from a heist flick not a courtroom drama.

### Card variety
You ll find a mix of action cards things your hero can attempt in the moment, consequence cards what happens if the action backfires or creates a new complication, and leverage cards which let someone s reputation or street cred swing a result. The variety helps prevent stagnation. You don t want every scene to feel like a talent show where you re all showing off your power tags; the Fortune Row deck injects the unpredictability of real urban hustle. It s a nice balance between mythic flair and street smarts exact texture City of Mist thrives on.

### Artwork and layout
The art across Fortune Row keeps a consistent vibe hand drawn linework with bold color blocks a nod to lamp lit alleyways and rain slick pavements. If you re the kind of GM who has a ritual of showing off the card art to the table while you build tension, you ll love this deck. The layout prioritizes readability big title lines a quick bullet of effect and a compact flavor text. It s not trying to win a design award it s trying to help you run a session without flipping through a dozen pages for a single effect.

## Mechanics and how they feel at the table

### Core interaction
The Power Cards work as an extension rather than a rewrite of the base rules. When a card is drawn or activated you typically gain a temporary tag or trigger a short cascade of narrative effects. The effect is close to a dramatic boon that doesn t derail the character s core identity but shifts the spotlight. In practice this means you can inject a flashy moment without rewriting your entire build which is exactly what you want when you re running a large cast with a city full of masks.

### Balance and pacing considerations
Balancing new cards in a game with a dynamic reward system is tricky Fortune Row does a fairly commendable job of keeping power spikes in check while still letting a clever or bold player pull off something memorable. The cards tend to reward creative play rather than raw power. You ll see cards that reward negotiation manipulation or clever misdirection more than I roll once and blow everything up. If your table loves social intrigue and plan and counter plan battles Fortune Row slides into that groove nicely.

### Interactions with existing tags and powers
Because the City of Mist system already uses tags to frame powers the Fortune Row cards slot in smoothly. They don t replace core mechanics they augment them. For players who enjoy playing the long game setting up a payoff across multiple scenes Fortune Row s leverage and fate cards can form a slow burn arc that culminates in a dramatic reveal. And that s the sweet spot you re not forced to lean into combat but you re given the option to swing the moment decisively when a window opens.

## Narrative impact scenes that feel cinematic rather than cinematic adjacent

### Scene design with Fortune Row
One of the most delightful aspects of the deck is its ability to compel a scene s tone without dictating the outcome. Cards that hint at a rival crew a hidden debt or a betrayal in the wings can turn a routine investigation into a tense standoff or a high stakes poker round under a flickering streetlight. The GM can use a card s prompt to pivot a scene from a straightforward clue hunt into a negotiation a standoff or a double cross that redefines the stakes mid session.

### Player agency and responsibility
The deck doesn t strip agency away from players it hands them interesting forks. When a card offers leverage through social maneuvering or misdirection players are motivated to engage creatively. The risk of course is that some cards can feel like they re nudging players toward a particular style. The remedy is simple lean into your group s preferred rhythm. If your table likes social gambits Fortune Row will sing if you re hard coded into punchy action you ll still find moments that feel like cinematic cameos.

### GM prep and pacing
Fortune Row requires a little prep but not the kind that makes you cry into your own character sheets. A quick pass to map out which cards align with the current arc and which meta narrative beats you want to emphasize is enough. The deck provides enough variety that you won t reach a point where you ve exhausted all interesting prompts in a single session. If your campaign has a running line about a particular gang or a debt that looms over the cast you can tailor the cards to emphasize that thread which is a nice touch.

## Practical table setup how to run Fortune Row smoothly

### Logistics and placement
Set aside a dedicated space for the Fortune Row deck. The current practice I ve adopted is to keep the deck near the GM s screen with a simpler draw pile and a discard fate pile accessible to both GM and players. Keeping the cards visible speeds up play and makes the moment when a card is revealed feel like a reveal rather than a rummage through a backpack of excuses.

### When to draw and who decides
Let the scene s momentum guide you. If a player is trying a bold social stunt draw a card to determine the kind of complication or leverage that will surface. If the scene is tense but not crucial to the main plot you can let fate cards shape a quick twist. The flexibility is the deck s strongest feature. It s not that you re forcing outcomes you re inviting outcomes to compete with your players plans.

### House rules and tweaks
As with any expansion house rules improve your life. Here are a few tweaks I ve found useful: 
- Limit the number of draws per session to avoid card fatigue and maintain surprise. 
- Allow players to call a card effect once per arc in exchange for a small narrative cost. This keeps tension high without making the deck feel like a free snack. 
- Pair a few cards with specific NPC factions in Fortune Row so that the deck reinforces the world building you ve already done.
