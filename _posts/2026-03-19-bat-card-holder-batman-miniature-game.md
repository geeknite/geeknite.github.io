## Card Capacity and Layout
### Two bays, flexible capacity
The Bat-Card Holder is not trying to replace your card binder; it’s designed to complement it. Each bay is wide enough for standard BMtG cards (and many sleeved cards) to sit comfortably with a card sans sleeve being the baseline. Sleeved cards crowd less, so you can pack a decent handful per bay, particularly if you choose a slim sleeve. In practice, you’ll likely keep 10–20 cards per bay during a standard BMtG session, depending on whether you favor light sleeves or fully sleeved builds.

### Sleeves and fitment
If your BMtG cards are sleeved (and you should sleeve them, heroes deserve protection), you’ll want to test fitment with your preferred sleeve thickness. The holder isn’t made for ultra-thick premium sleeves, but it handles common brands like Dragon Shield and Ultra Pro without grief. If you go with a particularly chunky sleeve, you may need to adjust your layout or opt for the alternate center tray to keep things from feeling crowded.

### Center tray utility
The center tray is small but mighty. It’s ideal for tokens, WIP counters, or a spare couple of cards you know you’ll reach for between rounds. The tray’s lip helps keep small items in place during a table shake or a casual Batman quote that goes a little too dramatic.

### Depth, height, and reach
The holder’s height is designed so you can glance at a card and flip it with minimal wrist movement. It’s not a full-blown “display stand,” but you’ll appreciate the ease of access when you’re mid-activation and need to check a power or a condition without pulling focus from the table’s narrative.

## Quick Start: Getting the Most from Your Bat-Card Holder
### Step 1: Sort your deck by function
Before you first game, sort cards by tactic type, character, or round order. This helps you populate the bays in the order you intend to play them, reducing on-table rummaging.

### Step 2: Test fit sleeved cards
If you sleeve, try a few standard sleeves to see how many you can fit per bay. Plan for a couple of cards you will reach for mid-game rather than the full stack. The goal is smooth flips, not card archery.

### Step 3: Position for reach and visibility
Place facing up the cards you’ll use most this round near the front of each bay. If you have a habit of consulting status cards mid-round, keep those within a reach-friendly area so you never reveal more than you want to the table.

### Step 4: Maintain the look
Light cleaning after every game night keeps the bat-silhouette crisp and prevents the finish from dulling. A quick wipe down also helps you notice any developing wear on the card slots before it becomes a problem.
