The 84 Origins concept
The core itch this expansion scratches is variety. in Dixit you lean on your ability to connect a story to a single card while opponents guess which card you played. With 84 new Origins, you get a wider spread of themes to pull from and more opportunities to tailor your clues to the players at the table. Some Origins encourage a shared memory angle, others lean into myth and fable, still others chase the silly side of storytelling with improbable combinations that nevertheless feel just right for your crew. Different players will feel energized by different kinds of prompts, and that variety translates into longer play sessions with less risk of friction from samey prompts.

How it changes the game
All of Dixit hinges on the storyteller bridging a clue to a card that matches the board in this round. Memories adds more bricks to your storytelling wall. More Origins means more potential angles to base your clues on — memory fragments, legends, the geometry of a dream, a strange origin myth, or even a playful subversion of the prompt itself. The practical impact is a twofold shift: first, it raises the ceiling on clever clue creation; second, it broadens the pool of possible cards that can be linked to a story in ways you might not expect. You can craft a story about a memory fragment from a distant era and pluck a card that visualizes that memory in a way your players will instantly recognize or exceptionally misinterpret. Either outcome is part of the fun.

Setup and components
Set up time stays within Dixit norms. Shuffle the extra 84 Origin cards into the existing deck if you wish to blend the memories with the base set, or you can keep them separate for a two deck approach that makes for longer, more varied sessions. The rules clarifications that accompany the expansion are minimal but useful. The added diversity means you may need to adjust your usual storytelling tempo; in other words, be prepared for more pauses as players craft the perfect phrase to anchor their chosen card to the Origin prompt.

Playing with relations and misdirection
Dixit works best when players trust a storyteller to be a little mischievous without being unfair. The 84 Origins in Memories encourage just that. You can run a round where the Origin nudges players toward a particular imagery cluster, but the card you choose might be from a different part of the visual spectrum. The result is playful confusion that still remains solvable with a bit of social deduction. The key technique here is to lean into the prompt without telegraphing the card too obviously. For example, if the Origin hints at a mythic birthplace, you can phrase your clue in a way that invites players to think of the place rather than the creature on the card. That balance between oblique and accessible is what makes this expansion shine in the hands of a capable storyteller.

Strategies and tips for storytellers
If you want to maximize your scoring potential with the Memories expansion, consider the following approaches:
- Build a throughline for the round that uses the Origin as a seed. A consistent thread makes it easier for other players to trace a path to your chosen card.
- Use humor as a bridge. A witty clue anchored to an Origin can lower the barrier for guessers to connect the dots without revealing the exact card.
- Don’t be afraid to be cryptic. The more you introduce ambiguity without breaking the shared context, the more delight players feel when they finally discover the card. Ambiguity is not weakness here; it is the engine of the game’s joy.
- Use reactions as clues. When players laugh or pause in recognition, you have likely found a strong fit between your clue and the card. You want those moments, not the silent uncertainty that saps momentum.
- Pair a memory oriented Origin with a card that clearly embodies a memory you want to evoke. The synergy between word play and imagery is where the Minds of Dixit shine the brightest.

House rules and variant ideas
To keep Memories feeling fresh, try one of these variants:
- Memory marathon: run a sequence of rounds where the same storyteller uses a new Origin each round but must link to a single evolving story line. This encourages deeper storytelling and long arcs across the session.
- Thematic rotations: group Origins by sub themes (mythic birthplace, long forgotten technology, elements of nature) and rotate the prompt type every few rounds. It is a nice way to keep the mental cogs turning.
- Cooperative storytelling: a light house rule version where all players contribute a line to a shared story and the chosen card must fit the evolving narrative rather than a single clue. This is a fun twist for families or groups who want a more collaborative vibe.
