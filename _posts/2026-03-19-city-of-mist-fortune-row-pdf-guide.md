## Running Fortune Row: Campaign and Session Planning
If you are planning a campaign arc around Fortune Row, consider the following guidelines to maximize cohesion and player satisfaction:
- Start with a strong anchor: a personal stake for one or more PCs that ties into Fortune Row’s fortunes. A clear personal line helps players engage with the neighborhood on a deeper level.
- Use the NPCs as catalysts, not obstacles: each major NPC should offer a path for the PCs to push the story forward, with clear consequences for choices made.
- Pace is king: alternate between social intrigue and investigation to maintain momentum. Don’t force a chase or a fight when a conversation can reveal a meaningful clue.
- Let luck and risk work both ways: fortune should reward careful play and clever interpretation as often as it punishes careless ambition.
- Allow room for consequences: the city’s mythic economy is a living thing. The players’ actions in Fortune Row should have ripple effects that alter future sessions or campaign outcomes.

A two to four-session arc is a sweet spot for Fortune Row, though it can be extended into a longer campaign if you want to deepen the city’s mythic web. The guide gives you enough scaffolding to adapt to your table’s preferences without requiring a complete rewrite.
