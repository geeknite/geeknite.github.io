## How it changes gameplay: the strategic layer
### Why an update pack matters in a deck-building ecosystem
Dominion’s genius is the ongoing tension between buying victory points and crafting engines. Prosperity expands that tension by giving you bigger fangs to sink into late-game dominance. An update pack like this is meant to reduce the “misalignment moments”—those little misreads where you misinterpret a card’s coin or action benefits and end up with a awkward turn you’d rather forget. A well-done update pack pushes your decisions from “am I making the best legal play this turn?” into “what’s the best long-term route to a Province or a mountain of Prosperity-level bonuses?”

### New cards: what they bring to the table
The exact card pool included in RGG625 isn’t just a random mix; it’s chosen to smooth power curves and clarify interactions that became traps for new players. Expect a bias toward cards that reward diversified approaches—card draw that synergizes with the money economy, cards that reward multi-Turn planning, and a few counterbalances that keep the late game from spiraling into a pure copper-to-Province arms race.

- Card interactions get cleaner: You’ll see fewer ambiguities about coin calculation, buy costs, or the timing of “draw until you…” effects.
- Engine viability gets more predictable: If you enjoy chain-drawing engines, you’ll find that some of the updated cards prefer you to build mid-game tempo rather than race to Province on the first two turns.
- Card variety with purpose: New cards tend to fill gaps in the Prosperity suite rather than duplicating existing ones, which means more interesting deck-building choices and fewer “auto-include” picks.

### Balance and pacing: does it feel fair?
Balance in Dominion is a tricky beast. You want to feel powerful without eliminating the joy of other players—someone should still be able to win without being crushed by a single overpowered combo. The update pack’s approach is to nudge in a direction where a good plan remains viable, but a clever opponent can disrupt it with smart play. For co-op lovers, that’s not your vibe; for competitive players, that’s the magic sauce. The pacing improves in the sense that games are less likely to feel “stale” or “out of hands” within the late midgame, which is where many Dominion nights either click or crash into the coffee table, depending on how much coffee you’ve consumed.
