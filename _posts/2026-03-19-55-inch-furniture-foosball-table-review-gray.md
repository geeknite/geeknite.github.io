### Assembly and Maintenance: Keeping It Smooth
Maintenance is the unglamorous-but-crucial part of owning this table. Regularly check the screws around the cabinet and the edge trim to avoid any unwanted wobble that might turn a friendly game into a tabletop demolition derby. Lubrication isn’t usually required on the rods, but if you notice a squeak during a heated rally, a tiny dab of silicone-based lubricant on the rods (away from the players) can restore silky action.

Cleaning is straightforward: wipe down with a damp cloth, then dry to prevent water marks on the laminate. The gray finish, while forgiving, still appreciates a gentle touch—particularly if you’re living with a messy household where snacks find their way onto the surface as if drawn by magnets. A quick weekly wipe-down keeps it looking showroom-new rather than “game-night-champion-studied-by-a-museum-curator.”

### Safety Considerations
Like all active toys, foosball tables carry safety considerations. Be mindful of small children around sharp edges or heavy rods when the handles are pulled out. Most modern tables have rounded corners and protective edges, which reduce the risk of accidental bumps. Teach kids early to avoid standing directly over the rods and to use the ball-release area with care. In a busy game room, you may want to designate a cue for “no-go” zones to keep the action friendly and safe.

Additionally, check the ball set for any loose stitching or small pieces that could be choking hazards for toddlers. A quick inspection before each session minimizes unexpected interruptions and keeps the vibe fun rather than frantic.
