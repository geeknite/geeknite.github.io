## Deck Building and Strategy Tips
### Leveraging the set’s strengths
- Identify multi-use cards: Look for cards that do more than one thing at once. A card that draws you into two different lines of play is worth more in your binder than a card that only supports one narrow decklist.
- Mana consistency matters: The most successful MH2-driven decks tend to have options for color fixing, allowing multiple color strategies to co-exist as you traverse the game’s early turns.
- Sideboard as a toolbox: If you assemble a modern deck with MH2 staples, your sideboard becomes a set of mismatched keys that still open the same doors—adjust depending on your local tournament field.

### Draft picks and pack strategy
When drafting, you’ll want to stay balanced across colors, looking for signals of where your pack’s power lies. Don’t chase a single color if you’re not seeing enough pick-ups in early packs; diversity in the early picks often pays dividends later as you discover synergies between your incidental bits and the set’s broader strategy.

### Budget and value-minded play
MH2’s value story isn’t just about chase rares; it’s about how much utility you can pull from the commons and uncommons. A well-tuned deck isn’t built on one super card; it’s built on the accumulation of several cards that, together, create a credible plan. If you’re playing a long game, you’ll want to consider the resale value of your pulls, but don’t forget to enjoy the moment—sometimes the best card is the one that makes you grin on turn four.
