### Packaging quality and seal integrity: a love letter to cardboard

Sealed product relies on the integrity of the shrink wrap and the box closure. Here are some practical notes for would-be owners and sellers:

- Shrink wrap condition: Look for uniform shrink wrap with no tears or suspicious resealing marks. A box that looks like it survived a kitchen prank is not ideal.
- Box corners: Corner damage is a telltale sign of rough handling or moisture exposure. The box should retain its shape; if the corners are crushed, you’re looking at a split in perceived value.
- Print quality: Exterior artwork should be sharp and vibrant. If the box looks blurred or washed out, it might indicate inferior printing or exposure to sunlight over years.
- Internal contents: You generally won’t be looking through contents in a sealed box, but if the shrink wrap shows any signs of tampering and the inner components look mismatched or non-uniform, you may want to treat the item with extra skepticism.

Sealed boxes aren’t just “not opened.” They’re a story about how the hobby values preservation. If you’re a player who craves tactile joy and new-scent cardboard, sealed might feel like a tease. If you’re a collector who fantasizes about a pristine shelf with a “rare” label, sealed is your wheelhouse. Either way, this box exists at the intersection of hobbyism and investment, and that intersection can be entertaining if navigated with a sense of humor and a calculator handy.

### Authenticity, provenance, and avoiding counterfeit thrills

The online marketplace is a carnival of possibilities—and counterfeiters know how to make a box look legitimate from three feet away. Here are a few practical checks to avoid regifting a counterfeit to your future self:

- Compare to known authentic photos: Look for print quality, color saturation, and signature marks that match official product photography.
- Inspect the seal and packaging materials: Counterfeiters often reuse imperfect seals. If you see inconsistent textures or packaging that seems off for a CMON product line, be cautious.
- Use serial or edition markers: Some exclusives carry a production-specific mark. If your box doesn’t have expected identifiers, double-check the source.
- Buy from reputable sources: If possible, rely on pre-vetted retailers or official campaigns. If you’re chasing a bargain, be prepared to pay in psychology and risk.

In the end, sealed authenticity is a dance between your trust in the seller and your personal risk tolerance. For many collectors, the thrill of owning a genuinely limited item outweighs the anxiety of potential fakes. For others, a well-worn copy that’s been played on a table with a napkin shield is a better match for their living room personality.

### Display, storage, and care for sealed boxes

If you invest in a sealed reboot box, you’ll want to treat it like a tiny time capsule. Here are practical storage tips:

- Temperature and humidity: Keep the box in a cool, dry space. Avoid basements with the potential for dampness or attics with constant sun exposure.
- Light exposure: UV light is the silent killer of cardboard. A shelf away from direct sunlight is ideal.
- Handling: Minimize handling. When you do handle it, clean hands help avoid oils and prints that can accumulate over the years.
- Stacking concerns: Don’t stack heavy items on top of the sealed box. The spine can collapse, and that’s the stuff of legend in a bad way.

If you decide to display the box, a protective case or a shelf with a slight tilt so you can appreciate the art without squeezing the box is a good move. A little decanting of the hobby into a display stand can turn a purely transactional object into a conversation piece that makes your friends pause and say, “That’s a lot of plastic.”
