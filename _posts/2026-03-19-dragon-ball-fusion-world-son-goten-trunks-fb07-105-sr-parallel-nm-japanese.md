## Care, storage, and preservation tips

For a card in NM Japanese condition, you want to minimize exposure to humidity, scratches, and sunlight that can fade the holo or blur the image. Here are a few practical steps you can take to preserve the surface and edges:
- Use soft sleeves designed for Dragon Ball cards. Matte or semi gloss sleeves help reduce glare while keeping the surface free from micro-scratches.
- Place the card in a rigid top loader or card holder to prevent warping during storage. If you plan to display, a frame with UV glass can help keep the printing vibrant for longer.
- Maintain a stable temperature and humidity range. Extreme humidity can lead to warping or edge whitening, so try to keep your display cabinet in a climate controlled spot.
- Avoid stacking heavy objects on top of your collection. The age of foil prints can make edges susceptible to bending if pressure is applied over a long period.

If you are building a display worth showing off, consider a small lighted shelf that highlights the SR Parallel shimmer. The science of display light can turn a mid tier card into a showpiece when you catch the holo from a favorable angle.
