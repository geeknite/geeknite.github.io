## How to Play: A Quick Guide
If you are familiar with Codenames, Marvel Codenames Card Game will feel familiar but with a Marvel flavor that adds extra spice. Here is the quick rundown:

- Two teams compete to identify their heroes on the board. Each round features a clue giver from one team and guessers on both sides.
- The clue giver selects a one-word clue followed by a number. Example: Power 3. The number tells the guessers how many hero cards on the grid are related to the clue.
- The guessers discuss and point to the hero cards they believe match the clue. The goal is to identify all the hero cards belonging to their team.
- The board contains team marked cards and a few danger cards. If a guess hits the villain or the assassin, the round ends badly for that team.
- The first team to identify all their hero cards wins the game.

The Marvel twist enters the moment you start pulling in references to cosmic abilities, team-ups, and iconic power moves, and you are expected to translate those mental pictures into guesses on a grid of hero portraits. If this sounds like a mashup between an emoji game and a comic convention, you are not far off.

## Example Round Walkthrough
During a typical round, the clue giver will aim to thread a few heroes that belong to their team through a single clue. Suppose the board features Captain America, Iron Man, and Black Widow on display. The clue giver might say Avengers 3. The players will debate whether this clue applies to those three cards and decide whether to take a risk and guess all three at once. If they succeed, their team sweeps those cards toward a victory in that round. If they guess a non-team hero by accident, the round continues or ends depending on the exact rules in your edition. If they hit the assassin, the round ends immediately and the other team begins their turn with momentum and swagger.

Rounds go by quickly and frequently end with a victor announced in short order. The speed fosters dynamic conversation, and the Marvel references fuel a sense of shared fandom that makes even a misstep feel charming rather than frustrating.

## Strategy and Tips
- Lean into your team knowledge base. If the group has a strong Marvel roster, you can craft connections that feel obvious to some and mystifying to others, which can be part of the fun.
- Start with controlled, clever clues. Rather than throwing out a huge list of potential hero connections, aim for two or three confidently linked characters. This gives your team a chance to guess accurately and quickly.
- Use pop culture shorthand effectively. Clues that reference famous pairings or widely known powers tend to produce the most satisfying rounds.
- Respect the group’s tolerance for Marvel lore. If some players want to keep things light, avoid overly deep lore references that require a glossary to decipher.
- Consider tempo and pacing. The game length can swing quickly, and knowing when to push and when to back off can decide the night.
