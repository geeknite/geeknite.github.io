## How to set up and start playing fast

Setup is intentionally quick. Open the box, shuffle the deck(s), separate Exploding Kitten cards and Defuse cards, and deal a hand to each player. A typical party round uses one Defuse card per player, plus a handful of Defuse options that you can draw during the game. The rest of the deck gets folded into the center, with a small number of explosions placed at the top so that the tension builds early.

Turn order is simple: on your turn you either play a card from your hand or draw from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card to save you, you’re out of the round but not the game. Defuse cards give you one lifeline per round to avoid the explosion and keep playing. The average round lasts around 20 to 40 minutes depending on how many players you have and how aggressively people bluff about their intentions.

Key cards you’ll encounter include See the Future, which lets you peek at the top few cards of the deck to plan your next move; Noop, which can cancel someone else’s action (or your own, if you’re feeling dramatic); Attack, which forces the next player to take extra turns; Shuffle, to mix up the deck; and a variety of Cat cards that form combos and can be used for advantage or comedy depending on your stand up level.


## Quick strategies for big groups

- Read the room: In a large group, you’ll want to measure how much drama your audience can handle. If people love chaos, keep the pace fast with Attack and See the Future to maximize opportunities for misdirection. If some players prefer slower, more deliberate play, give them space to build tension and plan their Defuses.
- Be selective with your Defuses: A Defuse card is not just about surviving one explosion; it’s about keeping the table honest. Save them for moments when you’re about to be out of the round or when the deck is running hot. Some players will attempt to read your tells; practice a poker face and avoid signaling you’re about to deploy a Defuse to the highest bidder.
- Cat card combos can be surprising: Use cat card sets strategically; some combos can force a shift in momentum, while others are simply a hobby for fans who collect puns. Watch for opportunities to misdirect, because the right bluff can save your bacon or ruin someone else’s carefully plotted turn order.
- Manage the pace: If the table erupts into chaos, that is not an accident; it’s a feature. Factor in quick rounds, multiple elimination, and optional speed rules to keep the energy at peak. The beauty of party pack is that you can scale up or down the energy level with a few switches to the cards.


## House rules and variants you can try

If you want to tailor the experience to your crew, there are a few simple house rules that work well with Exploding Kittens Party Pack. You can enforce a cap on the number of Defuse cards any player can hold, or you can allow players to trade a card with the deck at a cost to their turn. For families with younger players, consider a rule where you only include a subset of the more chaotic cards, or ban some of the more aggressive action cards that might derail a table full of kids.

Another popular variant is the team mode, where players are paired up and must coordinate to survive. Team mode creates a different social dynamic, giving shy players a delegate to speak on their behalf and letting collaboration replace cutthroat competition for a few rounds.

If you want to lean into the humor, you can incorporate a ritual of dramatic reveals after each explosion. The dramatic reveal is typically a moment where someone pretends to mourn the explosion while secretly celebrating their cunning move. It’s all part of the theater of the night, and it’s amazing how much a fake eulogy can lighten the mood when the tension rises.


## How to play well with everyone at the table

A successful Exploding Kittens Party Pack session hinges on a few social rituals as much as it does on card play:

- Keep hands visible: The moment you hide a card behind your chair or your elbow, the table starts forming opinions about you. Show your moves, keep it fair, and enjoy the extra suspense as players try to read your tells.
- Celebrate near-misses: When someone narrowly dodges an explosion or survives a clever bluff, celebrate with a little theatrical flair. A little applause goes a long way toward keeping energy high and egos in check.
- Embrace the chaos: The fun of the Party Pack lies in the unpredictable swirl of cards, turns, and reactions. Don’t overthink it; ride the wave. The table will remember the laughs more than the rules you accidentally bent.
