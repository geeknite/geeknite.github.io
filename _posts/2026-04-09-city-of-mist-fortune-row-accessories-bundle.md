## What exactly is in the Fortune Row Accessories Bundle?

The bundle is a curated kit designed to bring Fortune Row to life with physical props, printable assets, and a handful of narrative prompts that are tailored to the vibe of a noir-tinged, mythic city. It is not a replacement for your core rulebooks or your GM kit; rather, it acts as a tangible extension meant to enhance immersion and speed up scene setup. Think of it as a toolbox for mood, not a cheat sheet for solving the mystery.

### Contents mapped to table action
- A fold-out GM insert that serves as a city map overlay, perfect for quick scene setups and noir closeups. It’s not a full battle map for a heavy tactical session, but it shines in social intrigue scenes where you want to remind players where they are without burying them in minis.
- A card deck of character prompts and a handful of NPC quick-hook cards. These are ideal for last-minute sessions or for players who prefer inspiration over a fully planned arc.
- A dozen glamour tokens and a set of event cards that describe dramatic moments, misdirections, or plot twists. Glamour tokens double as permission slips for players to lean into their disguises or reveal a secret with flair.
- A petite GM screen insert that sits inside the main screen and provides session reminders, a quick scale of danger, and a few GM tips that seem to come straight from a noir screenplay.
- A postcard sheet that can be used as scene props or as a faintly silly reminder of the city’s mood between sessions.
- A compact dice set with a color scheme that matches Fortune Row’s aesthetic. The dice aren’t novelty pieces alone—they’re a tactile cue for the rhythm of a scene and a conversation piece when players argue about who gets to roll first.
- A sticker sheet and a small booklet with micro-adventure prompts and tips for weaving the Fate system into Fortune Row style scenes.

## How to integrate Fortune Row accessories into play

In real-world sessions, you don’t want a pile of props that consumes the night. The goal of the Fortune Row bundle is to provide prompts and tactile anchors that reduce decision fatigue and set the mood fast. Here is how you can integrate this bundle into a standard Fortune Row session without derailing your pacing:

- Start with the map overlay: use the GM insert to drop players into a scene location. Let the map cue the mood; then rely on the event cards to seed a clue at the right moment. The beauty is that you can keep the scene fluid while having a fixed anchor for the players to reference.
- Use the character prompts as quick hooks when players struggle to pick a direction. If the party is stuck, pull a prompt card and use it to nudge a line that ties the motive to a Fortune Row location. You will be surprised how often a simple prompt unsticks a stalled scene.
- The glamour tokens serve both as currency of influence and as a visual reminder of a character's disguise or status in a given scene. A player who uses a token to declare a character moment instantly conveys stakes to the rest of the table.
- The deck of dramatic prompts is best saved for mid-session twists. When the tension dips, a prompt card can flip the emotional plane of the scene, pulling players toward a revelation or a betrayal without requiring you to improvise a new beat on the spot.

A quick example one-shot scenario helps illustrate how these elements click together. You can run this with minimal prep: a Fortune Row street gang meeting, a missing sigil, and a city-belief that something in the neon fog is watching. Hook the players with a prop token and an event card that mentions a rumor about a missing courier. Use the card prompts for two or three scenes in a row, then unleash a reveal that forces all the players to confront a common truth about their own identities. The bundle won’t write the session for you, but it gives you a tactile spine to lean on while you improvise the rest.

## Narrative potential and player experience

The user experience of Fortune Row accessories lives in the space between the GM’s descriptive narration and the players’ tactile interaction with the props. The map cloth plus the marker tokens create a dynamic stage where players can point to a location and declare intent with a look and a flourish. The cards and prompts are less about giving you a scripted path and more about offering quick hooks that orient the players toward a shared mystery. It is a good balance for sessions that lean more toward social intrigue than straight dungeon crawling, which aligns with City of Mist core design—mythic identities colliding in a modern city.

Designers often walk a tight line between flavor and utility, and this bundle mostly stays on the flavorful side. The kit doesn’t claim to replace your creative input or your GM’s improvisational skills. It does offer triggers, mood cues, and a few structured prompts that can spark sessions in seconds rather than minutes. If your group prefers long, sprawling campaigns with a heavy focus on character arcs, you will likely find the prompts particularly useful in keeping threads from drifting into static scenes. If you like to improvise from the top of your head, the bundle will still be a helpful tool for quickly injecting a sense of Fortune Row into the room.

In addition, the extra texture helps players stay engaged. The props act as visual cues that anchor their character concepts in the city’s reality rather than in vague memory. When a player points to a map piece and declares that this is where their disguise will be tested, you can weave in a scene that feels earned, not forced. The bundle doesn’t do the heavy lifting of the plot; it gives you a stage to perform on with style and speed.

## Maintenance, care, and portability

The kit is designed for regular tabletop use, not a museum display. Cards shuffles well and the map cloth cleans up with a damp cloth and a gentle wipe. The tokens’ finish holds up to being handed around, but like any physical prop, you’ll want to avoid sharp edges and direct sunlight for long-term color retention. Storage in the provided tray or a shallow box saves the components from getting shuffled into the couch cushions during heroic chair escapes. If you’re traveling to a game night, consider a small padded pouch to protect the fragile cards and the map overlay from squashed morning bag chaos.

## Accessibility and inclusivity considerations

This bundle emphasizes tactile and visual prompts, which can be a boon for players who prefer concrete cues over abstract descriptions. However, if you have players with color vision differences, you’ll want to confirm color contrast on tokens and event cards (which sometimes rely on color as a cue). The printed prompts are legible, but if you’re running a session where visual tempo matters, keep a secondary set of high-contrast tokens or simply describe the cues verbally as well. The lineup of assets can be enjoyed by players who prefer different levels of sensory detail, from the purely narrative to the more tactile, and that is a win for any diverse table.
