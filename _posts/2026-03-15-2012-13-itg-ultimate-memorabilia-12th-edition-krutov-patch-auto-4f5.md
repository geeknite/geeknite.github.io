Patch auto cards are the diadems of sports collecting: a swatch of patch (the actual fabric from a team jersey) powering a signature from the player. ## Grading and Authenticity: How to Think About This Card

The hobby loves numbers, and this card is no exception. A typical high-end relic auto from ITG will flow through grading systems with an emphasis on surface, corners, edges, and centering. The Krutov 4/5 is expected to be in the upper echelons for a card of its print run, but numbers can vary. In our hypothetical scenario where this item is graded on a scale with eight or nine in certain categories, here’s how to think about it:

- Edges: Clean, with a crisp border and no white chipping. Expect a solid 8 or 9.
- Corners: Usually sharp but not perfect; a well-pressed card can hit 9, while a minor corner touch might drop to 8.
- Surface: The photos and foil work often cooperate to create a face that’s bright and reflective; expect a 8 or 9 here depending on printing quirks.
- Centering: ITG’s lines sometimes skew, especially across batches; a near-perfect centering could pull a 9.

Authenticity is a two-way street. The auto is genuine, the patch is nailed to the core of the card, and the slab (if slabbed) should preserve this state. If you’re eyeing this card for a high-visibility display, you’ll want to verify that the serial number (4/5) aligns with the actual print run and that the signatures haven’t been redone. In the world of relics, provenance matters almost as much as a flawless patch. If the card’s back includes a verification certificate or a small hash that ITG sometimes included, you’ll be well on your way to a confident purchase.

## Practical Guides: Care, Storage, and Display

- Display: Keep the card in a high-clarity, UV-resistant display case to preserve the autograph and patch. A (non-glass) display is a great option if you want to show it off but still protect it from environmental hazards.
- Storage: Use a top-load followed by a semi-rigid card holder. If you’re stacking multiple pieces, ensure the stack is in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The key is to avoid humidity and heat, which can impact the autograph ink and the integrity of the patch.
- Handling: Gloves help maintain pristine surface conditions, especially if you regularly flip the card for photography for your blog or socials. If you’re just admiring from a distance, a clean microfiber cloth is enough to keep fingerprints away.
- Grading readiness: If you ever plan to ship this for grading, choose a reputable service with a track record for relic autos. Ensure you understand their submission guidelines, especially around patch visibility and autograph clarity. A well-documented submission with clear photography can help maximize the grade you receive.
