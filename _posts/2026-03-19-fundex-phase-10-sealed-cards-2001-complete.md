## How to play a quick refresher
Phase 10 plays like a streamlined blend between Rummy and a light strategy race. The objective is to complete all ten phases before your rivals do. Each phase describes a specific combination of cards you must collect, in order. Phases might call for things like two sets of three, one run of seven, or a mix of sets and runs. The twist is that you must complete phase 1 before you can start phase 2, and so on, until someone conquers phase 10. The deck includes normal numbered cards and special action cards that influence draws, replacements, or the ability to block others from making a phase.

A standard round proceeds as follows: players draw one card from the deck or the discard pile, then discard one card to the discard pile. On your turn you aim to assemble the current phase, and you can lay down completed phases on your table as you achieve them. The catch is that you must be able to show a completed phase to end your turn and advance your progress. The first person to successfully complete phase 10 wins the round or the match, depending on the rules you adopt.

## Phase by phase: what you are chasing
Ten phases, a neat ladder of complexity. Here is a quick overview to jog your memory and help you map the journey you are about to undertake with this sealed relic.

- Phase 1: Two sets of two cards each
- Phase 2: One set of two and one run of four
- Phase 3: Three sets of two
- Phase 4: One set of four and one run of four
- Phase 5: Five runs of four
- Phase 6: One run of seven
- Phase 7: One run of four and one run of six
- Phase 8: Two runs of five
- Phase 9: One set of three and one set of four
- Phase 10: One set of three, one set of four, and one run of four

The exact wording may vary by edition, but the spirit remains consistent: move from simpler to more complex card configurations as you climb the ladder of glory.
