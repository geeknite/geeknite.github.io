## The humor you can expect

### Handling sensitive topics with a wink

## How to play with the third expansion
### Setup and flow
Playing with the third expansion is the same as playing CAH with any other expansion pack. Shuffle the new black cards into your black deck and mix the white cards for a full round of suggest-and-guess. The real challenge is ensuring the new content gets seats at the table without drowning out the classic jokes you know by heart. As a general guideline, add a set of 15 to 20 new black cards and a corresponding pile of white cards to your existing pile. Depending on your group size, that can stretch a game night into a comedy marathon or a chaotic sprint toward the end where someone starts reading a card aloud with the confidence of a stand-up veteran, only to realize the room is not ready for that level of honesty.

### Group size and dynamics
CAH tends to shine with 4 to 8 players. The third expansion is happy in this range, though it can handle larger groups with the right turnover. If your table includes a new player who is still acclimating to the world of irreverent humor, consider having them as a judge first for a couple of rounds. Giving new players a chance to see the pace and tone can prevent a few awkward moments and ensure your home gaming session remains more party and less courtroom drama.

### Round strategy and card pairing
Strategy with CAH and its expansions is less about optimization and more about reading the room. Some rounds benefit from pairing the most outrageous white card with the most provocative black card, while other rounds require a subtler approach. The third expansion is particularly good at giving players that moment of realization when a deftly chosen pairing lands with a perfect balance of shock and laughter. If you enjoy a blend of strategy and improv, this pack will feel like a playground of witty misdirection.

## Content notes and safeguards
### Sliding into your group’s comfort zone
The third expansion recognizes that not every joke lands in every room. It is designed for players who enjoy exploring boundaries, but it does not insist on stepping into the septic zone of humor. If you run a family-friendly game night, you should skim the prompts ahead of time and curate a safer subset. ### Moderation tips for great sessions

- Save the truly outrageous cards for late rounds when players are warmed up and a little reckless.
