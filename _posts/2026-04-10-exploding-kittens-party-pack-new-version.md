## How to play in a party pack setting (quick refresher)
Exploding Kittens is a hot potato of a game. The goal is to avoid drawing an exploding kitten card while using a combination of cards to shuffle, redirect, or pass the bomb to other players. The Party Pack version sticks to the core tempo but introduces new interactions that can turn a dead straight hand into a fireworks show in under 60 seconds.

- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a hand to each player, and place the rest facedown. The box includes a simple setup guide that is actually helpful, which is a small miracle in this genre.
- Turn structure: On your turn you can play one or more action cards from your hand, and then you draw a card. The strategy lies in timing your defuses, joue your attack cards, and risking a shot at dangerous draws if the table is begging for chaos.
- End condition: The game ends when the exploding kitten is drawn, and the last player standing is crowned the winner. If your friends are ruthless, you can also set a playful wager to heighten the stakes.

In the New Version, the balance leans more toward player agency. You can plan a couple of moves ahead, or just wing it with a defuse in hand and an eye on the budding opportunity to unleash chaos. The rules remain approachable enough for non-gamers to join in the fun while offering enough nuance to entertain seasoned party gamers.

- Defuse cards now vary in flavor. Some defuses let you manipulate the next draw, others grant you temporary protection or party-wide misdirection. It is all about timing and confidence, which is to say the typical cocktail of a successful party game night.
- New cat-themed cards introduce playful mischief, including sneaky peeks at other players’ hands, and a few board-flipping surprises that remind everyone why cats are plotting against us all the time.
