### Assembly and durability

Assembly is usually straightforward: lay out the pieces, attach a few legs, tighten a handful of screws, and you’re done—assuming the instructions aren’t written in a riddle by a very enthusiastic IT intern. We’ve seen sets that go together in under an hour with basic tools, and others that take a hair more time if you insist on checking every angle with a level. The key is to start with the most stable base: frame the table, anchor the sofa, and then attach the chairs. A little patience goes a long way toward eliminating wobble or squeaks that ruin a perfect sunset selfie.

Durability under weather conditions varies by climate. In coastal zones with salty air, you’ll want to rinse occasionally and cover during storms to prevent corrosion. In sunny climates, UV fading is a natural expectation, but higher-quality fabrics will resist color loss longer than cheaper blends. A simple seasonal maintenance routine—wipe down with a mild soapy water solution, dry thoroughly, and store cushions in a dry space when not in use—will extend the life of the set significantly. If you’re in an area with heavy rain and humidity, consider stacking or covering the furniture during the wet season to avoid moisture soaking into cushions or cushions shrinking over time.

## Maintenance tips to extend life

A few practical habits can extend the life of your set by seasons:

- Cushion care: Remove covers when possible and launder per manufacturer instructions. If covers aren’t removable, use a mild fabric cleaner and a soft brush to lift dirt without soaking the fabric. Allow cushions to air-dry completely before replacing them to prevent mildew.
- Cleaning the frame: Wipe the rattan with a damp cloth and mild soap. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can wear away the protective coating. If you live in a salty climate, rinse with fresh water every so often and dry thoroughly.
- Seasonal storage: If you have a garage or covered porch, store cushions in a breathable bag during wet seasons. If storage isn’t feasible, use weatherproof covers to minimize rain exposure.
- Protection from the sun: While UV-resistant fabrics help, prolonged sun exposure can still fade cushions over time. Consider rotating cushions or using shade to preserve color vibrancy.

By following these steps, you’ll keep your 4-piece set looking fresh and comfortable for years, not just seasons. And if your cat decides to audition for a role as “cushion inspector,” you’ll have an easier time convincing them to nap elsewhere when the cushions are stored properly.
