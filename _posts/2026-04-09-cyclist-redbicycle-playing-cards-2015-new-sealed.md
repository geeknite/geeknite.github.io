## Care, Preservation, and Everyday Use
### Everyday handling tips
Handling a sealed 2015 deck: let the deck acclimate to room temperature before any attempt to crack the seal. When you finally open it, rub your fingers along the edges to check for any gloss irregularities or tiny air pockets—these can indicate a slight variance in the production batch. If you plan to perform long sessions, consider re-batching the cards after a few weeks of practice. A well-curated rotation helps to keep the deck’s feel consistent across multiple practice sessions and live performances. 

### Cleaning and maintenance
Avoid harsh chemicals or water on the surface; a dry microfiber cloth is enough to wipe away finger oils after an intense practice. If you’re worried about dirt in your shuffling, you can run a quick air-blast (gentle, not intense) to remove stray dust. For people who use fan displays, make sure the cards aren’t stuck together by humidity or sticky residues. A light, controlled fan or a short shuffle test in a clean environment will reveal whether the finish wants to let go or holds true.

### Storage strategies for longevity
Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV light can over time alter the deck’s color tones, especially on older prints. Consider a display case with UV-protected glass if you want to show off your RedBicycle to friends or fellow collectors without risking the vibrancy of the design. The sealed state is your friend here—treat it as you would a vintage vinyl record: keep it safe, preserve it, and let it breathe only when you’re ready to enjoy it.
