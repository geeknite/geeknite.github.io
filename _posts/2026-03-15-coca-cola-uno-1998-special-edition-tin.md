### Care, storage, and shelf life: Keeping the magic from fading

To preserve this item for decades, consider a simple playbook:

- Store the tin in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to reduce color fade and tin oxidation risk.
- Use silica gel packs to manage humidity and keep the interior cards from taking up moisture as a souvenir of vacation storage.
- Avoid stacking heavy items on top of the tin to prevent lid denting or shoring up the edges with awkward pressure.
- Keep the shrink wrap intact for as long as possible; if you ever must open it, plan a controlled re-seal that respects the historical integrity of the item.
- When you do handle the card deck, consider using soft gloves or a clean surface to avoid extra oils on the cards that can cause glossy finishes to dull over time.

The broader point here is simple: environmental factors are the real villains of sealed collectibles. Temperature spikes, humidity, and light exposure are the trio of nemeses that can quietly erode value. A little foresight with storage can translate to decades of good looks on your display shelf.
