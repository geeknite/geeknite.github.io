## How to shuffle two decks properly
Shuffling 80 cards isn’t a rite of passage, but it does demand a method. Here’s a practical, no-nonsense approach that keeps cards square, players calm, and chaos at bay:

1) Separate and let the decks breathe. A moment of inter-deck mindfulness can save a million mismatched faces.
2) Stack the two decks into one tidy heap, edges aligned. If you’re feeling fancy, you can attempt a bridge-like overhand shuffle to bring the decks closer and reduce misfires.
3) Give the pile a solid shuffle. A few riffles, followed by a few controlled runs through your hand, will do. Resist the urge to do a two-second “expert” shuffle and pretend you’ve earned a PhD in probability. You need a measure of chaos to shake out any stubborn order.
4) Cut the deck. Have a volunteer cut, or perform a quick two-finger cut. It signals a rules refresh and clears space for new habits.
5) Optional but recommended: a quick double cut by the dealer to ensure fairness for everyone. If you’re playing with friends who treat rock-paper-scissors like a full-contact sport, this step becomes essential.
6) Deal with confidence. An 80-card deck increases the chances of opening-hand duplicates. If that bothers you, either accept duplicates as a rule or pre-remove one copy of each pair before dealing. It’s your call; chaos or control, you pick.

Two-deck shuffles can startle the uninitiated. The first time you deal 80 cards, you’ll feel like a magician pulling a rabbit from a towel. With practice, you’ll be dealing hands faster than a vending machine dispenses a snack.
