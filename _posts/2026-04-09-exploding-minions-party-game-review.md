If you thought a banana-scented chaos engine could not be scaled to a family game night, think again. Setup is designed to be breezy and breezier for bigger groups. Shuffle the minion deck and deal each player a starting hand calibrated to the number of participants. ### Turn structure
On a turn, a player typically draws a card, claims a cheeky action, or risks a move that could trigger the explosive finale. ## Strategy and tips for success
### Know when to push your luck
The core decision is risk management. Read the table, sense when a big boom might be looming, and strike with a calculated risk that shifts momentum without setting you up for a spectacular fail. The joy comes from precise timing and the art of restraint rather than reckless gambits that blow up in your face.

### Manage your hand with flair
Hold a few flexible cards to dodge exploding threats. A well-timed defuse-style card or a saving card can provide a near-miss that leaves opponents sweating and you grinning. If you can weave a feint that becomes a real counter-move later in the round, you’ll own the narrative of the game.

### Watch the banana track
Banana tokens aren’t just decor—they’re a live timer. When the track creeps forward, players start to plan around who will be left with a risky choice on the next turn. Use that rhythm to calibrate your decisions: protect yourself when the risk is high, and take opportunistic swings when rivals’ attention isn’t on you.

### House rules to amplify laughs
If your gang enjoys an extra punch, try a light house rule: allow a one-time redraw after a big misplay, but impose a small penalty on the trigger that created it. This adds a little extra back-and-forth, keeps tension high, and preserves the core risk-reward loop without plunging into frustration.
