### How Phase 10 Plays Out

- The deck is a mixture of colored cards that come in sequences (runs) and same-number cards (sets). Your objective is to complete ten distinct phases across the game, with each phase requiring a specific combination of runs and sets. For instance, Phase 1 might be two sets of two, Phase 2 could be a two runs of four cards, Phase 3 might require a set of five, and so on.
- On your turn, you draw a card and discard one, trying to assemble the exact configurations required for your current phase. The catch: you can only advance to the next phase once you’ve completed the current one. That’s where the real head-banging fun begins—watching your carefully laid plan crumble as someone drops a perfectly-timed Wild Card that wrecks your five-of-a-kind dream.
- The game ends when a player completes Phase 10 and has the lowest total score (or the first to meet a determined win condition, depending on how you play). Scorekeeping can be as simple or as elaborate as you like, which is where the Deluxe edition’s score pad shines.

The Deluxe Tin does not change these mechanics; it simply gives you a nicer container for those classic moves. It’s the same game you’ve known for years, but with a vibe that says you took the time to curate your gaming life with a little more panache.
