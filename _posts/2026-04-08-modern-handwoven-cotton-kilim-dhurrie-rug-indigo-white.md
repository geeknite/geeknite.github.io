## Overview: what you get when you buy a handwoven Kilim Dhurrie

Kilim and Dhurrie are terms that get tossed around a lot in rug talk. In practice, a Kilim is a flat weave with no knotted pile, created by weaving wefts and warps in a way that hides the edges and leaves a crisp, two-dimensional surface. A Dhurrie is a similar flat weave rug from India, typically thinner and lighter than a traditional pile rug, designed for easy rolling and sliding under furniture. The Indigo White version blends these ideas into a modern aesthetic with a geometric pattern that reads as both ancient tapestry and sleek urban design. The cotton construction means it is breathable, washable by hand if needed, and more forgiving than wool in high-traffic zones. 

This rug comes in a handful of standard sizes, with the most common options being 3x5, 4x6, and 5x8 feet. The exact size you see online will determine how much of the room you want to define, and the Indigo White palette keeps things versatile enough to layer with a bold wall color or a neutral backdrop.

## Materials and craftsmanship: cotton that holds up to everyday life

Cotton as a base fiber brings a few practical perks to this rug. Indigo is a classic dye that tends to hold up well in sunlight, giving the rug a forgiving fade profile if you have a sunlit corner. The dye process on handwoven cotton can vary slightly from batch to batch, which in the world of handmade textiles is not a bug but a feature — it adds a character texture and subtle variations that remind you that someone actually wove this by hand, not a machine in a distant factory.

From a sustainability angle, cotton rugs like this one are often produced with simpler dye recipes and fewer chemical treatments than some synthetic alternatives. ## Care and maintenance: keeping the Indigo White crisp

Caring for a flat weave cotton rug is surprisingly straightforward. Here are practical habits that keep the Indigo White rug looking sharp without feeling like you are micromanaging a messy pet:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting. Use a brush attachment to avoid pulling fibers, especially along the edges.
- For small spills, blot immediately with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the weave and create a spot that refuses to disappear.
- If a stain sticks around, a mild solution of water and a gentle pH-balanced detergent can do wonders. Test a small inconspicuous corner first to confirm colorfastness.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if your seating area sits on a sunny corner. The indigo hue will pale a bit faster in strong sun, so rotation helps keep overall color balance.
- Clean up pet messes promptly. Cotton dries relatively quickly, but moisture can lead to odor if left unchecked. A light wash with air drying is fine for older stains, but if in doubt, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in flat weaves.

A practical tip for busy geek dens: a simple, low-profile rug pad can dramatically extend the life of a flat weave rug by reducing friction with the floor and helping it stay flat against the surface. It also makes sliding chairs and rolling game stools less of a stumble than skating on an ice rink.
