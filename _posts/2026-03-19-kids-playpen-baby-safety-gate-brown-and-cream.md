## Setup and Installation: The Quick Start Guide You Wish You Had Last Weekend

If you’re the kind of person who reads the manual cover-to-cover before assembling a kitchen chair, you’ll be pleased to know this one isn’t a library of rage. Setup is straightforward and friendly to first-timers. Here’s a simplified flow:

- Measure your doorway or space with the included tape measure to understand how many extension panels you’ll need.
- Unbox, lay out the panels, and identify the top rails from the bottom rails—yes, there is a small orientation dance before you click everything into place.
- Fit the end panels into the corresponding wall mounts or doorway walls. The mounts have simple screw or pressure-fit options depending on your walls, and you won’t need a PhD in engineering to figure them out.
- Slide in the connecting panels and secure the latch mechanism. It’ll click into place with a firm, reassuring sound.
- Test the gate with a light push to ensure it’s stable and that the latch responds as expected. If you hear anything suspicious, re-check the connectors. The first setup should usually take about 15–25 minutes for most spaces.

A few pro tips: keep a small square of velcro on hand to keep extension panels from sliding when you clean the floor; wipe down the rails with a damp cloth after messy meals to prevent sticky residue from building up; consider placing the gate in a central area rather than tucked away in a corner to maximize visibility and accessibility.
