## Materials, Construction, and Durability
### The Fibers Behind the Magic
Most outdoor coastal rugs live or die by their fiber. The Ahoy rug is typically polypropylene with a weather-resistant backing (latex-backed is common in this family). Polypropylene is the unsung hero of outdoor textiles: lightweight, stain-resistant, and generally quick to air out after a rogue spill of sunscreen or a celebratory splash from the pool (we’ve all been there). The backing helps keep moisture from seeping through to your patio surface, which is nice when you’re grilling and don’t want the deck to feel like a wading pool.

### UV Resistance and Color Longevity
Color-fastness is the name of the game. UV exposure is relentless on outdoor fabrics, and this rug’s colorway is chosen to resist fading long enough for you to forget what the sun looked like in the 90s. While no outdoor textile is invincible, the Ahoy rug aims to hold its blues and ivories under typical sun exposure, rain, and the occasional salt spray from a thirsty seagull. If you’re a sun worshipper who leaves a rug out all year, consider rotating textiles or storing the rug during peak-wind seasons to extend its life.

### Pile Height, Comfort, and Foot Feel
This rug sits in the low-pile family—think less shag, more mat for feet. It’s designed for easy sweeping and quick drying after a storm. If you’re planning a barefoot Netflix binge on a rainy weekend, this rug should feel pleasantly flat underfoot, with enough cushion to prevent your coffee from tipping over when you dramatically kick off your sandals. For a dash of extra comfort, a quality rug pad underneath is recommended. Not because the rug needs a pillow fortress, but because a pad enhances grip, reduces curling edges, and gives you a little extra bounce when you walk in like you own the world.

## Outdoor Performance: Weather, Pet, and Party Tests
### Weather Resistance: Sun, Rain, and Windy Public Speeches
Outdoor rugs are supposed to shrug off the weather, and the Ahoy aims to do just that. In sunny climes, you won’t see color washout within a season or two. In rainy climates, the rug dries relatively quickly thanks to its low pile and moisture-friendly fibers. If you’re near the coast or in a place with morning dew, the rug’s quick-dry attributes are worth noting: you’ll be able to hose it down, rinse away the day’s sticky incidents, and be back to porch-optimism in a shorter window than you’d expect.

### Pet Life and Party Mode
Pets love outdoor spaces, and the rug must tolerate paw prints, muddy paws, and the occasional “designated snack zone” incident. Polypropylene is relatively stain-resistant, which means a lot of messes can be wiped away with a damp cloth or a mild detergent solution. For tea spills or red wine (we’ve all been there), treat the rug promptly to avoid staining—the quicker you attend to it, the less you have to blame the rug for your social life’s misadventures.

### Cleaning and Maintenance Tips
- Regular vacuuming helps prevent micro-sand buildup from your beachy lifestyle.
- For spills, blot with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent solution if needed. Rinse and air dry; avoid harsh chemicals that could dull the color.
- Periodically rotate or flip the rug to promote even wear and discourage creasing along walkways.
- Store indoors during off-seasons or when heavy snow is expected; long-term exposure to snow and ice can be tough on any outdoor textile.
