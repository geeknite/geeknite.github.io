## What is the Special Guest Box?

The Special Guest Box is a curated bundle that CMON releases to celebrate a guest artist or a thematic pivot within a game line. In this edition, Adrian Smith—famed for sprawling fantasy canvases that look like they borrowed a spell from a nightmare and filed it under “Master Class”—brings his aesthetic to the Black Plague universe. The box includes new heroes, special objectives, alternative zombie sculpts, and beautifully grim art that makes you want to repaint your entire shelf in moody chiaroscuro.

### The box contents (spoiler-light version)
- A set of new hero miniatures inspired by Adrian Smith’s signature characters and design language.
- New survivor cards with stat blocks that lean into classically grim medieval vibes rather than sparkly post-apocalyptic heroics.
- Themed boards or tiles that alter map aesthetics and provide fresh ways to lay out encounters.
- A roster of unique objective cards that tilt the odds toward high-stakes, cinematic outcomes rather than grindy board-to-board repetition.
- A few special tokens and markers with arcane runes that glow under the right lighting (or under your editor’s favorite lamp—no guarantees on actual glow-time from your LED strip).

If you're a painter or plan to cross-pollinate your minis across games, the content is a playground. If you’re a purely board-game purist who just wants to smash zombies in a dungeon, you’ll still get a lot out of the visual kick in the teeth this box provides.

## Adrian Smith: The Art The Box Brings to Your Table

Adrian Smith isn’t just an artist; he’s a mood, a vibe, and sometimes a diplomatic treaty with your paintbrushes. His work is chunky with atmosphere, heavy on shadow, and famous for making armor look like it could bite your eyes off. In this Special Guest Box, his style threads through every new element—hero cards, mini hero sculpts, and the overall art direction of the packaging. The result is less “zombie horde in a dungeon” and more “epic doom patrol stepping out of a smoky cathedral.”

The synergy between Smith’s linework and ZBP’s grimdark theme means your table doesn’t just play the game; it looks like it would deserve a gallery label. This is the kind of collaboration that makes you want to repaint your entire shelf to match the box’s color palette, then realize you’ve painted yourself into a corner and have to invite more zombies to solve the color debate.

## Gaming Experience: How the Special Guest Box Changes the Table

Zombicide Black Plague is already a crowd-pleasing co-op: up to six players, brutal undead onslaughts, dice-fueled mayhem, and enough chance to turn your well-laid plans into a dramatic pile of hero casualties. The Special Guest Box adds new hero dynamics and thematic tokens that can shift the pacing and strategy, but it doesn’t throw away the proven core of the game—the need to think tactically while everyone screams about their dice results like a medieval tavern on St. Patrick’s Day.

### Setup and components
The components feel premium. The miniatures aren’t “table army” level, but they’re solid: good base detail, clean sculpting, and enough heft to avoid the usual delicate-bits disaster after the first night of “someone’s drink spilled and the box glassy-glass.” The hero boards are well-illustrated, and the new tokens are both readable and visually distinctive even from the far end of the table. The new tiles, if included, change line-of-sight geometry in fun ways, making corridors and corners feel more strategic rather than a simple “go left or go right” choice.

If you’re a painter, you’ll be delighted. The Adrian Smith influence practically demands a slightly longer painting session than you planned—shadows, bone, metallic highlights, and a few punchy color contrasts that pop on the tabletop. If you’re someone who paints for the camera, you’ll be delighted by the dramatic lighting on the box art and the tokens—the sort of thing that begs you to post a shot with a neon-underlighting rig you’ve been saving for exactly this occasion.

### Balance and difficulty
The expansion isn’t trying to reinvent the zombie wheel; it’s adjusting the balance curve by giving players access to more tactical options through new abilities and scenarios. In practice, this means a single mistake—mismanaging a group of zombies or misallocating your best hero—can snowball into a dramatic failure, which is exactly what Zombicide fans crave: a tense moment where your table collectively mutters an expletive under their breath and then high-fives because someone rolled a clutch critical.

The new components are designed to mesh with the existing cards and boss monsters rather than creating a separate “hard mode.” If your group thrives on campaign-style play, the Special Guest Box can be threaded into a longer arc, letting Adrian Smith’s world-building arc drive your sessions as a narrative frame. If your group prefers quick, one-shot sessions, the box still offers enough flavor to spice up a single night of chaotic skirmishes.

## How It Compares to Standard Zombicide: A Quick Reality Check

The core game remains the same: survive against a growing tide of undead with a blend of cooperative strategy and occasional chaos. The Special Guest Box adds a layer of flavor and a handful of fresh mechanics that synergize with existing rules rather than complicate them. If you own the base Black Plague and are considering an upgrade, the decision comes down to: do you want more thematic depth and painterly inspiration, or do you want a leaner, cheaper route to more zombie-slaying action?

Compared to the standard box, you’ll probably notice:
- Higher visual impact on the table, especially under good lighting.
- More narrative potential in campaign play thanks to new objectives and character hooks.
- A few new strategic options that can change how you approach crowd control and objective capture.

If your gaming group prioritizes storytelling and aesthetics, the Special Guest Box is a strong win. If you just need more zombies for your weekend, you’ll still get plenty of undead hooliganism—just less of the cinematic flourish.
