### What is Codenames Card Game
Codenames Card Game is the card game manifestation of the classic word association phenomenon. Instead of a grid of 25 word cards on a table, you get a deck of word cards that are laid out and a secret key that tells you which words belong to which team. The premise remains simple: one clue, one number; your team tries to guess as many of its cards as possible without triggering the other team or the dreaded assassin.

## Setup and Play Flow
Setting up is part of the fun, not a chore. You shuffle the word cards, lay out a 5x5 grid, and flip the secret key to see which cards belong to which team, which are neutral, and which one is the assassin.

### Basic Rules at a Glance
- A clue is one word plus a number
- The number tells you how many word cards your clue relates to
- Players guess cards one at a time
- First team to find all its agents wins; a hit on the assassin ends the round

### Clue Crafting and Guessing
The brain-twisting part happens when you craft a clue that acts like a magnet for several cards. A good clue might connect two or three cards that share a theme; a perfect clue could link four or more, risking it all for glory. The interplay between bold risk-taking and cautious play is the source of the game s magic.

## Word Game Mechanics and Strategy
Codenames has a simple skeleton and a ridiculous-if-hilarious flesh.

### The Core of Codenames
Words are keys; a clue is a map; guessing is a dance that can end in triumph or disaster. The sealed edition preserves this dynamic, letting players focus on wording, interpretation, and shared jokes rather than worrying about the box condition.

### Building Effective Clues
- Start small and safe; test the waters with a two- or three-word clue
- Seek common threads that link multiple words
- Avoid cross-cultural traps; your clue may fly over the heads of some players

### Risk and Reward
Every clue carries risk; more words in the clue means more possibility of hitting opponent cards or the assassin. The simplest way to keep safety high is to use precise clues with clear ties, but the most entertaining approach is the improbable clue that somehow works.
