## Care Guide and Longevity Tips

### Routine care

- Vacuum regularly to remove dust and grit that can wear down the top fibers. A quiet vacuum with a brush attachment is best.
- Wipe away spills promptly with a damp cloth or sponge. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can degrade the surface.
- If machine washable, use cold water and a gentle cycle; air-dry completely before use to prevent mold.
- Flip and rotate occasionally to even out wear across the rug.

### Deep cleaning ideas

For stubborn stains, a mixture of mild detergent and water, applied with a soft cloth, can lift the stain gradually. Avoid bleach unless the manufacturer explicitly states that bleaching is safe for the rug. In most cases, you will find that a hydrogen peroxide-based stain remover in small amounts can help with set-in stains, but test in an inconspicuous area first.

### Storage and rotation

If you are rotating your rugs with seasons, roll the rug rather than folding it; folds can create creases that never vanish. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent fading.

## FAQs and Myths

### Is it machine washable?

Some variants of this rug are machine washable; others are spot-clean-only. Always check the care label. If it is machine washable, use a gentle cycle with cold water and lay flat to dry. High-heat drying can warp the backing or fade the fibers over time.

### Will it work on laminate and tile floors?

Yes. The TPR backing is designed to grip smooth, hard surfaces like laminate and tile. It reduces movement and helps keep you upright when your momentum is unfortunately a little too motivated in the morning.

### How long does the grip last?

Grip longevity depends on foot traffic, cleaning frequency, and floor texture. In most households, you can expect consistent grip for several months to a year with regular care. If you notice the rug creeping away from the sink after a spilled coffee episode, you may need to re-check the backing and potentially replace the rug sooner rather than later.

### Can I use it outdoors?

This rug is designed for indoor kitchen use. Exposure to direct sunlight and outdoor elements can cause fading and faster wear. If you need protection for a balcony kitchen or covered patio, look for an outdoor-rated runner with a different backing material.
