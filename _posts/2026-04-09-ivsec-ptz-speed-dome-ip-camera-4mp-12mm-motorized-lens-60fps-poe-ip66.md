## Installation and Setup: A Tiny Saga

Setting up the Ivsec PTZ Speed Dome is a familiar journey for anyone who has wrestled with a new security device after a long day of work. Expect some learning curve, but not a painful one. Here’s a quick walkthrough:

1) Mounting: Choose your location and ensure the surface is sturdy. The unit’s weight plus wind load means you’ll want a solid anchor. We recommend following the included mounting template and using appropriate hardware for your wall or ceiling material.
2) Network wiring: Run a PoE cable from your PoE switch or injector to the camera. If your network closet is upstairs and your device is on the garage ceiling, plan for a clean path and weatherproof seals at the point where the cable enters the camera housing.
3) Power: With PoE, you don’t need a separate power supply. If you’re not using PoE, ensure your power source matches the camera’s specifications to avoid brownouts.
4) Configuring: Access the camera’s web UI via its IP address, or use the vendor app. You’ll typically configure: motion detection, privacy zones, brightness/contrast, and recording schedules. If you’re a hoarder of video evidence, consider enabling on-device storage or a centralized NVR for archiving.
5) Fine-tuning: Set up the PTZ speed, acceleration, and limits. The last thing you want is a camera that crashes into a wall because you forgot to set its limits.

A note on integration: Ivsec cameras often play nicely with standard ONVIF-compatible NVRs and software.
