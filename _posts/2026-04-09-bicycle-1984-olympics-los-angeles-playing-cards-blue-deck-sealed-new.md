## Care, storage, and preservation
Preservation starts with good storage: cool, dry, and away from direct sunlight. The goal is to minimize humidity cycles that can warp edges or encourage mold growth on the tuck box. If you live in a humid climate, consider a display cabinet with a UV-filtered light. For long-term storage, a humidity-controlled environment is ideal. A shallow drawer with silica gel packs can help regulate moisture without introducing direct contact with the cards.

If you ever decide to open the deck for play or display, do so gently. Break the seal with patience, avoid forcing the cards out, and let them acclimate to room conditions before you handle them extensively. A little patience here goes a long way toward preserving the stock integrity for decades to come.
