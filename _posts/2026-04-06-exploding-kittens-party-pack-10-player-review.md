## How to Play: Core Mechanics and Card Roles
Exploding Kittens in Party Pack mode keeps the core rules intact with the famous high-stakes twist: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten unless you have a Defuse. The Party Pack’s extra cards mostly adjust the tempo and add a few dank, party-focused interactions. Here are the main toy-box pieces you’ll be playing with:

- Exploding Kitten cards: draw these and you’re one misstep away from elimination unless you have a Defuse.
- Defuse cards: your safety net. You can defuse an Exploding Kitten by using a Defuse, turning a near-miss into a shrug and a witty quip.
- Attack cards: force the next player to take extra turns. Great for swinging momentum or launching a dramatic late-game comeback.
- See the Future cards: peek at the top few cards of the draw pile to plan whether you want to risk stealing a Cat’s cradle of destiny.
- Skip cards: skip your turn when you realize you’ve drawn your luck’s last straw. In a large party, you’ll be glad you have these to stall for time while you plot your next clever line.
- Nope cards: counter opponents’ actions. The party doesn’t truly begin until you’ve landed a well-timed Nope that shrouds your fate in mystery.
- Cat cards and added Party Pack-specific twists: these modifiers and cat-themed cards add chaos without requiring you to memorize a million edge-case rules, which is exactly what you want on a loud game night.

The general flow remains simple: draw a card, decide whether you will play any number of action cards (which may alter the draw pile, force someone to reshuffle, or launch a chain of comedic sabotage), and survive long enough to avoid the overlap of strategy and luck that makes party games both enraging and beloved.
