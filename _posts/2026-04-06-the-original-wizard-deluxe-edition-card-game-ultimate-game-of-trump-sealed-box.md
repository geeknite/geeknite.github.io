## How to Play: Setup and Turn Structure
The core of The Original WIZARD Deluxe Edition Card Game is a quick setup and a turn structure that supports chaotic, high-energy play. The rules are designed to be accessible to casual players but with enough nuance to reward clever play. Here’s a digest that should get you from box to chaos in under 10 minutes, provided your group isn’t allergic to laughs at the expense of solemnity.

### Setup
- Each player receives a hand of Trump cards from the deck. The exact number depends on player count, but you’ll typically be looking at anywhere from five to fourteen cards in total circulation.
- A central board or a minimal set of stand-in tracks will indicate the current trump status and the victory conditions. The specialized tokens these games often include allow you to track the “influence” you accumulate as you win rounds or complete political faux pas with style.
- The remaining deck is shuffled and placed face down as the draw pile. A single card is flipped to start the round—the tone is set, and the room begins to anticipate who will say the sentence that sends everything spiraling into hilarity or chaos.

### Turn Flow
A typical turn involves playing a Trump card to outmaneuver a rival, followed by a reaction or a counterplay. The cool part is that you don’t need to rely on real-world political knowledge to play well; the satire and the interaction do the heavy lifting. If you’ve played trick-taking games before, you’ll recognize some rhythm here; if you’re new, you’ll quickly learn to read the room and adapt your strategy to the group’s energy.

Turn structure may resemble: draw, play a trump, pivot to a mini-debate or joke, counter with a reaction card, and tally the influence if you win the engagement. The judge of the round is usually a rotating role or a consensus rule that assigns points to the winner of the exchange. It’s not a deep strategic masterpiece, but it doesn’t pretend to be; this is a party game that thrives on energy, timing, and a healthy dose of good-natured ribbing.

### The Trump Mechanic in Practice
The Trump mechanic works as a higher-card-wins system across a set of categories or conversation prompts. In practical terms: you play a card that says I trump your claim with a stronger argument or a humorous twist, and if your opponent cannot counter effectively, you collect tokens. These tokens drive the score toward the victory condition—usually something like first to a fixed number of points or most points after a predetermined number of rounds.

Be mindful that the game thrives when players lean into the humor and improv. If you treat every card as a serious moral test, you’ll miss the point and potentially derail the fun. The beauty of this setup is that a silly pun can beat a well-constructed argument if delivered with perfect timing and a well-timed pause for laughter.
