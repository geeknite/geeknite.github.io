## How to Play: Quick Start and Platinum Tweaks
If you already know UNO, you know the drill: match color or number, shout UNO when you’re down to one card, expect a few hands to end in a dramatic, improvised performance of “how did that happen?” In Platinum Edition, a handful of small changes shape how you play, without requiring a separate rulebook cram session.

### Quick Start
- Shuffle the Platinum deck and deal seven cards to each player. The rest of the deck becomes the draw pile.
- The top card of the draw pile flips to start the discard pile. If it’s a wild or action card, follow the normal UNO action, but the Platinum edition keeps things clean and easy to follow because the card artwork is a touch easier to read at a glance.
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either color or number. If you can’t, you draw one card from the draw pile. If that card is playable, you may immediately play it; otherwise your turn ends.
- Special cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, Wild Draw Four) function as usual, but the Platinum edition emphasizes clear readability and a more tactile feel when laying cards down. You’ll notice a solid click when you place a card, which is oddly satisfying and gives your brain a tiny dopamine hit.

### Platinum House Rules (Optional)
- Color Lock Rule: If a color runs out in the draw pile, the next player must declare a color pair (e.g., “blue and green”) for the next turn, creating a playful memory game that speeds up or slows down the pace depending on how raucous the table wants to be.
- Platinum Draw: Once per game, a player may draw two cards instead of one at the start of their turn, but if they do, they must skip their next turn. It’s a mild chaos-accelerator that some tables adopt for a closer finish.
- Turbo UNO: If a player goes out with exactly one card, the next player may counter with a color-change card that requires all players to discard and redraw under certain conditions. It’s optional and largely used in friend groups that enjoy a little extra adrenaline.

These house rules aren’t mandatory, but they provide a gentle ladder between a laid-back game night and a full-on competition night. The point of Platinum is to create a smoother, more premium playing experience while not breaking the core DNA of UNO.
