## Custom size vs 8x10: what to pick

The main selling points of Brink Panther Taupe Sisal are the flexibility of custom sizing and the reliable 8x10 option. Here is a practical breakdown of when to go custom and when to keep it classic:

### Custom size pros
- Perfect fit for unusual rooms: you have a nonstandard living area or an oddly shaped space that begs for geometry rather than a standard rectangle.

## Maintenance and care: how to keep it sane

Natural fiber rugs like sisal require a simple, practical care routine. Here are tips to keep the Brink Panther Taupe looking sharp for years:

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting. Avoid beater bars that could catch and pull fibers. If you own a vacuum with a rotating brush, switch to a low setting or use a handheld attachment.
- Spot cleaning: blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Use a mild soap solution if needed, then blot again to remove moisture. Do not soak the rug; natural fibers dislike soggy afternoons.
- Avoid excess moisture: moisture can cause the fibers to swell or warp. If you spill something sticky, a light dab with water and a little mild detergent is usually enough.
- Rotate occasionally: rotating the rug every few months helps distribute wear evenly across the surface, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Protect from direct sun: prolonged exposure to strong sunlight can cause fading over time. If possible, use curtains or blinds during peak sun hours or rotate sun-exposed furniture so the rug wears evenly.

A note on spills and pets: while sisal fibers are resilient, pet accidents and long-term moisture can be problematic. If you have a particularly enthusiastic chewing dog or a cat that believes the rug is a scratching post, consider an additional protective layer or an alternative fiber for rooms with heavy pet activity.

## Measuring, ordering, and arranging for custom work

If you decide to go custom, here is a clean path to success:

- Get exact room measurements: measure length and width where the rug will sit, including any alcoves or door swing areas. Note any obstructions like vents or floor outlets that might affect placement.
- Decide on the edge: do you want a clean, straight edge or a slight border? Custom options may allow for subtle border detailing that frames the space nicely.
- Specify padding and underlay: if you plan to place the rug in a high-traffic zone, ask about a tailored rug pad to maximize comfort and longevity.
- Prepare for lead times: custom orders can take longer than standard sizes. Factor this into your decorating timeline and plan accordingly.
- Color expectations: natural fiber rugs vary slightly by batch. If you are matching with other decor, consider ordering a sample or two swatches, then plan for a tiny variation in color between boards.
