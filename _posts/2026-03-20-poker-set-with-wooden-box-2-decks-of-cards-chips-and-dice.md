## Maintenance: keeping the cedar-scented dream intact

Wooden boxes and card sets deserve a little spa day now and then. Here are a few practical tips to keep your set looking sharp and feeling premium:

- Keep it dry: water and wood are not best friends. If you spill a drink, wipe it up quickly and dry the interior to prevent warping.
- Gentle cleaning: a soft microfiber cloth can handle dust, smudges, and the occasional sticky finger. Avoid harsh chemicals; you don’t need to turn the finish into a science experiment.
- Card care: store decks in the box to protect them from bending and curling. If you’re a stickler for long-term quality, consider occasional shuffles to prevent the faces from wearing unevenly.
- Chip maintenance: keep chips in a neat stack. If you notice chipping or flaking, a light buff with a soft cloth can help maintain their luster. If chips do start to show wear, plan a friendly “chip tune-up” night with a DIY card protector and a few extra plastic chips as replacements.

### Storage solutions: portability vs. home-benchhog reality
The beauty of the wooden box is portability, but with that comes the need for safe transport. If you take this box on the road to a friend’s apartment, consider a padded bag or a small case to prevent jostling. A nimble setup can let you move from casual to competitive in seconds, which is essential in the modern era of mixed-up game nights where someone brings a drone and claims it’s a “tabletop stage.”
