## Foil Quality: Glitter or Glimmer?

Foil lands in Kaldheim are notorious for their crisp edges and the way the foil lines catch the light. Alpine Meadow foil is no exception: it tends to have a robust reflection, a crisp border, and a texture that whispers “don’t bend me, I’m expensive.” Some collectors report subtle scratches or slight edge wear on early foils from this run, but that’s mostly the risk you sign up for when you chase glossy, collectible cardboard that costs more than a casual lunch. If you’re trying to build a pristine set, expect to weigh the cost of perfection against the thrill of opening a fresh pack with a foil-aligned grin.

### Handling, Storage, and Care
Foil cards—lands especially—can be more prone to micro-scratches and fingerprint scuffs if you treat them like regular forest greens. Here are quick tips:
- Use high-quality sleeves designed for foils to reduce contact friction.
- Store in a climate-controlled, low-humidity environment to preserve gloss and prevent warping.
- Avoid excessive bending; foils aren’t fans of trapezoid storage strategies.
- If you’re a display fiend, consider a dual-sleeve approach (inner sleeve for protection, outer top-load for display) to keep the snow-shell pristine.
