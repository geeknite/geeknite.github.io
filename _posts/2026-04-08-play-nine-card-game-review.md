## How to play: the gist you need to know
### The objective
In Play Nine, your aim is to minimize your score across the course of nine holes. Each hole has its own hidden cards that you either reveal or swap in the hope of lowering your total. The tension comes from having to weigh the risk of discarding a card that your opponents might need against the benefit of seeing your own numbers drop like a stock market chart on a roller coaster.

### Core mechanics
- On your turn, you draw a card and choose to swap it with a card in your tableau or discard it. The core loop is quick and simple, designed for kids and adults alike.
- Occasionally you will encounter action cards that allow you to peek at others hands, swap holes, or apply a light penalty to your enemies. The flavor is light hearted and the effects are mild enough to keep the table friendly.
- After a set number of rounds or whenever a hole reaches a certain threshold, you tally up the points. The player with the lowest score wins, because in golf style card games, the lowest score is basically a high five to the universe.

### A sample turn explained (in plain language)
1) Draw a card from the draw pile.
2) Look at your current hole cards and decide whether to swap one card with the drawn card or discard the drawn card.
3) If you decide to swap, you replace one of your hole cards with the drawn card, keeping the rest face down. If not, you discard and the next player takes their turn.
4) After nine holes, tally the scores and declare the winner.

## Strategy tips for 2-6 players
### General guidance
- Pay attention to the visible cards in your own tableau and the discards. Memory is underrated in kid friendly games and parents will thank you for keeping a running tally of who took what when.
- Don’t be afraid to risk a swap if it improves your potential score by a few points. The key is not overvaluing one good draw; you want a balanced, flexible setup by hole, not a single heroic hand that fails to deliver on the other holes.
- The action cards are your leverage. Use them to disrupt opponents subtly. You do not need to outsmart them with complex math; you just need to outlast their excitement with a well-timed swap or peek.

### Strategy by numbers
- Hole 1 to 3: early game emphasis on reducing risk. Collect lower numbers and avoid those pesky midrange cards that tend to turn up again and again.
- Hole 4 to 6: midgame balancing act. Use action cards to peek at a neighbor’s cards or force a swap that improves your own odds while introducing uncertainty at the table.
- Hole 7 to 9: final sprint. You want to lock in low numbers but remain flexible. If you are behind, a bold move with an action card might flip the table chatter in your favor.

### Variant: family mode and learning pace
If you are playing with younger players or first timers, you can simplify the rules by reducing the number of holes to six and allowing extra grace in the scoring. This helps with pace and reduces table talk burnouts. For players who want a deeper dive into strategy, keep the nine holes but introduce a rule where you can trade a whole hole with a fixed number of cards. It slows things down just enough to discuss strategy without turning the table into a board meeting.
