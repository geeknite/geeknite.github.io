### Scent profile: what’s inside the amber cauldron?
Ambra EdP is built around warm amber as the anchor, but it isn’t a one-note candle. It’s a balanced composition with citrus brightness on top, a resinous heart, and a soft vanilla-musk finish that lingers like a friendly ghost in a velvet suit. Think of it as a scientific diagram of comfort: a little citrus energy to wake up the neurons, an amber core to warm the soul, and dry-down notes that feel like velvet curtains on a theater stage.

- Top notes: bergamot, orange zest, a whisper of pink pepper. The citrus kick is bright and clean, almost like lifting the blinds to a sunny Italian morning. It’s not aggressively zesty; it’s more that you’ve just opened a premium window from which the amber can glow without screaming.
- Heart notes: amber resin (obviously), vetiver, and a touch of vanilla. Here we’re dialing in the character: amber warmth with a resinous edge and a creamy counterpoint from vanilla. The heart feels classic and refined, not loud or boisterous.
- Base notes: sandalwood, musk, and tolu balsam. The dry-down is the good friend who shows up late but brings snacks: a smooth, soft, woody/musk veil that keeps the perfume intimate rather than project-on-the-wall loud.

If you’re into fragrance families, Ambra EdP slots into the amber-woody category with a sophisticated, understated elegance. ### How long does it last? Performance notes for the practical nerd
Longevity in amber-based scents tends to be solid, and Ambra EdP delivers in that department. Expect roughly 6–9 hours on most skin types, with a gradual fade rather than a hard exit. On cooler days, you’ll feel the scent project more in the first hour or two, then settle into a comfortable, intimate aura that makes people around you lean in for a closer whiff. On warmer days, the citrusic top notes may feel a touch more pronounced at first, but the amber core remains the star, so you’ll still get a respectable trail.

Projection (or sillage) sits in the moderate-to-good range. From a skin-first perspective, Ambra EdP behaves well across most tones.
