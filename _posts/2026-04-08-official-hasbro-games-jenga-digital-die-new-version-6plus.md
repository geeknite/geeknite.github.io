### The digital die mechanism

The digital die is designed to be intuitive. Players press a large button on the base, and a microcontroller handles the “roll.” LEDs light up to deliver the cue—often a number, sometimes a color, or a directive that relates to a particular row or block zone. The logic is straightforward: follow the die’s directive, remove that block, and pass the turn. There are also mode-based functions that can flip the cue to more complex directives, especially in the challenge rounds.

It’s important to note that this die is not a fussy, fidgety accessory. It’s responsive and relatively quiet. The die’s cues are quick, so there’s little downtime between turns unless your crew intentionally stares at the block like a stubborn orc blocking your path to victory.
