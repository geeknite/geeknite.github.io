## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance

### Routine Care
Regular vacuuming is your best friend here. A gentle vacuum with a curb on the suction is ideal to avoid pulling fibers out of place. The dense pile resists superficial flattening, but you still want to rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear along the length. Quick maintenance tasks include blotting spills as soon as they happen and performing a light clean with a mild synthetic-safe cleaner when necessary. Always test a small, inconspicuous corner first to verify colorfastness and fiber compatibility. The goal is to keep the cream base bright and the multi flecks lively without turning the rug into a science project about stain removal.

### Stain Management Strategy
For typical hallway spills such as coffee, juice, or sauce, act quickly and blot rather than rub. Use a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb the liquid starting at the edge of the spill and work toward the center to avoid spreading. If necessary, apply a mild cleaning solution suitable for synthetic fibers in a small area, then blot again until the moisture is gone. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the fibers or fade the color flecks. For persistent marks, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in synthetic carpets. The aim is to restore the look without compromising the back stain claim.

### Longevity and Care Schedule
A sensible care plan includes regular rotation every six months, a light vacuum schedule, and a yearly professional cleaning for heavy stain scenarios or odors. The combination of a solid backing and a durable pile means this rug should hold up to years of hallway traffic provided you give it proper care. If you neglect maintenance, you risk an uneven appearance due to wear patterns. A little upkeep goes a long way with long runners like this one.

## Installation Tips and Practical Scenarios
