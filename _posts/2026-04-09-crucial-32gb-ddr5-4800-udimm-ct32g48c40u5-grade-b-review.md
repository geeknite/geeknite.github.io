## Compatibility and installation notes: the practical steps

- CPU and motherboard: DDR5 support is essential. This typically means Intel 12th Gen and newer or AMD Ryzen 7000 series and newer. Confirm your motherboard BIOS supports memory speeds around 4800 MT/s and ensure you have at least one DIMM slot available for a single 32 GB module.
- BIOS settings: DOCP/DOCP-like or XMP profiles are your friends here. Enabling the correct profile will push the kit to 4800 MT/s. If your board is older or your BIOS is finicky, you may run at the default JEDEC DDR5-4800 rated speed with CL around 40 to 42; it s still a meaningful improvement over many DDR4 configurations, but not the full 4800 MT/s dream.
- Slot placement: If you plan to expand later with a second 32 GB module, install in the correct channel pairings to maximize interleaving. If you are starting with a single module, place it in the primary slot for your CPU/motherboard layout.
- Cooling and case airflow: DDR5 tends to draw less power than DDR4, but higher speeds and larger capacities mean more heat near the memory area when pushed. Ensure your case airflow is adequate and avoid a heat trap around the RAM sticks.
