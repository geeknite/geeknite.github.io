## Gameplay Experience and Special Guest Mechanics
Zombicide is a cooperative sprint through a zombie-infested landscape, with players sharing resources and trying not to become a perpetual zombie snack. The Special Guest Edouard edition adds a twist that keeps the game fresh without overcomplicating the core rules. In practice, you will notice a few things:

- A signature Edouard ability card that can alter the action economy. Think of it as a masterstroke move that you can execute once or twice per game to reveal a map section, snag a critical resource, or force a zombie to stumble into a trap that you set up earlier.
- A mission deck that includes Edouard-themed scenarios or variant objectives, giving your table a narrative spine beyond simply surviving the next wave.
- A design synergy between card art and tile layout that makes certain decisions feel thematically justified rather than arbitrary, rewarding strategic thinking and teamwork.

In play, you will notice that Edouard’s presence nudges players toward more thoughtful planning. It’s not one of those gimmick boxes that changes the game into a puzzle where you solve it on the first turn; it adds a layer of strategic depth that complements the frantic moments when a horde arrives. If you enjoy planning and then watching the plan either succeed or hilariously explode, this edition is right in your wheelhouse.

The special host mechanic does not overwhelm the base game; it acts as a spice that can be used to flavor several sessions rather than a mandatory system you must learn from scratch. For groups that adore narrative campaigns, this is a gift: Edouard as a recurring character in your mini-arc, a design mind you can reference when you describe why a map section requires extra attention or why a survival kit suddenly becomes priceless mid-siege.
