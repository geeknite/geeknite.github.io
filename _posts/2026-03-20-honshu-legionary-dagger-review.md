## Blade Performance and Maintenance

The blade is not a surgical instrument. It is a robust tool for everyday tasks, albeit with a price tag that is friendly to your wallet. Edge retention is modest; expect to sharpen after some hot peppers or a cardboard test. The 7Cr13 blade can be kept keen with a standard sharpening stone or a cheap steel rod, though you should avoid aggressive approaches that would ruin the edge quickly.

Rust resistance on budget steels is not glamorous, but the Legionary Dagger should hold up under normal care. After use, wipe the blade dry, apply a light coat of oil, and store in a dry place. In damp climates, you may want to reapply oil more frequently to prevent corrosion. If you are a collector who treats knives as display pieces, you will still want to maintain the blade to keep it looking crisp.

Sharpening this blade is straightforward; the steel accepts standard grits without drama. Use a 400 to 600 grit stone for initial sharpening and move up to a 1000 to 2000 grit for polish if you want to maintain a fine edge. Don t rush it. Budget steels respond well to patient filing.
