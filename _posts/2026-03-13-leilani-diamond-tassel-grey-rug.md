## Size, placement, and room dynamics

Rug size recommendations are always tricky because rooms come in all shapes, like a puzzle with missing pieces waiting to be found. This Leilani rug is offered in several sizes, including a common 5x8 and a larger 8x10 option for bigger spaces. If your living area is a standard 12x14 rectangle, a 8x10 rug can anchor the seating area and allow a comfortable border around the furniture. In smaller rooms, a 5x8 rug can still encapsulate a sofa arrangement, especially when paired with a coffee table that creates a neat focal point.

Placement logic matters more than most people realize. The rug should center beneath the main seating cluster and leave roughly 18 to 24 inches of bare floor visible around the edges if possible. This helps the room feel balanced and prevents the rug from overwhelming the space. In open floor plans, a larger rug can help delineate the living area from the dining area, and the tassel edges can act as gentle visual markers that signal transitions rather than abrupt delineations.

If you have a room with an unusual layout or a lot of doorways, you might consider a rug pad to prevent curling and shifting. The pad also adds a layer of insulation and keeps the rug in place as you move between floors, which is particularly beneficial if you have slick hardwoods or tile. The pad can help extend the life of the fibers and minimize wrinkling that sometimes occurs with larger, airy spaces.


## Maintenance and care tips

Care-wise, the Leilani Diamond Tassel Grey Rug is straightforward. Regular vacuuming on a low setting keeps dirt from embedding into the fibers. It’s wise to avoid high-suction vacuum settings that could snag tassels or loosen the weave along the edges. A gentle hand-cleaning method for spills works well: blot, don’t rub. If you’re dealing with a stubborn spot, a mild detergent mixed with water on a clean cloth can do the job, followed by a dry blotting to lift moisture. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could affect color or fiber integrity.

For tassels, detangle gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. If you ever notice tassels getting frayed, trimming them carefully to maintain even length can refresh the rug’s look without compromising its design. When storing or moving the rug, try to roll it rather than folding it to avoid kinks that may become a long-term memory in the fibers.

The rug is designed to be relatively low-maintenance, which is a big plus for busy households. It’s not “care-free” in the sense that you can ignore it forever, but it’s forgiving enough to handle the rhythms of daily life with minimal drama. If your home has kids, pets, or a combination of both, you’ll appreciate the practical balance of beauty and resilience that the Leilani line offers.
