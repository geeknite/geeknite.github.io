## Painting, Assembly, and Hobby Tips
### Prep Work
A quick wash with mild soap and warm water before assembly helps release any leftover mold release agents. After that, you will be rewarded with better paint adherence and crisper details. If you are a painter who envisions a table full of lifelike skin tones and grimy armor, you will appreciate the extra surface texture to work with.

### Assembly Sequence
Plan your assembly route before you clip pieces from the sprue. Dry fit first, then use a small amount of plastic glue to hold parts in place. The goal is to avoid wobbly arms or misaligned backpacks—both of which are tragic in a zombie apocalypse or, you know, a casual gaming night. If you are dealing with corners of armor or overlapping gear, a tiny touch of green stuff can fill gaps, but you won t need this on most standard pieces.

### Painting Techniques
Here are some practical tips to bring out the best in these minis:
- Start with a mid-tone base, then apply shadows with a thinned ink or wash to deepen recesses on clothing and skin.
- Layer lighter tones up on fabrics to create a natural gradient that reads well from across the table.
- Dry brush edges to highlight raised details like buckles, armor plates, and weapon rims. This makes the mini pop without requiring fancy techniques.
- Finish with a matte seal to protect your work during enthusiastic table edges and occasional dice thwacking.
