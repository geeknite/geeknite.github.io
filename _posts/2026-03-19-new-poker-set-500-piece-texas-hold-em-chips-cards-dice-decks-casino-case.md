## Maintenance and care: keeping the magic alive

Like any good tool for social ritual, this set benefits from some light care. Here are practical tips you can use to extend the life of your chips, cards, and case:

- Chip care: Wipe chips with a dry cloth to remove fingerprints and dust. Avoid soaking them or exposing them to moisture for extended periods. Chips that see a lot of action may develop a tiny edge chip here and there, which is not the end of the world but can affect feel if you are grading chips for a tournament or a precision home game.
- Card care: Keep the deck away from moisture and direct sunlight to prevent warping and color fading. After a heavy session, a quick wipe with a dry cloth can keep faces readable. If the gloss starts to feel sticky or gummy, a gentle wipe with a slightly damp cloth and air dry can help restore smoothness. Do not saturate the cards; they are not made of plastic and they do not respond well to water fights.
- Dice care: Clean with a dry cloth if you have spilled drinks on them or left them in a smoky environment. Dice coatings can wear over time, but a quick shake to re-roll in the right direction is usually enough to keep things moving.
- Case care: Keep the zipper running smoothly by removing any trapped debris with a small brush or a dry cloth. A little inside pocket organization goes a long way to prevent misplacement of your dealer button and your lucky coin 

If you are the kind of person who loves to customize gear, you might consider marking your chips to differentiate them in more complex game formats or simply redeploy the set for a themed night. The kit is flexible enough to support a little creative chaos while maintaining enough structure to avoid table chaos. The key is to enjoy your game, not to win a cleaning award for your table after the night ends. If your friends are a little too competitive, you will want this kit even more for its ability to handle a long evening without crying over a single misplaced card.
