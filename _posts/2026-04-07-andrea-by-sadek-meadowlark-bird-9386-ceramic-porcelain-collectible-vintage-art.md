Authenticity and condition
---------------------------
Like any collectible porcelain, authenticity matters in the long game. The Meadowlark 9386 benefits from a couple of quick checks:

- Marks: Look for maker’s marks on the base. Andrea by Sadek items often feature a stamped or painted insignia that identifies the piece as part of a licensed line. A clean, well-defined mark is a good sign; a smudged or irregular mark should prompt closer inspection.
- glaze and glaze crazing: Given its vintage vibe, you might encounter mild crazing or glaze lines developed over decades. That’s not always a red flag—sometimes it’s a charming character mark. If you see severe crazing, cracks, or a loose head, that’s a red flag and you’ll want to pursue professional restoration or a careful skip.
- chips and repairs: A quick twist of the piece with a careful eye helps. Look for shiny repairs that don’t quite match the surrounding glaze or seams that feel a little “too perfect.” Authentic vintage pieces generally show consistent aging, not a glossy repair that shouts “New!”

Care and display tips for longevity
-----------------------------------
- Handling: Porcelain is delicate; treat it like you’d treat a vintage action figure—gentle hands, soft padding, and a secure display shelf. Use museum putty or soft felt pads on the base to prevent wobble.
- Cleaning: Mild soap and warm water with a soft cloth work wonders. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the glaze. For stubborn spots, a soft brush can help without scratching the surface.
- Environment: Keep away from direct sunlight (to preserve the glaze color) and away from radiators (to avoid temperature shocks that might stress the porcelain). The goal is a display that age gracefully, not a porcelain version of a race car that overheated on a hot day.
- Handling frequency: If you’re not actively admiring the piece, it’s best kept in its position. The more you handle, the more you risk micro-scratches that accumulate over time. Your Meadowlark deserves respect, not a glove compartment tour.
