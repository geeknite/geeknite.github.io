## Gameplay Mechanics: What’s New Under the Hood
Season 3 brings a handful of new mechanics that change how you approach each turn. The Patient 0 exclusives add a new set of starting conditions that make the infection grow with a predictable yet tense cadence. The Rue Morgue flavor adds mission-based variants that encourage players to cooperate not just to survive, but to interpret clues that point toward the source of the outbreak. Angry Neighbors introduces a faction-level dynamic that modifies noise rules, door access, and incident frequency. All of these elements are designed to maintain the core Zombicide vibe — frantic cooperation, rapid planning, and the ever-looming fear that you might be about to trigger a cascade of bad dice rolls — while giving you new levers to pull when you want to mix up your sessions.

From a pure mechanics standpoint, you’ll see a few familiar features: upgraded equipment cards, special action tokens, and new objective cards. You’ll also notice some small but meaningful adjustments to terrain generation. For example, Rue Morgue scenarios may place a clue board in a library or a smoky tavern, which makes proximity to the action both thematic and perilous. Angry Neighbors could shift the focus toward crowd control and area denial, turning the board into a chessboard where the zombies and the neighbors occupy opposite sides of a slowly closing map.

The learning curve remains gentle for seasoned players. For new players, the new rule set adds some complexity but does not break the core experience. The design team has a knack for layering complexity in a way that rewards exploration rather than punishing you with a wall of text. In practice, you’ll spend a couple of games figuring out the best combinations of survivors and gear to maximize your burn rate on the undead, and then you’ll find that the new content slots nicely into your existing patterns of play without requiring you to re-learn how to play Zombicide from scratch.
