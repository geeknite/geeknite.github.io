## Comfort: cushions, cushions, cushions
Cushions make or break the experience. A good cushion converts a wooden seat into a tiny cloud and a long dinner into a memory. Here are the comfort factors you’ll notice:

- Cushion fill: Typically quick-dry foam or a foam-wrapped core. The result is comfortable, but not so plush that you lose your form during extended dining sessions.
- Cover material: Olefin or solution-dyed polyester are common. These fabrics resist fading and staining, which means your vibe can remain bright for longer while you host a ton of grilled veggies.
- Cushion depth: Usually around 2.5–3 inches, which is decent for a standard dining chair. If you’re taller or simply love to sink in, you can always layer in a seat pad for a custom feel.
- Removability: Removable covers are a boon for cleaning; you can toss them in the wash and avoid letting that marinade turn your cushions into a badge of honor that your grandkids will triage in the future.

### The real-world test: weather, sunlight, and campfire vibes
Outdoor furniture has to handle a trifecta: sun exposure, humidity, and occasional downpours. The PE rattan resists mold and fading better than natural rattan; however, it’s not fully maintenance-free. We recommend storing cushions during heavy rain or using weather covers to prolong the life of the fabric. For the wood, you’ll typically find maintenance tips like applying a sealant or oil every 6–12 months to keep moisture from creeping into the grain. In humid climates, you may also want to check the joints occasionally for loosening screws or wobble, which is normal with outdoor goods as wood expands and contracts with weather cycles.

## Care, maintenance, and longevity: keeping the shine intact
- Clean regularly with a mild soap and water solution. Avoid harsh chemicals that may degrade the finishes.
- Wipe spills promptly to prevent stubborn stains from setting in.
- Cushion covers: remove and wash as directed by the tag. If you use decorative cushions or extra cushions on top, consider rotating them to even out wear and sun fading.
- Protect the set from extreme weather when not in use. Use furniture covers or store cushions in a dry place during heavy downpours or winter months.
- Check bolts and screws periodically. Tighten any loose hardware to maintain stability and safety.
