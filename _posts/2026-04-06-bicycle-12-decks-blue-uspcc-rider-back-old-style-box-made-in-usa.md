## Care, Longevity, and Maintenance
Caring for cards isn’t glamorous, but it matters. Here are practical tips to keep your Bicycle Blue Rider Back decks in fighting shape:
- Avoid extreme humidity. Card stock loves a stable climate; too much moisture will cause warping and the dreaded curling edge. If you’re in a tropical or sauna-like room, give the decks a little extra space and keep them away from direct steam.
- Use a proper deck box or tuck case when transporting. The Old Style Box is sturdy enough for travel, but a snug carrier helps ensure the decks arrive as pristine as the day they left the factory.
- Don’t soak them in liquid for “education purposes.” This should go without saying, but it’s surprising how often a deck ends up soaking in a drink when a table collapses under pressure.
- Keep them clean. A quick wipe with a dry microfiber cloth can remove fingerprint residue. If you must dust, do it gently—these aren’t napkin-sized fans of cleaning.

Maintenance wise, these decks behave well in typical home conditions. If you’re performing heavy, liquid-laden magic acts or marathon gaming sessions, you might want to rotate decks to avoid any perceptible wear in the back design—the exact kind of wear that a cunning opponent might notice during a tense bluff.
