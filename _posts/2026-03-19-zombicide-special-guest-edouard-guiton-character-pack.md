## What you get in the Special Guest pack

The Edouard Guiton character pack arrives with a single new survivor card, an artwork sheet, and a small punch of tokens that modify standard actions. The survivor card grants a unique flavor ability that makes use of color tokens on the board. The color tokens act as optional boons that can be spent to dodge a zombie attack or convert a failed fight into a partial success. On paper this sounds simple, but the execution is where the pack shines.

### The Edouard Guiton survivor card

As with many Zombicide survivor cards, Edouard Guiton's ability translates into hot key decisions for early rounds. The core concept is art driven control of the battlefield. The redeploy mechanic is re branded as Palette Sweep which allows Edouard to reposition if he has color tokens available. The ability reads as a two part effect: during the actions phase you can spend a token to gain one extra move; during the zombie phase you may spend two tokens to block one zombie from entering a tile. This dual usage keeps him safe while still pushing the rest of the team forward.

### The vibe and token tools

The pack ships with three color tokens in primary shades representing energy, paint, and fear. Players can stash tokens on Edouard Guiton's card to accumulate energy. The tokens interact with the Palette Sweep by offering an extra dot of movement, or by guarding against a surprise swarm. It's a compact toolkit, but it enables surprising tactical flexibility when used with other survivors who can leverage Edouard's tokens for actions.

## How it sits in your squad

If you already run a crew with a classic mix of fighters, technicians, and specialists, Edouard adds a new dimension. He isn’t a brute tank, but a flexible operator who can flip a flank with minimal risk. Pair him with a heavy hitter and a scout style survivor, and you can run a two turn buffer to reposition your front line. The flavor text and card art also provide a nice thematic link to the other Special Guests and to Edouard's own artistic ethos.

### Gameplay tips and tricks

Tip one, make token management deliberate. Do not hoard tokens for the sake of it; when you invest tokens early, you create the conditions for a late game rescue that doesn't require you to risk a risky crawl to victory. Tip two, use Palette Sweep to reposition Edouard into elevated positions. A well placed Edouard can draw a mob onto terrain that your defenders can clear without risk to the rest of the team. Tip three, coordinate with your heavy hitters to maximize the tokens value when you push into a cluster.

## Design, art, and production values

The Edouard Guiton pack continues the trend of high quality art across the Zombicide line. The cover art shows the guest in a painterly style that matches his real world persona and the board game aesthetics. Inside, the card art continues the same tone: bold colors, clear silhouettes, and enough small details to keep you amused while you wait for the next wave. The tokens themselves feel sturdy and well printed, with crisp edges and a tactile weight that makes you want to poke at them instead of just looking. The printing is clean, the color balance is well chosen to avoid confusion on the table during a fever pitch zombie siege.
