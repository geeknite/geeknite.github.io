## Material Science: Why Sisal and Cloudburst Matter

## Size Options: Custom Size or 8x10
### The 8x10 standard
The 8x10 rug is the standard go-to for many rooms. It covers a central footprint or defines a seating area without swallowing the entire space. As a rule of thumb, you want at least 6–12 inches of rug under the furniture in each direction, depending on your layout. The 8x10 typically pairs well with a mid-century sofa, a pair of accent chairs, and a coffee table that’s perfectly sized to avoid your toes from becoming part of the furniture.

### Custom size advantages
Custom sizing gives you the flexibility to tailor your rug to your exact space. ## Care and Maintenance: Keeping Cloudburst Looking Sharp
### Quick spill response
For spills, blot with a clean cloth as soon as possible. Avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers. If the spill is oily, a small amount of baking soda can help absorb the oil before you blot. For tougher stains, a specialist rug cleaner designed for natural fibers can be used sparingly. Always test a small inconspicuous area first.

### Cleaning schedule
Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting. Use a vacuum without beater bars on the highest setting to avoid fluffing and edge wear. For high-traffic zones, rotating the rug occasionally helps distribute wear more evenly. If you notice uneven flattening, consider rotating the rug 180 degrees to refresh the impression underfoot.

### Backing care
Check the backing periodically to ensure the rug stays flat and doesn’t bunch up under chairs or furniture. If you have a slippery floor, consider a non-slip pad underneath to prevent shifting. The pad also helps with noise dampening—because nothing ruins a late-night gaming session like the sound of the rug sliding across the floor during a boss battle.

### Environmental considerations
Sisal is a natural fiber sourced from plants, which makes it more eco-friendly than many synthetics. If sustainability is a factor in your decorating decisions, this rug ticks a lot of boxes. That said, it’s not a wet-room rug; avoid soaking the fiber and wipe up spills promptly to maintain the rug’s integrity and longevity.
