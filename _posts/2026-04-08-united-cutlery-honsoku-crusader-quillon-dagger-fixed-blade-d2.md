## Maintenance and care tips

Like any non-stainless steel blade, D2 requires some care to keep a pristine look and functional edge. A quick wipe down after handling is a good habit. If you display the dagger in a humid environment, a light coat of oil can help prevent oxidation on the blade’s surface. Sharpening a D2 edge is straightforward with standard sharpening tools, provided you follow the right angle and technique. A medium grit stone or a guided sharpener will work fine. If you are unsure, consider professional sharpening to maintain the blade geometry. And yes, keep the sheath clean as well; dirty sheaths can nick the blade during insertion or removal and ruin your showtime moments.

For cosplay maintenance, you can add a little extra care to the handle and guard areas to preserve the finish. Wipe away moisture, avoid stacking heavy items on top of the dagger in display, and ensure your display lighting does not bake the finish over time. Basic care goes a long way in preserving the dagger’s dramatic appearance for many photo sessions and convention appearances.
