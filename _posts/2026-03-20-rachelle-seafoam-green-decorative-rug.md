## Practicality: Placement, Sizing, and Care

### Size Options and Room Fit

Rug sizes commonly offered include 4x6, 5x8, and 6x9. The 5x8 works well under a standard sofa-and-chair setup in a small living room or as a bedroom rug under the bed if you want a soft landing for your toes in the morning. The 6x9 is a better anchor in a larger open-plan space or in a living room with a bigger seating arrangement. Consider the rug as a visual anchor: you want the furniture legs to sit on or around the rug edges to create a cohesive area.

### Placement Recommendations

Place the rug so that at least the front legs of main seating furniture sit on the rug. This creates a defined space and avoids the “floating furniture” look. In smaller rooms, a well-placed rug under a coffee table can define your lounge zone without overwhelming the space. For bedrooms, consider placing the rug under the lower two-thirds of the bed so you have a soft landing when you swing your feet out of bed in the morning.

### Cleaning and Care

Cleaning is the boring-but-necessary part of rug ownership. For the Rachelle rug:
- Vacuum regularly, using a vacuum with a gentle height setting to avoid pulling on the fibers.
- Tackle spills immediately with a clean cloth to blot, not rub. For many stains, a mild detergent solution works wonders. Test in an unobtrusive spot first.
- For set-in stains, use a rug-safe cleaner according to the manufacturer’s guidelines. Avoid harsh chemicals that could alter color or fabric integrity.
- For deep cleaning, you can remove the rug and hand-wash or hire professional services if the product label allows it. Always check care labels before washing in a machine.
- Drying is essential. Do not use heat to dry; air-dry flat to prevent glazing or shrinking.

If you’re one of those humans who treats cleaning as a sport, you’ll appreciate how this rug tolerates occasional disasters—cake icing, pet hair, and the occasional gaming snack mishap—without looking like a crime scene after a single pass with the vacuum.

### Rug Pads and Longevity

A good rug pad not only prevents slipping but also extends the life of the carpet by reducing friction with the floor. Look for a pad with the right thickness for your space and a non-slip bottom to keep everything neatly in place. The better your rug pad, the longer you’ll keep the color and texture looking sharp instead of looking like a distressed vintage piece.
