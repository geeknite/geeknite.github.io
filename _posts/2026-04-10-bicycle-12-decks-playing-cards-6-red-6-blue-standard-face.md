## Practical tips for buyers
- Inspect the packaging when it arrives. If you’re buying in bulk, you may have a few decks that got a little dented in transit. A quick test riffle through a couple of decks should be enough to confirm you’re not buying a bunch of unusable bricks. If you see significant warping or warping patterns, contact the seller about replacements.
- Store them in a cool, dry place. Paper is a humble material but it doesn’t take kindly to humidity. If you’re planning to perform in a humid venue, consider keeping a small silica gel packet in your case to minimize edge-warp risk.
- Practice with both colors. It’s tempting to stick to your favorite color, but practicing with the other color trains your eyes and hands for a broader set of cues that can help you in real performances where conditions aren’t perfect.
- Keep a short cheat list in your case for the standard deck—saves time during practice to confirm card positions when you’re learning a new routine.
