## Materials and Construction
Cotton is the star here. The weave is tight enough to hold its shape, but the surface remains soft and approachable. Cotton is a practical fiber for rugs: it resists pilling more than a wool rug would, and it handles light foot traffic with a friendly sigh, rather than a groan. The woven construction provides a low-profile footprint, which is ideal if you have a couch with low legs or a coffee table that likes to drift into the rug’s space. The textile's feel says comfort first, durability second, and style is a tie for third place.

The small size of this rug means it is not going to be a heavy-duty hallway runner under daily treadmill traffic. It’s more suited to apartment living or a guest room where the rug can look sharp and remain easy to clean. That being said, cotton can handle typical living room traffic if you are mindful about placement and sometimes rotate your furnishings to even out wear.

One frequently asked question concerns edge fraying. With a rug of this size and weave, you should expect minimal fraying with normal use. The woven pattern locks the fibers together, reducing the chance of loose threads. If a rogue fiber pops up after a few months, you can snip it with scissors, and it won’t ruin the look or the integrity of the weave. This is not a thermal case for the lab, but you can treat it like a small software patch—patch, trim, and move on.

Care instructions are straightforward. Vacuum regularly with a routine that avoids beating the rug like a drum solo. Use a gentle setting or a handheld vacuum to avoid snagging the fibers. If you spill something, blot it immediately with a clean cloth. For deeper stains, test a mild detergent in an inconspicuous spot before treating the entire area. Do not machine wash; cotton rugs in this style typically dislike wet baths, and you may end up with a warped shape and a persona that looks suspiciously like a deflated pool raft. Air dry flat; resist the urge to throw it in the dryer, which is how you turn happiness into a fluffy disaster.

### Quick care tips

## Maintenance and Cleaning
Maintenance is straightforward. Vacuum regularly using a low setting. Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and to keep the stripes looking crisp. For spills, blot not rub as this prevents the fibers from becoming matted and dull. For tougher stains, a mild mixture of water and gentle detergent can do the job; test first in a corner, then treat the spot.

If you notice set-in odors from pets, a quick deodorizing spray can help after cleaning. Just be sure to use something safe for fabrics and avoid strong chemicals that can cause discoloration. Let the rug dry fully before placing back under furniture.

A quick note on underlay: a non-slip rug pad goes a long way toward preventing creeping and bunching under heavy furniture. It improves safety and helps preserve the rug edges if you have kids or pets who like to host impromptu dance parties.
