## Card basics and rarity
Erika's Gloom is an uncommon card from Gym Heroes, a set that dropped during the late 90s when the Pokémon TCG was still learning what it could be beyond cute creatures and dramatic trainer classes. The Gym Heroes set itself is famous for its trainer-centric flavor, featuring gym leaders as featured Pokémon trainers and giving players a taste of what a themed gym battle could feel like in card form. Card number 045 places Erika's Gloom squarely in the middle of the set, occupying a position that many collectors identify as a sweet spot between accessibility and the thrill of a chase card.

This particular copy is often graded as NM or near-mint, especially when you see it in the traditional 132-card layout of Gym Heroes. It’s the kind of card that looks understated at first glance but gives you the warmth of vintage art when you hold it in person. The print quality in Gym Heroes can vary depending on print run and whether you’re looking at 1st edition or unlimited, but Erika's Gloom generally holds up well as a display piece and a starter for a nostalgic binder.

From a rarity standpoint, uncommon cards in Gym Heroes were designed to be not as scarce as the hollows or holofoils, but still just scarce enough that a focused binder would feel satisfying when you finally snag one. The uncommons were the glue between the more obviously collectible rares and the abundant commons. Erika's Gloom embodies that role: not a showstopper in play, but a sturdy, beloved centerpiece for a gym-themed deck builder with a collector’s heart.

## Collecting care: grading, storage, and display
Condition matters more than most casual collectors admit. For Erika's Gloom, you want stable corners, clean edges, and a surface free of deep scratches or whitening. A near-mint copy should have a clean, vibrant image with no significant creasing or creases only at the margins. Protecting the card starts with the sleeve choice: use penny sleeves for protection, followed by a rigid top loader or a binder with protective inner sheets. If you’re aiming for a graded presentation, you’ll need to decide whether you want to cross the threshold to a PSA 8 or a PSA 9, both of which will demand different price brackets depending on market timing and the card’s centering.

Display considerations: Gym Heroes cards, including Erika's Gloom, look great in a binder display with color-coordinated dividers. The mid-set 045/132 has enough color to stand out when framed by other Erika cards or adjacent Grass and Psychic types. If you want to go the extra mile, consider a custom display featuring Erika herself, perhaps a print of the original art or a retro gym badge to accompany the card. It’s not a tournament optimization move, but it’s a mood booster for your desk or shelf.

Storage tips: avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight, as fading can dull the greens and purples that define the card’s aesthetic. A dark binder or a UV-protective card sleeve is your friend here. And if you ever plan to move or ship, ensure you use an appropriately sized toploader and a sturdy cardboard protector. The nostalgia deserves a safe ride.
