## Maintenance and Care

Cleaning is straightforward. Wipe the blocks with a dry or slightly damp cloth to remove dust, and ensure you dry them completely if a beverage leaks during a tense stretch. Avoid soaking the blocks, as prolonged exposure to moisture can affect the wood finish and the accuracy of a block’s slide. If a block begins to wear or roughen, a quick light sand with fine grit paper can restore the surface without compromising the block’s integrity. You are not rebuilding a spaceship, but you are maintaining a wooden block tower with the patience of a monk and the persistence of a gamer.

Storing the game in a dry, stable environment is essential. Hardwood blocks, while sturdy, benefit from a climate with moderate humidity to prevent minor cracking or warping over long periods of time. A simple approach is to keep the game in a closet away from direct sunlight and to rotate the blocks after a few games. Small rituals like this can extend the life of your 2012 edition and keep it performing reliably for many game nights to come.

### Accessibility and Dexterity Considerations

For younger players or those with dexterity challenges, the heavier hardwood blocks may require a bit more thumb strategy. The sense of accomplishment when a stubborn block finally slides out can be rewarding, but plan your setups accordingly. You can adjust the starting tower height or allow a two block cushion in early rounds to keep the game inclusive while preserving the tension that makes Jenga so delightful. The 2012 edition is robust enough to tolerate a few rule edits without losing its core identity, which makes it a good candidate for family game nights that include a broad age range.
