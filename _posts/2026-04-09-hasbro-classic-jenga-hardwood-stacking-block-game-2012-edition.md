### Setup and Turn Structure

Setting up is quick: stack the blocks in alternating layers, then press the full stack lightly to ensure the rows seat evenly. The 2012 edition reduces the number of blocks that feel abnormally tight right out of the box, which means less time adjusting the first tower and more time for actual playing. On your turn, you can slide a block out using only one hand if you wish to maintain a classy documentary vibe, or you can go full action mode with two hands and a little wiggle if your social group loves dramatic flourishes. The block you select should come from any layer except the topmost completed one. If your turn causes the tower to tumble, you have to accept the consequence and dutifully remove the final blocks that crash down with the finality of a grand ending to a mid budget epic.

## Maintenance and Care

Cleaning is straightforward. Wipe the blocks with a dry or slightly damp cloth to remove dust, and ensure you dry them completely if you happen to spill a beverage during a heated turn. Avoid soaking the blocks, as prolonged exposure to moisture can affect the wood finish and the accuracy of a block’s slide. If a block begins to show signs of wear or roughness, a quick light sand with fine grit paper can restore the surface without compromising the block’s integrity. Remember, you are not rebuilding a spaceship; you are maintaining a wooden block tower with the patience of a monk and the patience of a gamer.

Storing the game in a dry, stable environment is essential. Hardwood blocks, while sturdy, benefit from a climate with moderate humidity to prevent any minor cracking or warping over long periods of time. A simple approach is to keep the game in a closet away from direct sunlight and to rotate the blocks after a few games to ensure even wear. These small rituals can extend the life of your 2012 edition and keep it performing reliably for many game nights to come.
