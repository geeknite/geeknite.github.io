### Durability and Maintenance
Dhurries are flat-woven and thus wear well in high-traffic zones. The cotton fibers resist heavy pulling, but avoid rough abrasives that can fray the edges. Vacuum regularly with a light setting to pull out trapped dust; rotate every few months to prevent uneven wear. For spills, blot immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber lift. If a stain stubbornly sticks, treat it with a gentle fabric-safe cleaner and test a hidden corner first. The key to long life is consistency: rotate, vacuum, and avoid the same patch of floor getting all the sun or all the coffee spills.

### Practical Buying Guide
Expect a price range that reflects handmade craftsmanship and cotton materials. The cost is usually lower than premium wool or viscose rugs, but prices vary by region and retailer. When evaluating a Dhurrie rug:
- Inspect the selvages and edges for neatness; a well-finished edge indicates better durability.
- Check for consistency in the geometric pattern; slight asymmetries are typical in handwoven pieces but major misalignment should raise questions.
- Ask about dye sources: natural vs synthetic dyes affects colorfastness and environmental impact.
- Confirm care instructions; some cotton Dhurries are machine-washable, others require spot cleaning.
