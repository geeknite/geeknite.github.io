## What Fortune Row Is and Isn’t

Fortune Row is not a standalone game. It’s a supplement for the City of Mist core, a setting book that leans into the weather-beaten corners of your city to host a new neighborhood of myths. If the core book gave you the vibe of caped legends trying to survive in a world of grit and glass, Fortune Row trades a little of that broad-brush myth-making for a tighter, more lucrative corner of the map. It’s the difference between a city-wide epic and a heist at the edge of dawn.

This handbook is pitched to players who want more flavor, more tools for creating a myth-tinged life, and more hooks for a GM to drag players through a plot that is as much about fate as it is about fists. It adds new Assets, new Mythos/Mask pairings, and a toolkit for running Fortune Row as a recurring corridor of the city rather than a one-off adventure. It also supplies GM guidance on faction dynamics, leverage in social conflicts, and, crucially, a handful of ready-to-run scenarios that lean hard into the street-level myth play that makes City of Mist so deliciously messy.

If you’re a completionist grind-crawler who likes to collect every possible option, Fortune Row will scratch that itch. If you prefer a more minimal, keep-it-focused toolbox, you can still extract value here, but you’ll be pruning a few branches more aggressively than a bonsai enthusiast on caffeine.

To give you an anchor before we dive deeper: Fortune Row presents a palette of new “Fortunes” that a team can draw on—narrative prompts that tilt the scene toward luck, risk, and consequence. It also introduces new masks that players can embody or hybridize with existing archetypes. In practice, you’ll find yourself juggling a more nuanced risk/reward calculus at the table, where a well-timed bluff or misdirection can feel as potent as a display of superhuman prowess.

## Mechanisms: How Fortune Row Plays With Luck and Myth

Here is where we park the bus and talk turkey. Fortune Row adds structural elements to the City of Mist core that tilt the system toward social maneuvering, risk management, and a more explicit economic flavor. The core notions—Mythos, Masks, and Tags—remain central, but Fortune Row nudges players to think about Fortune as a resource and a narrative currency. Here’s how it tends to function in play:

- Fortune as a Resource: Think of Fortune as a meta-resource that players can spend to bend outcomes, reveal clues, or secure a temporary advantage. It operates alongside the standard die mechanic and the prompts that come from your Mask and Mythos. In practice, Fortune tokens can accelerate a scene or push a moment into a dramatic reveal. The pacing impact can be substantial: a single Fortune expenditure can turn a tense confrontation into a clean, cinematic exit—or a spectacular misstep that escalates the stakes.
- New Masks and Mythos Hooks: Fortune Row offers additional mask identities and mythic engine combinations, designed to feel current and urban. The new masks aren’t just cosmetic; they come with mechanical prompts, prompts for social interaction, and special moves that reward creative play. Expect opportunities to blend street-level cunning with mythic bravado: a character who can illusion-craft a crowd’s mood, or a mythic figure who can bend fortune with a well-timed aside.
- Social Terrain and Factions: The handbook leans into the social ecology of Fortune Row. You’ll encounter factions whose goals range from bail-bonds and backroom deals to arcane conspiracies that operate at the edge of legality. The GM tools emphasize how factions can negotiate with players, offering leverage, danger, and narrative threads that keep the group engaged beyond the action sequences. This is a big win for GMs who want a living city that breathes, blinks, and occasionally calls you by name in a crowded room.
- Narrative Tools and Scenes: Expect an expanded toolkit for crafting scenes that feel personal and consequential. Fortune Row leans into personal stakes: a character’s past returns as a hazard or a boon, and the city’s luck itself becomes a factor in the scene’s tension. The book provides scene seeds, hooks, and micro-plotlines that can be threaded into longer arcs or used as a quick-start for a one-shot. The best of these are nimble and adaptable, letting you pivot when your players surprise you with a witty, or reckless, plan.

From a GM’s perspective, the biggest win is how Fortune Row invites the table to treat luck as something that can be earned, spent, or risked. It makes the mythic feel more immediate and the urban environment feel more alive. The drawback, as with many City of Mist expansions, is that if you over-index on Fortune mechanics, you can slow the game into long “bankroll moments” where everyone is counting tokens rather than telling stories. The trick is to use Fortune Row as a spice rack rather than a recipe book: sprinkle generously, then let the scene simmer in character choices and consequences.


## Character Options: Masks, Identities, and Archetypes

Fortune Row does a nice job expanding the pallette of character options without overwhelming players who have already built a few characters in the core book. You’ll find:

- New Masks with Twist: The Masks in Fortune Row come with specific narrative hooks. They tend to lean into cunning, social leverage, and the “luck as weapon” angle. Some masks pair well with street-level disguises, others with mythic essences that allow dramatic reveals. The synergy betweenMask and Mythos is often where the flavor shines, especially in scenes that pivot on public perception and private knowledge.
- Cross-Pollination Rules: There are rules that allow players to blend a new mask with an existing mythic identity, creating hybrid characters who can ride two different narrative rails at once. This can be a lot of fun for players who like to push the boundaries of their characters but can also create tricky balance issues at the table if not managed with care.
- Promotion of Personal Stakes: The book emphasizes personal stakes—connections to Fortune Row’s factions, relationships with NPCs who populate the district, and the temptations of luck in a city that rewards risk. Expect prompts that push players to weigh moral choices against the pull of a bigger payout.

If you’re a player who loves a deep, character-driven game, Fortune Row offers you ample material to mine. If you prefer a lean, fight-first style, you can still mine value here, but you’ll want to focus on the micro-scales of scenes and use Fortune sparingly to avoid diluting the tempo.


## GM Tools: Running Fortune Row Sessions

For GMs, this section is the gold coin that doesn’t tarnish. Fortune Row supplies actionable guidance for running a campaign that centers on luck, social maneuvering, and moral ambiguity. Highlights include:

- Faction Playbooks: A set of faction profiles with agendas, weaknesses, and trigger events you can drop into your city’s political canvas. The playbooks help GMs improvise on the fly when the party disrupts a scheme or surfaces a hidden ally.
- Scene Seeds: A robust set of scene seeds designed to showcase the district’s flavor. These seeds are not rigid modules; they’re prompts you can adapt, expand, or merge with your ongoing arcs. If you love player-driven storytelling, you’ll get a lot of mileage out of these.
- Balancing Victory and Loss: The book doesn’t pretend fortune always smiles. It provides guidelines for handling success and failure in a way that still feels cinematic. Expect advice on when to let players bask in the glow of a win and when to pull the rug to heighten tension, because what is luck if not a cruel prank occasionally?
- Handouts and Player Tools: There are ready-made handouts that help players track Fortune reserves, reputation with factions, and personal stakes. These resources can simplify your table’s logistics and free up more brainpower for roleplay.

The caution here is straightforward: Fortune Row shines when the GM uses it as a framework for social storytelling and personal choice. If you wing it with a neon-tinted sense of chaos, you’ll likely end with a memorable session. If you wing it with a spreadsheet and a calculator, congratulations, you’ve invented a new form of planning anxiety. Balance is the key, and Fortune Row gives you more levers than a cockpit full of nerdy levers.


## Play Scenarios: A Quick Start to Fortune Row

If you’re itching to see Fortune Row in action, here are a couple of ready-to-run seeds you can drop into your table. Each is designed to be played within a single session or as a tight arc in a longer campaign.

1) The Lucky Club Heist: A fabled casino venue in Fortune Row hides a treasure that is less about cash and more about secrets. The party must navigate a web of comped drinks, old-money patrons, and a masked host who can bend luck itself for a price. Expect social confrontation, a few misdirections, and a finale where a well-timed risk can either break the case open or ruin the party’s best plans.

2) The Pact of Fading Neon: A street-level myth begins to unravel as a popular club’s fortune—a digital ledger that records every lucky moment in the city—starts to corrupt nearby magic. The PCs must decide whether to protect the ledger, which grants luck to the masses, or to sever the string that ties Fortune Row to the wider mythos, risking chaos and a city-wide blackout of luck.

3) A Night at the Market: The Fortune Row markets are a den of deals and whispered promises. The players encounter a broker NPC who can trade favors for futures—literally futures. The scene explores how each character’s personal stakes intersect with a web of minor players who can pivot a plan from plausible to doomed with a single clever move.

These seeds are designed to be malleable. The best sessions arise when players bring their own myths into the room and when the GM leans into the district’s mood—the electric gloss of opportunity and the shadow of a debt that will come due.


## The Geeknite Take: Who Should Read Fortune Row

If you enjoy City of Mist for its urban myth ambience, Fortune Row is likely to become a staple in your gaming bookshelf. It rewards players who like to lean into social dynamics and who are excited by narrative currency—luck, reputation, and moral consequence—over raw firepower. It also provides a toolkit that helps a GM cultivate a city that feels alive, where every corner hides a fortune and every fortune has a price.

For groups that lean toward tight combat and high-powered mythic displays, Fortune Row is still worth a look, but you’ll want to balance its social-scene flourishes with your table’s preference for action. The same book that gives you new masks also asks you to be mindful of pacing and the ethical lines your characters cross. If your table enjoys the drama of a moral gray area and your players are eager to debate a city’s rules as passionately as they debate its dungeons, Fortune Row will sing to you. If you prefer a cleaner, linear plot with a clear hero-villain dichotomy, you may find Fortune Row a touch runaway, but it has enough connective tissue to weave a memorable arc if you commit to it.

From a fan’s perspective, Fortune Row is an addictive blend of city noir and mythic whimsy. It gives you enough new material to re-light the fuse on your City of Mist campaigns without forcing a wholesale shift in how you tell stories. It respects the core system while giving both players and GMs new toy–narrative levers to pull when the room grows tense and the stakes feel high.

## Final Recommendation: Should You Buy Fortune Row?

Yes, and no. If you are already invested in the City of Mist universe and are hungry for new story engines, Fortune Row is a welcome upgrade. It gives you a more defined geographic locus for your urban myths, richer social mechanics, and a set of scenes and seeds that can fuel multiple sessions. It also leans into character-driven storytelling in a way that keeps the core strength of City of Mist intact: you are more interesting because you are not just a hero; you are a person with a myth to manage, a reputation to protect, and a debt to settle.

If, on the other hand, you crave a pure tactical sandbox with little emphasis on social dynamics, Fortune Row will require a deliberate shift in your table’s focus and a bit more spend-on-content time from the GM. It’s not a hard sell; it’s more of a gentle nudge toward a different rhythm at the table. The design team has done a solid job making fortune feel earned, not granted by some capricious city. It’s still City of Mist, still neon-lit metaphor, but with a taller glass and a louder heartbeat.

In short: Fortune Row is a strong addition for groups that want more humanity in their myth—and more opportunity to gamble with fate and fortune as a group. It’s a solid purchase for fans who love the urban mystic vibe, the noir mood, and the pleasure of watching luck steer a scene without anyone having to pull a lever on a spiky boss fight.
