## Glass tabletop safety and care

### Why tempered glass matters

Tempered glass is a standard safety feature for outdoor tables. If you ever have kids, grandkids, or the neighbor's dog with a curious tail, tempered glass reduces the risk of dramatic shattering in the rare event of an accidental impact. It also tends to resist moisture penetration better than untreated glass, which matters for outdoor use where weather can be unpredictable.

### Cleaning and maintenance tips

Cleaning is straightforward: wipe with a soft cloth and mild soap solution, then dry. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the surface. If you live in a region with heavy pollen or tree sap, consider a weekly wipe-down to prevent buildup. For cushions, removable covers simplify the process, but check the care label for specific washing instructions. A light spray of water after rain helps prevent mineral deposits from forming on the glass, and keeping cushions loosely tied or stored can prevent them from turning into curious projectiles during gusty days.

### Safety notes for glass management

Always place the glass top with care. Do not slide it across the wicker surface to avoid scuffing. If the glass is splashed with water, dry it promptly to avoid slippery patches during gatherings. In case of accidental chips or cracks, replace the tabletop segment or consult a professional rather than attempting a DIY repair that could lead to more dramatic shattering and a dramatic scream from your pet hamster.

## Maintenance tips for a long-lasting set

### Quick weekly routine

- Wipe down wicker with a damp cloth to remove dust and pollen.
- Vacuum or brush chair cushions to remove crumbs and pet hairs.
- Check screws and bolts after the first month and then twice per season to ensure everything stays snug.
- Use a mild fabric protector on cushion covers if offered by the manufacturer.

### Seasonal care recommendations

- In winter climates, store cushions indoors or in a dry, ventilated space. Prolonged moisture can lead to mildew or discoloration.
- Use a breathable cover to protect from sun and rain when the set is not in use. This helps retain color and keeps the cushions clean.
- If you notice color fading on wicker, consider rotating the cushions to different chairs to even out exposure.

### Troubleshooting common issues

If cushions sag, verify that the foam cores have not compressed beyond recommended levels. Some sets offer replacement cushions or foam inserts that can restore seating comfort without replacing the entire cushion. If you notice loose wheels or movable components, tighten bolts and ensure the legs remain level. If the glass top shows minor scratches, you can use a glass polish or consult a professional for refinishing.
