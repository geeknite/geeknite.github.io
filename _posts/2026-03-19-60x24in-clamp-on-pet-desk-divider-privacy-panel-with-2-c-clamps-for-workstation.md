## Installation and setup: how easy is easy?

### Quick-start reality check

The beauty of a clamp-on design is the no-drill promise. If you can lift a weight about 2 3/4 pounds and twist a knob, you can install this. Start by placing the panel against the desk edge where you want privacy to begin and identify the desk thickness that the clamps will grip. Attach the first clamp, tighten until snug but not, you know, too snug. Then attach the second clamp and repeat. Verify the panel is vertical and not leaning into your keyboard. If your desk has an uneven edge or a pencil drawer that sticks out, you may need to adjust the clamps or reposition the panel slightly.

### Fine-tuning the angle and distance

A lot of the practical privacy comes from the angle of the panel. You want it to block the direct line-of-sight to your screen while still leaving a comfortable space to reach your keyboard and mouse. A gentle tilt forward can help deflect ambient light and reduce glare on your monitor. If your monitor sits on a riser, you may want to offset the panel a bit to maintain symmetrical privacy across your field of view. The two clamps give you enough levers to find that sweet spot without the panel becoming a heavy totem that invites the cat to push it around when you’re not looking.

### A note on desk surfaces

If your desk is glass, a laminate with delicate edges, or a surface with a glossy finish, consider adding a small padded mat between the clamp pad and the desk. This reduces the risk of marring and helps the panel hold steady during tense video conferences. A little extra grip can go a long way toward staying put when your inner office ninja wants to rearrange the onboarding flow mid-zoom.
