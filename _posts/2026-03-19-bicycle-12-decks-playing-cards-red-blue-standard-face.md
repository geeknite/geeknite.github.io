### Durability and Longevity
Durability is a tricky metric with cards. You want them to last long enough for your daily practice, yet be cheap enough to replace without existential dread. The 12-deck set means you’re investing in multiple copies, which inherently reduces the risk of your entire game night crumbling if a deck shows wear or if someone spills something unfortunate on one deck. The stock and finish survive standard card house games and casual magic routines. If you’re a pro magician who handles cards aggressively, you’ll still want to rotate the decks and retire the roughest ones after a period of heavy usage; but for most hobbyists and casual players, you’ll likely reach the end of the set with decks that still feel and look serviceable.

Storage matters here. Keep the decks in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight. The color-laden red decks may show slight hue migration if left in a hot environment near a window for months on end, while the blue decks tend to hold tones a bit more stubbornly. The point is simple: treat these as tools, not as fashion statements that you leave on a sunny shelf year-round.

## Care and Maintenance Tips
- Keep the decks sealed until you’re ready to use them. The moment you peel away the plastic, humidity has a candidate for warping potential.
- Avoid exposing the decks to extreme heat. The color on the red decks can appear slightly bolder after sun exposure, which could be fun for certain visual tricks but confusing for others.
- Clean your hands if you’ve been handling sticky snacks mid-game. A quick wipe with a clean towel keeps the shuffles smooth and reduces residue transfer.
- Store stacked decks in a cool cabinet. A dedicated card box or organizer helps maintain order and prevents the color mix from turning into a color chaos.
