## Maintenance and durability: keeping the dream alive
If you plan to own a poker table, you probably also plan to own a few cleaning products. The table surface will eventually see spills, dusty chips, and the occasional rogue snack crumb that somehow makes its way into the felt seams. Here are some practical care tips:

- Cleaning: Use a soft cloth with a mild cleaner designed for fabric surfaces. Avoid harsh solvents that could harm the felt. A damp microfiber cloth can be a quick go-to for minor stains, but let the surface dry completely before your next game to avoid warping or warping jokes about the rug.
- Stains and spills: Blot spills instead of rubbing them. Rubbing can embed color into the felt and lead to a visible stain center. For stubborn marks, a tiny amount of fabric-safe cleaner along with gentle blotting is your friend.
- Legs and frame: Wipe down the legs and frame to prevent rust and to maintain the table’s glossy finish. If you live in a humid climate, consider checking for any signs of condensation or dampness around the leg joints.
- Longevity: Folding mechanisms benefit from occasional lubrication with a light machine oil, but avoid oversaturation. Over-lubrication can attract dust and create a grimy buildup that makes future assemblies feel more like a tai chi practice than a quick fold.

If you treat it right, your octagon will outlive a few of your early poker-night mistakes and become the trusty backdrop for many stories, a few bad bets, and several surprisingly generous bluffs.
