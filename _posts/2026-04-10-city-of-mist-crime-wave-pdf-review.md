## Overview

City of Mist: The Crime Wave is a PDF expansion designed to slot into the City of Mist ecosystem, offering a set of interconnected mysteries, a handful of new mythos-touched suspects, and a modular framework for running a city-wide crime spree that threatens to erupt into living legend. It isn’t a campaign-in-a-can that guarantees you an epic arc with three acts and a neatly tied bow; it’s more of a toolkit for a GM who wants to orchestrate a city-wide disruption with the same grace a street magician handles a handful of coins. The Crime Wave emphasizes tone, pace, and texture: long nights on rain-slick rooftops, a chorus of whispers about “the Mythos in the middle of everyone’s pocket,” and enough procedural flavor to make players feel like real detectives with a dash of mythic destiny.

What you get inside the PDF ranges from player-facing content to GM guidance, with encounter blocks, stat lines, tropes, and a few quirky twists that feel very City of Mist: “mythic identities collide with ordinary crime,” where your detective-hero could be as much a myth as a man or a machine. The Crime Wave doesn’t pretend to be a megadungeon or a modular adventure that you can slot into any old city and call it a day. Instead, it asks you to lean into the vibe: noir swagger, mythic undercurrents, and a city that’s always listening, always watching, and always ready to rewrite itself when you start rolling. If you’re allergic to mood and prefer pure crunch, you might find yourself adjusting the balance. But if you’re here for a stylish, thematically rich sandbox with a sharp eye for dialogue, this PDF is going to scratch that itch in very satisfying ways.

For fans of the core rulebook, The Crime Wave reads like a well-edited expansion pack: it respects the original design language while injecting a few clever experiments—new tags, new nemeses, and a city-wide network of rumors that your players can chase like smoking gun trails. It’s not a replacement for the core experience; it’s a complement, a layer you can peel back for a one-shot that feels grander than its square footage implies. It’s the champagne problem of City of Mist: you don’t need the biggest canvas to make the night feel legendary; you just need the right brushstrokes.

## Theme and Mood

### A noir city that never shrugs off its shadows

The Crime Wave leans into atmosphere like a neon sign leaning into the rain. The writing carries a rhythm that clicks with the swing of city nights, where every alley has a whispered rumor, every storefront a secret, and every citizen a hidden myth waiting to surface. It’s not about big punchy moments so much as a texture—the way the wind changes the way your trench coat drapes, the way a whispered nickname can unlock a memory that isn’t yours, the way a common crime becomes a microcosm of mythic power. The PDF leans into that vibe with style, giving you a city-wide tension that threads together individual scenes in a braid of noir with mythic undercurrents.

There’s a recurring theme of identity: who you are when your heart rate drops and the world slows to a hush, who you become when your myth takes center stage, and what happens when legends forget their masks. The Crime Wave uses these ideas to fuel both character-driven moments and large-scale set pieces. It’s not content to settle for “another heist”; it aims for “a mythic revelation wrapped in a crime spree.” If you enjoy the late-night, talky, character-driven crime fiction of old detective novels with a modern pulpy twist, you’ll feel right at home. If you prefer your mysteries to be mechanical gears and stat blocks with no poetry, you may need to fight the impulse to skim for the next encounter and let the mood wash over you instead.

### Workmanlike but playful tone

The text strikes a balance between the seriousness of a city’s engineering of doom and the playfulness of a group of players leaning into their mythic collectives. There are moments where the writing slows down to enjoy a character’s quick-silver insight or a snappy line that lands just right during a tense reveal. The humor isn’t a running joke; it’s a seasoning that makes the world feel lived-in. That said, a few pages drift a little too close to “here’s a cool idea, now go implement it,” which is fine for those who love GM prep, less ideal for someone who wants a plug-and-play sprint. You’ll want to pace yourself, especially if you’re prepping a multi-session arc in one sitting.

## Mechanics and System Fit

### Tag-based play meets city-scale tension

City of Mist is known for its signature tag-based system that blends character identities with a player-facing toolkit. The Crime Wave leans into this identity-heavy approach by expanding the set of tags, offering new dynamics and a few rules-light variants that let you push the plot forward without having to grind through heavy rules gymnastics. In practice, this means more freedom for players to lean into archetypes and myths while GMs are given practical cues on how to escalate stakes without tipping into unmanageable complexity.

What the Crime Wave does especially well is balancing micro-play (individual scenes, face-to-face reveals) with macro-play (city-wide consequences and the mythic underlayer). You might spend a session or two exploring a single lead, then watch a neighbor’s rumor cascade into a full-blown crisis that tests the party’s cohesion and the city’s collective memory. It’s a gentle reminder that in City of Mist, the line between detective work and mythic destiny isn’t fixed; it’s a dance you choreograph with your players as you go.

### Encounters, suspects, and modularity

The PDF includes a set of modular encounters with clearly defined hooks and a handful of suspects whose mythic identities ripple through the urban landscape. The writing encourages you to treat each suspect as a potential mirror for a player’s personal myth, a clever way to keep sessions emotionally invested even when the action plate is spinning. The encounters are structured to be flexible: you can expand, split, or collapse them depending on how your table is feeling that night. If you’re a GM who loves improv and loves to watch a group chase a trail of clues across different districts, you’ll find The Crime Wave to be a playground with a few well-marked paths that still feel fresh after multiple sessions.

## Content: The Crime Wave Scenarios and How They Flow

### A wave of cases that ripple through the city

The title says it all: a crime wave. The content thread connects a series of linked incidents that escalate in scale and mythic significance. Each scenario is built to function as a standalone vignette or as part of a larger arc that threads through multiple sessions. The escalation feels natural: an initial spark—a seemingly minor crime that hints at a larger, more ancient pattern—pulls players from local investigations into a sprawling city-wide mystery that implicates institutions, myths, and the very fabric of the city’s identity.

The scenarios are well-paced for groups that enjoy deep character moments and intense clashes with antagonists who aren’t just “a bad guy with a plan.” They are people with myths, with motives that tie into the players’ shared storylines, and with consequences that ripple through the city. Expect moral complexity rather than simple good-versus-evil formulas. The best moments come when a player’s myth collides with the reality of the case, forcing a choice that reveals something essential about their identity and the city they’re trying to save.

### Antagonists with bite and mythic flavor

Antagonists in The Crime Wave are not mere obstacles; they’re active participants in the city’s mythic ecosystem. Each one carries their own set of tags and a signature approach to manipulation, which makes them memorable NPCs for repeat play. The write-ups emphasize not just their stats but their narrative hooks, their relationship with the city, and how their actions reveal fissures in the mythos that power the city’s legends. You’ll find that some antagonists are better suited for late-game reveals, while others function as deliciously tense foils for early sessions. The diversity in approach means you can tailor the wave to your troupe’s preferred playstyle: investigative, cinematic, or a slower burn with a thriller texture.

### Scenes that reward clever play

There are plenty of opportunities to deploy clever, surprise moments. The Crime Wave rewards players who lean into their mythic identities and take creative risks—whether that means using a tag in an unexpected way or improvising a consequence that reframes a scene in a way the GM hadn’t anticipated. The best sessions feel like a collaborative duet between players and the city itself, with the wave cresting when someone finally cracks a single key tag that exposes a long-hidden thread running through the entire cityscape.
