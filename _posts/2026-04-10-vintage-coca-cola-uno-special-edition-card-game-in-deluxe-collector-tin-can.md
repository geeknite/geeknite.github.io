### Factors that affect value

- Condition of tin and seal: Mint condition in a sealed tin will command a higher price. 
- Card integrity: Are the UNO cards pristine, with no creases or bends? 
- Rarity and edition number: Some Coca Cola collabs were produced in limited runs, and collectors love that aura of exclusivity. 
- Completeness: Are all components present, including any inserts, rule sheets, or promotional material that came with the set? 
- Market demand: Nostalgia is a fickle beast; sometimes a particular Coca Cola edition becomes the hot item for a season.

### Grading and authenticity

If you’re serious about resale or insurance, you’ll want to get a professional assessment. A typical grade assesses the tin condition, the presence of seals, the condition of cards, and any signs of moisture or corrosion around the tin edges. Collectors also look for any misprints or edition-specific traits that confirm authenticity. A credible third party grading service can add a level of confidence for buyers who are thinking long-term.

## Maintenance and care

To maximize longevity, store the tin in a cool, dry place. Avoid humidity that can cause card stock to warp or tin edges to develop micro-rusts. If you notice a loose seal or a small dent in the tin, document it with photos for insurance or sale, but don’t panic—you can still adore the nostalgia and the art without full mint condition. A soft microfiber cloth can clean the outer surface; use gentle cleaning on the artwork, and never use harsh chemicals that could discolor the red branding. The cards themselves should be kept flat and free from moisture, ideally in a card storage box designed for UNO cards or standard playing cards.
