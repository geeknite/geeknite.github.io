## Using the Kit in Play
So you have the deck, the prompts, and a vibe that says you should rent a moody neon sign. How does this translate into an actual game session? The answer is not complicated, but it does reward a little preparation and a lot of play. Fortune Row shines when you lean into the image and structure rather than treating the prompts as rigid requirements.

### Step by step character creation
1. Set the tone. The GM and players agree on the mood of the session, whether it leans noir, pulpy, or something in between. The kit works best when everyone aligns on vibe before you start drawing cards.
2. Draw or assign Mythos prompts. Pick a power or domain that resonates with the city and the Icon you want to build. The prompts are crafted to feel evocative from the first glance, so you should be able to sketch a core image in a couple of sentences.
3. Choose a Logos prompt. Decide how the Icon expresses themselves to others, what trait defines their approach to trouble. Logos are the social armor, the way the Icon interacts with both allies and enemies.
4. Define a Fate prompt. What personal sacrifice or obligation tether the Icon to a larger story. This is the hinge that will drive decisions and create meaningful drama.
5. Add a City Hook. Fortune Row includes local flavor hooks that tie the Icon to a neighborhood, a guild, or an unsolved crime. This is where the setting starts to breathe.
6. Flesh out Deeds and Tags. The deeds you perform define how the Icon interacts with the world, while Tags mark recurring motifs the GM can lean on in scenes. The aim is not to overdefine but to create a few anchor moments the table can revisit.
7. Create a Support Cast. A Fortune Row kit character can include a mentor, a rival, or a curious civilian who plays a crucial role later. Cast the net wide enough to give players options but focused enough that the cast matters to the arc.
8. Final polish. Write a one paragraph summary that captures the icon in a single line and a short paragraph that describes the first scene. You should leave the table with a vivid image and a clear first beat.

## Sample Characters
Fortune Row invites you to spin up quick examples that illustrate how a deck of prompts translates into playable Icon concepts.

### The Clockmaker
Mythos: Time is a resource to be bargained with and traded during investigations. The Clockmaker sees moments as gears that can be slowed, sped up, or snapped into a new arrangement. The Clockmaker may rescue someone by rewinding a mistake or entangle a foe by stalling their plans.
Logo: Precision, patience, and a stubborn belief that little things change big outcomes. The Clockmaker feels the weight of every second and uses micro choices to tilt the scale.
Fate: A secret from the past will emerge at a pivotal moment, forcing the Clockmaker to make a difficult choice about whether to repair or erase a memory. The tension of memory vs progress drives the character into the heart of the plot.

### The Velvet Ghost
Mythos: The city itself is haunted, and the Ghost moves through the fog as if the alleyways remember every step. The Velvet Ghost can become unseen or reveal truths with quiet power.
Logo: Soft presence, ghostly charisma, and the power of truth delivered through quiet, persuasive action. The Velvet Ghost wins hearts and compels confession, often with a smile and a smile back.
Fate: A promise kept to a person who may no longer exist creates emotional friction that drives the Icon forward. The promise keeps the Velvet Ghost tethered to the living world while the phantom memories tug at their sleeves.

### The Lantern Keeper
The Lantern Keeper is a fuller take that appears in both the Step by Step example and as a stand alone concept.
Mythos: Light as power, exposure as revelation, and a willingness to burn away falsehoods. The Lantern Keeper can illuminate paths for allies but also scorch hidden threats.
Logo: A steady, compassionate presence; a guide who does not seek attention but offers it when necessary. The Lantern Keeper is the calm center in a storm of rumor.
Fate: The vow to guard a street corner becomes a test when the city demands a heavier price for protection. The character must decide how much of themselves they will invest in a single location and who pays the cost.
