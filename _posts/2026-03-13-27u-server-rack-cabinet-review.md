## Practical usage tips

- Plan your layout before you start. Sketch a rough diagram of where your servers, switches, and PDUs will live. This saves you from the “cable spaghetti with a twist” moment later on.
- Separate power and data paths. It reduces interference and helps airflow. Your future self will thank you when you’re debugging a network issue and not chasing a phantom EMI problem.
- Use cable management arms and vertical channels to keep things tidy. It’s not glamorous, but it looks professional and makes maintenance easier.
- Measure your equipment depth and ensure the rails can accommodate it. It’s frustrating to mount a server only to realize it won’t fully slide in.
- Consider optional add-ons like a PDU with monitoring or a door seal kit to improve energy efficiency and reduce dust ingress. These small upgrades can make a big difference over time.
