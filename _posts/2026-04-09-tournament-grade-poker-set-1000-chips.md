## Build Quality and Materials
This section dives into the tactile details that separate “nice” from “chef’s-k-kitchen-sink-extravagant.” Tournament-grade poker chips are often made with composite materials or polymer blends designed to mimic the weight and feel of hard clay. Pure clay chips, the gold standard for casinos, exist in the world, but home sets lean toward durable composites that survive enthusiastic players, coffee spills, and the occasional toddler with a crayon that plans to become a modern artist on the chip edge.

### Weight, balance, and grip
A good poker chip should feel substantial yet balanced. If you’re dealing with chips that feel too light, your opponents may question your ability to bluff with real intensity. If they feel too heavy, you’ll experience fatigue long before your brain runs out of excuses for bad calls. The best sets hit a middle ground: a weight that centers well in the palm, a smooth edge that won’t snag on felt, and a matte or lightly textured surface that minimizes glare under tricky lighting.

### Durability and wear resistance
Tournaments are not gentle with chips. They’re shuffled, slapped, stacked, dropped, and sometimes used as improvised weapons in heated discussions about “crushing bluffs.” A quality set will resist chipping and edge wear after long sessions. The chips should retain their color and legibility even after rough handling. If you examine the edges closely and notice chips being knocked loose or color fading, you’re paying for style without substance.
