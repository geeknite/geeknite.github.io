## Gameplay and Mechanics

This expansion keeps the familiar gloom base as the backbone, but it layers on campaign style elements that add progression and narrative weight. The core mechanics remain intuitive for players who know the original gloom, with a couple of twists to accommodate longer arcs. The changes are not explosive, but they are meaningful for a group that wants to explore consequences over multiple sessions.

### Setup and Flow
The setup remains simple enough for a one night campaign, but the campaign overlay gives it a new rhythm. You begin by selecting a campaign arc and laying out the story boards. Each session covers a beat on the board, and outcomes influence the arcs that follow. The flow is familiar if you have played gloom with a loose campaign mindset, but the expansion formalizes that approach into discrete arcs that can be completed over several sessions.

### Core Mechanics Tweaks
You will see a few new mechanics that support campaign progression. There are events that grant or deny boons based on past outcomes, which encourages memory of previous sessions. There are also optional ally cards that can join the team for a session or two, providing additional options for tackling the story beats. The expansion showcases the tension between risk and reward in a new light, inviting players to plan ahead without turning to min-max optimization only. The dice or card resolution remains consistent with the gloom mood, avoiding abrupt shifts that would ruin the atmosphere.

### Campaign Arcs and Play Style
Campaign arcs are the heart of this expansion. Each arc has a set of objectives, consequences, and a potential end that can alter the overall campaign tone. Some arcs lean into exploration and gathering lore, others toward moral decisions that shape the tone of the next sessions. The arcs are modular, which means you can run a long arc across three or four sessions or slot several short arcs into a single evening. The design purpose is to preserve the sense of continuing story while keeping each session meaningful.

### Solo and Cooperative Variants
For players who want to test out arcs solo or with a smaller group, the expansion includes a few variants that adapt the events and difficulty. Solo play emphasizes memory and resource management, as there is no party to rely on for shared decisions. Cooperative play emphasizes talking through the story choices and leveraging each character’s strengths to progress through the arc. The variants feel natural, not forced, which is a win for players who dislike artificial difficulty spikes.
