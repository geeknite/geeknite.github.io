## Cleaning and Maintenance
### Everyday care
Maintenance is where synthetic rugs typically shine. A quick vacuum a couple of times a week keeps dirt from embedding into the fiber. For small spills, blot with a clean cloth and a mild detergent solution. Avoid harsh chemicals; you don’t want to peel the label on your couch and the rug at the same time. Polypropylene fibers are generally stain resistant and relatively easy to clean, which means you won’t have to call in a professional cleaner every time a coffee cup experiments with gravity.

### Dealing with stains and spills
Act fast with spills. Blot, don’t rub, to avoid pushing liquids into the backing. For stubborn stains, a gentle cleaner designed for synthetic fibers is usually sufficient. Avoid bleach or ammonia-based cleaners that can degrade the color or fiber over time. If you have a stubborn red wine incident, you can treat it with a standard carpet cleaner and rinse carefully. After cleaning, let the rug dry thoroughly before putting it back into service.

### Deep cleaning and maintenance plan
Every six to twelve months, consider a deeper clean if you’ve noticed ground-in dirt or a change in appearance. A thorough vacuum, followed by a light soap-and-water clean, can refresh the fibers without weakening them. Ensure you dry the rug completely to prevent mildew. If you’re using a non-residential cleaner, confirm compatibility with synthetic fibers to avoid any dulling of the ivory hue.

## Safety, Sustainability, and Indoor Health
### Indoor air quality and materials
Synthetic rugs like the Moonstone Ivory are typically designed to be safe for indoor use, with low VOCs and stable dyes. If you have sensitive air quality concerns, look for certifications or manufacturer statements about low emissions. While synthetic fibers offer easy maintenance, they’re not always the best fit for every allergen profile. That said, a well-maintained rug cleans up easily, reducing dust accumulation that can otherwise become an issue in closed spaces.

### Sustainability considerations
Polypropylene is a recyclable polymer, though the recycling path for consumer rugs depends on local facilities. If sustainability is a priority, check whether the rug’s supply chain includes recycled fibers or sustainable manufacturing practices. Even if the rug itself isn’t fully recyclable in your area, a well-maintained synthetic rug can outlive several cheaper, low-quality options, which is a small sustainability win in its own right—less waste, more value.
