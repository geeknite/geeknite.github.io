## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
Like any dice, these aren’t immune to accidents—coffee spills, orphaned chair legs, or the occasional cat-sprint of doom. Here are practical tips to keep your All-Out Dice Pack looking sharp:

- Wipe with a dry microfiber after play to remove chalk dust and stray sugar from snacks. You don’t want the dice to taste like your last victory, even if your last victory tasted like cinnamon pretzels.
- For stubborn grime, a quick rinse in warm water with mild soap works wonders. Dry thoroughly to avoid water marks on the painted faces.
- Avoid harsh solvents or immersion in the dishwasher. The last thing you want is your red die turning pinkish and screaming “we were never friends.”
- Store in a soft pouch or dedicated box to prevent chipping on the corners, which can lead to hilariously broken dice faces during a dramatic roll.

Maintenance is less about vanity and more about consistency. If you can preserve the dice’ readability, you’ll reap long-term benefits on the table—especially during those marathon sessions where you’re not sure if you’ll survive the night or just survive another round of bragging about your “expert crawler control.”
