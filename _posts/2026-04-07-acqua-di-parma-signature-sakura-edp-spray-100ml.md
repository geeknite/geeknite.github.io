## The Experience

### Performance

Longevity sits in a comfortable zone around 6 to 8 hours on typical skin. In cooler environments you may notice it longer, in warmer days it might fade a touch sooner. Sillage is audible enough to be noticed by colleagues within a few feet but not loud enough to feel intrusive. If you are in a crowded transit or a busy cafe, Sakura leaves a refined impression rather than a wall of scent.

On more than one test day I noticed that the projection remains respectful while your presence lingers in a refined glow. It is the kind of fragrance that invites conversation rather than shouts it from across the room. You may catch someone catching a whiff and smiling, not recoiling in floral shock.

### Skin Chemistry

Fragrance interacts with skin differently for everyone. On some, Sakura blooms with a soft glow, on others it remains more intimate. For most, the scent stays close to the skin after mid afternoon, leaving a gentle trail around the wrists and neck area. It behaves well with dry or normal skin; very dry skin can extend the projection a touch, while oily skin can shorten it a bit but still maintain the core aroma. If you are the type who sweats perfume off in a gym, Sakura is not a workout buddy, but it will still smell pleasant if you apply lightly before heading to a stretch session.

### Seasonality and Wardrobe Pairing

Sakura is best in spring to early summer. It pairs well with lightweight suits, blazers, and casual linen attire. For a more formal vibe, it adds a refined floral note to a dark suit or a crisp shirt. Layering with a citrusy base fragrance can increase projection, but Sakura works best when it remains the star of the show. For casual weekends, a soft spray can accompany a denim jacket and clean sneakers without clashing with the vibe of a coffee run and a park stroll.
