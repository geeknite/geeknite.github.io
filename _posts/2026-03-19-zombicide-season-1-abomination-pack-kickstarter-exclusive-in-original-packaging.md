## Painting and assembly tips: do you DIY or hire a pro?
The Abomination is a centerpiece model, and painting it properly can feel like a rite of passage. Here are practical tips for getting the most out of assembly and painting without turning your hobby space into a disaster area:
- Prep work matters: wash the miniatures to remove mold release from the casting process. It’s not glamorous, but it pays off with cleaner lines and easier assembly.
- Prime with care: use a matte spray primer to give your paint a solid foundation. A good prime helps you apply layers evenly and reduces the risk of flaking later on.
- Break it into stages: paint the base coat first, then focus on the larger muscle groups or armor plates. Finally, add highlights and washes to bring out textures. This approach prevents fatigue from trying to tackle a monster in one go.
- Choose a color theme: the Abomination is a dramatic figure. You can go for a traditional gray/green zombie palette or choose something more theatrical—deep purples and sickly yellows, for instance—to emphasize the grotesqueness of the creature.
- Sealing matters: a brush-on or spray sealant helps your paint job survive the inevitable table collisions, accidental sauces, and the occasional enthusiastic player elbow.

If you’ve never painted a large plastic mini, you’ll be surprised by how satisfying it is to work through the process and see the model transform from a pale beige blob into a legible and terrifying monster. If you’re intimidated by the detail, you can always look for a community painting guide or commission support from a local hobbyist club. The hobby is funny that way: we all cheer for the same result, and we all have different ways of getting there.
