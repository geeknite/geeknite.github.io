A Quick refresher on the Rules (for Old Hands and Beginners Alike)

The core loop remains faithful to Codenames at a high level: two teams, a spymaster per team, and a grid of picture cards that you try to identify with a single hint. The twist in Pictures is that the pictures themselves guide the mental leaps. The spymaster gives a one-word clue and a number indicating how many cards on the board relate to that clue. The guessing team then tries to pick those cards. If they guess a card that matches the clue, great. If they guess a card from the rival team, that’s a misstep you’ll all remember for the rest of the night. If they pick a neutral card, the turn ends. If they hit an assassin card, the round ends immediately in defeat for that team. The kill switch is intense and thrilling, and learning to weigh risk vs. reward is where many tables either flourish or fold.

That said, there’s a gentle learning curve here. The Pictures variant often requires a slight mental adjustment: you’re not chasing a word association as much as you’re chasing a thematic or visual one. Some cards will cause you to laugh; others will cause you to pause and squint, trying to decide if that little red umbrella is supposed to be a clue about weather, fashion, or a subtle nod to a cultural reference that one player recognizes while another does not. The beauty is that you’re allowed to be wrong, as long as you’re making an honest attempt and keeping the game’s pace lively.

Gameplay Deep-Dive: Turn Structure, Strategy, and the Joy of Misinterpretation

Setup and Turn Order

- Setup is simple: lay out a grid of cards (the typical 5x4), select a starting team, and give the first spymaster a one-word clue and a number.
- The guessers discuss, interpret, and try to select the cards they believe correspond to the clue. You can reveal multiple cards in a single turn up to the number given by the spymaster, which creates tension and momentum during guessing.
- If you guess a card belonging to your own team, you keep going (up to the number limit) and reveal more cards. If you guess the rival’s card, that card is revealed and your turn ends. If you hit a neutral card, your turn ends as well. If you hit the assassin card, that ends the round immediately and gives the opponent a big swing of advantage.

The most delightful thing about Codenames Pictures is how a single word (or sometimes two) can unlock a surprisingly diverse web of mental associations. The Pictures variant amplifies this quite a bit because images are multivalent. Or, depending on the table, it could lead to three wildly different interpretations that nonetheless fall under a shared theme such as “things you find on a sea voyage.” The unpredictability is not a flaw; it’s the entire fun engine of the game, and the version in this Czech edition nails that spirit with polished visuals and crisp card stock to keep the joke from becoming a smear of ink across your dinner table.
