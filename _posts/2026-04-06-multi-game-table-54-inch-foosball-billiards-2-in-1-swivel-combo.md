## Setup, Assembly, and Maintenance
### Assembly Experience
Assembly is a mixed bag: the boxes are heavy, the instructions are readable, and the toolset is, for the most part, forgiving. Expect a couple of hours if you’re by yourself and a couple of friends to bring the energy and the memes. The most frustrating part tends to be aligning the foosball rods and ensuring the billiards table cloth remains taut after you’ve attached the rails. If you want a smoother experience, we recommend laying out all parts first, counting everything twice, and invoking the ancient ritual of “Where did that one screw go?”

### Maintenance and Longevity
Maintenance is not glamorous, but it’s essential. Wipe down the surfaces after boisterous sessions; keep the cue tips in good condition and check the rod screws periodically. The swivel joints appreciate a light lubricating oil every so often to keep rotation silky; avoid heavy lubricants that attract dust bunnies and turn your playing room into a science experiment. Regularly inspect the legs for wobble; a wobble is a sign that it’s time to tighten bolts or reorder your life decisions. In practice, with a little basic care, this two-in-one caravan of fun will outlive many of your impulse-buy gadgets.

### Space and Setup Tips
- Measure your room before purchase. The last thing you want is a grand reveal with your guests and a corner where a distracting potted plant insists on becoming a spectator.
- Place the table on a flat, stable surface. A shag rug is cute but can warp a roll if you’re not careful.
- Assign a “scorekeeper” role for at least the first few weeks. It prevents arguments about who actually sunk the winning ball and who drank more soda than shots were worth.

## Maintenance Tips for Long-Term Fun
- Clean the rails and foosball rods after heavy use to prevent grime buildup that could hamper smooth motion.
- Check the level of the playing surface periodically; a small tilt can throw off shots in billiards and foosball alike.
- Rotate and inspect the ball return system so that balls don’t become trapped in odd pockets.
- Keep the table away from direct sunlight to preserve the finish and prevent cloth fading.
- Use protective covers when the table isn’t in use to keep dust and pet hair at bay.
