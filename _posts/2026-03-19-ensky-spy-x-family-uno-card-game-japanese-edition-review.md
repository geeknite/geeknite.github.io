## How to Play: Quick Start and Theme Integration

### Core UNO Rules, with a Spy x Family Twist
Like classic UNO, the aim is to shed all your cards first. On your turn, you either play a matching color or number, or you play an action card that forces someone to draw cards, skip turns, reverse the order, or make the next player draw multiple cards. The Ensky edition adds flavor and occasional quotes that align with Spy x Family’s vibe, but the mechanics themselves remain faithful to UNO’s quick-fire loop. The presence of character art as face cards gives you a swift recognition cue when you are hurled into a chaotic multi-player round.

For players new to UNO, the essential steps are:
- Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player.
- Start a discard pile with the top card from the draw pile.
- On your turn, play a card that matches either color or number, or play an action card.
- If you cannot play, draw a card from the draw pile.
- When you have one card left, declare Uno to avoid penalty.
- The first player to empty their hand wins.

The Spy x Family flavor text occasionally nudges players to read flavor prompts aloud, turning a rote mechanic into a little improv moment. If your game group enjoys running gags, these prompts can spark extra laughter without derailing the pace.

### Rule Variations on the Japanese Edition
Some batches include a small insert that suggests regional house rules and clarifications, especially regarding the interaction between action cards that would typically force draws. The Japanese edition does not drastically change the standard UNO rules; it augments them with theme-based cues and slightly adjusted card illustrations to emphasize the family spy narrative. For players who prefer strict rule adherence, a quick skim of the included rule snippet suffices; for others, playing a few test rounds with the theme engaged can reveal how the flavor text affects decisions and the overall tempo of the game.
