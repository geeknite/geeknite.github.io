## How to Identify Fakes and Quality Issues

In today’s market, counterfeit concerns exist in almost every collectibles domain. When you are focusing on multi-autos and patches, a few red flags are worth looking for:

- Autograph legibility: If the autograph looks like it was drawn by a receptionist with a shaky hand, proceed with caution. Some autographs will be stylized; others can be smudged. Compare with known genuine examples from trusted sources to gauge typical signatures.
- Patch integrity: Verify the patch windows show actual fabric. Some counterfeit cards may have patches that look like fabric but are printed or simulated. A triple patch should be distinctively three patches that look real, not three counterfeit imprints.
- Card centering and edging: Production defects can creep in. Uneven borders, miscuts, or visible print lines can be telltale signs of lower quality runs. ITG sets from this era sometimes show a mild variance in print quality; be prepared to display patience with cosmetic imperfections that do not affect the card’s value but affect its aesthetic integrity.
- Serial numbers and labeling: If a card claims to be a limited run or a specific edition variant, verify the serial print run on the card label. If the information conflicts with the known edition guidelines, you could be looking at a misrepresented product.

If you are new to this part of the hobby, take your time to compare with other guides and reference cards. A methodical approach saves you from buying something that looks good in photos but feels underwhelming in person.

## How to Store and Care for Your Triple Patch Auto

Storage is boring, but it is the practical side of being a collector. Here are some guidelines to help preserve the integrity of your Jake Virtanen patch auto x3x:

- Sleeves and top loaders: Use a 35 pt to 55 pt top loader for the card, depending on the exact thickness of the card and patches. If the card feels tight, switch to a slightly larger top loader to prevent corner wear.
- Panels and binder storage: If you collect a lot of ITG cards, consider a binder with card protectors. A well-organized binder makes it easier to appreciate the triple patch composition without forcing you to handle the card excessively.
- Environment: Keep the collection away from direct sunlight, humidity fluctuations, and the occasional threat of your dog discovering the scent of fresh cardboard. Ideal storage is a climate-controlled room with a consistent temperature and moderate humidity.
- Handling: Minimize handling, especially for cards with autographs. Oil from your hands can affect the autograph surface over long periods. If you must handle, wear gloves or wash hands and dry thoroughly beforehand.

Maintenance aside, a well-cared-for card can age gracefully, maintaining both its defensive charm and its potential to generate conversation at card shows, family gatherings, or online auctions when you inevitably explain the backstory of a patch that looks like it could tell a poem about the 2013 season.
