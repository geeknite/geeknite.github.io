## Exploding Kittens NSFW Edition 2015 — The Adults-Only Card Game Review

If you thought a kitty armed with a laser pointer and a rubber chicken could conquer the table top world, wait until you meet Exploding Kittens NSFW Edition. This is not your grandma’s family game night. This is the grown up version of a card game that leans into sarcasm, cheeky humor, and a touch of adult whimsy. It was released in 2015 as an offshoot of the wildly popular Exploding Kittens franchise and proved that you can teach a roomful of adults a new way to push their luck, one cat pun at a time. And yes, it is absolutely NSFW in tone, though not in the sense of explicit art that would melt your office printer a second time. ## How to Play in 20 Minutes or Less

### Setup

- Shuffle the NSFW deck and give each player a Defuse card or two. Balance is important here; you don’t want the table to instantly tilt into an arms race of Defuses.
- Deal a starting hand to each player (the exact number depends on the variant you prefer, usually around 3 to 4 cards).
- Place the Exploding Kitten deck in the center. Make sure the Exploding Kitten cards aren’t all clumped together; you want suspense, not a guaranteed explosion at the first draw.

### Turn sequence

Play proceeds clockwise. On your turn you can play any number of action cards from your hand as long as you can legally chain them. The core action types include defensive moves, forced draws, or ways to skip a turn; there are also some saucy cards that play with the concept of adult humor. If you draw an Exploding Kitten card and you don’t have a Defuse, you are out of the game — imagine a dramatic dramatic ending where the cat wins by one whisker.

### Special cards explained

The NSFW edition revisits the standard EK card set and adds a few spicy options. Expect cards that warp the turn order, cards that force other players to draw, and cards that let you negate danger with a clever quip. The exact names vary by print run, but the vibe stays consistently cheeky. If you have played the base game, you’ll notice the same fast rhythm and memory-testing moments, just with more adult humor baked in.

## The NSFW Factor: Content and Safety

### Content and tone

The NSFW Edition leans into adult humor. That means jokes about relationships, dating mishaps, and a level of frankness about adult life that some players will love and others may wish to skip. It is not a children’s game; it is not polished for a corporate family night. It is a card game engineered to be enjoyed by adults who want to laugh at the absurdity of mortal embarrassment. It is about sharing a moment of raucous humor with friends who can handle a good punchline and a goofy death glare when the cards don’t go their way.

### Who should skip this

If you are easily offended, if your game night is a family event, or if you have a strict policy against adult humor at the table, this edition is probably not for you. For large groups, the NSFW flavor can be a hit or miss depending on the crowd’s tolerance for bawdy humor and the ability to separate game chaos from real life awkwardness. It’s also wise to consider the environment — a work party or a mixed-age gathering may not be the right stage for this edition.

### Safety guidelines

As with any party game that leans into adult content, consider a quick consensus before starting. A simple thumbs up from the room that says we are comfortable with mature humor goes a long way. It also helps to have a clear understanding of what counts as off-limits in your circle. A good practice is to set a boundary: if a card makes someone uncomfortable, skip it and move on. This keeps the energy high without letting the room descend into genuine embarrassment.

## Strategy and Tips (Because You Probably Want to Win at Some Point)

- Don’t hoard Defuse cards. You’ll want to deploy them strategically when the tension is rising and someone just swore that their next draw is a go-go for the glorious explosion. Timing is everything.
- Read the room. If laughter is the loudest weapon, you want to be the enemy of silence. Use cards that provoke reactions to keep the table engaged rather than letting the game become a monotone sprint to the end.
- Balance risk and humor. A risky move to steal a turn can pay off if it dumps the right moment on your opponents. Just don’t misread the room and turn a fun night into a high-stakes therapy session.
- Pair with a themed evening. Comedy and costume can amplify the NSFW vibe. A pirate night, a heist party, or a black-tie chaos event can make every draw feel like an episode of a reality show you would not cast in real life.
