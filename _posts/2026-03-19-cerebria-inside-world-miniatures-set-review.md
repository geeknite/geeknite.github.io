## Build, Paint, and Display: Practicalities for Hobbyists

### Quality and sculpting

Sculpts in this set lean toward a painter-friendly approach with clean lines and crisp edge details. You’ll find a nice balance between thin, elegant limbs and chunky torso details that survive handling during assembly. The faces are expressive without becoming caricatures, which is the sweet spot for Cerebria’s emotional aesthetic. If you love the “inside world” vibe, the sculpts do a good job of capturing that ethereal, otherworldly feel while remaining practical for tabletop use.

One caution is that some of the more elaborate robes and headdresses can take a bit of careful work to avoid stress marks or mold lines. Patience matters here; you’ll want to clean mold lines and gaps before prime time. But that’s no surprise for most modern miniatures, and the effort pays off visually.

### Scale and poseability

This set sits comfortably in the 28mm-ish range with slightly stylized proportions. That means they’ll look right with most Cerebria miniatures already on the shelf and will pair nicely with generic fantasy terrain. If you’re a gluing minmaxer, you’ll be happy with how stable many of these pieces feel once assembled properly.

### Assembly tips

- Pre-clean and wash: A quick bath in warm soapy water helps remove release agents. This reduces friction during painting and helps glue bonds.
- Test fit before glue: Dry-fit the parts to ensure the joints align. A light bend here or there is fine; you don’t want to force some delicate robes into awkward angles.
- Use a pin or a tiny steel rod for larger pieces: If a model has a heavy robe or a staff, pinning can prevent the model from snapping in the long run.
- Gap-filling and details: Use a high-quality putty or green stuff to fill minor gaps, then re-sculpt any missing surface details if needed.
- primer and paint order: Prime with a light gray or white; then base coat large surfaces before picking out details like eyes, jewelry, or magical auras.

For folks who enjoy step-by-step painting processes, you’ll find the approach is similar to other 28mm fantasy sets: base coat, wash, dry brush, highlight, and finish layer. The miniatures’ contrast levels often highlight natural focal points (faces, hands, magical auras) which makes life easier when you’re choosing color schemes.

### Painting ideas and schemes

If you’re looking for ideas, Cerebria’s themes open doors for a couple of interesting palettes:

- Ethereal force: pale skin tones with icy blue and lavender for robes. Add faint glazes to imply inner glow.
- Fire-born reaction: rich reds and oranges for energy-based characters, with charcoal black for shadows. A bright, almost fluorescent edge highlight makes the flames look alive.
- Thematic unity: pick a color pair that matches your core box heroes and use that as the unifying thread across all new minis. It helps unify your table even with a large cast.

Tip: use thin layers and allow each to dry fully before the next. The play of light on glossy or metallic accents can transform simple robes into something that reads as magic rather than plastic armor.

## Assembly Tips and Quick Fixes

- Use tiny clamps or bread-clip substitutes to hold parts while glue dries; you’ll avoid fingerprints on fresh paint jobs.
- If a limb is a stubborn fit, a tiny bit of warm water can help soften the plastic slightly for a better bend. Don’t go full hot-potato mode; you don’t want to deform the piece.
- For bases, consider basing flock or rubble with a sprinkle of static grass. It adds texture without being distracting.
- If you’re worried about breakage, pre-paint small areas to minimize handling after assembly. It’s easier to seal in the color before attaching the last two tiny pieces.
