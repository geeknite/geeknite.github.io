### Cleaning and maintenance quicktakes
- Regular vacuuming keeps the pile looking fresh; use a vacuum with adjustable height to avoid snagging.
- For spills, blot—not rub—with a clean cloth. Mild detergents can be used sparingly; test on a hidden corner first to ensure color stability.
- For tough stains, a light carpet cleaner prescribed by the manufacturer is recommended. Avoid bleaches that could interact with the blue-green dyes.

## Maintenance and Care Recommendations
### Routine care plan
- Vacuum weekly with a setting that avoids excessive agitation of fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color exposure.
- Catch spills quickly to prevent staining; act fast and you’ll save a lot of scrubbing later.

### Seasonal considerations
If you live in a climate with seasonal mud or pollen, consider a seasonal mat at entryways to reduce dirt transfer. This rug’s color can handle a variety of light conditions, but deep cleaning every season is a good practice to preserve vibrancy.

### When to call in a professional
If you notice persistent staining, dye migration, or unusual odor buildup, don’t hesitate to consult a professional rug cleaner. They have tools and solutions that can restore color fidelity without harming delicate fibers.
