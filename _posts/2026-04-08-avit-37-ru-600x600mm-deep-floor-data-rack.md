Airflow and cooling
-------------------

Airflow is the secret sauce of any rack. The Avit 37 Ru’s perforated doors facilitate front-to-back airflow, which is the canonical configuration for server cooling. If you add front-to-back airflow with a modest fan shelf or a set of quiet rear fans, you’ll enjoy better temperatures under load and a more pleasant working environment in the lab. You’ll still want to monitor temperatures with a few sensors—ideally one at the intake, one near the rear, and a central value to ensure you’re not chasing a phantom hotspot.

In practice, with typical 1U and 2U servers along with a modest switch, you’ll likely maintain under-load temperatures within safe ranges. If your devices generate more heat than usual, consider adding a small, controllable fan kit at the top or rear of the cabinet to boost airflow. A more aggressive cooling solution can be useful if you’re using dense GPU configurations or high-performance storage devices.
