## What is UNO H2O Splash? A quick primer
UNO H2O Splash is not your grandfather’s UNO. The deck is built from clear PVC plastic rather than the standard cardboard. It’s marketed as waterproof, floatable, and built to withstand the inevitable chaos of family game night by the pool. ## House rules and tips for smooth poolside play
- Lighting matters: If you’re outdoors, angle the cards slightly toward your eyes to reduce glare and improve readability.
- Keep a cup of water handy, not for drinking, but to wipe sticky fingers and keep the deck sliding predictably. A quick wipe prevents cards from sticking together and ruining a turn.
- Shuffle with patience: the plastic will slide, but a few rounds of gentle shuffling help you distribute the cards evenly and reduce friction during play.
- Define the order of play before you start. With translucent cards, miscommunications are more likely if you’re not in sync about color and number matching, especially for new players.
- Use a flat, dry surface. The PVC’s water resistance works best when there’s a solid base to rest the cards on. A picnic blanket or a dry table is your best friend here.
- Consider a quick “readability test” before the first round. Have each player pick a card at random and confirm they can read it without turning the deck on its side. If not, tilt or reposition the deck to suit your lighting.
