## Care, Storage, and Preservation Tips
- Keep the tin in a cool, dry place away from humidity to prevent metal tarnish and card warping. A closet with stable humidity is better than a sunlit windowsill.
- Store the sealed copy upright to avoid unnecessary pressure on the deck faces. The tin is sturdy, but gravity is not your friend when it comes to long-term storage.
- If you ever crack the seal for play, handle the deck with clean hands and consider a soft shuffle to preserve card edges. Glossy finishes can pick up oils, so a light wipe with a microfibre cloth is a lifetime friend of card integrity.
- Protect the artwork by avoiding direct sunlight on the tin. The vibrant colors you fell in love with in the 2000s will thank you for not turning them into a pastel souvenir.
- If you’re collecting multiple editions, consider a display rack that shows off the tins’ art without stacking heavy loads on top of fragile items.
