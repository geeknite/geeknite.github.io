## How to Play: A Short Guide for Time-Challenged Nerds

The base gameplay of Card Sharks hinges on guessing the placement of cards along a path, blending luck with a dash of strategic reading of the table. The Tears Edition keeps the bones but sprinkles on a few seasoning upgrades that make the process feel more interactive and audience-friendly. Here is a compact guide to get you from unboxing to dramatic reveal in under an hour, depending on your table talk frequency.

- Setup: Each player gets a few tokens for the audience and a character card if you want to assign persona vibes. The board is laid out with a path that cards will slide along. The deck is shuffled, and the first host prompts the players to anticipate where a card will land on a progressive scale from low to high.
- Round structure: A typical round involves predicting a card's position, the host revealing the actual card, and players adjusting their bets or confidence tokens accordingly. The Tears Edition adds an optional emotional bet step where players express a quick sentiment about their chosen range before the card is revealed. This is where the game stretches into the territory of performance art, which some players adore and others find hilariously awkward—and that is exactly the point.
- Scoring and endgame: Points accumulate as usual for correct predictions, with bonus points and missteps that ripple through the table. If you are chasing a dramatic finish, the final rounds offer heightened tension as the crowd cheers or sighs collectively. The judge's call on a good bluff can be a highlight, especially if a player nails a feverish monologue about why they chose a specific range.
- House rules: The Tears Edition accommodates house rules known to most families after one too many family reunions. There are suggestions for speed rounds, kid-friendly variants, and a caution about not using your outside voice at the dinner table while the reveal is underway. For those who love to hyper-analyze, the post-game analysis is half the fun, as players revisit the past rounds and recount the moment they almost pulled off a miracle and failed spectacularly instead.
