### The chairs
The chairs are usually woven rattan around a sturdy frame, most likely powder-coated metal or aluminum beneath the weave. The PE rattan weave should look uniform with no obvious gaps. A good weave will feel flexible enough to sit comfortably but rigid enough to resist sagging after months of use. The frame underneath is what you don’t want to notice—meaning you shouldn’t feel any wobble when you sit down for a casual meal. If wobble exists, it’s a sign to tighten hardware or add shims before your first outdoor feast.

## Assembly: getting from flat-packed chaos to outdoor dining glory

## Durability and weather resistance
Outdoor furniture is a test subject for climate: rain, sun, humidity, and the occasional wind gust that pretends to be a prankster. Here’s what to expect from this 9-piece set:
- PE rattan weave holds up well against UV exposure when paired with a protective finish on the frame and proper cushion covers. Expect some color fading over multiple seasons if you leave it exposed year-round, but it should still look presentable with occasional cleaning.
- Acacia wood is relatively resilient but not invincible. It benefits from a teak-oil-like treatment or a simple outdoor sealant to maintain its color and reduce cracking. If you’re in a wet climate, consider elevating the furniture slightly off the ground to prevent water pooling around the legs.
- Cushions: water-repellent fabrics dry faster than regular cotton, but heavy rain or prolonged moisture can still soak through. Quick-dry fabrics are great for casual use, but consider storing cushions indoors or in a breathable bag during heavy downpours.

## Maintenance and care tips
- Regular cleaning: wipe with a mild soap solution and water. Rinse thoroughly and let dry in the sun. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the finish.
- Cushion care: remove cushions when not in use and store in a dry place. If the fabric is removable, wash per label instructions; if not, spot-clean with a gentle fabric cleaner.
- Wood care: apply a suitable outdoor wood sealant or lightweight oil every season or as recommended by the finish you chose. This helps protect against moisture and UV degradation.
- Storage: for winter months or heavy storms, store cushions indoors and consider a breathable, water-resistant cover over the entire set to reduce weather exposure.

If you’re a stats nerd about maintenance frequency, you’ll be happy to know that a simple quarterly wipe-down and annual sealant application can extend the life of both the wood and weave dramatically, keeping this set looking better than it did on day one.
