## What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition?
Exploding Kittens Original Edition is a card-driven party game for 2 to 5 players (with expansions that gladly crank up the chaos). The core mechanic is delightfully simple: you draw cards from a deck that contains various actions and one or more Exploding Kitten cards. If you draw an Exploding Kitten without a Defuse card, you are out of the round. If you do have a Defuse card, you can save yourself for one more turn. The vibe is essentially a chaotic blend of a meme compilation and a friendly duel where the stakes are imaginary but the look on your friend’s face when they lose is priceless.

The art and writing by the Oatmeal give the game its signature personality. It is bright, silly, and loaded with cat memes that somehow feel timely and rewatchable at the same time. The humor lands because it’s meta without needing to beat you over the head with a meme hammer; it feels like you’re in on the joke rather than watching a meme perform its stand-up routine.

The cat imagery, the goofy card names, and the opportunity to bluff and predict what your friends will do next all contribute to a social experience that feels both simple and surprisingly strategic.

## How to Play in a Nutshell
If you have watched a clip of Exploding Kittens and thought you understood it, you probably don’t. The game is simple in concept, but the real fun comes from misdirection and timing. Here is a concise breakdown that will get you gaming without needing a degree in card calculus.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and remove all Exploding Kitten cards except one for each player minus one. The exact count varies by player count, but the base idea is to keep a constant threat that players will want to manage.
- Give each player a Defuse card and 6 other cards from the deck. Defuse cards are precious and unpredictable, so you’ll guard them like limited edition coffee. The rest of the cards form the draw pile.
- Shuffle the remaining Defuse cards into the draw pile as well. This keeps the endgame spicy and ensures that no strategy is 100 percent guaranteed.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile at random. You do not want to know their exact distribution; the suspense is part of the fun.

### Turn Structure
On your turn you may play as many cards as you want from your hand, in any order, and then you must draw a card from the draw pile. The logic is simple: you want to draw as little as possible on your turn while employing exact cards to manipulate outcomes. You can play See the Future to peek at upcoming draws, avoid an Exploding Kitten with a Defuse, or alter the flow with Attack and Skip cards.

A classic play on turn is to use a combination of See the Future and Attack to force the next player to handle multiple draws while you prepare your own defense. The mind games begin here, with you calculating not only your own risk but the risk tolerance of every other player who might try to bluff their way through a tricky situation.

### Card Types and Interactions
- Exploding Kitten: If drawn and not Defused, you’re out. The tension feels real, like realizing the boss miscounted the sprint budget and you might be the one left waiting for the last bus home.
- Defuse: This card saves you from the Exploding Kitten. It is the ultimate clutch move that enables you to reinsert the Exploding Kitten anywhere in the draw pile, effectively turning fate into a puzzle piece you can shift.
- See the Future: Look at the top few cards of the draw pile. This is cinematic. It lets you plan a few steps ahead and decide whether to risk a draw or push it onto someone else.
- Attack: End your turn and force the next player to take multiple turns. This creates a domino effect that can cascade into glorious chaos.
- Skip: End your turn immediately. Use it to dodge a nasty attack or to stall when you have control of the clock and a strong hand.
- See the Future variants include See the Future 3 and See the Future 5, giving you more information for more players.

### Endgame and Elimination
The round ends when all players but one have exploded or successfully completed their last draw and faced the inevitable—cat-shaped doom. The survivor gains bragging rights and perhaps a prize as imagined by the table. The game then restarts with a slightly adjusted deck or a new round based on house rules.

## Strategy and Tips
Exploding Kittens is not pure luck. It rewards cunning card play, a bit of psychology, and the ability to read the room better than you read a programming language: with careful testing and improvisation.

- Save Defuse cards for high-risk moments. You don’t need to use a Defuse on every Exploding Kitten draw; sometimes you want to ride the edge and turn the deck against others.
- Use See the Future cards to map out the top draws, but do not reveal too much if it tips off players that you’re in a state of desperation or confidence.
- Attack at the right moment. Forcing someone to take two or more draws can swing a round from a likely loss to a dramatic comeback. Timing is everything, just like in a boss fight where you press the button at the exact beat to maximize effect.
- Bluff occasionally. If you can make players fear your next move while you’re actually waiting for a more favorable draw, you gain a psychological edge that is as valuable as any card in your hand.
- Manage your hand size. Keeping a balance between offensive and defensive cards can give you the flexibility you need as the deck thins out and risk rises.

For beginners, a good rule is not to go all-in on a single strategy. The hot streaks in Exploding Kittens come from keeping your options open, reading the table like a social mastermind, and letting chaos do the heavy lifting when the moment is right.

### Host tips and party balance
- Start with a single demo round to show how Defuse and Exploding Kitten work, then let players ramp up.
- Use See the Future early to demonstrate how information changes risk calculations.
- Keep the mood light; the humor is the glue here and heavy criticism dulls the mood.
- If your group loves memes and quick banter, you will see cat energy surge in every interaction.
