## What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition?
Exploding Kittens Original Edition is a card driven party game for 2 to 5 players (though there are expansions to crank that number up). The core mechanic is simple: you draw cards from a deck that contains various actions and one or more Exploding Kitten cards. If you draw an Exploding Kitten without a Defuse card, you are out of the round. If you do have a Defuse card, you can save yourself for one more turn. The vibe is essentially a chaotic blend of a meme compilation and a friendly duel where the stakes are imaginary but the look on your friend’s face when they lose is priceless.

The art and writing by the Oatmeal give the game its signature personality. It is bright, silly, and loaded with cat memes that somehow feel timely and rewatchable at the same time. The humor is meta enough to work for adults but still accessible to younger players who want to torment their parents with the phrase Exploding Kittens on command.

## How to Play in a Nutshell
If you have watched a clip of Exploding Kittens and thought you understand it, you do not. The game is simple in concept, but the real fun comes from the misdirection and timing. Here is a concise breakdown that will get you in the game without needing a degree in card calculus.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and remove all Exploding Kitten cards except one for each player minus one. The exact count can vary by player count, but the base idea is to ensure there is a threat that players will want to manage.
- Give each player a Defuse card and 6 other cards from the deck. The Defuse cards are precious and unpredictable, so you will guard them like limited edition coffee. The rest of the cards form the draw pile.
- Shuffle the remaining Defuse cards into the draw pile as well. This keeps the final distribution spicy and ensures that no strategy is 100 percent guaranteed.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile at random. You do not want to know their exact distribution; the suspense is part of the fun.

### Turn Structure
On your turn you may play as many cards as you want from your hand, in any order, and then you must draw a card from the draw pile. The logic is simple: you want to draw as little as possible onto your turn while employing exact cards to manipulate outcomes. You can play See the Future to peek at the upcoming draws, avoid an Exploding Kitten with a Defuse, or alter the flow with Attack and Skip cards.

A classic play on turn is to use a combination of See the Future and Attack to force the next player to handle multiple draws while you prepare your own defense. The mind games begin here, with you calculating not only your own risk but the risk tolerance of every other player who might try to bluff their way through a tricky situation.

### Card Types and Interactions
- Exploding Kitten: If drawn and not Defused, you are out. The tension here is real, like realizing the boss miscounted the sprint budget and you might be the one left waiting for the last bus home.
- Defuse: This card saves you from the Exploding Kitten. It is the ultimate clutch move that enables you to reinsert the Exploding Kitten anywhere in the draw pile, effectively turning fate into a puzzle piece you can shift.
- See the Future: Look at the top few cards of the draw pile. This is cinematic. It lets you plan a few steps ahead and decide whether to risk a draw or push it onto someone else.
- Attack: End your turn and force the next player to take multiple turns. This creates a domino effect that can cascade into glorious chaos.
- Skip: End your turn immediately. Use it to dodge a nasty attack or to stall when you have control of the clock and a strong hand.
- See the Future variants include See the Future 3 and See the Future 5, giving you more information for more players.

### Endgame and Elimination
The round ends when all players but one have exploded or performed their last draw and faced the inevitability of a kitten shaped obstacle. The survivor receives the glory of not dying to a cartoonish feline and may win a prize as imagined by the table. The game then restarts with a slightly adjusted deck or a new round based on house rules.

## Strategy and Tips
Exploding Kittens is not purely luck. It rewards cunning card play, a bit of psychology, and the ability to read the room better than you read a programming language: with careful tests and a lot of improvisation.

- Save Defuse cards for high risk moments. You do not need to use a Defuse on every Exploding Kitten draw; sometimes you want to ride the edge and turn the deck against others.
- Use See the Future cards to map out the top few draws, but do not reveal too much if it tips off players that you are in a state of desperation or confidence.
- Attack at the right moment. Forcing someone to take two or more draws can swing a round from a likely loss to a dramatic comeback. Timing is everything, just like in a boss fight where you press the button at the exact beat to maximize effect.
- Bluff occasionally. If you can make players fear your next move while you are actually waiting for a more favorable draw, you gain a psychological edge that is as valuable as any card in your hand.
- Manage your hand size. Keeping a balance between offensive and defensive cards can give you the flexibility you need as the deck thins out and risk rises.

For beginners, a good rule is to not go all in on a single strategy. The hot streaks in Exploding Kittens come from keeping your options open, reading the table like a social mastermind, and letting chaos do the heavy lifting when the moment is right.
