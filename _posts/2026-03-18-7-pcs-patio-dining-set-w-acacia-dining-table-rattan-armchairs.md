## Durability and Maintenance: Weathering the Storms and the Sun
Outdoor furniture is a test of character—yours, your patio’s, and the furniture’s. Acacia wood is known to be relatively durable and resistant to decay, but it still benefits from a little love. A good finish or sealant protects against moisture, sun exposure, and the occasional rogue sprinkler. For maintenance:

- Clean with mild soap and water. Harsh chemicals are a one-way ticket to a weathered romance with your furniture’s finish.
- Treat the wood seasonally if your climate has heavy rain or humidity. A light coat of protective sealant every now and then can keep the grain from drying out and curling away like a bad pun.
- Cushion care matters. Removable, machine-washable covers are a blessing. If not, wipe down the cushions with a damp cloth and quick-drying fabric cleaner. Avoid leaving cushions soaked after a summer shower; mold is not a good dinner guest.
- Protect from extreme weather when possible. If you’re going away for a month, consider covering the set or storing cushions indoors to maximize color retention and structural integrity.

Beyond wood and fabric, the hardware (bolts, screws, and brackets) should be corrosion-resistant. Stainless steel or powder-coated aluminum brackets are common, and they’re the unsung heroes that make sure your table legs don’t decide to starfish on the lawn during a gusty evening.

From a geek’s perspective, the real litmus test for durability is how the set ages after a few seasonal cycles: does the finish fade evenly, do the cushions stay plump, and do the chairs retain their ergonomic shape after repeated use? If the answer leans toward “yes, mostly,” then you’ve got a set that’s built for the long haul—without turning your patio into a permanent construction zone.
