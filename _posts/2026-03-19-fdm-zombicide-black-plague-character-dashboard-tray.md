## Materials and print settings for FDM printing
Choosing the right material is the foundation of a durable dashboard. The recommended approach is to print in PETG for durability and wear resistance though PLA can be used for faster prints or if you are testing. PETG has the advantage of better impact resistance and resistance to moisture which can be valuable during long campaigns or when your table is in a humid living room.

Print settings that produce good results for the dashboard:
- Layer height 0.2 mm for a crisp edge on small tokens
- Infill 15-20 percent to maintain strength without a heavy weight
- Wall count 2 to 3 for sturdiness
- Top and bottom layers 4 to avoid visible layers on the outer surfaces
- Nozzle 0.4 mm typical
- Print temperature around 235 C for PETG 210 C for PLA
- Bed temperature around 75 C for PETG 60 C for PLA
- Supports only on overhangs where necessary the dashboard has relatively good geometry with minimal supports
- Filament diameter 1.75 mm

We have seen that the difference between PLA and PETG is not a matter of looks but durability. For players who travel to clubs and stores PETG is a safer bet. If your climate is extremely dry or humidity heavy PETG will hold up better. If your printer is new or you want to do a quick test print PLA can be helpful and saves time.

In addition to the material selection you should consider the post print finishing. Smoothing with a light acetone or sanding helps to reduce print lines on the outer surfaces. If you choose PETG a light wipe with isopropyl alcohol will help the material to finish cleanly.

When printing multiple parts the assembly plan helps reduce the risk of misfit. A dry run without glue is a good habit and allows you to see where the pieces align. Use small amounts of CA glue to join the edges and consider using a thin orange or pink paint pen to mark alignment points. This makes it easy to reassemble during your next game night.

## Durability and long term care
Durability will depend on the material and printing conditions PETG is the most durable option for long campaigns and heavy use. PLA should be replaced after a few rounds if you plan to keep your tray for a long time though it can be fine for casual play. The surfaces should be cleaned with a damp cloth to avoid dust buildup and you should avoid abrasive cleaners that might degrade the surface.

If you want to extend life you can treat the dashboard with a light coat of protective varnish. This helps prevent wear on the high traffic edges and keeps the tokens from chafing.
