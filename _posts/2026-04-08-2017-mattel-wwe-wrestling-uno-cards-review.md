## What are the 2017 Mattel WWE Wrestling UNO Cards?
WWE Wrestling UNO is a mashup product born from the era where collectibles meet party games and both collide in a neon-lit arena of cardboard triumph. The 2017 Mattel edition takes the familiar UNO mechanics and threads in WWE-inspired effects, mascots, and a handful of move-based action cards that change the pace of a standard round in surprising ways. Expect iconic logos, character silhouettes, and a design aesthetic that shouts WrestleMania, even if you are playing a casual game at a kitchen table.

The core concept remains simple: on your turn, you play a card that matches color or number, then you try to rid yourself of all your cards before your opponents. The WWE flavor adds a few extra layers: cards that represent wrestling moves, taunts, and crowd-pleasing shouts that can alter who draws cards, change the direction of play, or shift the odds in a dramatic, flash-bullet style. The result is a game that feels familiar yet unpredictable, like a good tag team partner who knows when to pull off a surprise move to swing the match in your favor.

If you are familiar with standard UNO, you will recognize the structure: number cards from 0 to 9 in four colors, plus Wild and Draw Four Wild cards. The WWE version adds a curated set of move cards that interact with the numbers and colors, creating moments of chaos that will delight fans who enjoy the theater of wrestling and the tension of a close mate night. It is not simply a slapstick gag; there is a method to the mayhem that invites players to strategize around momentum and crowd reaction.

## Design and Artwork
The packaging and card stock scream wrestling arena through and through. They are playful but not absurdly cartoonish, giving you the sensation that you might actually use them in a real moment of game-night strategy. The names read loud and clear on the cards, which is important because nothing kills the vibe faster than misreading a critical move when your table is already buzzing with laughter and mild chaos.

## Gameplay and Rules: WWE Flair Meets UNO Fundamentals
If you know UNO, you know enough to start. You match color or number, you draw from the draw pile if you cannot play, and you yell UNO when you have one card left. The WWE version adds a suite of move cards that can flip the table in a flurry of dramatic reveals. The core turn feels familiar, but the special cards introduce a twist that makes every hand feel like a mini match with a live crowd that you and your friends have invented on the spot.

The move cards range from taunts that force opponents to skip a turn to power maneuvers that allow you to steal a card, reverse the direction of play, or compel a player to draw extra cards. The exact distribution of these cards ensures that the game remains balanced enough to be entertaining for a group of four to six people, while still letting one charismatic player run away with the victory through clever timing and a little luck from the draw pile.

One of the clever design choices is the way the move cards interact with standard UNO rules. A Move card might not only trigger its own effect but also set up a favorable exchange when you subclass into a matching color. It creates moments of drama where you realize that your late game push is not just about speed but about properly baiting your rivals into misplaying a card or misreading a taunt that shifts the next two turns in your favor.

For example, a taunt card might swap the play direction while a wrestle move card causes the next player to draw extra cards. The effect is that you can build a rhythm of momentum, where the crowd reaction in your living room grows as you string together a sequence of well-timed moves and color matches. It is not purely random; there is a rhythm that, when mastered, feels incredibly satisfying, akin to landing a well-timed finishing move that sends everyone into a playful chorus of groans and cheers.

In practice, the game requires you to pay attention to the order of operations. Do you use a wild card to redirect attention to your strongest color, or do you hold onto it for a pivotal moment when you can slam down two or three moves in a single turn? The decision tree is not insignificant, which is a feature that makes this more than just a throwaway party game. It rewards players who play thoughtfully, but it will still make the rest of the table howl with laughter when fate decides to hand someone a demolition of a round with a tragic final hand.

## Gameplay Balance and House Rules
As with any licensed edition, there is a balancing act between fan service and solid gameplay. The WWE moves are flavorful and fun, but you do not want the deck to become so swingy that a single card in the last three rounds determines the entire outcome. The 2017 Mattel design nails a reasonable balance by distributing the move cards across the deck and constraining their effects so that they feel meaningful without rendering the game unwinnable for the players who have the misfortune of receiving a museum-quality starting hand.

In addition to the standard rules, a few light house rules can increase the dramatic potential without breaking the game. For instance, you can allow a quick taunt that forces a single player to reveal their next move card before playing, which adds an extra layer of cat-and-mouse tension. Or you can allow a crowd-pleaser move that enables a second chance if you guess right about the color of the next card in the draw pile. These adjustments can help tailor the experience to your group and maximize the performance of your best storytelling skills, because in a wrestling-inspired UNO, the story matters almost as much as the cards themselves.
