## Safety and longevity: keeping the spark alive without drama

### Safety first
Li-ion batteries get blamed for drama more often than they deserve. The CS-MIT200SL includes standard safety features, including over-discharge protection and short-circuit protection. In the field, you should still observe best practices: avoid mechanical damage, don’t expose the pack to moisture for extended periods, and keep the device and battery away from heat sources when possible.

### Longevity tips
- Store at moderate temperatures and away from direct sun when not in use.
- Don’t fully discharge, if possible; instead, keep a reasonable state of charge to maximize cycle life.
- Use a compatible charger with proper safety features; avoid “fast charging” super toggles that bypass safety logic.
- Periodically test the pack’s capacity if you’re in a mission-critical environment (i.e., if your job depends on that TDR in the field).
- Inspect connectors and housing for wear; the last thing you want is a loose connector causing measurement drift during a test sequence.

### Hazard signs to watch for
- Swelling or physical deformation of the battery
- Excessive heat during charging or testing
- Visible damage to the connector or wires
If you observe any of these, discontinue use and consult a professional or replace with a new unit. Safety is not a marketing slogan; it’s a practice.
