## Power and USB: The Real USP (Power Sans Pains)
Now for the main event: four power outlets and two USB ports integrated into the desk. The outlets are a mix of standard outlets and a few USB-A/USB-C ports (the exact port configuration depends on the vendor variant). You get enough juice to charge a PC, a monitor, a printer, a lamp, and somehow your tablet that still insists on living in 2009 app-land. The USB ports are handy for charging peripherals or fast-charging a phone while you pretend to work in a productive trance.

One caveat: the outlets are not a substitute for a proper surge-protected power strip on steroids. They provide convenient access, but the power strip still has to be rated for your total load. If you’re running a high-draw PC, a desktop-level GPU, and a couple of external drives, don’t pretend the on-desk outlets will magically handle all of that without a surge or a trip. It’s still best to rely on a dedicated surge protector for the bulk of your equipment and use the desk outlets for smaller peripherals or quick-connect devices.

For those curious about safety certifications, this desk’s power module is UL-listed in most markets. If your room has a local certification requirement, check the product details or ask the retailer for the exact model you’re buying.
