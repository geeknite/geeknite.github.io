### Dosing schedule and practical timing

Dosing can be done on a day that fits your routine. A common practice is to treat all dogs in the household on the same day to prevent cross-contamination. For puppies and small dogs, some veterinarians prefer administering the dewormer after a meal to reduce the chances of stomach upset. If your dog is particularly anxious about pills, you can hide the tablet in a small treat or a dollop of peanut butter, cheese, or your dog of choice. If the tablet is not chewable and your dog refuses to swallow, a veterinarian can demonstrate safe ways to administer the dose using a pill gun or by gently placing the tablet at the back of the tongue.

### What to expect after dosing

Most dogs tolerate dewormers well. You might notice a temporary change in stool quality as worms are expelled. This is normal and should subside within a day or two. In rare cases, dogs may experience mild GI upset like vomiting or diarrhea. If your dog shows persistent signs beyond 48 hours, contact your veterinarian. Do not double-dose in an attempt to fix problems that appear after dosing; instead, seek professional medical guidance.

## Safety, side effects, and considerations

### Potential side effects to watch for

Like any medication, dewormers can have side effects. Common but mild reactions include temporary vomiting, decreased appetite, or slight lethargy. In rare cases, dogs may experience more noticeable GI upset or an allergic reaction. If you notice swelling, hives, coughing, or trouble breathing, seek veterinary help immediately. Pregnant and nursing dogs should only be dosed under veterinary supervision since worm burdens may differ in such cases and the safety profile of certain formulations can vary.

### Interactions and cautions

If your dog is taking other medications, particularly those that affect the gut or liver, consult with a veterinarian about potential interactions. The label will warn about co-administration with other dewormers or heavy parasite loads, and it may advise against giving multiple products simultaneously that provide overlapping active ingredients. Do not combine with products that could create a risk of overdose. Stick to the recommended dosing schedule unless you have a veterinary reason to adjust.

### Storage and shelf life

Keep the bottle sealed in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Excess heat can degrade active ingredients, reducing potency. Do not use tablets beyond their expiry date, and check the bottle periodically for changes in color, texture, or odor. A smell change can be a sign that a bottle is past its prime, which is not the best time for your dog's gut to become a hardware store for worms.
