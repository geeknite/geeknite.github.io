## Tactics: Surviving and Thriving Against the Abominotaur
### Roles and Team Composition
In a typical party you’ll see the following roles emerge during the Abominotaur fight:
- The Tank: A survivable front-liner who can absorb hits and keep the boss attention. If you’re lucky, this role also brings zone-control tricks to the table.
- The Ranged Support: A shooter who can deliver consistent damage while maintaining safety behind the line of defenders.
- The Scout/Handyman: The team’s eyes and flexibility, handling mobility, board control, and quick responses to spawn waves.
- The Healer/Support: In a game where a single misstep can wipe a squad, support characters who can mend morale and provide essential boosts help the group endure.

The Abominotaur punishes sloppy positioning and rewards disciplined play. If your team blunders into the wrong quadrant of the board, you’ll discover that the boss punishes with a roar that shakes your confidence as well as your model’s base. The lesson: you need to think in terms of board control rather than pure raw damage. Positioning is king.

### Core Tactics That Work
- Control the Boss’s Outer Ring: Keep your units around the edges to prevent being flanked by spawned minions. A smart player can corral the lesser zombies into lethal choke points while the Abominotaur focuses on whoever is closest, which is often the biggest test of group dynamics.
- Use the Environment: If the board has hazards or terrain features that can be leveraged, do so. A well-timed trap or terrain advantage can turn the tide in moments. The game is as much about geometry as it is about dice.
- Diversify Damage Sources: Don’t rely on a single squad to whittle down the boss. A balanced approach that uses both ranged and melee attacks tends to deliver more consistent results and reduce the risk of a single bad roll wiping out your plan.
- Mitigate Minion Waves: The spawn bursts can overwhelm you if left unchecked. Learn to anticipate the wave patterns and have a strategy to clear or soak the extra bodies while the boss is occupied.

### Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
- Overcommitting to the Boss Without Support: It’s tempting to stack all your resources into a big strike, but the Abominotaur thrives on overexposure. Keep at least two units in reserve for immediate defense against spawn waves.
- Underestimating the Stomp: A single stomp can change the board’s geometry, knocking heroes into danger zones or creating chokepoints that the boss can exploit. Step back when you hear that telltale rumble.
- Ignoring Morale: Morale tokens matter. Don’t let the fear of retreat paralyze your planning. Sometimes retreating to regroup is a smarter choice than pressing on into a bad position with no backup.
