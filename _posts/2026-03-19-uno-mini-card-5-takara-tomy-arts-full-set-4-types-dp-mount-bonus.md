### The Four Types

The four variants—Type A, Type B, Type C, and Type D—each bring a distinct art language to the UNO universe. They’re not just color-changed skins; they carry micro-narratives that nod to retro gaming, modern pop-art aesthetics, and the quiet drama of a card deck when it’s left out on a sunlit desk.

#### Type A: Classic Reframed
Type A leans into the familiar UNO palette but with a glossy makeover. The reds skew warmer, the blues cooler, and the yellows glow with a sunlit optimism that somehow survived a marathon gaming session at 2 a.m. It’s the “comfort food” variant for players who want something recognizable but with a premium finish.

#### Type B: Neon Knight
Type B is where the set starts to embrace a more flamboyant personality. Neon outlines pop against a dark field, and the card art hints at a sci-fi arcade vibe. If Type A is comfort, Type B is cosplay for your coffee table. It’s bold, it’s bright, and it refuses to blink in the face of your purely RGB-lit dungeon desk.

#### Type C: Pastel Panic
Type C retreats into pastel tones that somehow still punch above their weight class. It’s the soft-spoken variant that invites a closer look, because every corner may reveal a tiny easter egg—maybe a micro-QR code, maybe a hidden smile in the corner, maybe just a very well-placed gradient. It’s the subtle drama queen of the quartet.

#### Type D: Shadow Spectrum
Type D leans into moody undertones and cinematic shadows. It’s the dramatic, late-night-watching variant that makes you want to tilt your head and pretend you’re deciphering a spoiler in a noir novel. If you’re chasing a display that looks like it belongs in a sci-fi film or a cyberpunk gallery, Type D is your go-to.

## Value, Care, and Display Tips

- Care: Keep the cards dry and away from harsh solvents. A microfiber cloth is enough to wipe minor fingerprints. Avoid harsh chemicals that could dull the gloss.
- Storage: If you plan to collect more from the line, consider a small acrylic display case to keep dust off and to preserve the glossy finish.
- Lighting: Use indirect lighting for photography and display. Direct sunlight can cause color shifts over long periods, and you’ll start to feel like you’re staring at a real work-from-home art piece that needs to be appreciated in the right mood.
- Rotation: If you own multiple mini-arts, rotate your display every couple of weeks to keep the visual interest high and the satisfaction fresh.
