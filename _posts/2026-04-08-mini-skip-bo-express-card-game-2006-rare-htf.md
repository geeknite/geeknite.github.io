## How to play in 10 minutes or less
If you have played classic Skip-Bo, you know the rhythm. If not, fear not: Express keeps the core skeleton but trims the meat until it is basically bone, with a little marrow of speed.

### Setup
- Shuffle the mini deck and deal a manageable number of stock piles to each player. Fewer piles, more sprint, less social drama about who is going to block the action this time.
- Each player draws a starting hand suitable for fast play. The remainder forms the draw pile.
- The objective is simple: discard all stock piles by placing numbers in ascending order, using wild Skip-Bo cards to bridge gaps when you get the luck of the draw.

### Turn structure
- On your turn you draw to reach your hand size, then play cards from your hand to build on the shared build piles. The build piles act as the road to victory, and your job is to keep the road clear enough to drive through with a sequence while your rival stumbles on the roadside with a mis-timed move.
- You can only play the top card of your stock piles or any cards from your hand that fit the current build piles. The constraint is small but real, and it keeps your brain on its toes as you juggle potential plays and lane closures.
- If you cannot play, you discard and end your turn. Repeat until someone hits the win line and erupts in a tiny victory dance.

### Winning conditions
The first player to discard all cards from their stock piles wins. The moment is not a cinematic explosion, but it is a crisp, satisfying sprint to a small, gleeful finish.

## House rules and variant ideas
Mini editions invite micro experiments. Here are a few ideas you can try on your next session without derailing immersion:

- Timed Turns: Set a 15 second clock per turn to push decision making and create a tournament vibe that is perfect for lighthearted rivalries.
