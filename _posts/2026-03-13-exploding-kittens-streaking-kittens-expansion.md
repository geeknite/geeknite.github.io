## How to play with Streaking Kittens

The practical bit: you don’t need to throw out the base game rules to enjoy this expansion. The recommended approach is to mix one expansion deck with the base in a 1:2 ratio for most table sizes, though you can throw more in if your crew enjoys chaos with a side of chaos. Here’s a quick how-to for the curious:

- Setup: shuffle the expansion into the base deck. Remove or adjust a few cards to suit the player count, then set the Exploding Kittens card into the appropriate pile per the base game’s guidelines.
- Turn structure: you keep the standard Exploding Kittens flow—draw, play, pass. The expansion adds new decision points, so expect more moments when a single card changes the entire tone of a round.
- End game: the last kitten standing wins, but with the Streaking Kittens, you’ll witness a few extra “I can’t believe this is happening” moments before that final wipe of laughter.

For those who love a little meta-game: talk about who’s playing “the safe game” versus “the bold game.” The expansion tends to reward bravado at the right moments, but punishes it if you misread the table’s appetite for risk. It’s a balance act with a laser pointer.

### Strategy tips

- Read the room, not just the cards. The humor in Streaking Kittens is as much about timing as it is about card text. If your table is loud and chaotic, lean into it; if it’s a nervous energy lull, you might want to hold back.
- Manage your hand size. There are moments when discarding or exchanging is worth it, and moments when you’ll regret showing a few cards that reveal your strategy long before the final rounds.
- Don’t underestimate the power of a well-timed reveal. The NSFW and reveal cards create tension that can win you a round if timed correctly. The trick is to avoid giving away your hand until you’re certain you’re about to turn the tide.
- Protect your nerves. If you’re playing with a group that loves bold jokes, use it to your advantage. If you’re at a table where delicate sensibilities still exist, keep a sense of humor and consider muting the most explicit elements at the start.
