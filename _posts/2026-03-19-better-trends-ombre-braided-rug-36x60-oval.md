Braided construction, while not designed for the rugged life of a heavy-traffic hallway, holds up well in living rooms and bedrooms with ordinary foot traffic. ### Cleaning and maintenance
Spills happen. The recommended approach for cotton chenille is quick blotting with a clean cloth or paper towel to absorb as much liquid as possible, followed by a gentle cleaning with a mild detergent and water. Avoid harsh solvents that can degrade the fibers. For routine care, a light vacuum works well; go with a setting appropriate for delicate fibers and avoid aggressive suction that might pull the fibers from their braided joints. For stains, consider a spot test on an inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness, though the beige gradient is generally forgiving and less likely to show small accidents than a darker hue.

External care guides emphasize quick action, but the reality is you may forget a spill until hours later. When that happens, patience and a gentle approach will still save you from a major rug catastrophe.
