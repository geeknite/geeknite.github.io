## Overview
Dixit is a party game built on vibes, imagination, and a little chaos. The Complete Collection bundles the base game with its expansions and a handful of extras that feel like a narrative Swiss Army knife—there's a tool for every storytelling occasion, and a few for when you want to tell a story about a penguin who learned to juggle oranges. The vibe is whimsical, with a dash of mischief, and the evenings tend to drift from lighthearted laughter into a creative snowball fight of ideas.

In Dixit, one player is the storyteller and gives a clue about a card in their hand. The other players pick a card they think best matches the clue and secretly submit their choices. All chosen cards are revealed and players vote on which card is the storyteller's. Points flow to players who guessed the storyteller's card or who picked a card that no one else selected. The twist is that a successful clue must be neither too obvious nor too obscure—classic design conundrum, solved by a chorus of whispered interpretations and a chorus of groans when someone hints at mushrooms again.

For new players, the Complete Collection offers a gentle onboarding: a bigger card pool, clearer art-interpretation signals, and a handful of expansions that bring new motifs without turning the game into a rules labyrinth. For seasoned Dixit fans, the collection is a treasure trove of fresh prompts and diverse art styles that remind you why you started playing in the first place: because you wanted to tell improbable stories with your friends and watch the room contort into giggles at every misread clue.

### Setup and Play Time
Setup is delightfully swift. Shuffles, five to seven cards per player, a few tokens, and you’re ready to begin. The actual rounds fly by with the pace depending on the group’s conversational bandwidth. If your table loves deep lore and long metaphors, expect longer storytelling turns; if your group is more of the fast-and-funny type, you’ll glide through a night of rapid-fire clues and quick round conclusions.

On average, plan for 15-30 minutes per game, but don’t be surprised if a single session stretches into a cozy, late-night storytelling marathon. The Complete Collection doesn’t force you to hurry through the content; it invites you to linger in the imagination. And if you’re lucky, the table will spontaneously erupt into a chorus of ridiculous descriptions that you’ll be retelling for weeks.

### Strategy and Replayability
Dixit isn’t a ladder-climbing game where you optimize your win rate through precise card counting. The fun comes from narrative crafting and group dynamics. Some subtle strategies include:
- Keeping clues balanced: enough hints to feel intimate, but not so obvious that everyone nails the answer.
- Reading your table: noticing who tends toward literary language, who leans on nostalgia, and who just wants something silly.
- Pairing cards to the clue: select cards that evoke a shared memory but leave some ambiguity to the audience.

The Complete Collection expands your toolbox. New artwork means new angles for clues, and the expanded deck ensures you’re never staring at the same few images night after night. The variety helps prevent fatigue and keeps the “aha” moment alive, even after multiple replays.

### House Rules and Storyteller Tips
To keep the energy high, consider house rules that preserve flow without dulling creativity:
- Time-box each clue to 60 seconds to keep rounds moving.
- Require at least one card that is truly whimsical or silly to encourage risk-taking.
- Allow a "nudge" mechanic where players can hint if they’re completely lost but not reveal the card.

Storyteller tips:
- Start with a personal hook: tie your clue to a real memory or silly inside joke to create emotional resonance.
- Use metaphor and sensory cues (sound, smell, texture) to broaden the interpretive space.
- Read the room: if players are pulling too literal or too abstract, adapt your clue length and style mid-game.

### Deck Management, Storage, and House Prizes
If you’re the kind who keeps games pristine for show at social gatherings, Dixit benefits from a little care. Keep the cards in their original box or a dedicated deck organizer, and consider a soft cloth to prevent surface scuffs on the artwork. A small prize for the storyteller’s victory (a goofy trophy or a homemade badge) can add a nice social incentive—though beware, this can also encourage escalating prank clues.

### Solo, Two-Player, and Family Play
Dixit works best with a table full of chatty collaborators, but the Complete Collection can accommodate a two-player variant with some house rules. For families, the playful tone and non-violent competition make this a welcome addition to weekend game nights.
