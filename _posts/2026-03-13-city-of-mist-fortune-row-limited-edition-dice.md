## Design and Aesthetics
Aesthetic is the first negotiation point in any limited edition item. Fortune Row leans into three cues: noir chic, arcane glamour, and the tactile luxury you expect from a modern premium dice set. The dice themselves feature glyphs that hint at fortune, misfortune, fate, and a few nods to urban superstition. The color choices tend toward deep jewel tones—emerald greens, midnight blues, and a wine-dark burgundy—that read well on a dark table and pop nicely under a lamp with a touch of amber light.

The engraving compared to standard City of Mist dice is sharper, with cleaner lines that don’t blur after multiple rolls. If you’ve ever worried about etched glyphs wearing down after a night of heavy use, you’ll be reassured by the relatively deep etching and the color fill that appears to resist primer wear. The dice balance feels solid without being lethargic; you don’t get the “rolling a boulder downhill” sensation, but you also don’t feel like you tossed a marble with a fancy sticker on it.

The translucent fate dice are a clever touch. They glow softly in dim lighting, which is perfect for a city that thrives in fog and neon. The juxtaposition of opaque base dice with translucent accents creates a dynamic on-table visual that can help players quickly identify different die types during a tense scene. It’s the small design flourish that matters in a game where narrative tempo matters as much as mechanics.

If you’re the kind of player who notices tactile sensations, you’ll also appreciate the feel of the dice. They aren’t slick, but they aren’t grippy either. The texture is somewhere between satin and suede, which is exactly the vibe of a hero who brushes past a rain-soaked doorway and still manages to look impeccable.

### Jekyll and Hyde: form meets function
From a functional standpoint, the glyphs serve a storytelling purpose. They’re not just decorative; they hint at how the dice should be read within the City of Mist system. The glyphs map onto outcomes in a way that can speed up the flow of a scene if your group uses symbol-based cues during dialogue and investigation. The design respects both the game’s noir aesthetic and the practical need to read dice quickly during tense moments.

## Build Quality and Materials
Materials feel premium without tipping into the absurd. The dice are resin-based, with the glyph fills baked into the surface. This reduces the risk of chipping compared to enamel-inlaid designs. The edges are slightly rounded, which makes rolling less grim when you drop a critical failure on your table—though in City of Mist, even a failure can have narrative flair, so the dice sometimes end up telling a story you didn’t expect.

The dice tray insert that comes with the set is a nice touch. It protects the dice between sessions and offers a little stage for dramatic rolls. If you travel with your game group, this tray and pouch combination can help keep everything organized in a chaotic car ride from convention hall to coffee shop to back alley of your favorite gaming store.

Care is straightforward: wipe with a damp cloth, avoid direct sunlight for long stretches, and resist the urge to use abrasive cleaners to achieve that showroom shine. The last thing you want is a dice set that looks like it has been through a blockbuster action scene and a thousand parking tickets. Fortunate dice should look like you earned them, not like you used them to elude a mob boss.
