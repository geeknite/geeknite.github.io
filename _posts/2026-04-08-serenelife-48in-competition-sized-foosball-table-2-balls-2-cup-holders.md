## Setup and Assembly
If you have ever assembled a piece of furniture with more than four steps and a stubborn hardware bag, you are in for a familiar journey. The Serenelife table divides assembly into a few logical stages:

1) Attach the legs and the frame to create a sturdy base. This is the moment where you decide you either love your neighbors or you only tolerate them for late night foosball sessions. 2) Install the rods and the players, making sure the players are oriented correctly. Misalignment will become obvious the moment you spin the first ball and it does an impression of a runaway comet. 3) Insert the tabletop surface and secure it. 4) Attach the cup holders and ballast items. 5) Perform a quick test run and adjust if the ball tracking feels off.

Overall, the process takes one curious afternoon and a couple of well earned snacks. The hardware is serviceable and the instruction manual is readable, though not exactly a page turning thriller. If you are a veteran of DIY assembly, you will breeze through this. If this is your first rodeo, bring a friend who is good at following diagrams and avoid hastily slamming screws into spaces that clearly belong to a different part.

### Maintenance tips that save your sanity
- Keep the rods clean. A quick wipe down with a microfiber cloth after a game keeps the surface free of smudges and minor fingerprints. 
- Check the screws from time to time. A weekly tighten will prevent wobble and keep your shots precise in the later rounds.
- Clean the surface with a mild cleaner and avoid harsh solvents that could dull the finish. You want longevity more than a glossy shine that wears off after the first party.
- Rotate the balls if you spare a moment to ensure consistent behavior and avoid pick up of grit that can affect ball spin across games.
