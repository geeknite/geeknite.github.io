## How to Play: Core Rules and Flow

No Mercy UNO preserves the skeleton of UNO but with a few spicy mechanics designed to tilt the advantage toward the bold. The objective remains the same: get rid of all your cards. The table consists of a central discard pile and a draw pile. On your turn, you must either play a card that matches the color or the number of the top card, or you draw a card. But here is where the merciless spirit enters: No Mercy modifiers can be played on top of matching cards, often altering the flow in unpredictable ways.

Typical turns follow a rhythm you recognize, but with mutinous twists. For example, you might have a No Mercy card that forces everyone to draw one card unless someone can quickly shout a color change. Another variant might flip the direction of play, then add a penalty for the next player who hesitates before slamming down a card. The exact interactions can vary from game to game depending on how your table chooses to deploy the modifiers, which is part of the charm and the chaos of No Mercy UNO.

The merciful question to trainees who want to optimize is simple: should you embrace aggression and chain together a torrent of draws, or should you lag behind, hoping someone else trips over their own strategy? It punishes hesitation and overthinking with a splash of luck, and that balance is what makes the experience consistently entertaining.
