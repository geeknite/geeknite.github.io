## Assembly and Maintenance
### Assembly Experience
The assembly is approachable for most DIYers, even those who count assembling a bookshelf as cardio. A few key tips to smooth the process:
- Lay out all parts before starting; a quick inventory reduces mid-build surprises.
- Use the included tools for a snug fit but don’t over-tighten; it’s easy to strip a screw if you go full NASA on the first turn.
- Take your time aligning shelves; a crooked shelf is not a feature (no matter what the fold-out memes say).
- Tighten all connections after the unit stands on its feet and the glue (if used) has had a moment to cure.

The result is a stable, plumb bookshelf that won’t wobble when you reach for a notebook or a smartphone charger. If you do stumble on a wobble, the fix is usually simple: adjust the feet, re-tighten a couple of screws, or swap in a little anti-slip pad under one corner. It’s the small, satisfying triumph of the weekend DIY: a completed project that actually improves your desk life.
