## Maintenance: care, cleaning, and longevity
Chips collect oils, dust, and the occasional crumb from late-night snacks during live-streamed games. Cleaning them is straightforward: a quick wipe with a damp cloth followed by air-drying, and a small amount of time under a fan if you’re dealing with a stubborn fingerprint or two. For the case, avoid harsh chemicals that could dull the aluminium finish; a mild soap and water solution followed by a dry microfiber cloth is enough to keep it looking sharp. If you’ve got a heavily used set, a periodic check on the foam insert makes sense to ensure no chips slip out of their slots and the cards stay flat enough to shuffle gracefully.

For the cards, consider a quick shuffle “conditioning” routine: riffle, bridge, and practice—all in a controlled environment rather than in the middle of an actual game where you don’t want to reveal your practice routine. If the cards start showing wear at the edges, it’s probably time to rotate in a spare deck or upgrade to a more durable plastic coating, especially if you’re playing weekly and you want to avoid the “looks like a dog-eared memoir” vibe at the river.
