## Introduction
City of Mist is a game about superheroes with a noir twist, a city where every alley might be a crime scene and every shadow could be a god. If you imagine Sherlock Holmes sharing a rooftop with Hercules and the Downtime Manager from your favorite sitcom, you’re halfway there. In City of Mist, myths are real, masks are business cards, and the city itself seems to have a say in the dice that decide your fate. The new expansion Mysteries of the City (yes, that is the actual title) promises to turn every investigation into an odyssey of clues, motives, and dramatic reveals. And yes, it does so with a wink, a cape, and a not-so-subtle reminder that you cannot bluff your way out of a mythic trap by wearing a clever hat.

## What Mysteries of the City Adds to the Table
Mysteries of the City arrives like a noir detective with a caffeine addiction and a dramatic cape clip snapping in the wind. It leans into the investigative playstyle City of Mist fans adore and turns it into a toolbox you can use over and over without needing to re-rack your jaw from the last dramatic reveal. Here’s the gist of what you get, organized so you can actually find your notes beneath the napkins and coffee cups on the GM desk.

### Theme, Tone, and Narrative Intent
In this expansion, the city itself is less a backdrop and more a character with a crippling fear of coincidences. Clues arrive as if the plot itself is sending you a very polite invitation to a murder party you cannot refuse. The tone remains equal parts gumshoe and mythic epic, but with a sharper focus on urban phenomena—the way a subway tunnel can echo with the whispers of a forgotten god, or how a coffee cart can become a warning system for a vigilante conspiracy. The designers lean into the "city as a living organism" metaphor, but they do not let it smother your agency; you still get to decide which mask you wear, what you believe about your past, and how much honey you want to spill from your mouth before the suspect starts to sweat.

### Mystery Kernels and Hook Layouts
Mysteries of the City organizes investigations into modular kernels—a kernel is the seed of a mystery that can sprout into a full arc with just a few tweaks. Each kernel has a hook, an array of clues, a set of suspects, and a recommended pacing track. This is where City of Mist shines: you can drop a kernel into a campaign and still feel like you’re improvising, not reading a recipe. The expansions provide a suite of ready-to-run intrigues, from cultist cabals that operate as a public health crisis to a rogue myth that manifests as a series of haunting public art installations. The trick is to watch players chase a clue into a place where their own mythos starts to react—your Virtue/Vehemence conflict becomes self-fulfilling prophecy rather than a wall you hit at the end of an hour.

### The GM Toolkit: Tools, Tables, and Tricks
GM-friendly tools are the secret sauce here. There are new GM buffers (things you slide along the table when your plan is collapsing), extra encounter tables for street-level chaos, and a streamlined process to escalate stakes while keeping the detective vibe intact. The wand-waving is replaced by careful paneling of scenes, where you can present a clue, a red herring, and an ethical quandary all within the same chapter. The toolkit also comes with ready-to-go NPCs who aren’t just cardboard cutouts; they have motives that intersect with major city sectors (Harbor, Financial District, Old Quarter) and with each other in ways that are delightfully messy.

##### Visual Aids and Layout
The book’s layout feels like a good board game night with a dash of coffee-stained genius. There are crisp diagrams, maps that double as clue boards, and a color palette that says noir without blinding you with neon. The production quality matches the rest of City of Mist line-up, which is to say: it’s handsome enough to justify showing it in your game group, but not so precious that you won’t throw it across the room when your investigations go off the rails.

### Mechanic Deep Dive: How It Plays
City of Mist uses a dice-pool system built from generic pool values (the City Dice) and character Tags that describe your abilities and the role you play in the mythic tapestry. Mysteries of the City expands this with new Tags that are specifically tied to investigations: forensic isometric analysis of crime scenes, urban legends that morph into logic, and a handful of “epilogue” tags that trigger after the final clue is found. The net effect is a more forgiving system for new players yet a deeper, more satisfying reward curve for veterans who like to chase thematic patterns through a maze of bullet points and footnotes.

Below are some key mechanic notes to keep in mind as you plan sessions:
- Clue Tracks: A new, flexible way to manage information flow. Clues can be acquired, linked, and resolved in ways that feel cinematic but remain trackable on a turn-by-turn basis.
- Mythos and Gears: Your masks and gadgets get new options and costs. The expansion doesn’t reveal too many new numbers, but it does expand your storytelling vocabulary—you can connect a mythic power to a mundane item in a way that makes the item feel legendary.
- Facade and Social Mechanics: The social layer is deepened with new prompts that test a character’s public image and how it interacts with the city’s mythos. This leads to more dramatic reveals and more awkward consequences when a public figure is discovered to be more than they seem.
- Factional Tides: Conflicts between city factions now have mechanical teeth. This means if your party stirs up trouble in the Harbor, you’re likely to face both police suspicion and a whisper network of smugglers who owe their overlord a favor.

### Play Experience: Tone, Pace, and Player Agency
The expansion leans into the detective process as a main engine of story rather than a rescue mission for your favorite hero moments. It’s possible to run a campaign that feels tightly plotted, with a clear investigative spine, while still allowing room for spontaneous mythic reveals that spring from players’ decisions. The best sessions feel like a puzzle with a clock—the clues arrive at just the right speed, so players get a sense of progress without the looming specter of “oh we’re stuck here for three sessions.” The worst sessions feel like you built a puzzle from a magazine cutout and forgot to glue the edges—the clues exist, but the players don’t quite see how they connect because the pacing didn’t coax them forward.

### Artwork, Accessibility, and the Hobby Experience
As much as this is a rules expansion, Mysteries of the City is an artifact for the hobby. The illustrations lean noir with hints of mythic grandeur, and the layout guides the eye with the same care you might expect from a well-designed mystery novel. Accessibility is decent; there are notes to help new GMs run investigations, but the book still expects players to engage with a fairly dense, Lovecraftian-sci-fi-noir vibe. If you love reading old detective novels while planning a heist with a shambling god as your partner, you’ll feel right at home. If you’re more of a surface-level gamer, you’ll still appreciate the toolkit—just brace yourself for a few extra pages of himbos and gods who don’t want to share their cookies.

## How It Compares to Other City of Mist Supplements
No single expansion has to be the final word on a game this stylish. Mysteries of the City slots into the line by focusing on investigations and city-scale intrigue rather than altering core mechanics drastically. Other supplements might broaden power options or add new masks; this one leans into the detective calendar, making sessions feel like a televised noir miniseries with an epic mythic overlay. If you’re deciding between this and a different add-on, ask yourself: do you want more table-time for investigation, or more table-time for punchy hero moments? If it’s the former, Mysteries fits like a glove.

## Campaign Design: Build Your Night One with Confidence
A big part of why Mysteries of the City works is its emphasis on starting a campaign with a solid night-one structure. You don’t want to throw players into a labyrinth of clues without a map, but you also don’t want to spoon-feed them a ready-made path that makes every session feel like a ride at a theme park. The recommended approach is to begin with a tight, high-emotion event that ties directly to a local location, then escalate by introducing new nodes in the city that are connected to each other through a web of shared secrets. The design notes encourage you to map out a few “beat sheets”—short outlines that describe what the players will experience in the first three acts, the central mystery, and the possible epilogue that will matter if they succeed (or fail) at the final confrontation.

This approach rewards players who pay attention to what the city is whispering to them. It also rewards GMs who are comfortable weaving together multiple threads: a small crime wave, a mythic call that the players cannot ignore, and a public crisis that demands a dramatic reveal. In practice, you’ll find yourself juggling NPC relationships, shifting clues, and the occasional deus ex machina that feels earned rather than forced—because in City of Mist, the gods don’t always show up with thunder; sometimes they arrive via a barista who has the exact right ricochet of coffee beans to trigger a memory.

## Running Mysteries: Night One Scenarios
To help you hit the ground running, here are two ready-to-run night-one frames that emphasize investigation, character growth, and a splash of mythic spectacle. You can adapt these as kernels or run them almost as-is if your group wants quick satisfaction.

### Night One Frame A: The Whispering Neon
Plot Hook: A string of streetlamps flicker out in a pattern that resembles a rune known only to a vanished street preacher. People report strange visions near the old theater while a local gang tries to control the narrative by flooding social media with unsettling footage.
Key Clues: A burned theater seat with a residue that resembles old ceremonial wax; a business card from a bar that disappeared from the city directory; a mural that shifts its message when viewed through a phone camera.
NPCs: The Night Editor (a journalist with a talent for finding the city’s hidden stories), The Fence of Sable Street (a dealer in rumors), The Whispering Apothecary (a medical mage who can read memories).
Outcome: The party uncovers a myth-bound conspiracy that uses the theater as a ritual site to bargain with a city god. If they intervene, they risk enraging the god or awakening a more benevolent aspect of the myth hidden within the crowd.

### Night One Frame B: Beneath the Green Grid
Plot Hook: Power outages ripple through a district as a mysterious green glow crawls across power lines. A public transit strike coincides with a surge in disappearances, and a tech startup claims it has built a device to map the city’s dreams.
Key Clues: A prototype device left behind by a frantic engineer; a shipment manifest for a rare mineral used in mythic gear; a graffiti tag that shifts its meaning when sounded aloud.
NPCs: The City Whisperer (a mythic presence that communicates through urban phenomena), The Velvet Underground (a faction of artists who weaponize myths through performance art), The Night Editor’s rival journalist.
Outcome: Investigations reveal a conspiracy to weaponize dream data, turning the city into a feedback loop where heroes become the heroes the city deserves—and the villains deserve, too.

## NPC Profiles for Mysteries of the City
Here are three example NPCs you can drop into your sessions right away. Each comes with a one-page hook, a motive tie-in to a kernel, and a suggested scene you can slot into any night with minimal prep.

- The Night Editor: a veteran journalist who knows more about the city’s shadows than the police, and has a habit of writing people into existence with her articles.
- The Whispering Apothecary: a strange shopkeeper who inventories memory-flowers and memory-harvests, capable of numbing pain or amplifying fear depending on who pays.
- The Fence of Sable Street: a mediator of secrets who can connect you to a higher tier of information but who always demands a steep price—the truth about your own past included.

## Customization and House Rules
The expansion invites you to tune the math, not just the mood. Here are a few optional rules that can help you tailor Mysteries of the City to your table:
- Clue Resolution Tokens: Instead of immediate reveals, players collect tokens that can unlock a larger reveal later in the arc. This keeps tension high across sessions.
- Street-Level XP: Instead of raw point totals, award XP for roleplay, creative interpretation of a clue, and effective use of a major Tag. This emphasizes storytelling over mere dice rolling.
- Consent-based Mythic Surprises: If a player declines a specific mythic reveal, offer an alternative path that preserves agency while still providing thematic payoff.

## Running Mysteries: A Quick Refresher
- Start with a strong Scene 1 that links to a local setting: theater, subway, bar, or cemetery. 
- Introduce a kernel and one or two clues that feel connected but not obvious.
- Use the city itself as a source of drama: crowd behavior, police chatter, graffiti moods, and ambient myths.
- Escalate by weaving in multiple suspects whose motives intersect with the players’ pasts.
- End the session with a choice: a dramatic reveal that could change the city’s myth, or a quiet victory that leaves room for a larger arc.

## Artwork, Accessibility, and the Hobby Experience (Revisited)
The visual package remains strong, with art that feels like it could exist in a parallel noir universe where gods ride on subway lines. Color coding helps you identify clues and scenes at a glance, reducing cognitive load during tense moments. Accessibility notes help new GMs avoid getting lost in the labyrinth of myths and mechanics, though the density remains real. The book respects the intelligence of both new players and veterans, and it shows in the way it spaces out sections, offers quick-start ideas, and provides a sense of forward momentum that makes you want to plan the next night immediately.

## The Final Judgment: Who Should Buy This
If you crave gloom and wonder in equal measure and you want a toolkit that makes every night feel like a late-night radio drama broadcast from a city that never sleeps, Mysteries of the City is a potent addition. It’s especially good for groups that enjoy long-form investigations and a mythic overlay that never feels gimmicky. If your table prefers brawler moments or heavy crunch, you’ll still find value in the GM toolkit and the new kernel structures, but you might prefer to borrow ideas rather than run every session straight out of the book.

Pros:
- Strong focus on investigations without sacrificing mythic flavor
- Modular kernels let you scale from one-shot to multi-session arcs
- Rich GM toolkit with pacing guidance and NPC templates
- Gorgeous layout and production value with practical play aids

Cons:
- Dense material may overwhelm absolute beginners
- Some players may want more combat-focused content, which this expansion deprioritizes
- A few ideas assume familiarity with the core world and recurring motifs

## Final Recommendation (Bottom Line)
Mysteries of the City is a love letter to the investigative soul of City of Mist fans. It gives you systems you can lean on without muting your players’ imagination. It respects the noir pulse while letting mythic energy surge through the story like a train that won’t stop for a red light. If you want to run a campaign that feels cinematic, layered, and a little chaotic—in a good way—this expansion is worth bringing to the table. If you already own the core book and crave more nights where the city itself seems to conspire with your party, you’ll thank the designers for this set.
