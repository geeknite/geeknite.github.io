## Mechanics in a Nutshell: How It Plays

Monopoly Deal is a card-drafting, hand-management game. You start with a small hand, draw cards, and use those cards to assemble property sets, block rent with action cards, and occasionally steal someone’s hard-earned hard-pressed properties. The Limited Edition doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it refines the surface. Expect:

- Property cards in themed color sets that you’ll want to collect as you chase a three-set victory.
- Rent cards that charge opponents based on how many properties of a given color set they own.
- Action cards like Deal, Sly Deal, and Forced Deal that let you swap or steal properties, skipping turns, or otherwise causing chaos.
- Wildcards that can be used as any property color, turning a near-miss into a dramatic, edge-of-seat moment.

The core loop is simple: draw two cards, play up to three cards, and try to keep or disrupt property sets. The Limited Edition adds a few extra action cards and more collectible properties to push the game into a bigger, bolder moment of table drama.

### Setup and Turn Structure
1. Shuffle the deck and deal five cards to each player.
2. Place the remaining deck in the center and flip a few to keep the stock fresh for early draws.
3. The first player is chosen by the usual audience of bored coworkers and enthusiastic unicorn enthusiasts—whichever you agree on, basically.

On a turn, you draw two cards, then play up to three cards from your hand. If you have more than seven cards at the end of your turn, you discard down to seven. And yes, in a world where your coffee cup is your only source of energy, seven cards feel like a suspiciously perfect number for a modular chaos engine.
