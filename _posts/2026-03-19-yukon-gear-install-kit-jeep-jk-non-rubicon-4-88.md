## Installation notes: common pitfalls and tips
- Pinion depth matters: Too shallow or too deep will burn the gears. You’ll know you’re in the sweet spot when you see consistent backlash measurements and a healthy, no-slack mesh. Use a dial indicator and verify several backlashes at multiple spots on the ring gear.
- Bearings matter: Fresh bearings reduce noise, heat, and premature wear. Don’t reuse old bearings unless you enjoy squeaks and constant adjustments.
- Proper lubrication: Use the recommended gear oil with the right viscosity. It’s not just about a good splash; it’s about staying in spec across temperature swings.
- Confirm fitment with your axle type: If you have a Dana 30 front or a Dana 35/44 rear, verify you’re using the correct carrier and shims. The kit is not a one-size-fits-all magic wand; it’s a precision instrument.
- Lockers and upgrades: If you’re running a locker, confirm if you need a different carrier or extra spacers. Some kit variants assume a standard carrier and may require an aftermarket solution if you’re using a locker that changes the internal geometry.
