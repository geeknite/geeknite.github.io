## Maintenance and Cleaning
### Routine Care
- Vacuum regularly with a low suction or without the beater bar to protect the fibers.
- Rotate the rug every 3‑6 months to promote even wear across high‑traffic zones.
- Use a rug pad to improve stability and reduce bunching under furniture.
- Address spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing; blotting helps prevent fiber crush and color migration.

### Stain and Spill Management
Wool generally tolerates spills well when treated quickly. For common culprits like coffee, wine, or curry sauce, start with blotting up the liquid, then apply a mild detergent solution (water and a gentle wool-safe cleaner) to a white cloth and dab—don’t rub—until the stain lifts. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure dye stability. If you’re dealing with more stubborn stains, a professional carpet cleaning service is a wise route; wool is resilient but not magic.

### Cleaning Compatibility and Service Life
Avoid steam cleaning or high‑moisture cleaning on wool rugs, as it can cause color bleed or backing damage over time. If you have a pet with occasional accidents, acting quickly minimizes odor and stain saturation. With proper care, a handmade wool rug like Cambridge Philomene can last many years, maintaining its structure and feel as if it were a tiny victory of craftsmanship in your living space.
