## What to look for when buying a poker chip set
If you’re in the market again in a few years, here are a few things to consider. First, weight matters: 10-11.5g chips are common for home-use sets and resemble casino chips closely enough to feel authentic during play. Second, the material: composite blends produce satisfying weight and edge texture; pure clay is expensive and brittle. Third, the case: aluminum is both durable and light; avoid sets with flimsy plastic boxes or cases that show cracks after a couple of drops. Fourth, color coding and denomination distribution: ensure you’re comfortable with the color mapping; if not, you could re-label or re-pack chips, but this is time-consuming and may upset purists. Fifth, included accessories: some sets include dealer button, plastic card shufflers, or a deck of cards; depending on your needs, you might want to choose a set that includes these extras or buy them separately.

For those who want to run a quick test before committing: weigh a chip on a kitchen scale (no judgment here), feel the edge for grip, and check the color for readability from across a standard 78-inch table. If all these checks pass with your threshold for \"professional vibe\" set by your own standards, you’ll likely be satisfied with a purchase that lasts through many nights of cards and comedic bluffs.
