## How to play: Rules, rhythm, and rapid-fire chaos
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll recognize the skeleton of the rules. If you haven’t, relax: we’re about to break the ice with a sledgehammer covered in glitter. The core flow remains similar: you draw cards, you avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten, and you try to force others into risky situations with action cards. The zombie edition adds a mutation mechanic that ramps up the tension by letting you upgrade your kittens’ abilities as the deck evolves. Here’s a quick, spoiler-light walkthrough:

- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a handful of cards to each player, give each kitten a tiny bit of courage and a decent amount of denial. Place the lantern tokens at the center. The “evolve” side of the tokens becomes active as soon as the first mutation hits.
- Turn structure: On your turn you may do up to two actions: drawing a card, playing a card, or using a mutation that has become available. The mutations are the interesting bit—these aren’t simply attacks. They alter how you can chain combos, how your cards interact, and whether you’re allowed to pass the mutation to the next player.
- Exploding Kittens: The core mechanic remains a bomb-timing joke: you don’t want to draw the Exploding Kitten card. If you do, you can mitigate with a Defuse card or a specific zombie-kitten shield. The 2022 edition adds a few “resurrection” options that can resurrect a fallen kitten under very particular circumstances. It’s not cheating; it’s undead resource management.
- Chaos cards: The new cards can reverse, steal, or randomly mutate someone else’s hand. These moments tend to be the ones your friends remember fondly (and then beg you not to repeat at Thanksgiving).
- Endgame: The last kitten standing (or last kitten smiling) wins. The game moves quickly enough that no one has time to overthink their regrets, which is exactly how a party game should behave.

The evolution mechanic is the standout feature for me. You’re constantly deciding between a safe but dull path and a riskier line that could yield a dramatic swing in your favor—or plunge you into a pile of cabbages (figuratively, we hope). The 2022 edition tightens the pacing without sacrificing the humor. The rule changes aren’t heavy-handed; they’re like “gentle memes” that nudge you toward more satisfying misplays.

## Tactics and tips: maximizing laughs and minimizing rage
Here are some practical ideas to tilt your table toward hilarity rather than heartbreak:

- Embrace the chaos: The mutation tokens reward aggressive plays, so don’t be afraid to go all-in when you’re sitting on a couple of safe cards. If your friends aren’t screaming with laughter by turn three, you’re probably not trying hard enough.
- Teach by example: Demonstrate a smart move early on to show others how the card combinations work. You’ll reduce table talk time and increase everyone’s confidence in their own ability to misplay with style.
- Tempo matters: If you can steal or force a mutation onto a player who’s currently toying with a fragile plan, you’ll create space for a dramatic comeback or a glorious collapse. Either way, it’s theater, and theater is cheap on the heart but rich on memory.
- Spite play: It’s okay to target the player who most deserves a little culinary chaos. The table respects a well-timed disruption as long as it’s funny and not personal. Remember: you’re all adults with the collective sense of humor of twelve-year-olds—this is normal.
- Endgame awareness: In the final rounds, calculate who has the most outs and who’s most likely to blow up. The person with the best swagger and a hand full of last-minute rescue cards usually wins, but the last laugh belongs to whoever can say, with confidence, the exact wording of the rule that just saved them.

## Setup tips for a smooth game night
- Clear the playing surface and designate a “litter box” discard area to avoid card pile chaos.
