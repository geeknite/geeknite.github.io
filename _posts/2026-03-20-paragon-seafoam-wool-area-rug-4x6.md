## Durability, Cleaning, and Care

Durability-wise, Paragon seems to have baked in a little extra patience for rug life. Wool fibers naturally resist wear, and the Seafoam colorway doesn’t show everything—dirt tends to be less obvious than on a solid white or pale gray, which is a stealth win for busy geeks who forget to vacuum between sessions.

Care tips in Geeknite fashion:

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting. Avoid high power on delicate wool, or you’ll turn your rug into a charging bull. 
- Blot spills quickly with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fiber. If you’ve spilled coffee while code compiling, you’ll be glad you wore that extra pair of socks—your carpet will still look calm and collected.
- For deeper stains, consider a wool-safe cleaner. Test a small area first, because even wool colorways deserve a fair trial before you ruin a good vibe.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and prevent localized flattening in high-traffic zones. It’s like rotating your swag in a cosplay closet—keep it fresh.

A quick note on shedding: wool sheds a little at first in many cases, but as you continue to use it, shedding tends to subside. If you’re neurotically inventorying every fiber, you’ll notice a few loose fibers for the first several weeks, then stability returns. If the shedding becomes stubborn, a light brush across the nap can help align the fibers and reduce loose hairs.

## Practical Tips and Quick-Start Guide

- Underlay vs. padding: A thin rug pad under the rug can improve grip and prolong wear, especially under wheeled office chairs. It also reduces creeping and makes chair movement smoother.
- Edge protection: If you’re placing the rug in a high-traffic doorway, consider a protective door mat at the entrance to reduce grit and dust that can wear the wool fibers faster.
- Layering: For a layered look in a modern room, put a smaller, solid-color rug (in complementary tones) under a portion of the Paragon Seafoam rug to create a curated, layered effect.
- Lighting: Pair your rug with adjustable lighting so you can switch from bright “work mode” to warm, cinematic “game night” vibes without changing the rug itself.
- Cleaning: Vacuum gently; avoid aggressive suction that can pull fibers loose, especially on the edges where wear tends to show first.
