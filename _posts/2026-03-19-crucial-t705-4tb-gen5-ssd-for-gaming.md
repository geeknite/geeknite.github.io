## Thermal behavior and cooling considerations

Gen5 drives run hot, especially under sustained IO. If you absolutely must push sustained performance—for example, streaming several gigabytes of assets at max texture quality while recording gameplay—you’ll want a robust cooling solution:

- A good M.2 heatsink is worth the investment. It helps maintain sustained speeds and avoids thermal throttling.
- Case airflow matters: front intake plus rear or top exhaust helps move heat away from the drive area.
- Some motherboards include an integrated M.2 heatsink; if yours doesn’t, add one or mount the drive with extra clearance to allow heat dissipation.

During testing, we saw rapid temperatures climb when the drive was left to chase long IO sequences with minimal cooling. With a proper heatsink and decent airflow, temperatures stayed in a comfortable zone and the drive maintained its speed longer. If you’re planning a compact PC or a small form factor build, pay special attention to cooling, because you’ll likely need it.

## Compatibility and build considerations

- Motherboard support: Ensure your motherboard supports PCIe 5.0 and has enough PCIe lanes for your GPU and other NVMe devices. Some boards share lanes and can throttle if the topology isn’t ideal.
- Heatsinking: An M.2 heatsink is highly recommended for Gen5 drives, especially in laptops or small form factors.
- Power: No dramatic power draw, but you want stable power delivery for peak performance.
- OS: Windows 10/11 and modern Linux distributions handle NVMe drives well; enable TRIM and consider enabling AHCI/NVMe optimizations in BIOS.
- Dual-use: You can place OS on a smaller NVMe and keep your game library on the T705; or you can put both OS and games on it if you have a large enough drive array and a robust system.

If you’re building a new system or upgrading an existing one, the T705 is a compelling option that balances capacity and performance without forcing you into a tiny 1TB drive you’ll outgrow in a week.

Real-world note: always compare price-per-gigabyte rather than just looking at the headline speed. Gen5 drives are fast, but you’ll glean better value by comparing the total package—capacity, price, warranty, and your cooling solution. In Geeknite terms, it’s not only what speed you get, but whether you’ll still be able to store your entire backlog of games without a storage sprint mid-year.

## Gen5 vs Gen4: the upgrade decision

- Speed: Gen5 wins in sustained performance, especially under heavy IO and large texture streaming workloads.
- Stability: Gen5 controllers and firmware have matured, but proper cooling remains critical for sustained performance.
- Price: Gen5 drives typically carry a premium; weigh the capacity you need against your budget and the size of your library.
- Compatibility: If you’re upgrading a Gen4 system, ensure your motherboard and BIOS are up to date and that you can actually extract Gen5 speeds via PCIe 5.0.

If you’re already running Gen4 and your library is manageable, a Gen5 upgrade might not be essential. If you’re building a new rig or want to future-proof your setup for the next few years, the T705 4TB Gen5 is worth serious consideration.
