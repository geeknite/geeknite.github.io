## Painting and display tips
The Miguel Coimbra line rewards a patient painter who wants to coax the best from their brush. Here are a few practical tips to maximize your results:

- Start with a dark base coat to bring out the dramatic shadows concealed in the sculpt’s folds and creases. They were designed to look dramatic under low lighting conditions, so a strong base helps your highlight work later.
- Use a mid-tone gray or blue for the metal parts to give a slightly cooler, more cinematic look. Warm golds can clash with the overall mood if you go too flashy.
- Dry brushing can help reveal the textures on fabric and armor without turning the mini into a wall of glitter. Be selective; you want the raised edges to pop, not the entire surface.
- A wash that mimics resin or oil-based shading can add depth to the recesses. If you are color-challenged, a simple black wash can often yield a surprisingly dramatic result.
- Varnish choice matters. A satin finish tends to photograph well on tabletop, giving you a balance between the painterly look and the practical durability during play.

## Assembly tips for folks who dislike chaos
If you dread miniature assembly, you will be glad to know this set is not a nightmare to assemble, but it does require patience. Here are practical steps to keep things smooth:

- Dry-fit first. Before cementing any part, try male-to-female connections to ensure you get the alignment right. This reduces the frustration of removing an overzealous plastic nub later.
- Trim the flash with a sharp hobby knife. Coimbra minis have fine lines in textures that can trap glue film if you go too fast. A quick careful trim ensures clean edges.
- Use a good quality glue or super glue designed for miniatures. A little goes a long way, and you want clean joints rather than wobbly limbs that detach during the first mission.
- Prime with a neutral color; black primer often works best for figures with dramatic shadows. White primers can highlight mistakes, especially on busy textures.
- Paint in layers, starting with base color, shading, and then highlights. The sculpt details call for a careful approach to bring the mood across without overdoing it with edge highlights.

If you want a fail-safe approach, consider pre-painting the models with a simple two-tone scheme and then finishing with washes and dry brushing. It looks striking but stays accessible for hobbyists who want results without a full multi-day painting campaign.
