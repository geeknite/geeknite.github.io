Cable drama is real, and this setup aims to minimize it. ## Setup, Maintenance, and Real-World Use

### Assembly timeline and tips

Expect a two-stage reality: first, the mental image of a sleek, sci-fi workstation; second, the reality of a 4–8 hour assembly day depending on team size and coffee breaks. A well-organized site with two people dedicated to assembly can halve the time. We recommend a slow-and-steady approach, labeling screws, laying out parts in sequence, and trying to avoid the classic “just one more piece” pitfall.

Tips for a smoother process:

- Pre-sort all hardware by size and type.
- Use a level to ensure the surface is flat before attaching panels.
- Don’t overtighten; it’s a desk, not a bicycle frame. Over-tightening can warp the panels or create annoying gaps at the joints.
- Keep a spare hour of clean-up at the end for cable tidy-up and to avoid stepping on a stray Allen wrench in the dark.

### Maintenance and longevity

The white finish will show fingerprints and dust more than a darker surface. Regular wiping with a microfiber cloth keeps it looking pristine. If you have access to a gentle furniture cleaner, use it sparingly—harsh chemicals can degrade the laminate edges over time. The modular nature of the desk means you can swap out a panel or add an accessory without disassembling the entire setup, which is nice when you want to reconfigure the space for a new project sprint or a sudden personality shift among your team members.

Durability-wise, the construction feels sturdy enough to withstand daily office life and occasional group huddles around a screen. It’s not a product designed for heavy abuse, but it’s robust enough for a busy corporate environment where people sometimes forget to be gentle with the furniture when they’re trying to explain their latest feature to a teammate.
