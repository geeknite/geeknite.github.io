## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Real-Life Use

Cleaning a rug this long requires a little strategy. Here is how I put the NTRL CRM through its paces:
- Daily care: Vacuum often with a beater bar turned to a gentle setting to avoid knitting the pile into a tuft. The rug responds well to regular suction and has a surprisingly memory-friendly pile, bouncing back after vacuum passes.
- Stains: For spill incidents on the surface, blot the stain promptly with a clean cloth. Avoid scrubbing aggressively; instead, use a mild detergent solution. The polymer barrier helps, but this is not a magic wand. We still recommend prompt action.
- Deep cleaning: Periodic professional cleaning is advised for long-term upkeep, especially in high-traffic areas, but you can also do a DIY shampoo with low-foam cleaners. Always test a small inconspicuous patch first other experiments on the main stage, folks.
- Drying time: In typical indoor conditions, expect a 6-12 hour drying window for surface spills. In humid climates, you may see longer. A fan helps, because nothing says I love you like airflow on a freshly cleaned rug.

To keep things fresh, you can pair the rug with a lightweight underlay in high-traffic zones. The underlay reduces bunching and makes vacuuming more efficient by preventing the rug from creeping toward the kitchen like a sneaky raccoon.
