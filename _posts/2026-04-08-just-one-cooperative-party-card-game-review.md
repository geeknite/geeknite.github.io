## What is Just One?
Just One is a cooperative party word game designed by Ludovic Maublanc and published by Repos Production. The premise is delightfully simple: one player is the guesser for each round, while the other players write a single word clue on their whiteboard or piece of notepaper. The catch is that if two or more players write the same clue, those duplicates are hidden from the guesser. The goal is for the guesser to deduce the target word based on the unique clues presented. If the guesser gets the word right, the team earns points; if not, no points, but the group still has a blast.

The game shines in its concept because it leverages the social glue of a group. The moment the clues appear, the room shifts from tense strategy to playful interpretation. A single word can be an inside joke, a random association, or a clever wink at a shared experience. The variability of the clues is where the magic happens. Your teammates might write the word that seems obvious to them, only to have the guesser accidentally dismiss it because it feels too close to an inside joke. The tension is delicious, the relief is real, and the result is usually a chorus of laughter.

## How to Play: A Quick Overview
The core loop of Just One fits neatly into any 20 to 30 minute window. Here is the flavor of a typical round:

1) The guesser draws a card and reads the word silently. The word is the target for the round.
2) On the count of go, all other players write a single clue on their boards. The clues must be one word and must be unique in the sense that duplicates will be removed if they match another clue.
3) The clue givers pass their boards to the guesser. Duplicates vanish from the guesser view, leaving a clean set of unique clues.
4) The guesser studies the clues and attempts to deduce the target word. If they guess correctly, the team earns a point; if they miss, the round is a miss but a learning opportunity in disguise.
5) A new round begins with a new guesser, and the process repeats. The game can end after a pre-set number of rounds or after a rotation of guessers.

The design nuance here is in the one word constraints and the removal of duplicates. The one word limit pushes players toward clever associations and sometimes ridiculous leaps of logic. Duplicates are not merely tolerated; they are actively punished in the sense that they vanish from view, forcing players to think beyond the obvious. This mechanic is what creates that signature Just One moment when a clue seems perfect, only to realize another clue holder wrote exactly the same thing, and the clue disappears just as the guesser is about to lock in on it. The result is a delightful scramble to reinterpret the remaining clues.

If your group wants to ease into the vibe, you can start with a low number of clue givers per round and gradually increase. If you crave chaos, rotate the guesser frequently, which is the fastest way to coax out those wow reactions and hilarious misreads. The balance between constraint and creativity is the real star here.

## Gameplay Depth and Replayability
Despite its light footprint, Just One offers substantial replayability. The pool of words is large enough that you can run many sessions without repeating the same target word too quickly. The novelty of unique clues keeps the rounds fresh, and the rotating guesser ensures everyone has a voice. The base game shines with different group sizes; it scales gracefully from a couple of players to a larger party, though the pace naturally shifts with more participants. For larger groups, you may lean into shorter rounds or allow more players to clue per round to keep things moving without losing the comedic cadence.

That said, some players crave deeper strategy or longer campaigns. Just One is not designed as a campaign game; it thrives on ephemeral moments and the joy of a single round that lands rather than a long thread of connected decisions. For many folks, that is precisely the appeal. If you enjoy expansions or variants, you can explore custom house rules that emphasize specific themes or tailor the one-word constraint to suit your group’s humor style. The key to extending its life is experimentation: switch up the number of clues per round, allow thematic word pools, or mix in a few targeted rounds where clues must be related to a category like travel, food, or pop culture.
