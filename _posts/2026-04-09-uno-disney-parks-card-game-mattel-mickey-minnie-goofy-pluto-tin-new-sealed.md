### Setting Up: Quick Start Rules
To get you from first glance to hysterical second glance, here is a quick start guide that mirrors UNO rules while leaning into the Disney theming:

- Shuffle the deck and deal 7 cards to each player.
- Place the remainder in a draw pile, flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- On your turn, match a card by number, color, or Disney character motif. If you cannot, draw a card. If that card can be played, you may play it immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.
- Special action cards work as per UNO: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, Wild Draw Four. Expect some Disney specific flavor text and associated art on those cards to add a touch of whimsy to each move.
- When you have one card left, yell Uno. If you do not, draw two as a punishment that your friends will lovingly to evil laugh about.
- The first player to get rid of all cards wins the hand. The rest of the table tallies points according to standard UNO rules, with some whimsical Disney modifiers based on who has the most characters left in their hand.

If you want to check the official baseline while we are here, UNO rules are evergreen and available through many sources; for some background, you can skim the canonical overview at https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNO. But you are here for the visuals and the fun; the rules are the same, just with more magic in the artwork.

The Disney Parks edition is UNO at its core, with a few light modifications that honor the theme without destabilizing the flow of the game. Expect to see:
