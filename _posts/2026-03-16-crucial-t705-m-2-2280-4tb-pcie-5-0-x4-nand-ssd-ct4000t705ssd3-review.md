## Thermal behavior and power profile

A high-speed drive like the T705 does not run hot by default, but sustained workloads can push temperatures higher than a casual gaming session would. In our testing with a standard air-cooled desktop, the drive hovered in the mid 50s C during light to moderate workloads and climbed into the high 60s C under long, heavy sequential writes. With a capable heatsink or chassis with improved airflow, you can keep thermal throttling at bay and preserve peak throughput for longer stretches.

Power draw tracks with activity. Idle power is minimal, but active transfers spike up, which is to be expected for a high-performance NVMe drive. If you are building a laptop with tight power envelopes, expect the drive to perform well but not necessarily hit peak TBW ratings during every session if the cooling solution is marginal.

## Installation tips and setup quick guide

Installing the T705 is straightforward:
- Power down, unplug, and ground yourself
- Locate an available M.2 slot that supports PCIe NVMe
- Insert the drive with the notch aligned correctly and secure with the screw
- Boot and enter your motherboard firmware to verify the drive is detected
- Initialize and format in your OS of choice (NTFS or exFAT for Windows; ext4 or btrfs for Linux, depending on your workflow)
- Update firmware if available, then run your preferred benchmarking software or clone utility to begin using the drive

In Linux, you may want to enable TRIM and periodic garbage collection for sustained performance. In Windows, enable rapid storage or equivalent NVMe optimization tools if your platform provides them.
