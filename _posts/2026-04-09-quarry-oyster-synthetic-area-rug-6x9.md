## Practicality: Cleaning, Maintenance, and Real-Life Scenarios
### Daily Use and Spills
Spills happen. It’s the universal truth of human civilization. The Quarry Oyster is well-suited to quick cleanups. Blot from the outside of the spill toward the center to prevent it from wicking deeper into the fibers. For more stubborn stains, mild detergents or specialized rug cleaners can help. Always test a small inconspicuous area first to avoid any color-shifting surprises. The synthetic fiber is less prone to staining than a natural wool rug, which is nice because your coffee addiction is not going anywhere anytime soon.
### Pet Hair and Cleanliness
If you share your house with a fur-magnet cat, a dog that treats the rug like a launchpad, or a family member who sheds as if auditioning for a snow globe commercial, you’ll appreciate how well things clean up. The short, dense pile resists embedded hair when used with a standard vacuum. A quick pass with a broom or vacuum helps keep it looking crisp between deeper cleans. For stubborn hair, a damp microfiber cloth can pick up the recalcitrant strands without pulling fibers loose.
### Routine Care and Longevity
Routine maintenance matters more than a single heroic cleaning session. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones. Use a rug pad to extend life and reduce movement; this saves you from tripping over the couch legs and from that existential moment when you realize you’ve become a rug-tripping hazard for your own family. Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to protect the fibers, and avoid high heat during any cleaning process—polypropylene hates to be roasted.
