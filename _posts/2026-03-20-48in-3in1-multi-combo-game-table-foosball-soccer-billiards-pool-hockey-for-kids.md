## Durability, Maintenance & Longevity

Like any kid-friendly gear, you want something that can endure at least a few years of enthusiastic play. The 48-inch table is built with a reasonable expectation of rough handling and teen curiosity. Some quick maintenance tips:

- Wipe down surfaces after use with a damp cloth to prevent sticky residue from popsicles and snack dust clogging the ball pockets. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the laminate.
- Check screws and bolts every few weeks. Kids may slam the foosball rods with reckless abandon, which can loosen fittings over time. A simple wrench and an occasional drop of lubricant on moving parts can go a long way toward longevity.
- Clean the ball return channels and pockets to keep balls from getting stuck or misaligned. A small brush is enough to keep the channels clean, minimizing the chance of a ball snag mid-flight. 
- Inspect the rails for wear. If you notice a misalignment, stops along edges will appear and you’ll need to re-seat the rail to prevent the ball from popping out unexpectedly.

Durability expectations are good but not exceptional. This is a mass-market product that tries to be a Swiss Army knife of games. It’s not going to feel like a premium arcade cab, but it will handle the rough-and-tumble life of kids, and the best part is that it invites family participation rather than solitary screen time.
