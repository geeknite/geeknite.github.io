## Setup, Maintenance, and Care Tips

- Set up is instant: open the case, place the chips in a familiar two-to-one color distribution, shuffle the cards, and you’re ready to roll. 
- Keep chips away from direct sunlight to avoid color fading—this is a common-sense tip, but one that pays huge dividends if you’re hosting long sessions. 
- If the foams start to sag, rotate the chips occasionally to keep them evenly padded and prevent pressure marks. It sounds silly, but those small indentations can become headaches later when you’re trying to count a stack under the party lights.
- Clean chips with a soft cloth if they pick up dust or kitchen crumbs from snacks. Do not use harsh cleaners; you’re not restoring an antique coin collection here, you’re maintaining a game night’s vibe.
- Card care: if you’ve got kids or clumsy enthusiasts, consider a card protector or a simple rule about not bending cards during dramatic bluffing moments. It helps maintain the deck’s edge and reduces card exchange friction during high-intensity hands.
