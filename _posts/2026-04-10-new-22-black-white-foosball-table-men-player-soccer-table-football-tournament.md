## Assembly, Setup, and Maintenance (A Geek’s Guide)

If you’re the kind of person who enjoys building IKEA furniture and feeling a sense of accomplishment while discovering four extra screws you didn’t need, you’ll likely enjoy the assembly process here. The New 22 arrives in a few manageable boxes, with clearly labeled parts and a surprisingly intuitive instruction layout. The instructions are not the kind you’d call “fun” at a party, but they are concise and logical, which we appreciate; there are no black magic diagrams that require you to consult a mystical assistant before you can proceed.

Key tips for assembly and maintenance:
- Take your time with the leveling. A slightly tilted surface will turn shots that should be easy into long-range windups with unpredictable bounces.
- Tighten the crossbar screws in a star pattern to distribute stress evenly across the frame. It keeps the table from rattling during the most dramatic goals.
- Use a light silicone spray on the rods for smoother action, but wipe any excess to avoid dust attraction.
- Check the ball return regularly; clear any debris after a few games to avoid a clogged pipeline of chaos.
- If you’re customizing, you can replace players with your favorite team’s mini-figures or even go entirely wacky with color-coded teams. It’s your living room; make it yours.

Storage considerations: Keep the table in a dry, temperature-controlled space if possible. Foosball tables are robust, but excess humidity and direct sunlight can cause the wood to warp over time. A simple protective cover can extend the life of the surface and help keep the table pristine between seasons of intense competition.
