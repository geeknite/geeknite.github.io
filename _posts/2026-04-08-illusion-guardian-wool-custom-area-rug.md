## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity

Care is part of the joy of owning a wool rug. Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth to avoid staining. For stubborn marks, a mild detergent solution with water can be used carefully, followed by blotting to absorb moisture. Avoid rubbing the nap, which can distort the pile and reduce the illusion effect. Vacuuming should be done regularly to prevent dirt from embedding itself into the wool fibers. Use a vacuum with a gentle brush or beater bar set to a low height so you don’t pull fibers loose.

Professional cleaning is recommended every 12 to 24 months depending on traffic, or sooner if you have a friend with a notorious coffee obsession who happens to visit weekly. A good rule of thumb is to treat this rug as you would treat your favorite sweater: with respect, a little care, and the knowledge that you will be wearing it in your living room for a long time.

External care tips:
- Rotate the rug every 6 to 12 months to promote even wear.
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and to cushion the fibers against furniture.
- Keep direct sunlight to a minimum to avoid fading; if fading is part of your design plan, then congrats on your avant-garde aesthetic.
- Clean spills immediately, using blotting rather than rubbing to preserve the nap.

## Quick Measuring Guide: How to Order Custom Fit

If you decide to go the custom route, here is a quick, no-nonsense measuring guide to help you get it right the first time:
- Measure length and width of the space, accounting for furniture clearance.
- Consider door swing space and traffic lanes to ensure you can walk comfortably across the room.
- Use masking tape to map the rug area on the floor to visualize the final footprint.
- Record measurements in inches or centimeters for the order form.
- If using a custom size beyond standard widths, confirm the final dimensions with the seller to avoid misinterpretation of the plan.
