## How to Play (A Quick Refresher)
Skip-Bo’s core loop is simple: you aim to deplete your stock piles by playing numbered cards into ascending sequences on the table, using wild cards to fill gaps as needed. The Deluxe 2001 version preserves this core cadence with clarifications in the rulebook to address common points of confusion.

### Setup
- Each player starts with a handful of stock piles face-down and a hand of cards. Setup time is brief, making this ideal for gamers who are antsy or easily distracted.
- The mesa holds a few active build piles where you’ll place cards in ascending order. The objective is to empty your stock piles as quickly as possible while resisting the urge to turn the dining room into a battlefield of discarded stock piles.
- Reserve cards and wilds are placed within easy reach, so you’re not rummaging through a tangle of loose cards mid-turn. The Deluxe edition emphasizes a clean and approachable table presence.

### Turn Sequence
On your turn, you draw up to five cards and then try to play as many as possible into the build piles. You can play from your stock piles, your hand, and any other piles according to the rules. If you can’t play, you discard and pass the turn. The spirit remains: quick, fluid, and a little chaotic in the best possible way.

### Winning the Game
The first player to deplete all their stock piles wins. The Deluxe 2001 edition emphasizes speed and accuracy, rewarding clean sequences and punishing misreads that cause penalty draws. The balance remains light, designed to keep the vibe social rather than punitive.

## Strategy and Tips for Winning
- Early build pile momentum matters. Starting faster reduces later pressure from stock piles.
- Wilds are a resource, not a free pass. Save them for critical moments when they unlock a cascade of plays.
