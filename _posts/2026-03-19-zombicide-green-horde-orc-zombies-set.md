## What is in the Orc Zombies Set and how it fits the Green Horde vibe

### The core concept
In the world of Zombicide Green Horde, you are dealing with orc factions as the zombies, which flips the usual undead trope on its head. The Orc Zombies Set compounds that idea by giving you a cadre of zombified orc models that still carry the warlike swagger—think rusted axes, mangled armor, and a shambling gait that still appears suspiciously military. The set is designed to slot into the main Green Horde box with no fuss, offering new threats that interact with the unique green-horde rules and the barbaric charm of orcish design. If you enjoyed the orc aesthetic in the base game, this expansion cranks it to eleven with a mix of new sculpts and grotesquely glorious greens.

### Miniatures and sculpt quality
The Orc Zombies Set delivers a varied handful of pleated chaos: grizzled bosses, braced thralls, and chattering mobs of headless zekes on the run. The sculpts capture the orc look with chunky shoulders, tusks that look like they misheard dental instructions, and armor that looks as though it survived a full-on brawl with a smithy’s forge. The plastics are firm but a touch squeaky; you will probably need a good pair of clippers and a gentle hand to avoid warping or bending any weapons. Once you pin them properly, the range reads exceptionally well on the table. The details translate nicely when you paint, which is a relief since Orc Zombies love a good color show. If you are a painter who enjoys grime and gore, you will be thrilled by the amount of texture on these minis, which ranges from hammered chainmail to carved bone adornments that scream metal meet marrow.

### Base size and board presence
Like many Zombicide minis, these orc zombies are designed to fit onto standard zombie bases and play nicely with the Green Horde’s terrain and factions. The bases are modest in size, ensuring you don’t overcrowd the board on turns where you are flipping a dozen survivors and a couple of orc zombies. On the table, the green skins with their rusty armor pop, which makes them easy to track during chaotic firefights and close-quarter melees alike. If you like a table with color variety and bold silhouettes, these minis hit that sweet spot: not so small that they vanish in a forest of neoprene, but not so gigantic that you misplace your dice trying to measure a movement of six inches.

## Unboxing, assembly, and painting tips

### Unboxing impressions
Opening the Orc Zombies Set is a little like a present from a friend who has seen too many fantasy loot crates: there is a hearty sense of abundance, with enough zombies to require a separate plan for storage. The sprues come pre-split into plausible units with plenty of little bits; however, you should expect to do some trimming and cleaning. A few of the spurs are delightfully sharp, which means you will want a fine file and maybe a magnetized tray to keep the pieces from rolling away while you assemble them.

### Assembly notes
Assembly is straightforward but not for the faint of heart. The key steps involve:
- Cleaning flash off the miniatures without sacrificing key details
- Dry fitting to ensure optimal pose and stable bases
- Pinning or at least careful gluing for weapons that could snap in play
- Optional pin alignment for larger orcs to preserve limb articulation
The sets include a mix of basic and more elaborate poses, so you will get some that are quick to assemble and others that require a little more care. If you own a rotary tool, you will be grateful for the opportunity to smooth out joins without destroying fine facial features. If you are a painter who loves to pre-assemble and then prime, you can batch the assembly and paint in groups by base coat color for efficiency.

### Painting suggestions
The greens used in the Green Horde set present an excellent canvas for experimentation. A few notes from the Geeknite painting desk:
- Start with a mid-tone green as a base, avoiding pure lime that can wash out under light. A mid-to-olive green works well to give you that earthy orc vibe rather than a candy-green or a swamp hue.
- Highlight with lighter greens on raised surfaces and edges; consider blue-green highlights for a shadowed look in crevices to simulate depth.
- Armor and weapons look great with metallics but benefit from a wash of dark brown or black to age the metal and add grit.
- Flesh tones should skew towards sallow greens, with darker shading around the eyes and bone earned teeth to emphasize the undead nature.
- Blood and gore: a rust-red wash on armor and bone chips on shields adds a nice contrast and helps readability on the table during late game clashes.
If you want a how-to gallery on painting or a step-by-step approach, there is an older Geeknite post you can consult for general techniques, but the Orc Zombies Set scales well with typical Zombicide painting methods and looks great with a little extra effort.

## Gameplay impact: balance, tactics, and table dynamics

### How they interact with the base game mechanics
Orc Zombies are not your standard reanimated infantry. They bring a mix of resilience and aggression that can turn a normal zombie push into a tricky cluster of threats. In gameplay terms, they tend to spike the tension in the early to mid game as you attempt to clear zones and prevent a surge of orcish aggression from sweeping across your board. Their presence forces players to consider crowd control and execu-tional choices that differ from standard zombie hordes. The teaching moment here is that the Orc Zombies Set encourages players to think about movement blocking, prioritization, and the timing of the fatigue or “blow it all up now” play that defines many Zombicide sessions.

### Board presence and threat variety
One of the biggest wins for this expansion is the variety it introduces. The orc zombies come in multiple varieties, from shambling grunts to more formidable captains whose size and gear grant them extra protection or special abilities. This creates a dynamic where survivors cannot simply push through the board in a single lane; you must consider alternative routes, synergy with survivors, and the use of environment to silence the orc threat. The different poses also help to differentiate threats on the table visually, which reduces fatigue and mistakes as the game intensifies. In short: variety is the spice of table life, and this set brings a lot of it to your orc-infected kitchen table of doom.

### Difficulties and edge cases
Like any expansion, these orc zombies can complicate things. They may spawn in numbers that feel manageable in isolation but, when combined with other greens, can quickly create a pressure cooker scenario. The key to avoiding frustration is to manage timing and coordinate with your fellow players so that you do not get overwhelmed at the wrong moment. You may want to lean on the standard Green Horde rules for group actions and the zombie surge cards to minimize surprise spikes. The set shines when paired with good table management and a bit of strategic planning about where to position survivors for defense and offense.

### Strategy tips for players
- Map control matters: use the board layout to cut off potential zombie routes and funnel orc zombies into choke points where you can manage them.
- Target priority: identify which orc zombies deliver the biggest threat and neutralize them first, particularly captains who can buff nearby enemies or unleash devastating effects.
- Resource timing: balance the need for healing and reloads against the looming threat of new spawns. Sometimes you need to gamble on a high-risk, high-reward move if you are near a critical objective.
- Collaboration: talk through moves with your team, especially when you anticipate a surge of orc zombies on a central corridor. A coordinated plan reduces the risk of bodies piling up in critical zones.
