## What is Codenames?
At its core, Codenames is a team-based word association exercise masquerading as a board game. Two teams vie to uncover their agents among 25 codename cards laid out in a 5x5 grid. A spymaster from each team provides one-word clues that link several of their words on the board. The rest of the team then guesses those words, one by one, in an attempt to clear their side of the grid. The kicker is that you must avoid selecting words that belong to the opposing team, and you must avoid the assassin word, or the game ends in a dramatic, if somewhat anti-climactic, defeat.

### The Core Loop
- Spymaster studies the key card that shows which words on the grid belong to red, blue, neutral, or assassin.
- Spymaster gives a one-word clue plus a number indicating how many words on the board relate to that clue.
- Team guesses the corresponding cards, trying to hit as many of their own words as possible without hitting an opponent word or, worst of all, the assassin.

## How to Play: Quick Rules
- Setup: Lay out 25 codename cards in a 5x5 grid. Pick a spymaster for each team. The red/blue split is provided by the key card.
- Clue: The spymaster gives a one-word clue plus a number. For example, "FRUIT, 3" might connect apple, orange, and grape.
- Guess: The team guesses cards one at a time. If it’s one of their own cards, it stays; if it’s an opponent’s, it’s marked as such; if it’s neutral, nothing happens beyond the mark; if it’s the assassin, the team loses immediately.
- End state: The game ends when one team has found all their agents or when the assassin is hit.

## Strategies for Teams
- Know your players: vocabulary breadth matters more than general knowledge. A clue that works for one group may completely flop for another.
- Start with safe bets: use clues that tie to your own words but avoid anything that could lead to the assassin.
- Build a narrative: sometimes you can craft a clue that flows with an ongoing story you are telling your team.
- Avoid overcluing: a clue that ties to many words is tempting, but it can also accelerate the opposition’s progress.
- Adjust the tempo: if your group tends to overthink, consider a time limit. The adrenaline can keep energy levels high.
