## What is OverPower? A quick primer
OverPower lives in the same family as other big 90s CCGs, but with its own Marvel superhero flavor. The core mechanics revolve around power levels, combat values, and a simple turn structure that allowed players to run through slick combos while admiring trade card art. Each card features a hero or villain with a numeric power, a combat value, and a few special abilities that trigger under certain conditions. There are twisty elements like team-up advantages and special mission cards that push your deck from a straight up punch-out to a little more strategy theater.

### Core mechanics at a glance
- Each hero has a power rating and a combat value that determine outcomes in skirmishes. Think of it as a tug of war between who can throw bigger numbers without tripping over their cape.
- Abilities fire off when you meet conditions like having a certain number of allies on the field or using a specific combination of powers. The flavor is fun and the balance is… well, mid-90s charming, which means some cards feel overpowered in a glorious, meme-worthy way.
- Deck-building constraints encourage synergy. Your hero lineup, sidekicks, and support cards must weave a narrative that makes sense on the board. It is not about maxing out numbers alone; it is about making a coherent plan that looks cool on a table of eager fans.

## The P-Promo concept: Pick From List
Promo cards have always been a bait that collectors adore. They offer a rare glimpse into what could have been, or a power that was available only to a select few. The P-Promo Pick From List in the OverPower universe is particularly intriguing because it implies a curated selection — a list of hero options, from which a player could pick one to add to their deck under promotional terms.

### What does Pick From List actually mean in practice?
- It implies scarcity: a limited run, perhaps distributed at a convention, through a promotional event, or via a special order.
- It implies choice: instead of receiving a fixed card, you select from a list, which adds a tiny DIY feel to a game that is already about deck construction.
- It implies value: promos tend to be more collectible, with fans trading for a chance to own a hero that might synergize uniquely with their existing lineup.

In the context of this review, we treat the P-Promo as a hypothetical or observed variant where players got to pick from a curated list of superheroes that tested the water for new archetypes in the game. The card involved would likely feature a signature hero, an art piece that fans recognized, and an ability that interacted well with the broader mechanics of OverPower. The exact list could vary, but the spirit is clear: give the player a small but meaningful degree of customization, and let the trade frenzy begin.

### The typical distribution model
- Con exclusives: A small number of promos circulated at comic conventions. If you were there with a badge and a friend with a suitcase full of comic book dreams, you might walk away with a coveted card that your local shop could only dream of stocking.
- Mail-in promos: A handful of offers allowed players to mail in a request for a promo, with a limit of one per household. Nostalgia aside, this was the pre-digital era of collecting, where patience and self-control had to substitute for algorithms and targeted ads.
- Store promos: A select few retailers could hand out the P-Promo to customers who purchased a set or a feature product. The risk here was time and location; if you missed the tables at a gaming weekend, you might be out of luck.

The upshot is that Pick From List promos blend scarcity with a dash of personal choice. The value, of course, comes from both the card itself and the story you can tell about how you scored it. It is a social artifact as much as it is a gaming piece.

## Authenticity, storage, and care
Promo cards require a little extra love. They are older, typically thinner in stock than modern cards, and more susceptible to edge wear, surface flaws, and color fading in exposure to sunlight. Here are some practical tips if you find a P-Promo card in your collection or think about buying one:

- Condition is king: A mint or near-mint promo card can fetch a premium, but even slight edge wear can drop value significantly. If you intend to play with it, consider a protective sleeve and a matte top loader to minimize friction.
- Protective storage: Store promos away from direct sunlight, heat, and humidity. A climate-controlled display box or a dedicated card binder works well. Avoid PVC-based sleeves that can discolor over time.
- Handling etiquette: Handle by the edges to preserve the surface and avoid fingerprint marks on a glossy card. Your future self will thank you when you pull it out for display at a convention and it looks pristine.
