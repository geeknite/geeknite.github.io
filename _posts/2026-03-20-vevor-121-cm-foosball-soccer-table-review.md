## Setup and Maintenance: Keeping It Ready for Games
Beyond the initial assembly, a few quick maintenance steps will keep the table performing well for years. Regularly check the screws and bolts for looseness after a handful of matches. The rods should glide smoothly; if you notice resistance, a light lubricant on the rod rails can rejuvenate the action without turning the table into a grease trap. Cleaning the playing surface with a dry microfiber cloth helps maintain a consistent ball roll and reduces the chance of sticky spots that soak up dirt and dust.

To keep the legs stable, periodically relevel the feet. A misaligned table not only reduces play quality but also invites accidental nudges that end in a rolled chair collision. If you live in a high humidity area, consider an occasional wipe-down of wood surfaces to minimize warping over time. A quick weekly check of the ball return path ensures you do not wake up one day to a bewildering pile of balls blocking your coffee mug’s travel path.
