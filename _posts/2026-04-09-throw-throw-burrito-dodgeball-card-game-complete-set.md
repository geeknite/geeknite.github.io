## What is Throw Throw Burrito, and what does the Complete Set include?
Throw Throw Burrito is, at its core, a fast-paced action game wrapped in a card-drafting structure. Players collect cards to three categories (attack, defend, and rescue), and when two matching “attack” cards appear in play, a chaotic burrito-flinging moment ensues. Think dodgeball meets a family-friendly food fight, all in a bright cardboard universe where the moral of the story is always: don’t throw the burritos too hard, and laugh when you miss entirely.

The Complete Set elevates this chaos with a couple of official add-ons and a robust accessory kit. In general, you’ll get:

## How to set up and start playing
Setup is refreshingly simple:
1) Open the box and reorganize everything. The Complete Set typically includes a couple of different decks and a handful of burritos, so a quick shuffle and a quick layout help.
2) Choose your arena: a standard table surface works, but if you’ve got carpet, you’re in for extra chaos—carpet + burritos is a hazardous tango.
3) Deal the initial cards according to the rulebook. Don’t worry; it’s mostly intuitive, with a few jokey notes about your first instinct to aim at your friend’s face (which we advise against).
4) Hand out burritos to the players. You want enough to ensure a few early misses, which makes the chaos more delicious later on.

A quick note on safety: Throw Throw Burrito uses soft, plush burritos, but this is still a physical party game. Establish a basic no-face-target rule and remind everyone that choking hazards, broken glass, or actual harm are strictly off-limits. It’s all about laughter, not lawsuits.

## How to play: the gist and the giggles
The core mechanic is simple enough that even your friend who “doesn’t like games” can join in and pretend they’re intense. The flow generally looks like this:
- Players take turns playing cards that usually say things like “draw from the deck,” “activate a hit,” or “deflect with a dodge.” The exact wording varies a bit depending on the expansion included in the Complete Set.
- When two matching attack cards appear in play, the burrito-lobbing moment triggers a free-for-all mini-battle. Everyone ducks, dodges, and maybe cries out a dramatic phrase about burrito destiny.
- Points are scored based on successful dodges, clever plays, and occasional “hit” bonuses from certain cards. The person with the most points at the end wins the round; rounds can be chained for a tournament vibe.

One of the joys of Throw Throw Burrito is how different the same game can feel with different groups. With a crowd that’s quick on their feet, you’ll get a rapid-fire session with near-constant banter. With a more laid-back group, you’ll enjoy longer pauses to do mock-commentary and to applaud dramatic burrito throws that miss by millimeters, which is honestly where a lot of the fun lives.

## Strategy and timing: when to throw, when to dodge, and when to dance
- Timing is everything. A successful burrito throw is less about power and more about opportunity: the moment someone just used their defense card, or when the table is already in hysterics because the cat gif on the box just triggered a new round of chaos.
- Card counting isn’t cheating here; it’s survival. If you know two attack cards are lurking, you can push harder for a dodge when you think you’re safe. The playstyle you bring matters: do you lean into misdirection, or do you become a sprinting blur of comedic chaos?
- Social dynamics matter. Throw Throw Burrito rewards showmanship and banter. The more your table is engaged with each other, the more memorable the game becomes. If you are worried about competitiveness souring the mood, set a playful tone from the start and designate hotel-lobby-esque rivalries only for show.
- In groups where kids are present or where parents want to maintain peace, you can adjust house rules to emphasize safe play and keep the “hit” times shorter, ensuring more laughs than groans.
