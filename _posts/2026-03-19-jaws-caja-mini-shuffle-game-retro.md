## How to Play: The Shuffle that Bites Back
At the heart of Jaws (Caja Mini) is a shuffle game with a narrative frame. You are a beachgoer on a tiny digital island, trying to outpace a shark that escapes from a pixelated crate every few rounds. The objective is to hold a line, shuffle the deck by pressing buttons at specific moments, and avoid getting swallowed by the big digital maw. The mechanic is simple in theory but surprisingly clever in practice, especially when you realize how a couple of well-timed button presses can alter the course of the shark and your on-screen fate.

### Core Rules in Simple Terms
- The screen presents a row of tiles, some safe, some sharky. The shark moves along the row in waves.
- You press the action button to shuffle the tiles, redistributing safe paths and hazard tiles.
- The aim is to reach the end of the row without a shark bite animation ending your run.
- Each successful shuffle earns you points or a small power-up that makes future shuffles easier.
- The game ends when the shark bites you or you run out of lives, whichever the unit decides first.

### The Strategic Layer
The clever part is not simply swiping left or right; it is reading the shark's pulsing motion and predicting where the board will tilt next. The shuffle mechanic creates a dynamic board that rewards anticipation and memory more than twitch reflexes. A patient player who studies the micro-patterns of the shark's approach can reliably thread the needle and grab a higher score, which makes repeat plays feel earned rather than accidental. The joy is in learning the tempo: when the shark slows, you can plan; when it speeds, you improvise. The rhythm mirrors the classic jaws motif of looming threat, punctuated by a cheerful chiptune that seems to shout, You got this, tiny hero, but barely.

### Difficulty Tuning
The difficulty scales gently. Early runs are forgiving, with forgiving margins and generous window for a shuffle. As you climb the ladder, the shark’s moves become a bit more predictable but the board also yields fewer safe routes. The developers have balanced it so that a casual play session remains accessible while offering a meaningful challenge for completionists who want to chase high scores and bragging rights in the comments at the bottom of your favorite post about retro gadgets.
