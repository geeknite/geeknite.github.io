## How Daydreams Affects Gameplay

If you’re familiar with Dixit, you know the game’s rhythm: a storyteller gives a clue, players pick cards that best match that clue, everyone guesses which card belongs to the storyteller, and points are awarded if you’re both too obvious or too obscure. Daydreams doesn’t rewrite the rules; it enriches the ecosystem. The expanded card pool adds depth to every round, but it also invites a new kind of risk—the possibility of generating too many strong cards that could outshine your own clue or, more entertainingly, tempt players into lying with misdirection that’s almost too convincing.

Key gameplay implications:

- More card options mean higher chances that a player will find a card that perfectly matches a story, which can be both a blessing and a curse. It’s easier to land on a satisfying narrative, but the tension around whether your clue will land on the exact card grows.
- With 84 additional images, the odds of someone else recalling a card that matches your clue increase, which means you’ll need to be craftier with your narrative to ensure your card gets the spotlight rather than a peer’s card that fits just as well.
- The balance between storytelling and guessing remains the backbone, but Daydreams nudges players toward more experimental prompts and subtler associations. If your group loves cryptic clues and inside jokes, you’ll feel right at home.

For players who enjoy a more strategic or competitive edge, there are moments when Daydreams can shift the dynamic. In rounds where several players converge on a similar vibe, the storyteller’s ability to differentiate their chosen card becomes the decisive factor. If you prefer a looser, more social atmosphere where misdirection reigns supreme, Daydreams will reward those who can harness the chaos and turn it into a shared giggle fit.

## The 84 Cards: A Closer Look at the Moments That Matter

Rather than going card-by-card (which would require a chess grandmaster’s patience and a caffeinated squirrel’s focus), I’ll highlight some recurring themes and a few standout images that typically get people talking around the table.

### The Dream-Logic Duo: Metaphor Pairs
Several cards are designed to slot into a pair of contrasting ideas—a dream about trust versus doubt, a memory you’re sure you’ve misplaced, a future you’re not sure you want to visit. These thematic pairs are not only thematic; they encourage storytelling that leans into the tension between what you intend to convey and what your audience perceives. A good clue might be: “Two faces, one decision,” with a card that shows a mask-like visage and a corridor splitting into two paths. The result: laughter, head-scratching, and the occasional surprised groan when someone nails the double-meaning.

### The Absurdist Invasion
Dixit has long succeeded on absurd imagery, and Daydreams leans even harder into the absurd. Expect cards with creatures that seem to have wandered out of a Dadaist diary: creatures wearing hats made of fish, trees that cough up tiny lamps, or boats sailing on air currents like helium-enabled rafts. These cards are not just pretty pictures; they’re prompts to micro-narratives that can feel like a short story prompt in a coffee-fueled writing sprint.

### The Subtle Mood Cards
Some of the 84 are not loud; they whisper. They invite a gentle, almost intimate storytelling approach—soft color palettes, muted textures, and figures engaged in quiet acts: a child listening to a seashell, an old man drawing constellations in a fogged window. These are the kind of cards that reward good listening and nuanced clue-writing. In groups that enjoy gentle humor, these cards shine by inviting playful misdirection that’s both endearing and clever.

### The Surreal Showstoppers
Then there are the showstoppers: images with bold shapes, dramatic perspective, and a punchy composition that makes it obvious to the viewer what you might be trying to convey—if you want to risk it. The payoff here is big if your audience is quick to pick up visual cues; a well-chosen clue paired with a daydream-born image can spark rounds of exclamations and the classic Dixit scene: players arguing about who wrote the clue for which card, all with smiles plastered on their faces.

- Use a storytelling palette: When you describe a clue, lean into the imagery on the Daydreams cards. If a card features a staircase that seems to float upward, a clue about “rising to a different version of yourself” can be a natural fit. - Mix and match: Don’t be afraid to combine Daydreams images with cards from base Dixit or other expansions. The interplay between different art styles can yield some surprisingly clever rounds as players try to interpret cross-visual cues.
- Consider a rotating storyteller: If you intend to play longer sessions, rotate the storyteller role so different people have the opportunity to set a clue against a custom Daydreams prompt. This helps maximize the 84-card pool’s storytelling potential and keeps rounds feeling fresh.
- Read the room: Some players love cryptic metaphors; others prefer more literal stories. Daydreams will reward the former, but with a little adaptation, it can still be delightful for all.
