## Assembly and Fit
As with most single-piece resin minis, assembly can range from straightforward to mildly fiddly depending on whether there are multiple parts to the cape or the wrists. The Batman Who Laughs is designed for tabletop play, so expect the joints to align well and the sculpt to read clearly in the assembled form. If there are separate cloak panels or added components, dry-fit first to ensure proper alignment before gluing. Pinning at any weighted points, like the cape attachment or the belt region, is a prudent step to improve durability on an active tabletop.

In practice, most hobbyists will find assembly to be a smooth process. The main constraints come from matching the cape edges to the torso and ensuring the posture remains natural after the glue dries. If you are new to resin kits, take your time with cleaning and prep, then proceed with precise glue application and a careful check of seam lines. You will be rewarded with a model that holds its dramatic pose in both display and gaming scenarios.

## Painting Potential and Tips
On the palette side, this miniature shines with the possibilities. The Batman Who Laughs works well with a dark, moody palette: deep blacks, charcoal grays, and a pop of color to emphasize critical features such as eyes or the belt buckle. A high-contrast approach looks spectacular: a near-black base with crisp highlights on the cloak edges can give you a dramatic silhouette that reads from across the table.

The eyes, should you choose to illuminate them with paint, can glow with a neon green or sickly yellow tint for a villainous effect.

If you enjoy weathering, consider a subtle dusting on the cloak edges to suggest movement and a touch of grime without obscuring the sculptural details.
