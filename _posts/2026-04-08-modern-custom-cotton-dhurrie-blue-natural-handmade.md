## Sizing, placement, and practical fit

Choosing the right size is the difference between a rug that feels like a decorative jewelry piece and one that legitimately changes the feel of a room. Here are practical guidelines to help you size up without overthinking it:

- Living room: For a sofa-based setup, aim for a rug that fits under the front legs or at least the front two legs of the main seating. A 5x8 ft rug often works well in medium living rooms, while a 8x10 ft rug can anchor a larger seating arrangement. A Dhurrie with a low profile will pair nicely with mid-century modern seating and a glass coffee table.
- Bedroom: A rug that sits at the edge of the bed or under the lower third of the bed can create a retreat-like vibe. A common choice is a 4x6 ft rug for a queen bed, placed with the longer side along the bed for a clean visual anchor.
- Entryways and hallways: A narrow runner or a tight 2x6 ft or 2x8 ft rug can set the tone as you enter the space. The flatweave style ensures the rug won’t bunch up under a doorway or under foot traffic.
- High-traffic areas: Cotton Dhurries are breathable and soft underfoot but can show wear in heavy use spaces. If your hallway or living room is a marathon of foot traffic, consider a slightly tighter weave and a stain-resistant cleaning routine. The rug will reward your care with long-term good looks.

If you want help visualizing fit, many retailers offer room-planning tools or you can pull screenshots from your space to overlay a rug shape. The bottom line is to leave 12 to 24 inches of floor visible on all sides for a balanced look, unless you are deliberately going for a bigger splash with floor-to-ceiling color.


## Care and maintenance: Keeping the blue natural rug looking fresh

Care is where a rug earns its keep without asking for your entire afternoon. Here are practical maintenance steps tailored for a modern cotton Dhurrie:

- Vacuum regularly: Use a low-suction setting and a bare floor attachment to avoid pulling fibers. Vacuum both sides if possible, especially for flatweave pieces that accumulate dust on the underside.
- Rotate periodically: If sunlight hits your rug unevenly, rotate every few months to prevent color fading and wear on one side.
- Spot cleaning: Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Use a mild detergent solution if needed, and avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade cotton fibers or alter the color.
- Avoid moisture: Cotton loves to hold onto water. Wipe up spills quickly and do not saturate the rug with water. Drying flat in a ventilated area helps prevent warping.
- Professional cleaning: Every 12 to 24 months, consider a light professional cleaning tailored for flatweave cotton. Avoid steam cleaning unless the maker confirms it is safe for your rug’s dye and weave.
