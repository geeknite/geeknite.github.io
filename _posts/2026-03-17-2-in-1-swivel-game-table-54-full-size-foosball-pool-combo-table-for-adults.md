## Assembly and Setup

### Unboxing and prep
Expect a few hours to assemble if you’re going solo; with two or more helpers, you can probably finish in under two hours. The process is straightforward but thick with hardware, so lay out everything methodically. Pro-tip: separate screws by size before you start; nothing kills the vibe of a party faster than hunting down a tiny Allen wrench while your friends pretend to be impressed by your DIY prowess.

### Step-by-step highlights
1) Attach legs and stabilizers to the main frame. 2) Assemble the foosball field on one side and the pool surface on the other. 3) Install rails, pockets, and cues. 4) Attach the swiveling top mechanism and test the lock. 5) calibrate the level using the included bubble level and a (friendly) neighbor to confirm you didn’t end up with a slanted playing experience. 6) Break-in rounds to ensure the balls aren’t sticking and the swivel makes a clean flip without heroic furniture gymnastics.

While most users will manage fine with the included tools, a power screwdriver can shave a half-hour off the job and keep you from cursing at stubborn screws that pretend to be “held in place by mystic force.”

### Time estimates and tips
- Typical assembly time: 1.5–3 hours depending on helper count and how aggressively you read the instruction manual.
- Tip: do a dry-fit first. Let each major component “click” into place before you commit to a permanent screw. It saves you from having to disassemble later when you realize you misread a diagram that looks nothing like your workspace.

## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Durability

A table like this requires a little upkeep to stay pristine and gust-free. Here are practical guidelines:
- Wipe down surfaces after use to remove fingerprints, smudges, and the occasional rain of snack crumbs.
- Check the swivel mechanism periodically for any loosened screws and tighten them as needed. If you hear a creak during swivels, stop and re-check the alignment.
- Vacuum or brush the pool cloth gently to prevent dust and debris from grinding into the felt. If you care about longevity, place it in a low-humidity environment and avoid direct sunlight on the play surfaces.
- Use a mild cleaning solution on the laminate surfaces; avoid harsh solvents that can dull the finish or warp the wood.

Durability-wise, this table is built to survive the occasional party atmosphere. It’s not a museum piece; it’s a lifestyle piece. If you’re the kind of person who rearranges furniture weekly, you’ll want to bolt it down and keep a couple of spare parts around in case a rod end decides to retire early.
