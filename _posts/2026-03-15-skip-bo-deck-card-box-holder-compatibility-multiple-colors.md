## Compatibility with Skip-Bo cards: fit, sleeves, and edge cases

Skip-Bo decks are distinctive in their card sizing and stacking behavior. The deck box holder is designed to accommodate standard, un-sleeved cards with a thickness that allows for a snug fit. If you adore sleeves, you’ll want to measure your deck plus sleeve thickness to ensure it still fits into the cavity with room to spare for occasional card swaps. If you’re unsure, bring a sample card and test it in-store before you buy or opt for a model advertised as sleeve-friendly.

## Maintenance, care, and longevity
Keeping the deck box looking sharp is easier than keeping a cat from sitting on your keyboard. Here are quick care steps:
- Regular dusting keeps the surface from looking dull. A quick wipe down after a night of snack debris helps maintain that showroom finish.
- Store in a cool, dry place. High humidity can affect plastics over time, and nobody needs a warped lid on a night of dramatic final draws.
- Avoid direct sun exposure for extended periods; the color can fade if you’re trying to recreate a sunset table vibe for every game night.
- Inspect hinges and locks (if present) from time to time; small tensions can develop as the box ages, similar to a well-loved gaming chair that creaks just enough to remind you it isn’t new anymore.
