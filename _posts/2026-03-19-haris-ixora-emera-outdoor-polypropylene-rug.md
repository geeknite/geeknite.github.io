Care, cleaning, and maintenance
- Routine cleaning: Vacuum or beat the rug occasionally to remove loose dirt. The rug’s weave is forgiving enough to handle a light dusting without losing shape.
- Spot cleaning: For spills, blot rather than rub (this is not a spaghetti scene; you don’t want to smear sauce across the entire weave). Mild detergent and water usually do the trick. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade fibers or fade colors.
- Deep cleaning: Hose it down and let it air dry flat. Don’t fold or roll while wet or you risk creases that can become permanent memory lanes for dirt. If you’re storing it for winter, ensure it’s fully dry before rolling to prevent mildew.
- Storage: Fold or roll for off-season storage, keeping it dry and away from damp basements. A high shelf is a noble, less-humiliating option than a laundry hamper, and it keeps the rug looking crisp for longer.
- Odor control: A quick rinse and air-dry can help with domestic odors; if your pet somehow managed to invent a new scent, a mild baking-soda solution can help neutralize it before reintroducing the rug to the living space.
