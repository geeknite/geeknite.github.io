H3: Teaching tips for new players

- Start with a warm-up round: a no-stakes test drive to calibrate how your group reads timing.
- Emphasize nonverbal signals: a nod, a tiny sigh, a slight tilt of the head can be as informative as a spoken strategy. Let players find their own language.
- Establish a soft pause rule: when you’re unsure whether to play a card, you wait one beat longer than you think is comfortable. The extra beat is where the magic happens.
- Use a timer for added pressure when you want a brisk session. Just be mindful that too much timer doom can turn a joyful game night into a stressful event, which is not the vibe you want for a mind game set.
