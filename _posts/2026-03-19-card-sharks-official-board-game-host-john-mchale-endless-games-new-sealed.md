## How to Play: The Quick Guide

If you’ve ever watched Card Sharks (the original 1970s/80s run or the newer revival), you know the vibe: risk, speed, and a little bit of panicked talk from contestants as they decide whether to flip cards or fold for more money. This board game captures the spirit in a compact, family-friendly format. Here’s a condensed guide to get you started without needing a PhD in “television starchitecture.”

### Setup
- Place the board in the center and give each player a handful of chips.
- Shuffle the question cards and place them in the designated draw pile.
- Each player chooses a host token and a color chip. The host card is used to “animate” your on-table persona, even if your actual hosting voice is more of a squeak.
- Decide the game length (we suggest a 20- to 45-minute session for the first run to avoid existential crises).

### The Core Rounds
The core rounds mimic the show’s two-pillared structure: toss-up questions and card-based risk management. In this version, you’ll encounter:

- Toss-Up Bets: Players place bets on a card drawn from the deck. The thrill is in how much you’re willing to risk to reach a prize tier, while the crowd (your family) groans in delightful empathy.
- Card Reveal and Contingencies: As cards are revealed, players make choices about continuing or stopping. The tension is never-ending because you never know when the “card gods” will smirk and turn your risk into regret.
- Face Cards and Bluffing: You’ll feel the heartbeat of the show as you decide whether to bluff with your voice or with your posture. The game rewards confident bluffing and punishing table resets when your posture says “I’m all in” and your mouth says “just kidding.”

### Scoring and Endgame
Points accumulate based on successful bets and correct assumptions. The endgame arrives when someone claims the final prize tier or when someone cries out, “Wait, we’re out of chips, but I’m not done calculating risk!” It’s a satisfying convergence of luck, math, and social chaos.
