## How to Play: Quick Rules and DBZ Flair
If you’ve played UNO before, you know the rhythm: match color or number, toss an action card to flip the table (with affection, of course), and shout “UNO!” right before you threaten to forget to draw. The Dragon Ball Z variant adds a few thematic spins, but the core play remains approachable for kids and adults alike.

- Objective: Be the first player to discard all your cards.
- How it differs: In addition to the classic Draw Two, Skip, Reverse, and Wild cards, you’ll encounter Dragon Ball Z thematic actions that may grant you extra turns, force a power-up move on an opponent, or unleash a field-wide effect that shakes up the stack order.
- Safe disclaimers: The game remains relatively quick, with typical rounds lasting anywhere from 5 to 15 minutes depending on the number of players and how stubborn your friends are about not muttering the word “UNO” until the exact moment it’s necessary.

A few practical tips:
- Pay attention to the color distribution in the deck. If you’re stuck with multiple cards of the same color, you can lean on the DBZ-themed power actions to shift the table’s focus and potentially force someone else to draw. It’s a game about tempo as much as luck.
- Use the “Wild” and “Wild Draw Four” moments strategically. In this DBZ edition they feel slightly more dramatic because you can narrate a short energy-attack moment before you play them. It’s silly, but it adds flavor and keeps players engaged.
