## Rules and Variations
Phase 10 has a simple core loop: draw, play phases, discard, and hope the hand you built actually matches one of the required phase patterns. The Special Collector's Edition does not rewrite the entire rulebook, but it does include a few flourishes that make the experience feel fresh without turning it into a sci-fi simulation.

### Core rules in brief
- Each player starts with ten cards, and the goal is to complete a sequence of phases from 1 to 10
- On your turn, you draw one card, then you can lay down any completed phase that you meet, then discard to the discard pile
- The wild and skip cards still exist, and the Special Collector's Edition adds a couple of variant cards that you can choose to include

### Variants included in the edition
- The cowbell variant, which adds a neon glow to the wild cards and makes you cheer for the right wheel of fortune
- A co-op variant where players attempt to collectively complete all phases before the deck runs out, albeit with the usual backstabbing and banter
- A timed mode that pushes you to complete a full phase sequence within a set number of rounds, which is fun for folks with a short attention span and long patience for miscounts

### Rulebook design and quick references
The rulebook has a quick reference section that reduces the cognitive load for new players. I know, revolutionary. The reference pages are color-coded and feature a few flow charts that visually clarify which phases can be laid down on which turns. The goal is to reduce the frequency with which players consult your smartphone to confirm whether you can place a blue 2 and a yellow 4 in the same phase. In practice, the quick references help, and that makes the game feel more accessible for casual players and families alike.

## TL;DR quick play guide
If you just want to play and you do not care about the extra pizazz, here is a quick guide to get started with the Special Collector's Edition:

- Shuffle the deck and deal ten cards to each player, plus a draw pile and a discard pile
- On your turn, draw one card, try to lay down a phase you can complete, then discard one card
- You must complete each phase in order from 1 to 10 as you advance through the round; you do not have to switch phases mid-round
- Use the wild cards and skip cards as dictated by the rules; if you are playing with variants, decide early if you want to include them
- The player who completes phase 10 first ends the round and wins based on the scoring system you have chosen
- Optional: try the timed mode if you want a faster pace and a little extra pressure at game night

## Maintenance and storage tips
With deluxe components comes the burden of storage discipline. The Special Collector's Edition benefits from a dedicated storage solution or a plastic insert that keeps the cards flat and the foil overlays pristine. Here are a few ideas to keep your edition looking fresh for years to come:
