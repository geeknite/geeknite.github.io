## Painting and Collecting Tips

Audrey's colors lean toward muted browns and grays, with the cardigan showing a splash of teal to give the figure some personality. Start with a dark brown wash to bring out the folds of her clothing, then dry brush to highlight the edges. Use a brighter color for the book to make sure it reads well on the board. Audrey's glasses can be a tricky area; a crisp black line and a small white dot can help the lenses catch light without looking cartoonish.

Amy wants a clean white lab coat, with shading to indicate the folds in the fabric. The trick here is to keep the coat light and yet not flat. Use a cool wash on the coat to give it a subtle blue undertone that reads well under table lighting. For her hair, consider using a gradient of browns to give depth and a sense of movement. If you want to push the vibe, add a small badge or pin to the lab coat with a tiny symbol that nods to her field.

Bases, as mentioned, should be clean and simple, but you can add a touch of narrative by placing them in a lab environment or library floor tile. Static grass or a few micro-flocked elements can help the minis blend into your board, but avoid overdoing it. The point of the base is to support the model and not steal focus.
