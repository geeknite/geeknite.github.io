## How to Verify Authenticity and Condition
If you are ever considering adding this piece to your collection, here are practical tips to evaluate authenticity and condition without turning your living room into a crime scene:
- Inspect the outer box for crushing. The corners should be sharp; a dented corner can degrade the seal integrity.
- Check the seal itself. A factory seal will have uniformity in color and shape; if the seal looks tampered or suspicious, proceed with caution.
- Examine the card stock for discoloration. A slight yellowing is normal with age, but severe dark patches can indicate moisture damage.
- If possible, verify the packaging text and logos align with 1986 design conventions. In some cases, counterfeit items attempt to mimic vintage packaging, but the devil is in the details—the typeface, the logo spacing, and the alignment of art assets can help you differentiate genuine articles from cunning reproductions.

These steps help ensure that you are not paying a premium for a replica while still allowing you to enjoy the magic of a sealed time capsule should you discover it is the real deal.
