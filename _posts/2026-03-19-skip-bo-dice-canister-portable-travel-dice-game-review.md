## How to Play in 60 Seconds
If you’re skimming for the gist, here is a rapid-fire framework you can teach your non-gamer friends without guilt or eye-rolling.

### The setup
- Each player gets a starting hand of dice. The exact number varies by play style, but the goal remains clear: roll a set of numbers that helps you assemble a sequence or complete a target pattern, depending on the variant you’re playing.
- The canister doubles as a dice tray, which reduces the number of pieces on the table and makes cleanup less of a scavenger hunt.
- A quick-reference rule sheet sits in the box for those moments when someone asks a question you forgot to memorize while scrambling to find a snack.

### The turn flow
- Roll the dice you have in hand. Your aim is to form combinations that match the game’s objective for that round.
- Use numbers on the dice to advance toward a target or to block opponents in a lighthearted, non-competitive way.
- End your turn by discarding or setting aside dice that you’re not using, allowing the next player to pick up the pace.

### Variants and house rules
If your crew loves a little extra spice, you can introduce a couple of house rules. For example, a wild-card die can substitute for any number in a sequence, or you can establish a timer to keep the pace brisk during family gatherings. The beauty of a travel-friendly kit is that you can adapt it without turning your living room into a board game lab. The rule sheet offers a few guidelines, and the rest is history plus a few friendly arguments about what counts as a valid sequence.
