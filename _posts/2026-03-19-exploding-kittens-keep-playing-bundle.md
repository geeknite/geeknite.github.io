## Gameplay Experience: Setup, Turns, and Endgame
A typical session with the Keep Playing Bundle follows the familiar Exploding Kittens structure with added spice.

### Setup
- Shuffle the base Exploding Kittens deck with the Keep Playing deck included in the bundle. The ratio is designed to keep the game brisk; you’re not waiting for a librarian to withdraw your card layout, but you’re not flipping cards while the dog barks either.
- Deal Defuse cards according to the instructions. Some groups like to deal extra Defuses at higher player counts; others prefer valiant risk. Either approach is valid as long as everyone agrees before the first turn.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards according to the rules. The suspense creeps into the room the moment you set the deck down, and someone inevitably jokes about the kitten being “too cute to explode.”

### Turn structure with Keep Playing
- Play continues clockwise. On your turn you may draw a card or play Keep Playing cards to extend or modify your current action.
- Defuse if you can, or brace for impact if you cannot. When an Exploding Kitten is drawn, the defuse path must be available; otherwise the player is eliminated as the rest of the table howls with laughter.
- The Keep Playing cards can be used to alter the order, swap hands, or force others into riskier draws. The pacing shifts from predictable to chaotic, and that’s exactly what we want in a party game setting.

### Endgame and victory conditions
The game ends when all but one player have drawn Exploding Kittens or have been forced out by a decisive Keep Playing sequence. The last player standing—whether they earned it with crafty plays or sheer luck—claims glory and a chorus of playful heckling. In Geeknite sessions, this last stage often devolves into a storytelling beat where the winner brags about “perfect synergy between my deck and the universe,” which is basically our way of saying they got lucky and also looked good while doing it.

## Strategy and Tips for Getting More Smile per Card

- Watch for telltale signs: if your table is already in on the joke, you can push more aggressively with Keep Playing cards. If someone’s face looks like they’re parsing a calculus problem, ease back and savor the moment.
- Use Keep Playing cards to shape the table’s momentum, not just your own. A well-timed extension can turn a corner of the table into an ally for a single life-saving play or a dramatic misplay that becomes table lore.
- Don’t overcommit on early turns. The bundle rewards patience and misdirection as much as bold moves; you want to avoid burning through your Keep Playing resources too soon.
- Pair with social dynamics. The best Exploding Kittens moments aren’t just about cards; they’re about who can tell the best joke at the exact right beat to derail a plan.

Strategy isn’t about being the cleverest person in the room; it’s about learning when to gamble and when to lean into the chaos, letting the group’s shared energy do most of the heavy lifting.
