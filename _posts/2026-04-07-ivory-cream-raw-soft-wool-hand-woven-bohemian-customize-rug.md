## Maintenance: Care That Keeps the Dream Alive
No rug review would be honest without a care plan. Wool is known for its resilience, but even the mightiest fiber benefits from routine care. Here are practical care guidelines to help your rug stay pristine longer.

- Regular vacuuming with a high quality vacuum—no aggressive beater bars. A gentler approach preserves the weave and reduces shedding. 
- Spot treatment: attend to spills promptly with a clean, damp cloth. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the fiber’s natural oils. 
- Rotation: rotate every 3 to 6 months to ensure even wear and prevent persistent traffic patterns from forming. 
- Deep cleaning: consider a professional rug cleaning every 12 to 18 months depending on use. Wool loves professional care as much as a gamer loves a new console. 
- Storage: if you ever have to roll it up for storage, wrap it with breathable fabric and keep it away from moisture. Wool and humidity are not the best of friends.

### Sick of Stains? Quick Fixes
For typical household mishaps, here are some quick, safe fixes:
- Beverage spills: blot, don’t rub. Rinse with a little water and blot again. If you must add an agent, use a pH-neutral cleanser designed for wool. 
- Pet accidents: act fast, gently blot, and consider a professional cleaning if odors linger. Wool has natural odor control, but heavy pet accidents deserve extra attention. 
- Food crumbs: a soft brush can lift stubborn particles from the pile without damaging the weave.

## Buying Guide: What to Look For When Purchasing
If you’re shopping with the goal of long-term satisfaction, here are practical considerations that help you avoid buyer’s remorse.

- Verified material: confirm that the rug is made from pure wool or wool blend with a reliable supplier. Look for certifications or a transparent supply chain. 
- Weave density: higher weave density generally means better durability. If your family participates in a pet parade, a denser weave will fare better over time. 
- Size accuracy: measure your space and compare with the rug’s stated dimensions. Small misfits in scale can ruin a mood and your feet. 
- Return policy: a fair return policy protects you from an unfortunate mismatch between expectation and reality. 
- Care requirements: understand the maintenance needed to keep the rug looking its best. If the care routine seems too onerous, adjust expectations or choose a rug that requires less upkeep.
