## How this game is played: setup, flow, and first impressions
Setup is straightforward: shuffle the money cards, shuffle the property cards, and deal a handful to each player. What follows is a dance of negotiation, quick decisions, and the occasional petty sabotage that would make any family dinner feel like a evenly matched corporate negotiation. The goal is to collect property sets by playing money cards to pay for purchases and by using action cards that let you steal property, charge rent, or swap pieces with your rivals. It is approachable enough for new players, with enough card flavor to keep veteran players from dozing off in the corner.

- Box contents: a standard deck or two in a format that resembles a premium deck of playing cards, plus property cards representing categories like Home, Hotels, or their cash equivalents. The complete 2011 edition includes a full set of action cards and money cards that drive the gameplay loop. If you are opening a sealed box, inspect for any tacky packaging that reveals the wormhole quality of the plastic wrap; sometimes you will find a stray advertisement card that references some old promotional bundle that never existed in your local store. 
- Setup time: 5-10 minutes for a typical 3-4 player game. The more players you have, the more dynamic the negotiation becomes. If someone loves trading and someone else loves heavy negotiation, you will have a party. If you have a player who likes to stall for time, you may finish the game before dessert is served.

## Durability, packaging, and how it ages
Durability in card games is mostly about the cards and the box. The 2011 edition uses standard card stock with a finish that resists minor wear but may show edge wear with heavy shuffles over many years. If you plan to keep the sealed box, store it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent yellowing and cracking of the plastic seal. If the box shows mild warping, it is usually not a fatal sign and can be mitigated by proper storage.

The good news is that the cards themselves, when kept sealed, stay in print quality that makes the game feel fresh when you finally crack the wrap after years of keeping it pristine.
