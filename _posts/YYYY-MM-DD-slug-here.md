## Comfort, maintenance, and real-world tests
In our tests, the rug felt plush without being so deep that you’d lose your steps chasing a rogue die across the floor. It’s comfortable to walk on barefoot and sturdy enough to handle the occasional pet-approved chaos. Cleaning wool rugs is not as intimidating as it sounds, especially with quick spill responses. A damp cloth and a mild detergent solve most everyday accidents, and for more intense incidents, a professional cleaning session keeps the fibers singing for years.

- Vacuuming: Use a vacuum with a gentle setting to avoid a rough pass that could loosen fibers. Avoid beater bars on high settings if possible, as the goal is to keep the texture intact rather than to create a bald patch in the middle of a meteor shower.
- Stains: Blot, don’t rub, to prevent fiber fraying. Treat with a wool-safe cleaner if needed, and test on a small hidden area first to make sure the color isn’t compromised.
- Rotation: Like any good space explorer, rotate the rug every few months to distribute wear and maintain even colors across high-traffic zones.
