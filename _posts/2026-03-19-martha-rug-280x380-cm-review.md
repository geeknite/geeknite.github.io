## Practicality: cleaning, maintenance, and surviving the daily trip through life on a rug

Cleaning a rug this size is not something you do on a whim; it’s an event. The size means more surface area for spills, footprints, pet messes, and the occasional celebratory confetti from a party you forgot you attended. Here are some practical guidelines to keep Martha looking fresh without turning your life into a cleaning montage:

- Immediate action beats stubborn stains. Blot—don’t rub—spills with a clean cloth to prevent deeper penetration. If you rub, you’ll risk fraying the pile and inviting a tiny textile disaster to move in.
- For larger spills, use a mild detergent solution (check the care label) and test in an inconspicuous spot. If you’re worried about colorfastness, consult a pro or do enough research to avoid turning the rug into a science fair volcano.
- Vacuuming frequency depends on foot traffic. A good rule of thumb is to vacuum every week in high-traffic zones and every other week in calmer areas. Use a vacuum without a beater bar on delicate fibers, unless you want to audition for a rug-taxing episode of your life.
- Rotate the rug occasionally. This helps even out wear and keeps sun-exposed areas from fading at uneven rates. If your rug is in front of a window, rotate it so that the light exposure is more symmetric—your future self will thank you when the color remains vibrant enough to photograph for social media without a filter.
- Consider a professional cleaning every 12-24 months, depending on use and household conditions. A professional can refresh fibers, remove deep-seated grime, and extend the rug’s life beyond the two years you’ll forget you purchased it.

## Installation and placement: how to lay down a rug that doesn’t guillotine your furniture plans
