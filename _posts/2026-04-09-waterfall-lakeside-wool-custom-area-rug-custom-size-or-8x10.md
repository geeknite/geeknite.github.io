## Care and Maintenance
Caring for wool rugs is not rocket science, but it does reward a consistent routine. Here is a practical guide that keeps your Waterfall Lakeside looking fresh without turning you into a professional cleaner.

### Cleaning tips
- Vacuum regularly with a brush attachment, following the nap direction to preserve the pile integrity.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to minimize fiber distortion. For stubborn stains, a gentle detergent solution tested on a hidden area is wise.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12 to 24 months, depending on traffic. Wool handles moderate cleaning well, but deep or persistent stains may need professional help.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear and color exposure, especially if you live in bright sunlit rooms.

### Longevity expectations
A wool rug that is well cared for can be a fixture in a room for many years. You may notice tonal variations as the pile compresses and rebounds, giving the rug its own lived-in personality. These changes are normal and part of the rug s aging story rather than signs of deterioration.

## FAQ About the Waterfall Lakeside Rug
### Will it shed
All wool fibers shed a little at first as the surface settles. Regular vacuuming and a brief break-in period usually resolve this, with shedding diminishing over time.
### How should I clean it
Blot spills immediately, use a mild detergent if needed, and avoid abrasive cleaners. For tough stains, professional cleaning is recommended. Always test a hidden section first.
### Can I return if mis-sized
Most retailers offer a standard return policy on rug purchases, but custom sizes may have different conditions. Check the specific retailer policy for custom orders before finalizing your purchase.
### Does it come with a pad
A rug pad is highly recommended for wool rugs, particularly on wood and tile floors. A good pad improves comfort, reduces slipping, and extends the rug s life by protecting the backing and pile.
