## Overview

Fortune Row is not just a bland GM screen with one side face down and the other side face up. It is a compact, story-forward toolkit designed to streamline GM decisions while pushing the narrative into the glorious chaos that is City of Mist. If you like the idea of villains who are actually patrons of a shady arcade, this screen gives you the prompts you need to keep sessions moving while preserving the mystique and the puns. The physical product pairs a two-panel GM screen with a handful of story prompts, quick reference cards, and a handful of players facing prompts that you can deploy to coax out dramatic scenes without reaching for a rulebook every two minutes.

### What Fortune Row adds to City of Mist

If you are new to City of Mist, you probably know the vibe: mythic figures compressed into ordinary lives, a city that kind of looks like every late 90s cyberpunk poster and sounds like a jazz club. Fortune Row doubles down on the GM-facing tools: it provides quick prompts for Clues, Masks, and Moves that are central to the game’s engine. It also streamlines the GM’s life by offering a few pre-made Psychological Effects and adds a Fortune Track that can influence scenes without making your players roll 20s to every action. This is not your grandma’s cardboard; this is a modern, neon-flavored baby that wants to accelerate scenes and keep the table engaged.

### Layout and physical design

The two-panel screen is sturdy enough to survive your table flips between scenes. It features a bold, matte finish with neon accents that scream City of Mist without being obnoxious at the local library. The front panel shows a live-updated quick reference for NPCs, factions, and antagonists, while the back panel holds a condensed set of Moves and Prowl and Investigate prompts that you actually want to reference during play, not only when the players say, Hey GM, what was that rule again? It is not a perfect design, but it is one you can justify with your budget and a quick story about how you spilled coffee on your last screen and vowed to keep your new screen within arm's reach.

- A few rule reminders that you can reference without hunting for a digital doc during a tense scene.

## Design and components

### Art direction and tactile feel

The art direction leans into neon noir with a modern edge. The characters on the cards look like they walked out of a panel from an indie web comic and then got lost in a smoky club alley. It is not hyper-realistic, but it doesn’t need to be. The design nails the vibe City of Mist fans crave: a world where every corner hides a myth and a potential plot twist. The tactile weight of the cards is satisfying; you feel the card edges bite back a little when you shuffle, which is exactly what you want when you are trying to convince a player that a mundane bus stop is actually a mythic anchor for a patron of a secret society.

### Screen layout and GM tools

The front panel is a compact, high-contrast cheat sheet. It gives you the essential moves, a handful of prompts for each major category of your city, and a quick reference to the Fortune Track. The back panel offers additional prompts and a few suggested scene hooks that can help you kick off sessions, especially if your players are stuck in a session’s rut. The real value is that it reduces the number of times you reach for the rulebook, which makes your table happier, your players more engaged, and your coffee laughs a little more caffeinated.

## Play experience

### Quick-start for players and GMs

If you are a GM who loves toggling the mood with a single prompt, Fortune Row is a treat. You can pull out a card, present a minor threat that feels dangerous but not crushing, and watch the table lean into the tension. The quick-reference prompts allow you to narrate moves quickly without losing the sense that your players are shaping the city with every choice. For players, the screen offers a sense of agency: you can reference your own checkpoints and have a few pre-sketched motifs that anchor your Masks in the city’s mythology. It’s not a full cheat code, but it is enough to reduce the time you spend flipping pages and more time building your character’s mythic identity at the table.

### The fortune track and narrative light switches

Fortune Row’s unique twist is the Fortune Track, a narrative resource that doesn’t simply track luck. It acts like a metronome for the session’s pacing. When used well, it can escalate conflict, reveal hidden relationships, or unlock a mythic consequence that resonates with your city’s archetypes. When used poorly, it can feel like a speed bump on a highway of improvisation. The screen gives you a few prompts to trigger Fortune effects that can escalate stakes or reveal thematic echoes, which is a fantastic way to guide session shape without forcing players to churn through the entire mythos manual in a single evening.

### The mythic flavor without gatekeeping

City of Mist is built on a mythic layer stacked on top of modern life. Fortune Row embraces that duality with cards and prompts that emphasize the mythic potential in the mundane, without requiring players to know every hero’s backstory in detail. The screen’s language invites quick, cinematic beats while preserving the game’s core ethos: a city where people are more than what they appear and where every alley tells a story to those who listen closely.

## Notable cards and effects

### Clues that matter

The Fortune Row product includes a handful of Clues that function as mechanical levers for investigators and GMs alike. These Clues aren’t merely plot prompts; they are narrative anchors that connect suspects, places, and mythic motifs. You can use them to pivot scenes toward revelation or to escalate stakes with a sense of inevitability. The best Clues feel like a light bulb going off above a player’s head and a shouted, This is the clue that changes everything, as a coincidence becomes a conspiracy in one clean breath.

### NPCs, factions, and the street-level cast

In City of Mist, NPCs are often layered, morally gray figures who can each carry a mythic pressure point. Fortune Row’s NPC reference cards distill the city’s power brokers into accessible quick references. This makes it easier for GMs to populate scenes with characters who carry motive as well as charisma. The result is that your sessions feel more like a living city, rather than a map of one or two antagonists and a pile of generic goons.

### Moves and quick actions

One of City of Mist’s charms is the concept of Moves that trigger when players do something thematic. Fortune Row adds a succinct shorthand for those moves, letting you adjudicate them with style. The slightly condensed wording helps you avoid the all-too-common pitfall of heavy rulebook crossing—the moment when you forgot which move applies to a given action and the table sighs in unison. With Fortune Row, you have a crisp reference that keeps the pace brisk and the tone cinematic.

### A few caveats

No product is perfect, not even a neon-drenched screen that makes you want to flex your inner noir novelist. Fortune Row can feel a little light on combat-ready content. If your table leans into combat or into heavy system crunch, you might find that the screen emphasizes investigation, mythic perception, and social maneuvering more than tactical encounters. That is not a flaw so much as a design choice that aligns with City of Mist’ core strengths. If you want the screen to be your go-to combat cheat sheet, you might need to supplement with a few extra references or rely on your own improvised shorthand.

## How it compares to other City of Mist supplements

For players who own the City of Mist core rulebook, Fortune Row is a complementary tool rather than a replacement. It doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it retools it, adds a glow-in-the-dark hubcap, and gives you a few extra spokes to keep the wheel spinning during long sessions. In comparison to other GM screens or supplementary packs, Fortune Row stands out for its emphasis on narrative pacing and its accessible prompts. If you have used other GM screens that feel like a wall of numbers, you will appreciate how Fortune Row leans into story-driven guidance rather than raw mechanical density.

### The GM screen in action at the table

We ran a short session using Fortune Row with a party of three players, two longtime City of Mist veterans and one curious first-timer. The session opened with a thin fog of noir and a deadline that felt like it could crumble the city’s fragile charm. As the GM, I leaned on the quick-reference prompts, letting the Fortune Track guide the tempo. The players nimbly leveraged their Masks to tilt the scene toward personal stakes: a vigilante singer with a mythic chorus, a corporate fixer who is really a mythic patron in disguise, and a civilian whose life is a thread connecting two rival factions. The result was a session that moved with momentum, punctuated by moments of witty banter and sudden shapeshift in motive. The Fortune Row screen acted as both a shield and a loudspeaker: it protected the GM from getting bogged down in rule minutiae while amplifying story beats that felt earned and surprising at the same time.

## Pros and cons

- Pros: Smooths GM workflow, strong narrative prompts, visually engaging, helps players stay connected to the mythic vibe, integrates easily with existing City of Mist campaigns.
- Cons: Light on crunch for combat-heavy sessions, some prompts may feel a bit generic until you personalize them, a few players might want more space on the screen for their own backstories and mythic arcs.

## Example session ideas

Here are a few quick concepts you can riff on using Fortune Row as your starting point:

1) The Lost Arcade: A mythic patron has a secret arcade where each game reveals an opponent from the city’s mythic past. The party must decide which legendary figure they will challenge to reclaim a stolen memory.
2) Masks of the Market: The city’s guilds collide in a street market where a rumored artifact changes who holds power. The PCs must decide which mask to wear in order to influence the artifact’s final destination.
3) Neon Choir of Night: A singer whose voice has the power to awaken a long-dormant mythic presence. The screen prompts help the GM escalate the tension as the singer becomes the battlefield’s emotional center.

## Final verdict

Fortune Row is a strong addition to the City of Mist toolkit. It is particularly useful for GMs who want to keep sessions moving and maintain the game’s mythic, noir vibe without constantly flipping to the core rulebook. It won’t fix a table that refuses to commit to narrative play, but it will nudge you in the right direction with style. If you like to run sessions where the city breathes between lines of dialogue and every scene becomes a miniature fable, Fortune Row will feel almost inevitable at your table.

### Final recommendation

- For GMs who enjoy a cinematic pace and want a sturdy, visually appealing source to pressure-test plot points, Fortune Row is highly recommended.
- For players, it’s a fair bet if you want the city to feel alive and the mysteries to feel solvable with just a handful of prompts and a keen eye for the mythic underbelly.
