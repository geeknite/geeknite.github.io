## Maintenance and Cleaning

Regular maintenance is the secret sauce here: a weekly vacuum on a low setting keeps fibers visible and prevents debris from grinding into the weave. For spills, act fast: blot, don’t rub, and use a mild detergent with water if necessary. Avoid harsh cleaners that might strip color or leave sticky residues. If you’re serious about keeping the color fresh, a professional cleaning every 12–18 months is a good bet for high-traffic areas.

Rotating the rug every few months is a small chore with outsized payoffs. It evens wear across the surface and helps the color stay balanced as sunlight drifts across the room. If your space has strong sun exposure, consider UV-blocking window treatments or rotation schedules to prevent one edge from fading more than the other.
