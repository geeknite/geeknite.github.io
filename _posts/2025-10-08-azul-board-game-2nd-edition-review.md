## How to Play

### Setup

Setting up Azul is as easy as pie. Or should I say, as easy as placing tiles? Lay out the player boards, fill the factory displays with tiles, and prepare for a tile-tastic showdown.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, players draw tiles from factory displays and place them on their player board. The twist? You need to match patterns and colors to score points. Sounds easy, right? Wrong! The strategic depth of this game will have you second-guessing your every move.

### Scoring

Scoring is where the magic happens. You score points by completing rows, columns, and specific patterns. But beware, if you can't place a tile, you'll lose points. So, plan carefully and keep an eye on your opponents.

### Pros and Cons

#### Pros

- **Stunning Visuals**: The tiles and artwork are absolutely gorgeous. It's like playing in an art gallery.
- **Accessible Yet Strategic**: Easy to learn but offers deep strategic decisions.
- **High Replayability**: No two games are the same thanks to the varied setups and new game modes.
- **Quick Playing Time**: Perfect for a quick game night session.

#### Cons

- **Tile Dependency**: Players who struggle with spatial reasoning might find the game challenging.
- **Analysis Paralysis Potential**: New players might overthink their moves, leading to slower gameplay.
- **Component Storage**: While improved, the storage could be even better.
