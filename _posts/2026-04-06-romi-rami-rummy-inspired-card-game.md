## How to Play Romi Rami
### Setup
- Shuffle the 60-card deck thoroughly. In a 2-player game, deal 7 cards to each player; in a 3- or 4-player game, deal 6 cards each. This keeps the rhythm brisk and the table not overwhelmed with identity-crisis cards.
- Place the remaining deck face down as the draw pile. Flip the top card to form the initial discard pile.
- Each player keeps their hand hidden from opponents unless you’re playing with kids who insist on a “visible strategy.” In that case, you’ll want to enforce a polite game where the only strategy is to pretend you’re not calculating your opponent’s next move.

### Objective
The aim is to be the first player to reach a target score (commonly 50–100 points per game, but you can adjust for shorter or longer sessions). Points are earned by forming melds and by playing out your hand. Unmelded cards left in your hand at the end of a round earn penalties, which is where the true drama happens—everyone discovers who has the most stubborn run or the wildest wild card in their back pocket.

### Core Rules: Melds, Draws, and Discards
Romi Rami uses two classic meld types inspired by traditional rummy mechanics:

- Runs: Three or more consecutive values in the same color (suit). For example, a blue 4, 5, 6 in sequence would be a valid run.
- Sets: Three or four cards of the same value but in different colors. For instance, 7 of red, blue, green, and yellow would form a set.

Wild cards add color to life and confusion to strategy:
- Romi wilds can substitute for any card in a meld. They’re flexible enough to repair a busted hand, but not so forgiving that they erase the tension.
- Rami Swap cards, when played, allow you to switch positions of two cards on the table—think of it as a shifty mid-round power that can result in a dramatic swing of fortunes.

On your turn you typically do the following:
1) Draw: Take the top card from the draw pile or pick up the top card from the discard pile.
2) Meld (optional): You may lay down any valid runs or sets from your hand. You can extend existing melds on the table by adding appropriate cards if you want to, but you don’t have to.
3) Discard: End your turn by discarding one card to the discard pile.

A round ends when a player goes out by melding all of their cards or by discarding the last card in a legal go-out. If you go out, you score points for the cards left in opponents’ hands. This pushes the mathy satisfaction into the next round instead of just letting the clock tick down.

### Basic Strategy: What to Aim For
- Early Melds vs. Delayed Melds: In Romi Rami, you have a tension between getting early melds to reduce your hand size and waiting for the right wilds to complete bigger runs. Early melding can force others into uncomfortable choices on their turns, but too early can also reveal your hands’ structure and invite targeting.
- Wild Card Management: Use Romi wilds to complete a critical run or set when a precise card feels out of reach. Don’t waste a wild on a marginal improvement; save it for a moment when it changes the lead or seals a go-out.
- Card Counting Light: Since the deck is relatively small (60 cards), you can start tracking which values have already appeared, but don’t obsess. Romi Rami is as much about social deduction as it is about arithmetic.
- Discard Strategy: Throw away cards that threaten to help others complete their melds. If you see an opponent demonstrating a love for a particular color or value, you’ll know what to prune from the center of the table.

### Variant Notes: Kid-Friendly and Quick Games
Romi Rami adapts well to a few house rules that keep the session snappy:
- The 2-player variant encourages more aggressive draws and quicker dries of the well. If you want a speedier experience, lower the score target to 40 and force a 12-card deal instead of 7.
- A cooperative mode is possible by pooling melds toward a shared objective, turning competition into a team challenge against a card-counting AI (aka the game’s internal difficulty). This is perfect for family nights where you want laughter to be louder than rivalries.

## Strategy and Skill: When to Lean on Luck and When to Lead with Wits
Like many light-to-midweight card games, Romi Rami isn’t about one perfect strategy; it’s about flexing with the moment. Here are a few practical guidelines to squeeze out more from your play sessions without turning it into a full-on brain teaser:
- Pattern Recognition as a Resource: The quicker you recognize a potential run or set in your opponents’ discards, the more you can influence the game’s direction. It’s not just memory; it’s situational planning.
- Wild Cards as Tools, Not Crutches: Use Romi wild cards to complete a critical meld or to expand an existing one that’s on the cusp of completion. Resist the urge to burn them on marginal improvements unless you’re about to go out and end the round with a flourish.
- Timing the Swap: The Rami Swap card is exactly what it sounds like—swap positions of two cards on the table. Don’t overuse it; a well-timed swap can disrupt an opponent’s plan and keep you in the hunt without tipping your strategy’s hand.
- Hand Management: Don’t hoard your hand to the point of instability. Romi Rami rewards making decisive plays that pressure opponents to adapt, not armoring your hand into stone.

In casual play, these tactics translate into more dramatic outcomes and more laughs at the table. If you’re a competitive group that loves chess-like planning, you’ll still enjoy Romi Rami; you’ll just need to adjust your tone to keep it light and friendly.
