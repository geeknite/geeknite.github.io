## Ergonomics and space efficiency: why this desk fits the real world

Perhaps the most underrated factor in a desk purchase is how the height, depth, and layout work for your body. The L-shaped design gives you freedom to position your monitors at eye level on the longer leg, while the keyboard and mouse sit comfortably on the tray or the shorter leg’s surface. If you’re using a standing desk converter or a sit-stand desk, check clearances because the combination can be tricky but not impossible.

A more ergonomic approach includes:
- Ensuring your chair supports a slight knee bend without the desk edge pressing into your thighs.
- Keeping your monitor at about an arm’s length away and at eye height to prevent neck strain.
- Positioning the keyboard so your elbows sit at about a 90-degree angle for long typing sessions.
- Using the keyboard tray to avoid bringing your wrists up to the desk height during long coding sessions.

## Assembly tips and tricks: getting it right the first time

- Sort bolts by length before you begin; it speeds things up and reduces the “did I use the wrong hole?” drama.
- Use helper hands for the long L joints; alignment is easier when two people can hold pieces steady.
- Route cables as you go; you’ll thank yourself for not having to wrestle a nest later.
- Don’t overtighten; the laminate won’t be grateful and you’ll understand the meaning of “warp” when you try to close a cabinet door.
