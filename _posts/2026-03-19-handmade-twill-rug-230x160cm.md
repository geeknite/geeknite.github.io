## Care and maintenance without turning life into a lab experiment

Maintenance is where the rug earns its keep. Regular vacuuming on a gentle setting helps remove dust and micro debris without beating up the fibers. Because the weave is tight, you’ll want to ensure your vacuum head isn’t too wide or aggressive, which could snag or pull at the surface. If a spill happens, the recommended approach is to blot gently with a clean cloth and avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers and push you toward a stubborn stain resistance scenario you didn’t sign up for.

Stains deserve special attention, especially from coffee, tea, or red wine. The general approach remains: blot, treat with a mild detergent solution, and allow to air dry. Don’t saturate the rug; test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. If you encounter a stain that refuses to leave, professional cleaning is worth considering. The goal is to preserve the texture and color long-term.

Practical tips: rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in rooms with direct sunlight on one side. A rug pad underneath provides grip, floor protection, and an extra layer of underfoot comfort. For cleaning frequency, plan routine vacuuming weekly and deeper cleaning every 6 to 12 months, depending on use and environment. If you’ve got pets or little humans who track every adventure into your space, slightly more frequent rotation and cleaning can be a lifesaver.
