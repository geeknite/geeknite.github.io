## Care and maintenance without turning life into a lab experiment

Maintenance is where a good rug earns its keep. Basic care should include regular vacuuming with a gentle setting to remove dust and micro debris. Because the weave is tight, you should be mindful of the vacuum head size to prevent snagging. If a spill happens, the best approach is to blot gently with a clean cloth—avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers and encourage a stubborn stain resist. For most common spills, it is best to act quickly but calmly, like a software update you actually want to install.

Stains deserve special attention, especially those from coffee, tea, or red wine. The recommended approach is to blot, treat with a mild detergent solution, and allow to air dry. Do not saturate the rug, and test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. If you find a stain that refuses to leave, you may consider professional cleaning, which is often the best option for complex issues or stubborn discoloration. The goal is to keep the texture intact and preserve the color over time.

Practical tips: rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in rooms with direct sunlight on one side. A little rotation goes a long way toward preventing a halo effect around high-traffic zones. A rug pad underneath not only protects the floor but also adds underfoot comfort and keeps the rug flat and even. In terms of cleaning frequency, you can handle routine vacuuming weekly and deeper cleaning every 6 to 12 months depending on use and environment. If you live with pets or tiny humans who track every adventure into your life, a slightly more frequent rotation and cleaning schedule can be a lifesaver.
