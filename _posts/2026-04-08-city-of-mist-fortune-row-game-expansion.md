City of Mist is the kind of game that makes you spill coffee on your notebook and then pretend it’s a cryptic clue from a noir deity. Fortune Row, the latest expansion, promises more mysteries, more masks, and a little more danger than your last character sheet signing a mortgage. If you thought the streets of New Shanghai or the City of Mist neighborhoods were crowded, wait until Fortune Row waltzes in with a new block of city, a new cast of misfits, and enough twists to make a magician drop their wand in shock. This review is a full field report from the neon-lit alleyways of Fortune Row, where every fate card might be a yes or a maybe and every fate needs a good alibi.

## Overview
### What Fortune Row Actually Is
Fortune Row is a structured expansion that broadens the setting and offers a curated set of new mysteries, new arcs, and a pinch of momentum that can push a campaign from a slow bake to a blazing chili of cosmic capers. It leans into the mythic noir vibe that City of Mist does best: ordinary people discovering extraordinary powers, then having them tested by a city that never sleeps, never forgives, and never mutual funds your dreams.

The spine of Fortune Row is a new district within the city—Fortune Row itself—a place where luck is tangible, outcomes are negotiable, and the line between luck and manipulation is thinner than a cheap coffee cup. It comes with a fresh set of character options, new mechanics to handle luck and fate, and a batch of story seeds designed to spark session zero into a proper direction without forcing you to play a specific archetype. If you want to keep your players guessing, Fortune Row will happily misplace their expectations and then return them with a bow and a mysterious envelope.

### Why Now and For Whom
If your table has squeezed all the juice out of the base game’s caseload and you’re itching for a new playground, Fortune Row offers a compact module that can slot into existing campaigns or stand on its own as a little mini-saga. It’s especially nice for groups that enjoy the noir vibe, the city as a character, and the ongoing joke that luck wins battles but loses when you forget to bring snacks to the table. The expansion is generous without being bloated, and it respects theMeter of the City of Mist system—mysterious, modular, and a little bit magical in a “your decisions matter but fate has the last laugh” way.

## Mechanics and Design
### Core Additions
Fortune Row introduces several mechanics that feel like a natural extension rather than a knock-off add-on. The most notable are: a refined luck economy that measures how often luck tilts in favor of players, a new set of Arc Cards that function as narrative prompts, and a district map that breathes new life into the location-based play. The luck economy is elegantly simple: you earn mentions of luck through scene outcomes and then spend them to catalyze dramatic reversals or character-driven moments. It gives the GM a tool to nudge play toward themes without micromanaging every die roll and die of misfortune.

The Arc Cards serve as a deck of mini-hooks and challenges that GMs can draw on mid-session. They’re not just plot devices; they are prompts that can trigger character backstories, reveal a hidden aspect of the city’s power structure, or escalate tension in a single beat. They allow for a flexible pacing style that suits long campaigns as well as short one-shot mysteries. The arc prompts feel well balanced: enough detail to be useful, enough vagueness to allow improvisation, and enough flavor to feel distinct from the base game’s mystery cards.

### Theme and Tone
Fortune Row leans into the mythic noir tone with flair. The district’s mood—slick, rain-slick streets, neon reflections, and a whisper of ancient magic—works well with City of Mist’s “masks and powers” concept. It’s not a goofy parody of crime fiction; it’s a love letter to pulpy detective stories infused with mythic elements. The writing stays sharp, witty, and concise, which is essential when you’re juggling a dozen moving parts at the table. The design crew clearly had fun with the vibe, and that fun translates into the play experience. Expect lines like a street poet monologuing about probability and fate to appear in the rules text more than once, and yes, you’ll laugh and then immediately try to reflect that humor in your own character’s dialogue.

### Character Layering
Fortune Row adds a handful of new arcs and masks that fit different street personas. If your table enjoys the daily-life-of-a-powerful-hero angle, you’ll appreciate the new masks designed to emphasize luck, influence, and the cunning skill of navigating a city that cheats with style. The new masks interlock with the original ones in predictable but satisfying ways, allowing players to create hybrids like Lucky Strategist or Velvet Negotiator without breaking the system’s established logic. It’s a good addition for players who want to expand beyond the classic three masks per character while keeping the same core system intact.

## Content Breakdown
### District: Fortune Row
The centerpiece district is the star of this expansion. Fortune Row is a block that glistens with opportunity and danger in equal measure. It’s not a flawless paradise; it’s a mosaic of storefronts, back-alley venues, corrupt influence peddlers, and cafes where the barista knows your secret better than you do. The map comes with encounter tables, environmental hazards, and a handful of micro-scenarios to keep sessions feeling fresh. The district itself encourages investigation-driven play: clues cluster around the row’s corners, each one whispering a different facet of the city’s power and a different version of luck’s moral ambiguity. If the base game’s neighborhoods were “coffee shops with supernatural undertones,” Fortune Row is “underground clubs, rowdy magistrates, and a fortune-teller who moonlights as a data broker.”

### New Mystery Cards and Hooks
The new mystery cards are crisp, cinematic, and highly compatible with existing cards. They’re designed to slot into ongoing campaigns or to kick off a fresh arc in Fortune Row. The cards lean toward personal stakes rather than purely external threats, which nicely aligns with City of Mist’s emphasis on mask-driven storytelling. Some cards are explicitly player-choice heavy, giving you the option to shape outcomes by leaning into a personal flaw or a tempting risk. The result is more dramatic tension per session without cramming the table with mechanical overhead.

### Luck and Fate Tracks
The luck economy is a standout feature worth highlighting. It gives players a resource that feels thematically appropriate and mechanically clean. Luck points can be earned through successful roleplaying moments, vivid scene descriptions, or clever manipulation of the city’s myths. They’re spent to accelerate reveals, justify dramatic reversals, or stage moments that feel cinematic rather than purely tactical. The players’ buys into luck feel earned because the GM can see it accrue in clear, narratively satisfying ways. And since luck is a finite resource, it becomes a question of timing: when to call in a favor, when to flip the scene, and when to walk away with a neat moral twist. It is a small but clever twist on the standard City of Mist pacing that yields big payoffs in session dynamics.

### Components
Fortune Row keeps the production quality high. You’ll find a compact rulebook, a set of Arc Cards, a deck of Mystery Cards, character sheets tailored to the new masks, a district map, and a handful of tokens for tracking luck, influence, and misdirection. The art direction leans into the noir aesthetic with a modern shimmer—glossy lines, rain-slick surfaces, neon typography, and a sense that every panel is best read at a certain angle under a streetlamp. The tactile experience matters here; the deck feels sturdy, the map lays flat, and the cards are glossy enough to be comfortable in long sessions without suffering from edge wear. It’s the kind of package that invites you to flip through it and say to your table, this looks fun, this is worth a night of pretending to be a city’s most dangerous lie.

## Play Experience: Session Flow and Pacing
### A Typical Fortune Row Session
In Fortune Row, you begin with a mystery card or arc hook as the table’s north star. Then you scatter a few location prompts around Fortune Row itself and ask players to stake positions on how their masks interact with the district’s peculiar economy. The luck economy provides immediate pacing feedback: as luck accrues, players feel empowered to take riskier actions; as it drains, tension climbs and the mystery tightens. The Arc Cards get shuffled in for a few mid-session prompts; a GM draw can trigger a flashback, a hidden ally reveals their true allegiance, or the city’s mythic undercurrent surfaces in a dramatic reveal.

The GM has a reliable flow: set the scene, present a personal hook tied to a character’s mask, reveal a Fortune Row clue, and let the luck mechanics decide if the group leans into the scene or pulls away. This is where the expansion shines. It respects the players’ agency while offering a scaffold that nudges the story toward thematic beats: loyalty under pressure, the cost of power, and the questionable ethics of using luck to bend reality. You’ll get moments where a character uses a mask to negotiate a power broker with a flourish, only to realize the broker’s own luck is a trap with a loop-back effect. The result is a chain of scenes that feel both cinematic and emotionally honest, which is exactly what a noir RPG should deliver.

### Bossy NPCs and Factions
Fortune Row introduces a handful of well-crafted NPCs and factions that feel like plug-and-play antagonists or potential allies. The city doesn’t exist in a vacuum, and these NPCs remind you of that. They bring a vibe of interlocking motives and backroom deals, with enough personality to keep players engaged even when the main mystery seems to stall. Expect NPCs who treat luck as a currency, who are charmingly dangerous, and who sometimes reveal their true colors in the most mundane circumstances—like a coffee shop barista who knows exactly when to press the right luck button to make or break your day.

## Theme, Tone, and Narrative Possibilities
Fortune Row excels at conveying a sense of living city myth. The mechanics support a narrative arc that is both compact and cinematic. The district feels like a character in its own right; its mood, its hidden corners, and its subtle moral ambiguities push players to examine what it means to rely on luck. Thematically, the expansion leans into questions like: When is luck a gift, and when is it a liability? How do masks shape who we are when the city’s shadows are always listening? How do you balance personal desires against the city’s demands? If you enjoy stories where characters chase a twist of fate only to discover a twist of their own design, Fortune Row rewards that taste.

If there is a potential pitfall, it’s pacing risk. A table that leans heavy on mechanical fiddling might slow down while sorting the new luck economy. The Arc Cards mitigate this by providing one-touch prompts; still, you’ll want a GM who is comfortable with improvisation and who isn’t glued to a prewritten beat sheet. If your party enjoys meticulous world-building, Fortune Row will give you a playground with enough texture to justify a long arc. If you prefer a lean, fast-moving mystery, you may want to treat Fortune Row as a mini-campaign setting rather than a drop-in module.

## Artwork, Production, and Accessibility
The production quality continues to be strong. The art style stays coherent with City of Mist’s branding and enhances immersion without overshadowing clarity. The maps are legible, the tokens are intuitive, and the overall package feels like a premium product you’d be happy to pull out at a gallery event or a gaming night at a friend’s apartment that has better lighting than the GM booth. In terms of accessibility, the expansion does a decent job of clarifying its new mechanics and giving GMs a quick-start guide. The gatekeeping risk is modest; the new energy around luck and fate can be grasped with a careful read and a single playtest session.

## How It Fits With the Base Game
Fortune Row is designed to slot into existing City of Mist campaigns with minimal friction. There are no mandatory prequels. The district acts as a new playground rather than a mandatory rebuild. You can introduce Fortune Row mid-campaign as a “cool expansion arc” that breathes new life into investigations that have started to feel routine. It’s a good choice for campaigns that favor urban exploration, mystery-driven plots, and a recurring theme of luck versus responsibility. If you are a GM who uses light homebrew to tailor your city map, Fortune Row provides enough solid material to seed new mysteries without overshadowing your own world-building.

## Content Quality and Value
For the price, Fortune Row delivers a tidy, well-polished package with a clear value proposition. The new district, the masks, the Arc Cards, and the luck mechanic offer meaningful additions that feel integrated rather than tacked on. You’re getting a lot of narrative leverage for a relatively small price, particularly if you run weekly sessions and want to push your players toward more personal stakes and dramatic complications. That said, if you’re not fond of the noir mood or you dislike game systems that emphasize narrative control over brute dice results, Fortune Row might feel like it leans too heavily into flavor at times. It is, after all, a love letter to mythic noir more than a math textbook on probability.

## Final Verdict
Fortune Row is a welcome addition to the City of Mist ecosystem. It preserves the core interactivity that makes the system so appealing—players narrating their own mythic actions, guided by a city that feels alive—and it injects a healthy dose of new life into the mechanics. The district’s ambiance, the balance of new masks, and the clever use of luck as a resource make this expansion feel like a curated upgrade rather than a random add-on. It’s particularly satisfying for groups who enjoy character-driven plots, personal stakes, and urban mysteries with a pulse. If your table loves mythic noir and you’re itching to chase a series of connected weirdnesses across a single neighborhood, Fortune Row will be right in your wheelhouse.

If you’re deciding whether to pull the trigger, this is the moment to trust your table’s appetite for flavor and drama. Fortune Row provides enough material to run a full arc while remaining modular enough to drop into an ongoing campaign. It respects the system you already enjoy and adds just enough new texture to justify a buy-in. The end result is play that feels fresh, stylish, and a touch mischievous—the best kind of vibe for a game about masks, power, and the unpredictable luck of living in a city that loves you and hates your character sheet at the same time.
