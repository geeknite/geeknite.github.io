## Practical Considerations: Care, Maintenance, and Everyday Realities
Raw wool requires different care than a synthetic or flat weave rug. Here are practical tips that align with Geeknite’s practical-but-fun vibe:

- Vacuuming: Use a vacuum with adjustable height and a gentle setting. A beater bar can be too aggressive for chunky loops, so switching to a suction-only mode or using the upholstery attachment can help maintain the weave without pulling fibers loose.
- Spills: Act fast but calmly. Blot with a clean cloth rather than rubbing, which can push stains deeper. Wool tends to resist some staining, but not all; always test a small area first if you are using any cleaner.
- Cleaning: For bigger messes, avoid harsh chemicals. A mild wool-safe cleaner or a professional rug cleaner is best. Drying is essential; avoid damp, humid spaces that could encourage mildew.
- Sunlight exposure: Direct sun can cause wool to fade over time. If possible, rotate the rug position or use window treatments to manage UV exposure. A little sun is fine, a lot of sun is not a fan of colorfastness.
- Pilling: Loopy textures can pill a little with wear. Gently shave or trim pills with a fabric shaver designed for wool. It’s not a sign of defect; it’s a sign of character—your rug’s natural personality showing up.

A point to call out: natural fibers like wool can shed short fibers in the first weeks of use. This is normal and should taper off as the fibers settle. Regular maintenance will help minimize the issue, but don’t panic if you find a few stray fibers on the surface in the early days.
