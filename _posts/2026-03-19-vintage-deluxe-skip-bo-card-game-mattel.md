## Gameplay Mechanics

Skip-Bo is a game of speed, memory, and the occasional strategic bluff that nothing is going as planned. The objective is to get rid of all the stock cards in your personal pile by playing them into sequential build piles in the center. The center piles climb from 1 to 12, and you can place cards from your hand or your stock pile on those stacks to advance toward victory. The wild Skip-Bo cards act as placeholders for any number, which adds a dash of flexibility and frustration all at once.

### How Skip-Bo Works

Each turn you draw up to five cards in your hand and attempt to play as many cards as possible to the building piles. You can also play cards from your stock pile but only the top card can be moved at a time. When you empty a stock pile you win the round and earn a victory point or tally depending on the house rules. The wild Skip-Bo cards come in as a lifesaver during those moments when you realize that you are one card away from finishing a stack but your deck is mocking you. A single Skip-Bo can act as the missing number, keeping you in the game and forcing others to respect your hooded premonition that you will triumph just after the next coffee break.

### Strategies and Tips

- Keep a mental map of what is likely to appear in the draw pile. If you know a lot of 4s and 7s are exhausted, plan around building piles that favor those numbers.
- Use wild Skip-Bo cards to bridge gaps on central piles that your opponents are racing to finish. It is not just about you it is about stealing momentum from someone else at the table.
- Maintain a small, flexible hand. Do not hoard all your good cards in your stock piles; you want to maintain options for your turns, not just a pile of numbers that will eventually bore you to tears.
- Endgame discipline: as soon as you have a couple of cards that can finish a stock pile, you should consider the risk of giving away the lead to someone else. The clock is your enemy and a well-timed Skip-Bo can be your best friend.
- Tempo matters: Skip-Bo rewards the player who can coordinate hand and stock usage to keep a smooth flow while opponents scramble to catch up.
