## Rails, Mounting, and Compatibility
The rails are the star of this show. They are standard 19 inch rails, and you’ll find most IT gear will pair nicely with them. The four post design means you can align the rails front and rear with consistent spacing, which is a godsend when you are mounting a lot of gear in a tiny space. ## Installation and Assembly Experience
Expect a straightforward assembly, with a few gotchas that lend themselves to the realities of home lab furniture. You’ll get a frame, four posts, rails, casters, and the usual hardware. The trick is to align rails to ensure a level mount for devices across the top and bottom. I recommend a calm approach: lay out the hardware, check the rails for any burrs, and lightly secure everything before you torque down the fasteners. A simple torque wrench is your friend here; over-tightening can warp the frame, under-tightening can lead to unwanted wobble. It’s a small dance, but you will eventually master it.

If you listen to the manual and take your time, you’ll have a rack ready in under an hour, even while sipping coffee and muttering about airflow. If you’re a multitasker with two kids and a dog, you might need to chunk the process into early morning sessions to avoid making your workspace look like a hardware store exploded.
