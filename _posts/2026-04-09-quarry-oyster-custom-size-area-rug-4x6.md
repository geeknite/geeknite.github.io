## Maintenance: Cleaning, Stains, and Longevity

### Vacuuming and General Upkeep
One of the core benefits of a low-pile rug is easier maintenance. Vacuuming on a standard setting should remove most surface dust and pet hair without pulling fibers. Pro tip: use a vacuum with adjustable height so you don’t unintentionally whisk the pile into a spa-like experience for your floor. Regular vacuuming is the antidote to the “rug has entered witness protection” syndrome where every coffee spill becomes a dramatic stain that won’t vanish.

### Spills and Stains: Quick-Action Protocols
Spill management matters more in a rug than in most of your life decisions. The recommended approach is to blot, don’t rub, and use a mild detergent solution if needed. Blotting helps avoid setting the stain, and mild detergents prevent harsh chemical reactions that could degrade fibers or alter color. For stubborn stains, a quick spot-clean with a fabric-safe cleaner can work wonders. If you’re a pet owner, consider a routine seal or stain guard and have a dedicated rug cleaner on standby. Remember: the goal is a rug that ages like fine wine, not like that suspicious leftovers in the fridge you promised to clear out last week.

### Durability in the Real World
In the gearhead terms: the rug’s durability is a function of fiber resilience, pile height, and backing traction. The Quarry Oyster usually uses a sturdy backing to prevent curling and a pile dense enough to resist flattening in high-traffic zones. If you’ve got a daily cosplay of chairs sliding, you’ll appreciate a rug that maintains its edge integrity and stays in place when you stand up mid-sentence during a Zoom call.
