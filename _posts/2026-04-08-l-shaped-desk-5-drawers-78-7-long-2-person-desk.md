Assembly is the usual sea of bolts, Allen wrenches, screws, and YouTube tutorials you’ve come to expect when buying a big desk. The package typically includes all the hardware you need and a surprisingly clear instruction set, though you’ll still feel tempted to declare a minor protest when you realize you’ve created a small, structurally sound sculpture in your living room rather than a desk. Here are the typical steps you’ll encounter:

1. Unpack all components and lay them out like a well-organized rack of tiny Lego bricks.
2. Attach the legs to the frame, using the included screws. Double-check for snugness and cross- threading—nobody likes a loose leg. 
3. Assemble the L-extension portion and secure it to the main surface. Make sure the orientation is correct (left vs right) according to your room plan.
4. Install the drawers into their slots. If a drawer sticks, you’ve probably misplaced a runner or the drawer is misaligned; remove it, realign, and retry. 
5. Route cables through the channels and attach any provided grommets. You’ll probably bleed a small amount of pride as you realize you now have a legitimate workstation management system.
6. Mount monitors, keyboard, and peripherals. Here’s where the fun begins: you’ll adjust height and distance for both comfort and competitive advantage in your next Among Us meeting.

The actual time to assemble depends on your DIY spirit and how carefully you follow the instructions. Expect a few hours from unboxing to full readiness, with the caveat that a coffee break or two may become a design pivot separating “assembled” from “glorious furniture art.” The user manual is thorough and includes diagrams that will please your inner identify-the-component puzzle solver. If you’re the kind that enjoys a little nap in the middle of a build (we won’t judge), this desk is a good pace for a slow and steady approach.

## Ergonomics, Comfort, and Day-to-Day Use

Two-person desks sound like a great idea until you realize that two humans who both want to be the captain of the space can create a dramatic tug-of-war over keyboard placement. The 78.7-inch length helps create two distinct zones, reducing the probability of elbow battles while still letting you share the monitor space if needed. Here are some ergonomic considerations:

- Monitor height and distance: With a large surface, you can place two monitors at eye level with proper stands and still maintain space for a keyboard and a mouse. A good rule of thumb is to position the top of the monitor at or just below eye level and keep the monitor about an arm’s length away. If you’re vertically blessed, you may adjust height to prevent neck strain after long coding sprints.
- Keyboard and mouse placement: The central area of the desk gives you flexibility. For comfort, place the keyboard in a way that your elbows are at roughly a 90-degree angle, with your wrists flat and relaxed. The drawers can house a compact keyboard tray if you prefer to maximize legroom under the desk. It’s a small step with a big payoff for your posture.
- Chair compatibility: The desk surface is generous, but your chair dynamics still matter. A chair with proper lumbar support and adjustable height will help you avoid the classic “slouch into the monitor” issue. The 2-person configuration benefits from chairs that can swivel and slide to the edge without collision—especially if you’re sharing the space with a co-worker or a recurring cat who loves to audition for a desk-warming role.
- Leg clearance: The L-shape helps create dedicated legroom zones. This means you won’t have to test whether your knees fit under the corner like a contortionist; you’ll know, and you’ll appreciate the extra breathing room during long builds.

Ergonomics matters because the best desk is the one you don’t notice while you work. If you can sit for hours with minimal fatigue, you’ve probably found the sweet spot. The L-shape helps you avoid neck and shoulder tension by letting both users position their monitors and keyboards on separate planes without fighting for the same ergonomic sweet spot.
