## Materials and Build: Polypropylene Power
### What is Polypropylene, and Why Should You Care?
Polypropylene (PP) is a plastic polymer known for being lightweight, relatively inexpensive, and highly stain-resistant when treated correctly. It’s a staple in budget-friendly indoor/outdoor rugs because it dries quickly, resists moisture, and isn’t fragile like many natural fibers. For a 2D material, PP is surprisingly happy to be stepped on, spilled on, and left in the rain for a weekend thanks to its hydrophobic properties.

### Construction: Weave Type and Durability
The Luna rug employs a machine-woven construction that yields a flat, dense surface. The tight weave matters: you want fibers that don’t shuffle around the room every time someone drags a chair. A dense weave also translates to better color retention and less visible wear in high-traffic zones. While it’s not a hand-knotted heirloom, it isn’t pretending to be one either—the rug is meant to survive the modern home’s day-to-day life and then some.

### Spot-Cleaning: The Marketing Promise Meets Reality
Spot cleaning is a big selling point. The polypropylene fibers are designed to resist staining and to respond well to a quick wipe-down or a mild detergent solution. In practice, that means you can spray a spill, dab with a cloth, and watch the stain disappear rather gracefully. There are caveats, though: you’ll want to avoid harsh solvents, and long-term neglect (think months of mud and grape juice) will require more involved cleaning. Still, if you’re the kind who believes “spot clean” is a lifestyle, this rug gets you there with minimal drama.

## Cleaning and Maintenance: Spot Clean, Don’t Sweat It
### Routine Care
- Vacuum regularly with a handheld attachment to prevent embedded dirt from grinding into the fibers.
- Shake out lighter dust when possible to keep grit from silently turning into micro-sandpaper on the fibers.
- Address spills immediately with a clean cloth. Blot, don’t rub, to minimize fiber distortion.

### Spot Cleaning 101
For most spills, a mild mixture of warm water and gentle dish soap works great. Apply the solution with a soft cloth, blot, and rinse with a clean damp cloth. For more stubborn stains, a gentle rug cleaner labeled for polypropylene or synthetic fibers can be used, but always test in a small inconspicuous area first.

### Outdoor Washing Tips
If your rug has spent a rough weekend on an outdoor porch, you can give it a rinse with a hose. Some families use a mild brush to loosen ground-in dirt, followed by thorough air drying. Avoid extreme heat during drying to protect the fibers’ shape and color alignment.

### Warning Signals
- Persistent staining that doesn’t respond to spot cleaning.
- Visible fading after long outdoor sun exposure.
- Uneven pile that suggests the fibers have shifted. If you notice this after months of use, you might be looking at a sign to rotate or replace the rug before it shows more wear.
