### Maintenance Cycle and Cleaning Tips
This rug is designed to be machine washable, which is a feature I consider non negotiable in the modern home. Quick spills should be blotted with a clean cloth and mild detergent; avoid harsh chemicals that could dull the color. For a deeper clean, you can run a standard cycle in cold water with a gentle detergent. Air dry or tumble dry on low heat—just like your favorite hoodies, you want to avoid high heat that could shrink or warp fibers. A weekly vacuum keeps dust from embedding itself in the fibers, and if you have pets, a lint roller and a quick brush once in a while helps keep the surface looking pristine. The rug performs best when kept away from direct sun for long periods to minimize color fading, but a little sun exposure now and then is fine and can help you reset the hues after a long Netflix binge.

## Care and Longevity Notes
- Regular vacuuming to remove dust and pet hair.
- Spot cleaning for stains with mild detergent and cold water; blot rather than rub to avoid fiber fraying.
- Machine washing on a gentle cycle in cold water when necessary; do not use hot water as it can shrink fibers.
- Air dry or tumble dry on low heat; avoid high heat to prevent fiber damage.
- Rotate every few months to even out wear patterns and avoid obvious high-traffic impressions.
