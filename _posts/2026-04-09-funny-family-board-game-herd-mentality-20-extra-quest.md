## What is Herd Mentality? A quick refresher
Herd Mentality is a social guessing game designed for groups. The core mechanic revolves around players answering prompts with what they think the majority will choose. The fun—and the chaos—arrives when you realize just how little your friends and family think alike. The player who best mirrors the “herd” with their answer earns points, while the others watch as their own answers spiral into the most inventive misreads imaginable. It’s light on rules, heavy on laughter, and perfect for a wide range of ages, making it a perennial favorite for family gatherings, game nights with friends, and that awkward, goldfish-brained moment you realize you’re somehow related to the person who just said something completely outrageous.

## How to play: a fast overview for up to 20 players
Herd Mentality is designed so you can teach it in roughly 5 minutes and start playing in roughly 10. The basic flow:

- Each round, one player acts as the judge, reading a prompt from the deck. The prompt asks for a belief, a memory, or an opinion that might be shared by the group (e.g., “What’s the best family car trip snack?”).
- The other players write down their answers secretly. The catch is that the answers should be something you believe the majority would choose, not necessarily what you personally believe.
- The judge reads all the answers aloud. The goal is to identify which answer the judge deems the “herd” would pick, awarding points to players whose answer matches the judge’s guess, with bonus points for cleverness or alignment with the herd’s vibe.
- After each round, players rotate as the judge, and the game continues until you run out of cards or patience.

The trick is that in a large group, the herd often spikes with loud opinions and creative twists. The prompts can be wholesome, provocative, or delightfully absurd. The scoreboard becomes less important than the spectacle: the moments when someone reads a ridiculous answer and the room erupts in laughter are where the gold lives.

## Setting up and playing with 4-20 players
A crucial note: while Herd Mentality scales up to a sizable audience, layout matters. A good, spacious table makes a huge difference. If your dining room furniture is built for a cozy family-of-four, you may need to rearrange chairs like a Tetris master to fit everyone in comfortably. If you’re playing with 10–20 players, consider forming scatter-rows so that everyone can see each other’s cards without a wincing neck workout.

For 4 players, the energy remains high, and the social dynamics shift to more personal interactions. The moderator can be someone who can moderate gently yet decisively—no judge should be forced to squint through 8 unreadable cards. For larger groups, you’ll want a couple of volunteers to help shuffle and manage the deck, allowing the host to focus on orchestrating the chaos.

The 20 Extra Quest expansion fits into this setup with a quick shuffle and a fresh icon code on the corner of each card to tell you it belongs to the expansion. ## The competition: Herd Mentality vs. other party games
Herd Mentality occupies a sweet spot between trivia-based party games and quick social prompts. If you enjoy something like Telestrations or Wavelength, you’ll find the vibe familiar but distinct—the emphasis on social reading and misinterpretation remains central, but Herd Mentality leans much more into the guessing game aspect rather than drawing or wordplay. The 20 Extra Quest expansion further differentiates it by injecting prompts that push players to debate, defend, and reconstruct the herd’s likely answer in real-time. Compare this to a game where you simply guess a word: here, the guess is a performance, and that performance becomes the currency of points.
