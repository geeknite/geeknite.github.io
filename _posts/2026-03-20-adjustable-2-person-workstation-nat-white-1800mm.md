### Cable management and organization

Cable management is the unsung hero of any dual-person workstation. Expect a triage system: a central channel to feed the main power through, plus a side channel for per-user devices, and a discreet back panel to hide the rest. In practice, it means you can route power for two keyboards, two mice, two monitors, and the occasional USB hub without turning your desk into a topographic map of tangled spaghetti.

A key advantage of a larger width is the ability to allocate separate cable zones for each user. You can arrange monitor height, power blocks, and USB-C hubs in a way that reduces cross talk and the occasional accidental unplugging incident during a particularly dramatic keyboard pull. The more disciplined you are with cable management, the closer you get to that showroom feel without the showroom price tag.
