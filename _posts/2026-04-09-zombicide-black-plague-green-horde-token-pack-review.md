Why Plastic Tokens Matter

You might be asking: why upgrade to plastic tokens in the first place? The short answer: durability and readability. Cardboard or punched tokens warp, wear, or get lost under the couch cushions faster than you can say “Zombicide.” Plastic tokens resist bending and scuffing, and they hold color longer under intense game-night lighting. They also feel weightier in the hand, which some players equate with a more “serious” vibe on the tabletop. And yes, they’re easier to identify during a dogpile of combat when you’ve got orcish zombies shuffling toward your survivors.

In practice, plastic tokens reduce the number of times you have to replace tokens mid-scenario, which saves time and sanity. You’ll spend less time arguing about whether that green disk is a wound token or a damage marker, and more time arguing about whether your goblin is a tactical genius or just a very lucky dice roller.

Token Handling and Gameplay on the Table

During a typical Zombicide night, players are juggling movement, combat, loot, and a cascade of counters. The token pack helps with the latter by giving you visible, tactile pieces you can reach for without fidgeting through piles of paper. On a crowded table, the plastic tokens stand out just enough to prevent misreads. One practical tip: keep a small token sorter or a mini-tray nearby. It’s easier to separate wound tokens from armor tokens when you’re in the thick of a key encounter. Efficient token handling accelerates the pacing and keeps the adrenaline high instead of turning into a token scavenger hunt.

Durability and Maintenance

Plastic tokens are built to last, but they’re not invincible. Clean them with a quick wipe between game nights to prevent residue from dice oil or players’ sweaty palms from accumulating on surfaces. If you’ve got particularly sticky players, a mild soap or a gentle rinse will do wonders. Just make sure they’re fully dry before you seal them back into their storage tray. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the color or scratch the surface.

If you’re storing this in a humid basement or a humid apartment, consider a small desiccant pack inside your storage box to prevent any humidity-related warping. It’s a small extra step, but it helps keep your tokens pristine for longer campaigns.

How to Buy and What to Look For

If you’re shopping for this token pack, a few practical checks can save you from disappointment:

- Verify the seal integrity: no tears, no re-wrapping, and no obvious resealing attempts. A factory seal should be uniform and crisp.
- Inspect the packaging for label accuracy. Look for the exact product name and compatibility statement for Black Plague and Green Horde.
- Check for tactile consistency. Run a quick feel test: the tokens should sit flat, not tilt, and the edges should be clean with little to no burrs.
- Compare photos from the seller with the product images you’ve seen elsewhere. Subtle color shifts can occur between production runs, but anything that looks obviously off should raise a red flag.
- Confirm that you’re purchasing from reputable channels. If you’re buying from an independent seller or a less-known online marketplace, request clear photos of the seal and the contents before payment.
