## About The Saboteur 2009
The Saboteur is an open world action stealth title set in Nazi-occupied Paris during World War II. You play as Sean, a resistance fighter who starts with a personal vendetta and ends up painting the city with color as you liberate districts. The game mixes driving sequences, exploration, puzzle-like stealth sections, and bombastic set-pieces, all wrapped in a melodramatic voiceover style that might have you thinking you are in a late-night action-movie marathon.

The art direction is one of its strongest talking points. The grayscale visuals with occasional splashes of color are not just a gimmick; they serve as a visual cue for progress and a mood that makes the city feel alive. The world feels large, but not bloated, with enough nooks and alleys to justify the time you spend roaming, listening to radio broadcasts, and collecting souvenirs to unlock upgrades.

## The CIB Complete Experience
CIB stands for Complete in Box, a badge of honor among retro collectors. It means the disc is present, the case is intact, the manual is included, and in some cases a map, soundtrack, or additional booklet is tucked inside. For Saboteur on Xbox 360, the CIB kit typically includes the original case artwork, disc, and a manual. When you open the box, you should feel a little like you are unwrapping a relic rather than a simple cartridge.

Packaging notes:
- The original case usually features the stark black and white cover with a pop of red from Paris' skyline. It is not a fancy box with pop-ups; it is a practical retail box that ages well if stored properly.
- The manual offers a concise primer on the control layout, stealth tips, and a few historical nods. It grows a small museum of your gaming memory as you flip pages that smell faintly of cardboard and ambition.
- A steel or plastic-case insert might be included to hold the disc snugly. In some variants you may find a poster or a small map folded inside; your mileage may vary depending on region and retailer.

## Gameplay and Mechanics
Saboteur mixes stealth, driving, and action in a way that feels like a late 2000s love letter to open world design. The core loop is liberate a district, fight through a wave of enemies, and watch the world shift from gray to color as you bring freedom to the people. In practice, this means you will sneak behind guards, take down sentries with a quiet takedown, hijack vehicles for a dramatic chase, and use a variety of gadgets to outsmart the fascists.

### Stealth and Combat
The stealth system is not as punishing as Metal Gear, but not as forgiving as a modern stealth game either. You have a blend of darkness, cover, and a few moves that make you feel like a spy who also enjoys a good explosion. Combat is generally action-packed; burst into a room, use pistols and melee strikes to clear enemies, then loop around a corner to collect a few health pickups. The pacing is what keeps Saboteur engaging: you can choose a quiet approach, or dial the excitement up to 11 and watch the chase scenes unfold with everything but the kitchen sink thrown in.

### Driving and Open World
The vehicle mechanics lean into the era of arcade driving in action games. Cars feel heavy enough to remind you that you are in a war zone, yet agile enough to weave through Parisian streets. The map is large but broken into digestible segments, with plenty of optional side tasks and collectables that do not punish you for exploring. If you are in it for the pure mechanics, you might find the driving sections derivative. But if you enjoy the sense of momentum and the occasional cinematic moment, Saboteur hits the sweet spot.

### Progression and Color Breakpoints
A central gimmick is the color bloom that washes across the city as you liberate districts from occupancy. The effect is more than aesthetic; it signals your influence on the game world. As you liberate, the atmosphere shifts, radio broadcasts become more hopeful, and the streets begin to feel again like places where people could live rather than just survive. It is a neat conceit that sustains the game’s tempo and gives a tangible sense of progression.

## Visuals and Audio
Visuals in Saboteur are a product of their time. The grayscale with color splashes was a strong stylistic choice that gave the game a unique identity. It also provided a practical way to hide performance limitations by focusing color usage where it mattered most. The environments are detailed enough to feel alive. The city is busy, voices carry through alleys, and the era-specific details—flags, storefronts, and propaganda posters—ensure you feel the World War II setting without turning Paris into a history book.

Sound design is robust: gunshots bark, radios crackle, and the period-appropriate score swells during key moments. Voice acting carries the dramatic weight of a blockbuster film, with lines that occasionally border on melodrama. If you enjoy games with a cinematic vibe and a strong sense of place, Saboteur provides a satisfying audio-visual package that makes the box art feel justified and the disc glow with nostalgic warmth.

## Collecting Perspective: CIB and Value
For collectors, complete in box items carry extra weight. The Saboteur, when found CIB for Xbox 360, can range widely in price depending on region, edition, and how well the manual survived the decades. The physical discs from late 2000s era often have minor scratches but still function, and the box art usually remains robust if stored away from sunlight. In good to very good condition, CIB Saboteur is a satisfying find for a shelf dedicated to open world games or WWII-themed titles.

Price guidance is not a fixed law, but a compass. On the open market you may see copies priced anywhere from a modest 15 to 40 dollars, with rarer variants going a bit higher. If you are a collector of complete-in-box European editions or promo materials, you might see extras that push value higher. The key to successful Saboteur hunting is patience. The game is not a hot commodity; it is a pleasant, underrated gem that surfaces with some regularity in retro gaming shops and online marketplaces.
