## How to set up and play: a quick primer

### Setup

- Shuffle both Phase 10 decks separately or together, depending on your desired chaos level. 
- Deal ten cards to each player (phase 1) or as per your preferred starting rule. 
- Place the remaining decks in the center, creating a draw pile, and flip the top card to start the discard pile.

### Turn flow

- Draw a card: either from the draw pile or the top of the discard pile. 
- Attempt a phase: you can lay down your phase cards when you meet the requirements (such as sets or runs). 
- End your turn by discarding a card. 

### Scoring and end game

- Each completed phase moves you forward on your personal track to finish. The player who empties their hand first will likely claim bragging rights and the eternal seat at the “cool uncle” table. 
- If you’re playing with house rules, you can add penalties for uncompleted phases to spice up the tension—just be prepared for negotiations and possibly a documentary crew.

### House rules worth trying

- Two-phase starter: two decks share a single table, requiring players to coordinate across decks to finish, which adds a delightful layer of coordinated chaos. 
- Time-boxed turns: 30 seconds per turn to keep the game moving and the peace+quiet at risk. 
- Silent mode: no talking except when calling out a phase; good for younger players who insist on dramatic pauses.
