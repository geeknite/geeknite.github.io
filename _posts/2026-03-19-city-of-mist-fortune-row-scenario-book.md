## Overview
City of Mist has always been a velvet rope moment between myth and neon, a tabletop RPG where you play as everyday people who are also legends. Fortune Row, the latest scenario book in the City of Mist line, drops you into a district that fuses glitzy fortune telling, neon rain, and the kind of intrigue that only happens when luck is a character with a personality disorder. If you thought you knew how a noir mystery feels, Fortune Row leans into the idea that fate can be negotiated, bribed, or hacked by people with the right charisma and the right conspiracy tattoos.

In short, Fortune Row is not just a collection of one shots; it is a sandbox for masks, mirrors, and misdirection. It offers a set of scenarios that feel like episodes in a late night TV serial—each one a little brighter, a little louder, and a little more dangerous than the last. It is also a surprisingly friendly entry point for GMs who want a city full of secrets but lack a full city full of extras to populate it. The book presents ready to run adventures, but it also invites you to remix, bend, and even break a few rules to chase a better joke or a sharper twist.

Fortune Row wears its neon with pride. The chapters wear a palm-print map on their sleeves, and the dice rolls feel like a drumbeat backing a montage sequence in a movie that knows exactly when to wink. As usual with City of Mist, the charm is less about what your character is and more about what your character might become if the city flirts back with a clever tip-off or a bad decision. And Fortune Row has a little more flirtation than most.

## What Fortune Row is up to
Fortune Row is a scenario book that leans hard into two classic City of Mist strengths: investigative play and mythic microdrama. In practice, that means you get:

- A strong sense of place: Fortune Row is a district that refuses to be a backdrop. It is a character in its own right, a place where fortunes are both made and mislaid on the same dusty checkered alley.
- A modular structure: Each scenario functions as a self-contained mystery with a clear start, escalating reveals, and a final choice that broadcasts how the players want to rhetorically define luck in a mythic city.
- New mythos flavors and clues: Expect brand-new logos that slot neatly into existing City of Mist masks. These are not gimmicks; they are narrative levers you can pull to align your crew with a stronger emotional arc.
- A GM-friendly toolkit: Each entry includes scene outlines, non-player character stats that feel personal and dangerous rather than generic, and hooks that can be dropped into any ongoing game, not just Fortune Row stories.

The tone walks a tightrope between slick noir and goofy mythic swagger. You’ll find lampshade humor balanced with hard choices, and the kind of cliffhangers that make your table lean in with a grin and a grimace at the same time.

## Inside the book: Contents and layout
Fortune Row unfolds in a compact, well-curated package. The layout reads like a well-organized map of a city you want to explore at 2 a.m. after a long session and several cups of questionable coffee. The pages are generous with pull quotes, illustrated icons, and sidebars that give you both atmosphere and actual mechanical flavor.

- An inviting setting primer: The district’s vibe, factions, and undercurrents are laid out with enough color to inspire a GM to riff but not so much that you drown in world-building.
- 6 to 8 scenarios: Each entry offers a complete arc, with a recommended party level, time scale, and a list of potential misdirections to keep players guessing.
- New assets and cards: Expect fresh tools you can add to your City of Mist kit, such as a new Fortune die mechanic in certain scenes or a small deck of Fate Cards that can be drawn for a quick narrative twist.
- GM guidance: Practical advice on pacing, tone, and content warnings. The book leans into the fact that Fortune Row can be chaotic in a fun way, but it also respects the need for clarity so players never feel punished for curiosity.
- Artwork and map: Neon art direction with a blend of streetwise grit and mythic symbolism. The map includes key locations and potential encounter zones that you can drop into your campaign with minimal prep.

The overall feel is something like watching your favorite crime show get hijacked by a pantheon of trickster gods — funny, haunting, and surprisingly cinematic.

## How the scenarios feel in play
If you are a GM who loves surprises, Fortune Row is your friend. The scenarios are designed to be tackled in multiple ways, with bite-sized acts that can be stretched across a single long session or split into two shorter evenings. The book gives you optional objectives that reward roleplay and deduction equally, so even players who are shy about heavy lore can still contribute meaningful actions.

- Investigation with a pulse: Clues are not just tiles on a grid; they are social breadcrumbs that lead players through scenes rich with character moments. The clues are written to feel logical and tantalizing, not random. When a PC follows a thread, the scene pays off with a reaction that makes the city feel responsive, dangerous, and a little bit ridiculous in the best possible way.
- Mythic turns that fit the mask system: The City of Mist engine thrives on the idea that everyone can unlock a part of the mythic persona by leaning into their Masks. Fortune Row doubles down on this, giving players opportunities to reveal corners of their Masks in ways that raise the stakes without dulling the mystery.
- Pacing for table talk: The book includes pacing tips that encourage you to lean into banter, misdirection, and dramatic reveals. The best scenes leave a crack of opportunity for a clever line or a last-second deduction, letting players feel like they earned the win with wit as much as strength.

One of my favorite things about Fortune Row is how it handles misdirection. There are moments in every scenario where the obvious answer would ruin the mood, so the book teaches you to lean into the tension rather than force a slam dunk. That approach creates table moments that feel earned rather than handed to you by a railroading GM. If your table likes clever reveals and dramatic irony, Fortune Row pays off with style.

## The new tools and how they work
Fortune Row introduces a handful of new ideas that slot into the existing City of Mist toolkit without turning the system on its head. Expect to encounter:

- Fortune Dread and Fortune Luck: A minor mechanical layer that adds a risk-reward dynamic to key decisions. The more you chase a lucky outcome, the more you might uncover consequences. It creates a nice narrative loop: you gamble, something interesting happens, and you decide whether the payoff was worth the cost.
- New Logos and Faction Elements: The book adds a couple of logos that feel thematically aligned with luck, fate, and streetwise cunning. They slot into the established Logos mechanics, offering fresh angles for your investigations and social encounters.
- Fate Cards and Clues: A light card-driven mechanic that can be used to add a sense of spectacle during scenes. The cards are flavorful and can be easily integrated into a standard session without requiring a new set of rules to learn.

These tools are designed to feel familiar to players who know City of Mist and accessible to newcomers who are still figuring out the system’s vibe. They blend into the density of Fortune Row rather than stand out as a shiny standalone gimmick, which is a pretty good balance for a scenario book.

## Art direction, accessibility, and production quality
The visual language of Fortune Row leans into the old neon noir with a modern twist. The art is bold, the typography is clear, and the layout respects your eyes after long dicey sessions. The neon pinks and electric blues feel like a postcard from a dimension where fate wears a leather jacket and a fedora. Accessibility choices are thoughtful: thumbnail maps are crisp, scene descriptions are concise, and the book provides guidance on tone and content so GMs can tailor the experience to their table, including players who prefer lighter horror or stronger roleplay moments.

The production values are solid for a scenario book. The maps are legible, the icons are intuitive, and the overall package feels sturdy enough to survive the occasional spill of soda and the inevitable dice-rolling tantrum. If you have a shelf where your RPG books live in giggling harmony with your other neon-noir obsessions, Fortune Row fits in pleasantly beside the rest of your City of Mist library.

## Practical tips for running Fortune Row at your table
- Start with a mood, not a map: The district’s tone is a character, so begin by describing the city’s hum—the way rain sounds when it falls on a fortune teller’s awning, the way a streetlight flickers just when someone important is about to say something honest for once.
- Let players own the mysteries: If a PC starts asking the right questions, follow their lead. Fortune Row rewards ingenuity and social maneuvering, not just the sharp knife of combat.
- Use the Fate Cards as party improv fuel: If players stall, flip a card for a dialog prompt or a sudden twist. It keeps the momentum without forcing you to break the city’s rhythm.
- Keep mask moments crisp: City of Mist shines when Masks surface in high-stakes moments. Let a character reveal a new facet of their Mask as a response to a scene, not as an abstract rule.
- Prepare for the unexpected: Fortune Row thrives on improvisation. Have a couple of contingency scenes ready for when players decide to go off-script or when a clue leads somewhere you did not predict.

If you are a GM who loves a good misdirection sequence, Fortune Row will teach you a few tricks about steering a noir mystery without strangling your players in plot weeds. The best sessions feel like a conversation with a city that knows your secrets and is itching to tell you new ones.

## How it compares to other City of Mist supplements
Fortune Row stands out by leaning into a location-driven arc rather than a single overarching campaign arc. It offers a strong sense of place and tight, repeatable scene structures that are ideal for ongoing campaigns or for a quick run on a club night. Compared to other scenario collections that hit you with a dozen loosely connected ideas, Fortune Row feels crafted to be dropped into a live city with minimal prep while still preserving the charm of sequels and recurring threads.

The new tools are well-balanced and compatible with the core system. They don’t feel like a re-skin or a gimmick; they feel like natural extensions that give you more ways to make a story feel personal to your table. If you crave a City of Mist experience that centers on place, reputation, and a moral puzzle about luck, this book is a particularly satisfying pick.

## Maps, assets, and how to use them in play
Fortune Row comes with a detailed map of the district and a handful of location cards that you can lay out on the table to cue scenes quickly. The map is a helpful anchor for players who like tactile references as they narrate their progress through the narrative maze. The location cards help a GM pivot when a scene goes off-script, providing a quick way to reframe stakes and reintroduce a fresh dynamic.

The assets section is a goldmine for quick sessions. You can pull a clue, an NPC motive, or a micro-dramatic beat from the prompts and drop it into your ongoing City of Mist campaigns as needed. This is especially handy if you want to weave Fortune Row into a currently running Noir city arc rather than running it as a stand-alone adventure pack.

## The verdict: who should buy Fortune Row
- GMs who love neon-noir mood and city mystique: This is tailor-made for you. The book respects your time and your table, giving you exactly the right balance of atmosphere and actionable content.
- Players who like social intrigue as much as combat: If your gang is into exploring rival factions, social maneuvering, and the mythic stakes of everyday life, Fortune Row gives you a playground that matches your taste.
- Groups looking for a compact, repeatable set of sessions: The structure is friendly to people who want shorter long-term arcs rather than a single long epic. It’s a good fit for a monthly campaign or a dedicated club night.

That said, Fortune Row isn’t a universal fix for all table vibes. If your group thrives on sprawling, open-ended campaigns with heavy dungeon-delving, you might want to pair this with longer-form City of Mist adventures to keep the momentum high while still allowing Fortune Row to shine as a mood-enhancing interlude.

## Final thoughts and rating
Fortune Row feels like a love letter to the part of City of Mist where a city breathes with secrets, and the people who live there decide what those secrets mean to them. It is clever, stylish, and surprisingly practical for both new GMs and veterans who want a fresh angle without sacrificing the core promise of the system. The scenarios are varied enough to accommodate different playstyles, while the pacing tips and GM guidance help you keep the table engaged through twist after twist.

If you want a game night that reads like a neon-noir screenplay but plays like a collaborative improv piece, Fortune Row is a strong pick. It delivers a distinct flavor without demanding a new rulebook, and it nudges the players toward character-driven solutions rather than brute-force clears. It’s not a blockbuster campaign, but it is a smart, well-crafted kit for people who enjoy the City of Mist vibe and want to spend more nights in that mood.
