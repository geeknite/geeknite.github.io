## Setup and Maintenance: Getting to First Goal

Anyone who has assembled a piece of furniture without a manual has lived this moment: you lay out the pieces, realize the screws are either too long or too short, and consider abandoning the project to become a professional couch potato. The VEVOR 140 cm table avoids most of that chaos with a sensible hardware kit and a well-illustrated manual. It may take a couple of hours the first time around, especially if you’re balancing chaos theory with a streaming show in the background, but the process is satisfying rather than frustrating.

Here are practical setup tips that’ll help you avoid the “I messed up the alignment and now the ball ricochets into the kitchen” moment:

- Inventory the parts before you start. A magnet ring will help you keep track of tiny washers.
- Leave the table partially assembled and tension the rods gradually. If you torque too early, you risk warping or misalignment.
- Lubricate the rods lightly with a silicone spray or light oil. Avoid heavy lubes; nobody wants a slippery table that makes shots feel unsatisfying.
- Align the players during installation. A misaligned row can ruin shots. If one team’s players are consistently out of sync, adjust the collars and tighten the screws.
- Test with a few mock passes before the first actual game. It’s easier to adjust at this stage than mid-tull-up tournament chaos.

Maintenance-wise, you’ll want to keep the table clean and dry. Dust and oils from hands can accumulate on the rods, which may affect spin and accuracy over time. A quick wipe-down after sessions and a periodic rod lubrication schedule will help maintain feel and reliability. If you’re serious about longevity, consider applying a light protective coating to the laminate surface to prevent scuffs and keep the finish looking fresh through weeks of intense color commentary from your living room audience.
