## What is Skip-Bo?

Skip-Bo is a card game where players race to empty their Stock Piles by playing cards in ascending order onto Build Piles. The trick is that you can use Skip-Bo cards as wild numbers, acting as any number. The game is about both planning and bluffing in a very friendly way, with the dicey part being that your opponents can steal your moment of glory with a well-timed Skip-Bo wild card move.

In plain English: draw cards, lay down runs, juggle your stock pile, and celebrate when you manage to place a giant stack of numbers onto Build Piles. The table is basically a scoreboard of who said "maybe" first and who said "yes".

## How to Play (Quick Start)

Skip-Bo’s objective is to use your Stock Pile and your hand cards to build up Build Piles in ascending order from 1 to 12. The wild Skip-Bo cards can stand in for any number. The rules are short, but the laughter and groans that come with misplacing a card are long.

Turn structure (short version):
- Draw to five cards in your hand.
- On your turn, you may play from your hand or Stock Pile onto Build Piles, as long as the numbers ascend.
- Skip-Bo wilds are wild and can fill gaps when needed.
- When your Stock Pile is empty, you win.

The sense of "one more turn" is real here. The luck of the draw can propel you to victory, but the sequencing and discarding decisions are what keep it interesting.

## Strategy Nuggets (The Quick Poker Face Guide)

Here are a few pro-tips that will help you not feel like a complete flop on the first round:
- Keep an eye on your stock pile; plan to cycle through cards in a way that opens Build Piles efficiently. A few moves ahead, a couple of wilds saved, and you’ll feel like a genius even if luck doesn’t agree.
- Use wild Skip-Bo cards strategically to unlock more advantageous sequences rather than filling small gaps early on. It’s like using a rocket pack to reach the top shelf, not the cookie jar.
- Watch opponents’ discard piles; sometimes a card you need is skipped by someone else and dropped into their hand rather than a wasteful discard. Reading table psychology helps more than you’d expect.
