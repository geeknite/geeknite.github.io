## H2: How to Use the Heath 21239 Like a Pro
### H3: Seed Selection and Feeding Schedules
Butterfly seed blends can vary by brand and batch. Start with a moderate amount and observe which birds show up first. If you’re lucky, you’ll get a steady stream of resident species—house sparrows, finches, maybe a chickadee or two—plus occasional visitors who swing by for the visual drama of a bird feeder’s daily life. A good practice is to rotate seed mixes every few weeks to keep the visitors curious and engaged. Rotating seed types also helps you avoid mold formation from overly damp, stale seed. In humid climates, you may need to clean the tray more frequently and ensure the top dome is securely sealed to minimize rain intrusion.

### H3: Positioning for Maximum Impact
Position matters. A feeder that looks at your neighbor’s barking dog or a busy street may experience constant distractions, but ultimately birds care about food availability and safety. Place the Heath 21239 in a sheltered spot with partial sun, away from heavy wind channels if possible. A nearby shrub or tree provides perches for birds between feeding sessions and can also act as a natural predator deterrent by giving feathered patrons an easy retreat route. The moral of the story: good positioning reduces stress in birds and reduces seed waste in your yard.
