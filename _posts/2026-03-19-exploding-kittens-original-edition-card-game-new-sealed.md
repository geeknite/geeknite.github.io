## Rules Overview
### Setup
- Shuffle the deck.
- Give each player a Defuse card or two (depending on house rules).
- Deal four cards to each player.
- Leave the remaining deck as the draw pile.
- Fill gaps if you started with too few cards, because chaos loves even numbers.

### Turn structure
On your turn you may play an action card or draw from the deck. See the Future lets you peek at upcoming cards; Attack pushes risk onto others; Skip ends your turn without drawing; and Shuffle reshuffles the deck, breaking any fragile patterns you thought you had. The fun part is that you can chain a few actions in a single turn to create a whirlwind of calculated risk and glorious mischief.

### Endgame and survival
The last player who has not drawn an Exploding Kitten wins. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you have a Defuse, you can defuse and return the kitten to the deck. If you don’t, you’re out of the round. Simple, savage, and perfect for when you’re trying to salvage a night that’s slipping into mild disaster.

### Variations and tactical notes
There are plenty of ways to approach the base rules. Use See the Future to map a few draws, hold Defuse cards for late-game salvation, and leverage Attack to build pressure on the most fragile player. For longer sessions, you can chain rounds with a simple score track and declare a winner after three or four rounds. ## Gameplay Deep Dive
The core mechanic is deceptively simple: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten. See the Future cards provide partial information about the next few cards, enabling cautious planning. The Attack card creates tension as each player juggles risk and social defense—do you target the most aggressive player or the one with a suspiciously confident pout? The Shuffle card resets a stubborn draw pattern just when you thought you had a rhythm. Cat Cards bring a delightful resonance to rounds, inviting playful banter, taunts, and cheerfully smug cat memes that somehow feel like permission to misbehave a little.

### Cat Cards and completion rewards
Cat Cards are a key flavor component that also adds a tiny layer of strategy. They often come in pairs, where you might need two of the same Cat Card to trigger a specific effect or to win a variant of a round. This requirement nudges players toward early game card tracking and light strategic calculus, but the emphasis remains on social chaos and shared laughter rather than serious optimization.

## Strategy and Tips (Expanded)
- Early game: chase Defuse cards with a light touch, because early Defuses reduce the immediate risk of elimination and keep your ego intact for a few extra rounds.
- Mid game: monitor the deck’s progression; if Exploding Kitten cards appear plentifully, tighten risk management and consider pushing the table into a few safe, chicken-out plays to avoid becoming the hero of a bad night.
- Late game: balance the right Defuse count with perfect timing on an Attack or See the Future to swing an unlikely victory your way.
- Interaction: treat other players as potential allies or scapegoats; this is psychology as much as it is card-counting in a circus tent.
- House rules: spice things up with cat-themed penalties or playful limericks for those who draw the Exploding Kitten. The aim is more laughter than humiliation, but a pinch of humiliation goes a long way when you’re already in the chaos zone.
