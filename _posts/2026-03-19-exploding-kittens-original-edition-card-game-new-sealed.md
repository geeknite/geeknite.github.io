## Rules Overview
### Setup
- Shuffle the deck
- Give each player a Defuse card
- Deal four cards to each player
- Leave the remaining deck as the draw pile
- Draw a card to fill any gaps if you started with too few

### Turn structure
On your turn you may play an action card or draw from the deck. The See the Future card lets you peek at upcoming cards, the Attack card pushes risk onto others, the Skip card ends your turn without drawing, and the Shuffle card reshuffles the deck.

### Endgame and survival
The last player who has not drawn an Exploding Kitten card wins. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you have a Defuse, you can defuse and return the kitten to the deck. If you do not, you are out of the round.

### Variations and tactical notes
There are several ways to approach the base rules: use See the Future to map out several draws, hold Defuse cards for late-game saves, and leverage Attack to build pressure on the most fragile player. If you want to approximate a longer session, you can chain rounds with a simple track of scores and declare a winner after three or four rounds. ## Gameplay Deep Dive
The core mechanic is deceptively simple: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten. The See the Future cards give you partial information about the next few cards, letting you plan a course and minimize risk. The Attack card can produce a cascade of palace-level tension as each player tries to survive a singe of the deck. The Shuffle card can reset a fearsome draw pattern just when you thought you could predict the next three cards. Cat Cards bring silly resonance to the rounds, and players often participate in playful banter around cat-themed jokes and playful taunts.

### Cat Cards and completion rewards
Cat Cards come in matching pairs; you often need two of the same Cat Card to trigger a desired effect or to win a particular variant. The requirement to collect a pair adds a tiny extra layer of strategy in play, but the emphasis remains on fun and social interaction rather than heavy tactics.

## Strategy and Tips (Expanded)
- Early game: establish a stable approach to obtaining Defuse cards, which reduces your immediate risk of elimination
- Mid game: monitor the deck's progression; the presence of many Exploding Kitten cards calls for tighter risk management
- Late game: the right defuse count and the right timing on an Attack or See the Future can be the difference between victory and elimination
- Interaction: treat other players as potential allies or scapegoats for your survival; this is as much about psychology as it is about card counts
- House rules: you can spice things up with house rules like playful cat-themed penalties or limericks for those who draw the Exploding Kitten
