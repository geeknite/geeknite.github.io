## Why Are Japanese Prices Cheaper?

This isn't a grey market trick. It's straightforward economics:

**🔻 Weak yen:** The JPY has been significantly weaker since 2022. As of March 2026, ¥1 ≈ €0.0056. Nintendo set Japanese prices when the yen was stronger and hasn't adjusted them upward, creating an arbitrage opportunity.

**🏪 Amazon marketplace competition:** Third-party sellers on Amazon Japan compete on digital code prices, pushing them below even the Japanese eShop listing.

**💎 Nintendo's Western pricing:** Unlike Sony and Microsoft, Nintendo rarely discounts first-party titles. A mainline Mario or Zelda might get 20% off once a year if you're lucky. This makes the Japanese price gap even more dramatic.

## How to Buy: Step-by-Step Guide

The whole process takes ~10 minutes the first time and 2-3 minutes after that.

### Step 1: Create an Amazon Japan Account

Your existing Amazon account won't work — you need a separate one at [amazon.co.jp](https://www.amazon.co.jp). Enter your name, email, and password. No Japanese address or phone number needed. Use Chrome's auto-translate for English navigation.

### Step 2: Buy a Digital Game Code

1. Find your game on [switch2digitalgames.geeknite.com](https://switch2digitalgames.geeknite.com)
2. Click **"Buy on Amazon JP"** — takes you directly to the product page
3. Verify it shows **"オンラインコード"** (Online Code) — not a physical cartridge
4. Pay with international Visa, Mastercard, or Amex

After purchase, the 16-character code (e.g., `B0DY HKWM R9JX P4FT`) appears in your email and order history.

### Step 3: Change Your Switch Region to Japan

1. Go to [accounts.nintendo.com](https://accounts.nintendo.com) in a browser
2. Navigate to **User Info → Profile**
3. Set **Country/Region** to **Japan** and save

⚠️ Your eShop balance must be **exactly zero** to switch regions. Existing games, saves, and online subscriptions are unaffected.

### Step 4: Redeem the Code

Open the Nintendo eShop on your Switch → **Enter Code** → input the 16-character code → confirm. The game downloads automatically.

### Step 5: Switch Back to Your Home Region

Return to [accounts.nintendo.com](https://accounts.nintendo.com) → change region back to your country → save. Done.

The game is permanently yours: updates work, online multiplayer works, DLC works, save data stays intact. Everything functions identically to a locally purchased game.
