## Practical tips and quick hacks
- Start by laying out the layout on the floor before you commit to screws. It’s amazing how many times you’ll discover you preferred a slightly different arrangement after actually standing the pieces up.
- Use labeled zip-ties to organize power cables at the back of the troughs. It keeps the visible surface clean and makes Zoom calls look professional even if your laptop battery is taking a nap.
- If you operate with multiple monitors, consider a monitor arm for the primary screen. It frees valuable desk real estate for notebooks, sketches, or a second coffee mug that has learned to balance on the edge without tipping.
- Create a dedicated station for reference materials and chargers. A small pedestal or shelf can transform chaotic desk space into a workflow corridor where you can sprint from design to delivery without losing momentum.

## Maintenance and care tips
- Clean the surface with a soft microfiber cloth and a mild furniture cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the laminate finish.
- Place a desk pad or blotter under your keyboard to reduce micro-scratches from daily use. It also adds a nice frictional surface for your mouse to glide gracefully.
- Periodically check bolts and screws to ensure the frame remains square and stable. A loose bolt is the silent performance killer, like a software update that asks for a restart during a critical build.
- If you notice fingerprints or smudges, a light wipe with a microfiber cloth should restore the finish without leaving streaks. For stubborn spots, a touch of water on a microfiber cloth usually does the trick without chemical drama.
