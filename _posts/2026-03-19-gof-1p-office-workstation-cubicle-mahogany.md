## Assembly and setup experience
Let’s talk about assembly, because a good piece of furniture that comes flat-packed can be either a hero or a villain depending on the instruction sheet. The GOF 1P’s assembly is very much the “step-by-step manual, no heroics” kind. The parts are labeled, the hardware is color-coded, and the included Allen key does a decent impression of a tiny exoskeleton for your hands. You’ll need a clean, flat workspace to lay out all panels and screws so nothing gets misplaced like a critical file in a busy inbox.

Most people report a couple of hours from box to fully standing workstation. The most common stumbling block is aligning desk panels with the frame after you bolt the first few joints. A helper can speed things up, but it’s not strictly required. Pro-tip: sort all screws by size before you start and loosely thread everything first. This prevents the “two screws too short, one screw too long” melodrama that plagues many a DIY furniture night.

If you’re concerned about tools, you’ll be pleased to know that the kit uses standard hardware that most households already own. You’ll be using an Allen wrench, a screwdriver, and maybe a rubber mallet for coaxing panels into alignment. The result, after you tighten the last bolt, is a solid, stationary unit that won’t politely wobble during a high-stakes Zoom meeting.

## Maintenance and care tips
- Clean the surface with a soft microfiber cloth and a mild furniture cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the laminate finish.
- Place a desk pad or blotter under your keyboard to reduce micro-scratches from daily use.

- If you notice fingerprints or smudges, a light wipe with a microfiber cloth should restore the finish without leaving streaks.
