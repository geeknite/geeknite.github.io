## Assembly and setup experience: more granular tips
Assembly is the make-or-break moment for many flat-pack purchases. The GOF 1P is designed for the kind of user who tolerates a little DIY drama but appreciates a successful reveal. The parts are labeled, the hardware is color-coded, and the included Allen key doubles as the tiniest exoskeleton you’ll ever wield. You’ll want a clean, flat workspace to lay out panels so nothing gets lost in the abyss of an assembling night.

From experience, the time-to-assembly typically lands in the 2 to 4 hour window depending on your helper count and how many times you second-guess your alignment. Common stumbling blocks include panel alignment with the frame after you bolt the first joints and discovering you ignored one screw type for a different panel. Pro moves:

- Loosely thread everything first to plan alignment, then tighten progressively as you go.
- Group screws by size and color to avoid the mystery of which one goes where in the 73rd step.
- Have a magnetic tray or empty egg carton ready for small hardware so you don’t accidentally launch a tiny bolt into the abyss of your couch cushions.

Tools required are standard issue — Allen wrench, screwdriver, and maybe a rubber mallet for coaxing panels into alignment. The result is a solid, stationary unit that won’t wobble during a high-stakes Zoom meeting or when you aggressively reach for that stapler you swore you placed exactly here last week.
