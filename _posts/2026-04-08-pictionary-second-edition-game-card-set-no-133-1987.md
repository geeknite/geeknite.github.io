## Preservation, Care, and Longevity
Preserving vintage cards requires a gentle touch. Here are a few practical tips we tested and can recommend:

- Keep it dry: humidity is a stealthy menace. Use silica gel packs in the storage box, preferably those that don’t look like candy. Avoid basement or attic storage if possible, as temperature flux can warp a card’s flexibility.
- Shield the corners: a little card-sleeve magic goes a long way. Clear sleeves protect the faces while letting the art shine, and they keep the cards from bending during enthusiastic rounds.
- Light exposure: direct sunlight is not your friend. If you display the deck, rotate it frequently so you avoid yellowing and print fade precisely where the eye lands on a long autumn afternoon.
- Handling: be mindful of oils from your fingers. Wash hands beforehand or use clean, dry hands—your cards will thank you by staying crisp and legible for longer.

If you ever decide to replace or supplement the deck, seek period-appropriate reproduction sets or carefully sourced used decks from reputable sellers. It’s a fine line between keeping the aesthetic authentic and inviting modern wear-and-tear that can degrade the experience. In the end, part of the joy is in seeing a card that’s clearly been loved, not just preserved in a glass case.
