## Performance in Real Homes
### Cleaning and Maintenance
A major selling point of polypropylene-based rugs is stain resistance. The Rachelle rug is no exception. Light spills can usually be blotted up quickly with a clean cloth; for tougher stains, a mild detergent solution and a gentle hand will do the trick. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can dull the fibers or remove color; the goal is to preserve the crisp navy and white contrast without turning the rug into a muddy gradient. Regular vacuuming with a beater bar on a low setting will keep the fibers looking sharp. Some Geeknite editors attempted to use a stiff-bristled brush; that approach can cause hair and debris to become embedded, so it is best to stick with gentle suction for daily maintenance. In daily life, this rug handles pet messes with relative ease compared to non-treated fibers, though you should tend to accidents promptly to prevent staining.

## Practical How-Tos for Buyers
### Determining the Right Rug Size
Measure your seating area first. A rough guideline: the rug should extend beyond the sofa by about 6 to 12 inches on each side. If you have a coffee table, you want the rug to offer space under the table and at least the front legs of the sofa on the rug. For smaller rooms, the 5x8 option is your friend; for larger rooms, the 6x9 is your choice, provided the layout accommodates it.

### Cleaning the Nautical Way
Spills happen. If something red spills, act quickly with blotting and a mild detergent solution if needed. Do not scrub; the stripes are designed to stay crisp with careful cleaning. For routine maintenance, a low-suction vacuum or a vacuum with a soft nozzle will protect the pile. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic zones like entryways or TV-watching zones.

### Lighting Considerations
Natural light helps the navy pop. If your room is naturally dark, consider warm lighting to avoid the navy feeling blue-tinged and heavy. The stripe pattern reflects light in interesting ways, so you may notice subtle shifts in color tone throughout the day. This can be a feature rather than a bug, giving your space a dynamic quality that changes with mood and time.
