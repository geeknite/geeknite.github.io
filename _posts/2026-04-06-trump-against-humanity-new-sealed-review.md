## How to Play (If You’ve Managed to Survive the Box Opening)
Trump Against Humanity follows a familiar structure: a Card Czar draws a Black Card, everyone else submits their best White Card, and the Card Czar picks the funniest or most fitting pairing. The game thrives on the chaos of political satire, but to get the most out of it, you’ll want a crew that’s comfortable with poking fun at everyone—including the players, the system, and, yes, the questionable decision-making in the political arena.

### Basic Rules in 6-ish Steps
1) Each round, the Card Czar reveals a Black Card. The prompt might be something like “The best way to deal with a chaotic administration is _____.”
2) Players choose one White Card from their hand that they think best answers the prompt.
3) The Card Czar collects, shuffles, and reads the submissions aloud without revealing who played what (for the dramatic irony).
4) The Card Czar picks the winner for that round.
5) The winner keeps the Black Card as a point (or not—your house rules may differ).
6) Rounds continue until you’ve either run out of cards or your attention span does a victory lap around the living room.

If you want to up the absurdity, the party mode adds time pressure and extra voting twists, which often leads to the moment where someone shouts “I literally cannot even” and everyone nods in guilty agreement.

### Sub-variants and House Rules
- Quick Draw: Everyone submits their White Card at the same time; fastest submission wins the round. Expect chaos, fast snarks, and accidental table shoves.
- The Conscience Clause: If a White Card hits too close to home, you can vote to discard and redraw—great for avoiding hard feelings while still keeping the giggles intact.
- Endgame Gambit: The final round earns extra bragging rights and perhaps a shield against that one friend who thinks they’re the Aquaman of puns.
