## Performance: Indoor and Outdoor

### Weather Resistance

The rug is billed as both indoor and outdoor. In practice, that means it’s designed to shrug off sun, rain, and the occasional spill of grape soda during a weekend get-together. The polypropylene fibers resist moisture and dry quickly after a rain. In our test, a light drizzle left a damp patch that dried within a few hours. Mildew? Not in this version of reality—though if you leave it soaking in a puddle for days, you’ll risk mold growth, as with any outdoor textile.

### Traction and Safety

On slick decking or tile, the rug can be a bit slippery without a rug pad. We recommend pairing it with a non-slip rug pad if you’re placing it on polished surfaces. On rougher surfaces or outdoors on gravel, the rug stays in place sufficiently without pad; just keep an eye on corners that may curl if left unweighted.

## Cleaning and Maintenance

### Routine Cleaning

Vacuuming weekly helps keep the Pearl looking bright. The low pile means you won’t fight with tangled fibers, and the color pattern hides small dust specks that would embarrass your white socks. A quick sweep or gentle vacuum is usually enough to keep things looking tidy between deeper cleanings.

### Deep Cleaning

For stubborn stains or a mid-season refresh, you can hose the rug down with mild soap and a soft brush. Avoid hot water on adhesives or backing because you want to maintain shape. If the rug dries slowly or you notice a musty odor, lift and air-dry in a shaded area rather than letting it vanish into the darkness of your laundry room. Do not machine wash—the fibers aren’t built for the agitation of a big washing machine.

### Care Tips
- Let the rug dry thoroughly before placing back on the floor to avoid moisture getting trapped under furniture.
- For pet accidents, blot with towels, then wash with a mild enzymatic cleaner appropriate for outdoor fabrics.
- Store off-season by rolling and securing with a strap; keep in a dry area to prevent mildew.
- If you’re storing it in a garage or shed, consider rotating to prevent sun bleaching where it is most exposed.

## Size, Shape, and Placement

### Availability and Sizing

Rachelle Pearl is available in multiple sizes to fit different spaces. For a small balcony or entryway, a 5x7 might suffice. For larger patios or open-plan living rooms, a 6x9 or 8x10 can anchor the space without dominating it. If you’re decorating a hallway or want a runner, look for elongated formats. Always check exact measurements on the product page before purchasing to avoid a rug that’s too small or too large for your vibe.

### Rug Pads and Floor Compatibility

A rug pad is a good idea for interior spaces to extend life and keep the rug from sliding. On wood floors, a pad reduces wear and helps prevent curling at the edges. On outdoor tile or concrete, a pad can add a touch of cushion and keep things from sliding around during windy days. If your floor is particularly rough, choose a pad with a moisture barrier to reduce sweat equity on your rug’s underside.

### Placement Tips

- Define a seating area on a large deck or sunroom to create a visual zone.
- On a balcony, align the rug edge with the railing to maximize usable space.

## Durability and Longevity

### Everyday Life Test

The rug’s construction and material choices are designed to survive everyday life: pet paws, stroller wheels, accidental drips, and the occasional sprinkler mishap. It’s not a magic shield against every mess, but it’s resilient enough to withstand months of routine use without looking like your living room attacked by a cartoon villain.
