## How to Play: Quick Start Guide

The beauty here is the quick start. You crack the box, you read roughly two lines on the back, you pick a color, and you’re in the game. The official rulebook is short, sweet, and surprisingly forgiving. We’ll outline the gist below (without spoiling the fun for your first game night).

### Setup (2-3 Minutes)
- Place the board in the center. Each player picks a color-spot pawn and places it at the starting tile.
- Shuffle the action cards and place them face down. Place a timer within reach.
- Decide on a difficulty mode if the game offers variants (some kids want extra challenge, some want extra snack breaks).

### The Turn Structure (One Round in 3 Easy Steps)
1) Draw a card: The card tells you what the action is. It might require you to perform a simple task, answer a goofy question, or move your pawn a certain number of spaces. The instructions are usually short and readable, which helps keep younger players engaged.
2) Act: Execute the instruction before the timer runs out. It could be something physical (like a tiny hop across the board), or a verbal prompt (name three animals that spell a certain color, etc.).
3) Race to the Finish: After each action, you move closer to the finish line. Some variants award bonus moves for speed or style; others keep things simple for calm evenings.

### Winning the Game
The victory condition is typically reaching the finish line first, but many households modify the endgame for drama: a “secret prize” for the winner, a silly victory dance, or awarding a sticker that marks the crown of “Champion of Snack Time.” The key is to reward participation and laughter rather than ruthless domination—this is, after all, a family game night, not a gladiatorial arena.
