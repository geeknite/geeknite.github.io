## How to Play: Core Rules with Launcher Flair
UNO Attack follows the same basic rhythm as standard UNO, with some added spice from the launcher. Here is a quick walkthrough to get you from setup to laughter:

1) Setup: Each player receives a hand of cards (usually seven). The remaining deck sits nearby, and the launcher sits at the center as a colorful, ominous sentinel. Determine who goes first by the usual method or by a totally arbitrary coin flip. Shuffle, deal, and prepare for takeoff.

2) Objective: Get rid of all your cards before anyone else. The first player to do so wins the round and, typically, bragging rights that last until the next family game night.

3) Playing a turn: On your turn, you must match the card in the discard pile by color or number, or play a Wild card that can switch colors. If you cannot play, you typically draw from the deck. The launcher adds a pop of chaos when activated. If a player triggers the launcher, it spits out a random number of cards into the center, which affects every player’s plan in unpredictable ways. You might suddenly have to play two cards at once or draw a handful of penalties—this is part of the charm and the occasional headache.

4) The launcher rule: When someone triggers the launcher, the house rules often say you must draw from the center pile that the launcher spits out. The exact effect can vary by edition, so check the pictured instructions in your box. It can range from a single card to a brutal cluster of five or more. The unpredictability is the core feature that makes UNO Attack feel different from vanilla UNO.

5) When a player has one card left: You must call UNO. This is a standard UNO rule, but the launcher’s dramatic soundtrack makes that moment feel like a high-stakes moment of truth rather than a casual check-in with the void.

6) Winning: The first player to empty their hand wins the round. The laughter and chaos that follow are part of the magic. You may decide to tally points across rounds just like regular UNO for a longer evening of family competition.

### The Strategy Behind the Chaos
People often worry that the launcher removes strategy, turning it into a press-and-pray situation. Not so. While the launcher injects randomness, there is still plenty of skill in the day-to-day selection of cards and the timing of wilds. Here are some tactical tips to maximize your odds without becoming that boring math person at the table:

- Manage your color wheel: If you hold two colors in your hand, you can pivot when the launcher hits. Consider keeping at least two reliable color options as the round wears on.
- Wild card timing: Use Wild cards strategically to flip the color that benefits your hand and hurts others. Resist the urge to burn a Wild card on a trivial moment; the best players deploy this tool when a color change will keep them in the game while others scramble.
- Observe others’ patterns: The person who hoards two-to-four cards of a single color probably has a plan. If you can disrupt that plan with a launcher-induced shuffle, you might swing the round in a single dramatic turn.
- Read the room: Family game nights are social experiences. If you see someone getting flustered, you can adjust your own tempo. Sometimes letting a launcher trigger early can defuse a tense moment by turning the evening into a shared spectacle rather than a cutthroat duel.

## Variations, Expansions, and House Rules
If you feel the need to dial up or dial down the chaos, there are a few paths you can take:
- Launcher intensity settings: Some editions allow for adjusting how many cards the launcher spits out. Use a softer setting for younger players or longer gatherings.
- Speed rounds: For a quick burst of competition, keep rounds to a few minutes and tally points at the end. This is great for a family night with multiple activities in the same evening.
- Cooperative twist: Instead of racing to zero cards, players team up to collectively hit a target number of rounds, aggregating points to determine a winner. This keeps the social aspect front and center while reducing the intensity.
- Themed variations: Some people like to create custom rule cards or color-coded events with themed nights like retro, sci-fi, or fantasy to keep the game fresh and entertaining.

## Practical Tips for Your UNO Attack Session
- Pre-game prep: Clear a safe play zone, make sure the table is sturdy, and have a snack zone away from the launcher to prevent accidental triggers during munching. It’s not a safety drill, but close.
- Sound management: If you have a light sleeper in the house, consider a shorter launcher session or a designated kids’ play area with a volume-limited sound option. You want laughter, not a pre-bedtime meltdown.
- Card handling: Cards can pop out with surprising velocity. Keep a soft surface nearby to catch stray cards and avoid chasing them under the couch or behind the TV. That is how you end up with a mystery deck a year later.
- Etiquette matters: UNO rules can spark debates. Use simple house rules for disputes before the launcher frenzy starts. A calm approach in the first round leads to smoother rounds later.
- Storage and maintenance: After the game night, store the launcher in its original box or a dedicated bin. Avoid forcing it into a cramped cabinet unless you enjoy amplifying the drama of an accidental launcher launch next time you pull the box from the shelf.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues
- Launcher not activating: Check for a proper battery installation, reseat the battery pack, and ensure nothing is obstructing the mechanism. If the problem persists, consult the manual or reach out to the support section of the Hasbro site.
- Cards jam: A gentle tap to dislodge cards can solve minor jams. If cards repeatedly jam, consider a house rule where the launcher is used only on specific turns to prevent constant disruptions.
- Unresponsive display: Wipe the surface around the display and replace batteries if needed. A glitchy display can throw off the entire rhythm of a round, so address it before you declare a winner.
