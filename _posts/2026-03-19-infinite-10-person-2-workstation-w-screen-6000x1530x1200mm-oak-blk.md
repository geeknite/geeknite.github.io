## Ergonomics and Comfort: Does Size Equal Comfort?

First, a caveat: bigger desks don’t automatically solve every ergonomic woe. But they do give you more room to implement sane arrangements. The Infinite system offers generous under-desk knee space, with the option to slightly adjust the desk height or slope to suit standing-desk hybrids. If you want to turn this into a standing-friendly environment, you’ll need to add risers or a platform approach, and perhaps invest in anti-fatigue mats that won’t look ridiculous under oak veneer.

From a comfort standpoint, you’ll want to plan monitor height carefully. The typical posture recommendation sits monitors at or slightly below eye level to prevent neck strain. The two-block arrangement of this system allows you to align monitors and keyboards so that the collective line of sight across the row remains consistent. The result is a workspace that can facilitate focused coding, collaborative reviews, or whiteboarding sessions with minimal neck craning.

The chairs, of course, are a separate conversation. A desk this wide deserves chairs that support posture without turning the entire room into a ballistic exercise in mobility. If your team rotates between tasks—design, development, QA—you’ll benefit from chairs that offer easy swivel, adjustable lumbar support, and wheels that don’t abrade the oak floors. It’s worth pairing this desk with a thoughtful seating plan to avoid the “huddle of doom” where everyone blocks the other’s screen with a knee arc and a headphone cable.

The marketing name “Infinite” is aspirational. In practice, you’ll want to map out how this 6-meter expanse fits into your floor plan. ## Build Quality and Maintenance: Longevity Considerations

Oak veneer carries a premium vibe and a reasonable degree of resilience under typical office wear. The finish should resist the occasional spill and wipe clean with standard furniture cleaners. The black powder-coated metal tends to hide fingerprints and dust well, which is nice given that you’ll likely dust less frequently when you’re busy fighting the eternal task of “one more sprint.” The integration of screens means you’ll want to keep the surface free of direct impact; a dropped coffee mug on a screen panel could turn a productivity hero into a vertical star of a very sad “crack.” A good rule of thumb is to maintain modest protective edge guards and place a small protective pad near the monitor arms to cushion any accidental knocks.

Durability-wise, this system is built to withstand daily office life: chairs rolling, people adjusting height, and the occasional maintenance person who appreciates a clean, fast-to-clean surface. For heavy commercial environments, you may want to look at the abrasion ratings of the veneer and ensure the finish matches your heavy-traffic expectations. In short, treat it right, and it will treat you well—most of the time.
