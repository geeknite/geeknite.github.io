## Durability and weather resistance
 Pine is a friend in moderate climates and an adversary in extremes. The wood’s natural appeal is matched by its susceptibility to moisture and temperature shifts. The set responds well to sealing; a good sealant can dramatically extend the life of the wood by reducing warping and water ingress. In dry, sunny summers, you can expect minimal movement; in wet seasons, seasonal checks and resealing help maintain structural integrity. If you live in an area with freezing winters, it is wise to store the modular pieces indoors or cover them with weatherproof furniture covers when not in use. Regular maintenance, such as cleaning and applying a protective layer, will keep the color consistent and the joints tight.

## Maintenance and care
- Clean with mild soap and water; avoid high-pressure cleaners that can raise grain and push dirt into pores.
- Apply a protective sealant at least once per year, or more often in rainy climates. A thin coat is enough; avoid pooling sealant on joints which can trap moisture.
- Inspect hardware every few months; tighten screws as needed to prevent wobble.
- Use furniture covers during off-season to protect from snow, rain, and the mysterious economy of falling leaves.
- Consider adding a color-safe UV protectant if you plan to keep the set near strong sun for extended periods.

### Cushions and fabric choices
If you opt for cushions, choose fabrics with high UV resistance and water repellency. Outdoor fabrics designed for marine use or those with solution-dyed fibers tend to withstand sun better and resist fading. Darker fabrics hide stains but may show pet hair more clearly, while lighter colors amplify every spill. If you do not want the cushions to compete with your plants for attention, pick neutral tones and add color with throw pillows or outdoor rugs. Cushions with removable, machine-washable covers will save you many a weekend morning cleaning session.
