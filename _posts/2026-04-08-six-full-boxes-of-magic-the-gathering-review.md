## What you’re getting: the unboxing reality

### Box-by-box reality check

Six full booster boxes typically means a lot of potential: sixty packs if each box has ten packs, sixty cards per box if each pack has one card? Just kidding—mostly. Realistically, a standard modern booster box contains 24 packs, each pack with 15 cards, so a single box can yield up to 360 cards. Multiply that by six and you’re flirting with well over a thousand cards in a single haul. In our case, we’re told “over 600 cards,” which suggests a mix of bulk, multiple sets, and perhaps some pre-modern density in the pull rates. ### Condition and sorting: how to avoid Cardboard Overload Syndrome

Condition matters almost as much as color—especially when you’re dealing with six boxes. Expect a range: from near-mint to well-loved, with plenty of-play test cards that have seen a few more battles than your kitchen knives have seen onions. Sorting is your friend here. A practical approach is to sort by set, then by color, then by rarity. If you’re a completionist, you’ll sort by card type (creature, instant, enchantment, artifact, planeswalker, land). If you’re a modern deck builder, you’ll sort by mana costs and synergy potential. And if you’re a collector, you’ll obsess over foil variants and old-border nostalgia, which is a vibe all its own.

Tool-wise, invest in labeled binders, a stackable tray system, and a whiteboard that helps you track what you’ve pulled and what you still crave. A good sorting workflow is like a dungeon crawl: you’ll encounter locks (uncommons), keys (rares), and monsters (mythics). The life total? Your patience. The loot? ## Sorting strategies and practical tips

### Quick-start sorting methodology

- Separate by set first. This helps you quickly identify rare blocks and nostalgia hits.
- Within each set, sort by color; then by card type; finally by rarity.
- Create a separate “bulk” pile that can be traded for store credit or sold in bulk on a market. You’ll be surprised how fast your workspace becomes a sanctuary for actual gameplay when the bulk is out of sight.

### Storage solutions that won’t break your budget

- A sturdy 3-ring binder for uncommon and rare cards. 
- A clear-tray organizer for foils and mythics. 
- Dividers for quick sorting: color, set, rarity, and whether it’s playable in your favorite formats. 
- A clean workspace with a mat to prevent the dreaded “bleed” on the kitchen table that makes you question your life choices.

### Card care and handling tips

- Handle cards by the edges to preserve the surface gloss. 
- Use card sleeves for anything you actually intend to play (or resell with confidence). 
- Avoid sunlight; it ages the ink and your mood. 
- If you drop a valuable card, don’t panic. A few deep breaths and a precise search around the desk can rescue the situation more often than you’d expect.
