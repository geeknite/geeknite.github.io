## Building Around Ravenform: Deck-Design Concepts and Practical Tips
If you’re serious about including Ravenform in a deck, here are some practical shaping tips to maximize its usefulness without turning your board into a budget-anarchy mess:

- Pair with cheap evasive threats: The more pressure you can apply with your small creatures on early turns, the more Ravenform can salvage a couple of trades or transform a board state in your favor. Look for complementary cheap threats that benefit from being bigger or more resilient after the spell resolves.
- Leverage tempo-friendly synergies: In a deck designed to win on the back of efficient trades, Ravenform’s value comes from you stabilizing the board while you assemble a more decisive threat. Avoid overloading on too many similar effects; balance is key.
- Consider race dynamics: If your opponent is a heavy aggro deck, Ravenform can be a lifebelt that buys you a turn or two—the sort of “one-turn answer” that is worth more in the late game than you might expect.
- Ensure color-synergy alignment: If your color identity is focused on disruption or creature manipulation, Ravenform slots in as a natural follow-up or a control-oriented pick. It’s less effective if your deck relies on a different engine entirely.
- Include tutor-like support carefully: If your cube or draft environment rewards predictability, Ravenform shines with targeted draws—but don’t overdo the density of similar effects, or you risk cluttering the deck with low-impact cards.
