## Overview

What you’re buying here is a highly stylized, retro-martial-art-inspired piece rather than a martial arts workhorse. United Cutlery’s Honshu line leans into the aesthetics of historical blades while leveraging modern manufacturing to keep costs reasonable and mass-producible. The 12in variant specifically aims at cosplay, display, and light, casual handling. It’s a nippy little blade with a big room for imagination.

The blade is 1065 carbon steel. In blunt terms, 1065 is a carbon alloy with about 0.65% carbon content. It’s the kind of steel that balances toughness with the ability to hold an edge, though it won’t win awards for corrosion resistance the way higher chromium stainless steels do. In a sword that’s meant for display or gentle cosplay, 1065 is a sensible choice: it’s tougher than many decorative blades and less prone to chipping than some milder steels, provided you treat it with reasonable care. The blade’s finish is a utilitarian polish rather than a gleam-from-the-moon kind of shine, which fits the Honshu look—an ode to historical shapes without claiming to be a museum piece.

The handle is brown wood, chosen for its warm, approachable aesthetic. It’s not a carved antique, but the grain and tone give you visual texture that reads as authentic without demanding hours of maintenance. The fittings are metal, locked in place with a basic but sturdy construction that holds up to tabletop display and light handling. This is not a sword you swing at weights or use for cutting practice; it’s a prop and display sword designed to look the part while staying friendly to your budget and your shelves.

## Maintenance and Care

Maintenance for a decorative or cosplay sword should be straightforward:

- Wipe the blade after handling to prevent fingerprint and oil buildup.
- Keep it dry; avoid prolonged exposure to moisture to reduce potential rust on the 1065 steel.
- For display, a light coat of oil (like 3-in-1 or mineral oil) can help maintain the finish, especially if you’re planning to keep it out where humidity fluctuates.
- Store it away from direct sunlight if you want to preserve the wood handle’s tone and avoid fading of any finish.

Because this model is designed for display and cosplay rather than heavy use, you don’t need to go overboard with maintenance. A little TLC now and then keeps it looking camera-ready.

Pro tip: if you’re combining this blade with another prop or a specific costume, consider ordering a matching stand or wall mount. It helps the piece look intentional rather than just “placed there.”

## Real-World Care and Longevity Thoughts

When you’re dealing with a decorative blade, the goal is longevity in appearance rather than long-term hard use.
