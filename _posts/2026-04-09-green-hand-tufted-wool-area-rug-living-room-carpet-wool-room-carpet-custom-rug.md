Care, maintenance, and longevity

Wool is known for resilience and natural stain resistance but not for becoming a crusader against spills. The recommended care involves regular vacuuming to remove dirt and grit that settle into the fibers. Use a vacuum with a soft brush attachment to avoid pulling fibers; rough vacuums are fun in theory but not in practice because they make you cry when you realize you’ve shaved a nap off your rug’s personality.

For spills, blot—don’t rub. Rubbing can push liquid deeper into the pile, which is a fancy way of saying “don’t use a rough roommate to fix your mess.” Mild detergent solutions can be used for spot cleaning; test a hidden area first to ensure colorfastness. If you’re dealing with stubborn stains, a professional cleaning is recommended. And yes, rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear—your floor deserves the limelight equally across all seasons.

Eco-friendly notes

Wool is a renewable resource, biodegradable, and often produced with less water waste than some synthetic fibers. Of course, the real eco-win is that wool is naturally flame-resistant and self-extinguishing, which is a fancy way of saying this rug won’t contribute to chaotic candlelit disaster drama in the middle of the night.
