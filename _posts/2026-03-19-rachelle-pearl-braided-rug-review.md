## Maintenance and care: practical nerding about cleaning

Cleaning a braided rug is easier than it looks, and the Rachelle Pearl is especially forgiving. Regular vacuuming is recommended, but you should set the vacuum to a gentle setting with the beater brush on its lowest height or turned off for most models. The braid structure makes it easy for dirt to be extracted without aggressively shoving it deeper into the fibers, which is the dream of every vacuum assistant: a rug that doesn’t fight back.

Spot cleaning is usually sufficient for everyday spills. Blot with a clean cloth, use a mild detergent, and then blot again until the stain disappears. Avoid soaking the rug, as too much liquid can cause the backing to loosen and the braid to warp in ways that would upset even the most patient mathematician; you want to preserve the geometry, not unleash a braided chaos universe.

If you have pets, you will want to occasionally groom the rug with a soft brush to lift hair and maintain a crisp surface. The pearl color can show dust more readily than darker shades, so a routine vacuum schedule becomes your best friend rather than a frenemy. For deep cleaning, check the retailer’s guidance; some braided rugs can be professionally cleaned, while others are labeled as machine washable. Always consult the care instructions included with the rug or on the retailer’s product page. If in doubt, err on the side of caution and treat it like a cherished vintage item from a world where fabric is alive and occasionally rebellious.

Q: Will it fade in direct sunlight? A: Some fading can occur with extended sun exposure. Use window treatments or rotate the rug every few months to maintain color stability.

Q: Can I put this under a dining table? A: You can, but you’ll want a rug with a non-slip back or an anti-slip rug pad to ensure it stays in place during mealtime chaos.

Q: How long will it last in a high-traffic living room? A: With proper care, expect several years of good service. If kids or pets regularly test your patience, you’ll want to schedule occasional cleaning and rotation to maximize longevity.
