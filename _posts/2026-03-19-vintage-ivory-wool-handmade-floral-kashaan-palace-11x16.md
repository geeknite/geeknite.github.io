## Condition and authenticity: reading the signs of age

Vintage means character. Expect some signs of life and use, which can include:
- Minor pile flattening in well-trodden areas. This is normal and can be mitigated with gentle vacuuming and occasional professional cleaning.
- Edge wear or slight fraying along the borders. If it’s otherwise solid and structurally intact, this can be stabilized without compromising the rug’s value.
- Fading or color variation that’s evenly distributed. Good vintage pieces display patina rather than uniform wear, which is often a sign of a well-preserved textile.

If you’re evaluating a purchase, a careful inspection for holes, moth damage, or loose knots is essential. A reputable dealer will disclose any repairs, and skilled restorers can address minor issues while preserving the rug’s antiquarian charm. The key is transparency: you’re buying a piece with history, not a pristine showroom reproduction.


## Care and maintenance: keeping the magic alive

The care routine for a wool Kashan rug is not a ritual performed by a guild of rug wizards, but a practical set of habits that protect the weave:
- Vacuum gently and regularly. Use a suction-only attachment or a beater-bar on a very gentle setting. Vacuum in the direction of the pile to avoid fuzzing.
- Rotate seasonally. This helps minimize uneven fading from sunlight and wear patterns from foot traffic.
- Use a rug pad. A high-quality pad reduces slippage, cushions the carpet’s foundation, and prolongs life by distributing weight and reducing friction on the backing.
- Protect from direct sun. Prolonged exposure fades natural dyes and can alter hues over time. Use window treatments or UV-blocking film if your room gets strong sun.
- Spills: Blot—don’t rub—with a clean, white cloth. If spills are persistent, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in wool and natural dyes.
- Cleaning cadence: Consider professional cleaning every 3–5 years, depending on foot traffic and household curiosities (pets, kids, coffee-averse roommates).

As with any heirloom-like textile, the goal is to enjoy it daily while addressing wear before it becomes a problem. Think of it as regular maintenance for a small, luxurious, fabric-based pet dragon—it needs feeding (cleaning) and care (rotation, padding) to stay alive and dazzling.

Ivory can be tricky; it’s a canvas that shows every blemish and breathes with light in a way that beige sometimes doesn’t. Here are tips to make the ivory pop without overpowering a room:

## Caring for authenticity: what to watch out for in a purchase

When evaluating a vintage Kashan rug, keep an eye out for potential red flags that might affect value or longevity:
- Inconsistent knotting: modern reproductions often have uniformity that lacks the organic variation of hand-knot work.
- Color bleed or unnatural dye patches: while patina is expected, large color inconsistencies can indicate repairs or dye issues.
- Suspiciously clean sidelines or appears overly restored: some restoration is normal, but excessive patching can compromise integrity.

Ask for provenance and a professional assessment when possible. A good dealer will provide a history, an appraisal, and a care plan. If you’re buying online, request high-resolution photos of the borders, pile height, and any fringe changes. A hand-knotted Kashan rug is a treasure, but it’s not a mystery novel—clear documentation helps everyone sleep better at night.
