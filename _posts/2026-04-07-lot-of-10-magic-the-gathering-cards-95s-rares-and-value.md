## Condition and Grading

### What to look for on a 1990s card
Because these cards are old enough to have survived without the modern reprint frenzy, their value is intimately tied to condition. Here are some pragmatic checklists:
- Corners: Hard corners that have not been rounded or rounded evenly tend to hold value better. A few micro-nicks are usually acceptable, but large dings can be deal-breakers.
- Edges: Look for whitening along the edges; this is common in older stock but can impact grade. If you flip the card and you see more white than expected, plan for a price adjustment.
- Surface: A clean surface with minimal scratches and no heavy scuff marks scores better. Some surface marks from play are often tolerated by collectors who want the card to whisper its history rather than scream about it.
- Print quality: A glossy finish that retains color depth and no obvious misprints can push a card into a higher value zone, particularly if the artwork by a famous artist is intact.

### Authentication and fake-busting basics
Fakes and reprints exist, especially when the idea of a 1995 rare is sexy enough to chug along on social feeds. Here is a practical approach:
- Consult trusted databases with your own eyes; use a secondary source to cross-check print runs and edition numbers.
- Compare with known authentic examples; look for small details that are consistent across true vintage cards.
- If you suspect a counterfeit, do not force a sale or purchase based solely on internet price expectations; seek a professional opinion or walk away with a smile.

## Care and Preservation

### How to store and display 95s rares
- Use protective sleeves that are designed for old stock; keep them clean and away from direct sunlight to preserve color and prevent yellowing.
- Tomes and displays: vertical holders or glass cases work well to minimize handling damage. If you want to keep them in a binder, use a safe binder with acid-free pages and avoid overstuffing sleeves.
- Humidity and temperature: Keep cards in a stable environment; avoid hot storage areas and damp basements. A small dehumidifier in the room helps keep cards from warping or sustaining moisture-related issues.

### Presentation ideas for geeks and collectors
- Create a mini gallery with a brief description of each card, including its set, year, and the artist behind the work. Fans love learning the lore behind the art and the printing history.
- Snap high quality photos for social media and for your own records. A clean background and even lighting make a world of difference when you share a vintage find.
- Pair your cards with related memorabilia from the era. Promo posters, play mats, or even a convenient 1990s playlist can turn a simple display into the vibe of a retro corner of your home.
