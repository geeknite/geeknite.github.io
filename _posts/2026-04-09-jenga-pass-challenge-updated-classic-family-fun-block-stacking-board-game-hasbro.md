## Overview
### What is Jenga Pass Challenge?
Jenga Pass Challenge is the evolution of the stalwart Jenga premise with a social twist. The core concept remains the same: remove a block from somewhere in the tower and place it on top, progressively creating a taller, more precarious monument. What changes is the tempo, the passing mechanics, and the party-friendly vibes. In practice, you’re juggling not just gravity but group dynamics: who dares to snatch a block from the middle? Who panics and knocks the whole thing down while insisting it was a “calibrated wobble”? The result is a game that leans into laughter as much as logic, which is exactly what many game nights secretly crave.

## How to Play: Rules, Tips, and Tricks
### Core Rules in Plain Nerdy English
At its heart, Jenga Pass Challenge is a social variant of the classic: remove a block from the tower and place it on top, with a minor twist—there’s a passing mechanic that’s designed to get players moving and talking rather than quietly focusing on gravity beats. The details may vary by edition, but the spirit remains consistent: keep the tower from tumbling while the group threads the needle of conversation long enough to keep the game going. Expect moments of suspense, hilariously tense pauses, and maybe a dramatic slow clap when someone saves the tower by, somehow, sheer willpower.

### Pass Mechanic: The Social Clockwork
The “pass” aspect is what makes this version sing in a room full of chatty humans. After each turn, the active player triggers a passing action that rotates the next player into the line of tower attention. In practice, this means you’ll be encouraging your neighbors to grab blocks near the base, or maybe the middle, in a way that feels like a tiny, strategic pep rally. The pass element turns a block-removal exercise into a social choreography: who’s up next, who’s whispering “I’ve got this” while secretly hoping someone else volunteers, and who ends up with a front-row seat to a cascading tower of dramatic fashion and block-falls?

### House Rules: Dialing Chaos Up or Down
If you’re hosting a game night with a mix of veterans and first-timers, you’ll appreciate the optional house rules that let you tune chaos. Quick examples:
- Timed Rounds: Give each player a fixed window to remove and place; this speeds up the game and fills the room with excited commentary.
- Safe-Tower Extensions: Add a few blocks on top at the end to prevent a single player from single-handedly launching a tower into orbit, which helps in houses with more enthusiastic players.
- Team Play: Pair up two players who must cooperate on each turn, turning Jenga into a cooperative vs. the tower experience.

### Strategy Tips for the Social Jenga Adventurer
- Read the room, not just the tower. The best Jenga players aren’t always the steadiest hands; they’re the ones who know when to lean into the social energy and when to simply stall for a moment while the awkward laughter subsides.
- Target blocks near the edges early, not because you’re a risk-taker, but because those pulls can influence the overall tower balance without destroying future options for your team.
- Use the passing mechanic to your advantage. If you’re in a group where someone tends to dominate the conversation, craft passes that give that player a slightly riskier block to pull—watch the chaos unfold in slow-motion as friendship tests are conducted in real-time.
