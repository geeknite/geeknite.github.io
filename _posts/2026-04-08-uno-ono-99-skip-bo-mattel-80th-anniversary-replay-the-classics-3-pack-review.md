## What’s in the 3 Pack
The 3 Pack is not just a random assortment of old friends in new shoes. It is marketed as a tribute to the Replay The Classics era, which basically means Mattel took a lazy afternoon and said, let us sprinkle some retro glitter on a couple of evergreen titles. In this pack you typically get:

### UNO O'NO 99
UNO O'NO 99 is a curious member of the UNO family, mixing the standard color cards with some rule twists that occasionally make you swear you are playing a game designed by a caffeinated librarian. The O in O'NO nods to a turn where you might have to shout out a number with dramatic flair or execute a wild card move that changes the flow of the game in a way that only a board game could justify at 2 AM.

### Skip-Bo variant
Skip-Bo is a builder card game, the one where you order cards into stacks like a tiny, controlled avalanche. The Skip-Bo variant in this pack is designed to be familiar yet slightly refreshed, so you can enjoy the stacking ritual without the fear of losing your mind to overly complex rules. It remains a deceptively simple game, one that rewards memory more than mathematical genius, and this reissue is no different.

### 80th Anniversary packaging
The packaging is a love letter to the era that birthed these games. Think bold typography, a dash of neon stapled to a retro color palette, and enough gloss to make the cards feel premium even if they are still just plain cardboard underneath. The 80th badge is the star of the show here, and it signals collector potential for folks who like to keep a neat shelf of nostalgia with a few modern trinkets stuck in for good measure.

There are also the usual suspects one would expect in a reissue: a glossy manual, a compact storage tray, and a set of operational rules that are mostly unchanged from the originals, which is both good and bad depending on your tolerance for rule nuggets from a bygone era.
