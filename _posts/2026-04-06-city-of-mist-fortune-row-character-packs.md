### What you get in Fortune Row

Fortune Row is not just a set of six masks; it is a curated experience. Each character pack comes with a central mythic concept, a modern social shell, a handful of signature tags, and a handful of relationships that make the mask feel grounded, not just glowy. You will find:

- A fully realized Mask concept with Concept, Mythos, and Story Tags tailored to the Fortune Row vibe
- Detailed backstory lines that weave into the city, including personal stakes, rivalries, and secrets
- A set of Relationships that help players anchor in the city’s underbelly and connect to other PCs or NPCs
- A ready-to-run Hook or two that you can drop into a session without spending an entire pre-game lobbying round
- GM tips and potential complications to keep the plot dynamic
- A few handouts or quick-reference cards to speed up table play

The design team behind Fortune Row leans into the noir mood with a playful wink. They want the players to feel like they are stepping onto a stage where the spotlight shifts with each new revelation, and you should not worry about memorizing every line of dialogue because the packs give you the cues to improvise on the fly.

And yes, there are six distinct masks, each with its own flavor and mechanical profile. They share a common thread – each mask is a lens through which the Fortune Row city reveals itself, showing you how luck, fate, and secrets twist in the neon rain. The result is a pack that feels cohesive rather than a grab bag of vibes. If you like your City of Mist campaigns to feel cinematic, Fortune Row is the kind of product that helps you lean into that aesthetic without becoming a labyrinth of stat blocks.

### Character Pack Highlights

Below are the six masks, each given a quick spotlight so you can see how they contribute to an ongoing session. Think of these as the core beats you will hear again as your players navigate Fortune Row’s streets.

#### The Brass Broker

A master of deals and whispers, the Brass Broker operates at the crossroads where money talks and secrets walk. The Hook for this mask is a debt that refuses to vanish, a secret you owe more people than your grandmother owes you favors. The Concept is urban avarice with a social twist; the Mythos leans into the idea that fate is transactional. This mask thrives on social scenes, negotiations, and the occasional double-cross that actually serves the greater arc. In play, expect a lot of social conflict, a few clever gambits, and a dramatic reveal in which a seemingly ordinary character pulls the string that unravels the entire night’s plan.

#### The Velvet Scribe

A poet of the city’s memory, the Velvet Scribe collects stories like pack animals collect snacks. Her notebook is a living archive of the city’s humiliations and triumphs, and each entry can alter reality in a pinch. The Velvet Scribe excels at information gathering, performance, and shaping the narrative to your party’s advantage. Hooks for this mask pivot on memory theft, rewriting a rumor into legend, or turning a past miscalculation into an opportunity for redemptive drama. The velvet tone adds a layer of elegance to the rowdy underworld, reminding players that even villains can be stylish.

#### The Iron Courier

Harsh, efficient, and perfectly punctual, the Iron Courier delivers more than packages; he delivers consequences. This mask is built around a delivery route that intersects with several major factions in Fortune Row, making him both indispensable and dangerous to cross. The Iron Courier offers a strong toolkit for action scenes and chase sequences, with a focus on timing, logistics, and collateral consequences. Expect the GM to use ticking clocks and the party to negotiate with a courier who has eyes everywhere.

#### The Neon Healer

A medic who treats wounds with not just bandages but secrets, the Neon Healer uses medical skill plus a mythic twist to mend both body and reputation. This mask invites ethical questions about healing, responsibility, and the price of mercy. The Hooks for the Neon Healer often revolve around moral decisions and the consequences of choices made for the good of the many, sometimes at the expense of the few. In game terms, watch for Moment of Truth style moments where the healer’s past comes back to complicate present-day decisions.

#### The Gutter Alchemist

A chemist who makes potions that do more than heal or harm, the Gutter Alchemist trades in experimental remedies and questionable shortcuts. This pack slides into Fortune Row with a scent of alchemy, not quite science, and a dash of street smarts. The Gutter Alchemist offers gadgets and one-shot options that can tilt a scene, opened through clever tagging and a little luck. Expect side effects, both narrative and mechanical, that spice up fights and investigations alike.

#### The Moonlit Ghost

A mask that feels like a whisper from the city itself, the Moonlit Ghost navigates shadows and echo-laden memories. This character is about misdirection, stealth, and the way information hides in plain sight. The Hooks and Relationships for the Moonlit Ghost emphasize miscommunication, accidental reveals, and the power of presence without loud actions. In play, this mask shines in social intrigue and stealth missions that require careful planning and a chilled sense of drama.

The six masks together form a spectrum from social manipulation to raw action, with enough overlap to let players improvise as the plot pivots. Fortune Row makes it easy to slot them into a city block, a single night, or a longer arc about who benefits from fortune and who pays the price for the city’s myths.

### Mechanics and Theme in Fortune Row

City of Mist uses a tag-based system that emphasizes flavor and narrative control over raw numbers. Fortune Row leans into that identity with tags that feel both specific and flexible. The packs provide a handful of signature tags for each mask that reflect their theme – debt, memory, delivery, mercy, experimentation, and shadow. These tags interact with the core mechanics in a way that rewards players for leaning into roleplay rather than brute force.

What makes Fortune Row click is not just the masks themselves but how they interact with the city around them. The row is a character, with factions that care about profit, prestige, and power. When a mask touches Fortune Row, they engage with a different dimension of threats and opportunities: the kind you don’t see until a door opens and a rumor becomes the new reality. The hooks offered by each pack are designed to escalate: a small betrayal becomes a bigger conspiracy, a whispered legend becomes a social contract, and a single choice can tilt the entire row toward fortune or ruin.

If you are worried about balance, the six packs sit well with the core rules. They are designed to be integrated into existing teams or used to seed new campaigns without the GM needing a full re-calibration. The masks are distinct enough to provide diverse play styles, yet compatible enough to be used together or swapped out depending on the tone your table wants. In other words, Fortune Row is a set of six masks that can be the backbone of a long campaign or the spark that sets a night on fire.

### How to integrate Fortune Row into your campaign

If you already run City of Mist, Fortune Row can slot into several entry points. Here are a few practical paths you can consider:

- One Night Caper: Pick two masks and a single hook to create a standalone session that introduces Fortune Row as a looming threat or opportunity. The pack’s hooks are designed to escalate quickly, so a well-structured session can feel like a cinematic trailer for a longer arc.
- Mid-Campaign Re-Seed: If your party has drifted into a lull, drop Fortune Row to re-energize the table. The masks provide new relationships and rivalries to weave into existing plot threads, which can reframe what the party thought they knew about the city.
- Therow-as-Location: Treat Fortune Row as a location rather than a set of masks. The six characters operate in a shared environment with interconnected threads. This approach is ideal if you want to experiment with city maps, faction politics, and the idea that fortune is a currency traded in back rooms and back-streets.
- Character Swaps and NPC-Heavy Campaigns: If your table enjoys a rotating cast, Fortune Row makes it easy to swap in new masks as NPCs who become PCs or vice versa. The hooks and relationships can be re-skinned to your party’s current needs while preserving the vibe.

To get the most out of Fortune Row, you should prepare a few things in advance:

- A one-page handout that lists each mask and its signature tags. This helps players remember their advantages in high-stakes scenes.
- A rough map of Fortune Row with key locations and factions. The physical geography of the row can drive immediate drama when a mask crosses paths with an old rival or an forgotten debt.
- A short faction cheat sheet that outlines who controls which block of the Row and what that means for the upcoming session. This keeps the players from accidentally stepping into a trap they cannot escape.

### GM tips and tricks

- Lean into the rhythm: Fortune Row thrives on a cadence of reveals, each one puncturing a soft moment with a sharper twist. Your job as GM is to maintain that balance, dropping a clue that seems small but reveals a bigger problem if chased.
- Use the masks to drive conflict: Each mask has a potential rival, debt, or secret that can spark a scene. Use these as anchors to structure scenes rather than letting players wander into random combat encounters.
- Let relationships carry the narrative: The bonds between masks and NPCs matter more than an elaborate combat encounter, so use the relationship webs to propel the story forward and to force players to make tough moral calls.
- Don’t over-plan: Fortune Row gives you the bones. Let the table shape the flesh. Leave some room for improvisation; City of Mist rewards players who lean into the mythic noise of the city rather than rigid, planned outcomes.
- Embrace the vibe, not the bullet list: The strength of Fortune Row is in mood and nuance. If you get the feel right, the mechanics will follow, and your players will feel like detectives, criminals, or both at once.
