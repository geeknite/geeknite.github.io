## How to Play: A Quick Refresher and a Few Twists
### The Core Mechanic
Dixit is a game of storytelling via abstract imagery. On your turn as storyteller, you pick one of your cards and say a clue—an adjective, a phrase, a memory, or a joke that only you can fully own. Then every other player picks a card from their hand that best matches that clue and places it facedown. The cards are shuffled and revealed, and everyone votes on which card they think was the storyteller’s. If your card receives votes, you gain points; if others guess your card, they gain points as well. The storyteller receives points if at least some players pick their card but not everyone. The objective is to strike a balance between clarity and ambiguity, which is essentially a masterclass in social psychology without the textbooks.

### Strategy Without Tears
The strategy in Dixit is not about constructing a perfect riddle. It’s about calibrating the distance between your clue and your card. Too obvious a clue and you risk everyone guessing your card; too obscure and you get zero points while your players collectively groan at your cryptic brilliance. The beauty is that there is no single optimal strategy. A clue that works brilliantly for a player circle with a shared sense of humor may completely flop in a different group. The new sealed copy doesn’t change this DNA; it simply gives you fresher art to pull into your storytelling experiments.

### Player Counts and Pacing
Dixit scales nicely from a core group to larger gatherings. In small groups, you can revel in how tiny misreadings become legendary moments that you retell at future gatherings. In bigger parties, it’s about coordinating who is the most confident in misinterpretation and who will quietly bend the rules to curry favor with the group’s storyteller. The pacing is strong: you can play a few rounds in under an hour if your group is enthusiastic and a little loud, which is basically Dixit’s natural habitat.

### Word of Caution: The Whisper Network
If you tend toward analytical games, Dixit can feel like playing a poetry slam with a deck of mood cards. You’ll want to lean into the vibe and let people riff.
