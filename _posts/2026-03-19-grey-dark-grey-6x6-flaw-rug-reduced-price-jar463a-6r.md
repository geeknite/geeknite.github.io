## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity Tips
We’ve all been there: you spill a beverage while binge-watching a sci-fi marathon, and you realize your rug is not just a piece of decor but a potential plot device for a tiny tragedy. Here are practical care tips to keep your rug looking sharp for as long as possible.

- Vacuum regularly: Use a gentle setting and avoid aggressive suction that could exacerbate any loose fibers near the flaw. The goal is to pick up crumbs, not introduce a new warp in the weave.
- Spot cleaning: Blot spills with a clean cloth and a mild detergent solution. Avoid harsh chemicals that could alter the dye or texture in sensitive spots.
- Rotation: Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. This is especially helpful if you have a consistent traffic pattern that tends to accentuate that one corner where the flaw is most visible.
- Rug pad: A quality pad reduces slipping and extends the rug’s life by absorbing some of the impact and reducing friction on hard surfaces.

## Quick Shopping Guide: How to Inspect This Rug Before You Buy Online
If you’re shopping online and the retailer allows you to view additional photos or a zoomed-in patch, here are quick checks to perform virtually:
- Zoom into edges and corners: Look for fraying, loose threads, or knot irregularities. If you notice more than a few threads, it could be a sign of a more significant flaw.
- Check the weave density: A uniform density across the surface indicates better quality. If the density varies dramatically near the flaw, that may be a sign to reconsider.
- Examine the dye consistency: A slight shade variation is normal in some designs; large patches of color misalignment could mean a bigger issue.
- Confirm return policy: If you’re purchasing online, ensure you have a viable return option if the flaw is more visible in person than in photos. A generous return policy is your best friend in uncertain territory.
