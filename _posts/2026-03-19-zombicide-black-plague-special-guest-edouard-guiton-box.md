### Painting and Assembly Tips

If you plan to paint these minis, treat them as you would any mid-range fantasy kit: wash the recesses to bring out definition, apply a medium-over natural skin tone for survivors, and choose a slightly desaturated base for zombies to keep the scene grounded. Edouard Guiton's color choices—vivid, heavily saturated shadows in key areas—offer fantastic starting points for color blocking. A good trick is to pick a single color to pop (a red cape, a glowing weapon, or a bright eye) and let the rest of the miniature stay in muted tones. This approach helps maintain readability on the tabletop, especially when you are facing a dense swarm of enemies.

If you’re new to painting, you can still enjoy the visuals without investing heavily. Even a basic wash and dry-brush on these minis will reveal the sculpted lines and shapes that Edouard designed. And if you are a veteran, you’ll appreciate the new layers of depth these sculpts can hold when you push for high-contrast lighting on the base coat.
