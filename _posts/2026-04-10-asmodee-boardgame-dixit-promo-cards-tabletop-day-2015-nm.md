## How the promos affect gameplay
Dixit is, at its core, a game about storytelling and guesswork. The role of the storyteller is to craft a clue that others will tie to their chosen cards, all while ensuring that a few players are able to discern your intention without giving away the answer to everyone immediately. Promo cards do two things here: they refresh the pool with new imagery, and they broaden the set of possible clues you can draw on during a round.

In practical terms, you can integrate these promos into your Dixit sessions in a few straightforward ways:
- As a replacement deck: Swap in the promo cards for specific rounds to refresh the pool without changing the core mechanics. This option keeps things varied but familiar, preserving the rhythm of the round while injecting new prompts.
- As a separate mini deck: Treat the promo cards as a small supplementary deck that you randomly draw from in addition to the base deck. This approach creates an extra layer of unpredictability and can spark more creative association when players mix cards from the two pools.
- Thematic nights: If you are hosting a themed Dixit night, pick promos that align with a theme and keep the rest of the game as is. The promos can reinforce your chosen mood, whether it is dreamlike wonder, quirky humor, or dark whimsy.

Whichever method you choose, the promos offer a gentle customization that does not break the game’s core balance. They are not overbearing; they simply provide fresh seeds for the storytellers. In many ways, the essence of Dixit is about encouraging imagination rather than constraining it, and these promos align with that philosophy quite nicely.

### Short play test: do they change the win condition?
No, not really. Dixit awards points based on how many players guess the storyteller card. The promo cards do not alter the scoring system; they alter the canvas on which the storytelling takes place. You might find that a certain promo card leads to more precise guesses, while others trigger amusing misinterpretations. The net effect is a richer set of clues to choose from, which can make rounds feel more varied and less predictable over time.

The term NM or Near Mint is used in collector circles to denote cards with minimal wear and original look.
