## Cleaning and Care

### Maintenance

Vacuum with a standard head on a low setting; avoid turbo brushes that might fray fibers over time. The rug’s color helps hide dust, but regular maintenance remains essential to keep fur tumbleweeds from rolling across the living room during a marathon binge of cat videos. A monthly refresh is a good rhythm for most households; more for busy families with shedding pets or for spaces with heavy traffic.

### Stains

Most spills wipe clean with a blot and a mild detergent solution. Avoid aggressive chemical cleaners that could degrade fibers or fade color; test any new stain-removal method in an inconspicuous corner first. For stubborn marks, warm water and a gentle soap mix usually do the job, followed by a thorough rinse and blot dab-dab-dab until dry.

### Rotation and Lifespan

Because this rug is large, rotation during cleaning sessions is a straightforward way to encourage even wear. It might feel like a quick forearm workout, but the payoff is uniform aging and a symmetrical look that keeps the room feeling deliberate rather than worn-in. Consider rotating the rug 90 degrees every 6–12 months to even out foot traffic and sun exposure, especially if your space has large windows that bathe part of the rug in sunlight.

### Care Tips and Maintenance Schedule

- Vacuum weekly on a low setting; in high-traffic areas, you may increase to twice weekly.
- Rotate every 6–12 months to promote even wear.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slippage and prolong lifespan.
