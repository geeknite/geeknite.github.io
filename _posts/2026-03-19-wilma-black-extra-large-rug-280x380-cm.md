## Cleaning and Care
### Maintenance
A standard vacuum on a low setting is all you need for weekly upkeep. Avoid turbo brushes that can fray fibers over time; the goal is to keep the color looking crisp and the pile uniform. The black hue helps disguise dust and pet hair, but regular maintenance remains essential to keep fur tumbleweeds from staging a weekend block party. A monthly refresh works for most homes, with a bit more attention for households with shedding pets or high foot traffic.

### Stains
Spills respond well to a blot and a mild detergent solution. Avoid aggressive chemical cleaners that could degrade fibers or fade color; always test a new stain-removal method in an inconspicuous corner first. For stubborn marks, warm water with a gentle soap mix usually does the trick, followed by careful rinsing and blotting until dry. If you’ve managed to get something stubborn baked into the weave, a professional cleaning can refresh the fibers without drama.

### Rotation and Lifespan
Because this rug is large, rotating it during cleaning sessions helps encourage even wear. It might feel like a quick forearm workout, but the payoff is a balanced aging process that keeps the room feeling deliberate rather than worn. Consider rotating the rug 90 degrees every 6–12 months to even out foot traffic and sun exposure, especially if your space has large windows that bath part of the rug in sunlight.

### Care Tips and Maintenance Schedule
- Vacuum weekly on a low setting; in high-traffic areas, you may increase to twice weekly.
- Rotate every 6–12 months to promote even wear.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slippage and prolong lifespan.
