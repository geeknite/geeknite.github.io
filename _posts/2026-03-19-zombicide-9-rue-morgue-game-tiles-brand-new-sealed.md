## Quality control: what to check when you open a sealed set

Brand-new and sealed is the ideal state, but anomalies do creep in. Here are some quick QC checks you can run when you crack open Rue Morgue:

- Tile flatness: Are there any cupping or warping defects? A slight bow is common in older stock but should not interfere with stacking or layout alignment.
- Print consistency: Look for color variance between tiles. A little deviation in hue is normal, but extreme discoloration might indicate a factory inconsistency.
- Edge cleanliness: Ensure there are no stray fibers or rough edges that would catch when you slide tiles together. Fine tweezers or a neat thumbnail can help trim away any accidental burrs, though this is rarely necessary.
- Compartment fit: If your set includes any token bags or accessories, verify they’re present and fit properly into their storage slots.

If you encounter any significant issues, the typical path is to contact the retailer or CMON customer support. Most reputable sellers stand behind new and sealed products with a straightforward replacement policy for damaged items.
