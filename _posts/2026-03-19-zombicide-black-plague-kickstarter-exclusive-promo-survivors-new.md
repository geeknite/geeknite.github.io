## Painting tips and tabletop presentation

For the painters among us, promo survivors offer a great palette. Here are some practical tips to make them pop without turning your hobby into a never-ending art project:

- Start with a strong undercoat: A nice black or neutral gray undercoat makes the colors sing and reduces the need for heavy priming.
- Layer with intention: Build up color in layers to avoid flat shading. The goal is depth that reads well at table distance rather than a studio-quality finish.
- Washes for character: A light wash in the recesses can bring out the textures of armor or fabric. Don’t overdo it; you want definition, not mud monsters.
- Edges matter: Clean edge highlighting on edges of armor and weapons can dramatically improve silhouette readability during intense zombie firefights.
- Base it for drama: A little texture on the base, perhaps a few broken boards or scattered coins, can anchor the mini in the scene and give it a narrative push.

If painting isn’t your thing, you can still get a lot of joy from these minis by displaying them, or using them as a quick visual cue for players who want to remember who does what in a big, chaotic battle.
