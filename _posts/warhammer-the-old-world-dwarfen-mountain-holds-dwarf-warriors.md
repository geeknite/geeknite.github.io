## The Basics

### Dwarfen Mountain Holds

The Dwarfen Mountain Holds are the impenetrable fortresses of the Dwarf race, nestled deep within the mountains of the Old World. These holds are more than just homes—they are symbols of Dwarven resilience, craftsmanship, and stubbornness (because let's be honest, Dwarfs invented stubbornness).

#### Design and Structure

Each hold is meticulously designed to withstand sieges from the mightiest of foes, including those pesky Greenskins and ever-meddling Skaven. The architecture is a blend of grand halls, intricate tunnels, and impregnable gates. Dwarfs take pride in their engineering prowess, and it shows in every stone laid and every mechanism crafted.

#### Key Holds

- **Karaz-a-Karak**: The capital of the Dwarf empire and the seat of the High King.
- **Karak Eight Peaks**: Known for its wealth and strategic importance.
- **Karak Kadrin**: The Slayer Keep, home to the infamous Slayer cult.

### Dwarf Warriors

Now let's talk about the true heroes of the mountains—the **Dwarf Warriors**. These stout, courageous fighters are the backbone of any Dwarf army, ready to defend their holds with grim determination and a whole lot of axe-swinging.

#### Characteristics

- **Stamina**: Dwarf Warriors are known for their incredible stamina and endurance. They can march for days without tiring, and their resolve in battle is unmatched.
- **Armor and Weapons**: Clad in heavy armor and wielding axes and shields, Dwarf Warriors are tough nuts to crack. Their equipment is crafted with exceptional skill, often passed down through generations.
- **Skills**: Expert in close combat, Dwarf Warriors use their sturdy build and powerful swings to overwhelm enemies. Their shield walls are legendary, providing an almost impenetrable defense.

## The Gameplay

### Strategy and Tactics

When playing as Dwarfs in Warhammer The Old World, strategy revolves around utilizing their strengths: resilience and close combat prowess. Here are some tips to dominate the battlefield:

#### Defensive Formations

Dwarfs excel in defensive formations. Position your warriors in tight shield walls to absorb enemy charges and protect key units like Thunderers and Artillery.

#### Counter-Attacks

Use your Dwarfs' sturdy nature to withstand initial assaults and then launch devastating counter-attacks. A well-timed charge can turn the tide of battle.

#### Terrain Utilization

Dwarfs are masters of using the terrain to their advantage. Position your troops on high ground and inside fortifications whenever possible.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **High Durability**: Dwarf Warriors are tough and can endure prolonged battles.
- **Strong Defensive Capabilities**: Excellent at holding lines and protecting allies.
- **Powerful Close Combat**: Their axes and shields make them formidable opponents in melee.

### Cons

- **Slow Movement**: Dwarfs are not the fastest troops, making them vulnerable to fast-moving enemies.
- **Limited Ranged Options**: While effective in close combat, they lack versatility in ranged attacks.
