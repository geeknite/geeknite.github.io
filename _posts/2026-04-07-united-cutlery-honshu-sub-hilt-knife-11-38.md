The TPR handle is forgiving—easy to wipe clean, resistant to most common solvents, and comfortable to hold even after long sessions. If you’re a hedonist for metal-care rituals, you’ll be delighted to know that a simple pass with a mild abrasive and some oil keeps the blade free from rust spots that make your inner Samurai cry. If you store it long-term, keep the blade dry and consider a light coat of oil to preserve the finish. It’s not rocket science; it’s blade science with a little flair.

Care and Feeding: Tips to Extend Its Life
-----------------------------------------
- Clean and dry after use. Water + 7Cr13 + time = corrosion if you’re not careful. A simple wipe-down and a light oil coat will keep rust at bay. 
- Sharpen regularly but mind the steel’s limits. A budget steel will benefit from a standard sharpening routine rather than heavy, aggressive bevel changes. 
- Store in a dry place with a sheath that protects the blade. A good sheath prevents scuffs, dulling, and accidental injuries caused by the blade banging around in a bag. 
- Avoid prying with the tip. The blade isn’t designed for that; treat it with respect and you’ll keep it performing well for years to come.
