## How to Play: A Fast, Feisty Draft-and-Score Experience

### The Basic Flow
In Sushi Go Card Game, the core mechanic is drafting. Each round, you’re dealt a hand of cards, you choose one card to keep, and you pass the rest to the next player. This happens simultaneously, so you’re perpetually trying to outguess what your neighbor will pick while optimizing your own path to points. The round continues with one round of drafting, and then you reveal your chosen card, sliding it into your personal collection. After a few rounds—depending on the player count—you’ll settle on a final score via the various scoring cards; some cards award immediate points, others reward you for specific sets, and some “cool” cards let you exult in clever combos when timed perfectly.

The scoring is where this little card-delight can feel surprisingly satisfying. You’ll juggle dumplings, sashimi, nigiri, maki rolls, and tempura sets, while also watching out for the occasional pillowy misdirection: a card that seems valuable now might block a more valuable armful later. It’s the kind of simple decision tree that rewards planning ahead, but not so hard that someone at the table can’t contribute to the fun even as they pretend to be a sushi-attempting grandmaster with a mouthful of giggles.

### The Rounds: Speed, Rhythm, and the Joy of Passing Things Around
Every game of Sushi Go Card Game unfolds in a handful of rounds. Depending on how many players you’ve invited to this culinary card caper, you’ll go through roughly three to five rounds per game. Each round is a micro-challenge in risk assessment: you glance at your hand, pick a card you hope others won’t block you from, and pass the rest. The passed pile keeps moving around the table like a conveyor belt of culinary chaos, until the round ends and you add your new cards to your scoring pile. The tempo is the soul of the game—snappy, energetic, and occasionally cutthroat in a way that never feels petty because you’re literally chasing tiny, adorable cards.

### The Scoring: Card Types, Sets, and Strategic Sweet Spots
The scoring in Sushi Go Card Game is elegantly modular. Each card type has its own scoring logic, which means you’ll need to plan your two or three most important goals per round and adapt as the hand you’re dealt shifts. Common scoring themes include:
- Set collection: Gather a certain quantity of identical or similar cards to unlock points. The more you collect, the closer you get to sweet victory—barring someone else who’s chasing the same set and ends up stealing your thunder with a perfectly timed grab.
- Mini-collections within a round: Some cards reward you for having multiple different sushi types by the end of the round. It’s a gentle reminder that variety can beat single-minded focus—if you don’t overextend, that is.
- Special combos: Certain card types synergize with others (e.g., multiple Nigiri cards of different types, or a Tempura + Makis pairing). The elegance here is that the scoring feels like a puzzle you assemble in your own head as you’re drafting, making each round feel like a clever mini-heist rather than a mindless draw-and-pass.

The catch, of course, is that you have only so many rounds to assemble your dream menu. This creates tension without the usual heavy burden of more sprawling games. The game stays lean, which makes it a perfect appetizer before the main course of heavier titles—or, more likely, a comfortable dessert after a long day of work-from-home emails.

### A Few Quick Rules That Make You Look Smart
- You may keep exactly one card each round.
- You pass the rest to the left or right depending on the round’s direction (the direction may swap between rounds, but the exact order isn’t the stuff of legend; it’s printed on the rule sheet).
- Some cards have special effects when paired with other cards or when used in specific ways. It pays to skim the card backs or the cheat-sheet that comes with most boxes before a game night so you don’t look like you’re playing from memory only.
- End-game scoring can be a bit of a race to see who’s got the most efficient path to victory. Don’t be surprised if a late-season swap suddenly flips the leaderboard in dramatic fashion.
