Care, Cleaning, and Long-Term Maintenance
------------------------------------------

No rug is completely stain-proof, but you can minimize drama with a good-cleaning routine. The Slub Charcoal’s pile is typically moderate to high, which means surface spills can be spot-cleaned relatively easily. Here are practical steps:

- Blot, don’t rub. When something spills, grab a clean cloth and press gently to lift the liquid. Rubbing can drive stains deeper and leave a ghost of the incident.
- Use a mild detergent solution or a specialized rug cleaner for stubborn stains. Always test a small, inconspicuous spot first to ensure colorfastness. If you’re a purist, a simple mixture of warm water and a tiny amount of dish soap works well for many common spills.
- For deep cleaning, consider professional cleaning every 12–18 months, depending on foot traffic. A professional can revitalize the fibers and maintain the rug’s plush feel.

Maintenance tips to keep things crisp:
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and avoid persistent high-traffic grooves.
- Vacuum with a gentle setting. A beater bar can be too aggressive for delicate pile structures; opt for a suction-only or a gentle rotating brush setting.
- Keep pet claws trimmed and assign boundaries. If your pet has a signature “let’s sprint through the living room” route, consider a protective layer underneath to minimize snags.

A Quick Look at Alternatives and Comparisons
---------------------------------------------

If you’re browsing, you might compare the Slub Charcoal with other contemporary tufted rugs. A few common comparisons include:

- Charcoal vs. charcoal with slightly different pile heights: A subtle variable height can add an extra dimension to the room but may require more frequent professional cleaning to keep edges crisp.
- Hand-tufted vs. machine-tufted: Hand-tufted often offers better texture and depth, but machine-tufted can be more budget-friendly; the choice depends on your tolerance for artisan charm vs. budget constraints.
- Wool blends vs. synthetic blends: Wool brings natural resilience and warmth; synthetic blends generally offer easier stain resistance and lower maintenance—though modern synthetics can mimic wool’s feel quite well.
