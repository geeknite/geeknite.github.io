## Gameplay overview
Face to Face Edition (Deluxe) blends social interaction with light dexterity. Each round comprises three phases:
1) Prompt phase: draw a card and read aloud; prompts can request a pitch, a story, or a quick physical task.
2) Face-off phase: the chosen player must perform within a time limit while the table observes.
3) Scoring phase: players judge the performance based on set criteria.
Key twist: you’re rewarded for engagement as much as clever mechanics, which aligns perfectly with the party-game ethos.

## The Mid-Game Pivot
What makes this edition stand out is its emphasis on how well your group reads the room. The scoring isn’t a rigid ledger; it’s a social barometer, rewarding genuine participation and energy. The Deluxe timer preserves suspense without turning rounds into a dental appointment, and the premium tokens add a sense of ceremony to each round.

## Playing time and player count

If you crave solo play, you’ll mostly be interacting with your own inner improv coach and the politely skeptical audience around you.

## How to play: Quick-start guide
1) Set up the board and decks; designate a host for timing.
2) Draw a prompt and read it aloud; decide if you’ll perform or narrate.
3) The chosen player performs within the time limit; others observe.
4) Score based on set criteria and collect tokens.
5) Rotate host and repeat for the chosen number of rounds.
6) Crown the winner with the most tokens.

## Tips and strategy
- Read the room: energy and timing often trump sheer cleverness.
- Play to your strengths; if you’re strong at storytelling, lean into it; if you’re comfortable with physical stunts, own it—responsibly.
- Pace matters: keep momentum but don’t rush; tension can fuel laughs.
- Be inclusive: invite and acknowledge quieter players; a good host draws them into the spotlight.
