## How the Abomination Pack Affects Gameplay

### Global Impact
The Abomination Pack doesn’t just add a new obstacle; it changes the pacing. Boss encounters introduce a dramatic tempo shift from “clear this zone clockwise” to “okay, what if we had to manage two threats at once while the Abomination stumbles into the kitchen and raids your ammo stash?” It’s a shift that rewards planning but punishes reactive play. The board becomes a chessboard of threats with occasional roars of chaos that remind you who’s written the check and who’s actually paying it.

### Boss Design and Abilities: A Quick Grasp
- Abomination: A multi-phase threat with scaling attacks that become nastier as it loses conditions or as time ticks. It punishes tunnel vision and punishes you for ignoring its area-of-effect zones. The Abomination sets the tone; if you can survive its early gut-punch, you’ll feel you’ve conquered a midboss in a high-budget action flick.
- Other bosses: Each of the extra bosses has a distinct gimmick—some may spawn extra zombies, others might gain temporary invulnerability, and a few may “mark” heroes so that certain tiles become more dangerous as the game progresses. The variety keeps the campaign from turning into a single-boss slog and ensures that different strategies become viable in different rounds.

### Player Interaction and Social Dynamics
Boss fights are rarely purely “roll the dice and see what happens.” They require negotiation, timing, and sometimes friendly conspiracies: who will guard the flank while someone else’s hero runs for the Abomination? Who risks a hero to distract a spawn while others go for the boss’s weak points? The Abomination Pack transforms your table into a living, occasionally petty battlefield where every turn must be calculated like a boardroom PowerPoint but with more guts and fewer HR compliance risks.

### Strategy and Tactics: How to Win (Or Not Die Trying)
- Positioning is king. Bosses punish overcrowded corners, and the Abomination’s reach is no joke. Practicing a well-choreographed approach across turns yields much better odds than “punch until something works.”
- Card management matters. Special boss cards can drain a typical turn into a single, tense decision. You’ll want to keep your cards aligned with your plan and avoid greed-induced misplays that end with your party feeling like a trapdoor orchestra.
- Ally synergy shines here. The more you optimize your team’s roles (melee specialists, ranged specialists, trap setters, etc.), the more you’ll feel like you’ve orchestrated a victory rather than a miracle.
