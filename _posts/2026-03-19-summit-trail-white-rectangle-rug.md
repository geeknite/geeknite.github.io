## Care, maintenance, and longevity tips
### Daily and weekly routine
- Vacuum at least twice per week in high-traffic zones to prevent grit from grinding into the pile.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slipping and add a touch of cushion underfoot.
- Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear and avoid obvious lightening in sun-exposed areas.

### Stain management and spot cleaning
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can drive stains deeper.
- For most common stains, mix a small amount of mild liquid detergent with water and apply to the spot with a clean cloth. Blot gently until the stain lifts, then rinse with a damp cloth.
- For tougher stains, test a small inconspicuous area first and consider professional cleaning if necessary.
- Avoid harsh solvents and bleach on white fibers, as they can degrade the pile and alter color over time.

### Sunlight and color preservation
Direct sunlight can yellow white textiles over long periods. If your rug sits in a sunlit zone, consider UV-blocking drapes or rotating the rug more often to maintain a uniform appearance. A light exposure plan can help you preserve that crisp white look for longer than a season or two.

### When to call in pros
If a stain refuses to budge after multiple attempts, or if you notice the backing loosening or the edges curling, professional cleaning is a wise investment. A pro can revive the rug without risking color loss or fiber damage. Think of it as hitting the reset button on your home aesthetics rather than a luxury expense.
