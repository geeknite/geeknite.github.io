## Cable management, power, and tech integration
One of the most celebrated features of the Eternal is its cable management backbone. In a row of ten workstations, cables can become a jumbled forest if not properly organized. The Eternal addresses this with a central channel that runs along the spine of the desk and distributes power and data to each station. The idea is to keep cables away from the main user surface, minimize cable tangling on the floor, and allow quick reconfiguration when teams reorganize or scale up.

Expect a combination of grommets, clip-in cable trays, and a handful of floor-to-ceiling channels if your room layout requires extended reach. The integrated screens also provide connectivity options. Depending on the model, there may be HDMI and USB-C ports that feed into the surface or a central hub. You can place a shared docking station in the middle and route everything from laptops to desktops to external storage devices through the trunk. A well-organized central cable trunk reduces tripping hazards and keeps micro-movements and stand-up meetings from becoming a tug-of-war with a tangle of lines.

If you run a data-heavy operation, you can add a provisioning panel with surge protection and a network switch to the central spine. This keeps the entire workstation cluster ready during a power outage and ensures that a sudden spike of devices does not trip the circuit. It is not glamorous, but it is worth its weight when you do not want the entire floor to go dark while someone uploads a 4K video demonstration to the cloud.

In addition to cables, you should plan for ventilation around the desks. Ten heavy workstations produce heat and could challenge climate control in smaller rooms. Consider airflow patterns and perhaps a small desk fan per block for hot summer days. The goal is to keep the environment comfortable so your team can focus on the task rather than on the temperature.
