## Installation and Maintenance Tips
### Preparing the space
Before bringing the rack in, measure doorways, corridors, and elevator clearance if you are moving it through a building. A 39-inch deep rack is an upright friend but can become a stubborn foe if you attempt to pivot it through tight spaces. Clear the area of clutter, ensure the floor is level, and check for proper grounding. A level rack reduces stress on the doors and keeps rails aligned, which is essential for long-term reliability.

### Grounding and surge protection
Like any data facility asset, this rack benefits from proper grounding. Ensure that your PDU and devices share a common ground. This helps reduce hums and ground loops, especially with long cable runs. Pair the rack with a reliable surge protector and consider a dedicated power budget to keep voltage drops at bay on busy days.

### Mounting strategy
When mounting equipment, plan the load distribution across the rails. Heavier devices should be placed lower to maintain stability. Don’t forget weight distribution across the rack floor; if you sense a tilt, re-check the mounting and consider anchoring to the floor or wall where applicable. Use proper rails and avoid over-tightening screws; you want a secure fit without warping the rails.

### Maintenance and sanitation
Dust happens. The mesh doors provide airflow but also invite the occasional dust bunny. Establish a quarterly cleaning routine using a mild vacuum and microfiber cloth to remove stubborn dust from the vent perforations and around the rails. If you operate in a dust-heavy environment, consider a pre-filter or an additional air intake filter near the room’s intake to keep the cabinet clean without sacrificing airflow.
