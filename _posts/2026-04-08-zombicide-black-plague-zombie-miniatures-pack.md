## Assembly and Painting Tips
If you’ve built more than two boxes of plastic figures, you’ll know the drill: you want clean lines, snug joints, and paint that won’t rub off during enthusiastic table maneuvers. The Zombie Miniatures Pack follows the usual Zombicide approach: you build the figures from sprues, paint them, base them, and prepare them for battle. Here are a few tips I’ve found useful.

- Clean-up and test-fit: Use a sharp hobby knife to remove any flash and test fit each limb with the torso. A little trimming now pays dividends later when you’re not chasing loose arms across the gaming table.
- Base priming: Prime with a matte gray or black primer to give your flesh tones and drab clothing something to bite into. If you’re feeling fancy, a quick white spray helps highlight details before you wash them with darker tones.
- Flesh tones: For undead skin, start with a desaturated base (pale green or sickly gray) and highlight with lighter tones to bring out the bone structure. You want a corpse that reads well from across the table, not something that looks like a party guest who forgot to shower.
- Clothing and textures: Use washes to bring out folds in cloth and damage in leather. A muddy brown wash can turn a plain shirt into a battlefield record of wear and tear.
- Eyes: If you want an extra pop, a tiny dot of white or pale blue on the eye sockets can transform a bland zombie into something alive enough to notice you, which is usually a misnomer but fun for photos.
- Basing: Use basing materials to tie the zombies into your terrain. Blood textures, tufts, and grit can create the sense of a world that’s just a step away from collapse, which is exactly what you want for a Black Plague game.

Painting the pack can be a social activity, too. If you’re painting in a group, you can do quick-dry midpoints and take photos for a before-and-after montage. If you’re solo, you’ll still get the reward of a table that’s more alive (pun intended) than your last 20 group games.

Another tip: if you’re short on time, you can opt for contrast paints on the larger surfaces and save the highlights for later sessions. The contrast method can yield decent results in a fraction of the time, which is handy if you’re prepping for a livestream or a tournament that’s not quite ready for a color-scheme.
