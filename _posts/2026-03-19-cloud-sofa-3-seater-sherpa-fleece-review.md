## Materials, Care, and Maintenance: Keeping the Cloud Cloudy without Cloudy Vibes
Caring for sherpa fleece is not the same as caring for leathers or microfibers. Here are practical tips to keep your Cloud Sofa looking fresh and inviting:
- Regular cleaning: Use a soft vacuum brush to remove dust and pet hair. A lint roller or a mild fabric brush can also help maintain the nap. You’ll be surprised how much a few minutes of gentle brushing every week can improve the appearance.
- Stain management: Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. For light stains, a mixture of mild soap and lukewarm water applied with a soft cloth can help. Avoid soaking the fabric; sherpa fibers don’t appreciate a bath. If you have a stubborn mark, test any cleaner on a hidden corner first.
- Rotation and fluffing: Rotate cushions if possible and fluff them to help maintain shape. This prevents the cushions from forming an elongated bottom line with time.
- Washing the covers: Some sherpa upholstery options come with removable covers. If yours does, follow the manufacturer’s washing instructions closely. If not, consider using a dedicated upholstery cleaner designed for fleece fabrics. Always air-dry; never tumble-dry as heat can shrink or damage the nap.
- Pet management: If you have a furry friend who loves the Cloud Sofa as much as you do, invest in a good pet hair remover tool. The fleece nap will attract hair, but with regular maintenance, you can keep it looking pristine enough for photos, even with a couple of whiskery “co-pilots.”

Note: Because sherpa fleece can attract lint, keeping a small roller handy for quick touch-ups before guests arrive is a smart move. It’s the kind of habit that makes you feel like a responsible adult with a secret weapon for maintaining home aesthetics.

Durability is not just about the first year; it’s about how the couch behaves after two, five, or ten years of daily use. The Cloud Sofa was designed with durability in mind, but two realities deserve emphasis:
- Sherpa nap behavior: The longer you own sherpa fleece, the more you’ll notice subtle changes in how the nap lays. It’s not a disaster; it’s part of the fabric’s evolving personality. With regular care, you’ll see a graceful aging rather than a rapid decline in aesthetics.
