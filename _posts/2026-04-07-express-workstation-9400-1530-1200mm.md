## Unboxing and assembly
Let’s be honest: unboxing a 9.4 meter long desk is less unboxing and more chartering a small ship. The Express arrives in a modular kit designed to be assembled along your living room wall, not carried through a hallway with a kaleidoscope of adapters. You’ll need a metric toolkit, a power drill, a friend with two extra elbows, and a heroic tolerance for hardware.

Approximate steps:
1) Lay out the frame components along the wall where it will reside. This is where you realize you may need to prune your furniture collection or move a few potted plants the size of a small child.
2) Attach the steel frame to the floor and wall anchors. Yes, wall anchors. The last thing you want is a desk that experiences a mild earthquake and decides to roll away like a skateboard.
3) Install the top panels: the laminate or veneer surface is pre-cut to length but needs alignment with the frame. Have your favorite sanding cloth ready for any micrometeorite-sized imperfections.
4) Route cables through the integrated grommets and cable trays. The modular cable management system is where you start to understand why people shout wire management in their sleep.
5) Install power rails and USB hubs optional. If you forgot about a power strip, you will remember soon when your laptop charges with the momentum of a city bus.

Time estimate: if you already own a metric ton of modular components and you are not allergic to manual labor, plan for an afternoon. If you are allergic to DIY or have a busy schedule, hire someone. It is not the sort of project you want to ask your cat to supervise.

## Maintenance and longevity
A desk this size needs a little more attention to care. The top surface can resist minor scratches, but it’s wise to use coasters for cups and to keep the surface free of heavy objects when you’re not using it. The steel frame should be checked periodically for loose bolts and dust buildup in the cable channels. With moderate maintenance, this desk will likely outlive more than a few chairs in your collection.

- Cleaning: A damp microfiber cloth is enough for the top; avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the veneer.
- Cable management: Inspect your cable channels every few months. Cable ties can break, especially during rearrangements, so replace as needed.
- Hardware: Periodically tighten screws and re-check wall anchors to prevent wobble.
