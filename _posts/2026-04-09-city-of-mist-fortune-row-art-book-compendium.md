## What is Fortune Row and what does it contain?
Fortune Row, as a concept, is a district where power, myth, and vice intersect on a grid of rain-slick streets and neon signage. The art book lives up to that promise by presenting a curated journey through the district, featuring character portraits, environment art, prop sketches, signage, and a handful of narrative beats that feel like ready-to-play vignettes. The content is a blend of concept art and final renders, with commentary in the form of annotations from artists and designers. These annotations are not a chore to read; they feel like whispered notes from someone who has walked the row and knows its every alias.

The book is structured to serve multiple purposes. For the GM, it is a visual and descriptive toolkit that can spark session ideas, flesh out locations, or anchor a mystery in Fortune Row. For players, it offers glimpses into the city’s mood and the masks its residents wear, without forcing a single interpretation. And for fans who simply want to soak in the atmosphere, it stands as a compact, high-saturation tour of a living, breathing setting.

Key contents typically include:
- Concept art and final renders of major locales and key figures
- Detailed environment pieces that capture the mood of Fortune Row at different times of day
- Portraits and design notes for notable NPCs and antagonists
- Maps and location sheets that help you frame encounters on the table
- A curated visual glossary that ties aesthetics to the game’s Power tags and Mythos influence
- Short captions and annotations that give context without breaking immersion

If you want a taste of the world beyond the text, the art book delivers queasy glitz, night-slick noir, and mythical majesty on every page. It does not pretend to be a novella with pages of prose; it trades some of that for the kind of bite-sized, image-forward storytelling that suits a game where pictures carry equal weight to words.

There are a few pieces that will instantly become favorites for GMs looking to stage a session in Fortune Row. A neon-lit entrance to a forbidden speakeasy, an alleyway where posters peel to reveal a hidden mural, and a rooftop overlook that captures the city’s layered mythology with a single panoramic shot. ## Art direction and visual language
The art direction in Fortune Row leans into a synthesis of noir shadows and mythic brightness. You will see heavy ink lines, high-contrast lighting, and a color palette that leans toward purples, teals, and electric pinks in the neon zones, tempered by city dirty whites and rusted browns in the back alleys. It is a look that matches City of Mist’s core idea: legends walking among us, their portraits both glamorous and dangerous. The artists strike a balance between stylized, almost cinematic figures and more grounded, street-level scenes. The result is a collection that feels both aspirational and immediate, a gallery that would not feel out of place in a city’s underground club nor on a wall in a detective’s desk.

One of the strongest choices in Fortune Row is its treatment of color and light as storytelling devices. Color in the images often hints at a character’s power or a location’s mood. When a shot leans toward a color outside the natural spectrum, it signals that something is wrong or unusual in Fortune Row — a clever shortcut for GMs who want to convey significance without lengthy exposition. The interplay between chalky whites and electric neons creates a mood that is unmistakably City of Mist: fantastical, dangerous, and just a little bit dangerous; the wordplay is intentional, and the art backs it up with flair.

The typography is purposeful and legible, with headings that resemble street signs and art deco influences that nod to the game’s slightly retro-futurist vibe. It's not just about looking good; the layout guides the eye through the page in a way that feels natural for a table run by a tabletop GM who wants to quickly extract a piece of inspiration and drop it into the session without breaking pacing.

## How to use Fortune Row in play
If you run City of Mist campaigns, Fortune Row can serve as both a setting and a source of adventure prompts. Here are a few practical approaches:
- Session inspiration: Use a single image as a prompt and build a scene around it. Let the art carry the mood and let your players fill the details with their Masks and Mythoi. The result tends to be cinematic and surprisingly cohesive.
- Location toolkit: Treat Fortune Row locations as modular blocks. A particular storefront could be a crime scene in one session and a sanctuary in another; the visual cues provide quick associations that help you pivot on the fly.
- Character bounce: When you need a new antagonist, pick a portrait and a caption. The art communicates an implied backstory that you can bolt onto a stat block and movement patterns without overthinking it.
- Pacing and tone: The book’s lighting and color cues can guide your pacing. If a scene is meant to feel rushed or dangerous, you can reference the brighter neon or the darker shadows in the art to cue your table without explicit narration.

For players who crave a deeper dive, Fortune Row can also serve as a character inspiration resource. A player who loves a certain villainous fashion aesthetic or a particular mystery motif can pick a portrait as a muse for their own Mask or Mythos angle. The artwork’s fidelity keeps the stakes high while inviting players to imagine the city as a living gallery, where every piece hints at a larger story waiting to be discovered.
