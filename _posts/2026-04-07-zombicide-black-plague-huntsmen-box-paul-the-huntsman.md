In the world of Zombicide: Black Plague, expansions come in many flavors: more walkers, more heroes, more bosses, and occasionally more reasons to argue about dice rolls. Whether that mix lands well depends on your table dynamics: do you prefer a surgical strike or a full-on medieval mob shouting, “Boo-yah, plague style?” Either way, Paul the Huntsman is here to swing a big blade and, occasionally, a bigger attitude.

## What's in the Huntsmen Box?

The box is a neat, compact package that feels a bit like a treasure chest for a dungeon boss, minus the treasure. Inside, you’ll typically find:

- Paul the Huntsman miniature with a menacing pose and a big two-handed blade. He’s not subtle about his job description.
- A cohort of Huntsmen grunts, which function as loyal, terrifying backup dancers for Paul’s solo performances.
- New weapons, abilities, and special rules designed to synergize with the Huntsmen’s predatory playstyle.
- Scan-worthy tokens, an extra mini-rulebook page, and a few mission cards if you want to weave the hunts into an ongoing campaign.
- A splash of pointy fantasy swords and a dose of medieval creepiness that fits the Black Plague aesthetic like a burlap cape fits a necromancer — awkward, but stylish.

If you’re curious about the unboxing ritual, the setup is straightforward. The miniatures require some assembly (as always with Zombicide), but they aren’t the type to demand a gift-wrap war with your hobby knife. The quality of sculpts and the dynamic poses are on-par with other CMON offerings: memorable, with enough detail to keep your painting motivation alive through a long winter of undead management.

## Meet Paul the Huntsman

Paul the Huntsman is the star of this show, and he brings a specific brand of hunter efficiency to the board. He thrives on the chase, pun intended, and his abilities tend to emphasize the following themes:

- He punishes clusters. If your survivors cluster to loot a chest or revive a friend, Paul is likely to Slipstream through those lines and land a clean, punishing hit.
- He’s about zone control. His rules often reward aggressive positioning, forcing players to consider spread and spacing rather than just “one more turn of murder-spree.”
- He benefits from a strong hunter-support cast. The Huntsmen box isn’t just about Paul; his pack helps him enforce a corridor of doom where your heroes are forced to think two steps ahead or go home with a reduced wallet and a shattered morale.

In terms of narrative flavor, Paul stands out. He’s not a mindless chaser or a single-purpose boss — he embodies the medieval hunter archetype with a well-timed brutality that can turn a safe safe-house into a panic room in one bad dice night. The model is well-painted out of the box, with a hungry look and a blade that seems to hunger for not just flesh but for the glory of a perfectly executed ambush.

## How the Huntsmen Change the Game

Introducing Paul and his crew isn’t just adding more minis to your table; it’s rewriting the rhythm of encounters. Here’s what to expect when you drop the Huntsmen into a campaign:

- Pacing shifts toward ambush scenarios. Paul’s abilities encourage players to anticipate threats from multiple directions, not just the standard path of your heroes marching forward like a well-munned parade.
- Risk management becomes more dynamic. Do you chase a loot token, or do you retreat and preserve your survivors’ health? The Huntsmen box tends to reward smart retreat and careful positioning as much as it punishes reckless daring.
- The box introduces a distinct playstyle. While the base game rewards methodical planning and resource management, the Huntsmen subscription to the table tends to reward aggressive, high-risk plays that can pay off handsomely with a successful ambush.

This isn’t to say the Huntsmen box breaks the game; it’s more of a spicy ingredient that can elevate a good round into a great one when used with a steady hand and a sense of theatrical flair. If you’ve played through several Zombicide campaigns and feel like your survivors are becoming too predictable, Paul’s box can shake up the meta just enough to reset the fear factor.

## Strategies and Tactics with Paul and His Hunters

If you’re staring down a board loaded with zombies and a menacing silhouette named Paul, here are some practical strategies you can deploy:

- Control the choke points. Paul excels when you funnel the survivors through narrow corridors where he can maximize his melee reach. Use obstacles and barricades to shape the map in your favor.
- Focus on priority targets. Don’t burn actions on every zombie; you’re better off taking down a few high-threat targets with combined firepower and strong positioning. Paul’s health pool isn’t infinite; deplete his momentum and he becomes less of a guarantee.
- Coordinate with your ranged heroes. The Huntsmen benefit from a friend in the backline who can pin down reinforcements or remove threats from range, allowing Paul’s pack to close in with less resistance.
- Painful but effective. The Huntsmen can apply status effects or forced movement that disrupts the survivors’ plan. If you’re using a campaign mode, plan for the long haul and exploit the earlier missteps to set up a sweep later in the round.

A practical tactic: pre-plan your turns with a rough “plan A, plan B, plan C” approach. The Huntsmen are all about momentum and misdirection; keeping your turn plan fluid will help you adapt when Paul suddenly lunges from an unseen doorway with a determined snarl.

## Painting, Assembly, and Visuals

As with most CMON miniatures, Paul and his Huntsmen aren’t just functional—they’re fun to paint. The sculpt lines are clean, with enough fingernail detail on Paul’s blade and his armor to give you satisfying work without an insane level of micro-detail. If you’re into a tabletop diorama vibe, this box gives you ample material for a small “hunt camp” display. A few carefully chosen washes, dry brushing, and some contrasting basing will turn the table into a medieval hunting scene that doubles as a grim warning to any zombie trying to breach your line.

Assembly tips:
- Use super glue sparingly. A tiny amount goes a long way, especially on the Huntsmen’s stitched leather belts and Paul’s cloak.
- Prime and base coat before you dive into shading. It helps the details pop and gives you a better canvas for your color choices.
- Plan out your color scheme. Paul’s armor can be accessible in metallics or a rusted steel look, depending on your table’s theme. Either way, the contrast with the undead is where the drama happens.

For painters who want a blocky-but-epic tabletop, this set is a winner. It won’t overwhelm your current army of heroes but provides a striking centerpiece for your display shelf that also happens to be terrifying on the gaming table.

## Value, Pricing, and How It Stacks Up

Value in collectible miniatures hinges on two things: the number of usable models and the fun factor of playing with them. The Huntsmen Box typically sits in a mid-to-high tier price bracket for Zombicide expansions. Do you get enough extra gameplay to justify the cost? If you enjoy a frequent campaign, a boss-style antagonist, and the theater of a hunter’s chase, yes, you’re getting your money’s worth. If you’re a more casual player who rarely finishes a single campaign and mostly plays the base game with the occasional extra scenario, you might feel the cost is a touch steep for the added complexity.

Pro tip: if you’re shopping around, compare the Huntsmen Box with other expansion bundles that feature boss-type adversaries. Ask yourself how often you’ll actually deploy Paul in your sessions versus how often you’ll use the extra terrain or missions you might be missing out on in a smaller expansion.

## Comparisons: Huntsmen vs Other Expansions

To give you a sense of where the Huntsmen Box sits in the pantheon of Zombicide expansions, here are quick contrasts with a few common alternatives:

- Base Game vs Huntsmen. The base game provides the core experience: dice-rolling chaos, resource management, and a steady stream of zombie numbers. The Huntsmen Box adds a narrative boss encounter and a unique mini-squad that can tilt the balance toward ambush strategies.
- Boss-type expansions. Some boss packs are simpler or more single-purpose. Paul’s Huntsmen promote team play and map control, encouraging more cooperative planning rather than stand-and-shoot tactics.
- Themed mini-expansions. There are sets that emphasize different factions or special rules. The Huntsmen are somewhat self-contained but shine if you lean into a medieval-hunting theme for your table.

If you’re trying to decide whether to buy, ask yourself: do you crave a narrative arc with a memorable boss that makes your survivors feel hunted? If yes, the Huntsmen Box is a solid contender.

## Scenarios, Campaign Integration, and Table Talk

Paul’s presence invites you to weave new narratives into your campaigns. Here are ideas to maximize integration:
- Campaign arc: start with Paul as a “local legend” that the heroes hear about in a tavern. The first encounter should be a cautionary tale, with Paul chasing survivors through a ruined courtyard before a boss fight mid-campaign.
- Tiered missions: design a sequence where the first mission is to locate a hunter’s cache, the second is to escape a narrow corridor, and the third culminates in a climactic showdown with Paul and his Huntsmen.
- Thematic loot and morale: reward survivors for delaying Paul’s assault or for surviving a “hunt” scene where the hunters must be outsmarted rather than simply shot down.

For players who like maps and campaign depth, this expansion offers an excellent canvas to craft memorable sessions. If you’ve got a recurring group, you’ll likely return to Huntsmen scenarios as a palate cleanser between longer, grittier campaigns.

## Solo Play, Table Talk, and Social Dynamics

If you’re a solo player or run a small-table campaign, Paul remains a formidable challenge. On a one-poster table, the Huntsmen’s tactics can feel both thrilling and punishing, depending on your survivorship pool. The key to making solo rounds enjoyable is to keep Paul’s threat level predictable and to give yourself a clear path to retreat or regroup after a failed ambush. If your solo runs begin to feel unfair, dial back the spawning rate, or introduce a “hunt counter” that unlocks safer zones after a set number of turns.

On the social side, Huntsmen nights often bring the “who’s the bravest” debates to the forefront. You’ll hear the old debates about risk versus reward, the classic “one more turn” justification, and the eternal question: did the dice sabotage you, or did you forget to bring a contingency plan? Either way, Paul and his crew give you material for hours of storytelling, even when the dice behave like a mob of groaning zombies.
