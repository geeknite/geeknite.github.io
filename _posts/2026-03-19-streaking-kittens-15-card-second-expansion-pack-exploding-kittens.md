## Card Breakdown and Play Feel (A Love Letter to Rowdy Rooms)
Let’s drill down into how the 15 cards perform in the wild. I’ll break it into bite-sized sections so you can skim for your favorite moment, then come back to savor the jokes and misplays.

### Streak Cards
Streak cards are a dare with a wink. They push you toward a fast tempo, rewarding players who commit to action rather than overthinking. In practical terms, you’ll often see someone sprint a card, then watch the tide turn as the next card shouts, Not so fast, friend. The humor lies in watching players respond to a moment they didn’t foresee, where a single card can convert a confident plan into an improvised comedy routine with dramatic pauses for snacks and commentary.

### Trick Cards
Trick cards are the narrative devices: they let you rewrite the moment, swap positions, or force someone else to reveal a plan to the room. The trick is not overuse. The more you lean on them, the more the table learns to anticipate your antics. You’ll feel like a magician who’s terrible at sleight of hand—except your audience is a crowd of cat lovers who appreciate both misdirection and fur patterns.

### Counter Cards
Counter cards exist to remind you that the game isn’t yours alone. They’re a fun counterweight to chaos: you need timing, nerve, and a sneaky little bit of luck. A well-timed Counter can turn a near-sure-winner into a dramatic dethronement, which is exactly the kind of dramatic arc this game loves to sponsor.

### Special Interaction Cards
These are the quirky tie-breakers. They might require you to perform a tiny ritual (okay, not really) or align a group of players on a bizarre, shared decision that doesn’t quite make sense until you see the board glow with laughter. The beauty of these is how quickly they move the momentum. The moment you realize a card will complicate a later move, you’re already grinning at the future social chaos you’re about to unleash.
