## How to play (at a high level, because we’re not trying to boil the ocean here)

Exploding Kittens is a take-that style card game where your primary objective is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. The Defuse card can save you, typically allowing you to shuffle the kitten back into the deck or otherwise duck the catastrophe with a clever trick. But there are twists: a See the Future card gives you a glimpse into the top few cards of the deck, which is surprisingly helpful when you’re trying not to blow up your own turn. Other cards nullify, reverse, copy, steal, or otherwise mess with your friends’ day. The game moves fast, so you’ll find yourself making snap judgments that would worry your domesticity if they were made in a different context.

The core loop:

1. Draw a card at the end of your turn.
2. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you must play a Defuse card if you have one, or lose a kitten (i.e., lose the game unless your skilled allies pull a miracle).
3. Use action cards to stall your opponents, peek into the future of the deck, or alter the math in a moment of dramatic bravado.
4. Repeat until only one kitten remains or you can declare victory with a flourish that makes your neighbors reconsider the friendship you share.

### The two most important cards: Exploding Kitten and Defuse

The Exploding Kitten is the star of the show. It’s a perfect symbol of what this game is about: risk, luck, and a dash of savagery toward your friends. The Defuse card is your lifeboat. It gives you a chance to survive an explosion when your name is on the deck’s list of doom. Some players hoard Defuses like ancient relics, while others bravely or foolishly spend them to flip the deck on a critical moment. The tension from this dynamic is where the game earns its reputation for “just one more round.”

## Strategy and tactics: beyond luck

Yes, there’s luck involved—this is a card deck, after all. But there’s a surprising amount of strategy in Exploding Kittens, especially if you’re playing with more players. Here are some practical tips to level up:

- Manage your Defuses like precious gems. If you’ve got two Defuses, you can push your luck more aggressively, knowing you have a safety net. If you’re down to one Defuse and the deck is thinning, you may want to pivot toward defensive plays.
- Use See the Future strategically. If you can glimpse a lot of Exploding Kitten cards in the next few draws, you can time your Defuse for maximum impact or force someone else into a bad decision.
- Attack cards can shift the burden. For parties with larger player counts, you can leverage Attack to force others to act first, increasing the odds that they’ll draw the dreaded kitten.
- Nope cards create social friction in helpful ways. They’re not just counters; they’re tools to shape the game’s tempo. Use them to stall, to save a friend from a critical mistake, or to create dramatic plot twists that get everyone involved.
- Always read the room. Some players want chaos; others want quick, clean rounds. Tailor your play style to your group’s vibe. If the room’s energy dips, a well-timed See the Future or a cheeky Nope can re-inject momentum.

Great players aren’t those who draw the most kittens—they’re the ones who leverage timing, misdirection, and a touch of theater to outplay their friends without turning the table into a battlefield. The game rewards creative problem-solving in a social setting, not just mathematical optimization.
