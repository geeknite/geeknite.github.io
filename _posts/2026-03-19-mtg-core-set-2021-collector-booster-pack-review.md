## The practical side: unboxing, care, and how to handle foils

Foils require care. If you want your investment to retain its shimmer, use protective sleeves and consider a top-loader for display. The last thing you want is a foiled card with a micro crease from a careless shuffle or a careless drawer where they get bent. We recommend storing foils in a cooler environment with moderate humidity—yes, the life of a modern MTG collector is basically a BIOS-level balancing act between climate control and card stock aesthetics.

In terms of handling, avoid excessive finger oils and watch out for scratches on the surface. Foil cards have a reputation for showing minor scuffs more readily than non-foil cards. If you intend to use your cards in play, the wear will accumulate differently, and that’s where the practical value — if you care about play value — intersects with the aesthetic value. This is a long way of saying: enjoy the stickers and the shine, but keep expectations grounded about play performance when foils are involved.
