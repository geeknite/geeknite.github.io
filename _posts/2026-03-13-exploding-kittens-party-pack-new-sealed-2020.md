## Unboxing: The Box, The Glorious Madness, The Slightly Questionable Glue

Unboxing is a ritual. It signals that your Saturday night is about to be either legendary or a disaster fueled by potato chips and questionable life choices. The Party Pack box is the size of a small hardcover encyclopedia of chaos. It’s sturdy, glossy, and slightly sarcastic in a way that only a box full of exploding cats can be. The seal feels ceremonial, like a tiny guardian of tabletop destiny.

Inside, you’ll find a mess of cards, tokens, and a rulebook that looks like it was designed by someone who owns three cats and a caffeine addiction. The card stock is decent enough to withstand a few high stress rounds; nothing says fun like shuffling a deck that could double as a kitchen knife handle for an impromptu slice and dice demonstration. The art is adorable and borderline chaotic—perfect for mood setting when your friends are debating whether to grab another beer or a therapy session.

## Contents Galore: What You Actually Get

The Party Pack is marketed as the king of party games for groups of friends and reluctant in-laws. It promises more players, new cards, and a deeper dive into the cat apocalypse theme. Let’s break down what you actually get when you crack the seal in 2020 glory:

- A fully compatible subset of Exploding Kittens base deck along with new monstrosities designed to mess with your friends heads.
- A set of Defuse cards, which are towels for those oh no moments when someone draws the dreaded Exploding Kitten.
- The all important Exploding Kitten cards that create a delightful mix of suspense and panic whenever you see a bright illustration of a bomb with a kitten face.
- New action cards that let you poke your friends in slightly creative ways, including See the Future that reminds you not to have too much hope.
- A rulebook that tries its best to be concise, but occasionally veers into fantasy territory where you are the hero who must save the kingdom using a kitty themed deck.

If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, the Party Pack adds an extra layer to the baseline experience: more players means more chaos, more iterations of who has the Defuse again, and more opportunities to unleash the smug grin of victory as you defuse with a reliance on pure luck and a dash of cunning.

## How to Play: Setup, Rules, and Minimal Brain Power

The quick setup is delightfully simple: shuffle and deal cards, place Exploding Kitten cards into the stack with a few Defuse cards, and make sure you’ve got a healthy supply of snacks to bribe players into staying at the table longer than planned. The rules are intentionally straightforward, so you can start playing within minutes of tearing open the packaging provided you can locate a table not covered in snack wrappers or the kind of clutter that makes you question your life choices.

A typical round revolves around players drawing cards and playing special abilities to thwart each other’s plans. You’ll see attacks, favors, and the classic no you cant have my Defuse drama, all while the bomb cat balance teeters on the edge of chaos. In practice, it’s a rhythm game in which you try to predict who will be the next to explode, and who can pull a lucky Defuse from under their sleeve. The pacing is brisk, which makes it perfect for a crowd that wants to switch between intense tension and goofy laughter in the span of a single coffee break.

As with any party game, the real victory is the stories you’ll tell after the game ends. The Party Pack generates moments you’ll quote at future gatherings—moments that trigger laughter and slight embarrassment in equal measure. If you’re aiming for an evening of high tension hilarity, this is a strong contender.

## Experience and Humor: Why It Works (Sometimes Too Well)

What makes Exploding Kittens appealing is the combination of quick rounds, cute but menacing artwork, and the chaotic energy of players trying to out think one another with a handful of defusal cards and cat based mischief. The Party Pack emphasizes party dynamics: a larger player count means more reaction shots, more rivalries, and more chances for someone to say I didnt mean to blow up the table I just meant to blow up your ego. The humor lands best when players embrace the silliness: the cards offer silly effects, the artwork leans into the cartoonish fear of explosions, and the rules never pretend to be more serious than a silly meme in card form.

However, with bigger groups come bigger problems: longer setup, longer rounds, and a higher chance that someone will blame the unboxing video for their predicament. The social energy is contagious, but you’ll want to set expectations early this is a party game, not a strategic simulation. If you’re after nuanced strategy and deep puzzle solving, look elsewhere; if you’re hoping for a riotous ride with friends who are comfortable admitting they once hugged a cat themed card for luck, you’re in the right place.

## The Party Pack vs The Base Game: What’s the Extra Bite?

The base Exploding Kittens game is a lean, fast paced experience. The Party Pack is essentially the same engine with a few upgrades and a larger capacity for players. The differences tend to fall into a few categories:

- Player count: The Party Pack supports more players, which is great for chaotic gatherings and family gatherings that involve reluctant teenagers or the dog who keeps stealing chips.
- Card variety: You get a wider pool of action cards that can alter the flow of the game in surprising ways. Some cards will encourage you to reroute your strategy mid round, which can be hilarious or terrifying depending on your luck and your willingness to bluff.
- Replayability: With more cards and options, you’ll enjoy more varied rounds and a higher likelihood that no two games feel the same—at least until someone brings out a variant that fixes the game by removing all the explosions.

That said, the Party Pack isn’t a wholesale reinvention. If you’re a die hard fan of the original, you’ll recognize the core mechanics and simply appreciate the additional chaos. If you’re both new and indecisive, the Party Pack is a comfortable gateway that won’t overwhelm you with rules while still offering a deliciously chaotic experience.

## Durability, Build Quality, and the Joy of Handling Cards

Card stock matters. In a fast paced party game, you’re going to shuffle, shuffle, and shuffle again. The 2020 sealed Party Pack cards hold up to enthusiastic hands but begin showing micro wear after a handful of sessions. The art is preserved, which matters because a scratched face on an Exploding Kitten card would be a tragedy of kitten proportions. The box itself is robust—adequate padding, sturdy lid, and a satisfying clack when you drop it on the table with the confidence of someone who knows they’re about to win the round. The storage solution inside is sensible, too, with compartments that keep decks separated and prevent the kind of wholesale chaos that happens when you accidentally shuffle in catnip and a couple of stray potato chips.

Visuals matter in party games because they cue mood. The art style leans toward cute but with a mischievous twist, a balance that keeps the product accessible to casual players while still appealing to geeks who enjoy slightly dark humor and a good pun about existential meowmentum.

## Value for Money: Price, Availability, and Sealed Status

New sealed 2020 editions of party packs can be a tricky beast in the wild world of board game scalpers and retro hoarders. You might pay a premium for a sealed edition on the used market, but if your goal is nostalgia laden chaos with a fresh scent of cardboard, a brand new sealed copy still has value. The question to ask: do you want to reopen the box with the satisfaction of a pristine seal or do you prefer the thrill of a preloved, well loved edition that has come to tell its own stories?

If you’re playing with a group on a modest budget, the Party Pack is often priced competitively for the entertainment it yields. The number of laugh out loud moments per dollar is a strong selling point for a party staple that can survive years of weekly casual nights and accidental edible accidents during snack breaks. For folks who crave the extra layer of novelty, a 2020 sealed edition may appeal to collectors. The new sealed label can add a sense of occasion to the unboxing. It’s the kind of thing you post to social media with a caption about vibes and a dozen cat emojis while your friends pretend to be impressed by your stack of cardboard glory.

## Pros and Cons: A Quick Hit List

Pros:
- High replayability with varied card interactions.
- Large player count that makes it perfect for parties and family gatherings.
- Charming and cheeky artwork that invites giggles rather than groans.
- Quick rounds; you can go from zero to chaos in under five minutes.

Cons:
- The chaos can be exhausting in extended sessions; not ideal for serious gamers who crave calm and methodical planning.
- Higher player count means longer turnaround times if your group is slow or indecisive (you know who you are).
- Some cards can feel overpowered or underpowered depending on who’s holding them, which might lead to debates rather than laughter.

If you want to avoid the chaos entirely, consider a smaller party game with less downtime. But if your goal is madcap energy and the occasional howl of laughter at the wrong moment, this is a strong pick.
