### Practicality: Durability and Maintenance
Wool fibers are naturally good at resisting wear, and a carefully chosen density helps the rug hold up under high-traffic areas. Routine vacuuming with a gentle setting keeps the pile looking uniform, and you’ll see the color staying vibrant with minimal brightening. Spills should be blotted quickly; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber fraying. A mild carpet cleaner or water with a touch of dish soap can help for stubborn stains, but always test a small area first. For those who maintain a busy home theater or gaming-zone vibe, investing in a rug pad is a good idea. It reduces slippage and adds a layer of insulation that helps with sound dampening—nice bonus for the room where the speakers do most of their singing.

### Maintenance Schedule (A Simple Plan)
- Weekly: Quick vacuum across the rug to remove surface dust and keep the texture even.
- Monthly: Rotate the rug 90 degrees to ensure even wear and avoid permanent fade patterns in sunlight-exposed zones.
- Biannually: Deep-clean with a professional service or a strong steam clean if you know what you’re doing.
- Annually: Inspect the backing edges for looseness and re-tighten if needed.
- After spills: Blot, blot again, use a mild detergent and water, and dry carefully.
