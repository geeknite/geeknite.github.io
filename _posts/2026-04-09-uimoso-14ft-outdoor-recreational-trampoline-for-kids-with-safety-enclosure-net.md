## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning routine

Dust, leaf litter, and the occasional stray acorn are part of backyard life. Clear off debris regularly and rinse the mat with a gentle hose-down if you’re dealing with sticky sap or bird droppings. Avoid strong chemical cleaners that could degrade the mat or padding. A soft brush can be used to remove stubborn grime, and a quick inspection after rain can help you catch damp spots that could cause mold or mildew if left unattended.

### Winter storage and seasonal care

If you’re in a climate with snowy winters, consider disassembly or at least covering the unit when not in use. A good weather cover can extend the life of the jumping mat and protect the net from heavy snow buildup. If you don’t take it down, ensure the trampoline is dry before covering to reduce mold risk. If you must leave it up year-round, place it on a flat, well-drained surface and anchor it properly to withstand wind gusts.

## Safety Tips and Best Practices

### Placement and ground clearance

Place the trampoline on level ground with ample clearance around and above. Avoid low-hanging branches, fences, or power lines. Do not place the trampoline on a slope where it could slide or tip during vigorous bouncing. A flat, open area with a soft surface inside a fenced yard is ideal to reduce the risk of falls onto hard ground.

### Net maintenance

Regularly inspect the enclosure net for rips or tears. Small tears can quickly become bigger holes if ignored. Repair or replace damaged netting promptly to avoid any accidental escapes during a dramatic dive from the top. Keep the zipper closed when in use and check the seam lines around the zipper for wear. A little preventative maintenance goes a long way here.

### Jumping rules for younger children

Set simple rules: one or two kids inside at a time for higher-energy bounces, or a maximum of three kids if you’re controlling the chaos. Encourage kids to keep both feet on the mat and to avoid rough play that could result in collisions. For activities like flip attempts or stunt jumps, it’s wise to supervise or steer those sessions toward a more controlled play area to avoid injuries.
