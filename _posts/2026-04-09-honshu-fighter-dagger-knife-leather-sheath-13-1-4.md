## Maintenance and Care

Maintenance for a stainless blade is typically straightforward. A quick wipe-down after use to remove moisture and oils, a light coat of oil in after-use storage, and periodic sharpening with a proper stone will extend the life of the edge. The leather sheath benefits from an occasional conditioning with a suitable leather conditioner to keep the material supple and prevent cracking in dry environments. Avoid soaking the sheath in water, which could cause warping or loss of color over time. If you live in a humid climate, consider periodic checks for mold or mildew, and address promptly to keep your blade and sheath pristine.
