## Maintenance: Cleanliness, Care, and Longevity
Wool carpets respond well to routine maintenance. Vacuuming with a beater bar on a low setting helps keep the pile upright and prevents matting. We found that the rug’s dense weave hides minor crumbs well; still, a quick weekly vacuum keeps everything crisp. For spills, act quickly. Blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and treat with a mild wool-safe cleaner. For stubborn stains, consult a professional rug cleaner. Wool fiber appreciates professional care every 1-2 years if heavily used.

A crucial habit: rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if your seating area is asymmetrical or if the sun hits one corner more than another. Prolonged sun exposure can lead to slight fading in the most exposed sections; if you have a sunny living room, consider drapes or blinds to minimize UV damage over time.

Maintenance also includes a protective rug pad. The pad reduces movement on slick floors, cushions feet, and prevents wear along the edges where foot traffic is most intense. We recommend a non-slip felt pad for hard surfaces and a slightly cushioned pad for carpeted floors.
