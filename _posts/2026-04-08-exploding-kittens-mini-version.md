## How to play in a handful of minutes
### Setup: speed-run edition
- Shuffle the deck and deal a handful of starting cards to each player (the exact number changes with house rules, so we’ll keep to the standard mini guidelines here).
- Place the remaining deck in the center.
- Decide who goes first, preferably the person who didn’t just spill their drink while reading the rules aloud.

### On your turn: the quick-fire rhythm
- Draw a card. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re about to perform a life-choice: use a Defuse card if you have one or take the exploding consequences. In the Mini-Version, there aren’t a million different permutations—the chaos comes from how you use your other cards to dodge, deflect, or derail.
- If you decide to play a card, you can trigger one of the action cards (like See the Future, which lets you peek at upcoming cards, or Attack, which passes a turn to someone else) or skip a draw with the cunning arrogance of a cat who’s just learned you’re allergic to chaos.
- The round continues around the table until someone draws their last “live” card without an exploding kitten. That player wins—unfortunately for the rest, because they’ve just become a delightful memory in the annals of table-top tragedy.

### Endgame vibe and pacing
In Mini-Version, the pacing is punchier. The shorter cycles mean you’ll likely finish an entire match in the time it takes to binge a couple of episodes of your favorite sci-fi guild drama. The risk factor is still there—the “I thought you were my ally” moment hits hard when the last dramatic card reveals itself. Expect gleeful groans, exaggerated gasps, and at least one person muttering, “I didn’t see that coming” even when the dice read the tiniest possible fate.

## Strategy and player dynamics in the Mini world
### The core approach: be decisive, be deceptive
- Don’t hoard Defuse cards forever. In Mini, you’ll want to balance saving Defuses for the late game with using them to avoid tempting danger in the early rounds. It’s a psychology game as much as a card game.
- Use your action cards to manipulate the turn order strategically. If you can pass the danger around without direct confrontation, you’ll keep yourself ahead in the late game where a single bad draw can ruin you.
- Keep track of what your rivals are likely to hold. If someone’s been defusing every turn, you can press your advantage by pressing the action cards you have left to maximize disruption.

### Player interaction and house rules
- The beauty of Exploding Kittens Mini is that you can insert quick house rules without heavy preparation. For example, you can implement a “no look-see” rule where players cannot peek at the next three cards, which increases tension—especially when the deck is thinner and the next card matters more.
- You can also implement “kitty-cat chaos” rounds where players must perform a silly action if they’re forced to draw a kitten. It adds a layer of humor and a small amount of social pressure to perform the silly action with maximum enthusiasm.

## Variants, tweaks, and house rules
### Quick-start variants
- See-the-Future fast-forward: Instead of peeking three cards, allow players to peek exactly one card from the top and then reveal it to the table. It speeds up or slows down the information flow depending on your Zen alignment.
- Double-defuse mode: If you want a longer game and more dramatic late rounds, permit two Defuse cards per player in a special game mode. It shifts the late-game tension and invites dramatic, mercy-kissing decisions.

### Narrative optional rules
- You can add a light narrative flourish, where each round corresponds to a silly scenario (e.g., saving a birthday cake, defending the last slice, or rescuing a stray drone from the cat). It won’t change the mechanics but will inject added giggles and a reason to heckle your friends for their heroic or cowardly plays.
