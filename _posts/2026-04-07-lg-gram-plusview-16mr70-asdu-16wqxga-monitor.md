## Calibrating and Color Accuracy

if color precision is a critical requirement for your workflow you will want to calibrate the display. (calibration tools like a colorimeter or a spectrophotometer can help you tame white point and gamma so you get consistent results across devices). after a quick calibration you may see more faithful color reproduction and more consistent greyscale response. the panel can handle standard srgb and dci p3 color spaces with proper calibration, though keep expectations aligned with the role of a portable display rather than a high end color grading panel. the goal here is not certification but practical reliability for day to day tasks.

calibration steps you might follow include:
- run the calibration tool and select the target color space
- adjust white point toward your workspace lighting (roughly 6500 K for neutral light)
- tweak gamma to around 2.2 for typical content viewing
- save the profile and set the OS to use it as the default color management profile
