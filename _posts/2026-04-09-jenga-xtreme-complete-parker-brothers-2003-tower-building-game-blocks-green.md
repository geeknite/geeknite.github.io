## Gameplay and feel
If you know classic Jenga, you’ve got the core mechanics down: take turns removing blocks from anywhere below the topmost level and placing them on the top, aiming to avoid toppling the tower. Jenga Xtreme adds a pinch more tension with the way the blocks shift and the subtle resistance you feel when you tug a block that’s flirting with gravity. The green edition has a slightly different “tactile memory” than the more natural-toned sets; you don’t forget which blocks you grabbed, because the neon green cues your brain with a high-contrast signal that says, This is the moment you must be precise and confident.

Row by row, the tower becomes a living organism. The blocks themselves have a quiet friction that helps with delicate pulls, which is essential since a miscalculated move can unleash a domino effect with the elegance of a cat in a sunbeam knocking over a vase. The play experience is a mix of calm, razor-thin focus and roaring laughter as players attempt to navigate the shifting center of gravity while the room’s ambient noise grows louder with every wobble.

### Rules quick-start
1) Build the tower as per standard Jenga: 18 levels, 3 blocks per level, 54 blocks total. 2) Decide who goes first with a fair coin flip or dramatic rock-paper-scissors. 3) On your turn, remove one block from anywhere below the top level and place it on the topmost level. 4) You may touch only one block at a time; if the tower falls, you’re out. 5) The last player standing wins. Simple, but the suspense is delicious and ridiculous in equal measure.

## Tips for players of all ages
- Start slow. The first few moves set the tone for the rest of the game. If you rush, you’ll become a cautionary tale about gravity and bravado.

- As the tower narrows toward the top, stay calm. Deep breaths help you stabilize your hands and resist the urge to overthink every move.
- If you’re feeling competitive, rotate the pulling style between players to add variety and keep everyone on their toes.
