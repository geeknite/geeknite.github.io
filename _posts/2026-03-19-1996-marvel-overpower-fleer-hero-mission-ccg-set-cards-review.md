## What is OverPower? A quick refresher
OverPower is not your garden-variety trading card game of the mid-90s. It pitched heroics and foes with a strong current of fantasy power. The Marvel line pushed iconic characters into a space where power levels mattered, but so did synergy and mission outcomes. You could end up with a fight where Wolverine and Captain America team up to tackle a mission card while Magneto’s schemes threaten to tilt the entire battlefield. The result is a game that’s loud, cinematic, and frankly a little chaotic in the best possible way. The art is bold, the text dense, and the whole enterprise feels like a time capsule of licensing ambition: more is more, and more glittery foil is definitely more.

## Maintenance: storage and care for vintage cards
To lock in the best possible condition, treat the 46-card lot with care. Use soft sleeves and rigid top-loaders for each card, store in a cool, dry place, and consider a small display binder with clear pages. Avoid heavy friction from constant flipping, and keep the cards away from direct sunlight that can fade color and gloss over time. If you decide to display the lot, rotate cards periodically to prevent any single piece from suffering prolonged light exposure.

## Authenticity and provenance tips
Counterfeits exist in the world of vintage CCGs, even for items with nostalgic value. Protect yourself by asking for seller history, offering up clear photos, and checking for telltale signs of reproduction: unusual backing, inconsistent font rendering, or incorrect year labeling. A reputable seller will happily provide information about the card back design, typical printing marks of 1996 Fleer sets, and any serial numbers or batch identifiers.
