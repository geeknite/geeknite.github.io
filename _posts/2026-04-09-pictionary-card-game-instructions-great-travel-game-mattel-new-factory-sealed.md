### How to play: quick start guide
Step 1: Set up
Split into two or more teams. Each team marks a place on the scorepad. The aim is simple: reveal a clue on the card, draw something that hints at the clue, and let your teammates interpret your artistic chaos before time runs out.
Step 2: Draw a card
On your turn, pull a clue card from the deck. The card reveals the word or phrase your team must guess. The prompts range from obvious objects to wonderfully perplexing phrases that will make your crew question your taste in art and humor.
Step 3: Draw within the timer
Start the timer and begin the doodle. You may not use letters or numbers, and you should avoid writing the answer on the drawing surface. The goal is to convey the clue with visuals alone while your teammates attempt to decode your creative chaos.
Step 4: Guess and score
If your team guesses the clue before the timer expires, you earn points. If they miss, the other team may steal by guessing the clue after your turn ends. Scoring methods vary by edition, but the catch remains: fast thinking, bold lines, and the occasional heroic misinterpretation.
Step 5: Rotate and rinse and repeat
Play continues with alternating turns. The game ends when a team reaches the victory threshold on the scorepad or after a pre-set number of rounds. The rules are simple enough for a kid to grasp, yet flexible enough for adults to twist into humorous chaos.
Step 6: Quick variant ideas
If you crave extra chaos, try house rules like double points for the most ridiculous drawing or a no-talking round to heighten the pressure on your doodle to convey meaning without sound.

### The drawing tips you never knew you needed
- Start with simple shapes: circles, squares, lines. The right placement of basic shapes can convey complex ideas quickly.
- Use icons to communicate: a bulb for idea, a paw for pet, a simple wheel for vehicle. Symbols beat long explanations every time.
- Color matters, but use it strategically: a dash of color can help differentiate concepts, but too much color can blur the message.
- Practice on the go: doodle on a napkin or notepad to get your hand used to drawing under travel conditions.
- Embrace the chaos: half the fun is seeing how wildly different team members interpret the same clue.
