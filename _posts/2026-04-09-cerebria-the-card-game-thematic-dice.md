### Turn structure and dice resolution

Turns flow with a rhythm that should feel familiar to anyone who has played card-driven games with a dice twist. Each turn you draw a card or two, roll your dice to generate mood resources, and then use those resources to trigger card effects. The dice act as both currency and narrative levers. Rolling a cascade of Joy and Courage while avoiding Fear can let you push a victory condition forward; rolling mostly Anger and Sadness might unleash a dramatic, not-quite-pleasant twist that your group will discuss for the next five minutes.

One of the clever design decisions is how dice power interacts with card text. Some cards require you to sacrifice emotional tokens to perform heroic feats; others reward you for balancing multiple emotions at once. It’s not just about luck; your choices mold the future of the game’s emotional landscape. You’ll find yourself planning a couple of moves ahead, then sacrificing a dice result to scythe through a tricky obstacle when your opponent least expects it. The tension is real, even when the dice are not cooperating with your original plan.

The scoring system is thematically flavored and provides a satisfying sense of progression.
