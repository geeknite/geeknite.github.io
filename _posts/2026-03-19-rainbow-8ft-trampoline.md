## Safety considerations
Safety is the most important factor when it comes to backyard trampolines, and the Rainbow 8ft Trampoline does a decent job of addressing these concerns within its price range. The net is securely fastened and the padding around the springs reduces risk of minor injuries. The key to safety is proper supervision, a soft landing area such as grass or a padded mat, and adherence to weight guidelines.

### Age and weight guidelines
We recommend children above the age of 6 with parental supervision. The weight limit sits around 150 pounds for the entire unit; keep in mind that multiple children on the mat at once increases the risk of collision. If your child is younger or smaller, consider a smaller enclosure or a freestanding pad to protect edges.

### Enclosure net and padding
The enclosure net is not a barrier to be trusted completely; always supervise. The zipper should be closed when the jumper is inside. The padding around springs reduces risk of pinch injuries, but kids should still avoid the edge and try to stay toward the center of the mat.

## Safety myths vs facts
There are a few myths that tend to pop up around backyards and trampolines. The first is that bigger is always safer. In fact bigger can be more dangerous if you overestimate a small space. The second myth is that safety nets guarantee safety. Nets reduce risk but do not remove it; supervision remains essential. The third myth is that a rainbow design makes it inherently safer for kids because it is bright and cheerful. Design is a factor, but the real safety factor is the user and the parent.
