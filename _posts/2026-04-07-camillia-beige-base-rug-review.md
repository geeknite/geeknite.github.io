## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance

### Routine Care
- Vacuum regularly with a standard setting to avoid pulling at the fibers. Avoid aggressive suction on delicate patterns.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if there’s a dominant light source or a frequently used seating area.
- Use a rug pad to prevent sliding and to add cushion underfoot. The pad also helps minimize noise when you drag chairs across the floor in a dramatic fashion during internal monologues about adulthood.

### Spill Response
Act quickly. Blot spills with a clean cloth or paper towel—do not rub, which can push stains deeper into the fibers. For many common liquids, a mild solution of water and gentle detergent can help; always test a small, inconspicuous area first to avoid color changes. If a more persistent stain appears, consult your favorite fabric care guide for synthetic fiber recommendations.

### Deep Cleaning
Occasionally, consider a light professional cleaning if you’re nesting for a long period or hosting a gathering that demands near-immaculate presentation. If you’re DIY-ing a deep clean, use a gentle rug shampoo and ensure thorough drying to prevent mold or odor. Always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines for your specific fiber blend.
