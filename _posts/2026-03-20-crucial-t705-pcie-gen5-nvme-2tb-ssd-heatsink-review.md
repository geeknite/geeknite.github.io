### Real-world performance: how fast is fast enough?
Let’s talk numbers beyond the marketing sheet. In synthetic benchmarks and practical tests, Gen5 drives often crush the Gen4 era in sequential workloads. The T705’s claimed read speeds scream “ready for the next-gen,” but the real question is: what about mixed workloads, random IO, and payloads that aren’t just sequential streaming?

- Sequential reads at high queue depths reach toward the upper end of the 14,500 MB/s range. Real-world reads from large, contiguous files (think game assets or media libraries) will come close to those numbers when the drive isn’t thermally throttling.
- Sequential writes depend more heavily on sustained thermal performance and idle background tasks. Expect high-speed writes to hold up well during long transfers, but not to exceed the higher end of the spec in every scenario—especially on laptops with less airflow.
- Random I/O (4K, 8K, etc.) tends to be where Gen5 shines, providing noticeably snappier boot operations, faster texture streaming in modern titles, and quicker app load times. The presence of a heatsink helps maintain these gains during longer sessions, which is where many drives without efficient cooling start to fall behind.

For gamers, content creators, and power users who routinely juggle large assets, the T705’s strengths are clear: consistent performance under load, and the peace of mind that you’re not throttling to a crawl just after you hit the “start render” button.

### How to install and optimize for best results
- Ensure your motherboard supports PCIe Gen5 x4 NVMe and that you’re using a compatible M.2 slot. Some laptops reserve PCIe lanes differently; a quick check in your BIOS or motherboard manual helps.
- If you’re on a laptop, confirm there’s enough clearance around the slot for the heatsink. The T705’s heatsink adds some height, which can matter in ultrabooks with tight tolerances.
- Enable NVMe optimization settings in your BIOS/UEFI if the option is present. Some platforms provide performance-enhancing options for Gen5 devices that can help maintain peak throughput.
- In Windows, keep the disk aligned, and consider enabling TRIM and periodic garbage collection to maximize long-term performance. This is standard practice for modern NVMe drives but worth reiterating when you’re chasing 14,500 MB/s read speeds.
- Use a tool to monitor drive temperatures. If you’re seeing throttling, verify that the heatsink remains properly seated and that your system has adequate airflow.
