## How It Plays: A Walkthrough That Won’t Spoil the Surprise (Much)

We’ll cover the fastest way to get started and explain why this package is ideal for a variety of gaming crews—from the “cards on the table” meta-gamers to that one friend who believes they are actually a stand-up comedian in a gamer’s body.

### Setup (Exploding Kittens flavor)
- Shuffle the deck. Remove a few Exploding Kitten cards if you’re with a light crew, or keep them in for maximum tension if your friends thrive on adrenaline.
- Deal a hand to each player. The exact number depends on your house rules, but two to five players is where the magic happens.
- The goal: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten while using your cards to defuse (or otherwise avoid) the blast.

### Turn Flow (Exploding Kittens flavor)
- On your turn, you can play a card to manipulate the board, steal a defensive move, or simply mess with someone else’s plan.
- If you draw the Exploding Kitten, you’re out—unless you have a defuse card that saves you using some wacky combo (a parrot, a clone, a laser pointer—okay, maybe not all of those, but defuse cards feel magical sometimes).
- The last player remaining wins. It’s popcorn-level suspense with a dash of “I can’t believe I pulled this off again.”

### Turn Flow (Throw Throw Burrito flavor)
- Gather players into a circle; it’s harder to actually throw burritos when you’re all standing in a line, so adapt as needed. The main thing: you’ll be drawing cards to score a pair of matching sets while also dodging or throwing burritos.
- When you perform a TTB action, you might trigger a burrito duel or a burrito dodge. The goal is to collect sets and use the dice rules to force interactions that get burritos flung in your direction.
- If you’re hit, you cough, you laugh, and you keep playing because the burritos are more of a “fun prop” than a weapon (soft is the keyword). The winner is the one who survives until a dramatic reveal with minimal collateral damage to furniture and dignity.

The beauty lies in the tempo: Exploding Kittens rewards strategic risk-taking and rare bursts of misdirection, while Throw Throw Burrito thrives on chaotic physical comedy and group participation. The new edition stitches these two experiences into a single, boisterous party experience that invites both tactical play and improvisational chaos. You don’t so much game as you perform a miniature, highly entertaining chaotic skit where you’re the star and your friends are your supporting cast, heckling you while secretly begging for mercy.
