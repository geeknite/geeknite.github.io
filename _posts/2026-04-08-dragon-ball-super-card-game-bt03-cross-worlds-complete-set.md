## What is BT03: Cross Worlds?
BT03: Cross Worlds is the complete set for the third main expansion cycle of the Dragon Ball Super Card Game. It aims to expand the game’s color wheel, introduce cross-world synergy mechanics, and push some familiar archetypes into new directions. Think of it as a cinematic crossover that finally lets you see your favorite Dragon Ball transformers unite on the same battlefield, except your battlefield is a 60-card headspace and your transformers are powerful, stylized battle cards.

This set leans into cross-world interactions, which means you’ll often see cards that reference “Worlds” in their flavor text and keywords, encouraging you to consider not just your own color plan, but also how your cards play with those from other “worlds” on the field. The design goal is to reward players who plan multi-color lines and to reward players who can time their Counter plays (the DBS term for negating opponent’s effects) to swing the momentum between turns.

## Card archetypes and notable mechanics
### World interactions and cross-archetype synergy
Cross Worlds emphasizes “World” synergy: cards from different color families that interact in unique ways when you combine their power. The core concept is to reward players who design multi-world lines that leverage color-to-world interactions, stacking synergy to create big tempo swings and unexpectedly sticky boards. If you’re new to the idea, think of it as a high-stakes team-up where your entire crew can chain effects in a way that feels both thematic and mechanically satisfying.

Within the box, you’ll find a mix of cards that care about a World’s presence on the battlefield, cards that care about your card count (board presence), and cards that escalate in power as your field evolves across turns. Expect some classic DBS motifs—the energy blasts, the fusion-style tempo swings, and the aura-burst finishers—but with a fresh twist that makes you rethink what “color identity” means on the table.

### Key card categories to look for
- Lead/Leader cards that set the tempo for multi-world lines.
- Boss and High-Rarity cards that act as finishers or game-defining plays when you align multiple worlds.
- Event-like or Counter-oriented cards that let you respond to a multi-world threat with precise timing.
- Support cards that smooth out your early game while enabling late-game power spikes.

What this means in practice is you should expect your deck-building to focus less on pure color ramp and more on cross-world scheduling. The best BT03 builds often feel like a well-timed cinematic moment: you stall the early aggression with clever counters, your mid-game swing cards land exactly when your opponent tops their plan, and your final boss card closes out in a splash of neon-colored drama.

### Sample card spotlight (thematic, not actual card names)
- World-touched leaders that convert your color diversity into tempo advantage.
- Multi-world finishers that require precise sequencing across turns.
- Utility cards that keep your resource pool healthy while enabling two-step synergy chains.

Note: specifics will vary by print run and regional distribution. The joy of BT03 is less in a single “must-have card” and more in the interplay of multiple cards that form robust, flexible lines.

## Building with Cross Worlds in mind
### Core principles
- Multi-color efficiency: BT03 rewards players who are comfortable weaving multiple colors into their game plan. If you’re a mono-color purist, you might feel the set nudging you toward experimentation, which is both terrifying and delightful.
- Tempo vs. raw power: Early tempo can be king in this set, but don’t underestimate the late-game power of its cross-world finishers. Find the right balance between speed and payoff.
- Card economy: Don’t overcommit to heavy multi-color plays if you can’t support your resources later. The best BT03 decks manage their energy/resource economy to ensure they hit the big moments when needed.
- Flexibility: Build to accommodate a couple of meta shifts. BT03 is a set that rewards adaptation, not rigidity.

### Sample budget-friendly deck skeleton
- Core two-world tempo line: a reliable lead, a few multi-color midgame swingers, and a late-game finisher that counts on two or three cross-world triggers.
- 2–3 removal or negation cards to manage early pressure.
- 2–3 draw or cycle options to maintain hand advantage while you build your cross-world plan.

This is a broad template; you’ll want to plug in cards you pull from your own box and from your local meta. If you’re new, a good approach is to pick a World that you’re fond of (character design, art, or flavor) and let that World guide your color choices for the rest of the deck.

### How to test and tune
- Playtest with a friend who’s equally curious. The aim is to identify two or three core combos that you can reliably execute under pressure.
- Run a mini-meta analysis: what counters the most common multi-world lines? Are there strong anti-meta picks that push you into a different build?
- Iterate quickly: swap a few cards between games, measure tempo shifts, and don’t fear throwing a few bad matchups to find your sweet spots.

BT03: Cross Worlds is a solid expansion that leans into new mechanics without throwing away what fans already love about the DBS CCG. It provides fresh deck-building angles, compelling cross-world interactions, and enough collectible appeal to satisfy both the collector’s itch and the gamer’s thirst for clever plays. Bottom line: BT03 Cross Worlds is worth a look, especially if you enjoy structured, multi-world lines and the satisfaction of pulling off a well-timed cross-world combo.
