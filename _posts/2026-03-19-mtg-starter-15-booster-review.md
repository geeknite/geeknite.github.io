## Inside the booster packs: what to expect from each pull

Each booster in a typical MTG pack contains 15 cards. The plain expectation is a balance of common, uncommon, and a rare or mythic, along with a basic land. There is usually a chance for a foil card, either as part of a slot or replacing a common, though the foil distribution is not guaranteed and can vary by set and printing. In a 15-pack bundle, this translates to a broad potential pool: better-than-average opportunities for a few noteworthy cards across the 15 packs, plus the everyday bread-and-butter commons that keep a deck construction budget in check.

From a practical standpoint, here is how you should think about the contents when you approach the STARTER factory pack:

- Commons: The backbone of most decks. You will find a large number of these, which is great for building cheap playsets and learning the rope of gameplay. If you are new to MTG, consider these cards a starting toolkit for understanding mechanics like tapping, mana production, and basic combat strategies.
- Uncommons: These are the real flavor of a deck. They often provide better effects, more efficient mana costs, or interesting interactions with other cards. Getting a handful of solid uncommons per booster increases the probability that you will be able to draft or build a functional deck without blowing your budget on premium cards.
- Rares and Mythics: The star of any pack. A rare could be a strong removal spell, a big creature, or a disruptive ability that shifts the board state. A mythic is rarer and generally a bigger swing, though the odds are not guaranteed. In a 15-pack bundle, you should expect to encounter at least a few rares across the collection, and possibly one or two mythics if you are lucky.
- Foils: Foil cards add a shine to your collection. They appear as variants in some packs and can be either a foil common or a foil rare. The foil pull is a nice surprise, but do not rely on it as your main value driver.
- Basic lands: Every MTG draft or casual game needs lands. You will not forget how land works after sorting through a big pile of basic lands; think of them as the digestive system that helps your spells go off without a hitch.

The key takeaway here is that 15 packs provide you with a broad, diversified portfolio of cards. The odds of hitting a powerful rare across 15 packs are not tiny, and the combined value of a few good rares or uncommons can push your enjoyment and potential resale value higher than you might expect from a single booster. The game is, after all, as much about the thrill of the chase as it is about the actual cards you end up playing.

## How to approach the unboxing and deck building

Opening a STARTER pack is where the real learning happens. Here are practical steps to make the most of your 15 booster experience:

1) Set the mood: Clear a table, grab a notepad, and prepare sleeves. You want the unboxing to feel ceremonial, even if you are just trading with a friend.
2) Organize by rarity: As you pull each pack, sort the cards into commons, uncommons, rares, and foils. This helps you see the big picture of what you are building toward.
3) Identify potential deck themes: Look for color pairs, synergy opportunities, and staple removal or ramp cards. Even straightforward strategies like aggro, midrange, or removal-heavy decks can emerge from a pool of 15 boosters.
4) Draft or casual play: Use the pulls as a quick draft pool or a casual two to three player game night. You do not need a perfect meta deck to have fun or to learn the rules of the game better.
5) Trade smartly: Trading is its own art. Be generous, be honest about card power, and aim to fill your missing pieces while leaving your friends with a fair seed of envy.
6) Store with care: Card sleeves keep values intact for a long time. You can also consider top loading rares and protecting foils to preserve that first magic moment for a long time.

## How to store, preserve and trade your cards

Once you have opened the box, the real work begins. Sorting is your friend. We recommend sorting by color, then by rarity within each color, and finally by set if you want to track potential future rotations. Here are a few tips:

- Use proper card sleeves for all rares and foils to prevent edge wear. This keeps your collection looking crisp when trading with others.
- Store in a cool, dry place to reduce the risk of warping and humidity damage.
- Create a simple inventory: a spreadsheet or a small notebook can save you a lot of headaches when you realize you have three versions of a card with slightly different art or a foil that is not as decorative as you expected.
- When trading, be fair and clear about card conditions. If you trade through a local store, ask for a receipt or record of trade in case you need to revisit later on.
