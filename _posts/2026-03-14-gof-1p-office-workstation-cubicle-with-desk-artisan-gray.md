Cable management is where a lot of cubicles either shine or fall apart. In the GOF 1P, the data and power cables are routed through a back channel with enough depth to accommodate common power strips. Here’s the short list of practical notes:

- Monitor arms: If you want to push this into ergonomic heaven, you can add a clamp-on monitor arm without compromising the stability of the desk surface. This is ideal for users who want to optimize eye level and reduce neck strain during long programming sessions or spreadsheet marathons.
- Keyboard tray: An optional keyboard tray can be added for people with deep-seated keyboard habits. It gives you extra desk surface for notepads and reference materials when you’re knee-deep in a data entry sprint.

The bottom line is: if you’re the kind of person who treats cable management as a sport, you’ll enjoy the sense of order GOF tries to deliver.
