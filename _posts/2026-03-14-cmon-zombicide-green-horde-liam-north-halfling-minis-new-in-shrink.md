## Assembly notes: keeping the vibes intact

As with most CMON mini releases, the assembly is straightforward but not entirely forgiving if you try to rush it while binge-watching a headshot montage from a streaming service. Here are a few tips we learned the hard way:

- Gently remove the sprues from their protective frames; work with a cutter and a fine file to avoid nicks in the smallest details around faces and hands.
- If you plan to customize paints right away, wash the minis first to remove any residual mold release and to help the paints adhere better. A quick dish soap bath and a rinse of warm water does wonders.
- Prime in light, even coats to preserve the zen of Liam and North’s expressions. The swampy green hues you will see underneath deserve the chance to pop without you fighting with chunky paint.
- Dry brush technique works beautifully on halfling fur and leather tunics. You want the texture to catch the light in a way that makes them feel alive rather than plastic trophies.

## Painting tips for maximum pop

- Start with a mid-tone swamp green as the base and gradually build up with darker greens in the creases to deepen the environment.
- Use a subtle brown wash on leather gear and belts to evoke weathering from swamp humidity.
- For Liam, emphasize the smile with a lighter skin tone and a quick dry-brush on his hair to create a playful hero vibe.
- North benefits from a higher contrast on the cloak and realistic fur textures on any animal fur accents. A few light highlights will turn an otherwise dull surface into a focal point.
- Finish with a matte seal to reduce shine from the plastic, which helps them blend into the swamp rather than scream plastic at your opponent.
