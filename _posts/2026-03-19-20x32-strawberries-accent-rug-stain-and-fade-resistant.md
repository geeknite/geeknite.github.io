## Cleaning and Care
### Everyday Cleaning
Vacuuming regularly is recommended to prevent dirt from embedding into the fiber. The rug responds well to standard vacuum heads and doesn’t shed aggressively like some lower-pile pieces. For small spills, blot, don’t rub; rubbing tends to push the stain deeper into the fibers. A mild soap solution or a dedicated textile cleaner can be used for spot cleaning. Always test a small inconspicuous area first to verify colorfastness. After cleaning, allow the rug to air dry fully before replacing furniture to avoid water rings or warping.

### Deep Cleaning and Maintenance Schedule
For periodic refreshes, rotate the rug to different spots in the room so wear is evenly distributed. If you’re using a rug pad, choose one with a non-slip bottom and a slightly cushioned top to keep the fibers flat and avoid shifting. Deep clean options include hand-washing if the fiber care label permits, or professional cleaning for more stubborn stains. The key is to avoid harsh bleaching or aggressive scrubbing tools; gentleness is the friend of color longevity here.

### Fade Prevention Tips
Position away from direct, intense sun whenever possible. If that’s not feasible, consider UV-blocking window film or sheer curtains to reduce sun exposure. Rotating the rug seasonally — even just a few inches around the room — helps even out any subtle fading that could arise from a single sunlit zone.
