## What is Codex: Card-Time Strategy – Core Set?

Codex is a card-based strategy game that veers away from the typical pure-mechanics drift of many deck-builders by injecting a time-centric twist into every encounter. You don’t just draw cards and play them for effects; you manipulate time to push, pull, and rearrange outcomes. The Core Set acts as the seed for your chronomancer growth, giving you the first taste of engines that can snowball if you’re not careful, and just as easily crash and burn if you’re too daring with your timing.

In the Core Set, you’ll assemble a small band of time operators, each with distinct strengths and quirks. Your goal isn’t merely to reduce your opponent’s life total to zero or to accumulate victory points; it’s to maximize tempo while keeping your own clock from spiraling out of control. Think of it as a high-stakes chess match where every move oohs and aahs at the counter-move potential, and every mistake is a dramatic two-step backflip into “you should have waited one more turn” land.

To illustrate the vibe quickly: imagine a racing game, but your engine is built from cards, and the track is a series of decision points in an hourglass. Timing becomes a resource; your deck becomes your calendar; and your opponent’s plays are gusts of wind through a sundial. Translation: this is a game about planning, but never so much planning that you forget to enjoy the turns as they come.

## How to Play: Core Mechanics and Turn Structure

### Turn Flow and Tempo Management

A typical match in Codex runs through a series of turns where players alternate performing actions that can change the tempo of the game. The core mechanic is the interplay between fast and slow actions. Fast actions are cheap and can be played the moment you draw them, enabling you to react to your opponent’s tempo. Slow actions require more setup or longer timing windows but provide more potent outcomes. Mastery is about balancing the urge to snowball a combo with the discipline to not overcommit when your clock ticks toward a cliff.

On each turn, you’ll manage a hand of cards and a pool of time tokens. You decide whether to push time forward to trigger multi-card sequences, or to hold back to ensure you have a defensive buffer against a timed counter-ploy from your rival. The risk-reward calculus is never far away, and it’s designed to feel like you’re playing chess on a moving sidewalk while someone else adds more lanes to the highway.

### Card Effects and Time Manipulation

Card effects typically hinge on two things: their raw power and their timing synergy. You’ll see cards that accelerate actions, cards that delay or rearrange events, and cards that generate resources when certain time conditions are met. Some cards reward you for precise timing, rewarding players who can predict the next few moves better than a Fortune 500 CFO can forecast quarterly results. There are also counters and disruption tools that let you interrupt or stall an opponent’s plans, which is where the game’s drama tends to spike: the moment you think you’re ahead on tempo, a well-timed interruption equalizes the playing field, or maybe flips it entirely.

### The Edge Cases and Snags

No system is perfect, and Codex has its share of edge cases. Sometimes timing windows feel a little too punishing for casual players, especially early in the game when you’re still learning the language of time tokens. Other times, you’ll stumble into a turn where a combo looks sweet on paper but requires specific card draws that your deck isn’t loaded to hit consistently. The Core Set acknowledges these edges with a handful of “teach-me” cards that smooth the curve for new players, but it’s not a miracle cure; you’ll still need to practice to make the math become second nature.

### Solo and Team Play Options
