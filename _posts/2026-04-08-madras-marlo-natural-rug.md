## Care and Maintenance
### Routine Cleaning and Stain Management
Spills happen, and coffee is essentially a small beverage eruption. The Madras Marlo Natural Rug handles beverage mishaps with a calm demeanor. Blot, don’t rub. Rubbing invites the curse of frayed threads and a rug that looks like it attempted a DIY haircut. For most spills, a light dab with a clean cloth followed by a gentle soap solution will lift the stain without saturating the fibers. Do not use aggressive cleaners that might strip color or degrade natural fibers. Think of cleaning as a gentle science experiment rather than a demolition derby.

### Deep Cleaning and Longevity
Every so often you may want to give the rug a deeper clean. A light hand wash with a mild detergent can refresh the fibers. Do not submerge the rug in water; natural fibers do not love being drenched in their own existential crisis. After cleaning, lay it flat to dry away from direct sunlight to avoid color fade. If you have a stubborn odor, airing out in a dry, well-ventilated space can help. The goal is to return the rug to its original glory without turning it into a fiber-soggy disaster that forces you to relegate it to the closet of regrets.

### Care Accessories
A soft bristle brush can be used to lift away settled dust and pet fur, making the rug look fresh without sacrificing its natural texture. Vacuuming with a low suction setting prevents fiber breakage. Avoid beater bars and harsh rotating brushes that might tempt fate and leave you with pilling and a saggy look. Remember, a supportive, gentle approach is the best policy for natural fiber rugs.
