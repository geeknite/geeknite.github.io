## Setup and rules at a glance

Setup is minimal. Shuffle both decks, place them face down, and decide on a lead player. Each round features a judge who will choose the best or worst answer, depending on the prompt. The judge then selects the winning response based on the vibes in the room, not the straight up best punchline. That last part is essential: it makes Go Fuc Yourself less about perfection and more about timing, risk, and a pinch of courage.

Key rules you should know:

- Each round, the judge draws a prompt card and reads it aloud
- Players respond with a card from their hand that they think best fits the prompt or makes the judge crack up
- The judge picks the winning card, and the player who played it takes a point
- Some prompt cards require you to pick a response from your own hand or from the opponent’s hand, which adds a layer of mischief
- Wild cards can alter the score or swap roles around the table
- The game ends when a player hits a pre-agreed score, typically around 8 to 12 points depending on the group size and tolerance for awkwardness

The core mechanic is simple, but the real fun lies in the social negotiation and the risk calculus you perform while deciding whether to play safe or go for the knockout punch. It is a game built for the moment and thrives on the energy of the table.

## How to play: step by step

1. Gather players and designate a judge for the first round. A good judge is someone who can stay impartial, but not immune to the hilarity around them.
2. Shuffle both decks and deal each player a hand of five prompt or response cards, depending on your chosen variant.
3. The judge reveals a prompt card. This is the central spark that will drive the round.
4. Each non-judge player selects a card from their hand that best responds to the prompt and hands it to the judge face down.
5. The judge shuffles the submitted cards and reads them aloud with maximum theatricality, choosing a favorite response.
6. The winner gains a point. The judge moves on to the next round, or roles rotate if you are using a rotating judge system.
7. If a wild card is played, follow its special instruction. These can flip scoring, steal points, or force a group challenge that adds a memorable moment to the night.
8. Repeat until someone reaches the target score. Then announce the champion of chaos and declare the victory inevitable.

The rules are accessible, but the joy comes from the unpredictable interactions. People who normally avoid confrontation may find themselves delivering a roast that would make a stand-up comic blush. This is not the game for someone who hates social slippage, but if you want a booster shot of party dynamics, you will want to invite this game to the table.

## Tactics, strategies, and what actually wins rounds

- Know your audience: The best players read room dynamics and adjust their approach. If the judge is easily startled, you might want to aim for a clever twist rather than a raw barb.
- Play the long game: Some prompts reward slow builds and callbacks. A subtle, well-timed callback can outperform a one-liner that relies on shock value.
- Save your best cards for the right moment: Don’t blow your legendary line in the first few rounds. Let others sweat a little as you prepare the perfect ending.
- Mind the tone: Adult games walk a line. The best players push boundaries without crossing into cruelty. If someone looks uncomfortable, pivot. The goal is to amplify laughter, not to break someone emotionally or socially.
- Use wild cards strategically: They can salvage a round or reset the table. If you can trigger a chain of unexpected reactions, you might ride the wave to a victory lap.
