## What is Dixit and Why It Works
Dixit is a party game about storytelling through pictures. Each round, one player becomes the storyteller and selects a clue — a word, phrase, sentence, or even a silly pun — that ties to one card in their hand. The other players secretly pick a card that best matches the storyteller’s clue. All chosen cards are shuffled and revealed; players vote on which card they think is the storyteller’s. If you were perceptive enough to pick the storyteller’s card, you earn points along with others who guessed correctly. If your card wins but you didn’t pick it, you get points while the storyteller earns more for a good hint, or suffers if everyone gets it wrong. The core tension is simple: balance between being too obvious and being too cryptic. The art on Dixit cards nudges players toward creative interpretations, which is the whole point here.

The genius of Dixit lies in turning obscure, dreamlike artwork into a social puzzle. You do not need complex rules to have a blast; you need a group that appreciates the near-poetic silliness of a card that looks like a moonlit teacup riding a bicycle into a forest of floating apples. If you have played Dixit before, you know the gasps and giggles that accompany a perfect misdirection or the moment a card you would never pick in a thousand years somehow wins the round because your storyteller nailed the vibe. If you have not played, prepare for a gentle initiation into a world where metaphor beats mechanics and imagination wins more often than not.

## Gameplay: How the Round Goes Down
Dixit is deceptively simple in concept and surprisingly rich in moments that make you groan with laughter or smile with wonder. Here is a practical walk-through of a typical round in this edition.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal each player a hand of six cards. The user interface here is minimal: you need only your imagination and a willingness to listen to the room.
- Choose a storyteller for the first round. The storyteller looks at their hand and chooses a clue that relates to one of their cards, but not so obvious that everyone will guess it immediately.
- The storyteller places the chosen card face-down on the table and reveals a clue to the group. The clue could be a phrase, a sentence, a pun, a song lyric adapted to your taste, or a quirky observation that only makes sense in a dimly lit dining room.

### The Guessing Phase
- Each other player selects one of their own cards that best matches the storyteller’s clue and places it face-down. The stack of possibilities includes the storyteller card and the other players’ cards.
- The cards are shuffled and laid out face-up for everyone to see. Players then vote on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. The voting happens more smoothly when you have a table of friends who enjoy dramatic pauses and careful eyebrow raises.

### Scoring
- If at least one player and no players are correct, the storyteller scores points for a successful balance, and the guessing players who chose the correct card get points too. If too many players guess the storyteller’s card, the storyteller loses points because the clue was too obvious. If no one guesses correctly, the storyteller may fall into the trap of not communicating clearly, and the table rewards the other players for their clever misdirection.
- The scoring encourages a balance between prompting and misdirection. You want your clue to be intriguing, not as helpful as a GPS beacon. You want your opponents to feel the whisper of a hint, not a loud, obvious siren in their ear. The fun lies in watching how a single image can spark five different interpretations and a dozen jokes.

### End of Round and New Leadership
- After scoring, the winner becomes the storyteller for the next round. The rotation is a simple mechanic that keeps everyone engaged and prevents the game from becoming a one-person monologue about weird art.
- The round ends and you repeat with a fresh hand and a new clue. The art continues to weave its spell, and the room tilts between giggles and thoughtful quiet as people process the clue and the gallery of images before them.
