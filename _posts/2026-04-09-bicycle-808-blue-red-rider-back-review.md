### Practical tips for new owners
- Inspect the seal before opening; a broken seal means you may not receive the guaranteed newness and can complicate resale value if you decide to upgrade to a sealed set for a display.
- Check the tuck boxes for any warping during shipping. Loose corners or creases do not automatically indicate a misprint, but they can affect the stacking stability if you intend to display them.
- Let the decks acclimate to room temperature. If you live in a chilly climate, bringing the decks out a bit before you start playing can help reduce warping and make the edge conformation feel even. A few minutes on a coffee table usually suffices.
- For magic practice, consider keeping a separate practice deck. The 808 blue and red Rider Back are great for routine performance days, but you may wish to use a separate, well-worn deck for gimmicks so your genuine cards stay pristine.
