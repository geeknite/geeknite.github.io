### Maintenance: Cleaning Without Losing Your Mind

Cleaning a beige rug is an art form that has ruined more households’ sense of style than any fashion trend. The Archie’s color helps in disguising small missteps: a splash of soy sauce, a rogue popcorn butter smear, a cat’s post-nap paw print—these all blend in the tonal complexity of the rug’s weave. Quick care steps:
- Vacuum weekly with a standard beater-bar setting; avoid high suction on frayed edges.
- For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers.
- If you’ve got a stubborn stain, test a small, inconspicuous area first, then consider a mild detergent or a color-safe solution.
- For deep cleaning, professional cleaning every 12-18 months is a good idea if you want to maintain the look without turning it into a science project.
- Rotate every few months to maintain even wear and to keep the dog from thinking the rug is a tunnel to the underworld.
