## Card lineup and what they do
- Exploding Kitten: the ultimate elimination threat. Draw one and you're out unless you can Defuse.
- Defuse: the lifebuoy that keeps you in the game after an Exploding Kitten slipstream straight to your heart (or your gut, depending on what you had for lunch).
- See the Future: peek at the top few cards to plan your pivot from “meh” to “boom, I survived.”
- Attack: force the next player to take two turns; chaos ensues.
- Skip: pass your turn without drawing—perfect for bluffing or tucking away for a rainy day.
- Shuffle: mix up the draw pile; the unknown becomes the new suspense.
- See the Future: peek again—yes, we love this for the extra plotting power.
- Nope: the sneaky veto card that can cancel someone else’s bold move. It’s the meme you didn’t know you needed.

Each card type is designed to create micro-tensions that are easy to understand but hard to predict. The pacing comes from the contrast between a few safe plays and a big swing with an Attack or a daring See the Future reveal. And yes, you can easily overthink a card for a story-worthy moment, which is exactly the kind of memory you want from a game night.

## How to play
The core loop is elegantly simple, which is why Exploding Kittens remains so approachable for mixed groups. Here’s a more detailed look to help you host with confidence.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand to each player. The number of starting Defuse cards tends to vary by number of players and luck; the important bit is that you want enough Defuses in the mix to survive a surprise explosion. 
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile in a way that keeps tension high but doesn’t guarantee immediate elimination. The exact distribution is less important than keeping the moment of discovery spicy and unpredictable.
- Keep the remaining deck nearby for quick draws after a turn or two of shenanigans.

### Turn structure
On your turn, you may play any number of action cards from your hand before drawing a card from the deck. Your options include skipping, attacking, shuffling, or peeking at the future. You can also use Nope to cancel someone else’s action, which often results in raucous laughter or strategic panic depending on who’s at the table.

After finishing your actions, you draw a card. If that card is an Exploding Kitten and you can’t Defuse, you’re out. If you can Defuse, the kitten gets discarded and you stay in the game. The pace is brisk, the turns are snappy, and you’ll likely see multiple rounds within a single play session.

### Endgame and strategy
The last player standing wins, but the real reward is the story you take home. The best players balance risk with memory and misdirection. Consider the following strategic beats:
- Save Defuses for high-stakes moments, particularly when the draw pile has started to show a pattern of near-misses.
- Use See the Future to peek the top cards when you’re not certain you can outplay the table, then tailor your next few moves to avoid catastrophic draws.
- Bluffs aren’t just for poker; a well-timed Nope can turn a bold Attack into a harmless waste of a turn—unless someone else decides to escalate.
- Remember that social dynamics matter. A table that laughs together tends to stay together; a table that insults each other tends to end with a tossed card and an awkward silence. Your call, but we recommend the former for the sake of everyone’s tea.
