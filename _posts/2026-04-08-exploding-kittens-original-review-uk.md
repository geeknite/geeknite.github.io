### Card lineup and what they do
- Exploding Kitten cards threaten elimination unless a Defuse is played.
- Defuse cards save you from an explosion and let you continue playing.
- See the Future lets you peek at upcoming cards to plan your next move.
- Attack, Skip, and Shuffle add the chaos and tactical misdirection that keeps players on their toes.
- Nope is the sneaky cancel card that can turn a bold bluff into a waste of a turn.

## How to play
### Setup
Shuffle the deck, deal a starting hand to each player, and place the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile in a balance that keeps tension high but not absurd. Each player will want a couple of Defuse cards if possible, but this will depend on your luck and the number of players. Keep the rest of the deck close by for a quick draw after a round of card play. 

### Turn structure
On your turn you can play any number of action cards from your hand before drawing a card from the deck. You may use action cards to skip your turn, attack the next player, shuffle the deck, or see the future. You can also use Nope to cancel someone else action. After finishing your actions you draw a card. If that card is an Exploding Kitten and you cannot Defuse it, you are out of the game. If you Defuse, the Exploding Kitten is discarded and you continue playing. The game flows quickly, so you are likely to see multiple rounds in a single session.

### Endgame and strategy
The last player standing wins. The pace is brisk and the tiny decisions can swing the result. You must balance risk, bluff, and memory. Do you save a Defuse for a rainy day, or gamble that you can outlast the others with See the Future and Attack? The answer depends on your mood, but the best players mix bluff with a careful defense.
