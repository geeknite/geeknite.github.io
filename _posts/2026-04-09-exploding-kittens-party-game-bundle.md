## How to Play: Quickstart and Variants
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you know the core loop: draw a card, decide whether to play a card to influence the outcome, and survive long enough to avoid exploding. The Defuse mechanic provides a safety net, the single most delightful insurance policy in a card game since “I’m not mad, just disappointed” was invented in family households everywhere.

The Party Game Bundle adds a handful of quick-start rules and optional twists that you can fold into your first game. The goal is to keep it breezy while amplifying interaction—without forcing everyone to take notes on a whiteboard. Here are the highlights you’ll likely encounter:

### Quick setup
1) Shuffle the Exploding Kittens deck and deal a set number of cards to each player (usually around 7 to 8, depending on the exact bundle). 2) Each player receives a Defuse card if the bundle includes more defensive options. 3) Place the Exploding Kitten cards back into the deck according to the rules of the mode you’re playing.

This sounds simple, but the party variants may introduce additional steps, such as “shuffle-in” cards that add new effects mid-game or alternate events that accelerate the pace. The goal is to create a rhythm where one big turn can swing the table, either by defusing a kitten with comedic timing or by using a new mechanic to force someone to draw twice before they’re eliminated. It’s a delicate balance, and the Bundle leans into it with flair.

### Turn flow (baseline)
- On your turn, you may play as many cards as the rules allow (the party variants often encourage chaining effects). 
- When you’re done, you draw one card. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse, you’re out. 
- If you have a Defuse, you can save yourself by playing the Defuse card immediately to neutralize the kitten (and you’ll probably hear a chorus of “nice defuse” from your friends, or the opposite—depending on the roll of the dice and the mood of the room).

The party variants may modify this by introducing alternate win conditions (e.g., first player to collect a certain number of successes or to trigger a chain reaction by discarding specific cards). The core remains approachable: you want to avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten while inflicting maximum chaos on opponents.

### Strategy and common pitfalls
- Don’t hoard Defuse cards like a dragon with a stash of gold. In a party game, the meme is strong, but you’ll pay the price if you get too greedy and end up with a hand full of counters when someone else sets off a chain.
- Timing is everything. The fun of Exploding Kittens comes from choosing the right moment to deploy a blocking or stealing card. In the bundle’s party variants, this is amplified by new interactions that incentivize bold moves when the table’s at its rowdiest.
- Read the room. If your group thrives on banter, lean into the humor and let the chaos unfold. If you’re playing with younger players or a family audience, you might want to switch to a more forgiving mode that emphasizes laughter over backstabbing.
