The GOF 1P ships with a straightforward instruction set that emphasizes modular assembly. If you’ve assembled a bed frame or a bookshelf before, this should feel familiar. The hardware is logically organized, with labeled screws and a helpful diagram. The instructions avoid the kind of cryptic arrows that look like a treasure map leading to an Allen key. In practice, you’ll likely complete the setup in under two hours with one person, plus one extra pair of hands if you want moral support. The biggest challenge is aligning panels during the initial dry-fit stage; once you lock in the frame, the rest is a surprisingly smooth ride.

### Tips for a Smooth Build
- Lay out all hardware before you start; it saves you from playing a life-quiz titled Where Did That Screw Go? 
- Have a cordless drill at the ready for the final tightening to avoid aching wrists; just don’t over-torque—this is furniture, not a spaceship engine.
- If you’re pairing with the desk, keep the weight distribution in mind; the weight of the top surface plus a monitor can make the unit a little front-heavy during assembly. A teammate can help align the desk edge with the cubicle frame.

### Initial Setup: Practical First-Run Thoughts
Once assembled, give the surface a light cleaning to remove any dust from transit. Then place your primary devices—your computer, keyboard, mouse, and a coffee mug that doesn’t get along well with humidity. If you’re using a dual-monitor setup, a monitor arm is recommended to optimize space and reduce neck strain. A simple test run of your typical workload will reveal whether the cubicle is living up to its promises or if you need to tweak the height or desk angle. The goal is a posture-friendly station with minimal cable chaos. If you can achieve that, congratulations: you’ve achieved efficiency chic.
