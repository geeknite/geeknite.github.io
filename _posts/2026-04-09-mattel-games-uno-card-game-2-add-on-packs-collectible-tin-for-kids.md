## How UNO works in this edition
UNO works by matching the color or number of the top card on the discard pile. Special action cards like Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four let you break patterns, force turn reversals, or send extra cards to opponents. In this edition, the two add-on packs introduce new variants of these mechanics. They are not earth-shattering deviations from classic UNO rules, but they do create moments that feel fresh without making the game inaccessible.

### A quick refresher on the house rules
- You start with seven cards each.
- Match either color or number to play a card.
- Special cards alter the flow of turns and the cards in players’ hands.
- If you forget to shout UNO when you are down to one card, you risk a penalty, depending on your house rules.
- The winner is the first player to run out of cards.

This edition respects those fundamentals while layering in new dynamics via the add-ons. The result is a game that can be learned in about five minutes and then explored for hours as players discover new combos and strategies.
