## What is City of Mist, and what’s a Starter Set for?
City of Mist sits at the crossroads of superhero fantasy and urban folklore. Instead of capes and laser eyes, you’re a modern-day investigator who uncovers that the city is a stage for legends. Powers are built from Traits, encoded as Tags, and your character’s identity is more a moodboard than a single, fixed identity. The Starter Set distills the core system into a friendly package so you can run a few sessions, meet the city’s strange inhabitants, and decide if you want to dive deeper into the full rulebook and the sprawling mythos. If you’ve ever wished Batman could be a living meme and your own everyday life could be reframed as a mythic journey, City of Mist is your jam.

In this review, we’ll cover component quality, what’s actually playable out of the box, the vibes and tone the kit pushes, how the rules feel in practice, and whether this is a good starting point for new players, or maybe a better gift for someone who already has a penchant for noir and mythic pulp. We’ll sprinkle in some humor because the city deserves some levity when its shadows keep telling you your fate is a prophecy about coffee fumes. ## Inside the Starter Set: what you actually get
What makes a starter set valuable to a GM and players who are more “curious bystander” than “seasoned dungeon crawler” is it’s all there in one place: a concise rules skim, a ready-to-run adventure, pre-gen characters, and components that minimize setup friction. Here’s what you’ll typically find, though pack-ins can shift with print runs:

- A Quickstart Rules Pamphlet: A compact version of the core mechanic, focusing on Tags, Traits, and the dice-pool resolution. It’s the sort of booklet that begs you to read a paragraph, then roll a die and decide your fate based on the third line of a poem on page two.
- A Short Adventure or Two: A self-contained scenario in which two investigators discover that a city’s convenience store is a portal to a legend, or that a local celebrity is a living mythic mask. The set gives you a ready-to-run arc with hooks you can riff on if the first session goes long or short.
- Pre-generated Characters: Think of these as the templates that unlock a city’s secrets without requiring a laundry list of character creation choices. They’re designed to spark ideas about identity, power, and the way a person’s personal myth spills into the world they’re in.
- A GM Screen: A handy divider that blocks the scene from view while also offering quick prompts and reminders for the GM. The GM screen is a compact fortress of “keep the mystery intact” without forcing you to consult a dusty tome every five minutes.
- Tokens, Maps, and Play Aids: Small tactile pieces that make the mythic and the mundane collide. Tokens for Fate, Clues, and Resources; a compact map you can lay out on a table; even dice that remind you the city runs on chaos and whimsy as much as logic.
- A few printed handouts: Clue sheets, character bios, and some little lore snippets that do two things: (1) seed session ideas, and (2) give players something to read and obsess over after you stop playing for the night.

The aesthetic is brutalist-neon: bold fonts, punchy color palettes, and art that looks like someone stapled a mythic dream to a subway poster. It’s not the “polished fantasy RPG coffee table book” vibe; this is a city you’d pass on the street and find a new legend in every storefront window. The Starter Set aims to capture that mood in a compact, budget-friendly package, so you don’t need to invest in a full core book before you know you’ll come back to the city again and again.

## Setup and first play: what to expect
If you’re approaching City of Mist with a stubborn idea of “how a tabletop RPG should feel,” you’ll be pleasantly surprised by how fast you can get to the core of a session. The rules emphasize collaborative storytelling over granular number-crunching. You’ll be juggling Tags (your powers and life themes), Conditions (narrative states like “exhausted” or “on the edge”), and the city as a living, breathing character. The Starter Set trims away a lot of the “what-ifs” and leaves you with the essential: a mood, a mystery, and a handful of tools to reveal the myth hidden in plain sight.

The first play session usually has three phases:
- Character orientation: Players pick or read through their pre-gen characters and discuss what their personal myth is. What is their hidden identity? How do they become a conduit for a legend? The answers aren’t strictly mechanical; you’re setting tone and motive.
- Investigation and discovery: The GM presents clues, the players pursue leads, and the city’s contradictions reveal themselves. The mechanics encourage you to think in terms of “how does this fit the legend?” rather than “what’s the optimal dice result?”
- The reveal: The final beat of the session pushes the party toward a choice—grow, adapt, or retreat into a new myth. This is where the neon glow of City of Mist lands with maximum impact. It’s not just a solved case; it’s a narrative turning point that reframes who your investigators think they are.

If you’ve played more traditional systems where combat is the primary driver, City of Mist might feel a little… lyrical at first. Don’t panic. The Starter Set is designed to show you that the real power is in story agency—the moment when you realize your character’s identity gives you a unique leverage in the scene, and the GM’s job is to present a city that pushes that identity into action. The pacing can feel deliberate, but it’s deliberate in a way that rewards players for leaning into the themes you’ve chosen together.

For players who crave crunch, there is some, but the emphasis is on vibe and narrative control. The dice mechanic is approachable: you roll based on your Tags, accumulate successes, and use those successes to shape outcomes. It’s not about “winning a battle” so much as “how this myth manifests in the moment.” That’s a refreshing shift if you’ve spent months arguing with a d20 about whether your hero’s truth can bend the physics of a building.

## Mechanics that sing when you lean into them
City of Mist uses a tag-based system where your character’s identities (your “myth”) are expressed as Tags—words or phrases that describe abilities, weaknesses, and thematic hooks. In a given scene, you use a Tag to justify an action, roll dice, and interpret the result narratively. The system prizes what you don’t roll as much as what you do: a failure isn’t a dead end; it’s a pivot point that reframes your myth in an unexpected way.

For newcomers, the Starter Set does a solid job of teaching this rhythm. You don’t need to memorize five hundred edges and synergies; you need to understand the idea that your identity shapes what you can do—and how the city will react to that identity. The pre-gen characters kick off the rhythm by giving you a myth immediately to play with. The GM is encouraged to weave the city’s legends into the scene, so the players feel like they’re solving more than a case; they’re unsealing a story about who they are beneath the mask.

Thematic play is the star here. If you enjoy that moment when your detective stumbles into a truth that connects a personal pain to a public crime, City of Mist is designed for it. The Starter Set keeps the moment accessible without forcing you to adopt a specific moral stance. It nudges you toward a collaborative, mythic storytelling approach rather than a zero-sum battle where you simply win by rolling higher than the other guy.

## Narrative flavor versus mechanical depth: who should buy this
- New players who crave a lighter, more cinematic experience will find City of Mist a gentle but persistent gateway to RPGs. You’re not drowning in pages of rules; you’re building a city around your legend, and the Starter Set gestures you toward deeper rules as you grow more comfortable.
- GMs who want a quick-start without constant prep will appreciate the boxed adventure and quick-reference materials. It’s not a full campaign, but it’s enough to run several nights with room to improvise.
- Veteran players who enjoy theme-driven games may be drawn more to the mood and the high concept of “identity shapes the world” than to the exact mechanical depth. They’ll likely want to upgrade to the full core rulebook for more Tags, more legends, and more ways to bend the city to their will.

If you’re in any doubt, try the Starter Set with a friend or two and see if you click with the city’s rhythm. The social contract matters more here than the rules rigidity, and the box gives you a shared jumping-off point to explore that contract together.

## A few tips for GMs and players starting out
- Lean into the myths: Encourage players to describe how their identity feels in the scene. A well-phrased myth can be a powerful narrative engine.
- Use the city as a character: The city should push back. It’s not just a backdrop; it’s an active participant in each scene, often throwing up a clue or obstacle that reveals a legend’s edge.
- Start small, dream big: The Starter Set is designed for a couple of sessions. Don’t try to fit a six-episode arc into your first night. Let the legend expand as you go and let the players grow into their roles.
- Keep it collaborative: If a player wants a new myth to fit a scene, let them propose it. The system is built around the idea that everyone’s story matters and that a myth can evolve with the investigation.
- Embrace failure as story fuel: A failed roll isn’t a catastrophe; it’s a prompt to twist the scene in a fresh direction. You’ll be surprised how often the best moments come from “bad” dice results interpreted by a sharp GM.

## City of Mist versus the full core rulebook: do you need more?
Yes, if you want deeper myth-building options, more robust mechanics, and a larger catalog of legendary templates, the core book is calling your name. The Starter Set is a baptism by neon: it gives you a taste of the mood, the mechanics, and the storytelling approach so you can decide if you want to dive deeper into the world’s expansive mythos. The core book doesn’t just expand options; it expands possibilities. You’ll be able to explore new legends, more complex moral dilemmas, and a broader range of investigative operations. It’s a natural escalation path for groups who find the Starter Set’s rhythm addictive.

That said, if you’re not sure you want to commit to a longer campaign or you primarily enjoy one-off sessions with friends who like to riff on mythic ideas, the Starter Set stands strong as a complete, satisfying experience. The vibe is the thing here—what you get out of your sessions is heavily narrative, not just mechanical glory. And that vibe scales with you as you move toward the full game.
