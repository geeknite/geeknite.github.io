## What is City of Mist: Character Folio?

City of Mist: Character Folio is a supplement that provides a dedicated, ready-to-use character sheet and supporting pages tailored to the City of Mist system. It is not merely a printer-friendly PDF; it is a designed instrument meant to help players articulate the dual identities that define every character in the game: the Mythos and the Logos, the masks behind the myth. The folio spaces out attributes, powers, and relationships with a noir-like elegance that wants you to doodle, annotate, and potentially spill coffee on it without crumbling into a hysterical meltdown.

The folio leans into the game’s core idea: your character is a living legend in a city full of myths, and this sheet captures that tension between epic identity and mundane life. The cover art announces the mood loudly and proudly in a way that feels midnighy cinema rather than a dull accounting sheet. The typography choices, the line weights, and the color palette all work in sync to create a sense that you are about to chronicle a story that matters.

## Design and Layout: form meeting function

The folio excels in two broad ways: readability and atmosphere. Readability means the sheets are laid out in a way that prevents a character from turning into a jumbled mess during a session. The sections are clearly labeled, with generous room for notes, prompts, and quick sketches. The atmosphere comes from the little flourishes that echo the game world: stylized icons that hint at Mythos and Logos, subtle gridlines reminiscent of blueprints, and a color system that guides you to what belongs to the city and what belongs to the hero hiding behind it.

Here is where the Jekyll image syntax shines in practice, because the folio uses visual cues to guide the eye without forcing you to squint at a wall of text. The folio is not an artbook, nor is it a sterile spreadsheet; it is somewhere happily in between, which means players actually want to fill it out, not just pretend to be careful with a tablet that never leaves the table.

## Contents at a glance: what you actually get

The core folio consists of a structured sheet for character creation and a handful of add-ons that help codify the player’s choices into the game’s signature structure. In practical terms, you’ll find:

- A streamlined character sheet that captures: name, backstory prompt, origin, and a quick sheet for the dual identities. It includes a dedicated space for unforgettable taglines that rise from the page like a neon caption.
- Mythos and Logos sections, with prompts that nudge players to articulate the magical or mythic aspect of their powers and the rational, human alternatives they present.
- A relationships block to formalize connections with allies, patrons, nemeses, and the city itself. The design encourages thinking about the social network of the character as much as the combat options.
- A compact item and equipment grid that emphasizes the practical toolkit your character uses to navigate the city’s fog.
- A quicknotes page for session-to-session reminders, plot hooks, or a future upgrade path that the GM can drop into play to keep the momentum going.
- Optional prompts and doodle space that invites players to storyboard scenes, a feature that will feel familiar to any player who loves cinematic sequencing.

The contents are presented in a modular fashion, which means you can pull out the parts you need for a one shot, or you can fold the folio into a longer campaign where Mythos slowly eats away at the character's everyday life. The design philosophy here is not to constraint the player but to invite a more expressive character creation process that aligns with City of Mist overall themes.

## Character creation experience: not a chore, a ritual

If you know City of Mist, you know that character creation is a ritual as much as a mechanical step. The Weight of a Legend sits on your shoulders before you roll a die. The folio embraces this with prompts that are both practical and poetic. Instead of forcing you through pages of numbers, the sheet nudges you toward identity first, then the details fall into place.

In practice, you’ll start with the character’s concept and tagline, which sparks the Mythos and Logos interplay. The lay-out of the folio guides this process: you fill in a few lines that describe your character’s mask, the life they hide behind it, and the moment that made them a legend in the first place. The design makes it easy to go back and tweak the backstory as sessions unfold, a boon for players who discover their character’s personality evolves faster than their power list.

For new players, the folio serves as a gentle onboarding tool. The prompts are accessible enough to avoid a fatigue spiral and specific enough to prevent the dreaded blank-page anxiety. For veteran players, the folio acts as a creative accelerator: you can capture a compelling moment in a neat corner of the sheet and still leave ample space for future expansions. The balance is key here, and the folio nails it by keeping the art direction strong while not sacrificing practical space.

## Mythos and Logos: a design philosophy in a sheet

City of Mist is built on the interplay between Mythos (the magical, mythic, and larger-than-life aspects) and Logos (the grounded, human, and rational aspects). The Character Folio frames this interplay with distinct sections that visually differentiate the two halves. The Mythos side invites grand adjectives and bold lines; the Logos side encourages concrete details about street-level life, relationships, and responsibilities.

The reader-friendly prompts encourage players to articulate how their mythic identity colors their daily decisions, which is essential for a system that thrives on the tension between epic potential and mundane reality. When you fill this out at the table, you will feel the game’s energy—where a hero might be chasing a villain but is also trying to avoid an overdue rent notice. It’s the charming contradiction that City of Mist wants you to explore, and the folio helps you do just that without forcing you down a single narrative path.

## Accessibility, accessibility, accessibility: who is this for?

This folio is accessible to newcomers and seasoned players alike, though the emphasis leans toward groups that enjoy narrative flexibility. The prompts work well for players who are less comfortable with long sheets or heavy numeric systems. You won’t find stacks of percentile tables here; instead you’ll find guiding questions and a structure that respects the player’s storytelling pace.

For GMs, the folio offers a quick-reference framework that helps you keep track of who knows what and when the city becomes aware of a rising Mythos in a given arc. The layout translates well to both print and screen use, so you can hand out the sheet to players and still run the session with your preferred GM screen or app. The only caveat is that the folio assumes a certain level of immersion in the City of Mist vibe; if you’re a hyper-technical group that loves precise mechanics above mood, you might need to supplement with a few quick sheets of your own.

## In play: how it holds up in a session

During actual play, the folio shines when used as a dynamic prompt rather than a fixed inventory. The prompts encourage a player to describe a scene, a location, or a piece of evidence that shifts the narrative. The GM can drop in windowed prompts to steer a scene toward a Mythos reveal or a Logos conflict, and the sheet serves as a memory palace for those threads.

A neat feature for groups who like to keep a session fast-paced is the ability to use the folio as a one-page recap. You can scan Mythos tags and Logos prompts and quickly reestablish a character’s identity in a new scene after a dramatic turn. It’s not a cheat, it’s a transferable memory aid that saves time and increases immersion.

## How this compares to the core book and other supplements

The core City of Mist book lays the foundation: a world, rules, and a toolkit for mythic noir storytelling. The Character Folio does not redefine rules; it refines how players interact with them. It is best viewed as a companion rather than a replacement for the core experience. Compared to generic character sheets from other systems, the folio’s design language and prompt-driven approach are tailor-made to the game’s unique philosophy. It captures the game’s mood without sacrificing the ability to fill out the practical details you need for play.

For players who have already embraced the Mythos and Logos framework, the folio serves as a recognizably efficient accelerant that respects the property’s core ethos. For newcomers, it provides a clear pathway into character creation that avoids the trap of getting stuck in the weeds too early, which can be a barrier in some other systems.
