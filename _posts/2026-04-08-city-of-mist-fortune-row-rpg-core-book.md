## Overview
City of Mist has a long tradition of turning urban fantasy into a neon noir mystery that feels like a fever dream you pay for with coffee and bad life choices. Fortune Row, the latest core book, doubles down on that premise by taking the city s favorite conspiracy of mythic cranes and rain soaked alleyways and giving them a bigger playground, more options for character Masks, and a set of investigations that look suspiciously like they were written after someone binged a season of true crime podcasts while balancing a budget of pizza slices and responsibility. If you liked the first two City of Mist books, Fortune Row is the one that slides into your brain with a velvet gloved hand and asks you to question whether your Mask is wearing you, or if you re wearing the Mask and the city is wearing you back like a raincoat that never dries.

Fortune Row arrives as a hardcover that sighs with the weight of a long commute and the glint of a new ability you didn t realize you desperately needed. It is both a setting book and a toolbox—one that tells you where the city s new myths like to gather and how the new Fate mechanics want to nudge you toward bold, melodramatic choices. The tone remains whimsical yet sharp, like a weekday coffee that s two shots too strong and a detective s notebook that s seen more rain than a river. In essence, Fortune Row asks: what happens when you mix a noir city full of disguises with myths that aren t shy about their own legends, and you give players the keys to pretend to be those myths?

In terms of the product experience, Fortune Row feels cohesive in how it builds on City of Mist core ideas while tossing in a handful of fresh design choices. The layout prioritizes playability (the book has plenty of sidebars, quick start tips for new GMs, and a robust set of quick transformations for Masks), but it also invites long form play by supplying multi part investigations that can be stitched into a campaign or run as one off sessions during a rainy weekend when your group s plans keep getting delayed by life s monsters chief among them, real life itself.

### What Fortune Row Adds to City of Mist
Fortune Row isn t just a setting dump; it is a re education in how to tell a story where identities, power, and myth intersect. The book s core additions include a revised approach to Masks and Abilities, expanded Mythos options, new locations and factions specific to Fortune Row, and a suite of adventures that feel designed to be both cinematic and playable, not just cinematic. It treats the city not as a backdrop but as a character with opinions, with a voice that murmurs through rain slick streets, neon reflections, and the occasional street magician who might actually be a deity in disguise.

One of the most compelling things Fortune Row does is give players more agency over how their Masks interact with the city. Masks in City of Mist are your character s themes—your identity as a hero, antihero, or gourmand of mystery—but Fortune Row expands how those Masks can be expressed in a live game. It introduces new Mask archetypes, each with a short cheat sheet of suggested Abilities, Skills, and Glimpses (short, cinematic prompts you can drop into scenes to reveal something about the Mask s true form). The writing emphasizes narrative alignment: choose a Mask that compels you to act in ways that reveal your true self, while still giving you enough flexibility to improvise on the fly when a scene goes off script.

A key design improvement is the tightened relationship between Masks and the game s Fate system. Fate in Fortune Row can be spent to trigger cinematic moments—think strip away reveals, last second saves, or a whispered truth that changes the stakes. The book clarifies how Fate interacts with the GM s control of the scene, which helps keep the table s momentum from stalling during tense narratives. The upshot: players feel empowered to drive the story without roping in their GM to fix the moment; GMs can escalate a scene with clean, mechanical grace instead of waving a hand and hoping for the best.

The setting of Fortune Row itself deserves praise. The city s fictional Fortune Row feels like a place you could actually wander: a maze of back alley parlors, secret dens beneath a billboard lit arcade, a floating tea house on a canal that glows with residual Mythos. It s the kind of place that makes you want to book your next session around a map rather than a numeral. The design team s guidance on how to convey Fortune Row s atmosphere, via descriptive prompts, NPC sketches, and a few evocative sample scenes, helps the GM create a living, breathing district rather than a static backdrop.

The art direction is consistent with City of Mist s aesthetic: a blend of vintage pulp and modern cyber noir, with bold linework and color palettes that evoke rain soaked streets and smoky jazz clubs. The visuals aren t just pretty; they function as storytelling cues. A well placed illustration can spark a whole investigation, a character moment, or a misdirection that keeps players on their toes. It s the kind of book that rewards the GM and players who enjoy letting the setting breathe while they chase clues.

### Mythos as Character: New Lenses on the City s Powers
Fortune Row s Mythos is where the city s legends live and breathe. The new options expand beyond the old pantheon and into more intimate, city specific mythicities: whispering neon, rain shadows that resemble faces, and a few gods who appear whenever the hod of a subway tunnel rustles just right. The book introduces Mythos Lenses, a mechanic that lets players and GMs tailor the mythic influence to their campaign s tone. Want a grungier, street smart flavor with a sprinkle of folklore? Or a glossy, film noir feel where sources of power look like corporate logos with teeth? The Mythos Lenses give you a vocabulary to rapidly shape the city s myths to emotional beats, not just mechanical ones.

### Investigations and Campaign Flow
The investigations in Fortune Row are designed to be modular. The book provides a series of linked investigations that can run as a contained arc or be stretched into a longer campaign with interwoven consequences. Each investigation includes a cast of suspicious characters, a handful of red herrings, and at least two non obvious twists that feel earned rather than tacked on for drama. The GM s section offers scene framing tips, pacing advice, and a set of GM moves that are easy to remember and fun to execute. If you ve ever run a game that started strong but drifted into a lull, Fortune Row provides a structured approach to keep the tension taut without requiring you to memorize 700 pages of plot beats.

### Combat and Pace
City of Mist isn t primarily a combat driven system, and Fortune Row respects that ethos. There are tactical options for players who want to bring a little physicality to the table, but the core of the game remains social, investigative, and myth driven. Fortune Row tightens the pace by offering compact combat rules that avoid bogging down the session in dice churning. The book uses a simple, intuitive framework for conflict resolution, where outcomes are tied to the story s stakes. It s the kind of system that rewards clever positioning and clever narration: you don t need to roll a dozen dice to feel like you ve accomplished something dramatically meaningful.

## Writing, Layout, and Accessibility
Fortune Row reads smoothly. The writing is confident, occasionally witty, and always mindful of how to keep a GM from drowning in rules. The layout prioritizes readability, with callouts that highlight key concepts, quick start tips for first time GMs, and enough flavor text to inspire a narrative moment without slowing play. The typography and spacing make it easy to scan for a quick refresher between scenes. There s a nice balance between generous examples and compact rules references, which means you can flip to a page and find what you need without pulling your hair out or flipping to a separate notebook.

Accessibility is treated with care. The design team also provides a short, practical guide on running Fortune Row for a group that s new to City of Mist. It won t turn a complete novice into a veteran GM in one sitting, but it will give them a clear path forward with a sense of confidence rather than dread.

Artwork and production quality are on par with City of Mist s previous releases. The color palette, illustration style, and map design all reinforce the setting s mood and help players visualize the city s world hopping, myth surfing vibe. The map sheets are sturdy and clear, with a level of detail that s helpful for navigation during sessions.
