## Overview
City of Mist is a roleplaying game that sounds like a comic book came to life and learned how to walk upright, drink coffee, and argue with a neon-lit alley about existentialism. It sits at the intersection between superhero fantasy and urban noir, where heroes hide behind ordinary lives and mythic power lives in the margins of a city that never sleeps, or really sleeps only when it can pretend to. The Core Book is less about tallying sheets and more about tall buildings, taller ideas, and the kind of storytelling that makes players grin and GMs nod wisely while muttering, That is absolutely something a noir detective would say in a rainstorm. The book wears its love for comics and pulp fiction on its sleeve, with lush art, bold typography, and layouts that scream, Let’s tell a story with style and substance, not just stat blocks.

The essential premise is simple and deliciously chaotic: you are a human with a mythic kernel—your Logos (your human side) and your Mythos (your supernatural or hyper-human side). You exist in a modern city, you fight crime and corruption, and you do so with the help (and hindrance) of metaphorical tags that describe who you are and what you can do. The tags are the engine: a good roll in City of Mist is less about raw numbers and more about weaving the right tag into the right moment. Want to punch through a wall of bureaucracy with a Mythos tag about ancient trickery and a Logos tag of stubborn pragmatism? The Core Book wants you to try, to see if the city will bend back or break you in a narrative snap.

The writing is as playful as a caped narrator who forgot to take off his trench coat before delivering a monologue. The book treats serious feeling and complicated themes with affection, humor, and an understanding that tabletop roleplaying is a shared story, not a lecture. If you crave crunch, City of Mist gently hands you a framework and says, Here is how we narrate the extraordinary inside the ordinary. If you want a game that rewards clever wording and creative problem solving over strictly optimized builds, you will feel right at home. The Core Book doubles as both a rules primer and a love letter to myth, so you get both design clarity and thematic depth.

There is a lot to love here: the art direction that leans into noir shadows and neon glow, the way chapters are organized to take you from concept to scene, and the way the system invites improvisation without spiraling into chaos. It is a book that wants to be read aloud, shared among friends, and then taped to a whiteboard so you can remix your city in real time.

## Thematic core: mythos, logos, and why your city matters
City of Mist builds around three core ideas: mythos, logos, and the city as a character. Mythos is the supernatural seed of your hero; it is the reason you matter beyond your day job and the thing that makes you a legend in the shadows. Logos is the practical, personal, sometimes messy human side that tries to keep you grounded when your mythic impulses threaten to pull you into a staircase that leads to the roof of your own legend. The city—the streets, the subway, the flooded parking garage—acts as a living entity that shapes and reacts to both myth and mundane life. The Core Book treats these three elements as intertwined strands of a braided rope: you pull one, and the others respond. This is not a system that pretends characters exist in a vacuum; it is a toolkit for narrative chemistry, where your choices literally transform the environment around you.

What this means for players is twofold. First, character creation is a chance to sculpt a figure who is both myth and person. You assemble a set of Mythos and Logos tags that describe not just what your hero can do, but what your hero is trying to prove about themselves. Second, and perhaps more importantly, the tags are not just cosmetic. They are the levers you pull during scenes. You want to narrate a moment where your Mythos tag compels the city to bend toward your will, but you also want a Logos tag that reminds you that your hero still fears the coffee-stains of their ordinary life. The Core Book leans into this tension with a respect for storytelling that makes you feel like you are writing a chapter of a living comic, not filling out a form for a game shop brochure.

## Mechanics: tags, dice, and the art of saying yes to a clever idea
The mechanics in City of Mist are built around a tag-based resolution system. The game asks you to lean into the adjectives and nouns that define your hero at their best and their most human. In practice, this means you’ll be rolling a pool of dice (the exact number depends on how many relevant tags you choose to apply to a given action). The aim is simple but can feel deliciously cinematic: succeed by using the right combination of Mythos and Logos tags in the moment, and you unlock a narrative beat that would be less flavorful or impactful if you played it straight. The advantage of this approach is flexibility. The rules encourage you to improvise, to propose a clever riff, and to justify the action with story justification. If you can present a tag that makes sense in the scene, you likely get your roll to work—often with the added spice of complications that push the story in unexpected directions.

### Tag system and narrative leverage
The tag system is the heart of the game’s cohesion. Tags are not just descriptors; they are narrative levers that can turn a failed roll into a scene with consequences worth playing through. For example, a tag like City’s Grime or Neon Alley can be invoked to justify stealth, misdirection, or a sudden ally’s arrival. The GM, or MC, uses the tags to frame a response that makes the next moment more dramatic. This cooperative storytelling rhythm makes the game feel less like a sprint through a stat block and more like a collaborative caper where everyone has a stake in the outcome.

Tags come in flavors: your personal traits, your powers, the city itself, and even the moment’s mood. The Core Book includes prompts and lists to spark ideas, which is a relief for new players who worry about, say, finding the right hook for a scene. Instead of forcing everyone to conjure the perfect ability on the first try, you can gravitate toward a tag that captures the vibe of the moment. The result is your hero becoming a walking x-ray of themes, with your choices resonating across the scene and the world around you.

### Character creation and progression
Character creation in City of Mist invites a mix of planning and improvisation. You begin by choosing a Mythos and a Logos, which establishes the core tension and the kinds of scenes you will shine in. Then you select tags—your powers, your limitations, your personal quirks, and the myths you carry. The book provides structured prompts to help you craft a hero who feels both epic and grounded. The progression is more about narrative movement than grinding for XP, rewarding players with story beats, revelations, and new tag economies that unlock different kinds of scene control. This design choice aligns well with a group that enjoys roleplay and the satisfaction of a clever plan coming together in a way that feels earned and memorable.

The benefit of this approach is accessibility. You don’t need to memorize a long spellbook or memorize dozens of numeric modifiers. You need to understand how your tags interplay with the scene, what you want your myth to reveal about you, and how to leverage the city’s mood to your advantage. The Core Book does a good job of guiding new players through this, offering examples of archetypal Tag Sets that illustrate how to mix the mythic with the mundane in ways that spark interesting scenes rather than just verbose combat descriptions.

## World-building and aesthetics: art, layout, and the mood you are buying
The Core Book’s art direction is a love letter to pulpy noir and neon-lit street corners. The pages carry a color palette that reads like a city at night: purples, pinks, and electric teals glow against dark shadows. The layouts are generous and readable, with clear section headings and sidebars that feel like posters plastered on a brick wall. The typography is punchy without being shouty, which suits a game about bold statements and subtle undercurrents. The art and layout work hand in hand to push you toward a particular storytelling posture: confident, stylish, and just a little haunted by what the city has seen.

The physical quality of the book matches its vibe. The cover design is striking, the interior art is evocative, and the overall production feels premium without turning the book into an artifact that intimidates new players. If you are someone who judges RPG books by how well they age as a coffee ring predictor, City of Mist passes the test with flying neon scribbles on napkins. The aesthetics aren not just for show; they’re a language that communicates mood, tone, and the shape of scenes you will likely experience. The result is a product that invites you to leaf through it on a rainy afternoon and then immediately plan a long campaign set in a city that exists in your group’s shared imagination.

## The Core Book as a tool for play: session zero to campaign arcs
If you are planning a short one-shot or a full campaign, the Core Book is designed to be flexible. It provides practical guidance for session zero, including how to establish tone, how to run scenes with a noir cadence, and how to manage tag economy so that players feel in control of their narrative choices. The guidance for running scenes emphasizes collaborative storytelling and ethical storytelling, particularly when dealing with power, trauma, and the responsibilities that come with mythic abilities. This is not a game that shies away from heavy themes; it invites you to explore them with grace, humor, and a willingness to let the city shape the story as much as you shape it.

For GMs, the book offers ready-to-run scene seeds that you can drop into a city teeming with conspiracies and choked by secrets. These seeds are not just plot hooks; they are micro-scenarios designed to be spun out into longer arcs. The GM tools section helps you pace a campaign so that tension ramps up in satisfying ways, with callbacks that reward players for returning to earlier threads. If you enjoy the feeling of guiding a group through a detective noir narrative that gradually reveals something larger than the case at hand, City of Mist gives you a sturdy chassis to drive that ride.

### Storytelling tips and common pitfalls
One pitfall to watch for is tag overload. It is easy to load your hero with a dozen powerful tags, turning play into a series of unstoppable moments. The Core Book acknowledges this and suggests ways to prune, refocus, and let the city challenge your hero in meaningful ways. Another pitfall is relying too heavily on combat or action beats at the expense of character moments. City of Mist rewards scenes where the hero’s mythos demands something of them beyond a quick victory. The best sessions balance the city’s demands with the character’s personal stakes, and the Core Book is explicit about encouraging those moments rather than rushing past them.

In practice, I found that the best sessions emerged when players leaned into the tension between Mythos and Logos and let the city’s mood push the story forward. A scene about a hero trying to salvage a normal life while a rising mythic threat threads its way through the alleys, subway tunnels, and rooftops tends to sing. The book’s tone supports this approach, offering language that feels both cinematic and grounded, which makes the play experience feel like a living, breathing story rather than a static exercise in dice rolling.

## Comparisons to similar systems: why City of Mist stands out
If you have played other indie RPGs with a narrative focus—things like Masks, Monster of the Week, or Chicago Typewriter—you will recognize the kinship in the emphasis on storytelling over stat-slog. City of Mist differentiates itself with its mythos/logos framing and its explicit desire to blend the extraordinary with the ordinary without becoming either silly or overworked. It isn’t trying to replace more traditional superhero tabletop systems; it is trying to be the kind of game you can play with a small group that wants to feel the thrill of a heist scene while also feeling the ache of a character who is hiding a truly singular secret.

Where some narrative systems lean into abstracted storytelling, City of Mist gives you a city that feels like a character in its own right. The urban setting, with its corners of danger and possibility, becomes a partner in the narrative, responding to your choices and foreshadowing what could happen if your hero pushes too far. If you crave a game that makes you feel like you are crafting a season of a comic book when you sit down for a play session, City of Mist is a refreshing option that sits comfortably between cinematic scope and personal stakes.

## Running City of Mist: session zero and beyond
Session zero in City of Mist is less about checklists and more about vibe. The Core Book suggests getting a feel for your city, your group’s comfort with heavy themes, and the kind of arcs you want to chase. A good session zero involves agreeing on what myth and reality mean to your table, agreeing on consent and content boundaries, and mapping a few threads that will thread through the campaign. From there, you can let the city take the wheel. The system rewards players who stay curious about how their tags interact with the city’s moods and who are willing to pivot when a scene demands a new kind of hero moment.

A practical tip: start small. Let the first few scenes revolve around a single case or rumor that feels personal to the players, then gradually widen the scope to reveal the city’s larger mythic conflicts. This approach helps everyone acclimate to the tag-driven resolution without feeling overwhelmed by the number of options. It also gives the GM a chance to demonstrate the narrative leverage of the system, showing that a clever tag use can transform a near-disaster into a scene with emotional weight and comic timing alike.

## Who should run City of Mist? Who should play it? And who should skip it (for now)
If you enjoy roleplaying that sits at the intersection of detective fiction, myth, and superhero lore, City of Mist is likely to click with you. It rewards players who value storytelling and clever scene construction more than raw power arithmetic. If you prefer highly crunchy combat simulations or a game that demands precise mechanical optimization, City of Mist may feel light on crunch and heavy on narrative choices. That is not a flaw so much as a design choice: the book leans into narrative compatibility and mood more than numbers, and that is precisely what makes it stand out for the right table.

The best groups for City of Mist are those that enjoy collaborative storytelling, a touch of melodrama, and a shared obsession with atmosphere. If your table loves swapping mythic prompts and constructing moral grey areas, City of Mist offers a superb home. If you are a GM who wants to slaughter foes with precise stat-lines every scene, you may prefer a more traditional system. Either way, the Core Book is a polished, thoughtful artifact that invites you to think about what your city means, what your hero represents, and how to tell stories that feel big without losing the intimate details that matter most.

## Verdict and final recommendations
City of Mist: Core Book is a strong entry point for players who care about tone, theme, and collaborative storytelling as much as they care about the occasional flashy moment. It excels at creating a city that feels alive, a hero who is both myth and man, and a play experience that leans into the drama of ordinary life colliding with extraordinary power. If you want a system that respects your creativity, that gives you the tools to craft scenes with style, and that treats narrative consequence as a feature rather than a bug, this book is worth your attention. The art direction, the layout, and the practical guidance for session zero and ongoing play all speak to a design that cares about the table as a living thing. It is not the densest crunch in the genre, but it is among the most satisfying, especially for groups that enjoy noir vibes, comic book swagger, and a generous dash of mythic mischief.

If your heart beats a little faster at the idea of a city that hums with secrets, and your brain lights up when you can spin a tag into a memorable moment, City of Mist will reward you with a play experience that might just outshine the sum of its parts. It invites experimentation, celebrates clever storytelling, and gives you a framework that makes collaborative world-building feel effortless and exhilarating.
