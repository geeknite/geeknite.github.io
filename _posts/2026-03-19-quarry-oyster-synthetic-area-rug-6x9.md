## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Care

### Everyday Care

A quick vacuum a few times per week is the expected routine. The goal is to keep loose grit from embedding in the fibers and to reduce the wear on high-traffic paths. For light dirt, a standard vacuum with a suction setting that won’t tug aggressively on the fibers should do the trick.

### Spills and Stains

Blot, blot, blot: don’t rub. Rubbing pushes stains deeper into the pile and sends the carpet into a self-justified panic. For most household spills, cold water and a mild detergent will lift the pigment and leave behind the least drama. For tougher stains, treat with a stain remover formulated for synthetic fibers, and always do a small test test on a hidden corner first.

### Deep Clean and Longevity

Occasionally, you may opt for a deeper clean. A steam-clean or carpet-cleaning approach can refresh the fibers, but be mindful of the manufacturer’s guidelines and the rug’s backing. Synthetic fibers generally tolerate damp cleaning well, but prolonged moisture can lead to issues with backing and edge curling. If you’re uncertain, a professional rug cleaning service can deliver a safe refresh without risking color loss or fiber damage.

### Odor, Off-Gassing, and Freshness

Like most new synthetics, there can be a mild odor when unpacked. It’s usually temporary—think coffee freshener rather than a chemical factory. Air out the rug for 24–48 hours in a well-ventilated space before placing it under heavy furniture or next to your breakfast nook. Once aired, the rug tends to settle into a fairly neutral scent that won’t hijack your living space with chemical vibes.
