## Care and Maintenance: Keep the Colors Vivid Without Losing Your Sanity
Care is a major factor in how long your rug remains a star rather than a faded memory of a bold idea. Here is a realistic, no-nonsense maintenance plan:

- Vacuum weekly with a lightweight setting or a gentle beater bar turned off. Do not use high suction on delicate colors as it can pull up fibers.
- Address spills immediately. Blot with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing to minimize color transfer. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution can help; test a small area first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fading patterns, especially if your space has strong natural light on one side.
- Use a rug pad or underlayment to prevent slipping and to reduce wear on the back of the rug. A good pad also adds cushion and reduces noise in high-traffic zones.
- Professional cleaning every 1–2 years is optional but recommended for larger rooms or if the rug sees heavy traffic and pets. A professional can refresh colors and lift stubborn odors without damaging the fibers.

If you want to keep the rug looking fresh without heavy maintenance, avoid placing it in direct hot sunlight for long periods. A sunny corner can bake colors into a newer pattern, and we all know color fade is nobody’s idea of a good time.
