## Drafting tips and value estimates
So you opened a dozen packs. Now what? Chaos drafting rewards improvisation, quick math, and more than a little luck. Here are practical tips to turn your 12 pack unboxing into a chaotic but enjoyable drafting session:

- Card selection and color balance: In chaos draft, you may see two or three strong uncommons that can anchor your two color or three color strategy. Think in terms of synergy rather than strict mana curves. It is about value and tempo, not perfect mana alignment.
- Rare acquisition: You will probably pull a few rares that you can turn into respectable deck pieces. The trick is to identify the ones that offer immediate playability or strong synergy with other cards you pick up during the draft.
- Land and mana: A common lament of chaos draft is too many non basics. Turn this into a theme by drafting around lands that support your color pairing or a few foil lands that look fancy in a deck photo. It is not just about hitting your colors but about staying in the match long enough to play those big spells you unearthed.
- Utility and sideboard: Some of the best values in a pack are not the rare but the options: modal cards, saga interactions, and creatures with strong ETB effects. In a chaos environment, these can provide surprising value even if they do not end up in your deck as a main piece.
- Deck building approach: Because you have a dozen packs, you can experiment with multi color strategies, but do not overcommit if you are missing necessary mana. Balance risk with the ability to cast your spells as early as possible. Sometimes you can pivot middraft to a more stable color pair if you realize the other colors are dead weight.

In terms of value, you should not expect to pull a billion dollar mythic from the 12 packs. The price point for boosters varies, but with Modern and Collector packs in the blend you may see a higher than expected return value if you pull a couple of strong rares or a couple of top-quality mythics. Still, do not rely on a 12 pack lineup as a guaranteed value machine. The goal of chaos draft is fun, experimentation, and the odd surprise more than a guaranteed monetary return. If your intent is purely financial, you might want to keep a budget, track cards that hold resale value, and consider trades with your local players rather than counting on online sold values.
