## Setup and gameplay flow
### Setup time and player count

The card holder, however, adds a little ceremony to the setup that makes you feel like a game night general rather than a mere mortal shuffling cards on a coffee table.

### Phase progression and how it works in deluxe mode
The heart of Phase 10 is the set of ten phases, each requiring a specific combination of cards. Typical phases include sequences like a run of the same color or sets of identical numbers. In this deluxe edition, you still chase those phases as the primary objective, but you gain a more reliable way to monitor your progress. The scorecard helps you keep a running tally of who has completed which phase, who is on track for the next phase, and who is likely to snap and declare Phase 10 in a dramatic fashion.

### The turn-by-turn rhythm
On your turn, you draw a card and then try to discard as you assemble the required phase. If you complete a phase, you advance to the next one and begin anew. If you cannot complete the phase, you draw and discard as usual, hoping for a helpful card or a cross-your-fingers moment when fate deals you what you need. The deluxe edition does not change the core mechanics, but the card holder and scorecard reduce the chaos factor and help you track which phase remains a mystery and which phase is already conquered by your table's resident strategy gods.
