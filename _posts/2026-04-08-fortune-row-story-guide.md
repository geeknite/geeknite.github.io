## Overview
Fortune Row is City of Mist's most theatrical micro-arc—a cobblestoned strip where palm readers murmur prophecies, jazz bleeds from the alleys, and shadows pretend to be customers. If the city is a theater, Fortune Row is its backstage; if the city is a skin, Fortune Row is the crone's underbelly where secrets pinch the mask. In this story guide, we’ll break down how to run Fortune Row as a one-shot, a two-session arc, or a longer arc that you harvest between side quests like a dragon hoard of clues.

### The premise and mood
The premise is simple on a napkin: a cherished fortune is foretold that upends the city’s balance of power, and the players must determine whether the future is real or just the city’s latest con. The mood blends noir, myth, and a dash of vaudeville. Expect scenes where divination collides with social manipulation, where a street magician can be a radical underboss, and where a single bad decision can rewrite who is masked and who is seen.

### Theme and how to run it
Fortune Row thrives when you treat it as a dance between consent and consequence. The players’ masks (as always in City of Mist) are not purely external; they are the characters’ chosen lenses that shape their actions and the city's response. The GM should emphasize friction: can a fortune teller’s prediction be trusted, or is it a trap laid by a faction? Can a saved life come at the cost of another life’s chance? The answer should be yes to both in different measures, which makes the story deliciously tricky.

## Adventure structure
Fortune Row can be played as a tight two-session arc or extended across a few nights of table time. It is built around a central clock—the Fortune Clock—that increments as players press into the mystery and slows when they step back to let the city’s power players do their thing.

### Act I: The Setup
- Hook: A neon-streaked night, a marquee that promises the “Prophecies of Fortune Row,” and a string of odd incidents: a shopkeeper who can’t stop laughing, a taxi that refuses to move after midnight, and a child who can describe the city’s future with perfect confidence.
- Keys and Masks: Introduce the row’s major players: Madame Fortuna, an elder fortune-teller whose visions are both seductive and terrifying; The Velvet Syndicate, a faction that uses social leverage and rumor to bend reality; and a courier who carries a single, priceless prop known as the Card of Fates.
- Clues: The players discover a handful of cryptic clues: a torn tarot card, a misdelivered letter, and a photograph of a masked gala that didn’t occur. The clues point toward Fortune Row’s backroom, a theater basement, and a hidden room beneath the old arcade.

### Act II: The Confrontation
- Rising Action: The Fortune Clock ticks forward as the players push to unmask the scheme. They face competing factions: a rival mystic who wants to erase a prophecy, and a corporate magnate who believes the prophecy will propel his stock price into legend.
- Scenes to Run: A parlor show that turns into a siege, a card reading that reveals a city-wide secret, and a rooftop chase across a skyline glimmering with neon halos.
- Complications: A decoy fortune that misleads the players, a betrayal by a trusted ally, and the revelation that the prophecy was created by multiple contributors—each with their own reason to hide the truth.

### Act III: The Payoff
- The Finale: The players decide what is real and what is merely a social construct. The Fortune Clock reaches its final tick as the key decision splits the city into two possible futures: one in which masks are worn and misdirection is king, and another where truth and vulnerability liberate the city’s underbelly.
- Epilogue Options: How the city’s power structure changes, how players’ masks shift after the reveal, and what remains of Fortune Row once the neon fades.

## Cast of characters
Fortune Row abundance is the style here. Below are sample non-player characters you can plug into your game, including a few defaults to help you get started.

- Madame Fortuna — The primary Fortune Row seer. She is old enough to remember every prophecy but young enough to be afraid of them. Her power is real, but she guards a secret that could topple the city’s entire mask system.
- Nightingale Pike — A rival seer who runs a competing fortune parlor. She uses charm and social manipulation to create the impression that she is the city’s only truth-teller.
- The Velvet Syndicate’s Messenger — A courier who can orchestrate events with the speed of gossip. They can move through Fortune Row with impunity, carrying messages that alter the city’s perception of the truth.
- The Card Dealer — A spry gambler who holds the Card of Fates, a device that can alter probability in the short term. They know which players have the most to lose or gain from the prophecy.
- The Wardrobe Keeper — A quiet technician who maintains the theater’s hidden rooms and who knows every costume as if they were a person.
- The Street Singer — An urban oracle whose songs unravel prophecies and sometimes misdirect the crowd, but always reveals a truth that someone would rather forget.

### Expanded cast notes
#### Madame Fortuna
Role: mentor and gatekeeper. Motivation: preserve the balance between truth and illusion. Flaw: memory is a living thing in Fortune Row, and hers is unreliable on the night of a major prophecy.
Mask alignment: Visionary with a soft spot for the underdog. Hook: her visions sometimes reveal more about the player characters than about the city, forcing introspection on why they chose their Mask.

#### Nightingale Pike
Role: rival seer who believes Fortune Row works best as a social playpen for power. Motivation: to be proven right about being the city’s true oracle. Flaw: stubborn pride.
Mask: Trickster. Hook: she can be an ally or a nemesis depending on how players handle social maneuvering and reputation.

#### The Velvet Syndicate’s Messenger
Role: the connective tissue of fortune and fear. Motivation: to keep Fortune Row in flux because flux equals control. Flaw: overreliance on information networks.
Mask: Guardian with a soft center. Hook: provides essential intel but demands debt that may come due later.

#### The Card Dealer
Role: wildcard and potential wildcard fix. Motivation: to test the players’ willingness to gamble with fate. Flaw: compulsion to collect wins rather than truths.
Mask: Executor. Hook: can flip the table with a single deal, altering probability and shifting outcomes in dramatic ways.

#### The Wardrobe Keeper
Role: keeper of entrances and exits. Motivation: to protect the theater’s secrets, including the truth behind the Card of Fates. Flaw: tends to protect what should be questioned.
Mask: Sage. Hook: provides critical access but demands the players respect the history that created Fortune Row’s current crisis.

#### The Street Singer
Role: chorus of the city’s truth. Motivation: to tell the city what it needs to hear, even if it hurts. Flaw: sometimes sings past the point of usefulness.
Mask: Visionary/Sage hybrid. Hook: a song that seems to be prophecy in disguise, but the real clue lies in the singer’s next verse.

## Masks, Mist, and Fortune Row
Fortune Row emphasizes rich interactions between masks and the city’s Mist—the intangible memory that shapes reality in City of Mist. Here are some guidance points to help you weave masks into every encounter.

### Masks to explore
- Visionary: paths of future possibility, often misread by the crowd.
- Trickster: social chess master who uses misdirection to reveal truth or to bury it deeper.
- Guardian: protective instincts that may clash with the row’s destabilizing forces.
- Sage: knowledge that sometimes comes with a price; their questions can unearth crucial clues.
- Shadow Caster: a step outside the line that reveals a different angle on events.

### The Mist in Fortune Row
Mist is not a generic fog here; it is the city’s memory of past prophecies seeping into present decisions. Use Mist to nudge players toward old secrets breached in new ways. A reading can alter a mask’s power in the moment, or a visitation from the Mist can rewrite a clue’s significance.

## GM tips for Fortune Row
- Maintain the noir vibe: gloom, rain-slick streets, and the soft echo of jazz. The music should occasionally cut out to emphasize a moment of truth.
- Keep the energy dynamic: Fortune Row is a place of showmanship. Let the players perform on a stage for an audience of extras and sometimes for a single crucial observer who might be the city’s next big reveal.
- Use clocks and tags: Keep a fast track of progress with a small clock, and use tags to guide players into quick decision-making rather than long dithering.
- Respect each player’s Mask: The players should be entangled with a set of Masks—either explicitly or implicitly—so ensure there are moments where their Masks are challenged or reinforced.
- Prepare misdirection: Fortune Row loves the misdirection that makes a mirror look like a door. Have at least one encounter where the obvious choice is a trap disguised as a gift.
- Balance social and action play: You’ll run into fights and chases, but the real drama comes from a conversation that changes who trusts whom.
- Keep prop artifacts tangible: Cards, letters, and costumes should have weight. A single prop can be a trigger for a revelation that redefines the entire arc.

## Scene ideas and extended encounters
- The Fortune Room Truce: Madame Fortuna hosts a private reading that reveals a moral dilemma: save three strangers at the cost of one life, or save one and condemn three. The choice should feel like a mirror held up to each player’s Mask.
- The Theatrical Escape: A chase through an abandoned theater, using collapsible scenery, a trapdoor, and a lot of theatrical misdirection. Use stagelight cues as emotional markers for the players’ decisions.
- The Card of Fates: The Card Dealer flips the card to reveal a choice with consequences that ripple through Fortune Row. A failed flip could reorder the city’s social order for weeks.
- Rooftop Rendezvous: A tense wind-swept moment where a crucial confession can occur, if the players can quiet the crowd below and face their own fears.
- The Gala That Wasn’t: A tapestry of memories that never happened; the players uncover a conspiracy that’s been rehearsed in a theatrical sense for years.
- The Backroom Gamble: A tiny room behind the parlor hides a device that stores the city’s greatest secrets—its power depends on who has the courage to pull the lever.

## Clues, investigation, and progression
- Clue 1: Torn tarot card – The card points toward the backroom, but the backroom has many doors.
- Clue 2: Misdelivered letter – A letter intended for a different fate reveals a secret alliance between two factions.
- Clue 3: The gala photo – A photo of a masked gala that never happened hints at a long-standing conspiracy.
- Clue 4: A piece of costume thread – It binds a hidden seam in the theater, which leads to a concealed exit.
- Clue 5: A cigarette stub with a logo – The logo ties back to a corporate sponsor who funded fortune telling in exchange for access to the crowd’s dreams.

Pattern: The clue progression should be trackable on a simple clock. The Fortune Row clock moves forward as players uncover information, but it also resets if a key figure persuades the crowd to look away. The clock’s face should be visible to everyone and used as a game mechanic rather than a flavor detail only.

## Mechanic notes: Masks and Mist
- City of Mist uses a pair of concepts: Masks and Mist. In Fortune Row, the Masks people wear shape their interactions; the Mist is the city’s memory of the past that informs current events.
- The Card of Fates card can be used to demonstrate how fortune can be manipulated. When flipped, it should suggest a risk that changes who the players trust.
- Time dilation in Fortune Row: a five-minute scene might feel like an hour if a character’s mask becomes the center of attention.

## Adventure hooks and variations
- Hook 1: The fortune tells of a future where Fortune Row will be burned unless the players intervene. Intervention may require compromising a rule the city holds dear, creating ethical tension.
- Hook 2: The Velveteen Syndicate tries to buy Fortune Row and convert the parlor into a hub of illegal activity. The players can interrupt or monetize the row’s power in a way that reshapes social hierarchy.
- Hook 3: A masked gala is rumored to be happening somewhere in Fortune Row. Attending could reveal the city’s hidden patrons and their real motives.
- Hook 4: A rumor whispers that one of the parlor’s readers is a living myth in disguise, and the reveal might force the city to decide how real myths can be among us.

## Final thoughts and recommendation
Fortune Row is one of those city adventures that looks small on the map but expands into a whole neighborhood of stories if you lean into the vibes and the masks. It’s not a knock-down, drag-out boss fight; it’s a cunning social puzzle, a chase, and a memory that refuses to stay buried. The best sessions use a strong opening scene, a clear midgame conflict, and a high-stakes ending that invites players to choose how the city moves forward. If you love City of Mist’s blend of mythic noir and social engineering, Fortune Row will feel like a homecoming with a twist.

### Playstyle notes
- One-shot elegance: Lock the arc into a single, cinematic night by tightening the Act I hook, compressing Act II, and delivering a punchy Act III with a moral sting.
- Long arc elegance: Allow recurring visits to Fortune Row as the city’s political weather shifts. Use new Clues and recurring NPCs to create a sense that the Row never fully sleeps.
- Player agency wins: The strongest sessions empower players to reframe the city’s memory. Let their choices redefine what is“true” and what remains “conjecture” in the Mist.
