And in the real world, the desk passes with flying colors unless you have a colleague who thinks the desk is a DIY racetrack for their robot vacuum.

## Space and Layout: The Grandest Desk You’ll See This Quarter

With a length of 5400 mm (that’s 5.4 meters if you’re asking a tape measure to multiply), this desk is designed to host two three-person pods with a shared central screen. It’s not just seating; it’s an architectural statement. The depth of 1200 mm gives ample room for keyboards, mice, tablets, and the mysterious “sixth monitor” you swear you’ll never need but always want when you’re doing a big project.

### Ergonomics: Sitting, Standing, and Doing Something in Between
The Eternal 6 includes a height-adjustable mechanism that supports sit/stand routines, which is essential for maintaining morale during marathon coding sessions or when someone proposes a “six-person whiteboard jam” at 4:20 PM. The range is enough to accommodate people of various heights and chair configurations, though you’ll want to ensure your chairs have good lumbar support. If you’re prone to shoulder tension, you may also consider a task chair with adjustable armrests to complement the desk’s generous surface area.

Line up six users with chairs, and you’ll find a surprisingly civilized workflow emerges. The central screen acts as a focal point; each pod gains pride of place for their own peripherals, and the absence of a chaotic sea of separate desks reduces the chances of a “Where did my stapler go?” moment that results in a minor HR incident.

### Assembly: The Epic Quest, One Allen Wrench at a Time
As with any large modular desk system, assembly is a two-person job unless you enjoy creating your own DIY comedy routine. The included instructions are clear enough to avoid tears, but the package contains a generous number of bolts, washers, and brackets. Plan for 60-90 minutes per person if you’re not in a rush, and maybe allocate an extra hour if this is your first time assembling a large piece of furniture. The good news: once assembled, the Eternal 6 feels like a unit and not a collection of separate surfaces. It’s the difference between a musical ensemble and a garage band with a single amp and questionable harmonies.

### Cable Management: The Secret Sauce
A six-person desk creates a cable orchestra: USB-C hub here, monitor power there, and perhaps a rogue gaming mouse you promised to retire five years ago. The desk’s integrated cable channels, grommets, and under-surface trays help tame the chaos before it erupts into a dramatic tangled mess. We tested with six laptops, three external drives, a docking station, and a coffee mug that kept escaping to the Newport desk corner. The result: manageable cables, easy access for maintenance, and a visible reduction in tripping hazards. If you’re a tidy freak, you’ll adore the raceways; if you’re a creative slob, you’ll find new places to stash stuff and still be able to locate it before the next sprint review.

Two workstations with a central screen create a unique social dynamic at the office. The central screen serves as the focal point for demonstrations, release planning, and the occasional “show-and-tell” where someone wants to present a prototype that still has more red squiggles than actual features.

### Visual Quality and Performance
Don’t expect cinema-grade color or HDR-level brightness from the center screen, but you won’t be left in the dark either. The screen hub is designed for productivity tasks: presentations, CI dashboards, dashboards, and the occasional team-wide update. For software teams, this is a dream because you can orchestrate a code review where every team member can contribute without the tyranny of passing laptops around the table. If you’re into media or design, you’ll likely want to connect your own high-end monitor for color-critical work and reserve the center display for the team’s general tasks.

### Collaboration: When Six Heads Are Better Than One
The beauty of a six-person layout is that you can pivot from individual work to quick collaborative sessions without lugging team members into a separate conference room. Standups feel more dynamic, demos are more interactive, and the team can collectively examine a problematic graph on the center screen. The risk, of course, is that in a space this open, conversations can become audible from the next building over. If your office is prone to noise issues, consider soft room dividers, sound-absorbing panels, or a white-noise machine that won’t wake the server rack in the next room. ## Maintenance, Longevity, and Daily Use Realities

With heavy usage comes heavy cleaning. The Oak White desk ships with veneer that resists minor scuffs, but you’ll still want to use a soft cloth and mild cleaners to keep the surface looking professional. Regular maintenance includes tightening screws every few months, checking cable fasteners, and keeping the central screen away from accidental spills (the coffee gods are not to be trifled with). In normal office life, this desk should hold up well over several years with routine care.

One real-world tip: if you’re wiring a six-seat configuration, put the most frequently used devices closest to the users who require frequent access, and reserve the central area for items that require collaboration. You don’t want the central footpath to turn into a bottleneck during a critical standup.
