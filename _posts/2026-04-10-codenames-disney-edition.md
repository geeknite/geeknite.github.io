### What is Disney Edition?
Codenames is a word game about guessing secret agents on a grid using one-word clues. In Disney Edition, the agents on the board are all Disney characters, films, parks, or icons. Two teams, red and blue, compete to locate their agents first, with a spymaster for each team giving a clue that ties together several on the board. The catch is that one word can tie several different Disney properties together, but if you go too far you risk helping the other team or triggering the assassin. The assassin, of course, ends the game instantly in the worst possible way, typically with a thunderous roar from your inner monologue.

### How to Play (Overview)
- Setup: Arrange 25 Disney themed word cards in a 5 by 5 grid. Clamp down the color-key card that indicates which cards belong to red and blue, which are neutral, and which is the assassin. The red and blue teams alternate, starting with the team that the chosen spymaster belongs to.
- The Spymaster's Clue: The active spymaster gives a clue that links multiple on-board words, expressed as a single word followed by a number, like Adventure 3. The number indicates how many words on the board the clue is connected to.
- Guessing: The team then tries to guess which of the board words are their agents. They may guess up to the number plus one (because sometimes your brain wants to go the extra mile). If they touch a card belonging to the other team, that card is revealed and the turn ends. If they reveal a neutral card, the turn ends without helping their own side. If they reveal the assassin card, the game ends immediately with a loss for the guessing team.
- End of Turn: After an incorrect guess or when the team has exhausted all their guesses, the turn passes to the other team.

### The Clues and the Disney Twist
Disney Edition really shines with clue making. The board is filled with a mixture of classic films, parks, characters, and even ships and sidekicks. This creates a range of potential clues that can be wonderfully clever or hilariously obtuse. Think of trying to tie together Aladdin, Genie, Jafar, and Magic Carpet under the umbrella word Wish. Or go for a more playful route like Hero 2 to connect Hero and Villain or Princess 4 to connect a surprisingly long list of regal figures.

This edition rewards players who know their Disney catalog well, but it also invites creative leaps. It is not unusual for a spymaster to deliver a clue that feels like a trivia trap, such as Night 3 when the words on the board include Nightmare Before Christmas, Night at the Museum, and Nightcrawler (the X-Men character). The room erupts in laughter as a hidden synergy reveals itself, and the whole table nods with grudging respect for the cleverness of a well-chosen clue.

### Modes, Variants, and House Rules
Codenames Disney Edition sticks to the classic two-team format, but there are a few ways to spice things up:
- Timed Mode: Each round has a timer to add pressure and keep the pace up. The spymaster can feel endearing and terrifying in equal measure when the clock ticks down while they search for the perfect monotone clue.
- Cooperative Mode: You and your friends all work together to locate all agents before time runs out. Good for large gatherings where everyone knows a little about Disney but not enough to start a standalone trivia night.
- Character Focus: A variant where you choose a subset of cards (for instance, only princesses or only sidekicks) to be the focus of a round. This can turn into a surprisingly strategic exercise in theme filtering.

### Strategy: Winning Minds in a Disney World
- Know your board: Before the game starts, take a few moments to memorize the characters and their general eras. If you know the villain cluster from classic animated features, you can often clue in a surprising number of words with a single clue like Villain 3.
- Build for overlap: Try to create clues that connect two or three related words. For example, a clue like Classic 4 might connect Princess, Castle, and Par- a mild spoiler: if your deck is heavy on classic Disney, a single well-chosen word can connect a surprising number of board items.
- Control the tempo: If your team is confident, push for more guesses on the same turn to maximize your turn's yield. If the conversation falters, switch to safer clues and end gracefully.
- Know when to switch to focus: In Disney Edition there are often cross-genre connections. A clue like Sidekick 4 can connect goofy friends like Timon, Pumbaa, and Buzz Lightyear’s robot dog (okay, not exactly, but you get the drift). When your options are thin, go for the safer pick and avoid the assassin.
