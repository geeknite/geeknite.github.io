## Core Mechanics: How the Streak Adds Chaos
The base game Exploding Kittens remains very much in control here, but the Streak expansion introduces several new layers that mix into the core gameplay without turning it into a different game entirely. The streak cards tend to shift the balance by creating chain reactions that can cascade through the table.

- Streak triggers: Each streak card has a trigger condition that often relates to what is happening on a player’s turn or what card is currently in play. The trigger might be something simple like a cat streak or something more elaborate like a multi-card combo that aligns with a funny phrase on the card art. 
- Cat tokens: These tokens are used to mark certain states on the table, and players may gain or lose advantage depending on how the tokens are placed or removed. It adds a tactile layer to the strategy rather than just a mental calculation.
- Card interactions: The expansion doesn’t overhaul the base game’s engine; it adds more cards that can be used to bluff, trap, or poke at the other players, often with a comedic twist. The interplay between base cards and streak cards creates a lively tempo where one wrong card can unleash a deluge of consequences.

What this means in practice is that rounds become more about reading your audience than counting your points. ## Strategy Tips and House Rules
To get the most out of Streaking Kittens, consider these practical tips:

- Read the room. The expansion thrives on social dynamics. If your group is loud and playful, lean into the chaos. If your group is more subdued, use streaks as a fun, slower burn to build tension.
- Control tempo. Use streak triggers to pace rounds. If you’re ahead, push for cards that stall or disrupt, if you’re behind, seek streaks that create flashy, comeback opportunities.
- Leverage bluffing. As with the base game, deception is part of the fun. You can tell a small fib about your intent to protect a key card, just to see who panics first.
- Build a story arc. Treat each streak as a scene in a play: act out a dramatic reveal, then let the audience decide how to react. It makes table talk memorable and increases the likelihood of a second or third round.
- Establish a simple house rule. A favorite among Geeknite readers is to award a small bonus to players who successfully navigate a streak without losing their composure or their dignity.

House rules, when implemented with care, can prolong the playtime and deepen the experience without undermining the core rules. This is not a mind-bending Euro game, but it is a well-tuned party game that rewards quick thinking and a willingness to commit to the bit.
