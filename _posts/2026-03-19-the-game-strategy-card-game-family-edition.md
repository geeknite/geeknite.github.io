### Card interactions and strategy for all ages

On the strategic front, you’ll discover a fun balance between building your own engine and poking at others’ plans. The better players learn to read signals from the board—the cards being played, the resource tokens circulating, and the timing of event cards—that hint at an opponent’s goals. The younger players learn to read numbers and colors, which is the important early literacy in modern board gaming anyway. The synergy here is to present accessible goals for beginners while leaving room for cunning plays from more experienced hands.

If you’re wondering about sequences and combos, yes, there are small but meaningful combos to explore. You might discover that drafting a particular resource early unlocks a powerful pair of actions a couple of rounds later, but the window on that is intentionally short to keep things from becoming a “lock-in” experience where someone can babysit a solitaire strategy to victory. In other words, it’s approachable, not greed-inducing.
