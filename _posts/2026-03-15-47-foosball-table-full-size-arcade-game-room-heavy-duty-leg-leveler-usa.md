Assembly typically takes a couple of hours, depending on your experience with similar setups and how much coffee you drink. The instruction manual is clear but doesn’t assume you’re a mechanical genius—this is the right balance. Here are some practical tips to make the process painless:

- Sort the hardware by size before you begin; a good workbench is a friend.
- Dry-fit parts before tightening; you’ll save yourself from rework when a screw line doesn’t match exactly.
- Use a small magnetic tray to keep tiny screws in check; losing a single screw is a tiny apocalypse for any DIY project.
- Have a helper for lifting the table onto its base; two people makes everything easier, especially when the legs are wide and the box is not light.
- Check your floor for levelness. If a floor is more curved than a mountain road, you’ll be grateful for those leg levelers.

Once assembled, the table stands as a single, sturdy unit ready for action. It doesn’t wobble on a hard surface, and even on carpet the levelers provide a surprising degree of stability.
