## Space and setup considerations

If you live in a smaller home, the 3 in 1 option is a godsend for party nights. It reduces the need for three separate tables and frees up floor space when you are not playing. That said, you should still measure the space and consider the clearance around the table for comfortable swings, shots, and paddle swings. The recommended clearance is similar to what you would want for a regular dining chair plus a foot. In other words, give yourself a little room to breathe, otherwise you will have to rely on the neighbor's dog to fetch the balls when you miss a shot. 

Assembly is a one time rite of passage. It is not a pick up from the local store and plop down plan, but it is not something that eats up your entire weekend either. If you have basic handyman skills, you will be disappointed by nothing. If you are new to tools, you will become a legend in your own living room after finishing the setup. 

## Maintenance and care

To keep this table looking good for years to come, wipe the surfaces after use and avoid heavy spills on the felt. Use the correct cues and paddles for their corresponding surfaces. Keep the table away from direct sunlight to avoid warping or discoloration of the laminates. A monthly check of the hardware is wise to tighten bolts and ensure the swivel remains smooth.
