## What’s in the Box

The expansion aims to be additive rather than disruptive, which is a polite way of saying: you won’t need a PhD in card-sorcery to use it, but you might want one for the end-of-round scoring.

- Card lineup: roughly 60 new cards that introduce new sushi varieties, wilds, and scoring modifiers. The new cards aren’t just more of the same; they’re designed to press players toward decisions you might regret for a whole last round.
- New tokens: six new turn-modifying counters that can change who gets first pick, who dumps a card, and who ends up with the spicy roll at the wrong moment.

The artistry stays crisp and charming; this is still a world where a tiny panda chef and a tuna slice have opinions about your strategy. ## New Mechanics at a Glance
- Momentum system: Completing certain sequences generates momentum tokens that can be burned for extra points or special bonuses. Momentum makes the mid-game feel like you’re piloting a tiny ship through a coral reef, with potential chaos around every corner. You’ll love the sense of forward propulsion until you realize you’re several points behind and you have to spend momentum to catch up.
- Sauce and wilds: A subset of cards functions as wilds or multipliers. They’re not there to break the game; they’re there to reward flexible planning and occasional improvisation. If you like to lock in a plan and then smugly watch your plan fail, you’ll get your fix here.
- Turn-order jitters: A handful of cards can shuffle the drafting order, creating moments where the second or third player can snatch a critical plate just before the round ends. It adds tension without turning the game into a frantic speed-draft nightmare.
- Mystery plates: A portion of new plates resolves with unusual end-of-round rules, nudging players toward diversification rather than chasing a single perfect combo.

## Why an Expansion? Thematic and Mechanical Touchpoints
Every good expansion for a lightweight family-friendly game has to deliver three things: more variety, reasonable learning curve, and a handful of moments that remind you why you fell in love with the base game in the first place. The Sushi Go! Expansion Pack checks all three boxes with a wink.

- More variety: With 60 new cards across multiple sushi types and wilds, you’ll see dozens of new combos and potential endgames. No two sessions will feel identical, which is essential for maintaining the “soon you’ll be bored” cycle.
- Cleaner balance: The new scoring curves are tuned to work with the original design, not against it. You won’t accidentally end up with a runaway leader who ate nothing but gunk rolls; instead, you’ll see players jockeying for both early leads and late-game bonuses.
- Table dynamics: The mechanic that shifts turn order and adds momentum prompts a broader distribution of attention around the table. It’s a subtle but meaningful way to keep everyone engaged, especially the players who typically retreat to the corner to avoid conflict.

## Strategy: How to Approach the Expansion
- Learn the new cards: The first few rounds should be about orienting yourself toward the new timing windows the expansion creates. It’s tempting to chase momentum, but you’ll pay in the next roll if you chase too hard. Balance is your friend.
- Watch opponents’ momentum: Momentum tokens accumulate, and cashing them in at the right moment can swing a round. Don’t be greedy; patience is a virtue in sushi cart accuracy.
- Diversify your drafting window: The presence of mystery plates incentivizes you to diversify your choices across the different sushi types. If you become too enamored with one type, you’ll miss out on endgame bonuses.
- Don’t fear the wilds: Wilds and sauce cards come with risk-reward timing. It’s often better to reserve them for a round where you know you’ll need a little extra credit to edge out a competitor, rather than use them on the next round where the lead might already be decided.
