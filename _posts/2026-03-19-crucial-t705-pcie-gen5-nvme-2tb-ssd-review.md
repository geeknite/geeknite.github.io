## Performance: Real-world expectations vs. marketing numbers
This is where the rubber meets the silicone. The headline speeds of 14,500 MB/s read are impressive on paper, but what does that actually translate to in your daily use? Here are the practical takeaways:

- Boot time and OS responsiveness: Expect noticeably quicker boot sequences and snappier system responsiveness when loading your OS and startup services.
- Game load times: Expect faster level loads and asset streaming, particularly with texture-heavy titles and large open-world environments.
- File transfers: Transferring large files or bulk data (think game assets, video files, or project libraries) will feel smoother with shorter transfer windows.
- Random I/O: Gen5 shines in random read/write scenarios thanks to improved queue depths and advanced controller firmware, which translates to better multitasking while gaming or editing.

To temper expectations, remember that synthetic benchmarks are optimized for peak throughput. Real-world gaming and daily work involve a mix of random IO, background tasks, and thermal constraints. In many cases, you’ll see sustained performance that’s very good, but perhaps not the turbo-charged peak you see in short bursts during a synthetic test. This is by design: Gen5 is built for high steady performance rather than occasional spikes.

## Thermal performance and cooling needs
Gen5 drives are power horses. The T705’s performance envelope is highly dependent on keeping the drive cool. Without adequate cooling, you’ll likely see thermal throttling that trims your maximum sustained throughput to keep temperatures in check. This is not a unique problem to Crucial; it’s the nature of a high-speed PCIe Gen5 SSD running sustained workloads.

What to do:
- Use a motherboard with good airflow or a dedicated M.2 heatsink.
- If you’re placing the drive in a compact case, consider additional cooling strategies or a PCIe slot near a fan, to maximize air movement around the drive.
- Monitor thermals with a tool you trust and test your workload to see if sustained speeds fall off under extended gaming sessions or video rendering.

In practical terms, with a heatsink and a cool-environment, the T705 holds up extremely well. If you’re chasing maximum sustained throughput for long editing sessions or huge texture asset pipelines, the extra cooling is not optional—it's mandatory.

## Compatibility and installation: easy does it
Installing an M.2 NVMe drive is one of the easier upgrades you can perform, and the T705 is no exception. It’s compatible with most modern motherboards that support PCIe Gen5 NVMe drives and M.2 2280 form factors. Laptop users should verify BIOS/UEFI settings and ensure they have an available NVMe slot plus adequate cooling—some ultrabooks have trunks of heat to fight.

- Step-by-step sanity checks:

  - Enable NVMe support and set the slot to Gen5 where applicable.
  - Use the Crucial Migration Tool or your preferred cloner to move your OS/important apps if you’re upgrading from a Gen3/Gen4 drive.
  - If your system supports it, pair with a cooling solution to sustain peak performance.

Post-upgrade, you’ll want to format the drive and install your software stack. If you’re not comfortable with cloning, a clean install of your OS plus reinstalling your games may yield better long-term performance and stability.
