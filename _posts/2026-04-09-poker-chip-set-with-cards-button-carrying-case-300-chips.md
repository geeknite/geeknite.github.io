## Setup, maintenance, and care
Setup is straightforward. Open the case, lay out the dividers, and place the chips in the compartments according to your preferred denominations. The carrying case is a real helper here; it keeps dust out and ensures that the bits you track through the year of gaming stay organized. Maintenance is equally simple: wipe the chips with a dry cloth if they get dusty, and use a soft brush on the edges to remove any stray fibers or lint from the felt. Cards can be kept fresh by a light dusting with a card brush, which also helps reduce sticking during shuffles. If you are playing in extremely humid environments, you may want to consider using a small silica gel pack inside the case to keep the cards from warping or the chips from feeling a little sticky after long sessions. It is not necessary, but it is one of those little touches that can extend the life of your gear and keep your table looking like it belongs in a home gaming den rather than a garage sale.

### Maintenance tips from geeks for geeks

- Use a small felt mat under the case during travel to prevent rattling and additional noise that might wake your neighbors during late night poker sessions.
