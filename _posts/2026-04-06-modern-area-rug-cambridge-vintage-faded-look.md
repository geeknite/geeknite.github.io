## Materials and construction: what you’re really buying
Brand names vary, but most Cambridge models sit in a practical, mid-range spectrum when it comes to materials. You’ll often find synthetic fibers like polypropylene or a blend of polypropylene with polyester. Some versions lean into a more natural vibe with viscose highlights for a subtle sheen, while a few premium lines might use a wool blend for that warm, fuzzy aura.

### Why material choice matters
- Durability: Synthetic fibers resist stains and wear and tear, which is great for high-traffic areas, households with kids, or if your dog thinks the rug is a chew toy simulator.
- Maintenance: Spills are easier to address on synthetics; most can be blotted up without immediate panic.
- Feel: Wool and wool blends offer warmth and a soft hand, but they require more maintenance and can be pricier.

If you’re going for a budget-friendly, high-traffic solution, a polypropylene blend Cambridge rug is a solid bet. For a touch more luxe and warmth, you might chase a wool-blend variant, but be prepared to pay a little extra for the plush factor.

### Backing, padding, and longevity
Backings matter, especially if you’re placing the rug on hardwood or tile. A non-slip natural latex backing helps keep the rug in place, while a separate rug pad can extend life and reduce shifting. Padding also enhances comfort underfoot and can help protect the rug’s fibers from flattening in high-traffic zones.

A common question: do these rugs shed? Most modern Cambridge designs are engineered to minimize shedding compared to older wool-only rugs. You’ll still see some fiber tumble initially, but a quick vacuum cycle should reduce it to a minimum after the first few weeks.

## Practicalities: cleaning, maintenance, and everyday life
No rug is perfect in every scenario, but the Cambridge is designed with real-life households in mind.

### Cleaning and stain management
- Quick-response spills: blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing (which can drive stain deeper). For common spills like wine or juice, a mild detergent solution or a solvent recommended by the rug brand will usually do the trick.
- Routine cleaning: vacuum regularly with a suction setting appropriate for the fiber type. For deeper cleaning, follow the manufacturer’s guidelines or hire a professional service.
- Pet-friendly notes: many Cambridge rugs resist pet-related stains fairly well, but it’s wise to clean up accidents promptly and consider a protective stain repellent after initial cleaning.

### Sunlight and color stability
Prolonged direct sun can fade any rug’s colors over time. If your Cambridge sits in a sunny spot, rotate the rug periodically to maintain an even fade and avoid hot spots where heat could degrade fibers unevenly.

### Rug pads and floor protection
A quality pad reduces slipping, prolongs life, and cushions footsteps. For hardwood floors, a non-slip pad with a low profile is ideal; this combo protects both floor and rug while preserving the vintage look.
