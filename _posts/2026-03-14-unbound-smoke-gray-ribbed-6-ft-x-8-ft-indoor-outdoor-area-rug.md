Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping It Spiffy Without a Full-Time Cleaning Crew

Maintenance matters more when you’ve got indoor/outdoor versatility. The Unbound rug handles spills and footprints with a straightforward routine:

- Regular vacuuming: A light pass with a vacuum (no behemoth suction needed) to keep dirt from embedding.
- Spot cleaning: Mild soap and water for most stains; avoid harsh chemicals that could fade the color or degrade the fibers.
- Weather considerations: If you’re in a climate with heavy rainfall, roll the rug up when possible during prolonged downpours to prevent sogginess and to keep mold at bay. After rain, a quick shake-out and a rinse will usually do the trick.
- Drying: Let it dry completely before returning to outdoor use to prevent mold/mildew growth on the backing.

If you’re the kind of person who loves the ritual of cleaning as a meditative practice, you’ll enjoy the few minutes it takes to bring this rug back to showroom-quality after a summer BBQ that went a little longer than planned. For those who prefer a “set it and forget it” approach, the rug is generally forgiving, and a quick wipe-down will typically suffice between deep cleans.

Internal Field Guide: Quick Tips to Maximize Longevity

- Rotate seasonally: A gentle rotation helps prevent uneven fading in areas with direct sun exposure.
- Use a rug pad indoors: If you’ll be placing the rug indoors on a hard surface, consider a thin rug pad for extra cushioning and to prevent slipping.
- Mind the backing: If you place it on a wooden deck, ensure the backing is breathable and won’t trap moisture. If you’re worried about deck staining, place a protective mat underneath.
- Store smartly: When not in use for extended periods, roll and store in a dry place. Avoid folding, which can create creases over time.
