## Assembly and toolset: what to expect
The original listing included hardware and a basic instruction sheet. The assembly is straightforward but not instantaneous; plan for about 60 to 90 minutes per desk if you are doing it solo, or 30 to 45 minutes if you have a friend who brings snacks and a good playlist.

A typical process looks like this:
- Unpack and inventory the pieces: desk tops, legs, crossbars, screws, washers, and the screen panel hooks. A helpful trick is to lay everything out as if you are building a scene from a sci fi video game inventory screen.
- Attach legs to the desk tops using the provided bolts and an Allen wrench. The Wrench typically lives in the hardware packet; if not, you can improvise with a nice Swiss Army multitool that your desk deserves.
- Install the screen dividers using the brackets. Make sure you align them properly with the desk edge. A little misalignment is fine; a lot is noticeable in photos and in your colleagues' morale.
- Tighten every bolt with equal pressure. A good practice is to go in a diagonal pattern to ensure even distribution of force. We all remember the tale of the wobbly desk that caused a domino effect in a conference room. Learn from it.

## Ergonomics and comfort: does it fit modern workflows?
Ergonomics matter, especially when you are spending long hours performing a mix of typing, designing, and streaming. The triple desk arrangement has a few ergonomics-friendly aspects:

- Screen divider height: The dividers are tall enough to provide privacy but not so tall that you feel boxed in. This is a balance trick that a lot of home office pieces ignore. Good job, product designers.
- Chair compatibility: The desks accommodate standard office chairs and gaming chairs. If you own a super ergonomic chair, you’ll be happy; if your chair squeaks, you’ll be happy to know these desks do not amplify squeaks—the floor does the amplifying, usually.

### A note on cabling and space planning
Three desks mean three power strips, three monitors, and probably three coffee mugs that demand their own sunlit corner of the desk. The key is to map out your cables first. Plan routes along the back edge of the desk where you can secure them with clips or a shallow cable raceway. You do not want a cable avalanche mid morning stand-up. Consider labeling cables by desk and by device to reduce the chaos you unleash when you need to troubleshoot at 9 am on a Tuesday.

## Maintenance and long-term care
Keeping these desks looking fresh involves a few routine habits:
- Regular wipe-downs with a mild cleaner to remove fingerprints and coffee splashes. For stubborn marks on the laminate edge, a magic eraser works wonders without stripping the finish.
- Inspect the leg bolts every few weeks. Loose bolts can contribute to a wobble. If you hear a rattle when you tap the desk with a finger, it is time to tighten a bolt and maybe re-seat a foot pad.
- Clean the divider surfaces with a fabric-safe cleaner if your panels are upholstered. If you have a vinyl wrap, use a gentle cleaner and a soft cloth to avoid stretching or discoloration.
- Cable management: clean as you go. Invest in a channel cover or a few zip ties to keep things tidy. This saves you from the moment you realize you can alphabetize your USB-C cables but still trip over the same power strip for the third time in a single day.
