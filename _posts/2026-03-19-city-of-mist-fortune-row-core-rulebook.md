## What Fortune Row Is Supposed to Do
Fortune Row is pitched as a focused sandbox within the City of Mist universe. The idea is not merely to add more heroes and villains; it’s to expand the flavor of the city’s core districts, the social web of factions, and the myths that haunt every alleyway and metro stop. You should expect:

- A dense city layer that feels lived-in, with new factions, locals, and a few sideways glances at the blurred line between myth and media.
- A fresh pass on Masks, the collectible character templates that anchor a lot of City of Mist play. Fortune Row adds a handful of new masks that slot into the existing system without breaking the vibe.
- Tools for GMs to weave the new district into ongoing campaigns or launch a brand-new arc from the first page. The GM toolkit aims to be practical rather than precious, with ready-made encounters and a system for generating emergent mysteries.
- Mechanics that keep the focus on narrative rather than crunchy math, because that’s what City of Mist does well when it’s functioning at peak GM-mode.

This is not a new core rulebook in the sense of a complete system overhaul; it’s a expansion and refinement that gives you more playground, more tone, and more of those gorgeous, neon-tinged pages you come to expect from a City of Mist product.

## Book Structure and Presentation
Fortune Row doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it refurbishes the wheel with chrome accents. The layout keeps the same feel of the core, which is essential for players who want to drop in mid-session without a 20-minute refresher course. The book is divided into several well-defined sections:

### 1) The City and Its Myths
This portion sets the stage: what Fortune Row looks like at night, who runs the blocks, and which myths are most active in a given season. The writing team leans into urban myth motifs—fortune tellers who predict outcomes with cosmic consequences, street racers who outrun fate itself, and a media machine that can alter a rumor into a reality in hours. If you enjoyed the city-building vibe of the core, you’ll be happy to see Fortune Row deepen it rather than dryly repeat it.

### 2) Masks, New and Old
Masks are the signature flavor of City of Mist: archetypes you slip into like a favorite jacket. Fortune Row introduces a handful of new Masks designed to feel at home in a district where luck, risk, and consequence mingle on a daily basis. These masks aren’t merely cosmetic; they shape how players approach scenes and how GMs slot in twists. The new masks come with tailored moves, some of which reward clever use of Fortune Row’s city lore, and others that reward deliberate storytelling choices (like “Orchestrator” or “Shadows of Fortune”).

### 3) Logos and Mythos, Rebalanced
Fortune Row tweaks the core mechanics of Logos (the modern, rational side of the city) and Mythos (the mythic, magical side). Expect adjustments that smooth out edge cases and help groups run sessions with fewer rule lookups. If you found some of the base game’s math fiddly or variable, Fortune Row provides a more robust rhythm for social scenes and encounters, letting the narrative drive the dice rather than the other way around.

### 4) The District: Fortune Row
The heart of the book is the district itself. You’ll find street-by-street beats, notable locations, and a cast of NPCs who are more than one-note antagonists. The writing captures the city’s mood—glossy storefronts, rain-slick rooftops, and a rumor that can spiral into a city-wide incident if not handled with care. The district is designed to slot into campaigns smoothly, with hooks for long arcs and one-shot adventures that feel satisfying without requiring a full character rebuild.

### 5) GM Toolkit
If you’re a GM who loves tools, Fortune Row shines in this chapter. It includes randomly generated incident seeds, vivid scene prompts, and a table of micro-consequences to color in a moment when the players steal a car, crash a gala, or misread a fortune that was never meant to be trusted. There are tips for pacing urban mysteries, balancing action and investigation, and-esque guidance on tone management so you can tilt toward noir, mythic fantasy, or something wilder without breaking the vibe.

### 6) Encounters and Scenarios
A good chunk of the book is built to get you from page one to table-ready sessions. The encounters feel punchy and are often written with a specific mask in mind, which makes them excellent for pre-gen sessions or when you want a quick setup after a late arrival. The scenarios are structured so you can adapt them to different player counts and campaign currents without tuning a calculator for three hours.

### 7) Art, Layout, and Debugging Notes
Visually, Fortune Row keeps the neon noir aesthetic that City of Mist fans expect. The art direction is strong, with a balance of moody imagery and character overviews. The layout is reader-friendly; you’ll notice consistent typography, clear encounter flow, and generous margins to scribble notes during a session. If you enjoyed the aesthetics of the core rulebook, Fortune Row will feel familiar, just with a sharper focus on the district’s flavor.

## New Mechanics and How They Feel at the Table
City of Mist has always walked a line between narrative-driven play and the occasional crunchy checkpoint. Fortune Row leans more toward narrative flow, but it does so with a handful of targeted mechanical tweaks that matter in play. Here are a few flavors you’ll likely encounter:

- Fortune dice or luck-based resources: Fortune Row expands the luck economy with a micro-resource pool that players and GMs can spend to tilt scenes, reveal hidden details, or buy a crucial advantage just when the city’s fortune turns. The design intention is to reward sharp observation and bold choices without turning into a resource drain disaster.
- Mask synergy and multi-mask play: The new masks encourage players to experiment with overlapping identities. You’ll see several scenes designed to leverage multiple masks at once, creating interesting tension patterns where the hero might have to hide in plain sight while revealing a hidden connection to a mythic rumor.
- Mythos-driven twists: Sets of twists tied to local myths, legends, or city legends. These twists are crafted to escalate stakes quickly, which is perfect for one-shot thrill rides or a tense mid-campaign reveal that reframes the city’s threat level.

The practical upshot: Fortune Row keeps mechanics approachable. If you enjoyed the core game’s emphasis on narrative control, you’ll likely appreciate how Fortune Row tightens the wheel without cranking up the complexity. If you were hoping for a heavy tactical overhaul, you may find Fortune Row to be more of a polish than a overhaul, which is exactly what most City of Mist fans want from a district-focused expansion.

## Character Creation and Story Hooks
Character creation in Fortune Row remains faithful to the spirit of City of Mist: you are a mythic persona wearing a modern life. The new masks add fresh flavors to the character palette, but they don’t force a new character archetype on you. Here are some pointers to get started:

- Start with the tone: Are you chasing a personal myth that ties to the district, or are you pursuing a larger city-wide fate? Fortune Row rewards characters who lean into both questions, because the district thrives on interconnected stories.
- Align with a local faction: Fortune Row’s new factions offer ready-made hooks for relationships, rivalries, and power dynamics. Your character might owe a debt to a street-press syndicate or have a long-standing rivalry with a fortune-teller clan. These connections provide both complication and opportunity.
- The balance of masks: The book’s masks encourage both masks exploration and story-driven complications. Expect prompts that help players justify why their Mask exists in this district and what mythic impulse fuels their actions.

If you’re a GM who loves a clean introduction, Fortune Row includes a short starter arc that uses a few signature masks, a handful of district characters, and a mystery that unfurls like a neon banner in a storm. If you’re a player who enjoys the idea of weaving personal myth with city-wide rumor, you’ll find these pages friendly and expressive rather than cagey and opaque.

## Factions, Locations, and Local Color
Fortune Row’s world-building earns its keep by giving you a cast of residents who feel real enough to care about. The factions are not merely villains with boats and tomes; they’re social ecosystems: who holds influence, who manipulates narratives, and who credibly claims to know the city’s true history.

Locations are designed as micro-settings that can anchor scenes: a 24-hour diner that doubles as a rumor mill; a rooftop garden that hides a high-stakes poker game run by an enigmatic mask; a disused railway station where myths drift at midnight like old cigarette smoke. The writing balances detail with flexibility: you don’t feel trapped in a single path, yet you have enough color to set a vivid scene within minutes.

If you want a quick table to drop into, Fortune Row includes ready-to-use encounter seeds and a few branching plot hooks. They’re not static; they can be shuffled into your ongoing campaign as easily as a mixtape selection. The net effect is a district that feels alive and reactive, a city that could plausibly get away with its own mythic press release.

## GM Toolkit: Running Fortune Row Live
The GM toolkit is the backbone for anyone running Fortune Row as a dinner-table show or a long campaign. Here are the highlights:

- Scene pacing guidance: How to keep sessions tight and cinematic without burning through plot too quickly.
- Mystery generation: Quick prompts to create an inscrutable mystery or an obvious misdirection, depending on your players’ appetite for clue-hunting.
- Sanity checks and mythic consequences: Not every mythic choice should be a slam dunk. Fortune Row gives you rules of thumb for escalating consequences when players push too hard, while also rewarding clever narrative investments.
- Handling factions and diplomacy: A district with power brokers needs a system for negotiating, betraying, and renegotiating influence. This section provides templates to simulate city politics without bogging you down in spreadsheets.

If you are a GM who loves session flow and scene-building aids, Fortune Row delivers. If you prefer total rule mastery before you play, you’ll need to be patient and treat the toolkit as a living document you adapt to your table.

## Pros and Cons: A Quick Verdict
- Pros:
  - Strong district flavor that feels distinct from other City of Mist settings.
  - Fresh masks and refined mechanics that stay true to the core experience while expanding play options.
  - Practical GM toolkit with ready-to-use scenes, seeds, and pacing guidance.
  - Excellent table presence; the art and layout support immersion without overwhelming the rules.
- Cons:
  - If you hoped for a complete system overhaul, Fortune Row is not that book. It’s an expansion that preserves the core while polishing the edges.
  - Some players may feel the new masks require additional table time to fully explore, which can slow sessions if you’re not careful with pacing.
  - New factions and locations are flavorful but can feel a touch dense to integrate into a fast-moving campaign without planning.

Overall, Fortune Row is a welcome addition for groups who enjoy a city that breathes myth on every block and a game that prizes story over simulation. It’s not a radical shake-up, but it is a sharper, brighter, more confident extension of the City of Mist ethos.

## Comparisons to the Core Rulebook and Other Supplements
If you come from the core rulebook, Fortune Row feels like a well-designed expansion that respects the original design goals: ease of entry, a strong emphasis on story, and a neon-lit sense of place. In comparison to other City of Mist supplements, Fortune Row is leaner on pure crunch and heavier on world-building and session scaffolding. It is particularly strong for groups who want a dedicated district to explore in depth, as opposed to a broad collection of new settings.

For players who prefer pre-built arcs to shuffle into sessions, Fortune Row’s encounter seeds and micro-scenarios are a godsend. For GMs who love building out an urban mythology from the ground up, the book offers a reliable toolkit and a few expertly crafted prompts that can spark weeks of gameplay without needing to write every scene from scratch.

If you want to see how Fortune Row stacks up against other City of Mist expansions, the best comparison is mood and utility: Fortune Row is polished, flavored, and practical. It doesn’t just tell you what the city is; it shows you how to make it feel alive at your table.

## Final Recommendation
Fortune Row is a strong addition to the City of Mist catalog. It does right by fans who crave a richer, more defined district; it gives GMs a set of practical tools to run sessions with fewer rule-checks and more narrative momentum; and it remains accessible to new players who are still getting their feet wet with the Mythos-Legos blend. If you’re looking for a focused, mood-forward expansion that enhances the City of Mist experience without turning the game into a math puzzle, Fortune Row earns its place on your shelf.

That said, if you’re chasing a revolution rather than a refinement, you might want to temper expectations. Fortune Row is not the system upgrade you might dream of; it’s a masterfully crafted extension that nudges the game toward a more cohesive district-centric playstyle. For most groups, that’s the sweet spot: enough new content to spark your imagination, enough structure to keep sessions sailing smoothly, and enough neon to light up the streets of your city when the night falls.

To help you decide, here are two quick playtest notes:

- If you like to anchor sessions around a single neighborhood vibe and have a few signature masks you want to explore in depth, Fortune Row will click with you immediately.
- If your group thrives on sprawling arcs with sprawling cast lists, you’ll want to select a subset of Fortune Row’s factions and scenes to avoid table fatigue.
