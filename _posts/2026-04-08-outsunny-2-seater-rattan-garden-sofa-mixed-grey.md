## Weather Resistance and Maintenance: Keeping It Looking Sharp
Outdoor furniture isn’t a one-and-done purchase. The sun, rain, wind, and the occasional sunscreen spill require a little maintenance to keep everything looking as sharp as day one. The mixed grey color helps hide minor dirt and minor weather flecks; the deeper tones don’t show water stains as readily as lighter finishes. Still, you’ll want to check cushions regularly for moisture buildup and consider storing them indoors during heavy rain or winter months, if possible. The typical care routine involves a light wipe-down with a damp cloth and a quick check of the cushions’ zipper and covers (if they’re removable) for signs of wear or fraying.

For the rattan frame, a rinse with a gentle soap solution and a soft brush will usually dissolve dirt that the wind leaves behind. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the weave over time; you don’t want to trade one weathering issue for another. If you live in a humid climate, you may want to spray a light coating of UV-safe sealant to maintain color and prevent cracking from prolonged sun exposure. It’s a bit like applying sunscreen, but to your furniture, which is a reasonable upgrade for a patio resident.

Cushions, if included, typically come with removable covers. If they’re not removable, you can still address stains using fabric-safe cleaners, but always test on a small hidden area first. A good practice is to store cushions indoors during heavy rain or winter to protect the filling from moisture—this dramatically increases their lifespan and keeps that fresh, plump look you crave.

### Cushion Care and Storage Tips
- Rotate cushions regularly so wear is even across the surface.
- Use a light fabric protector if available to help repel spills.
- Store cushions in a dry space or use waterproof covers when not in use for extended periods.
- If the cushions smell after a long season, a gentle sun-and-air-dry can work wonders, as long as you don’t leave them out in the rain after the sun goes down.
