## Maintenance: Cleaning Without a Degree in Chemistry

Let’s talk maintenance without boring you into nap-town. Cleaning an outdoor rattan set is straightforward:

- Wipe down with a damp cloth or a gentle brush to remove dust and debris.
- For tougher stains, mix mild soap with warm water and wipe, then rinse with clean water. Do not soak the cushions in water; you’ll be performing an accidental science experiment on your furniture. 
- Remove cushion covers and wash according to label instructions. Air dry only; tumble dryers are not your friend here.
- For the glass top, use a standard glass cleaner and a microfiber cloth to avoid streaks. If you’re feeling fancy, spray a little citrus oil on the rattan to boost that outdoorsy glow and ward off wooden furniture envy from the neighbors.

Storage is simple: during long periods of rain or winter, store cushions indoors or in a dry shed. The rattan strands are durable, but like any material, they’ll thank you for keeping them out of continuous moisture or wildly fluctuating temperatures. In a pinch, you can use furniture covers to protect it from dust and pollen, especially in spring when your patio doubles as a pollen field.
