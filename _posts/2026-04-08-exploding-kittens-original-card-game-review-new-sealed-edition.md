## How to play in a nutshell

### Quick setup
1. Shuffle the deck and remove a few cards from the game if you want a shorter session per person. In a standard 2 to 5 player game this usually means a full deck goes around until every player has a handful of cards.
2. Each player draws a starting hand of seven cards. You might feel good about this, or you might realize you just handed your future self a lose condition in the shape of a cat card.
3. Exploding Kitten cards are mixed into the deck according to the number of players. Defuse cards are kept in a safe place to be drawn and used when the kitten explodes in your face.
4. The remaining deck forms the draw pile.

### Turn structure
On your turn you have options, and you will be tempted to use more actions than you should, because we all love chaos. The main actions are played from your hand. See the Future cards peek at the top three draw cards so you can plan your next moves. Attack cards skip a player, forcing the next player to take two turns. Skip cards dodge your draw entirely for that turn. And Defuse cards, well, you already know where those go.

After you have played cards or passed on your turn, you draw one card from the deck. The goal is to avoid the exploding kitten draw while maintaining a reasonable hand so you can keep playing and messing with your friends.

### The moment of truth
If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you do not have a Defuse card, your character is out of the game and the fun continues with the remaining players. If you do have a Defuse card, you can save your skin and place the Exploding Kitten back into the draw pile in a location of your choosing after you defuse. This is the moment where your friends realize that luck is a fickle, dramatic beast and you just managed to trick fate by using a cat card and a strategy that you claim is a noble art.
