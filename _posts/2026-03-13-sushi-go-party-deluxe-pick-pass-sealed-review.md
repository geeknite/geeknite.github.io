## How to Play: The Core Mechanics
### The Pick & Pass rhythm
The core mechanism is simple on the surface and delightfully fiendish in practice: each round you draft a card from your hand, then pass the rest to your neighbor. You continue this pick-and-pass process until all cards are drafted. Score at the end of the round (using the dishes you’ve collected). In Sushi Go Party! Deluxe, the menu cards add various scoring rules—things like pairs, triplets, or unique icon combinations—so you’ll be chasing sets, timed bonuses, and occasional single-card glory.

This is not a game that wants your life story; it wants your attention for about 15–20 minutes of furious calculation and the occasional strategic shrug when someone snipes your coveted maki roll with that smug grin you only reserve for the last biscuit in the tin.

### The scoring dance
Scoring in Sushi Go Party! is where the mother-in-law of game systems shows up and insists you quietly admire the logic. Different dishes score in different ways: some reward duplicates, some reward diverse sets, and others reward a specific combination of cards. The Deluxe edition’s expanded menu keeps each round fresh and avoids the trap of “same-turn syndrome,” where you endlessly chase the same four-point combo across every playthrough. You’ll need to balance immediate gains with long-term potential, which is basically what every adult does in life—just with fewer sashimi tokens and more arguments about who gets to host the next game night.

## Strategy and Tips for Maximum Fun
### Beginners: quick-start guidance
- Prioritize variety. In the Deluxe edition, a diverse hand often yields more scoring opportunities than chasing one big combo.
- Watch what others take. The pass mechanic creates meta-information that you can use to predict what remains when your hand returns to you.
- Don’t chase a single highest-point card if it will block multiple scoring opportunities for others. In a party game, blocking is sometimes more delicious than collecting.

### Intermediate players: weaving combos
- Build synergy between cards that share tokens or icons. The menu cards often reward combinations of these features, so map a path where your picks align with a feasible route across rounds.
- Use early rounds to set up a flexible plan for later rounds. The later rounds are where the true calculator—your brain—gets to do the heavy lifting.
- Remember to adapt. If someone is aggressively targeting a particular dish, pivot to a secondary plan that still nets you points without a showdown you’re bound to lose.

### Advanced play: edge and counterplay
- Create micro-alliances for a round if your group allows it. If your group is comfortable with light banter and playful table talk, you can leverage a “exchange” moment that benefits both players without causing real drama.
- Exploit timing windows. Some scoring cards become available only in certain rounds. Time your picks to maximize these windows without leaking your plan too early.
- Don’t forget the joy of near-misses. Sometimes not scoring as much as you could yields a surprising strategic advantage later because you left the table with more flexible options.
