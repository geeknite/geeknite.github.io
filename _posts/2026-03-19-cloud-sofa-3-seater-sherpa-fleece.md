## Practicality and Maintenance

### Cleaning and Care

Sherpa fabric does require some regular care to maintain its fluff and shape. A gentle vacuum with an upholstery attachment helps keep the pile looking fresh, and a lint roller can be a quick fix for stray pet hair or dust bunnies. For spills, blot carefully with a clean cloth and avoid vigorous rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the fibers. Some owners report that light surface staining can be mitigated with a fabric-safe cleaner; however, test on a hidden area first to avoid pigment transfer. Sound like a job for a sofa that’s going to be loved by children and adults alike? It is. The upside is the fabric’s resilience and forgiving nature when stains are caught early.

### Longevity and Wear

In real-world usage, the Cloud Sofa shows typical wear patterns after a year or two in a busy household. The cushions retain their plumpness fairly well, though you may see minor flattening in the seat surface if you spend a disproportionate amount of time watching marathon seasons of your favorite show. Rotating cushions occasionally helps distribute the load and keep the seat feeling balanced. The frame remains sturdy through typical daily use, with no creaks or wobbles reported by owners who treat their furniture with respect and not as a trampoline during kids' play sessions.

### Maintenance Tips from the Geek Kitchen Sink

- Vacuum weekly with a soft brush attachment to keep the sherpa pile fluffy.
- Fluff cushions monthly to maintain loft and bounce.
- Use a fabric-safe protector spray if you have small children or pets, but do a spot test first.
- Keep the sofa away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading over long periods.
