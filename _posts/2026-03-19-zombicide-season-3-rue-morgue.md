## Introduction
Zombicide has a track record of turning your living room into a garage-sale of limbs and heroic blunders, and Rue Morgue, the Season 3 entry, does not disappoint. If you thought your table was safe from menace, think again Rue Morgue drags you into a labyrinth of alleyways where the only thing that runs faster than the zombies is your sanity.

![Rue Morgue box art](assets/images/zombicide-season-3-rue-morgue-box.jpg)

## What's in the box?
In this box you get a modular Rue Morgue district, new survivors with quirked-up personalities, a deck of mission cards that can be as benevolent as a grandma's knitting and as cruel as a cat in a sunbeam, plus a few new zombie types to ruin your day. The production values are solid: thick punchboard tiles, glossy card stock, and minis that beg you to spray paint them with your expert skill.

## Setup and first impressions
When you crack the seal, the scent of factory-seal plastic hits you like a wave. The Rue Morgue box is full of tiles that click together like a jigsaw puzzle designed by a sadist with a map of New Orleans. The first game is How much tea can you spill on your casualties before you die? The answer, of course, depends on your dice luck and your ability to pretend you are a tactician.

## Gameplay core loop
Zombicide is cooperative; you and your friends are a team of survivors trying to escape a zombie-infested city block. Each turn, players perform a few actions: Move, Search, Fight, and sometimes Aid a friend in the line of fire. The Rue Morgue box introduces a few new twists: alleyways that require careful maneuvering, doors that may only yield if you pass a test, and event cards that add curveballs at unpredictable moments.

### New features in Season 3
- The Rue Morgue map block uses a new layout with 3-4 connected streets and dead-end alleys.
- New zombie types with special behaviors to complicate planning.
- A campaign book that ties Season 3 to earlier seasons through linked scenarios.

## Solo mode and playability
This box plays great at 1-3 players, and with the standard solo rules you can simulate the rest of the team with a capable app or by controlling multiple survivors yourself. If you enjoy solving a puzzle while your friends roll dice and scream, this is your jam. It scales nicely with player count, offering more opportunities to mess up your pathfinding.

## Components and quality
The plastic minis are crisp and the paint lines crisp on the cards. The tiles feel sturdy, and the rulebook is approachable even after a night of caffeinated gaming. There is something satisfying about prying a zombie off your survivor and hearing the plastic groan in resignation.

## Art direction and theme
Rue Morgue embraces a moody, noir feel, with streetlamps cutting through fog and shadows that hide more than just zombies. The art direction helps you invest in the narrative: you are not just blasting undead at random; you are fending off threats in a city block that feels alive, maybe too alive.

## Campaign structure and replayability
Season 3s Rue Morgue introduces a campaign structure that encourages repeated play, with consequences that carry over across missions. By mixing up survivor skills and mission order, you can create a new experience each time you break out this box. If you are into choose-your-own-adventure with zombies, you’ll feel right at home.

## Comparison to Season 1 and 2
If you have played Season 1 and 2, Rue Morgue changes the tempo a bit. It leans more into stealth and area control rather than raw brute force, though there is plenty of brute force if you line up the dice just right. The balance between luck and strategy is still there: you will feel clever when you pull off a combo, and foolish when you roll a critical miss.

## How to play quick-start guide
- Set up the Rue Morgue district on the table.
- Place your survivors and spawn zombies according to the scenario.
- On your turn, spend actions to move, search, and fight.
- Use event cards to add obstacles and threats.
- Complete the mission objective to win; if all survivors are reduced to negative mood points, it is a loss.

## Final verdict
Rue Morgue is a strong Season 3 entry that emphasizes a more tactical approach, careful map navigation, and a moodier theme. If you enjoyed the earlier seasons and want a box that challenges you to think, plan, and adapt on the fly, this is a winner. It is not perfect; if you are allergic to modular tiles and a heavy rulebook, you might want to pass; but for most co-op fans, Rue Morgue delivers a satisfying and replayable experience.

Bright spots:
- Moody theme with strong production values
- Smart map design that rewards planning and adaptation
- Solid solo mode and campaign potential

Areas for improvement:
- Some mission variation could be more accessible to new players
- A few scenarios can feel luck-dependent due to event cards

## Final recommendation
If you are a fan of Zombicide and want a Season 3 box that feels like a proper expansion rather than a lazy retread, Rue Morgue is a strong choice. It adds depth to the co-op puzzle-solving and gives you a fresh city block to explore, with antagonists that punish reckless bravado.
