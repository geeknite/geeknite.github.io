## Practical performance: cleaning, care, and everyday wear
Care is simple but not mystical. Vacuum with a brushless attachment to avoid lurid fuzzing at the heel. If you spill something, blot with a clean cloth rather than rubbing; rubbing invites shiny coffee into your life. For stains, a mild detergent solution works well; test on a small hidden area first to confirm colorfastness. Avoid soaking the rug. Let it dry flat to maintain the integrity of the canvas backing and keep the pile from becoming a moody, warped sculpture.

If you’re worried about colorfastness, you can consult general rug care guides from reputable sources such as HGTV or Better Homes & Gardens, but remember: this rug is designed to be practical in real homes, not in a lab with sterile tiles. ## Comparisons: vs. cotton, wool, or synthetic alternatives
- Cotton-backed cotton-pile runners offer nice breathability but can flatten with heavy use.
- Wool piles tend to be plush and durable but can be pricier and heavier to maintain.
- Synthetic blends (polypropylene, polyester) can be budget-friendly and moisture resistant but may lack the lushness of chenille.

Chenille’s unique texture sits somewhere between softness and durability. It’s not as dense as a high-pile wool rug, but it’s easier to clean than a shag and more forgiving in busy areas. The canvas backing provides a sense of structure that polymer backings sometimes lack.
