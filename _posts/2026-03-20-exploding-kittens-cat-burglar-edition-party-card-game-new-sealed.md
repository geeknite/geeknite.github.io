## How to Play: A Solid Quickstart with Room for Depth
### Quick setup and player count
Cat Burglar Edition typically supports 3 to 6 players, though some households manage to squeeze in a gracious seventh if everyone agrees not to step on the dog’s tail. Setup is a breeze: remove any misprinted apologies from the box, shuffle the deck, distribute character cards if included, and deal a starting hand to each participant. The table should be ready for mischief within minutes. The simplicity makes it an ideal pick for a casual Friday or a pre-D&D snack break where people want something fast and funny.

### Core mechanics: drawing, playing, and dodging explosions
The core loop resembles the classic Exploding Kittens framework: you draw cards, you play cards to manipulate turn order, steal, or dodge danger, and you avoid the dreaded exploding kitten cards that end your run at the table when drawn. The Cat Burglar Edition spices this up with extra actions like burglary-themed card play, sneaky movement, and the occasional “catnip bonus” that grants temporary advantages. It’s a balance of luck and light strategy, with the emphasis on social interaction—watch your neighbors, because bluffing and misdirection are half the fun. Expect a few tense moments where everyone pretends not to notice the obvious play you’re counting on, only for your plan to unravel in the most glorious fashion when the card you least expect hits the table.

### Turn structure and pace
Turns are usually short and snappy, designed for a party vibe rather than a chess match. You’ll have moments where you conspire with others, moments where you double-cross, and moments where your cat clearly judges your life choices from the sidelines. The pacing tends to stay brisk, but there’s enough room for banter, improvised taunts, and the occasional dramatic pause to build suspense before the next draw. If your table thrives on back-and-forth banter, Cat Burglar Edition rewards you with a rhythm that’s both familiar and fresh, offering enough strategic lanes to feel meaningful without requiring a PhD in game theory to win.

### What changes with this edition versus base Exploding Kittens
Compared to the base game, Cat Burglar Edition emphasizes thematic cohesion. There are more flavor cards, more ways to manipulate the draw pile, and a few power cards that reward bold social plays. Some players love how these additions speed up the game and increase the number of “oh wow, I totally saw that coming” moments. Others prefer the more streamlined chaos of the classic edition. If you like a little more character-driven flavor and a touch of maneuvering room, this edition is a solid upgrade. If you want pure rapid-fire chaos with fewer decisions, you might still enjoy the base game more for its compact simplicity. Either way, you’ll get a robust party experience with high laughs-per-minute potential.
