## Durability and Care: Rug Maintenance Without the Fuss
### Cleaning Wool Rugs
Wool is famously resilient when cared for properly. Kos benefits from a straightforward maintenance routine: regular vacuuming with a gentle beater bar setting, rotating the rug every few months to even out wear, and addressing spills promptly with blotting rather than scrubbing. Wool can resist stains better than many synthetics, especially when you treat spills as soon as they happen rather than letting them become part of the rug’s personality.

For stubborn spots, a mild wool-safe cleaner or a DIY solution of water and a touch of wool-safe detergent works well. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could compromise the fibers or the subtle sheen that Kos wears with quiet confidence.

### Spot Cleaning Guide
- Blot, don’t rub. Rubbing pushes the stain deeper and creates friction heat that can damage fibers.
- Use cold water first, then warm water if needed. Hot water can shrink wool and create a less-than-ideal texture.
- For oil-based stains, a small amount of wool-safe solvent can help, followed by a gentle rinse.
- Let air dry completely before putting the rug back into service. A damp rug invites mold and a suspicious smell of damp laundry.

### Long-Term Care Tips
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and to extend life. A pad reduces pile flattening and protects the underside from wear.
- Rotate the rug 180 degrees every season. This ensures even exposure to foot traffic and sunlight, preventing one edge from looking more worn than another.
- Keep direct sunlight at bay when possible. Prolonged sun exposure can fade wool over time, albeit slowly, which is exactly the kind of drama you don’t need in your life.
