## Painting tips and tricks
- Prime with a neutral gray or black to better control contrast. The cloth textures pick up shading nicely when you go with a mid-tone base and a darker glaze in folds.
- Use a slightly lighter highlight on raised surfaces like knuckles, knifepoints, and weapon bindings to make the figures pop against the table.
- Weathering powders work well on metallic elements and leather straps. If you’re feeling fancy, a tiny pin wash around the gear pouches can imply decades of wear without needing a full weathering kit.
- If you’re painting for display, consider a simple tabletop-friendly scheme with a high-contrast sleeve or cloak. It’s a little trick to draw the eye to the character’s face and posture without going full “prince of dusk.”
