## Setup and maintenance

No review would be complete without discussing how much work it takes to get the board game equivalent of a Rolls-Royce rolling through your living room. Here are practical tips to keep your SereneLife table in top condition.

### Assembly tips
- Clear a large workspace: You’ll be surprised by the number of small pieces that appear during setup. A clean, empty floor is your best friend here.
- Follow the diagram: The manual uses simple visuals. If you’ve ever assembled a bookshelf without instructions, you’ll appreciate how this one reduces the risk of installing a rod backward.
- Check the alignment: Once assembled, ensure the rods have a smooth, straight path. A tiny misalignment can turn a routine pass into a dramatic, wobble-filled arc that would make a rollercoaster envious.

### Maintenance basics
- Regular cleaning: Wipe the playing surface with a dry microfiber cloth to remove dust and fingerprints. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could dull the matte finish.
- Lubrication: The rods are designed for smooth operation; you won’t need an entire laboratory of lubes, but a light wipe-down with a silicone spray (applied lightly) can keep things moving without sticking.
- Cup holders: Empty and dry them after use to prevent any residual liquid from degrading the finish. It’s not a lab experiment—just common sense.
- Ball care: Keep an extra ball or two around for when you inevitably lose one under the couch. It’s a small investment that avoids dramatic bouts of table-top tantrums.

### Storage and room considerations
If you have to move the table for a remodel or a sudden air hockey obsession, the SereneLife setup is still manageable. It’s not as heavy as a true commercial table, but it’s sturdy. When not in use, a proper cloth cover helps shield it from dust and curious pets who believe every surface deserves a test roll. In a home theater environment, the table can double as a dramatic prop for movie night—provided you promise not to chase your cat across the playing field in a heroic moment of “let the ball roll free.”

## Setup tips from the pros (and us, obviously)
Here are a few practical tips to level up your setup experience and your game performance:

- Dial in the shot: Practice straight passes down the center to reduce the number of scrappy bounces that ruin your momentum.
- Master the bank: Hitting the ball off the side walls can create surprising angles. If you’ve got the eye for geometry, you’ll enjoy how subtle angles open up variety in your offense.
- Angular defense: A proactive defense that shifts the rod angle slightly can adapt to fast passes and choked shots. It’s not a miracle, but it’s a cunning, humane way to keep your lead.
- Drink management: The dual cup holders are a blessing and a minor hazard. Practice keeping your beverage steady, or brace for a dramatic tumble when a two-hop pass ricochets off a cup and into an audience member’s lap. Pro tips: a smaller glass or a spill-proof cup can save you from looking like a chaotic experiment in a mid-century sitcom.
