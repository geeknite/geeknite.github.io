## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity
### Quick care routine
Outdoor furniture loves a quick spa day. A light brush to remove dirt, a rinse with a hose, and a quick blot with a dry cloth keeps the rattan weave looking brand-new. Cushions benefit from spot cleaning and occasional air airing to avoid moisture buildup. If you notice a minor weave snag, you can carefully tighten adjacent fibers with a soft hand and avoid over-stressing the weave. A yearly check on the metal frame for rust and tightening bolts will also contribute to longevity. 

### Stain resistance and fabric care
The cushion fabric is built to resist stains, but no fabric is invincible. Treat spills promptly, use a mild soap solution, and avoid harsh chemicals. If you need a longer-term solution, consider applying a fabric protector suitable for outdoor use. Always check the label for compatibility with the cushion material. The wetter climate or coastal air can accelerate corrosion if the metal parts aren’t properly coated or maintained, so keep an eye on joints and screws.

### Storage tips for off-season use
If you have a garage or shed, store cushions when not in use for extended periods. If storage space is tight, consider using breathable storage bags rather than sealed plastic containers, as they prevent mildew growth. For the frame, a light wipe-down and check on the weave are good habits before you tuck the set away for winter.
