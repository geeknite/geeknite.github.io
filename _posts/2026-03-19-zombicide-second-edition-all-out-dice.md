## Introduction: what is All Out Dice?
All Out Dice is an expansion for Zombicide: Second Edition that introduces an additional dice pool with bespoke faces designed to enhance the game’s telltale sense of narrative chaos. The pulse of the expansion is that dice are not merely numbers; they are narrative devices. The new dice add special faces that trigger unique effects, modify damage, or spice up movement. Think of them as a mini toolkit for injecting randomness with a twist. The core concept is simple: when you roll the All Out Dice, you are rolling a chance for something big to happen—good, bad, or gloriously bizarre.

Add to that a few new mini-dice types, some color-coded for emphasis, and a handful of unique counters to track the swelling intensity of a scenario, and you’ve got a kit that wants to tell a story about your team fighting to survive the night rather than a sterile grind of action tokens and normal hits.

## What’s in the box?
If you have the camera out and the lint roller ready, the All Out Dice box delivers on the promise of “more stuff, more chaos.” Inside you’ll find:
- A set of All Out Dice in multiple colors, each with a distinct face layout.
- A compact reference card that clarifies what each face means in the context of your current survivor and zombie mix.
- A handful of token stickers and quick-play aids to help you track the effects that the new faces trigger during a session.
- A small rulebook addendum that covers how these dice interact with the baseline rules and the existing Base Game expansions.

The component quality is solid, with the color palette chosen to avoid confusion around what each die is supposed to do in the heat of a tense round. The dice themselves feel well-balanced in the hand, and they chime nicely when you rattle them on the table (which might be the cheesiest satisfaction you’ll get from a plastic cube this side of a marble shooter).

## The dice mechanics: how it changes the game tempo
The All Out Dice introduces faces that interact with specialized effects tied to the base game’s objectives and zombie behavior. There are faces that explode into burst movements, some that reassign damage, and others that trigger morale shifts for your survivors. The intent is to add volatility that players can lean on but must manage. In practice, dice outcomes can turn a routine turn into a dramatic swing, especially when multiple players’ actions collide with a handful of dice rolls that want to narrate the scene in bold, loud chalk lines on a city map.

There are a few clear patterns to the new faces:
- Momentum faces: These add extra actions or allow a second action to be taken in the same round, which can accelerate the pace of a campaign turn.
- Catastrophe faces: These ramp up zombie spawns or modify how injuries are assigned. They are the adrenaline shot in the pulse of a nightmare city.
- Support faces: Occasionally the dice can grant healing, temporary buffs, or reroll opportunities. It’s a little mercy in the middle of a horde’s manic dance.

The interplay is the secret sauce. The base game already thrives on risk management—each decision is a window into how much you’re willing to gamble with your survivors. All Out Dice doesn’t replace that tension; it reframes it. Now your risk calculus includes a secondary engine of chance and luck that can amplify both the heroics and the calamities you’re likely to see on a typical night.

## How the dice affect team strategy and turn pacing
Strategically, the All Out Dice can influence several core choices:
- Zone control becomes more dynamic: You’ll tend to split your survivors into a few defensive lanes and a few agile movers, ready to chain effects from the dice to maximize mood and movement. The dice encourage tactical nudges toward high-risk, high-reward plays.
- Resource management takes on new dimensions: With potential extra actions or healing turns, players may re-prioritize which weapons or items to hold onto for the long fight rather than rushing all-in for an instant win. It’s a subtle but meaningful nudge toward longer-term planning rather than single-turn heroics.
- Coordination becomes trickier in the best possible way: When multiple effects can occur in the same round, players must coordinate to maximize positive outcomes while mitigating shared risks. This adds a social puzzle to every turn that is absent in a more straightforward dice mechanic.

In solo play, All Out Dice provides both a boon and a dragon: it can intensify game tempo and bring more variability, but it can also feel a little more punishing if you’re chasing a specific objective with a narrow path. If you enjoy a tight, methodical solo run, you’ll want to embrace the randomness; if you’re chasing a predictable beat, you may prefer to keep the expansion under a lid until your nerve is ready for a wild ride.

## Setup, components quality, and table presence
Setup is straightforward: you add the All Out Dice to the standard setup bag and follow the quick reference for any card or token interactions that are new or modified. The rule changes are intentionally minimal in text but substantial in practice; the expansion is designed to be drop-in compatible with minimal table-space overhead. If your play area is a crowded kitchen table with magnets, drinks, and a napkin fort, you’ll appreciate the streamlined layout and quick-resolve nature of most All Out effects.

Component quality remains in line with Zombicide’s current production standards. The dice are crisp, the tokens are clear, and the reference card is legible at standard table distances. The color-coding deters confusion during the maelstrom of a late-night zombie surge. The included insert for storage is adequate, though not revolutionary. As a reviewer who appreciates tiny quality-of-life touches, I’d have loved a slightly larger insert or a dedicated tray for the new faces and tokens to avoid misplacing a critical piece mid-campaign.

You may be asking how the dice feel at the table when a fresh wave of walkers is marching through your line. The answer has two parts: the emotional payoff and the mechanical payoff. The emotional payoff comes from the sense that luck is acting with you or against you in a way that feels cinematic rather than spreadsheet-driven. The mechanical payoff is typically a handful of additional actions or a sudden surge of zombie activity that tilts the balance toward a more dynamic clash. Both feelings are present in roughly equal measure, which is a rare treat for a dice expansion.

## Solo play and campaign flow
If you’re into solo Zombicide, All Out Dice is a mixed bag—but a rewarding one. It adds depth to a single-player climb through a night-city of undead, but the randomness can sometimes push you toward riskier gambits that intensify the grind. The more you play, the more you will learn to orchestrate a micro-saga where each dice result ties into a larger narrative arc—almost like a tabletop RPG-lite embedded inside a cooperative board game. The campaign mode can become a little chess-like as you plan for the next scenario with a mix of expected outcomes and the unpredictable dice results. The flavor is strong; the replayability is high, and the tension remains a consistent driver that makes each session feel distinct.

For two players, the expansion shines when both survivors are in on a plan that leverages the All Out faces to maximize timing. Communication becomes more critical as you attempt to sequence the dice-driven effects across turns. Three or four players will enjoy the extra chaos, but you’ll want to keep an eye on pacing to avoid the whole night devolving into a chaotic scramble that drags on longer than the actual zombie apocalypse on screen.

## Thematic vibe and humor
Zombicide has always done a good job of balancing bleak survival with moments of levity. All Out Dice leans into that duality hard. The new faces often produce comedic, absurd, or cinematic moments: a survivor pulls off a dramatic sprint, a zombie meta-gnaw bites into the air with a puncturing scream, or a lucky reroll saves a position that would otherwise be a wipeout. The dice become a storytelling device as much as a tactical tool. If your group loves storytelling elements in tabletop games—where a good narrative beat can overshadow a perfect dice roll—All Out Dice delivers a satisfying, meme-worthy experience without sacrificing core strategy.

On the downside, the same chaotic faces can feel unfair in the moment. If you’re typically playing with a group that prizes precise optimization, the random face outcomes can sometimes derail what you thought was a solid plan. The balance of “fun chaos” versus “frustrating randomness” is subjective, but the designers have clearly leaned into the fun side of the equation.

## Re-playability and long-term value
All Out Dice adds a meaningful layer of variability that increases a replay value curve for Zombicide: Second Edition. If you are a regular player who has bled the base game dry in a few weeks, this expansion offers a reason to re-engage with the map and the walkers with a new lens. Even if you’ve run every scenario multiple times, the dice introduce enough new outcomes to keep scenarios feeling fresh. If you own other expansions, you’ll find some synergies and some counterbalances—the expansion doesn’t feel isolated; it integrates with the wider Zombicide ecosystem in a way that respects the design language of the base game.

There’s also the potential for house rules. Some groups adopt a “dice fatigue” policy to curb the most punishing effects after an unlucky run. Other groups lean into the roll-with-it mentality and craft mini-arcs around the standout dice moments. This openness to house rules is a strength, not a weakness, because it invites players to tailor the chaos to their preferred playstyle rather than forcing everyone into a single rhythm.

## Pros and cons in a nutshell
Pros:
- Adds meaningful variability without overpowering the base game’s balance.
- Enhances narrative tension and table talk with memorable dice faces.
- Strong value for replayability with varied outcomes across sessions.
- Integrates smoothly with existing expansions and base game mechanics.

Cons:

- Setup and tracking for some effects can add minor table noise during play sessions.

If you genuinely enjoy the core engine and crave more storytelling-driven turns, this expansion is likely to become a staple in your rotation. If you prefer a lean, minimal setup with a predictable pacing, you may be better off sticking with the base game or selecting a more restrained expansion.

## Practical tips to maximize your All Out Dice night
- Establish a quick-reference ritual: at the start of the game, everyone gets a moment to check the new faces and whisper their own plans for how the dice will influence their turns. It helps with coordination and reduces analysis paralysis mid-round.
- Place a dedicated dice cup or tray right in the center. The All Out Dice deserve their own place at the table; rotating around a single generic cup can lead to confusion and accidental misfires.
- Use a timer for multi-player rounds to keep pacing in check. The dice can create a snowball effect of extra actions that slow things down; a gentle timebox keeps the session cinematic but not interminable.
- Consider a house rule: allow once-per-game rerolls on critical turns. It’s a fun nod to narrative heroism and helps redemption arcs when a bad dice roll threatens to derail a scenario.
- For solo play, seed your runs with goals beyond simply surviving. Create a micro-story arc for your survivor group and let the All Out Dice help you push the narrative toward a cathartic finale.

## Where does All Out Dice fit in a standard night of Zombicide?
If you typically play two to four hours and you enjoy a blend of tense planning and chaotic results, All Out Dice slots into your session as a mid-night spice rather than the main course. It is not a replacement for the strategic cores of the base game but an augmentation that invites risk-taking and dramatic moments. For a table that loves heroic last stands and cinematic escapes, the expansion is a natural fit. For a table that prizes precise scheduling and a predictable path to victory, it might feel a little too capricious to be worth the purchase.

External voices and fans will tell you that the best way to enjoy All Out Dice is to lean into the chaos and let storytelling lead the way. If you’ve got friends who love improv nights and emergency dice rituals, bring them to the table, because this expansion turns your game night into something that feels less like a board game and more like a living, breathing zombie film script playing out in plastic form.

All Out Dice is a well-crafted, characterful expansion that respects Zombicide’s design language while injecting a meaningful dose of unpredictability. It doesn’t rewrite the game into a party game; it enriches it with a new rhythm that can produce both grand fails and glorious wins. The set’s quality, its integration with other content, and the sheer narrative potential make it worth considering for groups who want more story-driven chaos without sacrificing core mechanics.
