## How the rules hold up in a big group

### Quick rules rundown

If you know Exploding Kittens, you know the gist: players take turns playing cards to influence the flow of the deck while trying to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten. A successful Defuse saves you from a fiery end, and the one who outlasts the rest wins. In the Party Pack Edition, the tweaks are designed to scale up for more players without turning into a heavy rule beast.

- Draw one card per turn or play a card that alters the flow. 
- See the Future lets you peek at upcoming cards to plan your moves. 
- Attack shuffles the turn sequence, forcing the next player to take extra actions. 
- Defuse keeps you in the game when luck smiles—or misbehaves—on your behalf.

With up to 10 players, the balance shifts. The deck needs to be depleted more quickly, but so does the capacity for dramatic reveals and bluffing. The new cards give players chances to alter the tempo with minimal setup, which helps keep the fun ticking even as the table grows loud and chaotic.

### House rules and variants

Our testing table loved the idea of a house rule where we allow one extra stack of cards to be swapped mid-game, just to accommodate late arrivals and the inevitable “I forgot we were playing” moment. Another fun variant is a time-boxed round: for example, every 8 minutes, everyone must reveal a silly running joke related to their last turn. The goal is to keep things light and avoid grim silence when someone draws the 10th card and the tension spikes.

If you want to go even deeper, you can experiment with variant win conditions, such as a team mode for teams of two or three, or a free-for-all winner-take-all chase where the Defuse pool acts as a resource the group negotiates. The Party Pack is surprisingly adaptable in this sense, which is one reason it shines at gatherings that blur into a social event rather than a quiet game night.
