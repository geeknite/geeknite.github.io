## What Fortune Row Is and Isn’t

Fortune Row is a hybrid product: a player-centric supplement that pairs with the main rulebook and a companion GM guide. It gives you a dedicated playground for character creation, a set of ready-to-use character sheets tailored to a street level mythic arc, and a suite of tips and tools to keep the narrative pace brisk without sacrificing the fog-tinged vibes that define City of Mist.

What Fortune Row is not is a comprehensive city-wide overhaul. This isn’t a full city primer with 100 new factions and a sprawling civil-engineering diagram of every alley. It is a focused slice. If you want to sprint through a compact arc that feels like a myth in a single district, Fortune Row aims to deliver that with style and some extra crunch you can wield as a GM. If you prefer sprawling city maps with dozens of factions, you may want to treat Fortune Row as a palate cleanser rather than a main course.

The design intent is clear: give players a bank of options that feel iconic and a practical sheet system that keeps things legible during a tight session. The result is a product that rewards prep and collaboration, with enough flavor to spark ideas and enough structure to prevent the table from drifting into a string of improvisational chaos.

## Character Creation in Fortune Row

Character creation remains the heart of any City of Mist product. Fortune Row puts a particular emphasis on the street-level masks that map onto your characters everyday life and the myths they claim or are claimed by.

### The Player’s Guide Approach
The guide starts with a reminder that in City of Mist you are never just a person; you are a living collage of origins, secrets, and legends. In Fortune Row this becomes more focused: your mythic signature is often tied to a local fixture rather than a global god or hero. The prompts lean into urban legends, backstage deals, and neighborhood lore. Players are encouraged to pin a couple of key tags to their character that reflect their allegiance to Fortune Row and their personal myth.

The guide also includes a quick-start path for new players. You can roll into a character from scratch in under an hour by following the prompts and using the provided templates. The prompts cover three axes: origin, emblem (the mythic identity they claim), and arc (the short-term goal that drives the character). The aim is to give you a character that feels distinct, flavorful, and ready to weave with the rest of the table rather than a vanilla stereotype.

### The Fortune Row Playbooks
As you might expect, Fortune Row includes a handful of playbooks that are tuned to the districts vibe. Expect a blend of traditional City of Mist archetypes with a fortune-telling, hustler, or fixer tilt. Some playbooks emphasize street-level cunning, others emphasize mythic echoes that tie to specific neighborhood businesses or micro-communities (think a bakery run by a forgotten trickster, or a taxi driver who is a portal to a legend of transit).

Each playbook comes with suggested entry points, starting moves, and a few ready-to-use tag-tables. The goal is to give players enough texture to feel distinct without forcing them into one clearly defined stereotype. The result is a group of characters that can drift toward rival factions, close personal stakes, or a heist-level caper with equal ease.

### Archetypes and Personal Hooks
Fortune Row makes the most of the urban myth motif by encouraging hooks that tie characters to real places, people, or local legends. You might create a character who talks to a streetwise guardian of a corner bakery, or someone whose power is triggered by a recurring street mural that changes over time. The hooks feel organic to the setting, which is a win for tables that want to stay close to the source material and avoid a raft of generic super-skill pilots.

The guide also suggests a few non-traditional twists, such as characters whose myths surface only under specific social conditions or who operate primarily in the background, letting the city do the heavy lifting while they orchestrate the plan. If you enjoy playing with moral ambiguity and social stakes as much as PnP combat, Fortune Row offers a playground that rewards creative thinking and collaborative storytelling.

### Character Sheets: Design and Usability
The character sheets are nothing flashy but sturdy. They feature clearly delineated spaces for the three masks a player can embody in City of Mist: the human veneer, the mythic ego, and the external tag that links to the setting (the neighborhood or the criminal organization, for example). You will find spots for your Luck or Fortune meters, a grid for your tags with short descriptors, and a place to note special moves or stunts that you unlock along the way.

One thoughtful touch is how the sheets encourage a narrative spine. There are dedicated lines for a short backstory, a driving question, and a handful of prompts that you can fill in between sessions. This minimizes the post-game fatigue and keeps your next session moving forward rather than grinding to a halt while players remember who they are and why they care about Fortune Row.


## Thematic Tools and Rules for Fortune Row

Fortune Row brings a few thematic tools that help the GM weave local color into the game without bloating the rulebook. The most visible is the local fortune mechanic that ties to the districts lore. It is a simple, elegant system that determines how much luck or misfortune you can lean on during a given scene. Players can spend fortune points to bend outcomes, but doing so often reveals a little more about the myth behind the character, which in turn deepens the narrative impact.

Another tool is the neighborhood map concept. Fortune Row focuses less on a global map of a city and more on a cluster of key locales that drive scenes. This is ideal for one-shot or short arc campaigns, and it scales nicely to longer games if your table enjoys slowly expanding the myth while staying anchored to a few iconic locations.

There are also tags and engine tweaks that help players and GMs align on tone. You will find suggestions for how to handle tense social scenes, how to escalate stakes in a way that feels organic to the mythic framework, and how to use allegiances to spin moral conflict into narrative momentum.

Overall the system remains recognizably City of Mist: a hybrid of myth and modernity, where a character rarely acts in a vacuum and the city around them acts back with flexible, story-forward consequences. Fortune Row emphasizes the street-level vibe, but it still leaves plenty of room for the grand, cosmic threads that City of Mist thrives on.


## Running Fortune Row: GM Tips

If you are running a Fortune Row session, here are some practical tips that map well to the books keys:

- Start with a hook that ties a local landmark to a legend. A bakery that curses bread after midnight, a bar that hosts a mythical open mic night, a taxi driver who is rumored to ferry souls around a legal gray area. Short, evocative hooks make it easier for players to latch onto a personal story rather than a generic hero journey.
- Use the fortune mechanic to create momentum. Fortunes should move the plot in ways players can influence, but also in ways that reveal something new about their character or the city. The GM can lean into the payoff of a well-timed fortune spend by weaving a new relationship, a betrayal, or an unexpected ally into the scene.
- Encourage short flashbacks to reveal the myths behind the characters. Fortune Row supports this with prompts that feel cinematic without derailing the present action. A well-placed flashback can alter the odds, the stakes, and the emotional color of a scene.
- Lean into the neighborhood ecosystem. The city is not just a backdrop; its power is in the way it folds back into the characters lives. Let locations be characters in their own right, with agendas and memories that intersect with the players goals.
- Keep the tone nimble. Fortune Row works best when the session balances noir mood with moments of levity. A bad pun at the right moment can lighten the heaviness and give players a sense of belonging in a world that wears a grimace as a badge of honor.

## Practical Play Examples: Mini Scenarios

To give you a feel for what Fortune Row enables, here are three quick scenario ideas you could run with a Fortune Row character group:

1) The Midnight Baker and the Cursed Crust: A neighborhood bakery holds a secret, and the bread literally whispers secrets to those who eat it. Players must decide whether to protect the bakery from an rival faction or exploit the bread for a bid at urban influence. The myth becomes a shared puzzle about trust and appetite.
2) The Neon Choir of Empty Street: A club has a mythic choir whose songs alter senses and memory. The players must navigate the chorus, the chorus masters, and a local politician who wants to harness the chant for a certain agenda. This scenario uses social tension and a dash of musical magic.
3) The Ledger on Two Blocks: A street ledger, written by an ancient trickster, reappears across the city in unexpected locations. Each page reveals a small fortune or misfortune and forces the players to choose between helping one neighbor at a cost to another. It is a test of loyalty and consequence.

These prompts are not hard rules. They are seeds you can adopt or discard. The design intent is to provide shared narrative soil where the players can plant their own legends and watch them grow, mutated by the realities of Fortune Row and the city at large.

## Production Value and Final Thoughts

Fortune Row is a strong entry for players who crave a compact, flavorful hook into the City of Mist universe. The character sheets are practical and resilient for the table, the player guide provides enough direction to prevent decision paralysis, and the overall package nails the mood without forcing tone that would feel artificial to play. It does not attempt to reinvent the wheel; instead, it polishes a segment of the wheel so it spins smoother in the dark streets of Fortune Row.

The combined effect is a supplement that is worth the price for groups who enjoy a fast, character-forward play experience with a heavy emphasis on setting and social texture. It is particularly well-suited for monthly sessions or smaller groups who want a cinematic, mythic vibe without the overbearing complexity some city-wide expansions bring to the table.

On the downside, if you are chasing a sprawling, city-shaking campaign with dozens of factions, Fortune Row may feel a touch narrow. It is an excellent supplement for what it is designed to be, but its strengths lie in the micro rather than the macro. This is not a bug; it is a design choice. Use Fortune Row to anchor a story beat or a mini-arc, then pivot back to the broader city if your group wants to expand again.

If you already have a fondness for City of Mist and enjoy the spiky charm of urban legends, you will likely find Fortune Row to be a welcome addition. It is not just a new set of sheets; it is a refined lens that helps players see the citys mythic underbelly with renewed clarity and a dash of neon glitter.
