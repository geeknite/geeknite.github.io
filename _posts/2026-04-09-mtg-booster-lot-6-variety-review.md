## Practical concerns: value, safety, and authenticity

Authenticity is a reasonable concern when buying any booster lot, especially online. Here are some practical checks to keep you from accidentally supporting counterfeit card farms or dubious merchandise:

- Buy from reputable sellers with clear return policies and transparent packaging photos. If the listing smells fishy or uses stock images without a real photo, proceed with caution.
- Inspect the packaging upon receipt for tampering. If you can see that a pack was opened or resealed, you should treat it as suspicious. MTG cards are pretty resilient, but you don’t want to be the person who ends up with a pack re-sealed with a different rare card stuffed inside.
- Compare card backs and engravings. If anything looks off—wrong border, odd font, or misaligned print quality—pause and investigate. The good news is that MTG cards are relatively robust against low-effort counterfeiting, but not immune.

Storage is another safety concern. Cards should be kept in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Card sleeves and protectors can help preserve condition for trading or grading later on. If you’re into long-term investment, you’ll want to consider storage boxes with dividers to keep your collection organized by set and color, rather than dumping everything into a single pile like a chaotic mage touting chaos magic.
