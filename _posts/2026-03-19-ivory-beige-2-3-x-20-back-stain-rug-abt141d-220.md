For long runners, a good rug pad is non negotiable. A pad helps reduce slip, softens foot traffic, and prolongs the rug life by decreasing friction against the subfloor. A well-chosen pad can drastically improve the user experience, turning an ordinary hallway into a refined passageway.

## Maintenance and care guide

### Routine care

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to preserve fiber integrity.
- Use a rug pad to reduce wear and keep the rug anchored in place.
- Rotate occasionally to distribute wear and sun exposure evenly.

### Spill response

- Blot with a clean white cloth, avoiding rubbing motions that could push stain deeper into the fibers.
- For most household spills, a mild cleaner with water will do. Test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure there is no color fade.
- For stubborn stains, consider a targeted fiber-safe cleaner following the product directions. Avoid harsh solvents that can degrade the backing or color.

### Deep cleaning schedule

- Professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months is a good baseline for high traffic rooms.
- For odor management, a deodorizing cleaner designed for synthetic fibers can help without leaving a heavy residue.
- Keep direct sunlight exposure in check to prevent color shift over time.
