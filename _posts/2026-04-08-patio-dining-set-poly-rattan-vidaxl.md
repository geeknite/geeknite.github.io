## What is poly rattan and why should you care?
### The tech behind the weave
Poly rattan is synthetic wicker, spun from durable plastics or resins that mimic the look of natural rattan without the fragility. Think of it as the cybernetic version of wicker: beautiful, weather-resistant, and capable of surviving your backyard experiments with a sprinkler, a stray garden hose, and a questionable attempt at a solar oven.

In the VidaXL set, the poly rattan is woven around a sturdy metal frame (usually powder-coated to resist rust). The result is a lightweight, easy-to-move ensemble that won’t crumble if you sneeze at it or forget to bring in the cushions during a sudden spring storm. The tabletop is typically tempered glass, which adds a sleek, modern touch while keeping the surface easy to clean after a midnight snack run.

### Why poly over natural wicker?
- Weather resistance: Rain, sun, humidity — poly rattan shrugs it off.
- Maintenance: A quick wipe-down beats weekly re-rattling of real wicker.
- Longevity: The synthetic weave has a longer lifespan in outdoor environments than natural materials that fade to a sad, pale straw color after a season.

Of course, there’s a trade-off. Poly rattan isn’t as “green” as bamboo, and some purists will insist that natural materials age with a certain romantic patina. But for the geek with a sunburn and a dwindling patience for maintenance, poly rattan is mostly a win.

## Weather resistance and care tips
Outdoor furniture is a long-term relationship, not a one-night stand. Poly rattan is surprisingly forgiving, but there are rules for keeping it looking sharp.

Care routine:
- Regular cleaning with a soft brush or cloth and mild soapy water removes grime and pollen.
- Rinse with clean water and air dry; avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the weave.
- For cushions, store them during heavy rain or use weatherproof covers; even UV-stable fabrics can fade over time with prolonged exposure.
- Inspect the frame annually for loose screws; tighten as needed to maintain stability and prevent wobble that could turn a quiet al fresco dinner into a physics demonstration about torque.

Storage strategies:
- If you live in a climate with long winters or heavy precipitation, store the cushions indoors or in a dry container during the off-season.
- Use a breathable cover to protect the weave from mildew and the cushions from weighty moisture, which can creep into seams and shorten cushion life.
