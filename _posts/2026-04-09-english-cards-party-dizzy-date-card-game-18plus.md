## Gameplay mechanics: how the chaos unfolds

The core mechanic is simple in bold font: players draw cards, respond, and either receive a friendly penalty or a reward, depending on the prompt and the surrounding dynamics. The prompts are designed to be accessible to a broad group, but also to nudge players out of their comfort zones—because sometimes your friends need a reminder that they are indeed capable of saying something ridiculous with confidence.

### Round formats
There are a few different modes you can adopt, depending on your vibe:

- Classic party mode: players draw English prompts and respond with a story or a truth crafted to entertain the table. This mode leans into conversation and storytelling more than dares, which makes it friendlier for newer players.
- Dizzy Date mode: here the romance energy spikes. You’ll encounter prompts that test dating banter, ability to read signals, and your willingness to reveal opinions you’d normally mask with sarcasm. It’s bold, it’s a little spicy, and it can be either charming or cringe, depending on your crew’s chemistry.
- Couples mode: this mode is designed for couples to navigate prompts together or against each other, often rewarding teamwork or punishing miscommunications with a silly consequence.

### Card themes and safety boundaries
The prompts cover a spectrum: silly anecdotes, hypothetical scenarios, truth-adjacent questions, and light dares. The 18+ edition adds a layer of flirtatious or sexual-themed prompts, which can be fun for long-term couples or adults who want a bolder night. We strongly recommend establishing boundaries before you start: what’s off-limits, what counts as a hard limit, and what kind of prompt triggers should be avoided entirely. Consent is not a one-and-done checkbox; it’s a continuous, evolving conversation. If a player isn’t comfortable, the host should offer a skip or a reframe option.

To keep things playful, we suggest a simple safety rule: if a prompt makes someone genuinely uncomfortable, they can use a skip card or choose to swap with a different card without penalty. This keeps the mood buoyant and ensures the game remains inclusive for all players.

## Safety, consent, and good vibes only

Let’s be real for a moment: 18+ content in any social game has a potential to push boundaries. You’re not obligated to engage with anything you don’t want to. The key is ongoing, enthusiastic consent and a group that communicates clearly about what’s comfortable. Here are some practical tips that kept our testing crew afloat:

- Start with soft prompts. If a prompt feels too personal or risky, plastic-wrap it with a playful substitute or a time-based twist (e.g., swap with a less spicy card).
- Use a consent card. If any player uses it, the prompt is automatically replaced with a neutral alternative that keeps the party rolling without pushing physical or emotional boundaries.
- Have a clear “pause” mechanism. A quick disagreement or discomfort should be resolved with a 2-minute break and a promise to check in again after the break.
- Respect pacing. If someone needs a longer break or wants to fade out of the round, you’ve got to respect that. The goal is fun, not coercion.

1) Set a boundary conversation before the first draw. Have everyone verbally agree to a few “no-go” zones. 2) Choose a vibe.
