## What is this 15-card expansion and why should you care?

Streaking Kittens is not just a novelty; it’s a compact toolkit designed to tilt a few dial knobs on the base Exploding Kittens engine without reprinting the whole game. The pack adds a streak mechanic—think of it as a vibe, a mood, or a brag-inducing in-joke among players who want their rounds to feel like a speedrun through a ridiculous obstacle course. The 15 cards are split into three pragmatic categories:

- Streak Cards: These are the core gimmicks. They accelerate or alter the pace of the game by triggering streak-related effects, nudging players to keep their cool, or, more accurately, their comedic timing.
- Interaction Cards: These cards increase player interaction. They push players to interact with others in wacky ways, such as forcing a quick “who can hold a straight face the longest?” contest or swapping the hand with another player in a goofy exchange.
- Counter/Setup Cards: The safety rails in this carnival ride. They provide light counters to avoid pure chaos taking over every round, while also enabling some unexpected plays that only work if you’ve got a pulse on the table’s energy.

This expansion is designed for casual group play, so you won’t need to overhaul your entire strategy. The best way to approach Streaking Kittens is not to “master” it, but to let its ridiculousness season your sessions just enough to produce memorable, goofy stories you’ll recount at future game nights. If you’re looking for a more serious strategic upgrade with epic combos, you’ll want to look elsewhere. If, however, you want a tiny theater of absurdity to punctuate your evening, this pack does its job with messy enthusiasm.

## How Streaking Kittens changes the game: mechanics that don’t break the experience

This pack doesn’t upend Exploding Kittens; it tweaks the tempo and widens the smiles. The following are the kinds of changes you’ll feel in your rounds when you drop these 15 cards into the mix:

- Tempo tension with a wink: The streak cards create short-lived windows where players feel their fortunes swing dramatically, which is perfect for short rounds or a game night where the group favors “moment-to-moment gasps” over long, methodical planning.
- Increased interaction: Expect more direct poking and prodding among players. You’ll see more “you go next” moments, more eye-rolls when someone misuses a card, and more triumphant bellowing when a daring bluff lands.
- Reduced stagnation risk: The new cards nudge players to act instead of overthinking their next move. That’s both a blessing and a curse—bless you if you want speed, curse you if you’re playing a strategic-tight-session in which every decision matters.

One practical note: if your group includes players who hate chaos or who strictly follow house rules, introduce Streaking Kittens gently. The goal is to amplify the party vibe, not to provoke a mutiny. A quick way to manage this is to designate a “chaos toggle” at the start of the night—on, off, or “we’re feeling lucky.”

### How to incorporate with the base game

- Step 1: Shuffle the 15-card pack into the main deck before the round starts. If you’re a purist, you can split it into two piles: one for the first half of the night and another for the second. This gives you a natural arc and a reason to yell “We’re not done yet!”
- Step 2: Explain the general vibe to new players. Emphasize that this isn’t a replacement for the original rules; it’s a flavor boost that rewards quick wit and good-natured chaos.
- Step 3: Run a quick test round. If the first stretch feels too chaotic, temper it by removing one or two cards temporarily and adding them back later as players become comfortable with the pace.
- Step 4: Have fun. If a streak card leads to a moment of hilarity, archive it in your memory and brag about it at future gatherings.
