## Durability and Care

Cotton wool blends walk a fine line between luxury and practicality. The goal is to keep the rug looking good for as long as you’re willing to invest in a simple maintenance routine.

### Cleaning

- Vacuum regularly on a gentle setting to remove surface dust and pet hair. Avoid high suction on delicate fibers as a precaution.
- Spot clean any spills immediately with a clean cloth. Use a mild detergent solution if needed, then blot and allow to air dry. Do not rub aggressively, as that can work the stain deeper and flatten the pile.
- For bigger stains, consult the manufacturer care instructions. Some blends tolerate light machine washing if the backing permits; most often, a professional cleaner is recommended for stubborn messes.
- Rotate the rug every few months if you want to minimize uneven wear and color fading in sun-exposed areas.

### Longevity

With proper care, a cotton wool blend rug can hold color and integrity for several years, especially if you avoid direct sun for long periods. The blend’s natural resilience helps resist pilling and excessive matting. Expect the most life when you pair the rug with a good non-slip pad that prevents bunching and reduces wear on the edges.

### Non-Slip Pad and Floor Protection

A non-slip rug pad is a non-negotiable accessory for this rug if you have hardwood or tile floors. It reduces movement, which lowers the risk of edge curling and accidental slips. It also adds a bit of extra cushioning and prolongs the rug’s life by distributing foot traffic more evenly. Look for a pad with a thickness that matches your rug’s pile height and your floor type for the best results.

### Comparisons

#### Cotton vs Wool

Cotton is generally lighter, dries faster, and is easier to clean. Wool tends to be warmer, more durable in high-traffic areas, and holds color with less noticeable fading. A cotton wool blend attempts to marry those benefits: greater softness and ease of care with better insulation and resilience than pure cotton. If you’re deciding between the two, think about climate, traffic, and how often you want to deal with maintenance.

#### Other Brands
