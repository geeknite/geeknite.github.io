## Care and maintenance

Wool is a resilient fiber, but even the best filament needs a gentle hand. Here is a practical care plan for the Illusion Author Wool rug:

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting and without a beater bar. The goal is to lift dirt without grinding it deeper into the pile. You can use a soft brush attachment for quick cleanups in high-traffic zones.
- Address spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing. Use a mild detergent and water solution for spot cleaning, and test in an inconspicuous corner first. The key is to blot, not scrub, to preserve the illusion and keep fibers intact.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear. This step is small but mighty in extending the life of the surface, especially if you have a favorite seat that tends to keep a predictable footprint.
- Rug pads are your friend. A good pad reduces slipping, adds a layer of cushioning, and keeps the fringe from curling. It helps maintain the illusion of a perfectly anchored surface instead of a sneaky, rug-on-the-move situation.
- Professional cleaning: Depending on traffic and the presence of pets, a professional cleaning every 1-2 years can refresh the fibers and restore the wool’s natural luster. Always follow the manufacturer guidelines for professional services to avoid compromising the backing or the pattern.

## Durability and performance

Durability hinges on the combination of wool quality, pile density, and backing. The Illusion Author Wool aims to strike a balance between comfort and resilience. Wool fibers naturally resist staining and recover well from compression, which matters for high-traffic households and rooms that see the occasional game night. The backing and the overall tuft density contribute to edge stability and long-term shape retention. In practical terms, you should expect the rug to perform well for several years with proper care.

In terms of performance, a few factors to consider:

- Foot traffic: The rug should handle daily traffic with minimal sagging or flattening of the illusion pattern. If you have a chaotic household with foot traffic from a dozen sneakers, the rug should still hold up.
- Pets: Wool tends to resist odors better than some synthetics and is relatively easy to clean after small accidents. Prompt cleaning and a good pad help here as well.
- Cat scratching: If you have cats, consider a deterrent or a designated scratching alternative near the rug. Wool can resist some scratching, but there is always a risk of surface wear if the pattern becomes a magnet for claws.
- Edge fraying: A well-constructed rug stands up better to fraying along the edges. If you notice loose threads, address them early to prevent unraveling.

All these factors contribute to a practical, longer-lasting piece that remains visually interesting even after a thousand coffee tables and a few remote-control battles.

## Installation and space planning tips

Getting the Illusion Author Wool rug into your space gracefully is part art, part science. A few practical pointers:

- Measuring matters: re-check the room dimensions before ordering a custom size. The goal is to preserve the illusion without causing furniture to look crowded or frozen in place.
- Subfloor considerations: If you have a slippery subfloor, a pad is a must. It helps align the rug with the room’s geometry and prevents creeping under the chair legs.
- Layout testing: Before committing, lay out tape or use painter’s tape to map out the rug’s footprint. Visualize how the pattern will read at different points in the room and adjust accordingly.
- Lighting: The illusion pattern responds to lighting. Place the rug to catch natural light during the day and soft ambient light at night to manipulate the perceived depth.
