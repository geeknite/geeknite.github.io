## Rules Overview
No Mercy UNO builds on the base of UNO and adds optional twists that amplify the chaos in a controlled way. The core rule remains the same: match color or number, and use action cards to twist the current player’s fate. The merciless twist introduces a handful of special cards and a few clarifications that reduce the need for constant house-rule chatter. It is quiet, but the effect is loud during the last two rounds when someone realizes they have not drawn a card yet.

## Play Session: A Typical No Mercy Night
### Setup
- Each player draws seven cards.
- The top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile.
- The deck includes a handful of merciless cards, which can trigger draw penalties or color-change effects. The exact number varies by edition.

### Turn Structure
- On your turn, you can play a card that matches color or number, use an action card to alter the flow, or draw from the pile if you have no playable card.
- If you have one card left, you must shout UNO. Forget to do so, and the penalty could be a mercy card or a small extra draw—depending on the table’s preferred rules.

### Mercy and Twist Cards
- Mercy cards modify how many cards the next player draws, or grant a brief shield from a penalty if you can respond with a matching color.
- There are a few color change wilds that keep the game moving when you are down to your last few cards.

## Strategy and Social Dynamics
No Mercy UNO rewards players who plan in the margins and manage their hand with a light, catlike cunning. You want to avoid getting stuck with a hand full of one color, but you also want to hold back your final card to land a surprise finish on the table. The No Mercy twist is that the table can merge into a chorus of triumphant groans as someone uses a color change to spike their opponents’ chances.
