## Design and Build Quality: The Box, the Closers, and the Feel

## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping It Sharp

Caring for a wood box with chips isn’t complicated, but it does require a little routine. Here are a few practical tips:

- Dust and wipe regularly: a microfiber cloth keeps the wood finish from looking dull.
- Avoid humidity extremes: wood expands and contracts, which can affect lid alignment and hinge performance.
- Check the flip closers periodically: a little lubrication (as recommended by the manufacturer) can keep them snapping reliably without sticking.
- Keep chips in their designated slots: it preserves the interior’s neatness and helps you count correctly during games.
- Don’t force a lid: if something seems misaligned, don’t pry; take a moment to realign rather than risking a crack in the wood.

These practices will help your set survive years of Friday-night smoke, popcorn, and dramatic yake-downs as players attempt to read you like a book. Not every home set is built to last a lifetime, but many are designed to be robust enough to withstand your occasional blunder and still look excellent on your shelf.
