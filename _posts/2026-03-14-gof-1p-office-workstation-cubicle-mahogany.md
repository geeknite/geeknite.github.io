### Ergonomics and Comfort

Ergonomics is where the rubber meets the ergonomic mat. The GOF 1P offers height adjustability options that can help you align your eyes with your monitor and your wrists with your keyboard. A good rule of thumb is to set your chair so that your elbows are at roughly 90 degrees when typing, and your feet are flat on the ground with a little clearance under the desk for leg movements. The cubicle walls provide a moment of privacy that can reduce daylight glare and coworker shouting matches. If you are tall, you may benefit from adjusting the desk height to avoid slouching and creeping toward the keyboard with a posture best described as curious. If you are shorter, you can tuck in a small footrest and pretend you are on a tiny spaceship that travels at the speed of emails.

For screen ergonomics, place the monitor at about an arm's length away with the top of the screen at or slightly below eye level. If you use a laptop, consider a stand so the text does not become a waffle in your neck. A simple keyboard tray can bring wrists to neutral while freeing up space for mouse movement and that random mug that somehow ends up in every desk setup.

### Cable Mythbusters and Real World Cable Handling

Cable management is the unsung hero of any workstation. The GOF 1P includes routed channels and grommets that make it possible to keep the desk surface clutter-free. The reality check: you still need to do some cable discipline. If you cram a dozen devices into the cubicle, you may end up with a small cable ecosystem that behaves like a web of tiny caffeinated caterpillars. Plan your setup with a limited number of power bricks, a USB hub, and a couple of surge protectors. Label cables if you can, because nothing slows down your momentum like unplugging the wrong charger mid email. For larger fleets of devices, consider adding a second oomph of power to your desk area or staggering devices to keep everything within reach without turning your workspace into a spaghetti monster of cords.
