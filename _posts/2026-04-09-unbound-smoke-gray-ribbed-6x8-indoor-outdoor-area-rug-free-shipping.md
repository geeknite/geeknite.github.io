## Maintenance and Cleaning Tips
### Regular care
Vacuum regularly to remove dust, hair, and the crumbs of the modern snack lifestyle. A routine once or twice a week is enough for most households. Empty the vacuum slowly and steadily to avoid tossing dust back onto the rug. If you have a pet, a quick brush-down will help keep the fur from turning into a feature on your furniture instead of the rug.

### Spills and stains
Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth to prevent saturation. For tougher stains, use a mild detergent mixed with water and gently blot the area. Avoid scrubbing the fibers, which can cause fuzzing and pilling over time. After cleaning, rinse lightly with water and let the rug dry completely in a shaded area. Do not expose to direct heat during drying, which can cause the fibers to become brittle.

### Outdoor care considerations
If the rug is used outdoors, a periodic rinse with a hose is perfectly fine. Let it dry in the shade to prevent color changes caused by intense direct sun. In climates with heavy rainfall, allow extra drying time before placing furniture back on the rug. A rug pad can help keep it flat and extend its life by reducing wear from furniture legs.

### Storage tips
When storing the rug during off seasons, roll it up rather than folding it. A rolled rug maintains its shape better and reduces creases. Keep it in a dry, well-ventilated area to prevent mildew. If possible, store it in a breathable bag or wrap to protect from moisture while still allowing airflow.
