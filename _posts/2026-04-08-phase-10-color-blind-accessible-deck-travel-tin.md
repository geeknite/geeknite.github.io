## How Phase 10 Works: A Quick Recap (For the Curious Color-Blind Reader)
If you’re new to Phase 10 (or you’ve been playing every Saturday since 1990 but forgot something due to your last long-form spreadsheet), here’s the gist:
- Objective: Complete ten specific phases, advancing through a series of card combinations, before anyone else does. Each phase has a distinct target (e.g., two sets of three, or one run of seven, etc.). The first person to finish a phase earns a hand that advances them toward the finish line of ten phases.
- Turn structure: On your turn, you draw a card and then discard one. You aim to complete your current phase using the seven or so cards you hold while also trying to prevent others from finishing first by laying down their required cards.
- Strategy: There’s a blend of luck and tactical juggling—when to go for a wild card, whether to hold onto a potential set or risk a new draw, and how to read the table’s apparent needs.

In the color-blind deck, those decisions become a little easier to track at a glance, not because you’ve memorized a rainbow of hues, but because you’ve learned to recognize the iconography that stands in for color. The result is a game that feels like it’s designed to be more inclusive without becoming less chaotic in a hijinks-filled way.
