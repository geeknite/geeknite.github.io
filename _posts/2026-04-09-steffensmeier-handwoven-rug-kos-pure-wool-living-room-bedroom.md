> Care note: Wool is naturally flame retardant relative to many synthetics and tends to resist staining when treated promptly. For stubborn spills, a damp cloth and a gentle blot will usually do, followed by air drying. For the long game, consider a wool-safe cleaner and a light vacuum with a brush setting. ## Practical Considerations: Sizing, Care, and Adaptability

### Placement Ideas and Room Flow
If you want to maximize your rug’s impact, place it so that at least two furniture legs sit on the rug in a seating area. This anchors the space visually and helps the room feel more cohesive. In bedrooms, align the rug with the bed’s footboard or place it under the bed such that the rug peeks out evenly from all sides. If your room has a lot of natural light, the Kos’s wool fibers will show off subtle color shifts throughout the day, giving the room a living, breathing quality.

### Cleaning and Maintenance Routines
Regular maintenance will do more for your rug’s longevity than any fancy cleaner. Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting to remove surface dust; rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth—no rubbing, no harsh scrubbing. If a stain persists, test a wool-safe cleaner on an inconspicuous corner first, then treat the stain with patience. For deeper cleaning, consider professional rug cleaning services that specialize in handwoven wool.

### Matching Rugs with Other Textiles
Pair the Kos with cushions and throws in natural fibers like linen or cotton to echo the wool’s texture. The goal is to create harmony, not a rope of mismatched fabrics. If you’re feeling bold, a solid color in the same family as one of the Kos’s hues can anchor the palette and let the rug sing without shouting.
