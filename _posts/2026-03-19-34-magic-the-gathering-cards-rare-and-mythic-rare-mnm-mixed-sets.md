## The 34 Picks Breakdown: NM/MNM and Beyond

### Jace, the Mind Sculptor — Mythic Rare (Worldwake, 2009)
Jace is the classic “brain in a jar” of blue control. In NM/MNM condition, he’s a centerpiece for control shells—still a talking point in Commander circles and a potential investment that doesn’t insult your wallet as much as a single toothy goblin with a matched toaster. Playability is timeless: bounce spells, card draw, removal, and a win condition baked into a blue mage’s dream diary. If you’re collecting, he’s a must-have for the nostalgia and the raw power swing in any 60-card format. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/jace-mind-sculptor-card-spotlight

### Liliana of the Veil — Mythic Rare (Innistrad, 2011)
Liliana is the emotional core of many black-based control and midrange builds. In NM/MNM, she’s sleek, imposing, and basically a top-tier value engine that punishes forced board states. Her minus abilities create inevitability, while the plus side is a world where your opponents fear the inevitability of no more blockers. If you want a collectible with a big narrative bite, Liliana is a prime target for your binder and for your “gravity well” decklists. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/liliana-veil-carinal-spotlight

### Ugin, the Spirit Dragon — Mythic Rare (Dragons of Tarkir, 2015)
Colorless color-smoothing, board-dominant, and unflinchingly versatile. NM/MNM Ugin sits nicely in a box and doubles as a mana-sink for late-game control and big-stomp strategies. He’s a planeswalker who feels like a safety valve as much as a game-winner, a symbol of mana-based control that scales with the game’s tempo. A good NM piece also makes a wonderful conversation starter about colorless strategies and the multi-set history of Sarkhan’s world. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/ugin-spirit-dragon-card-spotlight

### Teferi, Hero of Dominaria — Mythic Rare (Dominaria, 2018)
Teferi is proof that blue can be both a halt signal and a win condition. As NM/MNM, he’s a strong center for control and spell-attachment decks; his loyalty counters and ability to untap permanents create some delicious tempo plays. For a collector, Teferi’s a modern classic, with shows of planeshifted multi-set flavor and a design that lines up with the way players actually think about “draw-go” games. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/teferi-hero-dominaria-card-spotlight

### Chandra, Torch of Defiance — Mythic Rare (Battle for Zendikar, 2015)
Chandra brings the ember of risk and reward. In NM/MNM, she’s a multi-purpose engine: you can ramp, burn, and draw in the same turn—great for big modern-style decks and helpful as a casual finisher. She’s a personality card, requiring a mix of patience and aggression in play, which makes her a fan favorite for modern players who enjoy a little spice with their sorcery speed. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/chandra-torch-defiance-card-spotlight

### Karn Liberated — Mythic Rare (Worldwake, 2010)
Karn has always been the walking basement-dweller of control: big, powerful, and slightly intimidating to opponents who fear a “use-you-all” reset. NM/MNM Karn is a centerpiece for prison-style decks and big-muyx mana combos. He’s a diplomat of the artifact world, a card that makes friends and enemies at the same time. If you’re collecting iconic planeswalkers, Karn is a mainstay. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/karn-liberated-card-spotlight

### Nicol Bolas, Dragon-God — Mythic Rare (War of the Spark, 2019)
Nicol Bolas as an elder dragon planeswalker expresses the multiverse’s ultimate ego. In NM/MNM, he’s a showpiece and a story moment on the table—a rare that can anchor a midrange/control plan and command a huge respect aura in casual and tournament play. He’s also a conversation starter for your shelf about the Gatewatch era and grandiose villainy. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/nicol-bolas-dragon-god-card-spotlight

### Griselbrand — Mythic Rare (Avacyn Restored, 2012)
Griselbrand sits in the throne of reanimator decks and big-show black strategies. In NM/MNM, he’s a monstrous but elegant mana engine that asks your graveyard to cooperate—an all-star for casual and kitchen-table reanimation lists. If you enjoy dramatic commander outcomes, Griselbrand brings a hefty dose of “oh wow, that did not go as planned” flavor. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/griselbrand-card-spotlight

### Emrakul, the Promised End — Mythic Rare (Eldrazi, 2016)
Emrakul is the “I win if you don’t interact fast enough” moment in many decks. NM/MNM Emrakul is the big-budget, big-entertainment edge for control and graveyard strategies. She’s the kind of card that makes you want a longer pegboard and a bigger chair for the ritual victory dance. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/emrakul-promised-end-card-spotlight

### Emrakul, the Aeons Torn — Mythic Rare (Rise of the Eldrazi, 2010)
Another Emrakul, yet a different style of menace. In NM/mnM, Emrakul is a display piece and a serious power transition card in certain formats. Her art alone is a conversation starter, and her presence in a binder is the kind of bragging-rights moment that brings a small tear of joy to a collector’s eye. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/emrakul-aeons-torn-card-spotlight

### Wurmcoil Engine — Mythic Rare (Scars of Mirrodin, 2010)
Wurmcoil Engine is legendary for being a scalable six-mana threat that draws you extra cards and turns your adversaries’ day into a slow-mo montage. NM/MNM shows this card as a pristine centerpiece in artifact-heavy decks and a nostalgia beacon for the Scars block. It’s also a fantastic example of how a good mythic can age gracefully. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/wurmcoil-engine-card-spotlight

### Dark Confidant — Rare (Ravnica: City of Guilds, 2005)
Dark Confidant is a classic, a little edgy, and still a staple in many formats. In NM/MNM, he’s a risk/reward engine that rewards you for knowing your deck’s depth. He’s a reminder that green is not the only color of risk and that card advantage can be a vice as well as a virtue. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/dark-confidant-card-spotlight

### Snapcaster Mage — Rare (Innistrad, 2011)
Snapcaster Mage is the “I cast this later” dream. NM/MNM copies look flawless, and his flashback ability remains one of the most elegant design choices in modern-era blue decks. If you enjoy value and tempo, Snapcaster is the artful highlight of many a blue deck. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/snapcaster-mage-card-spotlight

### Noble Hierarch — Rare (Modern Masters, 2013)
Noble Hierarch is the quintessential mana acceleration creature for green and is a staple in modern-legal staples. In NM, she’s pristine and ready to spike your mana curve with green-pumping elegance. She’s a collector’s favorite for her art and her synergy with fetch lands and synergy with other green accelerants. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/noble-hierarch-card-spotlight

### Stoneforge Mystic — Rare (Eventide, 2009)
Stoneforge Mystic is the iconic equipment tutor. NM copies sit nicely on a shelf, and in the right build, she can fetch you a toolbox of swords and cores. If you’re aiming for a “feel-good toolbox deck” vibe at casual nights, Stoneforge Mystic is your friend. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/stoneforge-mystic-card-spotlight

### Batterskull — Rare (New Phyrexia, 2011)
Batterskull is part of the “equipment as a game plan” movement. NM copies look fantastic and carry the aura of a deck’s resilience and synergy with beacons of life gain and creature-based toughness. It’s the kind of card you showcase when you want your table to nod in appreciation. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/batterskull-card-spotlight

### Scapeshift — Rare (Zendikar, 2009)
Scapeshift is the quintessential “play the long game” card. In NM, you get a clean look at the symmetry between the threshold of your win condition and the opponent’s fear meter. Great in midrange and ramp strategies, it’s one of those cards that makes you whisper, “Yes, I shuffled well.” External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/scapeshift-card-spotlight

### Blood Moon — Rare (Eight Edition, 1998; reprint cycles since)
Blood Moon’s iconic red-soil effect has aged like a fine lava lamp. NM copies demonstrate the striking contrast of red border art versus the black mana economy it disrupts. For many players, it’s a nostalgic staple that still punishes fetch-heavy, multi-color synergy decks in casual and many competitive formats. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/blood-moon-card-spotlight

### Thoughtseize — Rare (Lorwyn, 2007)
Thoughtseize is black’s most polite mind-flay: a card that guarantees you know your opponent’s business, for a price. In NM, you see the art and feel the power as you disrupt plans before they hatch. It’s a cornerstone for many black control and tempo decks and a collector’s item that instantly earns you “serious gamer” cred. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/thoughtseize-card-spotlight

### Swords to Plowshares — Rare (Alpha/Beta era originals; reprints)
Swords to Plowshares is the classic green-fringed exhale: remove a creature and gain life. In NM, it looks pristine and ready for reunions with your early-2000s nostalgia. It’s a staple in countless formats and a reminder that white removal can be both efficient and beloved. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/swords-to-plowshares-card-spotlight

### Path to Exile — Rare (Conflux, 2010)
Path to Exile is the modern white removal baseline: efficient, clean, and a mirror for every tempo deck out there. In NM, you’ll notice the crisp borders and the clean lines in the art that still feels fresh after all these years. A well-loved, widely played staple you can rely on for years to come. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/path-to-exile-card-spotlight

If you want a “safe, reliable, legendary” white removal piece, Wrath is your go-to. External link: https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/feature/wrath-of-god-card-spotlight

### Final Notes and Observations

- The mix of Mythic Rare and Rare cards in NM/MNM condition from various sets creates a beautiful tapestry of Magic’s history. You’ll see art styles traverse eras, showing the evolution of card-design philosophy—from the early, more literal spell art to the modern, often more abstract aesthetic. It’s a gallery, a time capsule, and a playable toolbox all in one binder.
- NM/MNM status matters when you’re aiming for value and for long-term preservation. The slightly lighter wear and edge damage are not just about looks: they affect how a card behaves visually in a sleeve and, in some rare cases, can influence resale value or grading. If you’re chasing investment-grade pieces, you’ll want to standardize your NM checks: corner wear, surface scuffing, and edge nicks are your enemy—and your budget’s best friend.
- When building decks around these cards, think about multi-format viability. Some cards shine in Commander and Modern, others in Vintage or casual play. The key is to identify synergy with your favorite playstyle: control, midrange, aggro, or combo. If you’re not sure where to begin, revisit your local game store’s meta, and consider a few trial nights to see which cards perform best in your group’s environment.

## Final Recommendation

If you’re curating a 34-card NM/MNM showcase from mixed sets, this batch provides a strong balance of iconic power and timeless staples. It’s a mix that will please both new collectors and seasoned UW control enthusiasts who remember the days when cards came in dotted paper and the sleeves still smelled like laundry detergent fresh from the packet. The Mythic Rares offer awe-inspiring presence and collector appeal; the Rares provide reliable playability across formats and a sense of history you can physically hold. If you’re looking to invest in a starter collection or to fill a shelf with conversation-worthy pieces, this lineup is a solid blueprint.

For playability, you’ll find carriers of tempo, control, and big-game impact. For value, you’ll see the usual spikes of reserved lists and reprint cycles, but in NM/MNM condition, these cards tend to hold up well in collections, be it for nostalgia, future grading, or simply the joy of owning famous cards that have shaped the game’s evolution.

## Quick Deck-Building Suggestions (in 2-3 lines each)
- Jace, the Mind Sculptor: Reliable top-end for control, with a game plan built around card advantage. Ideal with heavy counter-magic and bounce support.
- Liliana of the Veil: Best in midrange-black shells focusing on removal and hand disruption; prune opponents’ boards while you accumulate value.
- Ugin, the Spirit Dragon: Works well in colorless or five-color decks with big-mold ramp and removal support; it’s a big finishing play late game.
- Teferi, Hero of Dominaria: Great in time-warp tempo builds; combine with bounce effects for massive tempo advantages.
- Blood Moon: An excellent sideboard card against multi-color, non-basic strategies; a strategic game-breaker in the right metagame.
- Snapcaster Mage: A universal value engine; fits into blue-based control, tempo, and combo shells with recursive spell play.
- Baneslayer Angel and Scapeshift: Depending on your deck’s bottle, these can anchor creature-advantage and big-storm or ramp strategies.
