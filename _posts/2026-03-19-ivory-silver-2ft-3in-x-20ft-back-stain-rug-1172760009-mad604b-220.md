## Cleaning and maintenance tips

### Vacuuming and routine care
Set your vacuum to a low setting to avoid pulling threads. Run the vacuum in different directions to keep the weave even and reduce the risk of a visible path in the rug over time. For high-traffic runners, a weekly vacuum plus periodic deep cleaning is a good rhythm. A shallow weave means you can capture most dirt before it settles into the fibers, which helps prolong color and texture.

### Stain response and spot cleaning
Treat spills as soon as possible. Blot with a clean cloth to lift as much liquid as you can, then use a mild detergent solution on a small area if needed. Always test on a hidden section first. If you prefer store bought carpet cleaners, choose one that is safe for synthetic fibers and avoid heavy solvent-based formulas that could degrade the backing or color. For tougher stains, consult the care label or the retailer's guidelines and consider professional cleaning when in doubt.

### Drying and storage
After cleaning, ensure the rug is thoroughly dry before walking on it again. A damp rug can invite more dirt and lengthen the time it takes for the backing to set properly. If storing the rug during a season, roll it up with a breathable fabric cover to avoid moisture buildup. Do not fold; folds can create creases that are hard to remove after long storage.
