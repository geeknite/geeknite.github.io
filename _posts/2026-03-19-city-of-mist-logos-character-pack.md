## What is the Logos Character Pack?

The Logos Character Pack is not a random bits-and-bobs add-on. It is a curated expansion designed to slot right into your City of Mist table and spark new storylines without forcing a total reboot of your ongoing campaign. At its core, the pack touts a set of new logos (the power signatures that define a character’s mythic identity) plus ready-to-run pre-gen characters who embody those logos in fresh, flavorful ways. Think of it as a character kit for the city’s secret theater troupe—but with more rain-slicked streets and fewer stage lights.

Two quick caveats before we go further: first, you should be comfortable with the City of Mist system. If you’re still figuring out the tag-based, investigator-meets-mythic-turnbook approach, you’ll want to skim the core book and perhaps the Logos primer included in the base set. Second, this is not a replacement for your existing campaigns; this is a supplement, a spark, a dare to your next session where you aggressively lean into the noir-meets-myth vibe.

### Logos and the mythic-identity engine

In City of Mist, the Logos are the power signatures that anchor a character’s identity. They aren’t just “powers” or “abilities” in the abstract sense; they are living, breathing myths your character steps into. The Logos determine what your character wants, what they fear, and how they express themselves as they interact with the city’s underbelly and its higher glories. The Logos Character Pack presents a curated batch of new logos—each with a short narrative hook, a set of mechanical hooks (that feel both thematic and functional), and some sample playbeats to get you started.

If you’re a GM who loves the sparkle of a good theme, you’ll appreciate how the pack leans into the “mask and identity” concept without turning it into a math problem. The writing aims to be evocative rather than encyclopedic; it’s about telling you what the character wants and what myth they are wearing like a costume, rather than forcing you to memorize a wall of numbers.

## How It Affects Gameplay

What does this pack actually do at the table, beyond the wonderful-sounding promise of more logos? Here’s a breakdown of the gameplay implications, written with the blunt honesty of someone who once tried to improvise a final boss while juggling three dice and a dead phone battery.

### Speed and Flow

- Short version: the Logos Pack can accelerate session flow if you use the pre-gen characters as springboards. The included hooks are designed to drop into an ongoing case without requiring a full re-orientation of the party’s goals.
- Longer version: the pack encourages players to lean into their identities earlier in a scene. You’ll see more conversations about what the myth represents, which can lead to faster act turns because the motive is already in front of everyone’s eyes. It’s not magic; it’s narrative economy dressed up with neon signage.

### Balance and Narrative Equity

- The pack’s logos are thematically rich but not overpowering. They’re designed to be meaningful without eclipsing other players’ arcs. If your table loves a party-wide ensemble, you’ll appreciate how the pack’s characters leave room for other players to shine with their own logos and mythic agendas.
- There’s an emphasis on moral ambiguity rather than pure power moves. City of Mist thrives on protagonists who are powerful because of their beliefs, not because they carry an overstuffed combat deck. The Logos Pack respects that ethos while giving you options that can escalate tension in interesting ways.

### GM Tools and Scene Framing

- The pack provides GM-facing prompts designed to help you nudge scenes into mythic territory without stealing agency from players. You’ll get scene seeds that feel “pull‑this-thread” ready: once a logo is introduced, the GM has a slate of consequences ready to unfold, sometimes in the same session, sometimes in the arc’s next chapter.
- There are also recommended abuse flags—things to watch for to ensure the table remains comfortable, inclusive, and fun. It’s nothing heavy-handed; just reminders that in a game about identities and myths, consent and tone are the real power-ups.

## Running with the Pack: GM and Player Tips

To help you translate the pages into sessions, here are practical tips and a few story-ready structures you can steal without feeling dirty about it.

### Pre-gen Scenarios as Launch Pads

- Use the provided pre-gen scenarios as one-shot launches or as anchor points for a longer arc. A one-shot helps players get their bearings in a compact session, while an arc gives you room to explore how logos evolve under pressure and how the city’s myths react when they intersect.
- As a GM, start with a single hook from the pack, then escalate by layering a second myth with a conflicting goal. The clash of mythic agendas is where the City of Mist truly breathes.

### Campaign Seeds and Long-form Narrative Arcs

- The pack’s seeds are modular. Tie them into a larger city-wide mystery about who writes the city’s myth and who profits from it. You’ll get a sense of scale that lets you shift from street-level encounters to city-wide implications without losing the intimate drama that makes City of Mist sing.
- If you like player-led arcs, invite your players to redefine their logos as the story unfolds. The pack supports this by providing prompts that encourage reflection on what the myth means to each character now, not just what it meant when the logo first showed up.

### Scene Craft and Tension Techniques

- Leverage “mythic pressure” as a scene lever. Let a logo’s power create a consequence—an event that challenges the character’s values—and watch the table pivot into a narrative-rich moment that feels earned rather than manufactured.
- Don’t be afraid to use noir rhythm: a slow reveal, a ticking clock, and a hard decision that changes the city’s mood. The Logos Pack gives you the tools to do this without overpacking a single session with rules tangles.
