Assembly and Installation
=========================
If you’re the kind of person who enjoys the satisfaction of a well-planned IKEA-like struggle, you’ll appreciate GOF’s assembly notes. They’re not heavy-handed, but they are precise. The process requires: a Phillips head screwdriver, the included Allen wrench, and a friend with basic spatial awareness. The main steps run something like this:
- Stage the panels in your space where you’ll end up assembling them. Gravity is a friend when panels aren’t yet attached.
- Attach the side panels to the base frame using cam-lock hardware. This is where you’ll feel the “engineered for modularity” vibe—no wobble when the whole thing stands up.
- Install the interior supports and the vertical panels for the 5' and 6' height options. If you opt for the higher configuration, you’ll appreciate the extra headroom and the sense of vertical conquest.
- Slide in shelf components and the top panel, then secure the desk surface with the supplied screws. The top panel has a neat lip that helps keep monitors from doing a gravity-assisted nap on your keyboard.
- Cable management channels: thread cables through the grommets and secure with the provided ties. If you’re picky about routing, you’ll have a field day adjusting cables until they disappear into a tidy little sculpture of order.

Estimated time varies by experience. A solo, OCD-free setup might finish in 2–3 hours; a more meticulous crew could approach 4 hours. Either way, patience beats frustration when you’re dealing with module alignment and pre-drilled holes that love to pretend they’re misaligned simply to test your patience.
