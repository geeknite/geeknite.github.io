## Game Overview

### What is Sushi Go?

Sushi Go! is a quick card-drafting game designed for 2-5 players. The theme revolves around a sushi conveyor belt, where players must pick and pass cards to create the best sushi dishes and score points. It's a game of strategy, memory, and a bit of luck.

### Components

The game includes 108 cards featuring adorable sushi illustrations, a well-laid-out rulebook, and a scorepad. The cards cover various sushi types such as Nigiri, Sashimi, Tempura, and more.

**Fun Fact**: The artwork on these cards is so cute that you might find yourself craving sushi mid-game. Just don't eat the cards!

## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setting up Sushi Go! is a breeze. Shuffle the cards and deal a hand to each player. Grab your chopsticks (okay, not really, but it adds to the ambiance), and you're ready to roll.

### Turn Phases

Each turn consists of the following phases:

1. **Drafting**: Each player selects a card from their hand and places it face down.
2. **Passing**: Players pass their remaining cards to the player on their left.
3. **Revealing**: Everyone reveals their chosen card simultaneously.
4. **Scoring**: After all cards are played, scores are calculated based on the combinations each player has collected.

### Winning and Losing

The player with the highest score after three rounds wins. Keep track of your score with the provided scorepad. Just like real sushi, the game is light and satisfying, and there's always room for one more round!

**Pro Tip**: Pay attention to what your opponents are collecting. Denying them the cards they need can be just as important as picking the right cards for yourself.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Quick and Easy**: Setup and gameplay are fast, making it great for impromptu sessions.
- **Accessible**: The simple rules make it suitable for players of all ages.
- **Replayability**: The game is different every time you play due to the variety of card combinations.
- **Engaging Artwork**: The cute sushi illustrations add charm and appeal.
- **Strategic Depth**: While easy to learn, the game offers plenty of strategic choices.

### Cons

- **Lightweight**: Might not satisfy those looking for deeper gameplay.
- **Player Count**: Best played with 3-5 players; it can be less engaging with only 2.
- **Luck Factor**: Luck plays a significant role, which might frustrate hardcore strategists.
