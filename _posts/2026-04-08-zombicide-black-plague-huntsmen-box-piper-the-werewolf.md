## Introduction
If you somehow missed the memo, Zombicide Black Plague loves its monsters almost as much as it loves your friends who borrow dice and never return them. The Huntsmen Box, a blast from the crossbow-wielding past, drops into the Black Plague line with the subtlety of a catapult and the grace of a guy who just discovered you can use a longbow to reach the top shelf. Inside this box lives not just more plastic heroes and horrifying undead, but a miniature personality disorder in the form of Piper the Werewolf and a crew of Huntsmen ready to raid your kitchen table at midnight.

As a Geeknite reviewer who has seen more cardboard cutouts than a cosplay convention, I approached the Huntsmen Box with two goals: to tell you what’s worth your gold doubloons and to make you snort-laugh while grimacing at the price tag. Spoiler: there is value here if you play your cards, or your axes, right. So pour a cup of caffeinated courage, and let’s dive into the bone-chilling, yet somehow charming, world of Zombicide Black Plague Huntsmen.

## What is in the Huntsmen Box?
If you picked up the Huntsmen Box hoping for a quiet, refined add-on, you might want to brace yourself for a loud, heroic, and slightly ridiculous package. The box expands the Black Plague experience with a healthy dose of new survivors, a major werewolf adversary, and enough tokens to clutter your table with a sense of impending doom.

### The Huntsmen Survivors
The core of the box is a quartet of Huntsmen survivors. These aren’t your average peasants with pitchforks. These are trained trappers, scouts, and—depending on your interpretation—people who read the fine print on monster contracts and somehow still look heroic when the sun goes down.

- Each Huntsman comes with a unique starting ability that nudges your team toward aggressive, punchy play. Think extra movement, better gear interactions, or a knack for triggering chain reactions when the board gets chaotic.
- Their equipment cards open interesting synergy paths. You’ll be juggling crossbows, swords, and the occasional improvised tool of destruction, all with the satisfaction of a well-timed dice roll.
- The sculpts are clean, with a decent amount of character to each mini. They don’t look identical on the table, which helps you remember who’s doing what when the blizzard of tokens starts piling up.

### Piper the Werewolf
The big fish in the tub is Piper the Werewolf, a classic Zombicide antagonist with a twist. Piper isn’t your regular werewolf—oh no, this one has a boastful personality and a set of rules that make him feel like a mobile mini-boss when you accidentally encounter him mid-round. Piper is the kind of creature that reminds you why you don’t leave the door open in a zombie apocalypse: he can howl into the night and affect the behavior of other werewolves and, frankly, most monsters around.

- Piper comes with a unique action deck that plays into a theme of feral cunning. He’s not just “strong”; he’s disruptive, which makes him a formidable target for your plan-B if your initial assault goes sour.
- The miniature itself has presence. It’s slightly larger than a standard zombie, with dynamic posing that reads well under a gaming light. On a well-lit table, Piper can become a centerpiece that your group questions if you should risk splitting the party just to take him down before he scatters your ranks to the wind.
- In terms of encounter design, Piper introduces a pacing shift. Your party will want to limit his leash on the board, carefully calculating the risk of drawing his attention while trying not to trigger a cascade that ends in your knights becoming late-night snacks.

### Tokens, Cards, and Other Bits
Like every good Zombicide expansion, Huntsmen includes a slew of tokens and cards to spice up the urban-slasher-paladin vibe. Expect new gear cards, new objective tokens, and a few special tiles that alter how you set up scenarios.

- The gear cards emphasize mobility and crowd control. You’ll see more ranged options, which is a pleasant surprise for those who fear close-quarters chaos.
- Objective tokens are balanced to encourage both aggressive expansion and tactical retreat. It’s a dance: press forward, then regroup, then sprint again as the enemies pile up.
- The insert quality is consistent with Zombicide standards: sturdy, with a reasonable stack height so you don’t have to spend an hour building a fortress out of extra cardboard.

### The Box Experience: Thematic Cohesion and Table Time
One of the strongest aspects of the Huntsmen Box is how well it threads into the Black Plague atmosphere. The Huntsmen bring a medieval-huntsman vibe that sits nicely with the plague aesthetic, while Piper introduces a more fantastical horror element that keeps the game from becoming too grounded in “grim survival.” The mix lands in a sweet spot where you feel like a cluster of doomed heroes are leaping between traps, while the truth is you’re all just trying not to cry on a plastic table.

In terms of component quality, you’re getting solid sculpts, good paintable surfaces, and miniature design that reads well on the tabletop even when you’re playing at midnight with a burrito tucked under your leg. If you’re a painter, you’ll appreciate the distinct silhouettes that don’t require heroic degrees in anatomy to look good. If you’re a gamer who just wants to slam dice and pretend you’re a dragon-slaying legend, you’ll still be happy with the visual clarity on every round.

## How Piper Changes the Game
Piper the Werewolf isn’t an “extra” boss; it’s a shift in tempo. The core game of Zombicide Black Plague rewards careful planning, risk management, and the occasional reckless sprint through a thunderstorm of bones and bad dice rolls. Piper injects a variable threat: a powerful single target who can disrupt your plans, drag more zombies into the fray, and force you to rethink your approach mid-round.

### Movement and Threat Radius
Piper’s presence tends to stretch the map dynamically. He’s not always the fastest threat, but he’s a high-impact one if left alone. When Piper roams toward your team, expect a mix of tense pauses and tense jokes as you decide: do we retreat, press, or try to trap him with a clever dummy move? The decision feels weighty because his actions can cascade into multiple zombie spawns and a potential chain reaction of bad outcomes for anyone who underestimates him.

### The Werewolf’s Card Deck: A Mini-Story on the Table
Piper’s action deck reads like a short horror novella where the villain occasionally interrupts with a menacing howl. You’ll have turn-by-turn choices that reward cunning and punish hesitation. It’s not all doom and gloom, though—the deck also includes moments that reward witty play, such as bait-and-switch moves that let you pull a dangerous zombie into a trap with a well-timed lure.

### Strategy: What to Do With Piper
- Target prioritization: Don’t prioritize Piper as your sole objective, but don’t ignore him either. He should remain a constant threat, forcing you to split attention across the board and disrupt the enemy’s feeding patterns.
- Board control: Use barriers, line-of-sight control, and choked points to slow his approach. The more you can funnel him into chokepoints, the more you can manage his influence without losing a critical survivor.
- Ally timing: Coordinate with your Huntsmen to maximize damage during Piper’s vulnerable windows. You’ll want to strike in bursts rather than slow-bleed-hitting him, so you don’t give him a chance to re-stabilize on the map.

## Assembly, Paint, and Table Presence
Like many competent minis, the Huntsmen and Piper shine on the painting table as much as on the battlefield. The assembly instructions are clear enough for even your least adventurous hobby friend to survive the experience, and the parts fit together with a satisfying snugness after a touch of glue and a healthy dose of patience.

### Assembly Tips
- Dry-fit first: Do a quick dry-fit of each limb before gluing to avoid that awkward “this should be fitting, right?” moment.
- Green stuff or putty for gaps: If you’re picky about gaps, a small dab of putty will make your minis look museum-quality on a four-foot high shelf (or at least on your display shelf between battle reports).
- Primer and base coats: A black primer undercoat tends to help the colors pop when you paint the Huntsmen’s gear and Piper’s fur. After all, a werewolf in the moonlight has bone-dry fur that looks better with some shading.

### Table Presence
On the table, Piper is a showpiece. The Huntsmen look like a disciplined unit of archers and melee specialists ready to push into the heart of the plague doom. The overall effect is cinematic: a group of heroes facing down a werewolf while the plague roars in the background. If you’re the GM or the host, you’ll find yourself narrating dramatic scenes with gusto, your friends swapping jokes about who’s brave enough to go first.

## Scenarios, Campaign Potential, and Meta Play
The Huntsmen Box isn’t just a one-off encounter; it’s a taste of long-term campaign potential. If you like to weave a narrative arc in your gaming sessions, Piper’s inclusion gives you a recurring antagonist arc to spice up your table. You can create a scenario progression where the party must track Piper across the map, intercept his lairs, and finally bring him to a climactic showdown that tests endurance as much as luck.

### Scenario Structure
- Introductions: A simple setup with one or two objectives that allow players to test the waters of Piper’s threat without feeling overwhelmed.
- Escalation: A mid-campaign scenario with more zombies, a more aggressive Piper, and a few new relics that must be collected to survive.
- Finale: A climactic confrontation that uses a double-threat dynamic—Piper the Werewolf and a wave of Huntsmen-inspired minions—that forces the party to rely on synergy, timing, and a little bit of luck.

### Campaign Crossover Potential
If you’re already running a longer Black Plague campaign, the Huntsmen Box can slot into your timeline as a mid-season pivot, giving your players a reason to re-evaluate their gear and their party composition. The Huntsmen can be integrated as a new faction of survivors who join the party, or as mercenaries who offer temporary alliances in exchange for completing a dangerous objective.

## Value, Comparisons, and Alternatives
Let’s talk value, because that's where many players decide if a box is worth another dent in the mortgage payment for hobby goods. The Huntsmen Box brings a balanced mix of new miniatures, cards, and tokens that feel purposeful rather than filler. It doesn’t reinvent the wheel, but it does sharpen the wheel’s edges in a way that makes you want to roll again.

### Price Per Feature
Compared to other Zombicide expansions, Huntsmen sits in a mid-to-high tier range. You’re paying for more miniatures, new rules, and the narrative flavor that Piper injects into the game. If you embody the “more is more” mindset, you’ll feel you got your money’s worth as the board fills with movement, spells, and a few scares that land with a satisfying thud.

### How It Stands Against Similar Expansions
- Against other Black Plague expansions: Huntsmen emphasizes mobility and narrative disruption, which can feel different from brute-force expansions. If you prefer tactical agility with a touch of theater, this box delivers.
- Against standalone zombie lines: The combined theme of huntsmen and a villain like Piper elevates the “immerse factor” in a way you don’t usually get from pure skirmish additions.

### Alternatives If You’re on a Budget
If you’re cost-conscious, consider guardedly mixing and matching: pick up Piper separately (if available) and combine it with a few other cheaper character packs. Alternatively, you could explore older expansions where recurrences of the werewolf are used as a recurring villain in your own self-made campaigns. The key is to maintain balance and avoid overloading the table with too many moving parts at once.

## Painting, Customization, and Community Creations
If you’re into miniature painting, Piper and the Huntsmen offer a delightful palette. The werewolf’s fur shading, the Huntsmen’s leather-and-metal textures, and the total level of detail lend themselves to a satisfying painting session that ends with you proudly showing off on social media (and secretly hoping your friend’s dog doesn’t lick the model off the shelf).

Community content around Zombicide is broadly supportive. You’ll find fan-made scenarios, alternate rules variants, and paint guides that can help you tailor the Huntsmen experience to your group’s tastes. If you enjoy reading or watching playthroughs, there are many creators who’ll break down how Piper changes engagement philosophy mid-session, which is entertaining and educational in equal measure.

## The Final Verdict: Should You Grab the Huntsmen Box?
If you’re a player who enjoys story integration, tactical diversity, and a little extra chaos to spice up nights with friends, the Huntsmen Box is a solid addition to Zombicide Black Plague. Piper the Werewolf provides a memorable antagonist that can be used as a recurring challenge, while the Huntsmen survivors deliver fresh mechanics that encourage you to think differently about how you approach each board state.

The box shines best when you lean into its strengths: dynamic movement, mixed objectives, and the willingness to split attention between a pack of fighters and a terrifying werewolf maestro. It’s not a “must-have” for every group—if you’re strict about your tabletop economy and you rarely play co-op narratives, you might postpone this purchase. But if you hunger for a campaign-friendly boost that revs up the tension without turning into a math-heavy Monty Hall problem, you’ll likely fall in love with the package.
