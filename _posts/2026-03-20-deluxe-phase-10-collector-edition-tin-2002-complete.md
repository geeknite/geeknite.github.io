How Phase 10 Works (A Quick Refresher)
--------------------------------------
Phase 10 is a race through ten distinct phases, from simple sets to more elaborate combos. Each phase has its own win condition: the first player to complete the current phase and discard down to a final hand’s worth of points wins the round. The catch is that you must complete the exact phase—no fudging with “I did Phase 9, but in a form I prefer to call Phase 8.” You must complete the required set or run in order before the next player does, and you must be able to discard down to a legal hand by the end of your turn.

Key mechanics include:
- Turns: On your turn you draw one card and discard one card, just like most traditional card games. The aim is to assemble your phase using a mix of your hand and the wild cards you’ve drawn along the way.
- Wild cards: They substitute for any card, making your path to Phase 7 or Phase 10 somewhat less certain and a lot more exciting when you’re one card away from victory.
- Skip cards: They cause the next player to lose a turn, creating opportunities for dramatic turnarounds in the late game, especially when your opponent has a near-complete phase that you would rather deny them.
- Phase progression: You must complete each phase in order; a failed attempt on one phase doesn’t wipe your progress on earlier ones, but it does reset the progress for the current stage, which is where the strategic agony comes in.

Gameplay is straightforward enough that a new player can grasp it in a single session, and deep enough that a veteran can refine their approach over dozens of rounds. We’ll get into strategy later, because phase optimization is where the true soul of Phase 10 lives—shiny with the thrill of narrowly beating your own best score and the agony of a poorly timed draw that ruins your carefully constructed plan.

Strategic Considerations: Phase 10 Tactics in the Tin
----------------------------------------------------
A few tactics are worth highlighting, especially when you’re playing with a group that has nostalgia in its eyes and a coffee habit that rivals a small rainforest:
- Early phase planning: It’s tempting to sprint toward the lowest-numbered phase, but the real trick is to balance your draws to collect the right mix of sets and runs for the upcoming phases. If you jump too aggressively into Phase 4 without enough keepers for Phase 5, you’ll be staring down the barrel of a late-stage scramble where you’re chasing cards you discarded two rounds ago.
- Wild card management: Use wilds to fill critical gaps in your current phase but avoid squandering them on a minor edge case. The real payoff comes when you can convert wasted wilds from earlier turns into a powerful Phase 9 strategy that your opponents didn’t see coming.
- Discard discipline: The discard pile is your memory of the table’s missteps. Pay attention to what others are collecting, and think a step ahead about which card you should hold onto or throw away to prevent someone from completing a phase you fear.
- Timing the skip card: When used at the right moment, a skip can swing momentum dramatically. But you must resist the urge to skip on a whim; timing is everything in Phase 10.
