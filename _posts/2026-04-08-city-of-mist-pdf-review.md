## Introduction: neon-noir meets myth in a PDF
City of Mist is one of those tabletop game concepts that sounds like a joke until you realize it’s a love letter to everything you secretly wanted to be as a kid. A game about people who are more than they appear—half-past fantasy, half-past detective pulp, all neon-bathed in modern city life—City of Mist sits somewhere between a superhero RPG and a cosmic fable. The PDF we're reviewing today is the official rulebook and companion file set that aims to capture that mood in readable layout and flexible rules.

If you’ve dipped your toes into indie RPGs or ever doodled bleary-eyed hero sketches in a notebook during lunch, City of Mist will feel familiar and fresh at the same time. The PDF version, unlike a print copy, has the advantage of quick reflowable text, searchable content, and hyperlinks for quick reference. It’s also the kind of document that rewards a player with a well-placed annotation and a GM who can bend the world to fit a narrative beat without tearing the mechanics apart.

In this review, we’ll parse the PDF from cover to index, talk layout and art direction, discuss the mechanics in the abstract (no spoilers for your personal campaign), share GM and player tips, and give you a verdict on whether this PDF is worth your time, your disc space, or your wallet’s patience.

## What is City of Mist, exactly, in the PDF form?
City of Mist conceives heroes as living, breathing myths embedded in everyday life. People become Mythos characters when they lean into a legend—think a librarian who channels the wizardry of Arthurian magic while still wearing a cardigan and a coffee-stained badge. The City of Mist PDF distills this premise into a modular system that blends investigative play with mythic scope.

The PDF includes the core rules, character creation guidance, archetype options, encounter methods, and a toolbox of game aids: quick-start rules, a sample setting scaffold, enemy design tips, and a framework for running a city-as-stage for mystery and grand gesture. It is deliberately flexible, inviting both one-shot sessions and sprawling campaigns, and it doesn’t force you into a single genre lane. You want noir police procedural with mythic overtones? You got it. You want urban fantasy with capes and streetwise slang? The PDF will happily lean into that, as long as your table isn’t allergic to a dash of melodrama.

The writing leans on mood over math. There’s a preference for narrative prompts, tag-based skill checks, and the sense that the system is designed to serve story first, mechanics second. If you’ve ever found yourself muttering “we’ll see what the dice say” and then immediately fail in a surprisingly cinematic way, City of Mist understands you. The PDF formalizes that vibe and gives you clear language to describe consequences, stakes, and character aspirations without bogging down in equations.

## The core mechanics in the PDF: a quick tour
City of Mist’s core mechanic rests on a “tag-based dice” approach, where players use a combination of character tags (skills, powers, aspects of personality) and situational tags (environmental cues, scene notes, and GM-revealed opportunities) to influence outcomes. The PDF lays this out with practical examples and ready-to-copy prompts that you can paste into a session plan. The engine is built to reward creative problem solving: if you frame a problem as a mythic or a noir hazard, you’ll often find a doorways toward success that a simple straight roll would never reveal.

What makes the system feel unique is its emphasis on “Mythos” versus “Logos.” Mythos covers the heroic, larger-than-life elements drawn from legends and public myth. Logos captures the mundane, everyday identity of a character—the mask they wear, the secrets they guard, the persona they project when the city demands it. The PDF does a strong job of clarifying how these two facets interact, how a character can shift emphasis mid-scene, and how a GM can nudge a scene toward mythic proportions without breaking immersion. You’ll find clear examples of how to resolve conflicts that feel cinematic rather than number-crunchy, with a few well-placed tips for keeping tension high while providing players with meaningful agency.

The rules text around advancement, limitations, and escalation is approachable. There are suggested play styles for different table vibes (investigation-first, action-forward, or character-driven melodrama), along with a quick-start section that helps you cut to the chase in under an hour. Even if you’re coming from a heavy resolution system, the PDF offers a gentle acclimation path, not a steep cliff. If you’re a GM who loves world-building but hates constraints that make creativity feel like you’re poking at a locked door, City of Mist is probably going to feel like a polite nudge rather than a boot to the shin.

## Character creation and the player experience
Character creation in City of Mist is where the PDF shines with practical, narrative-led steps. You start with a character concept, a mythic inspiration, and a handful of tags that anchor your stats and your narrative levers. The PDF provides modular archetypes and templates you can mix and match, plus a robust guide on how to weave a character’s private life into the city’s public drama. It’s a design philosophy that treats every PC as a potential legend-in-progress, not as a finished cosplay you’ll display for the party window.

The process is playful but not aimless. It invites collaboration: players pitch their concept, the GM feeds back with world hooks, and together you land on a “Mythos” that aligns with the city’s mood. The “Logos” elements—occupation, name, and secrets—are not mere flavor; they function as in-game tools that affect tag resolution and narrative leverage. In practice, this encourages players to lean into roleplaying choices that feel personal and consequential, rather than merely “roll the dice and see what happens.”

The PDF includes handy worksheets and example character sheets that are cleanly laid out for quick reference. It’s easy to track your character’s growth as you lean into a myth or as you learn to wear a new mask, and the design makes it straightforward to show other players how your hero’s public and private sides intersect. If you’ve played lighter, more GM-led indie games, you’ll find that City of Mist respects your ability to improvise while giving you reliable hooks to keep scenes on track.

## Campaign scaffolding and adventures: what the PDF gives you
The City of Mist PDF doesn’t pretend you’re getting a fully pre-written campaign in a single bind. Instead, it offers a scaffolding toolkit for city-wide drama, with sample settings and modular adventures you can drop into your table. The “city as character” approach means the setting isn’t a backdrop; it’s a living participant in every scene’s tension. The PDF provides scene prompts, investigation beats, and escalation ladders that you can adapt to a one-shot, a two-session arc, or a longer campaign.

What’s refreshing is the emphasis on episodic storytelling that still respects long-form goals. You’ll find guidance on running mysteries that unfold across sessions, along with tips for weaving recurring Mythos figures and recurring themes into your city’s ongoing narrative. The adventures aren’t rigid roadmaps; they’re flexible, with “if-then” prompts that let the GM adapt to a party’s choices. If you’re a GM who loves improv, you’ll feel supported here rather than blocked; if you prefer a plan-with-room, you’ll appreciate the exposed scaffold without the requirement to fill every blank perfectly.

## GM tips: running City of Mist like a showrunner of myth
If you want to run City of Mist as a long-running show in a city that feels alive, the PDF has a set of GM-facing tips that are worth their weight in neon chrome. One highlight is how it frames pacing around “Mythic reveals.” You don’t force a big reveal in every scene; you build a slow burn where each session peels back a layer of a character’s myth while simultaneously exposing a layer of the city’s own mythos. The PDF urges you to balance personal stakes with city stakes, a tricky but often rewarding dynamic.

It also provides a strong emphasis on player agency. You’ll find prompts that encourage players to select consequences for their actions and to work with the GM to keep a story moving, even when a plan goes sideways. There’s an appreciation for collaborative storytelling that goes beyond “the GM says yes or no.” You’ll see how to use the city’s mechanics to reflect theme, mood, and character arcs, which makes sessions feel cohesive and narratively satisfying rather than episodically random.

For those who love procedural detail, there are practical checklists: session prep notes, a quick-start encounter design sheet, and a “what to prepare for the next session” page. If your table enjoys running multiple plot threads in parallel, you’ll appreciate the templates for weaving threads in and out without losing grip on the central mystery. If you’re more of a macro GM, there are hooks to scale up challenges and escalate conflict as players push toward larger mythic outcomes without becoming overwhelmed by the world’s scale.

If you’re tired of crunchy crunch games where the story only happens when the dice align, City of Mist’s PDF offers a refreshing counterpoint: a rules framework that serves story rather than dominating it.

1) Pick a city hook and a Mythos: The PDF’s opening pages are perfect for a one-shot or a short arc. Pick a mythic motif and a city setting that resonates with your players’ characters. Use the quick-start prompts to stage your first scene—this gives players a reason to care about the mystery early on.
2) Build your cast and hook the players into the story: Use the character-creation guidance to place each PC’s Mythos and Logos into the city’s fabric. This is where the game shines—when players see their legends reflected in the environment, the session becomes a mirror that’s constantly being polished by choices.
3) Run modular adventures with a plan for escalation: The PDF’s modular adventures let you adjust on the fly. If a scene feels flat, pivot toward a Mythos reveal or a new Logos thread. If players chase a clue that leads to a larger conspiracy, use the city’s escalating stakes to push toward a satisfying climax.
4) Use the quick-reference tools: The PDF’s tables and sidebars are designed for on-the-fly use. During play, you’ll want to keep key tags and threat ratings within easy reach so you aren’t forced to pause the game while you hunt for a rule. Keep a quick sheet handy for the most common checks and outcomes.
5) Close with consequence and character growth: City of Mist rewards endings that acknowledge the city’s impact on a hero and a hero’s impact on the city. Conclude sessions by highlighting how the Mythos has altered the character’s life and how the city has responded to the new reality you’ve crafted together.

## Final verdict: is the City of Mist PDF worth your disk space?
Yes. The City of Mist PDF is a nimble, mood-forward rulebook that respects narrative play and offers a framework that’s flexible enough for both light one-shots and longer campaigns. Its strongest suits are its atmosphere, its accessible approach to myth and identity, and its practical tools for GMs who want to keep sessions moving without sacrificing narrative depth. The rules are not overly dense, and the layout is conducive to quick reference during a session. If your table loves neon-noir vibes, if you long for mythic storytelling that doesn’t require constant page-flipping, and if you want a system that invites collaboration over “roll-then-justify,” this PDF is a strong choice.

That said, if your group prefers heavy crunch or if you’re hunting for a superhero-might-be-OP experience where numbers drive every decision, City of Mist may feel lighter on the mechanics. It’s not a flaw so much as a design choice: the game and its PDF lean into story first, and mechanics second. If that’s your jam, you’ll likely find this a refreshing, memorable, and easily teachable experience.
