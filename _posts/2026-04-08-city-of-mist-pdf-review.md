## The core mechanics in the PDF: a quick tour
City of Mist’s core mechanic uses a tag-based approach: players weave together character tags (skills, powers, mythic traits) and situational tags (environment cues, scene notes, and GM-revealed openings) to influence outcomes. The PDF demonstrates practical examples and offers ready-to-copy prompts for session planning. At its heart, the engine rewards creative framing: you don’t simply roll to succeed; you frame the problem as mythic or noir, and the dice help reveal pathways you might not have anticipated.

Two big ideas anchor the system: Mythos and Logos. Mythos covers the heroic, larger-than-life elements drawn from legends and public myth, and Logos captures the mundane, the masks we wear, the secrets we guard, the personas we project when the city demands it. The PDF clarifies how these facets interact, how a character can shift emphasis mid-scene, and how a GM can nudge a moment toward mythic proportion without snapping immersion. Expect cinematic-resolution prompts that feel satisfying rather than sterile, with a few well-placed tips to keep tension high while preserving player agency.

Advancement, limitations, and escalation are explained in approachable terms. The book offers play-styles for different vibes—investigation-first, action-forward, or character-driven melodrama. There’s a quick-start section designed to have you up and running in under an hour. If you’re coming from a crunchy system, you’ll find the transition gentle rather than jarring; the PDF acts like a friendly showrunner, not a drill sergeant.
