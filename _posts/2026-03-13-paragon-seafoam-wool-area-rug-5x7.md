## Maintenance and Longevity: Caring for the Wool
We all know maintenance can feel like a long side quest. Here is a simple plan to keep the rug looking crisp:

- Vacuum regularly on a low setting. Avoid high suction that can pull fibers.
- Blot spills promptly with a clean cloth. Do not rub; you may push the stain deeper.
- For tricky stains, use a wool-safe cleaner and test on a corner first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to encourage even wear and to avoid color bias from light.
- Consider a quality rug pad to minimize wear and prevent slippage.

The rug’s natural wool fibers will shed a little during the first weeks. Regular vacuuming and a gentle brush can reduce shedding and keep the surface tidy. If shedding becomes a concern, you can use a lint roller between deep cleanings.

### Seasonal Care and UV Considerations
Natural fiber rugs are sensitive to sunlight. If your rug sits near a window with direct sun, you might see some fading over time. To mitigate this, rotate the rug and use UV-blocking window treatments. The color will remain more vibrant with these simple steps.
