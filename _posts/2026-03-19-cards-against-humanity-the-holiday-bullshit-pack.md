## Overview

The Holiday Bullshit Pack is one of those CAH expansions that tries to capture the zeitgeist of the season while preserving the core mechanic that made Cards Against Humanity a staple of dorm rooms and office parties. It packs in a set of white and black cards with holiday themes that range from the merry to the terribly inappropriate. The idea is simple: players use their white cards to complete the black prompts in ways that are clever, irreverent, or totally savage. The more you know your crew, the better the responses tend to land. The Holiday set leans into seasonal tropes like shopping hell, family dynamics, and gift exchanges that go wrong in glorious fashion, all wrapped in the familiar CAH style that has delighted, disgusted, and sometimes alienated participants across the globe.

For longtime fans of the franchise, the Holiday Bullshit Pack is a welcome reminder that cards can be humorous without needing a censor bar that stretches from Europe to the North Pole. For newcomers to CAH, this pack serves as a very clear taste of the ongoing experiment behind the game: how far can we push the lines and still call it a game and not a therapy session about awkward family dinners? The answer varies by table, but in general the pack delivers enough punchlines to keep a party moving, even when the jokes dip into the famously dark or the delightfully silly.

## Playing tips and house rules

- Establish a quick veto list before the first round. A few prompts can easily derail a game if the crowd is not in the mood. A simple veto list helps social safety while preserving the element of surprise for the rest of the table.
- Set a shot clock or timer for each round to keep the pace brisk. CAH is a game of quick wit; slow rounds tend to evaporate the energy and the laughter.
- Try variant rules such as pairs or teams for larger groups. This can reduce the pressure of delivering the perfect line and instead encourage collaborative humor which often yields the best lines.
- Have a personal rule for the most awkward prompts. If you feel a line crosses a line, simply skip it and move on. You will still get laughs and save a moment that would likely not age well in the morning light.
- Use the seasonal vibe to create a mini narrative. The holiday setting provides a great stage for recurring jokes about family quirks, gift exchanges that go wrong, and the impossible wish lists that lead to silly outcomes.
