## Real-life testing: how does it perform in the wild?

### Vacuuming and routine maintenance

Because it’s a low-pile piece, this rug responds well to regular vacuuming with a beater bar on a gentle setting (or better yet, a suction-only attachment if your vacuum has one). Leave the heavy-duty cleaning to your normal schedule, and keep an eye on corners that may curl up—this is a common trait for cheaper edges that didn’t get the extra seam reinforcement you’d see on premium products. If you notice any wrinkles, a gentle heat-free steam touch from a professional can flatten out the edges without harming the fibers, provided you test a small area first.

### Spills, stains, and spot-cleaning

Ivory is beautiful but a bit of a diva when it comes to spills. Water-based stains tend to be forgiving if treated promptly with blotting—not rubbing—to lift the pigment. Oil-based spills are trickier and may require a targeted cleaner designed for synthetics. Avoid abrasive scrubbers that can fray the weave or lift color unevenly. The good news is that most everyday spills respond to a quick blot and a dab of mild detergent solution. The key is prompt action; the longer a stain sits, the higher the chance it becomes a stubborn memory in the fiber.

### Durability in high-traffic areas

In living rooms with moderate foot traffic, a well-placed rug pad can significantly extend the rug’s life by reducing friction and preventing edges from curling. If you’re working with a hallway or entryway, a more durable pad can help shorten the time between cleanings and prevent scuff marks from dragging dirt onto the surface. If you have pets with nails or kids who like to race toy cars across the floor, be mindful of the potential for edge fraying over time—though a minor flaw in construction is what often contributes to that eventual wear. The rug’s overall durability is a function of fiber choice, weave tightness, and how aggressively you treat it with cleaners and abrasives.
