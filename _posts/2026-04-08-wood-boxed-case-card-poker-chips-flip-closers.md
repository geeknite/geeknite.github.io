## How to choose your wood boxed poker chip set

- Consider wood type and finish: darker woods look more formal, lighter woods read as casual. If glare at the table bothers you, matte finishes reduce reflections.
- Check the flip closer mechanism: a smooth flip with a satisfying click beats a wobbly lid every time. Test the lid before purchase if possible.
- Assess chip quality: weight consistency, edge spot visibility, and color accuracy matter for readability and for satisfying visuals when fanning stacks.
- Denominations and color scheme: ensure the hues are distinct enough to prevent misreads in the heat of a game. If you’re color-sensitive, opt for a set with high-contrast colors.
- Box dimensions and storage options: ensure there’s room for a dealer button, dice, and a few USB-chargeable torches for dramatic late-night sessions.
- Sustainability and sourcing: if you care about the planet, ask about the veneer source and whether the wood is harvested responsibly.

## Practical maintenance tips

- Keep the wood moisturized with a light, board-game-friendly polish every few months. Think of this as lotion for your wooden soul.
- Wipe the chips lightly with a dry cloth after a night of sticky snacks to preserve the edge spots and prevent sticky residue from creating a grimy finish.
- Store the set in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to avoid warping the box or fading the colors.
- If you ever notice the flip closer getting stiff, a tiny drop of mineral oil on the hinge can save you from a dramatic lid-fling incident during a crucial hand.
- Rotate chips if you use them for months on end. A subtle rotation keeps color wear even and avoids obvious hotspots on the edges.
