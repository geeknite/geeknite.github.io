## Maintenance and care: keeping it fresh
### Cleaning basics
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, light-colored cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing can push the stain deeper into the fibers.
- For basic vacuuming, use a low-suction setting or a vacuum with a brush roll that you can turn off. The goal is to avoid snagging the fibers rather than turning it into a sand trap for airborne debris.
- For stubborn stains, a gentle, pH-balanced rug cleaner works best. Always test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.

### Deep clean considerations
If a deeper clean is required, consider professional cleaning every 12 to 18 months depending on traffic. The Maize Linen’s color helps camouflage minor soil changes between professional cleanings, but you still want to keep an eye on high-traffic areas and pet-related messes. Detergents with harsh bleaching agents can dull the color over time, so opt for products labeled safe for color and fiber type.

### Stain resilience and real-world performance
The RF backing and fiber blend are designed with stain resistance in mind. In testing scenarios, minor spills—coffee, juice, and the occasional sauce—were negotiable, with prompt blotting curbing lasting marks. It’s not a miracle on day one; more like a reliable partner in crime when you’re juggling kids, pets, and the occasional oven mishap. For tougher stains, a gentle cleaner and a repeat blotting session can often lift the mark without damaging the pile. For color-rich stains, you may end up needing a professional cleaning to restore the original hue fully, but everyday stains can be managed with patience and a little elbow grease.
