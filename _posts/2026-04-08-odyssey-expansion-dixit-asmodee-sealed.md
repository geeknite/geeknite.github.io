## Gameplay: How Does the Expansion Change the Dance?
Let’s talk mechanics. The expansion claims to integrate with the core Dixit experience rather than reinvent the wheel. In practice, this means you can insert these new cards into the existing deck and run a standard round, or you can lean into thematic rules that the expansion suggests. The actual rules are often light-touch: you pick a storyteller, the storyteller selects a card (or a card from the new deck) that best suits their clue, and the other players pick from their own hands to match. The scoring remains as playful and forgiving as ever, with points awarded for correct guesses and clever misdirection.

A helpful trick here is to use the new prompts to loosen up players who always default to the same safe descriptions. You’ll probably see a wider range of clue strategies: some players gravitate toward esoteric references that require a shared pop culture vocabulary; others swing for the universal “that looks like it could be a thing” line. The moral of the story: the expansion is a nudge, not a shove, toward wilder storytelling without turning the table into a debate about semantics.

Keep in mind: Dixit is as much about the table as it is about the cards. The expansion encourages you to narrate more dramatically, to lean into the absurd, and to embrace the moment when a card’s meaning is as broad as a lighthouse on a foggy day. If your group tends toward dry, literal clues, you’ll want to remind them that this is a game where metaphor wins and the truth is often stranger than fiction.

For those who care about variety, the new cards also help shuffle fatigue. The more cards you have, the less chance of repeated prompts—unless your group is predisposed to asking, Is that a dragon or a mashed potato with wings? Either way, the humor sustains the session.
