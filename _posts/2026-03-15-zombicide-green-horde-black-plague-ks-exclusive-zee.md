## A quick primer: what exactly are Green Horde and Black Plague in board game terms

Zombicide Green Horde is the fantasy cousin to the classic Black Plague edition. In practical terms, they share a lot of core mechanics: players take on roles with unique abilities, you unlock gear as you level up, and you survive a hail of zombies by coordinating your party. The primary difference is theme and the type of Zombies you encounter. Green Horde leans heavy into forest goblins, orcs, trolls, and more organic, nature-based undead motifs. The plastic miniatures lean into mossy greens, bark textures, and a style that reads as “mighty, hungry, and a little bit crusty.”

Black Plague is the original dark knight of Zombicide. Think plague-infested streets, necromancers, dark magic, and a sense that you should have brought more anti-toxin. The undead here are more daunting, the spells more explosive, and the tactical calculation is steeped in medieval chaos. The two games share card types, action economy, and the shift from exploring to clearing the board, but the aesthetic and the flavor are what truly separate them. If you like the undertone of a fantasy epic balanced with a dash of grimdark, Green Horde scratches that itch. If you prefer the brooding atmosphere of plague-era fantasy with heavy necromancer energy, Black Plague scratches that itch more aggressively.

## Best practices: how to approach playing Green Horde and Black Plague together

Some groups prefer to play with a unified set of rules and a single campaign deck; others enjoy strict separation for each edition with Zee acting as a bridging mechanic. Decide early what your table enjoys most to avoid rule creep or confusion when a wave of undead laughs at your plans.
