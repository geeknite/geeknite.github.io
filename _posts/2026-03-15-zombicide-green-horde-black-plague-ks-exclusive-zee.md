## A quick primer: what exactly are Green Horde and Black Plague in board game terms

Zombicide Green Horde is the fantasy cousin to the classic Black Plague edition. In practical terms, they share core mechanics: players take on roles with unique abilities, you unlock gear as you level up, and you survive a hail of zombies by coordinating your party. The primary difference is theme and the type of undead you encounter. Green Horde leans heavy into forest goblins, orcs, trolls, and organic, nature-based undead motifs. The plastic minis lean mossy greens, bark textures, and a style that reads as mighty, hungry, and a little crusty.

Black Plague is the original dark knight of Zombicide. Think plague-infested streets, necromancers, dark magic, and a sense that you should have brought more anti-toxin. The undead here are more foreboding, the spells more explosive, and the tactical calculation stacks up with grimdark medieval chaos. The two games share card types, action economy, and the shift from exploring to clearing the board, but the flavor and aesthetic are what truly separate them. If you like the undertone of a fantasy epic balanced with a dash of grimdark, Green Horde scratches that itch. If you prefer brooding atmosphere with necromancer energy and a touch of plague-era dread, Black Plague scratches that itch more aggressively.
