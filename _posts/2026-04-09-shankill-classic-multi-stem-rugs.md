## Cleaning and Maintenance: Keeping the Stems Standing Tall

### Vacuuming Protocols
Hash out a simple vacuuming routine: weekly maintenance plus spot cleaning as needed. Use a vacuum with a brush roller set to a low-to-mid height to avoid pulling threads. You don’t want to tuck your rug into a medieval-torture device by accident. If you have a pet with a favorite muddy trail, keep a small, quick-clean kit nearby—microfiber cloths, gentle carpet cleaner, and a soft brush for any stubborn hair tangle from fluffy stems.

### Stain Removal Strategy
Act quickly after spills. Blot—don’t rub—to prevent fiber fraying or color migration. For most synthetic blends, a solution of mild dish soap and water works well. Test on an inconspicuous corner to ensure color stability. For tougher spots, consider a gentle carpet cleaner designed for synthetic fibers. Always follow the product’s instructions and patch-test before committing to a full clean. Your rug will thank you in the form of consistent colors and a longer lifespan.

### Odor Control
In households where the rug doubles as a pet bed or a gaming chair, odor control can be a concern. Regular airing, good ventilation, and occasional cleaning sessions will keep it smelling like a well-kept space rather than a gym locker. A quick sprinkle of baking soda prior to vacuuming can help neutralize mild scents, after which you can resume your life as the proud owner of a gloriously fresh, floor-based centerpiece.
