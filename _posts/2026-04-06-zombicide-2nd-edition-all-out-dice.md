## Mechanics and how it changes play

The core idea behind the All Out Dice is to provide a structured risk-reward dynamic during certain action moments. When the game flow allows you to trigger an All-Out Attack, you’re granted the chance to roll one or more of these dice in addition to your standard dice. The faces on the All Out Dice are designed to reward aggressive play with a higher likelihood of successful hits on the table, but with a cost or caveat that ensures you don’t just breeze through a crowd of undead without consequence.

### The dice faces and outcomes

In practical terms, you’ll encounter a few face types on the All Out Dice that influence outcomes differently than the base dice. Commonly, you’ll see faces that translate into:

- Extra hits on a target, letting you clear clusters of zombies more efficiently.
- A special icon that triggers a board-wide or mission-specific event, like spawning a clutch of reinforcements or activating a defensive obstacle.
- A penalty or a risk element, such as increasing the chance of a zombie activating on a following turn or forcing you to discard a card from your hand.

The approach is to create a short, intense burst window where you feel the adrenaline of a decisive moment. The risk is also spelled out clearly on the included rule sheet, so there aren’t any sneaky “you forgot to tell us about the hidden rule” moments in your game night. The net effect is a temporary power spike for players who lean into it, balanced by the potential backfire if you push too hard.

### Rules interactions and tricky corners

One potential trap with any new dice set is how seamlessly it threads into existing rules. The All Out Dice are designed to be modular; you can incorporate them without re-writing your entire game. However, there are a few nuances you’ll want to note:

- All Out Attacks usually require you to spend a resource from your character card or a mission card. Be mindful of your action economy. If you’re blazing through a round with multiple All Out Dice, you might burn through your actions faster than a zombie can moan the same two-word insult at you.
- The All Out Dice often interact with terrain or zombie spawn rules, so keep an eye on mission-specific sheets. A mission that has tight corridor chokepoints can become incredibly volatile with one extra spike in dice results.
- If your group uses house rules or an optional variant for “critical hits,” the All Out Dice can amplify those effects. Just ensure everyone at the table is on the same page before the final countdown begins.

As with any rules addition, a short test game is worth it. Run a quick scenario with and without All Out Dice in a single session to gauge the swing in power and how it affects cooperative play dynamics. If your crew loves big clutch moments, you’ll probably want to keep the All Out Dice in your rotation.

## Balance, pacing, and table dynamics

Now, does adding these dice tilt the balance? The honest answer is: it can, depending on your table and the scenarios you favor. For groups that enjoy high-octane, cinematic sequences where a small team bursts through a doorway just as a blizzard of walkers closes in, All Out Dice feel empowering and thematic. For crews who prefer meticulous planning and micro-optimizations, the dice can feel a touch swingy, introducing more variance into a system that might otherwise reward precise sequencing.

The key to making All Out Dice sing rather than singes the edges of balance is to:

- Use them in missions that emphasize momentum and danger, not stalemate scenarios.
- Limit the number of All Out Attacks per round if your group is sensitive to power spikes.
- Pair them with thematic objectives (like holding a choke point or rescuing a civilian) rather than raw point-slinging objectives.

In play terms, the All Out Dice shine during moments where you can push for a dramatic breakthrough. The table’s reaction is often a chorus of grins and gasps as one brave survivor wades into a zombie cluster with a resounding result that feels earned rather than merely lucky.

## How to incorporate All Out Dice into your sessions

Here are a few practical tips to maximize your All Out Dice experience:

- Start with a soft ramp. Use All Out Dice in a couple of missions that emphasize action sequences rather than every game night. This lets players acclimate to the new rhythm without overwhelming the rest of the table.
- Establish a cap. If your group is worried about too much variance, set a cap on how many All Out Dice can be used per round or per scenario. The payoff remains dramatic, but the swing becomes predictable.
- Pair with mission flavor. Choose missions where the objective feels like a perilous sprint, allowing the All Out Dice to shine as a thematic accelerant rather than a meta-crutch.
- Create a simple house rule. If the group enjoys the change, you can weave in a tiny house rule that occasionally replaces an All Out Die with a standard die during special circumstances. The idea is to keep tension high without distorting the game’s core loop.
- Track results for a campaign flavor. If you’re running a longer campaign, keep an unofficial tally of how often All Out Attacks succeed or fail. It adds a fun, ongoing narrative arc that players can celebrate or lament as the campaign evolves.
