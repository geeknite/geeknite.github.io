Gameplay 101: How to Play (The Quick Start Guide)
-----------------------------------------------
UNO is famously simple: match color or number, play a special card to scramble your friends’ plans, and don’t forget to shout “UNO!” when you’re down to one. The Pikachu Edition sticks to this vibe but sprinkles in Pokémon flavor in a few places. Here’s the quick-start flavor map:

1. Setup: Shuffle the deck and deal 7 cards to each player. The remaining cards form the draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile. If the starter card is a special action, the round starts with a tiny twist—sometimes you pass the action to the left, sometimes you keep it and skip a few rhythms.
2. On your turn: You can play a card that matches either color or number/symbol. If you can’t, you draw one card. Simple, bright, effective.
3. Special cards and Pokémon bits: There are the classic UNO special cards—Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, and Wild. The Pokémon edition tends to keep these but with Pokémon-themed icons or color variations. In practice, this means your “Draw Two” might feel a bit more like a cunning nudge from your favorite electric friend as you attempt to maintain control of the table.
4. Wild cards: Wilds let you declare the color of choice. In some editions, a Pokémon twist might be added, such as “Choose a Pokémon type” or a flavor text that nudges players toward a mischievous strategy. The net effect: you’re still playing UNO, but with a smile and a Pikachu cameo when you least expect it.
5. Declaring UNO: When you’re down to one card, you must shout UNO. If someone catches you, you’ll be punished with a penalty, typically drawing cards. The tension is real; the house rules you’ve been hiding behind for years suddenly look like genius moves.
6. Ending the round: The first player to shed all their cards wins the round. They celebrate by waving their last card like a sunburst of victory, or—if they’re feeling thematic—pretending to unleash the Pokémon equivalent of a victory pose. The rest of us clap politely as we wipe away our tears and consider the exact moment we’ll get to try again.
