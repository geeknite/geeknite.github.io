## Care, Maintenance, and Real-World Usability

### Cleaning and Longevity

Wool is naturally resilient, but that doesn’t mean you should treat the Illusion Artist rug as your personal autopilot for spills. For routine maintenance, vacuum with a low-suction setting to avoid pulling any loose yarns in the pattern. If you spill coffee or cola (it happens to the best of us during late-night coding sessions), blot the area gently with a clean cloth and a mild detergent solution. Avoid harsh chemicals, as they can affect coloring and fiber integrity. For tricky stains, professional cleaning is recommended to preserve the illusion’s crisp lines.

### Rugs, Pads, and Floor Protection

A good rug pad is essential in keeping the rug in place and reducing wear. A pad provides cushioning underfoot and reduces slipping on hard floors. When the aesthetics of the rug are tied to linework that depends on precise alignment, you want the rug to stay flat and level. The best pad for wool rugs is typically a dense, non-slip felt pad. It’s not glamorous, but it’s practical.
