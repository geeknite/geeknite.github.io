### Care and maintenance
Colorfastness is important for teal and white patterns. We discuss the risk of dye bleeding, especially with liquids. For spot cleaning, use a mild detergent and cold water, blot—do not rub—to prevent color transfer. This is the moment where you decide whether you’ll be wiping up coffee or red wine with the same approach. For vacuuming, use a gentle setting to prevent matting the pile. If the rug is machine-washable, follow the manufacturer’s instructions; otherwise, seek professional cleaning if you’re uncertain. The idea is to preserve the color contrast and ensure the white parts retain their brightness.
