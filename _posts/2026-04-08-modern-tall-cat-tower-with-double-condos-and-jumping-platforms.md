## Design and Build
### Materials and Craftsmanship
The tower uses an engineered wood core wrapped in a fabric that feels durable without being scratchy. The base is weighted to blunt the bold bravado of multi cat households and prevent the whole structure from becoming a dramatic lean-to during dramatic play. Sisal rope wraps the vertical posts so claws have a natural outlet for sharpening without shredding the furniture you actually care about. The condos come with removable cushions that are plush but not overly soft, striking a balance between comfort and support for longer cat naps. Hardware is predominantly metal screws and brackets that are easy to replace if a tiny hail storm of claws decides to test the resilience of the joint.

### Dimensions, Weight, and Stability
On the scale of vertical cat furniture, this tower aims for the tall end of the spectrum. Approximate height sits around 72 inches, with a wide, non-slip base of roughly 20 inches in both width and depth. The base adds significant vertical gravity to counteract the inevitable kitty tower music when your cat chooses to perform a leap from a condo to a platform. Between you and me, if your feline is the Houdini type, this is the sort of thing that makes them brag about their escape artistry without knocking over a lamp in the process. In real terms, stability is solid for one or two cats of average appetite for mischief; for a troop of determined furballs, expect occasional minor wiggles on very animated sessions.

### Aesthetics and Finishes
The finish leans modern-minimalist with two colorways offered: walnut and ash. It blends into clean apartment lines without screaming for attention, though it proudly shows off its geometric silhouette. ## Jumping Platforms: The Peak of Feline Gymnastics
### How the platforms work
Two mid-height platforms create a vertical workout path that can be used for vertical running, precision jumps, or a dramatic chuff as your cat asserts their supremacy over gravity. The platforms themselves are surfaced with a short nap carpet, giving traction without being so grippy that every leap ends in a dramatic tumble. The intention is to encourage a sequence of hops and sprints rather than a single dramatic dive, though a subset of cats will likely opt for the thrilling highwire routine that ends with a curl on the condo’s edge.

### Safety considerations
Corners are softly rounded, edges are smooth, and the jumping distances are scaled for average adult cats. Kittens and senior cats may require slower progression, and we always recommend supervising the learning curve. A stable placement on a hard floor mat helps reduce any occasional wobble, especially on hardwood where the floor has a bit more bounce than brick. If you hear the term gravity in a feline voice, you know the cat has found a new route to adventure and must be treated with the respect afforded to a tiny athletic crane.

### Cleaning and upkeep
Removable cushions are a blessing; they can be machine washed on a gentle cycle, and the fabric holds up to repeated wipe-downs with a damp cloth. The carpeted platforms can attract hair, so a quick vac or lint roller sweep every couple of weeks keeps the tower looking sharp. For deeper cleaning, remove the cushions, wipe the wooden surfaces with a dry microfiber cloth, and check the joints to ensure there is no loosening of screws. The design emphasizes modular parts, so if a panel wears down or a platform loses a bit of plush, you can swap pieces rather than replacing the whole unit.
