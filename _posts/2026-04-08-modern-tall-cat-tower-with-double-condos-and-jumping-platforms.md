## Design and Build
### Materials and Craftsmanship
The tower centers on an engineered wood core wrapped in a fabric that feels sturdy without being abrasive to whiskers. The base is thoughtfully weighted to blunt the bravado of multi-cat households and prevent the whole setup from performing a dramatic karaoke-style lean during a high-energy playsession. Sisal rope wraps the vertical posts, giving claws a natural outlet and protecting your furniture from the fanged assault of discovery. The condos come with removable cushions that are plush enough for serious lounging but not so soft that a cat could sink into a cloud and demand a rent cheque. Hardware leans toward metal screws and brackets designed for easy replacement if a tiny hailstorm of claws tests the joints.
### Dimensions, Weight, and Stability
In the world of vertical cat furniture, this tower is tall by design and pragmatic by temperament. Height hovers around 72 inches, while the base spreads roughly 20 inches in both width and depth to create a stable footprint on various floor types. The weight distribution is meant to keep the tower upright during dramatic leaps from condo to platform, which is crucial when your cat treats gravity like a suggestion rather than a rule. Real-world stability leans solid for one to two cats of average mischief; for a warehouse of furballs, you might notice gentle wobble during peak zoomies. Still, it’s far less wobbly than a broom closet full of laundry and a small Wi‑Fi router.

## Assembly and Maintenance
### Unboxing and Setup
Opening the box is like unsealing a tiny crate of future chaos. There are multiple panels, Allen screws, washers, and instruction sheets whose diagrams seem cryptic until you realize you were staring at a 3D puzzle in a galaxy where furniture has opinions. The good news: parts are labeled, hardware is standard, and the assembly time sits in the reasonable neighborhood of an afternoon if you ignore the memes from a curious roommate asking what on earth this tower is for exactly. The result is a tall, sturdy structure that rises above the litter box like a lighthouse of curious feline activity.
### Step-by-step build tips
- Lay out all parts and verify against the manual before you begin (trust us, you don’t want to discover you’re missing a panel after your cat has chosen the central condo as their throne).
- Start with the base and the vertical posts, ensuring each connection is snug but not overtightened (you’ll thank yourself when you disassemble for cleaning).
- Attach the platforms in a logical progression to maintain balance; test stability after each step.
- Install the removable cushions after the rough assembly; this makes adjustments easier and avoids fabric snagging during assembly tantrums.
- Use a simple level to confirm the base is square; a tiny tilt can become a dramatic cat-traction tour in minutes.
### Cleaning and upkeep
Removable cushions are machine washable on a gentle cycle, and the fabric holds up to repeated wipe-downs with a damp cloth. Hair tends to accumulate on carpeted platforms, so a quick vacuum or lint roller every couple of weeks keeps the unit looking sharp. For deeper cleaning, detach cushions, wipe wooden surfaces with a dry microfiber cloth, and re-tighten any loose screws. The modular design shines here: you can swap worn pieces or swap a platform for a new color without replacing the entire tower.
