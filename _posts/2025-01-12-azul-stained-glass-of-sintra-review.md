## The Basics

### What is Azul Stained Glass of Sintra?

Azul Stained Glass of Sintra is a strategic tile-laying game where players work to create stunning stained glass windows. Each player has a board with spaces for glass panes, and they draw tiles to fill these spaces. The game is played over several rounds, and the goal is to score the most points by completing windows and meeting various objectives.

### Components

The game comes with:
- **Glass Tiles**: Beautifully designed tiles in various colors. Try not to drool over them.
- **Player Boards**: Each player gets their own board to arrange tiles and score points.
- **Score Markers**: For tracking points during the game.
- **Rule Book**: Clear and concise rules. Well, mostly clear. You might need to interpret some sections like an ancient manuscript.
- **Tower**: For discarding tiles. It's like a fancy trash can for your tile-laying mistakes.

**Pro Tip**: Keep your cat away from the tiles. They seem to think it's playtime and will scatter them faster than you can say "Azul!"

## How to Play

### Setup

Setting up Azul Stained Glass of Sintra is straightforward. Arrange the player boards, place the glass tiles in the bag, and draw tiles to form the factory displays. Each player takes turns selecting tiles from these displays and placing them on their board.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn consists of:
1. **Selecting Tiles**: Choose tiles from the factory displays.
2. **Placing Tiles**: Place tiles on your player board to complete windows and score points.
3. **Scoring**: Calculate points based on completed windows and objectives.

### Strategy

The game shines in its strategic depth. Players must carefully plan their moves, consider the tiles available, and anticipate opponents' actions. The balance between completing windows and achieving objectives adds layers of complexity and excitement.

**Pro Tip**: Plan your moves wisely. You don't want to end up with tiles you can't use (or worse, give your opponents the tiles they need).

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
