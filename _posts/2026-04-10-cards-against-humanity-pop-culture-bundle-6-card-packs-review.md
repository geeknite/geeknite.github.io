## How to play with six packs at once
A standard CAH round involves one judge and N players who each submit a white card to answer a black prompt. With six packs, you’ll have more options to choose from, which is both a blessing and a curse. Here are practical tips to keep the energy high and the flow smooth:

- Establish the flow: pick a black card, gather white cards from all six packs, and let players submit their best responses. The judge then selects the winner based on wit, audacity, and the element of surprise.
- Rotate the judge: keep the power dynamic fair and the jokes fresh. If one player becomes the de facto MC, the vibe can turn stale quickly.
- Use a mixed approach: optionally draw cards from a subset of packs for shorter sessions, especially if you’re playing with new players who aren’t yet fluent in the references.
- Introduce two-card rounds: for extra chaos, allow players to place two white cards with the same black prompt. This often yields the most ridiculous or unexpectedly coherent pairings.
- Remember the human element: sometimes the funniest moment isn’t the car-wreck of a pun but the way players defend an absurd pairing with confidence worthy of a courtroom actor.

The core secret to CAH is not the quality of the prompts alone but how people improvise around them. The six pack setup intentionally invites more creative combinations, which means your group will either click or become a study in politely stifled laughter. The payoff is a session that evolves from a simple card game into a memory map of inside jokes and shared references.
