### How to Play: Core Mechanics Unpacked
The Exploding Kittens side is the familiar ride for anyone who has played the original. Drawing cards, using Defuse cards, and avoiding the catastrophic explosion are the core rhythms. The Barking Kittens side, meanwhile, introduces a canine chaos angle—think bark-based reactions, wag-inducing power cards, and a few canine-themed twists that require you to keep a straight face while describing your plan to your teammates.

Key mechanics across the bundle include:
- Draw cards in turn order, with a litany of “bad things” that can happen if you draw an Exploding Kitten without a Defuse card.
- Defuse cards to save yourself from an explosion, a mechanic that sits nicely with the comedic stakes; you’ll find yourself clutching your little Defuse like a lifeboat during a storm of meows.
- Special action cards that force players to skip turns, reverse the order, steal cards, or force others to draw extra cards. The interplay between Exploding Kittens and Barking Kittens cards creates a delightful tension: sometimes you want to push a bit, other times you want to hold your breath and wait for the chaos to unfold.
- Barking Kittens introduces bark-based effects—think cards that cause players to ‘bark’ and perform silly tasks, or that trigger a chain reaction of canine-adjacent chaos when a Barking Kittens card hits the table. It’s silly enough to be adorable, cunning enough to demand strategic thinking, and chaotic enough to turn a tense moment into a room full of laughter.

The two decks share a common language of humor and risk: you want to blow someone else up with a glorious miscalculation, but you also want to survive long enough to gloat about it. The tension is real, but the laughter tends to win the day.

### Tips, Tricks, and House Rules
- If you’re dealing with a hyper-competitive group, implement a soft-house rule: no table-talk during a turn. You’ll be surprised how quickly a simple turn becomes a talking headshow; the laughter will still flow, but you’ll keep the chaos contained just long enough for someone to win.
- Use a “no-cheer” turn for players who have just pulled an exploding kitten and survived with a Defuse. It sounds odd, but the moment of relief is a perfect cue for a grudging, friendly celebration.
- Build your own thematic tie-ins. If your group loves theme nights, assign each round a silly theme—like “Cats vs Dogs in Space” or “Noisy Neighbors Night”—and watch everyone slip into characters while playing, which adds a delightfully immersive feeling without a single extra card.
- Keep a small “garbage tray” or face-down discard pile to avoid clutter on the table. The better you manage the table state, the more time you’ll spend playing and less time shuffling.
