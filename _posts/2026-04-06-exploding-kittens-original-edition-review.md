## How to Play: A Quick, Not-So-Long Explanation
Exploding Kittens is a turn-based, hot potato-esque game where players draw cards from a single central deck. The key idea is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you do, you explode in a very cartoonish fashion and you’re out unless you can Defuse with a Defuse card. Defuse cards are your secret sauce; they let you survive a near-inevitable catastrophe by using things like laser pointers, kitten fur, or just sheer luck.

From a high level:
- The game begins with a shuffled deck of 56 cards
- Each player receives a starting hand of a few cards
- Players take turns drawing a card or playing a card from their hand
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re out unless you play a Defuse
- Defuses are rare and highly valuable
- When an Exploding Kitten is removed, the deck is reshuffled with the next round in mind

The flow is fast. Rounds rarely hit the 5-minute mark, and if you’re playing with a rowdy group, you’ll feel the chaos somewhere around the third prank card hit the table.

## House Rules and Variants: Crafting Your Own Chaos
Every group has its flavor of chaos, so here are some light house rules that can elevate Exploding Kittens from casual chaos to legendary party memory:
- Time-box rounds: 5 minutes per game, no extensions unless someone shouts glory.
- Defuse-pool variant: Start with a limited number of Defuse cards in the deck; players must rely on other cards to avoid disaster.
- Help a friend mode: If someone is about to explode and you possess a card that can help them, you may share it; this encourages teamwork and boosts morale.
- King of the chaotic hill: The winner becomes the designated chaos negotiator for the next round, designing one new rule that has to be followed for the rest of the night.
