## Gameplay and Rules
### The core loop
UNO Spin starts like classic UNO. Players take turns matching either the color or the number on the top card of the discard pile. When the spinner comes into play, you trigger a spin that determines a random twist for that play or for the next few turns. The exact mechanics can vary by edition and how your group agrees to interpret the spinner, but the vibe is clear: expect a few extra laughs and a lot less predictability.

### The spinner mechanic
The spinner acts as a wildcard referee that can force players into special actions. Examples might include changing the color everyone must follow for the next turn, awarding a playful penalty to a random player, or letting the spinner decide who must draw extra cards. The chaos keeps the pace lively and makes the game feel less like a memorization exercise and more like a chaotic mini game within a game.

### How to play with kids
Kids usually love the unpredictable nature of spins. For younger players, you can establish kid friendly spin outcomes such as:
- Spin and the color changes to a calming color for the next turn
- The spinner allows the upcoming player to skip a turn that would be too long or tedious
- A small bonus power that helps a kid catch up without giving away the house

Keep it simple and reward participation rather than mastery. The goal is to create joy, not to create a moral dilemma about who played the draw two too fast.

### For teen and adult players

The spinner can be used to spice up competitive play or to break stalemates with a light exertion of luck. If your group enjoys spicy table talk and friendly trash talk, UNO Spin delivers a robust platform for it without tipping into cruelty.

Rule: The spinner can trigger a swap mechanic where players exchange a card with a neighbor. This creates a social swap moment and a little tactical discomfort in a fun way.

### Theme nights
