## How to play: the long and short of it with your mouth and your mind
Codenames is a game about connecting words with a single clue. The spymaster must guide their team toward a set of word cards that match a clue without accidentally pointing to the assassin. It’s catnip for word lovers and a logistics puzzle for the table. Here is the high-level gist:

- Teams: Two teams, red and blue. Each team has a spymaster and field operatives. The goal is to reveal all of your agents on the grid before the other team does.
- Clues: Spymasters give one-word clues followed by a number. The clue word should relate to several of the words on the grid. The number tells your teammates how many words on the board relate to the clue. The challenge is to balance breadth with accuracy so you don’t accidentally pick the assassin card.
- Guessing: Field operatives discuss the association and decide which cards to flip. You may continue guessing up to the number given by the clue plus one extra guess if you want to be fancy about your risk tolerance.
- The assassin: If you pick the assassin card, your turn ends immediately and the other team gets a point in your dramatic, slightly humiliating fashion.

In the Czech CGE edition, the board’s typography and the word cards’ legibility contribute to a smoother experience than some cheaper editions. The visual hierarchy helps players quickly parse options under time pressure, which is critical if you’re playing with a group that loves to type your words into a group chat like there is a bug in the matrix.

### Spymaster strategies: how to do it without breaking the vibe
If you want to raise your chances without becoming a tyrant with a secret linguist empire, here are a few tips:
- Use broad but safe associations. A clue that connects two or three words cleanly is often better than a single word that could apply to almost anything.
- Avoid obvious trap words. If you can sense a word will likely lead to the assassin, skip it. Your team will thank you later.
- Coordinate with your team’s knowledge. If your team shares an inside joke, use it to your advantage, but be aware that not everyone will get it on the first try.
- Keep the tempo up. The longer you debate, the higher the odds of a misstep. Strike a balance between thoughtful reasoning and communal chaos.

For the opposite approach, the blue team might adopt a more aggressive playstyle, making riskier guesses in the hope of seizing momentum. The red team, on the other hand, can slow down the pace and force the opposing spymaster to reveal more cards to maintain the lead. The dynamic tension is what makes Codenames a standout at parties and game nights—the sweet tension of words, winks, and a tiny bit of chaos.
