## Setup and First Impressions

### Quick Setup
1) Unfold the target board and place it at a reasonable distance from the throw line (the manual recommends 6 to 12 feet depending on your space and confidence in your own arm).
2) Lay down the boundary rope to define your playing area; this helps prevent burrito trespassing into the neighbor’s garden and makes your landings feel legitimate.
3) Distribute the four burritos and the wind tokens within easy reach. Shuffle the tortilla chips if you plan on awarding bonus points for creative throws.
4) Read the quick-start guide. It’s short, which is perfect because your attention span is about the same length as the interval between burrito throws.

A key point here is that the exterior edition doesn’t require a perfect weather scenario to be enjoyable. ### Setup Tips
- Choose a soft, flat surface to place the target. Grass is ideal; concrete may magnify burrito bounce-off energy and cause more cleanup than you signed up for.
- Keep a towel handy. The burrito’s mission is to land on the board, not on your shoes.
- Use sun protection. The outdoor setting invites glare and the occasional sunburn for the diligent player.

## How to Play: The Mechanics, Simplified

### The Turn Structure
At its core, Throw Burrito Extrême is a turn-based targeting game. On your turn, you select a burrito and attempt to land it on the target board. The scoring zones determine how many points you earn for that throw. The wind tokens can be revealed before a throw to adjust the target dynamics, adding a layer of risk and theater to every shot.

Turns proceed clockwise, with players alternating until everyone agrees that the sun has won or a pre-determined score threshold has been met. The game can be as quick or as long as your sunburns and laughter allow.

### Scoring and Wind Mechanics
- Landing in the center ring yields the highest score, typically a premium for accuracy and risk management.
- The outer rings still grant points, but you’ll need to balance your ambition with the burrito’s tendency to curve away from your mouth of intent.
- Wind tokens modify the burrito’s path. A “gust” can push a burrito left or right; a “stiff breeze” might alter your plan entirely. Proper use of wind tokens requires a blend of geometry, trial-and-error, and a sense of humor about physics.
- Bonus points may be awarded for creative throws, such as underhand lobs or no-look attempts if you’re feeling especially showy. These are guaranteed to earn both cheers and groans in equal measure.

### Outdoor Nuances
Playing outside introduces variables that the indoor edition does not require. The wind is the primary antagonist and the sun is the quiet accomplice to mischief. Expect burrito trajectories to dance around the board, splash onto nearby chairs, or disappear into the lawn like a mischievous tortilla ghost. A good rule of thumb is to embrace the chaos. If a throw lands off the board in a dramatic arc that would make a blockbuster movie producer jealous, you earned your applause—whether you liked it or not.

### House Rules and Adaptations
If your group has a penchant for customization, here are a few practical house rules that often improve the experience:
- Wind-tolerant rounds: Each player must declare a wind token choice before their throw. This adds a meta-game of bluffing and prediction.
- Bonus snack zones: Place snack chips near the board as morale boosters. Hitting them yields extra points or honorary card draws.
- Safety first: Include a maximum number of attempts per round to keep the game’s energy high and the cleanup minimal.

## Strategy: How to Think Like a Burrito-Thrower

### General Principles
- Aim for consistency over flashy moves. Consistency beats chaos when you can repeat your method reliably across multiple rounds.
- Use the wind to your advantage. If you can anticipate gusts, you can adjust your throw and improve your odds. If not, embrace the comedy of failure and adjust your plan mid-shot with a flourish.
- Manage your space. The boundary rope is not just decorative; it’s there to keep the burrito within a playable area. Respect the line, or risk a cut from the referee (aka the neighbor with a loud whistle).

### Technical Tips
- Foot positioning matters. A stable stance, with one foot slightly ahead of the other, helps you transfer momentum more predictably.
- Release timing is everything. A crisp release reduces wobble and increases your chances of a clean, satisfying landing.
- Spin can be your friend or your enemy. A light spin can stabilize flight against a mild wind, but too much spin makes it unpredictable. Practice with a few throws to find your ideal torque level.

### When to Play It Safe vs When to Go Big
- Safe play is for gatherings with kids, slightly older relatives, or neighbors who don’t appreciate chaos. Safe plays still yield giggles, but they keep the game channeling a more gentle energy.
- Big throws and dramatic arcs are for showtime moments. They invite the crowd’s applause and the occasional dramatic fail, both of which are essential to the game’s charm.
