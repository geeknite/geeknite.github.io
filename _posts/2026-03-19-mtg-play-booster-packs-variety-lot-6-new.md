## What You Should Expect Inside Each Pack
Let’s be practical. Each standard MTG booster pack contains 15 cards: 10 commons, 3 uncommons, 1 rare or mythic rare, and 1 land card. In a mixed-sets lot, you’re riding the wave of variability: your six packs may feature a mix of evergreen cards, brand-new mechanics, and the occasional quirky reprint that makes you grin or groan in equal measure.

From a gameplay perspective, the real upside of a varied lot is the chance to stumble into synergy that you didn’t know you needed. If you’re new to booster packs, here’s a tiny primer on the rarities you’ll encounter:

- Common: The bread-and-butter of any draft; these fill your deck and ramp up your chance to draw a real game-changer later.
- Uncommon: The color of the butter-poised knife; these often enable your early game plan or shore up critical interactions.
- Rare: The central pièce de résistance of the pack; the one piece that can tilt games, spawn new combos, or create cherished nostalgia when pulled as a specific reprint.

- Land cards: Not glamorous, but vital. The dual-lands and fetch-lands sometimes appear in fold-out flavors that are almost as beloved as the rares for the strategic flexibility they offer.

## Card Quality, Condition, and Shipping Realities
One of the more practical considerations with any booster bundle is the condition and shipping experience. If you get six new boosters, you hope that the packaging is intact, the packs are factory-sealed, and the cards aren’t bent by a disgruntled postal worker who used the box as a makeshift drum. In the majority of cases, you’ll be fine: modern MTG boosters are designed for mass distribution, and reputable sellers take care to ensure packaging remains mostly pristine. That said, a few caveats:

- Handling: Expect some minor surface wear on the outer packaging from transit. This is usually cosmetic and doesn’t affect the internals, but it’s worth noting for the collector in you.
- Card condition: New packs can sometimes show tiny factory nicks or edge wear on the outermost cards due to the mass production process. It’s rare, but not unheard of. If you’re chasing pristine condition for display, you might want to inspect upon arrival and contact customer service if necessary.
- Shipping times: Depending on your location and the seller, shipping might take a few days to a couple of weeks. If you’re buying this as a gift or want to ensure a certain game-night alignment, factor in the delivery window and consider expedited shipping if available.

The key takeaway here is: treat booster bundles as a fun surprise, not as a guaranteed premium collectible. They’re fantastic for social play and hobby engagement, and the occasional perfectly preserved mythic is the cherry on top. The rest is all about the stories you’ll tell after opening those packs around a table full of friends.
