### Cleaning and Maintenance
Life happens, and rugs get dirty. The Colleen Slate Runner is no exception. Here is the practical routine most households will find adequate:
- Vacuum weekly to remove dust and pet hair. A canister vacuum with a rotating brush tends to lift dirt from the fibers without causing frizz or pile collapse.
- For spills and stains, use a mild detergent with water and blot—do not rub aggressively, or you risk color fade or fiber damage. Most common coffee, juice, or sauce spills clean up without drama.
- Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the fibers and dull the slate coloration. If you need a deeper refresh, a professional cleaning every year or two can extend life, but for many homes, routine spot cleaning suffices.
- A quick steam refresh can help dislodge embedded dust without pulling up the fibers. Just be mindful of heat and moisture on synthetic fibers, which can cause unwanted changes in texture if overdone.
