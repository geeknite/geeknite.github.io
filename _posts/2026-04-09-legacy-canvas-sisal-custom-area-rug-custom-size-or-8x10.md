### The Custom Size Experience: What to Expect

When you order a custom size or a standard 8x10, you may encounter minor differences in weave density or color due to the natural variations of sisal fibers. That is not a defect; it is the price of authenticity. The rug is not a perfect synthetic product that can be mass produced to mirror every inch, and that is part of its charm. The rug edges may appear slightly uneven if not cut precisely; you can remedy this with careful trimming or by using a sturdy edging with a good rug pad. It is worth noting that custom orders might add a little lead time, so plan accordingly if you are decorating around a move-in date or a home theater launch party.

## Durability, Underfoot Comfort, and the Real World

Sisal is a tough cookie. It handles foot traffic, chair wheels, and even a stray remote that falls on the rug during a 4 A M movie blackout. Underfoot, you will feel the fibrous texture, which some people describe as massage-like on bare feet. It is not a plush hallway carpet, but it is a solid foundation for a busy living space. In terms of noise, the rug helps dampen floor sounds slightly, especially on wooden or tile floors. The downside is that heavy furniture with sharp legs can cause indentations if left in place for too long; this is a general caution for all natural fiber rugs. If you plan to shuffle your living room around a lot, you may want to consider temporary furniture glides to protect both the rug and the floor.

### Spills, Pets, and Stain Risk

Sisal fibers are naturally resilient but not stain-proof. Quick action matters. For liquid spills, blot with a clean, dry cloth and avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper into the fiber. For stubborn stains, consult a reputable rug cleaning service; many specialists can handle natural fiber rugs without soaking the fiber. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade the fiber or cause discoloration. If you have pets, a small amount of cat or dog hair can be removed with a vacuum or lint roller, but you might want to use a floor mat near the kitchen to minimize the risk of muddy paw prints. For ongoing maintenance, a weekly gentle vacuum is enough to keep dust and grit from embedding into the fiber.
