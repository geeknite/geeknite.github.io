## Setup and gameplay: the core mechanics, with a few updates for 2024

### Setup
1) Open the sealed box and dump the decks onto the table. 
2) Shuffle the black and white decks separately. 
3) Choose your first judge. The judge role rotates around the table. 
4) Deal a fixed number of white cards to each player (typical play uses 10 cards per player, though many groups trim or expand this number depending on session length).
5) Determine the game pace: who reads fast, who puns slowly, and who silently judges the room to determine what’s allowed tonight.

### Turn structure
- The judge draws a black card (the prompt).
- Players submit one or more white cards to fill in the prompt, face-down.
- The judge shuffles and reveals all submission cards.
- The judge selects the winning submission; that player collects a token (or the black card, depending on your house rules).
- The role of judge passes to the next player in order.

The 2024 edition keeps the core flow intact: quick rounds, fast laughs, and the potential for a single line to define the night. It adds a little polish for new players while preserving the chaotic energy veteran players adore.

### Example round (without reproducing specific card texts)
Black card: “Why is it so hard to quit this job?” White cards submitted include a mix of earnest resignations, meme-worthy one-liners, and a few that show the room’s dark sense of humor. The judge, with timing that suggests both judgment and mischief, reveals the contenders and awards the round to the card that lands with the perfect balance of shock and cleverness. Laughter erupts, someone accidentally knocks over a water bottle, and the night continues with renewed momentum. It’s a rhythm game sometimes, a storytelling showcase other times, and always a conversation starter.
