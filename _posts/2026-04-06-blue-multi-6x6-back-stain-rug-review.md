### Care and maintenance

A big part of owning a rug is knowing how to care for it without turning your living space into a science project. Routine care is the foundation.

- Vacuum regularly using a gentle setting. The goal is to remove dust and soil without pulling fibers out of shape.
- Rotate the rug every 3-6 months to ensure even wear. Feet do not all walk in the same direction, and neither should your rug.
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and extend life. The pad also buffers foot traffic and seating from rough subfloor surfaces.
- Clean spills promptly. Blot, do not rub. Rubbing can push stains deeper into fibers. Use a mild cleaning solution appropriate for the fiber type and test in an inconspicuous area first.

Stain removal quick guide

- Water-based spills (coffee, juice): Blot, then dab with a mild solution of water and a mild detergent. Rinse with clean water and blot dry.
- Oil-based spills (butter, salad dressing): Blot, apply a little baking soda to absorb oil, then vacuum. For stubborn cases, use a stain remover suitable for the rug fiber type.
- Red wine: Act quickly. Blot, apply a small amount of white vinegar diluted with water, then blot again. For longer-term staining, repeat with a proper stain remover.

Note that the above are general guidelines. Always check the rug material specification and test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous area before applying it to a larger stain. Some fibers react oddly to certain cleaners, which may cause discoloration or fiber damage.
