## How the Complete Collection plays
Dixit is a storytelling party game with a simple mechanic that rewards creativity more than correctness. One player becomes the storyteller for a round and picks a clue inspired by one of their cards. The other players select a card from their hand that best matches that clue, and all selected cards are revealed. Players then bet on which card was the storyteller’s, scoring points for guessing correctly and for making others guess incorrectly. The ball rolls on and the storyteller role passes around the table.

In the Complete Collection the core loop stays the same, but the expanded card pool and additional variants elevate the table talk in several entertaining ways:

- More cards means more nuanced clues. You can craft clues that are intimately personal to your taste, or broad enough to create a friendly (and occasionally heated) debate about which card fits best.
- Expanded decks increase the likelihood of accidental theme crossovers. You will casually propose a clue that could apply to multiple cards, only to reveal that your clue fits the wrong dreamscape entirely. This results in laughter and a chorus of I knew it was yours but I still voted for mine because dramatic tension.
- Some expansions add new scoring twists or alternate rules, which can refresh the experience for seasoned players while keeping the core friction intact. Do not fear the new rules; treat them as spice rather than garnish and you will be fine.

## Mechanics and strategy for storytellers
If you are the designated storyteller, you are wearing two very tall hats: a guide and a trickster. Your clue should be just right — not too obvious, not so obscure that your best friend suspects you are auditioning for a reality show about cryptographers.

- Start with a personal angle: a memory, a mood, a color that only you know well. This gives you an authentic lead and reduces the chance that opponents will guess from sheer randomness.
- Pepper in mystery but avoid revealing the card outright. A good clue can feel almost on the nose while still being ambiguous enough to encourage discussion.
- Use your fellow players as a signal: if the room erupts in a chorus of a certain card’s laughter or sigh, you may be onto something. But be careful; laughter can be contagious and misunderstandings are part of the charm.

If you are not the storyteller this round, your job is to interpret, not to reveal. You want to weigh the unnatural dream logic against the group’s taste. The art can guide you, but your interpretation will decide whether you gain points or watch a close friend celebrate a bold misread. The expansion cards add new textures to this dance, so stay flexible and trust your instincts; sometimes the wrong guess is still the right vibe for the room.
