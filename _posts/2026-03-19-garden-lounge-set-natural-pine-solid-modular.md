## H2: Durability, Weather Resistance, and Maintenance

Pine can be a drama queen when exposed to the elements, but with the right care, it behaves like a dependable sidekick. Expect the wood to age gracefully with sunlight and seasonal rain, but plan for a maintenance routine to keep the finish in top shape.

Maintenance plan:
- Seasonal sealant: Reapply a protective sealant every 1-2 years, more often in harsh sun or salty coastal air. This keeps moisture at bay and reduces the risk of surface cracking.
- Cleaning routine: Wipe with a damp cloth, then dry. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the finish.
- Cushion care: If your cushions live outdoors most of the year, consider weatherproof covers or storage during heavy rain or winter. Quick-dry fabrics help when you forget to bring them inside after a sudden shower.
- Storage: When you’re not using the set for extended periods, store cushions in a dry, ventilated space to avoid mildew and odor buildup.

Real-world experience: After three months in a coastal climate, the pine developed a patina that enhances the natural grain without compromising structural integrity. The hardware showed no signs of corrosion, and the cushions retained color well, assuming you keep them away from the direct sun for long stretches.

If you’re evaluating, pine is part of a bigger family of options with different price points, maintenance demands, and aesthetics.
