## How to assess the condition and decide if you want to buy
The condition of a vintage deck translates into its price and charm. Here are practical tips:

- Inspect edges and corners: the edges are the most fragile part. If you see extensive rounding, creases along the edges, or deep gouges, the deck loses some of its display appeal.
- Card faces: color fade or discoloration can affect the deck's appearance. Light foxing is common, but heavy staining reduces desirability.
- Box integrity: a clean, intact box is a premium feature for collectors. Damage to the box can push prices down even if the cards are pristine.
- Completeness: confirm the card count and the presence of the associated instruction sheet or pamphlet if included. A sealed or near-sealed box adds a premium in many markets.

If you are shopping online, ask for high-resolution photos of both sides of the cards, the box, and the instruction sheets. A credible seller will appreciate your diligence and provide transparent details.

## How to store and care for vintage cards
Storage matters. You will want to protect the deck without altering its character. Consider:

- Sleeves: use card sleeves for each card if you plan to play occasionally. Sleeves preserve color and protect edges during handling.
- Box storage: store the deck in its original box if possible. Use silica gel packets in the storage area to control humidity—but not inside the box with the cards.
- Environment: avoid direct sunlight, high heat, and damp basements. Vintage cards love a stable climate more than a cosplay convention loves capes.
- Handling: wash your hands before handling to avoid oils and dirt transferring to the cards. If you have long nails, be extra careful to prevent scuffs.
