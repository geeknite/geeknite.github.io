### Gameplay Overview
Dixit is about telling a story, but the actual play structure is simple:
1) The storyteller picks a card from their hand and gives a clue.
2) Each other player selects a card from their hand that best matches the clue.
3) All cards are revealed and players vote on which card they think belongs to the storyteller.
4) Points are awarded based on how many players guessed the storyteller’s card.

The maths of Dixit is elegant in its simplicity: a subtle approach to clueing yields points for the storyteller if a few players guess correctly, but not so many that everyone knows the card. It’s a balancing act—clever but not too clever. In 3rd edition, you can lean into this balance with the new scoring tokens that are easier to parse at a glance, and with the quick reference guide in the rulebook that helps you decide when your clue might be too risky.

If you want to see a practical example, consider a round where the storyteller says a line from a poem you’ve all read and riffed on many times. Some players will recall the exact line. Others will imagine a moment that hints at the same image. The latecomer to creative wordplay may shout a phrase that is completely off base but still ends up charming someone. That joyous unpredictability is Dixit’s magic.

### Strategies and Tips
- Start with a safe bridge: Use clues that most players understand, then add a twist that only a few will catch. That way you reward clever interpretation without losing half the table.
- Choose cards with evocative imagery: Certain cards have clear visual motifs that can anchor a clue. Others are more abstract. Practice will tell you which ones work for your group.
- Think about your audience: Some players respond to literary references; others love pop culture. A good storyteller reads the room and chooses a clue that aligns with the group’s taste.
- Balance is everything: If your clue is too obscure, no one will guess; if it’s too obvious, you’ll lose points. The sweet spot is often a phrase that invites a shared memory that isn’t obvious to everyone.
- Leverage variant modes: If your group is new, start with standard rules. If you’ve played a bunch, try the speed round or team mode to add spice and a new dynamic.
