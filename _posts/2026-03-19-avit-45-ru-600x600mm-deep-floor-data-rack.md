## Assembly and Setup: Getting It Ready for Action
### Tools and prep
You will need basic tools: a screwdriver set, a couple of wrenches, and the patience to align rails with all the grace of a cat walking on a keyboard. The rails are adjustable, which is great for fine-tuning your internal clearance but can be fiddly if you’re in a rush. The packaging includes the necessary screws and bolts, but you may want to keep some extra cable ties (or Velcro) handy for the post-assembly cable wizardry we all love.

### Step-by-step notes
1) Unbox and verify all parts against the included hardware list.
2) Attach the bottom frame and ensure it is square. A square rack sits squarely in your life, much like a well-behaved USB drive in a chaotic USB universe.
3) Mount the rails and slide in the 19-inch equipment. Start with heavier items at the bottom to lower the center of gravity and keep the rack stable when you open the door during maintenance.
4) Install the rear panels and secure the doors with the provided locking mechanisms.
5) Route cables through the integrated channels. Start with power and network cables on the edges, leaving space for airflow between devices.
6) Perform a test boot and check for any clearance issues. If something screams, you probably forgot to align a mounting rail or tighten a bolt near the bottom. Listen to the rack; it will tell you what it needs.

### Weight considerations
A loaded Avit 45 RU rack will feel heavy, as you would expect from a steel fortress designed to house multiple servers. Casters, if installed, help with repositioning; always lock them when the unit is in a final position to prevent accidental rolling during maintenance.

## Performance: Airflow, Cooling, and Maintenance
### Airflow and cooling design
The Avit 45 RU uses a combination of a perforated rear panel and an air intake on the sides to promote airflow. In practice, this means you get decent airflow through the cabinet when the front doors are closed, which is fine for most standard server gear. If you have dense racks with hot-swappable blades and high-power devices, you might want to add additional cooling fans or an external cooling strategy. The idea is simple: air should flow from front to back with as little obstruction as possible. The less air turbulence, the happier your hardware and the more stable your temperatures.

### Cable management finesse
Cable chaos is a real thing, and it has a personality. The Avit includes integrated cable management channels and knockouts to keep things tidy. If you like a clean look with minimal tangling, you will enjoy running cables through the channels and securing them at the edges. The challenge comes when you have to route power and data behind a dense row of equipment and then re-cable after a maintenance window. In our experience, plan for extra slack in a few critical lines to avoid pulling connectors during service.

### Maintenance and accessibility
Maintenance is a game of access. The front door and rear panels are designed to swing open without obstruction, giving you access to devices for upgrades or maintenance without turning into a contortionist. If your rack sits in a tight closet, you will appreciate the depth and the door swing clearance. Remember, most maintenance tasks go faster if you label cables as you install them and keep your documentation up to date.
