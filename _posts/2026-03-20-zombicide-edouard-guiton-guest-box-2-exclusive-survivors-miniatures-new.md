## Painting, Display, and Hobby Notes
A big part of why people pick up guest boxes is the painting journey. Here are a few practical tips to maximize your experience:

- Prime early, then base coat with a mid-tone. The contrast in Edouard Guiton’s designs gives you natural shadow pockets that will benefit from deliberate shading.
- Use a brighter highlight on exposed edges to emphasize the dramatic silhouette—the black to white edge lighting works wonderfully here.
- Consider a base color that contrasts the weapon material. If the blade is metallic, a dark, almost charcoal base for the leather accents can make the metal pop.
- Practice posing photos. The dynamic swirls of fabric and the tension in the limbs are perfect for dramatic close-ups to share on social media or in your painting group threads.

For tabletop use, remind your players that these are heroic survivors, not world-ending gods. The scale remains consistent with standard Zombicide minis, which means you can slip them into almost any scenario without weird size mismatches. If you want to push the display-o-rama further, stage them against a backdrop of ruin-drenched urban terrain and let some subtle weathering on their gear tell a small war story.
