## Assembly and installation

### Tools required

You will typically need basic tools: a screwdriver (Phillips), an adjustable wrench, and a measurement tape. Some kits include an Allen wrench set. The real skill is patience; the first screw may pretend to be a Spartan, but perseverance pays off when the back panel slots in like a puzzle piece. 

### Step by step

1. Unbox with ceremony but not with a sledgehammer approach; inspect all panels for damage.
2. Assemble the common backbone frame on the floor in the desired orientation. This usually involves four corner posts and horizontal cross members.
3. Insert the desk surfaces into the frame and secure them with the provided fasteners. Do not over-tighten; your monitor will thank you later.
4. Install the glazing panels into their tracks, ensuring smooth glide and alignment with the frame. A shim here and there may be required for a perfect seal.
5. Attach the side panels to form the L shape, then snug down all hardware to spec. A moment of panic may arise if you realize you forgot a bolt, but rest easy: most kits include a spare. You are not the first and you will not be the last.
6. Cable management grommets and channels go in last, allowing you to route power, data, and maybe a USB hub without turning the workspace into a spaghetti bowl.

The standard desk height with these units balances comfortable typing with screen viewing. A monitor arm is recommended if you plan to swap between laptop use and a larger monitor.
