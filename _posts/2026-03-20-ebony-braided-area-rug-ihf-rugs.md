## Materials, Build, and Feel: The Braided Advantage
Braided rugs often walk the line between casual and durable. The Ebony braided rug in this line-up is built to be sturdy enough for daily life but soft enough to feel comfortable under bare feet. The core idea with braided construction is straightforward: multiple fabric strips braided into lengthwise cords, then braided again into a thick mat. ## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Lifespan Outlook
Cleaning a braided rug is a little more forgiving than a tight weave or a fluffy shag. The braided structure tends to trap less lint and pet hair than fluffy piles, which makes daily upkeep simpler. Here are practical guidelines to keep the Ebony braided rug looking fresh:

- Vacuum regularly with a carpet maker’s brush attachment on a low setting. Avoid aggressive suction that may loosen edge braids over time.
- Blot spills promptly with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fabric. Mild detergent or stain remover appropriate for cotton blends can help with tougher spots.
- For pet accidents, treat with a mixture of warm water and gentle detergent, then blot dry. If odors linger, a quick sprinkle of baking soda on a dry rug and a rest for a few hours can help, followed by a vacuum.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially in high-traffic zones.
