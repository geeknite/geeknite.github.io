## Painting and assembly tips (expanded)
When you get a batch of guest minis, you might be tempted to dive right in with a paintbrush that has seen better days. Here are expanded practical tips to avoid chaos:
- Dry fit the arms and weapons before glue. This prevents awkward gaps and misalignment.
- Use a reliable plastic cement or cyanoacrylate glue, depending on your preference; the key is to avoid excessive glue that can smear onto the miniature.
- For more dynamic basing, consider basing inserts that mirror the guest's theme, such as neon puddles for a cyberpunk guest or scorched earthy tones for a post-apocalyptic figure.
- Paint the faces early; it can be tricky around masks or helmets. If you prefer, you can go with a strong skin tone and let clothing carry the color load.
- Consider a quick dry brush for metallic highlights to bring out edges without overpowering the sculpt.

A common approach is to undercoat in black or gray, block the main colors, apply a wash or two to bring out detail, and then layer up highlights. If you are new to this, the guest set can be an approachable starting point for a small painting project that does not require perfect skill to look good on the table.
