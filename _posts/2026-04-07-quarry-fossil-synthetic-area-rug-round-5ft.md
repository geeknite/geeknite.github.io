## Maintenance and Cleaning
### Everyday care
Synthetic fibers are notoriously maintenance-friendly. The Quarry Fossil rug benefits from routine vacuuming (low to medium suction) to remove dust and debris. A quick weekly pass will do wonders for keeping the pile looking fresh and avoiding that “dryly dusty” aesthetic that can accumulate in high-traffic zones.

### Spills and stains
Spills happen. The recommended approach is to blot—yes, the classic blot technique—without rubbing, which helps prevent fiber fuzzing and color bleed. For tougher stains, a mild carpet cleaner or a solution of water and gentle dish soap can be used. Always perform a spot test in an inconspicuous area first to ensure no discoloration occurs. The synthetic fibers generally resist staining better than natural fibers, but no rug is invincible; treat staining promptly for best results.

### Cleaning frequency and washing
Full-on machine washing isn’t typically advised for most area rugs, and the Quarry Fossil is no exception. For deeper refresh, consider professional cleaning or shallow hand-cleaning for localized spots. If you’re meticulous about a pristine look, you can occasionally use a gentle steam clean, provided you allow the rug to dry thoroughly to prevent mildew or warping. It’s not a spa day for the rug, but it’s a spa day for your room’s cleanliness.

### Maintenance pitfalls to avoid
Avoid bleach or harsh chemicals that could degrade synthetic fibers. Don’t yank on snagged fibers; instead, trim them with sharp scissors if you notice any loose ends. And beware of the power vacuum setting that could lull you into thinking the rug is a trampoline; keep the suction at a moderate level to preserve pile height and prevent unnecessary wear.
