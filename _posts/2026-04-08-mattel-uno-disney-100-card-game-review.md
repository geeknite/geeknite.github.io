## How to play this 100-card variant

If you already know UNO, the transition to the Disney 100-card deck is mostly a matter of accepting a few packaging and pacing changes. The core rules remain the same: you match cards by color or number, play action cards to shift the flow, and try to be the first to shed all your cards. The 100-card deck naturally stretches the game, so you will encounter longer stretches of strategy and occasional stall moments where players try to maneuver around a lack of compatible cards.

### Setup and starting the game

- Shuffle the 100-card deck thoroughly.
- Each player draws seven cards to form their starting hand.
- The top card of the remaining deck starts the discard pile, and you are ready to go.

The sweet spot for family play is to designate a maximum number of turns before reshuffling if the same 7-card hand is repeatedly unhelpful for a few rounds. This keeps the session from spiraling into endless stalemates. It is a gentle way to manage expectations when playing with younger kids who may not yet grasp the nuance of card counting or color-flipping strategies.

### Course of play and pacing

Expect longer rounds thanks to the extra cards. The first few games often feel a bit slow as players learn how the Special Mystery Foil Card interacts with the rest of the deck. After a couple of rounds, you will start to sense the deck's rhythm. If you want a fast-paced variant, you can implement some house rules like limiting the number of turns before reshuffle, or introducing a timer for each turn to keep the action moving. If you want a longer, more strategy-heavy experience, allow more card-draw opportunities and linger on the interaction between the foil card and the wilds for additional mischief.
