## Overclocking and Tuning: Pushing for a Little Extra Pepperoni on the Slice
One of the irresistible joys of “OC” cards is the promise of extra frames, a few extra MHz, and the occasional heroic voltage tweak. The RTX 5060 Ti WindForce OC is designed with overclocking in mind, but you must respect the laws of physics, thermals, and your power supply’s patience.

What you can expect:

- Clock room: The card ships with a factory OC that gets you a few more FPS over the stock configuration. The true overclocking headroom varies by sample silicon, cooling integrity, and silicon lottery. For most users, a modest manual increase can yield measurable gains without pushing temperatures into the red.
- VRAM margins: With 8 GB of GDDR6, you’ll have some headroom for memory-heavy titles, but pushing memory clocks aggressively on this platform is a double-edged sword. If you do push memory speeds, monitor for stability and memory thermal throttling. 
- Power vs. performance: Expect the usual sweet spot to be around a modest core clock increase paired with a balanced memory offset. The WindForce OC cooler is capable, but you’ll want to fine-tune fan curves to avoid a sudden, surprising ramp that might interrupt your gameplay with audible noise.

Our take: if you enjoy tinkering and you have a case with decent airflow, you’ll likely enjoy some headroom here. If you’d rather plug-and-play with few variables, you’ll be comfortable with the pre-tuned OC profile and a respectable jump in performance without needing to dive into voltage settings.
