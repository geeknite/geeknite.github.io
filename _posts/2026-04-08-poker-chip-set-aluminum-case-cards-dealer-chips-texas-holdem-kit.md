## Maintenance and Longevity
This is where the real practicalities come into play. Longevity is not just about how long the kit lasts; it’s about how well you can keep it presentable and functional over the course of dozens of nights. Here are the maintenance habits we recommend:
- Regularly wipe the chips with a soft, dry cloth to remove oil and fingerprints. A microfiber cloth is perfect for this, and it helps keep the chips looking vibrant and new.
- Avoid exposing the aluminum case to heavy moisture or extreme temperatures. While the case holds up well, prolonged exposure to humidity can affect the foam inserts and adhesives over time.
- When not in use, store the kit in a dry, cool place. This minimizes the risk of warping or damage to the cards and accessories.
- If you shuffle aggressively, inspect the edges of the chips for chipping or cracking. If you notice any degradation, rotate or replace those particular chips to maintain consistency across the set.
- Clean the foam inserts gently with a vacuum or a soft brush to remove crumbs, dust, and stray card corners. The foam is resilient but not invincible; a little care goes a long way.

With a sane maintenance schedule, you’ll be able to enjoy this kit for several seasons of home games. It’s not a disposable novelty; it’s a steady workhorse that earns a reputation for reliability when used as intended.
