## Rules and Variants
UNO rules are famously simple: match color or number, play a special card to alter the flow, and aim to be the last one with cards in hand. The 40th Anniversary Edition adds a few optional variants to keep the adrenaline pumping beyond a standard game night. If you’re a purist, you can ignore these and still enjoy the core game; if you’re a collector who loves new twists, these are a nice touch. 

### Base Rules (for the newcomers)
- Each player starts with seven cards.
- The top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile.
- Players must match either color or number; action cards alter play (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, Wild Draw Four).
- If you don’t have a playable card, you draw one. If that card is playable, you may immediately play it, depending on house rules.
- When you have one card left, you must say "UNO" before your turn ends; failing to do so gets you a penalty drawn from the deck or a light-hearted scolding from your aunt. 

### Anniversary Variants
- Team Mode: Pair up with a teammate across the table; communicate through subtle eye twitches and the occasional nod if you want to sacrifice your pride for victory.
- Cooperative Nightmares: Everyone works together to reach a shared ending objective before the deck runs out of cards—think UNO meets Pandemic, but with more yelling.
- Collector’s Edition Flair: When you play a Wild or Wild Draw Four, the card reveals a tiny gallery card that features a retro UNO design; flip it to reveal your new favorite meme. 
- Quick Shuffle, Faster War: A recommended variant that shortens rounds by cutting starting cards to five to keep nights moving in a brisk, caffeinated tempo.
