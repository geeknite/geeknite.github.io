The kit under review is marketed as a fit for the 5-speed variant of the Caddy 3, so if you drive a 6-speed or a TDI with a twist of the knob that’s more than a five-speed romance, you’ll want to look elsewhere. ## Installation tips: a DIY guide worth a cup of coffee

If you’ve ever swapped a laptop sleeve or replaced a bicycle seat, you have some of the skills needed for this install. The key steps are simple, and the worst thing that can happen is you misplace a hex bolt while grinning at your own reflection in the rear-view mirror.

1. Gather your tools and the kit. A basic set includes a small screwdriver, pliers, and perhaps a torque wrench if you care about perfect spec. Some kits provide the hardware; others leave you to scavenge from old trim.
2. Remove the old knob and gaiter. Depending on your model, you may need to twist the shaft counterclockwise to unscrew the knob. Be mindful of any springs or clips that may be attached to the old assembly. Put them aside in a tiny dish you will forget later and then swear you never saw.
3. Clean the shaft and the surrounding area. A quick wipe-down removes dust, grease, and the aura of 2003 laziness that occasionally clings to older interiors.
4. Fit the boot frame. Slide or snap the frame into place, ensuring the gaiter sits correctly around the opening. If your kit includes an adjustable frame, set it to the shallow or deep seat to match your preference.
5. Attach the new shift knob. If the knob is threaded, screw it on gently but firmly; avoid overtightening, which can strip the threads and make you feel like you’ve installed a tiny, expensive paperweight. If the knob is push-fit, apply the supplied adapter and press until it clicks.
6. Check alignment and clearance. Move through all gears to confirm that the gaiter does not rub on the center console or the dash. If there is rubbing, you may need to adjust the boot frame height or trim a little of the gaiter material.
7. Clean up and admire. A quick wipe of the knob and gaiter with a microfiber cloth removes fingerprints and reveals a showroom-like finish that would make even a dealership blush.

If you want a more cinematic feel while performing the install, imagine you are a space engineer retrofitting a shuttle with a new, more tactile control stick. The more dramatic your narration, the more fun the process becomes.

Maintenance is the boring part that actually matters. Clean your gaiter with a mild soap solution and a damp cloth. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the fabric or the leather-like finish.
