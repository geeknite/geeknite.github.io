## Build quality: the tangible truth behind the hype

### Sculpting detail and silhouette

Fortune Row models use bold silhouettes that stay legible across the room. Cloaks billow with cinematic weight; jackets have crisp lapels; sigils catch light without turning into a blob. In bright light or macro shots you might notice slightly softer edges on some cape folds, but nothing that ruins tabletop readability.

### Material, casting, and cleanup

The resin is firm, not brittle. Casting is clean with minimal flash. Expect small nub nubs at cuffs or tiny feed marks along edge lines. A quick snip and light sanding will do the trick. Primers adhere evenly and surface textures hold paint well, which helps if you plan gradient transitions on cloaks.

### Poseability and assembly

Poses are purposeful but not overly fragile. Some variants include alternative heads so you can mix and match without a full toolkit. Joints provide enough room for natural posing while maintaining stability, so you won't need to anchor them to a diorama base to survive a game night.

## Paint quality and recommended approaches (Expanded)

Fortune Row favors a restrained palette with moody lighting—think urban twilight rather than neon rave. Painted with contrast paints or traditional layers, you can achieve a dramatic look with fewer steps than you might fear. Here are expanded tips:

- Prime with neutral gray to preserve fine detail.
- Block midtones to establish city gloom; avoid washing out with too many saturated tones.
- Use blue-gray washes on cloaks to simulate rain-soaked look and shadow depth.
- Pick metallics for sigils or badges to create focal glints without shouting cosplay.
- Seal with matte varnish to preserve the noir mood.
