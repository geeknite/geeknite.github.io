## Paint quality and recommended approaches (Expanded)

Fortune Row favors a restrained palette with moody lighting—think urban twilight rather than neon rave. Painted with contrast paints or traditional layers, you can achieve a dramatic look with fewer steps than you might fear. Here are expanded tips:

- Prime with neutral gray to preserve fine detail. A gray primer keeps the lines sharp, especially on sigils and crests.
- Block midtones to establish city gloom; avoid washing out with too many saturated tones. Build up shadows gradually rather than dunking the entire figure in a single wash.
- Use blue-gray washes on cloaks to simulate rain-soaked look and shadow depth. A touch of greenish or purplish bias can imply age and street-level weathering without looking cartoonish.
- Pick metallics for sigils or badges to create focal glints without shouting cosplay. A restrained metal on the emblem catches the eye as you’d expect in a smoky tavern, not a marching band.
- Seal with matte varnish to preserve the noir mood. A satin finish can also work if you want the glint of streetlamps to feel just a touch more real.

### Step-by-step painting plan (one path to 2k-word glory)

1) Prep the model with a light scrub under warm soapy water to remove any mold release residue. Pat dry. 2) Prime with neutral gray or a cold white. 3) Block the base colors with midtones matching your noir palette (slate, charcoal, muted browns). 4) Layer lighter tones on highlights along edges where light would hit—think cloak rims and cheekbone planes. 5) Apply a controlled wash to pull out the folds without muddying the silhouette. 6) Add accent colors for sigils and hardware. 7) Finish with a couple of precise edge highlights to push readability from tabletop distance. 8) Seal. 9) Optional: weathering powders brushed lightly along the hems for a rain-drenched urban vibe. 10) Take photos in soft light to capture the mood—remember, lighting is a miniature painter’s best friend.

### Tips for moody noir lighting at the table

- Place a single warm LED to cast dramatic shadows across cloak folds.
- Use a blue-tinted backlight to hint at night sky beyond the alley.
- Keep table clutter low to preserve silhouettes and avoid distracting reflections.
- Use a dedicated cloth under the miniatures to reduce glare on glossy paint surfaces.
