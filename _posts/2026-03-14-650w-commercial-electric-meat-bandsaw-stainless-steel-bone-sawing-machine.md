### Hygiene, Cleaning, and Sanitation
Sanitation is non-negotiable in meat processing. The machine’s stainless surfaces make cleanup more predictable, and the smooth table minimizes crevices that trap fat and bone fragments. Wipe-down routines should include a food-grade sanitizer after each shift, with special attention to the blade guard and any exposed edges. ### Maintenance and Long-Term Care
Long-term reliability hinges on routine maintenance rather than dramatic single-event repairs. Here is a pragmatic checklist:
- Regular blade tension and alignment checks. A mis-tracked blade increases wear on wheels and guides.
- Clean and lubricate the blade path per manufacturer guidelines.
- Inspect safety guards and switches to ensure they operate smoothly.
- Replace blades as soon as edge quality deteriorates or cutting becomes inconsistent.
- Keep the motor and belt area free of meat scraps and moisture to prevent shorts or corrosion.

## Maintenance and Cleaning: The Ritual That Keeps It Alive
This is the part where we tell you the truth: bandsaws are not a one-and-done purchase. They require ongoing care to keep delivering reliable cuts. The 650W model shines in this area because its design emphasizes straightforward access to the blade path and guard system, making routine maintenance less painful than you’d expect.

- After every shift: wipe down the table, guard, and blade path; check blade tension; ensure the blade tracks true.
- Weekly: inspect wheels and bearings for unusual wobble or noise; grease or lube where recommended by the manufacturer.
- Monthly: perform a more thorough cleaning and check for any signs of corrosion, wear on the blade guides, and blade replacement readiness.
