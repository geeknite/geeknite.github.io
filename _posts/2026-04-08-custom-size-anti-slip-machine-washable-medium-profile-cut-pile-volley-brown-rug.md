### Maintenance and Washability: Cleaning Like a Pro

Here’s where the anti-slip and washability features converge to create a practical magic trick. The “machine washable” label usually means the rug can survive a gentle cycle, not a demolition derby. It’s essential to check the care instructions—some piles are designed to tolerate machine washing on a delicate cycle at cool or warm temperatures (often around 30–40°C, which is roughly 86–104°F). A good rule of thumb is to use a mild detergent and avoid bleach that could degrade the fibers, color, or backing. Air drying is typically recommended to preserve the backing’s grip and prevent warping. If you’ve got a small home washer, the rug might need to be cleaned in portions or with a lighter load to ensure the agitation doesn’t distort the pile.

The idea behind machine washability isn’t just ease; it’s the freedom to reclaim your weekend from the tyranny of stubborn stains. The Volley Brown Rug accepts this burden with a certain stoic grace. If you’re worried about colorfastness, many synthetic fibers hold up well to repeated wash cycles, though you should still test colorfastness on a corner before a full wash. ### How to Measure and Order Custom Size

1) Measure the room: length and width, including any irregular corners. 2) Consider furniture layout: avoid placing edges flush with walls; leave a small mat around seating areas for ease of movement. 3) Add a border: a comfortable 6–12 inches around the room can create a defined space without making the rug look undersized. 4) Take into account door clearance: ensure the rug doesn’t interfere with opening doors. 5) Decide on pad compatibility: if you’re on slippery floors or you have a high-traffic zone, a rug pad is a wise companion. 6) Check the cutting tolerances: most makers offer a small margin for trimming after delivery to accommodate walls that aren’t perfectly square. 7) Plan for placement: if you’re working with a busy layout, you can use two or more smaller rugs to anchor different zones rather than one gigantic piece that swallows the room.

### Care Instructions: A Quick Reference

- Vacuum regularly with a beater bar off or set to a low setting to avoid wooling or excessive shedding on initial cycles. 
- For machine washable models, use a gentle cycle with cold or warm water and mild detergents. 
- Air dry flat to maintain the rug’s shape and backing integrity. 
- Spot clean any stains with a mild solution; avoid bleach that can fade or damage the fibers. 
- If curling occurs at the edges, place a weight along the border for a few days to encourage flatness.
