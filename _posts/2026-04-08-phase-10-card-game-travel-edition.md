## How to play (Travel Edition edition: a quick refresher)
Let’s get you up to speed fast. The core of Phase 10 remains: complete specific card combos across ten predefined phases, moving from one phase to the next as you succeed. The Travel Edition keeps the structure familiar, but it’s tuned for shorter sessions and easier setup in tight spaces.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a hand to each player. The exact starting hand size is consistent with the standard Phase 10 rules and remains short enough to be manageable in a hotel room.
- Place the remaining deck in the center as a draw pile.
- Keep the discard pile nearby for quick access.
- Determine the first player by a simple method such as the highest card or a coin flip, then proceed clockwise.

### Gameplay flow
- On your turn, you draw one card from the draw pile or the discard pile, then discard one card to end your turn. Simple, right? The Travel Edition keeps this mechanic, so you’re not learning a whole new set of moves on a red-eye leg.
- Your objective is to complete the current phase. This means putting together the specific combination of cards required for that phase. For example, you might need two sets of three, or a run of seven, or a combination of a run and a set. Once you’ve met the requirements, you lay down your cards to show you’ve completed the phase.
- Completing a phase advances you to the next phase in the sequence for the rest of the round. If you finish early, you still continue playing to improve your overall score and position.
- The Skip and Wild cards introduce chaos, allowing you to block others or adapt if your hand isn’t cooperating. In the Travel Edition, these cards behave consistently with the standard rules, meaning the strategic elements you enjoy stay intact even in a compact format.

### End of round and scoring
When every player has completed the current phase, the round ends. Players tally their penalties (if any) and move to the next phase for the next round. The exact scoring method mirrors the traditional Phase 10 scoring cadence, with the aim to minimize points across all rounds. The Travel Edition keeps the math approachable, which is critical when you’re juggling a layover and a snack break.

### Quick strategy notes for travel sessions
- Favor flexibility: in tight spaces, you’ll want to be able to adapt your hand quickly. Wilds are your friends here, allowing you to convert tricky cards into useful pieces of your phase.
- Watch your tempo: shorter sessions mean every discard matters. Don’t burn through your cards too quickly unless you know you can recover in the next couple of turns.
- If you’re playing with a mixed group, write down the players’ phase progress at the end of each round. It’s a small habit that pays off in big laughs later when someone tries to bluff their way to a better score with a dramatic “I’m this close” gesture.
