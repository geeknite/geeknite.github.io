## Size and Fit: What Works Where

Rug sizing is where many folks trip over their own furniture legs, quite literally. The Cambridge Contemporary Grey Rug is commonly sold in a handful of standard sizes that fit typical living room layouts, home offices, and bedroom ensembles. Here are general guidelines you can adapt to your space:

- 5x8 ft (150x240 cm): Ideal for small to medium living rooms, or under a compact coffee table in front of a sofa. It anchors a seating area without swallowing the space.
- 8x10 ft (240x300 cm): A classic choice for larger living rooms or open-plan spaces. This size helps define the main seating area and offers generous walking clearance around the furniture.
- 6x9 ft (180x270 cm): A flexible option for mid-size rooms or bedrooms where you want a larger feel without overdoing it.

If you are working with a space that has a long, narrow shape, try running the rug lengthwise under a sofa or bed to elongate the room. Tip from the field: you can use a rug under a desk or workstation to delineate your tech zone without visually overpowering the rest of the room.

To check if a rug is too small or too large for your room, use a simple rule of thumb: leave about 8 to 24 inches of floor visible around the rug in seating areas for balanced framing. If you cannot see the floor at all around the rug, you probably went too big; if you can see most of the floor around it, you likely went too small. These are the kind of universal guidelines that feel like a cheat code but are really just common sense dressed up as geometry.

## Cleaning and Maintenance

### Everyday Care

Maintenance is where this rug shines. Regular vacuuming on a low setting keeps the surface looking fresh and prevents grit from grinding the fibers into a dull finish. A quick weekly routine is all you need to keep it looking sharp. If you have kids or pets, you might want to invest in a quality stain remover or a gentle enzyme cleaner for occasional accidents. Most common spills clean up with blotting and a mild cleaning solution. Remember, always blot, never rub — you don’t want to fuzz the fibers into a shapeshifting monster of lint.

### Spot Cleaning and Stains

Stains happen to the best of us. Coffee, red wine, mysterious sauce blends from last night’s dinner experiments — all of these can be managed with prompt action. For most household spills, a blotting approach plus a mild soap solution does the trick. If a stubborn stain lingers, test cleaners in a small inconspicuous area first to avoid color transfer. The general rule is to treat as soon as possible; leaving a spill to dry is like letting a malware creature set up shop in your system — avoid the drama if you can.

### Rug Pads and Floor Protection

A good rug pad dramatically improves footing, prevents slipping, and reduces wear by absorbing some of the impact from foot traffic. For this rug, a thin to medium-density rug pad should work well on wooden or tiled floors. The pad also helps to keep the rug flat and prevents curling at the edges — a common ailment that makes your living room look unfinished. If your floor is particularly slippery, a pad with non-slip grip is a wise investment. Your future self will thank you when the weekend arrives and you step onto a perfectly aligned rug without performing a stealth check on the floor edges.
