## What is Baraja Española, and why 50 cards?
Baraja Española is one of the oldest and most characterful playing card traditions in the world. It is the deck that shows up in Spanish gaming culture, used for everything from serious trick-taking to casual family curbsides of pizza and memes. Traditionally, the deck features four suits: Oros (gold coins), Copas (cups), Espadas (swords), and Bastos (clubs or cudgels). Each suit usually carries cards labeled 1 through 7, plus 10 (Sota), 11 (Caballo), and 12 (Rey). That’s 10 cards per suit x 4 suits = 40 cards.

But… you found a listing for 50 cards. That usually means someone has added extras—jokers, or a variant that includes additional numerals or extra face cards to align with popular “packs” for family games or for “Tarot-like” gameplay, where the top end might be used for a tarot subset.
