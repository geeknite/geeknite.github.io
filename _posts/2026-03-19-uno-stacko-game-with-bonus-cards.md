## What is Uno Stacko and what do Bonus Cards do?
Uno Stacko is a mashup of Jenga style block manipulation with Uno styled colors. Players take turns removing a block from anywhere but the top, then place it on top of the stack. The goal is to avoid toppling the tower while strategically advancing color and block placement. The Bonus Cards deck adds a layer of unpredictability. These little cards alter the flow of the game by forcing swaps, changing rules for a handful of turns, or introducing mini objectives that can swing momentum in surprising ways. The combination creates a game that rewards careful nerve, memory of what remains in play, and the willingness to lean into a risk for a potentially big payoff.

The vibe is retro yet contemporary, a perfect gadget for geeks who enjoy tactile play and a little chaos as a spice. It is not a heavy strategy title. It is not a pure dexterity game either. It sits in the middle, like a well balanced drink at a party where someone forgot to turn the volume down on the soundtrack.

## Components and setup
### What you get
- 1 stack of 54 interlocking blocks in UNO inspired colors
- A Bonus Cards deck with a dozen or so cards, depending on edition
- A small instruction leaflet that doubles as a reminder sheet for quick reference
- A compact carry tray or sleeve for transport

### How to set up
1. Assemble the tower using the block pattern from the classic Jenga style play: level by level with alternating block orientation. The aim is a tower tall enough to feel dramatic without tipping over the moment a block is pulled. 
2. Shuffle the Bonus Cards and place them facedown within easy reach. Decide if you will draw new cards after each successful move or only at specific moments as dictated by the card text. 
3. Determine the turn order. A quick alternative is to start with the youngest player or to let the tallest among you declare themselves the Stack Overlord for a round of glory. 

### Why the setup matters
A solid setup is not just about aesthetics. A well built tower reduces arguments about whether the block snap was a fair toppling or a gravity ting with a mind of its own. The Bonus Cards add a little wiggle room in the early stages, which can be essential to keep the group engaged and the tower from becoming a predictable slog.

## Bonus Cards: what they add to the chaos
### Common card effects you might see
- Swap Hands: You and a neighbor trade your current hands. This can be hilarious or terrifying depending on how many colors you can’t stand to draw. 
- Draw Again: Reroll one more card and apply the effect to your next turn as well. It ratchets up the tension fast. 
- Color Lock: A color becomes locked for a set of turns, forcing players to think about which blocks still matter for the current state of the tower. 
- Block Multiplier: Add extra blocks to the top when you place one, giving the tower a bit more height and a lot more risk. 
- Skip the Top: The current top block cannot be pulled by anyone this turn. It buys time but cools off the room’s energy just long enough for someone to get nervous.

### How they shift the pacing
Bonus Cards turn a zen stacking exercise into a party game that can morph with every draw. A pair of bold cards can turn several safe turns into a mini stress test, as players calculate whether a risk is worth the potential reward of a new advantage or the possibility of a spectacular topple. The cards serve as narrative beats, letting players craft stories of near wins and dramatic collapses that your group will tell for weeks.

## How to play with Bonus Cards: a walkthrough
### The basic loop with a twist
- On your turn, you choose a block to remove from anywhere but the topmost level. If you remove the topmost block and the tower falls, that is a glorious fail that may spawn a post-game legend. 
- Place the block on the top, maintaining the alternating pattern of the tower. The tower grows taller and more precarious with every turn.
- If you hold a Bonus Card, resolve its instruction as part of your turn or at the end of the move, depending on the card text. Always read the card aloud to increase the communal amusement and to remind everyone that you are playing a game, not writing a software manifest.
- The round continues until someone makes the stack topple, or a pre set objective is achieved such as meeting a highest block count without collapse, or fulfilling a specific card directive that ends the game early.

### A quick play scenario to illustrate the vibe
Imagine a table of four players. The tower is tall enough to demand respect and a stable posture. Player A pulls a block that keeps the block from kinking and notices a Bonus Card on the table that says swap hands. They exchange hands with Player C, who discovers a color mismatch that requires a strategic swap next turn. Player D then uses a Color Lock to trap the color red for two rounds. The tension peaks as Player B pulls a green block that is just shy of tipping the tower. The stack shivers, someone gasps, and the entire table erupts in laughter when the tower finally yawns and collapses, leaving everyone dizzy with delight and a little bit dizzy from the echo of that plastic knock.
