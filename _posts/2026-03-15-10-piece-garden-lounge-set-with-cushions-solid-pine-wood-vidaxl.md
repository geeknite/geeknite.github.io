The not-so-good news: you will still need to torque things to a stable tightness, and a little patience helps. The assembly process is usually straightforward, with a logical sequence: build the base frames, attach the seating components, secure the table, and finally place cushions. If you’ve put together a bookshelf, you’ve got the feel. If you’re new to flat-pack furniture, be prepared for a learning curve and a sense of accomplishment when you realize that a stubborn screw finally goes in after you align the holes properly. 

### Tips for a smooth build
- Sort the hardware before you start. It saves you from playing a guessing game with Motherboard-Mode: Build edition.
- Don’t overtighten the screws. Wood gives a little, and overtightening can leave you with stress cracks that you’ll regret when the sun comes out and you want to lounge.
- Use a gentle touch when assembling the cushions into their covers. The last thing you want is to pop a seam before you even enjoy the first sunbeam.
- If you own a power drill, use it on low torque to speed up assembly, but stop before you over-tighten. A delicate touch is the friend of outdoor furniture longevity.

## Maintenance and longevity: pro tips for the long haul

- Wash cushions with mild soap and water, then air dry. Avoid submerging the cushion cores; they are not flotation devices for your next pool party. 
- Clean the wood with a soft brush and a mild cleaner to avoid staining or residue buildup that can affect the wood’s finish. 
- Re-seal or re-stain the frame periodically to maintain color and water resistance. Light maintenance now saves a potential repainting or replacement later. 
- Cover when not in use, especially during winter or prolonged rain. A good cover increases the longevity of both frame and cushions and helps keep your set looking showroom-fresh longer.
