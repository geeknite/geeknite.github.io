## How to play
Pocket Phase 10 GO! uses the same core mechanic as the original Phase 10. Plan ahead, collect sets and runs, and press your luck on discards. The pocket version streamlines some rules for speed and space, but it does not remove the tension.
Turn order: each player draws one card from the draw pile or from the discard pile. After drawing, you can lay down your current phase if your hand contains the required combination. If you already completed a phase, you can add cards to your own laid out phase or help your opponents by discarding strategically.
Phases: The typical progression goes from phase 1 to phase 10. In this edition, you might see minor adjustments to the exact set requirements to keep the game moving in a small package. The important thing is that you know that you must complete each phase in sequence.
Wilds and skips: The original has wilds and skip cards. The pocket edition keeps them, which is a relief for those who rely on a string of lucky draws to stay alive. A wild can substitute any card in a combination; a skip can disrupt another player's turn for the cost of losing your own chance to win that round.
Ending a round: When a player has laid down their current phase and has all the cards they need, they can go out. The rest of the players score points for the cards left in their hands. The scoring remains merciless, and that is part of the charm.

If you find yourself in a situation where you have to entertain a crowd of mixed ages, this game scales well enough to include novices and seasoned Phase 10 veterans alike.
In practice, the game plays quickly enough that you can squeeze in several rounds during a layover. The downside is that the shorter sessions can tempt you to cut corners on strategy. Resist this; there is no stealth mode in a travel edition; the game still rewards planning, memory, and a little bit of bluffery.

## Strategy tips
- Start with a plan, but be ready to pivot when the top of the deck reveals a set you did not expect.
- Pay attention to the discard pile; a single discarded card can reveal a lot about your opponents phase progress.
- Save a wild for a critical stage; you never know when you need a substitution to finish a phase.
- Do not be afraid to pick up a card even if you think it is not part of your plan; sometimes a single card can unlock a new path to a finished phase.
- Use the travel pouch to keep your space tidy; the last thing you want is to drop your entire stack of phases in the aisle while you are trying to catch a connecting flight.
