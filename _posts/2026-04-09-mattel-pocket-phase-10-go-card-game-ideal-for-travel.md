## How to play — a quick refresher with travel refinements
Pocket Phase 10 GO! uses the same core mechanic as the original Phase 10. Plan ahead, collect sets and runs, and press your luck on discards. The pocket version streamlines rules for speed and space, but it does not remove the tension.

### Turn order and actions
Turn order: each player draws one card from the draw pile or from the discard pile. After drawing, you can lay down your current phase if your hand contains the required combination. If you already completed a phase, you can add cards to your own laid-out phase or help your opponents by discarding strategically. The key is patience—on a plane, the patience is in your seat neighbor's yawns, not in the deck's willpower.

### Phases and progression
Phases 1 through 10 remain the backbone, with the pocket edition possibly tweaking exact set requirements to keep the game moving in a tiny package. The important thing is that you must complete each phase in sequence. Yes, you are forced to respect an order even when you're tempted to rebel and go straight to phase 7 via a magical wildcard loophole.

### Wilds, skips, and disruption
Wilds and skip cards survive the switch to pocket form. A wild can substitute any card in a combination; a skip can disrupt another player's turn for the cost of losing your own chance to win that round. It’s the classic tug-of-war—grit your teeth, drop the wild at exactly the right moment, and hope your seatmate doesn't have a mental tracker of your hand.

### End of round and scoring
Ending a round happens when a player lays down their current phase and finishes with the cards required. The rest score points for the cards left in their hands. The scoring remains merciless, and that mercilessness is part of the charm. Pocket Phase 10 GO! is a little more forgiving than its giant cousin in terms of setup, but it still punishes misplays with the slight sting of “if you only had saved that wild.”

## Strategy tips — tighten your scrappy travel toolkit
- Start with a plan, but be ready to pivot when the top of the deck reveals a set you did not expect. On a plane, a pivot is not just strategic; it’s cabin etiquette.
- Pay attention to the discard pile; a single discarded card can reveal a lot about your opponents phase progress. Reading the room also means noting who is too polite to grab a reflecting card and who is hoarding the wild like it’s the last square on a Tetris board.
- Save a wild for a critical stage; you never know when you need a substitution to finish a phase. In turbulence, a wildcard becomes your wind sock.
- Do not be afraid to pick up a card even if you think it is not part of your plan; sometimes a single card can unlock a new path to a finished phase. The travel edition rewards opportunism as long as you can convince the table you are not chaos incarnate.
- Use the travel pouch to keep your space tidy; the last thing you want is to drop your entire stack of phases in the aisle while you are trying to catch a connecting flight. A tidy setup equals fewer elbows in your future.
- Time your score-keeping; when the flight attendant drops a beverage cart nearby, you’ll thank yourself for not fumbling with a page of scribbles.
