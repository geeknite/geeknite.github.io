## Assembly and maintenance

Assembling a six-piece set is a delightful puzzle if you enjoy life’s little mysteries. You will need a few basic tools — typically an Allen key and a screwdriver; nothing that requires you to search for a metric windstorm in your shed. Expect about 2 to 3 hours for one person with some patience, a few choice swear words, and a helper who enjoys coffee breaks that resemble the productivity of a tiny independent nation. Labels are generally clear, but the diagrams might look like a modern art interpretation of Ikea’s more direct instructions. Take your time to dry-fit the pieces before you tighten the final bolts. The key is to avoid overtightening; wooden frame hardware can crack and then you end up pretending that the sofa is a performance piece about anti-symmetric geometry.

Maintenance tips: oil the acacia frame every season with a suitable exterior-grade wood oil or sealer. Wipe down with a damp cloth after use to remove dust and pollen that might accumulate like tiny campers on a camping trip. If you experience heavy rain or humidity, consider bringing cushions indoors or into a covered area when not in use for extended periods. Cushions with removable covers can be laundered per label instructions which typically involve machine washing on a gentle cycle with mild detergent. A word of caution: avoid hot water and heavy bleach unless you want a cushion color akin to a bad era neon. Polypropylene fiber or olefin fabrics fare better in the long run with minimal color bleed and high resistance to moisture.

## Maintenance myths debunked

A common myth is that outdoor furniture is maintenance-free; the reality is that there is a spectrum from nothing to everything.
