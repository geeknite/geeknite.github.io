## Authenticity, quality control, and what to inspect
If you’re serious about a Kashan rug, you’ll want to perform a careful inspection (or hire a pro). Here’s a practical checklist for evaluating a 7x12 ivory Kashan in person or via detailed photos:

- Pile height and evenness: A well-worn pile can be charming, but avoid rugs with irregular pile that suggests stretching or upholstery-grade wear. A smooth, even feel under your hand is a good sign of durability.
- Foundation: Look for any signs of backbone issues like loose threads or a sagging warp. A weak foundation means uneven wear and possible future repair costs.
- Repairs and restoration: Small knots replaced, fringe damage, or patchwork should be disclosed by the seller. If extensive repairs are present, you’ll want a clear plan for maintenance and potential impact on value.
- Odor and staining: Some scents fade with airing; others may linger. If the rug has a strong musty odor, factor in cleaning costs and potential dye bleeding risks.
- Dye stability: Check for color bleed or “fuzzing” around the edges when you rub with a white cloth. Good condition rugs should resist color transfer; fading in sun-exposed areas is normal, but abrupt color shifts can be a red flag.

An important note: pre-owned rugs can have minor repairs that are nearly invisible to the untrained eye. That’s not a red flag—it can actually be a sign of careful restoration. If a rug has undergone professional restoration, request documentation or a verbal summary of the work done, including padding replacement, knot tightening, or fringe repair.

## Care basics for a pre-owned Kashan rug
Caring for a vintage piece requires a blend of respect and practicality. Here are some practical guidelines to keep your ivory Kashan looking handsome for years to come:

- Routine vacuuming: Use a gentle vacuum with a wand attachment (no beater bar on high settings) to remove surface dust. Do this weekly to prevent grit from grinding the wool fibers, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Sun exposure: Prolonged direct sunlight can accelerate fading. Rotate the rug or use window coverings to protect the ivory background from turning into a warm beige color in some spots.
- Spills and stains: Blot (don’t rub) spills immediately with a clean cloth. For tougher stains, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in Persian rugs. Avoid DIY chemical cleaners that might erode dyes.
- Padding: A high-quality rug pad under a 7x12 rug helps reduce wear, keeps the rug flat, and improves sound insulation. It also gives you a little cushion under your footsteps—ping-pong without the risk of bruising your feet.
- Rotating wear patterns: A simple rotation every 6–12 months can equalize wear across the surface, especially if your furniture sits in fixed positions. This keeps the sheen even and the patina balanced.

If you’re curious about more formal cleaning and maintenance strategies, you can check resources like [Persian rug care guides](https://www.thespruce.com/persian-rug-care-2031109) for broad principles. It’s not a substitute for a professional cleaning, but it’s a good starting point for comfort and confidence.

## A practical care schedule: keeping ivory Kashans radiant
The best way to maintain a pre-owned Kashan is to adopt a simple, repeatable care routine:

- Daily: Quick light vacuuming in the direction of the pile to remove surface dust. Avoid aggressive back-and-forth motion that can loosen fibers.
- Monthly: Focused edge inspections and spot checks for fringe integrity. If you notice fraying, consult a pro for fringe stabilization.
- Seasonal: Rotate the rug to equalize wear and shield it from direct sun in peak hours. Consider using UV-blocking window coverings.
- Yearly: A professional cleaning by a rug specialist. They’ll refresh the pile, balance dyes, and re-tack the rug to its foundation if necessary.

A good rug professional will also be able to advise you on the best pad for your floor type and how to adjust the pad’s thickness to minimize wrinkles without compromising feel underfoot.
