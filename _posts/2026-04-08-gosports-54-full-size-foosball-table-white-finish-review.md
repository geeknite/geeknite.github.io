## Maintenance and durability: keeping the magic alive
Foosball is one of those games where routine maintenance pays off in dividends over time. Here are the practical care steps that will help you keep your Gosports 54-inch looking and playing well for years:

- Wipe the surface after play: A quick wipe with a microfiber cloth removes oils from hands and snack crumbs that accumulate during tournaments. If you spill something sticky, use a mild soap solution and dry thoroughly.
- Inspect and tighten periodically: After a few weeks of heavy use, check the screws and the rod joints. A few turns with the appropriate Allen key can resolve small wobbliness before it becomes a bigger issue.
- Keep the rods clean: If you notice resistance or stickiness, a quick wipe along the rods can restore smooth travel. Avoid aggressive lubricants on the metal surface; foosball rods do not need heavy lubrication and some products can attract dust and gunk.
- Protect the finish: While the white finish looks pristine, it shows smudges more readily than darker finishes. A regular wipe-down helps maintain that showroom look. Avoid harsh cleaners that can degrade the finish; a simple, soft cloth with a mild cleaner is enough.

Durability-wise, the Gosports table handles family life with reasonable resilience. It is not built to survive a storm of roughhousing, but it does handle everyday use with grace and a little elbow grease. If you are bringing this into a room with kids who treat the table like a gallery wall of triumphs, you might want to invest in a protective play mat or a low-profile table cover when the table is idle to prevent incidental scuffs.
