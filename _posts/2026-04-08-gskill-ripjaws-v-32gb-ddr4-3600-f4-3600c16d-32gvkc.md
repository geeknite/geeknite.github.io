## Specifications and compatibility
### XMP profile and timings
G.SKILL packs this kit with an XMP 2.0 profile that aims for 3600 MT/s with CL16 timings. That’s a sweet spot that gives Ryzen and Intel platforms a nice balance of latency and bandwidth. If your motherboard is a bit fussy or you’ve got a silicon lottery moment with your CPU, you might land on CL18 at 3600, but CL16 is the target for most compatible K-series boards. If you want to push a touch further, you can experiment with subtle BIOS tweaks—like slightly increasing DRAM voltage to 1.35V and adjusting subtimings—but this is not a “free upgrade” for everyone. Most users will be happy plugging in, enabling XMP, and enjoying the performance bump.

### Platform compatibility
- Intel 12th/13th Gen and newer family members typically handle 3600 CL16 without breaking a sweat.

## Installation and setup notes
### How to install
1. Power down, unplug, and ground yourself.
2. Open your case and locate the motherboard RAM slots.
3. Insert each module at the correct slot (usually the outermost slots first when using 2 sticks) until they click into place.
4. Power on and enter the BIOS.
5. Enable the XMP 2.0 profile for 3600 MT/s. Save and exit.
6. If your system POSTs and recognizes the kit with the right speed, you’re golden. If not, try reseating the modules or lowering speed to 3200–3600 to verify stability.

### BIOS tips
- Start with the default XMP profile to see if your motherboard agrees with 3600 CL16; if not, you may end up with 3600 CL18 or 3600 CL19 on some boards.
- If you’re chasing the last few percent of speed, you can attempt a very small voltage bump (e.g., 1.35V to 1.37V) along with tighter subtimings. Do this only if you’re comfortable with memory stability testing and know how to recover from a failed POST.
- Run a stability test (MemTest86, Prime95 blend, or your preferred memory stability tool) to ensure there are no instability issues.
