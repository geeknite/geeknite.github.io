## Box contents and first impressions
### What you get inside
The box opens with the kind of dramatic hinge that makes you feel you just bought a treasure chest rather than a board game. Inside, you will find a mix of upgraded components and a few playful luxuries that do not change the rules, but change the vibe. The player boards are replaced by compact, wipe-clean play mats that feel more like a tiny hotel check-in desk than a gaming surface. They are pleasant to handle and easy to store. The tokens are made of a slightly heavier resin than typical plastic, so they clink in the box and in your hand like tiny trophies you earned for not forgetting to yell Phase 2 at the right moment.

## Rules snapshot and how this edition treats them
### Core mechanics intact
Phase 10 remains a set of ten distinct achievements you must complete in order. Each phase requires you to lay down specific sets, runs, or combinations. The Special Collector Edition does not reinvent the wheel here; instead, it makes the wheel shinier. You still draw, discard, and attempt to complete phases in order, with one twist you might appreciate during a heated moment: the slightly more forgiving scoring track and a few optional variants that can speed up or slow down the night depending on how merciless you want your friends to be.

### Variant additions
The edition adds a couple of optional rules for veteran players who want a richer experience without leaving the table. A common variant is a scoring twist that grants bonus points for finishing certain phases with fewer cards left in your hand. The idea is to reward both speed and precision, mirroring the feel of a collector night when everyone is trying to top the leaderboard and look cool while doing it. If you are new to the game, you can safely ignore these variants at first; the classic flow works just fine for a comfortable family game night.

### Setup and pace
Setup is quick. Shuffle, deal 10 cards to each player, and set up the phase track. The Special Collector Edition makes the visual cues easier to follow thanks to the brighter phase icons and the clear separation of each phase on the score track. Expect a game to hover around 30 to 45 minutes for 2 to 4 players, with a longer tail when you add more players or start chasing volcanic stacks of points on the late game tracks. The pace remains lively, as players openly compete for the most efficient path through the ten phases while the rest of the table silently judges your card economy.
