## Cable Management, Power, and Maintenance

Cable management is the unsung hero of any large desk system. The Eternal includes a thoughtful set of grommets, cable channels, and an exit path that allows you to route power and data without turning your floor into a spaghetti factory. Here are some practical tips we gathered during testing:

- Group cables by function: power, data, and accessory cables in separate channels to simplify troubleshooting.
- Use labeled ties: they help you remember which dongle belongs to which monitor later when you upgrade your GPU for the tenth time.
- Plan for future expansion: leave some extra length in cable runs so you’re not forced to re-snake the entire under-desk area if you add a new docking station.

Cleaning is straightforward: wipe down the melamine surface with a soft cloth and a mild cleaner. Avoid abrasive products that might dull the finish. For metal parts, a microfiber cloth does the job nicely. If you’re a keyboard drool veteran, you’ll appreciate the slightly matte surface that doesn’t glare under harsh office lighting.
