## Overview
Skip-Bo is a deck-building-esque card game of speed, memory, and mild arithmetic in a chaotic card carnival. The objective is simple: be the first to empty your stock pile by playing cards in ascending order into building piles. The deck is a mix of number cards and Skip-Bo wild cards. This Mattel set contains all the standard cards you expect: a full deck, plus a box that will (theoretically) survive a backyard storm or at least two rounds of casual shuffling.

### Setup and rules in a nutshell
- Shuffle the deck, deal stock piles to players
- Build piles on the table starting from 1 up to 12
- Use Skip-Bo wild cards as you need
- Draw cards to replenish

For those new to Skip-Bo, think of it as a fast-paced race to play off your stock piles by laying ascending sequences on the four building piles. The details: you can only play cards from your hand or face-down discard piles onto the building piles, and the first player to empty their stock pile wins. The rules are simple enough for kids, clever enough for adults, and occasionally merciless toward the overconfident.

### Gameplay experience
Skip-Bo is a blend of luck and strategy. The order of your draw pile can swing a victory or a crushing defeat. Some rounds feel like you’re surfing a wave of 1s and 2s, while others devolve into a dramatic last-minute scramble to find a 9 to finish a building pile. The chaos is part of the charm, and the facemask-level concentration you’ll bring to the table will be the social glue that binds your group together (or tears you apart in good-natured fashion).

## Rules in depth
### Turn structure
A typical turn consists of drawing back up to five? Wait, no. The actual rules: On your turn, you draw from your stock pile and from your hand to replenish to five cards, you may play from your hand or face-down discard piles to the building piles, you can use wild Skip-Bo cards as any number, and you end your turn when you’re out of plays or decide to pass. The precise rules depend on the version, but the general flow is: draw, play, end turn.

### Strategy tips
- Use Skip-Bo wilds strategically to close gaps where you’re far behind
- Prioritize clearing the stock pile when you can
- Don’t overcommit to too many building piles at once; you’ll clog your options
- Pay attention to other players’ discards to anticipate what they might need
- Remember that a well-timed stock-pile dump can swing the whole table

### Common misreads and fixes
People new to Skip-Bo sometimes forget that building piles must be formed in ascending order. Don’t try to jam a 7 on a pile that’s currently at 9; instead, aim for the next available card. If you’re stuck with a hand full of duplicates, consider discarding aggressively to trigger better draws.
