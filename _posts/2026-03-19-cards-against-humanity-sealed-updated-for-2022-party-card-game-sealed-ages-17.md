## How to play: setup, flow, and a few host tips
Here’s the quick, no-nonsense flow that will get your group through a dozen games before anyone suggests a different activity that doesn’t involve a stack of cards and a referee:

1) Gather 4–8 players (or more, if you’re into rotating Card Czars and a little chaos). One person is the Card Czar for the round. The Czar can be rotating or fixed depending on how aggressively you want to enforce control over the room’s mood.
2) Shuffle the black cards and the white cards separately. The black deck contains prompts like “Why can’t I go to sleep at 3 a.m.?” while the white deck contains humorous, zany, or totally inappropriate responses.
3) The Card Czar draws a black card and reads it aloud. The prompt is now the gravity that anchors the room’s jokes.
4) Each player secretly chooses a white card that they think best completes the prompt and slides it to the Czar.
5) The Czar reads the white cards aloud (sometimes with dramatic pauses, sometimes with exasperated sighs) and picks a winner. The winner gains a point and becomes the new Czar for the next round.
6) Repeat until your group declares a winner, collapses into giggles, or someone forgets to say “card” before the next card is drawn.

Hosting tips for maximum chaos:
- Designate a “no-stare” rule for white cards to prevent tipping your hand early. It keeps the suspense alive and the laughter rolling.
- For bigger groups, consider dividing into two teams and having a rotating judge from each team; ego-protective and surprisingly funny in practice.
- Have snacks within arm’s reach. CAH is a gravity well for hunger and comedic timing: the moment you’re about to declare a winner, someone will request a snack break, which then becomes a punchline in its own right.
- Use a timer if you’re gaming with a live audience that has a tendency to turn every card judging into a courtroom drama. A short time limit adds adrenaline and keeps the game moving.
