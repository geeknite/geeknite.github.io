## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Longevity

### Everyday Care

Regular vacuuming is your friend. A weekly pass with a standard vacuum (brush head on low suction) will keep dust and pet hair from turning into a visible mural. For most spills, act quickly. Blot, don’t rub, with a clean cloth. If the spill is stubborn, a mild detergent mixed with water can be applied sparingly. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability. Avoid harsh cleaners that may degrade the fibers over time. For pet owners, a quick lint-roller pass can help pick up hair between vacuums without pulling threads from the tassels.

### Stain Management

Stains on a rug can feel like a boss fight with a quirky boss mechanic. The general approach is to blot, then apply a gentle cleansing solution and blot again. For red wine or brightly colored beverages, you may need a stain remover that is safe for synthetic fibers. The key is to act fast and avoid scrubbing, which can push the stain deeper and damage the fiber structure. If you are living with kids and snacks, consider using a rug pad underneath to minimize movement and reduce the chance of spills reaching the backing.

### Longevity and Care Cycles

With regular care, the Leilani rug should maintain its color and texture for several seasons. It is not a disposable fashion piece, nor is it a sacred heirloom to be stored away during spring cleaning. It sits in the sweet spot where it remains aesthetically pleasing and functionally practical without demanding constant maintenance.
