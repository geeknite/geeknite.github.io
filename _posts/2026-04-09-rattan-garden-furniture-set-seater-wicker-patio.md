### Maintenance: cleaning and care tips (the ritual of not losing your mind)
Caring for rattan and cushions isn’t a crisis; it’s a weekly ritual that yields a sense of accomplishment rather than existential dread. Practical tips:

- Wicker: wipe with a damp cloth to remove dust, then a gentle vacuum for stubborn debris. If you notice shedding or loose threads, trim carefully.
- Cushions: remove covers and wash according to the label. Mild detergent, air-dry. Avoid machine drying unless you want wetsuit vibes on your cushions.
- Fabric care: for stubborn stains, treat with a mild fabric cleaner and let air dry completely before reassembling. Monitor color fading if you face intense sun; consider UV-protective covers.

Pro tip: store cushions indoors during heavy rain or when you’re not hosting your legendary Sunday brunch that somehow becomes a weekly epic saga. A simple storage bin or a patio storage box can dramatically extend cushion life, turning your couch into a long-lasting star of your yard.

## Durability and weather readiness: will this last through your backyard epic ambitions?
A rattan outdoor set is designed to withstand sun and rain reasonably well, but it isn’t a miracle product. Key durability factors include frame construction, wicker weave quality, and cushion fabric. A well-made set resists fading in the sun and won’t crack under normal conditions. Cushions may show color changes if you’re near the ocean or near the kind of sea breeze that carries more salt than a pretzel factory.

Climate matters. If you live in a place with heavy rain and high humidity, keep cushions dry, use covers, and consider a set with sturdier fabric. If you’re in a sun-drenched zone with occasional showers, a basic maintenance routine and a little shade will keep things singing. The goal is to prevent the wicker from bending under the weight of heavy planters and to avoid the mysterious creak that preludes a dramatic backyard monologue.
