Colorfastness and care considerations
Red dyes can bleed especially on new textiles. The term colorfast is a promise that the rug will retain its colors over time under standard use. It is not a guarantee. If sunlight is intense in your room color migration can occur. Pre washing dye tests and a good rug pad are practical investments. A pad reduces sliding protects floors and helps extend the life of the rug while making footfalls more comfortable.

Care basics:
- Pre vacuum and shake to remove surface dust
- Treat spills by blotting with a clean cloth and using a mild detergent solution
- Do not rub vigorously to avoid fiber damage
- Color fast tests in a hidden area before applying any cleaner over large zones
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear

The Flaw in detail what could it be
In general rug flaws fall into cosmetic and occasionally structural categories. Cosmetic flaws include a seam misalignment a slight color variance a pulled fiber or a tiny snag that is not structurally threatening. Structural flaws include backing issues or uneven surface that may cause persistent buckling. The listing indicates a flaw its exact nature is not repeated in the body of the post. In daily use a minor seam misalignment or a tiny color variance is rarely the end of the world if the rug is placed in a space that can incorporate such a detail. If the flaw reveals itself as a visible irregularity during peak lighting or a dramatic photo moment then the buyer should decide whether that aligns with personal taste or with tolerance levels for detail. The good news is that most modern rugs are resilient to normal household wear and tear and a minor flaw is often invisible in normal living conditions.
