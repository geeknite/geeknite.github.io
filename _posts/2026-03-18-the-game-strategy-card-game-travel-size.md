## How to play in a sentence (and then in depth)

The core loop is simple on the surface but yields surprising depth as players lean into particular card synergies and momentary alliances. The travel edition emphasizes speed and interaction more than grinding engine building. You want clever plays that land quickly, not a three act epic about your decksmithing prowess.

### Core mechanics

- Turn order cycles with a straightforward decision ladder: attack, defend, or set up a mini combo for later.
- Card synergy matters. Your deck becomes a toolbox, not a random assortment of events, and the way you combine cards determines your pace and influence.
- Resource tokens are used to trigger power cards. There is a practical cap, so hoarding is not a viable long term strategy, which keeps the game moving and the table honest.

### Strategy and tactics

Travel size rewards sharp micro decisions and social improvisation. You do not need to memorize a thick rulebook to win; you need situational awareness, a dash of courage, and a willingness to read the room. Practical strategies for real world play include:

- Short term over long term. Focus on mid range objectives that swing momentum rather than chasing an endgame plan that requires the entire table to cooperate with your vision.
- Timing is everything. Power cards arrive on a schedule, and mis-timing a big move can give others a window to pivot and steal your thunder.
- Social misdirection can be a legitimate tactic. A well timed wink or a light jab about alliance politics can shift a round more than an extra card ever could.
- Tempo management. In a travel setting, rounds should feel punchy. If a round drags, the room loses focus and your tactical morale falters.
