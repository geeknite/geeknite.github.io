The grip feels comfortable after extended sessions and doesn’t rub the skin raw during long sequences. ### Safety considerations

Safety is the whole point of a practice blade. Even with a blunt edge, you should wear protective gear—eye protection is non-negotiable for dynamic sequences, and hand protection helps if you are practicing with a non-trivial form. Be mindful of your surroundings; your living room may have a coffee table that looks innocent, but a multisyllabic clumsy swing can turn coffee into a hazard. Treat the sword like a prop that is ready for action in a scene, not a toy you swing near unsuspecting pets or relatives with a curious sense of humor.

## Maintenance and care

Like any prop weapon that lives in a collector’s room, maintenance is your friend. Here are a few quick tips to keep the Honshu Boshin Practice Grosse Sword looking sharp and ready for action:

- Wipe down after use to remove moisture and fingerprints. A soft microfiber cloth is perfect for this task.
- Apply a light coat of protective oil if you live in a humid environment to reduce corrosion or tarnish on any metal finishes.
- Check the grip and guard for any loosening after extended use. If something feels loose, address it early to avoid a sudden wobble that ruins a dramatic pose mid-clip.
- Store in a dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve the finish and avoid plastic degradation over time.
