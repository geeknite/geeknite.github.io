## Maintenance and care: keeping the Honshu alive
Like any tool with a story, UC2629 rewards maintenance. Here are routine steps you can follow to keep your blade and sheath in fighting shape:

- Clean after use: wipe the blade dry to remove moisture, and store in the sheath briefly to prevent rust on the exposed edge.
- Oil care: apply a light coat of mineral oil or knife oil to the blade. Wipe off any excess; you’re not greasing a car part, just keeping the metal from singing hymns to rust.
- Sharpen and hone: if you’re a hobbyist sharpener, you’ll want to bring this up to a more refined edge if you’re into culinary prep or precise cutting. For casual use, a quick honing pass every few weeks is plenty.
- Sheath care: condition the leather occasionally to preserve the patina and prevent cracking. Keep it away from water when possible, and let it dry naturally if it gets damp. A little love goes a long way here.

### Storage and environmental notes
Storing your blade and sheath in a cool, dry place is key. If you’re in a hot, humid environment, consider periodic checks for rust on the blade and cracks in the leather. This blade is designed for everyday carry; it’s not meant to sit in a dusty drawer for years without attention.
