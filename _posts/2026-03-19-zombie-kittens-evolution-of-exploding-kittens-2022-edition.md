## Quick Setup and First Impressions
Setup is simple if you approach it like a ritual: remove the extra rule sheets you will never read again, shuffle the deck, deal 5 cards to each player, place the zombie tokens near the center, and draw the top card to start. The core was familiar from the base game, but the undead theme adds a glow of chaotic opportunity to even the most mundane decision.

This edition supports 2-6 players and typically clocks in at 15-25 minutes per round. The time frame is perfect for a quick party night or a longer chain of rounds if your group is feeling especially reckless.

### The zombie twist on the table
Zombie-themed cards amp up risk and misdirection. Zombie tokens can cause a turn to flip in unexpected ways, forcing players to reconsider whether the safest play is actually the most boring one. The energy on the table shifts from calm subterfuge to a choreography of who can survive a zombie surge without shouting at the room about how unfair the universe has become.

## Gameplay Deep Dive
### Core mechanics
- Turn order is clockwise. On your turn you draw one card and may play any number of action cards.
- Action cards include Attack, Skip, See the Future, and Nope; Defuse remains your critical shield against immediate doom.
- Exploding Kitten still ends your turn in a fiery, spectacular moment if you don’t have a Defuse.
- Zombie cards and tokens add a new dynamic that can turn the board on a dime and keep the table guessing about who is actually in control.

### The evolution concept in practice
The zombie veneer is more than just a cosmetic upgrade. The evolution mechanic threads through the deck with a light touch that keeps things approachable. Instead of a single dramatic moment, you get a cascade of small, sneaky upgrades that nudge players toward riskier decisions and bigger reveals. It’s chaotic, yes, but the chaos is orchestrated and surprisingly readable once you get a few rounds under your belt. The humor lands in the right places; it’s delightful without tipping into gross or cruel.

## Components and Artwork

### Palette and humor

## Strategy and Tips
- Keep track of your Defuse cards; they are the lifeline when everything goes bonkers on the table.
- Don’t overcommit to defense; sometimes a bold draw leads to the best payoff later in the game.
- Watch the table dynamics; players who seem calm at the start are often the ones who spark the most chaotic plays.
- Use zombie tokens strategically; passing a zombie surge around the table can be the difference between glory and embarrassment.

### Advanced strategy notes
If you like speed, trigger an Attack to push the pace and see who panics first. If you prefer a more cerebral vibe, save defense cards to counter the wave of zombie antics and force the table to adapt to your timing. The balance is sturdy enough that mixed-groups will have a fair shot at victory without needing to memorize a hundred special cases.
