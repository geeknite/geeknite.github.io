### Building a successful strategy with this Skip-Bo edition
The card sequencing nature means there is a heavy emphasis on planning for what you can play this turn as well as what you can set up for future turns. Here are a few tried and true strategies that tend to work well:

- Prioritize building piles on numbers you see frequently as wild cards can bridge gaps but you want to avoid using wilds on low-value piles unless necessary.
- Watch the discard piles of other players. In 2 to 6 player games, others may reveal cards that you can anticipate are likely to hit your hand soon. This helps you optimize your draws and your sequences.
- Manage your stock pile thoughtfully. You want to avoid getting stuck with a large stock pile you cannot easily use. If you think a long sequence might be possible, plan to hold on to those key cards until you have a chance to place them efficiently.
- Keep track of your opponent’s progress. In a quick game, you will often have to decide whether to push for a finish or to deny your opponent a strong move by using a card that blocks their sequence.
