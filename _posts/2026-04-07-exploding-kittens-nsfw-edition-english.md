## How to Play (A Quick Refresher for the Already-Exhausted)
Exploding Kittens NSFW Edition borrows heavily from the base game’s flow, which is basically a deck-building sprint through a minefield of colourful feline chaos. The objective remains simple: avoid drawing an exploding kitten. The twist? The NSFW cards introduce new take-that moves, new ways to force players into uncomfortable laughter, and a handful of new rules that require your brain to perform gymnastics while your mouth tries not to snort tea through your nose.

### Setup
- Shuffle the NSFW deck with the standard Exploding Kittens deck.
- Deal each player a starting hand of seven cards (or whatever your group finds funny; yes, we are improvisers here).
- Place the Exploding Kitten card facedown in the draw pile and shuffle. The Defuse cards should be kept close, because you’re going to need them to survive the first few turns.

### Turn Structure
1. Draw a card from the draw pile. If it’s an Exploding Kitten, you’re in trouble unless you can play a Defuse.
2. If you didn’t draw the Exploding Kitten, you can play up to one card from your hand to extend your endurance or to force a friend into a strategic corner.
3. If you drew the Exploding Kitten, you must either use a Defuse (if you have one) or take the consequences as dictated by the card. The consequences can be humiliating or hilarious, usually both.
4. After your action, the turn passes to the next player.

The NSFW edition’s new cards add twists like forcing players to swap hands, challenging them to perform silly tasks, or playing cards that defy the usual expectations in surprising and often awkward ways. The pacing is rapid, and the laughter tends to escalate as the pile of near-misses grows.

### Strategy Tips (From Benign to Banter-Heavy)
- Keep a Defuse in reserve for the late game. You’ll thank yourself when the table hits full chaos and you’re the sole sane person with a single rescue card.
- Use Nope cards strategically. They aren’t just loud; they’re loud and proud, the kind you deploy when a rival tries to force you into a disastrous move and you want to make them regret it.
- The NSFW cards reward risk-taking that is social rather than mechanical. If your friends love roasts and dares, leaning into the sentiment of the NSFW cards will maximize laughter and mischief.
- Don’t be afraid to negotiate with players. If you’ll let me take your turn, I’ll let you choose last on the next round. The social negotiation aspect makes the game feel less like a cold mechanic and more like a party roast with adjudication.

## The House Rules: Customizing NSFW to Your Crew
If your group wants to tailor the experience a bit more, here are some light, optional rules that keep the energy high without pushing boundaries too far:
- Boundaries First Rule: Before you begin, have each player declare a couple of boundaries they won’t cross. Respect them, then laugh when someone tries to push just past them.
- Time Limit Turns: To keep the chaos from spiraling, enforce a 10-second timer per turn. Quick decisions create sharper humor and fewer dead air moments.
- One Dare per Round: If a NSFW card asks for a dare, you’re allowed exactly one dare per round; multiple dares can lead to long pauses and awkward silences, which is not the vibe we want.
- Swap with Consent: If a card requires swapping hands, allow the target to consent to the swap by paying a small price, like singing a silly jingle or telling a quick embarrassing story. It’s more fun than a straight swap and helps everyone stay comfortable.
- Post-Game Meme Session: After the final score, give players 5 minutes to create memes using one or two NSFW cards. The laughter here often outlasts the game itself.
