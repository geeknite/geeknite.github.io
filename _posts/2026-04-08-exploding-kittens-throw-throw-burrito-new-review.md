## How to play at a high level
Before we start nuking your brain with rules, remember the premise: you want to collect a set of three Cat Cards to score points, you want to dodge burrito thrown at you, and you want to avoid a catastrophic draw that makes everyone cry in laughter and pain. The NEW edition streamlines a few touchpoints, but the core experience remains delightfully chaotic.

### Setup
- Spread the play area with the burritos and the deck in the center.
- Shuffle the Cat Cards separately and deal a few to each player (count depends on player count — more players means more chaos, less time to strategize).
- Decide the number of rounds, or simply race to a pre-agreed target score. In Geeknite fashion, we often improvise and declare that whoever can explain the rules the loudest at the start gets a bonus point. It’s not official, but it’s a lot of fun.

### Turn structure
On your turn, you have a few options, all of which involve risk and joy:
- Draw cards until you’re comfortable with your hand size.
- Play a card to trigger a swap or a swap-later effect.
- When a Rule is active, you may need to add some flair to your dodge (think quick step, shimmy, or a dramatic slide across the floor).
- If you flip the wrong card, you might draw a Cat Card that helps you collect a set or triggers a burrito toss in someone’s direction.

The thrill comes from the timing: you want to trigger a Burrito Toss when the table’s rhythm is broken, not when you’re mid-sip of your beverage. The NEW edition keeps the timing tense but adds clearer indications for when a burrito is in motion and when to jump out of the way without losing your dignity entirely.

### Card types and their roles
- Cat Cards: The star of the show. They’re what you’re chasing to complete a set and win a round.
- Action Cards: These can cause someone else to draw extra cards, swap cards, or alter the tempo of the game. They’re your tools for misdirection.
- Unicorn cases and weird little anomalies follow the same spirit as the base game but with a few flavor cards that only exist in the NEW edition. They’re not strictly necessary, but they add a lot of flavor and occasional chaos that makes your brain itch in delight.

### The dodge and throw mechanic
The central gimmick is the foam burritos. You throw them, your friends dodge them, and the table becomes a stage for a ridiculous ballet of improvisation. The foam is soft enough to be friendly, but not so soft that it’s merely ceremonial. You’ll get hit, you’ll laugh, and the burrito will punt in some direction that you didn’t think possible, possibly toward the cat, which is always a risk and a potential disaster for your furniture. Pro tip: practice your best slow-mo dodge for maximum satisfaction.

## Strategy and tips for winning (or at least not losing your mind)
If you’re reading this section, you probably want a little guidance that doesn’t involve sacrificing your social life to a safety manual. Here are practical, not-silly-at-all tips for getting better at Throw Throw Burrito:

- Focus on sets, not cards. It’s tempting to chase one powerful Card, but the real win comes from finishing a three Cat Card set. Plan your picks around that objective and use Action Cards to disrupt opponents on their path to finishing their own sets.
- Read the table tempo. The game’s energy shifts with each toss. If someone has just narrowly dodged and a new round is about to start, you might want to hold back inputs until the moment of impact. Good timing often beats raw aggression.
- Use defense cards strategically. If you see a potential chaos moment forming, hold onto a dodge card to survive a critical turn. You’ll thank yourself when that burrito crooks its arc and misses your face by inches while hitting your closest rival in a glorious arc that earns a chorus of laughter.
- Target the overconfident players. The ones who brag about their “perfect aim” are the easiest mark for a well-timed Action Card. Don’t be afraid to remind them that foam is lighter than their ego.
- Don’t take the loss personally. The beauty of this game is that a single bad throw can swing a round in unexpected ways. The laughter often comes from the unexpected, so embrace chaos and keep the vibe light. If you win gracefully, you get extra points in Geeknite culture. If you lose, you do a dramatic victory lap with a burrito as your baton.

If you’re coming from the base Exploding Kittens experience, here are the quick contrasts you’ll notice in the NEW Throw Throw Burrito edition:

- Increased player interaction: You’ll be nudged to pay attention to others, not just your own hand. That means more laughter and more opportunities to sabotage your friends’ plans in a friendly way.
- Visual refresh: The art style is bolder, the cards more legible, and the packaging sturdier. It’s not just pretty; it’s practical for game nights that occasionally degrade into a foam battlefield.

If you’re torn between the two, remember this: Exploding Kittens is a fantastic game for a quiet night with close friends and strategic card drafting. Throw Throw Burrito is the cousin who brings a megaphone to the party and insists on a chase scene in the hallway. Both are valuable, both are loud in their own way, and both are excellent additions to a gamer’s shelf as long as you’re ready to embrace the chaos.
