## Durability and maintenance
### Cleaning basics
Spill a glass of red wine? No problem—this rug’s synthetic fibers are designed to resist stains and ease cleaning. Blot spills promptly with a clean cloth, then use a mild cleaning solution for more stubborn spots. Avoid bleach or harsh chemicals that could affect dye stability. For routine refreshment, a gentle vacuum works wonders; an upright vacuum with a rotating brush is fine, but be mindful of fringe edges if present.

### Wear and longevity
Low-to-mid pile synthetics like this one tolerate foot traffic and furniture movement gracefully. If you have kids, pets, or both, you’ll appreciate the resistance to pilling and the ease of spot cleaning. The backing typically holds up well against flattening, though you may notice some flattening in high-traffic zones over time—common with many flat-weave-style rugs. Rotating the rug every few months helps distribute wear evenly and prolongs the life of the fibers.

### Stain-resistance myth vs. reality
While the rug is advertised as stain-resistant, no rug is invincible. Substances such as wine, coffee, and certain fruit juices can leave a stain if not treated promptly. The key is to act fast: blot—not rub—then treat with a mild solution. If you’re a persistently clumsy coffee aficionado, consider keeping a small spray bottle of mild detergent and water handy in the cleaning cabinet. Pro tip: test any cleaner on a discreet area first to avoid color transfer.

## Maintenance myths debunked
### Vacuuming frequency and technique
- Vacuum weekly in high-traffic areas.
- Use a beater bar setting if you must, but avoid aggressive suction that can pull fibers out.
- Rotate the rug 180 degrees every few months to even out wear patterns.

### Spot treatment protocol
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth.
- Mix a small amount of mild dish soap with water for stubborn stains.
- Rinse the area with a clean damp cloth to remove soap residue and blot dry.
- For tougher stains, consider a fabric-safe stain remover following the product’s instructions.

### Prevention tips
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and reduce wear.
- Keep shoes off in certain rooms to minimize dirt transfer.
- Place entryway mats at all exterior doors to reduce dirt load.
