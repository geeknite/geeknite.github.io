## How to Play: Quick Start Guide

This edition sticks to the classic UNO rules at heart, with charm additions to bring Pokemon flavor into the mix. If you already know UNO, you can jump right in with a few light modifications. If you are new to UNO entirely, the rule sheet has you covered with a concise summary of how to play in under two minutes.

### Core Rules (UNO flavor)

- The goal is to get rid of all your cards with the fewest points remaining as dictated by card values.
- On your turn you must play a card that matches either color or number of the top discard card.
- If you cannot play, you must draw a card. If the drawn card can be played, you may do so immediately; otherwise your turn ends.
- Special cards alter the flow: skip the next player, reverse play direction, draw two, wild cards to change color, and so on.

### Pokemon Flavor Changes

- The deck includes Pikachu themed art and extra flavor cards that reference Pokemon style effects. These are not a full Pokemon trading card game like intensity but are designed to feel Pokemon flavored and playful.
- Some cards carry subtle power icons that might resemble electric type energy on the card front; these serve as aesthetic flavor and do not alter the base UNO mechanics unless your house rules bring them to life.
- The rule sheet may suggest light house rules such as Pikachu power cards that allow Draw Four style effects once per game, as a playful nod to the Pokemon universe. If you want to implement this, decide before starting and note it in your first turn.

If you are a purist, you can play the vanilla UNO rules and treat the Pokemon cards as simply decorative extras. If you enjoy a lighthearted twist, lean into the flavor and use the suggested Pikachu power cards for a few rounds to ramp up the mischief factor.

### Quick Play Tips for Beginners

- Start with a simple one card draw rule when a player forgets to yell UNO. It keeps things equitable and reduces table chaos.
- Keep the Pikachu flavor cards optional; you can introduce them after everyone has warmed up to the core mechanics.
- Use a timer for turn duration in large groups to keep everyone engaged and prevent stall tactics from taking control.
