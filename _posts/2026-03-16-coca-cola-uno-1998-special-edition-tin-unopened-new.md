Verification tips for unopened time travelers

- Inspect the seal: look for intact shrink wrap or inner seal. Perforations or reseals can be a red flag. A pristine seal is not a guarantee of never-been-opened, but it is a strong indicator.
- Check the tin edge: the seam should be clean and free of dents or signs of tampering. A dented edge could indicate potential resealing or crafting work by a determined hoarder.
- Card stock condition: if you can safely inspect (without breaking the seal), examine the deck for whitening along the edges that would indicate exposure to air. A perfectly crisp deck implies minimal handling.
- Insert or booklet: see if there is a promotional insert, rule sheet, or any additional item within the tin. Its presence (or absence) can affect the perceived originality and value.
- Certification or provenance: in some cases, sellers provide photos of the seal, receipts, or provenance notes. While not a guarantee, it helps construct a credible story about the item’s history.
