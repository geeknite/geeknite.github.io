## Maintenance and Longevity: Cleaning, Care, and Love Letters to Your Rug

Velvet is not a lamprey; it does not attach itself to every passing pet hair and dust mote. But it does appreciate a little routine maintenance. Here are practical care tips to keep the Ivory Marine Faded Velvet Pattern Rug looking glorious for years:

- Vacuum regularly using a brush attachment or a low-suction setting to avoid lifting fibers. Velvet fibers can stand up to regular vacuuming, but you want to avoid aggressive, high-suction passes that could flatten the pile.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Do not rub, as rubbing can push the liquid deeper into the fibers and potentially set stains. For stubborn spots, a mild detergent diluted with water may help, but always test on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fading. The faded look is part of the design, but some areas may age differently depending on sun exposure and traffic patterns. Rotation helps distribute wear more evenly and keeps the appearance cohesive.
- Protect from direct sunlight when possible. Prolonged exposure to strong sun can alter the velvet’s color and sheen. If your room faces a sunny window, consider window treatments that filter UV rays to preserve the rug’s hues.
- Professional cleaning every couple of years can extend the rug’s life and refresh the pile. A pro can lift the fibers gently and restore that plush vibe without the risk of DIY mishaps.
