### Bashing Time Strategy: The Heavy Hitters Approach

Bashing is exactly what it sounds like: you load up your board with Time engines and you punch a hole in the other person’s defenses. The core idea is to build a tempo fortress where every move you make tears down your adversary step by step. In a vacuum, this is satisfying; in practice, it requires spatial planning, card memory, and the ability to read your opponent like a library catalog. The cards that support this path tend to be bigger, louder, and more punishing when they land.

What makes Time Strategy feel different in Codex is the rhythm. It loves momentum — you push, you pressure, you force errors, and you convert those mistakes into a fast track to victory. The downside is that if you miscalculate, you end up with a gap in your defenses that the Finesse player can exploit. A typical early game in Bashing mode involves quick Time boosts that threaten a strong strike in round two, followed by a careful hold to prevent overextension. If you can land two successful bashes before your opponents fully set their tempo, you typically ride a wave that is surprisingly durable.

Key cards in this path tend to reward sustained investment. You will see recurring Time engines that ramp your ability to threaten bigger plays. The payoff often comes in the mid game when you convert board presence into raw damage output and board pressure. The risk is a one-turn misplay that leaves you with a brittle board state and a target on your back. Strategy hinges on your ability to forecast your own card draw and how your opponent will respond to pressure — a skill that becomes easier with repeated plays but remains the real test of the Bashing path.

### Finesse Approach: Reading the Table and Hedging Risk

Finesse is the art of turning the smallest advantages into a victory that feels almost inevitable in hindsight. It thrives on timing, precision, and the occasional misdirection that makes your opponent question their own plan. In Codex, this road rewards careful calculation. You are looking for pockets of opportunity where a single card plays exactly the role you need at the exact moment you need it. It is a slower burn, but it often pays off in long sessions where the board state becomes a canvas for subtle, almost clinical play.

The Finesse deck is built around controlled tempo gains. You do not sprint for a knockout; you weave a thread of control with a few sharp anchor cards, and you let your opponent chase you into a position where every misstep is magnified. The synergy in this lane often revolves around precise card sequencing. The best turns feel like they were choreographed in a calm study, then suddenly erupt into a flurry of tactical precision that leaves opponents reeling and counting their lucky stars that you did not punish them sooner for a minor mistake.

Finesse cards shine when you expect friction. If the opening turns feel calm, you can still stage a dramatic late game by sneaking in a pair of strong reactions that swing momentum with surgical accuracy. The risk is a slower climb to late game power, which can feel bleak if your opening hand is unfortunate or if your opponent hits a harsh early tempo. The reward, however, is a sense of craft — you built a narrative of control and you finally get to show it when the plot twists occur at your terms.

- Turn 4 and beyond: The clock starts to matter.
