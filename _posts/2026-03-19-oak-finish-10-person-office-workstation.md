## Maintenance, Durability, and Longevity
Durability is a big deal when you’re buying a 10-seat workstation. The oak veneer resists routine wear reasonably well, and the finish is relatively forgiving of light scratches. You’ll want to avoid harsh solvents and ensure spills are wiped promptly; many common office liquids can leave residues or dull spots if left unattended. The underside of the desk frame is powder-coated steel, which resists corrosion and stands up to daily wear. If you have a clumsy coffee policy, there’s a good chance you’ll find a few marks that buff out with a mild cleaner and a microfiber cloth.

Warranty is typically a factor people overlook until a joint starts creaking or a panel shows a seam separation. Check with the seller for coverage; standard office furniture warranties often cover structural integrity for a reasonable term and finish defects for a shorter window. In our experience, keep your receipts, document assembly photos, and photograph any damage before the first week of use so you can avoid the “it arrived fine” argument later down the line.

Sustainability wise, oak is widely considered a renewable resource when sourced responsibly. If you’re the kind of buyer who cares about forest stewardship, request certification documents from the manufacturer and consider choosing options that specify chain-of-custody or sustainable veneer providers. It’s not glamorous, but it matters to many teams and to the planet.

## Maintenance and Cleaning Tips
A few simple practices keep this workstation looking sharp for years:
- Wipe spills promptly with a microfiber cloth and mild soap; avoid harsh cleaners that dull the veneer.
- Use coasters or desk mats for drinks to reduce water rings.
- Vacuum or wipe under the desks to prevent dust from grinding into the joints.
- For scratches on the veneer, a dedicated veneer repair marker or wax stick that matches the finish can work wonders.
- Rotate monitors and peripherals occasionally to evenly distribute wear across all modules.
