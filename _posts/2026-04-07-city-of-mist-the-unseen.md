## City of Mist The Unseen: A Geeknite Review

If you came here hoping for a straightforward dice throwing fiesta, you're in the foggy wrong area. City of Mist The Unseen invites you to stroll through fog doled alleys where legends are stitched into your coat and every street corner might be a portal to a mythic identity. This expansion dares you to ask not what you can do but what you should pretend to be in a world where truth is a rumor and danger wears a fedora.

### The Elevator Pitch

City of Mist is a table top RPG that masquerades as a pulp noir detective game with superhero flavor. The Unseen amplifies the idea that everyone carries a mythic identity beneath their ordinary skin and that discovering it can be glamorous and existentially nerve-wracking. It leans into the idea that your power is a secret you must manage, not a lightning bolt you cast.

#### Visuals and Layout

The Unseen keeps the same moody color palette and noir typography that fans expect, with a few new splash pages that hint at unseen factions and whispered histories. The art is glossy without feeling sterile, and there are enough character portraits to help players visualize more obscure archetypes.

![City of Mist The Unseen Cover](/assets/images/city-of-mist-the-unseen-cover.jpg)

### What's inside City of Mist The Unseen

The Unseen adds new play options that focus on stealth, social maneuvering, and narrative control rather than raw power. This expansion is not about giving you more punch; it's about giving you more channels to influence the world around you, and the world resists with equal cunning.

- New Unseen identities and masks that emphasize subterfuge and perception
- A revised power economy that makes social and investigative scenes as important as brawl sequences
- NPCs and factions that operate in the background, pulling strings and leaving only whispers in their wake

The book includes a handful of micro-scenarios that are explicit about the investigative beats—the kind of micro-scenarios that feel like a city is writing them in pencil on a steamed window.

### How The Unseen Changes Play

The Unseen sticks to City of Mist's core dice mechanic, where you roll 2d6 and try to hit a target number. The twist remains: tags and powers are not just numbers; they are the mythic identities you bring into the scene. This expansion leans into the concept of masks, which are the public personas that give you access to unusual arcs and unusual threats.

- Masks as social leverage: The Unseen expands the masks you can play, with niche identities like The Whisper, The Veiled Archivist, and The Night Thread
- Narrative tags: Tags that reflect your character's hidden history and the city’s secrets. The Unseen broadens tags to include more subtle, investigative options
- Scene framing: The expansion includes guidance on shaping scenes so that the detective work and the mythic reveal happen in tandem

For players who love this is the moment where the mask peels back and a secret is revealed, The Unseen is basically a stage set for dramatic reveals. If you want more crunch, you’ll find a few tables for consequence, cost, and complication that feel like they were designed to inspire a slow-burn reveal rather than a rapid explosion.

### Story and Theme: A City Where Myths Are Everyday

The Unseen leans into what City of Mist does best: placing mythic energy into ordinary life. The city is not a backdrop; it is a character that judges your choices, sometimes politely, sometimes with a velvet glove. The expansion adds a layer of intrigue about who is watching, who has power, and how you negotiate a world that blurs the line between heroism and complicity.

There is a strong sense of noir melancholy, peppered with a sense of wonder that can feel contradictory. The Unseen acknowledges that the myth you carry can be both a blessing and a burden, a tool and a leash. It invites players to consider who they are when the mask is on and who they become when the mask is off. The result is contemplative without tipping into pretension.

### Character Options: Masks, Identities, and Opportunities

This section is a playground for creative character concepts. The Unseen introduces new archetypes that fit the city’s vibe while offering enough variety to prevent duplication among players.

- The Veiled Archivist: A librarian of whispers who can extract information from places that don’t exist in any library
- The Night Thread: A weaver of coincidences with a talent for threading events together and turning near-misses into dramatic outcomes
- The Whisper: A spy in plain sight whose words can twist the perception of others without a single punch thrown

The core concept remains: a character’s power is inseparable from their identity. The Unseen nudges players toward roles that are meta-narratives rather than brute force. It is possible to build a powerful team around stealth and social manipulation, but you’ll need to coordinate because one good reveal can topple a delicate plan.

### GM Tips: Running The Unseen Sessions

GM guidance in The Unseen emphasizes careful scene design, pacing that matches the reveal, and a city that reacts in believable ways to the players’ choices. You’ll want to prepare a few threads that pull players toward each other’s secrets while maintaining the tension that makes noir feel earned.

- Build a city that bleeds lore: Have factions with different agendas but overlapping motives
- Use micro-beats: Short, narrative-driven scenes that build toward a bigger reveal
- Reward storytelling: Let players describe how their masks influence a scene, not just what their dice roll accomplishes

This expansion is not a cheat code for battles; it is a toolkit for shaping scenes so that a single moment one line of dialogue or one unexpected coincidence can rewrite the entire night.

### Art, Layout, and Production Value

The Unseen continues City of Mist’s tradition of moody art with cinematic cutscenes. The layout is reader-friendly, with sidebars that provide quick guidance for players and gamemasters alike. If you enjoyed the art direction of the core book, you’ll be happy here; the assets are well-used, and there’s a healthy balance between flavor text and mechanics.

One minor quibble: some pages lean a tad too far into mysterious aesthetics and forget to offer practical examples. You’ll find plenty of play-ready hooks, but a few more fully-worked sample scenes would have elevated the book from nice to have to must own.

### Balance and Replayability

City of Mist is not a crunch heavy game; it’s a storytelling engine. The Unseen does not attempt to dramatically overhaul the core system. Instead, it adds layers that encourage players to lean into roleplay, inference, and dramatic timing. Replayability isn’t about grinding a new build; it’s about exploring different masks and different secrets in each run. The more you lean into the city’s mythic underbelly, the more you’ll discover new ways to connect the dots.

### How It Feels at the Table

In actual play, The Unseen shines when the table trusts the concept enough to suspend disbelief and let the masks guide the scene. If your players enjoy noir vibes, seduction of secrets, and the thrill of a near-miss, this expansion will feel like a natural extension of your nights. If you prefer heavy tactical combat or a rules-heavy approach, you might find the book less appealing. But City of Mist has always been about mood more than metrics; The Unseen reinforces this ethos in a satisfying, if not entirely surprising, way.

### Interlude: A Quick Session Setup

To help GMs, the book includes a quick-start outline. It’s not the most robust recipe you’ll ever bake, but it works well enough to get a three-hour one-shot off the ground. You’ll want to stage your opening scene with a mystery location, a client with a secret, and at least one NPC who is a question mark with shoes. The Unseen guidance helps you sprinkle clues that feel meaningful without turning into a wiki dump.

### Who Should Play This?

- Players who want a heavier emphasis on investigation and social manipulation
- GMs who love weaving conspiracies and piecing together threads
- Groups that enjoy character-driven storytelling with a mythic twist
- Fans who want to explore the city not as a stage but as a partner in crime

If you’re a gamer who likes the idea of mythic identities sliding into everyday life and you find the core game pace a little too brisk, The Unseen offers a slower, more reflective tempo that rewards careful planning and keen observation.

### The Verdict: Is The Unseen Worth It?

Short answer yes, but with caveats. The Unseen is a strong addition to City of Mist if you’re already invested in the core concept and you want to explore more masks, more personal secrets, and more ways to steer the narrative. It isn’t a revolution; it’s a refinement, a polishing of the city’s lore and a deepening of its moral gray areas.

If you’re new to City of Mist, you may want to start with the core rulebook and a couple of lighter adventures before diving into The Unseen. The risk with any expansion is that it can feel overstuffed when you haven’t yet built a connected party. The Unseen doesn’t blow up the system; it invites you to live in the fog a little longer and see what emerges when the mask is held up to a streetlight.

### Minor Strengths and Minor Frustrations

- Strength masks get more flavorful and more valuable in social scenes
- Strength the city feels more alive as secrets spread and factions move
- Frustration a few new tables can feel redundant if you’ve got a packed session

### Final Thoughts

The Unseen is a worthy purchase for those who have embraced City of Mist as a mood-first, story-driven RPG. If your table thrives on character-driven reveals and a city that feels like a living character, this expansion will be a welcome addition. If you’re chasing dungeon crawls or min-max optimization, you may want to pass or treat The Unseen as a side quest rather than a main course.
