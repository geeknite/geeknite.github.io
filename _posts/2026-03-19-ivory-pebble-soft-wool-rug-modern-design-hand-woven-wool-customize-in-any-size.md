## Customization and sizing: the thing that makes this rug a tailor-made experience

### Why size matters (and how to measure it right)
Customizing size is the highlight reel of this product. Rugs that offer precise sizing options make more sense in real rooms. A rug that’s too small can make a room feel chopped up and pedestrian; one that’s too large can swallow furniture and create a “donut hole” around your seating area. The sweet spot for most living rooms? A rug that anchors the sofa setup while still leaving visible floor around the edges. The Ivory Pebble rug often comes in multiple standard sizes, but the real magic is when you can request a tailor-made dimension that aligns with your furniture layout. If you’re unsure, measure your seating area (the front legs of the sofa to the edge of the room) and add at least 6–12 inches on each side. Your future inner designer-self will thank you.

### Custom options beyond size
In addition to size, many sellers offer customization options such as pile height adjustments, fringe details, border treatments, and sometimes a slight variance in color for a batch that’s truly one-of-a-kind. If you want a looser, more organic edge, some craftsmen offer rounded or irregular shapes. Do you want a beveled edge? A soft V-shape? The more you can tailor, the less you’ll have to compromise later.

### Lead times and pricing: what to expect
Custom orders typically take longer than stock items. Plan for weeks rather than days, especially with natural wool and hand weaving involved. Once your ivory beauty lands, you’ll want to give it a few days to settle. Place it on a clean surface, do a gentle vacuum with a low suction, and avoid rough brushes that pull loops. The wool will acclimate to your room’s temperature and humidity, and you’ll notice a more robust loft after a few days.

### Durability for the long haul
Wool’s natural resilience helps resist crushing from everyday use. The dense weave minimizes visible footprints, and the fiber’s elasticity helps the carpet spring back even after heavier furniture is moved occasionally. Should a spill happen, wool’s natural properties make quick cleanups easier—though you still want to treat it as you would any natural fiber: act fast, blot, and avoid rubbing. For stubborn stains, test a gentle wool-safe cleaner in an inconspicuous area first.

### Maintenance routine that actually works
Light vacuuming weekly keeps dirt from embedding into the wool. Use a vacuum with a beater bar set to a low height and avoid aggressive suction that could pull loops. Rotate the rug every few months if you’re in a sun-heavy room; this preserves color uniformity and prevents one side from aging faster than the other. Wool loves some airflow, so if you have a basement or a damp environment, ensure proper humidity control to prevent moths (yes, it happens) and fiber fatigue. If you’re into tech analogies, think of it as firmware updates for your floor: not exciting, but essential for longevity.

### Cleaning tips you’ll actually use
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Do not rub, or you’ll bloom the stain and flatten fibers in a stubborn way.
- For dry dirt, a gentle shake outdoors or vacuum with a brush attachment is ideal.
- For matted areas, a light hand comb with a wool-approved grooming tool can help refresh pile height.
- If you must wash, consult the seller’s care guidelines and use a wool-safe product. Never soak wool rugs in water for extended periods.
