## How It Affects Gameplay
### Speed and Flow
The core purpose of a card holder is to streamline the physical flow of the game. In practice, a Card Holder can speed up turn-taking by reducing the time spent reaching for the deck and sorting out stack order. With more wild cards in the mix, there’s a higher chance of last-minute chaos—someone shouts “Wild!” and a cascade of reverses and skips can tumble onto the table. A sturdy holder reduces the friction caused by fumbling and makes the moment where you pick up a card feel like a small, satisfying victory rather than a cartoony pratfall.

### Card Visibility and Readability
In UNO games, especially with All Wild Card Game, players rely on quick pattern recognition: color groups, number groups, and of course, special action cards. A well-built Card Holder keeps the current hand in clear view for everyone at the table, which minimizes accidental cheating accusations and keeps the game moving. If the design nudges the cards into a pleasing fan, it’s a small but noticeable improvement for quick decision-making. Without crisp visibility, the game becomes a tangle of arms and dialogue that would impress a soap opera scriptwriter but frustrate a math teacher evaluating probability.

### Wild Cards and the Holder’s Role
Wild cards make the UNO world spin with extra vigor. A card holder that doesn’t obscure the wilds or force players to flip their wrists to recognize a Wild Draw Four can be a lifesaver. The All Wild edition amplifies the number of wildcard plays, which may lead to more dramatic, meme-worthy moments. The card holder doesn’t change the rules, but it can impact how easily players track the evolving board state. If the holder interferes with card flushing (the act of sliding cards across the surface with confidence), you’ll want to adjust the position or consider a slightly different model. The key is to maintain readability and fairness, not a new way to cheat by hiding wilds behind the holder.
