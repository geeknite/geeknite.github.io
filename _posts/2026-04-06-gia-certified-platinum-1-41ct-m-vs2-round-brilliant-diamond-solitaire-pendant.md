## The Numbers: Carat, Color, Clarity, and Cut

### Carat Weight — The obvious impact on perceived size
The 1.41 carat weight is not a round number because a round brilliant diamond is measured by carat weight, not mass. Carat is the metric that correlates to visual size as you hold the pendant at arm's length, though the cut plays a big role in how large the stone looks in life. A 1.41 ct round diamond tends to look substantial on a pendant but still comfortable for everyday wear, especially when mounted in platinum where the metal's reflection can amplify the stone's sparkle.

### Color Grade — M warmth and what it means in real life
Color grading runs from D (colorless) to Z (distinctly yellow). An M color indicates warmth, but not extreme. In most lighting you will notice a gentle warmth around the edges that can enhance the stone's fire and life. In a solitaire pendant the warmth is less likely to appear muddy, and the brighter cut of a round brilliant helps hide subtle color bias in the face-up view. If you crave a cooler profile, you might consider slightly higher color (G or H), but at the cost of price and possibly size perception. In practice, M color often reads as a sophisticated warmth that pairs well with platinum and the right chain.

### Clarity — VS2 and the practical eye
VS2 clarity means there are inclusions visible under 10x magnification that are typically not visible to the naked eye. In a pendant, the angle of light and the distance from the stone can reveal or hide inclusions differently than in a ring. The VS2 grade strikes a balance: you avoid the price tag of flawless stones while not sacrificing the general appearance. The rest of the diamond's anatomy — the crown height, pavilion depth, and girdle width — influences how inclusions interact with light. The round brilliant cut is designed to maximize scintillation, so even with VS2 you will see a pierce of sparkle rather than a dull surface.

### Cut and Shape — Round Brilliant expectations
Round brilliant is the most common and arguably the most well-optimized shape for sparkle. The cut grade matters as much as the carat and color. A well-cut round diamond will deliver a lively rainbow of light even when the pendant is in subtle indoor lighting. The 57 facets of the classic round brilliant, arranged with precision, behave like a tiny star against a black background (the chain and your neck). If the cut is too shallow or too deep, you lose brilliance; if it is well-proportioned, you win the cosmic sparkle lottery.

### The Setting — Platinum keeps the drama grounded
Platinum is a popular choice for solitaires because it is dense, durable, and naturally white, meaning it won’t cast color onto the diamond. It is also known for its strength and tension, which helps hold the stone securely. In a pendant format, platinum can read as a premium finish without being flashy. The combination of 1.41 ct stone with a platinum setting gives you a timeless aesthetic that suits both modern and classic wardrobes. The chain length is typically standard (18 inches) or adjustable; double-check with the seller if you want a different look.
