## Aesthetics: the look of lawful order riding in on chrome

The Palanite Justicars strike a pose that reads as a disciplined, municipal force with a dash of sci-fi pageantry. Their sculpts are clean, crisp, and balanced; they’re not attempting to be “grimdark badasses” so much as “police officers who still own their own swagger.” The armor lines are sharp, the capes and tabards are slightly ceremonial, and the insignia pop in that warm, brass-and-blue palette that screams “we got this under control, citizen.” If you like your warbands to tell a story at a glance, the Justicars do not disappoint.

From a painting perspective, they’re a dream for players who enjoy clean color separations and a bit of ornamentation. The torsos feature crisp panels, while the armor edges invite edge-highlighting and tiny details that catch the light without conflicting with the overall silhouette. It’s a fun kit to paint for mainstream hobbyists who want something a notch above “tabletop standard” but not so fiddly that you’ll need a full week to finish a squad. The balance between fabric (tabards) and hard armor also makes them forgiving for beginners who want to practice NMM or metallic highlights without getting overwhelmed by weathering or chipping. And yes, there’s plenty of room for a little whimsy—in a good way—when you want to add tiny narrative flourishes like a ceremonial badge or a personalized heraldry emblem on a shield or cloak.

### Tactical notes: how they fit into a list

- Role balance: Think of the Justicars as a disciplined backbone that can anchor one or two key lanes on the board. They work well with a gang that can exploit their ability to hold ground and punish advancing units. They’re not the kind of unit you rely on for “let’s run across the board” shenanigans; their strength lies in space control and concentrated fire.
- Engagement range: They excel at mid-range skirmishing. They demand the opponent to commit to a thoughtful movement plan rather than a rush straight into your lines. This is ideal for players who enjoy chess-like positioning in a skirmish game.
- Objective play: In many Necromunda games, securing and holding objectives is the name of the game. The Palanites’ profile tends to align with that approach, giving you a defensive edge and a credible threat to enemy objective runners. The trick is to pair them with faster, more mobile elements that can pressure other parts of the map while your Justicars watch the prize.
- Synergy and tactics: The idea is to use the Justicars as the immovable object while your secondary units become the moving parts. The more you lean into a “line, then pivot” approach, the more value you’ll extract from their leadership and mid-range weapons. ## Painting and converting: getting the squad from sprue to story

If you’re new to Necromunda, painting Palanite Justicars is a gratifying experience. The crisp lines and ceremonial feel invite a few different approaches, from clean-tabletop to high-contrast narrative schemes. Here are some practical steps:

- Basecoat: Start with a mid-tone blue or grey for armor panels, then a darker shade for recessed areas. This helps the metallics and sigils pop later.
- Armor and trim: Use a metallic or metallic-leaning paint for the trim; small patches of brass or gold can convey insignia and rank without turning the model into a bling-fest.
- Tabards and robes: White or light grey tabards read well with blue shading and brass highlights. Alternatively, for a more ceremonial look, you can go with a stark white robe and a deep blue sash.
- Weathering: A light dusting of weathering powder on the lower legs and base edge sells the “underhive custodian” vibe without looking like you spilled metallic dust all over the model.
- Freehand insignia: If you’re confident with a micro brush, a little freehand insignia on the shoulder pad or cloak edge helps personalize your squad and makes them feel official in a world where nothing is truly official except the paperwork.

A quick tip: you don’t need to overcomplicate the process. The Palanites reward clean lines and well-placed highlights. A crisp edge highlight on the armor and a couple of sharp white highlights on the tabards can deliver a strong look without turning painting into a second career.

## Lore, world-building, and the thematic core

The Palanite Justicars owe their existence to the broader architecture of Necromunda’s undercity: a place where power, law, and survival collide with spectacular force. They are the enforcement arm of the Palanite Underhive authority, a facet of the larger imperial system grafted onto a city that does not like to follow the manual. As a narrative element, the Justicars offer a compelling angle for campaigns and tabletop storytelling. They’re not just “good guys with guns”; they represent the imperfect, often ridiculous, sometimes tragic reality of attempting to maintain order in a city that is fundamentally made of rubble, rust, and rumors.

If you want to weave them into a story, here are a couple of quick hooks:
- A patrol must escort a controversial document through contested territory while resisting ambushes from rival gangs bent on derailing procurement. The Justicars’ presence creates natural tension and moral choices about enforcement vs. pragmatism.
- A high-profile official’s speech is interrupted by a riot in the undercity. The squad must push back the crowd while the players decide whether to risk escalation for the sake of a few lives.
- A data node in a derelict precinct is under threat, and the Justicars are tasked with protecting it while a courier demands safe passage for a confession that could topple a crime syndicate. Narrative possibilities abound, and the models work as a visible symbol of that struggle.

## A few practical tips and tricks for tabletop success

- Deployment: Start with your Justicars in cover where they can watch chokepoints while your faster units maneuver to pressure the opponent’s flanks. This creates a layered defense that can be tough to breach.
- Coordination: Use their leadership buffs to support nearby units as you advance. A timely leadership roll can turn a round of shooting into a decisive knockout of a fragile enemy model.
- Terrain: Favor terrain that offers line-of-sight blocks. In Necromunda, a line forming around a ruined wall or a collapsed stairwell can make your Justicars’ shooting more effective and your enemy’s approach more dramatic.
- Painting as narrative: If you’re not sure how to differentiate squads, paint a “rank” stripe on one or two troopers: a subtle way to nominate a squad leader or a non-commissioned officer on the tabletop without overcomplicating the model’s design.
