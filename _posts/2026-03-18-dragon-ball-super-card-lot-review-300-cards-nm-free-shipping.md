### Card quality and NM condition
Near mint is a sliding scale in the TCG world. NM means the card looks pristine, with sharp corners, clean surfaces, and no major scratches visible under casual lighting. In practice, NM in a used lot often means you will still find the occasional minor whitening on the corners, a subtle edge ding, or a soft crease that only shows up under direct, bright light. The important thing is that the majority of cards feel crisp, not glossy-yelled, and that there are no major surface defects that would scream bargain at a judge at a tournament. It is not unusual to see some miswraps in the stack or minor whitening on the back of foil cards. If you are planning to sleeve everything (which you should), these minor defects become essentially invisible during play and modern sleeves can reduce further cosmetic wear.

### Condition checks
As soon as your lot arrives, spread the cards out on a clean light source. Inspect the borders, corners, and centering. Look for:
- Edge wear, whitening, or nicks
- Creases and soft corners
- Surface scratches that catch the light
- Foil rings or print lines on holofoil cards

Bear in mind that a few duff cards can be swapped or traded and that many sellers allow returns if the majority of the cards are not NM. If you are serious about building a deck from the lot, sort by rarity, then examine the condition in batches. For foils, check for foil borders or staining that sometimes drips into the card surface. It is not unusual to encounter a small bubble in a holofoil; that is not a defect, but it is a quirk of the printing process that some collectors love.
