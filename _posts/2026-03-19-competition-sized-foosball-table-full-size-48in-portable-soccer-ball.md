## How to get the most out of it: tips and tricks
- Position planning: Place the table on a flat, stable surface to minimize wobble. If your floor isn’t perfectly level, use adjustable feet or small shims underneath the legs to stabilize.
- Ball care: Keep a small bowl of spare balls nearby and rinse off the grip if they get dusty. Clean balls roll truer and help your shots stay consistent when you’re practicing your trick shots in a hoodie.
- Maintenance routine: Check the rod bearings every few weeks and keep the rods lightly oiled to maintain smooth motion. Don’t over-lubricate—nobody wants a slippery ball that slides off your rod like a runaway meteor.
- Practice plan: Create a mini-training regimen—five minutes of quick passes to build rhythm, then five minutes of power shots to groove your aim. Your cat will still judge you, but you’ll feel like a champion.
