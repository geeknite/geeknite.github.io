### The Tax Stamp in a Nutshell
Tax stamps on playing cards are a curious artifact from the late 19th and early 20th centuries. A government would levy a small tax on production or sale, and the stamp served as proof of duty paid. On a sealed deck, the stamp signals not only authenticity but a moment when commerce felt a little more theatrical—like a miniature act of public service plus packaging design. ## How to Authenticate a Vintage TAX Stamp Deck

### A Simple Checklist
If you are considering a purchase or a trade, run through this quick checklist to separate authentic specimens from clever reproductions:
- Confirm the TAX Stamp is present and legible on the seal or packaging. The stamp should be an integrated part of the box and not a loose sticker.
- Inspect the tuck box for age-related wear that is consistent with the era, such as a slight yellowing, edge wear, or a marginal gloss loss that matches other vintage stock from the period.
- Examine the back design for uniform print quality across the deck. Inconsistent lines or misregistered patterns can indicate counterfeit production.
- Check the card stock for a vintage feel, typically a bit stiffer with a tactile finish that has aged gracefully.
- Look for provenance or markings that connect the deck to Cardy Lundmark or similar collectors, but beware of faked signatures or modern reinterpretations.
- Ensure the deck remains sealed if the seller claims mint condition; a freshly opened box loses a portion of its collectible value instantly.

## Care and Preservation: Keeping the Legacy Alive

The key to long term enjoyment is simple discipline:
- Store in a cool, dry place with stable humidity to prevent stock cracking or warping.
- Keep the deck away from direct sunlight to preserve the color fidelity of the back design.
- Use a dedicated storage box or a display case with padding to prevent accidental rubbing or edge damage.
- Avoid aggressive handling; the more you keep your fingers off the surface, the longer the gloss and pips stay fresh in the long run.

If you plan to actively handle the deck for display or practice, consider a high quality card stand or a magnetic display case that frames the chemistry of the TAX Stamp and the Rider Back pattern without forcing you to open the box again and again.
