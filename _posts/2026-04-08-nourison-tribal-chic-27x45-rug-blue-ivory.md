## Cleaning and maintenance
Cotton rugs are generally easier to wash than their woolen cousins. This rug should respond well to regular vacuuming with a suction-only setting to avoid fiber pull. If you spill something, dab rather than rub; cotton fibers like to hold onto a stain if you scrub it with vigor. For spot cleaning a mild detergent mixed with water is usually sufficient; test on a small inconspicuous area first to avoid any potential color shift. The blue and ivory combination tends to hide light traffic marks, which means you can pretend your space is professionally curated rather than lived in by a group of busy humans and possibly an enterprising dog. If you are a kitchen runner or hallway traveler, a quick vacuum every couple of days can keep the fibers looking crisp and the colors validating your decorating choices.

## Care tips and longevity
To maximize longevity, rotate the rug occasionally so the color wear is even across the surface. Avoid placing the rug in direct sunlight for extended periods as that can cause fading over time. If you fold or roll the rug for storage, do so loosely and avoid sharp creases that can weaken the weave. Consider using a non slip pad under the rug on smooth floors; while the rug is heavy enough to stay put on some surfaces, a pad helps preserve floor finish and keeps the edges from curling up. The cotton fiber is durable but soft, the combination you want when you want a comfortable surface that doubles as a piece of decor rather than a sole focus of the room.
