## Assembly and Setup
What you want in a furniture build is not a weatherman’s forecast but a reasonable expectation. GOF typically ships components in segmented panels, with small hardware bags organized for the average homeowner or the mildly ambitious DIY-er. Expect a step-by-step instruction flow that gradually snaps panels into frames, attaches desks, and tucks away cables. Durability is built into the sequence so you don’t feel like you are re-enacting a medieval siege with screws and Allen keys.

### Tools and Time
- A basic screwdriver and an adjustable wrench are all you might need. A power drill can speed things up, but hand assembly is perfectly workable.
- Most setups take between 60 and 120 minutes depending on your space constraints, whether you’re fiddling with cable trays mid-construction, and your experience with flat-pack furniture.

### Tips for a Smooth Build
- Clear the space: It’s amazing how much room a box or two takes up. Give yourself an open area for moving parts around.
- Do a dry fit: Lay out panels without fasteners first to visualize the flow; it saves you from having to disassemble and reassemble mid-build.
- Label and route: As you connect panels, label cables and route them through the built-in trays before you close up the panels. You’ll thank yourself later during cable management day.

### Post-assembly Check
- Make sure the desk surface sits level. If you notice a wobble, scan for slightly uneven floor surfaces or re-tighten the corner screws. A small elbow grease session can go a long way toward a rock-solid feel.
- Confirm the height setting you intend to use is functional across the desk surface when seated. A good ergonomic setup means your eyes line up with the top of the monitor and your forearms rest at roughly a 90-degree angle.
- Inspect cable management channels and grommets to ensure they are aligned with your workstation peripherals. A tidy cable system improves airflow and reduces accidental unplugging during late-night coding sessions.
