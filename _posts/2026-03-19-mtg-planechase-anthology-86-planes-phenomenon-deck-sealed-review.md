## Storage, care, and long-term preservation
Preserving a sealed anthology set is about preserving the moment you first found it. For long-term storage, keep the box in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. UV light can gradually yellow or fade card art, which is a petty but non-trivial concern for collectors who want the visuals to stay as crisp as possible. Consider using a display box or a climate-controlled cabinet if your collection grows into a small museum and you plan to display it rather than keep it hermetically sealed. For those who plan to actually play with the set, you might want to split the planes from the phenomenon deck into protective sleeves before you shuffle them into a play area, just to minimize edge wear during enthusiastic dice shakes.

In any case, a sealed copy offers a different kind of value than a played copy. The sealed status makes for a conversation starter and a investments can look good when the market vibes favor plane-chasing antiques. The ultimate care recommendation is to avoid the temptation to crack it open in the middle of a video call. Let the moment breathe. If you do decide to crack it open, treat it like a module for a tabletop adventure, not a grocery bag that just got used for a quick snack stash.
