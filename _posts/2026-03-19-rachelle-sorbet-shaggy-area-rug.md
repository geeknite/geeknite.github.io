Care, Cleaning, and Maintenance
--------------------------------
Let’s be honest: rugs pick up dirt like magnets pick up gossip. The Sorbet rug is no exception. It’s easy to care for if you approach it with a plan rather than a panic. Here are practical tips that do not require a PhD in rugology:

- Vacuum regularly with the suction setting tuned to a medium-high range. Use a vacuum with a beater bar but avoid aggressive agitation that can fray the length of the strands.
- Flip and rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. Yes, you may look a little suspicious rotating your living room furniture like a chessboard, but your carpet will thank you when it doesn’t show a singular traffic path.
- For spills, blot—not rub—the area with a clean cloth. Cold water is often enough for most minor stains; for stubborn marks, use a mild detergent diluted in water and test on a small inconspicuous corner first. Do not saturate; the goal is to lift the stain, not flood your floor with your hopes and dreams.
- For pet accidents, act quickly. Enzymatic cleaners work well because they target organic residues without bleaching the fibers. After treating, rinse with a damp cloth and allow to air-dry fully to avoid mold.
- Periodically shake or beat the rug outside to remove loose dust and hair. If you have a balcony, a sunny day is a great time to air out the shag and reset the fibers before the next binge-watching session.
