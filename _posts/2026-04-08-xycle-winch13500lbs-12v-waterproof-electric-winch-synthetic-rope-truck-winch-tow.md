### Safety first: operation, PPE, and best practices

Winching is one of those tasks where caution is the best feature of your toolkit. A few rules of thumb:

- Always wear gloves when handling synthetic rope; it’s not a good idea to touch bare rope when it’s under tension.
- Keep bystanders clear of the line. A running rope can cause severe injuries if it recoils or snaps. A dampener or a safety blanket can help reduce the risk of a rope whiplash.
- Use a proper anchor and a tree saver strap when possible. Never wrap the rope around itself or around sharp edges; use protective sleeves and fairleads.
- Check the rest of the vehicle’s electrical system before performing long recovery runs. A healthy battery and a sound wiring setup make a big difference.
- Maintain a mindful approach to load. Don’t exceed the rated capacity, and account for dynamic loads when you’re on uneven terrain. When you’re in doubt, re-evaluate the setup and consider lighter loads with multiple passes.

In our experience, the safe usage practices translate into a smoother, more predictable recovery. The unit’s remote control makes it easier to keep a safe distance while controlling the operation—an important feature when mud, tension, and a stubborn wheel combine into a dramatic, but mostly humorous, moment of “Why is this not moving?”

### Real-world take: scenarios and tips

We’ll close with a handful of practical tips drawn from weekend recoveries:
- Before you commit to a full pull, do a brief test: apply light tension to check the line’s alignment, rope condition, and anchor security.
