## Customization options: Custom size or 8x10

This Regatta rug lineup emphasizes two main tracks: a standard 8x10 and a robust custom size program. Here is how to think about each option and why you might choose one over the other.

### The 8x10 sweet spot
The 8x10 option is a versatile standard size that works well under a typical sofa setup with generous surrounding rug space. It anchors seating areas, defines zones in open-plan layouts, and tends to be the most readily available option in retailers. If your room measurements fall near common living room configurations, the 8x10 is the pragmatic choice that delivers a solid balance between coverage and proportion.

### Custom sizes: because one size does not fit all
Custom sizing is where the dream gets real for interior design geeks. The custom route accommodates odd-shaped rooms, multiple seating clusters, or generous walkways without forcing you into a compromise that makes the rug look like it tried too hard to fit in. Here are a few considerations when planning a custom size:

- Room measurement: Measure length and width at multiple points, then subtract any wall treatments or baseboards that would otherwise crowd the edges. Consider a slight padding around seating arrangements to preserve the rug’s presence under furniture.
- Proportion: The rug should occupy about 60 to 80 percent of the seating area, depending on your style. For an open plan, a larger rug can help zone a space and prevent the furniture from feeling crowded by negative space.
- Lead time and budget: Custom sizes will require extra production time and a higher price tag. If you are in a rush, the 8x10 might be your best bet; if not, a custom cut can yield a look tailored to your floor plan.
- Color consistency: If you are ordering in a non-standard size, you may want to double-check color consistency across the width. Wool can vary slightly batch to batch, and longer runs in a custom size sometimes show subtle tonal shifts.

### How to plan your order
- Take precise room measurements and sketch a quick floor plan, labeling where the rug will be placed. Include door swings and any furniture that may encroach on the rug space.
- Decide on a colorway that complements your dominant palette. If you are torn between two shades, a mid-range tone tends to be the safest bet for future decor changes.
- Consider a rug pad and furniture coasters. A good pad not only reduces wear but also dampens noise from chair legs and footfalls, which matters in apartments and late-night gaming sessions.
- Confirm lead times and return policies before placing a custom order. The best rug journeys happen with clear expectations on delivery windows and finishes.

## Care and maintenance basics

Caring for a wool rug is not a scavenger hunt, but it does require a little method. Here are practical guidelines to keep your Regatta Waterfall looking fresh for years:

- Vacuum regularly: Use a low-suction vacuum with the beater bar turned off or set to a gentle setting. Vacuum in different directions to lift fibers and avoid rutting in one path.
- Use a rug pad: A good pad reduces movement, protects the floor, and adds an extra layer of comfort underfoot. It also helps prevent slippage on hard surfaces.
- Spill response: Attend to spills promptly. Blot with a clean white cloth, avoid rubbing, and consider a wool-safe cleaner for stubborn stains. Test any cleaner on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Rotation: Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and sun exposure. This simple practice helps preserve the visual gradient across the surface.
- Sunlight and fading: Wool dyes are generally colorfast, but prolonged direct sun can dull colors over time. Use window treatments or rotate placement if your room gets intense sun during peak hours.
