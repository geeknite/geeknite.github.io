## Battery Life and Charging: Real-World Performance

### How Long Does It Really Last?
Battery life on AR glasses is a perpetual game of expectations. In our test with mixed usage (notifications, a handful of AR overlays, occasional navigation prompts, and some casual streaming audio), a refurbished Gen 1 Skyler posted roughly 2–3 hours of continuous overlay use. If you lean into light usage with the overlays only as needed, you can eek out a comfortable day’s worth of usage. The included charging case helps you top up on the go, much like a tiny portable power bank that doubles as a luxury accessory. Expect around 15–20 minutes of rapid top-up to restore a meaningful amount of overlay time.

### Charging and Longevity: Tips to Get the Most
Charge cycles differ between refurbished and new devices. The refurbished Skyler benefits from the recalibration of the battery management system. If you’re seeing shorter sessions, don’t panic—perform a factory reset if recommended by the refurbisher, update firmware, and allow a full discharge and recharge cycle to settle. Pro tip: avoid leaving the case plugged in at 100% all day long if you’re trying to maximize battery health; lithium-polymer batteries love a gentle life.
