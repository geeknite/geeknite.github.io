# City of Mist: Player's Guide

Welcome, investigators, dream-weavers, and people-wacking, to a guide that attempts to explain a game where the city itself forgets and remembers you at the same time. City of Mist is a roleplaying game that smooshes noir crime drama with mythic, larger-than-life heroes. It’s a tabletop adventure where you don a mask, follow clues, and discover that the real mystery might be who you thought you were. If you’ve ever wanted your character to be both your everyday self and a legendary myth in waiting, this guide is for you. And if you’ve never heard of any of those words before, fear not — we’ll translate the mystic breadcrumbs into desk-friendly tips you can actually use during a session.

![City of Mist cover](/assets/images/city-of-mist-cover.jpg)

As a player, you’ll be steering a “public face” that the world sees and a hidden “mask” that gives you access to powers, legends, and a narrative hook you probably didn’t realize you needed. The city you play in is a character, a living noir-sapient canvas where myths aren’t just about ancient gods; they’re about present-day heroes, frauds, and people who keep their cool under pressure while their alter ego tries to take the wheel. In short: City of Mist asks you to live in two realities at once without ever misplacing your keys.

This guide is going to cover: character creation through a player’s lens, how to run scenes that feel like a movie, tips for roleplay, and practical advice for surviving sessions that swing from routine stakeouts to mythic showdowns. We’ll pepper in some geeky humor, cross-references to related posts, and a few nerdy asides that only the true City of Mist stalkers will appreciate. If you’re itching to level up your table, you’ve landed in a good alleyway. Let’s dive in.

## What City of Mist is trying to do for players

City of Mist is not a traditional “dungeon crawl” or a numbers-first RPG. It’s a storytelling playground that uses a simple framework to push you toward dramatic choices and character-driven revelations. The game leans into a few stylish pillars:

- Stylistic storytelling: Think pulpy noir, mythic resonance, and occasional shorthand for epic stakes. The aim is to feel cinematic rather than strictly realistic.
- Dual identities: Each character has a public face (how the city sees you) and a mask (your mythic, supernatural, or super-powered aspect). The tension between those identities drives scenes.
- Clues and mysteries: The game uses a mystery structure where clues lead toward a bigger truth. Sometimes you’ll chase a lead; other times you’ll chase a feeling — or a missing memory from a life you’ve half-forgotten.
- The city as a character: The setting breathes; it bores you with its traffic jams and then surprises you with a rooftop chase scene. You’ll describe parts of the city with the same care you give to your character’s motivations.

If you’re a fan of “detective with a twist” or a superhero who wears a wrinkled suit to blend in, City of Mist nudges you toward playing that vibe with flair. It’s not about how many dice you roll; it’s about how boldly you lean into your character’s contradictions—how you reconcile the ordinary you with the mythical you that wants to break out and punch the problem in the face, preferably with a witty one-liner.

## Character creation: Motifs, Masks, and the Public Face

Character creation in City of Mist is a three-way dance: pick your Motif, sculpt your Mask, and define your Public Face. The three parts: Motif, Mask, and Public Face, each contribute a narrative thread, a mechanical hook, and a source of conflict. Here’s a practical way to approach it without getting lost in mythology rune-speak.

### 1) Choose a Motif (your mythic spine)
Motifs are the sources of your powers and the stories that give your character texture. They’re not just “I have fire magic” or “I’m a detective with a dark past.” They’re the motifs that define your moral compass, your aesthetic, and your one-line pitch to the world.

- Think about what mythic idea your character embodies. Are you a fallen hero who embodies the myth of redemption? A trickster who bends reality with cleverness and misdirection? A guardian who protects a memory you refuse to let die?
- Your Motif comes with a few recognizable hooks: a set of power ideas, a flaw that creates tension, and a couple of narrative levers you can pull when scenes demand a pivot. Don’t worry about having every capability fleshed out; you’ll refine them as you play, like sculpting a statue by repeatedly chipping away mistakes.
- Examples: The Detective with a Disguised Heart, The Oracle in a Skyscraper, The Beast with a Passport, The Street-Singer who Moves Mountains with Music. The point is: your Motif is the engine that fuels the story you want to tell, not just a collection of cool powers.

### 2) Craft your Mask (the mythic side of you)
Your Mask is the mythic aspect that grants you powers and shapes your actions in the city. It’s what you reveal under pressure, and it’s usually not something you can show in a corporate boardroom. The Mask embodies a myth, a dramatic identity you lean on when the going gets tough (or stylish).

- Your Mask should pair with your Motif. If your Motif is “The Guardian,” your Mask might be something like “The Unseen Emissary” who appears in shadows to protect folks who can’t protect themselves. If your Motif is “The Trickster,” your Mask might be “The Masked Benefactor” who uses misdirection to bend fate.
- You’ll define a handful of Mask traits: how you look, how you speak, and what you do when the moment calls. Your Mask should create unique, memorable moments in play—moments when you get to show off your mythic flavor.
- A practical tip: give your Mask a signature move or signature effect. A persona quirk, a catchphrase, a visual cue that makes you instantly recognizable when you step into a scene. The more specific, the more flavorful your scenes will be.

### 3) Define your Public Face (how the city sees you)
Your Public Face is your everyday identity—the job, the family, the irrelevant-but-vital details that make you human. It’s what keeps you grounded when the mythic stuff starts to whirl around your ankles.

- The Public Face should contrast with your Mask. If your Mask is bombastic and mythical, your Public Face can be ordinary, honest, perhaps a little funny in a mortifying way that makes your friends roll their eyes.
- Think about what you do in the daylight: a barista, a bus driver, a lab tech, or a private detective who occasionally orders coffee “black as space.” Your job is your anchor; it’s where clues into the city’s secrets often begin.
- Your Public Face becomes a reliable anchor for your fellow players’ investigations. They’ll see you in the city’s orbit, while your Mask does the dramatic heavy lifting when the plot demands it.

### 4) A quick example (to illustrate the flow)
- Motif: The Guardian (you’re a protector who can sense when people are in danger).
- Mask: The Whispering Sentinel (you can move through crowds unseen, and you utter a few lines that calm others in crisis).
- Public Face: A neighborhood clinic volunteer who spends evenings at the shelter, manning a desk and listening to stories that aren’t theirs to tell.

This trio gives you a living, breathing character who can respond to both the city’s crime files and its human fragility. It’s not about “how do I beat the boss?” as much as “how do I carry this place with my choices?”

## Gameplay flow: Scenes, Clues, and the Mythic Pulse

City of Mist games run in scenes, and scenes aren’t just cut-and-dried combat. They’re the heartbeats of a city that talks in whispers and car alarms. Here’s a practical map for running or playing through a typical session, without the wizards-and-dragons vibe you didn’t ask for.

- Scenes are your playgrounds. You’ll start with a Social Scene (pressure, talk, persuasion) and end with a Moment of Truth (the reveal, the twist, the mythic spark). Each scene should escalate the tension and bias toward a mythic reveal rather than a pure fight.
- Clues are the breadcrumbs that lead you to the truth about the city’s corrupted myths. They’re not just evidence; they’re narrative handholds that pull you toward a bigger question: what is the city trying to tell you through the crime you’re investigating?
- The Mythic Pulse: the city has a rhythm, a tempo that shifts from noir silence to myth-bloom when you connect the right clues. Learning to recognize the rhythm—when to push for a reveal, when to slow down and listen, and when to lean into your Mask’s power—makes the game sing rather than drag.
- Resolution and consequence: City of Mist emphasizes outcomes that feel meaningful. The point isn’t to “win” a dice roll; it’s to decide what you give up, what you gain, and how the city itself shifts because you chose a path in this scene.

If you like to play detective stories with moral ambiguity and a pinch of epic myth, you’ll feel right at home here. It’s less about heroics performed in a gym and more about choices made on a rooftop at dawn, with the city waking up behind you.

## GM tips: running City of Mist without breaking the vibe

If you’re the Game Master, your challenge is to keep the city’s mood consistent: moody but hopeful, dangerous yet gleaming with possibility. Here are pragmatic tips that help you keep scenes tight and memorable.

- Lean into the rhythm: set a vibe early. A streetlight flickering, a pigeon wandering into the shot, a distant siren. These little touches build atmosphere without slowing you down.
- Let players author the city’s myths: give them room to describe a memory, a rumor, or a hidden place that feels mythic in hindsight. The world grows through their voices as much as yours.
- Create flexible Clues: instead of a single “smoking gun,” consider multi-use clues that can lead to several outcomes. This keeps players guessing and reinforces the game’s mystery vibe.
- Reward risk and teamwork: emphasize scenes where players combine their Mask powers with Public Face resources. The most unforgettable moments often arise when two different approaches collide across a scene—someone uses street-level knowledge, someone else triggers a mythic power, and the truth surfaces in a surprising way.
- Tone management: City of Mist rewards narrative flavor. You don’t need to be a stand-up comedian or a Shakespearean actor to run it well, but you do want to encourage players to lean into the city’s mythic flavor rather than treating it as just a setting. If the mood veers toward “generic superhero,” nudge it back toward noir mystery with a sharp line, a personal stake, or a clue that redefines what the city is capable of.
- Prepare a handful of city-beat scenes: intersections where a mythic problem intersects with a mundane one. A grocery store blackout becomes a doorway to a hidden myth, a missing person becomes a legend-in-progress, and a late-night radio broadcast hints at a larger conspiracy. The city loves this kind of mashup.

## Player tips: leaning into the roleplay and storytelling

You're here to tell a story where you’re both the person next door and the hero who can’t reveal everything at once. Here are some player-focused tips to help you participate with energy and generosity.

- Lean into the dual identity: when you’re in your Public Face, ask yourself how you’d handle the situation with normal rules and how your Mask would blow it up in a good way. The contrast is where the drama lives.
- Give your fellow players room to shine: City of Mist is a collaborative show. If someone’s Mask is about a dramatic moment, support them with scene framing or a well-timed clue that helps them make the reveal they wanted.
- Embrace the unknown: if you’re unsure about what to say or do, describe a sensory detail first. A smell, a visual cue, a sound in the distance — these can become the springboard for your next action and a moment of truth for your character.
- Practice your signature line: a short catchphrase, a vow, or a quip that signals your Mask’s ethos. It’s not just flavor; it’s a cue to others about what your character is likely to do in critical situations.
- Be mindful of the mythic tone: you don’t have to be an over-the-top hero in every scene. You can be subtle and still be mythic. The weight of the city comes from what your choices ripple into, not necessarily from giant explosions every time.

If you’re new to roleplaying, City of Mist offers a gentle introduction to collaborative storytelling with strong vibes and a straightforward structure. If you’re a veteran player who loves a twist on familiar genres, you’ll find your sweet spot here in the form of myth meeting street-level life.

## Tools, resources, and practical table setup

To keep your sessions smooth, here are practical adjustments you can make without changing the core feel of the game:

- A simple character sheet that captures three axes: Motif, Mask, Public Face. Add a line for a signature move and a personal stake. Keep the sheet minimal so it’s easy to read at the table.
- A city map with a few recurring districts. The map should be enough to anchor scenes but not so detailed that players feel boxed in. The city should feel alive, not a blueprint.
- Clue cards: small prompts players can draw when a scene hits a lull. Ideas include “a memory fragment,” “a symbol in graffiti,” or “a call from an anonymous ally.” Clue cards keep momentum going without stalling for long.
- A simple clock or cue for “mythic reveal.” Having a mechanical moment when a truth is exposed helps players feel the weight of their decisions. It could be a timer, a dramatic line, or a visual cue in the narration.
- Audio-visual mood: a playlist with moody synth tunes, rain sounds, or distant traffic can be a powerful ally. The city’s mood is near-telepathic if you lean into it with the right sounds.

## Aesthetic and look: what you might want to bring to the table

City of Mist has a particular look and rhythm that benefits from thoughtful presentation. The artwork tends to blend noir grit with mythic flourishes, and the rules-light engine wants you to lean into dramatic storytelling rather than heavyweight crunch. If you’re planning to run or play, consider:

- A simple, consistent visual motif for each character: e.g., color cues, a piece of clothing that signals their Mask, an object that anchors their Public Face.
- A tall glass or mug of coffee by your notes for that “detective desk” vibe during long sessions. It’s not essential, but it helps set mood like a pro.
- A few props or images to anchor scenes: a flyer with a cryptic symbol, a photo of a missing person, a city map pocketed with post-it notes. The tangible details reinforce the storytelling.

If you enjoy tabletop games that reward atmosphere and character-driven scenes, City of Mist hits a sweet spot that many other games miss. You get cinematic energy with a tactile, human-scale focus on personal stakes.

## The moment of truth: final recommendation

Bottom line: City of Mist offers a refreshing take on modern myth storytelling that foregrounds character duality, mood, and collaborative mystery-building. It’s perfect for groups who want to play something stylish, personal, and a little magical without getting lost in heavy rulebooks. If you crave a game that treats you as a co-author of a living city rather than a cog in a stat-block machine, City of Mist will reward your willingness to lean into its vibe and lean away from “grind.”

The best sessions tend to emerge when players embrace the dual identity dynamic. When you stumble upon a clue that flatly contradicts what your Public Face believed, you’ve got gold: a moment where the Mask imposes a mythic twist and your character is forced to choose between safety and the truth that could burn their world to the ground. That is the pulse City of Mist is built to chase, and it’s glorious when it lands.

If you’re curious to try it, look for a local group or online play and bring a light daydream of a hero with a heavy heart and a sharper wit. The city will respond in kind, and you’ll likely leave the table with more questions than answers—and that’s exactly where great stories begin.
