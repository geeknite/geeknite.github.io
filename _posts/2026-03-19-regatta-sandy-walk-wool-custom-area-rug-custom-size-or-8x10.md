The Sandy Walk line is built with a focus on durability and ease of maintenance that suits an active home. ## Care, maintenance, and wool wisdom

Wool is naturally self-cleaning to a degree thanks to the lanolin in the fibers, but it isn’t a self-cleaning miracle. Regular maintenance goes a long way toward preserving color, texture, and structural integrity.

- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting or a brushless attachment to avoid fiber damage. Avoid beater bars on deep-pile surfaces.
- Attend to spills quickly. Blot (don’t rub) with a clean, dry cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible. For stubborn stains, test a gentle wool-safe cleaner in an inconspicuous area first.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12–24 months if you’re in a high-traffic area or with households that host lots of foot traffic and pet life.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to equalize wear and prevent concentrated traffic patterns from forming along doorways or heavy furniture bases.

## Underlay, installation, and the physics of stability

A rug is more than a piece of fabric; it’s a system. An underlay or rug pad can dramatically affect longevity, comfort, and safety, especially on hardwood or tile floors where slippage is more than a nuisance—it’s a liability. A quality underlay improves cushioning, guards against wear in high-traffic zones, and reduces noise—a win-win for apartments and homes with busy kitchens.

Installation tips:
- Align the rug so that its longer side runs parallel to the main seating wall to create a balanced focal point.
- Don’t stretch the rug taut; gentle give allows for natural wriggle room around furniture legs.
- Use furniture coasters or felt pads under heavy chairs to prevent snagging and flattening at chair points.
