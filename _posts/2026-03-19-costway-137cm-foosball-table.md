## Maintenance and Longevity

Maintenance for a foosball table is not a riveting topic, but it matters if you want your investment to outlive the next haircut. The laminate surface means you should avoid caustic cleaners that could dull the finish, and we recommend a mild cleaner with a soft cloth to prevent micro-scratches. Wipe down after play to prevent sweat and fingerprints from leaving a long-term impression. The rods should be checked occasionally for any looseness; tighten the screws that connect the handles to the rod ends if you hear rattling during play. Lubrication is optional for most home use; if you have a particularly dry climate or you notice stiff rod movement, a light application of silicone spray on the rod joints can help keep things silky. Do not spray directly onto the bearings; apply to a cloth and wipe, to avoid any risk of swelling or binding.

Maintenance also means keeping the table level. The leveling feet on the legs do a good job, but after a few months, you might notice a slight decline in performance if the floor shifts a bit. A quick re-leveling session will restore precision and help your shots land where you intend them to land, which is exactly what you want when you re fighting for bragging rights after a long day of coding and coffee.
