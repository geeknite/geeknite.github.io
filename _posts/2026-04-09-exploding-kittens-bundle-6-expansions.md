H3: Mechanics, pacing, and what makes this bundle sing

Exploding Kittens thrives on a simple core mechanic: you want to avoid drawing the exploding kitten card. You do this by playing action cards, using avoidance strategies, and bluffing your way to safety. The six expansions add layers: you’ll have more ways to defuse, more ways to terrorize, and more ways to force your friends to perform ridiculous tasks. The tempo changes depending on how you build your house rules. In a 4-player game, you’ll hit a brisk pace where rounds feel like micro-sagas. In a 2-player game, you’ll push the \"who blinks first\" meta to a fever pitch. The 6-expansion bundle adds new action cards, new defuse items, and new ways to leverage your opponents’ ignorance. Some players will love the speed; others will bemoan the inevitable chaos that comes with six expansions in one box.

A couple of practical notes on setup: you’ll want a lit place to read the rules, a few tokens to track who’s safe, and maybe a timer if you intend to run a tournament-style marathon. We found that separate \"play mats\" for each expansion help, but they’re not strictly necessary. The core idea is that you’ll be juggling a handful of new cards, and it’s the sort of juggling that looks chaotic but somehow works when the group dynamic clicks.
