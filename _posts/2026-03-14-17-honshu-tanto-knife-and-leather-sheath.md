Maintenance and Care
---------------------
Maintenance is the practical hero of any blade, and this one benefits from a straightforward care routine:

- Clean after use to prevent residue buildup; warm water and a mild detergent does the trick. Dry thoroughly to avoid moisture lingering in crevices.
- Lightly oil the blade and tang periodically to prevent rust; a thin film is enough—no need to soak it.
- Check the sheath retention periodically. Leather can stretch with time and use, so give the snap or retention strap a quick test every now and then.
- Store in a dry place. Leather sheaths like to breathe, but you don’t want it to dry out to the point of cracking either. A little leather conditioner every few months can keep things supple if you’re in a dry environment.

Maintenance tip: keep a small kit in your pack that includes a microfiber cloth, a light honing stone, and a bottle of oil. You don’t need a full-blown sharpening station for 90% of the tasks this blade is designed to tackle, but a quick touch-up now and then keeps the edge feeling fresh and ready for action.
