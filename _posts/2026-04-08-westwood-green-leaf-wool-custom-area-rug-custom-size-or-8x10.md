## Size options: Custom size or 8x10

### Custom sizing

Custom sizing is for spaces with odd dimensions, pull-out sofa nooks, or rooms with unusual angles. The process typically involves sharing your exact width and length, selecting the pile height and backing, and confirming color consistency under your room lighting. The time needed can vary based on production demand and shipping windows. The upside is a perfect fit that looks like it belongs—not like you tried to jam a standard rug into a space where it just won’t.

Pros:

- Perfect fit for irregular spaces.
- Better to anchor furniture, define zones, and align with wall art and window treatments.

Cons:

- Slightly longer lead times.
- Potential price difference versus stock options.

### 8x10 standard sizing

The 8x10 option provides a reliable and widely compatible footprint for many living rooms. It is large enough to ground a sofa and chairs without dominating the space, yet nimble enough to allow for flexible furniture placement. If your room’s dimensions work with a standard size, you’ll typically save on lead time and cost.

### How to decide

- If your room has unusual width-length relationships or you want the rug to define distinct zones, custom sizing is ideal.
- If you want a straightforward purchase and want to see how a leaf motif feels in your space without the wait, choose 8x10.

## Comfort, texture, and usability

### Feel underfoot

The wool pile provides a soft, welcoming surface that invites you to kick off shoes and linger a little longer. The texture offers an interplay of smooth and tactile, with enough cushion to keep you comfortable during a long reading session yet not so plush that you’ll sink into a couch-like abyss.

### Foot traffic and pet-friendly aspects

For households with pets or bustling kids, wool’s resilience is a boon. The fibers recover well from foot traffic, and the natural resistance to odors helps with daily living. Regular vacuuming keeps hair and dust at bay, and a rug pad can further reduce slippage and improve comfort.

### Usability tips

- Rotate periodically to maintain even wear and color distribution.
- Use a rug pad appropriate for wool to prevent slippage and protect floors.
- Protect from prolonged sun exposure to prevent color fading; use UV-blocking window treatments if necessary.

## Care and maintenance

### Cleaning basics

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers.
- Treat spills promptly by blotting with a clean cloth—dye transfer is less likely with wool, but quick action is still wise.
- For stains, follow the manufacturer’s care instructions; avoid harsh chemical scrubbing.

### Shedding and pile health

Initial shedding is normal; regular vacuuming helps manage excess fibers. After a few weeks, shedding should diminish and the pile should maintain a crisp pattern.

### Seasonal maintenance

In dry months, maintain humidity to preserve fiber elasticity; in humid months, a dehumidifier can prevent mold growth. Adequate air circulation helps keep the rug looking fresh year-round.
