Introduction
City of Mist is not your typical superhero RPG. It’s a neon-noir, detective-tinged love letter to the idea that gods and legends aren’t confined to the past—they’ve just taken up street-level residency in a modern metropolis. The Core Rulebook is the gateway drug to that mood: a sprawling, lovingly illustrated guide that teaches you to tell modern mythic stories without needing an army of dice or a thousand power cards. If the goal is to make players feel like they’re actual legends navigating a city that refuses to sleep, City of Mist nails it with unapologetic style and surprisingly robust tools for collaborative storytelling.

For fans of urban fantasy, detective fiction, and “mythology as personality” fiction, the Core Rulebook is a treasure chest. For newcomers, it’s an invitation to reframe what counts as magic, heroism, and danger. The rules encourage players to lean into recognizable tropes—the Kid Detective, the Femme Fatale, the Wise Elder, the Grimy Gadgeteer—while using those tropes as masks that can be peeled away on a scene-by-scene basis. The result is a game about myths that refuse to stay polite, about identities that choose you, and about a city that’s as much a character as any hood or goddess wearing a trench coat.

What the Core Rulebook Sets Up
City of Mist is built around two core concepts: Mythos and Logos. Mythos is your legend—the magical, mythic identity you’ve inherited or adopted. Logos is your mundane, name-and-face identity—the job, the reputation, the appearance you present to the world. The blend of these two identities forms a character that feels larger-than-life but anchored in real-world stresses. The Core Rulebook walks you through pairing a Mythos with a Logos, choosing relevant “Power Tags” that describe your character’s abilities, and learning how to use those tags in dramatic, cinematic scenes. The result is a game where a detective with a “Werewolf of Justice” Mythos might pull a clue from a seemingly ordinary streetlight, or a hacker with a “Techno-Sorcerer” tag warps reality by tapping into the city’s data streams.

The tone is deliberate: City of Mist wants you to have fun narrating coincidences into fate, and it provides clear mechanics that support that kind of storytelling. The core rulebook doesn’t pretend you’re playing a system that requires a centralized corp of designers to adjudicate every outcome. It’s a rules-light foundation that supports improvisation, with a dice mechanism designed to keep the focus on story rather than math.

A Look Inside the Core Rulebook
The book’s layout leans into neon-noir aesthetics: high-contrast art, synth-driven typography, and an abundance of tips for running sessions that feel like episodes of a late-night detective show. The writing balances enthusiasm with practicality. It’s full of examples—short vignettes, bits of sample dialogue, scene prompts, and quick-start threads—that make it easy to envision how a scene should feel before you roll a single die.

The core chapters cover: character creation (Mythos and Logos), the dice-pool mechanics (including Power Tags and how they inform outcomes), how to structure scenes (from investigation to confrontation to revelation), world-building tips for cities that feel lived-in, and guidelines for narrating with style rather than wrestling with a complex rulebook.

From a design perspective, City of Mist revels in its metaphor: the city is “mist” that shapes your reality, and your identity is the “token” you’re given to navigate it. That metaphor isn’t just window dressing—the mechanics reinforce it. Tags are the keys you turn to reveal hidden aspects of a scene. The GM uses Truths about the city to constrain what can happen but, more importantly, to inspire players to find clever angles on those truths. The book’s tone clearly communicates: this is about myth as personality, not about myth as endgame power fantasy.

The Dice System: Fun, Accessible, and Dramatic
At the heart of City of Mist is a simple dice-pool mechanic designed to stay out of the way of storytelling. Most actions are resolved with a pool of d6s equal to a relevant stat plus a Power Tag. Each die that comes up as a success (usually a 4+) contributes to the result. A few intricacies then add texture: the number of successes might determine how well a plan works, while the presence of hard complications or “twists” can reveal new clues or alter the scene’s direction.

What makes the system sing is the “tag economy.” Players don’t merely roll what they have; they strategically invoke Power Tags—short, descriptive adjectives or phrases that unlock new dice or modify outcomes. It’s a compact, narratively expressive mechanic that rewards clever framing and collaborative storytelling. For example, a tag like “Crowd-Source Evidence” might allow a player to roll with extra dice when gathering clues during a crowded street scene, while a tag like “Hollow Victory” could spice the aftermath of a fight by granting a complication that keeps the story moving toward a moral or plot payoff.

The advantage of this approach is that the game stays readable and quick. You don’t need a spreadsheet or a calculator to participate; you simply think about the story you want to tell and which tags fortify that story in the moment. The D6 mechanic supports dramatic tension; it’s easy to escalate a scene by introducing a narrative complication and watching the dice do the storytelling for you. The rulebook does a good job of clarifying what counts as a success, what the cost is for a strong result, and when the GM should lean into consequences that aren’t just “the bad guy loses a punch” but “the city reveals a new facet of itself.”

Character Creation: Mythos, Logos, and the Masks in Between
The character creation process in City of Mist is one of its most delightful aspects. It’s not about grinding numbers; it’s about discovering a character concept and then grafting it onto a noir city with mythic resonance. You begin by naming your Mythos—the legend your character channels: It might be a mythic archetype (The Trickster, The Warrior, The Oracle) or a specific myth from world folklore that you’ve reframed to fit the city. Then you pick a Logos—the mundane identity that anchors your character in the real world: a job, a relationship, a reputation, a vulnerability.

Next come the Power Tags. Tags are the character’s abilities and strong suits; they’re the levers you’ll pull when you’re rolling dice. You can have multiple tags that describe different facets of your Mythos and Logos, from “Guns & Gospels” to “Screens & Sleight of Hand,” from “Glamour of Masks” to “Tech Wizardry.” The interplay between Mythos and Logos creates thematic expectations for the table: the Mask you wear changes how you read clues; your public persona affects how allies and enemies perceive you.

The book provides a robust toolkit for building a cast. It prescribes prompts to help you pin down your character’s flaws, motivations, and relationships with other players’ characters. This is where the game shines as a collaborative storytelling experience. It invites players to fashion a shared world with recurring motifs and a tone that swings between mystery and myth. It’s easy to end up with a party whose members feel like a living noir vignette rather than a group of arbitrary power fantasies. That’s a win in a game about identity and how a city can mold you into either a champion or a cautionary tale.

Running City of Mist: Scenes, Truths, and the City Itself
City of Mist shines when you run it as a city-wide mystery with episodic arcs. The Core Rulebook includes a road map for structuring scenes so you move from investigation to confrontation to revelation in a satisfying arc. You’ll often start with a clue that looks trivial but gradually spirals into a larger truth about the city’s corruption, its legends, or a hidden faction manipulating the streets behind the scenes. The Truths about the city—these city-born facts—are essential tools for the GM. They shape what’s possible in any given scene and offer players anchors to tether their Mythos and Logos to the world.

In practice, the game rewards players who lean into the city’s contradictions. The GM presents a problem—someone is siphoning power from a utility substation, or a legendary figure is leaking memories into the subway walls—and the players draw on their tags to decode what’s really happening. The result is a scene that feels kinetic and cinematic, with players contributing evocative lines of dialogue, dramatic beats, and surprising twists. The Core Rulebook has a healthy balance of guidance and space for improvisation, which is exactly what you want in a game whose core premise is narrative discovery rather than predetermined outcomes.

Art, Layout, and Accessibility
The aesthetic of City of Mist is a big part of its appeal. The Core Rulebook uses neon-edged art, moody cityscapes, and a clean, readable layout that makes it easy to parse rules while still feeling immersed in the mood. For first-time players, there’s a gentle onboarding that guides you through the basics before letting you sprint into the deep end. The text is approachable, and sidebars offer quick checks for GMs who want to keep the pace snappy without losing narrative flavor. If you’re a visually oriented player, you’ll appreciate how the art reinforces the themes of myth meeting street-level reality.

Pros and Cons: Where City of Mist Shines and Where It Requires Patience
Pros:
- Narrative-forward mechanics that reward storytelling choices over calculations.
- A strong thematic premise: mythic identity meets modern noir in a way that invites character-driven stories.
- Quick onboarding for new players; you can start playing with a single session’s worth of guidance and expand later.
- The tag economy is elegant and scalable; it invites both players and GMs to contribute creatively to the scene’s outcome.
- Beautiful art, layout, and mood that make the book a pleasure to read and reference.

Cons:
- If you crave crunchy, numeric optimization, City of Mist isn’t going to scratch that itch. The system prioritizes narrative shape over optimization, which can feel underwhelming to players who love precise stats and power ladders.
- The “tag economy” can become a little opaque if you try to maximize it without investing in the story. It requires periodic reminders from the GM to keep the narrative lean and fun.
- Some players may find the two-identity concept a little abstract at first; onboarding and collaborative world-building help, but it’s not always intuitive on day one.

Who Should Buy This?
If you enjoy urban fantasy with a noir twist, if you love the idea of heroes who aren’t purely “power fantasies” but rather “mythic identities figuring out who they are in a complicated city,” City of Mist is a great fit. It plays well as a one-shot or a campaign, and its episodic structure makes it easy to slot into a game night. It’s particularly appealing for players who like roleplay-first experiences, cinematic action, and a setting that feels both timeless and timely.

A Quick Starter Plan for New Games
- Gather a group of 3–5 players who enjoy character-driven stories and a city with secrets.
- Read the core rules together and pick a city vibe: neon-drenched Gotham-in-spirit, or a smoky, rainy, street-level Chicago-of-myths. The core book gives you enough to begin quickly.
- Start with one short mystery arc: a missing memory, a rumored legend among the city’s tunnels, a power outage that reveals something the city wants to forget.
- Introduce two to three tags per character and a couple of Truths that shape the investigation. Don’t overstuff the roster; the aim is to encourage improvisation.
- Use the internal prompts and the city-wide Truths to guide the session, but let the players bring their myths to life and watch the city bloom around their choices.

Design Notes and Editorial Voice
The Core Rulebook reads as if a seasoned GM decided to write a love letter to a city that refuses to stay down. The prose embraces the vibe: lush, witty, and a little snarky about the bad things we do for the greater myth. It’s not merely a technical manual; it’s a companion for a shared narrative journey. The authors consistently remind you that the point isn’t to “outwit” the system but to tell a story where a single decision can flip the entire scene—the difference between a tragedy and a triumph, and the moment when a legend steps onto the street and the street itself remembers its name.

World-Building Tips: Making Your City Feel Real
City of Mist isn’t shy about making the city a real character. To make your city feel alive, you’ll want to emphasize three things: local color (neighborhoods with distinct personalities), recurring threats (corrupt institutions, rival legends, or a “mystery” that humans are too close to understand), and episodic rhythm (the order in which mysteries unfold, and how the players’ actions ripple through the city’s social fabric). The Core Rulebook provides prompts that help you pepper your sessions with human stakes: a loved one’s safety, a job on the line, a public image at stake. These stakes anchor the mythic elements to something recognizable, which is essential for keeping players emotionally invested.

Final Verdict and Recommendation
City of Mist: Core Rulebook is a confident, stylish invitation into a world where myths walk the city’s gutters and style matters as much as strategy. If you want a game that rewards cooperative storytelling, niche-driven character concepts, and a setting that feels like a living, breathing myth you can walk through at 2 a.m., City of Mist is a must-have.
