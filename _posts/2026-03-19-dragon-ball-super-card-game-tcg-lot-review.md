## Condition, Grading, and What Mint Gradable Really Means
Let’s talk about the elephant in the card shop: grading. Mint gradable is a term that walks a fine line between “perfect” and “nearly perfect.” In practice, mint gradable cards are typically those that would pass a professional grading service test with a respectable score, often around 8.0 to 9.0 on common scales, depending on the card and the grader. Here’s how to think about it:

- Centering: A lot of mint gradable cards will present with decent centering, not absolutely perfect but balanced enough not to drive graders into a frenzy. If a card looks symmetric in a rack of similar cards, you’re probably safe.
- Edges and Corners: Expect clean edges, with the possibility of minor whitening in extreme cases. The goal is to avoid major dings, creases, or edge chipping that would shout “I’ve lived a life on the battlefield.”
- Surface: In mint gradable territory, surface gloss should be clear and free of deep scratches or print defects that would ruin a grading panel’s day.

Meanwhile, NM holo cards are those with light wear but still strong holo reflectivity and overall eye appeal. Minor surface scratches or slight corner wear are not unusual in NM status, especially if the cards spent time in a well-loved binder or protective sleeves that did their best to battle the elements.

As a buyer or a dispassionate investor of sorts, you’ll want to visually inspect: are there any reversible surface scuffs? Do the holo patterns align cleanly with the artwork, or do you notice halo effects that hint at a misprint? A lot of NM holo cards can still fetch strong prices if they present with the right balance of shine and wear controlled by time and handling.
