## How to Spot Fakes or Reprints
- Look for the exact palette and line weight of the Rider Back pattern. Counterfeits often have subtle misalignments in line thickness or slightly off-center pips.
- Check the seal integrity. A resealed deck usually has irregular adhesive or a tape job that doesn’t match classic USPCC packaging.
- Verify the stock finish and feel. If the deck feels unusually slick or extraordinarily rough compared to the standard Rider Back, you might be looking at a reproduction rather than a genuine vintage sealed deck.
- Compare the box typography. Vintage boxes have a very particular look; fakes sometimes mis-spell or misplace logos, which is a dead giveaway.

If you’re uncertain, consult community resources. The Geeknite crew is always curious about real-deal vintage items, and there are multiple threads where collectors discuss small anomalies that reveal a deck’s true identity.
