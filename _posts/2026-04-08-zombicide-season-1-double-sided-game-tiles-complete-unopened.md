Storage and handling considerations

## Keeping it pristine

Unopened sets have a pristine advantage: you can store the tiles in their original packaging, which is designed to keep moisture and dust out while preserving edge integrity. When you eventually decide to crack the seal, you can expect the tiles to unfold with minimal friction—no adhesives or gluey residues to contend with. If you plan to transport to a gaming night, consider a dedicated tile tub or a rugged tote that keeps the sheets flat. The last thing you want is a bendy tile that looks a little suspicious and possibly invites a trapped-in-a-ghost-zombie moment when you try to straighten it later.

If you are a collector-type, keeping the box in a climate-controlled environment matters as much as anything else. Temperature swings can cause cardboard to warp a bit over long periods, and while a season of zombie chaos is unlikely to melt your tile stock, a well-kept unopened set will age with dignity. A quick mass check every few months is not overkill. A little maintenance habit avoids the dreaded shelf-shimmy when you finally decide to set up a game night that lasts longer than your last pizza order.

Collector tips and common pitfalls

## What to watch for

- Inspect the box seal: a tampered seal is not a great sign for unopened status. If you see the seal intact, you likely have the original product as intended. 
- Check the tile backs for consistent color and no obvious misprints. If you catch a print issue early, you can request a replacement from the retailer or CMON’s support on a case-by-case basis. 
- Avoid bending the sheets when opening. A small blade or gentle prying is often enough to separate the inner packaging without stressing the tile edges. 
- If you plan to sleeve or label the tiles, do so with care. Some players like to sleeving the tiles for added durability, which can help if you’re planning to use this set in a high-traffic gaming night.
