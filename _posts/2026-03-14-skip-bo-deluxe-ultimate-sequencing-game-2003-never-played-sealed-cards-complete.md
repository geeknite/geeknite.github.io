## The Playability Test: Open or Preserve, That Is the Question
Here is the classic dilemma: if you have a sealed copy, do you crack it open to actually play it, or preserve it as a pristine artifact? Each path has its own joy and its own regrets.

- Open it: You gain the full tactile thrill of shuffling the cards, hearing the soft clack as the plastic trays click back into place, and the smell of a new to you old title. You can test the rules in real life, enjoy a few rounds, and create living memory around your table. The drawback is that the time capsule loses a piece of its untamed mystery and you may end up with a well-loved deck rather than a perfect artifact.
- Keep it sealed: You retain the measure of nostalgia and maximize collectibility. It becomes a display piece, a topic of conversation at gatherings, and a reminder of the era when you could buy a game for the simplicity of pure packaging excitement. The downside is you miss the sensory joys of handling the cards and seeing a deck in action on your table.

Practical takeaway: if your shelves are cluttered with modern curiosities and you have a sealed copy that brings more display value than ready play value, consider acquiring a second, non sealed copy for regular nights. This is a classic case of balancing preservation with participation, a balancing act that many vintage collectors practice with care.
