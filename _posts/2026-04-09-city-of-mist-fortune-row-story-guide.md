It’s a place where the city’s rain tastes like scotch and every alley whispers a rumor bigger than a subway sponsor. If you have ever wanted to run a scenario where detectives don’t just chase the culprit but also chase themselves through a maze of myths, Fortune Row is your sandbox. This story guide aims to be a toolkit rather than a script: a way to frame investigative momentum, inject mythic resonance, and keep players giggling, gasping, and occasionally frightened by how on-the-nose reality can get when the Mists roll in.

What Makes Fortune Row Special

Fortune Row is not just a street; it’s a mood, a set of rumors, and a string of echoes from a city that never fully remembers a crime. In the City of Mist system, the line between investigator and suspect is intentionally blurry, and Fortune Row leans into that ambiguity with precision. The guide is built around three pillars: the mythic backbone (the heroes are anchored by a set of legendary archetypes), the mystery engine (the clues, suspects, and twists that keep players leaning forward), and the mood (a neon-drenched noir punctured by mythic symbolism and occasional antics). It’s a great way to test your GM timing: when to reveal and when to reveal a little more glow from the Mists that makes the players squint and whisper, “Is that planned, or did the dice just roll their own fate?”

Overview: Fortune Row in a Nutshell

Fortune Row is a micro-arc, meant to slot into a single- or multi-session game depending on your appetite. It centers on three things: a storefront clique of divinities with human faces, a mystery that ties personal pasts to city-wide mythic undercurrents, and a handful of encounters that can be swapped according to player choices. The guide balances investigative tempo with action beats, letting players chase leads that feel purposeful rather than procedurally random. You’ll get a couple of ready-made scenes, a suspects list, and several clues that interlock once you start piecing them together. If you have ever played a mystery where the clues only click after you’ve rolled a dozen dice, Fortune Row will feel like a relief—one that still delights in how bad decisions sometimes yield excellent outcomes.

Thematically, Fortune Row leans into the inevitable tension between personal myth and public identity. The Mist does not care about your real name or your daytime job; it cares about what your legend demands you become. Expect themes of trust, adaptation, and the old truth that the city will always reveal its own secrets on a rainy Tuesday when the coffee machine overheats and the neon sign above the corner bodega flickers in sync with your pulse.

Character and Faction Primer

Fortune Row’s core players in the myth soup are the Masks, the Personas that City of Mist players heroically or tragically inhabit. In this guide, you’ll encounter a trio of NPCs who embody the Row’s fragile equilibrium: a fortune-teller with a vendetta, a streetwise fixer who can prod the Mists for the right price, and a librarian who keeps the city’s forgotten legends in a locked cabinet that only opens when certain stars align. You’ll also meet a rival faction that wants to keep the Row “pure,” a shadowy benefactor who sees the Row as a useful tool, and a chorus of everyday people who are suspicious of anything that can predict their own doom and still offer a free cup of coffee.

Adventure Structure: A Flexible Skeleton

The Fortune Row guide is designed in layers. The outer layer gives players a sense of place and a timeline: an evening’s worth of events that escalate toward a choice with consequences. The inner layer consists of the city’s hidden logic: a network of relationships, a set of iconic relics, and a handful of dramatic reveals that hinge on the players’ decisions rather than on rolled dice alone. The skeleton is simple:

1) Hook: Something in Fortune Row goes wrong, but the wrongness is exactly what your players want to investigate. The hook should be both personal and public—something that hits close to home for at least one PC and feels like a small city-wide ember at the same time.
2) Investigation: Clues are distributed among scenes that emphasize social deduction and mythic resonance. Clues should be meaningful, not random. Each discovery should nudge the players toward the next scene, and each scene should have a chance to twist the story toward a different path.
3) Turning Point: A choice that changes the trajectory. It might be a decision to protect someone at the cost of a larger secret, or a moment where a character’s past becomes an enormous obstacle to a successful outcome.
4) Climax: A confrontation that blends the reality of the city with its mythic overlays. The Mists should feel close, not distant, and the Player Characters should feel the weight of their own masks.
5) Aftermath: Consequences ripple into future sessions. This is where you can plant seeds for ongoing arcs or tie Fortune Row into a broader campaign world.

NPCs, Clues, and Secrets: The Heartbeat of the Row

You’ll want a tight cast. In Fortune Row, each character has a clear motive, a personal motto, and a secret that intertwines with the others. Here are quick sketches you can drop into your table:
- The Fortune-Teller: A mercurial psychic who is convinced the Row is about to be rebalanced by a force from the Mists. They know a critical truth but refuse to reveal it until a price is paid in a way that makes sense to their own code.
- The Fixer: They know the city’s back alleys better than the police know their own precinct. They are capable of procuring almost anything, but every favor comes with a debt that has to be paid in myth or memory.
- The Librarian: A keeper of forgotten legends who has a cabinet that opens only when certain signs align. Inside is a relic that can rewrite a person’s story if used correctly, but doing so may unmask a much larger conspiracy.
- The Rival Faction: A group that wants to curate Fortune Row into a spectacle of order. They fear what a fully realized misfit myth would do to their carefully staged city for profit and power.
- The Benefactor: A figure who is rarely seen, but whose influence stretches across the Row. They want outcomes aligned with their own vision of what the city should be, whether the city agrees or not.
- The Everyday People: Citizens who live their lives in the shadows of the Mists. They offer texture, stakes, and a reminder that not every mystery is a monument—some are about the cost of keeping a quiet life intact.

You’ll find more depth if you weave each NPC’s personal arc into the players’ backstories. A simple trick: tie each NPC to one of the player characters’ Masks and give that Mask a rival or a past grievance that emerges during the course of play. This creates an organic social network that can drive scenes without the GM needing to force it.

Scenes and Encounters: A Starter Menu

Fortune Row offers a set of ready-to-run scenes, but the magic comes from letting the players pick their path through them. Here are three scenes to spark momentum, each designed to blend roleplay with a dramatic payoff:

Scene 1: The Fortune Row Café Encounter
- Setting: A café that sells coffee strong enough to wake a slumbering myth.
- Hook: The café’s owner claims the menu is haunted by a ghost who used to owe the place money; patrons claim the ghost is a metaphor, but the café’s espresso machine seems to hiss in languages no mortal tongue should know.
- Clues: A receipt that reveals a debt to a secret coven, a torn tarot card with a sigil the PCs recognize, and a whispered rumor about a Hidden Room behind the bookcase.
- Outcome: If the PCs decide to follow the debt, they uncover a past version of a local hero who was erased from every public record. If they ignore it, the debt spirals and a small disaster hits the Row—one that forces the city’s underbelly to take a stronger, less forgiving stance toward the heroes.

Scene 2: The Dockside Mists Encounter
- Setting: The docks during a fog that feels almost tangible, like a soft hand brushing the back of your neck.
- Hook: A cargo crate that should be empty resounds with a pulse of something alive and ancient. The pulse matches a character’s heartbeat in a way that is both comforting and terrifying.
- Clues: A map fragment, a coin that flips to reveal a hidden symbol when held under a lamppost, and a ship manifest listing names that aren’t quite human.
- Outcome: A choice about whether to reveal a trusted ally’s secrets to the Mists or shield them, with consequences that affect the city’s perception of the PCs.

Scene 3: The Tarot Rookery
- Setting: A backroom of a used bookshop that sells not just old stories but new destinies.
- Hook: A deck of tarot cards that rearranges itself when the PCs aren’t looking, predicting possible futures with a cruel, comic twist.
- Clues: A prophecy that hints at the city’s definitive end, a ledger of debts owed to the Mists, and a key that opens a door that should not exist.
- Outcome: The players decide whether to use the prophecy to rewrite the future or to confront the implications of letting fate run its own course. Either choice dramatically reshapes Fortune Row.

All three scenes are designed to reward careful listening and the players’ willingness to connect disparate clues. GM Tips: Running Fortune Row Like a Pro

- Embrace mood over mechanics when possible. A quick test roll should often be a stand-in for narration, not a hard gate. The players will fill the gaps with their own mythologies if you give them permission.
- Let NPCs have agency. A named NPC who speaks softly can derail a plan in the best possible way if their personal arc is strong. Don’t be afraid to let them argue with the players, or with each other, about what is truly happening in Fortune Row.
- Use misdirection with care. The Mists love to misdirect, and your players should feel the tug. But remember: a good misdirection should reveal something meaningful when it finally snaps into place.
- Tie back to the Masks. The more you weave a Town Myth (the Row’s underlying power) into each player’s arc, the more their engagement will deepen. It’s not just about solving a mystery; it’s about growing into what their Mask asks of them.
- Manage tone with light, witty flourishes. City of Mist thrives on noir and whimsy in the same sentence. Don’t fear a one-liner that lands just right, even if it undercuts a tense moment. The Row respects humor as a way to weather the storm.

Playing Fortune Row with Multiple Sessions

If you plan for a two- to three-session arc, you can split the Scenes into sessions that escalate. Session 1 can focus on the Hook and Investigation, Session 2 on the Turning Point and Climax, and Session 3 on the Aftermath and fallout that leads into another arc with a similar mood. This pacing gives players a sense of progression while preserving the Row’s mystique. If you need a ready-made schedule, you can adapt the module from the Quick Setup post linked earlier. It’s not a rigid clock; it’s a rhythm you can tune as you play.

Interludes, Props, and Visual Aids

A few well-chosen props can anchor the session in Fortune Row without breaking immersion. Consider a laminated map of the Row, a deck of tarot cards with hand-painted sigils, a ledger that glows faintly when held under a streetlight, and a small pocket watch that ticks in double-time when the Mists are near. For players who love immersion, you can print small “mask cards” that each PC uses during scenes to remind them of their mythic ownership and the element of the Row that matters most to their character.

A Note on Safety and Comfort

City of Mist is about masks, secrets, and power. It’s possible to push too far into dark territory. A good rule of thumb is to check in on player comfort levels after a few scenes, and to have an agreed-upon Safety Plan in place before starting Fortune Row. The Row’s mood works best when players feel both the heat of danger and the safety net of a shared joke when the night gets too heavy. So set an origin point, a soft boundary, and a few red lines you’re comfortable with, and let the Mists keep their own mysteries while your players keep their humanity intact.

Notes on Post-Game Reflection and Narrative Echoes

After you wrap Fortune Row, consider a brief post-game reflection. Let players talk about what they learned about their Masks, what secrets changed their characters’ definitions, and what new doors their choices opened for the next arc. The best City of Mist sessions end with a sense that the city is different now—more aware of the characters, or more aware of its own fragility. The Spark of Future Arcs often begins with a single decision made in Fortune Row, so give it space to breathe and to grow in the players’ imaginations.

Why Fortune Row Is a Great Starter Arc for New GMs

If you are new to City of Mist, Fortune Row is forgiving but flavorful. It lets you learn the rhythm of investigation, pacing, and mythic storytelling without requiring you to memorize a hundred NPCs. The module gives you a well-defined cast, a few clean hooks, and enough flexibility to grow with your table. It’s the kind of adventure that makes players think, write, reflect, and, crucially, want to come back for another session. Plus, it has more neon and more mythic weight than most mainstream campaigns, and that’s a selling point in any group that loves atmospheric storytelling, clever banter, and the occasional dramatic facepalm when the plan goes sideways in the best possible way.

Conclusion and Final Recommendation

Fortune Row is a compact, pulse-quickening, myth-rich street-level mystery that feels like it was designed by someone who watches noir flicks and reads fables in the same sitting. It gives you a lush setting, a clear investigative throughline, and a handful of moments that can become legendary at the table—the kind of moments that players will retell at conventions and late-night streams with the same pride as their character’s most outrageous lie. If you want a City of Mist arc that can stand on its own or slot neatly into a larger campaign, Fortune Row is a reliable, creative, and deeply satisfying choice. The mood is right, the clues click with a satisfying cadence, and the Masked heroes have enough agency to feel like co-authors of their own legend.
