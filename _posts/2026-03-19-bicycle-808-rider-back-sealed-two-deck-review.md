## Durability, Care, and Longevity
### Everyday Care
Like any card stock, these decks reward a light touch and a clean environment. Avoid bending or flexing the cards into shapes that would insult their geometry. The air-cushion finish is durable enough for typical home use, but it’s not invincible. If you’re playing in a high-stress environment (think crowded cafes with espresso machines at full volume and a toddler who thinks every deck is a teething toy), consider storing decks in a protective card case when not in use. A proper box or a soft case will extend the life of the finish, especially if you or your group tends to handle cards with sweaty hands after intense games.

### Longevity and Handling Woes
If you’re a heavy user who shuffles aggressively, you’ll notice that wear naturally occurs along the edges and the faces after sustained sessions. That’s normal. The important thing is to rotate decks and not keep the same pair in rotation overnight. Two decks, especially sealed, provide you with a built-in rotation cycle: when a deck looks tired, you swap it with its partner and bring out a fresh one from the shelf (or the sealed packet you haven’t opened yet). This rotation reduces the risk of early edge wear and keeps your two-deck night feeling fresh and professional.

### Storage Tips
Store the decks away from direct sunlight to preserve color fidelity. A cool, dry place is ideal; humidity can cause warping of the tuck boxes and may impact the feel of the card stock over time. If you’re a magician or a game night host, consider a small cabinet or display case that keeps the decks near-room-temperature and free from dust. A microfiber cloth can help keep the faces clean when you’re prepping a trick or setting up a poker table.
