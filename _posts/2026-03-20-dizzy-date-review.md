## What is Dizzy Date

## How to play
The rules are straightforward on paper, but the chaos comes from the deck and any twists that appear during a round. The game begins with each partner taking turns drawing a card from the top of the deck. The card indicates the action that must be performed. If the card says to perform a truth, both partners answer truthfully to the prompt. If the card says to do a dare, you perform a silly task.

If a Twist card is drawn, you must follow the new rule for that round. The rules can include:

- Swap seats
- Take a drink every time a thing is referenced
- Skip a turn but do a mini dance
- Swap a question answer with your partner

The spinner adds a random factor to the game. If the spinner lands on a dizzy zone you must drink twice or perform a more difficult dare.

A typical session lasts about 20 to 40 minutes depending on the mood and how stubborn your dog is about not letting you skip a round.

We played a few rounds with a couple of friends and found the group dynamic shift in unpredictable ways. It is also possible to moderate the intensity by setting a limit on the number of drinks per game.

## How to adapt for different play styles
If you are a couple who prefers light hearted fun, use fewer dares and more truth prompts. If you want more romance, swap in prompts that emphasize communication and understanding. For couples who enjoy competitive play, set scores and race to something fun like a weekend trip or a movie night.

Remote play requires a little creativity. The digital overlay helps with timer and prompts, but you will still need to maintain the vibe. Here is a quick cheat sheet:

- Agree on a safe word and a maximum number of drinks per round
- Use a video call with bright lighting so faces are readable
- Allow the other person to veto a card if the prompt feels uncomfortable
- Switch to a non alcoholic beverage for a responsible game session

We also included a variant that uses a spin wheel on your phone. It is not the same as a real spinning wheel but it does the job.
