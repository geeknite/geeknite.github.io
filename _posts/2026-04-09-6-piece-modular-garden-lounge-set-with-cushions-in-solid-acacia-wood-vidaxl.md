### Step by step, with a dash of humor

1. Clear a flat outdoor space where rain won’t sneak in from the sides to judge your taste. Lay out all modules and check that you have all the pieces. If you find a stray connector, it likely belongs to a different set in your attic of mystery.
2. Start with a basic configuration that resembles a long bench or a corner sofa. Connect armless pieces to corner pieces, establishing your base. The connectors are designed to slot in with modest torque – avoid turning the wrench like you are opening a secret vault. Patience is your friend here.
3. Add the coffee table and ottomans to fit your chosen arrangement. Remember that modular means flexible, so try a few shapes to see what feels most social without becoming a human Tetris challenge.
4. Attach cushions last. Cushions serve as the soft, forgiving layer that makes your configuration feel like a hug rather than a stern wooden AGM meeting.
5. Step back, take a breath, and adjust any misaligned corners with a gentle nudge. If you can do this without swallowing a screw, you’re a hero in our book.

### Maintenance basics to keep the wood singing

Acacia wood loves the outdoors but appreciates a little care. A light clean with mild soap and water can remove dust and grime. For the wood itself, consider periodic treatment with a suitable outdoor wood sealer or oil to maintain color and reduce the risk of cracking. The frequency depends on your climate and sun exposure; in harsher climates, you might reapply every 6 to 12 months. If you live somewhere with heavy rain or snowfall, it’s wise to store the cushions indoors when not in use or use weather-resistant covers. A cover can dramatically extend cushion life and keep the color palette looking crisp rather than sun-washed. The goal is simple: protect the wood and cushions from the elements while allowing the furniture to tell a story of sunlit days and midnight garden revelries.
