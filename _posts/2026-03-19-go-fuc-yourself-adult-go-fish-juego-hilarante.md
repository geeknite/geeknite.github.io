## How to play: the bones of it

### Turn structure at a glance
Each turn starts with a player requesting a card value from another player. In this adult edition, requests are accompanied by a humorous taunt and a dare card that can be claimed if the target refuses. If the asked player has the card, they must hand over all cards of that value and the requester gets another turn. If not, the turn passes to the next player and a card from the draw pile is revealed. That card may be a pass or a new dare, and the chaos continues.

### Sets, points, and the long arc of glory
A set consists of four cards of the same value. When a player completes a set, they lay it down in front of them and earn points. The player with the most points at the end of the game wins. In practice, this can become less a number based victory and more a trophy of shared stories, masks of embarrassment, and a chorus of hilariously improvised apologies.

### Dare cards and consent boundaries
Dare cards spice things up by prompting players to perform silly tasks, answer ridiculous questions, or make bold declarations. Boundaries are essential; you can agree to a no moral policing rule or a take it easy mode for first time players. The goal is to create shared laughs, not to corner anyone into a corner that feels less like a party and more like a therapy session with a bad sense of humor. If a dare crosses a line for anyone, that person can opt out with a safe word or a simple consent nod.

### End of round and villain origin stories
Rounds keep cycling until the draw pile is exhausted or until someone declares they have had enough therapy in a card game. At that point the score is tallied, and the stories from the round are recapped. Expect a few legends to emerge about the one time someone asked for a card and instead found their dignity in the bottom of a token stack.
