## Gameplay overview: how the 3 in 1 actually plays

### Setup and baseline rules
- Shuffle each deck separately or, if you’re a chaos connoisseur, mix them all into one big pulp of cardboard. The rules still apply: match color or number, watch out for the flip side, and keep an eye on those mercy tokens that appear sporadically in the expansion pack.
- Each player draws seven cards and the top card of the draw pile starts the discard pile. The standard UNO trick about sprinting toward the mercy of the group is irrelevant here; mercy rarely visits a 3 in 1 table—unless someone pulls off a spectacular combo that saves everyone from the brink. Then perhaps mercy will tip its hat and rejoin the table briefly.

### Turn flow and the flip mechanic
UNO Flip’s signature mechanic is the flip card. On your turn, you may play a standard card that matches color or number. When a flip card is played, the entire game direction toggles between the “light” and “dark” sides, potentially changing color demands and the type of cards you can play. In this pack, the Show Em No Mercy variants add interactions that encourage players to bluff, threaten, or loudly declare “mercy!” only to be undercut by a well-timed penalty card.

The expansion adds optional rules for late-game chaos. For example, there are cards that force players to swap hands with another player or to reveal a portion of their hand. The first time you taste the fear of exposing your secret weapon, you’ll realize why this pack exists: to force you to consider your own bravado and to question your values while you blurt out strategic excuses.

- Use a timer for rounds: a quick timer keeps the pace up and prevents one player from becoming the de facto game-master for 20 minutes. It also helps you retire from the table without feeling like you’re abandoning your friends mid-game.
- Set expectations before the game starts: remind people this is a three-in-one experience, with options to dial up or dial down the intensity. If someone is new to UNO variants, give them space to learn without judgment. Let the merciless banter be the seasoning, not the main course.
