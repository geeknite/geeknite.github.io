## Assembly and practical setup tips

Assembling a desk this size is not a life-or-death situation, but it’s not a light afternoon either. Here are some practical tips to avoid cursing the hex keys:

- Clear your space. The box comes in pieces that want to live under your couch for a year. Move the couch, the plant, and your lingering guilt about not returning that book you read once.
- Check alignment. Frame anchors and crossbars demand alignment with the desk top. A little patience goes a long way, and so does a friend who doesn’t mind pretending the Allen wrench is a tiny hammer of justice.
- Organize hardware. Keep bolts sorted by length and thread type. It’s a small thing that saves you from a scavenger hunt the size of a season finale.
- Don’t over-tighten. You’ll feel like you’re assembling a tiny spaceship, but you might strip a hole and cry into your warranty. Tighten snugly, then an extra quarter-turn for good measure.

Cable management is where this desk shines or hides its mischief. Grommets are typically provided, and there are channels for routing power cords and USB cables. A top-tip: plan your monitor, PC, and power strip placement before you mount things. The goal is a neat, accessible under-desk space so you can pretend you’re living in a magazine while the rest of your life looks like a hurricane crossed with a thrift store.
