## Assembly and setup tips
Assembling a colossal desk is not a weekend hobby; it’s a test of patience, pair programming with your hardware, and a playlist that won’t judge you for using every heavy-sounding tool you own. Here are practical tips to survive the build:
- Clear the space first: you’ll need a large clearing area for all the panels, screws, and the occasional “Where did that one tiny part go?” moment.
- Sort hardware by bag: if your kit includes numbered bags, lay them out in numerical order so you’re not hunting tiny screws while cursing your decision to DIY.
- Read the manual twice: step 1 may seem obvious, but you’ll thank yourself later when you realize you’ve misaligned a rail and spent two hours detaching and reattaching things.
- Have the right tools: most kits come with the basics, but a good screwdriver with magnetic tips and a rubber mallet for seat checks never hurts.
- Take turns: if you’re building with a partner, take shifts. This reduces bickering, speeds up assembly, and ensures you’re not both overcaffeinated at the same time.
- Cable plan ahead: sketch a rough map of where each cable goes, then route them through the channels as you go. It’s less sexy than a blueprint, but it saves your future self from a spaghetti-hour of disaster.

### Common pitfalls and how to avoid them
- Misaligned rails: double-check before tightening. Loose rails are the arch-nemesis of long-term stability.
- Tighten in the right sequence: most kits require a certain order for screws to ensure maximum squareness. Skipping the order is how you end up with a desk that looks like a modern art sculpture.
- Surface leveling: if your floor isn’t level, the desk will wobble. If you’re stuck with an uneven floor, consider adjustable feet or shims—gentle, deliberate, non-threatening shims.
- Monitor height check: the monitor stand should position screens at or slightly below eye level to reduce neck fatigue. If you end up with a “desk-eye view” that’s more like a ceiling gazing exercise, adjust the stand height before your posture becomes a character in a medical cautionary tale.
