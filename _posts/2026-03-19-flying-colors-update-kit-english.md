## First use and setup

Setting up is straightforward for hobby painters and perfectly approachable for newcomers. If you already know your way around acrylics, you will glide through the steps. If you are totally new, this kit offers a guided path that can accelerate your learning without overwhelming you. The basic steps:

1) Inspect caps and gently twist to open each pigment bottle. If you hear a tiny sigh, you are not imagining it; some pigments are dramatic about their debut.
2) Pour a small amount into the palette well and test on scrap paper or a spare sprue to gauge opacity and drying time.
3) Use the two charts to plan color families and mixes before committing to a glaze or highlight.
4) Clean brushes between colors and wipe the palette as needed to avoid muddying future layers.

The quick-start guide is helpful, but the real value comes from playing with the color wheel and charts. You will soon realize that the six pigments unlock new possibilities for shading, glazing, and edge highlights. The kit nudges you toward trying new combinations, even if your first few attempts look like a technicolor mistake that somehow worked its way into a painting you show off at conventions.

The included color wheel and mixing charts are practical and approachable. ## Maintenance, safety, and care

As with any art supplies, care protects longevity. Close pigment caps tightly to prevent evaporation or accidental drying. Store pigments in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight to avoid fading. Rinse brushes with mild soap and water after use and lay them flat to dry to preserve bristle shape. The palette tray cleans with a quick wipe, and the swatch cards can be stored in a protective sleeve for future reference. There are no hazardous solvents in this kit, which makes it friendlier to a shared studio space and to the environment when disposed of responsibly.
