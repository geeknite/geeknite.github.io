## Strategy tips and playstyle notes
### Getting ahead early
Momentum matters in Skip-Bo. In this Gift Set, you should aim to open with a clear plan for how to turn your stock pile into a sequence of plays. Don’t aggressively chase a single long run if it leaves too many gaps in your draw pile. A balanced approach—build a flexible sequence and use wilds strategically—will yield more reliable results across rounds.

### When to use wild cards
Wild cards are powerful, but they aren’t invincible. Use them to fill tricky gaps or to kickstart a chain that your opponents might disrupt with their own sequences. If you can, hold back a wild for a critical moment that could swing the trajectory of the game, such as when you’re itching to push a decisive turn and risk leaving yourself open to a counter-play.

### How to teach this to new players
The Gift Set’s best selling point for beginners is its clarity. Start with a single pass around the table, explaining the core rule: discard into a running stack in ascending order. Then show how Wilds can substitute missing numbers. As soon as the first person discards their first stock card, the table starts to hum. If you’ve got a kid who loves counting or a party guest who loves a little friendly competition, you’ll see eyes light up and a chorus of “do-over!” beginning to form.
