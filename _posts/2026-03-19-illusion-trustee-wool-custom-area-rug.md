### Custom size options vs 8x10: The size debate
One of the big selling points of the Illusion Trustee line is the promise of customization. You can order a rug that exactly fits your space, from the most awkward alcove in a studio apartment to a grand living room layout that resembles a game map. The two most common routes are:

- Custom sizes: You specify exact dimensions (length and width) and potentially a custom border or edge. Great for unusual rooms, or if you want to create a very specific focal plane for your furniture arrangement.
- 8x10 standard: The classic, scalable size that works for most mid-sized living rooms. It’s easier to estimate placement with an 8x10, and often slightly more budget-friendly than a bespoke dimension.

Pros of custom sizing:
- Perfect fit for irregular spaces (think pentagonal living rooms or a sofa anchored by a bay window).
- Flexible edge options and pile height adjustments.
- Potentially better for traffic flow if your layout is non-traditional.

Cons of custom sizing:
- Longer lead times and higher price point.
- Potential for mis-measures if you’re not precise in your room dimensions.
- Slightly more complicated return policies if it arrives and doesn’t fit your vibe.

Pros of 8x10:
- Quick to order, easy to envision within most rooms, and usually quicker shipping.
- Good balance of footprint and squishiness for under-table foot traffic and chair legs.
- Simpler maintenance scheduling and routine cleaning.

Cons of 8x10:
- May leave awkward gaps or space conflicts when you have an unusual seating arrangement.
- You might end up dreaming of a slightly larger or smaller rug after you see how the room flows with furniture.

To illustrate, imagine you’re equipping a home office that doubles as a streaming studio. ### Maintenance mindset
Wool rugs love routine. The recommended care cycle usually looks like this:
- Vacuum weekly with the beater bar off or on low suction to avoid pulling fibers.
- Rotate every few months to even out wear and sun exposure.
- Attend to spills promptly with blotting (don’t rub) and use a wool-safe cleaner for stains. The goal is to minimize stain set and fiber distortion.

## Care, Cleaning, and Keeping Your Illusion Trustee Jumping Lightly

### Cleaning wool rugs without turning your living room into a science experiment
Wool fibers are naturally somewhat resistant to staining and have great elasticity. The Illusion Trustee line spares you from dealing with a miracle-clean surface; you’ll still have to vacuum and do the occasional spot treatment.

- Regular vacuuming: Use a soft brush attachment and a low-suction setting to avoid friction that can push the fibers flat. Wool is resilient; treat it with a light touch.
- Spills: Blot—not rub. Start from the outside spill and move inward to prevent spreading. If the spill is stubborn (think red wine or coffee), a mild wool-safe cleaner is appropriate. Test on an inconspicuous corner first.
- Odor control: Wool tends to resist odors better than synthetics, which makes your space smell less like last night’s pizza and more like a curated library scent (okay, maybe not a library—more like a fresh-baked bread shop).

### Stain resistance and colorfastness
The Illusion Trustee buckles under some stain types; nothing is stain-proof except perhaps a cape made of Teflon. For most household accidents, prompt action plus proper wool care products will keep the rug looking vibrant. If you’re dealing with pet stains, consider enzyme cleaners designed for wool to prevent discoloration and fiber damage.

### Longevity and the care cycle
A well-cared-for wool rug can easily outlive a few living-room color palettes. Rotate layout and shield from direct sunlight (which can fade color over time). A light sun exposure test is good practice to prevent uneven fading across the rug surface. If you’re worried about sun damage, consider using curtains or UV-protective window films to preserve color integrity.
