## How Phase 10 Works (A Quick refresher)
### Core Mechanics
Phase 10 is a set-collection and hand-management game where players attempt to complete a series of 10 phases, each requiring a specific combination of cards (such as sets of the same number, runs, or mixed strategies). On your turn, you draw a card and then discard one, trying to assemble the phase shown on the current phase card. When you complete the phase, you advance to the next one. The twist comes from the fact that you can rearrange cards in your hand to fit the upcoming phase, while opponents can steal momentum by playing Skip cards on you or blocking your attempts to complete your phase.

The flow is simple enough for kids, yet it rewards planning, resource management, and a little bit of strategic risk-taking. The action cards add spice: Wild cards can substitute for any card in a phase, while Skip cards deny a turn to the next player. This isn’t a game of pure luck; it rewards players who plan a couple of phases ahead and who keep track of what their rivals need to complete their own sequences.
