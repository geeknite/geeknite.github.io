## The Basics

### What is Middle-earth Strategy Battle Game?

The Middle-earth Strategy Battle Game (MESBG) is a tabletop wargame produced by Games Workshop, based on J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-earth legendarium. Players collect, build, paint, and then battle with miniatures representing characters and forces from *The Lord of the Rings* and *The Hobbit*.

### Components

The game consists of beautifully sculpted miniatures, including heroes, villains, and armies from the rich lore of Middle-earth. Each model can be intricately painted and customized, providing endless possibilities for creativity.

## Painting and Modeling Essentials

### Tools and Supplies

Before you dive into painting, you'll need to gather some essential tools and supplies:

- **Brushes**: A variety of sizes for different details. Trust me, a good brush can make all the difference.
- **Paints**: Acrylic paints are preferred for their versatility and vibrant colors. Brands like Citadel, Vallejo, and Army Painter are popular choices.
- **Primer**: To ensure your paint sticks to the miniatures and provides a smooth base coat.
- **Palette**: For mixing and thinning paints.
- **Modeling Knife**: For trimming and cleaning your miniatures.
- **Glue**: For assembling multi-part miniatures.

**Pro Tip**: Don't forget a comfortable chair and good lighting. Your back and eyes will thank you later.

### Preparation

1. **Cleaning**: Remove any mold lines or flash from the miniatures using your modeling knife.
2. **Assembly**: Glue any parts together carefully, ensuring a tight and seamless fit.
3. **Priming**: Apply a primer to the miniatures to create a base for your paints. Black or white primers are common choices.

![Preparation](https://i.imgur.com/6s4IJiPm.png)

### Basic Painting Techniques

#### Basecoating

Start with a solid base coat for each area of the miniature. This provides a foundation for further layers and details.

#### Washing

Use washes to add depth and shadow to your models. A wash is a thin, dark paint applied over the base coat to enhance recesses and details.

#### Drybrushing

Drybrushing involves using a small amount of paint on a dry brush to highlight raised areas. It's perfect for adding texture and bringing out details.

#### Layering

Add layers of paint to build up color and create smooth transitions. Thin your paints with water to achieve a more natural look.

![Painting Techniques](https://i.imgur.com/Jm8ynpT.jpg)

### Advanced Techniques

#### Blending

Blending involves smoothly transitioning between colors. This technique requires practice but can create stunning effects on capes, banners, and magical elements.

#### Freehand

Freehand painting allows you to add custom designs and patterns to your miniatures. It's challenging but incredibly rewarding.

#### Weathering

Add realistic wear and tear to your models to give them a battle-hardened appearance. Techniques include chipping, rusting, and mud effects.

![Advanced Techniques](https://i.imgur.com/Jm8ynpT.jpg)

## Modeling Tips

### Dioramas

Creating dioramas can bring your miniatures to life. Use terrain elements like grass, rocks, and water effects to craft scenes from Middle-earth.

### Conversions

Customize your miniatures by converting them. Swap parts, sculpt new elements, or create entirely new characters to add uniqueness to your army.
