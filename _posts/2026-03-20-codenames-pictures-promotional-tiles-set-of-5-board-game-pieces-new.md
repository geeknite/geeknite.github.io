## How These Tiles Interact with Gameplay

Codenames Pictures hinges on players guessing which images correspond to the agents being hinted at. The five promotional tiles do not replace any core mechanic; instead, they act as optional wildcards that can be introduced to spice up a session. Here are a few practical ways to use them without breaking the game’s flow:

- Wildcard Clue Conduit: Assign one tile to be a wildcard image that can be treated as a bridge in a clue. For example, a tile with a picture that closely resembles two or more images on the board can be used to extend a clue through association, but you must declare the wildcard tile explicitly before you start the round.
- Photo-Roulette Round: Shuffle the five tiles into a small pool and draw one at random at the start of a round. The image on the tile becomes an additional constraint or boon depending on the group rule you agree on. This adds a tiny gambling edge to the round and a fresh, visual twist to standard play.
- Decoy and Diversion: Use a tile as a decoy. If a clue points toward one of the tiles in your pool, the tile can be bluffed as an actual target. The opposing team must interpret the clue in the context of the tile draw, which raises tension and merriment.
- Thematic Rounds: Tie a round style to a particular tile. If a tile’s image strongly aligns with a theme, use a community rule to grant extra time for deciphering or to allow a single additional guess. Thematic rounds can be a great way to celebrate a milestone night or to align with a pop culture moment.

The above are house-rule friendly and intentionally flexible. The tiles are designed to be a light-touch addition rather than a heavy-handed replacement for standard play. If your group values steady predictability, you can simply ignore them; if you crave a touch of improv and a dash of image-driven mischief, they will slot right in and deliver.

## Setup, Play, and House Rules Tips

- Keep the five promotional tiles within arm’s reach so players can consult them without a break in rhythm.
- At the start of a round, select whether to use none, one, or more tiles. A 0-1-2 rule often yields a nice balance between stability and novelty.
- If you implement a wildcard or decoy system, ensure all players agree before first clue is given. The last thing you want is a legend about a tile being a rogue agent during a heated moment.
- Use the tiles to create a short thematic mini-round, perhaps a 2-3 clue cap where the visuals must be connected to the clues given. This adds a pressure-cooking effect that seasoned players find satisfying.
- For streaming or content creation, prospective viewers love seeing how tiles influence deduction color and narrative style. If you want to annotate rounds, consider captioning tiles with a short, witty description as you play.

As always with promos, the key is to keep it light. The tiles are there to spark conversation, not to derail the core strategy. If you find a clever way to weave them into multi-round campaigns or a small narrative arc, you are doing it right.
