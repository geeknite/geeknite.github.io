## Maintenance and longevity

Keeping a carbon steel blade in decent health involves regular maintenance. A little oil after use, a wipe-down to remove moisture, and periodic sharpening as the edge dulls are part of the routine. Carbon steel benefits from light oiling to deter rust, especially if you’re using the sword for display and occasional handling rather than daily cutting sessions. Here are some practical steps:

- Wipe the blade with a clean, dry cloth after handling. Moisture is the enemy of carbon steel, especially if you live in a humid climate.
- Apply a thin film of mineral oil or sword oil to the blade to prevent rust. Don’t glob it on; a thin, even coat suffices.
- Keep the scabbard and handle dry. Humidity and moisture can seep in and affect the wood or composite materials.
- Check the fittings occasionally. If you notice loosening, address it quickly to prevent a wobble during handling.

Maintenance time can be a pleasant routine, a little hobby across a couple of evenings that teaches you about edge geometry, blade care, and the importance of a clean sword when you want to look cool on camera.
