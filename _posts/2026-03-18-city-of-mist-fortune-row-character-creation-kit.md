### What is Fortune Row?

Fortune Row is a character creation kit built for City of Mist players who want to kick off sessions with momentum rather than spend half the night debating a backstory that could haunt a campaign for years. The kit aggregates archetypes, prompts, and templates into a compact system that nudges you toward a concept that feels immediately cinematic while remaining compatible with the core mechanics of the game. The aim is twofold: speed and atmosphere. Speed means less time arguing about whether your character is a hacker with a heart of gold or a former cop who now solves crime with tarot. Atmosphere means you start with a character whose presence in the city feels inevitable, not a power fantasy wearing a new coat.

If you want a quick orientation: Fortune Row provides a set of archetype blocks called Fortune Row Profiles, each one containing a mask concept, a mythic tie, and a personal code of conduct. It ships with prompts for backstory, relationship suggestions for other PCs, and micro-goals that push the character toward interesting choices even before the first session begins. The result is a toolbox you can pull out mid-session when the party needs a spark, a villain with a soft spot, or a reason to roll the dice in a way that feels narratively satisfying rather than mechanically pedantic.

The City of Mist community loves both the idea and the drama of legend in the modern world. ## The Toolkit in Detail

### Fortune Row Profiles

Fortune Row Profiles are the backbone of the kit. Each profile acts as a ready-made foundation for your character concept and includes:

- The Mask: The public face your character wears in the City of Mist; this is the identity that interacts with the world.
- The Myth Tie: A link to a mythic energy that fuels your powers and shapes your decisions.
- The Core Goal: A driving purpose that propels the character forward and provides a straightforward hook for scenes.
- The Personal Code: A rough moral compass that affects how the character handles dilemmas and what they are willing to do to preserve their mask.
- Suggested Relationships: Quick ties to other PCs or key NPCs in Fortune Row to help you slot into group dynamics quickly.

The profiles are designed to be flexible, so you can tweak the details without feeling like you are breaking an ancient spell. If you have a character you like from a previous campaign, you can use Fortune Row profiles as a diagnostic tool to align the vibe with the city rather than forcing the concept to grind against the system rules.

### Mythos and Logos as flavor, not shackles
In City of Mist, the core concept revolves around the tension between Mythos and Logos—the mythic energy you channel and the grounded, human side of your identity. Fortune Row leans into this tension, giving you prompts that help you decide how your myth binds your human aspects and how your personal code clashes with the city’s demands. For example, a Fortune Row Profile might suggest that you can bend reality in small, meaningful ways, but only when the city is at risk or when your mask is being challenged. That kind of constraint creates drama rather than a loophole, and it helps players narrate the consequences of their choices.

This kit also provides micro-goals tied to decisions you make about masks and myth interactions. These micro-goals function as soft objectives that guide roleplay and help the GM weave you into story arcs without needing to stage a formal, two-hour backstory session. A good Fortune Row prompt becomes a story seed that the GM can pick up later when a twist in the plot requires a personal stake from your character.

### Quick-start character creation flow
- Step 1: Pick a Fortune Row Profile that resonates with the vibe you want, not just the power set you want.
- Step 2: Choose a mask that suits the profile and the city’s mood. The mask should be something you can play visually and narratively.
- Step 3: Tie your myth to a local Fortune Row anchor, a place, or a figure in the city that embodies the myth in a tangible way. This helps you anchor the energy in the setting.
- Step 4: Define your personal code and relate it to at least one other player’s character to build inter-character dynamics early on.
- Step 5: Align your goal with a small but specific arc, something that can be resolved in a few sessions while leaving room for a larger, citywide story.
- Step 6: Sketch one or two scene hooks that show how your character uses their powers in everyday life and in the city’s darker corners.
- Step 7: If your table uses Fortune Row cards or prompts, pick a few to have handy as a ready-made prompt for sessions when inspiration is running dry.

If you want a more guided approach, Fortune Row includes a sample run that introduces the first major Choice Card of the campaign. The Choice Card helps the group decide which mythic conflict will take center stage in the next session, providing a natural spark for session planning without derailing the narrative arc.

### Example profiles and quick stat lines
Note that in City of Mist you are not chasing raw numbers. Still, a quick stat line helps you speak the character aloud and negotiate scenes with the GM. Here are two sample profiles inspired by Fortune Row archetypes:

- The Silk Courier
  - Mask: The charming runner of secrets through the city’s back alleys.
  - Myth Tie: The spirit of a hidden courier network that knows every courier’s route and every pitfall on the road.
  - Core Goal: Deliver a message that could topple a micro-empire of corruption without getting caught.
  - Personal Code: Never reveal the full route; always leave a breadcrumb trail.
  - Relationship hooks: A rival street photographer; an old mentor who vanished into the Mist.
  - Quick hook: A midnight package requires a choice that reveals a larger conspiracy.

- The Neon Archivist
  - Mask: An urban librarian who stores the city’s myths like dusty tomes in a neon-lit archive.
  - Myth Tie: A living archive that can pull a city myth into the present when danger looms.
  - Core Goal: Protect a secret archive that contains the true names of the city’s monsters.
  - Personal Code: Preserve knowledge, even if it hurts people who deserve to know.
  - Relationship hooks: A corrupt detective who suspects your archive; a street kid who glimpses the city’s futures.
  - Quick hook: A break-in could expose a name that should never be spoken aloud.

These samples are not meant to be rigid templates; they are quick fuel for your imagination. You can adapt them or use them as springboards for entirely new concepts.

## Using Fortune Row in your City of Mist campaign

### How to integrate with existing material
Fortune Row is designed to slot into your table without forcing you to redo your current city narrative. If you already have a Fortune Row hub in your campaign, these profiles can fill gaps in the cast or offer fresh angles for old villains. For new campaigns, Fortune Row serves as a launchpad: you hand players a handful of quick prompts, set a Fortune Row hub in the city, and your table is ready to explore myths in the street.

If you want to maintain continuity with your existing NPCs and factions, Fortune Row profiles can be linked to those factions or locations as an extension. For example, the Fortune Row hub could be a new wing of the city’s archivist network or a hidden speakeasy for mythic energies. The kit provides enough scaffolding to fit with your current macro-storyline while still giving players a sense of fresh agency.

### Relationship with the GM’s toolkit
GMing City of Mist is about managing the balance between mythic drama and street-level reality. Fortune Row helps you do that by providing ready-made relationships and hooks that you can deploy quickly. The kit also includes guidance on how to pace the mythic elements and how to escalate a character-driven arc into a city-wide event. It is not a cheat sheet that makes players overpowered; it is a design aid that nudges you toward meaningful conflicts and memorable outcomes.

For the GM, the real payoff is the ease of weaving new players into the narrative. A couple of minutes to explain a Fortune Row Profile, plus a few prompts, can give you a strong opening for a session that feels cinematic rather than procedural. The end result is fewer hours spent brainstorming and more time spent playing and role-playing, which is exactly the kind of magic we geeks want in our weekly rhythm.

### A note on balance and tone
Fortune Row leans into the grit and glimmer of City of Mist. It is not about creating power fantasies that overshadow the world; it emphasizes the tension between the city’s myths and the people who carry them. Expect prompts that push moral complexity, difficult choices, and a sense that the city is a living, breathing character—one that asks your players to act, not just react. If your table loves noir banter, morally gray decisions, and a dash of vintage comic-book melodrama, Fortune Row will feel like home.
