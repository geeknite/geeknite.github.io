### Installation steps
Upgrading to high-capacity cyan is usually a straightforward swap, but a careful process helps ensure first-pass print quality and avoids air pockets that cause streaks:
1) Power down the DocuPrint and unplug it.
2) Open the toner cartridge access door and carefully remove the existing cyan cartridge.
3) Gently shake the cyan cartridge side-to-side a few times (not like a snow globe, but just enough to loosen settled toner).
4) Remove the protective seals and reinsert the cartridge into the correct bay with a firm, but not brutal, click.
5) Close the access door and power the printer back on. Run a test page to confirm alignment and color balance.

If you encounter color misregistration or streaks, a quick nozzle check and alignment page can fix many issues. For many DocuPrint users, these steps are part of a ritual: you perform the swap with the precision of a BIOS update, then bask in the glow of properly cyan-saturated output.

## Yield, cost, and value
### Page yield explained
The 11K claim is a best-case figure under controlled lab conditions with moderate color usage. Real-world yields depend heavily on image density, page coverage, and the mix of color usage across print jobs. For example:
- Lightly colored business documents with 2-5% cyan coverage per page will yield closer to the 11K mark.
- Colorful marketing collateral with heavy cyan sections (think skies, turquoise accents, or teal callouts) will reduce yield somewhat below the nominal 11K.
- Mixed-color documents will see yields in between, with cyan being a major cost driver if you print a lot of cyan-heavy material.

To estimate your own cost-per-page, take your typical monthly cyan page count, multiply by your per-page cyan toner usage, and divide by your cartridge capacity.
