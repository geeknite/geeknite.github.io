## How to Play (A Quick Refresher)

Skip-Bo is a casual card-drafting game with a core mechanic: players attempt to get rid of their stock piles by playing cards in ascending order into shared building piles. The numbers go from 1 to 12, and the wild Skip-Bo cards can stand in as any number to help you complete the sequence faster. The first person to deplete their stock pile wins. It’s a synergy of luck (the draw) and strategy (how you manage your hand and when you use wilds).

Key concepts to know:

- Stock piles: Each player starts with a face-down stock pile. The goal is to play cards from the top of your stock pile into the building piles.
- Build piles: In the middle of the table, everyone contributes to one or more build piles in ascending order. 1s, then 2s, then 3s, up to 12. It’s the classic “ladder to victory” trope, and it’s oddly satisfying when you place a 12 to finish a stack.
- Draw pile and hand management: You draw several cards into your hand and manage them to place onto builds or your stock pile. The decisions you make here drive the game’s pace and suspense.

In the deluxe version, the core rules remain intact. What changes is the packaging, the ease of setup, and the satisfaction of having a durable container to keep everything in one place. The game still revolves around quick turns, low downtime, and a social vibe that makes it easy to teach to newcomers during family gatherings.
