## Maintenance and Care: Keeping the Magic Alive

To keep the surface looking good and the play smooth, a light wipe with a dry microfiber cloth is usually enough. You’re not buried in a special wood cleaner requirement; the goal is to avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the veneer or remove the natural warmth that gives this table its charm.

If you notice the rods getting sticky after a few sessions, a quick dab of light mineral oil on the joints can help. Wipe away any excess; you don’t want to turn your foosball table into a slip-and-slide disaster zone. Periodically check the leg locks to ensure they’re snug. A loose leg can translate into wobble, which—let’s be real—destroys a good shot faster than a bad pizza choice ruins a party.
