## Maintenance and care
### Cleaning and long-term durability
Plastic dice towers are relatively low-maintenance. A quick wipe with a damp cloth, perhaps a mild soap solution for stubborn grime, keeps the exterior looking good. If you’ve got decals or weathering to apply, seal them lightly to preserve color and texture. Do not use harsh chemicals that could degrade the surface. A dedicated brush can help remove dust from the interior cracks where dice litter tends to accumulate.

### Storage tips
When storing, keep the lid securely in place and avoid stacking heavy objects on top of the tower. The last thing you want is an accidental dent that makes dice bounce oddly or a lid that doesn’t align properly on the next use. If you’ve customized the unit, keep your decals safe in a small bag so you can revert to the stock look if you decide to resell or loan the piece.

## Pro tips for getting the most out of your Dice Tower
- Align the tower with your primary line of fire for dice that minimizes obstruction of your terrain pieces.
- Use color-coded dice or labels to distinguish survivors from zombies; pride of ownership is a real thing, and it helps during clustered rounds when you’re counting successes for several combat sequences.

- Keep the table surface clean; a dusty surface can alter roll outcomes slightly, particularly with dice that have a matte finish.
