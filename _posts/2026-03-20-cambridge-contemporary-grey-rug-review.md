## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Care

### Vacuuming and Everyday Care
Regular vacuuming is important to keep the pile looking smart. A vacuum with a low to medium height setting is a good match for this rug, as it maintains fiber integrity without pulling fibers out of place. Try to vacuum in multiple directions to prevent a consistent pattern that can eventually appear in worn spots. If you have pets, consider a vacuum with a strong suction and a good beater bar to pull out fur and dust from the dense weave.

### Stains and Spills
Spills happen. The key to stain management is prompt action and suitability of cleaning agents. Blot the spill gently with a clean cloth to lift as much liquid as possible. Avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers. For typical household spills like coffee, tea, or light sauces, a mild detergent solution can work. Test any cleaner in an inconspicuous area first to prevent discoloration. For tougher stains, you might consider a carpet cleaner or a professional service if the stain persists beyond your patience window.

### Pet Care and Odors
Pets can be wonderful but they can also bring a certain risk of odors. The grey rug helps hide minor dirt and hair, but it does not magically become a deodorant. Regular cleaning, good ventilation, and a dedicated pet care routine will keep the rug fresh. If you notice any persistent odors, a routine cleaning cycle or a gentle deodorizing spray designed for fabrics may help. Always check the product label to ensure compatibility with synthetic fibers.

### Longevity Tips
Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear, especially in hallways or sunlit rooms where one side may fade at a different rate. Sunlight exposure can subtly lighten or darken the fibers over time; if your room has strong sun exposure, consider UV-protective curtains or blinds to prolong color fidelity. Keeping heavy furniture footpads can prevent indentations and crush marks from larger pieces that stay in place for long periods.
