## 1) Liliana of the Veil (MR)
Liliana of the Veil stands as one of the most iconic Planeswalkers ever printed. In the realm of casuals and judges alike, she’s a card that demands attention the moment she hits the battlefield. The ability to make opponents discard and to keep your own hand clean is a combination that tends to tilt the game toward Liliana’s user. The art whispering dark glamour, the card’s legendary status, and the value in NM condition make this a centerpiece for many collections. 

Art-wise, Liliana’s visage is pure Gothic chic—an emblem of dread and elegance that looks fantastic on a display shelf. Mechanically, she rewards you for controlling the table, punishing opponents who cling to their hands. For value-watchers, her rarity is Mythic, which often translates to hobbyist enthusiasm, long-term price stability, and a fun investment as a display piece.

## 2) Jace, the Mind Sculptor (MR)
The Mind Sculptor is the sort of card that makes casual observers nod and then whisper, That is a problem, I don’t want to play against that. Jace’s abilities cover draws, board presence, and a strategic mind game that turns every game into a chess match. Mint and NM copies keep their sheen and pop on the table under hot showroom lights. This is a quintessential centerpiece for any serious control deck and a gorgeous piece for collectors who love the clean lines of planeswalker design.

Flavor and function meet in a single seven-letter name—Jace. The artwork is timeless in its minimalism, and the card’s lasting impact on the game makes it a frequent choice for discussion in both casual and tournament circles.

External read: [Jace, the Mind Sculptor – MTG Wiki](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Jace,_the_Mind_Sculptor)

## 3) Time Walk (R)
Time Walk’s old-school aura is part nostalgia, part power spike. The idea of extra turns—especially with the hard-to-resist tempo swing—has kept this card in the “how did we survive without it?” conversations since its first printing. A Mint or NM copy sits gleaming on a shelf, catching the eye of newcomers and veterans who love the history of the game.

Let’s be real: Time Walk is a conversation piece that’s also a win condition when you need to push past a stubborn defenses. It’s not always the most practical in every modern deck, but in the right format and with the right setup, it’s a game-ender that feels like cheating in a goofy, glorious way.

External read: [Time Walk – MTG Wiki](https://mtg.fandom.com/wiki/Time_walk)

## 4) Ancestral Recall (R)
Ancestral Recall whispers of the golden age when draw spells were the crown jewels of every format. In NM condition this card’s art and printing glow with a classic vibe that many players adore. It’s not a typical staple in modern formats, but its historical significance and art-forward presentation make it a beloved collector’s piece.

This card is as much a piece of history as a practical tool in older formats. If you’re chasing the aura of early MTG, this is the NM display option that acts as a conversation starter and a reminder of how far the game has come.
