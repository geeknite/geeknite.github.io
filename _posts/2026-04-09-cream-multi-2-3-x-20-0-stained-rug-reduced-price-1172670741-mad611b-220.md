## Practicality: Cleaning, Care, and Everyday Use {#practicality}

Cleaning a stained rug may sound like a trap set by a mischievous HOA, but it can be a surprisingly chill experience if you approach it with the right plan. Here are practical tips to get the most out of your Cream Multi runner:
- Vacuum regularly to remove surface dirt before it embeds into the fibers. This helps keep the stains at bay and the cat from giving up on life.
- Attend to stains promptly. Blot, don’t rub, and treat with a mild detergent solution. Always test on a hidden area first, because a stain sometimes becomes a design feature when you least expect it.
- Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could damage the fibers or turn the cream into a pale beige crime scene.
- Consider a fabric guard or rug pad to extend life and improve tread comfort. A good rug pad also reduces slipping and makes mid-work moves feel like a dance routine rather than a slip-and-slide.

### Stain Tales: From Coffee to Red Wine to Mild Regret {#stains}
Stains on a light-toned rug are not disasters; they are stories you will (sometimes) tell at parties. The Cream Multi palette is forgiving enough to hide minor spills, but dramatic messes will require a measured approach. For coffee: blot with a clean white cloth, then treat with a mild detergent solution. For red wine: sprinkle a small amount of salt to absorb excess moisture before blotting, then use a stain-removing solution designed for synthetic fibers. The goal is to lift the stain without pushing it farther into the pile. Remember: patience is a virtue; impulse cleaning risks turning a simple spill into a permanent mural on your living room floor.
