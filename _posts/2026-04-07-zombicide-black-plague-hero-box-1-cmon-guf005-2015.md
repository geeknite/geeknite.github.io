Painting Tips and Quick Customization Ideas
If you’re planning to paint these minis, you’ll want to approach them with a simple but effective three-step plan: base coat, wash, and highlight. Start with a mid-tone base for armor and clothing, then apply a subtle sepia or brown wash to settle in the crevices and to give the model a sense of age and grit. For metallic parts, use a silver base and then a black wash to bring out engravings; add a touch of gold or brass on trim to highlight nobility without excessive shimmer. If you want to push the look further, consider edge highlighting along blade edges and a light dry-brush on fur or cloaks to create a sense of texture. For players who enjoy a heroic pop on the table, you can add non-metallic metal (NMM) highlights to shields and armor, but be warned: NMM is a bit of a paint-economy investment, requiring practice. If you’re a painting novice, just aim for clean contrasts, a crisp shield emblem, and good shading on weapons. Your table will thank you for it.

With that in mind, we recommend coordinating your color schemes with the rest of your Black Plague collection to maintain a cohesive visual style. If you’ve already invested in a blue-and-silver knight palette or a red-and-gold dragon emblem for another set, you’ll want to mirror that style across Hero Box 1 to keep your board looking like a living, breathing fantasy epic rather than a mismatched flea market of characters.
