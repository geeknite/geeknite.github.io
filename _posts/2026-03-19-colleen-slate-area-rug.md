## Cleaning and Maintenance

### Everyday care

Due to its low-pile design, the Colleen Slate Area Rug is pretty easy to vacuum. A standard vacuum with a beater brush can keep its surface neat without grabbing the fibers. Routine vacuuming every few days reduces the risk of dirt building up in the subtle veining and keeps the pattern crisp. If you have pets and you want to minimize odors, you could consider periodic spot cleaning with a mild enzymatic cleaner to break down organic residues. Always test on a small inconspicuous area first.

### Stain removal

Most common stains respond well to a quick blot and mild detergent solution. For tougher stains, a mixture of water and white vinegar can be used sparingly; avoid bleach-based cleaners as they may alter the tone of the slate pattern. The key is to act quickly, because procrastination is not a superpower you want to cultivate in your cleaning routine. Remember that the rug is not a superhero; it simply does not respond well to being submerged in water for extended periods.

### Care for long-term look

Rotate the rug every few months if you want even wear. This is particularly helpful in high-traffic areas or near entrances where shoes may leave behind more grime. Consider using a rug pad to reduce movement and add a touch of padding for comfort. A rug pad also makes a difference in sound reduction if you have a home office or studio where calm is a commodity. The literature on rug pads is not as exciting as a sci-fi manual, but in practice, it makes life easier.
