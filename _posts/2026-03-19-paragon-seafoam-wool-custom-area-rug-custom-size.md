## Custom Size: What It Means in Real Life
### How the customization works
Custom size means you do not have to fit your life to a rug — instead, the rug can be fitted to your life. Paragon typically offers width and length options in measured increments, sometimes via a lead time that lets them weave or assemble the rug to spec. Expect a few weeks of processing for non-standard sizes, depending on production schedules. The benefit is evident: you can optimize the rug to the room’s exact dimensions, coordinate with furniture layout, and avoid the awkward gaps that plague many spaces. The downside is the potential longer wait time. If you need your space to feel grounded within a week, standard sizes might be the safer bet. If you’re willing to wait for bespoke comfort, the custom route pays off with a tailored fit.

### Edge finishing and shape options
Custom size often pairs with choices in edge finishing. You can expect clean, minimal edges that lie flat and resist curling when paired with a rug pad. If your room features unusual angles or a bay window, you can request irregular shapes or non-rectangular cuts in some cases. The practical guidance here: speak with customer service about excess length, corner radii, and edge reinforcement. The devil sits in the details — if your room has a lot of nooks, a precise edge finish can make all the difference to the rug’s perceived quality.

### Measuring and planning like a pro
A good measurement workflow saves a lot of post-purchase grief. Here is a nerdy but practical plan:
- Measure the room floor space you want covered with a tape measure, including any door clearance. Make a rough sketch on graph paper or in a note app, labeling wall-to-wall dimensions, door swings, and any built-in furniture that will contact the rug.
- Add approximately 2–3 inches of breathing room around furniture for a visually balanced look. If you have a dining table, ensure chairs can tuck in completely while the rug still sits under the chairs when pulled out.
- Decide on a pile height that balances foot feel with cleaning practicality. A medium pile near 1/2 to 3/4 inches typically works well in living areas, while high-traffic kitchens may fare better with a lower profile.
- Confirm the final size with the retailer, and check the return policy if the rug arrives and reads like a slightly different geometry than promised. It happens; the universe loves a good plot twist.

### Shipping, returns, and handling
Custom goods often come with longer lead times. You will likely receive a shipping window rather than a guaranteed date. Inspect the rug upon arrival for any visible defects, verify the color is what you ordered, and lay it out to flatten if it arrived curled. Some retailers provide a return window on non-custom pieces; for true custom sizes, this window can be shorter or non-existent. If something looks off, contact customer service promptly; this is where a cooperative retailer turns a potential disappointment into a small victory dance.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Cleaning basics
Wool is naturally resilient, which translates into a rug that can handle routine vacuuming and occasional professional cleaning. For everyday care, vacuum with a nozzle attachment, avoiding a high suction that might pull the fibers. Spills should be blotted promptly to minimize sinking liquid into the pile. For more stubborn stains, test a gentle wool-safe cleaner in an inconspicuous area first. Avoid harsh chemicals. Wool fibers love gentle treatment and respond well to a mild cleaning routine.

### Rotation and fluffing
Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially in high-traffic areas. If the pile begins to look pressed in one direction, a gentle steam or a light brushing with a soft brush can help to re-fluff the surface. Let the rug air out after cleaning to prevent any damp odor from lingering in the wool. A well-ventilated room is your friend here, not your enemy.

### Dust concerns and allergies
Wool naturally resists some common allergens, but not all. If you or a household member has sensitivities, plan a more frequent cleaning schedule and consider a HEPA-filter vacuum to minimize dust. For small kids and furry friends, consider a washable area rug underlay that adds a layer of protection to the pile while keeping the surface comfortable for little feet.
