## Overclocking, tweaking, and staying safe in BIOS land

Overclocking DDR5 is a mixed bag of potential gains and risk management. The Crucial Ballistix 64GB kit supports enabling the platform’s memory profile to reach its advertised 4800 MT/s. If you’re curious about stepping beyond, you’ll likely venture into manual timings and voltage control. Here are some practical notes:

- Start with the manufacturer profile (EXPO/XMP). It’s the safe, guaranteed baseline that ensures stability. 
- If you want more headroom, you can experiment with DRAM voltage increments, but do so in small steps (e.g., 0.02V increments) and monitor stability with a stress test. 
- Test stability with a reputable memory test suite and real-world tasks. A single run of a stress test may not reveal all issues; you want to ensure your system is rock-solid under prolonged loads.
- Keep an eye on temperatures. DDR5 memory does generate heat, and while the heat spreader helps, you don’t want thermal throttling or thermal throttling-susceptible behavior during long gaming or encoding sessions.

In practice, many users will find the stock XMP/EXPO settings to be perfectly adequate for daily use. If your system is already memory-latency sensitive or your motherboard’s memory controller is on the conservative side, you may appreciate the stability and headroom that this kit provides without delving too deep into the BIOS rabbit hole.
