## Foil quality and card stock: shiny but sturdy?

Foil cards look great on a display shelf and can look even better in a black-light mood lighting scenario (for your inner Dragon Ball Z stage performance). The key questions you should ask yourself about foil quality in a larger lot are:

- Do the foils show fading or haze along the edges with repeated handling? A good foil will withstand casual flipping, but you’ll notice tape and humidity can cause odd hazing.
- Are the foils uniformly bright, or do some cards have a ding in the foil layer that makes the print look dull? In a 57-card batch, you’ll likely have a couple of outliers; that’s normal.
- How’s the card stock under the foil? Some foils ride on standard card stock, while others feel slightly stiffer or thinner due to foil overlay. It affects shuffle feel and long-term durability.

From our experience here, the overall finish is solid. The foil surfaces pop, and the color saturation on many character cards remains punchy. If you plan to sleeve and store these properly, you’ll be surprised at how good many of the prints look even after months of use.

## Handling, storage, and preservation: keeping the glow alive

Foil cards deserve better than being tossed into a dusty shoebox. Here’s how to preserve your 57-card constellation of shine:

- Sleeves: Use soft inner sleeves and standard standard outer sleeves once you’ve sorted and identified header cards. If you’re worried about scratch marks, consider square-cut sleeves and a basic top loader for display cards.
- Binders or deck boxes: A dedicated binder with card dividers or a slim, stackable deck box helps prevent warping and minimizes handling damage. If you’re fancy, a UV-protective binder can guard against sun-damage-induced fading.
- Humidity and temperature: Keep foils away from extreme heat and moisture. Foil layers can react to humidity over time, creating hazy patches on the surface. Basic room humidity control and a dry environment will keep the gleam intact.
- Sorting strategy: Create a simple taxonomy for the foil pool: by set, by rarity, by potential play viability, or by color. The more you sort early, the easier it is to locate your favorites during casual play.

If you treat these cards with care, the featherweight shine will stay brilliant enough to inspire a few “ooh!” moments at your next game night.
