## Overview

Fortune Row is not a traditional campaign supplement filled with stat blocks and encounter tables. It is, instead, a curated illustrated atlas of a district within City of Mist where luck shifts like a streetlamp in a windstorm. The art pairs with short captions, concept notes, and mood-driven prompts that GM and players can mine for inspiration. In a game where masks and myths blur with the everyday, an art book that foregrounds imagery can become a practical tool for session planning, mood setting, and character design.

The book is structured to be browsed, riffed on, and then used as a jumping-off point for an actual game night. It is not trying to replace the core rulebook or the modular supplements; it is trying to heighten the atmosphere around Fortune Row, a district where deals are made in whispered pacts, favors travel with a favorable wind, and the past leaves its fingerprints on every storefront.

## Art Direction and Visual Language

### Visual Language

Fortune Row leans into a neon-noir palette with bold, saturated colors and high-contrast lighting. The images feel like film stills from a crime drama shot at the golden hour, with rain-slick streets reflecting color and form. You will see characters whose silhouettes carry more story than their dialogue ever could, and you will sense the pulse of a city that conspires to be remembered. The linework oscillates between crisp ink and painterly washes, a choice that mirrors the tension in City of Mist between the masks people wear and the myths they become.

### The Artists

A rotating lineup of illustrators contributes to a tapestry rather than a single voice. The result is a mosaic where each spread introduces a slightly different texture—the result is deliberate, not discordant. You might flip from a leaf-thin line art page to a chromatic, almost watercolor panel, then land on a design sheet that feels like a blueprint drawn by a magician. The variety matters because it mirrors the core premise of City of Mist: every hero has many faces, and every face belongs to a different story.

### Characters in Focus

Fortune Row curates a gallery of masks that feel personal rather than mythical. You will meet the street-level versions of legends, the people who flirt with fate and are either rewarded or haunted by it. The character designs privilege silhouette and gesture, which is exactly what a GM needs when they want a quick visual read of a potential ally or antagonist. The book understands that a good character image can unlock a session’s tone far faster than a monologue about backstory ever could.

Fortune Row is presented as a map-like arrangement of micro-locations—each corner of the district has a mood, a myth, and a small set of visual cues. The art here gives you a sense of the social geography of a place where luck circulates like a currency and where the masks people wear are visible to the watchful eye of the city itself.

## How to Use Fortune Row in Your Campaigns

Here are a few practical ideas for leveraging the art book during actual play:

- Mood boards for sessions: Use a full spread as a backdrop for a night of roleplay. The images become speaking points to describe environments that players can react to.
- Seeded prompts for scenes: The captions can spark a scene or a confrontation idea. Present the image and ask players what myth or mask would be most likely to appear in that situation.
- NPC design renders: Let a character’s look drive their backstory. Show an image to players and reveal a few lines about the NPCs, their loyalties, and why they wear the mask they do.
- Session anchors: If a session drifts, return to a Fortune Row image as a reset note. Reintroduce the mood in a way that reminds players what the city is asking of them.

### A Note on Balance
If you are a GM who uses Fortune Row as a player-facing mood enhancer, keep a balance between image-driven inspiration and your NPCs’ actual goals. The art should fuel the moment, not replace the logic of the plot. The best uses pair a striking image with a short, concrete goal for the party that ties into the citywide themes of luck, myth, and consequence.
