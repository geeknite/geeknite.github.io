A note on assembly difficulty: the kit is not something you want to hand to a friend who claims to be a 'one-screw-turn' hobbyist. There are several connecting points and a couple of pieces that require careful alignment. But if you take your time, you’ll end up with a field of minis that look good on the table and stay on the board as you move across zones. If you’re a veteran painter, you’ll likely enjoy a straightforward clean-up and shading session; if you’re new to the hobby, you’ll still produce respectable results with a measured approach. The trick is to get a sense for which pieces are multi-part and which are stable in a single piece. The rule of thumbs: plan your assembly in small batches, dry-fit before glue, and clamp while curing so you don’t end up with weird limb angles.
