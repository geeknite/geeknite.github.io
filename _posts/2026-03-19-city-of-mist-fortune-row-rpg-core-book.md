City of Mist Fortune Row core book review: a neon noir stroll through a newly minted block, where every alley whispers a legend and every coffee cup might contain a miracle. If you thought City of Mist could not possibly get darker, slicker, or more tongue in cheek, Fortune Row slides into your play space with a grin, a badge, and a parable about luck that refuses to be boring. This is not just a setting supplement; it is a full on narrative toolbox designed to let GMs and players tango in a city that feels both recognizably real and gloriously improbable. Yes, prepare for the heart to race and the ego to smile in equal measure.

## Overview

Fortune Row is pitched as a complete add on to the City of Mist line, offering a new city district with its own myths, power hubs, notable NPCs, encounters, and a set of story seeds that scream use me right now. Think of it as a brand new playground with a fresh coat of neon and a soundtrack that never quite hits the same chorus twice. What makes Fortune Row stand out is not just the new place to explore, but how it reframes the core questions of the City of Mist system. It leans into the idea that myths are not just leadership figures and capes, but cultural currents, social echoes, and the stubborn stubbornness of human nature when confronted with a good mystery and a bad cup of joe.

Fortune Row is designed to be accessible to both newcomers and veterans. The book retains the core City of Mist ethos: players embody characters that are a mix of myth and street, your traditions, and your flaws. The Fortune Row block adds a distinct flavor to the myths that haunt the city and the ways those myths twist ordinary events into something you might call destiny if destiny wore sunglasses and sold you a latte with a price tag. The writing maintains the witty, slightly sardonic voice fans expect, with enough bite to keep the tone clear without ever turning it into a sour joke at the expense of the players.

## What Fortune Row Adds

Fortune Row brings several essential additions to the table:

- A fully fleshed out district with neighborhoods, landmarks, and a few signature sites that practically beg for investigation scenes. The district has its own rhythms, its own local legends, and its own set of micro-mysteries that can interlock with the citywide mysteries in interesting ways.
- A new set of character options that fit the Fortune Row vibe. Think legends reinterpreted as neighborhoods within a single soul, and myths that are more about social leverage, networks, and the power of collective belief than about swords and sorcery.
- A toolkit for running multi-session arcs that feel like a serialized noir with escalating stakes. The book gives you roadmaps for long games while still being friendly to one or two session one shots.
- A collection of ready to drop scenarios, case files, and scene seeds that make it easy to start right away. If you like to drop players into a tense moment and watch the mythic threads unravel, Fortune Row is built for that mood.
- GM guidance that leans into theme over crunch. The vibe is that the GM is guiding a gang of mythic misfits through a city that refuses to decide who its heroes are, letting the players decide with actions, not just dice.

The district is not a generic sandbox. It has identity. It has consequences. It has a rhythm that can be felt in the narrative beats of the scenes you run. That rhythm makes every session feel intentional, even when you are improvising a chase through neon rain or a rooftop conversation with a myth that has learned to wear conveyor belts as accessories.

## Mechanics and System Feel

City of Mist operates with a distinct approach to dice, tags, and storytelling. Fortune Row does not overhaul the system; it refines it and gives it a new playground. If you have played through the core book, you will find many familiar mechanics: the use of tags to define what a character can do, the focus on narrative outcomes over strict numerical power, and the GM's hand in shaping the mystery as it unfolds. Fortune Row leans into these existing elements while injecting a few fresh motifs that align with its theme.

- Tags get a new set aligned with Fortune Row themes. These tags influence how PCs interact with the district, how NPCs respond to them, and what the city itself seems to fight for or against.
- Scene pacing is emphasized. The book includes a recommended sequence of beat points that help you structure a mystery arc so it feels cinematic rather than episodic.
- Investigative play remains central, but there is more emphasis on social maneuvering and the networks of influence within Fortune Row. The myths are not just about brute force or flashy power; they thrive on whispers, leverage, and the way information travels through the district.
- The book provides a collection of micro-systems for unique scenes, such as a rumor mill where the players can manipulate public perception, or a market district scene where a single transaction can destabilize a local myth in a surprisingly meaningful way.

If you like the City of Mist approach but wish for a stronger social engine and a district with a defined emotional weather, Fortune Row should be a delight. The tone remains cheeky and confident, with plenty of space for heroes who are more puzzle than punchline, and more myth-teller than superhero.

## Setting and Tone

Fortune Row captures a different slice of City of Mist city life: the traffic of legends, the undercurrent of power that runs through the downtown blocks, and the quiet, inexorable pull of a city that never stops promising reputation and wreckage in equal measure. The district feels lived-in, with landmarks you can actually picture and a set of recurring motifs that give play sessions a throughline beyond the next mystery.

The tone is noir without being grim for the sake of grim. It leans into humor as a survival tool, not as a punchline. You will get moments of exaggerated swagger, but you will also see the human cost of myth through the eyes of characters who are trying to balance their own legacies with the demands of a city that refuses to stop teaching them hard lessons.

If you have previously struggled to keep a City of Mist game feeling cohesive as a long arc, Fortune Row has a toolkit to help. The suggested arc structures are built to allow you to escalate tension across sessions while preserving the emotional stakes that the players care about. The result is a vibe that is both intimate and big—private tragedies that echo across a district with city-wide consequences.

## Content Breakdown (What You Will Find)

Fortune Row is organized to support both GMs planning in depth and players who love diving into lore. Here is a snapshot of what you can expect in the main sections:

- Introduction and Vision: An opening that sets the mood and explains how Fortune Row should feel in play. The text leans into myth as social phenomenon and the role of rumor in shaping reality.
- District Anatomy: A thorough tour of Fortune Row including neighborhoods, key locations, and the myths tied to each site. Each location includes hooks, potential antagonists, and an example scene tailored to fit the district mood.
- Mythic Cast and Power Network: New options for characters and the myths that inhabit the block. This section helps you map a hero or criminal with a myth to the actual social network that defines Fortune Row.
- Case Files and Scene Seeds: Ready-to-run scenarios that demonstrate how to frame mystery across different play styles. These seeds include objectives, complications, and a path toward the reveal.
- GM Tools and Play Aids: A set of tables and quick tools to nudge scenes toward the theme of Fortune Row. Expect ready-made prompts for social maneuvers, mythic boons, and local consequences that resonate within the district.
- Artwork and Layout: Aesthetic choices that reinforce the neon noir mood. The visuals are darkly stylish without sacrificing readability, and the layout supports the GM who needs to flip between pages quickly during a session.
- Accessibility and Inclusivity: The book includes mindful content notes and character options designed to reflect a diverse city and a variety of player identities.

The content is dense but not opaque. It rewards careful reading and routine re-reads, especially if you want to align your pacing with Fortune Row's unique rhythm. The authors clearly want the GM to feel equipped to run a dramatic arc with minimal prep on the table, while still letting the players contribution steer the narrative.

## NPCs and Factions

Fortune Row features a cast of memorable NPCs who feel alive in a way that only a well crafted district can support. These characters are not mere foils; they are social nodes through which the city communicates, negotiates, and sometimes colludes. You will meet informants who trade in secrets, rulers who justify their power with myths, and ordinary people who keep the city moving with stubborn hope or desperate bravado.

Factions within Fortune Row operate on a spectrum. Some groups try to curate the myths that circulate in the district, while others attempt to bend fate to their own advantage. The book provides hooks for both kinds of factions and offers the GM a clear sense of how actions in one area ripple through the rest of the block. Expect political undercurrents, cultural tension, and the occasional supernatural event that seems to be less about a miracle and more about a long game being played out on the city’s stage.

## Scenes, Encounters, and Play Seeds

If you are the kind of GM who loves ready to run material, Fortune Row shines in its scene seeds. Each seed is designed to drop players into a moment that feels important, with clear links to the district’s myths and a path toward discovery. The seeds are not one trick ponies; they are adaptable frameworks that you can expand as your players engage with the city. The build options allow you to customize the seed to your group, whether you prefer drama, mystery, or social intrigue.

For players who enjoy social acumen and mythic leverage, these seeds offer excellent opportunities to experiment with how status, reputation, and rumor can become real power. The strongest seeds emphasize the interplay between personal story and city scale, illustrating that the fate of Fortune Row can be shaped by small, well chosen actions as much as by flamboyant feats of legendary prowess.

## GM Guidance and Play Style

Fortune Row leans into the GM as storyteller and orchestrator. It offers practical advice that goes beyond general encouragement. You will find tips on pacing, when to reveal a clue, how to manage multiple threads, and how to handle the emotional arc of a myth that is also a neighbor, landlord, activist, or rival barista. The guidance helps you maintain a consistent tone: noir, hopeful, a little dangerous, and always a touch irreverent.

One notable aspect is the emphasis on the city as a living organism. The district breathes, and its moods shift with time and social events. The book suggests ways to use the city calendar, local festivals, and recurring incidents to create a sense of ongoing life. This is a substantial improvement if your group likes campaigns with a sense of continuity rather than isolated stories. Fortune Row knows that the city is a character in its own right and writes accordingly.

The book is a pleasure to flip through. The art direction captures the neon glow of a metropolis where myths clash with everyday life. For groups that enjoy heavy roleplay, the district and NPCs provide ample opportunities to explore identity, culture, and the consequences of living with a myth built into your day-to-day life.

## How It Compares to the Core Book

If you already own the City of Mist core book, Fortune Row is best treated as a major expansion rather than a side quest. It offers a new canvas with its own mood, but its mechanics remain compatible with the core system, making it a natural next step for groups who want to push the storytelling envelope without learning a new rule set from scratch.

Fortune Row emphasizes social dynamics and mythic leverage more than brute force or high-stakes capes. The emphasis on the power of rumor, reputation, and social capital gives the system a fresh feel and expands the kinds of conflicts you can stage. It is not a replacement for the core experience; it is a refinement and an expansion that deepens the world and invites you to inhabit it more fully.

## How to Run a Fortune Row Campaign (GM Tips)

- Start with a district mood map: think about the weather in Fortune Row. Is it rainy and oppressive, or bright and charged with speculative energy? Give players a central problem tied to a myth and a local stake that keeps the city honest about what it means to live there.
- Build a social ladder: the myths in Fortune Row have influence networks. Map out who benefits from the myths and who pays the price when the myths shift in response to events in the district.
- Use flavor scenes: run micro scenes that do not require heavy crunch but give players moments to shape the myth in real time. A single conversation in a corner cafe can reveal a key truth about a location and its myth.
- Tie in outside threads: Legend and myth do not exist in a vacuum. Cross pollinate Fortune Row with other districts or with events that affect the entire city. This creates important cause and effect that the players can feel in both direction and consequence.
- Prepare a flexible climate: Fortune Row benefits from a dynamic mood. Have a few alternate beats ready in case players take the investigation in unexpected directions. A little improvisation goes a long way here.

## Sample Scene Seeds

- The Midnight Market of Whispers: the district hosts a night market where rumors are traded as currency. The players must intercept a rumor before it reaches the wrong person, balancing the risk of exposure with the potential payoff of a mythic opportunity.
- The Mayor's Shadow: a political figure who has built a myth on the promise of safety and prosperity. The players must uncover a hidden tie between the myth and a local conspiracy, all while navigating public opinion and media pressure.
- A Feast of Reflections: a festival that causes people to see their true myths in reflections, exposing secrets and hidden desires. The players must decide what truths are worth sharing with a city hungry for stories.

## Final Thoughts and Recommendation

Fortune Row succeeds as both a setting expansion and a toolkit that emphasizes mythic social power. It is not about turning City of Mist into a different game; it is about giving you a district with a distinct personality and a set of mechanisms that encourage you to tell stories where the city itself is a player. If you enjoy the noir flavor, the idea that myths come with social gravity, and the fun of running a campaign that blends investigation with character-driven drama, then Fortune Row will feel like a bright, noisy addition to your table.

The book balances accessibility with depth. It offers enough guidance to start a campaign quickly but also contains rich materials that reward careful, thoughtful play. The texture of Fortune Row is what makes it stand out: a block that breathes, a network of relationships that feels organic, and a set of scenes that are precisely the kind of micro-premises that spark big, mythic choices.

If you are weighing a purchase, consider your group speed and interest in social storytelling. Fortune Row is an investment in the atmosphere and the long-form arc potential of a City of Mist campaign. If your players crave a neon-drenched world where the limits of reality are negotiable and the currency of myth is reputation, then Fortune Row is likely to become a favorite corner of your campaign world.

In sum: Fortune Row is a thoughtful, entertaining, and robust addition that expands what your group can do with City of Mist. It respects the core system while giving it a new heartbeat that is both fresh and familiar. The result is a district you will want to return to again and again, a place where every session can feel like a step deeper into the myth that lives in the city streets.

If you loved the core book, you will fall for Fortune Row. If you are new to City of Mist, Fortune Row offers a contained, atmospheric entry point that still leaves plenty of room to grow your own myths and legends in the neon glow of a city that never sleeps.
