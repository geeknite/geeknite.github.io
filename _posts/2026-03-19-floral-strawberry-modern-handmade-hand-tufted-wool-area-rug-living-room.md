## Materials, Care, and Long Term Durability
Wool is a performance fiber. It has natural crush resistance, resilience, and a degree of moisture management that synthetic fibers often aspire to imitate. Hand tufted wool rugs are not just decorative; they can be functional floor coverings that stand up to the daily grind of life. A few practical notes:
- Vacuum with a low setting and preferably in the direction of the pile. Avoid aggressive agitation that can cause fringe wear and fabric fatigue.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear patterns if your space has inconsistent foot traffic across the surface.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Wool fibers will absorb liquids, but quick action reduces staining potential. For stubborn stains, treat with a mild wool-safe cleaner according to the label.
- Avoid direct sun for long periods to minimize color fading. If sun exposure is unavoidable, rotate or angle the rug to protect the brightest areas.

Caring for a wool rug should feel like caring for a living organism that occasionally drops meteor-sized dust bunnies. It wants your attention in small doses and rewards you with years of plush comfort if treated with a light touch and sensible maintenance routine.
