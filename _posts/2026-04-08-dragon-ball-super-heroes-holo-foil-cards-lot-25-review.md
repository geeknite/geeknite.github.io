## Practical Value: Playability, Display, and Long-Term Worth
### Use in a DBSCG Tournament or Home Rules Set
If you’re a player, you’ll want to assess whether the foil cards enhance your deck in a way that matters beyond aesthetics. Foils generally don’t change the card’s mechanics, but they do change visibility on a crowded table. In a tournament setting, a foil that catches the eye can serve as a psychological distraction—either by making your opponent think you pulled the “rare” version or by giving them a mini heart attack from the blinding light of your display. In casual play, the foil can act as a “prize” card or a visual anchor when explaining to new players why you think the art direction matters.

### Display and Preservation Tips
- Sleeves first, then top-loaders: this reduces friction when flipping through the stack while you’re fangirling over each artwork.
- Store in a cool, dry place away from sunlight: foils love to glow but hate to fade, and you don’t want a rainbow Safari on your shelf turning into a dull grayscale safari.
- Consider a dedicated binder page or a small display stand: the foil’s reflective quality looks best when given a clean, uncluttered backdrop.
- Photograph with diffuse lighting: a small LED panel at a 45-degree angle will minimize direct glare and show off the holo shimmer without washing out the artwork.
