## Practicalities: Size, Fit, and Placement
### Sizing and Shape
Koeckritz’s economy line typically offers standard rectangular options that fit most booths and patios. When you’re choosing size, aim to cover enough floor area to define your space without overpowering it. A good rule of thumb is to extend the rug a bit beyond your furniture footprint, creating a natural, inviting flow. In tight corners, you might opt for a smaller size that lets chairs slide in and out without catching the edge.

### Edge Issues and Fray Checks
Low-pile rugs can occasionally fray at the edges if left to fray over time or if dragged across rough surfaces. A quick routine: check edges after heavy foot traffic or chair movement, trim small strands if necessary, and consider a light rug grip underlay to keep edges neat. The goal is to maintain a clean, professional footprint rather than a “rug with a fringe of chaos.”

### Care Routine for Longevity
- Regular vacuuming to remove dust and debris
- Immediate spot cleaning for spills with a mild detergent solution
- Avoid high-heat drying; air-dry to prevent fiber damage
- For outdoor use, remove excess moisture to prevent mildew buildup

We also recommend checking the UV exposure of your space. If your setup bakes in direct sun for hours, rotate the rug periodically to mitigate uneven fading and color shift. This keeps your entire event space looking balanced across multi-day campaigns.

## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Troubleshooting
### Everyday Cleaning
A quick vacuum or a gentle shake is usually enough for daily upkeep. For stains, blot—not rub—with a clean cloth, then treat with a mild soap solution. Rinse lightly if needed and allow to dry completely. The goal is quick recoveries between sessions, not producing a movie montage of stain removal.

### Deep Cleaning Without Losing Your Mind
If you’re preparing a show that’s going to involve heavy usage and potential mess, you can do a deeper clean. Use a mild, non-abrasive cleaner and a soft-bristle brush. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the rug fibers. Rinse with clean water and ensure thorough drying to prevent moisture retention.

### Outdoor-Specific Concerns
Outdoor use raises concerns about mold and mildew in damp environments. After rainy periods or high humidity, flip the rug over to increase air circulation and promote faster drying. If you spot mildew, treat with a diluted vinegar solution or an approved outdoor-safe cleaner, then rinse and dry in a sunny spot. Prevention beats remediation here: keep the rug elevated slightly from the ground during extended outdoor use if possible.

### Quick Fixes from the Geek Desk
- Curl prevention: lay items on corners for a day or two to flatten. If needed, a light weight can help persuade stubborn corners to settle down.
- Stain emergencies: act fast. The longer a stain sits, the more stubborn it becomes. A timely cleanup saves you a lot of scrubbing later.
- Storage tip: roll the rug rather than folding to minimize creases and to protect the fibers when transporting between events.
