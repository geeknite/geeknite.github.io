## Materials, build quality, and why you might not need a separate insurance policy for it
The Sorbet rug uses a dense pile with a hand-tufted appearance. The surface is soft but resilient, which means it sucks you in with comfort and then fights back against the indignity of stair-step footprints. The fibers are designed to be stain resistant enough to survive coffee spills, wine incidents, and the occasional tomato sauce mishap—though not a disaster-level culinary accident, which would require a good cleaning routine anyway.

Care is straightforward. A regular vacuum on a gentle setting keeps the pile looking crisp, while quick blotting with a mild detergent solution is recommended for accidental spills. Always test in an inconspicuous corner first; your carpet’s self-esteem will thank you. For deep cleaning, you can follow standard rug care guidelines from reputable sources, but keep the process simple and avoid aggressive cleaners that could dull the vintage look you are chasing.

Durability-wise, the Sorbet has held up well in households with pets and kids in our tests. We observed minimal pilling after a few months of stroller-wheeled traffic and energetic play sessions. If you are counting years rather than months, a rug pad can extend its life by reducing friction and preventing slippage—a small investment for long-term cosiness.

## Maintenance musings: keeping the vintage vibe without turning the room into a laundromat
Cleaning a vintage-inspired rug should feel like a spa day for your interior space, not a medieval ritual. Vacuum regularly to avoid dirt grinding into the fibers. Treat spills promptly—blot, do not rub, and use a mild solution if needed. For stubborn stains, a professional cleaning is the safest route rather than aggressive scrubbing that could ruin the pattern or color balance.

A common question is whether the Sorbet rug is kid-friendly. The answer is yes with caveats. It can handle daily use, but you should address spills quickly and avoid letting markers, inks, or strong dyes sit on the surface for too long. For households with enthusiastic little artists, consider rug protection products or a washable slipcover for furniture that frequently drags crayons across the floor.
