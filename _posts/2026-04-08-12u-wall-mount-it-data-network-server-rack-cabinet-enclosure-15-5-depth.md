### Practical considerations

- Depth: 15.5 inches means you must check your devices’ depth with connectors and cabling attached. A 15.5 inch depth is not a guarantee of fit for every device’s protrusion. Always measure the equipment from the front to the back including any power connectors or cables that extend beyond the chassis.
- Airflow: If you have a small NAS or a couple of 1U devices, the front-to-back airflow is usually enough, provided you maintain space behind the rack for cables to breathe. If you fill the rear area with dense cabling, you risk choking airflow.
- Accessibility: Access to the front is straightforward; access to the back is more challenging if the rack is tight to the wall. Ensure you can reach the rear connections without tilting or jostling the equipment.

## Installation tips and real-world usage

A wall-mount rack is a little like a bookshelf for servers: you want it to look neat, be easy to reach, and not threaten to topple when you sneeze. Here are some practical steps and a few ideas to help you avoid the common pitfalls:

1) Location and anchors: Select a location with good proximity to network drops and power outlets. Use studs and heavy-duty anchors to secure the rack to the wall. The last thing you want is a display of cables cascading onto the floor.

2) Assembly order: Start with mounting the rails inside the rack, then install the devices from the bottom up. Lighter devices at the top and heavier devices toward the bottom are a good general rule. This helps with stability and reduces the risk of accident.

3) Device alignment: Align devices with the rails and ensure they are screwed into place to avoid movement. Use cage nuts to fix the 19 inch devices in position.

4) Cable routing: Use cable management channels and ties to route power and data cables. Group power cables together and run data cables separately from power where possible to minimize interference. Label cables so you know what’s what when you add the next device.

5) Dust control and cleaning: Wipe the interior to remove dust before you install devices. A clean environment improves airflow.

6) Testing: After installation, power on the devices one by one. Confirm connectivity and monitor heat.

7) Safety: Use protective gear if needed and avoid overloading the wall anchors.
