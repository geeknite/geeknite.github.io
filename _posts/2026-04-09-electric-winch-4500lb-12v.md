## Setup and installation notes
If you are comfortable with basic automotive hardware, installing this winch on an ATV or UTV is well within reach. Here are the essential steps, plus a few tips to keep you sane on a Saturday afternoon:

### Prep and planning
- Confirm bumper compatibility: Not all aftermarket bumpers are created equal. Check your bumper’s mounting pattern and weight tolerance to ensure it supports a 4500 lb winch. A sturdy plate and reinforced mounting bolts are worth their weight in comic relief during a muddy install.
- Gather wire and fusing: A dedicated inline fuse near the battery is non-negotiable. The kit includes basic hardware, but the fuse rating and cable gauge should align with the winch current draw. We recommend a fuse rated for at least 450A peak on a properly fused circuit.
- Battery health: A healthy 12V battery is a must. If your battery shows signs of weakness, upgrade to a higher capacity unit to protect the winch from voltage drop during heavy pulls.

### Mounting and wiring
- Position the winch where you can access it cleanly from the driver side and where the rope can lay cleanly on the drum without kinking.
- Attach the mounting plate to the bumper with the supplied bolts. Use thread locker on the bolts to prevent vibration from backing out in rough terrain.
- Run the power and switch wiring with careful routing. Keep wires away from hot exhaust routes and sharp edges. Attach the inline fuse near the battery, and connect the remote control plug in a weather sealed port.
- Test the winch by powering it up in a safe, unloaded condition. Listen for abnormal sounds and verify the rope wound evenly on the drum.

### Safe and simple operation
Always wear gloves when handling synthetic rope. Even though it’s safer than steel cable, the rope can still cause rope burn or pinching if spooled improperly. Keep a clear work zone up to a few feet around the drum during operation, and never stand in line with the rope when under load.

## Maintenance and safety reminders
A well maintained winch will outlast several seasons of off road mischief. Here are some practical maintenance tips to keep your winch singing rather than squealing:
- Keep it clean and dry: After each outing, wipe down the unit to remove mud and moisture. If you have a particularly muddy day, give it a light rinse and dry it thoroughly before storage.
- Inspect the rope regularly: Look for abrasion, fraying, or kinks. Synthetic rope benefits from a light coat of silicone-based rope lubricant to reduce friction, but don’t overspray and attract dirt.
- Check the mounting hardware: Periodically re-torque the mounting bolts and ensure the fairlead is in good condition. Vibration can loosen bolts over time, especially on rough trails.
- Test the safety features: Make sure the remote, the wired switch, and any emergency stops respond as expected. A quick test before a trip can prevent a day of frustration in the bush.
