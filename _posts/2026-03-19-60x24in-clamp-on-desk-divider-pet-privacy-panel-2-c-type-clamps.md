## Build quality and materials

### PET Privacy Panel material properties

The key talking point here is PET, which packs a nice combination of rigidity and light weight. PET panels resist warping under typical humidity without getting brittle in a cold air-conditioned office. The surface cleans easily, which is ideal for coffee leaks, ink smudges, and the occasional doodle that appears when conference calls drift off topic. Aesthetically, PET panels tend to have a subtle clarity that makes them feel less claustrophobic than frosted alternatives while still providing enough privacy to avoid staring contests with the screen side. If you are curious about long-term durability, the material should handle months of daily use without yellowing—subject to sunlight exposure and cleaning agents, as with most plastics.

### The two C-Type Clamps

C-Type clamps are the workhorse here. They clamp onto the desk edge and provide a secure hold without requiring permanent modifications. Two clamps offer stability and balanced pressure across the panel, which helps prevent wobble when you lean forward to compose that urgent email. If your desk edge is oddly rounded or features an unusual lip, you may need a small spacer or a tweak to the clamp alignment, but for standard desktops this setup is straightforward.

## Setup and installation

### Step-by-step install guide

1) Position the desk divider on your preferred side of the workspace where you want additional privacy. 2) Align the clamps with the desk edge, ensuring there is enough space for the panel to stand upright without interfering with chair movement. 3) Tighten the C-Type clamps evenly. 4) Check the panel for vertical alignment and adjust if needed. 5) Confirm there is no undue stress on the desk surface. 6) Wipe the surface to remove any fingerprints from handling during installation.

If you want to keep the setup neat, rotate the panel slightly toward you so your torso does not lean into the panel during long video calls. A little tilt can make the panel feel more integrated into your posture without sacrificing privacy.

### Troubleshooting tips

If the panel tilts or feels loose, re-tighten the C-Type clamps and check for desk edge irregularities. If the panel wobbles when you type, try repositioning the panel closer to the edge of the desk or adjust the clamp alignment to distribute pressure more evenly. If you have a very slick desk surface, consider a thin adhesive pad on the clamp contact point to increase grip. Remember, the goal is stable privacy without leaving marks on your beloved desk finish.
