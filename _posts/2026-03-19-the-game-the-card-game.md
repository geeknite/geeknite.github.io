## What is The Game - The Card Game?
The Game - The Card Game is a compact, cooperative card game that invites players to work as a team to discard all cards from their hands into two central piles. The catch is that the piles have to be built in ascending order, and the experience invites everyone to coordinate under the pressure of time, a handful of near-misses, and the occasional moment of surprising clarity when someone yells Not like that, try this! The original The Game was a lean product for a quick parliamentary debate on whether you can actually lose a card game by trying too hard. The Card Game edition expands the box time, adds new wrinkles, and remains delightfully bite-sized—perfect for game nights with folks who can make a spreadsheet cry with joy.

### Setup
Shuffle the deck and deal five cards to each player. Place the two piles in the center, and the draw pile nearby. The mood in the room ranges from cautious optimism to the dreaded uncertain glances that say we might fail but we can pretend it’s strategic risk management. The rules in this edition are designed to be friendly to newcomers while still offering bite for seasoned players who enjoy optimizing a tiny web of choices.

### Basic Rules
On your turn, you may discard one or two cards from your hand to either of the two piles, with the constraint that the cards must be placed in ascending order. You cannot place a larger number on a pile that already has a higher number; this is the game’s core mechanic and the source of glorious near misses. If you cannot legally play a card, you must draw from the draw pile until you can. The goal is to have all players discard their cards before the draw pile runs out or the group collapses into a chorus of sighing and friendly sarcasm.

Crucial nuance: conversation is allowed, even encouraged, but precise card calls are not. The game rewards you for discussing strategy without giving away exact cards, a design choice that makes the game accessible while still keeping tension high. The pace is variable: a good round can be a fast sprint, a long puzzle, or a dramatic standoff where everyone pretends to think aloud to buy a few precious seconds of calm.
