Safety and maintenance: the boring-but-important stuff

Let’s be honest: most people buy big gear because they want fewer headaches down the line, not more. This saw delivers a respectable safety proposition, but you still must respect it as a blade-kitty with teeth. Key points:

- Training matters: A quick safety briefing (gloves, eye protection, and a clear work area) will save you a world of regret when the blade is doing its best impression of a stealth bomber.
- Cleanliness is everything: After you’re done, disassemble, rinse, and sanitize. Frozen meat can harbor bacteria, and you don’t want to discover a sudsy mold colony creeping along your width and height calculator.
- Blade care: Keep the blade properly tensioned and clean. A dull or mis-tensioned blade wears the motor down faster and increases the risk of kickback—yes, that term is as dramatic as it sounds.
- Lubrication: A light lube on moving parts prevents corrosion and reduces friction noise. Not every shop needs a wind chime of squeaks when you slice through bone; you want a quiet, confident hum instead.

Cleaning, sanitation, and workflow tips you’ll actually use

- Pre-clean routine: Remove loose debris, then rinse to loosen blood and tissue residues. Sanitize and dry thoroughly before storage.
- After-work routine: Remove the blade (if your model permits), scrub the blade with a dedicated brush, and reassemble in a dry, ventilated space. Never leave a blade wet or damp as it invites rust and the Internet’s judgment.
- Workflow integration: Designate a specific station for bone processing with proper drainage and waste disposal. A good workflow reduces cross-contamination risk and speeds up your daily operations.
