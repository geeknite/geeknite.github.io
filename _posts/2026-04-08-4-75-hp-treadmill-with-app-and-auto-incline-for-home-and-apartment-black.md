For apartment living, the motor’s response is key: you want quick hill changes, not a 2‑second lag that makes you question whether you’re about to become a human escalate in a nature documentary.

The auto incline program is smart about how it escalates. It starts with a gentle rise, then uses a few preset scenarios (hill, mountain, sprint hill) that feel natural. If you’re new to incline workouts, the gradual ramps help you learn pacing without you losing your breath to the floor’s gravity well.

### Incline tips for the apartment gym

- Start with a light incline for 1–2 minutes to warm up quads. Then ramp up in 1% increments every 30–60 seconds.
- Pair incline with intervals: 2 minutes climb, 1 minute flat, repeat for 20–25 minutes. Your legs will thank you; your neighbors will hopefully forgive you.
- If you’re chasing fat burn, the incline works synergistically with higher speeds. Don’t stay glued to the 3–4 mph walking pace when you could be flirting with 6–7 mph on a gentle incline.
