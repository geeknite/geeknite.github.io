## Design and Craftsmanship

This is where the Fortune Row earns its keep. The dice are a resin blend with swirled colorways that evoke urban neon meets midnight alleyway—the kind of visuals you’d expect if you spilled a rainbow onto a wet street after a thunderstorm. The colorway I’m reviewing features a smoky midnight base with electric blue and emerald accents, but there are several palette options available or rumored to be in the wild if you’re hunting for a specific vibe, like red-amber for noir heat or violet-green for arcane vibes. The dice feel weighty without being rock-hard; they have a tactile presence that says, “I’m a piece of furniture, but I’m also a weapon in the fight for narrative momentum.”

The pips are crisp and deeply etched, which helps with readability in lower light—crucial for those late-night sessions when the neon is leaking through the blinds and the coffee is less of a beverage and more of a lifeline. The edges are smoothly beveled, reducing the risk of burrs catching on fingers during long rolling sequences. The resin’s transparency varies by colorway, with some dice showing more internal marbling than others; this is a feature, not a flaw, and it adds to the “artifact from a mythic city” vibe rather than detracting from crisp dice rolls.

One notable design feature is the “fortune glyph” on the d20: a tiny sigil etched into a corner face. It’s a neat homage to the Fortune Row concept, and it also helps you visually differentiate the random roll from the more predictable outcomes. If you’re someone who likes to express personality during play, this little touch doubles as flair that’s useful in streaming sessions or in-person games where you want your dice to say something about your character before you even announce the result.

Dice are not just math instruments; they’re tactile props for storytelling. The Fortune Row nails the feel in several ways:

- Weight distribution: The dice have a satisfying heft that suggests durability and a sense of gravitas. You’ll feel the dice when you roll, which adds to the ritual of a critical moment.

## Practicality: Playability and Maintenance

Performance is the real test. The Fortune Row stands up well to regular gaming sessions, but here are some practical notes:

- Sorting: The distinct colorways help you differentiate dice quickly. If you use a color-coded system at the table, you can assign dice to players or to character archetypes. A quick glance is enough to avoid accidental “mishandling” of a die that belongs to a different player.
- Cleaning: Resin dice can gather fingerprints and minor dust. A soft microfiber cloth is all you need. Avoid harsh cleaners; you want the dice’s finish intact for years of storytelling.
- Storage: The included display tray doubles as a rolling mat and a display platform. If you’re not moving your dice around, you can keep them on the display stand as part of your gaming space’s aesthetic.
- Longevity: The dice are robust enough for regular gaming, but like any resin piece with color swirls, you’ll want to avoid dropping them on rough surfaces that could chip the edges. A padded bag or soft tray will keep them pristine between campaigns.

Care tips: use a dice mat to reduce surface scuffs; store in the velvet pouch to minimize dust; and consider light lubrication for overly slick edges if your table is particularly sticky with gaming snacks (true adversity is a cinnamon-sugar spill, after all).

## Care and Longevity: Keeping Your Fortune Row Prime

- Storage: The display tray is helpful, but if you travel with the set, keep the velvet pouch and package components to shield the dice from bumps.
- Cleaning routine: Wipe with a soft microfiber cloth; avoid solvents; never use acetone on resin.
- Handling: Don’t throw dice—this isn’t a space battle. Gentle throws on a good table are best. A soft surface reduces risk of chipping and keeps the mosaic-like aesthetic intact.
- Display care: If you’re displaying the set, consider creative lighting to highlight the swirls. A small LED strip behind the shelf can bring the fortune glyph to life when it catches the light.
