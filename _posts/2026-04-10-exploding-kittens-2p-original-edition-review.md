## How to Play: A Quick Two-Player Rule Slice

### Setup and the First Turn

To begin, shuffle the deck and deal each player a starting hand, usually seven cards, depending on house rules. Place the Exploding Kitten(s) into the deck, mix well, and give the first player a glance at what kind of chaos lies ahead. The goal is simple: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten on your turn. If you draw one, you are out—unless you have a Defuse card that can save you, in which case you should quietly pretend you meant to do that all along.

### On Your Turn: Draw, Play, and Bluff

On your turn, you draw a card to replenish your hand and then can choose to play any number of action cards that suit your plan. Do you want to see the future and peek at what’s coming? Do you want to force your opponent to skip a turn, buying you precious seconds to plan? Do you want to launch a sneak attack and push the deck in a direction that forces the other player to make a dicey choice? The two-player mode makes those decisions feel personal, like a high-stakes game of cat-and-mouse where the mouse is very fond of cats and the cat is suspiciously fond of the mouse but also the cheese that the mouse is carrying.

### The Endgame: Who Laughs Last, Draws Last

The endgame arrives when one player either successfully defuses or avoids the exploding kitten, while the other is left to face the inevitable with a smug grin or a show of righteous despair. It is a spectacle of quick wits and quick hands. The tension is real, but the tone remains approachable, which is essential for a two-player game that you might pull out after a frustrating day when you want a quick laugh and an even quicker decision tree to solve your evening's boredom.
