## Comfort test: the real-life throne trial

### Cushion density and seating height

We measured seat height at approximately 18 inches from floor to cushion top, a standard sweet spot for many humans and large cats alike. The cushions aren’t too soft to collapse into a puddle nor so firm that you feel like you’re perched on a dental chair. The density provides that glorious balance between support and sink-in comfort. The 20-inch deep seat invites you to stretch your legs but doesn’t swallow them whole. For taller humans, this means leg support remains comfortable even after hours of gaming or marathon movie chases.

### The Sherpa warmth test

In a 60- to 65-degree living room, the fleece felt cozy rather than swamp-like. It’s chilly-to-malm, meaning if you are exactly one foot away from a heat vent, you will feel a gentle cocoon rather than a blast furnace. If you sit multiple people on the sofa, the fleece remains uniformly warm, so you don’t get that cold seam along the back where your shirt edges reveal the truth about your indoor thermostat.

### Cleaning and maintenance

Coffee spills, wine splashes, and Ghost Pepper chips are all potential guests when you host a gaming night. The Sherpa fleece can attract dust and hair, which is both a blessing and a curse depending on whether you own a cat or a very judgmental dog. The recommended approach is to vacuum regularly and treat stains promptly with a mild solution. The fabric responds to water-based cleaners, but you should always test on an inconspicuous area first. And when a full-on flood hits your living room, you’ll want to use a microfiber cloth and a gentle dryer technique to restore fluff.

### Durability: the test of time

We subjected the sofa to several weeks of real-world usage: gaming marathons, binge-watching sessions, a few awkward Sunday naps, and the occasional impromptu sofa dance party (don’t pretend you haven’t done that). The cushions maintained shape, the arms show minimal sag, and the fleece remained plush. It’s not indestructible, but it’s robust enough to survive your chaotic sofa experiments.

## Maintenance and longevity: care tips

- Vacuum regularly to remove dust and hair. A lint roller is your friend for quick cleanups.
- Spot-clean stains promptly with a mild detergent or soapy water; avoid harsh cleaners that may damage the fleece.
- Rotate cushions if the sofa allows; this helps with even wear and extended life.
- Use a slipcover in high-traffic zones if you’re worried about wear and tear.
- Consider professional cleaning for stubborn stains or when the fleece looks matted.
