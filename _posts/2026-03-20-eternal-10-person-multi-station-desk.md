Comfort, Ergonomics, and Health
Maintaining good ergonomics is crucial when you have 10 people working side-by-side. The depth and width of the desk provide enough space for comfortable postures across stations. The depth is generous enough that keyboard trays feel optional rather than mandatory. However, with ten people, you must build a social protocol to prevent elbows from becoming contact sports. We tested with a mix of chairs and found that the recommended 2 to 3-inch difference in chair heights across stations helps to reduce shoulder strain. The surface height adjustability is a useful feature for mixed teams that include shorter and taller users. For long sessions, a footrest and a monitor arm can significantly improve comfort and reduce neck strain. The design supports restful posture for extended editing sessions, code reviews, and long design critiques.

Keep the surface clean with a microfiber cloth and mild cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the laminate surface.
