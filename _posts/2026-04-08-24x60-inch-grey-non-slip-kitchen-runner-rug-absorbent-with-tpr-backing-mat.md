## Maintenance and care
A kitchen rug is only as good as its care routine. Here’s how to keep this runner looking crisp without turning your kitchen into a dry-cleaning nightmare.

- Cleaning: Most users report that the rug is machine-washable on a gentle cycle. We recommend using cold water and a mild detergent. Avoid chlorine bleach unless you’re going for a spa-like whiteness that never existed in your kitchen.
- Drying: Line dry or lay flat to air-dry. Avoid high heat in a dryer to prevent any shrinkage or warping of the backing. If you’re pressed for time, a gentle tumble on low heat is typically fine, but air-drying preserves the backing integrity.
- Maintenance: Shake out crumbs regularly; a quick vacuum helps keep the fibers looking fresh. Hair from pets or long-haired humans can build up; a quick lint roller pass keeps it neat.
- Stain removal: Blot fresh stains with a clean cloth. For stubborn stains, a gentle spot cleaner appropriate for microfiber surfaces usually does the trick. Avoid soaking the backing material; you want moisture to stay on the surface and evaporate rather than saturate the backing.

### Backing care and longevity
The TPR backing is rugged but not invincible. Over time, the backing can show wear in high-traffic zones, especially if you frequently move the rug or drag furniture over it. If you notice the backing peeling or curling at the edges, you may need to trim edges or re-anchor with double-sided rug tape. That’s not a failure of the product, just a reminder that floors move and rugs respond accordingly.
