## Care and maintenance

To keep the wood finish looking pristine and the cards in top shape, follow a few simple guidelines:
- Keep the case away from direct sunlight for long periods, or you risk fading and warping of the wood veneer
- Store the set in a cool, dry place. The cardboard shelves in some basements are not your friend; humidity can cause wrap-around warping, and you do not want a deck with bent corners showing up at your next bluff-off
- Clean the cards with a soft, dry cloth if dust accumulates. Do not apply household cleaners or solvents—they will ruin the finish and perhaps your sense of dignity
- Chips should be kept in a sealed container away from heat. If chips accumulate dents or chips of their own, you have probably played too aggressively and you know it
- If the dice start to feel sticky or gritty, give them a quick rinse and dry; this will keep the rolling feeling smooth rather than gritty and messy

If you worship at the altar of long-term gear, you will appreciate the long-game value of a well-kept kit. It is not glamourous maintenance fluff; it is the difference between a pleasant travel kit and a travel kit that looks like it is auditioning for a detective noir film.
