## Quick refresher: How Exploding Kittens works (in Case You’re New Here)
If you already know the drill, feel free to skim to the next section. If not, here’s the compact version tailored for Party Pack 2’s flavor:

1) The goal is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you draw one, you’re out unless you have a defusal card or the board state lets you escape the blast. Yes, it’s as dramatic as it sounds.
2) The deck is a mix of attack, defense, and luck cards. You play cards to influence who draws next, who can skip turns, and when to flip the inevitable hook that ends your round.
3) The party aspect comes from players declaring cunning moves, bluffing, or calling bluffs with Nope-style interruptions and other sneaky plays. The whole table becomes a witness to chaos and camaraderie—mostly chaos, with a dash of camaraderie when someone finally wins a round after a prolonged chain of “just one more card.”

## Strategy Tips, From “Hey, Don’t Panic” to “Strategy Time”
A common trap with party games is treating them strictly as luck-based experiences. Part of the charm of Exploding Kittens: Party Pack 2 is that you can influence outcomes through reading the other players, bluffing, and choosing moments to deploy your more defiant cards. Here are a few practical tips to maximize your fun and minimize the time you spend sulking when your cat-themed destiny fails you:

- Read the room before you act. If your table is already in hysterics about a previous misplay, you have permission to lean into the chaos instead of overthinking your move. The social energy matters as much as your hand.
- Use “defense” cards strategically. If you suspect a round might be dragging you toward a catastrophic draw, a timely defense can buy you the crucial turn you need to regroup and plan a better bluff.
- Save some cards for a late-game swing. It’s tempting to unload everything early; resist. The late-game cards are the ones that can determine who survives, who bluffs, and who ends up with a story about the night they narrowly escaped a card-induced apocalypse.
- Watch the tells. In variance-driven games, people often telegraph when they’re about to attempt a risky move. If you can identify these tells, you can anticipate and react in ways that keep you in the game longer—sometimes by just letting someone else take the heat first.
- Embrace the chaos. Part of what makes Exploding Kittens so endearing is the sense that any sane plan can Go Wrong in the most ridiculous manner. Lean into that, and you’ll spend less time frustrated and more time laughing.
