### Interface and Interaction

As with most AR experiences in Gen 1, the input methods rely on a mix of gesture controls, eye-tracking, and voice commands. Gesture controls work best with smooth, deliberate movements rather than frantic twitching. The eye-tracking is useful for in-headline navigation and selecting options from the menu, but you will still have to contend with occasional drift or misreads in bright light. Voice commands can sometimes pick up ambient noise, especially on busy streets or in coffee shops where the barrier between your voice and the world can feel a little unnatural. The long story short: you can have a pretty good time if you approach Skyler Gen 1 as a productivity accessory rather than a gaming device.
