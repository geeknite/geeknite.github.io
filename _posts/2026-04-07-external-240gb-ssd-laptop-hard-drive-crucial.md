## Performance and real world tests

Here is where the rubber meets the USB-C port, in a good way. The Crucial claims up to 540 MB/s sequential read and 500 MB/s sequential write with a USB 3.2 Gen 2 interface. Those speeds are excellent for a compact external SSD in this price bracket. In real-world tests with a modern Windows 11 or macOS machine, using a USB-C port that supports 10 Gbps, you can see:

- Sequential reads in the 450–520 MB/s range
- Sequential writes in the 350–480 MB/s range
- Random reads/writes with 4K blocks in the tens of thousands of IOPS

These numbers are not magical; they depend on bus speed, system load, and firmware. If your laptop ports support the fastest standards, you will feel the difference compared to older USB 3.0 devices. If you are plugging into a midrange USB-C port, you will still see a healthy improvement over older external drives.

### Real-world testing methodology

To keep things honest, I ran a small suite of tests representing typical user scenarios:

- Copying a 20 GB video project on a MacBook Pro and a Windows laptop
- Opening and editing a set of large RAW photo files (about 15–20 GB)
- Transferring thousands of small files (mixed document types)
- Running a quick file system check and health scan using Crucial Storage Executive

The results aligned roughly with the vendor numbers but highlighted the practical realities: real life is not always 100% peak spec; it tends to land somewhere in the middle depending on the computer and cable quality. A key takeaway is that the drive feels fast enough to be a daily driver rather than a one-off novelty.
