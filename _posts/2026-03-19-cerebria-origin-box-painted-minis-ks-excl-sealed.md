## What the Origin Box tries to do
### Thematic ambitions
Cerebria is a game about internal conflict made tangible on a table. The Origin Box leans into that concept by giving you more than just bigger miniatures; it gives you more dramatic presence. You can see this in the way the larger figures are posed—leaning into their cravings, fears, and virtues—while the smaller components maintain the game’s rhythm. It’s a tactile reminder that the inside world is not a flat abstraction; it’s a place you inhabit with your hands, your eyes, and your persistent need to win arguments with your own demons.

### Mechanical synergy
From a mechanical standpoint, the Origin Box is designed to integrate with Cerebria’s core rules rather than rewrite them. You don’t get new mechanics that overturn the math; you get new visuals that support the same ideas: control the emotional landscape, manage presence on the island of Cerebria, trigger powers through aura states, and outthink your opponent by exploiting mood shifts. The minis aren’t just pretty; they’re strategic icons you’ll consult during play, which is a nice tilt toward a more thematic experience.

## Assembly, painting strategy, and hobbyist tips
### Assembly reality check
The bigger minis are not a trivial assembly project. Expect to spend a good chunk of time on gluing and some careful pin-mark cleanup if you want a pristine finish. If you’re planning for a lazy Sunday, you might want to skip the Origin Box assembly in favor of a multi-week painting sprint. The upside is that you’ll get a satisfying sense of accomplishment once you’ve pinned, primed, and painted the figures. If you’re a completionist or a streamer who wants content, plan for several painting sessions rather than one long weekend.

### Painting workflow suggestions
- Prime in well-ventilated space, then base coat with a medium-dark shade to keep details readable.
- Use a contrast glaze to bring out the eyes and runes on the face. It gives personality without turning the figure into a disco ball.
- Pick a limited color palette for each faction to keep the models cohesive on the board.
- Finish with a matte seal to protect the delicate lines and prevent chipping during table chatter.

### Storage tips for the painting side of life
Once you’ve got a paint job you’re not afraid to show off, you’ll want to store the figures properly so you don’t end up chasing dust in your own emotional universe. The Origin Box provides trays and foam inserts that help, but you might also want to add external organizers to separate mood pieces from aura pieces, because a chaotic table is the enemy of a calm mind. A tidy storage setup saves you time and keeps the mood intact for longer sessions.
