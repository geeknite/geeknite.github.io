## The Cards in Detail

The back pattern is where Igarashi s language sings. On a stark black field, white lines and shapes collide in a geometry that feels both ancient and futuristic. It is the kind of back that looks effortlessly symmetrical from one angle and deliberately off-kilter from another, a design trick that keeps the poker face honest and your eyes busy.

The card faces carry a Blunt Force Minimalism you expect from Igarashi without losing the game s literacy. The court cards keep their iconic silhouettes, but the line work is lean and confident. Suits, if they survive at all in this deck, arrive as stylized glyphs that nod to the four seasons, or perhaps to four ideals, or maybe to four moods you can summon when your flush is just out of reach. The pips are not a crowded grid but a set of bold, distinct marks that read clearly at a glance. This is not a deck for casino floors; it is a deck for design studios, coffee tables, and late-night planning sessions for world domination, one well-placed comma at a time.

### Face Cards and Symbolism

The result is a playing card set that reads as both tool for gaming and artifact for display.
