## Shipping from the US to the UK
If you’re in the UK and you found this kit locally, lucky you. But more likely you’ll need to contend with international shipping. A central selling point of this replica variant is its US origin, which some sellers leverage to promise faster shipping or lower prices. Reality check: customs handling, VAT, and import duties can add a chunky layer of cost and delay.

### Delivery timelines
Expect a couple of weeks at least if your seller ships via standard mail, with possible delays if you’re unlucky (or the shipping gods are busy). If you opt for expedited options, you may shave a few days, but the price jumps can be noticeable. Plan for a cushion period: a shipping window of 2–4 weeks isn’t unusual for US-to-UK shipments, especially if you’re dealing with weekend handling and a backlogged warehouse.

### Import duties and VAT
UK buyers should expect import VAT on goods entering the country, plus potential handling fees charged by the carrier. The exact amount depends on the declared value and the VAT rate at the time of entry. Some retailers declare lower values to avoid higher fees, which could mean you’ll get slapped with a post-delivery charge or a hold at customs. We’re not here to moralize about tax policy, but we do suggest budgeting for VAT and possibly a small brokerage fee. If you want a smoother experience, look for sellers who quote all-in prices inclusive of duties and taxes.

### Packaging and risk
With any import, there’s a non-zero chance of dinged corners or minor shipping damage. Card decks are fairly resilient, but if your package takes a hit, you could arrive with a box that’s seen a better day and a minor crease on a corner. If that bothers you aesthetically, consider adding a layer of protective packaging or sleeve the cards upon arrival. And if you want to avoid the drama altogether, buy from a UK seller who has the kit in stock locally instead of a US-based shipper.
