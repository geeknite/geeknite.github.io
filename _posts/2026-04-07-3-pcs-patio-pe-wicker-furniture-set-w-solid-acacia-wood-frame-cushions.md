## Design & Materials: Wicker, Wood, and How They Play Nice

### PE Wicker vs. Natural Wicker
PE wicker is a synthetic alternative to natural rattan wicker. It’s designed to resist UV rays and moisture better than natural wicker, meaning less color fading and less fraying after a few summers of sun and rain. The trade-off is that PE wicker can feel stiffer and less tactile than the real thing. If you’re chasing the “Let me relax into this couch that feels like a cloud” vibe, PE wicker may feel less lush, but more practical for lakeside picnics and poolside resilience. The weave pattern matters too; a tight weave prevents snagging on loose socks and pet tails, which is some life advice you didn’t know you needed.

### Solid Acacia Wood Frame
Acacia wood is a popular choice for outdoor furniture; it’s relatively hard, resistant to rot when properly treated, and it looks good. The phrase “solid Acacia wood frame” in this set means you’re getting a backbone that won’t buckle under the weight of your mid-life coffee habit. It’s not teak, but it doesn’t pretend to be. The craft here is about providing a sturdy base for the PE wicker to do its dance on. Expect some natural wood variation—knots, color shifts, and occasional minor imperfections—that remind you this is a real wood product, not some flawless mass-produced thing. If you’re into sustainable sourcing, you may want to check if the wood is FSC-certified, though that might require a separate post to discuss certifications in a world where sustainability is trending on every tote bag.

### Care and Cleaning Tips
- Regularly wipe down with a soft cloth and a mild soap solution to prevent grime buildup.
- Check the ground clearance and ensure there is no water pooling around the legs; water accumulation can lead to wood warping over time.
- For cushions, store in a dry place during heavy rain or high humidity; use mildew-resistant spray if you live in a damp climate.
- Rotate cushions to prevent uneven wear; if cushions are reversible, rotate them to balance wear patterns.
- Use furniture covers during the off-season to extend the life of both wicker and wood.
- If the color begins to fade, consider a gentle color-protecting spray designed for PE wicker; test in an inconspicuous spot before applying widely.
- Keep an eye on joints and bolts; tightening occasionally will maintain the overall sturdiness and reduce wobble.
