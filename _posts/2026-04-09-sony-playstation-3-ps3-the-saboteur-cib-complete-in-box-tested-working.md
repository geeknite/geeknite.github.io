## Collector’s Checklist: Authenticity and Care for CIB
If you’re a serious CIB hunter, here are practical notes to verify and preserve your Saboteur PS3 copy:

- Disc readability: A clean disc with minimal micro-scratches is a good sign. If you can spin it without audible warping or stuttering audio, you’re likely in the green.
- Case integrity: Ensure the case is not cracked and the latch closes securely. The box art should align with the disc label, and the spine should be legible. A misaligned spine is not a deal-breaker, but it’s a tell about how the item was stored.
- Manual condition: The manual should be intact and legible. A damaged manual lowers the item’s collector value but doesn’t ruin your ability to enjoy the game—sometimes it’s still a fun read for the nostalgia factor.
- Inserts and extras: If the box contains a poster or promo sheet, check for creases or water damage. Small creases are common for older packaging, but major damage indicates rough storage history.
- Humidity and warping: Look for signs of moisture damage, such as warped pages or a warped case. If the item smells musty, it’s a sign that it’s lived a more dramatic life than your average cartridge.

Cleaning tips: use a microfiber cloth to wipe the disc surface gently, avoid harsh cleaners, and if you’re worried about the case’s integrity, consider a protective outer shell to preserve the box art. For the manual, a dry, soft brush can help remove dust that has settled into the crevices over the years.
