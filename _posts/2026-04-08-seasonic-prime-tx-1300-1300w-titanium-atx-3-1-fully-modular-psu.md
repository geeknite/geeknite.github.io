Fully modular PSUs exist to keep your slick build breathing easy. The TX-1300 includes a generous array of cables: PCIe, CPU power, SATA, and peripheral connectors. Cable management tips for this unit:
- Route the main 24-pin and EPS connectors along the same channel and keep them away from fans to reduce stray airflow obstruction.
- Neatly coil any excess lengths and secure with a couple of velcro straps. The outcome should resemble a modern art installation about power and efficiency.
- If you plan a dual-GPU build, separate the PCIe cables and label them so you can trace which rail is servicing which GPU later.

## ATX 3.1 and PCIe 5.0 Readiness
ATX 3.1 compatibility is not just a marketing buzzword here. The Seasonic PRIME TX-1300 is designed to be compatible with the latest form factors and the high power requirements of modern GPUs and gaming rigs. It supports the PCIe 5.0 ecosystem through its 12VHPWR connections, allowing for clean integration with future-proof GPUs that demand a strong, stable 12V supply.

This is important because the old 12V rails can struggle when multiple PCIe devices draw peak current simultaneously. The TX-1300’s architecture uses multiple 12V rails properly filtered and decoupled to minimize ripple and noise during surge events. In practical terms, this means more headroom for overclocking, smoother frame rates during long gaming sessions, and better stability during workloads that spike draw with the intensity of a caffeinated programmer finalizing a GPU-accelerated render at 3 AM.

However, do keep in mind that if you plan to run a multi-GPU setup with extreme power budgets, you should still size your entire build around a single PSU and not rely on the PSU to fix a poorly designed loop. The Seasonic TX-1300 is not a silver bullet for every power scenario, but it is a strong core for those who know how to plan a high-end system.
