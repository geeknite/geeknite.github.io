## Safety, Maintenance, and Best Practices
Safety is not optional; it’s a prerequisite. Here are practical tips to keep you and your crew intact during recoveries:
- Always wear proper gloves and eye protection. Synthetic rope can create nasty frays that sting when they poke through leather gloves.
- Never stand directly in line with the rope under tension. A misfeed or rope failure can snap back with enough force to cause injury.
- Inspect the rope and drum before every use. Look for frayed strands, flat spots, or any signs of deformation on the drum edge.
- Use a tree strap and anchors that can handle the load to avoid attaching to branches with loose limbs.
- Do not exceed the rated capacity for the operational scenario. If you’re on slippery terrain, assume a lower effective load and use multipoint anchor systems when possible.
- After recovery, spool the rope back evenly onto the drum to minimize line lay issues and kinks.

Maintenance routine:
- Regularly check the winch motor terminals for corrosion and clean as needed.
- Lubricate drum bearings per manufacturer guidance, but avoid over-lubing which can attract dirt.
- Inspect the protective housing and ground fault protection if present.
- Replace worn components promptly to avoid cascading failures during a critical moment.

Safety is sexy in concept but deadly in practice. Treat every winching operation with the respect it deserves, especially when a 10K machine sits between your pride and a muddy fate.
