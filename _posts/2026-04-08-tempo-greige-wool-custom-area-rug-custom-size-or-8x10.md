Maintenance and Care

Wool is renowned for its natural resilience, but it’s not a magic carpet that never needs attention. Vacuum regularly to remove grit that acts like sandpaper over time. For spills, promptly blot the area with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers. Water-based stains are usually easier to remove than oil-based stains, which may require a specialized wool-safe cleaner. Always test any cleaner in an inconspicuous corner first—because nothing says “grand design” like discovering a cleaner spot on your new rug.

Cleaning and Maintenance: Routine and Edge Cases

Wool’s natural lanolin helps resist soiling to a degree, but it’s not invincible. Regular vacuuming with a soft-beater brush (avoid the beater bar on high settings) preserves the fibers’ integrity. Address spills swiftly with blotting and light cleaning using a wool-safe solution. For a deeper clean, consider professional cleaning every 12-18 months, depending on traffic. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the wool’s fiber structure. The rug should avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight to minimize color fade; consider window treatments or rotating the rug’s orientation as needed to even out sun exposure.
