Introduction
City of Mist: On the Streets is the street-level add-on that makes your noir-city feel like it runs on coffee and bad decisions. For players, it sharpens the gritty, neon-drenched drama; for GMs, it hands you a toolbox full of corners, alleys, and the occasional deus ex latte. In this expanded look, we will break down what the supplement does, how it plays with the core rules, and whether it is worth your dwindling cash on a rainy Tuesday.

## Whats inside On the Streets?

This supplement focuses on the urban sprawl. It introduces new neighborhoods, new street-level myths, and a set of tools to keep your players from wandering into a big, loud boss fight every session. If City of Mist is your city-wide myth factory, On the Streets is the alley behind the factory with a vending machine that dispenses plot hooks in small caffeinated doses.

### New Loci and Neighborhoods
- The Neon Market: a sprawling bazaar of neon signs and questionable bazaars where information changes hands faster than a speedrun.
- The Whispering Docks: fog, plastic-wrapped packages, and someone always watching from the gangplank.
- Rooftop Haven: a hideout perched above a city that never sleeps, complete with a wind tunnel and a questionable potted plant that may be listening.

### Street-level Mythos and Clues
- Urban Mirages: short-term myths that anchor on a location for one session, perfect for weekly arc b-plot reveals.
- Informants: a new pool of contacts with varying reliability and a taste for dramatic reveal moments.
- Clue Chains: modular clues that chain from a street-level incident to a larger mystery, designed to avoid the choose your own adventure but make it a convention panel problem.

### Gear, Grit, and Hackery
- Streetwise Tools: gadgets that work under neon lights and when the bartender forgets to turn off the security cam.
- Quick Hacks: one-page rules for improvised tech and social engineering that do not require a PhD in CSI.
- Vehicle Options: a handful of affordable rides and the occasional cursed taxi that knows your name.

### Encounters and Seeds
- Ready-to-use scenes: compact encounters you can drop into a night, with a single act beat, a new clue, and a twist.
- Faction Tension: mood boards for rival groups that can push or pull the players like a streetcar on rails.
- Thematic Hooks: ideas that latch onto noir style and modern myths to make every session feel like a mini-film noir.

### Layout, Art, Accessibility
- A clean, readable layout that does not require a treasure map to navigate.
- Artwork that captures the glow of rain-soaked streets, a vibe that sits nicely between cyberpunk and mythic urban legend.
- Accessibility notes: a reminder that the book is not just vibes; it should be playable by people with different accessibility needs.

## Using On the Streets in Your Campaign

### Integration with Core Rules
On the Streets plugs into the City of Mist system with a few tweaks, not a complete rewrite. Mask usage, tags, and scenes get a little more blue-lit nuance, but you will not need a spreadsheet the size of a phone book to keep track. The focus is on micro-stories, not epic world-shaking threats, though a micro-threat can still feel epic when delivered with a rain-slick look and a saxophone.

### GM Tools
- Scene maps: compact, printable maps for alleyways, bars, and street corners that will not melt in the wash.
- Faction playbooks: quick-start guides for factions that want to be the cool bad guy with a soft spot for cats.
- Clue templates: modular clues that slot into a scene and can be swapped depending on how players approach the investigation.

### Ramping and Pacing
- Session pacing: a few well-placed reveals can take a night from meh to to-be-continued.
- Difficulty notes: tips for balancing street-level danger against the dramatic import of each clue.

### A Quick Scene: The Neon Alley
One night, you drop the players into an alley where the neon lights flicker in a rhythm that matches the beat in their bones. A boarded storefront hides a doorway into a forgotten tunnel. The clue chain leads to a street vendor who sells dreams in exchange for memories. The players must decide whether to steal a memory, bargain for it, or let it go. This mini-scene uses the new loci and clue chains to keep momentum high without collapsing into a boss fight.

## Art, Layout, and Production Values

The art direction stays faithful to City of Mist's neon-noir aesthetic, but On the Streets tightens the focus. You will see more day-to-day city life—the kind of details that make your players lean in: a rain-streaked window, a coffee cup left with a lipstick kiss, a mural whose eyes seem to follow you as you walk by. The layout is clean enough to be printable on the back of a napkin, but with the polish of an actual book.

## Pros, Cons, and Final Verdict

Pros
- Tight integration with the core system
- Strong focus on urban myth and street-level investigations
- Flexible clue chaining and flavor that can slot into many campaigns

Cons
- Some players may want a bigger city-wide conspiracy rather than slice-of-life mysteries
- A few sections assume a certain tone that might not fit every group
- Not all players may enjoy the heavy noir vibe if your table leans more sci-fi or fantasy

Final verdict: On the Streets is a welcome addition for groups who want to keep their stories grounded in a real, rain-soaked city with character, texture, and enough breadcrumbs to tempt a detective to follow them out of the bar and into the rain. If your table thrives on social intrigue, offbeat mythic reveals, and quick-start scenes you can drop mid-session, this supplement is worth a look.

## Final Recommendation

If you prize sharp, street-level myth-making and you want a toolkit that makes your sessions feel like episodes of a stylish TV noir, On the Streets earns a solid recommended rating. It’s not a hardcover conspiracy box; it’s a portable, moody toolbox for bringing City of Mist to life in the alleys, bars, and back doors of your city.
