### Weaving technique and loom talk

This rug is usually woven on a traditional loom, where the weaver passes the weft through the warp with careful tension. The result is a dense, even surface with a pleasant drape. ## Practicality and Care

Natural fiber rugs need a bit of mindful care to keep them looking their best. The wool component loves a gentle touch and benefits from regular but not overly aggressive vacuuming. Use a vacuum without a rotating beater bar or with a gentle setting to avoid rough agitation that can loosen fibers over time. If you get a spill, act fast but calmly. Blot with a clean cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible, then treat the spot with a mild soap solution and blot again. Do not rub or scrub, since that can push stains deeper and distort texture.

Jute loves moisture control. Excess moisture can lead to mold or a damp smell, so try to keep humidity at a reasonable level in rooms with this rug. If you have a spill near the edge, blot and avoid saturating the fibers. For tougher spots, test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness and avoid a color shift.

Rotation is often recommended for rugs with a definite direction in the weave or piles. Rotating every few months helps even out wear patterns, especially in high traffic zones such as the living room pathway or under a coffee table. If you own a large space with multiple seating zones, consider using the rug as a visual anchor only for one zone and layering with a smaller rug in another area to preserve balance.

### Quick care tips

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting.
- Blot spills promptly; avoid rubbing.
- Avoid direct sunlight for long periods to minimize fiber fading.
- Rotate periodically to even out wear.
- Use a rug pad to protect the underside and add comfort underfoot.

## Maintenance Tips and Tricks

### Spot care strategies
- For common spills, blot, blot, blot. Do not rub.
- For oils, sprinkle a small amount of cornstarch and let it sit before vacuuming away. It lifts the oil without grinding it in deeper.
- For pet accidents, rinse gently with water and blot until dry, then inspect for any lingering odor. If needed, repeat and consider a professional cleaning every couple of years for a deeper refresh.

### Routine care routine
- Vacuum weekly or biweekly depending on traffic.
- Rotate quarterly to even wear.
- Protect from bright sun to minimize fading.
- Use a rug pad to extend life and prevent slippage.
