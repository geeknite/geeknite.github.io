Fortune Row’s design loves a good silhouette and a strong grid. The balance here is deliberate: you can glance at a sheet and know exactly where to look, which underpins the game’s momentum. This matters because City of Mist thrives on momentum—the moment you’re flipping pages for rules references, you’ve broken the spell. The font choices deserve a shout-out. They’re crisp without feeling clinical, and they scale well for players who prefer larger print or who are playing on a smaller screen. Typography and spacing choices also support accessibility. If your table tends to scribble on character sheets (which many GM-narrated campaigns do), you’ll appreciate that Fortune Row keeps the content legible for everyone after a few sessions of in-world drama.

## Negatives and Limitations: Where It Could Improve
No product is perfect, and Fortune Row isn’t an exception. The most common nitpick you’ll hear from players is that the density of information on some pages can feel a touch busy at first glance. You’ll likely want a quick pass-through to identify a few most-used fields, and a parent table with a legend to help new players orient themselves quickly.

Another potential snag is that the district-specific sheets assume a certain level of familiarity with Fortune Row’s lore. If your group isn’t using the Fortune Row district as a core setting, you might find some prompts less immediate for your arc. The good news is that the sheets are still functional as general-purpose pc sheets; you’ll simply need to adapt the prompts to your own campaign’s locale.

Finally, as with many third-party packs, you should verify compatibility with your exact City of Mist edition. The core system is stable, but some minor field names might differ across printings or fan-made variants. The Fortune Row pack remains compatible in spirit, but if you’re running a campaign with house rules, a little tweaking could be necessary to keep everything aligned.
