## Play experience: the chaos calculus
### Player count, pacing, and social dynamics
The game scales surprisingly well. With 2 players, the chaos is still high, but the dynamic is more intimate and fast-paced. As you add players, the room fills with louder cheers, quicker throws, and the delightful sense that one misthrown burrito can derail a round and become the story you post on your social feed. The pacing tends to peak in the middle rounds when most players have started to learn how to dodge, toss, and coordinate a quick card flip for a potential match. The table becomes a stage, and you are all audience and actors at once.

### Strategy tips that won’t ruin the vibe
- Early rounds: focus on learning where the best throwing lanes are and identifying which players are most likely to panic under pressure. It helps to assign a couple of “lead players” who are better at calling out close matches and coordinating dodges.
- Mid game: start paying attention to which cards are appearing frequently and whether others seem to be hoarding or discarding more aggressively. This gives you a sense of which strategies are working and which are failing badly.
- Endgame: when the deck is thinning, the tension flares. Use misdirection and playful taunts as a legitimate tactic to throw off opponents, but remember to keep it light and safe. Laughter beats anger every time, and this game wants you to laugh more than you yell.
