### Maintenance and longevity: care for your wooden friends
Wood is a natural material that behaves differently depending on humidity and temperature. A little care goes a long way toward preserving your Jenga set as a beloved artifact and not something you will bury in the closet after two sessions. Here are practical steps:
- Keep the blocks in a dry place; avoid leaving the box in a car where the sun could turn a maple block into a tiny, warm hockey puck.
- If blocks start to squeak, you can rub them together to release some of the built-up friction, or you can apply a tiny amount of mineral oil. A little goes a long way, and you won’t want to over-oil them since you don’t want any blocks to become slippery eels in your hand.
- Store the blocks upright or horizontally; either method can work, but if you’re dealing with a high-humidity environment, stacking them with moderate airflow will help preserve the integrity of the wood.
- Clean with a dry cloth; avoid water, which can lead to warping. If a block becomes sticky after a spilled beverage, a quick wipe and a gentle rub will restore the natural feel.
