## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity: Keeping It Fresh
Regular vacuuming, rotating the rug every few months, and using a rug pad are your best friends here. A pad reduces wear on high-traffic areas, reduces bunching, and helps with stability in spaces where kids and pets play. If you do end up with a stubborn spill, act quickly with blotting rather than rubbing, then treat with a mild cleaning solution safe for synthetic fibers. We don’t want to transform this from a fashion statement into a science project about dye migration.

For the longer term, consider scheduling a seasonal refresh: swap cushions, rearrange furniture, move that nice LED strip to highlight the rug’s best angles, and pretend you did it on purpose to optimize for the “gram.” Not every decorating plan needs a full makeover; sometimes a single rug can salvage a tired room—especially when it was just what you could afford after the big upgrade to your PC’s cooling system.
