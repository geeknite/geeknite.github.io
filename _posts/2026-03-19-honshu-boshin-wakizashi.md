Maintenance and care: keeping steel honest

High carbon steel is a reliable performer, but it asks for a little love. Do not neglect rust prevention. After practice, wipe the blade down with a dry cloth and apply a light coat of oil to slow oxidation. If you live in a humid climate or enjoy coastal air, consider a more frequent maintenance ritual. Sharpening should be done with proper stones and a patient approach. The blade on this model is not a run of the mill wall hanger; it is meant to be used, touched, and sharpened with care. The sheath should be kept clean and dry because moisture in the scabbard can lead to rust or dulling of the edge upon withdrawal.
