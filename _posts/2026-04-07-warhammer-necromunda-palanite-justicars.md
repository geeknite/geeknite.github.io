## Who are the Palanite Justicars?
Palanite Justicars are not your average street gang. They are the hive city’s balancing act, a mobile courtroom with a gunmetal gavel and a stern stare. They operate as hired enforcers who can be used to supplement a gang’s firepower, defense, and objective control. The aesthetic is unmistakably bureaucratic-cool: heavy flak armor, ransacked-but-regal helmets, baton weapons, and pistols that say “you’ve broken a law” before you even roll dice. The mood you get on the tabletop is not just about who shoots better; it’s about who can push a narrative that the Underhive is a place where law is an act of will and muscle, and sometimes a big robot with a badge does both at once.

In game terms (as presented by the card art and the hobby community), they function as a dedicated, disciplined unit—great at holding lines, providing reliable ranged options, and using their heavy presence to shape how your opponent moves ideas across the board. They can be hired by a gang to deliver controlled, field-tested enforcement power, rather than pure lawless chaos. If your plan is to tighten the screws on the opponent’s advance, Palanites can be your glue unit, the one that keeps your plan from disintegrating into a firefight with no end. They aren’t just an extra pair of shooters; they’re a strategic piece that helps you manage space, control objectives, and pressure your opponent into a strategy that fits your narrative.

For the lore lovers, there’s a neat tension in Palanites: they embody the hive’s justice system, which means their presence can spark roleplay moments galore. You’ll get a few tabletop sermons about order and discipline in the middle of a shootout—which is exactly the kind of dramatic flavor that makes Necromunda campaigns memorable.

## The Miniatures and Aesthetics
The Palanite Justicars look like they could anchor a small municipal budget, if municipal budgets included heavy stubbers and power batons. The sculpts lean into that “uniforms that could arrest you” vibe, with layered armor, imposing helmets, and gear that reads as practical and brutal in equal measure. The details are crisp, the line work is sharp, and there’s enough variety in poses to avoid the static “trooper two” syndrome that plagues some enforcer kits.

What makes them feel special on the table is how their gear communicates a role. The baton or shock weapon suggests close-quarters control and intimidation, while their ranged options are solid enough to threaten a responding unit from a distance without demanding the risk that a flamer or heavy plasma would require. The models are sizeable without tipping into “overbearing” on a crowded board; they have a presence that feels credible on a busier Necromunda table where a dozen other squads are also trying to be seen.

Sculpts are clean, with clean separations between the armor panels and the cloth elements. The helmets—functional, no-nonsense with a hint of ceremonial glory—anchor the aesthetic. If you’re a painter who loves a slightly ceremonial vibe with real-world overtones, Palanites offer a perfect palette: neutral base tones punctuated by disciplined accent colors (think a dark gray-green with brass or red trim) that read both as authority and as “these folks aren’t on vacation.”

### Painting and modeling tips
- Base with a cool gray or cream base to evoke the underhive’s grime without sinking into too-dark a palette. A light base makes the details pop when washes go on.
- Choose a restrained accent color. Red, brass, or navy blue work beautifully to convey that formal, bureaucratic vibe. You don’t need every color under the sun to sell the Palanite look; less can be more.
- Weathering should feel deliberate, not messy. A few scratches and a bit of grime on the lower plates remind people they’re seeing a unit that fights in a polluted world.
- Clean edge highlights on armor plates to keep the models readable at gaming table distances. The more legible the armor, the more the “lawman” vibe sells.
- Bases can echo the hive while staying crisp. A light rubble texture with subtle green moss or a touch of ash works well to separate them from your other, more industrial units.

## Rules and Gameplay: What They Bring to the Table
Palanite Justicars are not the flashy, glass-dropping power of a heavy tank; they’re the steady hand on the wheel that says, we’ll get to the objective with precision. Their playstyle is about control, ranged firepower, and a tough frontline that can weather a skirmish or two while your other units do the dynamic stuff.

Key concepts you’ll encounter when you field Palanites:
- Endurance and discipline: they’re built to hold a position and deny space to the enemy. If you like controlling the flow of a game and punishing overextension, this is your unit.
- Ranged reliability: their weapons provide precise, dependable firepower that can threaten enemy units before they can react. They’re not one-trick ponies; they can contribute on multiple turns, which is valuable on a dense board.
- Objective pressure: their presence makes it easier to defend or contest positions, which matters heavily in scenarios that hinge on control points or strategic reserves.

Wargear and loadouts vary across the Palanite kit, but the general vibe is straightforward: a couple of high-value close-quarters tools paired with reliable ranged weapons, all wrapped in armor that says you’re not getting through the front line easily. If you’ve played other enforcement or guard-like units in Necromunda or similar games, you’ll recognize the tempo: you’re not chasing kill counts as much as you’re chasing tactical advantages—holding a corridor, pinching a flank, or forcing your opponent into a suboptimal engagement. This is the kind of unit that punishes bad distance management and rewards careful planning.

## Painting and Modeling: Getting Them Table-Ready
- Start with a clean, muted base, then bring in the accent colors to announce authority. A medium gray with brass trim or a navy accent looks classic and authoritative.
- Use a controlled wash (think dark brown or blue-gray) to bring out the armor lines. You want the panels to pop, not look smeared.
- Edge highlight carefully. The Palanites’ armor has sharp edges; a thin highlight on each edge can turn a table-ready model into a centerpiece.
- The weapons deserve a little extra attention—metallics, clean but not too polished. A bit of weathering on the lower sections helps sell the “lived-in enforcement” vibe.
- Bases can reflect the hive environment without stealing focus from the models themselves. A simple rubble texture with a touch of pigment dusting keeps the look cohesive with the rest of your gang.

## How to Run Palanites in Your Necromunda Gang
The practical question: how do you fit Palanite Justicars into an existing gang? The answer is: with a little planning and a dash of restraint. Here are a few tips:
- Start small: a two-to-three model squad can anchor a plan without slowing your list’s pace. You get the firepower and a sturdy melee presence without risking your whole roster to a misfit interaction.
- Decide their role early: is your Palanite unit a defensive anchor, an objective-controlling force, or a mobile support squad that punishes aggressive shifts? Their role will shape your entire list-building strategy.
- Support synergy: palanites pair well with gangs that can keep up with a disciplined, methodical pressure. A gang with reliable close-range options and objective holders can take full advantage of Palanite control while their opponents are faced with a dilemma—address the enforcers or lose the objective.
- Upgrade decisions: you’ll often want to invest in a bit of survivability for the Palanites, especially if your meta features a lot of backline exploitation or high-angle shots. Think about options that increase resilience or mobility to keep them in the right place.
- Campaign play: Palanites shine in narrative campaigns where you can build a story around their enforcement role. If you’re into campaign-rich play, consider tying your Palanite deployment to events in the Underhive, such as a “quentin audit” arc or a city-wide crackdown narrative that your group can track across sessions.
