## Stain Resistance and Care

Stain resistance is a big selling point for a rug that’s meant to be lived on. The Cream Silver runner is designed to handle everyday spills and traffic; the real test is how it responds to the unplanned coffee splash at 7 a.m. or the muddy paw prints from a pet who enjoyed an extra long outdoor stroll. Here are some practical care tips to keep it in fighting shape:

- Blot, don’t rub: When you spill something, quickly blot with a clean cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible. Rubbing can push the spill deeper and damage the fibers.
- Use a mild cleaner: For stains that don’t resolve with blotting, a mild detergent solution or a rug-specific cleaner can help. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Vacuum regularly: Light vacuuming helps remove dust and debris that can build up and dull the rug’s color over time.
- Address spills promptly: Prompt action can help prevent set-in stains, especially on light colors.

If you’re uncertain about a stain, check the care label or consult a rug-cleaning guide like The Spruce’s rug cleaning overview or House Beautiful’s rug care tips. The key is to treat stains early and avoid harsh chemical treatments that could compromise the fabric or color integrity.

External care guides can be helpful, but you should also consider your own space’s usage patterns. If you have children or pets, you may want to schedule a periodic professional cleaning every 12–18 months as a preventive measure, even if you don’t see obvious signs of wear.
