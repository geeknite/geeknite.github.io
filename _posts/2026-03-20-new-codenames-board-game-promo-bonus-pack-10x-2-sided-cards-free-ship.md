## Design Philosophy: Why the 2-Sided Concept Works
The two-sided card approach is a then-and-now story in one product. On the one hand, you preserve a familiar mechanic (clue giving and deduction) while on the other you inject variety without requiring a full new deck. This mirrors a lot of modern game design thinking: maximize utility while minimize complexity. The 10-card count is deliberate. It’s enough to keep sessions fresh across several game nights, but not so many that you overwhelm players with options and render every game a data-driven, spreadsheets-on-fire exercise.

From a production standpoint, the dual-face design is a smart balancing act between cost and perceived value. You get the feel of a premium promo without the actual premium price tag, assuming the shipping terms cooperate and you don’t live on a remote island where the courier romance is more expensive than the pack itself. The result is a product that feels like a thoughtful add-on rather than a cash-grab with a fancy foil stamp.
