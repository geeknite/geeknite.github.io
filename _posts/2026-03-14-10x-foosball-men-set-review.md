## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping Your Crew Ready for Action

- Regular cleaning: Wipe down players and rods to reduce dirt buildup that can hamper the spin. A soft cloth with a touch of light lubricant on the rods goes a long way.
- Inspect pins and washers: Over time, the internal pins may wear; check for wobble and replace washers if necessary.
- Avoid salt air and extreme humidity: Foosball men aren’t exactly pirates, but they do appreciate a stable climate. Excess humidity can lead to corrosion in metal variants and warped plastics in lower-cost builds.
- Replacement cycle: It’s wise to designate a few spare figures for quick swaps. A 10x set provides enough redundancy to make mid-game swaps feel like a strategic move rather than a panic replacement.

For players who treat foosball as a serious sport (you know who you are), investing in a spare set keeps your league nights running smoothly and avoids the drama of missing players when a key figure loses its leg to a rogue spin move.
