## Gameplay and UNO All Wild Mechanics
### The baseline: UNO All Wild rules, with a Todoroki twist
If you’ve played UNO, you already know the rhythm: match color or number, play special cards to change the course, keep an eye on the draw pile. All Wild adds a loopier layer: certain wild cards cause “All Wild” effects that affect every player, not just the next. The Todoroki cards are integrated as thematic “character icons” that sometimes activate special effects when drawn or played under specific conditions. The overall effect is a game-night vibe that’s slightly more chaotic but extremely satisfying for fans who enjoy a good boss fight against their friends in a living room.

Here are some sample mechanic ideas you might encounter in-card rules and the included guide:
- Todoroki’s Choice: A wild card that lets you name a color, and all players must draw until they find a card of that color, or a special Todoroki effect triggers for a 1-turn advantage.
- Quirk Shift: If you play a blue card while Todoroki is in the active player slot, you may swap hands with an opponent who has the fewest cards.
- Dual Quirk: Certain red or blue cards grant a “mirror” effect that doubles the next draw or skips the next player—but only if you manage to chain a Todoroki combo.

These rules are often printed in the small user guide tucked inside the pack, but the essence is straightforward: UNO remains intuitive; Todoroki adds a thematic mischief that rewards risk-taking and clever use of wild cards. If you’re used to balanced draws and predictable color changes, All Wild with MHA spins will remind you that life is a little less orderly and a lot more fun when you’re holding a card that reminds everyone to chill, then ignite, then chill again.

### Practical tips for winning with the Todoroki deck
- Learn to spot color triggers and early wild-activation opportunities. The “fire” side tends to be aggressive; the “ice” side is more defensive. Use them to control the tempo of the table.
- Coordinate with teammates in party-mode games. UNO All Wild is as much a social sport as it is a card game, so don’t underestimate the power of a quick real-time alliance on a mid-game turn.
- Don’t overestimate the rarity of the ultra-rare variants. They exist, but your odds are better if you’re not chasing them in a crowded display queue in Japan’s peak lunch hours.

## Collectibility and Value

The Todoroki card is not just a playable piece; it’s a collectible token that signals you were part of a moment in time when three popular media bubbles converged. ### How to store and protect your Todoroki deck
- Use soft card sleeves with a clear front that won’t obscure Todoroki’s art. The color work benefits from a bit of protection.
- Keep the packs in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve the gloss and color balance.
- If you’re planning to play with the cards, consider a rigid binder to keep the set pristine between game nights. You’ll thank yourself if a friend knocks over the coffee and the deck remains unscathed.
