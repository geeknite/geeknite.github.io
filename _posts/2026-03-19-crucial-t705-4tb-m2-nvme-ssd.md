## Installation: It’s Surprisingly Simple
Installing the T705 is the kind of task that makes you feel like you’ve hacked your own PC, even if you followed the manual to the letter. Here’s a quick, practical walkthrough for those who don’t live in a binary-only world:

1) Power down, unplug, and ground yourself. Yes, the world will survive if you don’t touch metal every two seconds, but it’s good practice.
2) Locate an available M.2 slot on your motherboard or laptop. Most current boards support PCIe NVMe M.2 drives; older laptops may require a BIOS update or a different form factor, so double-check compatibility.
3) Insert the T705 at a gentle angle, align the notch, and press down until you feel a click. Secure with the screw that inevitably rolls away when you need it most.
4) If your system supports NVMe, enter the BIOS/UEFI and ensure NVMe is enabled. Some laptops may require you to set the drive as the primary boot device.
5) Install or clone your OS to the T705. This is where the magic happens: if you’re cloning, plan for a longer initial transfer because 4TB wants to travel in style.
6) Boot up and enjoy the newfound speed. Optional: enable Windows Fast Startup or your Linux distro’s equivalent, and marvel at the responsiveness.

Installation notes: If you’re upgrading from a SATA SSD, you’ll see not just speed gains, but a fundamental shift in how your system feels. If you’re upgrading from another NVMe drive, you’ll mostly notice the additional capacity and possibly more consistent performance in heavy-load scenarios where the previous drive throttled under sustained writes.
