## Care and Maintenance: Keeping Peak Sap Green in Prime Condition

### Cleaning basics
For routine maintenance, vacuum the rug on a low to medium setting to avoid pulling fibers. If you share your space with pets, consider more frequent vacuuming to prevent pet hair buildup. When dealing with spills, act fast. Blot with a clean cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible, then treat with a mild detergent solution. Avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper. Rinse with clean water and blot dry. If you notice any color transfer from fabrics onto the rug, address it promptly before it sets. For larger stains, you may want to consult professional rug cleaning services, especially if you’re dealing with delicate settings like a hospitality space or a showroom.

### Vacuuming and grooming schedule
Aim for a weekly vacuuming routine, but adjust to your household’s traffic. If you have kids and pets, two to three sessions per week are not overkill. This keeps dirt from embedding and maintains the rug’s plush feel. A simple brush or comb attachment on your vacuum can help refresh the pile, especially if you have longer synthetic fibers that tend to lay flat after heavy foot traffic. Rotating the rug a few degrees every few months can also help distribute wear more evenly, especially in rooms with strong light sources that cause color shift on one side.

### Rug pads: undetectable help that changes the game
A quality rug pad is the unsung hero of many interior setups. It reduces sliding, protects floors, and extends the rug’s life by reducing friction. If your floor is prone to scratches (hardwood or tile), a pad becomes all the more essential. For areas with heavy chair use, a dense pad offers both stability and comfort. In this context, a pad is less of a luxury and more of a flooring investment you’ll thank yourself for later.

## Installation Tips and Quick Start Guide

- Measure the space carefully, including the area under the furniture legs that will rest on the rug. A good rule of thumb is to have at least 6–12 inches of rug visible beyond the furniture on all sides.

- Position the rug so the main seating axis is centered in front of the primary furniture group. If your room layout is asymmetric, an off-center placement can work, but you’ll want to balance the rug with other design elements.
