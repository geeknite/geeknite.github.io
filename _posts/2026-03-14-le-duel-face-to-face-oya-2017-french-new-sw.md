In a world where battles are decided on the turn of a card rather than a battlefield, Le Duel from Oya delivers a compact two‑player duel card game that feels like a fistful of dramatic anime poses shaken into a tiny box. ## What is Le Duel?
Le Duel is a two-player card game built around direct confrontation. Think of it as a micro duel where each player assembles a short arsenal from a deck of attack cards, defense options, and occasional special moves. The aim is to outsmart your opponent by landing the right combination of attacks while surviving their counter moves. The rules are written in a crisp, compact package, which makes it ideal for a quick weekend duel or a break between bigger games at a convention table.

The French edition paints the world with bold colors and expressive icons. The flavor sits on the lighter side of dramatic—plenty of swagger, minimal grimdark, and enough personality in the art to make you grin even when you lose a duel by drawing the exact wrong counter at the exact wrong moment. It is a game that invites banter, not a game that requires a silent, studious table.

## Components and Setup
A standard box of Le Duel contains a handful of finely produced cards, a few token markers, and a compact rule sheet. The components are simple, but the design communicates clearly: you can pick up the game and start playing within minutes of opening the box. Here is what you typically find:

- A duel deck for each player consisting of attack, defense, and occasional events
- A small pool of tokens used to track resources or to activate certain effects
- A concise rulebook with diagrams and examples (in the French edition, rich in flavor text and iconography)
- A compact score pad or life tracker depending on the print run

Setup is deliberately lean. Shuffle each player’s deck separately and deal a starting hand, usually five cards, with the first player determined by a quick flip or a mutual nod. Then you’re ready to start the first duel. The overall experience rewards quick-fire decisions: there is no long session to slog through, which is ideal for a warm-up before a bigger board game or a quick, light session when you’re hangry and need a win to keep the mood up.

## How to Play: The Core Loop
The core loop of Le Duel is straightforward, but within that simplicity hides the sweet spot of tension that makes head‑to‑head games so appealing. The turn structure typically follows a tight rhythm: draw, plan, execute, and react. On your turn you draw a card, play one or more cards from your hand, resolve the effects, and pass to your opponent. Victory comes when a victory condition is met—usually by depleting your opponent’s life or achieving a specific control state determined by the cards in play.

What makes the game interesting is the timing. You might hold back a powerful attack to bait a counter move, or you might drop a defensive combo just as your opponent tries to push a big burst. The interplay between attack and defense creates micro‑duels within the larger duel, and that is where the game earns its fun factor.

### Card Types and Examples
- Attack cards: The primary tool to press the offense. They come with higher risk but higher reward if your opponent cannot block them.
- Defense cards: Your shield. They nullify or mitigate incoming damage and can be used to set up a favorable counter strike.
- Skill cards: Unique moves that bend the rules in clever ways—forcing your opponent to draw, reordering hands, or changing the costs to play certain cards.
- Event cards: Swerve the momentum with a surprise effect. They can swing a round from doom to glory with a well-timed activation.

The exact card text will depend on the edition, but the general idea remains: mix aggression with smart defense, and don’t forget to leave room for misdirection and bluffing. In a purely mathematical sense, Le Duel rewards players who balance risk and reward and punish hesitation. In practice, the most satisfying wins are the ones you earned through predicting your opponent’s gambits and punishing a misread with a well-timed counter.
