## Outdoor performance: weathering the storm (literally)

Outdoor furniture has to survive more than the occasional rain shower. Here’s how this set stands up to weather:

- Water resistance: The weave dries quickly, but cushions can retain moisture longer. Quick-dry foam helps, but it’s wise to store cushions during heavy rain or winter months.
- UV exposure: UV rays can break down fibers over time. The color of the weave will fade gradually, more noticeable on lighter tones. If you’re worried about color fade, rotate cushions and use UV-protective sprays recommended by the manufacturer or a trusted outdoor-care brand.
- Temperature and humidity: The frame should hold up to typical temperature swings. In extremely humid climates, a periodic check for rust on metal connectors is prudent. If you see any sign of corrosion, address it early with a rust inhibitor and protective coating.

## Maintenance and care: how to maximize life and minimize headaches

Outdoor furniture is a long-term investment; treat it with a bit of love and a lot of planning. Here are practical strategies:
- Clean regularly: Wipe down the weave with a mild soap solution; avoid harsh solvents.
- Manage cushions: Use covers and store cushions during heavy rain or winter months. If you live somewhere with intense sun, rotate cushions to reduce fading on hotspots.
- Inspect hardware: Annually check screws and bracket connections. A loose connection can result in wobble and a dramatic “oh no” moment during a family toast.
- Storage: When not using it for extended periods, consider a breathable cover or storage bag to keep dust and moisture away. A breathable cover helps avoid moisture entrapment that can lead to mildew.
