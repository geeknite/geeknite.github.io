## H2: Care, maintenance, and long-term upkeep

### H3: Cleaning strategies for wool rugs
Wool rugs are not invincible, but they’re forgiving. Regular vacuuming with a beater brush set to a gentle setting, rotating the rug every few months to even out wear, and blotting spills promptly with a clean, white cloth will keep your Eden Midnight looking sharp for years. For spills like red wine or coffee, the goal is to blot (not rub) and use a mild wool-safe cleanser if needed. Avoid soaking—this is not a swamp rug. If you’re worried about stubborn stains, a professional cleaning every so often helps maintain color depth and fiber integrity. And yes, you can absolutely hire a pro for your custom-sized baby if you want to preserve the midnight mystique without turning your living room into a chemistry lab.

### H3: Rotation, placement, and traffic patterns
Consider traffic patterns when planning where to place the rug. Placing it under the main seating area tends to maximize both foot comfort and visual impact. If you’re a corner-dweller with a minimal, modern aesthetic, you might push the rug slightly under a sofa and extend the exposure toward a side chair—this creates the illusion of a larger space. Of course, if your space is in a frequented hallway or entryway, you’ll want to consider durability and cleaning frequency more carefully, since that’s where the midnight magic might get tested by muddy sneakers at the end of a long day of quests in the world of real life.

## H2: Durability and a bit of science talk

### H3: Wool fibers and resilience
Wool fibers have natural crimp, which gives them bounce-back ability and resistance to crushing. A well-constructed hand-tufted wool rug can maintain its pile height and edge integrity for years, even with heavy use. The Eden Midnight line, with a dense pile, can stand up to normal living-room traffic and still look sharp. If you’re imagining a rug that can survive both a week of kids’ arts-and-crafts projects and a weekly poker night, wool is a sensible choice. It’s naturally flame-resistant to a degree, but don’t push your luck—keep candles on stable surfaces and away from the edges of the rug. In Geeknite terms: it’s not invincible, but it’s the sort of durable character you want in your main room character arc.

### H3: Edge finishing and pile density
Edge finishing matters visually and practically. A clean, well-bound edge reduces fraying and gives a crisp outline. Pile density influences how the rug underfoot feels; a higher density tends to feel luxurious and keeps its structure longer under heavier furniture. If your style ranges from mid-century modern to “I paused at a sci-fi console and decided to make this space cozy,” you’ll likely appreciate a finish that remains faithful to its form as years pass.
