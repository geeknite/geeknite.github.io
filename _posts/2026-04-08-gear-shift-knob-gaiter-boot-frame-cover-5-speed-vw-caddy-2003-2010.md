## Fitment and installation reality check
Let’s get practical. You’re here to upgrade, not to audition for a DIY horror film. The installation is typically straightforward, designed for a user who can handle basic hand tools and a few minutes of focused fiddling. Expect a couple of common steps: removing the old boot, fitting the new gaiter, and snapping the frame cover into place. If you’ve swapped a car battery or changed a headlight, you’ll feel like a certified interior mod pro by the end.

### Step-by-step installation overview
1) Safety check: ensure the car is off, keys out, and you’re not wearing metal jewelry that can scratch plastic. You’re dealing with trim panels and fabric, not a quadcopter battery.
2) Remove the old gaiter: use trim tools to lift the edge gently and detach the boot while avoiding breakage of any plastic clips. Expect a couple of stubborn snaps—they like to pretend they’re playing hard to get.
3) Clean the area: coffee spills, dog crumbs, and the last number of your parking ticket—clean them away. A clean base helps ensure the new gaiter sits flat and looks premium.
4) Install the new gaiter: thread or clip the gaiter onto the shifter, aligning it so the seam sits out of the way of your handpath and won’t catch on clothing during shifts. This is where patience pays off more than brute force.
5) Fit the frame cover: snap or screw the frame cover into place as instructed by the kit. You’re looking for a snug fit that doesn’t rattle when you hit a speed bump with a dramatic flourish.
6) Final adjustments: ensure the gaiter sits evenly, the boot frame is flush to the console, and that there are no loose edges that could snag your sleeve during a shift.
7) Test drive: roll around your driveway or cul-de-sac, trying a few gear changes to feel the difference. If you hear odd creaks, you likely didn’t seat the frame properly; reseat and recheck.
