## Historical Context

The Battle of Shiloh, fought in April 1862, was a turning point in the Western Theater of the American Civil War. General Ulysses S. Grant, commanding the Union forces, was caught off guard by Confederate troops led by General Albert Sidney Johnston. The surprise attack was nearly catastrophic for Grant's army, but thanks to reinforcements and some strategic brilliance (and a bit of luck), the Union forces managed to turn the tide.

## Key Players

Grant, the Union commander, was known for his relentless tenacity and willingness to engage the enemy aggressively. At Shiloh, his leadership was put to the ultimate test as he faced the possibility of total defeat.

### Albert Sidney Johnston

Johnston, leading the Confederate forces, aimed to crush Grant's army before they could be reinforced. His surprise attack was initially successful, but his death during the battle left the Confederates without their skilled leader at a critical moment.
