## Condition, authenticity, and fraud beware
### Authenticity basics
When you buy a complete SR lot, you want to be confident about authenticity. Look for uniform print quality, consistent holo accents, and the absence of obvious counterfeit tells. If a price seems too good to be true, there is a high likelihood that something is off. A prudent buyer checks seller feedback, requests clear photos, and if possible, asks for a short video showing the card backs and the card faces in good light. A lot like this should show uniformity across the stack; if you spot a glaring outlier, treat it as a red flag and inquire before you drop the cash.

### Practical care to protect your SRs
Storage is a big deal. Use acid-free sleeves, a hard case, and a cool, dry place away from sunlight. UV light can fade holo effects and alter colors over time, which is a sad but common fate for many SR cards. The goal is to make sure you can pass down your collection to future generations of DAIS (Dragon Ball Inspired Superfans) without guilt or regrets. It is not glamorous, but it is essential.
