## Maintenance and Care
Caring for the Morgan bag is straightforward. After a trip, wipe down the exterior with a damp cloth to remove salt spray, dust, and sunscreen. If you encounter a stubborn spot, a gentle soap solution and a rinse do the trick. Let the bag air dry completely before storing to prevent any mold or mildew from taking hold in hidden corners. The interior liners are designed to be wiped dry quickly, reducing the chance of carryover odors from last trip. Regular checks on zippers and buckles will prevent surprises on the water, especially if you store the bag in a car trunk or boat locker where moisture can accumulate.

If you plan to store the bag over winter, empty the main compartment and ensure all dividers are removed or loosened so they don’t deform. A light coat of a fabric protector spray can help extend the life of the coating, although heavy coatings are not necessary for most users. The goal is to maintain flexibility and prevent cracking or stiffening of seams over time rather than to create a glossy shell that prevents any leaks. In practice, simple care routines keep the Morgan bag performing well trip after trip.
