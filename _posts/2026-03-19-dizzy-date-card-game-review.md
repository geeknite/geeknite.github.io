## How to Play (Expanded)

Dizzy Date is intentionally flexible. It’s designed for two players to chat, laugh, and occasionally perform a tiny stunt that makes your friends wonder if you’re secretly in a comedy duo. There are a few core play modes and optional twists to keep things spicy without spiraling into a rules nightmare.

### Setup

- Shuffle the 100-card deck and place it within arm’s reach. 
- Decide who goes first. If you’re feeling nerdy, roll a die; if you’re feeling relaxed, pick a number between 1 and 2. 
- Choose a play mode: standard, rapid-fire, or a themed loop like romance, goofy, or deep dive questions. 

### Turn Structure

On your turn, draw a card and read it aloud. Cards indicate the type of action you need to perform. Depending on the card, you might:

- Answer a question about your partner
- Perform a light dare or silly task
- Share a memory or a desire
- Team up on a short collaborative activity

The rules are intentionally flexible. If a prompt hits a boundary, you can negotiate with your partner. Consent, comfort, and maintaining a positive mood are the north stars. If you need a soft no, you still win points for clear communication.

### Card Types and Examples

- Icebreaker: Have you ever pretended to be a character from a movie while your partner narrated the scene? Reenact a tiny moment and let your partner guess the character.
- Dare: Do a goofy pose for 15 seconds while the other person narrates your dramatic life movie trailer.
- Romantic Prompt: Share a memory from your first date and describe how it made you feel then, and how it makes you feel now.
- Activity: Build a two-seat blanket fort in under five minutes and tell a story inside it.

Note: The actual cards vary in content, but the vibe remains consistent: playful, affectionate, and a little bold without venturing into cringe territory. If you’re worried a prompt might push you out of your comfort zone, you can always swap or negotiate mid-game. The aim is to be playful, not performative.
