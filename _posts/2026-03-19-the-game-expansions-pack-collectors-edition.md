One of the most notable hallmarks of this pack is the addition of asymmetrical factions. Each faction starts with unique resources, abilities, and victory-condition tweaks. This creates a dynamic where a table of experienced players can pivot strategies mid-game as alliances shift like a political drama on a tiny stage. The asymmetry boosts replayability: you’re compelled to try every faction because each run feels radically different, even when you’re playing on the same map.

Balance is a delicate art. Designers walk a tightrope between fun and frustration, ensuring some strategies feel stronger than others—but skilled players can outplay raw luck. It’s the kind of balance that invites heated debates about synergy, table talk that could rival a sports broadcast, and the occasional dramatic sigh when your plan relies on a card that shows up once per game.
