Care and maintenance: keep it easy, keep it breezy

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it is necessary. Regular vacuuming with a routine that avoids aggressive beater bars will help maintain the pile and prevent the braid from loosening. For spot cleaning, a mild detergent solution and a clean cloth usually take care of minor stains without dulling the color. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the natural fibers or cause discoloration over time. It is worth investing in a basic rug pad for the underside; not only does it help prevent slipping, it also slightly raises the rug to breathe—an essential feature if you live in a damp climate or an apartment above a noisy neighbor who loves trombone practice at 3 am.

If you want to keep your OakRidge rug looking fresh for longer, rotate the rug every so often to even out wear and maintain the color balance. While the colorway is intentionally versatile, consistent exposure to direct sunlight can cause gradual fading. A simple solution is to use window treatments during the sunniest parts of the day or occasionally rotate the rug to different light exposures. The goal is not to become a rug conservator but to preserve the soft, lived-in character that makes this piece inviting rather than sterile.
