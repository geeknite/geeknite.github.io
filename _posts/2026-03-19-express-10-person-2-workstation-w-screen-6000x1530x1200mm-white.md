## Assembly, Installation, and Maintenance

As with any large, modular system, you’re likely to see a split between enthusiastic installation days and the day-after maintenance schedule. The Express system ships in modular components with labeled hardware. The good news: you don’t need to be a furniture wizard to assemble this thing. The not-so-good news: it is a long build that benefits from at least two reasonably coordinated adults, preferably with a coffee-based incentive plan for teamwork.

Expect a few hours of assembly per block if you’re doing it with a team. The process is basically a large, mechanical Lego set with Allan keys instead of studs. Keep track of screws and brackets; misplacing a single nut will cause a small, silent eruption in your workshop that only the person who loses it notices. We suggest laying out components in the order of assembly and following the included diagram to avoid the sort of existential crisis that happens when you realize you’ve inserted a dowel where a bolt should be.

Once assembled, routine maintenance is straightforward. Wipe down surfaces with a microfiber cloth and a gentle cleaner to preserve the white finish. Check cable channels periodically for debris that could obstruct movement or trap dust bunnies. If you’re in a climate with a lot of dust or humidity, consider a monthly preventive check for loose fasteners and a quarterly inspection of the structural joints.

Two screens per staff should be easy to maintain too. Calibrate the monitors, update drivers, and set up a standard color profile for your design or development teams. It’s surprising how much difference a consistent color setup can make for a project’s perceived quality and your own sanity after a long debugging session.
