## Care and Maintenance
### Cleaning Guidelines
Spills: blot, don’t rub. Rinse with a mild soap solution if needed. Air dry and avoid heat that could warp the fibers. For deeper stains, lift and treat before they settle, because once a mark becomes a memory, it becomes a legend that guests ask about when you tell stories of the time you spilled red wine on your own rug. Regular vacuuming is recommended to minimize dirt accumulation. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the fibers over time.

### Frequency and Longevity
A weekly quick vacuum or sweep is sufficient for most households. The goal is to keep the hallway from turning into a petri dish of chaos. With normal care, the rug should maintain its color integrity and structural feel for a respectable amount of time. It won’t outlive your grand future home renovation, but it should stay standing long enough for your next decor phase to unfold without you feeling like you threw money down a trendy black hole.

### Care Tips from a Geek Perspective
If you like to program your living space, this rug is like a reliable firmware update: not flashy, but it makes the old stuff work better with the new. Vacuum when the hallway leads to a living room that has a lot of traffic or two humans who pretend to be gym enthusiasts by walking in place for 10 minutes when the coffee machine is taking too long. For stubborn stains, consider a gentle stain remover and always test on a hidden corner first. And if you have a fancy robot vacuum, set it to the eco mode so you can pretend you are saving the planet while your cat judges your cleaning choices from the comfort of the couch.
