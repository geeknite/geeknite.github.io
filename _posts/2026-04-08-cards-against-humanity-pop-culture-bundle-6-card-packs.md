## Setup and gameplay essentials

### Quick setup
1. Shuffle the black and white decks. 
2. Each player draws ten white cards. 
3. The first judge flips a black card and reads it aloud. 
4. Everyone else picks a white card that best fits the prompt. The judge selects the winner, clears the table, and passes the black card to the next player who becomes the new judge.

The six packs intermix cleanly, which means you can draw from your favorite theme or swap packs mid-game to keep things fresh. Some groups enjoy a strict rotate of black card readers, while others let the current winner become the judge for the next round. The bundle accommodates both styles, which is a nice GPS feature for varied crew dynamics.

### House rules and optional twists
- Stronger boundaries: If a prompt feels risky, set a boundary card or a personal vow to skip handling sensitive prompts. 
- Time pressure: Add a 30-second limit per round to keep the pace snappy and reduce analysis paralysis. 
- Thematic rounds: Dedicate a few rounds to a single pack to celebrate a specific area of pop culture before switching packs.
