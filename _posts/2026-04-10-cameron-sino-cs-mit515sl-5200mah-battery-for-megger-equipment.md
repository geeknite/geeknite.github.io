## Safety, care, and maintenance
### Handling and storage
Li-ion chemistry loves a good life if you give it a reasonable resting place. Don’t store fully charged packs in hot vehicles for extended periods; the heat is a silent crime against capacity. Conversely, avoiding letting the battery sit at 0 percent for months on end is equally wise. The field rule of thumb: store at a partial charge in a cool, dry place and rotate spare packs to avoid sitting on the bench for too long.

### Charging best practices
Use a charger that supports the exact charging profile for Li-ion packs. Slap a generic charger onto a Li-ion battery not designed for it, and you risk overheating or overvolting. The MIT515SL tends to respond well to standard smart chargers that balance current and voltage. If you’re stacking the device on a multi-bay charger, ensure the bays are not all zapping the same battery at once; balance the load so that the hottest packs get some breathing room between cycles. In practice, you’ll find a sweet spot where you can top off a couple of Megger test sessions without coaxing the battery into a hasty nap.

### Safety features to look for in a replacement
Over-discharge protection, short-circuit protection, and thermal monitoring are the big three. A good replacement should feel like a safety net rather than a ticking time bomb when the environment is not ideal. Cameron Sino packs generally carry the basics and avoid flamboyant safety features that complicate service. The result is a battery that behaves like a responsible teammate rather than a dramatic diva.
