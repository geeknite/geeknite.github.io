### Durability and edge retention
D2 steel is known for its hardness and wear resistance, which translates to good edge retention in daily use. In the Honshu, this translates to a blade that can stand up to repetitive tasks without dulling quickly. The drawback is that D2 can be more challenging to sharpen than simpler carbon steels; it’s not the worst, but it’s not the easiest either. If you’re not comfortable with a bit of elbow grease, you might want to invest in a proper ceramic or diamond stone and a proper honing guide to keep the edge at optimal geometry. The fixed blade design reduces risk of tip breakage during heavy work compared to a folding knife; the tip geometry is designed to maintain strength while providing enough acute point for piercing tasks.

### Maintenance and corrosion considerations
D2 steel is not stainless, which means you should not treat it as a forget-and-forget blade. After use, wipe the blade dry and apply a light coat of oil. In humid climates or if you’ve used it for seafood or other acidic tasks, wipe down and oil more frequently. The black coating on many Honshu blades helps to deter corrosion, but it’s not corrosion-proof. The sheath should be kept dry too; if moisture sits in the sheath, it can contribute to rust on the blade’s spine or edge over time. The takeaway: a little maintenance goes a long way with D2, and a lot of neglect will make it show its age quickly.

## Care, sharpening, and lifetime use tips

- Regular maintenance: Wipe after use, dry thoroughly, and oil the blade tip-to-hilt. A light coat of mineral oil is a safe, affordable option that helps reduce rust risk on D2.
- Sharpening approach: Start with a medium stone, progressing to a finer stone to achieve a sharp edge. Maintain a consistent angle to preserve geometry, and don’t forget to re-profile or touch up the bevel as needed.
- Storage tips: Keep the blade in its sheath when not in use to avoid accidental contact. If you live in a damp area, consider storing with a silica gel pack in your knife box to help reduce moisture buildup.
- Safety first: Always handle a fixed blade with respect. The lack of a locking mechanism on a fixed blade is not a license to go reckless—it's a reminder to respect the blade’s potential and to store it securely when not in use.
