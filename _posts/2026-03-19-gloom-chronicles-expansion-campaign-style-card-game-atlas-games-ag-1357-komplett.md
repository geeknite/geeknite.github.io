### Campaign Mechanics: How the Campaign Drives the Table

The heart of any campaign-style expansion is the campaign system. In AG 1357 Komplett, you’ll find a modular approach that lets you tailor tension to your table. Here’s how it tends to work in practice:

- Story beats and branching paths: Each session advances the narrative through a set of story beat cards. At decision points you choose a path. The chosen path unlocks future encounters and alters villain motivation. It’s like a choose-your-own-adventure novel with more gloom and fewer sunshine options.
- Progression and consequences: Your investigators gather resources, influence, and items that persist across sessions. This creates a sense of continuity and accountability. If you ignored an objective in Act I, Act III will remind you by presenting a consequence that feels personal, like your character’s haunted past finally DM-like-narrates itself to you.
- Campaign pacing: The book usually sets up short arcs that can chain into longer narratives depending on your table. You can play a one-shot style, or commit to a full arc that lasts several sessions. The pacing is flexible, but be warned: doom has a schedule and it’s not polite about reminders.

This design is friendly to newcomers while still providing meaningful decisions for veterans. The campaign path encourages collaboration and discussion, letting players steer the tone of each scene. You’ll find that the emphasis shifts from pure puzzle-solving to storytelling, character moments, and the occasional existential dread about whether your dice love you or hate you personally.

### How a Session Unfolds: A Sample Flow

A typical session of the AG 1357 Komplett campaign unfolds in a familiar rhythm, but with a sharper narrative focus than your average card game night. Here’s a taste of how a night might go:

1) Setup and mood setting: Players choose investigators and lay out the campaign boards. The room lights dim to a comfortable level; the soundtrack is optional but encouraged—the score of tension should be low-key, like a creaking floorboard under a cautious cat. 
2) Opening act: The first encounter card is drawn, and the story begins. The objective may seem straightforward, but the expansion’s charm is in the complications that arise from the campaign choices you make. 
3) Decision point: A critical decision triggers a branching path. You discuss, you debate, someone tries to flip the table, the GM sighs and says, “We proceed with option B.”
4) Resolution and carryover: After the encounter, resources are tallied and your investigators lose or gain items, which then influence future encounters. This is where the campaign aspect shines—your decisions persist and accumulate, creating a sense of ongoing story rather than a one-off puzzle.
5) After-action and setup for the next session: A quick recap, a few jokes about the night’s misfortune, and the promise of new twists on Act II. The cycle repeats, occasionally interrupted by dramatic plot twists recorded in the campaign book.

If you’re new to campaign play, don’t worry about memorizing every rule in one sitting. The expansion includes a streamlined cheat sheet that covers the core chain of actions, along with quick references for the most likely branching paths. The first session may feel like a long prelude, but once you hit Act II, you’ll find yourself eagerly discussing your next choice as if you’re planning a heist with your best friends—and a few haunted chairs that seem to whisper at the wrong moments.
