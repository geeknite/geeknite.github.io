## Safety and Setup: Rules of Engagement for Gravity and Giggles
Let us be clear: a trampoline is a fantastic accessory, not a daredevil tool. The Rainbow 8ft is designed with safety in mind but it is also a reminder that gravity is unapologetic. Here are practical safety pointers that will keep you from becoming a meme in your own backyard:
- Place the trampoline on level ground with several feet of clearance around the perimeter. No heroic improvisations around sprinklers and garden gnomes.
- Use a safety net if you have young jumpers. If your model includes a protective enclosure, ensure it is properly attached and zipped before the dog starts marketing it as a new chew toy.
- Check the springs periodically for wear. A missing spring is not just a bummer; it can lead to uneven bounce and a dramatic dramatic exit from the trampoline. If a spring is damaged, replace the entire set of springs or the whole frame if you are feeling generous toward your future self.
- Always supervise children during use. This is not a playground for solo stumbles and sudden flips unless you enjoy dramatic storytelling about a kid who outsmarted gravity with style.
- We recommend using the jumping mat compatible with your body weight. The bigger you are, the more care you should take with bounce intensity.

The Rainbow 8ft Trampoline is built for quick assembly times and a safe, durable experience. However, do not treat it as a substitute for proper gym equipment. It is a private stage for backyard acrobatics and a stage for your pets to claim as their theater of the skies.
