## Care and Maintenance Tips for Maximum Longevity
### How to Size Up Before You Buy
Measure the space carefully. For living areas, ensure you’ve left about 18–24 inches of bare floor around the rug to maintain the visual balance between furniture and floor. If you’re using it in a corridor or entryway, ensure the rug doesn’t block door sweeps or create a hazard for moving carts or strollers. Remember that 6×8 is a good anchor for a seating arrangement with a sofa and two chairs; if your space is particularly generous or awkward, consider pairing two smaller rugs or stepping up to a larger size for ultimate layout flexibility.

### Rug Pads and Non-Slip Solutions
Because this rug is designed for multi-environment use, pairing it with a non-slip rug pad is strongly recommended. A pad helps keep the rug in place on hard floors and reduces wrinkling in high-traffic zones. For outdoor decks or damp surfaces, a weather-resistant pad or a rug gripper that’s designed to withstand moisture will help keep everything aligned and comfortable underfoot.

### Routine Care Schedule
- Vacuum weekly to keep dirt and grit from grinding into the weave.
- Spot-clean stains promptly with a mild soap solution.
- Rinse thoroughly if you’ve used any cleaning agents beyond soap and water, to avoid residue buildup.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear across the surface, especially in high-traffic areas or under sun exposure.

### Seasonal Checks
In regions with cold winters or heavy rainfall, check for mold or mildew in the corners after extended moisture exposure. If you spot anything suspicious, give the rug a thorough rinse and ensure it’s fully dry before placing it back on the floor. Drying times can vary, but the low pile typically dries faster than its fluffier cousins, which means you’ll have your space back sooner rather than later.
