## Maintenance, Longevity, and Care

Chips are resilient. They’re designed to take fiddling fingers and the occasional beer spill. Still, a few best practices help extend their life:
- Keep them in the case when you’re not playing; this reduces scratches and color wear.
- Avoid exposing them to extreme temperatures or moisture—this can degrade color layers and chips might stain or warp.
- Don’t stack too tall; inappropriate stacking can cause chips to crack if they are squeezed in a bag or case.
- Wipe with a soft cloth if there’s dust or residue.

If you’re paranoid about color fading, test one chip first. If the color wears oddly, you’ll know early; if not, you’re probably good for quite a while. Your gaming nights deserve stable aesthetics; nothing kills the mood like a mottle-colored chip near the end of a hand.
