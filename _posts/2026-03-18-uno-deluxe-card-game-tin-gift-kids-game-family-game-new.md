### Core rules recap (brief)
- Match by color or number on each turn
- Cards include Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four
- When a Draw Two or Wild Draw Four is played, the next player must draw cards or respond in kind, depending on the card
- The first player to get rid of all cards wins the round; but the real winner is the person who triggers the dramatic finish

### House rules and variations you might enjoy
- 7-0 rules: When someone puts a 7, the players exchange hands. When someone plays a 0, pass hands around in the direction of play. This can be chaotic in a good way.
- Stacking: If the rules allow stacking Draw Twos and Draw Fours, the tension ratchets up quickly. If not, it can still be fun to collect the cards in a dramatic pile and pretend you are improvising the laws of physics.
- Five-card UNO: A quick spin for kids to ensure shorter games and bigger feelings when someone wins with a single card left in their hand.
