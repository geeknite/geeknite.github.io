## Throw Throw Burrito Extreme Outdoor Edition

### What is in the box

- Two oversized burrito plush toys that scream fun and occasionally panic when tossed too hard.
- A deck of cards stuffed with goofy illustrations, silly categories, and the occasional facepalm pun.
- A compact rule book that somehow manages to be clear while still encouraging chaos.
- Optional score sheets and a small play mat to mark zones on the grass.
- A weather-resistant storage bag that is somehow helpful and also a magnet for stray dog eyes.

### Setup and objective

Set up is delightfully simple: place the burrito props at opposite ends of a safe play area, shuffle the card deck, deal hands, and declare your intent to outscore the other players. The objective is to accumulate points by completing card sets while dodging burrito duels. When a duel is triggered, the room erupts in giggles as players fling their foam weapons toward each other while trying to keep track of who has what cards. It is a ritual of chaos that somehow feels wholesome and heroic, even when someone accidentally hits the neighbor’s lawn chair.

### Core mechanics

The core loop goes something like this: draw a card, play a card, trigger or resolve a duel, dodge a flying burrito, collect a set, repeat until the deck runs dry or someone yells the last victorious phrase. Cards sometimes force duels, sometimes grant points, and sometimes cause mischief, which is exactly what the designer intended when they wired up a game about collecting burritos and unleashing mayhem.

The Extreme Outdoor Edition adds a few tweaks to maximize sunny day play. The burritos are designed for windier environments, the action happens on a bigger stage, and there are optional outdoor rules for marking safe zones so no one ends up with a misadventure worthy of a sunset social media post. The result is a larger, louder, more kinetic experience that still respects the core rhythm of the original game.

### Outdoors suitability

If your crew loves big spaces and active interaction, this edition shines. It is not a precise strategy game; it is a loud, sweaty party with pockets of strategy and tactical social maneuvering. Expect sprinting, dodging, and the occasional dramatic tumble onto the picnic blanket. The foam burritos are soft enough to be friendly but still offer enough heft to feel like a real throw. The result is a short, intense adrenaline rush perfect for a backyard crew who live for silly moments that become future inside jokes.

### Pros and cons

Pros:
- High energy with enormous laughter potential
- Greatly benefits from an outdoor space and a bit of room to run
- Simple rules that scale with the number of players
- Durable components designed with casual outdoor use in mind

Cons:
- Chaos can derail beginners or nonparticipants if you are not careful with boundaries
- Outdoors means weather is a factor and the wind can influence uses of space
- Burrito props can wear down with heavy use or rough ground

### Play tips for maximum fun outdoors

- Define clear boundaries with chalk or a soft rope to reduce arguments about landing zones
- Encourage safe tossing and head up to protect faces; keep duels away from fragile items
- Rotate players to maintain energy and ensure everyone gets a shot at glory
- Use a timer for rounds to keep momentum high and avoid a single long epic duel that burns people out

### Quick take on replay value

The Extreme Outdoor Edition is a party amplifier. It ramps up the social energy and invites improvisation. Each round can take a wildly different shape depending on the players and the environment. In one night, we saw the same deck yield three distinct vibes: chaotic sprint, strategic zone control, and a goofy performance art piece where players acted out a pretend cooking show with burritos as ingredients. If you crave a game that grows with your group energy, this is your ticket.

## Exploding Kittens Brand New

### What is brand new here

Exploding Kittens Brand New reimagines the landmark card game with refreshed art, a handful of new cards, and a few small rule tweaks that speed up play while keeping the core sense of chaotic humor. This edition is designed to appeal to long-time fans and curious newcomers who want a faster tempo without losing the signature cat-powered chaos. The art is sharper, colors pop more, and the text is punchier, which makes the whole experience feel contemporary while still being a goofy nod to the original vibe.

### What you get in the box

- A complete deck of Exploding Kittens Brand New cards with updated art
- A few brand new card types that add twists to the turn order, the draw pile, and the fateful defuse moments
- Optional new power tokens and a fresh ramp for players to track their hand and luck
- A compact rule booklet that is surprisingly easy to digest during a chaotic session

### How to play in a handful of sentences

Each player starts with a handful of cards. You draw on your turn, hope not to draw an exploding kitten without a defuse, and use defuse cards or special actions to survive. The game thrives on a rhythm of quick decisions and last-minute saves, with the Brand New edition nudging the tempo a notch higher through its new card types and slightly streamlined rules.

### Brand New specifics that matter

- The art revamp is not cosmetic; it subtly guides you toward faster recognition of card types and potential combos.
- New cards add risk and reward dynamics that keep the pace brisk without pushing players into analysis paralysis
- Some players report that the new cards feel more thematic and punny, which aligns well with the overall tone of Exploding Kittens

### Pros and cons

Pros:
- Fast rounds that fit well into a casual game night
- Cleaner rules with fewer edge cases than earlier editions
- Strong appeal for groups that love humor, cats, and a bit of chaos

Cons:
- The humor is not for all audiences; better with a crowd that enjoys quirky jokes
- The game relies on positive group energy; the mood can dip if the table is not into the vibe
- A tiny learning curve if you are completely new to the series, but nothing brutal

It is a social lubricant in a compact package.

## Side by side: fast takeaways

- Pace: Burrito Extreme Outdoor is a sweaty physical experience; Brand New is a mental sprint with humor.
- Space: Burrito needs a larger play area; Brand New adapts to smaller rooms easily.
- Audience fit: Burrito outdoor nights suit larger groups that like action; Brand New suits players who love memes, quick rounds and cat chaos.
- Replay potential: Both deliver high replay value, but Burrito shines when energy is high and players are ready for improvisation; Brand New remains a top pick for social gatherings with a focus on banter and humor.

## Tips for both games

- Always pick a friendly referee who can call safe zones and boundaries when needed.
- Use a soft, open area to avoid prop damage, especially on lawn chairs and garden lamps.
- If someone is new, pair them with an experienced player who can guide them through the pace without dampening the energy.

## Final verdict and recommendation

Throw Throw Burrito Extreme Outdoor Edition wins best for outdoor parties where energy is high, space is abundant, and the goal is to burn calories while laughing until the tacos return. It rewards bold moves, teamwork in the sense of cheering for the silliest duels, and a willingness to accept that a foam burrito may end up in the lemonade. If your crew likes to move, shout, and make decisions on the fly, this is your pick.

Exploding Kittens Brand New wins best for indoor game nights or mixed groups that want fast rounds, colorful art, and a strong sense of humor. It is accessible, quick to teach, and offers a steady stream of laughs and light competition without needing a big play space. If memes, cats, and rapid-fire decision making appeal to your vibe, this edition will feel like a fresh sunny day. Both games stand strong on their own and complement each other in a game night library, offering different kinds of fun depending on mood and setting.

## Final call to action

If you have a crew that loves chaos, color, and quick wins, you can not go wrong with either title. When in doubt, pick Throw Throw Burrito Extreme Outdoor Edition for a boisterous backyard battle; or choose Exploding Kittens Brand New for a sharp, breezy session with friends who appreciate internet humor and rapid-fire rounds.
