## Care and maintenance

Care for these decks isn’t complicated. Keep them in a dry room, away from humidity and heat that could warp the stock. If you’re in a humid climate, consider using a card protector or a small dehumidifier in your playing card storage area—feel free to pretend you’re a medieval alchemist guarding precious parchment. Wipe fingertips before handling to reduce oils and sweat transfer, especially if you plan on performing close-up magic or intricate fan work. If you get a bit of dust or residue on the back pattern, a gentle brush is enough to restore a clean appearance. Avoid harsh cleaners—these stock types aren’t meant to be scrubbed like kitchenware.

Storage in the 807 box is straightforward: keep them flat, stacked evenly, and avoid bending the tuck box. The last thing you want is a distressed box that looks like it survived a small laser battle in a sci-fi film. The two-deck arrangement means you can rotate decks between performances or practice sessions, which can prolong the life of both decks without forcing you to re-mentalize your entire routine.
