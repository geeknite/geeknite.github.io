### What is Sushi Go! Spin?
Sushi Go! Spin is not a separate game in the canonical sense—it’s a playful take on the classic Sushi Go! concept where you take the same quick-draft mechanics and spin them around the table with new art, a few new twists, and the occasional rule tweak to keep the players on their toes. If you’re familiar with the base game: you get a hand of cards each round, pass them to the left, pick one, pass the rest away, and after three rounds you total up sets to score points. ### How to Play: A Quick Refresher
If you already know Sushi Go!, you’ll be right at home. If not, here’s the quick and dirty:

- Each round, you get a hand of cards and choose one to play face-down simultaneously.
- You reveal, then pass the remaining cards to the next player.
- After three rounds, you score points for various combos (like sets of maki, nigiri, etc.).
- The highest score at the end wins. It’s that simple—and that dangerous, because you’ll be forced to watch your friends brag about their dumpling-dominating strategies with a smug grin you’ll want to smack off their smug faces.

In Spin, there are a couple of minor twists: you might see a “spin” mechanic that lets you rotate a set of cards to simulate a roulette wheel of choices, and there may be a couple of event cards that alter scoring or force you to swap hands in dramatic fashion. The exact rules can vary a bit depending on which Dim Sum Box EX NM you crack open, but the spirit remains: quick rounds, satisfying combos, and a table full of giggles as you squeal over a perfectly timed sweet tofu hand or a suspicious-looking egg roll that somehow pays off.

### Strategy and Tips: How to Out-Sushi Your Friends
Let’s get down and dirty with some strategy that won’t insult your intelligence or your taste buds.

#### General Principles
- Always track what your neighbors are collecting. If you see a lot of maki rolls going around, you might want to pivot to sushi rolls that complement those maki sets.
- Don’t overcommit early. Spin games often reward late-round sneaky plays. If you burn your early rounds on a lone dumpling, you might end up with a sad tray and a disappointed dessert.
- Pay attention to endgame bonuses. The last couple of rounds can swing from “meh” to “you just ate my lunch” if you hit the right combos at the right time.

#### Card-Level Tactics
- Nigiri is a glass cannon: they can be worth a lot if you have the right accompaniments, but they’re not always reliable if you miss the expected sushi parade.
- Maki Rolls build up points quickly but require careful timing to maximize the stacking bonus. If you watch the table carefully, you can time a perfect swarm to outrun the room.
- Dumplings reward progressively more points with larger sets. They’re the “get rich slow” of the sushi economy.

#### Spin-Specific Strategies
- If Spin introduces rotation-based effects, use them to bait opponents into overvaluing certain card types and then swing your hand into a different combo at the last second.
- End-turn positioning matters. If you can predict when players will pass you the best cards, you can set up a late-run score explosion that makes your tablemates swear they should have drafted differently.
