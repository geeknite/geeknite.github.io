## How to Play with Masters Edition: A Quick Guide
- Each player starts with a hand of cards and the objective is to complete a defined sequence of phases. Your progress through the phases is tracked on a personal score sheet, not on a shared ladder—though you’ll still be watching your neighbors closely.
- The basic building blocks are sets (e.g., three or four of a kind) and runs (consecutive-number sequences). Completing a phase lets you advance to the next phase on your personal track, not necessarily the table’s entire progress. This open-ended progression is part of the game’s charm and its occasional frustration.
- Masters Edition adds Master cards that modify how you form phases and how you interact with other players’ hands. They can trigger surges of dramatic play or quiet, sneaky manipulations, depending on how your group uses them.
- The standard Skip, Wild, and other action cards remain, but Masters cards give you extra tools to accelerate or complicate the late-game sprint for phase ten.

Fast strategies to get you started:
- Early flexibility wins. When you have multiple potential phases to pursue, go for the one that keeps more options open for your next draw.
- Timing matters. The Masters cards should be saved for optimal moments when you can swing a phase in your favor or force a costly misplay by an opponent.
- Don’t underestimate the power of a calculated risk. If you can secure a Master card and complete a tricky phase in one turn, you’ll earn big points and a confidence boost for the rest of the game.

## The Practicalities: Tips for Storage, Maintenance, and Display
- Storage: Keep the tin in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve color fidelity and materials integrity. A shelf with a little ambient light is fine; direct sun is not your friend.
- Handling: Wash hands before handling to minimize fingerprints on glossy surfaces and avoid transferring oils onto the card backs.
- Display: If you choose to display cards outside the tin, consider using clear sleeves for a longer-lived display. The Masters edition’s art can be striking under good lighting.
- Cleaning: A soft microfiber cloth is all you need for the tin exterior. For cards, avoid chemical cleaners; gentle brushing or a dry cloth will do.
