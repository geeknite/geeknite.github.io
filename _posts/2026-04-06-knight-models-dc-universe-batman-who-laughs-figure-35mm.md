## Painting Guide: Getting the Look Right
### Primer and Basecoat
Start with a solid-primer layer to help with paint adhesion, especially if you’re using resin. A neutral gray or black primer works well for this character. The Batman Who Laughs has a coat that reads black with purple hints in the shadows; you can build the base coat with a charcoal gray to avoid mudding the tone. Be mindful of the cape, which often features a gradient-like texture that benefits from a mid-tone approach rather than a single flat color.

### Color Palette Suggestions
- Coat/Cape: Deep black or charcoal with purple edge highlights
- Suit/armor: Dark gray with metallic trim for rivets and strap buckles
- Facial details: Pale pallor with inked shadows for the grimace
- Accessories: Metallics for gear, subtle greens for the villainous aura around the eyes

A tip: you’re painting a character that thrives on menace and irony, so use contrasts to your advantage. The Batman Who Laughs should look slick in the light, but he should also appear as if he could melt into the shadows and orchestrate chaos at any moment.

### Techniques to Elevate the Figure
- Layering: Build up from basecoat to mid-tone to highlight with selective edge highlights along the cape folds and armor edges. This helps create depth in the dramatic silhouette.
- Dry brushing: A light dry brush on the cloak furthers texture and reveals micro-scratches, lending a “tortured elegance” vibe. Be careful not to overdo it; you want texture, not a snowstorm on your miniature.
- Shading: Thin washes in the recesses around the face and armor will bring the features to life and emphasize the pain and malevolence in his expression.
- NMM or freehand accents: If you’re feeling fancy, you can attempt some subtle non-metallic metal or a tiny insignia pattern in the cape lining. It’s not mandatory, but it reads as high-level hobby charisma if done well.

### Final Touches
The last touches are where the piece snaps into focus. A few glints on metallic edges, a gentle glaze to unify the color transitions, and a final matte varnish to reduce shine will give your Batman Who Laughs a polished, display-grade finish. If you want a more dramatic eye glow, a dot of bright color at the eyes or a faint teal wash can elevate the menacing charisma without breaking the palette.

## Display, Photography, and Streaming Your Hobby Glow

If you’re going to post your work, you’ll want light that reveals depth without washing the color.
