## What is Uno Stacko
Uno Stacko is a hybrid that feels like a crossover episode between UNO and Jenga. Instead of drawing from a deck and shouting UNO, players take turns removing a block from a tower and then playing a UNO card that matches either the color or the number on the block. The color and number theme keeps the game accessible while the physical stacking adds a tactile layer that digital games cannot replicate. The result is a game that blends memory, timing, and social energy into a single spool of chaotic fun. If you have ever watched a nervous librarian meet a mosh pit, you will know the vibe here — calm beginnings, glorious chaos at the tipping point, and a crowd pleaser ending.

## How to Play
On your turn you pull a block from anywhere in the tower except the bottom base. The goal is to remove blocks without toppling the whole thing, all while playing a UNO style card that matches the color or number on the block you pulled. If you can match, you discard that card and continue with the next player. If you cannot match, you draw a card and pass. The game ends when the tower finally turns into a polite pile of colorful rubble or when someone achieves the last laugh by keeping the stack upright until the deck runs dry.

The simplicity of the core loop is a major strength. You do not need a rulebook the size of a phone book to play; a couple of minutes are enough for first timers to join in, and seasoned players will quickly start mixing in house rules for extra chaos. A lot of the joy comes from the moments that nobody expects — the sudden tilt of the tower, the gasp from the group, and the quick banter that erupts as someone tries to recover the chain after a risky pull.

### Quick play tips
- Watch the table balance before you pull. If the tower looks like it is leaning toward your neighbor’s chair, you might want to grab from the opposite side.
- Use your UNO cards strategically. If you hold a color that matches many blocks, you can influence the flow and push a difficult turn onto someone else.
- Don’t be afraid to add a silly rule for a round. Extra time limits, talking restrictions, or a goofy victory condition can dramatically increase the fun without complicating the core rules.

## Variants and House Rules
Part of UNO Stacko’s charm is the room it leaves for customization. House rules can stretch a few games into a tradition. Here are a few ideas that our testers enjoyed:

- Time pressure round: Use a quick timer to limit each turn, injecting an extra layer of urgency and hilarity.
- Silent round: A blue whale of a rule where players must keep their comments to a minimum; expect intense stares and surprisingly strategic plays.
- Color swap mood: On a wildcard color change, all players must switch hands and play for a block count instead of normal turns for one round.
- Cooperative run: The goal becomes keeping the tower upright for as long as possible, turning the round into a shared effort rather than a cutthroat race.
