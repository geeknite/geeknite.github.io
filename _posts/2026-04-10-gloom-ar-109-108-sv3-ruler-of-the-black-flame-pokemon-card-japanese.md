## H2: Collectibility, scarcity, and market vibes

### H3: What makes AR cards special?
Alternate Art cards exist in a space between rarity and art form. They’re emblematic of a collector’s mindset that values not just “can I win with this card?” but “how does this item feel in the living room, on a shelf, or in a video intro?” AR cards like our Gloom variant typically command a premium due to limited print runs, distinctive art, and the excitement of a chase. In practice, this translates to higher resale values, long-term appreciation for the right set, and a strong social currency among players who trade online and in person.

### H3: Short-term and long-term value considerations
- Short-term: If you’re chasing an AR for a deck-building facet or display, you might see local price spikes around release windows, unboxing content, and showcase tournaments.
- Long-term: The value tends to be driven by rarity, art quality, and the card’s esthetic resonance with fans. If the SV3 line gains broader recognition or if the Gloom line becomes a fan favorite, AR variants can hold or grow value beyond standard holo versions.

### H3: Care, storage, and display tips
- Use soft sleeve protectors and a rigid top-loader to prevent the card from baseline scratches and edge wear.
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve color integrity.
- Consider a display binder with UV-resistant pages to showcase the AR artwork without accelerating fading.
- Avoid frequent pulling in and out of sleeves; this minimizes trauma to the corners.

## H2: Care and display — protecting your jewel of the night

Protecting a card as dramatic as Gloom AR requires thoughtful storage and handling. Here are pragmatic steps that blend care with flair:
- Sleeves: Use semi-rigid to rigid sleeves for display-grade protection, particularly for AR cards with gloss and texture that benefit from snug, non-slip handling.
- Binders: Choose a binder that offers acid-free pages and gentle curvature to avoid edge creasing over time.
- Handling: Clean hands, no eating spicy ramen halfway through a trade session. Your oils are the enemy of glossy finishes and color fidelity.
- Display: A small, glass-fronted case with controlled lighting can highlight the artwork without scorching the burnished embers of the flames.
