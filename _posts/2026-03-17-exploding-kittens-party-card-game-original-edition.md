# Exploding Kittens Party Card Game Original Edition Review

If you have ever looked at a party and wondered if chaos could be distilled into a few dozen cat-themed cards, you are in the right place. The Exploding Kittens Party Card Game Original Edition is not just a game; it is a compact festival of chaos, giggles, and surprisingly strategic mischief masquerading as a family friendly party title. ## What this edition actually is

The Original Edition of Exploding Kittens Party Card Game is the base version of the game designed for quick pickup with a group that wants to laugh their way through a couple of rounds rather than dive into a lengthy strategy epic. It centers on drawing cards while avoiding the dreaded Exploding Kitten card, unless you have a Defuse card, a device that can be used to avert catastrophe in dramatically cartoonish fashion. In this sense, the game is part pop culture meme generator, part light Euro-y luck fest, all wrapped in a cute catnado of art that makes even the most stoic gamer crack a smile.

## Setup and components

### The deck and the vibe

As with most party games, setup is the least glamorous but most essential part. You shuffle the deck, deal out a handful of cards to each player, and remove a few Exploding Kittens based on the player count. The exact numbers can vary depending on house rules, but the spirit remains the same: you want enough risk to keep things exciting, but not so much that someone gets stuck without a single viable move. The Defuse cards are your lifelines here, and they feel like getting a spare parachute in a dream where you are being chased by laser-eyed kittens.

### The cat intruders

Cat cards are the heart of Exploding Kittens. Set aside their fancy names for a moment and understand this: you will not be building a deck to win a war of attrition. You will be playing for the payoffs that come with matching, pairing, and sometimes screwing with your fellow players in glorious fashion. The Original Edition includes a variety of cat cards that come in handy for combos, sneaky plays, and a few moments of suspense when you realize you have a near perfect hand and a terrible table position.

### The bomb in the room

Exploding Kitten cards are the metaphorical ticking clocks of the game. You want to avoid them, or at least manage them with a Defuse. The tension is not the same as a heavy strategy game, but it does something clever: it channels group psychology into quick decisions that feel meaningful without requiring a chessmaster brain. The moment someone whiffs a draw to intentionally trigger others, you have captured the essence of party chaos in under five minutes. And yes, that is exactly the energy we crave at Geeknite.

### A quick note on card art and mood

The art style leans into cute but savage humor. It is a balance between adorable and vicious, like a kitten wearing a tiny villain cape. ## How to play in a nutshell

### The turn cycle

On your turn, you draw a card from the deck. Then you may play any number of action cards from your hand to alter the course of the round. Action cards do things like skip your own draw, force another player to draw more cards, or peek at the top few cards of the deck so you can plan your forced drama a turn ahead. The aim is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card unless you are ready to defuse or otherwise create a fantastically chaotic moment for your table.

### The role of Defuse and Exploding Kitten

Exploding Kitten cards are the social equivalent of stepping on a LEGO while your cat stares you down with judgment in its eyes. You want to defuse them, or you want to avoid them entirely. The Defuse card acts as a lifeline, letting you survive an explosion. It is a resource you hoard until the universe demands its dramatic usage, which is almost always when your best friend is wearing a ridiculous hat and cheering at your misfortune.

### The endgame vibe

As players are eliminated one by one, the endgame grows more intense and more ridiculous. The game ends when only one player remains who has not drawn an Exploding Kitten without a Defuse. The final moments are often a torrent of whispers, brash boasts, and a flurry of dramatic reveals that resemble a tiny talent show performed in under five minutes. If you think you want a long, heavy session, this is not your jam. If you want a brisk, energetic burst of chaos, you have found your home away from home.

## Card types and strategies you should consider

### Exploding Kitten vs Defuse

Exploding Kitten cards are built into the deck so that every play session has the possibility of a last second miracle. Your Defuse cards are scarce contracts with fate. You want to balance the risk and reward. The early game often sees players conserving Defuses for a potential late game blast while using other cards to manufacture chaos that distracts from the core danger.

### Action cards that shape the table

There is a toolbox of actions that changes the pace of the game:

- See the Future: peek at the top three cards of the deck. This is the map-reading moment where you decide whether to push your luck or set up a table turn that benefits you.
- Attack: end your turn and force the next player to take two turns in a row. This is your disruption tool, perfect for when someone is about to draw into a critical theory of probability and despair.
- Skip: bypass your draw entirely, effectively stalling for another turn to salvage a corner case situation.
- Shuffle: reshuffle the discard pile back into the deck, injecting a fresh dose of unpredictability.
- Nope: a counter card that can thwart others actions, except for the Exploding Kitten itself. This is where the table gets spicy, because timing an Nope with a crucial move can steal a victory from under a smug opponent who thought they had the game sewn up.
- Favor: make another player give you a card. This is social leverage in high gear, letting you shape someone else’s fate with a well-timed demand.

### Cat cards and combos

Cat cards come in various flavors, and part of the fun is collecting pairs or sets that encourage you to shout silly combinations across the table. The synergy between different cat cards can create entertaining miniature puzzles: how do you leverage a two card set into a meaningful advantage when the deck is pretending to be a roulette wheel? The fun here is less about deep strategy and more about extracting social momentum from the people around the table.

### House rules that can spice things up

One of the best things about Exploding Kittens is how easily you can tailor it to your group. People often introduce quick variations such as dramatic penalties for loser draws or a running gag that the winner must narrate the rest of the round as a dramatic monologue. The game thrives on social dynamics, not on strict number crunching. Embrace that and you will unlock hours of laughter with minimal frustration.
