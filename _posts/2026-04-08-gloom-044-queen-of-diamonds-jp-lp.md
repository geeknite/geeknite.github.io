## Practical notes for buyers and sellers

### Grading and authenticity: how to tell a real JP LP No 044 from a look-alike

- Look for the Japanese text imprint on the card front. Although Gloom is a well-known character, language-specific text and watermarking on the back can reveal the edition. Authentic Japanese LP prints tend to display a consistent border color and gloss finish that aligns with the print batch used during that era.
- Check for centering and edge wear. LP cards should have minor edge and surface wear but keep a sense of clean print across the card. A hallmarked LP grade will show minimal creasing, and the back should be uniform with no misprints that would hint at a counterfeit.
- Compare with reference images from reputable databases. While you should not rely on a single photo, cross-checking with multiple sources helps you detect suspicious differences in border width or color hues. A forward-facing blue/purple hue that feels out of place is a red flag.

### Storage and presentation tips

- Use sleeves appropriate for modern card sizes. The JP LP card should fit into standard 2.5 x 3.5 sleeves with room to spare on both sides.
- Consider a binder or display case with UV-protective glass. Japanese LP prints can be more sensitive to long-term light exposure, and you want to preserve the color balance that makes the card pop.
- Keep a small catalog sheet next to the binder entry. Jot a note on the edition, corner condition, and a few phrases about why the Queen of Diamonds vibe resonates with you. This is a memory container as much as it is a financial asset.
