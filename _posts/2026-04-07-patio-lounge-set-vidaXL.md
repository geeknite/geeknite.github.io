## Design and build: weaving, frames, and the spine of the set

### Materials and construction
The hallmark of poly rattan is the synthetic weave that imitates natural rattan without the fragility. The beads of plastic fibers are braided around a metal frame, typically powder-coated steel or aluminum. The result is a combination of durability and a lighter touch than traditional wicker sets. The poly rattan color—often a natural brown hue or a dark chocolate tone—works well with a wide range of exterior palettes, from classic neutrals to bold tropical schemes.

The table in this set usually features a tempered glass top; this is common because glass provides a stable, easy-to-clean surface and adds a touch of “adulting” to the setup. The glass is typically set within the weave frame, so there’s a clean, modern silhouette that looks good in both sun and the occasional glow of string lights at night.

The cushions are a big deal here. UV protection and fade resistance are basic expectations, but the real-world performance hinges on cushion cores, fabric weave, and zipper quality. A good cushion will resist flattening after a season of lounging—and won’t squeak or slide apart when you flop into the seat after a long office meeting that felt like binary search on a printer jam.

## Comfort and practicality: does it actually feel good to sit on?

## Durability, weather resistance, and maintenance

### Weather resistance
Poly rattan is designed for outdoor use, but no material is truly indestructible. Expect the following:
- Sun exposure: Fading can occur over time, especially with intense UV exposure. Lighter colors tend to fade less dramatically than jams of black-wind Kaleidoscope-green, but you’ll still see some change over seasons.
- Rain and moisture: The weave drains relatively well, but cushions should be stored during heavy downpours to prevent mold and mildew.
- Temperature swings: The frame should cope with typical outdoor climates, but extremely cold or hot environments can stress metal components and cushions. If you’re in a region with harsh winters, consider storing cushions indoors and using protective covers during off-season.

### Frame durability
The backbone of any outdoor set is its frame. A well-made frame will resist bending and corrosion, offering a stable seating experience even after a few years of use. If you’re a DIY-rooted reader who likes to quantify every bolt, you’ll want to inspect the feet and any corner joints. A loose joint isn’t just annoying; it can translate into wobble that makes you feel like you’re balancing on a soap bar.

### Maintenance routines
Maintenance is mostly about keeping the weave clean and cushions dry. A quick vacuum or brush to remove debris, followed by a wipe with a damp cloth, should suffice. For tougher stains, mild soap and water work well—avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fabric. Cushions should be stored or covered during rain or heavy dew to prevent mold and mildew.
