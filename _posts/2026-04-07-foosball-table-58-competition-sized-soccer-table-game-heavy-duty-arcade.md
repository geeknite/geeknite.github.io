## Setup, assembly, and maintenance
Assembly takes a bit of time, mainly because you want to savor the unboxing ritual and pretend you are about to publish your own product review. The instructions are clear enough, though you may find yourself re-reading a diagram or two to confirm that you’re not accidentally turning a defense rod into an attack rod (we’ve all been there). Expect roughly 1-2 hours for a careful build with two sets of hands. A single builder can get it done in about 3-4 hours if they enjoy an extended puzzle with occasional breaks for snacks.

Maintenance is straightforward: wipe the exterior with a damp cloth, check the screws every few months, and re-tighten if you notice a bit of wobble in the legs. The rods should glide smoothly after several passes with a light oil or silicone spray tailored for metal; avoid over-lubing though, or you’ll create a slip-and-slide carnival in your own living room.

### Assembly tips from the trenches
- Do a dry-fit before final assembly to ensure all pieces match up, like a jigsaw with a sense of purpose.
- Use a helper to hold the cabinet while you align legs; gravity is a friend, but not a helpful teammate.
- Tighten in a criss-cross pattern to avoid warping the frame and to keep the surface perfectly flat.
- If you can, mount the table on a non-slip rug or mat to reduce vibration during intense sessions.
