## What exactly is in the Special Guest Limited Edition Set?

The set aims to deliver a few core things in one glossy package: unique survivor miniatures inspired by a specific guest or pop culture icon; a batch of loyal but dangerously bitey zombies with distinctive silhouettes; and a handful of scenario cards that tilt the balance toward narrative play. The practical upshot is a slightly shorter learning curve for players who want a themed experience, plus a more dramatic setup on the table thanks to new board layout art and punchy character art.

- A curated selection of survivor miniatures, each with unique sculpting and alternate color schemes. The faces look characterful, the sculpts carry personality, and yes, some of them sport outfits you forgot existed in your fantasy world but suddenly recognize as the perfect cosplay one day later.
- A new cadre of zombie types that riff on the guest theme. They look cooler on the table and provide fresh tactical choices, especially in scenarios where crowd control becomes as important as resource management.
- A handful of exclusive survivor and zombie tokens and a set of scenario cards that unlock specific asymmetric goals. If you love chasing a flavor narrative, these cards will scratch that itch without heavy-handed storytelling mechanics.
- A glossy rulebook with a pinch of humor and art direction that makes the cornfield feel a little more cinematic. The typography is legible, which matters if you are like me and forget how to read after you siege a dozen basement floors.
- A collector-friendly box with lid magnets and a proper tray insert for the miniatures. It looks premium on the shelf, but you also will need some storage discipline so you don’t misplace a card while shoving a basilisk-guest model back into its bag.

If you own a Zombicide habit, this is the sort of box that makes you want to yell at the shelf in triumph, and then immediately hug the box as if it were a long-lost friend. The production values are high, the miniatures are well-sculpted, and the card art has a cohesive theme that ties into the guest character’s vibe while still feeling at home with the rest of the game content.

The art direction in the Special Guest edition tends toward cinematic grittiness rather than bright neon gore. You will see resemblances to familiar guest archetypes, but the sculpts avoid bordering on caricature, which helps them stay on-theme for intense co-op sessions rather than turning the table into a cosplay showcase.

The component quality is on par with recent Zombicide releases. The base game has a durable feel, and the Limited Edition keeps that momentum with sturdy cardboard, durable poker-sleeve tokens, and a box insert that keeps everything organized between sessions. The magnetized lid is a nice premium touch that gives the set a sense of permanence without adding an excessive weight penalty to the total package.

From a durability perspective, this edition should withstand typical game nights without showing obvious wear after 20 or so plays. If your table habits involve frequent shelf-dumping or enthusiastic placing of figures after a triumphant victory, you may want to consider an accessory kit—like extra trays or a dedicated storage organizer—for long-term care of your miniatures.

### The hobbyist’s checklist: painting, storage, and play cadence

- Painting readiness: The guest minis respond well to standard acrylics; thin layers and mid-tone bases tend to yield cleaner contrasts between skin tones and fabric textures. If you want a showpiece on camera, consider a glaze or two to pull out the shadows without muddying the details.
- Storage strategy: A modular tray system with labeled compartments helps you keep track of survivor cards, zombie cards, and special tokens. Magnetized trays reduce the “puzzle box” moments after a victory dance. Pro tip: label the guest’s ability cards so you don’t confuse their signature move with your normal scavenging rules.
- Play cadence: The guest abilities create micro-arcs within each session. You’ll want to assign one player to track the special conditions so turns don’t spiral into three controller debates at once. Hint: designate a “scenographer” who plans the narrative beats and a separate “tactician” who handles the dice and actions.
