## Maintenance and Cleaning: A pragmatic approach to keeping the pattern crisp

### Spill response and general care
Spills should be blotted promptly with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers and cause a halo effect around the stripe edges. For most everyday spills (coffee, juice, and the occasional pen), a mild detergent solution works well. Rinse lightly with water and blot dry to prevent streaking. If you’re dealing with red wine or berry juice, you may want to blend a bit of white vinegar with water for a gentle stain-busting approach, but do a spot test first.

### Vacuuming and wear patterns
Vacuum the rug weekly on a gentle setting. If your vacuum has a beater bar, consider turning it off or using low suction to protect the fibers and the integrity of each stripe. Rotate the rug 180 degrees every few months to keep wear even and to prevent a subtle “stripe fade” along one edge from forming under table legs.

### Stain resistance and professional cleanings
For stubborn stains or heavy traffic, a professional rug cleaning every 12-18 months (depending on footfall) is a good idea. The cleaner can refresh the color and restore the fibers’ bounce without compromising the weave integrity. If you’re the type who logs every cleaning on a calendar with sci-fi doodles, you’ll appreciate having a predictable maintenance schedule that makes you look like a floor-care wizard at the next dinner party.

### Additional tips
- Use a non-slip rug pad to prevent shifting and add a touch of cushion underfoot. 
- Consider a simple boundary rug in a neutral color if you want to layer textures without competing with the stripes.
- Avoid placing rubber-backed mats directly on top of the rug; some rubber compounds can react with the backing or dye and cause discoloration over time.
