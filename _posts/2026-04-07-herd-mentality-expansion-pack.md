## What’s New: A Deck By Deck Breakdown

### New prompt families and their vibe

The expansion introduces several prompt family clusters. Some lean into cultural memes; others flirt with the absurd; a few experiment with meta-humor that references the very act of playing a party game. The design intent here is to give players a variety of triggers—quick giggles, long pratfalls, and those delightful moments when the room collectively reveals a shared, ridiculous assumption.

- Meme-mode prompts: quick hits that reference popular culture and internet in-jokes. They arrive fast and expect fast responses, so be ready to ride the wave or get washed away.
- Situation-mode prompts: hypothetical scenarios with a social twist. They tend to spawn interesting debates about what constitutes social acceptability in a given moment.
- Role-reversal prompts: players swap expectations, forcing someone who usually stays quiet to vocalize a loud, confident stance. It’s surprising how often the quiet kid turns out to be the best motivator for a rowdy crowd.

### New mechanics: light touch, heavy payoff

Herd Mentality’s core idea is “vote with the herd,” but the expansion softens the edge with a few mechanical nudges rather than a full-blown rule overhaul. The major additions revolve around three concepts:

- Quick-fire signaling: players signal readiness with a subtle gesture rather than shouting out loud or waving arms like fools in a parade. It keeps the energy high without triggering a loud, unruly mess.
- Gentle peer-pressure tokens: a limited pool of tokens that can nudge players toward a shared answer, but with a safety margin so no one truly feels trapped in a forced consensus.
- Optional storytelling rounds: short, light narrative segments that add flavor to prompts, turning a simple opinion into a mini performance piece.

These changes feel additive rather than radical. It preserves the game’s social chemistry while offering more layers for teams that want to build a story around their choices.

### New cards and content quality

The new cards maintain the same lighthearted tone as the base game, but the expansion avoids the trap of repeating the same punny jokes. Instead, there’s a nice blend of clever references and goofy setups that can land with different players depending on their pop culture literacy. Some prompts skew observational humor; others lean into absurd hypotheticals that force players to improvise on the spot. The result is a richer, more varied play experience without losing the core appeal of Herd Mentality: you get to watch the room decide what counts as the correct answer, then celebrate when the herd chooses something hilariously wrong but perfectly aligned with the night’s vibe.

### Player count and session length

This expansion is designed to scale with the base game, so you can keep a party of 4–8 players or push toward 9–12 with some minor house rules. The maximum number of players may extend the session slightly, especially with storytelling prompts, but it also multiplies the moments of shared laughter. If your usual game night is a two-hour sprint, expect this pack to turn a neat sprint into a playful marathon with ad breaks for snacks and heckling in a friendly tone.
