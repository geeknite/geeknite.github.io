## Drafting in a 50 Card Pool: Strategy That Actually Feels Like Drafting
Drafting with a 50-card pool is a dance between risk and reward. You need to perform at a pace that ensures you do not spill mana on your keyboard while trying to figure out why people are sprinting toward a certain color identity. Here are practical tips for drafting this hypothetical set:

- Identify a core two-color pair early, but don’t glue yourself to it. The 50-card pool should provide enough alternatives that you can pivot after two or three rounds without lurching into a dead end.
- Prioritize reliable interaction. In a compact set, removal and disruption still matter. If you can answer your opponent’s early threats, you create space to execute your plan.
- Curate your curve and synergy. With fewer cards, your deck’s curve matters more. You want a clean early game that accelerates into your midgame plan rather than a choppy mess that collapses under a single removal spell.
- Don’t fear archetype polarization. The set’s design anticipates that some players will drift toward red-tempo, others toward blue-control, and some toward artifact synergy. Each path can win if played with confidence and a sense of humor.
- Sideboarding shines in a 50-card environment. Sideboards help you adapt to a known opponent’s plan without sacrificing too much in your opening game one.
