## What makes the Fortune Row Deluxe sheets deluxe
### Aesthetics that tell a story at a glance
The first thing you notice when you pull Fortune Row sheets from their box is not the nameplate but the level of care in the art direction. The noir-influenced illustrations lean into the City of Mist vibe without shouting, and the color palette is chosen to differentiate roles and affiliations without turning your table into a kaleidoscope. The printers spared you the cheap, glare-heavy finish in favor of a satin or matte stock that feels substantial in the hands. This isn’t paper that pretends to be a prop; it is a prop that knows it is in a game.

The design language uses clear typographic hierarchy: hero tags, logos, and stats are grouped intuitively so you can skim for what you need during a tense scene. It is not a coffee-stain detector test; you can actually read these sheets in dim lighting without needing a flashlight strapped to your character’s Arcane Signature. If you play with a sunlamp in the background of your gaming space, you might want to invest in a good desk lamp anyway, but the sheets won’t fight you on readability.

### Material and feel
Premium sheets mean premium materials. The cardstock is thick enough to survive a table flip or a miscast Brilliance surge, yet flexible enough to handle a few ritual-style foldings without cracking. If your campaign involves a lot of note-taking, doodling, or stamping tiny runes to indicate status effects, you will appreciate the surface that accepts pencil and pen without ghosting. For those who still prefer pens with a little flourish, the surface is smooth and forgiving—no scratchy battles with ballpoints trying to leave a ghost of your hero’s ego on the paper.

### The organizational magic
Fortune Row Deluxe sheets conceptualize the City of Mist philosophy: keep the story front and let the mechanics support it. The sheets are arranged so that the core elements of a character—identity, mythos, logos, and conditions—are not buried under a mass of lines. There are dedicated spaces for the character’s title, their major and minor powers, the consequences of conflicts, and a curated set of quick-reference icons for status and plot hooks. If you have ever played a game where you spend three minutes hunting for the correct stat, you will appreciate the designers’ attempt to reduce that friction.

### Jekyll-and-Hyde with clever features
What makes Fortune Row feel premium is not just the look but the dreamily practical features: hallmarks for status, badges for affiliations, and a modular approach to the character sheet that keeps pace with a growing hero. The sheets support the city-building vibe of City of Mist by offering a structured place to record your crime boss connections, your mythic powers, and your ever-changing personal narrative. It is like a living notebook that wants to be your campaign’s best friend rather than a dusty ledger you pretend to read during a tense encounter.

## Design and production: what you actually get on the table
### What is included
The Deluxe set provides multiple sheets designed to be used across a character’s arc, including a main sheet for the hero, a companion sheet for companions or minor NPCs, and a campaign log sheet to keep track of how the story evolves as you roll the dice. If your group uses tokens or placeholders for Logos and Mythos, there are dedicated spaces to attach or draw these elements, which makes it easier to track your power shifts mid-scene without losing your place.

There is also a small companion booklet that walks you through how to customize the sheet for different play styles. It includes suggestions for players who want to lean more into noir rhetoric, mythic symbolism, or a balanced approach that values both character development and tactical clarity. The booklet is short and snappy, not a syllabus, which is exactly what you want when you are balancing a break room coffee or a late-night gaming session.

### Layout and usability in play
In actual use, the sheets feel fast and ergonomic. The layout is designed for quick scanning, which means you can glance down to confirm your Title and your Ability tags in under a breath. The typography lends itself to quick reading, and the icons are clear enough to parse at a glance. The design protects you from the typical rhythm of a long City of Mist session: staring at a blank page while you try to recount a convoluted plot thread. Fortune Row’s sheets nudge you toward narrative flow rather than letting the rules chain you to a ledger-like experience.

If your group uses a screen for the GM, you may appreciate that the GM-facing notes section is clean and minimal, reducing table clutter. Conversely, players who enjoy a heavy roleplay vibe will find the character identity and personal narrative prompts useful for staying in character during long sessions.

### Printing and compatibility
The sheets are designed with standard RPG printer sizes in mind, so you should not face any weird border cutoffs or misalignment when you print. The print quality is reliable, assuming your printer is in decent health. If you are a digital-first group, you can print a few sample pages and keep a digital version for shorthand notes. The digital-to-physical workflow is smooth, not a chore, which is essential if you want to have both printed sheets on hand and a digital backup for campaigns that jump between devices.

There is a little caveat worth mentioning: the more complex the sheet, the more space you need on your table. If you play in cramped apartments or in a coffee shop with a suspiciously quiet barista, you might want to consider a compact alternative or a pre-cut binder. The Deluxe sheets are not huge, but they are not pocket-sized either. They are ideal for a dedicated gaming space where the table is a character almost as important as the players.

## How to use Fortune Row Deluxe sheets in your campaign
### Quick-start guide

3. Use the Logos and Mythos trackers to illustrate how the character connects with the city and its intrigues.
