## How to play a quick rundown
Skip-Bo is a game about speed, memory, and a tiny bit of luck. The objective is simple: be the first player to play all the cards from your stock pile by placing numbered cards in ascending order on building piles. The travel version uses the same core mechanic, so if you already know the standard rules, you will feel right at home. Here is a quick refresher for those who might be new to the game:

- Each player has a stock pile you must deplete by playing cards onto up to four building piles in ascending order from 1 to 12.
- You can save wildcard Skip-Bo cards to fill gaps as needed.
- On your turn, you draw from your stock or a draw pile and attempt to play cards to the building piles in front of you.
- When you deplete your stock, you win the round and score points based on the other players' remaining stock.

The travel size deck is designed to integrate seamlessly with the requirement for portable play. The digits remain large enough to read without pulling out a magnifying glass, which is a nice upgrade for folks who travel with older eyes or who simply left their multivitamins in the hotel minibar.

### Variations for the road
If you want to add a little spin to your travel sessions, there are a few house rules you can try:

- Sudden-Death Shuffle: When a building pile reaches 12, you reshuffle the drawn pile and restart, making the round a little more frantic.
- Sprints: Each player has a small time limit to make moves, ideal for tight layovers and short battery life on your devices.
- Cooperative Play: Instead of competing for points, you team up against the draw deck to clear stock piles as a group.
