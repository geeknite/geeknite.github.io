### Assembly, Maintenance, and Real-World Use
Assembling a 10-seater is a rite of passage. It’s not a project for a casual Sunday afternoon if you’re expecting to binge-watch the latest sci-fi miniseries while doing it. You’ll need a clear floor plan, some basic tools, and a friend who doesn’t mind the occasional “are we there yet?” from the instruction manual. The instruction set tends to be relatively straightforward, with the usual mix of “align A with B” and “don’t overtighten the bolts, or you’ll end up with a table that looks like it learned geometry the hard way.”

The good news? Capetown’s hardware kit is typically well organized, with the bolts, washers, and caps placed in labeled bags. The not-so-good news: if you’re not careful, you’ll confuse the #8 screws for the #10 screws and end up with a few extra washers that serve as decorative coinage for the coffee table. Our advice: lay out all pieces, count them twice, and don’t forget to mount the feet level—because Level 1.0 is the baseline for any great dinner party. If you’re worried about indoor vs outdoor use, the weatherproofing typically holds up to sun and rain, though we recommend using coasters and avoiding harsh chemical cleaners that might degrade the finish.

Maintenance-wise, wipe-downs after meals are the name of the game. A quick wipe with a damp microfiber cloth is usually enough to keep things looking sharp. For stubborn stains, a mild soap solution works well; avoid harsh bleaches that would erode the finish or taste the table for weeks afterward. If you’re in a coastal climate, rinse off any salt spray and dry thoroughly—salt and steel are a drama duo you want to avoid at the dinner table. The chairs, being a mix of solid wood and cushioned seats, respond well to a light vacuuming to remove crumbs and curious pet hair.
