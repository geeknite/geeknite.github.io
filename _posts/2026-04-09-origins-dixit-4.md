## How to Play: Quick Recap
The core mechanics remain delightfully simple, which is why Dixit has endured for more than a decade. Here’s a quick refresher, because you’ll want the memory of a good story to show up when you’re in the heat of the moment.

- One player becomes the Storyteller for the round. They think of a clue—either a word, phrase, or a short sentence—that describes one of the cards in their hand.
- The Storyteller picks one card from their hand and places it face down, then whispers or shouts the clue to the group.
- Each other player selects a card from their hand that best matches the clue, and places it face down.
- The face-down cards are shuffled and revealed. Players vote on which card they think was the Storyteller’s.
- Scoring: If everyone or no one guesses the Storyteller’s card, the Storyteller earns 0 points; otherwise, the Storyteller earns points for players who guessed incorrectly or correctly, depending on the variant. The players who voted for the Storyteller’s card also score points.

The 84-card deck means there are plenty of combos to keep the storytelling fresh. In Dixit 4, you’ll notice more cards that pose a challenge to literal description: you can describe a mood and let players determine which card fits best, then pivot with a twist in later rounds. The game rewards imaginative phrasing and shared “aha” moments more than precise descriptions. If you’ve ever squeezed out a story about a kettle that dreams of being a dragon, you’ll feel right at home.

Jekyll tip: In a noisy convention hall, use a short, evocative clue rather than a long, elaborate one. Your fellow storytellers will thank you later when the cards align with your vibe.

## Player Interaction and Strategy
One of the appeals of Dixit is the non-traditional competitive axis: it’s not about crushing opponents with resource management; it’s about crafting stories that others relate to and can misinterpret in a fun way. Dixit 4 leans into this with a few small but meaningful changes:

- A slightly larger emphasis on the “storytelling skill” rather than pure luck. The clue structure rewards players who can hint at the card’s identity while staying oblique enough to avoid obvious guessing.
- More consistent scoring across rounds. The types of points you can earn align with both accurate guessing and clever misdirection. This keeps players engaged even when luck seems to drift away from you.
- The ability to mirror the clue tone across a broader spectrum of players. The art is accessible but not overly literal, so kids and adults alike can enjoy the same moment in the same round.

Strategy tips:
- Always think about the cards you’re not playing. The most interesting plays are the ones that force players to weigh their own cards against your clue. If you’re a storyteller, consider starting with a broad mood and then zeroing in with a precise image in later rounds.
- Use social dynamics: in mixed groups, players often gravitate toward familiar artists or comfortable narratives. Use that to your advantage by tailoring your clue to players’ tendencies while still keeping the mood ambiguous.
- When teaching new players, keep the pace quick by suggesting a simple, evocative clue that’s easy to recall during voting. You’ll avoid the “twenty-seven clues” trap that can stall a table.
