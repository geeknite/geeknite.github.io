## What is the Wulfsburg Dice Tower?
The Wulfsburg Dice Tower is a plastic accessory included with the Wulfsburg expansion for Zombicide Black Plague. Its core function is to hold and drop dice in a controlled fashion, rather than having players flick dice across the table like rogue meteorites. In practice, it acts as a tiny obstacle course for dice: you drop the die in, and gravity does the rest, hopefully delivering a decent spread of results while also sparing your tabletop from the chaos of a stray d6 ricocheting into your neighbor’s coffee cup.

If you have played through the core Zombicide Black Plague experience, you know that dice management is a critical piece of the puzzle. The Wulfsburg Dice Tower is meant to add a little theater to the process. It’s a nice touch for players who like their game components to feel substantial and their dice to feel fancy. For others, it may feel like adding a spice rack to a peanut butter and jelly sandwich: not strictly necessary, but it might make you smile during the hoarding phase.

## In-game practicality: does it actually improve the experience?
Let’s talk about the core reason most of us considered this tower in the first place: does it improve the dice-rolling experience? For a single playthrough, you might not notice a massive improvement. Zombicide is a game about resource management, risk assessment, and planning, and the Wulfsburg Dice Tower doesn’t alter those fundamentals. However, the effect it has on the tactile experience can be surprisingly meaningful. When you drop a die into the tower, you get a momentary sense of ceremony—the same pleasure you get from dropping a marble into a miniature marble-run at a kid’s science museum, minus the risk of losing your finger to a tiny plastic dragon.

From a practical perspective, the dice tower reduces table chatter and potential dice-paint-splash across the table. It also helps keep the game table neater, particularly in games with multiple players where a cascade of dice can feel more chaotic than the zombie horde itself. If your table is small or your playgroup tends to lean into dramatic flourishes, the tower can become a small stage for the suspense built into each roll. The downtimes between rounds can feel a touch shorter as players anticipate the clack and tumble of the dice, a minor theater that makes even the dullest weapon mod feel like a high-stakes cinematic moment.

That said, the tower isn’t perfect, and there are caveats. The tower’s capacity is finite; if you’re playing a long campaign or a game night where you frequently roll large numbers of dice per turn, you may find yourself refilling or re-rolling as the channel fills up. It’s not a dramatic limitation, but in the most intense sessions you may notice the tower becoming a bottleneck rather than a feature. Another potential drawback is space. If your gaming area is tight, the tower’s footprint can feel a little imposing, turning a neat accessory into a space hog for the table. If you’re playing at a cramped cafe table or a corner of a busy kitchen table, you might wish you had a more compact alternative.

## Maintenance and care
Plastic accessories like this tend to be low-maintenance. A quick wipe with a microfiber cloth will usually do after a long night of clumsy battle chants and snack breaks. If the tower collects a bit of dust in the channels, a gentle brush or compressed air can help. The key with maintenance is to avoid harsh cleaners that could dull the surface or degrade the plastic’s color. A light touch is all you need to preserve its “almost-new” feel.

If you ever disassemble the tower for storage, do so with care. The interlocking pieces fit together with modest friction; forcing them apart can lead to minor wear along the edges. It’s a small investment of time now for clearer success in future unboxings, which, let’s face it, is what we all want—the joy of unwrapping again, even if the plastic has already lived a full life in a previous owner’s gaming den.
