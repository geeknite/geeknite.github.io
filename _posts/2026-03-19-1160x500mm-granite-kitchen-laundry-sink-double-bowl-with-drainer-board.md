## Maintenance and care
- Wipe with warm water and a mild detergent after use
- Avoid bleach or highly abrasive cleaners
- If spills sit, wipe promptly to avoid stains
- For tough stains, a baking soda paste and soft cloth work well
- Use a microfiber cloth to prevent watermarks

## Installation and fit
- Before buying, measure the cabinet opening carefully
- Check whether you want under-mount or top-mount
- If you have a garbage disposal, check compatibility with drain location
- Professional installation is recommended for granite composite sinks due to weight and sealing needs

## Comparison to stainless steel and ceramic
Stainless steel sinks are comfortable and inexpensive but show scratches and water spots. Ceramic or fireclay sinks look elegant but can chip with heavy use. Granite composite sinks strike a balance: sturdy and less prone to denting, with less risk of chipping than ceramic. They also tend to have better heat resistance than many metal basins, though you should still use a trivet for hot pots.

## Care and maintenance again
To maintain the look of your stone-sink, avoid highly acidic cleaners, as well as steel wool or rough scouring pads. A gentle cleaning routine keeps the finish.
