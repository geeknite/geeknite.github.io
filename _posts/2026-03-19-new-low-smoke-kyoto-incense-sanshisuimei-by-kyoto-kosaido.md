## How to Use Sanshisuimei: A Practical Guide
Burning incense well is about rhythm, not volume. Here are practical steps to maximize your Sanshisuimei experience without turning your home into a carnival of aroma.

1) Prep the space: Open a window if possible, or at least cough politely into a pillow to avoid the “first-burn surprise.” Have a small, stable holder and a lighter that won’t fail mid-sentence. A calm, draft-free environment helps with an even burn. 
2) Light and blow: Light the tip of the stick until the tip glows, then gently blow out the flame so the ember burns steadily. You’re going for a small, constant glow, not a raging campfire. 
3) Placement: Place the holder away from papers and textiles. A metal or ceramic base is ideal; keep it away from walls with sensitive paint (you’re not painting a mural here, you’re building an atmosphere). 
4) Enjoy the tempo: If the room feels a touch too intense at first, pause. Let the scent settle, then re-enter the space. Remember, more does not equal better with incense. It’s a symphony, not a dare. 
5) Post-burn care: Extinguish by gently pressing the stick into sand or a non-combustible surface once there’s no glow left. Store the rest in a cool, dry place for best longevity.

A common misstep is to burn too many sticks at once. If you do, you’ll get the “smoked out” effect that defeats the purpose of a low-smoke line. Sanshisuimei thrives on moderation and intent. Think of it as a co-op game mechanic: you gain more utility by coordinating your scents with your activities rather than stacking raw power.
