## What is the Citadel Medium Base Brush?
The Medium Base is designed to tackle bigger areas with speed while maintaining a decent amount of control. It is built around a synthetic bristle blend that Citadel markets as durable, easy to clean, and suitable for base colors and base shading. The brush shape is a slightly larger round-tipped head with a longer taper, which helps in laying down broad, even coats. It’s not a mop brush, but it isn’t a delicate shader either. If you’re painting a squad of orcs or space marines, this brush could be your go-to for the initial foundation pass.

### Build quality and grip
Citadel brushes tend to feel sturdy right out of the box, and the Medium Base is no exception. The ferrule is snug against the bristles, giving you that reassuring “I’m a tool, not a toy” heft. The handle is resin- or wood-like (depending on batch) and ergonomically shaped to reduce fatigue over long painting sessions. The grip section isn’t aggressively contoured, which means you’ll still have good tactile feedback if you’re a clamp-on-the-table kind of painter. There’s a small amount of game-voice excitement here: you know you’re using a brand that claims to be a fit-for-purpose instrument rather than a decorative prop. It’s a practical tool, not a gadget.

### Brush shape and bristles
The bristle blend is designed to balance stiffness with control. You want a brush that can sweep across a model without curling into itself or splaying into a texture you didn’t intend. The tip is semi-pointed, which helps in avoiding harsh edge lines while still letting you cover large surfaces. It’s not a fan of super-thin lines, but it’s not advertised as a precision brush anyway. A healthy tip retention means you won’t be chasing bristles mid-coating. Cleaning is important here, as this brush is rated for synthetic bristles, which respond well to soap-and-water routines and occasional conditioner swirls after a day of heavy use.

### Performance on base coats
Base coats are the foundation of your mini’s personality; you want color to lay down evenly, with minimal streaking. The Medium Base shines in this area, particularly on flat surfaces and large armor plates. Because it’s a larger brush, you’ll cover more area per stroke, which speeds things up. The bristles hold a good amount of paint, not so much that you risk pooling, and not so little that you’re constantly reloading. You’ll see a nice balance between coverage and control. If you’re the kind of painter who hates reloading every two seconds, this brush will feel like a gift from the paint gods.

However, there are caveats. If you’re working on a model with a lot of curved, deep recesses or very tight corners, you’ll still benefit from a smaller brush for the secondary passes. The Medium Base is best used for the primary coat, the even foundation. You’ll then switch to a more precise brush for details, edges, and tricky angles. It’s a tag-team partnership, not a single-slogan campaign.

### Precision and control
While the brush is designed for base work, you’ll still need a light touch and steady hand to avoid slopping. The bristles have enough stiffness to push paint into recessed areas, but you’ll notice some limitations if you push too hard or use too much paint. The arc of stroke is important: you want a smooth cone of color rather than a wide blob. If you’re new to painting, practice with a spare model or a practice sheet to learn how the brush responds to pressure. The trick is to combine quick, confident strokes with mindful reloading. The Medium Base rewards a consistent technique and punishes hesitation with uneven coats.

### Tips for base coating with the Medium Base Brush
- Load lightly: dip the tip and wipe off the excess on a palette tile to avoid pooling. 
- Use long, even strokes across flat surfaces rather than short choppiness. This helps maintain a uniform color distribution. 
- Work with thin paints. If your color is too thick, you’ll get clumps and brush marks that defeat the purpose of base coating. Add a drop of water or flow improver if needed. 
- Let layers dry between passes if you’re painting larger models. Rushing can lead to smearing or misalignment.
- Clean immediately after use. Synthetic bristles appreciate a quick rinse and gentle soap to prevent paint from bonding like a party guest who overstays their welcome.

## Cleanliness and longevity
A brush’s life is largely determined by how you care for it. Synthetic bristles like those in the Medium Base can hold shape longer when cleaned properly but can lose stiffness if neglected. Here are some practical care notes:
- Clean promptly after use. Rinse under lukewarm water, avoiding hot water that can damage bristles.
- Use a mild brush soap or specialized brush cleaner. Lather gently; you’re not washing dishes, you’re coaxing a small instrument back to life.
- Reshape bristles while damp. Gently point the tip and let it dry flat or hanging with the bristles downward to prevent water from wicking up into the ferrule.
- Store upright or in a brush holder to maintain shape. A rolling carry case is fine, just ensure the bristles aren’t bent back into a T-shape when stored.

Common longevity questions revolve around whether synthetic bristles wear out faster than natural hair. In practice, quality Citadel brushes are designed to hold shape longer than bargain-bin alternatives, especially when cared for. The Medium Base Brush should last you many hobby seasons if you treat it well, clean it regularly, and avoid using it as a weapon against unmated terrain pieces.

## Paint compatibility and recommended uses
The Medium Base is most comfortable with base paints and mid-range colors with medium viscosity. It handles contrast paints with some caution because heavy contrast paints can pull and pool. If you’re using a wash after base coating, consider switching to a more precise brush to avoid smearing your newly laid foundation. For layering and glazing, this brush can do a decent job if you keep layers thin. If you want to attempt edge highlights or tiny detail touches, you’ll probably want a smaller brush as a companion. Think of the Medium Base as your reliable ally for the bulk of the job, while your precision brush handles the delicate finishing touches.

For fans of brand synergy, you’ll find that Citadel paints behave predictably on this brush. The synthetic bristles hold enough paint to cover large areas without constant reloads, while the natural response of the tip to light pressure helps with consistent coats. If you’re a crossover painter who uses multiple brands, you’ll appreciate the brush’s ability to adapt to various paint formulas, provided you don’t load up the bristles with too much pigment on the first pass.

## Maintenance and care tips for long-term happiness
Here are the routine steps that will keep your Medium Base painting like a champ:
- After painting, rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water until the water runs clear. Residual paint can harden and make future cleaning sessions annoying.
- Use a mild soap or brush cleaner. A gentle wash helps remove oils and pigments that accumulate with use. Don’t be stingy—your brush is a tiny athlete that deserves proper conditioning.
- Gently reshape the brush tip and lay flat to dry or hang with bristles down. Avoid drying with bristles pressed against surfaces that can mis-shape the tip.
- Store with the cap off or in a dedicated brush holder. Anything that squashes or bends the bristles is a betrayal to the cause.
