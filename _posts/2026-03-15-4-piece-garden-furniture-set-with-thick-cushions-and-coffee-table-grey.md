## Weather Commentary: Keeping It Fresh Under Sun and Showers
Outdoor furniture has two modes: hero and zero on rainy days. The cushions’ fabric is resistant to fading and handles incidental sun exposure, but it’s wise to store the cushions during heavy storms or when you plan to be away for an extended period. The cushions dry quickly, thanks to the breathable fabric and the cushion covers being removable. Still, if you’re in a region that gets a lot of humidity or frequent downpours, you might want to bring the cushions indoors after a rain and give the set a dry-out. The powder-coated steel frame handles rust risks better than bare metal, provided you keep the protective coating intact. A good habit is to wipe the frame with a damp cloth and avoid aggressive cleaners that might strip the finish. For those who love maintenance rituals, a yearly light wax or corrosion inhibitor can extend the life of the frame and keep the color looking fresh longer.

Storage is easy enough if you have a small garden shed or a garage. The cushions can be stored in a breathable bag to prevent mildew, and the glass top should be handled with care to avoid chipping if you’re moving the table around during seasonal rearrangements. In a pinch, you can cover the set with a weatherproof cover that fits the footprint. It’s not strictly necessary, but it certainly helps if you live in a climate where weather is your main antagonist.
