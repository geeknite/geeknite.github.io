## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance: Keeping It Bright Without Losing Your Mind
### Cleaning Routine Overview
Rugs of this style generally tolerate light cleaning well. A weekly vacuum (with a beater-bar turned off or a floor setting) helps keep dust from nesting within the fibers. For spills, the rule of thumb is quick action. Blot, don’t rub, to avoid embedding color into the pile. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution, tested in a small inconspicuous area first, is your friend. Remember: patience is a virtue, but so is a good stain-removal plan.

### Stain Resistance Claims in Real Life
No rug is magic-proof, especially under the tyranny of red wine, grape juice, and the occasional coffee mishap. The NF141B-222 brand promises stain resilience, which translates roughly to “you can get away with fewer scrubs than you’d expect.” In real-life use, you’ll still see the occasional mark here and there, but they tend to be less noticeable due to the color and the weave texture. If you’re a fanatic about perfection, be prepared to treat stains promptly; otherwise, you’ll still enjoy a clean, vibrant look after routine care.

### Maintenance Schedule that Actually Works
- Vacuum once a week, edges included, to maintain shape.
- Rotate every few months to promote even wear if your space has uneven foot traffic.
- If you have pets: trim nails regularly and use a gentle enzyme cleaner for accidents to preserve the fiber integrity.
- Protect high-traffic zones with a small rug pad or anti-slip underlay to reduce shifting and friction.

## Performance in Real Rooms: Light, Shadow, and Everyday Life

In sunlit rooms, the rug shines; the maize color has that warm, welcoming glow that can make a room feel larger. ### Foot Traffic Realities
A family living space will see shoes, socks, and occasional spills. The Maize Linen NF141B-222 handles this well enough to earn its keep in mid-scale households. The texture hides minor fuzzing, and the pile’s density helps it retain its shape after regular use. If you’ve got a high-energy house with pets that sprint around the living room on a nightly basis, you may notice minor flattening in heavily walked spots; this is expected for most rugs in this category and is not a sign of imminent collapse.

### Pets and Kids: The True Test
Dogs and kids are often the final judges of any rug’s true worth. The Maize Linen holds up reasonably well to claws and crayons; wipeable markers tend to come off with mild cleaning solutions if caught promptly. For larger spills, treat quickly and avoid rubbing—gentle blotting is your best friend here. If you’re all-in on a groomed home aesthetic, you’ll want to place the rug in a pet-friendly zone, not under a feeding area, and consider a fabric-safe protector for those inevitable spill moments.
