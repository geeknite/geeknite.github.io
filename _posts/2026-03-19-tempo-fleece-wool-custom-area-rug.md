## Sizing Options: Custom Size vs 8x10

This is the heart of the Tempo fleece wool decision for many shoppers. Sizing can make or break the look of a room, and Tempo shines here by offering two practical routes: custom sizes and the standard 8x10 dimension.

### Custom Size: tailor-made comfort

Custom sizing is ideal when your room has unusual dimensions, a thick sofa layout, or you simply want the rug to extend into a defined area—like under a coffee table, or to help anchor seating without colliding with wall trim. The concept is simple: provide your exact length and width, select the edge treatment and backing, and wait for your rug to grow up to the exact scale of your dreams. The upside is obvious: zero wasted space and a rug that perfectly frames your furniture. The potential downsides are mostly logistical: longer lead times, a higher price than stock sizes, and the need to measure precisely so you don’t end up with a rug that is either too small or too large for the space.

From a decorating standpoint, custom sizing gives you creative latitude to define zones. If you have a generous lounge area, you can create a generous underfoot cushion between chairs. If you’re pairing a small seating arrangement with a larger open floor space, a custom rug can unify the look without visually dragging the room. The process usually starts with measuring the room and deciding where the rug will live. Some folks measure from wall to wall, others measure from sofa edge to wall, and a few do a hybrid by using painter’s tape to visualize where the rug should land. Our recommendation: bring your measurements to the ordering page with a sketch if helpful. It reduces back-and-forth and makes your life easier when you finally click buy.

### 8x10: the reliable workhorse

The 8x10 rug is a timeless standard for many rooms. It’s large enough to anchor a seating area, but not so large that it swallows the space. If you are furnishing a typical living room or a modestly sized family room, the 8x10 often lands as the sweet spot. The benefits are straightforward: predictable shipping, standard pricing, and fewer variables to juggle compared to a fully custom piece. Of course, the trade-off is that an 8x10 may require additional furniture adjustments to achieve optimal balance. For square rooms, a properly scaled 8x10 rug can create a strong central anchor; in narrow, rectangular spaces, you might prefer a longer, wider or differently proportioned rug to maintain proportional harmony.

If you’re torn between these two options, a quick heuristic helps: measure your main seating area and the space around it. If the rug will sit beneath a sofa, chairs, and a coffee table, and you want all those items to sit comfortably on rug pile, an 8x10 is a great starting point. If your room has unique nooks or an irregular sofa arrangement, go custom to avoid visually cutting off furniture or leaving hard floor visible where there shouldn’t be. And yes, you can still do a hybrid by purchasing an 8x10 and letting it contrast with a smaller side rug in a different texture to define zones. It’s not cheating; it’s interior design with a sense of humor.

## Construction and care: what you need to know

Caring for a Tempo fleece wool rug is not the type of chore that makes you want to swear off domestic life. It’s doable, it’s manageable, and with a light routine you can enjoy its cozy vibe for years. Here’s what to keep in mind.

### Vacuuming and general upkeep

Regular vacuuming is your friend. Use a vacuum with a gentle setting and avoid aggressive beater bars that can pull fibers. When you vacuum, go with the grain of the pile, not against it. This reduces pulling and helps the rug keep its shape. A weekly pass is usually enough for living room rugs, but if the rug is in a high-traffic area or near kids and pets, you may want to vacuum more frequently. Pay attention to corners and edges to prevent debris from maturing into stubborn lumps. If you notice shedding, a light grooming with a soft brush can help the surface look uniform without harming the pile.

### Stains and spills

Prompt blotting is your antidote to big stains. Use a gentle, pH-balanced cleaner or just warm water with a mild soap. Avoid harsh solvents that can affect fibers or backing. For stubborn stains, a professional cleaning once in a while may be the best route. Test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous patch first to ensure colorfastness. If you want to maximize stain resistance, there are protective sprays designed for textile flooring; read the product instructions carefully and follow up with routine cleaning to keep things fresh.

### Odors and ventilation

New rug smells can be strong for the first few days. Good ventilation helps. If you have sensitive noses, airing the rug outdoors for a few hours (on a dry, shaded day) before bringing it inside can help. Just be mindful of moisture and sunlight that might affect colors. Once it’s in place, the fleece wool blend tends to release less odor than some synthetic alternatives, making it a friendlier option for closed spaces with little airflow.

### Cleaning routines and professional care

While light cleaning can be a DIY job, deeper cleaning or situations like pet accidents, heavy staining, or significant odor buildup can require professional rug cleaning. When choosing pros, look for technicians who specialize in wool or fleece textiles, and ask about their process and drying times. If you want to DIY, stick to mild cleaners and always follow up with thorough drying to avoid mold growth.
