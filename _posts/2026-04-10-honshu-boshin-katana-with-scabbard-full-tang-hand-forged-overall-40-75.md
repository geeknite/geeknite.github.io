## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning and oiling

A disciplined maintenance routine pays off with a blade that remains responsive and sharp. After use or handling, wipe down the blade to remove fingerprints and moisture, then apply a light coat of oil. Do not skip this step; the modern environment is unforgiving and humidity is the enemy of carbon steel. A clean blade will reward you with consistent edge retention and a polished surface.

### Storage and handling

Store the blade in a dry environment, ideally in the saya to protect the blade from dust and humidity. If you decide to display it, keep it away from direct sunlight that could degrade the lacquer or fade the handle wrap. Avoid leaving the blade in a rack that could bend the arms of its owners; let us not tempt fate with gravity and a poor mounting.
