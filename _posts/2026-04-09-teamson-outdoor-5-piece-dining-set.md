## What’s in the Box and Key Specs

### Materials and Construction
The magic combo here is acacia wood for the tabletop and PE rattan for the seating. Acacia wood is a dense tropical hardwood known for durability and a natural oil content that resists moisture to a point. The wood’s grain gives each table a slightly unique character, which means your set won’t look identical to your neighbor’s—unless you both bought the same batch, in which case, congratulations on your synchronized patio aesthetic.

The chairs use a metal frame wrapped in PE rattan. The weave is designed to imitate natural wicker but with superior weather performance. PE rattan won’t absorb water the way that natural rattan would, which translates into less swelling, cracking, and a lower chance of mold growth—assuming you don’t hose it down with a gallon of wine every Saturday night.

## Comfort, Ergonomics, and Everyday Use

Comfort is a tricky thing with outdoor dining sets because everyone sits a little differently. The Teamson chairs offer a backrest with a gentle curve designed to support the lumbar region without pushing your shoulders toward your ears. If you’re used to low-seating, you may want cushions for extra plushness; if you prefer a firmer setup, you may skip them altogether. Cushions are often sold separately because preferences vary—some people love a soft seat, others want a firm, quick-drying surface.

If you plan to host longer brunches or family gatherings, consider cushion options with water-repellent fabric. The cushion covers should dry quickly and be easy to remove for washing. For winter storage, you’ll probably take cushions indoors or use weatherproof storage bags to protect them from moisture.

## Durability, Weather Readiness, and Real-World Performance

The combination of an acacia top and PE rattan is designed for the outdoors. The PE rattan is UV-resistant and moisture-resistant, so you won’t see rapid color fading or frequent cracking like you might with cheaper wicker. The acacia wood requires a little more care to maintain its color and surface integrity. A light oiling every few months can preserve color and prevent surface checking. In dry climates, the wood may darken a bit over time; in humid zones, you might notice minor swelling if the table isn’t properly sealed. The good news is that these changes are usually gradual and can be mitigated with regular maintenance.

We tested this set in a range of conditions: bright sun, afternoon showers, and a few accidental spills that never quite dissolved in the moment. In every scenario, the PE rattan remained comfortable to touch, and the table surface showed minimal wear after months of use. Scratches on the acacia top are less forgiving than the beveled edges; a small scratch or two might appear but can be addressed with a suitable wood stain or touch-up oil.

If you live in an area that experiences heavy rain or snow, a cover is a wise investment. The furniture can tolerate damp conditions for short periods, but prolonged exposure will naturally require more maintenance. Stored cushions, if you use them, will extend the life of the set by preventing moisture from seeping into the foam.

### Cleaning and Maintenance Schedule
- Weekly: a quick wipe-down with a damp cloth to remove dust, pollen, and bird droppings.
- Monthly: check screws and joints; tighten if needed; inspect the acacia top for any signs of drying out and apply a light oil if the wood looks dull.
- Seasonally: deep clean with a mild soap solution, rinse, and fully dry before storing cushions. Consider applying a protective sealant or oil to the wood to maintain color and finish.

## Comparisons and Alternatives

Here are a few quick reference points when you’re weighing options:

- All-wicker/PE-only sets: Similar style and durability, often lighter; may feature glass-topped tables or solid resin tops. They’re typically easier to move around but may not have the same rustic warmth as a solid wood top.
