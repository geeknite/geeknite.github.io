### Wool properties

Wool is a living fiber. It breathes, insulates, and resists pilling when cared for properly. It retains warmth in cooler months while remaining comfortable underfoot as temperatures rise. A gentle vacuum on low suction helps maintain the wool’s loft without breaking the fibers.

### Jute properties

Jute adds backbone. It is strong, eco-friendly, and has a rustic texture that people instinctively want to touch. It does not have the longest memory for heavy furniture indentations, so plan on a rug pad and some strategic furniture placement if you know your sofa misses its calling as a footrest for the cat. In balanced spaces, those indentations become features rather than flaws, adding to the rug’s lived-in personality.

## Practicality and Care

Caring for a wool and jute rug is less about science and more about consistent habits. Here are practical steps to keep the weave happy and the colors true.

- Vacuum weekly on low suction to remove grit without pulling fibers. Rotate occasionally to promote even wear.
- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping, extend life, and reduce fatigue on the fibers where you walk the most.
- For spills, blot immediately with a clean cloth. Use a mild soap solution for stains, then blot dry. Avoid harsh solvents or over-wetting the rug.
- In areas of high humidity, ensure good ventilation and consider a dehumidifier if you notice dampness creeping into the fibers. Wool plus moisture can be unkind if mismanaged, so mindfulness is your friend here.

### Durability tips

- If chairs sit on the rug long-term, distribute weight with furniture coasters to minimize indentations.
- For heavy traffic zones, ensure a solid underlay and avoid dragging furniture across the surface where possible.
- Consider professional cleaning every few years if you live in a very dusty or pet-heavy home; this helps maintain fiber integrity without harming the natural finishes.

### Cleaning guidelines

- Treat small marks with a mild detergent and water, then blot dry. Do not rub, as that can fray the fibers.
- Avoid soaking wool; excess moisture can lead to shrinkage or texture loss. If you must, run a gentle test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- If odor accumulates, air the rug outdoors on a dry day—out of direct sun to prevent color fading—and bring it back after a few hours.
