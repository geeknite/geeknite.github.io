## How to Play: Rules Overview

Skip-Bo Junior is designed to be a breezy, easy-to-teach experience that even the most sleep-deprived parent can explain in under a minute. The objective is simple: play all of your cards by placing them onto the four building piles in numerical order. Think of it as a tiny, friendly version of a solitaire puzzle that you play with other people.

- Each turn, you draw cards to fill your hand to a certain number (usually five or six, depending on the rules you adopt for the night).
- You may play cards from your hand or the top of your discard pile onto one of the four building piles, but they must follow the ascending order. For example, if a building pile currently shows a 2, you can place a 3 on top of it, then a 4 on top of that, and so on.
- Wild cards can fill any spot, making your “sequence of glory” a little more forgiving, which is perfect for kids who are still learning counting and order.
- If you can’t play on your turn, you draw cards to refill your hand and pass the turn to the next player.
- The first player to shed all their cards wins the game. It’s fast. It’s furious in a toddler-kind-of-way. It’s adorable chaos.
