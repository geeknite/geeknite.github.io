## Rules at a Glance

### Turn structure: what a typical round feels like
A typical round unfolds in a rhythm that feels oddly musical once you’ve tuned your brain to it. Each player starts with a pool of time tokens and a few action slots on their board. On your turn you perform a sequence of actions that may include drawing cards, playing cards from your hand (each card has a cost in time tokens), and executing one or more “time jumps” that move your tokens forward on a shared clock track. The objective is to maximize your scoring potential while preventing opponents from consolidating too much control over critical regions or time lanes.

What makes the system sing is the cadence: you’ll often be choosing between a safe, incremental gain and a risky play that could either vault you forward or backfire spectacularly. The designers clearly want you to embrace the tension rather than fight it, and the timing of your plays matters as much as the cards you hold.

### Card types and actions: a quick taxonomy
There are three primary card archetypes in the Core Set, each with its own flavor and timing considerations:
- Action cards: your bread and butter. They let you move time forward, deploy resources, or nudge board control in your favor. Every action has a cost in time and a payoff that can ripple across multiple turns.
- Event cards: often game-changing, these cards resemble grand twists you’d expect from a time travel caper. They might alter the rules temporarily, create a new objective, or rearrange the board dynamics in surprising ways.
- Character cards: these are your engines. Each character carries a unique ability that scales with your progression on the clock. The synergy between your character and your action cards is where the real optimization happens, especially as you reach later stages of the game.

The interaction among these types forms the game’s core loop. You draft or play cards into your personal tableau, trigger events that alter the surrounding landscape, and ride the momentum wave as the clock ticks forward. The interplay invites you to adapt on the fly, and that’s where the fun lies.

### Time resources and risk management
Time tokens are the currency that makes the clock go round. You’ll spend them to execute actions, draw more options, or lock in certain advantages. But you’re not the only one spending time — your opponents are too, and timing becomes a strategic weapon. A well-timed push can tip a regional contest, unlock a combo, or derail a rival’s plan when they least expect it.

The risk comes when you try to stretch too thin. If you chase too many future wins without securing present gains, you’ll find your clock hand spinning with only a few minutes left on the board and a stack of unplayed cards that looks ridiculous in retrospect. This is not a game where you can simply “out-resource” your opponent; you must out-think them about when to spend and when to hold back. The Core Set rewards players who can read the room, anticipate timing shifts, and commit to bold plays at the right moment.
