## Calibration and color workflow tips

If you’re pursuing color-critical tasks or want to squeeze every last drop of vibrancy for gaming, a quick calibration pass is worth it. Here’s a practical, no-nonsense approach you can apply right away:

1) Start with a neutral grayscale calibration for consistent brightness and contrast replication across scenes.
2) Then set a target gamma (2.2 is a good baseline for most games and Windows work). Adjust brightness to a comfortable level that preserves detail in dark scenes without washing highlights.
3) Calibrate color using a colorimeter or rely on a known-good preset and minor adjustments to saturation and hue to align with your preferences.
4) Save a specific “Gaming” preset and a separate “Content” preset for when you’re editing or watching videos—G-SYNC or FreeSync should stay enabled to minimize tearing.
