## Ergonomics and usability

This is where the geek magic happens. A 10 person workstation should not be a boulevard of cricks and neck strains. We test keyboard trays, monitor arms, chair compatibility, and desk height adjustability. The good models provide a few stove-pipe levels of height adjustability and a smooth glide in the seat height. If you have tall colleagues, a shared desk requires both anthropometry and a sense of humor. The privacy panels are not just for looks; they reduce ambient noise and provide a focus zone for people who want to avoid distraction without retreating into a private office. We also check lighting: glare on screens is the silent killer of productivity; a good privacy panel configuration helps, but you still want a lighting plan that avoids the dreaded monitor glare.

### Sitting vs standing

An ergonomic dream is to offer sit-stand options without turning the ten-person setup into a slider puzzle. We evaluate whether the desks support electric or manual height adjustment, and whether the sit-stand range covers a comfortable 65 to 125 cm range for different user heights. The result is a dynamic workspace where a developer can lean back with a cup of coffee while a designer stands to sketch. The trick in multi-person setups is to keep the transitional zone consistent; otherwise it becomes a game of musical chairs every 20 minutes. We discuss code languages, design software, and the inevitable debate about whether a sit-stand desk helps with posture or just creates a new kind of sedentary suspense.

## Cable management and electronics

We are not just decorating desks with privacy panels; we are wrestling with cables, power strips, and the occasional dragon of a surge protector. The best units include integrated cable trays, grommets, and a central spine that hides cables in a clean choreography. The reality check is that this is not a single cable run; it is an orchestra. A well designed unit provides easy route for power and data while enabling an electrician to do minimal drama. We test the ability to mount under desk power controllers and to route HDMI or USB-C cables to monitors without creating a tangled sculpture that would please a modern art critic. If your team runs on a coffee ritual, you will appreciate a discreet space for chargers and docking stations.

## Assembly and installation

If you enjoy puzzle games and stacking blocks in a satisfying order, you will enjoy the assembly. The instruction manual is a map; the parts are like little metallic Legos. Expect color-coded screws, a hex key the size of a cocktail sword, and a mental note to measure everything twice. We recommend a helper, a well lit room, and a contingency plan for coffee breaks. The installation time depends on your space, the model you choose, and how enthusiastic you are about aligning panels with laser precision. Pro tip: use painter tape to mark where each leg will stand before you commit to it with bolts.

### Unboxing follow up

The build sequence matters. Start with the long rails, level everything with a spirit level, and do your best to avoid cross-threading. If you can, assemble the core row first, then attach side modules. The last steps are the finishing touches: seat the privacy panels, tighten the last bolts, and run a final cable sweep to ensure nothing looks like a spaghetti sculpture.
