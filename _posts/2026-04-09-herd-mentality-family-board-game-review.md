## What is Herd Mentality?
Herd Mentality is a party game built around the hilarious idea that groups often think alike, but not always in predictable ways. Players take turns drawing a prompt card and guessing the majority answer before revealing the actual chosen answer. The twist? The closest to the majority wins points, unless the herd has misjudged itself, creating giggles, groans, and the most dramatic moral victories since the invention of the family group chat.

## How to Play: The Quick Rundown
- Each round, a player draws a prompt card and secretly writes or states an answer (depending on your house rules) while others attempt to guess the majority answer.
- After all players have submitted, the answers are revealed. The twist is that you don’t get rewarded for being right; you get rewarded for being close to the group consensus, which means you’ll often cheer a surprising outlier if it matches the crowd’s random vibe.
- Points are awarded for closeness to the majority, with bonus points for certain combinations.

The game shines when you have a big table, or a big willingness to ignore etiquette and just go with the silly. The 20 Extra Questions Inc edition adds 20 new prompts, some cheeky, some absurd, and often all of them designed to coax out the family’s inner tidepool of opinions.

## 4-20 Players: The Chaos Multiplier
One of the major selling points is the player count. Herd Mentality is not a two-handed party game; it’s the kind of game you drag out when a family reunion turns into a board game Olympics. At 4 players it’s witty and snappy, but at 8, 12, or 20 players you get something else: a living, yo-yoing chorus of opinions where the loudest voice often doesn’t win, the loudest laugh does.

With 20 players, you’ll need a bit of arrangement to avoid anarchy. A good technique is to assign a “captain vote” per round to streamline the writing phase and avoid twenty scribbles clogging a single card. Alternatively, embrace the chaos: designate two “scribes” who write for groups of five and then rotate.

## Strategy and Tips: How to Maximize the Laughter
- Read the room: The real skill is not knowing the correct answer; it’s predicting what the herd will decide. If your aunt tends to conform to whatever your cousin says, capitalize on that and craft answers that align with her cousins’ predicted responses.
- Use your “sway” wisely: If you know a particular round will involve a tight majority, avoid overthinking—lean into something outrageous that you think will win on sheer absurdity.
- Manage the chaos: For large groups, designate moderators to keep track of turns and ensure everyone gets a chance to write. It’s a team sport, not a free-for-all where the kitty litter box becomes the closest thing to a quiet corner.

Integrating the 20 extra prompts can revitalize a stale game night. They’re designed to be approachable for new players while offering enough misdirection for veteran participants who crave inside jokes and risqué punchlines.

A standard Herd Mentality session is flexible, and the family edition welcomes house rules. Some recommended options:
