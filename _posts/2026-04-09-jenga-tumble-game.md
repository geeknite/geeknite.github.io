## What is Jenga Tumble?
Jenga Tumble is a variant of the classic tower-building game that throws a dynamic spinning or wobbling base into the mix. The core objective remains the same: remove one block at a time from the tower without causing it to fall. But in Jenga Tumble, the ground shifts, the tower drinks a little adrenaline, and you learn very quickly that timing is everything—except in the game where timing is everything and the ground is a chaotic, giggling friend that occasionally forgets to be stable.

From a design standpoint, Jenga Tumble keeps the familiar wooden blocks—these are the same blocks your family has cheered on for decades—plus a base that introduces a kinetic twist. The base might tilt, jostle, or spin in small, unpredictable ways. The result is a game that plays with your perception of space and your risk tolerance. It’s as if Jenga and a carnival ride had a witty, human-friendly baby who refuses to nap.

## How to Play: A Quick Guide
The rules remain approachable for newbies, while still offering a few twists for seasoned Jenga veterans. Here’s the gist:

- Setup: Build the tower as you would for standard Jenga. Ensure the base is on a firm surface and that you’re in a comfortable distance from the tower so you don’t accidentally hit it with a elbow or exuberantly celebratory fist.
- Basic turn: On your turn, you remove one block from anywhere below the highest completed level. You then place that block on top, continuing the tower’s ascent.
- Base mechanics: When a block is removed and placed, the base might shift slightly. This can cause nearby blocks to settle unpredictably, creating new opportunities and new risks. The exact nature of the base’s “movement” is not strictly deterministic; it’s meant to be fun, not a physics experiment.
- The goal: Avoid causing the tower to crash in a dramatic, woodshattering fashion. The player who avoids the tumble while others fail is the winner.

What makes Jenga Tumble special is not the goal itself, but the moments when you think you have a safe pull, only to watch the base deliver a tiny earthquake and watch your block hold on for dear life—or watch it fly off in a perfectly timed arc toward the dog’s chair leg. It’s all part of the strategy: sometimes you pull the block you know you shouldn’t, because the consequence is unpredictable in the most entertaining way.
