## Dosing for a 20 kg dog: the math (without a nightmare)
Label dosing generally follows a rule of thumb: 1 tablet per 10 kg of body weight. For a 20 kg dog, that’s about 2 tablets per dose. In real life, several factors can nudge that number: age, health status, concurrent meds, and whether your pup has had worms recently. So, while 2 tablets is the baseline, veterinary guidance is important for precise, safe administration.

If you’re dosing multiple dogs, you can adapt the approach: keep a little dosing calendar and set reminders. A simple example schedule could be:
- Day 0: Dog A gets 2 tablets
- Day 14: Dog B gets 2 tablets
- Day 28: Both dogs get their next dose
The key is consistency and labeled dosing to avoid the classic “where did the pills go?” moment at 6 PM when the dinner chaos begins.

### Administering with food or without
Most dogs swallow pills best when tucked into something tasty. You can hide the tablet in a peanut butter glob, a sausage slice, or a specially designed pill pocket. Some dogs are wily opponents of pill administration and will stage an elaborate avoidance routine. If you have a pill-averse pup, you might try:
- Crushing the tablet (only if allowed by the label) and mixing with a strong-flavored wet food.
- Using a pill-pocket treat designed to disguise medicinal tablets.
- Engaging a little “positive reinforcement convoy”: a short game, a small treat, then the reward.

Palatability and acceptance vary by dog. A little patience goes a long way, and a good reward system can turn pill time from a battle to a quick, peaceful routine.

## Safety, storage, and responsible usage

- Do not exceed the recommended dose without veterinary guidance.
- Monitor for adverse reactions: vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, appetite changes, or unusual behavior. If you notice anything serious, contact a veterinary professional immediately.
- Store in a cool, dry place, away from children and curious paws. A dedicated cabinet with a small lock is a surprisingly good investment for peace of mind.

## The nerdy shopping guide: choosing a dewormer for your dog
When choosing a dewormer for a dog around the 20 kg mark, consider these factors:
- Parasite risk in your area: hookworms and roundworms are common in many regions; tapeworm prevalence varies by locale and lifestyle.
- Dog health status: puppies, elderly dogs, or dogs with liver/kidney conditions may require adjusted dosing or alternative regimens.
- Administration preference: tablets, chewables, or liquids; some dogs prefer pills hidden in food, others tolerate only direct administration.
- Veterinary oversight: a vet visit helps confirm the right drug, dose, and schedule, especially if your dog has concurrent medications.
