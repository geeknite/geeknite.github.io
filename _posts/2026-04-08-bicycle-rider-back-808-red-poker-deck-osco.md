## Care, Storage, and Display
### Protecting your 808 Red from the ravages of time
Vintage decks thrive on controlled environments. The first rule is to avoid direct sunlight which can fade red tones and warm up the storage area, causing potential stock issues. Store in a cool, dry place with minimal humidity to protect the edges and prevent warping. A simple silica gel sachet in the storage box can help with long term preservation. If you like to display your deck, consider a clear acrylic case that protects from dust while still letting the iconic Rider Back shine through.

### Handling tips for longevity
When you actually handle the deck, use clean hands and a relaxed grip to preserve the card edges. Consider using a cut for practice that favors fan-friendly decks and avoid brute force shuffles that might wear the stock prematurely. The aim is to enjoy the aesthetic while maintaining structural integrity so that you can savor both the looks and the feel of the cards for the next game night or trick practice session.
