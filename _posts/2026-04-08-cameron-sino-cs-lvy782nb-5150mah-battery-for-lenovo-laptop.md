### Battery health and longevity tips
- Calibrate after initial use: run the battery from 100 down to about 5 percent, then recharge to 100 percent to help Windows or your OS calibrate charge reporting.
- Avoid full discharges frequently. Try to keep the battery between 20 and 80 percent when possible.
- Store properly if you will not use the laptop for an extended period. A storage charge around 50 percent in a cool, dry place is a good rule of thumb.
- Use the Lenovo battery reporting tools or third party software to keep an eye on health indicators and cycles.

## Maintenance and care
### Best practices for longevity
- Use a reputable power adapter that matches your laptop input requirements. Mismatched adapters can stress the battery and cause incorrect charging behavior.
- Avoid leaving the device plugged in at 100 percent for extended periods if you are not actively using it. Unplugging after a full charge occasionally can help extend lifespan.
- Keep the laptop at moderate ambient temperatures. High heat is the enemy of lithium ion chemistry, and scorching your battery bay is not helpful for longevity.
- Regularly update BIOS and power management drivers. A well-tuned system reports battery health more accurately and can squeeze more life out of your pack.

### Battery care beyond the cable
If you are switching from OEM to Cameron Sino, allow the OS to re-learn the battery’s behavior with a few cycles. Battery reporting can take a little time to settle in, but after a handful of calibrations you should see stable runtime estimates. For most users, a simple cycle of full charge to full discharge every few months is more than enough to keep the system honest about its remaining run time.
