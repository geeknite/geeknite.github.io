## Maintenance, Chemistry, and Keeping the Water Swim-Ready
### Routine care you can actually keep up with
- Check water level and pump operation daily during peak season.
- Test chemical levels at least twice a week: pH, alkalinity, chlorine or sanitizer, and salt levels if you’re going salt-water adjacent.
- Clean the filter according to manufacturer guidelines; dirty filters reduce circulation and the “wow” factor of your glittering water.
- Brush the walls and corners to remove algae and scale buildup; we recommend a soft-bristled pool brush to avoid scratching the liner.
- Remove debris with a skimmer net; dramatic sunflowers in the yard are not actual debris, but they may float by looking like conga lines.

### Common issues and simple fixes
- Cloudy water: check for imbalanced chemicals and consider a filter backwash or a cartridge replacement if needed.
- Leaks: inspect the liner edges and fittings; small leaks can often be tightened or resealed with proper pool sealant.
- Algae: shock treatment and a bit more circulation can usually rescue you from the green apocalypse. You’ll thank us later when you’re not wearing swim goggles to see algae trying to photobomb your photos.

### Safety basics you should not skip
- Install and use a sturdy pool ladder with anti-slip steps; ensure it is placed on a stable, level surface.
- Keep a safety cover when the pool is not in use to prevent curious pets and accidental naps near the edge.
- Always supervise children; above-ground pools can lure the most enthusiastic splashers into a false sense of security.
- Ensure proper fencing or barrier requirements comply with local codes where applicable. Backyard rules and HOA quirks are real; read your local rules so you don’t audition for a legal drama at the city council.

## Rectangular vs Round: The Geek Debate of the Ages
### When to pick rectangular
- You want efficient use of space and a deck-friendly layout.
- You envision lounging areas around the pool with straight lines and right angles that optimize seating.
- You appreciate predictable water surface area for games and activities.

### When to pick round
- You crave classic pool aesthetics and easier geometry for beginners.
- You want potentially quicker setup due to fewer corners to align, and you don’t mind the circular flow of the water around a central point.
- You appreciate round pools for space efficiency in some yard shapes, but not all rectangulars lose to round in the physics of water.
