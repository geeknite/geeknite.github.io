## Care and Maintenance
### Routine Cleaning
Vacuuming regularly is your friend. A simple weekly pass keeps dust and pet hair at bay and preserves the rug’s surface texture. For everyday dirt, a quick blot with a damp cloth works wonders. Avoid harsh scrubbing; you’ll just flatten the fibers and invite a moodier pile to set up shop. If you have kids or pets, consider a gentle blotting schedule for spills—think of it as a tiny spa retreat for stains.

### Stain Removal and Quick Fixes
Spills happen, especially when you are cooking, snacking, or attempting a fancy latte art at 7 am. The key is to act fast but calmly. Blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth, use a mild detergent solution if needed, and rinse with clean water. The synthetic blend handles this well, but always test on a small, inconspicuous area first to avoid any color surprises. For stubborn stains, a gentle carpet cleaner can be employed, but patch tests are your best friend.

### Sunlight and Color Longevity
Color fade is the quiet enemy of any rug. The Kendall Oatmeal is relatively forgiving, but prolonged sun exposure can lighten the hues over time. Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear and color distribution. If your space basks in glorious daylight, you might consider UV-blocking curtains for the sake of the rug’s long-term health and your future Instagram grid aesthetics.

### Maintenance Myths Debunked
Myth: You must professionally clean your rug every six months. Truth: If your space is a zoo and your life is a sitcom, a yearly professional cleanse is sensible, but not mandatory. Myth: You can machine wash this rug. Reality: Not recommended. The synthetic weave is designed for ground-level life, not a spin cycle in the machine that promises to liberate you from chores but returns with the weight of a thousand lost socks.
