## Materials, weather resistance, and care
This set’s weather resistance hinges on the quality of the frame finishing and the cushion fabric. The metal surfaces are typically powder-coated to resist rust, fade, and chalking. The cushions, being made with outdoor fabrics, are designed to dry quickly after a rainstorm, though you’ll still want to store cushions or use protective cover during heavy rainfall or winter months. A simple annual wipe-down with mild detergent and water is usually sufficient; always dry completely before storing.

A word on condensation and moisture: the trick to longevity is to prevent moisture from seeping into the foam core. If you leave cushions damp, you risk mold and mildew, which is unwelcome even to the most enthusiastic backyard gaming group. Removable covers are ideal for washing; if your set uses zippers, ensure you dry them after washing to avoid rust on the zipper teeth.

Seasonal maintenance matters for those who want their furniture to last more than a couple of summers. If you live in a humid climate, consider storing cushions in a dry place during the wet months or investing in breathable, moisture‑resistant covers. For the frame, a periodic wipe with a mild cleaner and a quick inspection for loose screws will help preserve stability and aesthetics. A light re‑application of a protective spray recommended by the manufacturer can keep the wicker or resin from losing its sheen over time.
