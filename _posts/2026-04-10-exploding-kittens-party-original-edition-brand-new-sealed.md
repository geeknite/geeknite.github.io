The party edition narrows the focus to quick rounds with lots of chaos, rather than marathon epic battles that stretch until someone forgets what the original goal was. ### Core mechanics

- Exploding Kitten cards: If you draw one and you don’t have a Defuse, you are out of the round. It is the kind of doom that lurks quietly at the bottom of a deck where a mischievous cat has buried its tiny fangs of doom.
- Defuse cards: These are the lifesaver cards that give you another round of life if you pull the bad kitty. They can be used on your own explosion or, in some variants, as part of a twist involving the future or other mischievous tricks.
- Action cards: Attack, Skip, Shuffle, See the Future, and others let you poke at your friends or dodge disaster. The party edition tends to lean into rapid-fire turns, so expect buzzes and groans in near unison as players chain effects like a rowdy rhythm section.

## Strategy and tips for maximum chaos

- When you see a Defuse in hand, treat it like a slip of fate that could save your night. Don’t waste it on a round you’re already winning; save it for the moment when the knock-knock joke of fate arrives and it needs one last punch.
- Timing is everything. If you can force the next player to draw at a critical moment in the round, you not only push them closer to disaster but you also extend your own reign of glorious chaos.
- Pay attention to table dynamics. A room where everyone is giggling at memes will produce more dramatic reactions than a room where everyone is counting their points. Embrace the chaos; you’ll thank yourself when the last card reveals a dramatic twist you predicted half a move too late.
- Know your audience. If grandma is at the table, you might want to keep the intensity moderate, but if your college roommate who owns more gaming apparel than functional social skills is present, prepare for a speedrun of hilarity and near misses.
