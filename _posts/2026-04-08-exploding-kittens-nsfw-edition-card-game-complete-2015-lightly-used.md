## Overview

Exploding Kittens NSFW Edition is a fast, silly, push-your-friends-to-the-edge-of-awkwardness party game. The core idea is simple: players take turns drawing cards until someone hits an Exploding Kitten card and then tries to avoid blowing up. The NSFW twist adds raunchy humor, spicy one-liners, and a more adult tone in its art and card text. Rules are intentionally lightweight, which makes it accessible for gatherings where people arrived with a pizza, a soda, and a questionable playlist. The game is short, often finishing in 15 to 30 minutes, which makes it perfect for back-to-back rounds or as a palate cleanser between heavier titles.

## How to play: a fast, furious guide

- Setup takes about 1 to 2 minutes. Shuffle the deck, deal a hand to each player, place the Exploding Kitten cards in the deck, and you re good to go. In a lightly used scenario, you ll want to separate any damaged Defuse cards you discover during inspection.
- On your turn, you draw a card. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you re out unless you have a Defuse card in your hand. The Defuse card gives you a chance to defuse the explosive moment and re insert the Kitten back into the deck in a position of your choosing. It s basically a “not today, panic button” moment that makes a tense moment feel comedic rather than catastrophic.
- You can play action cards to affect other players or the state of the deck. The NSFW Edition introduces some extra taunts and nudge cards that encourage players to bluff, tease, or swap cards in ways that increase the table talk—sometimes charming, sometimes chaotic, usually loud.
- The round ends when the last kit has exploded and everyone else is left to discuss the moral implications of a cat with a cartoon bomb on its head.

## Lightly used condition: what to check before you cash in your giggles

Buying used card games is a small gamble with potential big payoff. With standard card wear, a lightly used NSFW Edition should still be playable and deliver the same comedic payoff as a brand-new copy. Here are practical checks:

- Card edges: look for curling, whitening, or significant edge wear. A few lift marks are fine; deep scratches can cause cards to catch on each other and ruin the draw.
- Ink quality: ensure there s no fading or blotching that obscures text. If the joke cards are illegible, the humor is lost and the game becomes a silent, awkward mismatch.
- Defuse cards: confirm there are enough Defuse cards in the deck. A missing or miscounted Defuse card can tilt the game into endless stalemates or early eliminations.
- Box integrity: a sturdy box is a sign of good storage; a cracked lid or loose insert means cards may take beading with the box s wanderings, which adds to the chaos but not in a good way.
- Completeness: verify that you have the full deck plus any NSFW promos included. Some used copies may miss promo cards that increase uplift and laughter in later rounds.

If all checks are green, you re likely looking at a fantastic value: a certified way to bring a raucous session without paying full price for a new copy. If you find a slightly higher price due to rarity or condition, it still might be worth it given the price-to-fun ratio you re likely to achieve across a handful of game nights.
