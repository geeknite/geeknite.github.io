## Workflow Tips: Getting the Best Out of Your Scans

- Calibrate color profiles early: A quick calibration at the start of your project saves you hours of minor color tweaking later. If you have a calibrated monitor, align your scans with display-referred workflows to minimize surprises.
- Clean often but not obsessively: Dust and scratches are the true antagonists here. The Digital ICE on film helps, but you still want to wipe down film and glass while the device is powered down and unplugged.
- Batch processing is your ally: Use the batch scanning feature to load a stack of photos or negatives and process them with consistent settings. This reduces the temptation to fine-tune every single image in a long project.
- Export with embedded profiles: When you export, embed color profiles to maintain fidelity across devices and when printing. This reduces the risk of color drift when the files leave your editing suite.
- Backups are a myth you can prove: Archival scans are your digital safety net. Create redundant backups and keep the originals if possible. The V850 Pro can facilitate high-quality digital copies that stand the test of time, but the human eye tends to forget to back up, so do not forget to back up in triplicate.
