## What City of Mist actually is (in case you’re new)
City of Mist posits a city—think of it as a living, breathing noir montage—where ordinary people discover that legends walk the streets in human skin. Your investigators are not heroes in capes; they are legends wearing masks. The world is not just haunted by monsters; it is made of myths that surface when the right Tags collide with courage, guilt, or a stubborn belief in something bigger than yourself. The Core Book PDF acts as your gateway into a system that trades D&D’s dungeon-stomping bravado for a mythic storytelling engine that thrives on atmosphere, theme, and your ability to leverage “tags” to shape outcomes.

The PDF leans into mood as much as method. It gives you a toolkit that can power a single one-shot about a masked culprit or spin up a sprawling campaign where the city itself is a character with a heartbreakingly fragile ego. In practice, you’ll see a structure that rewards collaboration between GM and players: ideas, scenes, and quotes become assets you call on, rather than random numbers raining down on your dice. If you like your RPGs with a little poetry and a lot of neon rain, City of Mist has your back—and your mask.

## Core mechanics (the heart of the system, in plain English)
City of Mist runs on a few clear ideas that you can summarize on a sticky note: masks, powers, and drives drive your actions; the city acts as a soul mirror that amplifies your character’s contradictions; and the outcomes of scenes are shaped by narrative tags rather than raw numeric power alone. If you’ve ever wanted a system where you can say, “My character uses the Mask to become the city’s ghost in the room,” and have the dice support that, you’ve found your people.

### The triad: Power, Mask, Drive
- Power: the capabilities that let you affect the world. It’s the “what you are” in the mythic sense. The Core Book PDF devotes generous space to the kinds of power you can wield and how it evolves as you lean into the story you want to tell.
- Mask: your secret identity and public persona. The Mask is where the existential tension lives. The PDF’s rules for masks give you mechanics to switch between personas, with consequences that ripple through scenes and consequences that feel real in the city’s glow.
- Drive: what pushes you forward, often with a stubborn, personal motive. Drives explain why your character does the things they do and how the story will twist when the city tests them. The PDF uses Drives to fuel both drama and character progression, which is where the game’s emotional punch comes from.

The synergy of these three elements creates scenes where characters must negotiate compromises between who they are, who they pretend to be, and what the city demands of them. The PDF gives you tools to choreograph those conflicts without bogging down players in endless stat arithmetic.

### Tags and dice: the engine underneath
The game uses a tag-based system to shape outcomes, not a rigid attribute ladder. In practice, you describe a task using tags such as “Underworld connection,” “Gritty streetwise,” or “Mythic resilience.” The GM (or the rules equivalent) then sets a difficulty and calls for a roll that fits the task’s vibe. The result is less about “did I succeed” in a vacuum and more about “what story do we tell now?” The Core Book PDF does a solid job of explaining how tag-based checks function, how many dice you roll, and how to interpret results with narrative consequences. It’s a workflow that rewards players for being imaginative with their phrasing and for leaning into the game’s style rather than grinding for numerical perfection.

### Action sequences vs investigation: a pace you can modulate
City of Mist shines when you mix noir investigative beats with mythic set-piece moments. The PDF helps you balance the calm interrogation scenes with the city-smack of a breaking myth. You can spend a scene on careful clue gathering and moral ambiguity, or you can pivot to a dynamic chase through a rain-slick alley where the city itself seems to twist around the chase. The book provides guidelines for pacing, session length, and scene goals, which makes it easier for the GM to manage tone across a multi-session arc.

## Quick start tips for your first session
- Start with a small, contained mystery connected to a city myth. This gives players something tangible to hold onto as they wrestle with their masks.
- Encourage players to describe how their masks alter their perception of a scene. The book rewards imaginative phrasing; allow a few beats of narration that show how myth seeps into the ordinary.
- Use the city’s myths as recurring threads. Let a single symbol or legend reappear in different neighborhoods, creating connective tissue across sessions.
- Keep a balance between investigation and scene spectacle. The city is happiest when you mix reflective talk with a moment of mythic revelation.
- Don’t forget the Drive. It’s the emotional engine of your character and the story a lot of your dramatic pivots will come from.
