## Maintenance and Care
Like any mechanical device that sees regular action, your foosball table benefits from a light maintenance routine:
- Regularly wipe the playfield with a soft, dry cloth to keep grit from drying into the surface.
- Periodically lubricate the rod bearings with a light machine oil designed for metal components. A tiny amount goes a long way; over-lubrication can attract dust and create a slippery mess during play.
- Inspect the leg levelers and tightening screws after a few heavy-use sessions. A loose screw can turn a championship bout into slapstick theater in no time.
- Keep players’ hands clean and dry. Sweat and oil on handles can affect grip and throw off accuracy.
- Store the balls in a dry place when not in use to prevent warping or minor deformation.

If you’re worried about spills or accidental damage, consider placing a protective mat under the table to guard against carpet-shredding kicks or beverage misadventures. In Geeknite fashion, you’ll appreciate the practical approach: protect the investment, respect the game, and enjoy the ride.
