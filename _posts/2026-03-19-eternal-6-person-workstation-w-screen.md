### Ergonomics and comfort

Ergonomics is the quiet hero of any desk review, and the Eternal does not shout about ergonomics. It delivers it in the form of adjustable chairs, a comfortable vertical range for the two workstations on each side, and a wide central field that invites collaboration without the need to shout across a long desk. A few practical tips to optimize comfort:

- Ensure each user has a chair with adjustable seat height and backrest tilt; the two teams must not fight over the same lumbar support.
- Try a 90 to 110 degree seating angle; this helps reduce fatigue during long analysis sessions and makes standups feel less like a sprint to the finish line.
- Use the central W Screen to reduce neck strain by centralizing the main visual source; this helps keep eyes from wandering into the room's lights and into the copy machine's endless cycles.

In practice, we found that the ergonomic experience depends heavily on chair choice and desk height calibration. The two banks of workstations give enough space for two 24 inch or 27 inch monitors per user while maintaining comfortable elbow room. It is not a magic fix for all neck stiffness, but it makes the day more tolerable than a cold coffee on a Monday morning.

### Tech integration and W Screen magic

The star of the show is the W Screen—an expansive central display that acts as the brain of the setup. You can configure this screen as a shared workspace for dashboards, design references, or a giant scoreboard for your internal hackathon. In our testing, the W Screen proved flexible enough to support four 4K streams concurrently when the GPU is feeling frisky, although this can demand a robust GPU, a strong power supply, and a person who knows how to manage fan curves without turning the room into a wind tunnel.

Pairing the W Screen with standard office PCs works best when you have a proper mounting system. We recommend a dedicated power strip with surge protection rated for data lines and a small UPS if you want to survive blackout-induced panic during a sprint to 5 pm. Cable management becomes even more essential; you will want to route HDMI or DisplayPort lines cleanly under the desk trough and label them clearly to avoid a scavenger hunt every time a user moves a chair. The result is a visually impressive workstation where the shared screen fosters collaboration rather than chaos.

### Space planning and layout ideas

With a desk this size, room planning matters. The recommended layout places two six-person banks facing each other with the central W Screen bridging the two, like a monorail in a starship. This arrangement helps to maintain visual contact without forcing someone to shout across a 5.4 meter expanse.

Practical tips:
- Reserve a service aisle at least 900 mm wide for accessibility and flanking chairs
- Use a cable trough with modular dividers to separate power, data, and display lines
- Position the primary doorway away from the central mass to minimize foot traffic disruptions during presentations
- If you want to keep a hint of personal space, assign a small corner to each user with a plant or a tiny desktop ornament
