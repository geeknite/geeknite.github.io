## Cleaning and Maintenance

Let’s be honest: a rug isn’t a cape for every mess. It will require some maintenance, especially if you have kids, pets, or a weekly board game night that involves snack crashes and beverage hazards. The recommended care steps are straightforward:
- Vacuum regularly to remove dust and hair. Use a setting appropriate for the pile to avoid loosening fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color distribution, especially if you have sunlight streaming through one side of the room.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. For more stubborn stains, test mild detergents on a hidden corner before applying them widely.
- Use a rug pad for floor protection and to reduce movement under chairs and foot traffic.

In practice, keeping a cleaning routine modest but consistent will extend the rug’s life and maintain its visual appeal. The goal isn’t perfection but a clean, comfortable floor that still looks good after a long day of screen glare and snack piracy.

## Care Tips for Longevity
- Rotate and flip the rug to promote even wear and prevent sun-bleaching asymmetry.
- Use a rug pad to protect both flooring and rug fibers.
- Vacuum with the grain of the rug and avoid aggressive agitation on the fibers to prevent pilling.
- For spills, blot, don’t rub, and treat with a mild cleaner suitable for synthetic fibers if needed.
- Store or roll the rug properly when not in use to minimize creasing and maintain its shape for future life chapters.
