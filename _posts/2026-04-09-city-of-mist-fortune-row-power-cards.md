## What Fortune Row - Power Cards Actually Is
Fortune Row is a deck of 60 or so Power Cards tailored for use with the City of Mist core rules. Each card offers a new way to leverage your Mask, pull off a dramatic moment, or bend the rules in the direction of narrative emphasis. Some cards feel like elegant, tight little extensions of the core mechanics; others punch in with a surprising twist that shakes up a tense scene and makes players reevaluate what counts as a successful consequence.

### Card types and examples
- Narrative Edge Cards: These flip the scene’s focus, turning a quiet moment into a thunderclap of character revelation.
- Power Modifiers: Cards that modify how you resolve a move or how your Power interacts with a particular Truth or Mythos in the city.
- Fateful Triggers: When you trigger this card, you move into a new beat with a consequence that echoes through the rest of the session.
- Complication Cards: Introduce new obstacles, but often with a price that makes players rethink their alliances and plans.

Each card has a compact, evocative title and a few lines of text that specify the effect, its cost, and its narrative guardrails. The writing in Fortune Row leans into City of Mist’s noir-romantic vibe: bitter coffee, neon glow, and the sense that every hero exists in the sticky underside of a city that never sleeps—only blinks really slowly and suspects everyone of being a monster in disguise.

### How the deck is structured
Fortune Row isn’t a random grab-bag; it’s a curated experience designed to slot into the existing rhythm of City of Mist sessions. The card text often gives you a trigger or a condition tied to your Masks, your Relationships, or your ongoing investigations. It’s not meant to replace the core play flow but to intensify it—the way a good side character can steal the scene from the protagonist for a moment, before you snap back to the main narrative thread.

## Components and Setup
Like any respectable expansion, Fortune Row comes with a boxy handful of components that feel premium without being pretentious. The cards themselves feature art that sits somewhere between comic book splash pages and cinema stills, with bold color blocking and hand-drawn textures that nod to the game’s pen-and-paper roots.

### Cards, art, and layout
- Type: Power Card deck with a consistent border and an action-oriented title.
- Text: Short, punchy instructions that tease a consequence or a conditional advantage.
- Art style: Neon gleam, street-level grit, and a subtle nod to mythic iconography (seriously, some cards feel like a mural they would have painted in the city’s old theater district).

The card backs keep things clean and uniform, which helps when you’re rifling through the deck mid-scene and don’t want to reveal who has the most dramatic glare. The production values are solid, and in a game where mood and storytelling matter as much as mechanics, Fortune Row’s visual language sells the vibe with minimal font gymnastics.

### Setup tips and streamlining
- Shuffle thoroughly and keep outputs visible to all players; the nature of Power Cards rewards quick, collaborative interpretation.
- Create a small local ritual for “draws” so you don’t accidentally glance during a tense moment and spoil the turn’s surprise. A quick fan-flick of the wrist is enough to signal a new beat without breaking immersion.
- Pair cards with specific Masks for easier recall. If you’re playing a Mask that focuses on manipulation, keep a couple of Control cards handy to reinforce the theme.

If you’re already comfortable with the base system, Fortune Row slides into your rotation with minimal friction. The key is to allow the deck to influence narrative tempo rather than overshadow player agency. When a card turns a tense investigation into a revelation that reframes the whole mystery, you’ll know Fortune Row was money well spent.

## Mechanics: How It Feels at the Table
Fortune Row doesn’t rewrite the core engine of City of Mist; it extends it in delightful ways. Expect tighter ties between fate, power, and narrative consequence, with several cards explicitly calling for players to leverage their Masks in new, sometimes surprising ways. This can create a feedback loop that pushes players toward more dramatic, movie-like reveals—great for sessions that lean into cinematic vibe, less ideal for groups that prefer a purely procedural campaign.

### Turn flow with Fortune Row
- Draw a Power Card at a set point in the round (often after a successful action or a moral choice moment).
- Resolve the card’s effect; some require expenditure of a resource, others pivot the scene’s comparisons from “Did you succeed?” to “What does this failure reveal about you?”
- Integrate the card’s consequence with ongoing Masks and Relationships to weave a thicker, more entangled mythos.

If you enjoy a game that rewards clever line-work and-behind-the-curtain moral choices—where a single card can bend how a Truth is interpreted—Fortune Row shines. If you prefer a more mechanical, strictly combat-focused table, you’ll still get flavor, but you might end up passing on some of the heavier text on the cards or using them as flavor tokens rather than active mechanisms.

## Thematic Flavor and Artwork
City of Mist is all about mythic urban mythos cloaked in noir. Fortune Row amplifies this by offering power moments that feel like chapters in a detective’s notebook. The cards give players a tangible sense that their character’s destiny is being written in heavy ink by the city’s own hand. The artwork—bold, slightly rough, with a dash of neon—brings to life the sense that you’re peering into a mural that moans softly whenever someone nearby whispers a lie.

From a flavor perspective, Fortune Row succeeds brilliantly. It helps players lean into the idea that the city is a living, breathing organ—pumping secrets through its veins, and the players are the doctors, applying the right diagnosis when the symptoms show up in the form of a card text. The rarely-stretched rule references feel like a wink to the audience rather than a course correction, and the best cards tend to serve as narrative accelerants rather than simple modifiers.

Artwork wise, the cards sit comfortably with the core art style, avoiding a jarring departure in tone or color. The designers clearly respected the game’s mood while still giving the expansion its own moment to shine.

## For Who This Is Worth It
Fortune Row isn’t a must-have for every city-dweller with a cape, but it’s a solid upgrade for specific playgroups:
- If your table loves mythic noir with a twist, Fortune Row will feel essential rather than optional.
- If you want a pool of narrative prompts that you can easily drop into sessions to spark dramatic moments, this is a reliable set.
- If you’re more of a “lean storytelling with random tables” group, you may want to sample a few cards at a time rather than the whole deck, to avoid overwhelming the session’s structure.

On the other hand, if your group is strictly rule-following, or if you prefer long-running investigations that stay relentlessly procedural, Fortune Row can feel like a shiny distraction. It’s not a “stale bread” mechanic by any stretch, but its emphasis on moments and narrative re-framing might not align with players who enjoy grinding through complex, mechanical optimization.

## Play Style and Strategy Tips
- Play to the card’s strength: If a Fortune Row card nudges a dramatic revelation, plan your actions to maximize the payoff. Don’t chase the card’s micro-effect if it breaks your broader objective; instead, lean into the moment it promises to unlock.
- Build around your Mask’s identity: Choose cards that echo your character’s truth. A Mask built on deception benefits from Complication and Trigger cards that reward clever misdirection.
- Use Relationship hooks: Fortune Row thrives when you weave NPCs into the stakes. Cards that force a relationship tie can turn a single scene into a turning point for several arcs.
- Balance risk and reward: Some cards demand an expenditure or carry a cost. In City of Mist, the thrill often comes from paying a price for a big beat. Use Fortune Row to reward daring play, not to punish cautious plans out of habit.
- Reserve a couple of “clean” cards for late-game moments: If the session is tilting toward a climactic reveal, a few straightforward, low-risk cards can keep momentum without derailing suspense.

## Comparisons: Fortune Row vs Other Decks in City of Mist
Fortune Row complements other decks by offering a more narrative-forward experience. Compare to the base card sets or other expansions that focus on power economy or combat optimization, Fortune Row tends to reward storytelling choices more than raw mechanical optimization. The balance shifts depending on your table’s preferences; some groups prefer Fortune Row’s vibe, while others might rotate in a different expansion for a change of pace. If you enjoy the City of Mist system but want more opportunities to turn a character moment into a city-wide revelation, Fortune Row is one of the best fits in the current ecosystem.

## Accessibility and Replayability
Since the deck introduces a finite pool of prompts and effects, replayability comes through the variety of Masks and Relationships you bring to the table. It’s not unlimited, but it’s enough to keep the early sessions fresh. The rules references are straightforward enough that new players can jump in without a steep learning curve, though the drafting of a few cards will require some reading. If your group tends to run the same kind of scenes, a rotation of Fortune Row cards will refresh your campaigns without needing new content every year.

## How to Run a Fortune Row Session (Quick Tips)
- Start with a short prologue: a single scene that primes players for a major reveal to make card triggers feel earned.
- Have a “card bucket” ready: a quick reference set you can pull from if the table wants an off-the-cuff narrative push without paging through the entire deck.
- Use a shared capture pad for Truths and Relationships: the faster you track connections, the faster the cards will weave back into the session’s fabric.
- Schedule a mid-session cliffhanger: Fortune Row shines when it creates a turn that makes players reconsider every decision they’ve made so far.
- Debrief after the session: which cards sparked the best moments? What kinds of triggers felt most thematically resonant? Use this feedback to tailor future Fortune Row runs.
