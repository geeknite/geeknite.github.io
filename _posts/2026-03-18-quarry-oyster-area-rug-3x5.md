## Size, Fit, and Placement

### Why 3x5?
The 3x5 size is the most versatile footprint for small to medium rooms. It defines a seating area in front of a sofa, creates a zone in a den, or anchors a reading nook in a bedroom. It’s large enough to hold a coffee table and cushions, but not so large that you feel like you’re performing geometry in a tiny planet. In a larger living room, you can pair two of these in an L-shape or layer one over a larger rug for that “designed-by-a-human-who-reads floor-plan PDFs” look.

### How to place it in different rooms
- Living room: Center the rug under the front legs of your sofa and chairs to create a unified anchor. If your furniture floats in a room, place the rug to connect the seating group visually.
- Bedroom: Position the rug at the foot of the bed or under the front third to provide a soft landing spot when you step out of bed.
- Home office: Define your workspace by placing the rug under your desk and chair, letting you roll your chair around without the carpet eating your legs.

### Do you need a rug pad?
Yes. A rug pad is worth its weight in keeping that cozy underfoot feeling and preventing slippage. Look for a pad rated for your rug’s pile height and room usage. A good rug pad reduces wear, keeps the rug stable on slippery floors, and helps with sound insulation—a small win for anyone who hates the sound of toes clicking on hardwood when they walk barefoot after midnight.

## Care, Cleaning, and Maintenance

### Routine care
- Vacuum regularly to remove surface dust and grit. Use a vacuum with a gentle height setting to avoid pulling fibers.
- Rotate every few months to ensure even wear. This is especially relevant in high-traffic zones or rooms with strong sun exposure.
- Protect from direct sunlight to maintain color fidelity over time. If your space faces a bright window, consider light curtains or blinds to reduce fading.

### Spill and stain guide
Spills happen. The key is to address them quickly. Blot (don’t rub) with a clean, dry cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible. Then, dab with a mild detergent solution or a carpet-safe stain remover following the product instructions. Rinse with a little water on a clean cloth and blot again. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade fibers and colors.

For stubborn stains, consider a professional cleaning every 12-18 months, depending on foot traffic and household contaminants. A quick online search for “rug cleaning near me” can help you find a service with decent reviews and a kindness toward pets and people who spill coffee just to test your reflexes.

### Routine-whenever tips
- Flip the rug occasionally to even out wear. This is especially helpful if one corner sits under a heavy sofa.
- Keep pet nails trimmed to minimize snags. If you have curious puppies or kittens that love to knead the rug, a good fiber choice helps, but a little extra care still goes a long way.
- Use furniture coasters to distribute weight and prevent pattern imprints or denting.

## Comparisons and Alternatives

### How does it stack against similar rugs?
- Against a high-back tufted rug: The Quarry Oyster trades heavy plushness for easier care and steadier performance in high-traffic zones. If you want cloud-soft underfoot, you might prefer a higher pile, but you’ll pay more and vacuum more often.
- Against a flatweave: It’s got more texture than a flatweave and better drape than a short-pile flatweave. If you want a crisp, modern outline with minimal bounce, a flatweave could be a competitor, but you’ll sacrifice some comfort.
- Against a heavily patterned rug: The Quarry Oyster’s subtlety shines in big rooms with loud furniture. If you crave bold visuals, pair this with a piece that carries a pop of color elsewhere in the room.
