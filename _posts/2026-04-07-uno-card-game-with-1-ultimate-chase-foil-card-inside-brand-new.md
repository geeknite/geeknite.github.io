### Rules and Gameplay Changes (House Rules)
Let’s be clear: this is not a replacement for the official UNO rules. It is a playful variant that introduces a single chase mechanic into a familiar framework. Here are the core ideas you can adopt: the Ultimate Chase Foil Card can be played as a wild; when played, the player designates a target. That target must draw four cards unless they counter with a wild or foil of their own. The “Chase” aspect introduces a little back-and-forth, a mini duel within a regular UNO round. You can adjust the numbers: you could require six cards for a stronger effect or keep it at four for a lighter touch. The key is to establish it before you begin playing. 

#### House Rule Variants to Try
- Normal chase: The target draws four cards; the round continues as usual. 
- Counter-chase: If someone has another foil or wild, they can counter; if successful, the original target draws two instead of four. 
- Group chase: If three or more players are in the hand, declare a group chase with shared draw effects. 
- Endgame chase: The chase has more sting if you end your turn after the chase and someone else must finish the round with added pressure. 

These variants are optional, but they help you tailor the chaos to the age range and attention span in your group. For families, keep things breezy; for gamer nights, lean into the longer, more strategic possibilities.
