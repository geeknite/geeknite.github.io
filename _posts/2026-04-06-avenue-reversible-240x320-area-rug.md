### Maintenance and Cleaning: How to Keep It Fresh

Maintaining an area rug this size is easier than you think, but there are some concrete steps you’ll want to follow to keep it looking sharp:

- Vacuum regularly: use a low-to-medium suction setting and avoid beater bars that could snag fibers. A regular vacuum pass will remove surface dust and hair without distorting the pile.
- Spot clean promptly: for spills, blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth. Use a mild detergent diluted in water for tougher stains. Test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness on the side you’re using.
- Rotation: as mentioned, rotate the rug every 2–3 months to even out wear. This is especially important in households with pets that have a preferred “nap location” or humans who keep rearranging the furniture for the aesthetic of a magazine shoot.
- Cleaning beyond home care: for a deeper refresh, you can hand-wash or steam-clean the rug if the backing allows it (check the care label). Always air-dry completely before returning to service to prevent mildew.

From a practical perspective, the rug is designed to be forgiving. It resists common stains and cleans up with relative ease compared to higher-pile options. If you have kids or pets, consider using a protective spray after cleaning sessions; that small extra step can extend the rug’s life and keep the pattern crisp for longer.
