## Materials, Craftsmanship, and the Warmth of Handwoven Cotton

This dhurrie rug is described as hand woven cotton. What does that mean in practice? It means you’re getting a tactile product whose charm lies in its human-scale touches. Hand weaving is the art of patience, pattern, and tiny human variations that give a piece character. It may not be perfectly identical from corner to corner, but that’s the point: it’s a textile with a heartbeat, not a 3D-printed replica.

- Material: 100% cotton. Cotton is a renewable fiber with a soft hand feel and good breathability. It’s not a velvet luxury, but it’s not a rough burlap face-punch either. Cotton handles foot traffic, vacuuming, and the occasional muddy boot print with a decent shrug.
- Weave: Flat-woven. Flat-weaves are lower in pile and tend to be more durable for high-traffic zones. They resist compression and keep edges crisp—great for hallway runs or under a console table where you want a steady platform for decorative accents.
- Craft cues: You’ll feel the difference when you touch the border—clean lines, consistent tension, and a geometry that looks both precise and lovingly imperfect in the best possible way.

### A note on care and longevity
Cotton, especially flat-woven, likes routine but not harshness. Regular vacuuming with a soft beater bar, rotating every few months to even out wear, and prompt spot treatment for spills will go a long way toward keeping colors vivid and fibers intact. The rug’s low profile means it’s less forgiving of deep-pile snags and more tolerant of daily foot traffic—your sofa crew will thank you for the lack of a tripping hazard with every Netflix-binge pause.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Geometry Sharp

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting or a hand-held vacuum to avoid pulling on fibers. A high-pile vacuum can nibble at the edges of flat-woven textiles, so keep it gentle.
- Rotate every couple of months. Even if you’re zealous about symmetry and order, a little rotation helps the rug wear evenly—especially if you have a sunbeam that craves a dramatic shadow on the border.
- Spot cleaning: For spills, blot—don’t rub. Use a mild detergent mixed with water and dab gently. If you’re dealing with a stubborn stain, test your cleaner on a small hidden area first to avoid color migration.
- Drying: Dry flat in a ventilated area. Avoid direct heat that could shrink cotton fibers. Patience, not speed, is the order of the day for a drying rug.
