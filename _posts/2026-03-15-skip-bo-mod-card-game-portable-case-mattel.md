## Durability, Maintenance, and Care
We put this case through its paces with a few rounds of lively game-night abuse. The exterior held up to several drops and the occasional “oops, I spilled coffee” moment without the plastic warping or shattering. The latches remained reliable throughout the testing period, and the interior foam showed no significant compression after multiple openings and closings. The foam did not shed any particles onto the cards, which is a huge plus for anyone who has had to wipe card powder off a King of the Hill deck after a rough play.

Maintenance is simple: wipe with a damp cloth, air-dry, and keep away from direct heat that could warp the shell. If you’re a meticulous player who prides themselves on long-term card care, you may want to invest in a small silica gel pack for the interior to keep the foam from retaining moisture during humid travel—especially if you’re in a climate where card moisture becomes a stealthy antagonist to your stock and draw piles.

A few practical tips for care:
- Avoid overstuffing the interior beyond its intended capacity. It’s durable, but pressure points can cause small cracks over time.
- Clean the exterior with a mild cleaner; avoid harsh solvents that could dull the surface finish.
- Store in a dry place; this helps preserve the interior foam’s crisp shape for longer.
