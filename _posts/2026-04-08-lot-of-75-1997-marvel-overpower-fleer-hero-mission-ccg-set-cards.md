## What is OverPower and what does Hero Mission add?
OverPower is a collectible card game that came out in the mid 1990s in a wave of games that tried to convert comic book drama into tabletop combat. The cards feature characters from Marvel Universe with power ratings, abilities, and special actions, all designed to evoke the feel of a brisk superhero skirmish. The Hero Mission expansion is a slice of added content designed to push the game into narrative territory. It introduces mission style objectives that can alter the flow of the game and new hero or villain options that weren t present in the base set. This combination gave players more reason to draft new decks, chase specific heroes, and craft strategies around mission outcomes rather than pure brute force.

For someone new to the concept, the main draw is not just about collecting pretty pictures. It is about assembling a rogues gallery and a squad that can navigate a mission while keeping track of energy costs, environmental advantages, and timing. The funky part is that some of these hero mission cards are written with the same winking humor that comic fans appreciate, and the art style often captures the retro vibe that 90s comic art had perfected by accident. It is camp, it is earnest, and it is oddly endearing in a way that only 1990s CCGs can be.

## Card quality and condition: what to expect
Card quality in 1997 Fleer print runs varies. The stock tends to be sturdy enough for casual play, but it is not the premium card stock you might see in modern CCG reprints. Here are practical notes to guide inspection and handling:

- Card stock and gloss: You can expect a standard gloss finish that looks slick but is not reflective to the point of glare during play. The corners might show minor wear, especially on cards that have seen table time rather than glass display cases.
- Edges and corners: In bulk lots, you may encounter edge wear, especially on the outermost cards in the stack. A common win is identifying cards that survived with barely any chipping or rounding of corners. It matters if you intend to play or display. If you are buying for investment, you want to be mindful of borderline condition cards and calculate their impact on the overall value of the lot.
- Centering and misprints: Older prints can show centering inconsistencies. In some cards you might note slight misalignment of art versus text. These quirks can add a touch of character; others will be simply undesirable. Decide early whether you are a perfectionist or a pragmatist about printing quirks.
- Surface wear vs. color fade: Some colors age gracefully, others fade with time under bright light. If you intend to display, consider a protective case with UV filtering to maintain the vivid retro visuals you are chasing.

In short, the quality is a mixed bag, but that is part of the charm. A lot like this is not about pristine museum pieces; it is about the experience, the memories, and the potential to salvage pieces into a playable or display-worthy collection. If you approach with reasonable expectations, you will find this a rewarding way to reenter a segment of the 1990s card game world without paying modern reprint prices.

## Storage, care, and value preservation
Preserving the contents of a 75+ card lot is a task that rewards organization more than brute force. Here are practical tips to maximize the look, playability, and resale value over time:

- Sleeving strategy: Use standard sized card sleeves to protect the cards you intend to play with. If you have a display piece, consider larger sleeves or top-loaders for extra protection. The combination of sleeving and a dedicated binder can do wonders for longevity.
- Humidity control: A controlled environment matters. High humidity can warp paper and spark color changes that age poorly. A dry cabinet away from sunlight is ideal for long-term storage.
- Light exposure: UV light can fade artwork. If you display the cards, choose a location with minimal direct sun or use UV protective glass.
- Handling etiquette: Wash hands before handling and avoid food stains near the cards. It is amazing how a crumb can become a lifelong card stigmatizer if it sits on a valuable hero portrait for weeks.
- Documentation: Keep a simple inventory of what is in the lot, noting any notable variants, misprints, or condition outliers. A quick checklist helps you when the time comes to sell or trade.

A well maintained lot not only maintains its value but also makes it much easier to enjoy a casual game night or to show off to fellow collectors who appreciate the depth of the 1990s CCG scene.
