## Care and maintenance
### Cleaning basics
- Vacuum regularly to remove dust and debris. Use a brush attachment to avoid pulling fibers.
- For spills, blot—do not rub—starting from the outside and moving inward to prevent spread. Mild detergent with water can be used for stubborn stains; test in a small area first.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear, particularly in high-traffic zones.
- Avoid placing near heat sources or direct sunlight for long periods to preserve color fastness.

### Long-term care tips
- Invest in a rug pad for cushioning and to prevent slipping. A good pad improves comfort and reduces movement when you walk.
- Schedule a professional cleaning every 1-2 years if your home sees heavy use or if you prefer to keep the rug pristine; otherwise, routine spot cleaning and vacuuming will suffice for most households.

### Maintenance in unusual situations
Hosting a party? Great. Parties often leave footprints in the form of glitter and spilled drinks. The sunflower rug can handle it if you jump on the stain promptly. Reserve your energy for the dance floor, not for scrubbing: a quick blot and a mild solution should get the rug back to its sunny glory.
