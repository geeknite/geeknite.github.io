## Weather Resistance and Maintenance
Outdoor furniture has to survive sun, rain, humidity, and the occasional stray raccoon sampling your pretzels. Rattan can be surprisingly resilient when treated properly, though it requires some routine care to keep it from looking tired after a few seasons.

### UV and Weather Exposure
The rattan weave is usually treated to resist UV radiation, but prolonged exposure can still cause fading or brittleness over time. A simple maintenance trick is to place the set in a lightly shaded area or use covers during peak sun hours. If your climate is especially humid or rainy, it helps to store cushions away when the season ends and wipe down the frame periodically with a mild soap solution and water. Avoid harsh cleaners that can strip protective coatings or degrade the weave. A light application of protective spray designed for outdoor wicker can extend the life of the finish, but test it on a small area first to avoid unexpected discoloration.

### Rain and Humidity
Rattan loves air flow and hates waterlogged cushions. If you are in a rainy climate, consider keeping cushions dry by elevating them slightly off the ground and using breathable covers. A gentle drying routine after a rain shower is ideal: wipe the frame with a soft cloth and let it air dry completely before sitting down. If you notice any wobble after heavy rain, check screws and fittings and tighten as needed. The goal is to preserve rigidity while preserving the woven look that makes this set so appealing in the first place.

### Cleaning and Care
Cleaning is surprisingly straightforward. A soft brush or vacuum attachment can remove dust and debris from the weave. For stubborn grime, use a mild soap solution and a soft cloth, then rinse with a light mist of water. Never use a high-pressure washer on wicker as the force can loosen fibers and reduce the weave integrity. Once dry, inspect for loose strands and trim if necessary with small scissors. Cushions, if included, should be cleaned following the manufacturer recommendations, which usually involve removing covers and washing according to care labels. If cushions are not removable, spot clean the fabric and let it air dry completely before using again.

## Assembly and Maintenance
The assembly experience varies by model, but most six seat dining sets in this category follow a similar blueprint. You should expect to spend about one to two hours assembling if you take your time and double check the hardware. A helpful tip is to lay out all parts before you begin and loosely fit everything to ensure proper alignment. Don t rush the process; this is not a race to see who finishes first and ends up with a wobble in the last chair. You want a stable, square installation and a surface that stays level when loaded with six plates and a pitcher of lemonade.

### Assembly Tips
- Clear the working area to avoid losing tiny screws to the grass like a kid losing marbles.
- Use the included hardware and torque to the manufacturer guidelines; over tightening can crack the weave and under tightening can mean a loose feel. Balance is key.
- Have a helper; two people reduce time and increase accuracy, especially when aligning the table top and chairs.
- Check for level surfaces post assembly and adjust adjustable feet if present. A small shim under a leg can fix a wonky stance more easily than moving the entire set.

### Cleaning and Long Term Care
Regular cleaning keeps the set looking fresh. Brush away debris after meals, wipe with a mild solution, and store cushions when not in use. A yearly tune-up with a weatherproof sealant can help maintain the finish. If you notice signs of wear around joints or weave starts to loosen, address those areas promptly to extend the life of the set. A proactive approach beats a reactive patchwork in the long run and keeps your yard looking showroom-ready rather than yard-sale ready.
