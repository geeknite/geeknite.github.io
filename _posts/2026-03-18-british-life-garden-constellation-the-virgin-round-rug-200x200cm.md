## Maintenance and Care
Let’s be honest: rugs are not self-cleaning portals to a pristine universe. They require a bit of routine care, especially in high-traffic rooms. Here’s how to keep your constellation looking as starry as possible without summoning the vacuum demon.

### Regular Care
- Vacuum regularly with a floor-head attachment to remove surface dust and loose fibers. Avoid rotating brushes that could snag the pile; gentleness is the key to preserving the star-field’s sparkle.
- Spot-clean spills immediately using a clean, white cloth. Blot, don’t rub, to prevent pigment migration. A mild detergent solution or a dedicated fabric cleaner generally does the trick.
- Rotate occasionally if you want even wear across the surface. This isn’t a spaceship stabilizer, but it helps prevent one side from looking worn while the rest remains plush.

### Deep Cleaning and Washing
Most serious rug care guidance would advise against putting this rug in a washing machine or submerging it in water. The general safe route is spot cleaning or professional cleaning if a large spill threatens to turn the constellation into a consternation. If you do decide to attempt any deeper cleaning, test a small inconspicuous area first and proceed with caution. The last thing you want is a dye bleed that turns your night-sky rug into something more like an apocalyptic coffee stain nebula.

### Longevity and Warranty
Rug longevity depends on foot traffic, pets, sun exposure, and the occasional gravity-related accident with a dropped remote. A good warranty can be a lifesaver if fibers begin to shed or backing starts to fray. Check with the retailer for care guidelines and any warranty specifics. The peace of mind is worth a little extra concern for the cosmic look you’ll be showcasing daily.
