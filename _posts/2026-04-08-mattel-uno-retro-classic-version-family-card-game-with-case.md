## How to play the Retro Version: quick refresher
If you’ve played UNO before, the Retro Version won’t surprise you with new mechanics. It adheres to the classic UNO rules but benefits from a kinder presentation and a case that makes re-sleeving cards feel optional rather than mandatory.

- Objective: Be the first player to get rid of all your cards.
- Turn order: Continue in the play order unless a Reverse card changes things up.
- Card actions: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Draw Four Wild remain the big players. Wilds let you choose the color; Draw Two and Draw Four add hostility to the table in the most loving way possible.
- Uno call: When you have one card left, shout “UNO!” before your last card hits the pile. If someone calls you out for not saying it, you draw two cards. The drama is real, folks.

This edition doesn’t introduce radical rule changes; it leans into the familiar flow with a tasteful dash of retro packaging. If you’re looking for cosmic rule variations, you’ll want to look at other UNO variants, but for family chaos that’s easy to moderate, Retro UNO nails it.
