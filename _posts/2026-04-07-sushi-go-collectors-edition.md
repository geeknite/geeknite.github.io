## Gameplay Overview (Collector’s Edition, in case you forgot how to play)
Sushi Go! is a fast-paced drafting game where players pass a hand of sushi cards around the table, selecting one card each round while trying to assemble the best possible meal. The score system is simple but satisfying: you collect combos (like set-based sashimi or nigiri with soy sauce bonuses) that trigger points at the end of each round. The Collector's Edition doesn’t alter the core math; it just makes the math feel more ceremonial.

- Each round, you pick a card from your hand and pass the rest to the left. You do this for three or four rounds (depending on the number of players).
- You’re chasing sets and combos: identical rolls for the same menu item, or special pairs that unlock extra points. It’s a rhythm game for your brain where the rhythm is “panic-but-fun.”
- The final scoring tallies up the value of your collected cards, and the person with the most points wins. The dynamic between risk versus reward—do you go for the obvious dumplings or gamble on a risky sashimi run—keeps the table lively, loud, and occasionally hungry for actual sushi.

## Playing with the Collector’s Extras: Practical Tips
If you’re ready to crack into the Collector’s Edition, a few practical tips can help you maximize the fun from round one:

- Set expectations: The more premium pieces feel weightier, which can make the table feel more serious than the rules require. Reiterate the light-hearted spirit at start so the table doesn’t get tense over a lost sashimi run.
- Sort and reference: Use the new reference card to remind everyone of obvious combos. It’s easy to forget you only need two dumplings to power up a bunch of points.
- Eye on the prize, not the polish: Don’t get distracted by how fancy the box looks. The goal remains the same: draft the best possible meal and outscore your friends.
- Quick-start variant: For newcomers or a busy game night, run a shorter round with a condensed deck (just the essential cards) to teach the flow quickly; then graduate to the full Collector’s Edition experience in a second round.

We also tested some homemade rules to spice things up: a “chef special” rule where you can swap one card with a neighbor’s hand once per game for a dramatic comeback. It’s optional, keeps the mood light, and doesn’t require you to replace any core components.
