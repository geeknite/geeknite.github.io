## Mechanics and Gameplay

### The Core Loop

Players take turns drawing prompt cards and either reading them aloud or describing the prompt in their own goofy voice. The goal is to score points by delivering the most entertaining, honest, or hilariously wrong take on a given prompt. Each prompt has a small scoring objective: be charming, be bold, or be painfully honest. The group votes, scores are tallied, and the next player passes the turn.

### Date Missions and Prompts

The prompts vary from romance flavored to absurd, with a few that feel like audition material for a reality dating show. Examples include prompts that encourage players to improv a first date scenario, or to describe their worst date in the voice of a villain in a superhero movie. The extension pack Dating Party Mate adds a new subset of prompts that are more party oriented: think quick-fire questions, challenges, and prompts that encourage you to break out a silly dance or culinary disaster story.

## How to Play: Step-by-Step

1. Gather the players and set up the table with all components in easy reach. Distribute any player boards and keep scoring tokens within reach.
2. Shuffle the prompt cards and place them in the center. If you are bringing out the Dating Party Mate extension, do the same for the extension deck.
3. Choose a starting player. This player draws a prompt and reads or performs it, depending on the prompt style. Others react by voting or scoring the performance according to the rules laid out in the booklet.
4. The scoring system may vary depending on house rules, but the typical approach is a simple majority or a majority with a few extra points for flair.
5. Each round rotates to the next player. The extension pack rules can introduce quick-fire rounds where players must complete a task before time runs out.
6. The game ends when one player (or team, in party mode) accumulates a pre-determined number of points. Celebrate by taking a victory selfie and posting it to your group chat with a clever caption.

### House Rules and Variants

If you want to dial the chaos up or down, there are a few house rules that can make things more interesting:

- Quick Prompt Roulette: Shuffle all prompts into a single deck and draw until someone calls time.
- Date Night Madness: Introduce a timer that forces players to respond quickly, preventing overthinking and enabling more ridiculous performances.
- Comfort Mode: Use fewer prompts and a shorter game length to ease new players into the vibe without feeling overwhelmed.

## Tactics and Tips

- Lean into your strengths. If you are a natural improviser, you can win more points by delivering high-energy performances.
- Use humor to build rapport but avoid being insulting or mean. The goal is to have fun with each other, not to trigger a meltdown.
- Pay attention to your partner's cues. If your friend is clearly uncomfortable, pivot to a lighter prompt and diffuse the tension with a shared laugh.
- Team-based rounds (found in the extension) reward collaboration and quick thinking. Try pairing players who have complementary dating profiles, like a romcom fan with a sci‑fi nerd.
- Don’t be afraid to repeat prompts in later rounds. A well-timed callback can win you extra points.
