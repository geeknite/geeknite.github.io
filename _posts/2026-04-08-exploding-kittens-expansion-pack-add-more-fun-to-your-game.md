### New Cards and Interactions
The expansion introduces a crowd-pleasing mix of new cards, each with its own flavor of mischief. Some of the highlights (names may vary by print run, but the vibe stays crystal):
- **Standoff** — pits two players against each other in a hand-reveal duel. The tension is real; the chances of someone bluffing with five Defuse cards in their hand are deliciously high. 
- **Copycat** — this little trick mirrors the effect of the previous card played. It creates a chain reaction that makes the table lean into chaos until someone yells, “Wait, what card even is this?”
- **Undercut** — a sneaky move that can tilt momentum by swapping out a card with something sneakily worse for your opponent. It’s the kind of petty sabotage that makes friendships weaker and stories stronger.
- **Dojo Nyan** — a playful nod to martial arts and feline poise. It rewards precise timing and a confident meow at just the right moment.
- **Catastrophe, Whisker Bluff, and Pixel Purr** (fiendishly nerdy fan favorites) — these cards push you toward riskier plays, brighter smiles, and louder laughter. 
The art remains charmingly cute, with crisp lines and enough sass to remind you that these cats know exactly how ridiculous the situation is. The cardstock feels sturdy, designed to survive the inevitable flinging of cards across the table when the stakes are either hilariously low or dangerously high.

## Strategy and Tactics for the Expanded Table
If you want to win at Exploding Kittens with the expansion, you’ll need more than luck. You’ll need to read the room, manage your courage, and learn to bluff with the confidence of a cat who just knocked over a glass of water on a keyboard.

### Card Counting, Not Cheating
Counting the number of Defuses left in the deck is still a good idea, but the expansion adds enough new cards to give you plausible deniability. The Copycat card, for instance, can create mini-streaks that flood the table with information and misdirection. When you can anticipate a potential Standoff, you’ll be ready to pivot and turn a disadvantage into a ridiculous — and memorable — comeback.

### Timing, Tempos, and Teasing
Timing matters more now. A well-timed Standoff can steal the wind from another player’s sails just as they thought they had the game under control. Dojo Nyan’s timing test is also a fun way to exercise patience: you wait until your opponent is overconfident and then strike with quiet confidence, almost as if you are a ninja-cat of destiny.

### Bluffing with a Smile
The cute-yet-menacing art helps create a social dynamic where bluffing feels less harsh and more like performance art. If you can maintain a deadpan expression while your hand is a chaotic mix of ridiculous cards, you’ll earn the respect (and the caution) of your fellow players. It’s not about winning quickly; it’s about turning a single card draw into a story worth retelling at work the next day.

## The Card Gallery: Highlights and Practical Tips
Here’s a quick tour of select cards from the expansion with practical play notes. This isn’t a full card list (that would require a sea of tiny text), but it gives you the rhythm of the new mechanics.

## Teaching the Expansion and Getting New Players Onboard
If you’re introducing the expansion to newcomers, keep a few things in mind:
- Start with one or two new cards in the deck so players aren’t overwhelmed by options on Round One. Gradually reveal additional cards in future sessions.
- Demonstrate a sample hand and walk through a couple of turns before you start the clock. A quick practice run helps everyone feel comfortable with the rhythm and tone of the chaos.
- Emphasize the humor: explain that this is a game where misfortune and laughter are the end goal, not a black-ops operation to ruin friendships. The cats are cute; their chaos is not personal—except when it’s directed at Uncle Bob who always takes too long to say “Defuse.”
- Provide a couple of prepared jokes or cat-themed puns to help break the ice and set the mood. If a player can deliver a good pun at scale, they’ve earned a little extra prestige and a free laugh from the table.
