The seal: a blessing or a curse?
--------------------------------
Seals aren’t just about keeping the box pristine; they also ensure the blocks don’t dry out too fast or take on moisture that could warp their geometry. In dry climates, the seal helps preserve the blocks’ straightness and uniformity, which matters when you’re aiming for a clean, even block take. In humid environments, a proper seal can help prevent swelling that would throw off the level alignment. In short: the seal isn’t just marketing fluff; it’s a quiet guardian of the playing field.
