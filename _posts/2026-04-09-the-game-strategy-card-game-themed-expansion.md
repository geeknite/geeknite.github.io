## New mechanics and how they feel at the table

The star of this expansion is a new mechanism that sits nicely on top of the base game’s engine without overthrowing it: the Theme Alignment system. In short, you’ll build a deck that leans toward a particular theme to unlock powerful combos and story-driven triggers. This has two big consequences: it rewards thematic coherence and also risks locking out players who want a broad, mixed deck. The designers seem to have accepted this trade-off in order to provide a richer flavor experience.

### Thematic factions and their flavor punch
Each faction has a distinct feel and a toolkit that rewards a specific play pattern:

- Science fantasy: emphasizes engine-building and probabilistic control. Cards in this family tend to reduce randomness by giving you more information about upcoming draws or by smoothing the curve.
- Mythic pirates: go big on disruption and tempo. They are all about bold plays that swing momentum and create memorable “oh wow” moments.
- Neon-druid cyber-shamans: hybrid control and tempo with a heavy flavor of modern esoterica. Expect cryptic card names and some clever interactions with the resource pool.
- Time-traveling tacticians: delayed gratification with a splash of inevitability. They can lock you into future turns or pull tricks from the fourth dimension when you least expect it.

The result is a rich tapestry of choices that encourages you to commit to a theme, but not so hard that you can’t pivot if your opening plan collapses.

### Event deck dynamics and the cause of glorious table chaos
The new event deck introduces random, thematic twists that occur at different timing windows. It’s not just “draw a card, then do something terrible to someone else.” There’s a rhythm to these events: some are solvable with careful planning, others punish hubris, and a handful create the kind of dramatic turn that becomes a legend at your gaming table.

### Resource management—because you were waiting for calculus, right?
You’ll see a new resource type tied to the theme, which interacts with other resources in a few clever ways. The trick here is balancing investment and return—the classic gambler’s dilemma, but with less risk of a casino exposure and more risk of your friend’s smug grin when you mismanage your tempo. The design makes it easy to dip a toe into the pool before committing your entire strategy, which is ideal for new players who want to learn without being drowned in a smorgasbord of symbols.

## Strategy deep dive: building and piloting a themed deck

A good deck in this expansion is not a random pile of sweet cards. It’s a story with a plan, a plan with a backup plan, and a backup plan that somehow relies on drawing the exact card you didn’t know you needed. Here are practical guidelines to help you avoid spinning your wheels:

### 1) Pick a primary theme, then fill in the supporting actors
The moment you declare allegiance to a faction, you unlock a few synergies that reward you for leaning into that choice. But you don’t want to be a one-trick pony. Add 1-2 cards from other factions that enhance your core engine. This creates flexible pathways and prevents you from becoming predictable.

### 2) Control tempo—don’t chase every shiny thing
The expansion introduces powerful tempo swings. The temptation is to snap up every “big swing” card, but if you overcommit, you’ll skip tempo and spawn awkward turns. Build your deck with a few consistent performers and save your “big plays” for moments when you’ve already pinned your opponent into a corner and need a finishing blow.

### 3) Understand the event deck timing
Events can break your perfect draw order if you aren’t prepared. Build a plan that accounts for occasional disruption. If you’re playing with friends, you’ll discover that the most satisfying wins often come from anticipating an event’s impact and turning it to your advantage.

### 4) Resource gating and pacing
The new resource type adds a layer of resource gating. The best decks don’t rush; they pace. You’ll want to save certain resources for late-game spikes while maintaining a steady trickle early on. This is not about math precision; it’s about rhythm and feel.

### 5) Practice lines of play
Run a few trial hands with low-risk, high-reward combos to understand the theme’s tempo. As you play more, you’ll notice patterns emerge: you’ll recognize which cards are “turn ends” and which cards are “turns late.” Practicing line-of-play helps you spot the best move when your brain short-circuits from adrenaline and refreshments.

### 6) Adaptability—expect the table to morph
A hallmark of this expansion is how it rewards responsiveness. If your plan A fails, pivot to plan B, which itself could morph into plan C. The more you practice the art of pivoting, the better your win rate becomes—and the more entertaining the table talk gets.

## Pitfalls and common mistakes to avoid

No expansion is perfect, and this one is no exception. Here are a few traps to dodge:

- Over-commitment to a single faction: It’s tempting to go all-in on your chosen theme, but you’ll want a couple of flexible cards to counter unpredictable events. The best decks feature a core synergy but keep a couple of “wild card” cards for late-game contingencies.
- Ignoring the event deck: The event deck is easy to forget until it’s not. If you ignore it, you’ll be blindsided when someone else uses an event to accelerate their plan. Remember to budget for anticipation rather than pure reaction.
- Not counting endgame effects: The thematic triggers can elicit a dramatic finish. If you’re not careful, you’ll miss a crucial threshold and watch a would-be victory drift away at the last moment.
- Skipping the quick-start guide: The expansion rewards familiarity. If you don’t skim the quick-start rules, you’ll spend too long dithering over basic mechanics and you’ll miss a perfect moment for a bold play.
