## How to play the Game Night Bundle (the quick version)

Exploding Kittens is a push-your-late-night luck game disguised as a party puzzle. The core loop is simple: players draw cards until someone draws an Exploding Kitten, at which point they must have a Defuse card or they are out of the round, and the game continues until one player remains. The Game Night Bundle adds extra layers on top, such as new Card types, expanded player counts, and optional rules that make the chaos more deliberate rather than random.

Key card types typically found in the bundle include:

- Exploding Kitten cards: the only way to instantly lose a round unless you have a Defuse card.
- Defuse cards: use them to neutralize an Exploding Kitten, with your own life and pride intact for at least one more turn.
- See the Future: peek at the top cards of the draw pile to inform your next move.
- Attack: force the next player to take multiple turns in a row, amplifying tension and risk.
- Skip: bypass your turn to dodge danger for a moment, often used after a risky draw.
- Shuffle: randomly reshuffle the draw pile when fate looks dicey.
- Cat cards: players collect sets of Cat cards to trade in for bonus effects or to trigger clever hijinks during the game.

These mechanics combine to create a rhythm that can feel like a micro sports routine with a punchy comedic twist. You are watching the other players attempt to stay calm as the deck grows louder and more unpredictable, and you are quietly calculating how much you can push your luck before defusing yourself into a safer future.

Setup is fast. Shuffle the cards, deal a starting hand to each player, and pick a starting button or order of play. The first few rounds are a calibration exercise, a social experiment, and a warm-up for the moment when someone yells, I GOT A DEFUSE and suddenly the whole table believes in their own luck, even if it’s a little questionable.

The Game Night Bundle specifically shines in a live setting: you have a room full of friends who love puns, memes, and the kind of banter that would make a dragon proud. The order of play becomes a performance piece with each player trying to one-up the last move, and the Defuse cards function like lifelines that allow for strategic misdirection rather than simply saying deal with it.

For those who want a more tactile, media-rich experience, you can pair your game with a short break between rounds to discuss highlights or to set up a quick, silly side challenge. The variety keeps the energy high and the laughter consistent, and the presence of card art that is actually funny enhances the social payoff more than you might expect.

## Strategy and house rules: getting the most out of the bundle

While luck is a major factor, there is a surprisingly robust layer of strategy available if you want to take the game more seriously or keep it light but structured.

- Defuse management: Defuse cards are precious. You want to spread them so that you are less likely to be completely out of the round if you face a cluster of Exploding Kitten draws. Don’t be too greedy with a single Defuse deck, because somebody else will remember your risky behavior and call you out in round three for running the table.
- See the Future timing: You can map a small part of your strategy to the information you glean by peeking at future draws. If you see a high likelihood of a kitten in the next stack, saving a Defuse for that moment becomes a viable risk management tactic.
- Attack and Skip usage: Attacks are best used when the player directly ahead of you is likely to draw, or when you want to push pressure onto someone who has meager Defuse resources. Skips are often underrated; they can reset tension moments and prevent a meltdown, especially when the group is on the edge of losing control.
- Cat cards: Sets of Cat cards can unlock special bonuses or additional effects. The more you collect, the more control you have over certain play dynamics, which can shift the balance from pure luck to a touch more strategy.
