## How to inspect a flawed rug before you buy (and how to return if you must)

Inspection is your superpower when shopping for discounted rugs. Here’s a pragmatic checklist you can actually use:

- Check for color consistency: Look at several areas under different light. If the color variation is within a small range, it’s likely a design feature. If there are obvious blotches, consider whether the price drop accounts for the additional risk.
- Examine the pile uniformity: Push your hand across the rug and feel for uneven areas. Inconsistent height may indicate a lower-grade weave, which could wear down faster.
- Look at the edges: Are the bindings clean? Are there loose fibers that could pull? A well-stitched edge is a good sign for durability.
- Backing and slip resistance: If you can, flip the rug and observe the backing. Non-slip padding is essential for high-traffic areas.
- Measure the actual size: Rugs can be printed or measured incorrectly. If you need a precise fit, measure yourself and compare to the advertised dimensions.

Return policy is the safety net you should rely on for improvised discoveries. Ensure that you can return or exchange if the flaw is more than you’re comfortable with. Read the warranty details: some rug warranties cover manufacturing defects but not wear and tear—obvious, yet easy to overlook when the console is blinking with a lightning-fast checkout.
