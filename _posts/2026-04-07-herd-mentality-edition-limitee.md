## What is Herd Mentality? Édition limitée
Herd Mentality is a social deduction-ish party game with a twist: players answer prompts with what they think the majority will say, not what they personally believe. The result is a blend of social psychology, mild embarrassment, and a lot of laughter as people realize their friends all think the same question is a test of their ability to lie to a friend. The Édition limitée does not rewrite the game; it polishes it with a new art style, better card stock, and the sense that you are getting something rarer than a dented copy of your favorite cereal.

The core of the game remains simple: a prompt comes up, players write down an answer they think the majority will choose, and then everyone reveals. If your guess matches the majority, you score points. If you were the oddball—congratulations, you have cemented your “stand-out” status in a room full of people who secretly want to be popular. The humor comes from the gap between what people say they value and what they actually do when the chips are down and there are no chips—just little wooden discs with a face on them.

## Gameplay and pacing: Chaos with a smile
Herd Mentality is not a game you play if you want to prove a point about your superior logic. It is a game you play if you want to watch your siblings commit to ridiculous lies, your roommate reveal a secret prejudice about pastries, and your partner realize they know more about your quirks than you thought. The pacing hinges on the number of players: with too few, the table talk can feel like a polite chat at a library; with a full table, it becomes a raucous, carnival-esque stream of one-liners and whispered schemes.

The addition of the Édition limitée mainly improves the sensory experience: the cards have a tactile gloss that gleams under a warm lamp, and the scoring tokens click with a satisfying heft. The actual mechanical tempo doesn’t change dramatically—the twists come from players’ willingness to play their cards with attitude rather than accuracy. That’s where the humor lives: the moment someone locks in a front-runner guess, someone else leaps with a bold, incorrect option that somehow resonates with the table’s collective paranoia about coffee preferences or fashion sense.

If you’re planning a game night, aim for a small army of players (5–8 is ideal) or bring the Edition to a party that tolerates loud cheers and dramatic gasps. The more people you have, the more outrageous the majority’s answer tends to be, and the more likely you’ll witness that beautiful moment when someone undercuts the expected moral calculus with a perfectly petty justification.
