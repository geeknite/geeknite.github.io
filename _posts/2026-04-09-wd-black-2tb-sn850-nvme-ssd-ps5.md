## Installation reality check: does PS5 actually accept this drive
PS5 expansion slots are particular about size, heat sinkments, and the ability to actually fit your chosen M.2 drive into the space. The SN850 2 TB you buy from WD often comes in two flavors: with a built-in heatsink (for the PS5, which requests heat dissipation) and without. The PS5 compatibility guide from Sony makes it clear that you should use a high performance M.2 SSD that fits within the dimensions of a drive with a heatsink, and that you can place within the interior of the PS5 while maintaining clearance for the side panel.

For those who are new to the PS5 upgrade ritual, here is a quick recap:
- Power down your PS5 and unplug it, just in case the controller tries to re-think its life choices.
- Remove the side panel by lifting the small lip at the top and sliding it off. This is where your video game heroism begins.
- Locate the M.2 slot cover and remove it; you will see the slot that accepts the M.2 drive. If you have a heatsinkless version, you will need to install a heatsink on the drive or ensure your drive’s own heatsink fits within the PS5 constraints.
- Insert the drive at a 30-degree angle, then press it down and secure with the screw provided.
- Replace the slot cover, the outer panel, and power on the console. You should get a prompt to format the drive; format it with the quick sanity check and a strong internet connection for extra drama.

WD’s SN850 2 TB heatsink variant is especially PS5 friendly because it avoids the need for you to conjure a separate heatsink accessory, which can be tricky to align with the dimensions and the PS5’s own airflow. If you buy a standard non-heatsink version, make sure you pick up a heatsink kit that is compatible with the drive and that won’t block a fan or press against the chassis. The last thing you want is an overheating drama during a long night of grinding in a live-service game.

We include some quick tips for the installation experience:
- Confirm clearance: The PS5 interior isn’t a luxury condo; there is a limited set of inches to maneuver. If you have a large heatsink, you might need to bend your patience more than your drive to make it fit.
- Thermal paste not required: The drive comes with a thermal interface material in most heatsink variants, which makes the installation simpler and cleaner.
- Firmware matters: WD frequently updates firmware to fix compatibility issues and to improve performance; check WD’s site post installation for any firmware notes that could improve PS5 stability or speed.

## Thermals, noise, and long term reliability
Thermals are a legitimate concern for PS5 users installing an NVMe drive. The PS5 itself has a robust cooling design, but when you push a drive to the limits with large textures and frequent patch downloads, you want to ensure it doesn’t run too hot. The SN850 with a heatsink stays cooler under sustained load, which translates into stable performance. A non-heatsink variant is not a no-go, but you will want a compatible heatsink if you plan to stretch the life of the drive or to keep performance consistent across long gaming sessions.

In our testing, the SN850 did not produce excessive noise—there is no fan on the drive itself of course—so the only audible drama comes from the PS5’s own cooling system. If you are worried about ambient temperatures, consider placing the PS5 in a well-ventilated space and not inside a sealed cabinet where air pools like bad vibes. The whole point of NVMe upgrades is speed; keeping the drive cool is the quiet, practical version of that goal.
