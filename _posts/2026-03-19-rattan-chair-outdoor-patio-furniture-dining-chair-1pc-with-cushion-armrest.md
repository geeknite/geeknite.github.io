## Weather resilience: how well does it survive sun, rain, and existential moisture?

Outdoor furniture lives a life of weather, which means it must withstand UV rays and the occasional rain shower without dissolving into a puddle of wicker regret. Rattan chairs like this one are usually finished with a protective coating on the frame to guard against moisture and color fading. The cushion fabric is typically designed to dry quickly and resist mildew when given a chance. Still, you should be mindful of placement: direct afternoon sun for long periods can warm the seat and cause the cushion to lose a bit of its bounce faster than you can say SPF 50.

One practical tip is to store the chair indoors or use a weatherproof cover during heavy rain or winter months. The last thing you want is a warped frame or a cushion that smells faintly of damp laundry due to prolonged exposure to moisture. If you live in a high humidity area, consider elevating the chair off a damp deck using rubber feet or small cushions to improve drainage and airflow underneath the chair, which helps reduce moisture buildup.

## Durability and maintenance tips

- Clean with a soft brush or damp cloth to remove dust and pollen. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the weave or cushion fabric.
- If the cushion becomes compact with moisture, remove the cover and let it air dry in a shaded area.
- Check the hardware periodically. Tighten bolts or screws to maintain stability and reduce wobble.
- Protect the chair from heavy weight or aggressive swinging, which can cause wear on the joints over time.

With a little care, this chair can remain a staple on your patio for seasons to come. It is not a throwaway solution; it is a long term friend who ages gracefully, like a vintage gadget that never loses its charm.
