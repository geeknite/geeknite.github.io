## Maintenance, Care, and Everyday TLC
A rug that’s easy to care for is a rug you’ll actually keep, and the Quarry Oyster fits that bill pretty well. Here’s the practical care plan we recommend:

- Vacuum regularly: Aim for 2–3 times per week in high-traffic areas. Use a vacuum with a beater bar that isn’t too aggressive to avoid pulling fibers. If you have pets that shed, a quick run over with a high-suction vacuum helps keep hair at bay.
- Spot cleaning: For most spills, blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth and a mild detergent solution. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the color or fibers over time.
- Deep cleaning: Depending on your climate and foot traffic, a light steam clean every 6–12 months is usually sufficient. If you choose to steam clean, test a small inconspicuous area first to ensure no color bleed.
- Pad use: A quality rug pad is your friend. It reduces bunching, increases comfort, and helps keep the rug in place. If you have an especially slippery floor, a pad can transform the hallway experience from “slip ‘n slide” to “hello, cozy media-walkway.”
- Sun exposure: Prolonged direct sun can subtly affect color over time. If your runner sits in a sunlit corridor, rotate the rug or use window coverings to minimize fading.

## Size, Fit, and Placement: Does 2x8 Hit the Mark?
A 2x8 runner is a versatile size for many homes. It fits standard hallways, a long entryway, or a compact kitchen corridor without overwhelming the space. Here are practical guidelines to optimize placement:

- Width alignment: If your hallway is around 3–4 feet wide, a 2-foot-wide rug creates a comfortable border on each side while preserving a clear path for doors and furniture.
- End spacing: Leave a little space at the ends so doors can swing freely and you can step onto the rug without tripping. A couple of inches of bare floor at each end often looks intentional and tidy.
- Transitions: If you’re bridging two rooms with a transition strip, ensure the rug sits flat against the floor across the threshold. A rug pad is again your best friend here.
- Padding: A good rug pad is essential. It reduces creeping, provides cushioning for foot traffic, and helps the rug keep its shape over time. It also makes vacuuming easier by giving the fibers something to push against.
