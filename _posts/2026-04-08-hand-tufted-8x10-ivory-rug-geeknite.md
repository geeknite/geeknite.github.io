## Materials and Build Quality
### New Zealand Wool
NZ wool is prized for resilience and feel. The wool fibers respond well to foot traffic and recover after being stepped on. The fiber durability translates to fewer visible wear lines compared to cheaper wools. The texture is soft but with a bite that prevents a squishy feel underfoot. If you have pets, the wool is often easier to clean than synthetic fibers and tolerates brushing. 

### Handmade Tufting
Hand tufting involves a tufting gun or needle that inserts tufts into a backing fabric. The precision in this rug comes from the artisan adjusting pile height to produce the high-low effect. It requires patience and skill, the sort of craftsmanship you admire while sipping coffee and streaming a late-night sci-fi series. The result is a rug with character rather than a perfectly uniform mass. 

### Backing and Durability
Backings vary by maker, but a robust backing ensures the tufts stay anchored. The care instructions will often specify rotating the rug and using a rug pad to prevent slipping. The dicey part of handmade items is sometimes subtle inconsistencies in the backing that add character; the good news is that a well-made rug will age gracefully and reveal layers of texture rather than degrade quickly.

## Care and Maintenance
### Cleaning Basics
Regular vacuuming with a beater bar on low suction helps lift dirt from the high-low texture without pulling tufts. You should rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. When spills happen, blot with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the pile. For stubborn stains, consult the maker’s care guide rather than chasing DIY hazard sequences on the internet. 

### Stain Strategies
Immediate action helps; blot, then treat with a wool-safe cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that can fade color or degrade fibers. Test any cleaner in an inconspicuous corner before applying widely. 

### Protection and Placement
Avoid direct sunlight for long periods as ivory can show color shifts. A rug pad is essential to prevent slipping, focus the sofa, and protect the floor underneath. If you have a pet, consider training or a small barrier to keep nails from snagging high-low ridges.
