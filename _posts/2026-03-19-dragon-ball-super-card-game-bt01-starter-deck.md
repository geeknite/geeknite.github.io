## Core mechanics and how BT01 uses them
Dragon Ball Super Card Game thrives on a handful of core mechanics, and BT01 is designed to demonstrate them cleanly. Here are the pillars you’ll encounter, explained with minimal spoilers and maximum sarcasm.

### The energy system and resource management
Energy is the fuel that powers your plays. In BT01, you’ll learn to manage a limited pool of energy each turn, deciding what to spend now and what to save for the epic firework display later. A good BT01 player will plan three moves ahead while pretending to be surprised when the other person counters your best laid plan with a card that defies your calculations. It’s chess, but with glowing orbs and more punching.

### Leading with a Leader card
Your Leader card is your central strategist and your face on the battlefield. It’s the symbol of your deck’s identity and the vessel for your game plan. BT01’s Leader is designed to comfortably accommodate mid-range aggression and a few surprising pivots, which means you won’t be locked into a single cheesed pathway after the first two losses. The Leader’s abilities usually set up a theme—like boosting your early pressure or enabling a defined late-game finisher—without requiring you to throw away your entire playbook when your draws misbehave.

### Card types and draftable synergy
Characters drive your aggression and control the tempo. Events give you powerful one-shot effects that can swing a turn in your favor, and Extras buff your Characters or alter the battlefield in subtle, delicious ways. BT01 demonstrates how these pieces fit together: you don’t want to overcommit to a single play; you want a deck that feels like it has both a plan and a few backup plans with emergency snacks for the long game.

### The attack window and timing
Timing is everything. The BT01 deck teaches you to balance aggression with defense, to read your opponent’s likely responses, and to weave in one or two surprise plays that force your opponent into a corner. The thrill of landing a well-timed combo is part tactical, part theatrical—think of it as a magical moment where you feel like you’ve hacked the narrative of the anime and pressed play on a signpost moment.
