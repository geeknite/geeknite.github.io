### Screens and Connectivity
The phrase with screens is a bold one, and the truth is that there are two flavors. Some markets ship with a built-in screen system, a turnkey cluster of four monitors that rest above the central hub, giving each user a dedicated window to doomscroll or spreadsheet through synergy. Other markets push a modular approach: you bring your own screens, mount them on arms, and rely on a central power spine that keeps the party alive. Either way, the ergonomic promise remains: screens at roughly eye level, chairs adjusted to a comfortable viewing angle, and a cable web that stays largely invisible during meetings.

If you do opt for built-in screens, you gain uniform brightness and color consistency across the crew, a boon for those who enjoy collaboration without the mutinous glare of mismatched monitors. If you choose a modular route, you can swap in ultra-wide displays or 4K panels as your budget and taste allow. Either way, the central hub provides a clean corridor for data, with USB-C pass-through and optional docking stations to keep laptops fully fueled without a trip to the power store every hour.

For nerds who care about postural health, the screen height is adjustable across the stations when used with compatible arms. We strongly recommend aligning the top of the screen to around 0.5 to 0.6 times your seated height for optimal neck comfort. If you have a taller coworker or a shorter one, you will likely want a shared monitor on an ergonomic arm with tilt adjustments so no one reads the spreadsheet at neck-breaking angles.

### Assembly and Maintenance
There is a reason modular desks exist, and that reason is not always patience. The Infinite 4P is designed to be assembled with a basic toolkit, and it benefits from two to three people helping, especially when aligning the corner joints. In our test, we opted for a small team of ambitious dreamers, a measuring tape, and a lot of sarcasm. The instruction manual is clear, though perhaps not as sassy as your favorite online supervisor; still, the steps flow logically and the resulting rigidity is solid enough to survive the occasional forklift of chairs and the rare attempt to stand up to the monitors when someone forgets to unplug a cable. Maintaining the desk is straightforward: wipe with a damp cloth, avoid abrasive cleaners, and dust the under-desk cable channels with a gentle breeze of forced air or a breath of secondhand chaos from the environment of your office.
