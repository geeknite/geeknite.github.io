## What is Zombicide Black Plague? A quick refresher
Zombicide Black Plague is the medieval-flavored reimagining of the classic Zombicide system: a cooperative dungeon crawl in a plague-ridden fantasy world where heroes sprint across a modular board, fight zombies, collect equipment, and complete mission objectives before the undead overwhelm the party. The game uses an action economy: each survivor gets a few actions per turn to move, attack, search for loot, or interact with the environment. The zombies are controlled by a deck of cards that dictates if they wander, surge, or unleash horde-level mayhem. The base game has multiple expansions and a thriving fan community, but the Black Plague line remains one of the most beloved boil-and-bite versions for its Grimdark vibe and tight, bite-sized missions.

What Adrian Smith brings to the table is a fusion of his epic fantasy art with a hero that has a few extra tricks up a necromancer cloak. Cul'Nar is meant to feel like a champion who can bend the battlefield to his will, rather than just a strong fighter who wades into an army of moldering corpses. The result is a campaign-ready vibe with a single battalion of miniatures waltzing around the board while painting bleeds onto the terrain like a dark sunset over a moat of blood.

### Cul'Nar: The hero with a brush in his hand
Cul'Nar isn’t just a stat block with a big number; he’s a character whose abilities feel like they could have stepped out of one of Smith's paintings. He offers dynamic action economy: he can carve paths through zombies, control space with aura-like effects, and pull off combo plays that cause arcs of damage to ripple across the board. The kit includes a unique miniature, a hero card, two or three artifact-style items, and a handful of objective cards that reward meticulous positioning and creative use of his powers.

#### Cul'Nar’s toolkit (highlights)
- A power that lets Cul'Nar place a zone of control, turning crowded corridors into chokepoints that punch through multiple zombies per turn.
- A special attack that scales with the number of enemies nearby, encouraging edge-of-the-board positioning rather than straight-forward slugfests.
- An artifact mechanic that adds one or two power-ups per mission if players meet tight environmental conditions.

The result is a hero who rewards careful planning, creative terrain exploitation, and a willingness to risk a few extra turns for a spectacular payoff. If you like playing chess with a cathedral of undead as your board, Cul'Nar fits the bill nicely.

## Mechanics deep dive: how Cul'Nar changes the game
The core engine of Zombicide remains intact in this edition: you move, you attack, you search, you survive. Cul'Nar changes things by adding a few layers of strategic depth that reward careful planning and spatial awareness.

### Positioning, zone control, and crowd management (H3)
Cul'Nar introduces a power that creates a zone of control—think of it as a temporary anti-zombie bubble that slows their advance and punishes reckless blunders. Around this zone, zombies have a higher chance of being swatted away by your party’s coordinated punches. This mechanic forces players to think about lines of approach, the timing of engagements, and the importance of chunking the board into safe lanes.

### The artful strike (H3)
His signature attack scales with how many enemies you can line up in a single sweep. It’s the sort of move that makes you grin when you pull it off and groan when the dice betray you. The payoff is big, but you pay a price in setup time and positioning—this is not a move you use on autopilot; it demands attention.

### Artifact economy (H3)
Cul'Nar’s artifacts encourage the team to pursue a few environmental objectives rather than brute force. They reward planful exploration and the use of space, not just the biggest swing. In practice, this can swing a mission from dice-buckled chaos to elegant, cinematic takedown sequences where teamwork shines.

### Difficulty balance (H3)
The guest edition does not simply crank up the zombie count. It nudges the difficulty curve by rewarding players who stay sharp and coordinate. For newcomers, it can be a steep but fair learning curve; for veterans, Cul'Nar offers a new toolkit that spices up familiar scenarios without feeling gimmicky.
