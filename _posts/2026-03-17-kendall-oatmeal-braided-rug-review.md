## Design and Materials: What’s Inside the Braids?

## Comfort, Durability, and Everyday Use
### Underfoot feel and living-room vibes
The underfoot texture of a braided rug is famously cozy. It’s not plush like a memory-foam paradise, but it’s not a flat, scratchy surface either. You get a springy, cushioned feel that makes you want to kick off your shoes and waltz through the room—though I wouldn’t recommend an actual dance party unless you’ve cleared the furniture first. The braided construction adds a tactile dimension that makes walking across the rug a little more interesting than stepping on a plain mat. It’s the kind of rug that invites you to linger while you sip tea and pretend you’re living in a home decor catalog.

### Durability in real life
Let’s keep it real: a braided rug is not a miracle shield against spills, stains, or toddlers’ art projects that involve glitter and stickers. It does, however, hold up better than a lot of cheaper flat-weave rugs due to the braided strands distributing wear more evenly. For households with pets, the texture can help hide minor fur tumbleweeds and small scuffs. The key is to vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to prevent braids from flattening. If you have a vacuum with a beater bar, switch to a low-suction setting or use a hard floor attachment to avoid snagging.

### Cleaning and maintenance: the not-so-glamorous truth
Stain removal with braided cotton blends can be a chore. The coffee, red wine, and spaghetti sauce scenarios will test your patience more than your appetite. For most common spills, blotting immediately is your best friend. Avoid rubbing or scrubbing, which can push the stain deeper into the fibers. A mild detergent mixed with water can help, followed by rinsing with clean water and thorough air-drying. If you’re dealing with a stubborn stain, consider a professional rug cleaner who understands braided textures. Pro tip: treat a small, inconspicuous corner first to see how the dye responds. 

Maintenance-wise, rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and sunlight exposure. If you notice fringe or braid edges becoming frayed, trim carefully with scissors and maybe pretend you’re trimming for a craft project rather than a crisis. Regular, patient care is the secret sauce to keeping Kendall looking fresh rather than “grandma’s attic couch cover.”

## Cleaning Tips and Tricks that Actually Work
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to preserve the braid’s structure.
- Blot spills immediately and avoid scrubbing to prevent fiber damage.
- For tough stains, test a small corner first before committing to the whole rug.
- If possible, rotate the rug every few months to distribute wear evenly.
- Use a rug pad for hard floors to minimize sliding and to prolong the rug’s life.
- If a braid starts to loosen, consider a quick stitch repair or consult a rug professional for edge stabilization.
