## Overview

### What is Fortune Row character sheets pack?

The Fortune Row character sheets pack is a curated set of printable sheets designed to accompany the Fortune Row playset. It riffs on the game’s mythic-noir aesthetic while providing practical tools for scholars of the city and its gods. You’ll find a polished primary character sheet, backstory prompts, relationship mapping, and quick-reference cards that speed up play without muting the mood. The design is tactile in the best sense—felt-tip friendly and printer-friendly at once.

### Who is this for?

If you’re running Fortune Row with a full table, or if you have a rotating cast of players who need something a little more than a blank page, this pack is for you. It’s especially handy for GMs who want a consistent visual language across sessions, and it’s great for players who like to keep a tidy, organized character sheet that still speaks to the setting.

## Design and Layout

### Typography and color

The typography is modern, with a slight noir tilt. The headers stand out just enough to guide eyes across the sheet during a tense moment, while the body text remains legible without demanding a magnifying glass. Color usage is restrained but purposeful: panels with a light blue for core attributes, a greenish accent for relationships, and a blush tone for backstory prompts. It’s not flashy in the loud sense; it’s flashy in the “I can actually read this under a bad lamp” sense, which is exactly what you want when you’re trying not to misread your own character’s motivations in the middle of a chase.

### Readability and accessibility

The pack plays nicely with standard printers and folds well for binder storage. The lines for fields are considered, leaving small margins that prevent text from getting chopped off when you print, a detail that won’t thrill you until you realize you’ve spent thirty minutes reprinting a sheet because you forgot to check the margins. The high-contrast color choices help players with mild visual impairments too. It’s not a universal cure for all readability problems, but it makes a notable difference compared to some other third-party sheets that look great on a screen but disappear in coffee-stained printouts.

### Art and flavor

The art is tasteful, with nods to Fortune Row’s mythic urban vibe. It doesn’t overwhelm the layout but gives enough flavor to feel immersive. The cover art and the interior illustrations feel like they belong to the same family, which helps with immersion: you’re not flipping to a completely unrelated style in the middle of a scene. The small icons used for status and conditions are intuitive and consistent across pages, which again helps reduce the cognitive load.

## Practical Use and Play Experience

### Character creation flow

In practice, character creation with the Fortune Row sheets is quick but flavorful. The prompts help players articulate a character’s pact with fate and their ties to the city’s factions. You can quickly settle on a look—and it’s surprising how often the “Look” element becomes a lever for a scene’s style. The relationship blocks help you decide which NPCs matter most, which is crucial when you want a session to feel personal rather than a random string of events.

### Session pacing and planning

The pack lends itself to faster planning. GM prep time gets reduced when you can rely on filled-out blocks for relationships and plot hooks rather than ad-libbing every feature from scratch. It also gives you a reliable prop for the table—the sheets themselves become a talking point. Players can point to a block on their sheet to remind the group about a key plot thread or a character choice, which helps maintain momentum.

### GM’s perspective

As a GM, you’ll appreciate how the sheets help you maintain consistent world-building. The prompts and the relationship map offer ready-made scaffolding to build session beats on. It’s not a cheat; it’s a framework that respects the game’s pace. The better you feel about the sheets, the more you can lean into the world’s atmosphere and the set’s mood, and that’s where City of Mist truly shines: when players are immersed in myth and urban grit.

### Playstyle flexibility

Fortune Row’s content is meant to be flexible. The sheets support everything from a long-running campaign to a one-shot, and the visual language scales well from a small table to a crowded event. Whether you prefer cinematic action or investigative noir, you can weave both styles into a session with the help of these sheets.
