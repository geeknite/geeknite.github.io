## Overview

### What is Fortune Row character sheets pack?

The Fortune Row character sheets pack is a curated set of printable sheets designed to accompany the Fortune Row playset. It riffs on the game’s mythic-noir aesthetic while providing practical tools for scholars of the city and its gods. You’ll find a polished primary character sheet, backstory prompts, relationship mapping, and quick-reference cards that speed up play without muting the mood. The design is tactile in the best sense—felt-tip friendly and printer-friendly at once. There are even little pocket-friendly tokens you can print and cut if you’re the kind of GM who loves tactile proof that the players are paying attention.

### Who is this for?

If you’re running Fortune Row with a full table, or if you have a rotating cast of players who need something more than a blank page, this pack is for you. It’s especially handy for GMs who want a consistent visual language across sessions, and it’s great for players who like to keep a tidy, organized character sheet that still speaks to the setting. If you’re the sort who hides a Post-it note in your sleeve with all your nemeses and the city’s latest rumor, you’ll appreciate the prompts and prompts-to-be prompts that keep you from forgetting your own schemes.

## Design and Layout

### Typography and color

The typography leans modern with a noir tilt. Headings pop just enough to guide eyes during a tense moment, while body text stays legible without requiring a magnifying glass. The color palette is restrained but purposeful: light blue panels for core attributes, greenish accents for relationships, and a blush tone for backstory prompts. It’s not loud in a “concert from a subway tunnel” way; it’s intentionally legible under dim lighting—the kind of light you actually want when you’re trying not to misread a god’s intentions in the middle of a chase.

### Readability and accessibility

The pack prints cleanly on standard paper and folds neatly for binder storage. Field lines are thoughtfully spaced, leaving margins that prevent text from getting chopped off when you print—yes, the indignity of reprinting because you forgot margins is a shared GM nightmare. High-contrast color choices also help players with mild visual impairments. It’s not a universal fix for accessibility, but it’s a meaningful improvement over some third-party sheets that look fantastic on screen but vanish in coffee-stained printouts.

### Art and flavor

The art is tasteful and nods to Fortune Row’s mythic urban vibe. It doesn’t overwhelm the layout but adds flavor that makes the set feel like it belongs to the same universe as the book. The cover art and interior illustrations share a family resemblance, which helps with immersion when you flip between sheets mid-scene. Small icons for status and conditions are intuitive and consistent across pages, reducing cognitive load when the tension spikes and you need to tell a story in real time.
