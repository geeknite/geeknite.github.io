## What Is a Mechanical Keyboard?

A mechanical keyboard uses individual mechanical switches for each key, providing a tactile feedback and audible click that is music to a geek's ears. Unlike the mushy feeling of membrane keyboards, mechanical keyboards offer a firm, satisfying response with each keystroke. It's like typing on a cloud, but without the risk of falling through.

### Types of Switches

Mechanical keyboards come with various types of switches, each offering a unique typing experience. Here are the most popular ones:

- **Cherry MX Blue**: The clicky and tactile switch, perfect for typists who love the sound of their own typing.
- **Cherry MX Red**: The linear switch with no tactile bump, ideal for gamers who need fast, smooth keystrokes.
- **Cherry MX Brown**: The best of both worlds, combining a tactile bump with a quieter click.
- **Cherry MX Black**: Similar to the Red but with a heavier actuation force, perfect for those who enjoy a bit of resistance.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Tactile Feedback**: The satisfying click and tactile response make typing an enjoyable experience.
- **Durability**: Mechanical keyboards are built to last, often outliving their membrane counterparts.
- **Customization**: From keycaps to switches, mechanical keyboards can be fully customized to suit your preferences.
- **N-Key Rollover**: Allows multiple keys to be pressed simultaneously without ghosting, essential for gamers.

### Cons

- **Price**: Mechanical keyboards can be pricey, especially high-end models with fancy switches and RGB lighting.
- **Noise**: The clicky switches can be loud, potentially annoying your co-workers or family members.
- **Weight**: They tend to be heavier and bulkier than membrane keyboards.

## Customization and Modding

One of the best things about mechanical keyboards is the ability to customize and mod them to your heart's content. From changing keycaps to lubing switches, there are countless ways to make your keyboard uniquely yours.

### Keycaps

Custom keycaps are a great way to personalize your keyboard. Whether you prefer a minimalist style or want your keys to look like tiny pieces of art, there's a keycap set out there for you.

![Custom Keycaps](https://example.com/custom_keycaps.jpg){: .align-right}

### Switches

Want to experiment with different types of switches? Many mechanical keyboards, like the Logitech G Pro X, offer hot-swappable switches, allowing you to mix and match without soldering.

### Lubing Switches

Lubing switches can make your typing experience smoother and quieter. It's a bit of a tedious process, but the results are worth it for the true keyboard enthusiasts.
