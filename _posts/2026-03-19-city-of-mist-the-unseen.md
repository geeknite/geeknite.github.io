## Introduction
City of Mist The Unseen lands in your inbox with a neon whisper and a promise. It is the kind of expansion that does not shout for attention but leans in and asks if you want to hear a rumor about a coffee cart that is really a doorway to an ancient power. The Unseen expands the base game without rewriting the manual, which is exactly the vibe we crave when the neon rain starts to fall. In this expanded review we look at what The Unseen adds, how it sings at the table, and where it might trip you up if you chase too many shadows.

The goal of The Unseen is to deepen the city as a character. The base game gave you a city that breathes and bleeds neon; The Unseen returns the favor with backtalk. It adds masks that link to information networks, introduces new playbooks that lean into faction intrigue, and hands the GM a few more knobs to twiddle when the clue train needs to drift rather than derail. If you are here for pure crunch, you will find some new options, but the real meat is mood, memory, and the space between a clue and a confession. The Unseen wears its mood like a trench coat with a secret pocket for every rumor you failed to notice last night.

As always with City of Mist, mood over math remains the guiding star. The Unseen does not overhaul the core engine; it oils the gears, nudges the gears to perform in steamier black-and-neon light, and invites you to dwell a beat longer on the consequence of a reveal. If your table tends to sprint through scenes, you may need to slow the tempo a notch or two to savor the texture this expansion offers. But if your crew thrives on atmosphere, this is a box full of velvet noir bookmarks you will want to keep flipping back to.

## What The Unseen actually is
The Unseen lands in your campaign as a curated expansion designed to push the city deeper into the spotlight. It doubles down on two core ideas: first, the city is not merely a backdrop but a coauthor; second, your masks and your information networks are your most potent weapons, especially when the world is listening through every glimmering storefront and every whispering alleyway.

In practical terms, The Unseen provides more masks, more intricately linked information tags, and a handful of new playbooks that explore less conventional angles on what it means to be a hero in a city that feeds on secrets. The new content emphasizes information control, mythic influence, and social maneuvering—think whispers that become warrants, orders that arrive as rumors, and memories that rewrite a scene after the fact. It is a dense harvest intended for players who want to mine meaning from misdirection as much as from danger.

The expansion also brings a stronger sense of continuity between the city and the people who police it, protect it, or exploit it. There is a playful ethical gray zone here: the city is personified as an active agent, sometimes helpful, often capricious, always under the influence of the masks that wear it. The Unseen does not turn City of Mist into a politics-lite game, but it does give GMs a built-in toolkit for running scenarios where you negotiate the boundaries between personal character arcs and the city-wide chorus that keeps humming behind every scene.

### Thematic core
City of Mist has always relied on a theme-first design: the truth is rarely a clean answer, and power is a performance. The Unseen amplifies that by asking new questions about what a mask is for: is it a shield, a disguise, or a trigger for the world to reveal its next layer of myth? The expansion treats the mask not as a costume but as a lens through which the city interprets events. A coffee cart becomes a front for a timeless power. A graffiti tag becomes a clue that requires a conversation with a memory long since forgotten. If you enjoy the sensation of a room slowly tilting as a secret walks into the light, The Unseen will feel like coming home.

Thematic payoff is strongest when play remains at the table rather than in a distant power point. When a player realizes that a memory they thought was private is now a public rumor tracked by the city, you feel the weight of your choices in both character and place. The Unseen understands that in City of Mist, theme is not ornament; it is fuel.

## Mechanics: tags, moves, and layering
If you have played City of Mist before, you know the drill: characters exist as a fusion of two things, a driver and a mask, connected through a set of tags that describe what your hero can do and what the world expects of them. The Unseen sprinkles in new tags and refines cooldowns that let you escalate tension without breaking the mood of the scene. The book also introduces additional playbooks that lean into the city hidden factions, allowing you to explore archetypes like the enigmatic courier of whispers or the gardener of forgotten memories. The net effect is a deeper storytelling toolbox rather than a radical overhaul of the system. If the base game was a jazz quartet, The Unseen adds a few more instruments and invites you to improvise around a shared theme.

A few notes on the tag economy: expect a few new tag types that interact with information control, memory manipulation, and environmental cues. The scaffolding remains familiar enough for newcomers to climb aboard quickly, but the branches are longer and more interesting for seasoned players who want to push a scene from a simple clue to a city-wide resonance. The pacing remains engine-like, but the gears are more nuanced, enabling longer investigative threads that still snap shut with satisfying fireworks when the masks align.

### The new playbooks: who do we meet in the shadows?
The Unseen adds several fresh playbooks that widen the range of the mythic noir you can tell. Some are direct upgrades to core archetypes; others are more experimental, inviting you to try nontraditional masks that still feel right for City of Mist. The aim is not to replace beloved options but to harvest new vibes from the same well. You will find playbooks that emphasize information control, deception, and mythic influence without sacrificing the grounded, characterful feel that makes the game sing in a dimly lit tavern rather than a loud convention hall.

These new options encourage collaborative storytelling and group ties that feel organic rather than arbitrary lines in a dating sim. Expect a few humorous mismatches where a mask idea seems perfect on a page but requires a buddy to really sing at the table. The result is a toolkit that rewards thoughtful, table-friendly play while still rewarding ambition and risk taking. Some playbooks lean into factional espionage, others into resonance forging, and a few into memory sculpting. The variety is a gift to tables that enjoy long arcs and slow-burn reveals.

## The city and the world: a living backdrop that talks back
One of the most satisfying aspects of City of Mist is how the city itself acts as a collaborator in the story. The Unseen expands this dynamic by giving the GM more levers to pull when players miss a clue or chase a red herring. The fog thickens, neon glows brighter, and there are more micro-moments when the city seems to whisper back, as if it has been listening to your party plan all along and has opinions about your chosen mask. The narrative payoff tends to be substantial when you lean into environmental interactions rather than treating them as cheap set pieces.

The city feels less like a backdrop and more like a chorus of voices that can answer if you ask the right questions. You will notice a subtle but important shift: scenes are not merely about solving a mystery but about aligning with a city that has its own agenda, even if that agenda is just to keep the neon dream alive. It is a charming, slightly mischievous shift that makes every encounter feel earned rather than handed to you by a game master alone.

> Quick note for new players: keep a notebook handy. The city in City of Mist is a web of short, cinematic moments. The Unseen trusts you to notice patterns and weave threads across sessions. It rewards attentive players with narrative payoffs that feel earned rather than gifted.

## Running a session with The Unseen: tips for GMs and players
Whether you are a veteran GM or a first timer, The Unseen offers practical guidance that translates well to actual play. The book includes ready-to-use encounter seeds and scene prompts designed to showcase the new masks and tags in action. You can weave a session around a single mystery arc or let a city-wide conspiracy unfold across multiple sessions. The guidance on escalating stakes without forcing a dramatic escalation is especially welcome; you get the sense that the designers want you to tell bigger stories without sacrificing the players enjoyment or the atmosphere youve spent sessions building.

### GM tips: balancing mystery and chaos
The best advice for GMs is not to overplan every reveal but to give players room to misinterpret signs. The Unseen supports this approach with modular scenes that can be inserted anywhere in your campaign. If a player notices a symbol that seems innocuous at first, the GM can drop a breadcrumb that links to a larger thread later. The book also encourages using the city as a character in its own right, prompting GMs to plant ambiance that reinforces the theme of unseen powers rather than relying on big fight sequences to resolve everything. The result is a game that feels cinematic and intimate at the same time, like a detective movie where every clue has a mythic undercurrent.

### Player tips: roleplay, masks, and collaboration
For players, The Unseen invites more collaboration and experimentation with masks. You can experiment with hybrid roles that blend investigative skills with mythic influence, allowing for creative problem solving rather than brute force. The new tags encourage you to lean into your character quirks and to craft scenes around your mythic identity rather than against it. If your group enjoys dialogue-heavy sessions, this expansion is a gold mine. If you prefer fast-paced action, you might need to adapt the pacing to accommodate the more literary beats the expansion favors. Either way, the design is thoughtful and encourages players to commit to the tone rather than chase the next set piece moment.

The Unseen rewards players who lean into the city s atmosphere and resist the urge to turn every cliffhanger into a boss battle.
