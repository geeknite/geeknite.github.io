## Condition, Grading, and How to Judge Value
In our little corner of the collecting world, the condition of a 44 card lot matters a lot more than your ability to pronounce Kamehameha on the first try. Grading in the DBS CCG space is not as rigid as sports cards, but it still has clear signals: centering, edge wear, surface blemishes, and whether the holo foil shows even wear. A lot with 30 good cards and 14 that are near mint can still be a win if the price is right and the rare ones are in good shape. The big trap here is buyers who assume every piece in a PR Promo lot is mint or near mint. That is seldom the case, especially in older shipments or regional promos where storage conditions varied.

If you want to judge value before you buy, here are practical steps:
- Inspect high resolution photos or request a video walk-through of the lot
- Look for obvious edge wear, creasing, or surface scratches on holo cards
- Check for misprints or misalignment in text that would degrade collectability
- Confirm authenticity with a trusted seller and cross-check the lot with known promo catalogs
