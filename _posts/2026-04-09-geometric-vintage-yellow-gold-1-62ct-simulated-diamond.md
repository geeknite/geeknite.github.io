## Materials & Build Quality
### Yellow Gold Plating: a practical luxury
Yellow gold plating offers a premium aesthetic without the price tag of solid gold. The plating layer sits over a base metal, typically a durable alloy, and is designed to resist wear for a reasonable period depending on daily activities. The good news: most wearers report minimal color transfer; the less good news: plating is a surface treatment, and heavy friction, exposure to chlorine, and aggressive abrasion will reveal the base metal or a slight shift in tone over time. If you are the sort of person who showers in a bubble of metal-loving atoms, you will want to consider re-plating after a year or two — or simply wear it with the knowledge that yellow undertones may appear with time, giving your ring a slightly warmer personality. The plating is uniform and glossy, yielding that lazy sun-kissed glow rather than a brass-like patina, which is always a win in photography.
