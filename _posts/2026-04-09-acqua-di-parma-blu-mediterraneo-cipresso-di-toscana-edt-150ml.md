## Fragrance Profile

A fragrance’s life cycle can be thought of like a mini-soap opera: an opening act (the top notes) that grabs attention, a middle where the main relationships form (the heart notes), and a finale that sticks around (the base notes). Cipresso di Toscana tells a story that starts bright, gets warm, and ends with a comforting woody hug. It’s the kind of scent you could wear on a first date and then still feel confident grabbing a leftover slice after midnight.

### Top notes

- Bergamot
- Orange zest
- Petitgrain

The top notes are citrusy, clean, and instantly refreshing. They provide the kind of first impression that makes you feel instantly sharp and bright, like you just opened a fresh tube of toothpaste before a big presentation. Bergamot brings that familiar sunshine tang, while orange zest adds a touch of sweetness that doesn’t cross into sugary territory. The petitgrain, with its green, slightly bitter edge, is the extra citrus note that helps Cipresso di Toscana avoid smelling like a candy shop.

### Heart notes

- Cypress
- Olive blossom
- Lavender

The heart is where Cipresso di Toscana leans into its namesake tree. Cypress notes give the perfume its signature evergreen, slightly resinous vibe, which anchors the citrus and keeps the scent from drifting away into a frankly too-sunny citrus cloud. Olive blossom introduces a soft, slightly vegetal nuance that pairs surprisingly well with lavender—an herby yet relaxing scent that evokes long conversations on a sun-drenched terrace. The result is a balanced, masculine-feminine hybrid that doesn’t scream either gender but quietly asserts character.

### Base notes

- Cedarwood
- Vetiver
- Patchouli

The base sticks to the timeless “woody and earthy” playbook, but Cipresso di Toscana does a nice job of weaving in vetiver without making it powdery or heavy. Cedarwood adds a dry, clean woodiness while patchouli inserts a subtle brown sugar warmth that lingers without becoming syrupy. Together, they craft a dry-down that remains elegant and restrained—a true showpiece of “smell good without shouting.”

## Sillage and Longevity

Sillage is the perfume industry’s nerdy way of saying “how far will this scent radiate from your body?” Longevity is about how long it sticks with you. Cipresso di Toscana sits on the moderate-to-solid end of the spectrum. In practical terms, you’ll notice a noticeable scent on your skin for roughly 6–8 hours, with a gentle fade that doesn’t vanish completely at the end of a workday. If you want a fragrance that survives a gym session and still smells pleasant on the drive home, this is a good bet.

If you’re in a crowded elevator or a subway car, expect Cipresso di Toscana to make its presence known without turning into a room-filling hammer. It’s not a “project-army-jet-engine” fragrance in the way some woody orientals can be; it’s more of a confident, steady drumbeat that reminds people you exist—without smashing their noses. In terms of longevity, you’ll likely get a comfortable reapplication window if you’re the type to reapply after lunch; otherwise, the tail end reads as a gentle, woody aroma that remains polished rather than falling into earthy-muddy territory.

## Packaging, Usage, and Layering Tips

A lot of fragrance enjoyment comes down to how you use it and how you stack it with other scents. Cipresso di Toscana works well with a light touch and a sensible approach to layering. If you normally spray two large pulses of fragrance, try one spray and see how the dry-down evolves. The citrus notes appear quickly and fade, but the heart and base linger longer and are more susceptible to your skin chemistry. The best practice is to apply to pulse points—wrists, base of the neck, and perhaps behind the ears—then give Cipresso a few minutes to mingle with your skin’s natural oils before you head out.

If you like to experiment with layering, Cipresso di Toscana pairs well with lighter, marine-inspired scents or citrus-forward fragrances that share a similar brightness. For something more daring, a gentle dab of a smoky or resinous fragrance on the same day can create a sophisticated “contrast-wear” that’s great for creative or social events. The key is moderation: Cipresso di Toscana is not a chameleon that blends into a wall of other scents; it’s a confident performer that becomes a better version of itself when not overshadowed by overpowering partners.
