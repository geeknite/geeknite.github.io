## Inside the box

### Card flavors and what they do

- Property cards: these are the assets you will accumulate. Colors matter for the three full sets, and the right combination wins you the game. They are your little cardboard empire, waiting for your next ruthless proposition.
- Rent cards: the income stream of Monopoly Deal. You can combine multiple rents to squeeze players for cash or property, depending on how much you want to tilt the table toward victory. Rent cards come with multipliers and strategic value that can swing a round from close to crushing in a snap.
- Action cards: think of these as the chaos engine. Steal cards, force discards, cancel actions, or demand payment with dramatic flair. They are the litmus test of your social skills under competitive pressure.
- Wild cards: versatile and valuable, wilds can substitute for any color property or act as a wild rent card. They are the chameleons of the deck, shifting to cover gaps in your plan.
- Money cards: used to pay rents and to populate your bank, money cards are the financial backbone of your strategy. Without them, you are just a sad collector of imaginary assets.

The exact card counts can vary by edition, but you can expect around 110 cards in a typical 2017 Hasbro Monopoly Deal pack. The Used Very Good labeling suggests most cards are fully legible, with occasional shuffle creases and mild edge wear that only adds a sense of character. ### How a typical turn unfolds

On your turn you draw two cards and may play up to three cards from your hand. You can add cards to your property portion, place money into your bank, or deploy action cards to disrupt opponents or accelerate your own progress. The end goal remains the same: build three complete property sets before everyone else does. The 2017 edition keeps this simple logic intact, while the art and card distribution give each round a distinct punchline moment as you outmaneuver your friends with clever deals and well-timed Just Say No plays.
