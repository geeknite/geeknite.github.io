## Overview

City of Mist: On the Streets is not a wholesale reboot of the core rules. It is a field guide to the city’s fringes—the blocks that never sleep, the storefronts that know your name, and the secrets that slip through the cracks when the sun goes down. The supplement leans into four core ideas: micro-investigations, neighborhood texture, street-level factions, and tools that help a GM turn a single evening into a gripping, mythic caper without losing the intimate vibe that makes City of Mist feel like a private eye novel written in neon.

### What’s inside
On the Streets organizes its content around practical, play-ready modules:

- A compact район map kit that translates big-city vibes into 6- to 8-block microsettings. Think Red Hook dodging rain, Neon Quarter pulsing with drones, or Old Town hiding a centuries-old grudge in a modern storefront.
- Five new Mythos hooks and six new Playbooks designed for street-level engagements rather than world-spanning epics. These are not the “god-tier” dragons; they are the barista with a secret, the courier who knows where the bodies are buried, and the librarian who can leak a memory from a newspaper clipping.
- A suite of ready-to-run encounters and seeded investigations that you can slot into any city map with minimal prep. The idea is: a GM should be able to run a 2- to 4-hour arc about a single block without feeling like they’ve forgotten to buy the ticket for a transcontinental flight.
- tables and prompts for locations, factions, and clues that help you weave a narrative where every location has a mood, every shade of neon tells a story, and every decision tangles the mythic with the mundane.

If you’re coming from the core book, On the Streets feels like a handoff: it takes the sweeping, mythic framework of City of Mist and folds it into a human-scale lens, converting abstract power dynamics into concrete, walk-around stakes.

## Thematic design and vibe

### Street-level mythos
One of the strongest features of On the Streets is how it keeps the mystic machinery visible while making it feel tangible. The core game’s “Mist” concept—your hidden self using mythic archetypes to interpret the world—gets a friendly injection of ground-level flavor. In practice, this means you’ll see more calls to action tied to tangible human wants: respect, safety, money, secrets, influence, and vindication. The city’s mood shifts with the weather, the crowd’s chatter, and the night-shift gossip, and the book equips you to chase a plot through this texture rather than around a map of gods and monsters.

### Playbooks with a beat
On the Streets introduces five new or revised street-level Playbooks that emphasize distinct social muscles: handling a neighborhood, controlling information, navigating a local criminal economy, or turning a doorway into a stage. These Playbooks aren’t just class flavors; they are micro-roles designed to coexist with the core rules while providing new moves and thematic hooks. The moves lean into practical outcomes: a successful stakeout, a clever bluff that shifts power in a crowded room, a clandestine exchange that reveals a city’s hidden hierarchy. The result is a toolkit that invites players to lean into their character’s local leverage rather than simply their combat prowess.

### Location as character
Cities in City of Mist are living, breathing characters—On the Streets gives you the bones to treat districts as characters in their own right. Each district template comes with a mood vector, a local faction map, a handful of iconic locations, and a set of “street-level legends” that hint at deeper myths just beneath the pavement. The intention is not to overwhelm the GM with lore but to spark the imagination: what rumor travels from the noodle shop to the police department to the rooftop garden? What hidden arrangement keeps the Marina afloat during the storm season? The answer is often a small act of trust or betrayal, rather than a grand ritual.

## Mechanics and balance

### New rules and moves
On the Streets stiffens the flavor with new investigative moves and micro-rules that feel natural within the City of Mist framework. You’ll see tweaks or additions to:

- Investigation tracks: a streamlined system for tracking clues across a single block, with a tactile sense of time passing as you walk from a coffee cart to a back alley vent. This helps the GM maintain tension while keeping players in the action rather than bogging them down in lore-heavy exposition.
- Social conflict and leverage: a few moves formalize how influence and information exchange shift power dynamics in a crowded room. Expect more opportunities for players to outsmart one another, use misdirection, or exploit a local tradition to gain access to a restricted location.
- Street-level Mist mechanics: these are simpler, scalable abstractions of the core Mist rules designed for quick resolution in a sprint session. They don’t dilute the mythic vibe; they just keep the action moving when you’re chasing down a lead instead of sheltering under a rooftop umbrella.

In practice, the balance feels like a good fit for one-shot investigations or a short campaign arc that sits comfortably between a coffeehouse table and a late-night stakeout. It is not designed to replace the core play style; it’s designed to enhance it by offering more levers for players to pull in a city that feels alive and a mystery that refuses to be muffled by a single meeting room.

### Compatibility with core rules
If you already own City of Mist, On the Streets slots neatly into your existing table. The districts and Playbooks have enough variety to keep sessions fresh, but not so much that you drown in options. The advice for GMs who want to scale a story from block-level intrigue to city-wide upheaval is clear: start small, let the neighborhood’s rhythm shape the plot, and then gradually widen the scope as the characters’ stakes escalate. The supplement respects the core sandbox design while offering a structured path to keep a campaign focused on character dynamics rather than map traversal.

## GM tools and encounter design

### Ready-to-run seeds
For GMs who crave instant sparks, On the Streets provides a batch of seeds curated for street-level sessions. Each seed includes:

- A brief premise and the block’s mood
- A list of key NPCs with clear aims and secrets
- A set of clues tailored to the district’s flavor
- A potential twist that reorients the investigation toward a new local power

These seeds are deliberately modular, so you can mix, match, and transplant them into your city without requiring a fresh sheet of notes for every table turn. They’re designed to be quick to run and satisfying to resolve, which is a blessing for time-strapped groups who still want to feel like hard-boiled detectives in a living city.

### Scenes and encounter pacing
The book pushes a scene-first approach: each scene has a purpose, a local color, and a clear spotlight moment for one or two players. You’ll find suggestions for pacing that align with City of Mist’s mood—pulsing, moody, and a touch dangerous. Expect prompts like: a crowd exchange that reveals a faction’s hierarchy, a back-alley deal that redefines a character’s Mist power, or a late-night chase through rain-slick streets that tests a player’s quick thinking and timing.

### Faction dynamics on a block level
One of On the Streets’ clever additions is a faction map tuned to a neighborhood’s tensions. You’ll see how rival storefronts, a union organizer, a street-preacher, and a police liaison drift into a delicate balance. The mechanics encourage players to engage with these factions, not merely as obstacles, but as living systems—the city’s corroded arteries that can either pump oxygen into a plot or pump it full of rust if mismanaged.

## Practical usage: running a street arc

If you’re planning a weekend arc or a four-session mini-campaign, On the Streets shines when you lean into its strengths:

- Start with a single district and its mood. Let a few iconic locations drive the investigation, then layer in NPCs who care about the district’s fate. The mystery grows as you switch from storefronts to backroom politics to a rooftop meeting under a flickering streetlamp.
- Use the seeds as your loose outline rather than a rigid script. A seed should feel investigatory and modular, giving players agency over how they pursue the truth and what costs they’re willing to pay.
- Swap in the new Playbooks to keep the table dynamic. The street-level angles complement the core rules by offering different routes to success: social leverage, information control, stealth and escape artistry, or a blend of all three.

### Sample scene outline: the night market misdirection
1) The block wakes up as a drizzle falls. Neon signs hum and puddles reflect the city’s underside. A vendor reports a missing courier who supposedly carried a map to a secret warehouse.
2) Players chase leads through a midnight market where rumors outpace facts. They’ll interrogate a chef with a memory like a crime scene and a cartographer who can redraw a map from memory.
3) The path leads to a warehouse where a misdirection trap has already siphoned two factions’ attention. A clock ticks toward a reveal that could shift the district’s balance of power.
4) The final moment hinges on a choice: expose the truth to the public, blackmail a key NPC for protection, or walk away with a secret that belongs to someone else.

## Multiplayer dynamics and storytelling tips

City of Mist thrives on a blend of interpersonal theater and noir mystery. On the Streets emphasizes collaboration and role-driven storytelling. Here are tips to maximize your table experience:

- Embrace the block as a character. Ask players what the neighborhood wants and how its identity could be a source of power or vulnerability for the characters. Let the district’s mood influence the scenes, not just the background.
- Balance spotlight time. With street-level play, you’ll often have several players who want a moment to shine in a scene that’s more about talk than about rolls. Use the new moves and seeds to ensure everyone has a moment to contribute to the arc’s forward motion.
- Leverage local stakes. The best moments come when a character’s personal story intersects with a district’s loyalty or grievance. Encourage players to reveal a street-block memory that explains why their Mist matters in this particular block.

## Playability and group fit

On the Streets is best suited for groups that enjoy roleplay-driven sessions with a dash of puzzle-solving and a love of character-driven mysteries. If your table's rhythm tends toward long-form campaigns, you can still incorporate On the Streets by running a city-block arc as a bridge between larger adventures, or as a season-long arc with a rotating cast of investigators. Veterans will appreciate the new moves and the way it reframes familiar mechanics in a way that makes the city feel more tactile and alive.
