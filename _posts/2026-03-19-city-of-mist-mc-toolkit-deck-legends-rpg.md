## Overview

City of Mist blends modern noir with mythic power, asking players to embody heroes who are more myth than man and more rumor than legend. The MC Toolkit Deck Legends RPG package attempts to streamline GM prep while expanding the mythic toolkit with deck based prompts and modular encounters. If you enjoy the vibe of alleys that reek of rain, neon that smells suspiciously like fate, and a city that could be a character all by itself, this kit is likely to charm you with its aesthetic and flexibility. If you prefer a rigid, rules-light game where you can measure every action with a calculator, you might want to temper your enthusiasm and consider a slower burn of games and snacks.

For the nerds among us who enjoy the micro details, City of Mist uses a Mythos and Logos framework to form the backbone of character facets and plot hooks. Mythos embodies the superhuman, the strange, and the fate whisperer inside a hero, while Logos anchors those powers to the world and the banal. The MC Toolkit is designed to help you run that tension without needing to conjure up a new cheat sheet every 15 minutes. The Deck Legends RPG part adds a deck of prompts and inspiration cards that can be shuffled into scenes, turning a normal encounter into a scene with a pulse and a citywide metaphor ready to break out.

## What’s inside the kit

The MC Toolkit portion comes with a GM screen style companion, a stack of encounter prompts, location cards, and a handful of villain and ally templates. It is the sort of kit that makes you feel like you are entering a retrofitted noir lair where the coffee is cold and the fate of the city is written in lipstick on a glass. The GM screen, while not a full-on wall, is sturdy enough to survive a night of frantic note-taking and the occasional coffee spill. The encounter prompts are modular, which you can mix and match to generate a scene on the fly when the players exemplify the surprising level of chaos that only a table full of grown adults can bring. You’ll also find a set of fate tokens, some basic NPC cards, and a few setting prompts that nudge players toward mythic interpretations of everyday places.

### The Deck Legends RPG components

The Deck Legends RPG deck is a treasure chest of prompts that push design toward legendary status. The art on the cards is strong, with bold color choices and moody illustrations that align with City of Mist vibes. The deck also includes some meta prompts to help players decide how their legends manifest in the real world, which is a nice touch for players who want their mythic identity to feel personally relevant rather than a distant aura they pretend to understand at the table.

The physical quality feels sturdy without being bulky. The cards have a pleasant heft and a matte finish that resists glare when a lamp is tearing through the blinds of your gaming space at 11 PM. ## How it plays

### Setup and pacing

Setting up requires fewer steps than teaching a cat to use a treadmill. You shuffle the deck and hand out a handful of the encounter prompts, then you position a few mythic seeds on the table as suggested by the GM toolkit. The deck legends can be introduced at a moment of need or used in a preplanned arc. The pace of City of Mist is inherently dynamic; expect scenes to pivot as players decide which mythic threads they want to pull. The MC Toolkit helps maintain a safe harbor of structure while allowing each session to drift into a mythic alley that is as surprising as the last pop quiz you forgot to study for.

### Turn structure and flow

The game typically uses a turn-based approach with players and GM exchanging actions that are specific to city themed powers and human-level consequences. The toolkit streamlines this with prompts that guide action prompts and outcome triggers. The Deck Legends RPG deck sits on the table like a living witness; pull a card and a new element becomes a twist that the players must acknowledge. The result is a session that can feel like watching a crime drama scripted by a poet who forgot to bring a calendar. It is chaotic in the best sense — players feel the weight of myth, even if it is a little ridiculous to watch a private eye attempt to arrest a dragon made of rain.

### Balancing myth and reality

One of the joys and perils of City of Mist is balancing larger-than-life myth with the grind of street level reality. The MC Toolkit leans into this by offering hooks that are both grand and grounded. The legend deck does not turn the game into a pure fantasy waltz; rather, it creates opportunities for myth to seep into the mundane world. You can end up with scenes where a legend chooses to reveal themselves during a routine grocery run, which is exactly the kind of low key chaos this system loves. The result is a game that can swing from brooding detective noir to a rooftop chase scene that would feel at home in a comic book panel, and the transitions happen with surprising grace given how deck based prompts can sometimes feel like chaos masquerading as design.
