## In-depth: Custom sizing vs 8x10 standard
### Custom sizing: pros and caveats
Pros:
- Perfect fit for irregular rooms, alcoves, or floorplans that refuse to be simple rectangles.
- The yarns often align with your space’s geometry, giving a tidy, built-to-fit appearance.
- You can specify pile height, backing preference, and edge finishing to suit your use case, whether that is a high-traffic living room or a dedicated streaming cave.

Cons:
- Lead times can vary; custom orders may require more planning, scheduling, and patience than the standard option.
- Slightly higher price tag, since custom work typically costs more due to labor and tailoring.

Practical tips: measure twice, ship once. Use a laser measure or a trusted tape to map room dimensions, then confirm with a floor plan screenshot. If you have corner walls or built-ins, note those measurements and consider a test mockup using painter’s tape before the final order to avoid any awkward surprises.

### 8x10: the classic compromise
Pros:
- Quick to order, widely compatible with most living rooms, and generally easier to move if you relocate dimensions change with your lifestyle choices.
- Works well under coffee tables, front of couches, and under dining room setups in mid to large rooms.

Cons:
- May leave under-furniture gaps that you notice visually but not in real life, which can feel either deliberate design or a missed opportunity depending on your eye. If your room is long and narrow, 8x10 can look a bit by-the-book; you may want to pair it with coordinating runners or area rugs to create layered zones.

### How to decide between custom size and 8x10
Think about three questions:
1) What is the room shape, and do you want a precise rug edge flush with furniture? If yes, custom size wins. 
2) Do you want speed and predictability, with a classic footprint? If yes, 8x10 wins.
3) Will you rearrange furniture often? If so, consider modular layering with a smaller 8x10 plus a secondary accent rug to create adaptable zones.

In Geeknite terms, it is like choosing between a modular motherboard (custom) and a bundled kit (8x10): both get you to your destination, but one gives you more knobs to tweak and the other gives you fewer headaches today.

## Care and maintenance
### Cleaning basics
Wool rugs handle dirt well and can be spot-cleaned with mild detergent and water. The key is to blot, not rub, to avoid smearing. For more stubborn stains, you can use a wool-safe cleaner. Always test a small, inconspicuous spot first. Regular vacuuming with a gentler setting helps remove surface dust without pulling fibers out. If your vacuum has a beater brush, consider turning it off or using a high pile setting that reduces friction with wool fibers.

### Stain handling magic tricks
- For liquid spills, act quickly by blotting up the excess and treating the spot with a wool-safe cleaner.
- For stubborn wine or coffee mishaps, a professional cleaning service familiar with wool rugs is a smart bet. Your local cleaner may offer spot cleaning services that preserve color fastness and fiber integrity.
- Avoid direct sunlight for long periods, which can fade color over time. Rotate the rug periodically to maintain even wear and color exposure.

### Longevity considerations
Wool is naturally resilient, but the real long game here is your care routine. A well-maintained Frontier Platinum rug can look vibrant and feel luxurious for years, especially if you commit to a simple routine of vacuuming, occasional professional cleaning, and a basic plan to address spills. If you treat it like a pet dragon rather than a floor decoration, you’ll be pleasantly surprised at how long it lasts.
