## Safety and maintenance
No product is perfect, and foosball tables are no exception. The foldable variant has a handful of safety considerations that can ruin a party faster than a glitchy video game. The most important safety notes revolve around assembly integrity, avoiding pinched fingers during folding, and keeping small parts away from curious pets and small children who believe the hex key is a shiny toy.

### Care and maintenance tips
- Regularly check the screws and bolts; loose hardware can turn a friendly spar into a tremor of wobble that makes the table feel like it is auditioning for a horror film.
- Clean the field surface with a mild cloth; avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the vinyl surface over time.
- Wipe the handles and rods after use to avoid sweat buildup and the inevitable metallic odor that accompanies long game nights.
- Inspect the folding hinges for rust or stiffness and apply a tiny amount of lubrication if necessary. A squeaky hinge is a party kill, and no one wants to be the person who makes the table sound like a creaking haunted house.

The safety factor is modest but important. The compact design means you should be mindful of small folks swinging their legs around the table or pets treating the ball as a chew toy. Use common sense, provide a safe play area, and you will maximize the longevity of your new party hero.
