## How to play (kid-friendly rules)
Note: always check the included instruction sheet, since editions can diverge a little. Here is the quick, family-friendly baseline.

### Setup
Shuffle the 45 UNO Junior cards and deal 4 cards to each player. Place the rest face down to form a draw pile, and flip the top card to start the discard pile.

### On your turn
You can play a card if it matches the color or the symbol on the top card of the discard pile. For younger kids, color matching is usually the easiest path.

### Special cards
If you play the Wild, you get to choose the next color. There are not the same Draw 2 or Skip explosions you see in classic Uno, which makes this a kinder introduction for a 3-year-old while still offering enough structure for older siblings.

### End of round
If you do not have a playable card, draw one from the draw pile. If that drawn card can be played, you may do so in the same turn. The round ends when someone plays their last card and shouts Uno.
