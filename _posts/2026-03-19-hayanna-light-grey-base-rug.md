## Maintenance and Cleaning
### Stain Resistance and Spill Management
Spills happen. The Hayanna rug responds well to quick blotting with a clean cloth, and synthetic fibers tend to resist deep staining better than natural fibers when treated promptly. For stubborn spots, a gentle detergent solution (water plus a small amount of mild soap) does the trick, followed by thorough rinsing and drying. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could break down color or texture. The key is to act quickly and avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper into the fibers. In the grand scheme of things, you want a routine that keeps the rug looking fresh rather than becoming a test of your carpet-cleaning folklore. 
### Cleaning Routine and Maintenance Schedule
A light weekly vacuum helps prevent the dust bunnies from forming a sabbatical in the fibers. Rotate the rug occasionally to even out wear and prevent compression in high-traffic zones. If you have pets or kids with a proclivity for dramatic spills, consider a rug pad to reduce shifting and to provide extra cushion for those spontaneous gaming marathons. For deep cleaning, many manufacturers recommend spot cleaning rather than full immersion. If you do decide on a deep clean, follow the care instructions and ensure the rug is thoroughly dry before placing it back on the floor to avoid mold growth.
