## Durability and Care: How It Holds Up Over Time
Durability depends on weave density, fiber quality, and exposure to sunlight. Wool is naturally resilient and can resist heavy foot traffic better than many synthetic counterparts. The Orchard Seafoam’s construction emphasizes a tight weave, which helps minimize pilling and fuzzy edges that can drive your inner perfectionist to the brink. However, every rug will show wear eventually; it’s the price of living in a space you actually use.

Care routines matter. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, vacuum with a gentle setting, and address spills promptly. Use a blotting technique rather than rubbing, which can push liquids deeper into the fibers and create a stubborn stain that refuses to surrender its place in rug history.

Another tip: consider a high-quality rug pad underneath. It reduces slipping, adds a touch of padding, and helps the rug hold its shape longer. A good pad makes a surprising difference in the overall underfoot experience and reduces the likelihood of creases or curling at the edges.

For outdoor-adjacent or high-traffic zones, you might want to invest in a professional cleaning every so often. The exact schedule depends on your lifestyle, but if you host weekly game nights with snacks that mysteriously migrate onto the floor, you’ll want a plan that keeps things looking shipshape rather than floor-shaped.

## Maintenance Tips: Quick Wins to Keep It Fresh
- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting; avoid beater bars for wool if possible.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Do not rub; you’re not trying to coax a stain into a feature.
- Rotate the rug 180 degrees every few months to promote even wear.
- Use a rug pad to extend life and reduce slipping.
- For tough stains, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in wool; DIY hacks can sometimes do more harm than good.

## Size and Customization Process: How to Get the Perfect Fit
Custom sizing can be the true game changer when you have a chunk of floor that refuses to be boarded by ordinary rectangles. The process generally involves a few practical steps:
- Measure the space precisely including door clearances and furniture footprints.
- Decide if you want a centered look or a docked look against a wall or furniture group.
- Place the order with your preferred dimensions; most shops will provide a template or conversation to ensure the edges will align with baseboard details.
- Confirm the weave, pile height, and finish options. Some shops offer subtle edging finishes that can alter how the rug behaves over time.
- Wait for production and shipping. Custom orders take longer, but the payoff is a rug that feels made for you, not cut from a catalog like a cookie-cutter clone.

If you want a direct, pain-free path to the exact dimensions you crave, consider drawing your floor plan to scale and sharing it with the seller. It helps minimize back-and-forth and gives you more confidence that your future rug will sit in the room like a long-lost telescope finally found its mount.
