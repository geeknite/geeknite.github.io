## Introduction
City of Mist keeps its vibe like a grizzled gumshoe in a coffee-stained fedora: stylish, a little goofy, and deeply convinced that myths still walk the streets. New Mythos, the latest PDF booster pack for the lightweight detective-noir RPG, arrived with the flourish of a powerfully typed exclamation point and a promise to expand the empire of legends you can chase with a piece of chalk and a typewriter. This review pretends to be objective, but between you and me, I am a sucker for mythic upgrades, especially when they come as PDFs that fit into a tidy digital library with the menace of a perfectly organized desk drawer.

In this edition we get new mythos options, new archetypes, a few fresh mechanics to tinker with, and some GM tools that feel like they were designed by someone who sneaks a little pirate flag into every plot hook. If you love City of Mist for its atmosphere, its character-driven scenarios, and its willingness to lean into over-the-top flair, New Mythos is not a revolution, but a well-timed remix that nudges players toward new shenanigans without forcing you to reconfigure your entire city map.

## What is New Mythos?
New Mythos is a curated set of additions to the base game. It does not rewrite the core rules, but it adds:

- New mythos options for character creation
- Fresh powers and tags that players can attach to their altered identities
- Scenario hooks and city locations that vibe with the updated mythos
- GM guidance for weaving modern myths into investigative campaigns

If you are the type who loves to brainstorm how to tie a folk song into a case file, New Mythos is basically a playground with more slides. It is not a module in the traditional sense; it is a toolkit for storytelling that lets you decide where the myth leads.

## Design, Layout, and Accessibility
The PDF reads like a well-edited zine crossed with a glossy magazine, which is exactly the vibe City of Mist leans into. The layout sticks to the orange and teal palette that has become a background flavor for this system, letting you recognize sections at a glance. The typography is friendly, not a prison sentence; you can lean in for flavor without feeling like you need a librarian's degree to parse a single line.

Artwork is a mixed bag in the best possible sense: some pieces breathe with neon-noir energy, while others lean into the more surreal, dream-like imagery that makes the game feel half reality, half street art. There are a few illustrations that feel like they were drawn by someone who had a particularly vivid dream about a myth that refused to stay in bed. It is charming in the way that a late-night doodle in a notebook can be.

As for accessibility, the PDF adheres to standard bookmarks, a clickable table of contents, and a reasonable page count for a supplement of this type. If you print it, you may want to consider a light resume of the art to avoid a nicotine-stained memory of the dozens of color bleeds that sometimes appear in digital art. In short: it is easy to navigate, and that matters when you are in a tense moment with a nervous player asking, what is my next clue again?

## Mechanics: New Mythos Threads and Tagging
City of Mist runs on a system that feels like a cross between a comic book and a spreadsheet. You create a character by choosing Mythos, a set of themes and powers that anchor your identity, and you attach Tags to your abilities to flavor how you can act in the world. New Mythos adds more Threads to the weave:

- New mythos options: each mythos offers a different flavor of legend, with unique tag sets and narrative prompts. You can mix and match, but the real fun is watching a hero become a walking myth of the city rather than a strictly superpowered figure.
- Additional Clues: clues in this PDF push players toward investigative paths that feel both investigative and mythic. It is the good kind of brain teaser, not the tedious kind.
- Powers and Upgrades: new powers give you more ways to juice your narrative economy. You can burn a bit of mythic energy to twist reality in a scene, or you can hold on to a cunning plan and let it ride for a whole act.

How this interacts with the base rules is the interesting part. The system remains accessible for new players, while the additional options give veteran players something to savor without requiring a rulebook-level reeducation. The big rush here is not just more powers, but more ways to tell morally gray stories about heroes who operate under a city that is as broken as a vending machine in a busy subway station.

## Scenery and Setting: The Mythic City
New Mythos leans into the core idea that the City is a character with secrets and ulterior motives. The mythic layer is not a separate game; it is a lens through which investigators interpret the world. The PDF includes additional city locations, mythic backdrops, and a handful of sample campaigns that feel like they were designed by someone who has seen one too many late-night episodes of a noir show.

The mood is polished but not sterile. You still get that patchwork Prague-to-Brooklyn vibe with the city’s skeleton of myths showing through every brick. The setting design encourages DMs to craft personal cues for players: a myth the player carries, a neighborhood that reflects it, and a case that tests the core question of each arc: what is this myth really trying to tell us about ourselves?

If you want to see how this plays out in actual games, you can chain a few of the included hooks into a mini-campaign that runs across a handful of sessions. It is not a module in the traditional sense; it is a toolkit for storytelling that you assemble on the fly, as a GM.

## NPCs, Factions, and Mythic Mirrors
One of the warm little surprises in New Mythos is how it scaffolds NPCs and factions around mythic motifs. You get a few ready-made archetypes—an urban oracle, a slumbering patron of a forgotten lane, a corporate ghost who speaks in footnotes—along with quick prompts to adapt them to your city and your players. The NPCs carry a tone that matches the base game’s noir-infused fantasy, but with a sharper appetite for mythic shorthand. If your players get annoyed with a one-dimensional villain, fear not: these NPCs come with motives and histories that invite improvisation.

Thematic mirrors are particularly well-executed. A hero who embodies a modern myth can reflect the party’s anxieties back at them, turning a simple clue into a moral crossroads. If your group loves conversations about power and responsibility, these mirrors provide conversations that feel earned rather than convenient plot devices.

## GM Tools and Campaign Ideas
One of the best things about New Mythos is the bag of tricks it gives to the GM. It is not a heavy-handed guide that tells you what to run; it is a toolkit with templates you can apply to your own city and your players’ mythic identities. The GM tips section includes:

- Quick-start hooks that work in almost any urban setting
- Narrative prompts that push players toward the mythic core of their characters
- Tips for balancing a session when the clock is running and the players are chasing a lead
- A handful of sample city blocks with potential mythic overlays

If you enjoy improv in GMing, you will love the way these tools lower the barrier to running an atmospheric session. You can riff on a myth that was dropped into a mugging scenario and watch the players pivot from investigative to mythic almost in a single breath.

On the topic of campaign pacing, New Mythos offers a sense of momentum that suits one-shot to short campaign runs. It’s a good match for groups that want a strong focal point in a few sessions without the burden of a sprawling city-wide arc. Whether you are a veteran GM or a curious newcomer, the toolkit is the most valuable asset.

## Story Seeds: Quick-start Adventures
Another virtue of the PDF is the handful of story seeds that you can drop into your campaign without reinventing the wheel. Each seed is designed to highlight a mythos thread, so you can pair a clue with a personal mythology for a session that buzzes with thematic resonance. If you love to let a session breathe, these seeds give you permission to linger on character moments rather than forcing hits on a pre-planned villain.

If you are planning a one-shot, these seeds can be your main course. If you are running a longer arc, they can serve as flavorful side quests that keep the mood consistent and the stakes high without devolving into mere chase sequences.

## Value, Accessibility, and PDF Quality
From a value perspective, you are paying for options, flavor, and a vibe that would cost a lot more if you were paying for a full-blown campaign rework. In practical terms, the New Mythos PDF sits in a sweet spot: not a replacement for the core product, but a generous supplement with enough meat to justify the price for a single evening or a mini-campaign.

Accessibility remains important. The PDF is navigable, with a clear hierarchy of sections. The writing is not overly technical; it reads like someone who is equally comfortable discussing a mythic side quest and a street-level clue. You will not need a degree in mythic linguistics to appreciate the content. The content is accessible to players of various experience levels, though the tone and themes lean toward mature, noir storytelling rather than lighthearted capers.

## Playstyle Fit and Group Dynamics
Your mileage will vary depending on how your group enjoys City of Mist. If your table is steeped in character-driven scenes and you like letting myths misbehave in the background, New Mythos is your friend. If you prefer a lot of procedural investigation with minimal character drama, you might find some parts skippable. The PDF does a good job of offering optional paths, so you can lean into what you enjoy without feeling obliged to follow every thread.

Additionally, if you run conventions or short-form games, New Mythos offers a few one-session templates that can be assembled quickly and still feel complete. The templates are not a chore to run; they feel like a well-choreographed small play where you get to see many different myths in action across a single night.

## The Lowdown: Pros and Cons
- Pros: Rich mood, flexible mythic options, strong GM tools, cohesive art and design, accessible layout for new players
- Cons: Not a full core upgrade; some players may crave more explicit balance guidelines for power scaling; a few pages could have been trimmed to make space for additional hooks

This is not a slam on the product; it is a note that you should consider your group’s appetite for mythic storytelling versus a leaner puzzle box.

## Final Verdict and Recommendation
New Mythos is a good addition to the City of Mist line. It does not reinvent the rules, but it enriches the mythic language you can bring to your table. The tone remains faithful to the original, offering a sense of wonder and danger that feels just outside of a familiar city’s daily life. If you want more character hooks, more mythic flavor, and more exploratory options to slot into your ongoing campaign, this PDF will reward you.
