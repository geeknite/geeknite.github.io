# Zombie Kittens: The Evolution of Exploding Kittens (New Sealed Edition, 2022)

If you’re reading this, you’ve probably survived a decade of tabletop fads that promised to destroy friendships in under 30 minutes and somehow didn’t—until now. Zombie Kittens is not merely a re-skinned Exploding Kittens set with a haunted-eyed redemption arc. It is a curated chaos engine that pretends to be a party game but secretly runs on your fear of cardboard cats detonating in your face. In short: it’s the kind of product that makes you say, "What have I done with my youth?", and then immediately pick up the deck again because the laughter is too loud to resist.

> Note: For the uninitiated, Zombie Kittens is the evolution of Exploding Kittens—the 2015 meme-turned-card-game that somehow became a cornerstone of every “basic fun night” in your friend group. The 2022 New Sealed Edition adds new kittens, new zombie vibes, and some controversial art choices that will either delight or terrify your social circle. Spoiler: your social circle is probably already terrified.

## What this review covers

We’ll walk you through the physical components, the setup, the core mechanics, and the moments that feel like you just peeked into a witch’s cauldron. We’ll compare this sealed edition to the original Exploding Kittens and the later expansions. We’ll also give you practical buying advice, a couple of house rules, and a verdict you can take to the pub without getting kicked out by a photosensitive dragon.

## The edition at a glance

- New sealed edition released in 2022
- Zombie cat theme with undead modifications
- A handful of revised cards and added variants
- Humorous micro-story cards that attempt to justify the undead apocalypse with puns

The packaging itself looks like it survived multiple unboxing videos and lived to tell the tale. It’s sturdy, slightly ridiculous, and the kind of thing you’ll leave on the coffee table until your roommate borrows it for 'just one game' and then forgets to return it for six months. The artwork mixes cute, grotesque, and punny in a blend that is somehow both adorable and terrifying. It’s not the kind of card game you bring to a funeral; it’s the kind of game you bring to a late-night gaming session when you want to remind your friends that death by kitten is totally a thing.

## Components and presentation

The 2022 sealed edition includes a deck of cards, some zombie-themed counters, and a rule sheet that looks suspiciously like it was designed by someone who watched a dozen YouTube explainers and decided to distill them into something readable in one sitting. You’ll find the following in the box:

- A full deck of Exploding Kittens variants, now with undead modifications
- Zombie tokens that replace the classic "defuse" mechanic in spirit if not in literal function
- A larger rulebook with variant modes that range from 'easy to teach' to 'weirdly competitive'
- A mini-card insert with jokes about literal brain-eaters and expansion doodads

The card quality remains decent; you won’t be worried about shuffling after a few games. The orange-ish punch of the sealed edition’s plastic wrap adds to the fantasy that you are about to unlock a secret game-changer. If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll notice the familiar rhythm but richer flavor: more taunts, more dramatic reveals, and more opportunities for your friends to pretend they’re emotionally mature adults while simultaneously attempting to explode kittens with a tactical flabbergast.

## Setup and first impressions

Setting up Zombie Kittens is straightforward, almost suspiciously so. The rules flip quickly from 'intro for the group' to 'unanswered questions about mortality' in under a minute. You shuffle, deal, and announce the first “boom” like a stage magician who forgot the rabbit was supposed to appear. The setup pace is friendly enough for new players but can drag if you’re a rule-nerd who loves reading the fine print. In practice, the setup takes about 2–4 minutes, depending on how many times someone asks, 'Are we playing the right game?' and how many times you respond with, 'We are, we totally are.'

One of the standout features in the sealed edition is the subtle shift in balance between the standard cat cards and the zombie variants. Some cards have a morbid, pun-filled sense of humor that lands well with fans of dark comedy; others can feel a tad unfair to new players who just want to understand the game without a mini-lecture about zombie ethics. Still, that sense of unfairness is part of the charm: it’s the gaming equivalent of stepping on a LEGO and laughing it off because the moral conclusion is hilarious and absolutely not worth the pain.

## Core gameplay: how it feels to play with undead cats

If Exploding Kittens is "Russian roulette with a calendar," Zombie Kittens amps up the factorials. The game moves quickly, with turns that often end in a throwaway, half-laugh moment, followed by the quiet realization that you have inadvertently started the chain that leads to mass kitten-lings doom. The core mechanic remains a push-your-luck-or-lose proposition: you draw cards, you dodge exploding kittens with Defuse cards, and you hope that your enemies draw more attention than you do.

In this edition, zombie cards add a layer of unpredictability. They frequently cause the table to collectively groan at the puns on the zombie-themed cards, which is exactly the kind of reaction you want in a party game. There are new "brains" cards that threaten to reclaim control from players who thought they had a handle on the match. It’s campy, it’s chaotic, and it’s surprisingly strategic once you learn the subtle dance between risk and reward that the zombies encourage.

## The humor valve: why this works (or not)

A big part of Zombie Kittens’ charm is its sense of humor. If you’re the kind of gamer who loves puns about necromancy, undead affection, and misfit kittens, you’ll be practically snorting with laughter at some of the card texts. The jokes land when they’re brief, sharp, and not too heavy-handed. The problem is that a few jokes stretch thin after multiple plays; you’ll feel like you’ve heard the same zombie pun too many times. Yet, the sheer number of puns and the tempo of reveals keep sessions fresh enough to justify repeated play.

If humor is your primary criterion for a party game, you’ll be delighted or exhausted in roughly equal measure. It’s a rollercoaster: you’ll groan at a pun, then instantly grin when a clever card interacts with a previous play, and finally sigh when you realize your plan collapsed like a bear trap with a spring.

## Strategy and tactics: reading the room, or reading the cards

Zombie Kittens rewards players who keep track of the table’s emotional state as much as their own hand. The board voice—your inner narrator—will tell you when to push your luck and when to fold. The zombies’ presence adds an additional layer of psychology: you’re not just trying to avoid exploding kittens; you’re trying to manipulate your friends into exposing their own defuse cards. It’s like playing poker with the aesthetics of a Halloween party and the stakes of a family game night.

Some practical tips:

- Watch the other players’ facial expressions. In Zombie Kittens, a glance can reveal a lot about whether someone is bluffing about their zombie presence or simply trying not to spill their drink when a dramatic card flips.
- Remember your Defuse cards aren’t just for preventing a doom; they’re currency in negotiation. If someone is low on Defuses, you can lull them into a false sense of security with a fake vulnerability.
- The new zombie tokens can be used to track who’s got what, but don’t get too hung up on counting. The chaos is part of the point; the counting rituals simply slow you down and erode the fun of absurd reaction shots.

## The art direction and thematic notes

The zombie kitten aesthetic is a curious beast. It’s cute enough to invite pockets of delight, but the undead angle pushes the theme into a quirky horror zone. It’s not scary enough to cause nightmares, but it’s just macabre enough to prompt a few jokes about brain-flavored milk and coffee that never quite existed. This is not a grim horror game, nor is it a pure feel-good party game. It sits somewhere in the middle, a sandbox for friends who want to pretend they’re darker (and smarter) than they actually are.

One note on art: some cards feature zombie kittens that are extremely expressive, which makes it easier to read a player’s intention based on their reaction to the illustration. If you’re into visual storytelling, you’ll appreciate the little cues that can tip the balance in a tense moment. If you’re not, you’ll still enjoy some of the cat memes and the way the colors pop against the white space of the cards.

## Pros and cons

Pros:
- High-energy, quick rounds that are perfect for casual game nights
- A fresh take on a familiar mechanic with enough new cards to feel different
- Humorous and charming in small doses
- Solid production quality for a sealed edition

Cons:
- A few cards lean into repetitive puns after multiple plays
- Some players may feel the zombie mechanic adds more chaos than strategy
- The box content may feel slightly oversized for the experience, depending on your space

If you’re chasing a pure, deeply strategic card game, you might want to look elsewhere. If you’re after lighthearted chaos with occasional moments of surprising ingenuity, Zombie Kittens is in your wheelhouse.

## How it compares to the original Exploding Kittens

The original Exploding Kittens set the tone for a new era of meme-driven tabletop games. Zombie Kittens keeps the core rhythm—draw, hope you don’t explode, and play a card that modifies fate. But the 2022 edition injects more personality into the deck. The zombie theme is more integrated than simply slapping a skull on a card: it changes how some cards interact, introduces new counters, and adds a thematic thread that makes the game feel like it has a consistent world rather than a collection of jokes.

For fans of the original who worried about fatigue, the sealed edition offers enough zest to feel fresh again, while still delivering the same quick-play, laughter-first experience. If you own several expansions, you’ll find that the new cards slot in smoothly; if you only have the base, you’ll still have a strong foundation that plays well with others.

## Comparisons to other contemporary party games

Zombie Kittens sits in a sweet spot among party games: not too long, not too intricate, with an emphasis on humor and social interaction. It’s less about card-drawing math and more about timing, bluffing, and reacting to the group’s mood. Compared to other meme-driven games, it holds up due to its thematic coherence and the quality of its production. It might not dethrone the king of your living room, but it certainly earns a respectable throne on the coffee table.

If you want alternatives with similar vibes, consider a few options: cooperative or semi-cooperative titles with a comedic twist, or light trick-taking games with strong social components. The trick is finding the balance between accessibility and replayability. Zombie Kittens hits that balance pretty well, especially for a sealed edition with new content.

## Post-game thoughts: the vibe, the chaos, the inevitable mess

After a few rounds, Zombie Kittens reveals its true strength: it fosters a specific kind of laughter. It’s the laughter that comes when you realize your cunning plan has been undone in spectacular fashion, or when someone uses a zombie card that triggers the domino effect you didn’t see coming. The game doesn’t demand ridiculous dexterity or brain-bending math; it wants you to lean in, banter, and enjoy the unpredictable ride. It’s perfectly suitable for game nights with friends who appreciate dark humor, quick turns, and the occasional dramatic flourish.

In terms of physical design and quality, the sealed edition delivers well on the promise of a robust, ready-to-pick-up-and-play experience. The components survive a few shuffle sessions and a couple of spills; while you should probably avoid letting coffee meet the cards, you’ll find that this edition is more forgiving than the average dice roller when it comes to wear-and-tear. The zombie aesthetic adds character without making the game feel difficult to navigate for new players.

## The verdict: should you buy Zombie Kittens (New Sealed Edition 2022)?

If you’re shopping for a light, fast, party-friendly game that leans into humor and a quirky undead theme, Zombie Kittens is a strong contender. It’s easy to teach, quick to play, and offers enough novelty to justify a revisit with different groups. You’ll have those moments when someone accidentally triggers a cascade of effects and the group erupts into a chorus of groans and giggles. The replayability is decent for a sealed edition, especially if you enjoy mixing it up with house rules or combining it with one of the expansions for more chaos.

If your group demands deep strategy, long narrative arcs, or heavy thematic gaming, this isn’t the right anchor title. But if you want something that is easy to pick up after work, can be broken out at a party, and has a strong comedic punch, Zombie Kittens fits the bill nicely.

## House rules and variations you might try

- The Flip-the-Table Rule: if a player claims they have a guaranteed win, everyone else must take a funny penalty (ridiculous hats, goofy accents, or singing a random jingle for 30 seconds).
- The Speed-Run variant: time-box the entire round to 2 or 3 minutes, forcing players to think fast and embrace chaos.
- The Story Mode: each time a zombie card is played, the active player must tell a micro-story about how the zombie kitten arrived at the game’s current chaos (bonus points for puns).

These tweaks can extend the life of a single sealed edition beyond the usual party-night orbit. They also provide additional opportunities to roast your friends for their questionable life choices, which, if you’re a good host, is part of the job description.

## Final recommendations

- Best for: casual gamers, social gatherings, and folks who love puns, cats, and a little undead mischief.
- Not ideal for: players looking for heavy strategy, long play sessions, or a quiet evening with a single, serious game.
- Value: solid—especially considering the 2022 edition’s refreshed visuals and the way it keeps the energy high without sacrificing accessibility.

If you’re considering a party game that can survive repetitive rotation and still feel fresh, Zombie Kittens deserves a place in your collection. It is not the most intense game you’ll own, but it is among the most reliably entertaining. The undead kitty theme gives it personality, and the quick rounds let you taste victory and defeat in the same breath, which is exactly what you want when you invite friends over and pretend that you’re hosting a sophisticated game-night experience.

## Quick pro-tips for your first few sessions

- Start with a rulebook skim, then jump into a 2-round practice to build comfort with the flow. You’ll thank yourself later when you’re not stuck in a 20-minute debate about whether a specific card interaction is allowed.
- Use the humor to set the mood. If players feel exposed or anxious about losing, lean into the ridiculous. A good joke can turn a bad round into a memorable anecdote.
- Don’t over-analyze. The charm of this game is its ability to deliver a quick payoff with minimal cognitive overhead. If complexity is your thing, pair this with a different title as a palate cleanser.
