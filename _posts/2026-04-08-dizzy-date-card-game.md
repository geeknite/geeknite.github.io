## How to Play (A Simple, Fast Setup)
If you’ve played a few modern party games, you’ll recognize the flow: draw, decide, perform, react, and switch. Dizzy Date keeps a brisk pace with rules that aim to be easy to teach and hard to master in the best way possible.


### Setup
- Each player or team sits in a circle. If you’re playing with partners, you can pair up as couples or mix it up to create a chaotic four-to-six-player experience.
- Shuffle the card deck and place it face down within easy reach. If you’re playing with a group, you can decide on house rules like “no peeking” and “no cheating by reading from the box.”
- Decide on a timer length. A standard Dizzy Date timer is short—usually around 60 seconds per prompt. In party mode, you can opt for a longer timer to savor dramatic readings; in couple mode, you might want a shorter timer to keep things snappy.
- Assign the “dizzy” role(s) as needed. Some groups rotate the role, while others designate one partner for the entire game. It’s your show, your stage, and your living room, so flex those creative muscles.


### Core Gameplay
- The active player draws a card and reads the prompt aloud. Prompts fall into roughly three families:
  - Conversation prompts: “What’s a memory you’d want to relive with your best friend?”
  - Dare prompts: “Perform a dramatic reading of your favorite grocery list.”
  - Story prompts: “Create a two-minute origin story for your relationship as if you were the hero and the sidekick.”
- The player then completes the prompt as the card dictates. If it’s a prompt that requires others to participate (e.g., a quick trivia or a short action), they chime in and judge the result with a playful, non-punitive scoring method.
- Scoring is optional in Dizzy Date. If you want a competitive vibe, you can assign points for creativity, humor, or vulnerability, but most groups use it as a shared laugh and a bonding exercise rather than a tournament.
- After the prompt is resolved, the turn passes to the next player, and the cycle continues. If the deck runs dry, reshuffle and press on with the same joyful chaos. The box is the limit.


### Variants and Modes
Dizzy Date is surprisingly adaptable. Here are a few modes you can try depending on your mood, guest count, and tolerance for chaos:
- Couple Mode: Best for two players, focuses on deep prompts with lighthearted challenges to enhance communication without turning into a therapy session.
- Group Mode: Perfect for a party, where you can split into small teams and rotate prompts. The more voices, the louder the laughs.
- Speed Round: Set a tighter timer (e.g., 30 seconds) for every prompt to keep the pace frenetic and the banter electric.
- Thematic Nights: Build a mini-theme around a night—90s nostalgia, sci-fi future, or fantasy fairytale—and tailor prompts to match.
