## How to Play: A Simple, Fast Setup

If you have played a few modern party games, the flow will feel familiar: draw, decide, perform, react, pass. Dizzy Date keeps a brisk tempo with rules designed to be easy to teach and hard to master in the right way.

### Setup

- Players sit in a circle; if you are pairing up, couples or mixed teams work fine.
- Shuffle the deck and place it face down within easy reach. In group play, consider house rules like no peeking or no reading from the box.
- Pick a timer length. A standard Dizzy Date timer hovers around 60 seconds per prompt. In party mode you may want longer timers for dramatic readings; for couples, shorter keeps things snappy.
- Assign dizzy roles as needed. Some groups rotate; others pick a single dizzy for the game.

### Core Gameplay

- The active player draws a card and reads the prompt aloud. Prompts fall into three families: Conversation prompts, Dare prompts, Story prompts.
- The player completes the prompt as the card dictates. If others participate, they chime in and judge the result with a playful scoring method.
- Scoring is optional. If you want competition, allocate points for creativity, humor, or vulnerability; otherwise, use it as a shared laugh and bonding exercise.
- After the prompt resolves, the turn passes to the next player; cycle continues. If the deck runs dry, reshuffle and press on.

### Variants and Modes

Dizzy Date is surprisingly adaptable. Try these modes depending on mood, guest count, and tolerance for chaos:
- Couple Mode: Best for two players, focuses on deep prompts with lighthearted challenges to improve communication.
- Group Mode: For parties; split into small teams and rotate prompts.
- Speed Round: Tighten the timer to 30 seconds per prompt to keep pace frenetic and banter electric.
- Thematic Nights: Build a mini-theme around a night and tailor prompts to match.
