### The sizing game
Custom sizing means you can tailor the rug to the space, not the other way around. The process typically involves providing your room measurements and the desired pile height. You may be asked about tolerance for trimming or shaping edges, which is important for withstanding daily wear and avoiding the “rug skirt” effect where corners curl up. In practice, the customization path is straightforward, with a confirmation step before production.

### How to order
Ordering usually begins online via the manufacturer or a retailer's site. You indicate your desired dimensions, pile height (usually between 0.5 and 1.25 inches for wool rugs), and backing type. Some retailers offer a small swatch kit to check color fidelity in your actual lighting. If you want to see the rug in action, you can sometimes request a 3D rendering showing scale with your furniture. We strongly recommend verifying the room’s light exposure, as wool fibers tend to look slightly different under warm LED lighting versus daylight.

## Care and Maintenance

Wool rugs require a bit more TLC than low-cost synthetics, but the payoff is a longer life and a more premium appearance. Routine vacuuming with a gentle setting keeps surface debris from becoming embedded. For spills, blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth and use a mild wool-safe cleaner if necessary. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the wool fibers and color.

### Cleaning tips
- Vacuum in the direction of the nap to maintain the rug’s weave integrity.
- Address spills immediately; wool is resilient but not magic.
- Use a wool-safe detergent and test on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to distribute wear evenly.
