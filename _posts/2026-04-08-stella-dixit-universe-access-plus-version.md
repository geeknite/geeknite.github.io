## What is Stella: Dixit Universe (Access+ Version)

### A design philosophy that puts players first
Stella is built on the same premise as Dixit: a storyteller uses a clue to guide others toward a card that matches the clue without revealing it. The twist is the Access+ emphasis on inclusivity. The designers lean into the idea of storytelling as a social art form rather than a speed-run puzzle. This shift matters because party games live or die on the energy of the table. If some players cannot fully engage due to accessibility hurdles, the whole table loses something essential: shared joy.

The Access+ version treats accessibility as a design constraint that leads to better choices across the board. It is not an afterthought. The large print options make card reading easier mid-round, while the tactile cues provide non-visual anchors that keep the story thread intact for players who may not be as adept at reading every stroke of an illustration in low light. The optional audio prompts in the companion app allow players to hear cues and possibilities without loud interruptions in the room. This keeps the flow going while still leaving plenty of space for creative interpretation.

### The art direction keeps the Dixit charm while dialing up clarity
Dixit games are famous for their dreamlike, occasionally abstract artworks. Stella Access+ respects the original art language but leans into clarity where it helps. The silhouettes are bolder, the characters are easier to distinguish, and the color blocks around the edges help the brain categorize stories faster. The result is art that feels familiar to Dixit fans yet more accessible to new players who might be overwhelmed by the sheer density of detail in some of the classic illustrations.

If you enjoy the micro jokes in the art, you will still find them here. The designers know that in a party setting, a few chuckles can be as potent as a well crafted clue. The universe feels like a cosmic library where every card is a book cover that wants to tell a story. The story is not forced; it is suggested through imagery, context, and group improvisation. This is where the name Stella earns its stripes: the act of storytelling becomes a collaborative journey through a constellation of possibilities.

## How to play Stella Access+ in a typical session

### Setup: you, your crew, and a small orbit of rules
- Players: 3 to 8 recommended for a lively night; the more souls at the table, the more stories you will launch into space. For a calmer session, two tables of players can play in parallel and then join for a grand finale.
- Components: The deck of story cards, a separate set of tactile tokens, the scoring track, and a compact instruction booklet that reads well beyond its tiny font when used with the high contrast option.
- Setup steps:
  1) Shuffle the story cards and deal a hand to each player.
  2) Place the scoring track where everyone can reach it. Set the number of rounds to something comfortable for your session length (usually 6 to 9 rounds works well for a typical night of gaming).
  3) Choose a storyteller for the first round. The storyteller will use the Access+ prompts to craft a clue for their chosen card, guiding the other players toward the target without making the answer obvious.

### Turn structure: a balancing act between secrecy and shared imagination
Each round proceeds in a familiar rhythm with a fresh twist for Access+. The storyteller selects a clue and a card that aligns with that clue but does not reveal itself. The other players secretly pick a card from their hand that they think best matches the clue. All chosen cards are revealed simultaneously and laid in a line for everyone to see.

The players then vote on which card they think is the storyteller card. The voting phase is where accessibility features shine: the clue text and card features provide multiple entry points for players to engage with the storytelling, whether they read the clue aloud, discuss the options, or rely on the tactile elements to guide their choice. The storyteller does not reveal their card until after votes are cast, preserving the mystery and tension that Dixit players adore.

### Scoring: the familiar Dixit cadence with some accessibility-friendly tweaks
- If at least one player guesses the storyteller card and not all players do, the storyteller receives 3 points. If everyone guesses the storyteller card or no one does, the storyteller receives 0 points.
- Each player who guessed the correct card receives 3 points.
- Players who were not the storyteller and who contributed a card that earned points (but not the storyteller card) receive 1 point for each vote their card received.
- The Access+ version introduces a slightly simplified track and an optional rule set that keeps the pace brisk without losing the storytelling flavor.

The scoring system remains simple enough to keep the table buzzing but nuanced enough to reward clever clues and thoughtful card choices. It also helps prevent that dreaded moment when everyone stares at the table and replies with the same card. If you have played Dixit before, you will recognize the cadence, with a few helpful nudges that make large groups flow more smoothly.

## Accessibility first, but not at the expense of flavor

### Visual accessibility that actually helps in real rooms
- Large text on key prompts, with high-contrast backdrops that reduce eye strain. If the room has ambient light that would normally bury the art in shadow, you can still read the essentials of the card without needing a flashlight and a magnifying glass.
- Card silhouettes and shapes that are easier to differentiate at a glance. This helps players focus on creative storytelling instead of deciphering the art in the moment.

### Audio and tactile aids that make sense at the table
- The companion app offers optional audio prompts for players who want a little extra context to guide their clues. The audio prompts are not controlling, but they provide a friendly hand when you lose track of the image on a card.
- The tactile tokens give non-visual cues about the cards in play; you can feel the texture to know which cards are in the trick lineup, a helpful addition for players with limited vision who still want to participate fully.

### Physical accessibility and setup efficiency
- A sturdier board and insert that holds up under the bowling pin-like chaos of a live game night. The pieces are less likely to slide off the table during intense moment-to-moment storytelling sessions.
- The rule book emphasizes plain language and succinct explanations, reducing friction for newcomers who may be overwhelmed by a long tradition of Dixit lore.

## Thematic and artistic analysis

Stella maintains the dreamy, surreal feel that Dixit fans love, but with a soft emphasis on readability and clarity. The artwork continues to celebrate imagination rather than practicality, which is exactly what makes this kind of game so engaging in the first place. It is not about replicating a literal world, but about inviting players to describe a world that a single image sparks in their minds. The Access+ upgrades do not flatten the art into a utilitarian form. They simply reduce the cognitive load, so more brains can participate in shaping the shared story in a single round.

At the table, you will notice the difference in how fast the room breathes with larger cards and clearer cues. The jokes come faster because players are not spending precious mental energy figuring out tiny details. The humor remains intact, whether you are lampooning a space pirate or debating whether a card actually represents a telepathic cactus or a cat wearing a spacesuit. The net effect is a warmer, more inclusive experience that still trades in the wonder that makes Dixit special.

Dixit is known for its almost infinite re-interpretations per card. Stella Access+ preserves this by ensuring that every round can be approached from multiple angles. The new prompts and cards add variety without feeling gimmicky. This is crucial for long-lasting games, because a party description game can quickly burn out if the prompts feel stale or repetitive. Access+ aims to avoid that trap by mixing in fresh prompts and a modular scoring nuance that can be adjusted for casual nights or more serious storytelling sessions.

The extended player count supports large groups who like to rotate storytellers. The interactions here tend to become a highlight because different personalities bring different clues. The storytelling becomes a cooperative performance that still respects each voice at the table.
