## How to play: a nerdy-friendly breakdown

Dizzy Date is built around a simple loop: draw a card, spin for fate, perform, and drink. The goal isn’t to win; it’s to feel the fun coursing through your date night and to collect memories you’ll laugh about at your next game night. Here’s a pragmatic step-by-step guide to get you started:

### Setup and the first spin

1) Set up on a flat, comfortable surface. A coffee table works best, but a couch cushion fortress can also work in a pinch. Ensure your spinner is accessible, your timer is charged, and you’ve got a beverage of choice within arm’s reach.
2) Shuffle the deck. You’re about to dive into prompts that range from “Share a goofy dating moment” to “Act out your dream vacation in 30 seconds.” You’ll want to keep the energy high and the judgment low.
3) Decide who drinks first. The easiest method is a quick coin flip; the winner gets to pick the first card and the first drink because optics matter in romance.
4) The spinner is your referee. Each card has its own fate on the spinner: Truth, Dare, Switch, Sip, or Spin Again. The spinner turns every question into a tiny performance piece—dramatic pause is optional but encouraged.

### The card language: Truth, Dare, and their cousins

The deck is a blend of classic truths, spicy dares, and a few cheeky twists that you won’t see coming. Most cards will ask you to reveal something about yourself, perform a silly task, or switch roles for the moment. The goal is to keep things light enough to be comfortable but bold enough to spark some laughs. Some sample prompts you might encounter include:

- Truth: Share a moment you found truly embarrassing but you’re comfortable admitting now. The blush is optional; the giggles are mandatory.
- Dare: Do a quick impression of your partner’s favorite movie character. Extra points for accuracy and dramatic shoulders.
- Switch: Swap a seat or a role for one minute. Exchange a drink and pretend to be the other person. Yes, you’ll learn how your voice sounds when you’re pretending to be your partner—this is not a test of imposter syndrome; this is comedy.
- Sip: Take a tiny sip and rate the flavor on a scale of 1 to 5. It’s a good way to gauge your tolerance for the topic at hand—also great for finishing the night with a culinary critique of your own taste buds.

### The dizzy twist: why the spinner becomes the star

The Dizzy in Dizzy Date isn’t just about spinning for a number; it’s about letting the moment do the work. After you draw a card and resolve the task, you spin again to determine the next prompt’s category or penalty. The effect is a playful whirlwind that pushes you to adapt on the fly, embrace spontaneity, and occasionally perform a dance you didn’t know you had in you. If you’re not careful, you might end up performing a duet to a song you’d normally skip in public—on the living room rug, with your dogs judging your cardio from the sidelines.

### Drinking rules and safety rails

The game is designed with the idea that drinking is optional and should never be the point of a date. Here’s how the drinking works in the drinking edition:
- Mild prompts: a small sip, enough to keep the conversation flowing without tipping into delirium.
- Moderate prompts: a longer sip or a short toast. You’ll feel the mood shift, but you won’t lose your train of thought—and that matters when you’re trying to recall the punchline you planned for your partner.
- Risky or failed tasks: a shared toast, a double sip, or a friendly penalty such as performing a quick goofy challenge together. The emphasis is on mutual fun, not on emptying your bottle.

Important safety notes: always know your limits, stay hydrated, and keep a clear path to the exit if you feel overwhelmed. If either of you feels uncomfortable, pause, take a break, or switch to a non-drinking variant with water or juice. Humor is your north star here, not coercion or pressure.
