## Custom Size or 8x10: Two Ways to a Perfect Fit
### Custom Sizing: The Duct Tape of Interior Geometry
The first big selling point is customization. Custom size means you are not boxed into standard dimensions. If your living space has odd angles, a bay window, or a chaise lounge that would not allow any of the standard rug sizes to live in peace, you can request a rug that snugly fills the space. The process usually involves measuring your area, selecting the rough border edges, and letting the manufacturer tailor the weave and edge binding to your exact specs. This is the dream of every interior designer who secretly wants to play architect but also wants to nap in the afternoon without daily chaos.

In practice, custom sizing is a bit like ordering a Pizza Constructōr pizza: you pick the width and length, the pattern, and the finished edge style. The difference is you are not assembling slices of joy; you are weaving them. Some customers opt for nearly square rooms and request a near-perfect 9x9 canvas; others go for long, narrow spaces and ask for a runner-length rug with leaf motifs continuing under the coffee bar. The beauty of a custom size rug is that you can decide if you require a larger border around the leaf motif to give the pattern breathing room or if you prefer a tighter weave for more definition in low light.

### The Classic 8x10: The Reliable Workhorse
If your space is more tradition than trailblazer, the classic 8x10 is a reliable choice. The 8x10 footprint is a standard that tends to work well under most coffee tables, dining areas, and living rooms with a central seating arrangement. The pattern scales nicely at this size, and the leaf motif retains its legibility from across the room. The 8x10 is also a great option if you want to avoid the potential maintenance complexity or lead times that sometimes accompany custom orders.

In addition, the 8x10 often balances price and space well. It gives you the room to anchor furniture without overpowering the room, and it still provides an underfoot experience that is luxurious enough to feel like a reward after a long day of staring at spreadsheets or code. The Westwood design team clearly keeps in mind that a good rug should be a stage on which the family drama unfolds, not a prop that vanishes into the background like a muted wallpaper.

### The Choice: How to Decide
So how do you decide between custom size and 8x10? Here is a simple nerd advisory board you can imagine as a tiny council of silicon and yarn:

- Do you have an irregular room shape or multiple seating zones? Go custom. You want the rug to start and end exactly where you want, not where the nearest stock size happens to land.
- Do you want a precise color and leaf motif scaling that harmonizes with your furniture? Go custom or confirm that your 8x10 will maintain the intended proportions. In some designs, a larger rug can slightly alter the perceived scale of the pattern.
- Are you juggling budget constraints and lead times? The 8x10 is generally quicker and offers predictable pricing. Custom sizes may incur additional costs and longer production times, but you gain the satisfaction of owning a truly one-off piece.

Whatever route you choose, Westwood makes it clear that the green leaf motif is the star of the show. ## Practical Details: Durability, Care, and Maintenance
### Cleaning and Care Routines
Wool is relatively low-maintenance but not maintenance-free. Routine care includes regular vacuuming with a gentle setting to avoid excess agitation of the fibers. If you have high-traffic zones, you may want to rotate the rug occasionally (roughly every 6-12 months) to ensure even wear. Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquids deeper into the fibers. For stubborn stains, consult a rug care guide rather than applying household cleaners directly, as wool can be sensitive to harsh chemicals.

Some owners use a mild wool-safe cleaning solution for spot treatment, but always test in an inconspicuous area first. If the rug is heavily soiled or shows signs of wear beyond the expected lifespan, professional cleaning is a sensible option. The goal is to preserve that leaf pattern and the wool’s natural bounce, not to turn the rug into a science experiment about stain resistance.

### Durability and Lifespan
A high-quality wool rug should stand the test of time with regular care. When properly maintained, a wool rug in a household environment can last for many years, outlasting trends and occasional fashion fads.
