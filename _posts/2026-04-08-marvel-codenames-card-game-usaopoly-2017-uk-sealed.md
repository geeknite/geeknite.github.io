## Overview

## How to Play (in Brief)
### The Core Loop
- Two teams: red and blue. Each round, one team’s spymaster gives a one-word clue with a number. The number indicates how many cards on the table fit that clue. The clue is a single word chosen to link multiple Marvel card identities.
- The field operatives discuss and select cards one by one, attempting to pick only cards of their own color. They can also stumble into neutrals or, worse, their opponents. If they reveal the black card, the round ends with a loss for the team that revealed it.
- The round alternates until one team reaches a pre-set victory condition (commonly a certain number of correct guesses) or until the black card resets the pace with a dramatic twist button pressed by fate.

The one-word clue is the heart of the game. It’s where pop culture meets wordplay. Clever clues can link disparate Marvel characters—like a clue about “Guardians” that pulls in Rocket, Star-Lord, and Drax—without accidentally steering the team toward Gamora and the villain side. It’s a balancing act. You want to be clever enough to string multiple cards together, but not so clever that your own team members get tangled in the web you’ve woven. And yes, there’s always that risk when you’re using Marvel character names as the connective tissue; you can feel the “gotcha” moment when a plan seems flawless and then someone blurts out the wrong hero with a cheer that turns into a groan.

### The Role of the Spymaster
The spymaster’s job is both the sacred and ridiculous. They translate a world of Marvel images into a compact clue that can unlock the entire grid. They must walk the line between obvious and cryptic, ensuring the clue isn’t so broad that it reveals the opposing team’s cards, while still being clever enough to be memorable. It’s a little like standing at a fireworks show and whispering, “Line up the rockets with these words” while the audience squints and tries to connect the dots. In practice, you’ll find yourself swinging between triumphant declarations—“web-slinging villains unite!”—and frantic corrections when a teammate misreads the hint and lurches toward a neutrally charged or opposite-colored card.

## Practical Tips and Tricks
### For Spymasters
- Build clues that stretch across multiple characters who are thematically linked in Marvel lore but are visually distinct enough to avoid confusion in a 25-card grid.
- Lean into pop-culture crossovers with a twist: “6-hero” clues that invoke a group identity can create a satisfying cascade of guesses if your team is sharp on recognition.
- Beware the neutrals. A well-placed neutral card can derail the best plan; keep the team’s attention sharp by avoiding overly clever hints that could accidentally trap a neutral in the crossfire.

### For Players
- Practice with a few known clusters: heroes who share affiliations (Avengers, X-Men, etc.), or iconic pairings. It makes it easier to map clues to the grid without losing track of which color they belong to.
- Don’t fear the misstep. A single wrong guess is not a political scandal; it’s an opportunity for banter and a reveal of team dynamics. Use it to recalibrate your future clues.
- Use humour. The best moments come from pun-heavy one-word clues that land just right or wildly miss the mark in the most entertaining way.
