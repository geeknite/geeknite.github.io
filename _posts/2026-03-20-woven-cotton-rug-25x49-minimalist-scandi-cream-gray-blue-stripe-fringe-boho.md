### Care, Cleaning, and Longevity
Cotton rugs can handle a light vinyl cleaner or a gentle detergent solution for stains. Always spot-test a small area first. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps reduce dust and micro-pet fur. If the fringe becomes tangled, gently detangle with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers, never pulling on the threads—this maintains the weave’s integrity.

Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear in high-traffic areas. If you find the colors fading in the brightest sun, consider a protective solution or moving the rug slightly to keep the palette balanced across seasons. This is not a miracle product; it’s a well-behaved, long-lived piece of decor that adapts to changing light.


## Maintenance and Longevity: What to Expect

Two big questions often come up: does this rug shed? and will it stay true to color? In our testing, shedding was minimal, and after the first few vacuumings, noticeable lint reduced dramatically. Color retention was excellent across a month of everyday use.
