## What is a Bicolour Reception Counter Feature Strip?

A bicolour reception counter feature strip is a small, flexible piece of material designed to be mounted onto the edge or surface of a reception counter. The version in black offers a dramatic, high-contrast outline that plays nicely with most color schemes—think dark espresso under soft amber lighting, or jet-black against a white marble-inspired top, if you want to paint a mental picture. The strip can serve several purposes:

- Aesthetics: gives a clean, modern look with a subtle but noticeable accent.

- Maintenance: often easier to clean than natural wood or fabric edges, with fewer nooks for dust to hide.

## Installation and Practicality: Easy Like Sunday, Efficient Like Monday

You don’t want a feature strip that requires a full remodeling project to install. The good versions are designed for quick application with minimal tools. Expect to encounter:

- Prep work: Clean the edge and ensure the surface is dry and free of dust. A microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner usually do the trick.
- Adhesive or mechanical fixings: Some strips come with double-sided tape or a light adhesive that holds during normal office hours and peels away cleanly if you want to switch styles later. Others might require screws or mounting brackets for a more permanent fit. Depending on your counter material, you might consider a combination of tape and small adhesive clips to secure long strips.
- Cut-to-fit: The edge length is rarely a one-size-fits-all. Most strips can be trimmed with a sharp utility knife to match the exact dimensions of your counter. A steady hand and a ruler are your friends here.

In essence, installation is a weekend DIY project that won’t ruin your manicure or your sense of self-worth. It’s close to the level of a simple wallpaper border but far more satisfying because you can actually see the impact immediately. If you’re assembling a small design team, this is a product that even non-designers can install with a smile and a coffee in hand.

The practical note: measure twice, cut once. And if you’re not sure about the adhesive’s compatibility with your surface, test it on a small, inconspicuous corner first. The last thing you want is a sticky regret that reminds you of that home improvement show you binge-watched at 2 am.
