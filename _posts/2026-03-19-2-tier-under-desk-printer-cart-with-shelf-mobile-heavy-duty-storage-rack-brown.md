### Assembly tips and tricks
- Use gloves if you bruise easily; the edges on some metal frames can be a little sharp until you’ve tightened everything.
- Don’t overtighten screws—the goal is snug, not strip-city. You’re building a cart, not a coffin for a space shuttle.
- If your space has baseboard obstacles, consider lifting the frame slightly to roll it into position rather than forcing it around like a stealth mission.
- Double-check the wheel locks work and don’t wiggle the way a cat on a rug does when you approach with a vacuum cleaner.

## Maintenance and Care: Keeping the Cart in Top Form
- Wipe with a damp cloth to remove dust; avoid harsh chemicals that could dull the laminate finish.
- Periodically check screws and wheel locks; a loose screw can turn your cart into a wobbly orbital satellite emitting a tiny squeak every time you move it.
- If you spill coffee or ink, clean promptly to avoid staining on the laminate. A mild household cleaner should do the trick without affecting the finish.
- Keep cords tidy; a cable sleeve or organizer can help prevent tangling and reduce the risk of pulling the printer off balance during a sprint to print the last page of a report.

## Tips for Maximizing Value: Setup, Customization, and Geek Flair
- Use small labeled bins on the top shelf to separate paper sizes, sticky notes, and pens. It keeps the top surface clean and makes the cart feel like a tiny, well-run laboratory.
