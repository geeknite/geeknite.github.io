## How to Play This Frankenstein of Fun (Quick Start Guide)

This isn’t a rules-dump; it’s a friendly nudge toward getting to the chaos as soon as possible. The game aims to build sequences or stock piles while using “wild” creations to shake up the stack order. If you like measured turns and careful planning, you’ll still find moments of delightful improvisation. If you love the sensation of chaotic improvisation, you’ll be in paradise.

### Setup: Get Your Colors Aligned and Your Cards Shuffled

- Shuffle the central deck (the bulk of the action). The tin’s design makes shuffling easier than it should be given the number of cards involved.
- Deal a starter hand to each player per the rules: typically something like 5 or 7 cards, depending on the edition. The exact numbers aren’t the vibe; the vibe is “you’ve got options.”
- Place the wild cards near the deck, clearly marked with the customizable surface awaiting your witty slogans or family nicknames.

### Core Gameplay: Build, Block, Boom

- Turn-by-turn play often revolves around laying down sequences in numerical order (Skip-Bo-style) and using color-coded Uno-like cards to disrupt other players’ progress. The multi-genre flavor keeps brain cells buzzing in a way that makes friendly competition feel like a brain gym with snacks.
- Wild cards: Use your customized symbol or phrase to disrupt, extend, or complicate the play. Add a little chaos; it’s part of the charm.
- Endgame: When a player empties their hand, other players can suffer a cascade of penalties or additional draws, just to preserve that drama-rich finish you crave.

### A Quick Strategy Tease

- Early aggression vs. patient queuing: If your group enjoys a slow burn, you can simmer your strategy and use the wild cards to pull a late-game twist.
- Color management: The more you align colors across your stacks, the smoother your sequences will go. Pro tip: don’t underestimate the power of a single red 3 held hostage by a blue 7—numbers have manners, but colors have clubs.
- Custom wild cards: Use humor to your advantage. A “Draw 2” that triggers a chorus of “we’re not mad, we’re just competitive” can convert almost any misplay into a laugh and renewed energy for the next round.
