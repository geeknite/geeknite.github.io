City of Mist Mythos & Legends is not a board game in the traditional sense, and it is absolutely not your tidy, dungeon-delving RPG you can finish in a rainy Sunday. It is a love letter to noir fiction and urban myth, a puzzle game for storytellers and improv nerds, and a product that dares to tell you that heroes are made of legends and the legends are made of people who forget what they are capable of when the city calls their name.

In this review, we dive into the Mythos & Legends expansion, talk about what it brings to City of Mist players, weigh its strengths and flaws, and offer practical tips for running or playing the new material without losing your sanity or your sense of humor. If you are seeking a compact recommendation: this is a must-have for fans who want more mythic flavor and a little more structure to their investigations, as long as you are prepared for a bit of a tonal ride.

## Introduction

City of Mist began as a clever twist on the modern noir pulps: superheroes not in capes but in myths. Real people with real problems become living myths when their city — your city — begins to leak with magic and danger. The Mythos & Legends expansion leans into that blend, offering new mythos (supernatural identities) and logos (human, rational flavors of power) that interact in surprising, often hilarious, ways.

If you have dipped into the core rules and enjoyed the hybrid of detective work with mythic flair, Mythos & Legends is the extension that plays more with the sandbox you already built. If you found the core system too crunchy or too light, this expansion will push you toward a more integrated storytelling style while also inviting a few pinch-me moments that only the weirdest of city tales can deliver.

## What City of Mist is at its core

City of Mist is a storytelling RPG that uses a two-layered identity system, largely built around the interplay between Mythos and Logos. Mythos stands for the legends, the magical or mythic identities that give a character their extraordinary abilities. Logos is the human identity, the rational, grounded part that makes a character capable of functioning in the ordinary world. Players build a set of Logos and Mythos tags that guide both roleplay and mechanics, then work with the GM to push a city-wide mystery into a personal crescendo.

The core mechanic is as much about narrative consequences as it is about dice results. The dice pool and success thresholds exist to juice scenes, not to grind players down with sheer probability. The books lean into mood, tone, and collaboration. The Mythos & Legends expansion doubles down on flavor and the mythic echo that powers the city and its denizens.

If you want a crisp, crunchy review, City of Mist is not exactly crunchy. If you want a game that feels like streaming a gritty supernatural drama while trading witty barbs with the GM, you have your treat.

## Mythos & Legends: what’s new in this expansion

The expansion is unapologetically ambitious. It adds:

- A larger roster of mythos identities, each with a concrete move set and thematic hooks
- A deeper toolkit for logos, enabling players to craft rational backstories that still feel mythic when pushed to the edge
- A curated set of new scenarios and case seeds that are written with a noir lens but paved with mythic imagery
- Expanded GM tools for orchestrating the city as a living creature rather than a static backdrop
- A handful of art-forward pages that reinforce the mood and tone, without becoming precious about it

What this means in practical terms is more variety in the mythic options and more prompts for collaboration at the table. The mythic identities feel distinct; the logos feel more grounded and more dangerous when combined with mythic power. The expansion leaves less up to chance in how a hero’s legend develops, which can be a boon for groups that want their character arcs to feel earned, not serendipitous.

### New Mythos and Logos options

The Mythos options in Mythos & Legends lean into a spectrum from tragic to gleefully over-the-top. You can appoint a mythic identity that feels like a modern take on a classic archetype: a detective who can hear the city’s whispers, a musician whose songs bend reality, or a librarian who can reach into the mythic archives of the city. Each mythos identity comes with a set of unique moves that drive both combat and social scenes, as well as a few limitations that keep the power in check.

On the Logos side, the expansion provides rational, scientific, or mundane foils that pair deliciously with mythos. The power of reasoning, when used in the right mythic context, reveals the city’s deeper truth. It is a delicate balancing act: you want to feel the pull of the legend while staying emotionally tethered to your ordinary life, which is precisely where the best City of Mist games live.

### Mechanics: dice pools, moves, and the mythic logic

City of Mist uses a dice-based mechanic where the number of dice rolled and the result distribution determine outcomes. The Mythos & Legends expansion adds a few new moves that are triggered by scenes that go mythic or rationally intense. It is not all-or-nothing; more often it is about the positioning of your antagonists in a web of relationships, clues, and escalating stakes.

One delight of the expansion is that the mythos moves often reward sequencing and improvisation. The GM can lean on a mythic prophecy or a legendary cipher to push the scene toward a reveal. At the same time, logos moves reward careful negotiation and the reading of social dynamics. The synergy between the two is where City of Mist shines: your hero is not simply powerful; they are a narrative instrument capable of bending both the mythic and the mundane to the city’s needs.

## Thematic texture: myth, legend, and the city

The Mythos & Legends book doubles down on the theme that the city becomes a mnemonic tapestry where legends and people collide. The setting feels lived-in: graffiti that glows when a myth is near, a subway tunnel that hums with old magic, a corporate boardroom that looks just like a temple to a forgotten god. It sounds cinematic, and it is, but the expansion also respects the grit that makes noir storytelling so gripping. The tension between a hero who wants to do good and a city that is messy, compromised, and sometimes cruel is where the system sings.

The expansion also leans into the moral ambiguity of heroes. In City of Mist, a hero's desire to be virtuous can collide with the raw needs of survival in a city that does not care about your intentions. Mythos & Legends provides tools to explore this tension through a broader cast of mythic identities and rational perspectives, which is exactly the sort of tension that makes for memorable sessions.

## Narrative structure and session flow

City of Mist sessions are a mix of investigation, social maneuvering, and occasional action where the mythic side of things becomes tangible. Mythos & Legends enhances this flow by offering: 

- Case seeds that begin with a vibe and then layer in mythic intrigue
- A narrative toolkit for building mythic anchors in the city that players can interact with across sessions
- Clear guidance for the GM on escalations and how to thread mythic reveals into a coherent arc

In play, you might start with a seemingly mundane disappearance, then realize a mythic sigil in the alley is a signpost for a larger legend that touches every major NPC. The expansion gives you more of those moment-to-moment pivots, which is where a noir RPG lives or dies.

### Case Seeds and GM tools

The GM tools are a highlight. You get a selection of ready-to-run seeds, but the real joy comes from the prompts that help you tailor them to your players. The seeds are written with a spicy mix of mythic flavor and modern stakes. If your group loves shadowy conspiracies, this is your playground. If you prefer high fantasy tropes, you can still bend the seeds toward mythic realism without losing the edges that keep City of Mist special.

GM tools include quick-start lore numbers, city-wide threads, and a frame for weaving minor myths into major arcs. It is not a toy for lazy sessions; it is a toolkit for sculpting an evolving urban epic where the line between myth and memory becomes thinner with every session.

## Accessibility, pacing, and learning curve

If you are new to City of Mist, Mythos & Legends is a doorway with a gentle slope. The core concepts — Mythos, Logos, and the idea of a city telling you who you are — remain the north star. The expansion, however, introduces more moving parts, so you may want to pair it with a couple of practicing sessions that focus on learning each mechanic in a controlled environment.

For veterans, the expansion is a breath of fresh air. The new mythos identities feel distinct enough to tempt you away from your old favorites, yet familiar enough to slot into your ongoing stories without wholesale rework. The pacing benefits from stronger case seeds; you can lean into a case in a single night or stretch it across a campaign if your group enjoys long-form storytelling.

## Artwork, layout, and overall presentation

The visual language of Mythos & Legends is strong. It maintains the noir glow and the mythic shimmer that City of Mist is known for, with art directions that feel like a collaboration between a graphic novel and a crime thriller. Layout is clean, with clear sections and ample space for your own notes. The images, while not overbearing, serve to push the mood into a place where you feel the city breathing on your shoulder.

If you enjoy illustrated tables or evocative chapter headers, you will appreciate the design choices here. The expansion does not feel like a separate product with a different aesthetic; it feels like a natural extension that deepens the world you are already exploring.

## Mechanic reliability and balance

Balance is a tricky word in a game that centers on narrative momentum. Mythos & Legends handles this with a light touch: the new moves reward creative uses of myth and human cunning, but they do not derail the GM’s control over the scene. The book provides guidelines for pacing and escalation that prevent a single hero from dominating a session, which is essential in a game built on a shared story guitar riff between player and GM.

If your table enjoys power fantasies or if you prefer a heavy crunch, you might find the system a touch light in raw numbers. That is by design; City of Mist emphasizes story over statistics. Mythos & Legends leans into this with prompts and turn hints that can turn a minor decision into a turning point in a city-wide mystery.

## Campaign potential and replayability

The expansion is built to be a long tail product. You can drop a new mythic case seed into an ongoing campaign, and it will usually spark new arcs that feel coherent with your prior sessions. The replay value comes from the diversity of mythos options and the way logos can react to mythos in different NPCs and environments. If you run a chronicle focused on a single mythic thread, the expansion can supply dozens of variations on that thread, ensuring you never run dry of new directions.

For groups who want a deterministic arc, Mythos & Legends can still deliver. The GM can seed a mythic chain and let the players decide which legend to chase, gradually weaving in the city as a living, reactive character. For groups who prefer improv, the new seeds act as dramatic prompts that invite quick collaboration and wild improvisation without losing the thread of a larger mystery.

## Comparisons to other expansions and games

If you like City of Mist but crave more mythic flavor with a noir edge, Mythos & Legends is a natural upgrade. Compare it to other urban fantasy RPGs that lean into combat crunch; City of Mist trades some of that crunch for a chance to tell a more layered story about how legends shape everyday life.

For fans of other systems that blend crime and the uncanny, you might see echoes of systems like Masks, Lacuna, or even some elements of Monster of the Week. What makes City of Mist distinct is the city as a living myth engine. Mythos & Legends amplifies that distinctive voice and gives you more dialects to speak in — both in terms of character identity and city-wide storytelling.

## How to run this with your group: practical tips

- Start with a short arc: pick two or three seeds and let the players pick one as their personal legend. This creates a compact, intense first arc that invites mythic escalation without overwhelming new players.
- Use the city as a character: treat locations as living entities with moods and agendas. A bar can be a safe harbor or a trap depending on the night and the players.
- Mix mythos with logos in every scene: let the investigators leverage human reasoning to unlock a mythic consequence, and let a mythic moment push a rational solution into a moral gray area.
- Lean into scene framing: the art direction, the moves, and the case seeds all offer strong prompts. Trust the prompts and your players to fill in the rest of the story.
- Have backup encounters ready: a few quick-scene ideas that can be slotted in if the session moves faster than expected will save you from a scene that fumbles for a resolution.

## A few cautions

- The tone is moody, not sunny. If your group loves bright, heroic epics, you might need to tilt the mood with a few lighter mythos identities or by pairing the expansion with a more optimistic companion game.
- Expect a strong emphasis on character interplay and investigation. If you prefer pure tactical combat, you may want to limit the heavy mythic elements to a few scenes per session.
- The book is dense with ideas. Take it slow the first time you read it; flag the moves you think you will actually use to avoid getting overwhelmed at the table.

## Final verdict and recommendation

City of Mist Mythos & Legends is a careful, enthusiastic expansion that respects the core game while expanding the possible mythic textures you can pull into your sessions. It is not a revolution, but it is a well-tuned evolution that rewards groups who lean into mood, character, and collaborative storytelling. If you liked the core concept and want more mythic flavor and robust GM tools, this is a strong buy. If your table prefers heavy crunch or a tighter combat system, approach with a little caution and a lot of curiosity.

What we loved most is the way this expansion invites the city to become a character in its own right. The myths are not merely skin-deep abilities; they are narrative engines that push players to decide who they want to be in relation to the legends they carry. The best sessions feel like watching a neon-lit myth unfold in a rain-slick street, where every clue resonates with a memory the city forgot long ago.

If you want a compact practical takeaway: Mythos & Legends is a perfect add-on for groups who want more mythic texture and better tools to shape long-form stories. It will reward you with memorable scenes, surprising character arcs, and the kind of noir mystique that makes you grin at the ridiculous and ache for the truth at the same time.
