## How to Play: A Quick Refresher for Retro Nights
If you know standard UNO rules, you can jump in with minimal friction. You match either color or number to discard a card. Special cards — Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four — add strategic spice and the chance for dramatic turns when an opponent least expects it. The Retro version retains these mechanics, so experienced players will find familiar ground, while younger family members catch on quickly thanks to brightly colored cards and the crisp card faces.

Setup is straightforward: each player receives seven cards, the draw pile sits in the center, and the top card is flipped to start the discard pile. The goal is to shed all cards before your rivals, with minor penalties or victory shouts depending on your family’s flair for theatrics. The retro packaging does not alter the basic flow of play, but it does add a little extra theater to opening the box and sorting the deck. The tactile experience matters here; the cards feel nice in hand and the glossy finish gives that satisfying slide as you flip through to find the exact color you need to fit your strategic plan.

### Setup and Rule Recap
- Each player starts with seven cards. The rest of the deck becomes the draw pile. 
- The top card of the draw pile becomes the starting discard. 
- Players take turns matching a card by color or number. 
- Special cards affect the flow in various ways: Skip ends the next player’s turn, Reverse changes the play direction, Draw Two forces the next player to draw two, Wild lets you choose a color, and Wild Draw Four makes the next player draw four while you also pick the color. 
- When a player is down to one card, they must shout Uno. If another player catches them not shouting Uno, a penalty is in play depending on house rules.
- The first player to get rid of all their cards wins the round. Points accumulate, and the game continues until a pre-agreed score is reached or you simply decide to crown a king or queen of chaos for the night.
