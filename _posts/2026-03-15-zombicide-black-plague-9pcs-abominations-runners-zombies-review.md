## H2: Abominations, Runners, and Zombies — a quick taxonomy
- Abomination: The heavyweight champ. In Zombicide Black Plague, Abominations are big, dangerous, and often the boss-of-bosses in many scenarios. They require careful planning, spellbinding dice luck, and an exit strategy that doesn’t end with your hero waving a hand and screaming, “Hold my torch.” They are the tanky, scary ceiling of your undead roof.
- Runners: The fast, furious, marathon-running undead who love to surprise you from the side of the board. They’re not as beefy as Abominations, but their speed makes them just as annoying when you’re trying to secure a single objective.
- Zombies (the grunts): The classic crowd controllers. They’re hardy, they’re numerous, and they have a surprisingly long memory for your failed saves. They’ll form a human barricade in front of your best piece and then shuffle one inch to the left, just out of reach of your heroic sweep.

This particular 9-piece set emphasizes a balanced mix of power and persistence. You’ll feel the weight of the Abomination every time it lumbers into a key chokepoint between you and the quest objective. You’ll curse the Runners every time one slips past your line while you’re busy trying to deal with the Abomination’s intimidating aura. And you’ll respect the Zombies for their stubbornness—like a parking ticket that refuses to fade away from the windshield of your plans.

## H2: Unpainted or pre-primed? tips for painting the 9-piece set
If you’re painting along with your campaign, here are a few practical tips to get the best look without turning your hobby into a full-blown science project:
- Start with a solid base: a neutral bone or dark gray base coat works well for zombie skin tones and makes the exposed bone details pop under every layer.
- Contrast and shading: use a dark wash to settle into the recesses, then dry-brush lighter tones on raised surfaces to highlight the bone and armor ridges.
- The Abomination is your dramatic centerpiece: don’t be afraid to push a slightly brighter wash into its gruesome crevices to make it feel alive (in a terrifying way). The Runners can be a touch more saturated to differentiate them as the “fast” threat.
- Matt varnish for a grimy look: a matte finish helps the models resist sticky fingerprints from your lucky dice and impatient gaming crew.

If painting isn’t your thing, you can always rely on a tabletop wash or rely on your own painting wizard—your local hobby store likely has folks who’ll mock-kinder you into a pro painter. Or, you know, you can pretend you’re a strategist and skip it entirely. Paints are optional, but a good gaming table looks better when the minis aren’t just cardboard cutouts wearing a default coat of invisible primer.

## H2: Miniatures and gameplay — how do these 9 pieces affect your plans on the table?
Zombicide is all about managing hordes with a mix of luck and tactical positioning. This nine-piece set contributes in a few key ways:
- The Abomination forces you to rethink your approach to zones of control. A single Abomination can shut down a corridor and turn a simple objective into a slog if you don’t coordinate your party.
- Runners add a “wild card” dynamic. They can threaten from the sides, threaten your objective, or pressure a hero who’s leaning too hard on brute force. Their presence requires you to consider flanking, line of sight, and obstacle management in a way you might not need with only standard Zombies.
- Standard Zombies serve as both distraction and bait. They keep your heroes busy and escalate the pressure. The trick is not letting the crowd overwhelm your squad.

In practical terms, you’ll want to juggle actions between board control, objective pressure, and “do-not-get-wiped” strategy. The Abomination might demand a dedicated focus or coordination to be neutralized efficiently. Runners provide twitchy threat management, forcing you to split attention among multiple threats. The Zombies, meanwhile, remind you that even a minor mistake costs you in precious card draws and potential extra turns.

## H2: Strategy tips for using this 9-piece set effectively
- Focus the first round on line of sight control. The Runners are fast enough to slip behind your lines, but if you can pin them with ranged support or force them into a choke, you’ve bought your heroes a couple of extra moves.
- Use the Zombies as a shield, but don’t rely on them exclusively. A clever placement of Zombies can force your opponent to waste actions chasing the crowd instead of going after your big threat.
- Coordination with the Abomination is crucial. Don’t approach solo unless you’re confident you can finish it in a few turns. If you can, lure it toward a hazard or a trap that undermines its movement or damages it over time.
- Resource management matters. In many campaigns, your heroes have limited equipment. The 9-piece set can be used to create a dynamic where you have to decide between buying a valuable card, healing a hero, or pushing your luck with a risky gambit.
