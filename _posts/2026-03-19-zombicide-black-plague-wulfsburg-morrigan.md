## What is Wulfsburg in Black Plague anyway?

Wulfsburg is a modular, doom-soaked fortress module that arrives in the Black Plague line to turn a simple zombie crawl into a siege scenario worthy of a fantasy RPG party that forgot to bring a heal spell. The module introduces new location tiles, a fortress-like layout, and a handful of special rules that turn line-of-sight into a tactical puzzle rather than a sitcom about people who forgot to bring their weapons. The setting—frost-bitten towers, iron gates, torch-lit courtyards—feels thematically coherent, which is sometimes the hardest thing to pull off in a board game about gnashing teeth and bad weather.

Compared to the base game, Wulfsburg nudges the gameplay toward boss encounters and multi-stage objectives. The map is split into zones that encourage the party to split their attention, which is exactly how you end up with one survivor LOADED with gear while another does a heroic, misguided solo charge into a clump of walkers. It’s chaotic in all the right ways, like a dragon who learned to juggle clubs—impressive, dangerous, and a little impractical for travel.

The design team also sneaks in some clever storytelling via the objective cards. No two games feel identical, even if you are fighting the same undead horde in the same corridor you walked yesterday. The objectives scale with more players and different difficulty levels, so your first game could be a tense sprint, and your fourth could be a siege that feels like a medieval HOA meeting where the HOA chair is a decomposing librarian with a spear.

## Morrigan: The Celtic Mage Survivor in a Black Plague World

Morrigan is the star of this show, a survivor who feels like she belongs in a moonlit glade and in a dungeon at the same time. Her abilities mix classic fantasy mage vibes with practical table-top game design. Morrigan’s card deck gives her spell-like actions that can manipulate the battlefield, debuff the undead, or simply make a single zombie roll double 1s, which is humorously chaotic in a cooperative game about planning strategies.

From a thematic perspective, Morrigan adds a layer of folklore flavor that Black Plague sometimes lacks in the base game’s grim and grimier necropolises. The Celtic myth aesthetic pairs nicely with the medieval ghouls, giving players a sense of mythic stakes without crossing the line into “grand mythology re-enactment.” Her figure is elegant in miniature form, with robes that flow like a good nonstick coating. The miniature sculpt catches details that matter in a close combat game: the arc of a staff, the crackle of magical energy around her hands, and a look that says I definitely did not sign up for this but I am going to own it anyway.

Her mechanics reward planning and tempo management. You can set up a turn by using her abilities to push, hex, and buff your teammates. The downside is that Morrigan’s power economy can feel a little tight in the crunch moments, so you learn to value your spells and conserve them for the big pushes. It’s a pleasant tension that mirrors how many players actually play board games: we overcommit early, then scramble to recover while the zombie horde continues its drumming behind the walls.

## The Wulfsburg Map: Layout, Encounters, and Thematic Cues

The map design is not just a pretty thing to behold; it’s a playground where you learn the difference between chasing a single zombie and chasing a strategy. Wulfsburg has multiple floors and a couple of vertical elements that encourage you to think in 3D, which is a fancy way to say you will be staring at the board thinking about stairs as you scream at your dice.

Key features include a central fortress hall, towers that host elite zombie spawns, and the risk-reward boarding actions that reward you for controlling choke points. The encounter cards push you to react quickly to new threats, while the objective cards give you a sense of progression, even if the progression includes a necromantic infested courtyard that makes your heroes sweat and your dice sweatier.

One of the neat things about Wulfsburg is how it uses environment to punish stubborn heroes who insist on charging through a dense cluster of walkers in a single round. You will sometimes be rewarded with a heroic chain of actions, and sometimes you will be rewarded with a spectacularly wrong decision, which is basically the game asking you to learn from your mistakes and bring a bigger weapon next time. The scenic design helps; the fortress feel almost like a character in its own right, a moody, ice-laden antagonist with turrets that refuse to stay quiet and a gate that bites back when you break it.

## Components: What You Get and Do They Make It Worth It?

Quality in Zombicide is a big part of the experience. Wulfsburg Morrigan doesn’t disappoint on miniature quality. You get a handful of new survivor minis, including Morrigan, that feel sturdy and well-balanced for table-top skirmish fun. The zombie sculpts vary from the classic shamblers to slightly more imposing variants that keep players on their toes. The boards themselves are sturdy, with clean printing and a weighty feel that says you can slam the table without worrying about warping.

Cards are durable and well-illustrated, and the tokens are clear enough to avoid accidental misreads in the middle of a heated dice roll. The art style stays consistent with Black Plague while introducing a cooler palette for the Wulfsburg environment. That color shift helps players distinguish environment from enemy hints quickly, which is something you appreciate when you are lost in a chaotic round and trying not to throw the board off the table in a fit of heroic overconfidence.

The only potential downside is that some players may feel the module adds a heavier cognitive load. You’re juggling terrain effect, special spawn rules, obstacle management, and Morrigan’s spell economy all at once. It can be a mental workout, especially in a night where caffeine levels are low and the Horde is high. But if you enjoy planning, this is a good problem to have. It means you’re engaging with the system rather than just rolling dice and hoping for good vibes.

## Scenarios, Campaign Feel, and How the Narrative Gels

Wulfsburg integrates with Black Plague through a series of scenarios that feel cinematic in scope. The narrative baits you with a fortress under siege, a mysterious mage, and a coven of undead that aren’t content to wander aimlessly. Instead, they coordinate in unsettling ways, forcing players to think in terms of zones, timing, and objective control rather than simply choosing which zombie to murder next.

The campaign feel is enhanced by Morrigan’s presence. A mage with a stake in the fate of the fortress adds dramatic stakes to the game’s usual survival vibe. It’s not just about clearing rooms; it’s about safeguarding corridors, holding the gate long enough for a final, climactic push, and managing a dwindling pool of resources as the night grows darker and colder. The sense of momentum is strong, and when you finally seal the gate in the final turns, you feel a genuine sense of accomplishment rather than relief that you didn’t spill coffee on the carpet.

For players who enjoy campaign-style play, Wulfsburg provides enough variability to keep things fresh across multiple sessions. The scenario deck reshuffles and introduces new combinations, so you won’t see the exact same beat repeat in every game. It’s not a marathon; it’s a sprint that occasionally trips you with a trapdoor, but you celebrate the occasional lucky chain of events that ends in a triumphant, albeit blood-streaked, victory.

## Strategy and Tactics: How to Approach Wulfsburg Morrigan

- Coordinate Morrigan’s spell economy with the team’s needs. The best turns often involve preparing a high-impact spell for a turn that will manage multiple zombie activations or reposition the group for a decisive push.
- Prioritize zone control. Wulfsburg rewards wrapping a single survivor around a chokepoint to buy time while others seal the gates or finish the objective. Do not be afraid to assign a scout or melee tank to hold the door while Morrigan and company do the heavy lifting elsewhere.
- Don’t underestimate the power of synergy. Morrigan’s buffs, combined with strong melee chokes from the front line, can create a push-pull dynamic that eliminates clusters of undead before they have a chance to swarm a single hero.
- Manage your resources. In Wulfsburg, the longer you stay in a given area, the more risk you face from escalating zombie spawns. Plan a few turns ahead to ensure you aren’t left stranded with underutilized special cards or a dwindling pool of weapons.
- Learn the rhythm of the objective deck. Some objectives are best tackled early; others reward delaying a bit to prepare a bigger combined action. Reading the table like a command center can turn an average run into a memorable win.

If you want a quick, tactical cheat sheet: Morrigan pairs well with a frontline survivor who can soak hits, because Morrigan’s spells can route around a bruiser’s vulnerability to crowding and let the team maintain pressure on the fortress core. In short, Morrigan wants a shield and a smile, while the rest of the squad brings the thunder and the coordination.

## Thematic Flavor and Flavor Texts Worth Remembering

The flavor text on Morrigan’s cards and the objective prompts in Wulfsburg contribute to a coherent mood: a siege at a frozen fortress, an alliance with a mage who seems to love precision over chaos, and a town that cannot decide if it should bow to the plague or fight it with zeal. The theme remains consistent: brave, somewhat reckless survivors who must improvise in the face of a relentless, rising threat. Humor seeps in through the tactical misadventures and the occasional heroic, completely unnecessary overextension by a player who insists on chasing a lone zombie through a courtyard like a heroic, but misguided, action clip.

One delightful note is the blend of dark fantasy with the folkloric touches Morrigan brings. The magical elements feel earned rather than tacked on, and the art direction of the miniatures reflects a world that could exist in a grim fairy tale rather than a straight dungeon crawl. It’s the difference between reading a war diary and reading a war diary with a dragon perched on the paragraph margins.

## Comparisons: Wulfsburg vs Other Expansions

Compared to earlier Black Plague expansions, Wulfsburg leans heavier into tactical positioning and multi-stage objectives. Other expansions often give you more zombies or new weapons; Wulfsburg gives you a fortress, a mage, and a puzzle. The end result is a product that feels fresh, even for players who have already poured dozens of hours into the base game and multiple campaigns. If you enjoy the siege feel and want a more cinematic experience, Wulfsburg is a strong candidate to upgrade your Black Plague library with. If you prefer pure skirmish chaos with more random elements, you may still enjoy it, but you might prefer other modules that lean more into randomness and less into deliberate planning.

In terms of balance, the module plays nicely with the rest of the line. Morrigan’s presence adds a new axis of play without dominating it. She doesn’t trivialize the game; she adds depth and a sense of character that the base game sometimes lacks. If you are a purist who wants nothing but base game content, you might skip this expansion, but if you crave a richer story, Wulfsburg delivers it with a grin and a mace.

## Settings for a 2-Player or 4-Player Night

The module scales well with different player counts. In a two-player night, you’ll feel every bit the siege in your own living room, with cunning planning and some friendly shouting about doors. In a four-player night, you get the full opera: sprawl, screeching zombies, and Morrigan calmly arranging a chain of spells that saves your bacon just as you realize you forgot to shuffle the event deck. Either way, the experience remains intense, fun, and occasionally ridiculous in a way that only a zombie apocalypse with a mage can deliver.

If you are planning a campaign night that lasts into the dawn, this expansion makes a compelling case for long-form play. The sense of progression, the evolving threats, and the central fortress all contribute to a feeling of ongoing narrative rather than episodic skirmishes. You will talk about the wins and losses as if they were epics, and you will be right about both.

## The Final Verdict: Should You Add Wulfsburg Morrigan to Your Shelf?

Pros:
- Strong thematic cohesion with a memorable mage character
- Engaging fortress map that rewards tactical play
- Scenarios that mix siege vibes with classic zombie chaos
- High-quality components and a robust campaign feel
- Great for groups who enjoy planning, timing, and dramatic finales

Cons:
- Can be cognitively demanding; less ideal for casual gamers who prefer fast, chaotic sessions
- Some players may miss the pure randomness of other expansions
- Morrigan’s power economy requires mindful management; you cannot just brute-force your way through everything

If you crave a more narrative, siege-focused Zombicide session and you don’t mind a deeper level of planning, Wulfsburg Morrigan is likely to become a favorite. It offers a different flavor from the base game and most of the other expansions, while maintaining the core cooperative, dice-chucking charm that defines Zombicide. It is not a tiny add-on; it is a substantial reorchestrator of how you move, plan, and fight for your life on a table that is almost always too small for the chaos unfolding on it.

Bottom line: Wulfsburg Morrigan takes the Black Plague line to a new stage of thematic intensity without breaking the core balance. If you are the type who wants a castle siege, a mage with practical spellwork that bangs with your strategies, and a game that rewards careful play as much as reckless bravado, this expansion will likely become a staple in your rotation. If your playgroup thrives on the randomness of a pure scavenger hunt, you will still appreciate the difference this module offers, even if you occasionally miss the good old mutation chaos a little.

## Final Recommendation and Practical Takeaways

- Best for players who want a campaign feel and a siege vibe in Zombicide Black Plague. Morrigan adds spell-powered flavor that can swing turns in dramatic ways.
- A solid choice for medium to larger groups who enjoy planning and teamwork under pressure. The fortress environment rewards coordination and strategic movement.
- Expect a welcome challenge; the rules are not a speed run but a mindful, exhilarating puzzle that pays off with a satisfying sense of accomplishment.
- If you own multiple expansions, Wulfsburg Morrigan integrates well and can refresh the experience without overshadowing other content.

If you are looking to deepen your Zombicide Black Plague experience and you want to explore something that adds story, strategy, and a touch of myth, then Wulfsburg Morrigan is your jam. It doesn’t just toss in more zombies; it invites you to think differently about how you approach a fortress siege while keeping the cooperative chaos that makes Zombicide fun in the first place.
