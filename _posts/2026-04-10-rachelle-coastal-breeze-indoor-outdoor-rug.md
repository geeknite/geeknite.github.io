## Performance in real life
### Indoor use
Inside the house, the Coastal Breeze rug works well under furniture that needs to be pulled in and out without catching on the rug edges. The color and pattern help hide light foot traffic marks and minor scuffs that accumulate over weeks of life in a busy home. Cleaning is straightforward: a quick vacuum to remove dust, followed by a damp wipe down if you spill something sticky. If you have a small dog that tends to drag in dirt from the yard like a tiny excavator, you will appreciate how the fibers repel and wipe clean without becoming permanently stained.

### Outdoor use
On a deck or patio, the rug fends off sun exposure reasonably well. You may notice slight fading over several summers if the rug is permanently exposed to direct sun; however, the rate of fading is not dramatic and the pattern remains legible and attractive. The rug can handle rain thanks to its moisture resistance, drying fairly quickly once the sun returns. In windy environments, the backing helps keep the rug in place, though you may want to use a rug gripper if you have a lot of gusts or if you place it on a smooth surface like ceramic tile.

### Cleaning and maintenance outside
Cleaning is straightforward: sweep or vacuum, then hose off if you have mud or pollen that would otherwise settle. For stains like red wine or sauce, treat quickly with a mild detergent and rinse thoroughly. Avoid harsh bleach or strong solvents, as they can alter color and affect the fibers’ longevity. The rug dries faster than you might expect, which is a big advantage for outdoor use. The pattern remains crisp after repeated cleanings, and there is no noticeable pilling or fuzzing that would make it look worn out after a season or two.

### Weather resistance
Coastal Breeze is built to withstand UV exposure and moisture. It handles rainstorms with ease and does not appear to degrade with repeated damp cycles. If you live in a region with heavy winter weather, bring the rug inside or store it during off-seasons to prolong its life. The key is to ensure that the backing remains dry and the fibers do not stay wet for extended periods, which can lead to mold or mildew in poorly ventilated spaces.

## Durability and care
Durability tests show good wear resistance in typical household scenarios. The rug can maintain its structure under heavy chair weight and with foot traffic from family and guests. A few potential issues to monitor:
- Edges curling after long outdoor use; solving by using a non-slip pad beneath to help anchor 
- Color fade if left in direct sun without a canopy or shade
- Occasional shedding during the first few weeks, which is normal for new synthetic fibers

Care tips to maximize life:
- Vacuum regularly to remove dirt that can act like sandpaper over the fibers
- Clean spills promptly to prevent staining; blot rather than rub to avoid pushing the stain deeper
- Use a rug pad for both indoor and outdoor installs to minimize slippage and extend life
- Bring it inside during extreme weather to prevent long-term moisture exposure
