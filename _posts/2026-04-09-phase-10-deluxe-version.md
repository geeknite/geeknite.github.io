## Gameplay Walkthrough

### Setup
To begin, shuffle the deck thoroughly and deal 10 cards to each player. The rest of the deck becomes the draw pile, with the top card flipped to start the discard pile. The ten phases are laid out on a sheet or in the rulebook, typically with Phase 1 as a simple set or run and Phase 10 as a final demanding combination. In some variants, players track their progress with tokens or a compacted score sheet; Deluxe versions often provide a nice, built-in way to track without resorting to sticky notes or spilled coffee on your dining room table.

### Objective
Your goal is to be the first player to complete all 10 phases in order. The phases are: 1 Run of 4, 2 Sets of 3, 1 Run of 7, 3 of a Kind plus 1 Run of 4, 7 cards of same color, etc. The exact list can vary a bit depending on edition, but the spirit remains the same: you must assemble the required tokens of cards to advance to the next phase. The first player to reach Phase 10 wins the round; players keep track of points across multiple rounds, typically with the lowest total winning best of three, or some custom variation the group uses to stretch a night into a saga.

### Turn Structure
On your turn, you draw one card from the draw pile or the discard pile, then discard one card to the discard pile. If you can lay down your current phase (turn in hand meets the required combination), you can place it on the table. After laying down, you can proceed to the next phase in subsequent rounds only when you complete the new phase requirement. Phase 10 is often about balance between aggressive play and careful planning: do you risk finishing a phase early and letting others react, or do you slow-roll and try to outlast them with fewer points? The Deluxe edition’s polished components make these decisions easier to see and more satisfying to execute, especially when you flip over a card and realize a crucial piece just appeared in front of you on the table.

### The Phases
The classic set of phases includes runs and sets with increasing complexity. In Deluxe, the list remains the same, but the clarity of tokens and the readability of the phase sheet are improved. For the first few phases, players can experiment with simple strategies: you can take advantage of the fact that the deck contains multiple copies of many cards, and you can sometimes pick up a needed card from the discard pile if it aligns with the current phase. As the rounds progress, the pressure grows and the deluge of cards can feel like a mini cardio session. The Deluxe version helps by providing easier-to-read phase references and a more satisfying tactile experience when you finally lay down your final phase.

## Strategy and Tips

Phase 10 is not a heavy strategy game, but there are multiple micro-skills that can lower your average score per round and speed up the process of finishing. Here are a few tips that tend to help in Deluxe nights:

- Plan backward from Phase 10: Keep an eye on the 10th phase and think about how to build toward it gradually. If you know your deck has a few characters that fit nicely into later phases, you can steer your discards accordingly.
- Use the wild and skip cards strategically: Wild cards can substitute for any card in a phase; skip cards can block an opponent’s turn. Use them to disrupt someone who looks like they are about to finish a late phase.
- Read the table, not just your hand: If a player has shown a pattern of discarding high cards or cards that block your potential runs, adapt your strategy accordingly. The Deluxe edition’s clearer tokens and scoring may help you read the table more quickly.
- Speed vs. safety: If a player looks close to finishing, you might choose to take more risks to complete your own phase, or you may opt for defensive discards to hinder others. The deluxe components make those decisions feel more crisp as you can visually track the phase progress with minimal confusion.
- Endgame awareness: As the game approaches a finish line, be mindful of your total points. A round with quick phase completion but high card values can backfire if the overall score is tracked across rounds. The Deluxe version’s improved score sheet can help you maintain a better mental model of your standing.
