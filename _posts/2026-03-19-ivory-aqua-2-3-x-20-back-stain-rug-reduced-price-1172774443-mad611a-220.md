## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Care

Rugs in high-traffic zones accumulate dirt, absorbed odors, and the occasional rogue snack crumb. Here is a practical care plan that keeps the Ivory Aqua looking fresh and spaceship-clean:

- Vacuum regularly: Use a low-suction setting to pull loose dirt without pulling fibers. Pro tip: vacuum in multiple directions to lift the pile and reveal the true color beneath the dust.
- Immediate stain response: Blot spills with a clean cloth—don’t rub, or you’ll work the stain deeper into the fibers like a rogue character in a platformer who refuses to jump.
- Spot cleaning: For common liquids, a mild detergent mixed with water can tackle small spots. Always test on a hidden area first to avoid color bleed, especially with ivory-based backgrounds.
- Deep cleaning: Every 6-12 months, consider a professional cleaning or a careful DIY wash if the rug is washable. If you attempt DIY, follow the manufacturer’s guidelines and avoid aggressive steam cleaning that could distort the backing.

### Stain-Resistant vs Stain-Prone Realities
The term back stain in product listings can be ambiguous. It might imply backing material that resists staining or simply a marketing flourish. In real-world scenarios, any rug with light tones like ivory benefits from being treated as a high-risk stain zone. The key is to be proactive: a quick blot, a mild cleaner, and a furniture layout that reduces foot traffic where spills are likely.
