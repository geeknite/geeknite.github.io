### The build: materials, finish, and colorways

Fortune Row markets these premium dice as a resin-polyurethane blend designed for durability and crisp glyphs. You don’t get the cold, hard feel of stone dice, nor the lightness of some plastic sets that feel more like props than tools. The edges are beveled cleanly, which helps with roll consistency and keeps the dice from catching on the felt—crucial when you’re trying to avoid a dramatic 2 a.m. re-roll that reveals your character is now officially a chaos magnet.

#### Colorways and glyph quality

The Fortune Row set features colorways that feel thematically aligned with City of Mist—dark tones with metallic accents rather than loud, candy-store palettes. ### The rolling experience in play

#### In-hand feel and grip

One of the biggest concerns with premium dice is the possibility of making the dice too slick or too rough on the fingers. Fortune Row hits a sweet spot: they feel satisfying to grip without being a chore to roll. The surface texture provides just enough friction to prevent rolling off the table during a dramatic reveal or a last-second dice-snap moment, but not so rough that your players walk away with calluses. The weight distribution feels gentle, not aggressive; you won’t feel your wrist protest after a long scene of rolling per turn.

#### Surface interactions

Different table surfaces can influence the roll. On a sturdy wooden table, the dice settle with a pleasing clack that sounds like a street performance in a smoky alley. On felt, the dice show a softer, almost ceremonial roll. It’s not a science experiment, but it does feel like you’re rolling something with intention rather than mass-produced luck. If your table has a gloss finish or an elaborate centerpiece, you’ll still get a crisp tumble that doesn’t slip into the oblivion of the carpet like a rogue stealth character trying to vanish in a crowd.

#### Roll consistency across sessions

Serial sessions can change the vibe of a dice set—your buddy’s cats, a coffee spill, or a late-night energy drink can all add character. Fortune Row dice show enough consistency that you’re not going to stake a whole campaign on a single errant roll. They aren’t miracle dice; they’re well-made tools that support storytelling rather than steal it. In a system like City of Mist, where outcomes often hinge on narrative prompts, predictable dice can be a refreshing counterbalance to slippery plotlines.

### Maintenance and care

Caring for premium dice is mostly about keeping them clean and avoiding direct abrasive surfaces that can dull glyphs. The glyphs are etched, which helps with longevity, but there is nothing wrong with occasional polish to keep that neon-pop gleam going strong.
