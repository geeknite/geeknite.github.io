## Synthetic Rope vs Steel: Practical Differences

If you are new to rope choices, here is a quick primer:

- Weight: Synthetic rope is lighter, which reduces load on the motor and on the mounting hardware. It also makes the rope easier to handle and reduces the risk of GI issues from wresting with weighty steel cable after a long day.

- Safety: Steel rope can store more energy and can whip dangerously if it snaps. Synthetic rope reduces the risk of serious injury, which is a win for your local trail crew and your own sense of safety.

- Friction and heat: Steel rope handles heat well; synthetic rope can weaken if you push the unit beyond its duty cycle. Respect heat build up and monitor the winch temperature during heavy pulls.

- Maintenance: Synthetic rope requires checks for fuzzing and wear, and should be replaced if fraying appears. Steel rope requires more maintenance and can be more challenging to manage on a muddy trail but can be very durable in the right conditions.

- Cost: Synthetic rope kits are pricier upfront but can pay off in safety, handling, and easier maintenance.

This winch uses a protective sleeve on the rope near the drum to minimize rope wear and it has a drum that promotes proper line payout. The sleeves are a nice touch and can extend rope life under repeated use.
