## Tips, tricks, and house rules
### Smart play strategies
- Use the new action cards to create chain reactions instead of letting your nemesis accumulate a big hand. The moment you see a color change or a shift in the active color, reposition your defenses and set up your endgame.
- Pay attention to your opponents’ redraw patterns. If someone is dodging a color change, you can optimize your own plays to force them into a corner on the next turn.
- Keep a loose memory of which cards have already been played. It is not necessary to memorize every card, but a quick scan of the table can help you anticipate what the next handful of turns might look like.

### Fun house rules you can try
- Cooperative twist: players work together to force a single target to unload a certain number of cards before the round ends. This adds a new layer of social play and can be hilarious in the right group.
- Timed rounds: set a timer for each round to keep the pacing brisk and to prevent any one player from dragging the game on. This is especially fun for larger groups or when kids are involved who want the next game ASAP.
- Island trader rule: allow a one-time hand swap between two players at a cost. It adds a strategic moment that can turn a near loss into a dramatic comeback.
