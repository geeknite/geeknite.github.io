## What is Sushi Go!?

Sushi Go! is a fast-paced card drafting game designed for 2-5 players, ages 8 and up. Each round, players simultaneously select a card from their hand and pass the rest to the next player. You’re drafting cards to form the best possible sushi set, because apparently the goal of every table is to create a menu so delicious that it makes the napkins cry. The game is built on the idea of value-led sets: collect Nigiri for big points, sashimi for a big boom, maki for a number of points depending on how many you have, dumplings for escalating value as you grow a set, and so on. The base game scratches that itch with a compact catalog of classic sushi items, leaving room for quick rounds, quick decisions, and lots of laughter.

The key hook is the drafting mechanic: you don’t know what you’ll get next, and your neighbors do not care about your strategic plans. They’re too busy trying to optimize their own plates. The tension comes from trying to predict what opponents will keep versus pass, while you themselves try to minimize the risk of a bad combination or a terrible turn of events. The absence of heavy math or long rulebooks makes Sushi Go! accessible to a wide audience, from families to students to casual gaming groups. It’s designed to be understood in a single sitting, and that initial spark often translates into a long tail of repeat plays.

## How to Play: The Quick Demystifier

The core loop of Sushi Go! is elegantly simple, yet delightfully sneaky in its depth. Each round follows these basic steps:

1) Each player starts with a hand of cards. The exact number depends on the round, but the game is designed to accommodate multiple rounds with scaling difficulty and value. 
2) Simultaneously, all players choose one card to keep and pass the rest to the left. The fun happens in the timing: you want to pick a card that benefits you without giving your neighbor the exact setup they crave. 
3) After all players reveal their chosen cards, you score for the round’s selected cards and set aside any cards that have ongoing effect or partial scoring. 
4) Repeat the passing and picking until the deck is exhausted. At the end of all rounds, you tally the total score and declare the winner.

The round structure is built to be fast and engaging: you’ll likely complete a round in 5-10 minutes depending on the player count. The game’s magic lies in its compact decision space. You’re not trying to optimize a massive engine; you’re trying to optimize a set of few card categories—Nigiri, Sashimi, Maki, Wasabi, Dumplings, and the occasional special card—so that your points accumulate with surgical precision.

The drafting mechanic is where Sushi Go! earns its stripes. Your decisions ripple through the table as you observe what others are collecting. Do you chase the immediate gain of a single high-value card, or do you seed a longer-term strategy that pays off when the end-of-round bonuses kick in? The thrill comes from measuring risk and reward with the simple math of card values that are both familiar and quirky—nigiri cards worth 1-3 points, sashimi that scales with two or more, and maki that rewards the player with the longest set.

Aesthetically, the game shines in the tiny details. The card art leans into the kawaii vibe, with cute expressions and expressive sushi characters that give personality to your choices. It’s not just edible eye candy; it’s a tiny theater of choices where you cheer when you snag the exact triple-sashimi combo or groan when fate deals you a dumpling that’s almost perfect but not quite. This lighthearted tone makes the game approachable for newcomers and a comforting background hum for seasoned gamers who want to let their competitive side emerge in a low-stakes setting.


### Strategy and Decision-Making Minimalism

If there’s a core lesson in Sushi Go! it’s that great power often comes from small, thoughtful actions. Here are some practical strategies to maximize your odds of victory without becoming a math savant:

- Read the room, not just your hand: You’re constantly predicting what others will keep or pass based on the cards you see on the table. A good mental model is to assume the person to your left is trying to complete a sashimi run and the person to your right is eyeing maki domination. Your choices should be reactive as well as proactive.
- Balance short-term gains with long-term plans: A single prized nigiri card might be tempting, but if it leaves you without a path to end-of-round bonuses, you’ll regret it. Consider whether to chase a small set now or risk a larger payoff later.
- Don’t ignore the endgame: If the end of the game approaches and a Pudding or Wasabi is lurking in the deck, think about how many rounds you have left to maximize those effects. The pudding card, for example, can swing final scores, so you should be mindful of its presence, even if it’s not part of the core sushi set.
- Watch the chopsticks card when present: In some editions, chopsticks allow you to swap or pick two cards later in a turn. It’s a strategic wildcard that can unlock surprising combos, especially when you’re behind and want to make up ground with a single clever move.
- Adapt flavor and theme: Sushi Go! is a game that thrives on social energy. The fun of bidding with your friends to assemble cantankerous sushi sets is often the differentiator between a decent night and a legendary one. Use humor, don’t take yourself too seriously, and you’ll find your best decisions come when you’re relaxed and enjoying the banter.
