## Durability and Maintenance

### Everyday Wear and Tear

Office furniture must withstand the daily grind — the occasional slip of a laptop bag, the heavy press of a chair during a sprint to a meeting, and the ongoing clatter of keyboard warriors. The Oak Finish 10 Person system holds up well under this test. The veneer remains resilient, with minor scuffs that can be buffed out with a soft cloth and a little elbow grease. The metal components resist rust and chipping, which is a blessing in an environment where humidity and coffee coexist with alarming frequency.

### Cleaning and Care

Cleaning is straightforward: a damp microfiber cloth works for most smudges, and a mild cleaner is enough for sticky residue. Avoid harsh solvents that could strip the finish or dull the grain. The key is to wipe in the direction of the wood to preserve the natural look instead of letting things smear across the surface like a time-lapse of a messy desk. Regular maintenance means a longer life, and the oak finish tends to show less wear than darker laminates when treated with a little respect and regular care.

## Tips for Setup and Optimization

### Layout Advice

Start with a central hub for shared tools like the whiteboard or communal printer. Position power outlets on the outer edges for easy reach by all team members. If your space allows, aim for two aisles to walk through comfortably and leave enough room for rolling chairs to glide without collision. A well-thought arrangement reduces friction and keeps people from turning their desk into a personal storage fortress.

### Cable Management Tricks

Utilize the built-in channels and grommets to route cables along the back edge. Group power cords and data lines separately to avoid interference. If you can, consider under-desk trays for power bricks and a slim cable spine running under the central spine of the module. The goal is a clean surface that invites collaboration rather than a hazard zone for feet and coffee cups.

### Maintenance Schedule

Have a quarterly check to tighten fasteners, inspect the veneer for wear, and relevel the furniture if the floor has developed a new contour due to heavy chair movement. A lightweight polish on the wood veneer helps maintain its warmth and extends the life of the finish. The little ritual pays off in not needing to replace sections of the unit prematurely.
