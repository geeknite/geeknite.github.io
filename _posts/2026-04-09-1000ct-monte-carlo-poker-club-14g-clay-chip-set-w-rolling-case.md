## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
Clay chips aren’t as fussy as you’d expect, but they’re not indestructible either. Here are some practical care tips to maximize lifespan:
- Clean chips regularly with a soft, dry cloth to remove oils from hands. For stubborn fingerprints, a gentle wipe with a slightly damp cloth followed by thorough drying is fine. Avoid soaking or using harsh cleaners.
- Store chips in a dry environment; humidity can affect the finish and slightly warp lids or liners in the case.
- Inspect chips for edge wear over time; rotate stacks to avoid uneven wear in high-rotation games.
- For the case, keep the latches clean and lubricate the wheels if they begin to squeak. A tiny amount of silicone spray can work wonders if you hear the infamous “creak” during transport.

Maintenance is straightforward and, frankly, a small price to pay for a nicer feel. If your group is serious about presentation, you’ll value the ritual of maintaining chips as much as you value the ritual of winning a hand.
