## Introduction: Welcome to a City of Myths, Fog, and Fedoras
City of Mist is not your grandma's noir—unless your grandma ran with a literal pantheon of myths and decided to wear a fedora that shoots rainbows when she gets excited. This is a game where you play everyday people who discover they’re more than they appear: detectives, ex-supervillains, artists, conspirators, librarians, and dreamers who carry a mythic spark inside their ordinary lives. The city is drenched in fog, neon, and secrets, and you, dear player, get to decide which myth you’re wearing on your sleeve this week.

The City itself is a character: a living, breathing, rain-soaked backdrop that can turn from slick asphalt into a stage for miracles with a single whispered clue. The rules are designed for quick, cinematic play, where you describe what your Hero wants, the City (and fate) conspire, and the dice, sometimes merciful, sometimes mischievous, decide the outcome. If you like pulp detective fiction, mythic fantasy, and a sprinkle of cyber-noir, you’ve found your jam. If you prefer crunchy, stat-heavy crunch, City of Mist is a big, glittery deceptively simple sauce you can add to almost any crime-fantasy dish.

Below you’ll find a practical, player-focused guide to building a memorable Mask, choosing your Mythos, and steering your tabletop crew through a city that refuses to stay ethical for more than seven seconds at a time.

## What City of Mist Is (And What It Isn’t)
City of Mist pitches you into a noir reality where the City itself is a living, breathing monster of possibility. You’re not just a hero; you’re a mask, a myth, and a human being wearing both at once. Your character is built around a few core ideas:
- Masks: Your public face—the role you play in the world. Think private-eye with flamboyant flair, a street poet who solves crimes through metaphor, or a gentle giant who keeps the peace but not the paperwork.
- Mythos: The mythic power that gives you extraordinary abilities, but often at a cost. Your mythic identity is never perfectly quiet; it wants attention, it wants a story, and it wants to be a part of who you are.
- Logos: The mortal identity underneath, your day-to-day life, your relationships, and your ordinary struggles. This is the anchor that keeps you human when the city tries to turn you into a statue of your own legend.

You’ll rarely meet a rigid “class” system here. Instead, you’re assembling a mosaic: the Mask (how the world sees you), the Mythos (your supernatural lens), and the Logos (your human life). The interplay among these layers is where the drama lives. The City of Mist wants to remind you that heroes are fragile, imperfect, and gloriously real.

In practice, a session in City of Mist is poised between two extremes: the intimate, character-driven scenes where you reveal a personal truth, and the high-stakes, city-wide intrigues where a clue about a fog-wreathed conspiracy can topple a corporation or awaken a god. It’s not about hitting a power fantasy button; it’s about discovering how your character’s past (and present) shapes what they’re willing to do with a mythic secret in their pocket.

## Character Creation for Players: Building Your Mask, Mythos, and Logos
Character creation in City of Mist is a dance of three layers. The book gives you a few prompts that you can bend, twist, or completely ignore—because art loves a good remix.

### The Mask (Your Public Face)
The Mask is how others see you in the world. It’s not just your profession; it’s your attitude, your vibe, the way you talk to a suspect or a barista with a question you don’t dare ask. Examples include: The Detective of the Impossible, The Night Photographer, The Poet with a Gun, The Whispering Mechanic. Your Mask includes:
- A rough description of how you look and behave.
- Your public goals and flaws.
- A few signature lines or catchphrases that become your social currency during the game.

Tip: Choose a Mask that provides immediate storytelling hooks. If you want a scene that revolves around trust and betrayal, pick a Mask who is notoriously loyal to a fault. If you want to chase a mystery, pick a Mask with a slippery public persona that makes people question who you really are.

### The Mythos (Your Power Source)
Mythos is the mythic energy you pull from the world to do extraordinary things. It could be a godling, a forgotten titan, a modern miracle, or a myth wrapped in urban legend. Mythos can grant you capabilities like heightened senses, reality-bending moments, or manipulation of time or memory—but it comes with strings attached: you’ll often face tests or costs when you lean into your power. A strong Mythos should be thematically connected to your Mask. If your Mask is a detective, your Mythos could be a covert-from-birth prophecy or a curse that makes you forget faces you should remember. If your Mask is a street poet, your Mythos might be the ability to bend language itself, turning a line of verse into a weapon or a shield.

### The Logos (Your Everyday Life)
Logos is the mundane you—the job, relationships, debts, and responsibilities that anchor you to reality. This is the contrast that makes your mythic moments land harder. Your Logos gives you motives that the City can leverage against you. For example, you might be a librarian who owes a debt to a villain, or a nurse who’s hiding a medical secret from your patients. The interplay between Logos and Mythos is where the drama happens: the City can threaten your day job, while you still chase down a supernatural clue.

### A Quick Build Roadmap
- Start with a single, clear Hook: What single line would the City whisper to pull you into a mystery? (E.g., The Mist has named you the next Keeper.)
- Pick a Mask that sounds fun to role-play for a session or two.
- Choose a Mythos that logically elevates your Mask into something extraordinary. Don’t overcomplicate it—the best powers feel magical and a little bit dangerous.
- Define a few Logos hooks: a personal relationship, a debt, a responsibility, or a dream you’re chasing.
- Flesh out 2–3 Minor details you can drop into scenes: a signature item, a fear, a calling phrase, or a quirk.

### Example Character: The Night Librarian
- Mask: The Night Librarian—keeps city secrets in the quiet between book spines; reads people like lines read in braille.
- Mythos: The Echo of the Archivist—a power that lets them recall forgotten events by touching a book’s page.
- Logos: A part-time barista who owes a library donation to a friend who runs a community center. A secret book he guards is the key to a city-wide conspiracy.

This combination yields immediate role-play gold: a public identity that’s calm and measured, a mythic power that’s quietly dangerous, and a life that continuously interferes with the investigation. It’s a classic City of Mist setup: you are ordinary until you’re not—and you’ve got a reason to push forward even when the city tries to pull you into its fog.

## Mechanics at a Glance: How the Dice and Tags Work (High-Level)
City of Mist uses a flexible, cinematic resolution system designed to reward storytelling over crunchy minutae. At its core:
- You roll a pool of dice (usually d6s) determined by your Mask, Mythos, and Logos when attempting actions tied to those aspects.
- A successful roll means you do the thing you intended. A partial success means a twist of fate or a complication that spins the scene in a new direction. A failure opens up a new set of problems—often even more interesting than the original plan.
- Tags are descriptors you can burn or spend to modify your dice pool or to justify extra narrative outcomes. They are the brass knuckles of your story: bold, adaptable, and sometimes a little dangerous to rely on.
- Narration is fluid: both players and the GM contribute to the scene. The rule of cool matters; the rule of character matters more.

If you’re a player, you’ll want to practice summarizing your action in a single sentence, then asking for a consequence that feels emotionally true—this keeps the session moving and ensures your choices have clear payoffs.

## Playing Tips: How to Get the Most Fun as a Player
- Lean into the friction between Mask and Mythos. The City shines when your public persona clashes with your supernatural instinct. If your Mask is calm and collected, your Mythos can be volatile—and that tension is great on screen.
- Use Tags as story leverage, not crutches. They’re there to flavor your dice and your outcomes, not to micromanage every move. If you find yourself listing too many tags before a scene, you may be overplanning a moment that should feel spontaneous.
- Build relationships as you go. The City of Mist thrives on networked stories: a sister who’s a detective, a rival who’s now in possession of a dangerous artifact, an ally who’s more than they appear. Invest in a couple of interconnected NPCs and watch your scenes become a living city rather than a string of disconnected adventures.
- Keep a personal thread. The best campaigns pulse with a theme: redemption, revenge, memory, or love. Your character’s personal thread guides decisions when you’re faced with a moral choice that doesn’t have a clean, right answer.
- Embrace the noir vibe, but don’t overdo it. The city’s fog can be a mood, not a monologue. Let dialogue, not exposition, do the heavy lifting.

### Narrative Tools For Players
- The Room Tone: A short phrase a character uses to set the mood (for example, a line like The city never sleeps, and neither do I).
- The Red Thread: A personal clue that never seems to resolve—until it does, in a way that makes sense but isn’t tidy.
- The Ethical Dilemma: A choice where every option has a price. Your best decision is the one that reveals character, not the one that’s the simplest win.

## Running a Session as a Player: Scene Goals and Flow
What makes City of Mist special is how scenes can flip quickly from intimate to epic. Here’s a practical flow you can borrow:
1) Open with a personal stake. A micro-scene about your Logos keeps you grounded.
2) Introduce a city-wide puzzle. A clue, an object, or a rumor connected to the Mythos appears.
3) Link the two: demonstrate how your Mask’s public persona affects your personal quest.
4) Resolve or complicate. The dice give you a result, but you decide the next beat. Your new situation should provide momentum for the next scene.

During play, you’ll want to keep a few quick prompts handy:
- What does my Mask want most right now?
- What’s the city trying to keep secret from me?
- Which tag can I burn to shift momentum without breaking the vibe?

If you’re ever stuck, lean on your fellow players. City of Mist is a collaborative story engine—two heads are often better than one when crafting a scene that feels unforgettable.

## Narrative Expansion: Deeper Scenes That Sing
City of Mist shines when players chase down a memory, a rumor, a half-forgotten plea whispered by the streetlight. Here are a few expansion ideas you can weave into sessions to turn a routine mystery into a mythic epic with noir chic:
- The Memory Map: A location tied to a forgotten event becomes a living map as they interact with artifacts connected to Mythos. Each artifact reveals a chapter of a city-wide tragedy and gives your party a choice that alters the balance of power in the city.
- The Masked Choir: A group of individuals who share similar Masks but have different Mythos. They can become allies or antagonists, depending on how your crew negotiates the rules of the fog.
- Echoes of a Price Paid: Every major reveal costs your character something small but meaningful. A signature item goes missing, a voice remembers a lie, a trusted contact grows distant. The price keeps the tension honest.

## Example Session Beats: A Tiny Scenario to Try in Your Mind
The setting: a rain-slick waterfront district, neon flickering like a malfunctioning heart monitor. Your Mask is The Night Librarian, a detective who solves crimes by reading the city’s unwritten stories. Your Mythos whispers you can hear the city memory, especially about events that never happened or should have happened. Your Logos shows up as a part-time courier who’s late delivering a package that’s not supposed to exist.
- Beat 1: While closing a late shift at the library, the Night Librarian discovers a book that shouldn’t exist—the kind of book that smells like rain and secrets. The book glows faintly at midnight and hums when they touch it.
- Beat 2: A contact at the docks reports a mysterious shipment arriving in the night, wrapped in chains of LED light, none of which should have anything to do with a private-eye bibliophile.
- Beat 3: The mythic energy in the book awakens the city memory of a long-forgotten crime. The Night Librarian realizes that the book is not a clue; it is a map to a memory the city wants to keep hidden from everyone, including the librarian themselves.
- Beat 4: The duo must decide whether to reveal the memory’s truth to the public or bury it to protect someone they care about. Either choice reshapes the city’s nemesis—an organization that thrives on secrets.

This mini-arc demonstrates how personal stakes mingle with city-scale elements. It’s compact, moody, and very much City of Mist: a story that grows darker, and more human, as you peel back each layer of fog.

## World-Building with Your Group: The City Helpers and Adversaries
A City of Mist table often benefits from a couple of recurring factions and a handful of memorable NPCs. You can spin these out as ongoing threats or occasional allies:
- The City’s Keeper: An enigmatic figure who appears in the background to nudge the party toward the truth—sometimes literally, sometimes by misdirection.
- The Syndicate of Fog: A network that profits from keeping secrets and corrupting those who pry too deeply.
- The Archivist: A character who guards a library not of books but of memories; their goal is to prevent the city from being forced to reveal its deepest regrets.

Your table can rotate NPCs or re-use the same ones, but the key is to keep them emotionally potent. They should feel like consequences of the characters’ choices, not just obstacles to overcome.

## Comparing City of Mist to Other Noir-Mythic Games
If you’re juggling multiple games, how does City of Mist stand out?
- Less crunch, more story: If you despise heavy combat counters and prefer conversations that matter, this game is your friend.
- Mythic content, intimate scale: You get big concepts (gods, legends) but in a human, neighborhood-level setting.
- Flexible tone: It can be dark and brooding or wildly hopeful, depending on how you frame the relationship between Mask and Mythos.

Of course, it’s not for everyone. If you crave grid-based tactical combat, City of Mist’s flavor might feel like a mismatch. But if you like lo-fi supernatural crime that makes you interrogate your own beliefs, you’ll likely fall for the fog like a cat to a radiator on a cold night.

## Final Thoughts and Recommendations
City of Mist is a celebration of storytelling constraints. It asks you to be bold with your identity, creative with your powers, and honest about your character’s flaws. The Masks give you a social hook; the Mythos gives you spectacular possibilities; the Logos gives you a heartbeat that the city can’t tune out. When you lean into the tension between these layers, the city will respond with something memorable—often something surprisingly human, and occasionally something shamelessly heroic.

If you’re a new player, start with a compact Mask and a single, strong Mythos concept. Play a short, contained mystery first to feel the rhythm: scene, twist, consequence, new question. If you’re a veteran, you’ll love crafting interlocking Masks in a larger city-wide conspiracy and watching the fog lift, just enough, to reveal the bittersweet truths underneath.
