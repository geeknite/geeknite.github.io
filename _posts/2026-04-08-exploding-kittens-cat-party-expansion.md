Exploding Kittens: Cat Party Expansion is the kind of package that makes you laugh before you even shuffle. It’s the board-game equivalent of inviting a dozen cats to your living room and telling them they’re in charge of the entertainment. If you’ve ever watched a cat chase a laser pointer and thought, “that’s also how my game nights go when I try to plan strategy,” Cat Party is the expansion you’ve been waiting for. It doesn’t pretend to replace the base game; it amplifies the chaos with a feline flourish that can feel like chaos with a whisker, which is basically the high art of party games.

In this review, we’ll explore what’s inside the Cat Party box, how it changes gameplay, whether the humor lands, and how it scales with different groups. We’ll pair practical setup tips with nerdy observations about game design, and we’ll drop a few house-rule ideas guaranteed to spark laughter and debates around cat ownership ethics (which we definitely do not endorse, unless you’re into questionable ethics and lots of giggles).

If you’re newer to Exploding Kittens, you can treat Cat Party as a turbocharged extra layer on top of a party game that has always thrived on quick turns and silly cards. If you’re a veteran of Exploding Kittens, Cat Party is your shiny, cat-shaped key that opens a door to even more unpredictable moments at your table. Either way, you’re in for a night where the phrase “just one more turn” will be whispered, then shouted, then surrendered, all with a chorus of meows in the background.

Box contents and physical quality

- A deck of Cat Party specific cards that slide smoothly into the base set’s rhythm. These aren’t gimmies; they’re designed to interact with the existing action cards in surprising ways.
- A handful of Cat Tokens that manage the special counters the expansion introduces. The tokens look good, feel sturdy, and survive the inevitable cat-nap of a table wipe-down after a game night.
- A compact rule sheet that’s surprisingly readable. It keeps the flavor while delivering the essential instructions, which is exactly what you want when guests are trying to remember who’s up next and why there’s a cat on every card.
- Optional play aids that help you remember the Cat Party mode rules without subvocalizing your entire life story to your fellow players.
- A couple of cat-themed card sleeves aren’t included by default (depending on edition), but the included cards are sturdy enough to stand up to enthusiastic shuffles and the occasional paw swipe that could become a legal move in some households.

Artistic style and production values

Cat Party sticks to Exploding Kittens’ cheeky, meme-friendly aesthetic. The new art integrates with the original style—bright colors, bold outlines, and a sense of chaos that feels playful rather than chaotic in a stressful way. The quality of the printing is excellent, with a glossy finish that’s easy to wipe clean after the inevitable gravy spill of a late-night gaming session. The cat imagery is not only funny but also legible; you can read the card names at a glance even when the table is at “meh, I’m tired but I’m still laughing” energy levels.

Jekyll-and-Hyde fun: how Cat Party changes the game’s feel

The expansion preserves Exploding Kittens’ core rhythm: draw a card, play an action, and avoid an explosive disaster. Cat Party introduces cat-themed cards that trigger additional effects, often layering with existing actions to create chain reactions. The net effect is a game night where every turn has a potential “oh no, that cat just flipped the table” moment, but in a way that’s lighthearted and very meme-friendly.

Key mechanics and how they play out at the table

- Cat-plicity: Cat cards can change the typical predictability of the draw pile. They introduce new triggers that can accelerate the game or redirect it into a dancing chaos that seems almost choreographed by a room full of kittens. Players learn to anticipate the weird momentum shifts, then immediately forget them as the next Cat card arrives.
- Cat token economy: Tokens provide a small, optional resource system that can fuel special Cat Party effects. The economy feels additive rather than essential, which is a good fit for a party game where you want players to feel empowered without turning the game into a math seminar.
- Card interactions: The cat-specific cards interact with base game cards in ways that feel deliberate and clever. You’ll find synergies that reward situational play (knowing when to deploy a particular Cat card) without locking people into a single “best move.” That balance is hard to achieve in expansions, and Cat Party mostly nails it, with occasional moments where chaos can become a tad too chaotic for players who prefer a smoother rhythm.

Setup, pacing, and group dynamics

Cat Party does not dramatically increase setup time. The new cards slide into the existing deck, and with a quick skim of the Cat cards, you’re ready to go. The real story here is pacing: cat-based effects can drum up a faster tempo in the mid-game, pushing you toward a lively finish. For groups that love fast, silly rounds, Cat Party shines. For groups that prefer longer, more strategic planning, it still offers entertainment, but be prepared for more “one more turn” moments that extend the session by design.

House rules and optional twists to boost the fun

- Meow mode: A light variant where each Cat card activation requires the player to meow—perfect for Zoom nights and anyone who has a similar sense of humor to a cat’s tail flick.
- Card reveal mini-games: When Cat cards are played, players reveal one card from their hand for a fraction of a second to influence others’ decisions. It adds a micro-bluffing layer and fosters lively discussion about who’s bluffing and who’s not.
- Cat nap diplomacy: If two players collaborate to counter a Cat card, they earn a temporary alliance point. It’s silly, but it can be a surprisingly strategic way to handle a chaotic night without turning the table into a tournament battlefield.

Strategy guide for cat-paws and human hands

- Early game: Focus on establishing a strong baseline—keep some non-cat defense options on hand to weather the first few rounds. You’ll start to notice the cadence of Cat cards and can time your reactions accordingly.
- Mid-game pivot: When Cat Party effects begin to bloom, you want to keep your options flexible. Don’t dump all your Cat power early; save some for the moment you can swing the table or stop someone from locking in a big move.
- Endgame art: The late game often turns on a few key Cat cards. You’ll want to balance risk and protection, reading the table to infer who might be holding the next game-changing Cat card and planning a backup move just in case you’re wrong.

Teaching the expansion to new players

Cat Party is accessible. The base game teaches quickly, and Cat Party adds a thin layer that’s easy to grasp after a few rounds. A short pre-game briefing—about what Cat cards do and how tokens are used—will have new players ready to contribute in a single session. The official rules sheet is concise, and the humor in the art tends to clarify intent; if something on the card isn’t clear, a quick group vote usually resolves it without breaking the mood.

What about length and replayability?

In a typical 4-6 player game, Cat Party adds enough new interactions to justify repeated plays without threatening the base game’s short, bite-sized rounds. If you’re the kind of table that loves long-running campaigns, you’ll still appreciate the novelty, but you may want to pair Cat Party with a backup lighter option to prevent fatigue. For casual gatherings and spontaneous game nights, this expansion is a stellar choice that delivers big laughs per minute.

Notes on balance and fairness

Cat Party is reasonably well-balanced for a party expansion. The cat-themed cards provide new opportunities for misdirection and counterplay, and the token system prevents things from spiraling into a runaway advantage for one player. In practice, you’ll still see wild swings—this is, after all, Exploding Kittens—yet those swings tend to land as humorous, memorable moments rather than frustrating, table-breaking disasters.

A few caveats to consider

- If your group hates randomness, Cat Party adds more randomness than some players prefer. It’s not chaotic from a design philosophy standpoint, but it is chaotic in practice.
- Families with very young children might want to supervise the more chaotic cards to ensure there aren’t misunderstandings about card effects. The humor is kid-friendly overall, but you know your audience best.
- If your table is already balancing on the edge of sensory overload (lots of jokes, memes, and commentary), Cat Party will amplify that energy. It’s good energy, but plan your room layout and seatings accordingly.

Conclusion and final verdict

Cat Party is not a revolution in game design, but it is a thoughtful and entertaining enhancement to a game that thrives on chaos and quick humor. It respects the base game’s tempo, introduces cat-driven interactions that feel fresh without becoming gimmicky, and it does so with a smile that hints at the internet’s obsession with cats and memes. If your gaming group likes to mix fast rounds with moments of ridiculous spectacle, Cat Party will become a staple in your rotation. If you prefer deeper strategy and longer comprehension arcs, you’ll still have a good time, but you may want to pair it with heavier titles to balance your nights.
