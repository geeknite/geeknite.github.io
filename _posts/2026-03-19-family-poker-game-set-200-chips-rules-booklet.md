## Setup, table talk, and getting everyone engaged
Setting up the game is straightforward. You lay out the table, distribute chips, shuffle the deck, and designate a dealer. The dealer role rotates to ensure everyone has a turn to orchestrate the moves. On a busy family night, rotating dealer duties also ensures that kids stay engaged and players stay mindful of the social aspect of the game rather than treating it as a solo sprint for victory.

To maximize engagement, consider the following tips:
- Establish a short rule for “start-of-hand” chatter. A 15-second rule ensures everyone can say something funny or strategic before the action begins.
- Encourage players to describe their thought process at a high level instead of detailing every card in their mind. This fosters social interaction and keeps the atmosphere light.
- Use house rules to manage risk. For example, set a strict no-tantrum policy and a time limit for each player’s decision. This keeps the game moving and prevents a single player from stalling the entire table.
- Introduce a “family-friendly mode” where exaggerated bets are replaced with silly counts or gestures, turning bluffing into a performance rather than a test of nerve.

In practice, the table talk becomes a feature rather than a byproduct. Children learn to articulate their thought processes, adults refine their ability to mislead without cruelty, and everyone gets to enjoy a sense of shared storytelling across the table. The result is not just a game of cards; it is a collaborative performance where each player adds to the night’s narrative.
