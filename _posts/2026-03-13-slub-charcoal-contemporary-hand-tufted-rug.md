### Sizing Guide: How Big Should It Be?

A rug’s size anchors a room’s layout. The Slub Charcoal works well in several configurations:

## Real-World Performance: Living With It Day to Day

### Foot Traffic and Durability

Rugs get trampled—often literally. The Slub Charcoal’s pile tolerates regular vacuuming and foot traffic without showing immediate wear. Light vacuuming with a standard beater bar setting should suffice for daily maintenance. For high-traffic areas, rotate the rug periodically to distribute wear evenly. This is especially helpful if you’ve got a cat with sticky paws or a dog who believes the rug is a personal carpet playground.

### Pets, Stains, and Spills

Pet claws can catch on any woven surface. The slub texture helps disguise tiny snags, but avoid aggressive tugging on yarns. For stains, treat promptly with a mild detergent solution and blot rather than rub—rubbing can embed the stain deeper into the pile. For more stubborn mishaps, consult a manufacturer-approved cleaner. While the fibers are resilient, they aren’t invincible; quick reaction to spills is your best ally here.

### Cleaning Schedule and Depths of Clean

A routine deep clean (seasonally, or every 6–12 months depending on usage) keeps fibers fresh and pliant. If you have allergies or a household with pets, consider a professional cleaning every year or so to remove embedded dust. It’s not fun to think about—but you’ll feel the difference when you brush past the rug and a thousand micro-dust motes don’t rise up like a paparazzi flash.


## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Looking Fresh

### Vacuuming: Tricks of the Trade

- Use a vacuum without a rotating beater bar on high-pile surfaces to prevent any fiber damage.
- Vacuum in multiple directions to lift the nap evenly and avoid permanent creases along the rug’s path.
- Pay attention to the edges; some tufted rugs can fringe a bit. A light hand keeps fringe intact and tidy.

### Stains and Quick Fixes

- Blot, don’t rub. The goal is to lift the liquid without pushing it deeper.
- For common spills, a mild dish soap solution (just a drop in water) works well; test on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Avoid bleach or harsh solvents that could affect color and fiber integrity.

### Rotating and Protecting the Nap

Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and fading. If you have direct sunlight streaming in, position the rug so the most sun-exposed side isn’t always facing the same direction; this helps preserve the tonal qualities of the charcoal shade.


## Rug Pads and Underfoot Comfort: The Practical Side

A rug pad beneath the Slub Charcoal improves grip, adds a layer of cushioning, and can extend the rug’s life by reducing friction on the underside. Look for a pad with a grip surface and a thickness appropriate for your floor type (hardwood, tile, or laminate). Avoid overly thick pads if your rug is already plush—the combo can heighten the risk of tripping if someone stands up quickly after watching a rom-com marathon.

Flooring compatibility matters too. For hard surfaces, a rubber or felt-backed pad is a winner. For carpeted floors, a thinner pad with added grip helps prevent shifting—and avoids a pillow-like sensation underfoot when you walk.
