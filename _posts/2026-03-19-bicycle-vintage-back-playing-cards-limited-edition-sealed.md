## Card stock, handling, and playability
Stock quality matters as much as aesthetics. The limited edition print uses a stock that sits between classic tightness and modern glide. The finish should feel smooth but not slick. A deck that’s too slick can slip away during a big reveal; too stiff and the fan may feel like a stubborn door. The vintage edition typically offers a crisper snap, a tactile feedback that’s satisfyingly audible during a fan or a one-handed cascade.

## Practical care and storage tips
A sealed deck is not indestructible; it’s a delicate artifact. Here are practical tips to keep the magic intact for years:
- Climate control: Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Harsh light and high humidity can subtly alter color and edge finish.
- Handling rules: When you do handle the sealed box, avoid tearing the seal or applying pressure that could bend the edges. If the seal shows the slightest tamper signs, treat it as a signal to inspect more closely.
- Display versus storage: For display, consider UV-proof display cases that keep dust off and light exposure minimal. For gaming, keep the deck in its original wrap and use a non-vintage stock for practice if you plan long sessions.
- Cleaning: Do not spray cleaners or liquids on the packaging. A soft microfiber cloth lightly brushing the exterior is plenty.
