## Assembly and Maintenance: Surviving the Real-World Boss Fight
Let’s be honest: any furniture that arrives in a flat box is a potential time sink. The test isn’t whether you can assemble it; the test is whether you can assemble it without yelling at the parts like they’re a glitch in a game. Here are common-sense tips to keep the process smooth:

- Take inventory before you begin. Lay out all screws, washers, and connectors. If you’re missing a critical piece, you’ll save yourself a lot of frustration by catching it early.
- Use the right tools. The kit typically includes a basic tool set, but you’ll thank yourself for having a torque-friendly screwdriver and a compact wrench handy to avoid overtightening.
- Follow the sequence. The instruction sheet usually outlines steps that build a sturdy, stable product. Skipping steps can result in wobbly furniture that makes your backyard look like it’s auditioning for a comedy of errors.
- Allow for a bit of “settling.” The first days may see minor wobble as the frame settles. A quick readjustment after a week can keep things tight and stable.

Maintenance is straightforward:

- Wipe down with mild soap and water to remove pollen, dust, and the occasional bird friend who decided to make your chair a nesting site.
- For cushions, remove covers and wash according to care labels. If the label indicates machine wash, use gentle cycles and air dry to maintain cushion shape.
- If you live in a damp climate, consider storing cushions indoors during heavy rain seasons to extend their life. A simple outdoor storage box or a dry garage corner works wonders.
- Periodically check the frame joints and fasteners. A quick tighten can prevent squeaks and wobble during your next backyard party.
