## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity Tips

### Daily Care

- Vacuum regularly at a low setting to lift loose dirt and prevent it from grinding into the fibers.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear across high-traffic zones.
- Place a rug pad underneath to reduce movement and add a layer of protection against floor scratches.

### Spills and Stains

Spills happen. The secret is to act quickly. Blot with a clean, dry cloth rather than rubbing. Rubbing can push the liquid deeper into the fibers and create a new colorway you did not ask for. For stubborn stains, use a mild cleaning solution that is safe for wool blends and test in an inconspicuous area first. If the stain persists, consult a professional rug cleaner who specializes in woven textiles.

### Cleaning Frequency and Deep Care

Deep cleaning every 12 to 18 months can help maintain the rug’s appearance and longevity. Professional cleaning is recommended for large or delicate stains, and you should avoid harsh chemicals that could compromise fiber integrity. A simple rinse with minimal moisture can refresh the rug without saturating it. If you are unsure, schedule a professional consult rather than risk a DIY fiasco that could lead to warped fibers or a color bleed into your hardwood floors.

### Pet and Child Friendly Tips

Pets and kids love to explore on rugs, which is a compliment and a challenge. The Colleen Slate Woven Rug is resistant to heavy foot traffic, but it is not invincible. Keep an eye on any snags from cat claws or stray toy teeth and trim stray threads promptly. A rug with a tight weave is less prone to catching on toys, but a little vigilance goes a long way in ensuring the rug remains pristine as long as possible.
