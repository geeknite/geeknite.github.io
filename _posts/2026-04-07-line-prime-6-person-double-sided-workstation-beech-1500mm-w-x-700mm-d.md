## Dimensions and Capacity: 1500mm W x 700mm D

Let’s talk numbers, because geometry matters in a world ruled by ergonomics and spreadsheet nightmares. The 1500mm width is wide enough to split into three “stations” on each side for a six-person setup without turning the table into a chaotic game of social Tetris. ## Double-Sided Advantage: Collaboration Without Collisions

The double-sided design is meant to enable six-person collaboration without the chaos of a single-faced desk where people glare at each other across the abyss of cables. Each side acts like its own mini-atelier, allowing pairs or small groups to brainstorm without stepping on toes or overt social cues. The central spine keeps everything aligned, which is critical for data-sharing handoffs, quick stand-up meetings, and the occasional in-depth debate about the correct way to coil a power strip.

In practice, the double-sided arrangement helps reduce head-to-head blocking. It’s not quite a chessboard for team strategy, but it’s close enough that someone might start calling out “Checkmate” when a document is finally saved to the cloud. The reality is more prosaic: you won’t stumble into a desk-sharing brawl over who gets the corner outlet, and that alone is worth three stars in an office drama-averse world.

## Cable Management: The Great Untangling Conundrum

A desk this size inevitably invites a spaghetti symphony of cords. Line Prime includes a cable tray kit designed to tame the tangle, which is the engineering equivalent of giving a cat a cat tree: it’s a clever, elegant structure that may or may not be used as intended but certainly looks good while you try to make it work.

We tested the cable management by plugging in six workstations’ worth of monitors, docking stations, and chargers. The tray did a reasonable job of guiding cables and keeping them off the floor. The real test is how well it handles daily chaos: the occasional hot-swapping of devices, a stray USB-C dongle that refuses to die, and the gnomic act of re-routing cables when you move a monitor arm to chase a Wi-Fi signal. In other words: it’s good but not magic. You’ll still want zip ties and careful cable planning, especially if you’re an overachiever who uses every single port on every single device.

## Assembly Realities: Time, Tools, and Tolerance for Puns

As with any workspace furniture destined to live in an active environment, assembly time depends on your assembly-line temperament. The instructions are straightforward, but there are a lot of parts. We’re talking a behemoth of cross supports, panel alignments, and the occasional moment where you’ll wonder if that extra screw was intended for a different model or if your flat-pack destiny includes a warranty that demands you question your life choices.

Tip for the first-timers: lay out all components before you begin. Match screws by size and color. Use a power drill like you mean it, but avoid over-tightening. The last thing you want is a panel that doesn’t align because you’ve stripped a screw head, leading to a triumphant but ill-advised improvisation with duct tape. The assembly can be completed within a couple of hours with a friend who understands that teamwork means someone fetches drinks and someone else does the instructions; or you can go full WeWork solo and pretend you’re conquering a dungeon puzzle while your cat judges you from a safe distance.

## Ergonomics, Comfort, and Human Factors

Here comes the nerdy portion: ergonomics. A six-person workstation is basically a belt-and-suspenders approach to reducing neck strain and shoulder tension by spreading the load across more torso real estate rather than forcing one person to lean into another’s laptop kaleidoscope of cables. The key ergonomic question is whether the desk height aligns with typical office chairs and whether monitor arms can be adjusted to a comfortable eye level for everyone. The Line Prime’s beech finish gives a sturdy top to rest the forearms on while taking notes during a brainstorming sprint, and the double-sided design can help keep everyone in the right plane of focus. If you tack on monitor arms with gas springs and ensure the monitor heights match, you’ll be maximizing comfort in a way that would make an ergonomic consultant shed a single tear of joy.

A caveat: you’ll want to pair this desk with a good chair, preferably one that encourages a healthy posture rather than a heroic slouch. It’s not a miracle cure for bad posture, but it’s a reliable stage for ergonomic theatre. Remember: your chair is the co-pilot; the desk is the cockpit.

## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping Beeches Beep-Boop-Ready

Beech finishes are relatively durable but do benefit from a little care. A light wipe-down with a microfiber cloth and a mild wood-safe cleaner will keep the surface looking fresh. Avoid abrasive cleaners that could rough up the veneer, and be mindful of heat sources that can mar or warp wood over time. Cable management remains the hero of longevity, as well-routed cables significantly reduce friction and edge wear on the desk itself. If you’re expecting this unit to survive a full STEM lab with constant rearrangement and espresso-fueled meetings, you’ll be pleasantly surprised by its resilience; just don’t test the warranty with a hammer—no matter how satisfying the sound.
