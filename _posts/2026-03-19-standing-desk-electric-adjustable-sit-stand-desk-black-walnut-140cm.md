## Ergonomics and Adjustability
A desk is not just a piece of furniture; it is a personal ergonomics experiment that you pass on to your spine and posture. The 140 Walnut offers a good range of height to accommodate tall and short folks. No more hunching over a laptop like you are a medieval scribe.

Ergonomic considerations:
- Monitor height and placement: The ideal height should align with eye level; the screen top should be at or slightly below eye level. A monitor arm helps keep things clean and adjustable.
- Keyboard and mouse position: A keyboard tray or shallow desktop keeps wrists in a natural, neutral position. If you use a split keyboard, that’s a whole extra level of mechanical geekiness.
- Posture: Standing desks encourage movement but you still need to move. Micro-movements prevent stiffness; change position regularly, and throw in some light stretches during coffee breaks.

The 140 Walnut top thickness and the frame allow for comfortable depths for two to three monitors and a laptop. It’s not a desk for the minimalist who wants to see only printouts; it is a desk for gear enthusiasts who want to maximize real estate.
