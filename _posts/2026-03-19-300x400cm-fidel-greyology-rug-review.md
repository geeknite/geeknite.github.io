H3: Cleaning and stain resistance

Stains happen. Coffee, red wine, mysterious sauces, and the occasional ink spill from a pen you forgot you left in your lap. The Fidel Greyology rug handles most spills with a damp cloth and a bit of patience. For tougher stains, a gentle carpet cleaner or a soap-and-water solution should do the trick without lifting the color. Quick tip: blot—don’t rub. Rubbing creates friction that can push the stain deeper and can also roughen the surface texture. If you’ve got pets, you’ll want to groom and rotate as needed to keep wear even. For longer-term maintenance, a regular vacuum with a soft brush helps keep dirt from embedding in the fibers.

H2: Material Care and Long-Term Care Tips

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers.
- Blot spills promptly with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slipping and to extend the life of the rug by distributing foot traffic evenly.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially if you have a preferred seating area or sunlight that bleaches one side slightly differently.
- For deep cleans, consider a professional service or follow the brand’s cleaning instructions to avoid color fade or texture damage.
