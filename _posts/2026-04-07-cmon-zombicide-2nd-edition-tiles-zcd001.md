## Tile Designs and Quality
Quality is the heart of a good tile set. ZCD001 delivers a clean, high-contrast aesthetic that reads well under standard gaming lighting. The lines are crisp, the colors are vibrant (without veering into garish), and the surface has enough texture to give the impression of gritty, post-apocalyptic geography without making it feel like a map from a watercolor class gone rogue.

The linen finish is a nice touch. It reduces glare when you lean in to scrutinize a zombie’s movement lane, and it also gives the surface a little resistance to minor spills—a good thing when you are balancing a drink, a d20, and a bag of chips in the same breath. The tile corners are sturdy enough to survive the occasional frantic shuffle, yet flexible enough to bend slightly if you happen to drop them on a carpet that loves to eat projectiles and tiles alike.

Edge quality matters here. The tiles’ edges are straight and true, which keeps a neat, seamless board as your table geometry evolves between sessions. There is nothing more frustrating than a misaligned corner tile turning your carefully planned route into a scavenger hunt with a map that keeps changing its mind.

If you are a color nerd, you’ll appreciate the faithful color reproduction. The tiles maintain a consistent palette that matches the base game’s aesthetics, which helps avoid the jarring effect of clashing tile sets. This makes your board easier to read at a glance, a crucial feature when you are trying to decide whether your survivor should sprint through a corridor or crouch behind a barricade while those hungry undead circle like a paparazzi outside a red carpet—only less glamorous and more bonk-on-the-head dangerous.

### Material durability and handling
The material is heavier than the light card stock you might expect from a cheap promo set, but not so heavy that you need a wheeled toolkit to move your board between locations. It sits on the table with a satisfying heft and a dignity that implies you take your zombie-slaying seriously, even if you use a heroic dose of whimsy to name your survivors.

In practice, the library of tiles lies flat, with minimal warping out of the box.
