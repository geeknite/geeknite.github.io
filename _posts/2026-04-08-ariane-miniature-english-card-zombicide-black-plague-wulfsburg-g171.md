## The Ariane Miniature: Pose and Proportions

### The Sculpt and the Stance
Ariane stands tall with a blade held high, cloak billowing, boots planted like a champ ready to wade through a crowd of undead. The sculpt captures a moment of charge and resolve. The proportions feel knightly rather than toy, which is a personal win when you plan to use the figure as a centerpiece for a small vignette on the table. The head tilt communicates intent without crossing into cartoonish. The right arm holds the blade with a sense that the weapon is an extension of will rather than a just-for-show prop.

### Base and Details
The base is a standard 28 mm scale, with a stylized stone motif that mirrors the Gothic vibe of Black Plague. The stone design provides enough texture to add shading without turning the miniature into an abstract doodle. The hood portion is integrated into the cloak which means you can do some fun shading to imply depth without losing the flow of the piece. The scuff marks on metal armor are subtle but present, which helps sell the chaos of the zombie apocalypse while keeping the model visually readable from gaming distance.

### Painting Tips
If you want a quick yet striking look, go for a two-tone metal scheme with a bright accent color. Start with a dark base, then dry brush lighter metal highlights across the raised edges. This technique helps the blade catch light the moment the board is in motion. For the cloak, choose a deep crimson or midnight blue; both contrast nicely with the metallics and the pale zombie flesh you will inevitably paint later. If you want a classroom moment, test a glaze on the cloak to push the color into a moody gradient from top to bottom. A little battle-worn weathering on the blade or shield tells the tale of many battles and a lot of coffee during paint sessions.

## The Wulfsburg Map Tile G171: What It Brings to the Table

### The Tile Aura and Terrain Interaction
G171, the Wulfsburg tile that often appears in fan maps, is a terrain piece hallmark. It brings a narrow corridor in a Gothic stone setting, with enough space to stage a dramatic ambush or to funnel zombies into a kill zone. The tile design encourages a little bit of micro-management: positioning units to maximize the line of sight, blocking necromancer reinforcements, and preventing a single unlucky roll from wrecking your entire plan. On tabletop scale, the tile is the kind of map piece that makes you realize why a good layout matters just as much as a good dice roll.

### Scenario Integration and Map Flow
When you add Ariane to the Wulfsburg map, you want to think about objective placement. The tile allows a hero to hold a doorway longer than usual, creating a tense moment in which the rest of the squad must decide whether to risk the breach or retreat and regroup. The presence of the tile increases the importance of line of sight, weapon reach, and cooperative play. You will find that a single turn can hinge on a successful ambush or on a counter-attack after the zombies collide with your front line.

### A Quick Painting and Assembly Note for G171
If you are assembling a Wulfsburg collection, consider painting the tile edges with a slightly lighter gray to mimic chipped stone. The effect helps define the tile under the table lights and makes the miniature silhouettes pop. Do not forget to seal the tile after painting to avoid scuff marks from table traffic, especially if your gaming group uses a busy dining room table that doubles as a board.

## Hobby Tips: Painting, Storage, and Display

### Storage and Care
Keep Ariane in a clear plastic bag or mini display case to avoid dust from a busy desk. The blade should be checked for any burrs after handling, and the cloak edges should be re-fed with a glaze after a few sessions to maintain color depth. The English card should be kept flat and away from sunlight to avoid fading; a binder or card sleeve works wonders. For long campaigns, you might want to create a small binder that holds the matching cards for each hero. This not only helps you keep track of statistics but also adds a nice, organized vibe to the gaming space.

If you want to show off Ariane at conventions or in your weekly gaming group, create a small vignette around the tile G171 with a few zombie models clustered at the edges.
