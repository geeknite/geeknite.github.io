Playing with shrink wrap: a practical note
New in shrink means you get a pristine, unplayed set, and that is a blessing and a potential hazard. On the bright side, shrink wrap keeps dust off the detail between unboxing and painting. On the flip side, you might discover you need a good hobby knife and a steady hand to remove a skittish piece that clings to its plastic wrapper as if it learned the word patience from a monk in a library. The trick is to carefully slice along the edge where the wrap overlaps, never at the joints of the miniature, and to have a soft workspace so you don’t ping a bowstring into someone else’s eye while you wrestle with the packaging. If you have a YouTube-enabled friend, you can watch their unboxing video while you perform your own unboxing ritual; it’s the modern hobby equivalent of a fireside chat with a goblin accountant.

Painting and assembly tips
- Surface prep: The minis benefit from a light scrub in warm soapy water to remove residual mold release; follow with a quick rinse and air-dry. Your primer choice matters: a gray or white primer will help you pop Liam’s fabric textures and North’s leather straps. A matte finish will keep the halflings from looking too glossy under shop lights, which is crucial when you want to stage a dramatic ambush against undead and the cat who sits in your paint booth watching you.
- Paint strategy: Liam thrives with earth tones and weathering around the knife edge; North wants deeper browns and greens with a slightly tattered cloak. Use a subtle highlight on the edges of sleeves and cloaks to catch light as they move on the board. For the eyes, a tiny dot of white or pale blue can give Liam that twinkle of roguish humor, while North benefits from a steady, longer-range paint where the brow line above the eyes casts a look of silent calculation.
- Assembly options: Some players prefer to pin parts for extra stability; with these two, you may or may not need it depending on how aggressively you pose them. If the sculpts allow you to mount them on 25mm bases without trimming, that makes for a clean table presence. If you want to spice your terrain, consider a small base with a carpet of leaves or moss to reflect their woodland halfling roots, which feels thematically appropriate for the Green Horde setting.
