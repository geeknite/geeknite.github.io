## H2 What is City of Mist and what is a Mythos Pack anyway?

City of Mist started life as a tabletop roleplaying game that blends urban noir with superhero myth. Players become living legends: normal people who discover they are masks in a city that adores paradox. The core game uses a clever dice system, a tonne of investigation, and the idea that your character’s backstory is really a myth you’re living in the present tense. The Mythos Pack is a supplemental expansion designed to deepen the mythic layer of the base game. It’s not a replacement; it’s adding more masks, more factions, more mythic flavor text, and—let’s be honest—a few shiny new bits to tempt the completionists among us.

If you want the quick pitch, think: more gods-in-a-hoodie, more urban legends, and more reasons for your players to quip with prescient poetry as a crime boss glides through a rain-soaked alley. For new players, the pack can be a gateway to the mythic grit you crave; for veterans, it’s a chance to pull the umbrella of the mythos over a brand-new angle on familiar streets. And if you’re like me, you’ll enjoy the extra art flourishes that make a coffee shop feel like a cathedral of secrets.

## H2 Mechanics: does the Mythos Pack change how you roll, or just how you feel?

City of Mist uses a dice-based resolution system that rewards narrative conditioning. The Mythos Pack does not reinvent the wheel. Instead, it gives you more spokes. Mechanically, you’ll find:

- Expanded Distribution of Talents and Powers: A handful of new Talents give players interesting misdirection options. The critical thing to notice is that these are not just “cool” but they tie into the mythic motif. A Talent that triggers when a myth is invoked adds a fresh layer of decision-making to the scene: do you push the myth to the foreground now, or conserve your narrative leverage for a more dramatic reveal later?
- Mythic Scenes and Scene Cards: The pack includes ready-to-use scenes that push players toward a mythic reveal without forcing a square peg into a round hole. If you’ve ever stared at a blank page and whispered “make it epic,” these prompts are a small mercy. They’re not mandatory, but they’re excellent as scene starters when your group hits a creative lull.
- Faction Hooks and Levers: A city is a living organism built of various interest groups. The Mythos Pack adds faction hooks that feel organic rather than tacked-on, which makes the world feel more alive. The GM can use these hooks to escalate tension in a way that feels natural, not like a forced conflict generator.

If you’re worried about game balance, breathe easy: this pack is designed to slot into the existing system rather than upend it. It’s a buffet—not a crash diet—where you can pick the bits you like and leave the rest on the plate. The biggest mechanical win is how a mythic motif drives choices, which often results in brighter, punchier scenes with better player collaboration.

## H2 Thematic depths: myth and metropolis collide

This is where the Mythos Pack shines for those who love theme more than pure crunch. City of Mist has always sold itself on the collision of everyday life with legendary possibility. The Mythos Pack leans into that contrast with a handful of birds-eye viewer moments:

- The city as a living archive: The pack invites players to see the city as a living myth—that is, as a repository for collective memory and whispered legends. Your regular street corner becomes a canvas where myth meets memory. The result is session vibes that feel closer to a heist movie than a pure dungeon crawl.
- Masks as narrative engines: Every mask you take is a promise and a risk. The pack gives you extra prompts to push a mask beyond a one-note persona into a living, narratively credible legend. It’s not just about power; it’s about the myth you choose to embrace in the moment.
- Tone and mood: If your group leans into noir realism, the Mythos Pack offers tools to tilt the mood toward mythic tragedy or mythic winks. It lets you calibrate between “we’re solving a problem” and “we’re rewriting a myth in real-time.” The tonal flexibility is one of the pack’s strongest features.

This isn’t a magic-items parade. It’s an invitation to explore how legends are formed in a modern city and how those legends shape choices under pressure. If you want a quick mood test, run a single scene that uses one of the new masks to pivot a confrontation from a tense standoff to a mythic revelation. You’ll see the switch happen in the players’ eyes—the difference between “I roll to hit” and “I tell a myth and watch it unfold.”

## H2 Story seeds and campaign potential: seeds that sprout wings

One thing City of Mist does well is seed ideas that can become full-blown stories. The Mythos Pack continues that tradition with story seeds that are both punchy and expandable. Here are a few sample seeds you might find in your GM’s notebook after opening the box:

- Seed A: The Clockmaker’s Griffin. A legendary timepiece is rumored to control fate itself. A faction believes it’s a myth in the sense of a legend; another faction believes it’s a weapon that can tilt a citywide election. Your players must navigate which myth is true—without becoming pawns in someone else’s legend.
- Seed B: The Echo Street Choir. A choir whose songs awaken old memories and unlock latent powers. The chorus is non-lethal but deeply disruptive, forcing a choice between letting the music reveal a truth or silencing the chorus to preserve the city’s fragile balance.
- Seed C: The Velvet Oracle. A mythic figure who speaks in ambiguous prophecies. Each prophecy comes with a price, and the city’s underbelly is now chasing those who know how to hear the truth. This seed is perfect for a long-form arc that slowly unfurls over multiple sessions.

If you want a jumpstart, try weaving Seed A into your existing city map by placing the Clockmaker as the proprietor of a forgotten train yard. The mystery unfolds through a chain of encounters, each revealing a new layer of the mythic underworld that sits atop the everyday city we all think we know.

## H2 The practical run-through: running a Mythos Pack session in one evening

Here’s a quick, practical run-through for a single-session table using the Mythos Pack as a focal point:

- Setup (10-15 minutes): Players pick a new Mask from the pack, or you assign one as a twist for the session. The GM seeds a Clockmaker or Echo Street moment as the hook for the night. Place a couple of Mythic Cards on the table as fates you might invoke.
- Investigation and rising action (45-60 minutes): Use a mix of normal clues and mythic prompts. When a clue aligns with a Mask, your players get a sense of momentum; when it clashes, you get narrative drama. The key is letting myth guide decisions rather than forcing them.
- The mid-session twist (15-25 minutes): A scene seed erupts into a choice that directly ties to the Masks the players have chosen. This is the heartbeat moment—the point where all the threads knit into a single, unforgettable reveal—or a spectacular disaster that leads to a better reveal next time.
- Climax and aftermath (20-30 minutes): The mythic reveal lands with dramatic weight. It’s not necessarily about victory; it’s about truth. The city learns something new about itself, and the players discover a new layer of their own legends.
- Wrap-up (5-10 minutes): Quick debrief, a note on where the myth goes next, and a few prompts for the next session. You’ll want to leave a sense of mystery lingering in the air like neon fog.
