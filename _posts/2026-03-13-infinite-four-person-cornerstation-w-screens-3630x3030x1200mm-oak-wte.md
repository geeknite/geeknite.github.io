If you’ve assembled modular furniture before, you know the drill: a box of hardware, a handful of Allen wrenches, and a manual that looks longer than a sci-fi novella. The Infinite cornerstation lands somewhere in the middle in terms of assembly complexity. The most time-consuming steps are aligning screen rails and ensuring the cable channels are properly seated before the top is dropped into place. Expect a couple of hours for one or two people, with more hands dramatically reducing the time (as always with flat-pack adventures). The hardware is standard, so you should be able to pick up spares at your local hardware store if you misplace a bolt after a coffee-mug-shuffle incident.

Maintenance is straightforward: wipe surfaces with a damp cloth, avoid harsh solvents, and periodically check the levelling feet to prevent creaks. There’s nothing within reach that requires a PhD in mechanical engineering to maintain; the design favors practical upkeep rather than secret rituals. A small caveat for those who love customization: if you swap out any screen components, ensure the weight distribution remains balanced, especially if you’re running multiple monitors on a single rail. A miscalibrated balance can lead to a wobble that ruins the perfect stand-up-presentation reveal.
