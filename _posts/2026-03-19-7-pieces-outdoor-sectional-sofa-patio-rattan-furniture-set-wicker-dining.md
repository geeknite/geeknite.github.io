### Weather resistance: can it survive a real season?
If you live where the weather throws curveballs (sun, rain, humidity, and occasionally a stray hail stone shaped like a tiny bowling ball), you’ll want to verify two key things:
- The weave and frame are designed to be weather-resistant. Synthetic wicker is the go-to for many sets because it resists cracking and splitting when exposed to sun and moisture.
- The cushions have removable, washable covers and fast-drying cores. Quick-dry foam is a common choice, but you’ll still want to store cushions indoors or in a waterproof storage box during heavy rain or winter to prolong their life.

A good rule of thumb: if you can’t leave it outside for several days straight, this may not be the set for you. If you can, you’ll be rewarded with a low-maintenance, high-zen outdoor living space.

## Assembly and maintenance: how painful is this in practice?
### Assembly vibe
Most seven-piece sets arrive with a surprisingly unexciting number of parts that somehow require more patience than a new video game console. Expect: legs for coffee/dining tables, chair frames, cushions, corner connectors, and a handful of screws. The general assembly timeline ranges from 1 to 3 hours, depending on how carefully you read the instructions and whether you’ve decided to wear gloves or go full “DIY scientist.” The good news is that the components are designed to click together with minimal jargon and more “we made this so you can figure it out” logic. If you’ve ever assembled IKEA furniture, you’ll feel right at home here.

### Maintenance that doesn’t demand a PhD in horticulture
- Clean regularly with a mild detergent and water. Don’t use abrasive cleaners, or you’ll be auditioning for a role in a failed plastic-surgery commercial for furniture.
- Cover cushions when not in use or store them in a dry space to prolong the fabric’s life.
- Periodically check screws and joints; tighten what feels loose and pretend you’re a professional. It’s okay—we’ve all done it.
- If you notice weathering on the weave, a light wipe with a soft cloth helps, and a mild sealer can prolong life but test on a small area first.
