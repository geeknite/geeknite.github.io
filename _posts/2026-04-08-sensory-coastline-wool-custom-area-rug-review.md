## Customization: Your Space, Your Size

Here’s the exciting part for space planners and interior enthusiasts alike: you can opt for a fully custom size or go with the classic 8x10. The custom option means you provide the exact length and width in inches or centimeters, and the rug team translates that into a woven product that respects your room’s geometry. ### How the Custom Process Typically Goes

Expect a few standard questions: room dimensions, door swing clearance, furniture layout, desired nap density, and whether you want a denser feel near high-traffic zones (hallways, entryways) or a more relaxed nap across the living area. The production team will confirm squaring to edge-to-edge coverage and offer guidance on pile height to match foot traffic and cleaning expectations.

### When to Choose 8x10

If your space is a straightforward rectangle or you want a rug that’s easy to replace if you move, the 8x10 is a practical choice. It pairs well with most couches and beds and still leaves a generous border of floor visible around the perimeter, which helps anchor a room without making the rug feel overwhelming.

## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity: Keeping Your Rug Happy

Wool is inherently resilient, but like any fiber, it benefits from a routine. In everyday life, you’ll be blotting spills as they occur, vacuuming on a low-power setting to avoid fiber frizz, and giving the rug a spin every so often to prevent nap flattening from forming in predictable patterns.

### Cleaning Guidelines

- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing the area as it can push the stain deeper.
- For light soiling, use a wool-safe cleaner diluted according to the product’s instructions and test a small, inconspicuous corner first.
- If deeper staining occurs, consider professional wool cleaning services—yes, even rugs wear tuxedos when needed.
- Vacuum regularly, with the beater bar off or on a low setting, to prevent fuzzing and keep nap looking fresh.

### Longevity Outlook

With proper care, a wool rug ages gracefully. You’ll notice a patina forming—a sign of life in a piece that grows with you. The more it’s walked on, the more character it develops, which is a nice way of saying your living room becomes a storybook with every step.
