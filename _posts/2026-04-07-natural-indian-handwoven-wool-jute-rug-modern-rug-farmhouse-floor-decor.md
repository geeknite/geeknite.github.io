## Practical considerations: durability, care, and everyday life

Natural fibers require a touch more care than synthetic options, but the payoff is worth it. Expect a modest shed of loose fibers when first unrolling, which tends to decrease as the weave settles. Vacuum with a gentle setting and avoid aggressive high-suction passes that could pull loose threads. Do not saturate the rug with moisture; water and natural fibers are a questionable romance that you don’t want to pursue. For spills, blot promptly with a clean cloth and move on to the next task in your life, preferably while wearing slippers for added drama.

### Sizing and placement: what works where

For living rooms, an 8x10 or 6x9 size is a solid starting point under a coffee table and seating arrangement, allowing a comfortable border around the furniture. In dining rooms, opt for a larger rug that extends beyond chair footprints to prevent chairs from catching on the edge when pulled out. In entryways or hallways, a narrower but longer rug helps protect the floor in high-traffic zones while maintaining a graceful flow. Always measure the space before purchasing to ensure harmony between rug, furniture and walking routes.

## Care tips and maintenance: keeping it looking honest

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting. A few light passes each week keep dirt at bay without stressing the fibers.
- Rotate periodically to ensure even wear across the rug and prevent localized flattening where foot traffic concentrates.
- If you must refresh, use a light fabric refresher or a mild, natural cleaning method. Avoid heavy chemical sprays that can degrade the fibers over time.
- Shield from prolonged direct sunlight to minimize fading, while acknowledging that a little sun can help natural fibers age gracefully.
- For stubborn stains, seek professional cleaning every couple of years depending on usage; natural fibers respond well to careful, low-impact cleaning.

### Quick care comparison in everyday life
