## Overview

Dragon Ball Super Card Game continues to ride the wave of nostalgia while delivering modern design that respects the game’s age and the players’ wallets. The Ultimate Squad Booster Box USBB aims to be a curated starter for teams: the team synergy, the squad theme cards, and a little extra shine for collectors. But is it more than a cosmetic upgrade? We'll dive in.

### What’s in the box

The standard USBB box promises 24 booster packs, with each pack containing 12 cards. If you're counting, that’s 288 cards per box (minus misprints and the occasional duplicate pity break). The big pull here is the squad-based card archetypes—cards themed around four iconic Dragon Ball teams Earthlings, Saiyans, Namekians, and Majins, for example. There are foil parallels and at least a couple of secret rares that spook purists and collectors alike. The box also includes one or more promo cards that are exclusive to the booster, plus a large poster showing the squads’ synergy matrix. Some posters are decorative; some are the kind of map you use to plan your next tournament route rather than hang on the wall.

In the wild, not all boosters are equal. Some packs can be hot, others not so much, and the ultimate value of the box hinges on your pull luck. If you're a pack hunter, you'll want to weigh whether your dice rolls align with your budget. If you’re a collector who loves shiny things, you’ll be chasing the limited foil versions, especially the Leader foil that sometimes appears with a dramatic misprint-free sheen.

### Card pool and rarity distribution

The Ultimate Squad set is designed to reward players who want to build around team themes. The distribution of rare and ultra-rare cards is tuned to balance the price per usable card with the thrill of chasing something more powerful. You’ll typically see: common cards that fill out your deck, uncommons that offer synergy for the squads, rares that unlock integral combos, and foiled versions of the more important cards. The rarities are similar to past sets, with a handful of chase foils that become conversation pieces in local meta scenes. For the competitive player, the big question is whether the new cards introduce unique interactions that can outpace existing builds or whether they simply add style points and new flavor.

### Build considerations

If you’re planning to use USBB as a core booster for your squad-themed deck, you’ll want to think about synergy first. Are you building around four core archetypes or leaning into a leaner, theme-centric strategy? The box’s design encourages you to collect multiple copies of core cards to unlock consistent combos, but that raises the bill. A careful budget plan is essential: you may decide to chase three or four target rares to anchor your deck and fill out the rest with more common cards that have good synergy.

## Artwork and Theme

The Ultimate Squad Booster Box doubles down on art direction. If you’re into the Dragon Ball aesthetic bold colors, dynamic poses, and crisp foil effects USBB delivers. The card frames, backgrounds, and character art are styled to evoke team dynamics and in-battle chemistry. The foil printing catches light in a way that makes the sleeves glow during sunny gaming sessions, which is important since a well-lit table is the difference between an epic moment and a shrug tape.

From a design perspective, the synergy between squad members isn’t just a marketing gimmick. Some cards provide cross-team bonuses when you place them in certain positions or phases of the match, creating a little puzzle you can enjoy solving across a dozen games. The artwork itself is a treat for fans and a potential distraction for your opponent, who might momentarily forget that you have a game-winning play in your hand just because a DRAGON FOIL catches their eye.

## Play Experience

This booster box shines when you actually get to play with the cards you pulled. The first thing you’ll notice is that the box encourages experimentation. The leadership cards—if you’re into the Leader Plus mechanic typical of DBS—present interesting decision points: do you commit to a high risk, high reward route, or do you build a safer mid-range plan that relies on the squad’s local synergy? Either way, you’ll have to adjust your expectations. In casual play, the squad synergy can create satisfying combos that feel creative but not overpowered; in competitive settings, the results will depend on your ability to pilot the card pool while anticipating the meta shifts caused by other players opening similar sets.

The box’s layout also influences opening order. You’ll want to lay out your packs in a visible order, so you can gauge your pull trend: are you hitting multiple rares in a row, or is it a run of common cards and the occasional rare? The tactile experience of ripping packs, hearing the familiar crinkle, and revealing a rare foil has a weirdly comforting effect that is worth noting for those of us who live in a world where our hobby budgets are seriously debated by our cats.

## Competitive Viability

Is the Ultimate Squad Booster Box a competitive centerpiece, or is it best left to the shelf for collectors who adore the shiny? It depends on your local meta. In a format where every deck needs multiple copies of a few key cards, the box can accelerate your pool-building and provide those crucial rares. If your meta rewards heavy synergy with team-based strategies, you’ll likely find that the new cards slot into existing builds or spark new synergy lines that weren’t feasible before.

However, for veteran players who track power level and combos with laser precision, the newness of USBB might not translate into a dramatic meta-shift. The Dragon Ball Super Card Game is a game with a strong design space, and while new sets introduce interesting interactions, the core strategies often revolve around tried-and-true plays. That doesn't mean you shouldn’t buy the box—far from it. It means you should manage expectations: think of USBB as a currency faucet that floods your collection with squad-themed cards rather than a pure power spike.

### Deck-building tips

- Start with a core squad: select a few synergy cards that can anchor your deck and then fill the rest with support cards that enable combos.
- Mind your card draw: new cards from USBB may provide draw power, but too many draws can lead to card overload where you see your key play too late. Balance is key.
- Foil economy: if you’re chasing foils for display or collection, plan your pulls by sorting out the foils that matter, so you don’t end up with eight redundant foil commons.
- Sideboard strategy: the box’s new cards can shine in sideboard configurations, particularly when the meta introduces a match-up you didn’t expect.

## Value Assessment

Let’s address the elephant in the room price versus value. Booster boxes are a known quantity in the hobby: you pay the price of admission for the draft experience, multiple pulls, and the chance of a chase. The Ultimate Squad Booster Box is not the cheapest box on the shelf, but it offers a curated, theme-driven experience that may pay for itself in two ways: (1) upgrade to your local tournament-ready deck with the key rares; (2) build a personal collection of the supers and foils that define the squad’s flavor.

From a pure fun per dollar perspective, USBB scores well for players who love the hunt and the aesthetic of a cohesive squad. If you’re a budget player who wants a single deck to take to a tournament, you’ll want to be selective about what you pull and consider trading for the missing pieces. If you’re a collector who enjoys the chase and the convertible value of foils, you’ll likely find yourself chasing the chase cards and accepting the rest as a cost of entry into a bigger collector’s set. In short the box is a mixed bag, but a well-tuned one for the right players.

## Collectibility and Value Retention

In the world of collectible card games value retention is tricky. Some cards from older sets spike in value due to playability, art direction, or historic significance. USBB cards may or may not retain value in the long run. It’s a good rule of thumb to treat this as entertainment value first and investment second. If you’re buying purely as an investment you’ll want to pay attention to: (a) the availability of chase foils, (b) the number of print runs, and (c) whether a reprint is likely in a future set. The last item is notoriously unpredictable in the world of limited print runs.

That said some fan-favorite characters and team-themed cards become iconic and maintain a degree of desirability in the community, especially when combined with unique foil treatments. If you’re a long-term collector, consider setting aside a few boxes as a time capsule of your early squad-building experiments.

## Comparisons to Other Sets

How does USBB stack up against previous booster boxes for the Dragon Ball Super Card Game? The short answer: it adds a cohesive flavor. Other sets offered more raw power or more aggressive chase rares, but USBB differentiates itself by focusing on theme and synergy. If your group enjoys themed deck-building nights, USBB can be a hit. If you’re chasing raw power in a vacuum, you might prefer a box with a higher number of must-have rare cards that slot straight into meta builds.

In terms of card art and production quality, USBB continues the tradition of high production values. The foiling is consistent, the card stock feels sturdy, and the packaging design is a plus for display. You’ll see the squad emphasis in both the front-facing art and the cards’ mechanical text, creating a cohesive feel across the set.

## Final Thoughts

The Ultimate Squad Booster Box represents a deliberate attempt by the Dragon Ball Super Card Game line to reward squad-first play and to celebrate the lore of team-based battles. It isn’t the most explosive drop in DBS history, but it’s a well-executed product that makes it easier for players to explore a team-centric strategy without blowing the budget on a lot of individual rares. The box shines in social settings local tournaments, game nights with friends, and content creator streams where the thrill of the pull and the joy of seeing a well-chosen foil can be the star of the show.

If your aim is pure efficiency in adding new tools to your deck, you’ll likely be satisfied with the core rares and foils that slip into your build. If your aim is to complete a set and chase collector-grade cards, USBB is a solid platform to start that journey, though you’ll want to brace for the financial side of chasing foils. Either way the immersion of seeing your team-themed strategy come alive in actual matches is a satisfying experience that few other booster boxes can rival in terms of theme coherence.

## Final Recommendation

- If you play casually and love the squad idea: Yes, pick up a box or two. The synergistic cards plus the foil chase feel will keep your evenings lively and your card binder growing.
- If you’re a tournament player chasing top-tier meta shifts: You’ll want to run the numbers. There are likely more powerful cards in other sets, but USBB can still bring powerful alternative lines that catch opponents by surprise.
- If you’re a collector who loves art and foil aesthetics: This is a home run. The foil variants and the squad-themed art feel like a celebration of the DBS IP.

In short the Ultimate Squad Booster Box is a solid, thematically cohesive addition to the DBS Card Game catalog. It may not crush every meta, but it makes the journey more enjoyable, and that matters more than most people admit.

For the brave, the bold, and the foil-obsessed, the only question is do you want to squad up now or later?
