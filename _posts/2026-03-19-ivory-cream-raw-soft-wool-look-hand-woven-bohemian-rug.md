### Cleaning and care: wool care 101
Wool is naturally resilient and stain resistant to a degree, but it still requires reasonable care. The standard guidance for wool rugs is to vacuum with a gentle setting and to blot spills promptly. A damp cloth is sufficient for most surface stains; avoid aggressive scrubbing that could push the stain deeper into the fibers. For deeper cleaning, you may consider a professional rug cleaning service with experience in handmade textiles. If you are the DIY type, you can approach spot cleaning with a mild detergent, testing first on a small, inconspicuous area. Remember, wool can felt slightly if it gets too wet, so always wring out liquids well and let the rug dry in a well-ventilated space. The end result is a rug that keeps its character without losing its charm after a few coffee spills and pet adventures.

### Rug pads and placement: keeping things steady
A rug pad is your quiet hero in this saga. It prevents slipping, reduces wear in high-traffic zones, and adds a layer of comfort under bare feet. If you have underfloor heating, check pad compatibility to maintain warmth while protecting the fibers. The pad also helps with sound absorption, which is a small but delightful upgrade for apartments where conversations happen at the speed of a nested laugh track. Placement matters as well: center the rug within your seating arrangement to create a balanced anchor for decor elements like sofas, coffee tables, and a mythical plant that refuses to stay upright.
