## Assembly guide: quick-start notes

If you love the smell of fresh hardware in the morning, you will enjoy the initial unboxing and layout phase. The assembly process is designed to be friendly for teams that are not professional installers, though a couple of hands helps. Key steps in the typical process:

1) Lay out the frames and verify all parts against the hardware kit.
2) Assemble the base row on a level surface, making sure there is a straight line from end to end. We tested with a laser level; if you don’t own one, a long straight edge and a bit of patience will do.
3) Attach the divider panels at the planned intervals. Height-adjustable panels ship in several ready-to-use heights, so you can dial them in for your team.
4) Route cables through the designated troughs and into the central spine. Label each outlet so you don’t create a web of mystery wires.
5) Install monitor arms, keyboard trays, and lighting if you chose the optional upgrades.
6) Do a quick sanity check: can everyone see their screen clearly? Can you still listen for the ping of a new PR without using a crowbar? If yes, you pass the test.

## Maintenance, warranty, and care

Durability is a central claim of the Infinite 10. Regular maintenance includes checking fasteners, cleaning surfaces, and ensuring dividers remain properly seated in their tracks. Wipe surfaces with a mild cleaner and a soft cloth to preserve the laminate finish. The divider fabric can be vacuumed or spot-cleaned; avoid aggressive solvents that might degrade the fibers. Most vendors offer a multi-year warranty on the frame and a separate warranty on the dividers, reflecting confidence in their long-term performance.

If you encounter any structural wobble, the fix is often as simple as re-tightening a few screws or swapping out a worn-out foot. If your team treats furniture like a living entity with feelings, you may want to schedule quarterly check-ins to ensure everything remains in alignment.
