## Maintenance, Longevity, and Staying in the Game
### Cleaning, care, and minor fixes
To keep the table playing like new, a simple maintenance routine goes a long way. Wipe the playing surface with a dry microfiber cloth after use to remove dust and fingerprint smudges. If you spill something—yes, even the heroic spill of coffee—the surface is forgiving but avoid harsh cleaners that may degrade the lacquer. The rods should be wiped down periodically; if they begin to squeak, a light drop of mineral oil on the bearings can quiet the chorus of squeaks, while not making the ball behave like it’s glued to the table.

The legs and frame benefit from a quick inspection every so often. Loose screws? Tighten them. Wobbly legs? Check the floor and adjust the levelers. You’ll want a level surface for fair competition; otherwise, the ball might decide to roll uphill and you’ll end up with a “home-field advantage” that isn’t exactly fair.

### Longevity and parts availability
The 22‑player configuration is not a fragile experiment; it’s a robust design meant to endure. That said, if you’re hosting large events, keep a small stock of spare phillips screws and tiny washers—these are the kinds of things that vanish into the void between couch cushions after a few games. The manufacturer’s accessories pack (extra balls and a spare set of rods) is worth looking into if you’re serious about a long-running league. Pro tip: label your parts so you don’t confuse the spare rods with the regular ones after a late-night purchase frenzy.
