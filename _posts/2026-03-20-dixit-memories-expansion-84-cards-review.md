## What’s in the Box and What Sets 84 Cards Apart

The standard Dixit box is a well-oiled storytelling machine. The Memories expansion arrives with 84 new illustrated cards, a handful of tokens that help track the rounds, and a refreshed rule pamphlet that’s perhaps 2 pages longer and 2 pages wittier than the original. The 84 cards are the star of the show here, and they arrive in themes that range from crystalline dreamscapes to rustic fairy-tale villages, from cosmic umbrellas to anthropomorphic vegetables. Yes, there are some wild, almost psychedelic pieces that look like they were sketched during a lucid dream in which you forgot to wear pants, and that is exactly the vibe Dixit loves.

In terms of balance, the expansion doesn’t overwhelm the base game’s structure. Dixit thrives on players interpreting imagery in unique, often wildly divergent ways; the additional imagery creates more anchors for different storytelling styles—some players will lean into literal interpretation, others will lean into the metaphorical, and a few will simply describe the card as if it were a famous artwork that happened to be in their living room coffee table. The 84 cards give you more ways to phrasing, more angles to approach a card, and more opportunities for a misdirection that lands like a well-placed pillow at a pillow fort championship.

If you’re a completionist who likes to have every theme covered in your collection, the Memories cards will feel like a treasure chest you can crack open during a slow game night. If you’re new to Dixit, the expansion may feel a bit intimidating at first—84 unique prompts is a lot to digest—but the art direction remains playful and intuitive enough that the new cards become second nature within a handful of rounds.

To set expectations: the expansion does not rewrite the core rules. It doesn’t require new equipment beyond the cards themselves and perhaps a more ambitious storyteller. The goal remains the same: describe a card in a way that nudges your fellow players toward guessing the card you picked, while you still get some points when someone else lands on your card’s intended meaning. The Memories cards simply broaden the field of descriptive possibilities and give you more ammunition for ridiculous metaphors, wild similes, and the occasional poetic monologue that would make a Shakespearean bard blink in confused admiration.

## Gameplay Dynamics: How the 84 Cards Shift the Dance

Dixit thrives on a cycle: a storyteller describes a card, others pick a card that best fits that description, and players guess which card was the storyteller’s. The Memories expansion injects more material into that cycle, and with it comes several practical implications:

- More anchors for description: The new imagery gives storytellers a broader vocabulary to conjure up plausible yet eccentric interpretations. If you’ve ever wanted to describe a card as a “teacup tornado in a library,” you’ll appreciate the expanded repertory.
- Greater variance in guesses: Because there are more cards in play, players may feel their guesses are less predictable. It raises the challenge for the storyteller to craft clues that are neither too obvious nor too obscure.
- Thematic cohesion with a twist: The cards often pair with the familiar Dixit vibe—dreamy, metaphorical, and a little enigmatic—yet the expansion pushes into more specific settings like memory vignettes or dream-adjacent scenes. It’s a subtle shift toward storytelling that feels more narrative, while still staying within the game’s accessible core.

From a practical standpoint, the 84-card pool reduces the chance of repetition in a single session, which is a blessing for long game nights with a rotating cast. It also means you can sprinkle in a couple of “theme nights” where you rotate storytellers with a consistent set of memory-invoking prompts. The result is a more lively, less predictable rhythm that keeps even veteran players on their toes.

## Strategies, Tips, and Toasts: Getting the Most from Memories

- Tell a story that is specific enough to guide but broad enough to remain open to interpretation. A well-chosen memory-lane description can be a tripwire for the right guess without caving too easily into the obvious.
- Use the card’s mood to balance risk. If the card feels warm and cozy, you can describe it with a partially cryptic, comforting metaphor. If it’s eerie or surreal, lean into the uncanny—call forth a narrative that feels almost like a whispered legend.
- Embrace collaborative storytelling. Dixit is, at its core, a social experience. The Memories expansion rewards players who can blend humor, pathos, and vivid imagery into a single, compelling mini-story that makes others reach for a card they hope will fit the vibe.
- For new players, consider running a few practice rounds with the base rules before introducing the Memories deck. Once everyone is comfortable with the tempo, the 84 cards become a playground that unlocks new storytelling modes.

If you’re a storyteller who loves a good misdirection, you’ll find that many Memories cards offer a personal twist on familiar themes. A single descriptor can conjure a scene that looks innocent at first glance but reveals a sly subtext once your fellow players pick up on the subtle hints you drop as you narrate. The trick is to calibrate your description to be just specific enough to guide intent without pinning it down so tightly that it becomes a riddle with a known answer.
