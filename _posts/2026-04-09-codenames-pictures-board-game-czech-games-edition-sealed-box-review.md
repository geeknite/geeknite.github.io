## How Codenames Pictures Differs
If you played the original Codenames you will recognize a familiar rhythm in the turn sequence: clue giver speaks, team guesses, round ends, next team takes its turn. The major difference is substitution of imagery for words, which shifts the mental load and opens up new possibilities and a few pitfalls. Some players will be more comfortable with visual metaphors; others may struggle to translate a concept into a striking image in a single clue. The rulebook invites you to discuss house rules if necessary, which is a polite way to say you might want to tweak the time limit if your group tends to overthink.

### Strategies for New Players
- Start with a safe broad category: big umbrella ideas that you know will land with your team
- Use distinct categories: avoid vague or overlapping themes that only look obvious to you
- Pace yourself: a well-timed concise clue beats long-winded explanations
- Watch your timing: use the timer to keep momentum but don’t let it force you into reckless guesses

### Strategies for Experienced Players
- Refer to shared pop culture shorthand: inside jokes can be powerful if your team is in on them
- Plan multi-card clues with risk management: connecting several cards is great if you can avoid the assassin
- Use negative clues carefully: sometimes a hint about what something is not can be as helpful as what it is
