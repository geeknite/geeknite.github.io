## Deep Dive: The Print, the Print Quality, and The Curious 192/190

Print numbers are supposed to be sequential, but the Pokemon card printing world loves a surprise party. The 192/190 label hints at a card that maybe started life in a larger set, got misnumbered, or simply wandered into a different printing lane during the production week. For the collector mind, that is intoxicating because it adds an aura of scarcity that can translate into UI-friendly resale chatter, even if the actual physical count is not a rare treasure in the wild.

But let us be precise and not fall into the trap of romance without a method. Here is a practical checklist for evaluating a NM Japanese Gloom of this nature:

- Inspect the holo foil pattern under a light: Shiny holo patterns in Japanese prints are often more nuanced than their English counterparts. Look for even distribution and no harsh scratches running parallel to the card edge.
- Check the surface gloss: Near Mint means you should not see major scratches, scuffs, or whitening lines along the edges. A faint swirl of gloss is common; a rough texture is not.
- Examine the corners and edges: NM needs crisp edges, sharp corners, and no visible whitening. In a rare holo, tiny micro-creases can slip into the background, and a true unicorn would still be close to NM with the right lighting.
- Verify the print line and font: Japanese fonts are distinctive; misalignment or font bleeding can be a sign of a reprint or counterfeit attempt. If you are new to the hobby, bring along a trusted guide or use a reliable online reference to confirm typography.
- Confirm the card's size and weight: Gloom should sit squarely within standard dimensions. If the card feels off, it may point toward a counterfeit concern.

In our review copy, the surface is clean, the holo has a lively sheen, and the corners are crisp enough to pass a casual field check. The caveat: you should run the same checks yourself in person or via a trusted seller’s close-up photos. ## Authenticity and Verification: Keeping Your Collection Safe

In the world of high-end Pokemon cards, authenticity is a guardian that must be treated with respect. While this Gloom in NM condition reads well in photos, you want to verify a few essentials when buying:

- Seller reputation: A long track record and positive feedback on reputable marketplaces matters more than a flashy listing. Look for sellers who provide detailed photos of each edge and corner.
- Card weight and texture: Genuine cards have consistent texture across the holo area. Anomalies in texture can indicate a counterfeit attempt or a misprint reproduction.
- Print-specific cues: The language and typography used in Japanese prints sometimes include subtle markers used by authentic distributors. If you are unsure, compare your card with trusted references and, if possible, request additional close-up shots from the seller.

To keep your collection safe, you should also invest in proper storage solutions. Our standard advice is to keep rare prints in fully sealed, acid-free sleeves inside a rigid top loader, then store them in a climate-controlled binder or storage box. A simple setup improves longevity and reduces the risk of color shifts and edge wear that plague a card’s long-term value.
