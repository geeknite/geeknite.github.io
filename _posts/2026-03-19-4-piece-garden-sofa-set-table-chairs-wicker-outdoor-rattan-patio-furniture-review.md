## Durability and Weather Resistance
Durability in outdoor furniture is an exercise in mastering the elements. The aluminum frame offers a light yet sturdy core that resists corrosion and heavy rust. The PE wicker is designed to resist UV rays so it will not fade quickly, though you should still expect a slow fade over years if you have a porch that faces the sun all day. Water exposure is not a crime, but you should avoid leaving cushions drenched to the point of stagnation. Water repellent fabrics are common, but you should not assume they replace shade or indoor storage during heavy storms. A well made set will handle wind gusts that are typical for a normal afternoon on your balcony without tipping over. If you live in a gale zone with hurricane level gusts, you should consider anchoring or bringing cushions inside. The durability is also affected by how you treat the cushions. The zipper closures should function after multiple cycles; if the zipper catches, you can treat with a small amount of lubricant or coax the slider gently to avoid damage.

## Maintenance and Care Tips
To keep the set looking fresh you should consider a few regular maintenance steps. Clean the wicker using a soft brush to remove loose dirt, followed by a gentle soap solution and water rinse. Do not soak the wicker as excessive moisture can degrade the weave over time. For the cushions remove the covers if possible and wash them on a gentle cycle. If the covers are not removable, you can spot clean with a mild detergent and a soft cloth. Allow the cushions to air dry completely before placing back to prevent mold growth. Use a protective deck cover during the off months to minimize dust and weather exposure. If you live in an area with heavy rain you may want to bring cushions indoors or store them in a waterproof container to avoid damp smells. The feet should be checked for wobble from time to time and tightened if necessary to keep the table stable.
