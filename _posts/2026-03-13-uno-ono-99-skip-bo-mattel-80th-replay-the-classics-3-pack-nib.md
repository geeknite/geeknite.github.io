### How to play this triple-pack: quick guides

#### UNO quick guide:
- Goal: be the first to shed all your cards.
- How to play: match color or number; number cards, wilds, and action cards change the pace.
- Special notes: the draw four and wild cards create tension, but remember your audience—these cards can escalate a family feud into a full-blown charter for a board game intervention.

#### O'NO quick guide:
- Goal: not getting knocked out by a sudden 'No!' or a color-blocking card. The core idea is fast, reactive play with a bit of chaos.
- How to play: players take turns; if you play the No card, you can stop others from your opponents' last action, and the game accelerates with shouted beckons of 'No!' from the crowd.

#### Skip-Bo quick guide:
- Goal: build sequential piles from 1 to 12 using stock piles and hand cards.
- How to play: plan ahead, manage wilds, and race to get your stacks to 12 before your rivals push back with a well-timed skip or junk move.

Of course, all three can be played in a rotation: you could start with UNO, pivot to O'NO for some chaotic energy, then close out with Skip-Bo to prove you’re the table’s ultimate planner. It’s a sandwich of gameplay styles that still holds together in a social setting.
