### Care and maintenance: practical guide

- Vacuuming: use a gentle setting and a nozzle attachment to avoid fiber pull on edges. Do not use a heavy-beater function that could loosen fibers.
- Spills: blot, don’t rub. Blotting with a clean, dry cloth or paper towel helps remove liquids quickly; if it’s oily or stubborn, use a wool-safe cleaner following product directions.
- Stains: treat promptly with a wool-safe stain remover; test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Aging and cleaning cycle: plan for periodic professional cleaning every 12–24 months, depending on traffic. Wool can shed a bit during first few months of use; regular vacuuming reduces this.
- Storage: roll, not fold, the rug if you need to store it; avoid plastic bags that trap moisture.

### Underlay and pad: the hidden hero

A rug pad isn’t optional; it’s essential. A good pad keeps the rug in place, protects floors, and adds extra cushioning. For heavy traffic zones, a thicker pad reduces wear and prevents the rug from creeping. On wooden floors, a felt pad prevents scuffing and helps preserve the finish. If you’re mixing textures (like a sisal chair mat or a leather couch), the pad prevents friction and keeps furniture from sliding.

### Room-ready styling: placement tips

- Living room: anchor the main seating with the rug so front legs of the sofa rest on the rug and not just the coffee table. If your space is long and narrow, a larger rug that stretches beyond the front feet of the furniture helps define the seating zone.
- Bedroom: a larger rug under the bed offers a soft landing when you rise. Ensure there’s rug visible on both sides of the bed to avoid a “bed floating in space” look.
- Office: a calmer rug reduces echo and adds a touch of warmth under a desk and chair.

### FAQs and common concerns
- Is Wool colorfast? In general, wool rugs resist fading well, but color stability depends on dyes used. If you have strong sunlight, consider rotating the rug to preserve color across the room.
- Will it shed? Wool sheds lightly in the first months, then settles down as the fibers settle. Regular vacuuming helps reduce shedding.
- How often should I clean it? Light cleaning every week or two and professional cleaning every 12–24 months depending on traffic.
