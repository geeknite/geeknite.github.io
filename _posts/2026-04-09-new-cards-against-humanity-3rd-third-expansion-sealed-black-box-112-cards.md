## How to Play with the Sealed Black Box

### Setup and player count
CAH operates best with a rotating deck of misfits. The 3rd Third Expansion works with the standard CAH rules: one player acts as the Card Czar while others submit their best matchups. The number of players influences round length; with 4–6 players you’ll enjoy snappier exchanges, while larger groups benefit from a longer session with more oddball combinations. The sealed black box contains 112 cards, but don’t worry about counting every card twice in a rush. A quick shuffle and distribution is all you really need to get things going.

### Turn order and scoring
Turns proceed as usual: the Card Czar reads a black card, players submit their best white card, and the Czars pick a winner. Scoring is straightforward: the winner collects a point, and the person with the most points at the end earns the coveted title of most dangerous improviser at the table. A friendly tip: if your group tends to escalate, set a soft cap on the number of rounds to avoid the room turning into a debate club with punchlines. The 3rd Third Expansion is designed to amplify energy, not exhaust it.

### Quick play tips
- Start with a warm-up round to calibrate humor levels. You’ll quickly discover who can pull the most absurd scenario from the box without needing a buffer of coffee.
- Use a timer for each round to maintain momentum. CAH is best when the banter remains rapid-fire rather than a slow chess match of wits.
- Mix in card prompts that require group improvisation. The social dynamics are where the expansion shines: the more players contribute to the story, the funnier the results tend to be.
