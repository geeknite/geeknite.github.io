## Technical Deep Dive: Ports and Power Delivery
The 120W G4 has you covered on the power front. It can deliver up to 120W to a connected laptop, ensuring that the battery never collapses into a sad, drained abyss during a long coding session or spreadsheet sprint. It also reallocates power intelligently between devices if you use multiple ports for accessories or a second monitor.

### Ports and Power Delivery
- 1 x USB-C with power delivery
- 2 x USB-A 3.x for peripherals
- 2 x Display outputs (HDMI and DisplayPort)
- 1 x Gigabit Ethernet
- 1 x Audio out
- 1 x USB-C data port
- 1 x 3.5 mm audio line in/out

### Display Support and Video Outputs
With a TB3/USB-C docking solution, you can expect robust external displays. The HP 120W G4 typically supports two 4K displays at 60 Hz or a single 5K if your GPU dreams big. The HDMI port is crisp, the DP handles higher color depths, and if you are a creator, this is the kind of docking station that lets you work with multiple dashboards and video timelines without the drama of broken cables.

### Audio and Networking
The dock ships with a reliable Ethernet connection and an optional audio line in/out. If you are into conference calls or streaming, the mic input on your laptop plus the dock’s passthrough audio makes life easier than juggling adapters.

## Real-World Performance

### Setup Experience
Plugging in is easy: connect the dock to a laptop via USB-C TB3, attach monitors to HDMI/DP, attach peripherals, and plug in the 120W power brick. The system negotiates for a moment, then the status LED announces the party has started. Windows, macOS, or Linux users should expect a consistent handshake.

### Multi-Display Scenarios
Dual-monitor setups are the sweet spot here. The dock handles two 4K displays, one 1080p monitor for chat, and your streaming mic all at once. This is the kind of setup that makes you feel you are in a real office, minus the fluorescent lighting and the HR emails.

### Compatibility Across Platforms
Windows users enjoy plug-and-play reliability; macOS users appreciate stable external display support; Linux users get raw power with occasional manual tweaks. If you are a Linux purist, you know the drill.

## Use Case Scenarios

### Remote Work Setup
For a home office that is more squiggle than square, the HP dock reduces cable chaos to a single data stream. A single cable to power the laptop, two cables to display fans, and a handful of USB ports for webcams, mics, or that fancy keyboard you pretend to be training with a dactylic meter.

### Creator Setup
For content creators, this is a hub that can handle two 4K screens, external storage, and a streaming mic. It helps you keep your workflow clean and your cable closet less of a horror show.

### Programmer Setup
Coders who like to run IDEs, Docker, and multiple terminals simultaneously will love the space to organize windows and projects. The dock makes the desktop feel less like a tangle of cables and more like a command center.
