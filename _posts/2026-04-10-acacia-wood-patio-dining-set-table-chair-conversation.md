## The Wood Behind the Curtain: Acacia and Its Superpowers

Acacia wood is widely used in outdoor furniture because it wears its weight well without getting grabby about weather. It is not fully maintenance-free, but it offers a reasonable balance between beauty and rugged durability. ## Weatherproofing, Maintenance, and Longevity

Acacia wood naturally handles moisture quite well, but no wood is completely immune to the elements. In our tests (which, admittedly, involved a few rainstorms and a lot of clip-on sunshades), the surface held up without warping or splintering, and the color remained warm without appearing washed out. To maximize lifespan, we recommend a light sealant or a teak oil-type finish renewed every season or after extended sun exposure. The goal is to preserve the color and prevent your table from becoming a chalkboard for the neighbor kids who scribble on everything except the living room wall—yes, even outdoor furniture has observers.

If you live in a high-humidity environment or near saltwater, consider applying a maintenance finish twice a year. It is not glamorous work, but it is the kind of maintenance that pays off in a long-lasting set that you won’t regret buying after two seasons of heavy lounging and unplanned after-dinner nerf battles.

## Maintenance Strategy: A Little Goes a Long Way

The most important maintenance move is to keep the wood clean and lightly oiled. Wipe with a damp cloth to remove dust and grime, then apply a protective finish every season to keep the grain looking bright and the surface sealed. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can strip the wood and any lingering character from your set. Instead, opt for mild soap and water or a wood-friendly cleaner, then wipe dry.

If you notice tiny cracks, sand them lightly and reapply your finish. This is not a sign of doom; it is the wood telling you to do a quick maintenance dance. Embrace the ritual, and your set will respond with many summers of companionship and the occasional casserole spill that cleans up easily.
