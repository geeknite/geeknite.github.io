## Overview

### What it’s trying to capture
Herd Mentality captures the social electricity of a room when a crowd decides together what’s funny. It’s not about flawless punchlines or custom references; it’s about reading the room, taking a risk, and experiencing that wave of laughter when a majority resonates with a shared thought. The design assumes a group that’s open to playful risk and ready to be surprised by each other’s tastes.

### Who should play
The optimal scenario is a group of 4–8 players who enjoy social banter and aren’t easily rattled by goofy, sometimes cringe-inducing humor. It’s ideal for friends who like board games but also want something they can play without spending an hour learning rules and another hour arguing about rules. Family game nights can benefit from its inclusive vibe, provided there’s a dynamic that’s comfortable for children and adults alike.

### What you get
The box typically includes prompt cards, answer slips, voting chips, and a simple score tracker. The prompts range from the silly to the relatable, with enough breadth to keep the table engaged across multiple sessions. The reward, of course, is the sense that the room is a living organism—the herd—whose collective laughter becomes the night’s soundtrack.

## Components and Setup

### Box contents
Inside the box you’ll find a spread that’s refreshingly modest for a party game. You get a deck of prompt cards, a set of blank answer slips or a dry-erase pad, voting chips, and a simple score tracker. The design favors quick setup and quick access; nothing here requires a three-page quick-start guide or a ten-minute read of a safety manual.

### Card design and text
Prompt cards are written to be legible at a glance, with concise prompts that invite quick comedic interpretation. The text leans into shared cultural ground and everyday experiences, allowing players to pull jokes from their own memory banks without needing a thesaurus or a calendar full of esoteric references.

### Setting up the table
To begin, shuffle the prompts and place them in a visible, accessible pile. Each player pulls an answer slip or gets a dry-erase board. Decide the reading order, ideally clockwise, to maintain a gentle flow. The round begins when a prompt is drawn and read aloud. Everyone writes their response in secret and places it facedown or stores it in a private space until reveal time. Then, answers appear, and the herd makes its move.

## Gameplay and Scoring

### The round flow
When a prompt card is read, players craft a response. Then, on a count of three or when the group agrees, all answers are revealed. Now the crucial moment hits: players vote for the answer they think will be chosen by the majority. The goal isn’t to bestow a prize on the cleverest line but to anticipate the room’s taste. Some rounds reward the author directly; others reward the room for picking a crowd-friendly answer. The tension and humor come from the tension between individuality and collective taste.

### Scoring
Points are awarded to those whose answers receive the most votes. If your answer is the one most players select, you earn points—sometimes a larger chunk if your answer is chosen as a majority pick. The exact numbers vary by edition, but the principle remains: the herd is rewarded for alignment with the majority or for predicting what that majority will want. The scoring is simple enough to keep the table moving but nuanced enough to reward social insight.

### Endgame
Most sessions run for a handful of rounds, with a winner declared once a pre-set score reaches a threshold or after a set number of rounds. It’s a party game, so the end is less a victory march and more a celebration of a shared evening that felt bigger than the sum of its parts.

## Humor: Why It Lands (or Misses)

### The versatility of humor
Humor in Herd Mentality lives in the room’s energy and the players’ willingness to take a risk. A good prompt invites jokers to present a line that could be both silly and oddly specific to the group’s memory. The best lines feel inevitable—like a private joke suddenly confessed aloud in front of a cheering crowd.

### When a joke lands and when it misses
A joke lands when the room’s sense of timing and taste align. It misses when the table is in a different mood, when inside jokes require too much setup, or when the prompt is too aggressive or unrelatable. The good news is that the herd can recover quickly; the next round offers a new opportunity to re-synchronize.

### Tips to maximize laughs
- Lean into the group’s shared experiences rather than chasing niche humor.
- Write with boldness and a dash of absurdity; commitment sells, even for simple lines.
- Use the reveal moment to gauge the room; if a line isn’t landing, pivot to a more universal tone for the next round.

## Social Dynamics and Group Play

### Group size, pacing, and energy
Herd Mentality shines in larger groups where the concept of majority becomes a live phenomenon. In smaller groups, the humor can still blaze, but the dynamics are more intimate, and the risk-reward may feel different. The game’s pacing scales with the energy of the room; quick rounds with a lot of participation generate the most memorable laughter.

### Comfort, safety, and consent
As with any social game, you should be mindful of players’ comfort levels. Establish a quick ground rule that prompts should be treated as prompts, not as invitations to target individuals. If someone is uncomfortable with a particular premise, skip or reframe the prompt. The best nights are those where everyone leaves with a smile and no one leaves feeling small.

### Handling sensitive prompts
Sensitive prompts require tact. In many groups, a simple approach is to have a standing policy: if the prompt touches a sensitive area, the group can veto it without penalty. This keeps the session on track and ensures the humor remains in good spirit.

## Host Tips and House Rules

### Quick-start hosting guide
- Explain the core loop quickly: write, reveal, vote, score, repeat.
- Set a soft time limit for responses (eg, 30–45 seconds per prompt) to keep energy high.
- Consider pairing shy players with more outgoing teammates or providing prompts that invite collaborative creativity.

### Custom prompts and table tweaks
- Create a mini-prompt deck focusing on specific groups (eg, a sci-fi club, a foodie-night crew).
- Use themed rounds to align with a current event or fantasy universe.

### Music, vibe, and tempo
If you’re running a party game night, a light playlist can help maintain energy without overshadowing the laughter. Avoid anything too loud; your goal is to maintain conversation while still providing a lively dynamic.

## Comparisons with Similar Games

### Quiplash
Quiplash rewards quick-witted lines; Herd Mentality rewards alignment with a room’s taste and social cleverness. If you want a rapid-fire duel of zingers, Quiplash is a strong choice; if you want the energy of predicting a crowd’s reaction, Herd Mentality will feel more natural.

### Cards Against Humanity
Cards Against Humanity leans into shock value and boundary-pushing humor, which can be polarizing. Herd Mentality emphasizes inclusive fun and shared laughter rather than isolating jokes. It’s the gentler cousin designed to bring a room together rather than push it apart.

### Wavelength
Wavelength focuses on agreeing on a concept along a spectrum, with players guessing where the group will land on that spectrum. It’s a more strategic flavor of social alignment, while Herd Mentality is about the humor that arises from the room’s collective taste.
