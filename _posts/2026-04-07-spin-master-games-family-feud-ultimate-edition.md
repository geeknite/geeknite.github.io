## How to play
### The quick setup
Set the tin on the table, riffle the cards, and decide who will be the host. The host reads a survey prompt from the top card and the team buzzes in with the top answer they think the board audience would say. If your answer matches the top one, your team claims the points; if not, you get a shot at the second best answer on the card. The general flow resembles a friendly high energy market where everyone wants to be the first to shout the right thing, except the prices on the goods are replaced by points and bragging rights.

### The mechanics you actually use
The Ultimate Edition leans into classic family feud vibes quick rounds fast scoring and a couple of twists that make it feel fresh. Expect a buzzer element a host card that keeps the pace and a deck of prompts that range from silly to surprisingly strategic. You will want to manage time especially if you are playing with folks who love to debate over top answers. The clock is a friendly presence that prevents long winded speeches and turns every round into a sprint.

### House rules and tips
If you are playing with younger players or forgetful adults who freeze when they see a top card you may add a few house rules. For instance you can let the first team guess two answers in a burst if they come up with a particularly good reasoning or you can award bonus points for flair in explaining why a specific answer should be the top one. The point is to keep the energy high while not turning every round into a courtroom drama.
