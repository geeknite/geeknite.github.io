### Strategy notes

- UNO is a game of tempo. You want to push your opponents toward drawing cards only when necessary and to leave yourself with a flexible set of options for the late game.
- Wild cards give you control but also reveal your patience level. If you save a Wild for a crucial moment, you can turn a losing streak around; if you waste one early, you may wake up with regret later.
- The gold coins are a fun social mechanism but they should not overshadow the main objective: be the first to shout UNO when you have one card left. The coins simply provide a playful scoreboard and a reason to taunt your friends a little more than usual.
