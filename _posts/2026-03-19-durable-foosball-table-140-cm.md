Maintenance Tips and Tricks
Leveling, Cleaning, and Quick Fixes
- Level the table regularly; a small bubble level is your friend. Uneven surfaces cause unpredictable ball spins that feel like a conspiracy against your shot accuracy.
- Clean the playing surface after a few sessions to remove chalk dust and sticky fingerprints. A microfiber cloth dampened with a tiny amount of water is enough; harsh cleaners can degrade the finish.
- Periodically inspect rod end caps and bearings. If you hear squeaking, a dab of light lubricant can quiet things down—avoid over-lubrication, which attracts dust and debris.
- Store spare balls in a cool, dry place. Sooner or later, someone will launch a ball into your favorite lamp, and you’ll thank the storage strategy you didn’t ignore.

Warranty and Support
