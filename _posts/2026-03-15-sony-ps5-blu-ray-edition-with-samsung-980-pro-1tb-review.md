## Installation Guide: Quiet, Easy, and Largely Self-Educational

Installing an M.2 NVMe SSD into the PS5 is not a witchcraft ritual; it’s a process you can do with a small screwdriver and a dash of confidence. Here’s a practical step-by-step that keeps it simple and safe.

### Step 1: Prep and Safety
- Ensure your PS5 is turned off and unplugged.
- Let it cool for a bit if you’ve been gaming for a while. The surface will still be warm enough to remind you that this device loves you and hates your wrist health.
- Remove all packaging and clear a space on your coffee table. You don’t want to drop the torque-screwdriver on the cat while you’re doing this.

### Step 2: Open the PS5 Expansion Slot
- Place the PS5 on its side or stand to access the expansion slot. Remove the screw cover and use a screwdriver (Phillips 1.5–2.0 mm) to unscrew the expansion slot cover.
- Slide the cover out and set it aside. You’re one step closer to storage nirvana.

### Step 3: Install the SSD
- Check that your SSD is M.2 2280 with a heatsink (the Samsung 980 PRO with Heatsink fits perfectly).
- Insert the SSD at a 30-degree angle into the M.2 slot, then push it flat and fasten with the small screw included with the PS5 expansion kit or your choice of scheme. Do not overtighten; we’re aiming for a snug fit, not a dental procedure.
- Reattach the expansion slot screw cover.

### Step 4: Finalize and Format
- Reconnect power and boot up the PS5. The system should detect the new drive and prompt you to format it.
- Follow the on-screen prompts to format the drive. The PS5 will create the necessary folders and set up the drive for games and apps.
- This step will wipe any data on the new drive, so make sure you haven’t accidentally filled it with a bunch of unplayed games you’ll pretend to care about later.

This process is straightforward enough that your grandma could handle it with a stern look and a cup of tea. The result is a swanky upgraded storage space that won’t ruin your living room feng shui with a mysterious, tangled cable mess.

## Compatibility and Caveats: What to Expect (And What Not To Expect)

Before you rush to Amazon or your favorite tech shop and drop a load of cash on a shiny new 1TB drive, a few caveats.

- Compatibility: The PS5 expansion slot accepts M.2 NVMe SSDs that meet the required dimensions and thermal design constraints. The Samsung 980 PRO with Heatsink adheres to these guidelines, but always double-check your model and the PS5 firmware version before purchase.
- Heat management: Heatsinks are not optional for sustained load. The PS5 push to install drives with proper heat dissipation is a small price to pay for longer gaming sessions without throttling.
- Warranty considerations: Modifying your PS5 with an expansion drive can affect warranties if there is damage caused by the install. Follow Sony’s guidelines and warranty statements; treat your hardware with care and consider professional installation if you’re unsure.
- Patching and system updates: Firmware updates can adjust how the PS5 handles third-party storage. If you encounter any odd behavior, verify that both the PS5 system software and the SSD firmware are up to date.
