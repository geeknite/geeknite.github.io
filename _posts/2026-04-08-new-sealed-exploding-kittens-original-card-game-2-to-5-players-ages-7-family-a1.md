## How to Play: The Core Mechanics
Exploding Kittens is a game about risk, timing, and a little bit of deceit. The core loop is simple, but the joy comes from the way you can twist the turn order with a single card draw. Here is a concise guide to the flow of a typical game session:

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand to each player. The exact number of cards you start with can vary by edition, but the basic idea is to give everyone enough tools to improvise without immediately triggering a meltdown.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile. Add a number of Defuse cards to each player’s hand if your edition requires it. Defuse cards are your lifelines — use them to nullify an Exploding Kitten instead of going out in a puff of cat fur.

### Turn Structure
On your turn, you perform two actions: draw a card and play a card (or more, if your hand allows). The draw happens at the end of the turn, so you can see what you’re risking before you take that final gulp of risk juice.

- If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out of the game. If you do have a Defuse card, you can use it to survive, and then place the Exploding Kitten back into the draw pile wherever you want — the gambler’s choice.
- Cat Cards let you copy and chain effects. Some Cat Cards require you to combo with other Cat Cards, some simply give you extra draws or the power to skip someone else’s turn. The Cat Card theme drives a lot of the humor and creative skulduggery.
- Other Action Cards let you alter the flow of the game: attack, see the future, shuffle, skip your turn, steal cards, and more. The exact mix depends on your edition, but the vibe remains the same — you can often turn a fail into a glorious comeback with a well-timed play.

### End Game
The game ends when all but one player has exploded. The last survivor is crowned the winner, typically with a flourish and a lot of dramatic pointing at the pillow fort you built around the table to protect your status as the last kitten standing.

The pacing is fast. Most rounds are over in 10–20 minutes, which makes Exploding Kittens ideal for a quick party session or a longer evening that hops between a few different games. If you prefer long, slow strategy, you might want to consider something heavier; if you want quick chaos with friends and family, this is your jam.
