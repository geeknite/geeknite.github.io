## Painting Mortimer: tips and tricks
### Prime, paint, and patience
- Start with a neutral primer: a light grey or white base helps your colors pop and gives you a reliable surface to work on.
- Build up layers: Mortimer’s cloak and armor have multiple planes; use thin layers to preserve the sculpt’s detail without obscuring lines.
- Washes for depth: a subtle sepia or brown wash can bring out the texture in the cloth, leather, and armor plates. Don’t drown the mini; a controlled wash yields better depth.
- Dry brushing for highlights: a light dry brush on raised edges highlights the sculpt’s edges. It brings Mortimer to life without requiring a master’s touch.
- Flags for personality: a pop of color on the cloak or a contrasting metallic edge can add personality to the otherwise monochrome palette.

### Assembly notes for loose minis
- Check for gaps: bindery seams are more common on loose minis. Fill tiny gaps with a bit of green stuff or modeling putty before priming.
- Base mounting: think about a scenic base. A small scenic base can anchor Mortimer and provide stability. If you don’t want to invest in a full base, you can mount Mortimer on a 32 mm plastic base and add a small scenic tile.
- Stabilization: if Mortimer is top-heavy or has a dynamic pose, consider pinning the miniature to the base for durability on the table during play.
