## What's Fortune Row and what are Power Cards anyway?

Fortune Row is the name of the new locus and its associated micro-genre within City of Mist, a city where myths live under street signs and vending machines. The Power Cards deck is the physical embodiment of that idea: a collection of one-shot tools, twists, and micro-mechanics you can pull during a scene to tilt the odds, shift a NPCs mood, or gently prank a PC who thought they were the protagonist of a noir cyberpunk opera. Think of it as a deck of cinematic props that your players can draw from to compress or stretch a scene in delightful, sometimes chaotic ways.

Fortune Row Power Cards are designed to complement the core rules instead of replacing them. They slot into your game sessions as a set of optional triggers and modifiers you can deploy when the story needs a gust of wind. Players don’t have to memorize a new system to use them; you don’t need to change your soak rules or your dice pools. You just grab a card, read the line, and suddenly your hero is negotiating with a fortune-teller dressed like a vending machine that ran out of change. It is equal parts flavor and rocket fuel.

For fans who love a good location hook, Fortune Row is a delightful engine. The deck contains both cue cards and situation cards, which means you can toss one down to launch a scene or to escalate a crisis you already set in motion at the table. It’s not just about getting a power; it’s about getting a moment. And in City of Mist, moments are currency.

## The mechanics behind the magic: how Power Cards work with Mythos and Logos

City of Mist rests on a dual-layer framework: Mythos for the superheroic, mythic, and magical vibes; Logos for the noir, investigative, and rational counterweight. Power Cards dovetail with both strands by providing tangible prompts that map onto both layers without forcing a rules overhaul.

### Card anatomy: what a Power Card actually does
Each card in the Fortune Row deck is built around a simple triangle:
- Trigger: when you can either influence a scene or rewrite a moment
- Effect: a short, cinematic payoff that doesn’t require a full scene rewrite
- Twist: a prompt that moves the story in an unexpected direction
The result is a deck that functions like a narrative propulsion system. You don’t use a card just to gain power; you use a card to recenter the scene around a new emotional or thematic anchor. It’s a mechanical shorthand for a moment that your players would otherwise have to produce with complicated negotiations or flashy set-pieces.

Mechanically, you don’t need to drop a die-for-power; you’ll typically resolve a card with a quick narrative beat and maybe a single roll to determine success. The intent is to keep the pace cinematic and the vibe cool, not to wobble into the worst part of a rules-light game where the table spends twenty minutes arguing about a modifier. Fortune Row respects the rhythm of City of Mist and preserves the drama, even when you fling a fortune at the table and watch the room light up.

### Power Curve and pacing: when to drop a card and when to hold
A hundred Pirates of Fortune Row could claim the deck is hyper-optimized; a more grounded read might say the deck is best used as a deliberate pacing tool. We found that using one or two cards per act gives you a sense of progression without derailing session goals. If you’re doing a heist arc, cards about distractions, misdirection, and bargaining add a layer of texture that you’d otherwise need to get through roleplay hours. If you’re running a mystery, cards that encourage confession, leverage, or misinterpretation can flip the entire deduction arc in a single scene.

That said, Fortune Row isn’t a replacement for good planning. The best sessions still need a strong villain, a motif, and a city map you can point to when the table asks, is this the right tool for the moment? The deck answers that question with a card for almost any mood, but you still pick the mood. The key is to preselect a handful of cards that align with your arc and then stash the rest for mid-session chaos when the crew senses a twist coming and demands something better than a mere fight scene.

## Integrating Fortune Row into your City of Mist table: hooks, pacing, and practical playguides

If you’re planning to adopt Fortune Row in a weekly game, here are some practical tips to weave the deck into your ongoing campaign without turning your table into a card table of chaos.

### Campaign hooks and arc integration
Fortune Row shines when you use it to seed or escalate an arc rather than replace it. Start with a single Fortune Row card as a recurring motif, such as a mysterious benefactor who keeps offering deals, or a recurring symbol that the party encounters in different contexts. Let the card’s twist echo in later scenes to build a cohesive narrative thread. You can also assign a consent-based card draw for sessions with new players to give the newcomers an anchor in the session quickly.

For a more structured approach, pair a Fortune Row card with a location-based arc. For instance, a city district known for fortune tellers and underground vaults can become a living character once your deck starts nudging the plot toward a choice between wealth and safety. The deck acts as a metronome, reminding you when the story has reached a natural pivot point and it’s time to introduce a new focal point for the players.

### Session pacing and card cadence
The deck is best used in bursts rather than as an every-scene tool. A good cadence is one fortune card per act, with optional extra pulls in climactic moments. If you’re running a heist, a mid-act card can sway a heist plan into a risky, cinematic turn that rewards careful planning while rewarding bold improvisation. If you’re running a mystery, use a card to force the players to reinterpret an earlier clue or to discover an unexpected ally in a place they didn’t expect.

If your table tends to sprint through chapters, you can slow things down with Fortune Row by asking players to describe how their character would respond to the card’s twist in cinematic terms. The card becomes a prompt for style rather than a get-out-of-jail-free card. The result is a more memorable scene that lingers like a good line of dialogue after the credits roll.

### The role of GM and players in Fortune Row sessions
The GM retains the ultimate narrative prerogative, but the deck shifts some of the power to the table by offering prompts that are inherently collaborative. Players can propose how the Twist impacts their relationships, and the GM can respond with a quick outcome or a narrative beat. That collaborative friction is precisely the engine that makes City of Mist great. The Fortune Row set emphasizes this by providing prompts that feel both cinematic and narratively integrated with the existing rules.
