## What is a Dhurrie rug
A dhurrie rug is a flat weave, usually from India, with a low pile that keeps it lean and mean for foot traffic. The stripes are a design staple; stripes can be subtle or bold, black and white or rainbow or earthy. The rug in question uses cotton and wool, which gives both durability and warmth. Handwoven means each stripe may have its own tiny mismatches, a feature not a flaw.

## Design and aesthetic
In this rug, the stripes run lengthwise; color palette leans on natural tones, perhaps ivory, taupe, and charcoal, with a few accent shades along the stripes. The design suits boho, modern farmhouse, and even urban minimalist spaces that want texture without shouting. This is where the geek in Geeknite does a little happy dance. Handwoven means each piece is created on a loom, possibly by a artisan whose name is on the loom ledger somewhere. The gap between weft and warp lines reveals the pattern; the craftsmanship is the product of time and care, not rushed manufacturing.

## Practicalities: size, weight, and care

### Size and room planning
Rugs like this typically come in several sizes, often around 4x6 feet, 5x8 feet, and sometimes larger for big rooms. The Stripe design can scale nicely, but the size selection affects how the pattern reads in space. A small 4x6 rug under a coffee table creates a cozy rope of color; a large 8x10 can anchor the seating area like a bold coordinate in a fashion show.

### Weight and placement
Handwoven rugs with cotton wool blends can be heavier than flat machine-made rugs of the same size. You may need two hands, a friend, or a tiny forklift. Nah, just a heavy-duty rug pad and a little elbow grease.

### Cleaning and maintenance
The best approach is frequent but gentle vacuuming; use a vacuum with a soft beater bar to avoid pulling threads. For spills, act fast: blot, do not rub, and use a mild detergent with water. For stubborn stains, test a small area first. Avoid direct sunlight for extended periods to minimize color fade, though natural light is nice for the mood.

### Care schedule and longevity
A well cared for handwoven dhurrie rug can last decades if protected from heavy foot traffic and moisture. Rotate occasionally to avoid uneven wear.
