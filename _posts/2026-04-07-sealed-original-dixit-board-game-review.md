## What is Dixit?

Dixit is a storytelling game designed around a central paradox: the storyteller wants to conjure a clue that is neither trivially obvious nor impossibly obscure. Other players select a card from their hand that matches the clue, all cards are revealed, players secretly vote for which card they think belongs to the storyteller, points are awarded, and then new storyteller is chosen.

The joy is in the narrative freedom: you can reference a memory, a vibe, a color, a scene, or a completely invented concept. The art on each card is intentionally abstract—think dream logic meets a paintbrush with glitter and questionable caffeine.

## How Dixit is played (quick start guide)

If you ve never played Dixit before, here s a brisk run-through so you can jump into a session without consulting the rulebook during the after-party playlist.

- Setup: Each player selects a character token and takes a set of voting tokens (numbers 1 through 6). The deck is shuffled, and each player receives six cards.
- The storyteller s clue: On each round, one player becomes the storyteller. They choose a card from their hand and give a clue that relates to the image. The clue should be clever, evocative, and just a hair deceptive.
- Card submission: The other players pick a card from their own hand that best matches the storyteller s clue and place the chosen card face-down on the table.
- Revelation and voting: All chosen cards are revealed face-up. The storyteller reveals their card and players secretly vote on which card belongs to the storyteller.
- Scoring: Points are awarded for correct guesses and for clever clues. The storyteller earns points if at least one other player guesses their card, but not everyone does. Other players score for guessing the storyteller s card and for catching deception.
- Next round: The role of storyteller passes to the next player.

## Large groups and practical play tips

3-6 is the sweet spot, but you can push to 7-8 with the right mindset. Here are a few practical tips:

- Shorten clues in larger groups to avoid dragging the round out.
- Rotate storytellers quickly to keep attention high and the laughter from dwindling.
- Use a parallel voting system for very large groups to speed things up and keep energy high.
- Encourage gentle humor the aim is to bring people together not to invest in mean spirited jokes.

## Flavor, time, and vibe: how long does a game last?

A typical Dixit round clocks in around 15-20 minutes, depending on the number of players and how long your group spends debating clues. A full session of 4-6 rounds might run 60-90 minutes, but it can stretch to two hours if your players keep inventing new inside jokes and the storytelling becomes a performance piece.

If you re scheduling a family game night, plan for a couple of rounds with one break to snack and discuss the meaning of a card you never will understand.
