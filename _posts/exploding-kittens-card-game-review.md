## What is Exploding Kittens?

Exploding Kittens is a party card game designed for people who are into kittens and explosions and laser beams and sometimes goats. Created by Elan Lee, Matthew Inman (of The Oatmeal), and Shane Small, this game has taken the tabletop world by storm with its quirky humor and easy-to-learn mechanics.

### The Basics

In Exploding Kittens, players draw cards until someone draws an Exploding Kitten card, which means they explode and are out of the gameâ€”unless they have a Defuse card. The deck is loaded with action cards that let you peak at cards before drawing, force your opponents to draw multiple cards, and more. The goal is to be the last player standing.

### Components

The game includes:
- **56 Cards**: Each with its unique and hilarious illustrations by Matthew Inman.
- **Instructions**: Simple and straightforward, just how we like it.

**Pro Tip**: Keep your cards away from actual kittens. They are not fans of their illustrated counterparts.

## How to Play

### Setup

Setting up Exploding Kittens is a breeze. Shuffle the deck, deal 7 cards to each player, add a Defuse card to each player's hand, then shuffle the Exploding Kittens and the remaining Defuse cards into the draw pile. Boom. You're ready to play.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, a player can play as many cards as they like before drawing a single card from the draw pile. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you must defuse it, or you're out of the game.
- **Defuse Cards**: These can save you from exploding.
- **Nope Cards**: Cancel any action except Exploding Kittens and Defuse.
- **Attack Cards**: Force the next player to take two turns.
- **Skip Cards**: End your turn without drawing a card.
- **See the Future Cards**: Peek at the top three cards in the draw pile.
- **Shuffle Cards**: Shuffle the draw pile.

### Winning

The last player left un-exploded wins the game. It's as simple as that.
