## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
A common pitfall of home poker gear is poor maintenance. The good news is that these sets are generally user-friendly when it comes to care:

- Keep the chips out of direct sunlight to prevent fading of the numbers and colors.
- Wipe chips with a soft, dry cloth to remove fingerprints and oils; avoid harsh cleaners that could degrade the paint or numbers.
- Card maintenance is straightforward: store decks in a dry place, avoid bending the cards, and replace them if edges start to fray.
- For the case, check the latches and hinges periodically. A light oiling of metal hinges (sparingly) can extend life, but avoid overdoing it.

If you do this, your kit should endure dozens of game nights without showing signs of fatigue. The “new set” luster tends to fade with heavy use, but the core materials can last a long time with proper care.
