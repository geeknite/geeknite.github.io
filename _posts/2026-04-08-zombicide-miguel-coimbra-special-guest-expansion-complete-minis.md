## Painting and display considerations

If you’re anything like me, the painting chair is the most important seat in the house. Coimbra’s minis are exceedingly paintable, with surface areas that respond well to shading and dry brushing. A few quick tips:

- Undercoat in black or gray to maximize contrast. The fur, leather, and cloth textures will pop once you lay down mid-to-dark tones.
- Use a contrast wash for skin tones to bring out cheekbone and jawline definition without drowning the face in pigment.
- Dry brush metallics on weapons to achieve a weathered, battle-scarred look. Silver or gunmetal on edges will catch light in a way that makes the minis feel alive on the board.
- Edge highlight the most dramatic ridges of the cloak or armor to emphasize the dynamic lines Coimbra built into the sculpt.
- Seal with a matte finish to prevent chalky residue and to protect the figure from sticky fingers after the second or third round of coffee and dice rolling.

Display-wise, these minis photograph particularly well. The high-contrast shading that Coimbra’s style invites translates nicely to museum-grade display photos. If you’re into painting competitions or simply want to show off your team on social media, you’ll get an extra level of “oh wow” with these pieces.

## Practical tips for your first game with Coimbra’s crew

- Decide on a “lead” survivor to mitigate the chaos of many new figures entering play simultaneously. This helps you track who has what card and limits the cognitive load during critical turns.
- Use a consistent painting scheme for the Coimbra minis so you can recognize allies and enemies at a glance on the table. A simple two-tone scheme with a bold accent color makes it easier to read the battlefield quickly.
- Consider rotating who paints which mini on your team. It’s a nice bonding exercise and also spreads the paint-slinging workload so no one gets burnt out by a single color palette.
- If you’re using magnets for bases, label the base with the mini’s name and role. It sounds nerdy, but it prevents mid-game confusion during those “who’s who again?” moments.
