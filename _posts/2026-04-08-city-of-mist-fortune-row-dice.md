Let us talk about playability and readability, because those are the two critical axes for any dice release worth its neon signage.

The Fortune Row dice excel in dim rooms. The pips are sufficiently large and well contrasted against the dice color, which reduces the chances of misreading a roll during an important scene. This is not a lighting demo, but a very real usability improvement for someone who frequently narrates a city-wide conspiracy while the lights flicker. If you run in a dungeon-like basement with a single lamp and a cat who knows your dice’s every move, these dice are friendlier than most.

## Practical Tips for GMs and Players
Here are a few practical ideas to get the most out of your Fortune Row set without turning your session into a fashion show for dice lovers.

- Use the colorway to cue narrative beats. For example, during a tense stakeout, switch to a subset of dice that visually reflect the mood you want to convey. It helps players enter the scene without constant exposition.
- Tie fortune tokens to dice outcomes. If a roll goes exceptionally well or badly, have the fortune token reflect that moment in a tangible way within the scene. This keeps the physical dice connected to in-game consequences.
- Reserve a dedicated dice tray or mat. The tactile ritual of placing a die in a tray before you roll deepens immersion and reduces table chaos when multiple players are rolling simultaneously.
- Consider a house rule for critical outcomes. The Fortune Row vibe fits well with a cinematic, fate-heavy approach. You might allow a crit to unlock a minor narrative twist that advances the current plot thread, rather than just a mechanical advantage.
