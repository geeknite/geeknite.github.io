## Safety and ASTM Approval
ASTM approval is a good indicator that a product meets recognized safety standards for residential trampolines. The SkyBound model we tested carries the label that it conforms to safety guidelines for family use. That said: safety is not a one-and-done checkbox. You still need to supervise kids, restrict one person using one trampoline at a time (if you want to maximize control over the bounciness), and regularly inspect:

- The frame for bent or cracked pieces
- The springs for rust, cracks, or missing hooks
- The mat for tears or stretched areas
- The safety net for rips and broken zippers
- The padding around the springs for wear and pinch points

## Setup and Assembly
Assembly is the part nobody loves, except perhaps the person who likes building Lego bunkers. SkyBound provides a straightforward kit with clear instructions and labeled parts. Expect a 45–75 minute build for a typical household with two adults present, more if you have a toddler who wants to “help by rearranging bolts” in a manner reminiscent of a tiny gremlin. The key steps:

- Lay out the frame and legs
- Attach the springs to the steel frame and mat
- Raise the enclosure netting and connect the posts
- Attach padding and ensure the zipper is aligned with the entry
- Test bounce with a slow approach to ensure everything is snug

A pro tip we learned from our builders’ guide: keep track of small washers and spacers; they tend to disappear with alarming efficiency. Also, wear gloves to avoid pinching your fingers, and don’t attempt assembly on windy days (the net tries to fly away like a sail).

## Durability, Maintenance, and Weather
Durability is where the SkyBound 12FT earns points. The steel frame is robust, the springs show little signs of fatigue after regular workouts, and the jumping mat holds up to years of energetic play. The padding around the edge protects springs and toes, and the net is designed to resist tears from accidental contact with elbows, backpacks, or a wayward dog.

Maintenance tips we recommend:

- Clean the mat and frame with a mild soap solution and a soft cloth to avoid corrosion
- Inspect springs every few months for rust or stiffness
- Check the net and zipper for wear and tear
- Bring the trampoline under cover during heavy winter months to protect from snow load and ice

Weather can be harsh on any outdoor equipment. In our tests, keeping the unit sheltered when not in use extended its life significantly. If you have a backyard with high winds or frequent storms, consider securing anchors so the unit doesn’t take flight on a breezy afternoon.
