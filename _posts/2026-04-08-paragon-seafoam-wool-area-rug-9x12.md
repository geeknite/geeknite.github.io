### Foot traffic and resilience

For households that host the occasional game night or gather for podcast sessions, the wool rug holds up well under shoes and socks alike. Wool fibers spring back after foot traffic, keeping the pile looking plush rather than matted. It’s not a miracle fiber; heavy chairs and rug corner peels may require occasional adjustment, but you will notice fewer flattening signs compared to lower-denier synthetics. If you anticipate heavy traffic from large families or multiple pets, you may want to consider pairing the rug with a non-slip underlay to minimize sliding and edge curling.

### Stain resistance and spills

Spills happen. The seafoam rug responds better to quick, blot-style cleanup than to a full-blown science experiment. Blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing aggressively, and use a mild detergent solution if needed. Wool fiber can imbibe moisture, so speed matters. For stubborn stains, professional cleaning is advisable, especially if you want to preserve the wool’s natural luster and tone. Regular vacuuming with a soft attachment helps prevent dirt and grit from embedding into the pile, which is the true enemy of any rug’s life expectancy.

## Care and Maintenance

### Routine care

- Vacuum weekly using a suction-only setting; avoid beater bars that could snag fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color distribution, particularly in rooms with strong sun exposure or high traffic.
- Use a rug pad or underlay to reduce slippage and add a layer of cushion that steadies the furniture and your posture during long Netflix marathons.

### Cleaning tips

- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth; never rub, as rubbing can push stains deeper into fibers.
- For delicate or persistent stains, consider a wool-safe cleaner and test on a hidden corner first.
- Professional cleaning is recommended every 12-24 months, depending on usage and household conditions.

### Odor and sunlight exposure

Wool wears well with age, but it can develop an odor if left damp for too long. Good ventilation and timely drying help. Sunlight can fade wool fibers over time, especially if the rug sits in a south-facing window. If fading becomes noticeable, you can rotate the rug to balance color exposure or pair it with window treatments to limit direct sunlight without blocking daylight benefits.

### Underlay and padding

A rug pad is not optional; it is a crucial accessory. The pad adds grip, cushions footsteps, and protects the rug’s underside from wear. A quality pad keeps the rug in place, helps with acoustics by dampening footstep noise, and offer a small anti-slip margin that reduces slip hazards. ### Accessibility and safety

For households with kids or older adults, consider edge protection and anti-slip underlays to reduce falls. A properly chosen rug pad minimizes curling and tripping hazards and supports the rug’s longevity by distributing weight more evenly across the surface.
