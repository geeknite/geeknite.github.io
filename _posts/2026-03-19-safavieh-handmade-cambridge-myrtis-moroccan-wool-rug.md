## Comfort, Feel, and Everyday Use
### Foot comfort and thermal balance
Feet rejoice when stepping onto a wool rug like this. Wool fibers inherently provide warmth in cooler temperatures and stay breathable enough for year-round use, which is particularly nice if your space fluctuates between chilly evenings and hot summers. In the winter, it feels like a small planetary core underneath your feet; in the summer, it maintains some subdued coolness without turning your floor into a cold sterile surface.

### Pets, kids, and the inevitable spills
Let’s be honest: spills happen. The Myrtis’ wool is relatively forgiving to common stains, and the natural properties of wool can resist staining when promptly treated. The key is quick action—blot, don’t rub—and a gentle cleaning approach. For persistent stains, test a small hidden area first, then consider an appropriate wool-safe cleaner. If you’ve got curious pets, the rug will likely hold up to their claws, given proper care and a dog that doesn’t use it as a scratching post (but loyal cats might decide it’s a new sunbeam throne, and that’s a risk you accept).

### Care routine that won’t ruin your weekend plans
- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper.
- For deeper cleaning, use a wool-safe cleaner or call a pro for spot-cleaning on stubborn marks.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and avoid UV-related fading in one spot.
- Use a quality rug pad to extend life and keep edges flat.

If you’re into nerdy optimization, think of rug care as a firmware update for your living space: small, regular updates keep everything running smoothly and extend the life of your investment.
