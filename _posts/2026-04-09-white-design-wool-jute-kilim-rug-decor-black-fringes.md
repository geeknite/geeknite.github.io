## Maintenance and Care: How to Keep It Looking Fresh
Wool and jute require slightly different care, but you can give them both a long life with a simple routine:

- Vacuum Regularly: Use a vacuum with a gentle setting and a brushless attachment to avoid pilling the wool. Do not use a beater bar on high power. Think of it as dental hygiene for your rug—gentle strokes, no scrubbing the enamel.
- Spot Clean Promptly: Wipe spills with a clean, absorbent cloth. Blot from the outside in to avoid spreading. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution can work; test in an inconspicuous corner first.
- Avoid Excess Moisture: Jute can react poorly to prolonged moisture. If you live in a humid climate, consider a rug pad that provides some airflow and reduces damp pockets under the rug.
- Rotate Periodically: To prevent uneven wear, rotate the rug every few months. This also helps it balance the sun exposure that may color the white fibers over time.
- Professional Cleaning: For larger stain events or a general refresh, a professional cleaning service familiar with wool and natural fiber rugs is a good call. They can handle the delicate balance of cleaning without soaking the fibers into a soggy sci-fi puddle.

## Care Scanner: Quick Cleaning Routine for Busy People
Here is a practical, no-nonsense cleaning schedule you can actually keep:
1. Vacuum weekly on a low setting to remove loose dirt.
2. Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing which can push the stain deeper into the fibers.
3. Treat noticeable stains with a mild detergent solution once a month if needed.
4. Rotate the rug every 2–3 months to even out wear and fade.
5. Have a professional clean every 12–18 months, depending on usage and pets.
