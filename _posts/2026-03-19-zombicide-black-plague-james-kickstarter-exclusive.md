## How the game plays: core mechanics and flow
Zombicide is not a heavy wargame; it s a narrative zombie-slaying fest where you decide which action to take on your turn. The core mechanic is simple enough to teach in a coffee shop during a rainstorm, yet deep enough to sustain long campaigns when you start to optimize your survivors and weapon combos. The Kickstarter Exclusive preserves this core flow while adding more flavor and more choices.

### The action point system
Each survivor has a certain number of action points per turn. You spend them to move, search, attack, or interact with the environment. The flexibility means pretty much every turn feels like a small chess match with undead pawns. The challenge is balancing risk and reward: do you sprint ahead to draw fire or stay back to man the doors and shoot like a god of the crossbow? The more players you have, the more conversations you ll have about who should commit to a trap or a risky dash into a hallway crowded with melee threats.

### Combat resolution and dice luck
The dice pool introduces a familiar risk-reward loop: you might roll well enough to clear a horde, or you might roll so poorly that you are forced to improvise. The exclusives can tilt the dice pool toward certain classes or weapons, which can be a fun way to lean into a particular playstyle. If you re the survivalist who loves long-range poke damage, you can try to exploit the archers and crossbow wielders that are often part of the exclusive lineup. If you prefer front-line brawn, the knights will look mighty delicious on the board when they wade into a horde and cleave through several enemies in a single turn.

Co-op games tend to shine with a large group; with five or six players, you get the most chaotic, hilarious, and tense moments when everyone tries to coordinate their moves in a living breathing panic.
