Setup and Play: A Quick Walkthrough
-----------------------------------
Codenames is a team-based word game that thrives on communication, miscommunication, and the occasional outrageous leap of logic. Here’s a quick guide to the original experience when you crack open that sealed treasure:
- Players split into two teams: red and blue. Each team appoints a spymaster who will give one-word clues to help teammates guess their words on the board.
- There are 25 codename cards laid out on the table in a 5x5 grid. Each card shows a single word. Some words are red, some blue, some neutral, and one is an assassin—the word you absolutely do not want to guess. Yes, the stakes are existential, at least for the table’s morale.
- The spymaster gives a clue word and a number. The clue must relate to one or more of the target words for their team. The number tells how many words on the board relate to that clue. For example, a clue like quick 2 might point to words like hare and sprint if those happen to be on the board.
- Teammates then discuss and guess. They flip the codename cards as they think they’ve identified their team’s words. The round continues until all team words are claimed, or someone accidentally taps the assassin and ends the round with a dramatic gasp.
- The game continues with each team taking turns, until one side flushes all its words from the board. Victory tastes like a hot cup of congratulatory cocoa, unless you mistook a clue for a checkout line and guessed the assassin instead—then it tastes like heartbreak with a hint of regret.

The core thrill here is not the complexity but the social pressure of making a single word do heavy lifting for your entire team. Your spymaster can be a genius of metaphor or a pun-obsessed chaos engine. The rest of the team will either click with perfect synergy or realize that you’re all basically two steps away from chaos and a tasteless dad joke.

Tips and Strategies for Winning More Often
-------------------------------------------
Spymaster tips:
- Think in extremes. A clue that spills into multiple target words is riskier but can pay off; a safe clue may waste turns if your team overthinks it.
- Use nuanced connections. Word pairs that share a hidden relationship can be genius if your team grasps the leap, but beware of cross-team misinterpretations.
- Don’t over-clue. A guess-limit won’t disincentivize risk-taking; it’s better to reward bold plays than anchor your team with cautious guidance.

Guessers tips:
- Listen for the pattern. If teammates keep returning to a common theme, you’re probably on the right track.
- Start with the obvious. If two cards scream at you with a shared thread, your team might have the connection the spymaster had in mind.
- Be mindful of the assassin. A misread clue can end a round in spectacular fashion—plan your guesses with a little caution alongside ambition.

Hosting a Codenames Night: Best Practices
-----------------------------------------
- Clear space: a 5x5 grid fits nicely on a large table. If your table is a coffee table, you might want to rearrange the seating so that everyone can reach cards without elbows from a neighbor.
- Talk time: encourage enthusiastic, polite banter. The best nights are those where you’re not just guessing words but also roasting your friends for their connections.
- Timing: you don’t need a hero timer, but a gentle reminder helps keep the pace lively. A five-minute timer can work well for quick rounds and prevent the game from dragging into the neighbor’s dinner.
- Card care: post-game, shuffle the deck, return it to the box, and seal it again. There’s nothing sadder than opening a sealed copy to find a creased card due to careless shipping or someone treating the deck like a stage prop.
