## The Basics of Phase 10

Phase 10 is a rummy-type card game designed for 2-6 players. The objective is to complete 10 different phases, each with its unique set of requirements. Think of it as a marathon of mini-games, where each phase presents a new challenge. The first player to complete all 10 phases wins the game. Simple, right? Well, not so fast!

### Components

The game includes:

- A deck of Phase 10 cards, numbered 1-12 in four colors (red, blue, green, yellow)
- Skip cards: Use these to drive your opponents mad by making them lose a turn.
- Wild cards: These can be used as any number or color, helping you complete those tricky phases.
- Instruction booklet: For when you inevitably argue over the rules.

## Gameplay

Phase 10 is played over a series of rounds. Each round, players aim to complete the current phase by forming sets or runs of cards. Here's a brief rundown of how a typical round goes:

### Turn Phases

1. **Draw a Card**: Pick a card from the draw pile or the discard pile. Choose wisely!
2. **Play Your Cards**: If you can complete your phase, lay down your cards. Otherwise, keep collecting.
3. **Discard**: End your turn by discarding a card. Pro tip: Don't discard a card your opponent needs unless you're into sabotage.

### Winning and Losing

To win, be the first player to complete all 10 phases. If the draw pile runs out before anyone completes Phase 10, the player with the most phases completed wins. It's a race against time and your opponents!

**Pro Tip**: Keep an eye on your opponents' cards and try to guess what they need. It's all fun and games until someone uses a Skip card on you.
