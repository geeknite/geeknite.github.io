## Maintenance and Preservation: Keeping 12 Treasures in Pristine Form
Preservation is the boring but essential part of collecting. The good news is that these decks aren’t fragile, but neglect will take a toll. Here are the practical tips you’ll actually use:

- Avoid extreme heat and moisture. Cards hate humidity and heat; they warp, and the tuck cases lose their snap. A constant 40-60% humidity range in a display cabinet is ideal if you’re serious about preserving the foil accents and the fidelity of the print.
- Keep them sealed until you want to handle them. It’s the oldest trick in the collector’s playbook: preserve the value by maintaining closure. If you plan to display, consider a light dust-free case that protects from accidental fingerprints and stray cat hairs.
- Temperature cycling is bad. Don’t keep them in a car trunk or a sunlit shelf that warms up and cools down. The stock and finish like stable across typical room conditions; frequent cycling can lead to warping and edge wear that reduces the pristine status of the sealed decks.
- Handling: use clean hands or white gloves if you’re going for display-only. If you’re actually unsealing for use, note that the seal itself is valuable as a collector’s asset; weigh the decision carefully.

Remember, the goal is to enjoy the set while preserving its long-term value. It’s not a race to open them, but if you’re planning to perform, you’ll eventually want to sample a few decks to keep the magic alive and kicking.
