### H3: Color origins and perception
Natural fancy blue-green diamonds owe their tint to minute amounts of boron (blue) and the absence of nitrogen (which would neutralize the blue) combined with other lattice imperfections that reflect light in unusual ways. ## H2: GIA certification: translating science into trust
### H3: What the certificate covers for a fancy color piece
For a fancy blue green diamond, the GIA certificate covers:
- Carat weight: the stone’s mass, usually to two decimal places.
- Color grade: a designation range that for fancy colors emphasizes hue, tone, and saturation rather than the traditional color scale used for colorless stones.
- Clarity: the presence or absence of internal inclusions and blemishes, described in a way that helps assess durability and light performance.
- Cut: the geometry and proportion that influence light return and color display.
- Floral or fancy color descriptors and any fluorescence if applicable.
- Overall assessment of origin and treatment, if any.
