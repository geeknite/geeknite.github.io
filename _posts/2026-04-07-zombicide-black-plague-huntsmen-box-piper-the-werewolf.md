## Piper the Werewolf: Design and Mechanics
Piper the Werewolf is the star of this show, and not just because werewolves are inherently dramatic. Piper isn’t a mere reskin of a standard zombie boss; this model is designed to be a strategic wrinkle in your campaign. Here are the standout design choices and how they feel in actual play:

- Theme and personality: Piper isn’t a mindless “clobber the survivors” boss. Piper represents a predator who uses terrain and timing to his advantage, howling to call in extra pressure at moments you thought you had a moment to breathe. It’s a narrative beat that adds tension and gives players a reason to coordinate instead of treating the game like a series of solo dice rolls.
- Unique ability kit: Piper brings a howl mechanic that triggers a temporary “pack” presence, adding extra activity on the board—think of it as a tide of wolves that you didn’t invite, but now have to manage. The timing is everything: mishandled pulses can lead to a cascade of casualties or an improbable rescue if your team can chain-litigate the next round.
- Interaction with Huntsmen: The Huntsmen themselves are designed to be more agile and mobile than standard zombies, bringing a hunter’s cunning to the table. When you pair Piper with Huntsmen, you get a layered challenge: you must cut off the boss’s pack before focusing fire, but if you allocate too much to one side, the other grows dangerously efficient in their own right.
- Visual storytelling: The model of Piper is designed to be intimidating at display scale. The werewolf’s posture, the way the fur flows under the lighting on your table, and the dynamic base design all contribute to a sense that this is not your garden-variety monster. If you’re into painting, Piper is a rewarding canvas—lots of sharp edges for edge highlighting, a clear silhouette, and a silhouette that tells a story even when you’re not actively fighting.

The net effect is a boss that feels like an actual boss: not just a higher number in a stat sheet, but a narrative obstacle that changes how you approach every round. Piper’s howls demand repositioning, timing, and a bit of risk management: you’ll be choosing between capturing the line of sight on a single survivor and blocking the glyph-laden retreat path—knowing that the next beat may pull in a new wave of threats.

## Strategy and Tactics: What Works and What Doesn’t
No review would be complete without practical advice you can actually apply next game night. Here are some tips to maximize your chances when Piper the Werewolf and the Huntsmen sit down at your table:

- Don’t ignore the howl. Expect the pack to arrive just after Piper’s activation, giving you one or two rounds of potentially overwhelming pressure. Position to minimize reactive disaster—set up choke points, place ranged units at advantageous sightlines, and keep at least one survivor ready to cross a danger threshold when the wolves arrive.
- Focus fire on the pack, not just the boss. The Werewolf is dangerous, but the packs can overwhelm you if left unchecked. Utilize traps and terrain to slow the pack’s advance, then concentrate fire to minimize the multi-front assault.
- Use hunters for support, not purely as damage dealers. Huntsmen can be excellent at controlling space, drawing moves that slow the pack while you set up the artillery or heavy hitters for a decisive strike. They’re not just “extra lives” in a bag; they’re tactical tools that can turn a high-pressure round into a controlled one.
- Manage morale and retreat routes. Piper’s presence increases the situational stakes. If a survivor is cornered, consider retreating to a favorable position rather than fielding a foolish assault that ends the round with several casualties.
- Paint and upgrade gradually. If you’re the painting type, the difference in visual clarity between a well-painted Piper and a bare lead miniature will translate into a psychological advantage on the table. It’s not just about looking cool; it’s about feeling confident in your tactical decisions when the table asks, “Are you sure you want to gamble with that corner?”

Remember: the real value of Piper and the Huntsmen lies in the way they force you to rethink your standard approach. It’s not simply “kill more zombies”; it’s “outthink the pack, outmaneuver the boss, and use every inch of the board like a chessboard in a thunderstorm.”
