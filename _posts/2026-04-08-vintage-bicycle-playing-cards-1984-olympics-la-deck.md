## Design Timelines and Authenticity: How This Set Came to be
A big part of collecting is provenance. The 1984 Olympics, hosted by Los Angeles, saw a lot of officially licensed memorabilia flood the market. The Bicycle brand, known for licensing popular events and producing period-appropriate thank-you gifts for fans, released a run of Olympic-themed decks to commemorate the Games. As with many vintage licensure efforts of the era, there were different tiers of packaging and licensing across regions and retailers. Finding a deck with authentic branding, legible print marks, and a genuine stock stamp is part of the thrill. If you’re shopping today, here are a few quick authentication steps:

- Check the box for official licensing marks and the correct city and year labeling (LA 1984). Some counterfeit decks will mimic the look but skip the license text or spray a different shade of red or blue on the back.
- Inspect the stock color and edge marks. Vintage Bicycle decks often show a uniform edge finish with tiny printer marks along the heel. If the deck feels unusually warped or the back pattern is misaligned, set it aside for a closer look.
- Examine the jokers and any extra cards for the same print quality as the face cards. Inconsistent margins or font weight is a common giveaway of a counterfeit.
- If a seller claims this is a “limited edition” or “rare variant,” request clear photos of the box, the seal, and any foil or embossing. Real limited runs tend to show uniform foil placement and consistent embossing across units.

## How to Care for Your Vintage Deck
Caring for a collectible deck requires a little extra TLC, especially if you intend to display one of the two main colors. Here are practical tips to keep your 1984 Olympics Bicycle deck in mint-ish condition:

- Store away from direct sunlight. UV exposure can fade the red and blue back designs over time.
- Temperature and humidity control matter. A stable environment reduces warping and edge curling.
- Use airtight display sleeves when you showcase them. If you plan to handle the cards, wash hands or wear clean cotton gloves to minimize oils.
- If you’re into DIY preservation, consider archival-quality tissue and a shallow, acid-free box. Avoid adhesive tapes that could react with the stock.
