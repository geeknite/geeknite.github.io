## What is OverPower Anyway?

OverPower is a Marvel-themed collectible card game produced by Fleer in the mid-1990s. It was designed to bring the cinematic scale of Marvel into a two-player tactical experience that was approachable enough for a kid with a starter deck and brutal enough to satisfy a seasoned gamer looking for a crunchy skirmish. The game leaned into the idea of heroes and masterminds, with battle concepts that encouraged players to build a tableau of strength from a rotating pool of cards. In practice, you’d assemble a squad centered on a heroic concept, counters and plots, and a little bit of luck from the card draw. If that sounds a bit like Magic: The Gathering, you’re not wrong, but OverPower had its own vibe—one that blended bright 90s art, license-approved characters, and a sense of speed that felt a touch more arcade than a chess match in a library.

The twelve starter decks in the sealed box were designed to give players immediate access to a handful of archetypes. Think of them as the first acts of a Marvel movie: each deck had a theme, a set of characters, and a plan for how to win, with enough variety to ensure showdown excitement on a rainy weekend. The excitement of unsealing a fresh deck, the possibility of pulling a rare foil, and the fantasy of piloting your favorite hero are all part of the nostalgic package here.
