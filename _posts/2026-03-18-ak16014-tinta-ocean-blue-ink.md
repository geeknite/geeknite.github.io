## Performance and Behavior in Practice

### Wetness, Flow, and Drying Time

Ocean Blue is not a bottle-chatterer; it’s a polite but not timid guest. On a typical 0.5mm to 0.8mm nib, you get a steady, moderate flow. It writes smoothly and consistently in longer notes and essays, with enough lubrication to feel luxurious without sacrificing reliability. Drying times are paper-dependent, but a reasonable range is about 8–12 seconds on standard copier-weight paper and 4–6 seconds on smoother high-quality notebooks. If you’re speed-writing and you can’t wait for the letter to finish its jazz solo, just a nudge of air will help it dry a tad faster.

### Bleed-Through and Feathering

The ink’s pigment density means you’ll sometimes see light bleed-through on thin stock, particularly with bold lines and generous nibs. Feathering is modest, usually noticeable on very cheap paper. If you’re compiling a field notebook that spends more time in your bike bag than at a desk, consider using a mid-weight paper or test-swatching on your chosen pad before you commit to a long day of journaling. The good news is that the ink does not feather aggressively on normal office- or art-oriented papers; the lines stay crisp, and the color remains consistent from start to finish.

### Water Resistance and Lightfastness

Ocean Blue holds up reasonably well against a light splash and a casual spill, which means if your lunch spilled coffee on your page, you’d still be able to make out most letters after the stain—good for those who write near the coffee machine or who treat their desk as a lab bench. In terms of lightfastness, the ink should resist fading under typical household lighting for a practical writing lifespan. If you’re designing a piece that’s meant to last decades, you might want to keep a scan handy; the physical page will do fine for daily references, but for archival quality, you’ll want the digital backup as well.

### Nib Compatibility and How It Feels to Write

In our tests, Ocean Blue felt delightful across nib sizes. It’s the kind of ink that doesn’t fight you when you push the nib; instead, it cooperates with a smile, leaving you with a sense that your pen is finally in its natural habitat.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

Like any good ink, Ocean Blue benefits from a little maintenance: flush your pen every couple of weeks if you’re writing heavily; use clean water and an optional pen flush to clear pigment build-up; wipe the nib nibs after heavy usage to prevent banding from color residue. If you’re storing pens with ink in them for extended periods, consider a mild flush and store the pen nib-up to avoid a color halo forming on the seal. In short: treat your pens as you would treat your power supply—careful, regular, and not allergic to the occasional quick spray of water to keep it honest.
