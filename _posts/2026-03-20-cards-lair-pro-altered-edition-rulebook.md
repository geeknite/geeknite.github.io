## The rules and structure: a practical guide
This section will walk you through the core elements of the rulebook, what the Altered Edition actually changes, and how to parse the pages without needing a pocket translator or a team of lawyers.

### Core mechanics at a glance
- Card drafting: players assemble a personal deck from a shared pool, selecting options that will influence both offense and defense throughout the game.
- Tempo and bluffing: information is scarce, but the game rewards those who can read micro-behaviors and subtle tells. Expect a mix of poker-like reads and real-time adaptation.
- Altered effects: these are the special rules that apply to cards or phases, flipping expectations in entertaining ways. They can be permanent, temporary, or conditional on a specific event that may or may not happen depending on how bravely you gamble.
- Endgame conditions: the game typically ends when a player achieves a specific victory condition, though Altered Edition adds a couple of alternative end triggers to keep things fresh and precarious.

### Setup, turn order, and round flow
The book outlines a clean setup that takes about 5–8 minutes for an average table. You lay out the central resource pool, distribute personal decks, and seed the table with a handful of tokens that track influence, luck, and the occasional existential crisis—aka the kind of tokens you’d expect in a game about intruding into a dragon’s lair.

Turn order is deterministic but with a chaotic twist: players execute actions in a fixed sequence unless an Altered Effect modifies that order. This is where the magic happens. It creates a slow burn of anticipation that can suddenly erupt into a flurry of steps that feel both familiar and new. The goal is to balance predictive planning with the flexibility to pivot when a card you didn’t anticipate flips the entire board on you.
