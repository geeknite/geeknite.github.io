### House rules and variations you can try
If you want to spice up your session with a Billie-inspired twist, consider the following house rules:
- Billie’s Echo Rule: If a Wild is played, the player must sing a short lyric (clean, family-friendly) before placing the card. This usually slows the game down, but it adds dramatic tension and comedic relief.
- Canvas Chaos: Once per game, a player can trade their entire hand with another player after a Wild is played. This introduces chaos, strategic mischief, and the eternal question of whether you should swap your 2s and 7s or your dreams and your reality.
- Keep the Beat, Not the Heat: If a player is stuck with a hand of all high-value cards, they can request a re-shuffle and redraw two up to three times per game. This keeps the vibe light when the table is about to combust from a streak of bad draws.

## Practical considerations: care, storage, and longevity

Canvas textures and premium finishes demand a bit more care than a generic plastic deck sitting in a junk drawer. Here are some tips to extend this deck’s life:
- Store in a cool, dry place to prevent warping and color fade. Avoid direct sunlight, unless you want your Billie Eilish vibe to glow in the dark like a retro arcade cabinet.
- Use a card sleeve if you are planning to play competitively or loan the deck to your friends who treat games like a physical sport. Sleeves reduce edge wear and keep the art pristine after a few nights of intense Uno drama.
- If you spill soda on the table, do not panic. Wipe gently and allow cards to air dry. The Canvas finish can be finicky with moisture, but a careful drying process will usually restore it to near-pristine condition.
