## Corded vs Cordless: what you can and cannot do with VP59

Corded reality
The VP59 is a corded device by design. It requires power and a network connection to operate. That said, the corded setup is exactly what many office environments crave: predictable reliability, consistent firmware updates, and a stable call experience. If you’re onboarding a team or setting up a new call center node, the VP59’s wired nature is a reassuring baseline. It also means fewer variables in a conference room: you won’t depend on a fragile battery or Bluetooth pairing quirks during a critical client call.

Cordless dreams (with caveats)
Is there any way to go cordless with VP59? Not directly—the VP59 itself doesn’t run on battery power and isn’t a cordless handset. However, you can absolutely add cordless flexibility to your VP59 workflow by pairing compatible wireless headsets or using DECT solutions that your office already supports. Here are some practical approaches:
- Wireless headsets with USB dongles: If the VP59 has a USB port (for headset compatibility), you can pair a wireless headset that uses a USB dongle. This gives you freedom from the desk while preserving the VP59’s high‑quality video and audio pipeline.
- Bluetooth accessories with a dongle: Some wireless peripherals rely on Bluetooth or USB‑BT adapters. If the VP59 ecosystem supports Bluetooth inputs, you can attach a Bluetooth headset for off‑desk calling. Be mindful of possible audio latency and occasional pairing hiccups, especially in environments with a lot of wireless traffic.
- DECT base compatibility: If your office already uses Yealink DECT devices such as a W60P base, you may be able to integrate VP59 into the same ecosystem for a consistent user experience, using the cordless handset base for other devices while the VP59 remains the anchored video phone. In practice, this is a matter of compatibility between your chosen DECT system and the VP59’s own provisioning. Check with your vendor for definitive compatibility guidance.

If cordless flexibility is a top priority, we recommend planning around a headset solution first, then verifying if your DECT environment can play well with a VP59 deployment. The key is to test in a real workspace; the last thing you want is a cordless headache when deadlines are looming.
