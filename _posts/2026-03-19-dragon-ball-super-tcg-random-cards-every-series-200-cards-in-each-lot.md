## What you get in a 200-card lot
200 cards is a lot. Mentally, emotionally, and physically—this is not a sampler, this is a saga. The exact distribution depends on the seller, but in practice you should expect a mix of commons, uncommons, rares, foils, and a handful of chase cards that glow under your desk lamp like Dragon Balls in a cave. The practical breakdown tends to look something like this, give or take a few cards:

### Distribution breakdown (typical, not guaranteed)
- Commons: 90–120 cards — the bread and butter of every DBS format. These are the workhorses that fill your deck-building dreams or nightmares, depending on your luck.
- Uncommons: 40–60 cards — the middle layer, where synergy starts to matter and you realize you could have pulled that perfect combo if only you had one more copy of Card X.
- Rares and Super Rares: 20–30 cards — the this might be a winner pile, with the potential for a foil variant or a misprint that becomes your favorite conversation piece.
- Foil variants: 5–12 cards — shiny foil variants that make you squint at the light and wonder if you should get your eyes checked or replace your countertops with card storage foam.

The unspoken reality: you will own duplicates. A lot of duplicates. It’s not a bug; it’s a feature for folks who want to craft multiple decks around a theme or simply draft with friends. The key is to embrace the duplicates as a chance to experiment with deck-building without hunting down a rare variant for months on end.

## Strategy tips: building decks from a random lot
Here are practical strategies to turn a 200-card lottery into a playable deck or two:

### Build around the best synergy cards first
Identify a few cards that work well with multiple others. These synergy anchors help you anchor your deck’s core strategy. If you’re lucky enough to snag a key searcher or a card that accelerates your energy, you’ve got a foundation to your plan.

### Diversify your energy curves
If your cards span multiple sets, you’ll likely have a variety of energy costs. Build your deck to smooth those curves so you’re not stuck with high-cost cards in the early turns. A balanced energy curve improves consistency and makes the random pool feel like a designed deck rather than a scavenger hunt.

### Include a few tech picks for local meta
Tech picks are those sideboard-like cards that counter a common strategy in your local gaming group. They often come from older sets and can be pulled from lucky foils or rares in a random lot. The trick is to keep them lean, so you don’t end up with a deck that’s only good against one build.

### Deck-building workflow
- Step 1: Sort your 200 cards by rarity and by set.
- Step 2: Identify a core archetype you want to play
- Step 3: Choose 18–22 core cards that support that archetype
- Step 4: Fill out with 6–12 flexible slots for synergy and tech picks
- Step 5: Test and iterate. The more you play, the more you’ll realize which pulls shine in gameplay

## Handling and storage for large pools
Storing 200 cards safely takes a plan. You don’t want to end up with a messy shelf with cards slipping out and curling at the corners. Our practical storage tips include:
- Use card sleeves and a sturdy binder for protection
- Invest in a labeled storage box to separate by series and rarity
- Consider a labeled post-it system for quick reference while sorting by set
- Keep a notepad to track which cards you need and which you’ve pulled in duplicates. This helps reduce duplication headaches later
