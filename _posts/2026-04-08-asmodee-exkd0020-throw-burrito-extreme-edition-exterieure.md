## How to play and the gameplay loop
### Setup and learning curve
Setup is brisk. You lay out the target board mid-table, shuffle the cards, and allocate a burrito to each player. The goal is to accumulate points by hitting targets during your turns and by triggering minigames that push your luck and your friends’ patience. The learning curve is gentle enough for casual players but layered enough to offer a handful of strategies for those who want to optimize their chaos.

During your turn, you pick up a burrito, aim toward a zone on the board, and release with a friendly flick. The soft projectile sails through the air with a satisfying arc, mirroring the drama of the dessert course at a family dinner. Scoring zones grant different points. The higher the zone, the sweeter the bragging rights—or the guilt of draining your friends’ sense of fair play. The penalty cards introduce silly tasks or setbacks that can shift the balance from “we’re all friends here” to “we might need a nap and a snack” before the next round.

### Turn structure and chaos management
The turn order is straightforward, but the chaos comes from the cards and mini-challenges. A typical session moves in waves: throw, dodge, switch to a penalty card, perform a quick cooperative challenge, and then pass the burrito baton. The key to fun here is tempo. If you sprint through every round, you’ll burn out fast. If you crawl with pensive plotting, you’ll lose your chance at dramatic comebacks. A well-paced game lets players enjoy the show—the moment when someone flicks a burrito from the board edge and it sails into the “no-go” zone, triggering a collective howl and a new meme for the group chat.

### Scoring and winning conditions
Points come from successful hits on target zones and from completing certain penalties or mini-games that reward clever, sometimes ridiculous, play. The moment you reach the winning threshold, you’ll hear the cheers, the playful teases, and possibly the neighbor’s curious dog joining the celebration. The exact numbers vary by edition and house rules, but the spirit is consistent: wins taste like victory, and victory tastes like cornmeal with a hint of lime. The real win, of course, is the memories you collectively build while you’re flinging burritos at your friends and their questionable life choices.
