## Yield, cost per page, and long-game economics
The claim “11K pages” sounds magical, like a unicorn that also prints invoices. In real-world terms, page yield depends heavily on the content you’re printing. A typical business document with 5% color coverage (mostly black text with a few yellow highlights) will yield close to the stated figure, while highly colored dashboards or marketing sheets may consume more toner per page. Our rough estimates:

- 5% color coverage: ~10,000–11,000 pages
- 10% color coverage (more color blocks): ~6,500–9,000 pages
- Heavy color images or photo printing: yield can drop significantly

Cost per page is a major calculator for office managers. The high-capacity cartridge often has a higher upfront cost than standard-yield variants, but the per-page cost tends to be lower. ## Durability, storage, and maintenance tips
A cartridge’s lifespan is not just about pages; it’s also about how you treat it when it’s not in the printer. Here are a few practical tips to maximize shelf life and performance:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Heat and humidity can degrade toner consistency over time.
- Keep cartridges sealed until used. The desiccant inside helps prevent moisture absorption, which can affect print quality when you finally install it.
- Do not shake too aggressively during installation. A gentle shake is enough to settle the pigment. Overzealous shakers may cause clumping and other headaches you don’t need.
- If you’re moving a printer housing around, try to keep the cartridge securely seated. This reduces the chance of powder leakage and the inevitable cleanup ritual that follows.

Maintenance-wise, the 11K Yellow doesn’t require extra cleaning rituals beyond standard printer maintenance. A routine internal cleaning cycle, combined with occasional alignment checks, is typically enough to keep things running smoothly. If your printer supports automated calibration, run it after the cartridge swap to ensure the color alignment stays on point.

## How to spot genuine vs counterfeit (and why it matters)
Counterfeit toner is a thing, and it’s not just embarrassing when you discover your “new” cartridge produces streaks that look like a zebra learned to print. Here are a few quick checks:

- Purchase from reputable retailers or official distributors. If the price looks too good to be true, it probably is.
- Inspect packaging and labeling. Genuine cartridges have consistent fonts, logos, and labeling; counterfeit ones often show misaligned text or typos.
- Check the serial number and model code printed on the cartridge. If it doesn’t match the supported models, walk away.
- Run a few test pages and look for consistent color and density. Inconsistent output is a red flag.
