## Mobility and stability

Mobility is where this rack shines. The wheels and optional lockable casters offer just enough mobility for lab reconfigurations without giving your gear a roller coaster ride. In carpeted rooms you may find the base needs a little more weight to prevent wandering; in a tiled shop floor you will be fine with wheels locked while you're connecting devices. ### Handling tips on different floors

- On carpet, use the locking casters and spread weight across the bottom shelf for best stability
- On hard floors, ensure caster locks are engaged before plugging in power cables to avoid any accidental movement

## Cable management and airflow: how open frame helps

Airflow is often the forgotten hero in rack reviews. The StarTech open frame design ensures that air can move around devices more freely than in a closed cabinet where many hot spots pop up. In practice, you’ll want to route power and data cables separately to minimize interference and keep things neat. Here are a few tips:

- Use cable ties or Velcro to bundle data cables together; run power lines on the opposite side when possible to avoid heat coupling
- Keep patch panels accessible and label ports for speed during maintenance
- Avoid burying cables behind shelves; give them room to breathe so airflow isn’t blocked
- If you have a dense setup, consider adding a small, intake-friendly fan or two to help with cooling

## Assembly and setup: step by step

If assembly isn’t your favorite pastime, this rack makes the process approachable. The four-post design simplifies alignment; you’ll populate the rails, slide the shelves, and attach the casters. The process typically involves:

1) Unpack the frame and verify all parts against the parts list.
2) Attach the four rails to the frame and tighten them so there is no wobble.
3) Install the shelves on the rails at your chosen heights.
4) Mount devices on the rails using standard rack screws (the ones included work perfectly).
5) Connect power and data cables, labeling as you go.
6) If you opt for casters, install them on the base and lock as needed.
7) Power up and perform a sanity check on airflow and temperatures.

Pro tip: take your time during the rail installation to avoid cross-threading or misalignment. A little patience now saves many hours of frustration later.
