## Maintenance and care
Maintenance is not glamorous, but it is essential. Stainless steel is forgiving but not invincible. The key maintenance steps for this blade are simple:

- Clean after use with mild soap and water, dry completely, and store in a dry environment. Avoid leaving the blade submerged in water or subjected to prolonged moisture.
- Lightly oil the blade to prevent surface rust and maintain the sheen. A thin coat of mineral oil or a dedicated blade oil works well.
- Sharpen as needed with a ceramic rod or a proper sharpening stone. The edge will respond best to consistent, gentle passes rather than aggressive grinding. Take your time; the goal is a sharp edge, not a meltdown.
- Check the leather sheath for wear and leather stiffness. Conditioning the leather periodically helps maintain its fit and protects the blade from scratches during draw.

We caution against aggressive use of solvents around the leather. If you want to keep the sheath looking fresh, use a leather conditioner; this keeps moisture at bay and helps maintain that vintage look that fans of the classic blade aesthetic crave.
