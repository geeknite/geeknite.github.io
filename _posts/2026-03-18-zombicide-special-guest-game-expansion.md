## What is in the box and what makes it special

The Special Guest expansion introduces a few core ideas that you will see cropping up across this review. First, the notion of a guest who is not your typical survivor but a narrative catalyst. Think of a celebrity zombie who struts onto the board with a special objective that can tilt the odds in dramatic ways. The box contents include:

- A handful of guest miniatures, each with a unique ability tied to their persona. They are not just stat sticks; they come with flavor text that may or may not match their sculpt. The art team clearly had fun with this concept, and you can feel the energy when you open the tray.
- A new guest deck with event and objective cards. These cards twist moral choices, mission conditions, and even the turn order in surprising ways. You may find yourself swapping a standard supply run for a catwalk moment where a guest demands a spotlight and your survivors groan in chorus.
- Additional tiles and indicators that enable new mission layouts and hazard types. The board layout can feel slightly more modular here, which means you get more variety across play sessions without needing an entirely new core map.
- Clean rule insert and a short reference sheet that knocks a minute or two off your setup time. If you have ever looked at a big rulebook and muttered about the luxury of a nap, this help sheet is your friend.

If you open the box and feel a small spark of nostalgia for a TV show where a mysterious guest arrives at a town meeting, you are not alone. The Special Guest design leans into that melodrama while letting the core game mechanics breathe. It does not attempt to reinvent the wheel; it gives the wheel a new coat of paint and a chrome bumper with a neon sign that says Welcome to the Apocalypse, Baby.

## How the new mechanics feel in play

A lot of expansions add a handful of new rules, and you blink at the page until your brain processes the gist. Special Guest is one of those expansions that introduces a few teeth into the system without turning the jaw into a complicated hinge. The standout mechanics are:

- Guest abilities that scale with the phase. The guests often come with one or two perks that trigger during specific turns. This design choice ensures you feel their influence without stalling the action. It is the kind of mechanic that makes you plan ahead but not to the point of micromanagement obsession.
- Narrative-driven objectives. The guest cards create short narrative arcs within the mission. You might encounter a scenario such as a broadcast that must be interrupted to prevent a horde from gathering, or a guest who demands an escort to a safe zone. The storytelling layer adds a nice flavor without overshadowing the core objective: clear the board of undead nastiness.
- Dynamic threat shifts. A guest can change the tempo by altering enemy spawns, hazard density, or the pace of the round. You will feel the shift in tension as the board goes from routine cleanup to a scrappy, late-night scramble that keeps you glued to the table.
- Special encounter cards. Some sessions include small side challenges that test negotiation, stealth, or brute force. These cards are typically optional, which is a smart move for groups with different appetite for risk and drama.

In practice, these mechanics feel integrated rather than tacked on. The guest theme is not the flavor of the week to be tossed aside after three missions; it becomes a character-driven thread that pulls your survivors into morally ambiguous choices and tense firefights. The design team deserves credit for weaving narrative flavor with mechanical bite rather than delivering a one-note gimmick.

## Strategy tips and tricks to maximize your fun

- Prioritize flexibility over brute force. The guest abilities tend to reward players who adapt mid-mattle rather than who is strongest on the first turn. Keep a plan B ready for when the guest flips the board in an unexpected direction.
- Coordinate your survivor roles. A guest may incentivize you to pair a stealthy scout with a bright talker who can negotiate with an in-world celebrity. The social angle can prevent unnecessary fights and buy you critical turns of safety in a tight corner.
- Watch the clock. Some guest effects are time-sensitive, triggering at the end of a round or during a specific phase. If you ignore the timer, you may lose the opportunity to leverage a guest power to its fullest. It is not about being fast; it is about being opportunistic.
- Don’t forget about equipment and supply management. In the heat of the moment, it can be easy to slip into a habit of lighting up kills but forgetting about healing, ammo, or lock picks. The guest cards encourage a more deliberate pace, because some effects rely on those scarce resources to work their magic.
- Tailor your expectations. If you are the kind of player who seeks ultra-tight optimization, you may want to monitor the spice of the guest flavor and adjust your table vibe accordingly. The appeal of Special Guest is in its ability to add narrative drama; you can still win without perfect optimization, which is a relief to many of us who lean toward the chaotic side of victory.
