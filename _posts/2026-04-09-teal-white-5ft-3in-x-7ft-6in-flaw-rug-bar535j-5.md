## Care, Cleaning, and Maintenance Prospects
Caring for the rug is a straightforward routine: regular vacuuming, occasional spot-cleaning, and a memory of where you place heavy items. Spills of coffee, tea, or a mysterious smoothie blend respond well to quick blotting and a mild detergent solution. Do not rub aggressively; you will signal to the dye that you are in a hurry, and the dye will respond by becoming even more dramatic than you anticipated. For stubborn stains, a fabric-safe stain remover used in small, tested areas should do the trick. Always air dry rather than heat-drying, unless you enjoy the scent of burnt fibers and a dramatic callback to hot cushions.

Rotation is optional but recommended for long life. If possible, rotate the rug 180 degrees every few months to distribute wear evenly. It also gives you the opportunity to reframe your seating area and notice new angles in your room. If you have pets, place a coaster under water bowls and keep claws away from the edges to avoid fray. The rug is resilient, but a little care goes a long way in preserving its vibrant teal and white punch.
