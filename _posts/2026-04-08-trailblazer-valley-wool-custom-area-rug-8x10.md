### How to Measure Like a Pro

- Measure the room length and width, then subtract space for any built-in furniture. The goal is to have rug margins around furniture frames that feel intentional, not accidental.

- Consider door swings: If a front door or sliding glass door opens over the rug, you may want to account for clearance so the rug does not snag or curl up.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

Wool rugs are among the most forgiving floor coverings when it comes to maintenance. They resist many common household accidents, and their natural fibers tend to bounce back after compression. Still, you will want to adopt a routine that keeps the rug looking fresh for years. Vacuuming is your friend, but not at maximum power. Wool fibers can shed if you vacuume aggressively; use a gentle setting and avoid beater bars that might pull fibers from the pile. Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear and to avoid a permanent impression from a single chair leg.

### Stain Management

Spills happen. The best approach is to blot, not rub, as rubbing pushes the liquid deeper into the fibers. For persistent stains, a mild detergent solution or specialized wool rug cleaner is preferable. Always test a small, inconspicuous area before applying any product widely. For colorfastness and long-term color stability, avoid direct sun when possible, or use UV-blocking window treatments. Wool fibers have natural colorfastness but are not immune to fading if exposed to bright, direct sunlight for extended periods.

### Maintenance Schedule

Set up a simple maintenance calendar: weekly vacuum, monthly rotation, quarterly deep clean if needed. If you have pets, you may want to implement a protective arrangement under the feeding area or a pet-friendly pad under the rug to reduce wear at the seams. The bottom line is that a well-maintained wool rug can look great for years, with only occasional professional cleaning to refresh the fibers and restore pile height.
