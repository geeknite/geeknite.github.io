## Color Accuracy, Calibration, and Image Quality
### Out-of-the-box performance
Like many mid-range monitors, the Gram View is usable straight out of the box. You’ll likely find the default gamma and color temperature acceptable for general tasks, with adjustable presets to tune for specific workflows. If you’re serious about color, plan a calibration routine: hardware calibration tools paired with a neutral profile will yield more repeatable results across sessions.

### Calibration tips
- Start with a neutral gamma around 2.2 and a color temperature around 6500K for standard daylight tasks.
- If you’re editing photos, consider a calibrated ICC profile to unlock more accurate skin tones and neutral grays.
- Test with a neutral gray ramp and a few reference images to verify uniformity and avoid banding in subtle gradients.

### SDR vs HDR in practice
 SDR content looks clean and sharp, which covers the majority of what most people view daily. HDR content benefits from the monitor’s vibrant tones, but don’t expect a showroom HDR experience—think polished, not cinematic. For most users, SDR will be the daily driver, with HDR giving that nice “pop” when watching a video or viewing a photo with dynamic range. If you’re chasing true HDR benchmarks, you’ll probably want a brighter higher-end panel, but for a mid-range setup, the Gram View handles HDR-like scenes with pleasing contrast.
