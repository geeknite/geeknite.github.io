## What’s inside the original 2015 edition

The core of Exploding Kittens is a cheeky deck of cat-themed cards with varied effects. The main pieces you’ll encounter include:

## Gameplay and mechanics: how the magic works (and why it’s so replayable)

Exploding Kittens is a turn-based card-drawing game where players dodge exploding kittens while using Defuse cards and other actions to shift risk around the table. The core mechanics are approachable for casual players but offer enough micro-choices to stay spicy on repeat play.

- The deck: Most of the action comes from drawing from a central deck. Each turn you draw a card unless an action interrupts the flow. The tension grows as the deck tightens and the probability of an Exploding Kitten lingers menacingly.
- Defuse cards: Your insurance policy. If you Defuse, the Exploding Kitten is reshuffled into the deck and the game continues—perhaps with a more treacherous distribution of future cards.
- Cat cards and action cards: Cat cards typically form sets that unlock special effects; action cards let you peek into the future, force others to draw, or reshuffle the deck with reckless abandon.
- See the Future and Shuffle: See the Future lets you peek at the top cards, while Shuffle resets the current risk landscape by mixing the deck anew. These moves invite a little bluffing, misdirection, and some strategy light enough for party-night chaos.

The real appeal is tempo. The game oscillates between serene, almost ritual draws and chaotic, who-stands-what-now energy when the last few cards are in play. You’ll watch groups of players calculate risk, bluff, and erupt in laughter when a clever chain of moves backfires or explodes in glorious fashion.

For sealed originals, the novelty factor compounds: the artwork, the packaging, the vibe. The rules are approachable enough that a casual crowd can jump in quickly, yet there’s enough room for small strategic nudges that keep it from feeling completely dumbed down. It’s a social experiment that rewards timing, boldness, and a willingness to let a running joke carry the night.

## Card types in more detail: a quick taxonomy

- Exploding Kitten: the central hazard. Draw one without a Defuse, and you’re out.
- Defuse: your lifeline. It’s the tiny superhero cape you slip on when the deck turns sulky.
- Cat cards: these are the social currency. Collect sets to unleash silly effects or power plays that influence the table’s mood as much as its fate.
- See the Future: a predictive peek that gives you window into the near future, though not perfect certainty. It’s basically weather forecasting for dicey cats.
- Attack: passes the baton of extra draws to the next player, which can swing momentum dramatically.
- Skip and Shuffle: utility cards that buy time or shuffle the risk around the table like a chaotic DJ.

In the 2015 print, these cards balance nicely for groups from 3 to a larger party. It scales, though with more players there’s more luck and more bargaining for who will end up drawing last.
