### Safety and Handling
This is where the rubber meets the test leads. Li-ion packs require respect. Here are the safety practices we recommend when using Cameron Sino or any replacement cells in professional gear:
- Inspect before use: look for swelling, damaged terminals, or any signs of corrosion.
- Use proper charging equipment: a charger designed for Li-ion packs with overcharge protection reduces risk dramatically.
- Don’t short the terminals: this is not a time to conduct improvised arc-welding experiments.
- Avoid extreme temperatures: battery performance and lifespan degrade outside the manufacturer-recommended operating range.
- Replace in pairs when possible: if you’ve got multiple MIT devices in a fleet, swapping in a matched pair can help maintain consistent performance across devices.
