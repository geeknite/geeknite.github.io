## Install and care tips

### Cleaning and maintenance for grasscloth wool combos

Natural fiber rugs require a different touch than synthetic ones. Here are some practical maintenance tips:

- Vacuuming: Use a vacuum without a beater bar for high pile surfaces or set the height to a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers. Vacuum regularly to remove dirt that can become embedded and dull the texture.
- Spills: Act quickly but calmly. Blot with a clean, dry cloth rather than rubbing. If the spill is a sticky substance, you may want to dab with a mild solution recommended by the manufacturer or a professional rug cleaner. Always test on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Friction and wear: Natural fibers show signs of use differently than synthetic carpets. You may see a few flattened fibers or slight changes in color where foot traffic is highest. This is part of the rug’s character and tells a story about your home’s daily rhythm.
- Rotation: If possible, rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and sun exposure. This helps the pile age gracefully and avoids a one-way patina in the sunniest spots.

### Stain management: quick rules

For most household spills, a damp cloth and a mild cleanser recommended by a carpet professional will handle it. Avoid aggressive scrubs or bleach on wool fibers. Wool is tough, but it is not invincible to harsh chemicals. If you are dealing with a red wine incident or pet-related mishap, consider calling in a pro for a deeper clean or using a specialized wool rug cleaner following the manufacturer’s instructions.
