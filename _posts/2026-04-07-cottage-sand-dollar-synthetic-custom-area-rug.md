## Materials and Construction: What is under the sea (or under your feet)

### Fiber tech and durability

The Cottage Sand Dollar rug uses synthetic fibers—think polypropylene blends and/or modacrylic variants—that are known for stain resistance and long life in high-traffic areas. The advantage here is straightforward: you can spill coffee, wine, or ephemeral weekend crafts, and cleanup will be a relatively quick wipe rather than a heroic laundry saga. If you have kids or pets, you are not shopping for a mom‑in‑chief wool wonder rug; you are shopping for a rug that will survive the chaos and still look presentable for guests who pop in unannounced.

### Backing and grip

Backing options for synthetic coastal rugs tend to be latex or latex-free, with a backing that minimizes slippage on hardwood and tile floors. The practical upshot is safety and ease—no dramatic sliding during a morning dash to the kitchen, and no need to feel like you must rearrange your coffee table every time someone walks in the room. If your floor is a notorious slipperiness champion, you might want to pair this rug with a silicone anti-slip pad. It is a small investment for a big confidence boost.

### Care for the fibers

Caring for a synthetic rug is refreshingly straightforward. Regular vacuuming to remove surface dust and hair is recommended. When spills happen, blot rather than rub to prevent pigment movement. For tougher stains, most synthetic rugs tolerate mild detergents or specialty rug cleaners; always test in a small inconspicuous spot before committing the entire area to a product. Drying is typically faster than you expect, which means you can stage a rescue mission and get back to binge-watching your favorite coastal documentary in record time.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Pattern Pristine

### Daily care

- Vacuum regularly to remove surface dust and pet hair. Use a crevice attachment to avoid snagging delicate fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Use a rug pad or underlayment to reduce slippage and add a touch of cushion. The pad also slows down wear in the most trafficked spots.

### Cleaning spills and stains

- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth or paper towel. Do not rub, as rubbing can push the stain deeper into the fibers.
- For light stains, a mixture of mild dish soap and water can do the trick. Test in an inconspicuous corner first to ensure color stability.
- For tougher stains, consider a gentle rug cleaner formulated for synthetic fibers. Always follow the manufacturer instructions and perform a spot test.

### Deep cleaning and maintenance cadence

- Consider a quarterly or semiannual deep clean if your space sees heavy use. Many synthetic rugs can be cleaned with a professional carpet cleaner on a low heat setting, or with a gentle hand wash and air dry. Always ensure the rug is completely dry before placing it back on the floor to prevent mold or odor.
- Avoid harsh chemicals like bleach or alkali cleaners that can degrade the fibers or alter colors. If a product label advises against fabric interaction, err on the side of caution.

### Storage tips when not in use

If you retreat the rug during a seasonal decor switch, roll it rather than folding to avoid creases. Store in a cool, dry place and avoid direct sunlight for extended periods to preserve color integrity.
