## Gameplay impact: strategy and table talk
So why should you add the Abomination Pack to your Zombicide Black Plague Green Horde sessions? Because it changes the strategic calculus in meaningful ways.

### The tempo changes
With a boss on the table, tempo matters more than ever. You cannot treat the Abomination as a distant nuisance because it can threaten multiple heroes in a single turn. This forces players to think in terms of zoning and tempo. You want to minimize the number of heroes in the same line as the boss if that line means a wipe, and you want to maximize the number of actions that can be used to set up future turns rather than simply grinding the boss down. In practice this means players coordinate who distracts, who traps, and who deals final blows when the opportunity arises.

### Resource management gets sharper
As with most Zombicide expansions, the Abomination Pack rewards careful resource management. You will want to pool your blades, potions, and traps rather than letting a single hero hog them all. The bosses punish tunnel vision that ignores the board’s other threats. The ability to control access to the boss’s line of sight or to block its preferred spawn points often determines whether you survive to tell the tale or end up as backup snack for decaying gnashers.

### Player roles and synergy
The expansion plays nicely with a variety of playstyles. Veteran players who love brutal boss fights will appreciate a challenge that requires planning and precise execution, while casual players will enjoy the clear and dramatic mechanics without feeling overwhelmed.
