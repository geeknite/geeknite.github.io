## Maintenance and Care: Keeping It Fresh Without Losing Your Sanity
### Vacuuming and Cleaning Routine
A weekly vacuum with a standard upright or canister vacuum should be sufficient to keep the Seyward rug presentable. For minor dust and loose fibers, a gentle pass is all that’s needed. If you encounter stubborn spots, a mild cleaner formulated for synthetic fibers is best. Always test any cleaner in a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure it does not alter the color or texture. Quick note: avoid harsh bleach-based cleaners that can degrade the fibers and ruin the trellis pattern’s crispness.

### Stains, Spills, and Their Management
In the event of spills, act quickly. Blot, don’t scrub, to lift moisture without grinding the stain into the fibers. For stubborn spills like coffee or juice, a light dab with a clean cloth followed by a gentle wash with a mild solution can work wonders. If you have pets, there are enzyme cleaners designed for synthetic fibers that can help with odors and stains. Regular rotation of the rug (so it wears evenly) plus occasional washing according to the manufacturer’s guidance will extend its life a good long time.

### Tips for Longevity
- Use a non-slip rug pad to reduce movement and wear.
- Rotate the rug every few months to distribute traffic evenly.
- Keep door movements gentle to prevent edges from curling upward.
- Address spills promptly to prevent staining and fiber discoloration.
