## Assembly and Setup
Assembling four desks is a test of your patience, your partner’s humor, and your ability to resist the universal urge to declare IKEA an enemy of civilization. Expect to spend a good chunk of your afternoon assembling the set, especially if you’re balancing cables, partitions, and a variety of screws that look suspiciously identical.

### Step-by-step reality
- Unpack everything and organize screws by length; this is the grown-up version of sorting candies by color and shape. 
- Attach the legs and frames, then mount the desktop surfaces. The process is fairly logical, but you’ll want two people for the heavy-pulling parts to avoid dropping the panel on your toes (spoiler: it’s not dramatic until you realize you’ve got a toe-pressing souvenir).
- Install the partitions with the provided hardware. The panels snap into place with a satisfying click, and if you’re lucky, your dog will think they’re fancy doggy dividers and try to nestle between them.
- Install the locking drawers and test the locks. A positive pro-tip: set up a simple key arrangement system so you don’t get a keychain with 28 keys that all look the same and somehow open the wrong desk.
- Cable management: route cables through grommets and channels; use zip ties sparingly to avoid the “snackbar of power cords” look underfoot.

### Time investment and tips
- If you’re a solo assembler, budget extra time and a few good playlists. If you have a partner, you’ll shave a decent chunk of time. Expect a two-to-four hour window depending on your experience with flat-pack furniture and whether you’ve had prior exposure to the sacred art of aligning panels without turning the desk into a parallelogram of pain.
- Tools you’ll need: a screwdriver (Phillips and flathead), a rubber mallet (for gentle panel seating), and a measurement tape to ensure your partition heights are uniform across the row. A power drill is optional but can speed things up — just be mindful of over-tightening.
