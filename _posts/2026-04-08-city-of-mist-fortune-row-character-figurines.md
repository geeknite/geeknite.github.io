## Overview
Fortune Row is a curated line of character figurines designed to embody the core archetypes that define the Fortune Row chapter of City of Mist. The line’s aim is not simply to decorate a table; it’s to invite players and GMs to lean into mythic vibes without losing the noir edge that makes City of Mist sing. Each figure is sculpted to convey a moment—a narrow escape, a whispered bargain, a key turn in a grimy door—capturing the tension between luck and choice that sits in the game’s fog-choked heart.

The set typically focuses on a handful of archetypes that thread through the Fortune Row narrative: the schemer who always appears one step ahead, the investigator who reads the city like a wall of rain, the guardian who protects the fragile line between myth and man, the trickster who flips fate with a wink, and the keeper of secrets who knows who you are before you know yourself. The idea is that, in the same way the city uses you, you can use the figurines to cue scenes, inspire dialogue, and remind players of the stakes without turning to long stacks of reference cards.

## Packaging and Build Quality
Unboxing is part of the show here, and Fortune Row doesn’t skip the curtain call. The figures arrive in a sturdy, glossy box with a window that frames the central piece while hinting at the rest of the crew. The packaging design leans into premium presentation without tipping into “fragile magic glass” territory. Inside, each figurine sits in a role-specific foam cradle with a tiny character card that lists the alias, a one-liner hook, and a quick stat nudge you can track on a card or on a whiteboard.

Materials: The figures sit in a resin/plastic composite that’s convincingly hefty, heavier than a typical 28mm plastic mini and a touch lighter than high-end resin statues. They feel solid enough to survive a tabletop tumble during a particularly cinematic session, yet delicate enough that you’ll want to handle them carefully when dusting or touching up paint.

Paint and finish: Base coats are clean, with crisp lines on the faces and costumes. The shading sits neatly in the recesses, giving the figures a cinematic depth rather than a flat toy aesthetic. Occasional slips of paint in very fine details are not unusual in mass-produced lines, especially within a mixed-scale or busy costume, and can be a minor distraction for a perfectionist. If you want a showroom finish, expect to airbrush or touch up a few figures—this is where Fortune Row shines as a kit for hobbyists who like to tinker.

Assembly: Most Fortune Row figures arrive pre-assembled. Where assembly is required, it’s usually straightforward with a ball-and-socket joint or a simple peg. Tiny accessories—like a dagger, a card, or a pocketful of secrets—can be fiddly and easy to misplace. For players who like dynamic poses, the modular approach provides a degree of customization, but set aside a few hours for a Saturday hobby session if you’re repainting or adjusting poses.

Display: The figures stand a touch taller than the typical 28mm mini, a design choice that helps them stand out as focal points during a reveal or a dramatic “show, don’t tell” moment. The paint job holds up under table lighting, and the cohesive color palette keeps the entire crew readable at a glance during a tense scene.

### Posing options and interchangeability
Despite being mostly pre-assembled, Fortune Row figures offer some degree of pose flexibility. Some characters feature alternate hand positions or removable accessories that can be swapped with a simple press or light glue. If you enjoy staging a reveal moment or switching a character’s stance to reflect a shifting mood in play, you’ll appreciate the extra touch that adds variety without converting your shelf into a DIY sculpture project.

## Aesthetics and Character Design
Visual style: Fortune Row leans hard into film-noir aesthetics—trench coats, fedoras, glimmering jewelry, and stylized mythoi insignia. The sculpting captures a single moment in time: a dramatic lean, a half-turned glance, a hand slipping into a coat pocket where a relic or a card might hide. The design language invites rain and neon to do the storytelling heavy lifting for you, turning every battle or revelation into a mini scene you can point to and say, “This is the moment.”

Individual characters: The lineup tends toward a well-chosen spectrum of archetypes—the schemer who always seems to sniff out the right bargain, the investigator who reads a city the way most people read a novel, the guardian who stands between the innocent and the abyss, the trickster who flips fate on a dime, and the keeper of secrets who seems to know your secrets before you do. Each figure features distinctive props, heraldic motifs, and color schemes that tie back to the Fortune Row’s noir narrative. That cohesion makes it easy to drop a figure into a scene and have players immediately identify its role.

Display value: Collectors will appreciate the cohesive look of the lineup when shown as a display piece under dim lighting or on a lighted shelf. The satin-like finish catches glow in a way that makes the figures pop without looking “glossy toy.” For table use, the figures read clearly in a crowded tavern or a back-alley negotiation. The balance between table-top practicality and coffee-table prestige is well struck here.

## Playability and Integration with City of Mist
Rules context: City of Mist is a storytelling RPG where atmosphere, single moments, and character-driven choices drive scenes more than tactical minis do. Fortune Row doesn’t replace the narrative—far from it. It acts as a kit of visual prompts that codify who these archetypes are and how they manifest in the city’s fog. They’re immersive props that can trigger mood shifts and set-piece moments in a way that a sheet of notes never could.

Token use: On a typical table, you’ll place a figurine on a character’s name card to serve as a tangible symbol of their mythic identity. Color cues and stat tweaks can be attached on an index card to reflect the mythoi’s influence on the scene. For example, a character leaning into Fate might gain advantage or an edge on social checks when negotiating in a rain-soaked alley under a flickering neon sign.

Story seeds and Mist: The figures come with built-in storytelling prompts—an amulet, a card, a scratched ledger—that pair with a Mist trigger (the story engine behind the city’s fog). The synergy between these physical prompts and your narrative prompts is where City of Mist shines, and Fortune Row gives you ready-made prompts without having to conjure them from scratch every session.

If you prefer a minimalist setup—tokens on a map and a couple of poseable stand-ins—Fortune Row may feel like a luxury addon. As a display piece, or as a storytelling tool, it brings a new layer of mood you can’t easily replicate with a stack of index cards.

If you’re on a tight budget, you might still enjoy the set for show-and-tell moments between sessions or as a centerpiece for a city-by-night vignette you run every other week.

## Value and Price
Pricing varies by retailer and region; typical sets sit in a mid-range bracket that balances fidelity with accessibility. If you’re a collector who loves display-grade, pre-painted pieces, Fortune Row gives you a compelling mix of aesthetics and play value. If you’re a GM budgeting for a campaign long-form, you may want to approach this as a payoff for a milestone rather than a daily-use set.

Pros:
- Strong visuals that capture the noir vibe
- Solid build; durable enough for regular play
- Paint quality holds up with light handling
- Excellent display piece for shelves or gaming table

Cons:
- Some small parts can be fiddly
- A few figures may have slight mispaint in mass production
- Not strictly necessary for playing City of Mist, but adds flavor
- Pricing can be a barrier for new players with limited budgets

Alternatives and customization: If you already own 28mm minis, Fortune Row can serve as a thematic stand-in with minor tweaks. Many players supplement their line with generic noir minis or non-branded archetypes. For the enthusiasts who want to channel their own Fortune Row vibe, repainting or customizing figures to match a house narrative is a fantastic option. You can rebadge or swap symbols to reflect house mythoi and personal stakes, turning the squad into your own city-wide legends.

Maintenance and care: Regular dusting with a soft brush keeps the satin finish looking fresh. Avoid aggressive solvents on painted surfaces. If you plan to repaint, use hobby-grade acrylics, followed by a matte or satin sealant to protect your hard-won paint job from table-top chaos.

## Final Verdict
Fortune Row offers a stylish, atmospheric extension to City of Mist that goes beyond “pretty miniatures.” It’s not about replacing the rhythm of your dice or the tempo of your narration; it’s about deepening the mood and providing tangible prompts that help players anchor a scene in a city that refuses to stay quiet. The line hits a sweet spot between hobbyist-quality production and accessible price, making it particularly appealing for new players who want to invest in the feel of the game without turning the hobby into a full-scale painting project.

If you crave a physical anchor for your crew’s mythoi and you enjoy the cinematic vibe of a rain-soaked noir, Fortune Row earns a strong recommendation. It’s a luxury that reads as a practical storytelling tool rather than a cosmetic vanity. For a group that thrives on atmosphere and character-driven scenes, these figures become part of the storytelling vocabulary rather than just tabletop décor.
