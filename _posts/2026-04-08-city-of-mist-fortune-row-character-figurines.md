## Overview
Fortune Row is a line of character figurines designed to represent key archetypes from the Fortune Row storyline in City of Mist. The line aims to capture the vibe of a bustling noir city where luck, fate, and the right choice at the right moment can turn a grim night into a glass of cheap whiskey and a fresh lead. The figurines are sculpted, painted, and packaged to serve as both display pieces and practical storytelling aids for your sessions.

## Packaging and Build Quality
Unboxing is half the fun. Fortune Row arrives in a compact, glossy box with a window that showcases a central figure and hints at the rest of the crew. The packaging uses a thick cardboard sleeve with foil accents that scream premium, but not precious. Inside, each figurine sits in a role-specific foam cradle, with a small card detailing the character’s name, alias, and a one-liner plot hook.

Materials: The figures themselves feel like a resin or hard plastic composite, somewhere between a wargame mini and a high-end collectible statue. They’re solid enough to survive a coffee-fueled session, but fragile enough that you’ll want to handle them with care when cleaning dust or retouching paint.

Paint and finish: The base coats are clean, with crisp lines on the face and costume. The shading sits well in the recesses, giving a cinematic look rather than a flat toy vibe. There are occasional soft paint bleed in the finer details, which is forgivable for a product in this price tier and mass-production. If you’re a perfectionist, you’ll want to airbrush and touch up, which is where this line shines as a kit for hobbyists.

Assembly: Most Fortune Row figures arrive pre-assembled. If a figure requires assembly, it’s usually straightforward with a snug ball-and-socket joint or a simple peg. Some pieces are a touch fiddly—tiny accessories like daggers or cards can be easy to misplace or drop into the carpet abyss. If you’re a gamer who rearranges poses mid-battle, you’ll appreciate the modular approach, but you’ll want to set aside an afternoon for hobby tasks.

Display: Given their size, they look sensational on a map or shelf. They stand slightly taller than the typical 28mm mini, which is perfect for a focal point during a dramatic City of Mist reveal. The paint job remains robust across the set; there isn’t an obvious miscast figure and the color palette stays cohesive.

### Posing options and interchangeability
Despite being pre-assembled, Fortune Row figures offer some degree of pose flexibility. Some characters feature alternate hand positions or removable accessories that can be swapped with simple presses or light glue. For players who like to stage a reveal moment in-session, this is a subtle but meaningful flourish.

## Aesthetics and Character Design
Visual style: Fortune Row leans into film-noir aesthetics of City of Mist, with characters garbed in trench coats, fedoras, glimmering jewelry, and stylized mythoi insignia. The sculpted poses capture a moment in time: a dramatic lean, a half-turned glance, a hand sliding into a coat pocket where a card or a relic might hide. It’s the sort of design where you can imagine the rain as a running commentary on the character’s moral flexibility.

Individual characters: The lineup includes a carefully curated range of archetypes: the schemer, the investigator, the guardian, the trickster, and the keeper of secrets. Each figure has distinctive features that help players quickly identify them at a glance, even in the glow of a neon-lit tavern. The characters’ symbols and color schemes tie back to the Fortune Row narrative, so you can weave in the lore without needing a separate reference sheet.

Display value: The collection looks good as a group and sings when placed in a display case with dim lighting, where the satin-like finish picks up the ambient glow. You’ll find a nice balance between table-top ready and coffee-table-worthy.

## Playability and Integration with City of Mist
Rules context: City of Mist is a storytelling-driven RPG where the Mist and mythoi shape scenes rather than rigid tactical needs. Figurines are not strictly required to play, but they can significantly enhance mood and memory cues for players. Fortune Row figurines act as visual prompts that codify who these archetypes are, both for players and the GM.

Token use: For a typical session, place a figurine on the character’s name card as a tabletop tangible stand-in for a mythic identity. You can assign a color cue or stat tweak on an index card to reflect how the person’s mythoi influence the current scene. For example, a character with a strong Fate motif might have advantage on social checks when negotiating with a Maelstrom gang in a rain-soaked alley.

Story seeds and Mist: The figures can carry small prop ideas—an amulet, a card, a scratched ledger—that you can pair with a Mist trigger. The synergy between physical prompts and narrative prompts is where City of Mist shines, and the Fortune Row set provides ready-made prompts you don’t need to conjure from scratch.

## Value and Price
Pricing varies by retailer and region; typical sets run in a mid-range price bracket that balances fidelity with accessibility. If you’re a collector who likes to display pre-painted premium pieces, Fortune Row gives you the aesthetic with practical play value. If you’re a budget-conscious GM who wants to decorate a campaign’s crime alley table, you might catch a sale or pick up a partial set.

Pros:
- Great visuals that capture the noir vibe
- Solid build; durable enough for regular play
- Paint quality holds up with light handling
- Nice display piece for shelves or gaming table

Cons:
- Some small parts can be fiddly
- A few figures may have slight mispaint in mass production
- Not strictly necessary for playing City of Mist, but adds flavor
- Pricing can be a barrier for new players with limited budgets

Alternatives and customization: If you already own a mixture of 28mm minis, Fortune Row can be used as stand-ins with slight adaptation. Some players prefer to use generic noir character miniatures as stand-ins rather than paying for a bespoke set. For those who want to go the extra mile, you can repaint or customize figures to better match your own version of the Fortune Row.

Maintenance and care: Clean dust from figures using a soft brush or a can of compressed air. Avoid harsh solvents on the painted surfaces. If you intend to repaint, use hobby-grade acrylics and seal with a matte or satin finish to protect the color.

## Final Verdict
If you crave a stylish, atmospheric way to anchor your City of Mist crew, Fortune Row is a solid buy. It is not about replacing the rhythm of your dice, but about enhancing the mood of the table with evocative visuals. The line hits a sweet spot where hobbyist-quality production meets accessible price. It’s particularly effective for new players who want to invest in the feel of the game without diving into an expensive painting project.
