### How to play (quick refresher)

Phase 10 GO! Pocket Size follows the same 10-phase track as the original, but the rounds are crisper, quicker, and less of a commitment. Here’s a quick refresher for those who haven’t touched the base game since college.

- Objective: Be the first player to complete all ten phases and go out by discarding your last card.
- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal each player a hand of ten cards, place the draw pile in the center, and flip the top card to start the discard pile.
- On your turn: Draw one card from either the draw pile or the discard pile. If you can lay down a phase, do it. If not, you continue collecting cards until you meet a phase requirement. After laying down a phase (when you’ve met the requirements), you can also lay down additional cards to form new sets or runs, if you have them. End your turn by discarding one card.
- Phases: Phase 1 through Phase 10 each require a different combination of sets and runs. The order is fixed; you must complete Phase 1 before moving to Phase 2, and so on.
- Going out: When you’ve completed your current phase, you can lay down the rest of your cards to go out. The goal is to empty your hand; the number of cards left in opponents’ hands adds to your score.
- Scoring: The GO! edition keeps a similar scoring vibe to the classic: lower is better, but you’re mostly playing for the thrill of finishing a phase and getting that sweet, sweet bragging rights.
