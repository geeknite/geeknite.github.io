The rules, reimagined for a giant stage

The core rules are familiar to anyone who has played Jenga, but the giant scale invites a handful of strategic twists. Each player, in turn, removes one block from any level of the tower that isn’t the topmost complete level, using only one hand. That block then gets placed on top of the tower, building an increasingly precarious spindle of wood and nerves. The goal is simple on paper: don’t let the tower fall. The reality, especially in giant form, is a test of micro-adjustments, calm breathing, and the occasional sprint to the safety of a nearby wall when gravity decides to stage a comeback.

A few practical tips to get you started

- Start with the outer blocks: Removing from the edges creates a more stable core and buys you a few extra seconds before the inevitable wobble becomes a full-blown earthquake.
- Mind your grip: Use a light touch; the blocks, while robust, respond poorly to “death grip.”
- Observe the top: If the top level looks suspiciously heavy, consider removing from a lower level first to balance momentum.
- Use a mat: If you’re playing on a hard surface, a soft mat under the tower can cushion the fall and reduce drama in the living room.

Maintenance, care, and safety notes

Wood is a living material; it breathes, it expands, and in very rare cases, a block can be nicked or warped if exposed to moisture. Here are practical care tips:

- Keep it dry: Wipe blocks with a dry or lightly damp cloth; avoid soaking or prolonged moisture exposure.
- Store with care: When not in use, store blocks in a dry bag or box to prevent warping and keep dust at bay.
- Refinish if needed: If you notice rough edges or end grain showing wear, a light sanding and a touch of food-safe oil can refresh the surface.
- Safety first: For younger players (under 6), supervise. The large blocks can pose a small choking hazard if small pieces ever detach, though in practice the pieces don’t disassemble easily.
