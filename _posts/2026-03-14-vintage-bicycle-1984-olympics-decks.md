The blue version usually carries a cooler, more restrained arrangement that reads elegant under glass. The symmetry is a deliberate nod to the Olympic rings, but without overdoing it. ### Packaging, Seals, and Condition (Expanded)

Sealed decks are the holy grail for many collectors. In this case, you want to see a wrap that remains intact, with no obvious tears or re-taping. The outer label should be readable and clean, with no heavy printing errors. Seals may be clear cellophane or a light waxy layer from the era, but the key is that the seal is not compromised. If a seal is broken, you lose a portion of the value and some of the mystery. The best way to assess condition is to inspect the packaging under bright light, looking for any signs of heat damage, moisture, or fading. A good test is to gently try to slide a finger along the edge of the wrap; if you can feel a loose corner, you should suspect possible opening.

### Authenticity, Provenance, and How to Verify

Establishing authenticity comes down to packaging integrity and licensing cues. Look for official branding near the label, consistent back patterns, and clear stock finish. If you’re uncertain, consider seeking a professional appraisal from a vintage games or card dealer. Documentation such as receipts, certificates, or a known seller with a track record can help, but even without formal papers, the packaging clues can often confirm a legitimate release. If you want to go deeper, comparing your items to documented examples in reputable catalogues or collector communities can help you separate the wheat from the chaff.

### Displaying and Caring for Your Vintage Decks

Storage is the key to preserving value. Keep the decks away from humidity and direct sunlight, ideally in a climate-controlled cabinet or display case. If you plan to show them off, a dedicated display case keeps dust away and protects from accidental handling. For long-term storage, consider placing the decks in acid-free sleeves or containers if you want to minimize sticker adhesion and tag wear on the packaging. The goal is to maintain the original seal while you enjoy admiring their vintage charm. If you decide to relocate your collection, wrap them in archival tissue to prevent any unintended friction along the outer wrapping.

### Should You Open Them? A Quick Decision Matrix
