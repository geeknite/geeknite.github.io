City of Mist is the kind of roleplaying game that makes you say, you know what, this is exactly the vibe I want at a table tonight: rain-soaked neon, private eyes with a dash of mythic destiny, and a city that negotiates with you in whispers and cigarette burns. The Core Book is both a map and a mood board, a pocket-sized manifesto for why you should lean into character and story rather than counting dice so much you forget there are people across the table. If you like noir cinema, pulp comics, and a pinch of mythic grandeur, City of Mist offers a playground where ordinary folks become legends, and legends sometimes forget they are supposed to be the heroes of their own stories.

## What City of Mist is trying to be
City of Mist sits at the intersection of detective fiction and superhero myth, wrapped in a neon glow that would make a vampire blush. The Core Book argues that power comes from identity, not from raw stat blocks. Your character is a Mantle, a mythic identity that bleeds into the ordinary world, granting you unusual tools and unusual problems. Your other big concept is Logos, the citys myths and legends that shape what can happen tonight. Put Mantle and Logos together, and you get scenes where a quiet stakeout might reveal a centuries-old oath, or a casual favor owed to a living legend becomes your best lead.

The tone is playful, the design is bold, and the book is unapologetically confident in its ability to deliver cinematic, myth-driven storytelling without turning the table into a spreadsheet dungeon crawl. If you want crunchy combat systems, City of Mist will feel like stepping into a club where the bouncer is a dragon in a trench coat. If you want a game that treats storytelling as a collaborative act of myth-building, City of Mist is your velvet rope entrance.

## System basics (without the math glow-up)
The Core Book embraces a rules-lite ethos that leans into narrative authority. You roll to determine outcomes, but the results are processed through the lens of the Mantle and Logos, not through a dry list of modifiers. The dice mechanic is designed to be fast, flexible, and forgiving, so sessions can keep pace with players riffing on character-driven beats rather than debating every possible die result. The emphasis is on moving the story forward, answering big questions, and letting the city react to the players actions in surprising ways.

Mantles are the heart of character creation. Each Mantle provides a thematic identity, a pool of narrative hooks, and a set of powers or capabilities that reflect mythic archetypes. Mantles are deliberately broad, so you can reinterpret them to suit your group. Are you a detective who channels a legendary trickster? A scientist who embodies a mythic healer? A vigilante with a shadow of a fallen king? The book rewards players who lean into the story implications of their Mantle, not just its mechanical bonuses.

Logos are the citys mythic weather: the underlying myths that shape events, drive the plot, and provoke twists. The city is not simply a backdrop; Logos push and pull the narrative, nudging players toward revelations at the moment when they feel most clever—and most uncertain. The interaction of Mantle and Logos creates scenes that feel inevitable in hindsight, yet surprising in the moment, which is a sweet spot for many groups who enjoy predictable surprises as opposed to random chaos.

The book also emphasizes mystery seeds, settings, suspects, and a toolkit for building adventures that feel episodic rather than a single long rail. You get ready-to-run seeds as well as prompts that you can mix and match to conjure nights of mythic neon chaos. It is the sort of resource that invites a GM to improvise with confidence while preserving a strong throughline for the players.

## Character creation in record time
Character creation in City of Mist is quick and narrative-first. You pick a Mantle, select a Logos topic, decide on a few tools and a flaw or two, and you are basically ready to roll. The pace is designed so you can form a compelling concept in a single session and then deeply develop it as you play. The process is deliberately opaque in the best possible way: you think up a legend you want to live, and the book helps translate that legend into game terms that will feel distinct at the table.

The systems reward creative alignment. If your Mantle is about cunning and your Logos leans into a myth of time, your character becomes a person who can navigate urban landscapes with a blend of wits and mythic leverage. The result is a cast of heroes that feels cinematic, with clear hooks for each character to contribute to the unfolding mystery. The book also provides advice for beginners on how to establish bonds between Mantles, how to weave personal stakes into the citys larger myths, and how to avoid bloat by keeping focus on character-driven narrative beats.

## Playing the game: scenes, not skirmishes
Gameplay emphasizes scenes—moments of decision that reveal character and move the mystery forward. The dice provide the possibility of failure and unexpected turns, but the emphasis is always on story direction rather than meticulous tactical computation. Scenes can range from a tense stakeout in a rain-soaked alley to a rooftop confrontation that unravels a citys long-held myth. The transition from investigation to confrontation to revelation feels natural and dynamic, like watching an episodic series where each episode ends with a cliffhanger that only the players can resolve.

Combat is intentionally cinematic. There are options to describe chunks of narrative action and then assign outcomes rather than tracking every hit and miss on a grid. If you want a precise, codified combat system built around hit points and damage dice, City of Mist will feel light by design. If you want to choreograph a chase through neon-lit streets, a rooftop dash, or a dramatic showdown that hinges on a Mantles mythic identity, you will find the rhythm satisfying. The Core Book contains guidance for when to lean into spectacle and when to stay grounded in character motives, which is crucial for groups who want a mixture of both.

GM tips abound. The GM section is a garden of ideas rather than a rigid blueprint. It teaches how to seed clues, orchestrate misdirection, and escalate action while preserving agency for players to explore their Mantles. There are prompts for tone, pacing, and scene economy, plus practical advice on balancing mystery fairness with dramatic risk. If you want to run a campaign that feels like a noir TV show with mythic undercurrents, this book gives you the toolbox to do it without turning the GM into a full-time stage manager.

## Art, layout, and the vibe of the city
The presentation is a feature in its own right. City of Mist uses a bold visual language: noir photos filtered through neon light, with typography that shouts energy and style while staying legible on the page. The arrangement of sections and the chosen artwork invites you to flip pages with a sense of anticipation rather than fatigue. It’s not just pretty; it’s a deliberate mood setter that helps players and GMs stay in the moment. The book also includes practical handouts and printable elements that you can bring to the table in minutes, making it easy to start a session with minimal prep.

Production-wise, the book earns its keep. The binding feels sturdy, the paper takes ink well, and the overall design holds up under long table sessions where you might annotate margins, doodle Mantle ideas, or scribble timelines. If you enjoy a product that treats your table as a living space within a larger myth, you’ll appreciate the attention to detail throughout the Core Book.

## Accessibility and audience fit
City of Mist is accessible to newcomers, thanks to its emphasis on theme and narrative resilience rather than heavy numbers. If you are wary of crunch, you will likely feel right at home. The initial entry point is approachable: a few Mantles, a couple of Logos, a mystery seed, and a group of friends ready to tell a myth-in-progress. For veterans who crave deeper options, the Core Book provides avenues to layer in more complexity around powers, investigation mechanics, or more elaborate social dynamics, allowing a smooth progression as players grow more confident with Mantle and Logos interplay.

One caveat: the myth-first approach can be a double-edged sword. For players who want a clean, linear plot with clearly defined victory conditions, the open-ended nature of stories driven by Mantles and Logos may demand a bit more improvisation. However, this is the intent of the game: to let players co-create a mythic city with a detective noir vibe rather than simply chasing a predefined arc. With the right group and a touch of cinematic language, this flexibility becomes a feature rather than a flaw.

## How City of Mist sits among peers
Compared to more crunchy superhero systems, City of Mist feels lighter on numbers and heavier on mood and storytelling agency. It shares its metaphorical vibe with other indie and story-focused games, but its urban-noir mythic lens sets it apart. If you enjoy a game that wants you to discuss what legends mean in a modern city more than it wants you to optimize your character’s abilities, City of Mist earns its place on the shelf. It also serves as a good stepping stone for players who want to branch into other mythic or pulp RPGs without needing a separate rulebook for every new concept.

As for how it complements or contrasts with other street-level, myth-driven games, a few players may find it an easier entry into the world of collaborative storytelling than titles with dense combat systems or those that demand intricate calendars of events. It invites you to imagine the city as a living character—the kind you’d want to interview after a long night of mythic mischief.

## Scenario ideas and sample play notes
The Core Book provides seeds designed to kickstart sessions quickly. A typical seed includes a location, a handful of NPCs with motives, a couple of clues that can be discovered in any order, and a central question that frames the mystery. Players’ Mantles interact with logos in ways that are not strictly deterministic, which means two things: players will occasionally surprise themselves, and the GM will have opportunities to adapt without feeling the need to redraw the whole plot.

A sample scenario might involve a missing artifact that supposedly bends time, a hooded figure who knows more about the citys myths than they let on, and a detective whose Mantle is tied to the artifacts history. The GM guides the players through acts that escalate tension, reveal ethical dilemmas, and eventually force a choice that reverberates through the citys mythic structure. The book also notes how to weave smaller mysteries into a larger arc so your campaign can feel cinematic without requiring a direct path from start to finish.

## Final verdict
City of Mist Core Book succeeds as a mood-forward, rules-light RPG that invites players to co-create a mythic urban world. It shines in sessions that prioritize character drama, exploration of identity, and narrative twists over tactical optimization. Its strength lies in helping players and GMs craft scenes where the legend of a Mantle collides with a Logos-driven city, producing moments that are memorable precisely because they feel both personal and legendary. It may not satisfy players seeking heavy combat mechanics, but it will captivate groups who want to tell a mythic urban story infused with detective grit and neon light.

Pros:
- Clear thematic core that rewards collaborative storytelling
- Lightweight mechanics that keep sessions moving
- Flexible Mantles and Logos enabling diverse character concepts
- Atmospheric art and thoughtful layout that support mood
- Practical GM tools for mystery design and pacing

Cons:
- Some players may crave deeper combat options
- The myth-first approach can feel open-ended for players who prefer a tight, scripted arc
- A few readers may notice slight balance quirks when trying to scale power levels across Mantles

## Getting started tips
If you are new to City of Mist, start with a small crew of Mantles and one Logos to anchor your sessions. Spend the first session establishing each Mantle’s mythic identity and how it interacts with the citys texture. Use a single mystery seed and a handful of locations to keep things simple. The aim is to let players contribute to the mystery and to the citys mythic atmosphere, rather than forcing a predetermined narrative. As you gain experience, you can layer in more complexity or run longer campaigns that bring the Logos into sharper and more dramatic relief.

Play aids and handouts can save you prep time; print a few clues, a location map, and a victim profile to hand to players as the session progresses. A well-designed handout can become a narrative instrument—something players will return to in later sessions as an anchor for their Mantles and the citys ongoing myths.

## Final note on style and vibe
City of Mist is unapologetically stylized. If you enjoy the romance of myth and the grit of urban life, you will feel at home here. The Core Book gives you a strong, cohesive tonal direction and a practical toolkit that makes it feasible to start a campaign with a minimal up-front investment of prep time. The game rewards players who contribute to the story and GMs who foster a collaborative atmosphere where legends are born and tested under neon rain.

If you want a game that treats myth as something living and changing rather than a fixed power taxonomy, City of Mist is a prime choice. It may not be for everyone, but for the right group, it delivers sessions that feel cinematic, immersive, and genuinely memorable.
