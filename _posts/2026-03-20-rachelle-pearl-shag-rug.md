## Cleaning and maintenance: keep the fluff in check
Maintenance is the dull but necessary cousin of style, and the Rachelle Pearl is surprisingly easy to live with. Vacuuming with a standard upright or a canister vacuum on a low to medium setting works well. You do want to avoid aggressive suction that can pull fibers out of place. A quick pass once a week keeps the pile looking fresh and the space smelling less like a gym sock party and more like a boutique retreat. If you are dealing with pet hair, a bristle brush or a detangling tool can help loosen hair that clings to the fibers, followed by another gentle vacuum pass.

For spills, the rug behaves much better than you expect. Blot, don’t rub, with a clean cloth, and treat it as you would treat any fabric that wears a delicate hat. A mild detergent solution can be used for minor stains, but avoid harsh solvents that may break down the fiber or cause discoloration. If you happen to spill something dramatic like coffee with cream and cinnamon, act quickly and treat with a spot cleaner designed for synthetic fibers. The goal is to minimize set-in stains, not to stage a chemistry experiment on your floor.

Regular fluffing can help maintain the plush look. A quick shake outside (weather permitting) or a gentle run with a wide-tooth comb or a rug rake can revive the pile and distribute fibers evenly. If you have a high-traffic area, rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. A little rotation goes a long way toward keeping the rug looking fresh after countless footfalls, desk chairs, and the occasional roll of a ferocious office chair wheel without complaint.

## Final styling tips and care checklist
- Use a non-slip rug pad to prevent movement and protect floors. This adds stability and helps preserve the rug fibers in high-traffic zones.
- Vacuum regularly with a soft brush attachment to minimize fiber shedding and keep the loft of the pile intact.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and avoid visible patterns from foot traffic.
- Address spills promptly with blotting and mild detergent designed for synthetic fibers. Avoid harsh solvents that can damage the fibers.
- Pair with complementary textiles in your room, such as throw blankets and cushions that echo the pearl hue or contrast with it for a layered look.
