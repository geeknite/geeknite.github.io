## Installation and Mounting: A Practical Adventure
Installing a wall-mount cabinet is not rocket surgery, but it is precise surgery. Here’s a sane approach to get you from bare wall to humming data whisperer:

- Locate studs and verify weight-bearing capability with a stud finder and a mounting weight estimate. If you’re mounting on drywall, you’ll want proper anchors and possibly a bracing plate.
- Measure the available space and confirm depth clearance for the 18-inch cabinet. If your wall is a little shy, you might need to move cables first or choose a shallower variant.
- Attach the mounting brackets to the wall, ensuring level alignment. A spirit level is not optional here; it is your best friend and your mortal enemy’s nightmare.
- Slide the cabinet onto the mounted brackets and secure. Tighten bolts to factory torque values; they don’t want to be overtightened, but they also don’t want to pretend their job is to hold a decorative plant.
- Install rails inside for your equipment. Some rails are fixed, some are adjustable; make sure your 1U devices align with the front mounting rails.
- Route cables via the rear cutouts and wrap loosely with velcro. Aim for a tidy look that makes your future self proud.
- Install your PDUs and power supplies as needed. Ensure you don’t block any air intakes or exhausts.

If you’re unsure about mounting to a specific wall type, consult a professional or, at least, watch a few “how to mount a server rack on drywall” videos for the vibe—because the vibe matters when you’re wrestling with a glass door and a handful of mislabeled patch cables.
