## How to play (quick recap)

If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you know the skeleton: draw until you draw an Exploding Kitten, unless you have a Defuse card. Use action cards to stop or frustrate others, and try to stay alive longer than the person who’s currently riding the adrenaline wave of competitive chaos. If you’re new, here’s a quick primer to get you started without needing a full tutorial:

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal a hand to each player (typically 7-8 cards, depending on your house rules and the number of players).
- Remove all Exploding Kitten cards except for as many as needed for the number of players minus one. The remaining players won’t survive if you keep them all in there; this is our version of trust-fall leadership in card form.
- Each player starts with a Defuse card or two. Because life is unpredictable and the world is full of surprises, Defuse cards are your best friends here.

### Turn structure
- On your turn, you may play any number of action cards from your hand (subject to card-specific rules).
- After you finish playing, you draw a card from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out of the round.
- If you do have a Defuse, you can ignore the explosion and place the Kitten back into the draw pile in a location of your choosing, then keep playing or pass the turn depending on the variant you’re using.

### Key mechanics to know
- Nope cards can interrupt almost any action—except, of course, Nope themselves (which is meta-nonsense in the most delightful way).
- Attack cards shift the timing, causing the next player to take two consecutive turns. It’s like playing a prank on a colleague who’s already suspiciously caffeinated.
- Shuffle cards force a sudden jostle in fate; suddenly the draw pile isn’t predictable, which is exactly what chaos loves.

### Endgame timing
As the deck thins, the tension rises. That moment when only two players remain and a single Defuse card can decide who wins — that’s when the room becomes a tiny, gleeful battlefield of smirks and mock-serious negotiations about “quiet victory” versus “epic chaos.”

## Cards and mechanics: a closer look

Exploding Kittens thrives on a delicate balance of risk and predictability. The 5th Anniversary Edition leans into that balance by widening the card pool with a few new options while preserving the core mechanic that makes players go “Oh no” and “Oh yes” in quick succession.

- Exploding Kitten cards: The recurring threat that keeps everyone honest and slightly paranoid. You don’t want to draw one without a Defuse in your hand.
- Defuse cards: Your lifeline when luck decides to give you a big ol’ hug that also cuts your throat. Defuses have the power to reinsert the kitten into the pile, creating a delicious roulette moment.
- Nope cards: A misdirection tool that helps you push back on another player’s plan. They feel like tiny, merciless trolling devices with a hint of chaos magic.
- Attack cards: These tilt the turn order and force the next player to face two turns in a row. It’s a glorious way to chain reactions and watch plans crumble like a poorly made soufflé.
- New anniversary cards: These add fresh flavors to the game’s mix. They might introduce unusual effects, like forcing players to take a riskier draw or to reveal part of their hand to the table. It’s like choosing a dare at a party, but the dare could instantly ruin your game night forever if you misplay.

### Strategy notes
- Don’t overcommit to a single plan. The deck is a living, breathing monster with unpredictable gaps and joyously cruel detours.
- Read the room. If your friends are the kind who overthink every play, you can be bold. If they’re the kind who snap decisions with a dramatic “I call your bluff,” you might want to reel in your bravado a bit.
- Keep a Defuse ready, but don’t hoard them like a dragon’s hoard if you’re in a multi-round game. Sometimes you’ll want to bluff with a tool you don’t intend to use.
- The late game is a dance of risk assessment. It’s not just who has more Defuses; it’s who can time their defuses to swing the odds when everyone’s adrenaline is at peak levels.
