## Maintenance and Care: Keeping the vibe without the wear

- Cards: Clean hands only—oil from fingertips can accumulate; a soft, dry cloth lightly brushing the cards after a night of play helps retain the finish. If a card edge curls, don’t panic; flattening it with a gentle press between heavy books overnight works in a pinch.
- Chips: Wipe with a soft cloth to remove fingerprints and any residue from snacks. If chips begin to show wear at the edges, you’re probably playing with a heavy-handed bluff technique, which is acceptable in moderation.
- Case: A damp cloth for the exterior and a dry interior wipe will keep the leatherette looking sharp. Avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight, which can cause the case to fade like a 90s brand logo left in the sun for five years.
- Storage: Keep the set away from extreme humidity and heat. The last thing you want is a poker set that looks “vintage” because the felt is slightly warped and the cards have that sun-bleached vibe. Your future grandkids will thank you for avoiding this tragedy.

Maintenance note: this is not a “set it and forget it” purchase. A little care goes a long way in preserving the aesthetic and the tactile satisfaction. Treat it like a prized piece of furniture that occasionally doubles as a gaming kit, rather than a disposable gadget.
