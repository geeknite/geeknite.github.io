## What is Throw Throw Burrito?

Throw Throw Burrito is a fast paced, party friendly card game for 2-5 players. The premise is simple: players draw cards to form sets while also trying to avoid being hit by the two plush burritos that come with the game when someone plays an Attack card. The combination of set collection, hand management, and chaotic social interaction creates a rollicking experience. The pair of plush burritos is the star of the show, and the cards are bright and punchy with goofy art. The game is loud in the most delightful way, full of gasps, shouts, and the occasional apology after an accidental burrito toss.

In practice, you take turns drawing cards and aiming to complete sets. Attack cards turn up and must be resolved; you may be told to catch a burrito toss or to pass a burrito to a new player. The objective is to complete your sets while keeping your team and friends safe from incoming burritos. When a set is completed, the player declares success and begins a new round while the next Attack cycle arrives. The energy is fast, the pace is quick, and the laughter is constant.

## Setup and Turn Structure

To set up Throw Throw Burrito, shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand to each player. Place the plush burritos in the center so everyone can reach them. The player to the left of the dealer goes first, and play proceeds clockwise. On your turn, draw a card, possibly adding to your hand or attempting to complete a set. Attack cards start appearing, and players must resolve them; this is where the chaos begins. You might be instructed to throw a burrito, or to pass it to another player as part of the Attack sequence. When you complete a set, you announce it and begin counting down to the next set, all while trying not to get buried under a flurry of Attack cards.

The pace is fast and the rules are forgiving, which makes it accessible for families, friends, and colleagues who want a light, energetic game night. The social interaction is the real highlight; you are not simply competing for points, you are reacting to others in real time, reacting to the burrito toss, and trying to anticipate the next turn.

## Strategy and Social Play

While I just described a game built for chaos, there is still room for planning and social reading. Here are a few practical tips:

- Keep your hand lean: Do not hoard cards you do not need, as you will want the flexibility to respond to Attack cards and to avoid getting stuck with too many potential penalties.
- Observe your opponents: If someone seems close to finishing a set or if a particular player is loud about a certain move, they may be predictable targets for Attack cards.
- Timing is everything: The best moment to throw a burrito is when your rivals are engaged in a plan you can disrupt. It is not just about who wins, but about who can keep up a chaotic vibe while still staying upright.
- Team dynamics: In larger groups, you can create temporary alliances to protect a friend who is close to winning or to set up a dramatic finish that involves multiple players.

Remember that the humor and chaos are not nil but part of the experience. If you want a strictly strategic game, this is not it; if you want a party game that is loud, quick, and inclusive, Throw Throw Burrito nails the brief.
