## New Mechanics and How They Change The Game

BT03 introduces a handful of mechanics that feel like a natural evolution rather than a radical overhaul. The game remains recognizably Dragon Ball: you still pay your energy costs, you still attack, you still defend, you still chant Tap at times at the wrong moment.

But there are new knobs to tweak:

- Cross World Synergy: A keyword that unlocks cross-universe effects when you include specific World-type energy or card types in your field. The effect often triggers on the same turn or the following turn and can net you extra draw power or a surprise blocker. It’s not game-breaking in every situation, but when it lands in the right window, you feel like you hacked the system with a cultural artifact from another universe.
- Energy Surge: A new energy-accumulation mechanic that rewards forward planning. It encourages players to balance aggression with defensive play and to foresee two or three turns ahead, which is exactly how my life looks when I realize I forgot to pay a bill and the Bank of Zeni is now eyeing me.
- Merge-Beam Finishes: A couple of high-impact finishers that can only happen if you managed to survive long enough to play an expensive, flashy card. The payoff is big: your opponent's eyes widen, their table posture changes to defensive crab, and you celebrate by taking a photo of your own dopamine spike.
- Reprints with Upgrades: A subset of classic cards returns with improved art or better variants. The nostalgia hits you like a Kamehameha and you instantly want to reframe your entire deck around that one card you opened and loved.

Playtesting notes: Expect some stumbles in the early meta as players experiment with the new synergy. Some strategies will be mediocre or even suboptimal until someone discovers the optimal timing for a World-spanning effect. Others will click instantly and you’ll wonder why you ever played a non-Cross Worlds deck in the first place.

In practice, these changes are not radical. They feel like a natural progression of the DBS CCG design. The core loop — draw, energy, play, attack, defend — remains intact, with the new mechanics acting as accelerants rather than replacements. If you enjoy puzzle-solving and the feeling of discovering a 4-card combo that actually works on the turn you need it, BT03 is probably your jam. If you hate the idea of tracking multiple world interactions, you might want to approach with caution and a cup of tea.

## Deck-building Recommendations

Here are some practical ideas you can implement in your own BT03 Cross Worlds deckbuilding experiments:

- Start with a Clear Core: Select a core engine from Cross Worlds cards that accelerates your mid-game tempo. This core will be the backbone of your deck and should be your first 15-20 cards.
- Add Flex Cards: Include 8-12 flexible cards that can swing the momentum in either player's favor depending on the turn and your opponent’s board.
- Decide on a Secondary World: If you’re going cross-world, choose a second world that complements your engine. The synergy will be easier to achieve if both worlds share compatible card types or energy costs.
- Finisher Spotlight: Reserve 2-4 finishers that require careful sequencing but deliver strong payoff when executed on the right turn. The satisfaction of landing one of these late-game finishers is unmatched.
- Budget-Friendly Options: If you’re building on a budget, identify a handful of cheap, consistent cards that can anchor your early-game plan and still allow for a few high-impact finishers.

Testing tips: Keep a notepad on the table to track your draw order, the timing of your energy usage, and the critical moments where cross-world synergy triggers. It’s surprising how much you miss when you rely on memory alone, and the stress from forgetting to activate a key effect can be a hilarious part of your session.
