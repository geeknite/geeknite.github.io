## How UNO Flip works
The core gameplay is straightforward. On a player’s turn, you must play a card that matches either color or number or use a Wild to change the color in play. When the deck is flipped to the dark side, new rules and actions apply. The flips can be triggered by specific cards or by call-and-response moments at the table. The dark side features more punitive actions, which adds tension and turns the “final few cards” moment into something more dramatic.

### Tips for smooth flips
- Plan for the flip: If you anticipate a flip, keep some flexible colors in your hand so you’re not left with a pile of unplayable cards.
- Read the table: If two players are near UNO, you might want to flip earlier to shake up the dynamic and protect your hand.
- Time it right: Don’t flip on a player who’s just one card away if you want to maximize your own odds—unless you want to trigger big laughs and chaos.
