## How UNO Flip works
At its core, UNO Flip still runs on color and number matching with Wilds as your color-changing cheat codes. On a player's turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number, or you can drop a Wild to shift the color in play. When the deck is flipped to the dark side, new rules and actions apply—usually more punitive, often swingier, and designed to keep the table honest in a high-energy way. The flip can be triggered by a particular card and sometimes by a house-rule moment around the table, turning a calm moment into a surprise drumroll that ends with a chorus of laughter or groans depending on your odds at that moment.

### Tips for smooth flips
- Plan for the flip: if you anticipate a switch to the dark side, keep a few flexible colors in hand so you aren’t left with a pile of unplayable cards.
- Read the table: if two players are near UNO and the countdown begins, you might flip earlier to shake up the dynamic and protect your hand.
- Time it right: don’t flip on the person who’s one card away unless your goal is to trigger big laughs and chaos. Strategic flips win hearts even when they cost you a few points.
- Embrace light-house rules: some groups enjoy a deliberate cadence where you flip after a designated turn or after a certain number of rounds. The two-pack design makes experimenting with those rules painless.
