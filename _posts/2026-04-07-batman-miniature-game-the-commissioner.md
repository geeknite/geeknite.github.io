### Base notes
The base keeps with the classic BMG aesthetic: a small scene element that suggests a Street of Gotham mood rather than a grassy field. The base provides a natural support for a tall model, eliminating the fear that the Commissioner will topple over like a villain in a dramatic monologue.

### Painting tips
If you want crisp lines on the lapel and a crisp white shirt without turning the figure into a polar bear, here are a few practical tips:
- Primer: Crisp gray or army green undercoat helps bring out the blues and the grays.
- Suit color: Navy blue with a subtle highlight; wash the recesses for depth.
- Skin tones: Neutral, not too pink; aim for a natural human tone.
- The badge: A touch of metallic gold or brass gives a focal point that reads at tabletop distance.

## Rules footprint: how the Commissioner moves, buffs, and orders
In Batman Miniature Game, leaders like the Commissioner have a different job from frontline bruisers. He operates as a force multiplier, a character whose real power is the aura of command and the ability to pull allies into stronger positions.

### Stats and abilities
- Command aura: nearby allies gain a small but meaningful bonus to morale and reaction tests.
- Tactical reposition: the Commissioner can reposition a light unit on the board to block an alley or to create a crossfire corridor for Batman to move through.
- Emergency protocol: a finite pool of actions that can be spent to call in support units, representing a tuned response rather than raw physical might.
- Detective’s intuition: when the Commissioner is in range, he can identify opponent plans more quickly, granting a small advantage on one card in play.

### How he fits with Batman and the family
The Commissioner aligns well with Batman as the voice of organization and legal authority in Gotham. ## Gameplay experiences: scenarios, balance, and table feel
This section will discuss how the Commissioner plays in practice and what players who want to include him in their rosters can expect.

### A typical turn with the Commissioner
On a standard turn, he issues orders to nearby units, then uses reposition and event cards to favor his side of the board. The result is a chess-like game with a city backdrop, where a well-timed reposition can turn a near loss into a stunning victory. He does not dominate a table by raw damage, but by shaping the flow of the game and the tempo of the encounter.

### Campaign and scenario compatibility
If you run a Gotham campaign or a scenario-driven league, the Commissioner can be a central figure in the early city stabilization arcs. He acts as a link between street-level policing and the more extraordinary modules featuring Batman and his allies. He is an ideal anchor for narrative-driven games, where you want a figure that embodies governance and order under pressure.

### Potential chiptunes of balance
We should note that the generically labeled balance issues can appear if the rest of your roster leans heavily toward brute force or stealth. The Commissioner thrives when paired with solid defense and reliable ranged options that can hold lines while he provides the strategic overlay. If your meta rewards reckless charges, you might find him underperforming. If your meta rewards patient play and table control, he shines.

## Painting and hobby notes: maintenance, display, and conversion ideas
Beyond the tabletop, the Commissioner is a fun subject for hobby projects. He can be built out with minimal conversion work to reflect changes in story arcs, from a straightforward police commissioner to a more stylized Gotham official. A few options:

- Swap the tie color to reflect a particular arc or season.
- Build a diorama base with a corner of a precinct hallway to pull all your Gotham minis together on the shelf.

### Tabletop tips for display
When you display this figure next to Batman or Gordon the detective, think about the narrative you want to tell. Are you portraying a tense moment in the precinct, with a bam at the door and a radio crackle? Or are you showing a cross-city chase where the Commissioner is orchestrating the pursuit from the corner?
