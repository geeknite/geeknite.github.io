## Playability and rule set
The rules for the 1999 Game of Life are intentionally approachable. It is a game about living through choices, not about calculating the exact odds of a lottery jackpot. You will spin, move, choose, and occasionally encounter life events that require you to decide on a course of action. The rules do not bury you under a mountain of exceptions; they offer a straightforward path that keeps the game moving and ensures that everyone gets a fair turn.

### A typical turn flow
1) Spin to move your car along the track. 
2) Land on a space that presents an event. 
3) Decide on a course of action that will impact your wealth or happiness score for the rest of the round. 
4) Collect or spend money as directed by the event card. 
5) Pass to the next player and repeat until retirement goals are reached.
The pacing is designed to be family friendly. It is not a deep strategic operation, but it rewards you with a light sense of accomplishment as you manage your funds and navigate life events with the people around the table.
