## The Basics

### What is UNO?

UNO is a classic card game where players take turns matching a card in their hand with the current card shown on top of the deck by either color or number. The goal is to be the first to play all of your cards. It's simple, it's fun, and it can turn family members into mortal enemies faster than you can say "reverse!"

### Components

The game includes:
- **Cards**: The standard deck with numbers and colors, plus action cards like Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, and Wild Draw Four.
- **Customizable Wild Cards**: A set of blank cards that let you create your own rules.
- **Instructions**: In case you've been living under a rock and need a refresher on how to play.

**Pro Tip**: Keep a pen handy for those customizable wild cards. And maybe a notary public, just in case a rule dispute turns ugly.

## How to Play

### Setup

Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player. Place the remaining deck face down to form a draw pile. Flip the top card to start the discard pile.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, match a card from your hand with the top card of the discard pile by either color or number. If you can't play a card, draw one from the draw pile. If you still can't play, your turn ends. Action cards add strategic twists, while Wild cards let you change the color to be matched.
