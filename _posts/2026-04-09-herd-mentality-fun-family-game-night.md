## What is Herd Mentality?
Herd Mentality is a party game designed to flex your social muscles, reveal your closest pretzel-twist of logic, and celebrate the art of guessing what the majority would say. The premise is simple: players answer questions, then attempt to align their answer with what the herd would pick. The more popular your answer according to the others, the more points you snag. It is cognitive weather forecasting with punchlines, a social experiment you can legally host in your living room.

The genius of the concept sits in the social psychology baked into the rules: humans are herd animals by default, and Herd Mentality hands you a structured way to examine that impulse. Do you double down with a bold contrarian choice, or do you drift toward the obvious crowd-pleaser? Either way, you’ll be talking, laughing, and perhaps revealing a few opinions the family didn’t know you kept tucked behind the couch cushions.

Edition matters here as well: some packs tilt wittier, some lean into pop culture, and some go wholesome enough for grandma. Pick the set that vibes with your crew, or mix and match packs until the table hums with chaos and camaraderie.

## How to Play: Rounds, Scoring, and Social Dynamics
### Round structure
1) A question card is revealed.
2) Each player secretly writes an answer on their card or sheet.
3) All answers are revealed and read aloud or displayed.
4) Players vote on which answer represents the herd’s choice. In some editions you may also vote for a favorite non-herd answer for bonus points.
5) Points go to players whose answer matched the herd’s selection. The herd itself can score if everyone lands on the same answer depending on house rules.
6) The next person becomes the reader for the next round; chaos resumes.

Key strategic intuition: you’re not aiming to be the cleverest person at the table; you’re aiming to be the one who most accurately predicts the majority. This creates a delicious tension. Some rounds honor boldness, some prize perceptiveness, and some reward the person who can cook up a red-soaked story about a fear of zucchini bread that somehow becomes the herd favorite.

### The Herd vs. the Individual
What makes Herd Mentality so endearing is the social pull. You watch as a player hopes their answer mirrors the crowd, then discovers the herd’s mood swings toward silliness. The tension can be mortifying in the best way, especially when a beloved uncle drops a punchline so perfectly on-brand that the table erupts in approval. This is a game about alignment, a gentle exercise in reading a room that sometimes smells like popcorn and family jokes.

### House rules and adaptability
Most groups adopt a few house rules to tailor the experience. For families with younger players, you might simplify scoring or add bonus points for family-friendly humor. If your crew loves rapid-fire, you can implement a timer that keeps rounds short and punchy, maximizing chaos and minimizing the risk of one player turning the round into a one-person show. If you’ve got a gamer-heavy crowd craving meta jokes, allow players to steal a round with a clever counter-argument to the herd’s choice. It is your living room; the herd is flexible, and so are the rules—so long as everyone signs off with a smile.

### Round-by-round tips and tricks
- Start with light questions to warm everyone up. A confident start makes people lean in for the rest.
- Read the table. If a player consistently lines up with the herd, that becomes a data point you can exploit or respect in later rounds.
- For kids, lean into goofy answers. Big laughs are the true currency of early rounds.
- For teens and grown-ups, sharpen the humor but stay mindful of the group’s comfort zone. The best moments are when a joke lands without crossing the line into discomfort.
- Rotate the reader role to share the spotlight and keep energy high. A rotating cadence helps prevent anyone from becoming the gatekeeper of the chalkboard of glory.

## Variants and house rules worth trying
- Team Herd: Split into two or three teams. A team’s score is the sum of its best answers, encouraging collaboration, showdowns, and glorious post-game debates.
- Lightning Round: Trim the thinking time to 15 seconds per answer for a breakneck pace. Expect chaos, laughter, and a few dramatic I did not write that moments.
- Family Edition: Use kid-friendly prompts and encourage kids to explain their logic. It’s adorable chaos with heart.
- Thematic Packs: Use question packs around birthdays, holidays, or school memories to tailor humor to family experiences. Theme nights become a calendar tradition with more confetti and less regret about the calendar.
- Quick draw for props: Add a tiny prop rule where players must present a one-liner with a prop from a drawer. Visuals + humor = maximum giggles.
