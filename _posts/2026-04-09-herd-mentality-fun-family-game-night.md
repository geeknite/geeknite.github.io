## What is Herd Mentality?
Herd Mentality is a party game designed to flex your social muscles, reveal your closest pretzel-twist of logic, and celebrate the art of guessing what the majority would say. The premise is simple: players answer questions, then attempt to align their answer with what the “herd” would pick. The more popular your answer according to the others, the more points you snag. It’s basically cognitive weather forecasting, with punchlines.

The genius of the concept is in the social psychology baked into the rules: humans are herd animals by default, and Herd Mentality makes you confront that instinct head-on. Do you double down with a bold, contrarian choice, or do you seek the safety of the most obvious crowd-pleaser? Either way, you’re going to be talking, laughing, and perhaps differing opinions about what counts as a “good” answer in front of the family group chat incarnate.

What edition you get matters a little: some packs lean wittier, some lean pop-culture spicy, and some lean downright wholesome. Pick the one that matches your crew’s energy, or mix-and-match packs until your gaming table starts humming with the sound of chaos and camaraderie.

## How to Play: Rounds, Scoring, and Social Dynamics
### Round structure
1) A question card is revealed.
2) Each player secretly writes an answer on their card.
3) All answers are revealed and read aloud (or displayed, if you’re using answer sheets).
4) Players vote on which answer they think represents the herd’s choice. Depending on the edition, you may also vote for your favorite non-herd answer for bonus points.
5) Points are awarded to players whose answer was selected as the herd’s answer. The herd itself can also score when they all share the same answer, depending on house rules.
6) The next player becomes the round’s “reader” who flips the next card, and the crowd roars in anticipation for a new set of chaos.

A key strategic intuition: you’re not trying to be the most clever person in the room; you’re trying to be the person who best guesses what the majority will pick. This creates delightful tension. Some rounds reward the bold, some reward the perceptive, and some reward the person who has the most confidence in a red-soaked story you cooked up about your greatest fear of zucchini bread. The scoring system rewards empathy with the crowd—and sometimes outright expediency when you sense the herd is leaning in a specific direction.

### The Herd vs. the Individual
The social dynamic at play is what makes Herd Mentality charming and occasionally mortifying in the best way. You’ll watch as a player quietly hopes that their answer mirrors the crowd’s, only to realize that the herd’s mind is in the mood for silliness. The tension is real, especially when a beloved family member drops a punchline that’s so on-brand you can almost see the “like” meter tick up in the air.

It’s a game about alignment—understanding the social temperature, predicting a collective impulse, and then either riding the wave or leaning into the delicious chaos of nonconformity. Either approach yields laughs, playful banter, and the occasional sibling rivalry that will be remembered long after the game box is tucked away. And yes, there will be moments where someone’s attempt to be contrarian is met with a chorus of enthusiastic agreement from the rest of the table. That’s the magic of the herd—the unpredictable blend of consensus and disruptors.

### House rules and adaptability
Most groups adopt a few house rules to tailor the experience. For families with younger players, you might simplify scoring or add bonus points for family-friendly humor. If your crew loves rapid-fire, you can implement a timer that keeps each round short and punchy, maximizing chaos and minimizing the chance of a single player monopolizing the spotlight. If you’ve got a heavy crowd of gamers who crave meta-jokes, you can allow players to “steal” a round by offering a hilarious counter-argument to the herd’s choice. It’s your living room; the herd is flexible, and so are the rules so long as everyone signs off with a smile.

## Round-by-round tips and tricks
- Start with lighter questions to warm everyone up. People who feel confident early on tend to join the herd later with gusto.
- Pay attention to the social dynamics. If you notice a particular player consistently aligns with the crowd, you can use that information to inform future answers (the practical psychology of the table, minus the lab coat).
- For kids: encourage silly, goofy answers. Big, loud laughter is usually the crowd’s favorite metric, and it signals safe engagement for younger players who are still finding the rhythm.
- For teens and adults: you can lean into sharper humor, but be mindful of the group’s comfort zone. The best moments are when a joke lands without crossing the line into discomfort.
- Rotate the reader role to share the spotlight. The ritual of handing over the “candles in the dark” to the next person keeps the energy up and prevents one corner of the table from becoming a scoreboard-obsessed mausoleum.

## Variants and house rules worth trying
- Team Herd: Split into two or three teams. A team’s total score is the sum of its best answers, encouraging collaboration and shouting matches that you’ll later pretend never happened.
- Lightning Round: Reduce the thinking time to 15 seconds per answer for a frenetic pace. Expect chaos, laughter, and a few dramatic “I did not write that” moments.
- Family Edition: Use kid-friendly questions and allow kids to explain their hilarious logic for why their answer would be chosen by the herd. It’s adorable and chaotic in equal measures.
- Thematic Packs: Use question packs around birthdays, holidays, or school memories to tailor the humor to your family’s shared experiences. Theme nights are basically an annual tradition with more confetti and less guilt about the calendar.
