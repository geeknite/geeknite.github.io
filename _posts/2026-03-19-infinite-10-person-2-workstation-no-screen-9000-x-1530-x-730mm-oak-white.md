## Assembly and Setup Experience

The assembly steps are straightforward but time consuming, especially if you are building with a crew of ten enthusiastic people who want to contribute. The instructions are clear—well, as clear as place A next to B can be—yet you will find yourself rearranging the order mid assembly to accommodate the space you actually have in your conference room. It is a rite of passage for any office build: you will measure twice, cut once, and eventually realize you only brought one set of allen keys and three coffee mugs.

Pro tip: designate a lead assembler who can coordinate the long row with laser focused patience. In terms of tools, you will need standard Allen keys, a screwdriver, and a measuring tape. ### Ergonomics, Comfort, and Real World Use

A common question about a large workstation like this is: Do you get any ergonomic advantages from a multi person layout, or does it simply create a sprawling data siphon that demands twice the cable management? The answer is nuanced. The design allows you to tailor each seat’s positioning with respect to the central cable trough. The absence of screens in the base configuration means you can adapt to a future where screens of various sizes can be added on a per station basis without dealing with a universal monitor height.

Because chairs and desks are individually adjustable, you can accommodate people from different heights and working styles. ### Cable Management and Tech Integration

Cable management is the unsung hero of any large workstation. The central trough is a delight for those who love a tidy desk. It eases the frustration of chasing cables across the floor and helps keep chairs from tripping over the spaghetti of wires you have created. The trough width is generous enough to accommodate multiple power strips, USB hubs, and a handful of network cables. Of course, the standard caveat applies: if you are going full throttle with high wattage equipment at every seat, plan your electrical plan accordingly. It is easy to underestimate the power draw of desktops, docking stations, and video conference devices.

To further improve the cable situation, you can install under desk cable trays, footrests with cable channels, or, if you are particularly bold, a small under table vacuum system to improve the clean air vibe in your space.
