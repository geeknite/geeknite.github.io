## Assembly and setup experience
Let us be honest: setup time can make or break a gaming night. The insert arrives flat-packed in a way that makes you question the box’s promised adventure. The tested version required a careful alignment of a handful of dividers and a moment of silence to appreciate how the foam pieces fit together like a jigsaw puzzle designed by someone who also designed a space shuttle. The good news is that assembly time is not an all-day affair. It is a one-off ritual that, once performed, yields a more efficient game nights going forward.

Here are practical tips that make assembly smoother:
- Start by laying out all pieces and confirming you have a stable work surface. A dining table that wobbles will turn your assembly into a tense, slow-motion comedy.
- Identify the base trays first and align them by color or shape; the design typically uses color-coded slots to reduce confusion. Trust the color cues; they are there for a reason.
- Don’t force pieces. If something snaps into place with a gentle press but requires a little patience, breathe, shift a micro-angle, and try again. Forcing parts can lead to stress marks that you will regret during your first return to the board.
- Use a light magnetic mat or a soft cloth when placing difficult tokens. This prevents chasing tokens across the floor when a sneeze-turned earthquake hits your gaming room.

The moment you finish the assembly and close the lid, you feel a sense of reward. It is not the same as finishing a campaign, but it is the soothing satisfaction of watching a complicated system organize itself. You are not merely storing components; you are performing modular maintenance for your tabletop hobby.
