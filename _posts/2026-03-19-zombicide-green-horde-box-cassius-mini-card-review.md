## How It Plays — Setup, Turns, and Tactical Tidbits

Setup is standard Zombicide fare: organize the new cards, insert tokens where they belong, punch the plastic mini out of its sprue (carefully), and remind your group that this is a cooperative game where you will fail together with style. The Cassius box doesn’t radically change the pace of setup; rather, it enhances the early rounds with a hero who can carve a path through a small pack of greens if you position him properly and roll the right die results. If you’re new to Zombicide, Cassius will be a fun first hero to learn with, since his toolkit encourages a mix of positioning, planning, and just a pinch of daring.

On the turn-by-turn level, you’ll notice Cassius often asks you to think in terms of channeled action economy: more movement, more attacks, and a few prompted choices that force you to weigh risk against reward. The new cards reward allies who coordinate. The best moments come when your team executes a two-step plan: Cassius engages the front ranks to prevent zombies from surging past, while a ranged hero or a melee-focused ally clears the way for a decisive strike on the boss or the more dangerous elite monsters.

Be prepared for occasional misfires, of course. Zombicide is built on the dice Gods and their capricious moods. The joy of Cassius is that even during a bad round, there’s a narrative reward — an epic fail that turns into a hilariously heroic stand that your group will retell for weeks. If you’ve ever done a “one more round” marathon, this box gives you plenty of excuses to squeeze in just one more round to see whether Cassius can pull off a miracle with a green horde nipping at his heels.
