## Strategy tips and house rules for rocking the table

- Read the room, then read the cards. The real skill is not just knowing what your card does, but anticipating what your friends think you’ll do next.
- Don’t be afraid to fake confidence. A well-timed grin can sell a bluff and tilt others into misreading your plan.
- Use disruption cards strategically. If you can force a turn where your opponents are forced to reveal information or commit to a risky action, you’ll often come out ahead.
- Consider tempo. Sometimes you want a quick burst of chaos; other rounds you want to slow-roll and let every misplay become a comedy of errors.
- Build a small “bluff library.” Include a couple of cards you’ll pretend are strategic power moves, then reveal them at just the right moment to trigger the table’s laughter and confusion.
- Custom rules for a longer campaign: add a “boss round” after a certain number of explosive rounds where everyone gets a special power card to balance the chaos and restore equilibrium.

Tip: If you want to give your group a fresh challenge, try a variant that reduces the number of auto-explosive cards or introduces a “team play” night where pairs team up for a synchronized set of strategies.
