### Durability and cleaning in the real world
We tested spills that represent the classic culinary CSI: coffee, red wine, and a suspicious smoothie with a texture your taste buds wouldn’t trust. The polypropylene helps with stain resistance; the polyester contributes to softness. For most household spills, a quick blot with a clean towel and a mild detergent solution is all you need. Bleach is a no-go; aggressive scrubbing is a no-no; and be gentle with the medallion’s delicate detailing to avoid flattening the edges.

A good practice is to vacuum on a low setting, using a brush attachment if you must—but avoid aggressive agitation directly over the medallion. The goal is to preserve the crisp linework while keeping fuzz to a minimum. If you discover a tiny pedestal of lint building up in the pattern, don’t panic; a quick pass with a clean vacuum head and a lint roller will usually do the trick.

### Maintenance tips for life with humans and pets
- Vacuum regularly on a gentle setting to prevent pilling and maintain the pattern’s sharpness.
- Treat spills promptly: blot, don’t rub; work from the outside inward to avoid ring stains.
- Use a rug pad underneath to prevent slipping, extend life, and add a touch of friction that stops your cat from turning it into a launch pad.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear, especially in high-traffic zones like entryways or under your coffee table where the legs of furniture constantly collide.
