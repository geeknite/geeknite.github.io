## Installation and Maintenance

### Quick Start: What You’ll Need

- A clean, dry counter surface (detergent or alcohol wipe will do; just avoid oils).
- A measuring tape or ruler, and a soft squeegee or card for smoothing.
- A utility knife or scissors for trimming, plus a little patience for perfect lines.
- Optional: protective gloves if you’re worried about fingerprints on the edges during installation.

### Step-by-Step: Measuring, Cutting, and Laying

1) Measure the length you need for each strip run. If you’re doing a long counter, you might want to pre-cut segments so you don’t end up with awkward butt joints mid-run. 
2) Lay the adhesive face down, align carefully with the edge or center stripe, and slowly press from one end to the other. The trick is to avoid trapping air bubbles; if one appears, lift slightly and re-seat.
3) Trim any excess with a sharp knife. A clean cut matters as much as the color choice; sloppy edges undermine the whole effect. 
4) Smooth out again with a card or squeegee to make sure there’s full contact. You want a seamless finish that invites customers to feel the counter’s surface rather than fear it.
5) If your package includes a small protective film, remove it after the strips are firmly in place. The result should be a sharp, professional look that’s still friendly enough for a coffee break chat with a guest.

### Step-by-Step: Maintenance Tips

- Clean with a mild, non-abrasive cleaner. Harsh chemicals can dull the matte finish or degrade adhesives over time. 
- Avoid heavy solvents near the edges, especially if the counter surface has a delicate veneer underneath. 
- If you must reposition, do it gently and reapply the adhesive for the best odds of a clean re-seal. 
- Periodically check the edges; a lifted edge can start to peel, and once it starts, the cycle of re-seating can become a mini hobby. Not ideal for a busy front desk.

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

- Air bubbles: small bubbles can be popped with a pin and pressed flat. Larger bubbles may indicate uneven surface; re-smoothing from the center outward helps.
- Misalignment: if you discover you’re off by a few millimeters, lift gently from the nearest joint and re-seat. Don’t panic; it’s not a dramatic moment in a sci-fi movie, despite the dramatic lighting.
- Adhesive residue: if you remove strips later and see residue, use a gentle adhesive remover or rubbing alcohol with a soft cloth. Test on a hidden corner first to ensure it won’t damage the surface.
