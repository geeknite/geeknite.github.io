## Visuals and performance
### Visual style
Infinite thrives on neon physics and crisp vector shapes. The color palette is intentionally saturated to differentiate puzzle zones, making it easier to identify interactive elements even when your brain tries to overclock. On Switch OLED, the glow pops in a way that makes you want to pause and gaze for a moment. The art direction is cohesive, charming, and a little bit cheeky in a way that suits the game’s sense of humor.

### Performance on Switch
On handheld, the game targets 720p with a steady 30fps, which is typical for a puzzle game of this scale on Switch. Docked, the resolution may climb a tad, but the real performance win is how the game maintains responsiveness during fast puzzle sequences. The occasional frame hitch is not a deal-breaker; it is the small price paid for a dense, colorful world that constantly presents new ideas. For players who demand a rock-solid 60fps, this title is not the ideal candidate, but for puzzle lovers who crave atmosphere, it nails the rhythm.

### Visual polish in the Tanya levels
The exclusive levels push the visuals into a more intense orbit. They use layered parallax, reflective surfaces, and a couple of new shader tricks to create a sense of depth that feels more immersive than many indie titles. The level design leans into the mind-bending motif, and the result is a delightful kaleidoscope that remains legible even when your brain tries to rearrange the space into something unrecognizable.

## Gameplay and design
### Core mechanics
The core puzzle loop in Infinite is about recognizing patterns, rearranging space, and timing actions to line up effects across multiple layers of space-time. The game uses a compact control scheme that feels precise yet forgiving when you misjudge a sequence by a fraction of a second. The puzzles reward experimentation; if you push a mechanic to its limit and fail, the game gently guides you back to a more manageable approach rather than forcing a brutal retry.

### Tanya Edition mechanics
The two exclusive levels introduce two new mechanics. One leans into gravity manipulation, letting you flip orientation and re-map pathways to create alternate routes. The other introduces a lightweight time twist that requires you to coordinate actions across two mirrored versions of the same map. The combination yields a sense of wonder rather than frustration, especially when you realize that the solution to one puzzle depends on understanding the previous puzzle’s logic.

### Difficulty curve and pacing
The game preserves a fair increment of difficulty. You are never too far from a checkpoint or a hint; the designers have built in a steady climb that feels earned rather than forced. The pacing is a highlight; exploration feels balanced with goal-oriented puzzle solving. The Tanya Edition adds pockets of challenge that feel like bonus chapters rather than new grind, which is exactly what a good expansion should be.

## Story and characters
The narrative threads are not laid out as a traditional arc but braided through environmental storytelling and a handful of dialogue moments. The Tanya character acts as a meta-navigator who occasionally interrupts to offer witty commentary about puzzles and about the act of solving itself. The writing is lively, sometimes tongue-in-cheek, and occasionally surprisingly poignant. The result is a narrative texture that complements the puzzle design without getting in the way of gameplay.

## Audio and atmosphere
The soundtrack threads neon synth tracks with sparse percussion, creating a soundscape that matches the visuals without becoming overpowering. The audio cues are well-timed to puzzle interactions; solving a level often triggers a triumphant arpeggio that serves as both a reward and a hint that you are progressing correctly. The ambient background tracks maintain the atmosphere and emphasize the sense of stepping through different dimensions or zones within the game.

## Accessibility and quality-of-life features
The game includes a set of accessibility options that is commendable for a puzzle title on Switch. Color-blind modes, adjustable subtitles, and scalable UI elements make the game more approachable for players with different needs. The control scheme is straightforward, and the game offers optional hints that can help players who get stuck for longer than they'd like. The experience is accessible without diluting the complexity of the puzzles, which is a tricky balance to achieve.

If you were hoping for substantial power upgrades or a longer campaign, you may feel the sting of the price. In Geeknite terms, this edition lands in the Goldilocks zone for puzzle fans: not too cheap to feel lazy, not too expensive to feel unfair.

### Longevity and replayability
Puzzle games have often rewarded careful replays with new insights. This edition adds a couple of new routes and a different perspective on the same challenges, which encourages second and third playthroughs. The Tanya levels become a nice incentive to revisit earlier puzzles with the new mechanics in mind, which is the sign of good design.
