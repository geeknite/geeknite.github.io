# City of Mist: New Mythos - PDF Review

![New Mythos Cover](/assets/images/city-of-mist-new-mythos-cover.jpg)

If you’re a GM who thinks your desk could use more fog machines, more neon, and more tragic umbrellas, City of Mist: New Mythos is the PDF you’ve been praying to the mythic gods of tabletop gaming for. It’s a supplement that grandeurs its own concept while insisting it’s not here to replace the core game so much as to whisper in your ear, Shogun-style, about a broader world where myths aren’t just costumes but living, breathing plot devices. In short: New Mythos doesn’t just add content; it adds a new lens for storytelling in the City of Mist universe. And yes, it ships with enough art to make your printer cry and your printer’s printer cry louder.

This review assumes you’re familiar with the core game and its basic concepts: players as nebulous, neon-lit “Masks” embodying real-world myths in modern times, with investigative tones, mythic powers, and an existential vibe that pairs well with late-night pizza and questionable life choices. If you’re new here, welcome to the city where every mystery wears a glowing hat and every neon sign seems to whisper, hey, you’ve got superpowers, don’t you want to use them to polish a myth you didn’t ask for?

## What is New Mythos, anyway?
New Mythos is a PDF expansion that leans into the idea that myths aren’t static archetypes but living, mutable forces that can be invoked, negotiated, and sometimes negotiated into new forms. It isn’t merely a toolbox for extra powers; it’s a reimagining of how myths operate within the City of Mist setting. The PDF introduces new mythological themes, a fresh suite of characters, new factions, and a set of GM-friendly mechanics designed to keep table flow intact while giving players and GMs more ways to brainstorm story arcs. Think of it as a mythic expansion pack that’s less “Add more cards” and more “Add more story hooks disguised as laws of magic that you broke and then rewrote.”

## The pack-in basics you’ll notice right away
- A robust 80–120 page range, depending on print edition and how aggressively you print the art in color. In digital form, the file size is respectable and the bookmarks line up with the table of contents like a well-behaved choir.
- A snappy, neon-drenched aesthetic that matches the core game's vibe but with its own distinct mood: louder, a touch more cosmic, and a pinch more philosophically chaotic.
- A refined layout that respects the player’s need to skim: clear section breaks, generous margins for annotations, and accessible typography that doesn’t punish your eyes after a single read-through.

If you’ve enjoyed City of Mist core and its well-paced, mystery-driven structure, New Mythos delivers a similar cadence with a few more cosmic spices thrown into the cauldron. It’s not a “reboot” of the system by any stretch; it’s a revisionist chorus that fits into the same chorus line, amplifying certain notes so the whole song feels bigger without sacrificing its recognizable identity.

## Layout, art, and production quality
### Layout and production quality
The PDF follows a familiar City of Mist cadence: a clean, readable layout with two-column text blocks during longer sections and a single-column breezy style for scenarios and GM tips. The font choices are bold enough to be legible but not so loud they punch you in the eyeballs after three hours of reading; the spacing is generous, which helps when you’re flipping between rules and campaign seeds.

Hyperlinks feel intentional and stable, with a robust set of internal anchors that let you jump between power classifications, myth classes, and the new lore anchors. The PDF uses bookmarks and an index that’s genuinely useful, especially when you’re hunting for that one seed you promised your players “will totally work in Act 2.” It’s not a perfect document—no RPG supplement ever fully escapes the inevitable errata bounce—but it’s the kind of product you can skim, annotate, and return to without feeling like you’re wrestling a dragon with a broken page binding.

### Visual design and art direction
The cover art pops in a way similar to the core book but with a distinct color palette that feels more electric, less noir, and more mythic-electric. Inside, there are illustrations that evoke both ancient iconography and postmodern signage—think Greek tragedy meets cyberpunk billboard. The balance between text and art generally favors readability, which is a strong plus for GMs who need to absorb content quickly during table time. A few pieces are wordless but emotionally charged enough to spark a scene idea, which is exactly what you want in a supplement that promises “New Mythos” as a core feature.

### Accessibility and printer-friendly aspects
New Mythos is designed with accessibility in mind, featuring alt-text on the images and a printer-friendly version that strips out some color to reduce ink use. For players with color vision considerations, the contrast is generally sufficient, though some of the neon-on-neon combinations can be a little dazzling in a dim gaming room. If you’re printing at home, consider the black-and-white mode for the interior art; the imagery still lands, and your printer saves toner, which makes your wallet sigh with relief.

## Content overview: what’s actually inside
Here’s the part where we break down the meat into digestible chunks, without losing the metaphorical bones. New Mythos isn’t just a bag of spells; it’s a rethinking of how myths function inside a modern metropolis of hidden heroes and shadowed teeth.

### New Mythos Frameworks
New Mythos introduces a set of thematic axes for mythic forces: the Echo, the Weave, the Primordia, the Liar, and the Archive (names and exact mechanics kept intentionally mysterious to protect the GM who wants to riff). Each axis offers a unique flavor of power and a corresponding set of prompts for story arcs. Rather than forcing players into a fixed mold, these frameworks encourage player-driven myth evolution. The GM toolkit invites improvisation and also provides guardrails so you’re not deploying random miracles that erase your city’s tension with a single roll.

### Power mechanics and resource management
If you’ve played City of Mist, you’ll recognize the core idea: powers are narrative currency, not just mechanical boosts. New Mythos adds layers, such as limited-use mythic sparks and a “web of consequences” mechanic that makes outcomes feel consequential without punishing players for making bold narrative choices. The result is less “I do a bigger thing now” and more “I do a thing, and the world responds in unexpected ways, which then becomes the seed of Act II.”

### Faction and factionless play options
You’ll find a fresh mix of factions and factions-adjacent organizations that exist both to oppose and cooperate with players. The book emphasizes the idea that myths can belong to groups, institutions, or even ambient city environments. This opens up opportunities for long-form city-wide stories, where the arc is less about a single villain and more about a city-wide mythic reorganization. It’s storytelling scale without requiring a 500-page campaign bible.

### New NPCs and mythic archetypes
There are a handful of new archetypes with evocative names and ready-to-run personality hooks. They’re designed to slot into ongoing campaigns or to serve as the seed of a fresh mystery arc. Each archetype comes with a short narrative hook, a set of signature moves, and a few “moral quandary” prompts that GM’s can nuzzle into a session’s pacing. If you enjoy running one-shot mysteries, you’ll find the seeds in New Mythos particularly suited to compact, mood-forward sessions.

### Sample plot seeds and micro-adventures
The PDF doesn’t just drop you into “mystery town; figure out myth” and leave you to improvise blindly. It includes micro-adventures designed to slot into your existing City of Mist campaigns. These seeds often start with a mythic anomaly and branch into personal stakes for the Masks. They’re flexible enough to adapt to your table’s vibe, whether that vibe runs darkly dramatic or playfully surreal.

## How to actually run this at the table
### Integrating new mythos into an ongoing campaign
- Start with a single seed: pick a myth axis and a single event that triggers a city-wide ripple. Let the players decide what myths manifest in response and how they align with their Masks.
- Use the web of consequences to map the fallout. Don’t rush to a boss fight; let the city bear the marks of what’s been invoked.
- Rotate GM POVs to give players the sense that the world is bigger than their party, which is essential for the mythic feel City of Mist aims for.

### Player guidance and character fit
- If your players want to lean heavy into roleplay, New Mythos provides hooks for moral ambiguity and dramatic irony. Use the new mythic threads to create moments where choices have semantic weight, not just mechanical outcomes.
- For younger players or newcomers, pick a safe seed and a single power line to integrate gradually. The book isn’t asking you to “go all in” in one session; it’s designed for a slow burn that reveals more myth as your table grows comfortable with the tone.

## Mechanics: how it plays with the core system
New Mythos respects the City of Mist core’s emphasis on player-driven storytelling, while offering a few rule-of-thumb adjustments that keep things fresh without turning the system into a spreadsheet hellscape. The punchy idea you’ll likely notice soon is that mythic power becomes more context-aware. It’s not just “I have a power” but “this power shifts depending on the mythic weather and the city’s mood.” That subtle shift changes how you narrate scenes, what kind of obstacles you put in front of the Masks, and how consequences cascade through the arc.

A few notable design decisions worth highlighting:
- Context-driven powers: Abilities scale or shift based on the current myth axis in play, encouraging players to talk with the GM about tone and setting during downtime between acts.
- Temporal nudges: Some powers respond to time-based prompts (what happens as the city’s myth evolves over days or weeks). It’s a small addition that yields big narrative payoff.
- Consequence mapping: The new web-of-consequences mechanic keeps outcomes from feeling like a binary success/failure. Instead, you track tangles in the myth’s fabric that can be exploited later for callbacks and reversals.

If you’re the kind of GM who loves to weave in long-term city-scale arcs, these mechanical ideas will feel like a natural extension of your play style rather than an unnecessary detour.

## GM tools: seeds, scenes, and scaffolds
New Mythos includes a curated toolkit for GMs who want to run a session that feels cinematic but still grounded in the everyday urban fog. Expect:
- Scene templates that guide you through beat progression with “mythic prompts” that hint at a theme you’re exploring.
- A library of cinematic beats you can drop into acts, including moral dilemmas, mythic reveals, and twist-heavy climaxes.
- A robust set of world-building prompts that help you expand the city’s mythic understructure without needing a fresh map for every session.

These tools are designed with a “choose-your-own-adventure meets improv theatre” vibe, which should resonate with players who enjoy narrative density and character-driven choices.

## Example scenarios: seeds you can steal tonight
Here are a few quick sample seeds you can test-drive in your next session. They’re designed to be drop-in ideas that you can flesh out with your group’s tone in about 30–60 minutes of table time:
- The Echoing Alley: A forgotten alley echoes back players’ unspoken fears, allowing them to glimpse a possible future if they push through the fear rather than embracing the pain. Great for a tense, character-centric scene.
- The Archive Heist: A hidden wing of the city library holds a mythic artifact that speaks only in riddles. The party must decipher a series of clues, each revealing a layer of the city’s mythic history.
- The Weave Street Festival: A city festival celebrates myths in a way that distorts reality. Players must navigate crowds, competing factions, and a few power shifts that threaten to tear the festival apart.
- The Liar’s Pact: A charismatic NPC bargains with the Masks to avert a disaster, but the pact comes with a moral cost that echoes across the party’s personal narratives.

These seeds are designed to be adaptable to any city you’ve already built, with room to insert your own NPCs, factions, and local color.

## The geeky, fun bits: what makes it sing
- The vibe check: New Mythos leans into the same neon-noir vibe City of Mist is known for, but it doesn’t seem to be coasting on past glories. It tries new mythic textures and flirtations with cosmic ambiguity that feel fresh without shouting gimmicks.
- The player-facing material is balanced. There are new idea pools that avoid over-bloating the character creation process; if you’re someone who loves constructing Masks from a blank slate, you’ll find “new mythos story hooks” both inspiring and practical.
- The GM-facing materials are practical without being clumsy. It’s easy to riff on the seeds, and you’ll also get a few ready-to-run scenes that don’t require extra prep beyond gathering the players’ current Mask lineup.

If you’re the type who loves to customize your city, the PDF’s scaffolding is friendly enough to adapt, but dense enough to keep experienced GMs engaged. It’s a product that respects your time while inviting you to dream bigger about the city’s mythic fabric.

## How it compares to the base game and other supplements
Compared to the core City of Mist book, New Mythos feels more ambitious in its macro-myth approach. It expands the mythic ecology of the city rather than just listing new powers or new cases. It’s not a replacement for the core rules; rather, it’s a companion that invites you to think about myths as social forces, as consequences, and as ongoing narrative engines.

Against other supplements (if you’ve collected a few like this one), New Mythos stands out in two ways:
- Narrative density without rules bloat: You get more storytelling hooks without drowning in options.
- Thematic coherence: The new mythos themes feel like they belong in the same universe rather than a disparate set of “cool ideas” that would require a cross-world gloss to fit in.

If you’ve enjoyed the mood and pacing of the base game but felt something was missing in terms of world-shaping potential, New Mythos should scratch that itch in a satisfying, not overbearing, way.

## Pros and cons at a glance
Pros:
- Rich mythic texture that feels new but familiar
- Strong GM tools for quick-session use
- Elegant, non-bloated mechanics that respect player agency

## Final verdict
If you’re already dancing with the neon gods of City of Mist and you’re looking to expand your city’s mythic architecture, New Mythos is a smart buy. It respects the core game’s tone while offering a fresh lens through which to view the Masks and their relationships with the city. It’s not a universal game-changer, but it’s a meaningful expansion that can unlock a dozen sessions’ worth of story potential with minimal stress.

If you’re starting from scratch, you can absolutely use New Mythos as a jump-off point to seed a campaign, but you’ll likely be richer if you pair it with at least the core book and perhaps a couple of other supplements to create a fully realized city-wide mythos landscape.
