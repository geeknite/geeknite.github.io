## What is New Mythos, anyway?

New Mythos is a structured expansion that treats myths as living forces rather than fixed backdrops. It retools how myths operate within the City of Mist world, giving you new thematic axes, fresh archetypes, and a set of GM-friendly tools designed to keep table flow intact while expanding the possibilities for storytelling. It is a mythic expansion pack that emphasizes story hooks disguised as laws of magic, and then invites you to bend those laws without breaking the mood of the city. If the core book is the tune, New Mythos is a new arrangement that lets you remix the chorus without losing the hook.

### The core conceit, reframed

New Mythos leans into the idea that myths are not static archetypes locked behind a GM screen. They are dynamic forces that respond to the city’s mood, the Masks' choices, and the evolving tension of the plot. Think of it as myth mechanics that react to your play style: when the city seems to breathe in dread, a myth axis can tilt toward caution or toward cosmic expansion. When a Mask leans into a moral choice, the mythic weather shifts in response. The result is a playground where the neon rain is not just ambience but a meta-game of consequences and tonal shifts.

## Content overview: what’s actually inside

New Mythos isn’t a bag of new powers; it’s a rethinking of myth in the modern metropolis. ### New Mythos Frameworks

At the heart of New Mythos are thematic axes that define the big mythic forces in the city: Echo, Weave, Primordia, Liar, and Archive. Each axis offers a flavor profile, a collection of prompts for arcs, and a few sample seed lines to help you grease the wheels of your session without heavy prep. The practical payoff is clear: you can pivot a campaign to emphasize personal mythos development, city-wide mythic economics, or a stubborn arc of mythic consequence, depending on what your group needs at the moment. The mechanics encourage player-driven evolution while giving the GM guardrails to keep the city from spiraling into a meaningless buffet of miracles.

### Power mechanics and resource management

Powers remain narrative currency, but New Mythos adds layers that reward careful orchestration. Expect limited-use myth sparks, a web of consequences that creates a visible thread through acts, and time-based prompts tied to the city’s evolving mood. The result is a world where a bold choice does not simply yield a bigger effect; it ripples through scenes, affecting obstacles, allies, and even the city’s infrastructure. You’ll feel the shift in tone as a power travels with the city through days or weeks of play, and you’ll start to map how a single decision can become a campaign hinge.

## How to actually run this at the table

### Integrating new mythos into an ongoing campaign

- Start with a single seed: pick a myth axis and trigger a first event that ripples through the city. Let players decide what myths manifest in response and how they align with their Masks. The GM should lean into causality: what is the mythic reaction to a single choice, and how does that reaction alter next session expectations?
- Map the fallout with the web of consequences: avoid rushing to a boss fight. Let the city bear the marks of invocation, in ways that create future hooks and tension for Act II and beyond.
- Rotate GM POVs to remind players that the world is bigger than their party. When the same city holds court over several sessions, you want a sense that different voices talk about the same myth from different vantage points. This is essential for the mythic feel City of Mist aims for.

### Player guidance and character fit

- For roleplay-driven groups, New Mythos provides hooks for moral ambiguity and dramatic irony. Use the new mythic threads to layer narrative weight that resonates with the Masks' choices and relationships. The goal is semantic weight rather than pure mechanical advantage.
- For newcomers or younger players, start with a single seed and a single power line to weave into a session. New Mythos is designed for a slow burn that reveals more myth as the table grows confident with the tone.

## Mechanics: how it plays with the core system

New Mythos respects the core’s emphasis on player-driven storytelling while offering a few rule-of-thumb adjustments that keep things fresh without turning the system into a spreadsheet nightmare. The central idea is context-driven power: abilities shift based on the active myth axis and the city’s mood during a scene. This makes narrating scenes more reactive and helps catalysts feel earned rather than handed to the party on a silver neon platter.

A few design decisions worth highlighting:
- Context-driven powers: Abilities scale or shift depending on the current myth axis in play. This invites players to coordinate with the GM about tone and setting during downtime between acts.
- Temporal nudges: Time-based prompts that respond to how the city’s myth evolves can influence which powers get stronger, what myths awaken, and which consequences surface as the story progresses.
- Consequence mapping: The web of consequences helps avoid binary outcomes. You track tangles in the mythic fabric, which become callbacks and reversals that reward players for risk-taking and creative problem solving.

If your style includes long-form city-scale arcs, these mechanics feel like a natural evolution rather than a detour from what makes City of Mist fun.

The tone here is choose-your-own-adventure meets improv theatre, designed for groups who enjoy dense narrative and character-driven choices without drowning in stats.

## Example scenarios: seeds you can steal tonight

These seeds are written to be drop-in ideas that you can flesh out with your table’s vibe in under an hour. They’re flexible and friendly to different playstyles, but they sing best when you lean into the Masks and their personal stakes.
- The Echoing Alley: A forgotten alley that echoes players fears, providing a glimpse of a possible future if the party refuses to face the fear.
- The Archive Heist: A hidden wing of the city library shelters a mythic artifact that speaks only in riddles. The party must decipher clues that reveal a layer of the city’s mythic history.
- The Weave Street Festival: A city festival that distorts reality, turning crowds into living mythic nodes. The party must navigate a shifting social landscape and a power struggle that could fracture the festival’s magic.
- The Liar’s Pact: A charismatic NPC negotiates with the Masks to avert a disaster, but the pact demands a moral cost that echoes across the party’s personal stories.
- The Titan at the Crossing: A mythic force emerges from the traffic grid, forcing the party to decide whether to let the city absorb a new legend or to push back and risk side-effects on the civilian population.

These seeds can be tailored to your city, your Masks, and your preferred emphasis on mystery, horror, or surreal whimsy.

## How it compares to the base game and other supplements

Compared to the core City of Mist, New Mythos leans more toward macro-myth ecology than a longer catalog of powers. It invites you to treat myths as social forces, as narrative consequences, and as engines for ongoing storytelling, rather than mere backdrops for investigations. It is not a replacement for the core rules; it’s a companion that nudges you toward thinking bigger about the conflicts that shape the city through mythic storytelling.

Against other supplements, New Mythos shines in two ways: narrative density without forced complexity, and thematic coherence that makes the mythic ecosystem feel like it belongs in the same city rather than a disparate collection of cool ideas. If you crave world-building depth that still moves at table speed, you will find New Mythos a collaborative partner for your city’s evolution.
