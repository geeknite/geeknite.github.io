Introduction
-----------
If you like your fantasy medievals served with a generous helping of biting undead and a dice-powered sense of doom, Zombicide: Black Plague is probably your jam. Think cooperative dungeon crawl meets chaotic zombie action, but with more cheese wheels (and yes, we mean the edible kind, because you’ll need carbs to fight a swarm of the shambling undead). The base game sets you up with survivors, weapons, and a city overrun by the undead apocalypse. Then the expansions swoop in like a caped vampire with better posture, offering new maps, more zombies, and enough mission variety to make your group forget that your coffee budget is a crime against modern living.

In this guide, we’re going to break down the core rules, walk through the structure of quests, and dive into the Wulfsburg expansion—what it adds, how it changes the game, and whether it’s worth dragging across your dining room table on a rainy Saturday. If you’ve never touched a Zombicide box, you’re going to want a snack handy and a friend who will agree to help you split the loot. Ready? Let’s raid the crypts of Wulfsburg.

What is Zombicide: Black Plague?
-------------------------------
Zombicide: Black Plague is a cooperative board game for 1–6 players where you and your band of survivors attempt to complete an objective while fending off hordes of zombies. The “Black Plague” theme adds a darker, more grim vibe to the humor of watching your survivor in plate armor charge into a couple dozen pawns of undead with nothing but a dull sword and a prayer. The core loop is simple, but the game scales in chaos as more zombies flood the board and the board tiles reveal new corners of the city.

In the base game, you’ll get a handful of survivors (each with a unique starting item), a deck of weapons, armor, and other gear cards, and a stack of zombie cards that spawn the horde. You’ll roll dice to perform actions, move across tiles, fight whether you’re the hero or the last one standing, and if you’re lucky, you’ll complete a mission before the city becomes a crypt with a view.

What’s in the Box (and what you actually need to know)
---------------------------------------------------
- Survivors: Each has a class feel—warrior, archer, mage-adjacent, or a light tank with a heart of something edible—and a starting weapon. They level up as they complete quests, which unlocks more actions and better gear.
- Zombies: Two flavors—walkers and runners. Runners hate your ankles and love a good sprint into your face. As the apocalypse progresses, you’ll encounter special zombies that function like mini-bosses, forcing you to think strategically rather than brute-forcing your way through the grid.
- Weapons and gear: Swords, axes, bows, holy crap-this-was-on-sale-for-a-reason items, and armor to keep you alive long enough to regret your life choices.
- Mission/Quest cards: Central to the replayability. Quests are your objectives, and there are more than enough to guarantee you’ll never run out of goals on a rainy weekend.
- Board tiles and setup: You’ll assemble a map from modular tiles that can be rearranged for infinite variations. Because we all know the best way to stay sane is to pretend you’ve never played this map before.

The Core Rules in a Nutshell
----------------------------
Zombicide is purposely a little chaotic, which is part of the charm. The rules balance accessibility with enough depth to keep the power-gamer in your group happy. Here’s the abridged version you’ll actually need to know to survive your first session:

Turn order and basic actions
- Turns alternate between players. Each turn, a survivor can take up to two actions. Actions include moving, searching for loot, opening doors, drawing loot cards, or attacking nearby zombies.
- Movement: Each square is a city block (or a corridor, depending on how dramatic the map looks). You can move a number of spaces equal to your speed, and you’ll usually fight any zombie that ends up in your square with you.
- Attacking: You’ll roll dice based on your weapon. The dice have different results (hits, critical hits, and blanks). You want hits, but you’ll also be counting “survival” value on your gear to see how much damage you can soak.
- Zed wave mechanics: Zombies spawn on the edges of the board or through special spawn cards that trigger as you complete certain tasks. The trick is to manage the crowd and not get cornered like a jack-in-the-box at a candlelight dinner party.
- Leveling up: When you complete quests or clear certain objectives, your survivors gain XP and level up, unlocking better weapons and more actions. The power curve is satisfying, but beware: more power equals bigger mistakes when you misread a tile or forget to escort your healer who’s hiding behind a boulder like a tiny, terrified ninja.
- Mission objectives: Each mission has a primary objective (e.g., reach a location, retrieve a relic, escort a civilian) and often a secondary objective (scoring extra loot, killing a special zombie, surviving until the timer runs out). Completing both typically yields the best score and the best loot.

Turn structure and combat nuances
- Initiative and timing: The order of actions can matter—especially if you’ve got a rogue-ish hero who can swipe a weapon or a spell-slinging survivor who can blast a line of zombies with a spray of magic. Pay attention to who’s taking their turn first to avoid getting overrun by a distracted group while someone is still rummaging for a cure.
- Combat math: Expected results aren’t deterministic, but with a little practice you’ll learn when to press your luck and when to retreat. The dice feel fair in a grim, hellish way, which is exactly what you want when your hero’s life depends on a lucky six.
- Terrain and line of sight: Keeping track of blocked lines and the terrain effects is crucial. You’ll find yourself circling a table edge like a knight chasing a dragon purely to avoid a pack of bitey zeds blocking your path.
- Morale and agony: The game doesn’t have a formal “morale” track, but the fear factor is real. When a zombie floods a square next to your hero, you’ll feel the tension—are you going to risk a shot at the danger or search for the safer loot on the far side of the room? The tension is a feature, not a flaw, and it’s where a party’s synergy gets tested.

Quests, Campaigns, and Replayability
-----------------------------------
The heart of Zombicide: Black Plague lies in its quest cards and the campaign structure. Each mission tells you what you’re trying to accomplish, and the path to victory isn’t just a straight line. Some quests reward careful planning and stealth, others reward bold, bat-out-of-hell charges. The campaign rules allow you to carry forward survivors or carry over items from one mission to the next, simulating a long arc of consequence across a season of apocalypse.

Quest examples (flavors you’ll encounter)
- Rescue mission: Retrieve a trapped civilian from a zombie-infested hall and escort them to safety. If you fail, the civilian becomes a stubborn liability that can still fetch loot—but at a price.
- Relic search: Find a relic shard in a maze-like map and return it to a safe zone. Expect poison doors, cruel spawn patterns, and a timer that makes your heart rate spike in a way that would impress a sprinting cheetah.
- Defend the beacon: Protect a light beacon for a set number of rounds while waves of undead attempt to extinguish your hope and your lamp oil budget.

Campaign flow tips
- Plan your side quests: Not everything needs to be the main objective. A few well-placed secondary objectives can buy you extra turns, more loot, and a few more inches of space to dodge a zombie wall.
- Looting as a spice, not a meal: Loot cards add variety but also risk—some contain powerful weapons, others contain curses or bad events. Balance risk with reward, especially in the later missions where the zombies bite back harder.
- Party composition matters: A well-balanced team with a front-liner, a ranged damage dealer, a support/medic, and a utility player (lock-picking, search, or a powerful spell) will have an easier time avoiding hubris and the dogpiling of doom.

Wulfsburg Expansion: What’s New and Why It Matters
---------------------------------------------------
Wulfsburg is one of the larger expansions for Black Plague. It introduces a sprawling new map area with urban and rural zones, new monsters, new heroes, weapons, and a handful of campaign scenarios designed to give your group a longer arc than a single afternoon can sustain. If you like your zombie games with a bit of Gothic Wolf Pack energy, this is your box.

What does Wulfsburg add?
- A new city map module: Wulfsburg expands the board with three linked boards, creating a larger, more interconnected space that rewards careful exploration and smart routing. The new map design encourages backtracking, strategic positioning, and a new sense of dread as you realize you can be boxed in by a wolf-pack of zombies and a grim-looking statue.
- Eight new survivors: The expansion brings fresh faces with unique abilities that complement existing classes or offer wild new playstyles. Some heroes lean into crowd control, others into stealthy rescues, and a few are just there to look cool while critting everything into the shadow realm.
- New zombie types and boss encounters: Wulfsburg spices up the undead family with more varied threats. Expect variants that hunt in packs, feature new movement patterns, and test your group’s ability to adapt on the fly. The presence of pack tactics makes early rounds count—kiting is no longer a luxury; it’s survival.
- New loot and equipment: The Wulfsburg loot pool expands with weapons and armor that lean into the Gothic vibe—heavy axes, moonlit daggers, and armor that glows faintly with moonlight or necrotic energy. These new items often offer exciting synergy options with the base game’s kits.
- Campaign-driven structure: The expansion nudges you toward a linked campaign, with scenarios that feed into one another. Think of it as the zombie apocalypse with DLC levels, a cliffhanger, and a boss that would make a lich jealous.

How Wulfsburg changes the game feel
- Pacing: The larger map and more aggressive zombie waves push players to coordinate more carefully. You’ll want to split up, trade information, and still keep a rescue target within reach—a classic nails-on-chalkboard tension.
- Thematic tone: Wulfsburg leans into a darker, more gothic mood. The setting evokes a medieval city under siege, with old-world architecture, narrow streets, and ominous torches. It’s not just “kill zombies” anymore; it’s “protect the last vestiges of civilization.”
- Difficulty and table time: Expect longer sessions and heavier decision trees. This is not a Sunday-sit-down game for the casual crowd. If you’re playing with a full crew of eight-year-olds who memorize every rule in three minutes, congratulations; you’ve found your tribe. For the rest of us, be patient, take breaks, and enjoy the dark humor as the wolves howl in the distance.

Strategy notes for Wulfsburg
- Emphasize mobility: The new map’s corridors and nooks reward heroes who can reposition quickly. If your party lanes up like a fan, you’ll get swept by wolves and overwhelmed at the worst moment.
- Target the leaders: Some zombie variants in Wulfsburg have leadership mechanics that cause more spawn when killed in the wrong order. Prioritize high-threat targets and use area-control tactics to limit their influence.
- Manage the beacon and escape routes: Many missions hinge on defending a location or reaching an extraction point. Keep an eye on your escape path, because running away with loot is still thrilling and far safer than staying to fight a hopeless battle.
- Synergize across classes: The new heroes bring fresh combos. Pair a ranged harrier with a tanky frontline and a healer or search specialist for the sweet spot of safety and loot. If you can synchronize actions, you’ll watch zombies crumble under a well-timed tag-team attack.

Run a Wulfsburg session: practical tips
- Prep for longer play sessions: Block out extra time or plan a multi-mission weekend. Wulfsburg demands time, coffee, and perhaps a friend who is willing to veto your “one more turn” habit at the last second.
- Organize your loot and cards: The expansions add new categories of loot and equipment. Create a shared “loot pool” and avoid the classic table chaos where someone pockets three epic swords and you’re left with a rusted spoon.
- Use house rules to survive: If your group is new to Zombicide, consider dropping the zombie spawn rate by 10–20% for the first scenario. Then increase the challenge in the second mission when you all have your sea-legs. It’s all about progress, not panic.

Artwork, components, and the tactile experience
----------------------------------------------
The tactile joy of Zombicide is part of the package. The miniatures look and feel sturdy; the dice have a satisfying heft; the loot cards feel like you’re opening loot from a dragon’s hoard rather than a cereal box. The Wulfsburg expansion brings visually striking new components that feel distinct enough from the base set to justify the upgrade. The larger map tiles have a nice weight to them and the iconography—doors, traps, and terrain features—stays consistent with the base game so there isn’t a cognitive crash when you flip from a city street to a lighthouse on the same turn.

Thematic safety and accessibility
---------------------------------
Zombicide’s brutal-but-friendly issue remains: the game can get punishing. It’s built around cooperative tension rather than pure luck; a bad series of spawns can wipe out half your team in a couple of turns. If you have players who crave a cooperative “win-or-lose together” vibe, you’ll feel the payoff. If your group tends toward “I’ll reroll until I win,” you’ll find yourself re-shuffling your entire plan in AI-generated horror mood. Wulfsburg amplifies this, but in a way that many players will find rewarding—given the table is prepared, the strategy is sound, and the snacks are plentiful.

Dungeon-to-dawnbreakner: The final verdict
----------------------------------------
- Accessibility: Black Plague is approachable for new players, with a gentle ramp of difficulty as you gain experience. The Wulfsburg expansion is slightly more complex but remains accessible with an evening’s explanation. It’s ideal for players who enjoy a challenge and want to see their survivors evolve in meaningful ways.
- Replayability: The modular map system and mission cards deliver a robust replay value. Wulfsburg adds more map configurations, more heroes, and a broader set of missions. If you’re a Zombicide purist or a completionist collector, you’ll want this expansion on your shelf.
- Thematic cohesion: The Gothic-wolf vibe is a fantastic fit for Black Plague’s atmosphere. If you enjoy bleak, medieval creepiness with a dark sense of humor, Wulfsburg nails it without tipping into parody.
- Long-term value: The product is not just “more zombies.” It’s a curated extension that changes how you experience the base game, offering new strategies and a more expansive campaign arc. If you’re in for the long run, this is a solid addition.

Pros andcons summary
- Pros: deepened campaign feel, more hero diversity, richer map layout, varied zombie threats, excellent for groups who enjoy tactical planning with a dash of chaos.
- Cons: longer sessions, more setup time, potential table clutter. If you’re not into long play sessions, you might want to pace it carefully or run two shorter sessions instead of one epic siege.

Would we recommend it? Yes—with the caveat you’ll want a regular group, a decent table, and some endurance snacks. Zombicide: Black Plague is a strong base, and Wulfsburg refinements push it from “fun party game” to “epic co-op quest campaign.” If you’re ready to commit to a longer chase through a wolf-haunted city, this is the perfect time to upgrade.

Where does that leave you if you’re starting now?

- If you’re completely new: Start with the base Black Plague core set to learn the rules and get a feel for the core cycle. Once you’ve got the hang of it, add Wulfsburg to kick things into high gear and test your team’s planning capabilities.
- If you’re a returning veteran: Wulfsburg is a worthwhile expansion that introduces new strategies, more challenging encounters, and a longer-lasting campaign arc. It’s time to test your party’s mettle and your ability to manage a larger map without losing your mind.

Final recommendation

Strongly recommended for players who:
- Love a good tactical challenge with a touch of gothic fantasy.
- Enjoy player progression and mission-based storytelling.
- Have a stable gaming group that’s willing to invest in long, satisfying sessions.

And for the brave and the snack-hungry: bold you may be, but you’ll be rewarded with a memorable zombie-slaying campaign where wolves howl in the distance and your party lives to tell the tale (or at least to tell it after another round of excellent pizza).
