## How to play: the basic flow and the glorious chaos of clues

The core concept remains faithful to the original Codenames: two teams, two spymasters, a grid of word cards, and the objective to locate your agents by offering a one-word clue that ties multiple words together. The XXL Party Edition scales this up, offering more players and more potential word pairings to justify that one-liner you’re about to drop on your partner. Here’s a practical walkthrough of the flow, with the caveat that in a party setting, flow is often more of a gust than a current.

### Setup
- Create two teams and give each team a spymaster. The rest are field operatives who will place bets on whether your clue made sense or whether you’ve just revealed the alias of a villain.
- Lay out the grid of word cards. In XXL, the board feels larger and the words feel more ambitious. You’ll be testing definitions you didn’t even know you had in you.
- Each spymaster takes a pad or a notepad to track whose turn it is and what the team has guessed. The secret is to pretend like you’ve got a plan, even if your brain is screaming, “Why did we bring a philosopher to a party game?”

### Turn structure
- The spymaster gives a one-word clue plus a number (e.g., “space 3”). The number represents how many words on the board connect to the clue, not how many drinks you should take while the clue is being given. This is a common pitfall in a rowdy party environment.
- The team members discuss, debate, and optionally use an interpretive dance to signal which words they think are tied to the clue. This is where the party energy ramps up—the more elaborate the gesture, the bigger the laughs, and often the bigger the misunderstanding.
- Guesses are made one at a time, until either the team hits the agreed-upon count or hits a red/black card and ends the round with a dramatic squeal from the timer. The tension is real, and so are the collateral damages to your relationships with colleagues who believed you would be the tactical genius of the group.

XXL amplifies the hilarity of this phase because more players means more interpretations. You’ll see a team unify behind a clue only to realize two words on the board have nothing to do with the clue and everything to do with a shared pop-culture reference that no one is willing to admit they got wrong this time around. You’ll also witness the moment where a spymaster tries to keep a straight face while the team’s consensus slides into ridiculousness, which is exactly the kind of moment that becomes a memory you’ll bring out at every future party gathering.

## XXL twists: what the Party Edition brings to the table beyond bigger cards

There are a few notable differences that make XXL Party Edition more than just “the regular game, but louder.” First, the grid can be configured to accommodate more players safely. Second, the timer system feels a touch more forgiving in the XXL variant. This is not a flaw; it’s a feature that suits long game nights where you want to stretch the chaotic joy longer without dissolving into petty bickering over a single word. The larger cards and bolder typography help with readability from across the room, which matters when someone has parked themselves too far from the table and is actively trying to cheat by glancing at another team’s cards. ## Strategy: how not to sabotage your team while performing a performance worthy of a reality show

Many players come to Codenames with a pocket full of “clever” one-word clues that attempt to cover several related words at once. XXL Party Edition rewards a slightly different approach. Here are some practical strategies to maximize success without sacrificing the joy of the moment:

- Lean into shared vocabulary. The best clues often hinge on words that your team actually uses in daily life. If your team members share a love of science fiction and bad puns, you can craft clues that nod to both without making the heroes of your group cringe in embarrassment. The synergy is real.
- Avoid overloading the clue. You might be tempted to go for a five-or-six-word association, but that’s a trap. The bigger the grid, the harder it is to manage dozens of potential connections. A shorter clue with a precise link can yield more correct guesses and fewer heartbreaks.
- Use a “bridge” clue sparingly. A bridge clue is a single concept that connects two or more words that are far apart on the board. It’s a high-wire move that, when done right, earns you style points. It’s a high-risk, high-reward technique that can rescue a failing round or derail it spectacularly. If your group loves a showy display, this is your moment to perform, even if your performance ends with a chorus of “What did you just say?”
- Practice the timing. XXL’s larger group dynamics mean conversations can become a chorus of competing voices. Learn to pause, listen, and then deliver a crisp, confident clue. The moment you hesitate, the room fills with “I’ve got it!” and a hundred opinions on what the clue should mean. The trick is to manage that energy, not to suppress it entirely.
- Develop a shared language. The best teams build a small set of go-to cues that all members recognize. It could be a gesture, a look, or a particular facial expression you all agreed on during a pre-game warm-up. If your group can do that, you’ll cut down on confusion and increase laughter per round.
