## How to play with the anime edition: rules and variants

The core gameplay remains UNO: match the card’s color or number, play action cards to disrupt opponents, and use wild cards to swing the table in your favor. The anime edition keeps this skeleton intact, which makes it accessible even for newcomers. However, there are a couple of thematic twists that you’ll encounter if you want to maximize the deck’s potential for fun:

- Shonen Strike: a reimagined Skip card with a bold character painting. When played, the next player loses their turn as the art cuts in with a dramatic pose. It’s not game-changing, but the added theatricality is a mood boost.
- Mystic Draw: a playful Wild Draw Four variant that imposes a small anime-influenced twist on the penalty: draw extra cards equal to a character’s “power level” you declare before you draw. It’s a silly addition that can tilt the table in surprising ways and encourage bluffing or calling out your opponent’s anime knowledge.
- Plot Twist Wild: a Wild card that demands the player to tell a short in-universe reason why they’re flipping the table (in a light-hearted manner) before choosing the color. It’s a conversational piece and a reminder that this deck wants you to talk and laugh, not just count cards.

For players who want a more traditional UNO experience, you can simply remove the extra variants and play with standard UNO rules. The anime art still shines, and you still get the satisfying color-matching mechanic that makes UNO such a staple for game nights.
