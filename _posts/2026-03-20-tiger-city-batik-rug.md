## Design and Craftsmanship
### Visual Impact
- The pattern draws inspiration from batik's wax-resist technique, giving each area a slightly irregular edge. The tiger stripes are rendered as stylized, fractal-like shapes that look hand-made but read as curated via algorithm.
- The color palette leans into amber, saffron, burnt orange, and espresso. It is not a pastel rug—this is a rug for people who consider color palettes to be a survival skill.

### Construction
- Pile and texture: The rug uses a dense synthetic pile, which yields a plush feel underfoot while staying resilient to foot traffic and spilled drinks. The top surface is soft, but not so plush that it hides your keys when you drop them on it.
- Backing and grip: A non-slip backing helps keep the rug in place on hard floors. The backing is not a guarantee against occasional sliding on very smooth surfaces; for that, we recommend a rug pad.
- Edge finishing: The edges show a clean, tight finish that resists fraying, which is essential for a rug that might serve as a gaming mat in an improvised set piece.

### Materials and Longevity
- Materials: The rug uses a synthetic fiber composition designed to balance cost, durability, and color retention. It’s not 100% wool; the trade-off is lower price and easier maintenance.
- Dye Fastness: The batik-inspired pattern uses dyes that resist fading under typical indoor lighting. It is not recommended for direct sun-drenched rooms without a little window-dressing to keep the color from mellowing too quickly.

### Care and Cleaning
- Routine care: Vacuum on a low-suction setting; avoid a beater bar that could snag the fibers along the edges.
- Stain management: Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing. For tougher stains, use a mild detergent diluted with water. Do not use bleach, as it can alter the color balance in unpredictable ways.
