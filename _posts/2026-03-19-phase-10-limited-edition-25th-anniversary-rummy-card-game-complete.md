## The Basic Rules (Quick refresher)
Phase 10’s core mechanic is charmingly simple: you are chasing a path of ten phases, each requiring a particular combination of cards. The phases ascend in difficulty, and you must complete them in order—no jumping from phase 3 to phase 6, unless you have a mind for mischief and a sudden talent for improvisation.

On your turn you draw a card and discard one, then if you have completed the current phase you reveal it and advance to the next phase. The “phase” you are attempting changes as you progress; your hand must be flexible enough to accommodate up to ten distinct layouts. The 25th Anniversary edition doesn’t rewrite this logic; it simply wraps it in a more premium package and a few optional house-rule tweaks that can speed up the pace or extend the drama.

### Setup and turn structure
- Shuffle the deck thoroughly (the surest sign of a serious gamer is how aggressively they shuffle)
- Deal 10 cards to each player (adjust for player count)
- Create a draw pile and a discard pile
- On your turn, draw a card, optionally lay down your phase if you meet the requirement, and discard a card
- When you complete a phase, you move to the next phase and reveal the card combination for the others to see your glory

### Strategy tips
- Plan ahead: think about two or three steps down the line, not just the current phase
- Waldo your own hand: don’t rely on luck entirely; you’ll need to pick up the right cards
- Watch your opponents: remember what they need and what they’ve discarded
- Manage your risk: don’t commit to a phase that requires a color you rarely see

## Phase 1–10: An escalating ladder
Phase 1 requires a simple set of two cards; Phase 10, the final, demands a very particular arrangement, and there are color-sensitive sequences that require you to dream in shades of red, blue, green, and yellow. The novelty here is that you are playing not against the deck but against a social algorithm: how often do you overcommit to a single phase before you realize that your neighbors are quietly building a comeback? It’s a dance of deception, memory, and occasional betrayal that makes you realize you are not just playing with cards but with the emotional lives of your friends.
