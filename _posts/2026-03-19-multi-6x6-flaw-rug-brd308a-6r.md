## Materials, Care, and Longevity
### Cleaning and Maintenance
Rugs of any color and fiber type require at least a passing level of cleaning discipline. For a multi-pattern rug, you’ll want to vacuum with a gentle setting and avoid aggressive agitation, which can push loose fibers into the pattern’s nooks and crannies. Spills? Blot, don’t rub. For fabric-based messes, a mild detergent solution can be your best friend. If you’re fancy, seek a stain-removal plan that’s tested for synthetic yarns and colorfast dyes.

There are plenty of guides online about rug care; a widely cited resource is https://www.hgtv.com/shopping/rugs-care, which provides general tips that apply well here: spot-clean when necessary, avoid soaking the rug, and test cleaners on a small, inconspicuous area first. If you own pets or small children, you’ll appreciate the rug’s resilience in the face of occasional chaos—but don’t assume it’s invincible.
