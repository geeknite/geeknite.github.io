## How to play: a quick run-through

If you’ve played standard Exploding Kittens, the NSFW edition follows a similar rhythm with extra flavor in the jokes. If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, don’t panic; this is a short learning curve, not a trek through a maze of legal disclaimers.

- Setup: Shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand. The exact hand size varies by edition, but you’ll typically start with around 5 cards each. Place one Exploding Kitten card in the deck. The rest of the deck is shuffled and placed face-down. You’ll have a safe card pile and a draw pile that’s ready to unleash chaos.
- Objective: Avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten card, which eliminates you from the round unless you have a defuse card or a special counter-card that can avert disaster. The specific defusal mechanics differ by edition, but the core concept remains: survive longer than your friends while keeping a straight face for as long as possible.
- Turn flow: On your turn, you draw a card from the deck and play one card from your hand. Cards may do a variety of things: steal cards from others, force someone to draw more, cancel a draw, or trigger a cat-fueled catastrophe. The NSFW edition adds specific actions that lean into adult humor, while keeping the core pace brisk and chaotic.
- Cat cards and defusal: Cat cards typically let you pair up cards for special effects; some can force a reshuffle or alter the draw order. Defuse cards save you from a blow that would end your round — a comic mini-rescue that often becomes a hotline for banter at the table.
- End of round: The round ends when all players but one have exploded. The last player standing wins the round and collects a point or continues to the next round depending on the house rules you adopt. The winner might get bragging rights, and maybe a silly “NSFW Champion” nickname for the evening.

This is a social game more than a strategic MBA exercise. The delight comes from the interactions — the taunts, the bluffing, the shocking but not exactly scandalous cards, and the occasional moment where someone tries to explain why a particular joke is “just for adults” and then laughs at their own explanation.
