### Notable Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
- You do not want to over-invest in dual lands if your deck is heavily color-fixed or heavily skewed toward a single color. If your plan is a black-white discard deck, Alpine Meadow might be a solid helper but not a necessity. In such cases, you might prefer a land that more directly tilts the color ratio toward the colors you use most often.
- If you frequently mulligan for mana or you play hyper-aggressive strategies, a four-copy inclusion could slow you down slightly, as some of your opening hands will contain two or more nonessential lands. Balance your 60-card deck to ensure you still hit a reliable one-drop or two-drop on turn one or two, even if you include Alpine Meadow in four copies.
- In a deck that already has a rock-solid mana base through fetch lands or fast duals in the correct color pairings, Alpine Meadow becomes more of a luxury than a necessity. In those scenarios, you could dial down to two copies and use the other two slots for utility lands, colorless ramp, or combo enablers depending on your chosen direction.
