## Great condition notes and care tips

A great condition Mana Drain is all about the edges, corners, centering, and surface. Here are practical tips to maintain or judge a great copy:

- Edge wear: A small amount of edge wear is expected in older prints, but you should look for clean, crisp edges. A mint copy should have minimal, if any, whitening on corners.
- Centering: Centering affects not just aesthetics but also potential grading. A copy with good centering feels more balanced in your display and in photos for sale listings.
- Color fidelity: Blue in Mana Drain should pop without excessive color bleed. If the card looks washed out or the blue hues appear muddy, it can impact both the display value and how it photographs for your online store.
- Surface: Look for scratches on the surface and verify that there is no scuffing on the art border. A clean surface with a sharp image makes a big difference when you lean into the card as a focal piece of a collection.
- Foil vs non-foil: Iconic Masters typically offers foil variants as well. A quality foil can add huge visual pop but can also be more prone to wear. If your goal is display, a non-foil can be wonderfully elegant too, while a foil might demand more careful handling and storage.

Storage and display tips: keep Mana Drain in protective sleeves, avoid prolonged exposure to sunlight, and consider a humid environment that stays within safe MTG storage standards. If you plan to grade the card, use a rigid top loader and a penny sleeve, and consider the UV-filtered display case for the best balance of protection and viewability.

## Care and display recommendations for a great condition Mana Drain

- Sleeve and top loader combo: Use a fresh, high-clarity sleeve and a rigid top loader for protection when not in use. This keeps the borders pristine and the color vibrant for photos.
- Environment: Store away from direct sunlight and humidity. A climate-controlled display case can preserve the card against yellowing and edge wear.
- Handling etiquette: Minimize handling with bare hands; consider gloves if you display regularly. The fewer fingerprints, the better the surface will photograph for future sale posts or show-and-tell nights.
- Display lighting: Use neutral lighting that does not overexpose any area of the card. A small lamp or LED light that avoids UV is ideal for preserving the art and the border lines.
