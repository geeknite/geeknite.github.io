### Material quality and feel
The Cross Worlds sleeves use a standard smooth outer finish with an inner layer designed to reduce friction at the card-face. The inner surface typically uses a non-glare, slightly grippy-ish texture that helps keep your cards in place during shuffles and accelerates the dreaded “mirror-fan” that we all secretly crave when showing off a seven-card combo in front of judges. If you’re into double-sleeving (inner plain sleeve + outer art sleeve), these sleeves can work, but you’ll want to verify that the outer sleeve’s dimensions don’t lead to card bulge or misfits in shuffle. ### Practical tips for using Cross Worlds sleeves
- Double-sleeving: If you’re a hardcore meta chaser, consider using an inner standard sleeve and the Cross Worlds outer sleeve. This approach gives you extra protection for the card faces while preserving the outer aesthetic.
- Shuffling technique: Spin, peel, and slide rather than a reckless bumper shuffle. A gentle riffle or two plus a cascade is enough to keep your cards pristine and your opponents from crying into their boochi grapes.
- Storage: When not in use, store sleeves away from UV light to preserve the print and avoid color fading over time.
- Cleaning: Gently wipe with a microfiber cloth. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the internal coating. A quick weekly wipe-down helps keep the prints crisp between events.

## Care and maintenance: keeping your sleeves looking fresh
To maintain their life and keepsakes for longer, follow these steps:
- Keep sleeves dry and away from humidity that could warp card faces. A dry, cool environment is best.
- Handle with clean hands; grime is the arch-nemesis of card sleeves.
- When you notice edge wear starting to appear on several cards, consider swapping to new sleeves for consistent play, as worn edges can affect shuffling and draw reliability.
- Store in a deck box designed for DBSCG sleeves to prevent bending or curling.

## A few quick play-by-play checks you can run yourself
- Check the inner sleeve surface for smoothness; run your finger along a few cards to feel the friction. If it’s too sticky, you may want to re-sleeve or swap inner sleeves.
