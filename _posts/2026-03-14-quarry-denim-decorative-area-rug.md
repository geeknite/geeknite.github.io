### Cleaning and maintenance: how it handles real life
Cleaning is a crucial factor for any rug. The Quarry Denim design typically cleans well with regular vacuuming and spot cleaning for spills. The fiber blend is chosen to resist staining better than a pure cotton rug, yet you should still address spills promptly to avoid setting stains. For most stains, a blot-and-dab approach with a mild detergent solution does the trick. Avoid aggressive scrubbing, which can flatten pile and dull the texture. If you spill grape juice, a quick blot, then a gentle wipe with a kid-friendly cleaner, followed by air drying, keeps the color fidelity intact. For tough stains, many dealers recommend a professional cleaning every year or two, depending on usage and exposure to sunlight.

### Maintenance routine that actually works
A simple maintenance routine goes something like this: vacuum once or twice a week with a gentle setting, rotate the rug every few months to even out wear, and immediately attend to spills. If you notice edges curling, you can place a non-slip pad under the corners and give them a light press with a clean cloth to flatten. This maintenance schedule helps preserve the rug’s texture and reduces the risk of snagging on long curtains or chair legs. In our tests, a consistent routine kept the denim texture looking fresh through several months of daily living, which is a win in our book. ## Cleaning, care, and longevity tips from testing

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to prevent fiber crush. This helps retain the rug’s texture and the denim-like surface appearance.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. For liquids, avoid rubbing; dab and blot to lift the stain without pushing it deeper into fibers.
- For stubborn stains, use a mild detergent solution and test on a small inconspicuous area first. Rinse with a cloth dampened with water and blot dry.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and color maturation. This is particularly important in rooms with direct sunlight that can fade areas unevenly.
- If you have pets, consider a furniture layout that minimizes direct animal traffic on the rug during the first few weeks as fur and paw traffic might require more frequent cleaning.
