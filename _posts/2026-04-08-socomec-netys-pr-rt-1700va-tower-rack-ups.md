## Performance: Runtime, Efficiency, and Noise

Runtime is the thing nobody wants to admit they care about until the power goes out. At 50% load, you can expect a modest but usable runtime, likely in the range of 8-12 minutes on a typical 1700 VA unit. At 100% load, you’re staring at a few minutes if you’re lucky. The exact runtime depends on battery health, temperature, and whether you have LED RGB strips attached for ambiance (we do not recommend powering LEDs directly from the UPS—sharing is caring, but not for your UPS battery life).

Efficiency in ECO mode is respectable; in normal mode, the UPS should be efficient enough for typical office equipment and home labs. The Netys PR RT is designed to minimize energy waste, which is good because we all pretend to be eco-warriors until we see the utility bill.

Noise: the hum of a Good Boy. It’s audible but not disruptive at typical room conditions; under load, you’ll hear a gentle fan that never quite reaches the volume of a spaceship launch. If you’re running this in a home office, you’ll notice the fan but not the meteor shower outside your window.

## Installation Tips and Best Practices

A well-set up UPS is not a luxury; it is a life support system for your digital life. Here are a few practical tips to get the most out of Netys PR RT 1700VA without turning your office into a wire labyrinth.

### Space planning and mounting considerations
- Measure your rack depth and ensuring the unit will fit with clearance for airflow
- If you go rack-mount, use the included brackets and ensure the weight is distributed to avoid tipping
- Leave room for cable slack so you can service or replace the battery without disconnecting half the room

### Cable management
- Label cables and separate power cables from data lines to minimize interference
- Use cable trays or Velcro straps for tidy rows
- Reserve a small space for the UPS display so you can read it without leaning

### Ventilation and placement
- Do not cram the UPS into a cramped cabinet. It needs airflow to stay cool
- If you install in a closet, consider passive ventilation to keep temperatures sane

### Battery care and maintenance
- Check battery health periodically; replace when the manufacturer recommends
- Avoid exposing to extreme temperatures; ideally keep around 20-25C
- Do not run the unit fully empty; plan for scheduled shutdown to preserve battery life

### Testing and validation
- Run a battery test monthly; ensure proper shutdown scripts are working
- Validate automatic shutdown of hosts and ensure log retention
