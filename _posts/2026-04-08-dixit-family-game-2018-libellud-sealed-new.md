## How to play in a nutshell
Dixit is a storytelling game built on a simple mechanism. The rules are short, the play is long on laughter, and the pacing can swing from thoughtful to chaotic in the blink of an eye. Here is the quick refresher you can share with non-gamers or kids who want to feel like they know the secret handshake of the grownups.

### Setup
- Each player receives a few voting tokens and a hand of cards from the 84-card deck. The exact hand size depends on the number of players, but you can usually expect a handful to choose from.
- A score board is laid out, and you place the storytelling marker in the starting position. The storyteller is a rotating role across rounds.

### The turn
1) The storyteller looks at one card in their hand and gives a clue that could describe the image in a few words, a sentence, or even a short riddle. The clue should be evocative but not so obvious that the other players will clearly know which card it is.
2) Each other player chooses one card from their hand that best fits the storyteller's clue and submits it face down.
3) The submitted cards are shuffled and revealed, without showing who played which. The storytelling clue is still fresh in the storyteller's mind, so they can identify their own card.
4) All players (including the storyteller) secretly vote on which card they think is the storyteller's. Scoring happens after votes are revealed.

### Scoring and round progression
- If all players or none of them find the storyteller’s card, everyone scores or no one scores depending on the variant rules. This keeps the tension light rather than turning the game into an elaborate trust fall.
- If at least some players guess the storyteller's card, the storyteller earns points, as do the players who picked the correct card. The more players who guess correctly, the fewer points the storyteller gains, encouraging sneakier, less obvious clues.
- The first player to reach a target score, or the player with the most points after a fixed number of rounds, wins.

### What makes it family friendly
The family edition emphasizes accessibility. Clues are meant to be descriptive rather than overly cryptic, and the imagery provides visual anchors for players. For younger participants, the art can spark conversation and imagination. For adults, the game still offers room for misdirection, sly humor, and the light competition that makes family game night memorable.

### Quick strategies for players of all ages
- Clue variety over precision: a hint that connects multiple cards in the deck tends to generate more interesting choices.
- Balance between obvious and obscure: you want enough players to be uncertain but not so unsure that the votes stall.
- Read the room: if you notice a friend tends to pick ultra-creative clues, tailor your hints to nudge them toward your card without giving it away.
- Embrace the art: the visuals are not just pretty; they are the real engine of the game. Let the images guide your clues and your guesses.

## Maintenance and care of sealed cards
Staying sealed is a choice with its own quirks. The sealed state protects the cards during shipping, but long-term storage should consider humidity and temperature to avoid any warping or edge wear when the shrink wrap finally comes off. If you plan to store the game for extended periods, pouches or a light storage box along with a cool, dry reserve corner can help preserve the cards in pristine condition until you are ready to host a night of wonder.

Once you break the seal, consider a light wipe with a dry cloth if there is any dust on the surface. Card edges can accumulate fingerprints and small scuffs after heavy play, but Dixit cards are generally robust and forgiving. The artwork tends to hold up well to repeated use, as the images are mostly flat color fields with lines that invite interpretation rather than requiring intricate details to enjoy.
