As with most cotton chenille products, careful maintenance matters more than dramatic tales of cleaning. We treated light spills with a quick blot — no rubbing — to avoid setting stains. The key is to act fast, avoid saturating the rug, and always air dry completely before placing it back in service. If your household includes kids or pets prone to snack accidents, consider investing in a fabric-safe stain remover that is recommended by the rug manufacturer and test on a small hidden area first. ## Cleaning and Care
### Care guidelines and tips
Cotton chenille rugs require a balance of care and avoidance of over-wetting. We recommend:
- Vacuum regularly using a gentler setting to preserve the plush texture.
- Treat spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing.
- For light soiling, spot-clean with a mild detergent and water, then air dry completely.
- If the rug needs a deeper refresh, check the care label for machine washing instructions. If machine washing is permitted by the manufacturer, use a gentle cycle with cold water, then lay flat to dry. Avoid high heat in the dryer, which can shrink or distort the fibers.
- Rotate occasionally to promote even wear and keep the gradient from looking washed out in high-traffic pockets.

### Maintenance myths and reality
Our experience suggests the rug handles daily life better than some synthetic rivals, in part due to its reversible nature. If one side starts looking a bit sad from sun exposure or pet hair buildup, you can flip it to the other side. This not only extends the life but also keeps your space looking fresh without buying a whole new rug every few years. Of course, if you insist on dragging chairs around on the rug, you may accelerate wear. Treat it like a cherished piece of fabric art rather than a playground for furniture and you should be fine.
