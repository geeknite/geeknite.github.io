## How to play: setup and rounds in a tiny package
The Mind keeps the setup extremely light, which is a huge part of its appeal for quick sessions or game nights when you’re juggling snacks, drinks, and a chaotic group dynamic.

- Player count: 2–4 players. The game scales gracefully; the dynamics change as you add players, but the core mechanic—silent coordination—remains intact.
- Setup: Shuffle the deck (the sealed NEW copy ensures you’re not dealing with pre-loved tension). Decide on the number of rounds (The Mind runs through 12 rounds in the standard variant, but you can tailor this for shorter or longer sessions). The players sit in a circle and keep their cards hidden from each other.
- Objective: On each round, players must play their cards in ascending order without verbal cues. You’re aiming to collectively play every card in the round without missteps. Simple in concept, tricky in execution.
- Turn structure: There’s no talking, no peeking, and no signaling beyond your internal clock and your compadres’ subtle cues. A single misstep—or a misread of the group’s tempo—costs a life. If you all lose your last life, the round ends; if you manage the round without depleting lives, you move to the next round with more cards in everyone’s hands.
- End of game: Victory means you reached the final round with some lives remaining. The more you survive, the more you’ve fine-tuned your group coordination. Some groups treat this as a soft victory, others as a mission to push deeper into the paradox of silent synergy.

Key mechanic notes to remember:
- No talking is allowed; you only rely on nonverbal cues such as micro-movements, facial expressions, timing of card placements, or the lack thereof.
- If two players inadvertently hold out or place a higher card before a lower number has been played by anyone, you lose a life. The group’s tempo must be precise, not chaotic.
- The order of play is determined by the revealed numbers; you don’t reorder cards; you just place them on the table when you think the moment is right.
- Strategy isn’t about who’s smartest; it’s about who can read the room fastest and with the most patience.

For players who prefer heavy ambidexterity in decision-making (or a lot of shouting), The Mind will feel like a paradox: minimal rules, maximal social pressure. For those who enjoy low-friction, high-delivery experiences, The Mind can become an instant staple in your game-night rotation.
