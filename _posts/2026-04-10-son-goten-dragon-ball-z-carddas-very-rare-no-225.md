## Collectibility, care, and maintenance

Storing a very rare card means you’ll want to invest in proper sleeves and a rigid binder. Here are practical tips that won’t break your bank but will preserve the shine:

- Use soft card sleeves with acid-free backing boards to protect corners.
- Keep your No.225 card in a cool, dry environment away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade.
- Store in a binder insert designed for Carddas or standard trading cards to minimize bending.
- Avoid magnetic storage solutions; Carddas aren’t magnets for your metal-like memory, but they can still get magnetized by your brain’s obsession if you stare at them too long.

If you’re new to Carddas, remember that the market loves stories. A well-documented history of how you acquired your No.225 adds to its charm and, frankly, its resale appeal. Consider jotting down the card’s origin in a notebook or a digital ledger so future you (or future you’s descendants) can tell the tale without needing a truth serum.
