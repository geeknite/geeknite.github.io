## How to play (fast-forward version)

Sushi Go Spin keeps the core tempo: draft, pass, collect, score. Spin adds a few new wrinkles that are delightful without becoming a brain teaser.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal each player a hand. The number of cards per hand depends on player count, but it’s usually 5–7 cards in a 2–4 player game, with a few extra cards for larger tables.
- Place the spin wheel in the center or near a player who enjoys smuggling snacks in a lazy Susan-like fashion.
- Determine the scoring round structure (three rounds is classic; Spin sometimes adds a fourth quirky round if you’re using the Dim Sum Box EX NM extra cards).

### The Spin mechanic (the star of the show)
- After you’ve drafted your cards for the round, you spin the wheel to reveal a temporary modifier: this could be a bonus for certain sushi types, a scoring multiplier, or a “steal” option that lets you peek at another player’s hand for a moment.
- The spin adds a touch of randomness that keeps players guessing. It’s not chaotic enough to derail chosen strategies, but it’s enough to make you think twice about every swap you make.
- If you’re playing with the Dim Sum Box EX NM extras, you’ll encounter more wheel modifiers, which increases the variety of each session. It’s a good way to keep the game fresh across multiple plays without needing a full expansion.

### The drafting and scoring
- Each round, you draft a set of sushi cards to form the best scoring combos. Classic combos include Nigiri, Maki, and Tempura in traditional Sushi Go fashion. The Spin edition introduces new scoring opportunities tied to the wheel results.
- Scores are tallied after a round, using the standard Sushi Go scoring rules with a few dim sum tweaks. You’ll typically see patterns like triplets of nigiri, sets of maki rolls, or special combos unlocked by wheel modifiers.
- The round ends when the deck is exhausted or a scoring condition is met, at which point you reset and deal again for the next round.

### Strategy cheat sheet (the essentials)
- Don’t be afraid to pivot: Spin introduces momentary opportunities that can dramatically alter which cards you should keep. If your hand looks light on high-value items, lean into wheel modifiers that boost your weaker categories.
- Watch the table-wide rhythm: If someone keeps grabbing the same type of sushi, you may want to diversify to avoid letting them snowball the ending. The Spin variant rewards balance as much as it rewards spectacular single-card plays.
- Timing matters: In some rounds, delaying a certain card by one pass can position you perfectly to snap up a larger, synergistic set on the next draw. The spin helps you see those windows earlier—if you’re paying attention.

## Production notes and durability considerations

Mint condition packaging is a joy, but it’s not a magic shield against the hazards of life. Here are a few maintenance tips to keep your EX NM edition in excellent shape:
