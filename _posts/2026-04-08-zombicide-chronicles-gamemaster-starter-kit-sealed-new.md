A few practical tips for GM longevity

- Pre build your encounter cards and scenario sheets before the session. A quiet 10 minute prep can save you from a chaotic 45 minute derailment that ends with someone accidentally discovering the secret door while you were trying to explain why the city has a curfew. Prepping helps you maintain narrative momentum and reduces the risk of the table spiraling into a purely chaotic improvisation that leaves everyone with cake crumbs for brains.
- Use the GM screen to manage suspense. The screen is not just a shield for your notes; it is a tool for storytelling. Use it to control what the players see and when they see it. This adds a cinematic quality to the session that makes your players feel like they are in a televised zombie apocalypse with you as the showrunner.
- Keep track of mini goals. The better you define small victory conditions for survivors, the more meaningful the session becomes. It helps the players push forward with purpose even when the odds seem insurmountable.
