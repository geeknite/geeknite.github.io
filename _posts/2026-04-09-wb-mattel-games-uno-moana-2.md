## How to Play: A Quick Overview
If you know UNO, you know Moana 2. The core flow remains: match color or number, draw cards when you cannot, and be the first to shed all your cards. Moana 2 adds a few twists that honor the theme without making the game feel like a completely new system. The rulebook is short and friendly, perfect for introducing newcomers without lengthy lectures.

### Setup and base rules
- Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player.
- Place the remainder as the draw pile and flip the top card to begin the discard pile.
- Players take turns matching a card from their hand to the top card by color or number. If no match is possible, draw from the draw pile until a card can be played.
- Special cards in the Moana 2 deck function as color changers, draws, and direction shuffles just like standard UNO, with the Moana variants adding a touch of tropical flavor.

### The Moana twists (flavor cards and rules)
Moana 2 introduces a few flavor cards designed to liven up the table without sink-or-swimming in complexity:
- Moana Wild: Acts as a normal wild card but features tropical artwork and a small rule cue that keeps players on their toes.
- Island Card: A new endgame trigger that is activated at specific times, encouraging a strategic push when the deck runs low.
- Thematic action cards: These are designed to prompt swaps, forced draws, or direction changes with a Moana flair. They are not wildly overpowered; they lean more toward adding fun misdirection than bending the game into a pretzel.

The intent is not to revolutionize UNO but to bring a splash of theme that makes the session feel special without adding cognitive overhead. The rulebook emphasizes inclusive play styles, encouraging families with kids and casual gamers to dive in together.
