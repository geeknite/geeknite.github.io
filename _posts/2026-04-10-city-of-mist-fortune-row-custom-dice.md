## DIY: Crafting Your Own Fortune Row Experience
If you are the kind of table that loves custom accessories, Fortune Row is a fantastic springboard for a DIY session. Here is a compact guide to building your own variant of the Fortune Row concept, either as a cheaper alternative or as a template for future expansions.

### Materials and tools
- A handful of six-sided dice (preferably with a consistent feel across the set)
- Waterproof paint or enamel markers for symbols
- Fine-tip brush or a set of sticker decals if you want non-permanent symbols
- A clear protective sealant to keep the art from wearing off during table abuse
- Optional resin blanks for custom faces if you want a fully unique look

### Design principles
- Symbols first: design each die face around a symbol that speaks to a game cue (mask for identity, scales for balance/fate, compass for direction, etc.).
- Readable at a glance: keep the symbol simple, bold, and high-contrast against the die color.
- Narrative alignment: each symbol should map to a potential story beat in City of Mist rather than a generic outcome. This helps players narrate with intention.

### A quick DIY sequence
1) Sketch your symbols on paper first. It is easier to adjust on a drawing board than on a curved face.
2) Transfer to the die face using non-permanent markers to test readability. Do a quick face-down test on the table to see if it looks good from seating distance.
3) If satisfied, switch to a permanent marker or enamel; seal after the paint dries.
4) Test with your group. Pay attention to whether the symbols prompt memorable narrative moments or feel arbitrary.

### How to integrate DIY dice into your campaign
- Use your home-made faces to mark specific character cues or episode goals. It becomes a light-weight trackable resource for session arcs.
- Create a small, rotating symbol glossary that players can reference. It makes the storytelling feel collaborative and less prescriptive.
- If your table uses post-session notes, record a few lines of what each symbol triggered in that session. Over time you build a shared mythos around Fortune Row-like cues.
