## How to Play: Rules, Flow, and Flair

UNO, at its core, is simple: color and number matching, a few wild cards, some “Draw 2” penalties, and the occasional “Skip” to ruin your best-laid plan. The Toy Story Special Edition preserves that skeleton while letting the branding do the talking. Here’s what you can expect in terms of gameplay, plus notes on how the Toy Story theming might alter the mood rather than the math.

### Basic Flow
- Each player starts with a hand of seven cards. The goal remains to empty your hand first.
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number of the top discard. If you don’t have a match, you draw a card.
- Special action cards—Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four—still exist, but a Toy Story edition might introduce small, themed twists on these actions (for example, a “Buzz Mode” that reverses play when Buzz is up, or a Woody “yodel” effect to praise a lucky draw).
- When a player is down to one card, they must shout “UNO!” to avoid a penalty. The edition’s branding adds a cute voice line or character sticker when players achieve this milestone, which is a big mood booster for kids and a nostalgic cue for adults.

### Thematic Twists That Matter (Only Slightly)
Because this is a Disney Pixar Toy Story edition, you’ll notice the cards and card backs leaning into the story’s vibe rather than introducing an entirely new gameplay mechanic. Thematic twists can include:
- Character art that aligns with each color or number, adding personality to the experience without complicating strategy.
- Thematic Wild Cards or “Edition Effects” that nod to Toy Story moments (for example, a rare card that calls a fun group action like “Everybody High-Five” or “Theme Song Break” for the table).
- Tiny rule insertions that encourage quick storytelling between rounds, which can be surprisingly entertaining for both kids and adults.

### House Rules Worth Trying
If you want to tilt the table toward a more modern or party-friendly vibe, consider these light house rules that complement the Toy Story branding:
- Woody’s Quick Draw: If you draw from the pile and get a card that can immediately be played, you may place it down instantly without waiting for your next turn. This speeds up games and keeps kids engaged.
- Buzz’s Strategy Pause: If someone plays a Buzz card, every player must pause and tell a quick Toy Story memory before continuing. It’s silly and breaks the ice during families’ game nights.
- Lotso-Lookaway: An optional rule where a Wild Card lets you swap hands with another player, giving kids strategic moments that feel like a cinematic twist while preserving the game’s simplicity.

These house rules preserve UNO’s essence while injecting a playful Toy Story atmosphere. They’re optional, easy to adopt, and they don’t destroy the flow of the game for adults who might be sipping coffee while arguing whether the red card should be treated as the color of Passion or Fire.
