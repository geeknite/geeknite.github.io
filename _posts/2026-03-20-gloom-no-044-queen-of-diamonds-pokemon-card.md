If you’re into card design as a language, the Gloom No. 044 Queen of Diamonds is a small but elegant dialect. ## Display, Preservation, and Care

If you are serious about preserving a Japanese NM Gloom No. 044, there are some sensible steps to follow:

- Use hard plastic sleeves and a dedicated binder with acid-free pages. The aim is to avoid the edges curling or minor surface micro-scratches that can accumulate in a binder full of nostalgia.
- Avoid direct sunlight. The last thing you want is a Gloom that looks like it spent too long in a tanning bed.
- Keep humidity moderate; too damp and you risk warping the card or causing the lamination to separate.
- Consider a graded or authentic certified option if you want to assert a level of legitimacy for shows or trade meets. Grading is not cheap, but it adds the aura of legitimacy in the most nerdy of ways.

If you collect strictly for display, a diorama or stand that shows the card leaning against a tiny Pokeball can be a delightful way to present it. If you like to handle your cards, minimize handling by washing hands and using a microfiber cloth to wipe only if necessary.
