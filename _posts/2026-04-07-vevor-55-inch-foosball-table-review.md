## Maintenance and longevity: real-world tips
- Do not neglect the legs. A slightly uneven surface can escalate into a wobble that makes centering shots a nightmare.
- Keep the surface clean. A simple wipe after sessions prevents dust and oil from building up, ensuring consistent ball behavior.
- Lubricate lightly. Tables enjoy a little oil now and then; go light to avoid attracting dust or making the surface slimy.
- Check hardware periodically. Tighten screws, inspect brackets, and swap out worn washers to keep the table playing like new.
- Store balls properly. If you have a lot of spares, keep them in a cool, dry place to avoid cracking or deformation over time.

## How to set it up: a practical mini-guide
1. Clear the space and check floor level. A level floor means fewer wobble surprises.
