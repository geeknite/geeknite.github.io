## Condition, care, and opening an old treasure
Opening a sealed NOS item should be a ceremonial event rather than a casual garage rummage. Here are practical steps to minimize risk and maximize the experience:
- Prepare a clean, dust-free workspace. A kitchen table is not recommended; a desk with a soft blanket is better to cushion potential unceremonious card shuffleings.
- Use a clean blade or a card-safe opener to cut the shrink wrap with a shallow notch, aiming to avoid breathing the full 30-year-old cardboard perfume directly into your lungs.
- Inspect the seal: look for broken plastic, dents in the box, or water staining. Any significant damage might reduce value, but a minor wrinkle is often still okay for display.
- Check the insert and rulebook: sometimes NOS sets have slightly yellowed pages or a faint musty smell; either is acceptable for the true collector who wants to prove the box is authentic.
- Store after opening properly: avoid direct sunlight, moisturize the storage environment, and consider a protective sleeve for the cards. If you intend to keep the box closed, re-seal with care or store upright in a climate-controlled cabinet.

If you decide to open the set, treat it like a relic rather than a weekly Sudoku. Phase 10 is about nostalgia and family competition as much as it is about collecting. The first time you shuffle, you will feel the 90s come at you in a glorious wave—like a dial-up tone, but more fun and less irritating.

## How to play Phase 10 today with a vintage twist
Phase 10 is typically described as a rummy-style game with a unique twist: players progress through ten specific phases, each requiring a different combination of cards. The basic play remains the same regardless of edition. Here is a quick primer for those who are curious about the experience rather than the insistence on strict legality of card designs.

### Quick primer for new players
- Objective: Be the first to complete all ten phases and score the lowest points in the end.
- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal ten cards to each player (or five if you want to speed things up), and place the draw and discard piles in reach of all players.
- Turn sequence: Draw one card, then you may lay down cards that fulfill the current phase, then discard one card. Then the next player goes.
- Phase progression: You must complete the current phase to advance to the next. If you fail, you keep the cards in your hand and try again on subsequent turns.
- Endgame: The round ends when a player completes phase ten; then the winner is determined by total points from cards left in players’ hands.
