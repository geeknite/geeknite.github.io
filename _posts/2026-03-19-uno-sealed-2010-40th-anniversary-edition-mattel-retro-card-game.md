## House Rules and Variants: Spicy Takes on a Classic

One of the joys of UNO is that people tinker with rules until something magical happens: a round where the wind shifts, everyone shouts UNO exactly at the same time, and the referee in the group mutters, Fine, you win. The 40th Anniversary edition is no different: you can adopt house rules to fit your crew’s vibe.

- Stacking Draw Twos: If a player has a Draw Two, they can stack it on someone else’s turn. This creates longer, more dramatic surefire losses, especially good for prodding the lower-batters in your circle.
- Seven-O Uno: If you play a card of rank seven, you get to skip someone or switch direction; this can be a chaotic addition in a multi-player game night. The exact rule set depends on your group, but this variant is a staple in many households.

## How to Store and Care for Your Deck

UNO cards, even the sealed kind, can get a little worn when you’re shoving them across the table or tossing them to your friends in rage at the end of the round. A simple care routine keeps your deck looking sharp for years:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight; otherwise, the colors can fade and the blues will start to sulk.
- Use a card case or a small box to prevent curling or bending of corners.
- If the deck is sealed, store the packaging in a place where it won’t be crushed by heavier items on a shelf.
- Avoid washing or cleaning with solvents; UNO cards are not made to be cleaned with bleach—your friends will not forgive you for the odd color fissure that appears during a dramatic font color change on the back.
