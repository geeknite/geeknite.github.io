### Mounting considerations: concrete, wood, or something in-between
If you’re mounting on a wooden deck, you’ll likely use wood screws or lag bolts that come in the kit. If you’re anchoring into concrete, you’ll need a masonry bit and some good Patience. ## Durability and maintenance: long-term love rather than one-night stand with your porch
### Cleaning and care tips
Maintenance is delightfully boring. A quick wipe with a damp cloth to remove pollen, dust, and mysterious garden debris is enough for most weeks. If you live somewhere with heavy pollen or tree sap, you’ll want to hit it with a mild detergent solution and rinse well. Avoid harsh chemicals that could nibble at the powder coating. If you’re not sure, test a small area first. The goal is a finish that still looks good after a season of backyard chaos and several grilled burgers.

### Weather resilience
The finish is designed to resist sun fading and moisture to a practical extent. It won’t stay glossy forever, but you’re not paying for showroom perfection; you’re paying for a fixture that does its job for a long time and looks good doing it. If you’re in a climate with salt air or heavy winter use, you’ll want to inspect yearly for any signs of corrosion or loosening of the mounting hardware. Tightening a few screws once a year is cheaper than replacing the entire post after a gusty week.
