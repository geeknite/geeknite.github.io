## Ergonomics, Comfort, and Health

Ergonomics matter most when you’re staring at a screen for eight hours or more. Here’s how this desk stacks up:
- Posture: The height range supports neutral elbow and wrist angles for most users when paired with properly adjusted chairs. If you’re under 160 cm or over 195 cm, you’ll want to test the fit to ensure your monitor height aligns with your eye line.
- Neck strain: The central screen option helps keep the neck in a good alignment, so you don’t get that morning-after neck stiffness from craning at a laptop perched too low.
- Movement: Sit-stand capability encourages short-range movement. If your day involves a lot of walking to the coffee machine or a quick stretch, you’ll appreciate the ability to adjust without leaving your workstation behind.
- Collaboration: The shared surface is perfect for collaborative tasks, but you’ll want to set some ground rules to avoid accidental laptop-swapping or “that corner is mine!” moments.

If you’re new to ergonomic setups, pair this desk with an adjustable chair, a monitor arm that matches your screen height, and perhaps a footrest. Small changes add up to big improvements in comfort and productivity over time.

## Maintenance and Care

- Clean with a soft, damp cloth. Avoid harsh abrasives that could dull the oak veneer or the white finish.
- Wipe down the monitor arm and screen mount periodically to remove dust and fingerprints.
- Check screws and fasteners every few months to maintain stability, especially if you frequently adjust the height.
- For the cable channels, use cable ties or Velcro straps to reduce loose spaghetti and make future disassembly easier.

A well-maintained desk ages gracefully. The Nat Oak finish will darken slightly over time, giving the desk a character that says, “I’ve seen a lot of meetings.” The white accents may require nothing more than an occasional wipe-down to keep them looking crisp on camera during your next Zoom call.
