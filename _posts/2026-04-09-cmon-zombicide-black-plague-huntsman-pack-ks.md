Unboxing the Huntsman Pack is like opening a fedora at a zombie convention: you’re greeted by a new angle on the same old problem, and you can already smell the cardboard and plastic smell that means you’re in for a long, loud night of gaming. If you’ve played Zombicide: Black Plague before, you know the drill: you roll a lot of dice, you move a lot of pawns, and you pretend that your table is a grim, plague-ridden courtyard rather than a clean kitchen table. The Huntsman Pack adds a longbow-wielding survivor and some fresh undead to complicate the math in a way that feels satisfying rather than punitive.

Contents and box lay-out

### The contents
Inside the box you’ll find a single Huntsman survivor miniature, a handful of new zombie variants, a small deck of equipment cards, a couple of scenario cards that spotlight ranged play, some plastic tokens, and an insert that helps you fit it into your Black Plague core box. The dwarfed portion of the packaging is clever: it’s compact enough to fit on a shelf without turning your entire display case into a tetris puzzle, yet it still holds the feel of a dedicated add-on rather than a throwaway promo.

### Minis and components
The Huntsman himself stands with a quiet swagger, bow drawn, cape fluttering as if he just remembered an arrow can travel through a lot of undead jokes. The other new minis look consistent with Black Plague’s aesthetic: gritty, with a dash of medieval fantasy, and the level of detail that makes you want to pick up a brush and start painting before you finish the rules explanation.

The cards are clean and readable, and the tokens have crisp silhouettes that look good on camera or on the dining table. If you’re a paint‑plus‑display kind of player, you’ll appreciate that the sculpts translate well under even modest tabletop lighting.

Thematic feel and narrative

### Light on flash, heavy on flavor
What the Huntsman Pack does exceptionally well is lean into an archer’s perspective without turning the game into a green arrow cosplay competition. You’ll feel the tension of the battlefield as you try to hold back the horde with ranged support. The new survivor brings a distinct playstyle that’s all about positioning and tempo, not mindless rolling. It’s a nice breath of air in a game that can otherwise lean into swingy melee chases.

Mechanics and gameplay

### How it affects the core loop
The core loop remains the same—move, attack, survive—but the Huntsman Pack adds a reasonable, well-implemented ranged angle. The new equipment cards can swing engagements in fresh ways: longer-range attacks, alternate damage types, and tactical moves that reward careful planning. It’s not a complete rework; it’s a thoughtful nudge toward more tactical play without making new players feel they must read a policy manual to finish a round.

### Line of sight and space
Black Plague’s boards aren’t tiny; you’ll notice that line of sight and space management matter more with archers. The new cards encourage you to think about where your team stands relative to doors, windows, and alleyways. You’ll pivot your squad composition mid-session, trading one party member’s brute strength for another’s ranged precision. The result is a smoother tempo that rewards careful moves and punishes reckless charges a little less than pure chaos—an effect most groups will appreciate.

Strategy and tips

### For the archer specialist
Take advantage of high ground whenever possible and avoid unnecessary exposure to melee brutes. Clear the line-of-sight corridor by focusing on zombies that would otherwise flood your line of retreat. Mastering the art of hitting from behind cover is the sweet spot; you’ll feel like a roving menace rather than a lone foot soldier stuck in melee.

### For the team: synergy and role allocation
Coordinate your turns. The Huntsman Pack thrives on timing: one player’s precision shot can create space for another to move into a more aggressive position. Use the new equipment to create “combo turns” where you combine a range attack with a setting that triggers a secondary effect on a later round. It’s not a puzzle you’ll solve in a single playthrough, but you’ll feel the rhythm begin to click after a few sessions.

Value, price, and Kickstarter background

### Is it worth it?
This is where a lot of players pause and consider. The Huntsman Pack is a small, shinier piece of the Black Plague puzzle, not an entire replacement for your core box. If you’re already deep into Black Plague and you love the idea of ranged combat with zombies, the value proposition is pretty decent. If your base game sits untouched on a shelf and you’re wondering whether to buy more stuff or save for future expansions, the answer hinges on two things: how much you enjoy the archery motif and how much you want to extend the game’s tactical tempo.

### Kickstarter origins
Kickstarter is a time machine for board games: you back something, you get the stuff, you forget the rest, and then you watch your shelf tell you the story when you forget what day it is. Huntsman Pack’s origin here sits among other promo expansions released during the Zombicide push that CMON has run across the years. Comparisons with other expansions

### How it stacks up beside other packs
Compared to some of the bigger expansions, the Huntsman Pack is compact and thematically cohesive. It doesn’t introduce a whole new board or a lengthy rules-heavy mechanic; instead, it expands the base game’s flavor with a focused, well-executed ranged option. If you’re someone who likes options that feel meaningful rather than gimmicky, you’ll probably appreciate the Huntsman pack more than the “new zombie type 17” that sometimes shows up in marketing blurbs.

### A note on painting and display
If you’re into painting, the Huntsman minifigure is a nice subject to tackle. The shading that a good painter can apply to the longbow and leather armor reads well on the table and in pictures. Playthrough sample scenario

Imagine a tight courtyard: a few survivors tucked behind low walls, the Huntsman peering across the narrow space, arrows nocked. The box is relatively compact for the number of components. It’s a nice-to-have addition that fits into the typical Zombicide tower of goodies with less fuss than many larger expansions.
