## What is the New Dice Tower For Zombicide Black Plague?
This section explains the product's intention. The first thing you notice is the tower's silhouette: tall but not imposing, with a dark finish that evokes the plague era without looking like a villain's prop. The goal is simple: give players an appealing and reliable place to roll dice that is also thematically consistent with the setting. The CMON tower is designed to accommodate standard d6s and a typical pool of zombie dice used in the game, allowing players to roll multiple dice at once and then drop them into the tray without chasing them across the table. It is not a life extension device; it is a tool to reduce chaos while preserving the cinematic moment of rolling dice and watching the dice bounce in a controlled manner.

The design is practical rather than precious. It uses durable plastic, with a matte black finish and subtle decorative reliefs that hint at Gothic architecture. The tower includes a small ramp that leads the dice from the top chamber into a perforated tray, which helps to dampen the bounce and prevent dice from landing in your water glass. The whole thing sits on a base that provides stability, even when the table surface is less than ideal.

If your game table is often a patchwork of sunlight and glare, the tower has a design that minimizes visibility-related dice misreads. The dice tower includes a guide lip and a simple chute that makes it obvious where the dice will exit. In practice we found that the top opening is generous enough to accept a decent swirl of dice without jamming, and the bottom tray is lined with a shallow grid that suppresses bounce. The result is a rolling experience that feels more controlled than a standard dice cup, and a little more theatrical than just rolling in the open air.

For players who appreciate the connection to the game setting, the look is sufficiently evocative. The finish is dark and the relief motifs feel like something a necromancer would keep next to a cauldron full of resin minis. The aesthetic does not overshadow the gameplay; it enhances it by adding a sense of ritual to the ritual of rolling dice.

In addition to the tower itself, CMON includes a couple of accessories that can be helpful for organized play. There is a small stand that can be used to hold a reference card showing dangerous outcomes or the expected dice pool for a given scenario. There is also a tiny brush that can be used to clear dust from the top chamber after a particularly dramatic wave of undead has been decimated. The brush is not a must-have, but it is a thoughtful touch for players who care about keeping their toy category clean and presentable on camera.

If you have not yet studied the differences between a dice tower and a dice tray, the answer is simple: a dice tower is an automated funnel that guides dice to a landing zone with a minimal amount of bounce, while a dice tray is a flat receptacle with walls that keep dice from rolling away. Both work; you can pick whichever fits your table size, your playgroup's vibe, and your tolerance for gadgetry.

## How It Affects Gameplay
For a lot of players, the main question is whether a dice tower changes the game beyond the visuals. The short answer is yes, it can make the dice results feel more consistent and less prone to accidental manipulation. The dice drop path reduces the chance of dice escaping the table due to impulse and the bottom tray captures the dice more predictably. The result is that you can focus on strategy rather than on chasing dice across the table.

However, there is a caveat: the dice tower is not a magic wand that eliminates variance. Zombicide is a dice-driven game, and many outcomes are determined by luck as much as by the players' decisions. The tower does not replace good planning; it only improves the way you access the dice results. If you are someone who wants to micro-optimize every roll, the tower might become a little too efficient for your taste if you are not careful about how you manage your dice pool.

In practice, players who frequently play with large pools of dice will appreciate the tower. It reduces the risk that a stray die skitters into the wrong hex or onto a mini. It also makes the moment of the dice drop more visually satisfying, which can contribute to the overall excitement of a session. There is a small bit of theater in watching a cascade of dice pass through a precise tunnel and land in a neat tray, and this can be a nice mood booster during tense sequences.

That said, you should not expect miracles. If your table is cluttered, or if you have a spilled beverage problem, the dice tower is not going to fix those issues by itself. The best practice is to keep a dedicated space for the tower and to use the tray as a catch basin for any stray dice. A tidy play space reduces the chance of things getting knocked over by accident and increases the table's overall readability.
