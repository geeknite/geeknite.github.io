## The Speed Score: How Fast Is It Really?
### The Marketing Figure vs Reality
The spec sheet fronts the headline: up to 1050 MB/s read. That’s a number you’ll see on the box and in marketing emails. In the real world, speeds hover around that zone, depending on a few key variables: the host computer’s USB port, the length and quality of the cable, the type of files you’re transferring, and whether the drive is warmed up from a previous transfer. If you’re moving large video files or long RAW sequences, you’ll appreciate sustained performance more than you will with small, fragmented copies.

### Sequential vs Random I/O
Sequential reads and writes drive the big-lift tasks: video transfers, raw photography bursts, and big 4K renders. The X9 shines here, delivering the kind of throughput you want when you’re juggling high-res projects. Random I/O, which is what you see when you’re launching dozens of tiny files for a project, is not the X9’s superpower, but it’s serviceable. For most creative workflows, the emphasis remains on large, linear transfers rather than micro-operations.

## Hardware Encryption and Security
This is a topic that often loops back to the same question: do I need hardware encryption? The X9, like many portable SSDs, offers robust options for data protection, but the details can vary by firmware version and the software you tie to it. If you require hardware-based encryption, pair the drive with a trusted software suite and enable password protection on your operating system, or use full-disk encryption while maintaining a separate backup. Always keep at least one unencrypted copy in a secure location for emergencies.

If you’re a security-minded user, you’ll appreciate the ability to implement encryption on the host side. Just remember that encryption adds overhead, which may shave a little off the peak throughput you see in best-case scenarios.
