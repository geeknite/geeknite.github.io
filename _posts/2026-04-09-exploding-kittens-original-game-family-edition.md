## How to Play in 10-15 Minutes
The core mechanic is simple: you draw a card, you hope you don’t draw an exploding kitten, you use defuse cards to cheat death, and you laugh at your friends when they accidentally catapult into a disaster zone.

Step-by-step:
1) Shuffle the deck and deal a starter hand to each player.
2) On your turn, do one action card or skip a turn, then draw a card.
3) If you draw an Exploding Kitten card, you must defuse it (if you have a Defuse card) or you’re out of the game.
4) If you run out of players, the last kitten standing wins.

The Family Edition streamlines this with color coded instructions on the cards so you don’t need a translator to decode boom or defuse. You’ll still end up giggling at the faces on the cards, which is really why we’re all here.

## Strategy Corner: Reading The Table without a Crystal Ball
While the game looks like pure luck, there’s a surprising amount of strategy hiding in the shadows of the card backs. Here are a few tips you can use tonight:
- Track what is likely to be left in the deck. If you can remember a few of the cards that were played, you can plan whether to draw or to use a defuse card now.
- Use casual conversations to mislead opponents about your next move. Bluffing is not just for poker; it’s for kittens who want to live another day.
- Use action cards strategically. If you can force someone else to draw, you might avoid a near miss yourself.
- Play for the table’s energy. In family games, sometimes the best move is to give someone else a chance to be the hero or the goat.

Remember: the more players you have, the more chaotic the table becomes. The Family Edition scales nicely from 2 players to a larger group, which makes it a nice fit for a family game night or a weekend party with cousins who show up in dinosaur costumes.
