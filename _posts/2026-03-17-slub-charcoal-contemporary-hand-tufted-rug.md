## Craftsmanship and Materials

### The tufting magic behind the curtain
Hand tufted rugs are made by pushing thread through a fabric backing to create a pile. The result is a sturdy, durable product with a dense feel underfoot. The Slub Charcoal rug embraces this technique, delivering a plush underfoot experience while maintaining a fairly tight overall density. Think of it as the Goldilocks version: not too soft, not too stiff, just right for foot traffic and cat napping alike.

### Color and texture dynamics
The charcoal color is achieved by layering charcoal and near-black fibers with a slightly lighter undertone in places. The effect is not flat; it shifts as light moves across the rug. This helps hide minor footprints and dust, which is an essential feature if you live with a busy dining setup or a curious corgi who dragged in a shoelace trophy from the hallway.

### Materials and care notes
Material composition can vary by manufacturer, but a typical hand tufted rug in this class uses a blend of natural and synthetic fibers for durability and softness. Wool blends are popular for their resilience and warmth, while synthetic fibers can improve stain resistance. The key takeaway: check the label for care instructions, vacuum regularly with a gentle setting, and use a rug pad to prevent sliding and wear.

## Maintenance and Longevity

### Vacuuming and cleaning basics
Regular vacuuming with a beater bar on low power can help maintain the rug’s texture. Avoid aggressive suction that can uplift fibers and create bald patches. For spills, blot — don’t rub — with a clean cloth. If you have a stubborn stain, test a small, inconspicuous area with a mild detergent solution before proceeding.

### Rotating for even wear
To maintain uniform wear, rotate the rug every few months, especially in high traffic zones. This prevents high-traffic paths from becoming obvious and preserves the rug’s texture across the room.

### Rug pads and protection
A quality rug pad will prevent slipping on hard floors and reduce wrinkling or bunching over time. Pads also absorb some impact, which reduces friction and prolongs the rug's life. If you have a slippery floor, a pad is not optional — it is a life choice for your living room and your sanity.
