## Practical Playthroughs and Tips
Here are practical, real-world tips to maximize fun and minimize the “Oops, I accidentally flung a burrito into the TV” moments.

### Tip 1: Clear the Battlefield
Move the couch cushions, stack the snack bowls, and designate a burrito-friendly zone. The Battle Pack invites longer sessions, which means more potential for burrito echoes across furniture and cushions for days after the game ends. It’s best to have a quick cleanup plan and a designated burrito container so you don’t have to chase the last stray piece around the living room like a tiny foam tornado.

### Tip 2: Warm-Up Shots
Do a quick “warm-up round” before the main game. This helps players gauge throwing power and trajectory. The burritos aren’t weighted like real food; still, you don’t want the first shot to be a misfired arc into your neighbor’s plant. A short pre-round helps everyone calibrate and laughs at the inevitable practice-toss misfires become a memory rather than a tragedy.

### Tip 3: Role Assignment
If you’re playing with a bigger group, assign fun roles like “Burrito Captain” or “Move-That-Tloor-Mat Supervisor.” These roles aren’t rules-infringing; they’re performance-art enhancements. The point is to give players something to do beyond simply throwing. The battles become mini-shows with judges, commentary, and dramatic music cues—yes, even if you’re just using the TV static as your soundtrack.

### Tip 4: Accessibility and Seating Arrangements
If someone has limited mobility, adapt the throw mechanics. The Battle Pack’s extra pieces can be used with shorter, under-arm throws or even with soft, slow-motion tosses. The point is to keep the laughter alive without compromising comfort. The core game already champions accessibility in a fun way; the expansion should feel like an extension that respects that ethos.

### Tip 5: Post-Game Debrief
Ask the room for the “Most Dramatic Throw” and “Best Burrito Save” moments. You’ll end up with a highlight reel that could be its own YouTube parody. If you’re into record-keeping, you can log the most frequent target and the most common misthrow to see if you’re improving or simply perfecting your chaos craft.
