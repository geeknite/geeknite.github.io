## Age groups and sizing: what “5 age groups” actually means
Five age groups sounds big, but think of it as a fashion line for golf clubs: XS, S, M, L, XL—and then some. In practice, Aspire translates this into club lengths that progress steadily as kids grow taller and more confident on the greens. The logic is simple: smaller kids get lighter, shorter clubs with more forgiving heads; bigger kids get longer shafts, slightly heavier heads, and more distance potential.

- Age group 1 (roughly 4–6 years): shorter shafts, larger grip surface for smaller hands, max forgiveness. This is the “stick your ball on the tee and see what happens” stage.
- Age group 2 (roughly 6–8 years): a balance of control and distance. The set starts to feel less toy-like and more like a real introduction to the sport.
- Age group 3 (roughly 8–10 years): more precise irons, better alignment aids, and a bit more effort required to swing. 
- Age group 4 (roughly 10–12 years): longer shafts; still forgiving, but a taste of adult golf’s learning curve.
- Age group 5 (roughly 12+ if you’re a late bloomer): near-adult reach, lighter swing weights, and putter alignment tools that aren’t a kid’s version of a space laser.

## Maintenance and care tips
To maximize the lifespan of junior gear, adopt a quick care routine:
- Wipe clubs after use to remove grass, soil, and snack remnants from practice sessions.
- Dry thoroughly before storing to avoid rust or mildew on the bag and hardware.
- Inspect grips periodically; kids’ hands sweat, and a sticky grip is a lost swing advantage.
- Store in a cool, dry, accessible place where curious pets or siblings cannot misuse the set as a makeshift treasure chest.
- Use a proper ball bin or rack so you don’t trip over stray practice balls mid-swing. Your future self will thank you.
