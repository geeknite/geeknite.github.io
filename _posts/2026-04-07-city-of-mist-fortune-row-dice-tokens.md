## Design notes and build quality
The dice themselves feel premium but not decadent. They have a tactile heft that suggests reliability, with edges that feel comfortable in the hand even after a long campaign session. The faces are etched and then painted, which gives the art some pop without smearing under a sweaty table lamp. The tokens are a good weight too, slightly larger than a typical coin, so you can spot them among character sheets and beverage cups without needing a magnifier.

One of the nice touches is the color language of the set. The designers leaned into noir blues and brass hints that evoke a city at night, full of secrets and streetlight halos. This is not simply aesthetic; it helps players quickly identify which tokens correspond to which narrative function, whether it is clues, conditions, or misfortune that is about to happen in the next scene.

Storage for the set is fairly standard: a shallow tray with molded sections, a lid that snaps shut, and a compact footprint that fits neatly in a typical CofM play kit. If you travel to game nights in a tote bag, the Fortune Row set slides into its own pocket with minimal fuss. The case also doubles as a quick way to set up scenes on a map; you can lay out tokens in rough alignment with your noir alleys and city districts.

## Usability at the table
The true test for any dice and token set is not how pretty it looks on a shelf, but how it helps or hinders the pace of the game. In City of Mist, tokens act as stand-ins for narrative leverage, while dice function as a quick way to resolve outcomes and determine who controls the scene. The Fortune Row set nails this in a few key ways:

- Clarity: the token shapes and colors map intuitively to in-game functions. Tokens representing danger or clues are easy to identify at a glance.
- Speed: the dice provide a rapid mechanism for scene resolutions without needing to consult a long table every turn. The custom symbols are simple to interpret during fast-paced moments.
- Theme support: the color palette and iconography are not arbitrary; they reinforce the game’s setting and help players think in terms of factions, masks, and mythic possibilities.

For new players, the set offers a gentle on-ramp: the quick reference cards walk you through a basic token economy, and the dice are sized for obvious visibility on a crowded table. For veteran CofM players, the set provides a polished, higher quality fabric for your games, giving a sense of gravitas and cohesion to scenes that hinge on fate and consequence.

## Thematic fit and narrative use
City of Mist thrives on mythic analogies and a blend of modern crime drama. Fortune Row contributes to this by giving you tangible artifacts of fortune and misfortune. Tokens can be used to signal a scene change, to remind players of a complication, or to mark a character s alignment with a particular arc of the storyline. The dice with its symbols can be used to represent outcomes or to add a layer of complication that players must address in a scene. The net effect is that cards and tokens become story prompts, not just numbers and shapes.

If you use other Note cards or play mats from City of Mist, you can combine them with Fortune Row by aligning token zones with your district map. Thematically, you can imagine the Fortune Row as a social club in the city where various factions meet, all drawing fortune toward or away from the investigators. The set shines when you lean into the story possibilities rather than forcing the dice to do all the heavy lifting.

## Playtesting notes
In my sessions, we used Fortune Row to mark both immediate story stakes and longer term narrative arcs. A misfortune token could appear after a misstep in a confrontation, forcing players to improvise a clever exit or a clever bluff. A clue token could unlock a flashback sequence that reveals a hidden truth about a suspect or a location. The dice became a quick gauge of risk vs reward, with higher symbol counts correlating with higher stakes in a given scene. The combination of tokens and dice adds a tactile element to the CofM experience that is especially satisfying when you describe a scene to your table and then produce a set of shimmering tokens that catch the candlelight just so.

One caveat is that the token economy can get a bit intense in very large sessions. If you have a high narrative tempo where players are flipping tokens left and right, it can become a rhythm that needs gentle oversight to avoid token bloat. A simple house rule that worked well for us is to cap the number of active tokens per player at any given moment and allow tokens to be exchanged for quick narrative prompts rather than saved up for a blockbuster move every five minutes. It keeps things nimble and still thematic.

## Price, value, and where to buy
Fortune Row sits in a mid tier for tabletop accessory sets. It is not the cheapest dice kit on the market, but the quality of the components and the sense of cohesion with City of Mist’s aesthetic makes it a good value for regular CofM players. The included storage tray and quick reference cards add value beyond the raw components, especially for groups that are just starting with tokens and want to reduce table clutter.

If you are comparing to other CofM related kits, there are tradeoffs. Some sets emphasize large numbers of tokens or a bigger dice suite, which can be nice for big campaigns but heavy on the cost. Others prioritize minimalism and portability but at the expense of thematic depth. Fortune Row seems to walk a sane line between these extremes, offering enough variety to keep sessions fresh while not requiring you to carry an enormous bag of bits.

As with any accessory, your mileage will depend on your play style. If your table loves heavy narrative shaping and a heavy dose of theatricality, Fortune Row is likely to become a core part of your kit. If you play more rules-light or prefer digital trackers, the tangible tokens might feel like a ceremony rather than a utility, but the tactile satisfaction remains undeniable.

## Tips and tricks for using Fortune Row in your sessions
- Use tokens to signal multi layer complications: a single token can represent a mundane obstacle, while two tokens can imply a layered mystery.
- Pair tokens with narrative prompts: after a scene, hand out a prompt card that connects the token to a new clue or a change of location.
- Create a table of symbol meanings for your group: quick references reduce tabletop chatter and keep momentum high.
- Use the dice to resolve contested outcomes: allocate dice outcomes to the player or to the GM and then let narrative control shift accordingly.

## Maintenance and care
To keep Fortune Row looking sharp, give the dice a light wipe with a dry cloth and store tokens in their molded sections after each session. If you have a lot of players using the set, consider a small bag or zip pouch to prevent tokens from wandering into the midnight of a tote bag. Do not expose the components to extreme heat or sunlight for extended periods; the art is subtle and you want it to remain legible across years of campaigns.
