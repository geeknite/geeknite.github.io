## How to Play: Core Mechanics Revisited
Dixit is, at its heart, a game about storytelling and interpretation. Access+ keeps that soul intact but plays with the tempo and texture a bit differently.

### The storyteller and the clue
On your turn as a storyteller you select one of your face-down cards and give a clue that relates to the image. It can be a single word, a phrase, a sound, or even a short joke—anything that can connect to the chosen card in a way that your fellow players can sense. The catch is that you want at least some players to guess your card, but not all of them. If everyone or no one guesses correctly, the storyteller loses some points. This is where the magic and the tension come from: you craft a suggestion that feels obvious enough to guide but ambiguous enough to keep the veil of mystery intact.

### The guessing round
Everyone else selects a card from their own hand that they think best matches the storyteller’s clue. The chosen cards are shuffled and revealed face up. Players then secretly vote on which card they think is the storyteller’s card. Scoring follows a familiar Dixit logic: if some but not all players guess the storyteller’s card, the storyteller earns more points and others who guessed correctly earn points as well. If no one or everyone guesses correctly, the storyteller loses points. It’s a balance between bold storytelling and shared imagination.

### The Access+ twist: timing and mood
Access+ introduces a few timing tweaks that matter more in practice than in the rulebook. For instance, the modular rules allow you to adjust the number of players, the number of cards in circulation per round, and even the storytelling time. The digital prompts in the app can be used to seed a theme night (dreamscapes, space opera, fairy-tolk) and push players toward a certain tonal direction. The result is a game that can feel almost like a collaborative writing exercise when you lean into the more narrative prompts. The art supports this shift—no longer are you simply guessing an image; you’re chasing a story thread through a constellation of scenes.
