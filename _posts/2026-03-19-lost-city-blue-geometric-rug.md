## Size and space fit: how to pick the right one

Choosing the right rug size is a design decision with practical consequences. The Lost City rug typically comes in standard living room sizes, like 5x8 and 6x9, and sometimes even 8x10 for larger spaces. The key is to let the rug “frame” the furniture rather than disappear under it. A common rule of thumb is to have all front legs of the sofa on the rug; at minimum, the rug should extend 6–12 inches beyond the seating area on all sides. In a room with a coffee table, the rug should extend at least to the front legs of the sofa so you get that anchored, cohesive feel.

A 5x8 often works well for smaller living spaces or apartments, creating a visual anchor under a coffee table and a couple of chairs. A 6x9 lends itself to medium rooms with a sofa and two chairs or a longer couch arrangement. An 8x10 is the hero size for larger living rooms and open-plan spaces, where the rug can act as a waypoint across the floor plan and tie disparate seating zones together.

If you’re placing the rug under a bed, consider the bed footprint plus nightstands: you’ll want enough rug visible on both sides for a balanced look. A good trick is to measure your bed width and add 12–24 inches on each side if possible. That way, you won’t have a tiny strip of rug peeking out under the bed like a shy geode.

## Care and upkeep: keeping the map pristine

Maintenance matters, especially for a rug that’s meant to be show-stopping rather than a one-wash-and-forget artifact. Regular vacuuming on a gentle cycle is your first line of defense against dust, crumbs, and the occasional rogue LEGO bit. It’s best to vacuum in the direction of the pattern to avoid flattening the pile along the lines of the map.

Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push stain deeper into the fibers. For more stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution or a rug-specific cleaner can be employed, following the product’s guidelines. Do a patch test in an inconspicuous corner first to ensure color stability.

Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and sun exposure, especially if your space gets a lot of natural light. The most sun-exposed areas may fade slightly over time, which is not a crime against fashion—it’s merely a weathering of the map as you navigate your daily life.

If you really want to go full immersion, you can pull out a soft brush to lift nap and vibrancy after heavy use. Just be gentle. The goal is to keep the lines crisp and the blues rich, not to create a frayed, threadbare version of a border town’s marketplace.
