### Cable Management and Power
One of the biggest pain points in any large desk setup is cable chaos. This system features an integrated cable trough along the back edge of each module and a central spine in the ten-pack that carries power and data lines. The design aims to minimize "spaghetti jungle" scenarios by providing separate lanes for power, display cables, and network lines. In practice, if your office is a labyrinth of chargers and dongles, the kitten is here to tame your wires, one zip-tie at a time. A well-implemented cable system reduces the monthly ritual of untangling cords before a video call, which for many teams is basically a second job.

But the real joy of cable management is the maintenance: you can upgrade to a better power distribution unit, run new Ethernet or fiber under the desk, and keep the top surface clean for a moment of mental clarity when you stare at the code you wrote at 3 AM and thought you found the missing semicolon. For those with IT squads who insist on a single network drop for the entire open area, the system supports central power distribution and a shared UPS solution to absorb the occasional power blip during a team presentation.
