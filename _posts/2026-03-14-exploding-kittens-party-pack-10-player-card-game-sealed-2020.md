### How to Play Core Mechanics (10-Player Edition)

1) Players take turns in clockwise order. The game is driven by a mix of luck what card you draw and strategy do you hold your Defuse do you come up with a cunning plan.
2) The heart of the game is the Exploding Kitten card draw it and you either explode lose or defuse with a Defuse card and continue.
3) Defuse cards give you another chance to stay in the game after you ve drawn an Exploding Kitten. If you don t have a Defuse, your fate is sealed though you can still watch the rest of the chaos unfold.
4) When you draw See the Future you look at the top three cards of the deck and then shuffle. It is your moment to pretend you are a psychic in a supermarket.
5) Attack cards let you pass the turn to the next player and keep the pressure on — perfect for a table full of mischievous friends who love to snipe at one another.
6) Skip cards let you skip your turn Shuffle reshuffles the deck and other action cards alter the flow.

### Strategy for Ten How to Survive and Make Them Laugh

In a ten player game your survival strategy is as much about social maneuvering as it is about card counting. Here are a few playful tips:

- The I am not in danger, you are approach defuse early and watch the chaos escalate around you.
- Use Cat Cards to set up trades. Collections and sets are less about completing them and more about distracting others with the possibility of a big payoff.
- See the Future becomes your best friend when there are ten people with nine different intentions in your ear. Use it to gauge who is likely to survive the next draw and who might be haunted by a late game comeback.
- Attack early to force others to draw at least once. The more you make people draw, the better your odds of survival and the funnier the moment when someone reveals a Defuse is a rubber chicken in a taco costume.
- Don t be afraid to bluff. In a room full of friends who love dramatic antics, a bold claim about having a Defuse might just be the misdirection you need.
