## How Dixit plays in this edition
### The gameplay loop, in brief
Dixit is famous for its simplicity and depth of interpretation. At the start of a round, one player becomes the storyteller. They look at their hand of cards and say a phrase, a sentence, or a single word that hints at one of their cards without giving too much away. The other players pick a card from their hands that they think best matches the storyteller’s clue. All selected cards are revealed, and players vote on which card was the storyteller’s. Points are awarded for guessing the storyteller’s card (if you’re not the storyteller, you want to guess it; if you are, you want others to fail to guess your card) and for others voting for your card as a clever match.

This mechanic encourages cunning misdirection as well as bravery in sharing a quirky or personal association with a card. The 1st Edition cards lend themselves to a parade of descriptions that range from the philosophically abstract to the delightfully silly. The humor often comes not from clever rule-lawyering but from the shared recognition of a card’s whimsy and how each player chooses to describe or mislead about it.

### The storytelling ethos: minimal rules, maximal imagination
Compared to heavier strategy games, Dixit thrives on social dynamics. ## Condition-oriented observations: VG+ NM in practice
### What “VG+ NM” usually means with Dixit boxes
VG+ NM is a measurement gamers use to describe the overall condition of a box and its contents. In this case, you’re looking at a box that shows minor signs of use, yet everything remains functionally pristine for play. Expect a few tiny box dents, a corner with intermittent resistance to opening and closing, and perhaps a card sleeve that shows a whisper of wear near the edges. The cards, in turn, should be free of major scratches or creases; you might see minor edge wear but nothing that interferes with shuffling or drawing. The inserts should hold cards and pieces securely, so there’s no rattling or misalignment during transport. In other words, a Dixit box in this state should still feel special when you open it and ready for rounds that feel just as magical as the night you first discovered the game.

### What to look for when you’re inspecting a used 1st Edition Dixit box
- Card backs: uniform, with consistent print quality and no obvious misprints. If you’re lucky, the edges of the deck will show the soft bevel that helps the cards shuffle gracefully.
- Cards: edges should be clean; flicking through the deck should reveal the artwork without abrupt contrasts that indicate heavy wear. A well-kept deck won’t have significant whitening on the edges.
- Inserts and tokens: check for warping; ensure the wooden tokens haven’t split or chipped. If the insert is loose, you’ll want to see if it still holds everything in place during handling.
- Box spine and lid: look for heavy scuffing or paint loss. A sturdy spine means the box has been treated with care and opened without fear.

If the box passes these quick checks, you’re likely looking at a collectible package that will continue to deliver storytelling magic for many nights to come.

## Quick care and storage tips for keeping the VG+ NM glow
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to avoid card yellowing and cardboard warping.
- Use card sleeves if you want to preserve the card backs and prevent edge wear during heavy social nights. If you sleeve, ensure the sleeves aren’t too tight to preserve shuffling ease.
- Consider a dedicated shelf or storage box that can handle a slightly heavier box without warping the edges. A little extra support goes a long way toward maintaining that slightly nostalgic sheen.
- For the artwork, lightly wipe with a dry microfiber cloth if you notice dust gathering on the surface. Avoid liquid cleaners on card art to prevent any long-term wicking of the coatings.
