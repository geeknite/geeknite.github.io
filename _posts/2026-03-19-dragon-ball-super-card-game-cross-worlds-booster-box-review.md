## What is Cross Worlds?

Cross Worlds is the latest expansion in the Dragon Ball Super Card Game line that aims to cross over characters, events, and aesthetics from multiple anime arcs into one big, spicy booster box experience. The marketing pitch is simple: bring your favorite Z Fighters together with foes from different timelines and see what happens when a Krillin can interact with a Goku from a universe you don’t even have a card for yet. The reality, as usual with card games, is a mosaic of art, keywords, and synergy that makes you feel like you are the writer of your own DBZ fan fiction, except this time your fan fiction comes in foil and has a very satisfying gacha vibe about it.

Disk-clacking aside, Cross Worlds leverages familiar game mechanics while adding a handful of new keywords and interactions. Card designers lean into the theme of crossing paths and alternate universes, which means you’ll see cards that reference previously released sets, plus new mechanics that reward multi-unit play, Bridge-type combos, and sneaky finisher plays that feel both clever and a little cruel—because what is life without a little dramatic plot twist during your combo turn?

## Box Contents and Packaging

### Box contents
A typical Cross Worlds booster box follows the general DBS Card Game formula: 24 booster packs, with 12 cards per pack. If you do the quick math, that’s a sizable pile of cardboard that can yield anywhere from a few SRs to the coveted SPRs and SCRs, depending on your luck and how many times you believe in the Blind God of Pulls. The box is compact enough to fit under most desks, yet heavy enough to make you question whether you should have exercised that cart-wheeling energy earlier in the day.

### Pack layout
Each pack tends to feature a mix of Common, Uncommon, Rare, and at least one foil slot that can be a Rare or better. In practice, many packs will deliver a steady cadence of upgrades, with your chance at a flashy foil card becoming the little dopamine hit you were hoping for when you first opened the box. The printing quality leans into the high-contrast, vibrant artwork DBS is known for, with the Cross Worlds theme giving a few new character art variants that look absolutely ready for a poster in a retro arcade.

## Pulls, Card Types, and Deck-building Synergy

### Card types you’ll encounter
- Common and Uncommon: These are the bread-and-butter cards that fill your deck with consistent plays and fill out the board. They are approachable for new players and cost-effective for seasoned builders who want to jam more strategies into a turn.
- Rare and Super Rare (SR): The mid-tier power spikes that give you bigger plays and more impactful effects. They are also the gatekeepers to the bigger surprises in your box, making your binder look more heroic than your first attempt at a fusion dance.
- SPR and SCR: The big chase cards. If you are chasing a specific card for a deck or a collection goal, these are the ones that usually end up on your watch list. They look gorgeous, feel premium, and often drive a lot of the conversation around set value.
