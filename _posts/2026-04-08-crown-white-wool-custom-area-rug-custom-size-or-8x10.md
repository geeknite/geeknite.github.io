### Sizing Options: Custom Size vs 8x10

This is where the Crown White Wool rug flexes its scheduling muscles. You have two main routes: a custom size tailored to your exact space or a standard 8x10 footprint that covers a common living room zone. Each option has its own pros and cons, depending on your floor plan, furniture layout, and how dramatic you want the rug to appear.

- Custom Size: The advantage here is precision. Your rug fits exactly under your sofa, coffee table, or around a quirky alcove without creating awkward gaps. It allows you to treat the rug as a design anchor rather than a random chunk of textile that happens to exist. The downside: lead times, price, and the possibility that your measurements were too precise for your carpet-y friend. Also, you may need to take extra care to ensure the optics do not distort the perception of your room’s geometry. If you have an irregular space or a high-traffic entry that needs a welcome mat with attitude, custom sizing can be the saving grace you deserve.

- 8x10 Standard: The 8x10 aspect ratio is a universal staple for living spaces. It provides a balanced footprint for most seating areas and aligns with standard furniture layouts. It is easier to source and typically ships faster than bespoke sizes. The downside is a risk of leaving bare floor edges if your space is long and narrow or, conversely, overpowering your furniture when your room is on the small side. If your home has an open floor plan, an 8x10 rug can help define zones without requiring a full room makeover.

To help you decide, we describe typical spaces where each size shines: a cozy apartment living room that uses a 8x10 rug under front legs of a sofa; a larger family room where custom dimensions allow you to tuck under the coffee table and still let chairs have their own carpeted mini-world; an entryway where a long custom rug can create a runway for muddy boots to parade through. ### Durability and Care: Keeping Your Crown Pristine

Durability is not just a marketing term in the rug world; it is an everyday reality. The Crown White Wool rug benefits from inherent wool resilience and a tightly woven base that resists crushing and matting. This is especially important if you have kids, pets, or a tendency to host midnight snack hunts in the living room. Wool has natural stain resistance, thanks to its cuticle scales, and it is less likely to absorb spills instantly, giving you a moment to act before a blot becomes a fossil.

Care is straightforward but not magical. Regular vacuuming on a low setting will remove surface dirt and keep the pile looking alive. Avoid beater-bar vacuum heads that can snag the fibers. If you have high-traffic areas, a rug pad is a must—not for fashion, but for longevity and noise dampening. A good rug pad prevents slipping, reduces wear, and adds an extra layer that your toes will thank you for when you sprint across the room in the dark.

When a spill happens, blot gently with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, which could push the stain deeper and create a little wool tumbleweed that hides the actual mess. For tougher stains, pull out a wool-safe cleaner or call in a professional, especially for red wine or coffee. A professional cleaning every 12 to 24 months, depending on traffic, can keep the fibers looking fresh as a daisy without requiring you to wear an industrial-grade breathing mask while scrubbing.

### Cleaning and Maintenance Tips

- Vacuum with a brush setting, not a beater bar, to avoid fiber snagging.
- Rotate the rug 180 degrees every few months to even out wear patterns.
- Spin the rug if possible to distribute sunlight exposure and avoid uneven fading.
- Use a rug pad to improve longevity and keep it from sliding away during your dramatic sofa repositioning.
- Address spills promptly with blotting technique and wool-safe cleaners.
