## Maintenance, Safety, and Care
No blade, no matter how robust, survives on charisma alone. D2 steel needs a little love to keep looking and working its best.

### Rust Risk and Protective Measures
D2 is not stainless. It benefits from a routine wipe-down after exposure to moisture and a light coat of oil to prevent surface rust. If you’re using the Honshu Black D2 in humid environments or salt air, consider a more frequent maintenance cadence. It’s not a “don’t use in humidity” blade, but it’s a “clean it after use and store dry” blade. A little rust prevention goes a long way toward extending the life of the blade and keeping that black finish looking menacing in a good way.

### Cleaning and Care
After use, clean with mild soap and water, dry thoroughly, and oil the blade. Do not soak the knife; fixed blades are robust, but you don’t want to invite water into the handle or tang junctions. The handle should be checked for looseness or wear; if you notice any movement, tighten or service as needed. The sheath also appreciates a light wipe-down—moisture trapped in a sheath can lead to mildew or degrade the retention mechanism over time.

### Safety Considerations
Keep the business end away from your non-weapon tasks and always practice safe knife handling. Fixed blades need a dedicated sheath, safe storage, and mindful use to prevent accidental injury. If you’re new to blades, consider taking a basic knife safety course or practicing with a trainer blade before going to work with a real one.
