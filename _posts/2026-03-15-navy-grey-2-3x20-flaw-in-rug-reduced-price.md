## Care, cleaning, and maintenance: keeping your rug dignified
Synthetic blends are typically easier to maintain than natural fibers. The rug in question is designed to resist stains, but you still want to avoid spills from a suspicious espresso or a rogue bottle of red wine that thinks it is a modern art installation. For routine cleaning, vacuum with a beater bar on low to moderate suction or use a hand-held vacuum to avoid damaging the pile. Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth, then use a mild detergent solution if necessary. Do not rub aggressively; you want to avoid a Shakespearean tragedy of fiber pills. If you have pets, you may want to designate an area for them to calm down after a chase scene, because the rug will collect fur and then shed it in an artistic installation in the corner. A roll of seam tape and non slip underlay helps keep the edges in place and reduces curling that can be a party trick for your socks.

Additionally, consider rotating the rug every few months to avoid pattern wear and to keep your space looking dynamic. The rotation also gives you an opportunity to reimagine the room as you go. The rug is not a life sentence; it is a rotating stage that refreshes your living space.
