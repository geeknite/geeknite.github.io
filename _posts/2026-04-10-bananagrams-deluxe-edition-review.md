## How to play (a quick refresher)
If you’ve never played Bananagrams before, you’re in for a pleasant sprint through a language labyrinth. If you have, you know the drill, but the Deluxe Edition adds a few nice pace-tweaks that keep the adrenaline up without making you cry into your mug of tea.

### Setup
- Everyone flips all the tiles into a big central pool. In Deluxe Edition, the bigger pool means more potential words at the start, which is either thrilling or terrifying depending on your confidence level.
- Each player grabs a set number of tiles (the official rules vary, but typical play has everyone drawing around 20 tiles to begin with). The tray sits in front of you, a tiny stage for your future vocabulary triumphs.
- When the countdown begins (usually signaled by “Go!” or a dramatic exhale), you begin building your own grid using only tiles you've drawn.

### The turn sequence
- On your turn, you draw tiles to maintain a constant rack, then rearrange your grid so all tiles are part of valid words connected to the grid. The trick is to rearrange while staying ahead of the other players.
- If you’ve used a word, you can continue by forming new words with the tiles in your rack and grid. If you run out of tiles at a point, you yell “PEEL!” and everyone draws more tiles from the central pool. The end condition is when a player uses up all their tiles, and then every other player gets a final round to try and beat that score with valid words.
- Scramble, swap, and rearrange. You’ll flip from “cheeky two-letter words” to “full-blown six-letter epics” in seconds, and somehow your table turns into a language lab where hyphenated chaos becomes the norm.

### Valid words and fairness
A big part of Bananagrams is the shared dictionary arbiters of the night. The rules typically expect words that are present in a standard dictionary and widely accepted in everyday usage. You’ll quickly develop a sense for which words are treated as plausible and which ones are simply your brain trying to invent a brand-new language to justify that last seven-letter word you just formed.

## Variants and modes
Bananagrams DH (Deluxe Hadron) edition recommends a few simple twists to keep things spicy.

### Classic vs. Tournament mode
- Classic: Everyone builds their own grid and the game ends when someone has used all their tiles. It’s the perfect baseline for a cozy evening.
- Tournament: Timed rounds, maximum word length constraints, and a leaderboard. Deluxe Edition can handle the extra rules without feeling bloated, thanks to its sturdy tray and tile set.

### Cooperative and chaotic co-op
In a pinch, you can try a cooperative mode where you all attempt to build a giant shared grid. It’s a guided chaos exercise, which can be delightfully chaotic and surprisingly satisfying as a team exercise—assuming nobody steals your plan with a last-minute two-letter word that breaks your entire grid apart.
