## Performance and Maintenance
Durability and care are the essential axes on which any rug’s performance is judged, and the Maccarel Grey Base Rug is both practical and a little bit forgiving. It’s designed for everyday living, not a pristine showroom.

### Stain resistance and cleaning
Spills happen. The polypropylene fiber is known for being stain resistant and relatively easy to clean. For most spills, a quick blot with a clean cloth and a mild soap solution will do. For tougher stains, treat as you would a petty sci-fi malfunction: act fast, avoid excessive moisture, and test a small inconspicuous area first. Do not rub aggressively; you’ll push the stain deeper into the weave and unleash the micro-aliens of carpet fuzz.

For regular maintenance, a standard vacuum will keep crumbs and pet hair under control. If your vacuum has a beater bar, it’s often a good idea to turn it off or use a gentle setting to avoid pilling. The rug’s pile height means it’s comfortable enough to stand on for a few minutes if you’re doing a quick stretch or a puzzle-solver session on your laptop.

### Heat, humidity, and the environment
Polypropylene holds up in typical home climates but can behave oddly under extreme heat. Don’t let a space heater kiss the rug for hours; that’s how you get a wavy warp where the pile gets crispy in the core. If you live in a humid climate, you may notice slight expansion or contraction with humidity, but it shouldn’t affect performance or look significantly.

### Safety and environmental notes
The rug’s materials are chosen for durability and practical cleaning. While polypropylene isn’t the most eco-friendly fiber, it is resistant to fading and has a long life when cared for properly. If sustainability is your personal quest, you can pair the rug with a good recycling plan for old textiles and consider reusing the rug in a different space if you redecorate.
