Intro
------
If you thought your zombie apocalypse collection was complete, CMON drops the Friends and Foes expansion for Zombicide Black Plague and suddenly your armory of plastic axes is jealous of your dice pool. Friends and Foes adds a handful of fresh heroes (the friends) and a couple of nasty new abominations (the foes), all designed to slot neatly into the Black Plague universe without requiring a degree in necromancy to assemble the rulebook. The package is compact, the art remains gloriously bonkers, and the gameplay remains as tightly wound as a feral cat in a ball of twine. If you own Black Plague already, this is the spice rack you did not know you needed. If you are new to the franchise, this box is a surprisingly friendly entry into the grimdark rainstorm that is Zombicide.

What you get
-----------
Friends and Foes comes with a curated mix of components that feel both familiar and refreshingly new. There are a handful of new survivor miniatures, each with their own backstory and goofy little gear quirks that make them feel like actual characters rather than cardboard standees with clothes. Then there are the foes, a couple of big bads that make even the bravest hero think twice before charging into the next zombie pile. The tokens, cards, and tiles align with the base game’s system, so if you know how to play Black Plague, you know how to play this expansion—just with more flavor and fewer existential questions about your life choices.

Component rundown
-----------------
- 6 new survivor miniatures with stat cards and gear tokens
- 4 new monster cards and tokens, including a couple of nasty boss variants
- 1 new set of objective cards tailored for the new heroes
- A compact rule sheet that cleanly explains how the friends and foes interact, with enough examples to keep a seasoned table from nodding off
- Updated reference card for quick peak scenarios during a chaotic turn
- New artwork across the cards that keeps the Black Plague mood intact while adding a dash of vibrant color

This is not a stunt expansion packed into a tiny box. It feels like a proper add-on that respects the system and nudges it in a direction that makes late game decisions more interesting rather than more punishing.

Setup and first impressions
----------------------------
Setup time is the most boring, mercifully short part of any Zombicide session. Friends and Foes slides into your game with the same elegance as a new season of a beloved show: you set up the same way you always do, but this time you notice the new icons and smile in a way that only a veteran maze-runner can. The new survivors slot into the existing character economy without clashing with old favorites. The new foes mix things up by introducing new kill orders and different spawn patterns that force players to adapt their usual routes.

On the table, the box feels like a well‑loved engine that has been tuned, not rebuilt. The art direction remains chaotic yet readable, with a bit more whimsy on the hero cards and a slightly darker edge on the monsters. The physical components are sturdy; the plastic miniatures have the chunky silhouette that fans know and love, and the cardboard tokens survive the harsh realities of weekly game nights with a certain level of smug resilience. If you are a collector, the Foes bring some delightfully ugly new models that could easily become poster children for your table’s personality.

How it plays
------------
Zombicide Black Plague uses a core system: you activate survivors, roll dice to determine hits, manage line of sight, and worry about your own little band of heroes getting overwhelmed by the tide of undead. The Friends and Foes expansion layers into that system without rewriting the rulebook. The new survivors have slightly different starting gear and a few unique talents that feel game changing without being overpowered. Some skills encourage synergy, others push you toward a riskier, high reward style of play. The foes are designed to create fresh failure modes: they may slow you down with different movement patterns, split up groups, or force you to consider crowd control in ways you did not need to before.

The combat loop remains the same at its core, but the new cards add flavor. There is a bit more emphasis on positioning and objective timing, which makes a four player co-op feel like a well-oiled squad rather than a chaotic meme about zombies crashing a party. The new bossy foes add a ticking clock element that can swing a game from a comfortable win to a white-knuckle race against time. It is satisfying to orchestrate a take-down with a group of friends who all contribute with their own quirks.

The balance question
---------------------
Does Friends and Foes tilt the balance? In practice, yes and no. The new survivors bring distinct abilities that can turn the tide in the late game, but the foes ensure you cannot rely on a single power spike to plow through waves of undead. The design team has clearly attempted to preserve the base game’s feel: you still need luck, you still need strategy, and you still need to watch your back while your friends draw attention away from your own hero’s scream of doom. It is not a miracle fix for a tired system; it is a well-crafted coat of paint that makes the same bones feel a bit fresher.

Thematic notes and art direction
--------------------------------
Zombicide Black Plague has always leaned into a high octane, cartoonish horror vibe. Friends and Foes respects that, peppering the cards with clever flavor lines and a number of easter eggs that long-time players will appreciate. The new survivors feel like they belong in the Black Plague universe rather than a random patchwork of fantasy tropes. The foes are not just menacing; they are memorably grotesque in a way that invites the table to lean in and whisper about what ridiculous method they will use to emerge victorious or fail gloriously.

Mechanics in depth
------------------
- Initiative and turn order remain familiar. You still use action points to move, search, open doors, and fight. The expansion nudges the decision tree by offering new action chains tied to the survivors’ special abilities and the foes’ spawn patterns.
- Equipment synergy gets a small but meaningful bump. You will discover that a stubborn survivor with a shield and a makeshift flame thrower can create a perfect zone control trick with the right ally on the other side of a choke point.
- The new monster cards introduce honest counters to brute force strategies. A typical table might need to rethink a simple charge into a cluster of zombies and instead coordinate a flexible plan that uses the environment to their advantage.

Co-op dynamics and table talk
--------------------------------
Friends and Foes is built for co-op excellence. It rewards communication and punishes silent solo plays. The additional survivor options give each player a chance to lead on a different turn and anchor the team’s plan around a shared objective rather than a personal glory run. Your group dynamics will shift toward a more collaborative vibe as people discuss which power token to deploy, which room to secure, and when to push the last wave of zombies toward the exit to trigger a win condition before the clock runs out.

Replayability and longevity
----------------------------
Like many CMON add-ons, Friends and Foes shines brightest when you have a core game you already love. It adds enough new tactical variety to keep repeat plays from feeling identical, while not breaking the rhythm that makes Black Plague accessible in the first place. The variety of new survivors means new playstyles emerge with every session, and the foes ensure that table fatigue does not set in after a couple of heroic escapes. If you pick up this expansion alongside a few strategic house rules or even a casual variant, you will find that it ages well rather than becoming a one trick pony.

Comparisons to base game and other expansions
------------------------------------------------
If you own only the base Black Plague, Friends and Foes is a meaningful upgrade rather than a weird side quest. It does not attempt to reinvent the wheel; it adds features that feel like they belong in the core system. Compared to other expansions that might lean toward big gimmicks or heavy wave-based feeds, Friends and Foes stays dry in the right places and flamboyant in the right moments. If you have played other CMON zombie games, you will recognize their DNA here but with a stronger emphasis on narrative synergy rather than pure power creep.

My verdict
----------
Friends and Foes kills a little time with style, and that is a compliment. It does not fix every little quirk of Black Plague, but it offers a robust invitation to revisit zones you might have skipped, and to experiment with combos you might not have thought to try. The new heroes give you fresh stories at the table, and the new monsters remind you that undead can be cunning as well as greedy. It balances charm and challenge in a way that most expansions dream of achieving. If you are a fan of CMONs zombie sandbox and you want more reasons to huddle around the board with friends while daisies of plastic blood spatter across the map, this is a worthy addition.

Who should buy it
------------------
- Players who crave fresh co-op modalities without sacrificing the pace of Black Plague
- Groups that enjoy building narrative through character synergies and thematic flavor
- Collectors who want a complete set and new sculpts that actually fit the world
- Anyone who has a regular game night and wants a reason to re-fall in love with the base game

Who should skip it
-------------------
- Casual players who barely finish a campaign in a single night and hate learning new rules
- People who own multiple heavy CMON expansions and are running out of table space for more plastic minis
- If you dislike co-op run-and-gun play and prefer competitive setups, this expansion is not going to scratch that itch

A few quick tips to maximize fun
--------------------------------
- Coordinate your turns. Don’t assume one hero will solo the objective; the expansion rewards planning and positioning more than pure dice luck.
- Try the new survivors in small groups first to understand their unique timing. Some abilities trigger on specific actions, and you will want to chain them carefully.
- Use the new foes to create tempo shifts. They are designed to force you to adapt; treat every spawn like a puzzle that needs solving rather than a random obstacle.
- Rotate tables to see if the social dynamic shifts in a good way. The game shines when everyone participates and nobody hogs a turn with a single plan.

Conclusion
----------
Zombicide Black Plague Friends and Foes is a thoughtful, flavorful expansion that respects the DNA of the base game while injecting just enough novelty to feel like a real improvement rather than a casual add-on. It keeps the pace brisk, the humor intact, and the tactile joy of slamming plastic miniatures into a painted cardboard cave. If you are building a weekly night of heroic horror, this box belongs in your shelves, ready for a session that might end with a triumphant chorus or a glorious disaster, depending on how well you listen to the friend and the foe.

Final recommendation
--------------------
Would I recommend Friends and Foes? Yes, with a caveat: if your group drinks deeply from the well of corporate zombie chaos and enjoys new table talk moments, you will be rewarded. If you are a casual once-a-month player, you may find it adds more complexity than you need. But for most enthusiastic table-goers who want a reason to argue about strategy and celebrate clever plays, this is a solid addition that will see more than a few nights of pure joy.
