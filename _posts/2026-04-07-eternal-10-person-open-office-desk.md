The apex of the Eternal is its integrated power hub with ample outlets, USB-C charging, and data ports along the central spine. ### Display setups and monitor arms
The bench supports multiple monitor arms on each side, allowing nearly every user to achieve a comfortable primary monitor height. The arms can be mounted at a uniform height or staggered to fearlessly accommodate monitors from 24 to 34 inches. This is crucial when people want to avoid the classic open office tragedy: the same screen angle for a dozen people, resulting in neck gymnastics of the worst kind. We recommend a consistent desk height plus a standard monitor height so that everyone can do the normal neck crane that makes your chiropractor happy to bill you by the hour.

### Connectivity for collaboration
Open space does not have to mean disconnected. The central trough includes network and HDMI or DisplayPort channels, so teams can present to the room without a tangle of laptops and adapters. The goal is to support quick ideation sessions and shared whiteboarding without turning the room into a cable spa. If your team uses cloud-based collaboration tools with high bandwidth requirements, you will appreciate the stability of this approach. The Netflix/wifi drama of connectivity is easier to manage when you have a single, centralized data spine rather than ten tiny cable jungles converging at a wall outlet.

## Buying guide: making this work in your space

Before you pull the trigger on a 10 seat bench, measure the room, confirm ceiling heights, door clearance, and any obstructions that might become an obstacle when the desk is installed mid-project. A few practical steps:
- Map out the seating orientation to maximize natural light without glare on screens.
- Plan for power drop points and ensure that the central trough aligns with the location of the wall outlets. You want a clean path for cables rather than an improvised spaghetti forest.
- Decide on how many privacy panels you would add to mitigate noise without sacrificing the collaborative feel. Startup teams often thrive on energy; privacy panels can offer the right balance.
- Confirm the floor load capacity and define how the base will be anchored if you are dealing with an older building or uneven flooring.
- Consider room acoustics: a few acoustic panels or soft furnishings can dramatically improve comfort and reduce the echo of talking points during sprint reviews.

If you are optimizing for a large, ongoing collaboration environment, the Eternal bench is worth considering as a backbone for your layout. It is not a magic wand, but it does a lot of heavy lifting in turning a room into a living, breathing collective workspace.
