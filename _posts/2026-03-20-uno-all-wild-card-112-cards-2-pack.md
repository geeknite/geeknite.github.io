## House rules and variants to try
Two decks open the door to a surprising array of house rules. Here are a few that tend to work well in Geeknite circles:
- The Double Dare Rule: If a player can complete a round with a no-draw, they gain a bonus point that can tip ties. It adds a spark of daring and creates moments where a player must risk a big move for a big payoff.
- The Color Slam: When a color is called, everyone must slap the table, and the last to do so picks a color for a critical next move. It’s silly but surprisingly effective at keeping energy high.
- The Wild Cascade: If a wild card is played, the next player must draw a card and immediately play a card of the same color if possible. It creates a mini-challenge for the next player and keeps the table lively.

House rules can extend the life of a game night by turning the game into a playful competition rather than a rigid exercise in rule-following. Just be mindful not to complicate things to the point where the simplest decision—play a card or draw—gets lost in a tangle of exceptions.
