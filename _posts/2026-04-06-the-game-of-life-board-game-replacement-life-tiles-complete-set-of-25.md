## Real world usage tips
### When to replace and when not to
- If you notice worn edges, fading, or a tendency for miscounted payouts due to illegible numbers, a replacement set is a smart move.
- If your existing tiles are pristine and your players are content, you may keep them as a backup while the new set remains in its protective sleeve for rare play sessions.
- If you host weekly game nights with a rotating cast, replacements can be a reliable way to keep the table feeling fresh, especially for new players who might be overwhelmed by the sheer chaos of an aging tile pool.

### Care and storage
Store the tiles in a dry, climate-controlled area to avoid warping or color degradation. Use a dedicated storage tray or a small drawstring bag to prevent mixing with other game components. While life tiles do not require a fancy display, keeping them organized makes setup faster and reduces the chances of a rogue tile wandering off to the land of lost items during cleanup.

### Cleaning and maintenance
A gentle wipe with a slightly damp cloth should handle routine dust and finger oils. Avoid harsh cleaners that could dull the gloss or strip color. If a tile ever loses its edge, a careful light sanding is generally enough to restore smooth handling, but do this only if you have a steady hand and a small set of sandpaper designed for card stock. If you are worried about messing up the set, contact the seller for a replacement rather than attempting a DIY fix on your own tile treasure.
