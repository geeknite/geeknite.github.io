### Why synthetic rope matters
Synthetic rope is safer to handle and lighter to manage, which makes setup and daily use much less stressful than wrestling with a heavy steel cable. It floats, which helps when you are recovering in water or mud, and it has a lower risk of splinters if it snaps. However, it can be more susceptible to abrasion and UV damage over many seasons, so inspect it regularly and treat it with care. For most weekend warriors, synthetic is the sweet spot between safety and performance.

## Installation and setup
Getting this installed is not complicated, but you should plan for a focused session rather than a casual afternoon.

1. Determine the mounting location: bumper, winch plate, or a custom mount. Ensure your mounting points are rated for the load.
2. Mount the winch using the provided hardware. Align the drum so the rope spools evenly and the mounting bolts are firmly fastened.
3. Connect the power leads, ensuring a proper ground. Faulty wiring is the leading cause of problems in early use.
4. Run the remote control line to the desired location in your cab. Position the switch for easy access.
5. Do a slow test pull with no load, then apply a small load to confirm alignment. If everything is aligned, you can try a light recovery test.

We strongly suggest watching a couple of install videos and reading the manual. The more you know, the less you will scream when you realize you installed it backward.

Tips for installation:
- Use a proper bumper or winch plate; single piece flexible mounting helps reduce stress on the frames.
- Wear gloves when feeding the rope to avoid rope burn.
- Avoid dragging rope across the ground; this can cause abrasion.

## Safety and best practices
Safety is not optional; it is the core of any winching operation. A 12K rating is impressive, but it does not exist to enable you to test your luck. A few guidelines to remain safe:

- Use a rope dampener to reduce the risk from ricochet in case the line fails or snaps.
- Stand clear of the line and never place body parts near the spool edge.
- Keep the remote control within reach but do not stand near the line while the machine is actively pulling.
- Inspect rope health regularly; synthetic rope can fray and degrade if misused.
- Use proper anchors (trees or solid ground anchors) that can resist the force of the load.

If you plan to operate for an extended period, consider heat buildup. The 12K rating is tested under controlled conditions; real life heat buildup is a real factor when you are pushing the limit for long durations. Let the system cool and avoid continuous heavy pulls if you can.

## Maintenance and care
After heavy usage, clean the unit to remove debris, mud, and salt. Inspect the rope for fraying or damaged spots. If you have rope, inspect the sheath for wear and ensure there are no kinks. Lubricate the gearbox as recommended by the manual and keep the electrical connections clean and dry. If you store the device for an extended period, ensure the rope has the correct tension so there is no memory or coil issues. Regular maintenance helps you avoid surprise failures when you least need them.
