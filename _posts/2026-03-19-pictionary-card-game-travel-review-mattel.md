### How to Play: Quick Rules Recap

The premise is delightfully simple in its chaos. Teams alternate turns. On your turn you draw a card, glance at the word, and then race to sketch something that leads your team to guess the word before the timer runs out. The trick is to communicate without saying the actual word, which is both a hilarious brain teaser and a test of your ability to convert abstract shapes into meaningful clues. If your team guesses correctly, you move along the track. If not, the other team gets a shot to steal the point based on a clever reinterpretation of your artwork. 

The travel version trims away extraneous frills and focuses on speed mode. You do not need a conference room to yield the perfect distraction; you just need a table, a couple of willing teammates, and the courage to own your doodle style. 

### Setup and Flow

1) Lay out the timer, tokens, and the deck in an accessible pile. 2) Decide which team begins. 3) The drawing player draws a card and stares at the prompt for a moment. 4) Start the timer and begin drawing. 5) Teammates shout guesses, and the drawer does not reveal the word unless the guess is correct. 6) If the word is guessed, award a point and rotate to the next team. 7) When all cards have been used or a predetermined score threshold is reached, declare a winner.

The flow is designed for quick rounds, which is perfect for travel where attention spans on a three hour flight can feel more like a marathon than a sprint. The friction is mild, mostly fun, and occasionally frustrating in the most endearing way. This is not a game for aspiring gallery curators; it is a game for people who want to steal a few minutes of ridiculous fun between meals and meetings.
