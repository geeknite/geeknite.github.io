Rattan (often PE rattan) is the star here. It gives you that classic wicker look without the organic fragility of natural rattan. What does all that really mean? It means you should expect:

The takeaway here is simple: the materials are chosen for a balancing act between aesthetics and durability. Expect a product that holds up well in a typical suburban climate, with a little extra TLC if you have a winter with extreme weather swings.

## Weather Resistance and Maintenance

The goal of outdoor furniture is to survive the weather and still look good enough to host a garden yoga class once a month. The set should resist UV fading from sun exposure, water from rain, and the occasional hail of popcorn kernels from enthusiastic snackers. Here are the basics to keep in mind:
- Regular cleaning with mild soap and water keeps the weave looking new and reduces the chance of mildew on cushions.
- Cushion covers should be removed during heavy rain and allowed to dry completely before storing to prevent mold growth.
- If you live in a climate with harsh winters, consider storing the cushions indoors and covering the chairs to protect the weave.
- The table’s leaf mechanism needs occasional lubrication. A light silicone spray on the rails (and a wipe-down) will help maintain smooth operation.

Weather resistance is not magic; you still need to provide a little care. But with a routine, you can expect this set to age gracefully and maintain its charm across multiple seasons. The result is a patio that continues to be a showpiece rather than a rust-dusted, sun-bleached eyesore.

## Maintenance Tips and Hacks

- Clean the weave with a soft brush or cloth to avoid scratching the surface.
- Use a mild soap solution for tougher stains; rinse thoroughly to prevent soap residue that could attract dirt.
- Air out cushions after rainy weather, and launder removable covers as needed.
- Consider a breathable outdoor furniture cover for extended protection during heavy seasons.
- If you live in a freeze-thaw climate, store the cushions indoors during winter and use a furniture cover for the frame to prevent moisture intrusion.

With these simple steps, you can ensure your set continues to look good and perform well, turning patio time into a monthly feature rather than an annual event of “let’s make this work for the barbecue.”
