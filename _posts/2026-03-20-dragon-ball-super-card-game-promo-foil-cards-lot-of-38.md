## Quality and Condition

Quality matters, especially when you are dealing with foils. The 38 cards in this lot vary in foil intensity and print alignment, which is standard in any foil batch. The foil finish tends to be consistent in most cases, with a few outliers showing minor streaking or edge whitening. It is worth noting that foils can curl slightly over time if not stored properly, so a good binder or deck box is recommended to maintain their shape and sparkle.

A few things to watch for:
- Even foil coverage across the card surface. Some cards might show slightly more or less foil in certain light angles. This does not drastically affect aesthetics but is something to be aware of if you are chasing a uniform look.
- Edge wear. Since foils are more prone to scuffing than regular cards, examine edges for chipping and rubbing when you first pull them from the lot.
- Centering and margins. A few foils might have minor miscuts due to manufacturing tolerances; these are typically cosmetic, but if you are a perfectionist, you will notice.

If you plan to sleeve the cards, the foil print tends to get along well with standard protective sleeves. Do not spring for the extra thick, anti-glare sleeves unless you want to turn your play area into a tiny art gallery and a slapstick comedy club for your table mates.

## Care, Handling, and Storage

Foil cards deserve a little extra respect. The mirror surface invites fingerprints and shows edge wear more easily than matte stocks. Here are a few practical tips to keep your 38-card glitter parade in mint condition:

- Use sleeves intended for higher-clarity foils. Regular sleeves can cause micro-mcratches that dull shine when viewed under inspection light.
- Store flat, not bent. A thick binder with proper padding helps avoid curling that foils are prone to when left in vertical sleeves for long stretches.
- Keep away from heat and direct sunlight. Excessive exposure can cause foil edges to warp and the gloss to dull in patchy patterns, which is a vibe you do not want to adopt as your signature look.
- Consider a collector’s binder with a pocket layout that keeps similar cards together. The more you segment by type and theme, the easier it is to flip or trade.

Anecdotally, the moment you see a card in hand and realize the foil catches the light like a tiny solar flare, you become a believer in the power of good storage. The glow is real, but the risk of fingerprint smudges is depressingly real too, so gloves are optional, but an enthusiastic hand-wipe after handling is recommended.
