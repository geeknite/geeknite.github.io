## Care and maintenance: keeping the glow without turning it into grayscale

Care is not glamorous, but it is essential. The key to maintaining the red rug is consistency and restraint. Regular vacuuming on a setting that does not aggressively pull fibers is your friend. A good routine is to vacuum once or twice a week in high-traffic areas and occasionally in less used zones to prevent dirt from embedding itself in the fibers like tiny math problems that refuse to be solved.

Spot cleaning is your go-to tactic for most everyday mishaps. Blot the spill with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and apply a mild detergent mixed with water if needed. Do not use harsh chemicals unless you enjoy watching color fade more rapidly than a meme goes viral. After cleaning, blot again to remove moisture and allow the rug to air dry. A quick tip: if you have a small stain that stubbornly clings, a little baking soda can help lift it when used sparingly.

Rotation can help prevent uneven wear. If feasible, rotate the rug every few months to ensure consistent color and pile height across the entire surface. This is especially helpful in rooms that have a lot of foot traffic from the same corners, which tends to wear down high-traffic zones first.

Seasonal care can also matter. In humid climates, you may want to lift and air the rug periodically to avoid damp spots. In dry climates, occasional moisture can help preserve fiber resilience, but you should test on a small area first to avoid any unintended color shift.

### Cleaning myths debunked
- Myth: Hot water cleans stains better. Truth: hot water can set stains and distort fibers. Stick to cool or lukewarm solutions.
- Myth: White vinegar is a magic elixir. Truth: it helps with certain mineral stains but may affect color if overused on red tones.
- Myth: You must scrub. Truth: blotting is the hero here; scrubbing can fray fibers and cause pile distortion.
