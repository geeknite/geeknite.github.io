## What is a Natural Wool Jute Rug

This rug is a marriage between two natural powerhouses: wool and jute. ### Care and cleaning basics

Vacuuming is the primary method to keep this rug looking fresh. A standard vacuum with a brushless attachment is ideal. Avoid aggressive agitation that can loosen fibers. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth, then use a mild detergent solution if necessary and rinse with a damp cloth. Do not saturate, as excessive moisture can cause warping or mildew in the base fibers. For stubborn stains, treat as you would a natural fiber item with care and patience. Always test on a hidden area first.

Rotation and occasional sun exposure (not direct noon sun for prolonged periods) can help even out fading and wear. If your home has floor heating, monitor the fibers for any unusual sensitivity and adjust placement accordingly. A good rule is to rotate every 2–3 months to ensure even wear across the surface.

We recommend a dedicated rug pad to improve underfoot comfort and to prevent slippage. A pad also reduces noise and provides an extra layer of protection to the fibers, helping the rug last longer in busy households. If you want a deeper layer of softness, consider pairing with a cotton or wool throw blanket in the seating corners, which not only feels cozy but gives you extra texture on top of the rug.

## Buying guide: what to look for in a handmade rug

Even though this rug is described as handmade, there are a few practical details a nerdy decor lover should consider. Look for: sturdy edge finishes, consistent knot density, and a weave that feels balanced rather than lopsided. The best handmade rugs tend to have a uniform feel across the surface with a subtle variation that adds character. Knot density gives you a rough sense of how robust the rug is; a higher knot count generally means better durability for high-traffic areas. However, natural fibers can be heavy, so the overall weight and bite of the rug should be considered for your floor type and room usage.

Edge finishing matters. If the rug has a clean, well-bound edge that prevents fraying, you are probably dealing with a well-made piece. Frayed edges can be charming in a boho setting but may require more maintenance over time. If you are particular about color variation, check the dye lot or fiber blend; even natural fibers can show subtle color differences. Do not expect a perfectly uniform color, and embrace the natural variations as part of its organic appeal.

Edge stability matters. A rug with a strong back and reinforced edges will stay flat and avoid curling at room corners. If your space features a high, sunlit window, the natural fibers may fade slightly over time; this is normal but you can minimize this effect by rotating the rug occasionally or placing it away from direct light for prolonged periods.

Quality check list to take into account:
-Pattern consistency and density across the entire surface
-Safe and non-slip backing accessible with a rug pad
-Appropriate size for the space and furniture layout
-Evidence of secure weaving where the fibers join the base

We have spoken of edges, but the heart of any rug is its texture. Expect a surface that invites foot traffic and scratches from your cat's curiosity in equal measure. It is not a plush cloud; it is a durable, natural fiber surface that becomes softer with time and care.
