## How to Verify a Sealed Copy Today
Verification is the practical half of the adventure.

- Inspect the seal: A genuine 1991 Parker Brothers seal should present a consistent heat-seal line and a crisp label. If the seal appears to be a modern adhesive or has been re-sealed, note that as a warning sign.
- Check fonts and typography: A font that looks too modern or inconsistent with 1990s Parker Brothers packaging is suspicious.
- Language consistency: If the multilingual text on the box looks inconsistent or misaligned, you may have a reproduction or a mis-bound edition.
- Ask for close-ups: A seller should provide high-resolution photos for the box front, the back, the seal edge, and the interior box corners. The more you can zoom in, the better you’ll understand whether this is an authentic, lovingly boxed artifact or a clever cloning job.
- Use trusted sources: Check with collectors’ communities. Ask for opinions from folks who regularly handle sealed vintage board games. The community can help you identify red flags and confirm authenticity.
