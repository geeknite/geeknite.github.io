## Cleaning and maintenance
### Daily care
For daily care, a quick vacuum every few days keeps things looking sharp. The rug resists shedding and does not appear to be a hair magnet the way some fluffy options are. If you have shedding pets, you may want to run a brush over the surface occasionally to nudge any loose fibers back into place. The backing clings to smooth floors, so you are unlikely to have a slippery surprise in the middle of a Zoom call.

### Stains and spills
Spills happen. When you spill something, blot the area with a clean cloth rather than rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers. A mild cleaning solution is usually enough for most common stains; test it on a hidden corner first, to ensure you do not turn your rug into a watermark gallery. If you have persistent stains from coffee or juice, a targeted cleaner designed for synthetic fibers can help. The rug dries fairly quickly, which is a blessing if you have kids or pets who prefer to treat the floor as a living canvas of experiments.

### Maintenance schedule
Every few months, rotate the rug to ensure even wear. Rotating helps to maintain color uniformity and avoid top-knotting from heavy furniture. If you live in a sunny room, consider using window coverings to slow color fade, because even modern materials have a limit to how much sunlight they will tolerate before they start telling stories about their youth.
