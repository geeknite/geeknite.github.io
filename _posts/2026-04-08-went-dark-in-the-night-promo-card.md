## Card Text and Theme (H3)
The essence of a Gloom promo card is to tilt the mood of the game without breaking its core rules. Went Dark in the Night does this by introducing a thematic event that tends to slide the game board toward dramatic storytelling, rather than mechanical supremacy. The card reads (in the spirit of Gloom’s tone):

- Went Dark in the Night: When this card is played, the player must narrate a short vignette about a character’s unfortunate nocturnal event. As a consequence, that character gains a temporary “Darkness” mood marker, which interacts with subsequent events in a way that nudges the table toward heavier storytelling and more dramatic outcomes.

In practice, this often means: a character’s backstory gets a new wrinkle, a siblings-in-law style dilemma reappears, or a haunting complication sneaks into an otherwise ordinary family incident. The elegiac humor is essential here—Gloom is not about grim mechanics; it’s about turning misfortune into a story that’s more entertaining than merely cruel. Went Dark in the Night invites players to lean into meta-narrative, to describe why the house has its own personal blackout, and to revel in the theatricality of bad luck being personified on a card.

### Why the theme works in Gloom
- It honors the game’s core concept: telling stories of misfortune for negative scoring, while making room for quirky narration.
- It creates a shared moment that isn’t purely win/loss — it’s about who can tell the best (or funniest) grim little tale.
- It fits Atlas Games’ aesthetic: a wink to fans who enjoy the macabre but can laugh at it in a cozy circle of friends.

## Gameplay Impact and Synergies (H3)
Because Gloom is all about balancing luck, misfortune, and narrative flair, a promo like Went Dark in the Night should be judged for how it influences play, not just how it looks. Here’s how it typically interacts with the base game and with other promo cards:

- Narrative elevation: It nudges players toward more elaborate storytelling. A well-timed description can recenter a round around the theme of “darkness” and prompt others to outdo the prior tale with a darker, funnier callback.
- Mood interaction: The Darkness marker (hypothetical) interacts with mood scoring in a way that might amplify the point swing of the scene, especially in a table with long chains of events.
- Player dynamics: Because the card centers on a vignette from a character’s life, it gives the storyteller role a moment to shine, allowing a more collaborative storytelling experience rather than a solo power play.
- Combos with other promos: If your promo deck includes other mood-altering cards, Went Dark in the Night can be used to chain dramatic effects. It’s the kind of synergy that makes veteran promo-spotting groups grin, because you know you’re about to stage a mini-drama on the tabletop.

If you’re planning a goofy-for-gloom night, this card is a great fit. If your table hates heavy storytelling, this might become a mild annoyance rather than a feature—it’s all about your group’s vibe.

## How to Use Went Dark in the Night in Your Nightly Game Night (H3)
If you’re itching to shuffle in a promo card at your next session, here are practical tips to maximize fun and minimize chaos:

- Prep a short narration warm-up: Before the round begins, do a 60-second “shadow reveal” where each player states a single, bob-your-head-with-suspense line about their family’s misfortune. It sets the tone for the Went Dark card and makes everyone ready for a storytelling sprint.
- Use a timer: A timer helps keep the storytelling anchored. Go for 90 seconds per vignette to prevent the night from turning into a 3-hour epic that would require an additional character sheet just for the narrator.
- Embrace the humor: The best Gloom moments are funny because of how miserable they pretend to be. Encourage clever puns and melodramatic flourishes. If someone misreads a line as a tragedy about a fridge magnet, celebrate the chaos.
- Rotate storytellers: The card is a great excuse to rotate who gets to tell the best mini-drama. It keeps the energy balanced and prevents the same voice from dominating the table.
- Pair with other mood cards: If your promo collection includes other mood-adding cards, sequence your rounds to escalate the storytelling arc. You’ll get a tabletop arc that rivals season finales in drama and silly jokes.
