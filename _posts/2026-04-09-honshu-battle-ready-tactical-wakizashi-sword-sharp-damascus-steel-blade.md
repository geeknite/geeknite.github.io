### Damascus steel: hype vs reality

Damascus steel has two big selling points: visual flair and a perception of strength. The diamond shaped, wavy patterns on the blade are beautiful, but they do not guarantee a mythical strength. The reality is that most Damascus blades available to hobbyists are often layered steel with a modern forging process designed to mimic old Damascus. This means you are getting good edge geometry and resilience for the price, along with a striking look. If you are chasing a true ancient Damascus, you are probably looking at a much more expensive model with a different set of maintenance demands. For the Honshu Wakizashi, the Damascus finish is mostly about aesthetics and cosmetic appeal along with a reasonable balance between hardness and flexibility.

### Maintenance and care

To keep your Damascus blade gleaming and your sheath fresh, follow a few straightforward steps:

- Clean after use: Wipe down the blade to remove fingerprints and moisture. A light oil coating helps protect against rust.
- Oil the blade: A light mineral oil or sword oil kept on a regular schedule helps maintain edge integrity and reduces corrosion risk.
- Check the fit: Periodically inspect the saya and the guard for looseness or movement that could lead to issues during use or display.
- Storage: Store in a dry area away from humidity. If you are an apartment dwelling wizard, you know this means keeping it in its sheath and within a secure display case.

Note: This Wakizashi is best used within the scope of cosplay, display, or light training with proper protective equipment.
