## How to Play: The Real-Time Trading Orchestra

Pit’s charm is in its tempo: there’s a frenzied rhythm to calling out commodities, signaling trades, and trying to corner the market before someone shouts out a counter-trade that flips the whole table’s strategy. The Deluxe Pit with Bell leans into this tempo with two notable additions: the Corner the Market twist and the physical bell to punctuate moments of near-mastery.

### Core Loop: Call, Trade, Rinse, Repeat
- Players sit in a circle and hold a hand of commodity cards.
- At a signal (often a loud call from a designated player), you call out a commodity and propose a trade with another player by exchanging any number of matching cards.
- The goal is to shed your entire hand as quickly as possible.
- The ring of the bell signals a major round deck change or the activation of a special market twist, depending on the variant in play.
- The first player to run out of cards wins, but not before a nervous doppelgänger round evaluates whether someone really knocked out their cards or fudged a necessary trade.

The Corner the Market variant spices things up by introducing hard-to-ignore market windows and temporary control mechanics. When a window opens, players have a limited amount of time to complete a set of trades that would make their position feel like they literally cornered a stall in a bazaar. It’s chaotic, it’s loud, and yes, it’s a little chaotic. That’s the point. If you dislike spontaneous chaos, Pit is not a puzzle you’ll enjoy solving slowly; if you relish the sensation of your table transforming into a crowded trading pit, the Deluxe edition delivers.

### The Bell as a Tactical Tool
The bell here isn’t just a bell; it’s a negotiation weapon, a moral support for loud declarations, and at times a reminder that you probably should have backed away from the table to take notes. Strategically, the bell is a cue: when you hear it, a new set of rules may apply, or you’re entering a critical moment in the round. It’s a great addition to a game-night ritual because it provides a shared audible tempo: everyone knows when a new round or twist is about to explode. The downside? It can derail a calm evening faster than you can say “Wheat.” If your group values quiet, slow-building strategy, you’ll need to pre-negotiate a bell etiquette: who rings, when to ring, and whether you can ring just to mock someone’s bad trade.

### Corner the Market Twist: How It Shakes Up the Table
Corner the Market isn’t simply a catchphrase; it’s a structured change in the game’s dynamics. In practice, it creates short windows where players can press for aggressive early wins or pivot their strategy on a dime. It also encourages more direct confrontation, because when you try to corner, your neighbors suddenly realize they have everything to gain by interrupting your plan with clever trades or a perfectly timed counterclaim. The signature humor here is that someone inevitably shouts, “I’ve cornered the market on chaos!” and then proceeds to trade a single card for a dozen other mismatched goods, which is both brilliant and equally ridiculous. If you enjoy watching people turn a stock-market fantasy into a chaotic, loud, table-top comedy, Corner the Market delivers with gusto.

### Strategy Without Tears: Practical Tips for Your First Session
- Start with a no-stakes practice round to get the hang of trading signals and the bell’s cadence.
- Agree on a house rule for bell etiquette to minimize confusion and table-flipping.
- Use the Corner the Market variant sparingly at first; it’s easier to manage in shorter play sessions.
- Pay attention to color-coding and card art; visual cues make it easier to identify wanted cards during the quick exchanges.
- Don’t forget to read the quick-start guide; the margins are generous for this edition, but the rules still require your attention—especially around cornering windows and endgame triggers.
