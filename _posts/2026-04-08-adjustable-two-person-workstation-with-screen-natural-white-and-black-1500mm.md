## Setup and Assembly
### What’s in the Box
Expect a well-organized box with a side of “you’ll thank us later.” The contents typically include the two tabletop halves, a shared frame, the motorized legs, cross-bracing hardware, monitor arms or mounts, cable management components, and a few Allen wrenches that become your best friends for the next hour. A printed quick-start guide is usually included, along with an online manual link for deeper dives.

### Assembly Time and Tips
If you’re assembling solo, plan for 2–3 hours of careful, methodical building. Two people working together will halve the time, but you’ll still want to set aside a quiet afternoon free of meetings and urgent emails. The key tips: sort hardware by size before you begin, lay the components on a soft surface to avoid scratches, and test the motorized height adjustment before fully securing the frame. Having a second set of eyes helps detect potential interferences with hidden cables or misaligned brackets.

For safety, ensure the unit is level before you apply power to the motors. An unlevel desk can cause noise, wobble, or in the worst case, a harmless but embarrassing clatter during a standing stretch.

### First-Power Test and Calibration
Once assembled, perform a low-speed calibration—extend the height range slowly to verify smooth travel, listen for unusual sounds, and verify both sides move in harmony. If you notice any asymmetry, recheck the connection points and ensure both columns are locked into their tracks. The goal is a silent yet confident glide, not a tremor that makes coworkers question your engineering prowess.
