## What is Skip-Bo Dice Canister?
Skip-Bo Dice Canister is a portable dice game built around the spirit of Skip-Bo but with a tub of dice instead of a deck of cards. The concept is simple enough for casual players, yet there are enough twists to entertain the board game veterans who enjoy a quick mental sprint between meals. It is designed for three to six players, though in practice it also works for two if you are feeling brave and secretly competitive.

## How to Play: The Quick Rundown
If you already know Skip-Bo the card game, you will recognize a few underpinnings here. If you are new, don’t worry — the game is designed to be approachable for families and casual players who want a quick, satisfying experience without a PhD in mathematics.

### Setup
- Shuffle the dice, place them in the canister, and set the score track within easy reach. 
- Each player gets a set of target dice values to chase, which keeps everyone honest and surprisingly competitive for a family gathering.
- Decide the play order with a quick roll-off. The winner gets to choose first whether to steal a die from the middle pool or push for a higher roll on their turn. It sounds fancy, but it is basically rock paper scissors with dice in a fancy hat.

### Core mechanics
- On your turn, you roll a handful of dice from the canister. The goal is to build a sequence or complete target values just like in the classic Skip-Bo card game, but with a dice-y twist. 
- Some dice show numbers, some show action icons; action dice can grant extra rolls, swap dice with neighbors, or force a reroll in a manner that makes the family groan and giggle in equal measure. 
- Points come from matching or surpassing the target sequences. The more efficient your run, the more points you snag before the round ends.

### Scoring and end conditions
- Scoring is straightforward enough for younger players: complete the sequence and tally the points from the dice used. Bonus points exist for completing multiple sequences in a single turn, which makes the table’s math look suspiciously like a secret code from a sci fi movie. 
- The end of a round triggers the next player in the order, and after a few rounds, the player with the highest score is crowned the Dice Whisperer — a title that counts for bragging rights and a slightly smug sense of triumph.
