Durability and Care

Durability in rugs is a hidden superpower you don’t notice until you have guests with muddy shoes. The Potter Ridge Teal Rug balances wear resistance with cleaning friendliness. Vacuuming regularly is recommended; a beater bar can be too aggressive for plush piles, so a gentle setting or a suction-only approach is wise. Spills dry quickly thanks to a fiber treatment that resists staining, though you should blot rather than rub. If you have pets, the rug’s color helps hide minor mischief, and its pile resilience prevents flattening in high-traffic zones, which is basically the rug’s way of saying it can survive the daily grind and still look presentable for that Friday evening glow-up.

If you’re curious about rug maintenance beyond the basics, we’ll get into the nerdy bits later. For now, know that the rug responds well to routine care and appreciates a good rug pad. Care and Cleaning Tips
----------------------

- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to maintain the plush look. Avoid high-velocity vacuuming directly over the pile, as it can lead to uneven wear.
- For spills, blot with a clean cloth immediately. Do not rub, as rubbing invites the universe to misbehave and scatter fibers everywhere.
- For more stubborn stains, test any cleaning solution on a small inconspicuous area first. If unsure, consult professional cleaning services that specialize in high-end textiles.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. This is like giving your rug a tiny vacation to reduce the risk of pattern fatigue.

Caring for Color and Longevity
------------------------------

The teal color is designed to be forgiving. It hides minor stains better than pure light neutrals but won’t forgive everything. If you’re worried about fading in direct sunlight, consider a sun-filtering curtain or blinds. The goal is to enjoy the color for as long as possible without turning your living space into a sun-bleached gallery that makes you regret every past upholstery choice.
