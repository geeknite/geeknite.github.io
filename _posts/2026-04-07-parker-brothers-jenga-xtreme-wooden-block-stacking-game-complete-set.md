## Safety, care, and maintenance notes
As with any wooden block game, there are a few basic safety and maintenance tips worth mentioning. First, avoid rough handling. If a block has a rough edge or small splinter, a quick sandpaper touch-up can prevent a small cut from becoming a bigger issue after a long session. Second, keep the play area clear of sharp corners and trip hazards. A tower collapse is entertaining until it’s rolling onto a neighbor’s leg and they decide to retaliate with a heroic, dramatic move that you definitely did not consent to. Finally, consider a play mat or soft surface for hardwood floors if you’re worried about damage to your floor or the tower. The safer your environment, the fewer after-game apologies you’ll be making.

If you care about longevity, a simple wipe-down every few weeks can prevent the blocks from absorbing oils and grime from hands. A light coating of mineral oil or furniture-safe conditioner can help preserve the wood’s appearance and feel. But avoid overdoing it; you don’t want the blocks to become too slick, or your highly dramatic pulls will turn into dangerously slippery slides that end with two people wearing the tower like a hat.
