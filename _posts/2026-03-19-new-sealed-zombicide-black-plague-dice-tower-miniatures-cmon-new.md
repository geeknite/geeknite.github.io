Zombicide Black Plague presents a cooperative experience that scales with the number of players and the pace of the table. The zombies come in waves, the rooms get tighter, and the stakes grow with each turn. The general consensus among players is that Zombicide Black Plague plays best with a group, but there is an appealing solo variant for those who want to test the waters without dragging along a full crew. ## Tips for getting started and avoiding early disaster

- Start with a plan for your first turn. Don’t wander off like a berserker; look at the board and decide who will trigger what actions to position the team for the next turn.
- Focus on loot distribution. Upgrades tend to pay off more than immediate heals, especially in longer campaigns where you’ll need stronger weapons to blunt the next wave.
- Communicate. Zombicide is a team sport with a lot of smaller decisions. Use a quick plan to prevent chaotic overlaps or wasted actions.
- Don’t ignore the terrain. Some tiles provide defensive bonuses or strategic choke points. Learn to manipulate the board to your advantage.
- Practice your painting. The more you paint your miniatures, the more invested you’ll feel in every mission. It’s not mandatory, but it makes the experience feel personal and earned.
