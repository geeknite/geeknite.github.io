### How to Play in 2 Minutes or Less

The core loop is simple, but the magic is in the cooperative timing. You set the board, pick your hero roles, reveal a threat card, and roll to perform actions like investigate, defend, or distract. The team must coordinate to unlock clues and seal gates before the monster roams the town. The rules are designed to be friendly enough for younger players yet flexible enough to accommodate a wider range of play styles as kids grow into more confident strategists.

- Setup: lay board, shuffle clue deck, deal a small hand to each player
- Turn order: clockwise
- Actions: investigate, defend, distract, or use gadget cards
- Goal: stop the monster and close the gate before doom arrives
- Threat cards: trigger multiple effects depending on the current game state, keeping turns fresh and surprising

A practical tip for families: begin with a gentle pace and use the quick start guide in the rulebook to plunge straight into the action. The first game should feel like a breezy demo – a bonding exercise where missteps become inside jokes. As you gain confidence, you can add complexity by tweaking the number of threats or gate events. The balance between luck and strategy is well maintained, so even a roll-heavy game becomes approachable when the team communicates and coordinates.
