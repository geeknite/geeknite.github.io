## What is BT03 Cross Worlds Complete Set?

BT03 Cross Worlds is the third booster chapter in the BT X line and centers around the concept of living in a multiverse of Dragon Ball fighting styles. The Complete Set collects all cards released in BT03, plus foils and promos exclusive to the set packaging. The result is a big box of possibility, ready to be opened at a table, on a couch, or in a subway station with the appropriate cloak of gamer confidence.

In practice, the set adds a handful of new Leaders and a large swing in the number of World Shift cards you can pull. The design aims to reward players who enjoy building around cross world synergies, rather than forcing you into a single rigid archetype. ## World Shift mechanics in action

World Shift lets you unlock added effects by playing cards from distinct Worlds during the same match. Think of it as a dance between color and concept. You align two or more Worlds, then you get a window to present a bigger threat, or to pull off a clutch board wipe that your opponent did not see coming.

## Play experience and archetypes

BT03 Cross Worlds opens up several playable routes that feel both distinct and familiar. You can go for an aggressive early plan that hammers your opponent with a two World tempo, or you can extend the game with a controlled pace and set up a late game cross world finisher. The new Leaders help you set up early pressure while supporting the mid game through World Shift triggers.

Possible archetypes include:

- World Shift Aggro: quick starts with World Shift cards that accelerate pressure, closing out games by turn 5 or 6.
- Hybrid Tempo: midrange strategy that uses small incremental advantages to position for a cross world finisher.
- Late game Control with Finisher: a plan that stalls the opponent, chips away at life, and uses a high impact play when two or more Worlds align.

For deck planning, we recommend starting with a two World setup and adding one or two more as your confidence grows. Our primer on deck building with BT03 covers these considerations in more depth: {% post_url 2025-11-12-dbs-deck-building-primer %}.

### Pacing and decision trees

- Early turns favor establishing a World presence and generating hand advantage. Those opening turns often decide whether you can reach the World Shift window before your opponent stabilizes.
- Mid-game is where you test your World Shift enablers. If you time your entries well, you can flip the board state dramatically on a single turn.
- Late game hinges on your finisher cards and your ability to maintain pressure while your opponent tries to stabilize. That moment when you align three Worlds and drop a leading finisher is where BT03 earns its keep.
