## How to play this triple threat: quick refresher and night-night strategies

UNO basics: match color or number, use special cards to derail opponents, shout UNO when you have one card left. The key moments come when someone tries to squeeze a Delta Draw Two while you hold a Wild or a Reverse card that flips the game momentum. DOS basics: you manage two piles and try to build up to ten with color-specific pairs. It adds a little more math-y flavor while preserving the social banter and dramatic yelling. Skip-Bo basics: you build piles from stock decks, playing the lowest numbers first, and use stock cards strategically to accelerate your own progress while stalling others. The joy comes from the tempo shifts — you can go from calm banter to shouting matches in which everyone pretends their strategy was perfectly inevitable.

A typical night with this triple deck setup tends to unfold like this: 1) quick setup on a coffee table with snacks within reach, 2) a warm-up game of UNO to establish who will be the table captain for the next hour, 3) interludes of DOS when the room needs a different pace, and 4) a closing spree of Skip-Bo to squeeze out the last bits of bragging rights. The variety keeps hands fresh and minds engaged, and the shared color language helps new players feel in on the joke even if their reflexes are still learning the definitions of “Draw Four” and “Skip-Bo stock pile.”
