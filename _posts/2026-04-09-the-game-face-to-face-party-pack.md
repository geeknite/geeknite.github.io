## How to Play: The Core Loop with a Social Twist

If you know The Game basics, you’ll recognize the core loop: you’re trying to fit cards into piles in ascending/descending order (the exact rule flavor can vary by edition). The Party Pack adds layers that encourage players to read the room and react to prompts. Here’s a practical walk-through for a typical round:

1) Setup: Each player takes a hand according to the base game rules. The new prompt deck sits within reach. A quick read through the prompt booklet helps anchor expectations—some prompts are cheeky, others are unexpectedly thoughtful.
2) Turn flow: On your turn you do two things: play a card as in the base game, and optionally engage with a prompt when your group agrees it’s the right moment. The prompts can be triggered by your team or by a specific card effect, depending on the variant you’re playing.
3) Interaction window: The social prompts encourage you to describe a card’s emotion, act out a tiny scene, or peer-pressure your neighbor into a comedic reveal. The key here is consent and tone; the rules give you the tools to be silly, but your table’s vibe should lead the level of intensity.
4) Endgame: Once you hit the target total or successfully organize your piles, you tally points (per the base rules) and add style points from the prompt rounds. The winner is the team that balances correct card placement with the most memorable moment.

The magic of this flow is not in the perfect play; it’s in the imperfect play that feels human. The prompts occasionally force you to do something surprising or reveal a tiny thing about your persona at the table. And yes, it’s absolutely allowed to blame your teammate for a misplay that happened because you both spent 20 seconds debating a prompt rather than physically moving a card.

### Variants and House Rules: Making It Your Own

Face to Face Party Pack is friendly to customization. Here are a few common variants you can mix in:

- Prompt-Only Round: At the start of a round, select a prompt and run a quick improv exercise before anyone plays a card.
- Silence Round: A round where players must communicate through nonverbal cues only (no words). It’s harder than it sounds and hilariously chaotic.
- Speed Round: A timer is set for each prompt, forcing rapid reactions and snappier banter.

Feel free to tweak the balance until your table hits a rhythm that feels right. The best party games are the ones that bend to your relationships, not the other way around.

## Strategy and Practical Tips for Getting the Most Out of It

- Lean into the prompts, but don’t let them derail you from the core card-play objective. This is a balancing act, not a 100-meter dash.
- If you’re hosting, assign one friend as the designated “prompt wrangler” to keep the energy moving and to filter out any prompts that might stumble into uncomfortable territory.
- Use the prompts to create micro-teamwork moments. The score can come later; the social value often compounds in surprising ways.
- Don’t force yourself to perform if you’re not feeling it. The game’s charm is in the shared moments, not in mustering a show-stopping act every round.
