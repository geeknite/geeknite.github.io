## Cleaning and maintenance: the practical playbook

### Spot cleaning and quick response

For day-to-day spills, the first step is to blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth or paper towel to lift as much liquid as possible. For common stains (coffee, juice, or pet accidents), a mixture of mild detergent and cold water is usually sufficient. After blotting, you can apply the detergent solution to the stained area and gently dab from the outside toward the center to avoid diffusion.

### Machine washing guidance (when necessary)

If the rug is machine-washable, follow the care label. Typically, you’ll use a gentle cycle with cold water and a mild detergent. Avoid chlorine bleach, high heat, and aggressive spin cycles, which can degrade the fibers and backing over time. After washing, air-dry flat to preserve the rug’s shape and prevent shrinkage. If you must tumble dry, use the lowest heat and monitor closely; some backings aren’t designed to endure high-heat tumbles.

### Vacuuming for longevity

Regular vacuuming is your best friend. Use a low-to-medium suction setting and a gentle vacuum head to avoid excessive pulling on the fibers. Pay attention to the edges to prevent fraying and move the vacuum along the length of the rug rather than using a circular motion at a single point. If you have pets with long fur, a weekly thorough vacuum will help keep the surface clean and plush.
