## Performance and Durability

Durability is not glamorous, but it is the friend of the practical. The Stockholm Jan Rectangle Rug is designed for a mix of high-traffic living spaces and occasional gaming marathons. The pile density helps resist flattening under the sway of heavy sofa legs. In our tests, repeated vacuum passes left the fibers looking upright and not compressed into a pancake of doom. Spills happen; we tested with a controlled coffee splatter, a red wine splash, and a hearty dash of soy sauce for marinade left overs. The coffee cleaned up with a quick blot and a dab of mild detergent, with no visible stain after air drying. The red wine required a second blot and a gentle wash, but the pigment did not bond with the fibers in any permanent way. The soy sauce is a trickier stain; canvas-like fabrics tend to trap protein-rich substances, but a quick cold-water rinse and a gentle roll with a dry towel left minimal staining.

### Care and Maintenance

Care is not glamorous, but it is simple. Vacuum weekly using a low-suction setting to prevent the pile from being pulled or frayed. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure wear patterns don’t show up as map-like illusions on your floor. For stains, act fast: blot with a clean cloth, then treat with a mild detergent solution. Avoid harsh solvents that could compromise the fibers. If you spill something oily, apply a light powder to absorb before blotting, then proceed with spot cleaning. For pet owners, the rug can work well with paws; the surface is not too plush for cats to knead, nor too stiff for dogs to drag their toys across. A quick check of the backing for loosened threads is optional but recommended; you want to ensure the rug lies flat and doesn’t become a magnet for dust bunnies.

### Underfoot Feel and Safety

The mid-pile feel is comfortable for bare feet but not so dense that it traps heat or makes you sticky in summer. The rug sits flat on most hard floors, aided by a non-slip backing on some variants and the optional rug pad for extra grip. If you are placing the rug on a slick surface like polished wood or laminate, we strongly recommend a pad to prevent edge curling and to reduce sliding, especially in households with playful toddlers or hypersonic cats.
