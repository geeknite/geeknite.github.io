## How to Play: Setup, Rounds, and Scoring
The core premise is delightfully simple: one player lays down a prompt card, everyone else tries to choose a Disney-flavored response card that creates the funniest, most on-point meme caption. The judge (the prompt card user) then selects the best fit, and points are awarded. The game tends to lean into rounds that last around 20–40 minutes, depending on how ruthless the meme economy around your table is feeling at that moment.

### Setup
- Shuffle both decks (Prompt and Response) separately.
- Each player draws a hand of response cards (the exact hand size varies by edition and house rules, but five to seven is typical).
- The first prompt card is drawn and placed face up to announce the round’s challenge.
- The judging player rotates clockwise each round, so everyone gets a fair shot at wielding the power of the prompt with the perfect punchline.

The setup is forgiving enough for casual players and robust enough for more competitive groups who actually want to form a tiny brain-trust of comedy. It also scales nicely for family nights: younger players can use simpler humor while older siblings or parents can throw in some sharp, witty references without toppling the table discussion.

### Turn Structure
- The Prompt Card is revealed and remains visible for the duration of the round.
- Players select one or more Response Cards from their hand to pair with the prompt. The number of response cards can vary with the prompt’s complexity. In Disney Edition, expect prompts that reference iconic moments and characters (which helps younger players recognize the references but still invites adult-level punchlines).
- The judge reviews the submitted answers and selects a favorite. The chosen player receives a scoring token.
- All discarded response cards return to the bottom of the deck, and a new round begins with a new judge.

The fun of the round comes from balancing familiarity with surprise: whether you land a clever Disney reference that everyone in the room immediately recognizes, or you subvert expectations with a subversive pop culture beat that still fits the Disney aesthetic. It’s a small miracle whenever the humor lands exactly right, but that magic moment is why we play games in the first place.

### Scoring and Endgame
Scoring is straightforward: a token equals a win for that round. The game ends after a predetermined number of rounds, or when someone hits a target score as decided by the group. The results aren’t just about raw comedy power; they’re about timing, knowing your audience (front-row parents vs. screaming toddlers), and the courage to drop a bold reference that makes the table groan in equal parts delight and embarrassment.

### Character Cards and Meme Prompts
Prompts are crafted to be inclusive: they’re not about obscure meme history. Rather, they lean on the collective memory of Disney moments that most players will recognize—something as simple as “When the side quest is definitely not a side quest” paired with a Disney character doing something obviously over-the-top. Response cards lean into a mix of quotes, character actions, and situational humor (some prompts invite puns; others invite visual humor). It’s that variety that helps prevent the game from stagnating. You’ll also occasionally get prompts that invite meta-humor, forcing players to comment on the meme-making process itself, which is a delightful meta-layer that can elevate the laughs beyond the obvious.

### Family-Friendly Boundaries
Disney Edition, as a family-focused product, does a great job of keeping humor accessible and non-threatening.
