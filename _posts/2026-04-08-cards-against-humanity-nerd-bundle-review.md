## The Pack Lineup (Six Themed Packs)

### 1) Galactic Gen X Sci-Fi Pack
This is where the warp drive meets the dad joke. If your group has opinions on warp speeds, photon torpedoes, and the ethical implications of AI on starship coffee consumption, this pack will become the default crowd favorite at any sci-fi watching party. Prompts lean into space operas, plausible (and implausible) tech, and the sort of future where humans still forget to charge their blasters. The card art stays clean enough to avoid looking like a t-shirt design from a mid-tier con, but the humor lands hard when a white card says something like a witty twist on a tired trope. Expect lots of space lingo and the occasional meme callback that will force you to groan and applaud in equal measure.

Gameplay tip for sci-fi: when you can pair a dark-humored prompt with a heroic, overly dramatic response, you unlock the true power of CAH. It becomes less about cleverness and more about how far you can lean into the melodrama without cracking the table. For bonus points, quote classic sci-fi lines and see who dares to respond with a parody that makes the room sigh in nostalgic agreement.

### 2) Majestic Realms Fantasy Pack
If your group includes someone who insists that dragons, wizards, and epic quests are the only real forms of cardio, this is your jam. The Fantasy Pack thrives on high drama and low wisdom, with prompts that mine from heroic legends to the awkward social rituals of medieval inns. Expect a lot of grandiose statements, heroic achievements, and the occasional pun that would make a dwarven king chuckle and then spit out his ale.

Pros for fantasy: this pack nails the archetypes you love to lampoon—noble knights, brooding wizards, dragons who need therapy, and quests that go off the rails when a rogue interrupts to flirt with a tavern wench who is clearly plotting a coup. Cons: if your party has a strict no-joke policy about dragons burning villages, you may want to set the ground rules before you begin.

### 3) Neon Circuit Tech & Startup Pack
This is the pack for the engineers who dream in code and the startup founders who dream in buzzwords. The prompts skew toward tech culture, conference spaghetti diagrams, investor jargon, and product names that sound suspiciously like a sci-fi submarine. The humor often lands by landing a punchline in the intersection of engineering and marketing, resulting in jokes that only a very specific kind of nerd will truly appreciate—and that is part of the charm. If you miss the days when your rig powered by a motherboard could run a game of Dungeons & Dragons, this pack will feel like home.

### 4) Pixelated Pulp Retro Gaming Pack
Welcome to a time before 4K, when CRTs glowed with moral ambiguity and your pixels were mostly shaped by engine limitations. This pack riffs on classic arcade, 8-bit RPGs, and the gloriously punishing difficulty curves that taught you perseverance and the fine art of swearing at a screen. The prompts lean into nostalgic moments, power-ups that were never that useful, and the unspoken sacred bond between friends who can cooperate to beat a boss after five long attempts. This pack is best enjoyed with a soundtrack that resembles a video game boss battle and a snack bowl that contains enough caffeine to power a late-night session.

### 5) Capes and Costumes Superhero & Comics Pack
The superhero pack is a love letter to the caped crusaders and the world-saving shenanigans you pretended to understand as a kid. It also pokes fun at the trope-y villains, the relentless moral calculus of being a hero, and the endless debates about who truly deserves the last slice of cake. Expect prompts that imagine the most ridiculous team-ups, the most awkward love interests, and the plot conveniences that only exist in comic book universes. This pack invites players to channel their inner hero—or anti-hero—without judgment and with plenty of stage whispers about secret identities.

### 6) Otaku & Anime Manga Pack
If you binge watched a season of your favorite anime and then immediately forgot you had real colleagues in the living room, this pack will feel right at home. It leans into tropes, character archetypes, and the glorious absurdities that anime fans adore, from plot armor to over-the-top transformations. The prompts are best served with a little extra enthusiasm and perhaps a dramatic reading of a favorite anime catchphrase. If you have headcanon for days, you will want to bring it out to play here.

## The Bonus Pack: Brand New 17+ Pack
The 17+ pack is the after-hours cousin of the Nerd Bundle. We recommend discussing comfort levels before cracking this one open, because the best CAH nights respect boundaries even when they punch them with a punchline.

## How the bundles actually play: card dynamics and table psychology
If you are new to CAH, the core mechanic remains delightfully simple: a player (the Card Czar) reads a prompt from a black card, then everyone else plays a white card that they think best answers or amplifies the prompt. The Card Czar then selects the winner. The nerd bundles add flavor by aligning prompts to familiar universes and tropes. The result is a game that feels like a friendly roast of your favorite franchises—an experience that can be hilarious when the crowd is ready to lean into the silliness and brutal honesty that CAH is known for.

Play sessions with this bundle tend to swing between two modes: quick-fire rapid rounds when you want to squeeze a six-pack of laughter into an hour, and longer, slightly chaotic storytelling sessions when you want to choreograph a narrative around a theme night. If you want to maximize laughs, aim for a variety show approach: a few science fiction rounds, a heavy dose of fantasy drama, some retro gaming nostalgia, and then a late-night run with the 17+ pack for those who want to push the envelope a little farther.

## How to incorporate this bundle into your next game night
- Prep and set expectations: announce the theme night, or mix up the packs for a surprise round. You can do a six-pack swing where each round uses a different pack, or you can assign packs per player to avoid overcrowding the table with the same flavor of jokes.
- Create space for guardrails: given the 17+ content, establish a plan for who can opt out without feeling left out. A simple method is to designate a no-CAH zone for certain rounds, and a separate table area for those who want to dive into the spicy cards.
- Use a mix of casual and championship vibes: in other words, have a few rounds of quick-fire jokes to warm up, then a longer stretch where players can showcase a mini-improvised monologue themed around their favorite franchise.
- Promote nerdy banter: CAH shines when players riff on the source material. Encourage players to quote lines, reference lore, and weave in fan theories for extra depth. Some players will get lost in the fan-fiction rabbit hole, and that is part of the fun.
