## How to Play: The Core Mechanics Demystified

The bundle actually contains two very different games with their own verbal announcements and victory conditions. Let’s separate the two to avoid confusion—though you’ll likely be mixing them up in the heat of a chaotic game night.

### Exploding Kittens: A crash course
- Setup: Shuffle the Exploding Kittens deck, deal a few cards to each player, and place the Defuse cards in the draw pile. The goal? Avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card.
- Basic turn flow: On your turn, you play cards that affect other players or your own fortunes, then you draw a card. The tension is real: you might reduce someone’s hand size, steal a card, or trigger a silly cat-based catastrophe.
- The Exploding Kitten card: If you draw this, you’re toast—unless you have a Defuse card. Defuse cards are precious: they let you survive and then reshuffle the exploding menace back into the deck. The heart of the game is bluffing, risk assessment, and whether you’re willing to gamble with the number of Defuses in the deck.
- Endgame: The last player standing with a non-exploded kitty on their board wins. It’s a weirdly satisfying victory where everyone mutters, “I should have kept that Defuse,” while secretly plotting your next move.

The strategy isn’t deep in a classic sense, but it’s a tapestry of clever card interactions, reading of opponents, and that universal gaming truth: sometimes the best move is the one that makes your friend groan loudly. The excitement is amplified by social pressure: if the table is loud, your defuse may be wasted on a bluff rather than a real threat.

### Throw Throw Burrito: A dodge-and-dance of chaos
- Setup: Each player grabs a burrito and the deck is shuffled. The goal is to collect sets of cards while navigating the burrito-duel atmosphere that the game creates.
- Game flow: The rounds alternate between “Throw” and “Burrito” cards. When a Throw card is played, players must throw a burrito at someone else; when a Burrito card is played, you better be ready to dodge. The core loop is simple but effective: collect pairs, dodge and throw, and hope your reflexes are better than your friends’ excuses for failing to dodge.
- Scoring and victory: The first player to complete a set of three pairs wins upon that round’s end risks a chaotic, triumphant burrito-landing moment that is as satisfying as it is ridiculous.
- The social layer: The burritos are the in-house physical comedy device. They create a dynamic of “who’s got the center of the table fearlessly?” and “who’s still laughing at the last throw?” It’s a living room dodgeball game that refuses to stay within the lines.

The mechanical density of Throw Throw Burrito isn’t about deep strategy; it thrives on tempo. It rewards quick thinking, improvisation, and a willingness to risk your best hand for a ridiculous moment that becomes a cherished memory of that game night. If Exploding Kittens is the witty one-liner in a crowded chat group, Throw Throw Burrito is the chaotic physical comedy that turns your living room into a tiny gymnasium of laughter.

The real joy is in the contrast. The two games both wear the same playful wig but behave differently under the rest of the night’s energy. ## Strategy Tips and Quick-Take Guidance

- Exploding Kittens tips
  - Save Defuse cards for the most dangerous moment. There’s always that one player who can turn the tide with a single Defuse, so keep track of the odds.
  - Pay attention to the discard pile: seeing which cards have been played gives you a clue about what’s left in the deck. It’s not a foolproof predictor, but it’s better than guessing with your eyes closed.
  - Bluff with confidence. A confident no-move can be more effective than wasting a Defuse on a less dramatic moment. It’s a theater of micro-bluffs that works especially well when the table is loud and everyone is riding high on cat memes.
- Throw Throw Burrito tips
  - Space matters. If you’re in a cramped room, you’ll feel the burritos flying around like a confetti storm. A little room to dodge makes the spectacle much more enjoyable.
  - Don’t just chase sets; time your throws. The best players aren’t always the ones who collect the most cards but those who throw at the precise moment to disrupt others’ rhythm.
  - Safety first, humor always. You’re throwing foam burritos, not projectiles. Clear the play area, emphasize safety, and let the comedic moment shine without injury.
