## How to Play: The Core Mechanics Demystified

Two games, two rulesets, one box: chaos with a side of charisma. To keep things straight, we’ll treat Exploding Kittens and Throw Throw Burrito as separate but complementary experiences.

### Exploding Kittens: A crash course
- Setup: Shuffle the Exploding Kittens deck, deal a few cards to each player, and place the Defuse cards in the draw pile. The objective is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card.
- Basic turn flow: On your turn, you play cards that affect other players or your own fate, then you draw a card. The tension comes from the possibility that a well-timed Defuse could turn doom into a dramatic save, reshuffling the peril back into the deck.
- The Exploding Kitten card: Draw it and you’re toast—unless you have a Defuse. Defuse cards are precious currency. They buy you a lifeline, but the deck remains a fickle adversary that loves to shuffle mischief back into the stack.
- Endgame: The last player standing with at least one non-exploded kitten in their hand wins. It’s a goofy, oddly satisfying victory that makes you mutter, I should have kept that Defuse, while secretly plotting your next bluff.

The strategy isn’t grand-scheme deep, but it is a tapestry of micro-decisions: when to deploy a card, which Defuse to hold, and how to read the table’s tempo. The social pressure at the table is real: the louder the room, the more likely a bluff will fool someone who wants to be fooled—and will pretend to be fooled for dramatic effect.

### Throw Throw Burrito: A dodge-and-dance of chaos
- Setup: Each player grabs a burrito and the deck is shuffled. The aim is to collect matching cards while steering through the burrito chaos.
- Game flow: Round structure alternates between Throw cards and Burrito cards. When a Throw card hits the table, players fling burritos at someone; when a Burrito card goes off, you better be ready to dodge. The central loop is simple: collect sets, throw and dodge, and hope you’re quicker than your friends at ducking the impact of a squeaky toy weapon.
- Scoring and victory: The first player to complete a set of three pairs wins when the current round ends. That moment of triumphant burrito contact is equal parts ridiculous and delightful.
- The social layer: Foam burritos are the built-in physical comedy device. They create a living-room dodgeball vibe, where control of space and timing is as crucial as a sharp eye for card combos.

The depth here isn’t about heavy strategy; it’s about tempo, rhythm, and improvisation. The best players know when to press their luck, when to seed chaos, and when to step back to let the room erupt in laughter. If Exploding Kittens is the witty one-liner at a party, Throw Throw Burrito is the pratfall that earns you the title of the night’s official chaos conductor.

## Strategy Tips and Quick-Take Guidance

- Exploding Kittens tips
  - Save Defuse cards for the moments that feel decisive. There’s always a hero with a Defuse-sized swing, so track the odds and don’t waste them on fluff.
  - Watch the discard pile. A quick scan shows which cards are exhausted and which might still haunt the deck. It’s not perfect, but it’s better than pure guesswork.
  - Bluff with confidence. A well-timed non-move can be as effective as a bold play. In a loud room, a dramatic silence can be worth more than a draw.
- Throw Throw Burrito tips
  - Space and pace matter. If you’re crowded, the burritos become confetti meerkats; you’ll get less dodge and more accidental chaos.
  - Time your throws. The best players don’t always chase cards; they disrupt rhythms, create openings, and make the table laugh when the timing is peak.
  - Safety first, comedy always. Foam burritos are safe under the right circumstances, but clear the play area to avoid unintended face-splat moments. Humor should be loud, not injuries.
