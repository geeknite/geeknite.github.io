### How Skip-Bo is played (a quick refresher)
If you have never played Skip-Bo, or if you need a refresher after last year’s family reunion, here is the high level:

- Each player starts with a certain number of stock piles, facedown, with cards in hand. The goal is to play all the stock cards onto building piles in ascending order from 1 to 12.
- The trick is that you can use cards from your hand, or from the top of your stock piles, or even from a draw pile to continue the sequences.
- Wild cards can substitute for any number, which makes for dramatic late game moments where someone turns a total chaos into a clean finish.
- The game ends when a player has emptied all their stock piles. Simple in concept, triumphant in execution when the table agrees to stop arguing about “just one more round.”

In the deluxe edition, the rulebook is your friend. It is the sort of booklet that uses diagrams to show the right order of play and includes a few scenarios that explain common edge cases. It is not a long read, but it is a welcome companion to ensure you do not accidentally turn this into a micro-mafia variant where you pass wilds like a political bargaining chip.
