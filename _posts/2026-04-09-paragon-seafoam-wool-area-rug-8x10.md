### Care and maintenance: keeping the seafoam fresh

Maintenance is where wool shines and where your patience really pays off. The most important part is to vacuum regularly with a suction head designed for area rugs. Use a slower pass rather than a frantic, heavy-pass vacuuming technique; you don’t want to pull fibers loose from the backing. For spills, blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and use a mild detergent if needed. If you’re dealing with pet accidents, a white vinegar dilution can help neutralize odors before you wash the spot with a proper rug cleaner. Always test cleaners on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure color retention.

One trick that helps longevity: rotate the rug every few months. Wool fibers settle over time, and a gentle rotation keeps wear evenly distributed, preserving the seafoam color and overall feel. If you’re into the data-driven approach, track wear with a simple photo log. You’ll appreciate the difference when you compare your rug after a year to your first-week photos.

### Maintenance myths debunked: wool vs the world

There are several myths about wool rugs that deserve a quick debunk. First, wool is not inherently fragile; it’s a robust fiber that resists wear when properly cared for. Second, wool does not require a special detergent or lavish cleaning ritual; simple, mild cleaners and mild agitation work just fine. Third, wool rugs are not impossible to clean after a spill; the key is quick action and the correct approach. The Paragon Seafoam is a good example of how a wool rug can be both surprisingly forgiving and very stylish at the same time.
