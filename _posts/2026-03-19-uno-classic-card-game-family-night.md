## What is UNO?
UNO is a color coded, number based shedding game. The objective is simple: play a card that matches the color or the number on the top of the discard pile or use an action card to shift the tempo. The deck is a neon rainbow with four colors, a handful of special cards, and a handful of wilds that let you alter the color in play. The core loop is quick: draw when you cannot play, chain moves, and try to empty your hand first. The moment you get down to one card, the pressure is on and the room collectively decides whether the winner will be you or your luck-chasing sibling.

## How to play: quick refresher
### Setup and initial draw
Each player starts with seven cards. The rest of the deck is the draw pile, and the top card becomes the starting card on the discard pile. You can play a card that matches color or number to begin the action. If you cannot play, draw a card and check if it can be played immediately.

### Turn flow
On your turn, you can:
- Play a card that matches color or number with the top discard.
- If you cannot play, draw a card. If the drawn card can be played, you may place it immediately.
- Use action cards strategically: Skip knocks out the next player, Reverse flips the play direction, and Draw Two forces the next player to draw two cards.

### UNO call and end game
When you have one card left, you must shout UNO. If another player catches you not saying UNO before your next turn, you draw two cards as a penalty. The first player to shed all cards wins.

### Variants to mix it up
UNO has a suite of variants that keep things fresh. UNO Flip introduces a two sided deck with a light side and a dark side that flips color strategy. UNO Dare adds goofy challenges that players must perform while playing. There are also themed editions featuring superheroes, space opera, and more. The core experience remains the same: fast paced, easy to learn, and endlessly replayable.

Observing which colors are thinning from the discard pile can inform your next move and create opportunities for clever plays or last minute blocks.

## Strategies and tips
- Use color changes to limit the opponents' playable cards. If you can force them into a color they dislike, they will struggle to respond.
- Keep wild cards for moments when you truly need to flip the table in your favor, rather than as a last resort.
- Pay attention to opponents counts and plan your endgame to maximize your chance of closing out on a strong color or with a wild card that can leave others scrambling.
- If the table rotates into a draw two cascade, you can turn the tide by playing a card that resets the cycle in your favor.
