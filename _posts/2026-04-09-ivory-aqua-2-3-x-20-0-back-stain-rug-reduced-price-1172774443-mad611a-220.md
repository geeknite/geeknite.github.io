## Cleaning and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh Without the Ritual
Maintenance is the boring yet essential part of rug ownership. The Ivory Aqua is designed to be less-than-lord-of-dirt on the daily, but you still have to show it some love. Regular vacuuming, with a light touch to avoid friction that can loosen fibers, should keep surface dirt at bay. When spills occur, act quickly: blot (do not rub) with a clean cloth, and if possible follow up with a mild cleaning solution appropriate for synthetic fibers. The key is to avoid harsh chemicals that might degrade the coloring or fiber integrity over time.

We recommend a gentle cleaning routine rather than a heroic rescue operation. If you are the accidental spill monarch, you’ll appreciate a rug that accepts your mess and invites you to try again with less drama. For stubborn stains, a dedicated stain remover designed for synthetic blends is a safer bet than a DIY experiment that ends in a blended disaster. Always test any cleaner on a small, inconspicuous corner first to avoid surprises.

As part of maintenance, rotate the rug every few months if possible. This helps even out wear and keeps the color balance consistent across the width and length. Consider whether you’ll need professional help to lay it down flat in a multi-room space. Long runners can be stubborn against curling edges, especially when poorly rolled.
