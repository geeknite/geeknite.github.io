## Divider system: privacy without isolation

The heart of this system is the divider module. Dividers do more than hide faces; they dampen sound, carve personal space, and give you a stage where you can stage your own productivity performance. The two main types—fabric and acrylic—each have use cases.

### Fabric dividers: calm in color

Fabric-dividers are wrapped in a sound-absorbent textile that reduces mid-range chatter and keyboard click noise from becoming your audio wallpaper. They come in a palette of muted grays, blues, and a surprisingly daring ember red. The advantages:

- Acoustic dampening: helps reduce the general office noise floor by a noticeable margin, especially in open-plan spaces.
- Temperature perception: fabric panels can feel slightly warmer on the cheeks, which in winter means you’ll pretend you’re getting a hug from your desk.
- Maintenance: spot-clean friendly; stubborn coffee stains? Not uncommon, but erasable with the right cleaner.

### Acrylic dividers: clarity with a side of transparency

Acrylic dividers provide visibility and a modern sci-fi vibe. They’re especially useful for roles that require quick visual checks, like a product manager who needs to glance at a screen without turning into a periscope. The downsides?

- Sound leakage: not as good as fabric at absorbing noise, so you’ll want to pair with a soft rug underfoot and maybe some white noise.
- Clarity concerns: glare management is essential—avoid direct sunlight or alien friend refractive angles. A matte protective film can help.
- Cleaning: finger smudges galore; you’ll be cleaning this weekly with a microfiber and a smile because you’re still in a future-grade workspace.

### Configuring dividers for ten: a practical guide

The system ships with ten divider modules that can be swapped across positions. If you want a bit of theater, you can create a “quiet zone” with fabric panels in the middle four stations and acrylic on the ends to keep a sense of openness. If you want each workstation to feel distinct, you chain the dividers in alternating fabrics to create a wallpaper of privacy. Our recommendation: start with fabric in the central zone and acrylic at the ends, then iterate after a two-week trial period. You’ll be surprised how the same space feels different simply by rearranging panels.

## Ergonomics: keeping comfort alive in a ten-person orchestra

Ergonomics isn’t just a buzzword; it’s a survival strategy when you’re juggling ten humans, each with a different set of preferences and an ergonomic stance they swear by. The Eternal System addresses ergonomics at three layers: desk height, monitor positioning, and chair compatibility.

### Desk height and stability

The desk height is adjustable within a practical range that suits the 5th percentile to the 95th percentile of the adult population. The system uses a screw-based leveling mechanism that holds firm after you tighten it down; there’s nothing more demoralizing than a desk that slowly migrates into a desk-shaped crater during a sprint. The ten-seat arrangement is anchored to a shared base, but each station’s leg can be adjusted independently to accommodate wheelchairs, tall folks, and those who prefer to keep their coffee cup at chest height for dramatic effect.

### Monitor arms and keyboard trays

Each station can be equipped with a modular monitor arm at a recommended height of approximately eye level when seated. Keyboard trays are optional but highly recommended if you’re one of those people who believe wrists are optional at work; in reality, we’re more concerned with long-term tendinopathy here. The trays can slide left or right, which is essential when the person on station 7 insists on a 27-inch monitor the size of a small TV.

### Chair compatibility and space economy

It’s a ten-seat system, so you’ll want chairs that are comfortable, not too bulky, and easy to swap if someone’s back is being rebellious. The system doesn’t prescribe a single chair model; you can use standard task chairs that fit under the desk with a moderate footrest. The result is a seating forest that doesn’t require a chair reservation app to navigate. In practice, you’ll want chairs with good lumbar support, adjustable armrests, and a seat depth that accommodates people who love to live in their chairs for eight hours straight, plus one more because the eternal desk never stops.

## Practical tips for implementing the Eternal Desk in your space

1) Measure twice, configure once. The ten-seat footprint glides into a large but precise area. Create a floor plan with scaled sticky notes before you commit.
2) Plan for power and data. Run a dedicated power strip row along the center to keep the spine of the desk free from tripping hazards. Don’t rely on under-desk daisy chains—this is not a cable opera, your office deserves better.
3) Schedule a pilot run. Before committing to a full office-wide deployment, test the system with a few volunteers. Their feedback will guide the final layout, and that matters more than you think.
4) Involve IT early. Network and device management should be architected in parallel with the desk installation. A well-oiled onboarding can shave weeks from your typical hardware refresh cycle.
5) Develop a maintenance schedule. Your future self will thank you for setting a routine: quarterly tightening, monthly fabric cleaning, and annual inspection of structural joints.
