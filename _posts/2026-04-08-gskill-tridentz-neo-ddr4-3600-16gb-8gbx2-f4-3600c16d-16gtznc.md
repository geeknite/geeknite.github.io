Overclocking and Tuning Options
XMP profiles are a friend to simplification, and enabling DOCP or XMP on most boards will drop the kit into 3600 CL16 with minimal fuss. For the tinkerer, you can try stepping up toward 3733 or 3866 MHz depending on silicon lottery and cooling. It is important to approach memory overclocking with patience and a willingness to dial back if stability issues appear. The variance between individual kits means one motherboard may happily push to 3733 while another refuses to boot above 3600. The key is to test thoroughly with a memory stress test and monitor for any instability or data corruption. If you manage to coax more speed, you may see incremental gains in lightly threaded workloads or specific game scenes with heavy texture streaming.
