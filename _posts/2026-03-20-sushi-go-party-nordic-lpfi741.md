## Setup and gameplay flow
Setting up Sushi Go Party Nordic is a ritual that takes less than five minutes even with a handful of players. The active menu set is chosen based on the number of players, then spread across the table. Each player receives a stack of cards equal to the required hand size, the player aids are distributed, and you are ready to roll.

The gameplay loop is a familiar rhythm:
1) Each round, you draft one card from your hand and pass the rest to your neighbor.
2) You reveal your chosen card and perform any immediate effects (some cards grant on the fly bonuses or immediate points).
3) The process repeats until all cards in the hand are drafted.
4) At the end of the round, you score according to the active menu cards.
5) The next round begins with a fresh hand, a new set of menus, and a renewed opportunity to chase the dragon of a perfect set.

What makes Sushi Go Party Nordic engaging is not just the speed but the way scoring goals shift with each round. The use of multiple menus means you cannot settle into a single safe path. You have to be flexible, read the table, and decide whether to chase a short term get rich quick plan or a longer term strategy that pays off later in the game. The pacing remains excellent for a social night: short rounds, lots of laughter, and a constant sense of momentum that keeps even non-gamers engaged.

## Tips for players new to Sushi Go Party Nordic
- Start with a simple objective: pick a couple of menus and build a plan around one or two core combos. This reduces confusion and accelerates teaching for new players.
- Pay attention to neighbors: The game is as much about what you pass as what you keep. If you notice your left neighbor is chasing a particular set, you can foil them by choosing cards that block or disrupt their build.
- Don’t chase every bloom: Some menus pay off with small bonuses, while others deliver big points for specific patterns. Prioritize high value combos, but do not ignore small boosts that can swing a round when the scoring is close.
- Use the table talk to your advantage: The social aspect of a party game is part of the charm. Encourage jokes, light trash talk, and cheers when someone hits a big combo to keep energy high.
