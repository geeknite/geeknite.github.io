## The Basics of Skip-Bo

Skip-Bo is a classic card game designed for 2-6 players. The game revolves around creating sequences of cards from 1 to 12 while strategically using Skip-Bo wild cards to complete your piles. Think of it as "Uno" meets "Solitaire," but with more opportunities to sabotage your opponents.

### Components

The game comes with a deck of cards featuring numbers from 1 to 12 and the coveted Skip-Bo wild cards. The artwork is simple yet functional, allowing players to focus on their cunning strategies and devious plans.

**Fun Fact**: The Skip-Bo wild cards can be used as any number, making them the ultimate weapon in your quest for victory.

## Gameplay

Skip-Bo is all about strategy, luck, and occasionally yelling at your family members. Players must work together (or not) to create sequences of cards while fending off the challenges posed by their opponents. Each player takes turns drawing cards, playing them in ascending order, and strategically using their Skip-Bo wild cards.

### Turn Phases

Each turn consists of several phases:

1. **Draw Phase**: Players draw cards to replenish their hands.
2. **Play Phase**: Players play cards from their hand to their build piles or discard piles.
3. **Discard Phase**: Players discard any remaining cards, preparing for the next turn.

### Winning and Losing

To win, players must be the first to deplete their stockpile of cards by creating sequences from 1 to 12. If you run out of cards in your stockpile, it's game over for your opponents.

**Pro Tip**: Always keep an eye on your opponents' discard piles. They might be setting themselves up for a big play, and your Skip-Bo wild card could be the perfect counterattack.
