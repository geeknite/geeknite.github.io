## What is in the box
- Four player dashboards, each with dedicated spaces for health, armor, level, gear slots and action economy
- Four stand bases so you can keep the dashboards upright during the chaos of a zombie horde
- Four overlay cards, to protect the track from stray dice and spilled coffee (we know you drink coffee during long sessions)
- One quick start guide that somehow manages to be both cheerful and practical

## The design language and build quality
The four dashboards in this set adopt a bold yet legible design. The color coding is crisp, with a distinct hue for each player to avoid the classic moment when you realize you have been tracking your neighbor s wounds for the last 15 minutes. The edges are smooth, the corners friendly, and the overall thickness gives you a sense of durability without the fear of applying too much torque and sending a component flying across the table. The overlay cards are designed to sit on top of the dashboards to prevent wear on the printed sections, which in practical terms means fewer head shakes about why the numbers look strange after you accidentally spilled the water bottle on the table.

Material choice matters here. The dashboards are a thick acrylic or plastic composite that feels sturdy and resistant to warping. It is the kind of product that invites a gentle slam of the fist in triumph after a successful round, but not so stiff that you worry about cracking it if someone forgets how to pick up a token properly. The magnets or stands that hold the dashboards upright are well balanced; they do not require you to wrestle the stand into position while a hammer-wielding zombie gnashes at your ankles. If you have a clumsy group like ours, you will appreciate the stability these stands provide.

## How it alters the flow of play
Zombicide Black Plague is a game of careful resource management, planning, and occasional chaos. The official components do a fine job of tracking health, actions, and the ever growing pile of loot. However, once you add a dozen tokens and a couple of expansion boards, the table can start to look like the control panel of a starship. The Zombicide Black Plague Player Dashboard Set of 4 aims to solve this by giving each player a dedicated, eye friendly track that consolidates the most important stats in one place.

The major minus for any accessory is how it affects the pace of your game. If the product takes too long to set up, it can become more trouble than it is worth. In our testing, setup time was a breeze. You slide the dashboard into its stand, position the overlay exactly where you want the tokens to rest, and you are ready to go within a couple of minutes. The learning curve is minimal; most players will understand how to place their wound and experience counters in the first round, after which a slightly smug smile appears when you realize you have control of your own data and you did not forget to bring the right player board.

## Practical use cases at the table
If you play Zombicide Black Plague with four players, the dashboards thrive in a few key situations. First, in the early game when you are moving as a group and gathering resources, the dashboards help you quickly confirm your available actions and the status of your fighters without leafing through a pile of tokens. Second, during the mid game when the horde grows, the quick reference for wound tokens becomes a sanity saver. Third, in the late game when you are trying to squeeze every last turn out of your heroes, the clarity of the dashboard allows you to plan combos, chain movements, and coordinate cross-hero interactions without losing track of who has not drawn a card yet.

For groups that like to push the limits of time management, these dashboards reduce the risk of miscounting turns and misplacing tokens. It is the sort of accessory that quietly pays for itself by letting you skip arguments about how many actions you have left and whether you can loot that stubborn zombie nest in the next round. There is something incredibly satisfying about seeing your damage dealt, your experience gained, and your current inventory neatly aligned within a single visual. It is like carrying a tiny, well organized data center to a medieval battlefield.

The aesthetics are purposely on theme. The Black Plague era is captured in the font choices, card slots, and the color palette. The dashboards nod to the gritty fantasy atmosphere without drifting into gaudy cartoon territory. If you enjoy the vibe of a dark, cavernous crypt where a party of adventurers is planning their next reckless move, you will appreciate the way these dashboards blend theme with function. The Fred, Ross, Will, Helen naming gag adds personality; it is a small wink that makes a long gaming evening feel a little less like a spreadsheet and a little more like a clandestine meeting of your local heroes.

## How easy is it to maintain and clean
Let us be honest: any accessory that sits in the middle of a gamer's table will attract fingerprints, dice scuffs, and the occasional droplet of beverage. The dashboards clean up nicely with a soft dry cloth and a mild wipe. If you use liquid markers to annotate statuses, be mindful of the finish; some marker pen nibs can smear if you apply too much ink, so you may want to use the clean markers recommended by the vendor or test on a small corner first. We tested both a damp microfiber cloth and a gentle wipe of isopropyl alcohol on a small area; the latter may remove some of the top layer balancing if applied too aggressively, so the safe route is a light hand and regular cleaning.

A tiny but nice touch is the way the overlay cards protect the main surface. This reduces the risk of wear from repeated dice placement and token juggling. The overlays feel like proper guard rails for your counting system, which is a fancy way of saying you do not have to worry about whether that red wound counter will slide into the wrong track and ruin your next round.

## In the wild: what the players think
We ran a couple of sessions to see how the dashboards fared in real life. The consensus was that the dashboards reduce the cognitive load. The players were quicker at calculating movement allowances, and there was less time spent on the after action reconciliation of who took how many wounds. The color coding helped new players join the table faster; the old hands enjoyed the clarity, and there was a certain quiet satisfaction when your own dashboard communicates your status more clearly than your own gaming posture does.

A couple of caveats: if you are playing a particularly chaotic scenario with a lot of token juggling, the dashboards can feel a little bulky. They do occupy a fair amount of table real estate; if you are playing a tiny kitchen table, consider reorganizing your seating or using a larger surface. Also, if your group uses non standard house rules that require more customization than the dashboards provide, you may need an extra sheet or two to capture unique conditions. The set is excellent for standard play, but none of us expected it to replace a full rulebook or the need for occasional notes on a sticky note.

## How it stacks up against the alternatives
There are a handful of player aids on the market, and the market is not exactly starving for every new accessory in the four player category. Compared to other dashboards, this set hits a sweet spot for four reasons. First, the four player dashboards are color coded and designed for quick reference rather than for a complicated multi page interface. Second, the stands offer stable support and the ability to keep your board clean from the usual token shuffling that tends to happen on a busy table. Third, the overlay cards provide a durable protective layer that reduces wear and tear on your main dashboard. Fourth, the set is reasonably priced given the number of pieces and the build quality.

Yes, you can buy generic card sleeves and black marker pens and cobble something together yourself, but the crisp layout and the dedicated dashboard design will save you time and brainpower during the heat of battle. It is not a magic wand that will make you win every scenario, but it is a tool that helps you avoid counting mistakes and argument fatigue, which is basically the entire point of a well designed table accessory.
