## Design and Build Quality
### Chips: Weight, Feel, and Color Code
Chips are the lifeblood of any poker kit. If they feel like cheap bottle caps in your hand, the entire experience collapses faster than a bluff on the river. The typical set in this package uses molded clay composite chips with weight distribution designed to feel substantial when you pick them up. You’ll notice a satisfying heft in your stack, with a solid “thunk” when you slam a rack on the felt. The color scheme often follows a classic casino palette—white for the blanks and a dark color set for denominations. The pigment is usually durable, so even with heavy use and occasional red wine spills, the chips retain their logo and display.

What to watch for: edge wear and center-insert wear are the two most common signs that a set has seen a lot of games. A well-made set will show contact wear on the denomination marks before the chips themselves crack or flake. If you handle a lot of chips in a single game session, consider investing in a chip-tray or a simple rack to prevent edge damage. Pro-tip from the Geeknite table: rotate the chips occasionally during longer sessions to distribute wear evenly and keep the visual appeal high.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Sharp
A thing that looks great can also be a thing that looks tired if you neglect it. Here are practical tips to keep your chips and case looking crisp:
