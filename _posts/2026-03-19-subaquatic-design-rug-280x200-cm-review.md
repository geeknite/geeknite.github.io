## Maintenance: Cleaning Without Drowning in Detergent
Maintenance is the villain you invite to your party only if you forget to feed it. The Subaquatic rug is designed for easy cleaning. A quick vacuum on a regular schedule keeps surface dirt from sinking into the fibers. For spills, blot gently with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper and make your living room smell like a lighthouse after an oil spill. If you do get a stubborn stain, check the care label. Many synthetic rugs tolerate mild detergents and water, but you should avoid harsh solvents that can damage colorfastness and fiber integrity.

Periodic deep cleaning may be advisable for households with heavy traffic or pets. A gentle steam-cleaning cycle, used sparingly, can rejuvenate the fibers and lift the undersea grime that invisible-hands might not notice until it surfaces as a damp memory. Let the rug dry completely before stepping back onto it to avoid a damp, unplanned spa day for your feet.

### Cleaning Hacks (Because We’re All Busy):
- Quick tip: Use a fabric brush to lift tufts that have settled into a more compact state. It’s like a tiny spa for the rug’s surface.
- Odor control: Sprinkle baking soda sparingly, wait 15-20 minutes, vacuum thoroughly. It’s a quick reset that won’t turn your rug into a sodium lake.
- Pet fur management: A rubber glove or a damp microfiber cloth can help lift fur before vacuuming. It’s not magical, but it’s the closest thing to a hair‑magnet spell you’ll cast in a living room.

## Care and Tips: Getting the Most Out of Your Subaquatic Rug
- Rotate seasonally: If your room gets a lot of sun, rotate the rug every few months to prevent uneven fading.
- Vacuum regularly: Keep dust and dirt at bay to maintain color and texture. Use a vacuum with adjustable height to avoid snagging the fibers.
- Use a rug pad: A quality pad underneath improves comfort, reduces slipping, and prolongs the rug’s life by absorbing some of the impact from foot traffic.
- Spill management: Act fast on spills, blot rather than rub, and test stain-removal methods on a small area before full application.
- Layering ideas: Use neutral furniture and add accent pieces in teal, coral, and lime green to complement the rug’s underwater vibe. It’s a balanced palette that won’t visually fight the rug but supports its theme.
