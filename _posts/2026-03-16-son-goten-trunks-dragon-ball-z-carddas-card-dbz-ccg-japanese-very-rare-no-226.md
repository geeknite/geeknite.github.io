## Condition, grading, and care tips
If you’re fortunate enough to own this card, you’ll want to treat it with the reverence given to any rare artifact. Here are practical care tips:
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade and edge whitening.
- Use acid-free sleeves and a protective binder that prevents bending in transit. A magnetic or ring-bound binder can keep it flat and pristine.
- When handling, avoid touching the surface with bare fingers. Oils can dull gloss and exacerbate surface micro-scratches.
- If you decide to get it graded, choose a reputable service that understands Carddas materials and print history. The graded value can increase substantially if the card is preserved in near-mint or mint condition.
- Document the card’s provenance if possible, particularly if you acquired it through a trade or a convention sale. A short note about where and when you obtained the card adds a little extra story value for future owners.

For those who love a simple, quick check: look at the corners and edges for whitening, verify the holo glow if applicable, and test the central image for any color bleed. A well-preserved No 226 stands out among other rare cards because it maintains the crisp fusion action with minimal surface wear.
