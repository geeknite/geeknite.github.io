### How to Play: Quick Start
Skip-Bo is a sequencing game built on speed, memory, and the occasional last-minute strategic gambit. Here is the high-level flow you can rely on when you crack open the tin:

- Each player begins with a stock pile of cards facedown. The top card of this stock pile is flipped face up to begin your visible playing surface.
- In your turn, you draw to a hand size that keeps you in the loop. The goal is to play cards from your stock pile by making sequences on up to four shared build piles that everyone can contribute to.
- You can play a card from your hand or the top card of your stock pile onto the appropriate build piles if it fits the current sequence, which runs from 1 to 12. If you can place a 1 on an empty build pile, great; if you can continue a run with a 2, 3, etc., even better.
- Wild cards can be used as any number to help complete a sequence, but if you are the one who places a wild, you will be forced to declare what number it represents for the rest of that turn.
- The moment you get rid of all the cards from your stock pile, you win on that round. The rest of the players keep playing to determine who takes the round and who gets the bragging rights until the next round.

The quick-start rhythm makes the game accessible for younger players while still offering a few lines for the more strategic among us. The pacing can swing from rapid-fire (when several players have usable cards on the same turn) to more contemplative (when you’re eyeing the last few builds and trying not to block yourself with a misplayed card).
