## Design and Materials

### The Acacia Table
The centerpiece is a sturdy Acacia wood table with a warm, golden-brown tint that pairs nicely with the reclined, sun-drenched vibe of most backyards. Acacia is a sustainably harvested hardwood known for its natural oil finish that resists moisture better than naive pine, though it isn’t invincible. The tabletop surface typically ships pre-finished with a sealant designed to resist UV degradation and water absorption. In real-world terms, that means you can spill iced tea on it a dozen times without a lasting white blotch, assuming you wipe it up within 12 hours and don’t leave a mug of lava-hot coffee on it during a heatwave.

### The Rattan Chairs
Eight chairs surround the table, each built with a synthetic rattan/wicker weave over a metal frame. The look is classic patio chic—a nod to resort vibes while staying practical enough for everyday use. The weave feels responsive to the touch and not the brittle, dusty kind of wicker you see if you rummage through grandma’s attic. The frames are powder-coated steel, which should hold up to a fair amount of weather waltzing before the rust decides to crash the party.

## Durability and Weather Resistance

### UV Exposure, Rain, and Humidity Tests
We subjected this set to a typical solar smackdown: full sun for weeks, a rain shower every few days, and a humidity level that makes a humidifier feel underachieving. The Acacia table shows the kind of moisture resilience you’d expect from a hardwood table in a back-alley BBQ scene: minor micro-scratches on the surface, a faint uplift in color in sun-facing panels, and a slight warping risk if you neglect sealing for an extended period. The chairs’ wicker weave stayed tight and the joints didn’t loosen under normal use; we didn’t observe any immediate cracks or loosening of weave after the initial assembly cycle. The cushions held their shape reasonably well and dried promptly after rain, thanks to the quick-dry fabric and the slight elevation from the legs.

### Maintenance Tips to Extend Life
Here’s the low-down from our trial: wipe spills promptly to prevent staining; use a mild soap solution and a soft brush for annual cleaning; store cushions indoors or under cover during heavy rain or winter months; consider applying a marine-grade sealant to the Acacia table once a year for extra protection against moisture and UV rays. If you see small color variations across boards or weave spots over time, don’t panic—the natural material difference is part of the character and not a defect.

## Assembly and Maintenance

### Time, Tools, and Tips
Assembly is fairly straightforward, but not a five-minute job for complete newbies. Expect 1 to 3 hours depending on your DIY confidence and your ability to decipher included hardware from the instruction sheet. Common tools: a set of Allen wrenches (often included), a screwdriver, and a little patience. The most time-consuming step tends to be aligning the table legs and ensuring the umbrella hole lines up perfectly with the table center as you’re tightening the bolts. Take your time, use a soft surface to avoid scratching the Acacia table, and double-check all screws before you call it a day. If you’ve assembled similar sets before, this will feel familiar.

### Cleaning and Care
Post-assembly, the best way to keep the set looking fresh is routine care: wipe with a damp cloth for daily grime, a mild soap solution for tougher stains, and avoid abrasive cleaners that could degrade the protective finish. Cushions benefit from occasional spot-cleaning; for stubborn stains, a fabric cleaner designed for outdoor cushions works well. When not in use, store cushions in a dry, ventilated space to prevent mildew. A lightweight cover for the entire set can extend life by protecting from dust and bird traffic—because the latter likes to treat unoccupied chairs as a buffet.
