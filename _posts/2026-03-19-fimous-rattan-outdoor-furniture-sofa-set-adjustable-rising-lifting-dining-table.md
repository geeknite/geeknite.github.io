## Durability, Weather Resistance, and Maintenance
Rattan finishes can go either way in the long run. The synthetic weave here is designed to resist UV damage and moisture exposure, which means fewer color shifts and less cracking than raw wicker. That said, you’ll still want to protect your investment with some basic care: cover cushions when not in use, bring the cushions indoors during heavy storms, and wipe down the frame with a mild soap solution when you notice pollen or grime creeping into the weave.

Aluminum frames typically stand up well to moisture and temperature fluctuations, which is why you’ll often see them in modern patio furniture for humid climates and seasonal changes. The weight of the pieces also matters for durability in windy climates. If you live on a hill or a coastline where gusts have opinions, you’ll be glad the set anchors down reasonably well and doesn’t topple over during a particularly dramatic sea breeze.

Maintenance tips from the Geeknite lab: use a soft brush or cloth to avoid scuffing the weave, avoid harsh chemical cleaners (they can degrade the fabric and the polymer coating on the weave), and consider applying a UV protectant on the cushion fabric if you expect heavy sun exposure. For cushions, keep an eye on seam integrity; a small rip can turn into a big gnaw of fabric if not addressed.
