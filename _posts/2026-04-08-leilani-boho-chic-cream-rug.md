## Materials vs Maintenance: Care Are Not Opposites
No rug is maintenance-free, but some are friendlier than others. Leilani leans toward easy care without demanding heroic effort. Here is a straightforward care protocol that keeps the cream fresh without turning your Saturday into a cleaning vigil:

1) Vacuum weekly using a light attachment to avoid piling. The plush surface can trap crumbs and pet hair, so a gentle pass is the rule rather than the exception.
2) Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers.
3) For minor stains, mix a small amount of mild detergent with warm water, apply to the spot, blot, and rinse with clean water. Air dry thoroughly before walking back onto the rug.
4) Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and to prevent sun bleaching in one spot if your room gets strong sun exposure.
5) For tougher stains or persistent odors, consult a professional cleaning service or a rug care specialist. Do not use bleach or high heat, which can damage the fibers.

We also checked machine washing feasibility just to be thorough. The Leilani is designed for surface cleaning and spot care; it is not recommended for machine washing. This keeps the color stable and avoids the risk of curling edges or color migration.
