## Setup and maintenance: getting from box to bonfire
### Assembly basics
If you’ve assembled a TV stand from IKEA you can assemble this pit. The setup involves placing the ring on a stable surface, dropping the pit bowl into the ring, and finessing the spark screen into its grooves. The poker is your Hand of Winter, used to move coals without sacrificing your fashionable gloves. Most owners report 15 to 25 minutes from box to backyard bravado, with a short pause for wondering if it’s upright and a longer pause for safety checks—totally fair and healthy.

### Placement tips
- Put it on a non-combustible surface. A stone patio or a concrete slab is ideal.
- Keep a 1–2 meter clearance from any overhanging branches or decorative screens you still have from your DIY phase.
- Use the included spark screen when you’re not actively roasting or telling jokes; it helps manage embers and your neighbor’s paranoia about fire hazards.

### Routine care
- After use, let the pit cool completely before you move it. The steel will stay hot long enough to become a character in your backyard soap opera if you’re not careful.
- Clean out ash with a metal scoop; don’t use a plastic bucket, unless you want to add a new dimension to your neighbor’s insurance claim.
- Wipe down with a dry cloth to remove dust and debris. If you shoulder a stubborn stain, a mild soap and water solution works; just don’t soak the metal—rust is a party guest you didn’t invite.
- Consider applying a rust-preventive coating every season if you live in a humid or salty climate. It doesn’t have to be a full restoration; a light layer of protective spray is enough to keep the finish looking new for longer.

### Safety basics you can’t skip
Fire pits demand respect, and this XXL steel contender is no exception. Always operate in a well-ventilated outdoor space, never leave the fire unattended, and have a water source or fire extinguisher within arm’s reach. This isn’t hyperbole; embers can surprise you and your lawn furniture alike. In case you’re curious, the National Fire Protection Association offers general guidelines for outdoor fires that are worth a quick skim before your first roast: [NFPA Outdoor Fire Pit Safety]({https://www.nfpa.org/Topics/Outdoor-Fireplaces}).
