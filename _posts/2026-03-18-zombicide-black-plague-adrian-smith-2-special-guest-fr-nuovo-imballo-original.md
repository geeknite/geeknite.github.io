## Gameplay experience and feel
Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative dungeon crawl set in a medieval city. The core loop is familiar: move your survivor, search for gear, open doors, fight zombies, and level up to gain better tools for the next skirmish. The FR edition with the Adrian Smith figure does not change core mechanics; it changes the mood, which can be a big factor when you are in for a long night.

Players coordinate, plan, and occasionally blame the dice when the undead coordinate their chaos. The balance remains solid: you will feel the tension when the siege grows, you will cheer when a doorway reveals a path to safety, and you will groan when your last survivor whiffs a critical swing and the zombies flood the corridor. The pacing is usually 60 to 120 minutes for a standard 2 to 4 player session, with longer nights for bigger tables and less efficient players. The premium art adds flavor but does not alter the learning curve; you still teach the same core actions and the same deck of encounters.

## Accessibility and learning curve
Like many modern board games, Black Plague has layers of rules and a few tricky edge cases. The FR edition is no exception, but the production quality makes the basics easier to grasp. The game scales from 1 to 6 players, though the pace can slow with a larger table. The rulebook is thorough but approachable, and the card design is user friendly enough that you can teach a newbie in a single round.

To teach a new player, try a quick two-turn mini tutorial: first teach movement and search actions, then open a door and engage. When the first wave of zombies is defeated through a combination of teamwork and sharp dice, you will feel the momentum swing your way. If you want to speed learning, you can use a helper card that summarizes core actions while you focus on the story beats that make Zombicide fun.
