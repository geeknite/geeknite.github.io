## Care and Maintenance
### Keeping Cards Fresh
A common question among buyers is how to keep decks sealed until you are ready to use them and how to keep them in peak condition once you open them. Here are a few practical tips that apply to these Rider Back decks:
- Store decks in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading or warping of the stock.
- When opening, handle gently to preserve the integrity of the core stock and finish. A quick, clean break is better than a rough spear of the seal.
- If a card ever becomes sticky or dusty, wipe it with a soft microfiber cloth or give it a light kiss of air from a can of compressed air. Do not rub vigorously; you are not erasing a chalkboard slate, you are handling a delicate piece of maple wood disguised as cardboard.
- For long-term storage, keep the two decks flat and away from other paper goods that could rub against the faces of the cards and transfer ink or dust. 

### Cleaning and Maintenance Tips for Pros
If you practice cardistry or perform regularly, you may encounter slight stickiness after heavy play. Try a light fan of cards to distribute oils or a mild air-out day to regain the original feel. Some players favor microfibers for quick wipe downs, but avoid aggressive cleaners that can degrade the finish. Your goal is a consistent glide, not a scuff battle with your own equipment.
