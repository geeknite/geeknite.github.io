## Practical unboxing tips and setup tips
If you are unboxing this bundle mid-evening, you’ll want a plan that reduces chaos and increases survival probability. Here are some practical tips that will help you maximize the first night’s play:
- Clear a large table space: Zombicide is big on components; you’ll need to layout miniatures, tokens, and the board without stepping on a zombie leg or your own snack stash.
- Sort the tokens by type: loot, tools, doors, and trash. A dedicated tray saves you the time you’d otherwise spend trying to locate the “loot 3” token when the zombie is about to bite your neck.
- Read the quick-start once before you play: It helps you avoid crippling mistakes, like forgetting to activate a survivor’s special ability during a crucial moment.
- Consider color-coded player aids: If your group includes newbies, a color-coding can be a lifesaver, especially when you have many survivors on the board.
- Use a timer for rounds: Zombicide can become a slow grind if you talk through every single action in meticulous detail. A soft timer (two minutes per turn) ensures the group remains engaged without turning the game into a lecture hall.
