## Playing the Classic Game — Rules and House Rules
Skip-Bo s core mechanic—playing sequences of cards in ascending order—lends itself to quick play and flexible strategies. The vintage edition follows the same spirit but with its own personality. Here s a quick, spoiler-light refresher for newbies and a nod to old-school players who crave a touch of period flavor:

### Basic Rules (Vintage Edition Flavor)
- Each player starts with a stock pile, draws on their turn, and attempts to play cards in ascending order on building piles. The twist: you can use wilds when you have them, but you must still obey the stock pile s constraints.
- You may play from your hand or your stock pile onto the building piles in a valid sequence. The deck s wilds act as legal substitutes, but you should plan their use to avoid early blowouts.
- The goal is to be the first to deplete your stock pile, which means you re not just playing a game; you re orchestrating a delicate escape plan from the clutches of a nicely arranged card universe.

### House Rules You Might Encounter at Vintage Tables
