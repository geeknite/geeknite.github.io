### Doors, panels, and seals
The front door on the AVIT 42U comes with a lock and a tempered glass option, depending on the market. We tested the lock and found it perfectly adequate for securing smoke alarms, cat dashboards, and the occasional film canister left behind by the last maintenance window. The rails are typical 19-inch rails with adjustable depth and consistent mounting holes. We appreciated that the rails lock into place and stay there under the weight of a few servers. ## Installation and day-to-day usability
### Unboxing and first impressions
The AVIT rack ships well-packed with foam corners and a basic set of assembly hardware. The instruction manual is not a novel you need to memorize, but it’s more helpful than your fridge’s user manual. The components are clearly labeled and organized so you can follow the step-by-step process without needing to call in your neighbor who once built a TV stand out of IKEA instructions. In about 60-90 minutes, with two people and a small amount of patience, you’ll have the base structure assembled and ready for rails.

### Floor prep and positioning
Before you start, you’ll want a flat, level floor. The feet on the AVIT 42U are adjustable, which is handy if you’re installing on a slightly non-flat surface (as many home basements are). A quick laser level check will prevent you from mounting the door and panels only to realize the equipment tilts like a leaning tower of routers. If you have to install on uneven tile or concrete, consider leveling shims; the rack is robust enough to hold its geometry if you take a moment to set the feet correctly.

### Mounting your equipment
We used a mix of roughly a virtualization host, a 1U switch, a 2U router, two 2U storage devices, and a handful of PDUs and patch panels. Everything mounted cleanly along the rails with enough clearance to avoid cable interference. There’s a little tolerance for heavy equipment near the bottom, but the 42U height allows you to spread your heavier devices across multiple rack units without forcing you to hump the entire unit forward every time you reach for a tool.

### Cable management: the hidden art
Cable management is half engineering and half origami. The AVIT 42U ships with standard cable management features and optional rod trays and vertical cable managers. We recommend setting up the vertical managers first and then running the cables in a logical pattern: power lines on one side, data on the other, and a bridge in the middle to keep them from tangling in that midnight moment when you discover a mismatched fiber patch that looks suspiciously like your childhood's spaghetti experiment. The depth helps. You have room behind the equipment to route cables neatly and use cable ties without resorting to Velcro torture devices.

### Cooling and airflow considerations
The 600 mm depth is a sweet spot for airflow. Deeper racks exist, but they cause you to wonder if you’ve built a small data processing city inside your living room. The AVIT 42U can be configured with a perforated front door, perforated rear door, and optional fan kits that can move a surprising amount of air through the front-to-back orientation. If your equipment runs hot, consider adding a front-to-rear cooling strategy. The space behind the front door and above the equipment allows for air movement so hot air isn’t getting trapped behind a server’s hip-height area. We tested with a modest load and observed a noticeable ambient temperature drop when the proper airflow kit was installed.

### Security and access control
Security matters. The front door lock plus rear door access provide basic tamper resistance. If you’re deploying in a shared office space, you’ll appreciate the lockable front cover and keyed rear door. The unit supports standard padlocks for extra security if you’re going to lend it to colleagues with questionable cable management skill sets. While not a fortress, the rack offers a predictable level of security for home and small-business environments.
