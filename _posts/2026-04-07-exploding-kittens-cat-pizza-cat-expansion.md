## How to Play with Cat Pizza Cat Expansion
The expansion is designed to slot into the base Exploding Kittens rules with minimal fuss, which is the nicest compliment you can give to a micro-expansion. Here are the essential adjustments you’ll encounter:

### Setup and Integration
- Shuffle the new Cat Pizza Cat cards into the base deck. No need for a complicated reroll of your life choices; just mingle the toppings with the base chaos and let destiny choose the pizza topping you deserve.
- Introduce the Cat Oven Card and the Pizza Tokens into the discard chain. They’re not weapons of mass hilarity, but they might feel like it when the discard pile turns into a cheesy desert mirage.
- The rest of the rules stay the same: cats, explosions, and the endless joy of not being the person who actually explodes first.

### Card Interactions
- Cat Pizza Cat cards add flavor effects that trigger during a player’s turn like tiny, fluffy micro-shocks of delight. They often reward bold plays or punish overly cautious passes with a pizza slice of doom.
- Pizza Tokens are a neat mechanic that modifies how discard piles behave. They can be spent to influence draws or to bend the fate of a card from the top of the deck. The tension here is real, because you are basically paying with your dignity to influence a pizza-themed fate.
- The Cat Oven Card creates a temporary zone where certain effects become more chaotic. It’s not game-breaking, but it does create delicious moments where players suddenly realize they should have planned a bit more for the kitchen sink of chaos.

### Strategy Adjustments
- The expansion rewards players who plan a couple of turns ahead, but it also punishes the overly cautious. You’ll want to balance aggression with caution, like a chef who knows when to add more heat and when to step back and let the toppings settle.
- Cat Pizza Cat cards often tilt the balance toward mid-game aggression, which is a delightful change from the very late-game doom cycles you sometimes see in base play. Expect more heated debates about who has to draw next and who owes the next round of pepperoni slices.

From a design perspective, the new cards are well-balanced for a micro-expansion. They don’t overwhelm the core mechanics, but they add enough spice to make late rounds feel fresh. Cat Pizza Cat nudges the pacing toward a more dynamic middle game where more players feel like they have a stake in every draw. It’s not a guarantee that every round will end with a dramatic pizza-themed catastrophe, but it certainly increases the odds of something hilariously memorable happening around the table.
