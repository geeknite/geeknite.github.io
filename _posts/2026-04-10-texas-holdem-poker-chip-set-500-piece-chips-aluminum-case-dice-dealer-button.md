## The chips: design, weight, and feel

A big part of any chip set’s personality is how it feels in your fingers. Chips made from a traditional clay composite feel sturdy and slightly grippy; resin-based chips can feel slick. A few marks to evaluate are:

- Finish and inlay: solid color layers with crisp inlays resist chipping. If you find a chip where the denomination rubs off after a few rounds, that’s a sign you might want to upgrade sooner rather than later.

## Setup, care, and maintenance

- Setup: Unpack, check the contents, and arrange chips by denomination. Use the dealer button to mark the starting seat. A quick mock round can help new players acclimate to the pace before real money or high stakes bluffing enters the chat.
- Cleaning: Wipe chips with a soft, dry cloth to remove fingerprints and dust. If you’ve got a sticky gaming area, a damp microfiber cloth (lightly damp, not soaked) can help—but avoid soaking the chips; moisture can cause discoloration or warp edges.
- Case care: Wipe down the aluminum exterior with a dry cloth. If your case has a soft interior, ensure it remains clean so chips don’t snag on fibers. Avoid slamming the lid; it’s elegant hardware, not a hammer.

Tips for longevity: keep the set in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight. The plastic-laminate on the chips’ color inlays can fade if exposed to UV rays over years, not days, but protection is cheap and sanity is priceless.
