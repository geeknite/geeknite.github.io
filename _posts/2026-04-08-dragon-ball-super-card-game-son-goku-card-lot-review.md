## What’s in a Son Goku Card Lot Anyway?
A Son Goku card lot is not a standardized product. ## Quality Check: How to Inspect Your Lot Like a Pro
Before you lay down cash or commit to a long-term trade, do a quick quality audit. This helps you avoid buyer’s remorse and the embarrassment of an accidental mint-in-sealed-bag-catapult moment when you realize your Goku is upside down or miscut.

- Inspect edges and corners: ensure there’s no heavy whitening, creasing, or corner bending. A little wear is normal in a lot, but major dinging reduces value.
- Check foiling consistency: foil cards should have even sheen and no missing foil patterns. Inconsistent foiling is a sign of printing variance or damage.
- Verify print quality: if you spot misprints or miscuts (slightly off-center text, border misalignment), consider negotiating or passing on that particular card. Major misprints could be misrepresented as rare versions—watch for that.
- Confirm authenticity: counterfeit DBS CG cards exist in the wild, often with suspicious font choices, odd holo patterns, or unnatural card stock feel. If a lot feels suspiciously cheap or suspiciously perfect for the price, you might be dealing with fakes. When in doubt, a quick side-by-side comparison with a known authentic card or a certified store’s return policy can save you headaches.

## How to Store and Use Your Lot
Storage and organization are the silent heroes of any card collection. Without a sane system, a killer Son Goku lot becomes a tangled mess that embarrasses you during a casual corner-side reveal. Here are practical tips:

- Use dividers and binders: separate by rarity, then by set. Use labeled dividers for easy retrieval and quick counting.
- Invest in card sleeves and top-loaders: even if you don’t plan to play every card, protecting the surface from scuffs and oils ensures the lot remains vibrant for the long haul.
- Create a “playable picks” subset: choose the best Goku cards that actually fit your deck strategy. Keep the rest in a loose tray if you intend to trade or someday flip them as a bundle.
- Document your collection: maintain a simple spreadsheet or a note with set names, card names, and condition notes. This helps you track what you have and what you might want to acquire later.

If you’re a streamer or a content creator, consider building a “Goku-focused” deck showcase or unboxing video that documents the lot’s contents visually. Visual content tends to outperform text for this hobby, and your audience will love a good slab of foil glare and ninja-Goku poses.
