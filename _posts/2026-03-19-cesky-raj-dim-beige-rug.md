## Practicality and Maintenance

### Cleaning and Care
Daily maintenance is simple: vacuum regularly with a gentle setting, avoid aggressive agitation on the fibers, and address spills by blotting to remove moisture. For liquid spills, work from the outside in to prevent spreading, and use a mild detergent if necessary. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can strip color or degrade fibers. For more stubborn stains, professional cleaning is recommended to preserve the integrity of the weave and the natural dyes.

### Longevity and Warranty
Expect many years of use with proper care. Natural fiber rugs like this typically resist flat spots and maintain a comfortable feel underfoot with time. Some retailers offer warranties on materials and workmanship for added peace of mind, especially for customers who want to keep their rug looking new for longer than a season or two.

### Pet-Friendly Considerations
The rug performs well in homes with pets thanks to its durable weave and moderate pile. It resists snagging and can tolerate light claws if your cat decides to test the fibers with a paw wiggle. Regular vacuuming helps keep pet hair at bay and maintains the rug's texture and color richness.
