## How to play: The basics with All Wild twists
### The core UNO rules remain intact
If you’ve played Uno before, you’ll notice the skeleton of the game remains the same: match color or number to discard, draw if you can’t, and play a Wild to change the color at your whim. The goal is still to empty your hand before everyone else, but the All Wild addition spices up the process with new mechanics.

### All Wild twists explained
The All Wild deck introduces a handful of new types of cards:
- All Wild Change Color: forces every other player to switch color on the table, resulting in synchronized chaos.
- All Wild Draw: similar to a standard draw card, but it often triggers cascading draws that create a mini avalanche across the table.
- Family Rule cards: these let players impose a temporary rule for the round, such as everyone must say a silly phrase when drawing or players must discard to the beat of a chosen joke cadence.
- Wild Stack: a card that allows you to stack up multiple wilds in a single turn if you play it correctly, leading to glorious table eruptions of laughter and groans.

The result is a game that respects UNO mechanics while leaning hard into party vibes. Strategy shifts from pure color-matching to reading the room and timing your All Wild plays for maximum dramatic effect.

### House rules and variations that work well
If your family enjoys a lighter touch, add a rule that All Wild Draws only apply if the player has at least three cards in hand. If you like chaos with a safety net, require a “color lock” for a round where players must try to keep the same color for at least three consecutive turns. If you want to push up the chaos, introduce a team mode where pairs collaborate to manage the effects of All Wild cards. It’s all about the vibe you want: cooperative chaos or competitive circus.

### A note on accessibility
UNO All Wild is friendly to new players. The deck’s bright colors and simple iconography help non-native speakers catch on quickly, and the comedic tone of the rule cards keeps the atmosphere light even when you are engulfed in a cascade of Draw cards. For players with dexterity concerns, the chunky cards mean less fumbling, and there is no memory-dump requirement to succeed if you embrace the social play rather than the perfect hand mentality.
