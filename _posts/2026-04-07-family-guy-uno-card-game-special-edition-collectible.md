## How to Play: A Quick Run-Through for the Time-Challenged

If you already know UNO, you know the core mechanics: match color or number to your discard pile, throw a Draw Four at the opponent when they’re about to cheat at life, yell UNO when you have one card, and try not to miscount your bathroom breaks while the table stares you down. The Family Guy edition adds a few flavors, but the skeleton remains intact:

- The objective remains the same: be the first to get rid of all your cards.
- You can still draw from the draw pile if you can’t play a card that matches color or number.
- Action cards (Skip, Reverse, Draw Two) behave as in classic UNO, with the edition’s art applying to the flavor text and show references.
- Special cards: Quote and Show-cue cards create moments of social interaction, but they do not replace the fundamental rule that you must choose a playable card on your turn.

This is important because you don’t want a deck that feels like it’s fighting against you at every turn. You want a deck that is friendly enough to invite laughter, but sharp enough to create moments of tension when someone tries to bluff about whether they have the right color in hand. In practice, the game plays smoothly, and the humor rarely distracts from the underlying mechanics for too long—though it does create the perfect setup for a “we’re all going to misread the color this round” moment that becomes the table’s memory for the night.
