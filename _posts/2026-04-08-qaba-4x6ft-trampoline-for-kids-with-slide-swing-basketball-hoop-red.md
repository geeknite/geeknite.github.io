## Durability and Maintenance
Durability is a big deal in backyard gear, and the Qaba trampoline does decently well here. The frame is bolted rather than welded, which can be a pro or a con depending on your DIY tolerance. Bolted frames mean you can tighten or adjust the assembly over time, which helps with long-term stability. The protective pads and enclosure netting should be inspected regularly for wear and tear, especially after rough play or windy weather. We recommend wiping down the frame after rain to prevent rust buildup and checking the anchor points to ensure that nothing has loosened. For families that live in damp climates, ensuring good drainage around the trampoline and covering it during rainy seasons helps preserve both the frame and the foam padding.

Regarding the slide, check for any cracks or splits on the plastic parts and keep the surface clean to avoid slick spots that could lead to slides that end in a tumble. The hoop and swing keep their quality with normal use but remain toys rather than professional playground equipment, so temper expectations accordingly.
