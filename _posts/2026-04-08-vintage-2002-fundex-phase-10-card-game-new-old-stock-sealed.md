## How Phase 10 Plays (Overview)
If you’re new to Phase 10, the premise is simple on the surface and delightfully sticky in practice. Your goal is to complete all ten phases in order. Each phase tests a different aspect of hand management: sets, runs, and a handful of flexible combos that require a little bit of math and a lot of bluffing in your own head.

### Objective and Phase Cards
Phase cards act as your targets for each round. A typical phase might require two sets of three (three of a kind) or a run of seven. The exact list varies by deck version, but the core idea remains the same: assemble the required cards, then advance. The draw-discard mechanic is straightforward: draw one, discard one, with a few special cards that shuffle the pace and keep players honest (or at least hoping for a lucky card draw).

### The Twist: Joker, Skip, and Wild
Phase 10 leans on a small ecosystem of action cards: Jokers (wilds for completing phases), Skips (deny opponents a turn), and Wilds (flexible placeholders). These cards shape pacing in meaningful ways. Jokers let you fill gaps when your hand isn’t cooperating, Skips stall a leading opponent, and Wilds provide flexible options that prevent the game from grinding to a halt. It’s a delicate balance of chaos and structure that keeps a party game feeling lively without devolving into endless table talk about probability.
