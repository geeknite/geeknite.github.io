### Installation and fit – the “open the box, play the game” guide
Setting up the Morgan Black protectors is refreshingly straightforward. Here’s a quick guide to help you replicate and (more importantly) optimize our setup:

1) Clean the posts and crossbar area where the protector will sit. A quick wipe with a damp cloth is usually enough to clear pollen, mud, and the occasional old sticker residue from last summer’s tournament.
2) Decide which sides get the most protection based on your goal’s orientation. If the field tends to funnel the action toward the left post, emphasize that side first.
3) Wrap the protector around the post and secure with the included strap or adhesive method. Most models come with adjustable straps that allow for a snug fit around varying post diameters.
4) Check for any loose edges and re-tighten as needed. The aim is a flush fit that doesn’t interfere with ball trajectories or player movement.
5) Repeat for the remaining sides. For crossbars, you may choose to place protectors on the corners first and then along the top bar to minimize wobble during a chaotic “five-goal-in-one-minute” moment.

In practice, the fit is consistent across standard 2–4 inch diameter posts. If your goal has unusually thick posts or a nonstandard crossbar, you may need to do a small bit of trimming or bending to get a clean seal. The design is forgiving enough that you can reposition them for a better look or better safety coverage.

Aesthetics aside, the installation process has a tangible imperative: quick donning and doffing.
