## Assembly Tips and Setup
Even the most robust storage boxes benefit from a kitchen-counter approach to assembly. Here are pragmatic tips to get you through the process without needing a spare set of bolts and a small ceremony:

- Clear a large working space. The worst thing you can do is attempt to assemble on a crowded coffee table while your cat decides it’s a climactic scene in a hidden corner.
- Lay out all dividers in a rough map. You don’t have to install every piece at once; start with the main compartments and add sliders as needed.
- Use the built-in guides. If the box includes labeled sections or color cues, follow them. It saves time and makes you look like a mastermind who knows exactly where every token belongs.
- Don’t over-tighten. If there are screws or snaps, snug them but avoid overt forcing. The goal is a snug, stable fit, not a mechanical symphony that squeaks every time you move it.
- Test with a light load first. Place a few decks and tokens to confirm spacing before committing to full organization. It’s easier to adjust now than after you’ve glued yourself to the table with a misaligned drawer.

If you’re new to this kind of modular storage, give yourself a little grace. The first setup often takes longer than you expect, but subsequent setups become a ritual you actually look forward to because you get to enjoy the fruit of your labor—an empty-ish table with everything in its rightful place.

## Design Notes and Practicality Tips
A lot of storage design is about little quirks that improve daily use. Here are a few you might appreciate if you go all-in:

- Label-friendly surfaces: If you decide to label the compartments, choose water-resistant labels. You’ll thank yourself after a spill or a rogue cup of tea at your table during a weekend-long campaign.
- Color coding: Use color-coded tokens or sleeve backs to quickly identify different card sets. Quick recognition saves you minutes per game night and prevents the Great Card Shuffle of 2026.
- Sleeve compatibility: If you store sleeved cards, verify that the inner dimensions accommodate high sleeves. Some gamers want the added protection of sleeves and prefer a slightly larger compartment for that extra room.
- Expansion growth planning: If you foresee expansions piling up, consider a future expansion tray. It’s always easier to add a new wing than to rebuild your entire organization from scratch after you realize you’ve run out of space.

The real magic lies in the little details that reduce friction. A well-designed storage box doesn’t just hold pieces; it acts as a game-night facilitator. The easier it is to find the right card, the faster you can dive back into the forest’s intrigues without tripping over your own organized chaos.
