## What you get in the set

### The three variants

The Set Of 3 is a simple concept made tangible: three card holders, each sized slightly differently to accommodate a spectrum of decks. The common claim is that you can keep action, inventory, and event cards separated, easily accessible, and less likely to wander off the table during a heroic shouting match. The three holders are designed to stack or stand alone, so you can run a heavy trench manned with the right card in the right slot and still have room for the rest of your minis to breathe. The color palette is subtle rather than garish, which is important when you are lining up on a table that frequently has a chaotic color palette competing for attention. The finish is a clean, slightly frosted acrylic that catches the glow of your lamp just enough to say, hey, I am fancy and functional, but I am not going to glare at you while you game.

### How they look on the table

In person, the Holders look like they belong on a modern desk shelf rather than a battlefield table. They do not scream zany novelty; instead they whisper that you value organization as part of the game experience. If you want a bit of character, you can decorate the surface with your faction color by using small stickers or a rubbery grip sleeve—though be mindful that you do not cover the card window, if you have one. The ergonomic angle on each holder makes it easy to thumb through a deck without tipping the entire set over, which is essential when your kitchen clock is laughing at you for starting a game at 11 pm because life apparently exists to complicate your dice rolls.

### Build quality and materials

The material is basically acrylic, which is a blessing and a curse. On the bright side, acrylic looks slick, resists warping in typical room temperatures, and cleanly wipes down after the occasional coffee spill near the gaming area. On the downside, you get the occasional fingerprint that looks like a tiny zombie print if you are not careful. The edges are smooth, the corners are gentle rather than razor sharp, and there is a satisfying heft that says these are not disposable little trinkets. The fit between the pieces is snug enough to feel sturdy yet forgiving enough to avoid card bending when cards are slid in and out with a bit of enthusiasm. If you have a particularly aggressive board game night with a lot of shuffles and slams, you will still want to handle the holders with care, but they are not likely to snap under normal use.

### Dimensions and fit

Exact measurements vary slightly from run to run, but the trio is designed to host a range of card sizes used in the Black Plague line. Expect the smallest to be ideal for action cards, a mid-range suitable for equipment or trait cards, and a largest that can cradle larger deck sections or even small piles that you might want to manage for a mission briefing. If you are the kind of player who likes to pre-sort before a big campaign, these will likely become your best friends. If you are one of those players who never pre-sort, these will politely remind you to do so with a raised eyebrow and a smug little stand look. In practice, the fit is comfortable, and the range accommodates a lot of card thickness variations that come with sleeves, double sleeves, or extra stiff protective inner sleeves.

### Practical setup during a game

Setting up these holders is basically a two-step dance: you place them on the edge of the table within arm reach, then you slide the current deck into the appropriate holder. The holders do not require any fancy adapters or alignment tricks. They are straightforward enough that a first time user can pick them up and be fine within a few minutes. The real test comes from a live game: does the holder stay put when a hero dives through a zombie horde, or does it slide, tipping max chaos into the already chaotic space? ## Design and build quality in depth

### Materials and finish

The acrylic is clear enough to see through without a significant color cast, which is good for readability and for not obstructing the glare of the play area. The frosted texture on the surface provides a nice tactile feel that is pleasant to touch after a long day of staring into a monitor. The edges are chamfered, which reduces the risk of card edge wear or finger nicks during the long sessions. A good sign of quality is how well the holders resist scratching from repeated card sliding. In my test runs, there were a handful of light micro-scratches after many sessions, but nothing that compromised readability or aesthetics. Some players prefer a fully glossy surface; the frosted look is more practical and less prone to showing every fingerprint, which suits the gaming habit of sometimes sticky fingers after a snack run mid-clarification of a zombie mechanic.

### Durability and everyday use

If you are in the habit of transporting your cards between sessions in a travel case or a backpack, you will appreciate the light but sturdy nature of these holders. They survive being jostled in transit with a reasonable amount of resilience. I would not go as far as to say they are bulletproof, but they hold their form, and that is more than you can say for the cardboard box that previously hosted your decks. The three-piece design is easy to disassemble for cleaning or storage, which is a nice touch for those of us who operate with a minimalist approach to table clutter when the night ends.

### Ergonomics and gameplay flow

The angle of the card slots is optimized for quick thumb retrieval. You do not have to lean over the table like a diplomat negotiating a cease-fire to grab a card. This matters during crowded turns when you need to flip a card, glance at a stat, and make a critical call without breaking immersion or causing a circular chain of murmured curses from your neighbors. The holders are not so tall that they obstruct line of sight for miniatures, and they do not block the view of the board edges in a way that would hamper a throw or a rescue mission ocassionally required in Black Plague. In other words, they are pragmatic design that respects the chaotic primary pursuit of a zombie survival game: stay alive and keep the cards accessible.

## Maintenance, care, and longevity

### Cleaning notes

Maintenance is straightforward: wipe with a microfiber cloth, maybe a gentle spray of water or isopropyl alcohol if you have a sticky gamer snack situation. Avoid aggressive cleaning solvents that could fog or cloud the acrylic. Let it dry completely before you resume play to prevent any water rings near your deck slots. A quick wipe after each session is sufficient to keep the frosted surface looking crisp and preventing fingerprint buildup. If you drop a card sleeve into the slot, do not panic; the design is forgiving and meant to accommodate sleeves, though you may need to give the holder a careful nudge to release the sleeve if you jam the corner.

### Longevity expectations

With normal use, these holders should last for many campaigns. They are not infused with some magical zombie repellent, but their durable construction means you will not feel like you are playing with a fragile relic. If you plan to run a mega-campaign spanning dozens of sessions, you can count on these holders to hold up, provided you treat them with reasonable care and avoid dropping them onto a hard surface from a great height during a dramatic finale reveal.
