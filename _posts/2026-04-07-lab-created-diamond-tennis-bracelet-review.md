## What is a lab created diamond

A lab created diamond is a diamond created in a laboratory rather than formed over millions of years in the crust. The two main growth methods are HPHT and CVD. HPHT simulates the natural process by applying high pressure and heat to a small seed, coaxing carbon into a crystal lattice. CVD starts with a small seed and deposits carbon in thin layers, building up a diamond from the bottom up like a high tech pastry artist layering sugar crystals.

What does this mean for a tennis bracelet? It means you can get stones with the same sparkle, the same brilliance, and the same hardness as natural diamonds, but with a more transparent supply chain and, in many cases, better value for the same carat weight. The color grades and clarity in lab diamonds follow the same scale as natural stones, so a fancy colorless D to high J or beyond is still a thing to chase on a lab stone. The real difference is provenance and price, not the glitter at the end of your wrist.

Hackers in the fashion lab have also noticed that factory precision helps reduce blemishes and rough edges. This matters in a tennis bracelet where stones are tightly strung along a curved chain. A consistently cut diamond translates into a more uniform drape on the wrist, less snagging on sleeves, and less drama when you do the hop skip and jump to answer a text about your latest hobby of collecting tiny rocks.
