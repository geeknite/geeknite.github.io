## Gameplay: The Core Loop With a Griddy Twist
UNO is inherently simple: match colors or numbers to shed cards. The NFL Core Edition keeps that sensibility but layers on thematic modifiers. Here’s the core loop you’ll feel for the first dozen rounds:

- Each player draws one card and takes turns playing a card that matches color or number, just like traditional UNO.
- Whenever a field goal card is played, everyone has to adjust pace: a penalty can be triggered that makes players draw extra cards or skip turns, reflecting the chaos of a high-pressure moment in football.
- Penalties are manageable if you’re paying attention. They reward players who strategically manage their color distribution and leave troublesome cards for later in the round.
- The end-game behavior is still classic UNO: when you’re down to one card, you must shout UNO; missteps lead to cute-but-cutthroat punishments that keep everyone on their toes.

What changes in practice is the tempo. The mode adds bursts of tension, especially when a “season-ending” field goal is called and a round ends in a flurry of draws and skips. It doesn’t completely alter the core flow, but it does nudge players toward a more aggressive pace. The net effect is a game that feels familiar enough for casual players to pick up quickly, yet fresh enough to sustain interest for multiple sessions without feeling like you’re playing the exact same deck shuffle over and over.
