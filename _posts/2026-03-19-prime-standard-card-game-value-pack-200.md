### How to Build Mini-Games on the Fly

If you’re the kind of player who loves to improvise rules, you’ll enjoy a few ready-made micro-games you can start with using the Prime Standard deck as a backbone:

- Quick-sort Showdown: Split into two teams. Each team draws five cards. In 60 seconds, they must arrange their five cards to form a valid sequence according to a simple rule (e.g., ascending numbers, color gradient, or symbol order). The fastest team with a valid sequence wins a point. It’s surprising how often a “clean room for thought” rule can become a chaotic sprint.
- Story-Card Relay: Use a subset of 20 cards with prompts (character, setting, action). Players take turns drawing a card and adding a sentence to an ongoing story, constrained by the prompt. The result is often ridiculous, and that’s exactly the point.
- Pattern Panic: Choose a card type (numbers, symbols, colors) and a target pattern. Players race to assemble a small pattern on their hand; the first to replicate it wins. It’s like a speed puzzle with a competitive edge.

These micro-games illustrate the core strength of a 200-card pack: you can almost instantly build a session around a few simple concepts and adapt as your group’s energy shifts.
