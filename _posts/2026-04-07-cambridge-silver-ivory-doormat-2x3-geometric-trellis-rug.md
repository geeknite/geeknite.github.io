## Maintenance & Cleaning
Maintenance is the boring but essential hero of any home accessory. For this mat, light vacuuming on a low setting can keep loose dirt from settling in. If you’re the kind of person who sweeps with a tiny broom twice a day, you’ll probably love how little upkeep this mat demands. For spills or stains, a mild soap solution and a gentle blot works well. Avoid harsh scrubbing which can fray fibers or degrade the backing.

Machine washing? It depends on the exact fiber blend. If your mat is labeled machine washable, follow the manufacturer’s directions. If not, spot cleaning with a damp cloth is your friend. In any case, ensure the mat dries completely before placing it back in its spot, lest you invite musty odors to throw a party in your entryway.

If you’re serious about longevity, rotate the mat occasionally so that heavy traffic isn’t always hitting the same edges. This is especially useful in households where the front entry sees the lion’s share of daily foot traffic. A simple rotation plan, say every month, can extend the mat’s life and keep the trellis pattern looking fresh instead of sagging in the middle like a cartoon couch.
