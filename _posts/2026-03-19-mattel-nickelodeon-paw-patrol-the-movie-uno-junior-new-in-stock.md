## How to play: Rules, rhythm, and the mission mood
UNO Junior is a simplified version of the classic UNO designed for younger kids. It usually emphasizes color-matching and number recognition, with fewer complex action cards than the adult version. The Paw Patrol edition keeps this skeleton but sprinkles in cartoon-era mission cues and pup-themed icons to keep the theme alive.

Setup:
- Each player is dealt a small hand of cards (the exact number varies by edition, but expect around 4-6 cards for younger players).
- The top card of the draw pile becomes the discard pile’s starter, and play proceeds clockwise.
- The mission cards (if you’re playing with them) sit separate and are drawn as instructed by the card effects.

Turn flow:
- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either the color or the number of the top card on the discard pile. If you can’t play, you draw one card.
- When you have one card left, you must say “Paw Patrol!” to alert others — a lighthearted mechanic to keep you honest and add a bit of silliness to the sprint to go-out.
- The game ends when a player empties their hand, or when the draw pile runs out, depending on house rules.

Special cards (the twist for Paw Patrol):
- Some mission cards trigger tiny mini-goals (such as “save three pups” or “find a color you haven’t used yet”), and completing these can grant a second wind in the form of a free card or a small hand reshuffle. The exact effects vary by print run, so check the included mission sheet to know what you’re dealing with.

The streamlined rules and bigger cards mean fewer slip-ups and more giggles. It’s not a marathon strategy session; it’s a cooperative sprint that ends with everyone yelling at the top of their lungs (playfully) when someone yells “Uno!”
