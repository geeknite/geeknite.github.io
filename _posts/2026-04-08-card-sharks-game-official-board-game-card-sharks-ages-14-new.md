## How to set up the field: quick start guide

- Place the game board in the center. Decide who starts as the host (the show’s tone reimagined as “Lead Shark”).
- Shuffle both card decks and place them in their respective draw piles.
- Each player selects a token color and places it on the starting space of the money ladder. Pro-tip: If your group includes younger players, give the under-12s a fun color to encourage participation and cut down on “who’s winning?” anxiety.
- Each player gets an initial stash of chips. The quick math here is simple: more chips equals more swagger; fewer chips equals more suspense.
- The host reveals the first question card for the Survey round. That’s the cue to start counting nerves and building your bluffing strategy.

Setup is quick enough to jam into a show recap while your players gather snacks. In our tests, a good 5-7 minutes to declare a few house rules (like “no googling during the table talk”) and you’re in business. It’s a warm-up, not a siege drill.

## Core mechanics: what actually happens on the table

Note: The CARD SHARKS Game is built around the two-card-ladder concept. It’s a blend of knowledge, guesswork, and a little strategic risk-taking, with a pacing designed to keep multiple players engaged at once.

### Survey rounds (the nerve-winder)
- The host draws a Question Card and reads the survey prompt out loud. Examples might include items like “Name something you’d never admit to buying on credit” or “Top reasons you pretend to like your in-laws’ cooking.” These prompts are fantastically silly and surprisingly sharp for laughter-per-minute output.
- Each player simultaneously privately selects a short answer (or uses a provided pad to write one if you want to game the board a little more seriously).
- Answers are revealed round-robin, and players score based on how popular their response was relative to the survey’s distribution. The more common your answer, the more money you earn; a rarer answer could be worth more bragging rights with the right bluff.

In practice, this is where the tension shines. People who “know” their friends well will nail the ladder by predicting popular answers, while the bold risk-takers aim for the spicy, less-obvious entries. This is the core thrill—knowing what your buddy might blurt out and watching the crowd react as the money ladder climbs.

### The Big Money Ladder and the Card Flip
- After a successful Survey round, players bid to flip cards on the ladder. The ladder has spaces that increase in value; hitting the right cards advances you closer to the top. The catch is that you’re balancing risk and reward: push too far and you risk a slip that resets progress or tosses you back down the track.
- The Value Cards determine how far you can move the ladder and how much risk you’re willing to take. Some cards give safe, small advances; others throw a player into a potential pratfall with a dramatic turn of fortune. This is where the show’s spirit bleeds into a modern tabletop rhythm: the audience (your fellow players) feel a pulse in the air, waiting to see if you’ll flip the next big card or settle for a safer bet.

### Face-off rounds (head-to-head drama)
- When a scale reaches a peak moment, two players engage in a face-off. This is basically a duel without swords—more cardboard, more suspense, and probably more sarcasm from your fellow players.
- Each contestant places a card face-down and then reveals it simultaneously. The higher card wins the round and some extra chips. Ties are resolved by a reflip, because the game wants to keep the drama crisp rather than devolving into “two minutes of silence and a stare-down.”

### Endgame and victory conditions
- The game doesn’t require a perfectly accurate long-term strategy to win; it rewards smart risk-taking and social sensing. Players accumulate chips as they climb the ladder, and a successful face-off can catapult you into a momentum swing that’s too fun to ignore.
- The moment you reach the top of the ladder or amass a threshold amount of chips, you’re declared the Shark for that round. The group cheers, the host congratulates you with the appropriate over-the-top fanfare, and you reset for a second round if you’re playing in a longer session.

Pacing-wise, the game does a commendable job staying brisk. The interplay between quick Survey rounds and the slower, nerve-wracking ladder ensures you’re never stuck in one phase for too long. If your group loves social deduction, bluffing, and a bit of luck on a neon-lit track, CARD SHARKS Game scratches that itch without turning itself into a mechanical rod of punishment.

## Rules, tips, and strategies: playing the long game without losing your cool

Despite being a party game, there are strategic threads worth threading through your evening. Here are a few to get you started:

- Listen to your table-mates. The best bluff is the one that doesn’t feel like a bluff; it feels like consensus. If you can sense the “crowd” and your opponents’ comfort zones, you’ll be able to ride the ladder more smoothly.
- Balance risk with pace. The ladder rewards momentum, but a misstep can cost you more than a single round. Decide early how aggressive you want to be about pushing higher-value spaces.
- Use face-offs strategically. A well-timed face-off can flip a game in a single round. Don’t waste opportunities on low-value duels if you’re chasing a bigger payday. However, if you’re behind, a bold face-off can alert your friends to your killer instincts and may discourage the crowd from sleeping on you.
- Manage your chips like a true card shark. Chips are not just victory points; they’re tokens of morale. If you’re down, you might need to gamble a bit to swing the mood—and possibly the ladder. If you’re ahead, protect your lead with measured bets.
- Read conventions and adapt. The game provides room for social play; if your group enjoys jokes, incorporate them. If you want a more serious vibe, keep the banter to a minimum and let the tension do the talking.
