## Display, Storage, and Care: Keeping Gloom Fresh (ish)

Let’s be realistic: keeping a vintage card pristine is a little like trying to keep a ficus perfectly un-kinked in a closet full of your cat’s favorite boxes. It’s not impossible, but it does require discipline. Here are practical, geeky tips to ensure your Gloom No. 044 stays in excellent shape for decades:

- Use proper sleeves and top-loaders. A semi-rigid 1-card top loader plus a soft sleeve helps protect against bending and surface wear while still allowing you to handle the card. If you’re a purist, consider double-sleeving with a protective interleaf inside the top-loader.
- Store in a cool, dry environment. Avoid direct sunlight, humidity fluctuations, and heat, all of which can degrade the printed colors and paper stock over time. A climate-controlled display case is ideal if you’re serious about a multi-language Gloom display.
- Keep track of the relative humidity. A constant RH in the 40–60% range is comfortable for card stock, albeit you should still avoid rolling the card into your sleeves after a long day at the desk unless you enjoy a risk of edge wear.
- Handling matters. Oils from your fingers can affect the surface gloss and cause slight discoloration over long periods. Wash your hands or use clean microfiber gloves if you’re doing a hands-on inspection.
- Display strategy. A single card deserves its moment in the sun, but don’t rush to place every eye glare into one ultra-bright corner of your room. A single Gloom No. 044 card can be a conversation piece on a shelf with a few related printings: consider grouping by country (Japan vs. English) or by the card art era to tell a story with your binder.
