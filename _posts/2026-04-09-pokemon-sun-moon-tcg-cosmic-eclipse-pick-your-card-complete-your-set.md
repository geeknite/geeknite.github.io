### Understanding NM LP and Complete Your Set
 NM LP stands for Near Mint or Lightly Played. In collectors speak, that means a card that shows minimal signs of wear—perhaps a slight edge scuff or a subtle corner chip on the back, but nothing that would cause practical degradation on display or in a sleeve. The Complete Your Set goal is to gather every card designated as part of the Cosmic Eclipse run that you care about, ideally in NM LP condition. The reality is more nuanced: some cards are notoriously prone to edge wear, hollow foil misprints, or centering quirks that complicate a clean NM LP grade. The trick is to define your own standard of NM LP and then adjust your expectations accordingly. If you are a serious collector, you may pursue a strict NM LP for the chase cards while accepting lighter wear on non chase rarities that still fit your binder aesthetic.

### Condition checks and what to look for in NM LP
1) Surface and holo integrity: Look for scratches, swirl marks, or rainbow lines that indicate surface wear. A clean holo should be even and vibrant, with minimal clouding.
2) Edges and corners: NM LP cards should have sharp corners and clean edges. Discoloration or whitening near the corners can indicate rough handling.
3) Centering and borders: Cosmic Eclipse run has various print runs; expect some centering variance. The key is to avoid obvious dominant tilt or off-center misalignment.
4) Card back and uniformity: The back should display consistent pattern alignment with no major scratches or white spots.
5) Potential misprints or damage: Some NM LP cards can have subtle misprints that still pass their grade, but if you want pristine, be vigilant.

## Practical Steps to Build Your Complete Your Set NM LP
A realistic, repeatable plan is worth its weight in holo foil. Here is a practical blueprint you can adapt to your own budget and appetite for risk.

1) Define your target: Start with the core set list you intend to complete. Create a Google sheet or a notebook that lists each card you still need, its rarity tier, and your target price in NM LP condition. This becomes your mission control.
2) Prioritize chase cards: Identify the most desired and most expensive cards, and budget for them first. If you can secure a couple of high value NM LP cards early, you’ll feel momentum and have something to show for your efforts.
3) Diversify your sources: Don’t rely on one seller. Check multiple marketplaces, local stores, and community groups. Diversification reduces risk and increases your chances of finding cleaner NM LP copies at fair prices.
4) Use watchlists and price alerts: Set up alerts for the exact card names or print runs you want. This helps you pounce when a good deal pops up, rather than scrolling at random.
5) Inspect photos in depth: Ask for high resolution photos of the front, back, and edges. If photos are murky, request more. The more you know, the less you risk.
6) Be mindful of misprints and variants: Cosmic Eclipse includes some interesting variants and misprints that can be valuable. Make sure you are chasing the exact card you want rather than a look alike variant that is less desirable to you.
7) Consider grading and store value: If you plan to have your set long term, a near mint copy with a grade can help protect value and make your binder display pop. ## How to Spot Scams and Avoid Common Pitfalls
The more valuable a card becomes, the more creative scammers get. Here are practical steps to keep yourself safe:
- Request multiple photos and verify the exact card boundaries. If a seller only provides a single photo, be cautious.
- Compare the card back pattern with known authentic prints. Counterfeiters sometimes miss subtle marks in the back pattern.
- Check the seller’s reputation and return policy. A solid buyer protective policy is a good sign you are dealing with someone who cares about customers.
- Avoid suspiciously low prices that seem too good to be true. Very low prices can indicate misrepresented conditions or counterfeit items.
