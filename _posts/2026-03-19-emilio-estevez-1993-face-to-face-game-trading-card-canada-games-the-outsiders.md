### Authenticity Checks: How to Separate Real Deals From Mirage Dust
If you’re hunting for a genuine Emilio Estevez 1993 Face To Face card, here are a few pointers that collectors swear by (and we’re not going to pretend we invented them):

- Documentation: The best items have a provenance trail—original promotional packaging, a receipt, or some form of marketing collateral that confirms it existed as part of a limited release.
- Print quality: The 1990s saw a lot of variability in print runs. If the card looks too perfect for its age, that’s a red flag you want to test against; for promo sets, a little edge wear is often the sign of real-life circulation.
- Card stock and finish: Many promo cards used a slightly different stock from mainstream trading cards to emphasize their promotional status. That slight variance can be a clue to authenticity.
- Cross-reference: Compare with other known cards in the same promo run. If the back design or the font choices align with the era’s standard, you’re more likely looking at an authentic piece.

If you’re serious about authenticity, consider talking to veteran collectors or professional grading services. They’ll help you assess centering, edge wear, surface gloss, and print flaws that tell the tale of a card’s journey from a promotional bin to your binder.
