## Assembly: A Two-Person Adventure
Ah, assembly—the ritual by which a piece of furniture either becomes your best friend or your sworn enemy. The two-person assembly requirement here is not just marketing mimicry. The desk is large; it’s meant to be assembled with a second person to hold panels in place while you bolt frames. The good news is that the hardware is clearly labeled, the instruction manual is readable, and the pieces fit together with satisfying precision. The bad news is that you’ll probably drop a few screws on the carpet and chase them into the abyss (or in your significant other’s slippers).

A typical assembly session might go like this:

1) Unbox, check all parts, and identify the central screen and side panels. 2) Attach leg frames to the desktop. 3) Mount the privacy screen in the center. 4) Route cables and check alignments. 5) Tighten everything and perform a wobble test. 6) Sip your celebratory beverage and realize you forgot to connect a power strip.

If you want to reduce the drama, allocate a couple of hours, set aside extra screws (they always disappear into the void), and don’t climb the walls in frustration. The result, however, is worth it: a solid two-person work surface that’s easy to clean, maintain, and rearrange as needed.
