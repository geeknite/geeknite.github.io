## Overview
Trio is a contender for the 2024 nominee spotlight in the party game category. It is designed for 3 to 6 players and promises quick rounds, bold ideas, and a healthy dose of chaos. The vibe is cheerful, a little chaotic, but always endearing. It is not a heavy strategy game; it is a social icebreaker with a light deck that rewards creativity and timely bragging. The aim is simple yet surprisingly tricky: assemble a trio of cards that line up with a prompt, then try to outmaneuver other players as they guess or block your moves. The result is a fast, frenetic session that can be repeated many times in a single game night.

## How to Play

### Turn structure
Each round flows quickly. On your turn you draw a card if you need one, then you either play up to three cards to form a trio or challenge someone else to show their trio. The challenge might be about guessing which trio a player intended, or it can be a bluff battle where players vote on which trio they believe is legitimate. The round ends when a player reveals a valid trio that matches the current prompt. If you succeed, you earn points; if you fail, you snatch a point from your own ego and lose a turn while licking your wounds.

### Card types
- Prompt cards that set the topic for the round
- Response cards that you combine to construct a trio
- Special action cards that mix things up, like forced swap or double-bluff

### Scoring and endgame
The scoring system is pleasantly simple: you gain points for successful trios and lose points for failed attempts or betrayed bluffs. The game ends when a player reaches a certain threshold or after a predetermined number of rounds. In practice, that usually means someone hits the finish line while the rest of the table decides to perform an improvised victory dance that will live in group memory for years.
