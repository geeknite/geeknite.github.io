## Overview

Liliana's Scorn is very much a creature of its color and era: a black instant that aims to control the battlefield by pruning threats and pushing you toward a favorable late game. It’s not the flashiest removal spell of the set, and it’s not the most aggressive tempo play you’ll ever draft, but it sits in that pleasant Goldilocks zone where you aren’t paying too much mana for a one-shot effect, and you’re often getting more value out of it as the game unfolds.

In terms of flavor, the card leans into Liliana’s lore as the ultimate planner who punishes hubris and hedges bets with a patient, calculating scorn. The art direction and texture of the card reinforce that vibe: the world is quieter when Liliana steps in, and the board becomes just a little more unforgiving. It’s not just about destroying a creature; it’s about sending a message to your opponent that the chessboard is under new management.

As a product of Core Set 2021, Liliana's Scorn also participates in the broader design philosophy of the set: more consistent card quality across color pairs, solid evergreen removal options, and a little more room for players to pilot strategic engines without getting blown out by random noise. Whether you’re a sealed enthusiast who enjoys the puzzle of a draft with limited picks or a constructed player who likes to dance around the curve with a black-control or midrange shell, this card has a lane for you.

## Limited Play: How It Shines in Draft and Sealed

Limited formats reward a different kind of thinking than Constructed. In draft or sealed, Liliana's Scorn often earns its keep for a couple of reasons:

- It provides a controllable answer in the midgame when both players are populating their boards with threats.
- It helps stabilize against aggressive starts by removing a critical early threat and keeping you in the game longer.
- It synergizes with a wider black plan that includes creatures, removal, and hand disruption, giving you a clear path to a superior late game as the format widens.

In a typical M21 draft pool, you’ll find that the card pairs well with other black removals and with threats that you can protect or recoup value from through attrition. If you’re in a sealed pod and see multiple black removal options, Liliana's Scorn can be the safety valve that keeps you competitive in the late picks.

Practical drafting tip: if you’re a little short on early threats and your color balance is shaky, it’s often worth taking removal options a tad earlier, especially if you see allied synergy spells that reward tempo. In this sense, Liliana's Scorn is a cement piece of a slow-black strategy rather than a splashy game-winner.

In practice, the card shines when your deck is built to leverage midrange stability. The more your plan revolves around controlling the board and building inevitability, the more Liliana's Scorn earns its keep. If you’re exploring a more aggressive tempo build, it still has a place as a stabilizing piece, but you’ll be balancing it with faster removal and more proactive threats.

## Interactions with Liliana Lore and Other Black Staples

Liliana’s character in Magic lore is all about control, inevitability, and fear as a strategic tool. This card’s naming is not accidental: it’s designed to feel like a piece of Liliana’s toolkit—cold, precise, and focused on dampening the opposition’s threats while preserving her own resources. In practical terms, that means the card tends to pair well with other black staples that want to leverage the same axis: removal, discard, card advantage, and reanimation or recursion in some shells.

- Planeswalker synergy: If your build includes Liliana planeswalkers or other black planeswalkers, Scorn can contribute to a plan where you draw into more planeswalkers and disrupt opponents trying to accelerate their wins.
- Hand disruption: If your deck includes discard or hand-pressure elements, Scorn becomes part of a multi-layered plan that punishes opponents for holding onto key threats.
- Recursion and value engines: In some shells, being able to remove something big and then replay it or fetch it back from the graveyard creates a long-term advantage. Liliana's Scorn can play into that dynamic by preserving your own engine while answering theirs.

As you experiment with different deck lists, you’ll notice that the card’s true strength emerges when it’s sandwiched between other flexible plays—removal, hand disruption, and card-draw or filter effects that help you stay ahead on cards even after you spend one spell on a single target.

Liliana's Scorn is the kind of card that makes a deck feel purposeful without requiring you to perform a hero-level topdeck. It’s a thoughtful, flavorful, practical addition to many black-centric strategies from Core Set 2021. It rewards you for reading the board, sequencing your plays, and embracing the tempo-shift that removal spells can create. It’s not the marquee bomb of the set, and it won’t single-handedly win you a match if you’re caught on the back foot, but in the right deck and the right moment, it resonates with a quiet, efficient elegance.
