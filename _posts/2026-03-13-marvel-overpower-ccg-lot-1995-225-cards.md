## Card quality and variations

Card stock in mid‑90s CCGs is famously sturdy, yet edges can be a bit brittle after decades of handling. Here’s what to watch for when you inspect a 225 card lot:

- Edges and corners: Look for dings, whitening, or creases. A lot with mild edge wear can still be a star in a display binder. Severe edge wear may indicate a lower overall grade, unless you’re building a “character story” binders and you love the patina.
- Surface wear: Slab-like gloss coatings were common back then; sometimes the surface shows minor scratches or scuffing. In many cases, surface imperfections don’t affect playability and can be enjoyed as part of the card’s aged aura.
- Print runs and misprints: You may encounter occasional misprints and color shifts. These little anomalies can be charming and sometimes more collectible depending on rarity and demand.
- Color and image clarity: Expect the morphological look of 1990s art—bold colors, dramatic shadows, and a vibe that screams comic book pages brought to life on cardboard. If an image looks too washed out, it’s probably due to lighting wear rather than a printing defect.

If you are considering grading, remember that graders often analyze centering, edge wear, and surface flaws. A lot like this one rarely comes in pristine mint; what you gain is story, personality, and the glow of a toy chest from the dot-com era turning into a classic binder staple.

## How to authenticate and evaluate this lot when you buy

- Request clear photos of the set’s entirety, with focus on edge wear and any visible misprints. A YouTube unboxing video can also help you gauge overall condition, but ensure the photos match the video.
- Ask for a rough breakdown: how many character cards vs how many special cards, plus an estimate of duplicates. This helps you gauge value for trade and binder completion.
- Inquire about any known missing cards. A rare missing card can affect value but never kills the charm of the lot if the rest is strong and you’re in for the memory ride.
- Confirm storage conditions: were the cards stored in a manner that avoids moisture, sunlight, and sticky fingers? A lot’s condition can rapidly deteriorate if not protected.

## How to store, protect, and enjoy the cards

- Sleeves and binders: Use clear polypropylene sleeves and 9‑pocket binders to protect cards while preserving readability. This is especially important for color‑rich 1990s art that can fade with time.
- Temperature and humidity: Store in a cool, dry place. Avoid attics with heat fluctuations or basements with dampness.
- Handling: Oils from fingers can degrade card surfaces over time. Wash hands or use sleeves as a barrier when flipping through cards.
- Display: A glass case or shelf display with proper backboard can let you enjoy the artwork without risking the cards to wear. The 1990s art deserves to be seen, not hidden in a desk drawer like a goblin’s receipt.
