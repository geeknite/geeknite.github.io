## How to Clean and Care: A Practical Guide for Humans
### Step-by-step washing
1) Shake or vacuum the rug to remove loose dirt.
2) Check care labels for machine wash settings; use a gentle cycle with cold water.
3) Use a mild detergent free of harsh whiteners or bleach.
4) Avoid overloading the machine to protect the fibers and backing.
5) Remove promptly after washing and lay flat to dry or use a low-heat tumble cycle if the care label permits.

### Spot cleaning tips
For small stains, blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth and a mild solution of water and gentle detergent. Work from the outside of the stain toward the center to prevent spreading. For more stubborn spots, a fabric-safe stain remover can be applied with care, followed by a gentle rinse and air-drying. Always test in an inconspicuous area first to make sure there’s no color transfer or fabric damage.

### Drying and storage advice
Air drying is typically the safest route if you have space. If you’re short on space, a low-heat dryer setting can be used, but avoid high heat, which can shrink or warp the backing. Once dry, give the rug a fluff with a vacuum to restore its texture. If you’ll store it for a season, roll rather than fold to minimize creases, and keep in a cool, dry place to avoid mold or mildew.
