## Dosing for a 40 kg (88 lb) dog

This is where the math nerd in all of us pops out like a character sheet: dosing is weight-based. The standard dosing for many allwormer products is roughly 1 tablet per 10 kg of body weight, given as a single dose. For a 40 kg dog, that’s typically 4 chewable tablets per treatment. The 50-tablet package is designed to cover a month or more of treatment for households with large dogs, depending on how often you’re deworming.

Notes and caveats:

- Always verify the exact tablet strength on your bottle and follow the label instructions. Popantel packaging can vary by country and formulation.
- Some dogs may need a second dose after a couple weeks if treating for tapeworms or whipworms, depending on your vet’s guidance and the parasites present. Don’t rely on a single dose for multi-day parasite management unless your clinician recommends it.
- If your dog has a history of sensitivity to dewormers or other medications, speak with your veterinarian before administration.

## When to deworm: timing and frequency

Deworming frequency isn’t one-size-fits-all. Typical schedules include:

- Annual or semi-annual wellness checks with deworming as recommended by your vet.
- Quarterly deworming for dogs in high-risk environments (e.g., hunting dogs, outdoor access, living in areas with heavy parasite load).
- After exposure to potential sources of infection (like raw meat diets, poor sanitation, or contact with wild animals).

For puppies, the frequency is often higher due to their developing immune systems and higher worm exposure risk. For older dogs, a vet-guided plan is best because age and health conditions alter drug safety and efficacy.

## Safety, side effects, and precautions

Popantel Allwormer is generally well-tolerated. Adverse effects are rare but possible. Common issues reported anecdotally include mild GI upset, temporary vomiting, or a brief decrease in appetite after dosing. Severe reactions are uncommon but can occur with sensitivity or if the medicine is not used as directed.

Important safety notes:

- Do not exceed the recommended dose. Tablet strengths and weight-based dosing should be followed precisely.
- If you notice persistent vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, or signs of an allergic reaction after dosing, contact a veterinarian immediately.
- Pregnant or lactating dogs should have timing and dosing guided by a vet to protect both the dam and the offspring.
- Keep tablets out of reach of children and store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.

### Side effects: what to watch for
Some guardians report mild drooling during or after dosing, which may be a sign of taste aversion rather than a sign of a problem. If your dog shows signs of severe GI upset that lasts more than 24-48 hours, or if there is blood in stool or vomiting that won’t stop, seek veterinary care promptly.
