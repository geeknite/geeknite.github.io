### Painting Tips and Practical Advice
Whether you’re a veteran painter or a hobbyist who has only recently discovered the joy of slathering on washes, the Season 1 minis are a friendly canvass. Here are a few practical tips to help you level up your paint job without sacrificing your sacred weekend hobby time:

- Start with a solid undercoat. A neutral gray or brown helps your colors pop and reduces the chance of your skin tones looking muddy on certain lighting setups.
- Pick a color rhythm. Survivors tend to benefit from brighter tones for urban outfits, while zombies thrive on muted, decomposed palettes. The contrast is both practical and aesthetically satisfying.
- Use washes strategically. A sepia or dark brown wash can bring out subtle textures on cloth and skin without making everything look dirty. If you’re feeling bold, a deep violet gloss in limited areas can add an eerie life-to-death glow under proper lighting.
- Dry brush to reveal texture. A light dry brush on the raised areas of clothing and armor helps pick out details that the base coat might miss, especially on walkers.
- Basing matters. A well-executed base ties your mini into the board’s world. Consider city rubble, busted brickwork, or a bit of moss on a cemetery wall. It sounds small, but it changes your entire gaming experience when you glance at the group and think, Yes, this is their neighborhood—this is their fear.

## Maintenance, Packaging, and Practicalities
Let’s talk shop for a moment. The Season 1 Miniatures Pack arrives in a standard retail blister or small box with a clear narrative title on the lid. Inside you’ll find the miniatures neatly separated, with polybags keeping the sprues and figures in place. The packaging is efficient for storage, which means you won’t need to perform a scavenger hunt for pieces in a tangle of plastic and plastic glue. From a maintenance standpoint, plastic miniatures are forgiving. They tolerate a wide range of primer and paint choices, and they respond well to typical hobby techniques. The only caution I’d share is to avoid excessive heat on storage, as plastic can become a bit wobbly if exposed to high heat in a closed trunk on a sunny day. If you’re the type to leave your minis on display in a bright room, there’s no harm in that either—just keep the humidity in check so the paint doesn’t bubble or crack.
