## How Skip-Bo Works, The Classic Rules in plain English

Skip-Bo’s core mechanic is simple enough to explain in a coffee break, but deep enough to reward careful planning. The goal is to be the first player to clear all the cards from your stock pile by playing them into ascending runs on building piles. The catch: you can use any number of stock cards in your hand if you can play them, but you must reveal the top card on your stock pile to begin. That top card becomes the figurehead of your turn and the rest of your actions twirl around it like a responsible chorus.

### The Core Mechanic

- You start with a stock pile in front of you. Your objective is to discard all cards from this pile.
- You deal cards to form a hand and use the play piles to build up in sequential order from 1 to 12, with Skip-Bo cards acting as wild cards that can assume any number.
- On your turn you draw to fill your hand, then place cards from your hand on any of your building piles in ascending order. If you can’t place any card, you discard one to your own discard pile and pass the turn.
- The first player to empty their stock pile wins.

Okay, there are some nuances (like when you can only place cards on your own or an opponent’s building piles, and the way you can wind up with multiple good options that fight each other like siblings in the backseat of a family road trip). But the beauty is its elegance: a few cards, a handful of rules, a race to zero. The 1986 edition keeps this core intact while letting the nostalgia do some of the heavy lifting for you.
