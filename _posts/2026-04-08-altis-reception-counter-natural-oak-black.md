## Assembly Experience: Step by Step Without Losing Sanity

### Prepping the Space

Before you even think about screwing in a single bolt, clear the space around the workstation. You want to avoid the moment you discover what your coworker called a “temporary storage area” has become a full-blown storage facility for generic office supplies that somehow replicate the clutter of a NASA launchpad. A clean workspace makes installation smoother and reduces the chance of losing a small hex key in the carpet fibers where it will haunt you for weeks.

### The Actual Assembly

- Lay out all components. Take a moment to marvel at how few instructions there actually are and how many diagrammatic icons there are that you can pretend to understand without reading the page. This is part of the charm of modern furniture manuals: the ability to feel both capable and mildly confused.
- Attach the main counter top to the frame with hardware that fits exactly as expected. If you use a power screwdriver, proceed with moderate speed to avoid over-tightening. The goal is a snug fit, not a modern art sculpture with bolts that look like a tech bro’s attempt at abstract geometry.
- Install the cable management tray. It clips neatly under the countertop and gives you a place to encourage your cables to behave. The result is a tidier look that even your IT department can respect without calling your space a tripping hazard.
- Level the feet. The adjustable feet are straightforward and make it easy to compensate for uneven floors. You might find yourself spending five extra minutes playing with the level, which is tantamount to minor meditation if you enjoy the tactile joy of perfect alignment.
