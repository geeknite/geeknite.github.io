## Zombicide Black Plague in a Nutshell
Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative survival game where players take on the roles of survivors trying to forge a path through a medieval zombie nightmare. The box is loud in all the right ways: color coded with panels that scream look at me, feel me, and roll some dice. The core loop is simple enough for newcomers, complex enough for veteran table hoppers, and spicy enough for people who enjoy arguing about which weapon is best at least twice per session.

In the Black Plague edition you will encounter a setting that fuses gothic architecture with pulp fantasy, giving you cathedrals, bonfires, and a map that is as likely to collapse as your plans for loot. The game uses an action point system, which means movement, combat, and looting all collide like an overexcited dragon at a swap meet. The dice work as the heartbeat of the session. Sometimes they cheer you on, sometimes they spit on your strategy, and sometimes they roll the perfect gadget you did not know you needed.

## House Rules and Variants
Every good table mutates a bit when stories of victory and defeat bounce around the room. Here are a few house rules fans have proposed to keep the pace from stalling:

- Quick Loot Shuffle: discard loot piles after every two rooms to keep the table moving and to avoid the loot pile becoming a permanent monument to procrastination.
- Heroic Ally Rule: allow one ally to perform a double move per turn when a specific objective is active, to speed up clutch escapes without breaking the tension.
- Night Mode Map: print a set of glow in the dark markers for certain rooms to simulate torchlight and create a cinematic experience during late night sessions.

If you want to experiment with these, the episode provides a blueprint for how to test ideas without destabilizing core mechanics. You can adapt variants based on player count, experience level, and how tilted you want the dice to be on any given evening.
