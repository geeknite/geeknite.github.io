## The Basics of Off with his Head!

### What is Off with his Head!?

"Off with his Head!" is a competitive board game designed for 2-6 players. The game revolves around players trying to outwit each other in a medieval court setting, where the ultimate goal is to avoid the wrath of the Queen and keep your head attached to your neck. Think of it as "Chess" meets "Monty Python", with a sprinkle of absurdity.

### Components

The game comes with a beautifully designed board featuring various locations in the medieval court, such as the Throne Room, Dungeon, and Royal Gardens. The components include character miniatures, a deck of cards with actions, events, and objectives, and a handful of dice for resolving conflicts. The artwork is top-notch, and you'll find yourself marveling at the intricate details.

**Fun Fact**: The game includes a special "Executioner" miniature that will make you scream "NOT THE HEAD!" every time it appears.

## Gameplay

"Off with his Head!" is all about strategy, humor, and a bit of luck. Players must navigate the court, complete objectives, and avoid the Queen's ire. Each player takes on the role of a courtier with unique abilities, ranging from the "Jester" with hilarious distractions to the "Knight" with valiant defenses.

### Turn Phases

Each turn consists of several phases:

1. **Action Phase**: Players perform actions like moving, scheming, and using special abilities.
2. **Event Phase**: Draw event cards that introduce new challenges or opportunities. (Because why not?)
3. **Conflict Phase**: Resolve conflicts with other players using dice rolls and card abilities. Prepare for chaos!

### Winning and Losing

To win, players must complete all objectives before the Queen decides to "off" everyone's heads. If a player's head is "offed", they're out of the game. The last player standing (or with their head intact) wins. Sorry, folks, it's a head-chopping world out there!

**Pro Tip**: Always keep the "Royal Pardon" card handy. It may not save you, but it will give you a fighting chance.

## The Humor Factor

**Funny Moment**: During one game, our "Jester" hero tried to distract the Queen using the "Chicken Dance" card. Needless to say, it didn't go well. The laughter echoed for hours.
