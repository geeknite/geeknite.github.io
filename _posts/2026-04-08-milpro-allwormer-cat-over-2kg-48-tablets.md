Milpro Allwormer is a broad spectrum dewormer for cats that targets a range of intestinal parasites. The label specifies usage for cats over 2 kilograms (roughly over 4 pounds). ### How Milpro Allwormer works

Milbemycin oxime works by paralyzing the worms in the cat's gut, which helps the body shed the parasites more easily. Praziquantel acts as a broad spectrum agent that kills tapeworms by causing their tegument to break down, which makes them unfit to live inside your cat. Combined, these agents handle the two major classes of worms you would want to cover with a single chewable pill every so often. In practice, most cats tolerate this treatment well, though as with any medicine, your mileage may vary depending on your cat’s temperament and prior exposures to pills.

## Safety and potential side effects

Like any medicine, Milpro Allwormer can cause side effects in some cats. The most common complaints are mild and short-lived, including vomiting, drooling, decreased appetite, or mild lethargy. If you notice persistent vomiting, unusual behavior, severe allergic reactions such as facial swelling or hives, or if your cat has a history of drug sensitivities, contact your veterinarian immediately. Do not give this medication to cats under the weight threshold or to kittens younger than the recommended age. Pregnant or lactating cats should only use this product under direct veterinary supervision. If your cat is taking other medications, including other antiparasitics or products with similar active ingredients, discuss potential interactions with your vet to avoid any adverse effects.

### Tips for pill-patients

- Use a calm, quiet environment. A chaotic kitchen makes a dramatic show and may invite dramatic meows from your furry choreographer.
- Try a pill pocket or a small piece of soft cheese to disguise the pill. Avoid forcing the pill down a throat if you suspect it causes choking; instead, try placing the pill at the back of the tongue and offering a small drink of water afterward, if your cat allows it.
- Break the tablet in half if your cat is small and only takes partial doses, but only do so if the label indicates variability in dosing by weight. If you are unsure, ask your vet.
- Reward your cat with a favorite treat after administering the medication, reinforcing a positive association with pill time.
