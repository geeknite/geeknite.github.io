## Cleaning and maintenance

### Routine care tips

- Vacuum regularly to lift debris that collects in the fibers. Since the rug has a compact pile, avoid aggressive agitation that might loosen fibers. A light pass with a standard vacuum head is enough for weekly upkeep.
- Shake out or rinse off light dust after heavy use. Outdoor rugs benefit from a good shake down to dislodge intangible party leftovers and pollen that decided to set up shop.
- For bigger messes, use a mild detergent solution and a soft brush, then rinse with a garden hose. Avoid high-pressure washers that might loosen the fiber structure over time.
- Let the rug dry completely before placing furniture back on it to avoid damp pockets that invite mildew. If you can, position it in a sunny spot or a breezy corner to speed up drying.

### Dealing with mold and mildew

Like any outdoor textile, this rug can attract mildew in persistently damp environments. If you notice a musty odor or visible mold, treat with a diluted vinegar solution or a specialized outdoor rug cleaner, and ensure thorough drying. Prevention is easier: ensure proper drainage, lift the rug off the ground in wet weather if possible, and rotate placement occasionally to avoid stubborn moisture zones.

## Care tips and optimization hacks

- Rotation schedule: Rotate the rug every month during blanket sun exposure to distribute color and wear evenly.

- Seasonal storage: If you live in a climate with heavy winters, you can roll the rug and store it in a cool, dry place to extend its life. Just make sure it’s completely dry before storage to prevent mold.
- Quick-clean tricks: For quick refreshes, a quick spray with a gentle outdoor rug cleaner and a rinse can remove surface grime without a full wash. This keeps maintenance manageable even for the busiest of households.
