## Authenticity and Verification: How to Avoid Fakes
In modern markets, counterfeits aren’t rare in the same way as the shiny card you’re looking at in a listing. They are rare in the sense that some fakes look “almost real” until you inspect the card under magnification: edging, printer dot patterns, and subtle misalignments can reveal inauthentic prints. Here are quick checks you can do or request when buying:
- Compare the holo pattern to known authentic cards from SV4a. Inconsistent holo distribution is a red flag.
- Check the card back for consistent color, font, and alignment with related SV4a cards.
- Request high-resolution photos from multiple angles, especially the corners and the surface under bright light.
- Look for the serial codes or set markings that appear on original prints and verify they match SV4a batch specifics.

When in doubt, ask for a video walk-through. A seller who can narrate the light angle and demonstrate how the card reflects will likely be legitimate and genuinely proud of their stock—not trying to “get rid of it fast.”

## Quick Care and Preservation Tips
- Store in sleeves and top-loaders, preferably with a soft inner sleeve to reduce friction.
- Keep away from direct sunlight and excessive humidity to preserve the holo and the surface.
- If you display, use a UV-filtered case and rotate displays to prevent cumulative light exposure.
- Regularly dust your display area and handle cards with clean hands or gloves to avoid oils that can sabatage the gloss.
- If you ever decide to resell, document the exact condition, include macro photos, and note any slight imperfections for transparency—trust builds value.
