## Performance deep dive

The promise of the Pro is more headroom for the future. We break down the potential performance improvements in practical terms, not just marketing talk:

- Load times and texture streaming benefit from faster storage: a big deal in large open-world games where the player looks at a horizon full of textures and wonders if they loaded yet.
- Frame rates: Expect smoother gameplay in titles optimized for 4K 120 Hz. Not all games will hit that mark, but a good handful will feel noticeably snappier, especially when paired with a VRR-enabled display.
- Ray tracing and lighting: With more compute power, the Pro handles more complex lighting scenarios, resulting in more realistic shadows and reflections that can be visible in the right environments.
- Thermal stability: The Pro is designed to stay cooler under load, which helps sustain performance during long sessions and prevents thermal throttling that can derail a high-end gaming experience.

We want to be clear: real-world numbers depend on the specific title, its engine optimizations, and how you configure the system. A well-optimized game can look and feel stunning on the Pro, while another title may see less dramatic gains if it is already exploiting the base system efficiently.
