## How to play (without spoiling all the fun)
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, the NSFW edition will feel like a familiar drumroll with extra cymbals. For newcomers, here’s the gist in Geeknite-friendly terms:

### Setup
- Shuffle the NSFW deck thoroughly. Remove any spoilers about counts or exact card names—this is a surprise party for your attention span.
- Deal each player a handful of Defuse cards (the de-fuser of doom), plus a couple of other safe basics so nobody feels left out.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards back into the deck, shuffle, and set the remaining deck in the center. The mood should be equal parts suspense and giggles.

### Turn structure
On your turn you have a few core options:
- Play cards to affect other players (steal cards, peek at them, push your luck, etc.).
- Draw a card from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and don’t have a Defuse, you’re OUT—unless you’re dramatic enough to still survive via a chaotic twist.
- Use Defuse or other protective spells from your hand to avoid a grim death-by-cat moment.

The NSFW flavor comes from the risk cards and the banter that accompanies each draw. The game thrives on storytelling; the more you riff about your “cat-astronaut” or “unicorn librarian” deck, the more the laughter rises and the tension tightens.

### Key mechanics
- Exploding Kitten cards: If drawn, you’re in danger of elimination unless you play a Defuse card.
- Defuse cards: Save yourself from exploding kingly trouble; they’re precious and often debated like a hot pizza topping.
- Attack, Shuffle, See the Future, and other action cards: Change the pace of the round, force someone else to draw, reposition the order, or reveal future outcomes to guide risk management.
- The NSFW cards tilt the tone: They’re not just jokes; they can alter who’s likely to survive a few rounds longer, which is often the real players’ goal anyway.

Important game dynamic: speed versus strategy. If you’re the kind of person who likes to rush through a game, you’ll enjoy the quick rounds. If you’re a meticulous planner, you’ll savor the micro-decisions around when to deploy a Defuse or when to risk a scary draw.
