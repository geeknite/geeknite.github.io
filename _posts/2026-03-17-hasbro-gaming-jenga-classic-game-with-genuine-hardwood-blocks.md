### How it plays: balance, bluff, and bravado
Jenga is a game of imperfect precision. The goal is to remove blocks without tipping the tower. That is a recipe for suspense that can feel almost cinematic, especially as you approach the last few moves. The Classic version with Genuine Hardwood Blocks is designed to keep that suspense intact, while offering a tactile pleasure that plastic just cannot match.

- Setup: Build the tower by stacking three blocks per layer, alternating directions as you go. The process is calming at first, like assembling a tiny architectural landmark with a whisper of sanded wood. You have a moment where you admire the tower before it invites you to unleash a drift of strategy and chaos. 
- On your turn: Remove one block from anywhere below the highest completed level. The choice is a mix of memory, risk assessment, and counting. You might recall which blocks felt slightly looser on the previous rounds or which neighbors have caused you trouble in earlier turns. Some players go for the edges, others choose middle blocks to apply pressure from multiple angles. The balance is delicate; a single misstep can send the whole thing tumbling. 
- After removal: You carefully place the block on top, continuing the stack’s growth toward the ceiling. The top becomes increasingly precarious as you bounce between calm precision and the urge to push your luck. The tension is real, and it travels through the table like a draft that cannot be seen but can be felt in the bones.

What makes the genuine hardwood version special is the tactile feedback. Each block offers a crisp edge that yields to a deliberate twist. When the block slides out, you can hear a clean release rather than a dull clunk or a plastic pop. The feedback helps you adjust your approach in real time. ### Maintenance, care, and longevity
Wood is beautiful but not invincible. Here are a few tips to extend the life of your Jenga Classic hardwood blocks:
- Keep blocks dry and away from excessive humidity to prevent warping. A simple tabletop game should not require caution tape, but a little climate awareness helps.
- Wipe occasional dust with a dry cloth to preserve the natural finish. Avoid harsh cleaners that could dull the wood or alter the texture of the blocks.
- If you notice a block becoming rough or dry, a light rub with a food-grade mineral oil can help restore feel, but test on a small area first to avoid altering color subtly.
- Store the blocks in their tray when not in use to minimize misplacement. The tray is part of the design ethos here, and losing the tray is like misplacing the set of car keys on a day you need to leave for work.
