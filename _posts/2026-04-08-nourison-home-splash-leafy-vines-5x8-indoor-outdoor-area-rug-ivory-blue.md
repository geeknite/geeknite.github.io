Care and maintenance
## Cleaning basics
Outdoor rugs earn their keep by shrugging off everyday chaos. For spills, blot up liquids immediately with a clean cloth, then wipe with a mild soap solution and rinse. For dust and debris, vacuum on a low setting or use a broom to sweep the surface. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade fibers or fade colors; a simple soap solution and water go a long way. If you plan to machine wash, check the specific rug care instructions from Nourison and the product label; many outdoor rugs are designed for quick cleaning and air drying, which is a godsend for lazy weekends. A quick periodic shake outdoors will help remove loose grit before it wears down the fibers over time.

### Stains and weather care
Red wine, coffee, or tomato sauce all have the potential to leave marks, yet this rug is built with stain resistance that makes cleanup feasible, not a reason to panic. For tough stains, a gentle scrub with a soft-bristled brush and warm water is usually enough. If you live in a climate with heavy rainfall, consider rotating or repositioning the rug periodically to prevent pooling in one area. If you get a stubborn spot, a diluted white vinegar solution can help brighten the fibers without causing damage. And if your patch of patio experiences extreme weather patterns, you might store the rug during the harshest months to extend its life. More on storage options later in this piece.
