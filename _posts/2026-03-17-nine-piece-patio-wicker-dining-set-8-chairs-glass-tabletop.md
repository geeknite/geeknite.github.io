## Build Quality and Assembly Experience
If you live for the sound of a good hardware metaphor, you’ll enjoy assembling this set. It’s not a three-minute job, but it’s not a full-blown engineering project either. Expect a few screws, some washers, and a bit of plotting to align arms and legs without turning the entire affair into a wobble-wagon.

### Tools and time
Most sets come with the necessary Allen wrenches or hex keys. A cordless drill can speed things up, but be careful not to over-tighten. It’s a balance between a sturdy build and not turning the exposed wood into a pattern of wood glitter. In general, allocate 60 to 120 minutes for a two-person assembly session, depending on how organized your workspace is and whether you’ve had your coffee or a double espresso shot of patience.

### Durability tests you can perform after assembly
- Sit and swing lightly to test wobble. If you notice significant flex, re-tighten the bolts.
- Place a plate and a few utensils on the glass to ensure there is no rattling or accidental banging when someone reaches for the corn on the cob.
- Shake the table gently to feel the frame’s rigidity around the base. The goal is to avoid that annoying rattle that makes you sound like a haunted house in a windstorm.

## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
The kitchen table of the outdoors is not a permanent kitchen, but it can feel that way if you neglect maintenance. Regular cleaning and protective storage will help extend the life of the set.

### Cleaning routine
- Wipe down the wicker with a damp cloth to remove dust and light debris.
- For tougher stains, use a mild soap solution and a soft brush. Rinse with clean water and air dry.
- Clean the glass tabletop with a non-ammonia glass cleaner and a microfiber cloth to avoid streaks.
- Remove and wash cushion covers according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Dry thoroughly before reassembly.

### Storage and seasonal care
In climates with heavy winters, consider storing cushions indoors and covering the entire set during off-season. If storing indoors is not feasible, a high-quality weatherproof cover can prevent major sun bleaching and moisture intrusion.
