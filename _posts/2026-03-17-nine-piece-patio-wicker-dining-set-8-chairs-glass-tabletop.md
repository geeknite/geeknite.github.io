### Durability tests you can perform after assembly
- Sit and swing lightly to test wobble. If you notice significant flex, re-tighten the bolts.
- Place a plate and a few utensils on the glass to ensure there is no rattling or accidental banging when someone reaches for the corn on the cob.
- Shake the table gently to feel the frame’s rigidity around the base. The goal is to avoid that annoying rattle that makes you sound like a haunted house in a windstorm.
- Do a quick rain test if you can’t wait for actual rain: wipe the surface after a light drizzle and check for any dampness seeping into cushions, which would indicate fabric breathability issues or drainage quirks.

## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity
The outdoors is not a permanent kitchen, but it can feel that way if you neglect maintenance. Regular cleaning and protective storage will help extend the life of the set.

### Cleaning routine
- Wipe down the wicker with a damp cloth to remove dust and light debris.
- For tougher stains, use a mild soap solution and a soft brush. Rinse with clean water and air dry.
- Clean the glass tabletop with a non-ammonia glass cleaner and a microfiber cloth to avoid streaks.
- Remove and wash cushion covers according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Dry thoroughly before reassembly.

### Storage and seasonal care
In climates with heavy winters, consider storing cushions indoors and covering the entire set during off-season. If storing indoors is not feasible, a high-quality weatherproof cover can prevent major sun bleaching and moisture intrusion. If you’re the forgetful type, set a reminder for annual deep-clean and cushion refresh to keep your set looking like new for longer.
