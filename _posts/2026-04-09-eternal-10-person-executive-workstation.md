## Design and Build

The Eternal desk system uses a blend of sheet steel, powder-coated frames, and thick, scratch-resistant laminates on the table surfaces. ## Comfort and Ergonomics

### Spacing and seating for ten
Ergonomics at scale is a challenge because you cannot assume every executive has the same torso length or the same tolerance for joint stiffness after a 90-minute design review. The Eternal system addresses this with adjustable height ranges on each module, a central spine that encourages a uniform alignment, and subtle tilt options on the shared surface to promote a comfortable leaning posture during long planning sessions. The result is a workspace that feels surprisingly friendly to people of all shapes and sizes, as long as you do not mind trading a little personal space for a collaborative vibe.

### Privacy and collaboration balance
Privacy screens are optional, but the design philosophy favors openness with the option to snap in privacy panels for moments when someone has to draft a legal document while others debate the color of the team t-shirts. The screens are lightweight enough to reposition during a lively whiteboard session, yet sturdy enough to resist the gravitational pull of an oversized coffee mug cup that thinks it’s a small planet.

## Tech Integration and Cable Management

### Power, data, and charging rails
The desk ships with integrated power rails along the length of the surface. Each module can host a combination of USB-C PD hubs, power outlets, and data ports. Cable management is the star here. The rails come with internal channels that route power, HDMI, ethernet, and thunderbolt cables neatly to their respective devices. There is a dedicated trunk for the main servers or color-coded connectors, which means you can push a project update through Slack while your monitor remains on a clean and presentable surface.

### Connectivity and peripherals
For teams that require seamless integration across devices, the Eternal desk offers modular monitor arms and configurable under-desk trays for keyboards and mice. The design favors a low-profile arrangement to maximize legroom and reduce the temptation to become a human pretzel while debugging a failing API call.

## Assembly, Setup, and Maintenance

Assembling this 10-person behemoth is not a trivial weekend project, but it is nearly as satisfying as watching a perfectly synchronized LAN party unfold in a living room. The modular nature means you can assemble the spine first, then click in each module like you are building a musical chair orchestra. The instruction manual is surprisingly readable, with clear diagrams that minimize the number of times you say the word ouch while stepping on a stray screw.

Maintenance is straightforward. The laminated surfaces resist fingerprints and coffee stains, which matters given the Friday afternoon sprint where someone inevitably tests the theory that caffeine is a replacement for willpower. If you need to clean the plastic privacy panels, a damp microfiber cloth is enough. If your team uses chalk markers for planning sessions, you can wipe them away with a standard glass cleaner—though keep in mind that marker residue sometimes invites more creative brainstorming and less clean aesthetics.

### Assembly tips
- Do not skip the leveling feet; this is the difference between a straight line and a wobbly parliament of desks.
- Use the central spine for stability and to ensure all ten modules share the same height reference.
- If you plan to mount multiple large monitors, consider an upgrade to the monitor arms to avoid crowding the surface with screens.
