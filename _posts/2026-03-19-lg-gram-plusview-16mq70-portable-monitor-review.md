## Connectivity and compatibility
### USB-C power delivery and data
The 16MQ70 is designed to be friendly to USB-C laptops like the LG Gram line. The key idea is power delivery plus data over a single USB-C cable, which minimizes cable clutter and makes it practical to add a second screen in places where AC power isn’t guaranteed. In practice, you plug in the USB-C, enable “Extend” or “Mirror” in your OS, and you’re off to the races. Some configurations might require a second cable for charging if you’re pushing high workloads, but for standard productivity you’re likely good with one cable.

### Compatibility with a Gram laptop
The Gram’s power-saving hardware and efficient motherboard make it a natural partner for a portable monitor. The 16MQ70 benefits from the Gram’s USB-C output and can run at reasonable brightness without sucking precious battery life from the laptop’s own cycle. If you’re using Windows, you’ll appreciate straightforward window management—snap layouts play nicely with two screens, and you can arrange apps in a way that reduces the “where did that window go” chaos when you switch from desk to café table.

### Practical setup tips
- Enable the external monitor in Windows display settings and choose Extend rather than Duplicate.
- Adjust the monitor’s tilt for a more ergonomic height, especially if you’re typing on the Gram while looking across to the external panel.
- If your content looks washed out or too cool/warm, perform a quick color calibration via the display settings or your favorite color tool.
- Use the case as a stand on non-traditional surfaces like a bed tray in a pinch, but avoid sweaty laps—gravity is not your friend when you’re trying to flip a monitor into a stable state on your thigh.

## Performance and power considerations
### Speed and responsiveness
As a secondary display, the 16MQ70 isn’t tasked with gaming-level refresh rates or ultra-fast response times. The panel is responsive enough for everyday tasks like scrolling through documents, browsing, and video editing previews. If you’re chasing the latest AAA gaming experiences on the go, you’d be better served with a more purpose-built gaming monitor. But for subtle productivity tasks, the responsiveness is perfectly acceptable.

### Battery impact and charging dynamics
Using a USB-C powered panel means your Gram can run the external monitor without a separate brick in many cases. If you’re plugged into a wall, the monitor will draw from the same AC supply as the laptop. If you’re running on battery power, you’ll notice some additional drain on the Gram as the external display is actively drawing power. The exact impact depends on brightness settings and what you’re displaying, but in typical office workloads you’ll get a balanced experience with minimal interference to the main battery life.

### Latency and day-to-day usability
Latency is not a major factor for this kind of device. You won’t feel input lag or screen tearing during normal productivity tasks. If you’re a pixel-peeping designer or a professional who requires a color-precise and ultra-stable external display for critical tasks, you might want to run a quick burn-in test and verify color fidelity post-calibration.
