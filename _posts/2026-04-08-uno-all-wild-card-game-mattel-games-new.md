## How to play: the basics with a twist (and some extra dancing lights)
UNO All Wild Card Game preserves the core UNO rhythm: match color or number to the top card on the discard pile, play a Wild Card when you can, and yell UNO when you’re down to one card. The twist is how often Wild Cards swing momentum—often dramatically—so the table’s fortune can flip in the span of a single turn.

### Core rules recap (for veterans and newcomers alike)
- Each player starts with seven cards. The goal remains the same: shed all your cards first.
- You can play a card that matches color or number with the top card of the discard pile.
- Wild Cards change the current color and introduce unique effects. In All Wild Card Game, those effects can be more unpredictable than a weather forecast in hurricane season.
- If you can’t play, you draw a card from the draw pile. In this edition, there are additional penalties or special moves that might apply when you draw, depending on the current Wild Card in play.

### Special Wild Cards mechanics (the star players)
The Wild Cards in All Wild bring a bouquet of spicy twists:
- All Wild: A rare card that triggers a multi-player effect, potentially reshuffling turns, swapping hands, or forcing a group challenge. It’s not something you save for the last card; you’ll want to deploy this when you’re in the danger zone and need a dramatic swing.
- Color Curve: Some Wilds force a two-color sequence, pushing everyone to rethink color-dominant strategies.
- Hand-Swap: A cheeky card that lets you swap hands with another player, creating opportunities for dramatic comebacks or devastating misreads.
- Cascade Draw: When triggered, it can cause multiple players to draw cards in a chain reaction, turning a tight race into a sprint for survival.

The design philosophy here is to maintain pace while injecting moments of chaotic humor. It’s not a rigid strategy game; it’s more like a party-sport where you’re encouraged to be loud, ridiculous, and sneakily clever with a well-timed Wild Card.
