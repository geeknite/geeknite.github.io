## How to play: the basics with a twist
UNO All Wild Card Game keeps the core mechanic that makes UNO so accessible: match color or number to the top card on the discard pile, play a Wild Card when you can, and yell UNO when you’re down to one card. The twist is how often the Wild Cards swing the table’s momentum. You’ll encounter new Wild Cards that can force a color shift, add or remove players from the round, or trigger a special mini-challenge that impacts everyone at the table.

### Core rules recap (for veterans and new players alike)
- Each player starts with a hand of cards, typically seven in UNO fashion. The goal is to shed all your cards first. 
- You can play a card that matches color or number with the top card of the discard pile. 
- Wild Cards can change the current color being played and offer unique effects. In All Wild Card Game, those effects can be more unpredictable than a weather forecast in a hurricane season. 
- If you can’t play, you draw a card from the draw pile. In this edition, there are additional penalties or special moves that might apply when you draw, depending on the current Wild Card in play.

### Special Wild Cards mechanics
The Wild Cards in All Wild bring a few spicy twists:
- All Wild: A rare card that triggers a multi-player effect, potentially reshuffling turns, swapping hands, or forcing a group challenge. It’s not something you save for the last card; you’ll want to deploy this when you’re in the danger zone and need a dramatic swing.
- Color Curve: Some Wilds force a two-color sequence, pushing everyone to rethink color-dominant strategies.
- Hand-Swap: A cheeky card that lets you swap hands with another player, creating opportunities for dramatic comebacks or devastating misreads.
- Cascade Draw: When triggered, it can cause multiple players to draw cards in a chain reaction, turning a tight race into a sprint for survival.

The design philosophy is to keep the pace moving while injecting moments of chaotic humor. It’s not a strict strategy game; it’s more like a party-sport where you’re allowed to be loud and ridiculous and still feel clever for outsmarting your friends with a well-timed Wild Card.

## Strategy: what to aim for and what to dodge
If you’re a theory-crafters’ dream, All Wild Card Game won’t disappoint, but it won’t replace your love for the pure, swing-free UNO either. The strategic landscape shifts toward timing and social risk management. Here are a few rough guidelines:
- Early aggression vs. late-game finesse: In classic UNO, you might hold back on Wild Cards until you’re closer to winning. All Wild adds an incentive to volatility early, especially if you sense others are sitting on a solid buffer of cards.
