## Maintenance and care: keeping seafoam fresh and fabulous

The care routine for a wool rug isn’t complicated, but it does require a touch more attention than a casual cotton throw. Start with regular vacuuming to pull up surface dirt and prevent it from embedding into the fibers. A rotating attachment helps reach the edges and corners where dust tends to collect. The standard recommendation is to avoid aggressive agitation on wool; you want to lift dirt, not fray fibers. If a spill occurs, blot with a clean, dry cloth first, then use a mild wool-safe cleaner following the product’s instructions. Always test the cleaner on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.

Fluffing is a seasonal ritual, and wool responds nicely to a light brush with a soft-bristled rug brush to restore its loft. Professional cleaning every few years is wise if you have heavy foot traffic, pets, or frequent entertaining that involves spilled drinks and enthusiastic dance moves. The rug deserves a spa day now and then, not a long stay in a cleaning closet. A brief mention for folks concerned about odor: wool naturally resists odors better than many synthetic fibers, so you may find it remains fresh between cleanings, which is a nice perk for busy households.
