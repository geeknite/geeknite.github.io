## Ergonomics and User Experience
### Comfort in a multi-seat world
Ergonomics are the name of the game when you’re sharing space with four other humans who also require a comfortable vantage point. The five-zone layout helps reduce the baseline ergonomic conflicts that pop up in open spaces. Each zone should be able to deliver a comfortable chair height, monitor angle, and keyboard position without resorting to the universal hack of stacking post-its between your chair and the desk like a medieval barrier. The screen system helps with alignment: instead of each person fighting for a new monitor angle mid-meeting, you can tilt and rotate within the allowed range to keep content legible for everyone.

One potential ergonomic caveat: when the whole row sits for long stretches with the same posture, it’s easy for a single person’s screen glare to cause a chain reaction of squinting and repositioning. The W Screen concept is meant to minimize glare through strategic light placement and a shared screen architecture. If your office has harsh overhead lighting or direct sun exposure, you may still want to install anti-glare films or adjustable task lighting that lets every seat glow with just the right amount of brightness.
