### Comfort and Texture
Walkability is high; the rug cushions joints without feeling like a swamp. It’s comfortable enough for long gaming sessions, but not so soft you feel the need to carpet-surf while looting in an RPG. ## Durability and Care
Durability hinges on maintenance. Vacuum weekly on low suction to prevent fiber damage; avoid aggressive beater bars. For spills, blot with a clean cloth and use a mild cleaning solution if necessary. Always test in a small corner to ensure no color bleed. The rug is designed to hold up to typical household wear, but heavy furniture should be moved occasionally to prevent indentations. A rug pad helps distribute weight and reduces curling at the edges.

## Size, Room Planning, and Placement

## Performance in High-Traffic Areas
The rug holds up well in high-traffic zones; kids and fur babies do not shred it in a single weekend. ## Care Deep Dive
- Rotate the rug’s face every 4–6 weeks to even out wear patterns caused by sunlight and chair movement.
- If you have a cat that loves to sharpen its claws on fabrics, deter it with a scratch post and gentle deterrents; the rug is more resilient than most but not invincible.
- For persistent stains, use a cleaner suitable for synthetic fibers; always test first on a small, inconspicuous area.
