## How to play in a nutshell
Saboteur is a hidden role game with a rushing tempo. The base game supports a wide range of player counts from 3 to 10, and the rules remain friendly for newcomers while still offering enough misdirection for veterans to feel sharp. The core objective is simple: create a path from the mine entrance to the treasure and claim the gold in the final round. But here is the catch: among you are saboteurs whose goal is to derail the path long enough so nobody reaches the treasure before time runs out. The miners, on the other hand, want to keep the tunnel together and reach the gold. The tension, hilarity, and occasional chaos come from this dual objective and the secrecy of each player s alignment.

### Setup
Each round begins with role distribution. Depending on the player count, you will have a certain number of saboteurs tucked into the group with the rest as miners. The cards are shuffled, the setup performed, and the path from the entrance toward the treasure begins to take shape as players take turns placing path tiles and using action cards. It is a short setup, quick to grasp, and then you launch into a play session that typically lasts 20 to 40 minutes depending on the number of players. The energy at the table rises as more and more of the tunnel gets laid down, and it becomes clear who is guiding the path and who is trying to bend it away from the exit.

### Turn structure
On your turn you draw a card and play one card from your hand. Path tiles extend the tunnel, curving through the cavern as players attempt to connect the entrance to the treasure. You can rotate, bend, or insert path cards, and you can use sabotage cards or tools that influence how the path is built or how other players interact with you. The round ends when a treasure card is reached or when the draw pile is exhausted, depending on the variant you are playing. The pacing is brisk, with moments of tension punctuated by bursts of laughter as players scramble to salvage a tunnel that has started to look like a medieval spaghetti junction.

### Roles and dynamics
At the start of a round, each player secretly receives a role card: miner or saboteur. The saboteur is a secret agent of chaos, while the miner is a truth-teller… mostly. The saboteurs try to derail the tunnel in subtle ways, while the miners try to keep to the plan and push the tunnel toward the treasure. The key is to read the room and decide when to place a card that may reveal your allegiance or when to bluff your way through a critical moment. The dynamic is similar to social deduction games you may know, but the mechanical focus remains on tile placement and card management rather than long, drawn-out debates.

## Strategy and tips for both sides
### As a miner
- Communicate without exposing your strategy: tell friends what you think is going on, not what you are planning to do. This helps reduce suspicion while still guiding the tunnel toward the exit.
- Use path tiles to create branching opportunities: a path that forks can force saboteurs to reveal themselves by their reaction to a tricky move.
- Balance cooperation and caution: there is a time to trust a player with a critical move, and there is a time to hedge your bets.

### As a saboteur
- Blame timing and misdirection: point out nonessential details about the path, especially when it benefits you by keeping suspicion off of yourself.
- Protect your identity until the last possible moment: the longer you stay hidden, the more dramatic the final reveals can be.
- Use action cards at the right moment: a well-timed sabotage card can derail a path when the group least expects it.
