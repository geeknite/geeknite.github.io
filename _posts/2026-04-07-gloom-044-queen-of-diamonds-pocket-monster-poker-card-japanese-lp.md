## Authenticity and Condition: How to Identify a Real LP Gem vs a Clever Fake

Authentication is not just about calling a card “real” or “fake.” It’s about reading the tiny details that separate a genuine LP gem from a counterfeit with a shiny smile and a slightly too-perfect corner. Here are practical steps if you’re serious about this card:
- Check the stock and lamination. LP cards should feel sturdy and consistent across the surface. A flimsy feel or inconsistent gloss can be a flag.
- Inspect the edge and corner wear. Real LP cards maintain their integrity; suspicious rounding, chipping, or whitening can indicate tampering or heavy handling.
- Look for color fidelity in the diamonds. The diamond motif should pop with a crisp, well-defined hue—over-saturated color blocks or bleeding can signal print anomalies.
- Confirm the No. 044 stamp and the title font alignment. Numerals and text alignment shouldn’t look rushed or misaligned.
- Seek provenance: original packaging, seal, or a credible seller with documented history increases confidence.

If you’re buying online, request close-up photos of the front and back, ask for details about any warranty, and consider a return window. The rarity of LP prints makes them a tempting target for fakes, so a cautious approach pays off in the long run.
