## Star Wars Force and Destiny: Core System Overview

### What is Force and Destiny?

**Star Wars: Force and Destiny** is a standalone tabletop RPG published by Fantasy Flight Games in 2015, using their signature Narrative Dice System. Unlike traditional board games, this is a full role-playing experience where players create Force-sensitive characters and embark on epic campaigns across the Star Wars universe.

#### System Foundations
- **Publisher:** Fantasy Flight Games (2015)
- **Game Type:** Tabletop Role-Playing Game (RPG)
- **Players:** 2-8 (including Game Master)
- **Session Length:** 3-6 hours typical
- **Compatible With:** Edge of the Empire and Age of Rebellion RPGs

### The Narrative Dice System

Force and Destiny uses FFG's innovative **Narrative Dice System** featuring:

**Custom Dice Types:**
- **Ability Dice (green):** Character skill levels
- **Proficiency Dice (yellow):** Advanced character abilities  
- **Difficulty Dice (purple):** Task difficulty
- **Challenge Dice (red):** Extreme difficulties
- **Boost/Setback Dice (blue/black):** Situational modifiers
- **Force Dice (white):** Force power attempts

**Results Beyond Success/Failure:**
- **Advantage/Threat:** Secondary effects and complications
- **Triumph/Despair:** Extraordinary outcomes and dramatic moments
- **Light/Dark Side Points:** Force power fuel and moral consequences

> **Pro Tip:** Embrace narrative results beyond simple success/failure - the dice system creates amazing storytelling opportunities when players succeed with threats or fail with advantages!

### Force Powers System

#### Core Force Powers
The base game includes **19 Force powers**:

**Universal Powers:**
- **Enhance:** Physical augmentation
- **Foresee:** Future glimpses and danger sense
- **Influence:** Mental manipulation and persuasion
- **Move:** Telekinesis and object manipulation
- **Sense:** Awareness and perception enhancement

**Specialized Powers:**
- **Battle Meditation:** Large-scale coordination
- **Bind:** Restraining and immobilizing targets
- **Heal/Harm:** Life force manipulation (Light/Dark versions)
- **Misdirect:** Illusion and concealment
- **Protect/Unleash:** Barrier creation and energy projection
- **Seek:** Finding people, objects, and information
- **Suppress:** Dampening others' Force abilities

#### Morality and Force Alignment

**Morality Mechanics:**
- Start at 50 (balanced)
- Actions shift toward Light Side (71+) or Dark Side (29-)
- **Paragon:** 80+ morality, maximum Light Side benefits
- **Dark Side User:** Below 30, corruption and power

**Conflict and Consequences:**
- Dark Side actions generate Conflict points
- End-of-session Morality rolls determine alignment shifts
- Physical and emotional corruption for Dark Side users

## Rise of the Separatists: Clone Wars Era Content

### Historical Context and Fact-Checking

**Rise of the Separatists** is a **career sourcebook** released in 2019, focusing on the Clone Wars period (22-19 BBY). This era covers:

**Key Historical Events:**
- **Battle of Geonosis** (22 BBY) - Clone Wars begin
- **First Battle of Kamino** (21 BBY) - Republic defense
- **Battle of Christophsis** (22 BBY) - Early Clone Wars engagement
- **Malevolence Campaign** (22 BBY) - Separatist superweapon
- **Battle over Ryloth** (22 BBY) - Twi'lek liberation

#### New Career Options

**Clone Trooper Career:**
- *Clone Officer:* Military leadership and tactics
- *Clone Pilot:* Specialized vehicle operation
- *Clone Trooper:* Infantry combat specialist

**Jedi Career:**
- *Padawan Survivor:* Order 66 survivors
- *Jedi General:* Military command roles
- *Knight:* Traditional Jedi roles

**Republic Navy Career:**
- *Captain:* Ship command and fleet operations
- *Gunner:* Weapons systems specialist
- *Flight Leader:* Squadron command

#### Clone Wars Technology

**New Equipment:**
- **DC-15 Series:** Clone trooper standard weapons
- **LAAT/i Gunships:** Republic transport aircraft
- **ARC-170 Starfighters:** Heavy assault fighters
- **Venator-class:** Star Destroyers of the Republic era

**Separatist Technology:**
- **Battle Droids:** B1, B2, and specialized variants

## Advanced Game Master Guidance

### Running Force and Destiny Campaigns

#### Session Structure
**Opening Crawl:** Set the scene with Star Wars style
**Character Moments:** Individual spotlight time
**Main Adventure:** Primary story progression
**Force Meditation:** Downtime and character development
**Cliffhanger:** Dramatic session endings

#### Balancing Light and Dark

**Light Side Campaigns:**
- Emphasize heroic themes and moral choices
- Reward compassionate and selfless actions
- Focus on protecting the innocent and preserving peace

**Dark Side Campaigns:**
- Explore power corruption and moral degradation
- Create tempting shortcuts and easy power gains
- Demonstrate consequences of selfish choices

**Gray Jedi Approach:**
- Balance pragmatism with idealism
- Present morally complex situations
- Allow players to find their own path

### Combat and Conflict Resolution

#### Lightsaber Combat Guidelines
- **Cinematic Description:** Make every clash dramatic
- **Environmental Interaction:** Use surroundings creatively
- **Emotional Stakes:** Personal conflicts drive combat
- **Consequence Focus:** Wounds tell stories

#### Force Power Adjudication
- **Narrative Integration:** Powers serve story, not mechanics
- **Collaborative Description:** Players help describe results
- **Unexpected Consequences:** Keep Force use interesting
- **Strain Management:** Balance power with sustainability
