## Scent Profile: What it actually smells like (in practical terms)
Scent notes aren’t a legal document, but they’re the map you’ll follow when you want to predict when to wear this and how it will come across in real life. Artisan for Men sits in the fresh, citrus-woody territory with a modern twist. It’s not as saccharine as some citrus fragrances; it feels more grounded, more wearable, and yes, a little more “dad-joke with a garden” than you’d expect from a brand that can pull off gladiator sandals with a blazer.

### Top notes
The opening sequence is bright and immediate, like you just turned on a light in a dim room. Expect a burst of citrusy brightness—think crisp bergamot, a wink of mandarin, and a green, sparkling edge that suggests you just chopped fresh herbs. It’s the kind of opening that invites you to breathe in, nod appreciatively, and say, “Hello, summer, we’ve been waiting.” It’s not overwhelming. It is energetic, almost caffeinated in its briskness, which makes it a refreshing starter for a morning routine or a post-work wind-down that wants to feel like a vacation but doesn’t forget you have a mortgage.

### Heart notes
As the citrus begins to settle, Artisan pivots to a softer, floral-herbal core. Orange blossom dances with a whisper of jasmine, and there’s a subtle green herbaceous touch that makes the scent feel modern and clean. It’s not sweet in the cloying sense; it’s more like a well-composed herbal tea—bright, comforting, and a little sophisticated. If you’re the kind of person who wants a fragrance that transitions well from desk to dinner, this is the moment where Artisan earns its keep.

### Base notes
The dry-down reveals a warm, woody foundation with cedar and subtle musk. The perfume coats itself in a breathable, dry-down warmth that doesn’t become heavy or dirty on the skin. It’s the kind of finish that lingers in a respectful, non-assertive way. You’ll notice the scent settling closer to your skin after about an hour, which is ideal for office settings where you don’t want to announce your presence like a brass band—but you also don’t want to disappear into the wallpaper.

### Scent memory and vibe
Artisan for Men is not trying to reinvent the citrus-woody wheel; it’s polishing it until it shines. The overall vibe is fresh, confident, and casually polished. It smells like a guy who snacks on aesthetics but wears practicality—like a tech geek who also enjoys a well-cut blazer. The fragrance reads as versatile: it works for casual weekends, socializing with friends, and the kind of professional setting where you want to feel energized but not shout your presence from the rooftop. ## Performance: Longevity, Projection, and Real-World Wearability
No fragrance review would be complete without a test drive on actual skin. Performance is where the rubber meets the road, or in fragrance terms, where the scent meets the atmosphere of your day. Artisan for Men sits in a zone that most daily drivers dream of: decent longevity with a scent-sensible projection.

### Longevity
In warm weather, you can expect Artisan to stay around for roughly 6–8 hours on the skin as a comfortable, soft halo. In cooler climates, you might see it linger a bit longer—perhaps into the late afternoon. It’s not one of those “spray and forget” fragrances; you’ll notice it if you lean in, but it won’t fill the room with a thunderclap of aroma. If you’re an all-day wearer who wants a fragrance that remains present without becoming overwhelming, Artisan nails that middle ground nicely.

### Projection and sillage
The projection is friendly but not theatrical. Think “you’ll be noticed by the person sitting next to you at a coffee shop,” not “the entire cafe smells you coming.” It’s the kind of sillage that makes a good first impression in a meeting, a date, or a quick social run, without requiring you to reapply every two hours. For folks who dislike overpowering scents, Artisan offers a pleasant balance: a light-to-moderate halo that can survive a casual conversation without hijacking it.

### Seasonality and climate considerations
Artisan leans spring-to-summer in terms of temperature and vibe. The citrus-woody blend feels more at home in warmer days, outdoor gatherings, and that post-work chill where the sky is still light and the air is at a comfortable humidity. In autumn, it can still perform well if you’re going for a “sunny day of work and a rooftop dinner” look. In winter, it remains wearable but might benefit from a light layering approach with a warmer base fragrance to avoid a flat finish. If you’re a fragrance shelf hopper who swaps scents with the seasons, Artisan is the kind of scent you’ll reach for as soon as the weather starts to loosen up.

## Wearing it: Occasions, outfits, and layering tips
Artisan for Men is the kind of fragrance that acknowledges you have a life outside your couch and your gaming chair. It’s versatile enough for office wear, casual weekends, and social events where you want to smell good without being the loudest scent in the room.

### Office wear
In an office scenario, Artisan shines when you want to present a cohesive, polished image. It’s not intrusive, so it won’t distract meetings or trigger fragrance sensitivities in open-plan offices. A couple of sprays on the chest and neck area is plenty for a full shift. The citrus top keeps things energized during morning hours, while the woody base helps the scent settle into a professional, non-offensive aura by mid-morning.

### Casual and social events
For weekend brunches, relaxed gatherings, or date nights, Artisan sits in a sweet spot. It’s fresh enough to feel approachable, but it carries enough depth to avoid smelling like a mere “summer spray.” It pairs well with denim, a light jacket, or even a sleek leather persona—the sort of ensemble that says, “I care about details, but I’m not trying too hard.”

### Layering tips
If you’re into scent layering, Artisan can be paired with lighter, citrus-forward products or a clean, unscented moisturizer to maximize the skin chemistry without muting the fragrance. A simple tactic: apply a small amount of the EDT to pulse points, then use a fragrance-free lotion on exposed areas (arms, neck) to extend the longevity and keep the sillage in a friendly orbit rather than a full-on perfume comet.

## How to apply and layering tips for the geek in you
- Start with two light sprays at pulse points (wrists, neck) and let the fragrance settle before you consider a third spritz. Overdoing it is a classic gamer move—defeat the boss, not the room.
