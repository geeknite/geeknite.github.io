### LED lighting system and ambient effects

LED lighting can transform a workspace from sterile to cinematic in seconds. This desk places LED strips along the underside of the main surface so the glow reflects on the desk surface and sometimes on your face during late night meetings. The color temperature range spans warm candlelight to bright daylight. The brightness has a wide range, so you can set a gentle glow for video calls or crank it up for streaming sessions. #### Controls and user experience

Control is intuitive. The inline controller on the edge lets you adjust brightness, color temperature, and mode. The LED system stays cool to the touch even after a long day, which is reassuring for folks who worry about heat rising from their desk like a small thunderstorm.

### Power outlets, USB ports, and safety

A built in power outlet strip is a major convenience. It supplies multiple standard outlets and USB ports, enabling you to plug in a laptop charger, a monitor, and a couple of peripherals without chasing a wall outlet around the room. In our test, the outlets functioned reliably and did not overheat after hours of use. Surge protection is included, which is a must for any home office battlefield. The only caveat is that the more devices you plug in, the tighter the cable route becomes. Plan for a clean cable path and use the integrated cable channels to keep the under desk area sane.

#### Cable management and layout

The desk employs a couple of channels and a modest cable tray. It is not the most elaborate cable management system on the market, but it does a solid job of keeping the majority of cords out of sight. The L shape creates a corner where cables can accumulate if you overdo the peripherals, but with a little planning you can route most cables through the back access holes and the tray. We recommend labeling cables as you route them to avoid the classic annual ritual of reorganizing the cable nest.

### Desk space, size, and ergonomics

Size matters, especially in a small room. The L shape provides two distinct zones: a main workspace for the keyboard, mouse, and primary monitors, and a secondary wing that can hold a laptop, documents, or a writing pad. The overall footprint is generous enough for a dual monitor setup, a mid size speaker, and a vertical webcam setup for streaming. The height sits around the standard desk height, which typically suits most chairs well. If you are particularly tall or short, consider a sit-stand converter or a different chair with adjustable height.

#### Ergonomic considerations

The main aim is to minimize reaching and twisting. We found the monitor height is most comfortable when the top of the monitor is at or slightly below eye level. The keyboard is placed on the main surface with enough space to rest wrists in a neutral position. The side wing offers a place to spread out papers or place a secondary keyboard if you want a dedicated workflow area. The LED lighting helps reduce harsh shadows on surfaces, which can reduce eye strain, especially during long coding sessions or late night editing.

### Assembly tips and durability over time

Assembling a large desk is a project that benefits from a plan. The kit is straightforward, with numbered parts and clear screws. Two people make it easier when aligning panels, but one person with careful patience can also do the job. Start with the main frame, attach the legs, then mount the top, and finally connect the LED strip and power strip. Tighten everything to the recommended torque but avoid overtightening to prevent warping the panels. In terms of durability, the desk holds up well under normal daily use. The surface resists minor scratches, and the legs stay stable when used at maximum height or with heavy items on the main surface, as long as you maintain the weight balance across the two surfaces.

### Maintenance and care

To keep the finish looking fresh, wipe with a soft cloth and a mild cleaner. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can dull the surface. Check the screws every few months to maintain stability. The LED system is low maintenance, but if you notice flickering or dim lights, address it sooner rather than later to avoid electrical issues. The power outlets should be kept reasonably free of dust and lint to prevent overheating and maintain reliability.
