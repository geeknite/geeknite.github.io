## How the kit changes character creation in Fortune Row
The core idea behind City of Mist is that the players bring a modern identity and a mythic power set named Mythos to the table. Fortune Row adds layers that are tailor made for a city where fortunes can be bought, hacked, or cursed. The Character Creation Kit brings these layers into a smooth, repeatable process.

### Step by step flow
1) Concept first pass. Players jot a street level identity that feels specific to Fortune Row. A street name, a business card vibe, and a personal code that the character lives by. This step seeds the character with texture that the GM can lean on in sessions to create scenes with purpose.
2) Mythos pairing. Each character is matched with a Mythos facet that reflects a facet of Fortune Row power. The kit suggests a few pairing patterns so that you get a mix of glamour and grit rather than a parade of generic hero types.
3) Backstory prompts. The kit includes 6 prompts that require players to reveal a pivotal moment that ties into a Fortune Row landmark or faction. These prompts are designed to produce hooks for sessions right away.
4) Relationship lanes. The 8 relationship lanes help players lock in ties to key NPCs, rival factions, and allies. This creates a ready made web of connections that the GM can pull for dramatic scenes.
5) Street level assets. The kit provides prompts for hardware and street level tools that the character uses to bring mythic power into the real world. These are not just items, but symbolic assets that echo the characters inner arc.
6) Fate and arc integration. The kit expands on how Fate cards interact with personal arcs. You will gain guidance on how to trigger arcs in play and how to wrap up a story beat into a satisfying narrative turn.

The flow is designed to feel natural and fast. It is still City of Mist at heart — you will still be negotiating mythic power while dodging street level dangers — but the kit gives you a more assured path to get there without heavy GM handholding. If you have ever felt that character creation in a tabletop RPG takes more planning than a space mission to Mars, you will appreciate the clarity this kit brings.

### Archetypes and mythos in Fortune Row
The 20 archetypes in this kit are built to slot into Fortune Row without forcing a misalignment with the city vibe. You get a spectrum of archetypes that range from the cool and composed to the untamed and dangerous. Examples include the Sleight Broker, the Neon Oracle, the Velvet Enforcer, and the Gilded Knife. Each archetype comes with a handful of suggested mythos pairings, starter backstories, and 2 to 3 hooks that tie directly into Fortune Row landmarks.

The design philosophy here is to provide enough scaffolding that players do not have to invent everything from scratch while leaving room for improvisation. The kit encourages players to borrow motifs from Fortune Row like fortune fires, gilded docks, and shadow markets without forcing them to become a walking encyclopedia of city lore. The result is a balanced mix of flavor and function that feels like a living, breathing city rather than a textbook of tropes.

## Play style notes and GM considerations
The kit provides a framework rather than a rigid path. That is a big win for groups that enjoy improvisation. If your table loves long form character development and character driven scenes, Fortune Row offers a structure that supports those aims without policing your creative choices. The GM tools in the kit include quick encounter templates and a small set of fate card prompts that can push a session toward unexpected twists.

A few GM notes that stuck with me:
- Keep the mythos in the foreground but let the street reality drive the scene. The contrast is where the juice lives in Fortune Row.
- Use the relationship lanes to seed conflicts, not to assign a membership card. Let players decide how they align with factions and then use those choices to drive narrative beats.
- When a Fate card is drawn, do not treat it as a hard constraint. Think of Fate as a narrative engine that nudges the story toward a new theme or reveal.
