### Color-Care Basics: Keeping the Cream Looking Creamy

We ran a round of care basics:
- Vacuuming frequency: twice a week for high-traffic areas, once weekly otherwise. Use a vacuum without a beater bar on delicate surfaces to minimize fiber pull.
- Spot treatment: blot (don’t rub) with a mild detergent solution. Always test in a hidden corner first. We learned this the hard way after a tiny coffee incident that attempted to become a blazing story for the yarn’s memoirs.
- Deep cleaning: consider a professional rug cleaning every 12–18 months if you’re in a pet-heavy household or you own a small barista empire. If you decide to DIY, be mindful of the moisture; cream plus water equals potential halo around the edges—no one wants a halo rug.

This rug is not a stain-proof cape, but with a quick action plan you can minimize long-term discoloration and preserve the cream’s charm for longer than a season of your favorite sitcom reruns.

## Cleaning and Care: Keeping the Dream Cream Clean

Caring for a light, cream-colored rug is a little like caring for a prized vintage action figure: you don’t want to overdo it, you want to act quickly, and you want to keep your kit accessible. Here are our practical guidelines:
- Immediate action: blot spills with a clean cloth as soon as possible. Do not rub, or you’ll push the stain deeper and possibly fray the fibers.
- Stain protocol: for common culprits (coffee, juice, sauce), mix a mild detergent with water and gently dab. If the stain persists, repeat in cycles, then consider a professional deep clean.
- Routine maintenance: a gentle vacuum once or twice a week keeps surface fibers fluffy and reduces ground-in dirt. If you’ve got asthma or allergies, frequent cleaning helps keep the room feeling breathable and not booby-trapped with micro-particles from everyday life.
- Deep cleaning: consult a professional rug cleaner every 12–18 months for a robust refresh. If you’re into DIY, use a rug cleaning machine with a low-moisture setting to minimize long drying times.

Pro-tip: if you’re selecting cleaning products, choose options that are dye-safe and cut-down on harsh solvents. The goal is to keep the cream tone true and the fibers happy, not to create a new shade named “Spa White” after a heavy chemical rinse.
