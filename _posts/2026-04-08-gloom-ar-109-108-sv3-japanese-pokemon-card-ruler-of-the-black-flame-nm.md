## Condition, Grading, and What NM Really Means
NM, or Near Mint, is often the sweet spot for collectors who want a card that looks pristine while avoiding the trouble of absolute mint-rare edge cases. For Japanese cards, NM typically implies:

- Corners: Sharp corners with minimal signs of handling; a whisper of edgewear is acceptable, but major dents are not. Be prepared to instruct graders to look for consistent corner sharpness across the four corners.
- Edges: A light, uniform border gloss with no major whitening or chipping. If you notice whitening near the corners, your NM rating may slip toward MP (Moderately Played) or even worse.
- Surface: A clean image surface with no visible scratches, scuffs, or printing flaws that break the artwork’s aesthetic. For ARs, surface gloss and tiny micro-scratches can be a big deal; collectors often debate whether such marks affect the “visual NM” rating.

When you aim for NM, you’re balancing the card’s beauty with its history—how many hands touched the card before it reached you, whether it was kept in a humid showroom, and how careful you’ll be maintaining it. A well-preserved NM Gloom AR can fetch a premium, especially in a language niche like Japanese SV3.

## Care, Storage, and Display Tips
If you want your NM Gloom AR to stay NM, treat it like a fragile piece of glass that smells like a rainstorm in a haunted garden. Here are practical tips:

- Sleeves first, then top loaders. Use a rigid, UV-protected top loader to reduce surface abrasion and to keep glare at a manageable level.
- Avoid humidity. Japanese cards can be particularly sensitive to humidity; use silica gel packs in your storage area and consider a dehumidifier if your basement doubles as a humid forest.
- Cool, dry, and dark. Display cases with subdued lighting tend to display the art without overexposure to glare. If your case has adjustable LED color, go for a cool white or neutral white to preserve color accuracy.
- Rotation policy: If you rotate displays, ensure you re-check the edgelines after a few weeks. The heat from lights can cause a surprising amount of “softening” along the borders.
- Handling habits: Use clean hands or nitrile gloves when handling NM cards. Oils from fingers can, over time, degrade gloss and color; a moment of care saves you a long-term glare battle with your own reflection in the plastic.
