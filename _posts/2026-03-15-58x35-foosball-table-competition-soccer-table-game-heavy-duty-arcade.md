## Durability and Maintenance: Built for the Long Haul
A table of this scale is an investment in time and future rinses of popcorn, beer, and enthusiastic shouts. The heavy-duty construction means it can withstand daily play without losing its edge. Regular maintenance is straightforward:
- Wipe the playing surface with a soft, damp cloth to remove fingerprints and smudges without scratching the lamination.
- Lubricate rods occasionally with a light machine oil to keep the motion smooth. Do not use heavy oils that attract dust and gunk; you want glide, not sludge.
- Check leg levelers monthly and adjust as needed to preserve the ball’s predictable bounce and to avoid any accidental dance performances on the carpet.
- Inspect endcaps and braces for any signs of loosening, and tighten as required. A loose endcap is like a loose cannon — entertaining in your living room, disastrous in your competitive moments.

If you keep up with basic maintenance, this table can remain a centerpiece for years. If you neglect it, you might end up with a creaky field that sounds suspiciously like a haunted arcade and players who refuse to admit that gravity exists when they miss a shot.
