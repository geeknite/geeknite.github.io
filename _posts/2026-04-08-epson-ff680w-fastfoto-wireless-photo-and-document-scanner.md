Intro: In the basement of every geek there is a shoebox full of photos that smells like nostalgia and old snacks. The Epson FastFoto FF-680W enters like a caped hero to rescue those memories and turn them into digital life forms that your cloud can adore. This is a wireless photo and document scanner designed to handle a riot of prints, snapshots, and receipts. The promise is simple yet bold: batch scan hundreds of photos without losing your soul to cables and fiddly settings.

What is the FF-680W? The FF-680W is a fast photo scanner with a built in wireless bridge. It uses an automatic feed mechanism that can stack a batch of prints and feed them through, sending the results to a PC or mobile device via wifi or USB. It ships with Epson ScanSmart software that organizes, cleans and exports to common formats like JPEG and PDF. The key selling point is speed and convenience if you have a backlog of prints and you want to rescue them with minimum drama.

Unboxing and first impressions: The box contains the scanner, a power adapter, a USB cable, a quick start guide and a few sample sheets. Build quality feels sturdy with a matte finish and a quiet presence on the desk. The feed tray is designed to accept a stack of photos and the lid fits neatly into place. There is a small LCD and a row of buttons that look like spaceship controls, but the tactile feel is decent without being plasticy.

Setup and network: The setup process is friendly for non tech folks. You plug in the unit, connect to wifi or a mobile hotspot, install Epson ScanSmart on your computer or mobile device, and you are off to the races. The interface is simple and the app lets you scan from a phone or tablet. The files can be saved to cloud storage with a couple of taps.

Speed and performance for photos: The FF-680W is built for speed. With batch scanning you can push through a stack of 4x6 photos in a hurry, with color accuracy that holds up for everyday use. You will notice some color drift on older prints, especially prints with strong red or blue tones. The scanner uses a 600 dpi optical resolution, which is sufficient for archival needs and for online sharing. The dynamic range is not designed for high end art reproduction, but it is good for family albums. Auto crop and deskew help keep prints aligned, and the dust and scratch removal works well after a few passes.

Document scanning and OCR: The FF-680W is not just for photos. It can handle documents too, though the workflow shines more for shorter runs. Built in OCR converts text to searchable PDFs and JPEGs, helpful for receipts and notes. Expect reliable text recognition on clean pages, with a bit more challenge on pages with handwriting or heavy watermarking. You can scan to a single file or split into multiple files.

Wireless connectivity and app: Wireless is a big plus. You can scan to a computer, tablet or phone, and you can keep the unit tucked away in a closet if you want quiet operation. The Epson ScanSmart app is a compact lab in your pocket and does a solid job of organizing scans. It includes basic editing functions like rotation, crop and color corrections, which save time when combining photos from different eras.

Image quality and color accuracy: For color accuracy you get faithful flesh tones in many lighting conditions and decent renders of landscapes. Old prints may show a touch of yellow, which is common with older stock. The FF-680W handles color restoration well enough for casual viewing and archiving. Dust and scratch removal helps cleanup, though it is not a substitute for careful scanning with a soft brush. For archival grade work you may want to do additional color grading after scanning.

Size and footprint: The FF-680W is not a compact device, but it is not a monster either. It sits on a desk with a tray that can hold a few dozen photos at once. In a small home office it offers a good balance between capacity and space. It is heavier than a typical consumer scanner but not something you cannot move with care.

Feeding and stacking tips: Load a batch of photos and let it run. Avoid brittle prints or photos with peeling edges to reduce jams. Organize by size and print type to maximize throughput. For bills and documents use the document feeder setting and keep tasks separate from photos.

Long term reliability and support: Epson devices are known for reliability. The feed mechanism is the most fragile part over time, so gentle feeding and occasional cleaning help. If you encounter jams, consult the included help and online forums for quick fixes. Replacement parts are available and the brand has a robust support network.

Comparison with other scanners: The FF-680W shines when you have a huge backlog of prints. Other brands may offer higher optical resolution or more advanced OCR, but the built in wireless workflow and batch scanning make the FF-680W a strong choice for family archives and casual use.

Who is this for: Home users with a stash of prints, hobbyists who want a quick way to create digital copies, and small offices that need to digitize a mix of photos and documents. If you regularly scan large 11x17 documents or demand ultra high end color reproduction, a more specialized unit may be a better fit.

Value and accessories: The unit typically includes standard cables and a starter bundle. The value rises with volume of prints and need for organized digital archives. There are third party accessories that can expand feeder capacity and simplify workflow.

Real world usage: In a real home office you can feed a batch of photos and step away for a moment. The job completes and you are greeted with a queue of files ready to view. The digital archive starts to form with structure and metadata.

Where to buy and price: The FF-680W is sold online and in electronics stores. Pricing varies with promotions and bundles. For heavy photo archiving it is worth considering.

Final verdict: The Epson FastFoto FF-680W is a strong choice for households that want to rescue a large photo backlog and do some light document scanning. It works well as a wireless batch scanner and is easy to use for non tech folks. It may not be the absolute fastest or most color exact unit on the market, but for the price and for the intended use, it offers a compelling mix of speed, convenience and reliability.

Tips for best results: 
- Have a plan for your archive and plan your batch runs
- Keep photos separate from documents to preserve workflows
- Use cloud storage to save time on file management
- Clean the glass and the feed path periodically
- Use the Deskew tool to straightened prints automatically
