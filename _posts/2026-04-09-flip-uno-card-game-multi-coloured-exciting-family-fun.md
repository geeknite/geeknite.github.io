### How to play (a family-friendly overview)

If you’ve played classic Uno, you know the rhythm: match color or number, play an action card, and try to rid yourself of your hand while dealing with a chorus of “Uno!” from your tablemates. Flip Uno adds a few layers:

- Flip to switch sides: when a Flip card is played, or on certain action cards, the entire draw and action dynamic can flip. This might turn a two-card lead into a sprint for survival.
- Two-sided strategy: you’re constantly consulting both sides of your deck (figuratively and literally). The Dark side adds different action cards such as Draw Two, Skip, and Reverse that either surprise your opponents or save you from a catastrophe.
- The color challenge: as cards flip, color matching can become trickier. It’s not just about what you have in hand, but what your opponents might force you to deal with next.

The rules remain accessible for newcomers. For seasoned Uno veterans, the new side adds a layer of anticipatory chaos—like watching a classic sci‑fi franchise re-energize with a clever reboot. For a family game night, that balance between familiarity and novelty is gold.
