## Custom sizing: how the sizing works and whether you should trust it
The big hook here is customization. You measure your space, send the measurements, and the manufacturer claims to deliver a rug that fits exactly, or as close to exactly as rug-making allows. In practice, sizing often hinges on a few practical details:

- Exact measurements: The more precise you are, the less likely you’ll be to corner-cut the rug into a trapezoid by accident.
- Border allowance: Some rug shapes need an allowance for edge finishing, which means the actual usable area could be slightly smaller than your floor math. Expect about a 0.5–1 inch variance per side, depending on the finish method.
- Edge stability: Custom rugs can sometimes curl at the edges if the backing isn’t quite right or if the rug sits on a slick floor. You may need a rug pad to tame the edges and keep the fossil vibe from curling into a wave.

The ordering process typically asks for your length and width in inches, sometimes including rounded corners if your space isn't perfectly rectangular. If your room has an alcove that’s 68 by 112 inches, you might propose a rug of 68 x 112, but the factory may suggest a slight rounding on the corners or a tad of extra length to accommodate trimming and finishing. In short: supply the exact size and be prepared for a hair of tolerance.

Pro-tip: ask for a sample swatch or test piece if your vendor offers it. A small swatch can save you a lot of back-and-forth once the rug shows up and looks nothing like the online mockup. And Yes, I’ve done this; no, you don’t want to know how many swatches I have in my apartment right now.

## Custom sizing process: order, receive, and assess fit
Custom rugs aren’t magic; they’re math and manufacturing. The process typically involves:

- Measuring your space with laser precision or, more commonly, a tape measure with a chipped nail from mid-morning DIY anxiety.
- Submitting length and width, with notes about any non-rectangular corners or alcoves.
- Reviewing a digital mockup or physical swatch (if offered) to confirm color and texture before production.
- Production lead time: often 2–3 weeks, though rush options sometimes exist for a premium. If you’re remodeling on a deadline, factor in shipping time and possible delays.
- Delivery and installation: rugs are rolled and wrapped with a bow of optimism, but you’ll want a friend to help carry it in and lay it flat for 24–48 hours to settle into place.

## Cleaning, care, and long-term maintenance
Let’s be honest: rugs aren’t a one-time purchase; they’re long-term roommates. The Quarry Fossil rug is designed with synthetic fibers that handle routine care well, but there are a few caveats:

- Vacuum regularly to remove dirt that settles into the fossil-like texture. Use a vacuum with adjustable height and a gentle setting to avoid snagging.
- For spills, blot gently. Don’t rub—rubbing may push the stain deeper into the fibers and disrupt the fossil texture. Treat with a mild detergent if needed, always testing on a small patch first.
- Deep cleaning: occasional professional cleaning can refresh the look, especially if the rug has accumulated years of coffee, pet accidents, or the occasional DIY project that didn’t go as planned.
- Drying: allow the rug to dry completely before walking on it again to prevent mold and odor. Place it flat on a clean surface and consider a fan if your space is humid.
