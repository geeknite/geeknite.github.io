City of Mist: Mythos Character Pack — A Geeknite Deep Dive

Introduction
City of Mist is a game built on the twin engines of noir grit and mythic might. You roll dice; you bend reality; you pretend you’re a detective who fights gods in the rain-soaked alleys of a city that never truly sleeps. When a new expansion called the Mythos Character Pack arrives, I feel like a kid who found a hidden door behind the vending machine—the squeaky hinge of a dream, and a handful of plastic keys that promise new doors. Spoiler: the doors are shiny, the keyring comes with a nice plastic clank, and the character art is so evocative you’ll want to print it and hang it above your gaming desk like a mythic trophy.

What is City of Mist Mythos Character Pack?
The Mythos Character Pack (MCP) is an expansion designed to slot into the core City of Mist experience. It adds four new Masks (the backbone of a character in this system), a handful of character sheets, new Power Cards, and a few GM aids to weave in a little extra mythic color. It’s not a full campaign book; it’s a character toolkit and a palette cleanser for your imagination. If the base game gives you a neon-lit framework for mythic noir, MCP gives you four extra paintings to hang in that frame.

What’s in the box?
In plain terms: cardboard, cardstock, and the kind of art that makes your inner monologue whisper, i need more mats. You’ll find:
- Four new Masks, each with a distinctive arc, a set of associated Power Cards, and a sheet that helps you roleplay the mask as a living concept, not a spreadsheet of numbers.
- Portrait sheets with bold, cinematic art that doubles as inspiration for table talk and cosplay motivation.
- A stack of new Power Cards and a small bundle of Mythos-Touched Gear that interacts with the core game’s mechanics in flavorful ways.
- GM aids: quick-start prompts, sample encounters, and a few refreshed city threats to slot into your ongoing noir saga.
Production quality remains a hallmark of City of Mist releases: glossy art, crisp iconography, and cards that can survive a gaming session or two (or three, if you’re particularly dramatic about your twists).

New Masks and characters
The heart of MCP is the four new Masks. Each Mask is designed to feel distinct, to generate ideas for scenes, and to slot into the city’s mythic ecosystem without feeling like a cosplay prop on a stage you’ve already used too many times. Here are the broad strokes:
- The Latchkey Paragon: A Mask built around resilience, late-night decisions, and the power of doors—both physical and metaphorical. It’s a great fit for players who enjoy puzzles, improvisation, and a moral dilemma that only unlocks after you’ve tried the obvious solution and it fails spectacularly.
- The Velvet Cipher: Mystery, secrets, and social manipulation as an art form. Expect social maneuvers, truth-baking, and the thrill of they dont know what you know until you say the right line at the right moment.
- The Iron Bell: An urban myth that rings with chaos and disruption turned into something almost lawful. It’s the they clock in the city suddenly ticks at once vibe, with power cards that emphasize timing, pressure moments, and large-scale mythic effects.
- The Glass Comet: A Mask of quick insight, flashes of genius, and lightning-fast actions. Think of someone who leaps between scenes and changes the battlefield before anyone else finishes a sentence. The associated moves reward speed, decisive action, and dramatic reveals.

Power cards and mechanics
The MCP’s Power Cards aren’t just new abilities; they are a toolkit that reinforces the Masks’ core themes. You’ll find:
- Signature moves that underscore the Masks’ unique narrative angles (for example, a Latchkey Paragon might create openings through doors and opportunities others didn’t see).
- Thematic actions that reward players for leaning into their Masks’ identities in conversation and at the table, not just in combat.
- Gear and intrigue options that plug into existing city mysteries, allowing you to graft the new material onto your current cases with minimal fuss.

Narrative hooks and micro-arcs
One of City of Mist’s greatest strengths is the way it uses suspect lines and mythic hints to drive scenes. MCP gives you micro-arcs designed to be dropped into a single session, or threaded across a mini-arc across an entire campaign. Expect prompts that highlight the Masks’ core concerns—identity, myth as accountability, and the tension between personal agency and the city’s unseen forces. If you like your sessions to feel like a detective novel where the chapter breaks are made of neon and myth, MCP delivers.

GM tips and session planning
If you’re running the Mythos pack with a full table, a few practical tips can help you maximize the experience:
- Pre-run the first scene with a one-page prompt that introduces the Mask and the city-wide hook. This helps players latch onto the concept right away.
- Use the Keepers’ Notes to seed a location-based arc. The notes provide ready-to-use ideas that you can adapt to your party’s style.
- Build a myth clock for your city: a simple narrative device that tracks the rising and falling of a Mythos-related event. The newer Masks lend themselves to a clock with a few additional ticks, letting you escalate tension over several sessions.

Comparison to base game and other expansions
If you’re curious how MCP stacks against the core set, think of it this way: the base game gives you a sandbox with a city’s mythic voice; MCP provides four new voices, each with their own language and rhythm. It’s not a replacement for the core rules; it’s an expansion that enriches the action, the mood, and the character dynamic. Compared to other City of Mist expansions, MCP tends to emphasize roleplaying hooks and atmospheric prompts over mechanical overhauls. If you love it when a new Mask changes how you approach a scene, this pack will likely feel like a fresh coat of neon on your favorite alleyway.

Accessibility and inclusivity
The Masks included in MCP enable a variety of playstyles and backgrounds, with themes that can accommodate many different real-world identities and experiences. The pack is a strong step toward more inclusive roleplaying, though as always, the on-table reality depends on your group’s dynamics and the GM’s sensitivity.

Practical tips for new players
- Start with the easiest Masks to incorporate: pick one Mask and one simple arc to test the waters. You don’t need to commit to a long-run arc—MCP is designed to be flexible.
- Use the printable sheets to quickly introduce a new character at the table. The portraits help players imagine the character’s vibe.
- Pair MCP with a small, two-session mini-arc to show off the new Masks’ strengths before you commit to a longer run.

Final thoughts and recommendation
The Mythos Character Pack is a strong addition for players who want more masks, more mythic flavor, and more prompts to fuel roleplay. It doesn’t force a campaign shift, but it does invite you to reframe cityscapes through new legends. If your group loves style, atmosphere, and the cinematic feel that City of Mist does so well, MCP will be a delightful purchase. If you’re on the fence, consider this question: do you want more masks to roleplay, or more power to wreck your rivals’ plans with a well-timed reveal? If the answer is both, MCP is a safe bet.

Final recommendation
Geeknite says: four out of five stars for player-facing content, with a plus one for GM-friendly prompts and a design sense that nails the mood. It’s not a must-have, but it’s a nice-to-have that makes nights feel a little more mythic and a little more daring.
