## The UNO-inspired mechanics, reimagined

If you’ve played UNO, you’ve got the basic rhythm: match color or number, throw a wild card if you want to be dramatic, and hope your friends don’t retaliate with a perfectly-timed DRAW 4 that ruins your vibe. - Card types: In addition to number cards, you’ll encounter moon-themed action cards that alter the flow of play in ways that honor the Sailor Moon ethos (e.g., “Moon Phase” cards that shift turns, or “Transformation” cards that protect you from a punishing draw).

- Card names: The narrative flavor text on some cards nods to Sailor Moon lore without demanding you memorize the entire lore bible. It’s enough to spark a grin and maybe a geeky aside about your favorite Senshi.
- Penalty twists: There are some new penalties and bonuses tied to “Moon Power” or “Star Burst” conditions. These add layers for players who love rules-light chaos and players who love to overthink the optimal path to victory.

### Special cards and their vibe
The special cards aren’t just “skip” or “reverse” with a cute sticker slapped on; they’re thematically aligned with the Sailor Moon universe. As with many retro-style games, there’s a little fog around the edges when you first open the box. Here are practical tips to get you from “What does this card do exactly?” to “I get it, and I’m having a great time.”

- Start slow: Run through a quick learning round where everyone just tries to match and play a couple of turns. This reduces the intimidation factor and lets players absorb the new “Moon Phase” mechanics without freaking out.
