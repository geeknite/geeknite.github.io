## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning Basics

Synthetic fiber rugs typically respond well to regular vacuuming, with attention to avoid aggressive agitation that could flatten the pile. A weekly vacuum in the direction of the pile helps maintain color and fiber height. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth rather than rubbing, which can push stain deeper into the fibers. Mild detergent solution can be used on tough stains; test on a hidden corner first to ensure color stability. Avoid soaking the rug; water can loosen dyes or cause backing damage over time.

### Stains and Hard-to-Remove Marks

Lights pink tones can show certain stains more readily than darker palettes. If you’re dealing with a stubborn mark, a mixture of water and a touch of mild detergent, applied with a soft cloth, can do wonders. For oil-based stains, a dry-cleaning solvent used sparingly may be required. Always air-dry completely before placing furniture back on top. If you have curious pets who like to leave paw prints, a quick wipe-down after an outdoor excursion can keep the pink from looking perpetually muddy.

### Cleaning Schedule and Longevity

A routine cleaning plan—vacuum weekly, spot-clean as needed, and deep-clean every 6-12 months depending on traffic—will help preserve color and texture. If you live in a sunny room, consider rotating the rug occasionally to minimize uneven fading in the most sun-exposed areas. The key is consistency: pink looks magical when it remains evenly vibrant rather than unevenly washed out by sunlight.
