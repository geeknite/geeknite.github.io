Painting and assembly tips that don’t require a megabase of time
For most hobbyists, Cassius is a breeze to assemble, but there are a few tips that can save you time and frustration:

- Dry-fit everything first. The cloak and weapon can look okay separately, but when you assemble them, you want to avoid a misaligned cape that won’t sit properly over the torso.
- Use a pin vise or a sharp pin for the weapon joint if you notice a loose fit. It’s not strictly required, but it helps with longevity on the table during tense moments when players lean in to inspect the model.
- Prime with a darker primer, especially if you plan on painting the scale mail or leather. This helps you build up highlights without missing details.
- For a quick tabletop finish, you can use contrast paints on the cloak and armor. If you pipe in a thin black wash into the recesses, you’ll bring out the textures without needing a lot of time.
- Fine-tune your brushwork by focusing on the main focal points: the face, the edges of the cloak, and the weapon. You don’t need to cover every tiny detail—Zombicide minis thrive on a bold, readable silhouette on the tabletop.

Tips for assembly and painting
If you’re like me and your hobby desk has become a detour for your calendar, you’ll want to assemble and paint Cassius with a plan. Here are practical steps:

- Unbox and dry-fit first: test-fit arms, cloak, weapons, and base pieces before you glue. This saves you from the “brain freeze” moment when you realize you glued something in the wrong orientation.
- Prime with a darker primer and paint in thin layers. Resist the urge to flood the model with paint in one go.
- Use a soft brush for freehand work and a slightly stiffer brush for edges to bring out crisp highlights.
- For the base, you can do a forest floor motif with a mix of flock and tufts. A few subtle tones in the earth can sell the mossy vibe without cluttering the silhouette.
