Fortune Row sits at the heart of a district that hums with meaningless chatter and meaningful consequences. The result is a night or two of play that feels cinematic, with a strong sense of place and a clear through line you can carry from session to session.

If you have played City of Mist before, Fortune Row will feel familiar but still surprising. A note on structure and use

Fortune Row is designed to be flexible. You can run the three adventures as a short arc over a few sessions, or you can cherry pick individual scenes as one shots and weave them into a larger campaign in your city. The GM tools included are designed to be portable, so you can drop them into your own campaign or use them to stage Fortune Row in a different venue or city.

The three adventures

Velvet Dice
Hook: a rumor about a cursed dice that always pays out to the house surfaces in a high roller room. The players must navigate a web of debts, rival factions, and an entity that rewards cunning more than strength.

What to expect in Velvet Dice
- A casino setting that is a character itself, with shifting lights and a floor that seems to reposition itself
- Clues scattered across the casino: a chipped chip, a coded note, a bartender who remembers every night but not every name
- A Mist memory scene where the players glimpse a version of the event that inspires them to change course
- A finale that tests the players moral compass more than their gun arms

If you want to add a twist, consider letting the dice land in a way that reveals a secret about a patron who seems innocent but is deeply connected to the city’s debt to mythic forces.

Brass Seahorse
Hook: a mobile casino ship called the Brass Seahorse glides along Fortune Row waterways, the crew chasing a ledger that could topple a city bank or reveal a mythic contract that binds the city to a power.

What to expect in Brass Seahorse
- The ship is a carnival ride that never stops rolling, with multiple entry points and a crew who all have secrets
- A sequence of deck by deck encounters that escalate into a chase across a moving platform and trundle into a stormy canal
- An artifact that rewrites the city’s ledgers, pitting friend against friend as characters discover who truly holds the power
- An ending that can be abrupt or a smooth pivot into a new status quo depending on how players handle the artifact

Luminous Ledger
Hook: an accountant who can alter memory by rewriting entries in a living ledger is the target. The party must outwit a powerful myth and a city that wants to forget certain truths to keep the peace.

What to expect in Luminous Ledger
- A living ledger that responds to touch and reveals different sections under different light
- Memory scenes that test the players sense of reality and force them to pick sides
- Subtle social manipulation that leads to a climate of fear or hope depending on how well players argue and negotiate
- A final reveal that ties Fortune Row back to an ancient debt owed to the city by the Mist itself

GM tips and tricks for Fortune Row

- Use cues to push players toward the right clues without holding their hands
- Lean into the Mist engine to escalate stakes in a natural way
- Alternate social scenes with action beats to maintain rhythm
- Scale encounters by the party size: if you go with fewer players you can compress scenes and lean more on investigation and dialogue
- Use recurring NPCs to anchor players and deliver callbacks that pay off later in the arc
- Don’t fear letting players improvise. Fortune Row rewards creative problem solving rather than rote planning

NPCs and their secrets

- The Dealer: a guide in the Velvet Dice, cryptic but protective of the city’s debt to myth
- The Gilded Archivist: keeper of the Luminous Ledger, a living person who holds memory as currency
- The Brass Captain: head of the Brass Seahorse crew, pragmatic but ruthless in pursuit of power
- The Whisperer: secret broker who trades in rumors, a test for players who want to pry secrets from the city
- The Concierge: the night manager of the Fortune Row district, knows the pulse of every alley
- The Banker: a shadow figure whose help might be the difference between a narrow escape and a total wipe
- The Bellhop: a friendly face who translates the city’s mood into actions that players can use to their advantage

A quick note on pacing

Fortune Row favors a tight, cinematic rhythm. You want to alternate short investigative beats with scenes that allow for a sudden reveal or a dramatic display of power. The presence of a single, strong location plus a trio of adventures gives you a lot of flexibility: you can run the three scenarios back to back in a weekend, or stretch them out across several sessions with connective tissue that keeps players curious about what happens next.
