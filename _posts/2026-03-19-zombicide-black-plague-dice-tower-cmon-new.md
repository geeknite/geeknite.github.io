## Design and Functionality: How It Plays

### Core Mechanism
The dice tower is designed to dump dice into a tray or cup via a vertical chute, ideally delivering a predictable outcome a gamer can respect and, perhaps, fear. In practice, you’ll drop your pool of dice into the top, listen to the satisfying rattle as they tumble through the ramps, and watch the results appear in the tray—usually with a bit more drama than your last luck-based roll that happened to land all wrong. The objective is to streamline dice rolling, reduce table chicanery (like dice theft, roll-hijacking, or “I thought you rolled a six!” disputes), and add a dash of cinematic flair to the act of rolling.

### Speed and Flow
Compared to dice executions performed by standard dice cups, the CMON tower tends to speed up sessions by cutting down the “one person monopolizes the dice rolls for three minutes while everyone else waits” phenomenon. It gives players a tactile, audible cue that the action is underway, and the dice appear to cascade with a sense of purpose rather than the chaotic sprawl of loose dice across the board. It’s not a miracle cure for all table drama, but it does shave some minutes off every round and reduces the inevitable “I swore I rolled a five” debate that breaks out when a die collides with a stray pencil and vanishes into the underworld of the gaming space.

## How to Use It at the Table

### Placement and Table Etiquette
Place the dice tower to the side of the players who will be rolling, ensuring it’s within comfortable reach of the primary caster or attacker. The goal is to minimize table gymnastics—no one wants to chase a stray D6 across the room during the “final scuffle.” Make sure there’s enough space for any cards or miniatures you’re using and that the dice tower isn’t in line with someone’s chair. This is etiquette in a professional sense, but it also helps you avoid drama that would otherwise spark in the middle of a crucial moment.

### Loading and Rolling Tips
Load the designated number of dice into the top, give them a quick shake to settle, and release. Listen for the dice clang as they cascade through the ramp; the auditory cue is part of the fun. If you notice a die sticking or a block forming, back off and re-seat the tower for a moment. It’s not a deal-breaker—but it’s the kind of small maintenance moment that keeps your game moving smoothly.

### Maintenance Between Campaigns
A quick wipe-down after use is sufficient. If you’ve got a group that loves snack breaks, you might want to keep a microfiber cloth handy to deal with sticky fingers and stray glaze that can accumulate near the ramp. A quick check every few sessions to ensure no pegs have loosened helps maintain longevity. The best part: you can disassemble the unit for storage with minimal effort, which makes cleaning a breeze and packing for cons a non-event.
