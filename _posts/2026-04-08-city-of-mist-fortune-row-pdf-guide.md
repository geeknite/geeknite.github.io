## Introduction
Fortune Row is not just a district, it is a mood. Neon rain on cobblestones, a velvet rope of fate, and a bartender who swears they saw your future in the ice cubes. In this PDF guide we get a deep dive into the Fortune Row district of City of Mist, a place where luck is as common as bad decisions and every alley ends with a revelation and a potential crime scene. If you are here for a dry city guide you may have mistaken this for a budgeting spreadsheet. Fortune Row reads like a jazz solo played through a noir lens, and we love it for that.

### What the Fortune Row PDF Guide brings to the table
The guide expands the core rules content with a new neighborhood locale, a cast of recurring patrons and rivals, and a toolbox of story hooks that feel both cinematic and plausible for a Mist based game. It integrates with core rules without forcing you into a new system learning curve. If you loved City of Mist but wanted a more cinematic playground where the mythic side of the city bleeds into every streetlight, Fortune Row is your jam. It also offers a few table ready adventures that can slot into a longer campaign or function as one shot frames when you want to run a quick night of mythic detective action.

### Layout and art values
The PDF guide uses a clean, readable two column layout that works well on laptop or tablet. The art keeps to a noir neon palette, with splash pages that feel like vintage crime pulp meets modern city sprawl. There is a full color map of Fortune Row that is surprisingly legible at table size and scales nicely when projected. The typography is crisp, margins are generous, and the PDF includes bookmarks that make it easy to flip between sections when you are GMing and your brain is running on coffee and adrenaline. The map options include a high resolution version for zoom hungry GMs and a printable version for players who still print their character sheets on actual paper. The production values speak for themselves and show a level of care that makes Fortune Row feel like a real, living neighborhood rather than a generic dungeon crawl with a neon sign slapped on top.

### New mechanics and how they feel at the table
Fortune Row introduces a handful of micro mechanics that feel consistent with the core system while adding a twist that fits the theme of luck and fate. Expect new tags or player prompts that emphasize fortune oriented plays, such as breaking a curse through a well timed wager, or winning a small fortune through a clever ruse. The mechanics emphasize collaborative storytelling rather than heavy crunch, which aligns nicely with the city of Mist design where players are detectives, legends, or a little of both. The new mechanics are not heavy to learn, but they add enough texture to feel meaningful without overshadowing the investigative focus that most Mist sessions rely on. If you are a GM who loves to reward players for clever social maneuvering as much as for physical action, Fortune Row will feel like a natural extension of the rules rather than a chaotic detour. It also provides cues for how to escalate tension in a social setting, which is often where Mist games either shine or stumble. If you have a house rule that cares about the drama of social encounters, Fortune Row is a good match for your kitchen table experiments.

## The Street and The People
Fortune Row is built around a central street that functions as a narrative spine for both investigation and mood. We have a set of recurring patrons who own or run the key locales along with a handful of rival factions who have skin in the fortune game. The city grows with you as a GM you will find a handful of ready to use hooks that feel like they could exist in any city with a noir vibe and a hint of mythic resonance. The guide also gives you a few more micro locations that can anchor a session, from a speakeasy that hides a fortune telling parlor to a back alley game den where whispers matter more than bullets.

### Important NPCs
The NPC roster reads like a guest list for a very dangerous gala. Each NPC has a short stat block for Mist style play, but most of the value comes from the description, the social agenda, and the potential for evolving relationships across sessions. A few standouts include a fortune teller who can see multiple timelines in a glass of tea, a club owner who secretly runs a network of informants across several neighborhoods, and a broker who taps dances between legal contracts and illegal bets. The party will likely encounter every one of these figures during a long arc, but you can also sprinkle their influence into a tight one shot with a careful hand. For players who enjoy character driven games, these NPCs provide strong footholds for emotional stakes and memorable scenes.

### Factions and power dynamics
Fortune Row weaves in a couple of factions that feel essential to the district identity. There is a movers and shakers crowd that wants to control the flow of information and money, an anti corruption league that resents the city hall influence, and an underworld network that thrives on rumor and misdirection. The dynamic between these factions creates a fertile ground for intrigue, betrayal, and of course, a little mythic interference that the Mist rules are built to accommodate. As GM you can use these factions to drive your sessions, generate misdirections, or create a rotating cast for players to interact with across sessions. The guide provides example scene seeds that align with each faction so you can quickly assemble a session without burning hours designing NPCs from scratch.

## Adventures and Hooks
This is where Fortune Row shines, delivering a mix of one shots and longer arcs that fit the Mist mood. The promises are dramatic, but the execution is grounded in detective craft, social manipulation, and the mythic undercurrents that define City of Mist.

### The Starter Case: The Dice and the Dagger
A perfect entry scenario for players new to Fortune Row, the starter case pulls players into a heist gone wrong where a lucky dice roll changes the course of a single night. The key is to keep the stakes intimate and the scope tightly focused on a handful of locations along Fortune Row. The case uses the districts landmarks to create a sense of place and invites the players to practice misdirection and social engineering rather than brute force. Expect a few red herrings as the story threads weave in and out of the same comic noir vibe that the guide leans on for tone. You will want to emphasize the theme of luck and the sudden velocity of fate, where minor decisions snowball into major consequences.

### The Long Arc: The Velvet Choir
For players who want a longer run, the Velvet Choir arc offers a series of connected investigations that gradually reveal a larger conspiracy involving the citys elite and a mythic force that has slept inside Fortune Row for decades. The arc uses a mix of investigative play, social intrigue, and action oriented sequences that let players explore different corners of Fortune Row, from the luxurious club to the dingier back streets. The pacing is well handled with a few high intensity sessions traded for quieter, character focused moments where relationships matter as much as clues. If you are planning a campaign that runs across several sessions, this arc gives you a coherent spine while still allowing you to improvise and adapt to the players choices.

### One shot seeds for faster game nights
If you need a quick session fueled by a strong mood and a tight frame, Fortune Row comes with a handful of one shot seeds. The seeds can be dropped into your regular Mist city or used as a standalone micro campaign. The best part is how these seeds lean into the sense of fate, so every scene carries a taste of inevitability that the players rarely escape. You can run a one shot in a single evening by focusing on a small cast of NPCs, three or four locations, and a handful of important clues that unlock the twist reveal. Remember to give your players a couple of touchstones to anchor their decisions in the citys mythic aura, and you will be rewarded with a memorable night that will have players begging you to run Fortune Row again next week.

## GM Tools and Guidance
Fortune Row is not just about pretty places and pretty murders, it also gives you the GM toolkit you need to run with confidence. The guide is built with practical table culture in mind, including suggested pacing, tips for weaving mythic elements into mundane scenes, and ready to use scene prompts that you can drop into your session when the players stall or drift off track.

### Mapping and scene planning
The map included in Fortune Row is a practical asset for table talk. For GM planning, you can overlay the map with numbered locations and tie lines to the key NPCs and factions. A good practice is to print out the map and label the places you want to emphasize during the session. You can use the high res version to annotate as you go, keeping the physical board tidy while you guide players through a maze of secrets and allegiances. The guide also suggests a few variants such as alternate lighting or weather conditions to quickly shift the mood and push players toward the next clue or confrontation.

### Clues and Rumors, not trivial puzzles
In Mist, clues are not just a set of locked solutions; they are nods to future possibilities. Fortune Row emphasizes this approach by providing clue bundles that align with different factions and potential outcomes. You can present a rumor that seems harmless but quietly nudges players toward a revelation that redefines their understanding of the district. The effect is a game that is rich in texture rather than a straight path to victory. Players will enjoy the sense that every clue could unlock a different tragic or comic outcome depending on how they choose to pursue it.

### Handling Mist and Fate at the table
The guide thoughtfully treats Mist and Fate as narrative forces rather than purely mechanical constructs. GMs can leverage fate bleeds to create dramatic irony or let the players roll with a major event that seems to be steering them toward a particular arc. The result is a narrative environment where players feel that their choices carry weight, but the world remains unpredictable enough to feel alive. If you enjoy games that reward clever social maneuvering and dramatic timing, Fortune Row will energize your sessions in ways that go beyond a typical mystery adventure.

## How Fortune Row fits with City of Mist core rules
Fortune Row is designed to be modular and additive rather than foundational. It offers a location heavy approach that can be slotted into any existing city or adapted to a new one. The new mechanics feel like a natural extension of the Mist philosophy, giving players additional levers to pull when they are exploring a location that runs on myth and rumor. You can combine Fortune Row with the core rules to craft a campaign that remains anchored to a single neighborhood while still delivering mythic scale moments. The writing is clear and the examples well chosen, ensuring that a GM can jump in with minimal prep. If you have run a few sessions of City of Mist and you want a richer setting to explore where fate and fortune intersect, Fortune Row is a perfect fit.
