Understanding value in this context means looking at a few components:

### Authenticity and common fakes

With any used or mixed lot, authenticity is a concern worth addressing. Here are a few quick checks you can perform when you receive the lot:

- Look for consistent card backs and edging that match the era of the cards in the batch. Most forgeries will show minor misprints around borders or inconsistent holo patterns.
- Inspect the holo foil alignment. Legit holo cards have a precise reflection pattern. If the foil looks jagged or smeared, investigate further.
- Compare print font and card text spacing against confirmed examples from trusted references. For older sets, even a small misalignment can be a clue that something is off.
- Check the card condition under bright light. If you see heavy whitening on the edges, a shallow bend in the middle, or significant corner wear, that will influence the grade you assign for resale or display.
