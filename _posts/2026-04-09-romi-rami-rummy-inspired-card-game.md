## How to Play: The Rules in Plain English
Romi Rami uses a fairly standard melding mechanic, adapted for a compact, fast session. Here is a practical overview that should have you up and running in under 10 minutes.

### Setup and Turn Structure
- Shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand to each player. The exact hand size can be defined in the rules as typical, but a common approach is seven cards per player for a 2 to 4 player game. The remaining deck forms the draw pile, placed face down in the center. A single card is flipped to form the discard pile.
- The player to the left of the dealer goes first. On a turn you will perform two actions in order: draw and discard. You may draw one card from the draw pile or take the top card from the discard pile. After drawing, you may lay down any eligible melds from your hand. A meld is typically a set of three or four cards of the same rank or a run of three or more consecutive cards of the same suit. The exact rules for what constitutes a valid meld are stated clearly but flex enough to accommodate differing styles of play in a family setting.
- After laying down melds, you must discard one card from your hand to end your turn. If your hand becomes empty after laying down melds, you have a successful round and begin scoring.
- Scoring is straightforward: at the end of a round, players add up the point values of their unmelded cards. The exact point values are listed in the rulebook but include common card values with aces and face cards receiving standard point assignments. The first player to hit a pre-defined threshold or the player with the lowest score after a set number of rounds wins the game.

### Melds: Sets and Runs
- Sets are three or four cards of the same rank in any suits. For example, three Queens from different suits form a valid set.
- Runs are three or more cards in consecutive order within the same suit, such as 4, 5, 6 in hearts. Some variants allow longer runs or require runs of a minimum length; check your preferred house rules if you are playing with a larger group.
- Wild cards: Romi Rami may include wild cards that substitute for any card in a meld. If wilds exist, the rules will specify how they are valued in scoring and how they can be used in a meld. The presence of wilds tends to accelerate the pace and increase the possible dramatic turns late in the round.

### Endgame and Scoring
- When a player goes out by melding all their cards, the round ends immediately. The other players tally up the points of the cards still in their hands. In some variants you may be able to reduce points by hitting a special meld or by playing a clean hand (no unmatched cards) in which case you gain bonus points.
- The game continues for a pre-defined number of rounds or until someone hits a target score. At the end of the final round, the player with the lowest total score wins.

### House Rules and Variants
- 2 player mode can feel more like a tug of war as there is less information hiding. Consider adding a rule that allows a brief peek at the opponent hand after each round to spice up the strategic discussion.
- A variant called quick melds encourages players to lay down any meld as soon as possible to speed up rounds. This can lead to more aggressive discard choices and faster table talk.
- Cooperative or semi-cooperative twists are possible with a rule that players can form a shared meld to reduce the opponents’ advantage. This is fun for a party twist but can dramatically change the game’s pacing.

## Strategy Tips for Different Play Styles
- If you love planning ahead, start tracking possible melds early in each round and watch for the discards that will unlock your own runs. You can often peek at which suits are being discarded more and adjust your approach accordingly.
- If you enjoy bluffing and misdirection, pay attention to opponents’ discard patterns. Sometimes a risky discard can bait opponents into following a path that you can exploit in the next turn.
- If you are playing with younger players, keep the pace up by framing melds in simple phrases like I need three of a kind or a run of three to six. The mnemonic style helps younger players understand the concept quickly and keeps them engaged.
- If you want to maximize social interaction, call out your intentions aloud. For example, say I am aiming for a run in clubs and watch for reactions. This creates a friendly atmosphere of ongoing discussion and shared surprise when the melds finally appear.
- If you want to speed up the late game, consider a variant that reduces the number of rounds and increases card draw options. This keeps players on their toes and prevents the game from dragging into a sleepy evening.
