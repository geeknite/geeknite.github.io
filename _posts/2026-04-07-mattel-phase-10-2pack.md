## How to Play the 2 Pack
Phase 10 is a turn-based, draw-and-discard game with a central goal: complete the current phase before anyone else does, then move on to the next phase and rinse and repeat until someone clears all ten. The 2 Pack is not a tournament-grade reimagining of the rules; it is two full experiences that you can either unify or separate, depending on your crowd and your tolerance for smashing good luck with bad draws.

### Setup
- Choose how many players you want. Phase 10 supports a good range of players, but with a 2 Pack you can easily run two games in parallel or combine the decks for a larger, campier table.
- Shuffle both decks (or keep them separate if you want two independent games). Deal 10 cards to each player per phase path. The remaining cards form the draw pile, with the top card flipped to start the discard pile.
- The objective is to complete a predefined phase pattern. Phase 1 might be sets and runs, phase 2 adds a twist, and by phase 10 you need a fully baked combo to win the round.

### Playing a Turn
On your turn you draw either from the draw pile or the discard pile, then you may lay down cards that match the current phase, and finally you discard one card to end your turn. When you complete the required phase, you get to advance to the next phase and potentially go out (finish) first, depending on the rules your group agrees to. The flexibility is one of Phase 10s strengths and also a potential doorway to chaos if you forget who is supposed to be on which phase.

### Phases and Patterns
Each phase is a small puzzle. Typical early phases focus on sets (three of a kind, four of a kind) and runs (consecutive cards of the same color). The later phases demand more complex patterns (e.g., combination of sets and runs). The 2 Pack simply doubles the potential patterns you can chase because you have two decks to choose from or two independent sets of patterns to tackle in tandem.

### Strategy Tips
- Plan ahead, but not too far ahead. Phase 10 rewards a flexible mind. If you aim for a specific phase too early, you may clog your hand with cards that are no longer relevant.
- Use the wild and skip cards with care. Wilds can smooth over a rough hand, but overusing them can lead to missed opportunities later.
- Watch your opponents. Part of the joy (and agony) of Phase 10 is reacting to what other players are collecting. A steady eye on what remains in the draw pile and what your neighbor might need can save you from a brutal loss.
- The 2 Pack shines in social settings. If you have twin groups or a big family, the two decks let you rotate players without long waits, keeping energy high and arguments at bay.
