## Setup and Rules at a Glance

Skip-Bo remains the same at its core: your goal is to be the first to play all your stock cards in numeric order from 1 to 12. The 2023 edition keeps the core rules intact while offering tweaks that support quick onboarding.

### Quick Start Guide
1) Shuffle the deck and deal a certain number of stock cards to each player depending on the number of participants (the base variant often uses 15 cards for each player, with a few adjusted for fewer players). In a 2- to 6-player game, this balance is tuned to keep the game snappy but not punishing.
2) Place the stock piles in front of every player. These are the piles you’re trying to deplete by playing cards in ascending order.
3) Create a draw pile in the center. The Draw-and-Play dynamic is what drives the suspense: you draw, you build, you shout, you rejoice or groan accordingly.
4) On your turn, you can play from your hand or from your stock pile onto the central play piles in ascending order. Wild Skip-Bo cards can be used as any number to fill gaps in sequences. It’s a bit like playing with a few “wildcards” in a puzzle game, which makes life fun and chaotic.
5) If you run out of cards in your hand, you draw back up to five cards (the standard rule) and continue. If you exhaust your stock pile, you’re basically winning, and the victory dance begins.

### Special Cards and Rules Variants
- Skip-Bo Wild Cards: Use them as any number to complete a sequence. The saving grace of every quick-paced game night.
