## Cooling and Airflow

Airflow is life for servers. The AR3100’s design acknowledges this with front-to-back airflow orientation and perforated doors. If you fill the cabinet with 2U servers and a 5-kW PDUs, you’ll want to maximize airflow and avoid hotspots like a desert in August. The front door’s perforation pattern is chosen to balance security and airflow. If you’re using high-density equipment, consider adding blanking panels in empty spaces to prevent hot air from short-cutting across the cabinet. Some models offer optional fan kits or vented panels to tailor airflow to your environment. If your lab runs hot, you’ll thank yourself later for taking airflow seriously rather than ignoring it until a bank of servers politely tells you to fix the cooling problem.

A tip: place your most heat-generating devices toward the center of the cabinet, away from the doors where airflow tends to be aggressive, and avoid clustering like devices that simply all require air at the same time. Upgrade your rack with an external cooling solution if your environment runs hot. It’s not glamorous, but it saves you from a meltdown scenario and a reimage or two.

## Assembly, Setup, and Maintenance Tips

- Plan your space: measure the clearance for door swing, wheel rotation, and freestanding clearance. Racks are not Tetris; you can’t cram UV-lighted cables into a 7-inch gap and pretend it’s a feature.
- Use the included wheels to move the cabinet into position before you mount internal rails. It’s easier to maneuver when you don’t have to wrestle a brick with a gravity problem.
- Label everything. A sharpie on a white label is your friend when you realize you’ve connected port A to port B and you have no idea what you did in month three of your lab project.
- Blank panels are not optional; they are mandatory for airflow control and aesthetic uniformity.
- Regularly check the door alignment. It’s common for doors to drift slightly over time. A quick adjustment will save you from the mobile mirror effect where your rack reflects your lab lights oddly every time you pass by.
- Clean and inspect the locking mechanism. Lubricant is your friend; friction is not when you’re trying to access a server at 2 a.m.
- If you need to add additional fans or ducts, plan for it during the initial setup; retrofits are possible but not always elegant.
