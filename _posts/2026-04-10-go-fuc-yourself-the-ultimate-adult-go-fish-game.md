How to Play: Core Rules, With an Adult Twist
-------------------------------------------

The core mechanic is familiar to Go Fish veterans: ask another player for cards to complete a set. In the Adult Edition, every successful draw or completed set unlocks a little extra fun—usually a dare or a racy-prompt, rather than a mere “got it!” The rules are simple, and that’s by design. The game wants to be accessible, not a puzzling cryptogram you need to memorize for tenure.

Setup

1) Shuffle the deck and deal five cards to each player (adjust for larger groups). 2) Place the remaining deck face down as the draw pile. 3) Prepare the Dare/Card Deck by placing it within easy reach. 4) Decide on a tone—safe-for-work (SFW) or “we’re all adults here” (NSFW-lite, with consent guidelines). The rules accommodate both modes; you’ll just keep the dares fun and non-demeaning.

Turn Structure (Go Fish Core)

- On your turn, you ask another player for cards of a rank. For example: “Do you have any fours?” If they have one or more cards of that rank, they must hand them over; you then continue your turn. If they don’t, you draw one card from the draw pile and you announce, “Go fish!” Then you must take a Dare Card from the Dare Deck if your draw is a certain result (the exact mapping is listed in the rule sheet).
- When you collect all four cards of a rank, you lay down a set and draw a new card for the next turn. Completing a set can trigger a lighthearted consequence or bragging-rights reward (see Variants).

Winning the Game

The aim is to complete as many sets as possible before the deck runs out. When no cards remain in players’ hands after a round, count completed sets. The player with the most completed sets wins the game and, by convention, gets to assign the first post-game dare.
