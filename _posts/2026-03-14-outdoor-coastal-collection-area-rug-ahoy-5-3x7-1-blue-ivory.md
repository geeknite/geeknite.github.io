## Performance Outdoors

### Durability

Durability is the headline feature for any outdoor rug, and the Ahoy is designed to endure sun, rain, and the occasional splash from a friendly water toy. The fibers are intended to resist fading from UV exposure, which means your colors should stay close to their original mood after a lengthy sunny experience. The rug should also withstand foot traffic, pet paws, and the occasional dropped beach toy without losing its shape. If you live in a windy area, you’ll want to secure the edges or use outdoor furniture to keep the rug from a surprise guest appearance on the neighbor’s terrace.

### Cleaning and Maintenance

Maintenance is where outdoor rugs earn their keep. Routine care includes normal vacuuming to remove leaves and sand, followed by an occasional rinse with a hose for deeper cleanups. The polypropylene fibers tolerate water well, so you can wipe away spills with a damp cloth or a mild soap solution if something stubborn decides to linger. Let it dry fully before rolling up or exposing it to heavy traffic again. If you’re in a high-sand zone, a quick shake-out every so often can keep the surface from feeling gritty. For persistent stains, a gentle scrub with a soft-bristled brush and mild detergent tends to do the trick without grinding the fibers down.

### Non-Slip Considerations

Outdoor rugs should stay put, not become a slip-and-slide hazard. The Ahoy rug’s backing is designed to help it stay put on smooth surfaces, and you can add a rug pad for extra security on wood or tile decks. A pad can also dampen noise and extend the rug’s life by reducing friction with the floor. If you have a particularly breezy patio, consider a low-profile furniture arrangement that anchors the rug edges while still allowing airflow. Your feet will thank you when you don’t end up tracing lines in the grass while chasing a runaway lawn chair.
