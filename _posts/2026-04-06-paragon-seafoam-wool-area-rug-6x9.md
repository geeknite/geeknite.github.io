## Care and maintenance: keeping your rug looking fresh

Caring for a wool rug isn’t rocket science, but it does reward consistency. Here’s a practical approach that felt like a cheat code for lazy homeowners yet yields real results:

- Daily/weekly care: light vacuuming with a standard suction setting (no beater bar on maximum), rotating the rug every few months to promote even wear, and avoiding direct sun for prolonged periods to prevent color fading.
- Spill handling: blot—do not rub—a spill with a clean, white cloth as soon as possible. For tougher stains, a mild wool-safe detergent solution can help. Always test a small area first.
- Cleaning depth: for a deeper clean, use a carpet cleaner designed for wool or bring in a pro for professional cleaning every 12–18 months depending on foot traffic. Avoid hot water extraction that can cause wool to shrink or felt.
- Stain resistance and protection: consider using a wool-safe protective spray if you anticipate frequent spills (coffee, red wine, glitter glue—yes, that one is real). Re-apply as directed by the product manufacturer.
- Drying and deodorizing: air-dry completely after any cleaning. For a quick odor refresh, sprinkle with a light dusting of baking soda, wait a bit, then vacuum away. Don’t bake your rug—this isn’t a casserole.

## Rug pad and floor interaction: the unsung heroes

A high-quality rug pad is not a luxury; it’s a necessity. It reduces sliding, provides extra cushioning, and extends the rug’s life by absorbing some of the friction that would otherwise wear down the fibers. For a 6x9 Paragon Seafoam rug, a medium-density felt pad works nicely. If you have hard floors (wood, laminate, tile), a non-slip pad prevents sliding and protects both rug and floor from scuffs.

Pro tip: place the pad slightly smaller than the rug so the edges of the rug still look like they’re floating gracefully on the floor rather than gripping the edges of your furniture. The result? A cleaner look and fewer “rug edge chevron” moments when you vacuum under the sofa.
