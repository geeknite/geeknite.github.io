## Collecting Tips

- Storage: Use top-loader sleeves and a quality binder with acid-free pages to minimize edge wear. Keep away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade.
- Handling: Wear cotton gloves if you’re dealing with pristine copies, especially if you’re evaluating a potential high-value find.
- Grading: While there isn’t a universal grading standard for Carddass, you can approximate condition with common-sense metrics: centering, corner sharpness, surface gloss, and any print defects. For the serious collector, create a checklist and photograph each card you inspect.
- Display: Consider a light-curtained display case for your No. 262 copy if it’s truly special to you. The pairing of Goten and Trunks shines under diffused light and can spark conversations with other Dragon Ball fans.
- Variants: Be aware that holofoil variants, border color shifts, or subtle print details can define value. If your goal is a complete No. 262 series, you’ll want to identify and document exact variant attributes for all copies you collect.
