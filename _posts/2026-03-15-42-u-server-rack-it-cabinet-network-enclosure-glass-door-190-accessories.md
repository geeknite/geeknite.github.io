## Assembly, setup, and best practices
### Getting it out of the box and into a sane arrangement
- Clear your workspace. A 42U cabinet is not a one-person job, especially if you’re alone in your garage lab wearing a hoodie and pretending you’re a scientist from a sci-fi movie.
- Check for missing hardware. The kit typically ships with a set of cage nuts, bolts, screws, and a simple instruction sheet. If you’re missing something critical, don’t improvise with drywall screws; contact the seller for replacements.
- Install rails first. Make sure the rails are aligned and latched. This is the point where you want to avoid a misalignment that causes a ladder of dead devices when you slide in a 1U server.
- Plan for cable management from day one. A good plan saves time during maintenance and makes cleaning up after a messy cabling day less painful.

### Cable management and airflow strategies
- Use vertical cable managers to guide cables from devices into the rear of the cabinet. The door may be glass, but your cables should stay behind the scenes.
- Consider PDUs with proper spacing. A good PDU reduces clutter and ensures power is available for your gear without causing a meltdown on a hot afternoon.
- Airflow is your friend. If you have a glass door, you might need a slightly more aggressive airflow plan. If your devices are getting hot, you can add vented panels or take advantage of optional side panels to optimize cooling.

## Maintenance, care, and longevity
- Cleaning: Glass is relatively easy to clean, but avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade finishes. Soft microfiber, mild cleaner, and a little patience go a long way.
- Security: If you’re in a shared space, ensure the locking mechanism engages properly. A simple lock with a key is good; a magnetized locking system is better if you’re supervising a high-value rack.
