### Practicalities of care and upkeep
Flat-woven cotton rugs like this Kilim are comparatively forgiving. They resist crushing under typical foot traffic and are easy to spot-clean. The trade-off is that cotton can absorb spills more readily than wool, so you might want to address accidents quickly with blotting and a gentle cleaning solution recommended by the rug manufacturer. Avoid soaking the rug; cotton dries quickly but prolonged moisture can lead to mildew or fiber distortion.

Regular vacuuming with a suction head that does not have a powerful beater bar is recommended. A gentle beat helps lift dust without pulling threads. Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear patterns, especially in sunlit corners where color may fade over time.

### Differences from other rug types you might consider
- vs wool rugs: wool rugs tend to be more springy and resilient to dirt; cotton Kilims are cooler underfoot and more breathable, which helps in warmer climates or rooms with poor airflow.
- vs tufted or machine-made: Kilims are flat and lightweight, which makes them easier to move, swap, or layer with other textiles. The craft behind Kilim implies a slower production timeline and a unique character, while machine-made options offer predictable uniformity.
- vs oriental or Persian rugs: Kilim patterns are more geometric, often minimalistic in feel, and the color story is typically bolder in modern colorways like sky blue. If you crave ornate curvilinear medallions, you might prefer something with a different weaving tradition, but if your vibe is modern, this Kilim hits the sweet spot.

## Durability, maintenance, and longevity
Durability in Kilim dhurries is a function of weave density and fiber resilience. Cotton is strong and easy to clean, but it is not as crush-resistant as wool. In high-traffic spaces, you may notice slight flattening in the central wear zone after several months, which is normal. The flat weave makes it easier to vacuum and clean, but it also means you should be mindful of spills and moisture. Quick action is your friend; blot spills with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and test any cleaner in an inconspicuous corner first.

Edge integrity is another thing to monitor. Kilims often feature minimal edging, relying on the weaving technique to hold the fibers in place. If you have pets or a household with frequent foot traffic, consider adding a GrippyPad or non-slip underlay to ensure the rug stays put and doesn’t shift beneath coffee table legs.
