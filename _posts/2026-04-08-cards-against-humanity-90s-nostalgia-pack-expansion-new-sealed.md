## Playability and Party Dynamics
### Setup and Flow
CAH’s core mechanic remains simple: players fill in the blanks with their funniest or most cringe-worthy card to earn the most points. The 90s Nostalgia Pack adds new prompts, which means you get to watch your friends shift between savage, nostalgic, and surprisingly personal humor in real time. Setup time remains minimal, but the content density means you’ll want to remind everyone that if someone’s played a particular decade show parody tonight, you’re all legally allowed to groan and roll your eyes introvert-ly.

### Group Dynamics
The magic of any CAH expansion is how it reveals your friends’ personalities. Some players lampoon the era with precise insider references; others lean on bold, silly exaggeration. This pack tends to reward players who can thread a joke through a flurry of pop-culture homages while keeping the topic approachable for group newcomers. If your table tends toward political humor or heavy sarcasm, you’ll still find moments to laugh, but you’ll want to watch for lines that veer into let’s-not-go-there territory; the 90s vibe can easily slip into cringe if left unchecked.

### House Rules and Moderation
As with any CAH night, a few simple house rules do wonders here. Consider a soft “90s buzzy mode” where all references must be anchored in widely recognized 90s culture, with bonus points for non-controversial, high-energy prompts. You can also add a “nostalgia immunity” safety rule to avoid overstaying a joke’s welcome. The point is to keep the tone light, playful, and inclusive — while still letting players own the punchline when the time is right.
