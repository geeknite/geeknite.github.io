## Real-World Use and Setup

### Outdoor Readiness

Outdoor spaces throw a lot at textiles: sun, rain, sprinklers, pets, and the occasional rainstorm that lasts longer than your patience. The Ahoy rug is designed for outdoor wear and tear, but there are still best practices. Place it in a covered or semi-covered area if possible to extend color life. If direct sun exposure is intense for hours at a time, you may notice some lightening in the most sun-exposed zones, which is normal for many outdoor fibers. A rug pad with a breathable backing can help reduce creeping and movement on windy days and can also add a little extra cushion for barefoot strolls. When selecting a rug pad, look for a product designed for outdoor use, ideally with a drainage-friendly bottom to avoid moisture becoming trapped beneath the rug.

### Placement Ideas

Think of the Ahoy as a visual anchor for your coastal aesthetic. On a deck, orient the longer edge along the seating arrangement to emphasize the conversational area. On a smaller balcony, you might position the rug under a compact bistro set and still have enough area to walk around. If you’re pairing with other outdoor textiles, such as throw pillows, outdoor curtains, and planters, the Ahoy’s color scheme makes it a versatile bridge between blues and natural tones. Consider pairing with white or gray furniture to let the blue fossilize into the space, or lean into citrus accents for a punchy, playful vibe. Creative lighting, such as warm string lights or lanterns, can transform the rug into a stage for twilight conversations and late-night board games.

### Cleaning and Maintenance

For routine maintenance, a quick vacuum to remove loose debris and a gentle rinse with a hose should keep the rug looking fresh. If you encounter a spill, blot (don’t rub) and clean with a mild detergent and water. A soft-bristle brush can help lift any stubborn spots without damaging the fibers. After washing, ensure it dries thoroughly to prevent mold and mildew. When not in use for extended periods, roll or store the rug in a dry place if possible. Avoid folding for long-term storage, as repeated flexing can cause creases. If you see color fade in heavily shaded areas or persistent sun exposure zones, rotate the rug seasonally to even out wear patterns.
