- Monitor alignment: With the built-in screen and the option to add up to five more, you can align viewing angles to reduce neck strain among the team. It won’t fix every posture problem, but it will reduce the neck-crank factor during long review sessions.
- Seating compatibility: The desk accommodates six chairs with comfortable legroom; you won’t be wedged in like a sardine, although you may feel a little like one in a high-stakes sprint planning session after coffee. The legs’ spacing allows most ergonomic chairs to breathe, which reduces the risk of a shared “chair pile-up.”

If you’re wondering about personal space, fear not: the design’s intent is to create a shared workspace while preserving individual zones. With six users, you’re unlikely to get a perfectly customized ergonomic setup for each, but the surface area makes it possible to create personal command posts without stepping on toes—literally and metaphorically.
