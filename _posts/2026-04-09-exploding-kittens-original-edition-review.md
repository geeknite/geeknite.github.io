## How to play: quick start and long laughs

The core loop is delightfully simple. On your turn, you draw a card from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you are not necessarily doomed—unless you don’t have a Defuse card. If you do have a Defuse, you can use it to avoid exploding, then you must place the Exploding Kitten back in the deck somewhere, ideally in a position that your opponents will hate you for discovering later. If you draw some other card, you simply play a card if you want to, or pass, depending on the card’s effect. The action cards add texture: you can shuffle the deck, skip your turn, or force someone else to take a turn. There are See the Future cards that let you peek at the top few cards, which is basically a party game’s version of cheating the mood indicator, and it is glorious when used cleverly.

The beauty of the game is that you do not need to memorize a binary tree of outcomes. You only need to know a handful of rules and the presence of Defuse cards, which are the lifelines you’ll clamor for when your friends are playing their best and your risk tolerance is low. The initial rounds are often lighthearted, with players learning the deck’s rhythm as they go. The later rounds tend to oscillate between suspense and hilarity as someone makes a daring move, or as the deck reveals a cascade of defused kittens that make everyone cheer for the underdog. And remember: the chaos is half the point. It is a party game that mocks the seriousness of strategy while still forcing you to balance risk and reward in glorious fashion.
