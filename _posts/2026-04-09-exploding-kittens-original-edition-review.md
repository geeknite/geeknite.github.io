## How to play: quick start and long laughs

The core loop is wonderfully simple. On your turn, you draw a card from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse, you are out—unless you’re a genius who can bluff their way to the next round. If you do have a Defuse, you can use it to survive, then you must place the Exploding Kitten back in the deck somewhere, ideally in a spot your adversaries will curse you for discovering later. If you draw a non Exploding Kitten card, you can play a card if you want to, or pass, depending on the card’s effect. Action cards let you shuffle the deck, skip turns, or force someone else to take a turn. See the Future cards let you peek at the top of the deck, which is basically the game’s version of cheating at a party—deliciously allowed and incredibly risky when used at the wrong moment.

The beauty of it is that you do not need to memorize a binary tree of outcomes. A handful of core rules, a few Defuse lifelines, and a dash of misdirection are enough to keep the evening humming. The initial rounds are a breezy learning curve; the later rounds escalate into suspenseful silliness as someone dares a risky draw, or as the deck reveals a cascade of defuses and near misses that feel like a cinematic climax in a living room. The energy is contagious, the banter is lighthearted and sharp, and the entire experience remains more about social interaction than about a complicated strategy puzzle.
