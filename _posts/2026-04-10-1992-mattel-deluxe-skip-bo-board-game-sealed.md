## How the Game Actually Flows (High Level, No Spoilers)

Skip-Bo is a sequencing game at its core. You have to build piles in ascending order, from 1 to a target number, with strategic use of your hand and the deck. In the Deluxe edition, the board or play surface provides a stage for you to visually track how many piles you have in play, how many are complete, and how close you are to closing out a round.

From a mechanical standpoint, here is the gist you would relay when teaching someone who has never played the game before:

1) Each player gets a hand of cards from the central deck. The goal is to play all of your cards into four build piles, which are constructed in ascending order from 1 up.
2) You can discard or place cards depending on the rules for your turn, and you can use special wild cards to fill gaps wherever needed.
3) You manage your stock pile, your hand, and your built piles concurrently, trying to optimize your chances to play more cards than your opponents in a single turn.
4) The first player to empty their stockpile wins the round; the game can be played as a single round or a series of rounds to determine an overall victor.

If you’re curious about actual play specifics, this preamble is enough to get you started and to ramp up on the fun quickly.
