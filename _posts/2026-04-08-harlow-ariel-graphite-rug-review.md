Maintenance and Cleaning

## Vacuuming and Care Routine

Maintenance is the unglamorous hero of rug ownership. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting is recommended to avoid fuzzing the pile. If you have a high-pile vacuum, you may want to switch to a gentler setting to avoid pulling on fibers. For spills, the quick-response rule applies: blot, not rub. Blotting helps prevent color migration and preserves the surface texture. A mild detergent solution or a specialized rug cleaner is a safe option for tougher stains. Always test on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.

## Stain Resistance and Longevity

The Ariel Graphite offers decent stain resistance thanks to its fiber blend, but like any rug in a busy home, it will show wear over time if neglected. The best approach is to treat it like a small, stylish dragon: keep an eye on it, feed it with regular cleaning, and it will be your loyal, non-snoring guardian for years to come. If you have pets, trim nails regularly and use a protective rug pad underneath to reduce friction and prolong life. A pad also helps with underfoot comfort and keeps the rug in place on slippery floors.
