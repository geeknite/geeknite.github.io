## What is the Blank On One Side Survivor Card?

The concept is simple on the surface: a survivor card with all the usual crumbly details on one side plus a blank canvas on the other. In practice, it becomes a canvas for the players to annotate their own survival plan, to sketch a route through a mall, or to jot down the unique ability you house-rule for your Friday night campaign. The card's blank side invites improvisation — which is exactly what Zombicide demands when hordes of undead are knocking at your door with improvised weapons and a questionable compass.

### The Story Behind the Card

Phil Purple 322, CMON's resident color wizard, reportedly splashed the palette with more purple than the original card required. The result is a blank on one side that still looks like it belongs to the Zombicide universe — a universe where color helps you memorize which gang is coming, and purple is not just royalty; it’s a warning: you might need to scribble something before the last rumble.

### Design and Artwork

The shades of purple in the production art are both a blessing and a curse. They draw attention to the blank side, which is exactly the point. The rest of the card’s framing remains faithful to Season 1's design language: bold type, crisp iconography, and a layout that begs you to stamp your own notes into the space. The card stock is sturdy enough to survive a brutal night of play; it flexes but does not bend, and it tolerates pencil graphite with a tolerance that only gamers who doodle during boss fights will appreciate.

### Materials and Print Quality

Zombicide's cards have always been a test of parent-level packaging: thick enough to survive a tumble, thin enough to slip into a deck without adding weight. The blank side, as printed, is a clean slate; the surface accepts graphite and ballpoint without ghosting, and the finish is resistant to smudges if you treat it with care. If you use permanent markers, you risk bleeding through, so we recommend light inks and careful pressure. The surreptitious glow of purple under certain lighting helps you identify which side is blank at a distance due to the reflective highlights placed by Phil Purple 322.

### Practical Uses and Homebrew Potential

Here is where things get genuinely interesting. The blank side can serve as a mini-editor’s note pad for your campaign. jot down:
- Custom survivor abilities or status effects
- Quick-paths around a map
- Special gear conditions or quest steps
- A reminder of day/night cycles or fatigue rules in your homebrew variant

In our session, we used the blank side to design a special one-shot scavenging route: the players had to mark safe caches, supply caches, and restroom breaks (which, for morale reasons, became a critical resource). The result was a lot of laughter and a surprising amount of strategy, as players navigated the mall’s corridors with a laminated map on one hand and a partially used Sharpie on the other.

### Gameplay Interactions and Rule Clarifications

If you intend to use the blank side for house rules, decide with your group how strict you want to be about marker permanence. A dry-erase marker can be a valid approach, especially for temporary notes, but it may look messy as the night wears on. If you plan to sleeve the entire card, you could opt for a water-based marker on the blank side, with a quick wipe between rounds. On the other hand, if you want to preserve the card’s value for a diorama-like display, you might limit usage to non-permanent notes and revert to a fresh blank side each game session.

### Customization Tips and Accessories

If you want to take the blank side to the next level, consider:
- A thin enamel pen for crisp lines on card stock
- Clear sleeves with indexing marks along the edge to preserve your notes
- A microfiber cloth for quick erasing if you’re using a whiteboard marker on a sleeve
- Label stickers sized to the card’s dimensions for color-coded stats

We also recommend using pocket-sized notebooks designed for tabletop RPGs. They pair well with a card’s blank space and give you a dedicated space to maintain your own scribbles.

### Collectibility and Market Value

Let’s talk about the elephant in the zombie horde: value. The blank-on-one-side variant isn’t exactly common, but it’s not the rarest thing in the CMON catalog either. That means you’ll likely see a few listings on the secondary market, especially with good condition sleeves and minimal wear on the edge. If you’re a completionist who adores Phil Purple 322’s work, this card is an attractive piece to display and a nice conversation starter on your display shelf. It’s not just about utility; it’s about the story the card tells: a nod to the more playful side of a grim universe.

### Comparisons to Standard Survivor Cards

Compared to standard survivor cards, the blank side card offers more potential for personalization and occasional laughter. It does not alter the standard stats or the baseline rules; rather, it introduces a new dimension for social play. For some players, that is a dream; for others, it’s a mild distraction during a tense moment. The balance factor is crucial: if your group uses the blank side too aggressively, the playing field could become cluttered or inconsistent. We recommend a light-touch approach: one or two signatures per campaign, or a strict house-rule about what can be written on the blank side.

### Care and Storage

Storing this card is straightforward: keep it in the deck’s protective sleeve, away from heat sources, and away from scoring tools that might dent it. If you’re going to scribble on it, consider taking a crisp photo for your digital collection. Our recommended practice is to use a non-permanent marker and a protective sleeve with a clear overlay to track changes to the card over time. If you’re one of those collectors who values pristine stock, you might want to obtain a second copy for display, leaving your primary copy in near-mint condition.
