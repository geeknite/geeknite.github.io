## Assembly and setup: how hard is it?

Let’s be honest, there’s a thrill in the “I built a thing that holds dinner” feeling but a danger in the “I just pieced together 3 hex keys and a screwdriver and now there’s a squeak” moment. The 9PCS set typically requires assembling the table frame plus attaching the chairs to their legs or to the base. The process can be summarized in a few steps:

1) Sort the hardware. You’ll usually have screws, washers, nuts, and the odd Allen wrench. Lay them out alphabetically if you’re a control freak or just line them up by color if you’re a human who likes color-coordinated chaos.
2) Assemble the table frame. The frame is a sturdy skeleton that the glass top rests on. Ensure the frame is level and the glass top sits securely on the frame’s top rail. Some manufacturers use clips or silicone spacers to keep the glass from wiggling.
3) Attach cushion supports to the chairs if required. Some chairs arrive with cushions already attached; others require you to slide the cushions into the fabric covers. Make sure the cushion covers are clean before you zip them on — nothing ruins a vibe like a coffee-stain red cushion wobbling in the sunset.
4) Fit the cushions into the chair frames. Ensure the covers are zipped up and the cushions sit evenly on each seat. If a cushion doesn’t sit flat, rotate it and check the frame for any misaligned slats.
5) Final safety check. Make sure the chairs are stable, the table legs are flush with the floor, and nothing is rubbing against the glass. A quick test with a hand on the glass top will reveal any wobble.

Pro-tip: lactic humor aside, having a cordless drill on low torque can speed things up, but you don’t want to over-tighten and crack a frame. Hand-tighten is often enough, and you keep your furniture honest.

## Durability, weather resistance, and maintenance

Weather is the ultimate test for outdoor furniture. The PE wicker is designed to resist fading and moisture. The aluminum frame is corrosion-resistant and light enough to move around without turning your arms into a metal detector’s dream. The tempered glass top is scratch-resistant but not indestructible, so avoid banging heavy objects on it and try not to clamp a grill tool to it in a moment of culinary genius gone wrong.

Maintenance tips to keep the set looking fresh:
- Cushions: remove covers when possible and machine wash on gentle cycle if the fabric is washable. If not washable, spot clean with mild soap and warm water. Dry fully before reassembling. Store cushions indoors or in a weatherproof storage box during heavy rain or winter.
- Wicker: wipe with a damp cloth and mild soap. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the weave. If you see the weave starting to separate, you can use a dab of outdoor-grade sealant or thread repair to address small splits, but large repairs may require a replacement piece.
- Glass: wipe with a glass cleaner or a vinegar-water solution for a streak-free finish. Don’t bang the glass or place heavy objects on its surface.

In terms of longevity, you’re looking at several seasons of use if you take a little care. If you’re in coastal regions with salty air, rinse occasionally to prevent salt buildup on metal parts and check the hardware for any corrosion signs.
