## What is UNO Flip?
UNO Flip is a two-sided deck game published by Mattel. It uses a double-faced card system: a light side with classic UNO rules and a dark side with new actions and tougher penalties. The flip mechanic is central; at any moment a Flip card or a specific action can flip the entire draw pile to reveal the other side. The flip is central to the experience and the theme; the dual-nature presentation helps keep players engaged and uneasy in equal measure. The graphic design emphasizes the dual nature, with bright colors on the light side and a darker, more ominous palette on the dark side.

## How to Play: The Quick Start
- Setup: Shuffle the double-sided deck, deal seven cards to each player, and flip the top card of the draw pile to start the discard pile on the light side. The rest sits there, waiting for the inevitable chaos.
- Winning: Be the first to get rid of all your cards. Don’t forget to yell Uno when you’re down to one card; if you forget, penalties await… sometimes in the form of a flip.
- Playing a Card: You can play a card that matches in color, number, or symbol. If you can’t play, draw from the draw pile.
- Flip Mechanic: Every time a Flip card is played (or a specific Dark Side action triggers), the draw pile and discard pile flip to reveal the other side. The effect can change the entire dynamic of the table, potentially turning a safe lead into a desperate sprint to the finish.
- Dark Side Changes: On the dark side, action cards replace light-side actions or add new consequences. They can swap colors, force immediate draws, or shuffle the deck in chaotic ways. The exact text on cards varies by edition, but the principle is the same: prepare for the unexpected.
- Uno Calls and Penalties: The standard UNO rules apply on the light side, but on the dark side, averted misplays can result in additional draws or forced flips. It’s a mini-mock test of your nerve.

More rules and extension notes are in the official rules here: https://www.mattelgames.com/us/game/uno-flip

## Light Side vs Dark Side: The Thematic Duel
On the light side, UNO’s cadence remains familiar: match color or number, play a Wild card, perhaps toss a Draw Two or Skip. The dark side introduces a new tempo: you flip the entire deck and draw piles can explode with penalties. The game thrives on this contrast: players who enjoy classic UNO will feel at home on the light side, while those seeking a pulse-pounding twist will relish the dark side’s new card texts and sudden twists.

- Light side highlights:
  - Classic color and number matching
  - Wild cards and color changes
  - Draw Two and Skip mechanics
- Dark side highlights:
  - Draw Five or similar heavy penalties
  - New Wild and Flip cards that shift control
  - Potential deck resets and reversed turns

## Variants and House Rules
No two UNO Flip games are identical; players often tumble into house rules that reflect their group’s vibe. Here are a few common ones:

- Turn-Timing Rule: Limit the number of flips per game to keep the pace manageable.
- Flip on Demand: Allow players to flip on their own turn rather than waiting for a card effect; this speeds up late-game drama.
- Limited Dark Side: Keep the dark side flips to a minimum in family games; use the dark side only for third or fourth players.
- Uno Quick Play: Shorten the number of cards to four or five in each hand to keep the game moving quickly.

## Social Dynamic and Accessibility

## Strategy and Tips
- Build a flip-aware deck: You’ll want to hold on to color-swap and wild cards so you can control the pace when you flip.
- Read your opponents’ tells: Watch their body language and reactions to flips; use this to anticipate upcoming changes.
- Endgame management: As the deck flips and the endgame approaches, you’ll want to have a plan for catching up or sealing the win before the other players exploit the dark side's ability to punish.
- Colour management: When you’re in the light side, focusing on colors that you have the most of helps stabilize your position. When you flip to the dark side, you may need to shift focus to cards that help manage sudden penalties.
