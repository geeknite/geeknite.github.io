## What is the Jenga Pass Challenge anyway?

The Jenga Pass Challenge is Hasbro’s playful riff on the classic Jenga concept. Instead of quietly tugging a block and hoping gravity behaves, this edition amping up the social angle: players pass the play space around the table (or around the couch, or around the cat—we don’t judge) as you take turns removing blocks. The main twist is that the tower doesn’t just sit there as a test of steadiness; it becomes a relay. You pull a block, pass the torre—pardon me—tower to the next player, and the clock, a timer, or perhaps the facilitator’s gleeful countdown, adds some extra zing. It’s Jenga’s chaos engine turned up a notch for groups that crave interaction, laughter, and the occasional dramatic gasp when gravity finally says nope.

For folks who remember Jenga as a quiet game of careful finger gymnastics, this edition is a delightful jolt—like being handed a calm cup of tea and then discovering it’s actually a spicy chai with chili flakes. It’s the same tower, but the social pacing makes every move feel a little more dramatic, a little more high-stakes, and a lot more hilarious when someone drops a block and the whole thing wobbles like a drunk Slinky.

## How to play: setup and flow

### Setup

- Build the tower as you would for classic Jenga: 18 levels, three blocks per level, alternating orientation. This is where you set the baseline of tense anticipation.
- Decide the turn order and choose a passer. The pass-around mechanic can be as theatrical as you want: you can pass like a hot potato, or you can do a ceremonial handoff with jazz hands and a finger-gun. The rules typically require that you remove a block without toppling the tower and then pass to the next player within a set window.
- If the game includes a timer, place it within easy reach. If not, a phone countdown works just fine—just don’t be that person who times blocks with a stopwatch app and judges everyone’s risk tolerance.

### Turn structure
- On your turn, you carefully remove a block according to the standard Jenga rules: only one at a time, using one hand, and without moving the tower as you pick.
- Once you successfully extract the block, you place it on top of the tower, as in classic Jenga. But here’s the twist: you then pass the tower (or pass the opportunity to move) to the next player. The passing could be instantaneous or part of a grand flourish, depending on your group’s vibe.
- If the tower falls, the round ends and the player responsible for the last legal move faces the classic consequences—usually last one to make the tower tumble lives to tell the tale while everyone else applauds or points and giggles.

### Scoring and variants
Some boxes include a simple scoring system to track who stays in the round longest while others opt for a no-scores, all-ages vibe where the fun is in the chaos. If your group loves a little competition, you can award points for:
- The difficulty of the block chosen (hard blocks get more points).
- The speed of the pass between blocks (faster passes yield more points due to adrenaline). 
- The style of the move (dramatic reveals or daring under-the-table steals). You can even add house rules like “no talking on turns” or “must narrate your block choice with a tiny story.” It’s your game, your rules, your night.
