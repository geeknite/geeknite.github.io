## Assembly and usage tips from the field

A few field notes from our simulated play sessions can help you avoid beginner missteps. First, keep the rods clean and free of dust. A quick microfiber wipe before play helps maintain a consistent glide. Second, check the leg locks after you fold and unfold the table several times because sometimes the latch can loosen with repeated use. Third, align the ball return paths so the balls do not linger or bounce unpredictably; if you notice a frequent ball jam, you may have a misalignment or a small foreign object in the path. Finally, take a moment to calibrate your expectations. The two ball setup is designed for fast, friendly games rather than marathon tactics. This is a table built for entertainment, not a lab for ball physics supremacy.

### Care and maintenance

Maintenance is straightforward. Keep the surface clean, and avoid harsh chemicals that could damage the playing field. A light conditioning with a damp cloth is usually enough. For metal rods, a quick wipe with a dry cloth to remove fingerprints keeps them looking crisp. If you find that the players lose their edge or the rods start to feel a touch stiff after extended use, a light application of silicone spray on the joints can restore smooth motion. Do not overdo it; you do not want a slippery battlefield. Regular inspection of screws and leg locks is prudent, especially if you frequently fold and unfold the table for storage. A little proactive maintenance goes a long way in preserving performance and preventing the dreaded wobble during a tense comeback moment.
