## Sealed versus opened: what should you do with your kit?
The age-old question in sealed product is whether to keep it sealed or crack it open and let the contents roam free. For Core Set Arena Starter Kits, the decision often boils down to two things: your collection goals and your desire to actually play with the cards.

- If you are a pure collector: Sealed is your friend. The pristine box, the potential for rare misprints (rarely, but occasionally), and the glow that comes with owning something pristine can be hard to resist. The downside is you miss out on immediate access to play material, which can be frustrating if you crave instant gameplay.
- If you are a player who wants to play: Opening the kit makes sense. The two 60-card decks, the digital tie-ins, and the ability to tinker around with real cards unlock a flexible starting point for your paper and Arena adventures. You can borrow, trade, or mix and match pieces with other decks you own and gradually shore up your weaknesses.
- Hybrid approach: Some collectors open the kit but retain certain sealed elements like the outer packaging or a subset of cards that hold sentimental value or potential for future investment. It is not unheard of for players to keep a blend of opened and sealed components to maximize both playability and nostalgia.

The decision ultimately rests on your personal thresholds for risk, ritual, and the thrill of a complete sealed box. If you want a definitive Geeknite approach: open the kit if you want to actually play the content; keep sealed if you want to preserve it as a collectible and still have a robust Arena experience through its digital code.
