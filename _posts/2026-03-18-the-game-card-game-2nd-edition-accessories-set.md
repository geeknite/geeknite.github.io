## What’s in the box

### Card sleeves
The set includes two sleeves options, a standard matte sleeve and a premium glossy sleeve variant. The standard sleeves are designed to fit the core deck without adding excessive bulk, preserving the deck’s shuffle feel while offering protection from frayed edges and accidental coffee spills. The two colorways help players quickly distinguish deck types mid game, which is a small but mighty feature when the board turns into a sea of pips and icons. If you are a sleeve snob, these are respectable contenders that avoid the plastic glare some budget sleeves reveal under lamplight.

### Token set and markers
A collection of acrylic tokens in several shapes and colors takes the place of pencil scribbles on a napkin. You get a healthy number of coins, plus a handful of special markers that correspond to various in-game effects. The tokens have a smooth edge that feels pleasant to flip and pinch during tense moments. The tactile reward here is real: you can tell when you are gaining an advantage just by the heft of your markers, which is exactly the kind of psychological delight we geeks embrace.

### D6 dice and optional components
If your table demands dice for speed rounds or variant rules, the accessories set slides in two solid dice that feel crisp when rolled, without the squeak of bargain dice. They fit into a tiny roll tray that will make you feel like a tiny corporate drone who became a dice-smith in a home game store. For players who prefer not to roll, there are conversion tokens that map dice outcomes to card effects—nice little design flourish that keeps the pace snappy.

### Storage insert and organizer
The centerpiece is a compact insert with labeled compartments. The insert keeps cards, tokens, and dice separated and accessible, which matters during heated play when you are trying to locate the exact marker in the last seconds of a timer. The fit is snug but not overly tight, reducing the likelihood of wear from repeated stuffing and removing during late night gaming marathons.

### Rulebook and quick-start materials
A slim rule sheet and a quick-start guide round out the package. They are concise, with diagrams that actually help rather than confuse. For rookies, this is a friendly entry point. For veterans, it’s a reminder that the game has depth but can be taught with a few well placed visuals.

### The aesthetic notes
The entire set leans into a modern minimalist aesthetic with pops of color for quick recognition. The typography is legible, the icons are consistent, and the tone of the artwork respects the game world without turning every component into a poster. It is a good balance between style and function, and the result is a table that looks less like a storm of chaos and more like a curated mini museum of gaming gear. ## Assembly, setup, and maintenance tips

Setting up the Accessories Set is straightforward. The insert snaps into the box with a reassuring click, and you can quickly partition cards into sleeves thanks to the labeled sections. A neat trick is to pre-sleeve the core deck in the standard sleeves before you start a session, then use the premium sleeves for any special deck variants you bring to the table. This helps with both durability and color coding, a small but meaningful advantage when you are juggling multiple piles. The tokens and markers are sturdy enough to withstand aggressive table tossing that occasionally accompanies heated debate about which move is optimal. If you want to extend the life of the acrylic tokens, a light wipe with a microfiber cloth after a session keeps them looking sharp and reduces micro-scratches that can accumulate under bright lights.

Care and maintenance are simple. Wipe surfaces with a dry or slightly damp cloth. Do not submerge the sleeves or tokens in water or any cleaning solvent that could affect color or finish. The dice tolerate occasional roll cleans with a dry cloth, but avoid exposing them to heat that could warp the plastic. If you drop the set during a dramatic endgame, there is a small chance you will hear the dramatic clack of fate as the tokens bounce—this is not damage, just your night delivering a theatrical finale.
