### How to Set It Up and Start Hurting Your Friends (with Laughter)

Setting up Exploding Kittens is a ritual that signals, to your group, that you are about to participate in a card game where the objective is to survive the deck’s deliciously risky cards. Here’s a concise setup:

1) Shuffle the deck. Seriously, shuffle it like you’re trying to predict the future — because you probably are. Add the number of Defuse cards to the deck if you’re using the full original edition setup. 
2) Each player receives a few cards based on the rule variations you want to run. In many groups, you’ll start with a small hand so players don’t feel like you’ve just handed them a nightmare to play. 
3) Remove or distribute a few Exploding Kitten cards depending on the number of players. You want the game to feel tense but not a single-player combat simulator. 
4) Place the draw pile in the center and decide who goes first. The first turn is always a moment of tense quiet followed by a chorus of “Go ahead, you monster.”

The core flow is simple, and the game thrives on the pace. On your turn, you perform one or more actions from your hand, then draw a card. The fear comes from the looming possibility that you’ll draw an Exploding Kitten and be forced to play a Defuse card to survive — or die laughing.

### Core Mechanics: The Joy of Risk and the Defuse Tax

Exploding Kittens thrives on a handful of core mechanics that feel both classic and modernly absurd. The deck is a mix of Exploding Kitten cards and a suite of Defuse and action cards. The Defuse card is your lifeboat; it buys you time, a chance to live, and maybe an opportunity to roast a friend’s plan by expertly defusing their moment of glory. The action cards add layers of strategy beyond “draw and hope.” See the Future cards let you peek into the next few draws. Attack cards force the next player to take multiple turns, which can be a delightful form of passive-aggressive cruelty. Nope cards can cancel other players’ actions, which leads to epic standoffs about who gets to blame fate for the misfortune that has just befallen them.

The balance is not about deep strategy; it’s about social dynamics and the joy of chaos. The game punishes being a lone wolf and rewards cooperative mischief in a way that is both chaotic and harmless enough for a family game night. If you’re into crunchy Eurogames, this isn’t your jam. If you enjoy chaotic party games that let you yell “Nope!” at your friends with the seriousness of a Shakespearean tragedy, you’ll likely adore Exploding Kittens.

### Replayability and House Rules

Exploding Kittens has a degree of replayability that comes from the randomness and the social nature of the game. With a fixed deck, you’ll experience many variations depending on which cards you draw, who you target, and how aggressively you use See the Future. Some groups love to introduce house rules to speed things up or to add new kinds of mischief. Examples include:

- Rule of Three: after three Exploding Kitten draws in a row, the game ends with the surviving players in a fashion that’s dramatic enough for a soap opera.
- Quick Defuse: each player starts with two Defuse cards instead of four, making the survival odds more evenly balanced.
- Noisy Defuse: Defuse cards require a physical action to activate, like clapping your hands three times before you defuse. Yes, this can lead to chaotic cheer and mischief.

We’ve found that keeping it light and avoiding overly heavy rules helps ensure the table remains a friendly battlefield rather than a courtroom drama. House rules are part of the charm, and if you’re bringing this to a family gathering, consider tailoring the rules so younger players stay engaged without getting overwhelmed by the chaos.
