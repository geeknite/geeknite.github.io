## Weather resistance and durability

Outdoor furniture is not magic; it needs to be smart about sun, rain, and the occasional winter drizzle. The rattan weave is designed to resist UV damage, and the underlying frame is typically aluminum or steel that resists corrosion. A quality set will have protective caps on the feet to prevent scratching decks and should come with quick-dry cushion fabric to minimize mold growth after a damp night. If you live in a climate with heavy rain, consider storing cushions indoors during the worst storms or using breathable covers with dedicated vents to avoid moisture build-up.

## Assembly and care tips

The assembly process is a set of modular pieces that click together with basic tools included in the box. It should take an enthusiastic adult somewhere between 45 minutes and two hours depending on your DIY patience and whether you took a coffee break at every step. The key is to lay out pieces in the configuration you want before you start screwing things together. Make sure to tighten all bolts evenly so the frame remains square and true. Once assembled, a quick wipe-down with mild soap and water keeps the rattan looking fresh. For stubborn stains on cushions, a light mix of water and a gentle detergent is usually enough; avoid bleach as it can break down the fabric over time.
