## Durability, weather resistance, and the saga of living outdoors

Bamboo, when treated, can stand up to sun and rain with grace. The frame’s protective coating helps resist moisture uptake and minor staining; the reality is: you’ll want to store cushions indoors or under a breathable cover during heavy rain or winter. The fabric’s water resistance handles the occasional spill—your beverage of choice won’t instantly become a permanent part of the cushion’s memory. Care wise: a mild soap solution and a soft brush will cover most surface cleaning needs. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fabric’s integrity. For bamboo, occasional conditioning with a bamboo-safe sealant or oil can extend the life and preserve color. The general rule of thumb is: give it a little love every now and then, and it will respond with reliability and a richer look over time.

## Maintenance and care in the wild (aka your backyard)

- Cushions: remove covers for washing; air-dry away from direct sun to prevent color fading. Quick-Dry foam helps with spill mitigation, but always dry thoroughly to avoid mold.

- Storage: during heavy weather or winter, store cushions indoors and use breathable covers for the frame to prevent moisture buildup.
- Reconfiguration: take your time, pretend you’re conducting a small orchestra of cushions and arms—your future self will thank you for the smooth rearrangements during summer weekends.
