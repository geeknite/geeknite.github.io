## About OverPower and the Fleer era
OverPower is a collectible card game from the mid-1990s that tried something bold: replicate the chaos of superhero battles in a way that felt like a board game, a trading card game, and a character deck all collapsed into one. Fleer picked up the license and delivered a set of card groups that could feel like you were assembling a dream team with the solemnity of a chess grandmaster and the swagger of a guy who collects tiny plastic plates with superheroes on them.

The Fleer Star of the show was not just the art; it was the concept. The cards often centered around “Heroes” and the Loser-hero Mission cards that dictated sometimes-chaotic objectives. The “Hero Mission” subset was a clever way to add narrative to a box of otherwise static power cards: you had to complete specific tasks to earn advantages, sometimes forcing you to collaborate with or thwart other players. If you’re a kid from the 1990s who traded for a ‘rare’ Spider-Man card, you know the feeling: it wasn’t just about damage numbers; it was about bragging rights and the chance to role-play your favorite web-slinger in a kitchen-table adventure.

For those who remember or are curious about the aesthetic, here’s a snapshot: border-heavy art, bold fonts, punchy color palettes, and the unmistakable whiff of “made-before-everything-online” charm. The line between game mechanic and pop-culture collage blurred in a way that makes you nostalgic for the chaos of trying to sort out your mission cards when your little brother kept shuffling the same deck for an entire afternoon.

## Condition, value, and care tips
One of the trickier aspects of any vintage card set is sorting out condition. The cards in this lot show typical signs of age: some edge wear, mild surface scratches, and a certain dryness you can only attribute to decades of indoor humidity management by collectors who loved their humidity-controlled closets more than their parents did. If you’re aiming for a pristine display, you’ll likely separate these into a graded group and another for casual play. If you’re a player who wants to enjoy the nostalgia while also feeling like you’re cheating at the game just a little bit, you can absolutely sleeve them and run a handful in a casual match.

In terms of value, you’re looking at a price spectrum driven by condition, complete sets versus partials, and the era’s overall market demand. Modern collectors who grew up with this game might shuffle these into their nostalgia stacks and be willing to pay a little premium for a cohesive batch rather than single cards. A typical 90s-era card without ultra-rare status might fetch a few dollars, especially if you present the lot as a coordinated set rather than a grab-bag of random pieces. If you’re bundling with other Fleer OverPower items or with a few relevant Marvel cards from the same period, you can craft a more compelling package for buyers who are chasing the feeling of that decade.

Storage tips: keep them away from direct sunlight, humidity swings, and hot coffee rings. Use card sleeves with acid-free sleeves for storage; consider a small binder with dividers for organization. If you’re goal is display, a glass-front cabinet that doesn’t sit in direct sun is your friend. The goal is to preserve the bright colors while minimizing the risk of fox-like curiosity from your pets knocking the cards around.
