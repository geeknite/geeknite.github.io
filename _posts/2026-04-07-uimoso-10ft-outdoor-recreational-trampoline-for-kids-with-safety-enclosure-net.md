The process is well documented, but here are some extra tips to make the experience smoother.

- Pre-sorting bolts and washers saves precious minutes that you could spend teaching your kids to count springs
- Use a helper to keep the frame square; a skewed frame is a tragedy for jumpers
- Conduct a light safety check before stepping on: inspect the bolts, the springs, and the zipper mechanism

### Assembly tips in detail
- Align the frame segments with the feet placed firmly on the ground; if you misalign, you’ll finish with a wobble that makes the trampoline feel like a ship in a storm
- Attach spring hooks in an opposite pairing pattern to maintain balanced tension
- Ensure padding rails hide springs completely to avoid finger pinches

## Maintenance and Longevity
Maintenance is not glamorous, but it is the dry humor that keeps your backyard gadget alive. Regular maintenance is the difference between a trampoline that lasts one season and one that becomes a family heirloom.

- Inspect springs for fatigue; replace any stretched springs promptly
- Check net zippers for functionality and repair as needed
- Keep the area around the trampoline clear of debris and toys that could cause tripping
- Clean the surface gently with mild soap and water; avoid abrasive cleaners
- Store or cover during long periods of non-use to preserve materials
