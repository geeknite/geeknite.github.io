## Durability and Care: Keeping the Green Velvet Fresh

Life with rugs means dealing with spills, pet hair, and the occasional muddy footprint after an outdoor stroll. The good news is that microfiber, when treated with a touch of respect, can hold up well. Here are practical care tips to keep your emerald circle looking crisp for years to come:

- Vacuum regularly on a low setting to prevent the pile from flattening and to pick up dust that loves to settle in. A quick daily pass is enough to maintain the plush feel without turning your living room into a science experiment.
- Blot, don’t rub, any spills immediately. Use a clean cloth to blot the area and then apply a mild detergent solution if needed. Rubbing can push the liquid deeper and create a stubborn stain that thinks it owns your rug.
- For tougher stains, consult the rug’s care label. If the label says machine washing is OK, follow the manufacturer’s instructions. If not, stick to spot cleaning and hand washing with gentle detergents. Do not use bleach or harsh solvents that can damage the fibers and alter color.
- Drying matters. Let the rug air dry completely before placing it back on the floor. Avoid direct heat sources like radiators or hairdryers that can distort the pile and shrink the fibers. A gentle fluff with your fingers once dry can help restore the loft.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear. This is especially helpful in high-traffic zones or under a coffee table where foot traffic is unpredictable and the cat has declared that spot to be the “ninja lair.”
- Periodically check for shedding. A small amount of loose fibers is normal for new shaggy rugs, but if you notice a lot of fiber coming loose after washing, consult the seller’s care guidelines. It is not the rug’s attempt to escape; it is just shedding being dramatic.
