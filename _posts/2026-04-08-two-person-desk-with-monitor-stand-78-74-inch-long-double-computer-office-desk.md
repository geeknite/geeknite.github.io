## Space, Ergonomics, and the Two-Person Equation
Two people, one surface, and a couple of chairs that may or may not be aligned. The 78.74-inch length gives each user roughly 32-34 inches of personal desk depth, assuming equal partitioning. That’s enough to fit a monitor, a keyboard, a mouse, and a handful of desk gadgets while leaving a margin for accidental elbows to collide during intense spreadsheet battles.

## Assembly Experience: A Shared Adventure
If you’re the patient type and your partner is a fan of assembling IKEA furniture while humming a catchy tune about “one more screw,” you’ll find this desk to be a mixed bag. The assembly is straightforward for a single person to handle if the pieces are clearly labeled and you have a basic toolkit. For a two-person arrangement, the process tends to be faster and more enjoyable—until one person insists on flipping a panel the wrong way because it “feels right.” The manual typically includes clear diagrams, some exploded views, and a small amount of humor that reminds you that someone at the factory also probably spilled coffee on the instruction sheet.

A typical assembly timeline might look like this:
- Unpack and inventory all parts.
- Attach legs or rails to the main top.
- Install the monitor stand and cable channels.
- Guide cables through the channels and connect power.
- Tighten the final screws and do a light wobble test.
- Place the desk in your designated space and adjust as needed.

We recommend two people for the final placement and a measurement check to ensure both sides have equal space. If you’re working in a tight corner, it might require some strategic moves and a few test seating positions to guarantee both occupants have comfortable access to their primary workspace.

A pro-tip: Have a spare flashlight or your phone’s flashlight ready to peer into the cable channels. Some of the best desk surprises happen in those dark, aluminum-scuffed corners where you realize that the “hidden” screw felt suspiciously heavy. We also suggest laying out all cables first on the floor to see how they’ll route under and behind the desk. This helps avoid a last-minute epic tangle that could otherwise derail your entire afternoon.
