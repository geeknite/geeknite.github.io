## How to play: core mechanics in digital form
### Turn structure and flow
Your hand starts with five cards. Each turn you may play up to three cards and then draw back up to five. The deck comprises three broad families:
- Money cards that you can stash to pay rents
- Property cards that form your sets
- Action cards that can force payments, steal sets, or cancel other actions
The wind-up is straightforward: accumulate property sets, manage your cash, and deploy powerful action cards to devour your opponents’ chances while keeping your own balance under control. The digital version tracks everything so you can focus on the theatrical aspects—the clever plays, the feigned calm, and the inevitable moment when you realize you just handed someone a full property set after they drew the perfect Rent scenario.

### Cards, effects, and interactions
Action cards are the heart of the strategic layer. Deal Breaker can snatch a complete property set, Just Say No blocks a counter, and Sly Deal allows you to steal a single property from an opponent. Because this game is built on negotiation as much as it is on luck, the timing of these cards matters as much as the cards themselves. The digital version preserves these interactions while exposing clearer counters, cost estimates, and the board state so you won’t have to rely on memory alone.

### AI behavior and player interaction
The AI offers several difficulty levels ranging from buddy-friendly to “I brought a calculator and I am not afraid to use it.” At lower difficulties, the AI is forgiving and occasionally predictable, which makes it a good practice partner. On higher difficulties, you’ll encounter more rigorous planning, more counterplay to your strategies, and a delightful sense of inevitability as you watch a rival build a ruthless set on the turn after you think you’ve got this in the bag.

## Tips, tricks, and advanced play
- Early acquisition of at least one complete color set is valuable; it creates a safe target and secures the potential for aggressive rent plays later. 
- Use money cards to manage debt pressure; you need cash to pay the rent demands, but keep some cash aside for a possible late-game twist.
- Time your Deal Breakers carefully; a well-placed steal of a key property can swing momentum, but you will usually want to back it up with additional protection or debt management.
- Don’t neglect the Just Say No cards; their counterplay can shift the entire rhythm of the round. If you time it right, you can turn a losing position into a dramatic steal.
- Practice with AI to learn the rhythm before stepping into online matches; rhythm is everything in Monopoly Deal, and the digital format helps you internalize it faster than shuffling a deck by hand.
