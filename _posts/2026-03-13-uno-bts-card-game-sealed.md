## Gameplay overview
UNO is a simple game with a deceptively sharp learning curve: the simpler it looks, the more you realize you’ve underestimated the social tactics involved. The BTS edition sticks to core UNO rules with a few twists designed to lean into the adaptation without collapsing under its own fad-chasing ambition.

### Setup
- Shuffle the BTS UNO deck and deal seven cards to each player. Players should not reveal their cards—unless you’re trying to start a trust fall exercise with your friends, in which case go ahead and reveal and see who truly loves you.
- The top card of the draw pile becomes the discard pile and starts the action. If it’s a BTS-themed action card, the rules say you handle it as if it were a standard action card but with extra flair. You’ll understand when it happens, and you’ll grin as you mime a sudden BTS pose while saying, “This is my mode.”

### Core mechanics
The core UNO mechanics stay intact: match color or number, play action cards, and yell UNO when you’re down to one card. The BTS-tinted action cards add moments of drama, such as:
- A “Dance Break” card that forces everyone to perform a quick knee-bop before resuming play (optional, but encouraged for the performance anxious among us).
- A BTS “Encore” card that can reverse the direction of play, because nothing shouts comeback more than an abrupt guitar riff from the other side of the table.
- A “Mic Drop” card that makes the next player draw extra cards and mock-whisper a chorus of confidence under their breath. It’s silly, slightly petty, and perfectly within UNO’s spirit of friendly sabotage.

The gameplay remains fast-paced and accessible. There’s a gentle learning curve if you’re new to UNO, but even veteran players will find a few fresh wrinkles. The BTS-themed add-ons are not so intrusive that they break the rhythm; instead, they punctuate the game with shiny, giggly interruptions that feel earned rather than gimmicky.
