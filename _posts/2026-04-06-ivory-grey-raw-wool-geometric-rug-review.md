## Care and Maintenance for Wool Rugs

### Routine Cleaning Pro Tips

Vacuum regularly with a low suction setting to avoid rough handling of the fibers. Use a handheld attachment for edges to prevent dust from creeping into corners. For light stains, blot with a clean, white cloth and a wool safe solution; never rub the fibers aggressively as this can distort the weave. Wool love gentle care and patience, not turbo sprays that leave the rug shouting with chemical aftertaste.

### Pet and Kid Friendly Living

If you share your space with furry friends or energetic youngsters, wool holds up well with routine care. The natural resilience helps recover from foot traffic while the texture renders a forgiving surface for occasional spills. For serious accidents, treat quickly with water mixed with a small amount of wool safe detergent, then blot and allow to air dry. If color bleed or stubborn stain appears, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in wool fabrics; the extra cost is a prudent investment for longevity.

### Deep Cleaning and Refreshing

Every couple of years a gentle deep clean or professional refresh can rejuvenate the fibers, especially if the rug sits under heavy use or in sunny rooms where sun exposure can pale the color slightly. Deep cleaning should be done by a cleaner who understands wool and hand woven textures; the goal is to refresh without warping the weave or pulling threads loose. A good cleaner can revive texture while preserving the natural beauty that makes the rug unique.
