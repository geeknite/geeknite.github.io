## Durability and maintenance: should you worry about the inevitable coffee spill?
Let’s get real about the maintenance myth: plush rugs can show footprints and vacuum marks, but those are not design flaws; they can be a feature of lived-in chic if you lean into them. The Leilani’s surface fibers are resilient enough for moderate foot traffic and everyday lounging. For vacuuming, a gentle, brushless head is recommended to avoid fuzzing the pile. Spills should be blotted gently with a clean cloth and a mild detergent solution. Avoid vigorous rubbing; we’re not trying to cosplay as a 90s carpet cleaner in a slapstick sketch. If you have kids or pets, you’ll notice that the Diamond Ash color helps mask minor smudges where a plain gray might reveal every crumb, and that is a win in most households.

Long-term maintenance tips:
- Vacuum weekly on a gentle setting to preserve the plush height.
- Rotate the rug every few months to encourage even wear; yes, even rugs have fashion cycles.
- Blot spills immediately and blot again; avoid rubbing to prevent pile distortion.
- Use a rug pad for stability, improved acoustics on hard floors, and a touch of anti-crinkle confidence.
- Consider a yearly fluffing or gentle shake to restore loft after heavy traffic seasons.
