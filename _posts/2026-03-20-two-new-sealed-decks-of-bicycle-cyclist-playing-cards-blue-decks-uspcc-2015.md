## Practical notes for buyers and collectors
- Condition check: With sealed decks, a gentle press on the seal and a careful look at the edges can reveal if the shrink wrap has shifted over time. A perfectly flat, undisturbed seal suggests a higher probability that the deck has not seen handling.
- Storage: Keep them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve the color and paper quality. Ultrasonic cleaners notwithstanding, we don’t recommend cleaning the tuck via any method that involves water or sunlight. A simple dry, soft cloth can help when you’re dusting the shelf.
- Display vs. use: If you plan to display, consider a small glass case that keeps the tucks pristine while letting you appreciate the blue hue and back pattern. If you plan to use them for tricks, ensure you have a spare modern deck handy for practice. The last thing you want is a one-of-kind deck to confound a misdirect when you needed it most.
- Documentation: If you’re adding these to a collection database, record the year (2015), the colorway (blue), the manufacturer (USPCC), and the deck name (Cyclist). Notes about the two-deck pairing (two sealed decks) are also worth preserving for future provenance.
