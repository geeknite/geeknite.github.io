## How to play and what makes it different

The core UNO rules still apply: match by color or number, draw cards when you cant make a match, and attempt to be the first to shed all cards. The giant format introduces a few practical twists. You will notice the draw two and skip cards are larger, which makes them more dramatic and slightly more intimidating to lay down, admitting your strategic bravado with a little more theatre. The wild cards retain their flexibility, but expect some extra attention from players who are trying to read the BTS vibe from the cardboard. The game supports a broad audience, including kids who can read and adults who forgot what a UNO rule even looked like.

### Playing with the full 108 card bag

Having 108 cards offers a buffer that makes misplays feel less punishing in the early rounds.
