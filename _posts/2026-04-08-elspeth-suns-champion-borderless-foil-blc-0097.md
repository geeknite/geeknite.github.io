## Card Basics and History

Elspeth, Sun's Champion is a planeswalker that fans love to hate (in a good way) and then love again as you stabilize your board with her loyalty to the cause. This version—borderless foil—belongs to the Theros era's glorious cycle of relatively straightforward, aggressively supportive walkers. In card-nutshell terms, it's a four-mana walker with four starting loyalty whose abilities lean into a token-driven, go-wide strategy while offering a board-wipe option for midrange duels. The NM borderless foil variant doesn't alter the text; it only makes the card's aura more radiant, which matters when your opponent's eyes are scanning for every possible line of play and you want to throw off their counting of your threats.

Mechanically, Elspeth's suite is about tokens, board control, and the classic planeswalker ultimate that can tilt the game in your favor. Here is the general gist of the iconic flow, as many players experience it in practice:

- +1: Create a 1/1 white Soldier creature token. This is the bread-and-butter of the plan: you flood the board with bodies, which can trade in combat, pressure an opponent's life total, and enable synergy with AOEs or anthem effects. The token also scales nicely with equipment or aura buffs, turning a single creature into a small army with a single activation.
- -3: Destroy all other permanents. This is the dirty trick in Elspeth's arsenal. It is not always the perfect wipe, but when timed well, it can reset a stalled board, clear problematic blockers, or simply present a moment where your opponent's fragile line of defense collapses under the weight of your growing number of soldiers. In formats where you mindfully deploy your tokens, this ability can feel like your plan is finally allowed to breathe again.
- -7: You get an emblem with You control creatures get +2/+2. This ultimate is the emblem-rich payoff that makes Elspeth a long-term threat; once you reach it, your board presence becomes a menace that tends to snowball until your opponent can no longer answer the sheer volume of power you’ve accrued. The emblem is a long-term investment, so plan your route to it with patience and careful timing.

The borderless foil treatment doesn't change the rules; it changes the optics. The art is nudged to spread edge-to-edge, the foil catches the light in a way that makes your playmat feel like a stage, and the overall presentation signals to your rivals that you aren't here to tinker—you are here to win with style or at least to win with the best-looking token army on the table.

## Mechanics and Gameplay Overview

The most important part of any planeswalker card is how its abilities interact with your board state, your deckbuilding choices, and your opponent's plan. Elspeth, Sun's Champion is a classic example of a walker designed to enable a specific archetype: go-wide tokens with incremental value that can be amplified by buffs, anthem effects, and late-game emblem power. Here's a deeper dive into how the card can be leveraged in various formats and how the borderless foil variant enhances the experience without changing the core rules.

- Token generation. The +1 ability is the engine that fuels your tempo. In a typical game, you start with a few lands in play and a couple of early plays; as you add Soldier tokens, you can overwhelm an opponent who is trying to stabilize with blockers or removal. Tokens are flexible: they can trade with a bigger threat, absorb a sweeper if you anticipate one, or simply become a megaflank of power once you reach the emblem.
- Board wipe utility. The -3 ability becomes a strategic weapon when you are ahead or when you can assemble a board of tokens that will still survive the wipe due to their numbers or their buffs. It can catch an opponent on the back foot and is particularly valuable in the late midrange or mid-to-late game where the board becomes chaotic and your token swarm is the best path to parity.
- Emblem payoff. The -7 ultimate is the classic finish line. In the right deck, you can drop the emblem and immediately shift the game's momentum in your favor, even if the opponent responded to your board with a handful of removal spells. The emblem's buff to your creatures persists beyond the turn you played it, making each subsequent attack more threatening than the last.

From a gameplay perspective, Elspeth is not a one-trick pony; she is a toolkit with a plan. The borderless foil only elevates the enjoyment of playing with her, by giving you a card that feels as dynamic as the deck's theme. It invites you to spend a moment looking at the art, then focus on the battlefield and the math of your next attack vector. It also helps in games with audience: when you reveal the borderless foil to your friends, you will hear a chorus of oohs and aahs, and maybe some whispers about your deck's synergy that you then promptly ignore as you focus on winning.

## Value, Collectibility, and Market Considerations

From a collector's lens, borderless foils are premium items. They tend to hold or even increase in value when the card is in NM condition and the borderless finish is particularly eye-catching. BLC 0097 is described here as NM, a rating that implies crisp corners, no soft edges, no heavy whitening, and a surface that gleams rather than fogs under normal lighting. Foil cards have an added dimension of value because the foil can augment the art's color depth and the overall feel of the card. For players who enjoy premium finishes, borderless foils like Elspeth offer a tactile and visual experience that many standard foils do not match. For budget-minded players, the non-borderless versions with symmetrical frames and standard foils can be a better entry point, allowing you to focus on deck building rather than chasing rarity. In any case, a well-preserved borderless foil of Elspeth is a centerpiece for token-driven white weenie, soldiers-tribal builds, or any strategy that values resilience and incremental advantage.

One thing to remember: while the thrill of opening and owning a borderless foil is undeniable, the real measure of value is how well the card performs for your strategy. In casual play, Elspeth is a showstopper; in competitive settings, she can be a reliable engine that accelerates a midrange plan or a go-wide board party. The price, of course, will reflect market demand, condition, and the novelty factor of being a borderless foil. If your budget prohibits a NM borderless foil, consider a standard foil or non-foil borderless version to maintain the aesthetic while keeping costs sane. The joy of MTG is not just the card's text but the story of how your table uses it in the moment—a friendly reminder that in this game, joy and strategy can share the same space.

### Playstyle considerations and matchup notes

- Against aggressive decks: Elspeth's tokens can stall early pressure, and if you draw the -3 at the right time, you may wipe the board and reset back to parity. The borderless foil's presence contributes to the intimidation factor while you figure out the defensive plan.
- Against control: Token generation compresses the battlefield, increasing your threat density quickly. Drawing Elspeth in the mid-game often gives you a foothold that can lead to a clean win by attacking into their removal game with a higher count of threats.
- Against midrange: The emblem can close games quickly if you manage to protect the walker long enough and avoid overcommitting to avoid a mass removal spell. The foil finish, while not adding to the mechanical complexity, adds a psychological edge that can tilt a few pivotal turns in your favor.
