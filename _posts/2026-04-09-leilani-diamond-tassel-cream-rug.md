## Size, Placement, and Room Fit
Size matters, and this rug comes in a few options to suit different layouts. The size you pick should reflect the furniture footprint, the room’s proportions, and how much of the floor you want to reveal or cover. A common approach is to place a larger rug under a seating arrangement so that at least the front legs of sofas sit on the rug. The diamond pattern reads well on both larger fields and smaller ones, so you can maintain the intended visual rhythm across sizes.

In living rooms, a 8x10 or 9x12 option typically works well under a sofa set or a sectional with a coffee table. In bedrooms, a larger version can extend beyond the bed’s footprint, creating a soft buffer that blankets the floor when you slip out of bed in the morning. In dining rooms, consider the table and chairs’ footprint plus a bit of surround so you can pull out chairs without catching the tassels or edge of the rug.

A practical tip: always pair with a rug pad for longevity and comfort. A pad keeps the rug steady under foot traffic, reduces edge wear, and pads the living surface to prevent cold floors from creeping in. If you’re working with radiant floor heating or delicate floor finishes, the pad choice becomes even more important. Test a small section or consult with the retailer about pad compatibility for your floor type.

If you’re undecided on size, a quick rule of thumb is to leave at least 18 inches of rug visible beyond the sofa’s edge and about 12-14 inches around the room’s perimeter. That guideline helps create a clean frame for your furniture and keeps the rug from visually overpowering the space.

## Care and Longevity
The practical life of a rug is in its care regime as much as its aesthetics. The Leilani Diamond Tassel Cream Rug benefits from a routine that respects its fibers while embracing the hustle of daily life. Here’s a concise care plan:

- Vacuum regularly with a soft-bristle or gentle setting to avoid micro-pulling of fibers.
- Address spills promptly. Blot with a clean cloth to lift liquid before it soaks deeper. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution applied with a blotting motion can help—test on a small area first to avoid any unexpected color shifts.
- Rotate sporadically to ensure even exposure to sunlight and traffic patterns. This reduces the risk of uneven fading or wear on one side.
- Use a quality rug pad that suits your floor type. A pad improves resilience, reduces friction, and keeps the rug in place so it doesn’t wander into the realm of accidental yoga mats.
- For tassels, a gentle brush every now and then helps prevent tangling. If you notice a few stray fibers, a light trim with scissors can keep the fringe tidy without altering the rug’s silhouette.
