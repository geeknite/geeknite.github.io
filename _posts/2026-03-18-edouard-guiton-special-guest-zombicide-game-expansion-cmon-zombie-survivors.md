## The balance question: does Edouard disrupt the game balance?

Balance is the quiet, unglamorous hero of any expansion. In this case, the designers have walked a tightrope. Too many new mechanics, and you risk turning a co op into a puzzle box where the survivors feel like they are solving a riddle rather than fighting for their lives. Too few changes, and you may ask why the expansion exists beyond the celebrity cameo. The good news is that Edouard’s contributions are carefully capped: the new survivals have enough power to feel meaningful but not game-breaking, the new zombies provide escalating tension without the entire board becoming a snowball of exponential difficulty, and the campaign arc remains optional enough to allow players to switch on and off the narrative mode depending on how much drama they crave.

In playtesting in our lab, the expansion felt like a club with a guest of honor: the room is bigger, the conversation stays lively, but you can still hear the baseline rhythm if you lean in and listen. We noticed that some of the new mission cards reward positioning and timing, which increases the value of table talk and strategic planning. If your group tends to silo into two camps one of which never communicates due to pride or dice rage, this expansion may coax more collaboration, which is a net positive for the health of your table in the long run.


## Replayability and longevity: will you still care after the first few plays?

As with most good expansions, the answer hinges on how you approach the subject. If you treat the added content as a one-off novelty, you may be disappointed once the initial excitement wears off. If, however, you integrate the new campaign motifs into longer play sessions, the new survivors and event cards become a resource you can mine repeatedly for interesting table moments. The presence of a narrative thread means you can chain a few sessions into a mini-campaign that feels distinct from your standard one-shot Zombicide round. On the replayability front, we found that the expansion compels you to experiment with different survivor compositions, new mission types, and a slightly different pace to the action. The better you are at leveraging coordinated moves and using the new traits, the longer the novelty lasts.

There is a caveat worth noting: the more you lean into the campaign mode, the more you want to ensure your group respects the narrative beat. If you loathe story hooks and prefer pure tactical crunch, you can opt to run skirmishes with the new components without following the narrative arcs. The design supports both modes well, which is a credit to the authors for offering a flexible framework rather than forcing you into a single playstyle.
