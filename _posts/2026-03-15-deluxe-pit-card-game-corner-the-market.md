## How to play (the fast and furious version)

Pit is famous for moving at the speed of a caffeinated cheetah. ### Setup and feel

- The game plays 3–8 players, which means you can either invite your extended family or your entire D&D party and still feel like you’re in the same room as the chaos erupts.
- Shuffle the deck and deal each player a handful of commodity cards. The remaining cards create the market pool in the center. The bell sits nearby, waiting for its moment to claim glory.
- The objective is simple enough to grasp after one round: form a corner and cash in before the round ends when the bell rings. The exact scoring can vary by edition, but the sense of impending triumph is universal.

### Core mechanics
- Real-time trading: Everyone shouts, bargains, and counters in a flurry of friendly (and sometimes not-so-friendly) banter. The larger card stock in the Deluxe edition makes reads quicker and misreads rarer, which is exactly what you want when someone yells “Sugar!” across the table just after you’ve found a key trade.
- The bell: The bell is the table’s heartbeat. A well-timed ring signals the wind-down of a round and often triggers a glorious scramble as players scramble to finalize last-second trades. It’s a ritual, a celebration, and a reminder that you probably should have planned your corner a little sooner.
- Corner the Market twist: The strategic layer adds a directional goal. You’re not merely swapping cards; you’re curating a portfolio that demonstrates you understand supply chains, at least enough to bluff convincingly that you do.

### End of round and scoring

When the bell rings, players reveal their hands and argue convincingly about why their corner should count for more points. The scoring is quick and light, designed to get you into the next round without bogging you down in arithmetic. The end result is a sense of accomplishment, even for the player who didn’t quite assemble the perfect corner—though they’ll probably do better next round after they’ve learned the table’s rhythm.

## Strategy and tips for winners, or at least survivors

- Embrace efficient communication: The speed of Pit is built on crisp offers. Short, confident, and clear statements help keep everyone moving and reduce misreads.
- Read the table, not just the cards: People tend to chase certain commodities early on. If you spot a pattern, you can time your trades to disrupt their momentum while keeping your corner intact.
- Balance range and risk: Don’t hoard everything on one commodity. A balanced hand gives you flexibility and makes it harder for others to predict your next move.
- Manage your tempo with the bell: The bell is your friend when used strategically. Ring it when you’re ready to finalize a corner, not after you’ve given up your best cards in a panic.
- Build social momentum: Pit thrives on table talk. Witty commentary, playful jabs, and a shared sense of ridiculousness make each round more memorable and slightly more likely to end with you on top.
