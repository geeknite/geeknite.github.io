## Painting and assembly tips for the Adrian Smith survivors

If you are primarily in it for the paint, you’re in for a treat. The Adrian Smith line rewards careful layering and crisp edge work. Here are practical tips to get the most from these minis:

- Prime with a light gray or white to maximize contrast. The bold lines on Smith’s design translate well when you start with a clean base.
- Use a mid-tone for skin and hands, then build up highlights with thinner layers. Don’t be afraid to use a glaze to bring cohesion to the shadows.
- Armor and cape work benefits from edge highlighting. A two-step highlight on metal and leather will make the sculpts pop at tabletop distances.
- For the dramatic cloth and fabric pieces, a controlled dry brush or light feathering of a lighter color can mimic the painted texture seen in Smith’s works without obscuring details.
- Shield designs or iconography on the armor are prime candidates for freehand and decaling, if you enjoy a little extra narrative on the miniature’s surface.

If you’re new to painting, these minis can serve as a gentle introduction to more ambitious techniques. If you’re a veteran, think of them as a canvas that invites you to push your lighting schemes and color theory into more theatrical territory.
