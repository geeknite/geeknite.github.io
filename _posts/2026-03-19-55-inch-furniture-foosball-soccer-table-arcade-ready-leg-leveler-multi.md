## Assembly Guide: A Tale of Screws and Victory
If there’s one thing that can dampen the mood after a long workday, it’s a DIY assembly with a hex wrench and a set of tiny screws that vanish into a black hole. But fear not: this table’s assembly is doable with a bit of patience and a couple of blank stares at the manual for entertainment value.

A typical assembly flow looks like this:
- Unbox all components and inventory parts to ensure you have everything. The boxes can be heavy, so consider recruiting a helper with good problem-solving skills and a tolerance for instruction diagrams that look like a cipher.
- Attach the legs to the frame. The leg levelers become important here, so have them aligned before you anchor anything to the floor.
- Install the playing rods and the foosmen. If you’ve assembled other tables, you’ll recognize the rhythm: slide on the handles, attach the end caps, and verify that the players can pivot smoothly without friction.
- Mount the play surface and secure the ball returns. A few malleable adjustments to the screws will guarantee a level playfield, so this step is crucial for fairness on the board.
- Do a quick test run: one or two practice games to ensure the ball travels correctly and that the scorers click back into place when you reset the score.

The process can take a few hours if you’re meticulously aligning every component, but it’s also a solid afternoon project for a weekend warrior with a sense of humor and a sturdy beer fridge nearby.
