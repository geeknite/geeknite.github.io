## What is Throw Throw Burrito

Throw Throw Burrito is a hybrid party game that sits somewhere between a card game and a dodgeball tournament with soft, plush projectiles. Players collect sets of matching cards to score points, while the game mechanics force players into physical interactions with two handheld plush burritos that are literally thrown at each other. The goal is to be the first to accumulate a certain number of points from sets while avoiding getting burritoed by your friends. The Complete Set keeps the DNA of the original experience but adds more of what fans already adore: bigger chaos, more opportunities to scream yes, and a few new quirks that will have your group debating which burrito tactic is the most cinematic.

For those who enjoy the brand’s signature humor, Throw Throw Burrito leans into goofy visuals, silly callouts, and a sense that you’re playing with your friends’ competitive spirit rather than simply playing a game against them. It’s goofy in the best way—unapologetically chaotic, but with enough structure to keep the game moving rather than devolving into a free-for-all where no one knows what’s happening. The Complete Set wires that chaos into a bigger package with more cards and more fluffy projectiles, which, yes, means more opportunities for dramatic flourishes and slightly questionable table manners.

## Setup and Gameplay

### Setup

Setting up Throw Throw Burrito is less about precise measurement and more about ensuring there’s enough room on the table for two burritos and a growing deck of cards. In the Complete Set, you’ll typically lay out the deck, shuffle, and place it within reach of all players. Each player gets a starter hand (the exact number varies by edition and house rules, but it’s usually a handful of cards). The plush burritos are placed in easy reach so you can rotate throwing duty as needed without a sprint across a kitchen floor. Pro tip: designate a thrower and a dodger, or just let chaos reign and figure it out in the moment. The point is: the setup is fast, the vibe is loud, and you’re encouraged to embrace the ridiculous energy from turn one.

### Turn structure

A single turn in Throw Throw Burrito generally consists of drawing a card and analyzing whether you want to play a set or an action card that can affect others. The exact mechanics can vary a bit between base game and complete set packages, but the spirit is consistent: collect matching cards to create sets, and leverage cards that force others to draw more cards, distract, or trigger a burrito dodge. When a pair of matching cards is collected, you reveal them and score points. The more sets you complete, the closer you are to victory—unless someone nails you with a burrito or triggers a rule that suddenly shifts the endgame into dramatic overdrive.

### Cards and actions

The card mix is where the strategy lives. Expect a blend of set collection options and action cards that encourage you to bluff, swap cards, or redirect the chaos. The Complete Set amplifies this by adding more of each type of card, which means you’ll see more turn-to-turn decision points, more table talk, and more possibilities for accidental collusion that disappears in the heat of the moment. You’ll encounter cards that urge you to take a risk, to call a bluff, or to call out the player who is most due for a burrito delivery. It’s a party game in which you’re constantly balancing the urge to win with the urge to witness a spectacular fail—because sometimes the best moments come when someone misreads a card and triggers a table-wide giggle fit.

### Scoring and endgame

Scoring in Throw Throw Burrito revolves around collecting sets and managing risk. You’ll accumulate points for completed sets, and there’s usually a wall of noise from the rest of the table as players shout counts, scores, and sarcastic you got burritoed remarks. The Complete Set tends to extend the endgame by adding more scoring opportunities, which means you can stretch a night into a second round where the same players end up delivering the same jokes in a slightly different order. The result is a memorable, high-energy finish that leaves everyone winded but delighted, like finishing a workout while laughing at yourself for trying something you clearly aren’t designed for—like being calm during a burrito barrage.

If you’re chasing elegance in a tabletop experience, this is likely not your lane. If you want a night that ends with everyone clutching their sides from laughter, Throw Throw Burrito delivers in saturated color and ridiculous energy.

## Strategy and tips

- Communicate and coordinate with teammates: While you’re not exactly playing a co-op, light coordination can reduce the chaotic blowback and save you from the dreaded burrito ambush. A quick glance or a whispered plan can help your team score more than pure luck.
- Watch the discard rhythm: Some decks lean on building momentum through card recycling. Pay attention to what cards tend to cycle back and plan around that knowledge. It’s not about memorizing every card, but recognizing patterns that let you anticipate what might come next.
- Bluff only when the table is paying attention: The action cards can drive the crowd into a frenzy, but the real leverage comes from reading the table’s mood. Bluffing at the right moment can turn a near-collapse into a victory lap, but do it when the group isn’t mid-chaos meltdown.
- Use the Complete Set to your advantage: With more cards and more burritos, you get more chances to swing a game in your direction. But more also means more opportunities for spectacular failures. Embrace the risk and celebrate the moments when the table erupts in laughter.
- Safety first, always: Soft projectiles reduce the risk of real injuries, but a burrito tossed with real enthusiasm can still sting. Establish a few light-ground rules with your group before you begin so everyone can enjoy the spectacle without hurting anyone or any furniture.
