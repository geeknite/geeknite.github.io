## Buying tips and how to verify buyers' sanity (and your own)
If you’re considering pulling the trigger on a sealed NTSC China edition, here are practical steps to reduce risk:

- Confirm the edition’s legitimacy by cross-checking the box art against known images from reputable sources. If your eyes tell you something looks off, trust that vibe and walk away. Better safe, et cetera.
- Look for obvious signs of tampering with the seal. A pristine seal is a key part of value; if it shows signs of being pried or resealed, you might still enjoy the game, but you’re not getting the “sealed” aura a collector craves.
- Verify the content language flags. If you’re not comfortable with Simplified Chinese, confirm that the in-game subtitles default to English or that you can toggle easily. This reduces the risk of disappointment during the first loading screen.
- Check seller reputation and return policies. Sealed items are vulnerable to misrepresentation, especially in cross-border sales. Good sellers will provide clear photos and a transparent return policy.
- Compare prices with standard NTSC-U/C sealed copies. If the China edition is dramatically more expensive, you should be prepared for the extra shine on the display shelf rather than the actual gameplay experience.
