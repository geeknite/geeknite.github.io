## How to Play at a Glance: Rules, Flow, and Timing

If you know the standard Exploding Kittens rules, this edition is a gentle remix rather than a complete rewrite. The objective remains simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten while using your arsenal of cards to manipulate the deck and your opponents. The NSFW edition spices up the interactions with new cards and humor-forward effects, but the core cadence remains the same:
- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a hand, and place the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck. Each player starts with a Defuse card or two (depending on your house rules).
- Turn order: On your turn you can play one or more cards from your hand to skip, peek, or sabotage. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse, you’re out for the round.
- Endgame: The last player standing with a non-exploded card pile wins. The game is short, punchy, and designed for repeated rounds when the party is warmed up.

What makes the NSFW edition interesting is how the new cards alter decisions. You’ll often weigh the risk of pulling a more adult-themed card against the immediate payoff of a powerful defuse play. The humor can change your risk calculus in the moment — a delightful feature if your group thrives on quick-witted banter and bold moves.

### Teaching the rules without dampening the vibe
Teaching Exploding Kittens is usually a five-minute affair, and the NSFW edition is no exception. The challenge lies in setting boundaries with new players who may be more sensitive to explicit humor. A few tips:

This approach helps, because the NSFW vibe should amplify social bonding, not trigger awkward silences. When the group is aligned on boundaries, the game becomes a rapid-fire example of social deduction plus silly chaos — a potent if slightly reckless combination.
