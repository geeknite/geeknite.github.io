## The 25K Page Claim: Myth or Magic?
One of the most alluring numbers in printer marketing is the page yield. Fuji Xerox claims up to 25,000 pages from this yellow cartridge. Translation? If you print the occasional yellow highlight, charts, and safety symbols at a modest 5% page coverage, you might reach the 25K mark. If you’re planning full-page yellow flyers or slides with constant neon accents, the yield will sag faster than your hopes for a quick Friday upgrade.

What does 25K at 5% mean in practice? It’s a standard testing convention that assumes light usage: a page with mostly white space and a small splash of yellow on headers or charts. Real-world performance depends on printer model, print density, image complexity, and how often you run heavy color blocks. In short, the 25K claim is a promising baseline, not a guarantee for show-stopping neon posters in every circumstance.

### How page yield is measured
To keep things honest (as honest as toner can be), manufacturers follow standardized test pages. Those pages feature a small percentage of color coverage to determine how many pages a cartridge can print before print quality degrades beyond an acceptable threshold. That means if your office prints high-coverage color spreadsheets or dense infographics with big yellow blocks, you’ll dip below that 25K sooner than you’d expect. Still, for everyday office use with light yellow emphasis, it’s a solid benchmark.

## Maintenance and longevity
No cartridge lasts forever, but you can extend the useable life with sensible care. Here are a few practices we recommend:

- Store cartridges in a cool, dry place to prevent toner clumping. A closet that’s too humid is the enemy of long-run performance.
- Keep the printer clean around the toner path. A dusty transport path can cause uneven prints and minor banding.
- If you notice faint bands across pages, run a color calibration page and consider swapping the cartridge if the performance dips significantly.
- Avoid letting the printer sit idle with a full cartridge for extended periods. Regular prints keep the toner evenly distributed and ensure consistent results when you need them.
