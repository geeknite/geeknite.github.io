### The Core Structure: Legends, Powers, and the City as a Character
City of Mist emphasizes that legends inhabit people, not the other way around. The Guide starts with a reminder: your players aren’t just fighting villains; they’re wrestling with identities that are more significant than their stats. The core structure—mythic legends, street-level grit, and a city that breathes—remains intact, but the Guide enhances this with concrete ways to weave myth into daily life.

- Legends as identities: The Guide provides frameworks for how a Legend manifests in a player’s life. It’s not just a “superpower” sheet; it’s a story engine that helps players explore who they are in relation to a larger myth.
- Powers that feel personal: Instead of listing generic abilities, the Guide offers power templates that tie to legends and to concrete city environments. A power might be “shadow-court influence” or “a public interference that only the Legend can admit,” which can be teased out in a scene with social or investigative angles.
- The city as a living stage: The Guide gives you tools to treat the city as a character with secrets, factions, and environments that respond to the players’ choices. It’s not a sandbox; it’s a stage with hidden doors and prop goods that encourage player-driven storytelling.

### GM Tools and Templates: Scenes, Encounters, and Pacing
A great GM doesn’t just know the story; they know when to tell it and how to hand players the reins at the right moment. The Storyteller's Guide offers a suite of practical tools:

- Scene templates: Quick-start templates that get you into a scene without a long intro monologue. These are especially useful when you’re running multiple sessions in a city that’s always in motion.
- Encounters with purpose: Each encounter template includes a narrative hook, a likely Legend involvement, and possible consequences. They’re designed to feel organic, not forced, so you’re never pulling a “random monster” out of a hat.
- Pacing guidelines: The Guide provides pacing cues that help you escalate tension, drop information at the moment players are most ready to process it, and then pivot when a thread leads somewhere unexpected.

The templates don’t assume a single playstyle. Whether your table vibes with noir-detective energy, mythic espionage, or street-level superheroics, the Guide has a tool that can be repurposed to fit your tone.

### Campaign Seeds: Quick-start Journeys for Your City
If you’re the type who loves starting a campaign with a one-page concept, you’ll devour the campaign seeds in the Guide. Each seed is designed to be dropped into a city in under an hour of prep. ### Mechanics and the Way It Feels to Play
City of Mist’s mechanical core is more about narrative leverage than brute-force numbers. The Storyteller's Guide leans into this by providing punchy mechanics that reinforce story outcomes rather than derail them with arcane rules.

- Narrative economy: The Guide explains how to allocate “narrative capital” to players across scenes. It’s a simple concept: you reward story beats that advance mythic goals, which keeps players motivated and invested.
- Consequences that matter: The Guide emphasizes meaningful consequences. When a Legend’s plan fails or succeeds, the city shifts in noticeable ways, forcing players to contend with a changed landscape in future scenes.
- Safeguards for tone and consent: Because City of Mist explores potent mythic themes, the Guide includes guidance on maintaining tone and ensuring player comfort. It’s not just about scary moments; it’s about storytelling that respects boundaries and encourages collaborative creativity.
