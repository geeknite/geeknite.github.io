## How to play: quick rules and flow

This is where Sushi Roll earns its stripes: it captures the tempo of a fast, friendly game night without devolving into a chaos of rules. The core loop is simple enough for a novice to pick up in a single sitting, yet there are enough micro- decisions to keep veterans engaged as the rounds roll by.

### Turn structure (high-level)
1) Roll the dice pool for the round.
2) Each player selects one die (or a small kept set, depending on variant) and passes the rest to the left.
3) When all dice have been picked, players tally the results against the round’s target combinations.
4) Points are tabulated on the score pad; the player with the most points after a predetermined number of rounds is the connoisseur of sushi and winner of the afternoon.

The “draft and pass” mechanic is the heartbeat of the game. It creates tension as you decide whether to commit to a safe combo early or gamble on a higher-value combination that might be blocked by a neighbor. That tension is the delicious grease that keeps the game lubricated and entertaining.

### Scoring and endgame

Scores accrue by putting together the right sushi combos across rounds. Some combos reward you for collecting all flavors of a particular sushi type, while others rack up points when you assemble a variety of items in a single hand. The endgame typically comes when a fixed number of rounds has been reached, and the final tally is a quick arithmetic sprint that will either leave you on a small victory shuffle or licking your fingers in anticipation of the rematch.

If this is your first rodeo with Sushi Roll, you’ll be delighted at how forgiving the scoring structure feels.
