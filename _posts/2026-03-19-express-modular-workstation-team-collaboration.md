## Build and Design
### Materials and Craftsmanship
Let’s start with the materials. The Express Modular Workstation uses a steel frame with powder-coated finishes and composite top surfaces that resist coffee spills long enough for you to pretend you’re not the reason the team sprinted into a caffeine crisis. It feels premium without tipping into pretentious, and the matte textures do a decent job of hiding fingerprints from people who pretend they don’t touch things, but insist on using the largest possible monitor on every surface.

### Assembly Experience
If you’re imagining a two-hour assembly ritual with hex keys that resemble medieval torture devices, you’ll be almost right. The good news is that Express includes a clean, color-coded hardware kit and a step-by-step illustrated guide. The slightly tricky part is aligning modular tops across a row of desks, which is less a DIY project and more a social exercise in teamwork. Pro-tip: assign a “stack captain” who handles layout alignment, while the rest of the crew focuses on cable routing. Expect a few “aha” moments, some triumphant high-fives, and maybe a minor swear word when you realize you’ve accidentally mounted a panel backward. It’s part of the experience, and in Geeknite terms, it’s basically the ritual dance before a boss-level product deployment.

### Aesthetics and Office Fit
There’s something delightfully Scandinavian about the Express system: clean lines, neutral tones, and a couple of bold color accents if you opt into the modular rails.
