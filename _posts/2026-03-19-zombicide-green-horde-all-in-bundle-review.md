## Gameplay and Mechanisms

Zombicide: Green Horde uses the classic core loop that fans of the series adore: move, shoot or hit, survive, loot, die, repeat. The All-In Bundle adds more knobs, levers, and shiny new tokens to twist, but the heart of the experience stays intact: a cooperative tactical skirmish in which you guide a band of survivors through waves of zombies that (in this edition) are green and likely to forget how to stand in a line.

### Core Rules, Core Fun
The basic rules revolve around simple, elegant dice rolls that determine outcomes for combat, stealth, and the moment you realize your plan is worth approximately the same as a soggy sandwich. The Green Horde introduces a few twists on standard Zombicide: different zombie behaviors, additional terrain interactions, and new character classes that help you mix up strategies. If you’ve played other Zombicide titles, you’ll recognize the rhythm: you plan, you move, you roll, you escalate, and somewhere in there you realize your survivors are just as likely to become heroic legends as casualties with exceptional dice luck.

Key rhythm beats:

- Activation: each survivor gets to do a little bit of everything per turn, but the order can matter, especially when you’re trying to corral a Z-formation that would make an orc CEO proud.
- Combat: you’ll often choose between ranged and melee; the greens are nimble, and your team may be carrying heavy equipment that requires careful usage. The variety of weapons and tools remains one of the strongest selling points, giving you multiple approaches to each encounter.
- Experience and progression: survivors level up, new abilities unlock, and the board becomes more dynamic with every round. It’s not just luck; it’s a dance, albeit a chaotic one where your dance partner is a zombie shouting “Grrr.”

### Mode Options and Replay Value

The All-In Bundle doesn’t hide the fact that Zombicide can get brutal. The Green Horde introduces tougher waves and more punishing zombie variants, which means you’ll be rewarded for careful planning rather than brute-force luck. The game isn’t unfairly punishing; it’s challenging in a way that makes each success feel earned. That said, the bundle includes so many variants that you can tailor sessions to your group’s skill level and patience for chaos.

- Difficulty curve: expect a learning cliff if you’re new to Zombicide, but the payoff is rapid as you begin to chain combos and create synergies across survivors.
- Replayability: with multiple expansion packs in the All-In Bundle, you’ll rarely hit the same scenario twice in a row. The variability of missions and enemy swarms keeps the game feeling fresh well beyond its first dozen plays.
- Solo and co-op balance: the game scales, though some of the most satisfying sessions come from co-op planning as a tight unit rather than solo heroics. If you’re a solo player, you’ll want to pick a few enemy variants and run through them to prove you can outsmart a zombie mob using only a coffee mug and a stubborn refusal to give up.
