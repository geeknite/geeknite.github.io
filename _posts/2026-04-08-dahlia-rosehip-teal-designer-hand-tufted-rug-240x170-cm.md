## Maintenance and Cleaning
### Routine care
Regular vacuuming is your best friend. Use a suction setting that is appropriate for the pile height to avoid pulling fibers or creating static drama. Rotate the rug periodically to promote even wear. If there are curious paw prints, treat them as a story line rather than an emergency plot twist. A quick weekly flip to the other side helps the backing to breathe and maintain shape.

### Spills and stains
Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper and create fiber damage. Mild soap and water can be used for most spills, but test in an inconspicuous spot first. For tougher stains, consult a professional cleaner with rug expertise. Quick action minimizes staining and keeps the teal color vibrant for longer. Remember that dye migration can occur with bright colors, so separate light and dark fabrics during cleaning sessions if you happen to be doing laundry nearby while the rug is on the floor.

### Protection tips
Place furniture coasters under heavy items to prevent indentations. Use a rug pad to reduce wear and to keep the rug flat. If you are worried about sun fade, use blinds or curtains during peak sun hours. Teal and rosehip hues can be sensitive to prolonged sun exposure, so consider placement where the rug receives indirect light for best color retention.
