## Assembly Tips and Common Pitfalls

- Sort hardware before starting; grouping screws, washers, and brackets saves time and reduces existential dread during the middle of assembly.
- Use a properly sized screwdriver; a bit with incorrect torque can strip the screws or dent the veneer edge.
- Don’t overtighten; the veneer is real wood and can crack if you crank it like a nutcracker.
- Align the panels carefully; the desk looks best when the joints are perfectly flush.

### Quick Start Checklist

- Clear the workspace and lay out all panels.
- Pair screws with the corresponding holes before attaching anything.
- Attach the frame first, then mount the top panel and side supports.
- Route cables through the back channel and drop the grommet in place last.

### Common Pitfalls

- Mixing up left/right panels during the initial stage leads to a wobble you can feel with a coffee cup.
- Overtightening screws can cause veneer cracking. Stop short of the feel of a door jamb and you’ll be fine.
- Skipping the level check will make your monitor look like it’s in witness protection. Use a simple spirit level and adjust feet as needed.
