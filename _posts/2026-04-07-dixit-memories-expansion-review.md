Dixit has always been the midnight snack of party games: whimsical, a little weird, and somehow still capable of starting a debate about whether a giant bowl of clouds is actually a metaphor for a midlife crisis. When the Dixit: Memories expansion from Asmodee (and brought to tables by ABU Games) landed in my living room, it came with two expectations: more gorgeous art and more opportunities to witness friends transform into overzealous storytellers. Was this expansion a mere deck of new dreams, or did it actually change the way we tell stories around a table? Buckle up, dear reader, because we’re about to unpack not just the cards, but the entire experience of memory, imagination, and occasional chaos that Dixit: Memories brings to the party.

## Introduction: What is Dixit: Memories?
Dixit: Memories is an expansion for the beloved Dixit line. If you’re new to the game, the core idea is simple: players use dreamlike illustrations on the cards to tell a story, and others must guess which card matches the storyteller’s clue. The more players you have, the merrier (and louder) it gets. Memories slides into the box as a deck of 84 brand-new cards with a distinct memory motif—images that feel like you found them in a dusty photo album or a surreal scrapbook. The theme leans into memory, nostalgia, and “remember when” vibes. It’s not a reprint; it’s a fresh infusion of imagery designed to spark new kinds of storytelling, not just new words to whisper at the table.

This expansion is a perfect example of how a well-curated art style can change the feel of a game without flipping the core rules on their heads. If you’ve played base Dixit or other expansions, you know what to expect: beautiful, sometimes bizarre art, a storyteller, and a jury of clever players trying to suss out which card matches the clue. Memories sits comfortably in that space while nudging the storytelling to lean a little more on personal memory and shared experience. The result is not just a game night; it’s a memory night with dice and a deck of constellations.

## What’s in the Box?
Dixit: Memories doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it refreshes the wheel’s hubcap with glittery, memory-laden imagery. Here’s what you get in the box:

- 84 new Dixit cards featuring the memories motif: family photos, dreamlike snapshots, and surreal scenes that tug on the emotional thread of “remember this.”
- A few small rule clarifications clarifying that these cards act like the existing Dixit deck; there’s no new turn structure or scoring mechanic introduced with this expansion. The emphasis remains on ambiguous clues and imaginative storytelling.
- A compact glue gun for the moment your brain tries to assemble a memory that isn’t there (okay, that’s not in the box, but you’ll probably feel it when someone nails a clue that references a long-forgotten inside joke).

What you won’t find is a new “mode” or a dramatically different way to play. Memories is designed to slot into your current Dixit setup with minimal fuss. If you’ve got a standard six-player game and a few hours to spare, you’re good to go. If you’re playing with fewer players, the standard Dixit rules still apply; the box doesn’t introduce a special solo mode. It’s a clean, art-forward enhancement rather than a new game system.

The quality of the art is where Memories shines. The illustrations mix nostalgia with surreal whimsy, producing images that feel personal and universal at the same time. It’s the kind of art that doesn’t just illustrate a clue; it becomes part of the clue itself. A card showing a chalk-drawn staircase dissolving into cloudlike feathers invites the storyteller to evoke memory and ascent, while another depicts a vintage photo frame oozing color into reality, pushing you to talk about remembered moments rather than literal objects. The more you lean into memory, the more the deck pays you back in surprising ways.

## Artwork and Theme: Memory as a Playground
If you’ve spent any time with Dixit, you know the art isn’t just decoration; it’s a gateway. The protagonist of Dixit is your gaze—how you interpret the arc of a story through the lens of a single image. Memories doubles down on this premise by curating imagery that feels like family albums, dream journals, and daydream collages rolled into a deck of cards.

Memories’ art often embraces architecture of memory: stairways that disappear into the sky, doors that flutter like pages, snapshots with silhouettes that resemble both the familiar and the uncanny. This isn’t about literal memory recall; it’s about the mental associations that tingle when a story is too close to home. The net effect is a game that invites players to mine their own memories and, in doing so, to create more intimate and unpredictable storytelling. If you’re the kind of player who loves sharing a ridiculous anecdote that somehow bridges the gap between a dream and a real-life event, this expansion will be your new best friend.

For players who adore thematic consistency, Memories offers a cohesive mood without forcing a specific interpretation. You won’t be handed a universal “meaning” to extract from every card; instead, you’ll be asked to take a moment to recollect and then fashion a clue that aligns with your memory or with a memory you want to evoke. The result is a shared storytelling experience that feels less like “guess the depiction” and more like “guess the memory behind the depiction.” It’s a subtle shift that makes the table a little louder about feelings and a little quieter about straight facts.

## Gameplay Experience: How it Feels at the Table
Dixit thrives on the tension between telling a clue that’s just specific enough for your fellow players to identify your card and just vague enough that a few others can still get it. Memories leans into that tension with a touch more emotional texture. The clues can feel more personal, and the memory angle often invites players to lean on inside jokes, shared experiences, or sentimental moments that only your group understands. If your circle loves storytelling, the Memories deck is going to be a treat.

The core loop remains the same: a storyteller selects a clue, players pick a card that best matches that clue, and everyone votes secretly on which card they think belongs to the storyteller. Scoring follows the standard Dixit method: if enough players guess the storyteller’s card, you score; if no one or everyone guesses, the storyteller gets punished. Memory’s advantage is that the new imagery tends to produce more “a-ha” moments rather than pure guesswork. Sometimes a card will click in the same moment for several players because it taps into a memory you all share; other times, a card will trip a solo memory that no one else fully understands, which can be a hilarious misdirection in the best way possible.

We also found that Memories plays nicely with the speed you expect from Dixit. The new cards aren’t overwhelmingly detailed in a way that slows down turn resolution; instead, they offer fresh angles for your clues. The pacing stays consistent with base Dixit, which means you won’t have to slow to a crawl to appreciate the new art. If you’re a fan of quick, witty storytelling rounds, Memories won’t drag your table down. If you’re the kind of group that enjoys extended, reflective storytelling sessions, you’ll feel right at home.

For players who want to scratch a slightly different itch, you can try a memory-themed variant: instead of giving a classic clue, storytellers can attempt a memory prompt—a short recollection that relates to the memory concept evoked by a card. This can lead to some of the most entertaining moments as players race to connect the memory to a specific card and to each other’s experience. It’s a subtle rules tweak rather than a new mode, but it can unlock deliciously chaotic storytelling fun.

## How It Compares to Base Dixit and Other Expansions
Base Dixit is a masterclass in “less is more.” The game provides a universal, adaptable framework with a deck of evocative art that invites players to interpret and narrate. Other expansions often add new cards with new vistas, some occasionally rebrand the experience with a slightly different feel. Memories sits in that tradition but leans more toward the human element—the memory, the nostalgia, the shared joke that instantly makes someone at the table laugh or groan.

Compared to earlier expansions, Memories tends to lean into a warmer, more familiar vibe while still delivering those punchy, surreal images that Dixit is known for. If you’ve tried the grand, starry-field vibes of other decks and found them slightly too abstract for your group’s sense of humor, the memory-centric art in Memories can feel like a more accessible bridge between the surreal and the sentimental. The balance between “how well does this card map to the clue?” and “how much memory does this trigger?” is where the expansion shines.

In terms of value, the 84-card deck is a sizeable addition. You’ll likely find at least a handful of cards that instantly slot into your recurring storytelling repertoire, and you’ll probably discover a few that are so evocative you’ll want to revisit them in future games merely to see how your group interprets them, six months later. The durability of the stock and the quality of printing are on par with the core game and other expansions, which means you’re paying for art and experience, not packaging gimmicks.

## Player Count, Table Time, and Group Dynamics
Dixit scales nicely to different group sizes, and Memories is no exception. The base game supports 3–6 players; the expansion cards work within that same window. In practice, you’ll probably notice a few things:

- With 3–4 players, the storytelling can become a little more intimate, making memory-based clues more pointed and personal. It can be delightful if your group enjoys the “inside-joke” vibe.
- With 5–6 players, the energy level spikes as more people weigh in on which memory they thought matched the clue. This is where the hilarity of misinterpretation shines and the room fills with laughter and playful accusations (the good kind, not the “you ruined the round” kind).
- The more you lean into memory prompts and shared experiences, the more the card pool feels cohesive with your table’s sense of humor. If your group tends to tell tall tales at game night, this expansion gives you more “fuel” for those stories.

One caveat: if your group is particularly sensitive about personal memories, be mindful with the prompts. Dixit has always thrived on ambiguity, but memory-based clues can occasionally touch on topics some players prefer not to discuss at the table. In our sessions, we found that preface like “this is just for fun” and a quick consent check helped keep the mood light and inclusive.

## Artwork as a Game Mechanic, Not an Ornament
A recurring theme here is that the art in Memories is not decorative wallpaper; it’s a participatory element. The cards invite interpretation, yes, but they also guide the storytelling arc. You’ll notice yourself leaning into a memory only because a card’s composition hints at a familiar place or moment. It’s hard to quantify, but the effect on the table is real: players feel seen in a playful way as they witness others interpret stories through the lens of their shared memories.

This dynamic also affects how you score. When a memory lands just right and multiple players pick the same card as the storyteller’s, the room realizes you’re in sync—proof that your memory bank is a collaborative tool rather than a private vault. That moment is the heart of Dixit: Memories. It’s not about “who’s the best storyteller” but about “how well did we weave a memory together.”

## Accessibility and Setup: How Easy Is It to Get Into?
If you’re already familiar with Dixit, Memories is a smooth upgrade. The setup remains simple: shuffle the new 84 cards into the existing deck, set out the board and tokens, and you’re ready to go. The card art is the showrunner here—visually striking and rich with metaphor—so you’ll notice players spending more time examining each image, which can lengthen a round slightly. If you’re working with a tight time budget, you can opt to simply mix the new cards into the discard pile and keep the gameplay tempo snappy.

The rulebook updates are minor and friendly: the mechanics are unchanged, and there are no new victory conditions to chase. If you’ve memorized the base rules, you’ll be able to run a Memories session in your sleep (or at least in the “we need a quick game” mode after a long day). The only caveat is to remind players that the memory-based prompts can be trickier for newer players who aren’t used to the full Dixit vibe. A short warm-up round helps everyone calibrate their clues and guessing energy.

## Final Thoughts and Recommendation
Dixit: Memories isn’t a radical departure from the Dixit formula; it’s a thoughtful expansion that leans into the human side of storytelling. The 84 new cards reframe the atmosphere, inviting players to mine their own memories and to weave them into a collaborative narrative. It’s not about tricking your friends with a clever clue; it’s about crafting experiences that feel personal, cozy, and gently surreal. If your gaming group already adores Dixit and you’re looking for a reason to gather again with a fresh cache of art and prompts, Memories provides that spark without forcing a new set of rules on your table.

The expanding library of Dixit imagery can also help keep your game nights inclusive and accessible to newcomers. The artwork is striking and evocative enough to invite engagement from players who might be quieter in more text-heavy games. The memory theme adds emotional resonance that can bring players closer, as long as everyone keeps the mood light and the jokes kind. In short: if you want more storytelling, more dreamlike visuals, and a touch of memory nostalgia that doesn’t derail the pace, Memories earns a spot on your shelf.

If you’re still on the fence, consider your gaming goals. If you crave rapid, high-energy social bluffing games, Dixit: Memories may feel a little slower than you want. If you crave collaborative storytelling that doubles as a shared memory archive, this is a perfect match. For most groups who already enjoy Dixit’s vibe, the Memories expansion stands as a strong candidate for your next game night rotation.
