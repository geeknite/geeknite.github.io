H2: What is Herd Mentality, and why does it feel so familiar?

H3: The premise in a nutshell
Herd Mentality is a party game about guessing what the majority of players will say to a prompt. The catch? The prompt can range from the hilariously ridiculous to the surprisingly philosophical. Everyone secretly writes down their own answer, then everyone reveals simultaneously. The catch (the strategic one, mind you) is that you earn points when your answer aligns with the majority—or the most unexpected minority, depending on the variant you’re playing. It’s a curious blend of social deduction and party-game absurdity, cooked up in a box that looks suspiciously like it was designed by someone who spends weekends optimizing group selfies for maximum chaos.

H2: How to play a round (the quick-and-dirty version)

H3: Setup
- Each player receives an answer sheet and a writing utensil (ideally something that doesn’t feel like a ceremonial wand, because the chaos needs to be portable).
- The prompt deck gets shuffled and placed within easy reach.
- The starting player role rotates clockwise, increasing the risk of a dramatic memory lapse about whose turn it is to write down something profound (or profoundly ridiculous).

H3: Round flow
1) The prompt is read aloud by the active player. The players then write down their own answers privately.
2) All answers are revealed simultaneously. You can use a reveal-as-a-team mechanic if you want to amplify the chaos, or the standard reveal-for-individuals approach if you’re feeling merciless.
3) Players vote on which answer they think will be the majority. The person whose guess matches the actual majority earns points. The more creative or surprising the majority, the more bragging rights you’ve earned—until the next round, when the bragging rights reset and everyone pretends they’re above it all.

H3: Scoring slices
- Matching the majority yields points; depending on the variant (classic vs. team mode), the exact point value can scale. It’s not rocket science, but it does reward social insight and a healthy tolerance for absurd conclusions.
- Secondary objectives, if you enable variant rules, can award extra points for “most creative” answers or “bold guess” plays. These optional paths give replay value without complicating the core loop.
