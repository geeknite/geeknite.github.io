## Performance and Maintenance

### Durability and Stain Resistance

Polypropylene fibers offer robust stain resistance, which makes this rug a practical pick for families, roommates, or tinkerers who treat their space like a test bench. It won’t repel every spill, but it can take the hits and bounce back with a clean, quick vacuum and a spot clean when needed. The colorway helps mask minor traffic patterns that appear over time, so your floor won’t look worn out after a few months of use by adventurous decor enthusiasts who do DIY projects in socks.

### Cleaning and Care Tips

- Vacuum regularly with a brushless attachment to avoid fiber damage.
- For spills, blot (don’t rub) with a clean cloth. Mild soap and water usually do the trick for everyday mishaps.
- For tougher stains, a gentle carpet cleaner designed for polypropylene fibers is advisable, tested on an inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if you have intense computer fan noise or direct sunlight in the room.

With these habits, SMT416M should maintain its color and texture for years—though your nerdy home aesthetic may evolve, the rug will still be there, quietly supporting your science-fair table dreams.

## Size Options and Room Fit

The Summit collection typically ships in a range of standard sizes. SMT416M Blue Creme is commonly available in dimensions like 4' x 6', 5' x 8', and 8' x 10'. Here’s how to map sizes to spaces, with a dash of geekyard wisdom:

- 4' x 6' — Perfect for entryways, small reading nooks, or under a coffee bar where a steely gaze meets your espresso machine. Good for smaller rooms or apartments where every inch counts.
- 5' x 8' — The workhorse. It works nicely in small living rooms or under a sofa with a generous distance to the TV. It also plays well as a landing pad for your VR headset when you forget where you placed your sensors.
- 8' x 10' — The cinematic rug. Ideal for larger living rooms, home offices, or gaming set-ups where you want the rug to hug the seating area and tie the room together without fighting the space.

If you’re unsure which size to pick, measure your seating arrangement and consider the rug’s footprint relative to your coffee table, sofa, or gaming chair. The rule of thumb is to allow at least 18–24 inches of rug visible around the perimeter under furniture, so the edges don’t look like they’re trying to sneak out of the room.
