## Overview

### What is this thing, really?
The Infinite 4 Person Cornerstation is a modular pod system designed for small offices or large bedrooms where your family collectively decided that “work” is a synonym for “constructor’s nightmare.” It aims to provide four individual workspaces with screens, privacy, and a dash of acoustic dampening. It’s essentially a high-tech cubicle enclosure that won’t make your hands smell like printer ink after a full day of typing. It’s big, it’s white, and it proudly declares that teamwork makes the dream work, especially when that dream involves fewer interruptions from the family dog seeking snacks at the exact moment you’re explaining your quarterly KPIs.

### Why a cornerstation, though?
Cornerstations solve a very modern problem: you’re balancing multiple tasks in a shared space with more adapters than a sci-fi traitor’s toolkit. A corner station uses the corner geometry to maximize desk length while minimizing the floor footprint. It also creates semi-private pods for each user—enough privacy to pretend you’re in your own universe, but not so much that you forget you’re in a house of people who will inevitably ask you to put the dishes in the sink. Spoiler: you’ll end up defending a cornerlike fortress against the onslaught of meetings, calls, and the occasional cat who insists that your keyboard is the best sunbeam in town.

### Materials and finish

### Screens and privacy features
Each work bay is designed to accommodate screens and personal gear with room to spare for a monitor stand and a mug that always slides toward the keyboard when you least expect it. ### Acoustic dampening and noise control
If you’ve ever tried to work next to a sewing machine or a neighbor’s karaoke night, you know that a little sound dampening goes a long way. This cornerstation uses panels and some clever geometry to reduce crowd noise, which helps when you’re on a sprint to finish a report or pretend you’re focusing while your brain cycles through the next meme idea. It won’t turn your room into a silent monastery, but it should help you fake it till you make it through that one Zoom call where you forgot to mute your mic while brewing coffee.
