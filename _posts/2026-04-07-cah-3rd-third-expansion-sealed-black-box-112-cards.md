## Humor, Sensitivity, and Responsible Gaming

### The collision between jokes and boundaries

CAH has always walked a line between hilarity and harm. The 3rd Third Expansion intensifies this dynamic because of its 112 fresh prompts, some of which push into territory that can be sensitive for players. The key here is consent, communication, and a shared understanding of what your group is comfortable with. The best practice is to establish a pre-game brief: what topics are off-limits? Are there any lines you refuse to cross this time around? Are we ready to laugh at ourselves while avoiding punching down?

### Tips for inclusive play

- Rotate the moderator and give the table a chance to veto prompts before a round begins.
- Use a simple consent framework: if someone isn’t comfortable with a card, skip it and move on.
- Consider a family-friendly or mild-night option if the group includes newcomers or younger players.
- Remember: humor often lands best when it surprises in a clever way, not when it hits a raw nerve.

### Is the humor worth it?

For many CAH players, the thrill comes from content that’s bold, self-deprecating, and a little scandalous. The 3rd Third Expansion delivers in that department, but it also respects the social contract of play. If your group wants to push boundaries with a clear safety net, this expansion can be part of a more chaotic but controlled night. If your group values gentler humor or is wary of edgy content, you can use the set sparingly, or pair it with a few higher-level prompts that lean into witty, non-offensive cleverness.
