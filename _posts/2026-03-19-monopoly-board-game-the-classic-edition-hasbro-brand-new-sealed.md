### House rules and optional twists

One of the strengths of monopoly is its versatility. The classic edition supports many variations that are not strictly official, but many groups will adopt them with the enthusiasm of a group therapy session. Here are a few house rules you might encounter or adopt:

- Auction all unowned properties. This can speed up the game and increase the chaos factor.
- The banker is the only person allowed to trade money with the bank. This reduces the drama of bankers who cloak their moves in business-speak.
- Mortgage in between turns. You can mortgage properties and collect rent only on your turn, not during other players turn. This makes the game more tense and strategic.
- Speed up the game with a forced negotiation timer. When a trade is proposed, it must be resolved in a set amount of time to prevent endless stalemates.
- The endgame rule where the last owner of a color group wins is a pure option. You could implement a neutral territory and define what counts as victory for the table.

### Strategy and tips for a classic edition session

- Focus on early liquidity and negotiation. In the early game, cash is king. You do not want to get stuck with nothing and have to mortgage your board to continue.
- Target color groups with popular auctions. It is often easier to get a color set if people are not paying attention and you can outmaneuver them with a well timed auction.
- Use the jail space as a strategic tool. Being stuck in jail in the midgame can be a protective position that prevents you from paying rent while assets accumulate on the board.
- Do not neglect the utilities. In the early game, they can earn a surprising amount of rent if built up with houses on the other properties.
