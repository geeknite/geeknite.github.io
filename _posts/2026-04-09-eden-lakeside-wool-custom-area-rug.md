## Custom Size vs 8x10: Pros and Cons

### Why choose a Custom Size?
- Perfect fit: If your room has odd dimensions, an alcove, or a measuring nightmare (think: odd corners that refuse to be right angles), a custom size is your best friend. You can dial in the exact length and width to maximize open floor space and align with furniture footprints.
- Proportional balance: Custom sizing helps you achieve the precise visual balance you’re after. Want the rug to sit flush under a coffee table and extend 6 inches beyond the sofa legs? You can do that.
- Accent zones: The right custom rug can define zones in an open-plan living space. It’s like giving each seating area its own micro-planet of cozy inertia.

### Why choose an 8x10?
- Most versatile standard: The 8x10 is a reliable, widely compatible dimension that covers many living rooms without feeling stingy or oversized. It’s the Goldilocks of rug sizes: not too big, not too small, just right for common furniture layouts.
- Quick turnaround: Standard sizes often ship faster than custom orders, which is critical if you’ve decided your living room needs a glow-up this weekend rather than next quarter.
- Price predictability: If your budget is laser-focused on optimizing other tech upgrades, the 8x10 route is more likely to align with mid-range pricing and fewer surprises at checkout.

### When to pick which
- Pick custom if: your room has unique geometry, you want to feature a particular furniture arrangement, or you’re chasing a perfect border-to-wall ratio that a standard rug simply cannot deliver.
- Pick 8x10 if: you’re chasing speed, you want a proven size that fits reliably under most standard sofas and coffee tables, or you’re testing how a larger rug will feel before committing to a full room-scale layout.

Note: Always measure your space, including door swing clearance and under-furniture footprint. A rug that’s too small can turn your room into a visual ping-pong table, while a rug that’s too large can swallow the furniture and your cat’s dignity. We at Geeknite recommend measuring twice and shopping once (or ordering a second time, if you’re into home decor regret). ## Materials, Craftsmanship, and Comfort

The Eden Lakeside Wool rug is, at its core, a wool product. Wool fibers are naturally resilient, able to spring back after foot traffic and chair wheels (if yours look like small shopping carts with legs). Wool is also known for warmth and insulation—ideal for temperamental climates or homes where the HVAC system pretends to be a dragon in a cave.

### Wool Quality and Warmth
- Softness: Expect a plush feel underfoot that’s comfortable for bare feet but not so soft that you risk tripping into a dream-sequence nap on a weekday afternoon. Wool fibers are fine-woven to provide a balance of cushion and support.
- Insulation: Wool’s natural crimp traps air, giving you a cozier floor and potentially fewer heating bills. If your living room is a little chilly, this rug could make it feel like a sunbeam teleported indoors.
- Longevity: Wool is tough. It resists crushing under heavy furniture and can handle pets with a bit of nibbling on the edge (we advise training your giant hamster, but we won’t judge if you don’t).

### Weaving Techniques and Build
There are typically two pathways: hand-knotted or machine-loomed. Hand-knotted rugs tend to have higher knot density, which translates to intricate patterns and a longer lifespan; machine-loomed options are often more uniform and budget-friendly. The Lakeside line blends traditional textures with modern colorways, making it a versatile canvas for everything from a minimalist vibe to a maximalist data-center aesthetic.

Backings and padding play a role too. A sturdy backing keeps the rug from sliding on slick floors, while a non-slip pad can improve safety in high-traffic areas. If you’ve got hardwood floors that love to squeak like a squealing pickle, the right padding will help you glide across the room instead of wringing your hands in despair.

### Care and Maintenance
Caring for a wool rug isn’t rocket science, but it helps to have a plan. Regular vacuuming (light, with a rug attachment) keeps the pile looking sharp. Spills should be blotted—don’t rub—so you don’t encourage the stain to burrow deeper than your concerns about the neighbor’s dragon tattoo on the wall. For deeper cleaning, you can opt for professional cleaning every 12–18 months, or follow the manufacturer’s guidelines if you enjoy the feeling of being a DIY hero who isn’t afraid to wrestle a stain into submission.

Stain resistance is not magical; it’s about fiber density and proper care. For coffee, wine, or questionable toddler art projects, act quickly and treat with a mild detergent solution if recommended by the rug’s care guide. Always test on a small inconspicuous area first. If you have curious pets, teach them that the rug is not their personal slip-and-slide. It’s a rug, not a pet park.

## Practicalities: Care, Longevity, and Everyday Use

### Cleaning and Care Tips
- Vacuum regularly to remove surface dust and avoid abrasive wear on the pile.
- Address spills promptly with blotting, then use a mild cleaning solution if recommended by care instructions. Always test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate periodically to even out wear patterns, especially in high-traffic zones.
- Protect high-traffic edges with furniture coasters to reduce snagging and edge curling.

## Ordering, Customization, and Delivery

### The Ordering Experience
Ordering a custom Eden Lakeside rug usually starts with a measurement check, material confirmation, and a discussion about the desired pile height and edge finishing. Pile height affects both feel and durability. If you want something plush enough to sink into while binge-watching a sci-fi marathon, specify a higher pile. If you want a low-profile solution for a room with a lot of traffic, a shorter pile may be better.

### Lead Times and Returns
Custom sizes typically take longer than standard 8x10 rugs due to the bespoke weaving and finishing processes. If you’re planning a redecorating sprint, plan ahead and order with a buffer, just in case there are supply delays or a secret axis of the rug world shifts its alignment. Return policies vary by retailer and custom orders often have stricter terms, so read the fine print and keep your receipt handy for dramatic purposes.

### What You Need to Know Before You Order
- Accurate measurements: Measure length and width, plus consider wall-to-wall clearance and door swing.
- Room lighting: Natural lighting can change how the rug color reads; you may want to test a sample or consult color charts.
- Edge finishing: Decide whether you prefer a finished edge or a raw look. The latter can be chic in industrial spaces; the former looks cleaner in traditional rooms.
- Padding: A rug pad reduces slipping and keeps your rug in place, which is especially nice if you have kids, pets, or a penchant for sudden interpretive dance routines.
