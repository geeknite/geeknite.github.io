## Rarity, Authenticity, and Value

Very Rare prints are the treasure chests of Carddas. The limited distribution, the unique finishing touches, and the potential for variety across print runs contribute to a market where well-graded copies command respect. If you’re evaluating No. 262, consider these practical checks:

- Print marks and batch codes: Some editions carry small batch codes or subtle marks on the back. Inconsistent alignment or odd textures can be telltale signs of counterfeit production.
- Card stock and finish: Minty No. 262 should feel sturdy with consistent gloss and no ghosting or smearing in the art. Inspect edges and corners for uniformity.
- Handling wear: Rare cards can fetch higher prices, which can attract counterfeiters. Look for crisp corners and an even edge without whitening or chipping.

## Collectibility, Storage, and Care

If you’re serious about No. 262, you’ll want to protect your investment. Here are practical care tips:

- Sleeves and protection: Use acid-free sleeves and a rigid top loader to guard against bending and moisture. A second sleeve can help reduce friction when you slide the card in and out for display or trade.
- Environment: Store cards away from direct sunlight, heat, and humidity swings. A stable climate around 65–70 F with 40–60% relative humidity helps preserve gloss and stock integrity.
- Handling: Handle by the edges, avoiding contact with the front artwork. This minimizes fingerprints and micro-scratches on glossy finishes.
- Display: For display, consider a glass-front case with a humidity indicator to preserve the card’s appearance over the long haul.
