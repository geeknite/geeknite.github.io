## What is Sushi Go Party! Exactly?

Sushi Go Party! is a deluxe, party-sized evolution of the beloved Sushi Go concept. You draft cards from a hand, pass the remaining cards to the next player, and score points based on delicious sushi combos. The party twist? Instead of a single fixed deck, you get to choose from several different menu cards to shape the round. Each round uses a different subset of dishes, so no two games feel identical unless you want them to—then you can just play the exact same menu again and pretend you are a sushi sommelier who takes their craft very seriously.

The Deluxe version expands the original by introducing a broader assortment of menus, colorful card art, and a streamlined setup that makes you feel like a high-stakes sushi chef who forgot to bring their own chopsticks. The game scales from 2 players up to a larger party size, which is where Sushi Go Party! truly shines. It’s a game that forgives mistakes, rewards clever drafting, and rewards the table with goofy, satisfying combos that don’t require you to solve a Rubik’s cube to keep score.

## How to Play: The Core Loop

Sushi Go Party! rides on the simple Pick and Pass mechanic that made Sushi Go a modern classic. The loop is elegant in its simplicity: you draw a hand, pick a card, pass the rest, and then repeat for the next player until everyone has drafted all the cards from the menus in play for that round. Scoring is a tad more nuanced than “most candy equals most points,” but only a tad. You’ll encounter combos like Nigiri sets (don’t overthink it, just collect the mackerel and salmon and hope the sea is on your side), Maki rolls (the longer the roll, the bigger the bragging rights), and Sashimi cards that reward you for collecting the pair or the trio, depending on the color-coded scoring rules in the current menu.

The deluxe menus add flavors that make drafting feel less deterministic and more like chess with fewer queens and more fish. For two players, the game keeps its tight, nimble feel; for larger groups, the tempo goes up and the room fills with friendly heckling that says, I’m watching you, you deliciously clever opponent who just snagged the salmon while I got stuck with the wasabi. The rounds are brisk, each lasting just long enough to keep energy high but not so long that your friend who thinks in Excel begins to mutter about standard deviation.

## Strategy: Basic Principles and Pitfalls

Sushi Go Party! rewards a few basic strategic ideas that work in any drafting game, but with a musical, foodie twist:

- Read the table, not just your own hand. A lot of the game’s fun comes from anticipating what your neighbors might go for on their next turn. If you see three people chasing a particular dashi, you might want to pivot to a complementary stack that punishes their oversaturation.
- Balance speed and foresight. The draft is fast, but the best players are those who can pause for a heartbeat to confirm whether a particular card will pay off by the end of the round. It’s a game about timing as much as it’s about cards.
- Don’t overvalue single cards when the combos unlock big points later. Part of the charm is learning to see when a momentary loss on a single discard yields a bigger payoff down the line.
- Watch your opponents’ table talk. Part of the social magic of the party version is that the drama lives in the banter. If you can glean their confidence level from their tone and body language, you might be able to craft your drafting decisions with a bit more misdirection.

A pragmatic tip: in larger groups, the rounds tend to finish in a blur. If you’re juggling multiple menus, keep a mental scorecard or a quick-reference card handy to avoid accidentally misreading a scoring rule. The beauty of Sushi Go Party! is that even mistakes become part of the storytelling, not a stick to beat yourself up with after dessert.
