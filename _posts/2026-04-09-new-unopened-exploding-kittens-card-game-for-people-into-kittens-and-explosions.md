## How to Play: A Quick Primer
### Setup and Turn Structure
The setup is straightforward: shuffle, deal a few cards to each player, place the draw pile in the center, and collect Defuse cards for later. On your turn, you typically draw a card, then may play one or more action cards if you have them. The main tension arises when you draw from the pile and hope you are not about to meet an Exploding Kitten without a safety net. The flow of turns is quick, which makes it perfect for a chaotic party night rather than a heavy strategy session.

### Core Rules You Need to Know
- Draw a card at the end of your turn; if it’s an Exploding Kitten and you have a Defuse, you can defuse and reinsert the kitten into the deck where you choose. You might smile, or you might silently curse your own luck.
- Cat cards require you to pair them with other cats to play their effects. This creates a natural social dynamic, where players negotiate, trade, and occasionally pretend to be psychic about which cards are coming next.
- Defuse cards can be used to buy time or to set up a future, more dramatic moment on the table.

### A Note on Strategy vs Chaos
This game thrives on balancing strategic safety with irresistible chaos. The more you plan, the more you risk making your plan look hilariously obvious. The fun lies in the tension between trying to control the pace and surrendering to the unpredictability of a sealed deck that might flip you into the spotlight or the cartoonish explosion you see coming from a mile away.
