## Practical Guide: Cleaning, Care, and Longevity
### Routine maintenance plan
- Vacuum regularly with a suction setting appropriate for pile height. Use a beater brush only if recommended by the vacuum brand for your rug’s fiber.
- Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear across high-traffic zones.
- Use a rug pad to reduce movement, protect floors, and extend the life of the rug.

### Spills and stains: quick responses
- Blot, don’t rub, to avoid embedding stain deeper into the fibers.
- For standard spills like coffee or juice, blot gently with a clean cloth, then use a mild detergent solution if needed. Rinse with a light amount of water and blot dry.
- For tougher stains, consider a professional cleaner or follow the manufacturer’s stain treatment guidelines. Always test on a small inconspicuous area first.

### When to consider professional cleaning
- If you notice persistent stains that don’t respond to home remedies, or if the rug has experienced significant pet-related soiling, a professional cleaning can revitalize appearance and extend lifespan.

### Cleaning frequency by foot traffic
- Low-traffic areas: quarterly cleaning is usually sufficient.
- Moderate-traffic rooms: every 4–6 months.
- High-traffic areas: every 2–3 months. Adjust as needed based on the presence of pets, children, and space usage.
