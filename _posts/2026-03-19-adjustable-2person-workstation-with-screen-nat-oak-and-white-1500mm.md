## Ergonomics and comfort
### Posture, desk height, and two-person ergonomics
Ergonomics is not about vanity; it’s about reducing fatigue and widening your weekend to-do list. With two adjustable zones, you can tailor each seating position to the occupant’s height and reach. The key is to align monitor tops at or just below eye level, with wrists in a neutral position when typing. If one rider is significantly taller than the other, consider adjusting their monitor height on their side (or even using a monitor arm that can flex independently). The shared surface should still accommodate keyboards, mice, and occasional snack diplomacy without creating a chessboard of cramped elbows.

### Screen distance and glare control
Two screens demand two sightlines. Place the main line roughly at arm’s length and angle to minimize glare—particularly if you’re near a window or a bright monitor‑friendly wall. A matte surface helps reduce glare, and a monitor hood or light-diffusing panels can make a big difference on late‑night bug-fix sprints.

- Typical recommended distance: about an arm’s length for each screen, with the top third of the screen near eye level.
- If glare is a constant, angle the desk or adjust the screen tilt to push the hotspots away from your field of view.
