## Setup guide: from box to boardroom (step by step)
1) Connect the VP59 to a network port that can provide PoE, or use the optional power adapter if PoE is unavailable.
2) Connect the device to your display device or conference room setup if you want video share and reflected content to be visible to participants.
3) Power on and navigate to the initial setup wizard on the touchscreen. You will be guided through language, time zone, and network configuration. If your IT team uses a centralized provisioning server, ensure the VP59 has the correct bootstrapping details.
4) Add a SIP account. This step typically requires server address, user name, and password. If you are in a Teams environment, select the Teams profile option and follow the on screen prompts or use a profile import if your admin provides one.
5) Configure audio and video preferences. Set the microphone sensitivity, camera privacy, and call recording options as per your policy. You can test a call within the UI to verify that audio routes through the VP59 and that the camera is functioning.
6) Optional: pair with a wireless headset or DECT handset if you want mobility away from the desk while on a call. This is where the cordless story comes to life. If you encounter trouble, the vendor and most admin manuals provide a standard reset procedure to restore default settings.

If you want a quick visual walkthrough, check the vendor s setup videos linked from the product page. They tend to illustrate the steps in a friendly, non frightening way.

## Compatibility and integration notes: what to watch for in deployments
