## Benchmarks and Real-World Testing: The Numbers People Care About

We ran both synthetic benchmarks and real-world scenarios to see how the T705 behaves under load. Think of it as the lovechild of a lab test and a best-case gaming montage.

- Sequential Read (CrystalDiskMark-like test): up to 12,000 MB/s (12 GB/s) in optimal conditions. In day-to-day use, expect something in the 9–11 GB/s range if you’ve got a fast motherboard, a PCIe 5.0 slot, and a healthy amount of RAM.
- Sequential Write: up to around 11,000 MB/s (11 GB/s) under sustained writes with a good cooling solution.
- Random 4K Read/Write IOPS: around 1.4–1.8 million IOPS read, 1.3–1.6 million IOPS write in typical workloads. For gamers and content creators, this translates into quick texture streaming, faster level loads, and smoother rendering pipelines.
- Latency: sub-millisecond for reads in cache, with occasional micro-delays when thermal throttling kicks in.

Important caveat: The numbers above are a mix of official spec claims and our own testing under lab conditions. Real-world results vary with motherboard support, CPU, cooling, driver versions, and the presence of a storage queue depth that doesn’t look like a brick wall.

## The Real-World Experience: Noise, Heat, and the Joy of Justworks

Launch day performance is great, but storage should disappear into the background. The T705 doesn’t scream for attention with lights and fanfare; instead, it quietly does its job. In a well-ventilated PC case with a modest heatsink on the M.2 slot, temperatures stay reasonable even under extended heavy IO. If you’re going to push sustained workloads—4K video editing, large-scale 3D assets, or massive database dumps—the optional heatsink kit (if your motherboard allows it) helps keep the drive from entering throttle mode on long sessions.

Hardware efficiency and thermals matter because we all want our shiny new toy to not become a toaster under load. The T705’s heat characteristics are typical of PCIe Gen5 NVMe drives: fast, occasionally hot, and thoroughly manageable with sensible case airflow.

## Compatibility, Cooling, and Install Tips

- Ensure your motherboard supports PCIe Gen5 x4 or else you’ll be stepping down to Gen4 speeds. Not a big deal, but if you’re chasing top-tier performance, you’ll want a board that supports Gen5.
- Check the M.2 slot keying and available space. 4TB Gen5 NVMe drives are long and can be a tight fit on some compact builds.
- Thermal considerations: If you’re building a compact ITX rig, consider a low-profile heatsink or a clip-on heat spreader to keep temps in check.
- Firmware updates: Crucial provides firmware updates; keeping firmware up-to-date helps with endurance and performance sanity.
