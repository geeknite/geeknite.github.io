## Assembly and Maintenance
### Assembly complexity
As with any large modular desk, assembly is a two-stage dance: unbox and realize you should have invited a friend with strong arms, then assemble while pretending you’re filming a DIY show for a social channel. Expect a hefty instruction manual with many small parts, dowels, screws, and a tool collection that resembles a small Swiss Army pack of doom. An afternoon to full day is a reasonable expectation, depending on your team’s experience with flat-pack furniture and the number of admin assistants who want to become mechanical engineers for a day.

### Maintenance and care
Oak finish demands a little respect. Use coasters, avoid direct moisture, and wipe with a dry microfiber to keep that finish looking sharp. If you occasionally spill red wine or soy sauce on a top-level surface, quick wipe-down will usually suffice. For deeper scratches, you can live with them as battle scars of a well-loved workspace or try a light sanding with a matching veneer repair kit. The key here is to maintain a consistent routine to preserve the veneer and keep your desk looking purposeful rather than a collage of coffee-stained memories.

## Ergonomics and Human Factors
### Ergonomic viability of a long desk
Ten people across a 9-meter span means you’ll be juggling multiple postures and sightlines. A fixed-height surface can be a friend or a foe. If your team spans a broad range of heights, you may want to explore adjustable task mats or sit-stand options at specific touchpoints. The no-screen approach pushes you toward ergonomic collaboration: shared monitor arms at central zones, adjustable task lighting, and clearly demarcated cohorts for screen-heavy tasks. The risk, of course, is desk-drift where some people drift into comfortable posture while others hunch over—giving you a mélange of ergonomic profiles within the same workstation.

### Personal space and privacy considerations
The long row invites a sociable atmosphere but can erode a sense of individual privacy. Partition panels, acoustic screens, or strategic plant walls can create micro-areas without breaking the open vibe. If the budget allows, consider lightweight modular dividers that can be reconfigured as teams shift. The aim is to strike a balance between collaboration and focus, a kind of office feng shui where people can share ideas without sharing the exact moment someone’s headphones slip off for the thousandth time.

## Practical Tips for Getting the Most from Your Purchase
- Map the room: Before delivery, sketch a floor plan with the monitor arms, seating zones, and aisle clearance. A little planning saves hours of post-install rearrangement.
- Plan cable management early: Central hubs, under-desk trays, and power rails should be part of the initial build. Don’t wait for someone to trip over a tangle of cables; that’s how an office myth #1 begins.
- Consider acoustic solutions: If you want collaboration without the chaos, add acoustic panels or fabric screens at strategic points. A measured amount of soft sound goes a long way toward preserving sanity.
- Align chairs with zones: Ergonomic chairs tailored to each zone can reduce fatigue. A mismatched chair situation is a fast track to an afternoon of backaches and misaligned posture photos for your HR portal.
- Prioritize lighting: A long row of workstations benefits from balanced lighting. Avoid glare on screens and ensure that the central area has broad, diffused light to encourage a comfortable working environment.
- Keep a maintenance schedule: Clean the desk regularly and perform occasional veneer touch-ups to preserve the finish. A well-kept workspace is a manifestation of respect for the people who sit there daily.
