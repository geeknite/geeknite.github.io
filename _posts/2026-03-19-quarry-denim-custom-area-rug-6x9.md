### Maintenance and cleaning
Cleaning a denim rug is easier than you might fear. Routine vacuuming keeps surface dust from settling into the fibers. For spills, quick blotting with a clean cloth usually suffices; harsher stains may require mild detergent and a gentle hand. The key is to avoid aggressive scrubbing that could fray the fibers or push stains deeper into the weave. In our test runs, minor spills dried clean with little trace. The color remained relatively stable after several clean cycles, though repeated heat from sunlight will always have an effect. For heavy foot traffic or persistent stains, professional cleaning can refresh the fibers without compromising the denim aesthetic.

Important care notes:
- Avoid chlorine-based cleaners; they can alter the dye and degrade fibers.
- Quick response matters; the longer a spill sits, the harder it is to lift.
- Use a rug pad for extra stability; this also reduces wear on the backing.
