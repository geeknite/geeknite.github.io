## Setup and Teardown Experience

If you’ve played any tabletop game for more than two sessions, you know setup can feel like a tiny exercise in self-flagellation. The ZCD001 tiles win some cool points here for being straightforward. You lay out a few tiles, align the edges, and you’re ready to go. The sort and storage process is neat too: a small zip bag or a dedicated box keeps all nine tiles flat and unbent, which is ideal if you travel to game nights or conventions where your tiles might get knocked around in transit.

One caveat: because tiles are cardboard-based, they are somewhat susceptible to humidity and droplet disasters. If you’re a frequent gamer in a humid climate or a household where someone forgets to wipe the coffee off the table, consider a simple protective sleeve or investing in a small tabletop mat to prevent warping. It’s not a deal-breaker, just a practical tip from someone who once watched a tile warp under a teacup like a tiny, dignified zombie rising from misfortune.

In practice, setup time is minimal. You’ll be dealing with the nine tiles, a few room shapes, and a handful of tokens in under five minutes once you’ve memorized the order of operations. Teardown is a breeze as well; stack, slide into a sleeve, and you’re done. The real challenge is deciding which tile to place first when the map still resembles the inside of your last failed escape plan.
