## AR Display, Interface, and Interactions

### What You See Through the Lenses
The RW4006’s star feature is, obviously, the augmented reality overlays. The display is a micro-projector system somewhere behind the glass that spools up content in your line of sight. The field of view is not exactly cinematic—think more “subliminal map in your periphery” rather than “full-on HUD display,” but it’s robust enough to show navigation cues, notifications, and context about what you’re looking at without requiring you to pull out your phone every five seconds.

The clarity is decent for outdoor use; you’ll want to adjust brightness in sunny environments, because white-hot sun hitting the overlay can wash out the content. The system performs best with simple overlays: arrows for navigation, pop-up emoji reactions for messages, and status indicators for battery life and connection quality. If you’re into dense data visualization, you’ll want to temper expectations; the RW4006 prioritizes practical overlays over a pixel-perfect sci-fi dashboard.
