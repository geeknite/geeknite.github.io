#### Blue
Blue mythics usually center on control, manipulation of opponents, or a splash of card advantage. In a well-curated lot, you’ll find a few that feel like a safety net for your mana curve and a couple that make your head tilt at the flavor text.
#### Black
Black mythics are often about resource denial and high-stakes spikes. They can be tricky to pilot in casual play, but they excel in commander games where political misdirection is a feature, not a bug. Expect some dramatic text that punishes opponents in clever ways.

## Storage, Care, and Display

Storage strategy matters. Matte sleeves? Foil sleeves? Rigid top-loaders? If you want to maximize condition retention, you’ll choose a system that keeps the cards protected from moisture, light, and the occasional goblin raid of your local card shop. The display options are also part of the experience: you can mount a glass case, set up a display binder with acid-free pages, or arrange the cards by color, rarity, or mood. The aesthetic payoff is real: a well-curated mythic row can look like a golden promenade across your desk and shelf.

We’ll include practical tips: keep the cards away from direct sunlight to prevent fading; avoid sticky shelf liners that claim to 'enhance color'; keep sleeves consistent to avoid friction; and consider desiccants for moisture control if you’re in a humid climate. Also consider a binder with color-coded dividers to help you locate a specific mythic when your thoughts drift toward the old school days of mono-green ramp.

- Identify which cards are the top-tier, set your price ranges, and check sold listings to gauge value.
