## What is Phase 10? a quick refresher
Phase 10 is a rummy-like, multi-phase card game designed around completing ten distinct hands (phases) in a specific order. Each player must complete the current phase on their discard pile to advance to the next. The killer twist is that you don’t just fish for points; you race against other players to “phase” before they do. It’s cooperative competition with a merciless side of math: you add up cards you’ve laid down, you subtract your grumpy neighbor’s success, and you keep the dream alive by not letting the wild cards waltz away with your victory.

The Fundex 1992 set lives in the sweet spot between the original Phase 10 release and the modern reissues. The deck’s makeup and card distribution reflect the era’s design choices, which means you’ll find characters in high-contrast colors, chipper typography, and a few quirks that remind you that the era’s designers were still figuring out how to keep players from inventing new house rules every five minutes.

## How to play this vintage deck today: a quick rules refresher
If you’re reviving a classic night, here’s a compact refresher for Phase 10 using the Fundex 1992 108-card deck. We’ll keep it tight; the goal is to respect the original rules while acknowledging how the deck’s quirks can shape play.

- Setup: shuffle the 108 cards. Each player draws a hand of 10 cards. The rest forms the draw pile; the top card becomes the discard.
- The phases: Players advance through Phase 1 to Phase 10 by completing a sequence of correct sets and runs. Each phase has a specific pattern; you must complete it before moving on.
- Turns: On each turn, a player draws one card and discards one. You can use your turn to lay down a completed phase or to add cards to an existing phase on the table, depending on the rules your table agrees on.
- Wilds and Skips: Wilds substitute for any card in a phase; Skips cause the next player to miss a turn, delaying their progress. The exact number and distribution of these can tilt late-game balance in this edition, so expect some spicy late-night arguments.
- Winning: The goal is to be the first player to finish all ten phases across rounds. That’s the rhythm of the game: early rounds teach you the structure; later rounds test your memory, risk tolerance, and poker-face during discards.
