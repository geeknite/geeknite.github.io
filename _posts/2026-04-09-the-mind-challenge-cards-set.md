### Setup and setup-tips

Setting up the Challenge Cards Set is almost identical to setting up the base game, with a couple of small tweaks. First, shuffle the challenge deck separately from the standard deck. Then lay out a short reminder card so players don t get too rebellious about the new twists. Remember, the whole point of the set is to add a pinch of chaos without turning your tabletop into a spill zone. A tip from our practice sessions: assign a silent referee who quietly reminds players when a card has ramped up or when the timer is about to scream for mercy. You do not need a referee, but it helps if your group has a designated nonverbal actor who can mime the moment the round ends.

We recommend adding a soft, background playlist to match the vibe. You want music that says telepathy is optional but highly recommended. If you are a purist who believes in absolute silence, you can go full theatre mode and pretend you are on a reality show where the prize is coffee and bragging rights.

### How to play with the challenge cards

Playing with the Challenge Cards Set is a test of your group s ability to align without talking. The cards alter the tempo, add constraints, or push your team to react to subtle cues. You will still have the same main mechanic of the base game, which is simple on paper but profoundly challenging in practice: you must place cards in ascending order of number without discussing your cards or signaling to others. The twist here is that the challenge cards will change the environment. In a typical round, you might encounter a card that reduces the window of opportunity or adds a rule that makes players think a second or two ahead.

The best part about these cards is that you rarely feel punished for errors in a dramatic, you-should-have-known-better way. Instead, you feel a shared sense of misfit hilarity. The group learns to anticipate, misread, and over-correct, which is basically the cardio workout of cooperative gaming. It s not about winning. It s about surviving the round with your dignity intact and maybe discovering your friend s silent cue for go is just them tapping their own knee under the table.
