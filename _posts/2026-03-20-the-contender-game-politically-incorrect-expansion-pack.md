## Overview

### What is this expansion pack

The Politically Incorrect Expansion Pack is a big splash added to the core Contender Game. It brings in four new candidate boards, a stack of controversy prompts, a handful of new mechanics that revolve around media spin and public perception, plus optional rules that push players toward bolder moves. In practice, it feels like the base game took a nap, woke up with coffee, and decided to have an opinion about everything. The expansion leans into adult humor and satirical framing, not to insult real people but to lampoon how public discourse can spiral from a single sentence into a full-blown national conversation—complete with fact checks, hot takes, and occasional chaos magic.

### What you get in the box

The contents are straightforward and gamer-friendly:
- 80 new Contender Cards featuring provocative, clever, and occasionally ridiculous stances
- 4 new candidate boards with distinct personalities and victory conditions
- 24 Spin Tokens that amp up media influence and compel players to manage image more than policy
- 2 Advice Decks offering reactive or proactive guidance for players who want to bluff in new ways
- 1 Rules Insert explaining the new mechanics and how to blend them with the base game
- 1 Campaign Timer to ramp up pressure and turn long conversations into punchy rounds
- A small handful of optional house rules for players who want to tilt toward satire or strategy

### What it adds to the game experience

If the base game is about shaping positions and winning over the audience, the Politically Incorrect Expansion Pack adds a mirror, sometimes a mirror held up to a quirky angle. ## How to play with the expansion

### Quick setup

To start, open the base game and lay out the new candidate boards alongside the original components. Shuffle the new Contender Cards and place them in a separate deck, keeping them distinct from the base cards. The spin tokens go near the center; the advice decks should be within easy reach of every player. You may opt to use the optional rules right away if your table loves the extra spice, or you can ease into it by introducing one new mechanic at a time.

### Core mechanics and flow

The expansion introduces two primary new dynamics: media spin and controversy prompts. When you draw a Spin Token, you must decide how it shifts public perception for your candidate. Do you spin to emphasize competence, or do you spin to torpedo your opponent with a well-timed scandal reference? The options are deliciously open-ended, which is the core strength of this expansion. It rewards players who can read the room and punishes those who attempt to bulldoze without reading the label on the can first.

Controversy Prompts are the wild cards. They can be used to force a hot take, demand a clarifying question, or mandate a quick rebuttal that must be performed in character. These prompts are designed to provoke laughter and gasps, a reminder that in politics as in improv, timing and delivery can be as important as content. Expect moments of brilliance, and occasional moments of regret when a card lands in the wrong hands at the wrong moment.

### Interaction and pace

The expansion does something clever by increasing interaction between players. It nudges you to engage more deeply with how your stance lands, how your opponents respond, and how the audience—represented by your fellow players—will react. If you are the kind of group that loves the back-and-forth of a heated debate while still keeping the mood light, you will feel right at home. If you prefer quiet, strategic play with minimal drama, you can still find value by turning up the satire gradually rather than blitzing straight into controversy.

### Scoring and victory conditions

The scoring remains familiar: points are earned by winning debates, securing audience support tokens, and successfully navigating media spins. The expansion adds a handful of victory paths that reward different styles of play, from the most persuasive posture to the most deftly managed crisis. The result is a game that can be played in many ways, which is a strength for rotating groups and new players who want to learn the ropes without being forced into a single, monolithic strategy.

## Thematic and tonal notes

### Satire done well

The Politically Incorrect Expansion Pack leans into satire, not insult. The cards skew toward biting humor about media cycles, public perception, and the emotional rollercoaster of modern discourse. The writing lands jokes without punching down, and the art direction leans into exaggerated caricatures that are clearly about the mechanics of politics rather than real people. If the base game felt like a roast of the political process, this expansion feels like a late-night talk show where the host knows the audience well enough to riff on the day’s chatter without turning it into a meltdown.

### Handling sensitive topics responsibly

While the expansion revels in provocation, it also includes subtle guardrails. The card design avoids explicit hate speech and instead uses hyperbole and absurd scenarios to drive the humor. This is not a license to be cruel; it is a reminder that satire works best when the target remains the systems and ideas rather than real individuals. If your group is sensitive to particular topics, you can pre-screen prompts or opt for lighter variants. The optional rules give you a knob to dial up or down the intensity.

## Strategy and tips

### Playing to your table

- If your group loves rapid-fire banter, favor stacks of Contender Cards that reward quick retorts and clever one-liners. The Spin Tokens will let you tilt perception in your favor during a heated exchange, so plan a few rounds where you can leverage that momentum before someone interrupts with a better joke.
- If your table values long-term planning, treat the new cards as opportunities to set up chains of reactions. A single controversial prompt can ripple through multiple rounds if you time it right with a slogan, a press statement, and a well-placed meme reference in the same sequence.

### Offensive lines and defensive moves

The expansion thrives on bold choices, but boldness needs a shield. Use the advice decks to prepare a quick recovery strategy if a line misfires. The best players treat a misstep as a pivot point: acknowledge a misread, pivot to a calmer stance, and spin the narrative toward learning and growth. Remember, the audience at the table often values cleverness over cruelty, and humor over hostility.

### Team play vs hot-seat play

If you are playing in a cooperative or semi-cooperative mode, the expansion can help groups coordinate responses and anticipate what others will do. In a hot-seat, competitive setup, the contention rises quickly, and the Spinner and Controversy prompts become your best friends or your worst nightmare depending on your temperament. Either way, expect the pace to quicken and the laughter to intensify as the table tries to outmaneuver each other with policy-sensitized bravado.
