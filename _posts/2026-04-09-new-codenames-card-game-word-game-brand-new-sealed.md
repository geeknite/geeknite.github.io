## Gameplay: The Spark that Sets the Table on Fire (In a Fun Way)
The core loop remains faithful to what makes Codenames addictive: give a clever clue and see if your teammates connect enough words to the clue without stepping into the assassin’s territory. The Card Game Word Game variant intensifies this by compressing the action into crisp, digestible rounds.
- Spymaster gives a clue that is a single word (or sometimes a short phrase) and a number. The number indicates how many board words the clue relates to.

## Spymaster Tips & Tricks
A few practical tips for the brave Spymaster who wants to maximize impact:
- Build a “clue safety net.” Before your first turn, skim the board and label words you know your teammates won’t connect with; this helps you narrow the field when crafting your clue.
- Use multi-word clues sparingly. A two-word cue can be devastatingly effective if it’s precise, but it can also mislead your team if not carefully chosen.
- Observe the energy in the room. If your group is buzzing with ideas and laughing, you may want to push for a more ambitious clue. If the table is tense, scale back and choose safer cues to maintain momentum.
- Leverage shared references. The more you’ve cultivated a common vocabulary, the stronger your clues will be.

## Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
- Don’t over-rely on wordplay alone. Word association is powerful, but context matters. If your clue is clever but fails to connect to the board, you’ll waste guesses and time.
- Avoid obvious traps. Some cards are designed to lure you into the wrong domain. If a word has multiple strong associations, consider a safer clue instead of gambling with luck.
- Keep the table calm. Overly aggressive or chaotic rounds can sap the fun—practice a tone that’s inclusive and supportive even when you’re losing.
- Don’t penalize a miscommunication. If a teammate misreads a clue, use it as a teaching moment rather than a scolding. It makes your group feel safer and more collaborative.
