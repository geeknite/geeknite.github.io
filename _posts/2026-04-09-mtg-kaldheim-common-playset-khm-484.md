## The NM Condition: What to Expect and How to Spot Fakes
Near mint means roughly: no creases, no heavy whitening along the edges, minimal surface scuffs, and corners that look sharp rather than crushed. You’ll want to inspect the following areas when you’re evaluating a KHM 484 NM lot:

- Edges and corners: Look for uniform wear and tiny nicks rather than deep scratches or rounded corners. If all four corners look mint, you’re probably good; if one corner is visibly softer, you’ve got a ding that might matter for display or trade value.
- Surfaces: Flat, clean card faces with no major scratches or stains. A little gloss variation is normal; huge gouges are not.
- Print quality: Some cards in bulk sets can have minor misprints or misalignments. It’s not a deal-breaker for casual use, but you’ll want to note anything that may affect playability or aesthetics.
- Backs: The MTG back should be uniform. In a bulk lot, occasional misprints or miscoloring on the back can exist. If the backs are wildly inconsistent, that’s a red flag. If they’re consistent and clean, you’re good to go.

If you’re worried about authenticity, buy from reputable sellers who photograph the run of NM condition cards in bulk. A good seller will provide close-up shots of several cards across the spectrum of colors and costs so you’re not surprised when the package lands on your doorstep with the aroma of cardboard and potential regret.

## Packaging, Shipping, and Storage Tips
There’s nothing worse than opening a dream box only to find bent corners and mis-sorted bundles hiding inside the packaging. Here are practical tips to keep your 484-card dream in pristine condition until you’re ready to play or trade:

- Packaging: If you’re buying, request or select a seller that uses solid, padded packaging. A cardboard mailer with a rigid top helps a lot. If you’re shipping, place the entire set in a protective poly bag or sleeve to deter humidity and minor scuffs.
- Sorting: Start with a color order (White, Blue, Black, Red, Green) and then sort by mana cost and card type. This makes it easy to examine, count, and decide which commons you want to ship to a new owner.
- Storage: An organized binder with four-pocket pages, or dedicated storage boxes with dividers, helps you maintain the NM condition and makes it easier to locate specific cards when you’re drafting or building a deck.
- Handling: Avoid bending or flexing the cards. The more rigid your handling practices, the longer your commons will stay visually appealing and playable.
