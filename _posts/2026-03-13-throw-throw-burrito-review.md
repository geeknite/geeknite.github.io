## What’s in the box (Sealed New, obviously)

A sealed box of Throw Throw Burrito is a charmingly suspicious bundle of color and potential chaos. Inside you’ll find:

- A deck of cards with intents to rival a stack of magic runes.
- Two plush burrito toys that urge you to throw, throw again, and then apologize to the neighbor for your reflexes.
- Quick setup cards that pretend to be instruction manuals but mostly tell you that chaos is legal in this house.
- A few token chips and plastic stands, which are basically the grown-up version of “you broke it, you bought it.”

The moment you crack open the plastic wrap, the air smells like cardboard dreams and the slightest hint of potential elbow jabs. If you’re buying sealed, you’re paying for two things: the thrill of unsealing and the opportunity to mock your friends for not respecting personal space.

## How to play (in plain, heroic, chaotic language)

Throw Throw Burrito is a party game built on three pillars: speed, memory, and empathy for the bracelet-wearing friend who can’t throw straight. You’ll notice three core phases: draw, action, and dodge (the dodge part happens when someone flings a burrito with ninja-like precision and you try to catch it with your face, or something similar).

### Gameplay flow (the short version)

1. Each player starts with a hand of cards. The deck is a mix of Burrito throw threats, dodge cards, and lucky escapes. 
2. On your turn you draw a card, then play action cards to set the burrito trap in motion. 
3. The burritos appear in pairs in the center as a capture-the-flag-meets-lawn-darts moment, and everyone scrambles to avoid them as if your snack stash depends on it.
4. When you collect certain card sets, you score points (or the table laughs at your misfortune, depending on who you ask). 
5. The round ends when someone hits a target score or when someone grabs the last slice of pride.

Yes, that’s the gist. The real joy of Throw Throw Burrito is that no one plays it the same way twice. One minute you’re calmly laying down a “Dodge” card, the next you’re dive-rolling behind a couch while a plush burrito skies toward your left ear.

### Card types you’ll encounter

- Burrito Blast cards: these are the “throw now or regret later” cards. 
- Dodges: the safe haven cards that let you pretend you’re a ninja of kitchenware. 
- Pairs: matching sets that trigger spicy mini-games and occasional bribery of fate.
- Wilds: chaos in card form, because 2026 would be boring without some unpredictability.

The actual card art is bright and cartoonish in a way that says “please ignore us while you’re busy laughing.” The plush burritos are soft enough to be charming, but not so soft that you won’t feel them ricochet off your shoulder and crash into a nearby glass top. And yes, in sealed new condition, you can brag about never having thrown them in someone’s direction (until you inevitably do).

## The sealed-new experience

There’s something to be said about opening a brand-new box and hearing that satisfying crackle of shrink wrap. In this case, you also get that unmistakable moment when you realize you will now be physically involved in a chaotic game where you must dodge burritos. The sealed-new experience is a pilgrimage to the temple of “I’m going to throw a burrito at my friend’s head and call it bonding.”

We’ll be honest: the game’s physicality is its biggest selling point. It’s not just about cunning cardplay; it’s about sprinting in disbelief, arms flailing, praying you don’t hit the family cat or the neighbor’s decorative plant. If you’re the type of gamer who collects games purely for the packaging, you’ll freshen your shelf with a new, glossy tomb of plastic joy.

## Strategy and social dynamics (a.k.a. how to win without losing your friends)

The beauty of Throw Throw Burrito is in how it rewards bold decisions and punishes reluctant planners. There’s no “dice-roll” fatigue here; it’s all about tempo, timing, and the willingness to risk an ankle-brace collison with an end table.

### Reading the room

- The player who is calm under pressure is usually the one who begins to lose grip on their composure as a burrito whizzes past. If you’re too calm, you’re probably not playing the game properly.
- Watch for cues. If your friend is wearing a grin that says “I am absolutely about to throw something at you,” you should brace yourself for impact and maybe offer a strategic tea break.

### Timing and risk

- The best plays are those that look reckless but are secretly orchestrated. If you can stage a “dodge” just as the second burrito is released, you’ll maximize your survival—though you’ll likely regret your decision during the “post-game debrief” where someone says, “We should have just played Uno.”
- Do not overcommit. Overcommitment is how you become the person who got splattered by a plush burrito while trying to save a friend from a darts-style catastrophe.

### Team vs. free-for-all

Often you’ll end up in a chaotic free-for-all, which is basically democracy in a burrito-slinging world. Occasionally, teams emerge—the kind of team where one person pretends to be in on a “please don’t hit me with the burrito” plan while another executes the plan with theatrical bravado. If you’re playing in a large group, consider a “designated burrito captain” to minimize collateral damage and maximize dramatic entrances.

## Components and design

Graphic design wise, Throw Throw Burrito nails the bright, silly vibe expected from Exploding Kittens games. The card stock is sturdy enough for repeated play without turning the box into a fragile origami of disappointment. The plush burritos feel like something you could actually cuddle during a particularly tense “draw a card” moment, which is both adorable and terrifying in equal measure.

The set’s physical footprint is moderate; it’s not a colossus board game, but it’s large enough to require a decent living room table. If you’re tight on space, this may require a little reorganization or a willing detour to the floor for a chaotic battle.

## How sealed-new affects value

If you’re a collector, sealed-new is the mint condition dream. The value proposition is twofold: you get a brand-new experience when you break the seal, and you have a pristine item for resale later—assuming you can resist the urge to open it immediately. The sealed state also helps preserve the plush burritos' fluffiness and keeps the cards pristine for showcasing in your next Instagram post.

In terms of replayability, the base game offers enough variety to keep a group entertained. There are enough action cards to keep things fresh after multiple sessions, but not so many that you feel like you’re learning a new game every time. If you pair this with the game’s social aspect, you’ll find a lot of value in the long run, particularly for house parties and casual gatherings.

## Value, caveats, and who should buy

- Best for: casual players who want to laugh, not grind for victory, and enjoy a bit of chaotic physical humor.
- Not ideal for: players who take board games too seriously or households with someone who might throw a burrito at an inappropriate moment (which, in practice, covers most families).
- Estimated playtime: 20–40 minutes, depending on how many friends you have who can throw with style and accuracy.
- GST barrier to joy: if you don’t have space to avoid burritos, you’ll be missing out on the full experience.

## Final thoughts

Throw Throw Burrito is a vertigo-inducing, smile-heavy party game that rewards chaotic energy and a willingness to be goofy. It’s not about deep strategy or weighty decisions; it’s about creating moments that you will remember long after the burritos have been forgotten. The sealed-new packaging is a nice touch for collectors, and the overall production quality matches the energy of the gameplay.

If you’re a fan of Exploding Kittens or simply enjoy games that push you to throw plush objects around the room, this title is a strong pick. It’s not the most complex card game you’ll ever play, but it might be the most memorable. It is, in short, a party starter that you can give to your friends without fear of long-winded explanations.
