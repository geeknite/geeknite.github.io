## What is Uno Flip and why a My Hero Academia collab makes sense

### Uno Flip basics (for the uninitiated)
Uno Flip is Uno’s moodier, more dramatic cousin. The game includes a stack of double-sided cards: one side is bright and cheerful (colors, numbers, matching), and the other side is a darker, more chaotic palette that flips over when you play a special flip card. The format introduces a moral dilemma: do you save your Flip card for a potentially devastating late-game moment, or do you risk it all now to seal a victory and bask in glory? It’s basically a microcosm of hero life: you survive a crisis, flip the script, and hope your friends don’t cry into their hot sauce packets.

### Why My Hero Academia fits Uno Flip like a hero fits a class roster
MHA is built on the idea that quirks (special powers) define a person’s destiny. That translates perfectly into Uno Flip’s game-changing flip dynamics. A collaboration with MHA allows card designers to layer “Quirk” inspired effects onto the flip mechanics, which means game moments that feel like epic anime plot twists—only you’re a slightly-competitive adult arguing over who gets to go first. The synergy isn’t just marketing; it’s a shared language of high-stakes moments, dramatic turns, and a lot of loud exclamations when you pull off a save with a well-timed reverse card.

## Gameplay twists: MHA meets Uno Flip

### The core Uno Flip rules (refresh for nostalgia or new players)
- Objective: Be the first to get rid of all your cards.
- On your turn, you must match by color or number. When a Flip card is played, the other side of the deck becomes active.
- Special action cards (Reverse, Skip, Draw Two, Wild) function as usual, but the dramatic flips can turn momentum in an instant while you’re mid-scene with your favorite hero.

### Introducing MHA-style quirks to the mix
With the MHA edition, you’ll encounter optional “Quirk” twists that can be integrated into house rules. Some examples (purely for fun and not a guaranteed part of every box):
- Deku Power Play: If you play a green card while you have a Deku card in your hand, you can steal a card from the person to your left instead of drawing. Drama, guaranteed.
- All Might Heroic Backup: If you draw a Wild card on your turn, you can reveal a quick All Might quote and skip a turn—your opponent, not you, experiences the fear of a thousand suns as they realize they must draw.
- Todoroki Freeze: When you flip to the dark side (the Flip side), if you have a blue Todoroki card, you can freeze the action and force the next player to skip a turn if they can’t counter with a blue card.
- Bakugo Boom: A play that features a red card can trigger a “boom” effect, allowing you to swap hands with another player if you have the right color match. It’s chaotic, but in a fun way.

Note: These twists are optional and can be adjusted for family-friendly gaming sessions. The real magic is in the vibe—the way the deck’s art and flip mechanic create a mini showdown that feels like a climactic anime episode, only with more nacho cheese residue on the couch cushions.
