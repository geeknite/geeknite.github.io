Cleaning and Maintenance: Keeping the Beast Clean Without Losing Your Sleep
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
### Routine Care
Purity of fiber and color is a big deal in a living space. The Martha Rug is designed to be stain-resistant in everyday life, which means you can spill coffee, red wine, or a dramatic sauce extent with less panic than you’d expect. Regular vacuuming is your best friend here. Use a suction-only setting (no beater bars) to prevent a carpet fray and to extend the pile’s life. The goal is to remove surface debris before it works its way into the fibers. A weekly pass is typical for living rooms; higher traffic spaces will benefit from more frequent attention.

### Stain Management
For spills, act quickly. Blot do not rub—rubbing pushes liquid deeper into the fibers. A clean cloth or paper towel will do. For tougher stains, test a small, inconspicuous area first with a mild detergent solution suitable for synthetic fibers. Avoid bleach and harsh solvents that can degrade color and fiber integrity. If you’re a risk-taker, there are rug-cleaning sprays designed for polypropylene blends, but always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines.

### Cleaning Schedule and Drying Time
After cleaning, ensure the rug dries evenly to prevent mold or musty odors. If you have a sunny window, you can use natural sunlight for quick drying, but be mindful of potential color fading over extended exposure. Some owners replace a portion of the rug’s fibers with a fresh batch from time to time as a long-term maintenance trick, though that would be an expensive patch job that feels less like a rug and more like a DIY patchwork quilt—fun for a weekend, not for daily living.

Installation Tips: Rolling It Out Like a Pro
---------------------------------------------
- Prepare the floor: Vacuum and clean the surface where the rug will live.
- Underlay matters: Add a quality anti-slip underlay to prevent creeping and to extend the rug’s life.
- Rolling vs. folding: For long-term storage or transport, rolling is better than folding to avoid creases that can become permanent.
- Furniture placement: Position your heavy furniture gradually to avoid dragging which can pull the rug edges.
- Final adjustments: Make sure all edges lie flat and trim any excess fringe along the binding (if your rug has fringe, keep it neat and untangled).
