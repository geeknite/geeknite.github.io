## How to Play the Sealed Edition: A Quick Startup Guide
Before you fear the word “rules,” rest assured: Exploding Kittens is built for speed and amusement. The base game is short, punchy, and designed so a table full of personalities can get a few rounds in during a family-friendly evening. Here’s a compact walkthrough to get you up and roaring in roughly five to ten minutes.

### Setup
1. Shuffle the deck. In the Sealed Cards Original Edition, you’ll find a set of cards pre-configured for the first playthrough. If you’re using a mixed setup with expansions, you’ll want to separate kitten cards, defuse cards, and the explosives to matchup with your seatmates’ risk tolerance.
2. Deal cards to players. Each player starts with a small hand (often a combination of small action cards and Defuse cards). The precise count can vary by edition, but the aim is to give everyone a few Tools to dodge the kaboom while keeping the suspense high.
3. Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck. The tension begins early because one wrong shuffle or an ill-timed “See the Future” could reveal the wrong consequence at the wrong moment.

### Core Rules (Keep it Light, Play with Gusto)
- The goal is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you draw one, you explode—that’s game-over for you unless you can defuse it with a Defuse card.
- Defuse cards save you in a pinch. They come in handy when fate decides to smack you with a kitten-sized disaster. You’ll use a Defuse card to neutralize the Exploding Kitten, after which you discard the Defuse and reshuffle the deck as per house rules.
- Action cards shake up the flow. Attack forces the next player to take multiple turns in a row; Skip lets you pass your turn without drawing; See the Future lets you peek at the top few cards to plan your next move. Shuffle mixes the danger back into the deck, creating uncertain outcomes that can be deliciously tense.
- The See the Future action is the real “strategic” piece in this light game. It gives you a window into two or three upcoming draws, letting you gauge if you should stay in the circle or pass the torch to someone else with a well-timed Attack card.
- The round ends when someone draws the Exploding Kitten. The eliminated player selects a new round’s starting position, but everyone else keeps going until one winner remains. It’s a party in a box, wrapped in a cat-themed mystery and a dash of strategic chaos.

## Strategies: How to Play Smarter, Not Just Faster
Even though Exploding Kittens is approachable, there are still subtle decisions that can tilt a round in surprising ways. Here are a few beginner-friendly guidelines to help you get started without dampening the fun.

- Manage your Defuse cards like your most precious snacks. If you hold too many Defuses, you’ll feel blessed; if you hold too few, you’ll be sweating bullets every time you draw. The sweet spot is the safety net for your hand without sacrificing too much of your potential future draw power.
- Use See the Future strategically. This tool is the table’s breath of clarity. When you know two or three cards ahead, you can decide whether to bait other players with attacks or skip your turn to let someone else sweat the bomb a little more. It’s about timing as much as luck.
- Attack and Skip are social tools. Use Attack to push pressure onto others when you suspect a player who’s getting too comfortable. Use Skip to bail out of a moment you know will end poorly. The skill is reading the table’s vibe as the deck’s tension escalates.
- Reading the room matters. The player who remains calm, funny, and composed often wins the social aspect of the game—the victory here isn’t merely who survives but who keeps others entertained while the deck refuses to cooperate.
