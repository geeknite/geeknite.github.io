## How to Play the Sealed Edition: A Quick Startup Guide
Before you fear the word “rules,” relax: Exploding Kittens is built for speed and amusement. The base game is short, punchy, and designed so a table full of personalities can squeeze in a few rounds during a family-friendly evening. Here’s a compact walkthrough to get you up and roaring in roughly five to ten minutes.

### Setup
1) Shuffle the deck. In the Sealed Cards Original Edition, you’ll find a set of cards pre-configured for the first playthrough. If you’re mixing in expansions, separate kitten cards, defuse cards, and Exploding Kitten cards to align with your group’s risk tolerance.
2) Deal cards to players. Each player starts with a small hand (often a mix of small action cards and Defuse cards). The precise count can vary, but the aim is to give everyone a few tools to dodge the kaboom while keeping the suspense high.
3) Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck. The tension begins early because one wrong shuffle or a mis-timed “See the Future” could reveal the wrong consequence at the wrong moment.

### Core Rules (Keep it Light, Play with Gusto)
- The goal is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you draw one, you explode—that’s game over for you unless you can defuse it with a Defuse card.
- Defuse cards save you in a pinch. They come in handy when fate decides to smack you with a kitten-sized disaster. You’ll use a Defuse card to neutralize the Exploding Kitten, after which you discard the Defuse and reshuffle the deck as per house rules.
- Action cards shake up the flow. Attack forces the next player to take multiple turns in a row; Skip lets you pass your turn without drawing; See the Future lets you peek at the top few cards to plan your next move. Shuffle mixes the danger back into the deck, creating uncertain outcomes that can be deliciously tense.
- The See the Future action is the real “strategic” piece in this light game. It gives you a window into two or three upcoming draws, letting you gauge if you should stay in the circle or pass the torch to someone else with a well-timed Attack card.
- The round ends when someone draws the Exploding Kitten. The eliminated player selects a new round’s starting position, but everyone else keeps going until one winner remains. It’s a party in a box, wrapped in a cat-themed mystery and a dash of strategic chaos.

## Strategies: How to Play Smarter, Not Just Faster
Even though Exploding Kittens is approachable, there are still subtle decisions that tilt a round in surprising directions. Here are beginner-friendly guidelines to help you get started without dampening the fun.

- Manage your Defuse cards like your rarest snacks. Holding too many Defuses can feel like a safety net; too few and you’ll be sweating bullets every time you draw. The sweet spot is a cushion that keeps you in the game without draining your future draw potential.
- See the Future strategically. This tool offers a clarity window for the table. When you know two or three cards ahead, you can decide whether to bait others with attacks or skip your turn to let someone else sweat the bomb a little longer. Timing, baby, timing.
- Attack and Skip are social tools. Use Attack to push pressure onto others when you suspect a player is getting too comfortable. Use Skip to bail out of a moment you know will end poorly. The skill is reading the table’s vibe as the deck’s tension escalates.
- Reading the room matters. The table’s calm, funny player who can keep their cool often wins the social battle—the victory here isn’t merely who survives, but who keeps others entertained while the deck refuses to cooperate.
