## Material Quality and Feel
### How do they actually feel in your hand?
The physical heft of the sleeves lands somewhere between “premium retail sleeve” and “tactical protectors.” The plastic has a pleasant rigidity that reduces the risk of tearing when you’re riffling through a pack, and it slides smoothly over the cards without snagging, which is essential for rapid gameplay. The inner surface is generally matte, minimizing glare when you’re trying to read your own card text in a tournament hall’s lighting. It’s the kind of design choice that matters more in practice than it does in marketing shots.

The sleeve thickness sits at a comfortable middle ground. It’s not so thick that it makes all your sleeves look like a fortress of cardboard, but it’s thick enough to avoid the usual “crinkly” feeling you get with cheaper plastic. The tolerance for card fit is decent: standard DBS cards slide in with a satisfying but gentle resistance. They’re not so tight that you’re worried you’ll warp a card, and not so loose that you can feel the sleeve sliding around during a hot-turn moment.

### Longevity and everyday use
If you’re a tournament player who photographs your matches for social media, you’ll appreciate that the art remains legible after a few weeks of use.
