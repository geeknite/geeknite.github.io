## Paint and finish: getting the rot right
The GUGKS03 set rewards patient painting with a lot of surface texture that reads well once washed and dry brushed. The skin tones skew toward pale greens, browns, and sickly yellows, which is perfect for that rot effect you want without tipping into cartoonish gore. The clothing is torn, which provides a natural canvas for dirt and grime layers. A couple of inoculated growths or fungal accents on a few figures give you standout minis that look almost edible in a horror movie poster kind of way. If you are new to painting undead, plan on slow builds and multiple sessions to bring out the nuances. A good primer, a mid-tone base, a strong wash, and a precise dry brush will produce a result that is worth showing off.

### Paint strategies and techniques
- Base coating: Start with a desaturated greenish-brown for flesh and a muted earth for clothing to establish a cohesive rot palette.
- Layering: Build up transitions with thin glazes to avoid chalky skin tones; you want rotted flesh with depth rather than a flat lime-green poster color.
- Texture highlight: Use a sea sponge to dab on lighter tones to suggest dried scabs and flaking skin.
- Stains and grime: Wash with a dark brown or grey to pull details into shadow, then apply a light dry brush to raise edge textures.
- Blood and gore: Use a very controlled red wash sparingly to suggest fresh wounds without going into cartoon territory.

To extend the technique beyond a basic table-ready finish, consider introducing subtle color variation between figures. Not every zombie needs the same green; a few with more yellow undertones can read as older decay, while the paler ones can be the newly infected who glow slightly under dim table lamps. A pin wash in the recesses between palms and rib cages can add depth, and a final gloss or matte varnish can control shininess on the exposed bone areas. The key is to keep the palette cohesive so the entire unit reads as part of a single infection motif rather than a random assortment of greens and browns.
