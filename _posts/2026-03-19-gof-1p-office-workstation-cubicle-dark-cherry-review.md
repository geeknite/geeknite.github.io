## Assembly and Setup

### Unboxing and What to Expect
Let us spare you the suspense: there will be assembly. The GOF 1P is built for DIY assembly, which means you’ll get a box of parts, a handful of screws, dowels, and the occasional hex key that somehow multiplies into missing pieces when you unpack. The instruction manual is surprisingly readable, with clear diagrams and a few humorous asides about not dropping the panels on your toes. It’s the kind of manual that makes you feel capable rather than a striker in a house of cards.

The typical setup time hovers around 60 to 120 minutes for a single person depending on how well you organize your workspace and whether you decide to do a thorough cable management pass or a quick and functional build. A pro tip: lay out all the hardware first, group by type, and do a quick dry-fit before you screw anything in. You’ll save yourself the existential crisis of realizing you dropped a cam lock while already 75 percent assembled.

### Tools and Space Requirements
The GOF 1P is friendly to common household tools: a screwdriver, a drill (optional for speed), a measuring tape, and a level to ensure you don’t create a leaning tower of cubicle. You’ll want roughly a two-by-two foot space next to where you plan to place the unit, so you don’t block the hallway with a panel that is still wobbling in the wind. If your office has a standardized ceiling grid or a wall-mounted cable channel system, you’ll be pleasantly surprised at how well the GOF integrates with those existing office infrastructure elements.

### Time-Saver Tips
- Pre-assemble the base frame on a flat surface before lifting the panels into place. This minimizes awkward juggling and reduces the risk of pinched fingers.
- Use a power driver with a low torque setting to avoid overtightening screws into veneered panels which could cause cracks or squeaks later.
- Keep a little rubber mallet handy for aligning stubborn panel edges without marring the surface. Listen for the satisfying click but beware the temptation to Hulk out if a panel refuses to align.

## Ergonomics and Cable Management

### Ergonomic Considerations
Ergonomics is not a one-size-fits-all metric; it’s a moving target defined by your height, chair type, desk height, monitor placement, and personal comfort. The GOF 1P’s height options offer a straightforward way to tailor the workstation to your body. If you are tall, the 6 ft variant helps place your monitor at an appropriate eye level with fewer head tilts. If you’re shorter or share the same space with a shorter partner, the 4 ft or 5 ft configurations can help bring the monitor line to a comfortable range with minimal neck strain.

For laptop users, consider a docking station with a stand to place the laptop screen roughly at eye level while using an external monitor for extended work. The keyboard tray, if present, should be at a height that allows your elbows to be at about a 90-degree angle with your wrists straight, not bent in awkward micro-angles that lead to wrist fatigue. If you’re using an external mouse, a vertical mouse or a trackball can reduce wrist pronation over long sessions.

### Cable Management Reality
Cable management is the unsung hero of a neat desk. The integrated channels on the GOF 1P are a solid feature, but they aren’t magical. You’ll still want to invest in a few cable ties and perhaps a simple under-desk tray to keep power bricks out of sight. If your desk is on wheels, you’ll also appreciate the ability to route cables down into channels that stay put as you roll between conference rooms. Beware of overstuffing the channels, as a crowded conduit can become a snag hazard for your chair and footrest during a heroic data-entry sprint.
