## Maintenance and Care: Keeping It Fresh Without Turning It Into a DIY Lab
### Everyday Care
Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to avoid pulling on the fibers. Use a rotating beater brush if your vacuum has one, but keep it gentle to preserve the weave. Spills should be blotted, not rubbed, to prevent the stain from moving sideways across the fibers. Cotton fibers are forgiving and respond well to quick, careful attention.

### Cleaning Tips for Stubborn Stains
For typical spills like coffee, juice, or sauce, a mild detergent diluted with water will usually do. Test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. For more stubborn stains, a dab of white vinegar or a gentle rug cleaner can help, followed by thorough rinsing and air drying. Avoid soaking the rug completely; cotton dries faster than it feels like it should, but soaking can lead to color bleed or warp the weave if left too long.

### Sunlight and Longevity
Direct sun can fade colors over time. If your rug sits in a sunlit room, consider rotating it every so often to distribute exposure evenly. A simple tip is to periodically switch the rug with a similar one from another room to keep color variance predictable and to give the fibers a little rest from the sun’s rays.

### Caring for the Weave as If It Were Your Favorite Gadget
Think of flatweave care like firmware updates for your floor. Keep the loom’s integrity by avoiding heavy footfalls on edges, address corners with mats to reduce wear, and handle the rug with care during furniture rearrangements. A moment of attention now saves you from an unwelcome ride to the land of frayed corners later.

## Buying Guide: What to Check Before You Click Buy
- Confirm the size you need for your space and check room measurements twice. A rug that’s 2x3 in a living room? Not ideal unless you love the intimate, den-like vibe.
- Inspect color variance. Cotton dyes can vary slightly from batch to batch. A tiny variance is normal and adds character, a large one may require choosing paired pieces with care.
- Look at the weave density. A tighter weave means greater durability and easier maintenance. A looser weave may feel softer but can show wear more quickly.
- Check care instructions from the retailer. Some have washing guidance that keeps your rug looking fresh; others assume you can carry it to the dry cleaners every month, which is not cost-effective.
- Consider anti-slip underlay when placing on smooth floors. This helps keep the rug in place and reduces movement that could cause wear at the edges.
- Evaluate the ethical and sourcing notes if that matters to you. Many artisans deliver great value with responsible practices, and knowing the story behind your rug can be part of the nerdy joy of decorating.
