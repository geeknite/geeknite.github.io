## Weather resistance and maintenance: truth or myth?
Outdoor furniture, while sturdy, isn’t immortal. The set’s wicker weave is designed to withstand sun, rain, and the occasional neighbor’s dog who believes every outdoor furniture seems like a chew toy in disguise. Here are the practical maintenance tips that actually matter:

- Clean regularly: A mild soap solution and a soft brush remove dirt without dulling the weave’s color. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could degrade the wicker fibers or strip the cushion covers’ color. 
- Cushion care: If the cushions have removable covers, wash according to the label. If not, spot-clean and be mindful of color transfer from sun‑dried foods, grass stains, and the occasional sunscreen spill.
- Storage: In climates with heavy winter moisture, store cushions indoors or use a breathable outdoor storage bag. Even the toughest cushions benefit from a seasonal break to preserve their structure.
- Mold and mildew: Guard against moisture buildup by ensuring proper drainage and keeping cushions dry when not in use. If you spot mildew, use a gentle solution of water and white vinegar to clean the fabric (test on a small, inconspicuous patch first).

With proper care, the wicker should remain pliant and the cushions should maintain their beige charm for seasons to come. It’s not a miracle cure for all weather conditions, but with a little routine care, your set can keep looking new enough to inspire your future self to demand a photo in a landscape mode that highlights the geometry of your patio.
