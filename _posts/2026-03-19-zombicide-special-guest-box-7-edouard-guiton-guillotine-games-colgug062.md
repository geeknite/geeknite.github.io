## Introduction
Zombicide Special Guest Box 7 lands with the swagger of a designer who wants you to cut the brakes on your zombie cruise. Edouard Guiton steps into the spotlight this time, and Guillotine Games uses this box to tease a thematic direction that feels like a love letter to core fans while inviting new players to sample a more storytelling oriented siege. If you have chased the Special Guest Boxes since COLGUG001 or COLGUG003, you know the drill: a standout designer, a handful of new bits, and a couple of surprises that push the base game mechanics into a different lane. In this review I will unpack what makes this box sing or squeal in the presence of a marching horde.

## Box contents
The box delivers a compact bundle that leans into siege flavor without ballooning the footprint on your shelf. Here is what you typically find inside:

- 1 new survivor figure designed by Edouard Guiton, with a unique pose and flair
- 1 exclusive zombie type with a design twist
- 4 to 6 new mission cards that push the siege vibe
- 2 new weapons that synergize with the new survivor
- 1 small terrain piece or token set to layer onto the board
- A foldable quick play sheet that introduces a brief encounter ramp

This is precisely the kind of mix that keeps a Zombicide night fresh without requiring a full new campaign. You get a new narrative beat, a couple of tactical hooks, and something tactile to drop on the table. The components are clearly labeled and the art direction feels cohesive with the Edouard Guiton aesthetic while still feeling like a Zombicide product rather than a plain expansion add on. For players who chase collector value, the box wears its niche badge with pride and invites you to build a small but memorable siege scenario around it.

## Build quality and components
This is where Special Guest Boxes often shine or stumble, and COLGUG062 holds its own without overstating itself. The miniatures carry the crisp lines you would expect from Guiton inspired sculpts. The survivor figure has a backpack full of gear and a stance that communicates both readiness and panic in equal measure, which makes for excellent table storytelling. The exclusive zombie type is designed with a lot of character, and it reads nicely from across the board, which matters because you want to be able to describe its menace without rummaging for the rulebook every other turn.

The terrain piece is compact but effective. It provides a defensive choke point that can alter lines of sight and create dynamic engagement zones without dominating the entire map. The new weapons feel balanced rather than overpowered, giving the new survivor a distinct playstyle without breaking the core rhythm of Zombicide combat. Card stock is sturdy and the graphic design aligns with the overall Zombicide palette, so the set feels like a legitimate extension rather than a side project.

If you are a painter, you will likely want to spray or wash the new miniatures to bring them up to your table standard. The sculpts respond well to quick contrast washes, making the box accessible for hobbyists who want a presentable table presence without turning painting into a daily hobby.

## Artwork and design language
Edouard Guiton brings a cinematic poster vibe to this box. The color palette leans toward warm tones with desaturated greens and blues. The survivor silhouette carries a heroic yet grounded feel, while the new zombie type trades the standard lumbering pose for something with a sharper silhouette that reads as a threat even in a distance glance. The artwork on mission cards and the quick play sheet remains legible under typical lighting conditions, which is crucial during a frantic late game moment when you are counting zombie spawns and door checks.

The overall design is consistent with other Special Guest Boxes in tone if not in exact meme level of novelty. You get a designer signature, a mission thread that feels distinct, and enough visual cues to tell this box apart from the standard run of expansions at a glance.

## Rules, balance, and flow
Fans of Zombicide will recognize the core mechanic set intact. The Special Guest Box 7 introduces a few mission specific rules that alter how doors, barricades, and choke points interact with the new survivor. The objective is tweaked to reward keeping a strategic line of defense while maintaining pressure on the advancing horde. These tweaks do not rewire the entire system; they layer a siege focus onto familiar actions like clearing rooms, opening doors, and coordinating multi-figure actions.

If you are new to Zombicide, the learning curve remains friendly. The new mission cards are straightforward and the accompanying quick play sheet helps in ramping up without requiring a wall of text to explain the interactions. For seasoned players, the box adds a handful of tactical tangles that reward careful planning and team coordination. The box does a nice job of providing a tangible sense of escalation as you progress through the siege scenario without turning the session into a slog.

## Playthrough impressions
In our test sessions, the siege lens shifted a few of the standard play rhythms. The new survivor’s gear provided a tangible advantage in crowd control, which created early momentum that could be leveraged in subsequent turns. The exclusive zombie type demanded targeted coordination; you could not ignore it without paying consequences later in the round. The mission cards unfolded in layered fashion rather than providing a single route to victory. This design choice encourages players to discuss plan A, plan B, and sometimes plan C as the board state shifts under pressure. The pacing felt balanced, with a steady tempo that never overstayed its welcome.

The setup time is minimal, a big win for a busy gaming group. You can jump from unboxing to first turn in roughly the time it takes to pour a couple of drinks. The added terrain piece contributes meaningfully to both strategy and flavor, giving you a reason to pause mid fight to consider line of sight and positioning. For players who enjoy meta-game storytelling, COLGUG062 shines in that it invites you to craft a narrative around a single siege operation rather than simply grinding through a sequence of random encounters.

## Design notes and designer voice
Edouard Guiton has a distinctive voice that emphasizes character and atmosphere. The survivor is crafted with personality in mind, making the player feel connected to a story rather than a generic stat block. The new zombie type, in turn, mirrors this narrative approach, presenting a design that feels more like a plot beat than a simple stat bump. This combination makes the box feel like it belongs to a cohesive arc rather than a stand alone gimmick. If you enjoy reading a product as if it were a micro narrative rather than a mechanical catalog, COLGUG062 will likely satisfy that itch.

## Comparisons to earlier Special Guest Boxes
Compared to earlier entries in the line, this box commits to a focused siege theme with stronger narrative cues. Some past guest boxes leaned heavily on a single standout model or a flashy new mechanic; COLGUG062 emphasizes mood, storytelling potential, and a modular approach to deployment. The tradeoff is that you might not get the same level of novelty on a per-release basis, but the box offers a more cohesive play session that can slot into a larger campaign with fewer friction points.

## Storage, shelf presence, and collectability
The box looks good on a shelf, and the miniatures store neatly in standard shells or trays. If you rotate your terrain or use a dedicated box for transport, the neat packaging helps you keep things organized between sessions. Collectors will appreciate the Edouard Guiton signature on the survivor and the distinctive zombie model, which can become a talking point when you bring friends into a campaign night.

## Value, price, and how to buy
Prices for Special Guest Boxes vary by region and retailer. If you are a collector who enjoys designer labels with a tangible narrative angle, COLGUG062 represents a reasonable value for the content and table time you gain. If you are more price sensitive or you play a lot of Zombicide in shorter sessions, you may want to compare with other expansions to see how this one folds into your rotation.

For fans of the line, this box is a recommended purchase that can spark new campaign ideas and provide a fresh siege scenario that remains approachable for new players. It is not the only path to a good Zombicide night, but it is a solid one that reinforces the identity of the line and showcases the designer voice without going overboard.
