## Assembly tips and troubleshooting

- Check the leg locks: Before you start, ensure all leg locking mechanisms click into place. A wobble is a drama you don’t want on a river hand.
- Level it: If your floor is uneven, use the adjustable leg feet or add a thin rubber pad under one leg. A level table makes for fair dealing and keeps your chips from sliding into a single bank’s victory lap.
- Test the cup holders: Fill a cup with an inch of liquid and gently shake the table. If the cup slips, you’ve got a warranty claim or you need to adjust your cup-holder compatibility.
- Cleaning protocol: Use a damp microfiber cloth on the felt. For the rail, a quick wipe and a no-scratch microfiber wipe is usually enough to keep the padding looking fresh. Let it air dry; avoid heat sources that could warp the padding or shrink the surface.

## Maintenance and care tips from the Geeknite desk

- Regularly check screws and bolts; tighten as needed. A loose leg is a dramatic line in a dramatic poker hand.
- Protect the surface with a felt cover when not in use. It keeps it clean and prevents the felt from collecting dust and curious cat hair.
- Use coasters or placemats for hot beverages. Spills are not the projectiles you want to test your upholstery against.
- Store with care: keep it dry, away from direct sunlight. The finish will thank you, and you won’t have a table that looks like it’s aged a couple of decades in one summer.
