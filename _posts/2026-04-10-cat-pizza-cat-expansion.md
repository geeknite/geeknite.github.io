Box contents and setup

The Cat Pizza Cat Expansion comes in a compact, glossy box that opens with the confidence of a cat who has just knocked over a glass of water for the ninth time this week. Inside, you’ll find:

- 20 new cards, featuring a blend of Cat Cards and Action Cards with a pizza motif
- 1 double-sided reference card to help you grok the new rules without re-reading the entire base game's manual
- 1 pizza-cat themed token sheet (spoiler: it’s mostly crust-colored and adorable)

The setup is delightfully familiar if you’ve played Exploding Kittens before: shuffle the new cards into the existing deck, deal out as usual, and announce to the table that you’re hereby granting the Cat Pizza Cat Universe permission to judge your life choices. If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, the expansion still slots in without complicating the core loop. You draw, you play, you try not to explode, and meanwhile a cat purrs in the corner as a silent moral judge.

New mechanics and card anatomy

The expansion introduces a few focused twists rather than a complete overhaul of the base game’s rhythm. The two big mechanical ideas are Pizza Slices and Cat Cards that reward pizza-themed play, with a dash of “cat-as-restaurant-owner” flavor woven through the rules:

- Pizza Slices: Think of these as a barterable resource within the deck—if you collect enough slices, you can trigger special effects or override certain outcomes in a pinch. They act as a bluffing tool as much as a resource, because any table worth its salt will try to guess who is hoarding slices and why. It also means late-game drama gets more dramatic, since the pizza cat has been eyeing the last slice since round three.
- Cat Cards: The team has introduced new Cat Cards with star power like Pizza Cat, Pepperoni Pounce, Mozzarella Mirage, and Basil Brawler. Each Cat Card has a whimsical effect that typically triggers when a player’s turn ends or when certain actions land in the discard pile. The flavor text leans hard into pizza puns and feline mischief, which is exactly the tone we crave during a late-night cram session.
- Action Cards: You’ll see a handful of new action cards that interact with slices and cat cards. Some let you steal slices from opponents, some let you burn (or bake) a pizza to dodge an exploding kitten, and a few deliver “hot take” moments that force players to read a rule aloud before continuing—the kind of silly carryover that makes a group feel both nerdy and heroic.

The centerpiece: Pizza Cat

Pizza Cat is the star of the show. If you draw or play Pizza Cat, the table’s energy shifts. In practical terms, Pizza Cat can grant you extra options tied to slices, like healing a near-miss explosion with a crust-based bargain, or allowing you to re-roll a misfired action at a cost of a few slices. It’s not a free pass—the mechanic is carefully balanced so that Pizza Cat remains powerful but not oppressive. The art depicts a chonky feline wearing a pepperoni scarf, perched atop a pie pan like a tiny queen of the oven throne. It’s silly, it’s adorable, and it’s entirely in character for Exploding Kittens’ brand of cheeky chaotic energy.

Strategies and synergy with the base game

For veteran Exploding Kittens players, Cat Pizza Cat Expansion offers new levers without destroying core balance. Here are a few practical takeaways:

- Slice economy awareness: Slices are a limited resource. Don’t chase them blindly. The best players use slices to hedge, not to hoard. If you see another player building a slice lead, consider slowing them down with a card that interrupts their plan, so you can swing back with a well-timed Pizza Cat play.
- Cat Card timing: Cat Cards in this expansion reward opportunistic plays. If you can trigger a Cat Card when someone else is about to stage a big move, you often shift the momentum in your favor. Don’t waste a Cat Card on a trivial moment; hold onto it until you can leverage it for maximum chaos.
- Pizza Cat as a tempo tool: Pizza Cat’s power isn’t about raw power—it’s about tempo control. Use Pizza Cat to force a table-wide moment of decision, and watch weaker players crack under the weight of plot twists and crusty metaphors.
- Don’t forget the base game rules: While the expansion adds new spice, it still relies on the base game’s core pattern. Maintain the rhythm—draw, play, explode, repeat—while weaving in slices and Cat Cards. The balance holds if you resist the urge to treat the expansion like a separate game and instead treat it as a flavored layer on top of the established flow.

Player comfort and table dynamics

Cat Pizza Cat Expansion plays nicely with players who love humor and shared chaos, not with folks who view card games as a solemn altar to strategy spreadsheets. The humor can be a double-edged sword: if your group does not appreciate cat puns or pizza jokes, you might lose interest quickly. On the flip side, if you’re the kind of player who enjoys a good natured sabotaging session while chanting “pizza time,” you’ll feel right at home. The expansion seems especially well-suited for groups of 4–6 players, where the chaos can spread without one player dominating the action. In smaller groups, you may find the pacing slows as players savor each new Cat Card effect; in larger groups, the room erupts with laughter and a flurry of rolling dices and gasps.
