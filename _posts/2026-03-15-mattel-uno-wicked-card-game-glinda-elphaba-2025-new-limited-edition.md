## How to play UNO Wicked: mechanics with a theatrical twist
### Setup
As with classic UNO, the Wicked Edition starts with each player receiving seven cards. The rest of the deck forms the draw pile, and the top card of that pile starts the discard pile. The theatrics come in with a few small textual deviations on the special cards and a few flavor phrases that appear on select draws and wilds. The overall flow remains UNO at its core: match color or number to discard, use action cards to shift the rhythm, and try to empty your hand before your fellow players stage an encore.

### Gameplay twists
The Wicked Edition introduces a pair of flourish cards that reflect the Glinda-Elphaba theme. They do not upend the basic UNO rules, but they add a deliberate theater swing that can alter the tempo of the game. Expect: a few extra prompts on Wild cards that encourage players to justify their bold plays with a wink and a nod to the narrative rivalry. The Draw Four card, for instance, might carry a flavor cue that makes players declare which Wicked character they blame for their misplay, which adds a playful role play element to a familiar mechanic.

The result is a more narrative leaning UNO evening, where players can pepper their turns with quick role play, mini monologues, or dramatic reveals when they lay down a crucial card. It is not heavy on extra rules, but it does reward players who lean into the theme rather than treat the deck as a strict math puzzle. If your group loves theater and theater-adjacent humor, the Wicked Edition can become a nightly ritual that adds a little performative value to a simple card game.

### House rules to spice things up
For groups that want more drama without additional complexity, here are a few light house rules you can adopt:
- Dramatic turn rule: when a player plays a Wild card, they must perform a 5 second monologue about why they chose the color, ideally tying it to a Wicked moment.
- Duel toast: when Elphaba or Glinda are referenced on a draw card, players must toast to the character with a silly beverage of choice (water, juice, or celebratory soda), then continue.
- Curtain call winner: at the end of the round, the winner gets to pick a short line from Wicked to recite before the next round starts.

These small tweaks keep the vibe fun and theatrical without derailing the basic UNO mechanics. If your circle prefers hardcore strategy, these rules can be ignored without affecting core playability much.
