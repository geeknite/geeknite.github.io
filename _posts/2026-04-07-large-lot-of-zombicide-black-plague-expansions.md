## How to evaluate a big haul: practical questions
Holding a big pile of expansions in your hands triggers a primal reflex: will this be worth the shelf-space? Here are practical questions to ask yourself before you dive in:

- Is the lot priced as a bundle or a grab-bag? Bundles tend to offer better overall value, but you’ll want to examine condition and completeness. Missing elements are annoying; missing everything is a dealbreaker.
- Are the components in acceptable condition? Some pieces may show wear, creases, or tiny tip of a mini’s weapon broken. If you’re buying used, negotiate on condition and potential replacement costs.
- Do you have a plan for organization? If not, you’re about to organize an expansion avalanche. Decide on a storage system first, or you’ll end up playing “Build the Fortress of Good Intentions” instead of your game night.
- Will these exact expansions play well with your preferred variant? If you love a certain campaign arc, you’ll want expansions that align with that vibe. Otherwise, you’ll end up mixing incompatible rules and coaxing your players to improvise chaos instead of strategizing.
- How will you incorporate ethics into your play groups? Some expansions introduce power dynamics that can tilt toward “tactical tyrant” or “communal hero.” Decide early how you’ll handle team decisions and who gets displayed on the victory screen.

## Organization and setup: turning chaos into strategy
A big haul presents two immediate tasks: organize and optimize play. Here’s a realistic blueprint you can adapt to your space and crew:

### Step 1: Inventory and categorization
Start by laying everything out in soft light and counting items. Create an inventory by category (core expansions, character packs, monster packs, promos, terrain). If you have a mobile app or spreadsheet, great; if not, a chalkboard wall works—chalk is cheap and provides dramatic flair when you write “The Horde Grows.”

### Step 2: Condition check and replacement plan
Set aside any pieces that look damaged or missing. Decide whether you’ll replace, proxy, or retire those items. If you’re a completionist with a budget, mark a replacement budget on your board so your spouse doesn’t think you’ve joined a zombie cult.

### Step 3: Storage strategy
Long-term storage should protect minis and cards from sunlight, humidity, and the occasional accidental tea spill. Consider: 
- Smalls in a baby food organizer or a modular storage tray to keep tokens from sliding around.
- Larger tiles and boards in a flat file or a padded box so the pieces don’t warp in transit.
- A labeled system for quick assembly on game night. You’ll thank yourself when you’re setting up a table for six in under 10 minutes.

### Step 4: Setup for play
For actual play: pre-sort by type, create a “campaign deck” of mission cards, and designate a table captain to keep things moving. If you’re new to Zombicide, run a simple tutorial mission with one or two expansions to calibrate group expectations before unleashing the entire horde.

## Handling the beast: maintenance, care, and longevity
A big haul isn’t a one-night purchase. It’s a long-term relationship with the undead. Here are best practices to keep your expansions in good order and your play nights smooth:

- Regular cleaning: minis dust up, cards get sticky when you spill energy drinks. A soft brush and mild, non-abrasive cleaner will do wonders. Do not spray solvents directly on the board—your board will thank you for the gentleness, and your patience will be rewarded with fewer “oops” moments.
- Temperature and light control: long-term sunlight can fade art, and humidity can warp boards. Store in a cool, dry place, ideally in labeled containers. The goal is to preserve the look of your undead enforcers for a long time.
- Card storage: sleeves can preserve readability for mission cards and equipment. If you sleeve everything, you’ll likely never regret it. If you don’t sleeve, you’ll be the hero who can still read the font after two years of frenzied campaigns.
- Collector’s quick fix: keep a quick-reference cheat sheet for the most common rules interactions introduced by expansions. You don’t want to be that player who spends ten minutes deciphering a card that should take 30 seconds to play.
