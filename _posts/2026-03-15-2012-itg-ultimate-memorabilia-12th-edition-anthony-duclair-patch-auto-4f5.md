## Grading, authentication, and how to verify a piece is authentic (without turning this into a chemistry lab)
Authentication in the patch auto space is less about a lab report and more about provenance and professional grading. Buyers typically rely on a combination of the card’s department and third-party grading or the seller’s documented history. When evaluating a 4/5 Anthony Duclair patch auto, ask these practical questions:

- Is the patch window intact and properly centered? A miscut or misaligned patch can detract from display value. 
- Is the autograph clean and legible? Faded ink or inconsistent stamping can be a red flag in older issues. 
- Does the serial number appear prominently, and is the card’s back consistent with ITG’s stamping style for the 12th Edition line? 
- Are there any visible surface imperfections that could affect the card’s grade or long-term display quality?

If you choose a card slabbed by a reputable grader, you gain additional confidence that the piece you’re adding to your shelf isn’t a clever replication. Always compare the patch’s color profile with known jersey palettes from that era to check for mismatches that could indicate tampering or replacement. In the patch economy, provenance and condition are your best friend after the initial wow moment.

## Display, care, and storage: keeping the magic alive
Display is a big factor in long-term enjoyment. A 4/5 patch auto deserves a display case that protects from dust, sunlight, and the occasional accidental coffee spill from a desk-shelf experiment gone rogue. Consider these practical storage tips:

- Use a high-quality acrylic display case or binder with protection that doesn’t bend or stress the card. 
- Keep it out of direct sunlight to preserve the autograph color and patch hues. 
- Use a well-labeled storage system so you can revisit the memory without triggering an archeological dig through your collection.
- If you’re upgrading to graded, choose a reputable service and compare the slab’s protective features, corner rounding, and glare on the case. 

Cleaning should be minimal: a soft microfiber cloth to wipe congealed dust, no solvents that could affect the surface or the ink, and careful handling when you slide the card in and out of the protective case. The patch’s visible threadwork deserves gentle admiration—think of it as thread art rather than fabric mischief.
