H2: Outdoor Durability, Weather, and Maintenance

Durability in the great outdoors is about more than raw wood strength. It’s about weather resistance, UV stability, and your willingness to give everything a little care. Acacia wood is naturally resistant to decay, but it’s not invincible. A light oiling every year helps maintain the color and protects the surface from moisture. In wet climates, you should consider storing cushions indoors during heavy rains or using weatherproof covers to shield from mold and mildew.

Maintenance quick tips:
- Wipe down with a damp cloth to remove dust and pollen.
- Do a yearly sealant or oil treatment to keep moisture from creeping into the grain.
- Use cushions and covers during inclement weather to prolong life.
- Store cushions indoors during the off-season or when you get a surprise snowstorm (yes, even in some parts of the globe).

If you’re moving this set around a lot, you’ll appreciate the relatively light weight for solid wood. It’s not a featherweight, but it’s easier to reposition than a heavy cast-iron sofa. That said, plan your layout in advance to minimize heavy lifting on a hot day—your knees will thank you.
