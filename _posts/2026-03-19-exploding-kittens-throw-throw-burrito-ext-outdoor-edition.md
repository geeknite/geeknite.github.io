## How the Extreme Outdoor Edition changes the gameplay

The core mechanic of Throw Throw Burrito — players throwing burritos at one another while collecting cards and trying not to be the one who wins the exploding card — remains intact. The Extreme Outdoor Edition adds a few wrinkles that feel intuitive for outdoor play, but there is a learning curve compared to the base game if you want to maximize the chaos without accidentally turning your backyard into a fashion catastrophe of burrito wrappers.

### The flow of a typical round outdoors

1) Setup and alignment. You spread out the play area on grass or a picnic blanket. The goal is to maximize movement space while keeping the play area clearly marked so you can avoid stepping on a rogue football or a sprinkler head.
2) The draw and play phase. Players draw a card or two, then the fun begins. The cards introduce new ways to fling burritos, cause mishaps, or create silly penalties for opponents. The exact card text will differ from the base edition, but the spirit remains the same: mischief with a comedic edge.
3) The burrito dodge. This is where the magic happens. You sprint, slide, or leap to dodge a burrito that has been launched in your direction. The outdoor version tends to involve a little more space for dramatic dives, which is both a blessing and a curse. You can end up with a glorious wipeout that would look perfect in a geeking out montage video.
4) Resolution and scoring. The scoring keeps everyone engaged. You want to avoid being the one who triggers the explosive card, but you also want to rack up points by collecting low-risk cards and avoiding penalties. The end result is a messy but well-earned victory dance.

The big variability here is space. Indoors, you are usually constrained by tables, chairs, and a sofa that keeps you honest. Outdoors, you have more room to move, but you also have more variables: uneven ground, wind that might carry a burrito off course, dogs that want to investigate an unusual, plush threat, and the sun that refuses to cooperate with your sleep schedule. The Extreme Outdoor Edition thrives on this tension between freedom and chaos. It rewards you for improvisation, not just rule-following.

### The burrito mechanics in the wild

If you have played the base game, you know the core mechanic: you throw a burrito and hope the other player dodges while you simultaneously protect your own score cards. The Extreme Outdoor Edition preserves this core idea but sprinkles in outdoor-friendly modifiers that keep the action unpredictable. Expect some cards that reward you for:
- Hitting two targets in quick succession while a sprinkler is active
- Using the environment in clever ways, such as bouncing a burrito off a bench and into a player’s lap (consent-based chaos only, please)
- Timing your throws to match the rhythm of a bird song in the background

The result feels less like a traditional board game and more like a live-action, family-friendly improv sketch where the props are soft burritos and the stage is your own backyard. It’s a cringe-worthy, glorious form of social energy, and I am here for it.

## Setup tips and playing tips for the outdoors

- Choose a long, flat strip of lawn or a clear patio area. Avoid bowling alleys, please. We are not ready for that level of chaos.
- Use the wipeable mat to create a defined play zone. It will save you from losing track of cards in the grass or mud and makes cleanup easier.
- Consider a designated referee to help explain the card effects and keep safety in check. The game is chaotic enough without a free-for-all of misinterpretations.
- Hydration matters. This is a sunlit activity, and the last thing you want is a dehydration drama that takes away from the gameplay. Water bottles within arm’s reach are a good idea.
- If you have pets, set a boundary. Plush burritos are cute, but you do not want a dog diving into the deck or your neighbor’s sprinkler turning the field into a fountain of chaos.

Tips for maximizing chaos without becoming a hazard:
- Encourage fast, light throws. This prevents people from stopping thinking and losing the momentum that makes the game hilarious.
- Use natural obstacles. A chair, a table, a low fence — these can turn a simple throw into a cunning bounce that catches players off guard and sparks applause.
- Don’t be mean. It is easy to push the line from hilarious to hurtful if you push too hard. Ensure everyone is in the spirit of safe play and that you are not aiming burritos at heads or fragile items.
