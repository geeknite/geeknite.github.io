## Comfort, Durability, and Everyday Use
### Footfall and Feel
Walk on it barefoot and you’ll experience wool’s natural responsive bounce. The Cambridge Philomene isn’t a shag rollercoaster; it’s more of a gentle hill that returns pressure with a pleasant give. ### Durability and Maintenance Realities
Wool has reputation for longevity, and this rug earns its stripes in that department. The fibers have natural resilience that helps resist pilling and crushing. Still, like any wool rug, it benefits from a rug pad to reduce wear on the underside and to keep it from creeping around on slick floors. A quality pad also adds a touch of bounce-back energy that makes pet zoomies feel cushioned rather than catastrophic.

Spills happen. The Cambridge Philomene handles minor mishaps with grace, especially if you act quickly. Blot — don’t rub — with a clean cloth to lift liquids. For tougher stains, you’ll want a recommended wool-safe cleaner. The most important part is to test any cleaner on a small inconspicuous corner first. No drama, just good rug karma.

### Cleaning and Care Tips
- Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting keeps surface dust from settling into the fibers. Use a vacuum without a beater bar on low-pile rugs to avoid fiber snagging.
- Rotate every few months to even out wear and sun exposure. This helps the colorway stay balanced rather than developing uneven fade patches.
- Blot spills promptly. For larger accidents, consider a wool-safe pre-treatment—follow product directions and always test first.
- Consider a professional cleaning every 12–24 months depending on traffic. Wool can handle it, and a professional pass can refresh color and texture without you having to break a sweat over a bucket and scrub brush.

## Size, Space, and Room Planning
### Picking the Right Fit
Size matters, especially when you want a rug to ground a furniture layout rather than become an afterthought. The Cambridge Philomene comes in several sizes, which makes it easier to orchestrate a cohesive space without the drama of trying to force a furniture set onto an undersized mat. For a compact living room with a sofa and two accent chairs, a mid-range size can anchor the coffee table and still provide a comfortable walking zone. In larger spaces, you might opt for a bigger variant to delineate seating zones while preserving that airy feel.

### Placement Do's and Don’ts
- Do measure your space. A rug that’s too small will visually shrink the room and trip over the edges; one that’s too large can swallow a room’s furniture arrangement.
- Do consider the furniture layout. Ideally, at least the front legs of hard surface furniture should rest on the rug to unify the seating area.
- Don’t forget a rug pad. It reduces sliding, adds a bit of cushion, and protects floors from the underlying furniture feet.
- Don’t stress about perfect symmetry. Rugs are design tools, not mathematical bonds. The beauty of handmade goods is their organic charm.

## Maintenance Cheatsheet
- Vacuum weekly with a low suction setting; avoid high suction on delicate weaves.
- Rotate every 3–6 months to prevent uneven wear.
- Blot spills with a clean, dry cloth; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber crush.
- Use a rug pad to reduce slipping and add a bit of anti-crush support.
- For stubborn stains, contact a wool-safe cleaner and test in a hidden corner first.
