## How to Play: A Quick, Humorous Overview

### Objective
Be the team with the most points by answering Disney trivia correctly and collecting completed quest cards that require you to name a character, recall a plot detail, or guess a Disney-verse on a whim. Yes, it’s basically a clever way to reward cultural memory skill through fun, family-friendly chaos.

### Core Mechanics
- Players split into teams. Each turn, a player draws a card and reads the question aloud for their team.
- If the team answers correctly, they advance a space. If not, the next team gets a chance to answer on the same card (stadium-style chaos, but friendlier).
- Some cards feature “Challenge Rounds” where teams compete in mini-games or quick polls that are more about consensus than exact trivia knowledge.
- A few rare “Family Dares” cards may prompt silly dares like acting out a scene or singing a line from a well-known tune. No judgment—just giggles and a slightly embarrassed look from mom.

### Turn Flow and Pace
Most games wrap up in 30–45 minutes, depending on how aggressively your sibling tic-tac habit manifests and how long your cousin insists on explaining why a 1996 direct-to-video release is canon. The pacing is brisk on standard rounds and deliberately slower during the occasional group challenge, which makes it ideal for a mixed-age crowd where younger players require a little extra time to process questions.

The game does a fine job of balancing accessibility with occasional brain-twisting prompts that spark debate and laughter alike.
