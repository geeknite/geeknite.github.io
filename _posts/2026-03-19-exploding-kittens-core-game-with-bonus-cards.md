## How to Play: A Short Guide
Exploding Kittens is, in essence, a take-that game with the possibility of defusing a catastrophe. The core mechanics boil down to drawing cards and using action cards to influence outcomes or to force others into draws they would rather avoid. The goal is simple: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten. The new Bonus Cards add twists that tilt the balance—sometimes saving you, often gifting a moment of schadenfreude to your most suspicious friend.

### Basic Round Flow
- Players take turns drawing a card from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re out unless you can Defuse the bomb with a Defuse card. Defuse cards are finite and precious; you never quite know when you’ll need to save your own bacon.
- On your turn, you may play Action Cards to alter the deck, skip turns, steal cards, or force others to draw. The variety keeps the rounds feeling fresh, even when you’ve played it a dozen times.
- If no one explodes, the last player standing wins. If all players are out, well, you’re part of the legend that can’t handle a cartoon cat and a bomb with equal ferocity.

### Bonus Card Dynamics
Bonus Cards introduce new layers of play—some protect you, some advantage others, and a few flip the entire table’s power dynamic in a single sentence on a card. They’re not just cosmetic; they alter risk calculation, which is the heart of any good card game. When you see a bonus card you recognize from a previous session, you’ll feel that familiar twinge of retro-cheese nostalgia—like discovering a VHS tape in your closet labeled with an arrow pointing to fun.

## Card-by-Card: What’s Under the Hood
While we won’t recite the entire deck here, a quick tour of the core components plus bonus cards gives you a sense of the rhythm:

- Exploding Kitten cards: Avoid these like a spoiler for your favorite series. Draw one and you’re out unless you have a Defuse.
- Defuse cards: The lifeboat. They let you survive a bomb, often with a humorous flourish of your choosing when you play them. They’re finite, which makes saving them a strategic decision, not a nostalgia vote.
- Attack, Skip, See the Future: These actions add layers of misdirection and tempo, letting you keep control or hand the reigns to someone who absolutely cannot be trusted with a lead.
- Bonus Cards: A mix of rules that modify the standard flow, often requiring players to adjust their risk tolerance. Some require you to perform a silly action; others simply force your opponents to draw more cards because that’s what friends are for when chaos beckons.

In a typical session, you’ll see a dynamic where early rounds are quiet, the mid game becomes feast or famine, and the late game devolves into a chaotic sprint where players beg their Defuses not to fail them in their moment of weakness. It’s part strategy, part improv, and part cat meme energy—delivered with a wink and a nudge to your inner chaotic neutral.

## Strategy: Reading the Room Without a Blood Test
The nature of Exploding Kittens is social deduction by way of random chance. Strategy emerges from knowing when to play what, when to draw versus when to hold, and how to bait your opponents into bad decisions with well-timed actions.

- Resource management matters. Defuses aren’t currency per se, but they’re your security blanket. If you squander them early, you’ll regret it when the devilish corner-case card combination hits you in the most inconvenient way.
- Pay attention to table tempo. If the table is in a fast swing, a cautious play might keep you in the game longer. If the table is stalled, you can push the envelope and be the catalyst for chaos—after all, you’re in it for the laughs as much as the bragging rights.
- Use Bonus Cards as misdirection tools. Some Bonus Cards let you reveal future draws, others shuffle the doom around in ways that can turn a losing hand into a surprising comeback. The key is to know when to pull the trigger and when to hold your nerve for the longer game swing.
- Reading your opponents is half the fun. Anyone who has played a party game knows that a sour expression, a smirk, or a suspicious eye twitch can signal that someone has a plan. Use those cues to guide your decisions, always remembering that you’re playing a game that revels in dramatic reveals and silly outcomes.
