## Setup, Rules, and Practical Play

The base rules of Cards Against Humanity are delightfully simple:
- Players draw ten white cards.
- The Card Czar draws a black card and reads it aloud.
- Each other player submits a white card to answer the prompt.
- The Czar picks the favorite answer; that player keeps the black card as a point.
- First to a certain number of points wins, though the real victory condition is rarely the number of points and more about who survived the night without a moral meltdown.

A new sealed deck is a blessing if you plan to introduce the game to a new group: you don’t have to worry about missing cards, duplicates, or someone’s cousin’s edition that’s missing half the set. It’s a clean slate, a factory-fresh invitation to chaos.

For those curious about how a modern mass-market party game handles inclusivity, CAH is not exactly a pipeline to social harmony. The humor tends toward dark, edgy, often offensive, and frequently provocative. If your group has sensitive topics or if someone might be triggered by raunchy humor, you should set expectations before you start; a good approach is a pre-game nudge about the general vibe and a safe-word for turning the laughter down a notch.
