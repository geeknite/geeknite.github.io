The Huntsmen Box for Zombicide Black Plague is a blend of new siege-era weapons, fearsome knights, and the kind of character that makes your party say, “We totally didn’t see that coming.” Inside the box you’ll find:

## Meet William: The Huntsman
William is a character who embodies the box’s thematic premise: a grim, practical hunter whose toolkit favors crowd-control and precision over brute brawn. In game terms, William brings a mix of ranged capabilities, quick repositioning, and a few “edge of the map” tricks that reward smart planning. He’s not the kind of meat shield who stands in the doorway and yells, “Hold the line,” while a tide of undead slams into your weak spots. No, William is the guy who takes a corner, lines up a shot, and watches the horde misinterpret his silhouette as an attractive challenge to a different direction.

From a mechanical standpoint, William offers:
- A signature ranged weapon with limited ammo, encouraging careful resource management.
- A mobility-focused ability that allows you to ping-pong around the board rather than grind through the middle like a rolling pin through dough.
- A few synergy options with the Huntsmen cohort, letting your team chain reactions where a well-placed shot triggers a cascade of mobility-based outcomes.

If you’re coming from the base game, Williams’s toolbox can feel a bit niche at first, but once you learn the rhythm—hit, reposition, cover, and reload—you’ll start to see how he can influence the pace of a mission. He excels in maps with chokepoints and long sightlines, where his range can punish the undead advance before they get close enough to feel like a nuisance.

For players who love the flavor angle more than the crunchy numbers, William is a character who looks the part of a medieval hunter. His base miniature is a visual treat, with layers of weathering on leather and metal, a cape that looks like it survived a skirmish with a dragon’s tail, and a bow that hints at quiet, deadly precision. The box also includes a handful of alternate sculpts and poses, so you can customize William to vibe with your table’s aesthetic. If you have a knight-y campaign vibe going on, William slots in smoothly as the archetypal hunter that your team deserves after a long siege.

If you enjoy the crunch, this box has enough new rules and interactions to keep your brain busy for several play sessions. If you prefer purist co-op that sticks to the core rules with minimal fuss, you’ll want to pace your adoption of the Huntsmen Box’s more advanced elements.

## Player Experience: Solo, Duo, and Full Table
### Solo Play
Zombicide shines in a group, but the Huntsmen Box scales surprisingly well to the solo experience if you enjoy roleplaying a lone hunter. William can be decked out with a set of “solo missions” that emphasize scouting, resource management, and risk assessment. The key is to carve a path that leverages his mobility while putting pressure on the undead spawn mechanics. Expect a longer playtime per session but with a richer sense of personal achievement when you pull off a difficult corridor run.

### Two Players
With two players, the Huntsmen Box becomes a test of tempo. You alternate control of William and the other Huntsmen survivors, synchronizing turns to cover multiple angles of attack. The tension in these sessions is palpable, as a single misstep can shift the board’s weight from “manageable” to “oh no, they are in the cathedral’s nave now.” The reward is a sense of “we did it together” that is incredibly satisfying, and it creates a long-tail enjoyment as you retrace the decisions that led to a hard-won victory.

### Full Table
In a larger group, Huntsmen Box William adds variety without major friction. The essence of the box—mobility, ranged planning, and terrain control—thrive when everyone is contributing. The key here is communication: set up a plan at the start, allocate roles, and rotate control of William so that everyone gets a moment to shine when a crucial crossbow shot lands exactly where it needs to land. The chaos remains, but it’s delicious chaos that your team can learn to harness rather than fear.
