## How to Play: Setup, Rules, and Flow

### Setup Instructions
1. Unbox and lay out the board, chips, and deck.
2. Shuffle the poker deck and place it within easy reach of all players.
3. Distribute a base amount of chips to each player (the exact count varies by game variant, but a good starting stack is usually 20–30 chips per player).
4. Decide on a starting dealer and rotate clockwise each round. The dealer plays a key role in setting the pace and announcing odds and outcomes.
5. The keno-style grid is placed front and center so all players can easily reference it. If you’re playing with younger folks, you can simplify by covering the grid with a cardstock shield to reduce distracting glances.

### Game Flow and Core Mechanics
The essence of this hybrid game is to combine two familiar rhythms: the betting and hand-building of poker with a keno-like number-draw mechanic. The exact rules can vary by edition or house rules, so here’s a solid baseline you can adapt:

- Rounds begin with a betting phase. Players wager chips on possible outcomes—these bets can be on cards, on numbers drawn, or on specific rows/columns on the grid depending on how you want to approach the hybrid concept.
- After betting, a number-draw phase occurs. A dealer draws a set of numbers (the keno-like mechanic) and calls them out. In a straightforward version, you map drawn numbers onto the grid and mark your cards accordingly.
- Simultaneously, players build or refine poker hands with their cards. The objective is to maximize your score by forming hands that align with the drawn numbers and the grid conditions. For example, you might win a bonus if your hand matches a certain pattern on the board.
- Scoring combines card hand value with grid-matching success. A standard hand ranking system can apply (pair, two pair, three of a kind, etc.), but you can also introduce category bonuses for hitting specific lines on the grid or completing a full house on the board itself.
- After scoring, chips are redistributed or awarded to the winning players. The winner of the round collects the pot; the rest concede a portion and prepare for the next hand.

### Variant Rules and House Rules
As with most party games, your mileage will vary. Here are a few common tweaks to keep things fresh:
- Progressive Pot: The pot grows with each round, encouraging more aggressive betting as the game unfolds.
- Team Rounds: Pair players up to form teams of two, combining hands and grid hits for shared victory.
- Quick-Play Variant: Shorten each round to five minutes by limiting the number of drawn numbers and reducing the deck size.
- Solo Mode: If you’re flying solo, use a timer and aim to beat your own high score by executing the most efficient melds and grid hits.
