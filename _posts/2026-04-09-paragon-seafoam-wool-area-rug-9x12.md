## Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping Seafoam Fresh

### Routine Care
Vacuuming is the primary upkeep task. Use a beater-barless setting if possible to prevent fiber damage, and vacuum in multiple directions to lift the pile evenly. Rotating the rug every few months helps distribute wear evenly and prevents any single corner from looking overly compacted due to chair scuffs or foot traffic patterns.

### Stains and Spot Cleaning
Spills happen—spaghetti-sauce-red, coffee-brown, or seltzer-sparkle stains. The best approach is to blot, not rub, and use a wool-friendly cleaner if needed. Avoid aggressive solvents or bleach. For larger or persistent stains, professional cleaning is advised to protect the color and fiber integrity. Before applying any product, test on a small, inconspicuous corner to ensure colorfastness and fiber compatibility.

### Professional Cleaning and Longevity
Wool rugs typically benefit from professional cleaning every 12–18 months, depending on usage. If your space experiences heavy foot traffic or if you host regular gatherings, you may want to schedule more frequent cleanings to preserve the rug’s color and texture. A pro can refresh the fibers and pull out ground-in dirt that a home vacuum can’t reach. Proper care extends the life of your rug and keeps Seafoam looking as fresh as the day you bought it.

### Backing, Edges, and Longevity
Look for sturdy edging and a reliable backing. This helps prevent fraying and curling, especially in a 9x12 rug where long edges are exposed to frequent movement from furniture. If you ever notice loose threads, address them early to prevent unravelling and to preserve the rug’s clean silhouette.
