Setup and Assembly

Setup is a two-person job with enough coffee to power a small research lab. Tools needed include a standard set of wrenches, a rubber mallet, and a willingness to tolerate loud jazz while wrangling a thousand springs.

Step-by-Step Assembly

1) Unfold the frame and lay out the legs on a flat surface. Make sure there is no hidden lego brick inside from your kid's last project—trust me, the universe will punish you with a twisted ankle if you skip this.
2) Attach the base frame pieces, align the legs, and tighten the bolts until they hum with barely contained energy. 
3) Attach the jumping mat ring and distribute the springs evenly around the circumference. Use someone to hold the mat in place while you hook in springs to prevent tangling the fabric. 
4) Attach the safety enclosure net around the top ring. Make sure the zipper faces inward toward the center of the trampoline to avoid a Houdini-like escaping attempt mid-jump. 
5) Slide the padding rails over the frame to cover the springs and frame. This keeps fingers safe and increases the aesthetic appeal. 
6) Install the ladder on a side, test the stability, and bolt it in place. 

First Bounce Check

With the frame assembled, give the first bounce a cautious test. Check for wobble, adjust tension if the mat has an odd sag in one quadrant, and check the zipper alignment. If all systems are go, you are officially allowed to pretend you are a gymnast who never took a single class in your life.

Common setup pitfalls: 
- Skipping the safety padding can lead to accidental shin sculptures. Always cover those frame rails.
- Uneven spring tension creates tipping? Re-distribute springs evenly around the mat, then test again.
- Zipper orientation matters. If it opens outward, you may end with a mid-jump fashion disaster.
