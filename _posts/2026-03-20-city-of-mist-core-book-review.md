## Introduction
City of Mist is a game about legends wearing human faces, a noir fantasy about ordinary people discovering that the world is a stage and they are, at last, the stars. In the Core Book, the designers invite you to build a city where the ordinary masks the mythic, and where a single decision can unlock a cascade of legends. If you ever wanted to run a session where your detective shows up wearing a mask shaped like a thundercloud with a line about fate, City of Mist invites you to try that out. Our verdict at Geeknite: it is stylish, clever, and surprisingly generous about letting players carve the story they want to tell.

## What City of Mist is in a Nutshell
City of Mist takes a simple premise: Heroes are defined by tags. Each PC has two or more tags that describe their real world identity and their mythic, heroic identity. The engine is built around tagging and combining these tags to determine success, complications, or dramatic twists. The result is a system that rewards story, not strict roll-diking. You are encouraged to lean into motifs, narrate the scene with a poet's eye, and let the city press your buttons. If you enjoy games where you can be a detective with a cape, this is your jam.

City of Mist thrives on the aesthetic of masks and myths. The art often centers on people with hidden faces, their inner legends peeking through the seams of reality. This approach makes the book feel less like a traditional game manual and more like a magazine from a parallel universe.

## Mechanics: The Tag System and Resolution
### The Core Mechanic
At the heart of City of Mist lies the tag system. Characters and scenes are built from a pool of tags that describe who you are, what you stand for, and what your current situation looks like. Checks involve rolling two six-sided dice and adding a pool of relevant tags and attributes. The rules emphasize flavor and narrative outcomes over rigid math. The result is a narrative engine that invites players to improvise, while keeping the world consistent.

### Tags in Action
Tags are both descriptive and functional. They describe what your character can do and what could go wrong. A tag like City of Angels might grant you streetwise charm in a scene in the city, but it might also lead to a tag that pulls you into higher stakes or a personal conflict. The Core Book includes guidelines on tagging and how to balance two or more players, ensuring there is always a moment for a witty one-liner or a dramatic reveal.

### Advancement and Story Progression
Progression in City of Mist is less about XP ticks and more about story beats. The book outlines how to escalate myths within a group, how to reveal the city as a living organism, and how to weave the heroes into the fabric of the metropolis. You level up not by numbers but through scenes that push your character toward a mythic identity, while also risking exposure to the city’s darker corners.

## Thematic Play and Setting
### Investigative Noir Meets Mythic City
City of Mist thrives when you lean into contradictions: normal people with extraordinary destinies, and ordinary streets with hidden legends lurking behind glow-in-the-dark signage. The Core Book guides you toward building a setting that feels lived-in and dangerous, where every alley could harbor a myth and every routine day could become a mythic origin story.

### Running the Game
GMing City of Mist is an art of balancing cues, seeds, and the occasional voice of the city itself. The Core Book provides example cities and templates to help GMs craft a setting within an hour or two. You can start with a single district and gradually expand into a metropolis of legends. The book also offers optional plot seeds and story arcs for players who want a more guided experience.

## GM Tools and City Building
### Worldbuilding Tools
The City in City of Mist is a flexible canvas. The Core Book explains how to structure a city with districts, factions, and mysteries. It gives you prompts for creating a sense of place, a vibe, and an ongoing dramatic question that props up every session. The approach is practical and creative: you can build a city with a few prompts and a map, then watch your players fill in the blanks with their myths.

### Encounter Design
Encounters in City of Mist blend investigative challenges with action set pieces. The book provides a range of encounter templates, from quiet stakeouts to dramatic confrontations with the city’s most dangerous legends. The emphasis is on narrative flow, consequences, and the interplay of tags that drive the scene. The GM is encouraged to let the city itself react to player choices, which can be both exhilarating and terrifying.

## Artwork and Production
### Quality Across the Board
The Core Book’s production values are high, and you could swap it onto a coffee table with pride. The quality isn’t just surface-level either; the internal art, graphic design, and layout all work in concert to support the game’s storytelling goals. The visual language communicates mood effectively and helps players identify with their mythic identity even during a frantic session.

### Images in the Book
The book uses imagery to reinforce the mythic tone of the setting. You’ll encounter portraits with subtle masks, city maps peppered with glyphs, and splash pages that celebrate the blend of modern crime drama and ancient legend. It’s the kind of aesthetic that begs for a live playtest with friends who enjoy dressing up for game night.

## Accessibility and Audience
### Who Should Play
City of Mist is accessible to players who enjoy storytelling, mystery, and a touch of epic myth. It’s ideal for groups who want to lean into character-driven plots, social dynamics, and cinematic action rather than crunchy tactical combat. If you’re the type who enjoys brainstorming with friends about clever motifs and the symbolism behind your mask, you’ll feel at home.

### Learning Curve and Play Style
The core mechanics are approachable, and the book provides player aids to help new players understand how to tag effectively. The narrative focus means less time spent crunching numbers and more time spent weaving a story with your fellow players. It’s not the fastest game in the world for combat-heavy groups, but it shines in investigations, social intrigue, and mythic transformations.

## Comparisons and Context
### Against Other Narrative Games
When you compare City of Mist to other narrative games, it sits in a sweet spot between noir RPGs and superhero tales. It has more focus on atmosphere and character than many hardline superhero systems, but more structure than a pure open world improv game. If you’re coming from games like FATE or Gumshoe, City of Mist offers a unique twist with its mask-based mythic identity.

### System Flexibility
One of the book’s strengths is how well it cooperates with different play styles. It’s not trying to be the most realistic urban sim or the most crunchy power fantasy; it’s trying to be a story you can tell on the night you play. If you want to run a campaign that leans into detective fiction, you’ll find that City of Mist gives you the tools to do so without forcing you into a rigid format.

## Pros and Cons
### Pros
- Strong mythic vibe and theme
- Easy for new players to grasp the concept of tags
- Great setting prompts and GM aids
- Captivating art and design that support mood
- Flexible structure for one shots up to long campaigns

### Cons
- The rules can feel unfamiliar to players expecting more numbers
- Combat can be slower in longer fights due to the narrative emphasis
- Some players might want more direct control over outcomes than the tag system provides
- The system shines in city-building and investigation but may feel light for groups chasing traditional dungeon crawls

## Who Should Buy City of Mist Core Book
If you love the idea of legends hiding behind ordinary lives, if you enjoy exploring identity and myth, and if you want a game that leans into storytelling rather than crunch, City of Mist is for you. It’s a fantastic gateway for new players who want to dip their toes into the concept of masks and legends, and it offers enough depth for more experienced players who crave a strong setting and flexible mechanics.

The system is designed to be augmented by new content without losing its core identity, which is a relief for GMs who want to keep games fresh without reinventing the wheel every year.

## Final Verdict
