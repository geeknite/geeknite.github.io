Installation tips
------------------
Installing new housing isn’t rocket science, but there are a few tricks to make your shifting feel like a spa day for your fingers:

- Measure twice, cut once. A 25' roll gives you margin for error, but you still want to avoid waste. Use a sharp cable cutter and a deburring tool to ensure clean ends.
- Use ferrules and end caps. Without these, you risk fraying the housing edges and creating extra friction. Yes, this is the kind of detail that makes a gear nerd giggle with satisfaction.
- Lubrication matters, but not in the way you think. For housing that carries a cable, you want a light oil or lubricant on the inner cable, not the outside. You want the cable to slide smoothly inside the housing, not to accumulate dust and grit like a space pod collecting space junk.
- Route with care. If you’re routing through a frame or under a bottom bracket, test the path with a thin wire to ensure there are no sharp bends. The goal is a smooth S-curve, not a figure-eight of regret.

Troubleshooting common issues
------------------------------
- Stiff shifts: Check inner cable tension, not just the housing. A tiny kink in the cable can behave like a dam that slows everything down.
- Slow return: If the derailleur takes a beat to return to the rest position, inspect the end caps and ferrules for proper seating.
- Noise: A squeak or rattle usually means the end caps aren’t seated or the housing is rubbing against a frame edge. Re-seat and cinch properly.
