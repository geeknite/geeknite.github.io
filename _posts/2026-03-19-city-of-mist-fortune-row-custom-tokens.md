### What’s new in Fortune Row

The core thrill of Fortune Row is that it reframes Tokens as customizable engines for storytelling rather than static archetypes. Tokens come with narrative hooks, thematic tags, and a bespoke set of moves you tailor to the character’s mythic identity. You’ll design a Token’s three signature moves, its elemental weaknesses, its dramatic opportunities, and the ritual that triggers its powers at pivotal moments. The result is a toolbox that makes every reveal feel earned and every misstep deliciously dramatic. It’s a design ethos that respects the game’s noir-mythic tone while giving your group something new to riff on at the table.

## Token Creation Philosophy: How Custom Tokens Fit City of Mist

City of Mist is built around the tension between ordinary life and extraordinary myth. Tokens, at their best, are living embodiments of that tension—two or more identities colliding in a single person or entity. Fortune Row leans into the metaphor with tools that help players codify a Tokens’ core concept: what myth it embodies, what personal story it enacts, and how it interacts with the city’s Mist-influenced rules.

### The three-tier design: Concept, Moves, and Mechanisms

- Concept: What makes this Token feel unique? What myth or motif do you want to explore? This is your Anchor—the image you want players to carry into every scene. A good concept is a spark that can fuel both the dramatic scenes and the quieter, character-revealing moments.
- Signature Moves: These are the three core actions your Token can take in pivotal moments. Each move should map to a facet of the Token’s identity and should create meaningful consequences when used. Moves are what the group witnesses as the Token comes alive in the scene.
- Mechanisms: These are the global knobs that determine how your moves interact with a City of Mist round: tags, relationships with other Tokens, and the consequences of failed or successful moves. These keep the Token from becoming a world-destroying deus ex machina and instead tether it to the shared reality of the city.

Fortune Row encourages you to think in terms of story beats and thematic leverage. If you can’t justify a move with a narrative reason, you probably don’t need that move. That keeps the table focused, cinematic, and a little ridiculous—in the best possible way.

## Mechanics in Practice: How Custom Tokens Move in the System

Custom Tokens in Fortune Row don’t just give you new flavor; they reorganize the conceptual engine of City of Mist in a way that preserves the game’s core cadence. Here are some practical notes on how they function in actual play:

- Token Hooks: Each Token begins with a hook, a sentence or two that defines how it intersects with the player’s life and the city’s wider web of Secrets. Hooks are your session hooks, not just flavor text.
- Signature Moves: Three moves that can be used to sway scenes toward the Token’s mythic identity. These moves should feel powerful and cinematic, but they shouldn’t trivialize the human stakes of the story.
- Clues and Twists: Fortune Row tokens can generate or consume clues—narrative prompts that propel investigation, confrontation, or revelation.
- Inter-Token Dynamics: Tokens aren’t island kingdoms. They influence and are influenced by other Tokens. Your Token’s actions might empower a rival, complicate a partner’s goals, or reveal a shared origin.

To keep things balanced, Fortune Row provides guidelines for power weighting, escalation curves, and playtest notes. It’s not about buffing every Token into an unstoppable force; it’s about making each Token deliberately potent enough to matter without breaking the city’s fragile mythic economy.

## How to Create a Custom Token: Step-by-Step

Here’s a practical workflow you can print out and stick on your GM screen (or ambient-neon wall if you’ve got one):

1) Define the Concept: Write a one- to two-sentence concept. This is your Token’s thesis statement. If you can’t summarize it quickly, revisit the concept.

2) Design the Signature Moves (three):
- Move 1: The primary dramatic action, often tied to the Token’s core strength or conflict. It should have a meaningful consequence if used.
- Move 2: A secondary action that creates complications or reveals something about the Token’s psyche.
- Move 3: A flexible, situational move that can respond to different scene prompts.

3) Establish the Weakness or Cost: What costs the Token its power? A weakness keeps the Token grounded and creates narrative tension.

4) Identify Mythic Ties: What myth or motif does the Token channel? How does that trend influence its behavior and choices?

5) Set Relationships: Does the Token have a rival, an ally, or a complicated family tree? Establish at least one significant connection to other Tokens or to the city.

6) Add Tags: Tags are shorthand for the Token’s flavor and its thematic bucket. They guide the GM’s call during scenes and help players describe actions with clarity.

7) Create a Short Narrative Arc: Sketch a three-beat arc that shows how the Token changes across a campaign or a story arc. This will help you run meaningful scenes and avoid token stagnation.

8) Playtest and Iterate: Expect iteration. Tokens are living concepts. You’ll adjust moves, refine the weakness, and maybe reframe the mythic ties once you see them in action.

This process emphasizes collaboration: players propose concepts, the GM tests balance, and everyone commits to a shared mythic mood. It’s cooperative storytelling with a dash of melodrama and a wink of neon.

## Examples of Custom Tokens (Inspiration to Steal or Steal-From)

Below are three fully-formed token concepts you can adopt, adapt, or use as springboards for your own designs. Each includes concept, moves, weakness, myth ties, and a quick narrative beat.

### 1) The Glasswrought Archivist
- Concept: A sentient database made of glass, living haunted memory that preserves every confession ever spoken in the city’s libraries.
- Signature Moves:
  - Echoing Glass: If used, the Archivist can replay a spoken confession from the city’s past, revealing truth but shattering glass around them, attracting attention.
  - Shard of Memory: The Archivist can extract a memory from a person’s mind and place it into a glass shard that can be used for leverage.
  - Breach the Seal: In moments of desperation, the Archivist can seal a piece of information away, forcing the truth to surface later in a dramatic reveal.
- Weakness: Each memory shard slowly erodes the Archivist’s own memories, risking personal amnesia during a high-stakes arc.
- Mythic Tie: Tied to the “Truth” motif; the Archivist is both custodian and curator of what is true in a city built on secrets.
- Narrative Beat: The Archivist helps the team uncover a hidden library beneath the city’s riverfront, but at the cost of exposing their own past to a dangerous faction.

### 2) The Neon Widowmaker
- Concept: A charismatic vigilante who uses light-based misdirection to strike at corrupt power players, leaving a signature glow on the crime scene.
- Signature Moves:
  - Luminous Trap: Creates a flash that disorients foes and reveals their vulnerabilities.
  - Neon Curtain: A blinding aura that protects allies while enabling bold, high-stakes moves.
  - Echoed Vengeance: The Widowmaker can confront the moral guilt of her enemies, triggering a dramatic flashback that exposes their worst moments.
- Weakness: The Neon glow draws police attention and can blind the user in darkness, compromising stealth.
- Mythic Tie: Embodies the myth of illumination and judgment.
- Narrative Beat: The Widowmaker dismantles a bribery ring but discovers a personal betrayal that reshapes her motive.

### 3) Harborweight, the Tidebound Cartographer
- Concept: A living map embedded in a tide-drenched wheel, Harborweight can chart not only streets but currents of fate.
- Signature Moves:
  - Tide-Draw: Scribes a path through the city’s crimes and reveals a hidden route of opportunity.
  - Current Reckoning: Forces a choice under pressure, revealing a hidden weakness in an antagonist’s plan.
  - Echo of the Deep: Calls upon sea myths to calm or disrupt crowds, depending on the Token’s needs.
- Weakness: The map mutters about past navigations, occasionally pulling Harborweight toward old decisions that hurt allies.
- Mythic Tie: Ties to sea gods and nautical legends; embodies the idea of destiny as a current you can ride or drown in.
- Narrative Beat: The cartographer reveals an underwater tunnel that connects two crime rings, shifting the balance of power—at a cost.

## Balancing Custom Tokens: A Word from the GM’s Notebook

Balance is less about numerical parity and more about narrative stability. A Token that can instantly reveal every Truth in the city is dramatically powerful, but you’ll want to ensure its use is earned and earned again. Here are practical balance guidelines when integrating Custom Tokens into your game:

- Escalation Curve: Each token should have a built-in ramp that increases the stakes during a session. Avoid flat power curves; you want moments when the Token’s moves tilt a scene toward a tense climax.
- Cost for Big Plays: Big moves should come with a cost—sacrifice, a distraction, or a personal consequence that resonates in the next scene. This keeps the story grounded and prevents Token usage from feeling gratuitous.
- Narrative Limits: Give Tokens explicit boundaries. Where can they operate? What counts as a rule-bend? Defining a few “no-go” zones helps keep play fair without strangling creativity.
- Interactions with Core System: Tie Custom Tokens to core Mist rules (the Logos and Mythos tension, the Mist-driven social consequences, etc.). The more you can show how a Token changes an existing interaction rather than replacing it, the better the integration will feel.
- Playtests: Start small. Run a three-session arc focused on a single Token’s discovery and growth, then broaden to a multi-Token campaign if the group vibes with the concept.

## Running Fortune Row in a Session: Tips and Tricks

- Build the vibe first, mechanics second: Let players describe the Token’s presence and mood; the moves should emerge from these descriptions rather than being forced into a rigid checklist.
- Use the city as a character: Fortune Row shines when the Tokens’ choices ripple through the city’s underbelly, spawning new intrigues, rivalries, and whispered rumors.
- Prepare narrative hooks tied to each move: Each move should unlock a mini-arc you can mine for a session. This ensures scenes feel connected and consequential.
- Don’t fear the dramatic pause: In City of Mist, a moment of hesitation can be as potent as a spectacular action. Let Tokens linger on decisions; the audience will feel the weight of the choice.
- Maintain a sense of rhythm: Balance big moments with quiet, character-driven scenes. Custom Tokens often shine in the latter, when their depth becomes a catalyst for personal revelation.

## Design Notes and Things We Loved (and a Few Tiny Quibbles)

What stands out in Fortune Row is the care given to turning Tokens into narrative engines rather than cosmetic flourishes. The three-move structure encourages a clean, cinematic rhythm; the concept-crafting process keeps players deeply engaged with their character’s destiny; and the explicit encouragement to weave the city’s Mist-inspired psychology into each Token’s behavior is a welcome breath of thematic air.

That said, there are a couple of points worth noting:

- Complexity vs. Accessibility: For brand-new players, the token-building flow can feel dense. If you’ve got newcomers, consider running a lighter version or a one-session “Prototype Token” before committing to a full Custom Token arc.
- Optionality Overload: With many options, it’s easy to wander into analysis paralysis. A simple “starter token kit” with 3 prebuilt templates can be a lifesaver for tables that want fast-start play.
- Thematic Density: Fortune Row nudges you toward mythic, cinematic storytelling, which is fantastic—until someone wants gritty realism. If you’re chasing grit, you may need to adjust tone and stakes to suit your group.
