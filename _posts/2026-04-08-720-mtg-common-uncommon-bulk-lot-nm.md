## Introduction
Welcome to the wild world of bulk MTG. If you ever wondered what 720 common and uncommon cards looks like when it comes out of the bag, this review is for you. Yes, it is mostly not the game-changers, and yes, there will be a lot of duplicates. Still, there is gold hiding in the corners if you know where to look.

## What you actually get in a bulk lot
A typical NM bulk lot of 720 MTG cards usually contains:
- A heavy majority of commons, a generous mix of uncommons
- No guaranteed rares or mythics, though occasionally there might be a stray rare
- Cards in near mint condition with minimal wear
- A mix of basic lands, tokens, and occasional promos or oddities
Managing expectations is key. If you were hoping for a surprise Black Lotus in every bag, you probably bought the wrong thing. But if your plan is to stock a budget cube or spice up a draft night, bulk is your friend.

### What NM means in bulk
Near mint means the card shows minimal signs of wear – sharp corners, clean fronts, no water damage. In bulk lots, you will still see sleeves, minor edge wear, or slight whitening on older prints. Decide how strict you want to be when you open. Are you trying to build a modern standard deck on a shoestring budget? NM is great for this. For a casual cube, heavily played copies can work if you know how to sort.

## Sort it like a pro
If you are serious, sort the pile into categories:
- By color (white, blue, black, red, green, colorless)
- By rarity (common vs uncommon)
- By set if you are chasing specific reprints
- By practical utility (lands, removal, creatures)
A few organization tips:
- Use stackable clear sleeves to keep cards in separate bundles
- Invest in a decent organizer with dividers
- Use a simple spreadsheet to track counts and values

## Value, pricing, and what to expect
720 cards bought as bulk will not make you rich, but there is value in the art and the cards themselves. You may be surprised by the number of playable uncommons or usable basics you can pull:
- Common basics that see play in sideboards or budget decks
- Uncommons that serve as efficient staples in commanders or modern pauper decks
Be mindful of condition and misprints. Sometimes a misprint can turn into a fun novelty for a collector or a dealer.

## Case studies and scenarios
### Scenario A: The Budget Mono-White Deck
Sort whites first, pull a set of early game creatures and a few heal basics. Pair with cost-efficient removal, and you are in business.

### Scenario B: The Multi-Color Dash
Uncommons shine here. Look for multi-color mana-fixing uncommons that can slot into midrange decks.

## Uncommons and rares you might encounter
While you should not expect rares in a bulk lot, keep an eye out for unusual uncommons that can elevate a deck. Cards like Gatekeeper or efficient removal can appear. Don’t get carried away chasing a specific 1999 rare, but keep an eye on value cards that can be used in trade.

## Building and using a bulk lot

### Cube-building
If you are into cube design, bulk is a surprising resource. Use a portion of the lot to fill your cube with playable uncommons and a handful of rares you are comfortable including. You can create a cube with a tighter power level and still produce satisfying games.

### Draft nights
Set up a sealed or draft event using the bulk as filler for the starter packs. You can pair with a mana-fixing color, and you can run a solution with a booster-like distribution.

### Trading and selling
If your local shop or online marketplace accepts bulk, you can trade or sell by bundles. Offer cards by group: commons first, then uncommons, with a few standout cards as bait. You will be surprised by how often players want to trade for a familiar common that they used to run in a long-forgotten format.

## Safety, counterfeit risk, and how to avoid scams
Bulk purchases can attract scams if you are not careful. The risk is not just overpriced lots; it is also the possibility of counterfeit cards or reprints that have been altered. To minimize risk:
- Inspect the lot thoroughly
- Buy from reputable sellers with clear return policies
- Start with a smaller purchase before committing to a large bulk
- Ask for close-up photos of a sample of cards

## A peek behind the curtain: unboxing a bulk lot
Before you cut the tape, set expectations. This is not the VIP lounge; it is a stack of potential. When you finally open, lay out the cards, separate the piles, and take your time.

## Comparing bulk with other options

- Singles from your local shop: If you only want a few specific cards, you might be better off buying singles. Bulk is the land of discovery, not the map to your treasure chest.
- Draft sets: If you are planning to draft, a bulk lot can serve as a cost-effective source of filler cards and sideboard staples.
