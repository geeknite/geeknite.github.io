## Design language and layout
Tile design is more than a pretty face in board games. Rue Morgue emphasizes irregular layouts that encourage creative route planning and back alley risk taking. You will find a mix of narrow corridors perfect for sandwiching a handful of survivors between two zombie swarms and larger room chunks that invite a more suspenseful approach to whether you open the door or blast through the wall. The balance between small rooms and long corridors creates a rhythm where players anticipate a hazard just around the next bend and you plan a few moves ahead to keep the team alive.

The art cues are tasteful rather than cartoonish. You will notice cracked tilework, exposed pipes, and dark staircases that hint at a history of neglect. The intention is to draw players into the atmosphere without distracting from the core tactical challenge of managing resources, line of sight, and the pace of the zombie surge.

## Gameplay impact and strategies
The actual impact on play is where Rue Morgue earns its keep. The set emphasizes routing and control, encouraging players to think beyond simply clearing rooms. Because the tiles present irregular shapes, you will rarely be able to predict the exact path of every zombie. Instead you create a plan for the earliest rounds and then improvise as the board reveals new bottlenecks and new threats. The tactical mind games are as much about positioning survivors as they are about calculating the probability of success against a stubborn horde.

Strategic tips for making the most of Rue Morgue:
- secure a defensive choke point early on, ideally at a door that can funnel zombies through a kill zone
- exploit the corner pieces to place survivors where they can cover multiple directions with ranged or melee support
- use doors strategically to force zombies to split their approach and create opportunities for coordinated eliminations
- keep an eye on objective placements and adapt routes to preserve critical survivors while maintaining grid control

If you run a small squad, Rue Morgue shines when you adopt a tempo that lets you balance aggressive exploration with careful retreat to safety. For larger groups, the tile variety adds a layer of complexity that can slow down the zombie flood just enough to keep tension high without grinding to a halt.
