## Installation and Setup

One of the biggest selling points of clamp-on designs is the absence of drilling and permanent fixtures. The VEVOR panel lives up to that promise for most standard desks. The installation process is straightforward and beginner-friendly enough to turn a half-committed DIYer into a hero without requiring a toolbox or a degree in structural engineering. The basics:

- Position the panel on the desk edge where you want privacy and glare reduction.
- Use the clamp mechanism to secure one or two clamps, depending on desk thickness and personal preference.
- If your desk is particularly glossy or unusually shaped, you may want to adjust the angle to maximize privacy and minimize any accidental tilt that could cause your panel to become a door in a windstorm.

The clamp-on approach means you can remove the panel with minimal fuss when you need to reconfigure your workspace or move to a different desk entirely. No wall drilling, no large holes, just a couple of clamps and a panel that behaves like a polite roommate: present, helpful, but not clingy.

## Cleaning and Maintenance

PET panels are generally forgiving when it comes to cleaning. A mild soap solution and a soft cloth are usually enough to wipe away fingerprints, coffee splashes, and the occasional sneeze (yep, we tested that too). Avoid abrasive cleaners or sharp implements that could scratch the surface. The beauty of the setup is that it is simple to maintain; if the panel starts to look a little dull after months of use, a quick wipe-down can restore clarity and color without the need for professional cleaning crews or a replacement panel.

If you use dry-erase markers or high-gloss accessories near the panel, be mindful of potential staining. It is not a whiteboard, so erase marks on the panel are best treated as a surface for minimal writing rather than a canvas for your next masterpiece. For regular cleaning, a quick pass with a damp microfiber cloth followed by a dry microfiber wipe does wonders. If you plan to mount the panel for extended periods, check periodically that the clamps maintain their grip. A loose grip can lead to wobble, which is not just annoying but can affect your monitor spacing and cable management subtly over time.
