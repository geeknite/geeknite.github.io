## Setup and Core Mechanics (What Changes with Access+)
Dixit is famously approachable: each round, one player is the storyteller, describes a card with a sentence or phrase, and others submit cards that they think fit the description. The group then guesses which card belongs to the storyteller. Access+ builds on that by offering more cards and a few optional scoring rules designed to vary pacing and encourage different storytelling strategies.

The Italian edition follows the same core mechanics. A round typically takes about 15 to 25 minutes depending on group size and how aggressively you describe your card. With Access+, you’ll experience slightly longer or shorter rounds depending on how you employ the new prompts and the additional cards. The new prompts bring more variety, with descriptions that lean into dream logic rather than literal storytelling. This makes some rounds feel like a whispered riddle shared between a poet and a goblin on a park bench.

A strong suit of Access+ is the flexibility it offers for different player counts. With more cards, you can keep an evening going with a larger group by rotating the storyteller role in a less predictable pattern. This variety makes it simultaneously easier to teach and harder to master, which is exactly what a good party game should be doing when it grows up.

## Quick Start Guide (for impatient players)
- Step 1: Shuffle the new deck and place it within easy reach. The more cards the merrier—your future storytelling decisions will thank you.
- Step 2: Choose a storyteller. They look at their card and craft a short, evocative clue in Italian. It can be a single word, a phrase, or a little metaphor—whatever feels right and not too spoiler-y.
- Step 3: Each other player selects one card from their hand that they think best matches the clue and places it face down. The storyteller’s card is added to the mix.
- Step 4: All cards are revealed. Players guess which card is the storyteller’s. Points are awarded for guessing correctly and for others who pick the storyteller’s card when appropriate.
- Step 5: Repeat with a new storyteller. Use the new prompt pool to keep creativity flowing.
- Step 6: When a player reaches the target score, celebrate with tea, laughter, and at least one lingering question about what that story was really about.

Tips for smooth play:
- Set a time limit per prompt to maintain rhythm.
- Encourage playful exaggeration in descriptions to maximize the fun factor.
- Use the Italian rulebook as a quick reference during the first few rounds; you’ll memorize the flow quickly after that.
- If you want extra chaos, mix Access+ with other Dixit Universe packs and let the prompts collide in glorious fashion.
