## What is Phase 10

Phase 10 is a card game designed around ten rounds, each with a unique objective that must be completed before a player can advance. The game uses a standard deck of 108 cards: numbers 1 through 12 in four colors, plus five wild cards and eight skip cards that inject a tactical layer into the personal race to complete each phase. The real hook is the phase progression: players do not simply try to get the highest score but to complete a specific combination of cards that matches the current phase s requirements. The twist comes from the timing, the table talk, and the occasional betrayals that feel surprisingly affectionate in a family setting.

## How to Play Phase 10

### Setup

- Shuffle the deck and deal 10 cards to each player. The remaining deck becomes the draw pile, and the top card is flipped to form the discard pile. The first player s objective is to complete the current phase s requirement by using the cards in hand, potentially mixing in cards drawn from the pile or picked from the discard pile. 
- Phases are specific: you do not just aim to collect cards, you aim to arrange them into the exact pattern required for the phase. 
- The first phase requires two sets of three cards each. If a player can lay down this pattern, they can advance to the next phase while others continue trying to keep their hands in motion. 
- If you finish your hand by laying down the phase and put the rest of your cards into your own personal board to reduce clutter, you will draw back up to ten cards and start the next round with a new phase for everyone who has finished and who is ready to face new challenges.

### The Rules in a Nutshell

- On your turn you must draw a card either from the top of the draw pile or the top card of the discard pile. 
- After drawing, you may lay down a phase if you have the required cards. You can also add cards to existing phase combinations on the table as long as you still have the ability to complete your own phase this turn. 
- You may discard one card at the end of your turn. Discards matter because they shape the late game possibilities for the next player. 
- If you cannot go out, your hand continues to the next round with increased pressure, since the next phase will be more challenging than the last.
- The goal is to be the first to complete all ten phases, thereby achieving victory. If more than one player completes the final phase in the same round, the player with the lowest total score among those who completed wins the game.

## Variants and House Rules

If you want to push Phase 10 beyond the base rules, there are a few house rules that can spice things up without breaking the core structure. Here are a few ideas that we have tested with friends and family:

- Speed Phase: limit time for each turn with a sand timer to keep the pace brisk. This adds pressure and reduces analysis paralysis.
- Reversal Round: after each round, swap the top one or two cards from the discard pile with the bottom of the draw deck, creating new possibilities for the next round.
- Wilds on Demand: allow players to declare a wild card for a single turn in exchange for a penalty later in the round. It adds a layer of risk management that can be very satisfying when pulled off correctly.
- Family Mode: award extra points for helping another player complete a phase if you are behind. This fosters teamwork and keeps the mood light even when competition heats up.

These variations are optional, but they can breathe new life into a familiar game. The key is to agree on a couple of rules before the first round and to keep the rest of the table in the loop so that everyone enjoys the added tension rather than feeling blindsided.
