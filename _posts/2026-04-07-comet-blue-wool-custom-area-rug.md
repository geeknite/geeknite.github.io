## Materials, build, and feel
### Wool composition and texture
The rug’s core is wool, a fiber beloved by designers for its warmth, softness, and natural resilience. Wool fibers have crimp that creates bounce and loft, resulting in a plush surface that isn’t so deep you’ll risk tripping on it—unless you’re intentionally going for dramatic slow-motion runway vibes. Wool also handles foot traffic well and tends to wear gracefully over time. Expect a comfortable, cushiony feel underfoot that still supports your stance during sofa battles or intense video game boss fights.

### Pile height and backing
Most wool rugs in this category sit in a medium-pile range, offering a balance between softness and ease of vacuuming. While the exact pile height isn’t always listed, the intent is a feel-good underfoot experience without turning maintenance into a gym routine. The backing typically uses a durable latex or cotton blend to maintain stability and reduce slippage on various floor surfaces. That means your decorative mission is not foiled by a stray rug tug during a dramatic movement scene in your living room.

### Color and design sensibility
The Comet Blue evokes a deep blue with celestial accents—imagine stardust trailing across a night-sky field. In this design direction, the rug pairs well with mid-century modern furniture, sleek black frames, and metallic accents. If you’re into a maximalist vibe, you can layer it with throw blankets in complementary hues or run a second rug nearby to create a galactic grid on your floor. ### Durability and maintenance expectations
Wool is naturally resilient, but like any natural fiber, it benefits from proper care. Routine vacuuming with a gentle setting helps remove surface dust and crumbs. It’s wise to rotate the rug every so often to promote even wear, especially in high-traffic areas. For stains, the recommended approach is to blot (not rub) immediately, use a mild detergent recommended for wool, and let it dry completely before resuming use. If you have pets, a quick inspection after any accident is a good habit—a little prompt care goes a long way toward preserving color and texture.

### Sustainability angle
Wool rugs are often prized for their natural, renewable fiber. If you care about eco-friendly choices, wool tends to be durable and biodegradable, which can be a nice counterpoint to synthetic fibers that shed microplastics. If you’re curious about the broader environmental footprint, you can explore wool’s lifecycle in general or look for certifications from reputable textile groups. This isn’t a sermon on sustainability, just a nudge toward a product that aligns with a greener, easier-to-care-for lifestyle.

## Care, cleaning, and care tips
### Routine care
- Vacuum on a low setting with a brush attachment; avoid aggressive agitation that can loosen wool fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to equalize wear.
- Use rug pads to prevent slipping and to extend the rug’s life.

### Spills and stains
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth—do not rub, as that can drive the liquid deeper into the fibers.
- For minor stains, a wool-friendly cleaner or a mixture of mild soap and water can work. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first.
- For stubborn stains, consider consulting a professional rug cleaner who specializes in wool fibers. Quick action minimizes the risk of permanent discoloration.

### Deep cleaning and maintenance routine
- Depending on foot traffic, plan a light cleaning schedule a couple of times a year.
- Avoid bleach-based cleaners that can degrade wool fibers and alter color.
- If you have pets, address hair and dander with a gentle vacuum routine and consider a stain guard option if offered by the retailer.

### Storage when needed
If you need to store the rug temporarily, roll it (don’t fold it) and protect it with a breathable fabric cover. Store in a dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve color and texture.
