## The Guest of Honor: Edouard Guiton
Edouard Guiton brings a sensibility to color, texture, and character silhouette that is unmistakable once you have seen it. In this box, his influence manifests in several layers: the way survivors are drawn to tell a story at the card edge, the kind of zombies that look more cunning than terrifying, and the occasional nod to a scene you might recognize from his other works. The art style is not about hyper-realism; it is about readability at a gaming table under mixed lighting, quick emotion cues, and the ability to tell a story at a glance.

One of the most satisfying aspects is how the box uses Edouard Guiton's palette to guide player expectations. The survivors wear armor pieces and clothing items that pop with contrast, allowing your eyes to quickly identify your character in the heat of the moment. When you flip a card and see a new action, the color accents act like a cheat sheet: red means danger, blue means calm planning, and yellow hints at improvisational ingenuity. This is not a new mechanic so much as a new way to read the board, which is a subtle but significant improvement to the pace of play.
