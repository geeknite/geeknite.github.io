## How to Play: A Lightweight Recap with Marvel Flair
If you’ve played Codenames before, you know the rhythm. If you’re new, you’ll catch on in roughly the time it takes to shout a one-word clue with a dramatic flourish. The Marvel Edition preserves the two-team structure, the spymaster role, and the core word-guessing mechanic, but injects a few thematic flourishes that can tilt the pacing and the diplomacy on display.

### Setup
- Lay out 25 word cards in a 5x5 grid. The cards are Marvel-themed in the sense that many words are tied to characters, powers, or cinematic concepts. The art behind each word is intentionally decorative, giving players a beachhead for their clues.
- Randomly select red and blue team markers and place the assassin marker in the standard position. The assassin card adds a risk element that keeps players from sprinting headlong into the red zone of the table.
- The spymasters from each team sit across, with the rest of the players forming two teams. If you’re playing with a larger group, you can rotate spymasters or appoint a rotating moderator to keep track of clues and timers.

### Objective
The goal is simple: contact all of your team’s words before the other team does, while avoiding the assassin. The Marvel overlay adds a few thematic triggers to safe-word choices that feel satisfying when they land. A good clue can be a single word like “Stark” that points to multiple red or blue words on the board, rewarding both shared knowledge and shared jokes.

### Gameplay Flow
- The spymaster for each team gives a one-word clue followed by a number. The clue must connect to as many target words on the board as the number indicates. For example, a clue like Stark 2 might hint at a couple of red words such as “Iron” or “Armor” if those align with the red team’s targets.
- Team members discuss, debate, and settle on the words they think match the clue. Once a word is guessed, the word card is revealed and the turn ends if an incorrect word is picked, the assassin is revealed, or the word is a correct target.
- The turn then passes to the other team, continuing until one side claims all their words or the assassin rears its red-hooded head.

### Word Cards and Heroes
A notable aspect of this edition is how frequently a given clue cascades into multiple relevant words. Marvel-themed words often come with a built-in semantic network—people will immediately draw associations like hero names, tech jargon, or cinematic moments—that can be exploited by a skilled spymaster. The tension curve is heightened by the ever-present possibility of an accidental trip into the assassin pool, which triggers an immediate ping of panic across the table. The drama is real, the laughter is loud, and the frustration is merciless in the best possible ways.

### Special Rules and Variants
Some houses rules you can try to keep the energy up:
- Quickstart mode: limit new spymaster instructions to 30 seconds to accelerate the action for a party night.
- Hero spotlight: after a round, the team with fewer points must designate a hero from the board to be the MVP of the game, and the group must do a quick 30-second heroic pose to celebrate. Yes, this happens more often than you’d expect.
- Cinematic timer: use a 60-second countdown to simulate a post-credits stinger that urges players to hurry their brainstorming before the end credits roll.

For players who crave deeper strategic nuance, the Marvel Edition adds a few subtle wrinkles. Clues tied to broader Marvel lore can allow for more multi-word connections, but they can also misfire if the table isn’t tuned into the same references. This is a clarifying reminder that you don’t need to be a cinephile to enjoy the game; you just need to be willing to laugh at yourself when your clue lands on a word you hadn’t considered.

## Strategy and Tips for Max Enjoyment
Here’s a compact bag of meta-level strategies that can elevate your evenings with the Marvel Edition:
- Master the art of the mini theme: a clue that loosely ties a handful of red words can pay dividends beyond the number called. A little narrative around the clue makes the reveal feel earned rather than lucky.
- Read the table before you clue: if your team is fresh or less confident, a simpler clue with a smaller number can reduce chaotic guessing. Only confident groups should chase bells-and-whistles multi-word play.
- Use the environment: if you’re in a dimly lit living room with a superhero poster, lean into it. The atmosphere can become the real advantage as players scribble notes and shout guesses with dramatic flair.
- Don’t fear the assassin, fear the silence: the presence of an assassin card introduces meaningful risk. A well-timed, cautious approach can be more valuable than a reckless dive into the red zone.
- Balance your references: you want enough cultural baggage to connect multiple words, but not so many obscure references that your team stares blankly and glances at the clock.
- Leverage the Marvel palate: the words you guess can evoke a character’s arc or a cinematic moment. When a clue leads to two or three of these, celebrate with a little fanfare—because nothing says victory like a shared wink across the table.
