### Care, maintenance, and the practical side of wool

Caring for wool requires a bit more nuance than a stubborn salt-and-pepper stain on a polyester rug. Wool resists staining better than some synthetics, but it’s not a free pass for a coffee river in the middle of a Monday. Here are some practical tips to keep Cliffwood green looking crisp:

- Vacuum regularly with a beater brush on low or without a rotating brush to avoid fiber damage. Wool can handle it; you just don’t want to pummel it like a drum solo.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper into the fibers.
- For spots, a mild detergent solution or a professional wool rug cleaner is recommended. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.
- Rotate periodically to avoid uneven wear in high-traffic zones. Your rug should age like fine wine, not like a bowling ball.
- Consider a rug pad. A good pad reduces slipping, extends life, and makes vacuuming easier by preventing bunching under the couch. Pad plus rug equals silent floor serenity.

### The practical side of choosing between custom size and 8x10: a decision matrix

- Room geometry: Rooms with alcoves or asymmetrical layouts benefit from a custom size to fit perfectly. If your room has a more conventional rectangle, 8x10 is typically sufficient.
- Seating plan: If your sofa and chairs hug the rug’s edges, a custom size helps ensure all furniture sits comfortably with the rug underfoot. If your seating sits on a standard rectangle rug, 8x10 should be plenty.
- Traffic and maintenance: The bigger the rug, the more surface area you cover, which can impact cleaning time. A custom rug that’s well-tailored to the space reduces the awkward corners where dust bunnies gather.
- Budget: Custom sizes can be more expensive due to bespoke production. If budget is a concern, the 8x10 option offers a robust compromise between coverage and cost.

Ultimately, the choice should reflect how your space lives. Do you host large gatherings where everyone needs a soft landing mat? - Q: Is wool safe for homes with pets? A: Yes, wool can be pet-friendly with proper maintenance; it resists staining better than many synthetics and can recover from minor snags with proper care.
- Q: How often should I rotate the rug? A: Every 6–12 months, depending on traffic and sun exposure. This helps prevent uneven fading and wear patterns.
- Q: Can I clean it myself or should I hire a pro? A: Light cleaning and spot treatment are typically fine at home, but consider professional cleaning for deeper stains or odor concerns.
