## Setup and Rules: What Changes (If Any) in UNO Moana 2?
UNO has a famously simple setup: shuffle, deal seven cards to each player, place the remainder face down, flip the top card to start the discard pile, and you’re in business. The Moana 2 edition largely keeps that structure, but adds a few thematic touches:

- The Wild Draw Four cards remain a core mechanic, but the visuals are Moana-inspired. If you’re playing with younger kids, you can spin it as “Maui’s rock trick” or “Moana’s ocean current” to keep the mood light.
- Some versions of the Moana 2 deck rework the color palette for readability on a bright table. If the used condition of your copy shows wear on specific color blocks, you might notice that red and blue are still easy to tell apart, which is crucial during sprint rounds.
- The tropical artwork does not alter the core probabilities or the turn order; it just changes the vibe. Your house rules still apply: draw cards when you can’t play, call UNO when you have one card left, and don’t forget to shout with enthusiasm.

For newcomers: UNO is a game of matching either color or number and strategically deploying action cards to disrupt opponents. MOANA 2 adds a dash of storytelling flair to those moments—your “Skip” card might be a scene transition to a wind-swept ocean moment, which is adorable and potentially confusing for players who are trying to count correctly, but oh well, we’re here for the vibe and the chaos.

### Setup Quick Guide for New Players
1. Shuffle the Moana 2 deck.
2. Deal seven cards to each player.
3. Place the remaining deck face down to form the draw pile.
4. Flip the top card to start the discard pile. If it’s a special card, follow its action immediately.
5. The player to the left goes first.
6. On your turn, you must match either the number or the color, or play a Wild card. If you can’t, you draw a card.
7. When you have one card, shout UNO. If another player catches you not saying UNO before your next turn, you draw two cards.
8. The first player to discard all cards wins.

That’s the baseline; the Moana 2 flavor comes from card art and some thematic language people may choose to add when narrating their plays.
