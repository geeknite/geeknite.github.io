## How to play the Lot Of 2: setup and basics
Exploding Kittens is a fairly simple game to pick up, which is exactly why it has aged so well in party settings. With two complete decks, you can mix and match players between rounds or keep things in-house with a two-team dynamic. Here is a quick refresher on the core flow:

- The setup is quick: shuffle each deck, deal a few Defuse cards to each player, then place the Exploding Kitten cards into the appropriate piles. The rest of the deck forms the draw pile.
- On your turn, you draw a card. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you have a Defuse card, you can play that Defuse to negate the explosion and shuffle the kitten back into the deck at a location of your choosing. If you don’t have a Defuse, you are out (for that round) unless some other magical card saves you. In the two-pack scenario, the risk is amplified because the other deck is quietly waiting to avenge you.
- Along the way, you can play various action cards to affect other players or the deck, from See the Future (peek at upcoming cards) to Shuffle (disrupt the order of fate) and Skip (pass your draw to the next person). The whole point is to stay alive long enough to torment your friends with ridiculous puns and mock-cat memes.

In short, two copies mean more opportunities to chain reactions, more dramatic near-misses, and more chances to accuse your friends of stacking the deck while you still have a smug smile on your face. It also means your table can have multiple rounds in a single night, which is perfect if your friends' attention spans are as short as a kitten’s memory of last week’s snack.

## Strategy, luck, and the role of chance
A lot of Exploding Kittens success is the rollercoaster of luck. You can be dealing Defuse cards like a benevolent magician and still be one bad shuffle away from a dramatic explosion. The Lot Of 2 approach nudges you toward more dynamic decisions because you have two decks to watch, two potential bomb occurrences, and twice the social pressure of bluffing and calling out your friends' strategies.

That said, there are a few subtle strategies worth remembering:

- When in doubt, defend your Defuse stock. A single Defuse can save you in a clutch moment, so don’t waste it on a safer round unless you’re truly confident you’ll survive the next draw.
- See the Future is your friend if you’re trying to time your risk. Peeking at upcoming cards gives you foresight into when to take a risk and when to pass. It’s a little meta, but it’s also wildly satisfying when you predict a cascade of exploded kittens and watch the table react in chorus.
- Be mindful of players who hoard Attack cards. If you don’t handle these folks early, you might end up with a domino effect where every turn becomes a threat you can’t ignore.
- The two-pack dynamic invites you to experiment with rotations. Run a quick two-round mini-tournament, swap partners, or let a guest host one of the rounds. It’s a chance to test social tactics as much as card tactics.

Remember: luck plays a big role, but social play and timing will win you more games than pure card strength. The more you practice with two decks, the better you’ll become at reading the room and deciding whether to risk a stack of See the Future cards or simply take a safe Skip to the next person.
