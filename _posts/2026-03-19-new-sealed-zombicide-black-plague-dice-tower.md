## Rules, setup, and gameplay rhythm
Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative board game for 1–6 players that pitches you into a medieval plague-infested city. The objective is to complete mission goals while eliminating or avoiding hordes of undead and other nasties. The core loop is simple on a first pass: players act in turn, moving their survivors on a grid-based board, performing actions like moving, attacking, searching for gear, or using special abilities. The zombies respond in the most inexorable way imaginable: the more you fail to clear them in a given turn, the more you have to face on the next. It is a satisfying push-your-luck game where decisions about where to explore and when to hoard gear matter.

The learning curve is not punishing, but there is a fair amount of tooling: player boards, card decks, weapon stats, and a mission deck that determines the path for each scenario. The rulebook is well laid-out, with icons to teach new players quickly, and the included quick-start guide is a godsend for those who want to jump in with minimal friction. One of the typical pitfalls of Zombicide is understanding the spawn rules and the order of play for combat; Black Plague does a solid job of keeping this readable while still delivering the satisfying tension of a late-game scramble. For new players, I recommend a practice scenario that focuses on learning the turn sequence before jumping into a full mission. For the seasoned board gamer, this title scales well with more players and can handle a longer, more cinematic session with crewed narrative commitments.

### A closer look at the action economy
The core mechanic hinges on the action economy, where survivors can perform a couple of actions per turn. Common actions include move, search, open door, attack, or use an equipped item. The weapon and armor cards add variety to your approach, letting you specialize your survivors into tank, rogue hunter, or mage-of-fire (yes, the magic users exist in this plague). The zombies are guided by simple AI rules: when they get a chance, they will converge on the loudest noise, then pile into the nearest living target until the board becomes a maelstrom of red markers. The tension escalates as more zombies spawn from the board edges or from special rooms, turning a quiet exploration into a frantic sprint for the door as the clock ticks toward the inevitable end of the scenario.

For players who enjoy puzzle-like planning, the mission cards offer a cherry-picked set of objectives, with varying degrees of difficulty and success conditions. Some missions emphasize rescue, others require collecting a certain number of loot tokens or triggering a chain of events that will unlock the exit. The variety helps prevent fatigue across a campaign, and the modular board layout ensures you rarely face identical maps twice in a row.
