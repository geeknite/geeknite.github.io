## How to care for your sealed decks

Even though these decks arrive sealed, it is wise to consider how you store them for longevity. Keep them away from direct sunlight, avoid humidity spikes that can cause warping or retensioning of the stock, and avoid exposing the tuck boxes to rough handling that could lead to cosmetic scuffs. If you intend to open and use one deck while preserving the other as a pristine keepsake, this is a sensible approach. For players who care about shelf life, you can place the sealed decks in a cool, dry closet or a display case that protects them from dust while still keeping them accessible for a late night game session.

For the best shuffles and a consistent lay, you might want to let your new decks rest in their packaging for a few days to acclimate to your environment. A little patience goes a long way toward ensuring that the first shuffle remains smooth and predictable, which makes your practice sessions more productive and less frustrating.
