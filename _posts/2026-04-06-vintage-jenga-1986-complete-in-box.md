## Preservation, Care, and Gentle Restoration

Vintage wooden toys share a secret: they respond to care in ways that plastic can only dream of. Here are best practices for keeping your 1986 Jenga in fighting shape for many more family game nights:

- Clean blocks with a dry microfiber cloth. Avoid water or harsh solvents that could raise the grain or cause swelling.
- If blocks have a rough edge or slightly sticky feel, a light palm rub can ease roughness. If you’re feeling fancy, a small amount of beeswax rubbed on each block can improve glide without compromising the wood’s breathability.
- Store in a dry environment. Wood hates humidity more than a cat hates baths. A dehumidifier in the game room can keep the blocks from warping and the box from turning into a small aquarium for mold.
- Inspect the box for splits or loose joints. A little glue and clamp time can extend the life of your vintage packaging, but document any restoration work for accuracy in your listing if you ever sell.

For the truly devoted, you can consider light sanding of blocks to restore uniform smoothness. This is optional and requires patience. The goal isn’t perfection; it’s preserving the playing feel while maintaining a hint of that retro texture that makes each move feel special.
