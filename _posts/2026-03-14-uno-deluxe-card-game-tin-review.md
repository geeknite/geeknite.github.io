## Setup and First Turn: A Quick Start Guide

UNO is famously simple to learn, and Deluxe aims to preserve that simplicity while offering a slightly more durable package. Here is a quick run-through of getting started with the tin edition:

- Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player.
- Place the remaining deck in the center as a draw pile.
- Flip the top card to start the discard pile. If this card is an action card that would impact the first move, you may reinterpret the rules for the opening hand or simply redraw. The rule sheet typically covers this scenario.
- The first player matches either the color or the number of the top discard card, or plays a Wild or Wild Draw Four according to the rules. The exact handling of Wild cards is typically explained on the included sheet, and most families adopt a straightforward approach: Wild lets you choose the color for the next turn.
- When a player has one card left, they must shout UNO. If another player calls UNO before them, the original player may have to draw cards as a penalty according to the rules, which adds that tiny spark of chaotic justice to the game.

If you are new to UNO, you might get tripped up by the Draw Four rule in some variants. The standard rule is that when you play Draw Four, you not only draw four cards yourself on the next turn but you also get the chance to change the color. The Deluxe edition keeps the core idea intact, so you can appreciate the nostalgia while still worrying about your neighbor’s last-minute color choice.

## Rules: A Quick Refresher and House Rules

UNO rules can be deceptively simple or gloriously chaotic, depending on how you interpret the card deck and whether you decide to throw in a few house rules. The Deluxe Tin aims to preserve standard rules while making sure the instructions are easy to access on a regular basis. Here is a quick refresher for regular players:

- Match the color or number of the top card on the discard pile. If you can’t, draw a card from the draw pile.
- If you have one card left, you must shout UNO before your turn ends. If another player notices you forgot and calls you out before your next turn, you may be penalized with additional cards.
- Wild cards let you choose the color of play. Wild Draw Four cards let you reset the color and force the next player to draw four cards, with the caveat that the play should be legal under the declared color change (house rules may vary).

If you want to add some extra spice, consider subtle house rules that are easy to enforce, such as a soft rule that players must declare the color of the next play. Or you can implement a soft “no-late-UNO” rule that ensures the UNO shout is heard by all. These small changes can help prevent the round from stalling and keep the energy up during a longer night.

Simple care is all you need. Here are a few practical tips to keep your UNO Deluxe Tin in top shape:
