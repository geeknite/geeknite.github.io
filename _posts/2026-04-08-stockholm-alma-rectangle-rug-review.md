## Durability and Care: built to survive your weekday chaos
### Wear and flattening tendencies
Like most low-pile rugs, Alma may shed a minimal amount of loose fibers during the initial weeks. Do not panic; it’s typically a seasonal rite of passage that smooths out as fibers settle. With regular vacuuming using a suction-only setting (no beater brush), you’ll keep the pile looking even and avoid that dreaded “line of glory” where the vacuum has left a perfect track across your rug.

### Cleaning basics
Spills? Blot, don’t rub. A damp cloth or paper towel tends to work best for most common liquids. For tougher stains: a mild detergent diluted in water can help, but test in an inconspicuous corner first. If you’re dealing with pet accidents or stubborn grime, you might want to consider a targeted rug cleaner designed for synthetic fibers. The goal is to avoid saturating the backing and promoting moldy drama in the subfloor. A quick note: always let the rug dry completely before walking on it with wet socks, or you’ll create a spa-like mud treatment for your toes.

### Backing and stability
The backing on the Alma rug adds grip, helping to keep it from creeping across slick floors. This is a tiny, unsung feature that makes a big difference if you have a busy family room or a vacuum-loving robot pet—yes, those exist in households that refuse to accept dust as a personality trait. If your floors are unusually slippery, consider adding a non-slip pad beneath to maximize grip and minimize edge curling.

## Cleaning and Maintenance: practical routines for real life
### Routine care
Vacuum weekly on a low setting to keep fibers upright and avoid matting. Rotate the rug 180 degrees every few months to even out wear patterns, particularly in high-traffic zones or if a window-facing wall has you tracking in more sunlight than you intend. A little rotation goes a long way in keeping the pile looking balanced and the color evenly distributed.

### Spot treatments and deeper cleans
For small spills, blot immediately with a clean cloth. If something stubborn sticks around, use a mild cleaning solution on a hidden corner first to ensure the color doesn’t bleed or fade. For larger cleanups, you might want to rent or borrow a carpet cleaner or hire a professional to give it a thorough refresh. The key is to avoid soaking the backing; soggy rugs invite a mood far less cozy and a floor that smells suspiciously like a science experiment gone wrong.

### Longevity tips
Rotate, vacuum, and avoid direct sunlight for long periods—color fading is a slow, vindictive villain. If your space gets a lot of sun, consider window shades or curtains to preserve the rug’s original tone. The Alma’s colorway is fairly forgiving, but nothing ages as gracefully as a rug that spends its life basking in natural light like a cat in a sunbeam—until it doesn’t.
