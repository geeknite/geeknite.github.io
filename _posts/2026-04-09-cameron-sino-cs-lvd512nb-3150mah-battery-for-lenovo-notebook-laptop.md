Battery health and cycle life
Replacement packs typically perform in the neighborhood of 300-500 full charge-discharge cycles before noticeable capacity fade. Expect a modest degradation curve if you treat the pack well and avoid extreme temperatures. If you routinely expose the notebook to 40C or higher, you may be trimming cycle life at a faster rate. A safer operating range for most Li-ion packs is roughly 20-30C during operation, with storage temps ideally between 0 and 25C when the device is not in use. In practical terms, keep the notebook out of direct sun on a hot day, and don’t leave it inside a car where the seats become a personal sauna.

Notes on maintenance and longevity
To get the most out of any replacement pack, a few habits help:
- Calibrate after the first few cycles to set the new battery health metrics in your notebook. This usually means a full discharge then a full recharge.
- Keep the device cool. If you’re a heat magnet, consider a laptop cooling pad. The combination of airflow and a cooler environment can extend the life of your pack dramatically.
- Avoid deep discharges; modern packs do not like being drained to 0% unless you enjoy the suspense of a device that refuses to wake up.
- Store at mid-range charge when not in use for long periods. A stranded battery in storage is a sad battery.
