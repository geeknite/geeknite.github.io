## How to play two players (expanded rules and vibe)
The two-player edition tightens the orchestration so you’re always one heartbeat away from either a ridiculous laugh or a tragic faceplant. Here’s a fuller walk-through with more flavor and fewer loopholes.

### Setup
- Shuffle the deck and deal five cards to each player.
- Place the remaining deck in the center as the draw pile.
- Randomly decide who goes first. Honest bragging rights optional but recommended.
- Keep Defuse cards handy; they are your memory of glory when someone threatens your day.
- Optional house rule: agree on a one-bite limit for the opponent to bluff before you call their bluff.

### Turn structure (the rhythm of chaos)
A turn is straightforward but there’s room for mind games to flourish:
1) Draw a card from the deck. If it’s an Exploding Kitten and you can’t Defuse, you’re not just out of the game—you’re out of dignity.
2) If you drew a Defuse, you keep it and usually pat yourself on the back for outlasting your own chaos.
3) You may play one card from your hand before or after drawing, depending on the card. Attack, See the Future, or a sneaky Nope if you want to poke the bear.
4) If your opponent’s turn starts and they drew an Exploding Kitten, they must either use a Defuse or accept their doom. Doom can sometimes be deferred by shenanigans, misdirection, and a hair-raising counterplay.

### Endgame
The end occurs when a player draws an Exploding Kitten without a Defuse. The other player wins, proving that they’ve mastered the sacred art of tempting fate and laughing at misfortune. In two-player mode, the end tends to come faster than the multi-player chaos—though the laughs are just as loud and the trash talk just as petty.

## Strategy and player experience (the real meat)
Luck is in play, but there is room for real strategy here. The core is card management and reading your opponent’s tells. You want to ensure you have Defuse cards when you need them most, but you also want to bluff your way into making your opponent nervous about your next move. See the Future cards are a tempo tool that can tilt momentum, while Attack and Nope cards provide counterplay that can swing a round in an instant.

### Practical tips for two players (expanded toolkit)
- Track what you’ve used: If you have multiple Defuses left, you can take calculated risks with your draw pile knowing you have a safety net.
- See the Future strategically: You don’t need to reveal the future every turn; a timely glimpse can force your opponent to react in ways that give you the edge.
- Bluff with Attack: Forcing your opponent to draw can create pressure even if you don’t have an immediate winning plan in hand.
- Don’t overcommit to one tactic: Sometimes the best move is to mix things up and surprise your rival with an unexpected play that disrupts their intended path.
- Mind the tempo: In two-player games, tempo is currency. If you can burn a card that slows your opponent’s momentum while preserving your own, you’ve essentially won half the duel.
