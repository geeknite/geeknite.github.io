## What is Exploding Kittens Deluxe Edition?

Exploding Kittens is a fast-paced party game created by Elan Lee and The Oatmeal (Matthew Inman). In the Deluxe Edition you’re not just getting a deck of cards; you’re getting a portable chaos factory designed to turn friendly game night into chaotic theater with a purring soundtrack in the background (okay, no soundtrack, but your brain’s soundtrack will do the trick). The core idea is simple: don’t explode. You have Defuse cards to avert disaster. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and have no Defuse, you’re out. Last kitten standing wins the internet and bragging rights.

The Deluxe packaging features premium components, art upgrades, and maybe a few extra silly cards to keep you on your toes. It’s premium impulse-buy territory for folks who treat their living room as a tiny convention and who could use a little more epic cat chaos in their weekly routines.

## How to play at a high level

The objective is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten. Each player starts with a few Defuse cards to set off the doom safely (for now). The deck combines Exploding Kitten cards, Defuse cards, and a bouquet of action cards that tilt the odds in unpredictable directions.

Turn structure is simple: draw a card from the deck, perform any actions called for by the card you drew, and then pass to the next player. Action cards like Shuffle, See the Future, Attack, and See the Future keep the flow lively and the table anxious in equal measure.

A crucial concept is Defuse cards. They are your lifeline. If you don’t have one when you draw an Exploding Kitten, you’re out of the running. If you do have a Defuse, you can defuse the kitten and place it back into the deck in a position that will be found by the next player. This can be pure luck or a deviously clever ploy depending on your mood and your appetite for chaos.

## Strategy tips and tricks

- Always value the Defuse cards; you don’t want to be the last person with no defuser when the kitten appears.
- Use See the Future to peek at the next few cards, but don’t become paralyzed by possibilities; sometimes misdirection and miscommunication are the best tools you’ve got.
- Shuffle strategically; you can swap or rearrange the top of the deck to maximize your survival or to manufacture spectacular doom for someone else.
- Attack cards can create chaos that benefits you or costs you dearly; use them at the right moment to force others to draw.

If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, these tips will help you survive your first rounds. If you’re returning, you know the rhythm: you survive a handful of rounds by luck and then gather a few house rules to keep things spicy.

### House rules (optional but highly recommended)
- The Quiet Page rule: every time a card is played that changes the turn order, the table must collectively applaud the dramatic moment for five seconds. This injects extra chaos and hilarious commentary, especially if someone tries a dramatic “defuse” pose.
- The Narrator Rule: pick one player to narrate the outcomes of all dramatic moments in an over-the-top voice. It typically lasts longer than the game itself and ends with laughter, a few inside jokes, and someone realizing they forgot to shuffle properly.
- The Quick Draw variant: shuffle the deck after every big blunder; this makes near-misses feel like cinematic cliffhangers.
