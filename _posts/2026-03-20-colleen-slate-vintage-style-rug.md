## Use, Comfort, and Performance
### Comfort Underfoot
The rug’s pile is comfortable enough for barefoot strolls and occasional lounging, but it’s not so deep that it becomes a magnet for pet fur and dust bunnies to hide away like tiny villains in a dungeon. Regular vacuuming will keep shedding at bay, and a rug pad will improve both comfort and stability.

### Pet and Family Friendly?
Yes. The mix of fibers typically tolerates pet claws and occasional spills fairly well, especially if addressed promptly. For households with kids and pets, the distressed vintage pattern acts like camouflage for minor messes, while the rug’s texture gives a forgiving feel when kids tumble across it during play sessions.

### Cleaning and Maintenance
- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting. If your vacuum has a beater bar, consider turning it off to reduce fiber pull.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Avoid rubbing, which can push the liquid deeper and spread the stain.
- For light stains, a mild detergent solution (water + gentle soap) can help. Always test on a small inconspicuous area first.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and sun exposure.
- Avoid prolonged direct sunlight to minimize color fading. If possible, use curtains or blinds during peak sun hours to preserve color over time.

A rug that’s easy to keep clean is a rug you’ll actually keep, which is a huge win when your climate alternates between dusty and rainy sessions.
