## Setup tips: getting from box to game in under 15 minutes
The setup is pleasantly forgiving if you follow a simple step-by-step approach. Here is a quick practical rundown:

1) Open the box and lay out all components on a soft surface to avoid scratches. Check for any small parts and place them within arm reach.
2) Attach the legs if they are not already fixed. Most models feature a tool-free mechanism, but a basic screwdriver can speed things up if the fit is snug.
3) Verify the alignment of the rods and players. If the players feel loose, re-seat them, then rotate the rods to ensure they glide smoothly.
4) Switch to the 2-in-1 conversion mode. Use the alignment guides to position the panels and secure them with the included screws. A little tightening goes a long way here, especially if you are moving the table frequently.
5) Add the balls and the ball return channels. The ball guide should offer a predictable path from kickoff to goal without too many detours.
6) If you are playing on a flat surface indoors, you may want to place a non-slip mat underneath to reduce movement. It helps keep the action tight and the table stable.

## Maintenance: keeping the 2-in-1 engine humming
Like all good gadgets, this table rewards a modest maintenance routine. A few simple practices will extend the life of your investment and maintain performance:

- Wipe down the rods after use to remove fingerprints, oils, and the occasional crumb of victory cake left on the table during post-match celebrations. A soft dry cloth does the job well; avoid aggressive cleaners that could degrade finishes.
- Check the rod stoppers and screws every few weeks, especially if you are using the table a lot or moving it around. If you hear a squeak or feel a loose component, tighten and re-check alignment.
- Keep the table away from damp areas. The 94 cm chassis is robust, but moisture can encourage rust on metal parts and degrade the leg joints over time.
- For the 2-in-1 conversion, recheck that the conversion panels sit flush against the main surface. Gaps can alter ball trajectories and ruin your best shots.
