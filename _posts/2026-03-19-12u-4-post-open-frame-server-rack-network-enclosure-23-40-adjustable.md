## Setup and Installation

Unboxing reveals the rails, posts, and mounting hardware. The four-post design makes alignment easier than a cubicle maze.

1) Attach rails to the posts at the correct height for your devices (12U in this case).
2) Mount devices in order from bottom up to maintain stability; place heavier items lower to avoid tipping when someone in the office sneezes near the rack.
3) Route cables with attention to airflow. Use cable managers where possible and keep power and data wires separated.
4) Slide devices into place, adjust depth as needed, and admire your new, less-chaotic lab.

As you finish, you’ll realize it’s a tool first, a fashion statement second. You can brag about the clean looks of your cabling and the almost-scientific airflow your lab now breathes. It’s okay to smile at your own neatness; your Ethernet cables deserve it.

## Cable Management and Thermal Performance

Airflow is the unsung hero of rack design. An open-frame rack keeps air moving like a futuristic breeze through a data center, which is exactly what you want for gear that tends to run warmer than your patience in a meeting. The absence of doors means less stagnation, but you still need to be mindful of cable routing.

- Use vertical cable managers to keep bundles aligned and off the back rails.
- Separate power cabling from data cabling where possible to reduce EMI intersections.
- Bundle cords loosely to avoid restricting airflow; remember, you’re not trying to win a cable-tie art contest—your devices want to breathe.

This rack’s depth-adjustable rails help adapt to devices with deeper footprints, so you don’t have to crowd a 2U blade server into a space meant for a 1U switch and hope for the best.
