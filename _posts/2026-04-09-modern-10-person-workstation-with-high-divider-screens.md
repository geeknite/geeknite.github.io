## Design Overview
The ten-person configuration is not just a number; it is a philosophy. The system arranges ten desks in two staggered rows, each with access to a shared power rail and data backbone. The height of the work surface is adjustable, allowing teams to alternate between sit and stand during a long sprint without turning the room into a warehouse of ergonomic contortionists. The dividers are tall enough to give each user a sense of personal space but not so tall that you lose sight of your neighbor when you lean over to ask about their new favorite keyboard shortcut.

The divider screens are not the cheap acrylic you find in some budget setups. These are lightweight but rigid panels clad in a felt-like acoustic layer on the inside and a wipe-clean exterior on the outside. The acoustic layer is not a miracle material, but it does a respectable job of damping the typical desk chatter that happens when ten people discover the miracle of a new project management tool at 9:17 am.

The frame is built from powder-coated steel with a matte finish that resists fingerprints better than a superhero resists boredom. The desktops use a laminated veneer with a subtle texture that hides the inevitable coffee ring marks and the occasional stray doodle from a team brainstorming session. The colorways skew toward a modern industrial palette — cool grays, charcoal accents, and a few warm wood tones for those who insist their office feel like a real space rather than a lab bench.
