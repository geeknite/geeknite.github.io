## Overview

City of Mist is a game about playing as modern myths — investigators whose power is rooted in their legends, their nicknames, their mythos as the players call it. Fortune Row is an expansion that leans into two core ideas: capers and consequences. It invites you to run a misfit crew through a string of urban legends, all while dealing with the consequences of living in a city that blurs the boundary between myth and reality.

In Fortune Row you won't just chase clues; you'll chase leads that lead to heists, deals with supernatural powers, and a social dynamic where factions, not just players, decide the next scene. The expansion adds a new setting (Fortune Row itself, a stretch of street-level power brokerages and myth-haunted bars), new Investigator options (mythic callouts that affect social dynamics and a variety of new triggers for your power sets), and a robust set of mechanics focusing on risk, fortune, and the moral weight of being seen.

### What's in Fortune Row

Fortune Row isn't just a module; it's a fully fledged arc system that scales from a one-shot to a multi-session campaign. It includes:

- A new setting: Fortune Row itself. A district where fortunes are traded like street-level miracles and where the local power brokers are myths who forgot their manners.
- New character options: investigators can lean into a Mythos-Driven package that slots into the existing system. You may take a streetwise fortune reader, an information broker who gathers secrets like poker chips, or a mythic persuader who can tilt a crowd with a single sentence.
- An expanded move set: Fortune-driven moves add modular drama to scenes. They can escalate a scene or flip its tone in a heartbeat.
- A modular heist framework: you can craft plan sequences that feel authentic while still allowing the players to improvise. It’s a structured freedom, like a jazz solo with a metronome.
- Prestige and consequences: this expansion forces players to weigh the benefits of a risky maneuver against the odds of the whole thing blowing up in your face. Yes, there are consequences, and they sometimes come with a shiny badge of mythic power.

The tone remains quintessential City of Mist: pulp noir meets mythic power, with a dash of contemporary satire. It remains a game about people, not about exploding power numbers; Fortune Row reinforces that by giving you social hooks that matter. Thematically, it nails the sense that the urban myth is not an abstract avatar—the Mythos has weight, and Fortune Row makes that weight part of the gameplay.

### Mechanics and Play Flow
Fortune Row introduces a handful of additions to core mechanics, each designed to make the city feel alive and never quite safe for sleep. The deck of fortune cards acts as both a resource and a plot device; players draw from it to unlock unique actions and narrative beats. The GM uses a more dynamic risk and reward scale when setting scenes, letting the players choose how far to push the envelope on a given plan.

One of the most clever design touches is how Fortune Row emphasizes planning without removing chance. The new moves give you a reason to map out your approach: you can do things like Stack the Deck to gain advantages in a scene, or Pay the Piper to trigger a consequence in exchange for a dramatic payoff. If that sounds morally ambiguous, you’re on the right track—the setting is built around the tension between heroic myth and the real costs of chasing a legend.

### New Investigator Options and Customization
The expansion broadens the pool of investigator options by unlocking new Mythos-Driven packages. Each package has a theme (for example, information networks, underworld fortune, or mythic persuasive power) and interacts with Fortune Row's mechanics in unique ways.

For players who love alignment-free progression, Fortune Row rewards creative problem solving and social negotiating as much as kill-steal-the-boss gameplay. It’s less about raw mechanical superiority and more about how your legend interacts with the city—the kind of synergy that makes your Mythos feel truly alive.

If you want to explore a concrete example, consider a character who calls upon Fortune Row's Reading Deck to influence the narrative. In practice, this reads like a cross between a tarot deck and a social maneuver: you reveal a card, describe its symbolic meaning, and then the GM uses that symbol to shape the next scene's obstacles or rewards. The result can be dramatic and funny in the best possible way.

### Scenarios and Campaign Structure
Fortune Row is designed to scale. It includes a series of scenario blocks that can be plucked and rearranged like a mythic mixtape. Each block has a clear objective, a set of antagonists who are not merely boss fights but living facets of the city’s mythic economy, and a tipping point that changes the tone of the arc.

The campaign structure supports both episodic play and an overarching arc. A savvy GM can seed threads early in the arc that only mature into a full-blown mythic core by the late sessions. This gives players a sense of long-game investment, a crucial factor for groups that like to see their legends grow with time rather than in one explosive session.

For players who enjoy puzzle-box scenarios, Fortune Row offers scenes that resemble heist fantasies—clever, planful, and just risky enough to keep the adrenaline high without needing to invoke the nuclear option every time. The balance is delicate but well-judged, which is a hallmark of City of Mist design in general: you feel like you’re in control of your fate while still being swept along by the narrative tide.

### Story and Theme: How Fortune Row Feels in Your City
Fortune Row leans into social myth: how does a city’s mythic economy affect daily life? The expansion makes the investigation less about how to survive an outer-planar encounter and more about how to survive in a place where everyone’s myths rub elbows at a cramped bar called Fortune Row. The atmosphere is vivid, with a neon-drenched vibe that fits perfectly with the base game's aesthetic. The writing supports mood—pulp, noir, and mysticism—without becoming precious or inaccessible.

If there is a potential pitfall here, it’s that the density of new rules can overwhelm new players. However, the design team includes a robust GM toolkit that helps you step through the process. The core idea is to equip the GM with an actionable framework for weaving the players’ personal myths into the city’s fortunes and misfortunes. The payoff is a story that feels inevitable once you’ve dropped your players into it—like fate, but with better snacks.

### Thematic Consistency and Game Balance
From a balance perspective, Fortune Row remains within the safe zone of City of Mist’s design ethos. It introduces meaningful choices and consequences without limbs of the system getting tangled in a Gordian knot of complexity. The new moves interlock with existing moves in thoughtful ways. The expanded move set does not overshadow the base mechanics; rather, it broadens the scope of what’s possible when myth and modernity collide.

In terms of balance, there are some concerns that the fortune mechanic could be exploited if a group presses a single path too aggressively. The design mitigates this through escalating costs and the requirement to commit to a path. The GM retains the veto power to keep scenes moving when things become too unbalanced; there’s nothing worse than a dessert buffet where someone keeps requesting another slice of the same mooncake. Fortune Row provides checks and balances to keep narrative momentum without letting one player dominate the table.

### Production Values: Layout, Art, and Accessibility
The production values in Fortune Row match the rest of City of Mist’s high standard. The layout is clean, with evocative art and consistent iconography that makes it easy to identify a move, a setting, or a character option at a glance. The physical book (if you have the printed edition) is comfortable to handle, with durable pages and a sturdy spine. The digital edition is equally navigable, with clickable table content and search-friendly indexing. For a game about myths and headlines, the readability matters a lot, and Fortune Row nails it.

As with many City of Mist supplements, there’s an emphasis on art direction that sells the mood: posters, mock cover art for in-universe fliers, and fonts that feel like they belong on a noir-pulp magazine. The aesthetic is not just window-dressing: it reinforces the storytelling possibilities and helps you immerse your players in a world that feels both sprawling and personal.

Comparatively, it has a stronger social and investigative tilt than some of the more action-oriented expansions, making it a natural pick for groups that enjoy social maneuvering and mythic politics as much as you enjoy roll-off battles. ### A Quick Campaign Roadmap
If you’re itching to run a Fortune Row arc, here’s a rough roadmap to get you started:

1) Establish the mysteries: what is Fortune Row, and what is its legend? Decide the central myth that anchors your arc. 
2) Recruit the right players: if the players aren’t curious about myths and social intrigue, you might want to reframe expectations. 
3) Build the cast: identify local myths and their mortal proxies who shape Fortune Row’s economy and politics. 
4) Start small: a two-session arc focusing on a single heist or a single big reveal can be a great entry point. 
5) Expand the mythos: as players gain power, reveal the consequences that ripple across the city. 

### Final Thoughts and Recommendation
Fortune Row is a strong addition to City of Mist. It emphasizes narrative storytelling, social maneuvering, and myth-infused problem solving in a way that both new players and veterans can enjoy. The system remains elegant and flexible, with enough new pieces to explore while preserving the core feel that makes City of Mist beloved among fans of modern myths. If you enjoy heisty vibes, character-driven mystery, and a city where every corner hides a myth waiting to check its watch, Fortune Row is a must-have addition.

If you’re deciding whether to dip your toes into Fortune Row, consider your group’s appetite for puzzles, politics, and plot twists. If you crave a mood-driven, character-rich experience with a few clever new tricks, Fortune Row will feel like slipping into your favorite pair of comfortable leather boots—the ones that squeak just enough to remind you you’re walking on a mythic street.
