## Strategy and Pro-tips for New Players

### Getting started with confidence
New players should focus on learning phase progression rather than chasing the highest score in the first two rounds. The express cadence rewards players who stabilize a single strategy early and adapt as the table changes. A solid starting approach is to identify which phases appear most frequently in the deck and plan your hand to support those. If you quietly assemble a couple of safe sets while others chase glory, momentum will start to accrue and you’ll find yourself snapping up wins in later rounds.

### When to push and when to hold back
In a fast sprint, there’s a delicate balance between pushing for a fast finish and holding back to avoid a hand clog. If you’re near the end of a round and you have a low-risk discard to complete a phase, take the risk. If your hand is fragile with several blockers, pivot to a different phase that minimizes exposure to high-value discards. Observing opponent patterns helps; if someone tends to hoard a certain suit or number, a well-timed discard to block them can be a satisfying victory that pays off later.

### Common missteps to avoid
A frequent mistake is chasing an ambitious phase too early when your hand lacks the supporting pieces. The Express format punishes this impulse by forcing redraws and reorganization, which can lead to momentum-killing chaos. Another pitfall is underestimating the power of the wild card. The wild can save a round or flip the table if used at the right moment. Timing matters more in Express than in the long slog of classic Phase 10. Stay sharp, stay flexible, and keep trash talk friendly and limited to jesting about miscounted runs.
