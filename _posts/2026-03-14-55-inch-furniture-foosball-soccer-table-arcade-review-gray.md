## Assembly, Maintenance, and Longevity

### Assembly time and user experience
Like many large game tables, the initial assembly is a weekend project that becomes a rite of passage for new homeowners. Expect a set of clear instruction diagrams, a handful of screws, and the moment you realize you had the tool you needed the whole time in the kitchen drawer. The good news is that the process is satisfying; the bad news is that your arms may enter a temporary relationship with the couch while you finish. Realistically, plan for a couple of hours if you have an assistant who can follow labeled steps and not treat it like a jigsaw puzzle that defies gravity.

### Maintenance and care
Maintenance is actually simpler than it looks. Regular wipe-downs with a microfiber cloth keep fingerprints and snack smudges at bay. The rods should be checked every so often for play and re-tightened if necessary. Occasional lubrication of the bearings will keep the action silky and reduce the chance of squeaks that escalate into dramatic storytelling from the audience on the couch.

### Cleaning and upkeep tips
- Wipe the surface after use to prevent sticky residues from coffee cups and high-fives.
- Inspect leg levelers monthly to ensure even play; uneven surfaces can turn into an unintentional pinball experience for the ball.
- Keep a spare set of balls handy; nothing kills a party vibe faster than a pair of deflated foosballs ruining a dramatic tie-breaker.
