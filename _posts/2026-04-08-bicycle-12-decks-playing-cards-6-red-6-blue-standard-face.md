Most Bicycle decks use a stock that’s a friendly compromise between rigidity and flexibility. ## Maintenance and Longevity

### Caring for your decks

Proper care is essential for longevity. The air-cushion finish offers a fair measure of resilience but is not impervious to water damage or heavy moisture exposure. If you perform outdoors or in humid venues, consider a small travel case and a silica gel pack to control humidity inside your storage. Wipe cards with a dry microfiber cloth if you notice oily residue or fingerprints. If a deck begins to cling or lose its glide, consider a light cycle of shuffling to re-distribute the factory dust and re-fluff the air in the finish.

During long sessions, keep an eye on deck pairs and ensure you do not mix decks, especially if you are performing a color change trick that relies on color-coded cues. A small misalignment could disrupt your routine and reveal the trick inadvertently.

### Re-shuffling and resets

If you have to reset the trick after a misdealt card, take a moment to re-rack the decks and ensure each deck returns to its proper color group. It’s a small step but you will notice the difference when your audience does not. Consistency is the silent partner of a good performance; when your decks reflect that consistency in color and feel, your audience experiences a sense of reliability that makes your routine feel polished.
