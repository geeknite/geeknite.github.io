## How It Plays: Setup, Draft, and Score

Sushi Go Party is a card drafting game built around a conveyor belt metaphor, surprising nobody and making the host say, yes, we are indeed ordering sushi in our living room. The setup is straightforward: select a menu card set for the session from the included pool, decide on the number of players, deal the starting hands, and you are ready to draft. In each round, players select one card from their hand, swap the rest with their neighbor, and reveal simultaneously. The drafted cards are then added to your personal plate, where the scoring will eventually happen.

One of the big differentiators in Sushi Go Party is the customization of menu cards. Instead of a fixed deck, you can tailor the rounds by mixing and matching different sets. This means you can tailor the difficulty and the strategies to match your group. Do you want a quick social jam with a light scoring splash, or do you want a deeper, crunchy web of synergy that will make you brag about your memory and your ability to pass a card without blinking? The choice is yours, and the game is flexible enough to handle it.

### Setup and Play Flow
- Number of players: 2 to 8. The base game scales well, and the Party edition shines with six to eight players because the social element becomes a bigger driving force than the pure math.
- Play rounds: Typically three to four rounds per game, but you can extend if your table has a thirst for more sushi pursuit.
- Draft mechanic: Each player holds a hand of cards, picks one, passes the rest. The process repeats until all cards are drafted. Scoring occurs after each round for those who need a mid-game mood check.
- Scoring: Points are earned by collecting sets of matching sushi items, completing special combinations, and achieving targeted goals from the menu cards. The variations in the menu cards keep the scoring fresh and sometimes sneaky, as you chase a perfect combo while the person across the table quietly secures a different kind of profit from another set of cards.

This is a game that rewards pattern recognition, risk assessment, and the occasional bluffing move of passing a card that your neighbor would love—knowing they probably need it more than you do. It is not a heavy strategy game with a stack of rulebooks; it is a light, satisfying puzzle you can solve in a few minutes and then slide into a few more rounds with your friends.

### The Rhythm of Rounds
The game tempo sits in a comfortable zone. You draft, you reveal, you score, you reset for the next round.
