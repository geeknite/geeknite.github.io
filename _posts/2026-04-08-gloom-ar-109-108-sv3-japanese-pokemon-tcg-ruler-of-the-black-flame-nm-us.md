## Collectibility, NM US condition, and regional quirks

The NM US condition is a standard benchmark that many collectors rely on to gauge a card’s value and desirability. For Gloom AR 109/108 SV3 in NM US, you’ll often look for the following indicators:
- Centering: Ideally close to 60/40 or better from left to right, with minimal miscuts. Sometimes ARs drift a touch more due to the printing process, but you want the border to be balanced and the image well-centered.
- Corners: Sharp corners with no obvious dents or whitening along the tips. ARs are more prone to edge wear due to their premium stock, so keep an eyelid on the corner radius.
- Surface: A clean surface with minimal scratches, fingerprint smudges, or scuffs. The AR texture can accentuate minor surface flaws, so a mint surface goes a long way toward grading confidence.
- Gloss and foil: AR prints often have specialized foil or gloss elements that may show delamination or micro-bubbling in extreme cases. If the seed of the flame flickers under the light in a random pattern, that’s typically a sign of good stock rather than a defect.
