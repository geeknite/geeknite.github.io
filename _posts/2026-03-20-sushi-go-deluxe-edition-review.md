## How to Play (A Quick refresher)
If you’ve played Sushi Go! before, the Deluxe Edition doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it refines the wheel and adds a few flourishes. For newcomers, here’s a brisk refresher to get you into the groove.

### Setup
- Each player gets a hand of cards (the exact count varies by number of players, but classic rules apply). 
- The deck is shuffled, and players will pass hands around the table in a clockwise or counterclockwise rhythm depending on a quick roll of the dice (okay, no dice—just choosing direction, but the drama remains).
- A scoring area or sheet is placed where everyone can see it, along with the cute little scorekeeping tokens provided by the Deluxe Edition.

### Turn structure
On each turn, you select one card from your current hand to keep, then pass the remaining cards to the next player. You continue this process until all cards are claimed. Your goal is to assemble a set of dishes that yields the most points when the round ends. The trick is to balance immediate, tasty picks with long-term scoring potential.

### Scoring (the crunchy part)
- Different dishes score in different ways: some reward you for collecting duplicates, others reward you for having a balanced menu, and a few reward you for completing specific piece counts. The Deluxe Edition keeps this familiar mechanic intact, but the improved readability helps you track what you’re chasing without a degree in culinary mathematics.
- The scorepad included in the Deluxe Edition gives you a clean, tactile way to tally points as the game progresses. No more hunting for a pencil and scribble disasters—the board game gods demand neatness, and the Deluxe Edition obliges.

### End of round and next rounds
After all cards are claimed, you tally points and start another round, repeating the process. In a typical session, you’ll play several rounds, and the player with the most cumulative points takes the crown of “Sushi Master of the Night.” Respect earned, glory achieved, stomachs moderated by calorie-conscious gulping of water between rounds.

## Strategy and Tips for Victory
If you’ve come here for a single-minded cheat sheet, you’re not entirely out of luck. The core strategy in Sushi Go! Deluxe Edition remains consistent: identify a few high-value combos, disrupt your opponents’ drafting, and time your big scoring moves for the late rounds when the field narrows. Here are a few practical tips that apply to most player counts:

- Prioritize flexible sets: Early rounds reward flexibility. Cards that can contribute to multiple scoring paths become anchors you’ll want to keep in hand as you pass cards around. In practice, this means you should sometimes draft a dish that looks “okay” in one round but becomes a critical piece of your endgame plan.
- Watch opponents’ intentions: If you see a rival snapping up multiple copies of a particular dish, you can anticipate their endgame strategy. Consider denying them a crucial piece by prioritizing it on a subsequent pass. This is the social deduction part of a light drafting game, and it’s often where the table laughs the hardest when someone misreads the room.
- Endgame timing: The last few rounds are where the math matters most. Don’t overcommit early unless you’ve built a ticket to a guaranteed lead. Conversely, don’t sleep on late-round opportunities—one or two perfect picks can swing the score dramatically.
- The Deluxed edge: In the Deluxe Edition, you’ll find more readable icons, which translates into faster decisions. The faster you decide, the more time you have to savor the moment when your plan comes together, or when you realize you’ve basically drafted a culinary catastrophe and your napkin tells a better story than your scorecard.
