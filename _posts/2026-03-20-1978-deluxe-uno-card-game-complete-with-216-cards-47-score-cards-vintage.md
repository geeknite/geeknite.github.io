## Care, Storage, and Maintenance Tips
A vintage deck deserves a little pampering, and the same rules apply whether you’re a serious collector or a casual weekend player:
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to preserve color saturation. Sunlight can fade those bold 70s hues, and nobody wants mustard-yellow fading into a pale daisy. 
- Use the original box or a sturdy card box to prevent warping and edge wear. If the original box is long gone, a rigid book bindery case can work wonders.
- Clean gently. A dry microfiber cloth can wipe away fingerprints after a high-energy sprint. Avoid any liquids—this era didn’t flourish with water-resistant finishes.
- Consider a protective sleeve for individual cards if you intend to display or handle them frequently. It’s not just for aesthetics; it helps long-term durability.
- Rotate play locations if possible. Different table tensions can affect wear differently; a change of scenery can be a small savior for the deck’s life.
