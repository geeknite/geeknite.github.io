## How to play in minutes
Sushi Go! is famous for its brisk pace and its almost unfairly approachable rules. The Special Edition preserves the spirit while giving players a few extra hooks. Here is a concise refresher and a few notes on how the Special Edition changes the flow:

### Basic mechanics
- Each round, everyone is dealt a hand of 7 cards (in a 2 player game, use a 9 card mini-deck variant).
- Everyone looks at their hand, selects one card to keep, and passes the remaining cards to the left.
- After everyone has chosen a card, players reveal them simultaneously and score the newly created combos for that round.
- The round repeats until all cards are exhausted, and then you tally up total points, including any end-of-game bonuses and special edition scoring twists.

In the Special Edition, the draw/pick action remains the same, but there are a couple extra hooks:
- The chopsticks card in the Special Edition can be used to swap up to two cards in a later turn, creating interesting decision trees without derailing the core drafting rhythm.
- Some new scoring cards reward sequence awareness and memory, encouraging players to track which cards are likely to pass back to them on the next cycle.

### Turn structure and pacing
The key to Sushi Go! is mental rhythm. You glance, you pick, you pass, you reveal. In the Special Edition you may find yourself pausing a moment longer to appreciate the subtle differences in the new cards. The tempo can feel like a conveyor belt of tiny joys, a sequence of reveals that consistently lands with a satisfying pop when a perfect pairing comes together.

Be mindful of tempo in a larger group. With more players, information spreads around the table faster, and your choices become a blend of deduction and luck. The new emphasis on certain combos in the Special Edition can push players toward riskier gambits. If you overcommit to a single track, you may find your opponents feasting on the sidelines while you end up with a pile of tofu you forgot to score.

### Endgame and scoring details
At the end of the game, you count points from your cards and any end-of-game bonuses for completing sets or achieving certain combos. The Special Edition adds a handful of new scoring criteria that keep veteran players on their toes and give newbies something tangible to aim for on the last few rounds. The scoring tokens add a tactile layer that some players will love and some will ignore; either way the tokens do not obstruct the core scoring logic.

To give you a sense of the math, here is a quick example of how the scoring can play out in a midgame moment: you have a run of nigiri cards, a couple of maki-type rolls, and you see the last conveyor belt turning toward the dessert cards. A well-timed chopsticks swap allows you to upgrade a single dish into a higher value set, netting more points without giving away the entire round to the table. The numbers on the scoring tokens are small but meaningful, adding up by the end of the game to a comfortable margin of victory or a narrow loss that you will brag about online for years to come.

## Strategy tips and caveats
Strategy in Sushi Go! is a little like cooking a sashimi platter: you aim for balance, you think ahead, and you pray to the kitchen gods that your timing is correct. In this Special Edition, a few tweaks can shift the approach without turning the game into a math marathon:

- Card counting basics: In a typical 2 to 4 player game, you get to see roughly 2 to 3 passes before a card leaves your hands for good. Try to read at least a couple of rounds ahead so you can anticipate what your neighbors are likely to collect and what you should avoid picking.
- Targeting the right sets: Nigiri and maki are classic staples, but the Special Edition adds optional cards that excel in late rounds. If you can see that your competitors have already started a maki stack, you may want to jump on a dessert or a fruit card to edge them out on the end of game bonuses.
- The chopsticks gambit: When used well, chopping your future moves into a single turn can swing the pipeline in your favor. But it is not a free pass to hoard cards. Use it to restructure your upcoming choices, not to force a perfect dream run at a table that is happily chaotic.
- Side dish discipline: Don t fall into the trap of chasing every minor scoring token you see. The Special Edition provides more tokens; the more you chase, the more you risk missing the big picture.

As with many light games, the best strategy often comes from playing with a couple of friends a few times and then discovering what everyone at your table tends to ignore. For many players, that is an unspoken rule: if you can make your opponents laugh while you dream up your next hand, you may end up with a better score due to moral victory and side bets.
