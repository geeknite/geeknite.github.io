## Materials, Build, and Care
### Fiber Content
Most Fontaine Green rugs use a high-density synthetic fiber blend engineered for resilience. Think durability that can handle chair wheels, footfalls, snack crumbs, and the occasional sprint to the door when a delivery beep goes off. The fiber is chosen for a balance between softness and support; your feet get a cushiony feel, your carpet gets a backbone. It’s the sort of "adulting" you want: comfortable, dependable, and not allergic to your life choices.

### Cleaning and Maintenance
Maintenance is not glamorous, but it doesn’t have to be a slog either. Quick spill: blot with a clean towel, then dab with a mild detergent solution. Do not rub, as that only pushes stains into the depths of the weave and costs you more mental energy than a patch note from your favorite game. Vacuum on low to medium power; avoid high suction that might pull threads. For stubborn stains, a gentle stain remover recommended by the manufacturer can tackle the mess without turning your rug into a science experiment. If a long gaming night leaves behind an odd scent, a light airing and a monthly refresh will keep things fresh.

## Care and Maintenance: Allergy, Stains, and Smells
### Allergen Considerations
Allergies are personal; some of us break out in a flourish of sneezes at the mere sight of a dust mote. Fontaine Green’s tight weave helps reduce dust buildup, though nothing replaces regular vacuuming and air purification for sensitive folks. The fiber is synthetic, so it’s less prone to mold growth in damp climates; still, you should avoid letting moisture linger on the surface, or you’ll invite damp, which is not a vibe for anyone.

### Stains and Spills
Spills happen; it’s the nature of living. The quick blot method is your best friend here. Rubbing can cause color transfer and wear; blotting lets you lift liquid without grinding color into the weave. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution is your ally. If you’re dealing with a white wine slip, act quickly and rinse with water after blotting; the sooner you address it, the less you’ll regret your past self later.

### Odors and Ventilation
Occasional odors are normal in busy spaces. A quick air-out, a little baking soda, or a gentle vacuums pass can keep smells in check. If you’re in a small apartment, crack a window during cleaning and let the rug breathe like a dragon that forgot to blow smoke into your neighbor’s yard.
