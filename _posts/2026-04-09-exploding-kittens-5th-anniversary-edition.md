## Rules, explained (brief refresher)
- Defuse cards save you from doom; you can hold onto them for future catastrophes.
- Cat cards let you steal a card from someone else, causing mutual chaos.
- Explosion cards create pressure; the goal is to avoid them while you can still defuse or pass the hazard.
- The game ends when all but one player have exploded or when someone holds the last escape card.

## Strategies for better chaos
- Location matters: sitting near the “Detonate” card can be thrilling if you’re on the edge of your seat and not on the receiving end of that Detonator.
- Card counting with a smile: knowing who holds what Defuse or which Cat cards can shape your risk calculus.
- Diplomacy and deception: a sideways compliment can soften a player enough to trade a card that changes the night’s outcome.

## Player dynamics and social flavor

## House rules and variants
- One common variant is “fast-defuse” where you allow defuses a touch earlier to keep momentum going.
- Another is “cat-alarm mode” where you all reveal a cat card before drawing to heighten tension.
- You can also implement a “timed round” rule to force faster draws and keep conversation snappy.

## Timing and pacing
A typical game runs 15-20 minutes, depending on group dynamics. With the 5th Anniversary Edition, the opener often triggers a cascade well before the third round. If you want longer sessions, simply invite a few more players; the chaos scales up without needing a complicated scoring system.
