## Ethical Considerations and Play Etiquette
A healthy CAH session is a conversation, not a battlefield. The PAX pack, like many expansions, thrives when players agree on boundaries and respect the limits everyone has set. Inside jokes and convention-specific humor can be incredibly funny, but they can also unintentionally alienate someone who doesn’t share that particular context. Here are a few practices that keep the mood inclusive without sacrificing the exhilaration of a well-timed punchline:
- Consent-friendly prompts: If a prompt feels risky, opt for a redraw instead of forcing someone to answer. The goal is laughter, not discomfort that lingers past the post-round sprint.
- Rotate the spotlight: By rotating the role of Card Czar, you give everyone a turn to shape the round’s energy, timing, and tone.
- Self-reflection as a rule: If you wouldn’t say it to your grandmother in polite company, consider rewording or skipping the moment. The group should feel like a safe space for playful mischief, not cruelty dressed as wit.

The PAX Prime 2013 Pack nods to the edge of humor while recognizing the value of consent and mutual respect. It’s a delicate balance, but with a little shared understanding, it becomes a cornerstone of a great game night rather than a cautionary tale.
