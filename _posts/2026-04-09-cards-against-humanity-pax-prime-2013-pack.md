In Cards Against Humanity, the sweets are white cards, the sour truths are black cards, and your group is the oven that bakes the toast. The PAX Prime 2013 Pack marches to that rhythm with a few dozen new cards designed to complement a party that is already knee-deep in nerdy vibes.

- White cards: The responses are the fireworks. They range from clever to aggressively silly, with enough variety to satirize pop culture, gaming tropes, and con-going hilarities. Some white cards arrive as perfect zingers, others as goofy non-sequiturs that your brain latches onto and then refuses to forget.

This pairing—timely prompts with perfectly misused punchlines—delivers the core satisfaction of CAH: the moment when your group shouts, "That’s it! That’s the line!" and then spends the next several minutes trying to top it with an even smarter or dirtier alternative.

## Ethical Considerations and Play Etiquette
This is not a carte blanche for cruelty, even in a CAH setting. The PAX pack, like many CAH expansions, thrives when the players understand consent and boundaries. The best memories come from a group that communicates its comfort level and respects the limits of what’s acceptable to joke about within their circle. If a line is crossed, a quick pause, a check-in, and a redraw can save a night from becoming awkward. Humor about conventions and gaming culture often sits near the edge; to keep it fun rather than alienating, aim for playful, self-referential humor rather than punching down.

If you’re ever in doubt, remember the simple rule: if you wouldn’t say it to your grandmother in polite company, don’t say it to your friends in a game night either. The goal is laughter that brings people closer, not discomfort that pushes them away. The PAX Prime 2013 Pack is best enjoyed when you lean into your shared experiences and celebrate them without turning the session into a moral debate.
