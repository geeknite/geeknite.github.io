## Setup Guide: Ready, Set, Tabletop

### Quick-start path
1) Unbox with a calm center of gravity and a beverage within arm’s reach. 2) Lay the play surface with the rods removed or retracted. 3) Attach the rods and players as per the quick-start guide. 4) Align the table on a stable surface, then verify ball return alignment. 5) Drop in your first balls and test a few passes before inviting guests to witness your first victory or glorious defeat.

### Assembly notes and tips
- If you own the open-box variant, inspect for tiny cosmetic nicks; they’re usually superficial and add character rather than compromise play. 
- Tighten screws gradually to avoid warping the frame; a light hand is better than the brag-grease approach. 
- Use the included wrench(es) to avoid stripping slots. If you lose them, standard small Allen keys will do—just don’t overtighten.
- Lubrication is not required for the rods, but if you live in a dusty environment, a quick wipe-down and light spray of lubricant can prevent squeaks from ruining your dramatic feints.

### First game: what to expect
Your first game will likely be one of two things: a glorious victory that makes you feel like you discovered fire, or a chaotic exercise in reflexes and counting as you chase a ball around like a nerdy raccoon. Either way, FX40 will reveal its personality through your play.
