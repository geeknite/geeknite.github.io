## Introduction
The Duke is a tiny titan in a world of ever-growing, hour-long epics. It fits in a pocket, looks like it could double as a chess set if the chess pieces were made of clever whimsy and tiny, adorable men with maxims of mayhem. When Catalyst Game Labs unleashed City Troops, Customization Pack, and Catalyst, the gaming universe drew its breath in a held-out whisper: could tiny cardboard be stretched into a full-blown urban war story with enough personality to power a dozen memes? Spoiler alert: yes, yes it can. And it does so with the kind of jaunty confidence you expect from a game that ships a tile-based combat system and a sense of humor about itself.

If you’re coming to this review from a base Duke game, you’re familiar with the concept: move little knights on little squares, chain a few combos, and try not to knock your own king on a casual Tuesday night. The City Troops expansion adds a city-flavored layer to the mix—think narrow lanes, chokepoints, and siege-esque moments that feel like a heist flick could happen on a coffee shop map. The Customization Pack, on the other hand, offers a belt buckle full of new weapons and attunements for your Duke troops, letting players paint their squad with more personality than a gamer’s favorite skin on a new console. Catalyst, the third part of this trio, introduces a set of house rules that tilt the game towards faster tempo and sharper counterplay. The result is not merely more pieces; it’s more personality, more tactical space, and more opportunities for you to brag about your “creative opening” while your opponent eyes the clock in a panic.

This review aims to unpack what each piece brings to the table, how they interact with each other, and whether the price tag (or the shelf-take distance on your game shelf) is worth it for a normal gaming night or a dedicated Duke corner in your living room.

## What’s in the box? City Troops, Customization Pack, and Catalyst, in detail
Each expansion pack in The Duke line tends to be lean, clever, and ready to slot into a handful of different playstyles. City Troops continues that tradition with a focus on urban warfare mechanics, while the Customization Pack leans into personalization, flavor, and asymmetry. Catalyst provides a rules layer that nudges the tempo and the tactics in new directions. Let’s break down what a typical player can expect when tearing into the trio.

### City Troops: urban lanes and siege-lite mayhem
City Troops introduces new tile types designed to recreate a gritty, cityscape battlefield. Expect narrow alleys, rooftops, and a few “city block” features that block line-of-sight in clever ways. The new unit types emphasize speed, urban maneuverability, and area control—think units that can sprint through a tight corner and appear where you least expect them. The art continues The Duke’s signature cheek: it’s bold, clear, and doesn’t waste space on fluff when it could be spent on a cool silhouette or two.

Components include:
- A set of city-themed tiles that scale up a standard board into a modular urban map.
- New Duke tiles with urban attributes, such as “Streetwise” and “Alley Sneak” that grant small, flavorful bonuses.
- A handful of new unit cards with slightly different stats that reward careful positioning and clever heroics.
- Custom tokens for movement and zone control that help players visualize block-by-block dominance.

The design philosophy here is to create enough space for misdirection and clever positioning without blowing up the base game’s approachable pace. You’ll see players choosing routes through alleys to avoid direct confrontation, then springing a calculated trap when an opportunistic gap in the guard shifts your way.

### Customization Pack: personalize, not complicate
Customisation is a word that sometimes means “more rules.” In The Duke, it means “more personality, with a sprinkle of strategy.” The pack adds a range of new weaponry and gear that can attach to Duke tiles, elevating famous champions with distinctive loadouts. You might end up with a Duke who favors long-range sniping from rooftops, or a frontline bruiser with a shield that makes him accidentally the most indefatigable block of cardboard you’ve ever seen.

Includes:
- A selection of weapon cards and gear tokens that attach to Duke tiles.
- A handful of “special rule” cards that grant unique effects when certain conditions are met.
- Revised stat blocks for several Duke sets to reflect the new armaments.
- Character backstories and flavor text that add a touch of humor to a game about tiny armored guys fighting in a square-shaped arena.

The Customization Pack doesn’t suddenly rewrite the Duke’s core identity; it reinforces it by letting players lean into a particular theme or style. If you’re a person who loves optimizing your roster, this pack is a playground. If you prefer playing with a handful of nuts-and-bolts units, you can still use it for a dash of variety without changing how you think about the game’s tempo.

### Catalyst: the tempo-restorer, the tempo-accelerator, the social glue
Catalyst acts as a dab hand at smoothing the edges of the base Duke experience. It’s a rules layer and a playstyle modifier rolled into one—designed to speed games up, sharpen counterplay, and add a dash of unpredictability without breaking the game’s core identity. If you’ve ever muttered, “This game would be perfect if only the turns were a touch faster,” Catalyst is aimed squarely at you.

Expect:
- Shorter turns with streamlined decision points, so you aren’t counting down the minutes of a 90-minute duel.
- A handful of “reaction” rules that let players respond to opponent moves more aggressively, creating a lively back-and-forth instead of stares across the table.
- Optional setup variations that can re-skin the game into tournament-style play without needing a tournament organizer’s manual.

Together, those three add-ons create a synergy that can push a casual game night into something a touch more strategic and a lot more cinematic.

## How City Troops and Customization Pack interact with the base game
The Duke’s charm is in its elegant simplicity: a few tiles, a few moves, a couple of dice, and a lot of micro-skill. City Troops expands the tactical canvas without forcing a entire new rulebook; it teaches you a new geometry: how to carve a path through a sprite of rooftops, how to funnel an opponent through a corridor when you have the superior numbers in a narrow space. The customization pack then gives you the tools to build a personality—your own board-gaming band of misfit blocks, each with a story and a skill that feels just slightly beyond “standard Duke.” The two together create a layered experience: you’re not just moving units; you’re crafting a unit composition that says something about your playstyle—bold, cautious, flamboyant, or sneaky.

Catalyst ties these threads together by aligning tempo and options in a way that makes the game feel flowy rather than stop-and-go. It’s the difference between a stand-up set where the comedian hits the same beats and a stand-up set where the audience’s reactions ripple through the room. Catalyst makes each decision point feel meaningful, while the City Troops provide the physical and tactical environment on which your plan can play out. The result is a more dynamic duel that still respects the Duke’s compact, tactile joy.

## Component quality and visual aesthetic
If you’ve seen The Duke on a shelf, you’ve seen what good boardgame components look like: compact, tall-lidded boxes with bold art and a nose for clear iconography. City Troops maintains that standard, with tiles that snap together with a satisfying heft and a color palette that makes it easy to distinguish your urban factions from your opponents’ troops. The Customization Pack adds more tiny attachments, and the quality of the add-ons matches the system’s inherently whimsical vibe—the pieces aren’t fragile, the print quality is clean, and the rulebook is short enough to actually be read before you start playing.

The artwork leans into a cartoonish noir—think bold shadows, bright highlight colors, and unit silhouettes that catch the eye even when you’re trying to focus on a chessboard-like layout. It’s not realism; it’s charm with a strategic edge, and that’s exactly what The Duke does best.

## Setup, teachability, and play flow
The Duke’s learning curve has always been one of its strongest draws: quick setup, fast teach, then a skirmish that breathes life into a two-player duel. City Troops adds new map tiles and movement rules, but the pace remains brisk. The teachable moment is the same basic rhythm: explain one or two new symbols, show a couple of example moves, and you’re off to a duel where every turn feels like a micro-choreography scene from a heist film.

The Customization Pack is easy to introduce in a mixed-player-night: you can implement a subset of the new gear in a single session, or go for a full “loadout” style game where players draft their Duke’s weapons before the duel. Catalyst’s rules layer can be introduced gradually, so you don’t have to go all-in on a new tempo from the start. It’s a trifecta of expansions that respects the pace of the base game while giving you a few more knobs to turn.

## Gameplay dynamics: what actually changes at the table
What changes most in practice is how you think about space and timing. City Troops invites more thoughtful positioning: you’ll want to block lines of sight not just to prevent direct shots, but to force your opponent into chokepoints where your terrain advantages shine. You’ll notice a greater emphasis on control of narrow corridors and rooftop approaches—moments where a single unit benefits from a single open square that flips the map from “I’m safe here” to “you’re going to have to dance your plan through a series of tight turns.”

Customization Pack tells you to lean into your character’s identity. Do you give your duke a reach weapon and turn him into a spear-wheeler, or do you grab a nimble kit that lets him flit around like a tiny, highly tactical pinball? Either way, the cards invite creative deck-building and a more granular decision process than the base game alone. Catalyst bends the tempo toward brisker exchanges. You’ll see turns that feel decisive in a satisfying way, followed by a couple of sharp pivots that remind you that the game is still a small, tactile puzzle rather than a long strategic slog.

If you enjoy asymmetry in your tactics, you’ll love how the Customization Pack can tilt a matchup. If you enjoy precise timing, you’ll savor City Troops’ new lines of movement and attack ranges. If you like a game that treats all three as a single evolving machine, Catalyst is your grease.

## Strategies, tips, and play advice
- Start with a plan, but be ready to abandon it in a moment. The Duke rewards improvisation and timely counterplay. City Troops gives you more improvisation space, not less.
- Use the city’s terrain to force your opponent into unfavorable trades. A well-placed alleyway can turn a simple advance into a stalemate, letting your lead unit slip in for a clean finish.
- Tailor your Duke’s loadout with the Customization Pack to suit your preferred tempo. If you enjoy rapid exchanges, pick a kit that improves reaction options. If you prefer controlled, patient play, favor gear that increases range or defensive potential.
- Catalyst shines in two-player games or small-group sessions where you want a snappier rhythm. If you’re bringing a bigger group to the table, consider a lighter application of Catalyst rules to avoid chaotic turns.
- Practice makes perfect. Run a few practice matches with just City Troops to internalize the new geometry, then sprinkle in Customization Pack gear to see how your options reshape the opening gambit.

## Thematic flavor and humor
The Duke is written with a wink, and these expansions keep that vibe intact. The City Troops’ alias-names for tiles and units feel like they walked straight out of a rogue’s gallery in a neo-noir comic. Customization Pack’s flavor texts are a gentle reminder that even tiny armored chess pieces can have big personalities—and that your strategy often looks cooler when your units have slightly too much swag for their own good. Catalyst supplies the pacing jokes, the little moments of dramatic pause between turns, and a reminder that sometimes the best tactic is to smile while your opponent mutters, “Seriously?”

## Comparisons: how this trio stacks up against other Duke add-ons
Compared to the original Duke expansions, City Troops offers more tactical options without overhauling the core rules. Customization Pack condenses the joy of “deck-building” into a compact, friendly format that you can teach in one sitting. Catalyst’s tempo-focused approach is reminiscent of other speed-tuning rulesets in the genre, but it’s tailored to the Duke’s tactile, board-game-night vibe rather than a hyper-competitive tournament feel.

If you’ve enjoyed other small-box expansions in the space, you’ll recognize the same design philosophy: bite-sized additions that emphasize variety and replayability without turning the game into a behemoth. This trio sticks to that formula while offering a little more personality and a little more strategy to the table.

## Pros and cons at a glance
- Pros:
  - Adds meaningful tactical depth without bloating the game.
  - City Troops provides exciting new map geometry that rewards clever positioning.
  - Customization Pack lets players express their playstyle and identity.
  - Catalyst keeps the game fast and engaging, without sacrificing nuance.
  - All three are cohesive and feel like they belong to the same family of Duke products.
- Cons:
  - The combined price may be higher than the base game for casual players.
  - If you’re not into heavier tactical decision-making, City Troops can feel a bit fiddly at first.

## Post-game reflections and final verdict
If you’re a Duke veteran who’s looking for something that nudges the game into new territory while still preserving the core charm, City Troops, Customization Pack, and Catalyst offer a delicious, compact upgrade. They’re not mandatory—nothing forces you to buy them—but they provide more reasons to pull the Duke out, arrange the tiles, and talk smack with style. The game remains approachable, but you’ll discover that it’s also capable of a surprising amount of depth when you lean into the added terrain control, personalized gear, and tempo-shifting rules.

If you’re a new Duke player wondering whether to start with the base game or jump straight into expansions, I’d recommend the base game to learn the fundamentals, then add City Troops and Customization Pack for a richer sandbox. Catalyst can slot into your later sessions as a “variance mode” that you pull out when you want a fresher cadence.
