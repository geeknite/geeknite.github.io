## Design philosophy and features
The tower of this product is modular by design. Instead of one monolithic desk, you get two long benches that seat three people each. The central screens act as both visual separators and acoustic dampers. In practice, the partitions reduce direct line of sight between adjacent users, which helps with focus when someone decides to test a new keyboard sprint at 10 am. The screen height is adjustable along a profile rail, allowing the desk to accommodate different monitor heights and user preferences.

The workspace is not just about raw space; it is also about smart integration with accessories. The manufacturer includes standard grommets for cable routing at each seat and a shared central cable trough that runs along the back edge, feeding power, data, and USB-C hubs to each workstation. The trough is sized to handle six workstation cords with a little jogging room for future upgrades such as a shared SSD server for a local backup run.

## Layout and space planning for six
The layout is intended to maximize team collaboration while preserving enough personal space for each operator. The 5400 mm run is typically divided into two 2700 mm bays, with three seats per bay. The central partitions provide privacy while still enabling visual cues for reading a teammate across the table. ## Assembly journey
As with any serious piece of furniture designed for six people, assembly is not a one-person job. The manufacturer ships the major components boxed with clear labeling. Expect a multi-person lift for the heavy panels, a couple of hours for the initial screwing, and then another hour for aligning partitions and verifying cable channels. Tools included typically cover the basics, but a power drill with a clutch setting helps avoid overtightening the ABS edges and striping screws. The central cable trough is usually pre-routed within the main frame; you will still need a little patience for the final connections.

## Ergonomics and user experience
The ergonomic goal here is to reduce neck strain by aligning the monitor height with the user’s eye level while providing enough elbow room for each person. The desk depth of 1530 mm is adequate for dual monitor setups with space for notebooks and a personal drink. The central partitions act as a visual barrier, but you do not want them to become a physical barrier to airflow. Leave some space at the edge to allow for air to circulate and to avoid a claustrophobic feel.

Under-desk clearance is a critical factor for six seat configurations. The cross-bracing and cable trough often require a little extra floor clearance under each seat. We recommend adjustable chairs with generous under-seat height to accommodate leg movement and occasional kneeling to fetch the remote.

Pro tips
- If you plan to run tall monitors, check the central partition height is compatible with VESA mounts and cable trays.
- Use monitor arms with weight limits that can be matched to your monitor sizes to avoid tilting or sagging over time.
- Consider adding a small footrest for long sessions to keep posture comfortable.

## Maintenance and cleaning
Cleaning a six-seat workstation is not rocket science, but it does require a plan. The white surface responds well to microfiber cloths and gentle cleaners. Avoid abrasive pads that can dull the finish. Regularly check the integrity of the edge bands; if you notice any lifting, reseal with the manufacturer’s recommended edge glue. Schedule a quarterly check of the cable channels to ensure nothing has slipped or kinked during rearrangements. A good practice is to label each cable with color-coded ties; it speeds up maintenance and daily wrap-ups when people leave early or new hires join the team.
