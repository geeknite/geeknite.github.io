## Setup and two-player duel rules
### Quick setup
1. Open the sealed box (carefully so you don’t disappoint the cat gods with your lack of inner monologue).
2. Shuffle the deck thoroughly; tell your opponent you love their face and you mean it, even if you secretly want to play See the Future and peek ahead.
3. Deal seven cards to each player (adjust if you want a shorter or longer duel).
4. Place a single Defuse card aside if you want to keep a pool, or keep it with the rest of the Defuse cards for the draw pile to feel spicy.
5. Decide who goes first. Pro tip: the player who complains less about the color of the table wins more often than you’d expect.

### Playing the two-player duel
On your turn, you can play one card from your hand or you can discard and draw a card to replenish your hand. The goal is to force your opponent to draw the Exploding Kitten card and then hope you have a Defuse to save yourself from a dramatic whimper of defeat that ends with a dramatic slow clap from the existential cat who watches you lose the game.

Key ideas to keep in mind:
- Attack cards escalate the pace. If your opponent doesn’t have a defense in hand, you get to push them toward a natural ending more quickly.
- Skip cards give you a moment to recompose your strategy and avoid the immediate doom of a big reveal.
- See the Future cards allow you to peek at the top few cards of the deck, letting you plan your last few moves with a smug confidence you’ll regret when reality hits.
- Shuffle cards can disrupt your opponent’s assumed strategy by reinventing the deck’s order, which in practice means you both stare at a deck with more questions than answers.

As in any duel, there are moments when your brain tries to calculate odds and your heart screams for comedic relief. In practice, you’ll find that the pace is brisk enough to fund a few rounds in a single sitting, but not so fast that you can’t appreciate the art on each card or savor the banter that erupts between plays. The two-player structure doesn’t try to replace the party vibe; it channels it into a fast, personal duel that still feels like you and your friend are co-starring in a goofy sci-fi drama where cats are agents of chaos.

## Rules overview: what to expect on a turn
### Core actions
- Play a card or pass and draw
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse, you’re out (or you do the dramatic “I am defeated” setup and pass the cat overlord’s authority to your opponent)
- Use Defuse to survive an Exploding Kitten card. If you misplace it, you’ll have to reveal you are the true king of chaos, and the game continues with a new depth of melodrama

### Common card types you'll encounter
- Defuse: The lifesaver that keeps you in the game when the universe decides to host a literal ticking kitten.
- Attack: Forces the opponent to take multiple turns in a single sweep. It’s the dramatic pause before the punchline.
- See the Future: A glimpse into the future that helps you plan your next move. It’s basically cheating—if cheating came with a disclaimer and a cute kitten hat.
- Shuffle: Introduces chaos by shuffling the deck, tossing your carefully laid plans into a doughnut of randomness.
- See the Cat Cards: A variety of cat-themed effects that give you either a power boost or a reason to groan.
- Exploding Kitten: The card that acts as the narrative pivot of the game. Draw this without a Defuse and you’re living in a dramatic finale you probably didn’t expect.

This is a fantastic structure for a two-player duel because it preserves the tension and the lighthearted humor while letting two players push each other to the edge of ridiculous outcomes. It’s not a game about careful economic planning; it’s a game about who can stay calm longest while a tiny cartoon kitten commits to chaos.
