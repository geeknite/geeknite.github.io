## Care and maintenance

Caring for a rug with a bold color story is a two-step dance: keep it clean, and keep it looking intentional. Regular vacuuming with a vacuum that has adjustable height helps prevent fiber crushing and reduces the amount of dust that can accumulate in the pile. Be mindful of the suction setting; too aggressive a setting may pull fibers and exaggerate the existing irregularity in dye distribution.

Spot cleaning should be done with mild detergent and lukewarm water. Avoid harsh chemicals that could alter the dye or texture. If a spill happens, act quickly but calmly, like you are defusing a friendly but enthusiastic robot that wants to help you out with a cleanup montage. Blot, don’t rub, and test on an inconspicuous area first to ensure color stability. For deeper stains, consider professional cleaning; the rug’s backing should hold up to professional methods without curling or warping if treated with care.

Seasonal rotation can help balance wear and color exposure. If your room has strong sun exposure, you might rotate the rug or use curtains during peak sun hours to preserve the color life. And yes, you can still use a rug pad. A quality pad reduces slippage, improves insulation, and adds a whisper of underfoot comfort that makes standing on the rug feel more like a cozy board game night than a bare floor sprint to the coffee maker.
