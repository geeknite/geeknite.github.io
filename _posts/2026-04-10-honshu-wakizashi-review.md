## Safety, Handling, and Care
Safety is not optional; it’s a feature of any blade, even one designed for cosplay. Treat the Wakizashi with respect and store it securely. If you’re using it for display only, consider mounting options that keep the blade away from curious hands and mischievous pets. If you’re using it for light practice, wear proper training gear and practice in a safe environment. Do not improvise with real objects as training targets; use proper foam or wrapped targets to ensure a low-risk cutting experience. Remember: even cosplay blades can surprise you if you’re not paying attention to your surroundings.

Maintenance basics:
- Wipe down the blade after use to remove fingerprints and moisture.
- Lightly oil the blade to prevent rust and corrosion.
- Inspect the grip and wrapping periodically, especially after any heavy handling.
- Store in a dry place away from humidity and dust.
