## What is Exploding Kittens Original Edition MISB?

Exploding Kittens, originally designed by Elan Lee, Matthew Inman, and Shane Small, exploded into the gaming world with a pledge to be the most ridiculous, fast-paced party game you can legally play while keeping a straight face. The Original Edition MISB is the first print run that many collectors seek: a box that has never seen the light of day, a deck untouched by the unforgiving hands of players, and a seal that whispers promises of mintness and potential resale value if you ever decide to sell to a person who cares about such things.

The premise is simple: take turns drawing cards from a deck that includes Defuse cards and an assortment of kitten-themed disaster cards. When you draw an Exploding Kitten without a Defuse card, boom, you’re out—er, or at least your friends get a good laugh and a dramatic pause in the game while you try to breathe through the foam of disappointment. The twist is Defuse cards and a bunch of wacky action cards that let you steal, peek at, or otherwise mess with other players. The result is a chaotic, silly, and surprisingly strategic game that can be played in as little as 15 minutes or as long as your friends keep insisting this is the perfect party game for a Tuesday night.

In MISB form, the box is not merely a container; it is a time capsule: uncrushed corners, intact seal, and the gentle patina of a product that has never known the joy of being opened and then immediately closed. For collectors, MISB means potential future value; for purists and players, it means you can decide whether you want to keep your box pristine forever or eventually crack the seal for a nostalgic thunderclap of laughter. Either way, the MISB version preserves the original art, the original card stock quality, and the exact humorous tone that made Exploding Kittens famous in the first place.

## Box contents and unboxing experience

A MISB box isn’t just packaging; it’s a ceremony. The moment you break the seal on the Original Edition MISB, you’ve entered a sacred space where the rules are simple: enjoy the chaos, don’t take the kittens too seriously, and pretend you’re cooler than you are while you shuffle like a caffeinated octopus.

### The feel and look of the box
The original box art is crisp, cartoonish, and unapologetically goofy. In MISB form, the box feels like a vintage video game cartridge—reliable, a little ashamed about its childish exterior, and hiding a wild party inside that only reveals itself once you pop the seal.

### Inside the box
Within, you’ll typically find a deck of Exploding Kitten cards, a handful of Defuse cards, and a collection of action cards that let you do everything from skipping turns to shenanigans with your opponents’ cards. The card stock is sturdy, the illustrations sharp, and the humor cuts through the noise with surgical precision. The MISB seal is like a dare: can you resist opening the box for one more day, or will you succumb to the temptation of the minty freshness released by peeling away the plastic? The original edition cards carry the same playful art you’ve seen in memes and on the official site, with enough zaniness to earn a few grins even on a bad day.

### Jekyll and Hyde of card design
One feature to note in the MISB original edition is the balance of defuses to explosions. The Defuse cards provide a lifeline, a moment of relief when you’ve just watched your plan derail spectacularly. The other cards—attack, skip, shuffle, see the future—provide a toolkit for either saving yourself or destroying someone else’s confidence. The result is a game that can feel light and silly or tense and strategic, depending on the mix of players and how much you’re willing to bet on a mischievous draw.

## The mechanics that make MISB magic and mischief

If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, here’s a quick refresher on the core mechanics, tailored for the MISB experience:

- The goal is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you draw one, you’re out unless you have a Defuse card to negate the explosion.
- Defuse cards are scarce enough to feel like cherished stashed loot, but common enough that no one should panic when you pull one out of a draw pile.
- Action cards shift the power dynamics, letting you manipulate the deck, your timing, or your opponents instead of relying on pure luck.
- The art keeps the tone light and silly, ensuring that even a near-elimination turns into a laugh instead of a meltdown.

This balance of luck and strategy is what makes the MISB version so compelling: you can depend on a strong, clean unboxing experience, then play multiple sessions with the same energy and unpredictability. The original edition especially feels like the purest distillation of the concept—no expansions muddying the waters, just you, your friends, and a deck of cats that want to explode into your faces with comedic timing.

## Strategy tips for MISB folks who want to win without losing their cool

- Defuse card distribution matters: If you’re lucky enough to snag multiple Defuses early, use them as your safety net while you test card discard strategies. The trick is not to hoard Defuses, but to balance timing between playing a Defuse now or saving it for a potentially global reset later in the game.
- Read the table, not just the cards: Part of Exploding Kittens is reading your opponents’ expressions, and if you’ve got a poker face, you’ll see the moment when someone contemplates the risk of drawing again. If someone looks too confident, they might be hiding nerves—that’s your signal for a bluff or a counter-move.
- Plan around the see-the-future cards: The see-the-future cards let you peek ahead in the deck. Use them to map out a rough landscape of what’s left. If you know a big-card explosion is near, you can plan a defensive move or a dramatic skip that shakes the table, even if you sacrifice a turn.
- Don’t underestimate the comedic power of misdirection: The game thrives on misdirection and banter. The best players aren’t always the ones who draw the least; they’re the ones who draw confidently, then pivot into a chaotic strategy that throws opponents off their game.
- Playing with MISB is a different kind of tension: The sealed box is a constant reminder that you’re stepping into a moment of pure chaos. The moment the box is opened, you’ve invited a certain level of risk into your night. Embrace the tension; it’s part of the experience—and it’s what makes Exploding Kittens such a memorable party game.
