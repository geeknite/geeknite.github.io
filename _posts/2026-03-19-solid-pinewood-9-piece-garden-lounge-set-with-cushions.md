### Weather and storage
Outdoors means outdoors, and Pinewood appreciates sun as much as rain and seasonal changes. The protective finish helps with percent chance of water seeping in, but a heavy downpour will require you to move cushions to shelter. The cushions dry relatively quickly, but if you leave them soaked for too long you risk mildew or an unfortunate faint smell that your guests pretend not to notice. A simple routine is to remove cushions when rain is forecast or keep them under a cover to extend their life. This habit also keeps the overall look fresh, because a damp cushion tends to dampen a party mood more effectively than a soggy pizza box late at night.

### Cleaning and upkeep
Cleaning is the boring but crucial part of any outdoor furniture routine. Wipe down the frame with a damp cloth to remove dust and pollen. If you notice any scuffs, a light sanding followed by a fresh sealant can breathe new life into the wood. Cushions fare best with occasional light vacuuming to remove dust and stray leaves. If a stubborn stain hits the fabric, check for colorfastness on a hidden seam first, then treat with a fabric-safe cleaner. The goal is to keep everything looking cohesive rather than letting a stubborn stain turn your patio into a memorial of careless summer afternoons.
