## How to Play Pit Deluxe: A Quick Start Guide
Pit, at its heart, is simple: players shout and trade commodities until someone shouts the magic number of successful trades and wins the round. Deluxe adds flavor, but the bones are still familiar.

### Setup and baseline rules
- Gather players (the more, the merrier—generally 3–8 works best, though we’ve seen 10+, with mixed results). 
- Shuffle the commodity cards and deal a hand to each player. In Pit you don’t keep cards in front of you—your goal is constant trading, and you’ll physically grab and push cards toward other players as you declare trades.
- Place Market tokens and the Market deck within reach. Ring the bell to start the first round.
- The goal is to complete a set number of trades before the market resets or the round ends due to Market effects.

### Turn structure and trading dynamics
- On your turn, you call out a trade—“Anyone want my Sugar for your Coffee?”—and attempt to swap with other players, either openly or via multi-party trades. The tension comes from timing: you want to trigger trades while your opponents are mid-banter and distracted by someone else’s shouts.
- When Market effects are triggered, expect twists: a sudden price shift reduces or elevates the value of commodities, forcing you to rethink your plan mid-crash-and-borrow-some-courage mode. The Market deck can be merciless, in the best possible way.
- The bell rings to end a round—unless you’re one of the bold, brash trade czars who want to push through a last-minute avalanche of deals before you’re out of the round, which leads to glorious chaos and a chorus of “Stop changing the rules!”

### The two-minute pit test: speed and chaos in a bottle
If you want a rapid-fire burst of energy, pit a group of friends who have committed to a “two-minute merciless trading sprint.” The bell becomes your metronome, the Market cards your wildcards, and the room your stage. The result is a performance piece about commerce, friendship, and the universal truth that some people are allergic to getting their trade done before the bell rings again.

## Strategy: How to Win Without Losing Your Voice
There are a few paths to victory in Pit Deluxe, and the best one depends on your table’s temperament: loud, boisterous, or strategically profane in a way that makes your aunt smile and your cousin shake his head.

### General tips
- Learn your neighbors’ favorite commodities. If you know who’s after Gold and who’s hoarding Coffee, you can orchestrate trades with surgical timing rather than brute force.
- Mind the Market. Don’t ignore the Market deck. It can flip your entire strategy in a heartbeat, so a flexible plan is crucial. Think of it as a tiny, merciless economist perched on your shoulder.
- Use the bell to your advantage. Ring it to end a round before others are ready, or to start a fresh trading frenzy. It’s both a weapon and a badge of honor—like a referee who occasionally loses control of the match.
- Don’t ignore the social aspect. Pit is as much about reading people as it is about counting commodities. The best players play the room as well as the cards.

### Advanced tactics
- The “feint and flood” trick: start a trade that you don’t intend to complete, causing others to retaliate, and then pivot to a different, more favorable trade when their guard is down.
- Market-conditioned prioritization: when Market tokens change values, decide whether to chase a hot commodity or pivot to one that’s about to spike. Predicting a Market swing can win you rounds before the bell even rings.
- End-game timing: as rounds wind down, the pressure rises. In Pit Deluxe, you can often force a handful of decisive trades in a short window by triggering a flurry of rapid negotiations—just be ready to shout your lungs out.

### Common mistakes to avoid
- Over-relying on shouting: tone and timing matter as much as volume. If you’re the loudest voice in the room but never seal a trade, you’ll be the comedic relief, not the victor.
- Forgetting the Market: ignoring Market effects can tank your strategy. It’s not cheating to check the deck for potential twists; it’s informed gaming.
- Cluttering the table with too many trades: you want a chaotic vibe, but too many simultaneous trades can freeze the table. Keep your eyes on the prize and let others do their dramatic performances too.

- Noise level: If you’re allergic to shouting, this is not the game for you. Pit Deluxe is loud in a way that could convince your neighbors you’ve started a small-town festival.

- Pit Travel/Junior variants: Deluxe sits at a sweet spot between portability and heft. If you want a game you can transport to a friend’s house while still feeling “special,” this is a strong contender.
