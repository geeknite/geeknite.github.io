## Overview of the ZCD001 Tiles Set of 9
### What’s in the box
The Tile Set of 9 is a modular pack designed to mix and match with the standard Zombicide 2nd Edition board. Expect nine sturdy cardboard tiles, each depicting a different room or corridor style common to the Zombicide world. The tiles are designed to slot together in multiple configurations, letting you craft mazes of interior hallways, hazard-filled labs, and claustrophobic corridors. The goal is to expand the map variety without needing to buy a whole new set of scenarios. It is a neat optimization move: more layout options with less box clutter.

### Material and build quality
These tiles are printed cardboard with a robust, paper-backed finish that holds up to game-night chaos. The edge cutting is clean, the corners are squared off reasonably well, and the surface print sports crisp lines and bold color treatment that complements the base game art. They are not glossy museum pieces, but they don’t look cheap either. The texture strikes a balance between a nice tactile feel and practical durability when stacked with tokens and minis. If you are prone to stacking boards in a carry bag, you will want a modest sleeve or a thin protective sleeve for the tile faces to prevent edge wear.

### Design and art direction
The art style stays faithful to Zombicide 2nd Edition with high-contrast interiors, zombie menace lurking in shadows, and enough architectural flavor to sell the mood. Expect boarded interiors, lab zones with hazard signage, and doors that scream a cinematic groan when opened in the heat of a turn. The color palette aligns with the base game for seamless map continuity. As with most CMON products, the tiles carry a cohesive look that feels like it belongs to the same family as your existing minis and tokens. If you are a stickler for visual consistency, you will appreciate the polish here.

### Size, scale, and compatibility
The tiles follow the standard Zombicide grid scale used by the second edition base game. They fit flush with the official map tiles and with the same linework that governs the board’s alignment. ## How the set fits into gameplay

The primary value of the ZCD001 tiles is a quick expansion of layout options without needing to redraw or reprint. In a typical session you can drop a couple of these into the map to create new sightlines, alternate choke points, or unexpected rooms that change the pacing of an objective. ## Tips, tricks, and game-night hacks
### Layout ideas for maximum tension
- Create a narrow U-shaped hallway with two doors on opposite walls to force sequential decisions and adds a choke point for the approaching undead.
- Build a small lab area with a single entrance and a risk zone in the middle. If a trap tile sits in the middle, you can trigger tense decision-making as players debate risk vs. reward.
- Pair a couple of tiles with a longer corridor to encourage tunneling behavior from zombies, which can generate suspense and quick pacing shifts during a late game surge.

### House rules and tweaks to boost drama
- Add a rule where certain tiles have a hidden objective token that unlocks a bonus survivor if found. This adds a scavenger feel and encourages exploration.
- Allow partial tile reveals: players draw a tile face-down and reveal it only when certain conditions are met, increasing uncertainty and tension.
- Introduce a dynamic spawn rule where zombie spawns are influenced by which tile is currently active, rewarding strategic layout design.

### Storage and care practices
- Use a slim binder or a shallow box with combinable dividers to keep tiles separated by type rather than shuffled loosely in a bag.
- Keep a small microfiber cloth handy to wipe down smudges from the surface after a long night of rolling dice and slamming table edges with enthusiasm.
- Consider labeling the back of each tile with a soft marker to help you remember its intended layout for quick setup in the middle of a long campaign.
