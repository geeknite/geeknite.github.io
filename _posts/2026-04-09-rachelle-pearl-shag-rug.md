## Practical Considerations
### Cleaning and care
Shag rugs demand a bit more attention than their flat-woven cousins. Regular vacuuming on a low to medium setting helps keep surface fibers upright and avoids matting. For deeper cleaning, a hand vacuum or a suction tool with a gentle brush setting is ideal to reach the base without tugging on fibers. Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean, dry cloth to prevent staining. For more stubborn stains, a mild rug shampoo following the manufacturer instructions will usually do the trick. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness and avoid chemical reactions that would awaken your inner drama queen.

If you have pets, expect some routine fur shedding and occasional pilling over the course of time. The Pearl rug is relatively forgiving, and with regular care it maintains a soft, inviting surface. For odor management, consider airing the rug on a dry, sunny day when possible; sunlight can help refresh fibers and reduce odor buildup after long gaming sessions or potluck nights.

### Allergy considerations
Synthetic fibers can be more hypoallergenic than natural fibers for some folks, but dust will accumulate. The Pearl rug is non-porous enough to resist some allergy-causing particles yet will still benefit from regular vacuuming and a quick shake-out if you can manage it. If allergies are a primary concern, pair the rug with a HEPA vacuum and keep air filtration in good shape to maximize comfort during long sit-downs with your favorite streaming service.

### Foot traffic and wear patterns
In high traffic zones, expect gradual flattening along walkways. The cloud-like texture is forgiving, but it is not invincible. Rotating the rug every few months helps distribute wear evenly and keeps the pile from taking on an unnatural sheen in the most-used areas. If you want to preserve the fluffy look in the long run, consider a rug pad and avoid placing heavy furniture directly atop the rug without adequate support. A few strategic furniture placements can protect the most vulnerable spots from flattening and keep the rug looking fresh longer.

## Quick Maintenance Checklist
- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting; avoid high suction that could pull fibers.
- Rotate the rug to encourage uniform wear every 2 to 3 months.
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing which can push stain deeper into fibers.
- Use a rug pad to improve comfort and reduce movement.
- For tough stains, follow the rug manufacturer’s cleaning instructions and test on a small area first.
