## Care, safety, and daily use: making it last

Safety and maintenance matter a lot when a rug is for a kid’s room. A few practical tips to extend the life of a handmade tufted rug include:

- Non-slip backing or rug pad: essential to prevent slips on hard floors. It also reduces bunching and keeps the dragon in its proud pose.
- Vacuuming: use a gentle setting and avoid aggressive suction that could loosen fibers. A handheld vacuum or a vacuum with an adjustable height works well for tufted rugs.
- Spot cleaning: for spills, blot gently with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent solution. Avoid harsh chemicals that could fade colors or degrade the fibers.
- Sun exposure: prolonged direct sunlight can fade colors. If possible, rotate the rug or use window treatments to limit sun damage.
- Rotation: nudge the rug a few inches every couple of months to distribute wear more evenly, particularly in high-traffic play zones.

A well-made tufted rug will hold up to daily activity and still look charming after countless bedtime stories. Expect slight color variation between the original skein and its later life; this is not a defect but a charming sign of a materials-based life story. The rug becomes a veteran of adventures and a script for little legends—the kind of thing that makes parents sigh while kids giggle.
