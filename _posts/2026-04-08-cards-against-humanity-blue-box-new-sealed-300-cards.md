## How to play with the 300 card deck: a quick refresher
### Setup and basic rounds
Set your players up with a stack of White cards in each hand, and place the Black card deck in reach of the designated Card Czar for that round. The Czar draws a Black card and reads it aloud. The rest of the players select White cards from their hands that best answer the prompt, trying to be amusing, shocking, or brutally honest in some cases. The Czar picks the funniest or most fitting White card for the moment, and that player wins the round. The winner collects the Black card as a badge of honor and shenanigans proceed with the next turn.

### Flow, pacing, and party dynamics
The flow is simple, but the energy matters. In a room of ten players, you’ll experience a wave of reactions from deadpan to gasping laughter. It’s not about who wins or loses; it’s about the stories you’ll tell later: the line that made the room collectively lose it, the moment someone accidentally reveals their own dark sense of humor, and the friend who decides to reveal a White card that ends up getting more roars than the entire deck put together.

### House rules and variations
House rules make or break a CAH night. Some groups implement time limits to prevent a slide into endless deliberation, others allow “stealing” rounds where players can swap in a card once per game. The Blue Box’s 300 cards are a good platform for experimenting with quick-fire rounds, where each player has to play a card within five seconds or take a forfeit sip of something non-fatal. It’s all about balancing speed, wit, and keeping the table comfortable for everyone involved.

## Gameplay tips and house rules for a smoother night
- Time boxes: Give each player 10 seconds to pick a White card per round to keep the momentum up. If they miss the window, they draw the forfeit card and reshuffle back into the deck. This avoids dead air and eye-rolling.
- Theme nights: Build rounds around a theme, like 90s nostalgia, sci fi mashups, or workplace culture. It’s amazing how many White cards suddenly become perfect for a “classic corporate retreat” prompt when you steer the night with a theme.
- House unlimited: Allow a one-time “bold choice” round where players can submit two White cards for a single Black prompt. The Czar picks the best or most ridiculous, creating a dramatic moment that can pivot a game night into a legendary story.
- Safe zones: Decide upfront which topics are off-limits for certain players and respect that. The Blue Box is great, but we’re not here to ruin someone’s weekend with a punchline swap that hits a sore spot.
