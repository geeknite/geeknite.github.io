## Overview
### What is Go Fuc Yourself
Go Fuc Yourself is a party card game for adults that leans into the kind of humor that would make a parent shake their head and a friend clutch their stomach laughing. The core loop is simple: players draw cards, respond to prompts, and the table votes on the best (or worst) answer. The result is a game that thrives on bold takes, quick wit, and the kind of brutal honesty that makes brunch conversations feel tame by comparison.

This is not a game for family gatherings or office parties where HR wonders why the holiday playlist has turned into a roast. It is, however, a superb choice for a group that enjoys improv, roasting sessions, and the occasional off-color joke that everyone pretends they didn’t just laugh at in a crowded room.

## How to Play: Setup and Turn Structure
### Setup
1) Gather players who are up for an adult humor ride. 2) Shuffle the deck. 3) Determine the first judge. The judge rotates, which is critical for keeping the game from turning into a single-person roast and ensuring every voice gets a chance to deliver spicy takes. 4) Deal each player a hand size that suits your group’s attention span. Typical setups range from manageable to ridiculous; the game encourages bigger hands for bigger laughter.

### The flow of a turn
On a typical turn, a player draws a prompt card and then crafts an answer card from their hand. The judge reads the prompt aloud, the responding player lays down their answer, and everyone votes on the best fit. The winning response earns points (and bragging rights, which in many cases is more valuable than the actual points). The round structure is designed to be quick and punchy, ensuring the room stays engaged and not doomscrolling on sociotechnical anxiety while waiting for someone to pick a winner.

### Handling the prompts
Prompts range from savage to silly, with a few that are so absurd you’ll hear choking laughter before you even read the end. The humor is deliberately adult, tackling relationships, dating, pop culture, and the universal truth that humor tends to escalate when people are just a tad worried about the implications of their own answers. If you’re not comfortable with a certain prompt, you can simply pivot—this is a game where reading the room matters almost as much as reading the card.

## Strategy and Playstyle Tips
### Know your crowd
The strongest strategy is reading the room. If folks are conservative, lean into clever, suggestive humor rather than anything explicit. If your crew is a roast-spirited bunch, embrace the bold, punchy, and slightly outrageous responses. The key is balancing shock value with cleverness; the cards reward wit over mere offensiveness.

### Manage your hand effectively
Because you’re drawing and playing prompts in rapid succession, card management matters. Save a few flexible answers for the prompts that ride the line between witty and rude. Consider holding onto your most outrageous responses for moments when the judge is most receptive to boldness. A well-timed zinger can turn the entire round in a single read.

### The judge as a co-star
Rotate the judge with care. A rotating judge who is in on the joke and keeps a consistent vibe helps maintain a cohesive energy. The judge should feel like a co-star who steers the scene, not a referee putting out flames with a water bottle. A good judge recognizes when a response is a crowd-pleaser and permits the room to enjoy the moment rather than forcing a forced consensus.

### Optional house rules for newcomers
To ease newcomers into the chaos, you can implement house rules like a soft-mantle limit on per-round outrage, or give players the option to exchange a filler card if a prompt is too spicy for the room. These tweaks help set expectations and make the experience more accessible while preserving the core humor that makes Go Fuc Yourself memorable.
