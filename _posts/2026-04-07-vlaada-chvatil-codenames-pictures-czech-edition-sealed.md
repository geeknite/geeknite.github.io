## Gameplay and mechanics

Codenames Pictures replaces the word clues with image clues. The spymaster gives a clue that is a single word followed by a number, guiding teammates to interpret a set of images that best match the clue. This is where artistry, symbolism, and the chaotic joy of misinterpretation collide. The distance between a clue and the pictures it touches is part art, part luck, and part your ability to resist overanalyzing a napkin drawing from your three-year-old.

### How the game flows

- Spymaster gives a clue word and a number. The number indicates how many images on the board are tied to the clue, but beware, you might miscount like a caffeinated puzzle solver.
- Teammates discuss and guess images one at a time. They may stop after hitting the correct images or go for more, but they risk hitting red agents or the assassin card, which ends the turn badly for the guessing team.
- The first team to identify all of their agents wins. The pace is fast, and the table usually breaks into laughter or heated debates about why a picture of a moon is not related to the word night.

### Strategy tips

- Look for clusters of images with a shared theme. A single clue can tie together a handful of images if the team is willing to interpret boldly.
- Avoid over-connecting. If you squeeze six images under one clue, you might wind up handing victory to the opposing team on a silver platter—plus painful groans about your creative license.
- Communicate clearly but cleverly. The best clues are memorable without becoming cryptic. Some players will try to bait you into overthinking a simple image; resist that urge or enjoy the chaos.

The sealed CGE edition helps in this regard by offering crisp imagery that reduces confusion during frantic rounds. In my testing, the clarity of the image cards is a welcome upgrade when the lights are low and the beverages are high-spirited.
