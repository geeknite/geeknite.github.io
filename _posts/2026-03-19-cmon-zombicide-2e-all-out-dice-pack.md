Welcome to another foray into the world of tabletop mayhem, where six faces of doom become five degrees of chaos when you shake the bag. The All-Out Dice Pack for Zombicide 2E is CMON's way of saying, we hear you, you glorious dice goblin, you want more feedback on your rolls, more color in your battlefield, and yes, more ways to curse a single undead that just won't stay down. Is this the kind of accessory that transforms a good zombie hungry table into a legendary, memory-laden epic, or is it a shiny piece of plastic that sits on your shelf like a trophy for the time your group argued about dice color for 37 minutes? Strap in, traveler, because we are going to roll through the numbers, the vibes, and the existential crunch of shipping costs that threaten to annihilate your entire budgeting plan.

What’s inside? The All-Out Dice Pack is not a mystery box that rattles only with potential; it is a curated set designed to complement Zombicide 2E's core mechanics. Here’s what you typically get: a complete set of combat dice in multiple colors, each die sporting zombie silhouettes and crisp pips that distinguish survivors from walkers in a split-second flash of color. There are also some mission-specific dice that remind you which actions are currently open in your campaign, a dice tray or bag to keep the living room from turning into a hazard zone, and a few spare spares for when your table becomes a dice hurricane. The packaging is sturdy enough to survive the siege of a typical gaming night; if you have a coffee addiction, it will continue to resist any impulse to throw the dice across the room in a fit of passion.

On the table, the All-Out Dice Pack delivers a sensory upgrade. The dice are visibly heavier than your average craps cup special; they thunk onto the table with satisfying weight. The colorways are chosen to reduce cognitive load during combat: you can tell at a glance which dice belong to your character, which dice belong to the Zombies, and which dice belong to the neutral zone that nobody should touch except in the event of a dramatic misclick. The extra dice reduce the mental overhead of counting or swapping dice mid-round; you simply reach for the color that corresponds to your current action and go. The tactile feel is a treat; the corners are rounded with a careful absence of sharp edges that would ruin your table or your manicure. If you are a devotee of resin dice with sharp corners that sting when you drop them on your gaming mat, the All-Out Pack errs on the side of comfortable.

In terms of mechanical impact, you should expect some improvements but not a miracle. The 2E version of Zombicide introduced new survivors, new walkers, and a new cadence to the combat cycles. The All-Out Dice Pack amplifies the palette of outcomes without rewriting the underlying probabilities. You still roll to determine hits, wounds, and critical outcomes; you still face the same odds you fell in love with and feared in the base game. What changes is the speed and clarity of decision-making. With color-coded dice, you can quickly judge whether a failed roll is a lack of dice, a misfortune in your math, or just a cruel twist of fate from the last session's save file.

The unboxing experience is a mixed bag of delight and practicality. If you are seeking the thrill of a perfect unbox, the All-Out Dice Pack offers a solid, pleasing presentation. The dice are tucked in a compact tray, with a minimal but effective insert that keeps them from rattling for the entire car ride home. The dice themselves are a tactile upgrade over generic dice, with smoother edges and more balanced weight distribution that makes each roll feel like a small performance. If you travel to conventions, you might appreciate the way the pack packs into a carry-on with room to spare for the rest of your game collection.

In terms of value, the pack sits in a tier that is not the cheapest on the block and not the most expensive either. If you already own a well-curated set of base dice, you may find yourself asking whether you need another set that is essentially the same shape but with different colorways and a couple of extra specialty dice. However, the value proposition becomes clearer if you consider the time saved in play sessions; fewer moments of counting dice and shuffling pieces mean more actual game time, more narrative, and less time spent rewriting your own notes after a miscount. The dice are durable; replacements for lost dice can be tricky when you are in the middle of a campaign, so having a dedicated set can prevent the moment when a lone D6 becomes a bargaining chip in the keeps the group together negotiations.

Compatibility with Zombicide 2E is a strong point. The All-Out Dice Pack is designed for this edition, with color palettes and die shapes chosen to complement the survivor and zombie icons on the tokens. If you play a lot of co-op play with a house rule that involves heavy use of new editions, this pack can help maintain a consistent baseline for dice results across players. Conversely, if you are playing older editions or different CMON titles, you might not notice as much synergy; your savings on mental overhead might be offset by the need to switch color themes mid-campaign if you swap games mid-night.

The community response to the All-Out Dice Pack has been enthusiastic. Fans of the game have praised the improved readability and the tactile satisfaction of landing a big hit with a bold red die or clutching a green die for a daring stealth mission. Critics point out that the pack does not fundamentally alter the risk calculus of the game; it does not make walkers faster, nor does it rewrite the damage tables. What it does do is create a more visceral sense of control and momentum, which can be the difference between a table that sputters and a table that roars.

If you are comparing alternatives, you might look at other third-party dice sets marketed for Zombicide or generic dice color themes for cooperative games. The trade-off is that many third-party attempts focus on aesthetics or the novelty of glow-in-the-dark dice rather than the crisp readability and official-tie-in color-coding that the All-Out Pack offers. In the end, the decision often comes down to your table's appetite for color and drama versus your budget and desire for official branding. For a lot of groups, the All-Out Dice Pack is the goldilocks option: not too flashy, not too bland, just enough difference to feel like a meaningful upgrade without tipping the balance of the game.

Aesthetic notes are worth a small paragraph. The design team behind the All-Out Dice Pack has leaned into the zombie-shell aesthetic without veering into gimmick territory. You will notice the silhouettes on the dice align with the mortis theme of Zombicide 2E, which helps with quick recognition during chaotic moments. The sense of life around the table is heightened by the dice's color variety, and for players with color vision concerns, CMON has included color choices that aid distinguishing survivors from zombies in most lighting conditions. If you have a streaming corner or a content creation side hustle, you will appreciate the crisp visuals and the way dice appear on camera when you roll them in your latest live session.

For the table's social dynamic, the All-Out Dice Pack tends to shorten bar-turned-weneed-to-count moments that happen early in sessions. You can tell at a glance who has the advantage, who needs to use a modifier, and who is simply hoping for a clutch die re-roll. In many campaigns, those micro-decisions accumulate into a meta-narrative: a shared memory of a bold risk that paid off because the blue dice rode to victory. The narrative value is hard to quantify, but if you ask any long-time Zombicide player, they will tell you that a good dice set can contribute to stories of near-misses and heroic saves that you will retell at future game nights.

One thing the All-Out Dice Pack cannot fix is the game's pacing if your group is prone to analysis paralysis or extended planning phases. Dice color-coding helps, but it won't magically reduce the time it takes to set up a scenario or resolve a stubborn stalemate. If anything, the pack encourages a little more speed because it minimizes the mental overhead of matching dice to action types. The real power is in that little nudge toward momentum, the moment where you decide to push forward on a risky plan and you can actually watch it play out because your dice are telling you, in thousands of color-coded micro-moments, that you likely have something to go for.

The audio-visual dimension matters. When you spread the dice on the table, the surface noise—the cadence of dice landing on a mat—becomes part of the performance. If you are a player who enjoys a little extra ambience, you can pair the dice with a background soundtrack and a dramatic voiceover describing your fate as you roll that red D8 into a critical hit and feel the group exhale in a chorus of celebration. If you play with a live stream, the color-coded dice look standout on camera and reduce the need to label every die with tiny stickers or permanent markers. In short, the pack contributes to a smoother, more cinematic experience without requiring a choreographed dance of dice management.

From a community perspective, the feedback loop around the All-Out Dice Pack has included a few recurring themes. First, players love the ergonomics and readability. Second, some players express concern about whether the added variety translates into meaningful differences in the actual dice outcomes. Third, a subset of players would prefer even more customization options, such as modular dice stands to help with portability or a larger set of replacement dice. CMON has historically listened to the fan base, with limited but meaningful updates in subsequent runs. If you are the type who collects every iteration of a product, you might be tempted to grab this pack for the shelf as much as for the table, enjoying the aesthetic accord it brings to your Zombicide 2E display.

In terms of long-term impact on campaigns, the pack's most tangible benefit is in streamlining the pace. With fewer moments spent peering at dice, you can devote more energy to narrative beats and plan-driven strategy. If you run a weekly campaign night with multiple players, the time saved per session can accumulate into a few extra turns worth of plot progression by the end of a month. That matters. The game is designed around a sense of escalating threat and temporary success; anything that enhances the sense of momentum without compromising balance tends to be welcomed by veteran groups and new players alike.

On the topic of build quality and maintenance, the dice should be field-serviceable for several campaigns. They will likely tolerate the occasional drop onto a hard surface and can be cleaned with a microfiber cloth if you want to wipe away the stray chalk dust that collects on a gaming mat. If you travel to conventions, you may appreciate the way the pack packs into a carry-on with room to spare for the rest of your game collection.

In conclusion, the All-Out Dice Pack for Zombicide 2E offers a well-executed blend of utility and style. It does not rewrite the game's core mechanics, but it enhances the sensory and cognitive flow of play. The dice are readable, balanced, and thematically appropriate. The packaging is practical, the unboxing experience earns a mild delight, and the on-table experience tends to speed things up in a productive way. If you enjoy Zombicide 2E at any depth, this is the kind of accessory that earns its keep in your regular rotation. The value proposition is strongest for groups who play regularly and/or stream their sessions, where the extra texture and speed can translate into a more engaging viewer experience and longer, more focused play sessions.

If you want to elevate your nights with a splash of color and a soundtrack of dice on a mat, this is the pack you want on your shelf.
