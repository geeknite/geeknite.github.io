### Assembly and Installation

The assembly process is where many modular office setups either shine or reveal their truest personality: chaos. The GOF 1P tries to minimize the chaos quotient with clearly labeled panels, a concise hardware kit, and a step-by-step progression that seems designed to test your patience in the most wholesome way possible. The instruction manual uses diagrams that are not shy about asking you to hold pieces at odd angles while you screw in a bolt with a magnetic screwdriver that is probably magnetized to your captain-level patience. Expect at least 60-90 minutes for a single person with moderate mechanical aptitude. If you’ve never assembled a desk in your life, you may want a friend with a basic tool kit and a sense of humor to time out the screws with you.

Tips for assembly:
- Sort screws by size and mark the panels with sticky notes so you don’t end up turning a left panel into a right one after you’ve had a coffee break.
- Use a soft mallet sparingly; you’re building furniture, not beating a drum solo.
- Don’t over-tighten; the veneer can crack if you treat it like a spaceship hull. You’re not building a rocket; you’re assembling a desk.

## Ergonomics and Comfort

Ergonomics is the silent hero of any workstation. If your back doesn’t hurt after eight hours, you’re probably either lying to yourself or you’re floating in a dimension where chairs come with built-in support and time-bending cushions. The GOF 1P is designed with a standard desk height (roughly 29 inches) that suits most adult humans. If you’re taller than seven feet, or your colleague is a giraffe, you may want to adjust your chair height and, if possible, your monitor height to avoid a downward glares at the screen that could cause neck strain.

The privacy panels are a double-edged sword for ergonomics: they help you focus on your tasks, but they can also impede airflow if your office’s HVAC system is a tad aggressive. The recommended solution is simple: place a small desk fan on the corner or orient the panels to avoid a direct wind tunnel on your forearms. The desk surface itself has enough space for a dual-monitor setup, a keyboard, a mouse, and a small horror of sticky notes that occasionally becomes a decorative mural of your mental state. If you’re a keyboard raver, you’ll appreciate the space to rest your wrists and the anti-slip surface to keep your keyboard from sliding during an epic typing session.

## Unboxing, Maintenance, and Care

Unboxing is half the nostalgic joy of a new desk. The GOF 1P ships in a box that says “this will be a thing you own and not a liability you’ll trip over during a late-night sprint to the printer.” Inside, you will find the panels, the desk slab, the privacy tall boards, and a surprising number of small fasteners that will eventually cohabit with your screwdrivers and the drawer magnets you haven’t used since 2012. The hardware kit is compact enough to feel like a museum exhibit about small metal parts that do important things. A good tip: keep a small magnetic tray handy so you don’t lose the 1/4" bolts to the void behind your desk where missing bolts go to retire.

Care is straightforward: wipe with a microfiber cloth to remove dust. If you spill coffee or juice, blot it quickly; veneer surfaces are surprisingly forgiving but prefer to be wiped rather than left to create a stubborn coffee ghost. For the metal frame, a light spray of anti-rust spray every few years is enough; you don’t need a full-on car-care regime for an office desk. If you live in a home with a pet hamster that runs on wheels and occasionally climbs your cables, you may want to add cable sleeves to prolong cable life and prevent the little menace from nibbling on cords.

Warranty details are essential for any major purchase, and GOF tends to offer a standard warranty covering manufacturing defects and parts. Always register your product for warranty support and keep your receipt handy in case your random coworker decides to borrow it for a demonstration on how to assemble furniture with minimal patience.
