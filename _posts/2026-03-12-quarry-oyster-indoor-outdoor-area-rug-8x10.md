## How to Choose an Outdoor Rug: A Quick Checklist
- Size and layout: For an 8x10 rug, confirm the room dimensions and furniture footprint to ensure there’s a comfortable border around the rug. It’s the difference between a rug that looks integrated and one that looks miscast in a scene.
- Material and maintenance: If you have kids or pets, prioritize moisture resistance and easy cleaning. If you’re climate-chasing with heavy humidity, consider quick-dry synthetic fibers that resist mold.
- Color and wear: Neutral tones like Quarry Oyster are forgiving, but you’ll still want a color that complements your furniture and the room’s natural light exposure.
- Weather exposure: If it’ll be left outdoors year-round, ensure it’s rated for weather and sun exposure. Indoor-only rugs tend to fade and show wear fast if used outside.
- Budget and warranty: Check for warranty coverage on fading, fraying, and structural integrity. A little protection goes a long way when you’re dealing with outdoor elements.
