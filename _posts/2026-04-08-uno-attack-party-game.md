## What is UNO Attack

UNO Attack is a special edition of the beloved Uno game with a built-in card launcher. The launcher sits like a tiny, cheerful warhead on the edge of the table, ready to spit out a handful of cards whenever the central device is triggered. The device works in the background, adding suspense to every turn. It is powered by batteries and is designed to dispense anywhere from zero to a surprising number of cards at unpredictable moments. This randomness is at the heart of the game’s appeal and also its potential downfall, depending on your tolerance for chaotic draw piles.

The core rules stay familiar: you must match either color or number or play an action card. There are still Draw Two, Skip, Reverse, and Wild cards, and the occasional wild card still lets you reinterpret the entire color landscape like a chess grandmaster rearranging a Rubik’s cube. The Launcher, however, changes the tempo of the game. A calm turn can suddenly devolve into a sprint as a cascade of cards erupts, sending not just momentum but laughter and mild chaos across the table.

## How to play UNO Attack

### Setup
Place the launcher on the table, ideally within reach of every player but not so close that it becomes a hazard. Shuffle the deck and deal seven cards to each player. The goal remains simple: be the first to shed all your cards. If your hand runs low, you shout Uno and hope the launcher doesn’t decide to surprise you mid-sentence.

The launcher is not just a visual gimmick; it interacts with certain cards. When a formulated action card is played that triggers the draw function, the launcher may spit out a random number of cards. Your strategy shifts from pure color matching to card-anticipation and the occasional bluff about imagined color sequences to coax a misplay from an opposing player.

### Basic Rules with a Launcher Twist
- On your turn, you play a card that matches color or number or use a Wild/+4 Wild to switch colors.
- If the card text or your fate requires you to draw, you do so from the launcher? or from the top of the deck depending on the exact edition. Some variants yield a standard draw of one or two cards; others can trigger a surprising cascade.
- The game ends when a player successfully plays their last card and yells Uno, or when the chaos demands a mercy pass due to a card avalanche.

Note that the exact function of the launcher can vary by edition and even by unit. It is not a precise machine; it is a chaotic engine designed to spark reactions more often than precise rule enforcement. Because of this, every playthrough has its own micro-narrative, a story of who dodged the flying cards, who laughed until their glass of juice spilled, and who politely called a cease-fire while the dog retrieved a fallen piece of the action.

## House Rules and Variants

One of UNO Attack’s charms is that it invites communities to tailor the chaos. In practice, many groups settle on house rules that preserve the core chaos while dropping the occasional card that drags the pace excessively. A few common house-rule tweaks include:

- Allowing a quick pause between explosions so players can catch their breath and make a ridiculous quip about the last draw.
- Integrating Uno Spin or other variants as a prelude or epilogue to the Attack session for players who crave additional spice.
