### The deck details: Standard Face, 18 Red, 17 Blue

#### Card design and standard face
- The pips: Standard, clean, and crisp. The numbers use large, easy-to-read fonts with generous spacing, which helps during rapid deals or when you’re performing a trick that relies on accurate counting.
- The face cards: Jacks, Queens, Kings with the classic, slightly stylized Bicycle artwork. The court cards retain enough character to be identifiable at a glance but not so ornate that they slow you down in speed games or cardistry.

#### Material and finish
- Card stock: Expect solid mid-weight stock with a durable feel. It’s not as flimsy as bargain-bin decks, but it’s not as heavy as premium setups used in professional magic. It’s the sweet spot for casual magic, home games, and the occasional board game adaptation that wants a tactile experience without the weight of a luxury set.
- Finish: Air-cushion finish. This finish is designed to improve handling by reducing friction between cards, which helps with shuffles and flourishes. It also tends to make a deck feel slightly grippier, which can be a blessing for beginners trying to control their deals and a mild annoyance for seasoned shufflers who prefer a slicker glide.
