## What Fortune Row is all about

Fortune Row brings you a compact toolbox of prompts designed to spark scenes, characters, and twists in neon-tinged districts. The deck contains approximately 120 story cards. Cards are divided into categories including Scene Seeds, NPC Seeds, Twist Seeds, Setting Seeds, Event Seeds, and Objective Seeds. Each card carries a concise prompt and a flavor blurb that hints at mythic underpinnings and urban grit.

The production is solid. Card stock is sturdy, and the finish holds up to a lot of shuffling and table-top legerdemain. The art direction continues the city’s neon-punk vibe with a consistent look across seeds and scenes. You will see small motifs that invite interpretation rather than chest-thumping exposition. The deck pairs well with the base game and can mix into existing campaigns with little friction.

A slim GM guide accompanies the deck. The guide offers quick-start prompts, pacing guidance, and suggestions for remixing seeds to fit short sessions or longer campaigns. There is also a micro narrative framework to help you string seeds into a coherent arc, but the real value is how easily you can improvise when the table is primed for chaos.

A note on accessibility: seeds are text-light but concept-rich. The deck shines when the GM translates a seed into a scene with stakes and details that players can engage with. If your table relies on a lot of precise mechanical triggers, fortune row is best used as a narrative springboard rather than a mechanical checklist.

## Contents and design cues

Fortune Row opens up with a neon gate of possibilities. The 120 or so seeds are divided into six categories, enabling quick browsing during play. The Scene Seeds are the quick entry points for action, often presenting a location, a situation, and a twist that invites players to react. NPC Seeds introduce individuals who may be allies, rivals, or double-crossing informants. Twist Seeds inject moral or strategic complications that shift the session’s axis. Setting Seeds establish mood and spatial texture, from a rain-soaked courtyard to a clandestine market in a forgotten district. Event Seeds bring an external disruption that can reframe the protagonists’ goals. Objective Seeds offer a narrative anchor, a goal that stays in play across scenes.

In practice, you can use a single seed to spark a session or draw several seeds to create a sequence of scenes that slowly reveals a larger conspiracy or personal arc. The seeds are designed to be seed-like rather than blueprint-like, giving you room to interpret, improvise, and adapt.

The accompanying two-page GM guide is compact but practical. It includes play-tested prompts, pacing recommendations, and tips for weaving seeds into a longer arc. It also suggests house rules to tailor the deck to your table’s preferred speed and tone.

The feel of Fortune Row is firmly in the City of Mist wheelhouse: mythic underpinnings with urban noir, a sense of cost and consequence, and characters who exist in a world where perception can shape reality. The deck invites you to treat the city as a character with motives and a hidden agenda, not just scenery.

## How to play with Fortune Row

Fortune Row is not a rigid supplement; it is an adaptable toolkit. Here are several modes that suit different playstyles and time budgets.

### One-shot spark

If you have a single evening, you can pull a Scene Seed to set the stage, a Twist Seed to complicate things, and an NPC Seed to populate the world with color. You can keep the session tight by choosing 2-3 seeds and rotating them to craft a short arc that resolves in one session.

### Night in the district

If you prefer a slightly longer arc, draw multiple seeds and weave them into a micro-campaign. Start with a Setting Seed to establish the district’s mood. Then add Scene Seeds to push events, NPC Seeds to populate conflicts, and a Twist Seed to escalate stakes.

### Improvisation plus arcs

Use Fortune Row as a constant companion in long campaigns. Routinely draw seeds to refresh a recurring storyline or to plug a new mystery into a character’s arc. The mix of seeds can help you pivot quickly when players chase a new lead or when the city shifts under new pressure.

### Integrating with core City of Mist rules

Fortune Row seeds sit atop the base mechanics: Mythos, Miracle, and the city’s aura of hidden powers. A seed can function as a prompt that a Mythos or a Miracle can respond to. The GM guide advises treating seeds as prompts that broaden the available choices rather than rigid instruction. The deck can be particularly potent if you use seeds to frame the clues and then let the players’ actions reveal how a mythic influence operates in the city.

## Sample seeds in action

To illustrate, here are a few seeds that capture the flavor of Fortune Row. Keep in mind these are prompts; you are free to adapt them to your table and your character’s motivations.

- Scene Seed: Under the ice-cold glow of the river market, a vendor hawks a truth that could topple a masked authority.
- NPC Seed: A street-musician who channels a minor Mythos and speaks in the cadence of weather reports.
- Twist Seed: The hero’s ally turns out to be working with a rival faction, but their motive is protective, not treasonous.
- Setting Seed: A crumbling arcade behind Fortune Row where old machines hum with the city’s memories.
- Event Seed: A carnival arrives from nowhere, bringing with it a rumored cure for a city-wide malaise.
- Objective Seed: Acquire a relic that could either uplift or unmask a dormant faction.

If you prefer a different balance of seeds, you can mix and swap seeds with the table’s mood and your group’s preferences in mind.

## GM tips and tricks

- Seed economy: Use a small handful of seeds per session to avoid overloading players with too many prompts at once. It’s better to seed the front half of the session and let later seeds emerge based on the players’ choices.
- Tie seeds to character arcs: Use seeds that intersect with players’ backstories to heighten engagement and emotional stakes.
- Lean into the city as character: Treat Fortune Row seeds as prompts that reveal city-wide interests, secrets, or power networks rather than simply obstacles in the players’ path.
- Manage tone and pacing: If a seed kicks off a grim turn, you can pivot to investigation, social intrigue, or a more action-oriented beat to maintain variety.
- Use seeds to generate moral dilemmas: Let seeds pose conflicts that require players to decide between personal loyalties, public duty, and mythic consequences.
- Track glamour and authority: The deck naturally encourages a mix of personal stakes and wider power structures. Use seeds to highlight who has influence over the city and who wants to change it.

## Quick player prompts

If you want to involve players more directly, you can adapt seeds into short prompts that players can read aloud at key moments. For example, a Scene Seed could become a player-facing prompt that invites a character to describe a location or to identify a stumbling block. A Twist Seed can become a question for a player to answer about a trait or a motive, driving the next scene.

## Example session excerpt (short)

In a night where Fortune Row’s neon fog hums with rumor, the players gather at the edge of the river-market. A Scene Seed sets the location: a vendor selling counterfeit charms that glow when they detect a lie. The GM describes the stall, the scent of street-food, and the weather of a city that never truly sleeps. An NPC Seed introduces a curious informant who speaks in clipped rhymes and carries a feather that seems to be a memory of a myth. The Twist Seed reveals that the informant is an ally with conflicting duties, and the objective seed gives the party a reason to pursue a relic before dawn. If the players choose to chase the relic, the story folds into a chase across rooftops, with a final confrontation at a forgotten arcade.

As the session unfolds, Fortune Row proves its value: it gives you prompts that can be interpreted in multiple ways, while leaving room for player choices to shape the path. The neon-lit city becomes a stage, and the players become players in the story rather than mere spectators.

## GM tips and tricks (continued)

- Seed economy: Use seeds to seed into the next session and keep a running sense of progress across weeks.
- Tie seeds to player characters: Seeds that touch on a character’s past or a secret can create compelling emotional stakes.
- Use a simple scoring mechanism to track progress and tension: glamour tokens, effect points, or narrative influence can help measure how seeds shift the balance of the story.
