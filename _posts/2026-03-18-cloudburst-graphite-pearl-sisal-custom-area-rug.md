### External notes on care and upkeep
Natural fiber rugs like sisal require a bit more attention than synthetic counterparts. They are generally more resistant to staining from minor spills than delicate silk or wool, but they benefit from regular maintenance. It is wise to vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting and avoid excessive moisture. For big spills, blot with a dry cloth and avoid rubbing, which can cause the fiber to fray. ## Size and fit: how to measure like a pro
Getting the right size is less about guessing and more about measuring the space with a laser pointer and a heroic dose of confidence. Here are practical steps you can take before you commit to custom sizing or an 8x10 purchase:
- Measure the room and identify the main furniture group that will sit on or around the rug.
- Add at least 18 inches of floor space on all sides for a standard 8x10 living room layout. If you have a smaller space, you might want to limit the overhang to 8 inches for a balanced look.
- For custom sizing, bring the exact layout to the rug maker and request a precise pattern alignment that respects the fiber direction and edge finishing.
- Don’t forget door clearance: test with the door open to ensure you can swing in and out without snagging the rug edge.

## Care and maintenance: long-term love for a natural fiber
Natural fiber rugs like sisal care a bit differently. Here are practical tips for longevity:
- Vacuum regularly with a canister vacuum on a low setting. Avoid beater bars that can snag the fiber.
- Mop or spray lightly only when needed. Excess moisture can cause the fibers to warp or shrink.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to even out wear and minimize obvious footprints.
- Address spills promptly; blot, then blot again, then blot some more. Do not rub as it can push the liquid deeper into the fiber.
- Consider applying a natural fiber protectant if you live in a high-traffic home with kids or pets.

## FAQ
- Is sisal comfortable under bare feet
  A. It offers a pleasant texture but is not as plush as wool or synthetic pile. If barefoot comfort is a priority, consider stepping up to a low pile wool blend or adding a soft rug pad to soften the feel underfoot.
- Can I put a rug pad under a custom rug
  A. Yes, using a non-slip or felt rug pad is recommended to keep the rug in place and protect flooring.
- How often should I rotate the rug
  A. Rotate every 3 to 6 months depending on traffic and sun exposure to minimize wear patterns.
- Is it suitable for kitchens
  A. Natural fiber rugs can work in kitchens if you select a dense weave and treat spills promptly. Avoid extremely wet areas or prolonged moisture exposure.
