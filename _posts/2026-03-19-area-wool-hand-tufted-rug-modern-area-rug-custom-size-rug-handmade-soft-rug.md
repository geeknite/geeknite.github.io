## Sizing, Fit, and Custom Experience
Custom sizes are the big win here. If your room is an odd shape or if you are trying to create a defined seating zone, a rug that can be tailored to fit precisely is a dream. When ordering, measure width and length carefully and consider the space around furniture to allow for natural movement of chairs and feet. You might want to leave space for a coffee table to overlap with the rug edges or pull back your sofa slightly to reveal more floor.

A good practice is to map the rug’s footprint on floor with painter’s tape. This gives you a visual of how much area will be covered and whether you like the rug to anchor the furniture or to sit more as a grounding plane for the room.

### Underlay and stability
A rug pad is a wise addition for high-traffic areas, slick floors, or homes with long-legged pets. A pad reduces movement, adds a layer of cushion, and protects against wear along the edges. It’s the unsung hero of comfort and longevity, tucked away under the rug like a secret agent of friction.

## Durability and Care
Durability in a hand tufted wool rug is a function of fiber quality, tuft density, and backing stability. Regular vacuuming with a soft brush helps maintain the pile’s integrity. For stains, act promptly with a mild detergent and cold water; avoid harsh solvents that may bleed color. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and avoid obvious high-traffic footprints.

Wool is naturally flame resistant, a nice bonus if you ever burn toast near your living space. It’s also a renewable resource, which is a nice way to feel slightly virtuous about your purchase. Keep it out of direct sunlight to minimize fading, and consider UV-filtering window treatments to preserve color.
