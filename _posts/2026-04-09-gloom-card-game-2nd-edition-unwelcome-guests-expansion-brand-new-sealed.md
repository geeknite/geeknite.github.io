## Setup and gameplay flow
Like many Gloom experiences, Unwelcome Guests is best viewed as a narrative engine with a few mechanical bumps that keep everyone honest (or dishonestly polite, depending on your mood).

### Setup overview
1. Shuffle the base game deck and the expansion’s guest, event, and condition cards as instructed in the rulebook.
2. Each player draws a starting family and a selection of loveable misfortunes for your doomed characters.
3. Place the new Guest cards into their designated deck, ready to rise from the gloom at any moment.
4. Decide the order of play, or throw it to luck by rolling a die you don’t actually own. The rules are flexible; your table’s chaos will fill in the gaps.

This setup is deliberately straightforward, designed to minimize downtime between deliveries of doom and the unstoppable drift toward inevitable tragedy. The expansion doesn’t drastically overhaul the base game’s core loop; instead, it folds in new guests and cards that feel like they’re tangentially connected to the original family’s melodramatic destiny.

### Core gameplay loop with the expansion
In Gloom, you want your characters to suffer, then you want your opponents’ characters to suffer even more. Unwelcome Guests fuels that by introducing guests who interact with the family’s happiness, misfortune, and relationships in new, often disruptive ways.
- Each turn, you may draw, play, and arrange cards to push your family toward an unfortunate fate while ensuring that a few prying guests have their say in the outcome.
- The new guests bring fresh mood shifts, which means you’ll be juggling narrative threads faster than a soap opera writer with commitment issues.
- Event and condition cards twist the story during rounds, letting you steal a moment of glow from another family member or grant a temporary advantage to a rival’s misfortune, depending on who’s drafting the drama.

The vibe remains lightheartedly grim, with humor threaded through the gloom. The tension you feel comes from both the strategic choices and the performance of players as they narrate the unfolding tragedy to your audience of cards and spectators. The Unwelcome Guests are designed to encourage banter, bluffing, and a dash of strategic misdirection.

## The Unwelcome Guests: who they are and what they do
The expansion introduces several guest archetypes that feel both thematic and mechanically prickly—in a good way. Each guest has a knack for making certain types of misfortune more likely, nudging you to rewrite your story as you go.

### Notable guest archetypes
- The Polite Specter: A guest who looks like they’re here for tea but quietly increases the odds of a doom on your least favored character. Their polite demeanor makes it hard to begrudge them the misfortune they unleash.
- The Gossip Phantom: Spreads rumors that alter how relationships are scored. This guest can turn a simple tragedy into a web of interlocking, dramatic consequences for multiple players.
- The Reluctant Wraith: A guest who wants to stay quiet but refuses to be ignored. They push for a dramatic reveal that can shift who is winning and who is losing in the same breath.
- The Helpful Horror: This one offers a tiny lifeline, but the cost—oh, the cost—usually multiplies the chaos later in the round. It’s the expansion’s way of saying, yes, you can get out of a jam, but you’ll owe it to someone else first.

Each guest card is not just a stat line; it’s a little vignette. The flavor text and art combine to make you feel like you’ve stepped into a haunted living room where your actions are the plot and your neighbors’ laughter is a valid threat. It’s storytelling masquerading as strategy, which is precisely what Gloom has been doing since the first edition.

### How they interact with base rules
- The guests often modify how misfortune cards affect a character, either by buffing the effect or by introducing a delaying countermeasure that requires you to plan multiple steps ahead.
- You may find yourself reordering your priorities to accommodate a guest that wants a dramatic theatrical moment on a late-game reveal. This is not a flaw; it’s the entire point of narrative-driven card design.
- Some guests create temporary alliances or rivalries. The social dynamic becomes part of the strategy, as you’ll want to time your doom to land when your opponents can least respond in kind.

Such interactions keep the game feeling fresh even after your tenth playthrough. The novelty lies not in endless new math but in new story threads and new ways to improvise your way to a glorious, spectacular failure.

## Strategy tips and recommended approaches
Like any good gloom-centric expansion, success comes from a blend of narrative control and tidy planning. Here are some practical tips to help you harness the chaos instead of letting it harness you:

### General strategies
- Prioritize flexible doom targets. When possible, set up a few potential endings rather than committing to a single narrative arc. The guests reward branching plans more than linear doom paths.
- Read the room. The guests react to player behavior; if someone is playing a heavily optimized, no-nonsense run, you’ll want to lean into mischief that targets that player’s favorites and vulnerabilities.
- Use the guests as misdirection. A well-timed reveal can derail a competitor’s plan just long enough for you to reclaim the narrative lead.

### Specific to the expansion’s guests
- The Polite Specter wants legitimacy; you can lean into social cleverness to coax favorable outcomes while masking your actual doom plan.
- The Gossip Phantom thrives on rumors, so use it to unsettle others and push them toward suboptimal decisions that look brilliant on the surface.
- The Reluctant Wraith rewards patience. If you’re behind, bide your time and let others burn their resources before unveiling a grand, dramatic culmination.
- The Helpful Horror rewards clever timing. Don’t overplay it; you’ll lose the moment the room realizes you owe a bigger price to someone else.

### Deck management and pacing
- Track your draws and plays. The more guests you bring into play, the more important it becomes to manage your deck’s tempo. Don’t flood the table with doom; let a few little tragedies accumulate like a well-seasoned stew.
- Don’t underestimate the power of a well-timed pause. A moment of silence to let players verbalize their fears is often worth more than a thousand doom cards.

### Solo or small-group play tips
If you’re playing solo or with a smaller group, the Unwelcome Guests can be a wonderful accelerant for narrative. Your own storytelling becomes the counterweight to the guest’s chaos, and you’ll experience the doom arcs at a crisper pace. Just be mindful that solo play amplifies any perceived unfairness, so apply a light touch when narrating dramatic reveals to avoid tipping into melodrama overload.
