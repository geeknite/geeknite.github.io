# Exploding Kittens Original Edition: A Nerdy Review of the Ultimate Party Card Game

Welcome, fellow geeks and meme connoisseurs. Today we peel back the glossy card stock and dive into Exploding Kittens Original Edition, the party game that somehow turned a cartoon cat into a cultural phenomenon and Kickstarter into a verb. If you found yourself curious about a game that mixes kitty visuals, terrible puns, and the existential dread of dodging a cartoon bomb, you are in the right place. We are going to dissect why this little box became a staple at family game nights and late-night nerd gatherings alike, and why it remains oddly relevant in a world of clone wars and crowdfunding chaos.

## Origins and why we are here

Exploding Kittens was born on the wild frontier of crowdfunding, where every creator with a decent punchline could try to coax a viable board game out of the void. The Original Edition hit Kickstarter in 2015, riding a wave of internet meme energy and a promise of quick, chaotic play that could be learned in under five minutes. The core conceit is simple and ridiculous: you are trying to avoid drawing an exploding kitten card that ends your turn (and your lifespan in this hyperbolic metaphor) while using defuse cards like laser pointers, catnip, and more bizarre tools to outplay your friends.

For context, the game was created by Elan Lee and Matthew Inman (of The Oatmeal fame) along with Shane Small. It was a perfect storm of approachable rules, snappy humor, and art that looked like it was drawn in a fevered doodle session after too many energy drinks. The Kickstarter success didn't just fund a game; it funded a culture moment. The spectacle of a cat-themed apocalypse card game became a symbol for nerds who wanted to show off their sense of humor without needing a G-rated blanket apology for the jokes.

## What is in the Original Edition

The Original Edition box is a compact fiesta of art, chaos, and a sizable deck of cat-themed chaos. Let’s break down what you actually get when you crack open the shrink wrap and pretend to be a responsible adult who knows how to handle a handful of explosive cats.

### Components

- A deck of Exploding Kitten cards and Defuse cards
- A subtle handful of other action cards with equally ridiculous names
- A rulebook that fits inside a shoebox kindness policy
- A generous dose of chaos and a box that is slightly sturdier than a procrastination habit

The art is a big part of the charm here. It leans into the cute-at-first-sight aesthetic with a twist of dark humor that lands well with a broad audience. The kittens look cartoonish enough to be friendly but just menacing enough to remind you that this is not your grandma’s card game. It’s a bold contrast that sells the party vibe while delivering a dose of savage whimsy.

### Artwork and humor

The humor lands because it sits on a spectrum between goofy and sly. You’ll see puns on the card names and pop-culture parodies that feel like Easter eggs for people who have spent more than a few nights binging cartoons and indie games. The jokes are not mean-spirited, but they do revel in chaotic absurdity. If you have a dry sense of humor, this game will likely be a moonwalk into your happy place.

Artwork is not just decoration here; it’s functional. Defuse cards and the special actions have a consistent visual language that helps players on a fast, breathless turn to understand what they can and cannot do. You don’t need to memorize a hundred pages of rules; you just need to recognize the shapes and colors and you are good to go.

## How to play in 60 seconds or less

If you want to know the bare minimum for a game night, Exploding Kittens is designed so you can learn the rules quickly and start playing almost immediately. The objective is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. Each turn, you draw a card and can play an action card to manipulate the deck, remove threats, or alter how likely you are to get exploded. The Defuse card is your lifeline, a chance to talk your way out of disaster with a witty display of deflective humor.

For those who crave structure, here is a compact walkthrough.

### Setup

- Shuffle the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck. The number of Exploding Kitten cards is one fewer than the number of players.
- Shuffle the Defuse cards into their own stack.
- Each player draws a starting hand (typically 1 Defuse and 1 other card, depending on house rules).
- Place the deck in the center. The game is ready to start when everyone is wearing their most determined game-night expression.

### Core mechanics

On your turn, you draw one card and then can play any number of action cards from your hand. Action cards might:
- Let you steal, peek, or force someone else to draw cards.
- Defuse an Exploding Kitten using Defuse cards or specific actions.
- Provide alternatives to simply drawing, like skipping or reshuffling.

The Exploding Kitten card is a trap disguised as a cute cartoon. If you draw one, you explode unless you have a Defuse card. The Defuse card can be anything from a normal defusal tactic to a comedic workaround that saves you from doom. If you don’t have a Defuse, you’re out of the game. The last player standing wins, which is an oddly satisfying end when you realize that the entire point of the game is to keep the chaos contained long enough to outlast your friends who keep “accidentally” triggering explosions.

### Exploding Kittens and defusing

The Defuse cards are the real MVPs here. They give you the option to defuse the bomb and continue the misery of the deck, turning a near-death moment into a comedic carnival of near-misses. The interplay between defuse cards and explosive cards creates suspense and surprise. It’s not just about luck; there is an element of strategic risk taking that makes you think about what to hold onto and what to play now. It’s a delicate dance of bluffing and risk management wrapped in a purring kitty bow.

### House rules and variants

Part of the charm is that you can tailor the experience to your crowd. If you have kids, you might lean toward simpler rules and shorter rounds. If you want more chaos, you can insert additional jokey cards or allow players to hold additional Defuse cards for later turns. Some groups opt for a “no looking” rule where players peek at a card before drawing, adding another layer of tension and mischief. The game is flexible enough to be a baseline for casual evenings and a springboard for wilder house rules.

## The party game fit: why Exploding Kittens endures

Exploding Kittens sits at the intersection of party games and casual strategy games. It shines in mixed-age groups where you want something quick to learn, with enough personality to spark conversation. The Original Edition is a great pick for family gatherings, game nights with friends who enjoy memes, or a nerdy party where everyone can contribute a joke and a wink.

The pacing is brisk. The moment a single Defuse card is played, the table loosens up and laughs start rolling. People lean forward, eyes widen, and you realize you’re playing a game that doesn’t demand intense strategy to be enjoyable, but it rewards clever misdirection and a bit of stingy banter. In a world full of heavy strategy games and long campaigns, Exploding Kittens offers a palate-cleanser option that still scratches the competitive itch without turning a simple night into a math lecture.

If you are looking for a social lubricant disguised as a card game, look no further. The humor, the timing, and the chaos create an atmosphere that invites participation. People who are not “gamers” can still jump in and feel like they contributed to the room’s energy. That is a rare and valuable trait in a hobby that sometimes feels like it’s only for experts with the right sleeves and sleeves of cards.

## Strategy and players: a light touch with a wink

Let’s be honest: Exploding Kittens is not a deep strategic silo. It is a social game whose value comes from interaction, not optimization. Still, there are micro-strategies worth noting: keeping a balanced hand to avoid giving opponents easy targets, timing your defuses for high-value moments, and reading the room to decide when to go all-in on an aggressive move or when to retreat into the safety of passing turns.

Do not expect a perfectly optimized plan that guarantees victory. The deck can fold you in seconds, and your best-laid plans can be undone by a hidden card or a mischievous buddy who swaps defuses like a magician swapping rabbits. The joy is in the ride, not the destination. The unpredictability is where the game earns most of its charm.

For seasoned players who want a hint of depth, we recommend pairing Exploding Kittens with short, high-energy house rules. For example, you can add a rule that certain action cards can only be used once every two rounds, which forces players to adapt and adds a mild layer of resource management without bogging the game down.

## Reprints, expansions, and the evolution of a vibe

The Original Edition has spawned a family of expansions, spin-offs, and variations that aim to keep the chaos fresh. The expansions introduce new cat-themed cards, new defusion options, and sometimes new mechanics that tilt the balance toward more aggression or more strategy. The expansions are a mixed bag in terms of quality; some feel like natural extensions that fit the humor and pace, while others feel like a cash grab wearing a cute fur coat.

If you’re chasing novelty, the expansions can provide new laughs and new card interactions. If you’re chasing simplicity and a reliable social experience, you may want to keep the base game lean and let your group decide if an expansion is worth their time. Either way, Exploding Kittens remains a recognizable emblem in the world of party games, a label you can put on a shelf and know it will spark a conversation.

### The Kickstarter myth and modern clone wars

Kickstarter is part of the story here. The Original Edition leveraged a viral concept and a straightforward pitch to fund a product that proved to be highly scalable. The success of Exploding Kittens inspired many creators to pursue crowdfunding with similarly simple premises and a lot of hype. In the years since, a flood of clone-style games followed, each trying to capture lightning in a box with varying degrees of success. The clone wars have taught the community a lesson: it’s not just about a gimmick; it’s about community, timing, and the ability to deliver on the promise of a fun experience.

## A couple of quick comparisons you might care about

- Exploding Kittens vs. UNO party edition: Both are quick, bright, and accessible, but Exploding Kittens leans harder into humor and unpredictability while UNO emphasises color matching and card counting. If you want a gut punch of laughter with your game night banter, Exploding Kittens wins on the meme front.
- Exploding Kittens vs. Exploding Kattens expansions: Expansions can add joy and variety but may fragment the group. If your circle is into trying new cards and new tactics, expansions give you more to experiment with. If you prefer reliability and speed, keep it simple with the base set.
- Digital adaptations vs. physical: Digital versions can provide a different pacing experience and accessibility for remote friends. The physical edition, however, has the tactile joy of shuffling, dealing, and throwing the deck on a table with a flourish you just cannot replicate on a screen.

## The final verdict and who should buy it

Exploding Kittens Original Edition is a celebration of chaos in a neat little box. It’s not the most profound game in your collection, but it doesn’t pretend to be. It knows its lane and plays to its strengths with finesse. If you want an ice-breaker that reliably delivers laughter in under 20 minutes, or you’re seeking a low-friction gateway game for non-gamers, this is a strong candidate. If you’re chasing deep strategy or heavy storytelling, you’ll want to look elsewhere and maybe borrow a copy for a quick dissolve of egos and a lot of cat puns.

In other words: buy it for the social energy, keep it for the memories made as your friends try not to explode from laughter. It’s a game that invites mischief and quick thinking without demanding your entire weekend to be booked around it. It’s a quirky artifact of the crowdfunding era that somehow still brings joy when pulled from a shelf and slapped onto a coffee table.
