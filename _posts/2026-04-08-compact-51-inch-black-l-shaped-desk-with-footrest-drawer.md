## Functionality: features that actually matter
### Cable management and ports
A desk is only as good as its ability to hide the chaos behind it. Our test setup included a tangle of HDMI cables, power strips, USB-C hubs, and a lint roller that somehow migrated into the cable jungle. The desk offers cable management features like a rear grommet and some space under the top to route cables. It’s not a full-blown cable management system, but it does enough to keep the spaghetti code of cables from being a headache. If you’re a power user, you’ll want to add zip ties and a pass-through grommet with a cable sleeve, but this desk gives you a clean baseline.

### Keyboard tray vs. drawer versatility
Some desks include a pull-out keyboard tray; this desk does not, but the main surface and the drawer can easily host a wireless keyboard when you adjust height and posture. If you do a lot of keyboard-heavy formatting, there are ways to repurpose the drawer as a shallow tray. Our guidance: treat the sliding drawer as a tiny workstation extension for stationery, not a full-on keyboard extension. For most users, the primary keyboard sits atop the desk while the rest of the workflow sits in the drawer where it’s easy to grab and forget you own a mechanical pencil from 2013.

### Ergonomics and posture
With a desk height typically around 29 inches, you’ll want a chair with proper lumbar support and a good footrest. The integrated footrest helps encourage a better leg angle during long sessions, which reduces the risk of the classic “my legs have become a second desk” syndrome. If you prefer to stand, you’ll notice this is not a standing desk; it’s a thoughtful compromise for those who want a sturdy, comfortable place to work without buying a new calibrator for your standing desk. We recommend pairing it with an adjustable chair and a monitor stand to minimize neck strain and maximize productivity in short bursts of intense focus.

### Surface quality and scratch resistance
In our tests, the top held up well to daily use. A few coffee spills, some snack crumbs, and a stray pencil line all clean off with a damp cloth. The matte finish tends to resist the worst of fingerprints, which is a small but satisfying feature for those of us who judge desks by the level of “new desk smell” after a week. If you’re a heavy-duty painter or a stamp collector that tends to leave paint on your desk, consider a protective pad or store a drop cloth on standby—just in case your inner MacGyver reappears.

## Maintenance and care
- Wipe with a damp microfiber cloth, then dry to avoid watermarking.
- Avoid harsh cleaners that can degrade the laminate. A mild soap solution works well for most spills.
- Periodically check the screws in the legs and the drawer slides; tighten as needed to prevent wobble.
- Keep cords organized with cable sleeves; it helps maintain the look and reduces the risk of accidental unplugging during late-night gaming sessions.

## Assembly tips and tricks
- Do not tighten every screw at once; alternate tightening to avoid warping the top surface.
- Label bags of screws; they all look the same until you’re three steps in and realize you used the wrong length for a critical bracket.
- Have a friend help with the longer panels; they’re heavy enough to audition for a role in a tech-noir movie if you drop one.
