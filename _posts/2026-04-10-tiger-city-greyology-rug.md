## Materials, Maintenance, and Longevity
### Everyday wear and stain resistance
Life happens. Spills occur. Pets sprint across the floor with a water bottle cap in their mouth and then demand to be celebrated for their athleticism. The Greyology rug’s synthetic fibers are engineered for stain resistance and easy cleanup. A damp cloth and a mild cleaner are usually enough for most mishaps. For tougher stains, a gentle blotting plus a light steam clean helps restore the pattern’s crispness without discoloration. It’s not invincible—no rug is—but the design and materials do a solid job of resisting the “end-of-the-world” stains that life loves to leave behind.

### Vacuuming and care routine
A weekly vacuum across the surface is sufficient, with attention to the edges to prevent fraying or curling. If you have pets, you’ll want a vacuum with strong suction for the pile but a brush setting that won’t snag the fibers. Pro-tip: rotate by a few degrees every month to keep the pattern from wearing in a single path. This not only extends longevity but also keeps the visual narrative fresh, which matters if you’re a blogger who covets “new rug, new vibe.”

### Durability and warranty
Most Tiger City products come with a standard warranty against manufacturing defects. For the Greyology rug, this typically covers craftsmanship issues in the first year. Do read the care quirks in the manual because the devil is in the details: some sprays and heat treatments can affect the pile or color. In short, treat it well, and it will treat your living room to many calm, geometric evenings.

## Comparisons: How it Stacks Up Against the Competition

### vs. Printed Nylon Rugs

## A Quick Buying Guide and Sizing Guide
### Sizing suggestions for common rooms
- Small rooms (<= 10x12): 5x7 or 6x9 works well as anchor pieces.
- Medium rooms (11x12 to 14x16): 8x10 or 9x12 gives a balanced look under seating groups.
- Large open-plan spaces: 9x13 or larger to define zones without literal walls.

### How to choose the right Greyology version for your space
Consider your furniture layout, light exposure, and the presence of high-footfall zones. If you have an oversized sofa and a cluster of chairs, a larger rug under the seating area helps unify the space. For smaller nooks or office corners, a smaller size can ground the desk and chair without overwhelming the scene. And if you’re pairing with a glass coffee table or metallic accents, the rug’s grayscale geometry provides a perfect contrast without clashing visually.

### Care tips to preserve color and structure
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber damage.
- Use a mild detergent or a dedicated rug cleaner; test in a hidden area first.
- Avoid direct high heat; opt for low-heat steam if needed, but keep it gentle.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear patterns.
