## A Quick Buying Guide and Sizing Guide
### Sizing suggestions for common rooms
- Small rooms (<= 10x12): 5x7 or 6x9 works well as anchor pieces.
- Medium rooms (11x12 to 14x16): 8x10 or 9x12 gives a balanced look under seating groups.
- Large open-plan spaces: 9x13 or larger to define zones without literal walls.

### How to choose the right Greyology version for your space
Consider your furniture layout, light exposure, and the presence of high-footfall zones. If you have an oversized sofa and a cluster of chairs, a larger rug under the seating area helps unify the space. For smaller nooks or office corners, a smaller size can ground the desk and chair without overwhelming the scene. And if you’re pairing with a glass coffee table or metallic accents, the rug’s grayscale geometry provides a perfect contrast without clashing visually.

### Care tips to preserve color and structure
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber damage.
- Use a mild detergent or a dedicated rug cleaner; test in a hidden area first.
- Avoid direct high heat; opt for low-heat steam if needed, but keep it gentle.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear patterns.
- Use a rug pad to reduce movement and to cushion footfalls—your knees will thank you later.

### How to stage it in a room: a quick checklist
1) Place under the main seating group to anchor the space. 2) Align the longer axis with the room’s dominant line (a wall or doorway) to emphasize a clean flow. 3) Use cushions that echo the geometric motif in textures or color. 4) Let lighting be your ally: a mix of warm and cool tones will reveal the pattern’s depth.

## Maintenance Tips: Quick Reference
- Vacuum weekly with a low-angle setting to maximize edge longevity.
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber distortion.
- Use a rug pad to minimize movement and extend pile life.
- Rotate seasonally to maintain an even look across high-traffic paths.
- For stains: dab with a mild detergent solution, then rinse lightly and blot dry.
- If you notice edge curl, don’t panic—airflow and a bit of weight will settle it in a few days. If not, a quick steam pass can re-fluff the pile.
