## How it plays in a modern group: tips for a smooth session
So you have the unopened pack now. How do you maximize the hilarity without spiraling into chaos? Here are a few practical tips to guide your game night:

- Set expectations: clarify the vibe you want. If your group loves roast humor, you are in for a full throttle experience. If you want milder banter, designate a few players as the tone keepers or choose a lighter subset of prompts.
- Player count and rotation: CAH is known for thriving with a larger pool of players. If you have fewer than four, you may want to add a rotating judge to keep energy high. If you have eight or more, designate a 60 to 90 second summary of why a card won and avoid endless debating.
- Card management: shuffle well, keep the dark jokes in the middle, and consider a card discard pile in case the same pun shows up too many times. A little turnover keeps the flavor fresh and reduces the risk of repetitive riffs.
- Theme nights: run a 90s nostalgia night with a defined theme, such as 90s cartoons, tech startup vibes, or late-night MTV. This helps the cards land with a sharper punchline when the group has a shared frame of reference.
- Safety valves: decide on a few ground rules in advance, such as no hits to sensitive topics that cross a line for your group. The beauty of CAH is in the risk, but the table should still feel safe for everyone involved.
