## Care and maintenance
Care for jute requires a light touch and consistent habits rather than dramatic interventions. Here are practical tips to keep Laila looking its best:
- Vacuum regularly with a low suction setting. Use attachments that don’t pull at the yarn loops.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially under seating groups and dining tables where chairs tend to damage edges from constant movement.
- Blot spills immediately with a dry cloth. Do not rub, as friction can push liquids deeper into the loop and lead to staining.
- For stubborn stains, apply a mild detergent solution to a small, inconspicuous area first. Test any cleaner on a hidden corner to confirm colorfastness.
- Avoid machine washing or drying; natural fibers typically don’t respond well to agitation and heat.
- Protect from prolonged direct sun exposure to minimize fading; rotate or shade pieces when possible.

### Sizing, placement, and layering ideas
Choosing the right size for Laila depends on your room’s dimensions and how you plan to arrange furniture. Here are practical guidelines:
- Living room: For a typical seating arrangement, an 8x10 or 9x12 rug works well under a sectional or sofa group. Ensure the front legs of sofas and chairs sit on the rug to create a cohesive anchor for the space.
- Dining room: The rug should extend beyond the chairs when they are pulled out. As a rule of thumb, add at least 24 inches on each side to accommodate chairs without catching edges.
- Bedroom: A rug under the bed should extend beyond the sides to peek out on each side, creating a soft landing mat when you rise from bed in the morning.

Layering is a stylish option with Laila. Place a smaller, high-contrast rug under a wood coffee table to create a vignette with depth, or layer a second, lighter rug at the edge of the room to add a sense of float and dimension. The key is to avoid overwhelming the space with too many competing textures; let Laila’s looped texture hold the floor’s visual weight while other layers provide color and pattern contrast.
