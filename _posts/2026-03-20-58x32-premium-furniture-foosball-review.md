## Assembly and maintenance

### Assembly experience
Premium tables aren’t usually “unboxed and play tonight” furniture. They demand a bit of patience and a willingness to read the instructions twice. Here’s what you typically encounter:
- A sturdy, well-labeled box that doesn’t require you to bring in a forklift to unwrap.
- A multi-page manual that explains the order of assembly and recommended torque specs. We found that taking short breaks helps prevent cross-threading and error spirals.
- A set of tools included in the package. You’ll likely end up using a few of them, but a high-quality torque driver can speed things up and reduce the risk of tiny mistakes that become big problems later.

In practice, most people can assemble this table in 2-4 hours, depending on how many snacks you’re consuming and how many friends you drag into the process. The key tip: take your time with the rod alignment. A couple of degrees off can cause wobble and mis-feeds down the line, which is not fun when you’re trying to defend a tie in the final minute.

### Cleaning and upkeep
- Wipe the playing surface with a soft microfiber and a mild, non-abrasive cleaner to preserve the turf texture.
- Wipe the frame and legs with a damp cloth to remove fingerprints and dust—do not submerge.
- Lubricate the rod bearings sparingly if you notice resistance. Use only a lubricant designed for metallic components and avoid petroleum-based products that can degrade plastics over time.
- Check the bead counters for accuracy every few weeks and recalibrate if needed.

A premium table rewards a light touch and regular care. If you’re the type who forgets to dust for months on end, you’ll want to budget time for occasional maintenance and a friendly reminder to do the quick wipe-down after big games.
