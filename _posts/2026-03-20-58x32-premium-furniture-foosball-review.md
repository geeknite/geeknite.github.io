## Assembly and maintenance

### Assembly experience
Premium tables aren’t usually unboxed and ready to roll the same night. They demand patience and a willingness to follow the manual twice. Here’s what you typically encounter:
- A sturdy, well-labeled box that doesn’t require a forklift to unwrap.
- A multi-page manual with assembly order and recommended torque specs. Short breaks help prevent cross-threading and spiraling errors.
- A toolkit included in the package. You’ll likely use a few items, but a quality torque driver speeds things up and reduces the risk of tiny mistakes that become big problems later.

In practice, most people can assemble this table in 2-4 hours, depending on snack frequency and how many friends you bring into the process. The key tip: take your time with rod alignment. A couple of degrees off can cause wobble and mis-feeds down the line, which is not fun when you’re defending a tie in the final minute.

### Cleaning and upkeep
- Wipe the playing surface with a soft microfiber and a mild, non-abrasive cleaner to preserve the turf texture.
- Wipe the frame and legs with a damp cloth to remove fingerprints and dust—do not submerge.
- Lubricate the rod bearings sparingly if you notice resistance. Use a lubricant designed for metallic components and avoid petroleum-based products that can degrade plastics.
- Check the bead counters for accuracy every few weeks and recalibrate if needed.

A premium table rewards a light touch and regular care. If you’re the forgetful type, budget time for occasional maintenance and a friendly reminder to wipe down after big games.
