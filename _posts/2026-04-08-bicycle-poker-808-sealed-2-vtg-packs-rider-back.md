## Handling tips and care
To make sure your two decks stay in top form, here are a few practical care tips:

- Store them in their original packs until use. The VTG packaging gives you a head start on archiving condition.
- Keep them away from heat and moisture. Cards hate extreme humidity; it makes corners curl and faces smudge in odd ways when you finally shuffle.
- Use a clean, dry surface for shuffles. Dust and grit can act like tiny sandpaper, making the finish wear faster.
- Avoid long-term exposure to sunlight. Fading is a thing, and even classic Rider Back tones aren’t immune.
- If you’re a cardist, practice on a clean, consistent surface. The two decks can be used interchangeably for color-coded routines or for contrasting demonstrations when you show an audience how a move works with the blue deck versus the red deck.

Maintenance isn’t glamorous, but it pays off in longer deck life and more consistent performance on the night you need it most.

## Where to buy and what to watch out for
As with any popular playing card release, there are fakes and rebrands. Here are some practical buying tips to help you navigate:

- Buy from reputable retailers or the official brand page if available. The two-pack should be clearly labeled as VTG in the listing. If the price seems too good to be true for a two-pack of sealed Rider Back decks, it probably is.
- Check the packaging integrity. The wrapper around each deck should be completely sealed and undamaged. Small tears aren’t a deal-breaker for a quick resell, but they’re a warning sign if you’re chasing mint condition.
- Inspect the back design for consistency. The blue and red Rider Back should feature identical back patterns aside from color. Mismatched patterns or color anomalies can be a signal of counterfeit products.
- Read the seller notes about VTG grading carefully. If they mention wear, open-box, or questionable seals, proceed with caution.
