## Build quality and materials
The first thing to assess is the bend and bend-ability of the product. No, not your ability to fold like a pro, but the physical build of the table top and the chip tray. The foldable table surface should feel sturdy yet light enough to move without needing a forklift operator. The edge rails are where the table top earns its keep. They should prevent chips from sliding off during a wild all in moment, which happens more often than you would think when your friend lovingly bluffs with a busted flush on the river. A good rail will have a slight groove or lip to catch chips as they fly away from an enthusiastic bet. In the best builds, the rail is robust enough that you can lean on the table without fear of cracking the surface in a dramatic manner that would deserve its own stand up routine.

A travel set aims to hide a little luxury under the hood, and by luxury we mean a smooth action when you pull out chips, a stable rest for the stack, and not a bunch of chips rattling around like a tiny percussion ensemble. Expect chips that feel substantial in the hand, not flimsy tokens that bend or crack under minor pressure. The denominations printed on the chips should be legible and, ideally, color coded to prevent the kind of confusion that leads to a meltdown at the table like a miniature fireworks show lining up to ruin your corner bets.

## Maintenance and care
Like any surface that gets smeared with chips, food, and the occasional drink, the upkeep matters. Here are a few quick tips:

- Wipe down the table surface after play with a microfiber cloth. A gentle wipe helps maintain the felt texture and prevents staining from snacks like red wine or grape soda.
- Keep the chips in a dry place. Wet chips are obnoxious and can ruin the surface of the table top if left to rest on it for long periods.
- Store the table top in a cool, dry place. If you fold it away, make sure the edges are aligned to prevent any bending that could affect future play.
- Periodically check the chip stack for warping or edge wear. If you notice chips that have become misshapen, rotate them to ensure even wear across the set.
