## Two-person ergonomics and space management
One of the central questions with a two-person desk is ergonomics. Two decidedly different postures in a trench-like shared space can lead to competition for space, elbows, and monitor real estate. The beauty of a 94.5-inch desk is that there’s enough room for two full-size workstations, each with its own monitor setup, keyboard, and a personal beverage within reach. The potential snag: you need to settle on where you want to anchor monitors and whether you’ll use a shared monitor stand or independent arms. We recommend starting with a layout plan: place your primary monitors closer to your own seating line, leave a comfortable separation in the middle, and consider a shared cable sleeve or channel that can hide a dozen cables without turning into a tangle monster.

Ergonomics wise, you’ll still want to account for chair height and desk height. A typical desk height hovers around 29 to 30 inches off the floor. If you’re a taller or shorter person, a small adjustable chair or footrest can make a surprising difference. The two-person configuration means you and your partner in crime (aka coworker) can adjust seating position to avoid shoulder collisions during long brainstorms. Yes, it’s adorable when two adults simultaneously lean in to show their screens—just be mindful of the keyboard’s central drift when you two decide to have a “friendly” typing competition.
