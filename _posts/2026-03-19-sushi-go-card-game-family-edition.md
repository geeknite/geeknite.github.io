## How to Play: A Quick Refresher

### The Core Loop

- Each round, every player is dealt a hand of cards. You pick one card to keep, place it in front of you, then pass the remaining cards to the next player. This continues until all cards from the deck are whisked away and drafted by everyone.
- After all cards are drafted, scoring happens. In Family Edition mode the scoring is simplified, but you still feel the tug of competition as points accumulate across rounds.
- The next round begins with a fresh hand and the passing pattern continues, weaving a rhythm that the table can ride with little talk and lots of laughs.

### Card Types and What They Do

- Nigiri: simple point power with a neat twist when combined with Wasabi. They are small, but they pack a zing in points when used strategically.
- Maki rolls: end-of-round rewards for the most rolls. The chase for the top spot adds a light competitive edge without devolving into war.
- Sashimi: aim for sets, but remember, missing a piece means you miss the big payoff. The tension comes from juggling risk and reward with a friendly grin.
- Wasabi: a little multiplier magic that can elevate a decent nigiri into a heroic scorer if timed correctly.
- Pudding: end-game bonus scoring that sneaks into the final tallies and can flip outcomes in amusing ways.

## Strategy Tips for Family Night

- Focus on early board development: in early rounds, identify a few categories you care about and draft toward them, but remain flexible. The bulk of the point swing will come from the end game bonuses and the ability to complete sets efficiently.
- Track the opponent's likely targets: if you see a neighbor chasing a particular set, you can pivot to nibble at a different target or secure a safe card that prevents them from completing a highly scored set.
- Use Wasabi strategically: a well-timed Wasabi boost can turn a modest nigiri into a winner when you marry it with the right ending cards. Don t overcommit to Wasabi early; you want it ready for the moment you genuinely can swing the score.
- Balance risk and reward: the end game pudding points can swing outcomes. If you are far behind, you might gamble on pudding to close the gap; if you are ahead, safely padding with low risk cards can lock in your lead.
