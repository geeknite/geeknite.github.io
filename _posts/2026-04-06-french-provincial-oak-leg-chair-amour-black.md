## Durability, Maintenance, and Longevity

Here’s the shop-floor truth: you’re buying a piece built to outlast trend cycles. Oak is notoriously forgiving of abuse; the Amour Black is forgiving in a stylish, not-quite-zen sense. With normal use, the finish will show light wear in the places where hands and seats meet most often. That’s the chair aging gracefully, not failing spectacularly. A gentle cleaning regimen—damp cloth, mild soap, no harsh polishes—will keep the finish from looking dull over time. If you have kids or pets and an appetite for chaos, you’ll want to remind yourself that this chair forgives, but it doesn’t forget.

### Care Tips (Without Turning You into a Housekeeper Extraordinaire)
- Dust weekly with a soft cloth; avoid abrasive products. 
- For smudges, a mild soap solution works wonders; wipe and dry. 
- Keep temperature and humidity steady to prevent wood movement that may cause minor cracks over years. 
- If you want to re-luster the finish, use a recommended wood conditioner sparingly. Too much oil and the seat will sparkle like a disco ball; too little and it will feel parched like a desert chair.
