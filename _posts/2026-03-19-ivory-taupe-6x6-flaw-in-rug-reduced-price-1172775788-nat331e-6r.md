## Materials, Construction, and Care 101

### Build quality and fiber details

Rugs in this price range are typically machine-made with synthetic fibers or a blend that aims for balance between softness and durability. Expect a dense, plush pile with a comfortable surface that can hold up to everyday living, but don’t expect the same ruggedness as a high-end hand-knotted piece. The backboard is usually a woven fabric or transposed backing that helps prevent slipping and keeps the rug flat on wood or tile floors.

### Cleaning and maintenance tips

- Regular vacuuming: Use a low-powered vacuum with a brushless attachment to avoid pulling out fibers. Vacuum in the direction of the pile to avoid a scruffy surface appearance.
- Spot cleaning: For spills, blot—don’t rub—with a clean cloth. Mild detergent solutions or rug-safe cleaners can help, but test a small area first to avoid color bleed.
- Deep cleaning: If the rug becomes heavily soiled, consider professional cleaning or a rental upholstery cleaner. Always check the rug’s care label for guidance.
- Flaw management: If the listed flaw is visual rather than structural, you may opt for strategic placement where it’s less conspicuous. Alternatively, use the flaw as a design feature by incorporating an accent pillow or art piece that echoes the imperfect detail.

## Careful Shopping: What to Verify Before You Buy

- Request close-up photos of the flaw from multiple angles. A well-shot photo can reveal whether the issue is a dye variance, a pattern misalignment, or a random irregularity that is inherent to the weave.
- Ask about return policies. If the retailer offers free returns on opened boxes or a limited warranty on workmanship, that reduces risk.
- Confirm the rug’s material and backing. If it’s a soft pile with a non-slip backing, it’s friendlier for households with kids or pets.
- Inquire about shedding. Some plush synthetic piles shed for a few weeks after unrolling; plan for vacuuming frequency accordingly.
