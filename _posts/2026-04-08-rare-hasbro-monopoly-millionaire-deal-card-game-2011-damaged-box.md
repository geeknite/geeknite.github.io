## Care, Storage, and Longevity

Storage is a surprisingly neglected topic when it comes to sealed decks. If you are assembling a little display shelf of vintage Hasbro stuff, a few simple practices can extend both the deck and the box’s life:

- Keep the deck away from direct sunlight. The sun is the silent villain that can fade the color index on property cards over years.
- Maintain a stable humidity level. Cards like a little humidity, but too much is a menace. Ideal humidity is around 40–60 percent; avoid damp basements and sun-drenched attics.
- Use proper storage sleeves if you ever decide to unseal for playtesting or for a photo shoot. Sleeves keep the surface scratch-free and preserve the vibe of the 2011 print.
- If box condition is a factor for your display, consider a display case or a dust cover to reduce light and dust exposure while still showcasing the artwork.

A practical tip: keep the box corners pristine by avoiding stacking heavy items on top. A light cardboard box that has endured a few transport miles will thank you for a gentle rest on a shelf with similar-sized collectibles.
