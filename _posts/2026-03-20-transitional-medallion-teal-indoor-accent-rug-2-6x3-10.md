## Materials and Construction

### Pile height and texture
The rug’s pile is intentionally low, which keeps it practical for daily life. It’s soft enough to be comfortable when you stand or walk on it, but not so plush that it feels like a cloud you’re sinking into each morning. A low pile is also easier to maintain because dirt doesn’t become embedded deeply, and vacuuming is quick and effective. ## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

### Cleaning routines
- Vacuum regularly (low setting) to keep dust and grit at bay.  
- For spills, blot rather than rub; this preserves the pile and helps prevent spread.  
- Use a mild detergent with water for spot-cleaning; always test in a non-visible corner first.  
- For tougher stains, consider a carpet-cleaning solution designed for synthetic fibers and follow the product’s instructions.

### Sunlight and color retention
Direct sun exposure can fade colors over time. If your rug sits in a sun-drenched area, rotate it every few months or use window treatments to limit UV exposure. A little sun control goes a long way toward preserving that teal vibrancy you paid for.

### Longevity and wear patterns
With normal use in living spaces, expect the rug to maintain its look for several seasons. The low pile helps reduce trap-prone areas where dirt collects, and the pattern’s scale remains legible even after foot traffic. If you notice edges curling or fibers wearing in high-traffic spots, you can rotate the rug or reposition nearby furniture to redistribute wear.

## Color Theory and Seasonal Use

### Why teal works in multiple seasons
Teal evokes sea air in spring and a richer tone in fall/winter lighting, making it a flexible color that can carry through seasonal decor changes. It pairs well with neutrals—creams, beiges, grays—and with wood tones, copper accents, and matte black hardware. To avoid visual competition, use solid colors for larger furnishings and reserve bold patterns—like a block teal rug—for smaller textiles such as throw pillows or a table runner. This approach helps maintain visual balance while letting the teal rug hold its ground as the room’s anchor.
