If you’re curious about how Wizards of the Coast categorizes instants, you can skim through their official magic site for the canonical definitions, but we’ll summarize: instants are spells you can cast at almost any time, including during your opponent’s turn. ## Quality, Condition, and Condition-Check Checklist

Condition matters, but not always as you’d expect. The lot might present a mix of near-mint corners and sports-cup wear that tells a story about how the cards have lived inside their binder until now. Here’s a pragmatic approach to quality control:

- Check for visible flaws: severe edge wear, creases, and water damage. If a card has any of these, it’s less about playability and more about displaying your own personal tragedy as a cautionary tale about humidity and card sleeves.
- Spin the sleeves test: if an instant comes in an older frame, you can still sleeve it up for casual play. If it’s a newer frame with a lot of gloss, treat it like a fragile prodigy that should be kept away from the dog and the dishwasher.
- Foil and non-foil mix: foils are shiny but can be misinterpreted as more valuable than they are in the long run. Don’t overvalue a foil just because it shines. The real value lives in the playability and the nostalgia attached to the card.

If you’re unsure about condition or price, consider reaching out to a local game store or an online community with a few sample photos. A quick poll can yield accurate crowd-sourced valuations and save you from overpaying for a mystery pocket of card trouble. ## Value Assessment: Pricing the 39-Card Mystery Box

Pricing a bulk lot is not science—it’s more like baking a cake with a recipe you found on a fortune cookie. Here are practical tips to price your instant lot without losing your mind:

- Start with the obvious: sum of common and uncommon instants that see regular play in your local meta. Those have the most reliable value, and you’ll find a way to build around them. 
- Consider playability vs. collectibility. If the lot contains instants that are highly used in competitive decks (counterspells, removal spells, or direct damage spells that finish games quickly), you’re likely to extract more value by building a playable budget deck around them.
- Don’t discount the flavor cards. Some players love quirky instants for casual and themed decks. They may not fetch top dollar, but they can be the glue that holds a themed build together and that counts for something.
- Factor in potential future reprints. Some instants might become more valuable if they’re from sets that won’t see reprints soon. Yet we’ll caution you not to overinvest in speculation—you’re buying a lot for fun, not a factory of futures.

Once you’ve cataloged your 39 cards, you’ll want to create order out of the chaos. Here are practical sorting strategies that have served Geeknite’s editors well in the past:

## Potential Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

No haul is perfect, and the 39-instant lot is no exception. Here are common pitfalls and how to sidestep them without losing your sense of humor:

- Overcounting value from random rares. A single rare can skew a lot’s perceived worth, but don’t rely on it to justify the entire purchase. Count it, but keep it grounded in the reality that most players won’t want a random set of mismatched rares.
- Assuming all instants are created equal. Some instantly outclass others in terms of synergy with your deck. Prioritize those that fit your current build plans rather than chasing every flashy meme card you glimpse in a sale post.
- Ignoring compatibility with your playing style. If you rarely cast instants in your group, a lot heavy on instants might not be ideal for you. It’s okay to pass and wait for a more tailored purchase.
- Underestimating the joy of the random. The fun of a mixed lot is discovering weird edge-case cards that become beloved in a casual format. Don’t undervalue the entertainment factor; sometimes the surprise is the real value.
