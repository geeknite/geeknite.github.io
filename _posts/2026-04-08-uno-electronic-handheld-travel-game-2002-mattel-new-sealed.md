## How the game works and what makes it tick

UNO is a deceptively simple card game: match colors or numbers, play wilds when you can, and do your best to offload your hand before everyone else. The handheld version compresses that energy into a digital loop. In typical fashion, you’ll have a screen that shows a few cards or LED indicators, and you’ll press color-coded buttons to indicate your actions. Draw cards, skip turns, perhaps a wild card that shifts the color of the game—these digital representations attempt to translate the tactile thrill of shuffling and slamming UNO into a quick, mechanical ritual.

What makes this device charming is how it stylizes the chaos. Instead of physically passing cards, you perform input sequences to simulate draws and plays.
