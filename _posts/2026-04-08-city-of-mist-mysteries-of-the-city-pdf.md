## What's inside the PDF
Mysteries of the City is built around a set of five or so connected threads that function as mini-adventures or investigation arcs. The exact count can vary depending on how you slice a session, but the structure is consistent: a moment of curiosity, a set of clues that force a couple of the city’s myths to collide, and a conclusive reveal that reframes everything you’ve seen so far. The intent is not to deliver a prewritten funnel but to hand you a toolkit—handouts, scene prompts, and a handful of GM tips—that you can remix into your own city.

- Five mystery threads, each with a core premise, a set of known PCs, and a few optional variants for different playstyles.
- GM tools designed to support investigative play: scene templates, clues, and a couple of optional clocks to keep track of time and consequences.
- Handouts and props that help players connect the dots without needing to memorize a dozen minor NPCs.
- Optional complications that nudge toward themes of myth and mortal limits, which is exactly the flavor City of Mist does best.

The PDF is written with the GM in mind, but it’s friendly toward players who enjoy piecing together a mystery. It doesn’t bury you in stat blocks or mechanical fluff; rather, it presents a readable flow and clear signposts that let you improvise when a clue heads in an unexpected direction. If you’re a GM who values atmosphere over crunch, you’ll feel right at home.

The design is clean, the fonts crisp, and the margins generous enough to leave room for your own marginalia. The PDF uses the same aesthetic as the core books—neon noir, inked linework, and a color palette that won’t cause your printer to faint—but the layout is friendlier to a one-shot or a weekend-long arc. It’s easy to skim for the sections you care about and to flip back to a clue you might have missed.

In terms of accessibility, the PDF is text-searchable and bookmarks are present for quick navigation. If you’re printing, there are printer-friendly pages with fewer images and more room for notes. If you prefer digital play, the PDF scales nicely on a tablet and supports annotation with your favorite note-taking app.

## Mechanics and system fit

### System-first play

City of Mist’s signature mechanic, the tag-based storytelling system, remains a good match for mysteries and urban fantasy. Mysteries of the City leans into narrative triggers: when you describe a scene, you lean on a tag (or a few) to shape how a clue lands with players. This keeps the pace brisk and the tone consistent with the game’s mythic realism vibe. The PDF does a good job of keeping the rules light enough to let the story breathe, while still providing enough structure to avoid a freeform maze that dissolves into confusion.

### Clocks, tags and themes

If you’re familiar with the core book, you’ll recognize the familiar clock mechanic adapted to the mystery format. Clocks surface as the investigation progresses: each completed arc tick brings you closer to a reveal, but also raises the risk that a suspect’s cover will slip. The document emphasizes explicit theme tags for each thread, which helps the GM maintain a cohesive tone—mythic undercurrents driving citywide stakes, with street-level consequences.

### Investigation and action

Combat is deliberately toned down in favor of investigation and social maneuvering. The PDF encourages players to use talk, deductions, and staged reveals to push the narrative forward. If you want a punch-up session, you can slip in a mid-scene action beat, but the content remains oriented toward mystery solving rather than big-scale firefights. For many campaigns, that’s a feature, not a bug. If your group loves the theatrical reveal as much as the chase through back alleys, Mysteries of the City will scratch that itch.

As for the dice and resolution, it respects the core system’s vibe: you often rely on tags and the right approach rather than raw numbers. The book doesn’t drown you in stat blocks; it gives you a handful of mechanical prompts and a couple of optional rules to bring the fantasy of the city into sharper relief.

## Story potential and themes

The book thrives on the interplay between myth and mundane life. The city you play in isn’t just a backdrop; it’s a character with moods, grudges, and a taste for dramatic irony. Mysteries of the City leans into stories where a mythic force hides behind a streetlamp or a convenience store’s neon sign, waiting for someone to notice. This is appropriate for players who enjoy the slow burn of investigative games and the satisfaction of connecting a breadcrumb trail to a larger, thematic conspiracy.

If you’re the kind of GM who loves foreshadowing, you’ll enjoy planning threads that intersect in unexpected ways. A single clue might be the keystone that reveals three different myths contorting behind the city’s facade. The PDF provides guidance for weaving these connections without overloading players with exposition or forcing them to remember every minor NPC from a dozen sessions ago.

For players who enjoy a mix of roleplay, deduction, and a few well-timed scenes of wonder, Mysteries of the City offers multiple entry points. Those who prefer a more old-school mystery approach—a locked-room vibe, a single per-session puzzle, and a big reveal at the end—will find plenty of material here to adapt to their table.

## Visuals, layout and accessibility

The design language remains consistent with City of Mist: moody photography, illustrated frames, and typographic choices that emphasize readability. The PDF uses a clean grid that makes it easy to separate clues from flavor text, and the headers do a good job of guiding you through the investigation without forcing you to slog through blocks of paragraph text.

From a user experience perspective, the navigation is strong. Bookmarks in the PDF let you jump to threads without scrolling through a long page of content. The printable handouts are legible, with enough contrast to be used as physical props during a session. If you’re running a live game, you’ll appreciate how the material invites players to lean in and contribute their own theories.

If you’re printing, I recommend a standard ink profile and a heavier weight paper for the cover and handouts. The color accents look great on screen, but a too-bright printer can make the neon palette glow a little too aggressively. It’s a minor quirk, but one worth noting if you like to hand players a colored fragment of a clue that really sells the mood.

## GMing tips and session flow

One of the strongest virtues of Mysteries of the City is its practical guidance for GMs. There’s a calm, friendly tone that assumes you’re playing with people who want to tell a good story and have fun with drama rather than wrestle with rules. The book emphasizes the moment-to-moment flow of a mystery: a session begins with a spark, then a string of clues, then a turning point, and finally a reveal that reframes the city.

A couple of GM tips stood out:

- Start with a strong hook that ties the mystery to one of the city’s mythic figures. The hook should be quick to summarize and rich enough to spark curiosity.
- Use the clocks to pace revelations. If you can press the right triggers at the right time, you’ll create a sense of momentum without forcing a grind of checks.
- Rally your players by connecting clues to their characters’ themes. The best mysteries feel personal rather than generic, and that’s something this PDF nudges you toward.
- Keep a few “red herrings” in your pocket. A well-chosen misdirection can heighten the big reveal without derailing the investigation.

## How it stacks up against the core books

Mysteries of the City doesn’t reinvent the wheel; instead, it refines a known wheel. The core books provide a broad toolkit for urban myth storytelling; this PDF specializes that toolkit for a curated set of mysteries with a built-in structure for investigating and resolving them. If you’ve played through a few campaigns, you’ll recognize the pattern: a mystery that requires players to locate a hidden thread, a set of symbols or clues that point toward a larger force, and a final beat that closes the loop while leaving the city’s mythic undercurrent alive and buzzing.

In terms of accessibility, the PDF remains easy to read and navigate, matching the standard you expect from City of Mist supplements. If you’re a completionist who likes every possible variation, you’ll want to pair this with other supplements, as the individual threads are designed to hand you enough material for several sessions without needing to craft every beat from scratch.

If you’re evaluating price, the supplement sits at a reasonable level for a PDF addon. You’re paying for focused content, not a full campaign arc that you’ll never get through. If you’re a new player, the content can serve as a gentle introduction to the system’s tone and mechanics.

## Final verdict

Mysteries of the City is a well-constructed, mood-forward expansion that captures the neon noir energy City of Mist excels at. It’s friendly to newcomers while still offering enough challenge and intrigue for veteran GMs who want to run a tight, mystery-driven arc. The structure is reliable, the handouts are typically well-designed, and the prompts for clues and scenes give you a lot of fuel for session planning. It’s not a sweeping epic that redefines the system; instead, it’s a well-made toolkit that helps you stage a handful of memorable investigations in a city that breathes myth and misdirection.

If you’re in the mood for a compact, well-tempered set of mysteries that you can slot into an ongoing City of Mist campaign or run as a one-shot while your regular group explores a fresh angle on mythic detectives, Mysteries of the City is worth picking up. It’s the sort of supplement that pays for itself in the first couple of sessions by saving you time on prep and giving players a sense of discovery that doesn’t feel forced.

One of the real joys of this PDF is how it treats the city as a character that talks back to you. You don’t just present clues; you present a city that recognises the myths behind the clues and nudges players toward ethical decisions with consequences that echo beyond the table. If that’s your jam, this is a solid addition to your collection.

In summary: strong mood, reliable structure, generous GM guidance, and enough flexibility to adapt to your group’s pace and preferred style of mystery solving. It’s not the most ambitious City of Mist release, but it’s one of the most dependable, and that makes it a practical pick for a long weekend of sleuthing with mythic stakes.

For players who enjoy exploration as well as deduction, and for GMs who want a few ready-to-run threads that still leave plenty of room for improvisation, Mysteries of the City delivers. If you’re the type who wants a clean, stylish supplement that respects the core system while offering a handful of ready-to-run mysteries, you’ve found a good match.
