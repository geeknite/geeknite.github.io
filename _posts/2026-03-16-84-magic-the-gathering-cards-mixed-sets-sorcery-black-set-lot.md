## A quick practical approach to building around a black sorcery lot
If you want to create a playable black deck from a bulk sorcery lot, here is a simple blueprint that works for many table dynamics:

1) Pick 2 to 4 cornerstone cards you want to lean into, such as a strong reanimation engine or a reliable control spell.
2) Add 4 to 6 removal spells to secure the early game and protect your threats.
3) Include 6 to 8 discard or hand disruption spells to gain tempo and disrupt opponents.
4) Add 2 to 3 graveyard recurrence spells that can bring back key threats with minimal cost.
5) Sprinkle in 6 to 8 lower cost threats and a couple of long game finishers or big threats that scale with the battlefield.
6) Finish with 2 to 4 draw or advantage spells that let you keep momentum and keep your hand full while staying within a budget mindset.

This modular approach keeps your deck lean, avoids dead draws, and makes the 84 card lot feel like a treasure chest instead of a big box of possible regrets.
