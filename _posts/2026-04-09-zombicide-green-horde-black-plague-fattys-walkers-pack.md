Overview
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Fattys & Walkers Pack is, in spirit, a love letter to the moments that make Zombicide memorable: the fat guy charging you with a blustery roar, the endless line of little guys shambling toward your barricade, and the delightful chaos of trying to manage multiple threats at once. These miniatures are not merely new models; they are stress-inducing, fun-making, dramatic plot devices you drop into the game when your cohort needs a break from the “spray-and-pray” of standard walkers.

What’s in the Fattys & Walkers Pack
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Fatty models: A set of hulking, boss-like zombies designed to feel like they earned their own mythos in the apocalypse. These guys aren’t just “big walkers with more health”—they have distinct mechanics that force you to rethink your approach to each encounter.
- Walker variants: Fresh sculpts or variants that alter how you approach each room, corridor, or alley. They look enough different that a table that’s seen a dozen zombicide games suddenly looks new again.
- New encounter and objective cards: A handful of mission tweaks to incorporate the Fattys and walkers in fresh, narrative ways.
- Tokens and boards: Additional punch-outs to track Fatty effects, sprint tokens, and maybe a few cursed markers to remind you that this game loves to prank you.
- Aesthetic upgrade: These miniatures aren’t just bigger; they’re characterful. Fattys have real character (even if their character is “I will ruin your plan with a roar”). The walkers are more varied than your average cannon fodder, and that variety matters when you’re trying to stage a tense clutch moment.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The pieces are clean, well-molded, and snap into their bases with minimal fuss. Gameplay and rules: how it shakes up co-op
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Fatty mechanics: These aren’t your standard undead pushovers. The Fatty’s presence forces the team to re-evaluate their approach to a given map. You’ll find that Fattys require specific tactics—from coordinated distraction to strategic targeting to ensure someone can deliver the killing blows before their damage turns the tide against you. Expect moments where one Fatty’s charge changes a round entirely, turning a “peaceful stroll to the objective” into a tense sprint-and-survive sequence.

Walker variants: The Walkers add flavor without destroying the core balance. They behave similarly to standard Walkers but offer different movement patterns or attack triggers. The aim is to keep the tension high without creating analysis paralysis—your goal is still to work as a team, not to solve a cryptogram every turn.

How it plays with Green Horde and Black Plague
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Green Horde: The green-skinned chaos of Orcs vs. undead is an interesting mix. The Fattys and Walkers from this pack adapt to the Horde’s flavor without clashing with its core rules. You’ll find the Fatty encounters feel suitably brutal on the greenskins’ turf; the added challenge keeps the pace brisk and punishes any laziness. If you like the “horde vs. horde” aesthetic, this pack scratches that itch with gusto.

Black Plague: The necromantic aura of Black Plague gets a twist with Fattys. In this setting, Fatty interactions can feel even more dramatic, as you juggle both the fear of bigger threats and the inevitability of a walker flood. The new cards lean into the dark humor of the base game while injecting dramatic climaxes in late-game scenarios.

The Fattys & Walkers Pack doesn’t just add miniatures; it adds narrative texture. Hero setups, balancing, and party dynamics

One of the joys of this pack is how it nudges the party toward smarter rotations. With a Fatty on the board, you can’t just run forward and bludgeon with your strongest survivor. You need to coordinate archers, melees, and specialists to create safe chords of actions that don’t end with a chorus of groans and “one more round, we got this.” The walkers add pressure to maintain line-of-sight control and target prioritization. If your group thrives on synergy and banter, Fattys & Walkers will feel like a second wind, turning your board into a stage for dramatic, heroic fails and occasional triumphant saves.
