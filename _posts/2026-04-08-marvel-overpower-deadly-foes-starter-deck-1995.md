## Strategy: How to Make the Most of the Deadly Foes Starter Deck
If you’re coming to Marvel OverPower with a single aim, it’s to enjoy the ride and maybe win a few games without losing your sense of humor. The Deadly Foes deck provides you with a toolkit, not a miracle, and forcing yourself into a few core strategies will make the deck sing.

First, balance is your friend. The beginner mistake is to jam as many high-damage options as possible into the deck. Instead, aim for a balanced approach: a few heavy hitters supported by midrange characters who buff or protect them. The slow burn often beats a flashy but inconsistent ramp.

Second, know your villain. Deadly Foes decks are built around a rogues gallery; learn the typical villain's patterns and how the hero lines interact with them. This doesn’t just improve your odds; it makes the game feel like you’re following a serialized comic arc rather than simply flipping cards.

Third, timing matters. In OverPower, timing a big attack or a critical power-up at the right moment can change the outcome of a turn. Use plot or support cards to create windows of opportunity. Do not squander these windows on a petty card draw; the objective is to convert momentum into board presence.

Fourth, manage resources. Cards will demand energy or resource tokens in some form. If you exhaust your pool too early, you’ll be staring at the board like a poker player who forgot his own name. The Deadly Foes deck rewards patient play, so don’t panic when the board looks gray. It will look even grayer after your opponent’s plan lands a heavy hit.

Finally, practice with friends. There is no substitute for repetition. The more you play, the more you’ll notice the subtle interactions that aren’t obvious on first read. You’ll hear the whir of plastic sleeves, the rustle of cardboard, and the dawning realization that you’re having fun in a way that only retro games can deliver.
