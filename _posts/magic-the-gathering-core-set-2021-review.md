## The Basics

### What is Core Set 2021?

Magic: The Gathering - **Core Set 2021** is the latest in the line of core sets designed to be a welcoming entry point for new players and a nostalgic trip for veterans. It features a mix of reprinted cards and brand new additions, making it an excellent choice for building or expanding your collection.

### Components

This set includes:
- **Booster Packs**: Each pack contains 15 cards, including at least one rare or mythic rare.
- **Starter Decks**: Pre-constructed decks designed to get you playing right away.
- **Collector Boosters**: For those who like their cards shiny and their wallets empty.

**Pro Tip:** Don’t eat the cards. They may look delicious, but trust me, your digestive system won’t appreciate it.

## The Gameplay

### Mechanics

Core Set 2021 reintroduces several beloved mechanics, including:
- **Scry**: Look at the top cards of your library and decide their fate.
- **Flying**: Because who doesn't want their creatures to soar majestically, like that one pigeon that stole your sandwich last summer?
- **Trample**: When you absolutely, positively want to make sure your opponent feels the pain.

### New Cards

Among the new additions, we’ve got some real winners:
- **Teferi, Master of Time**: This card lets you activate loyalty abilities on any player's turn. Remember that one annoying friend who always had tricks up their sleeve? Yeah, now you can be them.
- **Grim Tutor**: Search your library for any card. Yes, ANY CARD. Just try not to look too smug.

**Funny Moment:** During one game, I used Grim Tutor to find a land card because I was mana-screwed. The look on my opponent’s face was priceless.

## The Visuals

### Artwork

Magic: The Gathering is known for its stunning artwork, and Core Set 2021 is no exception. From the hauntingly beautiful Swamps to the majestic and slightly confused-looking Dragons, the cards are a visual treat.

### Card Quality

The card stock feels sturdy, though I'd advise against testing their waterproof capabilities in your bathtub (trust me on this one). The foils are particularly mesmerizing, and if you’re anything like me, you’ll spend a good chunk of time just staring at them under different lighting conditions.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Welcoming for New Players**: The set is designed to be accessible to newcomers.
- **Nostalgia Factor**: Veterans will appreciate the reprints of classic cards.
- **Beautiful Artwork**: Each card is a piece of art.
- **Balanced Gameplay**: The set manages to be fun for both casual and competitive play.

### Cons

- **Expensive**: Magic has never been a cheap hobby, and Core Set 2021 is no exception.
- **Reprints**: Some might feel the set leans too heavily on reprints.
- **Pack Luck**: The randomness of booster packs can lead to frustration (and maybe a few tears).

## Final Thoughts

**Magic: The Gathering - Core Set 2021** is a wonderful addition to the Magic universe. Whether you’re a seasoned planeswalker or a newbie just stepping into the arena, this set has something for everyone. From the engaging mechanics to the breathtaking artwork, it’s a journey worth taking.
