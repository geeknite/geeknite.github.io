## Care and Maintenance

Natural fiber rugs demand a touch more TLC than synthetic piles, but not a full spa day. The care card for Mineral 222 advocates regular vacuuming, prompt blotting of spills, and rotating the rug periodically to promote even wear. The key is to avoid excessive moisture and direct heat, which can distort fibers or shift color. For stubborn stains, a mild cleaner recommended by the manufacturer typically suffices, though you can also consult a professional rug cleaner if you want to minimize any risk of color change.

Breathability is a win for natural fibers in terms of odor management after long gaming sessions or late-night snacks. On the flip side, moisture can linger longer than it would in synthetic piles. Quick tip: use a dehumidifier in damp rooms and consider rotating the rug more often in spaces with high humidity. If you have kids who spill blue juice on the rug, address it promptly to avoid a science-fair-grade stain that refuses to vanish with time.

## Care, Cleaning, and Longevity: A Practical Lab Report

Natural fiber rugs require a little more attention than synthetic piles, but they reward you with character and longevity when treated well. Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to avoid dragging fibers. Address spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing, working from the outside in to avoid ring marks. For stubborn stains, follow the manufacturer’s gentle cleaning protocol, and if stress levels rise, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in natural fibers.

Rotation is your friend. Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and to prevent obvious footprints that could make guests think you are inventing a new carpet-based time-lactation diagram. If you live in a humid climate, consider a dehumidifier to keep moisture at bay and to prevent the fibers from swelling in ways that may alter texture and color.

### Stain Severity Scenarios (Fun but Helpful)
- Blue juice spill on a sunny afternoon: treat quickly with a blot method and a mild cleaner to avoid a stubborn stain that stubbornly refuses to go away.
- Coffee drip near the edge: blot toward the center and repeat as needed; avoid heat sources that could set color.
- Red wine on a party night: a professional cleaner is your amigo, especially if your guests insist on extra fancy drinks.
