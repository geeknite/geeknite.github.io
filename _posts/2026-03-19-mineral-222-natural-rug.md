## Care and Maintenance

Natural fiber rugs require a bit more attention than synthetic pile rugs, but not that much more. The care card included with Mineral 222 suggests regular vacuuming, quick blotting of spills, and rotating the rug every so often to promote even wear. The key is to avoid excessive moisture and direct heat, which can cause fiber distortion or color shifting. If you do spill something serious, treat it with a mild cleaner and blot from the outside toward the center to prevent ring formation. For stubborn stains, a gentle cleaning solution recommended by the manufacturer is often all you need, but you can also consult a professional rug cleaner if you want to avoid any risk of color change.

One of the benefits of natural fibers is their breathability. This means your rug is less likely to trap odors from long gaming sessions or late-night snacks. However, the flip side is that moisture can linger there longer than on synthetic piles. A quick tip: use a dehumidifier in damp rooms, and consider rotating the rug more often in spaces with high humidity. If you have kids who spill blue juice on the rug, you will want to address it promptly, because blue juice has a tendency to leave a stubborn, science-fair-sticky stain if you wait too long.
