## Assembly, care, and weather readiness: do you need a PhD in DIY?

### Assembly tips
- Check all parts against the hardware kit before you start. It’s the classic “I think we’re missing a bolt, but it’s too late to check” scenario.
- Lay out sections in the intended configuration before tightening anything permanently. It saves you from realizing you’ve put a left-hand chaise where a right-hand one should be.
- Use a torque guide to avoid over-tightening; you don’t want to pinch the wicker strands or strip the mounting holes.

### Weather readiness
- Resin wicker and aluminum/steel frames handle humidity and rain well, but prolonged exposure to sun can fade cushions. A simple canopy or store cushions indoors when heavy storms are coming can extend the life of the set.
- For mildew avoidance, wipe down with a mild soap solution and rinse; ensure cushions are completely dry before storing to avoid mold growth.
- If you live in a region with salt air (coastal folks), regular rinsing with fresh water helps prevent corrosion on the metal frame.

### Cleaning and maintenance
- Vacuum the cushions and wipe the frame with a soft cloth. For stains on cushions, use the gentle cycle recommended by the fabric brand or a mild upholstery cleaner.
- Use a fabric protector spray if approved by the cushion brand for added stain resistance.
- Cover the set during not-use periods to reduce dust and UV exposure.
