## Cable management and airflow considerations

Airflow is king when you run multiple devices in a rack. Open frame racks, by design, allow free air movement around each device. This is a big advantage for maintainers who want to avoid thermal throttling and want to ensure fans do not fight against choked airflow. The StarTech open frame supports straightforward cable routing. The wide spaces behind the front posts let you thread power cords and network cables with relative ease. Some users add cable management arms and tie down bars to keep cables organized as you repopulate the rack. If you plan a future expansion or a more complex mix of devices, consider adding a few hook-and-loop straps and a couple of cable organizers to maintain order. It is a small price to pay for a lab that is easy to maintain and easy to clean.

One practical tip is to separate power and data cabling as much as possible. This reduces electromagnetic interference and makes it easier to identify where a problem started in case you notice a degraded network signal or an odd boot sequence. The open frame design makes it tempting to just dump all cables in the back, but you will regret this on a hot day when you need to swap a power supply. A tidy cabling approach pays off with improved airflow and easier troubleshooting, which is a win for both your sanity and your uptime.
