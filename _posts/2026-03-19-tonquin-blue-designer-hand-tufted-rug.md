## Maintenance: Cleaning, Stains, and Longevity
### Daily Care and Quick Fixes
Regular vacuuming is the simplest path to longevity. A low-suction setting or a vacuum with a gentle brush helps prevent fiber pull and keeps the pile looking fresh. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear; this is especially important in high-traffic areas. For small spills, act fast: blot, don’t rub, with a clean cloth. A mild soap solution or a dedicated rug cleaner is usually sufficient for most household mishaps. The goal is to avoid setting stains by letting gravity do its job a little too well.

### Stains and Deep Cleaning
For tougher stains, you may need to treat with a spot cleaner suitable for the fiber type. Always test in an inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. If you’ve got kids who think the rug is a canvas for markers, or a pet who believes the floor is a launching pad for “paw-formance art,” you’ll appreciate the rug’s stain resistance and the fact that you won’t need a professional cleaning every time a coffee spill happens at 7 a.m. on a weekday.

For those who love a deeper clean, most Tufted rugs can be professionally cleaned. If you’re unsure about DIY deep cleaning, check your rug’s care guide. Some manufacturers recommend only light professional cleaning to preserve the pile’s integrity, while others allow gentle steam cleaning. In any case, avoid harsh solvents; we don’t want the blue turning into a purple pond in the middle of your living room.

### Longevity: How Long Will It Last?
