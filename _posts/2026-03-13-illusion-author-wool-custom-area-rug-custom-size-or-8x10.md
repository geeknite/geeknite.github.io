## Materials and Construction
### Wool quality and feel
The recipe for a good wool rug is partly physics, partly art, and mostly about selecting wool fibers that can stand up to daily life without sacrificing softness. The Illusion Author uses a dense, hand-finished wool pile that feels plush underfoot but resists flattening after months of weekend lounging and cat-swoop zoomies. Wool has natural temperature regulation properties, so the rug tends to feel cooler in the summer and warmer in the winter, which is nice for your HVAC bills and your comfort.

### Weave type and pile height
This rug is typically described as a medium-heddle, high-density weave. The pile height is designed to strike a balance between luxury and practicality: comfortable enough to sit on during game night, yet robust enough to withstand high-traffic hallways and the occasional obstacle course of toys and remote controls. If you’re counting millimeters, think in the neighborhood of 1/2 to 3/4 inch pile, a range that most homes can handle without tripping over the edge of fashion.

### Durability and care
Wool rugs have a reputation for durability when properly cared for. Expect resistance to everyday wear, a degree of stain resilience, and a color palette that ages gracefully. The aim is to preserve the illusion-thick pile while maintaining a surface that looks crisp and plush, not worn and threadbare.

## Sizing and Customization: How to order
### Deciding between 8x10 and a custom size
If your room is chair-swept and you want a classic anchor, the 8x10 is a reliable choice. It frames most sofas elegantly and leaves enough room to showcase a coffee table or a pair of accent chairs. If your space has an unusual footprint or you’re aiming for a wall-to-wall effect with a defined seating zone, a custom size will do the trick without forcing a compromise on the illusion patterns.

### How to measure your space
- Measure length and width of the area you want to cover
- Factor in furniture placement: ensure there is at least 18 inches of rug visible beyond the edges of the seating area (for the classic lounge look) and consider the clearance under doors
- Consider rug pad thickness for stability and comfort

### Ordering steps
1. Choose your desired size (8x10 or custom)
2. Pick a colorway and pattern that matches your vibe
3. Confirm pile height and weave details
4. Provide room dimensions for precise edge drafting
5. Approve a digital mockup and wait for the loom to work its magic

### Shipping, lead times, and returns
Custom options typically require a longer lead time than stock sizes. Be prepared for a manufacturing window that could range from a few weeks to a couple of months, depending on production demand and color customization. Shipping is usually careful, with protective wrappings to shield the fibers from the perils of transit. Return policies for customized rugs differ from standard stock items, so reading the terms is essential. If there is any doubt, contact the seller or sales support to confirm dimensions and color accuracy before production begins.

## Practical considerations and care tips
- Rug pads are not optional; they help prevent slipping and extend the life of the rug by reducing friction against hard floors.
- Vacuum with a gentle setting and avoid aggressive beater bars that can damage the pile.
- Clean spills quickly with blotting and a mild cleaner designed for wool. Avoid harsh chemicals that may alter the color or texture.
- Consider seasonal professional cleaning to maintain the wool’s integrity and the illusion’s crispness.
- If you own pets, trim nails and use deterrents sparingly; wool is resilient, but sharp claws can affect texture over time.
