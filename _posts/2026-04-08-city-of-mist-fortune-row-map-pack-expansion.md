## Overview

Fortune Row is pitched as a micro-urban expansion that lets you layer more streets, hideouts, and neon-slick corners onto the already humming metropolis of New York by Night. Think of it as a set of four fully realized micro-districts that slot into your ongoing city map, each one offering a unique mood, a handful of locations, and a handful more plot hooks than a coffee shop has loyalty cards.

This is not a surface-level add-on. The Map Pack respects the spirit of City of Mist, which is about storytelling through mythic identities, rogues galleries, and the tension between power and vulnerability. The Fortune Row expansion gives you more levers to pull when you want to push a scene toward a personal revelation for a character, a petty power grab by a faction, or a sudden twist that reframes what your players believed about a central location.

If you want to chase a pattern in your campaign that feels both grounded and mythic, Fortune Row is likely to scratch that itch. If you want a glossy set of glossy maps with nothing but eye candy and a few encounter prompts, you might still enjoy it, but you might end up using it sparingly. The difference is in the tables. This pack is about enabling stories, not just filling map space.

## What is in Fortune Row

The Fortune Row Map Pack Expansion provides a compact but dense bundle of content. Here is the lay of the land, so to speak:

- Four full-color micro-district maps, each with a distinct vibe and a couple of signature locations. These are not your typical generic blocks; they come loaded with environmental storytelling prompts and optional overlays that you can mix and match with existing maps.
- A set of encounter overlays that can be dropped onto any location to shift mood, stakes, and tension in a single turn. Think of overlays as mood lighting for your scenes, from neon rain to fog of rumors.
- A handful of new NPCs and factions that orbit Fortune Row. They interact with existing factions in interesting ways, letting you layer in subplots without having to rewrite city politics from scratch.
- A suite of story prompts and micro-arcs that are easy to drop into a session if your players hit a wall or you want a quick pivot when a scene stalls.
- New event cards that can be shuffled into your existing deck for a quick push toward a dramatic beat mid-session.
- Optional one-page GM aids and quick reference sheets to save table time. These include quick stat blocks for new antagonists and a small glossary of terms you can drop into narration on the fly.

The packaging feels thoughtful. The maps are glossy enough to look good on a screen but sturdy enough to survive a table that treats cardboard like a contact sport. The art direction leans into the noir chrome of City of Mist without tipping into self-parody. It is stylish, not ostentatious, and it respects the GM who needs to improvise while still maintaining a certain mood.


## Maps and Layout

Fortune Row gives you four districts, each with a strong sense of place. The composition tends to favor compact blocks with verticality and clear focal points that are easy to describe in a scene. You get details that matter for roleplay: a back-alley that smells like rain and secrets, a storefront that seems innocent until a memory slips into the wrong player’s hands, and a rooftop that offers a spectacular view of the city lights and nothing good happens after dark—per City of Mist tradition, of course.

The map layouts themselves are designed for tabletop play rather than wallpaper admiration. Each district supports multiple entry points for scenes, encouraging different teams to approach a location from a different axis. If your players love handoffs, there are plenty of doors and windows to exploit for escapes and misdirections. If you lean into investigative play, there are multiple sightlines and elevation changes that reward careful observation and well-timed tells.

One neat feature is the modular overlays. You can apply a single overlay to a map in minutes to shift the entire scene. A rain overlay can transform a street market into a slick chase sequence. A rumor overlay can turn a quiet block into a pressure cooker where every NPC has something to hide. The overlays do not feel gimmicky; they feel like legit tools to accelerate storytelling without forcing a hard rail.

## How Fortune Row Interacts with City of Mist Rules

City of Mist is built around the idea of the player characters as masked heroes with real world consequences. The Fortune Row expansion respects this core. It does not impose a new system, it extends the storyteller toolkit. Here are a few concrete ways it interacts with the base rules:

- Enhanced investigative depth: The new districts favor small-group play and one-on-one investigative scenes. The micro-areas include prompts that align with the Mist mechanics, helping players uncover a character’s true identity by connecting clues to their known symbols.
- Mood and tone control: The overlays give you narrative levers to press when players lean into a particular emotion. A shift from glitzy glamour to grim desperation can be achieved with a single map tint and a couple of well-chosen prompts.
- Faction dynamics: The new entities in Fortune Row bring fresh conflicts to the city. They can clash with existing factions or form uneasy alliances with the PCs, which is precisely the kind of morally gray tension that City of Mist loves.
- Time and pacing aids: The event cards and micro-arcs offer quick pacing hooks. If a session veers off track, you can deploy a micro-arc to re-center attention on a character goal or a city-wide consequence.

All of this is done with respect for the game’s core systems. There is no slapstick conversion or heavy retooling required. If you know the base rules, you can slot Fortune Row into your game with minimal friction and a maximum of storytelling vibes.


## Narrative Hooks and Encounters

Fortune Row shines when you lean into storytelling rather than map exploration for its own sake. The prompts are tailored to the city layer of City of Mist rather than the superpower layer, which is a smart choice for most groups. You will find hooks for identity revelation, mystery about a long-forgotten ally, and moral choices that ripple through the city’s underworld.

Here are a few sample prompts you can lift straight into a session:
- A memory shard surfaces in a storefront window. A player realizes a past identity is closer to the surface than they admit, forcing a confrontation that is less about power and more about truth.
- A rumor spreads through Fortune Row like a street vendor shouting a bargain. The rumor is true in one sense and false in the other, creating a breadcrumb trail that players must disentangle to prevent a disaster.
- A faction tries to court a player with a gift that comes at a price. The gift is practical and tempting, but accepting it would change how the city sees the player hero.

The micro-arcs can be dropped into sessions where you need a quick pivot. If a scene stalls, you can deploy a micro-arc in two sentences and watch a room wake up with laughter, tension, and perhaps a little fear.


## NPCs, Factions, and Interactions

New faces in Fortune Row come with clear motives and readable hand gestures. They are not mere set dressing; they have impact on the story, and their interactions with existing factions can send your plot in unexpected directions. The NPCs include:

- The Velvet Clerk, a fixer who knows what every person is hiding, and is happy to broker a deal with whoever can pay their price in secrets.
- The Signal Choir, a faction that communicates through coded songs in public places. They are not heroic, nor are they villainous in a tidy way; they exist to complicate choices and force players to weigh loyalty against truth.
- A small-time syndicate that suddenly finds itself outmatched by a player character who suddenly embraces a new identity, revealing a weakness that can be exploited by rivals.

These NPCs give DMs material to improvise around. They are not props; they are living elements of the city that respond to the players, just like a good noir plot expects.

Writing these characters into your campaign is a delight. They feel like they belong to New York by Night rather than a static map. They are not mere flavor; they push decisions, they elicit emotional responses, and they create moments that feel earned rather than contrived.

## How It Stacks Up Against Other Map Packs

If you are comparing Fortune Row to other map packs, a few distinctions emerge. Fortune Row leans into the City of Mist ethos more than most generic map expansions. It assumes players will engage with the city as a character and not just a playground for fights. If you prefer modular, self-contained adventures, Fortune Row still offers value via its overlays and micro-arcs, but you will get the best return if your table breathes through the city as a living organism, not just a stage for battles.

Compared to some other map packs that lean heavily into tactical grid play, Fortune Row rewards narrative finesse. It does not punish you for taking the scenic route, which is a big plus for groups that enjoy roleplay and atmosphere. If your last two campaigns had a bit too much combat and a bit too little conversation, this pack aims to rebalance that equation in a natural way.

## Final Verdict

Fortune Row is a well-crafted addition to City of Mist that elevates the urban layer of your campaign with mood, texture, and narrative momentum. It is not a problem-solver for every table, but for groups who love the city as a character and want to explore the edges where myth and neon collide, this expansion lands with style. If you run City of Mist primarily as a noir investigative experience, Fortune Row offers a toolkit that will feel new, useful, and satisfying. If your table leans more toward dungeon crawls and tactical maps, you may still appreciate the overlays and prompts for the vibes they bring, but you might not extract the full value as quickly.

In short: Fortune Row is a confident, moody, and practical expansion that respects the core mechanics while giving you new levers for storytelling. It is easy to slot into a campaign, offers a high ceiling for narrative brilliance, and comes with enough visual and tactile quality to justify its price. If you want to add a few blocks of soul to your city and a handful of unforgettable scenes to your sessions, this Map Pack will do the job with flair.
