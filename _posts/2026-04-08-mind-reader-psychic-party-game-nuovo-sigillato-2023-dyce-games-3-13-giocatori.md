## What is Mind Reader, really?
Mind Reader hinges on the idea that someone in the group is the \"Mind Reader\" while others serve as the minds behind the messages. The non-Mind Reader players each create a secret answer to a prompt on their card. The Mind Reader then tries to determine which answer matches the prompt or to guess the general direction of the group consensus. The mechanics vary with the edition, but in the 2023 signature experience, you typically have rounds of creative prompts, a guessing phase, and a scoring phase. The group dynamic is the key: quick wit, misdirection, and occasional chaos.

This is not a game about psychic powers in the dour sense; it\'s a social performance. The fun comes from how well players can read the room, how confidently someone can present an answer, and how aggressively others react to a misread—often with exaggerated disbelief or genuine astonishment.

## Setup and turn structure
Setting up Mind Reader for a 3- to 13-player session is a breeze, which is the saving grace of many party games. Here\'s the typical flow:
1) Each round, one player is designated the Mind Reader. Depending on the variant, this can rotate clockwise or be chosen randomly.
2) Prompt cards are drawn, each with a prompt that invites a personal, funny, or bizarre answer.
3) The non-Mind Readers write their secret answers on individual cards or slips. The goal is to craft an answer that you think the Mind Reader will connect to the prompt, or that will be misread by the Mind Reader as the strongest fit.
4) The Mind Reader reads the pile aloud (or reads the scene) and tries to pick out the answer that best matches the prompt or represents the collective vibe.
5) Scoring occurs. Depending on the edition, you gain points for correct guesses and/or for the Mind Reader’s ability to correctly identify the author of the chosen answer. In some twists, players can bluff with additional mechanics to shake up the results.
6) Rounds repeat, with the role of Mind Reader rotating.

If you have a full house (13 players), you may want to appoint a moderator to keep track of who wrote what, and to enforce a strict time limit so the pace doesn\'t derail into a marathon of giggles and interruptions.
