## What is Fortune Row in City of Mist Anyway?
Fortune Row is not just a catchy phrase; it represents a design space within the game where your characters connect through fate, relationships, and the scrawl of backstory. In practice, Fortune Row can be thought of as a governing mechanic that tracks how your character’s personal myths intersect with the city they inhabit. The digital sheet for Fortune Row attempts to capture two core ideas:

- The Mythos vs Logos balance: How much of the character is divine fate and how much is rational investigation.
- The Node of Relationships: A place to map significant contacts, mentors, rivals, and family that push the story forward.

This is not a mere cosmetic change. It affects how players narrate scenes, how GMs orchestrate clues, and how the table tells a coherent myth across sessions. If you have ever felt that your hero’s backstory is a sprawling map that doesn’t fit on a single page, Fortune Row is here with a digital pin to drop on every important clue you pick up during a session.

- Play Flow and Quick Checks: The sheet includes a few one-click checks and prompts to help players decide which Mythos or Logos virtue to lean on in a given scene. This is where the fun of dispatching a dramatic reveal meets the discipline of a rule-based table.

If you are allergic to clunky UI, you’ll appreciate the streamlining here. If you love tinkering with rules to extract the perfect dramatic moment, you’ll enjoy the customization options that let you keep the flavor while preserving the structure.

## Design Critique: What Works and What Could Be Beefier
Like any good gadget, Fortune Row digital sheets have strengths and opportunities for improvement. Here are the main takeaways from a seasoned table pirate’s perspective:

### What Works
- Clarity and Focus: The page layouts emphasize the most important data first. You won’t need to scroll through a mile of dead space to get to a key prompt.
- Narrative Prompts: The sheet’s prompts do a surprisingly good job of spurting ideas into players’ minds, reducing the dreaded “blank character sheet paralysis.”
- Relationship Intensive: Given City of Mist’s emphasis on relationships, Fortune Row nails the relational layer by making it easy to map connections and their significance to the story arc.
- Lightweight Customization: It doesn’t lock you into a single interpretation. Players can tweak fields to suit their character concept while maintaining a consistent framework.

### Could Be Better
- Offline Mode: A lot of players enjoy working with sheets offline, especially when travel-sized adventures pop up. A robust offline mode would be a welcome addition.
- GM Overlays: While there are player-facing features, a more robust GM toolkit within the same sheet could speed up session prep. Think scene cards, clue dependencies, and quick stat checks for antagonists.
- Richer Visuals for Clues: The clue cards could benefit from more visual weight, perhaps with icons or small artwork that captures the mood of the clue at a glance.
- Mobile Responsiveness: A lot of gaming happens away from a big desk. While the design is decent on tablets, ensuring a flawless mobile experience would extend Fortune Row’s reach.

That said, the balance of simplicity and narrative support is commendable. It avoids turning your sheet into a spreadsheet dungeon and instead gives you a stage on which your mythic self can act out the drama of the city you inhabit.

## Practical Tips for Using Fortune Row in Your Session
- Start with the Relationships: Before you delve into powers, map your core connections. It’s not just flavor; it informs your clues and your path through the story.
- Use the Clue Tracker Early: Jot down at least two clues per session. You’ll thank yourself later when the final reveal feels earned instead of forced.
- Pair with a Quick-Reference Sheet: Because City of Mist is about swapping myth and truth on the fly, keep a one-page cheat sheet handy for quick references to Mythos vs Logos prompts.
- Make a Ritual of Updates: End the session by updating the sheet with the new beats that emerged. This habit will make the sheet feel alive and essential rather than a post-game chore.
- Customize Prompts Cautiously: The prompts are powerful. Tinker around but avoid over-customizing to the point where you lose the standard Arc of the Fortune Row concept.
