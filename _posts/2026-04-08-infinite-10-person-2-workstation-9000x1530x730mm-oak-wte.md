## Assembly Experience: To assemble or not to assemble, that was the question
Let’s be honest: assembling large open-plan desks is not exactly a weekend spa retreat. The Infinite 10 Person 2-Workstation arrives with components that will remind you of a green LEGO set that you forgot to rain-proof before the dog walked on it. The good news is that the design is modular enough to allow for a two-person or three-person assembly crew, depending on your tolerance for hex keys and spare screws that seem to multiply when you’re not looking.

- Pre-assembly prep: Clear a large workspace, sort hardware by type, and have a friendly beverage on standby. You’ll thank me later when you don’t have to search for the tiny M6 bolts in a pile that looks suspiciously like a small LEGO disaster.
- Time investment: A typical two-bank assembly might take 4-6 hours for a release-ready installation if you’re collaborating with a team that knows the difference between a hex key and a butter knife. If you’re alone and determined, you can probably shave a few hours off by channeling your inner efficiency demon and listening to a solid playlist while you torque away.
- Practical tips: Keep a magnetic tray handy for small screws; use a level to ensure surfaces sit flush; and be mindful of the order of assembly—some brackets require you to have the frame partially anchored before you slot in the long panels. The instructions are clear enough to prevent a catastrophe, but not so strict that your inner rebel rebels against it.

In short, the assembly is not a dance with fate; it’s more like a well-choreographed group workout where everyone knows the moves, but the goal is simply to stand upright with dignity at the end. If you’re building in a team, assign roles: a lead assembler, a cable manager, and a tester who will pretend to be content with a bookshelf-level cable tidy at the end of the day.

## Ergonomics and Cable Management: The daily dance
Ergonomics on a large open desk system like this hinge on a few practical things: legroom, monitor placement, and cable chaos control. With a 9000 mm length, you’ve got room to accommodate multiple monitor arms, laptops, docking stations, and that mythical thing called “personal space.” The working surface height at 730 mm is within typical ranges for seated work, and there’s enough under-surface clearance to let taller teammates stretch their legs without a shin-bump paranoia epidemic.

Cable management in such a scale is a calling card. The unit includes integrated channels and grommets that allow you to thread power, data, and peripherals away from the public gaze. The goal is to achieve a clean look while still allowing easy access to outlets. The reality? You’ll still be chasing a tangle of cables behind a vent, but at least you’ll be chasing a curated, almost intentional, tangle rather than a chaotic monster that looks like a spaghetti factory exploded under your desk.

For office ergonomics, the desk surface is generous, inviting you to spread attachments without feeling like you’re playing a board game where every piece fights for space. If you’re into ergonomics metrics, you can pair this with monitor arms at varying heights to ensure eye-level alignment without your neck turning into a question you don’t want to answer on a Monday morning.

## Maintenance, Longevity, and Daily Realities
Oak White veneer tends to age well with proper care. Wipe-downs with a gentle cleaner, avoid harsh solvents, and try not to scratch the surface with DIY tool commandments. The laminate layer is designed to resist minor scuffs and coffee spills; however, prolonged exposure to high heat, prolonged sun exposure on unfinished edges, or direct contact with hot cookware is not recommended. Rotate chairs and accessories periodically to minimize wear in high-traffic zones.

The steel frame should hold up to years of use, provided you’re not forcing it to act as a doorstop or a makeshift trampoline. The hardware provided includes the standard assortment of screws, anchors, and washers you expect from a modular system, along with a few extras in case you misplaced something during assembly. If you’re paranoid about maintenance, consider setting up a quarterly inspection to tighten bolts and review cable management to prevent the occasional “flare-up” in visual chaos.
