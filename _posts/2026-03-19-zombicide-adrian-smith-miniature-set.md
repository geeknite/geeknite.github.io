## Painting and Assembly Tips

One of the big reasons to buy a special guest set is the potential to show off the painter you wish you were when you started this hobby.

- Prime with black spray and then build up to mid-tones with a blue-gray base coat. The high-contrast style from Smith often relies on strong edge highlights, so keep your highlights crisp.
- Use a glaze to push the shadows back into the deeper recesses. It’s easy to overdo shading, but a thin glaze is like giving the model a soft, cinematic glow rather than a church-choir halo.
- For zombie flesh, experiment with bluish-gray undertones beneath greenish hues to achieve a sickly but cinematic look.
- Weapons and armor: focus on metallics with a bit of weathering—tiny scratches and battle-damage go a long way toward selling the this party has seen some action vibe.
