## How to Play: The Cat Burglar Twist

### Turn structure and core loop

- Each player draws a card to start their turn (as usual in Exploding Kittens).
- You may play one or more Cat Burglar cards—these are the zingers of the set. The key feature is the ability to steal a card from another player’s hand when you successfully play a Cat Burglar action.
- After playing any cards you want, you end your turn by drawing one card from the deck. If you draw an Exploding Kitten and have a Defuse card, you can play it to survive (and likely pass the exploding joy to the next player). If you don’t have a Defuse, you’re out of the game in true dramatic fashion—cue the cat fireworks and mournful meows.
- The game continues until only one player remains.

### Cat Burglar actions and the new flavor cards

The Cat Burglar Edition introduces a handful of new action cards and counters that tilt the balance toward stealth and manipulation. Typical cards you’ll encounter include:

- Cat Burglar: Steal a card from any player’s hand. The stolen card goes into your hand—mischief achieved. You can chain burglaries if you have multiple Cat Burglar cards, but your opponents will wise up and start defending their hands accordingly.
- Lockpick: A card that allows you to bypass certain defensive moves or peek at another player's hand. It nudges the game toward strategic targeting rather than pure luck.
- Purr-suit or Paw Bank-type cards: These add a resource-management angle—save your defense for the right moment or spend a sneakier move to force an awkward setup on your neighbors.
- Counter cards: Designed to foil others’ Cat Burglar attempts, these keep the table lively and prevent one player from running away with a single blow.

A sample turn might look like this: you start with a modest hand. You play a Cat Burglar card to steal a card from someone who keeps a tight grip on their Defuses. You swing away a particularly pesky Defuse card or a high-value loot card, then you draw to end your turn. If you’re lucky, your stash grows; if you’re unlucky, someone else swipes your last-life Defuse card and you’re one step closer to the cat-astrophe of doom. The interplay between stealing and defending creates a dynamic dance that’s surprisingly tense for a game that’s basically about silly cats.

### Strategy quick-start guide

- Early aggression: Don’t wait for the perfect moment to steal. A well-placed Cat Burglar early on can disrupt someone who hogs Defuses or lock in a critical card you’ll need later.
- Hand management: Keep a balance of defense and offense cards. Cat Burglar cards are great, but if you burn through them too early, you’ll be left defenseless when the deck hits the ticking time bomb.
- Read the table: If someone seems to be fishing for a particular card, defend accordingly. Counter moves and Lockpicks work best when you anticipate where your opponents feel most exposed.
- Endgame awareness: In the final stretch, every draw matters. If you’re behind, you may have to take bolder risks; if you’re ahead, stab at a few safeties and wait for someone else to explode before you claim the victory.
