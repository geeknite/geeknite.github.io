## The Art: Style, palette, and mood
Guiton’s art rests on three pillars: silhouette readability, texture depth, and a sense of danger that feels immediate yet cinematic. Inside the pages, you’ll find portraits that feel like they could be spoken to, and environments that podrace between ruin and illumination. The lighting is decisive; breaks of daylight slice through shattered skylights, and the resulting chiaroscuro feels both classic and modern. It’s film noir in a city overrun by the undead, which is exactly the vibe we crave on a Monday when the coffee is weak but the undead aren’t.

The palette leans toward desaturated blues, ash-toned browns, and the occasional jolt of crimson or sickly green to flag danger. It’s a language that says apocalypse with style, not grim seriousness alone. And yes, there are memorable zombie designs—hulking brutes, stitched coats, eyes that glow with a story beyond their teeth. Edouard’s creatures sometimes seem to whisper, Make me a part of your world, and you’ll see I am not just a prop in your encounter table.
