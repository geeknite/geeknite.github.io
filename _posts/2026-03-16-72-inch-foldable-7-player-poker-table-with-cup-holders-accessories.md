## Assembly and setup experience
### Quick assembly playbook
If you have ever built a modular bookshelf or a flexible LED lighting strip, the assembly will feel familiar. The key is to sort hardware first, lay out the table top, attach the foldable legs, secure the cross bracing, and then test the folding mechanism. The instructions are typically straightforward, and the diagrams do a decent job of showing which leg goes where. Expect some minor adjustments as you align the top surface with the frame; like all good things in life, symmetry matters, especially when you are trying to coax a straight flush out of a ring of chaos.

### Setup tips for best play
- Clear the area: A poker table needs room to breathe. Move those plush throw pillows out of the way; your legs will thank you.
- Level the height: Use the adjustable feet to ensure a comfortable seating position for all seven players. The goal is to avoid neck strain and accidental table tipping when a big hand hits the river.
- Secure the top: Make sure the top is locked into the frame. A loose top makes for weird card bounces and questionable bets.
- Check the felt: Lightly brush the surface to lift any stray fibers from packaging; this helps with consistent card glide and reduces chances of card drag during tough hands.

## Durability and maintenance
### Long term wear and tear
A good foldable table should stand up to repeated setup, takedown, and the occasional game-night marathon that runs longer than your favorite sci fi binge. The prominent concerns include surface scratches, edge fraying, and the possibility of leg wobble after many uses. High quality tables tend to use robust frame materials and reinforced joints to minimize loosening over time. Regular maintenance such as wiping the surface with a mild fabric cleaner and re-tightening screws after a dozen sessions helps keep things solid.

### Cleaning tips
- Wipe spills promptly with a damp cloth to prevent stains.
- Use a light cleaner on the surface; avoid harsh chemicals that can degrade the felt color.
- For the cup holders, remove and wash them with warm soapy water to keep odors away and to prevent sticky residue.
- When folding, give the mechanism a light spray of lubricant or a dry silicone spray if the joints feel stiff after many uses.
