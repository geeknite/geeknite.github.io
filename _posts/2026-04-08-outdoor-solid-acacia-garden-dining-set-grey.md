## Weather resistance and maintenance
Acacia wood has natural oils that resist water penetration, but a sealant is still recommended. Apply outdoor wood sealer or penetrating oil once per season to preserve color and reduce surface cracks. Do not use oil meant for kitchen use. Routine maintenance keeps the color even and prevents patchy wear. For climates with harsh sun or salty air, you may wish to perform a mid-season refresh to maintain its uniform grey finish. The results speak for themselves when the sun hits the table and you realize you are the proud owner of something that looks good enough for a magazine spread while surviving a weekend of family gatherings.

### Cleaning tips and care
For routine cleaning, use a soft brush or cloth with mild soap and water. Avoid pressure washers at close range to prevent the wood from absorbing water. Wipe down after meals to prevent residue buildup. Stubborn stains may respond to diluted vinegar; test first on a small area. If the color begins to fade or develop uneven patches, a light sanding followed by a reapplication of sealant will restore the surface without breaking the bank. The aim is not to erase the character of the wood but to keep it healthy and resilient for another season of outdoor living.

## Maintenance routine and care accessories
- Cushions: opt for quick-dry fabrics and removable covers that can be machine washed.
- Covers: use fitted outdoor covers to shield from heavy rain or snowfall when not in use.
- Sealants: select a penetrating outdoor wood sealer recommended for acacia or hardwoods and apply per the manufacturer guidance. A refresh every year or two depending on climate will preserve color and reduce the likelihood of surface cracks.
