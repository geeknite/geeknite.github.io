## Playing the Game: Rules and Quick Primer
UNO Flip retains the familiar core of UNO: match a color or a number, play action cards to keep the chaos manageable, and try to get rid of all your cards first. But the twist is the flip mechanic. When you play the flip card, or when a certain card instructs you to flip, the entire deck is turned over to the Dark Side (the darker color with its own set of rules). On the dark side, the penalties can escalate quickly: more draw cards, more forced changes in direction, and a few twisty new actions that keep players on their toes.

### Core Mechanics
- Start with a hand of cards (usually seven). On your turn, you play a card that matches either color or number, or use a special action card.
- If you cannot play, you draw a card from the deck. If that card can be played, you may (depending on the variant) place it immediately or end your turn.
- When a flip card is activated, players flip their hands to the other side of the deck, exposing the Dark Side actions. This creates an added strategic layer: do you hold on to a powerful card on the light side, or risk flipping and losing the opportunity to control the pace?

## Practical Playability: Variant Rules and House Rules
A lot of families and groups like to implement house rules to tailor UNO Flip to their local chaos level. The GDG37 open box edition invites just enough variance to keep things lively without requiring a full-on manual rewrite. Some common variants include:
- Stacking: If you can stack “Draw 2” or “Draw Five” cards when matching, the penalties escalate rapidly, which is perfect if you want a crying-laugh track from the back row.
- Quick Flip: Limit the number of flips to 2 per round to prevent the deck from turning into a full-blown rave mid-game.
- Ruleableness: You can decide that some cards can be played on both sides, or that the dark side only triggers after a flip card is used, to minimize chaos on small tables.
