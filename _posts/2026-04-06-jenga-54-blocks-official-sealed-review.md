## Caring for the Blocks: Maintenance and Longevity

As with any wooden toy, the longevity of Jenga blocks depends on care. The sealed block set is designed for repeated use, so a little maintenance goes a long way. Here are a few tips to keep your blocks in perfect condition:

- Keep them dry. Wood loves moisture as a concept; not so much in practice. If a spill happens, wipe it quickly and let the blocks air-dry. If you’re in a damp environment, consider a light occasional wipe with a dry cloth to remove any residual humidity.
- Avoid sun exposure. Prolonged sunlight can cause the wood to fade slightly and the finish to become uneven. If you display your set as a decorative piece, rotate it with the seasons to minimize sun damage.
- Check for splinters. If you notice any rough edges developing, sand them gently with a fine-grit sandpaper. Then, wipe off the dust and seal with a light coat of a non-toxic finish if you’re worried about roughness affecting gameplay.
- Store properly. A sealed box lives to be opened again. Keep the set in a dry place, away from curious pets and playful children who may treat the blocks as building blocks for forts—only in this case, you don’t want to see a three-block column become a skyscraper.

Among the practical concerns, one often overlooked is the block alignment. Over time, blocks can wear into a slightly cupped shape if stored in a way that creates uneven pressure. The solution is simple: rotate the blocks in their tray every so often so no single block bears the brunt of gravity’s judgment day. It’s a small ritual that keeps the experience consistent and reduces the risk of a top-heavy catastrophe when the tower finally tips.
