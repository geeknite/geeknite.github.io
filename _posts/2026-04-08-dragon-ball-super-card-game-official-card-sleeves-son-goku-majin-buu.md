## Durability and Material
DBSCG sleeves generally use 60–80 microns of protection. These two sleeves feel sturdy, not overly stiff, with a smooth surface that should glide against card stock. The matte finish helps reduce glare in tournament lighting, though not so matte that cards feel glued to the sleeve like a museum exhibit. We tested with a 60-card deck and a handful of promotional cards; the sleeve fit was snug but not tight, which is exactly the sweet spot you want when you’re shuffling in a frenzy after you realize you forgot to attack last turn.

## Storage, Maintenance, and Care
Caring for sleeves extends their life. Keep them away from extreme heat or direct sunlight to minimize color fading. If you have a cat that loves to sit on your play area, consider a closed box or tray to prevent cat hair from embedding into the sleeve’s surface. A light wipe with a microfiber cloth can keep them free of fingerprints after long match nights.

### Practical tips
- Store stacks in a cool, dry place to avoid warping.
- Use a deck box with separate compartments for sleeves to minimize friction against other sleeves.
- Rotate sleeves for longer tournaments to evenly distribute wear.
