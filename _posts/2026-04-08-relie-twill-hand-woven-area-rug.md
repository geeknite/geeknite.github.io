## Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Looking Fresh
### Cleaning basics
- Vacuum regularly using a low-suction setting to avoid pulling fibers out of the weave. Use a brush attachment rather than a beater bar if you have a delicate hand woven rug. 
- Attend to spills quickly. Blot with a clean, dry cloth rather than rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers. 
- For general cleaning, use a mild soap solution or a rug-specific cleaner recommended by the manufacturer. Always spot test in an inconspicuous area first.

### Stain management and dye stability
Natural fiber rugs often respond differently to cleaners. Avoid harsh chemicals and bleach, as these can damage the fibers and alter the dye. If you are dealing with stubborn stains, professional cleaning is a safe route. The good news is that a well-made hand woven rug tends to withstand routine cleaning better than inexpensive machine-made options, and the weave itself offers a robust structure that can resist casual wear over time.

### Rotation and sun exposure
Consider rotating the rug every six months to a year to prevent uneven color fading from sun exposure. If you have strong, direct sunlight, UV protection or window treatments can help maintain the rug's color integrity. This is especially important for light or warm-toned rugs that can shift hue with prolonged sun exposure.
