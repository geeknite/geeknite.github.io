## Pull Rates and Value: The Economics of a Dragon Ball Pull
Here is the not-so-sexy truth: booster box economics are a game within the game. You’re paying for the thrill of the chase, the dopamine hit of a rare or foil, and the satisfaction of building a deck that might win you exactly one tournament before the local scene evolves beyond your favorite engine. In BT01, the distribution of rarity typically follows the standard pattern: a handful of commons, a reasonable amount of uncommons, and a handful of rares and foils with a few secret rares sprinkled in like a cherry on the top of a very fast, very shiny sundae.

Let’s break it down with some practical guidance (and no, there is no guaranteed ROI, unless your ROI is the personal satisfaction of a well-built deck and the joy of trading with friends):
- Common cards: These are your bread and butter. You’ll see a lot of them and, frankly, you’ll trade most of them away. They’re necessary for a functioning deck but not the stuff of legends.
- Uncommons: These start adding flavor to your deck and might contain standout effects that enable your strategy. Expect a few of these to see real play.
- Rares and foils: The real star power. A well-timed pull here can transform a deck’s viability or simply become a prized collectible for your binder.
- Secret rares: The unicorns. The mythical mint-condition rainbow of your card collection. They’re not guaranteed, but when you pull one, you know you’re playing the long game with a trophy you’ll show off at every meetup.

Value is highly context-dependent. A BT01 box can be a gateway into the hobby for a new player, providing a large pool of cards to experiment with. It can also be a thrilling gamble for a veteran who wants to chase a single big card while filling out their binder with other useful staples. If you’re chasing the long-term worth of your investment, remember that the secondary market for Dragon Ball Super cards fluctuates with the meta, tournament results, and the ever-present meme of the next big deck. The real value often lies in the social currency: trading, slamming a new deck on the table, and the joy of a hard-fought victory (or a hilarious failure) that becomes a memory you laugh about at future game nights.

For those who want a more concrete numeric estimate, here’s a simple way to frame your decision: consider your local box price, the average value of common and uncommon cards you’ll actually use in decks, and the potential upside of hitting a foil or secret rare. If the box price plus sleeves and deck-building costs lands you at a value where you’ll enjoy playing for a while, BT01 is likely a good purchase. If you’re aiming purely for maximum financial return, you’re probably in the wrong hobby and should pivot to stamp collecting or cryptocurrency memes, which have fewer misprints and more dramatic price volatility.

## Archetypes You Might Build from BT01
Archetypes are the DNA of any trading card game. They are the “color” of your strategy that gives your deck personality. BT01 introduces a mix of tools that can support a variety of playstyles. Here are a few archetypes you might explore, along with practical considerations.

### Red Aggro: Speed Over Everything
Red in many DBS formats is the color of tempo and aggression. If you like the idea of rushing your opponent down before they can set up, red aggro is your jam. Your BT01 pulls will ideally include early-game pressure and efficient removal to keep your opponent on the back foot. The challenge is to balance your threats with enough defensive play to survive the opening turns. A box with several solid low-cost battle cards is a good sign for a red-leaning strategy.

### Blue Control: The Slow, Calculated Dragoon
Blue control decks hinge on resource management, card draw, and removal that keeps your opponent from executing their plan. When you pull the right blue cards from BT01, you get a toolset that slows the game to your pace. The risk is overloading on counters and stalling so long that your own deck loses momentum. A healthy blue control build in BT01 should look for multi-purpose cards that serve as both counters and threats, letting you squeeze value every turn.

### Green Value: Ramp and Recursion
Green archetypes often lean into ramping up energy or using recursion to recycle important cards. BT01 might offer a handful of green options that help you reach late-game power without blowing all your resources early. Expect a few big bodies, some efficient blockers, and a handful of swift removal options that keep the field under control while you work toward a winning board state.

### Yellow Trickery: Mind Games and Disruption
If you enjoy the mind games and the in-between turns where your opponent second-guesses themselves, yellow can be a fun addition to BT01 decks. Look for cards that manipulate your opponent’s resources or force them to overcommit to a plan. Yellow often plays well with blue in DBS, creating a hybrid that can adapt to many threats.

If you’re new to deck-building, pick one archetype you find appealing and build outward. The BT01 set is not about locking you into a single strategy; it’s about giving you enough tools to run a few different lines and then adapt to what your local meta throws at you.

## A Quick Look at Card Roles in BT01
- Leaders: The face of your deck, dictating certain game plans and often providing a unique game-plan that can be trained around. Choose a leader that aligns with your preferred playstyle and your local meta.
- Battle Cards: The main attack vectors. These are your threats, your blockers, and sometimes your engines. Expect a mix of quick tempo plays and bigger, late-game threats.
- Energies: The resource backbone. Proper energy management is essential; mismanaging energy is basically begging your opponent to stub your toe with a well-timed removal spell.
- Extra Cards: Utility across the battlefield. They might let you draw more, search your deck, or disrupt your opponent in clever ways.
- Foils and Rares: The glittering prizes. These cards don’t just look cool; they can become the centerpiece of a deck or a coveted trade within your circle.
