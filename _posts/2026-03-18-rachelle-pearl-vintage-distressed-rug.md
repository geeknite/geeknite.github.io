## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Care
### Daily Care and Traffic Tips
Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and prevent hotspots under chairs. If you have pets, use a small handheld vacuum for quick cleanups and keep a tray of baking soda on hand for odors. The goal is to keep the texture lively while avoiding the mothball aesthetic that antique pieces sometimes develop.

### Spot Cleaning and Stain Removal
Treat spills promptly with a mild detergent solution and blot rather than rub. Rinse with clean water and blot again; avoid harsh chemicals that can fade the pattern. For stubborn stains, a mixture of water and white vinegar can neutralize odors without wreaking havoc on the color balance. Always test in an inconspicuous corner first.

### Deep Cleaning and Professional Care
Every couple of years, consider a professional rug cleaning to refresh the fibers and restore vibrancy. The frequency depends on traffic; households with high footfall or kids may benefit from more frequent sessions. In general, this rug holds up well to routine care and external grime but appreciates a crisp reset occasionally, much like a software update that actually improves performance.
