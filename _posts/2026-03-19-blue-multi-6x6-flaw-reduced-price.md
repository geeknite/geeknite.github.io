Materials, Build, and Care
--------------------------
Most consumer-grade geometric rugs like this one use a synthetic fiber blend that balances softness, stain resistance, and durability. Expect a pile that feels plush under bare feet yet robust enough for pet claws or the occasional toddler with a marker. Cleaning is usually straightforward: regular vacuuming (preferably with suction settings tuned for low pile since you don’t want to pull the weave), spot cleaning for spills, and a rotation schedule every few months to avoid permanent traffic patterns.

Pro tip: place a rug pad underneath. It reduces slipping, muffles noise, and can help extend the life of the rug by minimizing the friction that invites wear. If you own hardwood or tile, a non-slip pad keeps your rug in place and your coffee table from doing interpretive dance when you stand up.

Care and Maintenance: A Quick Guide
-----------------------------------
- Vacuum weekly to prevent dirt from embedding in the fibers.
- Rotate the rug every 3-6 months to minimize visible wear patterns from furniture placement.
- Clean spills promptly with a blotting method (don’t rub; you don’t want to push the spill into the weave).
- Use a mild detergent for tough stains and always test on a small hidden area first.
- Consider professional cleaning every 12-24 months depending on foot traffic.
