## How to Play in 2026 terms
Dixit Family sticks to the gut of Dixit: a storyteller, a deck, mystery, and a shared moment of storytelling and guessing. The twist is that the family edition emphasizes legibility, pace, and a little more leniency with clues. The storyteller picks a clue that should be evocative but not so obscure that no one can possibly guess. The other players select a card from their hands that best matches the clue, then all cards are revealed. The group votes to identify the storyteller card. Points are awarded based on whether players can guess correctly and on whether the storyteller manages to avoid being too obvious.

The family variant often uses a simplified scoring scheme: you score points for choosing the storyteller card if your guess is right, while the storyteller earns fewer or no extra points if the clue is too straightforward. The exact numbers in the rulebook are deliberate; the intention is to keep rounds brisk and the mood light. For younger players, especially, the fun lies in the shared interpretation rather than chasing victory at all costs. The result is a game that can end in a burst of giggles before the last round even begins to finish, which is a hallmark of a successful family game night.
