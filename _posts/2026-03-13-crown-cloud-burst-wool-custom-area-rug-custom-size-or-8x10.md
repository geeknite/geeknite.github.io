## Durability and Care: Keeping the Burst Fresh Through Real-Life Battles
### Cleaning and Maintenance
Wool repels many common stains thanks to its natural scales and structure. The recommended care usually includes regular vacuuming with a gentle beater bar or suction. For spills, blot—not rub—immediately with a clean cloth. If you ever encounter a stubborn stain, you can mild-clean with a wool-safe cleaner. Always spot-test first in a hidden area to ensure colorfastness. For deeper cleaning, many manufacturers recommend professional upholstery or rug cleaning services. You’ll want to follow the rug maker’s care guide to preserve the fibers’ bounce and color integrity over time. For general wool care, you can cross-check care tips with authoritative sources like Woolmark, which provides consumer-focused care guidance.

### Stain Resistance and Wool's Natural Properties
Wool fibers resist water-based stains more than synthetic counterparts, especially when treated with a protective finish at the manufacturing stage. That means your coffee spill will likely bead briefly and be easier to blot away. Of course, red wine remains a risk for any fabric, but wool’s fiber structure can handle a lot if you treat it promptly. The Burst pattern’s diverse color palette actually helps forgive accidental spills because some hues can disguise minor marks better than a solid color would.
