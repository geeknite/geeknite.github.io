## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity

### Routine Care

Wool rugs handle everyday life well but aren’t immune to the occasional spill, pet accident, or the mystery crumb that somehow knows the exact spot you sit to read. Regular vacuuming with a beater-bar setting on low power (or a brushless attachment for sensitive fibers) is a good start. Vacuum both sides if possible to remove accumulated dust and to maintain the pile’s integrity. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and to prevent the sun from bleaching one side more quickly than the other.

### Spill Response and Stain Management

Act quickly when spills happen, but avoid panic—the soap-and-water routine still works. Blot, don’t rub, to prevent pushing the spill deeper into the wool. For common household spills (coffee, wine, tomato sauce), a gentle blot and a mild wool-safe cleaner can do wonders. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first. Deep-set stains or odors might require professional cleaning, which is worth it to preserve the rug’s texture and color over time.

### Long-Term Maintenance and Odor Control

Wool fibers are natural odor absorbers, which is something you want when your rug is part of a well-used living space. Regular freshening helps, as does keeping humidity in check. In high-humidity climates, always dry the rug thoroughly after cleaning to prevent mildew. If you have pets, routine grooming of your furry friends reduces the amount of loose hair on the rug and can help keep the surface visually tidy longer.
