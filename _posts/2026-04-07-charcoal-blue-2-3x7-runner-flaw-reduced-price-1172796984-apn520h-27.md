## Materials, Build, and Longevity
This rug is described as a budget-friendly piece, so expectations should be calibrated. Here’s what to look for when evaluating material, backing, and construction:

- Fiber content: Many budget runners rely on synthetic blends (polypropylene, polyester, or a rayon blend) that resist stains and dry quickly. They aren’t silk, but they’re forgiving and easy to clean.
- Pile height: Compact to mid-density pile is common in 2-3 ft width runners, which helps with placement under doors without creating a tripping hazard. It also makes vacuuming easier.
- Backing and stability: A good non-slip backing matters in a hallway. If the rug feels a touch slick on hard floors, you’ll want a rug pad to prevent creeping and to protect your floor finish.
- Durability: Expect this rug to perform well with light to moderate foot traffic. In spaces with heavy traffic or pets that treat the hallway as the Great Outdoors, you may see flattening or wear in the long run.

Maintenance tips for longevity:
- Vacuum regularly with a canister vacuum or a lightweight upright. Avoid high-suction machines that pull at the fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing which can embed stains into the pile.
- For spots, a mild detergent solution and a soft brush should do the trick. Rinse with clean water and blot dry.

## Care and Cleaning Real Talk
Budget rugs need love, not fear. Here’s a practical care plan that keeps the color honest and the pile… well, piled high with joy.

- Vacuum weekly or as needed. Use gentle suction and a soft brush attachment.
- Blot spills promptly. For stain resistance, a mild detergent solution works wonders; avoid strong solvents that could affect dye consistency.
- Rotate occasionally. If your hallway gets a lot of sun on one end, rotate every few months to avoid uneven fading.
- Deep clean occasionally. A professional cleaner can refresh fibers and remove embedded dirt that household vacuums miss.
