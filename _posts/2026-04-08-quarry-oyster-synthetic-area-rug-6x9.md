## Cleaning, Care, and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh

- Regular Vacuuming: A weekly pass with a vacuum that has a beater bar or brush setting will help lift fibers and remove surface debris. Don’t go full-grid-mode heavy-handed; a gentle pass across the grain keeps the color looking even.
- Spot Cleaning: For spills, blot—not scrub. Use a mild detergent solution (a drop of dish soap in warm water works in a pinch). Work from the outside of the spill toward the center to prevent spreading.
- Deep Cleaning: If you’re really feeling adventurous, you can give the rug a light, shallow wash in a shallow tub or use a professional carpet cleaning service. Avoid soaking the rug for extended periods; synthetic fibers typically tolerate moisture, but excessive soaking can cause shrinkage or backing issues.
- Odor Control: If you’ve got two pets and a love for late-night snack experiments, occasional baking-soda light dusting can help neutralize odors. Let it sit for 15-20 minutes, then vacuum thoroughly.
- Rotating and Flipping: Every few months, rotate the rug by 90 degrees to distribute wear. This helps keep the pile looking uniform and avoids obvious high-traffic paths becoming resident landmarks of your living space.
- Pad Care: If you’re using a rug pad (and you probably should), clean or replace it as needed. A tired pad can cause slipping, creasing, and a general sense that your floor has mood swings.
