## How to play in 60 seconds or less

Exploding Kittens is not a marathon of rules. It is a sprint through brains and luck, stitched together with cat puns and dramatic shuffles. At a high level, you draw cards until someone pulls an exploding kitten, which ends their run unless they can play a defuse card to survive. Turn structure is simple: on your turn, you can play one or more action cards from your hand or use them to alter the effects of future draws. After you finish your actions, you end your turn and draw one card from the deck. The next player repeats, and the tension builds as the deck grows thinner and the bombs start to feel closer than your last group chat message.

Action cards spice things up. See the Future lets you peek at the top few cards, which is a fun peek into the chaos you are about to unleash. Skip and Attack cards alter the rate at which people draw; a well-timed See the Future card can give you the information you need to avoid disaster, while an Attack card can force someone else to take two pulls in a row. The Defuse card is your lifeline; it gives you a chance to diffuse the situation with a bomb by taking a defuse card, and sometimes that defuse is the difference between victory and a dramatic, cat-bomb flavored defeat. The real game is not about who wins; it is about who survives the most dramatic moments long enough to milk nine more laughs out of the table before bedtime.

The pacing is fast, which means you will be making snap judgments about who to target or which cards you want to keep for the right moment. The fairness of the draw can feel like a cosmic joke, but that is part of the charm. People who are worried about length should note that most rounds finish in around 10 to 20 minutes, depending on the number of players and the chaos you unleash. You can play multiple rounds in one evening if you want to build a silly little tournament where the winner gets some kind of silly victory trophy, like a plastic crown or a recovered, crusty cookie that somehow survived a toddler and a cat.
