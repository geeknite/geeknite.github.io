## How to Care for Your 1980s Blue Bicycle Cards

Care is simple, but it matters. Here are a few practical tips to keep your sealed deck as pristine as possible for the long haul:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The blue color is forgiving, but not immortal in a sunny window.
- If you must handle the deck, avoid moisture and oils on your fingertips. It’s okay to give yourself the occasional card-off on a clean, dry surface, but don’t “smear the nostalgia” with sweaty palms.
- Keep the tuck box from crushing by placing it in a display case or a shallow storage bin with dividers. A little air around the box helps the seal stay firm and the colors from bleeding onto adjacent items.
- Consider occasional gentle fan tests to verify flexibility; it’s better to discover a minor stiffness now than during a show-and-tell at a convention.

## Where to Buy, and How to Tell Fakes

If you’re hunting for a sealed blue 1980s Bicycle deck, start with reputable vintage game stores, auction houses that specialize in playing cards, and established collector markets. Look for clear photos of the tuck box, the seal, and any factory markings on the bottom of the card faces. Ask sellers for closeups of the seal edge and the top tuck area. Counterfeits, while not super common in this niche, do exist, especially in online marketplaces where stock photos are used ad infinitum. A few quick checks:

- Compare the back design and court cards with known references from the era. If something looks off in the artwork, ask for additional photos.
- Inspect edge wear around the tuck where the glue is visible; a deck that’s never been opened should show pristine edges.
- If the price seems too good to be true, it probably is. Vintage items, especially sealed ones, carry a premium for the story and condition.
