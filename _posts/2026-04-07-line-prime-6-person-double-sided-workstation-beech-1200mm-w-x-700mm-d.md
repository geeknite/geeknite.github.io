## Cable Management and Practicalities
One of the most important things about a shared workstation is how well it handles cables. The Line Prime 6P DS Beech is designed to keep things tidy, with an under-desk tray and channels that encourage the cables to behave. You’ll still have a handful of cable spaghetti if you try to run multiple monitors, docking stations, and a few clever AD adapters, but that is a feature of modern productivity—not a failure of the desk.

If your team is notorious for collective cable chaos, here are a few practical tips to keep things under control:
- Pre-plan your power distribution: a single power strip under each “node” can minimize trips to the power closet and reduce the risk of tripping hazards.
- Use color-coded labels for each workstation position. It makes life easier for beginners and saves your IT team from hosting a midfield volleyball match with the ethernet cables.
- Consider modular accessories: monitor arms, CPU holders, and cable organizers that integrate with the existing lines. If your plan includes heavy equipment, check that your cable tray is rated to handle the weight without complaints.

## Practical Setup Tips and Usage Scenarios
To get the most out of a six-person double-sided workstation, think of it as a shared command center rather than a row of individual desks. Here are some practical tips for maximizing collaboration while minimizing chaos:
- Assign zones: designate six seats per side and create a subconscious map of where to place favorite devices. This helps avoid a scenario where everyone stares at a single screen and wonders why the sprint appears to be looping.

- Plan for privacy when needed: use modesty panels or screen dividers to give your team moments of peace when someone on the team needs to focus on a tricky bug or a long email thread.
- Create channeling etiquette: designate specific places for power bricks and docking stations. It gives the room a sense of order that helps your team feel productive instead of chaotic.

In terms of day-to-day use, you’ll notice that the six-person design fosters collaboration without forcing constant proximity.
