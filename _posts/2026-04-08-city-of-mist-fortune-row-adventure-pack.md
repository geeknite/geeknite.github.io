## Introduction
City of Mist is a game about people who wear neon signs of myth and mischief on their bodies while doing questionable decisions in a big city. Fortune Row is the latest adventure pack that invites players to a street full of secrets, pacts, and maybe a bakery run by a caped villain who only bakes while singing. This review digs into the box, the micro DLCs, the story hooks, and whether you should drop your cash on this little piece of urban legend. If you are a GM who loves a good mystery with a side of drama, Fortune Row might be your next urban legend to punch up with some noir flair and a dash of mystic charisma.

## What's in Fortune Row
### A quick tour of the contents
Fortune Row ships with a well organized mix of components that scream professional product while still feeling like a friendly garage sale for mythic trinkets. Here is a snapshot of what you can expect:
- A concise adventure book that outlines a four act arc with escalating stakes and a few clever pivots for the GM
- A GM screen insert designed to be used during quick reveals and location snapshots
- A set of player handouts that hint at lore, factions, and the secrets behind a row that seems ordinary but hums with possibility
- Pre generated or easy to adapt archetypes that slot neatly into the Fortune Row motif
- A detailed map of Fortune Row with key locations, along with a few micro locations to spice a night
- Encounter cards or tokens that help track rising momentum and escalating mysteries
- Narrative prompts and side quests that let players chase down clues in a way that feels cinematic without sliding into railroading

### The vibe and setting
Fortune Row leans into the neon shadows and rainy sidewalks of a city that is as much a character as the players. The location is designed to feel like a living rumor mill, where fortunes are real in the sense that someone owes a debt to a power they barely understand. The content keeps the City of Mist tone intact by mixing mythic archetypes with modern street life. Expect a blend of noir intrigue, mythic symbolism, and a few moments that lean into the surreal in ways that make players grin and groan at the same time.

## How it plays with the core system
### Mechanics integration
City of Mist runs on a mythic framework where players embody legends as part of a sprawling narrative. Fortune Row slots into this framework without overhauling core rules. It introduces fresh prompts, a few thematic Legend arcs, and the kind of local color that makes a city feel alive. The mechanics stay familiar: you still lean on mythic attributes, make a few skill based checks when pursuing clues, and let the GMs hands be free to shift the narrative gear when players fail a roll in a way that feels fair and exciting.

The pack respects the balance between investigation and action. Expect a few multi layer scenes that escalate tension in a way that invites players to use both their Mythos and their Gimmick to get through doors and doors of misdirection. The pace management feels well designed, giving the GM scripts that can swing from slow burn to quick tempo without forcing a jarring mode shift. If you have run City of Mist before, Fortune Row should slide into your table with minimal friction and maximum dramatic payoff.

### Character options and new archetypes
Fortune Row adds a handful of archetypes crafted to fit the row and the neighborhood. These archetypes come with suggested Gimmicks, a few specialty moves, and a handful of legend prompts that tie directly into the location. The characters are designed to feel distinct from standard options, offering different angles for players who want to explore new mythic lenses while staying connected to the city around them. The Archetypes usually lean into social leverage, street level mysticism, and the kind of moral ambiguity that makes a good noir story more interesting than a straightforward hero tale.

### Investigative hooks and pacing
The pack delivers a set of hooks that can be dropped into a long campaign or used as a stand alone night. The hooks exploit Fortune Row as a locus where fate, politics, and myth intersect. Expect a rhythm where players poke at a mystery, reveal a new layer of myth, and then have to choose how to respond when a fortune teller predicts not just outcomes, but the shape of the next move in the night. Pacing recommendations are practical and gamer friendly, including tips on structuring scenes to maximize suspense and to give the players agency even as the plot tightens.

## Story hooks, play experience, and GM tips
### A toolbox of scenes
Fortune Row offers a toolbox of scenes rather than a fixed script. You will get a handful of well structured beats: a corner shop that is really a portal; a parade of masks that reveal a hidden faction in the city; a midnight auction where fortunes are traded in mythic tokens. The GM guidance helps you thread these beats into a cohesive arc without over directing players. In short, it invites improvisation without leaving you in the dark about where the next clue should come from.

### Tone and player agency
The tone of Fortune Row is noir with a mythic blush. It invites players to interrogate what makes a fortune in a city of myths, and what value they place on power versus responsibility. The best sessions shine when players decide to leverage their legends in unique ways that reveal both the city and their own shades of ambition. The pack respects that dynamic by providing prompts that reward creative problem solving and character driven choices rather than rote dice rolling for the sake of rolling.

### GM preparation tips
- Read the key scenes ahead of time and mark two or three flexible beats where player choice could shift the direction of the story.
- Prepare at least two alternate outcomes for major turns in the plot so you can adapt on the fly if players go off script.
- Think about the factions and how Fortune Row interacts with them; a good GM can turn a social encounter into a tense negotiation with a mythic twist.
- Use the handouts as narrative springboards; don’t over explain, let players fill in the gaps with their own insight into the mythic world.

## Design, layout, and production values
Fortune Row presents a visually clean product with a strong noir palette that fits the City of Mist brand. The layout is functional, with clear headings that help you navigate the acts, locations, and prompts. The typography is legible and the maps are printed clearly enough to be used at the table without constant squinting. The character sheets and archetype cards are sturdy, designed to withstand the occasional tabletop existential crisis that comes with a bad dice night. The overall aesthetic feels cohesive with the City of Mist worldbuilding; you get the same sense that every street corner could hide a mystery that binds the personal to the mythic.

Production quality matters a lot in quick pickup games. Fortune Row recognizes that, delivering readable prompts and accessible content that your players can engage with without needing a field guide. The map is multi scale enough to be used on a standard table, but compact enough to keep the action intimate. The included handouts give players something tangible to hold onto as clues—this is a small but significant win for pacing and immersion.

## Value, price, and replayability
As an adventure pack, Fortune Row sits in a comfortable sweet spot for players who want something that can be dropped into an ongoing campaign or run as a standalone mystery. The perceived value factor hinges on how often you play City of Mist. If you are a once a month group, the four act arc plus the supporting material can spice up several sessions. If your table meets weekly, Fortune Row can be a centerpiece for a mini arc that spans a few weeks. The new archetypes contribute to replayability by offering fresh lenses for your crew and encouraging different approaches to familiar puzzles.

In terms of price, Fortune Row is typically priced to be accessible for most RPG budgets. It does not attempt to overwhelm you with content in a way that feels exploitative; instead it provides crisp content that can be consumed and executed at a variety of pacing levels. In short, if you want a compact, high impact supplement that respects the City of Mist vibe, Fortune Row delivers.

## How Fortune Row fits into a campaign
### One shot to long arc bridge
Fortune Row shines as a bridging creature between long campaigns and new players. It is a nimble arc that can be slotted in during a lull or used to pivot a city wide mystery into a local street story. The content is structured to help you escalate tension while maintaining the possibility of consequences that ripple beyond the initial night. The design here makes Fortune Row a flexible anchor point for your campaign, whether you prefer a dark, city locked mood or a more surreal mythic atmosphere.

### Campaign integration tips
- Treat Fortune Row as a hub for rumors and minor factions that your players can leverage or subvert. This makes the world feel larger than a single mission.
- Use the archetypes to seed party dynamics; even players not built around a strong mythic identity can be drawn into the row through NPC interactions and personal stakes.
- If you run a longer arc, consider planting a repeating motif in Fortune Row that your players can revisit, either to gain advantage or to pay a price for playing with fate.

If you have read other City of Mist supplements, Fortune Row stands out by being compact and highly portable. ## Final verdict
Fortune Row is a strong addition for City of Mist players who want a tight, atmospheric experience with a strong sense of place. It does not attempt to reinvent the wheel, but it adds enough polish to make a memorable arc without requiring a master class in GMing. If your table loves noir vibes, mythic symbolism, and the idea that a street corner can hold a dozen different legends, Fortune Row is worth your time and your table space. It is especially recommended for readers who want a drop in that can be used as a standalone night or as a bridge between larger acts in a campaign.
