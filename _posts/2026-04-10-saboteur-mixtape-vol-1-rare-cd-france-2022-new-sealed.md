## A Quick Guide for New Collectors: How to Approach This Copy

If you are new to rare CD collecting, here is a short guide to making the most of this purchase:

- Inspect the shrink wrap carefully. Look for any tears or reseals, which could indicate the item was opened before, or worse, swapped for a counterfeit. The seal should be intact and free of obvious damage.
- Check the disc’s surface under a bright light. Look for scratches that would render the disc unplayable or at least degrade the audio quality. A pristine disc should have a uniform reflective surface with no deep scratches.
- Examine the booklet and label printing. Subtle printing inconsistencies can be a clue to press quality and regional variations.
- Compare with any available photos or listings of the international edition. Small differences in track order, artwork, or liner notes can reveal the unique character of the French press.
- Consider storage. A sealed copy should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Temperature fluctuations can degrade the plastic over time.
