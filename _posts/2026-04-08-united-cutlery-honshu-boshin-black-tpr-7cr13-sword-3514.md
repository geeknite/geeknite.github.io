### Blade material and finish
The Honshu Boshin 3514 is described as a 7Cr13 stainless steel blade. 7Cr13 is a common stainless used in budget blades; it’s the stainless steel equivalent of a dependable but not heavyweight draft pick. It offers reasonable hardness and edge retention for a decorative or cosplay blade and is relatively easy to maintain. It’s not the kind of steel you’d take into a cutting test with the intent to do repeated heavy cuts, but for display and light props it holds up well.

The grip on this model is a black TPR (thermoplastic rubber) over a core that’s typically metal or a robust plastic. The idea here is simple: comfort and grip during extended posing sessions. If you’ve ever used a foam-handled prop that somehow squeaks when you squeeze, you appreciate the TPR’s grippiness. It’s soft enough to be comfortable, but not so soft that it feels slack in your hand. For cosplay poses and display photos, the grip detail reads well on camera, giving your hand a believable silhouette without requiring you to clinch in a way that would draw questions at a convention.

## Maintenance tips for a budget stainless blade
Even budget stainless blades deserve a little care. Here are practical tips to keep your Honshu Boshin looking presentable and ready for your next cosplay shoot:

- Wipe and dry: After handling, wipe the blade with a dry, lint-free cloth to remove fingerprints and moisture. Stainless steel isn’t magic; it will show smudges if you leave them.
- Gentle cleaners: If you need to remove stubborn smudges, use a mild soap and water solution, then dry thoroughly. Avoid harsh solvents that could discolor the black finish.
- Handle care: Clean the TPR grip with a damp cloth; avoid soaking the handle if you’re unsure about the internal construction. The goal is to keep the grip comfortable, not to turn it into a sponge.
- Display doesn’t equal fortress: If you’re using a wall mount or stand, ensure it’s in a stable location away from kids and pets who might get adventurous with dramatic sword poses.
