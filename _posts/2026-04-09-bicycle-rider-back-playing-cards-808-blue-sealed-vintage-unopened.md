## Historical context and the 808 code
The Rider Back deck is iconic in the world of card games. The 808 code is a printing or catalog variant used by Bicycle to distinguish different runs of Rider Back decks. The colorways have varied over the years, with blue offering a slightly more modern feel than the original crimson or royal blue variants. ### Care and storage
Storage is the boring but essential part. Store the deck in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight to preserve the back color. Avoid humidity that could lead to warping or curl in the cards, and avoid extreme temperature changes that could accelerate edge wear. For collectors, the best option is to maintain the original seal and keep it in an archival box or a display case that protects it from dust and sunlight.

## Practical buying thoughts and how to validate mint condition
When evaluating a sealed vintage deck, you should inspect the shrink wrap for any signs of tampering or damage. Small creases or pinholes can be harmless if the seal remains intact, but they could indicate the deck has been exposed to humidity or mechanical stress. If you are shopping online, examine photos of the seal, the tuck, and the back of the box. If the seller can provide a clear shot of the bottom of the tuck, you can see if the packaging is genuine or a later reprint with minor changes.

If you want to feel secure about your purchase, you can choose to buy from reputable sellers who specialize in vintage Bicycle decks. They often provide grade descriptions and a history of the deck's production. In some cases, the seller may have additional images that show the exact wear on the seal. It is also worth negotiating a return if the deck arrives not as pictured.
