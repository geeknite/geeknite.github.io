## Overview

City of Mist has always thrived on the premise that myths live in the margins of everyday life. Fortune Row continues that tradition by zooming the camera from a citys soul to a single, humming district where deals, whispers, and fortunes intersect. If the core book gave you a map of the citys soul, Fortune Row gives you the blocks and corners where that soul haggles with fate.

This is a rulebook that wears its ambition lightly, like a trench coat that conceals a surprising number of pockets. It does not rewrite the base system so much as it re-weights it to emphasize social play, investigation pacing, and the moral gray zone at the heart of many street level myths.

### The vibe and design

Fortune Row leans noir, with a dash of supernatural carnival. The art direction adds warm amber tones and a lot of cigarette smoke in the margins. The writing is confident and witty, with enough in-jokes to please long time readers without alienating newcomers. The layout is dense but navigable, and there are careful notations for new players who do not want to slog through pages of the original core rulebook to find the exact rule about what happens when a suspect tries to use an ability to bluff the GM.

Now the meat: the mechanics and how they shift the game pace.

The Fortune Row principle is that fortunes are social capital as much as dice outcomes. Rather than purely luck, the book design emphasizes relationships, leverage, and the cost of success. The mechanics support scenes that tilt toward negotiation, shadowy deals, and the moment when a character realizes that their own fortune has a price tag.

## Mechanics and design choices

### Core systems updated

Fortune Row expands on the Mythos and Logos concept from the base game. It introduces a district scale where players track influence, contracts, and rivalries between factions. The rules use a blend of narrative dice and skill checks, allowing players to push for a dramatic result with consequences that ripple for sessions.

- Fortune tracks: A new resource that tracks how many times a character can wield a favorable outcome before consequences kick in.
- Row tokens: Represent the social capital that players invest in a scene. They can be spent to gain assistance, to force a reveal, or to bend a fact about a location.
- Investigation pacing: The book provides a pacing guide that helps the GM escalate tension by introducing red herrings and decoys that are thematically consistent with Fortune Row.

### NPCs and organizations

The book presents a cast of characters that feel like old friends and old enemies at the same time. Each NPC has a clear motive, a flaw that can be exploited by clever players, and a unique hook that ties them to a location in Fortune Row. The organizations are not mere backdrops; they are dynamic engines for plot, capable of shifting alliances as the city warps around each new chapter.

### Locations and investigations

Fortune Row includes location templates and encounter hooks that GM can drop into a session with minimal prep. The location cards that appear in the book bring a tactile element to table talk and help players visualize where the action is taking place. Many of these locations are designed to encourage social play rather than pure combat, aligning with the book's emphasis on social leverage and narrative risk.

### Storytelling tools

A strong portion of the book is dedicated to tools for running scenarios. There are sample investigations that unfold like episodes of a season, complete with cliffhangers, pivot moments, and redemptive arcs. The GM tools section also includes:

- A rumor deck that can be shuffled into the session to create quick, flavorful plot twists
- A set of GM prompts, designed to push players toward moral choices rather than brute force
- Guidelines for running multi-session arcs that converge on a central mystery

### Interfacing with the base game

Fortune Row is designed to slot into existing City of Mist campaigns. It does not require you to abandon your current power tags or mythic identities, but it does encourage you to think of fortune as a mutable resource. If you already run long campaigns, you can thread Fortune Row into your ongoing narrative by gradually introducing district-level conflicts while preserving the players tempo.

In practice, this means players are more likely to negotiate, barter, and engage in social manipulation as part of the core action. The dice still matter, but the emphasis is on narrative consequences.

## The GM experience

Being a GM for Fortune Row is a little like steering a ship through a fog bank while a neon sign blinks your name in Morse code. You know where you want to go, but your crew keeps asking where the treasure is hidden and the treasure keeps shifting its location.

Fortune Row offers:

- Clear guidelines for session pacing and scene structure
- A flexible approach to introducing new information so players can build a coherent theory
- Concrete tools for handling social conflicts and deals
- A sample arc that can be run in 4-6 sessions or adapted to a longer campaign

If you enjoy the feel of a noir show with mythic overtones, Fortune Row will satisfy your appetite for layered storytelling. If you prefer more mechanical crunch or a chessboard approach to encounters, you will need to lean into the GM notes and be ready to improvise.

## Artwork and layout

The art direction continues the warm palette seen in the core book, with a slightly dustier vibe that fits the city at night. The layout is clean but dense, with callouts that highlight important rules and a generous number of sidebars that offer quick tips for GMs. If anything stands out visually, it is the careful use of typographic hierarchy to guide the eye toward narrative beats (which is exactly how a GM should want it on a long session night).

## Reading experience and accessibility

For players new to City of Mist, Fortune Row can be a lot to absorb on first skim. The core mechanics are not replaced, but you will encounter a different rhythm of play. The recommended approach is to read the chapter on districts first, then work your way through the investigation templates. The book includes glossaries that help new players translate mythic concepts into game terms, which is a big win.

For veteran players, Fortune Row offers enough new toys to justify a second read. The structure invites you to plan arcs with a sense of momentum rather than a series of isolated scenes.

## Session ideas and example arcs

- A deal gone wrong: The investigators stumble into a network of street-level financiers who want to turn a fortune into control of a block. The players must decide whether to break the cycle or become part of it.

- The rumor that refuses to die: An ancient myth reappears in a new form as a rumor that circles Fortune Row. It becomes a test for how much power the players are willing to gamble to win a larger prize.

- The broken clock: Time is a currency in Fortune Row. A clock tower is stuck at a particular hour, and every hour corresponds to a different reveal about a location or NPC.

- Night market heist: In a district with a vibrant black market, one of the deals that leaves Fortune Row becomes a key to unlocking a larger mystery. The players must decide if the price is worth paying.

## NPCs and faction dynamics

Fortune Row increases the density of neighborhood power players. You will find NPCs who operate as brokers, dreamers, and enforcers who all exist within a handful of city blocks. Each NPC is built with a motive that makes sense within a single arc but also feeds into the bigger mystery that runs through Fortune Row. The relationships between these NPCs form the engine of your table, and it is not unusual to see alliances shift mid arc as new information breaks a tentative equilibrium.

## Thematic and narrative notes

Fortune Row emphasizes the moral tension of making a fortune in a world that is not sure you deserve it. This means the book occasionally pushes players into ethically gray decisions. The text treats this with nuance, avoiding heavy handed lectures and instead presenting outcomes that feel earned rather than forced. If your table enjoys noir mood pieces with mythic overtones, you will find Fortune Row to be a strong match. If your table prefers bright moral simplicity, you may want to modulate the tone with a few GM cues.

## Handling myths and truth

Fortune Row makes myths feel accessible by giving players a reason to pursue a rumor and a means to test a truth. The book highlights the tension between belief and pragmatism, showing how a single rumor can shift a city block away from harmony toward dissonance. This is a core theme of the City of Mist system, and Fortune Row leans into it with a careful hand.

## Price and packaging

Fortune Row is priced to be approachable for campaigns that want more than a one-shot but not an entire anthology of adventures. The packaging is sturdy, with a sturdy GM screen and a collector friendly layout. If you are a completionist, you may want to grab the core rules plus this district focus to get the most out of your table for the longest possible run.

## Final thoughts and recommendation

In summary, City of Mist: Fortune Row pivots from broad city-wide mythic intrigue to a district-centered, character-driven experience that still preserves the core mechanics of the base game. The writing is strong, the layout is practical, and the included tools mean GMs can run multi-session arcs with ease. The new mechanics feel like a natural evolution rather than a jarring change, and the emphasis on social leverage makes investigations and deals more flavorful than the usual dice sprint.

If your group enjoys noir vibes, urban mysticism, and rich cast interactions, Fortune Row is likely to be the best value on the shelf. If you prefer more crunchy combat systems, you might find Fortune Row a touch heavy on the narrative side, but the dice are still there when you need them.

- Pros: strong thematic cohesion, excellent GM tools, effective pacing guidance, high replay value
- Cons: dense learning curve for new players, a few sections feel like they crave a longer integration arc
- Prospective players: check out the sample arc and the rumor deck first to get a sense of the rhythm
