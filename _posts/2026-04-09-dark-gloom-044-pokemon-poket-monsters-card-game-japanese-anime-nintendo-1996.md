## Gameplay and Mechanics
The Dark Gloom N°044 card operates within the core mechanics of the original Pokemon TCG: energy acceleration, attack damage, HP thresholds, and strategic retreat. While modern decks often juggle synergy across dozens of cards, the 1996 era encouraged brass tacks: a handful of strong basics, some niche supporters, and a handful of energy cards to power up an efficient sequence of attacks.

### Core Rules (as they would have felt in 1996)
- Energy is your primary resource. You attach one energy card per turn per attack cost, and you must manage energy distribution across your bench and active Pokemon. The Dark variant intensifies this by encouraging riskier plays that trade early damage for later payoff.
- Attacks and HP decide who wins. Dark Gloom N°044 likely features a moderate HP pool with an attack that carries a status or energy drain effect. This creates a tug of war where the board state shifts slowly as both players jockey for position.
- Retreat and bench management. In this era, moving your active Pokemon to the bench before an opponent lands a heavy hit was an art form. The Dark Gloom card often features a higher retreat energy cost, nudging players toward riskier plays and more dramatic swings.
- Status effects and type matchups. A card with the gloom attribute tends to lean into poison and fatigue vibes, which in the deck-building meta adds a psychological layer: your opponent reads your mood as much as your hand.

### Deck Building Philosophy
If you wanted to run a Dark Gloom N°044 themed deck in 1996, you would likely aim for a compact, consistent early game that could weather the occasional power spike from more famous staples.
