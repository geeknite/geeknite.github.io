## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Bite Without the Scrub

### Cleaning Wool Rugs: A Practical Guide

Wool rugs are surprisingly forgiving, but they do have preferences. Here’s a concise care routine that will keep your Cat Eyes rug looking sharp for years:
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle suction setting. Avoid a high-roller attachment that could flip the fibers like a dragon breathing fire on dust bunnies.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean, white cloth. Don’t rub; that’s how you make a spill a feature instead of a setback.
- For stubborn stains, use a mild wool-safe cleaning solution or consult a professional rug cleaner. Test any product in a small, inconspicuous spot first to ensure color stability.
- Rotate the rug every few months to promote even wear and to keep the cat from getting nose-tickled by the same fiber forever.

### Longevity Tips: Protect Your Investment

- Use a rug pad to prevent slipping and to add a layer of cushion. It also helps reduce wear on the backing and extends the life of the rug.
- Keep direct sunlight to a minimum if you’re particularly attached to the rug’s original color. UV exposure is the silent color thief in many living rooms.
- Trim loose fibers promptly to prevent tangling and further fraying. Wool can tolerate some upkeep; it’s not as delicate as your grandma’s heirloom lace, but respect it nonetheless.
