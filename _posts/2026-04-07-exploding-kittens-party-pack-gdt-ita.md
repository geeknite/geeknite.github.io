## How to Play

### Setup
The setup is intentionally simple so you can jump into the chaos without a lengthy preface. Shuffle the deck and deal 1 card to each player, then draw until you have 4 cards in hand. The remaining deck forms the draw pile. The Exploding Kitten cards are shuffled into the deck according to the rules, and the youngest player starts. The goal is straightforward: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten, or defuse that kitten when you have the right card. The box promises that you will learn the rules in under five minutes, and if you do not, the table will remind you with laughter and friendly heckling.

### Turn structure
On your turn you can play one or more cards from your hand to attack opponents, skip turns, or manipulate the deck. A big part of the fun is using Attack, See the Future, and Defuse cards to outplay your friends. The general rhythm is fast: you play a card, someone fails to defend, and the next player darts a look that says, I know you want to bluff, but I am watching. The game thrives on social dynamics as much as on card interactions, so your personality will matter as much as your option economy.

### Endgame and Defuse mechanics
Defuse cards are rare lifesavers that can prevent a sudden pop from the kitty. They are not unlimited, which means you often have to choose between keeping the Defuse for yourself or using it to preserve your social standing by not exploding on camera. The endgame accelerates as players get squeezed by the draw pile and start to pile on counterplays. The last few rounds tend to become a frantic montage of wild accusations and dramatic bluffing, punctuated by a chorus of gasps when someone actually survives with a sliver of health and a single Defuse card hidden like a secret weapon.
