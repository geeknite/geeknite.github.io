## What is in Hero Box 1

The contents of Hero Box 1 vary by print run, but the core idea remains the same: a curated set of four or five heroic miniatures plus associated cards and tokens that unlock new tactical options. In this iteration, you get a selected roster that feels like a cross between knights of old and survivors from a post apocalyptic fantasy tavern. Expect a mix of melee specialists, a ranged combatant who has actually read a book about street fighting, and a support character who can flip a momentary advantage into a lasting smile for the players who lost their last turn to zombie shenanigans.

Inside the tray, you will find the hero miniatures themselves. The sculpts carry the hallmarks of Black Plague: dynamic poses, dramatic cloaks, and armor that looks both practical and ready for a well-timed dramatic fall. The sculpts are clean enough to require minimal flash cleanup, which is always a blessing when time is measured in minutes instead of hours. The bases come prepped for quick painting and have enough texture to catch washes, dry pigments, and the occasional heroic sneeze from the table.

Alongside the minis are the hero boards, each with a unique stat block that will interact with your campaign. The stat blocks are the nerve center for the characters, translating their fantasy chops into game mechanics. You will also find the action dice, ability cards, and the usual assortment of tokens used to track fatigue, injuries, and the occasional moment of heroic last stand when you realize you forgot to place a trap and the zombies have staged a musical number on your flank.

For the sake of nostalgia and because this is a hero box, the cards come with art that screams high fantasy and low tolerance for mortal peril. The card backs are uniform, making it easy to tell allies from undead at a glance. The mini cards contain the usual text heavy for a game that is about to become a small pile of plastic on the table. The quality here is consistent with CMON standards: sturdy stock, legible icons, and enough color to keep your eyes from glazing over in the middle of a brutal combat sequence.

If you are curious about the packaging, the box includes a couple of spacer elements to keep the minis from colliding in transit. The foam trays are not always the easiest to repackage after you have opened the box a dozen times, so if you plan to resell or loan out the set, you might want to consider a small storage solution to keep the heroes pristine and the zombies from using your storage as their personal luggage rack.


### The minis in detail

The hero minifigs in this box are well-proportioned with good legible sculpts. The hands grip weapons in ways that suggest practical combat rather than theater cosplay. If you enjoy painting, the faces have enough detail to show expressions without requiring a masterclass in skin techniques. If you prefer to play without painting, you will still have a strong sense of individuality among the survivors thanks to iconic silhouettes and varied gear.

One standout is the mage type whose robe design communicates arcane power while the face is crafted with a hint of mischief. This is not an overbearing wizard that makes you groan every time you hear a spell name; this is the practical mage who shows up with a stable glow of magic and a few witty lines when things get dark. The melee specialists bring a mix of knightly pluck and dirt-under-the-nails resilience, while the ranged character provides reliable coverage and a surprisingly strong sense of character when you realize they have to reload after every shot.

Represents of the undead are not included in Hero Box 1, but the upgrade in your campaign comes from the synergy between these new heroes and the existing zombie gangs. You will find that some combos feel overpowered on occasion, while others require the squad to work in tandem with limited resources. The joy of Zombicide is never fully balanced, and that is where the real thrill hides. If you crave a perfectly tuned solo game, this box might not be your speed; if you want chaotic cooperative play that still feels themed and cinematic, you are in for a treat.


## The cards, tokens, and overall usability

Beyond the minis, there are the hero cards and ability tokens that define how these legends function in play. The card art is consistent with the base game look, with clear icons that you can read without needing a magnifying glass. The ability text is compact but not cryptic; you will probably need to read it aloud once to your table for the full effect and then pretend you remember the exact rule when the zombies start teleporting around the board. This is a common experience with Zombicide expansions, and Hero Box 1 keeps the learning curve friendly enough for new players while offering enough depth for veterans to squeeze a few extra turns of glory.

The tokens are straightforward to manage. Fatigue and injury markers sit on the board in a way that is obvious and satisfying. The campaign flow remains intuitive, with the usual rhythm of plan, execute, and improvise when your plans go sideways and a spear-wielding necromancer decides to crash the party. If you have played any Zombicide before, you will feel right at home. If you are brand new, you might spend a few minutes explaining why your hero can turn a shield bash into a narrative moment that your friends will still talk about during lunch the next day.

Unboxing is also a quick reminder that you will want a proper space to spread out. The heroes deserve breathing room on the tabletop. Later on, you will be grateful that you did not try to cram everything into a single coffee table and call it a day.


### Painting and personalization tips

If you intend to paint these heroes, you are in a good spot. The minis offer clean surfaces and larger sculpted areas that respond well to common hobby techniques. A basic three-step approach works nicely: base coat, shade/wash, highlight. Start with a reliable brown or gray for armor, apply a wash to bring out the crevices, and finish with a few sharp highlights to bring back edges that the wash might have dulled. You do not need to go overboard to achieve a striking result; sometimes the simplest approach yields the most character on the tabletop.

If you want to push a little further, consider a themed palette for your party. A red coated knight, a blue robe mage, and a green archer—these color choices will help you instantly recognize each hero during dynamic combat sequences and preserve your sanity during late-night sessions when your eyes refuse to focus but your enthusiasm refuses to quit.


## Theme, balance, and narrative flavor

Zombicide Black Plague leans into high fantasy while retaining the core zombie survival premise. Hero Box 1 contributes new voices to the choir of survivors. The narrative flavor comes from the interplay between the heroes, the evolving threat of the undead, and the environment of the campaign you choose to run. The presence of fantasy elements means you will encounter magical traps, cursed weapons, and encounters that feel like a crossover issue between a classic fantasy novel and a zombie survival guide. This is where Zombicide shines—you can be heroic and ridiculous at the same time, and the game will reward you for recognizing the absurd poetry of a knight charging a zombie behemoth while shouting a battle-cry that sounds suspiciously like a recipe for success.

From a gameplay perspective, Hero Box 1 provides new options for party composition. You will likely experiment with different class synergies, trying to find the right balance between tanky protection, ranged suppression, and magic support. Some missions may require you to pivot your strategy as the board throws new challenges in your path. The joy of the expansion is that it rewards experimentation and chaotic cooperation rather than relying on a single well-known cheese combo. If you like table talk, you will get it in spades with these heroes cheering each other on while a zombie horde bears down and threatens to steal the last cookie from your victory cake.
