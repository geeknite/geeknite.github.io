## Care, Preservation, and Display of NOS Cards

NOS is a promise that the item inside is untouched by the world’s rough fingers. But untouched doesn’t mean invincible. Here are practical tips for keeping your 1985 UNO deck in pristine or near-pristine condition:

- Store in a cool, dry place away from sunlight. The bold 1980s yellow tones can fade with UV exposure, turning once-sun-kissed colors into a washed-out whisper of the original design.
- Use card sleeves if you intend to shuffle and play. Even if you preserve the deck, sleeves reduce the wear and tear from repeated shuffles and protect the box from losing its label integrity.
- Maintain a stable humidity level. Card stock isn’t a fan of moisture or extreme dryness; both can lead to warping and surface cracking. A simple cabinet with a humidity indicator is more than a luxury—it's a responsibility.
- If displaying, consider a glass case with a tiny LED light. The display will look crisp in photos and might even earn you the title of “retro curator” among your friends.
- Document your journey. A small notebook or a digital log of the deck’s condition at purchase, any restoration steps you attempted (if any), and a few anecdotal play sessions makes the deck more valuable as a memory artifact than merely a collectible.
