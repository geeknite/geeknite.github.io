## Core mechanics and changes in this edition
Zombicide is typically a cooperative game where players control survivors, slay or bypass zombies, and attempt to complete objectives. The Special Guest edition does not reinvent the wheel; rather, it tunes the wheel, adjusts the tires, and adds a few new gears.

- Movement and actions: Each survivor can move, attack, search, or interact with the environment. The new edition adds a twist by adding a guest card that introduces a temporary mechanic that can swing the tide if understood.
- Zombie spawn mechanics: Spawns scale in a way that rewards coordinated plays rather than lone hero stunts.
- Objectives and replayability: A mix of objectives encourages players to switch strategies across missions.

## Strategy toolkit: practical tips for the Special Guest edition
Here we compile a toolbox of tactics that survive the chaos while giving Edouard a whistle to signal when you are about to blunder into a pit of zombies.

### Survivor selection and role synergy
- Favor survivors with synergy potential across multiple tasks
- Consider a mix of combat specialists and scavengers to balance firepower and resource management
- Avoid one trick ponies who can do well in one scenario but crack under pressure in another
- When your group consists of mixed experience, assign a table lead who can coordinate the team

### Resource management and environment control
- Keep a tight leash on ammo and healing items; scarcity increases tension in a good way
- Use the environment to funnel zombies toward bottlenecks where you have the advantage
- Don’t forget that searches can reveal useful tools beyond gunplay

### Combat and danger management
- Attack in coordination to maximize damage and minimize risk
- Use terrain to avoid brave but foolhardy charges
- In closed spaces, the right weapon and the right timing can save your skin

### Endgame planning
- Don’t overcommit in the mid game; save some steam for the final push
- If your team is behind, adjust objectives to buy one more round of kills and actions
