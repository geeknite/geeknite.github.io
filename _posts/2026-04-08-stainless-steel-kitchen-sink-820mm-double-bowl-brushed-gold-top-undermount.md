## Installation and practical tips
Undermount installations depend on a solid substrate. If your countertop is a wafer-thin pancake of laminate, you will want to be sure to coordinate with a pro to avoid a sagging sink and an existential crisis about your countertop’s self-esteem. You will need a rigid lip to seal against moisture, and silastic sealant to create the water-tight boundary that stops water from playing hide-and-seek behind the cabinets. A good installer will verify the cutout size and ensure the ripple effect on the underside of the countertop matches the sink’s weight and your cooking cadence.

If you are a DIY enthusiast, you can attempt a basic undermount with a masonry countertop if you are comfortable with cutting precision and using clamps for alignment. Expect a longer install time but a potentially satisfying finish. For most people, a professional install is worth it for the flush look and the peace of mind that the water will stay put where it belongs.

## Maintenance and longevity: keeping it sharp
Stainless sinks age gracefully when treated with a light touch. Here are practical tips to keep your 820 mm double bowl in fighting shape:
- Dry after use to prevent water spots and mineral buildup on the brushed finish.
- Use non-abrasive cleaners and a soft cloth for regular cleaning. Skip steel wool or harsh scouring pads that could scratch surfaces.
- For stubborn stains, a paste of baking soda and water works wonders without aggressive scrubbing.
- Periodically check the seal around the rim and reapply silicone if you notice gaps. A small investment of time now saves you from bigger leaks later.
- Avoid chlorine-based cleaners around the gold trim as they can dull or discolor finishes over time.

With proper care, your sink will continue to perform while still looking presentable in your evolving kitchen aesthetic. It is not a cosmetic prop; it is a functional ally that can survive a week of heavy use if you treat it with a bit of respect and a lot of elbow grease when needed.
