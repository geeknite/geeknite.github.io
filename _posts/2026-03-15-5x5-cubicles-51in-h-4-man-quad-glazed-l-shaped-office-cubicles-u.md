Glazed, as the name suggests, implies a glass component. ## Assembly and Maintenance: The Art of Surviving a Flat-Packed Odyssey

### Unboxing and Planning
First, count your Allen wrenches. Then map the quad’s layout on the floor to ensure all connections align with your planned space. The 5x5 Quad will typically come in modular sections that snap together with panel connectors and screws. Preparation is key: lay down mats to protect floors, organize hardware by type, and recruit a helper who doesn’t mind wearing a tool belt borrowed from a DIY TV show.

### Step-by-Step Assembly: A Rough Sketch
1) Lay out the base frames and mark anchor points. 2) Attach the side panels and create the quad’s outer boundary. 3) Install the core partitions and ensure alignment across all four workstations. 4) Mount the top panels and secure the glass or glazed elements. 5) Route cables through grommets and install any power modules. 6) Tighten all bolts, re-check for wobble, and perform a final wipe-down with a microfiber cloth. 7) Place chairs, monitors, and personal touches. 8) Celebrate with coffee and a triumphant chair spin. You’ve earned it.

### Time and Labor Considerations
If you’re a small team, you’ll want at least two capable adults and a weekend with decent weather for moving parts and not losing your mind. In larger offices with a dedicated facilities team, the install can be completed in a day, sometimes two, depending on ceiling height and the number of cable runs. Pro tip: coordinate with IT early so you don’t discover you’ve blocked a server rack with a glorified glass dashboard.

### Maintenance: Clean, Don’t Be Mean
Glazed panels love a good wipe. Use a microfiber cloth with a mild glass cleaner to avoid streaks that make video calls more entertaining than your actual proposal. The laminate surfaces should be cleaned with a damp cloth and a mild soap; avoid harsh abrasives that could mar the finish. Regular inspections of the frame and panels will catch loose screws or panel misalignment before they escalate into a “where did that part go?” moment.
