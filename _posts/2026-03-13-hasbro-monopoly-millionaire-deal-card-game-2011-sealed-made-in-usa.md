A Quick How-To: Play in Five Minutes (Or Close Enough)
------------------------------------------------------
- Objective: Build a portfolio by collecting full property sets and leveraging cash through smart deals. The first player to achieve a set of properties with a certain value or to meet the game’s win condition wins the game.
- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a starting hand to each player, place money cards in a personal bank, and prepare the draw pile.
- Turn flow:
  1) Draw a card.
  2) Play money to your bank or lay down property cards to your collection.
  3) Propose or accept deals with other players using your hand’s cards, including action or “deal” cards that can alter who owns what.
  4) End your turn by discarding down to a hand size limit if needed.
- Endgame: When a player completes a winning condition (as defined by the specific edition rules), the game ends and the winner celebrates their fictional millionaire status with the gusto of someone who just found a spare pile of monopoly money under the couch.

Collector’s Notes: Grading Condition, Authenticity, and Care
----------------------------------------------------------
- Box integrity: Check for spine wear, corner dents, and any tape marks if the box has seen sunlight since 2011. A pristine box often correlates with a pristine deck inside.
- Card wear: Look for glossy finish consistency, no creases, and no card-face fading. Even a sealed item can harbor very slight warping from humidity changes over a decade.
- Storage advice: Keep it in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. If you’re serious about preservation, consider a display case with UV-protective glass and a cushioning support to keep the deck from sliding around inside the box.
- Desirability: For collectors, the combination of game design, era, and packaging matters. A purely nostalgic sealed copy may fetch modest premiums, while a minty-box item with crisp cards can command the higher end of the market spectrum.
