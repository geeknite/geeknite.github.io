In Dragon Ball games, the Leader is the heart and soul of your deck. It defines your color alignment, power threshold, and, often, a few utility options that help you reach your big turns. ## How to play this starter: a practical map for your first dozen games

If you’re brand new to Dragon Ball Super Card Game or you’re brand-new to structured deck-building, here’s a concise play path you can follow to get you from zero to confident in about a dozen sessions. You’ll hear the word energy a lot—the game’s core mechanic is managing your energy pool to play cards on future turns. The aim is to land a sequence where you generate momentum on turns 2 through 4 and deliver a decisive attack on turns 5 through 6.

- Early turns: establish your field with your Leader’s plan and a couple of early drops. Use energy wisely; don’t burn everything on a suboptimal line.
- Mid-game: start pressuring your opponent with a controlled barrage. This is where the deck’s synergy comes alive. If you see a window for a two-card combination, go for it; you want to claim tempo without leaving your life total dangerously exposed.
- Late game: you’ll have a couple of reliable finishers in your hand. Plan your last few plays so you can land a strong, clean attack while keeping enough defense to survive the opponent’s last push.

Key tips for beginners:

- Respect the energy curve: your turns depend on it. If you slam your entire energy pool into a big play too early, you’ll be left staring at a blank board with nothing to do on your next turn.
- Read the board state: your opponent’s line of play will reveal their plan. If you can predict their big swing, you can set up a counter-move that buys you a turn or two of breathing room.
- Don’t ignore promos: starter-exclusive cards can be surprisingly strong when used correctly. They’re a nice surprise that often pays off in the midgame.

## How to upgrade your Cross Worlds starter deck without breaking the bank

The starter deck is designed as a launch pad, not a ceiling. If you’re planning to upgrade to a more competitive build, consider the following paths:

- Double down on the Leader’s core theme with more copies of your strongest support cards. This helps you maintain consistency when the deck hits its optimal turn windows.
- Add energy acceleration options to reach your finishers faster. The faster you can cast your power plays, the less your opponent can respond with a timely knockout.
- Introduce targeted removal or counter cards to handle problematic threats. Against more aggressive meta decks, you’ll want some reliable answers to big early boards.
- Expand your toolbox with complementary archetypes that share your Leader’s core synergy. Cross Worlds is designed to be a flexible platform for experimenting with hybrid lines of play.

Budget-minded players can often obtain these upgrades by picking up a few booster sets or bundles rather than diving into a full-blown second box. It’s all about scaling gradually and seeing how your local meta evolves.
