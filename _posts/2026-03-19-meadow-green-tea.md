## Brewing Guide

### Water temperature and time

For the first brew, use water around 75 to 85 C. If you go too hot, you risk bitterness; too cool, you lose aroma. A good starting point is 80 C. Steep for 2 to 3 minutes. If you prefer a brighter cup, 2 minutes; for a slightly richer cup, 3 minutes.

### Tea-to-water ratio

A typical ratio is 1 teaspoon per 8 ounces of water. For stronger tea, go to 1.5 teaspoons; for lighter tea, 0.75 teaspoons.

### Brewing method options

- Traditional infusion: single steep.
- Quick brew for the busy coder: 1 short steep, pour over ice, top with water to lighten the brew.
- Gongfu style: multiple shorter steepings to unlock layers.

### Equipment notes

A standard teapot works fine, but a gaiwan can reveal more aroma if you enjoy leaf ballet.

## Practical Tips and Common Questions

### Is Meadow Green Tea caffeinated?

Yes green tea contains caffeine, but the amount varies. A typical cup can provide a gentle lift without the jittery aftertaste of coffee. If you are sensitive to caffeine, experiment with shorter steep times.

### How should I store Meadow Green Tea?

Store in a cool dark place in an airtight container. Keep away from sunlight and strong aromas that might drown out meadow brightness.

### Can I reuse the leaves for a second infusion?

Often you can, depending on the leaves. The second infusion is usually lighter but still pleasant.
