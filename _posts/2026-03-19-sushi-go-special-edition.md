## What Sushi Go Special Edition is all about

Sushi Go Special Edition remains a quick drafting game for 2 to 5 players. Each round, players draft one card from a hand of three and pass the rest to the next player, repeat, and repeat again. After several rounds, you tally the points from the various sushi types: maki, nigiri, tempura, sashimi, puddings, and the occasional bonus card that comes with the Special Edition. The new cards and variants lean into a bit more aggression in drafting and a dash more volatility in scoring, which translates to more tense reveals and, frankly, more excuses to cheer when someone fumbles a plan you could see coming from across the table.

## How to play in 60 seconds or less

1) Each player starts with a small display of cards in hand. 2) Pick one card to keep and pass the rest to the neighbor. 3) Repeat until the deck is exhausted. 4) Reveal and tally scores for the round. 5) Repeat for a few rounds until the end of the game. 6) The player with the most points wins.

For those who crave deeper meta, the Special Edition leaves room to experiment with nuanced drafting. You can pursue a tight, minimal approach, chasing a handful of high-value combos each round, or go wide with a broader, higher-variance strategy that risks more for spectacular payoffs when the stars align. The key is to keep the table light: a little bragging, a few jokes, and no scowling over a mis-timed pick.

## Strategy and tips

- Read the table, not just your hand. Observing what neighbors are collecting helps decide if a card is worth the risk or if you should pass it on and let someone else chase a different path.
- Early rounds set the mood; late rounds decide the destiny of the table. Don’t oversell a lead in the middle rounds; you want to preserve flexibility for the final tally and allow for a couple of sneaky, late-game flips.
- Balance your meal. It’s tempting to chase a single high-value card, but the real points usually come from a blend of dish families and +/– bonuses that combo well together.
- Use the new variants with discipline. A well-timed scoring card can swing the endgame, but wasted timing can hand the table a win on a silver platter. Patience, not panic, is your friend.
- Don’t forget pudding. In many Sushi Go lines, pudding can be the tiebreaker if the final counts are close. If you’re within a handful of points, a pudding pack can tip the scales in your favor.
