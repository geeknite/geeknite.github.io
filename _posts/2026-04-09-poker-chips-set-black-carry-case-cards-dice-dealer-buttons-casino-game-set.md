A good set in a black carry case should give you a nice balance of heft and durability, without turning your table into a quarry for micromachines.

- Material: The typical chips are made of clay composite or a polymer blend that’s designed to mimic clay. The best sets use a high-quality core with a banded outer ring so they don’t wear down after a few dozen boisterous tomfooleries on live streams.

## Maintenance and Longevity: Keeping Your Set in Top Form
Like all gear, proper care extends the life and preserves the bragging rights. Here are quick maintenance tips:

- Cleaning: Wipe chips with a microfiber cloth if they pick up fingerprints or small smudges. If you’re really fancy, a light wipe with a damp cloth and a quick dry is fine, but avoid soaking. You don’t want moisture warping the case foam or warping your dramatic poker face.
- Storage: Store the case in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading. If you live in a climate with high humidity, consider silicone desiccants in the storage area to reduce moisture-related issues.
- Handling: Don’t slam the case shut. The foam is there to protect, not to mediate your existential crisis about the next big bluff. Gentle handling extends the life of the case and keeps chips and cards in pristine condition for longer.
- Inspections: Periodically check the foam fit. If it loosens, reseat the chips and reinsert the dividers. If you notice chips rattling around when you move the case, it’s time to tighten the organizers or replace the foam insert.
