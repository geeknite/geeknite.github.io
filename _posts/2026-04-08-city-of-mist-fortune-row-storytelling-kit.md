## What Fortune Row Actually Is
Fortune Row is a themed storytelling kit designed as a companion to City of Mist, a game known for blending urban myth with modern crime drama (and the occasional talking car statue). The kit provides curated prompts, micro-adventures, and a mechanism to inject “fortune moments” into sessions. In practice, it means you can generate a street-level scene in seconds, sprinkle in a few mythic twists, and watch your players try to explain how a dragon-afflicted croissant fits into a stakeout at a pawnshop.

Fortune Row’s core promise is to reduce prep time without turning your game into a blank page. The kit includes a stack of prompts, ready-to-run mini-scenarios, and a handful of Fate-like cards that nudge players toward story beats rather than straight combat. It’s a nice bridge between the improvisational vibe of a pick-up game and the narrative scaffolding that a GM sometimes craves. It’s also unapologetically assume-the-role of “weird heroes in a city that loves conspiracies.” If you’re into the mood of City of Mist—neon-drenched alleys, mythic archetypes, and a sense that everyone has something to hide—Fortune Row is likely to feel like a familiar, albeit shinier, coat that you can borrow for a night.

## How It Integrates with City of Mist Rules
City of Mist, at its heart, is a game about identities and how legends shape the real world. Fortune Row doesn’t rewrite the rulebook; it gives you a toolkit to accelerate the storytelling process within that rule set. The kit’s prompts map neatly onto the game’s core concepts: PCs (Powerful Individuals) with mythic ideals, factions that contest for control of narrative space, and a city that is as much a character as any superhero.

Fortune Row tends to emphasize three operational lanes:
- Narrative Catalysts: prompts that spark a scene, reveal a new secret, or push a PC toward a moral or mythic choice. These are your “investigation meets myth” moments.
- Scene Pacing: micro-adventures are designed to slot into an evening with a few acts, allowing for twists without forcing a long campaign arc.
- Fortune Mechanics: cards or prompts that push players to consider what their legends demand (the costs and boons that come with power) and how those demands ripple through the city’s social fabric.

If you’re worried about dissonance between the kit and the core rules, fear not: the kit is designed to slot into established CoM games, not to replace them. You still roll, you still consult the Identity and the Mythos, but you have ready-made engines for turning a cold stakeout into a mythic revelation about who the city thinks they are—and who the city thinks they will become.

## Playstyle Options: Short Sessions, Long Shadows, and Everything In Between
Fortune Row excels at three playstyles:
1) One-Shot Spark: a single session built around a micro-adventure from the kit. This is where Fortune Row shines brightest, letting you run a complete arc with a couple of hours’ prep.
2) Interlude Booster: a mid-campaign spark that slots between larger City of Mist arcs, injecting a fresh legend or a new fortune moment into ongoing character arcs.
3) Inspiration Engine: even when you’re not running a formal game, the prompts work as a “writer’s room” for players who want to jam a personal mythic moment into a scene or a story pitch.

In practice, your table might do a 2–3 hour session using two micro-adventures from the kit, stitching them with a connective bridge act that the GM improvises. The prompts offer enough structure to feel guided without force-fitting every decision, which is the sweet spot for improvisation-heavy games like City of Mist.

## A Sample Micro-Adventure: The Neon Altar on Fortune Row
Let’s walk through a micro-adventure so you can see the kit in action. In Fortune Row, you’re likely to encounter a street known for its ephemeral neon glow and its whispered legends. Tonight, the players stumble into The Neon Altar, a storefront that appears only after midnight when the city’s mythic undercurrents surge to the surface.
- Prompt: A fortune-teller’s booth reappears in the same spot every night, but the signs spell your PCs’ hidden regrets in reverse. What do they discover about their own myth?
- Setup: The PCs are investigating a crime that didn’t happen but someone insists it did: a missing sculpture rumored to be carved from myth itself (think a sculpture that makes people confess their true identity when touched).
- Twist: The sculpture is a vessel for a minor god who promises to unlock a PC’s legend, but at a price: a memory they are not ready to part with.
- Beat 1: The group exchanges clues, with NPCs offering contradictory memories, forcing players to weigh whose truth matters most.
- Beat 2: The fortune moment triggers: a card suggests the hero’s legend seeks a sacrifice that will grant power, forcing a choice between personal memory and the city’s safety.
- Beat 3: Resolution: either the memory is given, the sculpture awakens, or the players realize the “price” is a test of trust within the party. The city notes this as a turning point, and a new mythic thread takes root.

This is not a rigid script; it’s a scaffold that the players fill with their choices. The Fate-like prompts are levers you can pull when the group looks to you for direction, and the micro-adventure provides enough texture to feel like a complete scene rather than a one-note encounter.

## GM Tips: Getting the Most Out of Fortune Row
- Use the prompts as conversation starters, not strict templates. Let players derail a macro plot by stepping into a micro moment that reveals something about their legend.
- Pair prompts with a simple scene card for each character—name, identity, memory one-liner—so players have a tangible way to thread their Arc into the micro-adventure.
- Don’t fear stepping out of the box. Fortune Row’s strength is in its flexibility; if a generator prompt nudges you toward a scene you wouldn’t normally run, go with it. The neon-night vibe often hides the best twists in the margins.
- Keep a running myth ledger. What legends appeared tonight? Which promises were made? Jotting down a few notes helps your next session pick up threads without you having to reconstruct the entire street again.
- Balance is king. If the fortune moments push too hard toward tragedy, sprinkle in a moment of bluster or humor to keep the table engaged. City of Mist thrives on tension, but your players crave relief too.
