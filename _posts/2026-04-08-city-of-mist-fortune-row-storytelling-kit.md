## A Sample Micro-Adventure: The Neon Altar on Fortune Row
Let us walk through a more complete micro adventure to show how the kit can feel in play. Fortune Row is a street buzzing with ephemeral neon and quiet whispers about legends in flux. Tonight, the players stumble into The Neon Altar, a storefront that appears only after midnight when the citys mythic undercurrents surge to the surface.
- Prompt: A fortune teller s booth reappears in the same spot every night. The signs spell each PC s hidden regrets in reverse when observed directly.
- Setup: The PCs are investigating a crime that did not happen, but someone insists it did: a missing sculpture rumored to be carved from myth itself. The sculpture is said to coax truth from those who touch it.
- Twist: The sculpture is a vessel for a minor god who promises to unlock a PC s legend, but at a price: a memory the PC does not want to part with.
- Beat 1: Clues mesh with contradictory memories offered by NPCs. Players must weigh whose truth matters most for the story s direction.
- Beat 2: The fortune moment triggers: a card proclaims the hero s legend seeks a sacrifice that will grant power, forcing a choice between personal memory and the city s safety.
- Beat 3: Resolution: memory given or sculpture awakened, or perhaps a twist reveals that the real price is trust within the party. The city notes the moment as a turning point and a new myth thread takes root.

This is not a rigid script; it is a scaffold that players fill with their choices. The Fate like prompts are levers you pull when the group looks to you for direction, and the micro adventure provides texture to feel like a complete scene rather than a one note beat.

## GM Tips: Getting the Most Out of Fortune Row
- Use prompts as conversation starters rather than templates. Let players derail a macro plot by stepping into a micro moment that reveals something about their legend.
- Pair prompts with a simple scene card for each character: name, identity, memory one liner, so players have a tangible way to thread their Arc into the micro adventure.
- Don t fear stepping out of the box. Fortune Row s strength is flexibility; if a generator prompt nudges you toward a scene you would not normally run, go with it. The neon night vibe often hides the best twists in the margins.
- Keep a running myth ledger. What legends appeared tonight, which promises were made, jot down notes so your next session picks up threads without reconstructing the city from scratch.
- Balance is king. If fortune moments push toward tragedy, sprinkle in a moment of humor to keep the table engaged. CoM thrives on tension, but players also crave relief.
