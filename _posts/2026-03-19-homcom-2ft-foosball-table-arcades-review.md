## Unboxing and Setup
### What arrives in the box
The unboxing ritual for a compact foosball table is both a rite of passage and a minor life hack. The HOMCOM comes neatly packed with the standard crew: the table slate, folding legs, two sets of rods with handles, a handful of foosball men per side, ball, two goal bins, and a manual that reads like a treasure map for people who hate manuals. Expect a lot of little screws, tiny washers, and the occasional hex-key you’ll swear you’ll put in the “screwdriver graveyard” later but somehow never do.

In our experience, the setup tastes like a DIY mini-project and a coffee mug: you’re going to need a stable surface for assembly, a patient hour or two, and a willingness to rearrange the living room to make room for a playing field that measures roughly the length of your forearm. The legs were the usual suspects—simple to snap on, with adjustable feet to compensate for wobbles on imperfect flooring. The ball, mercifully, is not a stubborn marble that refuses to roll; it has the aerodynamic maturity of a small ping-pong ball and the gravity of a bouncy coffee bean.

### Assembly tips and tricks
- Clear a 2- to 3-foot by 4-foot zone for the table’s footprint. You want room to swing rods without whacking a precious lamp, cat, or stubborn gaming chair.
- Use a level if you have one; otherwise, the floor will do the balancing act for you, which is not as dramatic as it sounds when you’re playing and trying not to topple over.
- The manual’s “beginner mode” is basically telling you to start with a basic two-on-two game. Don’t worry if you don’t instantly remember all the inhuman reflexes you had as a kid—this is foosball, not rocket surgery.
