H2. Cleaning, maintenance, and practical care (the boring-but-crucial part)

H3. Everyday cleaning: how to keep it fresh without drama
Vacuum on a low setting to preserve the loop integrity. Spills? Act fast with a blot and mild detergent, testing a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Harsh chemicals are a no-go; they can degrade fibers and dull the seahorse outlines. If you have pets, a lint roller becomes your best friend for fur-prone afternoons. If tassels get tangled, don’t yank—snip with small scissors if needed and preserve the integrity of the fringe.

H3. Stain resistance and colorfastness: nature meets nurture
The color profile is rich but not overpowering. Natural fading under direct sun is normal and can be a sign of a well-loved piece aging into a warmer story. If you’re a stickler for pristine color, try keeping direct sunlight away from peak hours or use a window filter. The overall action is a coastal sanctuary rather than a chalky museum piece.

H3. Sunlight and placement: where to put it for longevity
Position the rug away from the strongest sun or use window coverings during peak hours. Rotating the rug seasonally helps even wear and preserves the life of the pattern. If you live in a seriously sun-drenched locale, you may notice a mild, natural fade over the years—consider that an added patina rather than a defect.

H2. Practical considerations: real-world testing for real homes

H3. Traffic and pet safety: what to expect from daily life
Low-pile rugs are friendlier to paws and claws than high-pile options. Little ones running around won’t pull seams as easily, and vacuuming won’t require a surgical approach. If you anticipate heavy footfall or a lot of furniture rearranging, keep tassels tidy and use protective pads under heavy chairs to reduce snagging.

H3. Rugs under doors: clearance and practicality
If you have doors that slide or swing, ensure there’s enough clearance to prevent tassels from catching. A simple tweak—slightly shifting the rug’s position or trimming any extra tassel length—can save you from a morning snag incident.
