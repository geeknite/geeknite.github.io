## How to Play Quick Rules Overview
If you already know Skip-Bo, you will feel right at home. If not, fear not, for we will keep things crisp and friendly. The express edition focuses on speed, so the rules are a lean version of the grand saga.

### Setup
- Each player draws a hand of five cards from the express deck. The goal is to empty your hand into the discard or stock piles in a clean, orderly fashion.
- Build piles are created in the center by laying down cards in sequential order from 1 to whatever the stack requires. You can only start a new pile with the number that continues the sequence. See, not all numbers are just numbers; they are keys that unlock the next turn.
- The stock pile remains face down until you draw.

### On your turn
- Draw back up to five cards if you can; otherwise, you keep your current hand and hope the odds are on your side.
- Play as many cards as you can from your hand to the building piles or to your own stock as allowed by the rules.
- Use any wilds or Skip-Bo cards strategically to jump ahead in the sequence or to block opponents. If the rules say you can do something, you can do it—provided you have the card that makes it legal.
- End your turn by drawing up to five again, keeping your hand size in check.

### Winning
The first player to empty their hand and reveal the building piles wins the round. It sounds simple, and it is, until someone drops a perfect sequence that makes your jaw drop like a vendor at a convention dropping a rare toy on the table. The express variant keeps the pressure high, so victory feels earned and not just a matter of who drew the lucky wild card first.

## Strategy and Tips
This is where the magic happens, folks. The express edition rewards aggression, cunning, and a healthy disregard for the perfectly measured plan. Here are some practical tips to tilt the odds in your favor.

- Watch the tempo: In express mode, speed is not just a feature; it is a weapon. If you can force your opponents into a rushed mistake, you gain a precious edge.
- Manage your wilds: Wilds provide flexibility, but overusing them can backfire. Save them for moments when you can complete a sequence that unlocks multiple piles in quick succession.
- Plan two steps ahead: When you draw, consider not just the immediate card but the next turn. A single misfly can cascade into a domino effect that costs you the round.
- Reading opponents: Pay attention to what your rivals discard. If you notice they are short on a particular number, you can tailor your plays to prevent them from catching up.
- Endgame urgency: The last few turns are the most intense. Do not overthink simple plays. Focus on maintaining the pace and sealing the deal with a few clean sequences.
