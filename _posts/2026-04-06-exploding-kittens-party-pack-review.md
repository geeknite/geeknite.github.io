## How the Game Plays in a Party Pack

### Core Mechanics

At its core, Exploding Kittens Party Pack is a push-your-luck game with a heavy emphasis on card play and timing. Your goal is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card, unless you’re in possession of a Defuse card that can turn a doom missile into a harmless kitty with a zany hat. The party pack adds multiple new cards, which means more ways to manipulate, bluff, and outplay your friends. It’s a light engine of risk management: do you risk a peek into the draw pile, or do you hold back and let someone else take the gamble? The card text is the star here—short, often punny, and sometimes delightfully dark in a cartoon-cat-sort-of-way—so you can play quickly without wading through a heavy rulebook mid-game.

The expansion cards contribute to the ebb and flow of the round. Some cards force a neighbor to draw, others let you swap hands, and a few add special conditions that flip the usual expected outcomes. The beauty of the Party Pack is that it doesn’t require you to memorize a dozen new interactions to enjoy it. You can pick up the rules and start playing with minimal friction, making it a strong candidate for a casual game night where friends want to be entertained without filing a legal brief about the consequences of their card choices.
