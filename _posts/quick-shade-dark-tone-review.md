## What is Quick Shade Dark Tone?

Quick Shade Dark Tone is a shading solution designed for miniature painters who want to add depth and realism to their models without spending an eternity on washes and highlights. This product is a part of the Army Painter range, known for its accessibility and effectiveness.

## Application: Dip, Brush, or Splash?

There are three main ways to apply Quick Shade Dark Tone:

### The Dip Method

The dip method involves dunking your entire miniature into the can of Quick Shade. It's fast, it's messy, and it will make you feel like you're baptizing your miniatures in the holy waters of shading. ### The Brush Method

For those who prefer a bit more control (and less mess), the brush method is the way to go. Dip your brush into the Quick Shade and paint it onto your miniature like you're a wizard casting a shading spell. It's ideal for targeting specific areas without turning your miniature into a shade monstrosity.

### The Splash Method

Let's be honest, the splash method is not recommended unless you want to recreate a zombie apocalypse scene. But hey, if you're feeling adventurous and want to add a unique twist to your paint job, go ahead and give it a try. Just don't blame us when your miniatures look like they've had a mud fight.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Speed**: Quick Shade Dark Tone lives up to its name. It significantly reduces the time required to shade miniatures, allowing you to move on to the next steps faster than you can say "miniature painting marathon."
- **Depth**: It adds incredible depth to your miniatures, making them look more detailed and realistic. It's like giving them a personality, minus the existential crisis.
- **Consistency**: The product provides consistent results, so your army will look uniformly shaded, unlike your questionable decisions at the buffet table.

### Cons

- **Smell**: Quick Shade Dark Tone has a strong odor that could rival your gym socks after a three-hour workout. Make sure to use it in a well-ventilated area, or risk losing brain cells faster than you can paint.
- **Drying Time**: While it's quick in application, it takes a while to dry. Patience is key here, or you might end up with sticky, tacky miniatures that look like they've been dipped in honey.
- **Glossy Finish**: The final result is quite glossy, so you'll need to apply a matte varnish to tone it down if you're not into the shiny look.

## Comparisons and Alternatives

Quick Shade Dark Tone is not the only product out there for shading miniatures. Here are a few other options:

### Citadel Shade

Citadel shades, like Nuln Oil, provide excellent shading and are less glossy than Quick Shade. However, they require more time and effort to apply, making them less suited for quick jobs.

### Vallejo Washes

Vallejo offers a range of washes that are great for detailed shading. They are less smelly and dry faster, but they might not provide the same depth as Quick Shade.

## Final Recommendation

Quick Shade Dark Tone is a fantastic product for miniature painters who want to achieve great results without spending too much time. Its pros outweigh its cons, making it a valuable addition to your painting arsenal. Just remember to use it in a well-ventilated area and be prepared for the drying time.
