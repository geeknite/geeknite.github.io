## Weaving Technique and Fiber Blend
Let us geek out a bit on the weave. A dhurrie is a flat weave that creates a compact, durable surface. The warp and weft threads are interlaced in a straightforward but expert fashion, forming a sturdy grid that lies low to the floor. ## Cleaning and Care
The general rule of thumb for care is simple: avoid soaking the rug and deal with spills as soon as you can. Blot liquids with a clean cloth, working from the edges toward the center to prevent spreading. For tougher stains, a mix of mild detergent and water is usually enough; test on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can compromise the natural fibers or dye consistency. If you need a deeper clean, professional rug cleaning is a safe route that respects the integrity of handwoven textiles.

Regular vacuuming is your friend. Use a suction-only mode or a gentle beater brush setting to avoid pulling the fibers. Rotate the rug seasonally or every few months to ensure even wear and aging across the surface. That is not just a pro tip for furniture placement; it keeps the rug looking balanced, particularly if you have a room with strong lighting or a dominant color scheme.

For storage, roll the rug rather than folding it. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to minimize fiber degradation and color fading. If you plan to wash the rug, follow the manufacturer guidance or consult a pro; handwoven textiles deserve a careful touch that respects the weave rather than harsh chemicals.

## Ethical Sourcing and Sustainability
Sustainability in rugs is a multi-layered thing. Natural fibers like wool and jute tend to have lower environmental footprints compared to synthetic fibers, particularly when sourced responsibly. Wool is renewable, biodegradable, and can be produced in ways that support pastoral communities and animal welfare. Jute grows quickly, requires relatively little fertilizer, and is harvested with a minimal impact on soil structure. The combination in a dhurrie weave is a pragmatic choice for eco-minded interiors that still crave luxury underfoot.

That said, transparency matters. Ask about dye sources, loom locations, and worker welfare when possible. Ethical sourcing is not a marketing term but a practice that includes fair wages, safe working conditions, and honest supply chains.
