## Material Quality and Durability
Durability is the big question with any card sleeve product. You want the coating to resist minor moisture, prevent color transfer from the cards, and not wear down with daily shuffles. The Son Gohan sleeves use a standard PP (polypropylene) film with a smooth inner surface designed to reduce friction between cards. In practical terms, that means your cards will glide during shuffles, but you won’t experience the flop-and-snap popularity of cheaper sleeves that often bend or warp when you peak inside your own deck.

The thickness, while not published in a single consumer number, sits in the middle of the typical “standard deck” sleeves. It’s not super thin—so you won’t worry about scuffing long-term card faces—and not so thick they ruin the sleeve-to-sleeve interaction when you stack multiple decks. For players who run two nearly identical 50-card decks for fun or side tournaments, this thickness feels just right. The tradeoff is slightly bulkier deck profiles than ultra-thin sleeves, but you gain a reassuring sense of structure that helps your cards maintain their shape after repeated shuffles.

A few practical notes on wear: corners will show signs of use before the card faces degrade. That’s normal for any deck-building hobby. If you’re planning to keep a deck mint for a long time, consider rotating sleeves after several months of heavy play or using a protective outer sleeve to give the Son Gohan sleeves a second life.

## Care, Storage, and Longevity Tips
A little maintenance goes a long way toward keeping your Son Gohan sleeves looking fresh:
- Store sleeves away from direct sunlight when not in use to prevent color fade, especially the outer art. Royal blue and gold accents can dull over time with too much sun exposure.
- Keep your sleeves organized in deck boxes or sleeves-within-sleeves solutions to avoid edge wear caused by clutter and accidental scrapes.
- If you notice edge curling after long use, consider replacing them in a rotation with a fresh batch—wear isn’t the enemy; neglect is.
- Clean dust off the outer surface with a soft microfiber cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners; you don’t want to turn Son Gohan into Son Grubby.
- For travel and tournament day, a small protective pouch will protect sleeves from creases and accidental squashing.
