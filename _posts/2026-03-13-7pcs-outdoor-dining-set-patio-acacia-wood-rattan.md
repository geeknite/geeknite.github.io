### Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Fresh
Care is not glamorous, but it’s essential. Wipe the wood with a damp cloth periodically to remove dust and debris. If your climate experiences heavy rain, consider applying a protective sealant every year or two to maintain the wood’s integrity. Clean the cushions by removing the covers and washing according to the fabric’s care label; most outdoor fabrics handle a gentle machine wash, but always check the manufacturer’s instructions to avoid shrinking or color loss. If you notice minor staining, treat promptly with a mild soap and water solution.

Here are a few practical care tips:
- Use coasters or placemats to minimize staining from colorful beverages.
- Store cushions indoors or in a dry space during extended rain or winter months.
- Use a soft brush to remove dust from the rattan weaving to keep the texture looking crisp.
- Periodically check the screws and fasteners; a quick tighten ensures long-term stability.

### Assembly Tips and Common Pitfalls
- Lay out all parts before you start. A clean workspace reduces friction (and losing screws to the lawn).
- Don’t overtighten screws; you can strip the wood or strips threads if you push too hard. Tighten until snug, then give a quarter-turn. You’ll thank me later when you’ve swapped cushions and cleaned the surface.
- Use a rubber mallet to seat components gently. It prevents scratch marks and keeps joints aligned.
- If you’re unsure about the direction of a step, take a short break and re-check the manual. It helps to have a second pair of eyes to spot any misalignment before you’ve fully attached pieces.
