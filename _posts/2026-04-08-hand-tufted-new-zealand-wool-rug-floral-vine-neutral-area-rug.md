### The science of hand tufting and why NZ wool matters
Hand tufting is the craft sibling of carpet weaving and machine knotting. It involves punching yarn through a fabric backing to create a plush, high-pile surface. The result is a rug that feels cozy underfoot, with a structure that’s less likely to pancake under heavy traffic than some machine-made options. NZ wool—the real star here—is known for its long staple fibers, which translates to stronger fiber integrity, better spring, and a dye-absorption profile that yields rich, long-lasting colors.

From a geeky standpoint, think of NZ wool as a high-end GPU: not the cheapest option, but it runs cooler, stores more reliably, and scales gracefully with the rest of your system—ahem, room decor. It resists pilling better than many synthetics, which means fewer fiber hairs on your couch after year one. The combination of hand tufting and NZ wool yields a surface that’s both forgiving (soft underfoot) and resilient (handles foot traffic and pet claws without turning into a fuzz factory).

### Practical considerations: care, cleaning, and everyday life
Care is the unsung hero of any good rug. The Floral Vine’s NZ wool makes cleaning more forgiving, but there are a few best practices to keep it looking showroom-fresh:

- Regular vacuuming: Vacuum with a beater bar on a gentle setting. Go with shorter passes rather than long, heavy sweeps that could loosen fibers over time.
- Spills: Act quickly. Blot (don’t rub) with a clean, dry cloth to absorb the liquid. For stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution (water and a tiny drop of gentle soap) can help—test on an inconspicuous corner first. Avoid harsh solvents.
- Avoid sun-bleaching: Neutrals appreciate a little sun, but extended bright sun exposure can cause color fading. If possible, rotate the rug to distribute light exposure evenly.
- Rotation: Rotate every 6–12 months to even out wear patterns in high-traffic zones.
- Pets: The wool’s resilience helps with claws, but trim nails regularly and address accidents promptly to avoid lingering odors or stains.

If you’re curious about a more formal cleaning plan, many interior sites recommend a yearly or biannual professional cleaning for high-traffic rooms. For the curious minds among you, a quick skim of articles on rug care from places like Apartment Therapy and The Spruce reveals common-sense routines that won’t scare your budget into a cold sweat.
