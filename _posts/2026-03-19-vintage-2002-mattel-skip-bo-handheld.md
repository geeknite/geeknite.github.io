## Testing and care steps if you decide to power it up

If you want to proceed with testing the unit, here is a pragmatic, non-destructive checklist you can follow. This will help determine whether you have a functioning device or a gloriously decorative object that belongs behind glass.

1. Gather fresh batteries of the correct size. If there is a label on the back, follow it. If not, start with standard AA or AAA depending on the compartment size. Avoid mixing old and new batteries in the same run. The goal is to avoid a spectacular leak so dramatic it ruins your prized find.
2. Inspect the battery compartment for corrosion. If you see powdery residue, gently clean with isopropyl alcohol on a cotton swab. Do not scrub aggressively; you are not replacing a ship’s hull, you are reviving a toy.
3. Connect and power on. If you hear a beep, you see an LCD display, and you can navigate the menus, you are on a good path. If you get a bright flash, a singular beep that sounds like an angry duck, or nothing at all, you are in a more delicate repair situation and should proceed with caution or seek professional help.
4. Test basic functions. Try to start a game, draw a card, and attempt to place cards according to the rules. Pay attention to any lag, unregistered inputs, or screen anomalies. Document what works, what doesn’t, and what you suspect is age related.
5. If it seems functional, test with multiple batteries and check for battery life. If the console returns to life after a long period of inactivity or behaves erratically, treat it as a sign that a deeper cleaning or a more careful electronics restoration is in order.

If you do power it up and it behaves, you have not just a nostalgic object but a small beacon of your own courage in the face of ancient electronics. If it doesn’t power up, you still have a story to tell — yes, the machine is stubborn, but you conquered its stubbornness by writing about it.

## How to restore without losing the soul of the piece

Restoration is a delicate art that demands respect for the original design and a careful hand with the internals. If you decide to pursue restoration, here are some gentle guidelines to keep you on the right path without turning this into a plastic soup accident:

- Start with non-destructive cleaning: Use a soft brush and a little isopropyl alcohol to remove surface grime. Do not soak the device in liquids; you are cleaning with care, not dunking it in a cleansing river.
- Check the battery contacts: Clean corrosion with a small amount of contact cleaner and a cotton swab. If the contacts are pitted beyond recognition, replacement may be necessary, but be prepared for more invasive repairs.
- Inspect the buttons and membranes: If buttons are sticky or unresponsive, a careful cleaning under the membrane or gentle replacement of the button contacts can help. Do not force a stubborn button; you may break a trace or a plastic nub.
- Consider professional repair for the board: If you suspect a failed IC or a loose solder joint, a professional with experience in retro electronics can often bring a device back from the brink without destroying its historical value.
- Documentation matters: Take photos before you begin, during, and after. This kind of evidence helps you remember what you did and helps future owners appreciate the journey of revival.

If you approach restoration with a respectful mindset, you can preserve not only the device but the story that goes with it. The hardware may be aged, but the experience of reviving a piece of gaming history is, in its own way, an act of preservation.
