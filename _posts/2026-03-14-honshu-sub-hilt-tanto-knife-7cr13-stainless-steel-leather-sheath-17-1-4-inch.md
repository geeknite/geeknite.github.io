## Design language and build quality

Honshu has carved out a niche in the budget blade arena by focusing on dependable construction and a look that hints at tradition without veering into ostentation. The Sub-Hilt Tanto presents a blade that is clearly designed for piercing work with a robust tip that can handle contact with soft wood and cardboard as part of daily tasks. The blade geometry sits in a sweet spot that feels confident in hand for tasks like cutting notches in a piece of wood or slicing rope during a camping setup. ### Blade steel and edge dynamics

At the heart of this blade is 7CR13 stainless steel a common workhorse in budget blades. It isn t going to win a steel Olympics but it does deliver a workable edge with decent edge retention and easy sharpening. In practical terms you will be able to maintain a sharp edge with a few passes on a standard stone and you won t feel overwhelmed by the heat treatment cycle you often see on higher end steels. The edge life is good enough for everyday tasks and light outdoor use; it isn t designed to hold a razor for weeks on end but it won t disappear after a morning of cardboard slicing either. The blade thickness and grind keep a strong tip while preserving enough width behind the edge to maintain practical cutting performance. In short this is the kind of steel you reach for when you want reliable performance without a steel nerd lecture.

### Ergonomics and handling comfort

The Sub-Hilt design is a nod to traditional working knives with a grip that fills the palm without turning your hand into a clamp. The handle texture offers grip even when you have damp hands or your environment is humid. The balance point sits a touch forward which some testers enjoyed for chopping style tasks and others preferred for precise tip work. The guard itself adds a bit of security and helps seat the blade in a controlled draw motion. It is the type of design that pays dividends during extended use by reducing micro slips and offering a stable platform for controlled cuts. The overall ergonomics strike a balance between comfort and practicality making it approachable for users picking up a budget blade for the first time and still satisfying enough for seasoned folks who want a no frills companion.

The leather sheath that accompanies the Sub-Hilt is a classic carry option that evokes a vintage vibe while remaining perfectly serviceable for today s needs. ### Maintenance and care tips

7CR13 is forgiving but still benefits from routine care. After use wipe the blade dry to reduce water spotting and potential corrosion. Apply a thin film of oil on the blade to maintain corrosion resistance and keep the edge clean for quicker sharpening. When it comes time to sharpen a basic flat or ceramic stone will do the job you don t need expensive equipment for the majority of maintenance tasks. The blade geometry is forgiving enough that a steady hand and mid range stones will keep the edge true. Store the knife in its sheath when not in use and avoid leaving it in damp conditions where moisture can accumulate inside the leather sheath. A little routine maintenance will extend the life of both blade and sheath and keep this budget blade feeling reliable for years to come.
