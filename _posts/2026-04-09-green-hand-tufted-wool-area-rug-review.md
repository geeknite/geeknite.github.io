## Care and Maintenance: Keeping Wool Beautiful
### Everyday upkeep
Wool is relatively forgiving. A quick vacuum with a soft-bristle attachment a couple of times a week helps lift dust and keep the fibers looking crisp. Don’t jerk the vacuum head across the surface; instead, use gentle, overlapping strokes to avoid pulling at the tufts. For spills, act fast. Blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and treat the stain with a wool-safe cleaner if necessary. If you’re concerned about staining, consider applying a fabric protector designed for wool fibers, but test on a small inconspicuous area first to verify colorfastness.

For those who love DIY care routines, there are plenty of wool rug care resources. The Woolmark care guides offer practical steps for wool rugs, including stain treatment and maintenance schedules. Check their guidelines if you want to dive deeper into the science of wool fibers and long-term care: https://www.woolmark.com/fibre/wool/how-to-care-for-wool-rugs/

### Deep cleaning and professional care
Every few years, or if you have heavy foot traffic, you may want to schedule a professional cleaning. A professional rug cleaning helps restore the pile height, refresh colors, and extend the rug’s life. For households with pets or high spill risk, a professional cleaning every 1–3 years becomes part of the maintenance routine you never knew you needed until the math of time and dirt creeps up on you.

Avoid steam cleaning or excessive moisture on wool rugs, as wool fibers resist water to some extent but can become warped or distorted with improper moisture levels. If you must address a stubborn stain, consult a professional or use a wool-specific cleaning solution following the product’s instructions. Always test a small area first to avoid any surprises.

### Caring for color and pile over time
Over time, natural wear will reveal the rug’s character. A well-loved rug develops a patina that woven fabrics try to emulate but rarely succeed in duplicating. If you notice slight shedding or loose fibers, a light combing with a soft bristle brush can help realign fibers and reduce the look of fuzz. Don’t overdo it; you don’t want to turn your new rug into a fuzz-bomb. Regular maintenance keeps the pile tidy and the color stable, and it’s a small ritual that reminds you you’re an adult who vacuumed on a weekday without being forced by a reminder app.

### Environmental and ethical notes
Wool is a renewable resource with potential ethical considerations, depending on the farm and processing chain. If you’re mindful of where your wool originates, you can make an informed choice. Look for certifications or transparent supply chains, and consider brands that publish their sourcing policies. Wool also tends to be biodegradable, which is a bonus for eco-friendly folks who want to minimize landfill contribution. For general wool characteristics, see the Woolmark resources linked above.
