## How to play
### Setup
Each player draws a hand of response cards. One player takes the role of the judge for the first round; this rotates clockwise with every round. The judge shuffles the prompt deck and draws one card faceup. The remaining players pick a response card from their hand that best answers the prompt. They place their card face down, and the judge shuffles the submitted cards and reveals them. The judge picks the favorite, and that player earns a brag token or a round win. New round, new judge, and if you want to spice things up you can add a rule that the judge cannot pick their own line.

### Rounds and strategy
The core mechanic is simple enough to teach in two minutes, but the comedic nuance takes longer to master. You want to deliver a line that is audacious yet specific; you want to show that you understand the person you are lampooning. The best players know when to lean into self-deprecation or turn the heat toward a pokey, universal truth. The worst round is when everyone plays it safe and the table goes silent, which in Geeknite terms is a culinary tragedy larger than burning a microwave burrito.

### Variations and house rules
If you want to turn the dial on raunch and risk, you can add a couple of house rules. Example: each player must read their line aloud in a voice that matches the prompt, turning the round into an audition for a tiny improv show. Another rule: after a round, the judge must explain why a line was chosen in a short, five-word review for the audience. If your group loves meta humor, you can award bonus tokens for jokes that reference game mechanics, or for winking at the camera like a reality show contestant. The point is to keep energy high and the air charged with comedic adrenaline.

## Tips for great rounds
- Read the room: If the table looks like a high-stakes poker table, you might want to hold your best lines until the last possible moment.
- Lean into personalization: The best jokes draw on real experiences with the players at the table. The more you know about the person, the sharper the punchline.
- Use voice and performance: A dramatic reading can elevate a line from simple to legendary. Do not underestimate the power of a well-timed pause and a theatrical whisper.
- Keep the vibes inclusive: No one wants to feel attacked or excluded. If someone seems uncomfortable, switch to safer prompts or stop to double check the consent boundaries.
- Variation in winner criteria: Sometimes the judge should pick a line because it was the most underappreciated, or the line that made the judge snort the loudest. Let the table determine a new micro-rule once a session becomes chaotic in a good way.
