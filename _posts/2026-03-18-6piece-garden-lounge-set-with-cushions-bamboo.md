## Weather, Durability, and Maintenance
Bamboo is a strong material when properly treated. The frame holds up to sun, wind, and the occasional afternoon rain squall. Treat the wood with a protective sealant if you live in an area with heavy rainfall or intense sun exposure. It helps maintain the color and prevents surface cracking over the years. The cushions are designed to resist moisture to a certain extent, but we recommend storing them indoors during prolonged wet seasons to extend their life.

- Cleaning routine: wipe with a damp cloth for everyday grime; for tougher stains, mild soap and water do the job. Do not use harsh chemicals that could degrade the fabric or the frame finish.
- Storage strategy: store cushions in a dry space during winter or when not in use for extended periods. A breathable storage bag works wonders to keep dust off the cushions and maintain loft.
- Maintenance cadence: a quick quarterly check on screws and brackets keeps everything feeling solid and ready for action, whether you are hosting a barbecue or a micro film festival on your patio.

## How It Compares to Other Sets
For context, we often compare bamboo sets to teak and resin wicker options. Here is a quick, nerdy comparison:

- Bamboo vs teak: Bamboo offers a lighter feel, easier mobility, and a warmer natural look. Teak tends to be more water- and weather-resistant longer term but comes with a heftier price tag and weight.
- Bamboo vs resin wicker: Bamboo shines in aesthetics and eco appeal, while resin wicker can outlast bamboo in some very harsh climates and tend to dry out less in the sun. Cushion choice and maintenance will matter more for resin wicker if you want to keep the look pristine.

## Assembly and Maintenance Tips
The assembly is generally straightforward, with basic tools included and predrilled holes that make life easier. Here are a few tips to speed things up and keep your sanity intact:

- Do a dry fit of all parts before tightening anything permanently. It helps you visualize the final layout and ensures nothing is missing.
- Tighten screws in a diagonal pattern similar to tightening a car wheel; this helps distribute stress and reduces wobble over time.
- Place the set on a flat, level surface. If your deck has a slope, you may want to add small pad shims to keep everything level and prevent cushion deformation.

Cushion care is simple but essential. Keep covers on when not using them, wash occasionally with mild soap, and ensure they dry thoroughly before storage. If you notice fading, consider moving the set to a partially shaded area during the peak sun hours to preserve color life.
