## Gameplay and Mechanics
Sushi Go! is a drafting game: you pass cards around, pick one, pass the rest, repeat until all cards are used. The Collector's Box doesn't alter the core mechanics, but it does streamline the experience by providing a neat way to store and separate the card suits.

### The drafting flow
We ran dozens of hands with players of varying experience. First-timers picked up the flow quickly because the scoring is straightforward: nigiri cards score points, sashimi requires multiples, maki stacks accumulate points at the end. The Collector's Box doesn't add new scoring rules, but it does help keep track of the cards so you can glance at the table and not rummage through a messy pile of face-down wrappers.

### Pace and player interaction
The core joy of Sushi Go! is that you can play quickly while banter about your sushi preferences erupts around the table. The Collector's Box contributes to the vibe by providing a keepsake feel and reducing the set-up lip service you give to friends who show up late.
