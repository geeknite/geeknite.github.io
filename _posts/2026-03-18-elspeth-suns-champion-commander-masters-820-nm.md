## Usage in Commander Masters decks (strategies and archetypes)

### Token swarm allies: the bread-and-butter approach
Elspeth thrives in tokens—soldiers, knights, and aetherial constructs included. A token-centric deck can use Elspeth to bolster the board with multiple 1/1 units and use those bodies as a scaffold for bigger plays. Because Commander is a multiplayer format, the power of tokens compounds across multiple turns. The trick is to maintain board state: don’t overcommit if you’re seated at a table with a heavy removal suite, and always consider how your token army threatens more than one player at a time. In practice, you’ll want to pair Elspeth with token producers, anthem effects (like those that buff your entire army), and ways to reuse value from your board after a mass removal.

### Board wipes and resilience: timing matters
Part of what makes Elspeth valuable in Master sets is the synergy with broad-sweep effects. In a typical Commander Masters environment, you’ll sometimes leverage Elspeth as a stabilizer before a big wipe, making sure your token army survives or regrows quickly afterward. She becomes a tool in a larger kit: you can use her loyalty to bridge from early board presence to a late-game lockdown with a few well-chosen stand-ins like removal, bounce, or recursion. That’s the sweet spot where Elspeth remains relevant even as the table shifts power through other players’ turns.

### Build ideas: two pathways to glory
- Pathway A: Token push with protection. You pack token generators, a handful of protective spells, and recursions that ensure your tokens come back after wipes. The objective is to overwhelm the table with consistent, resilient bodies and gradually escalate your threat level.
- Pathway B: Aura/Equipment synergy. You can go airborne in a slot where Elspeth’s tokens become conduits for equipment or enchantments that magnify your battlefield presence. The result is a meta-layered approach where you don’t just rely on a single card; your board becomes a mosaic of stat buffs, protective enchantments, and repeated threats.

Either path benefits from careful mana base tuning and a sense of table etiquette: in Commander, you want to be a fun, reliable player rather than the “I win in three turns” archetype. Elspeth, with the right support, can fulfill both roles—offer a strong board presence on a monthly schedule while still giving you opportunities for clever plays and well-timed growth spurts.

## Practical deck-building tips for 820 NM copies

### Budget vs. premium considerations
- Budget builds: Focus on token engines you already own, cheap removal, and value a la carte synergy. Don’t chase the newest, shiniest support spells if they push you into a price bracket that makes your kitchen table wince. In many games, the joy of tokens is in the abundance, not in the absolute single-threaded power.
- Premium builds: If you’re chasing shine (mythic reprints, foil variants, or premium promos), you can open the door to a higher-power deck that still respects the table. The key is to balance a few high-impact cards with consistent token production and resilient win-con lines. You’ll want durably colored mana sources and reliable ramp so you don’t stall on early turns.

### Core card ideas to consider (non-exhaustive)
- Token producers: cards that generate multiple tokens per play or per turn.
- Anthem effects: global buffs that increase your token army’s aggression and durability.
- Recursion and duplication: spells or effects that return tokens or planeswalkers to your hand or board.
- Protection: counters, hexproof mechanics, or bounce that keeps your army safe long enough to convert into victory.
- Removal that doubles as card advantage: you want to keep momentum while pruning threats at the right times.

### How to handle multiplayer dynamics
In a multi-opponent format, you don’t want to present a single, obvious line of attack. Elspeth’s power scales with the board—and so does the risk of becoming a target. A few heuristics help:
- Don’t overcommit in the early game; you can often accelerate into a stronger mid-game with careful planning.
- Read the table: sometimes you can bait an opponent into a false sense of security by letting someone else take the lead, then swing back with a perfected board wipe or a surge of tokens.
- Keep a back-pocket answer for big blockers or a problematic planeswalker. If you can neutralize the biggest threat in a single play, you often swing the tempo in your favor for multiple turns to come.
