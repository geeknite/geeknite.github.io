## Who are the Palanite Justicars?
Palanite Justicars are the enforcement arm of the Palanite faction, a paramilitary-police hybrid born from the grim reality of the Underhive. Think of them as a squad that filed a crime-report into a gun and called it a day. The aesthetic is intentionally stark: polished armor that gleams with a bureaucratic pride, imposing helms that threaten to reveal a very specific personality trait (zero tolerance), and a vibe that says the squad is equally comfortable delivering a cease-and-desist order as they are delivering a plasma volley.

In-fluff, the Justicars operate as mobile magistrates: they move from precinct to precinct of the hive, delivering judgment with the precision of a next-generation laser pointer and the swagger of someone who has seen the paperwork and still chooses to fight anyway. They pair well with other factions because their presence signals a credible threat—enough to push a skirmish toward a cautionary tale rather than a free-for-all melee. They’re not here to be your fun, chaotic wildcard; they’re here to remind everyone else that there is a law, and it has a very big gun.

## Tactics and Build Options
The strength of Palanite Justicars lies in versatility and reliability. They’re the backbone of a mid-range roster or a surgical, lane-clearing squad—a flexible tool in a meta that loves mobility but respects a disciplined shot. Here are several build directions you might consider:

- Balanced Patrol: A sergeant with a close-combat option, two mid-to-long rifles, and a couple of mid-weight specialists. The aim is steady map control with decent coverage as you advance along mid‑line objectives.
- Heavy Enforcement: Emphasize heavier armaments and defense, pairing the squad with a couple of heavier hitters to punish breaches in a defended zone. This build trades mobility for a brutal, pinning-fire presence that can lock down a key corridor late in the game.
- Specialist Fuse: One or two specialists wielding unique equipment to disrupt enemy plans. Palanites thrive when you can leverage tools to hamper enemy movement or force poor engagement choices.

Tactical tips to maximize their effectiveness:
- Leverage cover and line of sight. The underhive rewards mobility and concealment; the Palanites’ armor helps, but you’re still better off taking a defensive stance and punishing exposed targets.
- Use synergy with other gangs. Pairing Palanites with a faction that can create marching-enemy forcing conditions or disrupt enemy lines can open up lanes for your squad to exploit.

## Painting and Basing Tips
A well-painted army elevates the mood of the underhive tabletop and helps players read intent over noise. Palanite Justicars strike a nice balance between high-contrast armor and grim urban grime.

Top painting tips:
- Armor: Start with a muted base (stone gray or charcoal) and build up to a brighter system on the chest plates. Metallic trims pair nicely with a blue or teal motif to evoke police-enforcer vibes without tipping into neon sci‑fi territory.
- Auras and glow: The underhive glows courtesy of scrap-light and hazard signage. A soft blue or green wash on lenses, plus a subtle edge highlight, can simulate the reflective signage. It communicates a patrol that exists in a city that literally never sleeps.
- Weathering: A restrained rust wash along edge transitions gives the sense of years of service in ash and heat. Don’t overdo it—the model should stay readable from tabletop distance.
- Basing: Urban alleyways with broken signage, shards of glass, and puddles of toxic sludge sell the scene. Static grass is fine for contrast, but keep gray-brown tones to preserve the city vibe. Add signage elements to bases to hint at Palanite patrol routes and municipal warnings.

Conversions are welcome, but keep coherence. A city-protected squad should feel like a single unit rather than a collage of parts from multiple kits. Maintain scale and uniformity so your Palanites read as a team rather than a cosplay group that forgot the script.

## Painting, Toppers, and Storefronts: A Final Look at the Aesthetic
The Palanite Justicars aren’t just a unit; they’re a mood—precinct bureaucracy in motion, with a marching cadence and a badge you don’t want to misread. If you’re aiming for a cinematic tabletop army, lean into the urban noir vibe: checkered signage reflected on polished armor, a touch of grime in the helmet creases, and a rigid marching pose that says, “We own this part of the city.”

A quick-start painting regimen:

- Basing should reinforce the urban look—pavement textures, rubble, street debris—with a restrained use of static grass for contrast.
- Signage elements on bases—tiny posters or municipal warnings—are a great way to tell a story about patrol routes and the underhive’s bureaucratic labyrinth.

Conversions are possible, but plan your themes. If you want to add a little extra narrative weight, pair Palanites with proxies that reinforce the enforcement vibe (without breaking the cohesion). The key is to preserve a unified silhouette and a consistent color language across the squad.
