## Who are the Palanite Justicars?
Palanite Justicars are the enforcement arm of the Palanite faction, a law enforcement corps designed to bring the city to heel in the sprawling, claustrophobic tunnels of the Underhive. They are the kind of unit that makes you feel like you accidentally drove into a dystopian police thriller and decided to stay for the artwork. The aesthetic is deliberate: polished armor, imposing helms, and a sense that every soldier is carrying both a badge and a loud, very loud, gun.

In fluff terms, the Justicars operate as a mobile magistrate squad. Their job is not just to shoot first and ask questions later; it is to project order into chaos, to remind the underhive who is in charge, and to look equally good while doing it. They pair well with other, more criminal factions because they give the impression of a credible threat that can turn a skirmish into a cautionary tale for would-be troublemakers.

## Tactics and Build Options
The strength of Palanite Justicars lies in their versatility and consistency. You can take them as the dependable backbone of a mid-range gang roster or as a surgical strike force that you deploy to clear a pivotal lane. Here are a few build directions you might consider:

- Balanced Patrol: A mix of a sergeant with a close combat option, two long rifles, and a couple of middle-weight specialists. The aim is steady map control with reliable coverage as you advance along mid-line objectives.
- Heavy Enforcement: Focus on heavier armaments and defense, pairing the squad with a couple of heavy hitters to punish attempts to breach a defended zone. This build trades a little speed for a brutal punch and can lock down a key corridor in the late game.
- Specialist Fuse: One or two specialists with unique equipment to disrupt enemy plans. The Palanites thrive when you can leverage a few tools to hamper enemy movement or force a poor engagement choice.

A critical tactical note is to leverage cover and line of sight. The underhive favors mobility and cover—the Palanites’ armor allows them to hold a defensive line longer, but you still want to avoid a straight-up gunfight in the open. The synergy with other gangs can be excellent too. For instance, pairing Palanites with a gang that can create forcing conditions or disrupt enemy lines can create openings your squad can exploit. ## Painting and Basing Tips
We all know how a well-painted army improves the mood of a table. Palanite Justicars have the right balance of high-contrast armor and grimy undercity aesthetic to look striking without drowning in fancy weathering. Here are our top tips:

- Armor: Start with a muted base like a stone gray and build up to a more reflective finish on the chest plates. Metallic trims work well with a blue or teal motif to give them that police-enforcer feel without veering into neon sci-fi.
- Auras and glow: The Underhive shines with scrap-light and hazard glow. Use a soft blue or green wash on lenses, and a subtle edge highlight to simulate reflected signage. It creates the sense that the palanite squad patrols a city that literally never sleeps.
- Weathering: A light dry brush with rust tones on the edges of armor and equipment suggests years of service in a polluted, ash-filled environment. Keep it restrained so the model remains readable at tabletop distance.
- Basing: Alleyway basing with broken signage, broken glass, and puddles of toxic sludge can really sell the scene. Use static grass for contrast, but preserve the urban vibe with sprayed grays and browns.

If you enjoy conversions, the Palanites play well with other urban-themed kits. A few cautions: maintain scale integrity and ensure any conversions still align with the squad’s visual identity. You want the unit to feel cohesive, not a montage of mismatched parts, even if the story behind the models provides a nice narrative wiggle room.

## Lore and Flavor: A City Under Watchful Eyes
Lore wise, the Palanite Justicars are more than just a unit of soldiers. They embody the idea of law and order in a city that would rather cave in on itself than admit defeat. In the underhive, justice is a product of speed, decisiveness, and the willingness to do what it takes to prevent crime from spreading. The Palanites are not shy about that last point; they know the moment you hesitate is the moment someone else writes your epitaph on a graffiti-streaked wall.

In game terms, that translates into a unit that can shepherd objectives into your control zone, pressure vulnerable enemy assets, and create the psychological edge you need to tilt the momentum in a tight fight. The tone of the palette—polished armor, stern helmet crests, and uniform color—underlines the sense that this is a disciplined force that does not let chaos flourish.

If you enjoy a good story alongside your dice, you can weave a narrative thread into your campaign where the Palanites chase a rogue enforcer or a corrupted magistrate.
