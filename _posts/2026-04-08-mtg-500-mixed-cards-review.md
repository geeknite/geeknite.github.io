### Friendly sorting tips to get the most out of your 500

Sorting is the secret handshake of any card collection. Here are practical tips to turn 500 random cards into a playable pool your friends want to draft again next week:

- Start with color and mana curve: Separate into white, blue, black, red, green, and colorless. Then sort by converted mana cost to get a sense of the curve. This helps you pool early-game plays and late-game corrections before you start building archetypes.
- Tag with quick labels: Use a spreadsheet or board to label cards as removal, temporary removal, ramp, card draw, etc. It speeds up deck construction and reveals gaps you need to fill.
- Identify staples and near-staples: You’ll encounter a handful of cards that almost always find a home in decks. Put those aside, even if it means a quick trade to get something more usable.
- Trade-off zone: Accept that some cards are only good for certain formats. If you’ve pulled a lot of value-distribution cards, stash them for future trades rather than forcing them into a single build.
- Sleeve and store: Use color-coded sleeves to protect the cards and keep them organized. A simple organizer box helps you move through a chaotic draft with minimal pain.

With sorting discipline, you can turn a 500-card mixed set into a surprisingly robust pool for casual and semi-serious play. The finished product is not a museum piece; it’s a living toolkit for the night you and your table create together.

## How to store and care for your new mountain of cards

Storage matters as much as selection. Here’s a simple approach to keep 500 cards in good condition and ready for action:

- Use a sturdy, stackable storage box with a lid to protect from sun and humidity. A breathable bag is fine for travel but won’t survive heat and humidity long-term.
- Invest in sleeves that fit your decks. For casual play, standard 100-count sleeves are fine; for more rugged use, consider colored or premium sleeves designed for frequent shuffles.
- Label your batches. If you end up with multiple batches, a simple label helps you track which cards belong together and their relative quality.
- Keep a small card-cleaning cloth on hand. It helps with dust and fingerprints that accumulate during long sessions.
- Avoid exposing cards to sunlight for extended periods. Color balance matters less than card stock health.

With proper storage and care, your 500 cards will fuel countless nights of chaos and laughter. And yes, you will still experience the magical moment when a single card changes the entire course of a game—the kind of moment MTG is famous for.
