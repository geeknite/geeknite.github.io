### Friendly sorting tips to get the most out of your 500

Sorting is the secret handshake of any card collection. Here are some practical tips that help you turn 500 random cards into a playable set that your friends want to draft again next week:

- Start with color and mana curve: Separate into white, blue, black, red, green, and colorless. Then sort by converted mana cost to get a sense of the curve. This helps you pool early-game plays and late-game corrections before you start building archetypes.
- Tag with quick labels: If you use a spreadsheet or a board, tag cards as removal, removal-lite, ramp, card draw, etc. It speeds up deck construction and helps you see gaps you need to fill.
- Identify staples and near-staples: You will encounter a handful of cards that almost always find a home in decks. Separate those out, even if it means a quick trade for something more usable.
- Trade-off zone: Accept that some cards are only good for certain formats. If you have a race of elusive value cards, set those aside and let them be your bargaining chips in future trading nights.
- Sleeve and store: Use color-coded sleeves to protect the cards and keep them sorted. A simple organizer box helps you move through a chaotic draft with minimal pain.

With the sorting discipline above, you can turn a 500 mixed set into a surprisingly robust pool for both casual and semi serious play. The key is to view the 500 as raw material rather than a finished product. The finished product is the night you and your table create together.

## How to store and care for your new mountain of cards

Storage matters almost as much as selection. Here is a simple approach that keeps your 500 cards in good condition and ready for action:

- Use a sturdy, stackable storage box with a lid to protect from sun and humidity. A breathable bag is fine if you travel a lot but will not hold up to heat and humidity.
- Invest in sleeves that fit your decks. For casual play, standard 100 count sleeves are fine; for more intense use you may prefer colored or premium sleeves that can handle more shuffles.
- Label your batches. If you end up with multiple batches, a simple label helps you keep track of which cards belong together and their relative quality.
- Keep a small card cleaning cloth on hand. It helps with minor dust and fingerprints that accumulate during nights of heavy play.
- Avoid exposing cards to sunlight for long periods. The color balance on the art is not as important as the health of the card stock.

With proper storage and care, your 500 cards will be ready for countless nights of chaos and laughter. And yes, you will still find a night where a single card changes the entire course of a game – that is the magic of magic.
