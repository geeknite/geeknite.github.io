## How to play in 60 seconds (the quick-start vibe)
If you want to teach this game in a single breath (or three quick rounds before your pizza arrives), here’s the gist:
- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a handful of cards to each player, set the status counters, and designate a first player. The box wants you to believe you’re about to launch a political campaign, but really you’re about to be dragged into a series of bluffing challenges and card-driven maneuvers.
- Core loop: On your turn, you’ll draw a card, play a card, or claim a power. Cards usually grant you influence, allow you to disrupt opponents, or force others to reveal a stance. The tension comes from balancing your options: do you push a bold move now or bide your time for a more advantageous moment?
- Trump mechanic: The ultimate trumping card is used to break ties or override an opponent’s claim, depending on the current variant you’re playing. Yes, you can feel like you’re negotiating in a tiny, satirical White House as you attempt to outsmart your friends with clever wording and the occasional cheeky insult.
- Endgame: The round eventually concludes when a player reaches a pre-set victory condition (points, influence tokens, or other consensus-based milestones defined in the rules). Victory, while satisfying, is often tempered by the knowledge that your political capital has just tanked in the eyes of your table.

This quick start is enough to get you into a round without a PhD in political science, which is exactly what the “Deluxe” edition wants: an approachable experience that rewards improv and social interaction more than strict arithmetic.
