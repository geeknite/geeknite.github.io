### Special cards and gameplay twists
UNO is a familiar battlefield: draw two, skip your turn, reverse the flow, wilds to choose colors. The MHA deck adds a thematic layer that is mostly cosmetic, but there are a few delightful touches that enhance the experience:
- Plus Ultra Draw Two: The classic Draw Two is rebranded with a hero catchphrase. When played, the opponent must draw two cards and skip their turn, all while a little “Plus Ultra” banner flashes across the center of the card.
- All Might Wild: Wild cards now come with a heroic glare and a power-up vibe. It allows the player to pick the color while invoking All Might s famous grin as a reminder that even in card games, the symbol of peace is watching.
- Heroic Interventions: A new variant on skip cards. When played, the next player must perform a small action decided at table, such as swapping hands with the person on their left or revealing their hand. It is light and fun, but not overly disruptive for casual nights.
- Villain Draw Four: The classic Wild Draw Four gets a villain theme nameplate and a pop of dramatic color. The swing here can be brutal, so plan your cards accordingly.

The rules remain UNO at heart. If you know how to play UNO, you know how to play these. The hero cards simply add flavor, a touch of story, and a few momentary tactical decisions that feel cinematic rather than strictly strategic. The objective stays the same: empty your hand first, avoid grabbing extra cards, and relish the occasional taunt about your opponent s “training montage.”
