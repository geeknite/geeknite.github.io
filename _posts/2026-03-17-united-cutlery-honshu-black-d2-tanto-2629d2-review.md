Edge retention on D2 is where the Honshu line tends to shine. A well-placed sharpening can bring back an edge that can slice through cardboard, rope, and light plastic with minimal effort. The tanto tip is robust for piercing tasks; if you’ve ever wished for a blade that can puncture a thick leather belt or a stubborn rope layer, the Honshu’s tip geometry is designed with that in mind.

Careful maintenance notes: keep the blade dry after use, wipe with a light coat of oil, and avoid prolonged exposure to moisture. D2 can rust if neglected, especially in saline or acidic soils, so a simple wipe-down is your best friend.

## Maintenance and care
- Clean after use, especially if the blade has touched moisture, salt, or acidic materials.
- Wipe dry, then apply a light protective oil to prevent rust on D2.
- Keep the sheath clean and dry to prolong blade life.
- If you store for long periods, consider a light oil coating and a silica gel pack in the sheath to reduce moisture.

D2 steel is a reliability workhorse when cared for; it’s not stainless steel and will require some maintenance if used in humid or harsh environments. The payoff is edge retention and durability, which many knife enthusiasts value.
