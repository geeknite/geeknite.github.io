### USB ports and power outlets: charging without chaos
Integrated USB ports at the desk edge are a nice touch for a workstation where cables always seem to gravitate toward your keyboard and monitor cable spaghetti. The desk includes two USB-A ports and two USB-C ports, which is a sensible mix for charging phones, wireless earbuds, or a streaming camera without having to lean over to reach a wall charger. Two built-in power outlets with surge protection provide the necessary backup when you juggle a second monitor, a printer, or a charging station for all the corporate swag you’ve collected. ## Maintenance, care, and long-term thoughts
A melamine-coated surface is relatively forgiving. Wipe with a damp cloth, avoid harsh chemical cleaners, and don’t scratch with abrasive objects. Cables should be routed through the channels and kept tidy with the included Velcro straps to prevent cable tangling, especially if you frequently rearrange your desk layout. The LED strip edges are flexible but stickier adhesives can degrade over time if exposed to heat and humidity; eventually you might want to replace the strip or reapply a new adhesive if you notice a flicker in the glow.

If you’re worried about weight limits, you’ll be glad to know the platform can handle a substantial load from multiple monitors, a speaker, and some desk toys. The drawers’ metal slides hold a surprising amount of light office gear without wobble, though you’ll want to distribute weight evenly across the surface to maintain the visual balance. The warranty (where applicable) should cover manufacturing defects; for normal wear and tear, you’ll probably be happy with the desk’s longevity given proper care.
