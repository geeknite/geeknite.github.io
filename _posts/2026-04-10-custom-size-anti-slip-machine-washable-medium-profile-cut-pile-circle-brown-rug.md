## Care and Maintenance
The beauty of machine-washable design is not just convenience; it’s peace of mind. When the inevitable spill happens—whether it’s red juice, coffee, or the slightly mysterious coffee-stain you discovered after a long night—the rug is designed to survive a proper wash cycle. To keep things fresh, follow general care instructions: cold water on tumble or gentle cycle, a mild detergent that doesn’t boost dye migration, and air-drying flat to preserve the circle’s shape.
When washing a custom-sized rug, you may need to roll or fold it carefully to fit your machine. An oversized rug—if your machine can handle it—will be better washed in smaller loads or with a companion rug. If you’re dealing with a stubborn stain, treat it with a gentle pre-soak, then launder as directed. Avoid high-heat drying; heat can shrink fibers, distort pile height, and set stains deeper into the fabric.
Vacuuming is still useful for daily maintenance. A standard vacuum with a gentle setting will pick up dust and pet hair without flattening the pile or dragging fibers out of the backing. If your rug has longer fibers in the cut-pile, a quick pass with a hand-held vacuum or a low-suction can prevent pilling.
