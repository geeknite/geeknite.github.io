### First Run and Tuning
Once assembled, the first ball run is a moment of truth. If the rods drag or the ball rebounds in a weird arc, you may need to tighten a couple of set screws or recheck the bearing alignment. The good news is that the table has a forgiving tolerance range; minor misalignments usually resolve after a couple of test games. Pro tip: have a friend mark the ball positions during the first few plays so you don’t get distracted by the table’s glossy finish and forget you are actually playing a game of foosball.

Durability is the name of the game for a table of this size. The cabinet is sturdy enough to survive the occasional accidental bump from a chair that wandered into the playing space at 2 AM.
