Overview

## What Fortune Row Adds to City of Mist
Fortune Row isn’t just a location expansion; it’s a design philosophy upgrade. The district is built to feel like a living organism—half city guide, half crime epic, half fashion show in which all the models are wearing masks and all the outfits are suspiciously expensive. The book breaks down into a few core pillars:

- A richly described district with districts, factions, and micro-odysseys that can seed an entire campaign arc.
- A new set of Power Tags and Mythos Options tailored to Fortune Row’s vibe: glitz, corruption, and a strange blend of organized crime with supernatural flair.
- A toolkit for running long-form investigations with a focus on social maneuvering, leverage, and the “is this a trap or a test?” gameplay loop that City of Mist players adore.
- Narrative prompts and NPCs that feel distinct but carry familiar echoes of noir archetypes — the fixer, the informant, the star-crossed diva, and the shadow broker, all wearing their own masks and telling you to choose your dice, or your loyalties, wisely.

As you dive into Fortune Row, you’ll notice the design is less “static setting” and more “dynamic character.” The district isn’t merely a backdrop; it’s an engine for stories that can twist and snap with a season’s worth of episodes. This matters because City of Mist thrives when players feel the world is actively responding to their choices— Fortune Row uses its NPCs, its venues, and its rituals to do just that.

## Inside Fortunes Row: What’s in the Box (and on the Table)

### District Overview and Key Venues
Fortune Row gives you a map that feels lived-in and slightly nervous. There are clubs where patrons wear more masks than the performers, pawnshops that deal in supernatural curios, and a bank whose vault is rumored to be more than a vault—perhaps a gateway. The venues aren’t just set dressing; they’re puzzle pieces that can be rearranged for different campaigns. Each location includes quick hooks, scene seeds, and a handful of secrets that can drive multiple sessions. One of the book’s delights is how it toys with your expectations: a seemingly glamorous gala might become the scene for a heist, a revelation, or a ritual work that has consequences far beyond the room.

### Factions and Power Brokers
Fortune Row leans into a factional terrain that feels spicy but not overbearing. You’ll meet the usual suspects—the movers, the shakers, the masked savants—plus a few new faces with genuinely fresh angles. The writing gives you a motive for each faction that’s easy to grasp and hard to outsmart in a single session. This is crucial because City of Mist games reward players who listen as much as those who talk. Fortune Row nails that balance: factions feel dangerous, non-trivial, and deliciously capable of escalating conflict with only a few well-placed lines of dialogue.

### Mythos and Power Tags Reimagined
Power Tags in Fortune Row aren’t just a list of abilities; they’re a language you’ll use at the table to describe a scene’s tone and danger. The new tags are thematically aligned with Fortune Row’s glamour-and-grit vibe, which means you’ll often find yourself narrating with more texture and color than you did in the Core Book. If you enjoyed building a scene around a single symbol in the base game, you’ll love how Fortune Row invites you to layer symbols, totem-like motifs, and ritualized actions into a single moment. It’s a party trick that makes the game feel more cinematic without forcing you into a predefined script.

### Rituals, Clues, and the Art of the Twist
Rituals are where Fortune Row shines. There’s a careful balance here: rituals feel meaningful and consequential, but they’re not so opaque that players give up trying. Clues are presented in a way that invites deduction without slowing the pace to a crawl. The ritual mechanics benefit sessions where the party doesn’t simply “beat the boss” but navigates a web of relationships, leverage, and moral risk. If your table loves the old-school investigative grind with a modern noir shimmy, Fortune Row serves it with a cherry on top and a mask long enough to hide the host’s grin.

### Artwork and Presentation
The production value is sturdy, with art that communicates mood and theme without getting in the way of play. This is a setting that respects the players’ time; you’ll find that the art supports, rather than dictates, the tone, which is exactly what a GM wants when you’re juggling multiple threads across a cadenza of sessions. The layout is clean, the chase scenes are legible, and the “look into the shadows” moments land with punch.

## Mechanics Deep Dive: How Fortune Row Feels at the Table

### A Quick Recap of Core City of Mist Mechanics
If you’ve played the Core Book, you know the drill: players summon Mythos and Power Tags to influence scenes, you negotiate with the world as a reflection of your identity, and you resolve outcomes with a mix of roleplay and dice. Fortune Row leans into this by adding tags and rituals that are tuned to the district’s flavor. You’ll still be rolling with the same tempo, but you’ll find new ways to cut through tension with style rather than brute force.

### New Tags and How They Shift Scenes
The new tags aren’t merely cosmetic. They provide thematic triggers for scene beats, which means you’ll be nudged into noir-appropriate interactions—bluffing, bartering leverage, or turning an ally into a liability for a better narrative payoff. The mechanics encourage players to lean into secrets—yours, theirs, and the city’s—and to treat every reveal as a potential catalyst for the next escalation.

### Group Play and Long-Form Campaigns
Fortune Row is designed with long-form campaigns in mind. The district’s layers encourage multi-session arcs with rotating focal points, so it’s feasible to let the city breathe a little between “big reveals.” The book also provides a structured approach to pacing a season around twists, betrayals, and social-power plays. If your table loves slow-burn intrigue, Fortune Row gives you enough rope to tie yourselves into a few very dramatic knots.

## GM Toolkit: Running Fortune Row Like a Pro

### Session Structure and Flow
Fortune Row offers a modular structure: you can run a stand-alone session that uses a single venue or spin an arc using multiple sites and factions. The book includes ready-to-run scenes and a selection of “harbingers” (foreshadowing sequences) that can be dropped into any ongoing campaign. The GM kit includes: scene seeds, NPC stats, social-momentum rules, and a clear pathway to escalate stakes without needing a full rewrite every time players pivot.

### NPCs You’ll Actually Want at the Table
The NPCs in Fortune Row aren’t throwaway. They have agendas, quirks, and a mask that tells you more about who they are than their business card ever could. Some NPCs are misdirection machines, others are possible allies who might do the wrong thing for the right reasons, and a few are just tragically funny in a way that makes you root for their schemes even when you know you shouldn’t. The writers do a good job of balancing charm with danger, so your players won’t dismiss them as background color.

### Adventure Seeds and Scene Hooks
There are generous handfuls of seeds you can drop into a session: a gala with a ring of misdirection, a heist that’s both a heist and a ritual, a rumor that spirals into a city-wide conspiracy. The seeds are designed to be stitched into your ongoing plot threads, not forced as a one-and-done puzzle. If you enjoy improvising with a bag of beans, Fortune Row’s seeds are the perfect pantry to raid.

### NPC Quick-Reference Cheat Sheets
To speed up game time, Fortune Row includes quick-reference pages that summarize each major NPC’s motive, mask, and possible alignments. These cheat sheets help you keep track of who’s lying to whom and who might be lying to themselves. It’s a small thing, but in a game that rewards conversation, it’s the kind of thing you’ll appreciate when your table is flailing toward the next dramatic moment.

## Thematic Fit: Fortune Row vs Core Book

### Tone and Style Alignment
Fortune Row retains City of Mist’s dual obsession with identity and power, but it leans further into the street-level glamor of a masked metropolis. If the Core Book felt like a tour through a neon labyrinth, Fortune Row feels like the insistent hum you hear when you stand in the heart of a crowded nightclub—complex, luminous, and a touch dangerous. The tone remains accessible; you won’t need a PhD in noir theory to enjoy it, but you’ll come away wanting to discuss metaphorical masks at length. It’s a good fit for groups who crave social intrigue and character-driven plots more than a pure “beat-the-boss” grind.

### Play Experience Compared to Core
In play, Fortune Row tends to reward patient players who listen to the city’s whispers. The core mechanics are not dramatically altered; instead, players gain new ways to influence outcomes through social leverage, ritualized action, and the symbolic currency of masks. The net result is a more cinematic, more orchestrated feel without sacrificing the spontaneous drama that makes City of Mist so intoxicating. Some groups may find the shift toward ritual and faction politics slower-paced, but the payoff—dramatic reveals with real consequences—can be spectacular.

## Production Values and Accessibility

Fortune Row shines in its writing and layout. The district map is readable, the NPC entries are compact but flavorful, and the scene hooks don’t require a thesaurus to interpret. The art, while not groundbreaking, effectively communicates mood, tension, and noir glamour. The book doesn’t pretend to be a quick-start rulebook; it’s a book that invites you to live in the city for a while, to learn its corners, and to trust that the next clue will be found in a velvet-lined pocket of some velvet-voiced informant.

If you’re a GM who loves to plan but also loves to improvise, Fortune Row provides a balanced toolkit. The modular approach means you can swap in venues and factions as your campaign evolves, without breaking the narrative fabric you’ve woven with your players. It’s a moving puzzle—in the best possible way—that invites collaboration, conjecture, and a little bit of predestined bad luck for dramatic effect.

## Reader’s Guide: Who Should Pick Up Fortune Row?

If you’re already invested in the City of Mist system and you want to run longer, more politically entangled campaigns, Fortune Row is a compelling purchase. It’s ideal for players who love social maneuvering, character-driven moments, and the thrill of a city that feels like a living organism with a thousand masks waiting to be pulled off (or pinned to a wall for a dramatic reveal). It’s also a great bridge for groups who found the Core Book’s urban noir vibes a touch abstract. Fortune Row grounds the conceit in a vivid, tangible location with a clear narrative throughline. It’s not a replacement for the Core Book, but it’s a powerful companion that expands your storytelling potential without requiring you to abandon the foundations that made the game work in the first place.

For fans of cross-media noir and pulpy crime in a magical city, Fortune Row gives you more texture, more faces, and more opportunities to push your characters toward decisions that aren’t merely about who wins the fight but who claims the city’s memory after the final curtain.

Conclusion and Recommendation
Fortune Row is a strong addition to City of Mist for GMs who want depth, mood, and multi-session arcs that revolve around power, secrets, and the city’s many facades. It respects the core system while offering a richer palette for storytelling. If you’ve been craving a more intricate social-drama vibe with the neon edge you already love, you’ll likely be satisfied, perhaps even delighted. It’s not a flawless upgrade—the pacing can feel slower for groups that want constant action, and some players may miss the punch of pure combat scenarios—but the payoff in narrative resonance is substantial. It’s a well-curated, thematically coherent enrichment that makes the City of Mist world feel more immersive, more dangerous, and more fun to roleplay in.

Final thoughts: Fortune Row doesn’t replace the core game, but it does make the city feel more personal and more dangerous, which is exactly the kind of thing that keeps a campaign from slipping into comfy mediocrity. If you’re ready to lean into the masks and dance with the city’s hidden hands, Fortune Row is the upgrade you didn’t know you needed.
