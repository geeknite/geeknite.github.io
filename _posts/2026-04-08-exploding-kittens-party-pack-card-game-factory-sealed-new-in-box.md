Gameplay basics and how to play with a crowd
------------------------------------------------
The Party Pack scales up from the core game to accommodate bigger crews. The basic loop remains simple: players take turns drawing cards, hoping to avoid an Exploding Kitten, and using Defuse cards to cheat the explosion long enough to keep your seat in the circle. The power of the Defuse card is that it buys you a single chance of survival, but there are plenty of ways your opponents will try to steal your fate through Nope cards, See the Future glimpses, and the occasional Attack card that forces someone else to draw twice in a row.

The core mechanic in a party setting is speed. You’ll be shuffling, drawing, discovering whether you were lucky or foolish, and reacting with cat-like reflexes to others’ plays. The Party Pack doesn’t require an in-depth tutorial; it’s designed for quick onboarding and a social atmosphere where everyone can lean into the silliness. If you’re a first-timer, here are a few tips to get you started:
- Start with 7 Defuse cards in the deck for a fair chance to dodge a few kittens in a row.
- Keep the pace high; the more cards you reveal in a short period, the more laughter and chaos you generate.
- Use See the Future as a way to gather information—but remember, you can only see one turn ahead in most versions, so don’t get cocky.

Card types and their personalities
---------------------------------
- Exploding Kitten: The dramatic nuclear option. Draw this and you’re out unless you hold Defuse. In a party setting, it’s the moment when someone yells “Not again!” and everyone pretends to be a calm, strategic mastermind.
- Defuse: Your lifeline. Use this to stop the explosion and flip the game back toward a possible victory. It’s the moderator of mayhem, offering a brief moment of relief before chaos resumes.
- Nope: The universal veto. It can stop opponents’ actions, but you’ll need to time it like a comedian timing a punchline: when it lands, it lands hard.
- See the Future: A sneaky peek into the next few cards. It’s not fortune-telling; it’s a reminder that in this game, fate can tilt in a single reveal.
- Attack/Shuffle/Other Action Cards: These perform a variety of chaos-enforcing tasks. Use them to accelerate rounds, shift leverage, and push your friends toward the edge of rational thought.
