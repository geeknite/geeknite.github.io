## Maintenance and care
A good saw is a patient saw, and a patient saw is a well-maintained saw. Here is a succinct maintenance checklist you can slap onto the workshop wall:

- Daily: wipe down surfaces, inspect blade for nicks, check tension and alignment
- Weekly: lubricate moving parts per manufacturer instructions, check guard alignment, ensure splash guard is clean
- Monthly: check electrical cord for wear, inspect stand and feet for wobble, verify emergency stop functionality
- Quarterly: blade replacement schedule depending on usage, sanitize thoroughly, inspect seals and bearings for wear

Lubrication is often the most overlooked element. Use a food-grade lubricant on moving steel parts where allowed by the manufacturer. Do not grease the blade teeth. It is a quick way to ruin your meat and your taste buds. Also, never run the machine dry or without its guard in place; these are not only safety rules but also tips for longer blade life and less maintenance drama.

For storage, keep the unit in a dry area with low humidity and away from corrosive cleaners. A small mute button for the shop’s gossiping could be a nice optional accessory, but you probably do not need it here. The machine is designed to be durable; treat it well and it will treat you back with years of dependable service.
