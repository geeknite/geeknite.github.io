## Care, Longevity, and Everyday Durability

Caring for decks is a surprisingly important part of the long-term experience. Here are a few practical tips to keep these two decks in top shape for as long as you want to pretend you are the card table’s designated adult:

- Keep them away from moisture and heat. Cards hate humidity and heat; it makes stock warp and glue fail. After all, you don’t want to cause structural abnormalities that lead to a suspiciously high rate of card pops during shuffles.
- Store in a cool, dry place inside their sealed tuck. The seal is not just for novelty; it preserves the freshness of the stock, the gloss level of the finish, and the little pieces of magic that give you that satisfying snap when you riffle.
- Avoid bending or forcing cards. If you try to bend a card, you’ll know instantly that you’ve crossed the line where nostalgia meets mechanical failure. Treat them with the respect they deserve and they’ll treat you to long sessions of dependable shuffles.

With normal use, these decks should last a long time. If you’re a heavy user who shuffles aggressively, you’ll still get a good run — you just might see some minor edge wear years down the line. That’s a small price to pay for a deck that remains reliable when you host a tournament-night vibe in your living room.
