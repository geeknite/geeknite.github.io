## The Basics

### What is Modern Art: The Card Game?

Modern Art: The Card Game is a strategic card game where players take on the role of art dealers competing to amass the most valuable collection of modern art. Designed by the legendary Reiner Knizia, this game captures the essence of art auctions, allowing players to bid on, sell, and influence the value of various artworks.

### Components

The game comes with:

- **Cards**: Beautifully illustrated cards representing different pieces of modern art, each with its own unique value.
- **Tokens**: Used to track the value of artworks and players' finances.
- **Game Board**: A simple yet elegant board that serves as the auction house.

**Pro Tip**: Don't let your artistic cat near the cards. Those claws are the real art critics.

## How to Play

### Setup

Setting up Modern Art: The Card Game is like curating your own art exhibition. It requires careful planning and maybe a snack break (or two). Lay out the game board, distribute cards and tokens, and prepare for an auction.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, players take on the role of auctioneer, putting one artwork up for bid. The other players bid for the artwork, trying to get the best deal. The auctioneer can choose different types of auctions, such as open auctions, sealed auctions, or fixed-price sales. The goal is to amass the most valuable collection and influence the market to your advantage.

### Winning and Losing

To win, players must have the most valuable collection of artworks by the end of the game. This requires strategic bidding, clever bluffing, and a keen eye for market trends. If you end up with a collection of art that’s worth less than your lunch money, it’s game over.

**Pro Tip**: Always keep an eye on your opponents' collections. Sometimes the best strategy is to make them overpay for a masterpiece.
