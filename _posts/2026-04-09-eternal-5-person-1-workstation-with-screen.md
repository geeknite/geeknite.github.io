## Ergonomics and comfort: one size does not fit all

Five workstations mean five sets of bodies, five different ergonomics. The Eternal 5 Person 1 Workstation addresses this by providing multiple adjustment points and a high degree of surface customization. Let’s break down the ergonomic considerations:

- Chair compatibility: The surface height offers a stable platform for most standard ergonomic chairs. If you’re inside a formal office environment that mandates sit-stand chairs, you’ll want to check your chairs’ base clearance; 730 mm of height might be a snug fit for taller users with bulky leg rests. The top line: you can set it and forget it, but individual users will still want their chair height tuned to their own leg length.
- Monitor height: With a central screen wall or individual mounts, you’ll be able to position each monitor at eye height, reducing neck strain during intense debugging marathons or long design reviews. It’s worth investing in adjustable arms for every screen and testing your posture after a week of use.
- Keyboard and mouse zones: The long surface allows for wrist-friendly keyboard trays or split keyboard setups. If your team favors mechanical keyboards or ergonomic mice, plenty of space remains for wrist rests and anti-fatigue mats. Pro-tip: consider a soft, non-slip mat under each keyboard to minimize shifts during tense sprint reviews.
- Desk surface material: The laminate surface should feel responsive under wrist movement but isn’t so slick that you’ll lose a mouse every few minutes. If you’re using a dramatic gliding ball of a mouse, you’ll appreciate a slightly textured finish that offers tactile feedback without sticking to the palm.

A no-nonsense approach to ergonomics is essential here: you want a space that encourages good posture for all five users without forcing them into shared compromises. This is the kind of desk that lets you address ergonomics in a modular, practical way rather than forcing everyone into a one-size-fits-all chair arrangement. If you’re serious about long-term comfort during multi-user sessions, you’ll probably want to run a quick ergonomic assessment after a couple of weeks of usage and adjust the setup accordingly.

## Cable management and power strategy: keeping chaos at bay

One of the trickiest parts of any large workstation is the cable ecosystem. With a 9-meter surface, you’ll be dealing with Power over Ethernet, USB-C hubs, monitor cables, audio, and perhaps a few external devices that like to live on the desk. The Eternal desk includes integrated cable channels, which is a godsend, but you’ll still want a disciplined approach to cabling.

- Start with a central power distribution unit (PDU) that matches your region’s outlets and surge protection needs. This keeps all the power connections in one predictable place rather than rat-holing across the room.
- Route data cables through the surface’s dedicated channels to minimize wear and reduce snagging risks. If you’re using a central screen wall, ensure the data cables won’t tug when someone leans or drags a chair past the workspace edge.
- Use color-coded bundles to differentiate the five users’ devices. It’s small but mighty for preventing the “why does my monitor signal flip every time someone passes by?” moment at 2 a.m.
- Consider a wireless charging pad in the surface for a few essential devices. A good balance of wired reliability and optional wireless convenience is the dream here.

In practice, cable management becomes a ritual more than a technical problem. The desk gives you the architecture; the team fills in the details. If you can invest in a solid cable management plan, you’ll protect both the appearance and the longevity of the setup. A neat desk is a productive desk—your future self will thank you when you’re not untangling a forest of cables before a team stand-up.
