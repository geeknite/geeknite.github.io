## Assembly and installation: the inevitable rite of passage

Let’s not pretend that assembling a four-person, double-sided workstation is what you do on a lazy Sunday when you want to test your assembly skills. It is. The good news is that the Line Prime kit usually ships with clear instructions and all required hardware. The steps are straightforward but expect a small team and a couple of hours if you want to do things without bursts of profanity and repeated head-scratching.

Pro tips for assembly:
- Organize hardware before you start. Label the screws, washers, and brackets like you’re assembling a tiny modular spaceship rather than a desk. It saves time and reduces the risk of discovering you’ve built a surface with the wrong leg alignment mid-assembly.
- Enlist a buddy with a measuring tape and a common sense of balance. This thing is long, and you’ll be surprised how easy it is to accidentally tilt while tightening the last bracket.
- Do a dry-fit before final assembly. Place the top surfaces, legs, and central panel in place to confirm alignment. If something doesn’t click, you haven’t cross-threaded fate—it’s just a misalignment. Loosen, reposition, and try again.
- Cable management takes planning. If you can, pre-route cables through channels or under-desk trays. This avoids a “snake pit” effect when you’re trying to mount power strips and keep your space tidy.

## Maintenance, durability, and care

Beech veneer is beautiful but not invincible. Maintain it with routine care like you would with a good wooden kitchen surface:
- Wipe with a soft, damp cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can scratch the finish.
- Use coasters for drinks and avoid placing hot items directly on the surface. Yes, we all know coffee loves the veneer and that’s a romance we don’t want to encourage.
- Clean up spills promptly to minimize staining. If a stubborn stain forms, a gentle cleaner recommended by the manufacturer is wise.
- Periodically check bolts and brackets. A quick tighten here and there keeps the desk from developing a wobbly reputation.

With proper care, the Line Prime Beech 1800mm desk can look good for years. It won’t be a “one-year wonder” if you treat it like a long-term investment in your workspace feng shui. The double-surface design also helps distribute wear more evenly than a single long surface would, assuming your team isn’t auditioning for a desk-turned-ninja-course during lunch.
