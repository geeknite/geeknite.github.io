## Card Design and Foil Quality: Does Extra Holo Deliver?
Foil quality is a two-sided coin in the hobby: the shiny side that attracts your gaze, and the maintenance side that traps fingerprints and dust. The Extra Holo variant for Kamehameha Goku aims to deliver a premium visual payoff without sacrificing readability or durability—two things that can be at odds in high-foil cards.

### The art and color palette
Goku’s energy aura is rendered with the bright yellows and blues you’d expect from a classic Kamehameha portrait, but the holo treatment adds a prism-like shimmer that makes the energy feel more like a plasma storm than a flat card. The artist often places emphasis on speed lines and a blurred background to convey motion, which translates nicely in holo form; the light catches the edges and throws little rainbows across your sleeve.

### Foil fidelity and consistency
Consistency across three copies is a real win here. In some sets, holo copies can vary slightly from card to card due to stacking or foil alignment; in FS01’s Kamehameha Goku trio, you’ll notice a similar sheen, with no obvious misprints or miscuts on the main surface. The foil texture sits smoothly on the card, with a minimal amount of roughness where the foil meets the card stock—an unfortunate byproduct of heavy foiling, but not a deal-breaker if you’re after the “wow” factor rather than a pristine gym-class-glitter effect.

### Readability under light
If you’re planning to show these off on a camera rig or at a tournament, you’ll want to be mindful of reflections. The holo’s glare can obscure the text if you tilt the card aggressively toward a bright light, but that’s standard for holo cards across many TCGs. The card text remains legible in typical play angles, which is a relief for folks who worry about their hands going all tsundere when a card’s backlight reveals everything.

## Collectability, Scarcity, and Market Pulse
Foil cards in collector circles occupy a curious niche: they’re more expensive than their non-foil counterparts, but the pricing is often more about desirability than strictly about utility in play. FS01’s Extra Holo line sits in a sweet spot for many collectors who crave display-worthy cards that still fit neatly into a binder, a shelf, or a trusted deck box.

### Rarity and demand
- Rarity: Typically labeled as an “Extra Holo,” which is a variant of the base foil treatment.
- Demand: High among foil chasers and Kamehameha enthusiasts. As with many holo sets, demand spikes when new collectors join the DBS scene or when competitive players decide to feature a holo centerpiece in a display piece.
- Investment value: Not a guaranteed ROI, but a credible long-tail collectible. If you’re in it for the long game, these copies won’t likely drop to obscurity quickly, simply due to the visual appeal and the chase-factor.

### Care and long-term display value
Foil cards are best stored flat and protected from prolonged direct sunlight. Ultraviolet exposure can degrade color vibrancy and foil integrity over many years. For those who want to show off the trio, consider a glass display case with ambient lighting that reduces glare without washing out the cards. When storing, keep them in upright, acid-free sleeves, ideally as part of a well-labeled FS01 subsection of your binder. For the display-minded, a small light behind the cards caught at a distance can create a stage-like effect that makes your Kamehameha Goku trio look like they’re about to strike a dramatic pose in a low-budget anime opening.

## Care, Display, and Collector Tips
- Use high-quality inner sleeves and a top-loader that fits standard cards to prevent edge wear.
- Keep away from magnetic or phone-based UV amusements; foils don’t love freestyle sunbathing.
- Photograph with diffuse lighting for social media; direct flash often ruins the holo glow by creating harsh reflections.
- Consider routine rotation: swapping these three into and out of your binder can keep your collection feeling fresh and exciting.
- Store upright or flat to preserve the card’s structural integrity; avoid bending or warping, which can alter foiling alignment and readability over time.
