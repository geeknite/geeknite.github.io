## How to Play: Quick Rules Overview
### Setup
Shuffle the deck, place holiday tokens in the center, deal each player a starting hand, and pick a starting player. The Holiday Edition adds a couple of new card types that reference seasonal events, so you’ll want to skim the introduction to understand how special abilities trigger. The rules are short enough to not require a law degree, but there’s enough nuance to keep you from simply grinning and blindly drawing cards like a caffeinated Spongebob.

### Turn Structure
On your turn, you draw a card, maybe play one or more cards from your hand, and navigate the ever-shifting tidal wave of effects that propagate around the table. The key mechanic remains: you’re trying to avoid accumulating a penalty—think of it as tripping over your own holiday warmth while others secretly cheer you on from behind a cookie platter.

### The Holiday Twist
This edition introduces special cards tied to seasonal chaos: Frosty Freeze, Merry Mishap, and Eggnog Gambit, among others. These cards modify turn order, force discards, or swap duties between players in ways that echo a chaotic family gathering. The net effect is a game that feels like a holiday tradition that forgot to consult the calendar first—and that is exactly the vibe the designers were aiming for.

### How Cards Interact
Certain cards punish you for bad draws; others reward you for clever misdirection. The core strategy is balancing aggression and restraint. Do you burn a high-impact action now to disrupt the table, or hold your powder for a grand finale when immunity tokens are scarce? The Holiday Edition rewards you for watching your tablemates’ tells—the way they reach for their drink, the way they laugh after a pun, the way they flush when a Santa card hits the table.
