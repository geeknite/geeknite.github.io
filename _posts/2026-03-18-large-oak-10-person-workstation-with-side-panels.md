Assembly and installation
Assembly of a 10-person workstation is not a weekend DIY Netflix binge. It’s a two-person or three-person job with moderate tool requirements and a willingness to wrestle a box monster named “Skrewmore” into alignment.

Prep
- Clear a large, flat workspace
- Check all panels and hardware against the packing list
- Have a tape measure and a level at hand

Steps (high level)
1) Build the base rows: assemble the frames and attach the horizontal desk surfaces, ensuring the frame is square with a level on each corner.
2) Attach side panels: align panels with the desk edge and fasten with the included hardware. Don’t over-tighten; you’ll want some give during alignment.
3) Connect power and cable management: install the central power hub (if chosen) and run the cables through the pre-cut channels. Label cables so future you doesn’t have to play “Find the USB-C.”
4) Fine-tune alignment: go station by station to ensure consistent gaps, level surfaces, and a clean run of cables beneath each desk.
5) Final checks: confirm the entire row is level, secure, and wired correctly. Sit at each station and pretend you’re the team’s lead dev to test ergonomics.

Notes on assembly
- This is not a one-person job; you’ll be much happier with a helper, two if possible.
- The hardware is robust, but you’ll want a rubber mallet or a gentle hammer to nudge panels into place without denting the veneer.
- If you opt for the power hub, plan wiring routes in advance to avoid a cable jungle.
