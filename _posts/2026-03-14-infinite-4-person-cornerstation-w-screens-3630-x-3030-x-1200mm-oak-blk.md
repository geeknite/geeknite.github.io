## The unboxing and first impressions

From the moment the packaging arrives, you realize this is not your average ready-to-assemble furniture. There are panels, brackets, screws, Allen keys that seem to exist solely to remind you of your favorite Swedish epics, and a manual printed in both genius and cautionary phrases. The assembly time is real, with assembly that rewards patience and penalizes impatience. Donning protective gloves is optional but recommended; your fingertips will thank you for the extra grip as you negotiate the alignment of the panel rails with the pre-drilled holes.

The oak blk finish is a treat. The grain of the oak offers warmth without the sticky sweetness of a pine-scented candle. The black framing provides contrast that makes the entire construction look more like a studio setup than a typical office piece. The privacy screens are a nice addition, not only for visual separation but also for a touch of acoustic control in a room that isn't all softly padded walls.

## Layout, dimensions, and space planning

The 3630 mm length allows four people to sit side-by-side with a dedicated corner for shared tasks, yet the arrangement still leaves a central walkway for the occasional team huddle. The 3030 mm depth is generous. It accommodates two monitors per user if you want to maximize digital output, or a mix of one ultrawide and a couple of monitors for a maximum multitasking experience. The 1200 mm height is comfortable for most chairs, though if your building uses tall ceilings or if you prefer standing, you might want to add a sit-stand converter at each station.

One thing to consider is the orientation of the corner. If your room has known traffic patterns, you may want to configure the corner to face the less busy area or toward the window to leverage natural light. Our tests suggest that a little sunlight can improve mood, which is a nice side effect of splitting up your space into four distinct workstations under one canopy of privacy screens.

## Ergonomics and comfort

Ergonomics is the quiet hero you discover only after you spend a day in less-than-ergonomic setups. The Infinite cornerstation supports standard office chairs with typical adjustability: seat height, backrest angle, and armrest position. The monitors can be mounted on arms, which is essential if you want to avoid cranking your neck to look at two monitors at 90 degrees. We found that the screens reach an optimal height with a slight tilt, reducing neck strain after marathon email sessions.

The surface of the desk is wide enough to allow for key placement of keyboard and mouse with space left for a notebook or a favorite snack. We tested with four keyboards, four mice, and four cups of coffee, all of which coexisted without driving the desk into a panic. The privacy screens contribute to a calmer environment by limiting direct lines of sight. It is not a soundproof suite, but it does a nice job of damping typical office chatter and the occasional loud keyboard click that would otherwise become your neighbor's soundtrack.

## Cable management and power strategy

For a four-person layout, the cable jungle is a natural hazard. The cornerstation includes a central cable tray and some routing options for power and data. The idea is to get the cords off the floor and tucked away, which reduces the risk of someone tripping or your cat thinking the desk is a new playground. We recommended planning the power strip location in advance and labeling cables to avoid the classic 'mystery cable' problem when someone changes devices mid-week.

We placed a mix of USB-C hubs, docking stations, and HDMI/DisplayPort lines across the stations. The result was a clean but dense under-desk space that required some discipline to maintain. If your team grows or adds more devices, you may want to consider smart cable management accessories or a secondary channel that sits above the primary tray for extra bandwidth. ## Privacy, acoustics, and collaboration dynamics

The screens don’t just block sightlines; they alter the acoustic character of the space. In practice, you’ll notice a modest drop in adjacent chatter. It isn’t magical soundproofing; if you want quiet on demand, you still need to go to a proper meeting room. But as a backdrop for team collaboration, the screens foster a sense of personal space that reduces social fatigue. The net effect is a room that feels like it can host four separate tasks simultaneously without turning into a noisy stadium.

If you’re worried about isolation, you can remove or reconfigure screens to create more open collaboration zones. The modularity is a major strength here; you can re-run the same setup for a different team composition or reorganize for a process shift without tearing out walls or purchasing a new desk every six months.

## Storage and accessories

Underneath and around the stations there is room for storage modules, drawer units, or monitor arms. The architecture of the cornerstation is friendly to accessories that help you keep the workspace in order. You can add a small filing drawer, a headphone cradle, or a compact storage shelf for manuals and spare cables. The key is to plan for four independent workspaces that still feel part of a single object rather than four separate desks jammed into a corner.

We tried a few configurations to see how much you can customize before hitting the limit. The answer depends on the modular kits you choose, but the base design offers enough flexibility for most four-person teams. You’ll find yourself adjusting the distribution of devices to balance weight and balance ideas—one seat gets a heavier monitor base; another gets a secondary docking station. It’s a small, satisfying little puzzle that beats reorganizing a single long desk four times a year.

## Durability, testing, and maintenance tips

Durability is about the long game. The oak veneer will hold up to daily use, as long as you wipe down the surface and avoid sharp abrasive cleaners. The hardware is robust, and the joints stay tight after repeated assembly and disassembly. If your office environment has frequent relocations, the cornerstation’s modular approach shines: you can move it as a unit or break it into smaller sections, depending on space and teamwork needs. Remember to check the hardware occasionally; a quick tighten here and there can prevent wobble creep over time.

Maintenance tips: use a microfiber cloth for the surface, avoid harsh chemical cleaners, and treat the screens with a gentle wipe if they accumulate dust. The privacy panels, in particular, can gather fingerprints; a microfiber dampened with a light cleaner can restore their matte finish nicely. If you encounter glare, reposition the desk orientation or add a monitor hood for critical tasks.
