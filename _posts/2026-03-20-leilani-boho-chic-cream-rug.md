## Cleaning, Care, and Longevity: Keeping It Fresh

### Daily and Weekly Care

Vacuuming is your best friend here. Use a standard vacuum with a gentle setting to remove dust and lint. The goal is to prevent dirt from embedding into the fibers rather than blasting it out with a power washer of a vacuum—this is not a cartridge-cleaning challenge. For everyday spills, blot rather than rub; this minimizes staining and helps maintain the pattern’s crispness. Mild detergents are fine for spot-cleaning; test a small inconspicuous patch first, because you don’t want your boho chic rug to become a patchwork experiment in color chemistry.

### Stain Management

Spills happen. Coffee, wine, and smoothie disasters can occur on a rug with creamy undertones, so you’ll want to act quickly. Blot with a clean cloth to remove as much liquid as possible, then consider a gentle cleaner suitable for synthetic fibers. If you’re dealing with stubborn stains, consult the warranty and consider professional cleaning if needed. With proper care, the rug should resist dulling and maintain its soft hand.

### Longevity and Rough Handling

The Leilani is designed to handle regular wear, but it’s not indestructible. Heavy furniture footprints can leave indentations, and high-traffic corridors may show wear more quickly. Rotating the rug every few months can help distribute wear evenly and preserve the uniformity of the pattern. If you own chairs with wheels, consider a protective rug pad to reduce friction and prolong the life of the backing. A simple pad also helps keep the rug in place on smooth floors, reducing the “walkway wobble” during movie nights.
