## How to play: core mechanics explained
Card Sharks thrives on a few core principles: reveal, bet, bluff, and score. The show influences are clear but the board game version makes them legible for a tabletop audience. A typical round includes a reveal of a card, a decision on your bet, and a moment of suspense when the next card is revealed. The combination of luck and strategy creates moments that feel cinematic even without a TV audience. You will cheer for good luck and you will grimace at bad luck, and that is exactly the energy a card game night needs.

### Turn structure and pacing
The game flows in phases. The host presents the current card, players decide to bet or fold based on the revealed information, and then the next card appears to confirm or refute the bets. The tension is intentionally simple: a big bet pays off with a satisfying reveal, a small bet preserves safety for another turn, and a bold, risky move can pay off handsomely—or crash spectacularly, in the way only a well-timed bluff can.

### Bluffing and memory requirements
Bluffing in Card Sharks is less about misdirection and more about channeling the confidence of a show contestant. It benefits players who can balance risk with logic, who can remember a few key card sequences, and who can read the table’s energy. You do not need perfect memory here, but you do need situational awareness: who has been pushing hard recently, who has folded too easily, who is likely to go for a high-risk bet when the pressure is on. It is a social experience as much as a numerical one, which is a compliment to the designers who wanted a party game that still feels like a competition.

### Reading the room without becoming a jerk

## Strategy notes: how to squeeze the most juice out of the cards
A recurring theme in Card Sharks is that you can influence the outcome through measured risk. A few practical tips to improve your odds without boring everyone into submission:
- Start with medium bets to gauge the room, then adjust based on the table tilt. If a player has been pushing, you may exploit that energy with a calculated risk rather than a safe play.
- When you have a strong read on the early cards, commit to a cautious but steady betting approach. You are building a platform for a dramatic reveal later, not burning through your chips in a single hand.
- If you are behind, consider a high-risk bluff that tests the line of the table. A successful bluff can flip the momentum and re energize the game.
- Observe your opponents’ patterns. In this game, people reveal more about their headspace than their cards; use that information to time your bets and your reveals for maximum suspense.

Remember that in Card Sharks the joy often comes from the misdirection you create with your voice, your body language, and your timing as much as from the actual math behind a card deck. A well-timed pause can be the difference between a spectacular success and an equally entertaining collapse.
