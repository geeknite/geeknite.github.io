## Practical play tips and strategies
- Start with Batman’s investigative powers to unlock information about the Man Who Laughs’ plan. Fast intel gives you the earliest window to secure the objective.
- Use the terrain thoughtfully. Chokepoints favor Batman and his partner, who can deny or slow down the villain’s advance. The Man Who Laughs loves to push you into suboptimal routes; keep options open and force him to expose his tricks earlier.
- Manage fear tokens as a resource. They may grant the villain an advantage, but they can also be a risk if Batman or your partner can convert fear into an opening or a counter move.
- Practice two-skill coordination. The Batman Team-Up setting rewards players who can blend a non-combat objective (investigation, control, objective capture) with a combat lane that punishes the villain’s maneuverability.
- Don’t neglect terrain denial. If you can create zones that limit the villain’s options, you improve Batman’s chances of staging a decisive strike. The Man Who Laughs benefits from a fluid battlefield; pinning him down reduces his ability to manipulate the scene.
