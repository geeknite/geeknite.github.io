## How to Play: Quick Start Guide

If you have played Exploding Kittens before, you will pick up this edition quickly. If this is your first exposure to the series, you will still pick up the rules rapidly due to their compact nature.

1) Shuffle the deck and deal a few cards to each player. The number of cards depends on your house rules, but five or seven is a solid baseline. 
2) Decide who goes first. The first turn usually includes a draw from the deck and then the option to play one or more action cards to affect the next player. 
3) Draw until someone draws the exploding kitten card and loses the round. You can try to defuse or dodge, but if luck does not go your way, you will have to watch a chain of outrageous consequences play out on the table. 
4) Use action cards to skip turns, steal cards, or force others into risk. Each action card has a playful hazard attached to it, and you will learn to time your plays for maximum impact. 
5) The round ends when one player remains, and the next round starts. The game moves quickly; most rounds can be resolved in under 20 minutes, which makes it perfect for a game night with frequent breaks for snacks and debate about who is the actual MVP of chaos.

Tip for success: keep a confident poker face when you play a risk card that will force someone else to draw. The drama matters as much as the outcome, and the table will remember your sense of timing more than your memorized deck counts. Also, never reveal your defuse cards in advance. The suspense adds flavor to the room and gives you a moment to bask in the glory of your own craftiness.
