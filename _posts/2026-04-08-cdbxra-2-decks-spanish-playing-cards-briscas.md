### Brisca Primer, The Nerdy Way
Brisca is a trick-taking game with a few simple but deliciously treacherous twists:

- The aim is to win the most tricks, not necessarily to win every card. The scoring is often based on the cards you collect, not just the number of tricks—so every play has a potential swing.
- Trump is complicated by the presence of a designated “brisca” suit or the top card of the deck depending on the variant. In some rulesets, the Ace is the highest, in others, the 7 takes the crown in certain suits. The CDBXRA decks stay faithful to the Spanish 40-card tradition, which keeps you honest during big moments.
- The face cards bring extra weight: the Rey often out-values lower numeric cards, but the particular order can vary by local rules. The two-deck setup gives you ample opportunity to establish strategic partnerships, bluffing lanes, and the glorious moment you slam down a perfect card that unexpectedly wins you the round.

### Care, Maintenance, and Longevity

Two decks can live a long life if you treat them properly. Here are a few tips to keep your CDBXRA cards in prime shape:

- Clean hands, clean cards: Dry, clean hands reduce oil buildup that can blur print and make the cards slicker than needed.
- Storage matters: A slightly stiff tuck box supports the cards and makes ricocheting them back into place less likely. Consider a card box or a small deck case if you travel with these.
- Sleeves are optional but recommended for collectors: If you’re a collector who loves to keep a pristine look for years, clear standard-sized sleeves will help protect the faces from wear and accidental spills.
- No sharp objects near the face: This is common sense, but it bears repeating—avoid razor-sharp letter openers and the like near your deck. Also, don’t try to use the cards as makeshift coasters or spell book pages in a pinch; the ink doesn’t thank you for it.
