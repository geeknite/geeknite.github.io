## How to play

Dizzy Date Night is designed for two players, though some variants allow a third wheel (friend, roommate, or the neighbor’s dog) to be observers or impromptu commentators. The core loop is simple:

1) Each player draws a card and follows the prompt. Some prompts require cooperation, others require competition, and a few simply require you to stare into your partner’s eyes while humming a tune.
2) If you fail a challenge or don’t want to complete it, you have the option to opt for a substitute that still acknowledges your fear in a dignified manner.
3) You progress around the table while the timer counts down. The goal is not always clearly defined; sometimes it’s to finish the deck, other times to raise a tiny flag of mutual satisfaction. In any case, the game ends when you decide you’ve had enough, or when your drink is empty, whichever comes first.

Key mechanics involve timing, social risktaking, and the willingness to reveal embarrassing habits without judgment. It’s not about winning in the traditional sense; it’s about the stories you’ll tell after. If you and your partner are into improv, slapstick, or the kind of humor that makes you go, oh no, we just did that, Dizzy Date Night nails you in the most affectionate way possible.

Every round ends with a small recap in which you rate the experience or note the most surprising confession. ## Variants and house rules

No game is complete without a few house rules, and Dizzy Date Night is no exception. Here are a handful of common tweaks that couples use to tailor the experience:

- The Cozy Rule: If either player is not feeling comfortable with a prompt, you can swap it for a milder alternative without penalty. The idea is inclusion, not coercion, and it keeps the mood light.
- The Slow Burn: Instead of rushing, you extend the timer so you can savor the moment and stretch a single card into a mini-scene. This turns a quick prompt into a tiny improv show for two.
- The Safety Card: Add a universal safe word or gesture that stops any prompt instantly. This keeps the game from drifting into uncomfortable territory while preserving the spontaneity.
- The Loyalty Round: At the end of a round, each player writes a quick compliment about the other on a sticky note and sticks it to a shared board. It’s a small ritual that can boost affection and remind you why you’re doing this in the first place.

The fun of house rules is that they let you shape the experience to your comfort level. It’s your date night, after all, and the game is a tool to unlock more conversation and more laughter, not a rigid master of ceremonies guiding you toward a predefined ending.

## Drinking rules and safety

Dizzy Date Night includes drinking prompts, but it is essential to approach this responsibly. The goal is to enjoy yourselves while staying safe and ensuring you both have stamina for the entire date. Here are a few practical safety tips you should consider:

- Hydration matters. Alternate alcoholic prompts with water breaks. Your future self will thank you when you’re not nursing a headache at 2 a.m.
- Know your limits. If a prompt asks you to drink beyond your comfort, swap it for a non-alcoholic version. The trick is to keep the energy high without crossing it into drunken mischief that you’ll regret in the morning.
- Have a plan for a quiet exit. If you both feel overwhelmed, take a pause, switch to a non-drinking round (card prompts only), and regroup.
- Never pressure your partner. If a dare or a prompt feels like it’s crossing a line for the other person, it’s okay to decline and pivot to something mutually agreeable.

The safety stance isn’t a dampener; it’s a friendship-based compliance to ensure the night remains about connection rather than sensational chaos. Responsible play is part of the Geeknite vibe, and Dizzy Date Night encourages a playful yet mindful approach.
