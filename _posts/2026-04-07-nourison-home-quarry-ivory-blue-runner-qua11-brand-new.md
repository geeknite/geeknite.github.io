H2 Maintenance and real-world care
Daily care
A quick vacuum once or twice a week is usually enough for low-pile runners in busy hallways. Use a vacuum with a gentle setting to avoid pulling at the fibers. If you own a robot vacuum, this is a great candidate because the pattern isn’t an intricate maze that confuses tech; it’s a calm, graceful pattern that plays nicely with automation.

Stain management
The Ivory Blue colorway can hide minor spills and smudges better than plain white, but you still want to treat stains sooner rather than later. Blot spills with a clean cloth, then use a mild rug cleaner or a mixture of water and mild detergent. Avoid harsh chemicals that could alter color tones. For pet accidents, act quickly and consider an enzymatic cleaner to break down odors rather than just masking them. If you happen to spill red wine, act fast and test a small inconspicuous area first—color transfer happens, and you want to minimize it.

Rotation and aging
Rotating the rug every few months can help balance wear and tear if you’re using it in a consistently used hallway. This reduces the chance of one side looking noticeably worn while the other remains plush. It’s like rotating a save file in a video game—consistency matters, and surprises just keep life interesting.
