City of Mist never promised you a simple roll and move. It promised you a neon-lit mythic soap opera where detectives with masks chase miracles through alleys slick with rain and long pasts. The Forgotten Stories, whatever the publisher may claim in cheerful press blurbs, is a love letter to those side streets where legends murmur like a beat in a club and players keep pretending their origami legends are real enough to sign a mortgage with the universe. In this review we will dive into what makes The Forgotten Stories stand out, what it borrows shamelessly, and what it leaves on the table like a damp umbrella after a godly downpour. If you came here expecting a canned expansion, buckle up, because this is City of Mist embracing its misfit mythology with the heart of a late night radio host and the budget of a high school theater production.

![City of Mist cover art for The Forgotten Stories](/assets/images/city-of-mist-forgotten-stories-cover.jpg)

## Overview

If you already know the City of Mist vibe, you know what you are getting into: noir-ish cityscapes, mythic archetypes masquerading as normal people, and a storytelling engine that treats your masks as both entry ticket and liability. The Forgotten Stories leans into the idea that what gets forgotten can still bite you in the ankle when you least expect it. Think of a zine anthology that could fit in a sleeve of old school TF cards, but with more pulsing neon and less existential dread about your printing costs.

The structure is modular in a way that will please both pick-up-and-play groups and long campaign veterans. It offers a handful of interconnected micro adventures, each designed to slot into a single session or to echo into a longer arc if your table wants to chase themes rather than outcomes. The emphasis is less on a single big bad and more on emergent stories that emerge from your mask, your contacts, and the city itself being a character almost as loud as the players. If you are after a crunchy boss-battle book, set your expectations to daytime soap with a budget of dramatic weather and a chorus line of mythic side hustles.

The art direction? If the core book wore a trench coat, this one took it to a 2am rooftop party. The palettes are punchy, the layouts sing in a way that might actually be obeyed by a curious GM, and the design feels intentionally patchwork and alive rather than pristine and clinical. It drifts from film noir to urban fantasy to street level myth with a confident, winking grin that says you will recognize every reference even as you pretend you did not notice the homage to your favorite crime show from eight seasons ago.

## What you get

The Forgotten Stories is not a single adventure but a set of modular experiences designed to slot into the City of Mist cosmos. The book is peppered with:

- A curated collection of micro adventures, each roughly suited for a single session, with options to scale up or down depending on party size and attention span.
- Updated or new masks and mythic motifs that align with the theme of forgotten legends and near-mythic everyday heroes.
- GM tools that feel tailor-made for tables that crave both structure and fireworks. Think short encounter tables, mood boards, and a toolbox for improvisation that does not require a PhD in improv.
- A design language that respects both the quiet moral drama of personal legends and the sudden, cinematic chaos that City of Mist mashes together like a neon smoothie.

The kit includes a quick-start guide for GMs, a few ready-to-run arcs, and a handful of side stories that can be dropped into any ongoing campaign. The effect is a repository of ideas rather than a rigid campaign bible. If you love building your own city from the ground up, The Forgotten Stories provides just enough scaffolding to keep you from knocking over the entire set while leaving plenty of room for improvisation.

To help you picture the experience, here is a snapshot of a typical session spine from the book:

1) Hook the players with a local legend that refuses to stay forgotten. A statue weeps every midnight, a subway car reveals a ghostly diner, a low-level crime lord has a mythic past that keeps catching up with him in the worst possible way. 2) Let the players pick masks that reflect their personal themes, not just their class or alignments. 3) Introduce a mythic complication rooted in the citys forgotten stories, something that reveals itself through clues, rumors, and the occasional brutal truth. 4) Allow scenes to orbit the center of the mystery while the players chase personal arcs that remind them that legends are a two-way street. 5) Resolve with a moment of pay-off that redefines the characters, the city, and the masks they wear.

If you have played City of Mist before, you will find The Forgotten Stories rewarding in small and big ways. If you are new to the system, the book still offers accessible entry points via its short form arcs and the GM tips that help you translate mythic energies into actual in-game consequences. The goal is not only to entertain but to teach players how to think mythically about everyday life without requiring a team of consultants or a budget the size of a small city block.

## Mechanics and feel

City of Mists core mechanic rests on the interplay between masks and myths. The Forgotten Stories leans into that interplay with a sharper emphasis on cause and effect: what happens when a forgotten legend tries to become real again, through the agency of your players who happen to be the kind of people who handle secrets for a living? The result is a game that can feel like a film noir, a magical realism piece, and a caper all at once, depending on how the table leans into the mood.

One of the strongest design decisions here is the way the book handles downtime and epilogues. The Forgotten Stories occasionally gates some outcomes behind narrative checks that reward creative problem solving rather than numeric success. You get the sense that the author wants you to actually talk through the ethical and emotional consequences of lifting a forgotten story from the citys memory. If your table loves aftermath, you will savor these sections. If you tend to rush to the ending, you might find yourself reined in by the more reflective tone of these chapters, which is not a bad thing but something worth noticing before you schedule a six-week arc.

The mechanics around introducing a mythic motif into a scene are clear and approachable. There are clear prompts for the GM to describe environment, mood, and symbolism, enabling a cinematic vibe without forcing the GM into a heavy-handed narration. Players can lean on the masks to sculpt their approach; a character who embodies a forgotten king might solve a problem by appealing to a long-lost authority, or a hero who embodies a dawn-touched trickster might disrupt plans with a sly misdirection. The book embraces caper energy while never shedding the emotional weight that makes City of Mist more than just a fantasy heist game.

There are also some helpful tools for session pacing. The Forgotten Stories includes a few optional encounter clocks and a loss of memory mechanic that asks players to track what their masks forget as the city forgets. This is not only thematically satisfying but also excellent for keeping sessions moving. It creates a sense of momentum that makes even the slow, character-driven beats feel inevitable rather than optional detours. If you like a good pacing trick that still leaves room for character growth and dramatic reveal, you will appreciate the way this book handles tempo.

For players who care about balance, the volume does a decent job of keeping challenges within the scope of a single channel arc. There are some encounters that feel a touch on the lethal side for newer groups, but the book provides guidance on adjusting difficulty and scaling opponents. It does not shy away from stakes, but it gives the GM the tools to control how much heat the city sends your way and when your protagonists are allowed to walk away with a sense of triumph rather than a cautionary tale.

If you are a long-time fan of the City of Mist world, you will notice that The Forgotten Stories respects the lore without becoming a retread of previously published material. It does not pretend to be the final word on every forgotten legend, but it does offer a curated snapshot that can be used as a seed for bigger campaigns or as a standalone suite of nights that feel like a gallery of myth in motion. The result is a product that feels confident in its own strange identity rather than shyly echoing the core book or earlier expansions.

## Art, layout, and accessibility

Visually, The Forgotten Stories is a treat. The neon-on-rain aesthetic remains intact, with accents that evoke street art, mythic sigils, and the urban mythos that City of Mist fans adore. The layout is clean but expressive, with sidebars that highlight thematic hooks and GM tips that do not interrupt the narrative flow. The typography balances readability with character and mood, which makes it easier to parse story beats while your players argue about whether the skull on a neon sign is a literal symbol or an inside joke.

Accessibility-wise, the book provides many small touches that help a wider range of players. There are clear triggers and content warnings for sensitive material, as well as tips for running sessions with fewer players or shorter blocks of time. The text avoids overly specialized jargon without sacrificing the flavor, which is a victory when you consider the ambitious mix of noir, myth, and urban fantasy that City of Mist embodies. The illustration choices complement the content rather than overshadow it, and there are enough visual cues to aid a GM who relies on ambience more than elaborate maps.

## Thematic notes and potential pitfalls

The Forgotten Stories leans into the bittersweet edge of legends that were once alive and are now mostly memories, rumors, and the occasional street rumor that inexplicably centers a character in a mythic web. Thematic hooks include memory loss, forgotten power, and the tension between what a city forgives and what it chooses to forget. When deployed effectively, these themes create compelling scenes that feel both personal and epic. When overused or hampered by weak pacing, however, the same themes can threaten to become melodramatic or mood-killing weight rather than narrative fuel.

Readers should be mindful of group tone. If your table craves lighthearted crunch and quick adrenaline, you might need to lean into the more playful stretches rather than the heavier, reflective passages that The Forgotten Stories sometimes invites you to explore. On the other hand, if you enjoy introspective moments and character driven arcs, the book offers a generous sandbox in which to explore the emotional consequences of wearing a mask that a city has forgotten how to protect.

The book also handles canonical risk with care. It treats memory and myth with respect and avoids trivializing sensitive topics by offering practical GM guidance for handling tricky content. If you are concerned with representing diverse mythic voices, you will find some thoughtful inclusions here, though there is always room for more. The best approach is to run a few sessions with clearly stated boundaries, and use the material as a prompt rather than a mandate for any particular representation.

## How it compares to other City of Mist products

Compared to the core book, The Forgotten Stories focuses more on episodic, bite sized adventures with a heavy emphasis on mood and theme rather than sprawling campaigns. It shares the same mood and masks system, but with a tighter scope that makes it easier for newer groups to jump in. It sits well next to other printed material in the City of Mist line by giving GMs a tool kit for one night or a handful of sessions that can feed into a larger arc later.

If you have previously enjoyed other City of Mist expansions that lean into mythic imagery and noir storytelling, you will likely feel at home here. If you are more into crunchy mechanics and do not care about atmosphere as much, you may want to skim these pages and focus on the encounters and GM tips rather than the mood pieces. The Forgotten Stories does not pretend to be a one size fits all dependably reliable rulebook; it is a mood generator with a well-polished mechanical backbone and a strong emphasis on narrative control for the GM and the players alike.

For readers who enjoy cross pollination with other LARP or tabletop systems, there are several cross references and design ideas you can borrow that translate nicely to other mythic noir titles. The book also makes a few nods to investigative mechanics that might inspire you to borrow some ideas for your own home games, even if you never plan to run a full City of Mist campaign again.

## Verdict and recommendations

The Forgotten Stories lands on the right side of the line between homage and innovation. It honors the glow and grit of City of Mist while giving GMs a portable toolbox of ideas that can spark a single session or spill into a longer arc. Its greatest strength is how it invites players to think about memory, myth, and identity in ways that feel personal and cinematic at once. The windows into forgotten legends are frequent enough to keep sessions vibrant, and the pacing hooks ensure you never fall into long lullaby stretches where nothing happens except dice rolling and reflections on what could have been.

The writing is solid, the art direction is lively, and the mechanics, while familiar, are deployed in ways that feel fresh when you use the product as a jumping off point rather than a strict play guide. If you have a group that enjoys atmospheric detective work with a mythic tilt, you will find The Forgotten Stories to be an excellent companion. If you prefer procedural murder mysteries or heavy tactical crunch, you might want to treat this as flavor and keep your combat lines light. Either way, it is worth having on your shelf for the moments when the city whispers to you and you decide to listen.

In short: The Forgotten Stories is a high energy, mood driven expansion that respects the core City of Mist vibe while offering enough new bits to justify adding it to your table. It rewards creativity, celebrates forgotten legends, and still leaves room for you to improvise a legend of your own that the city may or may not remember tomorrow.

## Final thoughts

If you are looking for a game night that wears a rain slick and a neon glow with equal enthusiasm, this is a book you will want on your table. It delivers memorable scenes, clever hooks, and an adaptable structure that keeps sessions moving without burning out your players with endless exposition. It is not a perfect product, and its best moments require players who lean into the myths rather than fight them, but for a neon myth noir experience, it is hard to beat.
