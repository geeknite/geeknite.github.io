# City of Mist: Mysteries of the City — A Noir-Soaked Expansion Review

If you’ve ever wanted your tabletop to feel like a crime-drenched novel narrated by a smoky saxophone and a streetlamp that’s just a little too judgmental, City of Mist: Mysteries of the City is here to tilt your fedora and squint at the horizon. This expansion for City of Mist, the RPG about legends with human faces, throws you into a city-wide web of investigations, secrets, and neon-colored lies. It’s not a radical overhaul of the system; it’s a deepening of the mood, a sharpening of the noir blade, and a few fancy new tricks that make those late-night sessions feel like they’re quoting a detective’s diary—and you know how much we geeks love diary entries with spiraling footnotes.

If you’re new to City of Mist, you’re in for a treat. If you’re a veteran, you’ll appreciate the way Mysteries of the City nudges you to lean into the edges of the map and chase threads you didn’t even know existed. Either way, prepare for a campaign toolkit that invites you to solve a grand city-sized puzzle while your characters wrestle with masks, myths, and murder solutions that aren’t as tidy as you’d hoped. For fans of investigative RPGs and anyone who’s ever wondered what happens when a city’s legends throw an alleyway party, this is the expansion you’ve been waiting for.

## What’s in the box and how sturdy is the foundation?

Mysteries of the City arrives in a compact box that feels like a deliberate sequel rather than a throwaway accessory. It’s not about reprinting the same stuff with a fresh gloss; it adds a set of curated investigations, new case files, and a few structural tweaks that make the core engine feel both familiar and newly dangerous. The production values carry over from City of Mist: the art direction leans into moody blues and coppery streetlight oranges, and the cardstock is sturdy enough to survive a table of enthusiastic players (we’re not naming any culprits who’ve ripped a corner in a moment of tense dice-rolling).

Component-wise, you get:
- A handful of new case files and investigative prompts that are ready to drop into a session—no scavenging required.
- A tidy stack of “Mysteries” cards that drive the narrative clock forward and add twist opportunities for the GM.
- Expanded mantles and background options that thread into the city’s myths without forcing a player to rebuild their identity mid-session.
- A compact scene map and a few new location cards that give you fresh districts to explore without needing a full city reprint.

Quality-wise, these pieces match the vibe of the base game: a tactile, collectible feel that rewards you for keeping track of little details—the exact thing that makes a mystery feel earned rather than cheated. The cards have readable text, sensible iconography, and enough flavor to spark inspiration during a tense beat. The map sections are legible, though they’re a touch small for table-hugging players who insist on scanning every lane and storefront at once. If you prefer a bigger tactile footprint, you’ll want to print or upgrade a few pieces, but the base components do not feel stingy.

## A refresher on the core engine (for the uninitiated or memory-challenged)

City of Mist runs on a Mythos vs. Logos dice pool, with players embodying investigators who are more legend than man. Masks grant them powers (or, more accurately, give them the identity under which they can channel their abilities). The shared language of the system—Mythos (the wild, unknown forces that shadows and myths ride on) and Logos (the human-made rules, devices, and beliefs that anchor reality)—drives both conflict and cooperation at the table.

Mysteries of the City leans into that duality by focusing more on narrative investigations. The expansion doesn’t turn the system into a new game; it adds investigatory scaffolding that helps groups structure multi-session arcs around a central mystery embedded in the city’s culture, past, and people. If you loved chasing clues in the core book but felt something was missing once you reached the resolution, Mysteries of the City is designed to fill that gap with a comprehensive, thread-based approach to solving big-city enigmas.

If you want a quick mental model, imagine a cinematic detective show where the city itself has a backstory and your crew are both the audience and the catalysts. The new content in Mysteries of the City gives you the scaffolding to run arcs where every clue points toward a larger truth, and the larger truth reshapes the city’s mythic map as you play. It’s an elegant fusion of character-driven noir with a mythpunk edge, and it works well even if you aren’t a “mystery specialist” in your regular gaming group.

## What Mysteries of the City changes about play

The expansion is designed around three connective ideas:
- Case-driven investigations that encourage longer play cycles
- A refined mystery clock that creates forward momentum and escalating stakes
- Expanded mantles and city anchors that help players align with a shared setting

Let’s break these down more concretely.

### Case structure and player agency
Mysteries of the City provides a structured approach to investigations that feels familiar to fans of long-form campaigns but remains friendly to shorter sessions. The core idea is to give each mystery a clock—think of it as a set of progress markers that, when filled, reveal a pivotal truth. The clock is not a rigid timer; it’s a narrative device that nudges the GM to drop new clues at defined intervals and invites players to chase leads in a non-linear fashion. This is a welcome shift from one-shot mystery hunts where players bounce from clue to clue in a pre-determined order. In practice, you get to leverage more improvisation as the group’s choices carve a path to the reveal.

### The mystery cards and case files
The expansion’s cards are a boon for GMs who like to pull fresh threads without writing new content from scratch. The case files offer ready-to-run setups with hooks tailored to different city districts, types of crimes, and mythic implications. The cards—when aligned with the GM’s map or the players’ masks—create a tapestry where every lead could plausibly connect to a larger conspiracy. The downside here is the potential for thematic overlap if your table is too fond of similar noir tropes. However, the quality of the anchor ideas is high, and that means you will likely get multiple sessions out of a single case file if your group is willing to lean into the broader city-wide arc.

### Expanded mantles and city anchors
Mysteries of the City doesn’t throw away the old mantles; it expands them. New mantles give players options to reframe their identity around the mystery at hand, which fosters ambitious roleplay moments. The city anchors—locations, factions, and recurring NPCs—get refreshed with political tensions, class conflicts, and mythic echoes. You’ll feel the difference when you revisit familiar districts; streets you thought you knew become quieter or louder, depending on what the mystery demands. It’s the old game’s DNA with a richer personality transplant. If you like to see a city breathe in your campaigns, this is a feature you’ll appreciate.

## A quick walkthrough of the components (and how they feel at the table)

- Case Files: Each file acts like a mini-arc starter, complete with hooks, potential clues, and a suggested closing beat. They’re well-balanced between mystery, danger, and character development. If you’ve got a layout-maximizing GM who loves handouts, these files answer that craving with crisp text and evocative prompts.
- Mystery Cards: These cards supply ninth-inning twists and optional opportunities to pivot the investigation when players press a path you didn’t anticipate. Expect some flavorful cards and a few that will become your table’s favorite “oh no, not that again” moments.
- Locations and Faction Cards: The city’s civic life gets more complicated. You’ll find districts that feel alive and factions that aren’t purely antagonistic but morally gray. This is City of Mist’s sweet spot: moral ambiguity dressed in trench coats.
- Expanded Mantles: The new mantles let players re-center their mythic identity around the arc’s core mystery. It’s a comfortable device for players who know their character isn’t finished growing—there’s always one more legend you can embody when the mystery deepens.

In practice, these components work best when you let the players and GM collaborate on the city’s answer to the question “what is really going on here?” The boxed content gives you the prompts; your group gives you the drama.

## A sample scenario: “The Velvet Ghost” (illustrative, not canonical, but representative)
Imagine a district known for hooves of neon and a theater that’s seen better days. The Velvet Ghost is a rumored patron who never sits in the same seat twice and leaves behind a ribbon with a coded message. The case files propose three potential mysteries: a curse on a performance, a heist of a legendary prop, or a vigilante whose signature is ghostly footprints. The players lean into their masks, weave the clues into their mythic abilities, and slowly discover that the theater is a gateway to a city-wide conspiracy touching on an ancient myth with modern consequences.

As a GM, you’ll want to lean into the misdirections while keeping a steady beat on the clock. The players’ choices will guide which mystery thread unravels first, and the clock will reward or punish risk-taking with escalating stakes. The result is a session that feels fresh, cinematic, and surprisingly personal for each investigator. If you adore “puzzle plus character drama” as a rhythm, this scenario—and many like it—will click immediately.

## Visuals, mood, and accessibility

Art direction remains one of City of Mist’s strongest assets, and Mysteries of the City doesn’t stray from that aesthetic. The noir palette, combined with mythic iconography, keeps players immersed in a world where the line between legend and the urban jungle blurs. The book’s layout is reader-friendly, with callouts for GMs and sidebars that remind players to lean into the mood rather than force a mechanical path. You’ll find quotes, the occasional street-lamp metaphor, and plenty of space to annotate clues—great for long sessions or campaigns that intrude upon multiple nights.

If you’re a new GM, fear not: the expansion’s case files are structured to guide you through the first few sessions without requiring you to craft your own city-wide arc from scratch. If you’re a veteran, you’ll relish the density of options and the opportunity to weave the new mantles into existing character concepts without forcing a hard re-write.

## How it plays with the core system: pacing, difficulty, and table flow

Pacing is where Mysteries of the City shines and where it also tests your table’s social contracts. The clock system nudges players toward progress, but you’ll want to calibrate your game night for longer arcs if your group enjoys a deeper dive. The recommended rhythm is a two-to-four-session arc for a single mystery with a handful of shorter side-chapters you can sprinkle in if you want to pad a slower week. The more experience your GM has with City of Mist, the better they’ll be at weaving clues without railroading players into a single ending. The beauty of this expansion is that it’s modular: you can drop in a couple of case files for a single evening, or you can commit to a multi-session festival of secrets.

In terms of difficulty, the system’s dice pools can feel forgiving or punishing depending on how you stage encounters. Mysteries of the City gives you more levers to pull to keep the table engaged: more dramatic twists, more NPCs with agendas, and more ways to test a mask’s limits. If your players are fond of moral complexity and relationships that evolve as much as the plot, you’ll find the expansion a natural home.

# Reader tips: running Mysteries of the City smoothly

- Start with a quick session-zero that introduces the district, the core mystery thread, and a few personal stakes for each investigator. You’ll want this night to set the emotional tone as much as the plot’s mystery.
- Use the mystery clock early and often. A good hack is to frame every player turn as a chance to move the clock forward via a clue, a confession, or a bold risk that reveals a new facet of the city’s myth.
- Lean into the city’s mood. When in doubt, escalate a noir moment with a small, symbolic reveal—a fingerprint in a fogged window, a song that repeats in the back of a suspect’s mind, a prop that ties every thread back to a myth.
- Don’t be afraid to pause for character beats. City of Mist thrives on the intersection of myth and mundane life; let your players explore relationships with their mantles, their pasts, and the city itself.
- If you’re a new GM, lean on the case files and tables for guidance. The structure is your friend, not a cage. If you’re a veteran, feel free to remix these threads to fit your city’s unique vibe.

## A quick note on accessibility and group dynamics

Mysteries of the City is designed to be accessible to players who enjoy narrative exploration and who are comfortable with a non-linear investigation. It is not a “kick in the door” combat-driven expansion; you won’t find an endless dungeon crawl here. Instead, you’ll find a sandbox of urban myth and human motivations that ask questions about what the city is willing to tolerate and what legends require to become real. For players who prefer a linear, puzzle-hard mode, this expansion can feel daunting—but that’s the point: the city’s mysteries demand a collaborative approach.

That said, the system’s learning curve remains approachable. Most of the core mechanics you already know from the base game carry over, and the new materials provide improved clarity on how to run multi-session arcs. If your table tends to benefit from session feedback and frequent pacing checks, Mysteries of the City will feel like a comfortable upgrade rather than a forceful rewrite.

## Final verdict: who should buy this and why

In Geeknite terms: if you enjoy noir vibes, mythic undercurrents, and a city that feels alive enough to push back when you poke it, Mysteries of the City is worth your time and money. It’s not a flashy re-skin; it’s a thoughtful expansion that deepens the “why” behind every clue and every choice your investigators make. The practical takeaway is this: if you want to run longer, more meaningful investigations with a built-in structure that still leaves room for improvisation, buy this box, carve out a weekend schedule, and let your city reveal its darkest corners.

If you’re newer to City of Mist, this expansion also holds value as a gateway drug to the core system’s strengths. It gives you a safe scaffold to learn how to balance player agency with narrative momentum, and it rewards players who think in terms of clues and consequences rather than “roll for success.” It’s not a game-changer in the sense of rewriting the base rules, but it is a masterclass in how to tell a city-sized mystery with a group of distinct voices.

If you’re a veteran looking for a fresh seam to mine: yes, this adds enough material to feed multiple campaigns without ever feeling like a re-skinned adventure. The new masks and case files are the kind of content you’ll revisit, not just once, but as you continue to shape your table’s evolving mythos.

## Final recommendations and a note on pairing

- Best for groups of 3–5 players who enjoy slower-burn mysteries and character-driven drama. If your table leans toward fast, action-driven play, you’ll still appreciate the mood, but you might want to shorten sessions or pick fewer case files per night.
- Pair with the core City of Mist core book for baseline familiarity; it makes it easier for new players to slip into the density of the expansion without feeling overwhelmed.
- Consider a campaign approach: run a trilogy of mysteries across several sessions that share a common antagonist or city-wide consequence. The clock mechanics scale well across multi-session arcs and encourage ongoing character development.
- For fans of the setting, it’s worth cross-referencing your city’s actual landmarks with the fictional equivalents in the expansion. The more you map real-into-fantasy, the more you’ll enjoy the sense of place.

If you’re ready to upgrade your city’s rumor mill and give your investigators a longer, moodier chase, Mysteries of the City is a strong candidate for your bookshelf. It respects the core system’s strengths while adding a generous dose of darkness and scale that noir fans will adore.
