## Introduction
Fortune Row is not a district you stumble upon in City of Mist; it is a rumor that learned to walk, then learned to zigzag across your map with neon shoes and a suspiciously charismatic edge. If cities are palimpsests of myths scratched onto the urban skin, Fortune Row is the fresh scratch that gleams at you every time you blink. It’s a place where the myths are not just legends you learn about; they are residents with mortgages, opinions, and an agenda that might involve you getting into trouble while you try to stay upright on the rain-slick sidewalks.

In this campaign setting, Fortune Row becomes a living, breathing character. The alleyway signage flickers with the names of forgotten gods. A bakery hums with the gossip of a thousand ruined prophecies. A pawnshop sells not just trinkets but memories. And at the center sits a tavern called The Coin-Operated Oracle that charges by the truth and gives back by the lie you most want to hear. It’s decadent, noir, and spicy with magical realism—the perfect playground for masks, myths, and mischief. If you like your investigations with a side of mythic swagger and your city blocks with a film-noir glow, Fortune Row is ready to roll like dice on a table that’s seen one too many all-nighters.

This post is a thorough, Geeknite-style tour through Fortune Row—what it is, how it feels, and how to run it without turning your players into myth-traffic. We’ll cover layout, factions, hooks, NPCs, and practical tips for keeping sessions tight, cinematic, and funny when you want to punch up the drama with a little levity. If you’ve previously cooked a City of Mist campaign in a more generic urban setting, Fortune Row is a perfect upgrade: it gives you a built-in mood, a cast of iconic archetypes, and a city-block worth of micro-plot seeds that click together like a well-aged cassette tape.

## Concept and core vibe
Fortune Row is a neighborhood with a split personality: half gilded, half gutter, all myth. The myths here are not distant gods; they are embedded into the very bricks, taverns, and transit lines. A myth in Fortune Row might appear as a fortune-teller with a robotic arm, a barista who brews coffee that reveals people’s secret wishes, or a taxi driver who claims to know the exact moment when your fate will flip. The stories you get to tell here are intimate, character-driven, and a little ridiculous in the best cinematic sense.

This setting emphasizes three core ideas:

- Myths aren’t abstractions in Fortune Row; they’re neighbors. They run businesses, scheme in back rooms, and occasionally owe you a favor you didn’t want to collect.
- Fortune is a living mechanic that interacts with Masks and Logos. The more a player leans into a myth, the more Fortune rows push back in witty, dangerous, or unexpectedly helpful ways.
- The city itself is a character. The rain-slick streets, the flickering signboards, and the smell of toasted cinnamon in the air all contribute to a mood that invites noir investigation with a pinch of surreal humor.

Fortune Row is ideal for groups that love character-driven mysteries, stylish action scenes, and conversations that feel like chess matches between a hero and a destiny that keeps changing its mind. It’s not a place for straightforward, grimdark grit alone. It’s a place where a bad decision made in a back-alley bake shop can ripple into a legend told at the Coin-Operated Oracle.

## Setting mechanics: what makes Fortune Row tick
City of Mist uses a mix of Mythos and Logos dice pools, Masks, and a setting-specific flavor. Fortune Row adds a few bespoke levers that help you lean into the mood without bending the core rules unnecessarily.

- Masks with a local flavor: Each player’s Mask should connect to at least one Fortune Row landmark—The Coin-Operated Oracle, a rooftop corn-maze garden, the Glass Foundry, or the Moonlit Arcade. These ties keep you anchored to the neighborhood while letting the player’s power fantasy glow like a neon sign.
- Fortune Cards (optional): A deck or a randomizer that represents the city’s mood—good luck, bad luck, a rumor spreading through the rye bread at the bakery, or a damper placed on a key power. When the GM draws or the players trigger the deck, Fortune Cards inject twists that tie to Fortune Row’s local myths.
- Local legends: The neighborhood has its own recurring myths—twisting versions of classic myths reimagined as city residents. They provide ongoing hooks, antagonists, and potential allies that a GM can pull from during a session.
- The Coin-Operated Oracle: A safe-haven of fortunes and misdirections. The Oracle is both a plot device and a social hub. It’s a place where players exchange information for favors, and where the truth is always a little bit for sale.

In practice, Fortune Row prefers dynamic scenes: a chase along rain-slick lanes, a tense negotiation in a bakery’s back room, or a rooftop confrontation under a choked neon sky. It invites cinematic turns, witty banter, and a willingness to lean into the surreal without losing sight of character goals.

## Factions and notable residents
Fortune Row has a web of factions that feel like familiar archetypes but tinted with local color. Here are a few core players you’ll encounter:

### The Velvet Choir
A cabal of performers, singers, and illusionists who believe every truth should be sung and every lie should have a chorus. They use performance to shape reality and influence the city’s perception of events. They’re charismatic, dangerous, and suspiciously fond of metaphors that rhyme.

### The Glass Foundry
A group of artificers and technomancers who tinker with the city’s artifacts and myths. They keep the street’s machines running and sometimes break them when the balance of power tilts. They’re pragmatic, a little cold, and always with a handy gadget on loan to the right mask.

### The Syndicate of Shadows
A loose network of information brokers and fixers who know that Fortune Row runs on secrets. They trade in whispers, favors, and rumors that can topple a hero’s reputation if mishandled. They’re not good or evil; they’re convenient or inconvenient.

### The Night Apothecary
A bizarre shopkeeper who sells remedies for mythic maladies. They understand the body’s symbolic wounds and can stitch up or sabotage a hero’s Mask with equal ease. They’re not always forthcoming with their own motives, which makes them a wildcard ally or a perilous foe.

NPCs in Fortune Row should feel like they could be players in their own right: imperfect, glorious, and capable of stealing a scene if the dice aren’t in your favor. The goal is to create a sense of a neighborhood with a long memory—where yesterday’s rumor might become today’s mission and tomorrow’s legend.

## Running Fortune Row: structure and pacing
Fortune Row shines when you let the setting breathe, but you still need a practical framework so sessions don’t drift into glorious chaos.

### Session zero and establishing the mood
- Confirm Masks, personal stakes, and how each character intersects with Fortune Row landmarks.
- Define two or three local conflicts that can be teased out across sessions (e.g., a rival faction’s bid for control over a specific location; a myth whose power grows when a certain rumor resurfaces).
- Agree on tonal boundaries. Fortune Row loves wit, glam, and danger; set your lines and veils to keep things fun and safe for everyone.

### Scene structure: Investigations, Interludes, and Consequences
- Investigations: The party gathers clues in places like the bakery, the arcade, and the Oracle. Clues may be symbolic rather than literal, encouraging players to interpret meaning through their Masks.
- Interludes: Downtime where relationships are tested or repaired. Interludes reveal character backstory and deepen the city’s mood. They’re also where Fortune Cards can swing the narrative with a light or heavy twist.
- Consequences: Fortune Row is a living thing; every choice nudges the neighborhood’s balance. The GM should remind players that there are costs to every reveal and rewards for restraint.

### Pacing tips
- Use environmental cues: rain that intensifies mythic power, a neon sign that flickers exactly at a character’s turning point, a bakery’s oven that seems to burn with prophecy.
- Keep the Oracle as a narrative engine: It can reveal truths, plant misdirects, or demand a favor in exchange for a future advantage.
- Normalize mythic exposure: When a character leans into their Mask, the city should react—sometimes with helpful context, sometimes with a twist that complicates the next scene.

## Hooks and sample scenarios
Fortune Row thrives on compact, evocative hooks that can be run as one-off missions or the seeds of longer arcs.

- Hook 1: The Fortune Thief
A thief steals a Fortune Card from the Oracle, triggering a cascade of misdirections across Fortune Row. The party must retrieve the card before the city’s myths latch onto it and start rewriting history.
- Hook 2: The Bakery’s Truth
The bread at the local bakery reveals a person’s deepest wish when consumed. A patron’s wish pulls the party into a conflict between two factions who both want control of the bakery’s power source.
- Hook 3: The Neon Broker
A new broker has arrived who can broker promises with literal neon signage. Each promise has a price, and the price may be your memory, your Mask, or a fragment of your own legend.
- Hook 4: The Silent Parade
A city-wide event where a myth attempts to erase a warded location. The party must decode the parade’s symbols to prevent a catastrophe that could erase a piece of Fortune Row’s identity.

## Visual and atmospheric tools
Fortune Row comes alive with sensory detail and visual prompts:

- Color palette: Neon pinks, electric blues, brass, and a constant drizzle that makes reflections look suspiciously mythic.
- Soundscape: A playlist that blends noir saxophone with chimey bells and distant whispers.
- Props: Tickets from The Oracle, a receipt for a wish never cashed in, a fortune cookie that tells the truth about a future you haven’t earned yet.
- Place names: The Coin-Operated Oracle, Moonlit Arcade, Glass Foundry, The Rusted Compass, The Cinnamon Cathedral, The Gilded Alley, The Echoing Market.

Feel free to drop in your own place names to tailor Fortune Row to your group. The goal is to create a shared mental map that players can narrate as they explore the district’s nooks and crannies.

## NPCs: memorable faces you’ll want at the table
NPCs are the lifeblood of Fortune Row. Here are a handful designed to spark intrigue and interaction:

- Lira the Oracle Keeper: The proprietor of The Coin-Operated Oracle, she isn’t a villain, but she isn’t a saint either. She speaks in parables and wears a cloak stitched from old prophecies.
- Vespera Glass: A founder of The Glass Foundry who believes that every myth needs a tool—and every tool needs an honest owner. Their loyalties are mercurial, and their workshop hosts more secrets than gears.
- The Choir’s Lead Tenor: A performer whose voice can bend light and memory. They prefer to settle disputes with a song-off rather than a duel, which can be either charming or terrifying depending on your Mask.
- Old Man Crux: A vendor who sells rumors as if they were currency. He never charges in coin; he charges in trust, which makes him invaluable or dangerous depending on your current debt to the city’s past.

These characters are templates. Feel free to remix, rehome, or retire them as needed. The idea is to give the players a sense that Fortune Row is teeming with life and that every NPC has a reason to talk to a Masked protagonist.

## Practical tips for the GM
- Start with atmosphere, not trivia. Let a scene’s mood hint at the conflict; reveal the specifics only as players push for answers.
- Use local legends as recurring motifs. A myth might appear in different guises across sessions, tying disparate events together and rewarding attentive players.
- Don’t rush the reveal of Fortune Cards. A good twist often happens when a player hesitates to reveal a truth that would upend a local power balance.
- Lean into humor. Fortune Row isn’t a grimdark dungeon; it’s a candy shop of myths where some sweets bite back. A well-timed joke can defuse tension and make a dramatic moment land even harder.

## Map and locations to spotlight in sessions
Fortune Row is best when you emphasize a few signature locations that recur across sessions. Here are some that consistently draw the party back:

- The Coin-Operated Oracle: A fortune-telling shop that can reveal necessities and liabilities in equal measure. It’s the hub where information exchange happens, loyalties are tested, and players sometimes pay with a memory rather than coin.
- The Moonlit Arcade: A late-night arcade where the games aren’t just games—they’re contests of nerve and destiny. Winning a round can grant a temporary edge; losing may reveal a secret you wish remained hidden.
- The Cinnamon Cathedral: A bakery that makes bread with a rumor-powered scent. Its goods don’t just fill a stomach; they fill a moment with meaning.
- The Glass Foundry: A workshop where myths are turned into tools, charms, or traps. It’s where inventions are born and moral lines are tested.

Use these anchors to anchor scenes, then scatter smaller touches around: a vending machine that speaks in cryptic forecasts, a mural that shifts its message when you walk by, a stray cat that seems to know more than it should.

## Final thoughts, pros, and cons
Fortune Row is a flavorful, cinematic setting with a lot of potential for memorable sessions. It rewards players who lean into their Masks and engage with the city as a partner in the storytelling. It also offers a nice balance of roleplay and action, with room for humor and heart as well as danger.

Pros:
- Strong mood and distinctive themes that differentiate Fortune Row from other urban settings.
- Flexible structure that supports both one-shots and longer campaigns.
- Rich NPCs and factions that can be adapted to different playstyles.
- Clear hooks, with a central hub (The Oracle) to drive investigations.

Cons:
- The heavy thematic focus can overwhelm new players who are still learning the core City of Mist system.
- If you’re not careful, the setting can tilt toward style over substance; you’ll want to actively balance mythic flair with character-driven goals.
- Fortune Cards, while flavorful, require a thoughtful implementation to avoid stalling sessions with constant twists.

If you’re a GM who loves to lean into the velvet noir featherlight fantasy that Chicago’s rain might wear if the gods came with streetwise attitude, Fortune Row will feel like coming home. If you prefer a tighter, procedural mystery with fewer melodramatic flourishes, you might want to dial the mythic vibes down a notch and treat Fortune Row as a flavor upgrade rather than the main course.

## Final recommendation and next steps
Fortune Row stands out as a standout campaign setting within City of Mist due to its urban mythos, strong atmosphere, and structured yet flexible approach to storytelling. It provides a playground where players can explore identity, fate, and community as much as they explore criminals, conspiracies, and capers. The coin flips both ways in Fortune Row, and that’s exactly the kind of risk you want when you’re chasing a story that’s equal parts courage and comedy.

If you’re gearing up to run Fortune Row, start with a two-session arc: introduction and the first major reveal. Build the Oracle as your anchor, seed two factions with conflicting goals, and assign each player a local landmark that resonates with their Mask. Gradually, reveal how the city’s myths interact with Fortune Row’s daily life, and let the players decide whether they want to protect the neighborhood or reshape it into something that better fits their ambitions. Either way, Fortune Row will leave a mark on your players and on the map they draw in their notes.
