## Condition and Grading: What to Expect When You Open the Lot
Condition is king in any card collection. For a 1995 lot like this, you’ll be dealing with a wide distribution of wear. Here’s a practical cheat-sheet for evaluating what you’ve got:

- Mint/near-mint: Unread edges, bright colors, minimal surface wear. These look the best in a display case and fetch premium prices.
- Excellent/Very Good: Minor edge wear, occasional small whitening. These are the sweet spot for functional display and casual play. They still look sharp in a binder or protective sleeves.
- Good/Fair: Clear signs of use—creased corners, scratches, nicked edges. These are more about the nostalgia than the aesthetic, but you can still make a playable deck if you’re forgiving with your rules cards.
- Poor: Visible water damage, heavy creasing, or missing corners. Unless you’re a scavenger for parts, these will serve as a cautionary tale for future storage.

In this particular 225-card lot, expect a mix of these grades. The practical approach is to sleeve the entire lot (soft sleeves for the backs, standard sized to preserve alignment) and separate the truly pristine cards for a premium display group. If you’re buying this as an investment item, remember that the market for vintage CCGs is volatile and condition-driven—the mint examples often do best, with price inflation tied to rarity and demand rather than strictly the number of cards included.

For authentication, here are quick checks:
- Look for standard 1995 Marvel branding and logo placement; a lot of fakes surface when someone tries too hard to recreate the era’s typography.
- Check for uniform back design across the lot. Inconsistencies can indicate a mix of reprints or counterfeit sleeves.
- Inspect the card stock for a slightly off-white core; authentic cards from this era have a certain grain and texture that later reissues lack.

## How to Authenticate and Care for Your Cards
Caring for a 1995-era collection requires a light touch and a splash of retro-charm. Here are practical steps to keep your 225-card lot in good condition for years to come:

- Use soft card sleeves: 100-micron sleeves are perfect for keeping the fronts pristine and the backs readable. Avoid harsh plastics that cause static and clingy edges.
- Store vertically in a cool, dry place: The 1990s era wasn’t shy about humidity; keeping cards upright with mild air circulation helps curb warping.
- Keep away from direct sunlight: The combination of fake neon backlighting and sunlight can yellow inks and dull contrasts in a way that makes collectors weep later.
- If you play occasionally, use a clean play space: Clear, flat surfaces reduce edge damage and frame fatigue when shuffling and drawing.
- Archive a card log: Note each card’s condition, any noticeable wear, and its potential value. This helps you track changes over time if you decide to upgrade, trade, or sell.
