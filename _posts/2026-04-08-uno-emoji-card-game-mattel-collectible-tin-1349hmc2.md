### Gameplay, rules, and house-rule friendly territory
If you’re new to UNO, here’s the gist: players take turns laying down cards that match either the color or the number of the previously played card. Action cards like Skip, Reverse, and Draw Two force the next player to either lose a turn or draw extra cards. The objective is to shed all of your cards, ultimately shouting “UNO!” right before you go out.

In UNO Emoji, the emoji cards add a layer of clarity and a dash of chaos. The changes can be summarized as:

- Emoji action cues replace or accompany traditional actions, preserving flow while giving players quick, non-verbal cues.
- Wild emoji cards keep the color-changing mechanic but pair it with emoji flair to signal tone—chaotic rainbow mode, is the emoji’s favorite, perhaps.
- Optional spicy rules: you can add house rules to lean into competition or silliness. Examples include “Emoji Attack” rounds where players must match the emoji on the draw face or do a quick group cheer. Or “Emoji-Only” rounds where players must announce a color change using emojis from memory rather than words, which is exactly the kind of ridiculous fun that makes game nights memorable.
