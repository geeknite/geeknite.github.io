### A Quick Round Tutorial (for the curious radical)
- Shuffle thoroughly and deal each player a hand of stock cards.
- Draw to fill your hand as the game progresses.
- Use your build piles to assemble sequential numbers, with the goal of exhausting your stock cards.
- Use wild cards strategically to block opponents or accelerate your own stacks.
- The first player to empty their stock pile wins.

If you’re reading this because you want a quick, spoiler-free gist, there you go. If you’re intrigued by the psychology of back-and-forth play at a cluster of kitchen tables, you’ll absolutely savor how tension builds in a way that modern “endless, endless” online games rarely deliver.

## Maintenance, Care, and Display: Keeping the Velvet Alive
If you plan to display this piece, choose a dry, stable shelf away from direct sunlight and extreme humidity. Velvet is delightfully dramatic but merciless to moisture and pale light. A glass display case is a great idea if you’re serious about preservation; a simple acrylic case can also work wonders without breaking the bank. For playing, keep the cards in their original box, and consider a card box insert to prevent further edge wear during rounds of raucous laughter.

For cleaning, a soft cloth is your friend. Avoid any liquids that might seep into the box’s fabric or the cards’ edges. If you’re particularly protective, a light archival sleeve for each card can help maintain edge integrity over many playful decades. The key is to treat this as a living artifact, not a museum piece you never touch. It wants to be used, but respectfully so.
