## Durability and maintenance
Durability is the name of the game with any full size table. This model s cabinet finish holds up to the kind of rough play that tends to happen after a few drinks and a couple of questionable plays. The rods can experience some wobble if the mounting hardware loosens over time, but that s a standard maintenance item. Regular checks on leg levelers, rod end bearings, and hardware tightening will keep the experience consistent. Cleaning the table is straightforward: wipe the playing surface with a mild cleaner, avoid abrasive solvents that could strip the finish, and use a soft cloth to avoid micro scratches on the finish.

Two balls are a nice touch; they offer a quick swap when one ball gets dinged from a particularly heroic kick that slices its way into the goal with a satisfying clack. The slide scorers, while convenient, can also be a potential maintenance pitfall if the mechanism collects dust and grit; a quick monthly cleaning with compressed air or a gentle brush will extend their life significantly.

## Care and maintenance tips
- Keep the rods clean and lubricated if your model supports lubrication; use the manufacturer recommended lubricants sparingly to avoid residue buildup.
- Check screws and leg levelers every few weeks if you re using the table heavily. An unlevel table makes a mockery of your perfect passes.
- Clean the playing surface with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner; avoid ammonia based cleaners that could damage finishes.
- Store extra balls in a cool, dry place to avoid cracking or warping.
- If you have kids, consider a protective cover to prevent dings and scratches when not in use.

## Gameplay tips for getting the most out of 22 players
- Establish roles: designate players as forwards, midfielders, and defenders. Rotate through the bench to keep everyone engaged and to avoid overuse injuries in a very short space of a time.
- Practice the passing lanes: with more players, there are more angles to exploit. Work on quick, accurate passes to the attack line rather than trying to launch long range shots from your own defense.
- Use bank shots sparingly but effectively: the corners and side rails can create surprising angles that beat a distracted opponent who s trying to chase the ball across the table.
- Embrace the slide scorers: use them to maintain tempo and ensure fair scoring, especially during the chaotic mid game moments. They can also serve as a quick visual reminder for the scoreboard so you don t have to rely on memory alone.
