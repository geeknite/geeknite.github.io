## Maintenance, troubleshooting, and longevity tips

Even the most reliable gear needs occasional TLC. Here are practical maintenance notes to keep your EL300952 happy:

- Regular roller cleaning: Paper residue and minor dust can accumulate on rollers. A quick wipe with a lint-free cloth and a touch of isopropyl-free cleaner keeps the grip reliable. Do not soak the rollers; that would be unnecessary drama and potential damage.
- Inspect for wear: Over time, the feed rollers can wear down. If you notice more frequent misfeeds or skews, consider replacing the rollers or investigating alignment again. The maintenance window is small, but the payoff is big.
- Paper stock discipline: Use consistent paper stock in the feeder. Avoid extremely curly or damp sheets, which can lead to feeding inconsistencies. If you must print on a novelty stock, run a small test batch to ensure compatibility.
- Sensor checks: If you see repeated jams, check the sensor window for dust or obstructions. Clean gently and re-test. A clean sensor means fewer false jam detections and smoother operation.
- Firmware and calibration: If your device offers a calibration routine, run it after the feeder has been installed for a while. It helps align the timing between feeding and the engine, reducing off-by-one errors in multi-page jobs.

### Common symptoms and quick fixes
- Symptom: Sporadic jams on the feed tray. Quick fix: reseat the feeder, ensure the alignment guides match your paper size, and check for any curled edges.
- Symptom: Paper skew or misfeeds toward the end of the batch. Quick fix: confirm stack height is within recommended range and restart with a fresh batch if necessary.
- Symptom: No response from the feeder. Quick fix: power cycle the main unit and the feeder, verify the connectors are seated, and confirm the status lights indicate ready.
