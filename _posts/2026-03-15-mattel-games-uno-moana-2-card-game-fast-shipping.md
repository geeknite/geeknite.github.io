## How to Play: Quick Start Guide and Rule Tweaks

UNO has always been about speed, color matching, and occasional chaos induced by “Draw X” cards. Moana 2 adds a safe number of new elements that don’t reinvent the wheel, but they do re-route the wheel’s path enough to keep players on their toes.

- Basic setup remains familiar: shuffle, deal seven cards to each player, one card face up to start the discard pile. The turn order remains clockwise, unless someone plays a special card that changes the tempo.
- New Moana 2 cards introduce two core mechanics:
  - Tide Cards: These can flip the current color or reset the color requirement to another color in the same turn. You’ll need to stay alert because the tide can turn mid-round and force you to pivot your strategy on the fly.
  - Island Power Cards: These let you perform a single, targeted intervention on another player (like forcing them to skip or draw cards) but with a strategic cost. It’s a small push that makes late-round comebacks interesting without implementing an absurd “kill switch” for the whole table.

As with any UNO variant, the most important thing is to agree on rules before you start playing. Moana 2’s quick-start guide is helpful, and the in-box reference card is fairly lucid, but if you’re planning a tournament-style night, you’ll want to discuss house rules around draw penalties and the use of Tide Cards in late-game scenarios.
