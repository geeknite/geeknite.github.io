## House Rules and Variations

Few games survive without some local adaptations. UNO Party is no exception. Here are a few house rules that can either accelerate the chaos or provide a more controlled experience:
- Five-Card Hand: Each player starts with five cards in a party setting; this reduces the randomness of a single lucky draw while preserving the social element.
- Color Rotation: When a Wild card is played, the color chosen must be wild enough to evoke a loud reaction, which adds energy to the room but also helps set the pace for the rest of the round.
- Penalty Auction: After someone calls UNO, the next player can propose a minor penalty to speed the round along. The group votes; the winner gives the penalty to the UNO caller.
- Swap Roulette: A player who draws a Wild swap must offer to swap hands with someone wearing a color that matches the new color on the discard pile.

These rules can be mixed and matched to fit your group’s temperament. The key is to keep the party inclusive so that everyone feels like they can contribute to the chaos in a way that is fun rather than punitive.
