## Installation and Fit: Getting It Right the First Time
### Setup and Steps
Here’s a practical walkthrough to help you avoid a late-night annoyance.
1) Measure your opening. Write down the width and height, and note any irregularities in wall surfaces. If you’ve got a slanted doorway or a corner with uneven walls, you’ll need the adjustable spacers.
2) Layout the panels in the configuration you want. Most designs allow for straight runs, but you can also angle panels for L-shaped openings in larger play areas.
3) Attach the end caps or mounting hardware as required by the kit. If you’re using tension mounts, you’ll tighten until the door feels firmly anchored, then test with a gentle push near the latch.
4) Install the door on a dry, clean surface. Make sure the latch is oriented for easy access by grown-ups but not child-friendly enough to be mind-reader’s level, because toddlers tend to be surprisingly persistent when there are snacks involved.
5) Double-check alignment. The door should swing smoothly, latch securely, and not bind at any point during a full-range motion.

This process tends to take around 20-40 minutes for an average doorway. If you’re a first-timer, give yourself a little extra time and approach it with patience—there’s nothing wrong with taking breaks between steps to chase a wandering toddler or to console a mislaid snack.

### In Narrow Spaces and Odd Doorways
One of the most common challenges is a narrow or slightly off-kilter doorway. The Brown and Cream gate performs well in many standard spaces thanks to adjustable panels and versatile hardware. If your doorway is uniquely shaped or you’re mounting near a baseboard heater, pay particular attention to the gap width and ensure the heater’s clearance doesn’t conflict with the gate’s operational area. A little extra spacer or a slight reconfiguration can fix most issues, and you’ll be glad you didn’t force a square peg into a rounded hole when you witness your child glide through the hinge at high speed.
