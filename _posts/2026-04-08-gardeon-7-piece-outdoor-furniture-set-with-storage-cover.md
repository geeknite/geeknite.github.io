## Assembly and setup tips
### Quick-start guide
- Clear a flat workspace to lay out all pieces; nothing ruins a good patio vibe like misaligned driftwood-sculpted legs.
- Sort bolts and washers by size to avoid hunting for the right piece while cursing under your breath.
- Start with the table frame to anchor the layout, then attach chairs. It helps to have a second pair of hands for alignment and moral support.
- Use a light lubricant on bolt threads to avoid cross-threading. You’re not building a spaceship; you’re building a backyard sanctuary, but the same principles apply: avoid pinching and cross-threading.
- Check for wobble and tighten gradually. If something looks off, back off and re-tighten in a cross pattern.

### Time investment
For a single person with a reasonable amount of mechanical intuition, expect roughly 60-90 minutes from unboxing to fully assembled. A duo or trio of helpers can shave this time down and also provide crucial moral support when you realize you attached the wrong chair to the wrong leg for the third time.

## Weather resistance, maintenance, and storage
### The storage cover as a hero
The storage cover is not just a nice accessory; it’s a shield against sun bleaching, fiber rot, and the occasional neighborhood drizzle. It’s designed to cover cushions and protect fabrics from UV damage and moisture, giving your cushions a longer life. Realistically, the cover’s fit is good but not perfect in extreme climates, and you may need to adjust the drawstrings or Velcro in heavy winds. For best results, store the cushions in a dry place during off-seasons and use the cover during light to moderate rain.

### Cleaning and care tips
- Wipe down frames with a damp cloth monthly to keep them looking fresh.
- Spot-clean cushions as needed and wash removable covers according to the care label.
- Let cushions dry completely before storing to prevent mold.
- Use mild soap for stubborn stains; avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade fabric.

### Climate considerations
In humid or rainy climates, plan for seasonal maintenance. If your area experiences snow, store the set indoors or under a heavier cover to reduce weather exposure. The materials are generally durable, but longevity increases with proactive care rather than reactive restoration.
