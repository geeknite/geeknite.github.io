## Gameplay quick tour
Exploding Kittens is essentially a pass the hot potato game with a few tactical wrinkles. The objective is simple to articulate yet delightfully slippery in practice: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card. If you do draw one and you cannot defuse it with a Defuse card or a clever trick, your character is out of the game. The last player standing wins. The rules nestle into your brain with surprising ease, which is part of the design brilliance. It is a party game masquerading as a quirky card battle that can be enjoyed by non-gamers and enthusiasts alike.

### How a round plays out
- Players take turns drawing cards from the deck, one by one.
- The deck contains action cards that let you peek ahead, skip turns, force others to draw, or manipulate the order in more chaotic ways.
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten and cannot Defuse it, you are out of that round.
- Defuse cards are the lifelines; they let you survive a peek at a kitten card, often by deploying a surprising plan that can turn the tide for the rest of the table.
- The game continues until only one player remains. The pacing is brisk, often finishing in under 20 minutes, which makes it a perfect filler between longer games, or a late-night warm-up when you want to blame the deck for your poor life choices.

The presence of the See the Future cards, which let you glimpse upcoming draws, adds a strategic flavor that keeps people from simply grabbing and hoping. It is not a deep strategy game, but it rewards memory, bluffing, and social manipulation with a level of depth that can surprise you if you lean into the absurdist humor.

### See the Future and other tactical flourishes
See the Future cards let you peek at the top few cards of the deck. This is a small but mighty tool that creates opportunities for staged mind games. The psychological element is the glue that holds Exploding Kittens together beyond the novelty. You can set up scenarios where your opponents squirm while you plan a safe escape route, or you can bluff your way into a risky move that pays off in the most dramatic fashion. The platform for chaos is well-suited to backyard gatherings, living room tournaments, and the occasional chaos of a late-night gaming session when you need a quick, loud laugh with friends.
