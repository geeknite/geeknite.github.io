## The Authenticity Guide: How to Tell Real From Replica
As with many 1990s promos, there are counterfeit dreams and genuine nick-nacks. Here are a few practical tests to help you tell real from replica, without needing a PhD in cardography:

- Look at the stock and gloss. Real promos often have a particular gloss level and a stock that feels slightly different from standard UNO cards. If the card looks unusually bright or has a suspicious sticker-like finish, you might be looking at a reproduction.
- Check the artwork fidelity. Polar bears granted a certain palette and line weight; if the art looks overly simplified or the colors shift in odd ways under light, it’s a red flag.
- Confirm the packaging tie-in. If your card is part of a promotional deck, there should be some catalog or ad imagery that matches the exact version you have. If there’s no corroborating promotional collateral, you’re probably looking at a fan-made item.
- Pro guidance: trust the provenance. A card that has been documented by collectors or listed in a catalog with a publish date from the era is more likely to be legitimate than one with a vague backstory and no images of the original pack.
