### Cleaning and Maintenance: The Short Version

- Regular cleaning: Vacuum or sweep to remove dirt, hair, and small debris.
- Spot cleaning: Blot spills with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper.
- Stain treatment: Use mild detergent and water; test a small area first to ensure colorfastness.
- Outdoor maintenance: If used outside, rinse with water to remove dirt during routine cleaning, especially after pollen season or a barbecue night. Allow to air dry fully before storing.

### Safety and Maintenance Tips: Practical Geekiness

- Use a rug pad for extra grip and to extend the rug’s life. A pad acts like a tiny force field against sliding furniture and accidental slide-offs during intense couch naps.
- Keep it dry when possible. Though water resistant, repeated soaking will challenge any fiber system and could lead to mildew growth if left to sit.
- For sun-exposed areas, rotate the rug every few months to even out color wear and fading. A simple rotation helps you avoid the “the sun moves, so should you” problem.
