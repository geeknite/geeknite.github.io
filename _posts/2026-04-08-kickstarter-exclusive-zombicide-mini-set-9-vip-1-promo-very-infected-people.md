The “Very Infected People” concept is design-forward: these are not just zombies with new weapons; they’re characters with personalities—right down to the scowls and the tattered outfits. As is often the case with Kickstarter exclusives, the quality varies slightly from piece to piece due to the nature of mini production. For Set 9 VIP 1 Promo, the miscasts are minimal and well within the tolerances you expect from a mainstream board game mini line. The unique “Very Infected People” minis are designed to be visually striking on the battlefield table, and they do land with the ‘these are showpieces’ energy. If you’re a detail-oriented painter, you’ll find plenty to sink your brush into; if you prefer to wash and dry-brush your way to tabletop glory, you’ll still be satisfied with the play-ready finish.

If you’re the kind of gamer who decorates the table with special units during story arcs or organizes a “VIP Night” where these minis lead your crew into a dramatic siege, the price becomes a feature rather than a flaw. If, on the other hand, you’re purely in it for the mechanical upgrade and a side of exclusivity, you’ll probably feel the same tug-of-war that many players feel with premium promos: is it worth it for a handful of assets, or would the cash be better spent on another big expansion?
