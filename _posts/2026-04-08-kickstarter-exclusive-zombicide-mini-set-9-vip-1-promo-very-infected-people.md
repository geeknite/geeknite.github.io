## Painting and Personalization Tips (New Section)

If you’re a painter or want to dip your brush into “prestige” gaming minis without the full commitment, these tips help elevate the VIP set without requiring a full-scale army painting session:

- Start with a muted base color in the greens and grays family, then layer in pale highlights for exposed skin to simulate sickness and mutation.
- Use a blue-tinted glaze on exposed flesh to imply cold infection and a strange luminescence under dim light.
- Pick a consistent base tone for all VIP minis to create a cohesive mini-collection read on the board.
- A quick, thin wash of green or brown into crevices will bring out textures and details in the folds of torn clothing.
- For eyes, a tiny dab of gloss or a bright highlight can give the infected a gaze that reads as unsettlingly alive in dim lighting—perfect for post-game photos.

If you want a visually striking display piece, consider pairing the infected with contrasting terrain: a dark, industrial table vs. a glow-in-the-dark entrance zone creates dramatic storytelling opportunities. And yes, you can comfortably display these minis in a dedicated cabinet or on a shelf with a small LED light strip to emulate “ambient infection light.”

## Photography, Display, and Shelf Life (Extended)

### How to Photograph Zombicide Mini Sets
Good lighting is your friend. A basic lightbox with neutral walls and a couple of reflectors can yield moody, cinematic shots that accentuate torn fabrics, pale faces, and the subtle green glow. Try a shallow depth of field to blur the background and keep the VIP minis sharp—ideal for social media posts or blog thumbnails. If you’re feeling fancy, a backlight behind the mini wave can simulate a streetlight glow, which adds a film-noir vibe to your pictures.

### Display Tips
When placing the VIP minis on display, anchor them with a single color that contrasts with their base. The eye is naturally drawn to the most saturated hue, so using a metallic or a bold color in the display can help the VIPs pop without competing with other elements on the shelf. For diorama setups, combine them with a few non-infected survivors to illustrate the narrative tension: “Will they flee, or will they show more of that infection face?”

### Shelf Life and Handling
Quality remains robust through repeated handling. Keep the boxes in a climate-controlled space and avoid direct sunlight to protect the paint layers and plastic. If you display the minis, rotate poses to avoid lane-marking wear and ensure a long shelf life for your display piece. It’s not just about owning the set; it’s about preserving its story for future sessions and guests who lean in to whisper, “Tell me about those VIPs again.”
