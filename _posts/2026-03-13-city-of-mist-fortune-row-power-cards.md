### What are Power Cards, anyway?
Power Cards in Fortune Row aren’t simply stat boosters wrapped in glossy cardstock. They’re narrative prompts, situational modifiers, and comic-book-style hooks that you can drop into a scene with almost zero setup. The deck is designed to push players toward story beats rather than pure metagame optimization. Think of them as the “plot twists you can physically hand to a GM” rather than “the numbers you need to win.”

The cards are color-coded by vibe: red for danger and bold action, blue for cunning and misdirection, gold for luck and fortune, and smoky-black for shadows and long-term consequences. Each card is a mini-scenery-mcentaur: a single sentence that, if read aloud with the right tone, can magnetize the whole table into a new thread of the story.

Implied in the design is a belief that Fortune Row isn’t about “beating” the story; it’s about letting the story beat you back in the most entertaining way possible. That’s a defensive description that actually translates into a powerful mechanic: these cards encourage character decisions that feel inevitable in a cinematic sense rather than mechanically optimal in a spreadsheet sense.

### How to use Fortune Row in play
Here are some practical tips for harnessing Power Cards without turning your table into a rule-cycling nightmare:

1) Treat cards as “dramatic prompts” first, modifiers second. If a card contradicts your tactical plan, lean into the narrative: what does this say about your character’s persona or the city’s mood?
2) Use a “card draw” moment strategically. In a tense beat, draw a card to determine whether luck turns or catastrophe strikes. This creates suspense and gives players something tangible to react to.
3) Limit usage in early sessions. In the early chapters, keep the number of active Power Cards modest to avoid overpowering the core mechanics. You want the cards to feel special, not ubiquitous.
4) Encourage collaborative storytelling. The cards work best when players propose how the card’s effect unfolds, not when a single player tries to meta-engineer the outcome.

If you’re worried about consistency, remember: City of Mist is a game where “consistency” sometimes means “everyone is invested in the vibe.” The Power Cards should amplify that vibe, not derail it with a math problem.

### A couple of cautionary notes
No product, no matter how inspired, is perfect for every table. A few caveats to consider:
- If your group loves strict crunch, Fortune Row might feel a little loose at times. It’s not a replacement for rules; it’s a narrative accelerator.
- If you have a newer GM, you’ll want to build a rhythm around card usage rather than letting it become a chaotic free-for-all. The key is to integrate it into the session tempo and avoid letting any one card dominate the discussion.
- If your table has a heavy focus on combat scenes, you’ll want to pair Fortune Row with a clear policy about when to use Power Cards in or out of combat. The cards excel in drama, not in numbers games, and you’ll want to manage expectations accordingly.

That said, for most groups who love the mythic-cop-with-a-heart-of-silver aesthetic, Fortune Row is a delightful upgrade that adds texture without derailing the core City of Mist experience.
