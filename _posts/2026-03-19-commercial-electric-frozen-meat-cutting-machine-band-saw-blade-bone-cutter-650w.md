### Working with frozen meat
Frozen meat requires a mix of power and patience. Pre freezing to a moderate level helps the blade bite rather than slide, and maintaining a steady pace reduces heat buildup and blade wander. The machine handles the cold with minimal impact on performance, and the cutting edge remains sharp for longer than expected given the workload. The result is a clean slice that preserves the integrity of the meat and reduces waste.

### Bones and bone cutting
Bone cutting is a test of the machine's resilience. This unit can handle bone fragments with careful technique and smaller pieces. Do not attempt to force the blade through bone; instead, feed with a steady hand and let the blade do the work. The guard and proper alignment prevent dangerous kickback, and the blade tension keeps the cut consistent. This is not a stand alone bone saw for a slaughterhouse, but for a kitchen that handles bone-in cuts, it is capable and efficient.

## Safety features and ergonomics
Safety is built into the design with a blade guard, interlock, emergency stop, and a splash shield. These features help reduce risk while you are juggling orders and a busy service. ### PPE and handling
We recommend cut resistant gloves, an approved apron, and eye protection when working near the blade, especially during blade changes. Training your staff to respect the guard is essential; this is not a gadget to show off in a demo and then forget in a rush.

## Maintenance and cleaning
Maintenance is straightforward if you follow a routine. Unplug the unit before servicing. Detach the blade guard and clean each part separately. Dry thoroughly to prevent rust on exposed metal parts. Periodically lubricate the rails and tension mechanism as per the manual. For sanitation, wash all contact surfaces with a sanitizer approved for food contact; avoid soaking the motor or electronics. After heavy use, reassemble and ensure the machine runs smoothly before returning to service.

### Cleaning steps
- Disconnect power
- Remove blade guard and wipe down the blade assembly
- Rinse rails with warm water and mild detergent
- Dry completely and apply light machine oil to rails
- Reassemble and test run with a dummy load (not ice blocks for your first attempt)
