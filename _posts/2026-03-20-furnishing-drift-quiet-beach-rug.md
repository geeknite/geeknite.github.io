## Care and maintenance: keeping the coast clean

### Routine cleaning tips

- Vacuum regularly with a low-pile setting to remove surface dust and stray beach grains that wander in from the outdoors. A weekly routine helps maintain the rug’s crisp appearance and prevents the buildup of dog hair in hidden corners.
- For spills, blot—not rub—with a clean cloth. Rubbing can push stains deeper into the fibers and create a souvenir you didn’t intend to keep. If you’re dealing with a stubborn stain, a mild detergent solution usually does the trick without dulling the fabric’s color.
- For deeper refreshes, rotate the rug every few months to prevent uneven wear and to keep the texture from taking on a one-directional memory. If you’re particularly ambitious, a light steam clean (on a safe setting) can resurrect the fibers, but avoid soaking the rug—the drift towards that “wet beach towel” vibe is very real and very hard to reverse.

### Seasonal care and long-term health of the fibers

In salt-air environments or spaces with high humidity, you’ll want to ensure the rug dries properly after cleaning to prevent mold or mildew. A well-ventilated room or a sunny window can help. If you’re prone to allergies, a standard vacuum with a HEPA filter can keep dust and pollen in check, contributing to a healthier indoor climate. The goal is steady maintenance, not a spa day for your rug, but a little pampering now and then pays off in the rug’s long life and continued good looks.
