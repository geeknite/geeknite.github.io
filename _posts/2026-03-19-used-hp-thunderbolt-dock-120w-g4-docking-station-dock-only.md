## Connectivity and Setup Tips
Here are practical tips to get the most out of a used HP TB4 G4 dock:
- Visual inspection: Check the TB4 upstream port for any lint or debris and test the monitor outputs with a known-good cable. Examine USB ports for springiness and signs of wobble.
- Firmware update: If you can, connect the dock to a laptop and perform any available firmware updates through HP’s software. This can resolve compatibility quirks with displays or USB devices.
- Cable plan: Create a cable plan before you buy. At minimum you’ll want: a TB4 cable (supplied by your laptop or the dock), two monitor cables (HDMI and DisplayPort or two DisplayPorts), a network cable, and at least one USB-C or USB-A cable for a peripheral if you plan to use it in the docked state.
- Power considerations: Confirm the dock’s power compatibility with your laptop. If you have a laptop that charges via USB-C or TB4, a 120W supply from the dock should be sufficient. If your laptop requires more, you may need to verify actual power delivery and potential workarounds.
- Cable management: A dock is only as good as the mess you don’t create. Consider cable clips and a simple cable tray to keep your desk from turning into a spaghetti jungle.
