## How the Community Pack Changes the Gameplay
The heartbeat of any expansion is how it modifies the rhythm of play. The Community Pack for Million Dollars, But... introduces three primary levers that influence outcomes:

- New prompts that lean into social dynamics and team interplay rather than solo one-liners.
- Expanded response options that unlock more creative or strategic replies rather than the same old punchlines.
- Optional mission cards that impose lightweight constraints to keep rounds fresh and a touch more chaotic.

Across a typical session, you will notice a more varied tempo. Some rounds swing to quick, witty exchanges when players quickly align with a single narrative thread, while others slow down as teams negotiate the direction of a story for points. The expansion does not attempt to make every round a grand narrative arc; instead it smuggles in enough variety to prevent fatigue while preserving the core fast-paced bite of the base game.

One notable improvement is the tiered structure of prompts. Some prompts are designed for pure humor and quick reactions, while others invite a bit of strategy and risk assessment. This gives organizers a convenient toolkit to calibrate the evening to the group size and energy level. For groups that like to lean into roleplay, the new prompts invite pockets of improv where players build mini narratives around outrageous offers and moral choices. For more competitive groups, there are prompts that reward clever pairing and timely callbacks. It is a flexible design that respects different play styles rather than forcing a single mode of engagement on everyone at the table.

## What I Wish Was Different
No expansion is perfect, and the Community Pack is no exception. A couple of minor areas could use a touch of polish. Some players reported a slight mismatch between certain prompts and the response cards, which could artificially tilt the round toward a particular player who has a knack for a given prompt. A tweak in card distribution or a small rule nudge could alleviate this slight imbalance. Another improvement would be a concise one-page cheatsheet summarizing new rules and how they interact with the base game. While the included sheet is clear enough, a quick reference on the table during play would reduce any momentary confusion and speed up the rounds.

Finally, for players who prefer an entirely different vibe, the expansion will not radically alter the game into a different genre. It remains in the same orbit as the base game, which is a strength for players who want incremental novelty rather than a totally new system. If you are seeking a dramatic mechanical overhaul, you may want to look at other titles or future expansions in the same line rather than expecting this one to reimagine the core experience.
