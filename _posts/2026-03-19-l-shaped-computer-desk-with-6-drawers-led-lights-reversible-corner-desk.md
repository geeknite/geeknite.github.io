## Assembly experience and initial setup
### Time, effort, and tips
Assembly is a two-state operation: parts housekeeping and actual assembly. Counting the screws, washers, and brackets, you’ll probably be stacking pieces for a couple of hours. The instruction manual is clear enough, but you should expect to pause for snack breaks and a few moments of lament for your lost spare tool that shows up only after you’ve bought a new one. A second pair of hands helps with the long leg alignment and the corner joint, especially when leveling the two arms on an uneven floor.

Pro tips: group screws by type, loosely assemble the frame first, then tighten progressively to ensure square corners. Use a level to check the top surface after you have mounted the drawers and panels. If you plan to mount a monitor arm, do so before fully tightening the long arm’s bolts to avoid clearance issues. The LED wiring is straightforward and typically tucks neatly into channels, but you may want to test the lighting before final concealing the cables so you can tweak brightness and color mode without disassembling anything.
