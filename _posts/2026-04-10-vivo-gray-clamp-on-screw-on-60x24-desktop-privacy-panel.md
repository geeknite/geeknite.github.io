## Mounting Options: Clamp-on vs Screw-on
A standout feature of this VIVO panel is the dual mounting approach. The clamp-on method is friendly to desks with rounded edges or thinner lips. It’s also a good option when you want to temporarily add privacy for a sprint project or a workshop session with a coworker who needs a bit of personal space without committing to a long-term desk modification.

The screw-on mount, on the other hand, provides a rock-solid attachment. It’s the friend you call when you want the panel staying put through cable pulls, keyboard swipes, and the occasional clumsy chair bounce. It takes a touch more time to install, but the payoff is a panel that doesn’t drift or shift under heavy typing, intense screen glare, or the occasional game-night LAN party where someone accidentally knocks the desk with a celebratory high-five.

If you’re in a rental or frequently swap work surfaces, clamp-on is the pragmatic approach. If you’re building a semi-permanent station or you know your desk layout isn’t leaving the same room anytime soon, screw-on is the more stable option. Honestly, most geeks end up preferring a hybrid approach: clamp-on while you test the fit, then move to screws for a longer-lasting installation once you’re sure the placement will stay. A little planning goes a long way here; you don’t want to discover mid-project that your monitor is now framed out by the privacy panel and your GPU loves this new angle about as much as you do.

## Installation Tips and Troubleshooting
- Pre-measure your desk thickness and edge profile. A one-minute measurement now saves you hours of post-install frustration.
- Start with the clamp mounting to test fit. If it stays stable, upgrade to screws for a more permanent solution.
- Use a rubber pad or cloth between the panel and your desk edge to prevent surface scratches.
- Bring a small flashlight so you can see the under-desk screws in low-light setups. If you’re a night owl, this is essential.
- If the panel wobbles slightly after installation, tighten the screws incrementally and recheck alignment. Avoid cranking until you crush the desk or accidentally launch a USB-C hub into orbit.

Maintenance: wipe the panel with a soft cloth and a mild cleaner. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the matte finish over time. It’s not a touchscreen — you’re not cleaning a laptop keyboard with incredible precision; just keep it clean and dust-free so your desk can maintain its sleek, minimal aesthetic.
