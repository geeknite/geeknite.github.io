# Dixit 2nd Edition Box Review

Dixit is a party game about stories and pictures, a title that has become something of a modern classic in casual gaming spaces. The 2nd Edition from Asmodee is not a radical overhaul; it is a refined edition, polished for new players while keeping the charm for longtime fans. The copy I am reviewing arrived in box fair NM condition, meaning the outer box shows a little wear but the interior is clean, the cards are crisp, and the tokens and boards are undamaged. It is a good state for a game that some players rely on to carry a night when the group includes people new to modern board games.

What you get in the box is a deck of 84 image cards, a handful of color coded voting tokens, a compact scoring track, and the simple yet sturdy inner components that allow the game to be set up in minutes. The cards are the main event here, a gallery of dreamlike scenes that look both cinematic and painterly. The 2nd Edition updates the art in a few places, adds a more uniform feel to the backs, and tightens up the rulebook language so that new players can pick up the flow without a heavy hand from the teacher at the table. The result is a game that invites you to tell a story, and then invites your friends to respond with their own interpretations, all while you chase points.

First impressions matter, and the box art is a good starter. The cover image sets a mood that says this game is about imagination, not about winning at the expense of your friends. The interior art continues that vibe, with cards that lean into surreal imagery rather than direct narrative cues. Some players love the dreamlike quality, others feel a little unsettled by it, and a few simply revel in the absurdity of a cat riding a fish through a cloudscape. In the spirit of Dixit, the 2nd Edition makes you lean into your imagination and trust the group to fill in the blanks.

Setup is extremely straightforward. Shuffle the image deck, deal six cards to each player, and place scoring markers on the zero space of the track. The storyteller selects a card from their hand and provides a clue that can be a single word, a phrase, or even a short sentence. The other players secretly pick a card from their hands that best matches the clue. All the chosen cards are shuffled and revealed face up, and players vote for which card they think belongs to the storyteller. The storyteller reveals their card, points are awarded, and the round advances. The round structure is intentionally simple, but the magic comes from the interplay between the clue and the visual prompt. How well your clue aligns with the interpretations around the table determines whether you reap the biggest rewards or quietly retire to the shadows with a small handful of pity points.

The art and the mood drive a lot of the experience. Dixit is not a game of perfect information or deep tactical planning; it is a conversation starter, a spark for shared memory, a prompt to tell a story that you would not tell in normal conversation. The 2nd Edition preserves that sense of wonder while not making the game feel like a chore to teach or to learn. The cards carry the weight of the tabletop storytelling tradition, with artists recalling folklore, dream sequences, and pop culture vibes that blend into something a little different on every play. The art is not only decoration; it is a crucial tool in how players craft clues and how others interpret them. The 2nd Edition strikes a balance here by offering a broader range of imagery that can be used in a number of different contexts, from family game night to a more boisterous party setting.

Mechanically, Dixit is about bluffing and interpretation. A strong clue that is too obvious can hand victory to the storyteller, while a cryptic clue can frustrate players who feel they cannot connect the card back to it. The art gives you signals about what kind of clues will work, but the social dynamics determine the actual success. In a cozy four to six player game you might see a lively exchange of ideas as players defend their card selections and debate whether the clue was crafted to misdirect or to illuminate. In a trio, the dynamic shifts a bit because there are fewer opinions at the table and each vote carries more weight. The 2nd Edition adapts gracefully to both extremes and offers a similar experience across a wide range of player counts. It is a game that rewards creative risk taking, but it does not punish players for choosing a more subtle approach either.

If you are debating the value of a single card deck versus expansions, the 2nd Edition has enough richness to stand on its own. The deck of 84 cards is large enough to provide variety over many sessions, and the curated nature of the imagery means you will not see the same prompt repeated too soon. There is also room for creativity in how you present a clue. A one word clue such as creepy, luminous, or curious can be enough in some rounds, while in other rounds you can craft a mini story that hints at the card without revealing too much. The fun is in watching the table decode the intended meaning while also enjoying the visual feast on the table before them.

A note about production value. The cards are comfortably heavy, with a linen finish that reduces glare and makes handling pleasant. The card backs remain consistent across the deck, a thoughtful touch that prevents accidental reveals during the round’s card reveal. The scoring tokens are bright and easy to read, which helps during a fast paced session after a long day of work. The box itself is sturdy enough to survive regular playroom use, though if you are a traveler or you move games often you will want to protect the surface with a bag or a sleeve. The inner layout is clean with a clear path from setup to scoring, which reduces the chance that someone will misplace a token or forget a rule during a late night session.

In terms of accessibility, Dixit shines for players who want to participate without heavy rulebooks and long turns. The 2nd Edition keeps this accessible approach intact by offering a short and friendly rulebook that is easy to teach in a single session. The prompts are universal enough to be understood by players who speak different languages, and the reliance on shared cultural references is minimal compared to a lot of modern party games. That said, the art is culturally rich and can evoke different associations depending on the players. The game invites everyone to share their personal perspective and to give everyone a chance to feel heard. This makes Dixit a good candidate for family gatherings, classroom settings, or casual game nights where the goal is to share a friendly experience rather than a rigorous competition.

If you want to compare Dixit to other storytelling games, there are a few strong options to consider. For players who want a more structured experience with a little more strategy, Hashik and Mysterium offer different takes on the storytelling mechanic with more pronounced attempt to guessers. Dixit remains a lighter option that emphasizes mood and conversation over heavy deduction. The second edition sits comfortably between the casual party vibe and the more serious storytelling experiments in the board game space. The differences are subtle but meaningful for players who care about the tone of their games as much as the mechanics.

Artistic and narrative games have a certain flavor of culture around them, and Dixit has carved out a distinctive space in that niche. If you enjoy a sense of wonder and a willingness to invest in a shared joke or a shared moment of awe, this game is well suited to your table. It is not a deeply mechanical game or a brute force competition. It is instead a social experience that invites imagination, humor, and gentle competition around the table. The 2nd Edition does not try to fix something that was not broken. Instead it refines what was already working by offering a more polished presentation and a few small rule clarifications that help avoid confusion during the moment of play.

For this review, I ran Dixit 2nd Edition with three different groups of players to see how the experience shifted with varying numbers and different comfort levels with storytelling. In all cases, the core delight remained: a set of strangers or friends can build a moment of shared imagination around a table that feels almost magical in its simplicity. The slow rise of laughter as a host of misinterpretations emerge is the heartbeat of the experience. There are too many memorable moments to count when someone guessed a card that felt almost perfectly aligned with the clue, and when someone misdirected the crowd with a seemingly obvious but actually clever card choice. Dixit can be chaotic in the best possible way, and the 2nd Edition embraces that chaos rather than trying to contain it.

To help with the ever present question of is it worth it, here is the bottom line. If you want a game night that revolves around storytelling, creative art, and social interaction with a gentle competitive edge, Dixit 2nd Edition is a strong pick. It scales well from three to six players, offers attractive production values, and presents a familiar yet refreshed take on the original. It is one of those games that people remember long after the night ends, because the stories told at the table become small legends in their own right. The art and the mood create a shared memory you can revisit on subsequent plays, and that is a rare thing in a hobby where many games blur into the background after the first few sessions.

As for the box condition labeled NM, a box fair NM is a good sign. It means that the outer packaging has survived well, and the interior is in excellent condition to support years of play. If you are a collector or you simply want a copy that looks good on the shelf, a near mint box adds to the feeling that this is not just a game but a keepsake of the nights you spent with friends, turning ordinary evenings into something a little more magical.

If you want to seek this exact edition on your favorite retailer, you will find the Dixit 2nd Edition listed as a polished reprint with the same cinematic feel that launched this line to fame. The price is often influenced by the edition and any bundled expansions or promos that come with the particular copy you choose. The best approach is to compare a few sellers and look for a certified description that confirms the edition and the deck count. If you are buying used, ensure the card backs show no discoloration and the cards remain flat with minimal edge wear, as this can degrade the overall aesthetics and the puzzling charm of the game.

With all of this in mind, the bottom line remains clear. Dixit 2nd Edition is a celebration of imagination. It invites you to share a moment of wonder with your friends and family, to tell a story, and to see your group respond to a set of images in surprising and delightful ways. It does not demand a deep strategic brain or years of training; it asks only that you show up with a willingness to participate in the shared theater of imagination. In a world full of heavy, crunchy games that require a degree in mathematics to operate, Dixit stands out as a gentle reminder that not every game has to be a battle to the top of the leaderboard.
