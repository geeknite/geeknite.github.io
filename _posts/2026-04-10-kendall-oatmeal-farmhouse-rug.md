## Cleaning and Maintenance

### Routine Care

For upkeep, a regular vacuum is your friend. Use a vacuum with a gentle setting or a brush attachment that is designed for delicate fibers. The goal is to remove surface dust and hair without pulling the fibers out of place. A weekly pass is enough for most households, with a deeper clean every few months depending on foot traffic and the presence of furry companions. If you have a stubborn crumb or a spill, treat it immediately by blotting rather than rubbing to avoid pushing the spill deeper into the fibers.

### Spot Cleaning and Stains

Spots should be addressed with a mild cleaner and a clean cloth. Do not scrub aggressively; you may fray the surface or alter the nap. Instead, blot from the outside of the stain toward the center to prevent spreading. For typical kitchen spills, a small amount of dish soap mixed with water can help lift the stain, followed by thorough rinsing to remove soap residues that could attract more dirt. For more stubborn stains, you may consider a dedicated carpet cleaner formulated for synthetic blends and follow the product instructions carefully. Always test a small inconspicuous area before applying any cleaner to ensure colorfastness.

### Odors and Refreshing

If your space begins to smell like a gym sock after long winter evenings, a light odor removal can help. Baking soda sprinkled lightly and left for a short period before vacuuming can reduce everyday odors. If you enjoy a scented atmosphere, consider rotating a safe, fabric friendly air freshener or using a gentle essential oil diffuser away from the rug to avoid direct contact. The goal is to maintain a fresh environment without overwhelming the fibers or your senses.

### Sizing and Placement Tips

Choosing the right size is a bit of a geometry puzzle but a satisfying one. A good rule of thumb is to allow enough rug under furniture to create a cohesive anchor for the seating arrangement. In a typical living space, a larger rug can help unify the sofa and chairs, while in a smaller room a compact rug can create a cozy, defined zone. The Kendall rug comes in multiple sizes, so you can tailor it to your space. Remember that the rug should feel like a natural extension of your furniture layout, not a random splash of fabric that floats in the middle of the room. If you are unsure, you can test the arrangement with painter tape on the floor to visualize your layout before purchasing a rug that might require returns.
