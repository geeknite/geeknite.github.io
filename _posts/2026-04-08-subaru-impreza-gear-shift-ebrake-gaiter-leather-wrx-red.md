## Materials and build quality: what to expect on the bench

- Leather type: Most gaiters use full-grain or top-grain leather for durability, with a thickness around 1.2–1.4 mm. Expect a grippy, slightly textured surface that ages gracefully rather than goes shiny under sweat and sun.
- Embroidery: The WRX lettering is typically stitched in a high-contrast red thread with a tight satin or chain stitch for durability. Proper embroidery should resist fraying and maintain colorfastness after routine cleaning.
- Edges and seams: Look for well-trimmed edges and double-stitched seams. A quality gaiter uses a reinforced ring or frame to hold its shape around the shift boot and e-brake lever, reducing sag over time.
- Fitment hardware: Depending on the supplier, some gaiters come with adapters or clips to secure the gaiter to the mounting ring. Expect standard sizes that align with common Impreza shifter boots and e-brake assemblies.

In practice, a leather gaiter with embroidery is not a “set-and-forget” piece. It benefits from a light, periodic care routine: wipe with a damp microfiber, apply a leather conditioner every 6–12 months depending on climate, and avoid solvents that could degrade the embroidery threads.

## Installation: how to swap your OEM gaiters for embroidered WRX-red leather

Note: Always consult your specific product’s included instructions. The steps below are a generic blueprint useful for most 2008–2014 Impreza interior swaps.

### What you’ll need
- A new leather embroidered gaiter (shift + e-brake) in WRX Red
- A set of basic hand tools (screwdrivers, pliers)
- A clean microfiber cloth and leather conditioner
- Optional: contact adhesive or a small clamp (only if your kit requires it)

### Step-by-step guide
1) Remove the shift knob: If your shift knob is threaded, turn it counterclockwise to unscrew. If it’s a press-fit type, gently pry upward with a thin flat blade or a trim removal tool.
2) Detach the OEM gaiter: Remove the surrounding trim ring or bezel by prying it away carefully. Some models use a small screw or clip that secures the gaiter base to the shifter housing.
3) Free the old gaiter: Slide the gaiter upward, disconnect any clips or adhesive strips, and set the old piece aside.
4) Prepare the new gaiter: Inspect the embroidery; if the threads look tight and the ring or mounting base matches your shifter housing, you’re good to go. Lightly condition the leather if recommended by the manufacturer (avoid over-saturating).
5) Attach the new gaiter: Fit the circumference of the new gaiter into the shifter housing and secure with clips/screws as required. Reinstall the trim ring around the base, ensuring a snug fit that won’t rattle.
6) Refit the shift knob: Screw or snap the shift knob back into place. Give a firm twist to ensure it’s secure.
7) Reconfirm e-brake alignment: Make sure the e-brake boot sits neatly with minimal twist and no interference with the lever’s travel.
8) Final check: Start the car or turn on accessories and test the movement of the shifter and e-brake to confirm there’s no binding or misalignment.

If your kit requires adhesive, apply a small amount to the base gasket only and clamp for a few minutes to hold the seal while it cures. Do not apply adhesive to threads or the embroidery—these are not friction surfaces.

## Care and maintenance: keeping WRX Red embroidery crisp

Leather gaiters love a light touch. To maintain both the leather and the embroidery:
- Wipe with a clean microfiber dampened with water or a mild leather cleaner. Avoid harsh solvents.
- Condition every 6–12 months, depending on climate and sun exposure. A well-conditioned gaiter remains supple and resists cracking.
- Keep away from direct heat sources. Sunlight can fade embroidery colors over time if the vehicle sits in strong sun for long periods.
- Inspect seams regularly for loose threads and repair early to prevent unraveling.

In everyday use, this gaiter should endure normal cabin wear with minimal fuss. If you notice significant fading or fraying within the first year, contact the seller or manufacturer for inspection or a replacement under warranty if applicable.
