### Durability and Maintenance

Let’s talk life with real humans. White rugs attract spilt coffee, red wine, and the occasional mystery sauce that seems to appear spontaneously on Tuesdays. The Summit Trail is designed to minimize visible wear thanks to a dense weave and stain resistance typical of polypropylene. Still, it's not invincible—if you use it under heavy traffic in a busy entryway, you might see a few marks after months. The trick: rug pads, regular blotting, and a gentle cleaning cycle. Many users report that spills blot out fairly easily if addressed promptly. For tougher stains like wine or sauce, a mild detergent and cold water work wonders; hot water can set stains and cause shrinkage (well, not the rug itself, but your hope for staying immaculate).

Care tips:

- Blot, don’t rub. Rubbing pushes the stain deeper.
- Use a mild detergent and cold water for stubborn spots.
- Vacuum on a low setting without the beater bar to avoid friction.
- Use a rug pad to prevent sliding and add cushioning.

### Care and Maintenance Deep Dive

Maintenance is where many white rugs either shine or splutter. The Summit Trail uses polypropylene fibers that resist staining and are relatively easy to clean. The key is to act quickly and avoid a buildup of grime that can dull the color. For everyday maintenance, a quick vacuum several times a week helps keep the rug looking fresh. For spills, blotting quickly is essential; if you wait too long, the stain will settle in and become more stubborn. For deep cleaning, consider a gentle wash cycle on your vacuum's upholstery setting or a light carpet cleaner, ensuring you test a small area.
