## How to Play: Quick Rundown and House Rules

Exploding Kittens is a rhythm game. Here’s a quick framework to get you started, followed by optional house rules that can stretch a single night into a series of unforgettable misadventures.

### Setup
- Shuffle the NSFW deck and deal a hand to each player according to the rules for the chosen variant (usually 7 cards per player, adjusted for number of players).
- Remove one Defuse card for each player who starts with it in their hand, or keep all Defuse cards in play depending on your preferred difficulty.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile according to the number of players. The remaining draw pile sits in the center.

### Basic rules (NSFW edition compatible)
- On your turn, you may play any number of cards from your hand that have valid interactions with the draw pile or other players. The NSFW cards mostly dictate who pays the price when a kitten lands on a player’s plate.
- If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you must defuse or suffer the consequences (exploding and eliminating from the round) unless someone else can save you with a Defuse or a mitigating card.
- Defuse cards can be retained for emergency use or used to defuse an exploding kitten. After defusing, the kitten is placed back into the draw pile at any position chosen by the defuser, often resulting in a very sneaky next-draw scenario.
- The winner is the last player standing when all others have exploded or been eliminated from the round.

### House rules for extra chaos
- The Blame Game: If someone blames another player for triggering an explosion, the accused must draw a card or perform a harmless dare before continuing.
- The Theme Night: Assign a silly theme for the night (e.g., “office party,” “space pirates,” or “80s arcade”) and require players to riff on the NSFW content in characters or accents that fit the theme.
