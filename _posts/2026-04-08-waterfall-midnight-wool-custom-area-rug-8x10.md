## Sizing options: Custom size vs 8x10 standard
### Custom size: your space, your rules
The custom-size option is exactly what it sounds like: you tell the rug folks your exact length and width, and they tailor the weave to match. If you own a quirky room shape—an alcove, a bay window, an oddly proportioned living area—the custom route helps you avoid awkward gaps and excess trimming. The upside of custom sizing is clear: you get a perfect fit around coffee tables, in front of a bed, or along an entry hallway. The downside is price and lead time: you’ll likely pay a premium and wait longer than for a stock rug. If you’re planning a room reveal or a redecoration with a tight schedule, factor this in.
### 8x10: the workhorse size
For many spaces, 8x10 is a safe, flexible choice. It anchors seating groups nicely in larger living rooms, offers a generous underfoot experience in front of couches, and can create a cinematic feel in open-plan spaces. If you’re pairing it with a modern sectional, an 8x10 can become the stage where conversation happens—without feeling like the rug has to pretend to be the star of the room. The 8x10 footprint also works well beneath dining tables in medium-to-large rooms (with a bit of extra room at the ends so chairs don’t drag across the pile when pulled out).
### What to consider when choosing
- Room shape and flow: do you have a rectangular, square, or irregular layout? Custom sizes can honor architectural quirks.
- Furniture arrangement: do you want all furniture legs on the rug or only the front two? This choice affects perceived scale and comfort.
- Traffic patterns: hallways and entryways demand durability; taller piles can show wear sooner.
- Lighting: darker rugs absorb light and can show footprints; plan for a space with enough brightness or choose a slightly lighter version if you’re in a dim interior.

## Durability and maintenance: what life looks like after you unroll it
### Everyday wear and tear
Wool is naturally resilient, but no rug is immune to everyday life. The Waterfall Midnight Wool’s construction suggests a pile that stands up to daily traffic, stroller wheels, spilled coffee, and the occasional pet misadventure. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting is your friend; avoid aggressive beater bars that can loosen fibers over time. If you spill something, the general rule is quick action: blot, don’t rub, and use a mild cleaner suitable for wool if needed. For stubborn stains, consult a wool-specific cleaner to avoid color loss or texture flattening.
### Cleaning and care routines
- Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting.
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear and exposure to sunlight.
- Address spills immediately by blotting with a clean white cloth.
- For deep cleaning, consider professional wool rug cleaning every 1–2 years depending on traffic.
- Protect from direct sun to minimize color fade—this is especially important for the midnight tone, which loves a long romance with shadow.
### Longevity and warranties
A quality wool rug typically offers excellent durability, and many manufacturers back their workmanship with a warranty period that covers manufacturing defects. While not all warranties are created equal, looking for coverage on fiber slippage, backing durability, and edge fraying can save you headaches later. If you’re a “buy it for life” shopper, the Waterfall Midnight Wool is the kind of investment that you’ll feel confident about as it ages gracefully rather than wearing out in a sprint.

Once your Waterfall Midnight Wool rug arrives, allow a day for acclimatization to room conditions. Unfold it carefully, align edges with walls, and avoid dragging during initial placement.
