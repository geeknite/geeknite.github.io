Design and build quality: what you’re really paying for

A good dice set is about more than the sum of its numbers. It’s about how it feels in your hand, how easy it is to read in low light, and whether you’d be comfortable rolling 20 resolutions on it in a tense street scene. The Fortune Row Standard Dice Set nails most of these aspects without turning into a luxury brand moment you can’t justify during a 2-hour session.

Material quality and finish

- The dice sport a smooth, almost satin-like finish that doesn’t attract fingerprints like a soap opera star attracts tabloids. They’re easy to grip and coax into a clean roll rather than a messy tumble through the napkin maze. 
- The color saturation is well chosen for readability. In a dim game room, you’ll still be able to distinguish the dice from a distance, which matters when you’re trying to keep the pace brisk while your players read a page of lore and roll for “Scene 3: The Alleyway Reveal.”

Iconography and readability

City of Mist thrives on iconography. You’ve got symbolic motifs that hint at your character’s myths and the city’s mythology. Fortune Row translates that vibe into the die faces by combining the standard pip arrangement with thematic symbols. On some faces you’ll see subtle silhouettes that echo the game’s tags and powers, a reminder that in Mist, a roll isn’t just a number; it’s a doorway to a possible myth taking one step closer to waking up.

The color code used in the standard set is also a practical boon. If your table uses color-symbol mapping to track a character’s status or a scene’s tone, this palette helps players stay oriented without needing a chalkboard full of scribbles. For new players, it reduces the cognitive load on a crucial moment when someone shouts, “I re-roll for a better fate!” and you need to know exactly which symbol has the effect you’re after.

City of Mist is all about themes, tags, and mythic outcomes wrapped into a modern-noir veneer. The Fortune Row set is designed to accentuate that experience rather than replace it. What I liked and what could be improved

What works well:
- Aesthetics that match City of Mist’s mood-forward design. The dice feel like they belong in a detective’s office, not a game shop display.
- Readability in low light and on busy tables. The color and iconography help players stay oriented without constant cross-checks.
- A satisfying roll feel with an appropriate weight that’s neither too heavy nor too light.

What could be better:
- A slight diversification in the line to include a larger set or color variants for players who want to customize their city-dweller clan of myths.
- Optional non-icon variants for players who prefer plain pips. Some people like to foreground the mechanics rather than the mood. A neutral surface option could satisfy both camps.

Care, maintenance, and travel tips

- Clean with a soft cloth. It’s not a space mission, but your dice do live in a city that pretends to be a detective and sometimes a magician. A quick wipe keeps the icons crisp.
- Use a dice tray or soft pouch when traveling. The noir vibe can be ruined by a loose roll in a backpack full of snacks.
- Store away from direct sunlight to prevent color fade on edge highlights.
