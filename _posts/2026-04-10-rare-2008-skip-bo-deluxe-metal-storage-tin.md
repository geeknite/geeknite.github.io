H2: Durability, Maintenance, and Long-Term Care

Durability is a mixed bag with any vintage packaging. The metal tin itself is sturdy and unlikely to crush under normal shelf conditions. The real worry is rust and dents in humid environments and edge wear on the lid’s latch. If you pick up a tin that’s spent a decade in a basement with a humid climate, you might see some rust speckles along the edges or some pitting near the lid catch. The cards, on the other hand, tend to fare better if they’ve been kept in a dry, cool area away from direct sunlight. Color fading on the card backs is a common tell of long-term exposure to sunlight, while the faces themselves tend to hold up better if the cards were sleeved or stored away from heavy handling.

Maintenance tips:
- Store the tin in a climate-controlled space, away from moisture and heat.
- If you notice minor rust spots, treat them gently with a light, non-corrosive cleaner and dry immediately.
- Use card sleeves for the card faces if you’re a stickler for longevity.
- Keep the rule sheet in a protective sleeve or a small clear binder page to preserve legibility.
