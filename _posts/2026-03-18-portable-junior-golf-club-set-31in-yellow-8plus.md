### Durability, Maintenance, and Care

Junior gear is a compound interest kind of investment: you want it to last through a season and still feel okay in year two. The 31-In set benefits from simple maintenance: wipe down after muddy sessions, dry thoroughly to avoid grip-slinging rot, and store in a dry place where the yellow finish won’t fade from constant sun exposure. Bags with reinforced stitching help keep all five clubs in good condition, even if your kid decides to carry the bag like a hero on a quest rather than a learner on a practice path.

If a club head ever gets loose, the fix is usually straightforward: apply a touch of torque to seat the head properly and ensure the ferrule sits flush against the shaft. For kids who like to disassemble their gear (the stage where curiosity becomes confetti), you’ll want to supervise as they explore how each piece fits into the whole. Keeping the kit clean and dry not only preserves the look but also keeps the grips from hardening and cracking with time.

### Maintenance Tips

- Clean grips with a mild soap solution and a soft cloth; dry thoroughly before reuse.
- Wipe club heads to remove dirt that can affect swing feel.
- Inspect shafts for hairline bends after particularly enthusiastic swings.
- Store in a dry, cool place; avoid leaving in a hot car where adhesives may soften.
- Consider a basic headcover for the driver to deter dings when the set is juggled by curious kids.
