## Grading, authenticity, and sniff tests: separating minty rumors from real deals

Gloom is not a card to be trifled with if you are chasing a flawless showpiece. A genuine NM Japanese print will show minimal edge wear and a holo that still sings. Here are some practical checks:
- Surface inspection: look for scratches on the holo, whitening at the edges, and any color bleed that suggests rough handling.
- Corners and edges: NM cards should have sharp corners with little to no whitening under bright light. A tiny, almost invisible edge nick can lower a grade, but it might still look stunning under a glass case.
- Text clarity: the Japanese text should be crisp and legible; blurred text hints at quality issues or misprints that affected collectability.
- Authenticity cues: if you are shopping online, rely on reputable sellers, verify packaging, and compare with known authentic examples. If something feels off, trust your collector spidey senses and walk away.

In the end, NM is a label that carries responsibility. It should reflect the card you hold in your hand and the story you tell while showing it off to friends who pretend to know more than they do about holo patterns. If you want to be extra thorough, consider a professional grading or authentication service. It can be worth the investment when you are building a long-term collection that you intend to pass down to a nephew who insists on speaking fluent Pikachu in public.
