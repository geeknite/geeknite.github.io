## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning and Conditioning

Maintenance matters most when you’re investing in outdoor furniture. Wipe the wood with a soft cloth and a mild soap solution occasionally to remove dust and grime. Avoid harsh chemicals that could strip sealants or stain the wood. For the wicker portions, a brush and a gentle vacuum can help remove debris that windy days decide to drop into your dining space. If your climate is humid or rainy, consider applying a lightweight outdoor wood sealant or oil once or twice a year to preserve color and reduce the risk of small cracks that can become micro-cracks under the weight of metaphorical winter drama. Keep an eye on joints and screws—tightening them after a season of sun and wind is a good habit, and it helps maintain the upright posture of your dining chairs’ legs.

### Weather Considerations

Acacia wood survives outdoors with a little extra care. If you live in a climate with severe winters or heavy rainfall, it’s wise to cover the set during the off-season or store it in a sheltered area. The wicker should be kept dry to avoid mildew, so using water-repellent cushions and removing fabric covers when rain threatens can extend the pleasant life of your chairs. The idea is to keep this furniture looking bold in late spring and behaving like a responsible adult in winter—i.e., not turning into a warped sculpture of regrets.
