## Care and Maintenance

Wool is naturally resilient, but it does have care needs. Vacuum regularly with a low-velocity setting and avoid the beater bar on high. The goal is to lift dirt rather than grind it into the fibers. For spills, blot gently with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent solution if needed. Do not rub aggressively; wool fibers have fiber memory, and you want to preserve that bounce rather than transform the rug into a compact pancake of despair.

Shedding is a common initial trait with wool rugs. A few light vacuuming sessions in the first weeks are normal and will subside over time. If you notice persistent excess shedding beyond a few months, it is worth contacting the retailer for advice and potential refinement options. Professional cleaning every 1 to 2 years is recommended for high-traffic areas. When cleaning, avoid soaking the rug completely and ensure thorough drying to prevent odor buildup or mildew—yes, the romance of a damp rug is not a mood you want to cultivate.

Dye fastness is another practical concern. Summer Sage is a stable shade designed to resist fading with normal daylight exposure. If you own a sunny room or live under theatrical lighting that could age fabrics quickly, rotate rugs occasionally for even sun exposure to minimize color shift. A simple rule: keep too much sun on one patch and you might end up with a story for your grandkids about your family rug that once resembled a green hillside and now looks like a plot twist in a color novel.
