### What is the Dice Tower for Zombicide?

If you thought Zombicide: Black Plague couldn't get any more cinematic, CMON's new Dice Tower arrives to shuttle dice from chaos to clarity with a smug little clack. In short: it's a theatre prop with a magnet for good rolls.

## Build Quality and Practicality

### Materials and Finish

The plastic feels sturdy in the hand, not brittle like a toy. The finish is matte enough to hide fingerprints but textured enough to provide grip when you’re assembling on a busy table. The tower’s joints are tight, with a quiet click that makes you feel like a wizard assembling a wand rather than a kit of plastic parts. It’s not going to wobble on a well-kept surface, which is a nice plus when you’re swinging sleeves to reach a zombie card while also weighing the odds of a five from the abomination.

## Gameplay and Functionality

### Notable Gameplay Impacts

The Dice Tower doesn’t affect the probability of outcomes; it simply changes the path of the dice. Results are easy to read as they emerge into the tray, which reduces the momentary guesswork that often comes with a sprawling dice cascade. In practice, this means fewer interruptions for “Wait, what was that die’s result?” and more time for shouting about brutal weapon combos and dramatic last-stands.

For groups that are large or especially kinetic, the tower acts as a central hub for dice. Everyone can see the results clearly, and you avoid the dreaded moment when a die slides under a chair and becomes a lost artifact from season two of your campaign.

## Integration with Zombicide: Black Plague

### Thematic Synergy

Zombicide: Black Plague is a game about speed, danger, and a little bit of tabletop chaos. The Dice Tower fits into that vibe by providing a tangible, audible cue that the dice are moving through a narrative arc. It’s not a replacement for good table manners; it’s a reminder that a well-run game has rhythm—clack, read, plan, smash, repeat.

If you’re playing a scenario with special dice rules or encounter cards that hinge on random outcomes, the tower’s consistent delivery helps your group stay in the moment rather than getting hung up on table jitter.

## Tips and Tricks
- Place the tower where you can easily reach the top for quick dice drops. It should be near your primary play area, not stuffed into a corner you’ll forget about after the first five minutes.
- Pre-sort dice by color if you like quick recognition of who rolled what. It helps players quickly identify if a roll produced a success or failure in the heat of battle.
- Use the lid as a mini storage for spare tokens or quick rule reminders. A few counters wedged into the top can prove surprisingly handy during a tense moment.
- If you’re streaming, consider the tower as a visual anchor: its clack can serve as an audio cue to transition between turns or scenes.
