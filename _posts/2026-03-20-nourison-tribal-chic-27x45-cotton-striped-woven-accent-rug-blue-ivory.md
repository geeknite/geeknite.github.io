## Build, care, and maintenance: practical notes

### Cleaning and spills
Cotton is relatively forgiving when it comes to spills. The best approach is rapid response: blot with a clean cloth, avoid rubbing, and if needed, mix a mild detergent with water and gently dab the spot. For tougher stains, a dedicated carpet cleaner designed for cotton fibers can be used, following the product’s guidance. Avoid harsh solvents that could discolor the blue or ivory tones.

### Vacuuming tips
Run a light vacuum over the rug with a gentle setting, ideally with minimal agitation. The woven texture can attract dust, so routine light vacuuming is better than aggressive, deep passes that could loosen fibers over time. If your vacuum has a beater brush option, use it sparingly to avoid fuzzing at the surface.

### Placement and floor protection
To preserve the rug’s shape and to protect floors, place a non-slip rug pad underneath. A pad helps prevent curling and keeps the rug in place in high-traffic zones. It’s small cost for big peace of mind and helps reduce wear on the fibers.

### Fray and wear prevention
Inspect edges for any signs of misalignment or loose threads after heavy furniture movement. If you spot a snag, trim with scissors rather than tugging. A quick edge fix now can prevent a larger repair later on.
