## Assembly and Setup: Two People, One Instruction Manual
Two-person desks imply two minds are involved in the assembly. Fortunately, the manufacturers provide a clear set of steps and all necessary hardware. Expect a straightforward procedure, typically split into the following stages:

1) Unpack and inventory: check panels, legs, supports, cable management bits, and the central screen mounting (if included). 
2) Frame assembly: anchor the white powder-coated legs to the main tabletop frame. It’s a good idea to do this with two people, as the pieces are wide and not particularly friendly to the solo-lifter. 
3) Surface prep: attach any cable channels or grommets, ensuring the channels align with the desk’s length and the future monitor placement. 
4) Monitor and screen integration: install the central screen panel or two monitor arms as per your chosen configuration. A test run with a couple of weights (laptops) helps verify load distribution. 
5) Cable management polish: route power cords and data lines through the channels, and zip-tie them neatly out of sight. 

Time estimates vary, but plan for roughly 60–90 minutes with two people, plus an additional hour if you decide to tune the height mid-setup. Pro tip: label the screws and brackets. It saves you from a “which bolt goes where?” moment at 9 PM when you’re tired and the cat has decided your desk is a new playground.

## Maintenance and Care: Keeping Nat Oak Looking Sharp
Oak veneers look warm and inviting, but they do need a bit of regular care to maintain their beauty. Here are practical tips to keep your desk looking fresh and professional:

- Cleaning: Wipe with a damp microfiber cloth and a mild, wood-safe cleaner. Avoid abrasive pads that can dull the finish. 
- Spills: Wipe immediately to reduce staining. If a splash lingers, use a gentle wood cleaner and a soft cloth to restore finish.
- Wood protection: Consider applying a light wax or conditioner every 6–12 months to maintain surface luster. Do a small test patch first, because even the best wax can go rogue on certain finishes.
- Metal frame care: Use a non-abrasive cleaner on the white powder-coated frame. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the coating’s integrity over time.

Airtight tip: keep the desk away from direct sunlight for extended periods to minimize color shift in the oak veneer. It’s not a dramatic change, but you’ll notice the difference after a few summers worth of sunlight exposure.
