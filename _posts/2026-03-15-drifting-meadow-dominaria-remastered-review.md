## Value and Market Perspective

### Price range for NM copies

Prices for NM Drifting Meadow copies in Dominaria Remastered can vary by condition, border treatment, and market dynamics. In a market that loves reprints but also loves nostalgia, NM versions tend to hold a stable baseline value with occasional bumps during buyout waves. If you’re a collector who buys for the long haul, NM is a sensible target. If you’re a deck builder looking for budget options, you can often find near-mint copies at more accessible prices. The key is to compare listings from multiple sellers and check for signs of subtle wear that can affect grade and price.

### Grading tips for NM copies

- Check for edge wear and whitening along the corners. These are the telltale signs that can push a card from NM to lightly played.
- Inspect the surface for scratches and scuffs, especially on the foil layer if you’re pulling a foil edition.
- Verify center alignment. A slightly off-center card can impact display value even if the mechanical condition is pristine.
- Test for any texture oddities in the print (some reprints carry slight print line variations that are functional and purely cosmetic).

As you shop, remember that NM is as much about presentation as it is about mechanical perfection. A visually pleasing card often reads as well as it plays, which is a nice dual win for collectors.

### Reprint impact on value

Dominaria Remastered is a curated homage to a beloved era of MTG design. The reprint nature of Drifting Meadow means that the card is more accessible to players who didn’t chase the original print, which can depress price somewhat, but it also broadens the market by expanding the pool of NM copies. In other words: you’re not chasing a scarce gem here, you’re chasing a well-preserved, nostalgically-flavored staple that adds flavor to your collection. If you’re buying with an eye toward long-term value, the NM copy typically remains a reliable piece of a larger collecting puzzle rather than a quick windfall.
