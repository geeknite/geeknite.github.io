## Customization Details: What You Can Expect

When you choose a custom size for Regatta Sandy Walk Wool, here is what you are likely to encounter during the ordering process:

## Size Matters: Custom Size vs 8x10—What Works Best for You?

Let's get to the heart of it: your space and your sanity. Size matters in the same way a battery matters to a flashlight—essential for function, optional for drama, but it affects everything else.

- Custom size advantages: precise fit for awkward layouts; negative space around furniture is minimized; you can tailor shape to odd corners or irregular rooms; the rug can become a design anchor rather than a casual background piece.
- Custom size disadvantages: longer lead times; higher cost; potential for miscommunication if you do not supply exact measurements. The last thing you want is a rug that needs a second production run because the entrance is not as wide as the hallway. Double-check and triple-check dimensions; consider using painter’s tape to map the rug’s footprint on the floor before ordering.
- 8x10 advantages: predictable price; faster delivery; widely compatible with most standard room layouts; easier to swap if you decide to redecorate. If you are testing the waters with wool rugs as a concept, the 8x10 is the more comfortable entry point.
- 8x10 drawbacks: if your space is non-standard (for example, a wide, shallow living room or a dining area with chairs pulled out), an 8x10 might either leave too much empty floor or force you to cram it under furniture in ways that detract from the room’s balance.

In our opinion, if your floor plan features few right angles and heavy furniture, the custom route pays off in the long term. If you can measure with precision and plan to use the rug in multiple spaces over the years, the custom size becomes a worthy investment. For many readers, the practical middle ground is to select the standard 8x10 while exploring a sub-variant within a reasonable extension (some manufacturers allow minor tweaks in length or width without breaking the bank).

## Maintenance and Care: Keeping Sandy Walk Fresh

Wool rugs require gentle care to stay good-looking. Here are the baseline care steps we recommend:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting. Use a brush attachment if possible, and avoid beater bars that can tug at the fibers. Regular vacuuming keeps the pile fluffy and the look crisp.
- Rotate periodically: give your rug a spin every few months to ensure even wear across the space. This is especially important in rooms with direct sunlight, which can cause color fading in localized areas.
- Deal with spills promptly: blot, don’t rub. For stains that are persistent, consider a wool-safe rug cleaner recommended by the manufacturer or a professional cleaning service. Always test on a small, inconspicuous area first.
- Avoid heat: do not place heaters or direct heat on the rug. Wool fibers respond to heat poorly and can warp or shrink, which is the opposite of what you want when designing a cozy floor oasis.
- Protect edges: consider furniture coasters or felt pads under legs. This reduces friction on the edges, which can slow down wear and prevent indentations from heavy furniture.
- Professional cleaning: schedule at least once every couple of years for a thorough refresh, depending on traffic. A wool rug can last a long time with proper care, but occasional professional attention keeps the look pristine.

If you want a quick at-home refresher, steam cleaning is generally safe for wool, but consult the manufacturer guidelines for your specific product. The goal is to refresh the fibers without over-wetting or shrinking the rug. A light refresh is often all you need to keep the Sandy Walk feeling as brand-new as possible between professional cleanings.
