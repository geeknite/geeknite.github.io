## How to Play: A Quick Start Guide
Dizzy Date is designed for two players, though you can expand to a group if you want to turn your love nest into a micro-board game cafe for a night. The core loop is simple: draw a card, read it, decide how to respond or complete the prompt, and then pass the turn. There’s no heavy setup, no long tutorial, and no need to memorize complex rules between sips of your preferred beverage.

Steps to play:
1) Shuffle the deck and place it face-down within easy reach. If you’re the “leader of the dice,” you can decide who goes first by a playful coin flip or a nerdier method—rock-paper-scissors, best two out of three of your favorite video game characters, whatever suits the mood.
2) On your turn, draw one card from the top and read it aloud to your partner. Some cards are straightforward: “Describe a future date night you’d love to plan.” Others are action-driven: “Do a ten-second improv scene about your partner as a secret superhero.”
3) Respond or perform. The goal isn’t to “win” anything; it’s to share, laugh, connect, and maybe reveal something you didn’t know you’d reveal tonight. If you’re worried about the level of vulnerability of a given prompt, you can set a comfort threshold at the start of the game. For example, you may allow the “skip” option twice per game, or allow a “mood pass” if a card feels too personal.
4) Pass or award points if you care about that. The game doesn’t come with a strict scoring system; some couples play as if they’re rating every prompt on the “cute-to-awkward” scale, while others simply collect stories for later social media nostalgia (with consent, of course).
5) After the deck runs out, you can reshuffle and play another round, or switch gears to a quieter activity like stargazing, a warm drink, or a spontaneous dance-off in your living room. The point is to keep momentum, not to enforce a forced sense of romance.

A few practical tips:
- If you’re new to the game, start with the gentler prompts. You can always escalate by design if you both want more laughs or more vulnerability.
- Keep a “no judgment” rule handy. The beauty of Dizzy Date is that it invites silliness and vulnerability in roughly equal measure, but that won’t land well if you’re sour about it.
- Use this game as a warm-up for real-time flirting or longer conversations you’ve been avoiding. People often say that a good game night helps them open up about things they’ve kept private for a long time.
- The cards are designed to be replayable; you can revisit prompts later in the relationship for interesting insights or nostalgia.

## Play Styles and Date Night Variations
Every couple has a different vibe, and Dizzy Date is more versatile than a Swiss Army knife with a sense of humor. Here are some guided variations you can try depending on your mood:

### Cozy Night Variation
- Setup: Dim lights, a playlist of soft tunes, and maybe a mug of cocoa or tea. The goal is warmth, not speed.
- Prompt selection: Favor questions and gentle prompts. Skip anything that feels intense.
- Pace: Slow and intimate. Give yourselves time to process answers and savor the moment.
- Outcome: A deeper emotional connection or a fond memory you’ll revisit when life gets loud.

### Spicy Night Variation
- Setup: A little more lighting, a dash of cinnamon on your lips (figuratively). Boundaries clearly established before hitting the deck.
- Prompt selection: Lean into the playful dares and flirty prompts. You can escalate with “double-dare” rounds if you’re both feeling bold.
- Pace: It’s about chemistry and laughter, not a sprint. It might get cheeky, but consent remains the star of the show.
- Outcome: A night that lands somewhere between hilarious and steamy, with memorable inside jokes you’ll tell years from now.

### The Quick-Dite: 30-Minute Blitz
- Setup: Timebox to 30 minutes. Shuffle and sprint through as many cards as you can handle.
- Prompt selection: Random. No overthinking; the point is spontaneity.
- Pace: Rapid-fire prompts, quick answers, and fast laughter.
- Outcome: A high-energy burst of connection that leaves you energized rather than drained.

### The Debate Round
- Setup: Each person chooses a handful of favorite prompts and you debate their merits (in good faith) for a few minutes each.
- Prompt selection: Cards that argue for different viewpoints on silly topics.
- Outcome: A surprisingly thoughtful exchange that still ends in giggles.
