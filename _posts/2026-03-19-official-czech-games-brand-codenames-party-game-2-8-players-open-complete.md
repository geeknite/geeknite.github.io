What is Codenames Party?

Codenames Party is CGE’s streamlined, party-oriented take on the original Codenames. The core conceit remains: two teams, a Spymaster per team, and a grid of word cards. Spymasters give one-word clues that relate to multiple words on the table, trying to steer their teammates toward their team’s words while avoiding the opposing team’s words and the dreaded assassin word. The Party version amps up the social component: quicker rounds, more room for banter, and a few design choices aimed at making the game easier to teach to new players while still scratching the itch for seasoned wordplay fans.

How to Play: A Quick refresher (and why this is still fun)

Codenames Party follows the same basic rhythm as the standard Codenames game with a few tweaks to fit a party vibe. Here’s the core flow:

- The table is set with a 5x5 grid of word cards in the center.
- Each team has a Spymaster who knows which words belong to their team, which belong to the other team, and which word is the assassin.
- Spymasters give one-word clues plus a number, indicating how many words on the grid relate to that clue.
- Teammates discuss and try to identify their team’s words. They may pass on a clue if it doesn’t feel right, or take a risk and guess more words.
- If a team guesses a word that belongs to the other team, that word is flipped and the turn ends. If they hit the assassin word, they lose immediately. If they guess all their words, they win.

Party twists you’ll notice include shorter turn timers (handy for keeping the energy high) and a slightly more forgiving clue-giving language that still rewards clever cross-references. It’s easy to teach in under 10 minutes and even quicker to start having fun with after a single demo round.
