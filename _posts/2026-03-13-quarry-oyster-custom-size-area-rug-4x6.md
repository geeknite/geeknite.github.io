## Durability, Build, and Care: Keeping Quarry Oyster Looking Sharp
### Materials and Backing
Durability depends heavily on the fiber composition, backing, and edge finishing. The Quarry Oyster line emphasizes a balance between softness and resilience. In a 4x6 room, you’ll likely place high-traffic zones across the rug’s shorter axis. The backing should be sturdy enough to hold its shape under vacuum and foot traffic without curling at the edges. Edge finishing—whether a serged border or a clean welt—helps prevent fraying with daily use and occasional pet mischief.

### Cleaning and Maintenance
Maintenance matters more for a rug than you might think. A 4x6 rug is small enough to handle quicker cleanups, yet large enough that you’ll want a routine rather than a “wait until it’s completely dirty” approach. You’ll want to vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting to avoid dulling the fibers. Spills should be blotted immediately rather than scrubbed, to prevent color bleed. For deeper cleaning, a professional rug cleaning every year or two can refresh the fibers without compromising the weave. If you have kids or pets, a gentle routine using a rug-friendly cleaner is recommended.

Care tip: rotate the rug every three months to ensure even wear across the high-traffic zones. This is especially useful in living rooms where the sofa’s position changes with the sun and the seasons.

### Color Fastness and Wear Over Time
In colorfastness tests, lighter neutrals can show more wear sooner, especially in households with sun exposure. Quarry Oyster’s neutral-to-warm palette tends to age gracefully, yet you’ll want to prevent long-term sun exposure on the same patch of rug. If you have a space with bright afternoon sun, consider UV-protective window treatments or rotating the rug’s position to minimize fading.

## How to Measure, Purchase, and Place Your Rug Like a Pro
### Measuring for Perfection
The most common misstep in rug shopping is measuring for a rug that looks right on the page but ends up misfitting your room. A 4x6 rug should sit under the front legs of the main seating furniture to visually anchor the space. If you’re placing a sofa and two chairs, the rug should be wide enough to sit under the front legs of the furniture and extend beyond the seating to create a cohesive anchor. A quick rule of thumb: the rug should be large enough to seat at least the front legs of the furniture, or it should be large enough to create a clearly defined boundary for your conversation area.
