## How the game plays: a quick tour

### The core Zombicide loop (brief refresher)

If you’ve played any edition of Zombicide, you know the core loop: cooperative heroes, a grid map, and a clock that ticks down as zombies flood the board. Players work together to complete a series of objectives—clear a path, rescue survivors, or reach a mission-critical tile—while managing resources, planning safe zones, and avoiding the inevitable horde.

### What this guest edition adds to the loop

The Special Guests edition maintains the same core loop but injects two flavors that alter pacing and tone. First, you’ll encounter hero cards and mission objectives with guest-flavored twists that encourage creative problem-solving. Second, a handful of event cards introduce quirky, zany effects—some humorous, some cunning—that tilt the battlefield in unexpected ways. The result is a session that can swing from “absolute panic” to “laugh-out-loud mischief” in the space of a single turn. It’s not chaotic for chaos’ sake; it’s chaos with a purpose: to challenge you to improvise with a sense of humor, which is exactly what fans of Kovalic’s work tend to crave.

We’ll walk through a hypothetical round to illustrate the rhythm. The team starts in a narrow corridor with a handful of walkers at the far end.
