### Hand management and stock piles

## Condition, Completeness, and Value
### What counts as complete
For vintage card games, completeness means all components are present and intact: the velvet box, the full deck of numbered cards (and any Skip-Bo wilds), the instruction sheet, and any original retailer inserts. A complete edition increases the value significantly, especially if the box shows minimal wear and the cards retain their color and legibility. A pristine box with crisp edges and no heavy foxing on the instruction sheet is the unicorn you hunt for when you start a vintage collection.

### Grading your find
Assess the following:
- Box integrity: Are the hinges intact? Is there any tearing or fading of the velvet? Does the lid sit properly?
- Card stock: Are there creases, heavy bends, or faded ink? Are the card fronts legible and free of stains? Do the corners survive a light shuffle without cracking?
- Edges and seams: Any signs of tight storage or moisture damage? A little edge wear is common and acceptable; extensive warping is a red flag.
- Completeness: Count the deck and verify that nothing is missing. An extra rule sheet is nice; missing a wild card is not.

Remember, rarity plus desirability plus condition equals value, and this particular trio tends to align well for the right buyer who appreciates the story as much as the cards.

## How to Play a Round: A Mini Demo
Let us walk through a quick, friendly round to illustrate the vibe. We will assume a four-player game, with a good mix of strategy and chaos.

1) Setup: Shuffle the deck and deal each player a hand of five cards. Each player also forms a stock pile face down, with the top card flipped for play. The goal is to empty your stock pile first by placing cards on the building piles in ascending order.
2) Turn flow: On your turn, you may play cards from your hand or from your building piles, placing them on the central build stacks in sequence. If you cannot play, you draw cards up to your hand limit (often five) and discard if possible.
3) Wilds in action: When you have a wild card, you can substitute for any number. The strategic moment is to decide whether to use a wild early for a quick advantage or save it for a critical late move.
4) The climactic finish: The moment when someone empties their stock pile triggers a victory declaration. Applause, high-fives, and a little trash talk follow as the winner revels while the rest politely pretend not to care.

Playthrough flavor: you will likely witness a dramatic chain of plays that feels almost like dicey investment decisions at a busy farmers market. The vintage edition rewards players who balance bold moves with risk assessment, and the laughter that accompanies near misses is all part of the experience.

## Care and Preservation Tips
- Store in a cool, dry environment away from direct sunlight to minimize fading and warping.
- Use acid-free sleeves for the cards if you plan to display or frequently handle the deck. It helps preserve the edges and front designs.
- Keep the velvet box closed when not in use to protect from dust and accidental spills; a silica gel packet tucked inside a rarely-opened corner never hurts.
- Avoid sharp objects near the box and cards; paper cuts are not a collectible's friend, and neither are coffee stains.
- If you want to display, consider a glass-front case with soft lighting that does not heat the deck. Your future self will thank you.
