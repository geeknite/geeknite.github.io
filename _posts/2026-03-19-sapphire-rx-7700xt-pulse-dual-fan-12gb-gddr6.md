### Overclocking headroom and efficiency

Overclocking the Pulse variant is a bit of a mixed bag, as with many midrange-to-upper-midrange cards. You can push the core clock higher with a modest uplift in power draw, but you will likely hit the thermostat wall before you break world records. The Sapphire TriXX software provides a friendly interface to adjust a few levers: memory offset, power limit, and fan curve. It is not a veins-and-bloodline overclocking tool, but it does the job for most everyday enthusiasts who want a bit more headroom without becoming accidental GPU farmers. If you enjoy the fine art of pushing hardware to legal limits, you will appreciate the dull but effective gains you can squeeze from a modest overclock with some patience and proper cooling margins.

In our tests, a gentle overclock achieved around a 5–8 percent uplift in certain workloads without causing heat spikes that would force aggressive fan ramp. If you crave extreme numbers, you should be prepared for louder fans and higher power draw, and you might want to consider a more robust cooling solution or a more capable chassis with better air intake.
