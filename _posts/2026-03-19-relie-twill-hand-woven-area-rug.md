Care and Maintenance: Keeping It Real Without Losing Your Mind
Caring for a hand woven rug typically means a little patience, a dash of routine, and an occasional rescue mission for small, stubborn bits of debris. Regular vacuuming is recommended, but you’ll want to avoid aggressive suction settings that could nudge the fibers into a shape you didn’t approve of during a design consultation at 2 AM. The rug’s natural fibers usually respond well to gentle care, and most manufacturers suggest spot cleaning with a mild detergent. In short, you don’t need to call in a squad of cleaners for every spill, but you do want to address accidents promptly to avoid set-in stains.

Care Tips and Cleaning Hacks: Keeping It Fresh Without the Drama
- Vacuum regularly with a gentle setting to avoid pulling fibers. 
- Address spills promptly with a blotting technique rather than rubbing, which can push stains deeper. 
- For spot cleaning, mix a mild detergent with water and test on a small inconspicuous area first. 
- Rotate the rug every few months to even out wear patterns, especially in high-traffic zones. 
- Consider a rug pad to prevent slipping and to extend the rug’s life by reducing friction with hard flooring. 
These are not just grandma tips; they’re practical guidelines that keep your rug looking sharp while your budget remains in the black void of adulthood.
