## Assembly experience and maintenance tips
If you enjoy a little puzzle-solving on a sunny day, the assembly process can be oddly satisfying. Here are a few practical tips to save you from a mind-numbing assembly experience:
- Clear your workspace. A flat, open porch or patio helps you manage the parts and prevents tiny screws from playing hide-and-seek.
- Use a drill with a low torque setting. If you’re new to assembly, a manual screwdriver will do, but a gentle electric twist is faster and less tiring unless you’re stubbornly aiming for a challenge.
- Keep a small magnet handy. Some loose screws find gravity irresistible and love to cling to metal surfaces, which can speed things up when you’re trying to salvage a dropped screw.
- Cushion care: remove covers and wash when needed. Most cushion covers are machine washable on a gentle cycle; air-dry to prevent fiber distortion. In the heat of summer, you’ll appreciate a quick wipe-down with a damp cloth to remove dust and pollen.
- Storage: when winter hits or storms threaten, store cushions in a dry place or use breathable storage bags to prevent mildew. A simple cover can prolong life and keep the set looking sharp for longer.

## Cleaning, care, and longevity tips
Here are a few practical tips to keep your rattan furniture looking shipshape across seasons:
- Wipe down the frame with a mild soap solution and rinse with a soft cloth. Avoid abrasive cleaners on the wicker that could compromise the weave.
- Vacuum cushions lightly to remove dust and debris. For stubborn stains, spot clean with a fabric-safe stain remover and air-dry thoroughly.
- Use UV protection sprays designed for outdoor fabrics to help preserve color intensity without altering feel.
- Periodically check the screws and joints and tighten gently as needed to maintain stability. A little maintenance goes a long way toward longevity.
