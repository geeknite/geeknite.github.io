## How to play in 60 seconds (ok, maybe 5 minutes)
Before we plunge into the deep sea of rules, here is the distilled gist: you roll a batch of dice, draft them into your own personal sushi menu, and score points based on the collections you complete. The scoring vibes lean on set collection: the more of a given sushi type you have, the higher your score—though every sushi type has its own quirks that can tip a lead into a loss if you misread the room.

### Setup
- Each player takes a score card and a dry-erase marker or pencil. Some sets include little tokens to help track points; if you don’t have tokens, you can improvise with coins or paper scraps that won’t cause World War III on the dining table.
- The dice are placed into the included tray or a trusty bag. You’ll decide whether to roll all the dice at once or in small batches depending on the variant you’re trying.
- The starting player is decided by a quick coin flip or a fun tradition your group secretly hates but pretends to love for the first round.

### Turn structure (the gist)
- Roll the dice to reveal dish faces. The face values range across sushi types and occasionally a “special” face that either helps you or sabotages others—depending on whether your joy is merciful or merciless.
- Draft dice into your personal menu. You can pick one die per draft, and pass the rest to your neighbors, continuing around the table. You want to curate a matching set of dishes that pays off when the round ends.
- End of round: tally scoring for the dishes you’ve claimed. Some rounds require you to optimize for small sets with big bonuses; others reward going wide with a broad dish repertoire.
- Reset and repeat for a total number of rounds specified by the rules. The total score wins the day, or at least wins you bragging rights until the next group chat arguments erupt about which sushi is the superior concept.

### Scoring highlights
- Traditional sushi types (like nigiri, maki, and gunkan) score in set patterns. The trick is knowing when to chase a rare dish and when to pivot to a safe, reliable set.
- There are multipliers and bonuses tied to particular combinations, which means your big round might hinge on your ability to read the table and block opponents from the exact dice you crave.
- Some faces grant extra roll opportunities or forces you to reroll certain dice, introducing a light tactical layer you didn’t expect from a cereal-box snack-sized game.

## Strategy tips and what to aim for on your first playthrough
- Read the room early: in a drafting environment, the ability to predict what others are likely to chase is a powerful tool. If you sense a table leaning toward a particular sushi type, you can pivot toward a complementary set that still yields gain.
- Don’t chase a single dish too hard: variety matters. It’s better to have a few solid sets than one towering, misfired plan that collapses when someone snags the last crucial die.
- Play the long game on endgame bonuses: some rounds reward the most balanced table of types; elsewhere, you’ll want a couple of big stacks that can carry you. Learn when to sprint and when to conserve your chips—metaphorically speaking—and you’ll score more consistently.
- Watch the dice economy: if you see a lot of the same face rolling around, consider blocking or drafting to prevent others from leaping into strong combos you fear. It’s not mean—it’s competitive table strategy with a comedic twist.
- Use the rule cheatsheet: if your set includes a small reference card, keep it visible and quick to consult. It’s easy to forget which combos yield the biggest bonuses in the heat of a round, and a fast reminder pays off in points.
