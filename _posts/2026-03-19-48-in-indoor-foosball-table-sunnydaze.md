## Assembly: From Box to Battle-Ready in Under an Hour
Unboxing is straightforward, but like every assembly project, it comes with its own code of minor mysteries. The package typically ships with a small set of Allen wrenches, a handful of screws, and a large dose of patience. Our recommendation: set aside a little gaming time with a friend who can whisper words of encouragement while you wrestle with the last two bolts.

- Leveling: You’ll want to level the unit so the ball has a fair chance of rolling straight, instead of making a dramatic exit stage left. A cheap bubble level or a phone app can help you dial in the perfect angle.
- Tightening sequence: Start with the main frame, then the legs, then the cross-bracing. Do not skip the cross-bracing step, or you’ll spend your next game night apologizing to gravity.
- Final checks: Ensure that the rods spin smoothly and the players return to their “home” positions without jamming. If you hear squeaks, give the screws a gentle retighten and pretend your ship is just a bit more aerodynamic.

It’s not complicated, and it’s certainly not a two-person job for a full afternoon. The goal is to be playing in under an hour, and with a little teamwork, you can hit that target without sacrificing your sanity.

## Maintenance and Care: Keep It Shiny, Keep It Honest
A little care goes a long way toward preserving the table’s life and performance. Here are practical maintenance tips that won’t bore you to tears:

- Clean the playing surface after games with a soft, damp cloth. Avoid harsh cleaners that could degrade the surface finish.
- Periodically inspect the rods for looseness. If you notice wobble, a quick tightening of the end bolts can save you from a long and dramatic thread-strip scenario.
- Protect the table from sunlight and heat sources. Excessive heat or UV exposure can warp the cabinet or fade prints on the leg panels.
- Cover it when not in use. A simple cover helps prevent dust buildup and keeps your foosers ready for action.

If you’re one of those people who keep track of every bolt and screw with obsessive precision, you’ll appreciate the straightforward assembly and maintenance; it’s not a museum piece, but it’s a workhorse you can feel good about performing basic upkeep on.
