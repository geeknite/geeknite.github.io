## How to Inspect Before You Buy (Practical Checklist)

Before you swap cash for a desk that promises excellence, run through this practical, buyer-friendly checklist. Treat it like a mini-quest: you’re hunting for the legendary “solid surface, clean edges, no cracks, no wobble.”

- Inspect the surface under bright light for scratches, chips, and water stains. A clean top is a sign of minor wear, but deep gouges can indicate poor maintenance or heavier use than advertised.
- Check the edges for delamination or peeling laminates. If you can gently press on a corner and feel it flex, you may be dealing with a future wobble disaster.
- Test the stability. Place a hand on the desk, tap gently, and try to rock it from one corner. A sturdy desk should have minimal movement. If there’s obvious wobble, ask for adjustment screws or consider alternatives.
- Look at the legs and frame. Are the screws tight? Do any of the feet wobble? A desk that’s been sitting in one position for months might have slight settling along the frame.
- cable management checks. Are the grommets present and functional? Do the holes align with your planned setup? A well-designed cable portal will make cable routing a breeze, not a maze.
- Monitor compatibility. If you’re planning to run a multi-monitor rig, measure the width and ensure your mounts or stands will fit. This desk handles a couple of monitors easily, but you want to avoid a situation where your monitors outgrow the space faster than your coffee consumption grows.
- Ask for the weight of the top and the frame. If you’re mounting a heavy PC on a shelf, you’ll want a robust CPU shelf with adequate load-bearing capacity.
- Check for damage and repairs. Look for signs of patching or repainting that might indicate a previous ding. Minor cosmetic issues are fine; major structural repairs may be a red flag.
- verify the included hardware. If screws, washers, and washers’ washers are missing or rusted, you’ll want replacements or to factor in a small extra cost.

## Setup Tips and First Week: Getting Started Right

- Plan your layout before you lift anything heavy. It saves back pain and makes the first coffee break much more satisfying.
- If you’re mounting monitors, consider a monitor arm. It often makes the desk feel larger and gives you more precise height adjustments.
- Use the cable grommets to route cords behind the desk, not across the surface. This reduces the chance of a dramatic coffee spill sending chaos through your workstation.
- Keep a small tool kit handy. A tiny screwdriver set, an Allen wrench, and a spare screwdriver head can save you time and avoid frustration when you’re tightening legs.
- Test the desk with your actual gear. Load up the monitors, CPU, and accessories to ensure the weight is well distributed and that there’s no unwanted wobble.
- Add a protective desk mat if you want to defend the surface from hot coffee mugs and scribbles. It’s a small touch that can extend the desk’s life while keeping your coffee ritual intact.
