## How to Play: A Quick Start for Busy Geeks

If you’re already familiar with the standard Catan flow, the Trade Build Settle edition will feel like a familiar riff on a beloved tune. If you’re brand-new, fear not: we’ll keep this brisk and friendly, as if your gamer friends are mildly competent pirates on a sugar rush.

### Setup (10-15 minutes of noble patience)
- Assemble the modular hex map according to the scenario or your own mood. The beauty here is that you can mix landscapes to create a unique geography every game. This is basically role-playing with bricks and sheep, and I am here for it.
- Place the starting settlements and roads in a symmetrical or chaotic fashion—your call, your destiny. The rules encourage a fair distribution of starting resources; your victory may hinge on whether you’re allowed to start with two brick cards or not. Rules vary, but the spirit remains: you’ll want to secure a few key spots early to avoid becoming the board’s version of a tourist in your own economy.
- Shuffle the development deck and place it within easy reach. Yes, even the dice want to be near the action.

### Turn Structure (Short version for people with actual lives)
- Roll the dice to determine resource production; the number on the dice correlates to terrain hexes that produce resources. If you’re new, think of it as a lottery where your numbers sometimes actually mean something.
- Trade with other players or the bank. This is where the “Trade Build Settle” philosophy shines: negotiation, strategic bluffs, and the occasional “I’ve got two sheep, you want a woolly disaster?” moment.
- Build roads, settlements, or even cities to gain victory points and expand your empires. The more you build, the more you ask yourself existential questions about whether you’re trading for survival or social validation.
- Play development cards when you can justify it with a flourish and a polite nod to your fellow players.

### Trading, Building, and Settling
Trading is the heartbeat of this edition. It’s not just bargaining; it’s a tiny mirror held up to your social abilities. Can you persuade someone to exchange brick for sheep without implying you’ll abandon the game and start a new life as a hermit? With this set, you’ll test your rhetorical finesse, your memory of which player has which resources, and your ability to calculate the long-term payoff of a risky trade. The Build aspect adds a satisfying loop: the more you invest in roads and settlements, the more you can block opponents from valuable resources, which may lead to gentle trash-talking, debate about the fairness of the robber, and a proud sense of strategic superiority when you squeak out a win.
