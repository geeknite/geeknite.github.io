## How the Expansions Change Gameplay
The base Exploding Kittens game is a clever blend of chance and bluffing. Add six expansions, and you’re stepping into a party that feels more like a chaotic improv troupe than a board game. The expansions do not alter the core objective—avoid exploding cards as long as possible—but they add new cards with distinct effects, new ways to shuffle your fate, and new opportunities to roast your friends verbally while maintaining a surprisingly solid strategic backbone.

Key shifts you’ll notice:
- More card types means more decision points per turn, which translates to longer, more suspenseful rounds. You’ll be balancing risk, bluff, and reading the room while trying not to break into a spontaneous, chaotic laugh fit.
- New interaction layers create “soft counterplay” dynamics. You can’t simply memorize the base deck anymore; you must adapt to the ebb and flow every single game.
- The NSFW expansion injects humor that reshapes group dynamics. If your table enjoys sarcasm, this will be a home court advantage. If not, you’ll still enjoy the novelty of the banter that accompanies it.
