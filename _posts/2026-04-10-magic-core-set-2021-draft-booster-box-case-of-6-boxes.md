### Build your decks: strategies by color
The six box case is perfect for exploring color pairings without the fear of empty wallets. Here are quick sketches of what you might aim for in each color, along with cross-color synergies:

- White: Efficient creatures and anthem effects, race to the finish with a splash of protection. White often shines in draft with quick, resilient boards and the ability to push damage efficiently.
- Blue: Counterspells and card advantage. If you like the control tilt, blue in a multi-box draft night can dominate the late game, provided you don’t get blown out by a surprise red deck that expands its threats and burns you out.
- Black: Removal and disruption. Expect a grindy plan that punishes people for overextending. The seduction of black is the promise of answers to almost any threat, albeit at the cost of some raw tempo.
- Red: Aggro and tempo. Red tends to be the loud kid in the classroom, but with careful sequencing and mana management you can deliver a pace that forces opponents to fold before they assemble a perfect curve.
- Green: Ramp and big bodies. Green can be a forgiving color for newer players, offering robust creatures and the occasional haymaker that ends games abruptly if left unanswered.
- Multicolor and Artifacts: The case provides opportunities to test splash strategies and more complex archetypes. If you like a challenge, this is your playground.

### Tips for getting the most out of a six box case
- Plan your nights: schedule drafts in blocks so you maximize the value of your six boxes without burning out. A well-timed break for snacks and memes is essential.
- Track your card pulls: keep a little tally of rares and mythics that appear across boxes. It helps you set expectations and gives you talking points for future drafts.
- Build with flexibility: aim for decks that can pivot to adapt to the flow of what other players are doing. The real skill is in reading the table and recognizing when to switch gears mid-draft.
- Trade wisely: the six box setup makes trading more interesting. You will have access to more cards, but you will also have more people who want the same rares. Pro tip: never trade away your winning card for a marginal one you think you might need later; your deck will be thanking you when you actually draw it.
- Foil hunting: if foils are your jam, keep an eye out for foil versions of the key cards. They can be a nice windfall when the set’s value trends up after the draft night.
