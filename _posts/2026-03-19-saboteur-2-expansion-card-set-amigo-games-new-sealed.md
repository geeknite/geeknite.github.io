## What’s New: Card Categories and How They Change the Game

The treasure cards in this expansion aren’t just more gold tokens. The expansion includes new role cards that tilt the social poker game. Some roles subtly tilt influence toward one team or another, while others create interesting edge cases that require quick mental gymnastics and a sense of fair play that may or may not exist until you announce it loudly at the table. ### Miscellaneous Cards and Rule Inserts

The integration process is delightfully painless, which is a rare thing in any modular game system—like discovering that a complicated spell actually just requires saying “please” and tapping your wand twice.

- Shuffle the expansion cards separately and place them in a draw pile adjacent to the base game pile. The exact method for mixing is explained in the included insert, which is short, clear, and less judgmental than your average fan forum.
- If you are playing with a group that’s new to Saboteur, you’ll want to establish a couple of house rules to manage the new roles: do you reveal them at the start, or do you keep them hidden until a special moment? - Determine victory conditions as you normally would for Saboteur, but keep an eye on the treasure distribution: the expansion’s new cards can spike the economy in unexpected ways.

### Gameplay Flow
- The round unfolds as usual with players taking turns building tunnels and laying down role cards that influence play. The new cards simply appear in the draw pile, and players will encounter them as naturally as a sinkhole at a beginner-friendly beach resort.
- The expansion’s twist is to introduce moments where a path card triggers a new effect or where a treasure card’s requirement creates a small but meaningful decision point for the group. These moments tend to arrive at the exact moment you think you’re about to win, which is Saboteur’s way of keeping you honest and slightly annoyed in equal measure.
- Endgame often feels sparkier: with more cards in the mix, the table is less likely to settle into a predictable rhythm. That unpredictability is the whole point of Saboteur, and the expansion nudges everyone toward more bluffing, more misdirection, and more dramatic reveals.

With more options on the table, you’ll enjoy longer sessions where players argue about routes, claim “this is obviously the best path,” then redraw their plans with a theatrical flourish.
