## Practical Buying Tips and Care Guidelines
- Inspect the outer box: Look for seals that are intact and packaging that looks consistent with other boxes in the same wave. If the seal is obviously broken or resealed, you might want to proceed with caution or request a replacement from the retailer.
- Check the promo card: When you first open the box, glance at the promo card’s foil quality and centering. If you’re serious about grading, you’ll want to separate and evaluate the promo card early before it’s exposed to oils on your fingers.
- Store away from sunlight and humidity: Even the most pristine card can degrade with poor storage. A cool, dry, and dark place is ideal for vast leisure shelves that hold your Pokemon treasures.
- Protect the contents: Use sleeves and top-loaders for any cards you pull that you intend to keep in good condition. For bulk pulls, standard binder storage is typically sufficient, but consider a binder with a proper anti-glare sheet to minimize light-induced color shift on holo cards.
- Verify authenticity: In the modern market, counterfeit concerns are present, though less common for sealed Korean ver boxes. If a price seems too good to be true, it might be worth verifying through serial numbers or retailer credentials before making a purchase.
