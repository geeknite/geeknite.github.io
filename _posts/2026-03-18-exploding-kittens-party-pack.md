### Setup and Components: What to Expect on the Table

Here is a quick rundown of the setup steps:
1. Shuffle the Exploding Kittens and Defuse cards into the main draw pile.
2. Deal a fixed number of cards to each player, typically seven, ensuring that every player has enough options to stall for time while you listen to someone tell a very long joke.
3. Remove a certain number of Exploding Kitten cards from the deck depending on the player count, so the game doesn’t balloon into a meltdown that takes three hours. The Party Pack calibrates this for you to keep the pace brisk.
4. Place the top X number of cards face down to form the draw pile and set the rest aside. It’s basically a game of musical chairs, but with more cat puns and fewer chairs.
5. Everyone selects a defuse card from their hand to start with. If you are lucky, you will not need it in the first round, but the odds are against you.

The box also includes helpful little reference cards that remind you of the turn order: draw, play a card, and hope you don’t draw an exploding kitten. ### House Rules and Variants for 10 Players
If you are planning a marathon game night with 10 players, you might want to experiment with some house rules to keep the energy high and the table engaged. Here are a few ideas to consider:
- The Meme Defuse Rule: If you use a defuse card, you must tell a quick meme-inspired joke that will be judged by the room for humor quality. If the room laughs, your defuse stands; if not, you lose your defuse for that round.
- The Draft Pack: Before the game, each player drafts one face-down card from the top of the deck to add a little unpredictability. This can be a defuse, another explosion, or a wild action that the owner must reveal at the start of the round.
- The Reaction Timer: If a player pretends to be calm while they draw, you give them 10 extra seconds of chaos to decide if they will confess the fear of exploding kittens or make a wild strategic move.

These house rules can help maintain pace and ensure everyone participates, which is crucial for a large group. The key is to keep the energy positive and the table light enough that no one feels singled out for a misplay.

### Tips and Tricks for Hosting a 10-Player Night
- Keep the table comfortable: With ten players, you need a table that supports everyone without forcing arms to collide during turns. If your living room is small, consider a fold-out table or a party setup on the floor with snack blankets. The objective is to keep everyone included and engaged.
- Create a simple rotation: If your group includes frequent players who know each other well, consider a quick rotation or a corkboard where players track seats to ensure everyone gets a turn to influence the game. It keeps the energy fair and prevents someone from feeling left out.
- Use house rules sparingly: The less you deviate from the core rules, the smoother the game will run. However, a few well-chosen house rules can add flavor and make the night more memorable. The Meme Defuse is a good starting point, as it adds a quick moment of performance that can break tension and create laughter.
- Prepare a snack strategy: The chaos at the table will generate thirst and appetite. Plan a snack bar with easy-to-eat options and a few cards that double as coasters for your drinks. The goal is to minimize breaks that kill momentum while maximizing the fun.
