### Raw material characteristics
Beech wood is known for a fine, even texture and a light color that can range from pale beige to warm honey. The grain tends to be straight, with occasional flecks of figure that appear as small waves along the surface. In practical terms, this means fewer surprises during sanding and finishing. The density of beech makes it resistant to dents relative to softer woods, which translates into a longer life for a frequently used tabletop. If you are into finish portraits of your table, you will likely appreciate how well beech accepts both oil and water-based finishes, while remaining relatively stable when acclimated to indoor conditions. ## DIY Customization Ideas
If you are the kind of person who loves personal touches, the Luxe Eternity Table Top Beech is a good starting canvas for customization. Here are a few ideas you can consider, each with its own set of considerations:

### Edge case: humidity and climate
Be mindful of changes in humidity and temperature. Wood expands and contracts with moisture; a well-finished top can tolerate small fluctuations, but extreme changes can create micro gaps or finish movement. In climates with high humidity, consider using a dehumidifier or humidity control for the room to preserve the optimal surface stability.
