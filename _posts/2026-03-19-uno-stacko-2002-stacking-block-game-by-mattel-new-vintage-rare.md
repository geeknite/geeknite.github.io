### How to Play Without Losing Your Sanity

If you have played UNO, you know most of the core flavor of Stacko. The object is simple: remove blocks one by one, following the color or number cue on the block you are touching, and stack them on top without letting the tower crash. The 2002 variant leans into that classic logic, but with a few twists that keep the game lively even for experienced players.

Setup is a gentle ritual. The blocks are shuffled face down and arranged into a stable tower on a sturdy surface. Players take turns removing one block from anywhere in the tower according to the color or number shown on the block being touched, then placing the removed block on top of a new stack. If the tower wobbles or falls, the player who caused the collapse usually takes a friendly roast from the table and one extra turn to re-center the chaos.

The rules are lean but not cruel. If you pull a block that matches the color or number of the block directly beneath it, you get a small bonus in your mind for a job well done. If the block you pull is a special cue, such as a wild color or a higher action, your turn can become a mini-game within the game. The official nuance is simple: keep your composure, stack carefully, and avoid the avalanche. It is a game built for social friction in the best possible way—a little tension, a lot of laughter, and a tower that sometimes looks like a modern art sculpture with a questionable color palette.

### Strategy and Personal Tips for Beginners and Veterans

- Take your time on the early pulls. The middle game is where towers tend to fail, so you want to position yourself for later blocks that blend color and number in favorable ways.
- Use a calm stance and a soft hand. This is not a game of force; it is a game of controlled precision.
- Observe your opponents. Friendly misdirection can be as powerful as a well-timed block pull. A joking taunt or a goofy face can be worth more than a perfect pull in a tense moment.
- Don’t fear the cascade, embrace it. A mishap can yield a dramatic reset and new laughs for everyone involved.
