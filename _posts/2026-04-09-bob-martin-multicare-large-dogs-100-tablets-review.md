## How to Dose This For a Large Dog
Dosing for large dogs typically depends on weight. The big question is: how many tablets should your majestic beast receive each day? The label will provide its recommended dosage, often tied to body weight ranges. A common pattern for large breeds is something like: one tablet for every 25–40 kg of body weight daily, sometimes split into two half-doses with meals. But every dog is a snowflake, and some snowflakes are extremely stubborn about taking pills.

Here are practical dosing tips to maximize compliance:

- Administer with food to improve palatability and absorption. Empty stomachs and crunch-time vitamins aren’t friends.
- Break tablets if your dog refuses the whole thing, or use a pill pocket if your pup has a taste for drama and deception.
- Start with the recommended dosage and monitor for changes in mobility, energy levels, and general attitude. If your dog seems to drag the paws more than usual, consult a vet before increasing the dose.
- Be consistent. Dogs thrive on routine almost as much as they crave table scraps—almost.

If you want a quick dose-by-weight cheat sheet, here’s a generic starter guide (check your product label first):

- 25–40 kg: 1 tablet daily
- 40–60 kg: 1–2 tablets daily
- 60+ kg: 2 tablets daily (and probably two very impressed humans watching a giant dog do a victory lap)

As with any supplement, start slow and watch for adverse reactions. If you notice gastrointestinal upset, allergic reactions, or a sudden change in appetite, pause the product and consult your veterinarian. And if your dog is already on another medication, verify there are no potential interactions that could trigger drama and a vet visit. We’re aiming for wagging tails, not veterinary bill epics.
