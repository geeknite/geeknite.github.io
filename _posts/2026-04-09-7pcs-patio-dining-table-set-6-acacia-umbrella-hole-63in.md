## Maintenance Tips: Keeping the Charms Without the Fretwork

- Clean regularly with a soft brush and mild soap solution. Avoid harsh chemicals that could strip the finish.
- Oil the wood occasionally to preserve color and flexibility. A simple DIY wood oil or outdoor furniture conditioner can go a long way.
- Use cushions if you want extra comfort; they’ll also protect the wood from direct contact with skin oils and food stains.
- Protect from extreme weather: during rain or snow, consider covers or indoor storage, especially in climates with heavy winter moisture.
- Check hardware annually to ensure screws remain snug, as loose hardware can make chairs feel unstable and dampen the “quality craftsmanship” vibe.

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it’s the responsible adult thing to do for your outdoor furniture. If you do it, you’ll extend the life of the set and keep it looking good enough to impress guests who notice the natural patina rather than the brand-new shine.
