## What is Meadow Green Tea
Meadow Green Tea is a green tea blend that leverages leaf from meadow grown bushes and a curated mix of herbs that evoke a sunlit field. The blend aims to balance the natural grassiness of classic green tea with a soft herbal lift. ### Brewing Meadow Green Tea
Brewing mode is where Meadow Green Tea shines. The recommended parameters balance caffeine content, aroma, and flavor without making the cup a science project. Here is a simple blueprint for gamers, developers, teachers, and folks who love the smell of a well brewed cup:
- Water temperature: 75 to 80 C (167 to 176 F).
- Steep time: 2 to 3 minutes for a bright cup; 4 minutes yields a deeper, more robust cup.
- Tea to water ratio: roughly 1 teaspoon per 8 oz (240 ml) water, or 1 bag per cup for convenience.
- Vessel: a clear mug or glass teapot helps you watch the tea blossom like a tiny lab experiment in a sci fi kitchen.

For those who enjoy variable infusions, Meadow Green Tea holds up well for a second and third steep. With each infusion the flavor shifts slightly toward the more mineral, toasted notes. Caffeine content decreases with each infusion, which is excellent for late night missions that do not require hero stamina.

### Geeky Experiments with Meadow Green Tea
Because this is Geeknite, we broke out a tiny lab notebook to test the tea beyond the kitchen bench. Here are a few fun experiments you can try at home, all while staying within a reasonable caffeine budget and a good sense of humor:

#### Temperature Variation Test
We prepared three cups with the same leaf mass and variables of temperature: 70 C, 80 C, and 90 C. The 70 C cup yielded a lighter, more delicate flavor with a mild sweetness and a faint mineral note. The 80 C cup hit the balance sweet spot with a grassy brightness and a hint of citrus. The 90 C cup extracted more astringent notes and brought forward the vegetal punch, making the cup bolder but with a shorter finish. The moral of the experiment: small changes yield big flavor differences, and a coder-friendly session might actually benefit from brew temperature stability rather than the latest patch.

#### Steep Time Showdown
Another test compared 2 minutes vs 3 minutes. The 2 minute cup was crisp, with a clean finish and less tannin bite. The 3 minute cup developed more body and a slightly more pronounced sweetness. For long sessions that demand a longer lasting flavor, the 3 minute infusion is the better partner, but if you want a quick pick-me-up between meetings, the 2 minute brew wins.

#### Infusion Fabric Comparison
A final experiment assessed a 2 minute first infusion and a 1 minute second infusion. The second infusion is lighter but still carries a whisper of the meadow and a touch of herbal lift. It is perfect for those who want to stretch their tea budget during long code days. The caffeine content falls with every infusion, which helps maintain a steady rhythm as you push through a feature flag sprint.
