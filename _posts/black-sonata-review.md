## The Basics

### What is Black Sonata?

Black Sonata is a hidden movement and deduction game designed by John Kean. It captures the essence of Elizabethan England, allowing players to experience the suspense and intrigue of tracking the elusive Dark Lady from Shakespeare's sonnets. One player takes on the role of the Lady, while others must use their wits to uncover her identity and location.

## Gameplay

### Setting Up

Setup is straightforward. The Lady player secretly plans her moves using a deck of cards, while the other players position their tokens on the board, tracking the Lady's movements based on limited information and clues.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, the Lady moves stealthily across the board, using various actions to avoid detection, such as moving quietly, creating distractions, or blending into the surroundings. The other players must use deduction and strategy to predict the Lady's movements and corner her successfully.

### Stealth and Strategy

The game shines in its strategic depth. As the Lady, you must carefully plan your moves, utilize cover, and think several steps ahead to outwit the trackers. For the tracker players, cooperation and deduction are key. You'll need to communicate effectively, share information, and anticipate the Lady's tactics to corner her successfully.
