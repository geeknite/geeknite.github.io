## Maintenance and Care

Like any worthy blade, the Midnight Forge Wakizashi requires routine care to keep it performing and looking its best. Here’s a concise maintenance plan that won’t require a black belt in chemistry or a shrine to petroleum products:

- Clean after use: Wipe the blade with a soft cloth to remove fingerprints, oils, and minor moisture. Use a light, non-corrosive oil if you notice any slight oxidation starting to creep in on the blade surface.
- Check the mekugi: Periodically inspect the pin to ensure the handle remains snug. If you notice play, reseat the pin or replace it as needed. Don’t force parts — if something feels off, consult a pro.
- Avoid harsh chemicals: Mild cleaners and water are sufficient for the blade surface. Avoid bleach or acidic solutions that can degrade the steel or the wood finish.
- Scabbard care: Keep the wooden scabbard dry and free of prolonged moisture. If you live in a humid environment, a light wipe-down and a small amount of wax or oil on the exterior can help prevent cracking or warping.
- Storage: Store away from direct heat and sunlight. The lacquer on the scabbard benefits from stable temperatures, and you’ll preserve the finish longer when you avoid extreme heat fluctuations.
