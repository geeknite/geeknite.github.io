### Cleaning, Maintenance, and Longevity

Synthetic fibers shine in the cleaning department. The rug resists most common spills and stains, and a quick vacuum keeps the surface looking fresh. For spills, blotting is your friend; rubbing can push the stain deeper and create a new abstract art piece on the rug. A mild detergent solution and water usually do the trick for most household messes. Avoid harsh chemical solvents that could degrade fibers or affect colorfastness.

Regular maintenance matters more in high-traffic rooms. A weekly vacuum helps maintain pile height and prevents dirt from settling deeply into the fabric. For deeper cleaning, a shallow steam clean can be effective, but always consult manufacturer guidelines to avoid compromising the rug’s integrity. If you opt for a custom size, you might have to adapt your cleaning approach to the rug’s shape, which can be a fun challenge and a great way to practice geometry in real life.

### How to Order: A Simple Roadmap

If you are leaning toward a custom size or the classic 5x7, here’s a straightforward pathway to order:

1) Measure your space precisely, including any odd corners or nonstandard widths. A good rule of thumb is to measure twice and cut once—metaphorically, since you’re not actually cutting the rug but you are confirming the fit.
2) Choose the option that matches your space. If you’re uncertain between custom size and 5x7, a quick mental experiment helps: imagine furniture placements and see which option creates a balanced feel in the room without crowding or leaving too much bare floor.
3) Pick colorways that align with your existing palette. Neutral tones tend to be more forgiving, while bold hues create a dramatic focal point. If you’re unsure, ask for swatches or request a virtual mockup if the retailer offers this service.
4) Review lead times and shipping. Custom orders require a longer window for production and delivery, whereas 5x7 options usually ship faster. Plan accordingly for moves, renovations, or special occasions.
5) Confirm cleaning and warranty details. A good rug is part of a bigger system, and you want to know the boundaries of care and protection.
