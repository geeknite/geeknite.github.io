## Assembly, Maintenance, and Day-to-Day Use

As with any substantial piece of office furniture, assembly is a rite of passage. The Line Prime arrives in a well-packed box with a modular design that assumes you know which end is the top and which is the bottom. The instruction manual is clear, though your inner technician might want to replicate the results with more power tools than strictly necessary. Expect a two to four hour assembly timeline, depending on your precision and whether you decided to pre-screw every fastener in the same direction as a form of meditation.

### Assembly Steps That Won’t Make You Cry

1. Unpack and identify each panel. A color-coded diagram helps you sort when the coffee hasn’t quite kicked in.
2. Attach the outer panels to the central spine first to establish the overall width. This is where a friend or two comes in handy to hold panels in place while you tighten bolts.
3. Install the underframe and add cross supports. The chassis should feel sturdy before you mount the top surfaces.
4. Attach cable management components if you opted for them. If you did not, consider a cord wrap daydream and a volunteer to manage the tangle later.
5. Install the top surfaces and perform a final alignment check. It should be flat and level, or you will discover every coffee spill from weeks past in the seam.

Maintenance is straightforward: wipe with a soft cloth, avoid harsh chemicals on the veneer, and periodically check fasteners for loosening. A simple lube on any sliding components (if you have adjustable legs, for example) can extend life and keep the sturdiest parts from squeaking like a haunted chair in a thriller film.

### Cable Management Without Tears

A major advantage of the Line Prime setup is how it handles cables. The central spine and optional under-desk trays let you conceal and organize power strips, HDMI/DisplayPort lines, and USB-C hubs. A neat desk is a productive desk, they say, and in the six-person configuration, neatness is a team sport. Use cable sleeves, Velcro ties, and a few thoughtful grommets. If your office space has ceiling-mounted power drops, you can even route power to each side to reduce the risk of end-of-day tripping hazards.
