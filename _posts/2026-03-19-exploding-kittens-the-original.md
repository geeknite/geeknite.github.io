## Gameplay and Setup

The setup is delightfully minimal. Shuffle the deck, give each player a couple of Defuse cards, deal a few life points in the form of a smile that may or may not vanish under pressure, and then reveal what could be the friendliest looking deck you will ever play with. The game uses a standard deck of cards that lean heavily on risk management and psychological warfare. The long and short of it is this: you draw a card, you hope the card you draw is not an Exploding Kitten, and you try not to blow up your own good time. If you draw an Exploding Kitten, you must play a Defuse card to survive. If you cannot, you are eliminated. The last player standing wins the glory of not being the one who exploded mid conversa-tion about a cartoon cat. The pace is quick, often measured in seconds between decisions, and the humor grows as the tension builds.

### Card Types and Functions

- Exploding Kitten: the dreaded card that signals the end for the unfortunate soul who draws it without a Defuse.
- Defuse: the lifesaver, usually a one time pass to live another day.
- See the Future: peek at the top three cards and plan your fate.
- Attack: end your turn with a mental what have I done moment for the next player.
- Skip: avoid drawing for a turn.
- Shuffle: disrupt the draw order, sometimes for the good, sometimes for the chaos.
- Nope: a wild no that can stop another player s action.
- Cat Cards: pairs of funny animals that let you steal cards or trigger combos when collected in pairs.

These are not just letters on a card; they are tiny engines of chaos that produce a surprising amount of satisfaction. The humor is not in the cruel mechanics but in the way players misread intentions at the exact moment when a card is about to redefine the game.

### Strategy for the Original

Strategy in Exploding Kittens is less about deep mathematical optimization and more about wearing down your table mates with timing and bluffing. A few practical tips to get you through the first few matches: 
1. Do not fear the Defuse card, fear the moment you realize that you are the one holding all the Defuses while the deck shuffles like a caffeinated blender. 
2. When you see a See the Future, think about whether you want to risk using the information to set up a combo or to bait your friends with a fake sense of safety. 
3. Save your Nope cards for the most ridiculous plays that win you the table s respect while also making your friends bleed with laughter. 
4. Cat Cards are about pairs; someone is bound to try to make a two-card collection while you try to read their tells. 
5. Expansions can tilt the balance; if you play with extra cards, expect a different rhythm and a higher probability of an epic table meltdown. 
There is no hidden formula that guarantees victory every time; it s all about misdirection, reading the table, and the occasional lucky draw. The better you are at balancing risk against reward, the more your victories will feel earned rather than inevitable. For the most part, Exploding Kittens rewards a quick sense of humor more than a perfect strategy. If you can bluff a little, you might survive longer than your more risk-averse friends.
