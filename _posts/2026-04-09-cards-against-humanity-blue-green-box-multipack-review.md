## How to play the MultiPack: setup, rounds, and rules etiquette

Cards Against Humanity is famously simple to learn and hard to master. The Blue Box and Green Box MultiPack doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it amps up access to a wider pool of prompts and responses by providing dual-sets that can be used independently or together. Here’s a practical guide to getting started quickly:

1) Assemble players: The typical sweet spot is 4-8 players, though you can go smaller or larger as long as you’re comfortable with “creative interpretations” of the prompts. If you’re hosting teens or coworkers who aren’t keen on spicy content, have a quick content boundary talk and consider hot-ands-not cards that your group agrees to avoid.
2) Decide the first judge: The judge rotates each round. The judge draws a black card and reads it aloud. The other players secretly pick a white card that they think best fits the prompt.
3) Play the round: The chosen white card is handed to the judge (face-down or visible, depending on group preference). The judge shuffles and reads them aloud, then selects the winning card. The player who submitted it earns a point.
4) Rounds and endurance: A typical night might run 6-12 rounds, but if your crew is on a roll, you can keep rolling until chaos becomes a contest of who can form the most outrageous sentence.
5) Safe words and consent: A modern CAH night works best when everyone feels comfortable. Set a couple of quick ground rules, agree on a “pause” option, and consider pre-emptive cards that you want to avoid to keep the laughter inclusive rather than exclusive.

Gameplay tips:
- Mix and match: Use the Blue Box for a set of prompts and the Green Box for responses, to keep the energy varied.
- Save the best for last: If you’re recording the night, save a handful of the most ridiculous prompts for a grand finale round.
- Theme rounds: Build a themed rotation (e.g., workplace humor, fantasy land, dating misadventures) to keep the comedic style fresh.

## Accessibility, inclusivity, and gameplay ethics

One important consideration for CAH nights (including this MultiPack) is accessibility. Card readability matters—font size, contrast, and the physical action of reading aloud across a noisy room can be challenging for some players. The Blue Box and Green Box MultiPack uses standard CAH typography, which works for most folks, but if you have players with visual impairment, consider a larger print variant or a simple card magnifier to keep the game inclusive.

Inclusivity is a moving target in humor culture. The CAH community often debates lines and boundaries, which is healthy as long as it remains consensual at the table. The MultiPack’s dual-deck design means you can curate a session with less controversial prompts and more universal humor, or you can lean into the edgy material if your group is comfortable with it. The key is to set expectations, check in with first-time players, and keep “ace up the sleeve” power moves like a quick skip or a veto available.

## Practical tips to maximize laughs and minimize chaos

- Create a “safe-swap” rule: allow players to trade out a questionable card for a more comfortable option before the round begins.
- Keep score optional: the game often works best as a storytelling exercise rather than a competition. If your group loves competition, you can happily award silly prizes for the most outrageous winning card.
- Use a timer for rounds: a fast-paced game night keeps momentum high and prevents the table from turning into a voting circle for moral philosophy.
- Theme nights: experiment with “office humor,” “nerd culture,” or “awkward dates” to unlock new types of punchlines and to keep the content fresh across multiple sessions.
