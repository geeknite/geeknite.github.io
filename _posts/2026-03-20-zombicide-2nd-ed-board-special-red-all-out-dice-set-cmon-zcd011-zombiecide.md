## How the All-Out dice actually change play
This is the core of the purchase: what do these dice do for your Zombicide game that the standard dice don’t? The Red All-Out set is designed to be used during All-Out Attack turns. In Zombicide 2nd Edition, All-Out Attack is a mechanic that can push a round from “we’ll see what happens” to “we’re going all in” at the expense of some risk and resource costs. The red dice are meant to emphasize those big moments—more dramatic results, bigger expected damage, and a clearer voice for the tactical choices players make when they commit to an aggressive push.

In practical terms, you’ll notice a few things when you switch to Red All-Out dice:
- Increased chance of high-damage outcomes on the red faces. The faces are tuned to feel like a “burst” of action rather than a steady trickle of cuts. When you’re rolling these dice, your board becomes louder in the most glamorous way possible: zambamboom, zombies go down, you revel in your dice’s dramatic performance.
- Clearer feedback on critical moments. The All-Out dice are meant to provide more obvious success markers so you can easily narrate the moment you removed a big threat and saved a teammate who was being chased by a slobbering horde.
- A tactile cognitive cue that you’re in a different tactical mode. Switching to red dice is like flipping a switch from “calm planning” to “dramatic hero moment.” It helps with immersion and pacing; it also lends itself to a bit of roleplay, if you and your group enjoy that kind of flavor play.

That said, the All-Out dice aren’t a game-breaking addition. They don’t add new rules to the core engine; they optimize the perception of the risk/reward trade-off. You’ll still manage your inventory, plan your actions, and roll dice as dictated by the card-driven turn order and the All-Out rules. The dice simply bolster the moment when you shout for the big play, and they look cool while doing it.

## Durability, maintenance, and how to store them
Dice are pretty simple in terms of maintenance: keep them away from heat and direct sunlight long enough to avoid warping or color fade. The Red All-Out Dice Set uses standard plastic dice material with a color treatment that should resist chipping under normal play. If you take your box to a convention with a thousand elbows and a dozen carts rolling across the floor, you might ask for a dice tray to minimize rolling spills. A little care goes a long way. If you’re one of those players who loves a dedicated dice tower for dramatic roll shots, these dice will look great tumbling through the tower as if they were tiny red meteors returning to the planet of the survivors.

Storage-wise, they slot nicely into most Zombicide accessory cases, thanks to their uniform size and the familiar cube shape. If you own a lot of dice, consider organizing by color or by game mode so you can locate the All-Out red dice in a pinch during a tense moment. You don’t want to waste a crucial turn digging through a multi-compartment box while the apocalypse rumbles outside your table legs.
