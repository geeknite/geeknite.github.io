## Assembly and setup: how long does it really take?
Assembly experience varies by model and how confident you are with tiny fasteners. A typical unboxed setup can be completed by one adult in 30–60 minutes with standard hand tools (or sometimes no tools if the design is tool-less). Quick checklist:
1. Lay out all parts and verify against the instruction diagram.
2. Assemble the frame on a flat surface—ensure corners are square so the mat sits evenly.
3. Attach springs or bands in a criss-cross pattern to distribute tension evenly.
4. Attach the jumping mat and secure it to the frame.
5. Install the enclosure net, making sure the zipper aligns with the entry point.
6. Place the ball pit components and fill with balls as desired.
7. Do a cautious test bounce with an adult—check for wobble, misalignment, or odd noises.

If you’re anything like me, you’ll probably spend more time rearranging furniture to fit the box’s footprint than you do on actual assembly. Pro tip: keep a magnetic screwdriver handy for tiny screws. And remember, safety first: test with your most forgiving family member before you put the kids on it.
