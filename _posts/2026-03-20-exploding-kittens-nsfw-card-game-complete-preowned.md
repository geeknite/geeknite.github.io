## Unboxing and preowned condition considerations
A COMPLETE, preowned copy is a chance to save a buck, but it also asks you to assess wear and tear with a detective’s eye. Here are the top things to inspect when you receive a used NSFW deck:
- Card condition: Look for edge wear, creases, or faded art. If the cards feel sticky or show signs of moisture damage, that’s a red flag that may ruin the experience more than a bad draw.
- Defuse cards: These are valuable. Ensure that you have enough in the deck and that the Defuse backs aren’t nicked. If your Defuses are misprinted or compromised, you might as well pretend you forgot to shuffle.
- Exploding Kitten cards: You want a reliable count and good color separation. Faded explosions or misprints might skew the game balance.
- The box and inserts: A battered box doesn’t ruin a game, but it’s a sign of heavy nights and lots of spilled beverages. If the inner tray is missing, you might end up with a chaotic shuffle that tastes like chaos and tangerines.
- Rule sheet: The NSFW edition sometimes includes errata. If your rule sheet is missing or ambiguous, you’ll want to compare to the online official rules to make sure you don’t invent a new way to explode your own game night.

As a buyer, you should also confirm that the deck is complete. The number of cards in a NSFW edition is specific, and missing cards can ruin a playing experience. If you are buying from a used marketplace, request close-up photos of faces and backs to verify that the colors match the standard edition. If the seller claims “mint condition” but the corners resemble a dog-eared map, you should probably negotiate or walk away. Peace of mind is worth a few extra dollars.

## The preowned buying guide for NSFW Exploding Kittens
Buying used means you can maximize value while still keeping the core experience intact. Here are our recommended steps:
- Confirm completeness: Count the cards if possible, and verify that the Defuse cards are present in expected quantities. Missing a handful of Defuses or Exploding Kittens is not a disaster, but it changes the balance and the odds in a way that can be frustrating.
- Check for moisture and stickiness: Card surfaces should feel smooth and dry. If the texture sticks to your fingers or smells sour, the deck might be radiating warning signals about humidity damage.
- Inspect for edge wear: A worn edge can produce roughness that affects shuffle quality. If a card’s edges are chipped, it may cause cards to misalign or jam in the deck tray.
- Evaluate the box and inserts: A battered box does not ruin play, but it can affect resale value and the ease with which you can store the deck. Check that the insert tray is present and intact; it keeps the cards from sliding during storage and reduces the risk of creases.
- Look for rogue stickers or markings: Some used copies include temporary signage from previous owners. These are not a functional problem, but they can be as loud as a cartoon explosion when you try to play.
- Sanity check with the official rules: If you cannot locate a rule sheet, the internet is your friend. Our posts linked above contain distilled rules and clarifications that help you avoid the dreaded misinterpretation of a “future see” card as something else entirely.

Pricing-wise, used NSFW decks vary widely based on condition and rarity of packaging. If you spot a bargain, do due diligence, but remember that a pristine vibe is a lot easier to maintain with practice and careful storage. You’ll probably find that a well cared-for, preowned copy delivers the best value without sacrificing the core experience of the NSFW edition.

### How to care for your used deck after purchase
- Keep it dry and away from heat sources to preserve card art and color depth.
- Use card sleeves if you plan to play frequently. It’s an inexpensive way to extend the deck’s life and keep the art crisp for longer.
- Store in a cool, dry place, ideally in its box with the lid closed. A little humidity control goes a long way for keeping the cards poppy instead of browning around the edges.
- Clean hands before handling: this reduces the risk of transferring oils or stains that can degrade card surfaces over time.

## Gameplay deep dive: setup, turn structure, and core cards
### Setup and flow
1) Shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand to each player (the number varies by edition but is generally around 7 cards).
2) Place the remaining deck in the center. The Exploding Kitten and Defuse cards should be mixed per the rules.
3) Decide who goes first, and let the chaos unfold.

On each turn, a player can play one or more action cards, depending on the card you draw and your strategy. The goal remains simple: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten. If you draw one, you can try to survive with a Defuse card or bluff your way into another round. The NSFW edition spices this up with some edgy commentary and card effects that can alter the turn order, force you to draw more cards, or force other players to deal with the consequences of your mischief.

### Key cards and their impact
- Exploding Kitten: The doom card. If you draw it and you cannot Defuse, you’re out for the round.
- Defuse: The lifeline. It typically lets you survive and reshuffle the Exploding Kitten card back into the deck in a way that’s friendly to you or your allies.
- See the Future: Look at the top few cards of the deck, giving you a sense of what’s coming and how to maneuver your strategy.
- See the Future (NSFW flavor): Still allows foresight, but with a wink toward the edition’s humor.
- Attack, Skip, and Shuffle: These cards shift the turn order, skip your draw, or shuffle the deck to spread the chaos in unpredictable ways.
- Favor: Forces another player to give you a card, creating a moment of strategic back-and-forth.

In NSFW, a few cards lean into adult pop culture or cheeky misdirections. The core mechanics don’t vanish; they just get more opportunities to misbehave and to spark laughter at the table. If you are playing with a mixed crowd, you can use a house rule that toggles some of the more explicit jokes to keep things comfortable while preserving the energy of the game.

### House rules and variants
- The Casual Roast Rule: Every time someone makes a joke about the art, that person draws a card as a playful penalty. It keeps the humor reciprocal and keeps players engaged.
- The Double Defuse Rule: If you draw two Defuse cards in a single turn, you keep both and reshuffle accordingly. This speeds up late game resilience.
- Content Warning Card: A one card that toggles a content warning banner to remind players of what they signed up for. It’s optional but respectful for groups with new players.

## Care, maintenance, and care of the preowned deck
- Proper storage: Keep the deck in a cool, dry place. Humidity is the enemy of card stock and glossier finish alike.
- Sleeving: Card sleeves can extend the life of your cards, preserve the art, and help prevent edge wear from happening in the first place.
- Regular checks: If your deck has been used heavily in the past, a quick inspection before every game night helps avoid unpleasant surprises mid-play. Check for stuck cards or subtle warping that can ripple into wrong draws.
- Cleaning: If you notice fingerprints or minor stains, use a soft cloth to gently wipe the surface. Do not scrub or submerge the cards; you want to preserve their paste finish and color depth for as long as possible.
