## How to play: setup and turn flow
The setup is quick and forgiving. Gather players, shuffle cards, stack dice, and lay out a few tokens. The first round typically flows like this:

1) Each player draws a hand of cards from the deck. The number of cards you start with is consistent, but the exact details can vary depending on the number of players. 2) In turn order, players will select cards from hand to play in front of them. These cards establish their intent for the round and may trigger immediate effects or set the stage for more complex plays later on. 3) After everyone commits their cards, players roll dice to determine which effects resolve this turn. The dice outcomes can amplify or cancel the cards effects, creating a dynamic moment where plans can unravel in a single roll. 4) End of turn effects may include reshuffling, drawing new cards, or earning points. The flow repeats for a handful of rounds until a victory threshold is reached.

The heart of the game is the tension between what you want to do and what your dice permit you to do. It is common to see players take calculated gambles, smiling bravely as the table calculates the odds of success while secretly praying to the deer gods for a lucky roll. The result is a tempo that alternates between quick, snap decisions and longer, more strategic plays as players gauge other people’s intentions.

For players who prefer a solo style or a cooperative tilt, there are variants built into the rule set or as easy house rules. The solo mode often emphasizes puzzle solving with a timer, while co op style adds shared objectives that require players to coordinate to reach a common goal.
