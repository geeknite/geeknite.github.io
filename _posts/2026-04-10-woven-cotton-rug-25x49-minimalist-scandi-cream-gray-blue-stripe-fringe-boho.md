Care and maintenance
### Cleaning and routine care
Cotton rugs are forgiving when it comes to spills and everyday dust. A light vacuum with a brush attachment on a low setting is usually enough to keep the surface fresh. For accidental stains, treat them quickly with a mild detergent and water. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that could dull the fibers or cause color migration. If you spill coffee or juice, blot gently rather than rubbing, which helps preserve the texture and stripe integrity. The fringe can accumulate dust; a soft brush or a gentle shake helps keep it tidy. Occasionally, rotate the rug to ensure even wear and to prevent one side from looking too sun-kissed if your room basks in strong daylight.
### Rug pads and placement
A pad under the rug is a quiet ally. It reduces slippage on hardwood or tile, protects the fibers from abrasion, and prevents the rug from curling at the edges. Look for a pad that is slightly smaller than the rug’s dimensions to avoid noticeable edges showing through. For a living room setup where a sofa sits on one long axis, a pad that provides grip under the entire rug while staying almost invisible is ideal. In a hallway or entry, a thinner pad works best to maintain a clean line and minimize trip risk. The goal is steady footing plus a calm, anchored look that doesn’t produce a squeak every time someone shifts their weight.

Maintenance cadence and tips
- Vacuum weekly with a low setting to avoid fiber pulling; rotate quarterly to prevent high wear in one area
- Treat spills promptly with a blot technique; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber distortion
- Use a rug pad for better wear, less slipping, and easier grooming of the fringe
- Consider a gentle whitening or stain treatment if you notice discoloration in cream tones; always test in a small area first
- Keep away from direct heat sources that could cause fiber shrinkage or color changes
