## Setup and rules
The setup is straightforward and designed to minimize pre-game chaos. Here’s a quick-start outline:
- Shuffle the party deck and deal seven cards to each player.
- Insert the Exploding Kitten cards into the draw pile according to the player count (the exact mix is in the rulebook; don’t worry, you won’t need a math minor to figure it out).
- If the pack includes Defuse cards (typical for most kits), deal enough to give each player one or two Defuses, as dictated by the rules, and shuffle the rest back in.
- Place the draw pile face down. Each player draws a starting hand on their first turn.
- The first player begins, and play proceeds clockwise. On your turn, draw a card; you may then play action cards to influence the flow of the round.

The core mechanic remains intuitive: avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten unless you can Defuse it, use action cards to modify who draws next, and rely on the Nope cards to cancel what you don’t like. It’s a simple framework that scales elegantly to big groups because the social friction becomes the main engine of entertainment.

## Card types and synergy
The Party Pack reuses the familiar card suite from Exploding Kittens, with a couple of party-oriented sensibilities added:
- Exploding Kitten: draw one and you’re out of the current round unless you Defuse.
- Defuse: your emergency parachute, letting you survive a kitten catastrophe.
- See the Future: peek at the top three cards to plan your next move.
- Skip: skip your draw phase for that turn.
- Attack: end your turn and force the next player to take multiple turns.
- Shuffle: shuffle the draw pile to introduce fresh chaos.
- See the Future: a direct peek at what’s coming so you can plan around risk.
- Nope: cancel the effect of any card or action, giving you a moment to pivot.
- New party cards: these are designed to tilt large-group dynamics toward more negotiation and back-room wheeling-dealing rather than pure luck.

Those card types create a network of decisions. In a 10-player table, you’ll see players use See the Future to peek at a string of Exploding Kitten cards and decide whether to press their luck, or hold a defuse for a late-game rescue. You’ll witness a tense standoff as two players trade a Nope for a decisive moment, effectively freezing the table in collective anticipation. The synergy between card types is what elevates the experience beyond simple luck; it’s a mini social negotiation within a single 30-minute window.
