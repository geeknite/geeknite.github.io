## Quick Setup and What You Need to Know
Zombicide Black Plague is designed for 1–6 players, though the sweet spot sits around 3–4 if you want lively turns without endless table flipping. The setup takes a little time, but the payoff is glorious. Here is a crisp rundown you can skim before the dice start clattering:

- Pick a board and lay out tiles as indicated in the scenario. The city blocks become your theatre; the zombies are the pesky chorus.
- Each player selects a survivor board. These boards tell you your character’s dice, special abilities, and starting inventory. Some survivors are foghorns of melee, others are stealthy scouts with a taste for loot. Mix and match for a balanced crew.
- Place zombie spawn tokens, objective tokens, and loot as shown by the scenario. The zombies will appear like uninvited guests who don’t understand personal space. Your goal is to corral them, not let them corral you.
- Determine the first player. This matters less than your strategy, but it helps keep the chaos organized. The rest of the turn order follows clockwise with everyone getting three actions per turn. Yes, three actions is a real thing; you will use them all, trust me.

To set the mood with a little visualization approach: imagine a medieval dungeon, but with more plot armor and less dragon fire. The little green and red dice on your hero cards decide whether you slice a zombie or politely invite it to a group hug—hug being a euphemism for “die loudly.”

## Core Rules Snapshot (In Plain English, No Goblin-Bite Required)
This section is a quick refresher of the essentials you will actually use in a session. If you’re a veteran, skim for the juicy bits; if you’re new, this is your first winter coat against a blizzard of zombies.

- Actions per turn: Each survivor gets three actions per turn. Actions include move, search, attack, open a door, and pick up loot. You can also chain actions in clever ways. The rulebook riffs about action economies the way a bard riffs about performance; it sounds fancy until the dice hit the table.
- Combat dice: Attacking a zombie rolls a number of six sided dice equal to your weapon’s Might (or a surge ability). Each die can yield hits that subtract from the zombie’s life, with some rolls triggering special effects like stunning, burning, or panicking adjacent zombies. Don’t rely on luck alone; you’ll need precise positioning and optimal weapon use.
- Zombicide movement: Zombies typically move toward the closest living survivor and teeter on the borderline of “ineffably creepy” and “clearly undead.” You’ll need to block chokepoints with terrain and space to avoid getting swarmed by more walkers than a blacksmith’s hammer has rivets.
- Loot and objectives: Finding loot adds weapons, armor, or special items to your stash. Objectives vary by scenario; some demand you rescue survivors, others require you to seal doors, and a few demand you craft legendary gear with runes and a little bit of dramatic flair.
- Experience and leveling: As you complete objectives and defeat higher tier zombies, your survivors gain XP that upgrades them to more powerful versions of themselves. This is the meta payoff where you feel your party’s brain cells click together as a single unit.

## Role Play Your Way to Victory: Survivor Roles and Team Composition
The fun of Zombicide Black Plague is that you assemble a party whose talents complement the chaotic undead world. Think of your team as a small, ridiculous, highly motivated hero squad. A good mix of roles will consistently outperform four identical swordsmen. Here are some archetypes and how they tend to contribute to a successful run:

### The Tank and the Shield Bashers
These survivors soak damage and disrupt crowds. They stand at the front line, taking hits so the rest can dump damage, loot, and morale into the camp. They’ll often carry heavy melee weapons to maximize crowd control. Pair your tank with a healer or ranged attacker to prevent stalemate in tight corridors.

### The Scout and the Sniper
Long-range specialists keep zombies from pinning you down as you cross the map. They tend to maximize map control by picking off threats before they reach your frontline. A good scout will also reveal loot and objectives in hard-to-reach spots, turning exploration into a strategic advantage rather than a grind.

### The Healer and Support Mage
Yes, magic exists here in the form of supportive abilities and items. Healers reduce the pain and the risk of losing your crew in a single unlucky zombie bite. Support mages offer buffs and sometimes unique one-off powers that tilt the tide in a pinch. When you’re facing a cluster of undead, a well-timed heal can mean the difference between a heroic exit and a dramatic slow walk to death.

### The Jack of All Trades
Some survivors have balanced stats and flexible gear. They’re the glue that holds the party together when random events spawn in a way that requires improvisation. They can adapt to shifting tactics mid battle, which is invaluable in long campaigns.

Tips for role selection:
- Start with a solid tank, a reliable ranged option, and a healer or support character if possible.
- Don’t overvalue raw DPS at the expense of mobility or survivability. Zombies love nothing more than a glitzy hero who can’t stay alive to use their loot.
- Consider how your chosen board’s special abilities synergize. Some survivors gain extra actions or unique reactions when others do certain things.

## Combat Strategy: Using Your Weapons Like a Well-Tuned Orchestra
Combat in Zombicide Black Plague is less about brute force and more about tempo, spacing, and weapon management. The dice, the terrain, and your decisions all fuse into a living chess match with a very loud soundtrack.

- Positioning is everything: You don’t want to be a one-hero show against a horde. Keep lines of sight clear, block chokepoints, and leverage doorways to funnel zombies into predictable patterns.
- Prioritize threats: Not all zombies are equally dangerous. Closer and tougher zombies require you to adjust your approach. A common tactic is to focus on a few essential targets first and then deal with the stragglers to avoid being overwhelmed.
- Weapon choice matters: Heavy weapons deliver big dice results but slow down your action economy. Ranged weapons give you range safety but can leave you hanging if a zombie closes the distance. Balanced teams use a mix of weapon types so you aren’t relying on a tiny sample size of dice.
- Use items strategically: Armor tokens and special items can dramatically swing a tense round. Don’t hoard everything for the final boss; use the equipment to hit key turns when the board demands it.
- Chain actions wisely: Remember that you have three actions per turn. You can combine movement with an attack, or move, search, and then attack in a perfect tempo if you plan your route carefully.

## Map Awareness: Reading the Board Like a Dungeon Master with a Cup of Tea
The board is more than a backdrop; it is a dynamic agent that affects your survival. Here are practical ways to read the map like a pro:

- Doors are your friends and your worst nightmare. Open doors to reveal new loot and create line-of-sight opportunities; the wrong door opening can invite a fresh wave of zombies to your location. Plan your door openings around where you expect your team to end up next turn.
- Loot locations drive movement. Loot isn t simply a nice add on; it is often the difference between victory and a long, blood-streaked walk back to the start. Map your loot routes so that you can collect essential gear without forcing yourself into high risk corridors.
- Objective distances matter. Some scenarios reward you for speed, others for precision. Keep an eye on the scoring timeline and pace your actions accordingly. If you sprint too aggressively you might run out of surviving bodies before the map reveals its final twist.

In addition to terrain tactics, a practical habit is to assess the board’s risk level every turn. If a single move would end your life in a dramatic but non-fashionable way, consider a safer path or a strategic retreat. The best counters to chaos are good planning and sympathetic dice luck.

## XP, Leveling Up, and Making Your Survivors Really Feel Important
Progression in Zombicide Black Plague is one of the most satisfying parts of the game. As your survivors gain experience points through completing objectives and defeating zombies, they level up and gain upgraded abilities. This is where you finally feel your crew become something more than a random assortment of heroic plastic miniatures.

Key ideas:
- Plan long arcs: Don’t just chase loot in every turn. Map how your XP will be allocated to upgrades and how those upgrades will unlock new abilities that change your future turns.
- Diversify upgrades: Special abilities often complement team roles. A good balancing act keeps you from hitting the same upgrade repeatedly and becoming a one trick pony.
- Token economy: Some boards incorporate XP tokens tied to objectives or specific zombie kills. Manage these tokens the way a medieval merchant manages coins; every piece counts toward a bigger goal.

## Scenario Playstyles: How to Approach Common Encounters
Scenarios in Zombicide Black Plague are a mix of puzzle + brawl with a generous dose of heroic luck. Here are archetypal playstyles you’ll encounter and how to tackle them:

- The Rescue Run: You need to grab survivors and escort them to safety. Prioritize pathing, avoid dead ends, and use scouts to scout the direct routes for safe extraction.
- The Blockade: You are stuck in a corridor with a horde pressing in. Use your strongest defender to hold line, let others slice through to a loot cache, and then retreat while the zombies shuffle aimlessly in your wake.
- The Boss Rush: The map pumps up the difficulty with boss-like zombies. Your team should accelerate their upgrade path and coordinate abilities to maximize damage while keeping the tank alive through crowd control.
- The Sealing Endgame: You’re closing doors or sealing a map region. Prioritize finishing the objective while keeping a safe exit path for your team. Don’t get too hung up on loot; finishing the objective is the real win condition here.

In short, learn the objective, plan your turn, and be adaptive. The board will throw curveballs, but with a careful approach you can hit the sweet spot between glory and cheese.

## Advanced Tactics: Guardrails for the Serious Strategist
If you’ve got your crew humming along and you want to push into true mastery, consider these high level tactics:
- Space control over raw DPS: Don’t chase a single powerful target if that chase leaves your squishy backline exposed. Create safe zones where your team can reset and re-strategize.
- Timing upgrades: Don’t upgrade every survivor at once. A staggered upgrade path ensures you don’t lose a key ability for an entire round due to a failed upgrade roll.
- Predict zombie behavior: The zombie AI tends to follow consistent patterns. Use this to bait them into ideal positions where you can clear multiple threats in one turn rather than fighting them piecemeal.
- Resource rotation: If you have limited loot picks, rotate who carries what. Don’t clamp down on one player’s loadout. The party is strongest when load distribution is balanced and adaptable.

## Common Mistakes, Dips, and How to Avoid Them
Every rookie mistake in Zombicide Black Plague is a learning opportunity. Here are the frequent missteps and how to sidestep them:
- Ignoring positioning: The map is a living entity; don’t chase loot without considering where zombies will appear next.
- Overcommitting to a single target: Focus is good, but over commitment can leave your backline exposed and your healer overwhelmed.
- Hoarding loot: Smarter players use items when they’re most needed, not at the end of the round when nothing else seems to matter. Use items to swing turning points, not to collect dust on a shelf.
- Skipping exploration: The game rewards thorough exploration. If you skip loot or essential paths, you may be condemning your team to a slower, bloodier fate.
