## Quick Setup and What You Need to Know (Expanded)
Zombicide Black Plague supports 1–6 players and scales surprisingly well, though the sweet spot in my kitchen-table lab tends to be 3–4. That’s when turns feel brisk but not breathless, and you’re not arguing about door placement with the neighbors through the wall of dice. Here’s a thorough setup refresher you can skim before the ghoul parade begins:

- Pick a board and lay out tiles as indicated by the scenario. The city blocks become your stage; the zombies are the rowdy chorus that keeps interrupting the finale.
- Each player selects a survivor board. These boards tell you your character’s dice pool, special abilities, and starting inventory. Some survivors are melee juggernauts; others are stealthy loot-hunters. A balanced crew is like a good sandwich: a little not-too-lean meat with generous veggies of mobility and defense.
- Place zombie spawn tokens, objective tokens, and loot as shown by the scenario. Zombies appear at inopportune moments and in surprisingly good moods for rotting shells of sarcasm. Your goal is to corral them, funnel them, or diplomatically ask them to leave, which usually yields the same result—dead, but with more dramatic flair.
- Determine the first player. This matters less than your overall plan, but it helps keep the chaos from becoming a free-for-all kitchen-sink episode. The rest of the turn order follows clockwise, with everyone getting three actions per turn. Yes, three actions is a real thing; you will use them all, often in ways you didn’t expect.

To set the mood with a little visualization: imagine a medieval dungeon, but your party forgot to bring a map and the zombies brought duct tape. The dice decide whether you slice a zombie or offer it a politely staged group hug—hug meaning die loudly in a heroic, cinematic fashion.

## Core Rules Snapshot (In Plain English, With Occasional Pauses)
This section is a brisk refresher of the core systems you’ll actually use during a session. If you’re a veteran, skim for the juicy bells; if you’re new, this is your fortress against the winter of zombie snow.

- Actions per turn: Each survivor gets three actions. Actions include move, search, attack, open a door, pick up loot, or perform a clever combo. You can chain actions in surprising ways, creating mini-symphonies of movement and mayhem. The rulebook likes to sound fancy about action economies; your dice will remind you why it’s all about tempo.
- Combat dice: Attacking a zombie rolls a number of six-sided dice equal to your weapon’s Might or a surge-produced bonus. Each die yields hits that erode the zombie’s life, with some rolls triggering special effects like stunning, burning, or panicking adjacent zombies. Positioning and timing are everything; luck helps, but not as much as a well-timed headshot or a well-placed spell.
- Zombicide movement: Zombies chase the closest living survivor and drift between “ominously creepy” and “undeadly efficient.” You’ll want to block chokepoints with terrain and space to avoid being swarmed by more walkers than a blacksmith’s toolkit can handle.
- Loot and objectives: Loot adds weapons, armor, or special items to your stash. Objectives vary by scenario; some demand you rescue survivors, others require you to seal doors, and a few demand you craft legendary gear with runes and theatrical flair. The drama is real, and the dice are louder.
- Experience and leveling: As you complete objectives and defeat higher-tier zombies, survivors gain XP that upgrades them into more powerful versions of themselves. This is the meta payoff where your party begins to feel like a single instrument rather than a noisy orchestra pit.

## Role Play Your Way to Victory: Survivor Roles and Team Composition (Expanded Edition)
The fun of Zombicide Black Plague is assembling a party whose talents complement the chaotic undead world. Think of your team as a quirky, earnest, highly motivated squad who somehow survived a dungeon and a masquerade ball simultaneously. A varied roster consistently outperforms a squad of identical swordsmen. Here are archetypes and how they tend to contribute to a successful run:

### The Tank and the Shield Bashers
These survivors soak damage and disrupt crowds. They stand at the front line, soaking hits so the rest can dump damage, loot, and morale into the camp. They’ll often carry heavy melee weapons to maximize crowd control. Pair your tank with a healer or ranged attacker to maintain momentum and avoid the “two wounded heroes in a hallway” risk mentality.

### The Scout and the Sniper
Long-range specialists keep zombies from pinning you down as you cross the map. They maximize map control by picking off threats before they reach your frontline. A good scout reveals loot and objectives in hard-to-reach spots, turning exploration into a strategic advantage rather than a grueling scavenger hunt.

### The Healer and Support Mage
Yes, magic exists here in the form of supportive abilities and items. Healers reduce the risk of losing your crew to a single unlucky bite. Support mages offer buffs and sometimes unique powers that tilt the tide in a pinch. A well-timed heal or a protective shield can mean the difference between a heroic exit and a dramatic slow walk to death.

### The Jack of All Trades
Some survivors are balanced and flexible. They’re the glue that holds the party together when the board throws curveballs. They can adapt mid-battle, shifting gear and tactics to meet evolving threats.

### Extra Archetypes Worth Considering
- The Explosives Expert: Area denial and clearing chokes with fire and boom. Great for denying zombies a convenient path toward your tank.
- The Ritualist: Rune-based abilities that alter the warp of the board—think temporary zones of safety or forced delays for undead reinforcements.
- The Scout-Sniper Hybrid: Combines lines of sight with mobility, enabling drop-and-sprint plays that avoid getting bogged down in a single corridor.

Tips for role selection:
- Start with a solid tank, a reliable ranged option, and a healer or support character if possible. It’s a proven foundation that survives the first wave of “why did we open that door?”
- Don’t overvalue raw DPS at the expense of mobility or survivability. Zombies love a flashy hero who can’t stay alive long enough to use their loot.
- Consider how your chosen board’s special abilities synergize. Some survivors gain extra actions or unique reactions when others do certain things. Synergy is a word that sounds fancy until you see it in action on the board.

## Combat Strategy: Using Your Weapons Like a Well-Tuned Orchestra (More Than One Movement Theme)
Combat in Zombicide Black Plague is less about brute force and more about tempo, spacing, and weapon management. The dice are the drummer, the terrain is the bassline, and your plans are the melody that sometimes forgets the chorus and then remembers it with a heroic howl.

- Positioning is everything: Don’t run a one-hero show against a horde. Maintain lines of sight, block chokepoints, and use doorways to funnel zombies into predictable patterns. The map is a boss with a generous tilt toward chaos; your job is to keep the tilt in your favor.
- Prioritize threats: Some zombies are more dangerous than others. Rushes of tougher walkers require strategic targeting to prevent a disaster in the hallway of doom. A common tactic is to focus on a few essential targets first, then mop up stragglers to keep the tempo manageable.
- Weapon choice matters: Heavy weapons deliver big dice results but slow down your action economy. Ranged weapons offer safety but can leave you stranded if someone blunders into melee range. A balanced team uses a mix of weapon types so you’re not banking on a tiny sample size of dice.
- Use items strategically: Armor tokens and special items can swing the tide of a tense round. Don’t hoard everything for the final boss; use the equipment to hit critical turns where the board demands it most.
- Chain actions wisely: You have three actions per turn. You can combine movement with an attack, or move, search, and then attack in a perfect tempo if you plan your route carefully. The key is to avoid knee-jerk moves in the heat of the moment.

## Map Awareness: Reading the Board Like a Dungeon Master with a Snazzy Teacup
The board is more than a backdrop; it’s a dynamic character that can either shield you or swallow you whole. Here are practical ways to read the map like a pro:

- Doors are your friends and your worst nightmare. Open doors to reveal loot and create line-of-sight opportunities; the wrong door can invite a fresh wave of zombies into your location. Plan your door openings around where you expect your team to be next turn.
- Loot locations drive movement. Loot isn’t merely a nice add-on; it’s often the difference between victory and a messy crawl back to the start. Map your loot routes so you can collect essential gear without forcing yourself into high-risk corridors.
- Objective distances matter. Some scenarios reward speed, others precision. Keep an eye on the scoring timeline and pace your actions accordingly. Sprint too aggressively and you might run out of survivors before the map reveals its final twist.

In addition to terrain tactics, a practical habit is to assess the board’s risk level every turn. If a single move would end your life in an epic, dramatic fashion, consider a safer path or a strategic retreat. The best counters to chaos are good planning and a little bit of dice luck.

## XP, Leveling Up, and Making Your Survivors Really Feel Important
Progression in Zombicide Black Plague is one of the most satisfying parts of the game. As your survivors gain experience points, they level up and gain upgraded abilities. This is where your crew stops feeling like random heroic minis and starts to resemble a well-oiled ranger-turned-warden squad.

Key ideas:
- Plan long arcs: Don’t chase loot every turn; map out how XP will be allocated to upgrades and how those upgrades unlock new abilities that transform future turns.
- Diversify upgrades: Special abilities often complement team roles. A good balance prevents everyone from chasing the same upgrade and turning into a one-trick pony.
- Token economy: Some boards incorporate XP tokens tied to objectives or specific zombie kills. Manage these tokens like a medieval banker—every piece counts toward a bigger goal.

## Scenario Playstyles: How to Approach Common Encounters (With More Flavor)
Scenarios in Zombicide Black Plague are a fusion of puzzle, brawl, and glorious chaos. Here are archetypal playstyles you’ll encounter and how to tackle them—and yes, there’s always room for some improvised chaos:

- The Rescue Run: You must grab survivors and escort them to safety. Plan a path with minimal dead ends, and use scouts to pre-scout direct routes for safe extraction. This is a test of both map knowledge and your ability to keep your squad from breaking formation under pressure.
- The Blockade: You’re pinned in a corridor with a horde pressing in. Use your strongest defender to hold the line, dump damage into the approaching wall, and retreat cautiously to a loot cache. The objective is to survive long enough to extract or seal a critical exit.
- The Boss Rush: The map morphs into a tougher encounter with boss-like zombies and elite threats. Accelerate upgrades, coordinate abilities, and keep at least one tank alive through the crowd-control gauntlet while the rest deliver surgical damage.
- The Sealing Endgame: You’re closing doors or sealing a map region. Prioritize finishing the objective while keeping a safe exit path for your team. Don’t get too hung up on loot; finishing the objective is the real win condition here.
- The Resource Crunch: A scenario that squeezes both your loot management and your timing. It rewards patient pacing and the occasional risky sprint when the clock is ticking. Adaptability wins this one.
- The Escort Ambush: An escort mission that requires careful spacing and robust positioning so you don’t lose your key survivor to a lucky zombie bite. Communicate, plan, and then execute with surgical precision.

## Advanced Tactics: Guardrails for the Serious Strategist (Plus a Dash of Swagger)
If you’ve got your crew humming along and you crave true mastery, consider these high-level tactics:
- Space control over raw DPS: Don’t chase a flashy single target if it leaves your squishy backline exposed. Create safe zones where your team can reset and re-strategize without inviting a knee-deep bite storm.
- Timing upgrades: Don’t upgrade every survivor at once. A staggered upgrade path ensures you don’t lose a key ability for an entire round due to a failed upgrade roll—and also avoids the dreaded “my teammate got a better upgrade while I rolled snake eyes” moment.
- Predict zombie behavior: The AI tends to follow reliable patterns. Use that knowledge to bait them into ideal positions where you can clear multiple threats in one turn rather than fighting piecemeal.
- Resource rotation: If you have limited loot, rotate who carries what. Don’t clamp down on one player’s loadout. The party is strongest when load distribution is balanced and adaptable.

## Common Mistakes, Dips, and How to Avoid Them (With a Smile)
Every rookie misstep in Zombicide Black Plague is a learning opportunity. Here are frequent missteps and how to sidestep them:
- Ignoring positioning: The map is a living entity; don’t chase loot without considering where zombies will appear next.
- Overcommitting to a single target: Focus is good, but over-commitment can leave your backline exposed and your healer overwhelmed.
- Hoarding loot: Smarter players use items when they’re most needed, not at the end of the round when nothing else seems to matter. Use items to swing turning points, not to collect dust on a shelf.
- Skipping exploration: The game rewards thorough exploration. If you skip loot or essential paths, you may condemn your team to a slower, bloodier fate.
