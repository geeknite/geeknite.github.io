## What’s in the box (the original deck plus the bonus cards)

The core Exploding Kittens deck contains a selection of card types that drive the action. You’ve got Exploding Kitten cards that end your run if they’re drawn, and Defuse cards that save you with witty fate-tampering. Then there are action cards that let you skip turns, attack opponents, peek at future cards, or force someone else to pick up a card. The bonus cards edition expands this with two kinds of additions:

- New action variants that tighten or twist the turn order, encouraging bolder plays and riskier bluffs.
- Extra Defuse-like options and countermeasures that let players survive longer than they usually deserve. These additions keep the core rhythm intact while injecting new opportunities to fail gracefully in spectacular fashion.

For the curious, a quick mental map of typical card families in a standard round:
- Exploding Kitten: the bomb you hope to never draw
- Defuse: items that save you from sudden doom
- Nope: counter-spells that can stop an action cold
- Attack/Skip/See the Future: manipulate the turn order, protect your options, and plan or misplan spectacularly
- Bonus cards (in this edition): add-ons that modify how those core cards behave, adding new combos and surprise endings

If you like lists, here’s a quick play trick: the more Defuse cards you hold, the more you can push your luck. The bonus cards sometimes reward risky play with additional points of pressure, but the risk is real: your friends suddenly become highly accurate at guessing your next move, which makes the cat-like chaos all the more delicious.

## How the game actually works (a quick-start guide)

Setup is simple and mercifully short, because nothing kills a party vibe faster than a long tutorial while a timer mocks you from across the room. In the original game with bonus cards, you’ll shuffle the Exploding Kitten piles with the Defuse deck, deal a handful of cards to each player, and set the rest aside for world-dominating shuffles later. Then you take turns playing cards or drawing from the deck until someone draws an Exploding Kitten, at which point the Defuse tries to save them if they have one. If they don’t, the bomb goes off and the game ends for them in a gloriously melodramatic fashion.

The bonus cards tweak this rhythm without replacing the core rules. They might add a card that forces you to draw two cards if you try to skip, or give you options to shuffle the deck in the middle of a round. It’s not just extra text on a card; it’s an invitation to rewrite the moment you were about to choke on your own bravado.

Turn flow in a typical round looks like this:
- Play an action card (optional) to alter the course of events
- End your turn by drawing one card from the deck
- If you drew an Exploding Kitten and don’t have a Defuse, you’re out (or you go out in dramatic fashion depending on your group’s vibe)
- The next player continues, and the chaos escalates

In the bonus-cards edition, you’ll sometimes see new combos like a See the Future that reveals a future stack of three cards and a Bonus Defuse that whispers, If used correctly, you can cheat fate a little longer. It’s not cheating exactly; it’s negotiating with the cosmic feline of probability.

A note on group size: Exploding Kittens scales wonderfully from 2 players to larger gatherings. The more people involved, the louder the table gets, and the more your party mood benefits from short rounds and rapid-fire decisions. The game can be over in 15 minutes or stretch into a memory you’ll tease for weeks—depending on how much you enjoy the theatrical despair of your friends failing their way to victory.

## Strategy tips and practical tips for your next session

- Calibrate your risk. The more Defuse cards you have, the longer you can gamble with Exploding Kitten draws. The bonus cards give you extra levers—use them to force luck to tilt toward your group’s strengths.
- Read the table. The biggest advantage of Exploding Kittens style play is social awareness. If someone is visibly tense, consider passing on the big wild-card moves and choose safer plays that keep you in the game without triggering a meltdown.
- Use Nope strategically. The timing of Nope can redefine a round. You don’t need to cancel every play; you only need to disrupt the moments when your odds look bleak enough that a single card could end your night.
- The See the Future card is your friend. Use it to peek, count, and plan. In the bonus edition, the extra info can reveal new opportunities that might lead to a glorious chain of outcomes—or a spectacular collapse if you misread the signals.
- Manage your attention span. A key aspect of Exploding Kittens is the joy of anticipation. Don’t let the table’s chaos erode your ability to notice subtle shifts in who’s about to pull an Exploding Kitten. A little attention goes a long way toward surviving another round.

## How it compares to similar party games

If you’re choosing between Exploding Kittens and a game like Cards Against Humanity, you’re not exactly comparing identical experiences. Cards Against Humanity leans into provocative humor and social edge; Exploding Kittens emphasizes suspense, bluffing, and a cat-themed risk economy. The bonus cards edition adds a pinch of unpredictability that can make it feel fresh even for players who think they’ve mastered the deck by heart.

In contrast with UNO, Exploding Kittens offers a modern aesthetic and a more chaotic social dynamic. UNO is about color matching and speed; Exploding Kittens is about timing, bluffs, and dramatic “oh no, you didn’t” moments that emerge when danger meets cuteness. Each game has its own place, but Exploding Kittens excels as a party game that encourages loud laughter and quick shifts in strategy.
