## The tasting journey

### Aroma and initial impression

Swirl, sniff, and prepare for a sensory ping. The 2017 edition greets the nose with a confident bouquet that hints at dark fruits—think black cherry and plum—seasoned with gentle vanilla and a whisper of cocoa. There is a subtle spice component that could be described as a chorus line of cinnamon and clove, dancing around the fruit without shouting. The aroma is not shy; it carries itself with the poise of a well-educated RPG character who remains polite while delivering a devastating crit.

As the glass warms, the aroma grows more complex. A hint of espresso emerges, not in a roasted punch but as a smooth coffee note that makes the overall bouquet feel grounded. There is a faint touch of tobacco, restrained and classy rather than smoky and loud. The aroma promises a wine with depth rather than a one‑note wonder, a good sign for what the palate will later reveal.

### Palate and flavor profile

On the palate, Vinhos Deluxe 2017 Edition offers a medium to full body with a surprisingly agile mouthfeel. The initial hit is fruity, but not syrupy; it carries a balance that suggests careful winemaking rather than a sugar rush masquerading as complexity. Tannins show up as an elegant support rather than a dominant force, providing enough structure to keep things honest without turning the wine into astringent cardboard. The acidity is present but not aggressive, keeping the flavors lively without causing your cheeks to pucker in alarm.

Flavor notes echo what the nose promised, but with additional layers. The black cherry returns with assurance, joined by blackberry and a dusting of cocoa that lingers like a soft aftertaste rather than a harsh finish. Subtle notes of vanilla and toasty oak appear midpalate, giving way to a finish that carries the memory of warm leather and toasted spice. It is a wine that reveals more with each sip rather than shouting all at once. If you are the type who savors a sense of storytelling in a glass, this bottle has chapters and footnotes worth reading aloud at the table.

### Mouthfeel and texture

The texture of the Vinhos Deluxe 2017 Edition is a key part of its charm. It feels velvety and integrated, with tannins that are polished rather than rustic. The body sits comfortably between medium and medium‑plus, providing enough density to feel substantial without heaviness. There is a gentle buoyancy to the palate that suggests the wine will age gracefully, yet it already drinks well on opening. If you like a wine that unfolds slowly, this one rewards time in the glass and patience at the table.

### Food pairings and service suggestions

Pairing is where the Vinhos Deluxe truly flexes its versatility. The wine can carry the weight of red meat with ease, yet it remains lively enough to accompany poultry and vegetarian dishes that rely on umami and roasting. For a dinner party test, try it with roasted lamb or a hearty mushroom risotto. The wine does not overwhelm the dish; instead, it elevates the dish by offering a complementary flavor chorus. Aged cheeses, particularly a sharp cheddar or a firm gouda, make excellent partners, allowing the wine to shine through without becoming overshadowed by dairy fat.

If you want a more experimental pairing, consider a dish with chocolate in it. Not a chocolate dessert, but a savory chocolate glaze on a meat or mushroom dish. The chocolate note can mirror the cocoa hints in the wine, creating a playful, nearly trojan- horse effect in the mouth. It is a risky move for a dinner party, but one that can yield a memorable dining experience for the brave and the curious.

The 2017 edition is positioned as a product of careful vineyard selection and thoughtful aging. The grape variety is described in the press notes as a traditional blend from a sun-kissed region, with careful viticulture to preserve acidity and fruit integrity. ## Practical tasting notes at home

- Serving temperature: Best at 60–62 F (15–17 C). If your room is warmer, a few minutes of chill time in the fridge before serving can help retain the wine’s crisp edge on the palate. If it is cold, a brief decant or a few minutes of air in the glass will wake up the aromas.
- Glassware: Use a standard Bordeaux‑style glass to allow the wine to breathe and to concentrate the aroma toward the nose. The larger bowl helps fat molecules spread silkily across the palate for a more generous mouthfeel.
- Pour size: A modest pour of about 5 ounces is enough to reveal the wine's complexity without overwhelming the senses. You can still go for seconds, thirds, and a fourth if the company insists, just be mindful of the growing risk of repeat jokes about the cork being the real hero here.
- Decanting: A gentle decant for 20 to 30 minutes can significantly improve the nose and midpalate, especially if the bottle has been stored for a while or if you opened it after a long day of meetings and emails that never end.
