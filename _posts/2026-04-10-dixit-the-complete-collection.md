## How Dixit Works (The Quick Guide)

### Clue Creation: The Liar, the Poet, and You

Every round, one player becomes the storyteller. They look at one of their cards and make up a clue that evokes the image without giving away too much. The clue can be a phrase, a memory, a line of poetry, or a random association that somehow makes sense to them. The rest of the players select a card from their hand that best matches that clue and submit them face-down. The cards are shuffled and laid out for everyone to guess which card belongs to the storyteller. If your card is guessed, you get points; if you correctly guess the storyteller’s card, the storyteller earns points; if no one guesses the storyteller’s card, the storyteller gets punished. The nuance here is that the clue must be clever enough to guide but not so obvious that everyone picks the storyteller’s card immediately.

### Guessing and Scoring: The Beautifully Annoying Part

After all the cards are revealed, players vote on which card they think the storyteller played. The scoring system rewards clever misdirection: a good clue earns the storyteller enough points to smirk at, while players who picked the wrong card have to choke back laughter at their own poor deduction. The dance between guesswork and storytelling is the core engine of Dixit; it thrives when people lean into the story and acknowledge that art invites more questions than answers. The Complete Collection, by expanding the pool of images, amplifies this tension: you’ll see more surprising tangents, more inside jokes, and more moments where someone says, That purple-hatted gargoyle reminds me of my sophomore year in art school, which is exactly the kind of ridiculous specificity that makes Dixit sing.

## Strategies and Tips for Dixit: The Complete Collection

### Crafting Clues That Hit the Sweet Spot

A successful clue is often a blend of subtlety and context. You want something that your friends can plausibly connect to the image without making the guess obvious from the first glance. A strong clue can be personal, tapping into a memory only a few players share, or it can be broadly thematic, triggering a common cultural reference that many players recognize but only loosely tie to the card. The trick is balance: too vague and no one guesses; too explicit and the round becomes a foregone conclusion. In the Complete Collection, you have more leeway to experiment with tone and style—humor, wordplay, nostalgia, or even a tiny amount of misdirection that makes players question their own memory of the clue.
