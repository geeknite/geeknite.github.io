## Setup tips and best practices
- Always measure your space before pulling the trigger. A 42U cabinet is not a bookshelf; it has width, depth, and a spine that needs to breathe.
- Decide on wheels vs feet beforehand. Wheels are great for repositioning during layout experiments, but consider locking wheels in higher-traffic rooms to prevent accidental rollaways.
- Plan for ventilation. If you plan to densely pack the rack with hot equipment, consider additional fans or vented panels on the sides or top, depending on your airflow strategy.
- Leave some slack in your cabling. A little extra length makes maintenance a lot easier and reduces the risk of accidentally yanking a connector during a high-pressure maintenance moment.
- Keep track of your keys. A pair of keys in a lab is like a pair of wrenches in a toolbox: you hope you never need them, but when you do, you want them immediately.
