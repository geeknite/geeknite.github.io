### Maintenance and Cleaning: Keeping It Fresh

No review is complete without a maintenance secchi—something to keep your space sane when life happens: snacks, spills, pet hair, and the occasional gaming burrito crisis. The La Paz Natural Nero Rug is designed for easy maintenance. A quick pass with a vacuum that has a standard beater bar does the job. For spills, blotting is recommended rather than aggressive rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the fibers and create a mysterious new colorway called “Stain Gray.” Mild cleaning solutions can be used on minor spots, but test a small area first; modern rugs can be surprisingly sensitive to harsher cleaners.

If you’re a pet owner, you’ll be relieved to hear that the rug doesn’t attract fur like a magnet and doesn’t retain odors beyond the usual. A simple routine involving a brush-down and a quick vacuum tends to keep fur to a minimum. For households with kids, there’s a built-in resilience to everyday chaos—crumbs, marker doodles, and accidental “science experiments” at the coffee table won’t instantly ruin your aesthetic if you approach them with humor and a damp cloth.

### Maintenance Recap: Keeping Your Rug Like New, Forever-ish

In short, the La Paz Natural Nero Rug is easy to live with. The key habits to keep it in top condition are straightforward:

- Vacuum regularly to avoid dirt grinding the nap into a dull finish. A weekly pass keeps crumbs and dust from turning into a textured sculpture on the floor.
- Rotate every few months to distribute wear evenly. You don’t want a hero stripe forming under your chair that shouts, “I have a life, just not a social one.”
- Blot spills promptly with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing; you’ll spread the stain and possibly darken the spot.
- For stubborn marks, test a mild cleaner in a discreet corner before committing to a larger area. If unsure, consult a rug professional or a trusted cleaning guide.
