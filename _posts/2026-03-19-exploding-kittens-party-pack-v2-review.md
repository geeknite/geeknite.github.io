## How to play: quick-start guide for chaos enthusiasts
### Setup and turn structure
- Shuffle the core deck; deal a hand to each player. Typical play assumes five to eight players, a sweet spot that delivers maximum chaos with a pinch of strategy for those who like to pretend they know what they’re doing.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards back into the deck, a ritual you’ll perform with the gravity of launching a satellite. Shuffle again and keep a few Defuse cards in your pocket—the kind of pocket where you forgot you had snacks and now you remember them when you draw a potentially fiery fate card.
- The turn order is simple: draw a card at the end of your turn, and if you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out. If you have a Defuse card, you survive, possibly by the sheer force of irony. The rest is history, as in, someone will quote a cat pun and you’ll all forget what you were arguing about five minutes earlier.

### Card interactions: what actually happens during play
The core mechanic is a tug-of-war between luck and manipulation. You can play attack, skip, or defense cards to alter what your fellow players are facing. The newly added card types in V2 lean into the chaos factory: some allow you to peek at the upcoming cards, some force other players to draw, and a few create moments where you wish you had a time machine to undo your last choice.

- Cat cards: These are the joke engines of the game. The art is silly, the text is punny, and the effect ranges from mild nuisance to catastrophic plot twist that makes everyone laugh or groan in equal measure.
- Defuse cards: The rescue parachute for when fate decides you deserve a dramatic, explosive end. Use wisely, because they are finite and priceless in the moment you need them most.
- Action cards: Attack, See the Future, Shuffle—these are your toolbox for navigating the inevitable chaos. See the Future is particularly satisfying in a room where everyone is trying to predict what card you’ll draw, and you’re too busy grinning at the mischief you’re about to unleash.

- You deal seven cards to each player (typical start). The room is already a bit loud because people are excited to see who gets to pull the next card.
- On your turn, you decide whether to draw or play a reaction card to shift the risk to someone else. You may see the future and sigh in relief when your prediction is correct, or you may misread the future and accidentally unleash a cascade of silly consequences.
- The moment the Exploding Kitten card hits the table and you don’t have a Defuse, the drama erupts: players cheer, someone shouts a pun that will haunt you later, and the winner is the one who can keep a straight face while the room erupts in chaos.
