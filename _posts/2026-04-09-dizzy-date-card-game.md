## How to play (quick-start guide)

1) Set up the play area and choose your mode. Dizzy Date typically supports several variants such as 2-player, co-op team play, and party mode for larger groups.
2) Shuffle the deck and place it face-down. Decide who goes first.
3) The active player draws a card and reads it aloud. The prompt could be a question, a challenge, or a lightning-round prediction.
4) Depending on the card, everyone else might vote, perform, or give a quick-fire answer. The timer ticks down, and the round ends when the time runs out or someone completes the challenge.
5) Points or social capital are awarded according to the card’s instructions. The joy is less about scoring and more about the stories you collect along the way.
6) The round concludes when a player reaches a pre-agreed point total or when you decide the chaos has peaked sufficiently for the night.

## The pacing: it’s all about energy management

Dizzy Date thrives on dynamic energy. The game rules encourage quick rounds, rapid-fire answers, and occasional detours for a ridiculous skit. That pacing is infectious; once a few players break into laughter, others join the chorus, and suddenly you’re in a loop of rapid-fire quips that can last surprisingly long.

That said, the pace can dip if you have too many players who need time to think through sensitive prompts. The timer helps, but you might want to implement a quick “rapid-fire” option for the next few cards in larger groups. This keeps momentum without forcing anyone to overexplain their life story during a 30-second sprint.

## A few tips and variations to try (H3)

### For couples
- Use the prompts as a monthly “get to know you better” ritual. Pick three cards that ask about future plans or favorite moments, and discuss them over a nice dinner.
- Add a boundary layer: before the game, each partner writes down one thing they’re uncomfortable sharing; you can opt to skip those prompts or rephrase them to respect boundaries.

### For party settings
- Create teams with mixed familiarity: invite a friend who’s new to your circle to observe before joining in. It’s a great way to ease into the banter.
- Use “bonus challenges” cards that reward teams for silly performances or quick played-out improv scenes.

### Quick variations
- Time warp round: ignore the official timer for one round and let everyone take their sweet time. The chaos is delicious.
- Silent mode: respond with a gesture or mime; the rest of the group has to guess the answer. This adds a level of silly physical comedy that can become the night’s highlight.
