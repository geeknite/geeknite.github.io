## How It Plays: Platinum Versus Classic UNO
If you know UNO, you know the basics: match color or number, play a card, draw a card if you can’t, last card wins. Platinum Edition acknowledges this but sprinkles in a few updates that change how you approach late-game tension.

### Quick Rules Recap for Newbies
- The deck contains standard UNO cards plus Wild and Wild Draw Four variants.
- Each player starts with seven cards. Play proceeds clockwise, with the goal of shedding all your cards.
- When you have one card left, you must shout Uno. If you forget, a challenger can call it out and you may have to draw cards as a penalty depending on house rules.
- You may only play a card that matches color or number. If you can’t, draw one card. If that card can be played, you may immediately place it, subject to rules.

The Platinum edition introduces a refined set of Cat-and-Mouse rules that emphasize speed and players’ ability to read the room: who’s keeping a poker face, who’s counting cards, who’s giving away strategic tells with a glassy-eyed stare. In practice, it makes late-game decisions more critical and adds a small amount of tension that is perfect for party nights or family gatherings where someone always tries to cheat by using a “two-handed draw” technique.

### Platinum Variant: What to Expect At Your Table
