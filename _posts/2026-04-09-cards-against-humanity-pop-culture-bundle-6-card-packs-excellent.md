### How to run a smooth six-pack night
- Pre-game setup: Display a quick baseline rule card so first-time players know what to expect. A few minutes of explanation can prevent rounds from spiraling into a long argument about what counts as a good punchline.
- Rotating judges: If you have a large group, rotate the judge every few rounds to keep the energy level high and give different players a chance to influence the game’s tone.
- Pack pacing: Decide whether you want to shuffle the six packs together or run them in a curated order. If you expect more familiar players, you might mix with the familiar core deck to maintain a baseline rhythm.
- Moderation tips: Have a light safety measure in place. If a prompt is getting too rough for some players, allow a gentle push to replace it with a less intense prompt. The goal is laughter, not discomfort that lingers long after the night ends.
- Sound and lighting: A little mood lighting can enhance comedic timing, but ensure that everyone can read the prompts clearly. A quick call to dim lights only if necessary can help avoid misreads that derail a good joke.
