## Setup and Playthrough
We'll walk you through a typical session. The deck is shuffled; each player receives ten cards; the rest sits as a draw pile. The player to the left of the dealer goes first. On your turn you draw a card, then optionally lay down a phase or add to existing ones if you can, then discard one card. The twist is that you must complete the phase you are aiming for before you can move on to the next one; you can't skip ahead. That constraint is what gives Phase 10 its puzzle-like flavor.

### Strategy and Tactics
- Early planning: You will want to pick a potential phase that uses cards you see in a bunch of players' discard piles, to reduce risk.
- Card counting: It's less math and more memory; watch what your opponents are collecting. If you notice a player discarding a lot of cards that can help someone else, you can adjust your expectations accordingly.
- When to commit: You might snag a phase early; alternatively you might wait in hope of a better combination, since delaying can produce a bigger payoff in a later hand if you have the right cards.
- Wilds: Use wild cards to save your day when you are one card away from a phase. In some variants the wild can substitute for any card; but you must decide whether to use it for a quick payoff or save it for a bigger opportunity.
- Discard strategy: When you hold on to a key card that blocks others from finishing a phase, you risk stalling yourself; dropping it early might speed up your own progress.
