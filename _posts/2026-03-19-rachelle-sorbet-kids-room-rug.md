## Care and Maintenance

No home decor item is truly maintenance-free, and rugs are no exception. The Sorbet rug is designed with easy care in mind, but you still need to treat it like a living part of your home, not a throw pillow that’s been repurposed as a coffee-table excuse. Here are practical care guidelines for everyday life with kids:

- Vacuum regularly: A weekly vacuum helps to remove dust, crumbs, and the small bits of glitter that seem to have a gravity all their own. Set the vacuum to a gentle setting and avoid aggressive beater bars that can loosen fibers over time.
- Spot clean: For spills, blot with a clean cloth and use a mild detergent diluted in water. Work from the outside of the stain toward the center to avoid spreading. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that might fade color or degrade fibers.
- Deep clean: If the manufacturer’s care instructions allow, you can perform a deeper clean periodically. Use a gentle fabric cleaner compatible with the fiber type and ensure it dries fully before the room returns to use. Always air-dry thoroughly to prevent mold or mildew.
- Odor control: If your kid sleeps with their rug (which some do, and we salute the dreamers), consider airing it out in a shaded area or using a scent-free deodorizer compatible with fabrics.

Consistency is key. Your rug won’t remain pristine forever, but with a good routine, it will remain a bright, inviting stage for play and reading. The more regularly you clean, the longer it will keep that fresh, vibrant look you bought it for. And if you forget for a week, don’t panic—just remember: your child’s imagination doesn’t care about a few spilled sips; it will still use the rug to stage new adventures.

## Replacement and Longevity: When to Upgrade

Let’s face it: kids grow, rooms evolve, and rugs eventually become memories of spilled juice and sticky fingers. The Sorbet rug is designed to be a durable piece that can ride out a few seasons, but there are signals you should watch for:

- Fraying edges: After a few years of daily tumbling, your rug may begin to fray at the edges. If that happens, it’s time to assess the upgrade or re-edge the piece if your budget allows.
- Flat spots: If the rug loses its plushness in high-traffic areas, you may want to consider a replacement. A too-flat rug can create a cold floor feel and hamper comfort during reading time.
- Fading: If colors begin to fade unevenly due to sunlight exposure, you may want to rotate the rug’s position or consider a new color scheme to keep the room feeling fresh.

Don’t panic if you find yourself eyeing a different rug after a few years. A well-chosen rug can act like the stage crew: not flashy, but crucial for the show. If you decide to upgrade, you can reuse the Sorbet rug in another space, donate to a local charity, or repurpose into a play mat under a craft table—your options are as limitless as a child’s imagination.
