## Size Options: Custom Size vs 8x10

### Custom size: freedom with measured precision
Custom sizing is where Mesa Sterling truly leans into the modern home theater of decor. If your space is unusual—round, octagonal, or a trapezoid that looks suspiciously like an abstract art piece—the custom option is your friend. Here are practical tips for getting it right:

- Measure three times, cut once (metaphorically speaking). Start with the length and width of the floor area you want to cover, then consider door swing and furniture placement. You’ll want enough rug to anchor the seating area without forcing you to moonwalk around the coffee table.
- Consider the rug’s pile height in relation to door thresholds and treadmill feet. If you have a high-traffic entry area, a slightly shorter pile may experience less friction with shoes and boots.
- Order lead-time. Custom sizes may require more time in production and shipping, so plan accordingly if you’re staging a room reveal.

Pros: Perfect fit, maximum use of space, less visual wasted area, and a cleaner aesthetic where everything lines up like a well-edited storyboard.
Cons: Higher price, longer wait, and a slightly higher chance of measurement hiccups if you rush the pre-check.

### The classic 8x10: the most requested size for living rooms
The 8x10 rug remains the default for many interiors. It strikes a balance between coverage and furniture layout flexibility. In spaces typical to Geeknite readers—open-plan rooms, apartment living rooms, and home offices—the 8x10 provides anchor points for your sofa, coffee table, and media setup. ### Practical guide to choosing between custom size and 8x10
- Room size: If your room is long and narrow, a custom size might better emphasize the geometry. If it’s a broad rectangle, 8x10 usually works well.
- Furniture layout: If your seating area sits across an unusually shaped layout, custom sizing can keep the rug edges aligned with furniture lines to avoid awkward gaps.
- Traffic and cleaning: Custom sizes can be tailored for higher-traffic zones; for lighter use, 8x10 is typically sufficient.
- Budget and timing: Custom orders are typically more expensive and take longer; consider whether the payoff in aesthetics and layout is worth the extra wait.

## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Real-World Durability

### Everyday cleaning
Spills happen. Coffee, wine, tomato sauce, and barbecue sauce can leave their mark. The synthetic fibers used in Mesa Sterling resist staining better than many natural fibers and stand up to regular cleaning routines. For most minor spills, a quick blot with a clean cloth coupled with a mild cleaning solution and warm water will do the job. Avoid harsh bleach solutions unless you enjoy dye-matching experiments with your floor. Vacuuming on a regular basis is recommended to prevent dust accumulation and to maintain the rug’s shape.

### Deeper cleans
For more stubborn messes, a light shampoo or a professional cleaning is advised. Always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines; if you can access a cleaning code on the label, that will be your best friend. Let the rug dry fully before placing it back under traffic; moisture can lead to mold or a musty odor if left unattended. In a humid climate, you might leverage a dehumidifier in the room while the rug dries to speed up the process.

### Durability in households with pets and kids
Pets and kids tend to test the resilience of floor coverings. The Mesa Sterling rug holds up well to routine fur, dirt, and minor scratch marks, thanks to the fiber construction and the backing that minimizes shifting. If a stray toy climbs onto the rug and gets stuck in the fibers, you can usually extract a few tangles with your fingers or a simple comb. For more intense wear, rotating the rug every few months helps even out the wear patterns and preserve color fidelity.

## Maintenance Schedule and Pro Tips

- Vacuum weekly, with a gentle setting. Avoid high-power vacuums that pull fibers. Use a nozzle attachment to reach tight spaces near furniture.
- Rotate your rug every 3 to 6 months to even out wear and color fading due to sun exposure.
- Rotate furniture occasionally to prevent permanent indentations if you have a cable-laden setup under a coffee table.
- For spills, blot—don’t rub. Rubbing pushes the stain deeper and may cause fluffing or color changes.
- For deeper cleaning, consider a professional rug cleaning service that specializes in synthetic fibers or rent a rug cleaning machine with a low-moisture setting.
