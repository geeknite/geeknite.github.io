## Play patterns and matchup notes

### Against aggro
Against aggressive decks, your goal is to stabilize quickly and use Alpine Meadow to ensure you have the white mana to cast early removal and blockers. Early pressure and efficient removal can blunt an aggro plan before it becomes too explosive. Alpine Meadow helps you keep up the tempo, avoiding the frustration of being mana-flooded on the wrong color.

### Against control
In control-heavy matchups, you want to maintain a steady flow of threats and worry less about mana color issues. Alpine Meadow supports this by letting you execute your early plays on turn 2 or 3, opening lines for card draw or more resilient threats. Sideboard options often include additional removal or disruption to push through the final points of damage once you’ve built a favorable board position.

### Against midrange
Midrange decks tend to be bested by efficient, low-cost threats and timely removal. Alpine Meadow keeps your white mana stable while you leverage your 2- and 3-drops to outpace them. If your meta favors bigger creatures or value engines, your fixed mana helps you deploy your answers and pressure at the same time.
