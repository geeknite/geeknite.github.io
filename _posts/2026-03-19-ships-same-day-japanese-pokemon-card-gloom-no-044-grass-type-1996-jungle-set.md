Grading trends for 1996 prints differ a little from modern slabs. For MP Gloom, most collectors look for a pattern of wear that is consistent with age but not excessive. ### Authenticating MP no 044 Gloom

Authenticating requires a patient eye. The absence of a holo is part of its everyday charm, a reminder that not all legends are born from foil.

## A Practical Buying Guide for the MP Jungle Gloom

### Verifying MP in the wild

When shopping in markets or online, use a simple checklist for MP status. Look for light edge wear, consistent corner conditions, minimal surface scratches, and an overall glossy yet not peeling surface. Compare multiple images, if possible, and ask for a short video showing the card under natural light, not a studio glare. If you can, request a short clip of the back to confirm readability of the text and the absence of significant whitening along the edges. 

### Packaging and shipping considerations

For a card that survived the last two decades, packaging matters a lot. Ask for sleeves, top loaders, and a rigid mailer to preserve the card during transit. In the world of vintage Pokemon cards, the packaging is more than a container; it is a shield against the harsh realities of postal transit. A well-packed card is a sign that the seller respects the item and the buyer. If you are buying multiple items, ask for combined shipping to reduce risk and costs. 

## Display and Care: Making Your Gloom Stand Out

### Sleeves and binder strategies

A good display strategy starts with the sleeve. Use premium penny sleeves for the card front, then a semi-rigid protective sleeve, and finally a top loader for structural integrity. If you want to really flex your display game, consider a custom binder page with a small caption about the set and No 044. A little context goes a long way for onlookers who might not immediately identify Gloom as the star of this print. 
### Preservation tips

Avoid direct sunlight, high humidity, and extreme temperature changes. The Jungle Set prints from this era are resilient but not invincible. Using a moisture absorbers in your display room is a small step that pays off in the long run. If you must handle the card, do so with clean hands and avoid fingerprints on glossy surfaces. A micro cloth can help remove tiny scuffs without removing the gloss.
