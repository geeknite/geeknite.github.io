### Painting and Assembly Tips
If you are new to Zombicide Black Plague minis, Orc Zombie 3 is a forgiving starter. The details are crisp but not microscopic, which means you will have a fun but not frustrating painting session. Here are some practical tips:

- Cleaning: A light wash after removing mold lines goes a long way. The head area plus the armor plates collect resin bits as a natural aftertaste of the mold process. A careful shave with a sharp hobby knife should do you justice without removing essential details.
- Base color: Start with a dark green base as a primary tone. Layer with lighter greens toward raised areas to simulate rotted muscle and highlight the facial ridges. The decayed texture can be emphasized with a thin brown or gray glaze to provide depth.
- Flesh and rot: Use a mix of rotting greens, olive, and a bit of dirty yellow for exposed flesh. The undead look benefits from subtle contrast across the face and exposed limbs. Do not overdo the rot; a little goes a long way.
- Armor: For the armor plates, a dull metal tone plus rust washes can give a realistic worn steel look. If you like a greener armor vibe for undeads, a descontaminated copper wash can be fun for variety.
- Hair and features: A touch of bone color around teeth or exposed bone helps exaggerate undead features. It also assists in making the Orc Zombie 3 readable on the table during frantic moments.

Assembly wise, there are a couple of small joints, mostly around the arms and shoulder region. The model is designed to fit together with a little glue, but you can optionally pin heavier parts if you intend to use it in a diorama or for prolonged table top use. The balance is generally good, so you should not need heavy reworking to get a stable pose.

Jekyll or Hyde aside, the Orc Zombie 3 welcomes conversions with open arms. Swap the pose, adjust the left arm to hold a different zombie-feeding grip, or combine with other Zombicide undead to create a small horde that would make even the bravest fighter swallow a healing potion with grace.
