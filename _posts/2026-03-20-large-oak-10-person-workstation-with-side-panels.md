Oak is a hardy wood when properly sealed and maintained. Regular dusting and a periodic wipe-down with a damp microfiber cloth will keep the desk looking sharp. For deeper cleaning, avoid harsh chemicals that can break down the finish. If you accidentally spill coffee on the surface, promptly blot (do not rub) and consider a quick re-seal of that area if you notice any dulling of luster. The beauty of oak is in its resilience; with reasonable care this desk can live through many sprints, onboarding ramps, and the occasional office mascot parade without losing its soul.

## Pitfalls and Practical Tips

- Measure the space carefully. Nine to ten feet is substantial, and you want to ensure there is enough room for chair movement, foot traffic, and office trip hazards. Do a test run by laying out tape in the intended footprint before you commit to moving heavy furniture around.
- Plan for cable management from day one. The desk does a solid job of channeling wires, but you still need to map your power requirements, monitor connections, and audio equipment so you do not end up with the mother of all cable tangles.
- Consider your topology. A single long row feels imposing at first glance; you may prefer to break it into modules or add end caps for a friendlier silhouette. The panels can be used as visual anchors to delineate work zones without making the room feel like a maze.
- Ensure compatibility with your chairs. Some chairs pair better with large workstations than others; test the range of motion and leg clearance to avoid elbows colliding with panel edges during tight turns.
- Maintenance matters. For heavy daily use, you might want to apply a periodic wax or conditioning layer to preserve the surface and maintain the wood’s warm glow. It’s like giving your desk a spa day and it helps it feel confident in board meetings.
