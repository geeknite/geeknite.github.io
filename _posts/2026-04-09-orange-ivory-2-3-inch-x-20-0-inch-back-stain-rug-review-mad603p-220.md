## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Stain Resistance

This is the part where we separate the dreamers from the pragmatists. A rug that claims back-stain backing needs to deliver when life happens. The reality here—most days, it does.

- Daily care: Vacuum regularly to remove dust and crumbs. A modern rug of this weave holds up well against daily traffic; frequent vacuuming helps preserve the color and texture.
- Immediate spill response: Blot—do not rub—the spill with a clean cloth or paper towels. For a coffee spill or juice, dab gently; avoid pressing hard to prevent staining from migrating into the fiber. For darker spills like red wine, act quickly. The sooner you blot, the better the result.
- Stain treatment: If a stain sets in, you can use a mild detergent solution or a dedicated rug cleaner suitable for synthetic fibers. Test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. Avoid bleach or harsh solvents that could dull the orange or ivory sections.
- Deodorizing and freshening: A light sprinkle of baking soda, left for 15-20 minutes, followed by vacuuming can keep the rug smelling fresh. If you’re dealing with pets, a peroxide-based cleaner may help; always test first.
- Cleaning frequency: For heavy-traffic areas, plan a professional cleaning or deep clean every 12-18 months. For lighter use, you can rely on regular vacuuming and spot cleaning to maintain the rug’s life.
- Drying: After cleaning, make sure the rug dries completely to prevent mold or mildew. In humid climates, waiting longer and using a fan can help.

Maintenance caveats: The color balance will hold up better if you keep the rug out of direct sun for extended periods. If your space has strong natural light, consider UV-protective window film or rotating the rug occasionally to minimize sun exposure.

Care tips:

- Rotate the rug every few months to even wear.
- Use a rug pad to reduce movement and wear.
- Avoid machine washing unless the label explicitly states it is safe for machine cleaning.
