What is the Wulfsburg Dice Tower?

The Wulfsburg Dice Tower is a standalone accessory designed to funnel dice from the player’s hand into the table with a satisfying clack, minimizing spills, chasing embers of dice across the board, and the occasional existential crisis about rerolling for the nth time in a row. In practice, it’s a plastic tower with a funnel mouth, a couple of internal baffles to create a cascade, and a base that clamps (or sits) on the game table depending on your setup. In a game system as heavy on dice as Zombicide Black Plague, a good dice tower can shave seconds off turns and save you from aging wrists as you roll tens of dice per round.

The Wulfsburg version—named after the in-universe fortress that features in the Black Plague campaign—emphasizes a chunky, fortress-like aesthetic. It’s not subtle. It’s not delicate. It’s the sort of accessory that says, “Yes, your undead army deserves a throne… even if it’s a plastic one.” And that’s part of the charm.

Materials and Build Quality

The tower itself feels sturdier than a lot of “budget” gaming gear. It’s not so heavy you’ll accidentally bench-press the teammate who keeps complaining about your dice pool, but it’s substantial enough to survive a few table bumps and enthusiastic shakes when a noble zombie charges. The plastic finish is a bit glossy, which gives it a sense of sci-fi sturdiness rather than “war-torn dungeon,” but it still reads as a thematic piece that belongs on a Black Plague table. The edges are smooth, with no sharp plastic burrs that would threaten dice or fingers, which is nice when you’re flinging a handful of dice back into the tray like you’re conducting a dice storm orchestra.

The tower’s assembly, if necessary, is cosmetic minimalism. A couple of snaps, a twist or two, and you’re done. There’s nothing particularly complex here unless you’re someone who uses the tiny screws you saved from a 3D-printed fence post project as a ritual to bless your gaming night. The Wulfsburg model keeps that intimidation factor, while still being easy enough for a casual gamer to set up without consulting the instruction scrolls that come in some hobbyist kits.

Hardly Used Plastic: The Reality Check

The query that kickstarted this review is the “hardly used plastic” vibe. You’ll see this a lot in used-market listings: item described as “hardly used” with a price tag that makes it borderline suspiciously nice. If you’ve ever bought a used furniture piece that looks pristine but smells vaguely like the previous owner’s cat, you’ll know the feeling. In our experience, the Wulfsburg dice tower ships with minimal wear on the base and no obvious scuffs on the sides. The paint, where present, holds well; there’s no chipping or cracking around the mouth. The internal towers show no evidence of heavy use—no deep gashes from rogue dice or improvised catapults. In short, it’s the sort of used item that makes you suspiciously check the fine print for “mint condition” and “rarely used during a dragon-slaying expedition.”

We should note a practical consideration here: dice towers aren’t indestructible. The more you shake them, the more you test the seams. If you’re someone who treats your gaming apparatus like a sacred relic, you’ll appreciate the tower more for its presence than for its tolerance of your dice-rolling theatrics. If you’re a brutal optimist who loves rolling dice in a frenzy, you might want to check the underside for reinforcement and the top for any wobble. In our tested instance, nothing significant popped loose, and the tower stayed upright through multiple turns, which is a win in the world of tabletop chaos.

On-Table Performance: Does It Improve the Experience?

Clacking dice through a tower has a surprising effect on the rhythm of your turns. It creates a tactile, almost cinematic moment: you lift the dice, you hear the cascade, they land in a neat pile, the board responds like a chorus line. It’s not strictly game-improving, but it’s mood-improving in a soft, satisfying way. For new players, the tower offers a visual cue that dice are rolling and decisions are being made, which can help with pacing and focus. For veterans, it’s a comforting ritual that signals you’re back in the zone after a poor zombie roll.

In a game as heavy as Zombicide Black Plague, where the action is often a flurry of movement, the dice tower can help contain chaos, prevent runaway dice (both literally and figuratively), and keep players engaged rather than chasing mislaid dice under the couch. However, it’s important to manage expectations: the tower won’t automatically level up your tactical game or translate poor luck into better planning. It’s a tool for speed, comfort, and a little theater, not a magic wand that rearranges the zombie apocalypse.

Pricing, Availability, and the Used Market

New, this tower sits at a premium that reflects its popularity and the broader appeal of Zombicide accessories. In the used market, you’ll often find it listed as “hardly used” or “mint condition,” sometimes with a sticker price that makes your wallet sigh in relief. The trick is to verify that the base and mouthpiece aren’t separated or warped, and that the paint doesn’t show an obsessive battle against gravity in the form of micro scratches. If you’re shopping used, ask for photos of the interior and underside, and request verification that the dice well is clear of stray shards of plastic that could become miniature landmines for your dice collection. If the price is right and the condition checks out, you’re getting a fantastic piece that both looks cool on the shelf and actually functions on the table.

Maintenance and Longevity

Plastic wear is a reality with any gaming accessory. You won’t necessarily see a long-term test results infographic here, but you can take a few practical steps to keep your Wulfsburg tower in mint condition:

- Clean with a soft cloth after heavy rolling sessions to remove dust and stray resin from the dice well.
- Avoid leaving the tower near radiators or heat vents; heat can warp plastic and degrade the fit of the mouthpiece.
- If you’re transporting it, consider a soft bag or a dedicated tray to prevent cosmetic scuffs.

Compatibility and Setup Tips

While the Wulfsburg tower is designed to be used with Zombicide Black Plague, it’s not limited to one game. It can, with caution, be used with other dice-heavy tabletop games that feature similar dice pools. Here are a few practical tips for setup:

- Place the tower at a comfortable angle and height so your dice cascade lands on the board where you intend them to land.
- If you’re using custom terrain with elevated boards, test a trial run to ensure the dice don’t topple off the table during cascades.
- For players who prefer a more “hands-off” approach to dice control, set the tower on a non-slip mat to prevent skidding during roll sequences.
