## What’s inside the Ghost of Harrenhal (2nd Edition) Pack?
This Chapter Pack ships with a curated mix of new cards designed to tilt the action economy toward intrigue, manipulation, and a dash of risk-versus-reward bluffing. Here’s the gist of what to expect, broken down for your post-game analysis board and your weekend-read-by-fireplace session.

### Card themes and mechanics
- Intrigue-heavy control options: Cards that harvest advantage from your opponent’s hesitation.
- Shadowy events and kneecapping the opponent’s tempo: Timed effects, ambush plays, and cancelation tools.
- House-specific bonuses and cross-house combos that feel thematic yet surprisingly practical in actual play.
- A set of neutrals that offer flexible utility without forcing you into a cookie-cutter archetype.

If you’re already bookmarking card names and trying to remember which ones create synergy with your old deck, you’re not alone. Ghost of Harrenhal is designed for players who like to mix deception with raw card advantage, with a little bit of “you thought you had me cornered? Think again” energy.

### Notable cards (high-level snapshot)
- The Power in Shadows: A signature intrigue card that rewards patience and punishes overextension.
- The Raven’s Decree: A control-oriented card that flat-out hates overconfident setups.
- Harrenhal’s Echo: An event that amplifies any effect that targets your opponent’s character with a notable cost benefit.
- Gatekeeper of Harrenhal: A defensive option that also doubles as a tempo engine if you time it carefully.

We won’t spoil every name here (you deserve the mystery), but these are the kinds of tools that make you rethink your approach to early tempo and late-game inevitability. The design leans into a more “think two steps ahead” playstyle, and the art direction on the cards carries the same dark, looming vibe the chapter pack title promises.

### Jekyll and Hyde: When the pack shines
There are builds where Ghost of Harrenhal acts as the quiet engine enabling a stealthy finish, and there are other builds where it’s the loudest disruptor in the room, forcing your opponent to reevaluate every attempt at aggression. The pack plays well with both familiar and emerging archetypes, which is a nice change of pace from a straight “pump out power characters” approach.

### Visuals and art direction
Harrenhal’s aesthetic remains a moody, storm-laden canvas—perfect for the kind of narrative you want to tell when your opponent’s counter-plot fizzles out. The art team nails the atmosphere, and the card frames fit into the current edition’s visual language without feeling shoehorned. If you’re one of those players who collects for the vibe as much as the function, Ghost of Harrenhal is going to look great in your display shelf, and your group chat will probably have a side debate about whether the art captures the exact moment you realize you’ve miscalculated the turn order.

## Archetype breakdown and strategy guidance
This section is designed to help you translate the new cards into practical decks. Whether you’re a veteran builder or a casual jam-matcher, you’ll find something worth slotting into your next game night. We’ll break down potential directions by house and discuss how the Ghost of Harrenhal Chapter Pack interacts with generic strategy pillars like tempo, card advantage, and inevitability.

### House Stark: The cautious hunter
Stark players may gravitate toward a “patrol and punish” tempo where you build incremental advantage while denying your opponent the ability to execute a predictable plan. The pack adds tools that help Stark stretch two of their core strengths: efficient 2-for-1 exchanges and ring-fenced board control.
- Strengths: Predictable bounce and pursuit mechanics that punish misplays by your opponent.
- Weaknesses: If you overcommit to the chase, a single cross-cutting trick from the opponent can swing the game quickly.

Core idea: Use Harrenhal’s echo effects to force your opponent into suboptimal actions, then close the game with a decisive single-card swing. This is a realistic and satisfying approach for players who enjoy a long, chess-like game with occasional dramatic finish lines.

### House Lannister: The flavor of manipulation
Lannister has always been about snappy economic moves paired with the ability to bend outcomes via events. Ghost of Harrenhal supplies a handful of cards that amplify your deception and set you up to win the long game through careful resource management and effective cancelation plays.
- Strengths: High value compared to cost for many of the pack’s effects; the control suite allows you to lean into the “I’ll take what you don’t expect” vibe.
- Weaknesses: You’ll want to maintain a calm head and avoid telegraphing your endgame plan too early, or your opponent may start hedging around you.

Core idea: Use Harrenhal-labeled effects to pre-commit your opponent into a corner and then let the board state do the heavy lifting. The pack makes Lanni feel sly and sharp, like a clever barbs-tipped dagger wrapped in velvet.

### House Baratheon: The tempo dictator
Baratheon players may enjoy the pack for its tempo-centric options, enabling aggressive starts with the promise of a mid-to-late game lock-in. The pack’s timing tricks align well with Baratheon’s theme of swift, decisive plays that pressure your opponent to respond immediately.
- Strengths: Early pressure and reliable draw/swap utilities.
- Weaknesses: If your early plan is stuttered, you may lose the tempo race to more stable factions.

Core idea: Build a deck that punishes hesitation, then ride a mid-game surge into board dominance. The pack’s cards give you the toolkit to push hard in the first few turns while maintaining a backstop for late-game resilience.

### House Targaryen and neutrals: The flexible wildcards
Neutral and Targaryen-aligned combinations remain a staple for players who enjoy improvisation. Ghost of Harrenhal contributes cards that can slot into almost any deck, depending on your preferred balance of lyes, sneaky cancelations, and pure value engines.
- Strengths: Flexible, viable in multiple archetypes; offers DIY opportunities for creative deck-builds.
- Weaknesses: Requires a good sense of the board state to extract full value; it’s not for players who want a single, universal “one-don’t-need-to-think” plan.

Core idea: The pack is a toolbox; it rewards players who want to tinker and experiment with different character pools and event timing. If you love the “one more card, one more trick” ethos, this is your playground.

### A balanced take: synergy vs. raw power
Ghost of Harrenhal shines when you balance synergy with occasional raw power. You’ll get enough cross-housing synergy to justify a shared theme, but you won’t feel hamstrung if your meta is particularly heavy on cancelation or board wipe effects. The design encourages you to think in a layered way: set up a threat, threaten multiple lines of attack, and preserve the ability to respond as needed.

## How to build around Ghost of Harrenhal: practical deck-building tips
Here are a few guardrails to help you start your list. Think of these as the “starter knobs” you dial up or down depending on your local meta and table dynamics.

- Prioritize tempo and control cushions: Build around two to three reliable tempo engines that also offer suboptimal-but-effectively-cancelable plays to stall for time.
- Secure the backline: Ensure you’re not leaving your strongest defenders exposed to a single-blow concept. You want fallback cards that let you stabilize even when the opponent spikes their plan.
- Balance your house (if you’re not mono-house): Ghost of Harrenhal’s multi-house potential can be exploited if you pick two or three core interactions and stick to them rather than chasing every new tech card that appears.
- Include one or two “fixer” neutrals: A couple of flexible neutral cards can adapt to a surprising range of matchups and playstyles, making your deck more resilient to last-minute meta shifts.
- Play for the long game: Don’t overreact to the first two turns. The pack rewards patient planning and the occasional mid-turn reshuffle.

If you want a practical example list, here are two generic skeletons you can refine for your playgroup. Note that these are templates designed to show the arc of thought rather than exact power rankings:

- Skeleton A (Stark-Led, tempo control):
  - Core: 3x Harrenhal-related control cards, 2x tempo engines, 2x neutral supports
  - Agenda: Non-specific, emphasis on control and board parity
  - Traits: Stark core with two neutrals for flexibility
  - Win condition: Gradual attrition with a late-game push when opponent is stretched

- Skeleton B (Lannister-centric, deception and value):
  - Core: 2x Harrenhal synergy events, 3x cancelation tools, 2x draw engines
  - Traits: Lannister core with neutrals for flexibility
  - Win condition: Edge-case ultimatums where you leverage fear and misdirection to force bad plays

For precise card names and exact counts, you’ll want to assemble the exact pool from the pack and experiment at the table. Remember, deck-building in this game is as much about the narrative you want to tell as the numbers that prove the point on your mat.

## Gameplay flow: what to expect when you sit down
- Early game: You’re often balancing two competing concerns: building your board while denying your opponent the tempo advantage. Ghost of Harrenhal’s suite supports both, so you’ll want to use the early turns to identify which card pools are your immediate threats and which threats you can mitigate with a timely cancel or a forced discard.
- Mid game: The pack’s real power comes into focus here. You’ll be alternating between resource management and card advantage, with a few “gotcha” moments that pivot the momentum. A well-timed Harrenhal interaction can swing a game in a surprising way, especially if your opponent misreads your intent.
- Late game: If you’ve managed to weather the storm, this is where your engine cards shine. You’ll want to have at least one protective countermeasure left and a plan to pressure the last few points of dominance. Ghost of Harrenhal’s finishers aren’t always brute-force heavy; sometimes they’re a patient sequence that ends in a single devastating move.
