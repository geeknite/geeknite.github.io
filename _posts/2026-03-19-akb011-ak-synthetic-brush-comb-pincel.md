### Maintenance and Cleaning

Synthetic bristles are relatively easy to clean compared to natural hair. After use, rinse the brush head under lukewarm water and run a tiny drop of mild soap through the bristles. Gently work out any debris, then rinse again. Squeeze out the excess water and lay the brush flat to dry. The comb teeth can accumulate dust and hair; a quick rinse or wipe with a microfiber cloth should suffice. Avoid soaking the entire tool for long periods, as this can compromise the joint between the brush head and the handle.

A common-sense tip: keep the device away from high heat, especially if you’re exactly the kind of person who leaves gadgets on a sunny window sill to “charge by sunlight.” The synthetic materials hold up well, but prolonged heat can degrade plastic and reduce grip over time. If you’re a mass gadget collector, you can rotate your cleaning schedule to ensure you don’t overexpose any one part.
