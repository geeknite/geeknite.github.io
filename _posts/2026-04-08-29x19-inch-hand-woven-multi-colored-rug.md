## Care, Longevity, and Real-Life Maintenance
### Cleaning 101: How to Treat a Hand-Woven Jewel
All rugs require some maintenance, but hand-woven pieces tend to reward gentle care. Vacuum with a low-suction setting and avoid the beater bar to protect the fibers. If a spill occurs, blot—don’t rub—right away with a clean cloth to prevent staining. For deeper cleaning, consider professional rug cleaning or a targeted wool cleaning solution. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially if sunlight hits it in a predictable pattern. Rare, but possible, is a slight shedding in the first few weeks; that’s typical for wool-based weaves as the loom settles the fibers.

### Materials: Wool vs Cotton and How They Weather the World
Wool rugs have natural resilience and a luxurious feel underfoot, but they may require a tad more care than cotton. Cotton blends, while easier to wash, can flatten a bit over high-traffic areas. The rug you’re reading about is likely a wool or wool-blend piece that balances durability with a gentle hand. This means you’ll get that soft underfoot feel while still enjoying a colorful, long-lasting surface. If you’re a pet owner, the rug’s woven complexity and pile height provide enough texture to hide minor pet-salon scenarios while staying stylish.

### Practical Longevity: How Long Will It Last?
With proper care, a hand-woven rug in this size can last for years—maybe a decade or more in a busy home. The secret is twofold: protect the front half of the rug from direct sun and rotate it periodically to avoid uneven fading or flattening in high-traffic spots.
