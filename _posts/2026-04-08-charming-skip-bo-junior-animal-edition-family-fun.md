## How to set up and play: a quick ramp for grownups and little ones alike

### Setup (in under 60 seconds, pinky promise)
1) Shuffle the animal-number deck. 2) Each player draws a hand as dictated by the rules (usually a handful of cards that you’ll want to replace after each turn). 3) Create the stock piles for each player face-down in front of them. 4) Keep a small draw pile within reach to replenish your hand as you go. 5) Decide who goes first—the child with the loudest victory cheer usually wins the honor.

### The basic turn (simple, friendly, fast)
- On your turn, you draw cards to refill your hand to the prescribed limit.
- You can play cards from your hand onto any build pile in ascending order starting from 1 upwards, or use a wild/multi-use card if your edition includes them. The goal is to play as many cards as possible, then discard or pass by completing a stock-pile move.
- If you manage to play all the cards from your stock pile, you declare victory. If not, you pass the turn and the next player goes. The pace is brisk, but not merciless, like a friendly dragon who only occasionally breathes fire when you forget to say “please” and “thank you.”

The Junior edition is designed to minimize setup friction and keep turns short. That means you’ll spend more time laughing at the animals’ antics than shuffling or consulting a giant rulebook. The rules are explained in kid-friendly language, with a few parent-friendly clarifications you can skim as needed. It’s the kind of game you can teach in under five minutes and start playing within ten.

### The role of the wilds and the strategy toggles
In Skip-Bo Junior Animal Edition, wild or flexible cards (when applicable) allow players to place any card on an appropriate build pile, as long as the numbers align.
