### Collectibility: Care, Curation, and Condition
#### Grading and Preservation
As with any rare card, preservation matters. The condition of a Carddass card—centering, edge wear, surface gloss, and any potential scratches—drives value. A NM-M (Near Mint to Mint) copy will fetch a better market price and be more satisfying to display. Keep it in a protective sleeve and a rigid top loader to protect against bending and moisture. If you’re lucky enough to own a foil variant, consider adding a second card protector to prevent surface abrasions that can dull the reflective finish.

Storage suggestions:
- Use a binder with page protectors designed for trading cards, ideally with acid-free sleeves.
- Store away from direct sunlight to preserve color saturation; UV exposure can cause fading.
- Control humidity; Carddass stock benefits from a stable environment to avoid warping and edge wear.

Display tips for the No. 263 card:
- Place it in a light-catching display case where the gloss or foil (if applicable) can shine without requiring you to handle it constantly.
- If you frame it, consider UV-protective glass to maintain color fidelity over time.
