## What Fortune Row Is and Why It Exists

Fortune Row is marketed as a Narrative Toolkit, which means it is less about new rules and more about new ways of thinking about how stories unfold in a city where every NPC is wearing a myth in disguise. City of Mist already leans into duality: mundane reality vs divine or mythic undercurrents. Fortune Row doubles down on that by giving you: 

- A set of narrative templates you can adapt to any city seed
- A collection of scene prompts that trigger character revelations
- Tools to manage multiple myths without lunging into a spaghetti bowl of subplots
- GM tips for pacing, tension, and the dreaded moment when a session stalls because everyone forgot to bring the mask
- Guidelines for inclusivity and accessibility so your players feel seen rather than surveilled

If you have ever faced a campaign where the city feels like a museum exhibit (beautiful, but static), Fortune Row promises to inject life, whispers, and plot threads that feel both personal and cosmically important. In short: this toolkit is about making myths relevant to modern urban chaos, not about selling you a prepackaged myth soup.

For a quick anchor, think of Fortune Row as the set of drumsticks you use to improvise a Tonight Show band around your heroes. It gives you beats, cues, and a few wild riffs that you can adapt to the tempo your players set.

## How the Toolkit Is Organized

Fortune Row is structured as a modular set of components. You can treat it as a reference book, a tabletop toolkit, or a set of prompts to curate your own city narrative. The sections typically include:

- Narrative templates that seed scenes with clear stakes and masked identities
- A library of motives, myths, and masks you can mix and match
- Scene prompts organized by mood and intensity, from intimate character reveals to city-wide upheavals
- GM tips that focus on pacing, reveal timing, and how to manage player agency with coherence
- Guidelines for combining multiple mythic threads without creating a tangled mess
- Ready made micro-scenarios you can slot into a one shot or weave into a longer arc

The design philosophy is to give you a toolbox rather than a storyboard. That means you will do a lot of stitching at the table, which is exactly how City of Mist feels in practice: collaborative, improvisational, and sometimes surprisingly poetic when you let it breathe.


### A closer look at the core components

Fortune Row includes several core elements that you will likely reuse again and again:

- Masks and Myths Collection: A curated set of masks (identities) and myths (power sources) that can be recombined to generate unique character angles. The toolkit is careful to avoid overloading players with too many options at once, presenting the material in digestible bites.
- Scene Seeds: Short, punchy prompts designed to spark scenes quickly. Each seed includes a suggested mask, a mythic hook, and a potential complication that can escalate at the table.
- Plot Hooks: These are more ambitious than scene seeds and intended to drive longer arcs that tie back to city-wide secrets and relationships between factions.
- GM Aids: Quick reference cards, pacing tips, and guidance on handling reveals, foreshadowing, and the cadence of a session so it never feels like a boring confession booth.
- Multimyth Thread Management: A framework for juggling multiple myths at once. If your table has three players who each have a different mythic axis, Fortune Row gives you a way to weave those axes into a cohesive city-wide resonance without creating an unwieldy web.

The presentation favors modularity and clarity. Each component stands alone but can be combined with others to create a richer experience. It is the kind of toolkit that makes you feel clever for using it, even as you juggle a camera, a crowd of NPCs, and a player who wants a twist that will rewrite their own backstory in real time.


## What a Session with Fortune Row Feels Like

If you are familiar with City of Mist, you know the vibe: a neon noir night that hums with mythic energy and human drama. Fortune Row amplifies that vibe by giving you explicit shapes for scenes, the emotional gravity of reveals, and a structure that helps players lean into the masks they wear. A typical session flow with Fortune Row might look like this:

1) Opening Scene Seed: A short hook that places a mask at the center of a mystery. The seed includes potential misdirection and a moral dilemma.
2) Mask Reveal: A player character confronts a hidden truth about their myth. The GM uses a prompt to guide discussion and dramatic tension.
3) Mythic Interplay: Several myths in play intersect, creating consequences in the city that feel both intimate and cinematic.
4) Escalation: A plot hook from the toolkit triggers a larger city event, forcing players to react in unpredictable ways.
5) Resolution and Reflection: The group closes the loop with a clear consequence, a new fragment of the city myth, and a setup for the next session.

The toolkit gives you language and prompts to push players toward these beats without dictating every beat. You will still improvise, but now you have reliable anchors to return to when the night gets hazy. If you like your City of Mist sessions to feel like a well-rehearsed street performance rather than a lecture, Fortune Row hits that sweet spot with style.

Fortune Row emphasizes narrative techniques over crunchy additions. You will not find a pile of new dice mechanics or a river of new stats to memorize. Instead, you get:

- A ledger of prompts that encourage players to reveal their masks, their flaws, and their goals in a way that feels earned.
- Practical advice on running multi-myth campaigns: how to track connections between myths without losing track of the players’ personal arcs.

If your table loves the idea of masks and myths but hates getting lost in the weeds of abstract theory, Fortune Row translates the philosophy into actionable steps. City of Mist is built on masks and myths. Fortune Row treats them as living tools rather than static costumes. When a mask feels personal, it becomes a lever for dramatic tension rather than a costume party prop.

The toolkit includes prompts for how masks interact with the city and other masks. It also suggests relationship maps so you can visualize how factions, allies, and enemies interlock. The visual aids are helpful for keeping everyone oriented. If you want to bring in new masks, Fortune Row offers guidelines for integrating them without breaking the city’s internal logic. ## How to Use This Material in Your Sessions

- Start with a Seed: Pick one seed to kick off a session. Let the mask and myth drive the opening scene, then surface a conflict that forces a choice. The payoff should feel personal and reflective, not just a chase sequence.
- Layer Myths: As the city reveals more, introduce a second or third myth that intersects with the first. The interplay should create moral ambiguity and raise questions about loyalty, truth, and power.
- Move to the City: Don’t forget to reference the city as a character. Let districts, neighborhoods, and street corners become active players in the plot. Fortune Row encourages you to craft a sense of place that matters beyond the immediate scene.
- Close with Reflection: End sessions with a moment of catharsis and a clear consequence for the next session. Whether a window into the character’s soul or a new city-wide tension, the resolution should feel earned and resonant.
- Build a Narrative Arc: If you plan a longer arc, use Fortune Row to map the major beats, side quests, and reveal timing. Revisit seeds that were dormant to deliver a sense of continuity and growth.
