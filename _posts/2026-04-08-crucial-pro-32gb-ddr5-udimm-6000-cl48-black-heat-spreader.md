## How to Buy and Set Up
- Verify your motherboard supports DDR5 and that 6000 MT/s is within its expected speed range.
- Update the BIOS to the latest version from the motherboard manufacturer.
- Power down and install the two DIMMs in the correct slots (usually the first two DIMMs; consult your motherboard manual for optimal placement).
- Enter BIOS, enable XMP (DOCP on AMD boards) to run at 6000 MT/s CL48. If instability occurs, back off to 5800 MT/s CL46 and test stability.
- Boot into your OS and run memory diagnostics. Use your regular workloads to validate stability across typical tasks: gaming, editing, rendering, and multitasking.
- If you’re using a custom loop or an extremely silent build, consider additional airflow to ensure memory and VRMs stay cool during long sessions.

### Post-installation tips
- If your system boots but memory tests fail, reseat the modules and recheck slot clarity. Ensure the modules are fully seated in their DIMM sockets.
- If you see instability after BIOS updates, reset to default, re-enable XMP, and test again.
- Track temperatures under load. If memory or motherboard temps are high, consider adding case fans or re-evaluating your cooling plan.
