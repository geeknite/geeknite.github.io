## Maintenance, safety, and little hacks

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it pays dividends in longevity and safety. Here are practical tips to keep your machine reliable:

- Regularly inspect bolts and pins; tighten as needed. A loose pin is a one way ticket to a dramatic alarm on a quiet Saturday morning.
- Lubricate moving parts as recommended in the manual. A little oil goes a long way in preventing squeaks that sound suspiciously like a sci fi weapon in action.
- Clean the padding and frames after sweaty sessions. Sweat is corrosive in the long run, and we want your gear to resemble a friend who survives the apocalypse, not a risk of rusted metal in your home.
- Check the weight stack plates for rust or plate shifting. If a plate does not slide smoothly, it is time to inspect the selector rod. Don’t force it, or you might end up with a séance in your garage asking for a heavier weight.
- Replace worn grips and gloves as needed. Your knuckles deserve to shout loudly, not scream in pain on day two.

Safety first is not silly. Use proper footwear, a stable surface, and a clear workout zone. Your future self will thank you when you are still able to perform those circuits without a suspicious pop in your knee or back. If you are new to multi station machines, consider a session with a trainer or a knowledgeable friend who can guide you through correct form and safe progression.
