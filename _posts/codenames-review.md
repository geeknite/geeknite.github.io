## Introduction

## What is Codenames?

Codenames is a competitive word game designed for 2-8 players, although let's be honest, the more, the merrier! Players split into two teams, each with a spymaster and operatives. The spymasters know the secret identities of 25 words on the board, and their job is to give one-word clues that help their team guess the correct words while avoiding the words belonging to the other team and the dreaded assassin.

### Components

The game includes:
- **200 double-sided cards**: Each card has a word on one side and a colored grid on the other.
- **40 key cards**: These cards show the layout of the words and which team owns them.
- **Sand timer**: For those who need a little extra pressure.
- **Spy cards**: Representing agents, innocent bystanders, and the assassin.

## How to Play

### Setup

Setting up Codenames is as simple as arranging a family photoâ€”except with more intrigue and fewer awkward smiles. Place 25 randomly chosen word cards in a 5x5 grid. Select a key card that shows which words belong to each team. The spymasters sit on one side of the table, facing their team and the word grid.

### Turns and Actions

Each turn, the spymaster gives a one-word clue followed by a number that represents how many words on the board relate to that clue. For example, if the words are "apple," "banana," and "fruit," the spymaster might say "fruit:3." The operatives then discuss and decide which cards to reveal based on the clue.

But beware! If the operatives reveal a word belonging to the other team or, worse, the assassin, chaos ensues. The game continues until one team successfully identifies all their words or steps on the assassin.

## The Experience

### Cooperation and Deduction

One of the most exciting aspects of Codenames is the cooperation and deduction involved. As an operative, you're relying on your spymaster's clue to guide you while also trying to decipher hidden meanings and avoid traps set by the enemy team.

### Funny Mishaps

Codenames isn't just a game of strategy; it's also a riot of hilarious missteps. Whether it's a spymaster giving a clue that's way too vague or operatives confidently choosing the assassin card, the game guarantees laugh-out-loud moments.

**Funny Moment**: During one game, our spymaster gave the clue "hot:2" to describe "fire" and "sun." One operative confidently revealed "soup." The confusion that followed was priceless.
