## How Skip-Bo Works (A Friendly Refresher)
Skip-Bo is a shedding-style card game where players attempt to be the first to empty their stock pile, using building piles in numerical order from 1 to 12. The wild card, the Skip-Bo, acts as a joker—your wildcard is a lifeline when you’re faced with a pile that stubbornly refuses to cooperate.

Here’s a quick mental model for new players: you’re managing two things at once—your stock pile (your personal countdown) and the shared building piles (the communal ladder you all climb). On your turn, you draw cards, play as many as you can from your hand to the building piles in numerical order, and discard the rest strategically into your discard piles. The moment your stock pile is depleted, you win. Easy, right? Well, not exactly. The beauty of Skip-Bo in its vintage form is the strategic tension: do you play conservatively to keep options alive, or do you gamble for a dramatic finish while your opponents glare with the unspoken thrill of near victory written on their faces?

If you’re an UNO fan who wants a more puzzle-y version of “play a card, draw a card, chant a victory chant,” Skip-Bo scratches that itch with a different rhythm. It’s less about matching colors and more about sequencing and anticipation. And yes, you can absolutely swap stories about family strategy, the cousin who never plays by the rules, and the time you misread a “0” as a “12” and caused a joyous cascade of chaos (or so you claim decades later).

## Care, Storage, and Preservation Tips
To keep your two sealed decks in tip-top condition, here are a few practical tips:
- Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The box art will thank you with colors that pop rather than fade.
