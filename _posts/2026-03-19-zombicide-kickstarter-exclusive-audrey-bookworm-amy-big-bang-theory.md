## Audrey the Bookworm — Design and Function

Audrey’s stat card reads like a proud spoiler for your next gaming session. She’s not the strongest fighter in a straight-up brawl, but her specialty lies in information, planning, and the occasional book-based trap-breaking. The ability text reads: When this survivor ends a turn in a library location, gain +1 to a knowledge check or unlock a special event card. In practice, that means Audrey shines in scenarios with locked doors, clue tokens, or boss fights that reward careful planning.

In terms of sculpts, the librarian vibe reads loud and clear. Audrey wears a cardigan over a shirt with elbow patches, glasses perched on her nose, and a tote bag that doubles as a weaponized tote of doom. The book accessory isn’t just a prop; it’s a mini-object that can be used to block line of sight (in the fiction sense), and the book’s cover art is a cute easter egg paying homage to classic fantasy novels. For the painterly nerds among us, Audrey is a solid candidate for a neat, calm, intel-nerd palette: warm browns, faded ink blues, a splash of crimson for the bookmark, and perhaps a glowing eye from the book’s magical aura.

## Amy Farrah Fowler — The Nerd with a Heart (and a Lab Coat)

Amy’s promo is a cultural grenade in a smoke-free lab: sharp eyes, a lab coat that looks practical enough to survive an elevator ride with a zombie, and a pair of safety goggles perched like a halo. The game text for Amy often emphasizes deduction, planning, and the occasional nerd diplomacy card that helps your team talk its way through a trap rather than brute force your way past it. ## Painting and Display Tips

Painting Audrey and Amy is a joy that would make any hobbyist clap like a delighted seal. Here are a few tips:

- Start with a clean primer. A neutral gray or zenith white base helps the details pop without fighting you at every step.
- Audrey’s browns and beiges can be enriched with a wash to simulate old paper and leather-bound library aesthetics.
- For Amy, lean into the lab coat with a slightly glossy finish to evoke the clinical, sterile vibe, and give the goggles a metallic shine to catch the light on the tabletop.
- Use a small brush for the hair and accessories to ensure the contrast is visible at board distance, especially if you play at a high table.
