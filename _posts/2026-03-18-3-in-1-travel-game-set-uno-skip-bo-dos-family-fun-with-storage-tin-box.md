## How to play quick rules in a travel context
UNO, Skip-Bo, and DOS share a DNA of simplicity with a dash of competitive chaos. Here are quick refreshers for on-the-road or in-flight sessions.

### UNO: quick summary
- Match color or number to discard; draw cards if you’re stuck.
- Special cards spice things up: Reverse, Skip, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four.
- The first player to empty their hand wins. If you’re playing with kids, set a goofy victory condition (like “last card must perform a victory dance”).

### Skip-Bo: quick summary
- Build appropriate piles with stock cards to organize your play.
- The goal is to deplete your stock piles as quickly as possible.
- Strategy matters: hold back key numbers to block others while maintaining your lead.

### DOS: quick summary
- A two-deck duel where players attempt to play two matching numbers in sequences, adding a splash of color strategy to the mix.
- A mini-boss mode for UNO and Skip-Bo fans who want something a little more brain-tickling.
- DOS gives you a nostalgic twist with a twist of modern puzzle energy.

Note: DOS contains its own quirks and a setup that rewards you for paying attention rather than daydreaming about snacks.
