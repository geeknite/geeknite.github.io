## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity: Because Nothing Lasts Forever Except Maybe Your Last Hand
Chips of this type usually hold up well under the stresses of house games, but they do benefit from a little care. Here are practical tips to ensure your set lasts longer than the novelty of a saintly dealer button:

- Store chips in a dry, cool place away from direct sunlight. Fading happens, even to the most well-intentioned set, and sunburn chips are not sexy chips.
- Keep decks of cards clean and replace them after significant wear. A card deck with sticky fingers or gouged corners can ruin a hand at the river, so consider a spare deck nearby for those epic moments of bad luck turned comedic relief.
- Avoid exposing chips to moisture and heat; a damp coffee table is not your friend and you’ll be counting misreads instead of outs.
- Regularly sweep the playing area. Nothing shouts “amateur night” like a table covered in popcorn kernels and snack crumbs that resemble a pot full of gold at the end of a river.
- If chips begin to chip on the edges, a gentle buffing with a soft cloth can restore their look without compromising the integrity of the edge designs. Avoid any harsh cleaners that could strip the paint or miscolor the surface.
