### How to play: the quick refresher
If you know how Cards Against Humanity works, you know enough to play this edition. A host draws a black prompt card, a player selects a white card from their hand that best, or worst, aligns with the prompt, and the host picks the winner of that round. The pink edition adds a subtle social twist in the prompts, nudging players toward experiences and scenarios that feel more connected to everyday life while preserving the irreverent humor that CAH fans love. It is not a child-friendly game, nor should it be. The humor remains intentionally edgy, but the color palette and some prompts are designed to invite a more playful, less aggressively offensive tone. If you want a game night that dips its toe into raucous laughs without completely torching the room, this edition offers a balance you can tune to your group.
