## How to Play with Sealed Cards

### Setup

- Shuffle the base deck and lay it out in the center
- Each player draws a starting hand (the number varies by rules, typically 7)
- Add any sealed cards as directed by the edition you hold
- Decide who goes first with a ceremonial coin flip or a completely random method such as rock paper scissors

### The Flow of a Turn

On your turn you will do a few things in this order:

1) Play any action cards from your hand that you want to, including Nope cards that cancel others if your friends are feeling spicy

2) End your turn by drawing a card from the deck, risking a possible explosion

3) If you draw an Exploding Kitten and you do not have a Defuse, you are out of the round

4) If you do have a Defuse, you can save yourself and place the Exploding Kitten back into the deck somewhere your opponents will never suspect — the classic misdirection move

The sealed cards may trigger under specific round conditions, such as when a player uses a particular action or when a timer runs low. The rules are typically straightforward, but the sealed cards add a few layers of surprise that keep even veteran players guessing.

### Key Cards in Play

- Exploding Kitten: The doom card that makes anyone anxious about the next draw
- Defuse: The lucky lifeline that saves you from immediate gloom
- See the Future: Peek at the top few cards to plan your mischief
- Nope: A wild cancel that can shut down someone else action with comedic timing

The sealed cards insert a few new flavors into those categories, but the core loop remains intact: draw, risk, react, and outlast.

## House Rules and Variants

If you want to dial up or down the chaos, try some simple house rules:

- Quick Start, No Holds Barred: Remove any cards that slow the game down and start with a bold 5 card hand per player. This accelerates the chaos and keeps things snappy for a family with a lot of energy.
- See the Future Remix: If you have See the Future in your hand, allow a group peek of three cards instead of the standard two. It tilts the balance toward planning and bluffing rather than panic.
- Sealed Cards Mini-Tournament: Each player holds a sealed card until a designated round marker, then reveals it for a spontaneous effect. It becomes a gag that each player tries to one-up with witty banter.

House rules are a great way to tailor the experience to your group, and the sealed cards give you extra room to improvise without breaking the core engine of the game.
