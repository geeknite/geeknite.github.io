## How to Play: A Quick-Start Guide for the Impatient
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll recognize the core loop: draw until someone reveals an Exploding Kitten and can’t defuse it. If you’re new to the series, fear not—there’s a short learning curve here, and the rules are designed to be learned in the moment rather than studied for hours.

- Set up: Shuffle the deck and deal a starting hand to each player (number depends on the official rules variant you’re using). Place the exploding kitten cards into the deck according to the setup instructions and ensure defuse cards are accessible.
- Objective: Avoid drawing an exploding kitten card without a defuse card in your hand.
- On your turn: Play any number of action cards for effects like forcing opponents to draw, peeking at future cards, or swapping hands. Then draw a card from the deck.
- End game: The last player remaining who hasn’t exploded wins.

Because Grab & Game is a sealed edition, there’s a little more focus on speed and flow. You’ll probably be able to run a complete game in under 10 minutes, which makes it terrific for a quick round between classes, on a lunch break, or during a gaming night that’s had one too many dramatic back-and-forths on another title.

## Strategy: Tips for Winning (Or at Least Not Exploding in Public)
Let’s be honest: Exploding Kittens is as much about psychology as it is about card counts. Here are a few light strategies to maximize your survival odds in Grab & Game:
- Read the table, not just your hand. The tempo of turns often reveals who’s about to draw a dangerous card. If someone looks too confident, they might be bluffing or about to do something unexpected with a card draw.
- Use attack and see-the-future cards strategically. These cards turn your turn into a mini-ante where you hope to shift the risk away from yourself. Don’t overplay them; timing is everything.
- Save defuse cards for the late game. If you can survive a few rounds while others whittle down their defenses, you’ll have a better shot at the final pull.
- The quick-start guide is your friend, but don’t be afraid to bend the rules to fit your group’s vibe. The best games are the ones where players feel comfortable improvising while keeping the core rules intact.
