## Setup, maintenance, and daily use tips
- Start with a level surface. A wobbly base is not your friend. Use the leg levelers to dial in stability; a minute of tweaking beats an hour of chasing a ball that slides off the table because the entire unit jitters with every player swap.
- Keep the playing surfaces clean. Dust and oils can degrade ball control. A light microfiber cloth is enough for most households. If you notice a rough patch on a rod, check for burrs and remove them with a small file or light sandpaper, then re-oil.
- Lubrication: use a light silicone-based spray on the rods periodically. Don’t overdo it—excess lubrication can attract dust and make the playing field slick in a negative way.
- Ball care: if your ball set is dull or scuffed, replace them with new competition-grade foosballs. A well-maintained ball improves shot consistency and reduces jamming risks in the ball return.
- Storage and placement: place the table away from heat sources and direct sunlight to prevent warping of wooden components. Let a good 2-3 feet of clearance around the table exist for a comfortable stretch during play and for safe ball retrieval.

## Maintenance: keeping the table in peak condition
The long-term love affair with your 58x35 foosball table rests on simple routines:

- Regular cleaning: wipe down the cabinet and panels after play to prevent grime buildup that can dull the finish and invite sticky fingers. 
- Rod care: check for burrs and ensure the rods remain straight. Light oiling a couple times a year helps prevent squeaks and improves the feel of the shot. Do not over-oil; too much lubrication can attract dust and degrade play realism.
- Ball care: replace worn foosballs early. A scratched ball can alter bounce and throwing off ball control.
- Leveling: recheck leg levelers if you notice wobbling. The best way to test level is to roll a ball along the length of the table and observe whether it travels smoothly or veers toward a corner.
