## How to play: transform this box into a night of gameplay

1. Shuffle and deal a hand as you normally would in a friendly five-card draw or Texas Hold’em variant.
2. Use chips to place bets. The multicolor chips help newcomers identify bet sizes without needing a long glossary of rules.
3. Build a small ritual around shuffles, discards, and reveals that keeps everyone engaged even when the table devolves into friendly banter about sci‑fi trivia.
4. For a party-friendly twist, designate a “tech handicap” where the person who previously hacked the living room’s Wi‑Fi must perform a penalty shot (or a silly improv line) before the hand continues. It’s silly, but it helps with social lubrication.

### Keno mode: quick-start improvisation

1. Assign a Keno-style grid on a piece of paper or a printed pad (the set’s quick-start sheets are perfect for this).
2. Players pick a set of numbers (e.g., 1–15) and place chips on the corresponding positions on the grid.
3. Use a simple random draw to pick numbers (draw from a deck, use dice, or spin a small wheel if you want to keep it tactile).
4. Compare drawn numbers to player selections and tally wins with chips as prizes. The more numbers hit, the bigger your bragging rights, and perhaps the more ridiculous the prize—think a forever badge of “Keno Master” or a silly crown you can wear for the rest of the night.

These modes aren’t official rulebooks; they’re ways to bend the rules with style. The beauty of a kit like this is that it invites improvisation.
