## Assembly and Installation

If you have assembled a piece of furniture in the last decade, this kit will be familiar. The GOF 1P ships in a handful of panels and bags of hardware that look suspiciously similar to the items in a sci-fi toolbox.

### Step-by-step assembly

- Step 1: Lay out all panels on a soft surface. It’s best to avoid the marble-like coffee table you’re currently using as a workspace — you don’t want to scratch the espresso finish before you even finish the first step.
- Step 2: Attach the side panels to the base frame using the included bolts. A little torque goes a long way here; over-tightening can freeze the bolt and require a heroic screwdriver extraction next week.
- Step 3: Install the desk surface and secure the desk-set shelf. Ensure the screws align with the predrilled holes; if a hole refuses to accept a screw, back off and re-align rather than forcing it. That is not a life hack; it’s basic common sense.
- Step 4: Route cables using the built-in tray and grommets. This is where you realize how many cables you actually own that need a home. If you have a monitor, a webcam, a laptop, and a charging dock, congratulations; you now know what a spaghetti junction feels like.
- Step 5: Attach the height-adjustable or fixed components (depending on your model). Double-check the unit is level; a small tilt can translate into a coffee mug that slides off and creates a new hazard in your cubicle.

Yes, it’s a DIY project, but a reasonable one that doesn’t demand a manual with more pages than your quarterly report. For a typical adult who can read simple diagrams, it should take roughly 30-45 minutes with a moderate amount of triage for missing screws and misplaced allen wrenches.

### Common pitfalls and how to avoid them

- Don’t force parts into misaligned holes. It will cause cosmetic damage and may void the warranty.
- Use a magnetic screwdriver if possible; it helps keep screws from disappearing into the abyss behind the desk now and then.
- If cable management isn’t enough for your modern device ecosystem, add a small under-desk hub or a USB-C dock to keep your desktop neat.
