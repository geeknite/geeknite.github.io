## Condition and Maintenance — What Good Condition Really Means
The label Good Condition in a used card game can mean many things. The seller may have lightly handled the items, or it could be a box that lived under the bed of a child who discovered the joy of face cards during a thunderstorm. When evaluating Good Condition, consider:

- Card surface: Look for surface scratches, edge wear, and corner dings. A Good Condition deck should be playable with minor blemishes. If there are large whitening lines or creases, that is a sign of heavy use.

- Case integrity: The carry clip mechanism should function, the shell should not crack easily, and the latch should maintain closure. If the case has visible cracks or the clip is loose, you might consider a replacement.

- Box interior: A well-preserved interior is a good sign. Missing inserts or loose cards can cause the deck to misbehave in play.

- Cleanliness: The cards should be free of odors and sticky residue. A faint mustiness is common in older games, but heavy odor or sticky surfaces may indicate poor storage.

- Sleeve compatibility: If you plan to sleeve the cards, confirm that the sleeve size fits the deck; a deck that is too snug or too loose can hamper shuffling.
