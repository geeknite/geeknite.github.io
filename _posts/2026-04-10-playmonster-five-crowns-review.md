The five suits are the feature that makes Five Crowns more than a simple number-matching game. ## How to play in a nutshell
If you want a compact mental model of Five Crowns, here it is: meld, discard, draw, repeat. The objective is to shed all your cards by forming valid melds and layering in strategic discards to block others. Each round is typically structured with a draw, a meld, and a discard. While the core loop is straightforward, the presence of five suits introduces a delightful combinatorial nuance that rewards players who are willing to rearrange their hands on the fly.

- Draw phase: Start your turn by drawing either from the deck or taking the top card from the discard pile. Your choices should align with a plan to form one or more melds.
- Meld phase: You can lay down one or more melds if your hand contains valid sets or runs. This is where you drop your hand’s blood, sweat, and tears onto the table in neat little groups, like a chaotic but organized nebula.
- Discard phase: End your turn by discarding a card onto the discard pile. The color of that discard might signal a clever strategic cue to the next player, or at least a mild hint that you are still alive in this round.

The first player to go out typically wins the round. Scoring varies by variant, but commonly you tally up the cards left in everyone’s hands, with lower is better. In a family game, this can turn swiftly into a joke-filled arithmetic lesson that your aunt will lovingly remind you about for years.

## Rule notes and common questions
A few quick notes that new players tend to ask about Five Crowns:

- Do you need to use all five suits in a meld? Not necessarily. You can form sets and runs that utilize a subset of suits. The flexibility here is what makes the game approachable and replayable.
- How many rounds do you play per game night? It depends on your group size and how long you want to play. A typical session might involve several rounds to accumulate scores until someone hits a pre-agreed target, like 50 or 100 points, but you can tune it to suit your vibe.
- Are wild cards in this game? Some variants include wild cards or jokers that can stand in for other cards in the melds. If you are playing with younger players, you might want to skip wilds to keep things straightforward.
- Can you play seated on the sofa while juggling snacks? Absolutely, as long as you have enough space for the cards and the discard pile. In Geeknite fashion, we celebrate the couch gaming lifestyle.
