## Care, Cleaning, and Maintenance

### Quick Cleanups
For most spills, blots are your friend. The rule of thumb is: absorb, don’t rub. Start with a dry cloth or paper towel to lift as much liquid as possible, then dab with a mild cleaning solution or a specialized rug cleaner. Always test in an inconspicuous area first, because even the most friendly fibers can throw a tantrum if you surprise them with something neon and acidic.

### Deep Cleaning
Every so often, you’ll want to give the rug a deeper clean to revive its colors and texture. A gentle vacuum with the beater bar turned off helps maintain the pile height while removing dust and hair. If you’re brave, you can do a light shampoo with a mild detergent; otherwise, a professional cleaning every couple of years is a sensible approach for most households.

### Stain-Specific Tips
Red wine, coffee, and tomato sauce all have reputations for staining. The Fossil Creek Multi tends to hide minor staining gracefully, but persistent, set-in stains benefit from prompt action. Blot, treat with a color-safe cleaner, and rinse. If you can, move the rug outdoors on a sunny day to air out—sunlight can help break down residues and freshen the fibers.
