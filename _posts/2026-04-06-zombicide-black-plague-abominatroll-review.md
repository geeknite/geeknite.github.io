# Zombicide Black Plague: Abominatroll — Boss of the Soggy Trolls and Otherworldly Stomps

If you’ve ever thought your game nights were missing a smelly, towering behemoth with hairier knuckles than your ex’s skeletons, congratulations: the Abominatroll is here to ruin your day and make your dice cry themselves to sleep. Zombicide: Black Plague introduced a lineup of bosses that feel like they were designed by someone who watched a documentary about medieval moats and said, “Hold my ale.” The Abominatroll stands at the top of that list as the boss you’ll both fear and secretly admire for its chaotic charm. In this guide, we’re going to clown-car through its design, abilities, tactics, and why it’s the perfect boss for players who want to feel like they’re in a grimdark fantasy RPG while still holding onto a ball of yarn and a spare wooden spoon.

> For quick context: Abominatroll is a boss zombie that doesn’t just walk up and punch you. It stomps, it howls, it disrupts your carefully-laid plans, and it likely smells like a gym sock that forgot its deodorant. If you’re lucky, you’ll defeat it with flair; if you’re not, you’ll redefine your defeat as a performance art piece titled “The Ballad of the Bleeding Dice.”


## Abominatroll in a Nutshell

- The Abominatroll is a boss zombie with a distinctly lumberjack-chic vibe and a health pool that makes your average zombie look like a warm-up act. In practice, you’ll see itHP-tank through the party with a mix of brutal melee and disruptive special rules.
- It isn’t just “big and tough.” The Abominatroll also punishes poor positioning and coordination, turning clumsy tactics into epic failure montages that you’ll be quoting to your future grandchildren (or at least to your friends who never learned to roll initiative).
- Its abilities revolve around crowd control, forceful movement, and the occasional “I dare you to break my defense” challenge that tests your team’s ability to plan ahead and adapt on the fly.

If you’re not into heavy math and you want a savage dragon’s cousin in a hood, you’ve found your monster. The Abominatroll isn’t a puzzle you solve with straight lines and raw DPS; it’s a test of teamwork, tempo, and your ability to fake confidence when you’re really hoping the other guy rolls well.


## Design, Theme, and Thematic Coherence

The Abominatroll nails the vibe of a humanity-crumbled anti-hero. Its art and token design lean into the grimy, mossy aesthetic of a medieval forest that time forgot. The model (in the physical game) looks like a rotund troll that’s eaten enough corpses to achieve a new layer of “tough.” The card art gives you a sense of scale and menace: this is not a minion, this is a fortress with teeth. The design team did a nice job making the Abominatroll feel like a legitimate boss that you wouldn’t just punch into a corner and ignore. If you’re playing the campaign mode, you’ll want to read its card thoroughly to understand how each of its abilities interacts with the rest of the board. This is not a boss you fight with go-with-the-flow vibes; it’s a boss that demands you adapt and improvise.

For fans of the setting, the Abominatroll fits perfectly into the Black Plague era: a game where magic is scarce and roving bands of wizards are replaced by rot and resilience. The Abominatroll’s flavor is less “spectacular break-the-map” and more “grim reminder that some problems require a scalpel, not a sledgehammer.” It’s chaotic enough to surprise you, but predictable enough to be beaten if you learn its rhythm.


## Mechanics, Abilities, and How They Play On The Table

Like most bosses in Zombicide: Black Plague, Abominatroll’s card text is a mix of straightforward stat lines and strange, memorable modifiers. You’ll likely remember its rules after a single encounter because they revolve around one central idea: force the survivors to choose between disappointing positions and catastrophic mistakes.

- Health and Armor: Abominatroll is a high-HP, armored threat. It requires multiple hits to bring down, and its armor reduces some of the early round pressure you’d like to apply. This makes early skirmishes slow and punishing, which can lead to tense stalemates if your team isn’t careful.
- Movement and Stomping: It can perform powerful, ground-shaking moves that rearrange the board. The result is a game state where your plan from the first round is suddenly out the window. The Stomping action is not just a cosmetic flourish—it reshapes the map, forcing survivors to rethink their escape routes and engagement distances.
- Special Rules: Abominatroll often has a few signature rules that interact with terrain, lighting, and the presence of other zombies. For example, it might gain bonuses when standing on certain tiles or when there are a lot of survivors on the board. These rules encourage creative thinking: you don’t just stand your ground; you manipulate the board to force a mistake.
- Attack Patterns: Expect a mix of melee and disruptive effects. An Abominatroll encounter often blends dangerous bites with “earth-shaking” effects that can throw a squad’s formation off-balance. You’ll need to coordinate to avoid being picked off one by one and to avoid giving the boss easy opportunities to punish clumsy moves.

In short: Abominatroll punishes hesitation and rewards players who communicate, plan two or three steps ahead, and accept that sometimes a trap is a better friend than a clean kill. It’s not a pure dice-fest; it’s a tactical exercise masked in a hilarious, gnarly skin.


## Tactics: Survive, Outsmart, and Maybe Smile While It Bleeds

Warning: the Abominatroll plays dirty. It doesn’t care about your feelings; it cares about your dice—and your friends’ dice too. Here are some practical strategies to handle this behemoth without quitting the game and adopting a new life as a hermit crab:

- Stage the Ingress: Because the Abominatroll’s movement can rearrange the board, it’s crucial to control the initial approach. Don’t send all your peels in at once. You’ll want to create choke points, force it to commit to a direction, and capitalize on the few moments when you’re certain you can land a killing blow.
- Split the Party (Carefully): The classic “split the party” can work here, provided you aren’t throwing your teammates to the wolves. Use ranged support from the edges to chip away at its HP while a smaller, coordinated group keeps pressure on from a safe, controlled position.
- Terrain as an Ally: Abominatroll loves open spaces because it can roam and stomp; but if you force it onto rough terrain or a narrow corridor, its mobility becomes a liability. If your board includes obstacles, you’re going to win some extra time and maybe an extra hit with luck and planning.
- Resource Management: In longer campaigns, the Abominatroll becomes a test of resource management. Save your best carry-ons for the late rounds. Don’t burn every card in the first firefight—keep a couple of stunners or special actions for the exact moment you need them to get out of a bad engagement.
- Ally Timing: If you’re playing co-op, timing your “aid” actions—the healing, the re-positioning, the backup—becomes more critical than raw DPS. The Abominatroll’s pressure is best countered by crisp turns where someone else cleanly finishes the job its aggression has created.

If you want a quick mental model: think of the Abominatroll as a “boss fight” that wants you to forget your plan. It’s the kind of boss that tests your team’s ability to stay calm under chaos, adapt to misdirection, and still send a few good dice toward a weak spot. The payoff? A triumphant moment you’ll talk about for weeks, even if nobody wants to clean up the sticky floor after the battle.


## Board Setup and Scenarios: What Works, What Sucks, and What Makes You Laugh

The Abominatroll scales more dramatically with board complexity than most other bosses. In smaller maps, you’ll have fewer places to run and less room to maneuver. In larger maps, you’ll find more opportunities for missteps and spectacular fails, which your group will quote in future sessions. Here are some tangible tips for different scenarios:

- Small Map, One Boss: Focus on the choke points and use long-range damage to reduce its threat before it can reach your front lines. Try to keep converts alive to maintain a defensive wall that can buy enough time to land the final, satisfying blow.
- Medium Map, Two Bosses: When multiple bosses appear, your best strategy is to use overwatch lines and synergy. Coordinate your actions so that you can suppress one threat while you chip away at the other. The Abominatroll’s area control becomes less overwhelming when you don’t let its friends roam freely.
- Large Map, Varied Terrain: This is where you shine if you’re the planning mastermind of the group. Use terrain advantages and planned retreats to whittle it down while your squad takes advantage of the open spaces to maximize success on your next turn when the moment arrives to close the game.

If you’re new to Abominatroll fights, don’t be afraid to emulate the patterns you see online, but feel free to improvise. The fun of Zombicide is that you don’t always have to do it “perfectly.” You can do it with style, with a dash of luck, and with your house Rule: “Always bring a snack.” Remember to enjoy the chaos; the Abominatroll is here to remind you that the universe is not just a persistent threat—it’s also your probably-out-of-tortilla-chips, always unpredictable, best-excuse-for-drunken-silliness friend.


## Interaction and Theme: How the Abominatroll Plays with Other Bosses and Cards

The Abominatroll doesn’t exist in a vacuum. It interacts with the rest of Black Plague’s boss lineup and other game mechanics in ways that can turn a moral victory into a moral question about what is the right amount of groaning for dinner. Team composition matters as much as luck:

- When paired with other bosses, the Abominatroll can create compounded pressure that forces you to distribute attention across multiple threats. This is where your team’s communication and leadership shines. If you can keep your group from panicking, you’ll have a better chance of surviving the round where everyone wants to be a hero.
- Environmental synergy can amplify the Abominatroll’s worst traits. If you play on a board with lots of open spaces, you’ll be tempted to over-extend, which is precisely what the Abominatroll wants. Conversely, a board that rewards clever blocking and deliberate progression can turn the fight into a chess game instead of a brawl.
- The card interactions are where the boss truly earns its keep. Abominatroll’s own deck has surprises that can escalate the tension and require players to adapt their plan mid-battle. It’s not a one-turn wonder; it’s a multi-turn test of nerve and coordination.

As a result, you’ll often see the most memorable Abominatroll fights become those where the survivors rotate roles, misdirection works, and someone nails a perfect timing of initiative that makes the entire table cheer—then immediately groan when the next round starts and the chaos resumes.
