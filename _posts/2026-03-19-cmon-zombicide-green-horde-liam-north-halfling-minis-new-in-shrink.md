## Painting and assembly tips

### Getting them on the table fast
If you’re the type who dreads assembly, these minis are a pleasant surprise. Each figure is fairly simple to assemble, with minimal parts and a forgiving fit that isn’t going to demand pinning expertise or a magnifying glass the size of a postcard. A thin base tap, a dab of plastic cement, and a quick primer are all you need to get the show on the road. A light gray or white primer helps the paint adhere and ensures you don’t end up with a muddy color wash obscuring the sculpts’ crisp lines.

### Painting suggestions for Liam and North
- Skin tones: A mid-range flesh tone with a soft ink wash to bring out cheeks and ears. Halflings tend toward a sun-kissed look rather than pallor, so a warm hue reads well on table lighting.
- Clothing palette: Earth tones—browns, greens, with a pop of color for belts or sashes. Small metallic accents on buckles and clasps catch the eye without stealing focus from the greens of the Horde.
- Freehand details: If you’re feeling bold, a tiny insignia on a leather pouch or a rune on a cloak can be a satisfying touch. Even a simple highlight along the cloak’s edge will help the silhouette pop under the board’s lighting.
- Basing: Add a few tufts of grass or small rocks to ground the halflings in their environment. It helps sell the “top of a ruined mill” vibe and makes them easier to read on the table amid the green fog of the Horde.

### Quick assembly checklist
- Check for mold lines; a light sanding before priming helps paint grip and prevents a rough, chalky surface.
- Attach to the base with a tiny amount of plastic cement or CA glue; keep the application neat—these are tiny figures, and glue drips are harder to correct than paint smudges.
- Prime in thin coats; multiple light passes beat a heavy, silhouette-clogging primer that hides details.
- Paint in thin layers; build color gradually to preserve sculpted detail. If you rush, you risk muddy edges and a halfling that looks like a blob in the forest darkness.
- Seal with a matte varnish to protect from chipping during table chatter and enthusiastic dice tossing.
