## Painting and assembly tips
### Getting them on the table fast
If you’re a hobbyist who hates lengthy assembly, these minis are a welcome addition. They come in a single piece per figure with little to no assembly required beyond the base and perhaps a thin pin for a slightly dynamic pose. If you want to prime quickly, use a light spray of gray or white to improve paint adhesion. For the base, a quick wash to bring out texture is all you need to avoid dull, flat surfaces.

### Painting suggestions for Liam and North
- Skin tones: A mid-range flesh tone with a light ink wash to bring out the cheeks and ears. Halflings are not as pale as elves or goblins; they have a warm, sun-kissed hue that reads well under game lighting.
- Clothing palette: Think earth tones—browns and greens with a pop of color in belts or cloaks. A dab of metallic color for buckles adds a little shine without stealing the scene from the goblins.
- Freehand details: If you’re feeling bold, a tiny insignia on a leather pouch or a simple rune on a cloak can be a nice touch. If not, a clean, crisp edge on the cloak ensures the mini remains legible at table distance.
- Basing: A splash of green tufts or small tufts of static grass can ground the halflings in their environment, making them pop against the undead silhouettes.

### Quick assembly checklist
- Check for any mold lines; a light sanding before priming helps the paint grip better.
- Attach to the base with a tiny amount of plastic cement or super glue; don’t gush—these are tiny figures, and glue stains are easier to spot than paint mistakes.
- Prime in thin coats; multiple light passes beat a heavy, silhouetting primer that clogs detail.
- Paint with thin layers; build up color slowly to preserve the sculpted detail.
- Seal with a matte varnish to prevent chipping during table chatter and heroic dice-tosses.
