## How to Set Rules That Don’t Break Friendships
Here are a few practical tips we’ve tested in the trenches:

- Pre-game Agreement: Decide which wild-card effects are allowed and which are banned. If you’re playing with kids, keep it simple. If you’re playing with seasoned players, allow the more chaotic variants but set a cap on the number of wild cards that can be played in a row.
- Card-Writer Etiquette: Use dry-erase markers or the included writing guide to avoid leaving permanent marks on the cards. You will thank yourself later when you’re not explaining “brand-new” stains to your host’s carpet.
- Turn-Taking Rules: If a Wild card changes the turn order, clarify who goes next and whether a Wild Swap affects turn progression. Trust us: the only thing worse than a rule change mid-game is a rule-change that everyone forgot about five minutes later.
- Endgame Guardrails: If someone is one card away, avoid letting the wild-card chaos derail the final stretch. A simple pre-agreed rule like “no more than one wild card can be played in the last two turns” goes a long way toward keeping the peace—and the laughs flowing.
