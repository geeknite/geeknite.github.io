Durability and Maintenance

Wear and Tear Tolerance

A hand tufted wool rug doesn’t instantly become indestructible, but it’s built to handle typical family life. If you have a busy living room with kids and pets, expect the colors to age gracefully with a patina over time. Wool’s natural resilience helps the pile spring back after foot traffic, though you will want to rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear. Avoid heavy dragging of furniture and use a rug pad to protect both the rug and the floor underneath.

Cleaning Basics

Wool rugs prefer gentle care. The best approach is regular vacuuming with a low-suction setting and a brushless attachment to avoid pulling fibers. Spills should be blotted immediately; avoid rubbing, which pushes liquid deeper into the pile. For deeper cleaning, use a mild wool-safe cleaner and test on a hidden corner first to ensure colorfastness. Avoid harsh chemical cleaners that can degrade wool fibers and alter color. If you’re adventurous, a local rug cleaner with wool experience can refresh the pile without harming the structure.

Care Guide and Pro Tips

- Rotate every 6–12 months to promote even wear. 
- Use a quality rug pad to prevent slipping and to preserve pile height. 
- Blot spills with a clean cloth; avoid heat. 
- If the colors look dull after heavy use, consider a professional cleaning to restore vibrancy. 
- Protect against UV exposure by placing the rug away from direct sun for long periods; wool can fade over time with prolonged light exposure.
