## Ergonomics and Comfort for Four

Ergonomics for a four-person workspace is a tricky topic. You want each user to have enough knee clearance, elbow room, and screen height to avoid neck strain. The cornerstation answers by providing adjustable legs, thigh clearance, and individual monitor mounts that allow each user to align their screens to their own eye height. The general guideline is to keep the monitor at or slightly below eye level, with the top of the screen at about 2-3 inches above seated eye level. For groups, you may want to consider a shared seating arrangement that encourages ergonomic conversation rather than the classic hunch over the keyboard. In practice, you will want to pick chairs with good lumbar support and height adjustability, and set up a rotation schedule for monitor height checks. The four-person configuration can encourage healthy work habits; multiple people can remind each other to take breaks, stand up, or stretch, which is a healthier alternative to the traditional office where a single person yells at others for “taking a walk” during a coding sprint.

From the perspective of comfort, you will find the chairs that come with the set to be fairly serviceable, offering adequate support for standard work sessions. If your team does long creative sprints, you may want to add personal footrests or a small under-desk gym to keep your circulation from turning into a pineapple-tasting coma. But overall, the four-person corner arrangement fosters a sense of shared purpose while also preserving personal space; you can collaborate when you want to, and retreat when you don’t—without stepping into someone else’s coffee spill zone.
