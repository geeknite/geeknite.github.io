## What Exactly Is the Card Game Variant?

Codenames, as many know, is a word game that places you as a spymaster in a race to connect agents with a single clue. The standard board game uses a 5x5 grid of word cards, and teams take turns giving one-word clues that relate to multiple words on the board. The twist is critical: some words belong to your team, some to the opposing team, and one is the dreaded assassin. The ultimate goal is to reveal all your team’s agents before the other team does, with a dash of risk and a sprinkle of linguistic gymnastics.

The Card Game variant distills that core essence into a card-driven format. It’s designed for shorter sessions, easier handling in smaller spaces, and the joy of a quick turnaround before someone yells, “Let’s go again!” This version often trades in the board and 25-card grid for a deck of word cards plus a handful of clue cards. Instead of a central board, you get a faster rhythm: you draw cards, you issue a clue, you reveal, you react, you pivot, you laugh at yourself for misinterpreting the clues, and you move to the next round.

From a design perspective, it’s a neat evolution. It preserves the core mechanic—one person gives a clue that binds multiple target words together—while removing the spatial element. That means you can enjoy intense wordplay with a smaller footprint. It also means you can play on nearly any surface: a table, a coffee shop’s high-top, or your kitchen counter while you’re pretending to bake something that requires precise language instead of actual measurements.
