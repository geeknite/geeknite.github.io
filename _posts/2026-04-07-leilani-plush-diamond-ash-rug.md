## Cleaning, Maintenance, and Longevity

Cleaning this rug is not a ritual; it is a sensible weekly habit that yields long-term payoff. The plush fibers resist soaking up every spill to the point that you can dab up most messes with a microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner. For stubborn stains, a gentle spot-clean with warm water and a mild detergent does the trick, followed by air drying. Do not machine wash unless the manufacturer explicitly states it is safe; this is one of those moments where the line between convenience and cost of a ruined rug is important to observe.

Vacuuming frequency depends on foot traffic. A mid-sized family or a lively gamer apartment will benefit from a weekly vacuuming routine. In high-traffic zones, a quick daily brush with a soft-bristle vacuum head keeps the nap looking plush and prevents grime from setting into the pile. For pet hair, a rubber broom works wonders and makes quick work of fur on the surface before you vacuum. In our tests, the rug shed fewer hairs than expected for a plush pile, a pleasant surprise that reduces the amount of time you spend chasing after tumbleweeds of fur.

Longevity wise, this rug is built to last. The color tone holds well after months of use, and the pattern remains crisp without visible flattening in lines of heavy traffic. If you expect to flip the room twice a year and require a rug that ages gracefully, Leilani is a worthy companion that pairs nicely with a rotating cast of furniture while staying true to its soul — which is plush and tough at the same time.
