## Gameplay overview: quick and chaotic, in a good way
The Tricky Egg Card Game is designed to be fast and social. The core loop is simple: on your turn, you draw, you play, you resolve a mini-challenge, you might sabotage someone else, and you pass the turn. The tasks are quick, the effects are flavorful, and the table energy tends to spike in waves as players anticipate the next betrayal—often delivered with a grin, a wink, and a punned one-liner.

Key card types to watch for include action cards that let you steal or swap eggs, event cards that force everyone to react in a silly way, and “trap” cards that punish reckless plays with a gentle nudge toward responsibility for your own eggy chaos. The balance is surprisingly good: you’re not guaranteed victory by luck alone, but luck does help you swing the table’s mood in your favor when you need it most.

Turn structure is predictable enough to teach quickly: draw, play, resolve, end turn. Yet there’s enough subtlety in card effects and combinations that you’ll discover small synergies that feel clever. The “Tricky” in Tricky Egg is real, but it’s the friendly kind of tricky—less labyrinth, more playful riddle doodles that you can solve with your friends and a few hearty groans.
