## Practical tips for hosting an NSFW game night

To maximize fun and minimize awkwardness, here are a few practical suggestions you can fold into your ruleset, especially for first-timers:

- Pre-game briefing. A quick chat about tone and boundaries helps set expectations. If your group loves outrageous humor, you’re likely in for a blast. If not, a quick pivot to a milder mode keeps the vibe positive.
- Boundary nudge. Have a simple consent rule: if someone at the table says stop, the card hand is paused and a different card is drawn. It keeps the game from becoming personal or uncomfortable.
- Spotted drinks? Place a tray or coaster system to protect cards and keep drinks away from the play area. The last thing you want is a splash of soda on a defuse card or a pun-powered insult.
- Quick reset ritual. After a tense finish or a buzzy round, take 60 seconds to reset the table, recap the best lines, and celebrate the most ridiculous moment. That rhythm makes the next round even more epic.
- Mix and match with care. If you’re running multiple expansions in one session, stagger their introductions so players aren’t overwhelmed by too many new rules at once. It keeps the chaos manageable and the laughs genuine.
