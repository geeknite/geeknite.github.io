## Sizing, care, and maintenance: Keeping it gorgeous without turning it into a full-time job
Care is where rugs reveal their true personality. The good news is that a hand-woven wool rug can handle real life—if you give it a little TLC:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting. Don’t scrub; let the vacuum’s suction do the work to avoid pulling out fibers. A gentle pass every few days is plenty; you’re not trying to polish a surface, you’re trying to preserve a textile companion.
- Spot-clean promptly. If you spill something, blot—don’t rub—using a clean cloth. For more stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution can work, but always test in a hidden area first. Wool loves gentle handling; harsh solvents can cause color runs or fiber degradation.
- Rotate occasionally. This helps even out wear and prevents visible patterns of use from forming in high-traffic zones.
- Professional cleaning: Consider a professional wool rug cleaning every 1–2 years depending on traffic. You don’t need to be a casino dealer to appreciate a clean rug; sometimes you just need a pro and a little downtime.
- Fringes and edges: Handle with care. The fringe is a design feature that can snag on pets, shoes, and loose threads. If snagging occurs, trim carefully with scissors rather than pulling—this preserves the weave’s integrity.

In short: this rug rewards routine, gentle care rather than aggressive scrubbing or constant flipping. Wool will naturally resist some dirt, but you’ll get longer life with reasonable upkeep. The more you treat it like a living textile—checking for spills, avoiding heavy direct sunlight, and giving it a clean occasional spa day—the more it will age gracefully with you.
