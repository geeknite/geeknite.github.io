### Build and Playability: From Draft to Commander Table
Draft boxes are all about building the engine on the fly, and Battle for Baldur's Gate gives you enough leash to experiment without feeling punished for taking a few creative risks. Here are practical tips to translate your draft into a playable commander deck:

- Focus on a strong Commander shell that supports synergy across multiple colors. Baldur's Gate favors decks that can adapt to the game state, so pick a commander who rewards flexible play rather than one-trick combos.
- Prioritize removal and interaction. In a multiplayer game, having answers to opponents' key threats buys you breathing room and tempo to assemble your own. Don’t overspecialize; a few generic removal spells are worth their weight in gold.
- Build around card draw and card filtering. A draft format rewards you with a larger card pool than a standard two-player game; you want to maximize your access to solutions and threats as the board evolves.
- Consider mana fixing and ramp that scales with your color identity. You may be tempted to splash into a third color, but efficient mana development will keep you from collapsing under the weight of your own mana base.

In practice, the Baldur's Gate set invites you to test a few different shells before you settle on a favorite. The best drafts feel like a well-run heist: careful planning, a few near-misses, and a big plan that comes together in the latter acts of the game when your commander finally earns the spotlight.
