### Contents clarified: what you get and how it fits

Unwelcome Guests adds a handful of new mechanics that weave seamlessly into the gloom engine of the base game. The core concept remains: tell a story of misfortune while trying to push your family’s sad fate toward the pinnacle of doom. The expansion introduces a cadre of guests who inhabit the house and influence the score, the mood, and the storytelling rhythm.

Key components include:

- Guest cards with unique abilities that trigger at different stages of the round. Each guest adds a new layer of strategy, whether it’s a cheeky interruption or a petty trap designed to turn the mortals against one another.
- Event cards that force players to adjust their plans on the fly. The unpredictability here is essential to the chaos that keeps Gloom deliciously tense.
- A handful of new curse tokens that can ratchet up or soften the gloom depending on who’s holding them and which family member is feeling dramatic in that moment.

The interplay between guests and events creates narrative beats that feel both cinematic and delightfully petty. One or two guests might demand you sacrifice a downtrodden family member’s happiness to keep a sneering guest quiet for a round. Others may unlock chain reactions that cascade through several players, which leads to those glorious moments when the table erupts in synchronized groans and grudging laughter. It’s the kind of cooperative competition you only get when everyone agrees to pretend they are a villain auditioning for a soap opera.

### Setup and pacing: getting into the gloom

Setup follows a familiar rhythm if you’ve played Gloom before. Separate the new cards, shuffle the expansion deck with the base deck (if you’re playing with the recommended 2-4 players), and deal your starting mortals. The pacing benefits from the expansion because the guest dynamics inject immediate tension. The first couple of rounds are all about establishing a baseline of gloom for your family, while the guests begin to loom in the wings, waiting for that moment when you’ll either give them a dramatic exit or willingly invite them to stay a bit longer.

A happy accident is that the expansion doesn’t add enormous load to the turn structure. You won’t need a PhD in macroeconomics to track the score. Instead, the events and guests appear as callbacks to previous turns, turning the narrative into a loop-de-loop of storytelling where each player tries to out-emote the previous player. If you love dramatic monologues and over-the-top narration, you’ll feel right at home.

### Mechanics spotlight: guests, events, and gloom tokens

The meat of Unwelcome Guests is the trio of mechanics that keeps the table lively:

- Guests with special abilities: Think of these as celebrity cameos who show up, deliver one-liners, and then flip the tables in a way that changes the round’s probabilities. Each guest has a slightly different vibe, some encouraging risky storytelling, others punishing players who become too predictable.
- Events that ripple through rounds: Events create forced actions that can derail even the most careful plan. They ensure the narrative stays in motion and prevent stagnation. You’ll learn to anticipate the rhythm of events: scud in a few rounds, then a dramatic twist, and finally a sudden act of mercy that never actually feels merciful.
- Gloom tokens and penalty track: The expansion introduces new tokens that represent misfortune. They show up on cards at crucial moments, nudging players toward the dramatic turn that makes a story memorable. The tokens keep track of status in a way that is visually intuitive and narratively satisfying.

The result is a balancing act. You’re never overwhelmed by new rules, but you are continually drawn to adjust your story’s arc in response to the guests’ dramatic demands. In practice, this means more opportunities to outwit your table, more chances to foreshadow calamity, and more moments that will be repeated in future table sits as the punchline to your personal gloom saga.

### Strategy and synergy: when to invite guests and when to lock the door

This expansion rewards flexible storytelling over rigid optimization. A few guidelines help you leverage the new cards without turning the game into a spreadsheet of doom:

- Read the room before inviting guests: Some guests love dramatic turns, others want you to speed-run the gloom. If your table is in a mood for long-form storytelling, pick guests with long-term effects. If you want faster, punchier rounds, prefer guests whose abilities trigger early.
- Build a narrative arc around key events: Don’t treat events as mere inconveniences. Instead, treat them as plot devices that you can steer toward a satisfying arc. A well-timed event can turn a slow burn into a major twist, increasing the fun and the dramatic tension.
- Use gloom tokens as plot devices, not as end goals: Tokens exist to accentuate your misfortune, but the real win is the memory you’ll be replaying later. If you can thread a token into a moment that becomes a table-close reference, you’ve done your job as a storyteller.
- Coordinate with your fellow players: The expansion thrives on cooperative storytelling with a layer of competitive tension. Communicate, improvise, and occasionally break into a dramatic whisper to maximize the mood. The best moments come when someone at the table commits to a character voice to sell the emotional gravity of a moment. If you’re shy about roleplay, you can still have fun by imitating a Victorian narrator who keeps over-dramatizing even the smallest misfortune.

A practical setup tip: designate a “narrator anchor” for the table, someone who can help frame each guest’s entrance and each event’s consequences. This role isn’t mandatory, but it helps a lot when you want the gloom to feel cohesive rather than a random shuffle of misery. If you don’t have a narrator, a dedicated storyteller will emerge naturally as players seize the opportunity to spin a tale with exaggerated gravitas.

### Solo and party dynamics: how many players, how much gloom

Unwelcome Guests scales reasonably across player counts just like the base game. At two players, the gloom remains personal and intimate. It’s a perfect setting for a dramatic mini-drama between two stubborn narrators who each want the other to suffer more for the sake of the plot. At higher counts, the game shifts toward collaborative storytelling with room for rivalries to simmer. The guests’ abilities tend to scale in impact with more players, offering bigger reactions as more voices contribute to the narrative. If you’re chasing the classic “table of doom” vibe with friends, this expansion is a reliable crowd-pleaser. If your group tends to be quieter and more rules-averse, you’ll still get laughs, but the pace might feel a touch labored as everyone finds a comfortable narrative rhythm.
