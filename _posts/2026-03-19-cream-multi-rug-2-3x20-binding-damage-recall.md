### Cleaning and care (the boring-but-important bit)
Cleaning a cream rug is a bit like handling a whiteboard marker in a server room: you want to avoid permanent marks, yet a little cleanup goes a long way. Vacuum regularly, of course, but avoid aggressive agitation on the binding edge. For spills, blot—don’t rub—and use a mild detergent solution if you must. Test on a small corner first. The goal is to preserve the cream tone while preventing color bleed into the multi-tone pattern. A gentle approach beats a DIY dye job every time.

For those who love a good hack, a simple repair option is to re-seal the binding edge with a color-matching thread or a seam grip that’s designed for rug edges. It’s not a professional fix, but it can extend the rug’s life and keep the edge looking neat between bigger projects.
