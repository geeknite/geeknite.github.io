## Authenticity and variants: How to tell the real thing from a clever clone
As with many vintage card decks, fakes and reproductions exist. Here are a few guidelines to help you spot a genuine 1984 LA edition:
- Back design: The red and blue back variants should share a consistent pattern, with the Olympic spirit implied but not explicitly licensed on every element. If the back says “Olympic Games 1984” in a font reminiscent of early 80s sports branding, you may be looking at a legitimate variant—or a clever counterfeit. Compare the alignment and the stock texture to known photographs or reference decks.
- Stock, finish, and handling: Vintage Bicycle stock from this era has a distinct linen texture, and the gloss on the back is premium without appearing cheap or overly slick. Fakes often use a cheaper gloss that scratches easily or a stock that feels “too smooth.” A gentle bend or a subtle feathering along the edge should feel organic, not robotic.
- Tuck and print quality: Small misalignment on the tuck, slightly imperfect corners, or a misprinted edge card can hint at a non-authentic run. Real vintage sets show consistent typography, correct spacing, and robust color fidelity on the tuck art.
- Serial cues: Some legitimate runs included subtle serial or batch marks on the tuck or inside the card flap. While not universal, a quick comparison against documented print characteristics can save you from a rash of counterfeit fear.

## How to take care of your vintage two-deck set
Caring for this deck is about humidity, temperature, and gentle handling. Here are a few practical tips:
- Store in a cool, dry place: Aim for a stable environment away from sunlight. UV light is a subtle thief that will yellow the tuck over decades.
- Use protective sleeves if you want to display: Some collectors slip the two-deck set into a clear Ultra Pro sleeve for display while keeping it in a tasteful display case.
- Avoid moisture: Card stock can warp if exposed to humidity. A small dehumidifier in the display area is a good investment if you’re serious.
- Play with care: If you actually intend to play with these cards, consider using a modern protective coating or a dress-down deck for games and keep the Olympic set for display or occasional, low-stakes games.
