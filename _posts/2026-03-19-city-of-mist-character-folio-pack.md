## What is in the Folio Pack and who is it for?

City of Mist is a game about ordinary people stepping into extraordinary roles. The Folio Pack is designed to help you manage the two pillars of the game: the Masks that characters wear and the relationships they weave with the city and its secrets. The pack typically includes a set of color-printed character folios, spend-it-now type sheets for Powers, Identity cards, a quick-reference hero sheet, a few clean blank sheets for new players, and a sticker/label sheet to customize identities and liens. There is also usually a small fold-out GM sheet to help the Keeper outline Scenes and Clues on the fly.

In practice, this is a tool for two types of players: the meticulous min-maxer who wants every detail to line up with a character arc and the GM who wants to keep the table from turning into a recycling bin full of half-filled doodles. The Folio Pack shines when you are gaming in a busy cafe, a convention hall, or your apartment where the lights dim and the neon flickers—where you need a portable, stylish, and durable way to hold a concept in your hands while the city holds you in its grip.

## The sheets: what each piece is really for

### Character Folios
Each folio is a compact, fillable sheet built around a central concept: who are you when you wear a Mask? The folios usually include fields for the character name, real identity, the Mask, the City of Mist tagbook, Powers, and Scenes. The formatting is designed to encourage players to document the moment when a clue flips a scene and their identity becomes as unstable as a late-night elevator ride. The content is flexible enough to handle new players who are still learning the exact flavor of a given Mask and veterans who have a dozen campaigns behind them.

### Quick Reference and Stats Cards
City of Mist is a game of narrative economy. The quick-reference cards condense the most frequently used numbers, the key tags, and the essential mechanics into bite-sized chips you can slide into a pocket. These are not a replacement for reading the core book; they are a memory jogger and a status monitor. You can use them to decide when to pivot a scene, when to escalate a conflict, or when to cash in a few clues for a larger reveal.

### Identity Labels and Clue Sheets
To keep the city feeling alive, you need to mark relationships between characters and their world. Identity labels and clue sheets give you tactile ways to manage the web of alliances, rivalries, and secrets that feed the fiction. You can write clues, tag them with a color or icon, and then trade them back and forth during sessions as the story evolves. This is where the Folio Pack earns its keep: you can rearrange connections on the fly with physical labels rather than scrolling through a dozen digital notes while a Neon Detective monologue plays in the background.

### Sticker Sheet and Laminated Tabs
Stickers are not optional in a city of legends. They let you annotate sheets quickly for in-play tracking. Laminated tabs also help you mark sections easily—Power, Identity, Clues—so a table of players can move with a shared sense of rhythm. It is not just glitz; it reduces downtime and helps non-native readers understand where to find things during tense moments.

### GM Fold-Out Sheet (optional)
If the Folio Pack includes a fold-out sheet for the Keeper, consider it your battle plan. This sheet helps you map Scenes, Clues, and the arcs that tie two or more Masks into a single city-wide mystery. A good GM sheet keeps you from collapsing into the pit of improv and instead helps you weave a cohesive story that still allows players to improvise freely when their Masks collide with the city’s contradictions.


## How to fill and use the folios without breaking the vibe

City of Mist thrives on mood, tone, and the interplay between identity and city power. The folios should be filled with intent, not just data fields. Here are some practical approaches to getting the most from the Folio Pack:

- Start with the Mask first. The Mask is the lens through which the story unfolds. Write down its core trait, the city-locale tie it uses, and a single line of aspiration. This helps you anchor who the character wants to become when the story reveals a new secret.
- Define the identity arcs. Real identities are tools for tension. Use the Identity labels to map relationships with the city power players, other Masks, and the NPCs who populate the Map of Mist. The goal is not to have a long cluster of names; the goal is to have a web you can pull threads from.
- Use the Power cards as flashpoints. The Powers should be described in terms of how they affect scenes, not as pure numbers. A good Power line includes a thematic edge (thematic consequence), a possible complication, and a cue for a memorable reveal.
- Clues as narrative currency. The clue sheets should be used to create triggers for Scenes. Each clue is a list item that can prompt a Scene, a twist, or an ally reveal. Keep a running list of how many clues you have and how many Scenes you expect to unlock.
- Keep track of relationships. City of Mist is about the people behind the masks. The Liens and Relationships sheets are the social fabric of your game. Use color-coded relationships to visualize alliances, rivalries, and betrayals.

If you are new to the system, the folio pack acts as training wheels for your brain’s muscle memory about where to look for the mechanic you need. If you are a veteran, it acts as a reminder that every revelation should have a tangible footprint on the table—the character's papers, the city’s map, and a spectral hint that something bigger is waiting in the wings.

## Critiques, caveats, and how to mitigate them

No product is perfect, especially for a game as idiosyncratic as City of Mist. Here are a few caveats and practical workarounds:

- The artwork may not align with every player’s taste. If you want a more minimalistic aesthetic, you may want to switch to the basic folio templates and skip some of the more elaborate iconography. Mitigation: treat the sheet as a scaffold and decorate it with your own art or stickers.
- The format may take some getting used to for new players. A lot of the game is narrative, not numeric, and the folio design leans into that. Mitigation: pair the folio pack with a short, rule-light primer that explains Masks, Powers, and Lenses in a few minutes.
- It is not a substitute for the core rulebook. It is a companion. Make sure your group has the core book or a reliable digital reference on hand for edge cases and house rules. Mitigation: designate a single reference person who keeps the group aligned on the essential mechanics.
