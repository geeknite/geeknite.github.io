## Packaging, Grading, and Preservation Tips
For those of you who want to guard a No. 044 for decades (or at least until your grandkids discover the joys of binder archaeology), here are practical tips:
- Handling: Always wash hands before touching cards. It’s not superstition; it’s science. Oils ruin corners and can cause patina that some collectors adore and others shudder at.
- Sleeves and top-loaders: Invest in high-quality sleeves and top-loaders. A misplaced sleeve can scratch the gloss, and in the long run, you’ll thank yourself when a future buyer asks about the edition and you can say, with pride, “no skippers on this one.”
- Storage: A cool, dry, dark environment is ideal. Heat and humidity are your arch-nemeses; they’re the distant cousins of the card that would rather live in a closet dungeon than in a sunlit display case.
- Grading expectations: If you’re chasing a high grade, be aware that older cards may suffer from subtle surface wear that isn’t obvious at a casual glance.
