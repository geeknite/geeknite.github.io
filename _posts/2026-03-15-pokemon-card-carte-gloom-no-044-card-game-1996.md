## Packaging, Grading, and Preservation Tips
For those of you who want to keep a No. 044 in mint condition for decades (or at least until your grandkids realize they can print a better version on a 3D printer), here are practical tips:
- Handling: Always wash hands before touching cards. It’s not superstition; it’s science. Oils ruin corners and can cause a subtle, unavoidable wave of patina that some collectors love and others shudder at.
- Sleeves and top-loaders: Invest in high-quality sleeves and top-loaders. A mislaid sleeve can scratch the gloss, and in the long run, you’ll thank yourself for the extra protection when a future buyer asks about the edition and you can say, with pride, “no skippers on this one.”
- Storage: A cool, dry, dark environment is ideal. Heat and humidity are not your friends; they’re the long-lost cousins of the card that would rather live in a dungeon of your closet than in a sunlit display case.
- Grading expectations: If you’re chasing a high grade, be aware that older cards may suffer from subtle surface wear that isn’t always obvious during casual inspection. A professional grader can help, but the real value in these is the story you’ll tell while showing your binder to a friend who asks, “What on earth is No. 044?”
