## Care and Maintenance: Keeping Your Chips in Top Form
Chips and acrylic cases aren’t invincible, but they are remarkably forgiving if you treat them with a modicum of respect. Here are maintenance tips that will extend the life of your set without requiring a degree from the Institute of Chip Technology:

- Cleaning: Wipe chips with a soft microfiber cloth. If you notice residue or oils from hands, a tiny dab of mild soap and water on the cloth should do. Never submerge the chips in water; the edge spots may wear and the inlays can take on an odd glow you won’t like at the table.
- Storage: Keep the acrylic case away from direct sunlight. Prolonged sun exposure can cause colors to fade or veneer the plastic with micro-fogging over time. The case’s latch should remain closed when the set is not in use to prevent dust and curious pets from turning your chips into a crunchy snack.
- Handling: Avoid dropping the case on hard floors. The acrylic, while tough, can crack if you drop it from a fair height. If your table has a corner or a sharp edge, consider a soft cover to cushion the case during transport or sudden moves.
- Chip wear: You’ll notice edges can wear slightly after repeated shuffles and heavy use. It’s normal. If you’re particular about uniform wear, rotate chips between denominations occasionally to keep aesthetics consistent across the set.

This is the kind of practical care that keeps your gear looking sharp and your plays looking sharp too.
