## Mechanics and gameplay basics
### The core rules, simplified
- Build the tower by placing blocks in layers of three, alternating direction each level. This is familiar territory if you have played Jenga before.
- On your turn, you must remove a block from anywhere below the highest completed level and place it on the top, rebalancing the tower as you go.
- You can only remove blocks from those that can be slid out without toppling the entire stack. Movement becomes a delicate dance of patience and finger dexterity.
- The player who makes the tower tumble loses the round. Or, if you decide to go full BBQ mode, the last person to stop the tower from collapsing wins a bragging rights trophy and a ceremonial high-five from the party universe.

## Play styles and party scenarios
### Classic alternating play
This is the pure method: remove a block, add to the top, repeat until disaster. It’s a test of steady hands and even steadier nerves. It’s also an exercise in dramatic pauses—because the moment you choose the wrong block, the crowd stops breathing. The suspense alone is worth the price of admission.

### Timed chaos
Add a sand timer or a smartphone timer (but please resist the urge to z local authorities for a moment of cinematic speed). The timer forces you to work quickly, trading precision for speed. This variation makes voices louder, bets bigger, and hands sweatier. It’s a recipe for hilarity when the tower trembles under a hurried removal.

### Card and dare rules
Some sets include a deck of challenge cards. When you pull a card, you must perform a silly task before placing the block on top. Tasks range from making a silly face to reciting a geeky quote about physics or starships. It helps keep the energy buoyant and makes the game more of a social ritual than a pure dexterity test.

### Team play
Split into two teams and take turns in a relay-like fashion. Team A removes a block, Team B decides which block to place, and so on. Tension rises as the baton passes around the table; the soundscape becomes a chorus of sniffing, whispered bets, and the occasional triumphant yell when someone performs a perfect block swap.

## Strategy vs chaos: what really matters
### Where to aim your focus
- Block choice: The block you pull can be a game-changer. Avoid blocks near the base unless you want to risk an early collapse. If you’re feeling bold, go for a block from a lower level that is not under much pressure from above. It’s like chess except every piece is a wooden block and the opponent is gravity.
- Top placement: The top block must be placed carefully to maintain balance. Don’t be the hero who places a block and then wobbles the entire tower mid-sentence with a dramatic sigh. Focus on steady, deliberate movement.
- Table dynamics: The surface you’re playing on matters. A wobble or an uneven surface can be the difference between a dramatic victory and a dramatic crash. Mats, tablecloths, or a carpet with a tiny hamster hiding under it can all influence the outcome.

### When to take risks
If you’re the designated jokester, you will likely take more risks than necessary just to entertain the room. The riskier the move, the bigger the payoff in terms of audience laughter. But caution: a crazy move that causes the tower to tumble on your watch can lead to the kind of mockery that outlives your snack choices. You know who you are, and your friends know it too.

## Care and maintenance
### Cleaning and storage
- Wipe blocks with a dry or slightly damp cloth to remove dust. Do not soak blocks; water and wood plus gravity equal a potential disaster, and no one needs that level of chaos in their life.
- Store the set in the supplied box or a similar container to protect from humidity and curious pets who might mistake blocks for chew toys.
- Inspect for cracks or splits after heavy use. If a block looks rough around the edges, consider sanding it lightly or replacing that block to ensure a smoother game experience for all participants.

### Longevity tips
- Rotate players to distribute wear evenly. If you are trying to keep a set pristine, formal rules can be adapted to include a rotation system where each player gets a fixed number of blocks before passing the turn.
- Use a leveling mat underneath to keep the playing surface consistent across all sessions. This reduces the chance of wobble as less stable surfaces can escalate the drama quickly.
