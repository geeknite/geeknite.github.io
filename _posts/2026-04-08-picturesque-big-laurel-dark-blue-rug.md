## Size, Fit, and Placement

Size matters, especially if you’re trying to anchor a seating area without creating a rectangular trap for your furniture. The Picturesque Big Laurel Dark Blue Rug is offered in multiple sizes to adapt to different rooms. The ideal layout typically includes enough floor visible around the rug to frame the seating cluster without making the space feel disjointed. In a larger living room, a bigger version can extend under the front legs of the sofa and armchairs, creating a cohesive zone that invites conversation and cinematic lounging. In smaller rooms, a compact size still offers a visual anchor and can pair with a compact coffee table or a single chair for a cozy reading nook.

Layout tips:
- For a 12 × 14 ft living space, aim for a rug that leaves roughly 18–24 inches of floor on each side of the furniture to maintain balance.
- In a dining room, the rug should extend at least 24 inches beyond the chair when pulled out to seat everyone; otherwise, it feels cramped and inviting guests to slide off the edge of the rug while dining.
- In bedrooms, placing the rug under the bed so that it extends beyond the sides and foot of the bed creates a warm, inviting landing when you rise in the morning.

## Practicalities: Maintenance and Durability

Regular vacuuming keeps dirt from grinding into the fiber; rotating the rug every few months avoids uneven wear. If you spill, blot—do not rub—to avoid matting or color disturbance. For persistent stains, test a small area with a mild detergent solution before applying to a larger patch. Colorfastness is generally reliable with modern synthetic blends, but it never hurts to do a spot test.

A rug pad is a smart investment. It reduces slippage, extends the life of the rug by distributing weight and movement, and improves overall comfort underfoot. If you have kids or pets, a pad helps keep chairs from slipping and scuffing the floor beneath.
