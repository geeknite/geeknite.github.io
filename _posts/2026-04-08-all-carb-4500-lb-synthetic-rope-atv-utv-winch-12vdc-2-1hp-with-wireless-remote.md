### Synthetic rope vs steel cable: what’s the real difference?

The classic debate: synthetic rope or steel cable. The ALL-CARB uses synthetic rope, and there are good reasons for that.

- Weight: Synthetic rope is lighter. You’ll notice the difference when you’re loading, carrying, or replacing rope between sessions.
- Safety: Synthetic rope floats, and it’s less prone to dangerous recoil if it snaps under tension (though you should never test that theory on a sunny day in your driveway).
- Handling: It’s easier on your hands and requires less maintenance than steel, and you won’t get rocked by a shower of metal splinters when you mess up a wrap.
- Longevity: If properly cared for, synthetic rope can offer excellent longevity, but it does require some rope care discipline (more on that in the maintenance section).

That said, steel cables have their place—especially when you’re chasing heavy-duty industrial use or extreme-duty winching in harsh environments. If you’re shopping within the ALL-CARB line, the synthetic rope is the pragmatic choice for the majority of ATV/UTV users who want a balance of performance and ease of use.
