## Care and Maintenance

### Cleaning routines and stain management

Caring for a raw wool rug requires a touch more mindfulness than a synthetics mat. Regular vacuuming with a brush attachment helps remove loose fibers and prevent matting. Avoid aggressive suction that could pull at the weave. For spills, the classic rule applies: blot, do not rub. Use a clean cloth to absorb as much liquid as possible, then treat with a mild wool-safe cleaner if necessary. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness. For larger spots or stubborn stains, consider professional cleaning to preserve the integrity of the fibers and weave. Spreading a little baking soda on odors before vacuuming can help keep the space smelling fresh without harsh chemicals.

### Maintenance tips to extend life

Rotate the rug periodically to ensure even wear, especially in areas with concentrated traffic. Use a rug pad to reduce slipping and cushion foot traffic. If you have pets, place protective covers or designate a pet-friendly zone to minimize claw damage and shedding. The more you invest in a basic maintenance routine, the longer your rug will carry that warm, artisanal glow through the decades.
