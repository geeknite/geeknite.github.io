## The Batman Miniature Game in a Nutshell

Batman Miniature Game is a skirmish-level miniature game from Knight Models. It focuses on tight, cinematic engagements rather than sprawling force-on-force battles. You’ll build a small squad of heroes or villains, select a scenario, and then spend a game mastering the moment: one heroic dash across a rooftop, one wrong turn at the alley mouth, and one dramatic encounter with a villain who is basically a walking punchline made solid plastic.

The system leans on a hybrid of stat cards, activation controls, and dice-based combat. Characters have unique abilities, equipment options, and special rules that push you to think in cinematic terms: who is in the best position for a flanking attack, who is about to trigger a plot-based effect, and who can soak a hit because they are built to be a durable knife-twisting nuisance. The art direction is clear: Gotham is a living, breathing noir comic world, full of neon, fog, and a dash of pink toxin when you least expect it.

### Why the Laugh matters in BMG

Expect to see him used as a backline menace or a front-row disruptor, depending on your list and your opponents’ patience for mind games.

## Meet The Batman Who Laughs

### The Sculpt and Presence on the Table

The Laugh’s miniature is designed to be unnerving in a way that only a villain who smiles while planning a catastrophe can be. The cloak, the mask, the grin—everything is exaggerated just enough to read well from a distance, which is important when you’re telling a story across a 24x36 inch board. The sculpts capture a mid-flight moment: a tilt of the head, a finger to the lips as if to say, “shh, your doom is about to arrive.” He’s the centerpiece in any showcase moment, and that isn’t just flavor; it’s board presence. You’ll likely want to mount him on a slightly larger base to emphasize his looming, almost theatrical scale on the table.

### The Base, Assembly, and Construction Considerations

Expect a couple of small metal or plastic parts that need reasonable trimming and fit. If you’re a painter who likes crisp edges, you’ll have a field day with the Laugh: the cloak edges present a great surface to practice edge highlighting, and the face – while monstrous in expression – benefits from a careful repaint of the grin and eyes. A few hours of work will yield a piece that steals the show when the floodlights hit the table.

### Painting Tips: Getting the Laugh Right

- Start with a black primer to give you a deep base. Batman colors look great when the shadows are strong, and the Laugh’s look benefits from that cinematic contrast. 
- Use a slightly desaturated purple for cloak tones. The Laugh isn’t cartoonish; he’s ominous.
- Flesh tones should be cold and pale; think “dead color with a hint of menace” rather than warm living flesh.
- The toxin effect (green glow) can be achieved with a glaze of green over a teal base and a final edge highlight with lime green to lend a radioactive vibe.
- Metal accoutrements should pop with a dull chrome or gunmetal, not shiny mirror-like chrome. Gotham doesn’t do showroom gloss well; it does moody reality.
- Finishing touches are the eyes and the teeth. A tiny white dot on each eye makes him feel alive on the table; a tiny dry brush of off-white on the teeth creates a jawline that reads at tabletop distances.

If you’re painting to tie into a narrative scenario, you can lean into the toxin motif. A glow around the weapon’s mouth, a subtle bone-white skull motif on the cloak, or even a splash of toxic green on the base can tie the figure into your story arc beautifully.

### Scale and Display Considerations

BMG miniatures tend to be around the 28–32mm range, with some variation by sculpt. The Laugh is a showpiece, so you’ll probably want to assign a bit more display time to him if you’re planning a showpiece board or a themed night. If you’re playing with a painting club or streaming your games, the Laugh’s color choices—green glow, dark purples, and black fabrics—will photograph nicely and translate well on camera.

## How The Laugh Plays: Abilities, Tactics, and Tinkering

The strength of Batman Who Laughs in BMG isn’t just raw power; it’s how his toolbox changes the tempo of a match. He has a suite of abilities that reward aggressive positioning and smart counterplay. He invites you to push forward, but he punishes hesitation with cunning, misdirection, and a bit of grim humor.

### The Laugh’s Toolbox

- Fear and Influence: A classic Laugh-style power that makes opponents think twice before engaging. He can force sub-optimal engagements or split attention across your opponent’s force.
- Toxin Tricks: A dash of toxin can create a problem for any squad—disrupting order, forcing redraws, or triggering a chain of small, nasty effects. The Laugh makes you play through a fear knot rather than through pure brute force.
- Illusion and Counter-Play: The Laugh’s toolkit often includes moments where an opponent’s plan seems to misfire in a way that looks choreographed. You’ll be rewarded for reading the board and anticipating your foe’s best moves.

The most important thing to remember is that the Laugh rewards bold, cinematic plays. If you’re the kind of player who enjoys turning a corner and delivering a perfect line of narration to your table, you’ll find him deliciously satisfying to pilot.

### Playing Against The Laugh

If you’re on the receiving end, the Laugh can feel like a puzzle with a grin. Your best defense is to control the tempo and avoid giving him easy targets. Deny him space, force him into suboptimal positions, and never forget the narrative payoff of every round: he should feel like a villain who is always one step ahead—until you pull the rug with a clever counterplay.

## Building an Army Around The Laugh: Strategy and List-Building

In BMG, building a list around a character like Batman Who Laughs means balancing thematic flavor with practical efficiency. The Laugh is a potent centerpiece, but you don’t want to rely on him alone. Here are some guiding thoughts for list-building:

- Support is king: Choose 1–2 reliable support pieces that can handle days when the Laugh’s plan requires a second line of defense or a quick flush of additional actions.
- Range and threat management: Integrate at least one ranged specialist to threaten objectives and provide a means of dismissing light targets before they reach the Laugh’s zone of influence.
- Counterplay options: Include at least one option that can break the Laugh’s control or mitigate a dangerous activation. This might be something with a stand-and-deliver defense or a counter-attack capability.
- Theme over power (sometimes): If you’re playing for the story, lean into a narrative arc with a few “could happen on a rooftop near you” moments rather than chasing only the most efficient build. Your table will thank you for it, and your friends will forgive a few punny names for the sake of drama.

Sample starter list around The Laugh could look like:
- Batman Who Laughs (the centerpiece) 
- 1-2 henchmen or minions who can hold board control or create a distraction
- 1 ally with ranged capability or gadget support
- An anchor character who can soak hits and maintain presence on the board

Of course, your mileage will vary with the scenarios you pick and the board layout you’re playing on. The Laugh shines in tight terrain where he can wield the element of surprise, so don’t be afraid to choose or design missions that require you to close the distance and make a statement early.

## Scenarios, Campaigns, and Thematic Play

One of BMG’s strengths is the way it invites narrative play. You can run a quick 2–3 scenario set where Batman and his allies chase or outsmart the Laugh in a Gotham-tinged mini-campaign. You’ll likely want to couple scenario design with a loose campaign track: victory points, specific mission closures, or even “bad guy’s twist” cards that trigger after your best (or worst) moments in a match. The Laugh is especially fun here because he invites you to craft a story where misdirection and momentum swing are the main characters.

For players who enjoy setting the mood, consider a “Court of Owls” style night where your board features towering architecture, long shadows, and a non-stop interplay of fear and cunning. If you’d rather keep to a simpler route, a few linked missions with escalating difficulty and a closing confrontation with a signature hero can provide a satisfying arc without turning into a rules marathon.

## The Verdict: Fun Factor, Fit, and Price Point

If your goal is to capture cinematic Batman moments on the table, the Batman Who Laughs in BMG nails it. He’s a character that invites narrative play and rewards players who plan ahead while remaining flexible enough to pivot when a plan comes apart like a cheap card trick. The miniature’s design is bold and readable at table distance, which matters when your opponent is a few inches away delivering a monologue about fear toxin and moral decay.

From a hobbyist perspective, the Laugh is a fun build-and-paint project with a lot of personality. The cost is reasonable for a centerpiece model, especially if you’re already collecting for BMG or DC skirmish games. The real value comes from the story you can tell with him on the table—the kind of moments that become “that game where the Laugh did something you never saw coming” when your gaming group looks back.

On the downside, there’s a learning curve to both the rules and the narrative phase of the Laugh’s playstyle. If you’re the type who loves pure crunch and minimal theme, you may find that you spend longer thinking about effects than simply rolling dice. That’s a trade-off you’ll find in many narrative-driven skirmish games: more flavor equals more thinking. If you’re okay with that, the Laugh is absolutely worth your time and table space.
