## Assembly and setup

If you enjoy puzzle solving, the assembly of a trampoline is a mild form of home entertainment. The Premium range uses a fairly straightforward process with color-coded parts, stainless clamps, and a multi person approach recommended for the larger sizes. A typical setup goes something like this:

- Clear the area and lay out the frame rails and legs in the correct orientation. Check for sharp edges and ensure no pinched fingers on loose parts.
- Assemble the frame on the ground to form the base rectangle, then stand it up while another person secures the corners. It is not a solo 2 minute job, but it rewards patience with a solid frame.
- Attach the springs in a specific sequence to ensure even tension. This step is the art of controlling physics in your own backyard. Don’t skip or speed through this stage, or your first bounce might feel like an unplanned obstacle course.
- Attach the jumping mat and stretch the springs to fit no matter which size you have chosen. A helper can keep the mat taut while you fix the last few springs.
- Install the enclosure net, ensuring zippers align with the entry direction. It is a good idea to perform a tug test to ensure the net does not come loose mid bounce.
- Finally, attach the safety pad to cover the springs and frame. This pad serves multiple purposes, from aesthetics to mitigating impact on limbs during enthusiastic landings.

The key to a smoother setup is to follow the manual, check all connections, and allow a couple of adults to co-pilot the process. A little planning goes a long way and reduces the number of times you mutter under your breath while your kids do interpretive dance on the legs.

### Pre use safety checks

Before anyone even steps onto the mat, perform a quick safety checklist:

- Ensure the ground is level and free of hard obstacles under the unit. A sudden bounce into a rock is a party ending for the mat and possibly your day.
- Check the enclosure is fully zipped and the latch is secured. A rogue pupil attempting escape mid bounce is not on the agenda.
- Inspect springs for rust, tears, or deformation. Replace any questionable springs to avoid spring snapping drama.
- Confirm the ladder steps are secure and stable. You want confident climbs rather than gymnastic fails before you start.

Regular checks should become a weekend ritual. Think of it as car maintenance but with more laughter and fewer fuel refunds. Your trampoline will thank you with longer life and fewer unplanned flips.

## How to choose the right size for your garden and family

Choosing a size is less about your desire for more bounce and more about the geometry of your garden and the people who will be using it. Here is a practical approach:

- Measure the available space. A 12ft trampoline needs broader clearances around the unit to avoid collisions with fences, trees, or garden ornaments. If space is tight, the 6ft or 8ft options are often the better choice.
- Consider age and number of users. If you have multiple kids or large friends visiting, a larger model allows more than one person to bounce simultaneously without crowding the airspace.
- Think about the weight and bounce profile. Larger models generally provide a more stable, balanced bounce that can be easier to manage for beginners and older children.
- Plan for weather. UK weather means you might leave the unit outdoors during dry spells but may want to cover or store it during wet seasons. A cover and the enclosure help with this plan.

In the end the best choice is the one that fits your garden, matches your family energy, and leaves you with a manageable setup once a fortnight when you tidy up the yard. A bigger unit can be more entertaining, but it also demands a bigger commitment to maintenance and supervision.

## Maintenance and care

The maintenance regimen for a Premium trampoline is not extreme, but it is necessary for long life. Here are some practical tips:

- Regularly inspect the frame and springs for signs of wear and rust. Replace worn springs promptly to avoid sudden tension loss that can throw your bounce out of balance.
- Clean the mat and enclosure with mild soap and water. Harsh chemicals can degrade the mat material and the durability of the net. Rinse and allow to dry fully before storage, especially during damp UK seasons.
- Store the protective padding and ladder when not in use or during extended bad weather. Prolonged exposure to rain and wind can cause padding to degrade and wood to warp if a wooden ladder is used.
- Check the anchor points and ground stakes if you live in a windy area. A little breeze becomes momentum if the unit is not anchored securely, especially in the larger sizes.

The trick is to treat upkeep like a slow burn rather than a sprint. A little weekly check and an annual deep inspection go a long way toward keeping the bounce fun intact year after year.
