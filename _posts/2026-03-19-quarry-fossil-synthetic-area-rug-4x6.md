## Care and Cleaning: Keeping the Fossil Fresh
Care for a synthetic 4x6 rug is usually straightforward. Here are practical steps to maintain its look without turning it into a full-time chore:
- Vacuum regularly to prevent grit from grinding into the fibers. A low-suction setting helps avoid excessive fiber lift.
- Treat stains promptly with a mild detergent mixed with water. Blot—don’t rub— to prevent fiber damage and patina formation.
- For common spills like coffee or juice, dab gently with a clean cloth, apply a small amount of solution, and rinse with a damp cloth. Avoid saturating the backing, which can lead to moisture getting trapped and odors forming.
- If you must spot-clean with a machine, check the manufacturer’s guidance first. Many rugs like this prefer hand-cleaning or spot-clean cycles rather than a full wash.
- Dry flat to prevent the backing from warping or swelling. Avoid direct heat sources like radiators or sunny windows for prolonged periods.

Careful maintenance keeps the fossil pattern legible and the color palette true. The rug’s synthetic fibers aren’t miracle fibers; they are effective workhorses for busy households, but a little love and routine cleaning go a long way toward keeping them looking good.
