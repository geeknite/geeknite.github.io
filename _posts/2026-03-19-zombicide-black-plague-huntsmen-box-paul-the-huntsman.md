## What’s in the Huntsmen Box

The Huntsmen Box is a compact package with a lot of personality. It’s designed to feel like a “golden ticket” expansion—small enough to justify a purchase on a whim but substantial enough to shift your campaign’s momentum. Inside, you’ll typically find:

- Paul the Huntsman miniature (the star of the show) with base and stance options
- A handful of new zombie sculpts (the Huntsmen aren’t shy about showing their teeth)
- A hero sheet and ability tokens that slot neatly into your existing inventory
- A set of narrative tiles or mission cards that thread Paul’s backstory into your campaign
- A few cardboard punch-outs for track boards or token holders

The box doesn’t reinvent the wheel; it polishes the wheel’s edges until they gleam with a mad scientist’s smile. The result is a product that’s both collectible and playable—two things that often don’t get along in the same sentence without someone shouting, “But the minis are adorable!”

### The Miniatures: Quality and Assembly

Paul the Huntsman is a surprisingly dynamic sculpt for something marketed as a “huntmaster” in a plague-infested medieval realm. The design language leans into the dark fairy-tale vibe the Black Plague line has perfected: long coats, edge-of-chaos weaponry, and a silhouette that makes you say, “Yes, I am going to bash undead with a broom handle.” The plastics are sturdy, with good trenching in the armor and confident musculature in the arms—perfect for partial assembly with super glue and a steady hand.

Assembly is not a horror show. The typical process involves detaching the sprue, trimming excess plastic, pinning joints if you’re fancy, and then painting. If you’re a new hobbyist, you’ll find the Huntsman’s components are forgiving enough to allow a few wobbly lines before you paint: the major joints snap into place with a satisfying “click,” and you’ll be rewarded with a heroic pose that somehow captures “I just chased a zombie with a cleaver” energy. For veteran painters, Paul offers enough depth to flex your brushwork skills: subtle leather tones on the belt, weathered metal on the weapon, and a face that can be brought to life with a careful wash.

Pro-level painters will appreciate the sculpt’s negative space—the way pockets in the cloak catch shade and how the weapon’s blade cleanly slices through the light. It’s a mini that refuses to be a wallflower on your tabletop. If you’re wondering about durability: Black Plague minis have historically withstood the occasional mis-toss across a coffee table, which is exactly what you want when your friend insists that “Just one more round.”

### The Role of Paul: The Huntsman on the Table

Paul isn’t just a pretty face with a pretty cleaver. He brings a unique kit of abilities that can alter the pace of a round. His card set typically threads into the broader “hunting” motif: improved movement, focused attacks against certain zombie types, and perhaps a dash of misdirection that makes you think you can pull off a miracle when odds are stacked higher than a goblin’s trivia night.

In practice, Paul’s rules interact with the standard Black Plague mechanics in three main ways: mobility, utility, and risk-reward. Mobility lets him react to undead threats with a speed that can quickly reposition you from the open street to the alleyway’s safety—or so you hope. Utility often includes extra actions or situational bonuses, turning a mediocre turn into something that resembles a heroic montage. The risk-reward element comes from abilities that demand you commit to a risky plan in exchange for a payoff later in the round. It’s a well-balanced approach that makes Paul feel like a real character rather than a generic “extra guy.”

## Gameplay Experience: How Paul Changes the Dance

Zombicide is a game of choreography under pressure. The Huntsmen Box adds a few steps to your dance card that can alter pacing and decision-making across a lot of rounds. Here are the key gameplay shifts to look for when you slot Paul into your party:

- Enhanced mobility: Paul tends to move well in crowded boards. This makes him a natural fit for scavenging routes and ambush strategies where you want to maximize movement efficiency.
- Attack synergy: Paul’s abilities often play well with teams that emphasize coordinated strikes or focus-fire on clusters. If your party has multiple heroes with power moves that trigger when certain conditions are met, Paul acts as the catalyst to align those moves in a single decisive turn.
- Risk-reward calculus: Some of Paul’s options demand a premium resource or a riskier position. The thrill comes from choosing the big swing in a moment when a roar of blood and chop-chop would be more fun than a cautious stroll through the market square.

What does this mean for the table dynamic? It means more plan-ahead moments, more counterplay against the zombie waves, and yes, a few glorious blooms of chaos that remind you you’re playing a game about a plague city that’s basically one bad day away from steam-punk heroism.

### Strategy Tips for Running Paul the Huntsman

- Pair him with a front-line tank hero who can absorb hits while Paul sets up the next big strike.
- Use his mobility to corral zombies into chokepoints, maximizing the value of AoE or high-damage single-target attacks.
- Don’t discount the power of positioning. Zombicide rewards clever cornering—Paul’s toolkit is designed to exploit that, so think multiple steps ahead.
- Remember that less is sometimes more. You don’t always need to go for the biggest swing; a well-timed poke on a weakened zombie can set up a turn so you can chain reactions with your teammates.

Compared to other Zombicide expansions, the Huntsmen Box excels in three areas: thematic cohesion, compactness, and the balance of power.
