## Maintenance and Cleaning

### Routine care: vacuuming and daily upkeep

Plush rugs require regular maintenance to keep their shape and appearance. We recommend vacuuming with a low-speed setting or a gentler brush attachment to avoid pulling on fibers. It’s tempting to brute-force a plush rug with the same suction power you use on a hardwood floor, but the Pearl rewards patience. Slow, methodical passes help prevent tangling and fiber fraying and keep the pile looking lush longer. Rotate your rug every couple of weeks to ensure even wear across the surface. This is especially important if you have a sunlit window—inevitable sun exposure can cause slight shade variation over time, giving your rug a unique, almost artisanal patina.

### Stains and spot cleaning

Spills happen. When they do, blot—not rub—the area to prevent the liquid from seeping deeper into the fibers. A mild detergent mixed with water works well for most stains. For stubborn spots, a gentle stain remover suitable for synthetic fibers can be applied, but test it on a hidden corner first. The key is to treat quickly and patiently, like a boss fight where you’ve got a health bar and a lot of environment to manage. Don’t expect a magic cure, but expect a clean victory with the right routine.

### Deep cleaning options

Machine washing is not typically recommended for plush rugs unless explicitly stated by the manufacturer. The Pearl’s care guidelines favor surface cleaning and professional cleaning for extensive soiling. If you’re shopping for a parent-friendly or pet-friendly solution, a rug pad is your best friend in the prevention department, and lightweight, washable throws can be layered over the rug to manage daily crumbs. In the long run, you’ll save more time and cash on maintenance by avoiding the “full-face wash” approach and sticking to spot treatments and routine vacuuming.

### Odor and humidity control

The rug doesn’t trap odors aggressively, which is great news for spaces that occasionally host spicy meals or late-night snack fests. If you live in a humid climate, make sure the rug is dry before you reintroduce furniture and electronics. A quick sun outside or a fan-driven air-out hour can do wonders for minimizing any musty notes that sometimes creep into plush textiles.
