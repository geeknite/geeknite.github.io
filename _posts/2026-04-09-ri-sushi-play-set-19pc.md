### Design and materials

The RI Sushi Play Set uses plastic that feels reasonably rigid, which is good for kids who drop things and then pretend it didn’t happen. The paint is bright and generally trophy-worthy, though some edges show faint seams where you can tell this was manufactured with typical toy tolerances. ### Safety considerations

As with any play food, there is a small risk for very young kids due to small decorative parts. The set is recommended for ages 3 and up, and parental supervision is wise if your child is under 4 and tends to put things in their mouth. None of the pieces present a choking hazard in the sense of magnets or batteries; still, keep tiny bits away from curious toddlers. Cleaning is simple: a quick wipe with a damp cloth, and you’re good to go. The set does not include magnets or sharp edges, which is a win for a toy with serious sushi swagger.

## Realistic features and playability

One of the delightful aspects of this set is its attempt at realism without turning the kitchen into a plastic hazard zone. The nigiri toppings are simple discs of color, but the way they sit on the rice base feels satisfying. Makis have a slight texture that mimics the idea of seaweed wrapping, and sashimi slices are flat and substantial enough to handle with small fingertips. The soy sauce dish is shallow enough to avoid spills, which is a win if you’re trying to preserve your tablecloth in the middle of a busy pretend restaurant. The chopsticks are lightweight but resilient; they spring back to shape when you let go, which is important for a toy that’s meant to be picked up repeatedly without fracturing.

## Age guidance and accessibility

RI Sushi Play Set 19pc targets kids aged 3 and up, but the toy has broad cross-generational appeal. Preschoolers get fine motor practice and color recognition, while older kids (and adults who still enjoy silly toys) get to tell stories and stage elaborate dining scenes. For children with special needs, the set can be a gentle way to practice turn-taking, language development, and sequencing. The bright colors make it visually stimulating for kids who learn better with high-contrast cues. ## Maintenance and care

Cleaning is straightforward: wipe with warm water and mild soap, then air dry. Do not soak the pieces for long, as prolonged moisture could dull the finish. If you notice the paint wearing, resist the urge to scrub aggressively; instead, use a gentle cloth and, if needed, a tiny amount of plastic-safe paint marker to touch-up small areas. For families who want to extend play value, consider rotating the pieces with other pretend food sets to keep play fresh.
