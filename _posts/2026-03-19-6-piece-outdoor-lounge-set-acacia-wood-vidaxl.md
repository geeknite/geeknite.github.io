## Durability, Weather Resistance, and Longevity

### Wood treatment and maintenance
The wood is treated to resist moisture and minor weather drama. Expect some natural aging if you leave it uncovered for long periods; the grain will deepen and the color may patina a bit. Regular maintenance—oil or sealant every year or two—helps prolong the life. If you live in an area with harsh winters, a cover during the off-season is a wise choice. It’s not a guarantee against all possible catastrophes (like a surprise hailstorm that looks suspiciously like a cartoon meteor), but it’ll help keep the wood from showing wear and tear you don’t want to notice during a Sunday brunch photoshoot.

### Cushion durability
Cushion covers resist outdoor weather well for the price point, but like anything designed for outdoors, prolonged exposure to sun and moisture can lead to fading or wear. Rotate cushions and flip them occasionally to keep wear even. Remove covers for washing according to the label, and avoid harsh cleaners that can degrade fabric fibers. In short: treat them with reasonable care and they’ll keep that “new furniture” vibe longer than you expect.

### Longevity in real-life scenarios
If you treat this set as a daily ride-or-die companion for warm evenings and weekend lounging, it will earn its keep. If you treat it as a “set it and forget it” project that you’ll never maintain, you might find yourself with a slightly pebbled surface and cushions that remember every spilled drink. It’s a balanced setup: not the most rugged option on the market, but a solid, stylish addition for most moderate outdoor lifestyles.

## Quick Maintenance and Longevity Tips
- Protect the wood from direct rain and harsh sun with a breathable outdoor cover when not in use for extended periods. This helps maintain color and stave off the graying effect that sun exposure can trigger.
- Clean cushions with mild soap and water; never soak the cushions for too long. If you have a stubborn stain, treat it promptly and air-dry completely before replacing the covers.
- Use furniture-grade sealant or oil every couple of years to maintain wood integrity and enhance longevity. Always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines for your specific finish.
- Store cushions indoors during heavy storms or during winter to extend their lifespan. If indoor storage isn’t possible, at least keep them under a covered area to fend off excess moisture.
