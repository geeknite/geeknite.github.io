Introduction:
CMONs Zombicide: Black Plague has a shelf of expansions, and the Wulfsburg set is one of the more talked about entries because it injects a brutal winter ambiance and a new wave of tactical decisions into the core loop. If the base game is a brisk stroll through a plague-ridden medieval town, Wulfsburg hurls you into a frostbitten maze with a pack of howling undead wolves and a fortress ready to test your party positioning. In this review, we will unpack what is in the box, how it plays, how it compares to other Zombicide expansions, and who should consider adding it to their collection.

What’s in the box and what it adds:
- 2 survivor miniatures with new campaign specific ability cards
- 4 zombie variants including wolves and iceborn variants that alter threat patterns
- 8 double-sided scenario tiles forming a compact fortress district
- 1 imposing boss figure with frost armor and a multi-phase attack plan
- new faction tokens and encounter markers
- a slim rule insert with three interlinked scenarios

Thematic authenticity and art direction:
Wulfsburg leans into wintery Gothic vibes with icy blues, frost-bitten textures, and a city under a constant wind hiss. The sculpts retain Zombicides chunky, tactile vibe, and the mix of urban terrain with snow-loaded rooftops creates a very tangible sense of space. The new tiles arent just pretty, they reshape the battlefield by introducing chokepoints, alternate routes, and forced sequences that push a players planning from decent to deliberate.

New mechanics and how they feel in play:
The wolves are not simply faster zombies. They bring a distinct movement and spawn system that rewards careful route planning and anticipatory positioning. The iceborn variants introduce movement penalties for stepping onto icy tiles, making players consider every footstep as a resource to be managed rather than an abstract threat. The wolves also interact with doors in novel ways, sometimes forcing a player to think about flanking routes that the base game never asked you to consider.

The boss encounter:
The centerpiece is a boss fight that is not a single, swing-and-win moment. It unfolds in phases, with public and hidden cues guiding your team toward a coordinated plan. The boss punishs hesitation but rewards bold, well-timed plays. It serves as a crescendo for the scenario arc and reminds you that in Zombicide, unity usually beats bravado.

Scenario design and variety:
Three scenarios give you a spectrum of challenges:
- Scenario 1 acts as a gentle onboarding with a compact layout and a couple of strategic perturbations to the map. It teaches you how to leverage chokepoints and how to move as a team.
- Scenario 2 introduces a mid-boss encounter and weather tokens that alter layout dynamics and threat density. It’s where the game starts to ask serious questions about resource allocation and timing.
- Scenario 3 is a siege-style finale. It escalates objective complexity, includes a multi-stage objective chain, and uses weather effects to ramp up tension as the round clock ticks down.

Strategy and player interaction:
Wulfsburg pushes players toward coordinated action, space control, and efficient tile usage. The additional survivor cards give you opportunities for synergy but do not guarantee victory. You still need to discuss routes, targets, and when to pull the trigger on a big trap. If your group thrives on talk, planning, and a little bit of misdirection, Wulfsburg pays off handsomely.

Solo and two-player experiences:
For solo players, Wulfsburg remains a robust challenge. The boss encounters demand precise planning, and you will feel the weight of solo decision making. In two-player games, you can distribute tasks more easily: one player handles scouting and terrain control while the other concentrates on the core fight. At higher player counts, the tactical space expands, and the expansion shines with multiple streams of action.

Storage and setup:
Like most CMON products, setup can be lengthy but is straightforward with organized trays. You will appreciate a dedicated map area and some extra space for tokens if you plan to run through all three scenarios back-to-back. The added components are sturdy, and the tiles interlock with confidence, which is a relief after a long night of frantic zombie-kiting.

Comparison with other expansions:
Wulfsburg stands out for thematic consistency and mechanical depth rather than sheer volume of content. It emphasizes terrain and tactical positioning, making it feel like a natural extension rather than a random assortment of new zombies. If you already own a handful of expansions and enjoy deeper planning and dynamic environments, Wulfsburg is a compelling upgrade.

Final thoughts and recommendations:
- Beginners: Wulfsburg adds rules complexity and strategic depth. It is best enjoyed after you have a handle on the core game and a couple of smaller expansions. Don’t jump in if you are still wrestling with the speed of base Zombicide and simple co-op planning.
- Veterans: If you crave a winter-tinted challenge that still respects the core Zombicide DNA, Wulfsburg is a strong choice. It brings a new tempo that can rejuvenate long-running campaigns without reinventing the wheel.

Value, price, and accessibility:
The box offers a good amount of new content for the price and provides a tangible thematic lift that can carry a campaign for several sessions. It’s not the absolute cheapest expansion, but the depth of play and the cohesion with Black Plague make it worth considering if you’re planning to run longer sessions or a series of games with a consistent group.

House rules and optional modes:
If you want to tailor the challenge, consider a house rule that modifies weather tokens or reduces the number of wolves on a given tile for a more controlled encounter. If you enjoy longer, multi-session campaigns, you could add a milestone system that unlocks additional objectives after every completed scenario, increasing the stakes without adding random chaos.

Thematic fit and final note:
Wulfsburg is a strong, well-realized expansion that respects the core game while injecting a winter atmosphere and fresh tactical challenges. It feels like a natural next step for players who have exhausted a few other expansions and are looking for something that changes the tempo without turning the game into a completely new title.
