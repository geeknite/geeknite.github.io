## Condition, grading, and the NM sweet spot

### Understanding NM and the ex 192 190 motif
Near Mint (NM) is the sweet spot for many collectors who want to balance display aesthetics with practical investment. The 192 190 in the name reads like a hidden code to some, a dream to others. In practical terms, NM means no major defects, crisp corners, clean surfaces, and a gloss level that makes you squint with wonder. The SV4a Shiny Treasure version often exhibits subtle edge wear in the real world; the key is whether those tiny flaws distract more than the collector’s prized holo rainbow. If you can catch a soft rainbow arc under a 60-watt lamp and the card’s surface still shines like a new badge of honor, you’re in NM territory.

### Grading into the modern era
Grading is an art and a small, expensive game of chance. PSA, BGS, andCGC players will point out that the “true NM” is a precise state—no whitening, no major scratches, perfectly centered, no misprints. The SV4a Shiny Treasure ex 192 190 NM card has the potential to hit multiple grading sweet spots if you keep it in a soft sleeve, within a top-loader, and out of the jaws of fate (aka your enthusiastic dog or the humidity of a tropical apartment). The challenge is that hologic surfaces sometimes suffer in transit; micro-scratches can appear that are visible only under macro photography. If you intend to grade, inspect under a light, photograph with macro clarity, and be prepared for the possibility that the card will receive a “9” rather than the coveted “10” from a strict grader. Still, as far as NM goes, this card has a good chance to maintain its luster and fetch a healthy price for the right buyer.
