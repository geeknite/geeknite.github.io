## How Skip-Bo Works: The Basics
The object is simple, but the rush is real.

- Players: 2–6
- Goal: Be the first to empty your stock pile
- Deck and stock: Each player begins with a face-down stock pile; flip the top card to reveal it. The rest of the deck is the draw pile, and there are discard piles for easy access to flexible plays
- Building piles: Build piles that rise from 1 to 12 by playing from either hand or discard piles. A completed 1–12 sequence disappears, and you can start a new pile
- Wild cards: Skip-Bo cards can stand in for any number needed to continue a sequence
- End of turn: When you can’t make a legal move, you draw to five cards if possible, then pass to the next player

## Rules and Variants
The base rules are friendly, but the variants are where you can tailor the night to your crowd.

- Basic rules recap: Empty your stock by building sequences in ascending order, with wilds filling gaps
- Beginner-friendly: Remove one or more building piles for younger players or first-timers
- Speed variant: Introduce a timer, or reduce stock piles to make rounds shorter and punchier
- Team variant: Pair players into teams, sharing discard piles to emphasize cooperation
- Solo variant: A puzzle mode for calmer times when you want a brain teaser rather than a social event
- House rules: Add penalties for long draws or small rewards for impressive saves to keep energy high

## Strategy and Tactics: How to Win More Often
Luck gets you in the door, but strategy buys you the tickets for a winning night.

- Manage the stock pile: Your stock pile is both a ticking clock and a countdown to victory. Watch the top cards as they flip and plan your hand around those reveals
- Use wilds strategically: Don’t burn a wild for a trivial move; save it for the moment you can close a pile or unlock a sequence across multiple piles
- Pace the discards: Your discard piles are not trash; they’re your toolkit. Reorder them as you learn which cards are likely to come next
- Read opponents: Subtly watch how long other players hold certain cards and how quickly their stock piles shrink
- Endgame drama: As you approach victory, every move becomes a chess match in a small, noisy arena
