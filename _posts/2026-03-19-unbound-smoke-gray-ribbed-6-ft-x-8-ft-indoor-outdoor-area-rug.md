## Maintenance and care: how to keep it looking legendary

### Day-to-day care
- Vacuum or shake regularly to remove dirt and hair clumps. A lightweight vacuum head or a broom does wonders here.
- Blot spills immediately with a damp cloth. Don’t rub; blot to avoid fiber distortion.
- Use a mild detergent if needed. Avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the fibers or the UV stability that preserves color over the years.
- For outdoor setups, consider periodic rinsing with a garden hose to remove dried debris; ensure the rug dries fully to prevent mildew.

### Deep cleaning and stain removal
For deeper grime, a gentle rinse with water and air-drying is usually enough. If you’re dealing with stubborn stains from kids or pets, mix water with mild dish soap and apply with a soft brush. Rinse thoroughly and lay flat to dry; avoid direct heat that could warp the backing. If you’ve got a machine-wash option, verify the manufacturer’s guidelines first. Some indoor-outdoor rugs tolerate machine washing, others don’t; in true Geeknite fashion, we salute the sacred ritual of reading the manual before attempting a space-roll of doom.

### Cleaning schedule as a minimalist protocol
- Weekly quick clean: vacuum or shake.
- Monthly deep clean: spot-clean with a mild cleaner and rinse as needed.
- Seasonal refresh: rotate the rug if you’ve got inconsistent foot traffic or if you’re staging a decor moment.

### Stain-specific tips
- Coffee and tea: blot, then a mild detergent solution; rinse and air-dry.
- Red wine: treat promptly with blotting plus a gentle cleaner; avoid rubbing to prevent fiber damage.
- Mud and soil: a quick rinse outdoors plus a brush can lift embedded grime before indoor drying.
