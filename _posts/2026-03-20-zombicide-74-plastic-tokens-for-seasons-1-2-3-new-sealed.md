### Why plastic over cardboard?

Because while cardboard tokens are charming in a quaint way, they are not built for repeated hand-offs across a table. Plastic tokens resist wear and tear, stay legible, and feel satisfying when you flip them to reveal a fresh token underneath. You also avoid the risk of the token rubbing off numbers or symbols after years of use.

## A quick comparison: plastic vs cardboard

- Durability: plastic tokens resist bending and tearing, especially on clumsy table surfaces.
- Legibility: fonts and icons printed on plastic often stay crisp longer than print on cardboard.
- Storage: a lump of plastic tokens can be heavier than cardboard, but you’ll rarely lose a piece.

The main caveat is that plastic tokens can take up more storage space in your game box. If you’re tight on space, you might want to swap only the most-used tokens first.
