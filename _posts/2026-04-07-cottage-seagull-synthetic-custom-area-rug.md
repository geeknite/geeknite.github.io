### Materials and Build: Synthetic that Still Feels Sophisticated

The term synthetic often conjures low-cost vibes, like a bargain-bin sweater that makes you itch in the wrong places. The Cottage Seagull rug aims to debunk that myth. The synthetic fibers are designed to mimic the soft, plush feel of natural fibers with the added benefits of stain resistance, colorfastness, and resilience under daily wear. ### Installation and Layering: Tips to Get It Right

- Floor prep: Ensure the floor is clean and dry before laying the rug. A tidy floor means the rug lies flat and does not trap dust underneath.
- Underlay: Depending on the surface, you may want a non-slip underlay to prevent curling or movement. This is especially helpful on slick floors or in high-traffic areas.
- Layering: A common trend is to layer toppers or smaller accent rugs. If you decide to layer, make sure the sizes complement each other so the top rug remains the focal point.
- Furniture placement: Position the rug so the front legs of the sofa sit on the rug to anchor the seating area. If your furniture layout requires all legs on the rug, that can also work, provided the rug is large enough.

### Care and Cleaning: Easy Does It

Synthetic fibers respond well to routine care. Here is a simple care routine you can implement this weekend without sacrificing your shows.

- Vacuum weekly to keep dirt from embedding in the pile. Use a vacuum with adjustable height to avoid pulling fibers.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Avoid rubbing, or you will push the stain deeper into the rug like a DIY hair gel experiment.
- For stubborn stains, use a mild solution of water and dish soap. Test in a small area first to ensure you do not create a new color palette you never intended.
- For deeper maintenance, consider a professional cleaning every 12-18 months, especially if the rug is in a high-traffic area.
