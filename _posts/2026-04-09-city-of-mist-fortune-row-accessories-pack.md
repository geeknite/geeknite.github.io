## How to Use the Fortune Row Accessories Pack in Your Campaign

### Running Scenes with Fortune Row
In the early scenes, use the cards as mood prompts. A Fate card placed on the table can nudge players toward a clue or an obstacle, without forcing you to narrate every beat. The players will discover that Fortune Row’s environment is as relevant as their own backstories. Use the posters or map fragments to anchor the players in the physical space. A wall poster might mark a district boundary or highlight a rumor, which should in turn spark new questions for the investigators.

Mid-game, bring in the tokens during scenes of pursuit or confrontation. The tokens become tokens of consequence: a policeman’s badge that’s under pressure, a merchant’s business card that reveals a partnership, or a sigil that triggers a special ability in a character’s line of dialogue. You can even convert a token into a “misdirection” mechanic to keep players guessing who to trust.

Late-game, the printable handouts can serve as the final reveal. A single rumor sheet could rewrite a motive, or a street map could reveal a hidden alley where the climactic encounter unfolds. The aim is to keep the action focused on character choices while giving Fortune Row’s atmosphere a tangible presence in the room.
