### Maintenance and Care
Maintenance is the part where rugs either shine or crumble into a pit of despair. The Cambridge rug is straightforward to care for. Regular vacuuming on a low to medium setting keeps most debris at bay without suction pulling up loose fibers. For spills, immediate blotting with a clean cloth is recommended; avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper into the fibers. A gentle spot clean with mild detergent should handle most everyday accidents; for stubborn stains, a professional cleaning is a wise choice rather than a heroic DIY with questionable chemicals.

One handy trick is to rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear. This is especially important if you have a busy hallway leading to the kitchen or a desk that looks out over the living room like the captain’s chair on a starship. Regular rotation helps the rug age gracefully rather than developing a telltale wear patch in the exact spot where your chair wheels love to do their impromptu power slides.

## Installation and Placement Tips
No review is complete without a how-to that saves you from epic furniture shuffles and the quest to align the rug by eye using only your sense of balance and a frustrated measuring tape.

- Measure the space before you buy. A rug that is too small creates a space in the middle of the room that makes furniture look like it is floating in a crime scene. A rug that is too large can overwhelm the space and collide with doorways. A good rule of thumb is to leave about 8 to 12 inches of floor visible around the rug if you want a classic framed look.
- Align furniture with care. For the most cohesive look, have the front legs of the sofa resting on the rug while the back legs remain on the floor. This creates a grounded look and helps the rug register as an intentional design feature rather than a last-minute add-on.
- Use a rug pad. A pad adds cushion, prevents slipping, and extends the life of the rug. Yes, this increases the cost slightly, but it is cheaper than an accidental new coffee table because the rug slid into the coffee table and created a gravity-defying catastrophe.
- Consider lighting. Greys read differently under natural light versus artificial lighting. If your room is gloomy, the rug will lean towards charcoal; if your space has warm tungsten lighting, you will get that inviting graphite vibe. In either case, you end up with a room that looks thoughtful rather than one that looks like a stock photo from a catalog.
