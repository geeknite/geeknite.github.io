How to use this pack at the table: quick-start tips

- Pre-session ritual: print the hero sheets a day ahead, then laminate the pages you know your players will reuse. You’ll be surprised how much time you’ll save when a sheet doesn’t require hunting for a pen every other round.
- Character introductions: start with a short roleplay snippet using the sheet’s prompts. Let the players describe their mask, their mythos, and how they carry a personal secret into a public crowd. This helps everyone connect to the sheet before rolling dice.
- Use the location sheets to build scenes around Fortune Row’s vantage points: the nightclub with a hidden back room, the alley where a deal goes wrong, or the arcade that glows with a truth no one wants to admit.
- Pull the villain sheets into focus by giving the group a single mystery that ties three locations together. The sheet’s format makes it easy to track clues, red herrings, and the villain’s plan without scraping the bottom of the barrel for plot hooks.
- Mentor and ally: rotate in a mentor or ally sheet mid-campaign to shake up dynamics. A new ally can push the heroes toward a different angle of the mystery and refresh the party’s approach to problem-solving.
