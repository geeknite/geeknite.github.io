## Space Planning and Installation Considerations
A pod is not a purchase you can tuck into any corner and pretend you did your due diligence. It requires space planning and some setup work. Here are practical pointers to help you avoid installation drama:

- Footprint and clearance: Expect a footprint around a couple of square meters with space for door swing and chair movement. Measure your room last and then add a little more space for people to pass by without performing a yoga routine to avoid collision.
- Power and data: You will need a dedicated power outlet and potentially network drops for video devices. Confirm your building's electrical capacity and plan for circuits that won t trip during a full day of meetings.
- HVAC considerations: The pod should not be placed directly on top of a supply vent or under a heavy draft from a floor vent. A bit of air movement is fine, but you do not want the pod to double as a wind tunnel during winter.
- Aesthetics and alignment: The pod looks best when centered in the room or aligned with a wall that provides easy access to a door. Consistency with furniture layout tends to improve movement flow and reduces the risk of accidental bumps during busy days.
- Installation timeline: Depending on the complexity and the configuration you choose, installation can range from a few hours to a day. If you need it in a rush, a bare bones configuration can be deployed faster but might miss a few niceties.

For teams upgrading a room with a pod, it helps to map a few common use cases ahead of time. Create a short checklist for typical meetings and test run a few scenarios to verify that people can unclip from their day and step into the pod without a ceremony or a lot of rearranging.

## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Longevity
Keeping a pod clean is easier than scrubbing a carpeted hallway after a group project. The interior surfaces are chosen for durability and ease of cleaning. You will want to wipe down the surfaces occasionally with a mild cleaning solution, avoid abrasive pads, and keep the glass free of smudges. A micro fiber cloth in one pocket and a spare roll of microfiber in another is not excessive; it s practical, and it makes you look prepared rather than chaotic.

Ventilation components should be checked per your maintenance schedule. Dust buildup can reduce efficiency, so a quick clean of any intake grills and the interior surfaces is enough to keep things running smoothly. If you opted for a HEPA filter, be mindful of filter replacement intervals. A good rule of thumb is to replace or clean filters as recommended by the manufacturer to preserve air quality and ensure the pod remains a comfortable refuge rather than a stale cave.
