- Thematic alignment: Each character archetype aligns with common noir tropes—fortune, risk, secrecy, movement, leverage, and city-wide awareness. Placing them on the table alongside a scenario map encourages players to lean into character, not just dice rolls.
- Tactical use: While the figures themselves don’t replace tokens or effect cards, they can be used to map the dramatic beats of scenes. For example, The Gambler can anchor a high-stakes encounter—placing him at the edge of a map to symbolize brinkmanship, while The Spymaster lurks in the shadows to emphasize information warfare.

In short: Fortune Row is less about mechanical edge and more about narrative ambience. If you’re chasing the “feel” of a cinematic noir in your City of Mist sessions, these figurines deliver in spades. If your table prioritizes min-max optimization or a strictly tactical play style, you’ll still benefit from the aesthetic and storytelling cues, but don’t expect them to replace core gameplay components.
