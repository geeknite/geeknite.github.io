## Care and Maintenance
Caring for it is straightforward: vacuum with a gentle setting, rotate every so often to prevent uneven wear, and for spills, dab—not rub—the liquid away with a clean cloth. If you spill wine, coffee, or a heroic amount of tea, we suggest blotting with a towel and using a manufacturer-approved cleaner. The key is to avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the braided yarn. For deeper cleaning, some owners have reported success with a cold-water wash on a delicate cycle, but you should always test on a small corner first.

### Cleaning Steps
- Routine vacuuming on a low setting to preserve the braid crest
- Rotate 180 degrees every 3–6 months to even out wear
- Address spills promptly with blotting and a mild soap solution
- Test cleaners on an inconspicuous spot first to avoid discoloration
- For stubborn spots, gently dab with a mixture of water and mild detergent; avoid scrubbing, which can loosen the fibers
