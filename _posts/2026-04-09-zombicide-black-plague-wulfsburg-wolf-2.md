## The Wolf 2 Encounter: What Makes It Tick
Wolf 2 is not just a harder version of a boss fight; it’s a whole mini-dungeon within a dungeon. The box introduces a new boss dynamic and a set of rules that change the tempo of the game in meaningful ways. At its core, Wolf 2 emphasizes speed, coordination, and resource management. Here are the big factors you’ll encounter:

- The Wolf Boss card introduces a new set of thresholds that trigger stronger attacks or additional effects as the game progresses. You’ll need to manage your survivors’ cooldowns and the board’s threats more carefully than in the base game. 
- Wolf mechanics rely on ambush tactics. Wolves can move quickly through certain terrain, and Wolf 2 often punishes players who stay put in exposed areas. Expect more forced movements, tighter paths, and a few surprises that crop up when you think you’ve got the most efficient route planned.
- A new “Howl” mechanic that periodically resets certain benefits or damages, nudging players toward a cyclical rhythm of risk and relief. If you find yourself staring down a corridor with a chorus of howls echoing behind you, you’re probably in Wolf 2 territory.
- The synergy between wolves and terrain invites you to think in three dimensions. Elevation, walls, open spaces, and narrow hallways become part of the strategic calculus rather than mere scenery. It’s good news for players who enjoy tactical positioning and bad news for those who prefer brute-force dice rolls without planning.

The design intent is clear: Wolf 2 isn’t a mere obstacle to overcome; it’s a test of whether your party can adapt its habits mid-air, like a tight-rope walk performed in a zombie apocalypse with frostbite and a cold ale shortage.

### Wolf 2 in action: a typical turn sequence
A rough sketch of how a typical Wolf 2 turn might unfold:
- Wolves surge into a hallway, threatening from one or two angles.
- The Wolf Boss uses a multi-turn ability to alter the battlefield, forcing a retreat or a fishing-for-loot moment where you gamble on a risky push.

## Strategy and Tactics: Surviving Wolf 2
If you want tactical tips for Wolf 2, here are a few guidelines that tend to work in most groups. They’re not foolproof, but they do tend to produce results if your crew communicates well and respects the terrain.

- Coordinate with elevation: Use higher ground to gain line-of-sight advantages while keeping your most vulnerable survivors behind cover. Elevation isn’t just pretty art; it’s a mechanical lever you can pull when the wolves close in.
- Split the team strategically: Don’t funnel all your best fighters into one corridor. Consider sending a ranged survivor to a flank that can harry Wolf 2 while your melee team holds the line. The goal is to create two or more simultaneous threats rather than a single concentrated one.
- Reserve loot for the right moment: There’s often a moment when you can grab a powerful weapon or a healing item, but you’ll need to delay long enough to avoid a wolf-led ambush in the next turn. Timing is everything.
- Manage Howl triggers: Howl is a tempo-shift mechanic; you’ll need to choose when to push forward and when to hold. If you unleash a push right after a Howl, you may regret it. The opposite can be true as well, so watch the clock.
- Protect your healers: Your clerics or healers will probably be the first target of Wolf 2 if you aren’t careful. Ensure they have a route to safety and a backup plan if you lose the line-of-sight advantage you counted on.

### A sample squad build
- The frontliner (tank) who can absorb the first blow and hold a choke point.
- A ranged damage dealer who can threaten Wolf 2 from a safe distance while also providing alternate angles for the wolves.
- A healer or support who can keep the front line alive through the boss’s punishing sequences.
- A mobility specialist who can slip behind enemy lines or recover loot without becoming ambush bait.

With a squad like this, you’re not guaranteed victory, but you’ll find Wolf 2 to be a test of your group’s communication and improvisation. The design encourages you to adapt, re-evaluate, and sometimes retreat in an orderly fashion rather than charging recklessly into a well-planned ambush.
