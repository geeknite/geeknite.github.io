## What is The Game: Strategy Card Game? (A quick refresher)
In the base experience, players cooperate to play cards in a way that minimizes the total penalty accumulated by failed plays. The board is more of a social contract than a literal map; attention must be paid to what cards have already left your hand and what remains dangerous to hold onto. The tension peaks as the deck thins, forcing players to make snap judgments that feel almost like real life—except in real life you’d probably just text your boss after you push too hard.

The Expansion Pack leans into that feeling, not by dictating every move but by broadening the decision space. It introduces new card types, a new risk/reward economy, and rules that occasionally tempt you to break your own ethos about “don’t reveal your hand” for a moment of glorious shared chaos.

### New Cards and Mechanics
Here’s where the expansion delights and occasionally confuses—in the best possible way.

- Momentum Tracks: A new tempo metric that rewards players who string together clean plays. You’ll feel like a card-counting supervillain in a casino, except the casino is your living room and your audience is a cat that judges your life choices.
- Risky Gambits: Some cards offer a powerful payoff if you’re willing to pay a price on the next turn. It’s the board game version of posting a questionable selfie on a Friday night and praying your friends don’t notice your stamina.
- Twist Cards: These cards shuffle the entire session's expectations, forcing players to adapt on the fly. It’s like a plot twist in a heist movie—except you’re all wearing mismatched socks and pretending to be adults.
- Ally Cards: A few cards grant temporary teammates or “assist” effects that encourage cooperative play and situational heroics. It’s a reminder that in tabletop heists, you often do better when you pretend you’re not the most ambitious person in the room.

## Strategy with a Smile: Tips and Tricks
Here are a handful of practical tips that can help you enjoy the expansion without turning your table into a debate club.

- Communicate minimal signals: you don’t need to reveal all card counts, but subtle hints about intentions can save your group from a catastrophic misplay.
- Time your risk-taking: do not start a momentum sprint on a turn where you only have a single safe option remaining. Momentum loves momentum, not misplayed cards.
- Use allies as social currency: allies are great for enabling dramatic comebacks or delivering a hard-wought victory after a protracted struggle.
- Practice makes better: run a few quick two-player games to learn how the momentum tokens shift in your favor or against you.
