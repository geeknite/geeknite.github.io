## Care and Maintenance: Not a Monster, Just a Gentle Giant
Care for a viscose wool rug is not an extreme sport; it’s more like a gentle cardio workout with a broom. Here are practical tips for longevity without turning your hobby into a full-blown obsession:

- Regular vacuuming: Use a low-suction setting and minimize agitation to preserve the pile. Avoid beater brushes that could tug on the fibers. Think of it as a data-cleaning routine for your floor data; gentle, consistent, and thorough.
- Spot cleaning: Blot spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Use a mild detergent solution if necessary, and test it on a small, inconspicuous area first. The goal is to lift stains without turning the rug into a DIY chemistry experiment.
- Sun exposure: Prolonged sun can fade viscose. If your window faces the rug directly during peak sun hours, consider UV-blocking curtains or rotating the rug’s orientation periodically to distribute wear evenly.
- Professional cleaning: A yearly professional cleaning can help maintain the sheen and fibers. This is not optional magic; it’s the spa day your rug deserves to remove years of everyday grime and the occasional pizza grease from late-night gaming sessions.

Pro tip: If you have pets, brisk tail-wagging and the occasional muddy paw will happen. The combination of wool and viscose handles light traffic well, but a stubborn stain is easier to treat sooner rather than later. The good news is, this rug buys you extra time to pretend you are a responsible adult who reads care labels—while secretly wishing you could hire a cleaning robot to finish the job.

## FAQs: Quick Answers for the Curious Shopper
Q: Is this rug suitable for underfloor heating? 
A: Viscose wool blends can tolerate moderate heat, but check with your manufacturer and ensure your underfloor heating is compatible with textiles of this type. If in doubt, place a breathable underlay and keep the heat on the sensible side.

Q: How often should I rotate the rug? 
A: If you’re aiming for even wear and consistent lighting, rotate every 6–12 months. If your room has strong sun in one corner, consider rotation to balance fading across the surface.

Q: Can I clean this rug at home? 
A: Light cleaning and spot treatment can be done at home. For deeper cleansing, rely on a professional service to protect fibers and sheen.
