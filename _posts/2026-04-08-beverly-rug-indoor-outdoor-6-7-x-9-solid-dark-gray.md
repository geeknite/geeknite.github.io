## Cleaning and Maintenance: Keeping It Looking Sharp

Maintenance is where a practical rug earns its gold star. The Beverly rug is designed for easy cleanup. Spills blot up quickly with a dry cloth, followed by a gentle rinse with water if needed. For stubborn stains, a mild soap solution and light scrubbing with a soft-bristle brush will usually do the trick. Rinse and air-dry fully before returning to its spot; dampness can lead to mildew, which is not the vibe you want.

For routine care, a light vacuum with a low-suction setting works well to remove dust and pet hair without roughing up the fibers. If you have a long-haired pet, you’ll appreciate that the tight weave resists tangled fur and minimizes hair entrapment compared to fluffier rugs. Outdoor storage during off-season is straightforward—roll it up, tie it, and stash it in a dry place. It’s not a fashion statement about weatherproof fashion, but it’s a practical solution that keeps the life of the rug intact across the seasons.

Pro tip: if you happen to spill something particularly stubborn (red wine on a beige rug is a nightmare), don’t panic. Treat it as a science problem: identify the stain type, blot, then treat with a mild cleaner. Always test on a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure colorfastness.

## Durability and Weather Resistance: Will It Stand the Test of Time?

Durability is the heartbeat of any indoor/outdoor rug. The Beverly’s fiber blend is designed for longevity in variable climates. In daily use, it resists wear and retains its shape, making it suitable for high-traffic areas and outdoor patios where weather isn’t exactly polite. The color holds up reasonably well in sunny windows and covered porches; you may notice some slight fading after several summers in direct sun, but the rug won’t become a sun-bleached slab overnight.

In wet conditions, the rug dries quickly, reducing the risk of mold or mildew if left outdoors for a short period after rain. If you live in a place with harsh winters or heavy precipitation, bringing the rug indoors during the worst weather is prudent to extend its life. The rug’s binding and edges will hold up to seasonal shifts if you give it occasional attention and avoid yanking it across rough surfaces.
