## Maintenance routines that actually help
Once you commit to a 47-inch foosball table, your maintenance routine can be straightforward and quick. Here are a few practical habits:
- Wipe down the surface after use to prevent sticky residues and fingerprints from taking root.
- Lubricate the rods every so often to maintain smooth action; don’t overdo it, or you’ll be polishing the table for hours instead of playing.
- Check leg levelers monthly or after a few heavy games; ensure they are snug and not inducing wobble.
- Inspect the ball track and goal areas for burrs or misalignments; a minor file or adjustment can improve ball behavior dramatically.
- Keep the area around the table free of clutter to avoid accidental shoves that could unbalance the table or injure a curious bystander.

If you stay on top of these routines, the table will stay in good shape with minimal frustration, making it a friend during those long marathon sessions rather than a constant nag of misalignment and squeaks.
