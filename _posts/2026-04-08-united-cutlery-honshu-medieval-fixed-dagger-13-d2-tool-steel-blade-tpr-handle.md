## Maintenance and safety

### Care routines

D2 steel benefits from routine maintenance. After use, wipe the blade dry and apply a light coat of oil to prevent surface rust. The handle, being TPR, is relatively forgiving; it cleans easily with a damp cloth. Avoid soaking the blade in water for extended periods to preserve its finish and edge alignment. If you notice any rust spots forming, address them early with a light abrasive and oil to extend the life of the tool. The sheath should be kept dry as well; water trapped in a sheath can lead to humidity-induced issues over time. This is not a precision surgical instrument; it is a robust fixed blade that asks for sensible care and a sense of humor.

### Safety reminders

Always handle with care, and respect the edge. This is a real blade, not a prop in a slapstick routine. When drawing and returning to the sheath, keep your other hand away from the cut line and save the dramatic twirls for the stage. If you have kids around or you are teaching beginners, this dagger should be treated like a real tool with a sharp edge. Use proper protective gear and follow basic safety protocols. The weapon also looks fantastic in a display case, but you should avoid letting it tempt you into daily reenactment battles with your roommates. Humor aside, the blunt truth is that fixed blades require the same respect as any other blade of this size and weight.
