Some small form factor boards or laptops may require you to remove the heatsink for fitment. The good news is that the heatsink is modular enough to be installed or removed with a standard set of tools, and it does not block other nearby slots in most motherboards. If you plan to upgrade in the future, you will be glad you used a drive with a robust warranty and a track record for reliability.

Firmware updates are straightforward with the Samsung Magician software on Windows. If you are on Windows, you can use the software to check firmware versions, monitor health data, set up TRIM, and run secure erase when you want to prime the drive for a new OS install. If you are on Linux, you can rely on standard NVMe tools, but you may want to check for firmware updates via the official site and apply them manually if needed.

- Ensure your motherboard BIOS supports the drive s PCIe generation and that PCIe speed is enabled for the M 2 slot
