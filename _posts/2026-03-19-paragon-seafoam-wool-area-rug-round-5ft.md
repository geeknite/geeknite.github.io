## Material, durability, and care
### Wool wonders
The Paragon Seafoam Wool Area Rug is described as a wool piece, and that choice matters. Wool is naturally resilient, flame resistant, and tends to spring back after foot traffic. The elasticity helps the rug resist permanent indentations from chairs and heavy furniture, which is the kind of thing you appreciate after you rearrange the living room for the 12th time this season. Wool also tends to shed less than some synthetic fibers when the pile is properly constructed, though some initial shedding during the first few weeks is not unusual for wool rugs out of the loom. Regular vacuuming will keep this under control.

### Cleaning and maintenance
Caring for a wool rug revolves around a few simple steps: regular vacuuming (on a low setting to avoid pulling fibers), quick spot-cleaning for spills, and periodic professional cleaning for a deep refresh. Wool fibers naturally self-clean to some extent and resist staining better than many synthetic piles, but that doesn’t mean you should treat it like a kitchen towel. If a spill occurs, blot gently with a clean, dry cloth, then use a mild detergent solution if needed and blot again. Avoid harsh solvents that could affect dye consistency. ## Care routine and longevity tips
- Vacuum weekly with a gentle setting to prevent fiber pull. Avoid high-velocity suction that could pull fibers over time.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear across high-traffic areas.
- Blot spills immediately; avoid rubbing, which can push stains deeper into the fibers.
- For deeper cleaning, consider a professional service that specializes in wool rugs. A good professional will know when to use water and detergents and when to leave the fibers dry-fresh.
- Protect from direct sunlight to minimize color fading. If possible, use curtains or blinds to regulate sunlight exposure.
