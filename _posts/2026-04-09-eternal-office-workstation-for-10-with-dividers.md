## Assembly and Setup: Minimal Startup Drama, Maximum Satisfaction
Let’s talk about what it takes to get this thing from box to boardroom. Our experience was a mix of adulting and the occasional “where does this screw go?” moment. The package typically includes:

- Benchtop panels and finish pieces.
- Divider panels and mounting hardware.
- Cable troughs, grommets, and the associated hardware for inside-the-trench routing.

What you actually do:
1) Unbox the components onto a clean, clear floor surface (yes, we’re going to stage this the way you stage a big kitchen remodel, but with more Allen wrenches).
2) Attach the main frame segments end-to-end to form the long bench. The alignment is straightforward thanks to mechanical pegs and clearly labeled parts. If you’re using a drill, go slow and keep the torque moderate; you don’t want to strip a hole in the first five minutes.
3) Install the divider panels between the stations. They’re modular; you can reposition them later if your team grows or your politics shift from “privacy” to “open concept 2.0.”
4) Mount the cable troughs under the bench and slide the grommets into their slots. Run power, network, and a suspiciously large amount of USB-C cables through the trough. Channel management is the unsung hero of any multi-user workstation.
5) Place the benchtop panels, add edge banding if included, and tighten everything down. Do a quick wobble test to ensure there’s no carnival ride effect.
6) Plug in power and test the surface. Stand back and pretend you’re an IT project manager presenting the latest quarterly metrics. If nothing falls apart, you’ve done it right.

Assembly time is certainly longer than a single-desk setup, but it’s designed for teams and facilities crews. ## Cable Management and Power: Keeping the Spaghetti Monster at Bay
In any busy 10-seat setup, cables become a living, breathing creature that sometimes crawls toward the coffee machine. The Eternal workstation’s cable troughs and grommets aim to tame that beast:
- Troughs run along the length, keeping power and data out of sight but within easy reach from each seat.
- Grommets at regular intervals mean you can add or relocate outlets without unscrewing half the bench.
- A well-organized power distribution unit (PDU) or surge protector can live under the bench, so the top remains clean for monitors and laptops. If you’re brave, you can add a small, shared USB-C hub for quick charging after sprints.

The practical upshot is fewer accidental unplugged networks and fewer “where did my charger disappear to” moments. It’s not glamorous, but it’s the kind of feature that saves meetings from devolving into “whose cable is this?” side quests.

## Maintenance, Durability, and Warranty: Keeping the Beast in Check
No piece of furniture is perfect forever, but you can maximize its lifespan with a few good habits:
- Regular cleaning: A quick wipe-down every couple of weeks prevents sticky residue and dust buildup that can degrade finishes and panel edges.
- Inspection schedule: Check fasteners and crossbars every few months. If you’re in a high-traffic environment, you might want to run a light inspection monthly to catch any looseness before it becomes a wobble.
- Protective mats: If your floor is carpeted or prone to scuffing, place mats under the legs to reduce wear. It also makes rolling chairs less slippery, which is a blessing for safety.
- Warranty and parts: Most systems come with a warranty for manufacturing defects and a spare parts kit. The real test is whether the manufacturer can ship you a replacement panel or a small bracket quickly. In a busy office, this is a legitimate service feature, not a mere afterthought.

Value-wise, durability comes at a price, but you’re trading a long-term solution for a reliable space that can accommodate a growing team. If you plan to stay in the same floor layout for several years, the Eternal system is typically a good bet under normal wear and tear.
