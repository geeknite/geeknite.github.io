### Cable management and airflow sanity checks
Airflow is the silent killer (in a good way) if your servers run hot enough to power a small Sucrose factory. The AR3100 supports standard cable-management practices, but the efficiency of airflow isn’t about the rack alone; it’s about how you route cables and where you place mounting hardware. A well-organized rack with blanking panels and neatly routed power cables will keep intake temperatures from turning your data center into a sauna.

- Use blanking panels to minimize rear-channel recirculation and reduce hot spots.
- Route power and data cables neatly in separate channels or conduits.
- Consider a cable-management arm if you’re placing a lot of horizontal patch cables or midplane-heavy devices.
- Keep vents clear of obstructions. If you can’t resist stacking gear to the ceiling, you’re doing it wrong.

Remember: a tidy interior makes life easier for technicians, auditors, and the occasional braggy Slack message about “zero cable spaghetti.”
