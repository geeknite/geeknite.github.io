### What is Tank Duel about, and why rules-only matters
Tank Duel is, at its heart, a duel-based tank combat game. Two players (or teams) vie for the most advantageous position on the battlefield, trading shots, maneuvering around terrain, and exploiting line-of-sight advantages. The Rules Only edition focuses on the rulebook itself: how the core mechanics interact, what the edge cases look like, and where common misreadings tend to creep in. If you\'re the sort who has argued about line-of-sight rulings until the sun goes down, this edition will feel like a relief period. If you prefer variable components that generate drama, you might want to pair this with the full game.

### The layout and the learning curve
The rulebook in this edition is compact but dense. It throws you into the action quickly and then asks you to slow down to study. The clarity jumps out in the diagrams and examples that accompany tricky interactions. It is the rare rules set that rewards rereading; it rewards replays; and it rewards building your own mental map of how a duel should unfold. The diagrams are not just pretty pictures; they are the visual shorthand your mind uses to avoid argument the moment someone says \"line-of-sight.\" If you tolerate a steep learning curve, you will be paid back in the form of crisp, repeatable outcomes and fewer common misreads at the table.

### Movement, distance, and positioning: the heart of the duel
#### Movement and Distance
In Tank Duel, movement is not a minor flavor; it is the entire strategic drumbeat. The rules map movement to a grid or abstract distance and then tie that movement directly to your offensive and defensive options.
- Each activation can move a tank an amount determined by the current speed value and terrain modifiers.
- Positioning matters: a single square or hex can be the difference between a clear shot and a miss that would have hurt a lot more in real life.
- Distance affects accuracy, armor effectiveness, and the potential for rare, satisfying hits.

#### Line of Sight and Terrain
Line of sight (LOS) is the arbiter of most turns. The edition handles LOS with a clean rule set that rewards careful terrain use. Hills, woods, and ruins act as modifiers that can turn a potential cry-out-loud-the-enemy-has-LOSc advantage into a cautious, chess-like standoff. The rules also include practical examples that show how LOS is resolved when there are multiple vehicles in play and when terrain interacts with movement.

### Combat resolution: hitting the target without turning it into a math problem
#### Armor, Damage, and Outcomes
Armor values mitigate damage; the higher the armor, the harder it is to land a successful hit. The Rules Only edition gives you a straightforward damage table and a consistent mechanism for applying hits and armor. The math is not whimsy; it is repeatable and predictable, which is exactly what you want when you are trying to teach players how to read a battlefield rather than play a single hand in a carnival.
