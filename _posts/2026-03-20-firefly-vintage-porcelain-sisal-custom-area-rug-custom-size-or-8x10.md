## Materials and Construction

Sisal is a natural fiber sourced from agave plants. It’s known for durability and a certain rugged elegance, which makes it a good candidate for high-traffic areas or rooms where people actually live and drop things. The Firefly rug uses a tight weave that balances resilience with a soft footprint on your feet—the kind of balance that makes you feel like you’ve officially leveled up in home decor.

Important care note: sisal is moisture-sensitive. You’ll want to avoid heavy spills that sit on the surface, and you’ll definitely want a good rug pad to prevent creeping and to cushion underfoot traffic. Clean spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing, to avoid fiber crush. If you’re dealing with pet messes, act quickly, then consider a professional clean for deeper stains.

## Care and Longevity

Sisal fibers resist wear, which helps protect high-traffic zones like entryways and living rooms with frequent foot traffic. However, because sisal fibers are relatively stiff compared to wool or synthetic fibers, it’s not the best option for rooms with constant chair swivels or a lot of foot-tapping. A rug pad under the Firefly helps preserve the fibers and reduces slipping, which is a feature both your health and your furniture will appreciate.

For maintenance, vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting or a handheld vacuum that won’t pull threads. Avoid beater bars that can fray the material. If a spill occurs, blot immediately with a clean cloth, then treat with a mild cleaning agent recommended by the manufacturer or a professional rug cleaner. Do not saturate; water is the enemy of sisal and can lead to shrinking, warping, or a permanent scent that smells suspiciously like damp socks.
