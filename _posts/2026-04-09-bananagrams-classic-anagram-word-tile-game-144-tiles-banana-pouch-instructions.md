## How to play in a normal night of chaos
Bananagrams is not complicated, but it thrives on tempo. Here is a streamlined guide to the core gameplay:

### Setup
1. Each player grabs a number of tiles from the banana bag. In classic play, the standard start is 15 tiles per player. You can adjust for younger players, shorter sessions, or a brutal drill instructor of a game night.
2. Each player places their tiles on a rack in front of them. No peeking at other players’ tiles unless you want to spark a friendly mutiny.
3. The rest of the tiles sit in the central bag or pool. The central pool is the so-called fuel that will be burning through your attention span in the next few minutes.

### Basic rules
- The first player to use all their tiles in a legal grid shouts Banana! and wins the round. If you are playing with a group that treats Banana as a ceremonial word, you can declare victory based on your own rules, but Banana is the usual call.
- A valid grid means all tiles must be connected by letters, forming horizontal words or vertical words. Diagonals are not allowed unless the designer famously said diagonals are a feature and not a bug, which is not the case here.
- Words must be legitimate; you can challenge a word, and if it fails to appear in standard word lists, it must be amended. This is where the group becomes a democracy and chaos collides with philology.
- If you get stuck, you can play a Dump, returning any number of tiles back to the central pool and drawing the same number anew. This resets your mental weather forecast a bit and creates room for dramatic comebacks.
- Peel: at any time after someone shouts Peel, every player must take one more tile from the central pool. Peels keep the pressure up and the corner of your brain warm with panic.

### The endgame and victory
The round ends when someone has used all their tiles in a valid grid. The speed of your victory depends on how well you can juggle tile distribution, word viability, and the ability to squeeze a long theme word into a tiny grid with your neighbors watching in awe and slight horror.

## Playing styles and strategy tips
Like any lightweight speed game, Bananagrams rewards practice, pattern recognition, and the ability to rearrange a grid with surgical precision. Here are some tips to help you outperform your friends without turning the table into a literal word soup:
- Build flexibly: Start with short, two- to three-letter words that lay out a flexible grid. Don’t commit to a single course too early; you will regret your commitment when the central pool starts to shrink.
- Avoid dead ends: If your grid has too many edges that could become dead ends, you are asking for trouble. Try to connect tiles in ways that keep options open as you add new letters.
- Plan for the long game: If possible, keep a few high-value letters in reserve and pair them with common suffixes or prefixes. This expands your word possibilities while you agonize over what to do with the rest of your tiles.
- Learn tile distribution: Remember which letters tend to cluster and which appear rarely. This helps you anticipate when you might be stuck and plan a better Dump or Peel sequence.
- Watch your opponents: If someone is about to finish, you may want to speed up your grid to keep pace rather than letting the pressure mount for everyone else. The social energy of a banana-powered race is half the fun.
