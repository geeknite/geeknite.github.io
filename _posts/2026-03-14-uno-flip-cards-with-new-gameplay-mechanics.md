## Overview

Uno Flip introduces a two sided deck and a set of action cards that operate on the dark side. The objective remains the same: be the first to discard all your cards. The twist is that you can flip the entire round to a different set of rules and chaos at any moment. The flip is not just a gimmick; it reframes the entire strategic landscape and makes players re evaluate how they read the color wheel.

### Whats new in this edition

- Dual sided cards with light side and dark side personalities.
- New action cards on the dark side that punish or reward depending on your mood.
- A flip mechanic that can swap the entire pile from light to dark and back again.
- Optional wilds that allow color changes on both sides, increasing the likelihood of a wrong color call turned right by luck.

## How the gameplay flows

### Setup

Shuffle the double sided deck, separate the two sides, and determine who will flip first. The rest of the players lean in with anticipation as if they are about to witness a magic trick performed with index cards and a dream.

### Turn structure

Turns follow the light side rules until someone plays a Dark Flip card. Then the pile flips, and the new set of rules apply. The quick change creates a moment of chaos where everything you thought you knew about the round evaporates and you must relearn the color wheel on the fly.

### The flip moment and strategic implications

The flip moment is the heart of Uno Flip. A simple card that used to just pass a color can now trigger a cascade of effects that affect everyone at the table. The dark side often adds extra cards, wild color changes, and the potential to make a single player the hero or villain of the night. The odds shift with every flip and the energy in the room shifts with it.

### Advanced strategies

- Keep track of light and dark color demands. If you have a big hand on the light side, prepare to flip when your opponents have too many cards.
- Use flips to force opponents into suboptimal moves. When possible, time your flips to interrupt a player who is about to claim Uno.
- Use color management to steer the round toward a more favorable outcome. If you see a color that opponents struggle with, aim to push the round that way.
