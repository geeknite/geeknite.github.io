## The Lineup: A Quick Look at Each Game
Here’s how the three titles stack up, and why they’ll either light up your party or become the reason you need a group therapy session after game night.

### Exploding Kittens
Exploding Kittens is the archetypal ‘easy to learn, hard to master’ card game. The objective is simple: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten unless you have a Defuse card. The deck includes a handful of Defuse cards, some “Nope” cards to thwart others, and a handful of zany action cards that tilt the balance in ways you didn’t see coming. The rule set is compact, which makes it ideal for quick rounds between other games or as the main event for a lean, party-sized session.

- Core loop: draw a card, hope your fate isn’t sealed by the next kitten card, use Defuse when you’re in trouble, and hope your neighbor gets eliminated in dramatic fashion.
- The humor meter: off the charts for a group that enjoys meme-level whimsy. The captions on the cards are mostly punny with a dash of “that’s so stupid, I love it.”
- Replayability: high, because every round unfolds differently depending on the Defuse cards you hold and the fate of your opponents.

### Throw Throw Burrito
If Exploding Kittens is a party in a card deck, Throw Throw Burrito is the chaotic physical performance of that party—complete with plush burritos and a high-speed chase for perfect pairs. This game turns your living room into a dodge-em-up arena where you collect sets while simultaneously dodging burrito-flinging moments. It’s loud, it’s silly, and it’s almost impossible to play without one person yelling “I got you!” as a burrito sails past the cat tossers.

- Core loop: collect sets of cards to earn points, while repeatedly dodging or throwing burritos at your friends. There are penalties for miss throws and for getting hit by a burrito, which keeps everyone engaged and constantly scanning the table for chaos opportunities.
- Interaction: through the roof. You’re not just playing your own hand; you’re actively influencing others’ hands and what gets tossed when.
- Spatial considerations: this game requires a bit of space. If your living room doubles as a cardio gym, you’re good; otherwise, create a soft boundary to prevent accidental furniture destruction.

### A Dodgeball Card Game
The included Dodgeball Card Game (let’s assume for a moment it’s an actual product rather than a metaphor) marries tactical card play with the theme of dodgeball. Players set up teams, play action cards to dodge, dodge, and out-maneuver opponents, and try to outscore with smart card management. It’s more about planning and tempo than sprinting around a gym, which makes it a great counterbalance to the frenetic Burrito section of the night.

- Core loop: manage your deck to trigger “throws,” plays to dodge, and cards that defend you from the opposing team’s strategy. It’s a nice change of pace after the unbridled chaos of Burrito.
- Strategy vs luck: you’ll feel the tug of luck early, then sharpen your decisions as rounds progress. The longer play sessions reveal more of the subtleties, which is a nice surprise for a trio marketed as “party games.”
- Group dynamics: the game rewards collaboration and subtle manipulation, but in a lighthearted way that keeps everyone smiling rather than sneering.

## Tips, Tricks, and House Rules
- For Exploding Kittens, treat Defuse cards like tiny time-bombs of safety. Use them strategically; you don’t want to burn them all too early, but you also don’t want to leave your fate in the hands of a coin flip.
- In Throw Throw Burrito, establish a safe throw zone and agree on a consistent “pause” rule to prevent furniture disasters. Want more chaos? Use a timer to force rapid-fire throws—this will inspire riotous reactions and probably a few heroic falls onto cushions.
- For the Dodgeball Card Game, consider a few house rules: allow one mini-break for a snack mid-round, or introduce a “coach’s challenge” where a player can argue a borderline card interaction with the table judge (you, your friends, or the cat, if you’re into dramatic verdicts).
