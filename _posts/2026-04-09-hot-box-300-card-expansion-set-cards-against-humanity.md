### How to play with the expansion: rules, etiquette, and a few house notions
Cards Against Humanity is famously flexible with house rules, and the Hot Box is no exception. Here are a few practical guidelines to maximize fun without breaking the vibe:

- Start with a clean deck: mix the base game with the 300-card expansion in roughly a 1:2 ratio (one black prompt to two or three white cards from the expansion, depending on your group size). This keeps the competition fresh and avoids overfitting the prompts to your friends’ known punchlines.
- Set a light “safety” rule: if a prompt is too cringe, play a white card that softens the edge. It’s not cheating; it’s negotiating the vibe of your night.
- Use a rotating judge model: the person who picks the winning white card rotates each round, ensuring everyone gets their moment on stage (metaphorically speaking).
- Introduce occasional “wildcards”: a few black cards that mix cards from base and expansion to create unexpected prompts. It’s chaos, but a controlled chaos that keeps players on their toes.
- Respect the 17+ boundary: this box is designed for adult humor, so keep it out of reach of younger players, and remind your group to stay mindful of comfort thresholds.

If you’re new to this game, you can still enjoy the expansion by pairing it with gentle prompts from the base game and gradually layering in more from the Hot Box as you establish your group’s comfort level. If you’re a veteran of the original deck, you’ll likely feel a few fresh sparks align with your favorite absurd scenarios, and you’ll probably discover new favorites in the 300-card pool.

### Playability and session planning
A typical Cards Against Humanity session with the Hot Box tends to last longer than you expect—especially if your group likes to talk through every prompt, argue about the best punchline, and then decide to start a side quest around a particularly ridiculous card. The 300-card pool is generous enough that you won’t exhaust all options quickly, which matters if you want to stretch a single game night across two or three sessions without repeating the same prompts ad nauseam.

To maximize fun, consider a few practical planning tips:
- Set a timer for rounds (e.g., 60-90 seconds per prompt) to keep the game moving and maintain momentum.
- Mix players’ strengths: let story-driven players handle prompts that benefit from personal anecdotes, while quick-witted players tackle the clever wordplay prompts.
- Use “round-robin” final rounds when the deck runs low: if you’re nearing the end, switch to more collaborative or lighter prompts to preserve energy and keep everyone engaged.
- Have a snack-and-hydration plan: laughter and salty snacks go hand in hand, and dryness slows down your punchline production.
