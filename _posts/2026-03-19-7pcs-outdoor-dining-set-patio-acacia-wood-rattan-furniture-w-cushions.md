## Weather resistance and maintenance

Outdoor furniture must endure sun, rain, and occasional stray sprinkler spray. Acacia wood thrives with a little love and routine care. The finish helps it resist weathering, but a periodic sealant application can go a long way toward keeping color depth and luster. Resin wicker is naturally more water-resistant than natural cane, but it appreciates a quick wipe-down after a heavy storm to prevent water absorption in the weaving pattern.

Maintenance tips:
- Wipe down with a mild soap solution and a soft cloth.
- Dry thoroughly to prevent moisture from sitting in the wood grain.
- Consider a breathable cover during harsher seasons to minimize UV exposure and moisture buildup.
- Rotate cushions occasionally to prevent uneven fading if they are exposed to direct sun for long stretches.

If you want to go the extra mile, apply a seasonal wood conditioner to the acacia to preserve color and prevent cracking. The effect is subtle, but you will notice fewer surface cracks and more uniform aging over time.

## Assembly insights and tips

If you enjoy a good assembly challenge, you will find the process satisfying rather than soul-crushing. The included hardware is thoughtfully laid out, and the instructions use straightforward diagrams that minimize guesswork. A typical two-person assembly session, with breaks for coffee or a quick joke about missing screws, will take about 60 to 120 minutes depending on your DIY fluency. Here are some practical tips to streamline the process:
- Sort all screws and washers first, then lay them out in the order of assembly to avoid frantic rummaging later.
- Use a cordless drill on a gentle setting to avoid over-tightening which could strip wood or crack joints.
- Check alignment before final tightening to prevent wobbly seating and the precious moment when you realize the table is permanently crooked.
- Have a helper to hold parts in place while you tighten the screws; this saves time and reduces the risk of cross-threading.
