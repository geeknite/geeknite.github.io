## The NM Seal: What “Near Mint” Means Here

When we say NM in the context of Dragon Ball Super, we’re looking for cards that look as if they’ve traveled straight from the factory line to your table without a single rough laugh in the process. Here are practical checks:
- Corners: sharp, minimal to no whitening
- Edges: no chipping or scrapes that disrupt the line of the card
- Surface: clean foil areas free of scratches, print blemishes, or scuffs
- Centering: close to perfect or within the acceptable range for that print run
- Flex: minimal warping or bending, especially for rare/super rare cards

If you find a lot where the majority meet these criteria, you’re looking at a solid NM bundle with good resale or play value. If the lot has a few deeper flaws, you’ll want to price accordingly and possibly separate those pieces for a different sale path.

## How to Verify NM and What to Watch For

- Inspect the edge wear under good lighting; even minuscule whitening matters for high-grade cards.
- Check for surface issues like indentations or holo-foil flaking. Foiled cards are gorgeous until you notice the micro-scratches.
- Confirm the set’s consistency by comparing with high-quality reference images. A good seller will save you hours of “is this real?” drama by providing close-ups.
- Verify that any included extras (promo cards, sleeves, mats) are in line with NM expectations and aren’t sticky or faded. 

If you’re unsure, a reputable seller will offer a return window or a grading option. A pre-release NM lot is a higher-stakes purchase than a casual pack opening, so extra diligence pays off.

## Shopping for an NM Pre-Release Lot: Red Flags and Green Flags

Green flags:
- Clear photos and close-ups of several cards in the lot.
- A detailed description of condition, inclusions, and any known issues.
- A reasonable return policy and visible seller ratings.
- Evidence of proper storage (sleeves, top-loader protection, no moisture signs).

Red flags:
- Vague descriptions or no images beyond a single stock photo.
- Ultra-low pricing without explanation (may indicate hidden defects or counterfeit risk).
- No return policy or restrictive shipping terms.
- Reports of mis-packaging or counterfeit cards from the same seller in past listings.

## Storage, Display, and Longevity of NM Cards

If you’re serious about keeping NM cards in peak condition, storage is not optional—it’s part of the hobby. Here are practical tips:
- Use acid-free sleeves and top-loaders for every card you care about; never skip protection for rares or foils.
- Store in a climate-controlled area with humidity around 40–60% to prevent warping or edge chipping.
- Keep your cards upright or flat, depending on drawer space, but avoid stacking too high to reduce warping risk.
- Periodically re-check for signs of foil flaking or edge wear, especially after transit or extreme temperature changes.

A well-maintained NM lot can retain value and look stunning on display shelves, in photos, or in a streaming session where you explain why your collection deserves its own mercy.
