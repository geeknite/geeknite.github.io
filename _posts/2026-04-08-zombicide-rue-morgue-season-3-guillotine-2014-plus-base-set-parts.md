## Assembly tips and hobby notes
### Building and painting tips
If you enjoy bringing your minis to life, Rue Morgue Season 3 offers a gratifying canvas. The sculpts invite quick undercoats and simple washes to highlight textures on clothing, armor, and gear. A light dry brush on the survivors can help them pop against the gloom, and a shaded wash on the zombies helps them recede into the background while still reading clearly on the tabletop. A common approach is to base coat with a neutral color, wash with a brown or gray shade, and dry brush with a lighter color to bring out the edges. This technique is forgiving for beginners while still delivering a visually striking result for seasoned hobbyists.

### Storage and organization
With a larger box comes a bigger need for organization. Shuffle trays or compartment boxes can help you separate survivors, zombies, tokens, and map tiles. The Season 3 content is not chaotic in the sense of being unorganized; rather, it rewards a tidy approach so you can quickly set up and begin playing instead of rummaging through the box. If you keep a sleeve of baggies for smaller tokens, you will save precious board time and avoid misplacing essential components between sessions.

### Maintenance suggestions
The materials are relatively durable, but like all intricate board game components, they will benefit from occasional care. Keep the board tiles flat, avoid stacking heavy piles directly on top of the cards, and store the miniatures in a dry, cool place to preserve paint and detail. A simple silicone dividers kit can help you reassemble scenarios quickly and reduce the friction at setup time. For players who enjoy painting, you will find Season 3 a satisfying project with enough variety to sustain several sessions before you feel the urge to switch to a new theme.

## Strategy and tactical tips
### Survive, don’t just shoot zombies
Rue Morgue Season 3 rewards players who think beyond the kill shots. Positioning, line of sight, and resource management matter as much as brute force. Prioritize objectives that give your team flexible win conditions and keep an eye on the timer tokens that push you toward a conclusion. It is easy to get tunnel vision with the zombies closing in, so always designate a lookout role or a bait character to draw the bulk of the threats away from your more valuable survivors.

### Resource management and loot planning
Loot is a currency that matters. Season 3 introduces new items that can drastically shift risk versus reward calculations. A smart plan is to target a loot route that offers a reliable upgrade path for at least two survivors. Don’t chase a single powerful item at the expense of the rest of the team; you want a balanced loadout that supports multiple survival strategies, particularly if you are dealing with new zombie types that punish greedy moves.

### Team roles and synergy
A balanced team with a mix of melee and ranged specialists tends to perform best. You want a couple of survivors who can hold line against crowds, and others who can slip through the shadows to claim objectives. The new mission cards emphasize cooperative plays that require tossing a gear token to a teammate in need or coordinating with a pull and surge tactic to disrupt a zombie wave. If you shop around for survivor profiles that complement each other, you can build a synergy that makes even tough scenarios approachable.

### Endgame awareness
A common pitfall for new players is ignoring the end conditions until it is almost too late. Rue Morgue Season 3 nudges you toward urgency with time pressure and escalating threats. The smarter approach is to set objectives early that reduce the number of open ends in a single turn. It is tempting to chase a single loot item, but the wiser move is to secure the path toward a guaranteed victory condition while keeping a watchful eye on the clock.
