## How to Play: Quickstart and Core Rules
### Setup
1. Shuffle the Moana 2 deck thoroughly. Yes, even the kid who thinks “shuffle” means “make it magical.”
2. Deal 7 cards to each player. The rest becomes a draw pile. Place the first card face up to start the discard pile. If that card is a Special Card, follow its instructions at the start—no one wants to start a game with a Hawaiian hurricane on turn one.
3. Each player takes a turn by matching the top discard by either number or suit, or by using a Special Card. If you’re new to UNO, think of it as a carnival ride: the cards do the safety checks; you, the rider, provide the risk appetite.

### Basic Gameplay Flow
- On your turn, you must either play a card that matches the color or the number of the top card on the discard pile or play a Special Card. If you can’t, you draw one card from the draw pile.
- When you have one card left, you must shout “UNO!” If you forget, and another player catches you, you draw two cards as a friendly penalty. This is the social currency of the game—yes, you’ll owe someone a snack later for that.
- The first player to get rid of all their cards wins the round. The Moana 2 deck keeps a running tally using the Island Score Tracker, so you can measure who is the most “legendary navigator” around the table.

### Special Cards in Moana 2
- Maui’s Trick: Reverse the turn order and, if you’re feeling extra cheeky, you must perform a tiny victory dance that could end up on your family’s memories forever.
- Te Feti: Changes the active color and triggers a small, thematic splash of water-like animation on the table (or in your imagination—the game is quite forgiving if you lack the physics). 
- Spirit Card: Allows a pass or swap option with a fellow player, adding a layer of strategy and social maneuvering that makes this more than “hit the color” UNO.

### Strategy for the Balanced Table
Despite the colorful chaos, Moana 2 rewards players who think ahead. Here are a few guiding principles:
- Balance speed with foresight. It’s tempting to clear cards as fast as possible, but you’ll frequently get burned by a hidden Maui twist. Take a breath and evaluate your options, especially when you’re one card away from UNO and a shower of laughter erupts from the other players.
- Use color-switch opportunities strategically. If you’re holding a critical card that matches a color you know others are weak against, wait for the moment of maximum embarrassment—the “I can see your cards through a telescope” moment.
- Don’t underestimate the power of a well-timed Te Feti. Color changes don’t just delay doom; they reset the emotional tempo of the game, allowing you to steer the table toward a more favorable finish.
