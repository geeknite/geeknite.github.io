## The Phase 10 Core Mechanics — a Quick Refresher
Phase 10 is a classic take on rummy style play. Each player is dealt a hand, and over the course of 10 rounds you work to complete a phase that requires you to collect and lay down specific sets or runs of cards. The phases escalate in difficulty, escalating from simple two of a kind or a run of four to more complicated combos as you progress. The first player to complete a phase on their turn ends hand and scores points for all the cards left in other players’ hands. The goal is to have the fewest points by the end of the 10th phase, or to simply glide into the sunset by being the first to clear all phases in a single round, depending on your house rules.

The Deluxe Edition preserves the core flow: draw from the draw pile or discard pile, manage your hand, plan a path through the 10 phases, and throw shade at your friends as you maneuver for the final point grabbing moment. It is friendly to newcomers and satisfying to long-term players who want a little extra sparkle during the critical turns.

### How It Feels at Table
This is the kind of game that benefits from a relaxed, social vibe. It is not a brain burner; it is more akin to a group puzzle with a lighthearted competitive edge. The Deluxe Edition does not attempt to speed through the phases with a million micro-decisions per turn. Instead, it invites a gentle rhythm: draw, decide, possibly swap, play your phase, and then watch your opponents scramble to catch up. The social dynamic is the backbone here, and the Edition leans into that successfully.
