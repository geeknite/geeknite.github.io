## Maintenance and care
Like any good instrument, these decks perform best when treated with care. Keep them away from humidity and heat; store in a cool, dry place. If a card becomes sticky from a spill, you can gently wipe it with a clean microfiber cloth and let it air dry. Avoid using liquid cleaners on the surface because you don’t want to ruin the air-cushion finish or smear the ink.

Opening the seal is a moment of truth. If you see a deep indentation or the seal looks loose before you even open the box, that’s a sign the deck may be compromised. If you’re a perfectionist, you can keep the sealed deck for display and use the other one for daily practice. This dual approach helps to preserve the condition of your investment while still giving you access to a deck that is ready for action.
