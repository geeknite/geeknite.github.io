## What is the Infinity 10 Person Workstation with Screens?

## Setup and installation tips
Installing the Infinity 10 requires a little planning, but the learning curve is not Everest-height. Here are practical tips from a seasoned Geeknite editor who has assembled more flat-pack furniture than a DIY reality show:

- Measure the room and the doorways twice before ordering. You want to avoid receiving a behemoth that can only be moved by a small troop of colleagues with a crane.
- Sketch your power plan. Decide which modules will connect to the main spine and where surge protection belongs. Run a quick test with a power strip before you push the entire unit into place.
- Plan screen order based on primary workflows. The central cluster should host the screens most frequently accessed by the team lead or project manager. Outer screens can carry secondary dashboards or reference materials.
- Cable management is your friend. In a large setup, neglected cables become a knotty problem that rivals a plot twist. Use the spine channels, grommets, and zip ties (tasteful ones) to keep things neat. You will thank yourself at the end of the day when someone needs to move a module and not get tangled.
- Calibration takes time. Once assembled, give the system an hour of calibration time. Align the vertical height across all arms, test each monitor, and make sure the center screen is at eye level for the highest comfort. If your eye height differs from your teammates, consider adjustability zones so nobody gets stuck with an awkward angle.
