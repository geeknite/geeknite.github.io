## Practical Considerations: Placement, Cleaning, and Longevity

Durability matters when you’re selecting a rug for a space you actually live in. The Brilliance Gold Jute rug performs respectably in high-traffic settings, but like all natural fiber rugs, it isn’t omnipotent in the face of heavy wear, moisture, or heavy staining agents. Here are practical notes to help you maximize its lifespan:

### Placement Ideas

- Living room: Place under the front legs of your sofa to anchor seating while preserving a wide, comfortable traffic flow.
- Dining room: If you have a wood dining set with a larger footprint, a well-sized rug can pull the table into a comfortable zone. Just ensure you have enough clearance for chair movement and consider a stain-resistant finish on nearby surfaces.
- Bedroom: A rug under the bed with visibility on both sides creates a soft morning ritual underfoot and a luxurious sense of space.
- Hallway: For a custom-size runner, you can measure the corridor and create a steplike path that feels like a design feature rather than a simple floor covering.

### Maintenance and Cleaning
Jute is relatively forgiving, but it’s not a magic stain eraser. Here are practical care tips:

- Regular vacuuming: Use a vacuum with a brush attachment on a low setting to reduce fiber pull and keep dirt from embedding. Vacuum in the direction of the weave rather than against it.
- Spot cleaning: Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth. Use a mild detergent solution if necessary and test in an inconspicuous area first. Avoid soaking; excess moisture can weaken fibers and invite odor or warp.
- Rotation: Rotate every few months to promote even wear and prevent one side from showing more traffic-based flattening.
- Rug pads: A quality pad under the rug reduces slipping, muffles noise, and extends the rug’s life by reducing abrasion against hard floors.

If you’re thinking long-term, note that jute can be sensitive to moisture and humidity. Avoid placing the rug in damp basements or bathrooms where humidity can cause the fibers to warp or mildew. If you live in a humid climate, you might want to consider a rug with synthetic backing or pair the rug with a dehumidifier to keep things breezy and aroma-free.
