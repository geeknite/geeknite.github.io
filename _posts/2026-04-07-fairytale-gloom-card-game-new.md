There’s also a healthy variety of “gloom” cards that can derail all sorts of plans, which means you need to stay flexible and be ready to pivot your narrative on the fly.

## Strategy, Tips, and Deep Dives

Now, for those who want to take their game to the next level, this section provides some practical pointers. We’re not promising wizard-level omniscience, but a few strategies can help you navigate the complexities of the tale you’re trying to tell.

- Build toward narrative momentum: Focus on arcs that set up a strong run of events. The more you can push through a sequence of cards that escalate the tension and reward clear narrative arcs, the better your endgame scoring tends to be.
- Balance gloom and empathy: It’s tempting to maximize gloom and watch your fate meter climb. But if your gloom becomes too single-minded, you’ll risk alienating the other players who want a satisfying, well-rounded ending. The best stories weave sadness with hope, tragedy with generosity, and a dash of humor with a touch of redemption.
- Use events as misdirection: The event cards are your best friends and your worst enemies. They can derail a plan, but they can also unlock a route to a better ending if you learn to read your opponents’ tales. The key is not to overcommit to a single strategy until you’ve seen what your fellow players are cooking up.
- Pay attention to character synergies: Some character abilities interact in surprising ways. A small, well-timed combo can yield a bigger payoff than running a raw power build. If you enjoy optimization, you’ll love chasing those synergies. If you prefer flavor, you’ll enjoy the drama of watching two characters’ fates collide in a moment of witty exposition.
- Don’t forget the venue mechanics: The places where your scenes happen can alter how cards interact. Venues can offer boosts or impose constraints. A good venue choice can unlock new narrative possibilities you hadn’t considered.
