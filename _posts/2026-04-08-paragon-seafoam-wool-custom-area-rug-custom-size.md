## Materials and Craftsmanship

### Wool quality
Wool is the vibe here. The Seafoam Wool rug uses a dense fiber blend that feels plush but resilient. Wool fibers naturally resist stains and shed some of the everyday grime that plagues synthetic fibers. This means your rug can handle high-traffic zones with less of that slippery-sock-on-ice feeling when you step on it with bare feet.

### Construction method
Most Paragon wool rugs use a machine-assisted tufting process to create a tight, even pile. The Seafoam rug benefits from a dense pile height that balances softness with a clean, modern look. ## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning tips
Spills should be blotted immediately with a clean cloth. For light stains, a mild detergent solution followed by gentle blotting helps avoid water rings. Avoid harsh chemicals that could affect wool fibers and color integrity. For deeper cleaning or bulky stains, professional wool cleaning is recommended. Regular vacuuming with a suction-only setting (no beater brushes) helps preserve the pile height and prevent fuzzing at the tips.

### Longevity and wear
Wool rugs resist wear well when rotated every few months and when traffic patterns are varied. A durable construction is especially important in high-traffic zones like entryways or living rooms where people remember to remove shoes only when the mood strikes. With proper care, your Paragon Seafoam rug can maintain that vibrant color and plush feel for years.

## The Buying Guide

### How to order a custom size
Start by measuring room dimensions, factoring in furniture and door clearance. Decide where you want the rug to sit and whether you prefer a framed look with edges showing or an extended rug that underlays the sofa. The custom sizing process will require you to specify width and length in inches or centimeters, edge style, and pile height preferences. Some retailers offer faux-lamb options, but Paragon focuses on wool for a luxurious feel and longer-lasting color.

### Shipping and lead times
Custom orders typically carry longer lead times than standard sizes. Expect production to take several weeks plus transit time. If you’re on a tight schedule, consider ordering a standard size that matches one of your room measurements and then arrange a return or exchange window if needed. Always check current shipping estimates during checkout and ask for a rush option if available.
