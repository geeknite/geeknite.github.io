## Maintenance and Longevity Tips

### Calibrating the new battery

New batteries often report inaccurate charge levels until they go through a calibration cycle. We recommend: full charge to 100%, use until 5-10% left, recharge to 100%. Repeat once or twice to align battery metrics with actual capacity. If you’re in a hurry, at least do one full cycle to reset the gauge.

### Temperature and charging habits

Keep the laptop on a hard, flat surface to promote airflow. Don’t use the laptop on soft surfaces like a couch, where vents can clog. High ambient temperatures degrade Li-ion cells faster; avoid charging in hot environments. If you’re always plugged in, consider enabling battery preservation mode (where available) or setting a charge cap around 80% to extend overall life.

### Storage and longevity

If you’re storing the replacement during a break, store at around 40-60% charge in a cool environment. Don’t leave it fully charged for long durations or fully drained for extended periods; both states stress the cells over time. The same advice applies to all Li-ion packs, including OEM variants.
