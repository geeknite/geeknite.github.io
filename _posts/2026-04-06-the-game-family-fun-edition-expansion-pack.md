## How the expansion changes the game rules (without breaking your spirit)

If you’ve played the base game, you’ll spot a few familiar patterns, plus some delightful detours. The expansion’s rules are designed to be tunnable for families who like a mild tease or for groups that treat the game like a sport. Here are the core mechanics you’ll encounter:

- Family Mode and Chaos Mode: You can toggle a lighter mode for younger players or a wilder mode for late-night sessions. The Rulebook calls it “mood-based balancing” but we know it’s really just an excuse to scream jokes across the table.
- New Character Abilities: Each of the four new characters has a unique ability that can alter the current round—think of it as a power-up in a board game version of a family sitcom. These abilities encourage unplanned collaboration, or spectacularly misguided solo plays.
- Laugh Cards: These replace some of the standard laughs with “grandparent-approved chuckles” that may require a cooperative nod (or a silent “oh no” in the background). They add a layer of social negotiation: who gets to wield the laugh and earn the bragging rights?
- Event Cards: These shift the cadence of the round, forcing players to react to wacky prompts, unexpected interruptions, or “quick snack breaks” that feel dangerously close to real life.
- Snack Tokens: Collect snacks to cash in for bonus actions. It’s basically a sugar-fueled incentive program, which, honestly, is how most of our best gaming nights go.

The rules section is the heart of the expansion’s charm: not overly complicated, but with enough new knobs to make you feel like a genius… or a chaotic genius who forgot to pass the turn.

## Setup and deck management (don’t blame the cat if you forget step seven)

1. Clear a comfortable table space. If your dining room table doubles as a “museum of half-eaten crackers,” you’re ready.
2. Shuffle all new cards separately from the base deck. This keeps your “Grandma’s Cookie” jokes distinct from “Banana Peel” calamities.
3. Place the Kitchen Counter and Living Room Stage tiles on their designated spots. They’ll be your new playgrounds for trick shots and dramatic reveals.
4. Distribute new character cards face up or face down depending on your preferred chaos level. Each player should choose or be assigned a character that speaks to their soul, or at least their snack preference.
5. Systematically ignore the rulebook’s first two pages while you discuss who’s actually in charge of snacks.
6. Start the first round and pretend you didn’t see the timer blinking in the corner like a tiny, judgmental lighthouse.

If you’re a stickler for precision, the Rulebook includes a few variants for speed runs or family-night marathon sessions. If you’re not, the timers are there to remind you that you still don’t know how to manage time when chips are involved.

## New modes: balancing chaos with family happiness

- Chaos Mode: A wild mode that adds “chaos tokens” to the pool. When someone draws a Chaos Card, the room experiences a tiny hurricane of laughter, confusion, and possibly a new family chant about who actually did the dishes.
- Family Mode: This is the “kid-friendly” lane. Fewer tripping hazards, more inclusive prompts, and an emphasis on teamwork. It’s not less chaotic; it’s simply more organized chaos, which is safer for the local humans with allergies and the table’s scar tissue.
- Co-op Stretch: A mini-mission that requires all players to reach a shared objective before the round ends. This mode is ideal for families who want to avoid siblings stealing the spotlight mid-speech, because now you all win or all lose together.

These modes feel like different game moods: Saturday morning cartoon energy, and Sunday evening sitcom timing. The expansion respects diverse playstyles—ranging from “let’s get silly” to “let’s see if we can survive the snack break without a food fight.”

## Strategy tips for maximizing fun (not just winning)

- Embrace the chaos: When you see a Chaos Card, lean into it. The night becomes way more memorable once you accept the ridiculousness of the moment instead of fighting it.
- Rotate the roles: If someone keeps drawing the short straw, swap character cards mid-game to keep energy high and reduce stagnation.
- Snack discipline is not a real thing: Snack Tokens are your currency; don’t pretend you’ll save them for “the perfect moment.” The perfect moment is right now, preferably paired with a chocolate chip cookie.
- Use Family Mode for younger players: If you’re gaming with kids, tailor the experience to their attention spans. It’s okay to pause the main deck and do a mini-challenge that lasts 2–3 minutes.
- Pair rules with humor: The expansion encourages playful rule-bending within a safe, lighthearted frame. Remember: the goal is to create smiles, not chaos that leads to midnight apologies.

## Accessibility, inclusivity, and a few caveats

The expansion is designed to be inclusive—its content centers on family-friendly humor and avoids anything too edgy. The “Family Mode” is explicitly intended to be accessible to younger players, and the art and prompts are crafted with broad audience appeal in mind. However, some jokes depend on shared family context. If you’re gaming with folks who don’t share your inside jokes, you’ll still get the basic mechanics and the core joy, but a few specifics might land differently.

If you’re someone who stresses about board-game-on-a-budget, the inclusion of reusable tokens and sturdy boards helps offset occasional wear. The mental load of keeping track of multiple card types is real, but the kit is legible enough that you won’t need a law degree to interpret it—just a sense of humor and a willingness to chase a kid around the table with a sugar packet “token.”
