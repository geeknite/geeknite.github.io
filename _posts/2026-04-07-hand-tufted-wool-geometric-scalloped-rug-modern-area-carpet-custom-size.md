## Materials and Craftsmanship: Why Wool and Why Hand Tufting?
Wool is the backbone of this rug, and it’s not just because it feels like a warm hug on a cold morning. Wool fibers have natural resilience, self-cleaning tendencies when you brush off surface dust, and the ability to bounce back after foot traffic leaves their mark. Hand tufting is the artisan’s equivalent of a guitarist’s riff: it requires patience, precision, and the ability to keep rhythm while repeatedly pulling yarn through the base fabric. The result is a dense, durable surface with an even nap that wears gracefully over time.

From a maintenance perspective, wool rugs tend to resist crushing better than synthetic fibers. The texture remains lively even after months of use, which is exactly the kind of practical magic you want in a centerpiece rug. If you want to nerd out on the science, wool’s natural crimp and elasticity play nicely with high-pile freedom and foot comfort, making it a solid pick for living rooms and play spaces alike.

The hand tufting process also means each rug carries a touch of its maker’s personality. You can imagine the craftsman or craftswoman counting to the rhythm of a loom, counting again when the light changes, and counting again when the cat decides it’s a new scratching post. The result is something with character you can feel under your toes—and something you can brag about without sounding like you’ve joined a rug cult.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping the Wooly Genius Alive
Wool rugs are not maintenance headaches when you follow a simple routine. Regular vacuuming with a gentle setting helps keep the nap fluffy and reduces the risk of the fibers packing down into a dull carpet of doom. Spills happen—be quick, blot, don’t rub, and treat with a wool-safe cleaner. The goal is to lift the spill without driving it deeper into the pile. If you’re unlucky enough to get a stubborn stain, a professional cleaning every so often will keep colorfastness and texture in check without turning your living room into a science fair demonstration of dye migration.

Rotate the rug occasionally to ensure even wear. Even the most beautifully engineered patterns can show uneven signs of life if traffic favors one path. A simple rotation every few months helps keep the geometric pattern crisp and the scalloped edge lively, like a dance floor that never goes out of style.
