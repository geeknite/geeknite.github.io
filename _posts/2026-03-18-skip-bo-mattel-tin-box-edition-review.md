## Gameplay and strategy
Skip-Bo is a race to get rid of your stock piles by playing through a central layout of building piles. The rules are simple, but there is room for mischief and cunning. We will outline a few strategies that usually pay off on typical family game nights.

### Basic flow and turn structure
On your turn you draw until you have five cards in hand, then you try to place cards onto the building piles in sequential order starting from 1. Skip-Bo cards act as wildcards that can substitute any number. The deck contains Skip-Bo cards as wildcards that allow players to cut a path through the numbers, which frequently becomes a dramatic last-minute sprint to victory.

### Strategy tips
- Pay attention to what your opponents are collecting. If someone is hungry for a certain number, you might want to block or accelerate their path when possible.
- Use Skip-Bo wildcards strategically rather than greedily. A well-timed wild can crack the table dynamics in your favor.
- Keep an eye on the stock piles; small mistakes here can cost you the game.

### Common pitfalls and house rules
Some families introduce house rules like allowing simultaneous draws or enforcing strict 3-card hand limit. Neon green sticky notes can help remind players of the agreed-upon changes. If you want to complicate things, add a timer and pretend you are on a game show.
