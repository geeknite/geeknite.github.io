## How Phase 10 Works on This Edition
Phase 10 is a race to complete ten phases in order. Each phase requires a specific combination of cards, and players must complete their current phase before moving on to the next one. Here’s a quick refresher for new players and a reminder for veterans:

- Each player is dealt ten cards (the standard Phase 10 setup).
- Players take turns drawing one card and discarding one card, with the aim of completing the current phase. A phase is completed when a player has the required set and/or run of cards.
- The wild and skip cards add strategic layers that can disrupt an opponent’s progress or protect your own hand.
- After completing a phase, players advance to the next phase in sequence. The first player to complete Phase 10 on a hand over all players scores the round; others score points based on the cards left in their hand.
- The scoring system is consistent with Phase 10 tradition: lower scores are better, and you want to minimize points as you progress through the rounds.

The 2019 tin edition does not overhaul the rules; rather, it presents them in a package that makes setup and cleanup feel a touch more ceremonial. The core mechanics remain intact, so if you are a seasoned Phasenator, you will feel right at home. If you are new, you will get the basics quickly, and as you play more, you will discover the little micro-decisions that make a single round feel interesting rather than purely luck-driven.

The experience is often about tempo: you lay down a phase, another player digs in with a counterplay, and the round progresses through mini-cat-and-mouse chess while you try to optimize your hand and bluff your intentions with a smile. ## Maintenance and Storage Tips
To keep your Phase 10 tin edition looking sharp for years to come, follow a few practical steps:

- Store the tin in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent fading or warping of the tin exterior.
- Keep the cards inside the interior tray and avoid bending the corners; a simple card sleeve can help preserve edges if you use the cards for routine play.
- If the box starts to show signs of wear, consider a light polish for the exterior with a microfiber cloth to maintain the finish without scratching.
- Use a small, soft storage container inside the tin for any small accessories like the score pad and reference cards to prevent rattling around during transport.

These habits will ensure the tin remains a proud, durable home for your Phase 10 cards through many rounds of memorable nights.
