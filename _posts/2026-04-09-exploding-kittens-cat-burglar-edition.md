## What is Cat Burglar Edition? A Quick Primer
Cat Burglar Edition is a playful twist on the core Exploding Kittens mechanic, adding cat-heist vibes to the pile of doom cards. The design leans into the idea that every cat has a plan, and every plan ends with a snack-shaped catastrophe if you’re not careful. The theme is light, the humor is sharper, and the art is capricious in the most delightful way. You’ll still encounter the pressure-cacking mechanic—draw a card, hope you don’t draw the dreaded Exploding Kitten—yetEKCBE tosses in a caper atmosphere: what if your cat were stealthier than your poker face, what if your defusal cards had cat-ifying sass, and what if you could snatch slightly better cards from a neighbor’s calculated misfortune?

The Cat Burglar Edition stands on the shoulders of the original EK formula (draw cards, avoid Exploding Kittens, use Defuse, and try not to explode in front of your friends’ disappointed gaze). But this version adds a few flair cards, heist-themed action cards, and art that jokes about marble counters, velvet rope, and the universal temptation of a cat’s nap while you’re attempting a daring heist of your opponent’s hand. The result is a product that feels like a meme came to life, sneaking a few additional laughs into a game night while encouraging more interaction than a standard party game.

## Components: What’s in the Box (And Do You Care About It)
In EKCBE, the deck remains familiar in its skeleton—action cards, defense options, and the occasional bomb-shaped surprise—but the bones have new, whiskered skin. Here’s the lay of the land:

## How to Play: A Lightweight Heist You Can Learn Between Sniffs of Catnip
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, EKCBE will feel familiar, with some delightful detours that keep the pace lively. If you’re new to the series, fear not—this isn’t a math problem disguised as a joke. It’s basically “don’t blow up,” with the occasional theft attempt and a lot of cat-related chaos.

- Setup: Shuffle the deck, deal a starting hand, and let the cat burglar countdown begin. The exact number of cards you start with doesn’t radically alter the game’s core dynamic, but it affects early safety—the more you start with, the longer you avoid the moral hazard of the first inevitable explosion.
- Turn flow: On your turn, you may play one or more cat-themed action cards from your hand, depending on the card type. You can attack, defend, steal, or misdirect. Your objective remains: avoid drawing the Exploding Kitten and, if you must, survive with Defuse cards or clever counterplay.
- Explosions and Defuses: The ritual remains the same: if you draw an Exploding Kitten and you don’t have a Defuse card, you’re out of the game. Defuse cards still save you, butEKCBE spices things up by offering Defuse alternatives that align with the cat burglar theme (e.g., distracting the kitten with a mouse toy, or trading your fate for a “purr-alibi”).
- Cat Burglar cards: The new category of cards adds heist-specific drama. You might have the option to steal a card from another player or force them to discard, which in turn influences who’s laughing last when a kitten goes boom. The exact names of these cards are pun-heavy and designed to evoke laughter rather than confusion.

The pacing benefits a lot from the cat burglar edits: it keeps players engaged with decisions that matter, rather than forcing everyone into a purgatorial stalemate where nobody dares to take a risk. In practice, the game becomes a playful dance of risk assessment: do you steal a card now and risk a counterattack, or do you wait and hope your defenses stay intact? The Cat Burglar Edition rewards bold but sensible moves, especially when you manage to stage a good misdirection that makes your opponents overcommit to their own plan.
