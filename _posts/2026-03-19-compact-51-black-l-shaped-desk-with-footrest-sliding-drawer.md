## Assembly and Personal Experience
### Getting everything out of the box
Most users report that the box contains the main desk panels, the steel frame, the footrest, and a handful of screws that look suspiciously like they were culled from an IKEA hex key family reunion. Expect a moderate level of assembly difficulty. If you’ve assembled a bookshelf with more than two screws, you’ll handle this with the poise of a person who just discovered a secret door in their own apartment. Pro tip: group screws by size and color to avoid the age-old mistake of trying to mount the wrong rail on the wrong leg.

### Step-by-step vibe check
- Lay out all parts and verify you have the hardware list. 
- Attach the main frame, ensuring legs are aligned with the corner sections. This may require a friend who won’t abandon you halfway through. 
- Mount the desktop surfaces. Be mindful of the L-shape orientation—you want the longer panel to sit where the lights don’t glare into your screen. 
- Install the footrest and the sliding drawer. A gentle hover is better than a slam; you’ll thank yourself later.
- Cable routing and fine-tuning. Adjust levelers to eliminate wobble.

### Time investment
Most folks finish assembly in 60–90 minutes, depending on whether you’re distracted by a cat, a streaming playlist, or the temptation to rearrange your home office like a minimalist interior designer with a caffeine problem. The good news is that you’ll be able to use the desk almost immediately after the main frame is up; the drawer and footrest don’t require a PhD in mechanical engineering to function.
