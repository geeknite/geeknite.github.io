## How to play in practice
### Setup discipline
Lay out a fresh grid of image cards in a standard pattern. Ensure both teams have their markers on standby and pick a clue giver from each team. The board decoration and the number of images per round is a matter of setup, but the rhythm of play stays constant.

### The core exchange
On each turn the clue giver reveals a clue that ties to a subset of pictures. The team then discusses and selects cards one by one. Each selected card is turned over to reveal whether the guess hits their own team, the other team, neutral cards, or the dreaded assassin.

### The pace and pressure
The pace of the game scales with the group. In a busy night you can burn through rounds with rapid-fire guesses and lots of banter. On a slower night you may savor the misreads and the art that inspired them. The group dynamic is the secret sauce here, and that is what makes this variant live or die.

## Strategies for success
### Read the board like a pretty picture
Look for visual motifs that overlap across several cards rather than trying to force a single image into a big clue. The best clues often tie images that share a common theme like environment, color palette, or subject matter rather than a literal object. The cunning clue giver will craft a clue that guides the team toward multiple targets while avoiding dangerous misreads.

### The art as a map
Treat the artwork as a map that reveals relationships between images. If you see a cluster of cards with ocean, boats and sea life, a clue about travel or exploration can be a strong start. If there are urban scenes with neon and motion, a clue about city nights might cover several cards. The trick is to keep your team focused while preventing them from wandering into the wrong cluster.

### How to handle the assassin risk
Do not push the team to guess too many cards at once. A well timed single card guess can be safer than a big multi card leap that includes the assassin. The safe path is not always the most exciting path, but it tends to be celebrated in the after party when the laughter is loud and the mood is high.
