## How to set up (the table arrangement is half the battle)
Let’s be real: the setup is rarely the star of a review. However, with 10 players, you must consider seating logistics, rule clarity, and who’s going to be happy sitting near the “cat-ocalypse” corner. The setup flow usually goes like this:
1) Clear a big table or assemble a lounge of chairs around a surface with enough space for 10 players to fidget without knocking over drinks. If you’re in a condo, you will be the hero for squeezing everyone in and pretending the living room is a board-game amphitheater.
2) Shuffle the deck and deal Defuse cards to each player as per the rulebook. Defuses are hot commodities, so you’ll see negotiations in real time. It’s like a micro-economy of calm resilience.
3) Place the Exploding Kitten deck in the center, and lay down the built-in Play Pile cards per the guidelines. The center feels like a throne room for chaos—your table becomes the arena in which the kittens perform their dramatic entrances.
4) Explain the special cards before starting. It’s not cheating to remind people that “Nope” is a mighty card late in the game; it’s a public service to avoid constant misplays.

Pro tip: In a 10-player setting, a quick “house rules” moment at the start saves you 15 minutes of audience questions. You can customize a few local rules to prevent someone from hoarding all Defuses like a dragon hoarding bacon-flavored treasure. It’s your party, so you can rein some things in—just enough to keep it fun for everyone, including the cat-lovers and the cats-are-absolutely-not-okay-with-your-antics crew.

## How to play: a quick primer for chaos enthusiasts
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll feel right at home. If you haven’t, there’s a learning curve that’s gentler than trying to teach a dog to fetch a cat-proof card. The core loop remains simple: draw cards, try to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten, and deploy Defuses and special-action cards to outsmart your opponents. The Party Pack adds more players, which means more opportunities for mischief and more chances to outsmart someone by sacrificing a bit of humor.

Screenshots and diagrams aren’t required for the procedures—the rulebook is readable, and the new cards have big, readable icons that tell you exactly what to do. The kicker here is the dynamic: with up to 10 players, you’ll experience a level of unpredictability that makes the base game feel quaint by comparison. It’s like moving from a cozy cottage to a chaotic open-world action RPG where the dragon is also your neighbor’s cat who has opinions about your card choices.

Key mechanics that keep the energy high:
- Exploding Kitten cards: cause panic, quick table-turns, and dramatic gasps that echo through your apartment complex.
- Defuse cards: the lifelines. Each Defuse purchase becomes a small mind game as players attempt to hedge their risk and gauge the mood of the table.
- Special action cards: See, Attack, Nope, Shuffle, and plenty more that let you pivot the game from “your turn” to “my turn again, and you’re now terrified.” The more players, the more you realize these cards become social tools as much as functional ones.

One of the highlights for larger groups is the tempo. The Party Pack’s design encourages constant table talk. People lean into the chaos, trade jokes about the “cat-astrophe” they’ve drawn, and the sense of competition becomes a shared story you’ll retell at later gatherings. It’s a game that thrives on storytelling—each round a chapter in the great saga of your living room. If you want to keep things snappier, you can adopt a house rule: shorten turns, reduce the number of Defuses in reserve, or allow players to swap a single card with a neighbor once per round. It’s your stage, so tune the drama accordingly.

## The 10-player dynamic: chaos, camaraderie, and occasional betrayal
The real magic of the 10-player Party Pack is how the dynamics shift when you multiply the players. With more participants, you’ll see:
- More alliances, short-lived as they are, forming around a common enemy: the Exploding Kitten that sits quiet in the deck like a final boss waiting for you to slip.
- More dramatic “one more draw” moments. The crowd around the table becomes a chorus of gasps and clever banter, with everyone trying to predict the next big move.
- More dramatic loses and wins. You’ll experience the thrill of defusing while your best friend stares at a tiny card that says, essentially, “you’re still not safe.”
- More variety in player personalities. Some folks are patient strategists who hoard Defuses; others are “grab-and-go” risk takers who savor every risky bluff. The interplay creates a rhythm that’s unique to a 10-player table.

This scale-up also invites a few cautionary notes. With more players, turns can lag when the table is large and chatty. A short pause to remind everyone of the current card effect helps keep the energy from sinking into confusion. And yes, there’s always that one person who insists on narrating every draw and every misstep as if you’re watching a sports highlight reel. It’s part of the fun, but be ready to manage the commentary if you want to prevent a meltdown or rolling laughter at a table near the kitchen.

## Strategy and shenanigans: what to aim for and what to avoid
There’s no cheat-sheet that makes you a card-dealing oracle, but there are a few practical guidelines that help players survive the kitten apocalypse with their dignity intact:
- Manage your Defuses: Don’t save every single Defuse for a god-like hand. Use them strategically, especially when the deck starts to tilt toward an Exploding Kitten draw. The more Defuses you employ, the more you can influence late-game outcomes.
- Read the table: The social cues are as important as the cards themselves. If someone is desperate for a card, you can take advantage with a “Nope” or a bluff that makes them second-guess their next move.
- Use Attack and Skip cards to control tempo: The goal isn’t always to survive; sometimes it’s to force your opponent into a dicey draw. The Attack card, in particular, can reset momentum and create new opportunities for mischief.
- Seat choice matters: In a 10-player setting, the distance between players matters more than you’d think. People who sit in the “noise” zone are loud in a good way; those who sit far from the cat-flames get a break from the chaos but might miss the best opportunities for witty banter.
- Embrace unpredictability: The real charm of the Party Pack is that it’s designed to be unpredictable. Don’t be afraid to lean into the absurd and craft entertaining stories around the cards you draw. A little theater goes a long way.

Pros:
- Excellent for large groups who want a fast, chaotic game that doesn’t require a referee or a long setup. The 10-player cap is a sweet spot for social energy and pacing.
- The card art remains charmingly ridiculous, and the memes translate well across generations of players.
- The rules are approachable, with enough nuance to reward sharp bluffing and careful defusal planning.

Cons:
- The chaos can be overwhelming for newcomers. It takes a few rounds to figure out what each card does and how to respond to big moments in real time.
- A 10-player game length can feel long if breaks aren’t taken or if players spend too much time debating each move. A timer or a quick-start rule break can help.
- Some players will want to hoard Defuses or over-explain strategies; this is part of the experience, but you’ll want to manage it to prevent the session from dragging.

If you’re new to Exploding Kittens, the sealed 2020 Party Pack is a great entry point for a live audience that enjoys humor and cat-based mischief. For the more seasoned players who crave depth and strategy, it’s still accessible but the emphasis is less on deep tactical play and more on social interaction, bluffing, and timing. It’s a party game first, and a tactical card game second. The trade-off is that you get a lot of laughter and memorable moments—often at the expense of a few fragile egos, which is exactly what you paid for when you opened the box.
