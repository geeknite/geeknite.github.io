## What Fortune Row Is

Fortune Row isn’t a standalone game; it’s a modular expansion that slides into your City of Mist campaign like a glamorous outro song into a moody montage. It introduces a new location layer—the Fortune Row district—where players chase fortunes, tempt fate, and occasionally get slapped with the bill for their own luck. The expansion emphasizes social manipulation, risk, and the way a city’s glittering surface often hides a blueprint for disaster.

Think of Fortune Row as the casino side-quest that never asked permission to exist in a city already brimming with gods, secrets, and motive. It’s designed to slot into the existing Masks and Mythos framework, so you don’t need to relearn the wheel—just tune the dial toward opportunities (and possible calamities) that only a place named Fortune Row could cook up.

## Box Content and Components

A well-packed box is half the fun of any expansion, and Fortune Row doesn’t disappoint. Inside you’ll find a balanced spread of bits that feel tactile, thematic, and just a touch over-the-top—in the best way.

### What’s inside
- A double-sided game board featuring Fortune Row on one side and a continuation of the standard city map on the other. The art leans into casino-glitz with moody noir lighting and enough chrome to make a cyberpunk casino manager feel underdressed. 
- 6 new Masks that tilt the player power balance toward risk-taking and social manipulation. 
- 2 new Mythos cards that weave Fortune Row into the city’s larger mythic tapestry. 
- 100 Fortune Cards that drive the main gambit of the expansion: luck, leverage, and the occasional metaphorical brick of fate. 
- 40 Story Cards that slot into the ongoing chronicles with new prompts and twists.
- 60 Tokens, 20 Event markers, and a fresh bag of little-town-crime-chits to track favors, debts, and reputations.
- 1 Rulebook supplement plus a mini-campaign guide for a three-act arc that uses Fortune Row as a spine.

If you’re the kind of player who line-items all your loot in a spreadsheet, you’ll be delighted at how many tiny, tactile pieces exist to remind you that luck has a cost and a schedule. If you’re not, you’ll still enjoy the satisfying clack of chips and the satisfying clatter of promos that remind you to actually use the Fortune Cards rather than pretending they’re decorative coasters.

## How Fortune Row Changes Core Rules

Fortune Row threads into the core rules rather than replacing them, which is exactly the vibe we want when a game already runs on a knife-edge between myth and mischief.

### New mechanics: Fortune Cards and the Gambler’s Dial
Fortune Row introduces a deck of Fortune Cards that players draw under specific circumstances: when you push your luck, when your Mask compels you to bet on a bluff, or when Fate itself wants to remind you who’s really running this show. These cards can grant momentum, shift a Mask’s stance, or trigger a temporary alliance with a rival in the Roll-of-Fortune sense. There’s a risk-reward tension here: you can spend a Fortune Card to guarantee a bold action, but doing so might corrupt your alignment with your chosen Mask or Mythos, which is a deliciously thematic trade-off.

### The Fortune Row board: a parallel arc
The Fortune Row board runs in parallel to the main act track. It’s not a separate game mode; it’s an overlay that creates opportunities for side quests, heists, and social gambits. The side quests feed into the main narrative, giving players a reason to explore the district even when the city’s primary mysteries seem urgent. The new District tiles you flip during play also grant temporary bonuses and unleash micro-events that can shift the mood of a session in between big reveals.

### Integration with Masks and Mythos
Fortune Row leans into the game’s core philosophy: your Masks are not just classes, they’re performances. The expansion adds two new Masks that are particularly suited to a Fortune Row-style play session: one that thrives on social manipulation (a master of bluffing and negotiation) and another that thrives on risk (someone who can lean into danger and come out with a story worth telling). These new Masks interact with existing Mythos in interesting ways, often amplifying the dramatic tension when a player makes a bold choice on Fortune Row—a choice that might save someone’s life or ruin it in the most spectacular fashion.

### Pace and balance considerations
Fortune Row can accelerate the session pace if you’re not careful. The new resources create a temptation to chase every lead, sometimes at the expense of the core mystery. The designers seem to anticipate this and provide hooks to pull players back toward the central plot without crushing the side content under a merciless doom-well. The result is a balanced blend: a fast-paced, fractal of micro-stakes that culminates in a larger narrative payoff.

## New Characters, Factions, and Narrative Threads

City of Mist is, at heart, about people with mythic names and real-world problems. Fortune Row doubles down on that premise by giving players fresh lenses through which to view the city’s power brokers.

### New Masks
- The Gambler: This Mask thrives on risk and misdirection. Abilities include forcing a re-roll under high-stakes conditions, and a social badge that makes NPCs more likely to concede a bluff after a dramatic reveal. The Gambler’s buy-in is personal style: heists, high fashion, and an intimate knowledge of when to fold.
- The Fortune’s Fool (name subject to table consensus): A Mask that embraces misfortune as a weapon. They can turn a bad card into a narrative leverage, and their presence makes the table more forgiving of spectacular mistakes—as long as the story remains compelling.

Both Masks unlock new dialogue options, social quirks, and signature actions that can dramatically tilt scenes toward casino-level drama.

### New Mythos and Faction Dynamics
Fortune Row fans a set of mythology that centers on luck, fate, and the consequences of tempting fate. You’ll see new ratios of risk vs. reward, and new faction dynamics that encourage players to form uneasy alliances for a night of fortune-telling and card-tricks. It’s not all glamour and glitter, either; debt, favoritism, and long shadows from past betrayals creep back into the narrative, reminding everyone that luck never comes for free.

## Campaign Play and Narrative Flow

Fortune Row offers a three-act mini-campaign that can slot into your longer City of Mist chronicle or sit nicely as a one-shot to test new players’ appetite for long-form storytelling.

- Act I: Enter Fortune Row. Characters discover a new district map, a set of rival factions, and a promise of “one night of luck” that could rewrite someone’s backstory.
- Act II: The House Always Wins. A more dangerous proposition arises: a heist, a double-cross, or a public spectacle where truth must be masked behind a charming lie.
- Act III: The Aftermath and the Reckoning. Consequences ripple out through the city. A Mist-powered consequence loop ties the events of Fortune Row back into the core mystery, ensuring your players can’t ignore the debts they’ve accrued.

If you like the idea of weaving a casino-night aesthetic into a noir-tinged city, this mini-arc scratches that itch without demanding you rewrite your entire campaign schedule. The pacing is flexible enough to accommodate both long-running campaigns and casual weekend games, which is a win in our book.

### Story prompts and player agency
Fortune Row shines when players wrestle with callbacks to previous acts and with NPCs who demand repayment for favors. The Story Cards provide a neat, modular toolkit: you can pull a new prompt mid-session, swing in a sharp plot twist, or give a player a reason to care about a side character who isn’t the center of the mystery. It’s a clever way to keep players engaged without forcing them into a single predetermined arc.

## Art Direction, Theme, and Production Quality

If you’ve enjoyed City of Mist’s aesthetic, Fortune Row will sit well on your shelf. The art direction leans into bold neon palettes, noir silhouettes, and a casino’s gleaming but morally questionable optics. The card stock feels sturdy; the tokens have a satisfying heft; and the graphic design maintains the series’ signature readability even when the table is crowded with someone’s coffee, someone else’s dice, and someone’s cat refusing to accept a chair as a throne.

The two new boards look great side-by-side with the core map, and the Fortune Row art has enough character to feel distinct from the rest of the city while still fitting the overall vibe. If you value aesthetic cohesion in your tabletop storytelling, Fortune Row checks that box with gusto.

## Value, Price, and Why It’s Worth It (or Not)

Price is a sensitive topic in the land of expansions, where a few shiny bits can spiral into a handful of “one more cards.” Fortune Row lands in a middle tier for City of Mist expansions: not the cheapest, but not absurdly priced either. The main question is: does the content justify the cost? For most City of Mist players who crave new story hooks, new social dynamics, and a fresh district to explore, the answer is yes.

If you’re a player who tends to burn through modules in a single weekend, Fortune Row might feel potentially undercooked. However, if your group loves long-form chronicles, the three-act arc and the variety of new prompts can sustain sessions for quite a while. As always, the best value comes from how often you actually incorporate the new story threads into your ongoing city and how well you integrate Fortune Row’s mood with your core mystery’s stakes.

## Balance and Replayability

The addition of new Masks and a new district naturally adds variety. The Fortune Cards encourage a different risk calculus each session, which keeps things fresh. The replayability is okay-to-good rather than spectacular, mainly because many campaigns tend to lock into particular storytelling rhythms. If you want to stretch new life into Fortune Row, you’ll want to run multiple arcs, mixing and matching Story Cards to avoid predictable outcomes.

If you’re curious about how new content compares to other expansions, you might consider your preference for modular storytelling vs. linear campaigns. Fortune Row leans modular—great for one-shots or occasional side quests, less so for a rigid, rule-heavy multi-season arc.

## Tips, Tricks, and Strategy for Fortune Row

- Lean into the Gambler Mask’s social leverage. The social edges let you talk NPCs into revealing critical details or bending their own rules, which fits Fortune Row’s high-tension vibe.
- Use Fortune Cards strategically. Don’t exhaust the deck early; save cards for moments when the narrative cadence is about to stall. The best players use a well-timed Fortune Card to flip a scene from tense to cinematic.
- Don’t forget the debt mechanic. In a city where luck has a price, debts and favors can drive negotiation scenes with NPCs who know where every body is buried—or at least what coffee shop their alibis hang out in.
- Tie acts together with recurring NPCs. A villain who reappears across acts gives the players a through-line to chase, which is more satisfying than one-off encounters.
- Keep an eye on the core mystery. Fortune Row should enhance, not derail, your central questions. Use the three-act arc to weave Fortune Row’s events into your city’s larger destiny rather than letting it feel like a separate carnival.
