City of Mist Roleplaying Game Starter Set Review

Unboxing a neon noir dream, City of Mist wraps you in a scent of rain on asphalt and the electric hum of hidden power. The Starter Set is not just a sampler for a ruleset, it is a doorway into a city where myths stalk the ordinary, wearing a grin and a trench coat. If you crave a game that leans into mood and character as much as it leans into dice and tactics, this box is a promising starting point. If you approach it as a dungeon crawl with a mythic twist you may be surprised by how much personality lives in the margins.

In this piece we will break down what the Starter Set does well, what it leaves to be desired, and how to get a first night of gaming that feels like a small film festival in a coffee shop. We will also point you to a few tips for GMs, an outline for future nights in the same city, and a few scene seeds to spark ideas when you go beyond the included adventure.

What City of Mist is really about

City of Mist has a secret at its core that is both simple and elegant. Ordinary people acquire extraordinary powers by stepping into a mythic identity called a mask. The city itself is a character with its own pressures and mysteries, and the players tell a story in which visibility and concealment matter as much as any result on a die. The Masks are not just power constructs; they are prompts that shape the character arcs, their choices under pressure, and the way the city respond to their actions.

The two axis of play are Mythos and Logos. Mythos is the spark that makes a character formidable, sometimes dangerous, and always a catalyst for dramatic moments. Logos is the tool of investigation, the careful reasoning that connects clues and people to a larger pattern. Good play in City of Mist happens when players feel the tug between Mythos impulse and Logos restraint, when a scene can pivot on a single revelation or a bold action that redefines whats possible in the moment.

The Starter Set as a gateway

The Starter Set is designed to lower the barrier to entry. You get a compact quickstart rulebook that distills the core ideas into digestible chunks. It is not a cheats sheet masquerading as a game, it is a legitimate introduction that respects your time and your willingness to lean into a new system. If you have been away from tabletop gaming for a while, the tone will feel welcoming; if you are new to RPGs, the book will help you understand the syntax of a story driven table without making you memorize a thousand rules.

The content is organized around a small but robust package that includes pregen Masks. Those masks provide ready built identities with clear motives and a handful of narrative hooks. Players can jump in with a ready made identity and get a sense of how a character with a mythic twist would interact with the world. If you want more customization, you can later replace the pregen masks with your own designs while keeping the core rhythm of the system intact.

The starter adventure is a focal point. It offers a noir flavored mystery with a mythical core, a cast of suspicious NPCs, and a city that feels alive rather than a backdrop. The scenario is structured to guide the GM through key beats without feeling like a rigid script. Alongside the adventure you get a GM screen with quick references and a handful of tokens that can stand in for suspects, informants, or mythic events in play. The inclusion of dice and counters is utilitarian rather than glamorous, but that is fine because the experience is about storytelling and mood, not about rolling a dozen different dice for a single scene.

Design, art and accessibility notes

The visual language in City of Mist is a blend of neon signage, moody rain, and the glint of mythic possibility. The art direction uses bold silhouettes and a color palette that leans into electric blues and magentas to signal the presence of power. The design makes it easy for players to identify the differences between a mask and a regular citizen at a glance, which helps new players get oriented quickly.

Accessibility in this kit is a real consideration. The themes can be heavy, especially for players who are sensitive to noir tropes or stories about corruption or power. The Starter Set presents these ideas with care and offers options to dial up or down the grimness. It also provides strong prompts to include and exclude content so that a GM can tailor the session to the group without having to rewrite the entire adventure.

How a first night might unfold

To illustrate how a first session can play out, here is a typical flow you might see in a group using the Starter Set. You begin with introductions to masks and the city, then you move into a scene where a rumor about a mythic figure disrupts a quiet neighborhood. A player leans into their Mask to reveal a hidden power at just the right moment, and the dice or narrative points you are using to resolve the moment push the scene toward a dramatic beat.

The GM guides the pace by offering small, cinematic choices that invite players to reveal more about their masks. A scene in a dimly lit bar can turn into a confrontation with a figure who knows more than they admit, forcing a choice to either lean into Mythos or to bring Logos to bear on the problem. The adventure scaffolds these moments with prompts that keep everyone engaged and prevent the game from stalling in a lull.

Running tips for GMs

- Emphasize mood over mechanics: let the players describe the scene in vivid terms and then use a few prompts to determine outcomes rather than calculating every variable.

- Use masks as narrative levers: when a player reveals a facet of their Mask, lean into the consequences. The city should react as if a new myth had just walked through a door.

- Balance action and investigation: some sessions will feel like a chase, others like a detective story, and a few will tilt toward character drama. The box supports all of these modes, but the magic happens when you mix them in the same night.

- Keep the pace brisk: the adventure is short for a reason. If you want longer campaigns, you can expand with your own scenes or add additional Masks to the pool.

Character growth and narrative arcs

The Masks in City of Mist are designed to evolve. A hero does not simply gain power, they become more themselves through the pressure of the city. Relationships with NPCs can shift as a result of a revelation or a choice that reveals the city’s deeper truth. This is not a game that rewards simply collecting victories; it rewards the emergence of meaning as players and GM negotiate the meaning of the myth they carry.

Aesthetic notes and component quality

The physical components are well made. The rules text is legible, the GM screen is sturdy, and the dice are enough to run a couple of nights without running out. The pregen Masks are nicely designed; they fit into the world without feeling like an afterthought. If you have a preference for high production values or for tactile, well crafted pieces, the Starter Set delivers.

Conclusion and final recommendation

If you want a night of mythic noir that centers on character, mood, and collaborative storytelling rather than rule crunch, the City of Mist Starter Set is an excellent entry point. It gives you the right balance of guidance and freedom to explore the city with your friends. It invites you to grow into Masks and to embrace the tension between Mythos and Logos as you uncover mysteries that matter. The box is a thoughtful product, well suited to curious groups who crave a story first experience with a cinematic vibe.

For groups already warming to the CoM vibe, the Starter Set serves as a springboard into longer campaigns and a broader array of Masks and adventures. Its value lies not only in the hours of play it can spark but in the playstyle it promotes a style that favors mood, character, and a shared sense of mythic possibility over strict optimization.

Final thought

If you are new to City of Mist, this Starter Set is worth a try because it welcomes you into a world where you can narrate a mythic urban tale with your friends without being buried under a pile of rules you will forget by morning. If you are a veteran who likes to introduce groups to new vibes, you will appreciate the approach as a gentle gateway that respects the players and invites you to co create the story.
