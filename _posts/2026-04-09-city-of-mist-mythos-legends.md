## Introduction
City of Mist has always ridden that fine line between pulp noir and mythic fiction, a game about ordinary people who discover they are legends in disguise and must decide how heroic they want to be while dodging a choir of gods, myths, and their own doubts. Mythos & Legends is the latest supplement to the core City of Mist system, a game that takes a dim streetlight, a rain-slick alley, and a dash of mythic juice and serves it up with investigative zeal and a touch of dream logic. If the core game is a caper movie where everyone is a demigod, Mythos & Legends is the director’s cut that adds more mythic bite to the bite-sized clues you chase.

This review will walk you through what Mythos & Legends adds, how it feels to play with its pages spread like a glowing map across your table, and how it stacks up against the long-term love affair City of Mist has with players who want to punch above their weight but not break the bank. Spoilers? Only for your sense of awe, not for the plot twists, which are kept under a stack of luminous tarot cards and streetwise epithets.

![City of Mist Mythos & Legends cover](/assets/images/city-of-mist-mythos-legends-cover.jpg 'Mythos & Legends cover')

The cover art, as with many City of Mist supplements, leans into the neon glow of a city that fuses myth with modernity, a theme that runs through the pages like a persistent DJ beat. The design language of Mythos & Legends stays true to the game’s aesthetic: bold typography, iconography that reads fast, and a palette that says we are here to chase legends, not to argue with them in a quiet library.

If you are new to City of Mist, a quick primer: you are a normal person in a sizable urban sprawl, except your life is financed by a hidden set of myths and legends you embody as you navigate a world where miracles happen — but usually only at the edge of your perception. The core system uses pools of dice and a strong tag-based mechanic to create scenes where the mundane and the marvelous collide. Mythos & Legends doubles down on this premise by expanding mythic options, storytelling hooks, and the way legends intersect with everyday life.

## What Mythos & Legends is trying to do
Mythos & Legends is not a revision of the base rules so much as a broadening of the mythic umbrella. It leans into three core themes: expanded mythic archetypes, more granular mythos settings, and tools for the GM to craft mythology with the texture of a late-night talk radio program. The supplement introduces new layers for the Mythos and the Legends that players wear like badges or scars, depending on how dramatic the moment in the scene feels. These layers are designed to be used across campaigns, not just in standalone one-offs, which is a welcome shift for groups that want to sustain a tone over months of sessions.

From a design perspective, Mythos & Legends stays faithful to what makes City of Mist sing. It respects the players' agency by offering flexible prompts rather than rigid quest lines. It gives GMs more leeway to weave personal myths into the city’s fabric, while keeping in mind the need to balance power and bias so that no single legend dominates the table. The result is a supplement that feels like it belongs to the same world, but with more room to improvise, to riff on themes of identity, fate, and the stories we tell about ourselves when the city yells back at us.

## Core mechanics revisit: how Mythos & Legends plays with dice and fate
The core dice system and tag-based modifiers remain the heartbeat of City of Mist. Mythos & Legends introduces new mythic tags and a handful of optional mechanics that deepen the interplay between normal life and legend status. The book offers expanded tag lists, including mythic tags tied to specific legends and cultural motifs, which inject flavor into scenes that would otherwise limp along as a string of average clues.

One clever inclusion is a set of narrative prompts that help players and GMs surface personal mythos alignments without derailing the scene. In practice, this translates to more meaningful character moments, a few extra opportunities for dramatic irony, and less time spent debating whether a die roll should have yielded a better result. That last point matters — in City of Mist, the best moments come from the collision between who your character believes they are and who the city insists they might become.

If you enjoy mechanical depth, you will appreciate the way Mythos & Legends integrates mythic motifs into investigation scenes. For example, a scene in which a mythic artifact surfaces during a stakeout can use a new set of modifiers that reflect the artifact’s mythic resonance rather than simply granting a flat bonus. The result is less about chasing numbers and more about chasing meaning — a hallmark of Wyld-style mythic noir that the core game has been quietly refining since day one.

## Mythic archetypes and expanded mythos settings
Mythos & Legends shines when it leans into its bread and butter: archetypes that feel both iconic and personal. The expansion introduces new mythic archetypes with a modern twist — not generic gods, but legendary figures who are rewritten to fit the city you play in. This is not about replacing your existing Investigator with a demigod; it is about letting your Investigator carry a mythic burden in a way that makes sense in a contemporary urban setting. The legends you encounter are not museum pieces but living, breathing myths that react to your choices in real time.

The mythos settings offered in the supplement cover a broad spectrum — from urban legends that haunt abandoned warehouses to myths embedded in the city’s infrastructure. Think of a myth that lives in a subway tunnel, or one that whispers from a billboard at midnight. These settings give GMs multiple textures to choose from when creating a session, which increases the odds that a party will encounter something truly strange and narratively satisfying.

The visual language of these mythos settings is strong. The art direction leans into the vibe City of Mist does best: neon, analog textures, and mythic symbolism that feels both timeless and timely. The book does a good job of showing rather than telling how a legend can shape a scene, which is crucial for a system that relies on players narrating their way to truth through a haze of clues and bravado.

# Thematic flavor: mythic noir in a city that never sleeps
City of Mist is built on mood as much as on mechanics. Mythos & Legends leans into that mood with scenes that feel cinematic from the first page. The city is described not as a static backdrop but as a living character with mythic fingerprints. The text invites GMs to stage investigations that turn into mythic tableaux, where a cracked mirror might reveal a hidden past, or a windstorm outside a loft might signal a shifting allegiance among legends.

The book also offers tone guidance to help groups calibrate their sessions. If your table leans toward gritty realism, you can dial the mythic elements down to a simmer; if you want a more pulpy vibe, you can crank them up. This flexibility preserves the core identity of City of Mist while letting mythic vibes bloom where you want them most. The result is a supplement that feels like a well-tuned instrument rather than a baton that smacks you over the head with lore.

## Characters, investigators, and how legends wear them
The Investigator concept remains one of the most charming portions of City of Mist. Mythos & Legends expands the toolkit for developing characters who feel mythically resonant without becoming walking tropes. The expanded archetypes are designed to be modular: you can attach a mythic mantle to your existing Investigator or craft a new legend from scratch with a clear path for growth and consequence.

One of the standout additions is a streamlined method for merging personal backstory with mythic arc. The drama of a legend does not simply exist to justify a powerful scene; it creates a personal story arc that players want to follow across sessions. It can be tempting to view mythic elements as an optional garnish; Mythos & Legends insists they are core flavor, the essential spice that makes your city feel alive.

The mechanics support this by tying mythic choices to scene outcomes in a meaningful way. You will find that your legends are more than labels; they become active forces in the narrative, influencing how NPCs react, what clues surface when, and how the city itself responds to your presence as a living myth.

## Narrative potential and GM prep: a rhythm that respects the table
If there is a challenge City of Mist has wrestled with, it is the balance between GM prep time and playtime. Mythos & Legends offers a toolbox rather than a blueprint, which means you still need to put in the work to run it well. The good news is that the toolbox is elegant and approachable. It focuses on giving you ready-to-use prompts, event seeds, and mythic motifs that you can drop into your city with minimal friction.

As a GM, you will likely appreciate the book's emphasis on flexible encounters. Instead of a rigid beat sheet, you get modular scene skeletons that you can stretch or compress depending on the party’s pace. The prompts can drive a two-hour one-shot or a month-long campaign. The flexibility is the game’s secret weapon, and Mythos & Legends uses that to great effect by giving you options that scale with your players' appetite for myth and mischief.

One aesthetic note that bears mentioning: the book is generous with examples. The sample scenes demonstrate how mythic motifs can be integrated into investigations, chases, and social encounters. These vignettes are not only entertaining but also instructive, serving as mini-lessons in how to translate legend into action without breaking the illusion of reality the game so lovingly maintains.

## Visuals, layout, and accessibility
In terms of layout, Mythos & Legends sticks to a clean but evocative design. The art direction is consistent with City of Mist standards — bold symbols, a painterly glow around key ideas, and a typography scheme that suggests both myth and modernity. The layout supports quick reference during play, which is a big plus for GMs who prefer to skim a page and dive into the scene rather than reading a novella about the backstory of every legend.

Accessibility is an important thread. The supplement includes guidance on running sessions with new players, both in terms of entering the mythic vibe gradually and providing provided hooks that don’t overwhelm newcomers. The designers also offer tips for pacing and for balancing the “legendary” moments with the more grounded procedural aspects of investigations. If your table includes players who are new to City of Mist, you’ll appreciated the gentle onboarding embedded in the text.

## Setting expansion and the city as a living myth
Mythos & Legends treats the city as a character with its own mythic gravity. The expansion introduces new mythic loci and urban anomalies that are designed to feel omnipresent yet personal. You will encounter streets where legends are waiting to be awakened, back alleys that act as gateways to mythic realms, and institutions that function as gatekeepers between the normal world and the mythic undercurrents that power the city’s dreams.

One of the most effective ideas in the book is how it encourages GM and players to co-create the city’s mythic geography. Rather than a fixed set of locations, you get a dynamic suite of possible sites that evolve as the players’ legends grow. The city is not a map; it is a living map that redraws itself whenever a legend asserts itself in a particularly strong way. This approach aligns perfectly with the core game’s ethos of myth being personal and location-bound at the same time.

## Sample scenes and encounter ideas
No review would be complete without a few concrete morsels you can snack on during a session. Mythos & Legends offers a handful of ready-to-run encounters that illustrate how the new mythic motifs interact with the core mechanics. One scene might feature a mythic artifact that surfaces during a stakeout, forcing investigators to decide whether to seize the power or walk away with a wary glance over their shoulders. Another scene might revolve around a local urban legend that begins to bleed into reality when a miscast ritual goes wrong, turning a routine investigation into a race against time and a test of personal myth about what the investigators truly value.

The encounters aren’t merely spectacle; they’re designed to push players toward meaningful choices. Will your legend protect the city, or will it demand personal sacrifices that leave lasting consequences? The answers aren’t always clean, and that is exactly the point. City of Mist has always rewarded the kind of storytelling that breathes in the spaces between answers, and Mythos & Legends leans into that space with grace and humor.

## System balance and post-game impressions
From a balance perspective, Mythos & Legends nudges the baseline of power in a way that is mindful but not punishing. It avoids turning myth into an overpowering force that trivializes challenges, yet it grants just enough extra leverage for players to feel that their legends matter in the city’s fabric. The math remains approachable, and the addition of new mythic tags blends with existing tags in a way that feels like a natural extension rather than a patchy add-on.

Players who enjoy the investigative aspect of City of Mist will likely find the supplement satisfying. The mythic moments are cinematic, but not so frequent as to derail the fiction. The GM will appreciate how the new tools can create opportunities for dramatic reveals without forcing players into a predetermined path. In short, Mythos & Legends respects both the players and the GM, which is the hallmark of a well designed expansion.

## Accessibility and value for money
If you are on the fence about purchasing Mythos & Legends, consider what you want from City of Mist. If you crave deeper mythic textures and more ways to weave legends into everyday life, this book delivers. If you only want a handful of new tricks, you might find it a tad dense. The supplement is best approached with an intention to revise your campaign frame rather than a one-off buy to spice a single weekend session.

From a value standpoint, Mythos & Legends sits in the middle of the City of Mist product range. It is not the cheapest option, but it offers a substantial set of mechanics, prompts, and settings that can extend a campaign for months. If you run regular sessions, the investment pays off in richer scenes, more memorable NPCs, and a city that feels alive enough to warrant a serialized narrative rather than a collection of one shots.
