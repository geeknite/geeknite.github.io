### Assembly: a brisk sport
Setup is a one-person sport designed for people who own a basic toolkit and a stubborn streak. ## Acoustic Performance: Quiet as a Cozy Nook
### Quiet is the vibe
We judge pods by their ability to banish the neighborhood ruckus and the coworker whose keyboard sounds like a herd of tiny typewriters colliding. The Solo Pod boasts substantial sound attenuation, with panels tuned to dampen mid-range noise while preserving a friendly level of external awareness. In practice, it makes a one-on-one meeting feel intimate, even in a bullpen that occasionally doubles as a stadium for responsive chatty sims. If you’re listening to a podcast during your working hour, you’ll notice the relative hush; if you’re talking, your voice tends to stay inside the pod with minimal spill-out to the outer world.

### Real-world decibel drama
In a normal office, background noise hovers around 50–60 dB. Inside the Solo Pod, you’ll likely experience something closer to 28–35 dB when the HVAC is quiet and the door is shut. It’s not a soundproof vault, but it’s a massive upgrade over a typical meeting room where a ceiling squeal turns your TED Talk into a mime show. For executives who worship quiet as a productivity deity, this is a sacred grove you’ve been seeking. That said, if you’re hosting a karaoke-tinged group call, you’ll still want a proper room or a more robust acoustic setup.

## Space, Placement, and Installation Tips: The Perfect Nook
### Where to put it
Sightlines matter. You don’t want the pod facing a busy corridor unless you enjoy people-watching while you talk to your own reflection on a video call. Ideal placements are near walls to minimize isolation while offering enough ambient space so you don’t feel trapped in a glass capsule that forgets you exist. A corner nook can feel cozy, as long as airflow isn’t blocked and you maintain a sane distance from fluorescent lighting.

### Throughput and capacity planning
If you manage a team that occasionally needs solo time, plan for multiple pods or a reservation system. The Solo Pod is intended for one occupant at a time, though some adventurous teams experiment with two people and a carefully positioned chair. It’s not recommended for two adults doing a podcast, a meeting, and a dramatic sigh at once—the elbows will revolt.

### Maintenance rituals
Weekly wipe-downs and monthly door-seal checks go a long way. The interior is designed for easy cleaning, which is nice if you’re apt to spill coffee on the path to genius. A little disinfectant spray and microfiber cloth will keep surfaces pristine and the vibe intact.
