## Playability: Deck Building and Meta Considerations

### How the pack fits into deckbuilding

A 12-card sealed experience is about making the most of a tight card pool. You will identify 1-2 core synergies and round them out with flexible threats, midrange removal, and some blockers to slow down your opponent. The goal is to draft a coherent plan from a small pool rather than chase a card that requires a complete overhaul of your deck concept.

### Typical archetypes and how to approach them

In many Cross Worlds sealed packs you will find archetypes that reward temperature control and tempo. You may draft a small, efficient aggro line to apply early pressure, or you may build a midrange board that curbs the opponent with resilient units. The key is to identify your best 2-3 cards early and build around them, even if that means including some cards that do not look flashy but offer solid value in the late game.

### Rarity dynamics and their impact on drafting

Rarity is a factor, but it should not be the sole driver of your choices. A well-timed uncommon that fits your game plan can be more important than a flashy rare that sits in your hand doing nothing. In sealed formats, the best card is often not the card with the highest rarity but the one that makes your deck function as a cohesive unit.

## Practical Tips for Openers and Opponents

### Opening etiquette and the art of the reveal

Open the pack with intention, maybe even narrate your moves if you want to entertain on stream. The first reveal should set the tone for your draft and help you calibrate your expectations. Don’t skip the second and third look; sometimes the surprise is in the order of pulls rather than the actual cards.

### Reading your opponent’s tells

In a sealed format, you are playing against both the cards and your own nerves. Watch for tells like how quickly they lock in on a plan, whether they linger on the foil, and how they respond to a marginal pull. A good tactic is to keep your own plan on track and not let your emotions dictate your decisions.
