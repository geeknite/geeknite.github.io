## How It Differs From the Original
The NSFW Edition keeps the same core mechanic—play a card, draw a card, avoid exploding kittens—but swaps some of the jokes, adds more mature-themed humor, and tosses in art that pushes boundaries without crossing into full-on pornographic territory. You’ll see clever puns and adult references that land better with a crowd that appreciates slightly-edgy humor. Some cards reference adult situations, spicy food, and pop culture figures in a way that’s more laugh with your friends at a bar than lecture at the family dinner. If you're allergic to risqué content, this edition might not be your jam, but if your group loves edgy memes and ridiculous cat puns, you’re in for a wild ride.

## Rules Recap (In Case You Forgot the Fun)

- Set up: Shuffle the deck, deal a few cards to each player, and place the Exploding Kitten card somewhere in the draw pile. The rest of the deck stays mysterious, like that coworker who never reveals their snack stash.
- On your turn: Play a card to avoid picking a random card from the deck. Some cards defend you, some force someone else to draw, and some do both in a delightfully chaotic fashion.
- Exploding Kitten: If you draw it, you’re out unless you have a mitigating card. The last player remaining is the winner.
- NSFW flavor: Some cards introduce adult humor, but no explicit images. It’s cheeky, not pornographic—think raunchy comedy rather than softcore cinema.

## Strategy Tips
- Don’t hoard all your defense cards; you’ll need them in critical moments, but you don’t want to become predictable.
- Pay attention to opponents’ tells: the way they react to certain cards can reveal who’s likely to get you into trouble next.
- Balance risk and reward: sometimes a bold move pays off; other times, it’s better to wait for a safer opportunity.
- Communication matters: part of the fun is reading the table, delivering punchlines, and keeping things light.

### House Rules for Maximum Chaos
- Caption Contest: between rounds, each player writes a one-liner in the style of the card; the best line wins a tiny prize.
- PG-13 Round: choose a subset of cards that stay within the PG-13 boundary for mixed company.
- Silent Round: for one turn, nobody talks; players must convey intent via dramatic eyebrow raises and hand gestures. It’s harder than it sounds and makes the explosion cards feel even more dramatic.

## Variants and Custom Rules
