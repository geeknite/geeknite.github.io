## Practicality and Maintenance: what it takes to keep it charming
### Cleaning and stain removal: a gentle approach
As with any natural fiber rug, immediate action matters. Blot spills gently with a dry cloth to prevent the liquid from sinking deeply into the weave. For more stubborn stains, a mild detergent solution—test on a small area first—can help lift the mark without saturating the fibers. Vacuuming on a gentle setting is recommended to avoid pulling threads. Avoid aggressive scrubbing, hot water, or harsh chemicals that can degrade the natural fibers. A pro-tip: treat wine or coffee spills as soon as possible; time is the friend of the cleaner here. In the long term, rotating the rug every few months can help minimize uneven wear and sun exposure that could fade the floral motif unevenly.

### Rug pads and underlays: the quiet protectors
A good rug pad is the unsung hero of a well-behaved floor accessory. It reduces slipping, cushions foot traffic, and helps preserve the backing. For a linen backed rug, look for a pad that provides balanced grip and breathability. A pad that is too thick can cause buckling at the edges; too thin and you lose the stability you crave. Ideally, choose a pad with a non-slip texture and a thickness that suits your floor type—hardwood, laminate, or tile all have their quirks. Layering in a pad is like installing a tiny air mattress for your rug; it makes life comfier for both the rug and your ankles.

### Maintenance routine: simple routines that pay off
- Vacuum weekly with a low-suction setting.
- Rotate every 3–6 months to balance wear.
- Immediate blotting on spills; avoid rubbing to protect the fibers.
- Keep away from direct heat sources that could dry out or discolor the linen backing.
