## How to play: a quick refresher (for the busy geek and the tired parent)
If you know UNO, you know it well: match the color or number of the top card on the discard pile, play an action card to disrupt your friends, and be the first player to get rid of all your cards. If you’re new, here’s the quick cheat sheet:

- On your turn, you must play a card that matches either color or number of the card on the discard pile, or play a Wild card to change the color in play.
- Action cards do what they say: Skip makes the next player miss a turn; Reverse reverses play order; Draw Two forces the next player to draw two cards; Wild Draw Four changes color and makes the next player draw four (use responsibly; UNO rules police is not real, but your friends will judge you).
- When you’re down to one card, you must shout UNO. Forget UNO and you’ll have to draw a penalty card if you’re caught without saying it.
- The first player to get rid of all their cards wins the round. The rest of the deck? It’s a reminder that sometimes chaos is a feature, not a bug.

That’s the toy-store version. If you want the official, real-deal rules, you can check the link below. For our purposes, the 42003 is a robust, idiot-proof edition that keeps the chaos to a tasteful minimum while still delivering the excitement of a last-second reversal.
