## What is Marvel OverPower Anyway?
OverPower is Fleer’s attempt to bottle the lightning of collectible card games into a single, chaotic mix of superheroes, powers, and dice. From the jump, OverPower is less about immaculate synergy and more about high contrast art, brisk play, and the occasional “I can do what?” moment when a hero’s power interacts with a villain’s power in a way that feels simultaneously cinematic and unfair. The cards feature Marvel heroes—Spider-Man swinging into action, Wolverine with their keen claws, the invincible Captain America, and a rogues gallery that would make any villain blush with envy.

Mechanically, the game leans on a blend of ability scores, power cards, and a dice mechanic designed to keep games short enough to binge-watch a season of your favorite TV show while still feeling epic. It’s not a perfectly balanced game by modern standards, but it has a certain chaotic charm that reminds you of trading card games before the internet made every rule clear with a flashlight and a laptop. The P-Promo Pick From List promo fits into this puzzle by offering a controlled way to customize your roster, a little nudge toward your favorite heroes, and a reminder that promos can be more than collector’s items—they can shape your gameplay in tiny, delightful ways.

If you’re new to OverPower, a quick note: the cards are vividly illustrated, the life totals are big enough to be confusing, and a lot of the joy comes from reading what a hero can do and how you can spin a response that makes your friend groan in both admiration and mild jealousy. In the world of 1990s CCGs, this was peak “comic book side quest in a plastic sleeve.”

## The P-Promo Pick From List: What It Is
P-Promo stands for promotional card, a distribution tactic designed to reward early adopters, independent shops, and the enthusiastic collector who happened to wander into a booth with a stack of glossy freebies. The Pick From List mechanic, as the name implies, gives you a selection from a set list, allowing you to tailor a little bit of your squad in a way that feels personal without requiring a full unique card for every possible permutation.

The Superheroes P-Promo Pick From List is a compact piece of hardware for your deck-building joy. It typically has a few bullet points of text that indicate how you can pick from a list of heroes or powers during a given phase of the game. The exact text can vary by print run, which is part of the charm and the risk of owning a 1995 promotional card: it’s possible your copy has a slightly different phrasing than your friend’s. This confusion is not a flaw; it’s a little time capsule stamp reminding you that you are part of a living, slightly chaotic ecosystem rather than a pristine museum exhibit.

In practice, you use the promo to add a hero to your lineup from a fixed list, which could be safer than gambling on a random draw in a high-stakes moment. The idea is to give players more agency without creating a monetized “must have” of the rarest card in the set. The Pick From List mechanic, when applied to a superhero roster, can swing a match in surprising ways if you time it right, especially in quick skirmish formats where a single hero’s presence can push a dice-laden outcome toward victory or spectacular misfortune.
