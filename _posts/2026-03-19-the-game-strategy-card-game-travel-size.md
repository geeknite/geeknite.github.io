## House rules and quick variants
If you want to spice things up or tailor the game for different travel realities, here are a handful of light house rules you can try between security checks:

- Timed rounds: Use a 5-minute sand timer to keep every session brisk. When time runs out, total up points and declare a winner. This works nicely when you have a hard boarding call.
- Draft parity: If players draft the same card back-to-back, allow a one-card swap to break ties. It keeps momentum up and avoids stalemates in cramped spaces.
- Multi-pack variant: For a three-player session, introduce a rotating neutral zone card that can be claimed by any player on a turn, adding a mild curveball without expanding the box footprint.
- Speed mode: Remove a couple of late-game cards to accelerate the finish and increase punchiness for transit day play.

These tweaks preserve the core identity while scaling tension to fit your actual travel rhythms. Try them with a buddy who shares the same love of gadget-laden tabletop challenges, and you’ll have a portable rivalry that still respects the spirit of design.
