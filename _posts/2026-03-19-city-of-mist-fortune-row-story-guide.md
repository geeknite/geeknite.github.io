City of Mist: Fortune Row is the kind of micro-arc that makes you believe the city itself is a living NPC. It tilts the world toward myth with a wink and a nod, then hands you a pocket full of clues you can barely fit into your coat. This guide aims to help players and the Master of Ceremonies (MC) breathe life into Fortune Row, weaving a story that feels intimate at the table but expansive in implication. Expect neon rain, fortune-teller prophecies that may or may not come true, and a cast of NPCs who all believe they’re the hero of a bigger story.

## Overview: What is Fortune Row?
Fortune Row is a street-level, clue-driven City of Mist scenario built around a chain of fortunes that ripple through a single night (or a tightly scoped arc across a few sessions). The premise leans into the city as a character: a metropolis that punishes arrogance, forgives a few missteps, and occasionally gives good odds to those who listen to the whispers between the ads on the subway.

In this story, the players explore an urban myth where every fortune contains a sliver of truth—and every truth comes with a price tag. The row itself is a marketplace of secrets, where a fortune chalked on a storefront window might lead to a deal with a mythoi patron or a revelation about your own past. The tone is noir with a dash of magical realism: you should feel like you can smell rain and pennies, hear distant jazz, and recognize that something ancient is watching from the shadows even as the present demands your attention.

### Why Fortune Row fits City of Mist best
- It foregrounds investigation and social manipulation over pure combat, aligning with the city’s mythic detective premise.
- The setting supports a flexible cast of mythoi, including Fortune-bringers, Tricksters, and Builders of Fate, without forcing rigid alignment.
- The structure rewards careful play: clues interlock, but secrets are layered and sometimes intentionally misleading.

## Core Themes and Tone
Fortune Row leans into three core ideas:
- Fortune as debt: Every fortune has a cost, whether in time, memory, or something more arcane. Players should feel the pull of obligation as they chase luck.
- Masks and truth: The lie a character tells may be as powerful as the truth they hide. Expect scenes that reveal how masks function as both protection and trap.
- City as a living accomplice: The urban environment reacts; it nudges, obstructs, and occasionally aids, depending on the players’ choices.

MCs should emphasize mood, pace, and the tug-of-war between desire and consequence. Players should explore personal myths while actively engaging with the row’s ecosystem of businesses, alleys, and backrooms where fortunes change hands.

## Characters, Mythoi, and Hooking the Row
Fortune Row invites flexible mythoi that mesh with a street-level investigation. Here are recommended fits and how to weave them into the plot:
- Fortune Bearer (Seer/Prophet): The character who can glimpse a possible outcome in a fortune but must interpret it without getting trapped by a single interpretation.
- Trickster (Manipulator): A mythic shape-shifter who negotiates with players through bargains and puzzles. They may act as a dealer, a courier, or a rival investigator.
- Builder of Fate (Maker): A mythoi who believes in shaping outcomes—sometimes outside the law, sometimes inside the mythic rules of the city.
- Mystic Archivist (Knowledge/Guardian): Holds the city’s oral history and a few critical clues that could change the course of fortune if unlocked.

When building a party, aim for a balance of investigators, social manipulators, and a mystery quarterback who helps connect disparate clues. Fortune Row thrives when players feel that every action has a ripple effect through the entire night’s web of fortunes.

## Plot Structure: Beat Sheets and Scene Flow
Fortune Row is designed to be played in scenes that interlock like a puzzle box. Here is a flexible beat sheet you can adapt to your group:

### Act I: Opening the Row
- Hook: A vivid fortune appears in an unexpected place, triggering a chain reaction. It could be a storefront window, a fortune cookie given by a mysterious vendor, or a street mural that someone is determined to decode.
- Inciting Incident: The fortune foretells a council or secret meeting at a specific time, drawing players into a mystery bigger than any single character.
- First Clues: A set of three clues emerges—each connected to a different mythoi and each promising a distinct piece of the truth if the players pursue it.

### Act II: The Web Expands
- Competing Interests: Factions or NPCs also chase the same clues, creating a race that forces players to decide who to trust and which paths to follow.
- Rising Stakes: The consequences of ignoring or mishandling a fortune become tangible—loss of memory, a temporary curse, or a debt that must be paid before dawn.
- Midpoint Twist: A seemingly minor clue flips into a major revelation that redefines motives and forces players to reconsider their goals.

### Act III: Confrontation and Consequence
- The Big Decision: Players decide whether to honor the fortune, break the rules to escape it, or make a new pact with a mythoi who may not be trustworthy.
- Climax: A final reveal ties the row to a city-wide consequence—perhaps a ritual that could reshuffle the city’s power structure or unlock a hidden mythic thread in the players’ lives.
- Aftermath: A reflective coda that shows the cost of their choices and sets up a potential continuation for future sessions.

## Investigations: Clues, Clocks, and Ciphers
Fortune Row thrives on puzzle-like clues that require social interaction, deduction, and creative problem-solving. Consider distributing clues in three categories:
- Physical clues: Objects, locations, or symbols that the players can observe or collect. Examples include a torn fortune, a misfiled invoice, or a chalk-mark on a brick wall.
- Social clues: Hints dropped in conversations with NPCs, such as a confidant whispering a rival’s plan, or a bartender recalling a past decision that matches a fortune’s line.
- Mythic clues: Abstract hints tied to mythoi motifs, like a debt ledger that refuses to close, or a calendar that skips days the players didn’t notice.

Bury clues across the city to encourage exploration. Let players chase multiple leads at once and reward them for revisiting earlier locations with new information.

## NPCs: The Cast of Fortune Row
A successful Fortune Row session needs memorable, thematically tight NPCs. Here are a few ideas you can drop into scenes with quick stat blocks and hooks:
- Madame Fortuna, the Fortune Oracle: A clairvoyant who can read a single destiny-path at a time. She’s not a villain, but she’s expensive and morally ambiguous.
- Red Ledger, the City’s Banker of Fates: A banker who trades in arcane IOUs. He knows everyone’s debts but never reveals his own.
- The In-Between Tiefling: A courier who delivers fortunes between rival factions. They’re loyal to whoever pays the most and never reveal their true allegiance.
- The Whispering Bartender: Keeps memories of customers in a bottle in the back room. The bartender’s memory can reveal crucial past events if someone earns their trust.

Each NPC should have a personal stake in the row’s outcomes. Give them a one-line goal and a secret that shapes how they interact with the players.

## Scenes and Sample Moments
Here are some ready-to-run scene ideas you can drop into Fortune Row. Each scene is designed to be flexible, offering room for improvisation while delivering a satisfying payoff.

### Scene 1: The Neon Ledger
The players discover a ledger glowing with faint mythic sigils beneath a street market stall. It contains debts and promises connected to real-world fortunes. The scene invites negotiation, bargaining, and the possibility of a trade that benefits a mythoi patron—or costs the players dearly.
- Objectives: Decode the sigils, identify the debtors, and decide whether to pay, renegotiate, or discard the ledger.
- Obstacles: A rival group attempts to steal the ledger; the sigils shift when observed directly by the right witnesses.
- Key twist: One debtor is a childhood friend who unknowingly owes a debt tied to a past lie.

### Scene 2: The Bar that Keeps the Memory
A speakeasy-bar hosts patrons who trade memories for favors. The players can retrieve a memory that unlocks a critical clue, but each memory exchange reshapes the holder’s personality and motives.
- Objectives: Obtain the memory that reveals a hidden alliance in Fortune Row.
- Obstacles: The memory has side effects; the character who remembers too much may become unreliable.
- Key twist: The memory is actually a false recollection implanted by a mythoi to protect a secret.

### Scene 3: The Pact of the Two Alleys
Two back-to-back alleys form a hidden corridor that only appears when a fortune is spoken aloud in the right syntax. The corridor leads to a midnight market where fortunes are traded like stock options.
- Objectives: Enter the corridor, secure a signature on a binding pact, and extract a token that unlocks another clue.
- Obstacles: The alley shifts its layout; time flows differently inside, eroding what players remember.
- Key twist: The token belongs to a mythoi who doesn’t want the row to continue unaltered.

### Scene 4: The Final Bargain
As dawn approaches, a final bargain appears: either seal the alleged fate by gifting something precious or refuse and face an irreversible consequence that could reshape the characters’ identities.
- Objectives: Decide on a final bargain, defend your choice in a last confrontation, and prepare for the aftermath.
- Obstacles: The city itself seems to push against your decision, offering temptations or distractions.
- Key twist: Refusing the bargain triggers a different player-driven ending that could set up a future arc.

## Clues, Consequences, and Thematic Payoffs
Fortune Row thrives on a delicate balance between risk and reward. Each clue should offer a payoff that touches at least two themes: truth vs. myth, debt vs. gain, city vs. self. Here are guidelines for crafting thematic payoffs:
- Tangible payoff: A clue grants a character a practical advantage (better access to a location, a new contact, or a minor mythoi boon).
- Narrative payoff: The clue reveals a truth about the city, about a player, or about a mythoi that redefines motives.
- Moral payoff: A morally gray choice delivers a lasting imprint on a character’s mask or personal legend.

Keep a small, reusable clock on the table to track time-sensitive elements, like the approach of dawn or the closing of a pact. The ticking clock adds tension and helps pace the session toward a satisfying conclusion.

## The Ending: How Fortune Row Concludes
Fortune Row can end in several ways depending on player choices and the debt they accept or refuse to honor. Here are three plausible endings:
- The Debt is Paid: The players settle the row’s core debt with a dramatic bargain. The city shifts a little as a new balance of power emerges. The players gain a lasting mythic benefit that helps in future adventures.
- The City Refuses to Break: The row collapses into chaos as a last-ditch ritual goes wrong. The players must improvise a rescue that tests their bonds and leadership. Losses are real, but so are new opportunities for next time.
- The Masks Crack: A bold choice reveals the true face of at least one player’s mask. Personal arcs explode into the open, creating a powerful cliffhanger that invites a sequel of sessions exploring what players really want from Fortune Row.

## Play Tips for the MC
- Start with mood, not mechanics: Set the tone with sensory details, then layer in clues and conflict.
- Foster trust and betrayal: Make sure each NPC has a reason to help and a reason to hinder. The row’s strength lies in the friction between factions and the players’ evolving relationships.
- Use non-linear reveals: Don’t dump everything on the players at once. Let them piece things together over scenes that loop back to earlier clues.
- Reward creative risk: If a player suggests an unusual solution aligned with their mythoi, give them a small boon or permission to bend a rule slightly for a scene.
- Leave room for player-driven endings: The best Fortune Row conclusions emerge from choices players make, not from a pre-scripted finale.

## Final Recommendation
If you’re looking for a City of Mist session that sings with atmosphere, Fortune Row is a gold mine. It rewards roleplay, clever negotiation, and careful attention to the city’s uncaring but oddly cooperative ecosystem. The structure supports both quick one-shots and longer arcs, allowing you to scale the mystery to your group’s preferred pace. A strong MC will lean into the city’s music—the neon hum, the clink of coins, the whispered bargains—and let the players feel the weight of the debts they accrue. The rules-support here is scalable: you can lean into the investigative grind for a tense, cerebral night, or you can lean into mythic payoffs for a more dramatic, cinematic vibe. Either way, Fortune Row promises a memorable night where fortunes bend, but the city always keeps a little more of its own secret than you expect.

Bold final thought: Fortune Row isn’t just a story about luck; it’s a reminder that we are all walking a street paved with stories, some of which we’ve been told to forget. If you tell the right ones, you might just redraw the map of your own myth.
