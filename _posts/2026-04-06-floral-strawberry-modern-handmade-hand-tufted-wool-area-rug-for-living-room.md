## Materials and Craftsmanship

### Wool quality

Wool is the star here. The rug uses high-density tufts of premium wool that resist pilling and feel irresistibly soft under bare feet. The fiber has a natural warmth and a slight sheen that catches the light, making the pattern come alive as room lighting shifts.

### Hand tufting technique explained

Hand tufting is the method where artisans push tufts through a fabric backing using a manual tool. The result is a plush pile with depth, texture, and a human touch. Each loop is cut to create a soft, sculpted surface. Because this is handmade, you may notice tiny variations in pile height—that's the rug saying hello with personality.

### Durability and colorfastness

Premium wool fibers are naturally resilient. The dyes used for the floral strawberry palette are selected for colorfastness and fade resistance, so your rug should maintain its blush over years of sunlight exposure. If you own pets or kids, you will still want to deal with shedding initially, but that will diminish as the loom settles.

### The Tech Behind Hand Tufting

Tuft density and pile height determine the rug's cushion and resilience under footfall. Higher tuft density means a plusher feel and longer wear life; lower density keeps costs down but reduces the squishy factor. Backings are typically cotton or a synthetic blend that provides a stable foundation, while the edge finishing through a technique called binding or overcasting prevents unraveling. The entire process, while preposterously precise, has a human element—little imperfections in the knots are a feature, not a flaw, giving each piece its own personality.

### Dye chemistry and colorfastness

Colorfastness is not just about how vibrant a rug looks on the showroom floor; it’s about how well the dyes resist fading after months of sunlight and cleaning. The floral strawberry palette uses fiber-reactive dyes that bind well with wool fibers, helping to maintain the crisp pinks and greens you see in the pattern. The color story remains coherent across the different lighting conditions, which means your rug will cooperate during brunch and still look chic at 2 a.m. in the living room after a cereal-fueled gaming marathon.

## Care and Maintenance

Regular vacuuming with a brush attachment is recommended to avoid fiber abrasion. Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear, especially in high-traffic areas. Address spills quickly by blotting with a clean cloth—avoid rubbing, which can push liquid deeper into the pile. For stubborn stains, consult a professional cleaner who specializes in hand tufted wool; do not throw harsh chemicals on a hand tufted surface.

### Spot treatment tips

- Blot, don’t rub
- Use lukewarm water and a mild wool-safe detergent
- Test cleaner on a small corner first

### Seasonal care cycles

In the spring, lint and pollen can cling to the wool fibers; a light vacuum followed by a quick fluffing with your hand helps. In the fall, the rug can trap dust from the dry air, so more frequent vacuuming is beneficial. A professional cleaning every 12 to 24 months, depending on footfall and pets, will help maintain the rug’s color and texture. Rotate placement occasionally to expose different sections to light; this prevents uneven fading and keeps the pattern lively.
