## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity

The good news: you won’t be polishing this table every weekend if you follow a few simple steps. The SPORTNOW 20 is designed for busy households where cleaning means “wiping the snack debris off the table before the next match.” Here are some practical tips:

- Wipe the surface after play sessions with a damp cloth to prevent sticky residues from building up. Kids sometimes forget which side is “their” and you’ll want to keep the surface presentable for the next game.
- Inspect the rods occasionally for loose screws or wobble. If you notice a squeak, apply a tiny amount of light lubricant to the joint and tighten the relevant hardware. No, you don’t need to become a minor mechanical engineer; a quick adjustment is often enough.
- Store in a dry environment. A damp basement can lead to soft edges and a less pleasant tactile feel. The last thing you want is a soggy foosball table that becomes the city’s most uncooperative paperweight.
- Replace lost or damaged balls promptly. A missing ball makes the game feel less like sport and more like the long, luxurious string of “just one more” rounds that never ends well for your sofa cushions.

Durability-wise, this table holds up pretty well to the daily chaos of family life. It’s not a museum piece, but it’s sturdy enough to handle the kind of rough-love that kids dish out to toys during after-school hours. If you have a pet who likes to treat everything as a chew toy, you might want to keep the ball out of reach during non-play times, just to preserve the toy’s longevity.
