Durability and Rust Resistance: The 7Cr13MoV story

7Cr13MoV is a stainless steel often found on affordable blades. Its corrosion resistance is decent in normal use, and it takes a good edge for its class. If you are planning to leave this blade in a humid environment for months, you should consider additional care steps—dry thoroughly after use, oil the blade, and avoid prolonged exposure to saltwater conditions.

Maintenance and Care: Low-Muss, High-Utility

Like any budget steel blade, regular care pays dividends. Clean after use, dry completely, and apply a light coat of mineral oil to the blade if you expect long-term exposure to moisture. The handle scales can be wiped down with a damp cloth; avoid soaking the knife in water, which is good practice for any fixed blade regardless of price.

Note that this is not a survival tool born of a special alloy lab; it’s a workhorse that wants to be used. Treat it with the respect you would give to any tool that wears a subhilt badge and expects you to make good use of it.
