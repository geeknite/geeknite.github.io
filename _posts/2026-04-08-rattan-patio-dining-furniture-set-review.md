## Materials and Build Quality

### Rattan vs Wicker: The Great Debate

Rattan is a plant-based vine used in traditional wickerwork, and it’s beautiful—when it isn’t subject to weather. Resin wicker, which this set uses, delivers the same aesthetic with significantly better durability. The resin weave is UV-stabilized and engineered to hold up against sun, rain, and your dog’s curious nose. The result is a look that feels exotic yet is practical enough to survive a few seasons of weekend meals and spontaneous backyard karaoke sessions.

### Frame and Joinery

The frame beneath the weave is typically powder-coated metal or aluminum, chosen for rust resistance and light to moderate heft. The joinery is straightforward, designed for simple tightening during periodic maintenance. You won’t need a professional engineer to assemble this set, but you may want a friend who respects your DIY hopes and has decent hand-eye coordination.

## Durability and Weather Resistance

The resin weave resists fading and damage from typical sun exposure, while the underlying frame is designed to hold up against moisture and minor corrosion. This is not marine-grade gear, but it is well-suited for most backyard climates. If you live in a region with heavy rain or coastal winds, consider storing cushions indoors during storms to prolong their lifespan and avoid the “dried fish” look that can occur when fabric dries out in the sun.

### Weather Tips

- Use outdoor covers during winter or extended rain periods.
- Bring cushions indoors when storms are predicted.
- Consider a light sealer for occasional spills on the table surface, but test in an inconspicuous area first.
