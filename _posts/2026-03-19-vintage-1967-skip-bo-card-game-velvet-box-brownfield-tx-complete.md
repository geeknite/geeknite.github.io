## Preservation, Care, and Best Practices for Aging Cards
Preserving vintage cards requires a light touch and some sensible storage. Here are practical steps you can take to maximize longevity without sacrificing the charming feel of the set:
- Keep the velvet box closed when not in use to minimize dust infiltration. Dust is a chronic villain in any aged game collection, particularly when you consider the microfuzz that can dislodge from the velvet.
- Store the set in a cool, dry place. West Texas can be sunny and dry, which is atmospheric in a movie, but not ideal for card stock. A stable climate reduces warping and color bleed.
- Use insert trays or small organizers to keep the five or six different stock piles separate. The goal is to maintain a clean shuffle and avoid accidental mis-sorts that could lead to confusion during play.
- Avoid direct sunlight and high humidity. Paper and ink will thank you if you treat them like delicate artifacts rather than generic paper stock.
- If you discover a slightly bent card, don’t press it back flat with brute strength. Instead, place the card under a light weight with a sheet of acid-free paper on top for a few days. The gentle pressure helps; the goal is to restore a semblance of the card’s original geometry, not to crumple it into oblivion.
