## How to play without needing a manual in the emergency room

### Setup and the first round

1) Shuffle the UNO Attack deck and deal seven cards to each player, just like regular UNO. The draw pile anchors the chaos, while the launcher sits ready, a coiled spring of questionable life choices.

2) Decide who goes first. Play proceeds clockwise. If you have a matching card by color or number, you lay it down. If no playable card exists, you press the launcher to draw cards, which can trigger a cascade of chaos that would make a referee raise an eyebrow.

3) When a player dips to one card, UNO must be called. Forgetfulness is punished not with a stern glare but a potential eruption from the launcher, paid in laundry lint witnesses and the sigh of a grandma who has seen it all.

4) The power of the launcher is the central mechanic. If you cannot or will not play, you press the launcher's button, and the device spits out a random number of cards. It can be a small drizzle or a full-on avalanche. You resume play with whatever appears on the table, and the round keeps moving like a chaotic carousel with better lighting.

### The power cards and strategy

Power cards in UNO Attack mirror classic UNO with Draw Two, Skip, Reverse, and Wild variants, but the launcher adds a layer of tempo and risk assessment that traditional UNO lacks. The thrill of a well-timed launch can pivot a losing hand into a dramatic comeback, while a misjudged press can bury you under a pile of face-down fate. The trick is balance: aggression tempered by caution. If you press the launcher at every opportunity, you risk turning your table into a battlefield of regret and glittering drop cards that refuse to go away.

### Variants and house rules

Geeknite hosts lean into house rules because every table has its own rhythm. Common variants include swapping a player after a launcher blast, giving the next player a sharper hand to reclaim momentum. Another favorite is to limit launcher usage to a couple of times per round to avoid one player gleefully dominating the table with relentless pushing. Some groups save the launcher for the final rounds, transforming UNO Attack into a last-person-standing moment where coordination and timing beat brute card count.

### Jekyll and Hyde moments at the table

UNO Attack is a social experiment that thrives when voices pile up, when the launcher hums its cartoonish chorus, and when playful heckling breaks out in the background. It is not a dry strategy masterpiece, but it delivers unpredictable moments that become table stories. The best attackers don the crown of chaos, turning those moments into legendary anecdotes—plus a few gently watered jokes about counting to seven while a motor roars in the background.
