## Overview

Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative fantasy zombie romp from CMON. The Green Horde expansion adds a new faction vibe, a batch of boss zombies, and a couple of oversized monsters. The result is a bigger, louder, and more cinematic game night. The Zombie Bosses provide epic encounters that feel like the finale of a campaign, while the Abomination Pack adds two massive threats that can swing a game from manageable tension to edge of your seat disaster.

### The Green Horde: Quick take

The Green Horde brings corrupted brutes and nefarious leaders who push the horde forward in a relentless fashion. It changes spawn patterns, adds new objective flavors, and introduces a big picture feel to the board. The look is striking: greenened corpses, gnarly armor, and grotesque weaponry that makes you fear a simple step onto an open tile.

### The Zombie Bosses

The Zombie Bosses are the centerpiece of this trio. Expect a satisfying sense of scale and genuine threat, tempered with moments of humor when you realize your survivor just dodged a lethal attack by a fraction of an inch.

### The Abomination Pack

Two enormous monsters arrive with big reach, big hits, and big moods. They demand attention, threaten to gobble survivors, and require careful planning to defeat. You will want to use terrain to funnel them, spread out your survivors to avoid a single wipe, and keep healing resources in reserve for a big moment.

## How it changes the game

Green Horde adds several rule twists that affect pacing and tension. Bosses act on their own rhythms, sometimes moving before or after normal zombies, which can disrupt plans and force you to rethink your turns. The Abomination Pack introduces two monstrous threats that require multiple survivors to coordinate their actions. The new loot cards offer better gear, which helps survival odds but also adds new ways to spend your resources.

One big plus is the new mission design that encourages you to manage a larger board area. You will need to position survivors for crowd control, create choke points, and ensure you have a plan for escaping dangerous zones. The added complexity is a boon for experienced players but can slow a first run for new folks. The key is to stay calm, communicate your intentions, and resist the urge to trigger every card in one round.

## Strategy and tips

Boss encounters demand more planning than the standard zombie rush. Here are practical ideas to improve your odds and keep your table from turning into a laughing stock:

- Learn each boss abilities and track the exact order of actions they perform. A little study goes a long way because most big threats have predictable patterns that can be exploited with proper sequencing.
- Control the map with terrain. Elevation, barriers, and narrow corridors are your friends. Use them to slow down the boss and separate swarms, which makes it easier to survive.
- Balance speed and healing. Do not waste heals by using them too early for minor threats. Save healing for the moment when the boss makes a big move that could wipe a survivor group.
- Don t chase a boss into the open if there is a better option to funnel it toward your strongest survivors. Bosses love being chased, but they hate being cornered.
- Spread out your survivors to reduce the impact of splash damage or multi-target attacks. A tightly packed group is a buffet for the big enemies.
- Plan a safe retreat route. Boss fights can trap you; you want a way out so you can regroup and fight another day.

## The Abomination Pack: Two big threats

The Abomination Pack provides two extremely large targets. They feel different from the standard zombies and require unique tactics. Use the environment to block their line of attack and coordinate your team to funnel the monster into kill zones where you can target it with a concentrated assault. Expect big damage and dramatic changes on the board as these monsters interact with the other undead.
