Introduction

If you are the kind of GM who keeps a spare notebook for the neon gods you pretend to be, Fortune Row is the latest character pack that promises to tilt your City of Mist table into a new shade of noir—bright as a vending machine at 3 a.m. and black as the coffee you spilled on your character sheet. This isn’t just a collection of stat lines and flavor text; it’s a compact, well-curated toolbox designed to inject a handful of fresh Mantles, motives, and mechanical shortcuts into your campaign. It’s the tiny, caffeine-powered engine that keeps a long campaign from grinding to a halt and from becoming a mirror version of itself each session.

In this review, we’ll wander through what Fortune Row actually contains, how well it plays with the City of Mist system, and whether it’s worth your table time and your literal table space. We’ll also sprinkle in some GM tips for leveraging the pack’s seeds without turning your game into a vending-machine of stereotypes. Spoilers? A few, but nothing that ruins the mystery; think of them as sneak peeks behind the velvet rope, not full-on spoilers of a heist you haven’t yet run.

What Fortune Row Is and Isn’t

Fortune Row is a Character Pack, which in City of Mist terms means a trio (usually) of pre-built character options with full sheets, backgrounds, and a handful of adventuring hooks designed to slot into your ongoing Neon City adventures. They’re not a whole new campaign or a boxed adventure, but they’re a ready-made sandbox of archetypes you can drop into any session with minimal prep. If you’re the kind of GM who values quick-start options that still feel deeply flavorful, you’ll appreciate Fortune Row’s balance of punchy concept and workable crunch.

The aesthetic here is neon-laced street-level mythos: characters who straddle the line between mortal struggles and mythic whispers, who use everyday props (a forgotten coin, a cracked phone screen, a weathered subway token) as conduits for power and meaning. The pack leans into the City of Mist flavor of “mythology in the margins,” which is to say the gods aren’t in temple altars so much as in alleys, back rooms, and the kind of places where the truth is messy and always slightly damp with rain.

What’s Inside Fortune Row

The contents are typical of a well-constructed Character Pack: three fully realized character sheets with Mantles, Mythos associations, and a set of Clues, as well as ready-to-use story seeds, optional scoring for Moves, and a handful of location hooks that feel tailor-made for investigative play. The writing is crisp, with personality notes that make it easy for a GM to roleplay the NPCs who appear in flashbacks or on the street, and for players to sink into a character’s voice without needing a separate thesaurus of slang to memorize.

In addition to the character sheets themselves, you’ll find:

- New Mantles and Mythos twists that fit Fortune Row’s vibe. These twists are designed to feel organic to the Neon City’s backdrop, not shoehorned in as afterthought flavor.
- A trio of story seeds that can spark a one-shot or seed a longer arc. Each seed includes a brief setup, a few clue snippets, and suggested NPCs who orbit the main character, providing a scaffold for your GM to improvise around.
- Quick-start GM tips, including encounter ideas and suggested pacing tweaks to keep a session energized even when the players spend a long time arguing over a single clue.
- A short asset pack, including a few location props and a couple of small, printable handouts that feel tactile rather than decorative.
- A couple of optional house rules that preserve the core feel of City of Mist while letting you tailor difficulty and pacing to your table.

Artwork and Production Quality

Fortune Row’s art direction stays within the feel you expect from City of Mist: moody, slightly grainy imagery that feels like a rain-soaked billboard for forgotten legends. The cover image sets the mood instantly, and the interior art complements the character concepts rather than fighting them for your attention. It’s not a coffee-table art book, but it doesn’t try to be. It respects your time and your budget with clean layouts, legible fonts, and consistent terminology across tabs and stat blocks. If you’ve run multiple City of Mist campaigns, you’ll appreciate the way the pack respects the system’s language (Mantles, Mythos, Clues, and the constant negotiation between mask and truth) while offering some fresh examples that don’t rely on the same dozen clichés.

Mechanics: How Fortune Row Fits the City of Mist System

City of Mist uses a mashup of noir detective vibes and mythic numerology, where characters aren’t just humans with spectacular powers—they’re vessels for myths and mantles that the setting uses to tell its story. Fortune Row slots into this beautifully by giving players a structured set of Mantles that map cleanly onto the pack’s backstory. The Mantles act as engines for the character’s capabilities and their approach to conflict, but the pack doesn’t drown you in a new rulebook; it gives you just enough to feel distinct without breaking the flow of your ongoing game.

- Mantles and Abilities: The new Mantles are designed to be immediately recognizable, yet they avoid cliché. They offer strong storytelling hooks and manageable mechanical power, ensuring players can feel rewarded for pursuing the pack’s hooks without overpowering the narrative balance of a typical City of Mist session.
- Clue Economy: The Clues scheme remains the heart of the City of Mist experience, and Fortune Row crafts several clues that feel specific to its three characters while also being useful for the party at large. Expect a mix of character-specific prompts and city-wide conundrums that GM and players can chase together or pursue separately.
- Partnerships and Rivalries: A nice plus is the emphasis on social dynamics within and around Fortune Row. The pack nudges you to explore alliances and antagonisms that arise from the characters’ backgrounds, offering opportunities for party-wide tension that isn’t just powered by combat.

Play Style and Session Flow

Fortune Row shines in sessions that mix investigative play with quick, dramatic reveals. Its seeds work well for a two-to-four session arc or as a longer interlude between bigger plotlines. The pack doesn’t demand long planning sessions; it rewards improvisation and the kind of “yes-and” storytelling that makes City of Mist sessions feel alive rather than a series of dice rolls. If your table loves split-party exploration, Fortune Row is particularly well-suited; two of the three characters often pursue parallel threads, providing you with convenient cutaways to ramp up tension or pivot to a new scene when players need a breather.

For GMs who enjoy running city-wide mini-arc threads, Fortune Row includes hooks that align with existing factions and settings you may have in your campaign. It’s easier to weave their scandals and secrets into your broader Neon City mosaic than to try to shoehorn them into unrelated plotlines. If you’re already running a “Fortune Row” adjacent storyline, this pack will feel like an extension pack in the best sense: it expands your toy box without changing the rules of the game you’ve grown to love.

Character Spotlight: The Pack in Practice

Let’s talk about what it actually feels like to play these characters. The pack centers on three character concepts designed for quick adoption and robust roleplaying potential. You’ll find the background textures are sufficiently complex to justify roleplay, but not so dense that a new player will feel overwhelmed. Here are quick sketches of the three archetypes you’re most likely to encounter:

- The Coin-Toss Oracle: A character who leans heavily on luck and divination, but with a price. Their powers vibe with uncertainty and dramatic reversals, making them a natural focal point for scenes about fate and consequence. The Oracle’s abilities let GMs stage “what if” moments that feel meaningful rather than arbitrary.
- The Velvet-Glove Fixer: A streets-level negotiator, this PC manipulates social networks and the city’s underbelly with elegant precision. Expect a lot of talk, a few well-chosen threats, and a few misdirections that keep both players and NPCs on their toes. This character excels in social challenges and investigative sequences that hinge on public perception and misinformation.
- The Clockmaker of Fate: A mechanic who turns time, causality, and machine-like precision into a narrative tool. They’re not just about gadgets; they’re about pacing and control—an excellent match for sessions that hinge on clocks ticking, decisions made under pressure, and the ever-present fear of being late to the next disaster.

If you’re curious how these characters can interact with your party’s existing Mantles, you can think of them as narrative accelerants. They don’t replace your ongoing cast; they complement them by offering new angles on familiar conflicts: personal ambition, public responsibility, and the ambiguous line between hero and myth.

Artwork, Accessibility, and Tone

The pack keeps the tone of City of Mist intact: moody, witty, and just a little bit sardonic. The writing is accessible enough for new players but dense enough to satisfy veterans who love the systems-driven storytelling. The three character concepts are distinct enough to prevent overlap with your existing cast, yet flexible enough to slot into most campaigns without forcing you to twist your plots into unnatural shapes. The accessibility is a standout feature; you won’t spend a session chasing a missing rule or trying to reconcile an odd power with the rest of your world. Fortune Row gives you a sensible, ready-to-run package that respects the table’s time and attention.

GM Tools and Session Utility

One of the pack’s most valuable aspects is its GM-facing content. You’ll find a curated set of GM tips designed to help you orchestrate scenes with the minimum of friction. These tips aren’t generic; they’re anchored in the pack’s characters and the city’s mood. You’ll get concrete scene templates, including:

- Investigative beats that leverage the Clues economy without devolving into a binary “find clue, get cash” rhythm.
- Social encounter templates where an NPC’s motive pulls your players into a moral gray area rather than a simple ally/villain dynamic.
- A handful of “deadline” moments to escalate tension; these can help you push a campaign toward a cliffhanger without feeling forced.

Comparison with Other Packs

If you’ve sampled other Character Packs for City of Mist, Fortune Row slots in as a natural successor to the best examples you’ve seen: it’s compact, flavorful, and efficient. It doesn’t try to reinvent the wheel; it refines the wheel and adds a few extra spokes. Some packs lean too hard on a single theme or rely on a gimmick to carry the content. Fortune Row, by contrast, presents three distinct personalities who feel like they belong in the Neon City’s mythic ecosystem—each with meaningful hooks that you can deploy across multiple sessions. The result is a pack that behaves like a small expansion to your table, not a one-note add-on.

Potential Pitfalls and How to Mitigate Them

No product is perfect, and Fortune Row has a few caveats worth noting:

- It’s compact: If your table craves sprawling epic arcs, you may want to couple Fortune Row with another larger expansion. Treat it as a flavorful layer you can add on top of your ongoing campaign rather than a standalone replacement for longer arcs.
- Three characters, one story tether: You’ll want to avoid the trap of letting all three PCs chase identical vibes. Use the pack’s seeds as starting points, but customize NPCs and tie-ins to create a multi-thread narrative rather than a single storyline.
- Maintenance of momentum: Because City of Mist thrives on intrigue and clues, be careful not to bog down sessions with excessive discussion about what the clues mean. Use time-limited scenes, quick reveal moments, and a few cinematic pivots to keep players engaged.

Where Fortune Row Shines Best

- Short campaigns and one-shots that aim for a distinctive vibe: Fortune Row’s flavor and hooks are perfect for single-session adventures or short arcs where you want a bold mood and strong narrative questions without bending the table to a long-term campaign.
- GMs who value prep-light play: The pack’s guidance helps you run scenes with minimal prep while still giving players real choices and consequences that matter in the city’s mythic fabric.
- Tables that enjoy social intrigue: If your players love social combat, manipulation, and moral ambiguity, Fortune Row provides generous material that supports those playstyles without turning the table into a courtroom drama.
