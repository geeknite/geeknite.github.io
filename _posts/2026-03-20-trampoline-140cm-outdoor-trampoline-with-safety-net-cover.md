## Maintenance and Longevity

We are big fans of a maintenance checklist. For trampolines, the tasks are simple:

- Inspect springs and frame for rust or bending weekly during heavy use.
- Tighten any loose bolts, especially after two or three big bounces (you’ll know when the noise changes to a more metallic moan).
- Clean the jumping mat with mild soap and water to remove chalk, grass, and the occasional leaf that tries to sign up for a stunt show.
- Check the safety net for tears or loosening; replace warnings if you notice fraying.
- Store the net and mat in a dry place if you plan to store the unit for the winter, or at least cover it to protect from rain and snow if you live in a climate with seasons.

In our long‑term test, the 140 cm unit performed admirably with regular maintenance and a careful eye on rust. The coating held up to a couple of sun‑heavy days, and the net did not stretch beyond its original dimensions. It’s not news that neglect leads to failure, but with a small bit of routine care, this trampoline stays in service for years. If you want to extend the life, consider a weatherproof cover and a set of spare springs that you can swap out as the chorus line of your yard grows old.

## Safety Tips and Practical Tips

- Always supervise young jumpers and enforce a single jumper per session unless you have a designated two‑kid adult presence rule for two kids to bounce at your own risk.
- Keep the playing area free from toys and obstacles, and ensure there is a soft landing zone around the trampoline.
- Use protective padding around the frame and springs; the padding helps reduce the risk of injury if a swing goes off track.
- Teach kids proper bounce etiquette, such as no flips or stunts without supervision and always climbing on and off the trampoline in a controlled manner.
- Check local safety guidelines on trampolines and consider your local rules for outdoor equipment in shared spaces.
