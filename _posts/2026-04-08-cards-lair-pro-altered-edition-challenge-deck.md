## Card Design and Mechanics

### Card Types and Mechanics

The deck leans into a familiar triad: resource cards, action cards, and cheat sheets of rules. The Altered Edition adds a fourth dimension: the Altered Card. These cards flip things on their head—steal a tile you didn’t know you wanted, force an opponent to draw, or re-roll a dice that didn’t exist in your version of the game. The balance remains reasonable if you’re paying attention; the risk is that Altered Cards can snowball quickly if you don’t contain them with counters or smart timing.

The resource system is intuitive. You’ll collect chips, coins, or mana-like tokens that power actions and “builds.” The synergy between resources and actions is where the deck really shines. You can see draft strategies emerge as early as round two: you either go heavy on card draw to fuel your hand, or you pivot to tempo—shaping the board state so you can win on the next turn before your opponents realize what hit them.

### The Altered Edition Twists

The Altered Edition is the game’s wild card—see what I did there? It introduces two core ideas:
- Altered Effects: Cards that flip outcomes, bend rules, or provide alternate win conditions. These cards encourage improvisation and push players to re-evaluate what “winning” even means in the moment.
- Frame-Changing Rules: Each round can add or remove fundamental rules, creating an evolving play space. This is where the deck becomes a test bench for your brain: can you adapt quickly without losing your mind? The deck demonstrates that you can have meaningful decision points even when the ground shifts beneath your feet.

The design philosophy here is clever: the deck rewards bold plays while keeping the structure recognizable enough to avoid total chaos. It’s a careful balancing act between “delightful chaos” and “unfair chaos”—the kind of line you often walk when your party agrees to play a drinking game that turns into a strategic masterpiece halfway through the second round.
