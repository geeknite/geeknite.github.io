## PS5 compatibility and installation notes

The PS5 is picky about M.2 drives, and the SN850P is among the models that make the cut with a heatsink included. Here are practical notes for installation:

- Ensure your PS5 has the latest firmware; the system has a habit of treating certain drive configurations as “special” until updated.
- Remove the side panel, locate the M.2 slot, and slide the drive in at a gentle angle. The heatsink does add a bit of width, so you might need to move the cooling fan guidance slightly, but in most cases it fits without major gymnastics.
- After installation, format the drive within the PS5 setup flow. You’ll get a clean slate to store games, apps, and saved data. The PS5 will treat this like a new storage device and you’ll be asked to install items there by default.
- If you’re dual-booting a PC with the same drive (not recommended for most folks), ensure you back up data first. The PS5 uses the drive in a very specific manner; PC usage can complicate things if you try to swap back and forth too quickly.

The net of it is simple: the SN850P is PS5-friendly right out of the box, and the integrated heatsink is a nice, non-surgical upgrade from a bare drive with a separate heatsink that you’d have to buy separately.
