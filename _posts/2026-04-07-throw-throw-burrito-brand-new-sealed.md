## How to Play (Very Rough Guide for the Time-Constrained Geek)

Throw Throw Burrito is a duel-meets-dodgeball game that unfolds around three main concepts: match cards, burrito throws, and the chaos that ensues when someone yells, “Doppelgänger burrito!” or something equally ridiculous. Here’s the gist:

- Setup: Each player draws cards and collects a working deck. Guard your burritos like precious fossils, because you will throw them, and you will sort of regret it once the adrenaline wears off.
- Objective: Collect the fewest points by dodging opponents’ burrito throws and completing “Burrito Battle” rounds triggered by certain card combinations. It’s a race to avoid getting smacked in the face with a plush orb while smiling for the camera and/or your neighbor’s dog who thinks the burrito is a chew toy.
- Turns: You take turns drawing cards and attempting to complete sets that trigger a burrito battle. When a battle happens, everyone else might grab their burrito and start to strategically lob it (not at people’s faces, preferably at their cards or the table). The energy heightens as cards collide and players realize they’ve basically invented a dodgeball heist at a dinner party.
- Endgame: The round might end with a final “sweep” of cards and a last-second burrito throw that changes the leaderboard faster than you can say, “Is this a friendly game or virtual reality?”

Rinse and repeat until the box has accepted your fate and your living room resembles a chaotic but joyful carnival. The rules are straightforward enough for a weekend group to grasp within a couple of rounds, but the additional layers (like card-based “missions” and optional house rules) give you plenty of leverage if you enjoy inventing silly variants with your squad.

If you’ve played any light-party game before, you’ll notice a familiar DNA: approachable rules, quick rounds, and enough chaos to feel like you’ve stepped into a funny alternate universe where physics is a suggestion and laughter is mandatory.

## Tips for Maximizing Your Chaotic Fun

- Embrace the chaos: If you’re trying to optimize, you’ll end up overthinking. The joy comes from the ridiculousness of the moment, not from analyzing the probability of two people colliding in a burrito-sandwich of chaos.
- Wear safe eyewear if your group is extra dramatic: A tiny fraction of throws can travel in surprising arcs. It’s not dangerous, but you’ll want to protect your eyes and also ensure the burritos don’t misfire into hazardous zones like the lamp or your glass of soda.
- Create a “dodge zone” per round: It’s funny to delineate a space where you cannot be targeted, and equally funny when that zone becomes a moving target mid-game.
- Use battle music or a quirky playlist: The right soundtrack elevates the experience, making the burrito battles feel like the climactic boss fight of a silly RPG rather than a random table hobby.
- Keep the table talk spicy but friendly: The banter is part of the experience. Don’t worry about staying perfectly quiet; the louder the room, the more you’ll remember the giggles later.
