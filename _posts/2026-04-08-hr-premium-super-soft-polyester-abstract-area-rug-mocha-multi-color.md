## Performance: Everyday Use and Easy Care
What good is a fancy rug if it demands a lab full of cleaning supplies? Not this one. The HR Premium rug is designed for real life: spills, crumbs, pet hair, and the occasional mud smudge that never quite dries on the bottom of your shoes. Cleaning is simple:
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth to prevent staining. 
- For most stains, use a solution of mild dish soap and water, then blot again. Avoid harsh chemicals which can degrade the color or texture.
- Vacuum regularly with a suction-only setting (no high-beam beater bar) to prevent fiber damage.
- If you have a pet, a quick weekly vacuum should keep fur at bay, and a lint roller can handle any stubborn tumbleweed dander left behind after playtime.

For deeper cleaning, you can spot-clean with a damp cloth and mild detergent. Allow the area to air dry completely before placing furniture back on top. Do not machine wash, as the fibers are not designed to handle agitating cycles that could twist the pattern or ruin the dye balance. ## Care Hacks and Troubleshooting
- Spills: blot with a clean towel, don’t rub; you’ll push liquid deeper. 
- Stains: treat with a mild soap solution and rinse with cold water. Avoid hot water that can set certain stains.
- Odors: air out the rug in a shaded outdoor area for a short period to dissipate chemical smells from the manufacturing process; then bring it back inside to finish drying.
- Curl: if edges curl, place heavy books or a weighted object on the corners for 24-48 hours or use a rug gripper to keep them flat.

If you want a deeper dive into stain removal science, we reference a dedicated guide here: https://www.thespruce.com/how-to-clean-area-rugs-4763361. We’ll note that different stains require different approaches; test any cleaning solution on a small, inconspicuous area first.
