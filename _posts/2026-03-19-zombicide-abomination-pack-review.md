## Condition and Preowned Real Talk
Preowned is a fun word because it covers a spectrum of reality. It could be a barely touched relic of a collector, or it could be a gamer’s accidental swamp coin collection that somehow became a boss fight accessory. Here are the checks that matter in play:
- Miniature condition: Look for scratches, paint chipping on raised edges, and warping in the base. A tiny crack in the leg is not a dealbreaker if you can hide it with a bit of green pigment or a good basing job.
- Card readability: Boss cards should be legible. If you see water damage or heavy wear on crucial stats, you’ll want to decide if you can live with occasional misreadings during late-night campaigns or if you will replace the card entirely (either with a print-and-play or an official reprint, which sometimes exists loosely online).
- Tokens and tiles: Ensure counters are present and intact. Missing tokens can be annoying, but not the end of the world if you have spare zombie counters from other packs or you’re comfortable substituting similar shapes.
- Adhesion and connectivity: If the boss miniature has a base that feels loose or a magnet that has lost its magnetism, you can salvage with a bit of glue or magnetic tape under the base. It’s not perfect, but it is a functional workaround for a preowned treasure.
- Aesthetics vs. function: Remember, this is a game piece. If the paint job is uneven, you are not entering a painting competition; you are entering a battle, where fearsome presence counts more than flawless shading. A little wonky skin tone can add character and character is 80 percent of the fun in Zombicide.

From our perspective, a complete, loose, preowned Abomination Pack can be a fantastic value if the price is right and you are comfortable with a few cosmetic quirks. The joy of owning a boss that has seen a few campaigns is part of the story, not a defect. The trick is to set expectations: in preowned territory, you are buying a story, not a museum piece.

## Storage, Maintenance, and Long-Term Care
Storage considerations are not glamorous, but they matter. For a no-box, loose set, here are practical tips to keep your Abomination pack in good shape for years of post-apocalyptic gaming:
- Use a dedicated mini tray or small parts organizer for the small components like tokens and cards. Label the compartments so you know exactly where your boss card lives when you are rushing to set up a game.
- Keep the mini in a padded container with a snug foam insert. If you travel to gaming nights, you’ll want protection against the inevitable board-game tumble that happens when you forget to bring a carry bag and end up carrying everything in a suspiciously full backpack.
- Protect the paint job with a light clear coat if you’ve painted the figure. Even a subtle matte finish helps prevent chipping during transport and play.
- Clean off dust with a soft brush. A tiny bit of dust can affect the look of a large, dark monster more than a minor paint flaw, so a quick dust-off goes a long way.
- Avoid humidity extremes. In damp basements, cardboard and counters can absorb moisture, warping or sticking together. If you cannot avoid a humid environment, consider sealing the components in a zip-top bag with a silica gel packet.

Maintenance philosophy: your Abomination will outlive several of your gaming chairs if you treat it with care. A little respect for the model goes a long way toward preserving the drama and the effect of that big, terrifying presence on the map.
