## Josh in the 1st edition deck: what you got on the table
The 1st edition Josh card set is all about bold, legible signaling. The typography feels chunky, the icons are large, and the layout screams core mechanic first. The essence of Josh in this edition is mobility with a twist of frontline reliability. He is the go-to guy when the team needs a fast pivot, a flank maneuver, or an escape hatch opened by decisive action rather than patience.

### Core traits and expected playstyle
- Mobility first, safety second. Josh is the roaming agent who wants to be where the action is, but not at the expense of the squad. His card lines emphasize movement and opportunistic plays.
- Special actions that reward positioning. Expect actions like dash maneuvers, corridor clears, or quick door checks that let you push a hero past a chokepoint.
- A dash of flavor text that sells the vibe. The 1st edition flavor hints at humor and bravado, a character who talks big and then backs it up with quick feet.

### Visual design and readability
Because this is 1st edition, the card sheet has a tactile, cloisonne feel to it. The art is a bit rough around the edges, but that roughness is part of the charm. The icons carry weight and the spacing makes it easier to scan a turn in the middle of a tense moment. There is something honest about the way the board text breathes when you flip through Josh cards in the heat of a game night. If you are someone who enjoys a little texture with your tabletop, 1st edition Josh is a warm hug from a familiar, slightly chaotic friend.

### Sample card text from 1st edition (illustrative, not exact)
- Action: Move up to 3 spaces
- Special: If you end your move adjacent to a zombie, gain one extra loot token
- Flavor: The pace is not speed, the pace is purpose

The takeaway here is that 1st edition Josh rewards aggressive positioning. The longer you stay out of a crowd, the more opportunities you create for your squad to capitalize on a moment of weakness in the horde. The cards reward you for planning, but they also hold a mirror to your timing ability. You will find the rhythm of a 1st edition Josh is a little less forgiving of misreads and a little more forgiving of gutsy, well-timed gambits.

## Josh in the 2nd edition deck: a cleaner, smarter upgrade
When you shift to 2nd edition, the Josh cards come with a polish that makes the entire survivor deck easier to read at a glance. The core identity remains the same: Josh is the nimble mover who can link objectives and shepherd teammates through peril. The difference lies in readability, icon alignment, and minor balance nudges that keep the card relevant as rules evolve across the line.

### The readability upgrade
- Streamlined action keywords reduce cognitive load. If you have spent a long night fighting a rolling cloud of walkers, you will appreciate fewer moments of hunting for a symbol that means a different thing on the card.
- Consistent iconography across the survivor deck. The 2nd edition tends to reuse icons you already know from other survivors, which reduces the time you spend cross-referencing during a crucial turn.
- More legible text blocks. The spacing is better, so you can parse the entire turn sequence without needing a magnifying glass or a dedicated pre-roll of coffee.

### Gameplay impact and pacing
The 2nd edition changes are subtle, but they matter in a campaign setting. Josh remains a high tempo agent who wants to keep the squad moving, but with better reliability on the timing of his actions. In practice, this translates to fewer stalled turns and a higher chance that your setup moves will land in a single clean rhythm rather than a patchwork of partial successes.

### Sample card text from 2nd edition (illustrative, not exact)
- Action: Move up to 4 spaces
- Special: If you end your move in line of sight to an ally, you may perform a free assist action
- Flavor: Speed is the alive version of a plan

The subtle improvements here are meaningful for players who value quick turns and clean sequencing. A 2nd edition Josh tends to feel like a more confident driver who knows the map well enough to deviate from the planned route and improvise with less risk.

Season 1 injects a campaign vibe into the base game, and Josh becomes a central piece in the evolving narrative. For Josh, this often means his mobility becomes even more valuable because the campaign rewards dynamic repositioning and rapid responses to evolving threats.

During Season 1 you will likely find yourself planning around a series of linked objectives. Josh can serve as the chain that connects those objectives, moving along a route that threads together multiple mission goals. The campaign also provides new loot tokens and specialized items, allowing Josh to pick up gear that further enhances his mobility or his capacity to contribute to team objectives in meaningful ways.

### Mission pacing and Josh as a narrative engine
A well run Season 1 campaign leverages Josh as a narrative engine, a character that makes it plausible that the team moves as a unit rather than a loose collection of heroes. His cards become a reflection of the campaign tempo: if the season asks for rapid pushes to outpace a looming threat, Josh will be your accelerant. If the season demands careful extraction and careful planning, his mobility becomes a flexible tool that lets your squad stay one step ahead of danger.

### Section tips for running Season 1 with Josh
- Map out a few preferred routes that connect safe houses to loot caches. Josh becomes the map maker who determines whether a run is reckless or calculated truth.
- Synchronize event draws with Josh movements. If an event card offers a temporary shield or a reposition option, hold it in reserve for a moment when Josh can sprint into an advantageous position.
- Pair mobility with support actions from teammates. When Josh can draw attention with a fast dash while a friend takes a long range shot or heals someone, the entire squad wins on tempo and morale.

## The art and the feel: a design language across editions
The visual language of Josh cards follows a practical arc. In 1st edition the aesthetic is rugged and tactile; the feel matches the instruction booklets of the late print run period, with a bit of retro charm. The text blocks feel compact, the icons robust, and the overall design communicates a sense of urgency without sacrificing clarity.

In 2nd edition the design language shifts toward clarity and consistency. The icons are more uniform with the rest of the survivor deck, the color coding is more pronounced, and the layout supports a quicker read even for players who might have just stepped away from a long day at work. The flavor text remains, but it is more integrated into the visual layout, allowing it to act as a flavor boost rather than a distraction from game decisions.

If you savor the micro-arts of tabletop games, you will find both editions offer something to appreciate. The 1st edition cards give you a sense of the game’s early energy; the 2nd edition cards give you the sense of a polished, modern board game that still respects the soul of the original concept.

## Strategy notes: playing Josh like a pro in Season 1
Josh is a character who rewards flexible thinking and careful risk management. The Season 1 context amplifies this, because the campaign is designed to reward smart maneuvering just as it punishes reckless sprinting into a back alley of snarling zombies.

Here are concrete strategies to get the most from Josh across editions:
- Use Josh as a leash for your squad. His presence in the front line creates space for teammates to cycle through objectives without getting bogged down in a single location.
- Prioritize routes that connect multiple mission goals. Josh can hop between objectives quickly, turning a two objective run into a single turn sequence with the right setup.
- Balance risk and reward on loot. In Season 1 you may gain access to loot that improves your next move or adds a new ability. Track how much you value the next turn versus the potential threat you face in the current turn.
- Build a small synergy triangle. Pair Josh with a sturdy melee fighter to anchor the front line and a ranged hero who can pick off threats at a distance while Josh moves past.
- Practice quick reads. The 2nd edition helps you read the turn faster, which is essential when you are staring down a door that has more than two silhouettes behind it.

## House rules and optional variants that keep Season 1 fresh
If you want to extend vibes beyond the core experience, you can try a few friendly house rules that complement Josh and the Season 1 flow:
- Pause actions for cinematic effect. Allow a hero to declare a momentary extra action once per game night in exchange for a minor penalty at the end of the round. This keeps the storytelling aspect strong while maintaining balance.
- Progressive fatigue for survivors. After a fixed number of rounds, survivor cards gain a fatigue marker that reduces your action pool slightly. This can simulate the toll of a long campaign and test your ability to leverage mobility with restraint.
- Emergency backup cards. Create a small extra deck of one time use cards that can be drawn when you really need a boost. These cards can reward a bold move by Josh or give a safety net for the rest of the team.
