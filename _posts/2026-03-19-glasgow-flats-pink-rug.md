### Cleaning and Care 101
- Regular cleaning: Vacuum weekly to remove dust that you can't see but your lungs can feel. A low-to-mid suction works best to avoid pulling fibers.
- Spill management: Blot, do not rub. Rubbing invites the stain to migrate like a bad plot twist.
- Stains: For common kitchen mishaps, a mild detergent solution is enough. For tougher stains, consider professional cleaning every 6-12 months depending on your usage and pet situation.
- Dander and hair: A quick run with a pet-specific brush or self-cleaning vacuum can keep the rug looking fresh without turning your living room into a lint roller convention.

### Non-Slip Reality Check
In a Glasgow flat, safety is not a joke; it is a necessity. The rug should come with a non-slip pad or be paired with one to prevent the old carpet slide. In small spaces, even a tiny slip can become an inconvenient adventure during midnight snack runs. If your rug doesn’t have its own grip, add a rug pad underneath to anchor it. This keeps it stable during your most dramatic chair swivels and also protects the floor beneath from wear and tear.

### A Quick Rug Buying Guide for Small Spaces
- Size matters: measure your seating area and leave 6-12 inches around the furniture to create a framed space.
- Pile height: for high-traffic areas, go for lower to medium pile for easier cleaning and longer life.
- Backing: non-slip backing or a pad reduces movement and protects floors.
- Color and pattern: a consistent color family is easier to style; patterns can be used to add interest but may make a small space look busier if not balanced.
