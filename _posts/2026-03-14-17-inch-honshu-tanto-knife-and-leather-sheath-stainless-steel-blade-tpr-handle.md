## Maintenance and Care

The stainless nature of the blade makes cleanup relatively straightforward. After use, wipe with a dry cloth to remove moisture and wipe away any sap, oils, or grime. If you’ve taken it into a damp environment, a light coat of oil helps prevent rust and keeps the blade’s surface looking clean rather than heroic. When it’s time to sharpen, a standard sharpening stone or a guided sharpening system will work. Given the tanto geometry, you’ll want to maintain the primary edge and tip with a careful approach rather than aggressive grinding. Regular honing will help preserve edge geometry and reduce the need for heavy sharpening sessions.

### Quick care tips

- Clean, dry, and oil after use in corrosive environments (sea air, acidic foods, etc.)
- Store in a dry area to prevent moisture buildup in the leather sheath
- Inspect the leather sheath for stiffness and treat with leather conditioner to extend life
- Sharpen with light passes and maintain the bevel angles to avoid removing too much material
