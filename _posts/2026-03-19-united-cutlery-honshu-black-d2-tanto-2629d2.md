## Maintenance and care: keeping D2 honest

D2 steel rewards proper care. It’s not a stainless steel, so you’ll want to wipe it down after use in wet conditions and apply a light coat of oil to prevent surface rust, especially on the blade’s edge and fuller areas. This is not a knife that can be neglected in the name of “set it and forget it.” A quick wipe, a touch of oil, and a gentle blade pass on your preferred sharpening system will keep the edge performing well for longer.

Sharpening D2 is quite doable with standard sharpening stones or belts. The edge retention is good, so you won’t be chasing a new edge every day. But unlike soft stainless steels, you’ll notice the need for a bit more effort to bring back that pristine edge after a tough session. If you’re new to D2, you’ll learn fast that a patient, deliberate approach yields the best results. Pro tip: maintain a consistent angle and use a proper hollow or flat grind as appropriate for your chosen sharpening method.
