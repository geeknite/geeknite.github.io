H2: Build quality, minis, and paintability

H3: The sculpt quality and design language

If you’ve handled Zombicide minis before, you’ll know there’s a baseline quality that CMON tends to hit. The Edouard, Doud, and Yomiko sculpts in this box raise the bar in a few key ways. H3: Assembly and ease of painting

As with many Zombicide minis, you’ll likely enjoy the challenge of assembly but you should expect a little fiddling. Edouard’s figure is a showpiece, which means you’ll want to prime and basecoat with care to avoid losing details during cleanup. Doud and Yomiko give you nice contrasts for color blocking, and if you enjoy painting, you’ll appreciate the variety of textures on the armor, clothing, and gear. CMON’s sculpts tend to tolerate aggressive basing, which means you can push your basing creativity without fear of ruining the core sculpt—though you’ll want to keep the basing simple if you’re speed-painting a league night. Also: a small note on scale. This box mostly aligns with standard Zombicide minis; you won’t suddenly be staring at towering giants or impossibly tiny riveted details that break imagination. It’s consistent enough to mix with your existing Zombicide collection, which is important if you’re trying to present a cohesive map of survivors and adversaries to your gaming group.

H2: Tips for getting the most out of the Special Guest Box

H3: Assembly and setup tips

- Plan your painting session: given Edouard’s prominent design, consider batching the three figures in a dedicated session to avoid fatigue and maximize detail retention.

H3: Game-night pacing ideas

- Rotate survivors between players to keep the dynamics fresh; Doud and Yomiko offer slightly different playstyles, which can balance teams that lean heavy in one direction.
