## Introduction

City of Mist has always been a love letter to myths wearing hoodies and sneakers. Fortune Row feels like a surgical upgrade to the brain of that system: a district-focused expansion that gives players more tools to chase legends in a city that refuses to stay quiet. In this review, we’re going to break down what Fortune Row does well, what it stumbles on, and whether your table should dive into its neon-lit alleys.

If you’re new to Fortune Row, you’re not behind, you’re early. If you’re a veteran, you’ll recognize the vibe—neon rain, alleyway poetry, and the sense that the city itself is a character with a few choice opinions about your choices. This expansion is less about reinventing the wheel and more about giving it a sharper edge, a louder horn, and a handful of new features that fit snugly into the City of Mist system without steamrolling the core experience.

> For the curious, a quick refresher: in City of Mist, players are modern mythic figures—Legendary Detectives, Urban Prophets, or Shadowy Prodigies—who juggle dual identities while chasing a hidden city of legends. The Fate-like resource is Fate (a.k.a. Fortune at times), which powers moves, triggers dramatic scenes, and unlocks story momentum. Fortune Row doubles down on that premise with a district-centric lens that makes the city feel like a stage where legends perform in a way that actually affects the players’ lives, not just the story on page two.

## What Fortune Row Brings to the Table

### A New District, A Fresh Flavor
Fortune Row introduces a distinct district in the city map, drenched in neon, rain-slick pavement, and banners that whisper the city’s secrets. This isn’t just a fresh backdrop; it’s a sandbox where locale drives plot. The district comes with its own assets (locations you can drop into scenes), conditions (things the players can leverage or be trapped by), and a menu of hooks ready to be dropped into a session. The design makes it easy to pivot a campaign toward a Fortune Row arc without feeling like you’re bending the rest of the city to fit a pre-packaged story.

### New Syndicates and Social Lattice
Fortune Row isn’t shy about giving you more factions with personality. The syndicates aren’t mere villains; some act as patron networks, information brokers, or mythic intermediaries. They operate in a legal grey area that fits the City of Mist aesthetic—friendly at the right moments, deadly when crossed, and always with a glare that asks, What story do you want to tell with them? The dynamics between syndicates create natural tension, making both GM-led conflicts and player-driven negotiations feel plausible and thematically satisfying.

### A Reimagined Fortune Mechanic
The heart of the expansion is a reimagined Fortune mechanic. You’ll find a suite of tools—cards, prompts, and duties—that turn luck into a narrative resource with clear costs and benefits. The mechanic emphasizes player agency while still leaving room for the GM to shape outcomes. The result is a cycle of risk and reward that remains in line with the core game’s ethos: mythic scale, street-level stakes, and a generous helping of melodrama.

### Story Seeds, Scenes, and GM Tools
Fortune Row doubles down on GM-facing tools, offering seeds that are immediately runnable either as one-shot adventures or as springboards for longer arcs. The seeds lean into urban legends, corporate machinations, and mythic undercurrents—from a haunted theatre that’s literally auditioning for a larger role to a corporate sponsor whose PR campaign is a front for something far more dangerous.

### The Look and Feel
The art direction continues the neon-noir vibe, with panel-ready splash pages, moody character portraits, and location art that begs to be described aloud. It’s a book that makes you want to flip to the next page just to see the city breathe again. The production quality matches the rest of City of Mist: sturdy paper, clear layouts, and art that invites you to imagine a city where legends live in plain sight—just behind a street mural or inside a nightclub’s back room.

### Crafting the Table Experience
Fortune Row shines most when you lean into the collaborative storytelling. The district-centric framing helps players articulate why their legends matter in a specific part of the city, which keeps scenes grounded even as mythic concepts surge to the surface. If your group thrives on party dynamics, intrigue, and morally gray choices, Fortune Row provides a rich set of levers to pull without forcing you into a railroady rhythm.

## Mechanics Deep Dive

### The Fortune Gauge Revisited
Fortune Row updates how luck operates in play. The Fortune gauge interacts with new moves that let characters tug on fate more aggressively—or protect themselves from its worst impulses. The balance is crafted to reward clever play while punishing reckless gambles in a way that feels fair and thematically on-brand for a city that lives and dies by its legends.

### New Cards and Narrative Techniques
One of the expansion’s standout features is a set of Fortune Cards that players can draw or trigger during scenes. These aren’t just one-off bonuses; they shift narrative momentum and can unlock a chain of consequences that ripple through the arc. Think of them as narrative accelerants: you get a powerful moment, but there’s a price to pay later on. The cards promote creative problem-solving and give players memorable hooks to describe how their legends alter the city’s mood.

### Interaction with Core Mechanics
Fortune Row doesn’t rewrite the wheel. It refines it. The district’s assets and the new syndicates slot neatly into the core rules, so you can mix Fortune Row chapters with existing city sessions without a rulebook wrangle. The design ensures backward compatibility: you can run a Fortune Row one-shot using mostly material from the base game, or you can weave it into a larger arc with the district acting as a recurring focal point.

### NPCs and Conflict Resolution
Non-player characters in Fortune Row feel anchored in the city’s mythic logic. They aren’t just plot devices; they’re a chorus of myths who sound suspiciously human until the moment they don’t. This makes conversations and confrontations feel organic, rather than a string of “fight scenes interspersed with exposition.” The NPCs have motives that tie into the Fortune mechanic, giving GMs clear prompts for escalating tension and delivering wow moments.

## The GM’s Toolkit: How to Run Fortune Row

### Session Planning and Pacing
If your sessions tend toward episodic arcs, Fortune Row provides a structured approach to pacing. It suggests a rhythm of setup (scenes within Fortune Row), escalation (the consequences of failing or overreaching with luck), and reckoning (the moment when the city’s mythic ledger demands payment). The pacing guidance helps you avoid getting stuck in a rut and gives you a reliable cadence to keep players engaged.

### Adventures and Hooks by Theme
Power, memory, deception, and transformation anchor Fortune Row’s seeds. Some narratives lean into corporate intrigue masquerading as philanthropy; others hinge on personal revelations that force characters to reconcile their legend with their real lives. There’s also room for pure mystery solving, should your table crave investigative tempo embedded in a mythic frame.

### Balancing and House Rules
Fortune Row respects the core balance of City of Mist but invites a few table-specific tweaks. It’s easy to adopt a lighter version for shorter campaigns or a heavier version for long-running arcs. The text is explicit about common edge-cases and offers alternative rules to tune the experience for players who prefer fewer dice rolls or more narrative control.

### Player Tips and Hot Takes
New players will appreciate the focused district framing—it gives them a clear starting point for character motivation. Veteran players will enjoy the density of symbolism and the opportunity to thread their backstories into Fortune Row’s iconic locations. One friendly reminder: embrace the city’s contradictions. Fortune Row thrives when players lean into mythic symbolism while negotiating real-world consequences.

## Thematic and Aesthetic Analysis

Fortune Row captures the essence of City of Mist: a modern myth toolkit that lets players own their legends while the city itself tests their resolve. The expansion’s best moments emerge when a player’s character taps into a local myth and the district’s atmosphere amplifies the scene’s stakes. It’s easy to over-index on neon aesthetics, but Fortune Row keeps the focus on the human (and often fragile) choices at the heart of legend.

The art and layout reinforce this by using composition that mirrors the theme of visibility and performance. You can picture a character stepping into a crowd and becoming larger-than-life as the panel titles cue a dramatic beat in the background. The result isn’t just “look how pretty this is”—it’s “this is how it feels to live inside a myth in a city that never sleeps.”

## Pros and Cons at a Glance

- Pros:
  - District-focused conceit adds a tangible playground for stories.
  - Flexible Fortune mechanic that scales from one-shots to long arcs.
  - Rich NPCs and plot hooks that feel integrated, not tacked on.
  - Strong thematic consistency with the neon-noir vibe.

- Cons:
  - Some players might crave more combat hooks; Fortune Row leans narrative rather than crunch.
  - The synergy with other City of Mist expansions can vary depending on table taste.
  - A few seeds assume a certain level of GM confidence with pacing; beginners may want to sandbox more.

## Final Verdict: Should you pick Fortune Row up?

If you love City of Mist’s core premise and you want a refreshing district-driven lens to tell character-driven mythic stories, Fortune Row is a strong addition. It doesn’t erase the core game’s identity; it sharpens it. It gives your GM more threads to pull, more moments to describe, and more opportunities for players to feel like legends who can shape the city—without steamrolling their humanity. It’s particularly recommended for tables that enjoy noir mood, mythic symbolism, and a healthy dose of dramatic tension.

That said, Fortune Row isn’t a necessity for every group. If your table prioritizes fast-paced combat or you’re not as interested in district-level storytelling, you can still enjoy the base game and other expansions. Fortune Row shines brightest when used as a focused arc or a seasonal theme rather than a blanket replacement for your usual sessions.

## How to Integrate Fortune Row into Your Campaign

- Start with a single Fortune Row arc to test the waters. Use one or two seeds to establish mood and see how your players respond to the new mechanics.
- Use the new Syndicates as antagonists for a mid-season arc, letting their choices pull the players into district-level conflicts.
- Weave Fortune Cards into pivotal scenes. They’re perfect for a cliffhanger moment or a dramatic push toward a big, mythic reveal.
- Alternate between Fortune Row sessions and your regular city sessions to prevent the expansion from overshadowing your ongoing story.

## Story Seeds: A Quick Menu for GMs

- The Neon Baptism: A ritual in a Fortune Row church-like venue that promises clarity but delivers memory loss. Players must recover a memory that reveals a city secret and decide whether the truth is worth the pain.
- The Ledger’s Echo: An information broker’s ledger is said to rewrite fate—but every entry erases something someone values. The team must decide what they’re willing to lose for a glimpse of the bigger picture.
- The Velvet Chain Gala: A charity gala turns into a ritual performance where each attendee’s myth is showcased on stage. The players must interrupt or direct the show before the city’s myths decide to stage a coup.
- The Archive of Murmurs: A library that stores whispers—the city’s regrets. Accessing it could grant a master plan to bring down a corrupt syndicate, but it could also awaken a memory the team would rather forget.

## Final Recommendation

Fortune Row is a stylish, well-crafted expansion that respects City of Mist’s DNA while giving the table a fresh playground to explore myth and city life. It’s not a replacement for the core game, but a robust extension that can uplift a campaign with district-level storytelling, new tools for both players and GMs, and a strong mood that translates well to one-shots or mini-arcs. If you enjoy urban fantasy with a poet’s eye for symbolism and a detective’s instinct for puzzles, Fortune Row should be on your must-buy list. It’s a companion that invites you to narrate the city as a living myth rather than as a backdrop for caper after caper, and that distinction matters.

If you’re sitting on the fence, start with a one-shot to test the new mechanics and then decide whether to fold Fortune Row into a longer arc. The district’s prompts are easy to drop into a session, and the synergy with existing City of Mist rules makes integration painless rather than a chore. In short: Fortune Row earns its neon glow, handles the mythic toolkit with care, and gives your table a few new stories that feel both personal and cosmic in scope.
