## Authenticity, Grading, and How to Spot a Real 1984 Olympic Bicycle Deck
If you have a keen eye for authenticity, you will want to do a few quick checks. First, verify the year of issue. The packaging on these is vintage, but not so fragile that a careful inspection reveals obvious reproduction telltales. Look for a stock that feels sturdy, and check for the presence of the official USPCC imprint somewhere on the tuck box or the bottom edge. The two-deck set will have consistent imagery across both boxes, with identical typography and back designs that align when the boxes sit side by side. A believable seal is rarely perfect after 40 years, so minor imperfections in the cellophane wrapper or slight edge wear to the outer packaging do not automatically doom authenticity; they can be expected in well-preserved sealed decks. 

### Common red flags for counterfeit or later reissues
- Highly glossy or overly vibrant back colors that look too bright for the era
- Inconsistent font kerning or misalignment of the Bicycle logo across the tuck box
- A modern plastics feel to the wrap that seems out of sync with the paper stock
- Any claims of “new old stock” with dubious provenance that doesn’t line up with recognized distribution channels

### Authentication steps you can do at home
- Compare the back pattern to period references; the fan design should be consistent with mid-80s Bicycle prints
- Check the weight and feel of the card stock; early 80s Bicycle cards had a particular heft that differs from many post-90s reissues
- Inspect the Joker card for the customary Bicycle joker artwork, which should align stylistically with the rest of the deck
- If the cellophane is torn, look for replacement tapes or visible resealing that would indicate someone opened and resealed the deck
