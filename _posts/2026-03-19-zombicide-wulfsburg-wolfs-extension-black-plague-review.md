## Rules and Mechanics: What the Wolves Change

The core concept of the Wulfsburg Wolves is that wolves are not just extra bites; they have a distinct tactical footprint that shifts how you plan your turns. The wolves act as wild guardians of the night, often using terrain to their advantage and sometimes working as a pack that can trap sloppier players into cornered risk. For players used to the rhythm of Black Plague, the wolves introduce a new tempo that can feel like a boss fight in a side quest. You will need to coordinate movement, zone control, and callouts with your team to avoid letting a pack take you apart piece by piece.

Here are a few of the core ideas you’ll encounter in play:
- Wolf behavior emphasizes pack dynamics. They move in ways that encourage you to consider space and line of sight more keenly than with standard zombies.
- Encounter cards featuring wolves tilt the balance in subtle but meaningful ways. They may spawn in waves, ambush you from edges, or force you to split attention between multiple threats.
- Terrain matters more with wolves present. Certain tiles may become chokepoints that wolves exploit, so map layout gains a new layer of significance. This is not a heavy simulation of AI; it is a thematic nudge toward smarter positioning and timing.
- A few wolves may possess special abilities that can surprise you if you are not paying attention. The learning curve is there, but not insurmountable; after a couple of rounds you start to picture the board as a forest with a full moon and a plan rather than a chaotic crowd of red cardboard.

In terms of rules integration, the extension uses the standard Zombicide approach: you still coordinate with your teammates, your survivors carry multiple actions per turn, and the wolves function as a threat that rewards careful planning. The wolves push you to re-evaluate how you allocate your actions across the turn rather than simply trying to maximize damage output. That shifted emphasis is what makes the extension feel fresh rather than just a new layer of attrition.

## Strategy and Tactics: Making the Most of the Wulfsburg Wolves

Here are a few tips that we found useful in actual play. You do not need to memorize every nuance, but a couple of guiding ideas can help you reap the benefits of this expansion without becoming a slave to the wolves:
- Positioning matters more with wolves on the board. Take a minute to map potential wolf routes and anticipate where a pack could come from. The wolves love a good ambush, so think a turn ahead rather than just the current moment.
- Use terrain to your advantage. If a wolves pack can be funneled through narrow corridors or open spaces with chokepoints, you can control their movement and set up a deadly counter-attack with your survivors. The board is not just a battlefield; it is a stage with theatrical entrances.
- Coordinate your actions. The wolves reward team-aware play. Talk through your turn order, call out threats, and avoid letting one wolf arc into a multi-zombie melee on a whim. The more you communicate, the more you feel like a well-run, albeit briefly chaotic, pack of survivors.
- Mix it with a robust scavenging plan. The Wulfsburg Wolves tend to force you to choose between a quick objective and a safer, more controlled approach. If your squad can keep the objective in sight while managing fear of the wolves, you will have the best of both worlds: progress and survival.
- Consider house rules for group size. In larger tables, wolves can be introduced in waves to ensure there is always something to react to. In smaller groups, you might want to stage a single pack per turn so the suspense remains high without overwhelming a lone team.
