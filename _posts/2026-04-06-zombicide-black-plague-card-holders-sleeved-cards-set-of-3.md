Sleeved Card Compatibility

Sleeves introduce thickness to the card’s profile. Your standard Dragon Shield or Ultra Pro sleeves add a bit of swagger to those chonky little rectangles. The Card Holders claim compatibility with standard sleeved cards typically found in Zombicide groups. In practice, you’ll want to verify a couple of details:

- Sleeve thickness: Most standard sleeves hover around 0.3–0.5 mm thick. The holders feel most comfortable in this range. If you’ve gone heavy on the protective gear with oversize sleeves, you may experience a bit more friction as you slide cards in and out.
- Card height and width: Zombicide cards are not all created equal—some characters and items are slightly larger. The holders are optimized for the most common dimensions in the base game and common expansions; you’ll likely find that the fit is snug but not unyielding for standard-sized sleeves.
- Chamfer and lips: The shallow lip helps guide the card into place without needing a magician’s touch. You’ll notice sleeves don’t get stuck on the edge, which is a nice win when you’re trying to set up a mission before the first zombie grumbles out of the box.

If you’re the experimental type who loves pitting odd-ball sleeves against everything in your collection, you may want to test fit before a critical game night. The good news: these aren’t fragile like a porcelain teacup; they’re more like a reliable, boring utility knife—works every time, looks neat, and doesn’t demand your soul after you’ve used it once.

Setup Tips and Best Practices
============================

If you’re new to using card holders, a few tips can make a noticeable difference:

- Pre-sort your sleeved decks by mission type or player count before the game begins. The holders encourage organization, and pre-sorting saves you from a post-mit-sis scramble that ends with a stray card sneaking under the terrain like a zombie wearing sunglasses.
- Use the three-holder setup to separate core decks from expansion decks. This not only makes it easier to locate the cards you need mid-mission but also reduces the chance of an accidental reveal when you’re flipping boards for a new encounter.
- Rotate sleeves periodically. If you’re using sleeves that are starting to wear at the corners, consider updating them and re-inserting into the holders. It helps to maintain a clean, consistent look across your table and reduces the chance of corner wear on sleeved cards.
