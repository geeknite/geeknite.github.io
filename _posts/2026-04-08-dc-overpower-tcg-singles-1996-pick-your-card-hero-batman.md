## What is DC Overpower? A Quick Refresher (With 90s Flavor)
DC Overpower is a collectible card game released in the mid- to late-1990s that pitted DC Comics heroes and villains against each other in a battle of oddly specific powers and triggered abilities. The singles angle—our focus today—refers to cards sold individually or in small sets rather than the big booster packs. The Pick Your Card Hero concept is all about choosing a hero and building a tiny, focused deck around that character’s unique power suite.

From a design perspective, Overpower borrowed a few ideas that felt both clever and chaotic in the best possible way: you had a variety of power icons, a blend of cosmic-level feats and street-level brawling, and a sense that every card could be the one that tilts the entire matchup. Batman, in particular, offered a compelling package: detective work, gadgetry, contingency plans, and a moral compass that somehow translated into mechanical advantages when you got your act together.

### Practical Advice for Collectors
- Condition matters more in older sets: near-mint to mint will fetch a higher premium.

- Keep a small binder, a few top-loaders, and an eye on condition—the best way to preserve value is to keep cards sealed in a humidity-controlled environment, preferably away from the kind of snacks that turn your card collection into a snack collection.
