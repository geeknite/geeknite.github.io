It is loud, it is chaotic, and it is surprisingly tactical once you learn to herd your party like cats chasing a laser pointer that spawns too many walkers.

## Overview

Zombicide Black Plague is a cooperative board game for 1–6 players (the base game supports up to 6, with expansions pushing it further into the chaos zone). Players control Survivors who each bring unique abilities to the table. The board is modular, built from tile decks that can be rearranged to create new cities of doom every time you play. Zombies spawn in waves, guided by a deck of spawn tokens and the ominous yellow-ish glow of the town’s ever-present candlelight – a texture of doom that would make even a friendly NPC scream for mercy.

Key selling points include:

- A rich progression system where Survivors level up by earning XP. New abilities open up, letting you tailor your team to your play style (or your mischief level).
- A vast array of weapons, armor pieces, and items that let you customize your Survivors with swords, axes, crossbows, magical staffs, and ridiculous armor ratings. The table will become a loot pinata, and you will love every moment of it.
- A satisfying balance between strategy and chaos. You can plan your route, but one failed dice roll can derail even the best-laid plan – and that wreckage usually leads to hilarious or horrific outcomes.

The game shines when you lean into cooperation. If you try to power through solo or ignore your teammates, you will facepalm your way into a pile of tokens you forgot to pick up. The trick is to use each other’s strengths, share items strategically, and manage noise tokens like a social influencer managing spoilers for a big reveal.

## Components and Setup

The box is packed with your typical board game papercraft: punchboard zombies, plastic miniatures, hundreds of cards, and tiles that create a city map that would be terrifying if it wasn’t so charmingly medieval. The components hold up under time and use, though the plastic miniatures can be a little fiddly when you’re shoving them in and out of the board gaps. The art direction is superb, with moody browns and greens that scream fantasy horror rather than grimdark realism. It’s the kind of game that makes you want to host a “dark dungeon dinner party” and wear a cloak while you roll dice.

Setup, while slightly fiddly, becomes routine after your first two sessions. You lay out a modular map, place item decks, spawn points, and the initial infection/wave track. Survivors choose their characters, equip them with a starter kit, and you’re ready to march toward doom with confidence that you’ve watched at least one tutorial video and 2 YouTube short clips about zombie movement – yes, the nerd in you still has followers.

Two quick reminders before you dive in:

- Always check the stash of weapons before you commit to a path. It’s not unusual to find a legendary blade that absolutely changes the math on a corridor ambush. 
- Don’t forget to keep track of noise tokens. Noise is your friend and your worst enemy in equal measure; use it to lure zombies away from your group, but never forget that noise also accelerates the zombie spawn deck. It’s a double-edged dagger that really wants chores and pizza night to end cleanly.

## Core Mechanics in a Nutshell

ZBP is a balance of movement, combat, and item management with a sprinkle of randomness. The Survivors have action economies that require you to manage turns carefully. Zombies are drawn from a spawn deck and placed on the board, then they advance, chase survivors, and bump into you in waves. The combat system uses dice: you roll a number of attack dice equal to your weapon and your skill, and you try to get hits to defeat the wave of corpses, the board, and whatever else the designer threw into the crafter’s cauldron of chaos.

Two big design choices drive the game’s feel:

- XP and leveling: As you eliminate zombies and complete objectives, your Survivors earn XP, which unlocks new abilities and better gear. The progression gives players a tangible sense of growth and a reason to cooperate beyond “we might win.”
- Gear variety: The item deck is a treasure map with a twist. You can pick up swords, axes, crossbows, and magical items that do everything from increase your attack dice to adding special effects like poison or stun. The strategy lies in choosing which items to equip and when to swap for something better, all while not leaving your buddy in the lurch with a rusty dagger and a dream.

Strategically, the aim is to split the party just enough to cover more exits while keeping your most valuable Survivors within reach. The game rewards planning, but punishes hubris when a single misstep cascades into a zombie avalanche that would embarrass a Tokyo subway rush hour.

## Turn Structure and Phase Breakdown

Each round is broken into distinct phases. Knowing the order matters far more than your dice goblin’s mother. A typical round includes:

- Activate Phase: Each Survivor gets a number of actions (usually 2) per turn. You move, search for loot, pick up items, and attack. Movement is essential to controlling the map, especially when the board is tight and corridors demand careful choreography.
- Zombie Phase: The zombie engine chugs along. Zombies come out of spawn points, and then they move toward the nearest survivor. Runners sprint, Walkers polish their aim, and Fatty zombies threaten to ruin your elegant spa day with their sheer bulk. You’ll want to position your team to funnel zombies into chokepoints where you can whittle them down safely.
- End Phase: Cleanup. You discard used items, resolve any effect timers, and check the fate of any survivors who’ve barely avoided the jaws of the undead. The objective at this phase is to stay alive and not lose your soul to the great coffee machine in the sky.

The trick is to plan multiple turns ahead. You’re not just playing chess with zombies; you’re playing chess with possible brains. The board will change every time you play, so be ready to adapt and improvise with a smile or a groan, depending on the outcomes of your dice rolls.

## Strategy by Phase

### Early Game (Rounds 1–2): Establish the Anchor
In the opening rounds, your objective is simple but not trivial: set up strong Survivors with decent weapons, map the board’s critical zones, and secure some early loot that gives you options for future rounds. Movement should be deliberate; don’t sprint into a crowded hallway unless you’re ready to stage a surgical retreat.

- Position your primary damage dealers near chokepoints to maximize the efficacy of your first line of defense. The zombies will push through in waves; you’ll want capable Survivors to hold the line without being overwhelmed.
- Prioritize items that improve defense and survivability. Armor is cheap insurance; a well-timed shield can save you from a brutal turn that would otherwise turn your party into a cautionary tale about group dynamics.
- Search whenever you can, but not at the cost of your survivability. Sometimes the best move is to stay put and clear a few adjacent tiles rather than risking a stray spawn that destroys your fragile lane.

### Mid Game (Rounds 3–5): Exploit Momentum
The mid game is where your team should start pushing objectives and clearing critical zones. You’ll likely have some Survivors at higher XP levels, granting you more flexibility.

- Use XP-powered abilities to control the battlefield. If you have access to a power-up that allows extra actions or extended attack range, plan your routes to unlock those benefits before a large zombie wave hits.
- Coordinate your team’s actions to maximize kills per round. A well-timed collaboration (one Survivor lures zombies into another’s line of attack) can net you more loot and xp than a lone hero’s heroic but reckless charge.
- The loot you find mid game often changes the plan completely. An artifact that grants extra movement or a damage multiplier changes your route choices and macro-strategy for the rest of the session.

### Late Game (Rounds 6+): Boss Alignments and Last Stand Mentality
When you tip into the later rounds, the city’s defense hinges on a few Survivors who managed to keep their wits and health intact. This is the moment to go for the throat, but only if you survive the next toast of doom.

- Clear strongholds and secure critical exits. The goal is not just to survive but to redeem the last few turns by reaching an objective that ends the scenario. Plan your endgame moves to end on your terms, not the undead’s whim.
- Use the XP unlocks to unlock late game tools, whether that’s enhanced defense or an extra attack die. A well-timed upgrade can flip a near-loss into a triumphant victory.
- Don’t neglect escaping with survivors. Saving the day is meaningful, but if your team can get a critical survivor out, you’ll be impressed by the satisfying light at the end of the token-laden tunnel.

## Survivors, Gear, and XP: The Vicious Circle

A large portion of ZBP’s flavor comes from how Survivors level up and how gear empowers them. You do not win with brute force alone; you win with clever, cooperative fishing for the right items at the right moments.

### Survivors and Abilities
Each character starts with a unique ability that defines their role. You’ll want to balance your party with a mix of front-line brawlers, ranged specialists, and scouts who can explore corners or swing into a newly opened zone. The XP system rewards you for kills, completing objectives, and using your abilities effectively. As you level, you unlock new skills that let you personalize your character into a sniper, a shield-bearing tank, a rogue who dodges doom, or a mage who hurls elemental chaos at the undead.

Strategic takeaway: avoid stacking too many of the same archetype. A balanced party will have fewer blind spots and should be able to react to varying threats in different zones of the map.

### Gear and Weapons
Weapons vary in attack dice, range, and special effects. Some are straightforward, like swords and axes that give you additional attack dice. Others are more exotic: magical staves that trigger extra effects, armor that reduces incoming damage, or gadgets that reposition zombies without losing a turn. The loot economy is a balancing act: picking a weapon too early can lock you into a suboptimal path; waiting too long can leave you bare for a brutal spawn phase.

Pro tip: early upgrades that increase survivability (armor) tend to pay off in the long run because they keep you in the game long enough to reap more XP. However, if you have a chance to grab a weapon that significantly boosts your damage, do the math and think about your team’s ability to clear the board quickly. Sometimes a fast clear is better than a slow, heroic but doomed stand.

### XP, Levels, and Abilities
XP lets your Survivors unlock powerful new tactics and abilities. The early levels grant practical, incremental advantages, while later levels swing the game in your favor by giving you access to stronger actions or additional dice. It’s a simple loop: survive, earn XP, use XP to unlock tactics, survive longer, earn more XP, etc. It’s the best kind of RPG loop: satisfying, measurable, and mercilessly addictive.

Important note: don’t chase XP at the expense of team cohesion. A lone hero sprinting on a two-turn rampage is fun, but the game is designed for cooperation. You get more long-term XP by completing objectives as a group than by a glory run that leaves your teammates in a corner crying over a broken weapon and a pool of regrets.

## Tactics: Movement, Combat, and the Dance of Noise

ZBP rewards careful positioning and the strategic use of space. The board is a living, breathe-with-dust character; your path through it should be a choreography, not a marching band.

- Movement is your friend, often your best tool to control the pace. Use corridors to bottleneck zombies and pick off stragglers with ranged weapons. The fewer directions a zombie can come from at once, the smaller your dice pool becomes to fear. This is a math problem that also involves loud noises, and the bigger your noise footprint, the bigger your problems.
- Combat requires a risk-reward calculus. Do you spend extra actions to shoot the gap and clear a tile, or do you hold to defend a more valuable position? If you’re facing a Fatty, you might have to commit more resources than you’d like, but the payoff is worth it. Remember that some weapons have special effects that can stun, poison, or push zombies away, which can change the whole board state in a single turn.
- Noise management is the real boss fight. Every action can create noise tokens, which accelerate spawn rates. If you’re careful, you can bait the undead into predictable patterns that let you thin the herd without inviting a bloodbath. If you’re reckless, you’ll get swarmed like a pumpkin spice festival on a sunny day: fun for the first five minutes, but then you realize you’ve made a terrible life choice.

## Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them
- Over-committing to a single lane. You’ll get ganged up on and lose a critical Survivor in a single round. Rotate survivors and maintain cross-fire coverage.
- Ignoring upgrades. It’s tempting to cling to your starting weapon for bragging rights, but upgrades are expensive for a reason. Don’t be afraid to swap out stale gear as soon as a better option emerges.
- Forgetting the objective. In the chaos, it’s easy to chase zombies and forget the goal. Always keep the scenario’s objective in sight and plan a path toward achieving it, even if it looks a little boring at times.
- Underestimating the small stuff. A single weapon with a range boost or a single piece of armor can dramatically increase your team’s survival odds. Don’t sleep on the tiny upgrades; they are the glue that holds your strategy together.

## Scenarios, Campaigns, and Difficulty

The game ships with a set of scenarios that vary by objective and map layout. Some are straightforward, while others demand surgical precision and near-microscopic teamwork. If you’re new, start with a simpler scenario to get the hang of the pacing and the rhythm of the zombie waves. As your group grows more confident, you can try more complex setups and longer campaigns that teach you new tricks and test your coordination under pressure.

If you want more variety, you can mix expansions or combine multiple scenarios into a campaign. Be aware that this cranks up the difficulty and can make you feel like you’ve just launched a small anarchist colony of undead on your dining room table. But in a good way — it’s the kind of chaos that makes for legendary stories when you finally beat it.

## Co-op Communication: The Real Hack of the Game

The core of ZBP’s replayability is how well your team communicates. It’s not enough to say “I attack.” You must coordinate your actions with timing and awareness:

- Callouts matter. Let your team know which tile you’re targeting, which zombies you intend to chase, and which weapon you plan to swap in. If you don’t talk, you’ll end up with duplicates and a messy hand that doesn’t do anything.
- Plan a tempo. Decide on a rough beat: advance, hold, heal, retreat. The tempo lets you maximize your team’s synergy and ensures you don’t accidentally trap someone behind a wall of clumsy decisions.
- Laugh at the chaos. The best sessions feel like a movie montage with a sound effect for every misstep. The laughter is the glue that keeps your party functioning, even when you’ve just lost a strong survivor to a doorway ambush.
