## How to Play with the Add-On Pack

### Rules and integration with standard UNO
The good news is: you can drop these cards into a standard UNO deck with minimal adjustments. The core rulebook remains intact: match color or number, draw cards when you’re forced, and be the first to empty your hand. The Billie add-on introduces a handful of new card types that operate within the existing rules framework, but there are a few caveats:
- Wild Mood cards: Activate a mood, change color, and sway the turn order with a theatrical flourish. Use them sparingly; too many mood flips can devolve into table chaos or group therapy.
- Lyric-quote Skip cards: If you use one, you must read the lyric aloud (or attempt to). This adds a social performance element that can be entertaining or cringe-worthy, depending on your group’s tolerance for performance art.
- Draw-Plus cards: These require a mini-challenge (hum a Billie lyric, name a Billie track, or perform a tiny impromptu dance) before the next player draws. It’s optional but highly encouraged during family gatherings and awkward friend reunions.

### Strategy notes and practical tips
- Use mood cards strategically to force color changes that disrupt a player who seems impossible to beat. Billie’s mood prompts create momentum swings, which can be fun for a while but must be controlled to avoid endless rotation around the table.
- Lyric-quote timing is everything. Don’t let someone derail the game by reading a lyric too early; let it come at a moment that amplifies tension—like when someone’s down to their last card and they desperately need a color that you can block with a well-timed mood card.
- Be mindful of the Draw-Plus cards: they’re designed for social fun, not for serious advantage. Use them to prompt jokes or mini-challenges, not to humiliate your friend who can’t carry a tune in the car’s AC mode.

### Variants and house rules
If you want a more streamlined experience, drop the lyric-quote and mood cards entirely and play a purer UNO variant. Conversely, if your group lives for karaoke and improv, lean into the extra rules and declare a Billie night with a playlist and a scoreboard for best performance. The add-on is versatile enough to accommodate both extremes, which is the sign of a well-designed thematic kit.
