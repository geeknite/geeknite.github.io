Honey Fun for the Curious: a Quickie Science Beat
Honey’s complexity is part chemistry, part biology, and part time-travel routine that makes you wonder how something so simple can be so nuanced. The bees collect nectar, dehydrate it, and enzyme-introduce it into the process. The result is a stable sugar solution that the bees then store in honeycomb cells. The antioxidants and trace minerals in raw honey can vary with the flora, season, and local microbe ecosystem.
