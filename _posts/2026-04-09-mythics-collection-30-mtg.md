## Quality control: how to evaluate the standouts in a mythics lot
A 30-card lot is a mixed bag. If you’re starting a collection, here are practical tips to help you identify the stars (and the maybes):

- Centering and edge wear: Look for cards with decent centering from top to bottom and left to right. Edge wear is a vibe killer for graded values, so if you’re chasing a set that might ever grade, you’ll want to separate the likely grade-friendly cards from the ones that look like they’ve endured a long voyage through the wilds of a binder.
- Bleed and borders: Some mythics have artwork that bleeds toward the edges. That’s not necessarily a negative, but it is a factor when you’re considering sleeving and display. If you’re going for a pristine binder, you’ll want to pick the cleanest examples for the front row.
- Reprints vs originals: A reprint mythic with a modern border might be more approachable for casual play, while a vintage original can be a talking point on the shelf. The line here is personal preference and budget. The good news is a 30-card lot usually gives you a spectrum to choose from.
- Playability: If your goal is to actually play with the cards, identify which mythics fit your playstyle, color identity, and local meta. Some mythics are meta-defining in Commander while others are pop-culture icons with minimal direct impact on the board state. It’s about how you balance aesthetics with function.

If you want to maximize value, a practical approach is to sort by potential game impact, identify any sleepers you can sleeve up for casual games, and save the collector-grade candidates for display or resale.
