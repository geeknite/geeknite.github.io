This expansion promises new neighborhoods, new masks, and a toolkit for running sessions that feel like a heist meets prophecy. Does it deliver? ## What is Fortune Row?
Fortune Row is a self contained expansion designed to slot into your existing City of Mist campaign without forcing you to rewrite the city map in a fit of creative panic. The core concept is a satin velvet rope that sections off a nefarious yet glamorous district within the city, a place where luck is a currency, danger is a familiar face, and secrets are traded like stock on a midnight exchange. The box promises three big things: a new district full of hooks, new Masks that feel tailored to the gambler mythos, and a batch of GM resources that turn a one off into a campaign starter that can keep players entertained for several sessions.

If you are a long running GM you might be thinking that this sounds a lot like a standard expansion where you get extra cards and a splash of new art. And you would be right to think that, but Fortune Row goes beyond surface level by weaving its theme into the mechanics. It does not merely append; it recontexts the core rules around fortune, risk, and reputation in a way that makes the city feel alive in a fresh way. The designers lean into the idea that mythic identities can be fluid, and that fortune can swing doors open or slam them shut with a single decision. The effect is a campaign that plays with mood as a feature and not just a backdrop.

Forever a staple of City of Mist is how it invites you to mix the mythic with the mundane. Fortune Row doubles down on that recipe. The district is not just a place you visit; it is a living, breathing character with a rhythm all its own. You will hear the city pulse differently when you step onto the neon streets of Fortune Row. The vibe is noir with brass and myth, a cocktail with an unexpectedly savory twist. If you enjoyed the core game, Fortune Row feels like waking up in a new, glamorous city that still looks suspiciously like your own but with more glitter and higher stakes.

There is a practical note here: Fortune Row is best enjoyed when you already love the core game's fusion of Masks and myth. The expansion assumes you are familiar with the driving mechanics and the structure of a scene. It does not drown you in new rules. Instead it offers a concentrated set of additions that can be dropped into most campaigns with minimal fumbling. If you want a few dozen new plot hooks and a toolkit to spin a fortune arc, this expansion delivers. If you want an overhaul of the system, you will find some interesting ideas here but you might still need to do the heavy lifting yourself.

## The Box Contents and What You Get
Fortune Row presents a compact but dense selection of goodies that are easy to bean out on a table and get players into the mood fast. Here is a quick tour of what you will find inside the box, and why those pieces matter to your playstyle.

### The District Map and Scene Cards
Fortune Row includes a glossy fold out map of the district with key locations you will want to visit. The art style evokes neon rain and slick pavement, a blend of 1940s film noir and urban fantasy. The Scene Cards are the real heart of this release. They are modular, enabling you to piece together a variety of encounters in minutes, which is a godsend for GMs who want to keep sessions moving without burning through prep. The cards come with short hooks, potential complications, and a quick note about where the mythic energy infuses the scene. In practice, this means you can turn a quiet moment into a dramatic arc without rummaging through a pile of index cards at the worst possible moment.

### The Fortune Masks: Gambler, Oracle, and Grifter
No City of Mist product would be complete without new Masks, and Fortune Row brings three very distinct ones designed to resonate with the fortune theme. The Gambler embodies risk and reward, turning near misses into opportunities and turning a bad roll into a memorable character beat. The Oracle acts as a seer for probability itself, guiding the group toward choices that feel inevitable, if not always safe. The Grifter thrives on social manipulation, a Mask that uses public perception and clever misdirection to bend reality in small, story changing ways.

Each Mask comes with a full backstory, move sets that are tuned for Fortune Row's arc, and a handful of sample Deeds to help the group quick start a character within the district. The synergy between the Masks is where the expansion shines. The Gambler and Grifter can push the party into gambits that require careful timing, while the Oracle can pull the kite strings that tether the narrative to Fortune Row’s destiny without forcing fortune to behave.

### New Deeds, Tags, and Plot Hooks
As with other City of Mist expansions, Fortune Row adds a number of plot hooks that are easy to drop into an ongoing campaign. The Deeds encourage the Masks to interact with the district, while the Tags help the GM guide scenes toward the themes of luck, risk, and social tension. If you are a GM who loves to stage a moral dilemma or a high stakes choice with no obvious right answer, Fortune Row provides you with several ready to run scenarios that resist the temptation to solve in one session. The Deeds and Hooks scale nicely with the party, offering a few stronger anchors for a longer arc and several shorter skirmishes you can string into a full night of gaming.

### GM Tools and Encounter Design Kit
One of the underappreciated joys of a good expansion is a GM toolkit that respects your time. Fortune Row delivers with a compact but robust suite of tools: encounter templates, pacing suggestions, and a handful of optional rules to help you weave the district into city wide plot threads. The pacing tips are particularly welcome; they remind GMs that the pace of a noir narrative is a dawdle and a sprint all at once, and Fortune Row gives you the levers to hold that tension without burning a table’s attention. There are optional rules for fortune flows—how luck shifts throughout a scene—and for escalating consequences as the plot threads spiral toward a finale. If you enjoy running long campaigns with escalating stakes, these tools are gold; if you prefer shorter one shots, they still deliver a solid blueprint to scale your session length.

### Artwork, Design, and Production Quality
The art in Fortune Row continues the City of Mist aesthetic with a glossy noir sheen, sharp line work, and moody color palettes that scream both elegance and danger. The layout remains clean and readable, which is essential when you are elbow deep in a tense scene and trying to find the exact clause on a Mask card without taking your eyes off the players. The production quality lives up to the core line: sturdy card stock, well thought out typography, and art that helps reinforce the mood rather than fight you over it. In short, Fortune Row looks like a premium accessory to your City of Mist table, not a budget add on that might as well be a dice bag with pictures on it.

## How it Plays: A Typical Fortune Row Session
If you want the quick mental picture of what running Fortune Row feels like, imagine a casino heist set in a city where fate has a heartbeat and every card you draw could tilt the entire night toward glory or disaster. The Masks provide predictable arcs that players can lean into, while the district gives you the on the ground set pieces to anchor those arcs. A typical session might begin with a glitzy invitation to Fortune Row itself, a place where players are introduced to NPCs who speak in charming euphemisms and offer choices with real consequences.

From there you thread through a sequence of Scene Cards that steer you toward the central questions of the arc: who is the real power behind Fortune Row, what price will players pay for a lucky break, and how will the district respond when luck runs out? The interesting trick is that the Fortune Row mechanics reward both cunning and collaboration. If someone in your party misreads a Fortune Card or misreads a mask, you will see the group pivot in a way that feels organic. This is the metaphorical heart of the product: luck is not a static resource; it moves with the narrative and with the choices of the Masks themselves.

In practice, a four hour session flows something like this. You open with a quick briefing scene in a glitzy lounge or a rain slick street outside a back alley. Then you bounce into a chain of Scenes using the Scene Cards, with the Masks driving the action and the Fortune Flow determining whether luck favors or mercilessly betrays the players. Mid session, you hit a choice point where multiple outcomes exist, each with a different level of risk and reward. If you have run City of Mist before, Fortune Row slots into your rhythm with easy familiarity but still offers new beats to discover. If this is your first run, the district and Masks give you an approachable, cinematic entry point that feels like a little film you can steer with your friends.

## Theme and Tone: Noir, Glamour and Myth Intertwined
Fortune Row leans into a mood that is both glamorous and dangerous. The district is a playground for individuals who believe luck is a muscle you can flex with style. The Masks embody this concept with flavor that feels both fresh and familiar. The Gambler is the type who treats risk as a performance, the Oracle as the whispering wind that suggests outcomes without guaranteeing them, and the Grifter as the persuasive force that can turn a confrontation into a friendly wager. The tone invites players to lean into deception, improvisation, and moral ambiguity, all while chasing a sense of destiny that feels earned rather than handed to them by fate.

If you love the mythic underpinnings of City of Mist but crave a tighter, location based narrative spine, you will find Fortune Row compelling. It gives you a city block that can be revisited, re explored, and re interpreted as your campaign evolves. The risk of the noir energy wearing thin over time is mitigated by the constant possibility of a new angle on fortune itself. This is not a lightweight add on; it requires investment and attention, but the payoff is a more cohesive atmosphere and a richer arc that players can latch onto for multiple sessions.

## Artwork and Surface Quality: Visuals That Move the Story Forward
Fortune Row is a treat for players who enjoy tactile table presence as well as cinematic vibes. The artwork reinforces the themes of glamour and danger, from the glint of a streetlamp on a rain slicked street to the opulent interiors of a hidden casino. The design choices on the scene cards are thoughtful and practical; the icons and text align in a way that speeds up play without sacrificing flavor. The map is not just pretty to look at; it is a tool that helps the GM guide the party to the right locations as the narrative unfolds. You will find yourself describing rooms with more confidence, because the visual cues support your storytelling rather than fight you for airtime.

## How Fortune Row Fits into the City of Mist Ecosystem
Fortune Row is designed to slot into most City of Mist campaigns without requiring major reworks. It is not a replacement for the core mechanics; it operates as a thematic extension that expands the vocabulary available to masks and narrative beats. If you have a campaign that leans into street level intrigue, Fortune Row will feel right at home. If your city already sprawls into darker corners with elaborate political plots, this expansion provides a microcosm that can be dropped into the larger pattern, offering one more stage for your characters to perform miracles of myth and mischief.

For readers who like to compare expansions, Fortune Row sits comfortably next to other City of Mist products without stepping on their toes. The district concept complements the big city approach rather than trying to supplant it. The new Masks are strong enough to deserve play time in their own right, but not so overbearing that they overshadow existing Masks in the party. The design philosophy here seems to be: give players a new flavor, not a new set of constraints. If you want a dramatic shift in your tone, you may still need to adjust your pacing and some scene prompts, but Fortune Row provides just enough frame work to inspire those changes rather than demanding them.

## Play Style Notes: Who Should Buy Fortune Row
This expansion is best suited for groups who enjoy a fixed location arc with a strong thematic through line. If your table loves city based storytelling and players who enjoy masks with clear narrative ambitions, Fortune Row is a solid addition. It rewards players who are willing to engage with social intrigue as a currency and who are not afraid to fail forward. The Fortune Masks provide a strong social engine; use them to push players toward scenes where conversations carry as much weight as combat. If your group prefers a quick dungeon crawl style with minimal role playing heavy lifting, Fortune Row can still work, but you may want to adjust the emphasis toward action oriented Scene Cards and reduce the reliance on dialogue heavy encounters.

If you are new to City of Mist, you can still use Fortune Row as an introduction to the core ideas. It is a friendly entry point that provides a contained environment and a small collection of ready to run scenes that illuminate how Masks interact with a setting. It might not be the place to start if you want to learn all the core mechanics at once, but it is a great next step after you have a feel for the game and want to apply that feeling to a more cinematic, location based narrative.

## Pros and Cons in a Nutshell
- Pros: Focused district with a strong thematic hook; a troisième Masks that feel distinct and usable; scalable Scene Cards that make prep faster; integrated GM tools for pacing and tension; high production value that enhances immersion.
- Cons: It is a location based expansion, which means if your campaign is sprawling across several districts you might find Fortune Row sits on the shelf for a few sessions before you slot it in; some players may want more Masks or mechanics to interact with the district beyond the core concept; those who prefer heavy mechanical changes may find this expansion more mood than rule rework.

If you want to know how Fortune Row compares to other expansions, think of it as a well tailored side quest with a strong thematic spine rather than a general purpose expansion. It adds flavor and a few crisp tools, but it does not overstep into rewriting the rules. In the end, you get a more vivid city and more dramatic moments per session, which is exactly what many players crave from a City of Mist release.

## Final Recommendation
Fortune Row is a confident addition to the City of Mist family. It does not pretend to reinvent the wheel; instead it refines the wheel by adding a district that feels alive, new Masks that feel distinct, and a GM toolkit that makes on the fly session design easier and more satisfying. If you run campaigns that lean into noir mood, social intrigue, and myth backed by compassionate chaos, this expansion will sing at your table. It strikes a balance between new content and narrative focus, giving you plenty of options to craft memorable stories around fortune and fate. The production quality remains high, with art and components designed to support the storytelling rather than overshadow it. If you seek a product that adds real atmosphere and helpful tools without turning your table into a rules heavy gauntlet, Fortune Row is a solid buy.
