## Practical Considerations and Caveats
### Space requirements and room planning
Let us be candid: a 9 meter desk demands a room that can handle it. You need a generous corner footprint and wall clearance for cable management and access to the ends for cleaning and QA. If your office is in a converted closet or a space with little natural flow, you may end up with a desk halo that makes the room feel smaller than it is. Measure your floor plan, confirm door swing clearance, and make sure you have a path for deliveries. The last thing you want is a forklift misadventure caused by trying to squeeze a long desk into a narrow corridor. If you are tight on space, consider a two-module variant that splits the 9 meter length into two 4.5 meter pods, which keeps the wow factor while reducing misfit risk.

### Assembly timeline and team logistics
We estimate a two-person installation crew to spend half a day on assembly, plus another half-day for cable routing and fine-tuning. If you have a larger team or your floor has a check-in ritual for deliveries, you might want to schedule the installation as an event, not a task.
