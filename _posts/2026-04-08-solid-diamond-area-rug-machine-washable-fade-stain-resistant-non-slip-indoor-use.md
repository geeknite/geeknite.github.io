Stain resistance is where many people decide to upgrade from cheap rugs to plans that won’t require a catalog of spot-cleaner folklore. The fibers are designed to repel common liquids and to respond well to prompt blotting. Practical tips:

- Blot, don’t rub. Rubbing can embed color into fibers and create a longer-term stain footprint.

## Care and Maintenance: Keeping Your Rug in Heroic Shape
Even the most robust rug benefits from a simple care routine. Here are practical guidelines to maximize performance and longevity:

- Vacuum regularly to remove loose dirt and grit. A quick daily pass keeps debris from milling into fibers and helps maintain the rug’s texture.
- Treat spills immediately with blotting. Wait for a few minutes of heroism; then blot with a clean cloth. Don’t rub, don’t teng—the future self will thank you.
- For machine washing, follow the label: cold water, gentle cycle, mild detergent. Consider washing with other light loads to optimize your machine’s performance and energy use.
- Drying: low heat or air dry. Avoid high heat, which can cause fibers to shrink slightly or lose some resilience. If you air dry, lay flat to maintain shape.
- Periodic rotation: If your room has strong sunlight for part of the day, rotate the rug to equalize wear and fading. This helps prevent slight color imbalance along the rug’s sunniest edge.
- Spot-cleaning for stubborn stains: Use a gentle cleaner, test in an inconspicuous corner first, and blot until the stain lifts. If you need help, consult the manufacturer’s guidelines for safe cleaners.
