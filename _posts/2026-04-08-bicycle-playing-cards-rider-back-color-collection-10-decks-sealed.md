## Maintenance, Care, and Longevity

Maintenance isn’t glamorous, but it matters. The stock on Bicycle Rider Back decks handles a surprising amount of abuse if you treat them with basic respect:

- Keep them in a cool, dry place. Heat and humidity can affect the finish and the handling, especially if you’re doing a lot of shuffles or fancy holds.
- Avoid direct sunlight. Not only can this fade the back color over time, but it can also affect your seating arrangement when you’re performing for an audience.
- Use a proper deck box or a small drawer organizer. Ten decks don’t require a complex storage solution, but a tidy setup helps you find the color you need without turning your gaming night into a scavenger hunt.
- When opening and handling, use careful blade work on the seal. If you’re a compulsive “open and inspect” person, create a ritual that preserves the seal—perhaps after you’ve performed your first card flourish for the day, you can break one seal with ceremony and keep the rest pristine.

If you want to keep the color on the back as vibrant as possible, consider a light dusting with a soft brush now and then to remove any fine paper dust that accumulates with prolonged use. It sounds nerdy, but your future self will thank you during a dramatic color reveal.
