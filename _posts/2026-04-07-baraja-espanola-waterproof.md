## Materials, durability, and care: What to expect after months of outdoor gaming
The waterproof feature isn’t a magic spell; it’s a design trade-off. You’ll notice a slight difference in feel compared to uncoated decks. This is by design: a deck that’s too slick or too vinyl-y would ruin the tactile joy of shuffling. The sweet spot here is somewhere between a premium deck and a rugged field tool. If you’re someone who takes hand ages seriously, you’ll appreciate the deck’s balance between flexibility and sturdiness.

Caring for the Baraja Española Waterproof is straightforward:

- Wipe gently with a damp cloth after outdoor sessions; avoid harsh solvents that could degrade the coating.
- Let air-dry; don’t return wet cards to a box or they may stay damp longer than a shy gecko. A light dusting of powder or a quick pat with a microfiber cloth works wonders.
- Avoid exposing the deck to direct sunlight for prolonged periods; the coating is resilient, but UV exposure can degrade colors over time, just like your favorite meme’s punchline when repeated too often.
- Store in a cool, dry place. The container matters; a rot-resistant box or a dedicated deck sleeve keeps the edges neat and avoids warping from moisture fluctuations.

Maintenance tip: if you’re traveling, a small ziplock with a silica gel pack kept nearby can help keep humidity at bay in transit. You’ll thank me later when you whip out the deck at a bus stop and still manage to draw a flawless hand.
