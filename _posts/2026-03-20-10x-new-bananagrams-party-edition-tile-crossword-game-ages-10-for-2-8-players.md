## Setup and First Impressions
The setup is deliberately straightforward. Here’s what you do:

1) Open the box and set the central pool on the table so everyone can reach it. The pool acts as the shared source of tiles to draw when players need a new letter.
2) Each player takes a rack and draws a starting handful of tiles from the bag. The exact number isn’t the headliner here; you’ll be told by the quick-start sheet, but the standard approach is to have just enough to begin building a personal grid.
3) Decide on the game mode. The Party Edition invites you to either a pure classic drive (build and expand without many interruptions) or to explore some optional rules designed for bigger groups.
4) When someone completes a legal grid on their rack and uses all their tiles, they shout out a variant of the well-known call and the round toggles into the next phase. If you’re a purist, you’ll end with a traditional “Bananas!” to signal victory. If you want something more festive, you can sprinkle in a custom chant that the group agrees on before the first table flip.

### How to Play in a Nutshell
The core mechanic remains elegantly simple and deliciously chaotic: build a personal crossword with your tiles until you can’t hold any more, then press your luck for a new tile via Peel, and watch as your competitors scramble to rework their grids to accommodate the new letter camps. Here’s a compact play outline:

- Each player sets up a rack with their tiles and begins filling a personal grid that doesn’t cross in illegal ways with other people’s grids (think of it as a private city map that shares the same neighborhood).
- When someone finishes their grid, the round goes into a Peel phase: everyone draws one more tile from the central pool. This keeps the game moving and ensures that no one can hide behind a fully formed grid for too long.
- If you can’t form a valid word with your rack, you can use a Dump action to swap a tile from your rack for a new tile from the pool. It’s a safety valve that keeps players from stagnating in a terrible rack and preserves momentum.
- The first player to use all their tiles and declare victory wins the round; the other players tally their tiles to determine the status (and the next round’s starting advantage if you’re using a house rule to lengthen the night).
- Optional rules include special tiles or events that reward or penalize bold word choices, encouraging players to take a few deliberate risks for big payoffs.

The key not to miss: the fun comes from the tension of building quickly while also keeping a coherent and valid word grid. The party version makes that tension more lively in a mixed group, where some folks will immediately leap to a 7-letter triumph while others crunch vowels and wonder if the letter Z can be its own word if you squint at it long enough.

#### Variations and House Rules
Here are a few optional rules that can spice up a session without making the game feel like a homework assignment:
- The All Letters Rule: allow players to use letters to form longer words by stacking across multiple words on a single grid. This encourages bigger vocabulary choices and can lengthen rounds in a delightful way.
- The Quick Peel: shorten the peel phases to speed up the game for younger players or for extremely chaotic groups. It’s less about strategy and more about social excitement.
- The Cooperative Mode: teams of two share a rack and collaborate on a combined grid. This adds a cooperative flavor that still preserves the race element, which is unexpectedly satisfying when your teammate lands a genius cross-letter combo.
- The Alphabet Sprint: for a few rounds, restrict vocabulary to words that begin with the current rack’s first letter, with the rest of the alphabet unlocked only after a successful wave of word-building. It’s silly, but it creates memorable moments when a seed word becomes a sprawling network of crosswords.

### Word Crafting and Strategy Notes
Some practical tips from our playthroughs:
- Build small, testable word-chains early. Even a few short words that connect can give you flexibility for later longer combos.
- Don’t fear the Dump action; it’s your friend when tiles aren’t cooperating. The right tile swap can turn a dead grid into a roaring crossword skyline.
- Pay attention to vowels; in party settings, vowels can save you when you’re off the treadmill and need a quick, legal word to re-anchor your grid.
- Observe your neighbors’ islands; sometimes the best move is to block an over-ambitious cross-link that would swallow your own plan and give someone else the spotlight.
- If you’re aiming for speed, keep movements brisk and call out your changes with confidence. It’s as much about performance as it is about vocabulary.

For additional play testing, we also tried a couple of “thematic rounds” where players had to use at least one word from a specific category (animals, tech, fantasy, etc.). The results were delightfully chaotic and produced some real show-stoppers. If you’re new to themed rounds, start with a gentle theme and allow a short adjustment window before you escalate to tougher ties.
