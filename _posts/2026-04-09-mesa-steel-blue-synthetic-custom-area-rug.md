### Care and maintenance basics

Synthetic fibers are generally resilient when it comes to stains and wear. Vacuum regularly to remove loose dirt. For spills, blot rather than rub to prevent the stain from migrating deeper into the fibers. Most household stains can be lifted with mild detergent mixed with water; test on a small area first. Avoid harsh solvents that can degrade the fiber and color. For pet accidents, a quick blot plus a gentle cleaner designed for synthetic fibers usually does the trick without leaving a watermark.

### Stain resistance in daily life

Stain resistance is rarely a magic shield. It helps to avoid spills that sit for hours and to clean up promptly. The synthetic fibers are treated to resist staining to a degree, but a stubborn sauce or dye can still leave a mark if left untreated. The trick is to act fast and to use proper cleaners designed for synthetic fibers. Always test in a small area before applying any chemical solution widely.

A rug pad is not a luxury; it is a necessity for most synthetic rugs. It prevents shifting, minimizes bunching, cushions impact on hard floors, and prolongs the life of both the rug and the floor.
