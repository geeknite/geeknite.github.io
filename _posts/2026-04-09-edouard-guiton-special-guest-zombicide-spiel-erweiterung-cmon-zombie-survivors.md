## Overview
If you are reading this with a coffee mug in one hand and a plastic zombie limb in the other, welcome to the latest geekout from Geeknite. Today we dig into Zombicide 1.5, the special guest edition that CMON pushed into the world with the fanfare of a brass band in a graveyard. Edouard Guiton is listed as a special guest designer for this expansion, and yes, our inner nerds did a little happy dance that sounded suspiciously like a decapitation screech from a zombie. This is not just a simple add on. It is a full fledged exploration of how a big game brand lets a guest designer put its own stamp on the core experience. Spoiler: the stamp smells a little like coffee and gun oil, in a good way.

Beyond the novelty of a guest designer, this expansion aims to scratch two classic gamer itches at once: more crunchy strategy for the die hard Zombicide crew and fresh thematic flavor that feels like a proper bridge to a new apocalypse. The Zombie Survivors edition, as the name cheekily implies, leans into the story arc of survivors who on paper are just as capable as the walkers, yet somehow manage to stumble into better survival decisions than their zombie peers. If you have ever muttered that the humans feel underpowered in a zombie world, you are the audience this expansion targets with surgical, comedic precision.

For the uninitiated, Zombicide is a cooperative zombie apocalypse board game where players take on the roles of survivors, hacking their way through undead hordes while collecting loot and fulfilling objective cards. The base game works, but the real joy comes when you invite new mechanics and new faces into the kitchen table buffet. The Edouard Guiton special guest edition leans into that appetite with two new campaigns, a handful of fresh zombie types, and a set of play changes that weave Edouards signature quirks into the rhythm of the zombie siege. The result is a product that can be enjoyed by mixed groups of veterans and newcomers alike, provided you like your brain snacks with a side of wit.

If you want to skim the high points before diving into the minutiae, here is the capsule summary: more survivors with new abilities, a rebalanced conflict between risk and reward, and a narrative thread that lets you feel the apocalypse rather than merely survive it. Also, the box art looks so glossy you might want to frame it next to your actual zombie minis. We will return to all of this in the sections that follow, but first a quick note on how this expansion fits into the larger Zombicide ecosystem.

### What this expansion is not
This is not a reboot. It does not erase the feel of the base game or pretend the previous expansions never happened. If you come in expecting a radical departure from the Zombicide formula, you might feel a little let down. What it is instead is a lovingly crafted augmentation. Think of it as a well staged cameo in a long running TV series. Edouard Guiton shows up, delivers a few zingers, but mostly helps the team tell a larger story with better pacing and a few new toys under the hood.

## Unboxing and Components
Unboxing is part of the ritual of any board game expansion, because the tactile joy can carry you through the inevitable rules misreads and misplacements of tokens. The Zombie Survivors edition does not pretend to be a small add on. It arrives with a respectable chunk of content that will demand table space and a bit of governance at the start of play.

The box is sturdy, with a tactile matte finish that resists fingerprints like a zombie resists a clean kitchen knife. You get dedicated slots for new survivor miniatures, new zombie types, tokens, and the two campaign boards. The aesthetic is in line with CMON's production values, which means it looks good on the shelf and holds up to the inevitable table chaos without shame.

### Card stock, tokens, and counters
There are several new decks and lots of tokens. The cards are crisp, with durable stock that will survive the occasional beer spill or last round of hot sauce during a late night game. The icons are legible and consistent with the core juego, which makes it easier to grok the new rules without endless consulting of the rulebook. If you are a stickler for production quality, this expansion hits most of the right notes: compact rulebook, tidy card layout, and tokens that are easily distinguishable at a glance.

### New survivors and new zombies
Two new components steal the show here: the two new survivor miniatures and a handful of new zombie types. The survivors come with their own backstory and a tiny dose of flavor text on the card backs that will make you grin as you shuffle. The zombie adaptations align with the theme of survivors navigating a world where the undead have learned to be cunning enough to put up a fight. The art remains faithful to Zombicide’s tall, dynamic style, which means you get dramatic silhouettes, bright color accents, and enough gore to make your aunt faint without crossing into “blueprint of a horror movie” territory.

## Design and Theme: Edouard Guiton's Signature Touch
Edouard Guiton’s involvement is not a mere footnote. The expansion borrows his knack for combining thematic storytelling with crisp mechanical design. You will notice two recurring ideas across the rules: situational humor that rewards clever play and a focus on survivorship that feels earned rather than handed out by luck.

### The two campaigns as a narrative spine
The expansion centers around two campaign boards that thread an evolving narrative through the sessions. Each campaign has its own set of objectives, a recommended progression, and a little arc that ties into the special guest concept. You are not simply collecting loot and killing zombies; you are building a narrative of human grit under siege, with occasional moments of levity to remind you that the world went to heck because someone forgot to refill the coffee machine.

### New mechanics that amplify decision making
Two new mechanics stand out. First, a survivor ability system that improves with use, giving players character growth over the course of a campaign. Second, a modular hazard deck that can alter the scenario in meaningful ways between rounds. Both are clever, not gimmicky. They promote thoughtful planning without turning the game into an Excel spreadsheet of risks and rewards. The result is a system that feels fresh while still being recognizably Zombicide at its core.

## Gameplay and Mechanics: A Deep Dive
If you have played Zombicide a few times, you know what you are getting into: cooperative play with a dash of chaos, some dice rolling, and a lot of pushing zombies around with your plastic weapons. The special guest edition adds a layer of nuance that is easy to underestimate until you are in the middle of a game with four players negotiating a critical swing turn.

### How the new survivors change the math
The two new survivors bring abilities that scale with use. They reward clever placement and cooperative synergy in ways that the base game sometimes fails to reward. This encourages players to coordinate, timing their actions to maximize damage, healing, loot collection, and objective completion in the same turn. The net effect is a slightly more cooperative experience that still maintains the frenzied pace of a Zombicide session.

### The new zombie types and their quirks
The expansion introduces several zombie variants, each with its own set of rules. Some types emphasize overwhelming numbers, others rely on speed or stealth to pressure the party into making tough choices. The variety is more than a cosmetic sprinkle; it changes the cadence of a session. You are not simply mowing through the walkers with the same routine. The zombies present new threats that require adaptation, which is exactly what a good expansion should do.

### Event and hazard systems
The modular hazard deck is the real wildcard. It acts as a shared burden on the group, injecting random twists that can escalate tension or turn the table on its head. The hazard modules feel thematically consistent with the idea of a struggling community trying to avert a full blown collapse. They encourage players to think beyond the next four rooms and into the broader survivability arc of the campaign. It is not merely extra rules; it is a way to make every session feel distinct without needing a separate scenario booklet.

### Campaign pacing and table feel
Pacing is critical in Zombicide because the tempo dictates how tense a session feels. The Guiton edition nails pacing through a carefully designed sequence of events, with each round building toward a set of critical moments. The two campaigns provide a natural arc for the evening—one where you chase a lead that might save the survivors, and another where you fight a losing battle to prevent a total collapse of the zone. The pacing is not perfect; there are moments where a misread could cause you to lose momentum. However, those moments are outweighed by the overall structure, which keeps you engaged without forcing you into a pure dice rolling sprint.

## Box to Table: Setup and Play Time
Setup time for this expansion is a touch longer than the base game due to the extra components and the two new campaign boards. If you are a veteran of Zombicide, you can shave some minutes by pre-sorting tokens the first time and pre-assembling the new survivors. If you are a new player, expect a little more initial friction as you wrap your head around the new mechanics. Once the initial chaos subsides, you will be rewarded with a smoother, richer experience.

Play time varies with player count. At three to four players, you are probably looking at 90 to 120 minutes for a single campaign arc. If your table consists of marathon gamers who like to tension-simmer their games, you could push beyond two hours, particularly if you are hunting for every objective in a single run. The expansion does not pretend to shorten you. It wants to give you a more textured, story driven siege experience, and the numbers line up with that objective.

The game's tone remains faithful to Zombicide while injecting a playful, designer voice through Edouard Guiton. This is not a sugar coated headline; it is a balanced integration with enough new content to justify a reprint on a game shelf. The result is a product that feels like a proper expansion rather than a mere add on. Your group will be rewarded for focusing on the storytelling elements and for using the new survivors to create tactical synergies that the base game could only hint at.

## Replayability and Longevity
Two campaigns, two boards, and a set of new enemies guarantee a substantial bump in replay value. The fear of repetition is always present with expansions; here it is less of a concern because the new mechanics create distinct play patterns across campaigns. The modular hazard deck means that even if you replay the same mission, the way hazards trigger will present you with different dynamic decisions. If you are the kind of player who enjoys chasing the perfect ladder of tactical optimizations, you will find plenty of material to chew on across multiple sessions.

One potential drawback for some players is that the campaigns feel best when you commit to them in a single sitting or over a couple of sessions. If you enjoy shorter micro sessions, you may need to carve out dedicated time blocks to fully appreciate the campaign arcs. For those who savor the long form campaign experience, this edition is a treasure trove that invites you to return again and again to refine your approach and celebrate the small wins that come from clever planning and a bit of luck.

Compared to other Zombicide expansions, Zombie Survivors stands out for its emphasis on narrative and the incorporation of a guest designer’s voice. It does not throw random extra rules at you for the sake of creature count. Instead, it channels Edouard Guiton's design language into a polished package that emphasizes teamwork and story. For fans who want a deeper world, a few more tactical choices, and a few more inside jokes to share around the table, this is a well crafted upgrade.

## How to Play It: Table Talk and Strategy Tips
If you want a smoother entry, here are a few practical pointers for maximizing the first session with this expansion:
- Use the two new survivors as your lead players to test the new synergy mechanics early on.
- Plan for a slightly longer first round to set up the hazard deck and campaign boards.
- Read the flavor text aloud in a light voice; it helps set mood and reduces the inevitable table drama when dice go cold.
- Keep the objective cards visible and discuss the strategic goals early on to avoid a late game scramble.

For players who enjoy streaming or content creation around board games, the expansion provides some great talking points. The Edouard Guiton influence lends itself to commentary on how guest designers shape a flagship product while staying true to its core DNA. You can discuss the interplay between the new mechanics and the familiar base game rhythms, which makes for good analysis between turns and after the table clears.

### A note on solo and two player variants
The base Zombicide rules support solo and two player modes, and this expansion does not abandon that flexibility. In solo play, you control two survivors with the new abilities, which creates interesting micro-situations. There is a bit more planning in solo mode because you are juggling multiple objectives and hazard triggers without the luxury of a co-op partner to cover a gap. In two player games, you will feel the tension magnified, because two minds are trying to navigate the same hazard deck and the same set of objective requirements with limited resources.
