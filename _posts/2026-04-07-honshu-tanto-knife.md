## Maintenance and Care
Maintenance matters when you own a sharp instrument. The Honshu Tanto benefits from a light maintenance routine that any busy geek can handle:
- Clean and dry after use to prevent corrosion and keep the edge happy.
- Light honing to maintain edge geometry; a few strokes on a fine stone or a ceramic rod will do wonders.
- Store in its sheath or in a protective sheath to prevent accidental dulling and to keep the edge away from other metal cabinets in the kitchen or on the trail.
- Occasional oiling for the blade if you notice any chalky or dry feeling on the surface; a drop on a clean cloth is often enough.

If you plan to go on a long trip or live in a salty environment, consider a rust-preventer spray or a simple wipe-down routine as part of your daily ritual. The blade appreciates not being treated like a disposable gadget and rewarded with a little respect and care.
