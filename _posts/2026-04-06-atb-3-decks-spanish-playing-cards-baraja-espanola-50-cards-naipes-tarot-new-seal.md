## Cards, suits, and ranks: a quick map for the uninitiated

Let’s lay down the basics for those who haven’t memorized the Baraja de España by heart since middle school. The suits are four: oros, copas, espadas, bastos. The ranks typically run from 1 to 7, plus 10 (Sota), 11 (Caballo), and 12 (Rey). That yields 10 distinct cards per suit, totaling 40 cards in a classic deck. If your ATB set is truly 50 cards per deck, threefold, you’ll want to verify whether the extra cards are traditional deck-adjacent or something specific to the Tarot-oriented variant you’ve bought. In any case, you’ll find the familiar icons—coins for oros, goblets for copas, swords for espadas, and clubs for bastos—with face cards that behave predictably during most standard Spanish games.

### Tips for reading the cards quickly
- In many games, you’ll count by suit. A quick glance at your hand should tell you which suit-heavy strategies you can pursue.
- The court cards (Sota, Caballo, Rey) have special value in some variants; in Mus or Brisca, you’ll often use numerical values for the “bones” of the hand rather than chasing a single “face card” score.
