### Gameplay and new rules
If you are a UNO purist, the 2025 rules will still feel familiar. You draw one card when you cannot play, you match color or number, and you shout UNO when you have one card left. The new rules add a dash of modernity:
- Color shift wilds: instead of a standard wild, a wild card now causes the color to shift to the most represented color on the table. This means if red and blue are the dominant colors, you get to choose which of those two becomes the active color when you play a wild.
- Power tokens: you earn tokens for knocking out rivals and for perfectly predicting the next color. Tokens can be cashed in for a free draw or to peek at the next three cards in the deck, adding a tiny misdirection element to the game.
- The amarillos are not just color cosmetics: certain rounds will assign a mini objective like forcing a certain color before the deck runs out. It is lighthearted, but it changes the pace enough to keep repeated rounds fresh.

### Strategy tips
- In UNO Elite Core 2025, the yellow theme should tempt you to lean into a go big or go home color strategy. If amarillo is on the table, you can risk playing a wild toward the color you want to appear. This is particularly effective when you have a color gap in your hand.
- Look for color momentum: the new color shift wilds reward players who track the color cadence across several turns.
- Token economy: use power tokens strategically. If you can force an extra draw on a rival who is closing in on UNO, you may swing the game in a single move.

### Variants and party modes
UNO has always thrived on social chaos, and the Elite Core 2025 edition leans into it with two optional modes:
- Quickfire rounds: a 10-minute timer that encourages rapid decisions and silly banter.
- Cooperative mini-games: in which players work together to achieve a shared objective before the deck runs out.

These modes keep the table lively and prevent the more intense players from monopolizing the table for an entire afternoon.
