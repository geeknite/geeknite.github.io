### Authenticity and preservation
Like many Japanese collectibles, the No. 148 Son Goten card benefits from careful handling. You want to preserve the gloss on the surface, avoid exposure to direct sunlight that may fade the aura around Goten, and keep it away from humidity that could warp the thin card stock. Packaging from the Carddas era often included protective sleeves; a simple, acid-free sleeve with a thin top-load is a good baseline to prevent curling or edge wear.

### Grading and value bands
When you send a card like this to a professional grader—or show it off at a local convention—the big questions are about corners, centering, and surface wear. Very Rare cards often grade higher than their plain counterparts if kept in mint condition. The value band can swing widely depending on market activity, demand for Dragon Ball Z nostalgia, and the condition of the foil or holo accents if any are present on that print run. Remember, a well-centered, clean front with minimal corner wear can sometimes fetch more than a card with slightly better print quality but worse physical condition. This is one of those collectibles where the physical state matters more than the potential numeric power listed on the card itself.
