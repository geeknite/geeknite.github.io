## Cleaning and Maintenance
In Geeknite terms, this rug is the RTX 4080 of surfaces: powerful enough to handle modern daily life, but not something you need a dedicated lab technician to maintain. The key is routine care:

- Vacuum regularly with a suction setting that avoids aggressive brush agitation to prevent fiber fraying. A weekly pass tends to keep the pile looking plush and crisp.
- Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing, which can push stain deeper into fibers. For liquid spills, a light blot and a shallow wipe with a mild cleaning solution (test in an inconspicuous area first) is usually sufficient.
- Avoid steam cleaning unless you have rug-specific instructions from Rug Culture; heat can affect the fiber’s integrity over time.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear in high‑traffic zones. This is especially relevant if you have a favorite TV-watching spot or a play area for kids.
- For stubborn stains, consult professional rug cleaners who have experience with mid-pile synthetics. DIY remedies can work, but you don’t want to risk color bleeding or fiber damage.

The practical takeaway: Parker Cream is not a “do nothing” rug. It rewards consistent care with a long, luminous life. If you want a rug that tolerates everyday chaos and still looks presentable after a quick vacuum and spot clean, this model earns points.
