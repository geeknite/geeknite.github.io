## What Fortune Row Is, and Why It Might Be Your Next Favorite Corner

Fortune Row is not just a location; it is a mood, a rhythm, and a set of questions you ask your players about luck, risk, and the price of a wish. The handbook presents Fortune Row as a semi autonomous precinct that knits into the larger mosaic of the City of Mist. This district is built around the idea that luck is a resource you can trade, manipulate, or misplace. The vibe is late-night arcade, velvet rope clubs, and rain-streaked alleyways where the neon signs flicker with old myths that still demand attention. You will find places to shop for clues, to gamble with fate, and to confront the characters you have made real with your own choices.

### A quick pitch
Fortune Row wants to remind you that the city is a living ledger. Each Mask has a line in the ledger: a debt, a promise kept, a fortune earned, a future owed. The Players are not merely living stories; they are the custodians of those debts, and Fortune Row gives them more tools to manage, allocate, or trade those obligations. The Player's Handbook leans into social dynamics and the friction between what a Mask wants and what Fortune Row demands in return. The tone is more velvet noir than punchy mystique, which aligns well with many groups that want slower, more character-driven moments with flashes of spectacular powers when the moment turns.

### How it fits City of Mist
Fortune Row expands the core rules in two major ways. First, it introduces a new resource economy tied to Luck, executed via a tailored mechanic that uses a stat called Fortune Reserve. Second, it presents several new Tag archetypes and Mask templates that emphasize the tension between personal myth and the city’s collective myths. The system remains approachable for new players while offering a secret handshake to veterans who want to push their Masks in new directions. The balance feels considered: you are not overwhelmed with options, but you are given meaningful choices that alter how scenes unfold, who gets a say in the city’s whisper network, and what your characters owe to the row.

If you want to see how Fortune Row sits within the city’s broader mythos, you can glance at the GM-facing sections that discuss Scene Framing and Clue Distribution. The book does not pretend that the world is simple. It presents a monster you can negotiate with, a fortune you can ruin with a single misstep, and a club you can choose to walk away from or stay inside until the dawn comes with a different color lipstick on the night.

## Mechanics and Content: How Fortune Row Changes the Game

### New resources and economy: Fortune Points and Fortune Reserve
Fortune Row introduces a refined resource system that gives players more agency without overturning the core balance. The Fortune Reserve acts as a social currency that powers dramatic scenes, special maneuvers, and once-a-session miracles that would otherwise feel contrived. Players accumulate Fortune through narrative milestones, clever use of their Masks, and successful negotiation with the city’s hidden mechanics. They can then spend Fortune to do things that feel mythic but grounded, such as bending a scene to reveal a crucial clue, or surviving a near miss by the skin of a myth-made nail. The system rewards story-driven decision-making while keeping a tense economy that the GM can manage through the book’s encounter design tools.

### Masks, archetypes, and new tone
The Handbook adds a handful of new Mask archetypes tailored to Fortune Row. These include a fortune-teller who has become a walking weather forecast, a fixer who trades rumors the way other people trade stocks, and a sentinel whose power is less about offense and more about protecting the fragile balance of grace and consequences in public spaces. Each archetype comes with a few evocative prompts, a suggested Fortune Row backstory that ties a character to key locations, and a handful of hooks for the GM to trigger a session arc. The new masks feel thematically cohesive with City of Mist: they are dramatic, morally complex, and designed to spark both personal storytelling and urban intrigue.

### New blocks for scenes: micro-scenarios that spark play
One of the book's strengths is its suite of micro-scenarios designed for quick pick-up sessions or to seed a longer arc. These scenes are written with a GM-friendly tilt: clear objectives, stakes, and a set of optional complications that can escalate the tension. They range from heist setups in a rain-drenched alley to a tense meeting in a fortune teller shop that might reveal a city-wide conspiracy or simply expose a character’s fear of vulnerability. The best part is how these micro-scenarios connect through Fortune Row’s currency and the city’s mythic pipeline, giving players a sense that every choice has a price attached to it, and the price might be paid in a direction they hadn’t anticipated.

### How it supports GM prep and pacing
Fortune Rowstreamlines penguin-walking through a session by providing ready-made hooks, locations, and NPCs who are more than props. They breathe, they misbehave, and they want something from the party. The GM toolkit helps avoid the trap of random encounters that offer little narrative payoff. It pushes toward scenes that are thematically connected, emotionally resonant, and memorable for the kind of players who remember a good line more than a good die roll. If you enjoy a city where myth and money collide, Fortune Row gives you the scaffolding to build a season around those collisions without burning through your prep time. You get a stronger sense of place and purpose, which translates into sessions that feel like chapters in a larger myth rather than one-off tableaus.

## Lore, Tone, and World-Building: What Fortune Row Adds to the City of Mist Universe

### The city as a character
Fortune Row leans into the city itself as a protagonist. The district is a living, breathing actor with a voice, a moral center, and a willingness to make you squirm if you forget to pay attention to the clues scattered in plain sight. The book treats urban myths as a financial ledger, which is a clever conceit that gives the GM a tangible way to tie player decisions to city outcomes. It also invites players to imagine themselves as both beneficiaries and borrowers of the city’s fortune. The result is a tone that can be intimate and tense, with moments of explosive spectacle that feel earned rather than crammed into the margins.

### Themes and motifs
Fortune Row emphasizes themes of trust, debt, risk, and the unpredictability of luck. It nods to noir, but it wears its mythic sensibility on its sleeve in a way that makes room for both heartfelt character drama and fantastical reveals. If your group enjoys characters who carry the weight of their past choices and who negotiate with a literal paying client called Fortune, you will recognize a familiar cadence: a rhythm built on delay, revelation, and sometimes, a consequences-forward approach that makes the city feel alive and a bit mischievous.

### How it plays with other City of Mist sources
If you own the core rulebook and the earlier supplements, Fortune Row slots neatly into the published power rankings of the city’s Masks and harmonizes with the city’s mythic structure. You can conjure up a session arc by weaving Fortune Row’s micro-scenarios with the larger arc threads you already have in play. It does not replace any existing material; it enriches it. The book also references familiar places and factions with fresh angles, so long-time players will feel the world has grown a little more personal and a lot more intriguing.

## NPCs, Factions, and Hooks: A Sample of What You Might Meet in Fortune Row

To give you a sense of the flavor, here are a handful of sample NPCs and how they might interact with your group:

- Madame Fortuna: A velvet-voiced oracle who knows just enough about your past to be dangerous, and just enough about your future to be terrifying. She runs a storefront that doubles as a fortune parlor and a discreet information broker’s front. She tests the players with choices that feel morally slippery, but the rewards are emotionally resonant when risk becomes right.
- The Clockwork Oracle: A once human machine who became a living clockwork device after a catastrophe. They keep time in a city that forgets to remember, and their clockwork voice is a chorus that hints at the city’s hidden timelines. They offer precision and inevitability, but the price is loyalty and a willingness to become the city’s most honest liar.
- The Velvet Gambler: A gambler who has somehow learned to win by listening to the city’s whispers. They are charming, dangerous, and slightly chaotic. They offer fortunes that seem too good to be true, which is a hint that truth in Fortune Row is more fragile than it appears.
- The Watchdrops: A faction of surveillance artisans who stitch together information from rain-slick streets and alleyway confessions. They are not villainous by default; they simply believe the city should have a more efficient memory. Your players may cross paths with them when they try to hide a clue in plain sight, only to discover that the city can recall a lot more than they bargained for.

These NPCs are not sprinkled in as flavor at the table; they are engines of scene conflict. They have reasons to engage with the players, and each has a tiny web of connections to Fortune Row locations, making it easy to chain scenes together in a way that feels organic rather than arbitrary.

## GM Tools: Running Fortune Row without Losing Your Mind

### Scene framing and escalation
The GM tools in Fortune Row emphasize three-stage escalation: setup, reveal, consequence. The steps are intuitive enough to guide a new GM through a session while still leaving room for improvisation when the night turns on a dime. The book also includes ready-to-run arcs that use Fortune Row as the center spine. These arcs are not one-page adventures; they are modular arcs with a strong thematic throughline, enough NPC depth to fuel character-driven scenes, and a natural progression toward a finale that is both satisfying and surprising.

### Clues and foreshadowing that actually pay off
A common pain point in RPGs is a clue that feels important in the moment but vanishes by the time the players reach the payoff. Fortune Row treats clues as a currency that accrues through interaction with Fortunes and NPCs, letting players discover threads that hold weight later. The book suggests multiple ways to seed clues across a city map and to tie them to Fortune Row events, so your players feel like they are uncovering a coherent conspiracy rather than collecting circumstantial hints.

### Adventure pacing and length considerations
Whether you run a long arc or a few fast sessions, Fortune Row provides pacing guidance. It helps you gauge when to slow the tempo for a character reveal and when to push for a decisive moment that tests the Masks’ allegiances. If your table values character-driven drama and thoughtful consequences over flashy set-pieces, Fortune Row delivers a cadence that will feel like a natural fit, not a forced string of dramatic events.

## The Final Verdict: Should You Grab Fortune Row?

If you are chasing a City of Mist expansion that gives you a clearly defined district with a fresh vibe, new mechanics that feel integrated rather than tacked on, and a toolbox that makes GM prep finite rather than endless, Fortune Row hits a sweet spot. It respects the core of the game while inviting you to explore a different rhythm of storytelling. It rewards players who like to lean into character tension and the moral gravity of decisions, while still offering moments of spectacle that remind you why you fell in love with this city in the first place.

If your table prefers a brighter, faster, more action-forward game, this might feel a touch slower at times. And if you are a stickler for total system parity with the base book, you may notice a few light edge-case rules that deserve extra house rules to smooth out during play. None of these are dealbreakers; they are the sort of items that make a good GM want to tinker, which is exactly the kind of engagement a supplement should spark.

Overall, Fortune Row is a strong addition to the City of Mist library. It gives you a neighborhood with personality, a set of well-wrought tools for GM prep, and a pair of core ideas you can build a campaign around: money can buy a moment, but not a memory; and every fortune you chase comes with a shadow that often knows your name before you do.
