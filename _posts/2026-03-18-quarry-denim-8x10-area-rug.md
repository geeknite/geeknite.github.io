## Cleaning and Maintenance
No product is perfect without a reasonable care routine, and this rug benefits from a straightforward approach. Regular vacuuming prevents dirt from nesting in the fibers. A vacuum with adjustable height and a standard beater brush works well. If you have pets that shed, you may want to run the vacuum more frequently to keep hair from building up in the nap. When spills occur, the recommended approach is to blot (not rub) and use a mild cleaning solution. Avoid harsh chemicals or hot water that can cause color fading or fiber damage. For more stubborn stains, a mild carpet cleaner designed for synthetic blends typically the job.

Rotation every six months can help even out wear across the rug. This practice ensures that no single area wears down faster than others due to furniture placement or foot traffic patterns. A rug pad is highly recommended with this rug. It increases grip, adds cushion, and helps preserve the rug’s shape over many moves around the sun. For those with children or pets, a good rug pad is a small investment with big returns in longevity.
