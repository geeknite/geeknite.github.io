Design and Build Quality
Each die in Fortune Row is a standard six sided cube, but with a twist. The icons on the faces are stylized glyphs that align with the vibe of City of Mist — an obvious nod to noir shadows, mythic sigils, and the grit of a city that never sleeps. The gems used for color fill are vibrant yet restrained, so you can appreciate the details even in low light during a late night run. The dice are resin based, with a smooth finish that resists smudges and oil slick handprint drama after long sessions. There is no wobble on the table, no misaligned faces, and no ghost images when you glance across the table under a bar-lamp. The six dice share a consistent aesthetic: the noir set uses a deep obsidian hue, the mythic dice glow with a midnight blue, the trickster dice feature a vibrant emerald, the hero dice a warm amber, the mystic dice a purple lilac, and the anti-hero dice a rust red. The color palette is not random; it is calibrated to evoke mood before you speak a word.

### Iconography
The glyphs on each face are designed to be readable from a distance, yet subtle enough to reward close inspection. You will not mistake a color for a face; the glyphs carry the information you need at a glance. The icons are meant to signal not a strict numeric outcome but a storytelling cue: a masked face to indicate noir, a sigil to suggest mystic resonance, a blade to imply action, and so forth. This is where Fortune Row aims to excel: not by giving you a higher or lower result but by injecting narrative levers into the scene. For example, a roll from the hero die might prompt a bold action sequence; a mythic die might hint at a larger-than-life consequence; a trickster die can push for misdirection or unexpected twists. The interplay of these signals can transform a straightforward check into a moment that your GM seizes on to push the story forward.

## Using Fortune Row in City of Mist
City of Mist relies on stylized checks and a noir mythic tone. The Fortune Row dice are designed as a storytelling accessory rather than a core mechanic rewrite. Here is how we found them to fit the table:

### Story signals, not outcomes
Each die serves as a tonal cue rather than as a separate resolution. If your table is running with narrative prompts, Fortune Row can be used to seed a twist, a reveal, or a fresh complication. For example, a scene in the alleys of a rain-soaked city might be prompted by a roll on the noir die, which suggests that your character is chasing a lead in an alleyway rather than in a crowded plaza. The mythic die can hint at a fate that is bigger than your character, a sign that a larger pattern is at work. The trickster die invites misdirection and a playful pivot that keeps the session from stalling.

### Mechanics, not rules
Do not expect Fortune Row to replace the core dice used for skill checks and combat in City of Mist. The dice are introduced as a storytelling tool to guide tone, mood, and direction. They can pair with normal dice to trigger story prompts, but you should not rely on them for the die pool that governs mechanical outcomes. In short, Fortune Row works as a mood ring for your session, not a calculator.

### Maintenance and Longevity
Like any premium accessory, Fortune Row benefits from a little care. Wipe the dice with a dry cloth between sessions to avoid fingerprint smudges that obscure glyphs. If you drop one, do not panic; the resin is fairly resilient, and the faces will not crumble into a sad mosaic of pips. For deeper cleaning, a soft damp cloth works fine, followed by a quick dry with a microfiber. Avoid harsh solvents that could alter the color fill, which is a shame because the colors look so confident you would not want them to fade. If you want to extend the life of these dice, store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to reduce color fade on their vibrant surfaces.

### Value for Money and Comparisons
Fortune Row sits in an interesting price bracket; not the budget end where you get a no-name set with questionable fonts, and not the elite tier where you pay for a single design on a second run with gold leaf inlays. The cost reflects the build quality and the design work that went into the icons and color coding. If your table values a strong aesthetic and a storytelling prompt that can accelerate scene pacing, this set is value for money. If you prefer purely mechanical resolution or you do not care for the mood signaling either way, the purchase might feel less compelling.

When you compare Fortune Row to other themed dice sets, you will likely notice three things: the theme alignment with City of Mist is strong, the dice are built to be durable on long campaigns, and the potential for table talk and prompts is high. The downsides are that the dice do not change the math of the game, and that the color-coded faces may be a little much for players who like subtlety. If you are the sort of gamer who collects dice as a storytelling toolkit, you will probably love what Fortune Row offers. If you want something purely functional, you may want to consider a more minimal design.

### Color Blindness and Accessibility
One potential concern with color-coded dice is accessibility. The team behind Fortune Row has done their best to encode the mood in glyphs as well as color. If you or your players have color vision deficiency, you should test the dice before you buy them. The glyphs help, but the output may still rely on color cues in some faces. If accessibility is a priority for your table, we recommend pairing Fortune Row with a silent story language you agree on as a fallback, such as a shared list of prompts or a quick reference card that you can reference at the table.
