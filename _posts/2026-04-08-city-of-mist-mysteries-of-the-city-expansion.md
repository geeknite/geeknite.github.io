Overview and vibe
Mysteries of the City sticks to the same neon-noir DNA that made City of Mist a cult favorite among gamers who love pulp detective fiction with mythic undertones. The expansion leans into the core concept: city as a living being, myths as costumes, and your character as a rumor you try to dodge while making a mess of city halls, back alleys, and the occasional supernatural crime scene. The tone remains playful but sharp, with a wink to pulp-era art and a nod to contemporary storytelling where characters are morally complicated and often unreliable narrators.

What’s new, at a glance
- New Investigation Sets: The city has more secrets than a conspiracy theorist’s diary.
- Shadow Masks Expanded: More masks, more powers, and more ways to misinterpret what your “true self” means when the city’s fog is in your lungs.
- City Management and Districts: A more tangible map of the city that you can actually use to plan sessions, not just pretend to know where everything is.
- Dramatic Consequences and Fate Points: A new flavor of momentum that makes even your most innocuous actions feel like they could tilt a skylight and reveal a dragon behind it.
- Ready-to-run Scenarios and GM Tools: If you’re tired of spending three sessions planning a single scene, this expansion aims to hand you fast setup with enough depth to feel crafted.

The big questions: is it worth it?
Our take: yes, with caveats. If you’re chasing new content to stretch a long-running campaign, Mysteries of the City provides enough fresh material to deserve a place on the table. If you’re new to City of Mist, you might want to start with the core game and then migrate to the expansion once you’ve built a stable group and a couple of city nights under your belt.

Thematic fit and design philosophy
The expansion leans into three things that City of Mist players love: narrative shorthand that feels cinematic, flexible systems that stay out of the way when you’re weaving a scene, and a willingness to let the city itself be a character you can argue with. Mysteries of the City adds new layers without overwriting the original rule set; think of it as adding more lenses to view the same city’s reflection in a rainy shop window.

The contents: a deeper dive
Note: all content below is designed for a GM’s table and is presented from the perspective of running a modern game in a vibrant, messy city. It’s not a rulebook ethics lecture; it’s a toolkit with some optional rules to consider.

## New Investigation Sets: more ways to chase the story
Investigations in City of Mist are prompts, not rigid scripts. Mysteries of the City adds several new Investigation Sets—thematic clusters of prompts, clues, and micro-arcs that you can sprinkle across sessions. Each set has a mood, a few key NPCs (non-player characters), a handful of clue cards, and a couple of “Plot Twists” that are guaranteed to make players reevaluate their masks.

Some examples include:
- The Velvet Network: a group of information brokers who can grant access to the city’s most guarded secrets, for a price, and sometimes just for a good story to tell at the neighborhood dive.
- The Neon Orchard: a district where the city’s mythic energy blossoms in murals and street vendors who swear they’ve seen the future in a bag of popcorn.
- The Clockmaker’s District: time becomes a weapon and a ransom note in a city that always seems to be running late for something.

For GMs, these sets provide plug-and-play hooks. For players, they offer a familiar structure: a few questions, a few NPCs with agendas, and a lot of moral ambiguity awaiting your character’s public persona to collide with your private motives.

Possible open-table flow with new investigations:
- Hook: a rumor or a witnessed event that doesn’t quite add up.
- Investigation: you follow clues through districts that reflect the city’s mood.
- Climax: a reveal that challenges what the players think they know about their own Masks.
- Aftermath: consequences ripple into a city that never forgets a good crime story.

## The Shadow Masks: more color, more conflict
The Masks are the identities your PCs inhabit—think strong archetypes with a twist. The expansion adds several new Masks and expands the tools available to blend or collide identities with the story’s stakes. You’ll also find subtle changes to how Masks influence the city and vice versa, underscoring that the “myth” in City of Mist is not just a costume; it is a social contract with the urban environment.

New Masks include:
- The Archivist: a keeper of forgotten rumors who can access hidden archives—physical, digital, and mythic—through unusual means.
- The Glass Poet: a performer who can manipulate perception, bending light, sound, and the city’s mood to reveal or obscure truth.
- The Iron Courier: a messenger who can move through the city’s edges—alleyways, service tunnels, and the undercity—with uncanny speed.

The mechanics for Masks remain delightfully modular. You can mix a Mask with a set of power tags and a few persona prompts, and suddenly your PC becomes a living rumor with influence, not just a collection of numbers on a sheet. The expansion encourages players to lean into the mismatch between public persona and private motive, which is where City of Mist prospers.

## Districts, map and city-as-character: a more tangible playground
Mysteries of the City provides an upgraded district map—less “generic city” and more “city with personality.” Districts come with flavor text, NPC rosters, event calendars, and heat maps that can be employed to track tensions as the investigation unfolds. The idea is to make location feel like a living stage, where every corner has a story, every storefront a rumor, and every alley a potential trap.

GM-facing tools include:
- District cards with mood and event triggers
- A “City Stress” track that increases when players press too hard on their Masks, representing the city’s reactions in the form of social unrest, media attention, or supernatural feedback
- Environmental hooks: weather changes, power outages, or festival season that can tilt the odds in surprising ways

The upshot: you’ll spend less time scribbling random city features and more time running scenes where the city’s layout actively influences the plot. It’s not a map painting; it’s a living mural that reacts to your players’ choices.

## Dramatic Momentum and Fate: a new pulse on storytelling
The expansion introduces refined momentum, known as Dramatic Momentum, which blends narrative punctuation with game balance. You’ll find new ways to gain and spend momentum that reward story-forward play while keeping game pacing healthy. In practice, that means you can trigger cinematic reveals, push scenes toward a dramatic cliffhanger, or secure a pivotal clue when the stakes are high. The system is intuitive and rewards players who lean into the mystery rather than simply rolling dice for a thrill.

GM tips for momentum:
- Use momentum to force a scene to pivot when players get close to a revelation.
- Allow players to spend momentum to gain a temporary advantage that still has consequences later, reinforcing the idea that power comes with a price.
- Place cinematic triggers in natural dialogue beats so it never feels forced or gamey.

If you’re worried about balance, don’t be. The expansion keeps core weight on story choices, with mechanics that support escalation rather than hijack it. It’s a smart dance between “puzzle box” and “open street party,” and it works well when you lean into both sides of the coin.

## Ready-to-run scenarios and GM tools
For GMs, the expansion ships with ready-to-run scenarios designed to get you from hook to payoff in fewer table hours, while still allowing a lot of room for improvisation. The scenarios are not “one-and-done” adventures; they’re laboratories that you can transplant into your ongoing campaign or sculpt into a season arc.

What you’ll find in the GM toolkit:
- Quick-start session templates: NPC sheets, city heat maps, and investigation prompts that don’t require you to reinvent the wheel.
- Scene-building prompts: tips on pacing, tone, and when to break away from gritty realism for a punchy mythic moment.
- Optional rules for escalating threats: if the city fights back too hard, you’ll have a structured way to raise the stakes without devolving into chaos.
- NPCs with agendas: you’ll get a cast of supporting characters whose motives intersect with the players’ Masks, creating moral tension and dramatic irony.

One of the best parts is the emphasis on the GM’s enjoyment. The expansion respects the GM’s time and creativity, offering ready-to-use content while still leaving enough blank space for your own city’s flavor. It’s the right balance for veteran GMs who want a fresh canvas, and for newer GMs who want fewer blank pages to fill with their iconic neon doodles.

## Production quality: art, layout, and accessibility
Mysteries of the City continues City of Mist’s love for graphic-novel aesthetics. The art direction is bold but not flashy, with poster-like panels that give a quick tease of mood and stakes. Layout remains clean, with color-coded sections that make it easy to skim for a session’s essential bits—hook ideas, suspect list, clue cards, and the like. Card stock for clues and masks remains sturdy, with a nice tactile feel that adds to the “you’re in the city, hold onto it” vibe.

Accessibility remains a priority. The book is readable enough for newcomers who grasp the core concept of Masks and mythic power, while providing enough depth to satisfy veteran players who want to min-max for narrative impact. The expansion does require a base understanding of the core mechanics, but the included quick-start tips are genuinely helpful if you’ve just finished a citywide spree of coffee-fueled sessions.

## How this expansion plays with others: compatibility and flow
If you own the core game and possibly the earlier expansions, Mysteries of the City slots neatly into the ecosystem. It doesn’t rewrite how Masks work; instead, it expands the possibilities. That means you can add it to a current campaign without doing a major system-wide house rule cleanup. Some groups might want to selectively pull in new Investigations while keeping other elements untouched, and that’s absolutely fine. The design respects player agency and GM fiat, two pillars that City of Mist has always adored.

If you’re new to the line, you can still enjoy Mysteries of the City as a gateway expansion, but there’s a better value path: start with the base game to learn the rhythm, then treat Mysteries as a second act that widens the stage without forcing you to relearn the choreography.

## The verdict: who should buy Mysteries of the City?
- Best for seasoned City of Mist players who crave more reflective, character-driven mystery rather than pure action.
- Great for groups who enjoy long-form campaigns that explore the city as a living character, with evolving tensions among Masks, factions, and districts.
- A solid pick for GMs who want to cut down prep time but still want rich, mythic storytelling. If you’re a first-timer who wants a “checklist to go,” you’ll likely want to pair this with a few sessions of the core game before diving into the expansion’s deeper waters.

That said, the expansion is not an essential purchase for every table. If your group is in a “short campaigns, quick sessions” rhythm, you might be happier with a lighter supplement or a single-brick scenario pack rather than a full expansion. But if you want a city that reacts to your players in real time, if you want new masks that spark thematic diversity, and if you want a more robust system to push dramatic moments, Mysteries of the City is a strong candidate for your bookshelf.

How to integrate into a session: practical tips
1) Start with a single Investigation Set for a session rather than a full arc. This helps your players adjust to the expansion’s rhythm without feeling overwhelmed.
2) Use the District cards to anchor the scene’s mood. If the players walk into the Velvet Network, for example, you’ve got a ready-made social minefield where information is a currency and risk is tangible.
3) Map a city event to a Mask’s vulnerability. If your Archivist is chasing rumors that clash with the city’s political climate, you have a layered conflict where information warfare becomes the primary battleground.
4) Encourage player collaboration. The Masks shine when players decide that two separate identities must work together to survive a larger reveal.
5) Don’t fear the consequences. The city is a collaborator that occasionally punches back. If the players bend too hard toward one outcome, introduce an unexpected complication to remind them that myth and reality share a street corner.

Artwork and narrative voice: a quick observation
Mysteries of the City maintains the flamboyant, comic-book-inspired cover art and interior layout that fans already loved. The writing stays sharp, often witty, and never pretentious. It acknowledges the genre’s tropes while bending them into something that feels both familiar and new. The text respects readers’ intelligence without sacrificing entertainment value, which is a tricky balance. If you’re drawing a comic panel in your head while reading, you’re in the right zone.

Conceptual challenges you might run into
- It can be easy to over-scope a city night. The expansion will reward careful session planning and pacing; otherwise, you might end up chasing a clue through three districts and losing track of the central mystery.
- The new Masks can tempt players into playing a character that feels “cool” but doesn’t serve the story. The remedy is straightforward: remind players that Masks are tools for storytelling—when in doubt, tie a Mask’s power to a personal stake.
- The district system is excellent, but you’ll need to commit to a consistent city calendar to maximize its impact. Otherwise, it becomes a decorative overlay rather than a living backdrop.

Final recommendation

It’s especially recommended for groups who relish roleplay-heavy nights and long-form mysteries—the kind of campaign that ends up with more questions than answers and more friends than you expect to make along the way.
