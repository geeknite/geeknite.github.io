## How to authenticate a 1967 Red Velvet Box
If you’re evaluating a potential purchase, here are a few practical checks to perform without turning your living room into a business school:

- Exterior examination: Look for uniform color, texture consistency, and signs of aging that match a 50-plus-year-old box. Velvet-like finishes can shed fibers; a few are normal, a lot could indicate heavy use.
- Interior layout: A proper kit should have a neat, organized insert or tray to hold the cards. Warped inserts or missing dividers are red flags.
- Cards: Inspect for color fade, rounded corners, and surface wear. A complete deck will have a predictable distribution of ranks and no major creases that render numbers illegible.
- Card stock and finish: The feel should be sturdy but not rigidly brittle. If the cards crack or crackle when you bend them slightly, you might be dealing with a compromised deck.
- Documentation: The presence of original rules sheets or inserts adds significant value. If the set includes a dealer’s slip or an advertisement insert from J. T. Bowman, that’s a nice cherry on top.

## How to care for vintage SKIP-BO while you game
Care and storage are the eternal companions of any vintage game. Here are a few practical tips to keep your 1967 Red Velvet Box in today’s playing shape:

- Store away from direct sunlight to preserve the hues and prevent warping of any interior components.
- Keep the box in a temperature-stable environment. Extreme heat or cold can compromise both the box and the cards.
- If the velvet exterior shows wear, treat it gently. Soft brushings can reduce dust buildup without scuffing the surface.
- Consider a protective sleeve for the cards if you plan to play. It reduces edge wear and preserves legibility after many rounds of enthusiastic play.
- Clean hands, clean table: oils from your fingers can leave marks on cards over time, especially if the stock is older and a bit more delicate than modern equivalents.
