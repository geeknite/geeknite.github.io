### Strategy Tips: How to Not Feel Personally Attacked by a Card Draw
- Learn the mana rhythm: This isn’t a deck that benefits from sloppy plays. You’ll want to master your mana curve and keep a mental tally of your outs. It’s unglamorous, but it’s effective. Thickness of play is what wins games.
- Timing is everything: A perfect play is often about when to commit resources and when to hold back. You’ll regret blithely deploying a removal spell on the wrong target if you don’t keep your eyes on the broader board state.
- The “two-for-one” mindset: If you can achieve a two-for-one exchange (your card for two of theirs) without weakening your own position, do it. It’s a tiny victory that compounds into a credible path to victory.
- Expect the unexpected: Don’t be afraid to leverage odd-line plays. The deck rewards you for thinking in probabilistic terms and for embracing creative line choices when the obvious path stalls.
- Learn the opponent’s deck as you learn your own: A big part of mastery is predicting what your opponent is likely to hold, based on board state, turn history, and the deck’s typical strategies. This is where your poker face gets a real workout.
