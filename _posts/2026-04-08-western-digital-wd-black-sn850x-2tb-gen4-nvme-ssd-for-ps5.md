## Installation journey: PS5 side quest
Now, this is where the real nerdy drama begins. Installing an M.2 SSD in a PS5 isn’t as terrifying as replacing your GPU in a PC, but it’s close. Here’s a practical, reader-friendly guide that even a controller-wielding cat could technically follow (if your cat had thumbs and a PlayStation account).

### Tools and prep
- WD Black SN850X 2TB Gen4 NVMe SSD (with heatsink, if you’re not adding one separately)
- A small Phillips-head screwdriver (PH0 or PH1 depending on your PS5 model)
- A clean, well-lit workspace
- Patience and a sense of humor

### Step-by-step install (PS5)
1) Power down your PS5 completely and unplug all cables.
2) Place the PS5 on a stable surface and remove the side panel cover according to Sony’s instructions.
3) Locate the M.2 slot cover and remove the single securing screw to open the expansion slot.
4) If you’re using a drive without a heatsink, attach one that meets the PS5’s thermal requirements.
5) Insert the SN850X at an angle, then push it down flat and re-secure with the screw.
6) Replace the M.2 slot cover, reattach the side panel, plug in, and boot up.
7) In PS5 settings, format the new drive when prompted (this will erase nothing else on your system and only formats the new SSD).
