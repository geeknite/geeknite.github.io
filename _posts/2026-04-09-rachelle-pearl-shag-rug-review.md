### Materials and construction
The Rachelle Pearl uses high density polyester fibers, heat set to maintain loft while resisting flattening. The fibers are not wool, which means simpler cleaning and less worry about bold stains turning into long term memories. The dye lot gives a consistent Pearl finish across the entire rug, avoiding the dramatic shade shifts that can happen with cheaper dyes. The underside features a woven fabric backing that breathes and helps the rug lay flat. The result is a rug that feels substantial but not heavy, like a good laptop you can actually carry across the room.

### Maintenance and cleaning
The rug is easy to clean. Vacuum regularly with a brushless attachment to avoid pulling the fibers. If you prefer a lightweight vacuum with a beater bar, set it to a gentle mode and keep the height adjusted to the top fiber level to avoid damage. For spills, blot and then lightly clean with a mild detergent. Do not rub vigorously, which can distort loft.

Sheds? A minimal shedding period is normal for most shags. After a few cycles, shedding decreases, and the rug returns to its plush state. For pet odors and stains, baking soda helps with odor control; a light sprinkle and a few minutes to sit then vacuum works well.

### Durability and long term care
The rug is designed for long life with a dense pile that resists flattening. The back is strong and the edges are well bound. Rotating the rug every few months helps even wear and reduces the risk of permanent indentations from heavy furniture. If you have kids or heavy traffic, consider placing a hardy coffee table or ottoman on a larger rug to distribute weight more evenly.

### Sustainability and dyes

### Size and room planning
We tested 4x6, 5x8, and 6x9 sizes in different spaces. A 4x6 rug defines a compact seating area in a small living room or studio apartment. Always measure doorways and the space between furniture legs to ensure the rug will fit well and not feel cramped.
