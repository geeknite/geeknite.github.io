## Getting the most out of a sealed copy

If you are considering buying this sealed edition, here are a few practical tips to maximize your enjoyment and protect your investment:

- Inspect the seal visually for tears or reseal indicators. Small dings are common in older stock, but a perfect seal is preferable.
- Verify the region labeling on the back and the spine. Region mislabeling can complicate future resale.
- Check that the manual and any inserts are present and undamaged. Even if you never read the manual again, these pieces add to the item’s completeness.
- Store the sealed box in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent warping and fading of the artwork.
- When you eventually unseal, consider keeping the outer packaging for display; it’s a compact piece of history that pairs well with a glass cabinet or a well-lit shelf.
