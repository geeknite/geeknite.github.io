1) Position the cabinet where you want it, using the leveling feet to compensate for any floor irregularities.
2) Open the front and rear doors to plan the cable routing strategy. Map out your PDU placement, power distribution, and how you’ll route data cabling so you don’t resemble a spaghetti monster.

5) Seal up and test. Power up one side at a time, check for airflow, and listen for any strange grinding sounds that might indicate something is in conflict with the frame or your enthusiasm.

In daily use, the cabinet is straightforward. It’s easy to access the rear for maintenance, which reduces the time you spend rearranging cables whenever a critical patch is needed.
