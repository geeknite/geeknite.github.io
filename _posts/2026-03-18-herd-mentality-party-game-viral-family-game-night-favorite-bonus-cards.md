## What is Herd Mentality?

Herd Mentality is a party game designed by Dom Ruse and created by North Star Games. It’s a fast-paced, social experiment where players write down answers to prompts, then attempt to align with the “herd” in a way that scores points. The core charm is that it celebrates the human tendency to cluster around popular opinions, even if those opinions are absolutely ridiculous when you inspect them with the cold light of rational thought.

This is the kind of game that makes you realize you’ve been a secret explorer of your own group’s collective brain for years. The prompts lean into everyday life—things people say, things people do, and the odd little quirks that make a family reunion feel like a microcosm of society. If you’ve ever felt the urge to high-five the person who says the wrong answer at the right moment, Herd Mentality is your spiritual successor to that moment you’ll tell your grandchildren about.

### The vibe
The vibe is casually chaotic. You don’t need a fancy game room or a dedicated board game shelf to play. The rules are approachable, the components are simple, and the humor arises from shared experiences—embarrassing anecdotes, inside jokes, and the universal experience of realizing your siblings are all suspiciously good at guessing the crowd’s favorite pick. It’s a party game by design, not by accident; it rewards quick thinking, a generous sense of humor, and a willingness to be just a touch ridiculous on cue.

## How to Play: A Quick-Start Guide

If you’ve somehow avoided every meme about Herd Mentality since it first appeared on your social feed, here’s the streamlined version to get you from zero to hilarious in under 10 minutes.

### Setup
1. Gather players (the more, the merrier). The game works best with at least 4, but you’ll get the most satire per square inch with 6–10 people.
2. Shuffle the prompt deck and place it face down.
3. Each round, a non-writing player (or a volunteer role) reads the prompt aloud.
4. Everyone writes down their answer on an answer card or a sheet, depending on your edition. The key is secrecy—no peeking at others’ thoughts.
5. When the round time ends, everyone reveals their answer to the group.

### Round flow
1. The herd discusses, debates, and assigns the role of “the most popular” answer verbally for fun while still submitting written answers.
2. Players secretly place their answer cards on a central area or hand them to the round moderator.
3. The moderator or the group reveals which answer appeared most frequently (the herd’s pick). Everyone who matched the herd’s pick scores points.
4. If the Bonus Cards are in play, a few extra twists may apply—more on that below.

### Scoring basics
- Match the herd’s top choice and you gain a point. Simple, right?
- If you nail a rare but correct crowd pick (as dictated by a Bonus Card), you might earn bonus points.
- If your answer is unique, you might miss out unless a Bonus Card says otherwise. The balance here is what often drives the humor: you can be delighted you were right, or you can be absolutely mad you were alone with that choice.

### The role of randomness vs. skill
Herd Mentality is a social game that thrives on the unpredictability of human behavior. It’s not about being a “perfect” strategist; it’s about reading the room, being willing to pivot, and laughing at the inevitable moments of groupthink. The more you play, the more you’ll start noticing patterns—like which prompts tend to flood with the same answers or which players tend to tilt the herd without even trying.

## Bonus Cards: The Expansion, in a Nutshell

If you’ve played Herd Mentality and found yourself wishing for more curveballs, the Bonus Cards expansion is basically the after-hours lounge for this game’s party vibe. It introduces additional rules that can dramatically alter scores and round dynamics. Here’s what to expect:

### How to integrate Bonus Cards during a session
- Decide at the start whether you’ll play with a set number of Bonus Cards or a full deck per game.

## Strategy: How to Play Smarter, Not Just Louder

While Herd Mentality is a party game built on social energy rather than hardcore strategy, a few light tactics can help you maximize your fun (and your points) without killing the vibe:

- Know your crowd. The more you understand the group’s common experiences and inside jokes, the easier it is to predict the herd’s likely choice. If you’re always the quiet one, you’ll want to observe the room rather than overthink.
- Let the vibe guide you. Sometimes the funniest action isn’t the perfect crowd-pleaser pick but a bold, unexpected guess that changes the room’s dynamic.
- Use the energy, not the rules, to your advantage. If you notice the group tends to cluster around popular answers, don’t fight it—lean into the herd and ride that momentum to victory.
- Rotate the moderator role. A fresh moderator who enforces time limits and keeps the pace up makes the game feel fresh. The moderator becomes the conductor of chaos, and a good one can elevate the entire session.
- Respect the tone. The point of a family game night is bonding, not breeding acrimony. If a round goes sideways into “too intense,” soften the moment with a quick joke or table-wide grin before continuing.

## Thematic Variants and House Rules

If you’re a seasoned host, you’ll probably enjoy adding rules that tailor Herd Mentality to your group’s quirks. Here are some light options that keep things playful without turning into a rules-heavy dungeon crawl:

- Family Edition: Remove prompts that rely on very specific knowledge and replace them with prompts that lean into universal experiences. The aim is laughter, not head-scratching.
- Team-rounds: Split into two teams. Each team writes an answer collectively and then faces off against the opposing team’s chosen herd answer. This adds a cooperative twist to the typical head-to-head format.
- Rapid-fire rounds: Set a strict 15–20 second limit for submitting answers. Faster rounds fuel quicker banter and reduce filler content.
- Nostalgia mode: Use prompts that reference favorite 90s cartoons, early internet culture, or classic family dinners. The nostalgia factor often triggers stronger laughs and more shared memory jokes.

## A Deep Dive into Bonus Cards: Card-by-Card Flavor Explosion

The Bonus Cards are the real spice rack for this dish. They don’t replace the core rules; they remix them. Here’s a closer look at the flavor you’ll get when you crack open that expansion:

### Card types and effects
- Risk-Reward Cards: They push you to gamble on an answer that might be wildly popular or completely off-the-wall. The payoff can swing the game in dramatic fashion.
- Mimic Cards: Force someone to imitate a public figure or a fictional character’s catchphrase within the round, adding a layer of performance.
- Time-Pressure Cards: Shorten the window to answer, increasing tension and hilarity as players scramble to craft something witty in record time.
- Chaos Cards: Randomly assign a temporary rule that everyone must follow (e.g., write answers in napkin crumples or with the non-dominant hand).

### How to weave cards into sessions
- Decide in advance how many Bonus Cards will be used per game to avoid overwhelming new players.
- Have a quick parenthetical note ready when a card changes the game’s rhythm. A 5-second breather works wonders to reset the room between rounds.
- Keep the Bonus Cards visible, not buried. A quick hands-up to draw your next card keeps momentum high and attention sharp.

### The “fan” questions that nail the humor
We’ve observed that prompts that touch on pop culture, family quirks, and everyday social rituals tend to produce the best results when combined with Bonus Cards. For example, prompts like “What’s the loudest thing you’ve ever heard at the dinner table?” or “Name a celebrity you’d trust to plan your family reunion” typically yield the most shareable moments. Bonus Cards that riff on these themes tend to amplify both the humor and the energy of the room.

## Practical Tips for Hosts

- Play area: Clear a space where everyone can see the prompt and the answers without leaning over the same table. A circular seating arrangement works wonders for visibility and laughs.
- Timer etiquette: If you’re using a timer, be consistent. A 60-second limit is often plenty for most prompts, but some games benefit from a slightly longer window when the group is just warming up.
- Accessibility: Consider kids and players with different accessibility needs. Use larger writing surfaces, bright prompts, and allow for a longer lobby between rounds to accommodate slower players.
- Photo moments: The best memories come from the reveals. Consider snapping a few candid photos (with consent) to capture the hilarity. You’ll thank yourself later when you’re compiling year-end “Best Family Moments” posts.
- Cleaning up: Herd Mentality is pretty forgiving to reset. A quick shuffle of cards, a wipe of the table, and you’re ready for round two or a quick after-dinner wind-down session.
