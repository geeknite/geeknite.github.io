## What Fortune Row actually is
Fortune Row is a compact, mood-forward accessory pack for City of Mist. It is designed to provide quick, usable seeds for scenes, micro-encounters, and world-building prompts that align with the games mythic, noir aesthetic. It is not a replacement for the core rules, nor a canned adventure. It is a toolbox that gives you immediate prompts to drop into a session when you need a spark, a scene pivot, or a prop that suddenly matters in the hands of the players. The design ethos is to help the GM achieve a sense of place and mood quickly, without forcing players down a single, predetermined path.

The pack typically contains a mix of items you can hand to players, cards your GM can draw on the fly, a fold-out map fragment, and a short zine with bits of lore, hooks, and tips. The content is written with the City of Mist vibe in mind: modern noir, a city that is more myth than concrete, and heroes whose powers come with a price tag stamped in the margins of fate. This is the kind of product you can drop into a single session or use as a regular hobby booster while you craft longer arcs.

## Inside the box: what you actually get
Fortune Row is designed to feel tactile, usable, and thematically coherent. Here is a rough idea of the components you are likely to encounter, with tips on how to use each one at the table:

- Fortune prompts deck: Short prompts that ignite a scene or reveal a piece of a character arc. They are designed to be read aloud, then used as a springboard for description, investigation, or confrontation.
- Prop and gear cards: Small, tangible items that players can reference during a scene. They function as MacGuffins, clues, or just cool props that make the noir mood land in a tangible way.
- GM tips and hooks: Crisp guidance on how to prime a scene, how to escalate tension, and how to thread the prompt back into the mythos and the PCs backstories.
- Fold-out map fragment: A compact piece of a map that gives players a sense of place and can anchor a micro-arc in Fortune Row itself. It is intentionally small so you do not have to commit to a full neighborhood map during a one-shot.
- Zine-style guide: Short essays or bullet lists on how to present Fortune Row, how to pace scenes, and how to link prompts to recurring themes in City of Mist. The tone remains playful, with nods to noir tropes and mythic revelations.

In practice, Fortune Row feels like a well-curated gift bag for a GM in the throes of session planning. It respects your time, but it also respects the players' appetite for mythic moments. It is not a heavy lift; it is a gentle tap on the shoulder that says hey, something mythic and dangerous is just around the corner if you lean into it.

## Thematic anchors and flavor
Fortune Row is built to live in the same neighborhood as your PCs mythoi and the city’s shadow economy. The prompts lean into two core ideas: fortune as a weapon and fortune as a trap. The deck often juxtaposes luck with consequence, shining a spotlight on protagonists who chase luck in a city that is more likely to turn a blind eye than to bless them. Expect prompts that trigger a moral choice, that reveal a hidden relationship between a PC and Fortune Row, or that pivot a scene on a small prop that suddenly takes on enormous significance.

This is not a mechanical overhaul. It is a mood engine. When you read a prompt, you should feel a tug to describe a place, a vibe, or a consequence in a way that heightens the noir and mythic tone of the game. The writing tends to be compact and evocative rather than lengthy or encyclopedic, which is exactly what you want when you are on a tight clock with a room full of players waiting for you to describe a neon-lit alley that hums with mythic electricity.

## How to use Fortune Row in play
If you are new to City of Mist, Fortune Row can feel like a gentle gateway into the deeper, more myth-driven play style the game encourages. If you are a veteran GM, it can function as a quick-start mood engine that helps you improvise without feeling like you are improvising blindfolded.

Here are practical modes of use:

- Scene openers: Start a scene by drawing a fortune prompt and reading it aloud. Let the prompt set the mood and trigger a first action from one the PCs or from a supporting NPC. The prompt becomes a guiding star for the rest of the scene.
- Prop-driven investigations: Use a prop card as a clue that leads to a location or to a mythic tie-in. The prop should feel valuable enough for players to argue about its significance and dangerous enough to justify risk.
- Micro-arcs inside Fortune Row: Use the fold-out map fragment to anchor a micro-arc around a handful of locations in Fortune Row. The players explore, uncover a ritual, and either rescue or wreck the neon-drenched block.
- Mythoi checks and ties: The prompts can reveal or test a PC's mythoi—how the hero's identity interacts with luck and fate. A prompt can push a PC toward a choice that exposes a mythic weakness or unlocks a new power in a moment of crisis.
- Mood and table dynamics: Fortune Row is as much about what the table feels like as it is about what the players do. Use descriptive language, sound cues, and light adjustments to emphasize the city as a living character.

GM tips to maximize the pack
- Do not try to force a long arc out of a single prompt. Let prompts seed beats and lines of inquiry. The players will drive the story if you give them interesting prompts and a safe space to interpret them.
- If your table tends to over-index on action, use the prompts to slow the pace and emphasize mood. The contrast between fast action and a moment of reflection is where City of Mist shines.
- If you have players who love backstory, tailor prompts to align with PC mythoi. A fortune prompt that echoes a PC’s fear or desire can create memorable, character-defining moments.
- Use the map fragment as a pacing tool. When you hit a lull, show the fragment and reorient the scene toward place and agency. People respond to place; Fortune Row gives them a place to stand and act from.

## Lore, flavor, and style notes
Fortune Row sits comfortably in the noir corners of City of Mist. Its language hints at the metropolis that never sleeps and the secrets that cling to the walls like damp neon. Expect conversations that begin with a street vendor peddling a mysterious trinket and end with a revelation that ties into a mythic lineage a PC never fully realized they possessed. It is not a tutorial on the city; it is a reminder that the city is a living myth, and the players are the agents who re-purpose that myth into their own legends.

The art direction stays on brand: moody, highly legible, with a tasteful use of gold accents and high-contrast noir palettes. The packaging feels sturdy enough to survive a few campaign seasons and also light enough to carry in a backpack during a one-shot run or a convention slot.

## How it plays with existing material
Fortune Row is designed as a supplement, not a substitute. It respects the core City of Mist rules and the principle that the city itself is the ultimate antagonist and ally. It can be used with almost any arc you’ve already started, from long-form myth arc to micro-episode. It does not require you to drop your existing mythoi out like a deck of outdated cards; instead, it nudges the deck toward new combinations and reveals a few fresh angles on familiar figures.

If you want direct comparisons, this pack is closest in spirit to mood-focused expansions rather than story-driven campaigns. It gives you atmosphere, a handful of plot seeds, and a handful of props that can become focal points of investigation. If you are accustomed to heavy, long-form expansions, you may find Fortune Row refreshingly compact and wonderfully versatile for quick sessions.

- Beat: A race to recover the device before a rival faction uses the signal to bend fortune for themselves.
- Outcome: The players gain leverage against a local foe or an ally who has a secret of their own.

Scenario C: The Hand of Fate
- Setup: A street magician performs at a storefront window and the hand of fate manifests as a visible glimmer on a prop.
- Prompt: The crowd becomes a stage for a choice that will shape a PC destiny.
- Beat: You escalate tension by blurring reality and myth, forcing players to decide if they want to accept a new power or reject it.
- Outcome: A critical decision redefines a PC myth and sets up a future choice in a later session.

## Final thoughts and a verdict you can take to the bar with you
Fortune Row is not a revolution in City of Mist play, and that is not a criticism. It is a pragmatic, stylish, mood-forward add-on that makes sessions feel more alive without demanding a full refactor of your city or a new pair of mythic glasses for every player. If you want a pack that respects your time, adds gentle elevation to the mood, and gives you ready-to-use seeds for sessions you would otherwise run on autopilot, Fortune Row earns a solid recommendation. It shines brightest when used as a mood engine in micro-arcs, a prop-driven investigation tool, or a quick-start spark when you want to jumpstart a scene with a confident, noir-flavored prompt.

Pros:
- Quick mood setup and scalable prompts
- Strong noir aesthetic that aligns with City of Mist mythos
- Flexible use across different arc lengths
- High-quality, tactile components that feel good to handle

Cons:
- Not a full campaign; you will still need core material for longer arcs
- Some prompts are best paired with improvisation and a GM who loves to riff
- The fold-out map is small and best used as a seed rather than a complete urban plan

If you are a GM who loves a spark, a prop, and a moment of mythic choice, Fortune Row will become a go-to toolbox in your GM kit. If you prefer heavy, structure-forward expansions, you may want to treat Fortune Row as a spice rack rather than a main course. Either way, it embraces the spirit of City of Mist: a city where fortune is a weapon and fate is a choice you can negotiate with.
