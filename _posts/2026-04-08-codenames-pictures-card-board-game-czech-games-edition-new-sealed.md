## Gameplay and mechanics

Codenames Pictures keeps the core structure: two teams, a grid, a spymaster delivering clues, and players trying to identify which pictures belong to their team’s narrative. The twist is that clues connect to images rather than words. The spymaster gives a one word clue followed by a number, like flight 3, and players debate which three images best align with that concept. The challenge is to choose images that point to your team’s cards without accidentally picking those for the other team or the dreaded assassin card that ends the round instantly.

Here is a quick refresher on the core rules:
- The grid is usually 5x4 or 5x5, depending on player count and edition
- Each image is a potential target (blue or red) or neutral
- The spymaster must craft a clue that relates to as many of their team’s images as allowed by the number
- The other team gets to guess after hearing the clue and players can discuss and debate freely, as long as they do not reveal the exact code
- The first team to identify all their cards wins; hitting the assassin ends the game immediately

In practice, the Czech Games Edition version follows the same core mechanics, but the art and card density can affect pacing. The density of images can slow or accelerate as players try to decode visuals, and the number of images per game can push the length of sessions. The more you know your teammates, the better your clues will land, and the more you will sanity check your own choices.
