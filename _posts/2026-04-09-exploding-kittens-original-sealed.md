## How Exploding Kittens plays

Exploding Kittens is a passively chaotic experience that rewards bluffing, quick thinking, and a willingness to embrace the ridiculous. The goal is to avoid drawing an Exploding Kitten card while using Defuse cards or other strategic actions to manipulate the deck. The rules are straightforward enough that you can pick up the game in a few minutes, yet there is enough room for misdirection and social antics to fill an entire evening.

### Setup and turn structure

- Shuffle the deck and deal each player a handful of cards. The exact number depends on the number of players, but you usually end up with enough to start a few cat-card shenanigans and a couple of Defuse cards for safety.
- Place the Exploding Kitten cards into the deck according to the number of players, minus any that are removed by Defuse or other effects. The idea is that someone will eventually take a risk and draw the kitten. That is the moment the room collectively holds its breath.
- Each player starts with a certain number of Defuse cards. A Defuse is a lifeline that lets you evade doom and laugh in the face of cat chaos for at least one more turn.

On a typical turn, you either play one or more cards or draw from the deck. You can cancel certain actions with Nope cards, forcing a small victory dance while those around you groan in disbelief. See the Future cards let you peek at the top few cards of the deck, which can be incredibly powerful in a crowd of risk-takers who love to pretend they are psychic.

### Card types and what they do

- Exploding Kitten: The dread card that ends your run unless you defuse it. It is the reason your friends chant unintelligible things while the timer on your phone already buzzes with the next message in your group chat.
- Defuse: A lifeline that uses a cat-themed event to prevent elimination. It is the antidote to despair and the reason you still have a seat at the table after someone plays a ridiculous combo.
- See the Future: Look at the top N cards of the deck. You are suddenly allowed to pretend to be a fortune teller, and your friends pretend not to be terrified of spoilers.
- Attack, Skip, Shuffle, See the Future, Nope: These action cards modify turns, force opponents to draw, or cancel plays. They create a dynamic tempo where every draw could be your last, or perhaps your turn to shine.
- Cat Cards: Tacocat, Hairy Potato Cat, Achoo, and friends. Collecting matching pairs allows you to steal a turn or gain other advantages, depending on the exact edition. The cat cards add whimsy and humor to what would otherwise be a simple draw-and-hope game.

This is a game that thrives on interaction. The social dynamics—bluffing, taunting, backseat strategy, and occasional petty revenge—are what make Exploding Kittens more than just a deck of randomness. It rewards you for paying attention to who is likely to blow up the moment, who has a Defuse tucked away, and who is just bluffing about their card advantages.
