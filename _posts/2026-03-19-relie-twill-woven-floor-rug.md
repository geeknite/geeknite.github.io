## Durability, Cleaning, and Caring for Your Rug Over Time

Durability is the sort of quiet virtue you notice only after you’ve owned the rug for a few months. The Relie Twill Woven Floor Rug is built to withstand everyday wear while maintaining its shape and texture. ### Cleaning routines that fit a busy life

## The Tech of Twill: Why It Matters in a Rug

Twill weave is famous for its durability and texture. In a rug, the weave determines how fibers respond to wear and how easily it hides minor imperfections. The diagonal pattern distributes stress more evenly than a plain weave, which is a neat way of saying your rug won’t look tired after a few seasons of high foot traffic. The technical takeaway is simple: if you want a rug that keeps its aesthetic without fuss, a twill weave is your friend. It’s the difference between a hero shot in your home staging and the home’s real life where shoes come off, crumbs accumulate, and life continues.

Use a mix of ambient, task, and accent lighting to highlight the rug’s texture. The morning light will reveal a warm glow, while evening LEDs can make the fibers pop with a subtle sheen that photographs nicely for those midori-green Instagram angles you’re into.

### Flow and traffic considerations
Place the rug where people walk most and where chairs glide in and out without snagging. The goal is to create a visual and tactile pathway that invites people to linger rather than step over the edge of the furniture like a cartoon character stepping onto a cliff.
