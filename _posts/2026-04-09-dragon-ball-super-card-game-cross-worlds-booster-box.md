## What's inside the Cross Worlds Booster Box
A typical Cross Worlds box follows the usual DBS booster box cadence: 24 packs per box, with 12 cards per pack. The math isn't sexy, but it pays the bills when you want to estimate the odds of a foil chase. You should expect a mix of common and uncommon cards that power up to rare or double rare, with at least one hollow foil or premium card per few packs. And yes, there will be special foil variants that glitter under the living room lamp like a dragon ball summons itself.

If you enjoy the thrill of a fresh pull, consider that box openings are part performance art. YouTube videos aside, the unboxing experience matters: the reseal weight, the crispness of the card stock, the scent of new cardboard, and the moment when you realize you just pulled a card that might carry a price tag bigger than your lunch budget.

For those who like a little reading, the Cross Worlds set includes new characters and cross-universe interactions that affect deck-building philosophy. It’s less about brute power and more about pacing, tempo, and the art of the bluff—do you push a threat here or wait for the next turn to tempo your opponent into misplays?

## Card Design and Flavor
Cross Worlds continues the DBS tradition of bold artwork and high-contrast frames. The cards showcase dynamic poses, vivid color palettes, and a level of polish that makes your local tournament feel like a cinematic event. The card frame remains familiar to veterans, but with slightly adjusted borders and holo accents that pop when viewed at angle under game-store lighting.

Collectors will love the foil patterns, which vary by rarity. The chase cards tend to feature more elaborate borders, gold foils, or texture treatments that catch the eye the moment you spread the pile on the table. The artwork does not shy away from iconic moments, and there are several unexpected crossovers that feel like Easter eggs for long-time fans.

## Rarity, Pull Rates, and Value
Let’s talk numbers without turning your brain into a Super Saiyan meltdown. Box pull expectations for Cross Worlds align with typical DBS box dynamics: multiple rares per box, a handful of foils, and the occasional ultra-rare that makes you wonder how the universe conspired to give you that exact pull on that exact night.

- Common and uncommon cards serve as the backbone for deck construction and fun, playable experiences in draft-like formats.
- Rare and double rare cards give you that spine-tingling moment when your opponent realizes your deck’s synergy window opened on a turn where you just landed the right combination.
- Foil variants pop in a way that can affect the perceived value of a box. If you’re a collector, you’ll chase those shinier versions; if you’re a player, you’ll care more about the cards that actually improve your deck.

Price-wise, plan for $90–$130 per booster box, with local shops sometimes pricing higher or lower. If you’re new to sealed product, factor in tax and shipping if you’re buying online. If you’re a bargain hunter, compare prices across retailers and watch for limited-time promos that might sweeten the deal.

## Box Value for Collectors and Players
A key question is whether the box price translates into real-world value. If you’re chasing a handful of premium foils or a coveted card that becomes the backbone of a top-tier deck, you’re likely to extract meaningful value over time. However, the sport aspect—buying a sealed box for future price appreciation—requires patience and tolerance for market fluctuations.

Keep an eye on supply and demand metrics in your region. In some markets, Cross Worlds boxes vanish from shelves quickly, driving secondary market prices up; in others, supply outpaces demand, and boxes sit in inventory longer than your local gaming group’s interest in lapsed hero cards.

We also must mention that sealed product has intangible value: a sense of anticipation, a social ritual around unboxing, and the satisfaction of finishing a complete set with a few of your friends. That intangible value can sometimes outshine the raw card value, especially for fans who value the experience of the hobby.
