### Variants and House Rules
One of the joys of Throw Throw Burrito is that you can adapt the rules to fit your crew. The Extreme Outdoor Edition invites a few outdoor friendly variants:

- The Sprint Round: A timer is set, and players must complete a series of actions before the clock runs out. If you survive, you score extra points.
- The Team Duel: Players form teams and use the card effects to support team members or hinder the opposing team. It is basically a chaotic version of capture the flag with burritos.
- The Wind Challenge: A more advanced variant that requires players to adjust their throw strength based on the wind direction. It is a test of physics literacy and competitive banter in equal measure.

## Tips and Tricks from Real World Play
- Always scout the playing area for obstacles and pets that might misinterpret floating burritos as chew toys. The last thing you want is a heroic chase scene with your neighbor dog involved.
- Communicate clearly. The humor of the game comes from the misfires and the misreads. Short calls and exaggerated reactions help everyone follow along and maximize laughter.
- Do not overdo the throws. Foam burritos are safe but dents to your pride occur when you launch a throw that is too ambitious for your own spine. Pace yourself, then surprise the group with one truly dramatic arc that looks like a slow motion montage.
- Embrace the wind. The wind can transform an ordinary round into a heroic sequence. Use it wisely and you get more content for your social media captions.
- Bring a buddy to pause the game for a water break and to take photos. A game like this can generate better content when the players look like superheroes for a brief moment.
