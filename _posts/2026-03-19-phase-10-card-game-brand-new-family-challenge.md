## What Phase 10 is
Phase 10 is a card game built around the idea of completing ten distinct phases. The deck is a little dramatic, featuring numbers 1 through 12, plus wild cards and skip cards. The goal is not to accumulate the most cards but to cross the finish line of phase after phase, like a marathon where the track is formed by sets, runs, and enough tactical misdirection to keep everyone honest. The learning curve is gentle enough for kids to grasp, yet the strategic layer invites adults to plan ahead, memorize opponents moves, and decide when to risk a wild card or a strategic skip on the person who looks most likely to finish their current phase first.

## How to play in a nutshell
1) Shuffle and deal 10 cards to each player. The rest form a draw pile and the top card starts the discard pile.
2) On your turn, draw a card from either the draw pile or the discard pile, then discard one card.
3) If you can, lay down a completed phase on the table. Phases must be completed in order, and they are a mix of sets (cards with the same number) and runs (cards in sequence).
4) After laying down a phase, you can add to your own phase or to others who have laid down, with the goal of shedding your hand faster.
5) If you get rid of all your cards, you go out and the round ends. Everyone tallys points based on the cards left in their hands.
6) Start a new round with a new deal and continue through the rest of the phases.

Skip cards untie the social knot by skipping the next player; wild cards substitute any needed card in a set or run. The tension comes from watching the table while you try to stay one step ahead of the phase ladder.

## Strategy tips for Phase 10
- Start with a flexible plan: have a few possible paths for your current phase and adapt as the draw pile behaves like a mischievous imp.
- Weigh your draws: pick from the discard pile only if it accelerates your phase progress or disrupts a rival without tipping them off to your strategy.
- Use skip cards thoughtfully: they are your social leverage. A well-timed skip can derail a neighbor who looks poised to climb through a difficult phase.
- Don’t overuse wilds: they are powerful but can encourage sloppy endings. Save them for the moment you are close to finishing a phase and need a precise card to seal the deal.
- Watch the table: track who is likely to complete which phase and adjust your own strategy to block or outrun them.
