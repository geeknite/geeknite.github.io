### Assembly and setup: Step by step
1. Clear the desk and lay out all components on a soft surface. You don’t want little screws rolling around like tumbleweeds or, worse, disappearing into the void behind your monitor.
2. Attach the side panels to the base frame using the included screws. Use the Allen wrench to tighten gently; overtightening can strip the threads, and that’s not a boss move, it’s a maintenance nightmare.
3. Insert the middle shelves and align the pre-drilled holes. Some kits use plastic dowels to align, others rely on metal screws—either way, aim for snug, not Frankenstein-tight. A little space is okay; even the best gamers know that you can’t win if your desk drawer is a war zone.
4. Attach the top shelf and set the entire unit upright. Ensure the piece sits flush on the desk and doesn’t rock. If it rocks, you probably forgot the anti-slip pads. Add them and test again.
5. Place your items on the shelves. It’s a desk organizer; don’t try to store a full encyclopedia in there, or your monitor will start scolding you about the back strain you’re causing.

If you’d rather watch someone else do the hard work for you, there are plenty of online video guides that show similar assemblies. Just keep an eye out for the ones that show you how to use the wrong screws and end up with a leaning tower of desk, which is funny until your monitor slides off and you realize the shelf was a bad life choice.
