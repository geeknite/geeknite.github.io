## How to Use It: A Structured Yet Flexible Path to Mindful Play
The box is designed to be used multiple ways, depending on your rhythm. Here is a flexible path that preserves the spirit while staying practical.

1) Unbox and choose your mode. Will you treat this as a daily micro-practice or a weekend personal project that unfolds at its own pace?
2) Start with the Brain Model. Spin it, identify the lobes, say their names aloud if you want to dramatize it, and reflect on your own attention dynamics during a tricky task.
3) Dive into the Mental Models Deck. Pick a card at random, read the concept, and map it onto a current challenge. Write one concrete action that applies the model to your next decision.
4) Journal your findings. Sketch a map of your biases, then annotate a plan to test one bias in a real-world setting. The aim isn’t to win a debate but to understand how you think when outcomes matter.
5) Run a quick experiment. The Guided Experiment Cards provide templates you can adapt to a kitchen, a dorm, or a transit ride. If you’re in a public space, you can pretend you are a mindfulness trendsetter and watch the reactions.
6) Posture the poster into your daily routine. Put it where you’ll see it and run small, structured thinking sessions around it.
7) Listen to the QR-Pod on the commute. The podcast adds context and humor that helps land the ideas, making them feel practical rather than theoretical.

If you want a simple, step-by-step progression, the included guides do a solid job of scaffolding without turning thinking into a graduate seminar. If you prefer improvisation, you’ll still find open prompts to shape your own journey. The core value is in the slow, deliberate pace that lets you observe patterns in your own thinking rather than rushing toward conclusions. In a world of instant gratification, that is a small rebellion and a big win for curiosity.
