## How to play: a quick start guide

Monopoly Deal is a card game, not your typical Monopoly board slog. The objective is simple: collect three complete property sets. The rest is a delightful flurry of card plays, tiny economic decisions, and occasional betrayals that would make any in-game banker consider a career in standup comedy. Here is a quick start guide to help you warm up the family engine:

1) Setup: Shuffle the deck and deal five cards to each player. Place the remaining deck in the center. The game moves quickly when players know what their cards do.
2) Turn sequence: On your turn, you may play up to three cards from your hand. You can lay down property cards in front of you, play money to your bank area, or use action cards to affect other players or the rent total. The main goal is to complete one or more property sets by collecting color groups.
3) Cash and rent economy: Money cards go into your bank to pay rents or to pay a bank-run challenger who wants to claim your property. Rent cards are used to charge other players for your property sets. Some rent cards force other players to pay, while others are designed to cheat or subvert. The interplay of rent and property is the spicy sauce of this game.
4) Special cards: Deal Breaker can steal a full color property set from another player. Sly Deal can steal a single property from another player. Just Say No counters many of these moves; it’s your best friend when someone tries to pull off a last-m-minute steal of your prized color set.
5) Endgame: The round ends when someone makes three full property sets. If you suspect a late-stage villainy from your table, save a Just Say No for the final heist and savor the victory or prepare for your own epic meltdown.

The gameplay loop is brisk, with enough misdirection and backroom deals to keep adults entertained and kids engaged.
