# City of Mist: Fortune Row — Pack d'aventures

If you thought your city was weird before, welcome to Fortune Row, the pack of adventures for City of Mist that insists on turning every lamppost into a neon-tinted interrogation room and every alleyway into a velvet-clad stage. ## What Fortune Row is, in plain words

Fortune Row is an adventure pack — a curated set of scenarios designed to slot into the City of Mist toolkit. Think of it as a gin-soaked dossier handed to your GM, with three or four distinct story threads, villainous vignettes, and enough sticky notes about city myths to wallpaper every corridor in your fictional megapolis. The vibe? Moonlit skylines, retro-futurist signage, and a turf war between factions who each claim the street belongs to their mythos. The result is a pack that leans into the core strengths of City of Mist: a detective-noir that wears a mythic mask like a statement piece of jewelry.

Fortune Row isn’t just a collection of encounters; it’s a spine to string several sessions around, with connective threads that help you weave a cohesive arc across a city that feels alive, contradictory, and a little bit dangerous. If you’re itching to run something that smacks of old-friends-and-new-enemies meeting in a backroom where deals are struck in whispers and neon, this is likely to scratch that itch with precision and flair.

### The three pillars of Fortune Row

Fortune Row leans on three intertwined pillars that inform both character and encounter design:

- The street-level noir that makes your heroes feel like private eyes with a license to improvise.
- The mythic understructure that lets suspects carry an extraordinary burden — or a hidden power — beneath everyday clothes.
- The procedural puzzle that treats clues as both plot devices and character reveals, rewarding players who think with both their brains and their masks.

In practice, Fortune Row masterfully balances stagecraft with investigative complexity. You’ll get a mix of linear threads and open-ended investigations, allowing you to push players toward big discoveries while still giving you, the GM, the room to riff and adapt on the fly.

## What’s inside Fortune Row: a tour through the contents

Fortune Row ships with several components designed to support sessions of varied length, from one-off nights to week-long arcs. Here’s a breakdown of what you’ll likely encounter:

- A mapped district kit. A city map with key locations, faction nodes, and mythic linkages. This isn’t a page of abstract locales; it’s a fully realized grid that makes navigation feel tactile. The art is moody and a little flirtatious with the uncanny.
- Four to six fully prepared scenes. Each scene includes a hook, a set of goals, a handful of complications, and a few “mask” interactions that push characters toward dramatic reveals.
- NPCs with clear masks and motives. Expect well-rounded characters who aren’t merely obstacles; they’re people with beliefs, fears, and loyalties that ripple through the entire arc.
- Clue bundles and investigation prompts. Clues aren’t random; they’re connected to the city’s myths and to the players’ evolving Masks. The design nudges players to piece together a bigger picture rather than just collect shiny evidence.
- A GM’s quick-start guide. A few pages with tempo suggestions, encounter pacing charts, and a short checklist to keep sessions moving when the neon lights start to blur.

If you’re a GM who’s comfortable with the City of Mist toolbox, Fortune Row lets you lean into the game’s strengths: letting players steer the investigation with their own unique mythic identities while you supply the atmosphere and the reveals.

### A walk through the adventures

The pack typically threads through three to four adventures that can be run standalone or stitched into a longer arc. Here’s a snapshot you can expect (with spoilers kept light and non-mechanical):

- The Velvet Ledger: A mystery wrapped in a gilded promise. The heroes chase a ledger that reveals a city-wide secret society tied to a mythic figure who wore the mask long before the first neon sign flickered. It’s a classic Fortune Row opener: investigative, mood-driven, full of red herrings and sharp dialogue.
- Neon Alibi: An evening of wrong places at the right times, and consequences that reach through the luxury districts into the undercommons. It’s a heist-adjacent scenario where timing, misdirection, and who is wearing which mask drive the tension. Expect clever set-pieces and a final reveal that lands with a satisfying thud.
- The Paper Rats: A more intimate, character-driven thread that explores the city’s memory and who gets to decide what stories survive. It’s a perceptual puzzle: clues aren’t just facts; they’re memories shaped by the city’s mythic currents. The stakes feel personal, and the emotional payoff sticks with players after the last page is closed.

### A closer look at the Masks and Mythos

City of Mist runs on a dance between Masks (the legible aspects of characters) and Mythos (the hidden patterns shaping the city). Fortune Row leans hard into this dichotomy by presenting villains and allies who are recognizable in daylight but operate under a different calculus after dark. The pack offers:

- Clear triggers for Masks to interact with the investigation, such that players aren’t forced to “perform” their alter ego but naturally reveal facets of themselves as clues accumulate.
- Mythic hooks that let GMs escalate the arc toward a city-altering revelation instead of a simple one-shot win condition. The result is a sense that Fortune Row is a chapter in a larger, living city rather than a stand-alone issue.

If you’re new to the system, you’ll appreciate how Fortune Row teaches players to use their Masks as tools for discovery rather than mere power-ups. It’s not just about who you are on the street; it’s about who you become as the clues accumulate and the mysteries deepen.

Fortune Row’s presentation prioritizes atmosphere without sacrificing clarity. The layout is clean, with:

- A grid-based map that’s easy to follow during a session, supplemented by callouts for key locations and their mythic significance.
- Art that complements the mood rather than shouting over it. The visuals tend to favor noir color palettes and understated symbolism, which is exactly what you want when you’re trying to evoke mood rather than incredulity.

If you care about accessibility, Fortune Row does a considerate job of flagging potential content concerns and offering options for players who want to keep the darker edges a touch more veiled. It’s not a perfect safety blanket, but it’s a thoughtful attempt to make the vibe inclusive without dulling the noir edge.

## Running Fortune Row at the table: tips and tricks

Here are a few practical facets to consider when you’re ready to bring Fortune Row to life:

- Start with mood, not mechanics. The pack provides a strong atmosphere; lean into it for the first session. Let players acclimate to the Masks and the city’s rhythms before you bolt on the larger puzzle.
- Use the district map as a character. The city isn’t merely the backdrop; it’s a driving force for the clues. Have NPCs reference streets and districts in a way that pushes players to physically move and explore rather than rely on a static dungeon map.
- Encourage social cleverness. City of Mist rewards players who think in terms of factions, myths, and cunning. Sessions should reward dialogue, deception, and alliance-building as much as action sequences.
- Plan for the inevitable detours. Fortune Row offers connective threads that can deviate; expect players to chase a scent into a side alley that reveals a better story than your published outline.
- Keep the tone flexible. The pack is noir with a neon wink. If your table gravitates toward pulp, lean into it; if they prefer dour realism, emphasize the tragedy and humanity within the masks.

### Mechanics notes: how the pack fits with core rules

Fortune Row uses standard City of Mist mechanics but emphasizes a few narrative beats to encourage investigation-driven play:

- Clues that require consensus interpretation. Players should feel that the truth is collective in nature and often lies in how a group of masks connects clues rather than in a single character’s discovery.
- Mask-driven consequences. Decisions should surface emotional or social consequences for the characters, nudging players to lean into the relationship between their masks and their actions.
- Thematic escalation. Each scene builds toward a larger revelation, ensuring the campaign doesn’t stall during a mid-arc lull.

## Thematic resonance: urban myth meets private eye

Fortune Row isn’t shy about leaning into City of Mist’s core thematic tension: reality is porous, and everyone wears a mask that might be more true than the person underneath. Fortune Row amplifies this with a district that feels almost too real to be fictional. It’s where you’ll see the thematic sweet spot emerge:

- Masks as social tools. Characters aren’t just powerful; their masks determine how others perceive and react to them in social scenes. Fortune Row gives you opportunities to explore how identity shapes influence and leverage.
- Mythic bureaucracy. The city’s myths aren’t just eccentricities; they’re forces that shape policy, crime, and opportunity. The pack handles this with care, weaving mythic logic into the everyday fabrics of urban life.
- Noir moral ambiguity. The villains aren’t clear-cut; they’re people who believe they’re doing the right thing for the right reasons. That complexity is what makes Fortune Row a fertile ground for roleplay and debate at the table.

If you enjoy games that encourage moral nuance and stylish storytelling, Fortune Row offers more opportunities than a neon sign has blinking options.

## Final verdict and recommended play style

Fortune Row shines as a thoughtfully designed, mood-forward adventure pack that respects the core City of Mist experience while offering robust, replayable content. It’s best for:

- GMs who want a ready-to-run arc that still feels flexible and personal.
- Players who enjoy a blend of detective work, mythic drama, and character-driven scenes.
- Groups that appreciate high-concept themes presented with a noir sensibility and a splash of neon glamour.

That said, Fortune Row isn’t a turnkey solo act; it expects a GM who is comfortable improvising and guiding players through a layered, mythic city. If you crave a strong sense of place and a structure that invites both puzzle-solving and character growth, this pack is a strong fit.
