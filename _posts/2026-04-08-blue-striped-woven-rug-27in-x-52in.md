## Cleaning, Care, and Real-World Maintenance
### Quick daily care
Regular vacuuming on a low setting is the backbone of rug longevity. Avoid beater bars that can snag the weave; instead, use a gentle suction to pick up dust and debris. Spills? Attend quickly with blotting and a mild cleaner appropriate to the fiber type. Always test a small, inconspicuous area first to ensure color integrity.

### Spot treatment tips
- For most spills, blot, don’t rub, since rubbing can push the liquid deeper into the fibers.
- Use a mild solution of water and gentle detergent on synthetic blends; rinse by blotting with clean water until no residue remains.
- For stubborn stains, consult the product’s care label or reach out to the retailer for recommended cleaners that won’t strip color.

### Stains and colorfastness
Blue dyes can be surprisingly forgiving, but it’s still wise to address spills promptly. Avoid harsh bleach-based products that could dull the stripes. If you have kids or pets, consider implementing a quick-entry routine that minimizes tracked-in debris on the rug by using door mats and shoe-off zones near entrances.

### When to call in professionals
If you encounter a stubborn stain or a fraying edge, a professional cleaner or rug specialist can assess the fibers without risking further damage. In most homes, however, the Blue Striped Woven Rug holds up well to routine care and occasional refreshes with a thorough vacuuming session and a targeted spot-clean.
