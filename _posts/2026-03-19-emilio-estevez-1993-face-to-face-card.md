## Condition, Grading, and The Subtle Art of Not Pucking the Card
Condition is everything, and 1993 cards were no exception. A card that has survived a road trip in the glove compartment of a 1990s Dodge may look ancient, but if the corners are sharp, the surface is unscuffed, and the gloss hasn’t peeled away at the edges, you’ve got something that might be worth a few dollars more than a single-hop soda from a vending machine. Grading was a mystery people pretended to understand. For most of us, a careful fix of corner rounding and gentle cleaning with a microfiber cloth was enough to turn a “meh” into a “hmm, not bad.” And yet the market still loves graded cards, where a pristine PSA 10 or BGS 9.5 becomes a badge of honor you can flash at a convention and pretend you’re a serious collector rather than a guy with a cardboard problem.

## The Collector’s Checklist for This Card
- Confirm the year: 1993. If the card says 1994 or 1992 on the back, be wary; the market loves a good mismatch, but it’s not exactly rare.
