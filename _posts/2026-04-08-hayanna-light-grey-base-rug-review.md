### Stain Resistance and Cleaning

Yes, stains are a thing. Fortunately, Hayanna’s materials are chosen with everyday mishaps in mind. Spills—coffee, red wine, mysterious sauces—tend to bead on the surface and can be blotted up with minimal staining if treated promptly. For tougher messes, a warm soapy solution and a gentle blot usually does the job. Avoid harsh chemicals that could degrade the fibers or alter the color. Regular vacuuming is your best friend; this rug hides dirt well, but that only works if you actually vacuum it. Bonus: the low-to-mid pile makes vacuuming quick and relatively easy, letting you get back to whatever you were doing (likely procrastinating on a gaming session or a binge-worthy show).

### Foot Traffic and Snags

Low-pile rugs are prone to snags if you have pets that adore claws or if you drag heavy furniture around. The Hayanna’s weave is generally snag-resistant for normal use; just be mindful when moving large items across it or using the rug as a makeshift gym mat. If you have a curious feline who thinks every new texture is a potential scratch post, you might want to keep a close eye during the first few weeks to ensure no unintended purring-induced snags occur.

## Care and Maintenance

- Vacuum regularly with a standard vacuum. A gentle setting will help maintain the pile without causing excessive wear.
- Blot spills immediately. Do not rub, as rubbing can push liquids deeper and set stains.
- For spot cleaning, use a mild detergent and a soft cloth. Rinse with a damp cloth and blot dry.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color consistency across the surface.
- Consider a rug pad underneath to improve stability, reduce friction, and extend the life of the rug by preventing shifting.

If you’re the type who treats a rug like a long-term gaming companion, you’ll appreciate how low-maintenance the Hayanna can be without requiring a full-time rug-care routine. It’s not a “set-and-forget” miracle, but it is a credible, no-drama friend that blends into your life rather than shouting for attention.
