## Materials & Craftsmanship: The Wool Story

This isn't a mass-market vinyl print; it's a hand tufted wool piece. The wool material brings a confluence of durability, resilience, and a comfortable footbed. Wool fibers naturally resist wear better than many synthetics, and they compress less under heavy foot traffic. The tufting process itself is a show — a skilled artisan uses a tufting gun to push loops of wool through a primary backing, creating a dense textural surface. The result is a rug that feels substantial underfoot and maintains crisp edges where geometry matters.

### The hand tufting technique in plain geek speak

Imagine a tiny loom-powered exoskeleton that burrows into the backing and tucks wool loops into a patterned grid. That grid forms the geometric motif, while the scalloped outline is carved through careful edging and lap-stitch technique. The effect is similar to a 3D-printed map with actual depth: you look down and see a cityscape under your feet.

### Durability, pile height, and wear patterns

The pile height is typically moderate—enough to feel soft but not so tall that you risk tripping over your own design. The density is what matters: a well-packed tuft results in better bounce-back and less flattening in high-traffic zones. Expect years of use if you regularly rotate your furniture and use a quality rug pad. A pad not only reduces slippage but also extends life by distributing pressure more evenly across the wool.

For care, wool is naturally stain-resistant to an extent, but not immune to coffee spillstorms. Quick blotting is your friend; avoid rubbing, which can push the stain deeper. For stubborn marks, a mild wool-safe cleaner is advisable, along with periodic vacuuming with a soft brush attachment.

## Size, Fit, and Customization: Fit to Your Little Planet

One of the big questions is whether you can get this rug in a precise, room-defining size. The answer is yes, with some caveats: custom size options are available to tailor the rug to your floor plan. Customization typically involves selecting the width and length that suits your space—from a compact 4x6 for a reading nook to a generous 9x12 for a living room that doubles as a stage for your furniture set.

### How the custom process typically works

- Confirm edging: scallops require careful edging to maintain the silhouette at larger scales; expect slight refinements to ensure a clean finish.

### What to expect in terms of delivery and care for custom sizes

With custom orders, you may see slight differences from batch to batch due to the hand tufted nature. Plan for delivery windows and ensure you have enough space to move it into the room—these rugs are not feather-light, but they are incredibly satisfying to behold when laid down.

External considerations: if you’re dealing with a floor that’s not perfectly flat, a high-quality rug pad helps to maintain evenness and cushion. ## Practicalities: Cleaning, Maintenance, and Longevity

Wool rugs benefit from regular care. The natural fiber resists stains better than many synthetics, and the looped tufts are less prone to crush under average wear when properly maintained. Here are practical steps:

- Vacuum regularly with a low-suction setting and a brushless attachment to prevent fraying of the tufts.
- Address spills quickly: blot with a clean cloth; avoid rubbing.
- For deeper cleaning, consider a professional wool rug cleaning service every 1-2 years depending on foot traffic.
- Rotate periodically to ensure even wear and exposure to sunlight.
- Lightly brush with a soft upholstery brush to lift the nap after vacuuming, giving you that just-installed look.

Sunlight can fade colors over long periods, especially if you opt for bold palettes. Use room layouts to minimize prolonged sun exposure on the most vibrant hues.
