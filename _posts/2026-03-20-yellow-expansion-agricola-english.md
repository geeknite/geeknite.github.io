## Introduction
In Agricola the sun rarely shines, except on the occasional meadow tile. The Yellow Expansion brings a splash of color and a handful of fresh wrinkles to the farming schedule. The Yellow Expansion is designed to feel like a natural extension rather than a bloated add-on. We tested it across 8 games with two to four players and recorded our notes on laughter, slightly louder exclamations when someone harvested a field that bloomed into a solid engine, and the occasional eye-roll when the sun card forced a riskier move than we were comfortable with.

### Quick verdict
If you like Agricola but crave a more lively early game, this expansion is for you. If you hate new card sleeves or extra pieces cluttering your table, you may want to skip and use colored pencils instead.

## What’s in the Yellow Expansion
- A full yellow deck with 40 cards: 20 Occupations and 20 Improvements.
- A set of yellow tokens representing new resources and markers.
- A modular board overlay with yellow action spaces that can be added in, removed, or swapped for variety.
- A handful of yellow milestones that alter endgame scoring in a subtle but meaningful way.
- A quick start guide and a few variant rules to tailor the experience.

The art direction leans into a sunny vibe while preserving the rustic energy of Agricola. The cards are crisp, the icons are legible, and the color coding makes it easier to parse even in the heat of a game night.

### Thematic hook
The idea behind yellow is warmth as a resource, not just a mood. In practice this translates into a little extra farming momentum early on, a few safe but efficient options, and the occasional path that rewards players who can connect the dots between food production, storage, and scoring.

#### How it changes setup
- Separate yellow deck is shuffled and introduced after the third round, replacing one of the normal draws.
- The yellow board overlay is added if players agree, creating additional action spaces with yellow markers. 
- Three new yellow milestones appear later in the round when you reach certain production thresholds.

## How the Yellow Expansion integrates with base Agricola
This section delves into the rules interactions that matter in practice.

### Phase interplay
The expansion does not alter base phase lengths; instead it adds optional overlays that modify early actions. For example, two yellow actions can be used to produce a meal more efficiently than a standard action would allow, but at the cost of a small resource.

### Card synergies
Some yellow Occupations pair well with classic improvements like Fertilizer or Field Upgrades. Others create new lines of play that feel distinct enough to justify their own strategy.

### Scoring and endgame
Endgame scoring retains the base structure. The yellow milestones tilt scoring toward early efficiency, fast expansion, and careful resource conversion. If you enjoy when engineering a rapid finish yields extra points, you’ll appreciate the yellow milestones.

## Components and component quality
The expansion uses standard Agricola components: wooden tokens in yellow, simple but crisp card art, and the familiar resource trackers. The yellow color is not overwhelming, but you’ll instantly recognize yellow tokens when you lay them on the board.

### Artwork and design notes
The designers leaned into a sunny pastoral vibe that fits the Agricola setting, but with a modern twist. If you’re one of those players who complains that card art matters less than the tiny icons, you’ll still appreciate the readability of the yellow-coded cards.

## Gameplay impact
This expansion is not a revolution; it’s a glow-up. It changes pacing and risk-reward calculations without breaking Agricola’s core rhythm.

### Early game tempo
The yellow actions generally give you access to two types of advantages: extra food and additional space for activities. You might find yourself planning two moves ahead to avoid the starving omen, and the expansion rewards players who optimize resource conversion.

### Mid game engine building
As more yellow cards come online, players can chain actions into small engines. For example, Yellow Longhouse converts a typical produce action into a multi-step pipeline that yields wood, reed, and a small amount of grain in a single turn. The challenge is to manage space and family growth, because the Yellow Board imposes some extra restrictions on where you can place workers.

### Late game scoring
Yellow milestones push for aggressive improvement placement. They reward efficient use of the new yellow actions and punishing the player who hoards resources without converting them into points.

## The best strategies with Yellow Expansion
- Use early yellow actions to secure a stable food supply for the first two rounds; this helps you avoid a starving round and sets you up for a strong middle game.
- Balance your engine: don’t over-optimize a single action if it means neglecting early round resource generation.
- Use yellow cards to supplement your existing strategy rather than replacing it entirely.
- Watch for the dissonance between yellow actions and base actions. Sometimes the best move is to ignore a yellow action space in favor of a more stable base path.

### Beginner-friendly routes
If you’re new to Agricola, start with one yellow Occupation and one Yellow Improvement to test the waters. You’ll likely discover that the synergy is real but not overwhelming.

### Advanced routes
For veterans, build a minimal-yield engine around the Yellow Longhouse and Sunlit Shed. You’ll feel the satisfaction of a perfectly tight loop that squeezes every meal out of a cornfield and a pigsty.

## House rules and variants
If your gaming group likes to tinker, here are a few variants to try:
- Gentle yellow: reduce the number of yellow cards in play for a slower ramp.
- Turbo yellow: double the effects of a few select yellow cards for a high-risk, high-reward game.
- Yellow-only mode: every action board uses only yellow actions with no base actions—this is a spicy challenge for the bravest.

## How long does a game with Yellow Expansion take?
Expect roughly 15–30 minutes more per game compared to base Agricola depending on player count and how well your group handles the extra thinking. The shorter the game, the more likely the yellow deck will impact your strategy in noticeable ways. In a longer session, some players may enjoy more time to build up their yellow engine. In all cases, the expansion adds a new dynamic that gives players something to fight about at the end.

## Thematic notes and table presence
If you like your games to be visually appealing, Yellow Expansion adds a nice bright color to the board. It’s not garish; it’s sunny. The new tokens and cards give the table an extra sense of texture, and the new milestones add a tangible sense of progress that can be satisfying to players of all levels.

## Accessibility and learning curve
The expansion adds some new rules and exceptions, but the learning curve is moderate rather than steep. If your group is comfortable with base Agricola, you should be able to adapt quickly. For beginners, take it slow and allow for a few slow rounds as you parse the yellow deck’s language.

## Comparisons to other expansions
- Red vs Yellow: Red expansions emphasize action density and space control; Yellow emphasizes resource conversion and early tempo.
- Blue: Blue adds AI opponents; Yellow is a human-scale engine builder, bridging base rules with new synergy.

## A mini round-by-round playthrough (example scenario)
Round 1:
- Place a worker on a standard action to collect wood.
- Then branch into Yellow Longhouse to seed food production in a novel way.
- Watch the table respond as others chase Milestone 1.

Round 2:
- The Yellow Milestone triggers if you now have two fields and a storage capacity. You may gain extra points if you hit that criterion.

Round 3:
- A critical decision: do you use Yellow Occupation to accelerate storage and convert goods into points OR focus on a large expansion that will pay off later?

This mini run shows how the yellow deck can influence choices without completely overshadowing base mechanics.

## Final verdict
The Yellow Expansion is a bright and clever addition to Agricola. It doesn’t disrupt the core gameplay; rather, it introduces a new rhythm that rewards careful planning and adaptive play. The cards are flavorful, the components carry the theme well, and the simplified approach to triggering milestones means you can inject new energy into sessions without a steep learning curve.

If you enjoy resource management with a bit of spice and lighthearted competition, this expansion will provide a fresh lens through which to approach your farm. If you prefer the austere pace of the base game or you dislike color-coded extras, you may want to skip this one or try it in a short, casual session first.

## Final recommendations
- Best for: Players who want a brighter, more dynamic early game and enjoy engine-building.
- Good for: Casual groups seeking a quick change of pace without heavy rule gymnastics.
- Not ideal for: Groups that dislike extra components, color-coded extras, or milestone-centric endgames.
