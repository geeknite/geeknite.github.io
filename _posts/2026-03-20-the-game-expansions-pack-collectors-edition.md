## Gameplay Upgrades and Design Intent

The core design ambition of this expansion is to deepen the strategic envelope without losing the compact clarity that made the base game accessible in the first place. You get more moving parts, but the rules remain coherent and approachable. The tempo management layer adds a new dimension, turning what used to be straightforward sequences into a dynamic dance where timing and reading your opponents becomes as important as dice luck.

New modular quest cards allow you to stitch together campaigns in multiple ways, ensuring that no two sessions feel exactly the same. This is a rare accomplishment in expansions where content is often repetitive or only marginally different from the original. The designers have done a good job balancing novelty with familiarity so that new players can join in without feeling overwhelmed and veterans can savor the deeper tactical options.

### New Mechanics and How They Shift the Game

Tempo is the star here. It introduces a pressure track that pushes players to make timely decisions. If you wait too long, you start losing opportunities as the board state evolves in ways that can close off previously viable strategies. This mechanic incentivizes decisive play and promotes dynamic planning across multiple turns, not just the next move. The modular quest cards function as a campaign engine, allowing you to customize your narrative arc and tailor difficulty. This results in a more personalized experience that scales with your group and your appetite for challenge.

An important design note is that these mechanics are implemented in a way that does not invalidate existing strategies from the base game. Old-school players can still lean on their established routines while gradually incorporating new tools that unlock advanced tactics as they become familiar with the expansions program. It is a smart blend that encourages experimentation without punishing tried and true approaches.
