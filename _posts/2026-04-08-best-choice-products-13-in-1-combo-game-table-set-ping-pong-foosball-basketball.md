## Assembly Experience: A Test of Patience, Humor, and Allen Wrenches
Assembly isn’t glamorous, but it’s a rite of passage. The process tests your teamwork, your ability to decipher cryptic diagrams, and your willingness to sacrifice a few hours to the altar of “fun.”

- Step-by-step clarity: The instruction sheets attempt a cinematic, stepwise approach. In practice, you’ll refer to videos or scratch your head more than you’d expect. Expect a few iterations as you align surfaces, tighten rods, and chase the occasional loose screw that keeps rolling away like a tiny mechanical elf.
- Time investment: Plan for roughly 60–90 minutes for a first-timer with basic DIY experience; less if you have a helper who doesn’t mind holding pieces in place while you scrub the grease off your hands.
- The inevitable improvisation: You’ll find yourself improvising with household tools, using a screwdriver as a makeshift ruler, and catching yourself muttering phrases you swore you’d never say in front of guests. It’s all part of the fun.

One trick for smoother assembly: organize hardware by size before you start. Put the smaller screws in labeled cups, the medium ones in another, and reserve the big bolts for the frame. This minimizes the frantic rummaging that makes assembly feel like a scavenger hunt with a deadline.
