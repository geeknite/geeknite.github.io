## Introduction
City of Mist has always been a game about masks, myths, and the city as a living rumor. The Forgotten Stories is not a reboot; it's a sandbox expansion that asks: what happened to the legends that never quite got their spotlight? The book collects investigations that drifted into the shadows of the City of Mist era, where noir lights flicker and the old gods shuffle behind the vending machines.

## What is City of Mist: The Forgotten Stories?
### Contents
The Forgotten Stories collects seven investigations, three new Masks, a few artifacts, and a toolkit for GMs. It’s not a mere add-on; it’s a way to take your players deeper into the city’s memory palace.
### New Mechanics: The Forgotten Archives
The big addition is the Forgotten Archives, a memory-based mechanic that lets players pull fragments from the past to gain clues, flashbacks, or a temporary ability boost. The Archives sit on the edge of the core rules, not rewriting them, but enabling a different rhythm for investigations.

## Design, Art, and Components
The art direction keeps the neon-noir vibe but introduces a slightly warmer palette for memory sequences. The layout is friendly, with Investigator briefs that are easy to skim and not too dense for a single-session playthrough. The cover art shows a silhouette that could be a myth, memory, or both—a hallmark of City of Mist’s interpretive style.

### Art direction
The illustrations work as mood generators more than mechanical diagrams; they invite players to imagine the city in layers of myth and memory.

### Cards and memory fragments
The memory fragments are well-structured and fit nicely into the archival mechanic. They’re small, tactile, and a pleasure to shuffle, which is essential for any GM who loves to watch players speculate about a trace they hold in their hand.

## Mechanics Deep Dive
### Masks and logos
Three new masks bring fresh thematic ground: the Night Librarian, the Echo, and the Talisman Prosecutor. Each mask has a unique set of logos that can be mixed and matched with others for hybrid mythic flavors. If you’ve ever wanted a mask that thrives on whispers and returns, you’ll appreciate the design here.
### Memory spool mechanic details
The memory spool lets players “borrow” a memory from a past life or a recent event to gain a small, temporary advantage. It’s not infinite; there are limits for pacing, which helps avoid runaway power inflation. The system rewards players for paying attention to clues and for roleplaying the emotional resonance of memory.

## GM Guidance
### Session zero and tone setting
The Forgotten Stories shines when GMs spend a moment to calibrate tone. The book suggests tying memory motifs—loss, longing, awakening—to each investigation. It’s a good reminder that City of Mist runs on feelings as much as facts.
### Memory economy and pacing
The memory economy provides a structured, yet flexible, way to pace sessions. Don’t fear if your players get too excited about a memory fragment; the book offers safe anchors to guide them back to the main arcs without losing momentum.

## Narrative Design and Theme
### Tone and mood
The Forgotten Stories leans into memory and urban legends without tipping over into melodrama. It walks the line between noir grit and mythic whimsy, letting players feel weight without becoming overwhelmed by tragedy.
### Story seeds
- The Velvet Pigeon: a myth that manifests as a menu that rewrites memory inserted in a restaurant’s night-late hours.
- The Clockmaker’s Daughter: a time-tinker villain whose backstory is a collage of memories from multiple centuries.
- The Lantern That Fades: a memory spool that grants glimpses of a past life’s truth in dim, subway-car lighting.

## Play Experience: One-shots, Arcs, and Campaigns
One-shots benefit from the Archives by focusing on a single fragment’s arc across a city block. Short arcs can build to a multi-session wave where several investigations converge on a larger myth. For long campaigns, weave the central myth through multiple Forgotten Archival pieces so that the city itself becomes a character with a memory.

### Player experience and balance
New masks offer flavorful options without eclipsing existing cores. The artifacts scale with memory use, adding tension and temptation without unbalancing core mechanics.

## Accessibility and Inclusivity
The book continues City of Mist’s track record in accessibility: clear guidelines, varied sample characters, and inclusive content handling. It’s designed to be approachable for newcomers while still offering depth for veterans who want to mine memory-driven themes.

## Final Thoughts and Recommendation
If you enjoyed City of Mist’s core aesthetic, The Forgotten Stories will feel like coming home after a long rooftop chase. It adds a well-structured memory mechanic, a handful of memorable investigations, and enough narrative hooks to keep your table talking for months. It’s especially good for groups that like to savor investigations and explore character backstories. The balance is thoughtful, the tone is consistent, and the new tools genuinely help you tell memorable tales that linger like the faint smell of rain on a neon street.

### Who should buy this
- Groups that love noir-mythic themes and are hungry for more investigations.
- GMs who enjoy memory-driven puzzles and want a reliable toolkit to run longer arcs.
- Players who crave new masks and a sense of history for their city’s legends.

### Final rating
4.5/5 for fans of the franchise, with 4/5 for casual players who might find some of the deeper memory mechanics a little dense at first.
