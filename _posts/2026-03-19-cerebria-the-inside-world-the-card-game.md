## How to play in under two minutes
The core loop is lean, but you can still squeeze a lot of drama into a few minutes:
1) Each player draws a hand from the central deck.
2) Players take turns playing cards that influence mood, zones, or opponents.
3) Resolve card effects, chase objectives, and adjust the mood track.
4) The round ends when triggers fire; tally points or zones, and the highest mood pressure wins.

Tempo and safe decision-making are the name of the game. Bluffs exist: a seemingly harmless card can lure an opponent into overreaching, and a well-timed combo can swing a match. The game rewards good reading of the table, not just your own hand. It is not a party game built on loud chaos; it invites quiet, strategic thinking that sits nicely between a filler and a mid-weight card duel.

## Card types and what they do
Key archetypes include:
- Emotion Cards: define the mood and grant mood tokens or set up future plays.
- Action Cards: the engine room, enabling zone manipulation, disruption, or acceleration.
- Event Cards: topple the board's status quo, forcing re-evaluation of priorities.
- Objective Cards: the path to victory, often a blend of zone control and mood-synchronization thresholds.

The interplay creates a rhythm that rewards planning and timing over brute force. You feel clever when you connect two plays that seem obvious with hindsight, and you smile when a perfect draw arrives at just the right moment.
