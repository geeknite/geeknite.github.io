## Maintenance and Care

### Cleaning and surface care
Keep the surfaces clean with a soft microfiber cloth to remove dust and fingerprints. Avoid harsh chemicals on wood veneers; mild soap and water works well in a pinch. For the play surfaces, use a dedicated billiard chalk remover or a gentle cleaner that won t degrade the felt or the paint around the pockets. If you spill soda during a victorious celebration, wipe promptly to avoid stickiness that ruins cue ball movement.

### Lubrication and moving parts
The swivel hinge benefits from light lubrication every few months, especially if you use it frequently. A silicone spray or light machine oil on the pivot points will reduce squeaking and ensure smoother transitions. Remember, avoid over-lubricating as that can attract dust and grime, turning your game night into a sticky situation you will regret at 2 AM.

### Storage considerations
When the table isn t in use, you can swivel to the stored position if your space allows. Close-by wall mats or protective covers can guard against accidental knocks. If you have children who enjoy climbing on furniture, remind them that this is a gaming device, not a climbing wall. A little discipline goes a long way toward preserving the life of the table and your sanity.
