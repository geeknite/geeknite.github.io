### The Practicalities of Custom Orders

A pro tip—when you request a custom size, you’re not just asking for a longer version of the same rug. You’re requesting a rug that should maintain the same density, pile height, and color consistency across a size that could stretch the fabric beyond typical production tolerances. The best custom orders come with clear spec sheets: exact width and length, pile height, backing type, and any edge finishing preferences (fringe, rolled edges, or bound edges). Also worth noting is the potential for slightly longer lead times and a maybe-more-committed price tag. If you’re decorating on a deadline, plan accordingly and push for a provisional sample swatch if possible.

## Maintenance, Cleaning, and Care

Care is key if you want a rug to remain a beloved floor friend for years. Wool is naturally resilient and can be kept clean with regular vacuuming using a low-suction setting and a brush attachment to avoid fiber pulling. Blot spills immediately with a clean cloth—avoid rubbing, which can embed fibers further and cause pilling. For tougher spills, a mild detergent solution or a wool-specific cleaner is your friend. Test any cleaner in a small, inconspicuous area first to avoid color changes.

The big myth: wool rugs cannot be cleaned effectively. While it’s true you shouldn’t go full steam-cleaner on a wool rug (which can shrink or warp edges), properly maintained wool rugs respond well to professional cleaning services with wool-specific techniques. For periodic deep cleaning, consider professional services, especially if you’ve chosen a larger custom size. It’s not just about stain removal; it’s about maintaining pile height and overall evenness across a broader surface.

Edge finishing is another maintenance area. Some custom orders come with bound edges that resist fraying; others use rolled edges designed for a more delicate look. The practical tip: if you have pets that like to claw at edges, or kids who treat the rug like a playground, consider reinforced edges or heavier binding. And if you’re obsessive about shedding, remember that wool fibers can shed a bit initially. Regular vacuuming will reduce this and help you sleep at night knowing you aren’t turning the rug into a sofa cover clone.

## Sizing Process: How to Order Custom or 8x10

Ordering a rug isn’t a ritual, but it can feel like one. Here’s a pragmatic walkthrough to help you navigate this without losing your mind or discovering three new gray hairs you didn’t know you had.

1) Measure accurately. Use a tape measure, a notepad, and a quiet space where no one will move your furniture mid-measure. Sketch the room layout and note where you want the rug to sit in relation to sofas, tables, and architectural features.

2) Decide on a pile height and backing. The Eden Lakeside Wool rug typically offers a comfortable pile with a durable backing. If you’re placing the rug on top of wood floors, consider an anti-slip rug pad to keep things in place.

3) Choose color and pattern. If the room has dynamic color stories, pick a tone that complements rather than competes. Ask for swatches if possible to confirm your choices in the actual lighting of your home.

4) Confirm sizing and production. For a custom order, specify exact width and length, pile height, edge type, and backing. Confirm lead times and shipping windows. If your room needs to be ready by a deadline, schedule accordingly and ask for a production window that’s clearly communicated.

5) Review maintenance expectations. Some custom sizes can affect cleaning cycles or deep-clean intervals. Confirm with the retailer if you’ll need special care instructions for the larger format.
