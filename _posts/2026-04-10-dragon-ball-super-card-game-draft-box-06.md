### Build and draft strategies with box 06
Drafting with Draft Box 06 should feel like co-pasting a story from your favorite shonen anime fandom into a playable deck. You will not always land the perfect engine on the first pick, but you will usually be able to cobble together a credible game plan by the end of round two. Here are practical strategies to maximize your experience:

- Early pick priorities: prioritize cards that offer immediate tempo or board presence. If you can secure a lead on tempo in the first two rounds, your later picks can be used to fill compatibility gaps instead of trying to force a brittle engine already behind.
- Color balance and splash potential: keep an eye on color distribution and be mindful of splash options. A balanced color approach often yields better late game options than a hyper specialized one that collapses when the draft pool shifts against you.
- Synergy windows: some cards in 06 provide synergy that shines when combined in a specific turn window. If you are able to spot these windows early, you can adjust your picks to line up for the right moments and avoid awkward one off plays that stall your pace.
- Sideboard thinking: in standard DBS drafts, your sideboard choices matter. The ability to adjust your deck to answer a plan your opponent is pursuing becomes almost as important as your initial engine. Use the latter half of the draft to prune and refine your build in a way that keeps you flexible against the field.

For players who love to theorycraft, 06 offers plenty of room to experiment. You can push into a midrange engine that wins through a couple of key cards, or you can pivot toward a control style that aims to slow down aggressive starts and close out with a clean finish. The beauty of a well designed draft box is that you can play three or four rounds and still uncover new combos or angles you did not expect the first time around.

### Build ideas and practical tips
If you are planning to bring a couple of Draft Box 06 decks to a local event or a friendly tournament, here are some pragmatic build ideas you can try. These are not hard meta asserts; they are a set of reasonable frameworks you can adapt based on your pool:

- Tempo engine deck: focus on cards that help you drop early threats and maintain pressure. Use a small but efficient creature or action suite that can win in the first three turns if your curve goes cleanly.
- Hybrid midrange: aim for a balanced approach that mixes midrange threats with a few disruptive tools. The goal is to win via attrition with a couple of carefully placed power plays that blow open the game in mid to late phases.
- Control-lite: a patient approach that seeks to deny your opponent key turns, forcing mistakes and capitalizing on error windows. This is risky in a pure draft box but extremely satisfying when you land a handful of pivotal cards in the right order.

Tip for beginners: do not chase a single perfect card. Collect multiple smaller synergies that work well together. The drafting format is designed to reward such cluster thinking, and you will find your deck becoming more coherent as you draft more rounds.
