## How UNO Canvas Billie Eilish Works
The edition sticks to UNO’s core: match color or number, play action cards, and empty your hand to win. The Canvas twist sits on top of that base, offering a light collectible track that rewards players for engaging with the art cards without blowing up the game's balance.

### Core Rules, with a Billie Twist
- Standard UNO rules apply for your color/number matching.
- Action cards still matter: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, and Wild Draw Four remain the backbone of the game and can swing a round quickly.
- The eight art cards introduce optional modifiers that can influence a round’s flow without dictating victory. They’re designed to be used at the player’s discretion, not forced into every hand.
- Canvas Points: An optional scoring track that grants small bonuses for playing art cards in the right moments. It’s optional and can be turned off for a purist experience, which makes this edition friendlier to mixed groups.

This design lets you decide how deep you want to ride the art tide. If your friends are in it for style and conversation, you lean into Canvas Points; if you’re only here for the core game, you ignore them and still have a solid UNO night.

### The Collectible Subsystem
The eight art cards are the collectible hook. They’re not game-deciding powers; they’re flavor with occasional tiny boosts. Collectors have incentive to chase and trade, as some prints are rarer than others and can be displayed with pride. The guidebook offers a quick primer on each art card, including its backstory and the vibe it echoes in Billie Eilish’s catalog—think moody, rebellious, and elegantly haunted canvas imagery.

To keep things approachable, the designers opted for a “soft rarity” approach. You’re unlikely to feel a brutal chase for a single ultra-rare card, but you’ll still enjoy the moment when you pull a coveted print from a booster pack or win a round that unlocks a particular art card’s effect.

## Strategy and Tactics
Want to maximize your odds without sacrificing fun? Consider these tips:
- Treat art cards as tempo-shifters, not game-changers. Use them to nudge a round toward a favorable ending rather than to rewrite the entire endgame.
- Keep track of what art cards you’ve seen and what your opponents have played. A little memory goes a long way in predicting when someone is about to hit UNO and slip into the clutch endgame.
- Balance your hand by prioritizing the core UNO moves over chasing art cards. The art track is a garnish; don’t let it overpower your path to victory.
- Use your Wild cards strategically. In combinations with art plays, a well-timed Wild can swing a round by forcing your opponent to draw or by changing color at a critical moment.
- If you’re playing in a larger group, consider limiting art-card plays per round to keep the pace lively. Too many art-driven turns can drag the endgame and cool the tempo, especially for new players.

A practical tactic is to keep a mental list of “safe” art cards you can play without giving away your final card, which helps maintain tension as the endgame approaches.
