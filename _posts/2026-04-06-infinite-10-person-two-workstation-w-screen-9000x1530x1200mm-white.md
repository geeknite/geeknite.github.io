## Design and Build Quality

The crown jewel in this configuration is the W Screen in the middle. ## Ergonomics and Comfort
### Ten-Person Layout
A 9-meter long desk invites questions about privacy, noise management, and who gets the corner seat. The design assumes ten equal work zones along the surface with a central communication artery—the W Screen—that acts as the focal point. The result is a social workspace that encourages cross-pollination of ideas without forcing people to physically crowd onto two narrow lanes.

The chair compatibility is straightforward: any standard ergonomic chair with adjustable height and lumbar support works, though you’ll get more value if you pair this beast with chairs that offer generous seat depth and breathable fabric for long sessions. The desk itself doesn’t impose a strict seating ratio; it’s more about how you manage the space. In practice, you can fit ten adults with comfortable elbow clearance and a reasonable amount of desk depth for a keyboard, mouse, and a pile of sticky notes that will eventually morph into a post-it sculpture.

### Keyboard, Mouse Zones, and Personal Space
The top surface breathes a little in the keyboard and mouse department. You’ll likely want to arrange a few keyboard trays for those who prefer them, but the majority can rely on a flat surface with comfortable access to their primary input device. The central elevation around the W Screen creates a natural urge to keep the main display in sight, reducing the need to twist and tilt your neck constantly during a demo. If your team tends to reach across desks for shared resources, you’ll appreciate the generous surface real estate that minimizes cross-desk contention.

### Lighting and Glare Management
With a white surface this large, lighting matters. We found that an even distribution of lighting reduces glare and hot-spotting across the surface. If your space comes with bright overhead fixtures, opting for a lighting plan with diffusers or task lights at strategic angles will save eyes from fatigue during long standups. The bright white finish helps reflect ambient light, which can be a blessing or a curse depending on how the sun tracks across your floor plan. Plan for adjustable blinds and wall-mounted ambient lighting to keep your team from squinting through a critical call.

## Features and Tech
### The Screen Setup and Connectivity
The W Screen is the centerpiece, and it deserves a few words on positioning and connectivity. In a ten-person environment, you want a screen that can display a shared slide, a design mockup, or a live code demo, while still letting individuals monitor their own tasks. The provided mounting hardware supports multiple configurations, including wall-mounted and desk-mounted options. We tested both configurations and found that the desk-mounted approach keeps more of the central area free for collaboration while still delivering a robust focal point for participants.

For inputs, the center hub offers HDMI, DisplayPort, USB-C, and a dedicated data line for screen mirroring. If you’re dealing with modern laptops and a few aging display adapters, you’ll be thanking the engineers who included a sensible mix of ports and a few spare USB hubs tucked away under the surface. The result is a clean, minimal surface with the screen integrated in a way that doesn’t obstruct workflow when the central deck is used for demos.

### Cable Management and Power
A 9-meter surface equals a potential spaghetti avalanche if you don’t have a plan. The desk ships with a cable management backbone that runs along the underside, plus channeling that allows you to route power strips and data cables along the full length. It’s not a miracle solution—it cannot conjure conductive miracles out of thin air—but it does make maintenance much easier and reduces the risk of a tripping hazard from loose cords.

A word on power: we strongly recommend a centralized UPS or robust power distribution unit (PDU) for this kind of installation. In an environment with frequent brownouts or heavy streaming loads from the center screen, a little battery backup can prevent a mid-presentation black screen that morphs into a ghost story about lost productivity.

### Assembly and Maintenance
Set-up is not a casual weekend project; it’s more like preparing for a small, very organized parade. The long components arrive in multiple boxes and require a team with planning capability and a good set of tools. The manual is thorough and includes a step-by-step path to align the frame and attach the center screen mount. If you’re a solo operator, you’ll want a buddy or two to help with the heavy lifting and the fine alignment. The upside is that once assembled, the table remains rigid and stable, even when the office crew uses it for impromptu product demos that last longer than the rent on a stadium seat.

Maintenance-wise, the laminate surface is pretty forgiving. A microfiber cloth and a mild cleaner will handle coffee spills and marker ink, while the underside cable channels stay neat with periodic checks. We recommend quarterly inspections to ensure the mounting joints stay snug and the screen arms remain properly aligned.

## Real-World Use Scenarios

For software teams, the W Screen becomes a shared whiteboard and debugging console. ## Comparisons and Alternatives
Compared to traditional desks, the Infinite 10P is a leap toward communal work while preserving the ability to perform individual tasks. It trades some privacy for collaboration and scales the social energy of the office up with its long bench profile. If you want similar collaboration without sacrificing personal space, you might consider a cluster of two to three 2 to 3 meter benches with a central screen rather than one long single slab. The advantage of the Infinite is the unified aesthetic, reduced distractions from mismatched furniture, and a central focus point that’s always visible to the group.

If you’re wary of the initial scale, you can explore modular alternatives: two or three mid-size benches with screens at each end, enabling more personalized spaces while still sharing the demo surface in the middle. For some teams, this hybrid approach hits the sweet spot between collaboration and privacy without insisting on a single, giant piece of furniture.
