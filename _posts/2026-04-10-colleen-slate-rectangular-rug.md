## Cleaning and Maintenance Tips
Routine care is the best friend of your rug’s longevity. A quick vacuum every few days to prevent grit from grinding away at the fibers can help maintain the rug’s look. For spot-cleaning, use a mild solution of water and a gentle detergent, then blot—don’t rub. Rubbing can fray fibers and push stains deeper. If you have pets, a pet-specific stain remover may be helpful, but always test on a small area first. For deeper refreshes, a professional cleaning every 12-18 months can extend the life of the rug without removing all of its character. If you’re curious about deeper maintenance routines, you can explore a few guides such as [Better Homes & Gardens rug care](https://www.bhg.com/home-decor/rugs/rug-care/) for seasonal upkeep ideas and practical techniques. 

### When to Consider Replacement
Even with careful maintenance, rugs can reach a point where the fibers show signs of wear, flattening, or faded color. If you notice a noticeable loss of plushness across a large area, persistent staining that won’t respond to standard cleaners, or frayed edges, it might be time to consider replacement. Your decision to replace should factor in room aesthetics, foot traffic, and budget. Geeknite’s stance: you don’t need a rug that looks brand new forever, but you do want one that remains a source of comfort and style for several years. If you’re planning a room refresh, it could be a good moment to pair a replacement with a new furniture piece or a fresh paint color to achieve a cohesive update.
