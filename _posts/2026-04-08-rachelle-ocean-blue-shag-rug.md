## Durability and Longevity: Will It Survive My Life?

Durability in shag rugs depends on fiber type, pile height, density, and care. TheRachelle Ocean Blue is built with durability in mind. The pile height offers a luxurious feel without sacrificing practicality, and the density helps resist quick flattening under everyday traffic. The rug’s colorfastness is a point of pride: deep blues are tricky, but the dye is chosen to resist fading with routine light exposure and gentle cleaning. Real-life wear will show in the form of compressed areas near heavy furniture or the occasional flattening at the edge; the remedy is simple: a few fluffing sessions with a rug rake, or a gentle vacuum with the suction set to avoid fiber pull. In other words, it’s not a one-and-done purchase; it’s a long-term companion that benefits from some care and attention.

### Cleaning and Care: Treat It Right, Enjoy It Longer

Maintenance is a part of the rug’s value proposition. Vacuuming with the beater bar turned off and with a wide nozzle helps maintain the fluff without pulling fibers out. For spills, blot, don’t rub. A mild detergent solution following the manufacturer’s guidelines is usually enough for most common stains. If you’re dealing with pet accidents, act quickly and rinse thoroughly to prevent any lingering odors. For heavier stains, you might consider a professional cleaning at recommended intervals or per your rug’s care instructions. A protective underlay is also a smart addition that reduces wear and helps prevent slipping, especially on slick hardwood floors.

## Size and Room Planning: How Big Should Your Rug Be?

Rug sizing is a design conversation. A 6x9 or 8x10 rug works well in many living rooms, anchoring seating arrangements while leaving floor visible around the perimeter. In bedrooms, a slightly larger size under the bed extends beyond the sides, giving a plush feel to step onto upon waking. The Ocean Blue color helps soften the room’s edges, but you’ll still want a layout plan to avoid the dreaded “rug from the 90s” look of too-small rugs under large couches. If your room is unusually shaped or you’re mixing different flooring materials, consider a rug pad to ensure flatness and to prevent any sliding that could break the flow of your design.

## Buying Tips and Final Recommendations

- Check the pile height and density. A taller pile gifts more plushness but demands a touch more maintenance. A mid-range height balances comfort with practicality.
- Inspect the fiber composition. Synthetic blends deliver stain resistance and resilience; natural fibers offer warmth but require more care.
- Consider a rug pad. A padded base reduces slipping, extends lifetime, and increases underfoot comfort, especially on tile or hardwood floors.
- Test lighting in your space. The rug’s color responds to natural light and bulbs differently. If your room leans warm or cool, the blue can appear more vibrant or muted.
- Plan for cleaning. Ensure you have vacuum compatibility and a cleaning strategy for spills and pet accidents. A weekly maintenance rhythm will keep the rug looking fresh longer.
