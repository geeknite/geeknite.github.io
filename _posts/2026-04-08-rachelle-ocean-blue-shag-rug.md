## Durability and Longevity: Will It Survive My Life?

Durability in shag rugs depends on fiber type, pile height, density, and care. The Rachelle Ocean Blue is designed for resilience in real homes, not just glossy catalog shots. The pile height provides a plush, luxurious feel without tipping into maintenance overload. The dye is colorfast enough to resist fading under ordinary daylight and common household lighting. Real-life wear surfaces will show up as compressed areas near heavy furniture or occasional flattening at the edges. Remedy: a quick fluff with a rug rake or a gentle pass with a vacuum on a low setting. Stain resistance is a practical advantage, especially for households with kids and pets. The rug is not a miracle cure for every spill, but it makes cleanup less dramatic and more forgiving.

### Cleaning and Care: Treat It Right, Enjoy It Longer

Maintenance is part of the rug’s value proposition. Vacuum with the beater bar turned off and use a wide nozzle to avoid pulling fibers. Blot spills, don’t rub, and work from the outside in to prevent spreading. For stains, mild detergent or a manufacturer-approved cleaner is usually enough. If you have stubborn stains, repeat gentle blotting or consider a professional cleaning on a routine schedule. For households with pets, quick response to accidents minimizes odors and keeps the rug feeling fresh. A protective rug pad is a smart addition that reduces wear, prevents slipping on hard floors, and helps extend the rug’s life.

## Size and Room Planning: How Big Should Your Rug Be?

Rug sizing is a design conversation. A 6x9 or 8x10 rug anchors many living rooms, spaces between seating, while still leaving a comfortable border around the perimeter. In bedrooms, a larger rug tucked under the bed frame can extend beyond the sides to give a plush step onto waking feet. The Ocean Blue color helps soften room edges, but you should still draft a layout to avoid a rug that looks like it’s trying to be a wall-to-wall carpet on a budget. If your room is unusually shaped or you’re mixing different flooring materials, consider a rug pad to ensure flatness and prevent sliding that disrupts the room’s rhythm.

## Buying Tips and Final Recommendations

- Check the pile height and density. A taller pile yields more plushness but demands a more rigorous care routine. A mid-range height tends to balance comfort and practicality.
- Inspect the fiber composition. Synthetic blends offer easier maintenance and stain resistance; natural fibers add warmth but require more care.
- Consider a rug pad. A padded base reduces slipping, extends the rug’s life, and increases underfoot comfort, especially on slick floors.
- Test lighting in your space. The rug’s color shifts with natural light and artificial bulbs; if your room trends warm or cool, the blue can read differently throughout the day.
- Plan for cleaning. Ensure you have a compatible vacuum and a cleaning routine for spills and pet accidents. A weekly cadence helps the rug maintain its loft and color.
