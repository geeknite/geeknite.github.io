## Quick Start Guide: How to Play at a Party
If you’ve played Exploding Kittens before, you’ll feel right at home. If not, fear not—this is one of those games that teaches you the basics in minutes and then becomes a fun, social experience. Here’s a quick-start outline designed for a party setting, with minor party-specific tweaks:

1) Set up the deck and tokens as per the instruction sheet. Make sure the party space is clearly delineated so people can reach cards without shouting across the room.
2) Decide on a party theme for the evening (birthday cake, candy party, sci-fi birthday, etc.). Use the included themed art as a visual cue for the players.
3) Each player draws a hand of cards. The rest of the deck sits face-down as the draw pile. The timer (if you’re using one) is ready to go and the birthday cake is within sight for motivation.
4) On your turn, you draw a card and may play a card from your hand according to the rules. The goal remains to avoid drawing an exploding kitten card unless you’ve managed to defuse it with a defuse card or party-specific twist.
5) The party-specific twists kick in at designated moments (these are printed on a subset of the cards). They can include things like “Birthday Burst,” which forces everyone to show a card, or “Make a Wish,” which allows you to swap cards with someone else.
6) The round ends when the last player dodges an exploding kitten or when time runs out (if you’re using the timer). The winner claims the glory and perhaps a slice of cake if the host is feeling generous.

If you want to add a little extra zing, consider introducing a party host rule where the winner can select a quick bonus reward—an extra slice of cake, a recommended party game for the next round, or the power to impose a silly penalty on a rival (like wearing a party hat that makes them speak in rhyme for a turn).

## Tips for a Hilarious Party Night
- Build a pre-game ritual: let the birthday person pick a theme song that plays as you turn over the first card. The energy instantly elevates.
- Use a “cake break” rule: every time a particular card is played, everyone takes a one-bite cake break before continuing. It creates a shared, edible moment that keeps hands from going too twitchy with cards.
- Enforce a friendly banter policy: jokes are welcome, but ganging up on a player should be done in jest. The social tone matters almost as much as the cards themselves.
- Add a quick “award” segment: the winner of each round gets a silly, predetermined prize, like a crown made from party hats or a goofy trophy that doubles as a snack holder.
- Have a backup game ready: not every group wants to end the night on a chaotic explosion. Pair Exploding Kittens with a lighter party game for the wind-down portion of the evening.
