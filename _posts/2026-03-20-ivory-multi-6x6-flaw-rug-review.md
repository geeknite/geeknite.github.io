## Care and Maintenance

### Cleaning basics
- Regular vacuuming: Use a vacuum with adjustable height and a wide nozzle to avoid snagging the fibers. Vacuum from multiple angles to prevent a single path of wear.
- Spot treatment: For spills, blot rather than rub. A mild detergent or rug-cleaning solution designed for synthetic fibers usually does the trick. Always test in an inconspicuous area first.
- Odor control: If your space gains a dog’s-day-in-the-park scent after a week of traffic, sprinkle baking soda lightly and vacuum after 15–20 minutes. Your nose will thank you.

### Deep-clean considerations
If you live in a high-traffic home or have kids who treat your floor like a lab bench, you might want to schedule a professional clean every 12–18 months, depending on use. A professional cleaning helps refresh the pile, reduce embedded soil, and return some of the rug’s original bounce. Avoid steam cleaning if possible unless the manufacturer’s guidelines explicitly allow it; heat can affect color and backing over time.

### Quick maintenance tips for the occasional clutter-creator
- Rotate the rug every few months if you notice uneven wear in a particular zone. It can help extend the apparent life of the fibers.
- Consider a door mat in high-traffic entry zones to minimize dirt being tracked onto the rug.
- Use furniture coasters under heavy items to prevent indentations and keep the surface even over time.
