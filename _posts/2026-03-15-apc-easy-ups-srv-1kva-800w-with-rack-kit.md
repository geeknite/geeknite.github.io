## Recommendations and Notes for Buyers
- Assess your load: List all devices you intend to connect and total their wattage. If you’re near or over 800 W, consider adding another UPS or a higher-capacity unit to avoid battery run-time anxiety.
- Factor in battery health: Like any battery, performance declines with age and temperature. If you’re buying used or from a dusty cabinet, run a battery test and be prepared for replacement timelines.
- Rack space planning: Confirm your rack depth and rail compatibility. Some racks require deeper shelves; others are perfectly content with short profiles. Plan the cabling so you don’t dislodge your rails during maintenance.
- Software setup: Install PowerChute or your preferred monitoring tool to maximize the automation benefits. Set alerts for battery health, event logs, and load so you don’t wake up to an unplanned power-down.
- Future-proofing: If you anticipate rapid gear expansion, map out a scalable strategy. A single 1kVA UPS is great for a lean setup, but you might want a modular approach or a higher-capacity unit once you cross a certain threshold.
