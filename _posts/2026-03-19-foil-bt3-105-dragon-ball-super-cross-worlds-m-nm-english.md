## Foil quality and handling: how to treat this beauty

Foil cards beg for the right treatment. Here is a practical guide to handling BT3-105 foil without turning it into a coffee-stained souvenir:

- Sleeving: Use a high-clarity sleeve that does not yellow with age. Matte sleeves reduce glare and can help you manage the reflection if you play in bright lighting. Some players prefer double sleeving for protection during travel and tournaments.
- Toploaders/binders: If you travel to events, consider a rigid card holder or a dedicated foil-friendly binder page to prevent edge wear in your binder. Foil edges are more prone to micro-tears if bent aggressively.
- Handling: Clean hands only. Foil cards love fingerprints as much as a cat loves tuna. Use sleeve clean wipes and avoid touching the foil surface directly whenever possible.
- Environment: Avoid extreme heat and humidity; foil cards can become tacky or warp with poor storage. A cool, dry, dark place is your friend. The glitter of the foil tends to love a stable climate more than drama-loving players do.
- Cleaning: If your BT3-105 foil gains a smudge or fingerprint, a microfiber cloth with a tiny amount of water is enough for minor surface cleaning. Do not rub too hard; you want to avoid micro-scratches or foil pitting, which can ruin the shine and your mood.

In other words, treat it well, and it will look like a gleaming beacon in your binder, a trophy that says you both opened the set and survived the build phase without a defeat montage.

## Storage tips for long-term happiness

- Protect the foil with a top-loader for travel and tournaments. This keeps the card straight and safe from knocks. 
- Use a binder page specifically designed for foils or for Ultra Rare cards. Foils love to flirt with binder edges, so give them a gentle space.
- Keep away from direct sunlight. Foils can fade under UV exposure, which would be a tragedy for your investment and your mood.
- Rotate display periodically. If you have multiple foils, rotating which one is facing outward is a nice way to prevent any single card from chlorophylling or showing wear due to constant exposure to bright lights.
- Avoid humid environments. Foils can react to humidity, and you may see a slight warp or edge lifting if the card sits in a damp binder for too long.

With these steps, your BT3-105 foil will stay in mint condition longer, letting you enjoy its glow during every game night and every casual chat that turns into a mini art critique.
