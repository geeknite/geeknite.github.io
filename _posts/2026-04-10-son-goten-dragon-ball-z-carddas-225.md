### Authenticity, fakes, and how to spot them
Fakes exist in almost every collector space, and Carddas is no exception. With a card that has no shortage of desirability, counterfeiters may attempt to imitate the foil sheen, the card stock, or the exact color balance that the Very Rare tag implies. Here are a few practical tips to reduce risk:

- Compare against trusted references: Look for known print-run details from reputable collectors’ databases. If a listing lacks clear photos of the back (which often carries a card series stamp and a small identifier), treat with caution.
- Check the foil quality: Fake foils often have a slightly different hue or a flatter texture. Real No. 225 prints tend to gauge a consistent foil brilliance when rotated under light.
- Inspect edges and corners: Fakes often use inferior stock or miscut dimensions. The edges should be crisp, corners sharp, and the card should lie flat without curvature.
- Feel the card: Run a gentle finger along the surface. If it feels suspiciously slick or oddly rough, you may be looking at a counterfeit.

If authenticity is a concern, consider professional grading or purchase from reputable dealers who offer return policies and provenance. It’s not cheating to ask questions; it’s smart hobbying.
