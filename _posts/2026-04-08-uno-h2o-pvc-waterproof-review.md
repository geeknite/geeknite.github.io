## How UNO H2O differs from standard UNO
If you know UNO, you know the basics. You match colors or numbers, use special action cards, draw cards, and shout UNO when you’re down to one card. UNO H2O preserves that rhythm but adds a water-resistant twist. The main differences you’ll notice:
- Card feel: The PVC coating changes the tactile feedback. It’s still flexible, but it’s a touch slick, which can be both helpful (easy to flip) and frustrating (slippery when wet).
- Durability: The coating protects against light splashes and damp conditions, but don’t pretend you can clean the deck with a garden hose and call it a day. Spiking a card into the pool is still a bad idea.
- A few new rule nudges: Some variants emphasize safety around the pool—no leaning over the water with a handful of slippery cards; keep both feet on dry ground when you’re dealing. The rule sheet covers these, and it’s wise to read before you start the chaos.

In short, UNO H2O preserves the soul of UNO while embracing a pool party aesthetic. It’s not a dramatic departure, but it’s a meaningful one for environments where water is more than a villain; it’s a feature.

## Maintenance and care
- Wipe down after play: A quick wipe with a damp cloth helps preserve the coating and keeps the cards feeling fresh for the next game night.
- Store dry: While the coating resists water, it’s best to keep the deck in a dry place when not in use. Avoid leaving it in direct sun for long periods, which can lead to color fade and awkward looks from your neighbors.
- Inspect for wear: If a card starts peeling, replace it through a replacement pack if available. Don’t try to glue it back—this isn’t a science project; it’s poolside chaos.
- Cleaning: Mild soap and water will do. Avoid harsh solvents that may degrade the PVC coating or the card edges.

Maintenance is not glamorous, but it’s essential if you want your UNO H2O to endure a few seasons of splashes and spontaneous lifeguard cosplay.
