Maintenance and Longevity

A good sleeve requires a little care. Here are practical tips to extend the life of your Son Gohan sleeves:

- Keep them dry and away from heat. Polypropylene and PET-based sleeves are resilient, but prolonged exposure to heat can cause warping or faster edge wear.
- Store sleeves in a cool, dark place. This isn’t just a nostalgia tip; it helps preserve the color fidelity and prevents edge whitening.
- Use a deck box or sleeve organizer to minimize friction when moving sleeves in and out of packs. The goal is to reduce rubbing against hard surfaces, which is a sneaker’s arch-nemesis and your cards’ arch-nemesis.
- Consider “maintenance shuffles.” If you notice any stubborn corners that resist sliding, a gentle, single-pass shuffle can help distribute wear evenly and prevent localized thinning.

For the collector who wants to keep things pristine, you can also pair these sleeves with a protective deck sleeve case. The combination offers a two-tier approach: outer protection for the whole deck and inner protection for the individual cards. It’s like wearing a raincoat over a suit—practical, stylish, and a little ridiculous in the best possible way.
