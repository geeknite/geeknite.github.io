## What is Skip-Bo?
Skip-Bo is a competitive, low-science, high-fun card game that sits somewhere between a math puzzle and a friendly brawl over who gets to deplete their draw pile fastest. The core idea is simple: you are trying to build four ascending piles on the board, from 1 to 12, using cards from your own stock pile and from a shared draw pile. The wild card, appropriately named Skip-Bo, can stand in for any number. When you play a card, you reveal the next one on your stock pile. The first player to use up their stock pile wins the round.

### Components
- A deck of Skip-Bo cards with colored numbered cards plus wild Skip-Bo cards
- Four construction piles on the table for everyone to build up in order
- A stock pile for each player with face-down cards and a face-up top card
- A draw pile that feeds players cards as the action unfolds

The joy of this setup is that you have a tangible, tactile system that rewards planning and improvisation in the same breath. The cards feel good in your hand; the stock piles look like tiny, chaotic forts; and the wild cards are the perfect chaos agents that make you rethink your strategy mid-shuffle.

### How to play (the quick-and-dirty version)
1. Each player has a stock pile of face-down cards and one face-up card on top. Draw from the draw pile to replenish your hand as needed.
2. On your turn, you try to play cards from your hand onto the four construction piles in ascending order from 1 to 12. If you don’t have a card that fits, you draw until you can play or until your hand is empty.
3. Wild Skip-Bo cards can be used as any number to help you complete a run. If you drop a Skip-Bo card into the construction piles, you must declare what number it represents for that run.
4. The first player to empty their stock pile wins the round. Play again if you’re into marathon night mode.

A lot happens at once, and that is the point. The game thrives on the friction between planning and accident. You think you have the perfect move, and then someone flips a wild into your plan and you gasp at the comedic chaos of it all.

## Strategy tips for the aspiring table DJ
Skip-Bo rewards micro-decisions as if the cards themselves whispered, hey, maybe try this. Here are some practical tips you can actually use when the card gods align against you:
- Know when to play aggressively and when to hold back. If your four construction piles are nearly full and you’re staring down a pile of dead weight, consider nudging a wild card into service to unlock a long-awaited 1 or 2 run.
- Keep an eye on your stock pile order. If you see a 5 on top, but you’re staring at a mountain of 1s and 2s in your hand, you might want to play those to set up a future turn rather than forcing a suboptimal 5 into the mix.
- Use wild Skip-Bo cards strategically. Wilds are your emergency power. Don’t waste them on small gaps; save them for critical runs that let you dump multiple cards in one turn.
- Card memory practice: as you play, try to mentally log what draws are likely to appear next. This is the “table poker” aspect of the game. It may not save you from chaos, but it will make your decisions feel more intentional.
- Adapting to group size. With more players, the pace accelerates and the chaos multiplies. With fewer players, you can craft longer, more deliberate sequences. Adjust your tempo accordingly.

In short, strategy in Skip-Bo is less about calculating exact probabilities and more about reading the room, managing your own deck, and making the most of wild cards when the moment feels right.

### Variants and house rules
Every table offers its own flavor of Skip-Bo. Common house rules include:
