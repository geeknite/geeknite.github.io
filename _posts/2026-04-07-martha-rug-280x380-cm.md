## Durability, maintenance, and cleaning
### How well does it hold up under real-life chaos?
Let’s be honest: life happens on rugs. Spills, pet trails, and the occasional heroic food experiment can leave their mark. Martha is engineered to resist staining and to be resilient enough to handle everyday traffic. Vacuuming works well with a standard household vacuum. For deeper cleaning, you’ll want to treat stains promptly. The general rule is: blot, don’t rub, and avoid harsh chemical solvents that could degrade the pile over time. If you have kids or a pet, you’ll appreciate the way Martha hides small disasters without turning them into permanent battle scars.

### Cleaning tips and tricks
- Blot the spill immediately with a clean cloth. Work from the outside in to prevent spreading.
- For dried stains, a mild detergent solution or a carpet cleaner suitable for synthetic fibers works well. Test a small area first.
- Vacuum in multiple directions to lift the pile and keep it looking even.
- Rotate the rug occasionally to distribute wear, especially in high-traffic zones.

### Pet-friendly considerations
For households with a cat or a dog, Martha offers a forgiving surface where claws won’t instantly flatten the texture. Regular brushing of shedding fur helps; a weekly vacuum tends to keep the look fresh. If you’re dealing with persistent pet odors, consider a mild deodorizing product designed for synthetic fibers and always follow the manufacturer’s guidelines.
