## What is Gloom: Unhappy Homes?
Unhappy Homes is the expansion that leans into the haunted domestic sphere with a wink and a moan. It introduces new components designed to tilt the emotional gravity of each scene: additional family members, house-specific condition cards, and a handful of new gloom tokens that don’t merely exist to complicate your life but to remind you that home is where the heartbreak is. The premise remains the same in spirit: you arrange a cascade of sorrow cards so that your family’s life-story ends in spectacular tragedy—preferably with a punchline that makes the other players question their life choices.

But the expansion isn’t just more cards. It adds modular pieces you can mix and match depending on how mean you feel about your friends’ families that evening. The intention is not just to increase difficulty, but to deliver the punishing cunning of a well-timed cumulative gloom effect. If the base game is a slow-burning candle, Unhappy Homes is a hurricane candle—shaped like a house, and it still rings true: things are going to get dark, but at least you’ll be entertained while the walls weep.

## New Mechanics and How They Change the Tempo
One of the joys of a Gloom expansion is how it can nudge the tempo without turning the game into a complicated engine of math. Unhappy Homes achieves this by layering in two or three fairly intuitive mechanics that feel fresh but not bloated.

### House-Linked Conditions
The expansion makes house cards more than a backdrop. They now interact with the family cards in ways that force you to think about positioning and timing. You won’t simply play a gloom card and wait for fate to smile. Instead, you’ll need to manage where families live, what rooms trigger, and how a single badly-timed mood card can cascade into a domino of misfortune across the entire home.

This adds a little more attack-and-response flair to the game. It’s like adding a spice rack to a roast—suddenly every bite has a hint of something dangerous and delicious. It also means you can craft cunning strategies around a particularly unlucky upstairs or a cursed study, which is exactly the kind of spatial storytelling that Gloom fans adore.

### Expanded Mood and Event Card Deck
Unhappy Homes introduces more mood and event cards, with a sharper focus on domestic calamities. You’ll see combinations that reward careful sequencing and punishing misreads. The result is a game that can feel faster and more chaotic in the middle rounds, which is perfect for players who enjoy the adrenaline of a ticking clock crew.

If you’ve played with a few expansions before, you’ll notice a density in these cards that nudges you toward more dramatic reveals. The sheer volume of doom in the deck means you’ll rarely have the same two games in a row. That’s a win for variety, and a mild warning for players who hate repeating the same punchlines.

### Breakable Narratives and Narrative Hooks
A good Gloom expansion adds narrative hooks you can lean into during the setup. Unhappy Homes does this with shorthand story prompts that help players improvise a family sagas with a shared sense of pace. It’s not a strict storytelling game, but the additions invite you to riff and exaggerate. In practice, this means the table is more likely to be roaring with laughter (and lament) as you weave the performances needed to squeeze out every last drop of gloom from the cards.

### Optional Residents and House Variants
To keep things fresh, Unhappy Homes quietly encourages you to improvise with alternate resident cards—the sort that turn a routine tragedy into a character-driven mini-series. And because no one wants a one-trick pony, there are house-variant modes that let you mix in a cursed wing, a haunted attic, or a moody basement that insists on narrating your actions with sarcastic captions. It’s not mandatory, but it sure makes your table feel like a tiny television writer’s room where every room has its own star.

## Strategy Tips and Tactics
Here are a few practical ideas to help you maximize your gloom power without turning your living room into a haunted asylum:

- Prioritize house cards early. The sooner you align a family’s misfortunes with a house trigger, the more coherent your narrative will feel across the game. This helps you stage a big turn later where multiple gloom checks line up in a stunning, disastrous crescendo.
- Manage tempo with mood cards. The new event cards reward careful timing. Don’t reveal a devastating mood card on a round where your opponents have just drawn a perfect sympathy card. The meta game is real, and it often hides behind a smile and a teardrop.
- Syndicate with caution. If you’re playing with others who are quick to grab the most lucrative doom, you can end up with a table that looks like a suburban cathedral to misfortune. That’s fine, but keep it friendly and fun. Part of the charm of Gloom is watching the chaos unfold without crossing the line into real-world family drama.
- Leverage synergy with other expansions. If you know your group loves a particular card type from a previous module, plan a setup that emphasizes those cards. The result is a more satisfying arc for your session, with a payoff that feels earned rather than forced.
