## What’s in the box

The Special Character Expansion is, in true Zombicide fashion, compact but punchy. You’re not getting a sprawling new campaign system; you’re getting a character-driven boost to your standard sessions. Here’s the lay of the land:

- 6 uniquely “special character” survivors, each with a signature ability and a starting equipment kit. These aren’t just color swaps; they introduce new rhythms for your missions.
- 6 dedicated ability cards that govern how each survivor’s specialty triggers during a turn. These aren’t one-trick ponies: the design aims to reward tactical play and memory, rather than brute force.
- A set of tokens and markers that integrate with the base game’s economy of weapons, equipment, and zombies. The new tokens feel familiar enough to slot into your existing boards, yet distinctive enough to matter in clutch moments.
- A compact booklet that outlines the character-specific rules, recommended team compositions, and a few optional house rules for those who want to lean into “boss-level chaos.”
- A few artful, thematic components that echo Black Plague’s grim-but-silly vibe. Nothing revolutionizes the art direction, but it scratches that itch we all have for more flavor in the apocalypse world.

The contents feel premium enough to justify a standalone purchase, yet lean enough that you won’t feel like you’re buying a small expansion for a larger game library. The quality of the cards and tokens matches the core set’s production standards, which is a nice consistency bump for players who care about tactile feedback as much as board state.

### New characters at a glance
The six survivors each occupy a familiar archetype: brains, brawn, magic-lightning, speed, support, and sabotage. But the fun comes from how their abilities interact with standard Zombicide mechanics—think token management, movement economy, and the occasional “oh no you didn’t” moment when a character’s special action lines up with a perfect turn order.

- Archivist: A memory-themed support character that can swap or copy actions under specific conditions. Victory often relies on planning several moves ahead without losing your cool. Yes, this is the player who always asks, “What if I chain this?” and then actually pulls off something you hadn’t considered.
- Trickster: A rogue-like character whose power grows with risk. The Trickster can leverage misthrows and rerolls to pivot a mission in a dramatically unexpected direction. Also the one most likely to startle the neighbors with celebratory giggles.
- Pyro (name may vary by print run): A risk-taker who uses heat-based equipment to alter the zombie density on board, introducing the occasional controlled burn in a world that’s already burning. If you like tempo swings, Pyro is your spice rack.
- Healer: The “this mission won’t be a total loss” character. They keep teammates alive longer by healing with flavor and a touch of strategy that matters when the table gets crowded with both undead and adrenaline.
- Knight: The armored frontline presence that can soak up zombie traffic and hold a choke point. Knights aren’t simply “tank-y”; their presence buys space and time for other characters to weave clever plays.
- Beast: The ferocious close-quarters brawler that thrives in tight corridors where brute force has room to sing. Expect some satisfying moments where a flood of zombies is repelled by a single, thunderous swing.

Across the six characters, the design team leans into the idea that special characters aren’t just “more hit points” but “more story in the moment.” Each character’s ability nudges you toward different mission goals: rescue, control, board-clear, and objective-first play all get new lenses to view their problems through.

## How it integrates with the base game

### Setup and balance
If you’re already comfortable with Zombicide: Black Plague’s core systems, the expansion slides into your setup with almost uncanny ease. The game scales naturally because the new characters share the same baseline attributes—movement patterns, combat dice, and zombie behavior—while adding a new decision layer via the character cards. The rulebook suggests a few balancing adjustments when mixing the core set with expansions; in practice, these feel optional but helpful if you want to ratchet up the chaos without derailing your campaign’s progression.

### Turn structure and tempo
The expansion doesn’t redefine how a turn works; instead, it redraws where you want to spend your actions. The Archivist, Trickster, and Knight, in particular, encourage you to think several steps ahead. You’ll often find yourself staging two-turn sequences: one to position for a high-impact play, another to capitalize on the opportunity when the dice finally line up. The Pyro and Healer contribute to tempo shifts in the opposite direction: Pyro creates flashbang moments—moments of “we’re not sure if it’s brilliant or reckless”—while Healer keeps the table in the game long enough to enjoy those big swings.

For veteran players, the expansion is a welcome palate cleanser. It gives you new levers without forcing you into a new set of rules for every game. For newcomers, the six-character roster can feel a little intimidating at first; the booklets are concise and the actions are clearly labeled, but you’ll want to take a few sessions to internalize each character’s pace.

## The six characters — a closer look

### The Archivist
The Archivist is about memory and clever misdirection. On a successful turn, you can recall a previously spent ability and reuse it in a new context, effectively letting you chain plan-ahead plays. The social dynamic around the Archivist is interesting: your team starts to value information as a resource, almost as much as weapons. It rewards coordination without making everyone feel like they need a PhD in calculus to survive.

### The Trickster
If you like risky plays, the Trickster is your meta-narrative solo. Their special action often triggers benefits when you “gamble” with a risk, like re-rolling a failed combat with a cost staggered across future turns. This character is the heartbeat of adrenaline-pumping turns, where everything could be glorious or hilariously catastrophic—sometimes both.

### The Pyro
The Pyro isn’t just about burning things; it’s about converting heat into strategic pressure. This character can modify zombie density or force a zombie spawn window to slip into a half-open door—giving you a moment to set up a dramatic barricade or a multi-zombie trap. It’s bold, dynamic play, and when it clicks, it’s cinematic in a “good-fantasy-mhaster” sort of way.

### The Healer
The Healer keeps the table in the game longer. They’re not a pure support pick; they bring meaningful survivability to frontline teams, especially when combined with Knight or Trickster’s more aggressive lines. It’s satisfying to save a team member’s life just as the last zombie-boss-thing is about to bite their last breath.

### The Knight
The Knight is your classic shield wall, but with some tasty twists. The character can draw and hold attention from multiple zombies, buying space for teammates to execute their bigger plays. Knights often become the linchpin of a solid plan, and you’ll start to notice that your “go-to” tactic isn’t always the same—sometimes you need to embrace a slower, heavier pace.

### The Beast
Beast is the close-combat bruiser who thrives in cramped spaces. When the board presents a tight corridor, Beast can unleash a sequence that converts a dozen clumsy stomps into a single, decisive moment. It’s satisfying to see a group of hissing normals fall to a well-timed surge, and this character helps maintain that satisfying “aha!” feeling many Zombicide fans chase.

## Why this expansion lands (and where it trips)

- Pros:
  - Adds meaningful, asymmetrical choices without tossing out your existing strategy.
  - Encourages team coordination and multi-turn planning in a low-friction way.
  - The six characters cover a broad spectrum of playstyles, ensuring something for everyone.
  - High replayability thanks to different character combos and mission objectives.
- Cons:
  - New players might feel overwhelmed by option density at first; a quick primer session helps.
  - The price-to-content ratio isn’t revolutionary, but the value is in the experience rather than raw numbers.
  - Some players may prefer to stay strictly within the core set’s constraints for a purist experience.

Overall, the expansion is a welcome addition for groups who enjoy narrative flavor mixed with tactical depth. It doesn’t “fix” the core game but absolutely enriches its high-variance, high-joy moments.
