## Core Mechanics in BT01 (A Quick Overview)

Dragon Ball Super Card Game has a few core ideas to get used to: energy management, attacking, block and defense, and a bit of trickery via your deck’s unique interactions. The BT01 Starter Deck emphasizes a straightforward approach to these ideas. Here are the basics you’ll notice as you play:

- Energy turns: On your turn, you play a card by paying its energy cost from your energy zone. The flow is similar to tapping a resource in other games, but with a Dragon Ball twist. You want to maximize your energy by planning turns ahead, aiming to deploy a bigger threat when you have enough energy banked.
- Leader abilities: Your Leader provides a powerful effect that can influence your entire match. The BT01 deck centers around a leader that enables a strong tempo swing in the early game, but the real payoff comes a couple of turns in as you unlock the synergy in your deck.
- Attack chains: You’ll line up a series of attacks to pressure your opponent’s life total. The deck uses a rhythm of cheap moves and a few midrange threats that push for a decisive moment, ideally on turns where your opponent is trying to stabilize.
- Energy management: A core part of the game is planning when to commit energy and when to hold it back for a later, bigger play. The BT01 deck helps a new player feel this tension without becoming overwhelmed by micro-optimizations.
- Triggers and effects: The deck includes cards with triggers that add a little unpredictability to each play. This is where the game's anime energy becomes especially satisfying as a combo chain unfolds and you find yourself staring at a board that suddenly looks far more dangerous.

## A Closer Look at Card Roles in BT01

A starter deck cannot be a collection of random cards; it needs a coherent strategy. The BT01 set is designed to give you a central theme and a plan that you can execute from turn one. Here are some typical card roles you will see in BT01 and how they contribute to the deck’s power curve:

- Lead attacker cards: These are your main punches, cards with enough power to threaten your opponent while leaving room for follow-up plays.
- Support and synergy cards: These provide buffs, draw, or minor reductions to energy costs, helping you stretch your plan across a few turns rather than giving you a single burst of power.
- Defensive pieces: Early game survivability often decides the match, and BT01 contains cards that help you survive those first couple of rounds while you assemble your combo.
- Utility cards: These are the little pieces that make your overall plan possible, like search effects or small accelerators that help you fetch your core pieces from the deck.

Understanding these roles is key to using BT01 effectively. As you get more comfortable, you’ll start recognizing when to pivot from a straight offensive plan to a more defensive approach depending on your opponent’s board state.
