## Practical ownership: how to store, display, and enjoy it

### Display considerations
A high-end relic deserves a display that respects its presence. A proper top-loader or screw-down case, a soft microfiber liner, and a stable shelf away from direct sunlight are good starting points. The patch’s color spectrum is best enjoyed under soft, indirect lighting that reduces glare while highlighting the metallic accents. If you have a gallery wall, consider a dedicated sports relic frame with a lighted back panel to give the patch an extra glow at night.

### Handling and protection
While you can flex the card for a quick celebratory pose, avoid overhandling. Oils and fingerprints can show on the card face and foil accents. Wash your hands, handle with clean gloves if you’re going full curator, and store in a climate-stable environment. If you’ve ever watched a streaming show about vintage vinyl collecting, you know the importance of temperature and humidity control — relic cards deserve the same respect.

### Cleaning and care tips
For the display case, a gentle wipe with a microfiber cloth should suffice. If you encounter stubborn smudges, a minimal amount of water on the cloth is safer than rubbing with solvents. A quick check of the patch window for any loose clear protective film is a good habit. The goal is to keep the patch protected without altering the surface or the look and feel of the card.
