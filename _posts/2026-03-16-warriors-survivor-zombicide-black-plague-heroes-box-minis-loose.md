## Cleaning, priming, and painting tips
If you are reading this, you probably want to paint these five into something memorable. Here are practical tips to get you from gray plastic to a squad you are proud to display in victory photos:

- Clean first, obsess later: A quick soak in soap and warm water can banish residual sprue bits and mold release. A gentle scrub with a soft brush helps reveal hidden details. Do not scrub so hard that you reveal the model’s inner monologue.
- Remove flash carefully: A hobby knife or fine-file works wonders. The goal is to create clean lines without shaving off essential armor edges. Patience is a virtue here, especially if you care about shield textures and belt buckles.
- Prime with purpose: A good primer helps paint pop. A mid-gray primer is a safe bet; it gives you contrast for shadows and makes metallics less prone to looking flat. If you prefer black primer, you’ll want brighter colors for highlights to prevent your minis from turning into charcoal silhouettes.
- Paint for readability: Choose a limited palette first. A cohesive color scheme helps the squad feel like a unit rather than a patchwork of random hues. Simple shading, with a touch of edge highlighting on armor edges, can do wonders.
- Seal for longevity: A matte sealant will protect your hard work from finger oils, accidental coffee spills, or the occasional zombie sneeze. Yes, this is a real hazard during late-night gaming sessions.

For hobbyists who want a quick win, you can also experiment with metallics on armor and then use a wash to bring out creases. The five Warriors provide a manageable canvas for a painting night that doesn’t spiral into a multi-day quest of “just one more mini.”
