## Durability, Care, and Maintenance: Rug Life Is a Marathon, Not a Sprint

Durability is not sexy on the surface, but it’s what makes a rug worth owning beyond the initial thrill of purchase. Wool, by its nature, resists wear better than most synthetic alternatives, maintaining loft after years of use. The Sensory Golden Hour Wool Rug is designed with that longevity in mind, offering a dense pile that remains comfortable underfoot while withstanding daily foot traffic, chair wheels, spills, and the occasional cookie crumb disaster.

Care instructions are simple but important:
- Vacuum regularly with a high-quality vacuum that has an adjustable height setting to avoid snagging fibers.
- Rotate the rug every few months to ensure even wear and color fade if there’s direct sun exposure. This prevents the rug from developing a “sunny side” and a “not-sunny side.”
- Use a non-slip underlay if your floor is slippery or if you have a childless-but-active household where blankets become playgrounds for sneakers.
- Treat spills promptly with blotting rather than rubbing to avoid fiber matting. Mild soap with water is usually sufficient for most household mishaps, followed by thorough drying.
