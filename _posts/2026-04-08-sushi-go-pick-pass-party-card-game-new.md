## How to Play: The Pick & Pass Mechanic in Action
The heartbeat of Sushi Go! Party is the pick & pass loop. You look at your current hand, pick one card you want in your future victory banquet, and pass the rest to your neighbor. The twist in party form is how these passes line up with more players and sometimes extra scoring opportunities.

### Round structure
- Each round lasts just a few minutes, which makes it perfect for party games where attention spans may waver after the 400th snack joke.
- After everyone picks a card, you reveal what you took and immediately apply any scoring effects that card triggers for that moment.
- The passed cards move, and you repeat the process until all cards in the current hand have been drafted.
- After the last round, you tally points from your collected cards. The player with the most delicious, well-assembled sushi platter wins. Or at least the most bragging rights.

### Scoring: the crunchy bits that matter
Sushi Go! uses familiar sushi language that fans love: nigiri, maki, sashimi, dumplings, and special cards like “chopstick” that spice up the drafting in later rounds. In the Pick & Pass edition, you’ll notice a few modifications to scoring emphasis to account for more players and more opportunities to combo cards.
- Nigiri points accumulate by type and size; some nigiri grant more if you have a certain set of other cards.
- Makis are stacked up to show who’s crafting the longest rolls. The bigger the stack, the more points—though there’s risk of being viewed as the “roll thief” by your tablemates.
- Sashimi requires collecting sets; missing a single piece in a set can doom your dreams of creamy sesame glory.
- Dumplings scale with quantity; a common secret strategy is to jam a few dumplings together to push your score past the next player’s sushi tower.
- Dumpling cards can be a trap: too many of them may crowd your score with diminishing returns if you’re not balancing with other types.
- Special cards can grant quirky bonuses or disruptors like negating an opponent’s scoring in a given round.

The balance is designed so that luck plays a role, but smart drafting and reading your tablemates’ tells can swing the outcome dramatically. It’s not just about the cards you choose but about the narrative you weave as you pass, bluff, and bribe with compliments about your neighbor’s extremely impressive chopsticks.

## Strategy and Tactics: Don’t Be a Sushi Rookie
If you’re after a stronger edge without turning the table into a spreadsheet, here are some practical tips you can try next game night:
- Read the room, not just the deck. If you notice a player hoarding a certain card type or playing aggressively for a specific combo, you may want to pivot your plan and deny them the necessary piece.
- Balance risk and reward. In a jam-packed round, going for a high-value but volatile combo can win you the race or cost you the whole table a lot of points. It’s a classic gambler’s dilemma but with more seaweed.
- Use the chopsticks card (if included). In some variants, chopsticks let you pick two cards in a later turn, enabling you to build more elaborate combos. Saving a chopstick for a late round can create a dramatic swing.
- Don’t ignore nudges from tokens. If the edition uses tokens for extra scoring or end-game bonuses, pick tokens that complement your poised card set rather than chasing an existentially cool but weakly synergistic token.
- Watch the endgame rush. In party versions, rounds can accelerate quickly as players near the scoring threshold. Don’t blunder into an over-scored sushi stack simply to squeak out a few more points.
