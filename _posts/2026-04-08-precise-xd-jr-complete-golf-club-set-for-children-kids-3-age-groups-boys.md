If the ball goes linearly with a decent launch, you’re in the right range. If the ball barely travels or shoots skyward in a vertical tantrum, you might be in “too short” territory and need to lengthen. The XD-J kit intends to reduce those trial-and-error moments for both kids and parents.

## Care, Maintenance, and Longevity
Like any tool, the XD-J requires a little maintenance to stay in peak form:

- Wipe the grips with a damp cloth after practice to keep hands from slipping when sweat builds up.
- Check the shaft locks or adjustment mechanisms every few uses to ensure the clubs stay at the correct length.
- Store in a dry place to avoid rust or corrosion on metal components.
- Inspect the heads for chips and cracks; while minor wear is normal, a severely damaged club head can impact alignment and performance.

A well-kept set can serve multiple seasons, reducing the need for constant replacements as your kid grows. The key is to treat the clubs with care and to connect practice days with a sense of enjoyment rather than a forced drill.
