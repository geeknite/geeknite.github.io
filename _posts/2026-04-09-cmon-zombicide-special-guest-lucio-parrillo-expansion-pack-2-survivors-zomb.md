## What you actually get in the Lucio Parrillo Expansion Pack
A common point of confusion with expansions is wondering if you are buying a clever box of pretty miniatures or a functional toolkit that changes the way you play. Parrillo’s take on Zombicide tries to be both: it breathes new life into the aesthetics while introducing a couple of structural changes that keep the game feeling fresh without going off the rails.

- Two new survivors: These are the core of any expansion that pretends to bring a new voice to the table. The two survivors come with their own unique starting kits, a quick reference card, and a handful of starting trait tokens that influence what you can do in the first handful of turns. They push players toward different styles of engagement—one favors aggressive machine-gun tempo, the other rewards careful positioning and resource management. If you enjoy creating small narrative threads for your survivors, these two are ready-made protagonists.
- A cadre of zombies featuring Parrillo’s artwork: Expect new sculpts that feel both monstrous and characterful. The undead here aren’t just bad guys; they’re actors in a play who bring mood and menace to every encounter. The aesthetic emphasis is on bold contrasts, dynamic poses, and dramatic lighting that pops on the table under typical game-room lighting.
- A compact mission book and tokens: The expansion includes a couple of scenarios that leverage the two survivors and the new undead types. The scenarios are designed to be approachable for newer players but also offer a crunchy edge for veterans who like to optimize. Tokens help track new statuses and special rules introduced by the pack.
- Rule clarifications and card materials: You’ll get updated or additional cards that explain the new relationships between the survivors and the zombies. It’s not a full rules rewrite, but it does give you new levers to pull when planning your turns.

In short, you are paying for art-forward minis, two distinctive survivors, and a couple of mission hooks that can extend your Zombicide night without forcing you to buy a whole new system. The balance between new flavor and familiar rules is the expansion’s main trick, and it mostly lands.

### The two survivors and their playstyles
Survivor A and Survivor B are designed to complement and contrast. One tends toward an offensive posture, wielding equipment that rewards aggression but pays a cost in risk exposure. The other leans into positioning, line-of-sight, and resource management, drawing strength from careful planning and altitude of the battlefield.
- Survivor A: A frontline brawler who can push into clusters of zombies with heavy hits. She benefits from armor and melee-focused gear, which makes her feel like a rock among rocks—the kind of character you send to stand in the center and soak hits while your other survivors do the math behind the scenes.
- Survivor B: A tactician with access to better scouting and trap-like items. She is all about tempo and board control—flanking, setting up safe zones, and denying the zombies space to move efficiently. This survivor rewards patient plays and careful risk assessment, which can make for dramatic late-game swings.

While these are archetypal roles, the real joy comes from combining them with your base crew. The synergy with the base game’s zombie pool is well-implemented, allowing your group to lean into either the brute-force approach or a more surgical, strategic plan without feeling like you are playing a separate game.

## How the gameplay shifts with this pack
This expansion does not overhaul Zombicide’s core rules. It adds a new layer of flavor, a couple of new mechanics tied to the survivors, and some new zombie art that can intimidate all the right players. Here’s how it tends to shape the table:
- New survivor-driven tactics: With two survivors offering different paths, your early rounds tend to branch into two distinct gears. You might have one player race toward a heavy-hitting plan while another sets traps and flanks. The tension between these approaches creates a nice dynamic that keeps players engaged beyond simple dice rolls.
- New zombie types or variants: The Parrillo pack introduces variants that feel distinct from the regular walker/runner enemy roster. They demand different responses from the team, which can lengthen the planning horizon and reward good turn sequencing. Expect some moments where a smart trap or a precise melee chain pays off in a memorable way.
- Scenarios to extend campaigns: The two included scenarios aren’t multiplayer-only party games; they’re designed to slot into ongoing campaigns. The missions offer a gentle ramp in difficulty and a paragraph of narrative flavor that gives players a sense of progression and stakes.
- Rule cohesion: The included cards and tokens align with the standard Zombicide rule framework. They do not complicate the game into an unreadable tangle. If you’re comfortable with base Zombicide, you’ll be comfortable here, and if you’re a rules lawyer, you’ll appreciate the attempt to keep things tight and consistent.

From a balance perspective, the expansion sits in a comfortable middle ground. It isn’t an “easy mode” for beginners, nor does it attempt to render the base game obsolete. It’s a tasteful, art-forward add-on that nudges the experience into the Gothic hero’s journey rather than the survivalist slog of more generic zombie games.

### Surviving and thriving: practical tips for these two survivors
- Early game aggression vs mid-game control: If you lean into Survivor A’s front-line role, your early turns should focus on creating safe spaces for the rest of the team to reposition. Use your creature-clear power to reduce immediate pressure. As the board tightens, you’ll want to pivot toward clearing clusters and creating corridors for your teammates.
- Positioning is everything for Survivor B: With the tactician’s toolkit, you can optimize turn order by prioritizing line-of-sight and trap-like items that block zombie advances. Don’t be afraid to pull a few monsters into range and then retreat to a safer area with your more durable teammates.
- Resource management matters: The new gear and tokens add choices for how you allocate actions. The smarter your turnovers, the more you’ll squeeze value from every action. Remember: turning an inefficient turn into a well-placed shot or a precise trap is often worth more than two dice rolls that don’t land.
