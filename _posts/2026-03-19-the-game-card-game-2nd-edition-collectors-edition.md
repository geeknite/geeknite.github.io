## Setup and First Impressions

Setup is the moment where you either feel like a genius or you quietly weep into your oversized player mat. The first run-through will likely produce two outcomes: either the opening hand is a perfect storm of synergy, or you’re staring at a handful of landlocked assets and muttering about your failed RNG. Either way, that is part of the charm. The game rewards persistence, meaning the more you play, the better you become at recognizing how the new cards slot into your existing strategies without turning your brain into a pretzel.

We’ll break down a typical turn sequence here for clarity, though your actual game flow may vary with the whims of your luck and your ability to manage your deck’s foibles.

- Draw phase: You draw up to your hand size. Sometimes you get a clutch set of cards that make you feel like you invented deck-building that day. Other times, you draw your cousin’s old lullaby card that does nothing but remind you that family gatherings can be brutal.
- Action phase: You may play cards, gain resources, or perform actions that affect the table state. The choices matter, but so does the drama of watching a card brag about its own glory text.
- Buy phase: You purchase new cards from the market, layering on more options and more potential paths to victory. It is here you decide whether you want a flashy tactic with immediate payoff or a longer-term engine that could win you the game two rounds from now.
- Cleanup: You discard after a certain limit. This is where you pretend life is simpler than it is, and your deck does some self-actualization therapy as you shuffle.

The flow is familiar if you have dipped your toes in any deck-building title, but the Collector's Edition adds some fresh wrinkles that keep the experience lively without tipping into chaos. The fresh cards often bring new combos, new counters, and new ways for your strategy to be interrupted by the universe of bad luck you adore so much.
